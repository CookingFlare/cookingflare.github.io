Mexican Tacos al Pastor Recipe with Pork and Pineapple


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of Tacos al Pastor. This iconic dish is a flavorful and mouthwatering combination of marinated pork, pineapple, and a variety of spices. Originating in central Mexico, Tacos al Pastor has a rich history and a taste that will have you coming back for more. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the process of making authentic Tacos al Pastor right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 3 pounds of pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried guajillo chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 cup of pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup of white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of achiote paste
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Diced pineapple
  • Chopped onion
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Salsa verde or your favorite hot sauce
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chilies: In a medium saucepan, bring water to a boil. Add the guajillo and ancho chilies, then remove from heat and let them soak for 15 minutes until they soften.

  2. Make the Marinade: In a blender, combine the soaked chilies, garlic, pineapple juice, white vinegar, achiote paste, cumin, oregano, cloves, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  3. Marinate the Pork: Place the thinly sliced pork shoulder in a large bowl and pour the marinade over it. Toss to coat the pork evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.

  4. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If using a gas grill, make sure to oil the grates to prevent sticking.

  5. Grill the Pork: Thread the marinated pork onto skewers. Grill the pork for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until it develops a nice char and is cooked through.

  6. Assemble the Tacos: Warm the corn tortillas on the grill for a few seconds on each side. Fill each tortilla with the grilled pork, diced pineapple, chopped onion, and cilantro. Serve with salsa verde or your favorite hot sauce and lime wedges on the side.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an authentic touch, consider using a vertical spit for grilling the pork, if available.
  • If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook the pork using a cast-iron skillet or a regular skillet on the stovetop.

Conclusion

Tacos al Pastor is a beloved Mexican dish that captures the essence of traditional Mexican flavors. The combination of marinated pork and sweet pineapple, wrapped in a warm corn tortilla, creates a culinary experience that is truly unforgettable. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of Mexico, these Tacos al Pastor are sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create these delectable tacos in the comfort of your own kitchen. Enjoy!

In this blog post, we’ve explored the art of making authentic Tacos al Pastor, a classic Mexican dish that’s bursting with flavor. From marinating the pork to grilling it to perfection, this recipe offers a delightful culinary adventure. So, whether you’re a seasoned chef or a cooking enthusiast, try your hand at crafting these mouthwatering Tacos al Pastor and savor the vibrant taste of Mexico.
