Decadent Salted Caramel Apple Cake: A Fall-Inspired Delight


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to indulge in the warm, comforting flavors of fall. And what better way to celebrate the season than with a luscious and decadent salted caramel apple cake? This delightful dessert combines the sweet-tartness of apples with the rich, buttery goodness of caramel, creating a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re hosting a fall gathering or simply want to treat yourself to a taste of autumn, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 3 cups diced apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)

For the Salted Caramel Sauce:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt

For the Caramel Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup salted caramel sauce (reserved from the above recipe)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Salted Caramel Sauce:

    • In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, heat the granulated sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly with a high heat resistant rubber spatula. The sugar will form clumps and eventually melt into a thick amber-colored liquid as you continue to stir. Be careful not to burn.
    • Once the sugar is completely melted, add the butter. Be careful in this step because the caramel will bubble rapidly when the butter is added. Stir the butter into the caramel until it is completely melted, about 2-3 minutes.
    • Very slowly, drizzle in the heavy cream while stirring. Since the cream is colder than the caramel, the mixture will rapidly bubble and/or splatter when added. Allow the mixture to boil for 1 minute. It will rise in the pan as it boils.
    • Remove from heat and stir in the sea salt. Allow to cool down before using.

  2. Prepare the Cake:

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x13 inch pan.
    • In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
    • In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla. Beat in the flour mixture alternately with the sour cream. Fold in the diced apples. Spread the batter evenly into the prepared pan.
    • Bake for 45-50 minutes in the preheated oven, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow to cool.

  3. Prepare the Caramel Frosting:

    • In a large bowl, beat the butter until creamy. Gradually add the confectioners’ sugar and beat well. Finally, beat in the salted caramel sauce until smooth and creamy.

  4. Assemble the Cake:

    • Once the cake has cooled, drizzle the remaining salted caramel sauce over the top. Spread the caramel frosting evenly over the cake.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Slice the cake into generous portions and serve with a drizzle of extra salted caramel sauce if desired. The combination of moist apple cake, rich salted caramel, and creamy frosting is a true indulgence for the senses.

This Decadent Salted Caramel Apple Cake is a must-have for any fall gathering or as a special treat for yourself. The warm, spiced flavors of the cake combined with the luxurious salted caramel will transport you to a cozy autumn day with every bite. So, embrace the season and savor this fall-inspired delight with your loved ones. Happy baking!
