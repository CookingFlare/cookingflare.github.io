Vegan Pumpkin Bread: Moist and Spiced Fall Treat


Fall is a magical season filled with cozy sweaters, warm drinks, and delicious treats. One iconic flavor that dominates this time of year is pumpkin. From lattes to pies, pumpkin seems to find its way into every recipe. And today, I’m here to share with you a delightful and vegan-friendly twist on a classic fall treat – Vegan Pumpkin Bread.

Ingredients

To make this moist and spiced pumpkin bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ cup unsweetened applesauce
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Mix well to ensure even distribution of the spices.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, vegetable oil, applesauce, and water until well combined.

  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it evenly. Give the pan a gentle tap on the countertop to release any air bubbles.

  6. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 60-70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The baking time may vary, so keep an eye on the bread starting from the 50-minute mark.

  7. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a handful of chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter.
  • If you prefer a sweeter bread, you can sprinkle a cinnamon-sugar mixture on top of the batter before baking.
  • To make it gluten-free, simply substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend of your choice.

Serving and Storing

Serve this vegan pumpkin bread with a hot cup of tea or coffee, and savor the warm, spiced flavors of fall. It makes for a delightful breakfast or an afternoon snack.

To store the bread, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or place it in an airtight container. It will stay fresh at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, you can refrigerate it for up to a week or freeze it for up to 3 months. Just make sure to thaw it before enjoying.

Conclusion

Vegan Pumpkin Bread is a delightful and moist fall treat that captures the essence of the season. With its warm spices and rich pumpkin flavor, it’s the perfect recipe to enjoy as the leaves change color and the air turns crisp. Whether you follow a vegan lifestyle or simply want to try something new, this recipe is sure to satisfy your autumn cravings. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and let the aroma of freshly baked pumpkin bread fill your home. Happy baking!
