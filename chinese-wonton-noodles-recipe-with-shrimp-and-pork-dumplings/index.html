Chinese Wonton Noodles Recipe with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings


If you’re craving a taste of authentic Chinese cuisine, look no further than this mouthwatering recipe for Chinese Wonton Noodles with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings. This classic dish combines the delicate flavors of succulent shrimp and tender pork, wrapped in a thin dumpling skin and served in a flavorful soup with springy noodles. It’s a delightful combination that will surely satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients:

For the dumplings:

  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (Chinese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

For the soup:

  • 1 liter chicken stock
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For serving:

  • 250g fresh egg noodles
  • 2 cups baby bok choy, blanched
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 red chili, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the shrimp, ground pork, spring onions, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, grated ginger, salt, and white pepper. Pulse until well combined, but not too smooth. Set aside.

  2. To assemble the dumplings, place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of a wonton wrapper. Wet the edges with water and fold the wrapper in half to form a triangle. Press the edges to seal, making sure there are no air pockets. Repeat with the remaining filling and wrappers.

  3. In a large pot, bring the chicken stock to a boil. Add the minced garlic, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, oyster sauce, sugar, and salt. Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. While the soup is simmering, cook the fresh egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  5. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the wontons and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Remove the wontons with a slotted spoon and set aside.

  6. To serve, divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls. Top with the blanched baby bok choy and wontons. Ladle the hot soup over the noodles and garnish with sliced spring onions, red chili (if desired), and fresh cilantro leaves.

  7. Enjoy your homemade Chinese Wonton Noodles with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings while it’s hot! The combination of the tender dumplings, flavorful soup, and springy noodles will surely transport you to the bustling streets of China.

Conclusion:

This Chinese Wonton Noodles Recipe with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings is a perfect dish to impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. The delicate flavors of the shrimp and pork dumplings, combined with the savory soup and springy noodles, create a harmonious and satisfying meal. So why not give it a try and bring a taste of China into your own kitchen? Happy cooking!
