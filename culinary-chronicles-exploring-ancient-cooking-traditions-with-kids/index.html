Culinary Chronicles: Exploring Ancient Cooking Traditions with Kids


Our culinary journey today takes us back in time, to uncover the secrets of ancient cooking traditions and share the fun with our little ones. Strap on your aprons, gather the family, and let’s dive into the rich tapestry of history that shaped both our culture and our cuisine. Come join us on this engaging adventure, as we break down ancient recipes into simple steps suitable for kids to perform and enjoy!

1. Roman Figgy Pudding

Ah, the Romans, the amazing builders of aqueducts and impressive sculptures – and culinary innovators of their time! Let’s start our exploration with a simple adaptation of their famous dessert, known as ‘Rustican Pugilatus,’ later renamed ‘Figgy Pudding’ by the British during medieval times. The Roman Pudding was typically stuffed inside a pig’s belly and roasted over a fire. But fret not, for our more sanitized version involves a slow cooker or even a dutch oven along with the following ingredients:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed dried fruit (raisins, currants, and chopped figs)
  • ½ cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1 apple, grated
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter for greasing
  • Warm custard or cream for serving

To prepare:

  1. Soak mixed dried fruit in warm water overnight and drain.
  2. Grease the slow cooker (or a heat-safe bowl) and add the soaked fruit.
  3. Prepare the batter by whisking the egg and milk in a large mixing bowl. Add the brown sugar, vanilla extract, and grated apple, and mix well.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift the flour, baking powder, ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Add this mix to the wet ingredients and combine until just smooth (avoid overly mixing).
  5. Pour the batter over the soaked dried fruit in the slow cooker (or lined dutch oven) and bake, covered, in a preheated oven at 325°F (165°C) for 60-90 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Serve warm with custard or cream – voilà!

2. Ancient Egyptian Falafel

Fun, vibrant, and full of mystery, Ancient Egypt is a powerful inspiration today. Did you know that their love for lentils and fava beans is evident in many old records and recipes? They marveled at the protein-rich seed, blending the finest spices to create a magical taste. Let’s follow their culinary footsteps:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried fava beans (chickpeas may also be used)
  • ½ medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt to taste
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying

To prepare:

  1. Soak the fava beans in warm water, preferably overnight, then drain.
  2. Combine all ingredients, except breadcrumbs and vegetable oil, in a food processor. Blend until a rough, coarse texture is achieved. Avoid over-processing!
  3. Transfer the mixture to a bowl. Add breadcrumbs, then mix carefully using your hands (or a wooden spoon, if you wish) until the paste reaches a shape-able consistency.
  4. Form small balls or patties with the mixture and layout on a plate.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer until it reaches a medium-high temperature (approximately 350°F or 175°C). Gently place the falafel balls or patties into the hot oil and fry until golden brown (about 5-7 minutes).
  6. Serve the falafel hot, with tahini sauce, tabbouleh, or any fresh vegetables you have on hand.

3. Aztec Chocolate Corn Cake

For our final culinary stop, let’s jump on board the Aztec flavor train – intense, bold, and flavorful! Did you know that the classic combination of chocolate and corn was first encountered by the Europeans during their encounter with the Aztecs? Intriguing, isn’t it? Let’s taste this incredible fusion with our homemade corn cake:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup masa harina (you can find this in ethnic or specialty food stores)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or thawed from frozen)
  • 1/3 cup mini semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract

To prepare:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a 10-inch cake pan or cast iron skillet.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Stir together.
  3. Add the corn, chocolate chips, and mix well.
  4. In a separate bowl, beat the milk, egg, vegetable oil, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth. Pour this mixture into the masa mixture and combine until the ingredients are fully absorbed.
  5. Transfer the batter into the greased cake pan or cast iron skillet, and carefully spread it out to cover the entire surface.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

In conclusion, history can be enticing, engaging, and adventurous when we cook our way through time. Grab your kids and wield the magic of the past as you create delicious memories in the present. Happy cooking!
