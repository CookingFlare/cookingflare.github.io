Explore the Exotic Flavors of Indian Street Food with These Delicious Recipes


Indian street food is a culinary delight that has captured the hearts of foodies around the world. The vibrant streets of India are filled with the aroma of spices, the sound of sizzling street food, and the colorful sights of vendors serving up delicious treats. In this post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey to explore the exotic flavors of Indian street food with these delicious recipes.

Kachoris

Kachoris are a popular street food in India, especially in the northern states. These crispy, flaky pastries are typically filled with a spicy potato and pea filling. Here’s a recipe to make kachoris at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • Filling ingredients: boiled and mashed potatoes, peas, onions, ginger, garlic, cumin, coriander, chili powder, amchur powder, and salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder.
  2. Add the ghee or oil and mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Gradually add the lukewarm water and knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls and roll out each ball into a thin circle.
  6. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each circle and fold the dough over to form a triangle or a square shape.
  7. Seal the edges by pressing them with your fingers or a fork.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the kachoris until they are golden brown.
  9. Drain the kachoris on paper towels and serve hot with a dollop of butter or a sprinkle of chaat masala.

Pav Bhaji

Pav bhaji is a popular street food in India, especially in the western states. This spicy vegetable dish is served with soft, buttered buns and a dollop of butter. Here’s a recipe to make pav bhaji at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as cauliflower, carrots, potatoes, and peas)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt, to taste
  • 4-6 pav buns
  • Butter, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the butter in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it is translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the cumin seeds and sauté until they are fragrant.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, and cayenne pepper.
  6. Sauté the mixture for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Add the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth.
  8. Season the mixture with salt to taste.
  9. Serve the pav bhaji hot with soft, buttered buns and a dollop of butter.
