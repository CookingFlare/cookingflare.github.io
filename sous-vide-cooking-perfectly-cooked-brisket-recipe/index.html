Sous Vide Cooking: Perfectly Cooked Brisket Recipe


Sous vide cooking has taken the culinary world by storm, allowing home cooks and professionals alike to achieve restaurant-quality dishes with precision and ease. If you’re looking to elevate your cooking game, there’s no better place to start than with a perfectly cooked brisket. This method not only enhances the flavors but also ensures an incredibly tender texture that will leave your guests begging for seconds. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a mouthwatering sous vide brisket that will impress even the most discerning palates.

Understanding Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide, French for “under vacuum,” is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a bag and cooking it to a very precise temperature in a water bath. This method allows for consistent results and retains moisture and flavor that traditional cooking methods may lose. The beauty of sous vide lies in its ability to break down tough cuts of meat, such as brisket, making them tender while preserving their juiciness.

Benefits of Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Precision: Sous vide allows you to set the exact temperature you want your food to reach, ensuring it’s cooked perfectly every time.
  2. Flavor Retention: Cooking in a sealed bag prevents moisture loss and infuses flavors more effectively than conventional methods.
  3. Consistency: With sous vide, you can replicate the same results time and time again, making it a reliable cooking technique.
  4. Hands-Off Cooking: Once you set your sous vide machine and immerse your food, you can focus on other cooking tasks or enjoy time with your guests.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Brisket

To create your sous vide brisket, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Brisket: A 3 to 5-pound flat brisket is ideal for this recipe.
  • Salt: Kosher salt works best for seasoning.
  • Black Pepper: Freshly cracked for optimal flavor.
  • Garlic Powder: For an aromatic touch.
  • Onion Powder: Adds depth of flavor.
  • Smoked Paprika: For a hint of smokiness.
  • Beef Broth or Stock: For added flavor.
  • Fresh Herbs: Thyme and rosemary pair well with brisket.
  • Vacuum Sealer Bags: Essential for sous vide cooking.

Preparing Your Brisket

Step 1: Seasoning the Brisket

Before you begin cooking, it’s crucial to season your brisket well. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Trim the Brisket: Start by trimming excess fat from the brisket. You want to leave about a quarter-inch of fat for flavor.
  2. Season Generously: In a mixing bowl, combine salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and smoked paprika. Rub this mixture all over the brisket, ensuring every inch is covered. For added flavor, you can let it sit in the refrigerator for a few hours or overnight.

Step 2: Vacuum Sealing

Once your brisket is seasoned, it’s time to seal it for sous vide cooking:

  1. Place in Bag: Place the seasoned brisket into a vacuum sealer bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, a resealable freezer bag will work as well; just remove as much air as possible.
  2. Add Liquid: Pour in a bit of beef broth or stock to keep the brisket moist during the cooking process. You can also add fresh herbs to enhance the flavor.
  3. Seal the Bag: If using a vacuum sealer, follow the manufacturer’s instructions to remove air and seal the bag. If using a freezer bag, lower the bag into water to push out air before sealing.

Setting Up Your Sous Vide

Step 3: Preheating the Water Bath

Before you start cooking, you need to preheat your sous vide water bath:

  1. Fill a Container: Fill a large pot or container with water. Make sure it’s deep enough to fully submerge the brisket.
  2. Set the Temperature: For brisket, the ideal temperature is between 155°F to 165°F (68°C to 74°C) depending on how tender you want it.
    • 155°F (68°C): A firmer texture, sliceable brisket.
    • 165°F (74°C): More tender, falling apart brisket.

Use your sous vide immersion circulator to heat the water to your desired temperature.

Step 4: Cooking the Brisket

Now it’s time to cook the brisket:

  1. Submerge the Bag: Once the water reaches the desired temperature, carefully place the sealed brisket in the water bath. Make sure it’s fully submerged.
  2. Cook Time: Brisket should be cooked for a minimum of 24 hours for optimal tenderness. You can go up to 48 hours if you prefer a more tender outcome.

Finishing Touches

Step 5: Searing the Brisket

After cooking, the brisket will be tender and juicy but may lack the appealing crust that traditional cooking methods achieve. To add that finishing touch, follow these steps:

  1. Remove from Bag: Carefully take the brisket out of the sous vide bag and pat it dry with paper towels. This step is crucial for achieving a good sear.
  2. Preheat a Skillet or Grill: Heat a cast-iron skillet or grill over high heat. Add a bit of oil with a high smoke point, such as canola or avocado oil.
  3. Sear the Brisket: Sear the brisket for about 1-2 minutes on each side until a rich, brown crust forms. Don’t forget to sear the edges as well for added flavor.

Step 6: Resting and Slicing

After searing, it’s essential to let the brisket rest before slicing:

  1. Rest the Meat: Allow the brisket to rest for about 10-15 minutes. This will help the juices redistribute throughout the meat.
  2. Slice Against the Grain: When ready to serve, slice the brisket against the grain. This technique helps to ensure tenderness with each bite.

Serving Suggestions

Your perfectly cooked sous vide brisket is now ready to be enjoyed! Here are some serving ideas:

  • Classic BBQ Style: Serve with your favorite barbecue sauce, coleslaw, and baked beans for a traditional barbecue feast.
  • Tacos: Chop the brisket and serve it in warm tortillas with fresh salsa, avocado, and cilantro for a delicious taco night.
  • Brisket Sandwiches: Pile slices of brisket onto a soft bun with pickles and barbecue sauce for a hearty sandwich.
  • Sides: Pair with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad to round out your meal.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (though we doubt you will!), here’s how to store and reheat your brisket:

  • Refrigeration: Store leftover brisket in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
  • Freezing: For longer storage, wrap slices tightly in plastic wrap and then in aluminum foil before freezing. It can last up to 3 months.
  • Reheating: To reheat, place the brisket back in a sous vide bath at 140°F (60°C) until warmed through. Alternatively, you can microwave or use a skillet over low heat, adding a splash of broth to keep it moist.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide cooking is a game-changer for anyone looking to elevate their culinary skills, and this perfectly cooked brisket recipe is a testament to that. With its tender texture, rich flavor, and endless serving possibilities, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. So next time you want to impress your family or friends, remember this sous vide brisket recipe. Happy cooking!
