Homemade Peach Cobbler Bars: A Taste of Summer


There’s nothing quite like the taste of fresh, juicy peaches in the summertime. If you’re a fan of this delightful fruit, then you’re in for a treat with our homemade peach cobbler bars. These bars are the perfect combination of buttery crust, sweet peaches, and a hint of warm cinnamon – all the flavors of traditional peach cobbler in a convenient handheld form. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, attending a picnic, or simply craving a taste of summer, these peach cobbler bars are sure to impress. Let’s dive into the recipe and bring a taste of summer to your kitchen!

Ingredients

For the Crust and Topping:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Peach Filling:

  • 5 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Pan:

    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking pan and set it aside.

  2. Make the Crust and Topping:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Stir in the beaten egg and vanilla extract until the mixture comes together.

  3. Prepare the Peach Filling:

    • In another bowl, gently toss the sliced peaches with sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice until the peaches are evenly coated.

  4. Assemble the Bars:

    • Press two-thirds of the crust mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking pan, creating an even layer. Reserve the remaining crust mixture for the topping.

  5. Add the Peach Filling:

    • Spread the prepared peach filling over the crust in the pan.

  6. Finish with the Topping:

    • Crumble the remaining crust mixture evenly over the top of the peach filling, creating a crumbly topping.

  7. Bake to Perfection:

    • Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the peach filling is bubbling.

  8. Cool and Serve:

    • Allow the peach cobbler bars to cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares. Serve at room temperature and enjoy the taste of summer!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, consider adding a handful of chopped pecans or almonds to the crust and topping mixture for added crunch and flavor.
  • These peach cobbler bars are delicious on their own, but they’re even better when served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream.

Embrace Summer with Peach Cobbler Bars

With their irresistible combination of sweet, tangy peaches and buttery, crumbly crust, these homemade peach cobbler bars are a delightful way to savor the flavors of summer. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is a fantastic way to showcase the abundance of fresh, seasonal peaches. So, grab a basket of ripe peaches from your local farmer’s market or orchard, and get ready to impress your family and friends with a taste of summer in every bite. Happy baking!
