Kid-Friendly BBQ Recipes for a Fun Fourth of July


The Fourth of July is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious food! As the summer sun shines brightly, it’s the perfect opportunity to fire up the grill and create memorable meals that everyone can enjoy. When it comes to cooking for kids, it’s essential to strike a balance between fun and flavor. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of kid-friendly BBQ recipes that are sure to delight the little ones while keeping the adults satisfied. From colorful skewers to tasty sides, let’s dive into the world of BBQ that’s perfect for your Fourth of July festivities!

1. Colorful Veggie and Fruit Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers (red, yellow, green), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 1 cup mini mozzarella balls
  • Wooden skewers
  • Optional: balsamic glaze for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Skewers: Soak wooden skewers in water for about 30 minutes to prevent burning on the grill.
  2. Assemble the Skewers: Alternate threading the cherry tomatoes, cucumber slices, bell pepper pieces, pineapple chunks, strawberries, and mini mozzarella balls onto the skewers. Get creative with the colors and patterns!
  3. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until the veggies are slightly charred and the cheese is warm.
  4. Serve: Drizzle with balsamic glaze if desired and serve warm. These skewers are not only visually appealing but also packed with nutrients!

2. BBQ Chicken Sliders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 12 slider buns
  • 1 cup coleslaw mix
  • 1/2 cup ranch dressing
  • Pickles for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Grill for about 6-7 minutes on each side or until fully cooked. Remove from the grill and let rest for a few minutes.
  2. Shred the Chicken: Once cooled, shred the chicken using two forks and mix it with BBQ sauce in a bowl.
  3. Prepare the Sliders: In a separate bowl, combine the coleslaw mix with ranch dressing. This will add a creamy crunch to your sliders.
  4. Assemble the Sliders: Slice the slider buns in half. On the bottom half, add a generous scoop of BBQ chicken, followed by a spoonful of coleslaw. Top with pickles and place the other half of the bun on top.
  5. Serve: Arrange the sliders on a platter and watch them disappear! These sliders are perfect for little hands and big appetites.

3. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Herb Butter: In a bowl, mix the softened butter with parsley, chives, dill, salt, and pepper until well combined. Set aside.
  2. Grill the Corn: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the husked corn directly on the grill grates. Grill for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until the corn is tender and slightly charred.
  3. Butter the Corn: Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and slather it with the herb butter while it’s still hot.
  4. Serve: Serve the corn on a platter with extra herb butter on the side. This dish is a classic summer favorite that kids will love!

4. Mini BBQ Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Make the Meatballs: In a large bowl, combine ground meat, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Form the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter.
