International Brunch Recipes for College Students


Welcome to our world tour of international brunch recipes! As a college student, it’s essential to have a repertoire of easy, affordable, and delicious recipes that can be whipped up for a leisurely brunch with friends. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey around the globe, exploring mouthwatering brunch dishes that are perfect for college students. From savory to sweet, these recipes are sure to impress your friends and satisfy your taste buds without breaking the bank. So, grab your apron and let’s dive into the wonderful world of international brunch cuisine!

French Toast Croissants

Ingredients:

  • 4 croissants
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Butter for frying
  • Maple syrup, for serving
  • Fresh berries, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and cinnamon.
  2. Slice the croissants in half lengthwise.
  3. Dip each croissant half into the egg mixture, making sure to coat both sides.
  4. In a skillet over medium heat, melt a knob of butter.
  5. Place the dipped croissant halves in the skillet and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Serve the French toast croissants with a drizzle of maple syrup and a sprinkle of fresh berries.

Spanish Tortilla

Ingredients:

  • 4 potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced potatoes and onions to the skillet and cook until the potatoes are tender.
  3. In a bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the cooked potatoes and onions in the skillet.
  5. Cook on low heat until the edges are set and the center is slightly runny.
  6. Flip the tortilla using a large plate and cook for a few more minutes until fully set.
  7. Slide the tortilla onto a serving plate and cut into wedges.

Japanese Okonomiyaki

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 strips of bacon, chopped
  • Okonomiyaki sauce (or a mixture of ketchup and Worcestershire sauce)
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Bonito flakes (optional)
  • Aonori (dried seaweed flakes, optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, green onions, flour, water, and eggs.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a bit of oil.
  3. Pour a ladleful of the cabbage mixture into the skillet and spread it out into a round pancake shape.
  4. Place a few pieces of chopped bacon on top of the pancake.
  5. Cook until the bottom is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side.
  6. Drizzle the okonomiyaki sauce and mayonnaise over the cooked pancake.
  7. Sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori, if using.

Mexican Churro Waffles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Cinnamon sugar for coating

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your waffle maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the eggs, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  5. Pour the batter into the preheated waffle maker and cook until golden and crisp.
  6. While the waffles are still warm, coat them in cinnamon sugar.

Conclusion

There you have it, a delightful array of international brunch recipes that are perfect for college students. Whether you’re craving something sweet like French Toast Croissants or something savory like Spanish Tortilla, these recipes are simple, affordable, and bursting with flavor. So, gather your friends, put on some good music, and enjoy a global brunch feast without leaving the comfort of your college dorm! Happy cooking and bon appétit!
