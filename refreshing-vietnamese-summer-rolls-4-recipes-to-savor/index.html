Refreshing Vietnamese Summer Rolls: 4 Recipes to Savor


As the warm weather rolls in, there’s nothing quite like the light, fresh taste of Vietnamese summer rolls to keep you feeling cool and satisfied. These delightful rolls, known as “gỏi cuốn,” are a staple in Vietnamese cuisine, offering a burst of flavors and textures wrapped in delicate rice paper. Not only are they simple to prepare, but they are also versatile and can be customized with a variety of fillings. In this blog post, we’ll explore four unique recipes for refreshing summer rolls that will tantalize your taste buds and impress your family and friends.

What Are Vietnamese Summer Rolls?

Vietnamese summer rolls are fresh, un-fried rolls that typically consist of rice paper wrappers filled with an array of ingredients. They often include fresh vegetables, herbs, protein, and sometimes noodles. Served with a dipping sauce, these rolls are not only delicious but also visually appealing, making them perfect for gatherings or a light meal on a hot day.

The Basics of Making Summer Rolls

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s cover some essential tips for making perfect summer rolls:

  1. Rice Paper Wrappers: You can find them at any Asian grocery store. They need to be soaked briefly in warm water to become pliable.

  2. Filling Ingredients: The beauty of summer rolls lies in their versatility. You can use shrimp, chicken, tofu, or even keep them vegetarian with an abundance of fresh veggies.

  3. Herbs: Fresh herbs like mint, cilantro, and basil are essential for providing that authentic Vietnamese flavor.

  4. Dipping Sauce: A flavorful dipping sauce complements the rolls beautifully. Peanut sauce or hoisin sauce is a popular choice.

Now that we’ve covered the basics, let’s get into our four refreshing summer roll recipes!

Recipe 1: Classic Shrimp and Avocado Summer Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 16 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup lettuce leaves (such as romaine or butter lettuce)
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 cup vermicelli noodles, cooked according to package instructions

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Shrimp: Boil the shrimp in salted water for about 2-3 minutes until pink and cooked through. Remove and let cool. Slice each shrimp in half lengthwise.

  2. Soak the Rice Paper: Fill a large shallow dish with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper in the water for about 10-15 seconds until it becomes soft and pliable. Remove and place it on a clean, damp kitchen towel.

  3. Assemble the Roll: On the lower third of the rice paper, layer 2 halves of shrimp, a few slices of avocado, a small handful of lettuce, some vermicelli noodles, and a couple of mint and basil leaves.

  4. Roll It Up: Starting from the bottom, roll the rice paper tightly over the filling, tucking the sides in as you go. Repeat with the remaining ingredients.

  5. Serve with Sauce: These rolls are best served fresh with a side of peanut sauce or hoisin sauce for dipping!

Recipe 2: Tofu and Vegetable Summer Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 cup julienned carrots
  • 1 cup cucumber, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced thin
  • 1 cup sprouts (like bean sprouts or alfalfa)
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tofu: Slice the tofu into thin strips and sauté in a pan with sesame oil and soy sauce over medium heat until golden brown. Allow to cool.

  2. Soak the Rice Paper: As in the previous recipe, soak a rice paper wrapper in warm water until pliable, then lay it on a damp towel.

  3. Fill the Wrapper: On the lower third of the wrapper, place a few strips of tofu, a small handful of carrots, cucumbers, bell pepper slices, sprouts, and cilantro.

  4. Roll It Up: Roll the rice paper tightly, making sure to tuck in the sides.

  5. Dipping Sauce: Serve these rolls with a spicy hoisin or a sesame peanut sauce for added flavor.

Recipe 3: Mango and Crab Summer Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup fresh crab meat (cooked)
  • 1 ripe mango, sliced thin
  • 1 cup cucumber, julienned
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, mix the crab meat with lime juice and fish sauce for added flavor.

  2. Soak the Rice Paper: Soak a rice paper wrapper in warm water until soft, then place it on a damp towel.

  3. Layer the Ingredients: On the lower third of the wrapper, add a scoop of the crab mixture, a few slices of mango, some cucumber, and a handful of mint and basil leaves.

  4. Roll It Up: Roll the rice paper tightly, tucking in the sides as you go.

  5. Serve: These refreshing rolls are perfect for a summer appetizer and pair wonderfully with a sweet chili sauce.

Recipe 4: Spicy Chicken and Cucumber Summer Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 2 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup cucumber, julienned
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the shredded chicken with sriracha and rice vinegar. Mix until well coated.

  2. Soak the Rice Paper: Dip a rice paper wrapper in warm water until soft, then lay it on a damp towel.

  3. Fill the Wrapper: On the lower third of the wrapper, place a portion of the spicy chicken, cucumber, carrots, and cilantro.

  4. Roll It Up: Carefully roll the rice paper tightly, tucking in the sides to secure the filling.

  5. Dipping Sauce: Serve these rolls with a tangy lime and soy sauce.

Tips for Perfect Summer Rolls

  • Freshness is Key: Use the freshest vegetables and herbs you can find. The quality of your ingredients will significantly affect the taste of your rolls.

  • Practice Makes Perfect: If you’re new to rolling, don’t worry if your first few rolls don’t look perfect. They will still taste delicious, and you’ll improve with practice!

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the fillings in advance and assemble the rolls just before serving to keep the rice paper from drying out.

  • Storing Leftovers: If you have leftover rolls, store them in an airtight container in the fridge. They are best enjoyed within a day.

Conclusion

Refreshing Vietnamese summer rolls are the perfect dish to enjoy during the warm months. With their vibrant colors, fresh ingredients, and endless possibilities for customization, they are sure to be a hit at any gathering or a light meal at home. Whether you choose shrimp and avocado, tofu and vegetables, mango and crab, or spicy chicken, each recipe offers a unique taste that captures the essence of Vietnamese cuisine.

So gather your ingredients, invite some friends over, and savor these delightful summer rolls that are not only a feast for the palate but also a feast for the eyes. Enjoy the freshness, the crunch, and the burst of flavor with every bite!
