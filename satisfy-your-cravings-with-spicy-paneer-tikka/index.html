Satisfy Your Cravings with Spicy Paneer Tikka


Are you a fan of Indian cuisine? Do you crave that perfect blend of spices and flavors? Look no further because today, we’re going to satisfy your cravings with a mouthwatering recipe for Spicy Paneer Tikka!

Paneer Tikka is a popular Indian appetizer that consists of cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) marinated in a spicy yogurt-based mixture, skewered, and grilled to perfection. It’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as an appetizer, a side dish, or even as a main course. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 400 grams of paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup of thick yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth marinade.

  2. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and gently toss to coat them evenly. Allow the paneer to marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat. If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for about 15 minutes to prevent them from burning.

  4. Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto the skewers, leaving a little space between each cube.

  5. Brush the skewered paneer with oil to prevent sticking and enhance the grilling process.

  6. Place the skewers on the grill or in the preheated oven and cook for about 8-10 minutes, turning them occasionally, until the paneer is lightly charred and cooked through.

  7. Once cooked, remove the skewers from the grill or oven and let them cool slightly.

  8. Garnish the Spicy Paneer Tikka with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with mint chutney or your favorite dipping sauce.

Tips and Variations

  • If you want to add an extra kick to your Spicy Paneer Tikka, feel free to add some chopped green chilies or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the marinade.
  • You can also experiment with different spices and herbs to customize the flavor of your Paneer Tikka. Try adding some crushed kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) or a sprinkle of chaat masala for a tangy twist.
  • If you don’t have a grill or oven, you can also cook the Paneer Tikka on a stovetop. Simply heat a non-stick pan over medium heat, brush it with oil, and cook the skewers for about 8-10 minutes, turning them occasionally.

So, there you have it - a tantalizing recipe for Spicy Paneer Tikka that will surely satisfy your cravings for bold and flavorful Indian cuisine. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply looking for a delicious snack, this dish is bound to impress. Give it a try and let your taste buds embark on a culinary journey to India!

Enjoy and happy cooking!
