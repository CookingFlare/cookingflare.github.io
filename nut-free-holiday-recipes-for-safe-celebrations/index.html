Nut-Free Holiday Recipes for Safe Celebrations


With the holiday season upon us, many individuals with nut allergies or sensitivities must navigate the often treacherous culinary landscape of festivities. Stores and homes are filled with nut-based baked goods, candies, and decorations, which pose a significant risk to those who require a nut-free diet. Fear not, dear readers, for there are numerous delightful and delicious holiday recipes that don't contain a single nut. In this post, we'll explore a variety of creative and tasty ideas that cater to those with even the strictest dietary requirements. Start with Classic Favorites Consider starting the holidays with a nut-free rendition of the beloved Sugar Cookie. These tender treats are made with creamy butter, shimmering sugar, and a delicate tang from lemon zest. Lemon Sugar Cookies

1 cup (200g) unsalted butter, softened 1 1/2 cups (330g) granulated sugar 2 large eggs 2 teaspoons vanilla extract 2 3/4 cups (315g) all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 cup (60g) freshly grated lemon zest

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugar until fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract. Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add to the wet ingredients, mixing until combined. Stir in lemon zest. On a lightly floured surface, roll out dough to 1/8" (3mm) thickness. Cut into shapes and bake for 10-12 minutes.

Other classic holiday treats that can be adapted to be nut-free include Gingerbread, Shortbread, and Bruce Fogel's special dressing cookies.
