Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken: Sweet and Tangy


Are you craving a delicious, tropical-inspired dish that practically cooks itself? Look no further than Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken. This recipe combines the sweet and tangy flavors of pineapple and barbecue sauce with tender, juicy chicken, all effortlessly prepared in your trusty slow cooker. Whether you’re planning a laid-back family dinner or hosting a casual get-together with friends, this dish is sure to impress with its irresistible flavors and minimal prep time.

Ingredients

To recreate this mouthwatering Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken at home, gather the following ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce (use your favorite brand or homemade)
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1 cup diced pineapple (fresh or canned)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Start by placing the boneless, skinless chicken breasts in the slow cooker. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

  2. Mix the Sauce: In a mixing bowl, combine the barbecue sauce, pineapple juice, diced pineapple, soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, and ground ginger. Mix well until everything is thoroughly combined.

  3. Pour Over Chicken: Pour the sauce mixture over the chicken breasts in the slow cooker, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated.

  4. Cook Slow and Low: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low heat for 6-7 hours or on high heat for 3-4 hours. Cooking times may vary depending on your slow cooker, so check for doneness after the minimum time.

  5. Shred the Chicken (Optional): Once the chicken is cooked through and tender, you have the option to shred it using two forks directly in the slow cooker for a pulled chicken texture.

  6. Thicken the Sauce (Optional): If you prefer a thicker sauce, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water in a small bowl. Stir the cornstarch slurry into the slow cooker with the chicken and sauce mixture. Cover and cook on high for an additional 15-30 minutes until the sauce has thickened.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken hot over cooked rice, quinoa, or your favorite side dish. Garnish with sliced green onions or sesame seeds for added flavor and presentation.

Tips for Success

  • Chicken Selection: Opt for boneless, skinless chicken breasts for this recipe as they cook evenly and absorb the flavors well.

  • Barbecue Sauce: Choose a barbecue sauce that complements the sweet pineapple flavors. Hickory or sweet and smoky varieties work particularly well.

  • Pineapple Juice: Use unsweetened pineapple juice for a natural sweetness without added sugars.

  • Adjusting Sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter dish, increase the amount of brown sugar slightly. For a tangier flavor, add a splash of apple cider vinegar.

  • Meal Prep: This dish is perfect for meal prepping. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze for longer storage.

Why Choose Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken?

Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken is not only a crowd-pleaser but also a convenient option for busy weekdays or lazy weekends. The slow cooker does all the work for you, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully while you go about your day. The combination of sweet pineapple, tangy barbecue sauce, and tender chicken creates a harmony of tastes that will transport you to a tropical paradise with every bite.

This dish is versatile too. Serve it over rice for a complete meal, or use it as a filling for sandwiches, tacos, or wraps for a delicious twist. Whether you’re feeding a family or entertaining guests, Slow Cooker Hawaiian BBQ Chicken is a reliable choice that promises to satisfy even the pickiest eaters.

So, fire up your slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to a taste of the islands with this easy and flavorful recipe. Your taste buds—and your dinner guests—will thank you for it!
