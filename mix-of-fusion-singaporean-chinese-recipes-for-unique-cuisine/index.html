Mix of Fusion: Singaporean-Chinese Recipes for Unique Cuisine


Imagine tantalizing your taste buds with the vibrant flavors of Singaporean and Chinese cuisines, artfully blended to create truly unique, mouth-watering dishes that are sure to astound your dining companions. In this blog post, we will introduce a blend of Singaporean-Chinese recipes that artfully coalesce the best of two worlds into unforgettable culinary experiences.

Chinese cooking techniques, spices, and ingredients seamlessly combine with Singaporean flavors to form an unparalleled taste adventure. By fusing diverse elements, this fusion cuisine challenges traditional boundaries and encourages experimentation in the kitchen. For those up to the challenge, the results are sure to be rewarding.

Let’s dive right into a selection of standout Singaporean-Chinese fusion dishes and explore how to bring these inspired recipes to life in your own kitchen.

  1. Haekee Mee (Spicy Prawn Noodles)

This dish combines the piquant flavors of Singapore’s iconic Hae Mee with the rich, savory taste of Chinese-style steamed dumplings. You’ll find yourself enveloped in a symphony of flavors with every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 8 - 10 medium prawns, deveined and butterflied
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 red bird’s eye chillies, sliced
  • 2 tbsp thick soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 150g egg noodles
  • 4 medium Chinese mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 4 spring onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fried shallots

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or large frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and chillies, and stir-fry until fragrant.

  2. Add the prawns and cook until pink and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.

  3. In a bowl, mix the thick soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce. Set aside.

  4. In the same wok, heat a little more oil and add the egg noodles. Stir-fry for 2 minutes before adding the mushrooms, bean sprouts, and sauce mixture.

  5. Once the mushrooms are tender and the noodles are cooked, return the prawns to the pan and mix well.

  6. Garnish with sliced spring onions and fried shallots before serving.

  7. Char Siew Burger with Pineapple Slaw

Bring the robust taste of Chinese BBQ pork to life in this gourmet burger that reinvents the classic Singaporean chili crab with a Caribbean flair by using pineapple slaw. Line a warm brioche bun topped with rich char siew and tangy pineapple slaw that is sure to please any palate.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 200g pork belly, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 brioche burger buns
  • 100g green cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 pineapple, flesh cut into small chunks
  • 2 small red onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, five spice powder, and sugar. Coat the pork cubes and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Preheat a large frying pan with vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated pork until well-browned and tender. Set aside.

  3. Slightly toast the burger buns, if desired.

  4. In a bowl, mix the shredded cabbage, pineapple, red onion, lime juice, salt, and pepper.

  5. Assemble the burgers with a dollop of char siew on the bottom bun, followed by generous portions of pineapple slaw, and topped with the remaining bun top.

  6. Optionally, serve with a side of sweet chili sauce or your preferred burger condiments.

  7. Orh Nee Chendol

Meld the sweet, smooth taste of the traditional Singaporean dessert orh nee with the refreshing textures of chendol for an unforgettable Asian fusion dessert. Your sweet tooth will thank you as you enjoy this refreshing treat.

Ingredients:

  • 150g sweet potatoes or yam, peeled and grated
  • 200g palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1.5 liters water
  • 8 - 10 pandan leaves
  • 1 cup red beans, soaked overnight and cooked
  • 2 cups green rice flour jelly or cendol
  • 8 - 10 tablespoons thick coconut milk
  • Jackfruit, sliced to decorate

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the coconut milk and drain the water before adding the pandan leaves and water to a boil.
  2. Add the grated sweet potatoes or yam and cook until soft.
  3. In a separate bowl, dissolve the palm sugar in water.
  4. Add palm sugar syrup into the pot and let it simmer for a few minutes before adding in the cooked red beans.
  5. Remove the pandan leaves, then serve the Orh Nee Chendol in small bowls.
  6. Add a generous scoop of green rice flour jelly or cendol along with a spoonful of the sweet potato mix, thick coconut milk, and sliced jackfruit on top.

By crafting these Singaporean-Chinese fusion dishes, you’ll be on an authentic taste adventure, blending two worlds and testing the boundaries of traditional cooking. The key to success in fusion cooking is to strike a balance between different flavors, spices, and textures while keeping the common themes intact. These recipes are just a starting point - feel free to experiment and let your culinary imagination run wild!
