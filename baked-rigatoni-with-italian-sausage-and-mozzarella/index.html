Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella


If you’re in the mood for a hearty and comforting meal, look no further than Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella. This dish brings together tender pasta, savory sausage, and a melty, cheesy top to create the perfect baked pasta casserole. It’s a crowd-pleasing recipe that works just as well for a family dinner as it does for a gathering of friends.

This dish is loaded with flavor, combining the richness of Italian sausage, the creamy texture of mozzarella, and the depth of a well-seasoned tomato sauce. Whether you’re an experienced cook or someone just starting to explore Italian cuisine, this baked rigatoni recipe is easy to follow and sure to impress.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to gather. Each one plays a key role in making this dish stand out. While you may already have some pantry staples, make sure to get fresh mozzarella and quality sausage for the best results.

Pasta:

  • Rigatoni (1 pound): The large, ridged pasta tubes hold the sauce and cheese beautifully, creating a delightful bite.

Meat:

  • Italian sausage (1 pound): Use mild or spicy sausage depending on your taste preferences. The sausage will give the dish a rich, savory flavor that balances out the cheese and sauce.

Sauce:

  • Tomato sauce (2 cups): You can use store-bought marinara sauce, but homemade tomato sauce will elevate the dish even more.
  • Crushed tomatoes (1 can, 14 ounces): Adding crushed tomatoes to the mix creates a slightly thicker texture and gives the sauce more body.
  • Garlic (3 cloves): Fresh garlic adds depth and an aromatic touch to the sauce.
  • Onion (1 medium): A finely chopped onion provides sweetness to contrast the savory sausage.
  • Olive oil (2 tablespoons): Used to sauté the sausage and vegetables, providing a smooth, rich base for the sauce.

Cheese:

  • Fresh mozzarella (2 cups, shredded or cubed): Mozzarella is the star of the show here, providing a stretchy, gooey texture that’s essential for baked pasta.
  • Parmesan cheese (½ cup): A touch of grated parmesan adds an extra layer of flavor and helps create a beautiful golden-brown crust on top of the dish.

Seasoning:

  • Dried oregano (1 teaspoon): Oregano is a classic Italian herb that enhances the flavor of the tomato sauce.
  • Dried basil (1 teaspoon): Basil adds a fragrant note and complements the sausage and tomatoes.
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, ½ teaspoon): If you like a bit of heat, red pepper flakes will give the dish a pleasant kick.
  • Salt and pepper (to taste): Season the sauce and pasta water to perfection.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s walk through the process of making this incredible dish. While this may seem like a complicated recipe, it’s actually quite simple and rewarding. Here’s how you’ll prepare Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella.

1. Prepare the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the rigatoni and cook according to the package instructions, usually around 9-11 minutes, until it’s just al dente. The pasta will continue to cook in the oven, so be careful not to overcook it during this stage.

Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and set it aside. Make sure to reserve about a cup of pasta water – this starchy liquid will help bring the sauce together later on.

2. Cook the Italian Sausage

In a large skillet or sauté pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the Italian sausage to the pan, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Brown the sausage for about 5-7 minutes until it’s cooked through and has developed a nice color.

Once the sausage is browned, remove it from the skillet and set it aside. Don’t worry if some of the browned bits are left in the pan – they’ll add even more flavor to the sauce.

3. Prepare the Tomato Sauce

In the same skillet, add a little more olive oil if needed and toss in the finely chopped onion. Sauté the onion for 3-4 minutes until it becomes soft and translucent. Add the minced garlic to the pan and cook for an additional 1 minute, allowing it to become fragrant.

Next, add the crushed tomatoes and tomato sauce to the skillet. Stir everything together and bring the mixture to a simmer. Allow the sauce to cook for about 10-15 minutes, letting the flavors meld together. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking.

4. Season the Sauce

Now it’s time to season the sauce. Add the dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give the sauce a final stir and let it simmer for a few more minutes.

Once the sauce has thickened slightly, return the cooked sausage to the pan. Stir it into the sauce so the sausage is evenly distributed. If the sauce feels too thick, you can add a bit of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.

5. Combine the Pasta and Sauce

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) while you finish assembling the dish. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked rigatoni and the sausage-tomato sauce mixture. Stir until the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce. If you want, you can reserve a little bit of sauce to spoon over the top of the dish later.

6. Layer the Dish with Cheese

Take a 9x13-inch baking dish and spread a thin layer of the pasta-sauce mixture at the bottom. Next, layer half of the shredded mozzarella on top of the pasta. Sprinkle some parmesan cheese over the mozzarella for extra flavor.

Continue layering the pasta, followed by the remaining mozzarella and parmesan cheese. The cheese will melt beautifully as it bakes, creating a rich, golden crust.

7. Bake the Rigatoni

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake the rigatoni for 25-30 minutes, or until it’s bubbling and the cheese is melted. Remove the foil during the last 5 minutes of baking to allow the top to become golden and crispy.

Once done, remove the dish from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This helps the sauce thicken and makes it easier to serve.

Tips for Perfect Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella

To make sure your baked rigatoni is perfect every time, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  1. Use Fresh Mozzarella – For the best texture and flavor, always use fresh mozzarella. It melts beautifully and provides a creamy, gooey consistency that’s perfect for baked pasta dishes.

  2. Don’t Overcook the Pasta – Since the pasta will bake in the oven, it’s important not to overcook it during the boiling stage. Al dente pasta works best for baked dishes because it holds up better during the baking process.

  3. Make It Ahead of Time – This dish can be assembled ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. When you’re ready to bake it, just pop it in the oven. This is a great option for meal prep or hosting dinner parties.

  4. Add Veggies – For extra flavor and nutrition, you can add vegetables to the sauce. Spinach, bell peppers, or mushrooms all pair beautifully with the sausage and tomato sauce.

  5. Customize the Meat – While Italian sausage is the star of this recipe, you can substitute it with ground beef, turkey sausage, or chicken sausage depending on your preference.

  6. Try Different Cheeses – While mozzarella and parmesan are traditional, you can also experiment with other cheeses like ricotta, provolone, or fontina for added richness and variety.

Why You’ll Love This Baked Rigatoni

Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella is the epitome of comfort food. The combination of al dente pasta, savory sausage, and gooey cheese makes it a satisfying meal for any occasion. It’s a complete dish in itself, packed with protein, vegetables, and hearty pasta, all brought together with a flavorful tomato sauce.

This recipe is incredibly versatile and can be adjusted based on your preferences. Whether you prefer a spicier sausage or a milder sauce, the flavors are fully customizable. It’s a perfect dish for a cozy family meal or a gathering with friends, and it’s sure to leave everyone asking for seconds.

So next time you’re craving something filling and comforting, give this Baked Rigatoni with Italian Sausage and Mozzarella a try. With its rich layers of pasta, savory sausage, and melted cheese, it’s guaranteed to be a hit at the dinner table. Enjoy!
