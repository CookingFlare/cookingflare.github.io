Savory and Spicy Mapo Tofu Recipe for Authentic Chinese Cuisine


If you’re a fan of authentic Chinese cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this savory and spicy Mapo Tofu recipe. Originating from the Sichuan province, Mapo Tofu is a beloved classic known for its bold flavors and fiery kick. This dish perfectly balances the numbing sensation of Sichuan peppercorns with the rich umami of fermented black beans, creating a symphony of flavors that will tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 1 block of soft tofu
  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons fermented black beans
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 2-3 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons chili bean paste
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by preparing the tofu. Cut the tofu into small cubes and set aside. In a small bowl, combine the garlic, ginger, and fermented black beans. In another bowl, mix the chili bean paste, light soy sauce, and sugar.

  2. Toast the Sichuan Peppercorns: In a dry pan, toast the Sichuan peppercorns over low heat until fragrant. Be careful not to burn them. Once toasted, grind the peppercorns into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.

  3. Cooking: Heat the cooking oil in a wok or a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the ground pork and stir-fry until it’s no longer pink. Add the garlic, ginger, and fermented black bean mixture, and continue to stir-fry for another minute until fragrant.

  4. Add the Flavor Base: Next, add the chili bean paste, soy sauce, and sugar mixture to the pan. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Pour in the Broth: Once the aromatics and seasonings are well combined, pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  6. Incorporate the Tofu: Gently slide the tofu cubes into the simmering sauce. Be careful not to break the tofu cubes. Allow the tofu to simmer in the sauce for 5-7 minutes, allowing it to absorb the flavors.

  7. Finishing Touch: Finally, sprinkle the ground Sichuan peppercorns over the Mapo Tofu. Add the chopped spring onions and give everything a gentle stir. Season with salt to taste.

  8. Serve: Transfer the Mapo Tofu to a serving dish and serve hot, with steamed rice on the side.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, you can substitute the ground pork with finely diced shiitake mushrooms for a meaty texture.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less chili bean paste according to your preference.
  • Sichuan peppercorns provide a unique numbing sensation. If you’re not a fan of this sensation, you can reduce the amount of peppercorns used.

Conclusion

This savory and spicy Mapo Tofu recipe brings the authentic flavors of Sichuan cuisine to your kitchen. With its complex blend of spicy, savory, and numbing flavors, it’s a dish that never fails to impress. Whether you’re a seasoned fan of Chinese cuisine or new to the flavors of Sichuan, this Mapo Tofu recipe is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Enjoy the bold and authentic flavors of this classic Chinese dish right at home!
