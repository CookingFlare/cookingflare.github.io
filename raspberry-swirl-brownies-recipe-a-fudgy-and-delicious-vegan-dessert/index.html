Raspberry Swirl Brownies Recipe: A Fudgy and Delicious Vegan Dessert


Craving a decadent and indulgent treat but looking for a vegan option? Look no further than these delectable Raspberry Swirl Brownies! Rich, fudgy, and bursting with the sweet-tart flavor of raspberries, these vegan brownies are a delightful dessert that will satisfy your sweet tooth and impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

For the Brownie Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Raspberry Swirl:

  • 1 1/2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.

  2. In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, maple syrup, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds. Set the raspberry puree aside to cool.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

  4. In a separate bowl, mix the applesauce, melted coconut oil, almond milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  6. Pour the brownie batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly.

  7. Spoon dollops of the raspberry puree on top of the brownie batter. Use a knife or toothpick to swirl the raspberry puree into the brownie batter, creating a beautiful marbled effect.

  8. Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are set and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs.

  9. Once baked, remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan.

  10. Once cooled, lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang, then slice them into squares.

  11. Serve the raspberry swirl brownies and enjoy the rich, fudgy texture complemented by the vibrant raspberry swirl.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, you can sprinkle dairy-free chocolate chips on top of the brownie batter before swirling in the raspberry puree.
  • If you prefer a sweeter raspberry swirl, adjust the amount of maple syrup to taste.
  • These brownies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Final Thoughts

These Raspberry Swirl Brownies are a testament to the fact that vegan desserts can be just as rich and satisfying as their non-vegan counterparts. The combination of dark, fudgy brownie and the bright, tangy raspberry swirl creates a flavor experience that is sure to please anyone, regardless of their dietary preferences. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan baker or just dipping your toes into the world of plant-based baking, these brownies are a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in a delicious treat that’s both vegan and utterly irresistible!
