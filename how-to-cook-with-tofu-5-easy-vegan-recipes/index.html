How to Cook with Tofu: 5 Easy Vegan Recipes


Tofu, also known as bean curd, is one of the most versatile ingredients in the world of vegan cooking. With its ability to absorb the flavors of any dish, tofu is a perfect protein-packed addition to countless meals. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, tofu offers a wealth of opportunities for creative and healthy cooking.

In this post, we’ll explore five easy and delicious vegan tofu recipes that you can whip up in no time. These dishes are perfect for weeknight dinners, meal prep, or impressing friends at your next gathering. From crispy tofu to creamy tofu desserts, we’ve got something for everyone.

1. Crispy Tofu Stir-Fry

A crispy tofu stir-fry is a quick and satisfying meal. The key to making tofu crispy is to remove excess moisture and then pan-fry it until golden brown. This stir-fry is customizable with your favorite vegetables and can be paired with rice or noodles for a filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu (pressed)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or any neutral oil)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu: Start by pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture. Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel and place something heavy (like a skillet) on top. Let it press for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the tofu: Once pressed, cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes. In a bowl, toss the tofu cubes with soy sauce and cornstarch until evenly coated.
  3. Cook the tofu: Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden and crispy on all sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add the garlic and ginger and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the bell pepper, carrot, zucchini, and broccoli. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Combine: Add the crispy tofu back to the pan. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the tofu and vegetables, tossing to coat evenly.
  6. Serve: Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles for a complete meal.

This dish is not only full of flavor but also loaded with nutrients. The crispy tofu adds a satisfying crunch, while the vegetables provide a variety of textures and colors.

2. Tofu Scramble

A tofu scramble is a classic vegan alternative to scrambled eggs. It’s protein-packed, quick to make, and can be customized with a variety of vegetables and seasonings. This recipe is perfect for a hearty breakfast or brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu (pressed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh herbs (optional), for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tofu: Press the tofu to remove excess moisture. Once pressed, crumble the tofu with your hands or a fork into small, scrambled-like pieces.
  2. Cook the vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the spinach and cook for another 1-2 minutes until wilted.
  3. Scramble the tofu: Add the crumbled tofu to the skillet with the vegetables. Stir well to combine. Add turmeric, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tofu is heated through and slightly golden.
  4. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro, if desired. Serve the tofu scramble with toast, avocado, or roasted potatoes for a complete meal.

This tofu scramble is a great way to start your day with a nutrient-dense meal. You can experiment with different vegetables, spices, and herbs to make it your own.

3. Tofu Buddha Bowl

A Buddha bowl is a nutritious, colorful meal that is as satisfying as it is beautiful. Packed with vegetables, grains, and a protein source like tofu, this meal can be easily customized based on your preferences and what you have on hand. The tofu in this dish is marinated and baked to perfection, creating a savory flavor that pairs beautifully with fresh vegetables and a tangy dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu (pressed)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 cup quinoa or brown rice, cooked
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 1/2 cup edamame beans
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Water, to thin the dressing

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu: Press the tofu for 15-20 minutes to remove excess moisture. Once pressed, cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes.
  2. Marinate the tofu: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and grated ginger. Pour the marinade over the tofu cubes and let it sit for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Bake the tofu: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and arrange the marinated tofu cubes in a single layer. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the tofu is golden and slightly crispy.
  4. Assemble the bowl: In a large bowl, layer the quinoa or brown rice, shredded carrots, cucumber, edamame, and avocado slices.
  5. Make the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and a little water to thin it out. Adjust the consistency to your liking.
  6. Serve: Top the Buddha bowl with the baked tofu and drizzle with the tahini dressing. Enjoy!

This Buddha bowl is a perfect example of how tofu can be a versatile addition to a plant-based meal. It’s packed with healthy fats, fiber, and protein, making it a well-rounded dish.

4. Tofu and Vegetable Curry

A rich, flavorful tofu and vegetable curry is an excellent way to enjoy tofu while incorporating vibrant spices. The tofu soaks up the curry sauce, creating a savory and comforting dish. Serve it over rice or with naan for a delicious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu (pressed)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, bell peppers, peas)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Press and cube the tofu: Press the tofu for 15-20 minutes and cut it into cubes.
  2. Cook the tofu: Heat the coconut oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook for 5-7 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove the tofu from the pot and set aside.
  3. Prepare the curry: In the same pot, add the onion, garlic, and ginger. Cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, and cumin and cook for 1 minute to release the flavors.
  4. Make the curry sauce: Add the coconut milk, vegetable broth, soy sauce, and maple syrup. Stir well to combine and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Add the vegetables: Add the mixed vegetables to the pot and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until they are tender.
  6. Finish the curry: Add the cooked tofu back into the pot and stir gently. Let the curry simmer for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve: Serve the tofu and vegetable curry over cooked rice and garnish with fresh cilantro.

This tofu and vegetable curry is a hearty, flavorful meal that will warm you up on a cold day. The combination of coconut milk and spices makes the dish rich and satisfying.

5. Chocolate Tofu Mousse

Tofu isn’t just for savory dishes! It can also be used to make a creamy, decadent dessert like chocolate mousse. This tofu mousse is rich, smooth, and surprisingly easy to make. Perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth without any dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block silken tofu
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: In a heatproof bowl, melt the chocolate chips using a double boiler or in the microwave, stirring every 20 seconds to prevent burning.
  2. Blend the tofu: In a blender or food processor, combine the silken tofu, melted chocolate, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Chill the mousse: Pour the mousse into serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until set.
  4. Serve: Serve the chocolate tofu mousse chilled, topped with fresh berries or whipped coconut cream if desired.

This chocolate tofu mousse is a dairy-free and healthier alternative to traditional mousse, but it’s just as rich and indulgent. It’s perfect for dinner parties or a special treat.

Final Thoughts

Tofu is an incredibly versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of ways, from savory stir-fries to indulgent desserts. These five easy vegan recipes showcase the flexibility of tofu and its ability to absorb and complement the flavors of other ingredients. Whether you’re a tofu enthusiast or just beginning to experiment with plant-based cooking, these dishes are sure to inspire you to cook with tofu more often.
