How to Make Homemade Coconut Macaroons


Coconut macaroons are one of those timeless treats that never go out of style. Whether you’re baking for a holiday, a special occasion, or just because you’re in the mood for something sweet, these chewy, delicious coconut macaroons are a perfect choice. Not only are they simple to make, but their delightful flavor and texture will have everyone asking for the recipe.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make homemade coconut macaroons from scratch. With just a handful of ingredients and a bit of patience, you’ll create a batch of macaroons that are crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside. Plus, we’ll share some tips and tricks to elevate your macaroons to the next level, so let’s get started!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create these perfect coconut macaroons, you’ll need just a few simple ingredients:

  • Sweetened shredded coconut: The star of the show! You’ll want to use sweetened shredded coconut to give the macaroons that signature sweetness and chewiness.
  • Egg whites: The binding agent for the coconut, ensuring the macaroons hold together.
  • Granulated sugar: For sweetness and to help the macaroons crisp up around the edges.
  • Vanilla extract: To add a touch of warmth and flavor to balance the sweetness of the coconut.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt helps enhance the sweetness and round out the flavors.
  • Optional – Chocolate for dipping: While not essential, melted chocolate can make a fantastic addition, giving your coconut macaroons a luxurious, indulgent touch.

The best part about these ingredients is that they’re all pantry staples, so you can make these macaroons whenever a craving strikes.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (163°C). You’ll want to make sure the oven is nice and hot before baking, so this step ensures that your macaroons will bake evenly and get that perfect golden color on the outside.

Step 2: Prepare Your Baking Sheet

Next, line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. This will help prevent the macaroons from sticking to the sheet while baking and make cleanup much easier. If you prefer, you can also lightly grease the baking sheet, but parchment paper is the most reliable option.

Step 3: Whisk the Egg Whites

In a large mixing bowl, start by whisking the egg whites with a pinch of salt. You don’t need to beat them into stiff peaks; just whisk them until they become slightly frothy. The goal is to give the egg whites enough structure to hold the coconut mixture together but without over-beating them.

Step 4: Combine the Ingredients

To the egg whites, add the sweetened shredded coconut, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract. Stir everything together until the coconut is evenly coated with the egg whites and sugar mixture. The mixture should be sticky and hold together when pressed.

At this point, you can taste the mixture and adjust the sweetness if needed by adding a little more sugar. Remember, the macaroons will sweeten even more as they bake, so don’t overdo it at this stage.

Step 5: Form the Macaroons

Now comes the fun part: shaping the macaroons! Use a tablespoon or a small cookie scoop to scoop out portions of the coconut mixture. Roll each portion into a ball and then slightly flatten it to form a mound shape. Place each macaroon onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart to allow for even baking.

If you want uniform macaroons, a cookie scoop is especially useful. This ensures each macaroon is the same size and bakes evenly.

Step 6: Bake

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the macaroons are golden brown on the edges. Keep an eye on them during the last few minutes of baking to avoid over-browning. The macaroons should be slightly crisp around the edges and soft in the center.

Step 7: Cool the Macaroons

Once the macaroons are golden and baked to perfection, remove them from the oven. Let them cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. This cooling time helps the macaroons firm up and hold their shape.

If you plan to dip the macaroons in chocolate, make sure they are fully cooled before moving on to that step.

Optional: Add a Chocolate Drizzle or Dip

If you want to take your coconut macaroons to the next level, consider adding a rich chocolate coating. You can either drizzle the melted chocolate over the macaroons or dip them halfway into the chocolate for a more decadent touch.

To melt the chocolate, place your choice of chocolate chips or chopped chocolate into a heatproof bowl and microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until completely melted and smooth.

Once the macaroons are cooled, dip the bottoms or drizzle the chocolate over the top. Place the macaroons on parchment paper again to allow the chocolate to set. For an even more luxurious touch, you can sprinkle the dipped macaroons with a pinch of sea salt before the chocolate hardens.

Tips for the Best Coconut Macaroons

  1. Use fresh coconut: For the best flavor and texture, try to use fresh shredded coconut. If you can’t find it, sweetened shredded coconut will work perfectly fine, but fresh coconut will give your macaroons a richer, more authentic flavor.

  2. Don’t overmix the coconut mixture: Once you combine the egg whites with the coconut, don’t overwork the dough. Stir it just enough to coat the coconut and egg whites. Over-mixing can lead to tough macaroons.

  3. Shape them carefully: Be gentle when shaping the macaroons. Press them lightly into a mound shape, but don’t squish them too tightly, or they may not bake properly.

  4. Watch the baking time: Every oven is different, so start checking the macaroons a couple of minutes before the suggested time. They should be golden brown on the edges but soft in the center. If they seem too soft in the middle, leave them in the oven for an extra minute or two.

  5. Make them smaller for bite-sized treats: If you want bite-sized coconut macaroons, use a teaspoon or a mini cookie scoop to form them. Just keep an eye on the baking time, as smaller macaroons may bake faster.

Variations to Try

Coconut Almond Macaroons

For a nutty twist, try adding some chopped almonds to the coconut mixture. The combination of coconut and almonds is always a winner, and the added crunch will make each bite even more satisfying. You can also place a whole almond on top of each macaroon before baking for a lovely garnish.

Coconut Lime Macaroons

If you’re a fan of tropical flavors, try adding some lime zest to the coconut mixture. The zesty lime will complement the sweetness of the coconut and add a refreshing flavor to the macaroons. For an even more citrusy kick, you can drizzle some lime-infused glaze over the top once the macaroons are cooled.

Chocolate Coconut Macaroons

For those who love the combo of coconut and chocolate, dip your macaroons in melted dark or milk chocolate. Alternatively, you can mix mini chocolate chips into the coconut mixture before baking. The chocolate chips will melt into the coconut, adding pockets of gooey chocolate in every bite.

Storing Your Coconut Macaroons

Once your macaroons are fully cooled and set, you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. If you’re making them in advance, you can also freeze them. Just place them in a single layer in a freezer-safe container, separating layers with parchment paper. Frozen macaroons will keep for up to 3 months, and you can enjoy them straight from the freezer or allow them to thaw before serving.

Why You’ll Love Homemade Coconut Macaroons

Making coconut macaroons at home is a breeze, and they’re so much more satisfying than the store-bought variety. The chewy texture, golden-brown crispness, and natural coconut flavor will make these macaroons a new favorite in your baking repertoire. Plus, these treats are gluten-free, making them an excellent option for those with dietary restrictions.

Whether you keep them simple or add a chocolate drizzle, these coconut macaroons are sure to please everyone who tries them. Their simple ingredients, easy preparation, and delicious flavor make them a perfect addition to any dessert spread.

Final Thoughts

Making coconut macaroons at home doesn’t need to be complicated, and the end result is a treat that’s hard to resist. With just a few ingredients and a little time in the kitchen, you can create delicious coconut macaroons that are crispy, chewy, and irresistibly sweet. The best part is that you can customize them to your taste, whether you want to add chocolate, lime, or even nuts.

So, grab your ingredients and start baking today! Your homemade coconut macaroons will become a staple in your dessert rotation, and you’ll always be ready to share them with friends and family.
