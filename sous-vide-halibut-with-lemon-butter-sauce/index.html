Sous Vide Halibut with Lemon Butter Sauce


If you’re a seafood lover and looking for a way to elevate your cooking game, then Sous Vide Halibut with Lemon Butter Sauce is the recipe you need to try. Sous vide cooking is a game-changer when it comes to preparing fish, ensuring it’s cooked to perfection every time. Whether you’re an experienced home chef or just starting out, this method guarantees tender, flaky fish with a burst of bright flavors. Paired with a rich and creamy lemon butter sauce, it’s a dish that feels both elegant and comforting.

What is Sous Vide?

Sous vide, French for “under vacuum,” is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method results in incredibly tender and evenly cooked dishes because the food is cooked at a consistent low temperature for an extended period. The sous vide technique is especially effective for delicate proteins like fish, as it prevents overcooking and retains all the natural juices, ensuring a moist and flavorful result.

When you cook halibut using sous vide, you eliminate the risk of overcooking, which can often happen with traditional methods. Instead of trying to monitor the cooking time carefully, sous vide lets you relax and focus on perfecting the flavors in the sauce. The result is a dish that is both foolproof and impressive.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Halibut with Lemon Butter Sauce

For the Halibut:

  • 4 halibut fillets (about 6 oz each)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • 1-2 sprigs fresh thyme (optional)
  • 1 garlic clove, smashed (optional)

For the Lemon Butter Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (1 stick)
  • 1 small shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth (or fish stock)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

The Perfect Sous Vide Halibut: Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Sous Vide Water Bath

Start by filling your sous vide container or a large pot with water. Set your immersion circulator to 132°F (55.5°C) for perfectly tender and flaky fish. Halibut is a delicate fish, and cooking it at this temperature will yield a moist and buttery texture without it falling apart. The beauty of sous vide is that you can set it and forget it while you focus on preparing the sauce.

Step 2: Season the Halibut Fillets

While your water bath is heating up, season the halibut fillets with salt and freshly cracked black pepper. This is a simple seasoning, but you can always add extra herbs or spices if you desire. For a touch of flavor, add a sprig of fresh thyme and a smashed garlic clove to each fillet. This subtle infusion of aromatics will enhance the overall taste of the fish.

Drizzle each fillet with a little olive oil to ensure they cook evenly and stay moist during the sous vide process. The oil helps create a seal around the fish, locking in the flavors.

Step 3: Bag the Halibut for Sous Vide Cooking

Place each seasoned fillet in a vacuum-seal bag or a resealable ziplock bag using the water displacement method. If you’re using vacuum-seal bags, ensure that the fish is flat and the bag is tightly sealed. For the water displacement method, slowly lower the bag into the water until most of the air has been displaced, then seal the bag tightly.

Step 4: Cook the Halibut Sous Vide

Carefully place the sealed bag into the preheated water bath. Since halibut is a delicate fish, it only needs to cook for about 30-45 minutes at 132°F (55.5°C). The sous vide method ensures that the fish will cook gently and evenly, retaining all of its natural moisture. You don’t need to worry about overcooking; even if you leave it in the bath a little longer, the fish will remain tender and juicy.

Step 5: Searing the Halibut (Optional)

After cooking the halibut sous vide, it’s time to sear it for a beautiful golden crust. While this step is optional, it adds a nice touch to the texture and appearance of the dish. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a little olive oil or butter. Once the pan is hot, carefully remove the halibut fillets from the bag and place them in the skillet.

Sear the fish for about 1-2 minutes on each side, just until a light crust forms. Be gentle when flipping the fish, as it will be very tender after the sous vide cooking. The goal is not to cook the fish further but to create a nice contrast between the crispy exterior and the tender, flaky interior.

Making the Lemon Butter Sauce

While the halibut is cooking, you can prepare the lemon butter sauce. This rich, velvety sauce is the perfect complement to the mild flavor of the halibut. It’s also incredibly easy to make.

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

In a small saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Once the butter has melted, add the chopped shallot and sauté for about 2-3 minutes until it becomes translucent and soft. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, just until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

Step 2: Add the Liquid Ingredients

Next, add the dry white wine to the pan, stirring to combine. Let the wine simmer for about 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly. This step adds depth of flavor and helps balance the richness of the butter.

If you’re not using wine, you can substitute it with more chicken broth or fish stock. Add the broth to the pan, stirring to combine, and let it simmer for an additional 2 minutes. This creates a flavorful base for the sauce.

Step 3: Add the Lemon

Now, it’s time to add the bright, zesty flavors of lemon. Add the fresh lemon juice and zest to the sauce and stir to combine. The acidity from the lemon will cut through the richness of the butter and enhance the overall flavor profile.

Step 4: Finish the Sauce

Reduce the heat to low and whisk in the remaining 6 tablespoons of butter, one tablespoon at a time. This will create a smooth, silky sauce. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and freshly cracked black pepper as needed. Finally, stir in the chopped parsley for a fresh, herby finish.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the halibut is perfectly cooked and seared, place the fillets on a serving plate and drizzle the luscious lemon butter sauce over the top. The combination of the buttery sauce with the tender fish creates a melt-in-your-mouth experience that’s sure to impress your guests.

For an extra touch, garnish with additional fresh herbs or a few lemon slices for added visual appeal. Serve this dish with your favorite sides, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a simple salad.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Halibut with Lemon Butter Sauce

  • Don’t skip the sear: While searing the halibut after sous vide is optional, it’s highly recommended for that extra flavor and texture. The crisp exterior complements the soft, flaky interior perfectly.
  • Adjust the cooking time: Depending on the thickness of your halibut fillets, you may need to adjust the cooking time. For thinner fillets, 30 minutes is enough, while thicker fillets may need up to 45 minutes.
  • Experiment with flavors: While the lemon butter sauce is classic, feel free to add your own twist. You can infuse the butter with herbs like dill, tarragon, or basil, or even add a touch of cream for a richer sauce.
  • Temperature matters: For the perfect texture, it’s important to cook the halibut at the right temperature. A range between 130°F (54°C) and 134°F (56.5°C) is ideal, but adjusting slightly will still give great results.
  • Use high-quality fish: Since the fish is the star of this dish, use the best quality halibut you can find. Fresh, sustainably sourced halibut will make a huge difference in flavor.

Conclusion

Sous vide halibut with lemon butter sauce is a dish that takes seafood to the next level. The sous vide method ensures that the fish is perfectly cooked, moist, and tender, while the rich and tangy lemon butter sauce adds brightness and depth of flavor. Whether you’re preparing a special dinner for guests or just treating yourself to a fine meal, this recipe is sure to impress.

With simple ingredients, an easy-to-follow process, and exceptional results, this dish will quickly become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Give it a try, and enjoy the delicious rewards of sous vide cooking!
