Flaky and Delicious Peach Cobbler Recipe


Are you ready to indulge in a mouthwatering dessert that captures the essence of summer? Look no further than this flaky and delicious peach cobbler recipe. Bursting with the natural sweetness of ripe peaches and topped with a buttery, flaky crust, this classic dessert is sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a comforting treat, this peach cobbler is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 6 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup boiling water

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, brown sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cornstarch. Gently toss the mixture until the peaches are evenly coated, then transfer it to the prepared baking dish.

  3. In a separate bowl, prepare the crust by combining the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or fork to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Pour the boiling water into the flour mixture and stir until just combined. The dough will be quite sticky.

  5. Use a spoon to drop spoonfuls of the dough over the peaches, covering the surface as evenly as possible.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  7. Once baked, remove the peach cobbler from the oven and allow it to cool for 10-15 minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This flaky and delicious peach cobbler is best enjoyed warm, either on its own or with a scoop of creamy vanilla ice cream. The contrast of the warm, fruity filling and the cold, creamy ice cream is simply irresistible. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also add a sprinkle of cinnamon or a drizzle of caramel sauce for an extra layer of flavor.

Storage

If you happen to have any leftovers (which is rare with this crowd-pleasing dessert), you can store the peach cobbler in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat individual servings in the microwave for a warm and comforting treat.

Conclusion

This flaky and delicious peach cobbler recipe is a celebration of summer’s bounty. With its juicy peach filling and buttery, flaky crust, it’s a timeless dessert that brings joy to every bite. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is a wonderful way to showcase the season’s freshest peaches. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of this classic peach cobbler.
