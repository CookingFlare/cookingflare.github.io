Canning and Preserving Fresh Cherries for Sweet Treats


Cherries, with their vibrant color and sweet, tart flavor, are one of the most beloved fruits of summer. Whether you enjoy them fresh, in pies, jams, or syrups, cherries bring a burst of natural sweetness to every dish. But what do you do when cherry season ends, and you still crave that delightful taste? The answer lies in canning and preserving cherries, allowing you to enjoy their juicy goodness long after summer fades.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best methods for canning and preserving fresh cherries, offering you a variety of sweet treats to enjoy throughout the year. From making homemade cherry jam to creating delicious cherry syrup, these techniques will help you savor the taste of fresh cherries no matter the season.

Why Can and Preserve Cherries?

Canning and preserving cherries not only extend the shelf life of this luscious fruit but also allow you to create a wide array of sweet treats and preserves that can be enjoyed year-round. Fresh cherries are highly perishable, so preserving them through canning ensures you can enjoy their rich flavor even after the harvest season.

Preserved cherries can be used in a variety of ways, including in pies, cakes, tarts, smoothies, sauces, and syrups. Moreover, canned cherries are perfect for topping ice cream, yogurt, pancakes, or waffles, adding a burst of flavor to your desserts. The process also offers the benefit of reducing food waste, as you can preserve cherries when they are in abundance and enjoy them later.

Essential Supplies for Canning Cherries

Before diving into the canning process, it’s essential to gather the proper tools and supplies to ensure a successful and safe preservation. Here’s what you’ll need:

  1. Fresh Cherries – Choose ripe, firm cherries with no blemishes or soft spots. Bing cherries are popular for canning, but you can also use sour cherries or other varieties.
  2. Canning Jars – Mason jars are the go-to choice for canning. Make sure the jars are clean and free of cracks.
  3. Lids and Rings – Ensure you have fresh lids for sealing the jars, and the rings are in good condition.
  4. Water Bath Canner or Pressure Canner – A water bath canner is typically used for high-acid fruits like cherries, while a pressure canner is required for low-acid foods.
  5. Jar Lifter – A tool that helps you safely remove hot jars from the canner.
  6. Ladle and Funnel – For transferring hot liquids and fruit into the jars.
  7. Knife and Pitting Tool – To pit the cherries easily.
  8. Canning Salt (Optional) – A small amount of salt can be added to the canning liquid to enhance flavor.

Once you have your supplies ready, you can proceed with the canning process.

How to Can Cherries

Canning cherries involves preserving the fruit in jars with a syrup or juice, and then processing them in a water bath or pressure canner. Here’s a simple method for canning cherries:

1. Prepare the Cherries

Start by washing the cherries thoroughly in cool water. Remove the stems, and then pit the cherries. You can either use a cherry pitter, which makes the job quick and easy, or you can cut the cherries in half and remove the pits manually. If you’re using sour cherries, you can leave them whole if desired.

Once the cherries are prepped, set them aside in a clean bowl.

2. Prepare the Syrup

To preserve the cherries, you’ll need to prepare a syrup. There are different syrup options you can choose from depending on the level of sweetness you want. A light syrup works well for a delicate taste, while a heavier syrup offers a sweeter result. Here’s how to make each:

  • Light Syrup: Combine 4 cups of water with 1 ½ cups of sugar. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar, and simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Medium Syrup: Combine 4 cups of water with 2 cups of sugar. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Heavy Syrup: Combine 4 cups of water with 3 cups of sugar. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes.

You can also opt to use fruit juice, such as cherry juice, to can your cherries. This method gives a more natural taste and can be a great option if you’re looking for an alternative to syrup.

3. Pack the Jars

Sterilize your canning jars by boiling them in water for 10 minutes or by running them through a hot cycle in the dishwasher. Once the jars are sterilized, use a ladle and funnel to pack the cherries into the jars. Make sure the cherries are tightly packed, but avoid overstuffing the jars.

Leave about ½ inch of headspace at the top of each jar to allow for expansion during the canning process.

4. Add the Syrup

Once the cherries are packed, pour the prepared syrup or juice over them, ensuring that the fruit is fully submerged. Use a clean utensil, like a knife or spatula, to gently remove any air bubbles by running it around the inside of the jar. Add more syrup if necessary to cover the fruit, making sure to maintain the ½ inch of headspace.

5. Seal the Jars

Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to remove any syrup or juice residue. Place a canning lid on each jar and secure it with a metal ring. Make sure the ring is tightened, but not too tight, as the air needs to escape during processing.

6. Process the Jars

Now it’s time to process the jars. If you’re using a water bath canner, follow these steps:

  1. Place the jars into the canner, ensuring they’re covered by at least 1 to 2 inches of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil and process the jars for 20 to 25 minutes for pints or 30 minutes for quarts.
  3. Once the processing time is up, carefully remove the jars using a jar lifter and place them on a clean towel or rack to cool.
  4. Allow the jars to cool completely, which can take several hours.

If you’re using a pressure canner, follow the manufacturer’s instructions for processing times and pressure settings based on the type of cherries and your altitude.

7. Check the Seal

After the jars have cooled, check the seals by pressing down in the center of each lid. If it pops back, the jar hasn’t sealed properly, and you should refrigerate it and consume it within a few days. Properly sealed jars should have a concave lid that doesn’t move when pressed.

Once sealed, label the jars with the date of canning and store them in a cool, dark, and dry place. Properly canned cherries can last up to a year or more, allowing you to enjoy their sweet taste long after the season ends.

Alternative Ways to Preserve Cherries

While canning is a great way to preserve cherries, there are other methods you can try, depending on your needs. These include freezing and making cherry preserves or fruit leather.

Freezing Cherries

Freezing cherries is another easy method for preserving the fruit. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash and pit the cherries, removing the stems.
  2. Lay the cherries in a single layer on a baking sheet and freeze them for 2-3 hours.
  3. Once frozen, transfer the cherries to airtight freezer bags or containers. Label them with the date and store them in the freezer.

Frozen cherries are perfect for smoothies, baked goods, or even as a cold treat in the summer.

Cherry Jam and Preserves

Making cherry jam or preserves is a classic way to preserve the fruit, offering a delicious spread for toast, pastries, or as a topping for desserts. You can use a variety of sweeteners, including sugar, honey, or even maple syrup, depending on your preference.

To make cherry jam, simply cook the cherries with sugar and pectin, following your chosen recipe for the desired consistency. Once the jam reaches the perfect texture, jar it and process it in a water bath canner for long-term storage.

Cherry Fruit Leather

For a fun and creative way to preserve cherries, try making cherry fruit leather. This method involves pureeing the cherries with a sweetener, spreading the mixture on a dehydrator tray or a lined baking sheet, and drying it until it forms a chewy, candy-like treat.

Making Cherry Syrup

If you enjoy cherry-flavored beverages, consider making homemade cherry syrup. This simple syrup can be added to drinks, used as a topping for desserts, or incorporated into sauces. To make cherry syrup, simmer fresh cherries with sugar and water, then strain the mixture to remove the solids.

Conclusion

Canning and preserving fresh cherries is a rewarding way to enjoy this delectable fruit all year long. Whether you choose to can them in syrup, freeze them, or turn them into jam or syrup, these preservation methods ensure that you’ll always have a supply of cherries for your favorite sweet treats. With a little time and effort, you can enjoy the taste of summer cherries long after the harvest season has ended, adding a burst of flavor to your dishes whenever you desire.

By following the steps outlined in this guide, you’ll be able to preserve your cherries effectively, allowing you to create delicious homemade treats that will delight your taste buds all year. Happy canning!
