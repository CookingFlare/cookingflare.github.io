How to Make Vegan Macarons: A Perfect Treat


Macarons are a classic French pastry that has captured the hearts of dessert lovers worldwide with their delicate texture, vibrant colors, and melt-in-your-mouth sweetness. Traditionally made with egg whites, sugar, and almond flour, macarons are often off-limits to those following a plant-based diet. However, with a little creativity, it’s possible to recreate these elegant treats in a way that’s 100% vegan. In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of the process to make vegan macarons, from preparing the meringue to filling them with your favorite plant-based filling.

Ingredients for Vegan Macarons

For the Macaron Shells:

  • 1 cup almond flour – The key ingredient for macarons, giving them their signature texture.
  • 1 cup powdered sugar – To sweeten the shells without overpowering them.
  • 1/2 cup aquafaba (liquid from canned chickpeas) – This will replace the egg whites in the traditional recipe, providing the necessary structure and texture.
  • 1/4 tsp cream of tartar – This helps stabilize the aquafaba, allowing it to form stiff peaks.
  • 1/2 cup caster sugar – A fine sugar that dissolves quickly when whipped with the aquafaba.
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract – For a subtle, aromatic flavor.
  • A pinch of salt – To balance the sweetness and bring out the flavors of the almonds.

For the Vegan Macaron Filling:

  • 1/2 cup vegan butter – Use a high-quality, dairy-free butter substitute for the best texture.
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar – To create a creamy, sweet filling.
  • 2-3 tbsp non-dairy milk – Add as needed for the right consistency.
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract – For a rich, sweet flavor.
  • Food coloring (optional) – If you want to add a pop of color to your filling.

Equipment You’ll Need:

  • Piping bags and tips (round tip for both the macaron batter and filling)
  • Stand mixer or hand mixer
  • Sifter
  • Mixing bowls
  • Baking sheets lined with parchment paper or silicone mats
  • A spoon or spatula
  • Candy thermometer (optional but helpful)
  • Macaron template (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Macarons

Step 1: Prepare Your Macaron Template

If you’re using a macaron template, now’s the time to set it up. You can easily find templates online that provide guidelines for evenly-sized macarons. Place the template underneath your parchment paper or silicone mat, ensuring the circles are aligned. This will give your macarons uniformity, which is important for both their appearance and baking consistency.

Step 2: Sift the Dry Ingredients

Sifting is crucial to achieving the smooth, lump-free macaron shells. Start by sifting the almond flour and powdered sugar together into a large bowl. This ensures that there are no clumps, which could affect the texture of the macaron batter. Set this bowl aside as you prepare the meringue.

Step 3: Prepare the Vegan Meringue

In a clean, dry mixing bowl, pour the aquafaba (the liquid from canned chickpeas). Using a stand mixer or hand mixer, whip the aquafaba on medium speed until it begins to form soft peaks. You’ll want to continue whipping until it reaches stiff peaks, which will take about 5-7 minutes. The meringue should be glossy and firm.

Once your aquafaba has reached stiff peaks, add the cream of tartar. This will help stabilize the meringue and prevent it from deflating. Gradually add the caster sugar, a tablespoon at a time, while continuing to whip the mixture. This process helps to build the meringue’s structure. After all the sugar has been added, whip the mixture for another 2-3 minutes until it’s stiff and glossy.

Step 4: Combine the Meringue and Dry Ingredients

Now it’s time to fold in the dry ingredients. Gently fold the almond flour and powdered sugar mixture into the aquafaba meringue. Be careful not to deflate the meringue during this step. Use a spatula and a technique called “macaronage,” which involves scraping the sides of the bowl and folding the mixture from the bottom. You’ll want to fold until the batter reaches a smooth, flowing consistency. When you lift the spatula, the batter should form a ribbon that slowly falls back into the bowl.

Step 5: Pipe the Macaron Shells

Once your batter is ready, it’s time to pipe it onto your prepared baking sheets. Transfer the macaron batter into a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Hold the piping bag at a 90-degree angle over the template and pipe small circles of batter. Keep the size consistent, as this will ensure that the macarons bake evenly.

Once all the batter is piped, gently tap the baking sheet on the counter a few times to release any air bubbles and help the batter settle into the shape of the circles. This step is essential to get smooth, round shells. You can also use a toothpick to pop any remaining air bubbles.

Step 6: Rest the Macarons

Let the macaron shells rest at room temperature for about 30-60 minutes. This allows the batter to form a thin skin on the surface. You’ll know they’re ready for baking when you can gently touch the tops of the shells without the batter sticking to your finger. The resting time is key to achieving the characteristic “foot” (the ruffled bottom edge) on the macaron shells.

Step 7: Bake the Macarons

Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). Place your macarons in the oven and bake them for 18-20 minutes, or until they have developed their feet and the shells are firm to the touch. If you’re using a fan-assisted (convection) oven, reduce the temperature to 275°F (135°C).

After baking, let the macarons cool completely on the baking sheet. This will help them firm up and make it easier to remove them without breaking.

Step 8: Prepare the Vegan Filling

While the macarons are cooling, it’s time to prepare the filling. In a mixing bowl, beat the vegan butter until it’s light and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar and continue to mix until the filling is smooth and fluffy. Add the non-dairy milk, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach the desired consistency. You want the filling to be thick enough to hold its shape when piped but soft enough to spread easily.

You can also add food coloring to the filling at this point if you’d like to match the color of the shells or create a contrasting look.

Step 9: Assemble the Vegan Macarons

Once the macaron shells have cooled, carefully peel them off the parchment paper or silicone mat. Pair up the shells that are of similar size.

Using a piping bag, pipe a small dollop of the vegan filling onto the flat side of one macaron shell. Gently press another shell on top, creating a sandwich. Be careful not to press too hard, as this can cause the delicate shells to crack.

Step 10: Let the Macarons Mature

Macarons are best after they’ve had some time to mature. Place your assembled vegan macarons in an airtight container and refrigerate them for at least 24 hours. This allows the flavors to meld together and the texture to soften. While they can be eaten immediately, maturing the macarons enhances their flavor and texture, making them even more delicious.

Step 11: Serve and Enjoy!

Once your vegan macarons have matured, they’re ready to be enjoyed! Serve them at tea parties, gatherings, or simply as a sweet treat for yourself. Their light, airy texture, combined with the creamy filling, makes them the perfect indulgence.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Macarons

  • Don’t Skip the Resting Time: Allowing the piped batter to rest before baking is crucial for creating the foot and preventing cracks in the shells.
  • Use a Silpat Mat or Parchment Paper: This ensures that the macarons bake evenly and don’t stick to the surface.
  • Weigh Your Ingredients: Macarons are a delicate pastry, and even small differences in ingredient measurements can affect the outcome. Using a kitchen scale can help ensure accuracy.
  • Be Patient: The process of making macarons can take time and practice. Don’t be discouraged if they don’t turn out perfectly on your first try. With each batch, you’ll improve!

Conclusion

Making vegan macarons from scratch may seem like an intimidating task, but with the right ingredients and techniques, you can create these exquisite treats in your own kitchen. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, following this step-by-step guide will ensure your vegan macarons turn out beautifully. Not only are they a delightful treat for those following a plant-based diet, but they also offer a sweet and satisfying dessert for anyone who loves French pastries. Happy baking!
