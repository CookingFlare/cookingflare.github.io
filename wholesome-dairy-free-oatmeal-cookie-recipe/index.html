Wholesome Dairy-Free Oatmeal Cookie Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious and wholesome treat that’s dairy-free, look no further than these delightful oatmeal cookies. Packed with flavor and nutrition, these cookies are perfect for anyone looking for a healthier alternative to traditional cookies. Whether you have dietary restrictions or simply want to explore new flavors, this recipe is sure to become a household favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1 flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup raisins or chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the rolled oats, whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, flax egg, and vanilla extract.

  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until well combined. If desired, fold in the raisins or chopped nuts.

  5. Using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop the dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each cookie for spreading.

  6. Gently flatten each cookie with the back of a spoon or your fingers.

  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.

  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a chewier texture, use old-fashioned rolled oats instead of quick oats.
  • If you’re not a fan of coconut oil, you can use melted vegan butter or a neutral-flavored oil like grapeseed oil.
  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding in your favorite mix-ins, such as chocolate chips, dried cranberries, or chopped almonds.

Why Dairy-Free?

Dairy-free baking has gained popularity in recent years due to an increased awareness of lactose intolerance, milk allergies, and the desire for plant-based alternatives. By using coconut oil and a flax egg, this recipe provides a satisfying and flavorful dairy-free option that doesn’t compromise on taste or texture.

Nutritional Benefits

Not only are these oatmeal cookies dairy-free, but they also offer nutritional benefits. Rolled oats are a good source of fiber and whole grains, while coconut oil provides healthy fats. The addition of maple syrup adds natural sweetness without refined sugars, making these cookies a wholesome treat for any occasion.

Conclusion

In conclusion, these wholesome dairy-free oatmeal cookies are a delightful way to satisfy your sweet tooth while staying true to your dietary preferences. Whether you’re vegan, lactose intolerant, or simply looking to explore new flavors, these cookies are a must-try. With a perfect balance of flavor and nutrition, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire. Enjoy the delightful taste of these dairy-free oatmeal cookies while reaping the nutritional benefits of wholesome ingredients.
