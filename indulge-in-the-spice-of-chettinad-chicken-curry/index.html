Indulge in the Spice of Chettinad Chicken Curry


Who doesn’t love a good curry? The aromatic blend of spices, the rich and creamy sauce, and the tender pieces of meat or vegetables - it’s a dish that can transport you to a different world with just one bite. Today, we’re going to take a journey to the southern part of India and explore the tantalizing flavors of Chettinad Chicken Curry.

Chettinad cuisine originates from the Chettinad region in Tamil Nadu, known for its bold and spicy flavors. Chettinad Chicken Curry is a perfect representation of this culinary tradition, with its fiery red color and complex blend of spices. So, let’s dive in and discover how to recreate this mouthwatering dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Chettinad Chicken Curry, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500 grams of chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 3-4 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons of red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons of coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 cinnamon sticks
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 tablespoons of oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan or a kadai over medium heat. Add the fennel seeds, black peppercorns, cloves, cinnamon sticks, and green cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute until the spices release their aroma.

  2. Add the finely chopped onions and slit green chilies to the pan. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown and translucent.

  3. Now, it’s time to add the ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  4. In a small bowl, mix together the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder with a little water to form a paste. Add this paste to the pan and cook for a minute, stirring well to coat the onions and spices.

  5. Add the pureed tomatoes to the pan and cook until the oil separates from the mixture. This indicates that the tomatoes have cooked down and the spices have blended well.

  6. Now, it’s time to add the chicken pieces to the pan. Mix well, ensuring that the chicken is coated evenly with the spice mixture. Cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken starts to brown slightly.

  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the chicken cook in its own juices for about 20-25 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  8. Once the chicken is cooked through and tender, remove the lid and cook for a few more minutes to thicken the gravy to your desired consistency. Add salt to taste and adjust the spices if needed.

  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick, you can add a teaspoon of black pepper powder along with the other spices. This will give the curry an additional level of spiciness.

  • If you prefer a thicker gravy, you can add a tablespoon of cashew paste or coconut milk towards the end of the cooking process.

  • Chettinad Chicken Curry can also be made with boneless chicken if you prefer. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly, as boneless chicken tends to cook faster.

  • This recipe can be easily customized to your taste preferences. If you like it spicier, add more chili powder or green chilies. If you prefer it milder, reduce the amount of chili powder or omit the green chilies altogether.

Conclusion

Indulge in the rich and vibrant flavors of Chettinad Chicken Curry, a dish that will transport your taste buds to the heart of Tamil Nadu. With its aromatic spices and tender chicken, this curry is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, unleash your inner chef, and embark on a culinary adventure that will leave you craving for more. Enjoy!
