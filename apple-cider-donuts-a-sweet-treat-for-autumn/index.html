Apple Cider Donuts: A Sweet Treat for Autumn


Autumn is the season of crisp air, falling leaves, and the enticing aroma of freshly baked goods. One treat that perfectly captures the essence of this cozy season is the apple cider donut. These sweet and comforting treats are a staple of fall festivals and apple orchards, and now you can bring that delightful taste into your own kitchen. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create your own batch of homemade apple cider donuts that will leave your taste buds dancing with joy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a donut pan with non-stick cooking spray or butter.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, salt, nutmeg, and cloves. Set aside.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the apple cider, milk, melted butter, egg, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold the mixture together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the donuts tough.

  5. Spoon the batter into a piping bag or a large zip-top bag with one corner snipped off. Pipe the batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about 2/3 full.

  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the donuts comes out clean. Remove from the oven and let cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. While the donuts are cooling, prepare the cinnamon sugar coating. In a shallow bowl, combine 1/2 cup granulated sugar and 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon. Mix well.

  8. Once the donuts have cooled, gently toss them in the cinnamon sugar mixture until coated on all sides. Shake off any excess sugar.

  9. Serve the apple cider donuts immediately, and enjoy the warm, comforting flavors of autumn in every bite.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of apple flavor, consider adding a tablespoon of applesauce or grated apple to the batter.
  • If you don’t have a donut pan, you can use a muffin tin instead. Simply spoon the batter into the greased muffin cups and adjust the baking time accordingly.
  • To make these donuts even more indulgent, drizzle them with a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and apple cider.
  • For a fun twist, try adding a sprinkle of chopped pecans or a dollop of caramel sauce on top of each donut before coating them in cinnamon sugar.

Now that you have this delicious recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to embrace the flavors of autumn and treat yourself to some homemade apple cider donuts. Whether you enjoy them with a warm mug of apple cider or as a sweet snack on their own, these donuts are sure to bring a smile to your face. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let the aroma of fall fill your home with warmth and comfort. Happy baking!
