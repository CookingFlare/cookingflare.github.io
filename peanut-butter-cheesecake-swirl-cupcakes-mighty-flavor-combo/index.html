Peanut Butter Cheesecake Swirl Cupcakes: Mighty Flavor Combo


Ah, the classic peanut butter and chocolate combination - a flavor combination that just won’t quit, am I right? But today, let’s delve into a similar taste tandem that still gives a delicious twist to the beloved PB & chocolate love story: peanut butter cheesecake swirl cupcakes.

This scrumptious treat combines velvety, smooth cheesecake with the rich and nutty taste of peanut butter, all nestled within a perfectly risen cupcake. Picture it - moist, fluffy cake with a hidden center of creamy, peanut butter-infused cheesecake swirled within. The generous dollop of whipped peanut butter cheese frosting atop completes this oh-so-pleasant taste journey.

Ready to take your taste buds for a spin?Get your apron on, gather your ingredients, and let’s embark on this delightful baking adventure!

Ingredients:

  • For the cupcakes: 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon baking powder 1/4 teaspoon baking soda 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 cup unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/3 cup smooth peanut butter 1/2 cup whole milk

  • For the cheesecake filling: 5 tablespoons unsalted butter (melted) 3/4 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour 2 tablespoons smooth peanut butter 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For the peanut butter frosting: 8 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened) 2/3 cup creamy peanut butter 3 3/4 cups powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 2-3 tablespoons whole milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 12-cup muffin pan with cupcake liners. Give these liners a gentle spray with non-stick cooking spray to ensure easy removal of the cupcakes.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Using a stand mixer or a hand mixer in a large mixing bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract followed by the smooth peanut butter until fully incorporated.

  4. Gradually pour in the dry mixture, alternating with the milk, and ensure you start and end with the dry mixture. Mix until just combined.

  5. Fill each cupcake liner halfway with the batter, roughly 2 tablespoons, then transfer the cheesecake filling mixture to a separate bowl (as we’ll be using the same bowl later for the frosting). Whisk together the melted butter, sugar, eggs, flour, peanut butter, and vanilla extract until smooth for the cheesecake filling.

  6. Drop a spoonful of the cheesecake filling mixture on top of the batter in each liner, followed by another spoonful of the cupcake batter. Use a toothpick or a small knife to create a swirl effect. Be careful not to mix the two batters thoroughly, as we want to maintain the distinct layers.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the pan for about 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. While your cupcakes cool, prepare the peanut butter frosting. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and peanut butter for 3-4 minutes, until creamy and light. Gradually add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and milk, continuing to mix until you reach your desired frosting consistency.

  9. Once the cupcakes have cooled, it’s time to frost! Use a butter knife, an offset spatula, or a piping bag to generously apply the peanut butter frosting. If desired, garnish with chopped peanuts or a drizzle of melted chocolate.
