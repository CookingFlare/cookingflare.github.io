Savoring the Flavors of Yakitori: Grilled Skewered Chicken


Yakitori is a beloved Japanese dish that captures the essence of simplicity and flavor through its grilled skewered chicken. This culinary delight is not just a meal; it’s an experience, steeped in tradition and enjoyed in izakayas (Japanese pubs) and homes alike. In this post, we will explore the rich history of yakitori, various techniques to prepare it, and a variety of mouthwatering recipes that will allow you to savor the flavors of this delectable dish.

The History of Yakitori

Yakitori, meaning “grilled chicken” in Japanese, has its roots in the Edo period (1603-1868). Initially, it was a street food, offering a quick and satisfying meal for the working class. Vendors would grill chicken over charcoal, serving it with a simple tare sauce made from soy sauce, sake, mirin, and sugar. Over time, yakitori evolved, and today it encompasses a wide range of chicken parts, including thighs, breasts, skin, and even organs, each offering unique textures and flavors.

The Art of Grilling Yakitori

Choosing the Right Chicken

The foundation of a great yakitori lies in the quality of the chicken. Opt for fresh, organic chicken to ensure the best flavor. Popular cuts include:

  • Thighs (Momo): Juicy and flavorful, they are ideal for grilling.
  • Breasts (Negima): Leaner but can be dried out easily; they are often paired with green onions.
  • Skin (Kawa): Crispy and indulgent, skin is a favorite among yakitori lovers.
  • Liver (Reba): Rich and creamy, it requires careful cooking to avoid toughness.

Preparing the Skewers

To create authentic yakitori, you’ll need bamboo skewers. Soak them in water for about 30 minutes before grilling to prevent burning. The traditional method involves threading the chicken pieces onto the skewers in a way that allows for even cooking. You can alternate chicken pieces with vegetables like green onions, bell peppers, or mushrooms for added flavor and color.

The Grill

The best way to cook yakitori is over binchotan charcoal, which imparts a unique flavor. However, if you don’t have access to it, a grill or stovetop grill pan will work just fine. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat, and make sure it’s well-oiled to prevent sticking.

Essential Yakitori Sauces

The flavor profile of yakitori largely comes from the sauces used. Here are two popular options:

1. Tare Sauce

Tare is a sweet and savory sauce that enhances the chicken’s natural flavors. Here’s a simple recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/2 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 small piece of ginger, grated (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. If desired, add garlic and ginger.
  2. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
  3. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Allow it to cool before using on your yakitori skewers.

2. Shio (Salt) Seasoning

For those who prefer a lighter taste, shio is simply salt seasoning. It highlights the chicken’s natural flavors without overpowering them.

Ingredients:

  • Coarse sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Lightly sprinkle the salt over the chicken pieces before grilling.
  2. Optionally, add freshly ground black pepper for an extra kick.

Yakitori Recipes to Try at Home

Now that you have a grasp of the basics, let’s dive into some delicious yakitori recipes that will surely impress your family and friends.

Recipe 1: Classic Yakitori Momo (Chicken Thigh Skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked
  • Tare sauce (from above)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the chicken pieces onto the skewers, leaving a little space between each piece.
  3. Brush the skewers with tare sauce generously.
  4. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 5 minutes on each side, basting with more tare sauce as they cook.
  5. Once cooked through and slightly charred, remove from the grill and serve hot.

Recipe 2: Negima Yakitori (Chicken and Green Onion Skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 300g chicken breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked
  • Shio seasoning (from above)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Alternately thread chicken pieces and green onion onto the skewers.
  3. Sprinkle shio seasoning over the skewers.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 4 minutes on each side until cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Serve immediately, drizzled with any remaining shio seasoning.

Recipe 3: Yakitori Kawa (Grilled Chicken Skin)

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken skin, cut into strips or squares
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked
  • Tare sauce (from above)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the chicken skin onto the skewers, folding them to ensure they are secure.
  3. Brush with tare sauce and sprinkle with salt.
  4. Grill for about 6-8 minutes, turning frequently until crispy and golden.
  5. Serve hot as a delicious appetizer or snack.

Recipe 4: Reba Yakitori (Grilled Chicken Liver)

Ingredients:

  • 300g chicken liver, cleaned and trimmed
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked
  • Shio seasoning (from above)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the chicken livers onto the skewers, leaving a little space between each piece.
  3. Sprinkle with shio seasoning.
  4. Grill for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until cooked through but still tender.
  5. Serve immediately, perhaps with a squeeze of lemon for added brightness.

Sides and Pairings for Yakitori

To create a complete meal, consider serving your yakitori with some traditional Japanese sides:

1. Japanese Rice

Fluffy, sticky rice is the perfect companion to soak up the flavors of yakitori. You can serve it plain or seasoned with a bit of sesame and seaweed.

2. Pickled Vegetables

A variety of pickled vegetables (tsukemono) can add freshness and balance to your meal. Common options include pickled radish, cucumber, and eggplant.

3. Miso Soup

A warm bowl of miso soup can round out your yakitori feast. It’s comforting, savory, and easy to prepare with just a few ingredients.

4. Japanese Beer or Sake

To truly savor the flavors of yakitori, pair your meal with a cold Japanese beer or a glass of sake. Both complement the smoky, savory notes of the grilled chicken beautifully.

Conclusion

Yakitori is more than just grilled chicken on a stick; it’s a celebration of flavors, textures, and culinary traditions. With the right techniques and recipes, you can create an authentic yakitori experience right in your own kitchen. Whether you stick to classic styles or experiment with new ingredients, the joy of savoring homemade yakitori is unmatched. So fire up your grill, gather your friends and family, and enjoy the delightful world of yakitori together. Happy grilling!
