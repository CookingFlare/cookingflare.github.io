10 Family-Friendly Recipes for a Summer Picnic


Summer is the perfect time for family gatherings, and what better way to enjoy the warm weather than with a delightful picnic? Whether you’re heading to a local park, your backyard, or a beach, having a selection of delicious, easy-to-make recipes can elevate your picnic experience. Here are ten family-friendly recipes that are sure to please both kids and adults alike. Each recipe is designed to be portable, easy to prepare, and packed with flavor.

1. Classic Chicken Salad Sandwiches

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup celery, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup red grapes, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole grain or white bread
  • Lettuce leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, mayonnaise, Greek yogurt, and Dijon mustard. Mix well.
  2. Add the chopped celery and halved grapes, stirring until evenly distributed.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the chicken salad onto slices of bread, adding lettuce leaves for crunch.
  5. Top with another slice of bread, cut into halves or quarters, and pack for your picnic.

Tips:

  • For a twist, add chopped apples or nuts for extra texture.
  • Serve with whole grain crackers for a crunchy side.

2. Veggie and Hummus Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 4 large whole wheat tortillas
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup carrots, shredded
  • 1 cup spinach or mixed greens

Instructions:

  1. Spread a generous layer of hummus over each tortilla.
  2. Layer the cucumber, bell peppers, carrots, and spinach on top of the hummus.
  3. Roll the tortillas tightly, then slice them into pinwheels or halves.
  4. Wrap in parchment paper for easy transport.

Tips:

  • Experiment with different hummus flavors, such as roasted red pepper or garlic.
  • Add avocado slices for a creamy texture.

3. Mini Caprese Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella balls
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers or toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. On each skewer, thread a cherry tomato, a basil leaf, and a mozzarella ball. Repeat until the skewer is filled.
  2. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  3. Arrange on a platter or pack in a container for easy serving.

Tips:

  • These skewers can be made a few hours in advance; just keep them refrigerated until serving.
  • For added flavor, marinate the mozzarella in olive oil and herbs before assembling.

4. Quinoa Salad with Black Beans and Corn

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, bell pepper, red onion, and cilantro.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Pour over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • This salad is great served cold and can be made a day in advance.
  • Add diced avocado just before serving for extra creaminess.

5. Fruit Kabobs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, cut into chunks
  • 1 pound strawberries, hulled
  • 2 cups grapes (red or green)
  • 2 bananas, sliced
  • Skewers

Instructions:

  1. On each skewer, alternate between pineapple, strawberries, grapes, and banana slices.
  2. Arrange the fruit kabobs on a platter or pack them in a container.
  3. Serve with a yogurt dip if desired.

Tips:

  • Use seasonal fruits for the best flavor and variety.
  • Drizzle with honey or sprinkle with cinnamon for an extra touch.
