Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream: A Citrusy Crunch


Who doesn’t love a refreshing scoop of ice cream on a hot summer day? If you’re looking for a unique and zesty twist to your frozen treats, look no further than Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream. This delightful citrus-infused dessert will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more. With the perfect balance of tangy lemon and crunchy poppy seeds, this homemade ice cream is a surefire crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream and whole milk over medium heat. Heat the mixture until it begins to steam, but make sure it does not boil.

  2. In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and lemon zest until well combined. This will help release the aromatic oils from the zest and infuse the sugar with a vibrant lemon flavor.

  3. Slowly pour the sugar and lemon zest mixture into the saucepan with the warm cream and milk. Stir gently until the sugar is completely dissolved.

  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, stir in the freshly squeezed lemon juice, poppy seeds, and vanilla extract. The lemon juice adds a tangy punch, while the poppy seeds bring a subtle crunch to each bite.

  5. Transfer the mixture to a lidded container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Once the mixture is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes, or until the ice cream reaches a creamy consistency.

  7. Transfer the churned ice cream to a lidded container, and freeze for an additional 4-6 hours, or until firm.

  8. When ready to serve, take the Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream out of the freezer and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften. Scoop into bowls or cones, and garnish with a sprinkle of poppy seeds or a twist of lemon zest for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra creamy texture, you can add an additional egg yolk to the mixture before chilling and churning.

  • If you prefer a more intense lemon flavor, increase the amount of lemon juice and zest according to your taste.

  • Feel free to experiment with other citrus fruits such as lime, orange, or grapefruit to create your own unique variations.

  • Serve the Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream alongside a slice of lemon pound cake or a warm blueberry crumble for a delightful dessert pairing.

  • This ice cream can be stored in the freezer for up to two weeks, though it’s best enjoyed within the first few days for optimal freshness.

So there you have it, a simple yet refreshing recipe for Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream. With its citrusy tang and satisfying crunch, this homemade treat is bound to become a summer favorite. Whether you’re enjoying it on a lazy afternoon or serving it at a backyard gathering, this unique ice cream flavor is sure to impress. So grab your ice cream maker and get ready to indulge in a scoop of Lemon Poppy Seed Ice Cream: a citrusy delight that will leave you dreaming of sunny days.
