Vegan Cream Puffs Recipe with a Light and Airy Pastry


Cream puffs are a delightful dessert that can bring joy to anyone who takes a bite. Traditionally made with eggs and dairy, these fluffy pastries can also be transformed into a vegan version that’s just as light, airy, and delicious. In this post, we’ll guide you through making vegan cream puffs filled with a luscious dairy-free cream that will satisfy your sweet tooth while keeping it plant-based.

Understanding the Components of Vegan Cream Puffs

To create the perfect vegan cream puff, it’s essential to understand its two main components: the pastry (also known as choux pastry) and the filling.

The Pastry

Choux pastry is known for its unique ability to puff up when baked, creating a hollow center perfect for filling. The classic recipe uses eggs to achieve its light and airy texture, but we can create a vegan alternative using a combination of flour, water, and plant-based ingredients.

The Filling

The filling can be made from a variety of plant-based ingredients, but for this recipe, we’ll focus on a rich, creamy filling made from coconut cream and sweeteners. This combination creates a luxurious texture that pairs beautifully with the airy pastry.

Ingredients

For the Vegan Pastry

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (full-fat for richness)
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter
  • 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water (flax egg)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

For the Coconut Cream Filling

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk, chilled overnight
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thicker cream)

Optional Toppings

  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Cocoa powder for a chocolatey twist
  • Fresh berries for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Flax Egg

In a small bowl, combine the ground flaxseed and water. Stir well and let it sit for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes gelatinous. This flax egg will help bind the ingredients in our vegan pastry.

Step 2: Make the Vegan Pastry

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine the water, coconut milk, vegan butter, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat until the vegan butter melts and the mixture begins to simmer.

  3. Add Flour: Once the mixture is simmering, remove it from the heat and add the all-purpose flour and baking powder all at once. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon or spatula until the dough clumps together and pulls away from the sides of the saucepan. This should take about 1-2 minutes.

  4. Incorporate the Flax Egg: Allow the dough to cool for a couple of minutes, then add the prepared flax egg. Mix until fully incorporated. The dough should be smooth and slightly sticky.

  5. Pipe the Pastry: Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a large round tip. Pipe small mounds (about 1.5 inches in diameter) onto the lined baking sheet, leaving space between each puff to allow for expansion.

  6. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the puffs are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped. Avoid opening the oven door during the first 20 minutes, as this can cause the puffs to deflate.

Step 3: Prepare the Coconut Cream Filling

  1. Whip the Coconut Cream: While the pastries are baking, remove the chilled coconut milk from the refrigerator. Carefully open the can and scoop out the solid cream into a mixing bowl, leaving the liquid behind.

  2. Add Sweetener and Flavor: Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract to the bowl. Using a hand mixer or a whisk, whip the mixture until fluffy and creamy. If you prefer a thicker consistency, add cornstarch and mix until combined.

  3. Chill the Filling: Cover the whipped coconut cream and place it in the refrigerator until the pastries are ready to be filled.

Step 4: Assemble the Cream Puffs

  1. Cool the Pastries: Once baked, remove the cream puffs from the oven and let them cool completely on a wire rack.

  2. Fill the Puffs: Once cooled, use a sharp knife to cut a small slit in the side of each cream puff. This will be your filling hole. Alternatively, you can use a piping bag to inject the cream directly into the center for a more elegant approach.

  3. Pipe the Coconut Cream: Fit a piping bag with a small round tip and fill it with the whipped coconut cream. Gently squeeze the filling into each cream puff until they feel full.

  4. Dust and Serve: If desired, dust the filled cream puffs with powdered sugar or cocoa powder. Serve immediately for the best texture, or refrigerate until ready to serve.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Cream Puffs

  • Chill the Coconut Milk: Ensure your coconut milk is chilled overnight for the best whipping results. The solid cream separates from the liquid when cold, making it easier to whip into a fluffy filling.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to flavor your filling with different extracts like almond or mint, or add a tablespoon of cocoa powder for a chocolate version.

  • Storage: Vegan cream puffs are best enjoyed fresh but can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two days. The pastry may lose some crispness over time.

Why Go Vegan?

Transitioning to a vegan lifestyle can be beneficial for various reasons, including health, environmental sustainability, and animal welfare. Making vegan desserts like these cream puffs allows you to enjoy sweet treats without compromising your values.

Conclusion

Vegan cream puffs are a delightful treat that showcases how delicious plant-based desserts can be. With a light and airy pastry filled with creamy coconut goodness, these treats are perfect for parties, gatherings, or simply a sweet indulgence at home. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and enjoy the delightful experience of making and savoring your own vegan cream puffs. Happy baking!
