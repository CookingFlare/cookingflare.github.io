Easy Spanish Rice Recipes: From Arroz con Pollo to Seafood Paella


Spanish rice dishes are a cornerstone of Iberian and Latin American cuisine, known for their vibrant colors, rich flavors, and satisfying textures. Whether paired with chicken, seafood, or vegetables, Spanish rice is a versatile and delicious addition to any meal. In this post, we’ll take a journey through some easy-to-make and flavorful Spanish rice recipes, from the comforting Arroz con Pollo to the festive Seafood Paella, and everything in between. Let’s dive into the heart of Spanish cooking and discover how to make these classic dishes in your own kitchen.

The Foundations of Spanish Rice

Before we explore specific recipes, it’s important to understand the basic components of Spanish rice. Typically, Spanish rice is made by first sautéing the rice in oil, which helps the grains separate and absorb flavors more effectively. From there, vegetables like onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers are often added, along with a variety of seasonings and broths. The signature flavor of Spanish rice often comes from saffron, paprika, and garlic.

The beauty of Spanish rice lies in its ability to absorb the flavors of whatever ingredients it’s paired with. Whether it’s the savory richness of chicken or the briny freshness of seafood, Spanish rice is a blank canvas that enhances whatever it’s cooked with.

Key Ingredients for Spanish Rice Dishes:

  • Rice: The type of rice you use can significantly impact the texture of your dish. Short-grain rice, like Arborio or Bomba rice, is commonly used in Spanish rice dishes because it absorbs liquid well and becomes tender.
  • Saffron: This aromatic spice is often used in Spanish rice to provide a warm, golden color and a subtle, earthy flavor.
  • Broth: Whether chicken, vegetable, or seafood broth, using a good-quality broth adds richness to the rice.
  • Tomato: Tomatoes, whether fresh, pureed, or in the form of tomato paste, provide a tangy undertone to the dish.
  • Garlic, Onion, and Bell Peppers: These vegetables form the base of many Spanish rice dishes, adding savory and aromatic layers of flavor.
  • Spices: Paprika (smoked or sweet) is often used in combination with saffron to enhance the depth of flavor in the rice.

With these foundational elements in mind, let’s dive into some of the most popular and easy-to-make Spanish rice dishes that you can whip up at home.

Arroz con Pollo (Spanish Rice with Chicken)

One of the most beloved and traditional Spanish rice dishes is Arroz con Pollo, which translates to “rice with chicken.” It’s a comforting one-pot dish that combines the earthy flavor of chicken with the vibrant colors and flavors of Spanish rice. Arroz con Pollo is a staple in many Spanish-speaking countries, and it’s easy to see why. The combination of tender chicken, savory rice, and rich seasoning is a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 1 ½ cups long-grain rice
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large tomato, chopped (or 1 cup canned diced tomatoes)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Chicken: Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, and paprika. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, add the chopped onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables soften and the garlic becomes fragrant.
  3. Add the Tomatoes and Rice: Stir in the chopped tomato and cook for 2 minutes. Add the rice and sauté for 3-4 minutes until the rice becomes slightly translucent.
  4. Cook the Rice: Add the chicken broth and saffron (if using). Stir to combine, then return the chicken to the pan, placing it on top of the rice. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Add the Peas: In the final 5 minutes of cooking, add the frozen peas. Once the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed, remove the pan from heat and let it sit for 5 minutes before serving.
  6. Serve: Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro and enjoy!

This dish is the epitome of comfort food, and it’s packed with flavor from the paprika, saffron, and rich chicken broth. It’s a meal that’s sure to satisfy your cravings for a hearty and flavorful dinner.

Seafood Paella (Paella de Mariscos)

When it comes to iconic Spanish dishes, paella is probably the first one that comes to mind. Originating in Valencia, paella is a rice dish typically cooked with a variety of meats and seafood. Paella de Mariscos (seafood paella) is a variation that focuses on fresh seafood, making it a perfect choice for a coastal-inspired feast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Arborio or Bomba rice
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 3 cups seafood broth
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1/2 pound squid, sliced into rings
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Paella Base: Heat olive oil in a large paella pan or wide, shallow skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, cooking for 4-5 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Cook the Rice: Stir in the rice, ensuring it’s coated in the oil and vegetables. Cook for 2 minutes, then add the white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the pan. Let the wine reduce for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Simmer the Rice: Add the seafood broth, saffron, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Let the rice cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the Seafood: Once the rice is almost tender, arrange the shrimp, mussels, and squid on top of the rice. Continue to cook, covered, for another 5-7 minutes until the seafood is cooked through and the mussels have opened.
  5. Finish and Serve: Stir in the frozen peas and let them warm through. Remove the paella from the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

Paella de Mariscos is a showstopper, perfect for dinner parties or special occasions. The mix of seafood, saffron, and smoked paprika creates an irresistible flavor profile that will transport you straight to the shores of Spain.

Arroz a la Cubana (Cuban-Style Rice)

Arroz a la Cubana is a popular dish in Cuban cuisine that combines rice with fried eggs, fried plantains, and a savory tomato-based sauce. It’s a simple, yet satisfying meal that reflects the fusion of Spanish and Caribbean influences in Cuban cooking.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 ripe plantains, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Plantains: Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the plantain slices until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove them from the pan and set them aside on paper towels to drain.
  2. Make the Tomato Sauce: In the same pan, add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing for 2-3 minutes until softened. Stir in the tomato sauce and sugar, cooking for another 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, then remove from heat.
  3. Fry the Eggs: In a separate pan, fry two eggs sunny-side-up or to your desired level of doneness.
  4. Assemble the Dish: Serve a portion of rice on each plate. Top with a fried egg, a few plantain slices, and a spoonful of tomato sauce.

Arroz a la Cubana is comfort food at its finest. The sweetness of the plantains, the richness of the fried egg, and the tanginess of the tomato sauce come together in perfect harmony.

Arroz con Mariscos (Rice with Seafood)

If you love seafood, Arroz con Mariscos is a dish you’ll want to try. This Spanish rice dish is typically prepared with a mix of seafood such as shrimp, clams, mussels, and squid. It’s flavorful, fresh, and perfect for summer gatherings or special celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon saffron (optional)
  • 2 cups seafood broth
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 mussels, scrubbed
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or paella pan over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking for 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Cook the Rice: Stir in the rice and cook for 2 minutes. Add the paprika, saffron, seafood broth, and salt. Bring to a simmer, reduce the heat, and cook the rice until nearly tender, about 15 minutes.
  3. Add the Seafood: Arrange the shrimp and mussels on top of the rice. Cover and cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the seafood is cooked through and the mussels have opened.
  4. Serve: Stir in frozen peas, then remove from heat. Let it sit for 5 minutes before serving with lemon wedges.

Arroz con Mariscos is a vibrant, seafood-infused dish that brings the flavors of the ocean to your table. The rice absorbs the rich broth, and the seafood provides a burst of freshness that is simply irresistible.

Conclusion

Spanish rice dishes are some of the most flavorful, satisfying, and diverse meals you can prepare. Whether you’re craving a comforting bowl of Arroz con Pollo, the vibrant, seafood-rich Paella, or the simple but delicious Arroz a la Cubana, there’s no shortage of ways to enjoy Spanish rice. These dishes are perfect for family dinners, special occasions, or any time you want to bring a little bit of Spain to your kitchen. With just a few basic ingredients and some patience, you can recreate these iconic dishes in your own home and enjoy the bold, comforting flavors of Spanish cuisine.
