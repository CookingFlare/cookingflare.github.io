Chinese Xiaolongbao with Pork and Soup Dumplings Recipe


Xiaolongbao, also known as soup dumplings, are a beloved Chinese dish that originated in the Jiangnan region. These delicate little dumplings are filled with savory pork and a burst of hot soup, making them a delightful treat for any occasion. In this recipe, I will guide you through the process of making these mouthwatering Xiaolongbao from scratch.

Ingredients

For the dumpling wrappers:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup boiling water

For the filling:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the soup:

  • 2 cups chicken or pork stock
  • 1 tablespoon gelatin

Instructions

  1. Start by making the dumpling wrappers. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and boiling water. Stir until a rough dough forms. Transfer the dough onto a clean surface and knead for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  2. While the dough is resting, prepare the filling. In a large bowl, mix together the ground pork, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, minced ginger, minced garlic, sesame oil, sugar, white pepper, and salt. Stir until all the ingredients are well combined.

  3. After the dough has rested, divide it into small portions and roll each portion into a thin circle, about 3 inches in diameter. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each wrapper.

  4. Now comes the tricky part - sealing the dumplings. Start by folding the wrapper in half, creating a half-moon shape. Use your fingers to pinch the edges together, making small pleats as you go. This will create a tightly sealed dumpling. Repeat this process with the remaining dumplings.

  5. In a small saucepan, heat the chicken or pork stock until it comes to a gentle simmer. Add the gelatin and stir until it dissolves completely. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  6. Place the sealed dumplings in a bamboo steamer lined with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Steam the dumplings over high heat for about 10 minutes, or until the wrappers become translucent and the filling is cooked through.

  7. Serve the Xiaolongbao hot, with a small dish of soy sauce or black vinegar for dipping. Be careful when biting into the dumplings, as the hot soup inside can be quite hot. Enjoy the explosion of flavors and textures that these delightful dumplings offer.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can substitute the ground pork with finely chopped mushrooms or tofu. Add some additional seasonings to enhance the flavor.
  • To make the process easier, you can use store-bought dumpling wrappers instead of making your own. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.
  • Xiaolongbao can be frozen before steaming. Simply place the sealed dumplings on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze until firm. Once frozen, transfer them to a freezer bag or container. When ready to cook, steam the frozen dumplings for an additional 5 minutes.

Now that you have the recipe for Xiaolongbao, it’s time to gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure. These delightful dumplings are sure to impress your family and friends with their unique combination of flavors. So, put on your apron, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor the authentic taste of Chinese cuisine. Happy cooking!
