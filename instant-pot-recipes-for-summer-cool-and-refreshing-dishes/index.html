Instant Pot Recipes for Summer: Cool and Refreshing Dishes


When the summer heat kicks in, spending time in a hot kitchen is the last thing on anyone’s wish list. That’s where the Instant Pot becomes your secret weapon. This versatile kitchen gadget isn’t just for cold-weather soups and stews—it’s equally brilliant for creating cool and refreshing dishes that are perfect for summer dining. From crisp salads to chilled desserts, the Instant Pot offers convenience, efficiency, and the ability to prepare impressive meals without breaking a sweat. Let’s dive into some innovative recipes that will keep you refreshed all summer long.

Zesty Lemon Herb Quinoa Salad

A light and refreshing quinoa salad is perfect for sultry summer days. The Instant Pot drastically shortens the cooking time for quinoa and ensures it’s fluffy and perfectly cooked every single time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 lemon (juiced and zested)
  • ½ cup chopped fresh parsley
  • ½ cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1 small cucumber (diced)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa thoroughly under cold water to remove its bitter outer coating.
  2. Add the quinoa and water to the Instant Pot. Secure the lid, set to “Sealing,” and cook on Manual (High Pressure) for 1 minute.
  3. Allow a 10-minute natural pressure release, then carefully release any remaining pressure. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and transfer it to a large bowl to cool completely.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Once the quinoa has cooled, toss it with the lemon dressing, parsley, mint, cucumber, and tomatoes.
  6. Chill the salad for 30 minutes in the refrigerator before serving.

This lemon herb quinoa salad tastes even better the next day, making it a wonderful make-ahead option for outdoor picnics or barbecues.

Instant Pot Poached Chicken for Salads and Wraps

Instead of purchasing pre-cooked chicken from the store, use your Instant Pot to poach juicy and tender chicken breasts. This method is perfect for whipping up a batch for versatile summer recipes like salads, wraps, or sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 garlic cloves (crushed)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Fresh herbs (optional, such as thyme or rosemary)

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts in the Instant Pot and cover them with water or broth. Add the bay leaf, garlic, salt, and any optional herbs of your choice.
  2. Secure the lid, set the Instant Pot to “Sealing,” and cook on Manual (High Pressure) for 10 minutes.
  3. Allow a natural pressure release for 5 minutes, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
  4. Remove the chicken from the pot and let it cool. Shred or slice it to your preferred thickness.

Use this poached chicken in light and refreshing dishes such as a chicken Caesar salad, a Mediterranean wrap with tzatziki, or a tangy mango chicken salad.

Chilled Summer Corn Chowder

When you think of chowder, heavy soups for winter months probably come to mind. This summer corn chowder flips the script, offering a lighter, chilled version that is both creamy and refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 5 ears of fresh corn (kernels removed)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 medium onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium potato (peeled and diced)
  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Heat the olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant and translucent (about 2-3 minutes).
  2. Add the corn kernels, potato, vegetable broth, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Close the lid, set the Instant Pot to “Sealing,” and cook on Manual (High Pressure) for 5 minutes. Perform a quick-release when cooking is complete.
  4. Using an immersion blender, puree the mixture until smooth. Stir in the coconut milk for a creamy texture.
  5. Let the soup cool to room temperature before transferring it to the fridge to chill for at least 2 hours.
  6. Serve the chilled corn chowder topped with fresh chives or parsley.

This dish is perfect as an appetizer for a summer dinner or a light lunch alongside a crusty baguette.

Effortless Instant Pot Ratatouille

A colorful ratatouille is the epitome of summer’s bounty. The Instant Pot streamlines this French vegetable stew, preserving the integrity of the vegetables while melding their flavors perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant (diced)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced into rounds)
  • 1 yellow squash (sliced into rounds)
  • 1 red bell pepper (diced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (diced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” and heat the olive oil. Cook the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, bell pepper, tomatoes, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together to combine.
  3. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to Manual (High Pressure) for 3 minutes.
  4. Perform a quick pressure release and taste the ratatouille, adjusting salt or seasoning if necessary.
  5. Garnish with freshly torn basil leaves and serve hot, cold, or at room temperature.

This ratatouille can be enjoyed on its own, over rice, or even as a topping for grilled chicken or fish during backyard barbecues.

Quick and Creamy Yogurt Parfaits

Cooling down with a dessert doesn’t have to mean store-bought treats. The Instant Pot can even help you make homemade yogurt that forms the base for creamy and refreshing parfaits.

Ingredients for Yogurt:

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt with active cultures

Ingredients for Parfaits:

  • 1 cup granola
  • 1 cup mixed summer berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional)

Instructions for Yogurt:

  1. Pour the milk into the Instant Pot and press the “Yogurt” setting. Heat the milk up to 180°F (you may need to use a thermometer).
  2. Allow the milk to cool to 110°F. Whisk in the plain yogurt until fully combined.
  3. Cover the Instant Pot with its lid and keep it on the “Yogurt” setting for 8-10 hours, or overnight, until the yogurt thickens and tang develops.
  4. Transfer the yogurt to the refrigerator to chill.

Assembling Parfaits:

  1. In a glass or bowl, layer homemade yogurt with granola and mixed summer berries.
  2. Drizzle with honey or maple syrup for added sweetness if desired.

These yogurt parfaits are a fantastic make-ahead breakfast or healthy dessert option for summer.

Conclusion

With the Instant Pot, summer cooking becomes a breeze, offering meals that are not only quick to prepare but also light and refreshing for the season. From cool salads to chilled soups and even homemade yogurt, these recipes prove you don’t need to sacrifice flavor or variety to beat the heat. So, crank up your Instant Pot, embrace the fresh ingredients of the season, and enjoy the ease of summer cooking that keeps you cool and satisfied.
