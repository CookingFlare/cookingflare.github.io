Mexican Pozole Verde with Chicken


Rich in tradition and bursting with flavor, Pozole Verde with Chicken is a beloved dish that showcases the vibrant essence of Mexican cuisine. This comforting soup, with its deep layers of meaning and taste, has been served at celebrations, family dinners, and festivals for centuries. The bright green broth infused with herbs and spices complements the tender chicken and hearty hominy perfectly, making this dish a must-try for anyone who appreciates authentic Mexican flavors.

In this post, we’ll guide you through how to prepare Pozole Verde with Chicken from scratch. By the end of this journey through ingredients, preparation techniques, and cultural insights, you’ll have the expertise to bring this nourishing dish to your own table.

What is Pozole Verde?

Pozole, derived from the Nahuatl word ‘pozolli’ meaning “foam,” is a traditional Mexican soup that dates back to pre-Columbian times. It’s made with hominy (maize kernels processed to remove the hull and germ) and enriched with proteins such as chicken or pork. While there are different variations of pozole, ranging from Pozole Blanco to Pozole Rojo, Pozole Verde distinguishes itself with a fresh, green sauce made from tomatillos, cilantro, and other green ingredients.

This dish is not only a culinary delight but also a cultural symbol in Mexico. It’s often served during special occasions like weddings, birthdays, and celebrations for Mexican Independence Day.

Ingredients for Pozole Verde with Chicken

To create the perfect Pozole Verde, it’s essential to use fresh ingredients. Each component of this recipe plays an important role in achieving the harmonious balance of flavors for which this dish is known.

For the Pozole Base:

  • 2 pounds shredded cooked chicken (You can use boneless chicken breast or thighs)
  • 2 cups hominy (canned hominy is convenient, but dried hominy can be used if soaked and cooked beforehand)
  • 8 cups chicken broth (homemade broth works best for depth of flavor)

For the Green Sauce:

  • 8-10 tomatillos, husked and rinsed
  • 2 poblano peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1 jalapeño pepper (optional, adjust for spice preference)
  • 1 bunch fresh cilantro
  • 1 cup spinach, optional, for added green color and nutrition
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • ½ medium onion
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Garnishes (Optional but Recommended):

  • Thinly sliced radishes
  • Shredded cabbage
  • Diced avocado
  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Dried oregano
  • Crispy tostadas or tortilla chips

Step-by-Step Preparation

Creating Pozole Verde is more than just cooking; it’s about immersing yourself in a culinary tradition that has been lovingly passed down through generations. Follow these steps to prepare this vibrant dish:

Step 1: Roast and Prep the Vegetables

Begin by roasting the poblano peppers over an open flame or in the oven until the skins are charred and blistered. Once roasted, peel off the blackened skin, remove the seeds, and set the peppers aside.

Tomatillos require less prep; simply husk, rinse, and roughly chop them. Their tangy flavor is essential for the verde sauce.

Step 2: Blend the Verde Sauce

In a blender or food processor, combine the tomatillos, roasted poblano peppers, jalapeño (if using), cilantro, spinach, garlic cloves, onion, and cumin powder. Blend the ingredients until smooth, adding a dash of chicken broth if necessary to achieve a pourable consistency. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 3: Cook the Verde Sauce

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Pour the blended sauce into the pot and cook for approximately 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. This cooking process reduces any acidity from the tomatillos and deepens the flavors of the sauce.

Step 4: Assemble the Soup Base

Add the hominy, shredded chicken, and chicken broth to the pot containing the cooked verde sauce. Stir gently to combine and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, simmer the soup for 30 minutes, and allow the flavors to meld beautifully. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

Step 5: Prepare Garnishes

While the soup simmers, prepare your garnishes. Thinly slice radishes for a crunchy, refreshing addition. Shred cabbage to top the soup with a satisfying texture. Dice avocados for creaminess, and slice limes to squeeze fresh juice into each bowl.

Serving and Enjoying Pozole Verde

Pozole Verde is traditionally served hot and garnished with an array of fresh toppings. Ladle the soup into large bowls, ensuring each serving has a generous helping of hominy and chicken. Set up a garnish bar on the table with radishes, cabbage, avocado, lime wedges, dried oregano, and crispy tostadas or tortilla chips, so everyone can customize their bowl to their taste.

Take a moment to appreciate the aroma of fresh cilantro, roasted tomatillos, and garlic wafting from the dish. Each spoonful brings together smoky poblano peppers, hearty chicken, chewy hominy, and bright, zesty verde sauce—a true celebration of flavor.

Tips for Making the Perfect Pozole Verde

  • Hominy Options: If you’re using dried hominy, soak it overnight and cook it in a separate pot until tender before adding it to the soup. This extra step rewards you with a deeper flavor and better texture.
  • Customizing Heat Levels: For a milder soup, skip the jalapeño or reduce its quantity. For more heat, add serrano peppers or increase the amount of jalapeño.
  • Make it Ahead: Pozole Verde tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to deepen and meld together. Prepare it in advance for an effortless meal during busy days or gatherings.

The Cultural Significance of Pozole Verde

Pozole Verde is more than just a dish—it’s an experience steeped in Mexico’s culinary heritage. It’s a recipe that binds families, preserves stories, and honors traditions. The dish is often central to celebrations, offering warmth, comfort, and connection.

As you prepare Pozole Verde in your own kitchen, you’re not simply cooking; you’re embracing a piece of history. Whether it’s for a casual dinner night or a festive occasion, this dish has the power to transform a meal into a memorable event.

Pozole Verde with Chicken is a dish that deserves a spot in your recipe collection. Its bold flavors, deep cultural roots, and wholesome ingredients create a culinary masterpiece that brings people together. With its vibrant green broth and customizable garnishes, it’s a crowd-pleaser that can be tailored to suit your palate.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather fresh ingredients, and prepare to experience the rich tradition of Mexican Pozole Verde in your own home. With this recipe in hand, you’ll be serving up more than soup—you’ll be sharing the spirit of Mexico one bowl at a time.
