The Central Crux- Start Your Chinese Culinary Journey from the Capital City with These Recipes


Embark on a scrumptious and mouth-watering journey by diving into the heart of Chinese cuisine, a culinary art that dates thousands of years back. Home to an impressive array of flavors, ingredients, and techniques, Chinese food is much more than just about noodles and fried rice. Let’s teleport to the mesmerizing capital city and learn how to whip up authentic flavors that will tantalize your taste buds. Here are some delectable recipes to try at home, that will transport you straight to the bustling streets of Beijing.

The dumpling capital of the world, Beijing is an ever-flowing treasure trove of mouthwatering delicacies and no Beijing culinary journey is complete without sampling the astounding variety of dumplings. Let’s begin with the traditional Pork and Chive Dumplings that form an integral part of the city’s gastronomic landscape.

Ingredients

*300g ground pork *3 tbsp finely chopped chives *1 tbsp grated ginger *1 tbsp soy sauce *1 tbsp sesame oil *1 tbsp cornstarch *1 tbsp minced garlic *Salt to taste *30 wonton wrappers *Boiling water to seal the dumplings

Instructions

  1. Combine the ground pork, chives, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, garlic, and salt in a large bowl, and mix well until all ingredients are well-incorporated.

  2. Lay out wonton wrappers on a flat surface, and place a spoonful of the pork mixture at the center of each one.

  3. Wet the edges of the wrapper with a little water, then fold the dumpling in half to form a semi-circle, pressing the edges together to seal.

  4. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and carefully add the dumplings. Make sure not to overcrowd the pot.

  5. Once the dumplings float to the surface, cook for another 2 minutes, then seive out and serve hot.

Beijing is not just about dumplings, its folklore is punctuated with the robust flavors of stir-fried dishes. For those who crave an explosion of bold flavor in their meals, Peppery Beef with Eggplant is the perfect dish.

Ingredients

*1 pound beef (flank or sirloin), sliced thinly against the grain *1 large eggplant, cut into bite-sized pieces *5 cloves garlic, finely chopped *2 tbsp finely chopped ginger *3 tbsp chili bean sauce *2 tbsp rice wine (Shaoxing wine) *2 tbsp soy sauce *1 tbsp sugar *3 tbsp water *1/2 cup chicken stock *2 tbsp vegetable oil *Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the chili bean sauce, rice wine, soy sauce, sugar, and water. Mix well, and set aside.

  2. In a wok, heat vegetable oil over high heat.

  3. Add the beef slices and cook until browned, but not quite done. Remove from the wok and set aside.

  4. In the same wok, add more oil, if necessary, then add the ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.

  5. Add the eggplant, and stir-fry until it starts to soften. At this point, add the reserved sauce mixture and bring to a simmer.

  6. Return the beef to the wok, along with the chicken stock, and let it simmer until the beef is fully cooked and the eggplant is soft.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot with rice.

Last but not the least, Beijing is famously synonymous with Peking Duck – a dish whose celestial flavors and aroma make it the centerpiece of any Chinese feast. Learn how to prepare Peking Duck at home and it might just transport your dining table straight to the imperial kitchens.

Ingredients

*1 whole duck (5-6 lbs) *1/2 cup Chinese five-spice powder *1/2 cup hoisin sauce *1/2 cup plum sauce *1/2 cup scallions, chopped *Sliced cucumbers *1 small packet of crepe wrappers or lettuce leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).

  2. Prick the duck all over with a fork and rub the five-spice powder all over the skin.

  3. Roast the duck in the preheated oven for 1 hour, basting it with its juices every 15 minutes.

  4. Remove the duck from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes before carving.

  5. To serve, spread a layer of hoisin sauce onto the crepe or lettuce leaf, add some sliced cucumber, a few pieces of duck meat, and drizzle with plum sauce. Roll up and enjoy!

Each of these classic dishes from the capital city of China reveals a unique insight into its culinary traditions. So, heat up your wok, roll up your sleeves, and start your Chinese culinary journey right from the heart of Beijing. Buon appetito!
