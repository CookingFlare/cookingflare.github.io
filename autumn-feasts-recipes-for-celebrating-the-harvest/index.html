Autumn Feasts: Recipes for Celebrating the Harvest


Autumn is a time of harvest, a season of abundance and gratitude. As the leaves turn golden and the air grows crisp, our thoughts turn to the bounty of the earth. In many cultures, autumn is a time to come together with family and friends to celebrate the harvest, and what better way to do so than with a delicious feast?

In this post, we’ll explore some of the most mouth-watering recipes for an autumn feast, perfect for entertaining guests or simply enjoying with loved ones. From hearty stews to sweet desserts, we’ll cover it all.

Autumnal Soup

A warm, comforting bowl of soup is the perfect way to start an autumn feast. Try this recipe for Roasted Butternut Squash and Apple Soup:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup diced apple
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet. Roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender. Sauté the onion and garlic in a large pot until softened. Add the roasted squash, cumin, smoked paprika, and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes. Purée the soup with an immersion blender or regular blender. Stir in heavy cream and diced apple. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired.

Main Course

For the main course, we recommend a hearty and comforting dish that’s sure to please. Try this recipe for Braised Short Ribs with Root Vegetables:

  • 4 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed

Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Season the short ribs with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the short ribs until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the ribs and set aside. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, and thyme. Stir to combine, then return the short ribs to the pot. Cover the pot and transfer to the preheated oven. Braise for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender. Add the sliced carrots, parsnips, and potatoes to the pot for the last 30 minutes of cooking. Serve the short ribs with the root vegetables and spoon some of the rich sauce over the top.

Dessert

No autumn feast would be complete without a sweet and satisfying dessert. Try this recipe for Pumpkin Spice Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup

Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease two 9-inch (23cm) round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. In a large bowl, whisk together the butter and eggs. Add the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, whisking until just combined. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of each cake comes out clean. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. For the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter until smooth. Add the vanilla extract and whisk until combined. Gradually add the powdered sugar, whisking until smooth and creamy. Add the maple syrup and whisk until combined. Assemble the cake by spreading a layer of frosting on top of one of the cooled cakes, then top with the second cake. Frost the outside of the cake with the remaining frosting.

Conclusion

Autumn is a time to come together with loved ones and celebrate the harvest. These recipes for Roasted Butternut Squash and Apple Soup, Braised Short Ribs with Root Vegetables, and Pumpkin Spice Cake with Maple Cream Cheese Frosting are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply enjoying a quiet evening at home, these dishes are sure to bring warmth and comfort to your autumn feast. So go ahead, gather your loved ones around the table, and indulge in the flavors of the season.
