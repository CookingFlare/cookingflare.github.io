How to Make Chole Bhature: A Delightful North Indian Street Food Recipe Step 1 - To Make Chole Chole, a spicy Punjabi dish, is a classic North Indian recipe made from chickpea curry. First, begin by draining soaked chickpeas and then cooking them in water mixed with salt until tender. Mashing half of the chickpeas coarsely to prepare a smoothened grain. then sauté garlic, ginger, onions, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and tomatoes in oil to make the paste for the curry. To Make the Gravy Prepare the curry by mixing together the sautéed paste, mashing chickpeas, water, ground cumin, ground coriander, red chili powder, garam masala powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pickle powder. Then, add a mixture of equal parts gram flour and water to thicken the curry and prevent it from becoming too thin. Next, add yogurt and simmer on low heat to allow the flavors to blend and thicken the gravy. Finally, add chopped fresh cilantro for garnish and serve hot. Step 2 - To Make Bhature, the Leavened Bread To create the delicious Bhature, start by mixing together wheat flour, potatoes, onions, ajwain, and salt in a large bowl. To give it the right texture and taste, add baking soda and ghee to the mixture and knead it well. Once the dough is soft and smooth, divide it into big balls to prepare individual Bhatures. Cover these balls and let them rest for about an hour to allow the dough to relax. Later, shape each ball into a round flatbread by pressing it gently with a little bit of force, and fry the bread in slightly hot oil until puffed and golden brown. Drain and serve hot with chole for a delicious breakfast or snack. A word of advice: Bhature should be soft inside and crispy outside. Kneading the dough for an extended period will help to achieve this goal.
