Vegan Baked Ziti: Cheesy Goodness Without the Dairy


If you’re looking for a comforting and indulgent dish that’s entirely plant-based, look no further than this Vegan Baked Ziti recipe. This dairy-free take on the classic Italian-American favorite is loaded with savory flavors and creamy textures that will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings for cheesy goodness without compromising on flavor.

Ingredients

For the Ziti:

  • 16 oz (450g) ziti pasta
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 24-oz jar of marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Vegan Cheese Sauce:

  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked in water for 4 hours or overnight
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the ziti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the marinara sauce and add the dried oregano and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the sauce simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, make the vegan cheese sauce. In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, almond milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked ziti with the marinara sauce and the vegan cheese sauce. Mix until the ziti is evenly coated in the sauce.
  7. Transfer the ziti mixture to a greased 9x13 inch baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  8. Sprinkle the breadcrumbs over the top of the ziti.
  9. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes.
  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the breadcrumbs are golden brown and the sauce is bubbly.
  11. Let the baked ziti cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Serving Suggestions

This Vegan Baked Ziti is a satisfying meal on its own, but you can also pair it with a fresh green salad or some garlic bread for a complete and comforting dinner. Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days and reheated in the oven or microwave.

Final Thoughts

With its rich and creamy texture, hearty marinara sauce, and a golden, crispy breadcrumb topping, this Vegan Baked Ziti is a testament to the fact that you don’t need dairy to enjoy a delicious, cheesy pasta dish. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or meal-prepping for the week ahead, this recipe is a winner that will please vegans and non-vegans alike. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to indulge in a dairy-free dish that’s full of flavor and satisfaction.
