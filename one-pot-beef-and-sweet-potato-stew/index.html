One Pot Beef and Sweet Potato Stew


Are you looking for a hearty and delicious meal that requires minimal effort and cleanup? Look no further than this One Pot Beef and Sweet Potato Stew! Packed with tender beef, nutritious sweet potatoes, and a medley of aromatic herbs and spices, this stew is sure to satisfy your cravings and warm you up from the inside out. Plus, with just one pot to cook in, you’ll spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your meal.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the beef stew meat and brown it on all sides. This step will add depth of flavor to the stew.
  2. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic becomes fragrant.
  3. Add the sweet potatoes, carrots, and celery to the pot. Stir everything together and cook for a few minutes to soften the vegetables slightly.
  4. Return the beef to the pot and pour in the beef broth and diced tomatoes. Stir in the dried thyme, dried rosemary, bay leaf, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a lid. Allow the stew to simmer gently for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  6. Stir the stew occasionally to prevent anything from sticking to the bottom of the pot. If the stew becomes too thick, you can add more beef broth to thin it out.
  7. Once the stew is ready, remove the bay leaf and serve hot. Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh herbs, such as parsley or thyme, to add a pop of color and freshness to the dish.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water and stir it into the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  • Feel free to add additional vegetables, such as peas or green beans, to the stew for extra color and nutrition.
  • This stew can also be made in a slow cooker. Simply follow steps 1 and 2, then transfer everything to a slow cooker and cook on low for 6 to 8 hours or on high for 3 to 4 hours.
  • Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for longer storage. Just make sure to cool the stew completely before storing it in an airtight container.

So there you have it – a simple and satisfying One Pot Beef and Sweet Potato Stew recipe that will warm your soul and fill your stomach. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this stew is sure to impress. Give it a try and let the flavors of tender beef and sweet potatoes transport you to a cozy and comforting place. Enjoy!
