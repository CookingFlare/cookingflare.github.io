Spring Pasta Dishes: Light and Fresh Recipes with Seasonal Peas


Spring is a season of renewal, and what better way to celebrate the vibrant flavors of this time of year than with light and fresh pasta dishes? In this blog post, we will explore some delicious recipes that incorporate seasonal peas, adding a burst of sweetness and texture to your plate. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or an impressive dish to serve at a gathering, these recipes are sure to please your taste buds and impress your guests.

Recipe 1: Lemon Pea Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of pasta (linguine or spaghetti works well)
  • 1 cup of fresh or frozen peas
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the peas to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until they are bright green and slightly tender.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, along with the lemon zest and juice. Toss to combine and coat the pasta evenly.
  5. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the lemon pea pasta hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves for an extra burst of flavor.

Recipe 2: Spring Pea Pesto Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of pasta (penne or fusilli works well)
  • 1 cup of fresh or frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup of pine nuts
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a food processor, combine the peas, pine nuts, grated Parmesan cheese, garlic, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is well combined and forms a chunky paste.
  3. With the food processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture becomes smooth and creamy.
  4. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the pea pesto to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until heated through.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat the pasta evenly with the pesto.
  6. Serve the spring pea pesto pasta hot, garnished with fresh mint leaves for a refreshing twist.

Recipe 3: Creamy Pea and Bacon Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of pasta (fettuccine or bowtie works well)
  • 1 cup of fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove from the skillet and crumble into small pieces. Set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the peas to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until they are bright green and slightly tender.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and grated Parmesan cheese. Cook until the sauce thickens slightly, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, along with the crumbled bacon. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the creamy sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve the creamy pea and bacon pasta hot, garnished with fresh parsley leaves for a burst of freshness.

Conclusion

These light and fresh pasta dishes featuring seasonal peas are perfect for welcoming the arrival of spring. Whether you prefer a zesty lemon flavor, a vibrant pea pesto, or a creamy and indulgent dish, there’s something here for everyone. So grab some peas and pasta, and get ready to savor the flavors of the season. Happy cooking!
