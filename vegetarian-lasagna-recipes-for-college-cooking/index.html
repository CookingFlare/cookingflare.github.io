Vegetarian Lasagna Recipes for College Cooking


Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is loved by many, but it often contains meat. For college students who are vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet, here are three delicious vegetarian lasagna recipes that are perfect for college cooking. These recipes are easy to make, budget-friendly, and packed with flavor. Let’s dive in!

Recipe 1: Classic Tomato and Vegetable Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup sliced vegetables (such as zucchini, bell peppers, and mushrooms)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced vegetables to the skillet and cook until they are tender. Season with salt, pepper, and dried oregano.
  5. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of the greased baking dish.
  6. Place three lasagna noodles on top of the sauce, then spread half of the ricotta cheese on top.
  7. Layer half of the cooked vegetables over the ricotta cheese, then sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers with the remaining ingredients: lasagna noodles, ricotta cheese, vegetables, and cheeses.
  9. Finish with a layer of marinara sauce and sprinkle with more mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  10. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  11. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Recipe 2: Spinach and Mushroom Lasagna Roll-Ups

Ingredients:

  • 8 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups chopped spinach
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they are tender. Season with salt, pepper, and dried basil.
  5. In a bowl, combine the chopped spinach, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  6. Lay out a lasagna noodle and spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on top.
  7. Place a spoonful of the spinach and ricotta mixture on the noodle, then top with some cooked mushrooms.
  8. Roll up the lasagna noodle and place it seam-side down in the greased baking dish. Repeat with the remaining noodles.
  9. Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the lasagna roll-ups and sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  10. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  11. Allow the lasagna roll-ups to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Recipe 3: Butternut Squash and Sage Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups butternut squash puree
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped sage leaves, and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add the butternut squash puree to the skillet and season with salt and pepper. Cook until heated through.
  5. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  6. Spread a thin layer of the butternut squash puree on the bottom of the greased baking dish.
  7. Place three lasagna noodles on top of the puree, then spread half of the cheese mixture on top.
  8. Repeat the layers with the remaining ingredients: lasagna noodles, cheese mixture, and butternut squash puree.
  9. Finish with a layer of butternut squash puree and sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  10. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbling.
  11. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before serving.

There you have it - three delicious and satisfying vegetarian lasagna recipes that are perfect for college cooking. Whether you prefer a classic tomato and vegetable lasagna, spinach and mushroom roll-ups, or a butternut squash and sage variation, these recipes are sure to impress. Enjoy your homemade lasagna and happy cooking!
