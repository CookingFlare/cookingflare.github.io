One-Pan Mediterranean Chicken with Olives and Herbs


When it comes to cooking, simplicity often brings the most delight. One-Pan Mediterranean Chicken with Olives and Herbs is not only an enticing dish bursting with flavors but also a convenient option for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. This recipe harmonizes the savory essence of chicken with the vibrant tastes of Mediterranean cuisine, all while keeping cleanup to a minimum.

Ingredients

To prepare an unforgettable One-Pan Mediterranean Chicken, gather the following ingredients:

For the Chicken

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (or breasts for a leaner option)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Vegetables

  • 1 medium red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or yellow), sliced
  • 1 zucchini, cut into half-moons
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted and sliced (Kalamata or Castelvetrano work beautifully)

For the Marinade

  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried)

For Garnishing

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Equipment Needed

  • A large oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven
  • A sharp knife and cutting board
  • A mixing bowl
  • Measuring spoons

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken to infuse it with the Mediterranean flavors. In a mixing bowl, combine olive oil, smoked paprika, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken thighs, ensuring they are well-coated. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes—if time permits, marinate it for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator for a more intense flavor.

Step 2: Prepare Your Vegetables

While the chicken marinates, slice the red onion, bell pepper, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes. The colorful medley contributes not just to the dish’s aesthetics but also enhances its flavor profile. Pitting and slicing the olives will add a salty, tangy element that complements the chicken beautifully.

Step 3: Preheat Your Oven

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). A well-preheated oven is essential for achieving perfectly cooked chicken with a crispy skin.

Step 4: Sear the Chicken

In a large oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven, heat a splash of olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, carefully place the marinated chicken thighs skin-side down in the pan. Sear for about 5-7 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy. This step locks in flavors and adds a delightful texture.

Step 5: Add the Vegetables

Once the chicken thighs are beautifully browned, flip them over and arrange the sliced vegetables around them in the pan. Scatter the olives on top, allowing their briny goodness to mingle with the other ingredients. Drizzle the lemon juice and zest over everything for a bright finish.

Step 6: Bake to Perfection

Transfer the skillet to your preheated oven. Bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and registers an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C). The vegetables will become tender and soak up all the delicious juices.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Once the dish is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. This brief resting period allows the juices to redistribute within the chicken. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side for an extra zing.

Pairing Suggestions

To elevate your One-Pan Mediterranean Chicken experience, consider pairing it with:

  • Couscous or Quinoa: These grains absorb the flavors from the dish beautifully and add a hearty element.
  • Crusty Bread: Perfect for soaking up the delicious juices left on your plate.
  • Greek Salad: A crisp, refreshing salad filled with cucumbers, tomatoes, feta, and olives balances the richness of the chicken.

Tips for Success

  1. Choose Quality Ingredients: The flavor of this dish largely depends on the quality of your ingredients. Opt for fresh, high-quality chicken and seasonal vegetables whenever possible.

  2. Don’t Skip the Marinade: Allowing the chicken to marinate not only enhances its flavor but also keeps it juicy during cooking. Aim for at least 30 minutes, but longer is better.

  3. Use Fresh Herbs: While dried herbs are convenient, using fresh herbs will take your dish to the next level with brighter flavors.

  4. Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a bit of spice, consider adding crushed red pepper flakes to the marinade or garnishing the finished dish with a drizzle of spicy olive oil.

  5. Make It Your Own: This recipe is highly adaptable. Feel free to swap out the vegetables based on what you have on hand—artichokes, eggplant, or even spinach work wonderfully.

Leftovers and Storage

If you find yourself with leftovers (which is often the case since this dish is incredibly filling), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in the oven or on the stovetop, adding a splash of water or chicken broth to keep the chicken moist.

For longer storage, you can freeze the chicken and vegetables in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Conclusion

One-Pan Mediterranean Chicken with Olives and Herbs is a dish that embodies the spirit of Mediterranean cuisine—simple, wholesome, and packed with flavor. This recipe not only simplifies your cooking process but also delivers a delightful feast that your family and friends will adore. Whether you’re seeking a quick weeknight meal or an impressive dish for entertaining, this one-pan wonder is sure to shine. Gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and savor the essence of the Mediterranean right in your kitchen. Enjoy!
