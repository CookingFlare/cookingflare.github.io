Make-Ahead Easter Recipes for Stress-Free Entertaining


Easter is a time for family, friends, and festive feasts. As a busy host, you want your Easter meal to be both delicious and stress-free. That’s where make-ahead recipes come in. These dishes can be prepared in advance, saving you time and energy on the big day. So, let’s explore five fantastic Easter recipes that you can create ahead and enjoy without any fuss.

  1. No-Fuss Easter Lamb

A feast wouldn’t be complete without the star of the show - the main course! This tender and succulent roasted lamb makes a beautiful centerpiece and is easily prepared in advance.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lb boneless lamb shoulder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup fresh rosemary leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).

  2. Rub the lamb with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

  3. In a small bowl, mix minced garlic, chopped onion, chicken broth, and fresh rosemary leaves to create a tasty marinade.

  4. Coat the lamb generously with the marinade and let it sit at room temperature while you preheat the oven.

  5. Place the lamb on a roasting rack in a roasting pan and cover with aluminum foil.

  6. Roast the lamb in the preheated oven for 4-5 hours, until it falls apart easily.

  7. Remove the lamb from the oven and let it rest for at least 15 minutes before carving.

  8. Savory Easter Casserole

A hearty casserole is the perfect side dish for your Easter feast. This make-ahead dish can be prepared and refrigerated for days before baking.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 lb asparagus, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C).

  2. In a large pot, cook the sliced potatoes in boiling, salted water until tender.

  3. In a separate pot, steam the asparagus and broccoli until they are cooked but still slightly crisp.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the potatoes, asparagus, broccoli, Swiss cheese, butter, flour, milk, eggs, salt, pepper, and thyme.

  5. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.

  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and refrigerate for up to 2 days.

  7. When ready to bake, remove the foil and bake the casserole in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, until bubbly and golden brown.

  8. Sweet Easter Delight

No meal is complete without something sweet for dessert! This classic Easter dessert can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator until it’s time to dig in.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 large egg, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup vegetable shortening
  • 2 cups powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  2. In a separate large bowl, cream the butter and egg together until light and fluffy.

  3. Stir in the vanilla extract and milk.

  4. Gradually incorporate the flour mixture into the wet ingredients, alternating with the vegetable shortening, until a soft dough forms.

  5. Cover the dough and let it rest at room temperature for 30-60 minutes.

  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/4-inch thickness.

  7. Using Easter-themed cookie cutters, cut out shapes and place them on ungreased baking sheets lined with parchment paper.

  8. Bake the cookies in a preheated 375°F (190°C) oven for 12-15 minutes, until golden brown.

  9. Allow the cookies to cool completely.

  10. In a small bowl, mix the powdered sugar with enough milk to make a smooth, pipeable glaze.

  11. Pipe the glaze over the cooled cookies and decorate with colorful sprinkles or edible glitter.

  12. Springtime Salad

A refreshing salad is the perfect way to round out your Easter meal. This vibrant salad can be assembled (without the avocado) ahead and dressed just before serving.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 cup sliced almonds
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded carrots
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped fresh apricots
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small skillet, toast the sliced almonds over medium heat, stirring frequently, until lightly browned.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the spinach, shredded carrots, chopped apricots, and diced avocados.

  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, honey, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

  5. Just before serving, add the cranberries and toasted almonds, and give the salad one more gentle toss.

  6. Colorful Deviled Eggs

No Easter meal is complete without deviled eggs! These pretty, flavorful eggs can be made ahead and refrigerated until ready to serve.

Ingredients:

  • 6 hard-boiled eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp yellow mustard
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • Chopped fresh chives, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Carefully hard-boil the eggs by placing them in a pot filled with cold water and bringing the water to a rolling boil.

  2. Cover the pot, remove it from heat, and let the eggs sit in the hot water for 12-15 minutes.

  3. Remove the eggs from the pot and immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to cool.

  4. Once the eggs are cool, peel them and slice them in half lengthwise.

  5. Gently remove the yolks and place them in a medium bowl.

  6. To the yolks, add mayonnaise, mustard, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, salt, and ground cumin. Mash and mix the ingredients until smooth.

  7. Pipe or spoon the yolk mixture back into the hollowed-out egg whites, making a slight depression in the center.

  8. Garnish each egg with chopped fresh chives.

With these make-ahead Easter recipes, you’ll be able to enjoy a stress-free celebration with your friends and family. All that’s left to do is set the table, serve some festive drinks, and enjoy the bounty of the season. Happy Easter!
