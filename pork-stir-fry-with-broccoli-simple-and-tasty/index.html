Pork Stir Fry with Broccoli: Simple and Tasty


When it comes to quick and satisfying weeknight dinners, few dishes can match the simplicity and flavor of a pork stir fry with broccoli. This classic combination brings tender slices of pork together with crisp, vibrant broccoli florets, all coated in a savory, slightly sweet sauce that’s bursting with umami. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just getting comfortable in the kitchen, this dish is incredibly accessible while still delivering on taste.

In this post, I’ll walk you through everything you need to know to master the perfect pork stir fry with broccoli — from ingredient selection and preparation techniques to creating the ultimate sauce and serving ideas. By the end, you’ll have a versatile recipe on hand that’s as easy as it is delicious.

Why Pork and Broccoli?

Pork is a wonderful choice for stir-frying due to its tender texture and ability to absorb flavors well. When thinly sliced, pork cooks quickly, making it ideal for a fast meal. Broccoli adds a delightful crunch and a healthful dose of vitamins and minerals, balancing out the richness of the pork.

Together, pork and broccoli create a harmonious blend of textures and flavors. The bright green broccoli provides freshness, while the pork adds heartiness. The stir fry technique locks in the nutrients and creates that signature crisp-tender bite.

Ingredients Breakdown

Let’s explore the key ingredients that bring this dish to life:

  • Pork: Tenderloin or loin are the best cuts because they’re lean and cook quickly. Avoid fattier cuts as they might release excessive grease.
  • Broccoli: Fresh broccoli crowns, cut into bite-sized florets, ensure a crunchy texture after a brief stir fry.
  • Garlic and Ginger: These aromatics infuse the dish with their pungent and zesty notes.
  • Soy Sauce: The backbone of the sauce, adding saltiness and depth.
  • Oyster Sauce: A secret weapon that lends a rich, slightly sweet, and savory complexity.
  • Sesame Oil: Used for finishing to add a nutty aroma.
  • Cornstarch: To thicken the sauce and give it a luscious texture.
  • Brown Sugar or Honey: Balances the saltiness with a touch of sweetness.
  • Vegetable or Canola Oil: For high-heat stir frying.
  • Optional: Red chili flakes or fresh chili for a bit of heat.

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Preparing the Pork

Start by slicing the pork into thin, uniform strips — about 1/4 inch thick. Thin slices ensure the pork cooks quickly and evenly. For best results, partially freeze the pork for about 20 minutes before slicing; this firms up the meat and makes cutting easier.

Once cut, marinate the pork briefly with a pinch of salt, a teaspoon of soy sauce, and a teaspoon of cornstarch. This technique, sometimes called velveting, helps keep the pork moist and tender during cooking.

2. Prepping the Broccoli

Cut broccoli into uniform florets for even cooking. If you like your broccoli extra crisp, consider blanching it—briefly boil for 1-2 minutes then immediately plunge into ice water. This step is optional but helps maintain a bright green color and crisp texture.

3. Stir Fry Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or honey
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water or chicken broth

This concoction provides a balanced flavor base and smooth consistency.

4. Cooking the Aromatics

Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat and add 1-2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Once hot, add minced garlic and ginger and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, careful not to burn them.

5. Stir Frying the Pork

Add the marinated pork strips to the wok in a single layer. Let them sear for 1-2 minutes undisturbed to get a nice caramelization, then stir-fry for another minute until the pork is nearly cooked through. Remove the pork from the wok and set aside.

6. Cooking the Broccoli

Add a little more oil if needed and toss in the broccoli florets. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the broccoli is tender-crisp. If you prefer softer broccoli, add a splash of water or broth and cover the pan for a minute.

7. Bringing It All Together

Return the pork to the wok and pour in the stir fry sauce. Stir constantly while the sauce thickens and becomes glossy. Drizzle a teaspoon of sesame oil over the dish for a fragrant finish.

Remove from heat and serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

Pork stir fry with broccoli pairs beautifully with steamed white or brown rice, which helps soak up the delicious sauce. For low-carb options, try serving with cauliflower rice or noodles such as rice noodles or soba.

A side of pickled vegetables or a simple cucumber salad adds a refreshing contrast.

Tips to Elevate Your Pork Stir Fry

  • Cut ingredients uniformly: Ensures even cooking and a professional appearance.
  • Use high heat: Stir frying is a rapid cooking method; high heat prevents sogginess.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan: Cook in batches if necessary to maintain that crisp texture.
  • Experiment with add-ins: Bell peppers, snow peas, carrots, or water chestnuts complement the dish well.
  • Adjust sauce balance: Feel free to tweak soy sauce and sugar quantities to suit your taste.

Meal Prep and Storage

This dish reheats well, making it ideal for meal prep. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a skillet over medium heat to avoid overcooking the broccoli.

Nutritional Highlights

Combining lean pork and broccoli creates a balanced meal rich in protein, vitamins C and K, fiber, and essential minerals. Using minimal oil and controlling seasoning helps keep the dish light and healthful.

Final Thoughts

Pork stir fry with broccoli is a shining example of how simple ingredients and straightforward techniques can come together to create an unforgettable dish. It’s quick to prepare, packed with flavor, and adaptable based on what’s in your pantry or fridge.

Whether you’re cooking for busy weeknights or aiming for a wholesome family meal, this recipe ticks all the boxes. Grab some fresh pork and broccoli next time you shop, and enjoy the delicious harmony of tender meat, crisp veggies, and savory sauce in just around 30 minutes.

Happy cooking!
