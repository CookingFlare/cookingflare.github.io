Salad Fanatic: 53 Wholesome and Delicious Recipes to Consume Daily


Salads are not just limited to your everyday lettuce and tomato mix tossed in a mundane vinaigrette. The possibilities are endless, and this list of 53 wholesome and delicious recipes will take your salad game to a whole new level. This fantastic collection of salad fanatic recipes will make you wonder why you ever deemed salads boring. By offering a variety of ingredients, flavors and cooking methods, you will surely findsomething that not only satisfies your taste buds but also encourages you to consume salads daily. So, without further ado, become a salad fanatic by incorporating these fresh recipes into your daily meals!

Whipped Feta and Pear Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe pears
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, and honey until well combined.
  2. Set the dressing aside and slice the pears, then arrange them on a layer of mixed salad greens on a large serving platter.
  3. Next, add crumbled feta cheese and chopped walnuts to the top of the greens and pears.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad, and then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Roasted Vegetable and Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 3 cups assorted chopped vegetables (such as bell peppers, zucchini, eggplant, and red onion)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F. Toss the chopped vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for about 25-30 minutes or until they are tender and lightly caramelized.
  3. Meanwhile, cook the quinoa following the package instructions.
  4. Once cooked, let the quinoa cool for a few minutes, then add it in a large mixing bowl with baby spinach and roasted vegetables.
  5. Finally, top it off with crumbled feta cheese and chopped parsley before serving.

Avocado and Chickpea Salad Po’ Boy

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 2 medium ripe avocados, cubed
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cups French bread, sliced
  • Lemon, olive oil, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together chickpeas, cubed avocado, sliced red onion, halved cherry tomatoes, and chopped cilantro. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and gently toss to combine. Let the salad sit for 15 minutes to let the flavors mingle.
  3. Toast the sliced French bread until crisp and golden brown.
  4. Layer the avocado and chickpea salad over the toast and enjoy a satisfying and filling salad Po’ Boy.
