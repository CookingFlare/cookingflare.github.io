1. Smoked Salmon and Avocado Stacks

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz smoked salmon, thinly sliced
  • 2 ripe avocados, cut into vertical slices
  • 1/2 cup crème fraîche
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped chives
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices of whole grain bread, toasted

Preparation:

  1. Stir the crème fraîche, chives, and lemon juice together in a small bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. On each bread slice, arrange a layer of crème fraîche mixture followed by an avocado slice and smoked salmon. Serve immediately.

2. Quinoa-Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 6 large bell peppers, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) corn kernels, drained
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Place bell pepper halves in a baking dish and set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, bring quinoa and vegetable broth to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  5. Combine quinoa, sautéed onions, garlic, black beans, corn, cilantro, green onions, salt, and pepper in a large mixing bowl.
  6. Stuff each pepper half with the quinoa mixture, and cover with aluminum foil.
  7. Bake for 25 minutes until peppers are tender. Uncover and bake for an additional 5 minutes.

3. Vegan Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries and mint leaves for garnish

Preparation:

  1. Scoop out the avocado flesh and place it in a food processor.
  2. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler, stirring occasionally. Once melted, let it cool slightly.
  3. Add the cooled chocolate, maple syrup, almond milk, and vanilla extract into the food processor.
  4. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed.
  5. Divide the mousse into individual serving bowls, garnish with berries and mint leaves, and serve chilled.

4. Spinach and Ricotta-Stuffed Chicken Roulades

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup fresh ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup packed fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes (in oil, drained)
  • 2 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, combine the ricotta, spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, feta, basil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Slice a pocket into the side of each chicken breast, making sure not to pierce through to the other side.
  4. Stuff each chicken breast with the ricotta and spinach mixture. Close the opening with toothpicks.
  5. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Sear the chicken breasts for 2-3 minutes on each side.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  7. Remove toothpicks, and slice the chicken roulades before serving.

5. Healthy Mac and Cheeze

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gluten-free elbow macaroni
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric (for color)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed

Preparation:

  1. Cook macaroni according to the package instructions, and drain.
  2. In a separate saucepan, whisk together almond milk, cornstarch, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, turmeric, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened.
  4. Slowly pour the almond milk mixture into the saucepan while whisking constantly.
  5. Bring the sauce to a simmer, stirring frequently, until it thickens - about 5 minutes.
  6. Combine cooked macaroni, peas, and cheeze sauce in a large mixing bowl, tossing to coat evenly.
  7. Serve hot, optionally garnished with extra nutritional yeast, black pepper, or chopped scallions.
