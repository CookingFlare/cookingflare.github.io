Tuscan Pork Ragu: A Hearty Italian Meat Sauce


Tuscan Pork Ragu is a classic Italian recipe that will transport you to the heart of Tuscany with its rich flavors and comforting aroma. This hearty meat sauce is traditionally served with pasta, but it can also be enjoyed with polenta or crusty bread. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of creating this delicious dish step by step. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the pork chunks to the pot and season with salt and pepper. Brown the pork on all sides until it develops a golden crust. This will take about 5-7 minutes. Remove the pork from the pot and set it aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté the vegetables until they become soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Deglaze the pot by pouring in the red wine. Scrape the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits stuck to it. Let the wine simmer for a minute or two to allow the alcohol to evaporate.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste until it is well incorporated with the vegetables.
  6. Add the crushed tomatoes, beef or vegetable broth, bay leaves, dried thyme, and dried oregano. Give it a good stir to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Return the browned pork to the pot, along with any accumulated juices. Make sure the pork is submerged in the sauce.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the ragu simmer gently for about 3 hours. Stir occasionally to make sure nothing sticks to the bottom of the pot.
  9. After 3 hours, the pork should be tender and easily shred with a fork. Remove the bay leaves and discard them.
  10. Use two forks to shred the pork into smaller pieces. Stir the shredded pork back into the sauce, allowing it to absorb all the flavors.
  11. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.
  12. Serve the Tuscan Pork Ragu over pasta, polenta, or crusty bread. Garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully prepared a delicious Tuscan Pork Ragu, a hearty Italian meat sauce that will delight your taste buds and warm your soul. The slow-cooked pork, combined with the aromatic vegetables and flavorful herbs, creates a sauce that is rich, savory, and full of depth. Whether enjoyed with pasta, polenta, or crusty bread, this dish is sure to satisfy your cravings for authentic Italian comfort food. Buon appetito!
