The Best Fruit-Based Dessert Recipes for Spring


As spring arrives and the weather warms, the abundance of fresh fruits provides the perfect opportunity to indulge in some light, refreshing, and delicious fruit-based desserts. Fruits are not only packed with natural sweetness, but they also bring a burst of color and nutrition to any dessert. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, looking for a healthy treat, or simply want to enjoy the flavors of the season, these fruit-based desserts will inspire you to make the most of the season’s bounty.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best fruit-based desserts for spring. From citrusy tarts and berry-packed cakes to fruit sorbets and light parfaits, there’s a recipe here to suit every craving. So, let’s dive into these vibrant and delectable recipes that will make your spring gatherings unforgettable.

1. Citrus Tart with a Coconut Crust

Citrus fruits, particularly oranges, lemons, and grapefruits, are in full swing during spring, offering a tangy contrast to the sweetness of baked goods. This citrus tart is a perfect way to bring out the best of these fruits while creating a light and airy dessert.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 cup shredded coconut
    • 1/2 cup almond flour
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup
    • 2 tbsp coconut oil, melted
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
    • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
    • 1/4 cup honey or agave syrup
    • 4 large eggs
    • 2 tbsp cornstarch
    • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cubed

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust:

    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • In a bowl, mix the shredded coconut, almond flour, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, and salt until everything is well combined.
    • Press the mixture into the bottom and up the sides of a tart pan to form a crust.
    • Bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Let it cool while you prepare the filling.

  2. Make the filling:

    • In a saucepan, combine the orange juice, lemon juice, honey, eggs, and cornstarch. Whisk the ingredients together until smooth.
    • Place the saucepan over medium heat and whisk continuously as the mixture thickens. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
    • Once the mixture has thickened to a custard-like consistency, remove it from the heat and stir in the butter and vanilla extract until fully incorporated.

  3. Assemble the tart:

    • Pour the citrus filling into the cooled crust and smooth it out with a spatula.
    • Refrigerate the tart for at least 3 hours to allow the filling to set completely.
    • Serve chilled, garnished with fresh citrus zest or mint leaves if desired.

This tart is the perfect combination of sweet, tart, and creamy, with a coconut crust that adds a fun texture and tropical flair.

2. Berry Sorbet

Nothing says spring like the sweet and juicy taste of fresh berries. This berry sorbet is a refreshing dessert that captures the essence of the season and is incredibly easy to make. It’s a healthier option that doesn’t compromise on flavor, making it a great choice for any springtime occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries, blackberries)
  • 1/2 cup sugar or sweetener of your choice (adjust based on berry sweetness)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Combine the berries, sugar, and lemon juice in a blender or food processor. Pulse until the mixture is smooth.
  2. Pour the berry puree into a fine mesh sieve set over a bowl. Use a spoon to press the puree through the sieve to remove the seeds and pulp.
  3. Transfer the strained berry juice into a shallow baking dish. Cover and freeze for about 2-3 hours.
  4. Once the mixture has frozen, scrape the surface with a fork to create a fluffy, snow-like texture.
  5. Serve the sorbet immediately, garnished with a few fresh berries or mint leaves.

This berry sorbet is a delightful, cool treat for those hot spring days. The natural fruit flavor is complemented by a subtle hint of lemon, creating a dessert that’s as refreshing as it is flavorful.

3. Mango Coconut Parfait

For a tropical twist on a classic dessert, the mango coconut parfait is a light and vibrant treat that’s both indulgent and healthy. Layers of creamy coconut yogurt, juicy mango, and a crunchy granola base make this dessert a refreshing choice for spring.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup coconut yogurt (or regular yogurt if preferred)
  • 1/2 cup granola
  • 1 tbsp shredded coconut
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a glass or dessert cup, layer a spoonful of granola at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of coconut yogurt on top of the granola, followed by a layer of fresh mango cubes.
  3. Repeat the layers until the glass is filled, finishing with a layer of mango.
  4. Sprinkle the top with shredded coconut and drizzle with honey or maple syrup for extra sweetness if desired.
  5. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

The combination of sweet mango, creamy coconut yogurt, and crunchy granola creates a balanced dessert that’s perfect for a spring afternoon. The tropical flavors are light yet satisfying, making it a perfect end to any meal.

4. Peach and Berry Crumble

A warm fruit crumble is a comforting dessert that never goes out of style. This peach and berry crumble is a springtime twist on the classic, combining the juiciness of ripe peaches with a medley of fresh berries. The crumbly oat topping adds the perfect texture contrast, making this a dish that’s both satisfying and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe peaches, peeled and sliced

  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries)

  • 1/4 cup sugar (or to taste)

  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • For the topping:

    • 1 cup rolled oats
    • 1/2 cup flour
    • 1/2 cup brown sugar
    • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
    • 1/4 cup butter, cold and cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, toss the sliced peaches and mixed berries with sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Spread the mixture evenly into a baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the oats, flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon.
  4. Add the cold butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to combine until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Sprinkle the crumble topping over the fruit mixture and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the fruit is bubbling.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra treat.

The combination of warm fruit and crunchy topping makes this crumble a perfect dessert for spring evenings when you’re craving something comforting yet light.

5. Strawberry Lemon Shortcakes

Shortcakes are a classic spring dessert, and when paired with juicy strawberries and a refreshing lemon twist, they become irresistible. These strawberry lemon shortcakes are light, fluffy, and bursting with flavor, making them the perfect way to celebrate the arrival of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced

  • 2 tbsp sugar

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour

  • 1 tbsp baking powder

  • 1/4 cup sugar

  • 1/4 tsp salt

  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed

  • 2/3 cup heavy cream

  • 1 tsp lemon zest

  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • For the whipped cream:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 2 tbsp powdered sugar
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and sugar. Stir and set aside to macerate for about 30 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  4. Add the cold butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut it into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream, lemon zest, and vanilla extract, stirring gently until the dough just comes together.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick rectangle, then cut it into rounds using a biscuit cutter or a glass.
  7. Place the rounds on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. While the shortcakes bake, whip the cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until soft peaks form.
  9. To assemble, split each shortcake in half, top with a spoonful of macerated strawberries, and finish with a dollop of whipped cream.

These strawberry lemon shortcakes are a sweet, zesty treat that perfectly captures the flavors of spring. The tangy lemon pairs beautifully with the juicy strawberries, and the soft, flaky shortcakes provide the ideal base.

Conclusion

Spring is the season to embrace fresh fruits and create desserts that are both vibrant and delicious. From citrusy tarts and berry sorbets to tropical parfaits and fruit crumbles, these fruit-based desserts are sure to brighten up any spring gathering. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply enjoying a relaxing afternoon, these desserts will add a burst of flavor and color to your table.

So, next time you’re craving something sweet and light, try one of these fruit-filled recipes. They’re the perfect way to savor the season’s best produce and treat yourself to a dessert that’s both satisfying and refreshing. Happy spring baking!
