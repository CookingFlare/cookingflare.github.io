Unwind: The Most Rejuvenating Chinese Herbal Tea Recipes


As you wrap up a long and tiring day, what better way to relax and rejuvenate than sipping on a warm cup of tea? Chinese herbal teas are not only useful in de-stressing, but they also boast a variety of health benefits making them a perfect choice for unwinding. In this blog post, we will explore a selection of Chinese herbal tea recipes that are not only simple to make but also packed with natural goodness for a perfect end to your day.

  1. Rose Wood Tea - A Tea Fit for Emperors

The Rose Wood Tea, also known as Kwei Hsieh Chun, dates back to the Han Dynasty and was a favorite of Chinese emperors for its rejuvenating properties. To prepare this exquisite tea, you will need a handful of dried roses, a bunch of dried osmanthus flowers, and water.

How to make:

  • In a teapot, combine the dried roses and osmanthus flowers.
  • Pour in boiling water and let the mixture steep for about 5 minutes.
  • Strain out the flowers, and voila, your Rose Wood tea is ready!
  • Toss in some honey or sugar for that extra touch of sweetness if preferred.
  1. Chrysanthemum Tea - A Wise Choice of Ancient Times

Chrysanthemum tea, or Sushi Cha, hailed as one of the 10 best herbal teas in the world, has been enjoyed in China for more than a thousand years. Packed with vitamins A, C, and E, this tea boasts a bouquet of benefits, including its ability to remedy insomnia and induce relaxation.

How to make:

  • Bring water only to 70 degrees Celsius (about 158°F) to preserve the aroma and nutrients of the flowers without scorching them.
  • Add two chrysanthemum flowers per cup to the warm water.
  • Let them steep for about 3 minutes, then remove the flowers and enjoy your brew.
  1. Jujube Tea - A Taste of Life’s Sweetness

The Jujube Tea or Da Zao Chun, made from dried red dates or jujubes, is a staple in Chinese households thanks to its numerous health benefits. Drunk to soothe the nerves and aid digestion, this sweet and comforting tea is also said to provide one with a sense of vitality and longevity.

How to make:

  • Rinse a handful of dried jujubes to remove any dust and dirt.
  • Place the jujubes in a pot of cold water, bring to a boil and let them simmer for about 20 minutes.
  • Strain out the jujubes and serve the tea hot.
  1. Goji Berry Tea - An Immortal’s Elixir

Celebrated for its antioxidant properties, the Goji Berry Tea, or Gou Qi Cha, is said to be the fruit of immortality - a treat even immortals crave. These vibrant red berries boast a variety of health benefits including improved eyesight, enhanced metabolism, and a strengthened immune system.

How to make:

  • Add a handful of goji berries to a pot of cold water.
  • Bring the water to a boil, and let the goji berries simmer for about 10 minutes.
  • Strain out the berries and indulge in your ’elixir of immortality.'
  1. Ginger Lemon Tea - A Warm Embrace for the Soul

For those days when you need a bit more warmth and comfort, the Ginger Lemon Tea, or Gan Jiang Cha, is the perfect potion. Loaded with antioxidants and rich in anti-inflammatory properties, this invigorating tea is not only a delicious way to unwind but also a natural treatment for colds, coughs, and sore throats.

How to make:

  • Using a traditional teapot, add a piece of sliced ginger about the size of your thumb.
  • Pour in boiling water and let the ginger-infused water steep for about 10 minutes.
  • Remove the ginger slice and squeeze in the juice of half a lemon.
  • Stir well and sweeten to taste, with honey or sugar if desired.
  • Fill your cup up with this invigorating blend and sip your way to relaxation.

Conclusion

With their rich flavors and an array of health benefits, these soothing Chinese herbal teas can be your ultimate escape from the daily grind. Give them a try and discover a brand new way to unwind and de-stress. Happy brewing!
