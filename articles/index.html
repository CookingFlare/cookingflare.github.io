Allergy-Free Barbecue: Grilling Recipes for Outdoor Gatherings

When it comes to outdoor gatherings, there’s nothing quite like a barbecue. The sizzling sounds, the mouthwatering aroma, and the delicious flavors make it a favorite summer pastime. However, for those with food allergies, attending barbecues can be a challenge. But fear not! We’ve got you covered with these allergy-free grilling recipes that will satisfy everyone’s taste buds. Grilled Vegetable Skewers Ingredients: 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks 1 zucchini, sliced 1 red onion, cut into chunks 8 cherry tomatoes 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating colors for an appealing presentation. Brush the skewers with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. Remove from the grill and serve hot. These skewers are a colorful and tasty addition to any barbecue spread. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Ingredients: 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts 2 lemons, juiced and zested 2 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: In a bowl, combine the lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, chopped parsley, chopped thyme, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place the chicken breasts in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated. Allow it to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard the excess liquid. Grill the chicken for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Remove from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. The combination of zesty lemon and aromatic herbs will make this chicken a crowd-pleaser. Grilled Pineapple with Coconut Glaze Ingredients: 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and cored 1/4 cup coconut milk 2 tablespoons honey 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Instructions: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Cut the pineapple into rings or wedges, depending on your preference. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, honey, and vanilla extract. Brush the pineapple slices with the coconut glaze, making sure to coat both sides. Place the pineapple on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the pineapple is caramelized. Remove from the grill and serve warm. The combination of sweet pineapple and creamy coconut glaze will satisfy any sweet tooth. Conclusion With these allergy-free grilling recipes, you can create a barbecue spread that caters to everyone’s dietary needs. Whether you’re a vegetarian, have food allergies, or simply want to try something different, these recipes are sure to impress. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy a delicious and inclusive outdoor gathering. Happy grilling! ...
