Irresistible Cinnamon Roll French Toast Recipes for a Decadent Brunch

Who can resist the heavenly aroma of freshly baked cinnamon rolls? Imagine taking that indulgence to the next level by transforming them into a decadent french toast dish. In this blog post, we will explore three irresistible cinnamon roll french toast recipes that are perfect for a luxurious brunch. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking! Recipe 1: Classic Cinnamon Roll French Toast Ingredients: 4 large cinnamon rolls 4 eggs 1/2 cup milk 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon Maple syrup, for serving Instructions: Preheat your griddle or frying pan over medium heat. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon. Slice each cinnamon roll horizontally into two even pieces. Dip each slice of cinnamon roll into the egg mixture, making sure to coat it evenly. Place the coated slices onto the preheated griddle or frying pan and cook until golden brown on both sides, around 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the heat and serve immediately with a drizzle of maple syrup. Recipe 2: Stuffed Cinnamon Roll French Toast Ingredients: 4 large cinnamon rolls 4 ounces cream cheese, softened 1/4 cup powdered sugar 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 4 eggs 1/2 cup milk 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon Maple syrup, for serving Instructions: Preheat your griddle or frying pan over medium heat. In a medium bowl, mix together the softened cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth. Slice each cinnamon roll horizontally, but this time, leave one side attached to create a pocket. Spread the cream cheese mixture onto the bottom half of each cinnamon roll. Carefully close the cinnamon rolls, creating a sandwich with the cream cheese filling in the middle. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, ground cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Dip each stuffed cinnamon roll into the egg mixture, ensuring that both sides are coated. Place the coated rolls onto the preheated griddle or frying pan and cook until golden brown on both sides, approximately 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the heat and serve warm with a drizzle of maple syrup. Recipe 3: Cinnamon Roll French Toast Casserole Ingredients: 8 large cinnamon rolls, cut into quarters 4 eggs 1 cup milk 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional) 1/4 cup melted butter Cream cheese glaze (included with the cinnamon rolls) Instructions: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon. Add the cinnamon roll quarters to the bowl and gently toss until all the pieces are coated in the egg mixture. Transfer the coated cinnamon roll pieces to the greased baking dish. Sprinkle chopped pecans (if using) evenly over the cinnamon roll pieces. Drizzle the melted butter over the top. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is golden brown and set in the center. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Drizzle the cream cheese glaze that came with the cinnamon rolls over the top of the casserole. Serve warm and enjoy! These three cinnamon roll french toast recipes are guaranteed to impress your friends and family at your next brunch gathering. Whether you prefer the classic version, the stuffed variation, or the indulgent casserole, each recipe is a delightful twist on a brunch favorite. So, go ahead and treat yourself to a decadent morning feast that will leave everyone craving for more. Bon appétit! ...

Indulgent Nutella French Toast Recipes for a Decadent Brunch

Who doesn’t love a delicious and indulgent brunch? And when it comes to brunch, French toast is an absolute classic. But why settle for ordinary French toast when you can take it to a whole new level of decadence with the addition of Nutella? In this post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering Nutella French toast recipes that will make your brunch a truly indulgent affair. Classic Nutella French Toast Ingredients: 4 slices of thick bread (brioche or challah work best) 2 eggs 1/2 cup of milk 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Nutella Butter, for frying Powdered sugar, for dusting Instructions: In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract. Spread Nutella generously on two slices of bread, then sandwich them with the remaining two slices. Heat a knob of butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Dip the Nutella sandwiches into the egg mixture, ensuring they are fully coated on both sides. Place the soaked sandwiches in the skillet and cook until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Repeat with the remaining sandwiches. Serve the Nutella French toast warm, dusted with powdered sugar. Nutella-Stuffed French Toast Roll-Ups Ingredients: 8 slices of bread Nutella 2 eggs 1/2 cup of milk 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Butter, for frying Maple syrup, for serving Instructions: Trim the crusts off the bread slices and flatten them with a rolling pin. Spread Nutella on each slice of bread, leaving a small border around the edges. Roll up each slice tightly and set aside. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract. Heat a knob of butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Dip each Nutella roll-up into the egg mixture, ensuring they are fully coated. Place the roll-ups in the skillet and cook until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Serve the Nutella French toast roll-ups warm, drizzled with maple syrup. Nutella-Stuffed French Toast Casserole Ingredients: 1 loaf of French bread, cut into cubes Nutella 6 eggs 2 cups of milk 1/4 cup of granulated sugar 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Butter, for greasing Maple syrup, for serving Instructions: Grease a baking dish with butter and set aside. Spread Nutella on half of the bread cubes and place them in the greased baking dish. Top with the remaining bread cubes. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, sugar, and vanilla extract. Pour the egg mixture over the bread cubes, ensuring they are evenly coated. Cover the baking dish with foil and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the Nutella French toast casserole, covered, for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until golden brown and set. Serve the Nutella French toast casserole warm, with a drizzle of maple syrup.
