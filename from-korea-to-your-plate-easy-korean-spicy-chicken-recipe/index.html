From Korea to Your Plate: Easy Korean Spicy Chicken Recipe


Korean cuisine has taken the world by storm, and for good reason. The bold flavors, vibrant colors, and spicy kick of Korean dishes have won the hearts of foodies everywhere. One of the most popular and accessible Korean dishes is spicy chicken, also known as “yangnyeom chicken” in Korean. In this recipe, we’ll take you on a culinary journey from Korea to your plate, sharing a simple and delicious spicy chicken recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

The Story Behind the Recipe

Spicy chicken is a staple in Korean cuisine, particularly in the summer months when the heat is unbearable. The dish is believed to have originated from the Korean-Chinese community, where chicken was marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, and gochujang (Korean chili paste) before being stir-fried with vegetables. Over time, the recipe evolved, and different regions in Korea developed their own unique variations of spicy chicken. Today, you can find spicy chicken on menus across Korea, from street food stalls to high-end restaurants.

The Recipe

Servings: 4-6 people

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken pieces (legs, thighs, wings, and breasts)
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (for garnish)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (for garnish)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, sugar, and sesame oil to make the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well to coat. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off.
  4. Add 2 tbsp of vegetable oil to the skillet and swirl it around to coat the bottom. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until browned and cooked through.
  5. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the remaining 2 tbsp of vegetable oil and swirl it around to coat the bottom.
  6. Add the Korean chili flakes and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
  7. Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and toss it with the chili flakes until well coated.
  8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  9. Garnish with minced garlic and chopped green onions.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra crispy exterior, coat the chicken with a mixture of cornstarch and potato starch before cooking.
  • Add some crunch to the dish by tossing in some toasted sesame seeds or chopped nuts.
  • For a spicier dish, increase the amount of Korean chili flakes or add some diced jalapeños to the marinade.
  • Serve the spicy chicken with a side of steamed rice, kimchi, or pickled vegetables for a well-rounded meal.

Conclusion

Spicy chicken is a staple in Korean cuisine, and with this easy recipe, you can bring a taste of Korea to your plate. The combination of gochujang, soy sauce, and Korean chili flakes creates a bold and spicy flavor profile that’s sure to please even the most discerning palates. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and requires minimal ingredients. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the bold flavors of Korea in the comfort of your own home!
