1. Seared Scallops with Herb Cream

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs large scallops
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup minced shallots
  • 2 tbsp chopped garlic
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/3 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Season scallops with salt and pepper; set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter with olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Cook shallots and garlic until softened, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add scallops to the skillet (cook in batches if necessary, to avoid overcrowding) and sear until browned on one side, about 1 minute.
  5. Flip scallops and cook for another minute until the internal temperature reaches 120°F.
  6. Remove scallops from the skillet, keeping the heat on.
  7. Pour heavy cream into the skillet and let it simmer for 5 minutes until it reduces by half.
  8. Mix in the chopped herbs and Parmesan cheese, stirring until the cheese melts.
  9. Return scallops to the skillet, spoon the sauce over them, and heat for a couple of minutes.
  10. Plate your scallops with the herb cream sauce and enjoy this truly sin

2. Coconut-Lime Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup plus 1 tbsp coconut flour
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1/4 cup gently packed Vietnamese or Thai basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup coconut flakes
  • 1-2 green onions, thinly sliced (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix coconut flour, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.

  4. In a third dish, spread the coconut flakes.
  5. Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels, then dredge them first in the flour mixture, shaking off the excess; then dip in the egg, allowing excess to drip off; finally, coat them with coconut flakes, pressing to adhere.
  6. Heat 4 tbsp of butter in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Once melted and hot, add shrimp, leaving space between them, and cook undisturbed until the bottom is golden, about 2 minutes.
  7. Flip the shrimp, add remaining butter to cover the pan, and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven.
  8. Bake for 7-8 minutes until the shrimp are cooked through and the coconut flakes are toasted and lightly browned.
  9. Drizzle lime juice over the shrimp and sprinkle with chopped cilantro and basil. Plate with a bit of lime wedge and sliced green onions, if desired. A scoop of cauliflower rice or a light mixed salad is a great compliment on the side.

3. Crab-Stuffed Avocados

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe Hass avocados
  • 1 lb cooked crab meat (preferably jumbo lump)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp Old Bay Seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 tbsp pomegranate seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Cut avocados in half lengthwise, remove the pits, and spoon out some additional avocado flesh so there’s enough room to hold filling (be careful not to pierce the bottom).
  3. Brush the avocado flesh with a bit of lemon juice to avoid browning.
  4. In a bowl, combine crab meat, green onions, mayonnaise, lemon juice, Old Bay Seasoning, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  5. Stuff each avocado half generously with the crab mixture.
  6. Place the stuffed avocados on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, until the avocado is warmed through.
  7. Top each avocado half with a sprinkle of pomegranate seeds, if desired.
  8. Grab a fork, sit back, and enjoy this out-of-the-ordinary yet luxurious seafood delight!
