Paleo-Friendly Indian Beef Recipes: Spices and Flavors Galore


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine and following a paleo diet, you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’ll explore some delectable Paleo-friendly Indian beef recipes that are bursting with spices and flavors. These recipes will not only satisfy your taste buds but also keep you on track with your dietary goals. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Spicy Beef Curry

Ingredients:

  • 500g grass-fed beef, cut into cubes
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee or coconut oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well to coat the onions evenly with the spices.
  4. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  5. Add the beef cubes and season with salt. Stir well to coat the beef with the spice mixture.
  6. Cover the pan and let the beef cook on low heat for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until it becomes tender.
  7. Once the beef is cooked, garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with cauliflower rice or steamed vegetables.

2. Slow-Cooked Beef Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 500g grass-fed beef, cut into small pieces
  • 2 cups cauliflower rice
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee or coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the onions with the spices.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add the beef pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Transfer the mixture to a slow cooker and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  8. In a separate pot, steam the cauliflower rice until it becomes tender.
  9. Serve the slow-cooked beef with the cauliflower rice and garnish with fresh mint leaves.

3. Spiced Beef Kebabs

Ingredients:

  • 500g grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ground beef, chopped onion, minced garlic, grated ginger, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  2. Take a handful of the beef mixture and shape it into a cylindrical kebab around a soaked wooden skewer. Repeat with the remaining mixture.
  3. Heat a grill or a grill pan over medium heat. Place the kebabs on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are cooked through and browned on all sides.
  4. Serve the spiced beef kebabs hot with a side of mint yogurt dip and fresh salad.

These Paleo-friendly Indian beef recipes are sure to impress your family and friends with their aromatic spices and rich flavors. Give them a try and experience the vibrant taste of Indian cuisine while sticking to your paleo lifestyle. Happy cooking!
