Spicy and Tangy Korean Tteokbokki Recipe


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just looking to spice up your cooking routine, then you’re in for a treat with this Spicy and Tangy Korean Tteokbokki recipe. Tteokbokki, also known as spicy rice cakes, is a popular Korean street food known for its addictive combination of spicy, sweet, and tangy flavors. This dish is not only delicious but also easy to make at home, allowing you to bring the vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine right to your kitchen.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to prepare this mouthwatering dish:

  • 1 pound of cylinder-shaped rice cakes (tteok)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 4 dried anchovies
  • 1 6x6-inch kombu (dried kelp)
  • 1/2 cup of gochujang (Korean red chili pepper paste)
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of rice syrup
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili pepper flakes)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • 4 scallions, chopped
  • 1 cup of cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of Korean fish cakes (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

  1. In a large pot, combine the water, dried shiitake mushrooms, dried anchovies, and kombu.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes to infuse the broth with umami flavor.
  3. After 20 minutes, remove the mushrooms, anchovies, and kombu from the pot and discard them.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

  1. In a bowl, mix together the gochujang, soy sauce, sugar, rice syrup, minced garlic, and gochugaru to create the spicy and tangy sauce.

Step 3: Cook the Tteokbokki

  1. Add the rice cakes to the broth in the pot and bring it to a gentle boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the broth is boiling, add the prepared sauce to the pot and stir well to ensure the rice cakes are coated evenly.
  3. Cook the tteokbokki for about 10-15 minutes, or until the rice cakes are tender and the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency.

Step 4: Add Toppings

  1. Gently add the hard-boiled eggs, chopped scallions, sliced cabbage, and Korean fish cakes (if using) to the pot, and simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat the toppings through.
  2. Drizzle the sesame oil over the tteokbokki and give it a final stir.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Transfer the tteokbokki to a serving dish, garnish with toasted sesame seeds, and serve hot.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, you can omit the dried anchovies and use vegetable broth instead.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less gochugaru according to your preference.
  • Feel free to customize the toppings with your favorite ingredients such as sliced fish cakes, boiled dumplings, or even cheese for a unique twist.

Now that you have the recipe for this Spicy and Tangy Korean Tteokbokki, it’s time to head to the kitchen and whip up this flavorful dish. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or hosting a gathering, this recipe is sure to impress with its bold flavors and comforting appeal. Enjoy the experience of creating a taste of Korea right in your own home!
