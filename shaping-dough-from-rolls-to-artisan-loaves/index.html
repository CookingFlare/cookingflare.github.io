Shaping Dough: From Rolls to Artisan Loaves


Have you ever wondered how professional bakers achieve those perfectly shaped rolls and artisan loaves? The key lies in the art of shaping dough. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques and tips to help you master the art of shaping dough, whether you’re making dinner rolls or rustic artisan bread. So let’s dive right in!

Tools You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s gather the essential tools you’ll need for shaping dough:

  • A clean, lightly floured surface
  • A bench scraper or a sharp knife
  • Your hands (clean and lightly floured)
  • A rolling pin (optional)
  • Parchment paper or a baking sheet

Shaping Rolls

Rolls are a staple in many households, and shaping them can be a fun and rewarding process. Here’s a step-by-step guide to shaping rolls:

  1. Start with a piece of dough, about the size of a golf ball. Using your hands, gently roll it into a smooth ball.

  2. Place the ball of dough on a lightly floured surface and cup your hand over it. Move your hand in a circular motion, applying gentle pressure to create tension on the surface of the dough. This will help create a tight and smooth roll.

  3. Once you’ve achieved the desired tension, transfer the roll to a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing them evenly apart to allow room for rising.

  4. Repeat the process with the remaining dough, ensuring each roll is shaped consistently.

  5. Cover the rolls with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let them rise until doubled in size, usually for about 1 to 2 hours.

  6. Preheat your oven to the recommended temperature while the rolls are rising.

  7. Once the rolls have risen, you can optionally brush them with an egg wash or sprinkle them with seeds or herbs for added flavor and visual appeal.

  8. Bake the rolls in the preheated oven according to your recipe’s instructions, or until they are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Shaping Artisan Loaves

Artisan loaves have a rustic and free-form shape, which adds character to your bread. Here’s a simple method to shape artisan loaves:

  1. After the dough has completed its bulk fermentation, gently transfer it onto a lightly floured surface.

  2. Using a bench scraper or a sharp knife, divide the dough into the desired number of portions, depending on the loaf size you want to achieve.

  3. Take one portion of the dough and gently stretch it out into a rectangular shape. Fold the top third of the dough towards the center, then fold the bottom third over it, like folding a letter.

  4. Rotate the dough 90 degrees and repeat the folding process. This helps create tension on the surface of the dough, resulting in a well-shaped loaf.

  5. Once you’ve folded the dough a few times, use your hands to gently shape it into a round or oval shape, tucking the edges underneath to create a smooth surface.

  6. Transfer the shaped loaf onto a parchment-lined baking sheet or a well-floured proofing basket, seam-side down.

  7. Cover the loaf with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it proof until it has risen and feels puffy to the touch, usually for about 1 to 2 hours.

  8. Preheat your oven to the recommended temperature, placing a baking stone or a cast-iron skillet on the bottom rack to create steam.

  9. Once the loaf has finished proofing, gently transfer it onto a parchment-lined baking sheet (if it wasn’t already on one) or directly onto a preheated baking stone.

  10. Slash the top of the loaf with a sharp knife or a razor blade to allow for expansion during baking and to create a beautiful pattern.

  11. Place the baking sheet with the loaf into the preheated oven and carefully pour a cup of hot water into the preheated cast-iron skillet or a baking dish on the bottom rack to create steam.

  12. Bake the loaf according to your recipe’s instructions, or until it is golden brown and has a crispy crust.

Conclusion

Shaping dough is an essential skill that can elevate your baking to new heights. Whether you’re making rolls for a family gathering or a rustic loaf for a cozy evening at home, these techniques will help you achieve professional-looking results. So next time you’re in the kitchen, don’t forget to put your shaping skills to the test. Happy baking!
