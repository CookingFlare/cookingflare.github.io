Thai Pad Thai with Stir-Fried Noodles and Shrimp Recipe


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’ve probably indulged in the delicious flavors of Pad Thai at some point. This iconic Thai dish is a harmonious blend of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors, combined with tender stir-fried noodles and succulent shrimp. While the dish may seem complex, creating an authentic Pad Thai at home is not as daunting as it may appear. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of crafting a mouthwatering Pad Thai that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Bangkok.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of dried rice noodles
  • 1/2 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of firm tofu, cut into small cubes
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of crushed roasted peanuts
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preparation:

    • Start by soaking the rice noodles in lukewarm water for about 30 minutes until they are pliable but still firm to the touch. Once ready, drain the noodles and set them aside.
    • In a small bowl, combine the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Stir well to dissolve the sugar and set the sauce aside.

  2. Stir-Frying the Shrimp:

    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Add the shrimp and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Once cooked, transfer the shrimp to a plate and set them aside.

  3. Creating the Pad Thai:

    • In the same wok or skillet, add another tablespoon of oil and minced garlic. Sauté the garlic until it becomes fragrant.
    • Add the cubed tofu to the wok and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until it starts to turn golden.
    • Push the tofu to one side of the wok and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are just set.

  4. Bringing It All Together:

    • Add the soaked rice noodles to the wok, followed by the prepared tamarind sauce. Use tongs to toss everything together, ensuring that the noodles are well coated with the sauce.
    • Toss in the stir-fried shrimp, bean sprouts, and half of the sliced green onions. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through.

  5. Serving:

    • Transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates and sprinkle the crushed peanuts and remaining green onions on top.
    • Serve the Pad Thai with lime wedges on the side for a burst of citrusy freshness. Garnish with fresh cilantro for an extra pop of flavor.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Customize Your Pad Thai: Feel free to customize your Pad Thai by adding other protein options such as chicken, beef, or tofu. You can also include additional vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or bok choy for a vibrant touch.

  • Tamarind Alternatives: If you don’t have tamarind paste on hand, you can substitute it with a mixture of equal parts lime juice and brown sugar for a similar tangy flavor.

  • Prep Ahead of Time: To streamline the cooking process, prepare the tamarind sauce and have all your ingredients measured and ready to go before you start stir-frying.

  • Authentic Garnishes: For an authentic touch, consider serving your Pad Thai with a side of banana flower, Chinese chives, and extra lime wedges.

With this recipe, you have the power to recreate the magic of Pad Thai right in your own kitchen. The symphony of flavors and textures in this dish is sure to captivate your senses and leave you yearning for more. So, gather your ingredients, unleash your culinary prowess, and embark on a culinary journey to Thailand with this tantalizing Pad Thai recipe. Enjoy!
