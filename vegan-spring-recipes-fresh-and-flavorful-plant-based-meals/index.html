Vegan Spring Recipes: Fresh and Flavorful Plant-Based Meals


As the weather warms and the days stretch longer, there’s no better time to embrace the vibrant flavors and fresh ingredients that spring has to offer. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to add more plant-based meals to your repertoire, spring provides a wealth of fresh produce that can be turned into delicious, wholesome dishes. From crisp salads to hearty grain bowls, this season is all about eating fresh, light, and flavorful meals that nourish the body and soul.

In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best vegan spring recipes that make the most of seasonal produce. These plant-based meals are packed with nutrients, easy to prepare, and sure to delight your taste buds. Let’s dive into the world of springtime vegan cooking and discover how to create fresh and flavorful dishes that celebrate the season.

1. Spring Vegetable and Quinoa Salad

This light yet filling salad is the perfect way to celebrate spring’s bounty. It combines nutrient-rich quinoa with a variety of fresh vegetables, creating a dish that’s not only packed with fiber but also incredibly satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth (or water)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh peas (or snap peas), blanched
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by cooking the quinoa. In a medium-sized pot, bring the vegetable broth (or water) to a boil. Add the quinoa, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 15 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.

  2. While the quinoa cooks, prepare the vegetables. Slice the cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and radishes. If you’re using snap peas, blanch them in boiling water for about 2 minutes, then cool them under cold water.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, fresh veggies, and parsley.

  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Toss everything gently to combine.

  5. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for an hour to let the flavors meld. This salad can be enjoyed as a main dish or a side dish with other meals.

This quinoa salad is as versatile as it is delicious. Feel free to swap in other spring vegetables like asparagus or artichokes, depending on what’s in season in your area.

2. Creamy Avocado and Pea Pasta

Pasta is a go-to comfort food, but during the spring months, it’s time to lighten it up with some fresh, seasonal ingredients. This creamy avocado and pea pasta is not only easy to make but also full of creamy goodness without any dairy. The avocado adds a rich, silky texture, while the peas give a burst of sweetness and freshness.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz whole wheat or gluten-free pasta
  • 1 ripe avocado, pitted and peeled
  • 1 cup fresh peas (or frozen)
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions, making sure to reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.

  2. While the pasta cooks, prepare the avocado sauce. In a food processor or blender, combine the avocado, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Blend until smooth. If the sauce is too thick, add a little bit of the reserved pasta water to thin it out to your desired consistency.

  3. In a small pan, sauté the fresh peas over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes, just until they’re tender but still vibrant green. If using frozen peas, simply heat them through.

  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the creamy avocado sauce, adding more pasta water if necessary. Stir in the sautéed peas, and season with additional salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, and serve immediately.

This avocado and pea pasta is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or as a light spring lunch. The combination of creamy avocado and fresh peas makes it both indulgent and refreshing.

3. Roasted Carrot and Chickpea Salad

Carrots are a spring vegetable that often gets overlooked, but they’re delicious when roasted to bring out their natural sweetness. Paired with chickpeas and a zesty dressing, this roasted carrot and chickpea salad is a satisfying meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups mixed greens (such as arugula, spinach, or spring mix)
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or agave syrup)
  • Water, as needed to thin the dressing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the carrot sticks and chickpeas with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in an even layer.

  2. Roast for about 25–30 minutes, or until the carrots are tender and slightly caramelized, and the chickpeas are crispy.

  3. While the vegetables roast, prepare the dressing. In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, and a pinch of salt. Add water a tablespoon at a time until the dressing reaches a creamy but pourable consistency.

  4. Once the carrots and chickpeas are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.

  5. In a large bowl, toss the mixed greens with the roasted carrots and chickpeas. Drizzle the tahini dressing over the salad and toss again to coat.

  6. Serve immediately, or store the salad in an airtight container in the fridge for a few hours.

This roasted carrot and chickpea salad is a perfect example of how simple ingredients can be transformed into a vibrant and flavorful dish. The creamy tahini dressing complements the roasted vegetables and adds richness without overpowering the natural flavors.

4. Spring Veggie Tacos with Cilantro-Lime Slaw

Tacos aren’t just for the summer months—spring veggies make for a perfect filling in a taco. These vegan spring veggie tacos are loaded with seasonal vegetables and topped with a zesty cilantro-lime slaw. They’re fresh, flavorful, and make a great meal for Taco Tuesday or any night of the week.

Ingredients:

For the tacos:

  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1/2 cup red onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

For the cilantro-lime slaw:

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage (green or purple)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup (or agave)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the asparagus, bell peppers, and red onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 15–20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.

  2. While the vegetables roast, prepare the slaw. In a medium bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, cilantro, lime juice, olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper. Toss to combine and set aside.

  3. Heat the corn tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 1 minute on each side, until warm and slightly charred.

  4. Once the vegetables are done, remove them from the oven and assemble the tacos. Layer the roasted veggies onto each tortilla, then top with a generous amount of cilantro-lime slaw.

  5. Garnish with extra cilantro and lime wedges, and serve immediately.

These spring veggie tacos are a fun and flavorful way to enjoy the season’s best produce. The combination of roasted veggies and tangy slaw makes each bite a delightful experience.

5. Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble

No spring menu would be complete without a sweet treat, and what better way to end a meal than with a fruit crumble? Strawberry and rhubarb are a classic pairing, and this vegan strawberry rhubarb crumble brings together the tartness of the rhubarb with the sweetness of the strawberries in a warm, comforting dessert.

Ingredients:

For the filling:

  • 3 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 3 cups rhubarb, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the crumble topping:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the strawberries, rhubarb, maple syrup, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Toss to coat the fruit evenly, then transfer to a greased 9-inch baking dish.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, and cinnamon. Stir in the melted coconut oil until the mixture forms a crumbly texture.

  3. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the fruit mixture.

  4. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the fruit is bubbling.

  5. Let the crumble cool slightly before serving. Enjoy it on its own or with a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream.

This strawberry rhubarb crumble is the perfect spring dessert—sweet, tart, and totally satisfying.

Spring is the perfect season to embrace vibrant, fresh ingredients in your cooking. Whether you’re preparing light salads, hearty grain bowls, or indulgent desserts, these vegan spring recipes offer a range of flavors and textures that will make your meals exciting and delicious. So, grab your favorite seasonal produce and start cooking!
