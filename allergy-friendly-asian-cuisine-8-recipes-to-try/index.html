Allergy-Friendly Asian Cuisine: 8 Recipes to Try


Asian cuisine is known for its rich flavors, diverse ingredients, and unique cooking techniques. However, for those with food allergies, some traditional Asian dishes can be a bit tricky to navigate. Many recipes rely heavily on soy, peanuts, sesame, shellfish, or gluten, which can cause reactions for those with sensitivities. Luckily, with a little creativity and ingredient substitution, you can enjoy delicious, allergy-friendly versions of your favorite Asian dishes.

In this blog post, we’ll explore eight tasty and allergy-friendly Asian recipes you can easily make at home. Whether you’re avoiding dairy, gluten, soy, or nuts, these recipes are designed to be safe for people with common allergies without sacrificing flavor.

1. Sweet and Sour Chicken (Gluten-Free and Nut-Free)

Sweet and sour chicken is a classic Asian dish that typically uses a variety of ingredients that may contain gluten or nuts. This allergy-friendly version is both gluten-free and nut-free, making it safe for most people to enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 green bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup tomato ketchup (check for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for thickening)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked white rice (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook until golden brown and cooked through (about 7-8 minutes). Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the onions and bell peppers. Sauté until softened, about 4-5 minutes.
  3. Add the pineapple chunks and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the vinegar, honey, ketchup, and cornstarch. Pour this mixture into the skillet and stir well.
  5. Bring the sauce to a simmer, allowing it to thicken, then return the chicken to the skillet and toss to coat with the sauce.
  6. Serve the sweet and sour chicken over a bed of cooked white rice for a satisfying and allergy-friendly meal.

2. Vegetable Fried Rice (Gluten-Free, Soy-Free, and Nut-Free)

Fried rice is a popular and versatile dish, but traditional recipes often contain soy sauce, which can be problematic for people with soy allergies. This vegetable fried rice is free from soy, gluten, and nuts, making it a perfect allergy-friendly option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably chilled)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/4 cup corn kernels
  • 2 eggs (scrambled)
  • 2 green onions (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos (soy sauce substitute)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the carrots, peas, and corn. Stir-fry until the vegetables are tender (about 5 minutes).
  2. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and crack the eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked.
  3. Add the chilled rice to the skillet and break up any clumps. Stir everything together, mixing the vegetables and eggs with the rice.
  4. Pour in the coconut aminos and rice vinegar, and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the chopped green onions and cook for another minute. Serve hot as a side dish or main course.

3. Chicken Satay with Peanut-Free Sauce (Nut-Free)

Chicken satay is a popular street food in Southeast Asia, typically served with a peanut-based dipping sauce. For those avoiding nuts, this version uses a delicious sunflower seed butter sauce instead, providing a similar nutty flavor without the allergens.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken tenders
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sunflower Seed Butter Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup sunflower seed butter
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • Water (to thin the sauce as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Drizzle the chicken tenders with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes per side or until fully cooked through.
  4. While the chicken cooks, whisk together all the sauce ingredients in a bowl. Add water gradually until the sauce reaches your desired consistency.
  5. Serve the grilled chicken with a generous drizzle of sunflower seed butter sauce.

4. Miso Soup with Tofu (Soy-Free and Gluten-Free)

Miso soup is a staple in many Japanese meals, but it’s typically made with miso paste, which contains soy and sometimes gluten. This version swaps traditional miso with a gluten-free and soy-free miso alternative made from chickpeas.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup chickpea miso (soy-free and gluten-free)
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu (cubed)
  • 1/4 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup green onions (chopped)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed), torn into small pieces

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the vegetable broth to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the chickpea miso and whisk until fully dissolved.
  3. Add the tofu, mushrooms, and green onions. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms are tender.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the torn nori.
  5. Serve the miso soup hot as a light and comforting appetizer or meal.

5. Thai Green Curry (Coconut-Based, Nut-Free, and Soy-Free)

A fragrant and creamy Thai green curry can be made allergy-friendly with a few simple swaps. This version uses coconut milk and a homemade green curry paste, free from nuts and soy, for a rich, flavorful dish that’s perfect with chicken or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast (sliced thinly)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 can coconut milk (full-fat)
  • 2 tablespoons homemade green curry paste (recipe below)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced)
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon coconut sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

For the Green Curry Paste:

  • 2 green chilies (seeds removed for less heat)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemongrass (fresh or powdered)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. To make the green curry paste, blend all the paste ingredients in a food processor until smooth.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for 2 minutes to release the flavors.
  3. Add the chicken slices to the pan and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and stir to combine. Add the red bell pepper, zucchini, and spinach. Simmer for 10-12 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with lime juice, coconut sugar, and salt. Serve over cooked rice for a flavorful meal.

6. Vegetable Spring Rolls (Gluten-Free, Soy-Free, and Nut-Free)

Fresh spring rolls are a popular Vietnamese dish, but the dipping sauces often contain soy and peanuts. This version includes a simple, soy-free dipping sauce and is perfect for a light lunch or appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cucumber (julienned)
  • 1/2 carrot (julienned)
  • 1/4 cup cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup cooked shrimp or chicken (optional)
  • Cooked rice noodles (optional)

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling ingredients (shredded cabbage, cucumber, carrot, cilantro, mint, shrimp or chicken, and rice noodles) in separate bowls.
  2. Dip the rice paper wrappers in warm water for about 10 seconds, then lay them flat on a clean surface.
  3. Place a small amount of each filling ingredient in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the sides over the filling and then roll tightly from the bottom.
  5. Mix the coconut aminos, rice vinegar, honey, and ginger in a bowl for the dipping sauce.
  6. Serve the fresh spring rolls with the dipping sauce on the side.

7. Korean BBQ Beef (Gluten-Free and Soy-Free)

Korean BBQ is a beloved dish, often featuring soy sauce-based marinades. In this allergy-friendly version, a simple yet flavorful marinade without soy or gluten brings out the savory flavors of the beef.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef short ribs (or flank steak)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (optional, for flavor)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, coconut aminos, rice vinegar, honey, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the beef in the mixture for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours).
  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the beef for 5-7 minutes per side or until desired doneness is achieved.
  5. Slice the beef against the grain and serve with steamed rice and vegetables.

8. Japanese Teriyaki Chicken (Gluten-Free and Soy-Free)

Traditional teriyaki chicken often contains soy sauce, which can be a concern for those with soy allergies. This recipe uses coconut aminos as a soy-free substitute, creating a perfectly balanced and sweet marinade for the chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the coconut aminos, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the chicken breasts in the sauce for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill or pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side or until cooked through.
  5. Serve the teriyaki chicken with a side of steamed rice and vegetables.

With these eight allergy-friendly Asian recipes, you can enjoy the vibrant and diverse flavors of Asian cuisine without worrying about common allergens. From sweet and sour chicken to vegetable spring rolls, there’s something for everyone in this collection. Don’t hesitate to experiment with these dishes and modify them to suit your specific dietary needs. Enjoy!
