Amazing Thanksgiving Roasted Chicken Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones, enjoy a hearty meal, and create lasting memories. While turkey often takes center stage at the Thanksgiving table, there’s a rising trend of opting for roasted chicken as a flavorful and succulent alternative. Roasted chicken offers versatility, a rich depth of flavor, and a juicy tenderness that rivals traditional turkey. If you’re considering changing up your Thanksgiving dinner, these roasted chicken recipes will surely become a favorite on your holiday table.

In this post, we’ll explore several amazing roasted chicken recipes for Thanksgiving that are not only simple to prepare but also deliciously satisfying. From traditional herb-infused options to more adventurous flavor profiles, there’s something for every palate.

Why Choose Roasted Chicken for Thanksgiving?

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to explore why roasted chicken makes an excellent choice for Thanksgiving dinner:

1. Less Time-Consuming

Unlike a large turkey, which can take hours to cook, roasted chicken cooks much faster, leaving you with more time to focus on other aspects of your holiday meal preparation. A roasted chicken can be ready in about 1 to 1.5 hours, depending on its size.

2. Easier to Handle

For smaller gatherings, a whole roasted chicken can be more practical than a large turkey. It’s easier to carve, and you won’t have leftovers for days unless you intentionally cook multiple chickens.

3. A Flavorful Alternative

While turkey has a mild flavor, chicken offers a rich, tender taste. Roasting a whole chicken ensures that the meat is juicy and full of flavor, and with the right seasoning, it can be just as satisfying, if not more so, than a turkey.

4. Affordable

Roasted chicken is often more budget-friendly than turkey, especially if you don’t need to feed an enormous crowd. It allows you to serve a flavorful holiday dinner without breaking the bank.

Now that you know why roasted chicken is an ideal choice, let’s explore a few mouthwatering recipes that will take your Thanksgiving dinner to the next level.

Classic Herb Roasted Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 2 teaspoons fresh rosemary (or 1 teaspoon dried rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature will help create a beautifully crisp skin on the chicken.

  2. Prepare the Chicken
    Remove the giblets and pat the chicken dry with paper towels. This step is important to ensure the skin crisps up during roasting.

  3. Season the Chicken
    Rub the chicken inside and out with olive oil and softened butter. Sprinkle the garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and herbs (thyme and rosemary) all over the chicken. Squeeze the juice of one lemon half inside the cavity, and place both lemon halves along with the smashed garlic cloves inside the bird. This will infuse the chicken with a wonderful aroma as it roasts.

  4. Roast the Chicken
    Place the chicken on a rack in a roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan to help keep the chicken moist during roasting. Roast the chicken in the preheated oven for about 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) at the thickest part of the thigh.

  5. Rest the Chicken
    Let the chicken rest for 10-15 minutes before carving to allow the juices to redistribute, ensuring each piece is tender and juicy.

  6. Serve and Enjoy
    Carve the chicken and serve it with your favorite Thanksgiving sides. The combination of herbs, garlic, and lemon makes this roasted chicken a delightful alternative to turkey.

Maple-Glazed Roasted Chicken with Butternut Squash

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups cubed butternut squash
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C).

  2. Prepare the Maple Glaze
    In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, Dijon mustard, olive oil, thyme, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

  3. Prepare the Chicken
    Pat the chicken dry and season it generously with salt and pepper. Place the chicken in a roasting pan.

  4. Glaze the Chicken
    Brush the chicken with the maple glaze, making sure to coat it evenly. You can reserve a small amount of glaze to brush on during the last 15 minutes of roasting for extra flavor.

  5. Roast the Chicken and Butternut Squash
    Arrange the cubed butternut squash around the chicken in the roasting pan. Dot the squash with butter and pour the chicken broth into the pan. Roast in the preheated oven for 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the chicken’s internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C), basting it with the glaze once or twice during cooking.

  6. Rest and Serve
    Allow the chicken to rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve the roasted chicken alongside the sweet and tender roasted butternut squash. The maple glaze adds a beautiful caramelized finish, and the butternut squash pairs perfectly with the savory flavors of the chicken.

Lemon and Garlic Roasted Chicken with Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano (or 1 teaspoon dried oregano)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Season the Chicken
    In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the chicken, making sure to get it into the cavity and under the skin.

  3. Prepare the Vegetables
    Place the halved baby potatoes, sliced carrots, and Brussels sprouts in a large bowl. Drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and toss to coat. Arrange the vegetables around the chicken in a roasting pan.

  4. Roast the Chicken and Vegetables
    Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan. Roast everything in the oven for about 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). During the last 30 minutes, toss the vegetables to ensure they cook evenly.

  5. Rest and Serve
    Once the chicken is cooked through, let it rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve with the roasted vegetables for a wholesome, flavorful meal.

Spicy Herb Roasted Chicken with Cranberry Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup homemade or store-bought cranberry sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C).

  2. Prepare the Chicken
    Pat the chicken dry and rub it all over with olive oil. Sprinkle the smoked paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, salt, and pepper all over the chicken, making sure it’s well-coated.

  3. Roast the Chicken
    Place the chicken in a roasting pan and roast for about 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Baste the chicken with its own juices halfway through to keep the skin moist and flavorful.

  4. Serve with Cranberry Sauce
    Once the chicken is done, let it rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve it with a generous spoonful of cranberry sauce for a touch of sweetness that balances the spicy flavors of the chicken.

Conclusion

Roasted chicken is an excellent choice for Thanksgiving, offering both flavor and flexibility. Whether you prefer classic herb seasoning, a sweet maple glaze, or a tangy lemon and garlic combination, these roasted chicken recipes will make your holiday meal both delicious and memorable. Experiment with these recipes or adapt them to your tastes, and you’ll surely have a Thanksgiving dinner that’s just as festive and flavorful as the traditional turkey. Happy cooking and enjoy your holiday feast!
