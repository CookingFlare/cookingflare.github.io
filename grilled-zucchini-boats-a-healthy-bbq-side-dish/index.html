Grilled Zucchini Boats: A Healthy BBQ Side Dish


Summer barbecues are the perfect time to fire up the grill and feast on delicious, grilled veggies. One tasty addition to your BBQ spread is grilled zucchini boats, a light yet satisfying side dish that’s sure to be a hit at any outdoor gathering. In this post, we’ll walk you through a simple yet flavorful recipe that invites creativity and customization.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional toppings:
    • Fresh herbs (basil, parsley, cilantro)
    • Sliced tomatoes
    • Chopped onions
    • Crumbled feta or goat cheese

Instructions

  1. Begin by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up,prepare the zucchini boats. Cut off the ends of each zucchini and slice them lengthwise to create two long halves. Use a spoon or melon baller to scoop out the flesh, leaving about 1/4-inch of flesh along the sides for structural integrity.

  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine the minced garlic, olive oil, and a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper. This flavorful blend will act as both a marinade and a basting agent for the zucchini boats.

  3. Brush the inside and outside of each zucchini boat with the garlic oil mixture, making sure to generously coat them. This will ensure each bite is infused with delicious flavors.

  4. Once the grill is hot, place the zucchini boats on the grill flesh side down. Cook them for about 5-7 minutes, until the zucchini flesh starts to soften and grill marks appear. You may want to use grill tongs or a spatula to press down on the zucchini boats slightly to ensure even cooking.

  5. Carefully flip the boats, and continue grilling for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the skin is crispy and the interior is tender.

  6. Remove the zucchini boats from the grill and allow them to cool just slightly. At this point, you can opt to sprinkle the Parmesan cheese over the zucchini boats or add your choice of toppings. We love a combination of freshly chopped herbs, sliced tomatoes, and crumbled feta or goat cheese.

  7. Serve hot as a side dish to round out your summer barbecue feast. These versatile zucchini boats can be customized with a variety of herbs, cheeses, or vegetables of your choice.

Variations and Customizations

Grilled zucchini boats are an incredibly versatile side dish that can be adapted to suit your personal tastes and dietary preferences. Why not try adding a little kick by brushing the zucchini with spicy chipotle mayo or a tangy barbecue sauce? Looking for a vegetarian option? Consider topping the zucchini boats with grilled or sautéed seasonal vegetables, such as bell peppers, eggplant, or mushrooms. You can also experiment with different types of cheese or non-dairy alternatives for a heartier dish.

There you have it, grilled zucchini boats – a healthy and delicious side dish perfect for your next barbecue. These crowd-pleasers are easy to prepare, customizable, and sure to become a go-to recipe in your Summer cooking repertoire. Bon appétit!
