Deliciously Nutty: 5 Fall Recipes Featuring Pecans


As the cool breeze of fall sweeps in and the leaves begin to turn shades of amber and gold, it’s time to embrace the flavors and textures of the season. Among the bounty of autumnal ingredients, few are as versatile and delicious as pecans. These nuts, with their rich, buttery flavor and satisfying crunch, make the perfect addition to a variety of dishes, from savory mains to sweet treats.

In this post, we’re diving into five fall recipes that feature pecans in all their glory. Whether you’re looking for a savory side dish, a hearty main course, or a sweet indulgence to satisfy your autumn cravings, these pecan-filled recipes will inspire you to get cooking.

1. Pecan-Crusted Chicken with Maple Mustard Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup pecans, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides. Set them aside while you prepare the crust.
  2. Make the Pecan Crust: In a shallow bowl, combine the chopped pecans, breadcrumbs, garlic powder, and thyme (if using). Mix well. In a separate bowl, lightly beat an egg.
  3. Coat the Chicken: Dip each chicken breast into the egg mixture, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Then, dredge the chicken into the pecan-breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently so that the crust adheres well to the chicken.
  4. Cook the Chicken: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken breasts and sear for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown.
  5. Finish in the Oven: Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
  6. Make the Maple Mustard Sauce: While the chicken is baking, combine the Dijon mustard, maple syrup, and apple cider vinegar in a small saucepan. Cook over medium heat for 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. Serve: Drizzle the maple mustard sauce over the pecan-crusted chicken before serving. Pair this dish with roasted vegetables or mashed sweet potatoes for a complete fall meal.

This recipe combines the sweetness of maple syrup with the nuttiness of pecans, creating a deliciously balanced dish that’s perfect for any fall dinner.

2. Autumn Pecan Salad with Roasted Squash and Cranberries

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or kale)
  • 1 cup roasted butternut squash, cubed
  • 1/2 cup pecans, toasted
  • 1/3 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese or feta
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Squash: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the cubed butternut squash with a bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread it out on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. Toast the Pecans: While the squash is roasting, heat a small skillet over medium heat. Add the pecans and toast for 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fragrant and golden. Set aside to cool.
  3. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  4. Assemble the Salad: In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed greens, roasted squash, toasted pecans, cranberries, and crumbled cheese. Drizzle the dressing over the top and toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve: Serve immediately as a fresh and vibrant side dish or add grilled chicken or roasted turkey for a more substantial meal.

The combination of sweet roasted squash, tangy cranberries, and crunchy pecans makes this salad a perfect reflection of fall’s flavors. It’s also a great way to enjoy some seasonal produce in a light yet satisfying dish.

3. Pecan and Apple Pie Bars

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tbsp ice water

For the filling:

  • 3 medium apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups pecans, chopped
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a food processor, pulse the flour, sugar, and salt together. Add the cold butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add the egg yolk and ice water, then pulse again until the dough comes together.
  2. Bake the Crust: Press the dough into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch baking pan. Use a fork to prick the crust a few times. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Make the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, brown sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss to coat the apples evenly. Stir in the chopped pecans, flour, and beaten egg.
  4. Assemble the Bars: Pour the apple-pecan mixture over the partially baked crust. Spread it evenly, then drizzle the maple syrup over the top.
  5. Bake the Bars: Return the pan to the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and golden. Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares.
  6. Serve: These pecan and apple pie bars are perfect for fall gatherings or as a sweet treat to enjoy with a hot cup of coffee. They offer all the flavors of traditional apple pie, but in a convenient, bite-sized form.

4. Pecan and Sage Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of French or sourdough bread, cubed (about 8 cups)
  • 1 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 tsp dried sage
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bread: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the cubed bread on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, tossing halfway through, until lightly toasted.
  2. Toast the Pecans: In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the pecans for 3-5 minutes, until fragrant. Set aside to cool.
  3. Cook the Vegetables: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and celery and sauté for 5-7 minutes, until softened. Add the garlic, sage, and thyme and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Assemble the Stuffing: In a large bowl, combine the toasted bread cubes, sautéed vegetables, toasted pecans, and parsley (if using). Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake the Stuffing: Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Cover with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the top is golden and crispy.
  6. Serve: This pecan and sage stuffing is the perfect accompaniment to roast chicken or turkey. Its nutty crunch and savory flavors make it a fall classic that will impress your guests.

5. Pecan Praline Cheesecake

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar

For the cheesecake:

  • 3 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

For the praline topping:

  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups pecans, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and brown sugar. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 8-10 minutes, then remove and set aside to cool.
  2. Make the Cheesecake: In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the vanilla extract and eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the sour cream.
  3. Bake the Cheesecake: Pour the cheesecake mixture over the prepared crust and smooth the top. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until the center is set but slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven with the door slightly ajar for 1 hour. Then, refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Make the Praline Topping: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the brown sugar and heavy cream and stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens, about 3-5 minutes. Stir in the chopped pecans and cook for another 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature.
  5. Top the Cheesecake: Once the cheesecake has chilled, pour the praline topping over the top, spreading it evenly. Let the topping set before serving.
  6. Serve: This rich and indulgent pecan praline cheesecake is the perfect way to end any fall meal. The creamy texture of the cheesecake paired with the sweet, nutty praline topping makes it a showstopper dessert.

From savory dishes to indulgent desserts, these five fall recipes featuring pecans showcase the versatility of this beloved nut. Whether you’re preparing a festive dinner, a casual family meal, or a special dessert to share with friends, these recipes are sure to bring warmth and flavor to your table this season. Enjoy the rich, nutty crunch of pecans and embrace all the delicious possibilities that fall has to offer!
