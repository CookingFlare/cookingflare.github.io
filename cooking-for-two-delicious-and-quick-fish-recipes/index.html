Cooking for Two: Delicious and Quick Fish Recipes


When you’re cooking for two, you want recipes that strike the perfect balance between simplicity, flavor, and portion size. Fish, with its versatility, quick cooking time, and health benefits, is an ideal main ingredient for small-scale meals. Whether it’s a romantic dinner for two or just an easy weeknight meal, fish can deliver restaurant-quality dishes without hours of effort. In this post, we’ll explore several quick and delicious fish recipes, perfect for two people, that are sure to elevate your home-cooking game.

Benefits of Cooking with Fish

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to appreciate why fish is such a fantastic choice for cooking.

  • Quick Cooking Times: Most fish fillets cook in under 15 minutes, making them perfect for busy evenings.
  • Nutritional Value: Fish is high in protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and essential vitamins, making it one of the healthiest proteins available.
  • Flavor Versatility: Fish works well with a variety of herbs, spices, and sauces—from bold Asian-inspired flavors to light Mediterranean styles.

With the basics in mind, let’s move on to the recipes!

1. Lemon Herb Pan-Seared Cod

If you’re looking for a simple, fresh, and healthy meal that’s ready in no time, this Lemon Herb Pan-Seared Cod is an excellent choice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cod fillets (about 6 oz each)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of half a lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or ½ tsp dried thyme)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Pat the cod fillets dry with paper towels and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  4. Place the cod fillets in the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes on one side, until golden brown.
  5. Flip the fillets, lower the heat, and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  6. Drizzle the lemon juice over the fish, and sprinkle with lemon zest and thyme.
  7. Serve with your favorite side dish, like roasted vegetables or quinoa.

2. Blackened Salmon with Avocado Salsa

This Blackened Salmon recipe takes less than 20 minutes but feels like a gourmet meal. The smoky, spiced crust pairs beautifully with a creamy avocado salsa, making it perfect for a cozy dinner for two.

Ingredients:

For the salmon:

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the avocado salsa:

  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 2 tbsp red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper in a small bowl to create the blackening spice mix.
  2. Pat the salmon fillets dry and coat both sides evenly with the spice mix.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the salmon fillets and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, until the exterior forms a rich crust and the fish is cooked to your desired doneness.
  4. While the salmon cooks, prepare the avocado salsa by gently mixing all salsa ingredients in a bowl.
  5. Serve the salmon immediately with a generous scoop of avocado salsa on top.

3. Garlic Butter Shrimp Scampi for Two

Okay, so shrimp technically isn’t fish, but it’s close enough and deserves its place in this collection. This Garlic Butter Shrimp Scampi is luxurious, quick, and perfect to serve over pasta or alongside crusty bread to soak up the buttery sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup white wine (or chicken broth)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 6 oz pasta (such as linguine or spaghetti)

Instructions:

  1. If serving with pasta, cook according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Heat butter and oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Remove and set aside.
  4. Deglaze the skillet with white wine (or chicken broth), scraping up any browned bits, and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss with lemon juice and parsley.
  6. Serve the shrimp scampi over pasta or with crusty bread to enjoy the sauce.

4. Baked Tilapia with Garlic Parmesan Crust

For a fuss-free option, this Baked Tilapia with Garlic Parmesan Crust is a winner. The crispy topping adds a delightful texture, while baked fish keeps the dish light and healthy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tilapia fillets
  • 3 tbsp breadcrumbs (panko works best)
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix breadcrumbs, Parmesan, garlic, parsley, olive oil, salt, and pepper to form a crumbly topping.
  3. Arrange the tilapia fillets on the prepared baking sheet. Evenly spread the Parmesan mixture over the top of the fillets.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the tilapia is cooked through and the topping is golden brown.
  5. Serve with a side of steamed asparagus, mashed potatoes, or a fresh green salad.

5. Spicy Coconut Curry Halibut

For something that packs a bit of spice and a whole lot of flavor, this Spicy Coconut Curry Halibut will tick all the boxes. Pair it with rice to soak up the fragrant curry sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 halibut fillets
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Optional: jasmine rice, cooked, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger and sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add the red curry paste and cook for an additional minute until aromatic.
  3. Stir in coconut milk, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Simmer gently for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the halibut fillets, ensuring they’re partially submerged in the sauce. Cover and cook for 8-10 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Finish with a squeeze of fresh lime juice and garnish with cilantro.
  6. Serve with jasmine rice for a complete meal.

Final Thoughts

Cooking for two doesn’t mean you have to skimp on flavor or creativity. These quick and delicious fish recipes are designed to be both impressive and accessible, whether you’re a seasoned chef or just getting started in the kitchen. The next time you’re planning a meal, pick one of these options and enjoy the simplicity of creating a beautiful dish for two.

Experiment with different types of fish, side dishes, and garnishes to make each recipe your own. Cooking for two is about savoring both the process and the meal itself—and with recipes like these, you’re sure to create memorable dining experiences.
