Kid-Approved Veggie Recipes: Sneaky Ways to Add Greens


Getting kids to eat their vegetables can often feel like a daunting task. The bright colors of candy and the allure of fast food can easily overshadow the humble green veggie. However, with a little creativity and some clever cooking techniques, you can sneak nutritious greens into dishes that even the pickiest eaters will love. In this post, we will explore various kid-approved veggie recipes that incorporate greens in delicious, unexpected ways.

1. Spinach and Cheese Stuffed Shells

Ingredients

  • 12 jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Start by boiling a large pot of salted water. Add the jumbo shells and cook until al dente, about 9-11 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, chopped spinach, Parmesan cheese, egg, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Stuff the Shells: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Take each cooked shell and fill it generously with the spinach and cheese mixture. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish.

  4. Add Sauce and Bake: Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the stuffed shells and sprinkle with extra mozzarella cheese if desired. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil if using, and serve warm. Your kids will never know they’re eating spinach!

2. Sneaky Green Smoothie

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1/2 cup frozen mango chunks
  • 1/2 cup spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any milk of choice)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender, combine the banana, frozen mango, spinach, and almond milk. If you want to add sweetness, include honey or maple syrup. For added nutrition, toss in chia seeds.

  2. Blend Until Smooth: Blend on high until the mixture is completely smooth. If the smoothie is too thick, add a little more almond milk until you reach your desired consistency.

  3. Serve: Pour into a fun cup or bowl and enjoy! The vibrant color will appeal to kids, and the taste will keep them coming back for more.

3. Zucchini Muffins

Ingredients

  • 1 cup grated zucchini (about 1 medium zucchini)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (or whole wheat flour)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (or coconut sugar)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the vegetable oil, eggs, sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  3. Add Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.

  4. Fold in Zucchini and Chocolate Chips: Gently fold in the grated zucchini and chocolate chips if using.

  5. Bake: Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Cool and Serve: Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. These muffins are perfect for breakfast or a snack, packed with hidden veggies!

4. Cauliflower Pizza Crust

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, riced (or 2 cups store-bought cauliflower rice)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Rice the Cauliflower: If using a fresh head of cauliflower, remove the stems and leaves, then pulse florets in a food processor until it resembles rice. Steam or microwave the riced cauliflower for 5 minutes and let it cool slightly.

  3. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, combine the riced cauliflower, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix until a dough forms.

  4. Shape the Crust: Transfer the dough to the prepared baking sheet and press it into a round or rectangular shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

  5. Bake: Bake the crust for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.

  6. Add Toppings and Finish Baking: Remove the crust from the oven, add your favorite toppings, and return it to the oven for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Slice and Serve: Cut into slices and serve your kids a delicious, veggie-packed pizza that they will love!

5. Broccoli Cheddar Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup milk (or a non-dairy alternative)
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until translucent. Then add broccoli and carrots, cooking for an additional 5 minutes.

  2. Make the Roux: Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes before slowly whisking in the vegetable broth.

  3. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the broccoli is tender, about 10 minutes.

  4. Blend for Creaminess: Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, blend only half of the soup.

  5. Add Cheese and Milk: Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and milk, allowing it to melt and create a creamy texture. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and serve warm. This comforting dish is perfect for a chilly day and an excellent way to sneak in greens.

6. Kale Chips

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch of kale, stems removed and leaves torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional seasonings: garlic powder, paprika, nutritional yeast

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the Kale: In a large bowl, toss the torn kale leaves with olive oil and salt until evenly coated. For added flavor, sprinkle in your favorite seasonings.

  3. Spread on a Baking Sheet: Lay the kale in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even crisping.

  4. Bake: Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the edges are brown but not burnt. Keep an eye on them, as they can go from perfectly crispy to burnt quickly!

  5. Cool and Serve: Let the kale chips cool for a few minutes before serving. They make a crunchy, healthy snack that kids will love.

Conclusion

Getting kids to eat their greens doesn’t have to be a battle. By incorporating vegetables into familiar dishes in sneaky, creative ways, you can ensure that your little ones are getting the nutrients they need without even realizing it. These kid-approved veggie recipes are not only delicious but also packed with the goodness of greens. So roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and watch your kids enjoy their healthy meals!
