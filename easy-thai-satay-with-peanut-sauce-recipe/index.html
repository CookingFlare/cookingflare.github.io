Easy Thai Satay with Peanut Sauce Recipe


If you’ve ever been to a Thai restaurant, there’s a good chance you’ve fallen in love with the irresistible combination of tender grilled meat paired with a creamy, flavorful peanut sauce. Thai satay is a sensational appetizer or snack that has won the hearts of food lovers globally. This Easy Thai Satay with Peanut Sauce recipe will guide you through making this classic dish right in your kitchen — no exotic equipment or hard-to-find ingredients needed. It’s simple, quick, and bursting with authentic flavor.

What Makes Thai Satay So Special?

At its core, satay is meat marinated and grilled on skewers. Thai satay distinguishes itself through its marinade’s aromatic spices and the luscious peanut dipping sauce that accompanies it. The marinade typically includes coconut milk, turmeric, coriander, garlic, and a touch of sweetness that provides a beautiful depth of flavor.

Originally from Indonesia and Malaysia, satay became widely popular throughout Southeast Asia. Thai satay emphasizes a balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors, amplified by the creamy peanut sauce that has hints of spice and acidity.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Satay Marinade and Skewers

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into thin strips or bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (full fat preferred for richness)
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder (mild or medium heat)
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or fish sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or palm sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning)

For the Peanut Sauce

  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter (natural or regular)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or honey
  • 1 teaspoon red curry paste or chili garlic sauce (optional, for heat)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, finely minced
  • Warm water as needed for consistency

Step-by-Step Preparation

Marinating the Chicken

  1. Prepare your chicken: Begin by slicing your chicken into thin strips about 1-inch wide. You want pieces small enough to cook quickly on skewers but substantial enough to stay juicy.
  2. Mix the marinade: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, curry powder, ground coriander, turmeric powder, soy or fish sauce, minced garlic, brown sugar, and lime juice.
  3. Combine chicken and marinade: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and stir well to coat evenly. Ideally, marinate for at least 1 hour, but up to 4 hours for maximum flavor infusion.
  4. Skewer the chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto bamboo skewers, leaving some space between pieces for even cooking.

Making the Peanut Sauce

  1. Combine peanut sauce ingredients: In a small saucepan or bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, brown sugar, red curry paste, lime juice, and minced garlic.
  2. Heat gently: Place the saucepan over medium-low heat and stir until smooth. If the sauce is too thick, add warm water a tablespoon at a time to loosen it to a creamy yet dippable consistency.
  3. Adjust flavors: Taste and adjust the balance — add more lime juice for tang, sugar for sweetness, or chili for spice if desired.

Cooking the Satay

You have several options depending on your available gear:

  • Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the chicken skewers on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, brushing occasionally with leftover marinade or oil to keep moist. The chicken should be golden and have nice grill marks.

  • Pan-fry: Heat a cast-iron skillet or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add a little oil and cook the skewers for about 3-4 minutes per side, until cooked through and caramelized.

  • Oven broil: Preheat your oven’s broiler. Lay the skewers on a baking sheet lined with foil or parchment. Broil about 4–5 inches from the heat source for 4-5 minutes per side.

No matter which method you choose, do not overcook the chicken or it will become dry and tough. Chicken thighs are usually juicier and more forgiving than breasts.

Serving Suggestions

Thai satay is often served as an appetizer, but it works equally well as a main dish with the right accompaniments:

  • Rice or Noodles: Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice complements the rich flavors. Alternatively, try thin rice noodles tossed with herbs, lime, and a drizzle of the peanut sauce.
  • Fresh Vegetables: Cucumber slices, shredded carrots, and red cabbage provide a refreshing crunchy contrast.
  • Pickled Vegetables: A side of tangy pickled red onions or carrot slaw cuts through the richness beautifully.
  • Herbs: Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro, mint, and sliced green onions for a vibrant finish.
  • Extra Peanut Sauce: Serve a generous bowl of peanut sauce on the side for dipping.

Tips for Perfect Satay Every Time

  • Marinate longer for deeper flavor: While one hour works, leaving chicken marinading overnight in the refrigerator allows the spices to permeate fully.
  • Soak skewers: This simple step prevents burning on the grill while ensuring your satay cooks evenly.
  • Balance salt and sweetness: Taste your marinade and peanut sauce before cooking to ensure the right harmony. A splash of lime juice brightens salted ingredients.
  • Substitute proteins: Pork, beef, shrimp, or tofu all work well for satay. Adjust cooking times accordingly.
  • Peanut allergy alternative: For those avoiding peanuts, try swapping peanut butter for sunflower seed butter or a coconut-based dipping sauce.

Why This Recipe Stands Out

This recipe strikes the perfect balance between authenticity and accessibility. Using simple ingredients available in most supermarkets, you get the layered complexity of Thai spices without hours of prep or specialty shopping. The homemade peanut sauce is creamy and zesty, far superior to bottled versions. Whether for a casual weeknight dinner or an impressive party appetizer, this easy Thai satay recipe hits all the right notes.

FAQs About Thai Satay

Can I make the peanut sauce vegan?
Absolutely! Use soy sauce instead of fish sauce and ensure your peanut butter contains only peanuts. Coconut milk adds richness without animal products.

How long can I keep leftover satay?
Store cooked satay in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently to avoid drying out. Peanut sauce can be refrigerated separately for up to a week.

Can I freeze the satay skewers?
You can freeze marinated chicken skewers (before cooking) in a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before grilling.

What if I don’t have coconut milk?
You can substitute regular milk mixed with a tablespoon of coconut extract or almond milk, though the flavor will be less rich.
