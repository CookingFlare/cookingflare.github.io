Simple Cheese Ravioli with Marinara Sauce


If you’re craving a comforting and flavorful Italian dish, nothing hits the spot quite like homemade cheese ravioli paired with a rich and tangy marinara sauce. This simple yet delicious meal brings together the creamy goodness of cheese-filled ravioli and the classic Italian flavors of tomatoes, herbs, and garlic. Whether you’re making this dish from scratch or using store-bought ravioli, the combination of these two components is always a crowd-pleaser. This recipe is perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner or for impressing guests at a dinner party.

What is Ravioli?

Ravioli is a type of pasta made by filling square or circular pockets of dough with a variety of fillings, ranging from meats to vegetables or cheeses. In this recipe, we focus on the simplicity and richness of cheese-filled ravioli. Traditional fillings for cheese ravioli often include ricotta, mozzarella, and parmesan cheeses, which blend together beautifully to create a creamy and satisfying bite.

The Joy of Homemade Marinara Sauce

While store-bought marinara sauce can be convenient, there’s something special about making your own sauce from scratch. The aroma of simmering tomatoes, garlic, and fresh herbs is a sure way to elevate the entire dish. Homemade marinara sauce also allows you to control the flavors and adjust the seasoning to your liking. Plus, it’s surprisingly easy to make.

In this recipe, we keep the marinara sauce simple and straightforward, using just a few basic ingredients that you probably already have in your pantry. The key is to let the sauce simmer slowly to develop the full depth of flavor.

Ingredients

For the Cheese Ravioli:

  • 1 package of fresh cheese ravioli (or homemade if you prefer)
  • Salt (for boiling water)
  • Olive oil (for drizzling)

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Optional Garnishes:

  • Freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Cheese Ravioli with Marinara Sauce

Step 1: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

The first step in preparing this dish is to make the marinara sauce. While the ravioli is cooking, the sauce will have time to develop its flavors, creating a harmonious balance with the cheese-filled pasta.

  1. Heat the Olive Oil: In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until it becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Seasonings: Stir in the crushed tomatoes and tomato sauce. Add the sugar, oregano, basil, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir everything together to combine.

  3. Simmer the Sauce: Lower the heat and allow the sauce to simmer uncovered for about 20-30 minutes. This slow simmer will help the flavors to meld together and thicken the sauce. Stir occasionally to prevent burning and ensure the sauce doesn’t stick to the bottom of the pan.

  4. Taste and Adjust: Taste the sauce towards the end of cooking and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If the sauce is too acidic, a pinch more sugar can help balance it. If you prefer a more herb-forward flavor, feel free to add more basil or oregano.

  5. Finish with Fresh Basil (Optional): Once the sauce is ready, you can add some fresh basil leaves to the sauce for an extra burst of flavor. Remove the saucepan from the heat and set it aside while you cook the ravioli.

Step 2: Cook the Cheese Ravioli

While your marinara sauce is simmering, it’s time to cook the ravioli. You can either use fresh or frozen ravioli, depending on what’s available to you.

  1. Boil Water: Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil over high heat. Add a generous pinch of salt to the water—this will help season the ravioli while it cooks.

  2. Cook the Ravioli: Once the water is boiling, carefully add the ravioli. Fresh ravioli typically cooks in about 2-4 minutes, while frozen ravioli may take 5-7 minutes. The ravioli is done when it floats to the surface and is tender when pierced with a fork. Make sure not to overcook it.

  3. Drain the Ravioli: Use a slotted spoon to carefully remove the ravioli from the water. If you want to keep the ravioli from sticking together, you can drizzle them with a little olive oil after draining.

Step 3: Assemble the Dish

Now that both the ravioli and marinara sauce are ready, it’s time to put everything together.

  1. Plate the Ravioli: Place a serving of ravioli on each plate, making sure they’re evenly spaced and not stacked on top of each other.

  2. Top with Marinara Sauce: Spoon a generous amount of marinara sauce over the ravioli. You can add more or less sauce depending on your preference.

  3. Garnish and Serve: For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle some freshly grated parmesan cheese over the top of the ravioli. You can also garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and freshness.

  4. Enjoy! Your simple cheese ravioli with marinara sauce is ready to be enjoyed! Pair it with a side of garlic bread or a fresh salad for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfect Cheese Ravioli with Marinara Sauce

1. Choosing the Right Ravioli

While homemade ravioli is always a great option, store-bought fresh ravioli is incredibly convenient and still tastes fantastic. Look for high-quality ravioli, ideally from an Italian market or a local store that offers freshly made varieties. If you’re feeling adventurous, you could also make your own ravioli at home.

2. Use Quality Olive Oil

The olive oil in both the sauce and when cooking the ravioli plays an essential role in enhancing the flavor of the dish. Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil for the best results.

3. Simmer the Sauce Slowly

The key to a flavorful marinara sauce is to let it simmer slowly. The longer it cooks, the more the flavors develop. Don’t rush this step—give the sauce time to meld together and reach its full potential.

4. Experiment with Cheese Fillings

While this recipe calls for traditional ricotta and mozzarella cheese-filled ravioli, feel free to experiment with other cheese combinations. For example, you could use a mix of goat cheese, parmesan, or even fontina for a richer, more complex flavor.

5. Serve with Fresh Herbs and Cheese

Garnishing your ravioli with fresh herbs like basil or parsley adds a burst of freshness that contrasts beautifully with the rich, savory flavors of the marinara sauce. Freshly grated parmesan also enhances the dish’s creaminess and adds depth to the flavor.

6. Pair with a Delicious Drink

To complete your Italian meal, serve the ravioli with a glass of red wine, such as a Chianti or Sangiovese. If you prefer something non-alcoholic, try a sparkling water with a slice of lemon or a refreshing iced tea.

Why You’ll Love This Simple Cheese Ravioli with Marinara Sauce

This recipe is the epitome of simple Italian comfort food. It’s easy to make, even on a busy weeknight, but it still feels like a special treat. The cheese ravioli is soft and creamy, while the marinara sauce adds the perfect amount of tanginess and depth of flavor. Whether you’re serving it for a family dinner or a dinner party, this dish will quickly become a favorite.

What makes this dish truly stand out is how the flavors of the ravioli and the marinara sauce complement each other perfectly. The richness of the cheese-filled ravioli works harmoniously with the bright, herb-infused tomato sauce, creating a meal that’s both satisfying and comforting.

If you’re looking for a dish that combines simplicity with flavor, this cheese ravioli with marinara sauce is a winner. Plus, it’s versatile! You can adjust the sauce to suit your taste, experiment with different ravioli fillings, or serve it with a variety of sides.

Final Thoughts

Making a simple cheese ravioli with marinara sauce is a rewarding experience that brings together the best of Italian cuisine. The key to success lies in using fresh ingredients, taking your time with the marinara sauce, and making the ravioli a star of the dish. Whether you opt for store-bought ravioli or decide to make it from scratch, the result will be a meal that’s rich, satisfying, and full of flavor.

Give this recipe a try, and you’ll soon find yourself making it again and again. It’s perfect for any occasion, and best of all, it’s incredibly easy to whip up with minimal effort. Buon appetito!
