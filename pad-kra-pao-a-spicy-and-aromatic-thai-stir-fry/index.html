Pad Kra Pao: A Spicy and Aromatic Thai Stir-Fry


If you’re looking to spice up your dinner routine, look no further than Pad Kra Pao. This classic Thai stir-fry dish is bursting with bold flavors and aromatic spices that will transport your taste buds straight to the streets of Bangkok. With its perfect balance of heat, sweetness, and herbal freshness, Pad Kra Pao is a go-to meal for Thai food lovers around the world.

Ingredients

To recreate the authentic flavors of Pad Kra Pao, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 400 grams of protein (chicken, beef, pork, or tofu)
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 5 Thai bird’s eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice tolerance)
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 cup of holy basil leaves (Thai basil)
  • Steamed jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing your protein of choice. If using chicken, beef, or pork, slice it into thin strips. If using tofu, cut it into bite-sized cubes. Set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the minced garlic and chopped Thai bird’s eye chilies. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Add the protein to the wok and continue to stir-fry until it is cooked through and slightly browned. This should take about 5-7 minutes, depending on the type of protein you’re using.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar until well combined. Pour this sauce mixture over the cooked protein in the wok, stirring to coat evenly.

  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the holy basil leaves to the wok. Stir-fry for another minute or two until the basil leaves have wilted slightly and released their aroma.

  6. Remove the wok from the heat and serve the Pad Kra Pao immediately with steamed jasmine rice. The combination of the fragrant stir-fry and fluffy rice is simply irresistible.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, simply replace the meat with firm tofu or your favorite assortment of vegetables.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of Thai bird’s eye chilies according to your spice tolerance. If you prefer a milder dish, remove the seeds from the chilies or reduce the amount used.
  • If holy basil is not available, you can substitute it with Thai basil or even regular sweet basil. While it won’t be exactly the same, it will still add a lovely herbaceous flavor to the dish.

Final Thoughts

Pad Kra Pao is a quick and easy stir-fry that packs a punch in terms of flavor. The combination of garlic, chilies, and holy basil creates a harmonious balance of spicy, savory, and aromatic elements. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or looking to experiment with new flavors, this dish will not disappoint.

So why not bring a taste of Thailand to your kitchen tonight? Give Pad Kra Pao a try and let the vibrant flavors transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok. Enjoy!
