Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Pie: A Classic Combination


There’s something truly special about the combination of sweet strawberries and tangy rhubarb. The contrast of flavors creates a symphony of taste that is sure to delight your taste buds. And what better way to showcase this classic combination than in a homemade strawberry rhubarb pie? In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating a pie that is bursting with flavor and made with love.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ¼ - ½ cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 3 cups rhubarb, diced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the crust. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add in the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, starting with ¼ cup, and mix until the dough comes together. If necessary, add more water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough is moist enough to hold together when pressed between your fingers.

  3. Divide the dough into two equal parts and shape each into a disk. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat as well. This will help ensure a crisp bottom crust.

  5. In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries, diced rhubarb, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Toss gently until the fruit is evenly coated.

  6. Remove one of the chilled dough disks from the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish and press it gently into the bottom and sides.

  7. Pour the strawberry rhubarb filling into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly.

  8. Roll out the remaining dough disk and cut it into strips to create a lattice pattern on top of the filling. Alternatively, you can cover the filling with the entire rolled-out dough and cut a few slits to allow steam to escape.

  9. Trim any excess dough, then fold the edges of the crust under and crimp them to create a decorative edge.

  10. Place the pie on the preheated baking sheet in the oven and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  11. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool completely on a wire rack before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This homemade strawberry rhubarb pie is delicious on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The creamy sweetness of the ice cream or the lightness of the whipped cream pairs perfectly with the vibrant flavors of the pie.

Conclusion

Homemade strawberry rhubarb pie is a true classic that never goes out of style. With its sweet and tangy filling encased in a buttery, flaky crust, it’s a dessert that will have your family and friends coming back for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s create a pie that will make your taste buds dance with joy. Happy baking!
