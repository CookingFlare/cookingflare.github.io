Vanishing Tide: Seafood Dishes Before Seafood Becomes a Fading Memory


Tides were once a symbol of life and abundance along the shorelines. But now as waters recede and ecosystems deteriorate, the delicious bounty of the sea is vanishing before our very eyes. It’s time to celebrate and cherish these forgotten flavors by cooking up some sumptuous seafood dishes. From fresh fish tacos to sumptuous oyster gratin, here are eleven mouthwatering recipes to enjoy seafood before it becomes a memory of a bygone era.

  1. The Classic: Steamed Clams and Mussels with Garlic and Herbs

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs clams and mussels, rinsed and scrubbed
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 cloves minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh herbs (like parsley, dill or cilantro) for garnish

Instructions

Begin by pouring a generous glug of olive oil into a large pot over medium heat. Cook the garlic for a couple of minutes until fragrant, then add the white wine and let it bubble away for a few minutes. Add the clams and mussels to the pot, cover with a lid, and let them steam until they are all opened completely, around 7-10 minutes, depending on the size of your shellfish. Discard any that don’t open. Add the butter and stir until well combined. Season with salt and pepper and serve immediately with crusty bread and a sprinkle of fresh herbs and a lemon wedge on the side.

  1. Celebrating a Sustainable Catch: Grilled Local Salmon with Mango Avocado Salsa

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (approximately 6 oz each)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 mango, diced
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

Combine the paprika, salt, and pepper in a small bowl, then brush the salmon fillets with olive oil. Sprinkle the seasoning mix generously over the fillets, ensuring both sides are well-coated. Heat your grill to medium-high heat and cook the salmon fillets for approximately 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F. In a separate bowl, combine the mango, avocado, diced red onion, lime juice, and cilantro. Mix gently to combine, and adjust seasoning if desired. Drizzle with olive oil and serve alongside the grilled salmon for a delightful fresh touch.

  1. An Oceanic Twist on Italian Cuisine: Linguine with Seared Scallops and Chorizo

Ingredients

  • 1 lb linguine
  • 1/2 lb chorizo, sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 pint of seared scallops
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup fresh Italian parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Instructions

Cook the linguine according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain and set aside. In a large pan, brown the chorizo slices over medium heat, then remove from the pan with a slotted spoon and set aside. Add the olive oil, garlic, and cherry tomatoes to the pan, and cook until the tomatoes start to soften. Deglaze the pan with a splash of white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Return the chorizo to the pan along with the scallops, cooked pasta, and reserved pasta water. Toss everything together over low heat, allowing the flavors to meld for a few minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of fresh parsley before serving with a generous grating of Parmesan cheese.

  1. A Bounty from the Sea: Adéquines of Hake with a Saffron Potato Coulis

Ingredients

  • 4 hake fillets (about 6 oz each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 8 oz potatoes, peeled and cut into small chunks
  • 1 cup fish stock
  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions

Preheat your oven to 400°F. Season both sides of the hake fillets with salt and pepper. Heat a large oven-safe pan over medium-high heat, then add the olive oil. Once the oil is shimmering, place the hake fillets into the pan, and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden-brown. Transfer the pan to the preheated oven, and bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. While the fish is in the oven, prepare the potato coulis. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, then drain and return them to the pan over low heat. Mash the potatoes with a fork or potato masher, then stir in the fish stock, saffron threads, and heavy cream. Allow the coulis to simmer for a few minutes, then adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve the hake fillets alongside the saffron potato coulis, and garnish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley.

  1. A Blast from the Past: Delicately Poached Lobster with Vanilla Beurre Blanc

Ingredients

  • 2 lobsters (1 1/2 lbs each), halved
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 vanilla beans, scraped of their seeds

Instructions

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Reduce heat to a gentle simmer, and carefully add the lobster halves, ensuring they are submerged. Add the bay leaves, cinnamon stick, star anise, and wine. Poach the lobster for 8-10 minutes, or until the tails turn a bright red color. Remove the lobsters from the water and set aside. Meanwhile, make a vanilla beurre blanc by combining the white wine, heavy cream, and vanilla seeds in a small saucepan. Cook until the mixture has reduced by half. Reduce heat to low, then gradually whisk in the unsalted butter, 1 tbsp at a time, until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the halved lobsters on plates with a drizzle of the luxurious vanilla beurre blanc.

  1. Bold and Spicy: Spicy BBQ Shrimp with Braised Greens

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 cups braised greens (like kale, collard greens, or Swiss chard)

Instructions

Whisk together the BBQ sauce, honey, and chili powder in a bowl until fully combined. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Cook the sliced onion for a few minutes, or until softened, then deglaze the pan with the white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Stir in the BBQ sauce mixture and bring to a simmer. Add the shrimp, and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until they’ve just turned pink. Stir in the chicken broth, then the braised greens, ensuring the greens are evenly coated in sauce. Cook until the greens are tender, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately, spooning plenty of sauce and shrimp over a bed of rice or couscous.

  1. The Seafood Paella: A Medley of Flavors and Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 1 cup bomba rice
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup mussels
  • 3 sprigs saffron
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, shelled and deveined
  • 1/2 lb chorizo, diced
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions

Start by heating your paella pan or cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add the olive oil, then cook the diced chorizo for a few minutes, until it begins to release its oil. Add the chopped onion, and cook until softened, then stir in the minced garlic and diced tomato. Cook for a few minutes, then stir in the rice, ensuring it’s well-coated in the fat and juices from the pan. Pour in the white wine, stirring until absorbed, then add the saffron and broth. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce heat to low and let it simmer. After 15 minutes, arrange the mussels and shrimp on top of the rice, and cook for another 5 minutes without stirring. By now, the liquid should have mostly absorbed, and the rice should be tender, with a slightly crunchy top layer (known as “socarrat”). If your rice isn’t quite ready, feel free to keep cooking it uncovered, stirring occasionally. Remove the pan from heat, and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before serving. Garnish with a sprinkle of freshly chopped parsley.

  1. A Taste of Luxury: Lobster Thermidor

Ingredients

  • 2 lobsters (1 1/2 lbs each)
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp shallots, minced
  • 1/4 cup brandy
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped chives, for garnish

Instructions

Split the lobsters in half lengthwise, and remove the meat from the tails and claws. Finely chop the tomalley and roe, then set aside with the lobster meat. Cook the butter, shallots, and brandy in a saucepan, until the liquid has reduced by half. Stir in the heavy cream, and let it reduce until thickened. Whisk in the Dijon mustard, and season with salt and pepper. Fold in the grated Parmesan cheese, chopped tomalley, and roe, then gently mix in the lobster meat. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Divide the lobster mixture between lobster halves, then sprinkle with fresh breadcrumbs. Cook in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the topping is golden and crisp. Garnish with chopped chives, and serve immediately.

  1. The Pacific Kiss: Seared Scallops with Ginger-Miso Glaze

Ingredients

  • 1 lb sea scallops
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed and blanched
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • Chopped scallions, for garnish

Instructions

Pat the scallops dry with a paper towel, then season both sides with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat, then sear the scallops for 1-2 minutes on each side, until golden-brown. Remove the scallops from the pan and set aside. Using the same pan, add the butter and cook the ginger until fragrant, then whisk in the white miso paste and lime juice. Let the sauce reduce for a few minutes, then stir in the sugar snap peas and return the scallops to the pan. Drizzle the sauce over the scallops, and cook for another minute to heat everything through. Remove from the heat, and let the scallops rest for a minute before plating. Drizzle with a bit of toasted sesame oil, garnish with chopped scallions, and serve immediately.

  1. A tender Braise: Halibut with White Wine Shallot Sauce

Ingredients

  • 4 halibut fillets (6-8 oz each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 cup fish or chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup shallots, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Chopped chives, for garnish

Instructions

Season the halibut fillets with salt and pepper, then heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Sear the halibut for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden-brown. Remove the fish from the pan and set aside. Deglaze the pan with a splash of white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Add the fish stock, and let it reduce by half. Whisk in the butter, shallots, and heavy cream, allowing the sauce to thicken until it coats the back of a spoon. Return the halibut to the pan, spooning the sauce over the fillets. Cook until the fish is cooked through, around 3-5 minutes, depending on thickness. Garnish with chopped chives, and serve immediately alongside your choice of sides.

  1. Ocean Delicacy: Smoked Trout and Cucumber Salad

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked smoked trout, flaked
  • 2 cups sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup capers, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

Combine the flaked trout, sliced cucumber, red onion, capers, and fresh dill in a large bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, then toss everything together gently. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld, then serve with crusty bread or over a bed of mixed greens.

Concluding Thoughts

Seafood dishes have long held a valued and iconic place in culinary traditions around the world. Although the future of our oceans’ bounty remains uncertain, these recipes offer a delectable nod to the splendor and abundance that once was, giving us one last chance to savor and cherish these irresistible delicacies. Whether you’re dining alone or hosting a gathering of friends, these sumptuous dishes are a celebration and a last hurrah, an unforgettable and sought-after treat that’s harder to come by now more than ever. So go on, treat yourself and your loved ones to these mouthwatering delights while they’re still available, and make memories (and dishes) that are nothing short of unforgettable.
