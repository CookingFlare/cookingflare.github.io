Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken: A Fragrant Thai Dish


If you’re looking to elevate your culinary repertoire with a dish that captivates the senses, look no further than Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken. This fragrant Thai dish combines the zesty notes of lemongrass with the rich flavors of marinated chicken, creating a delightful meal that is both comforting and refreshing. Whether you’re an experienced home chef or a culinary novice, this recipe will guide you through the steps to create a dish that transports you straight to the vibrant streets of Thailand.

The Origins of Lemongrass in Thai Cuisine

Lemongrass, known as “taan phak” in Thai, is a key ingredient in many Thai dishes. Its citrusy flavor and aromatic qualities make it a staple in Thai cooking. The use of lemongrass dates back centuries, with its roots intertwined in Southeast Asian culinary traditions. It is often used in soups, curries, and marinades, providing a fresh and tangy element that enhances the overall flavor profile of the dish.

In this recipe, we’ll harness the unique qualities of lemongrass to create a marinade that infuses the chicken with a depth of flavor, making it the star of the meal.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make this Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken, gather the following ingredients:

For the Marinade

  • 4 stalks of fresh lemongrass, trimmed and finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 1-2 Thai bird’s eye chilies, sliced (adjust for spice preference)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

For the Chicken

  • 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

For the Side

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 4 cups water
  • Optional: steamed vegetables like broccoli or bok choy

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the chopped lemongrass, minced garlic, shallots, and sliced chilies. Add the fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, vegetable oil, and black pepper. Mix well until the sugar is dissolved and all ingredients are integrated.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken thighs in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or for best results, overnight. This step allows the flavors to penetrate the chicken, making it tender and flavorful.

Step 2: Cook the Rice

  1. Rinse the Rice: Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch and keeps the rice fluffy.

  2. Cook the Rice: In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice and water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the water is absorbed. Remove from heat and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork before serving.

Step 3: Grill or Sauté the Chicken

  1. Preheat Your Grill or Pan: If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. If using a skillet, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked with a nice char. If sautéing, cook the chicken in the skillet for about 6-8 minutes on each side, depending on thickness, until golden brown and cooked through.

  3. Rest the Chicken: Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill or skillet and let it rest for a few minutes. This helps retain the juices.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Slice the Chicken: Once rested, slice the chicken into strips or serve whole on a platter.

  2. Garnish: Sprinkle fresh cilantro leaves on top for a burst of color and flavor. Serve with lime wedges for an extra zesty kick.

  3. Pair with Rice and Vegetables: Serve the Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken alongside fluffy jasmine rice and steamed vegetables for a complete meal. The rice acts as a perfect base to soak up the delicious juices from the chicken.

Tips for the Perfect Lemongrass Chicken

  • Choosing Lemongrass: Select fresh, firm lemongrass stalks that are free from blemishes. The outer layers can be tough, so use the tender inner part for maximum flavor.

  • Adjusting Spice Level: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the number of bird’s eye chilies or omit them altogether. You can also substitute with a milder chili variety.

  • Marination Time: For the best flavor, try to marinate the chicken overnight. This allows the flavors to deeply penetrate the meat.

  • Cooking Method: While grilling adds a wonderful smoky flavor, baking or pan-searing the chicken is also effective. Ensure the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C) for safe consumption.

Health Benefits of Lemongrass

In addition to its delightful flavor, lemongrass is known for its numerous health benefits. It is rich in antioxidants, which help combat oxidative stress in the body. The herb is also believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and can aid digestion. Incorporating lemongrass into your meals not only enhances the taste but may also contribute to your overall well-being.

Conclusion

Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken is more than just a meal; it’s an experience that brings the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine into your home. With its fragrant aroma and zesty taste, this dish is sure to impress family and friends alike. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet night in, the combination of tender chicken, aromatic lemongrass, and zesty lime will transport your taste buds to Thailand.

So, roll up your sleeves and dive into this culinary adventure. Create a plate of Savory Thai Lemongrass Chicken and savor the journey of flavors and aromas that this dish has to offer. Happy cooking!
