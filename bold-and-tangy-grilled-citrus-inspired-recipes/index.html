Bold and Tangy: Grilled Citrus-Inspired Recipes


If you’re looking to add a zesty twist to your grilling game, look no further than these bold and tangy grilled citrus-inspired recipes. Citrus fruits have always been known for their refreshing flavors and vibrant colors, and when combined with the smoky char of the grill, they create a match made in culinary heaven. From appetizers to desserts, these recipes will take your taste buds on a tantalizing journey of bold and tangy flavors.

Grilled Citrus Shrimp Skewers

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 10 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice and zest of 1 lime
  • Juice and zest of 1 orange
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, lime juice and zest, orange juice and zest, minced garlic, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the shrimp to the marinade and toss well to coat. Let marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  4. Grill the shrimp skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are pink and opaque.
  5. Serve the grilled shrimp skewers with a squeeze of fresh citrus juice and garnish with extra zest.

Grilled Citrus Chicken Salad

Preparation time: 20 minutes Cooking time: 15 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice and zest of 1 lime
  • Juice and zest of 1 orange
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup sliced red onion
  • Citrus vinaigrette dressing (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, lime juice and zest, orange juice and zest, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the chicken breasts with the citrus marinade and let them marinate for 10 minutes.
  4. Grill the chicken breasts for 6-8 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through.
  5. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes, then slice it into thin strips.
  6. In a large bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, cherry tomatoes, avocado, and red onion. Toss with the citrus vinaigrette dressing, if desired.
  7. Top the salad with the grilled citrus chicken slices and serve.

Grilled Citrus Pound Cake

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 10 minutes Serves: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pound cake
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Citrus syrup:
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • Juice of 1 lime
    • Juice of 1 orange
    • 2 tablespoons honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Slice the pound cake into thick slices.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter and citrus zest.
  3. Brush both sides of each pound cake slice with the citrus butter mixture.
  4. Place the pound cake slices on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are lightly toasted.
  5. While the pound cake is grilling, prepare the citrus syrup by combining the lemon juice, lime juice, orange juice, and honey in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat until the honey is dissolved.
  6. Remove the pound cake slices from the grill and drizzle them with the citrus syrup.
  7. Serve the grilled citrus pound cake slices with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

These bold and tangy grilled citrus-inspired recipes are sure to impress your family and friends at your next barbecue. The combination of grilled citrus flavors with the smoky char of the grill creates a unique and delicious dining experience. So fire up the grill, grab some citrus fruits, and get ready to take your taste buds on a flavorful adventure. Enjoy!
