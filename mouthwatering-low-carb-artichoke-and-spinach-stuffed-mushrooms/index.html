Mouthwatering Low Carb Artichoke and Spinach Stuffed Mushrooms


If you’re on the lookout for a delicious appetizer that fits perfectly into a low-carb lifestyle, look no further than these mouthwatering artichoke and spinach stuffed mushrooms. Bursting with flavor and loaded with nutrients, these stuffed mushrooms are not only a treat for your taste buds but also a great way to impress guests at your next gathering. Let’s dive into this delightful recipe that combines the earthiness of mushrooms with the creamy goodness of artichokes and spinach.

Why Choose Low Carb?

Low-carb diets have gained popularity for their potential benefits in weight management, blood sugar control, and overall health. By reducing your carbohydrate intake, you can encourage your body to burn fat for fuel instead of relying on sugars. This artichoke and spinach stuffed mushroom recipe is a perfect low-carb option, making it suitable for anyone looking to enjoy a guilt-free indulgence without sacrificing flavor.

Ingredients

To make these stuffed mushrooms, gather the following ingredients:

  • Mushrooms: 16 large portobello or cremini mushrooms, cleaned and stems removed
  • Cream Cheese: 8 oz, softened
  • Sour Cream: 1/2 cup
  • Artichoke Hearts: 1 can (14 oz), drained and chopped
  • Fresh Spinach: 2 cups, chopped (or 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • Parmesan Cheese: 1/2 cup, grated
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Onion: 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste
  • Red Pepper Flakes: Optional, for a kick
  • Fresh Parsley: For garnish

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Before you begin preparing the stuffing, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This will ensure your mushrooms bake evenly and develop a delightful golden color.

Step 2: Prepare the Mushrooms

  1. Clean the Mushrooms: Gently wipe the mushrooms with a damp cloth to remove any dirt. Avoid soaking them in water, as mushrooms can absorb moisture and become soggy.
  2. Remove the Stems: Carefully twist and pull out the stems of the mushrooms. Finely chop the stems and set them aside for the stuffing.

Step 3: Make the Stuffing

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until they are translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add Spinach and Artichokes: Stir in the chopped spinach and artichoke hearts, cooking until the spinach wilts (if using fresh) or is heated through (if using frozen). This should take about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Mix in Cream Cheese: Lower the heat and add the softened cream cheese and sour cream to the skillet. Stir until everything is well combined and creamy.
  4. Season the Mixture: Add the lemon juice, Parmesan cheese, and season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if desired. Mix until the filling is smooth and creamy, then remove from heat.

Step 4: Stuff the Mushrooms

  1. Fill the Caps: Using a spoon or a small cookie scoop, fill each mushroom cap generously with the artichoke and spinach mixture. Press down slightly to ensure the filling holds.
  2. Arrange on a Baking Sheet: Place the stuffed mushrooms on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper for easy cleanup.

Step 5: Bake the Mushrooms

Bake the stuffed mushrooms in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the mushrooms are tender and the tops are golden brown. The aroma that fills your kitchen will be irresistible!

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Once the mushrooms are baked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness. Serve warm, and watch your guests devour these delicious bites!

Nutritional Benefits

This low-carb artichoke and spinach stuffed mushroom recipe is not only tasty but also packed with nutritional benefits:

  • Mushrooms: Low in calories and rich in vitamins D and B, mushrooms also provide antioxidants and support immune health.
  • Spinach: A powerhouse of nutrients, spinach is high in vitamins A, C, K, iron, and calcium, contributing to overall health and wellness.
  • Artichokes: Known for their fiber content, artichokes support digestive health and are rich in antioxidants, helping to combat free radicals.
  • Cream Cheese: While creamy and indulgent, cream cheese adds a rich texture without the carbs found in traditional stuffing ingredients.

Tips for Success

  • Choose the Right Mushrooms: Opt for larger mushrooms like portobello or cremini for a hearty bite. Smaller varieties like button mushrooms can also work but may require more filling.
  • Experiment with Cheese: Feel free to mix and match different cheeses in the stuffing. Goat cheese or feta can add a delightful tanginess.
  • Make Ahead: Prepare the stuffing in advance and store it in the fridge. When you’re ready to bake, simply stuff the mushrooms and pop them in the oven.
  • Variations: Add cooked bacon, diced bell peppers, or different herbs to customize the flavor to your liking.

Conclusion

These mouthwatering low-carb artichoke and spinach stuffed mushrooms are a delicious and nutritious option for any gathering or a cozy night in. They provide a satisfying bite that’s perfect for those adhering to a low-carb diet, without sacrificing taste. From their creamy filling to the hearty mushroom caps, every bite is sure to impress. Whether you’re serving them as an appetizer or pairing them with a salad for a light meal, these stuffed mushrooms are bound to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.

So, gather your ingredients, invite some friends, and get ready to indulge in a low-carb delight that’s bursting with flavor. Enjoy the culinary journey!
