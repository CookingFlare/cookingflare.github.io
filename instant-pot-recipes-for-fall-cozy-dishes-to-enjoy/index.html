Instant Pot Recipes for Fall: Cozy Dishes to Enjoy


As the crisp autumn air sweeps in and the leaves begin to change, the desire for warm, comforting meals becomes irresistible. Fall is the perfect season to embrace the cozy vibes of homemade dishes, and there’s no better way to prepare them than with the Instant Pot. With its ability to drastically reduce cooking times without compromising on flavor, the Instant Pot is an essential tool in the fall kitchen.

In this post, we will explore a variety of Instant Pot recipes that will make your fall evenings warm, delightful, and full of comforting flavors. From hearty soups to rich stews, creamy desserts, and savory main dishes, these recipes are designed to satisfy your autumn cravings and make the most of the season’s bounty.

1. Instant Pot Butternut Squash Soup

There’s something about the creamy texture and subtle sweetness of butternut squash that makes it the perfect fall ingredient. When paired with aromatic spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and garlic, it transforms into a bowl of pure comfort.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional, for a creamier texture)

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Once it’s hot, add the olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until soft and fragrant (about 3-4 minutes).
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, vegetable broth, cumin, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Seal the Instant Pot and set it to cook on high pressure for 8 minutes.
  4. Once done, perform a quick release and carefully open the lid. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. (Alternatively, transfer the soup to a blender and blend in batches.)
  5. For a richer flavor, stir in the heavy cream. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with a sprinkle of cinnamon or roasted pumpkin seeds for added texture.

This velvety soup is the ultimate autumn dish – full of warmth and flavor with minimal effort.

2. Instant Pot Beef Stew

Beef stew is a fall classic, and making it in the Instant Pot is a game-changer. The pressure cooker locks in all the hearty flavors and tenderizes the beef to perfection, creating a dish that’s rich, flavorful, and ideal for cooler days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups carrots, sliced
  • 2 cups potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons flour (optional, for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add the olive oil and brown the beef stew meat in batches, ensuring the beef gets a nice sear. Remove and set the beef aside.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until soft.
  3. Add the beef back into the pot along with beef broth, carrots, potatoes, celery, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Seal the Instant Pot and cook on high pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. Once done, perform a quick release and open the lid. If you’d like to thicken the stew, mix 2 tablespoons of flour with a bit of water to form a slurry, then stir it into the stew. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and cook for an additional 5 minutes until thickened.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary before serving.

This beef stew is perfect for a chilly fall night. Serve with crusty bread for the ultimate cozy meal.

3. Instant Pot Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal

Fall is apple season, and there’s no better way to start a crisp autumn morning than with a warm bowl of apple cinnamon oatmeal. The Instant Pot makes this breakfast dish easy, quick, and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup steel-cut oats
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup milk (or non-dairy alternative)
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup or honey (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add the steel-cut oats, diced apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, water, milk, and salt to the Instant Pot. Stir to combine.
  2. Seal the Instant Pot and set it to cook on high pressure for 4 minutes.
  3. Once done, allow for a natural pressure release for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Stir the oatmeal and add maple syrup or honey if desired. If you prefer a creamier texture, you can stir in extra milk.
  5. Serve hot, topped with additional diced apples, a sprinkle of cinnamon, and a drizzle of maple syrup.

This oatmeal is the perfect autumn breakfast to keep you energized and cozy throughout the day.

4. Instant Pot Pumpkin Risotto

Pumpkin is synonymous with fall, and when combined with creamy risotto, it makes for a luxurious yet comforting meal. The Instant Pot ensures the risotto cooks to creamy perfection in less time than traditional methods.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (canned or fresh)
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add the olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until soft and fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the Arborio rice to the pot and toast it for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Pour in the white wine (if using) and cook for 1 minute, allowing it to evaporate.
  4. Stir in the pumpkin puree, broth, sage, salt, and pepper. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 6 minutes.
  5. Once done, allow for a natural release of pressure for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  6. Open the lid and stir in the Parmesan cheese and butter. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with extra Parmesan and a sprinkle of fresh sage.

This creamy pumpkin risotto is the perfect side dish for any fall meal, or it can stand alone as a delicious main course.

5. Instant Pot Cranberry Sauce

No fall feast is complete without a homemade cranberry sauce, and making it in the Instant Pot is quick and easy. This tangy and slightly sweet dish is a great accompaniment to turkey, chicken, or even just served on toast.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add the cranberries, orange juice, sugar, cinnamon, ginger, and salt to the Instant Pot.
  2. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 5 minutes.
  3. Once done, allow for a natural release of pressure for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Open the lid and stir the cranberry sauce. It will continue to thicken as it cools.
  5. Let the sauce cool before serving. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week.

This simple and flavorful cranberry sauce brings a burst of freshness to any fall meal.

6. Instant Pot Spiced Pear Jam

Pears are another autumn favorite, and this spiced pear jam is a perfect way to capture the essence of fall in a jar. This jam is easy to make in the Instant Pot and can be enjoyed on toast, added to desserts, or paired with cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe pears, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice

Instructions:

  1. Add the chopped pears, lemon juice, sugar, cinnamon, cloves, and allspice to the Instant Pot.
  2. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 5 minutes.
  3. Once done, allow for a natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the jam until smooth, or leave it chunky if preferred.
  5. Transfer the jam to sterilized jars and let it cool completely before sealing. Store in the refrigerator.

This homemade pear jam is a delightful treat that captures the flavors of fall in every spoonful.

Conclusion

The Instant Pot is a versatile kitchen tool that can help you create a wide range of cozy, fall-inspired dishes in no time. Whether you’re craving creamy soups, hearty stews, or sweet desserts, these Instant Pot recipes will elevate your autumn meals and bring comfort to your table. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your Instant Pot, and enjoy the delicious, warm flavors of fall.
