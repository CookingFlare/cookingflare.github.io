Lomo a la Sal: Spanish Salt-Crusted Pork Loin


If you’re looking to impress your guests with a show-stopping dish that’s as delicious as it is visually stunning, then Lomo a la Sal is the perfect recipe for you. This traditional Spanish dish features a succulent pork loin encased in a crust of salt, herbs, and spices, resulting in incredibly tender and flavorful meat that will leave everyone at the table in awe. The process of encasing the pork loin in salt may seem unconventional, but it’s a technique that has been used for centuries to lock in the meat’s natural juices and infuse it with a delicate seasoning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pork loin, approximately 3-4 pounds
  • 5 lbs of coarse sea salt
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 2 tbsp of black peppercorns
  • 4 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp of olive oil
  • Additional herbs and spices of your choice (such as thyme, sage, or paprika)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pork Loin:

    • Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is heating up, carefully trim any excess fat from the pork loin, leaving a thin layer to enhance the flavor and juiciness of the meat.

  2. Create the Salt Crust Mixture:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the coarse sea salt, egg whites, black peppercorns, and the leaves from 2 sprigs of rosemary. Mix the ingredients together until you achieve a consistency similar to wet sand.

  3. Encase the Pork Loin:

    • Spread a layer of the salt mixture on the bottom of a roasting pan, creating a bed for the pork loin. Place the pork loin on top of the salt mixture and gently pack the remaining salt mixture around the pork loin, ensuring that it is completely encased in the salt crust.

  4. Add Flavor:

    • Smash the garlic cloves and the remaining 2 sprigs of rosemary, and then stuff them inside the cavity of the pork loin. Squeeze the juice of one lemon over the pork loin and drizzle it with olive oil. You can also add additional herbs and spices of your choice at this stage to further enhance the flavor.

  5. Roast the Pork Loin:

    • Place the roasting pan in the preheated oven and roast the pork loin for approximately 25-30 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare or 160°F (71°C) for medium.

  6. Rest and Serve:

    • Once the pork loin is cooked to your desired doneness, carefully remove it from the oven and allow it to rest for 10-15 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender final product. After resting, carefully crack open the salt crust and brush away any excess salt before slicing the pork loin into thick, juicy portions.

Tips and Serving Suggestions:

  • Flavor Variations: Experiment with different herbs and spices in the salt crust mixture to create unique flavor profiles. Consider using a blend of thyme, sage, and paprika for a more aromatic and complex seasoning.

  • Accompaniments: Lomo a la Sal pairs beautifully with a variety of sides, such as roasted vegetables, creamy mashed potatoes, or a fresh green salad dressed with a zesty vinaigrette.

  • Wine Pairing: The rich and savory flavors of Lomo a la Sal are complemented by a bold red wine, such as a Spanish Rioja or a full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon.

By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a culinary masterpiece that captures the essence of Spanish cuisine. The process of encasing the pork loin in a salt crust not only results in a memorable dining experience but also provides a visually striking presentation that is sure to leave a lasting impression on your guests. Whether you’re hosting a special occasion or simply craving a remarkable meal, Lomo a la Sal is a recipe that promises to elevate any dining experience.
