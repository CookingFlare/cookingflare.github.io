Spring Brunch: Recipes with Seasonal Berries and Yogurt


Spring is the perfect time to gather with friends and family for a delightful brunch. And what better way to celebrate the season than by incorporating the vibrant and refreshing flavors of seasonal berries and creamy yogurt into your menu? In this blog post, we will share three delicious and easy-to-make recipes that will elevate your spring brunch to a whole new level.

1. Mixed Berry Yogurt Parfait

Preparation time: 10 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup mixed seasonal berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/4 cup granola
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In two glass jars or bowls, layer the Greek yogurt, mixed berries, and honey.
  2. Repeat the layers until the jars are filled to your desired height.
  3. Top the parfaits with granola for a satisfying crunch.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  5. Serve chilled and enjoy!

This mixed berry yogurt parfait is not only visually appealing but also a refreshing and nutritious way to start your spring brunch. The tanginess of the Greek yogurt complements the natural sweetness of the berries, while the honey adds a touch of indulgence. The granola provides a delightful crunch, making this parfait a well-balanced and satisfying dish.

2. Berry Spinach Salad with Yogurt Dressing

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups baby spinach
  • 1 cup mixed seasonal berries (blackberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese

Yogurt Dressing:

  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the baby spinach, mixed berries, sliced almonds, and crumbled feta cheese.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, honey, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Drizzle the yogurt dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat all the ingredients.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors!

This berry spinach salad is a delightful combination of fresh greens, juicy berries, and crunchy almonds. The tangy yogurt dressing adds a creamy and zesty element to the dish, while the feta cheese provides a salty kick. It’s a light and refreshing option for a spring brunch that will leave your guests wanting more.

3. Berry Yogurt Pancakes

Preparation time: 20 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed seasonal berries (blueberries, strawberries)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; lumps are fine.
  4. Gently fold in the mixed berries.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or melted butter.
  6. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the pan for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve the pancakes warm with a dollop of Greek yogurt and a sprinkle of mixed berries on top.

These berry yogurt pancakes are fluffy, flavorful, and a true crowd-pleaser. The Greek yogurt adds moisture and richness to the pancakes, while the mixed berries burst with sweetness in every bite. Whether you serve them with a drizzle of maple syrup or enjoy them as they are, these pancakes will make your spring brunch a memorable one.

Conclusion

Spring brunches are meant to be enjoyed in the company of loved ones, and what better way to celebrate the season than with recipes featuring seasonal berries and luscious yogurt? From the refreshing mixed berry yogurt parfait to the vibrant berry spinach salad and the indulgent berry yogurt pancakes, these recipes will add a burst of flavor to your brunch table. So go ahead, embrace the freshness of spring and treat yourself and your guests to a delightful feast of seasonal delights!
