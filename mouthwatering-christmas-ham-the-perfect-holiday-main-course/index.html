Mouthwatering Christmas Ham: The Perfect Holiday Main Course


The holiday season is here, and what better way to celebrate than with a mouthwatering Christmas ham as the centerpiece of your festive feast? This classic main course is sure to delight your family and friends, filling your home with the irresistible aroma of slow-roasted goodness. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the steps to create the perfect holiday ham that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in, fully cooked spiral ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the spiral ham on a large baking dish or roasting pan.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, and ground cloves. Mix until well combined.

  3. Using a basting brush, generously coat the entire surface of the ham with the glaze mixture, making sure to get into all the crevices between the spiral slices.

  4. Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for approximately 10 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C) when measured with a meat thermometer.

  5. While the ham is baking, baste it with the glaze every 30 minutes to ensure a flavorful and moist result. This will also help to create a beautiful caramelized crust on the outside.

  6. Once the ham reaches the desired internal temperature, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 15-20 minutes before serving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and succulent ham.

  7. Carve the ham into thin slices and serve it warm. The sweet and tangy glaze will complement the savory ham, creating a flavor combination that is sure to impress your guests.

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra touch of flavor, you can add a sprinkle of ground cinnamon or nutmeg to the glaze mixture.
  • If you prefer a spicier glaze, you can add a dash of cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the mixture.
  • To make your ham extra festive, you can garnish it with fresh herbs such as rosemary or thyme before serving.
  • Leftover ham can be used in a variety of dishes, such as sandwiches, salads, or casseroles. Get creative with your leftovers and enjoy them in new and delicious ways.

Conclusion:

A mouthwatering Christmas ham is the perfect centerpiece for your holiday table. With its tender and juicy meat, caramelized glaze, and irresistible aroma, it’s sure to become a family favorite. This recipe is simple yet impressive, making it suitable for both experienced cooks and beginners. So, gather your loved ones, create cherished memories, and indulge in the deliciousness of a perfectly cooked Christmas ham. Happy holidays and bon appétit!

Note: This recipe is based on a fully cooked spiral ham. If you are using a different type of ham, be sure to adjust the cooking time and temperature accordingly.
