Easy Nut-Free Stuffed Mushrooms Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious appetizer that’s easy to make and suitable for those with nut allergies, then this Nut-Free Stuffed Mushrooms recipe is the perfect choice. These savory stuffed mushrooms are filled with a flavorful mixture of ingredients that will tantalize your taste buds and impress your guests. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a tasty snack, these stuffed mushrooms are sure to be a hit. Let’s dive into the recipe and get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 24 large white mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare a baking sheet by lining it with parchment paper or lightly greasing it with cooking spray.

  2. Prepare the Mushrooms: Gently wipe the mushrooms with a damp paper towel to remove any dirt. Carefully remove the stems from the mushrooms and set them aside. Arrange the mushroom caps on the prepared baking sheet, cavity side up.

  3. Prepare the Filling: Finely chop the mushroom stems. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant. Add the chopped mushroom stems and cook for a few minutes until they release their moisture.

  4. Mix the Filling: Once the mushroom stems are cooked, transfer them to a bowl. Add the breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together until well combined.

  5. Stuff the Mushrooms: Using a small spoon, generously fill each mushroom cap with the prepared filling, mounding it slightly. Arrange the stuffed mushrooms back on the baking sheet.

  6. Bake the Stuffed Mushrooms: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the stuffed mushrooms for 18-20 minutes, or until the filling is golden and the mushrooms are tender.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stuffed mushrooms are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Sprinkle the freshly chopped parsley over the top for a pop of color and added freshness. Arrange the stuffed mushrooms on a serving platter and enjoy them while they’re warm.

These Nut-Free Stuffed Mushrooms are a fantastic appetizer that can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of nut allergies. The combination of savory flavors and the earthy goodness of mushrooms makes this dish a standout. You can serve these mushrooms as a starter at a dinner party, or even as a delightful snack for a cozy night in. Get creative with the presentation and watch these tasty morsels disappear in no time!

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a simple yet impressive appetizer, give this Nut-Free Stuffed Mushrooms recipe a try. Your guests will be amazed by the burst of flavors in every bite, and you’ll be hailed as a culinary genius. Happy cooking!
