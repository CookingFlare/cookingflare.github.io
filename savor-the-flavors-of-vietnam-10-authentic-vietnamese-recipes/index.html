Savor the Flavors of Vietnam: 10 Authentic Vietnamese Recipes


Vietnamese cuisine is a fusion of different culinary traditions, with influences from Chinese, French, and Indian cooking. The country’s long coastline, rich soil, and tropical climate have also contributed to the unique flavors and ingredients used in Vietnamese cooking. From spicy and sour to sweet and savory, Vietnamese dishes are a reflection of the country’s cultural heritage and its people’s love for good food.

In this post, we’ll explore 10 authentic Vietnamese recipes that showcase the country’s culinary diversity. From popular street foods to traditional family dishes, these recipes will take you on a gastronomic journey through Vietnam.

Recipe 1: Pho Bo (Beef Noodle Soup)

Pho is Vietnam’s national dish, and for good reason. This comforting beef noodle soup is a staple of Vietnamese cuisine, and its popularity has spread to restaurants around the world. The broth is made with beef bones, onions, ginger, and star anise, and served with rare steak, brisket, and rice noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef bones
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 pound rare steak, sliced
  • 1 pound brisket, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large stockpot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, and ginger and cook until the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the beef bones, star anise, fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and black pepper. Cook for 1 hour, or until the bones are tender.
  3. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot. Discard the solids.
  4. Add the beef broth, water, rice vinegar, and sugar to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. Slice the rare steak and brisket into thin strips.
  7. To assemble the pho, place some noodles in a bowl, then add slices of steak and brisket. Ladle the hot broth over the top and garnish with scallions, bean sprouts, and a lime wedge.

Recipe 2: Banh Xeo (Sizzling Pancake)

Banh xeo is a popular Vietnamese street food that consists of a crispy rice flour pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts. It’s often served with fresh herbs, lettuce leaves, and sweet sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup pork, ground
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup sweet sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, water, salt, and sugar. Mix until a smooth batter forms.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the oil and swirl it around to coat the pan.
  3. Pour 1/4 cup of the batter into the pan and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the edges start to curl.
  4. Add the shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts to one half of the pancake. Fold the other half over to form a half-moon shape.
  5. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the pancake is crispy and golden brown.
  6. Serve the banh xeo with fresh herbs, lettuce leaves, and sweet sauce.

Recipe 3: Goi Cuon (Fresh Spring Rolls)

Goi cuon are fresh spring rolls filled with shrimp, pork, and vegetables, wrapped in rice paper and served with a sweet and sour sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package rice paper wrappers
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup pork, ground
  • 1/2 cup vegetables, such as carrots, cucumber, and lettuce
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup sweet sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large bowl with warm water.
  2. Place a rice paper wrapper in the water and let it soak for 10-15 seconds, or until it becomes pliable.
  3. Remove the wrapper from the water and place it on a clean surface.
  4. Arrange the shrimp, pork, and vegetables in the center of the wrapper.
  5. Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll up the wrapper to form a tight cylinder.
  6. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling ingredients.
  7. Serve the goi cuon with sweet sauce and garnish with scallions.

Recipe 4: Banh Khot (Mini Savory Pancakes)

Banh khot are mini savory pancakes made with rice flour and coconut milk, often served as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup pork, ground
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup sweet sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, coconut milk, salt, and sugar. Mix until a smooth batter forms.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the oil and swirl it around to coat the pan.
  3. Pour 1 tablespoon of the batter into the pan and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the edges start to curl.
  4. Add the shrimp and pork to the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the pancake is crispy and golden brown.
  5. Serve the banh khot with sweet sauce and garnish with scallions.

Recipe 5: Canh Chin (Pork and Egg Soup)

Canh chin is a hearty and comforting soup made with pork, eggs, and vegetables, often served as a main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots
  • 1/2 cup sliced zucchini
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped scallions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and cook until the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the pork and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken broth, water, mushrooms, carrots, and zucchini to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Crack the eggs into the pot and stir gently to distribute them evenly.
  5. Add the fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Simmer the soup for an additional 5 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked through.
  7. Serve the canh chin hot, garnished with chopped scallions.

Recipe 6: Banh Bot Loc (Clear Dumplings)

Banh bot loc are clear dumplings made with tapioca flour and filled with shrimp and pork, often served in a light broth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tapioca flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup pork, ground
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup sweet sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tapioca flour, water, salt, and sugar. Mix until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for 5-10 minutes, or until it becomes pliable.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter.
  4. Flatten each ball into a disk shape and place a shrimp and a piece of pork in the center.
  5. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges together to seal the dumpling.
  6. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling ingredients.
  7. Serve the banh bot loc in a light broth and garnish with scallions and sweet sauce.

Recipe 7: Com Tam (Broken Rice)

Com tam is a popular Vietnamese street food made with broken rice, grilled pork, and a variety of toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup broken rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup grilled pork, sliced
  • 1/2 cup fried egg
  • 1/2 cup pickled vegetables
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the broken rice according to the package instructions.
  2. Heat the oil in a non-stick skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the grilled pork and cook until crispy.
  3. Add the fried egg to the skillet and cook until the whites are set and the yolks are cooked to your desired doneness.
  4. Serve the com tam with the grilled pork, fried egg, pickled vegetables, and a drizzle of fish sauce.

Recipe 8: Banh Mi (Vietnamese Sandwich)

Banh mi is a popular Vietnamese sandwich made with a crispy baguette, pickled vegetables, and a variety of meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, cut into 1/2-inch slices
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup pickled vegetables
  • 1/2 cup grilled pork, sliced
  • 1/2 cup chicken, sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a non-stick skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the grilled pork and chicken and cook until crispy.
  2. Assemble the banh mi by spreading mayonnaise on the baguette, then adding the pickled vegetables, grilled pork and chicken, and a sprinkle of scallions.

Recipe 9: Canh Ga (Chicken and Vegetable Soup)

Canh ga is a comforting and flavorful soup made with chicken, vegetables, and a variety of spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots
  • 1/2 cup sliced zucchini
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce, for serving
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and cook until the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the chicken to the pot and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken broth, water, mushrooms, carrots, and zucchini to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Season the soup with fish sauce, soy sauce, and a pinch of salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the canh ga hot, garnished with chopped scallions.

Recipe 10: Che (Vietnamese Dessert Soup)

Che is a popular Vietnamese dessert soup made with coconut milk, rice flour, and a variety of fruits and nuts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sliced mango
  • 1/2 cup sliced pineapple
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the coconut milk, rice flour, sugar, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the soup for 5-10 minutes, or until it reaches your desired consistency.
  3. Stir in the sliced mango and pineapple.
  4. Serve the che hot, garnished with chopped peanuts and scallions.

From spicy and sour to sweet and savory, each dish is a reflection of the country’s cultural heritage and its people’s love for good food.
