Alone, But Together: Intimate Pizza and Flatbread Recipes for Celebrating Special Moments


Sharing a meal with loved ones has the power to create meaningful connections and memories that last a lifetime. With today’s social distancing guidelines, it’s more important than ever to find ways to celebrate special occasions while keeping a safe distance from others. Enter the world of intimate pizza and flatbread recipes. These versatile dishes are perfect for making special moments cheery and heartwarming, all while celebrating the togetherness of loved ones.

1. Classic Margherita Flatbread

Who can resist the iconic combination of mozzarella, fresh basil, and ripe tomato? The Classic Margherita Flatbread will transport you to a sun-drenched Italian piazza. The simplicity of this dish does wonders for showcasing the quality of the ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole wheat flatbread or pizza crust
  • 2 cups fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce, homemade or store-bought
  • Handful of fresh basil leaves
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Ensure the rack is in the center.
  2. Stretch or roll the dough to form a circle slightly larger than your flatbread or pizza crust. Transfer it onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Spread an even layer of tomato sauce onto the dough. Arrange the mozzarella slices on top, distributing them evenly.
  4. Sprinkle salt and pepper to taste, followed by the fresh basil leaves.
  5. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of olive oil over the flatbread, ensuring an even distribution.
  6. Place the flatbread in the oven and allow it to bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust turns golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Remove the flatbread from the oven, allow it to cool slightly, then slice and serve.

2. Veggie Delight Thin Crust Pizza

This pizza is the perfect way to show someone how much you care, without losing sight of the importance of a balanced diet. It’s packed with colorful and nutritious veggies that will delight even the most skeptical foodie.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ready-to-bake thin crust pizza
  • 1 red bell pepper, roasted and sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or vegan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and prepare a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Arrange the roasted bell pepper, zucchini, mushrooms, cherry tomatoes, and red onion on the pizza crust. Toss them with olive oil and season according to preference.
  3. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella or vegan cheese on top and slide the pizza onto the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for around 10-12 minutes, or until the crust turns golden and the veggies are cooked through.
  5. Remove from the oven, slice, and serve immediately.

3. Midnight Mushroom Flatbread

For those quiet moments, when the night’s stillness calls for a tender connection, indulge in the earthy flavors of this sensational flatbread. There’s something comforting and mystical about mushrooms that transform a simple meal into a memorable experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole wheat flatbread or pizza crust
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups sliced button mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup caramelized onions
  • 1/2 cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes, optional

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and prepare a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook until they turn golden brown and tender.
  3. On the flatbread, layer the cooked mushrooms and caramelized onions. Sprinkle grated Gruyère, fresh thyme, and red pepper flakes (if using) on top.
  4. Slide the flatbread onto the baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust turns golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

The Secret Ingredient for Connection

While these recipes may seem simple, the true magic happens when you sit down with the ones you love and immerse yourself in the flavors, conversations, and moments that make life so wonderfully rich. Cooking and sharing food may look different now, but it continues to be the heartbeat of meaningful connections fueling our relationships.

As we navigate through different times and situations, recognize the importance of finding quality moments with our loved ones and how creating intimate experiences, like cooking, can connect us deeply, like never before. Happy cooking and bonded dining!
