Succulent Roast Goose Recipe for a Memorable Christmas Meal


As the festive season approaches, it’s time to elevate your Christmas dinner with a succulent roast goose that will leave a lasting impression on your guests. This classic dish is a perfect alternative to the traditional turkey, offering rich, flavorful meat and crispy skin that is sure to delight your taste buds. In this recipe, we will explore the step-by-step process of preparing and roasting a goose to perfection, along with complementary side dishes that will make this Christmas meal truly memorable.

Ingredients

For the Goose:

  • 1 whole goose (approximately 10-12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 4 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 large onions, quartered
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, roughly chopped
  • 1 orange, quartered
  • 1 lemon, quartered

For the Stuffing:

  • 1 pound of pork sausage meat
  • 1 cup of fresh breadcrumbs
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Gravy:

  • 4 cups of chicken or turkey stock
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Goose:

    • Remove the goose from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Use a sharp knife to score the skin of the goose in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to pierce the meat.
    • Season the goose inside and out with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper.
    • Stuff the cavity of the goose with the rosemary, crushed garlic, onions, carrots, celery, orange, and lemon.

  2. Prepare the Stuffing:

    • In a large bowl, combine the pork sausage meat, fresh breadcrumbs, chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried sage, salt, and pepper.
    • Mix the ingredients together until well combined.

  3. Stuff the Goose:

    • Fill the cavity of the goose with the prepared stuffing, packing it in firmly but not too tightly.

  4. Roast the Goose:

    • Place the stuffed goose on a rack in a roasting pan, breast side up.
    • Roast in the preheated oven for 20 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
    • Baste the goose with its own fat every 30 minutes to ensure crispy, golden skin.

  5. Make the Gravy:

    • Once the goose is fully cooked, transfer it to a carving board and let it rest while you prepare the gravy.
    • Discard the excess fat from the roasting pan, leaving behind the browned bits.
    • Place the roasting pan over medium heat and deglaze with white wine, scraping up the browned bits from the bottom.
    • Sprinkle the flour over the pan and cook, stirring constantly, for a few minutes.
    • Gradually whisk in the chicken or turkey stock until the gravy thickens. Season with salt and pepper.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Carve the succulent roast goose and serve it with the flavorful stuffing and gravy alongside your favorite Christmas side dishes.

Conclusion

This succulent roast goose recipe is a show-stopping centerpiece for your Christmas feast, guaranteed to impress your loved ones and create lasting memories. The rich, tender meat, crispy skin, and flavorful stuffing, combined with a savory gravy, will elevate your holiday meal to new heights. Embrace the tradition of roast goose and savor the joy of creating a truly memorable Christmas dining experience for your family and friends. Happy cooking and Merry Christmas!
