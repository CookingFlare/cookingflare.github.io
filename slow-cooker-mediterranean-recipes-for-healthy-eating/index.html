Slow Cooker Mediterranean Recipes for Healthy Eating


Looking for delicious and healthy meal options that are easy to prepare? Look no further than these flavorful slow cooker Mediterranean recipes! Packed with fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and nutritious elements, these dishes are perfect for anyone looking to embrace the Mediterranean diet and enjoy a taste of the sunny shores of the Mediterranean.

Slow Cooker Greek Chicken Pita Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat pita bread
  • Tzatziki sauce for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts at the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. Add the cherry tomatoes, red onion, Kalamata olives, and minced garlic on top of the chicken.
  3. Sprinkle the dried oregano, thyme, and rosemary over the ingredients in the slow cooker.
  4. Pour the chicken broth over the mixture and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Once cooked, shred the chicken using two forks and mix it with the cooked vegetables.
  7. Warm the pita bread and fill it with the chicken and vegetable mixture.
  8. Drizzle with tzatziki sauce and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Mediterranean Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the lentils, onion, carrots, celery, garlic, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, thyme, oregano, and cumin in the slow cooker.
  2. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 7-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the lentils and vegetables are tender.
  4. Once cooked, use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup, leaving some lentils and vegetables whole for texture.
  5. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Slow Cooker Mediterranean Stuffed Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops removed and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the quinoa, chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, Kalamata olives, minced garlic, dried oregano, and dried basil.
  2. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff the mixture into the bell peppers.
  4. Place the stuffed peppers in the slow cooker and pour the vegetable broth into the bottom of the slow cooker.
  5. Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the peppers are tender.
  6. Once cooked, carefully remove the stuffed peppers from the slow cooker and serve hot.

Conclusion

These slow cooker Mediterranean recipes are not only delicious and satisfying, but they also offer a host of health benefits. The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its heart-healthy properties, and these recipes are a perfect way to incorporate this beneficial way of eating into your lifestyle. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or meal prepping for the week ahead, these flavorful dishes are sure to become staples in your kitchen. Enjoy the rich flavors and nourishing ingredients of the Mediterranean with these convenient and wholesome slow cooker recipes!
