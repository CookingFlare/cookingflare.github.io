Chile Relleno Burritos: A Cheesy and Flavorful Delight


If you’re looking for a dish that combines the robust flavors of traditional Mexican cuisine with modern convenience, look no further than Chile Relleno Burritos. This mouthwatering recipe is not just a meal; it’s a delicious experience that merges the smokiness of roasted peppers with the creaminess of cheese, all wrapped in a warm burrito. Perfectly suited for lunch, dinner, or even a satisfying snack, these burritos are sure to please any palate.

The Origin of Chile Rellenos

Before diving into the burrito-making process, it’s essential to understand the origins of the dish. Chile Rellenos, meaning “stuffed chilies” in Spanish, is a classic Mexican dish that features poblano peppers filled with cheese, meat, or a combination of both. Traditionally, these peppers are battered and fried until golden brown, creating a crunchy exterior that contrasts beautifully with the gooey, melted cheese inside.

While Chile Rellenos are typically served on their own, wrapping them in a burrito adds convenience and portability. This fusion not only enhances the dish but also allows for endless variations, making it a versatile option for any meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create the perfect Chile Relleno Burrito, gather the following ingredients:

For the Chile Rellenos

  • 4 large poblano peppers
  • 2 cups of shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • 1 cup of cooked ground beef or shredded chicken (optional)
  • 1 cup of cooked rice (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs (for batter)
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • Oil for frying

For the Burritos

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup of refried beans
  • 1 cup of salsa (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 avocado, sliced (optional)
  • Sour cream for serving (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Roast the Poblano Peppers

Begin by roasting the poblano peppers to enhance their flavor. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the peppers on a baking sheet and roast them for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally until the skin is charred and blistered. Alternatively, you can roast them over an open flame on your stovetop for a more authentic flavor.

Once roasted, remove the peppers from the oven and place them in a bowl covered with plastic wrap. Let them steam for about 10 minutes, which will make peeling the skin much easier.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the peppers are steaming, prepare your filling. In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded cheese, cooked ground beef or chicken (if using), rice, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully combined.

Step 3: Peel and Stuff the Peppers

After steaming, carefully peel the charred skin off the peppers. Make a lengthwise slit in each pepper and remove the seeds and membranes. Be gentle to ensure the peppers remain intact.

Next, generously stuff each pepper with the cheese and meat mixture. Set aside while you prepare the batter.

Step 4: Batter and Fry the Peppers

In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs until frothy. Spread the flour on a plate. Dip each stuffed pepper first in the flour, shaking off any excess, then into the beaten eggs to coat thoroughly.

Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the battered peppers into the skillet. Fry them for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Remove them from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 5: Assemble the Burritos

Now that the Chile Rellenos are ready, it’s time to assemble your burritos. Lay a large flour tortilla flat on a clean surface. Spread a layer of refried beans in the center, followed by a generous spoonful of salsa. Place one of the fried stuffed peppers on top.

Carefully fold in the sides of the tortilla and then roll it from the bottom up, ensuring the filling is secured tightly inside. Repeat this process for the remaining tortillas and fillings.

Step 6: Optional – Toast the Burritos

For an extra layer of flavor and a delightful crunch, you can toast the assembled burritos. Heat a skillet over medium heat and place the burritos seam-side down. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and crispy.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Slice the burritos in half and serve with sliced avocado, a dollop of sour cream, and fresh cilantro for garnish. Pair with additional salsa on the side for those who love a little extra spice.

Tips for the Perfect Chile Relleno Burritos

  1. Pepper Selection: While poblano peppers are traditional, feel free to experiment with other types of peppers such as Anaheim or jalapeño for different flavor profiles.

  2. Cheese Options: Mixing different types of cheese can add depth to the flavor. Consider using pepper jack for a spicy kick or goat cheese for a tangy twist.

  3. Meat Alternatives: For a vegetarian option, omit the meat and load up on extra vegetables like sautéed onions, peppers, or zucchini.

  4. Make Ahead: If you’re prepping for a busy week, you can roast and stuff the peppers ahead of time. Store them in the fridge and assemble the burritos when you’re ready to eat.

  5. Freezing: These burritos freeze well! Wrap them individually in foil and freeze. When ready to eat, bake them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) until heated through.

Nutritional Benefits

Aside from being delicious, Chile Relleno Burritos offer a variety of nutritional benefits. Poblano peppers are low in calories but high in vitamins A and C, boosting your immune system. The addition of beans provides protein and fiber, making these burritos a satisfying meal that can keep you full for longer.

Conclusion

Chile Relleno Burritos are a fantastic way to enjoy the rich flavors of traditional Mexican cuisine in a modern, portable format. They are customizable, easy to make, and perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, looking for a hearty family dinner, or simply craving something cheesy and flavorful, these burritos are sure to impress.

Get creative with your ingredients, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different flavors. Once you try these Chile Relleno Burritos, they might just become a new staple in your cooking repertoire. Enjoy your culinary adventure!
