Top 5 Comfort Food Recipes for College Students


College life can be a whirlwind of classes, late-night study sessions, and social events. Amidst the chaos, one thing remains constant: the need for comfort food. Whether you’re looking to whip up a quick meal between lectures or indulge in a hearty dish after a long day, these top five comfort food recipes are perfect for college students. They are easy to make, budget-friendly, and most importantly, delicious!

1. Cheesy Broccoli and Rice Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of uncooked rice
  • 2 cups of broccoli florets (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) of cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook the Rice: In a medium saucepan, cook the rice according to package instructions. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Broccoli: If using fresh broccoli, steam it until tender. If using frozen, microwave it according to package instructions.
  4. Mix Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, broccoli, cream of mushroom soup, milk, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  5. Add Cheese: Fold in half of the shredded cheddar cheese.
  6. Transfer to Baking Dish: Pour the mixture into a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top. If desired, add breadcrumbs for a crunchy topping.
  7. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top.
  8. Serve: Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish is perfect for leftovers and can be easily reheated.

Why It’s Great for College Students:

This casserole is not only filling but also packed with nutrients from the broccoli. It’s a one-dish meal that can be made in advance and stored in the fridge, making it ideal for busy students.

2. Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Spaghetti: In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the rest.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until the garlic is golden and fragrant (about 2 minutes).
  3. Combine: Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet, tossing to coat in the garlic oil. If the pasta seems dry, add a bit of the reserved pasta water until you reach your desired consistency.
  4. Season: Season with salt to taste and toss in the chopped parsley.
  5. Serve: Plate the spaghetti and top with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Why It’s Great for College Students:

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a classic Italian dish that requires minimal ingredients and can be made in under 20 minutes. It’s perfect for those late-night cravings or when you need a quick meal before heading out.

3. One-Pot Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, green onions

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Meat: In a large pot, brown the ground meat over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until the onion is translucent.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Stir in the kidney beans, black beans, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Cook: Reduce heat to low and let the chili simmer for at least 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the better the flavors meld.
