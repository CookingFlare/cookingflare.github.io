The Delightful Taste of Japanese Melon Pan: Sweet Bread Treat


Japanese cuisine is known for its unique and mouthwatering flavors, and one popular treat that perfectly embodies this is the Melon Pan. Melon Pan is a sweet bread with a crisp cookie crust on the outside and a fluffy, buttery interior. Despite its name, Melon Pan doesn’t actually contain any melon; instead, its name comes from the characteristic appearance of its crust, which resembles the texture of a melon.

The Origins of Melon Pan

Melon Pan originated in Japan during the late 19th century and has since become a beloved snack and dessert across the country. It is often enjoyed with a cup of tea or as a quick on-the-go treat. The bread dough used in Melon Pan is similar to that of sweet buns or rolls, but what sets it apart is the cookie dough that is wrapped around it.

The Perfect Melon Pan Recipe

Making Melon Pan from scratch may seem like a daunting task, but with a little patience and some basic ingredients, you can recreate this delightful treat in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • For the bread dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
    • 1/2 cup warm milk
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
    • 1 large egg

  • For the cookie dough:

    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm milk and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, softened butter, and egg. Mix until a soft dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  5. While the dough is rising, prepare the cookie dough by creaming together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the vanilla extract and mix well. Gradually add the flour and salt, mixing until a smooth dough forms.

  6. Once the bread dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into small equal-sized portions. Roll each portion into a ball.

  7. Take a ball of bread dough and flatten it with your hands. Place a small portion of the cookie dough in the center and wrap the bread dough around it, sealing it well.

  8. Repeat this process with the remaining dough portions.

  9. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C).

  10. Place the Melon Pan on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving enough space between each one for them to expand.

  11. Using a sharp knife, make crisscross cuts on the surface of each Melon Pan, creating the characteristic melon-like pattern.

  12. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the Melon Pan turns golden brown.

  13. Remove from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack before serving.

  14. Enjoy the delightful taste of Japanese Melon Pan with a hot cup of tea or coffee!

Variations and Tips

Melon Pan is incredibly versatile, and you can customize it to suit your taste preferences. Here are a few variations you can try:

  • Matcha Melon Pan: Add a teaspoon of matcha powder to the cookie dough to give it a unique green tea flavor.

  • Chocolate Chip Melon Pan: Fold in a handful of chocolate chips into the cookie dough for an extra burst of sweetness.

  • Cream-filled Melon Pan: After baking, let the Melon Pan cool completely, then slice them in half and fill them with your favorite cream or custard.

To ensure your Melon Pan turns out perfect every time, here are a few tips:

  • Make sure the yeast is fresh and active. If your yeast doesn’t foam or bubble when dissolved in warm milk, it may be expired.

  • Don’t skip the step of punching down the dough after the first rise. This helps release any excess air and ensures a more even texture.

  • When making the cookie dough, make sure your butter is softened but not melted. Softened butter will cream better with the sugar and give the cookie dough the right consistency.

  • Experiment with different baking times to achieve your desired level of crispness for the cookie crust. Some prefer a softer crust, while others enjoy a more crunchier texture.

Melon Pan is a delightful treat that combines the best of both bread and cookies. Its unique texture and flavors will surely leave you craving for more. So why not give this delicious Japanese sweet bread a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
