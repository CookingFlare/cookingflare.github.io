Irresistible Caramelized Onion and Mushroom Gravy Recipe


If you’re looking to add a touch of richness and depth to your meals, look no further than this irresistible caramelized onion and mushroom gravy recipe. This savory, umami-packed gravy is the perfect accompaniment to mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or even a juicy steak. With its complex flavors and velvety texture, this gravy is sure to elevate any dish to new heights. So, let’s dive into the steps to create this mouthwatering concoction.

Ingredients

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of assorted mushrooms, such as cremini, shiitake, or oyster, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of beef or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of dry red wine
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Caramelize the Onions: In a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are deeply golden and caramelized, about 30-40 minutes. The slow caramelization process is crucial for developing the rich, sweet flavor of the onions.

  2. Sauté the Mushrooms: In a separate skillet, melt the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter over medium-high heat. Add the chopped mushrooms and minced garlic, and sauté until the mushrooms are golden brown and have released their moisture, about 8-10 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme leaves.

  3. Create the Roux: Sprinkle the flour over the caramelized onions and stir to combine. Cook for 2-3 minutes to allow the flour to cook and thicken the mixture slightly.

  4. Deglaze the Pan: Pour in the red wine and stir, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Let the wine reduce for a few minutes, allowing the alcohol to cook off and the flavors to concentrate.

  5. Add the Broth: Slowly pour in the broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the gravy has thickened to your desired consistency.

  6. Blend and Adjust: If you prefer a smoother gravy, you can use an immersion blender to blend the mixture until it reaches your desired texture. Taste the gravy and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Serving Suggestions

This irresistible caramelized onion and mushroom gravy pairs beautifully with a variety of dishes. Serve it over mashed potatoes for a comforting side dish, drizzle it over a grilled steak for a gourmet touch, or use it as a flavorful topping for roasted vegetables. The depth of flavor from the caramelized onions and mushrooms will undoubtedly impress your guests and elevate any meal.
