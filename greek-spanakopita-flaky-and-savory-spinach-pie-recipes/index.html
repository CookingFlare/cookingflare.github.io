Greek Spanakopita: Flaky and Savory Spinach Pie Recipes


Greek cuisine is known for its delicious and flavorful dishes, and one that stands out is Spanakopita. This traditional Greek dish is a savory spinach pie with a flaky phyllo dough crust. Spanakopita is not only a crowd-pleaser but also a great way to incorporate healthy greens into your diet. In this blog post, we will explore two different recipes for Greek Spanakopita that you can easily recreate at home.

Recipe 1: Classic Spanakopita

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough sheets
  • 1 ½ pounds of fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • ½ cup of ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup of fresh dill, chopped
  • ¼ cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.

  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until it wilts down, about 5 minutes. Make sure to remove any excess liquid from the spinach.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked spinach mixture, feta cheese, ricotta cheese, dill, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  5. Lay out the phyllo dough sheets on a clean surface. Brush each sheet with olive oil, making sure to cover the entire surface.

  6. Place a layer of the oiled phyllo dough sheets in a baking dish. Spread a generous amount of the spinach mixture on top of the phyllo dough.

  7. Repeat the process of layering the phyllo dough and spinach mixture until you have used all the ingredients. Make sure to brush each layer of phyllo dough with olive oil.

  8. Once you have finished layering, fold over any excess phyllo dough on the edges to seal the pie.

  9. Bake the Spanakopita in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  10. Remove the Spanakopita from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Cut into squares or triangles and enjoy!

Recipe 2: Spanakopita Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough sheets
  • 1 ½ pounds of fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • ½ cup of ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup of fresh dill, chopped
  • ¼ cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.

  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until it wilts down, about 5 minutes. Make sure to remove any excess liquid from the spinach.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked spinach mixture, feta cheese, ricotta cheese, dill, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  5. Lay out a sheet of phyllo dough on a clean surface. Brush it with olive oil and place a spoonful of the spinach mixture at one end of the sheet.

  6. Roll the phyllo dough tightly around the spinach mixture, folding in the sides as you go. Brush the rolled phyllo dough with olive oil to seal it.

  7. Repeat the process with the remaining phyllo dough sheets and spinach mixture.

  8. Place the Spanakopita rolls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops of the rolls with olive oil.

  9. Bake the Spanakopita rolls in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  10. Remove the Spanakopita rolls from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve them as an appetizer or a light meal.

Spanakopita is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as an appetizer, a side dish, or even a main course. Its flaky and savory nature makes it a favorite among both vegetarians and non-vegetarians. Whether you choose to make the classic Spanakopita or the convenient Spanakopita rolls, you are sure to impress your friends and family with this delicious Greek delicacy. So, dust off your apron and give these recipes a try – your taste buds will thank you!
