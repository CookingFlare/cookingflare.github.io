Katsu Curry: The Perfect Fusion of Japanese and Indian Flavors


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine and Indian spices, then you’re in for a treat with Katsu Curry. This delightful dish is a beautiful fusion of Japanese tonkatsu and Indian curry, creating a unique and flavorful experience that will tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll take a deep dive into the history of Katsu Curry, explore its key ingredients, and provide you with a step-by-step recipe to recreate this culinary marvel in your own kitchen.

The Origins of Katsu Curry

Katsu Curry, also known as “Katsu Kare,” has a fascinating history that reflects the cultural exchange between Japan and India. The dish first made its appearance in Japan during the Meiji era (1868-1912), when the Japanese were embracing Western cuisine. Tonkatsu, a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet, was introduced to Japan by the Portuguese in the 19th century. Meanwhile, the British brought curry to Japan during the same period, and it quickly became a beloved part of Japanese cuisine.

The ingenious combination of tonkatsu and curry resulted in the birth of Katsu Curry, which has since become a staple in Japanese cuisine. Its popularity continues to grow, not only in Japan but also around the world, as food enthusiasts appreciate its harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

Key Ingredients

For the Tonkatsu:

  • Pork loin or chicken breast
  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Curry Sauce:

  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Potatoes
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Curry powder
  • Garam masala
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Bay leaves
  • Vegetable oil

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Tonkatsu

  1. Begin by tenderizing the pork loin or chicken breast with a meat mallet.
  2. Season the meat with salt and pepper.
  3. Dredge the meat in flour, dip it in beaten eggs, and coat it with panko breadcrumbs.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan and fry the breaded meat until golden brown and crispy. Once cooked, set the tonkatsu aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil.

Step 2: Making the Curry Sauce

  1. Sauté chopped onions, garlic, and ginger in a pot until fragrant.
  2. Add curry powder and garam masala, and continue to cook until aromatic.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add diced carrots and potatoes to the pot, then pour in chicken or vegetable broth.
  5. Season the sauce with soy sauce, honey, and bay leaves, then let it simmer until the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together.

Step 3: Assembling the Katsu Curry

  1. Slice the tonkatsu into strips and serve it alongside steamed rice.
  2. Ladle the rich and aromatic curry sauce over the tonkatsu and rice, ensuring that each bite is infused with the delicious flavors of the curry.

Enjoy Your Culinary Creation

Katsu Curry is a delightful marriage of crispy, tender tonkatsu and fragrant, savory curry sauce. The crispy texture of the tonkatsu complements the rich and aromatic curry, resulting in a dish that is both comforting and satisfying. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, Indian flavors, or simply love to experiment with new culinary creations, Katsu Curry is sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a culinary adventure that celebrates the beautiful fusion of Japanese and Indian flavors. With its rich history and irresistible taste, Katsu Curry is a testament to the power of culinary creativity and cultural exchange. Cheers to good food and the joy of cooking!
