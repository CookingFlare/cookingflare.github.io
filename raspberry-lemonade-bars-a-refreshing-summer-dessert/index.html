Raspberry Lemonade Bars: A Refreshing Summer Dessert


When the summer heat is in full swing, there’s nothing quite as satisfying as a refreshingly tangy and sweet dessert to cool you down. Raspberry lemonade bars are the perfect treat for those warm days when you need a burst of citrusy flavor. With a buttery shortbread crust and a luscious raspberry lemon filling, these bars are a delightful balance of sweetness and tartness. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a picnic in the park, or simply craving a summery indulgence, these bars are sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

For the Shortbread Crust:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt

For the Raspberry Lemon Filling:

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal of the bars.

  2. Make the Shortbread Crust: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the flour and salt, and mix until the dough comes together. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool slightly.

  3. Prepare the Raspberry Lemon Filling: In a blender or food processor, puree the fresh raspberries until smooth. Pass the puree through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds, collecting the raspberry puree in a bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, eggs, lemon juice, lemon zest, and flour until well combined. Gently fold in the raspberry puree until the mixture is evenly pink.

  4. Bake the Bars: Pour the raspberry lemon filling over the cooled shortbread crust. Return the pan to the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are golden. The center should still have a slight jiggle. Remove from the oven and allow the bars to cool completely in the pan.

  5. Chill and Serve: Once cooled, refrigerate the bars for at least 2 hours, or until fully chilled and set. Once set, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the pan. Dust with powdered sugar, if desired, and cut into squares or bars.

Serving Suggestions

These raspberry lemonade bars are best enjoyed chilled, making them an ideal dessert for hot summer days. Serve them at a backyard party alongside a pitcher of iced tea or lemonade for a delightful combination of flavors. You can also top the bars with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Conclusion

Raspberry lemonade bars are a delightful fusion of tangy raspberries and zesty lemon, encapsulated in a buttery shortbread crust. This recipe is a perfect balance of sweet and tart, making it a crowd-pleaser for all ages. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply looking for a treat to enjoy on a sunny afternoon, these bars are a must-try. So, embrace the flavors of summer and whip up a batch of these delectable raspberry lemonade bars for a truly refreshing dessert experience.
