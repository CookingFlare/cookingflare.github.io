Middle Eastern Comfort Food: 10 Recipes to Warm Your Heart


The Middle East is home to some of the most vibrant, flavorful, and soul-warming comfort foods in the world. From hearty stews to fresh, herb-laden dishes, Middle Eastern cuisine offers an incredible variety of options that soothe both the body and the spirit. Whether you’re in need of a dish that feels like a warm embrace after a long day or you’re exploring the culinary traditions of this rich region, these ten Middle Eastern comfort food recipes are sure to lift your mood and satisfy your hunger.

1. Shorba (Middle Eastern Lentil Soup)

Nothing beats the comfort of a warm, hearty soup on a chilly evening, and shorba is the epitome of cozy Middle Eastern cuisine. This lentil soup, often made with a rich blend of tomatoes, carrots, and onions, is a staple in many Middle Eastern homes. The comforting texture and earthy flavor of lentils paired with the aromatic spices make this dish perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent.
  2. Add the carrots and chopped tomatoes, and cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the cumin and coriander, letting the spices toast slightly before adding the lentils and vegetable broth.
  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the lentils are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

This soup is wonderfully filling on its own or can be paired with a warm pita or crusty bread for a heartier meal.

2. Koshari (Egyptian Street Food)

One of Egypt’s national dishes, Koshari is a fragrant, filling combination of lentils, rice, pasta, chickpeas, and crispy fried onions, all smothered in a rich tomato sauce. This hearty dish is both affordable and satisfying, making it a popular comfort food in Egyptian homes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup lentils
  • 1 cup elbow macaroni
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice, lentils, and pasta in separate pots. Set them aside once they are done.
  2. In a frying pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil and fry the thinly sliced onions until they turn golden and crispy. Set aside on paper towels to drain the excess oil.
  3. In a saucepan, heat the remaining olive oil, and sauté the garlic for a minute before adding the tomato sauce, cumin, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. In a large serving bowl, layer the rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas.
  5. Pour the tomato sauce over the layers, and top with the crispy fried onions. Serve warm.

Koshari is a filling and savory dish, often enjoyed as a main course, and its complex flavors are sure to satisfy your comfort food cravings.

3. Falafel (Crispy Chickpea Fritters)

A favorite street food across the Middle East, falafel is a deep-fried patty made from mashed chickpeas, herbs, and spices. It’s crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and packed with flavor. Served in a pita with tahini sauce, salad, and pickles, falafel is a delicious and satisfying meal for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 onion, coarsely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas in water overnight. Drain before using.
  2. In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, parsley, cilantro, salt, and baking soda. Process until a coarse paste forms.
  3. Let the mixture sit in the refrigerator for a couple of hours.
  4. Roll the mixture into small balls or patties.
  5. Heat the oil in a frying pan or deep fryer to 375°F (190°C) and fry the falafel until golden brown, about 4-5 minutes.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve with tahini sauce, pita bread, and your favorite salad toppings.

4. Fattoush (Lebanese Bread Salad)

If you’re in the mood for something light yet satisfying, fattoush is the perfect dish. This vibrant Lebanese salad is made with fresh vegetables, herbs, and crispy pieces of pita bread, all tossed in a tangy vinaigrette. It’s a refreshing and filling dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as a side to your favorite Middle Eastern meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup radishes, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 small pita breads, toasted and broken into pieces
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the lettuce, cucumber, tomato, radishes, parsley, and mint.
  2. Add the toasted pita pieces on top.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, pomegranate molasses, salt, and pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss everything together. Serve immediately for the best texture.

5. Maklouba (Upside-Down Rice and Eggplant)

Maklouba, meaning “upside-down” in Arabic, is a traditional Levantine dish featuring layers of rice, meat (often chicken or lamb), and vegetables. The dish is cooked in a pot, and when it’s served, it’s flipped upside down to reveal the beautiful layers of food. It’s a rich, savory dish that feels like a warm embrace on a chilly day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 lb chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil and sauté the chicken pieces until browned on all sides. Set aside.
  2. In the same pot, fry the eggplant slices until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the chopped onion, then add the spices (cinnamon, cumin, and allspice). Stir to coat the onions in the spices.
  4. Layer the rice, chicken, and eggplant in the pot, making sure the rice is at the bottom. Add the chicken broth and bring it to a boil.
  5. Cover and simmer for 30-40 minutes, until the rice and chicken are cooked through.
  6. Flip the pot onto a large serving platter to reveal the layers. Serve hot.

6. Baba Ganoush (Smoky Eggplant Dip)

Baba Ganoush is a smoky, creamy eggplant dip that’s perfect for dipping pita bread or fresh vegetables. The grilled eggplant gives the dish a deep, charred flavor, while tahini adds a rich, nutty undertone. This simple yet flavorful appetizer is a true Middle Eastern classic.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Grill or roast the eggplants until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  2. Let the eggplants cool, then scoop out the flesh into a bowl.
  3. Add tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and salt. Mash the mixture until smooth and creamy.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and garnish with fresh parsley.
  5. Serve with pita bread or vegetable sticks.

7. Kebabs (Grilled Meat Skewers)

Kebabs are a quintessential part of Middle Eastern cuisine. Whether you’re grilling beef, lamb, or chicken, these skewered meats are marinated in a blend of spices, herbs, and yogurt, resulting in juicy, flavorful bites. They’re great for a casual family dinner or a gathering with friends.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs, cubed
  • 1 onion, grated
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the grated onion, yogurt, olive oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the cubed chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for deeper flavor.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers.
  4. Grill the kebabs on medium heat for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Serve with flatbread and a side of hummus or tahini.

8. Hummus (Chickpea Dip)

Hummus is a beloved Middle Eastern dip made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic. It’s creamy, smooth, and perfect as an appetizer or snack. Whether spread on pita bread or used as a dip for vegetables, hummus is a versatile dish that’s sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and salt until smooth.
  2. If the hummus is too thick, add a little water to achieve the desired consistency.
  3. Transfer to a serving dish, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with paprika.
  4. Serve with pita bread or raw vegetables.

9. Manakish (Lebanese Flatbread with Zaatar)

Manakish is a popular Lebanese breakfast item, often enjoyed with a warm cup of tea. This flatbread is topped with a mixture of zaatar (a Middle Eastern herb blend), olive oil, and sesame seeds, creating a savory and aromatic dish that’s perfect for a comforting morning meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup zaatar spice blend
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, salt, olive oil, and warm water. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  2. Let the dough rise for about 1 hour, until doubled in size.
  3. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). Roll out the dough into small flatbreads.
  4. Mix the zaatar and olive oil, then spread it evenly on top of the dough.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes, until the bread is golden and crispy.
  6. Serve warm for a delicious breakfast or snack.

10. Sfeeha (Lebanese Meat Pies)

Sfeeha are savory meat pies typically filled with spiced ground lamb or beef, pine nuts, and onions. These bite-sized pastries are perfect for serving at parties or as a comforting snack. The spiced meat filling wrapped in flaky dough creates a mouthwatering combination that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons pine nuts
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm water

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté the onions until they are soft, then add the ground meat. Cook until browned.
  2. Stir in the pine nuts, cinnamon, allspice, salt, and pepper. Let the filling cool.
  3. For the dough, mix the flour, yeast, sugar, salt, and warm water to form a soft dough. Let it rise for 1 hour.
  4. Roll out small pieces of dough and fill them with the meat mixture.
  5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve hot as an appetizer or snack.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern comfort food is all about rich flavors, hearty ingredients, and the warmth that food can provide. From soups and salads to meat dishes and savory pies, these recipes will bring the soul-soothing essence of the Middle East to your table. Whether you’re cooking for family, friends, or simply treating yourself to a comforting meal, these dishes are sure to bring joy and satisfaction with every bite.
