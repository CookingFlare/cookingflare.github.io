Quick and Easy Focaccia Bread: Perfect for Beginners

Focaccia bread is a delicious, Italian oven-baked flatbread that’s soft on the inside and crispy on the outside. The ideal bread for any occasion, it’s scrumptious on its own or served alongside a bowl of soup, as a sandwich or even as a tasty snack. With this simple and straightforward recipe, even the most inexperienced cooks can create a delectable homemade focaccia in no time. Ingredients 4 cups all-purpose flour 1 tbsp sugar 2 tsp active dry yeast 2 tsp salt 1 1/2 cups warm water 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil Optional: fresh herbs, dried oregano, rosemary, or basil Instructions Combine the ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Mix well. Add water: Gradually pour the warm water into the dry ingredients, stirring constantly. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable. Add olive oil: Add 3 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil and mix until the dough becomes a little more elastic. Knead the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and consistent. If it seems too sticky, you can add a little more flour. First, let it rest: Place the dough back into the bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rest in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size. Prepare your pan: Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan with extra-virgin olive oil and preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Punch down and shape: After the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to the prepared baking pan. Stretch and shape the dough to form a uniform rectangle, about 1/2-inch thick. Add toppings: Insert your choice of fresh herbs or dried oregano, rosemary, or basil into the dough generously. Don’t be shy; the more fragrance, the better! Let it rise again: Let the dough rise in the pan for another 15-20 minutes, or until it becomes slightly puffed and has nice air pockets. Time to bake: Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for about 20 minutes, or until the crust is a deep golden brown. Cool and serve: Once the bread is baked, remove it from the oven and cool on a wire rack for about 10 minutes before cutting into squares or triangles and serving. Tips If you do not have a warm place to let the dough rise, you can place the dough in a turned-off oven with the lights turned on or near a warm radiator. Adding some garlic to the olive oil can provide a robust and delicious flavor. Substituting part of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour can give the focaccia a bit more texture. The charm of homemade focaccia lies in its simplicity and the comforting tangy flavor. Enjoy it hot out of the oven, warm the next day or serve it at room temperature as a beautiful side dish. Try out this quick and easy recipe and show off your culinary genius at your next gathering or meal. Happy cooking! ...
