Roasted Cauliflower with Turmeric and Cumin: A Flavorful Fall Side

There’s something about the crispness in the air during fall that makes us crave warm and comforting dishes. And what better way to embrace the flavors of the season than with a delicious and nutritious side dish? Enter roasted cauliflower with turmeric and cumin – a flavorful combination that will leave your taste buds singing. Ingredients 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon turmeric 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional) Instructions Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, combine the cauliflower florets, olive oil, turmeric, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Toss gently until the cauliflower is evenly coated with the spices and oil. Transfer the cauliflower to the prepared baking sheet, spreading it out in a single layer. This will ensure even roasting and maximize the flavor. Roast the cauliflower in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until it is golden brown and tender. Be sure to give the cauliflower a gentle toss halfway through the cooking time to ensure even browning. Once roasted, remove the cauliflower from the oven and let it cool slightly. Garnish with fresh cilantro, if desired, for an added burst of freshness. Serve the roasted cauliflower as a side dish alongside your favorite protein or as a flavorful addition to salads, grain bowls, or wraps. Health Benefits Not only is roasted cauliflower with turmeric and cumin a delicious fall side dish, but it also packs a nutritional punch. Cauliflower is a cruciferous vegetable that is rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. It is known for its anti-inflammatory properties and is a great source of antioxidants. Turmeric, on the other hand, contains curcumin, a compound with powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Cumin, a spice commonly used in Indian cuisine, is not only flavorful but also aids digestion and may have antimicrobial properties. ...
