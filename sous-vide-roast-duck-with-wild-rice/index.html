Sous Vide Roast Duck with Wild Rice


When it comes to preparing a decadent, restaurant-quality dish at home, few methods offer the same precision and ease as sous vide cooking. If you’re ready to elevate your cooking game and impress your guests, there’s no better choice than sous vide roast duck with wild rice. This flavorful, tender dish combines the rich taste of duck with the nutty texture of wild rice, offering a satisfying and unique experience for your taste buds.

In this guide, we’ll take you through each step of cooking sous vide duck breast, paired with a beautifully cooked wild rice side dish that complements the richness of the meat. Whether you’re an experienced sous vide chef or a newcomer, this recipe is designed to deliver a perfect, tender roast duck every time, without the hassle.

Why Choose Sous Vide for Duck?

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique where food is sealed in a bag and cooked in a water bath at a precise, low temperature for an extended period of time. The main benefit of this method is that it allows the food to cook evenly and retain its natural juices, resulting in an incredibly tender texture and enhanced flavor.

For duck, which can often be tricky to cook just right, sous vide is a game-changer. Traditional roasting or pan-searing methods can easily leave duck either undercooked or overly dry, but with sous vide, the duck breast is cooked to the perfect level of doneness without any risk of overcooking.

Additionally, sous vide cooking allows you to infuse flavors into the meat by adding herbs and spices directly into the bag. This ensures that every bite is bursting with flavor, while the duck remains moist and succulent.

Equipment You Will Need

Before diving into the recipe, here’s a quick overview of the equipment needed for sous vide cooking:

  • Sous Vide Precision Cooker: This device regulates the temperature of the water bath to ensure even cooking.
  • Vacuum Sealer or Zip-Top Bags: Used to seal the duck breasts along with any seasonings.
  • Large Pot or Container: For the water bath.
  • Skillet (Optional): To sear the duck after sous vide cooking for a crispy skin.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Roast Duck

For the duck, we’ll keep it simple with just a few key ingredients to enhance the natural flavors of the meat. Here’s what you’ll need:

Duck:

  • 2 boneless duck breasts, skin-on
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for searing)

Wild Rice:

  • 1 cup wild rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh herbs (parsley or thyme), for garnish

Preparing the Duck for Sous Vide

The first step in preparing the sous vide duck is seasoning the duck breasts. Begin by patting the duck breasts dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This is important because any moisture left on the skin will prevent it from getting crispy later.

Next, generously season both sides of the duck breasts with salt and freshly ground black pepper. This simple seasoning will help bring out the natural flavors of the duck. If you like, you can also add some garlic and fresh rosemary to infuse the duck with additional aromatics. Simply place the garlic cloves and rosemary sprigs on the duck breast before sealing.

Once seasoned, place the duck breasts in a vacuum-seal bag or a zip-top bag if you’re using the water displacement method. Vacuum-seal the bag, ensuring that the meat is tightly sealed but not squished. The goal is to keep all the seasoning in contact with the duck and prevent any air from interfering with the cooking process.

Cooking the Duck Sous Vide

Now that the duck is prepped and ready, it’s time to cook it sous vide. Fill your large pot or container with water and set your sous vide precision cooker to 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare duck. If you prefer a slightly more well-done duck, you can increase the temperature to 140°F (60°C).

Submerge the sealed duck breasts into the water bath, making sure the bag is fully submerged. Let the duck cook for 2 to 3 hours. The beauty of sous vide is that you don’t have to monitor the cooking process closely; the low, steady temperature ensures that the duck will be cooked perfectly, no matter when you take it out within that time window.

Once the duck is cooked, carefully remove it from the bag. You can now proceed to searing it to achieve a golden, crispy skin. This step is optional but highly recommended for adding a delightful contrast to the tender, juicy meat.

Searing the Duck (Optional but Recommended)

To achieve a beautiful, crispy skin, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, place the duck breasts skin-side down in the skillet. Sear for 2 to 3 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the duck breasts over and sear the other side for another minute to warm the meat.

This quick sear adds a wonderful texture and caramelized flavor to the duck, taking the dish to the next level.

Cooking the Wild Rice

While the duck is cooking, it’s time to prepare the wild rice. Wild rice is a fantastic accompaniment to duck, with its nutty flavor and chewy texture perfectly complementing the richness of the meat.

To cook the wild rice, start by rinsing it thoroughly under cold water to remove any excess starch. In a medium saucepan, combine the wild rice with chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian version). Add a pinch of salt, cover, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the rice simmer for 40 to 45 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.

Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork, and stir in a tablespoon of butter for added richness. Season with freshly ground black pepper and adjust the salt to taste. You can also garnish the rice with chopped fresh herbs like parsley or thyme to brighten up the dish.

Plating the Dish

To plate your sous vide roast duck with wild rice, start by arranging a generous scoop of the wild rice on each plate. Slice the duck breasts on a slight bias, showcasing the tender, rosy meat inside. Lay the slices of duck over the wild rice, and drizzle with any rendered duck fat from the searing process for extra flavor.

You can add a simple garnish of fresh herbs or a light sauce if you like. A balsamic reduction or an orange glaze would complement the dish beautifully, but the duck and wild rice are flavorful enough on their own.

Tips for Perfect Duck Every Time

  • Rest the Duck: After searing the duck, let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring the meat remains juicy.
  • Temperature Control: If you don’t have a sous vide precision cooker, you can cook duck in the oven at a low temperature (around 300°F) for similar results, though it won’t be quite as tender.
  • Flavor Variations: Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices. For instance, thyme, sage, or a touch of orange zest can complement duck beautifully.
  • Wild Rice Variations: Wild rice can be customized with additional ingredients, like sautéed mushrooms, cranberries, or toasted nuts. Don’t be afraid to get creative with your sides.

Conclusion

Sous vide roast duck with wild rice is an exquisite dish that’s surprisingly easy to prepare at home. The sous vide method ensures your duck turns out perfectly tender, while the wild rice side brings texture and earthiness to balance the richness of the meat. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or preparing a special meal for yourself, this dish is sure to impress. With a little patience and the right technique, you can enjoy a gourmet-quality meal in the comfort of your own kitchen.
