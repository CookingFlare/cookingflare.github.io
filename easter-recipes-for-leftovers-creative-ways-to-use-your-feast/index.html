Easter Recipes for Leftovers: Creative Ways to Use Your Feast


Easter is a time of celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, a feast that often leaves us with a bounty of delicious leftovers. While it’s tempting to simply reheat the same dishes the next day, there are countless creative ways to transform those Easter leftovers into exciting new meals. In this post, we’ll explore innovative recipes that will not only help you reduce food waste but also delight your taste buds. Let’s dive into the world of Easter leftovers and discover how to make the most of your holiday feast!

1. Savory Easter Ham and Cheese Quiche

One of the most popular Easter dishes is glazed ham, and it often leaves you with more than enough to spare. A savory quiche is a fantastic way to repurpose that ham into a delightful breakfast or brunch dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 cup diced Easter ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Swiss, or your favorite)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: diced bell peppers or spinach for added flavor

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the pie crust in a pie dish and layer the diced ham, cheese, and green onions (and any optional veggies) evenly on the bottom.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the top, ensuring everything is well combined.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the top is golden brown.
  6. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy your delicious ham and cheese quiche!

2. Creamy Potato and Pea Soup

If you have leftover mashed potatoes from your Easter dinner, turning them into a creamy soup is a fantastic option. This comforting dish is perfect for a light lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (like parsley or chives)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic over medium heat until translucent.
  2. Add the vegetable or chicken broth and bring to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the leftover mashed potatoes and frozen peas, mixing well.
  4. Allow the soup to simmer for about 10 minutes, then blend until smooth using an immersion blender or a regular blender.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs. This creamy potato and pea soup is sure to warm your soul!

3. Easter Leftover Salad

Transform your leftover roasted vegetables and proteins into a vibrant salad that’s both nutritious and satisfying. This dish is perfect for a light lunch or as a side for dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover roasted vegetables (carrots, Brussels sprouts, asparagus, etc.)
  • 1 cup diced leftover turkey or ham
  • 4 cups mixed greens (spinach, arugula, or your choice)
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese or goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup nuts (walnuts, almonds, or pecans)
  • Olive oil and balsamic vinegar for dressing
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, roasted vegetables, diced turkey or ham, cherry tomatoes, and cheese.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Toss gently to combine all ingredients.
  4. Top with nuts for added crunch and serve immediately. This colorful salad is a great way to enjoy your leftovers while adding freshness to your meal!

4. Easter Egg Fried Rice

If you have leftover hard-boiled eggs from your Easter egg hunt, why not turn them into a delicious fried rice dish? This recipe is quick, easy, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté until tender.
  3. Stir in the cooked rice, breaking up any clumps, and cook for a few minutes until heated through.
