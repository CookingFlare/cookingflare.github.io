Spicy Creole Jambalaya with Shrimp and Sausage


If you’re looking to add some Creole flair to your dinner table, then this Spicy Creole Jambalaya with Shrimp and Sausage is the perfect recipe for you. This dish is a true representation of the vibrant and diverse flavors of Louisiana, blending the richness of sausage, the succulence of shrimp, and the bold spices of Creole cuisine. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a hearty and flavorful meal, this jambalaya is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 oz andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced andouille sausage and cook until browned, then transfer to a plate and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion and bell peppers. Sauté until they begin to soften, then add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  3. Stir in the fire-roasted diced tomatoes, paprika, dried oregano, dried thyme, and cayenne pepper. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the vegetable and spice mixture. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Once the jambalaya is boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid.

  6. While the jambalaya is simmering, season the peeled and deveined shrimp with salt and black pepper. After the rice has cooked, gently fold in the seasoned shrimp and the cooked andouille sausage.

  7. Cover the pot again and let the jambalaya cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp is pink and cooked through.

  8. Once the shrimp is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  9. Before serving, garnish the jambalaya with chopped fresh parsley for a vibrant finish.

Serving Suggestions

This Spicy Creole Jambalaya with Shrimp and Sausage is a complete meal in itself, but it also pairs wonderfully with a side of crusty French bread or a simple green salad. For a true taste of New Orleans, consider serving it alongside some cornbread or hushpuppies.

Final Thoughts

The Spicy Creole Jambalaya with Shrimp and Sausage is a celebration of bold flavors and comforting textures, making it an ideal choice for a cozy dinner at home or a festive gathering with friends and family. The combination of succulent shrimp, smoky andouille sausage, and aromatic spices creates a dish that is both satisfying and memorable. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the essence of Creole cuisine with this delightful jambalaya!
