How to Make Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal)


Korean cuisine is celebrated for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and the perfect balance of spicy, sweet, and savory elements. One of the most beloved dishes is Samgyeopsal, which is typically grilled pork belly served with a variety of accompaniments. However, this recipe takes a creative twist on the traditional method by turning it into a flavorful and spicy stir-fry, making it perfect for a weeknight dinner or an impressive dish to serve guests.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the steps to make Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal), also known as Samgyeopsal-bokkeum. The dish combines the rich and tender pork belly with a spicy marinade, vegetables, and a hint of sweetness. It’s easy to prepare, incredibly flavorful, and can be customized to suit your spice tolerance. Whether you’ve had it at a Korean BBQ restaurant or this is your first time making it, you’re in for a treat!

Ingredients for Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal)

To create this spicy stir-fry, you will need the following ingredients. These components come together to build a dish that is satisfying, spicy, and packed with umami.

For the Pork Belly Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) pork belly – Ideally, thinly sliced for quick stir-frying.
  • 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste) – The key ingredient for the spicy kick.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce – For saltiness and depth of flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar – To balance the richness of the pork.
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar – For a touch of sweetness.
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil – Adds a nutty aroma and richness.
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic – For a savory base.
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger – To brighten up the dish with a fresh, aromatic flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – This enhances the heat and gives a beautiful red color to the dish.

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 1 medium onion – Thinly sliced to add crunch and sweetness.
  • 1 bell pepper – Thinly sliced; red or yellow works best to bring color and sweetness.
  • 1 zucchini – Sliced into half-moons for texture.
  • 1 cup kimchi – Optional but adds a tangy depth to the stir-fry.
  • 2 green onions – Chopped for garnish and added flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil – For frying.

For Serving:

  • Steamed rice – To soak up all the flavorful sauce.
  • Sesame seeds – For garnish.
  • Lettuce leaves – Optional, for wrapping the pork belly stir-fry, Korean BBQ style.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry

Follow these simple steps to create this mouthwatering Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry. The dish is relatively easy to prepare, but the flavor profile will leave your taste buds dancing with delight!

Step 1: Prepare the Pork Belly

Start by slicing the pork belly into thin strips, about 1/4 inch wide. The thinner the slices, the quicker and more evenly they will cook during the stir-frying process. Pork belly is the star of this dish, and its tender, fatty texture makes it the perfect choice. If you can’t find pre-sliced pork belly at your local market, you can also buy a whole piece and slice it yourself.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a medium-sized bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic, minced ginger, and gochugaru. Mix everything together thoroughly until the sugar is dissolved, and the marinade is smooth. This spicy-sweet marinade will infuse the pork belly with bold flavors as it cooks. Feel free to adjust the amount of gochugaru or gochujang depending on your heat tolerance.

Step 3: Marinate the Pork Belly

Add the sliced pork belly to the marinade and toss it well so that each piece is coated evenly. Allow the pork belly to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour, but if you have more time, marinating it overnight in the fridge will deepen the flavors.

Step 4: Prepare the Vegetables

While the pork belly is marinating, prepare the vegetables. Slice the onion, bell pepper, and zucchini into thin pieces, and set them aside. If you’re using kimchi, chop it into smaller pieces so that it can be easily incorporated into the stir-fry.

Step 5: Stir-Fry the Pork Belly

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and add vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork belly to the skillet. Stir-fry the pork belly for about 5-7 minutes until it starts to get golden and crispy on the edges. The pork will release some of its fat during this process, which will help with the cooking. Keep stirring to prevent it from burning and ensure that it cooks evenly.

Step 6: Add the Vegetables

Once the pork belly has started to brown and crisp up, add the sliced onion, bell pepper, and zucchini to the skillet. Stir-fry for another 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still slightly crunchy. If you’re using kimchi, add it at this point and stir everything together. The kimchi will give the dish an extra layer of flavor, adding tanginess and a little bit of fermentation that complements the richness of the pork belly.

Step 7: Final Touches

Once the pork and vegetables are fully cooked, check the seasoning. If you prefer a sweeter taste, add a bit more brown sugar, or if you like more heat, increase the gochujang or gochugaru. Toss the stir-fry to ensure everything is well combined and evenly coated in the spicy sauce.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the stir-fry to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Serve the Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry over steamed rice to soak up the flavorful sauce. You can also serve it with fresh lettuce leaves on the side, which can be used to wrap the stir-fry and enjoy it in a Korean BBQ style.

Tips for Perfect Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry

  1. Adjust the Spice Level: Korean cuisine can be quite spicy, but the level of heat is customizable. If you’re sensitive to spice, reduce the amount of gochugaru or gochujang. Alternatively, you can add a little more sugar to balance out the heat.

  2. Use a Cast-Iron Skillet: If you have one, a cast-iron skillet is great for achieving the crispy, caramelized edges on the pork belly. It holds heat well and helps evenly cook the pork.

  3. Serve with Side Dishes: This stir-fry pairs wonderfully with traditional Korean side dishes like banchan (small side dishes) such as kimchi, pickled radish, or sautéed spinach. These side dishes will balance the richness of the pork belly and add a variety of flavors to your meal.

  4. Use Fresh Ingredients: For the best flavor, use fresh vegetables, especially the zucchini and bell pepper. The crunchiness of these vegetables adds texture and freshness to the stir-fry.

  5. Optional Garnishes: For an added touch, you can top the stir-fry with toasted seaweed flakes or a drizzle of extra sesame oil for an aromatic finish.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal-bokkeum) is an incredible dish that combines the rich flavors of tender pork belly, the heat of gochujang, and the freshness of vegetables. It’s the perfect way to enjoy the bold, spicy flavors of Korean BBQ right in your kitchen. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or guests, this dish is sure to impress with its vibrant colors, addictive flavors, and delightful texture.

With its balanced mix of savory, sweet, and spicy elements, this stir-fry is a versatile and comforting meal that works well for any occasion. It’s easy to make, customizable to your taste preferences, and pairs perfectly with steamed rice and a variety of side dishes. So, gather your ingredients, and let’s get cooking! Enjoy the delicious experience of Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry at home.
