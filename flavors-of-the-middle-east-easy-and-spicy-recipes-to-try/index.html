Flavors of the Middle East: Easy and Spicy Recipes to Try


The Middle East is renowned for its rich and vibrant culinary heritage. The region’s cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors and spices that tantalize the taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore some easy and spicy recipes inspired by the Middle East, allowing you to bring the exotic flavors of this region into your own kitchen.

1. Shawarma Chicken Wraps

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern street food that is loved for its succulent and aromatic flavors. To make Shawarma Chicken Wraps, marinate boneless chicken thighs in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, and a blend of spices such as cumin, paprika, and turmeric. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least an hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.

Once marinated, grill or pan-fry the chicken until cooked through and slightly charred. Slice the chicken into thin strips. Warm up some flatbread or tortillas, and fill them with the chicken, along with fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomatoes, and cucumbers. Top it off with a generous drizzle of tahini sauce for added creaminess.

2. Falafel with Tahini Dressing

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices, and then deep-fried to perfection. To make falafel, combine soaked and drained chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, coriander, cumin, and a touch of baking powder in a food processor. Blend until the mixture forms a coarse paste.

Shape the mixture into small patties and deep-fry them until golden brown and crispy. Serve the falafel with a refreshing tahini dressing made from tahini paste, lemon juice, garlic, and water. The creamy and nutty flavors of the tahini perfectly complement the crispy falafel.

3. Spicy Harissa Lamb Skewers

Harissa is a spicy chili paste commonly used in North African and Middle Eastern cuisines. It adds a fiery kick to any dish it is added to. For these Spicy Harissa Lamb Skewers, marinate chunks of lamb in a mixture of harissa paste, olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, and a medley of aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and cinnamon.

Thread the marinated lamb onto skewers and grill them until cooked to your desired doneness. The smoky flavors of the grilled lamb combined with the heat of the harissa create a tantalizing taste experience. Serve the skewers with a side of couscous or a fresh salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

4. Moroccan Spiced Carrot Soup

This Moroccan-inspired carrot soup is a comforting and flavorful dish that showcases the spices of the Middle East. In a large pot, sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant. Add in diced carrots, vegetable broth, and a mixture of spices such as cumin, turmeric, and cinnamon. Simmer the soup until the carrots are tender.

Once cooked, blend the soup until smooth and creamy. Adjust the seasoning to your liking, and serve it hot with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a drizzle of olive oil. The warm and earthy flavors of this soup make it the perfect comfort food for a chilly evening.

Final Thoughts

The Middle East is a treasure trove of flavors and spices that can elevate any dish to new heights. These easy and spicy recipes are just a glimpse into the culinary wonders of this region. So, take a culinary journey to the Middle East and discover the joy of cooking with these exotic flavors in your own kitchen.
