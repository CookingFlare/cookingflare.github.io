5 Amazing Chinese Dumpling Recipes for Beginners and Advanced Cooks

Dumplings, a beloved Chinese dish, come in an assortment of shapes, sizes, and fillings. Whether you’re a novice cook or a seasoned chef, there’s always a dumpling recipe to delight your taste buds.Below are five fantastic dumpling recipes that will transport your palate straight to the heart of China. 1. Classic Pork and Chive Dumplings Ingredients: 1 lb ground pork 1 cup chopped chives 4 green onions, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tbsp soy sauce 2 tsp sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste 2 packages of dumpling wrappers Cincinnati chili (optional) Instructions: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chives, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, pepper, and mix well. Lay out the dumpling wrappers and place a small spoonful of the pork mixture in the center of each wrapper. To seal the dumplings, you can either pleat the edges (for an elegant finish) or simply fold and pinch the edges together. If using Cincinnati chili, prepare it according to your preferred recipe. To cook, place the dumplings in a steamer basket, ensuring they don’t touch each other. Steam for 10-12 minutes, or until the wrappers turn a light gold color. Alternatively, you can fry or boil the dumplings, although steaming helps to keep them moist. Drain off any excess water or oil from the dumplings and serve with the prepared Cincinnati chili as dipping sauce. 2. Vegetable Dumplings Ingredients: 1 cup shredded cabbage 1 cup finely chopped carrots 1/2 cup chopped leeks 1/4 cup chopped enoki mushrooms 1/4 cup chopped water chestnuts 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 egg 2 tbsp soy sauce 2 tsp sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste 2 packages of dumpling wrappers Soy-ginger sauce for dipping Instructions: In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, carrots, leeks, enoki mushrooms, water chestnuts, garlic, egg, soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt and pepper. Mix the ingredients well. Lay out the dumpling wrappers and place a small spoonful of the vegetable mixture in the center of each wrapper. Seal the dumplings by pleating or folding and pinching the edges together. Prepare the soy-ginger sauce by combining 2 tbsp of soy sauce, 1 tbsp of minced ginger, and a pinch of sugar. To cook, you can steam, fry, or boil the dumplings in a similar manner to the pork and chive dumplings above. Steam for 10-12 minutes in a steamer basket or fry/boil until the wrappers turn a light gold color. Serve the cooked dumplings with the soy-ginger sauce for dipping. 3. Shrimp and Scallop Dumplings Ingredients: 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 cup scallops, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tbsp ginger, grated 2 tbsp soy sauce 2 tbsp sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste 2 packages of dumpling wrappers Spicy red chili sauce for dipping Instructions: In a mixing bowl, combine the shrimp, scallops, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Lay out the dumpling wrappers and place a small spoonful of the shrimp and scallop mixture in the center of each wrapper. Seal the dumplings by pleating or folding and pinching the edges together. Prepare the spicy red chili sauce by combining your preferred store-bought or homemade red chili sauce with a bit of water or soy sauce to thin it out. To cook, steam, fry, or boil the dumplings as directed in the previous recipes. Steam for 10-12 minutes, or fry/boil until the wrappers turn a light gold color. Serve the cooked dumplings with the spicy red chili sauce for dipping. 4. Pan-Fried Pork Dumplings Ingredients: 1 lb ground pork 1 cup finely chopped napa cabbage 1 cup chopped green onions 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tbsp ginger, grated 2 tbsp soy sauce 2 tsp sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste 2 packages of dumpling wrappers 1/4 cup vegetable oil Hot mustard sauce for dipping Instructions: In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, pepper, and mix well. Lay out the dumpling wrappers and place a small spoonful of the pork mixture in the center of each wrapper. To seal the dumplings, you can pleat the edges, ensuring that both halves are sealed together to prevent any filling from spilling out during cooking. Prepare the hot mustard sauce by combining mustard powder, water, and a bit of soy sauce to your desired consistency and spiciness. In a large non-stick frying pan, pour in the vegetable oil and heat over medium heat. Working in batches, place the dumplings in the pan, ensuring they don’t touch each other, and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until the bottoms turn a light golden brown. Pour in a small amount of water to the pan, enough to come halfway up the sides of the dumplings. Cover the pan with a lid and let steam for about 7-8 minutes, or until the water has evaporated and the wrappers are crisp and golden. Serve the pan-fried dumplings hot with the hot mustard sauce for dipping. 5. Szechuan Spicy Dumplings Ingredients: 1 lb ground pork 1 cup chopped green onions 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns, ground 2 tbsp soy sauce 1 tbsp chili bean paste 1 tbsp Chinkiang vinegar 1 tsp sugar 2 packages of dumpling wrappers Dipping sauce (peanut sauce, soy sauce, or chili oil mixed together) Instructions: In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, green onions, garlic, Szechuan peppercorns, soy sauce, chili bean paste, vinegar, sugar, and mix well. Lay out the dumpling wrappers and place a small spoonful of the pork mixture in the center of each wrapper. To seal the dumplings, pleat or fold the edges together. To cook, steam, fry, or boil the dumplings as previously mentioned, for 10-12 minutes, or until the wrappers turn a light gold color. Serve the cooked dumplings with your dipping sauce of choice. These five dumpling recipes offer varying flavors to satisfy any dumpling lover. Regardless of skill level, one is sure to find a recipe that will be the perfect addition to their culinary arsenal. ...
