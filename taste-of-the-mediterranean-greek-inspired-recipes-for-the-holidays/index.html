Taste of the Mediterranean: Greek-Inspired Recipes for the Holidays


The holidays are a time for gathering with loved ones, celebrating traditions, and indulging in delicious food. This year, why not bring a taste of the Mediterranean to your holiday table with some delightful Greek-inspired recipes? From mouth-watering appetizers to hearty main courses and decadent desserts, these Greek dishes will add a flavorful twist to your festive meals.

Appetizers

Spanakopita (Spinach Pie)

Spanakopita is a classic Greek appetizer that features layers of flaky phyllo dough filled with a savory mixture of spinach, feta cheese, and herbs. It’s perfect for serving as an appetizer or side dish during your holiday feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed
  • 1 cup butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onions and garlic until soft.
  3. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool.
  4. In a mixing bowl, combine the spinach mixture, feta cheese, ricotta cheese, dill, parsley, eggs, salt, and pepper.
  5. Brush a baking dish with melted butter. Lay a sheet of phyllo dough in the dish and brush with more butter. Repeat with several layers.
  6. Spread the spinach mixture evenly over the phyllo layers.
  7. Continue layering phyllo sheets on top, brushing each layer with butter.
  8. Bake for 45 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool slightly before cutting into squares.

Tzatziki Sauce

Tzatziki is a refreshing Greek yogurt-based sauce that’s perfect for dipping vegetables or pita bread. It’s also a great accompaniment to grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the grated cucumber in a clean towel and squeeze out excess moisture.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the yogurt, cucumber, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, dill, and salt.
  3. Mix well and refrigerate for at least one hour before serving.

Main Courses

Moussaka

Moussaka is a quintessential Greek comfort food, featuring layers of eggplant, seasoned ground meat, and a creamy béchamel sauce. It’s a hearty and satisfying dish that’s perfect for the holiday table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • Salt
  • Olive oil for frying
  • 1 pound ground lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk, warmed
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Sprinkle the eggplant slices with salt and let them sit for 30 minutes to draw out excess moisture. Rinse and pat dry.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat and fry the eggplant slices until golden brown. Set aside on paper towels to drain.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the onion and garlic until soft. Add the ground meat and cook until browned.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, cinnamon, allspice, red wine, parsley, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  6. To make the béchamel sauce, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for a minute. Gradually add the warmed milk, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Let cool slightly, then whisk in the beaten eggs.
  7. In a baking dish, layer half of the eggplant slices, followed by the meat sauce, then the remaining eggplant slices. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top.
  8. Bake for 45 minutes until the top is golden brown and bubbly. Let cool slightly before serving.

Souvlaki (Grilled Meat Skewers)

Souvlaki is a popular Greek street food that consists of marinated meat skewers grilled to perfection. These skewers are versatile and can be made with chicken, pork, or lamb.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds meat of your choice (chicken, pork, or lamb), cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the meat cubes to the marinade and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best flavor.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and nicely charred.
  6. Serve with pita bread, Tzatziki sauce, and a side salad.

Side Dishes

Greek Salad

A traditional Greek salad is a fresh and vibrant side dish that complements any meal. It’s made with crisp vegetables, olives, feta cheese, and a simple dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, onion, bell pepper, and olives.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Sprinkle the feta cheese on top before serving.

Lemon Potatoes

Greek lemon potatoes are a zesty and flavorful side dish that pairs perfectly with grilled meats or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large baking dish, toss the potato wedges with olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the chicken broth into the dish around the potatoes.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, turning occasionally, until the potatoes are golden brown and tender.

Desserts

Baklava

Baklava is a rich and sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and a honey syrup. It’s a traditional Greek dessert that’s perfect for the holidays.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed
  • 2 cups chopped walnuts
  • 2 cups chopped almonds
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the chopped nuts and ground cinnamon.
  3. Brush a baking dish with melted butter. Lay a sheet of phyllo dough in the dish and brush with more butter. Repeat with several layers.
  4. Sprinkle a layer of the nut mixture over the phyllo layers.
  5. Continue layering phyllo sheets and nut mixture, brushing each layer with butter, until all the nut mixture is used. Top with several layers of phyllo dough, brushing each with butter.
  6. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  8. While the baklava is baking, make the syrup by combining the honey, sugar, water, vanilla extract, and lemon zest in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  9. Pour the hot syrup over the baked baklava. Let it cool completely before serving.

Kourabiedes (Greek Butter Cookies)

Kourabiedes are traditional Greek butter cookies that are often enjoyed during the holidays. They are crumbly, buttery, and dusted with powdered sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup ground almonds
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the egg yolk and vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, ground almonds, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. Shape the dough into small balls or crescent shapes and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes until the edges are lightly golden.
  8. Let the cookies cool slightly, then dust generously with powdered sugar.

Conclusion

Bringing a taste of the Mediterranean to your holiday celebrations with these Greek-inspired recipes is sure to delight your family and friends. The vibrant flavors and comforting dishes will create a warm and inviting atmosphere, perfect for making cherished memories. Enjoy the holidays with the rich culinary traditions of Greece, and may your table be filled with love, laughter, and delicious food.
