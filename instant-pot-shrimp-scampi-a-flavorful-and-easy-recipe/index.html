Instant Pot Shrimp Scampi: A Flavorful and Easy Recipe


Shrimp scampi is a beloved dish known for its rich flavors, buttery sauce, and tender shrimp. Traditionally prepared by sautéing shrimp in garlic, butter, and white wine, this Italian-American classic has been a favorite for home cooks and professional chefs alike. However, what if you could achieve the same depth of flavor and succulent shrimp using your Instant Pot, all in under 30 minutes?

In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making Instant Pot shrimp scampi, a quick and easy version of the classic dish that’s perfect for busy weeknights, special occasions, or a casual dinner with friends and family. The Instant Pot simplifies the cooking process, ensuring that you get a dish that’s flavorful, satisfying, and bursting with that signature garlic-butter goodness.

Why Choose the Instant Pot for Shrimp Scampi?

The Instant Pot is an excellent tool for preparing shrimp scampi because it’s a versatile kitchen appliance that can sauté, pressure cook, and even keep food warm. By using the Instant Pot, we can streamline the cooking process, infuse the shrimp with flavor more efficiently, and achieve the perfect balance of textures and tastes in record time. The pressure cooking feature locks in moisture and concentrates flavors, ensuring that your shrimp remains tender, juicy, and full of flavor.

Furthermore, the Instant Pot makes cleaning up a breeze. There’s no need for multiple pans or worry about dirtying every surface of your kitchen. The dish comes together in one pot, saving you time and effort, which makes it a fantastic option for busy evenings when you want a restaurant-quality meal without the hassle.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Shrimp Scampi

To prepare this Instant Pot shrimp scampi recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Shrimp: Use medium to large-sized shrimp, preferably peeled and deveined. Frozen shrimp will work as well, but if using frozen, make sure to thaw them before cooking.
  • Butter: A generous amount of unsalted butter provides the signature richness of scampi. You’ll need both for sautéing and for finishing the dish.
  • Olive Oil: This helps to prevent the butter from burning during the sautéing process.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a beautiful aromatic base to the sauce.
  • Lemon: Fresh lemon juice and zest add a refreshing citrusy kick to the dish, balancing the richness of the butter.
  • White Wine: Dry white wine, such as Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay, is the perfect ingredient to deglaze the pot and add depth of flavor.
  • Chicken Broth: Adds additional flavor to the sauce and helps build the base of the scampi.
  • Parsley: Fresh parsley provides color and a fresh, herbal note that complements the buttery sauce.
  • Red Pepper Flakes: Optional, but a pinch of red pepper flakes adds a subtle heat that elevates the overall flavor profile of the dish.
  • Pasta: Linguine, spaghetti, or any pasta of your choice. You’ll cook the pasta in the Instant Pot alongside the shrimp to absorb the delicious sauce.

How to Make Instant Pot Shrimp Scampi

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this flavorful and easy shrimp scampi in the Instant Pot.

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

If you’re using frozen shrimp, make sure they’re fully thawed and patted dry with paper towels. This will help to ensure they cook evenly and don’t release too much water into the sauce. Season the shrimp lightly with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Sauté the Garlic and Shrimp

Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter until melted. Add the garlic to the pot and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn it. The garlic should be golden but not brown.

Next, add the shrimp to the pot in a single layer. Sauté the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side, just until they start to turn pink. You don’t want to fully cook the shrimp at this point, as they will cook more during the pressure cooking phase. Once the shrimp are partially cooked, remove them from the pot and set them aside.

Step 3: Deglaze the Pot

With the Instant Pot still on sauté mode, add the white wine to the pot to deglaze it. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up any flavorful brown bits from the bottom of the pot. This step is crucial, as those bits are packed with flavor that will enrich the sauce.

Step 4: Add the Liquid and Pasta

Add the chicken broth, lemon juice, lemon zest, and red pepper flakes (if using) to the pot. Stir to combine everything and bring it to a gentle simmer. Add the pasta, making sure it’s submerged in the liquid. It’s important to layer the pasta in a crisscross pattern rather than just piling it all in, as this will help it cook more evenly.

Step 5: Pressure Cook the Pasta

Secure the Instant Pot lid, making sure the valve is set to sealing. Set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 4 minutes if using linguine or spaghetti. If you’re using a different type of pasta, adjust the cooking time according to the package instructions (typically 4-6 minutes for most types of pasta). Once the cooking time is complete, perform a quick release of pressure.

Step 6: Finish the Dish

After releasing the pressure, carefully open the lid of the Instant Pot. Add the cooked shrimp back into the pot, along with the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Stir everything gently to combine, allowing the butter to melt into the sauce and coat the shrimp and pasta.

Taste the dish and adjust seasoning if needed, adding more salt, pepper, or lemon juice to suit your preferences. If the sauce looks too thick, you can add a little more chicken broth or reserved pasta cooking water to reach your desired consistency.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy!

Once everything is well combined, sprinkle the shrimp scampi with fresh chopped parsley for a burst of color and flavor. Serve the dish immediately, ensuring each plate gets a generous helping of shrimp, pasta, and that irresistible garlic-butter sauce.

For an extra touch, you can sprinkle some freshly grated Parmesan cheese on top, or serve the dish with a side of crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

Tips for the Perfect Instant Pot Shrimp Scampi

While this recipe is simple and straightforward, there are a few tips that can take your Instant Pot shrimp scampi to the next level:

  1. Don’t Overcook the Shrimp: Shrimp cook quickly, and they can easily become rubbery if overcooked. Keep a close eye on them during the sautéing phase and remember that they will finish cooking during the pressure cook phase.

  2. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic, parsley, and lemon can make a huge difference in the flavor profile of your dish. While you can use frozen shrimp, fresh shrimp will yield the best results for tenderness and flavor.

  3. Choose the Right Wine: A dry white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay will provide the acidity and depth of flavor needed to balance the richness of the butter. Avoid using sweet wines, as they can make the dish too sugary.

  4. Adjust the Spice Level: If you like a bit of heat, don’t skip the red pepper flakes. You can easily control the spice level by adjusting the amount, so feel free to add more or less depending on your taste.

  5. Pasta Choices: While linguine is the traditional choice for shrimp scampi, you can use other types of pasta, like spaghetti, fettuccine, or even penne. Just ensure that the pasta is submerged in the liquid for even cooking.

  6. Add Vegetables: For a more balanced meal, consider adding vegetables such as spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, or mushrooms. These can be added along with the shrimp in the final steps to infuse even more flavor into the dish.

Conclusion

Instant Pot shrimp scampi is an easy, delicious, and quick way to enjoy a classic Italian-American dish without the fuss. With the help of your Instant Pot, you can prepare a restaurant-quality meal in less time, making it the perfect choice for busy nights when you want something flavorful yet simple to prepare.

The combination of juicy shrimp, buttery sauce, and pasta creates a comforting dish that will surely become a family favorite. Plus, with just one pot to clean, you’ll have more time to relax and enjoy the meal.

Next time you’re in the mood for shrimp scampi, skip the stovetop and reach for your Instant Pot—your taste buds will thank you!
