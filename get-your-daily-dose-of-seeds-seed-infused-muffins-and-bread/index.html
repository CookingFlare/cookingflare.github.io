Get Your Daily Dose of Seeds: Seed-Infused Muffins and Bread


Muffins and bread are staple comfort foods for many of us, whether enjoyed as a quick breakfast or a soothing midday snack. To further uplift these favorites, let’s infuse them with a delightful and nutritious addition – seeds! Seeds are nutrient-rich powerhouses, providing essential micronutrients and minerals for our bodies. Blend them into your daily food routine with these scrumptious seed-infused muffins and bread recipes.

  1. Seed-Infused Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup milk (room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup seeds (a mix of sunflower, chia, flax, and pumpkin seeds)
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • Seeds for garnishing

Steps: Begin by combining the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large mixing bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk together honey, melted butter, egg, and milk, then slowly incorporate the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Fold in the seeds and raisins gently, allowing them to be evenly distributed throughout. Fill greased muffin cups with the batter and sprinkle extra seeds over the top for added texture. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

  1. Seed-Filled Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 1 cup seeds (a mixture of flax, chia, sunflower, and pumpkin seeds)

Steps: First, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and gradually whisk in honey, vanilla extract, and melted butter. Slowly pour in the yeast mixture and stir well. Allow the dough to rest for about 5 minutes, then fold in the seeds gently. Turn the dough out onto a slightly floured surface, knead for a few minutes, and shape into a loaf. Place it in a greased loaf pan, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise for about 30 minutes in a warm area. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake the bread for 25-30 minutes or until the loaf is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.

Introducing seeds into your daily meals can be a fun and delightful way to enhance their flavors and nutrition. Celebrity chefs across the globe are recommending seed-infused baked goods as a tasty way to increase our daily intake of essential micronutrients. So why not join the trend and enjoy a wholesome, healthier version of your favorite comfort foods?

partner with local farmers to source the freshest and most sustainable seeds for your baking endeavors. Support local businesses and enjoy the freshness and variety of seasonal seeds - from sunflower and flax to chia and pumpkin - that nature has to offer. Happy baking!
