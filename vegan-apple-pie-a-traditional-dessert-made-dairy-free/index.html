Vegan Apple Pie: A Traditional Dessert Made Dairy-Free


Apple pie is a beloved dessert that has been passed down through generations. Its flaky crust, warm, spiced filling, and perfect balance of sweet and tart flavors make it a timeless classic. But for those who follow a plant-based or dairy-free lifestyle, the idea of enjoying this traditional dessert can sometimes feel out of reach. Thankfully, with a few simple substitutions, you can make a delicious, dairy-free vegan apple pie that’s just as comforting and mouth-watering as the original.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make a traditional apple pie completely dairy-free, without compromising on flavor or texture. Whether you’re baking for someone with dietary restrictions or simply want to try a new spin on an old favorite, this recipe will give you all the tips and tricks to create a mouthwatering vegan apple pie from scratch.

Why Choose Vegan Apple Pie?

The beauty of apple pie lies in its simplicity. The filling consists mainly of apples, sugar, spices, and sometimes a little lemon juice to balance the sweetness. It’s the crust that usually contains dairy products such as butter. However, by switching out butter for plant-based alternatives, we can create a vegan version of this comforting treat.

By making the pie dairy-free, you’re not only catering to those with dietary restrictions but also supporting plant-based eating. Vegan apple pie is suitable for vegans, people with lactose intolerance, and those who simply prefer a more plant-based lifestyle.

In addition to being dairy-free, this pie is also naturally free of animal by-products and can easily be made gluten-free if necessary. It’s a versatile dessert that everyone can enjoy.

Ingredients for Vegan Apple Pie

For the crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (or gluten-free flour blend)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) vegan butter, chilled and cubed (or coconut oil)
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water (as needed)

For the filling:

  • 6 cups apples, peeled, cored, and sliced (use a mix of tart and sweet apples like Granny Smith and Honeycrisp)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (or coconut sugar for a less refined option)
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (or arrowroot powder)
  • A pinch of salt

For brushing:

  • 2 tablespoons plant-based milk (almond, oat, or coconut milk)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional, for added shine)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

The crust is a key component of apple pie, and when it comes to making it vegan, the most important substitution is replacing the butter. You can use a variety of plant-based fats, but for the best flavor and texture, we recommend vegan butter. It gives the crust that same rich, flaky texture that butter would.

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Cut in the Vegan Butter: Add the cubed vegan butter into the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, fork, or your hands, cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter.
  3. Add Ice Water: Slowly add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and gently mix until the dough starts to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. You want it to remain slightly crumbly but cohesive.
  4. Chill the Dough: Divide the dough into two equal portions. Shape each portion into a disk, wrap them tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough helps ensure that the crust remains flaky when baked.

Step 2: Make the Filling

The filling is where all the magic happens. The apples should be sliced thinly so they soften and cook evenly, creating that beautiful syrupy filling that apple pie lovers adore.

  1. Prepare the Apples: Peel, core, and slice your apples. Use a variety of apples for the best flavor—Granny Smith apples give a tart kick, while Honeycrisp or Fuji apples offer a sweet contrast.
  2. Mix with Sugar and Spices: In a large mixing bowl, toss the apple slices with the granulated sugar, brown sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, cornstarch, and salt. The cornstarch helps thicken the filling and gives it that gooey texture you expect from a classic apple pie. Stir until all the apple slices are evenly coated with the sugary, spiced mixture.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

Now it’s time to put everything together!

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out one of the dough disks into a 12-inch circle. Carefully place the dough into a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it into the bottom and up the sides. Trim any excess dough around the edges, leaving about a 1-inch overhang.
  3. Add the Filling: Pour the apple filling into the prepared pie crust, making sure to evenly distribute the apples.
  4. Top the Pie: Roll out the second dough disk to create the top crust. Place it over the filled pie, gently pressing the edges together to seal the crusts. You can crimp the edges for a decorative touch. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape while baking.
  5. Brush with Plant-Based Milk: For a beautiful golden crust, brush the top of the pie with a small amount of plant-based milk and maple syrup. This will give the pie a nice sheen and a slight sweetness.

Step 4: Bake the Pie

  1. Bake: Place the pie on the middle rack of the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling through the slits in the top crust. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, you can cover them with aluminum foil or a pie shield.
  2. Cool: After baking, allow the pie to cool for at least 2 hours before slicing. This will give the filling time to set and firm up, making it easier to slice.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once your vegan apple pie has cooled and set, it’s time to slice and enjoy! Serve it warm or at room temperature, with a scoop of dairy-free vanilla ice cream or a dollop of coconut whipped cream on top for an extra indulgent treat.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Apple Pie

1. Use a Variety of Apples

For the best flavor and texture, mix different types of apples. The tartness of Granny Smith apples paired with the sweetness of Honeycrisp or Fuji apples creates a perfect balance in the filling.

2. Chill the Dough

Don’t skip the chilling step! It’s essential for ensuring a flaky crust. If you’re short on time, you can chill the dough for as little as 30 minutes, but the longer you chill it, the better the texture of the crust.

3. Adjust the Sweetness

Depending on the apples you use, you may need to adjust the amount of sugar. Taste the filling before assembling the pie and adjust as necessary to suit your preference.

4. Gluten-Free Option

To make this pie gluten-free, simply swap out the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend. You may also need to add a little more ice water to the dough to achieve the right consistency.

5. Add Extra Flavor

Feel free to experiment with additional spices such as ginger or cloves for a little extra kick. A touch of vanilla extract in the filling can also add depth and warmth to the flavor.

Conclusion

Making vegan apple pie is easier than you think! With just a few ingredient swaps, you can enjoy a classic dessert that’s not only free of dairy but also full of delicious flavor. Whether you’re serving it for a family gathering, a holiday dinner, or just because you’re craving something sweet, this vegan apple pie will satisfy your taste buds and impress anyone who tries it. So, get baking, and enjoy this warm, comforting dessert without the dairy!
