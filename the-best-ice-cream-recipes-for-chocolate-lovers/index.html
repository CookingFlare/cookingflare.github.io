The Best Ice Cream Recipes for Chocolate Lovers


If you’re a chocolate lover, there’s nothing quite like the creamy, indulgent taste of homemade chocolate ice cream to satisfy your cravings. Whether you like it rich and dark, light and sweet, or somewhere in between, chocolate ice cream is one of the most versatile and comforting desserts you can make.

In this blog post, we will take you on a sweet journey through some of the best chocolate ice cream recipes. From classic recipes that bring out the best in cocoa, to creative twists that incorporate different types of chocolate and fun mix-ins, there’s something here for every chocolate enthusiast.

1. Classic Chocolate Ice Cream

Let’s start with the tried and true: a classic chocolate ice cream recipe that never disappoints. This rich and creamy treat uses simple ingredients but delivers a deep, velvety chocolate flavor that will make any chocolate lover swoon.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chocolate Base: In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, heavy cream, sugar, and cocoa powder. Whisk everything together until smooth, and then heat over medium heat. Stir frequently, making sure the mixture doesn’t boil. Once it starts to steam and bubble around the edges, remove it from the heat.

  2. Make the Egg Custard: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and salt. Gradually pour a little of the hot milk mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling. Once tempered, pour the egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining milk mixture.

  3. Cook the Custard: Return the saucepan to the heat and cook the mixture over medium-low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon. You’ll want to cook it until the custard thickens enough to coat the back of the spoon. This should take around 5–7 minutes.

  4. Add the Chocolate: Once the custard is ready, remove the saucepan from the heat. Stir in the chopped dark chocolate and vanilla extract until fully melted and incorporated.

  5. Cool and Churn: Pour the mixture into a clean bowl and allow it to cool to room temperature. Then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. Once the custard has chilled, churn it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  6. Freeze and Enjoy: Transfer the churned ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for an additional 2–3 hours until firm. Scoop, serve, and enjoy your classic chocolate ice cream!

2. Double Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

For those who can’t get enough of chocolate, double the chocolate means double the fun. This recipe features a creamy chocolate base and is loaded with extra chocolate chips for added texture and bursts of chocolatey goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup milk chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Make the Chocolate Base: Follow the same steps as in the classic chocolate ice cream recipe to create your chocolate base. Once the custard has been cooked and the dark chocolate has melted into the base, let it cool down.

  2. Mix the Chocolate Chips: Once your custard has chilled, stir in the semisweet and milk chocolate chips into the base before churning. The chocolate chips will add both a delightful crunch and an extra burst of flavor in each bite.

  3. Churn and Freeze: Churn the mixture in your ice cream maker and freeze the churned ice cream as instructed in the classic recipe above. The result will be a creamy, chocolate-packed dessert with pockets of chocolate chips in every scoop.

3. Chocolate Hazelnut Ice Cream

This decadent ice cream recipe takes chocolate lovers to a whole new level with the addition of hazelnuts. The rich, nutty flavor of the hazelnuts paired with the deep chocolate creates a luxurious treat that’s perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/2 cup hazelnuts, toasted and chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: Just like the classic chocolate ice cream, start by heating the milk, cream, sugar, and cocoa powder together. Once the custard has thickened and the dark chocolate has melted, let it cool down completely.

  2. Add the Hazelnuts: Once the custard has cooled, stir in the chopped toasted hazelnuts. They will add a delightful crunch and a lovely nutty flavor to the ice cream.

  3. Churn and Freeze: Churn the mixture in your ice cream maker and freeze as per the classic recipe instructions. When ready to serve, you’ll have a creamy chocolate ice cream with a rich nutty undertone.

4. Chocolate Mint Ice Cream

For those who adore the combination of chocolate and mint, this chocolate mint ice cream recipe is sure to be a hit. The cool mint paired with creamy chocolate is an irresistibly refreshing treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • A few drops of green food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chocolate Custard: Follow the steps to make the chocolate base as described in the classic recipe. Once the custard has thickened and cooled, stir in the peppermint extract. You can add a few drops of green food coloring if you want the ice cream to have that signature minty hue.

  2. Churn and Freeze: Once the custard is ready, churn it in your ice cream maker. After churning, transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze until firm. The result is a delightful chocolate ice cream with a cool, refreshing mint flavor.

5. Chocolate Peanut Butter Ice Cream

Peanut butter and chocolate is one of the most iconic flavor combinations, and when it comes to ice cream, it’s no different. This recipe combines a creamy chocolate base with swirls of smooth peanut butter for a decadent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chocolate Custard: Start by following the steps for making a chocolate base as described in the classic recipe. Once your chocolate custard is done, allow it to cool to room temperature.

  2. Swirl in the Peanut Butter: Once the custard has chilled, gently fold in the creamy peanut butter, creating swirls of peanut butter throughout the chocolate base.

  3. Churn and Freeze: Churn the mixture in your ice cream maker and freeze as usual. The result will be a rich chocolate ice cream with ribbons of peanut butter running through each scoop.

6. Chocolate Lava Cake Ice Cream

For a unique twist, try turning the flavors of a molten chocolate cake into a creamy ice cream. This recipe features gooey pockets of molten chocolate in every bite, much like a lava cake.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/4 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate ganache (for the lava effect)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: Follow the steps for the classic chocolate base. Once the custard has chilled, fold in the mini chocolate chips to create little bursts of chocolate throughout the ice cream.

  2. Add the Lava: Before freezing, drizzle the cooled chocolate ganache into the ice cream base. You’ll want to fold it in just a little, so that it creates pockets of molten chocolate throughout.

  3. Churn and Freeze: Churn and freeze the ice cream as usual, and when you scoop it out, you’ll find gooey chocolate pockets inside—a perfect molten lava cake experience in ice cream form.

Conclusion

Making chocolate ice cream at home is an incredibly satisfying experience, and the possibilities are endless! Whether you prefer a rich and creamy classic, a nutty hazelnut variation, or a fun combination like chocolate peanut butter, these recipes are sure to make your ice cream dreams come true. The best part is that you can tweak the recipes to match your preferences, making each batch unique.

So, what are you waiting for? Get your ice cream maker out and treat yourself to the best chocolate ice cream you’ve ever tasted.
