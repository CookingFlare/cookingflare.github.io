A Taste of Spain: Bacalao a la Vizcaina Recipe


Bacalao a la Vizcaina is a traditional Spanish dish that originates from the Basque region of Spain. This flavorful recipe features salted cod fish cooked in a rich and savory tomato sauce, with a hint of smokiness from the added roasted red peppers. It’s a true taste of Spain, and today, we’ll be sharing with you our own take on this classic dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound salted cod fish
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 roasted red pepper, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by soaking the salted cod fish in water overnight. This will help remove some of the excess salt. Change the water a few times during the soaking process.

  2. Once the cod fish has soaked, drain the water and pat the fish dry with a paper towel. Cut the cod into medium-sized pieces and set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become translucent and fragrant.

  4. Add the diced roasted red pepper to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes.

  5. Stir in the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, paprika, dried thyme, and cayenne pepper (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer the sauce for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Gently place the cod fish pieces into the skillet, making sure they are covered with the sauce. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the fish simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.

  7. Once the cod fish is cooked, remove the skillet from the heat. Allow the dish to cool slightly before serving.

  8. To serve, garnish with fresh parsley and accompany with crusty bread or rice, if desired. The Bacalao a la Vizcaina is traditionally served as a main course, but it can also be a delicious appetizer or tapas dish.

Notes and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, you can include some sliced olives or capers to the sauce while simmering.
  • If you prefer a spicier dish, increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add a chopped chili pepper to the sauce.
  • This recipe can be easily adjusted to serve more or fewer people. Simply adjust the quantities of the ingredients accordingly.

Conclusion

Bacalao a la Vizcaina is a delightful Spanish dish that showcases the unique flavors and culinary traditions of the Basque region. The combination of tender cod fish, rich tomato sauce, and smoky roasted red peppers creates a harmonious blend of tastes that will transport you to the sunny streets of Spain.

Whether you’re looking to impress your dinner guests or simply indulge in a taste of Spain at home, this Bacalao a la Vizcaina recipe is a must-try. So grab your apron, gather the ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of Spain in every bite. ¡Buen provecho!
