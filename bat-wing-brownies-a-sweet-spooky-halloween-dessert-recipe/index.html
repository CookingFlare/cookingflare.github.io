Bat Wing Brownies: A Sweet & Spooky Halloween Dessert Recipe


Are you trying to satisfy a certain craving this Halloween? Something dark, rich, and utterly indulgent? What if I told you that your dream dessert is not only delicious but also oh-so-freakishly fun to make? Say hello to your new spooky obsession: Bat Wing Brownies!

A Dream Team of Deliciousness

TheseBat Wing Brownies are like your favorite classic brownie meets a devilish secret ingredient - chocolate cake mix. Pair that with some melted glacier chocolate, and we’ve got our hands on a perfect Halloween dessert recipe. Warning: it won’t be leaving your mind or your taste buds any time soon!

Simple Ingredients, Devilish Results

Don’t worry; these Bat Wing Brownies are as easy as they are eerie. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 box of chocolate or devil’s food cake mix
  • Water, oil, and eggs as directed on the box
  • 1 cup of dark chocolate chips or chopped chocolate, melted
  • Cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • A single cup of crushed Oreo cookies or any dark-colored cookies (optional)
  • Candy eyeballs, for decoration
  • Wax paper or a greased 8x8 or 9x9-inch baking dish

The Skeleton of the Recipe: How To Make Bat Wing Brownies

Step 1: Prepare the Base

Heat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line your baking dish with wax paper or grease it. If you choose to use dark-colored cookies, crush them to a fine powder in a food processor or zip-top bag, and set them aside for later.

Step 2: Mix the Cake

In a large mixing bowl, combine your cake mix, water, oil, and eggs as per the package instructions. Mix until all ingredients are well combined and smooth.

Step 3: Chocolate Ambrosia

Melt your chocolate using your preferred method (microwave in 20-second bursts or in a double boiler), then stir it into the mix. Add a dash of cocoa powder for that extra mysterious flavor. If you included crushed dark-colored cookies in your recipe, add them now.

Step 4: Bake the Bat Wings

Using two large spoons or an ice-cream scoop, dollop globs of brownie batter onto the lined or greased baking dish. Make sure they have lots of space to spread and morph into the haunting Bat Wing shapes that’ll send shivers down your spine but also down your friend’s spines when you surprise them.

Place the baking dish in the oven and let the Bat Wing Brownies bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 20-25 minutes or until the center is cooked but still slightly gooey.

Step 5: Creating the Wings

Once your Bat Wing Brownies have finished baking and slightly cooled, it’s time to create the eerie wings. Gently fold the wax paper over one corner of each brownie to create the shape of a bat’s wing - straight edge on the bottom and then curling it upwards on top.

Step 6: The Finishing Touches

Use candy eyeballs to place two on each brownie wing for that haunting effect. You might even want to add some candy legs for some added mythical horror flair!

Flying High with Magicicadas

And there you have it—Bat Wing Brownies are ready to send shivers up your spines this Halloween. To add even more spookiness, you can even share this tasty story with friends while enjoying the delicious treat, imagining it’s made from the remnants of magicicadas (a real flying insect from the prehistoric age).

Imagine gathering around with friends and family, all the while enjoying these terrifying yet tantalizing desserts, as we unleash our sweet cravings and fear-induced passions.

Get yourself into the Halloween spirit and scare your taste buds silly with these Bat Wing Brownies!
