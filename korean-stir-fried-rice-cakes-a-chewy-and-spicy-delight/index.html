Korean Stir-Fried Rice Cakes: A Chewy and Spicy Delight


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or are simply looking to expand your culinary horizons, then you’re in for a treat! Korean Stir-Fried Rice Cakes, known as “tteokbokki,” are a beloved dish that’s not only delicious but also fun to make. This chewy and spicy delight is a popular street food in Korea, and for good reason – it’s a perfect blend of flavors and textures that will leave you craving more. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the process of creating this mouthwatering dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to prepare this delectable dish:

  • 1 pound of Korean rice cakes (tteok)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 4 ounces of fish cakes, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 tablespoons of Korean red chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic
  • 4-5 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Toasted sesame seeds for garnish
  • Roasted seaweed flakes for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s start cooking!

  1. Prepare the Rice Cakes: Soak the rice cakes in cold water for about 10-15 minutes to soften them. This will help them cook evenly and achieve the perfect chewy texture.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a bowl, combine the Korean red chili paste (gochujang), soy sauce, sugar, and minced garlic. Mix well until the sugar is dissolved and the sauce is smooth.

  3. Cook the Ingredients: In a large, deep skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the fish cakes and stir-fry for a few minutes until they start to brown. Then, add the rice cakes and the green onions to the skillet. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the rice cakes are slightly browned.

  4. Add the Sauce: Pour the sauce over the rice cakes and fish cakes. Add 2 cups of water and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat to medium and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and the rice cakes are tender but still chewy.

  5. Finish and Serve: Once the sauce has thickened, drizzle the sesame oil over the dish and give it a final toss. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. If you like, you can add some hard-boiled eggs to the skillet at this point, allowing them to soak up the delicious flavors.

  6. Garnish and Enjoy: Transfer the stir-fried rice cakes to a serving dish and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and roasted seaweed flakes for an extra burst of flavor and texture.

Tips and Variations

  • For extra heat, you can add a pinch of Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru) to the sauce.
  • Feel free to customize the dish by adding vegetables such as sliced carrots, cabbage, or mushrooms.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can omit the fish cakes and add more vegetables or tofu for protein.

Final Thoughts

Korean Stir-Fried Rice Cakes are a delightful combination of chewy rice cakes, savory fish cakes, and a spicy, flavorful sauce that’s sure to tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re enjoying them as a satisfying snack or as a main course, this dish is bound to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. Embrace the bold flavors of Korean cuisine and bring a taste of Seoul right into your home kitchen with this simple and rewarding recipe. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the chewy and spicy delight of Korean Stir-Fried Rice Cakes!
