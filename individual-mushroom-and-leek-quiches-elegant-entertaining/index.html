Individual Mushroom and Leek Quiches: Elegant Entertaining


Quiches are a versatile and elegant option for entertaining guests or enjoying a cozy meal at home. These individual mushroom and leek quiches are packed with flavor and can be easily customized to suit your preferences. With a buttery crust, creamy filling, and a delightful combination of earthy mushrooms and sweet leeks, these quiches are sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, diced
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 leek, white and light green parts only, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold diced butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced leek and mushrooms and cook until they are softened and any liquid has evaporated, about 5-7 minutes. Set aside to cool.

  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, black pepper, and dried thyme until well combined.

  6. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Cut out four circles, approximately 6 inches in diameter, and gently press them into four individual tart pans.

  7. Divide the cooked leek and mushroom mixture evenly among the tart pans, spreading it out in an even layer. Pour the egg and cream mixture over the vegetables, filling each tart pan to the top.

  8. Place the tart pans on a baking sheet and transfer them to the preheated oven. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiches are set and lightly golden on top.

  9. Remove the quiches from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

Serve these individual mushroom and leek quiches as an appetizer, a light lunch, or as part of a brunch spread. The buttery crust provides the perfect complement to the creamy filling, while the combination of mushrooms and leeks adds depth and richness to each bite. Customize the recipe by adding your favorite herbs or cheeses for a unique twist. These quiches can be made ahead of time and reheated, making them a convenient option for entertaining. Pair them with a fresh salad or some roasted vegetables for a complete and satisfying meal.

Enjoy the elegant and delicious flavors of these individual mushroom and leek quiches. Your guests will be impressed, and you’ll have a delightful dish to savor.
