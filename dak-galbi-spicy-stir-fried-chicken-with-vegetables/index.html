Dak Galbi: Spicy Stir-Fried Chicken with Vegetables


If you’re a fan of spicy food and enjoy exploring different international cuisines, then you’re in for a treat with this Dak Galbi recipe. Hailing from South Korea, Dak Galbi is a delicious and fiery stir-fry dish that features tender chicken, an assortment of fresh vegetables, and a mouthwatering spicy sauce. It’s a perfect dish for those who crave bold flavors and a bit of heat in their meals. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure as we dive into the world of Dak Galbi!

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients required to make Dak Galbi. You’ll need:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of cabbage, chopped
  • 1 sweet potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • 1 cup of rice cakes (tteok)
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons of Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil

Instructions

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s move on to the cooking process. Follow these simple steps to create your own Dak Galbi masterpiece:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the thinly sliced chicken with the sauce ingredients - Korean chili paste, soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, minced garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil. Mix well to ensure the chicken is evenly coated with the flavorful sauce. Allow the chicken to marinate for about 20-30 minutes.

  2. Heat the Pan: In a large, deep frying pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until it’s almost cooked through.

  3. Add the Vegetables: Next, add the sliced onion, chopped cabbage, sweet potato, and rice cakes to the pan with the partially cooked chicken. Continue to stir-fry the ingredients together, allowing the vegetables to soften and the flavors to meld.

  4. Spice it Up: As the vegetables and chicken cook together, the spicy aroma will start to fill your kitchen. Adjust the level of spiciness according to your preference by adding more Korean chili paste if you like it extra hot.

  5. Finish and Serve: Once everything is cooked through and the flavors have infused, sprinkle the chopped scallions and sesame seeds over the Dak Galbi for a burst of freshness and nuttiness. Remove the pan from the heat and transfer the steaming hot Dak Galbi to a serving dish.

Serving Suggestions

Dak Galbi is traditionally served on a sizzling hot plate or skillet, keeping the dish warm throughout the meal. You can enjoy this spicy stir-fry on its own or pair it with a bowl of steamed rice to balance out the heat. For added authenticity, consider serving it with a side of crisp lettuce leaves, perilla leaves, and sliced garlic - allowing you to create your own lettuce wraps with the Dak Galbi.

Final Thoughts

Dak Galbi is a dish that not only tantalizes your taste buds with its spicy kick but also brings a sense of communal dining as it’s often enjoyed with friends and family. The combination of tender chicken, vibrant vegetables, and the fiery sauce creates a harmony of flavors that will leave you craving for more. So, the next time you’re in the mood for a culinary adventure, whip up a batch of Dak Galbi and transport your senses to the bustling streets of South Korea.

Give this Dak Galbi recipe a try, and let the tantalizing flavors of this spicy stir-fried chicken dish elevate your home cooking experience!
