Candy Corn Cake Pops: A Halloween Treat Recipe


Halloween is just around the corner, and it’s the perfect time to whip up some delightful treats that capture the spirit of the season. One such treat that combines fun, flavor, and festivity is the Candy Corn Cake Pop. These adorable bite-sized sweets are not only delicious but also visually appealing, making them a hit at any Halloween gathering. In this blog post, we will guide you through the steps of creating your own delicious Candy Corn Cake Pops, along with tips, tricks, and variations to make this treat truly your own!

What Are Cake Pops?

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to understand what cake pops are. Cake pops are small, round cakes that are molded onto a stick, dipped in chocolate or candy coating, and often decorated. They’re the perfect bite-sized dessert, combining the moistness of cake with the sweetness of chocolate. The fun part about cake pops is their versatility; you can customize them for any occasion, and Halloween is no exception!

Ingredients You Will Need

To make Candy Corn Cake Pops, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Cake:

  • 1 box of vanilla cake mix (plus ingredients required on the box: eggs, oil, and water)
  • 1 cup of buttercream frosting (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional)

For the Coating:

  • 16 ounces of white candy melts
  • Gel food coloring (yellow and orange)
  • 1 tablespoon of coconut oil (optional, for thinning the candy melts)

For Decoration:

  • Candy corn (for topping)
  • Sprinkles (optional)
  • Lollipop sticks or cake pop sticks

Equipment Needed

  • Mixing bowls
  • Baking pan
  • Electric mixer or whisk
  • Cookie scoop or melon baller
  • Microwave-safe bowl
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper
  • Styrofoam block or a cake pop stand (for drying)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Bake the Cake

  1. Prepare the Oven: Preheat your oven to the temperature recommended on the cake mix box. Grease and flour your baking pan or line it with parchment paper.

  2. Mix the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cake mix, eggs, oil, and water. Mix according to the package instructions until you achieve a smooth batter.

  3. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and bake for the time indicated on the box (usually around 25-30 minutes). Once baked, allow the cake to cool completely in the pan.

Step 2: Crumble the Cake

  1. Crumble the Cake: Once the cake has cooled, remove it from the pan and place it in a large bowl. Use your hands or a fork to crumble the cake into fine crumbs.

  2. Mix in the Frosting: Add the buttercream frosting to the crumbled cake. You can adjust the amount of frosting based on the moisture of the cake; start with half a cup and add more if needed. Mix until the mixture holds together but is not overly sticky.

Step 3: Shape the Cake Balls

  1. Form the Balls: Using a cookie scoop or melon baller, take portions of the cake mixture and roll them into uniform balls, about 1 inch in size. Place the balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. Chill: Once all the cake balls are shaped, place the baking sheet in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes to help them firm up.

Step 4: Prepare the Coating

  1. Melt the Candy Coating: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the white candy melts according to the package instructions. If the melted candy is too thick, you can mix in a tablespoon of coconut oil to achieve a smoother consistency.

  2. Color the Candy Coating: Divide the melted candy into three separate bowls. Leave one bowl white, color the second bowl with yellow gel food coloring, and the third with orange gel food coloring. Mix well until you achieve your desired shades.

Step 5: Dip the Cake Pops

  1. Insert the Sticks: Remove the cake balls from the refrigerator. Dip the end of each lollipop stick into the white candy coating, then insert it into the center of each cake ball. This helps the stick adhere better.

  2. Dip in Candy Coating: Dip each cake ball into the corresponding colored candy coating (white, yellow, or orange). Gently tap off any excess coating and place the dipped cake pops back onto the parchment-lined baking sheet.

  3. Create the Candy Corn Effect: For the candy corn effect, you will need to dip the bottom third of each cake pop in orange candy coating and the middle third in yellow candy coating, leaving the top white. Use a toothpick or small spatula to create clean lines between the colors.

Step 6: Decorate the Cake Pops

  1. Add Candy Corn: While the coating is still wet, press a piece of candy corn into the top of each cake pop for a festive touch.

  2. Sprinkle: If desired, add sprinkles before the candy coating sets for an extra layer of decoration.

  3. Drying: To ensure your cake pops are stable, place them upright in a styrofoam block or a cake pop stand to dry completely.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the candy coating has hardened, your Candy Corn Cake Pops are ready to be served! These treats are perfect for Halloween parties, school events, or just a fun family activity. Arrange them on a festive platter, and watch as they become the star of the show.

Tips for Success

  • Use Quality Ingredients: For the best flavor, use high-quality cake mix and frosting. If you have the time, consider making your cake and frosting from scratch!
  • Ensure Even Coating: Make sure the candy coating is at the right consistency. If it’s too thick, it won’t coat the cake pops evenly.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different cake flavors! Chocolate, pumpkin spice, or even red velvet would add a fun twist to your cake pops.
  • Storage: Store your cake pops in an airtight container in a cool place. They can last up to a week, but they are best enjoyed fresh!

Variations to Try

Once you master the classic Candy Corn Cake Pop, consider these fun variations to expand your Halloween treat repertoire:

  • Monster Cake Pops: Use colorful candy melts and fun edible eyes to create silly monster cake pops. Add different colored sprinkles for an extra pop!
  • Ghost Cake Pops: Dip your cake pops in white candy coating and use black edible markers to draw ghostly faces.
  • Spider Cake Pops: Use chocolate-coated cake pops, add pretzel legs, and a small candy for the spider’s body to create spooky spider cake pops.

Final Thoughts

Candy Corn Cake Pops are a fun and festive treat that embodies the spirit of Halloween. They are not only delicious but also allow for creativity in both design and flavor. Whether you’re hosting a Halloween party, looking for a fun activity with your kids, or simply want to indulge in a sweet treat, these cake pops are sure to delight.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to make a batch of these cute and tasty treats! Happy Halloween!
