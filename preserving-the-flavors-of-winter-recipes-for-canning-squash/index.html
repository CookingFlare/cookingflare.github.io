Preserving the Flavors of Winter: Recipes for Canning Squash


Winter squash is a staple of the season, providing a sweet and nutty flavor profile that’s perfect for soups, stews, and baked dishes. But what if you want to enjoy the flavors of winter all year round? Canning squash is a great way to preserve the bounty of the season, and with these recipes, you can stock up your pantry with delicious and nutritious squash purees, soups, and relishes.

Canning Squash 101

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s cover the basics of canning squash. Canning is a great way to preserve food because it allows you to sterilize the food and seal it in airtight containers, preventing the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms. However, canning requires some special precautions to ensure that the food is safe to eat.

  • Use a pressure canner: Squash is a low-acid food, which means that it requires a pressure canner to kill off any bacteria that may be present. A pressure canner is a specialized canner that uses steam pressure to heat the food to a high temperature, killing off any bacteria that may be present.
  • Follow tested recipes: It’s important to follow tested recipes and guidelines for canning squash, as this will ensure that the food is properly sterilized and sealed.
  • Use the right equipment: Make sure you have the right equipment, including a pressure canner, canning jars, and lids, as well as a canning funnel and utensils.

Recipe 1: Canned Squash Puree

This recipe is a great way to preserve the flavor and texture of winter squash. Simply cook the squash until it’s tender, then puree it in a blender or food processor. Add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon juice, then transfer the puree to a canning jar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs winter squash (such as butternut or acorn)
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 canning jar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the squash in half and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  4. Let the squash cool, then scoop out the flesh and puree it in a blender or food processor.
  5. Add the butter, salt, pepper, and lemon juice to the puree and stir until combined.
  6. Transfer the puree to a canning jar and add the water.
  7. Process the jar in a pressure canner for 30 minutes, or until the jar is sealed and the squash is sterile.

Recipe 2: Canned Squash Soup

This recipe is a great way to preserve the flavors of winter squash in a delicious and comforting soup. Simply cook the squash until it’s tender, then puree it in a blender or food processor. Add chicken or vegetable broth, then transfer the soup to a canning jar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs winter squash (such as butternut or acorn)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 canning jar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the squash in half and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  4. Let the squash cool, then scoop out the flesh and puree it in a blender or food processor.
  5. Add the butter, onion, garlic, broth, salt, pepper, and nutmeg to the puree and stir until combined.
  6. Transfer the soup to a canning jar and process the jar in a pressure canner for 30 minutes, or until the jar is sealed and the soup is sterile.

Recipe 3: Canned Squash Relish

This recipe is a great way to preserve the flavors of winter squash in a tangy and crunchy relish. Simply cook the squash until it’s tender, then chop it into small pieces. Add vinegar, sugar, and spices, then transfer the relish to a canning jar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs winter squash (such as butternut or acorn)
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup chopped bell pepper
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 canning jar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the squash in half and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  4. Let the squash cool, then chop it into small pieces.
  5. Add the onion, bell pepper, celery, vinegar, sugar, salt, pepper, and mustard seeds to the squash and stir until combined.
  6. Transfer the relish to a canning jar and process the jar in a pressure canner for 10 minutes, or until the jar is sealed and the relish is sterile.

Conclusion

Canning squash is a great way to preserve the flavors of winter, and with these recipes, you can stock up your pantry with delicious and nutritious squash purees, soups, and relishes. By following tested recipes and guidelines for canning, you can ensure that your squash is properly sterilized and sealed, making it safe to eat all year round. Happy canning!
