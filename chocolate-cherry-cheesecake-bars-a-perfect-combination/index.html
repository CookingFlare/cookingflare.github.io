Chocolate Cherry Cheesecake Bars: A Perfect Combination


Indulge in the heavenly combination of rich chocolate and sweet cherries with these delectable Chocolate Cherry Cheesecake Bars. This recipe takes the classic cheesecake to a whole new level, with a touch of fruity goodness. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet treat, these bars are sure to impress. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of crushed chocolate sandwich cookies
  • 1/4 cup of melted butter
  • 8 ounces of cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup of cherry pie filling
  • 1/4 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Line a 9x9-inch baking dish with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal later.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the crushed chocolate sandwich cookies and melted butter. Mix well until the crumbs are evenly coated with the butter.

  3. Press the cookie mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared baking dish, creating an even layer. Place the dish in the refrigerator to chill while you prepare the cheesecake filling.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth and creamy. Add the sugar and vanilla extract, and continue beating until well combined.

  5. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Make sure to scrape down the sides of the bowl to incorporate all the ingredients.

  6. Pour the cheesecake filling over the chilled cookie crust in the baking dish. Use a spatula to spread it evenly.

  7. Spoon dollops of cherry pie filling onto the surface of the cheesecake filling. Use a toothpick or a skewer to swirl the cherry filling into the cheesecake, creating a marbled effect.

  8. Sprinkle the semi-sweet chocolate chips over the top of the cheesecake, gently pressing them into the filling.

  9. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.

  10. Remove the baking dish from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. Once cooled, transfer the dish to the refrigerator and chill for at least 2 hours, or overnight, to allow the cheesecake to set.

  11. When ready to serve, remove the cheesecake bars from the baking dish using the parchment paper overhang. Cut into squares and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, try using graham crackers instead of chocolate sandwich cookies for the crust.
  • Experiment with different flavors of pie filling, such as raspberry or blueberry, to create your own unique version of these cheesecake bars.
  • To make the bars extra decadent, drizzle melted chocolate over the top before serving.

These Chocolate Cherry Cheesecake Bars are the epitome of indulgence. The rich chocolate, creamy cheesecake, and burst of sweet cherries come together in a symphony of flavors. Perfect for any occasion, these bars will leave your taste buds wanting more. So, give this recipe a try and experience the magic of this irresistible combination!
