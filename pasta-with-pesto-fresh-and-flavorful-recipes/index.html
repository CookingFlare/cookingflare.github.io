Pasta with Pesto: Fresh and Flavorful Recipes


Pasta with pesto is a timeless dish that combines simplicity with vibrant flavors. With its rich, nutty, and herbaceous base, pesto can transform even the simplest bowl of pasta into something extraordinary. Whether you’re cooking for a quick weeknight dinner or hosting a gathering, learning how to make pasta with pesto will add a versatile favorite to your culinary repertoire.

In this post, we’ll explore everything you need to know about preparing pasta with pesto, from crafting the perfect homemade pesto to pairing the right pasta shapes for a mouthwatering meal. Along the way, we’ll also discuss tips, tricks, and variations to elevate your pesto pasta experience.

The Foundation: What is Pesto?

Originating from Italy, specifically Liguria, traditional pesto is made from fresh basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil. The word “pesto” comes from the Italian verb “pestare,” which means to crush or pound. Historically, pesto was prepared using a marble mortar and wooden pestle, emphasizing the handmade nature of this wonderfully aromatic sauce.

While the classic basil version is the most well-known, the world of pesto is vast and adaptable. Variations can be made with different herbs such as parsley, arugula, or cilantro, and even with non-herb ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes or roasted peppers. For a creative spin, you can also experiment with substitutes for pine nuts, such as walnuts, almonds, or pistachios.

How to Make the Perfect Homemade Pesto

There’s no substitute for freshly made pesto. While store-bought options are convenient, a homemade version bursts with flavor and allows you to control the texture and taste. Here’s a basic recipe to get started:

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (packed)
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (lightly toasted for added flavor)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/3 to 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil (depending on your desired consistency)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prep the Basil: Wash and dry your basil leaves. Make sure they are completely dry to prevent the pesto from becoming watery.
  2. Toast the Pine Nuts: Heat a dry skillet over medium heat and toast the pine nuts until they are lightly golden and aromatic. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. Blend: In a food processor, combine the basil, pine nuts, and garlic. Pulse a few times until the ingredients are coarsely chopped.
  4. Add Cheese: Add Parmesan cheese to the mixture and blend briefly.
  5. Emulsify with Olive Oil: While the food processor is running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the pesto reaches your desired creamy consistency.
  6. Season: Taste and season with a pinch of salt and freshly ground pepper. Adjust as needed.

If you prefer a chunkier pesto, use a mortar and pestle instead of a food processor—it’s more labor-intensive but delivers a more rustic texture.

Choosing the Right Pasta

While any type of pasta can technically be paired with pesto, certain shapes hold the sauce better, enhancing the overall experience. Here are some top choices:

1. Linguine

The flat ribbon-like shape of linguine allows the silky pesto to coat each strand evenly, making it a classic pairing.

2. Trofie

A traditional Ligurian hand-rolled pasta, trofie’s unique twisted shape is perfect for capturing pesto in its ridges.

3. Fusilli

The spiraled structure of fusilli hugs the pesto, ensuring every bite bursts with vibrant flavor.

4. Penne Rigate

Ridged penne is another great option, as its tubular design soaks up the sauce.

Choosing the right pasta is more than just personal preference—it’s about maximizing the flavors and textures of the dish.

Cooking Perfectly Al Dente Pasta

One of the cornerstones of delicious pasta with pesto is cooking the pasta al dente, or “to the tooth.” Here’s how to achieve perfectly cooked pasta every time:

  1. Boil Properly Salted Water: Always season your pasta water generously with salt. The water should taste like the sea. This enhances the flavor of the pasta itself.
  2. Follow Pack Instructions: Check the cooking time on the package of pasta, but taste test as you cook. Start checking for doneness about a minute before the recommended time.
  3. Reserve Pasta Water: Before draining your pasta, reserve at least half a cup of the starchy pasta water. This liquid gold can be used to adjust the consistency of your pesto sauce.
  4. Do Not Overcook: Keep in mind that al dente pasta will continue to cook slightly as you mix it with the sauce, so undercook just slightly if necessary.

Tossing Everything Together

Now comes the fun part—bringing the pesto sauce and pasta together:

  1. Combine While Hot: After draining your pasta, immediately toss it back into the pot or a large serving bowl. The heat from the pasta will gently warm the pesto without cooking it, preserving its fresh herbaceous flavor.
  2. Thin the Sauce if Needed: If the pesto feels too thick, gradually add the reserved pasta water, one tablespoon at a time, to create a silky, smooth sauce.
  3. Coat Evenly: Use tongs or a large spoon to gently toss the pasta until it’s evenly coated with pesto.

Variations and Add-Ons

Pesto pasta is highly customizable. Here are some ideas to elevate the dish:

  1. Protein Boost

    • Add cooked chicken breast strips.
    • Stir in crispy pancetta or bacon.
    • Top with grilled shrimp or seared scallops.

  2. Vegetable Mix-Ins

    • Add roasted cherry tomatoes for a burst of sweetness.
    • Toss in sautéed zucchini or artichoke hearts.
    • Include blanched green beans, as traditionally done with Ligurian pesto pasta.

  3. Cheese Finish

    • Top with extra Parmesan or Pecorino Romano for added richness.
    • Garnish with a dollop of ricotta for a creamy twist.

Tips for Storing and Freezing Pesto

Making a big batch of pesto is both efficient and practical. Here’s how to store or freeze it for future use:

  • Refrigeration: Store pesto in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. To prevent oxidation, cover the surface with a thin layer of olive oil.
  • Freezing: Freeze pesto in ice cube trays or small containers for individual portions. Once frozen, transfer the cubes to a resealable plastic bag and store them for up to three months.

Wine Pairings for Pesto Pasta

A well-paired wine can elevate the meal to a fine dining experience. Consider these options:

  • White Wines: Opt for a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or a light, aromatic Vermentino.
  • Rosé: A dry rosé complements the freshness of the basil and the nuttiness of the pine nuts.
  • Light Reds: Chianti or Pinot Noir are excellent choices if you prefer red wine with your meal.

Why You’ll Love Pasta with Pesto

Pasta with pesto is more than just a meal—it’s a celebration of fresh ingredients and perfectly balanced flavors. It’s quick, easy, and endlessly adaptable, fitting into both weeknight dinners or more sophisticated occasions.

Whether you stick to the classic version or experiment with creative twists, you’ll find that pesto pasta is a dish you’ll come back to time and time again. So gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and enjoy the fresh, flavorful world of pasta with pesto!
