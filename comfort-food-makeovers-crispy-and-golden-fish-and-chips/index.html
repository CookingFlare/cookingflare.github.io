Comfort Food Makeovers: Crispy and Golden Fish and Chips


There’s something undeniably comforting about a plate of crispy, golden fish and chips. The crispy coating on the outside, the tender flakiness of the fish, and the satisfying crunch as you bite into each piece – it’s a classic comfort food that never fails to bring joy. However, traditional fish and chips often come with a hefty dose of oil and calories, making it a less than ideal choice for those looking to maintain a healthy lifestyle. But fear not, because with a few simple tweaks, we can transform this beloved classic into a healthier yet equally delicious version.

Ingredients

For the Fish:

  • 4 fillets of cod or haddock
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup sparkling water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Chips:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into thick strips
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tartar Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons capers, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Chips

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the potato strips with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the potato strips in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 30-35 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy.

Step 2: Making the Tartar Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, capers, dill, salt, and pepper. Mix well and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Step 3: Preparing the Fish

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Slowly pour in the sparkling water and olive oil, whisking until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.
  3. Heat the oven to 220°F (105°C) to keep the fish warm while the chips finish cooking.
  4. Dip each fillet of fish into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off.

Step 4: Cooking the Fish

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add enough oil to coat the bottom of the pan.
  2. Carefully place the battered fish fillets into the hot oil and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.
  3. Transfer the cooked fish to a baking sheet and keep warm in the oven while you finish cooking the remaining fillets.

Step 5: Serving

  1. Serve the crispy fish and chips alongside a dollop of the homemade tartar sauce and a wedge of lemon for an extra zing.

Final Thoughts

By making a few simple swaps, like using whole wheat flour and baking instead of deep-frying, we’ve managed to create a lighter, healthier version of the classic fish and chips without sacrificing any of the flavor or texture. This makeover proves that comfort food can be both indulgent and nutritious, making it a win-win for anyone craving a taste of nostalgia without the guilt. Give this recipe a try and let the crispy, golden goodness speak for itself!
