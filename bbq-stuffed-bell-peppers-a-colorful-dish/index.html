BBQ Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Colorful Dish


Have you grown tired of the old, tried and true dinners? Desiring a twist with a comforting, yet vibrant flavor and color? If yes, try BBQ-stuffed bell peppers. This dish is perfect for any day of the week, whether for a family dinner, hosting guests, or enjoying a rewarding meal after a long day.

Ingredients

6 large bell peppers – colors of your choice 1 teaspoon table salt 1 teaspoon ground black pepper 1 lb. ground beef or ground turkey 1 cup raw rice – uncooked 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce ½ cup barbecue sauce 1 cup frozen corn kernels, defrosted 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1 small onion – chopped 1 garlic clove – minced Optional toppings: chopped fresh cilantro, sour cream, avocado

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with foil, and then place a wire rack onto the sheet.

  2. Rinse the bell peppers and place them on the wire rack, allowing them to drain. To help prevent the peppers from sinking during cooking, stand the peppers upright and spoon a small amount of tomato sauce or water into the cavity of each pepper. Alternatively, you can simply cut off the bottom of the pepper and use a bowl filled with water to keep them upright.

  3. In a large skillet, heat over medium heat, combine the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook for about five minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is softened.

  4. Add the ground beef or turkey and cook for approximately 10 minutes, stirring and breaking up any large chunks.

  5. Stir in the uncooked rice, crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, barbecue sauce, and defrosted corn kernels. Season with salt and black pepper. Cook for an additional five minutes over low heat.

  6. Take off the heat and allow the mixture to cool for roughly 10 minutes.

  7. Preheat your grill for direct grilling over medium heat.

  8. Carefully, stuff the bell peppers with the cooled filling and then place them on the grill. Cover the grill and cook for approximately 30 minutes, turning occasionally, so the peppers don’t burn.

  9. After the peppers have completed cooking, remove them from the oven and sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese on top. Optionally, add toppings like chopped cilantro and a dollop of sour cream or avocado.

  10. Serve warm, savoring each vibrant, unique color of your BBQ stuffed bell peppers.

Conclusion

Barbeque-stuffed bell peppers take an ordinary meal and elevate it with not only a mix of colors but a lushness of flavors you’ll want to dive into time and time again. Whether you choose colors ranging from red to yellow to green to orange, there’s a visual appeal that will excite your senses. Once you’ve chosen your colors, it’s time to Go ahead and embark on this delightful culinary journey! Give barbecue-stuffed bell peppers a try today. Even as a novice cook, you can create a wholesome and delicious dish you’ll want to share with others. It’s not only a perfect main dish but a splendid addition to any potluck or picnic. After all, it’s nearly impossible to resist a meal that’s good for the soul and the eyes!
