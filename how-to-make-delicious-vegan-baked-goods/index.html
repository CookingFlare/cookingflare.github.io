How to Make Delicious Vegan Baked Goods


The world of vegan baking is a journey of endless creativity, flavor, and possibilities. Contrary to popular belief, creating delicious baked goods without traditional ingredients like eggs, butter, or milk isn’t a challenge—it’s an opportunity to explore alternative, plant-based ingredients that bring a whole new dimension to your favorite treats. Whether you’re new to vegan baking or looking to refine your techniques, this guide will walk you through all the tips, tricks, and essential practices you need to create irresistible vegan baked goods.

Understanding the Basics of Vegan Baking

Before diving into recipes and techniques, it’s essential to understand how vegan baking works. Traditional baking relies heavily on animal-based products for structure, moisture, and richness. In vegan baking, these roles can be filled by plant-based alternatives that mimic their properties without compromising flavor or texture.

Let’s look at some key substitutions for common baking ingredients:

1. Replacing Eggs

Eggs serve as binders, leavening agents, and moisture providers. Luckily, vegan baking offers a wide range of substitutes:

  • Flaxseed or Chia Seed “Eggs”: Mix 1 tablespoon of ground flaxseed or chia seeds with 3 tablespoons of water and let it gel for 5 minutes. This works well in muffins, cakes, and cookies.
  • Applesauce: ¼ cup of unsweetened applesauce can replace one egg, adding moisture and subtle sweetness.
  • Mashed Banana: Similarly, ¼ cup of mashed banana serves as a great alternative, particularly in recipes where a light banana flavor is welcome.
  • Silken Tofu: ¼ cup of blended silken tofu brings richness to recipes, ideal for brownies and dense cakes.
  • Aquafaba: The liquid from canned chickpeas can whip up like egg whites, making it a perfect substitute for meringues, mousses, and airy cakes.

2. Replacing Dairy Milk

Swap dairy milk for plant-based alternatives that mimic its creamy consistency. Popular choices include almond milk, oat milk, soy milk, cashew milk, and coconut milk. Each offers subtle flavor nuances, so choose one based on your recipe’s requirements.

3. Replacing Butter

Plant-based oils, vegan butter, or coconut oil can readily replace dairy butter. Earth Balance and Miyoko’s Creamery are widely loved in the vegan community, but you can also use cold-pressed oils like olive oil or avocado oil for a slightly healthier twist.

4. Sweetener Alternatives

Many sweeteners are already vegan, including cane sugar, coconut sugar, maple syrup, agave, and molasses. If a recipe calls for honey, substitute it with maple syrup or agave for the same viscosity and sweetness.

5. Binding Agents

When the recipe requires additional binding (especially for gluten-free recipes), ingredients like psyllium husk, arrowroot powder, or xanthan gum can be incredibly helpful.

Essential Techniques for Vegan Baking Success

1. Understand Your Ingredients

Familiarize yourself with the properties of plant-based ingredients you’re using. Knowing how flaxseed eggs gel or how coconut oil reacts to heat gives you better control over your recipe’s outcome.

2. Opt for High-Quality Flours

Flour forms the backbone of baked goods, and choosing the right type makes a big difference. All-purpose flour works for most recipes, but whole wheat, almond, or oat flour can add unique textures and flavors. For gluten-free recipes, consider blending flours like rice, teff, and tapioca.

3. Balance Moisture and Texture

Vegan baking often requires extra attention to moisture content. Adding a touch more liquid and fat than traditional recipes can ensure your baked goods stay moist and fluffy. Always evaluate the consistency of your batter or dough before proceeding.

4. Don’t Be Afraid to Experiment

Vegan ingredients work differently than traditional ones, so don’t be afraid to test, tweak, and refine your recipes. Keep notes on successful measurements—you’ll build confidence with every batch!

Staple Recipes to Get You Started

Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

These chewy, gooey cookies are a crowd-pleaser and easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup vegan butter (softened)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons aquafaba
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup vegan chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line your baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream the vegan butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add aquafaba and vanilla extract, mixing well.
  4. Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Fold in chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop dough onto the baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  6. Let cool for 5 minutes before enjoying.

Vegan Banana Bread

Perfectly moist, subtly sweet, and full of comforting flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 ripe bananas (mashed)
  • ½ cup coconut oil (melted)
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar
  • 2 flax seed eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. Mix the flour, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  3. In another bowl, combine mashed bananas, melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, flax seed eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually fold dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Stir in walnuts, if using.
  5. Pour the batter into the loaf pan and bake for 55-60 minutes. Cool before slicing.

Vegan Brownies

Fudgy, chocolatey, and indulgent—perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup melted coconut oil
  • ½ cup almond milk
  • ¼ cup applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup vegan chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix sugar, melted coconut oil, almond milk, applesauce, and vanilla extract.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, folding gently. Stir in chocolate chips.
  5. Spread the batter evenly in the pan and bake for 25-30 minutes. Let cool before cutting into squares.

Tips for Elevating Vegan Baked Goods

  1. Flavor Boosters: Enhance flavors with spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, or cardamom, as well as extracts like almond, lemon, or peppermint.
  2. Experiment with Oils: Use avocado or coconut oil to enrich your baked goods with a slightly nutty or tropical flavor.
  3. Textural Variety: Add nuts, seeds, shredded coconut, dried fruits, or vegan marshmallows to create texture and interest.

Vegan baking is an art that rewards creativity and curiosity. With the right ingredients and techniques, you can turn out delectable treats that rival their traditional counterparts. Whether it’s cookies, cakes, or bread, every recipe can be reinvented with plant-based substitutes and personalized touches.

Dive in, experiment boldly, and share your creations—because vegan baking isn’t just about what you leave out; it’s about what you add in to craft delicious, guilt-free indulgences. Happy baking!
