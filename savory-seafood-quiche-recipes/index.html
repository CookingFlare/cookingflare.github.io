Savory Seafood Quiche Recipes


Quiche, with its rich custard filling nestled in buttery pastry, has long been a beloved dish for brunch aficionados and dinner party hosts alike. Elevate this classic French tart by adding the fresh, vibrant flavors of the sea—shrimp, crab, smoked salmon, scallops, and more. A savory seafood quiche strikes the perfect balance between creamy, flaky, and briny, creating a centerpiece that is both elegant and comforting.

In this comprehensive guide, you will discover a variety of seafood quiche recipes, tips for perfecting the crust, and ideas to customize your quiche to suit any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a kitchen novice, these recipes will inspire you to bring coastal charm to your table.

Understanding the Building Blocks: What Makes a Great Seafood Quiche?

Before diving into recipes, let’s briefly explore the essentials of a standout seafood quiche:

The Crust

A flaky and tender crust is crucial—it forms the vessel for your flavorful filling. Some prefer a traditional buttery shortcrust pastry, while others opt for a gluten-free almond flour crust or even a savory crustless version for simplicity and lightness.

The Custard Filling

Classic quiche filling combines eggs with cream or milk to create a silky custard. When paired with seafood, the custard acts as a mellow canvas, allowing the seafood’s natural sweetness and saltiness to shine through.

The Seafood

Fresh or properly thawed frozen seafood makes the difference. Shrimp, crab meat, scallops, smoked salmon—each brings unique tastes and textures. Pre-cooking seafood gently ensures it remains tender without becoming rubbery after baking.

Complementary Ingredients

Adding fresh herbs, vegetables like spinach or leeks, and appropriate cheese enhances flavor complexity. Gruyère, cheddar, or feta cheese can provide a creamy and slightly tangy counterpoint to seafood’s richness.

Recipe 1: Classic Shrimp and Spinach Seafood Quiche

A timeless combination, shrimp and spinach create a quiche that’s both hearty and fresh.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made savory shortcrust pastry or homemade crust
  • 1 cup cooked shrimp, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup fresh baby spinach, roughly chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the crust: Roll out your pastry and line a 9-inch pie dish. Prick the base with a fork. Blind bake for 10 minutes using pie weights or dry beans to prevent puffing. Remove weights and bake an additional 5 minutes. Set aside.

  3. Cook spinach and aromatics: In a skillet over medium heat, melt butter, then sauté onion and garlic until translucent. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.

  4. Mix custard: In a mixing bowl, whisk eggs, cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.

  5. Combine filling: Stir shrimp, spinach mixture, and Gruyère cheese into the custard.

  6. Assemble and bake: Pour the mixture into the partially baked crust. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden. A knife inserted into the center should come out clean.

  7. Rest before serving: Let the quiche cool for 10 minutes so the filling firms up.

Serve warm with a crisp green salad or roasted vegetables.

Recipe 2: Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche with Feta

Smoked salmon adds a delicate smoky depth while dill and feta provide herbal brightness and creaminess.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry or shortcrust pastry, to cover a 9-inch quiche dish
  • 6 oz smoked salmon, cut into strips
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk or half-and-half
  • 3/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon capers (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 small leek, cleaned and finely chopped
  • Butter or olive oil for sautéing

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven: To 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare crust: Line your baking dish with pastry and blind bake as described above.

  3. Sauté leek: Heat butter or olive oil in a pan; cook until soft but not browned. Cool slightly.

  4. Whisk custard: Beat eggs and milk or half-and-half together. Season with salt and pepper.

  5. Build filling: Gently fold in smoked salmon, sautéed leeks, dill, crumbled feta, and capers if using.

  6. Bake: Pour into crust and bake 35–40 minutes or until just set.

  7. Cool and serve: Let sit for 10 minutes and serve with lemon wedges for added zest.

Recipe 3: Crab and Asparagus Delight Quiche

This one brings in fresh asparagus tips and sweet crab meat for a sophisticated flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust for a 9-inch dish
  • 1 cup cooked lump crab meat
  • 6-8 asparagus spears, trimmed and blanched
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded sharp white cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 small shallot, finely diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh ground pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Blind bake crust: As before.

  3. Prepare asparagus: Blanch asparagus in boiling salted water for 2 minutes, immediately transfer to ice water, then pat dry and slice into 1½-inch pieces.

  4. Sauté shallots: In olive oil until softened but not browned.

  5. Mix custard: In a bowl whisk eggs, cream, salt, and pepper.

  6. Assemble filling: Stir in crab meat, asparagus, sautéed shallots, and cheddar cheese.

  7. Pour mixture into crust, sprinkle Parmesan on top.

  8. Bake for 35-40 minutes until golden and set.

  9. Rest and serve: Allow to cool for a little before slicing.

Tips for Perfecting Your Seafood Quiche

  • Don’t overcook seafood before adding it to the quiche—it will continue to cook in the oven and can become tough.
  • Blind baking the crust prevents it from becoming soggy once the custard is poured in.
  • Try a light hand with salt—seafood and cheeses often add enough natural saltiness.
  • Customize with fresh herbs: Tarragon, chives, or basil pair wonderfully with seafood.
  • Let quiche cool before slicing to achieve clean pieces with neat edges.

Variations to Spark Creativity

  • Scallop and Mushroom Quiche: Sauté scallops and wild mushrooms with thyme for a rich umami-packed quiche.
  • Lobster and Corn Quiche: Use fresh lobster chunks and sweet corn kernels for a luxurious, summery twist.
  • Shrimp and Artichoke Quiche: Combine tender shrimp with marinated artichoke hearts for tangy zest.
  • Crab and Jalapeño Pepper Quiche: Add a kick of heat with diced jalapeños balancing savory crab sweetness.

Serving Suggestions and Pairings

Seafood quiche fits beautifully into brunch, lunch, or a light dinner spread. Here are some ideas to elevate the dining experience:

  • Side salads: Think peppery arugula with lemon vinaigrette, or a crunchy fennel and orange salad.
  • Roasted seasonal veggies: Asparagus, cherry tomatoes, or young carrots bring color and texture.
  • Fresh bread: Crusty baguette or herbed focaccia complements creamy quiche.
  • Beverages: Pair with crisp white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc, light rosé, or sparkling water with lemon for a refreshing contrast.

With these flavorful seafood quiche recipes and tips, you have all it takes to bring a touch of the ocean’s charm to your kitchen. From the flaky crust to the tender seafood filling, each bite offers a rich, savory experience perfect for celebrations or casual gatherings. Try experimenting with different seafood and herbs and discover your signature seafood quiche!
