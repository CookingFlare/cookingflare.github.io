How to Grill Scallops and Shellfish


Grilling scallops and shellfish is an art that combines the natural sweetness of the sea with the smoky, charred flavor of an open flame. Whether you’re a seafood lover or a curious cook looking to elevate your summer grilling skills, mastering the techniques behind perfectly grilled scallops and shellfish will take your outdoor cooking to a new level of deliciousness.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know—from selecting fresh scallops and shellfish to preparing, seasoning, and grilling them to juicy, tender perfection. Expect expert tips, foolproof recipes, and creative serving suggestions that will impress your guests and bring the flavors of the ocean right to your plate.

Understanding Your Ingredients: The Basics of Scallops and Shellfish

Before firing up the grill, it’s essential to understand the characteristics of scallops and shellfish to ensure the best preparation and cooking methods.

Scallops

Scallops are prized for their tender texture and subtly sweet flavor. They come in two types:

  • Sea scallops: Larger and ideal for grilling due to their size and firm flesh.
  • Bay scallops: Smaller and more delicate; better suited for sautéing or smaller cooking methods.

Look for scallops that are creamy white with a slight sheen and no strong odor. Avoid any with a slimy texture or discoloration.

Shellfish

The term “shellfish” encompasses a broad category, including:

  • Clams
  • Mussels
  • Oysters
  • Lobster tails
  • Shrimp
  • Crab legs

Many shellfish varieties can be grilled either directly on the shell or after shucking. Each has its ideal grilling approach, which we’ll explore below.

Preparing Scallops and Shellfish for the Grill

Preparation is key. Properly cleaning and seasoning your seafood ensures a flavorful, safe, and successful grilling experience.

Cleaning Scallops

  • If using sea scallops, rinse them under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  • Remove the small side muscle (the “foot”) if present; it can be tough.

Cleaning Shellfish

  • Clams and mussels: Scrub shells under cold water. Discard any with cracked or open shells that don’t close when tapped.
  • Oysters: Use an oyster knife to shuck, or grill whole in the shell to open them naturally.
  • Shrimp: Peel and devein, leaving tails on for better flavor and handling.
  • Lobster tails: Cut shell tops down the middle to expose meat for easier grilling.

Marinating and Seasoning

While scallops and shellfish are flavorful on their own, a simple marinade or seasoning can enhance their taste without overpowering their natural ocean essence.

  • Basic marinade: Olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  • Spicy option: Add chili flakes or smoked paprika for subtle heat.
  • Herbal touch: Use fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or tarragon.

Marinate scallops and shellfish for no longer than 30 minutes to prevent the acid from cooking or toughening the meat.

Grilling Scallops: Techniques and Tips

Grilling scallops requires precision to get that perfect caramelized crust and tender inside. Here’s a detailed method:

1. Preheat the Grill

Set your grill to a high heat (around 450-500°F). Scallops cook quickly, and a hot grill gives a nice sear.

2. Skewer or Use a Grill Basket

  • Skewers: Thread 3-4 scallops per skewer to make turning easier. Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes beforehand to prevent burning.
  • Grill basket: Ideal to avoid scallops falling through grates.

3. Oil the Grates

Brush the grill grates with a high smoke point oil (like grapeseed or canola) to prevent sticking.

4. Grill Time

  • Place scallops on the grill.
  • Cook for 2-3 minutes on one side until opaque with grill marks.
  • Flip and grill for an additional 2 minutes.
  • Avoid overcooking; scallops turn rubbery when done.

5. Serve

Plate immediately, perhaps drizzled with a bit of fresh lemon juice or herb butter.

Grilling Shellfish: Methods for Different Types

Shellfish grill best when you understand their unique textures and cooking needs.

Clams and Mussels

  • Grill in their shells directly on the grill.
  • Heat until shells open (~5-7 minutes).
  • Serve with melted butter or a garlic-white wine sauce.
  • Discard any unopened shells.

Oysters

  • Place oysters cup side down on the grill.
  • Close the lid and cook for 4-6 minutes until shells pop open slightly.
  • Carefully open, add a squeeze of lemon, hot sauce, or a compound butter.
  • Grill for an additional 1 minute if desired.

Shrimp

  • Thread peeled and deveined shrimp onto skewers.
  • Grill over medium-high heat for 2 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  • Brush with garlic butter or a citrus glaze mid-way.

Lobster Tails

  • Split the shell on the top, expose the meat.
  • Grill flesh side down for 5-6 minutes.
  • Flip and cook shell side down for another 4-5 minutes.
  • Baste with lemon butter regularly.

Crab Legs

  • Usually pre-cooked, so grilling is for heating and flavor.
  • Place legs directly on grill and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  • Serve with drawn butter and lemon wedges.

Flavor Combinations and Serving Suggestions

Grilled scallops and shellfish pair wonderfully with fresh, bright flavors and complementary sides.

Sauces and Toppings

  • Citrus Herb Butter: Melt butter with lemon zest, parsley, and garlic.
  • Chimichurri: Fresh parsley and cilantro blend with garlic and chili.
  • Mango Salsa: Diced mango, red onion, jalapeno, cilantro, and lime juice.
  • Garlic Aioli: Creamy dip with roasted garlic, lemon, and olive oil.

Side Dishes

  • Grilled Vegetables: Asparagus, zucchini, bell peppers, and corn.
  • Light Salads: Arugula with shaved fennel or a tomato and cucumber salad.
  • Rice or Quinoa: Flavored with herbs, lemon, and toasted nuts for texture.
  • Crusty Bread: Perfect for soaking up juices and sauces.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

  1. Overcooking: Scallops and shellfish become tough and rubbery if cooked too long. Stick closely to grilling times.
  2. Using low heat: Without a hot grill, you won’t get a nice sear, and seafood will steam instead of char.
  3. Not oiling the grill: Seafood sticks to grates without oil, tearing the delicate meat.
  4. Skipping cleaning: Dirt or sand in shellfish ruins texture and flavor.
  5. Marinating too long: Acid in marinades can “cook” scallops prematurely and make shellfish rubbery.

Step-by-Step Recipe: Grilled Scallops with Lemon Garlic Butter

Ingredients:

  • 12 sea scallops
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers (soaked)

Lemon Garlic Butter:

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to high heat.
  2. Pat scallops dry and season with salt, pepper, and olive oil.
  3. Thread scallops onto skewers (3-4 per skewer).
  4. Grill scallops for 2-3 minutes on each side until opaque with grill marks.
  5. In a small saucepan, melt butter over low heat, add garlic, lemon zest, juice, and parsley, stirring for 2 minutes.
  6. Remove scallops from grill, drizzle with lemon garlic butter, and serve immediately.

Final Thoughts: Elevate Your Grilling Game with Scallops and Shellfish

Grilling scallops and shellfish need not be intimidating. With the right knowledge, some patience, and a few easy preparation steps, you can enjoy the coastal charm of perfectly grilled seafood at home. Experiment with flavors, explore different shellfish varieties, and remember that the grill’s heat is your ally in creating that memorable ocean-inspired meal everyone will rave about.

Next time you reach for seafood at the market, grab a mix of scallops and shellfish and give them the grill treatment. Your taste buds will thank you!
