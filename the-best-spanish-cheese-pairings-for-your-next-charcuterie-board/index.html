The Best Spanish Cheese Pairings for Your Next Charcuterie Board


Charcuterie boards have evolved into an art form, combining textures, flavors, and colors to create an edible masterpiece. When crafting a Spanish-inspired charcuterie board, the rich and diverse world of Spanish cheeses offers an abundance of pairing opportunities. From sweet to savory, mild to bold, the possibilities are endless. Let’s explore the best Spanish cheese pairings to create a mouthwatering charcuterie board that celebrates the flavors of Spain.

Manchego: The Iconic Spanish Cheese

No Spanish charcuterie board would be complete without Manchego, the crown jewel of Spanish cheeses. Made from sheep’s milk in the La Mancha region, Manchego boasts a nutty, buttery flavor that intensifies as it ages. Its natural pairing versatility makes it an anchor for any board.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Pair a wedge of aged Manchego with a dollop of quince paste (membrillo) for a classic combination. Its slightly salty and nutty profile also pairs beautifully with Marcona almonds, Spanish olives, and a drizzle of honey. For a more savory twist, team it with slices of chorizo or smoked ham like Jamón Serrano or Jamón Ibérico.

Mahón: The Balearic Island Beauty

Hailing from the island of Menorca, Mahón is a semi-hard cheese made from cow’s milk. It offers a tangy, almost citrusy flavor with a creamy, salty finish that reflects the coastal breezes of the Balearic Islands. Aged Mahón brings nuttier, sharper undertones to the palate.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Pair Mahón with roasted red peppers or sun-dried tomatoes for a Mediterranean flair. Its acidity is complemented by a robust red wine or cava, and it partners excellently with smoked meats like lomo embuchado. For an added touch of sweetness, consider pairing it with dried figs or apricots.

Idiazabal: Smoky and Bold

Crafted in the Basque and Navarra regions, Idiazabal is a smoked sheep’s milk cheese with a firm texture and an earthy, nutty flavor. The subtle smokiness of Idiazabal adds complexity to your charcuterie board.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Idiazabal pairs well with thinly sliced chorizo or morcilla sausage, as its smokiness complements the spiced meat. For a surprising sweet-and-savory mix, serve it alongside orange marmalade or fig jam. Add a handful of walnuts or hazelnuts to the mix for extra texture.

Cabrales: A Creamy Blue Cheese

Cabrales, a prized blue cheese from the Asturias region, is robust and intensely flavored. Made from a blend of cow, sheep, and goat milk, this creamy blue cheese is aged in natural caves, giving it a tangy, slightly salty profile that’s not for the faint of heart.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    To balance Cabrales’ boldness, pair it with a drizzle of Spanish honey or fresh pears. Its strong flavor also complements crusty French bread or spiced cured meats like chorizo. A sweet dessert wine, such as Pedro Ximénez sherry, can cut through its pungency and enhance its complexity.

Tetilla: The Comfort Cheese

Tetilla is a mild, buttery cheese from Galicia, named for its signature cone-like shape. Made from cow’s milk, it’s soft and creamy with a mild tang, making it a crowd-pleaser on any charcuterie board.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    This versatile cheese pairs beautifully with green apples or grapes for a refreshing contrast. Tetilla’s creaminess also complements slices of salchichón, a Spanish salami, or pickled vegetables like guindilla peppers. Try spreading Tetilla on rustic bread for a comforting bite.

Los Beyos: A Rare Gem

Los Beyos is a little-known artisanal cheese from Asturias, made with cow, sheep, or goat milk, depending on the variety. It’s slightly crumbly with a rich, tangy, and earthy flavor profile, perfect for those seeking an authentic taste of Spain.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Combine Los Beyos with a sliver of guava paste or mountain honey for a sweet contrast, or pair it with dry-cured meats like cecina (smoked beef). A light Spanish beer or a crisp white wine like Albariño further heightens its unique flavor.

Torta del Casar: A Spoonable Delight

Torta del Casar is a creamy, spreadable sheep’s milk cheese from Extremadura. Its texture is incredibly smooth, and it has an intense, slightly bitter finish due to the use of wild thistle rennet. This cheese is a showstopper and adds an elegant touch to any charcuterie board.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Serve Torta del Casar with soft, crusty bread or breadsticks for dipping. It’s heavenly with roasted nuts, like hazelnuts or almonds, and works wonders when paired with dried fruit like raisins or prunes. For beverages, a robust red wine or dry sherry is your best bet.

Roncal: The Rustic Shepherd’s Cheese

Roncal, from the Navarre region, is one of Spain’s oldest cheeses. Made from sheep’s milk, it has a firm texture and a nutty, slightly tangy flavor. Its aging process imparts a savory depth that makes it a staple for traditional charcuterie.

  • Suggested Pairings:
    Add Roncal to your board alongside piquillo peppers or artichoke hearts for a rustic, earthy pairing. Pairing this cheese with a slice of dry-cured chorizo or a handful of Spanish olives highlights its rich, umami-forward profile. A full-bodied Rioja wine enhances its dynamic flavor.

Creating a Balanced Charcuterie Board

When assembling your Spanish charcuterie board, focus on balance. Incorporate a mix of textures—hard, semi-soft, and creamy cheeses—to keep things interesting. Select accompaniments that complement different flavor profiles, such as sweet (honey, fruit), salty (olives, cured meats), and acidic (pickles, marmalades).

Add visual appeal by arranging the cheeses and their pairings in sections, dividing the board into flairs of color and texture that entice the eyes as much as the taste buds. A variety of crackers and breads, including baguettes or seeded loaves, adds a neutral base that allows the cheeses and their accompaniments to shine.

Beverage Pairings for Your Spanish Board

To complete the experience, choose beverages that enhance the unique qualities of your cheeses and pairings. Spanish wines like Tempranillo, Albariño, and Rioja are excellent choices. For a bubbly option, Cava adds a festive touch. If you prefer beer, opt for a Spanish lager or a sessionable craft brew to cleanse the palate between tastes.

Crafting a charcuterie board with Spanish cheeses is an opportunity to explore the rich culinary traditions of Spain. From the familiar Manchego to the bold Cabrales and the indulgent Torta del Casar, the variety of Spanish cheeses ensures there’s something for everyone. With thoughtful pairings and a touch of creativity, your next charcuterie board will transport your guests to the heart of Spain, one bite at a time.
