Easy One-Pot Fall Meals: 7 Recipes for Busy Weeknights


As the days get shorter and the air grows crisp, there’s no better time to embrace the comforting warmth of hearty fall meals. Whether you’re looking for something filling after a busy day at work or craving the familiar flavors of autumn, one-pot recipes are a lifesaver. These meals are not only easy to prepare but also perfect for cozy evenings spent with family or friends.

The beauty of one-pot dishes is that they simplify your cooking process while delivering rich, savory flavors. From savory stews and soups to creamy risottos and casseroles, here are seven easy one-pot fall meals that will quickly become your weeknight go-to recipes.

1. Hearty Pumpkin and Sausage Stew

Nothing says fall quite like pumpkin. This hearty, comforting stew combines the sweetness of pumpkin with the richness of sausage for a delicious meal that will warm you from the inside out.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Italian sausage (ground)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of pumpkin puree
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned. Remove the sausage and set it aside, leaving a bit of fat in the pot.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the potatoes, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir for a minute to combine.
  4. Pour in the pumpkin puree and chicken broth, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes until the potatoes are tender.
  5. Stir in the cream and cooked sausage. Simmer for another 5 minutes to blend the flavors.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with crusty bread.

This pumpkin and sausage stew is the perfect blend of creamy, savory, and slightly sweet. It’s an ideal one-pot dish for a chilly fall evening.

2. One-Pot Apple Cider Chicken and Rice

The combination of tender chicken, fragrant rice, and the subtle sweetness of apple cider makes this one-pot dish a standout for fall. It’s an easy, flavor-packed meal that requires minimal cleanup, making it perfect for busy nights.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 ½ cups apple cider
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 apple, chopped (optional)

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of cinnamon. Sear the chicken in the pot until golden brown on both sides, about 5-7 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion for 2 minutes until softened. Add the rice and stir to coat with the oil and onion.
  3. Pour in the apple cider and chicken broth. Stir in the cinnamon, sage, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is fully cooked.
  5. If using, add the chopped apple during the last 10 minutes of cooking for an extra touch of fall flavor.
  6. Serve hot with a drizzle of the cooking liquid.

This dish is the perfect balance of savory and sweet, with the apple cider adding a unique depth of flavor that makes this one-pot meal a winner.

3. Butternut Squash and Kale Risotto

Fall is the time for creamy, comforting dishes, and risotto is the perfect choice. This one-pot butternut squash and kale risotto combines the rich flavors of autumn vegetables with creamy, tender rice for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 2 cups chopped kale
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced butternut squash and cook for 5-7 minutes, until it starts to soften. Remove the squash from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion until soft and translucent, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir for 2 minutes, allowing it to lightly toast.
  4. Pour in the white wine and cook, stirring, until the liquid is mostly absorbed.
  5. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one cup at a time, stirring frequently and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more.
  6. Once the rice is tender and creamy, stir in the kale, cooked butternut squash, and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy the creamy texture and earthy flavors.

This risotto is the ultimate fall comfort food, perfect for a cozy dinner on a chilly night.

4. One-Pot Beef and Sweet Potato Chili

This beef and sweet potato chili is a perfect fall recipe to warm up after a busy day. The combination of hearty beef, sweet potatoes, and bold spices creates a comforting and filling dish that will keep you coming back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Remove excess fat if necessary.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté for 3-5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the diced sweet potatoes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix to combine.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, and beef broth. Stir well, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat and simmer, covered, for 30-40 minutes until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro if desired.

This hearty chili is not only packed with flavor but also loaded with nutrients, making it an ideal choice for a balanced weeknight meal.

5. Creamy White Bean and Spinach Soup

This creamy white bean and spinach soup is a light yet satisfying dish that’s perfect for fall. It’s rich in flavor and texture but easy to prepare, making it an ideal one-pot meal for busy nights.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans white beans (15 oz each), drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a bit of olive oil over medium heat until softened.
  2. Add the white beans and vegetable broth. Stir in the rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Using an immersion blender, blend part of the soup to create a creamy base, leaving some beans whole for texture. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can blend the soup in batches in a regular blender.
  4. Stir in the chopped spinach and heavy cream. Simmer for another 5 minutes until the spinach wilts.
  5. Serve warm with a slice of crusty bread.

This creamy soup is light, healthy, and packed with fall flavors—perfect for those nights when you want something comforting but not too heavy.

6. One-Pot Spaghetti Bolognese

This one-pot spaghetti Bolognese is a twist on the traditional Italian classic. With rich, savory flavors and minimal cleanup, it’s a fall favorite that can easily be put together in under an hour.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (28 oz)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the ground beef, onion, and garlic, cooking until the beef is browned and the onions are softened.
  2. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, beef broth, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer.
  3. Break the spaghetti in half and add it to the pot. Stir to ensure the pasta is submerged in the sauce.
  4. Cover the pot and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the pasta is al dente and has absorbed much of the sauce.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil if desired.

This one-pot spaghetti Bolognese offers the same deep, rich flavor you’d expect from a slow-cooked sauce, but it’s ready in no time.

7. Crispy Chicken and Brussels Sprouts Skillet

For a quick and nutritious dinner, this crispy chicken and Brussels sprouts skillet is a perfect fall meal. The crispy chicken paired with roasted Brussels sprouts makes for a balanced, flavorful dish that’s easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lemon wedges for serving

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the chicken skin-side down in the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes until the skin is crispy.
  4. Flip the chicken over and add the halved Brussels sprouts to the skillet around the chicken.
  5. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the Brussels sprouts are tender and slightly crispy.
  6. Serve with fresh lemon wedges for a zesty finish.

This one-pot crispy chicken and Brussels sprouts skillet is an excellent choice for a simple yet flavorful fall meal.

With these seven easy one-pot fall meals, you’ll have everything you need to enjoy a delicious and comforting dinner without spending hours in the kitchen. From hearty stews to creamy risottos, these recipes are perfect for busy weeknights when you need something quick, satisfying, and packed with the cozy flavors of the season.
