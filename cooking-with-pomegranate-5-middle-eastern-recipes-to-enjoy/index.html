Cooking with Pomegranate: 5 Middle Eastern Recipes to Enjoy


Pomegranate, often referred to as the “jewel of winter,” is a fruit that has captivated culinary enthusiasts for centuries. Its vibrant red seeds, known as arils, burst with flavor and offer a delightful balance of sweetness and tartness. In Middle Eastern cuisine, pomegranate is not just a garnish; it is a key ingredient that enhances the depth of flavors in various dishes. In this blog post, we will explore five delectable Middle Eastern recipes that showcase the versatility of pomegranate, allowing you to enjoy its unique taste in your cooking.

1. Pomegranate Molasses Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh pomegranate seeds for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine pomegranate molasses, olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken thighs, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  3. Bake the Chicken: Place the marinated chicken thighs in a baking dish, skin side up. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and the skin is crispy.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh pomegranate seeds and chopped parsley before serving. This dish pairs beautifully with rice or a fresh salad.

2. Pomegranate and Walnut Tabbouleh

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bulgur: In a large bowl, combine bulgur wheat and boiling water. Cover and let it sit for about 30 minutes until the bulgur is tender and the water is absorbed.

  2. Mix the Ingredients: Fluff the bulgur with a fork and add the chopped parsley, mint, pomegranate seeds, and walnuts.

  3. Dress the Salad: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Pour the dressing over the bulgur mixture and toss gently to combine.

  4. Chill and Serve: Let the tabbouleh sit for at least 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled or at room temperature as a refreshing side dish.

3. Pomegranate-Glazed Eggplant

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh pomegranate seeds for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggplant: Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat. Brush the eggplant slices with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.

  2. Grill the Eggplant: Grill the eggplant slices for about 4-5 minutes on each side until they are tender and have nice grill marks.

  3. Glaze with Pomegranate: In a small saucepan, heat pomegranate molasses and minced garlic over low heat until warmed. Drizzle the glaze over the grilled eggplant slices.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Transfer the glazed eggplant to a serving platter and garnish with fresh pomegranate seeds and chopped cilantro. This dish makes a fantastic appetizer or side dish.
