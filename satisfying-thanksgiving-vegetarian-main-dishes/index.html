Satisfying Thanksgiving Vegetarian Main Dishes


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones and indulge in a feast of gratitude and warmth. While the holiday may traditionally center around a turkey, vegetarians don’t need to feel left out of the celebrations. In fact, Thanksgiving offers an excellent opportunity to showcase the incredible flavors and textures that plant-based dishes can bring to the table. Whether you’re hosting vegetarian guests or embracing a meat-free lifestyle yourself, these satisfying Thanksgiving vegetarian main dishes will be the star of the spread, ensuring everyone leaves the table with full bellies and happy hearts.

Stuffed Acorn Squash with Wild Rice and Cranberries

Acorn squash has a natural sweetness and creamy texture that makes it the perfect vessel for a hearty stuffing. By filling the squash halves with a blend of wild rice, cranberries, pecans, and fresh herbs, you create an elegant and nourishing dish that embodies the flavors of fall.

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup wild rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh or dried cranberries
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Brush the cut side of each acorn squash with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place them cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 25 minutes, or until tender.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil and add the wild rice. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 40 minutes, or until the rice is tender.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the cranberries and pecans, allowing them to toast lightly in the pan. Add the cooked wild rice and thyme, stirring to combine.
  4. Flip the roasted squash halves over and fill each cavity generously with the rice mixture. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 15 minutes. Serve warm and garnish with fresh thyme sprigs if desired.

Savory Mushroom and Lentil Wellington

A vegetarian Wellington is a stunning, crowd-pleasing main dish that combines earthy mushrooms, protein-packed lentils, and flaky pastry. Seasoned with fragrant herbs and spices, this dish offers layers of flavor and texture that evoke the spirit of Thanksgiving.

Ingredients:

  • 2 sheets of puff pastry (thawed if frozen)
  • 2 cups finely chopped mushrooms (such as cremini or shiitake)
  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1 small leek, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp fresh rosemary, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 egg (for egg wash, optional for non-vegan)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat, then sauté the leeks, garlic, and mushrooms until they are tender and any liquid has evaporated.
  2. Stir in the lentils, soy sauce, rosemary, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper and allow the mixture to cool slightly.
  3. On a floured surface, roll out one sheet of puff pastry. Spread the mushroom-lentil filling in a long mound down the center, leaving a border around the edges. Carefully fold the pastry over the filling, pinching the edges to seal.
  4. Place the Wellington seam-side down on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Brush with the egg wash or plant-based milk for a golden finish. Bake for 30–35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and flaky. Slice and serve warm.

Butternut Squash Lasagna with Sage Bechamel

Lasagna is always a comforting choice, and this version swaps traditional tomato sauce for a silky sage-infused bechamel layered with roasted butternut squash and spinach. This festive dish is creamy, satisfying, and packed with the colors of autumn.

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles (use gluten-free if necessary)
  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 3 cups fresh spinach
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter or plant-based butter
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups milk (or plant-based milk)
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tsp fresh sage, minced
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella (or vegan alternative)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast for vegan)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then roast on a baking sheet for 25–30 minutes, or until tender. Mash the squash lightly with a fork.
  2. While the squash is roasting, prepare the bechamel sauce. Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat, then whisk in the flour to create a roux. Slowly add the milk, whisking constantly until the sauce thickens. Stir in the nutmeg, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  4. Assemble the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of sage bechamel at the bottom of a greased baking dish. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, followed by mashed squash, spinach, shredded mozzarella, and Parmesan. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a generous layer of bechamel and cheese on top.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then remove foil and bake an additional 15 minutes, or until the top is bubbling and golden.

Portobello Mushroom Pot Pies

Individual pot pies made with meaty Portobello mushrooms and rich gravy are an excellent way to offer personalized servings at the Thanksgiving table. These pies boast a golden, flaky crust and a hearty filling that warms you from the inside out.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushrooms, chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1 stalk celery, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 sheet puff pastry (thawed if frozen)
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Heat olive oil in a skillet and sauté the onion, carrot, celery, and mushrooms until tender, about 8 minutes. Sprinkle with flour and stir to coat.
  2. Gradually add the vegetable broth, stirring until the mixture thickens into a gravy-like consistency. Add thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Divide the filling among oven-safe ramekins or small dishes. Cut the puff pastry into rounds large enough to cover each ramekin, then press the edges firmly against the rim to seal.
  4. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown. Serve hot and enjoy the comforting flavors of this dish.

Herbed Polenta Bake with Roasted Vegetables

For a simpler yet equally satisfying dish, consider a baked polenta casserole. Topped with roasted seasonal vegetables and finished with a drizzle of olive oil and fresh herbs, this dish is both hearty and visually stunning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup polenta
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan (or vegan alternative)
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tsp fresh rosemary, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Toss the bell pepper, zucchini, yellow squash, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Roast on a baking sheet for 20 minutes, until caramelized.
  2. Bring the vegetable broth to a boil in a saucepan. Gradually whisk in the polenta and cook, stirring often, until the mixture becomes thick and creamy (about 5 minutes). Stir in the Parmesan and butter.
  3. Pour the polenta into a greased baking dish and spread evenly. Top with the roasted vegetables, pressing them gently into the surface.
  4. Bake at 375°F for 15 minutes, or until the casserole is fully heated. Serve warm with additional rosemary for garnish.

From creamy casseroles to flaky pastry enclosures, vegetarian Thanksgiving main dishes offer plenty of options to please everyone at the table. These recipes prove that a meat-free feast can be just as satisfying, flavorful, and celebratory as a traditional one. Whether you’re planning your own vegetarian spread or simply want to add some creative flair to the menu, embrace the versatility of these dishes and create a holiday filled with delicious memories.
