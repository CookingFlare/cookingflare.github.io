Sous Vide Corn on the Cob: A Summertime Favorite with a Twist


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious corn on the cob. But why settle for the same old boiled or grilled corn when you can take it to the next level with the sous vide cooking technique? Sous vide corn on the cob is a game-changer that will elevate your summer cookouts and leave your guests begging for more.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s quickly talk about sous vide cooking. Sous vide is a French cooking technique that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method allows for precise control of the cooking temperature and ensures that the food is evenly cooked from edge to edge.

Why Sous Vide Corn on the Cob?

So, why should you try sous vide corn on the cob? Well, the sous vide technique allows the corn to cook gently and evenly, resulting in kernels that are tender, juicy, and bursting with flavor. Plus, cooking the corn in a vacuum-sealed bag helps to lock in the natural sweetness and nutrients, making it even more delicious and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • Optional toppings: grated Parmesan cheese, chopped herbs, or a squeeze of lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 183°F (84°C).
  2. Place the husked corn in a large vacuum-sealed bag, ensuring that the kernels are in a single layer.
  3. Add the butter, salt, and pepper to the bag, distributing them evenly over the corn.
  4. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.
  5. Gently lower the bag into the preheated water bath, ensuring that it is fully submerged.
  6. Cook the corn for 1 hour, maintaining the water bath temperature at 183°F (84°C).
  7. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and let it rest for a few minutes.
  8. Open the bag and transfer the corn to a serving platter.
  9. If desired, top the corn with grated Parmesan cheese, chopped herbs, or a squeeze of lime juice for an extra burst of flavor.
  10. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations:

  • For a smoky twist, you can char the sous vide corn on the cob on a hot grill for a few minutes after cooking.
  • Experiment with different seasonings and spices to customize your sous vide corn. Try adding garlic powder, smoked paprika, or chili flakes for a kick of heat.
  • Add a touch of sweetness by brushing the corn with honey or maple syrup before sealing it in the bag.
  • For a creamy finish, you can slather the cooked sous vide corn with mayonnaise or sour cream and sprinkle it with chili powder or Tajin seasoning.

Final Thoughts:

Sous vide corn on the cob is a simple yet impressive dish that will take your summertime cookouts to new heights. The precise temperature control of sous vide cooking ensures that every kernel is cooked to perfection, resulting in tender, juicy, and flavorful corn that will leave you craving more. So, why settle for ordinary when you can have extraordinary? Give sous vide corn on the cob a try this summer, and get ready to wow your taste buds and impress your guests. Enjoy!
