How to Make and Can Homemade Tomato Paste


If you’ve ever tasted homemade tomato paste, you know it’s far superior to store-bought versions. The rich, deep flavor and velvety texture are unmatched, and when you can your own, it gives you the satisfaction of preserving summer’s bounty for the months to come. Whether you’ve grown your own tomatoes or picked them up fresh at the farmer’s market, making and canning tomato paste is a rewarding project that’s well worth the effort. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make tomato paste from scratch, step-by-step, and how to can it for long-term storage.

Why Make Homemade Tomato Paste?

Tomato paste is a kitchen staple that enhances the flavor of everything from pasta sauces to soups, stews, and pizza. While store-bought versions are convenient, they often contain added preservatives, artificial colors, and excessive salt. Homemade tomato paste, on the other hand, is made from fresh, ripe tomatoes and requires no unnecessary additives. Plus, by canning your tomato paste, you can enjoy the essence of fresh tomatoes throughout the entire year, giving your cooking an authentic, homegrown flavor.

The Benefits of Canning Tomato Paste

Canning tomato paste is an excellent way to preserve the tomato harvest. Here are some reasons why you should consider making and canning your own paste:

  • Cost-effective: Tomatoes are often in season in abundance, making this a frugal way to store the flavor of summer. When you can your own paste, it can be more affordable than buying small cans of store-bought paste year-round.
  • Control over Ingredients: You get full control over what goes into your paste. No added sugar, preservatives, or artificial flavors.
  • Long Shelf Life: Canning tomato paste gives it a long shelf life of up to a year or more when stored properly.
  • Rich Flavor: Homemade tomato paste is much more flavorful, giving your dishes a deep, concentrated tomato taste.

What You’ll Need

Before diving into the process of making and canning your tomato paste, gather the following tools and ingredients:

Ingredients

  • Fresh Ripe Tomatoes: You’ll need about 12 pounds of ripe Roma or plum tomatoes to make a batch of tomato paste. These varieties are ideal because they have fewer seeds and a meaty texture that’s perfect for paste.
  • Salt: Just a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor of the paste. You can adjust to taste later.
  • Lemon Juice or Citric Acid: This will help maintain the acidity level necessary for safe canning.

Tools

  • Large Stockpot: To cook the tomatoes.
  • Blender or Food Mill: To puree the tomatoes and remove the skins and seeds.
  • Saucepan or Wide Skillet: To cook the tomato paste down.
  • Canning Jars: Quart or pint-sized glass jars with proper sealing lids.
  • Canning Funnel: To easily fill your jars without making a mess.
  • Lid Lifter: For handling hot lids during the canning process.
  • Jar Tongs: For safely lifting hot jars out of the boiling water.
  • Canning Pot: For the water bath canning method.
  • Strainer or Cheesecloth: To remove excess skins and seeds.

How to Make Tomato Paste: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

Start by washing your tomatoes thoroughly. Cut a small “X” at the bottom of each tomato with a sharp knife. This will help with the peeling process later.

Step 2: Blanch the Tomatoes

To easily remove the skins from the tomatoes, blanch them. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Prepare an ice bath in a large bowl by filling it with cold water and ice. Drop the tomatoes into the boiling water for about 30 seconds, or until the skins start to peel away. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the tomatoes into the ice bath to stop the cooking process.

Once the tomatoes are cool enough to handle, peel off the skins. The skins should come off easily, leaving you with the juicy tomato flesh.

Step 3: Remove the Seeds

After peeling the tomatoes, cut them in half and gently squeeze out the seeds. You can use your fingers or a spoon for this. While it’s not necessary to remove every seed, minimizing their presence will give your paste a smoother consistency.

Step 4: Puree the Tomatoes

Once you’ve peeled and seeded the tomatoes, it’s time to puree them. You can use a blender, food processor, or food mill to do this. For a smoother paste, a food mill works best, as it helps to further remove any remaining skins or seeds. Puree the tomatoes until they reach a smooth, thick consistency.

Step 5: Cook Down the Tomato Puree

Transfer the pureed tomatoes into a large stockpot or wide skillet. Set over medium heat and bring the puree to a simmer. Stir occasionally to ensure the mixture doesn’t burn. This part of the process can take some time—typically 1-2 hours—so be patient. The goal is to cook the mixture down until it reaches a thick, paste-like consistency.

You’ll need to reduce the tomato liquid by about half, and the paste will thicken significantly as it cooks. Stir frequently to prevent the paste from sticking to the bottom of the pan. As the paste thickens, the rich tomato aroma will fill your kitchen!

Step 6: Season the Paste

Once the tomato paste has reached a thick consistency, add salt to taste. You can also add a dash of other spices or herbs if desired, but keeping it simple will give you the purest flavor. Be careful not to over-season, as the paste will become more concentrated during the canning process.

Step 7: Prepare the Jars

While the tomato paste is simmering, it’s a good idea to prepare your canning jars. Sterilize your jars and lids by placing them in a hot water bath for 10 minutes or running them through the dishwasher on the highest heat setting.

If you’re using pint jars, you’ll need about 6-7 jars, depending on how thick your paste is. Make sure the jars are warm before filling them to prevent cracking.

Step 8: Fill the Jars with Tomato Paste

Once the tomato paste is ready and the jars are sterilized, it’s time to can the paste. Using a canning funnel, carefully ladle the hot tomato paste into each jar, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top of the jar. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to ensure there’s no paste residue, which could interfere with the sealing process.

Step 9: Process the Jars in a Water Bath

Place the filled jars into a canning pot or a large pot with a lid, ensuring that the jars are covered by at least an inch of water. Bring the water to a boil, and once boiling, process the jars for 35-40 minutes. This ensures that the paste is fully sterilized and safe for long-term storage.

Step 10: Cool and Store

Once the jars have been processed, carefully remove them from the water bath using jar tongs. Allow the jars to cool completely on a clean towel or cooling rack. You should hear a popping sound as the jars seal. This indicates that the vacuum seal has formed and the jars are properly sealed.

Check the lids after they’ve cooled to ensure they are sealed by pressing down in the center. If it doesn’t pop back, the jar didn’t seal, and it should be refrigerated and used within a few days.

Once sealed, label your jars with the date and store them in a cool, dark, and dry place. Properly canned tomato paste can last up to a year, although it’s best to use it within 6-8 months for optimal flavor.

How to Use Homemade Tomato Paste

Now that you have a pantry full of rich, flavorful tomato paste, it’s time to start cooking! Here are some ideas for using your homemade tomato paste:

  • Pasta Sauces: Use tomato paste as the base for a rich, flavorful tomato sauce.
  • Soups and Stews: Add a tablespoon or two to soups and stews for a concentrated tomato flavor.
  • Pizza: Spread homemade tomato paste on pizza dough for a more flavorful sauce.
  • Braising: Use tomato paste as a base for braising meats, adding depth and richness to the dish.
  • Savory Dips: Combine tomato paste with olive oil, garlic, and herbs for a savory dip or spread.

Final Thoughts

Making and canning homemade tomato paste is a fantastic way to preserve the essence of fresh, summer tomatoes. With a little effort and patience, you’ll have a pantry stocked with rich, flavorful tomato paste that will enhance your cooking for months to come. Plus, the satisfaction of knowing that you made it from scratch adds an extra layer of pride to every dish. Enjoy the fruits of your labor and let your culinary creations shine!
