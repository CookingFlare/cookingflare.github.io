Simple Christmas Recipes to Enjoy With Family


Christmas is a time for family, laughter, and, of course, delicious food. It’s a season to gather around the table, share meals, and create lasting memories. Whether you’re hosting a festive dinner, preparing a cozy breakfast, or looking for fun treats to make with the kids, simple yet delightful recipes can bring the joy of the season into your home. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of easy-to-make Christmas recipes perfect for celebrating with your loved ones.

1. Classic Roast Turkey with Herb Butter

No Christmas meal is complete without a beautifully roasted turkey. The secret to a perfectly golden, juicy turkey is all in the preparation, and a simple herb butter rub is a game-changer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (10-12 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 cups chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Remove the turkey giblets and pat the turkey dry with paper towels.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter with the rosemary, thyme, sage, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Gently lift the skin of the turkey and rub the herb butter mixture underneath the skin and over the entire surface.
  4. Stuff the turkey cavity with the lemon halves and onion quarters.
  5. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour chicken broth into the bottom of the pan.
  6. Roast the turkey for about 13 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). Baste the turkey with the pan juices every 45 minutes.
  7. Let the turkey rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. Serve with your favorite sides and enjoy!

This roast turkey will be the star of your Christmas dinner, boasting a crispy, golden skin and tender, juicy meat that’s bursting with flavor.

2. Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

A vegetable dish that will steal the show, crispy roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon are a savory treat that everyone will love. The combination of earthy Brussels sprouts and crispy bacon creates the perfect side dish to complement your holiday feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Scatter the chopped bacon over the Brussels sprouts.
  5. Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the Brussels sprouts are crispy and golden, stirring halfway through for even cooking.
  6. Once done, drizzle with balsamic vinegar for an extra burst of flavor (optional) and serve warm.

The combination of crispy bacon and roasted Brussels sprouts will add a savory depth to your Christmas spread that’s hard to resist.

3. Cheesy Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a classic side dish that can easily be made festive with a few tweaks. Add cheese, garlic, and a touch of cream to elevate this simple dish into something extraordinary.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes and garlic in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil and cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  2. Drain the potatoes and garlic, then return them to the pot.
  3. Add the butter, cream, and milk, and mash the potatoes until smooth and creamy.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese, salt, and pepper. Continue mashing until the cheese is fully melted and incorporated.
  5. Transfer the mashed potatoes to a serving bowl and serve immediately.

These cheesy garlic mashed potatoes are the ultimate comfort food, bringing a creamy and indulgent flavor that’s perfect for a holiday dinner.

4. Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread cookies are a holiday classic, and making them with your family can be a fun and festive activity. These soft, spiced cookies are perfect for decorating with colorful icing or simply enjoying as they are.

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, molasses, and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  4. Divide the dough in half, wrap each portion in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one portion of dough to about 1/4 inch thick. Cut into desired shapes using cookie cutters.
  7. Place the cookies on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are golden.
  8. Let the cookies cool on a wire rack before decorating with icing, sprinkles, or simply enjoying as they are.

Gingerbread cookies are not just tasty; they also make wonderful gifts or decorations for the Christmas tree. Get creative with your designs and share the joy of baking with your family.

5. Eggnog Pancakes

Start Christmas morning off with a special breakfast that’s sure to impress. These eggnog pancakes are fluffy, aromatic, and flavored with all the holiday spices that make Christmas so memorable.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/4 cups eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, eggnog, vanilla extract, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or butter.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with maple syrup, whipped cream, and a dusting of cinnamon.

These eggnog pancakes add a festive twist to your breakfast and are perfect for creating a cozy holiday morning.

6. Christmas Fruit Salad

For a lighter option that’s still festive, a Christmas fruit salad is a colorful and refreshing addition to your holiday meal. It’s a simple yet vibrant dish that pairs beautifully with the richness of holiday main courses.

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples, diced
  • 1 orange, peeled and segmented
  • 1 cup grapes, halved
  • 1/2 pineapple, peeled and chopped
  • 1 pomegranate, seeds removed
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced apples, orange segments, halved grapes, pineapple, and pomegranate seeds.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the Greek yogurt, honey, and cinnamon until smooth.
  3. Pour the yogurt mixture over the fruit and gently toss to combine.
  4. Chill the fruit salad in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes before serving.

This Christmas fruit salad is a light, healthy option that adds a burst of freshness to your holiday spread. The creamy yogurt dressing ties everything together beautifully.

Conclusion

These simple Christmas recipes are perfect for bringing family together around the table. From the savory delights of roasted turkey and mashed potatoes to the sweet indulgence of gingerbread cookies and eggnog pancakes, each dish is designed to make your Christmas celebrations extra special. Whether you’re cooking for a large gathering or a small intimate family meal, these easy recipes will ensure that your holiday season is filled with delicious moments and cherished memories.

Happy holidays and happy cooking!
