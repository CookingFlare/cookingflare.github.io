Turkey and Wild Rice Soup for Leftovers


Turkey and Wild Rice Soup is the perfect dish to make with leftover turkey, transforming your Thanksgiving or holiday meal remnants into a warm, comforting, and flavorful bowl of soup. This recipe is easy to prepare, packed with nutrients, and full of rich, savory flavors. Whether you’re looking to use up leftover turkey after a big family dinner or simply enjoy a cozy meal on a cold day, this turkey and wild rice soup is sure to satisfy your cravings.

In this post, we’ll walk through the step-by-step process of making this turkey and wild rice soup, provide tips for customization, and offer suggestions on how to store and serve leftovers. Let’s dive into this comforting soup that brings both convenience and taste to your kitchen!

Why Choose Turkey and Wild Rice Soup?

Turkey and Wild Rice Soup is a great way to utilize leftover turkey while creating a meal that’s both healthy and filling. Wild rice, a hearty and nutty grain, adds depth to the soup, complementing the tender turkey meat and vegetables. This soup is rich in protein, fiber, and vitamins, making it a nutritious option for lunch or dinner. The savory broth, combined with a variety of seasonings and the crunch of vegetables, makes for an irresistible dish that your family will love.

The Benefits of Using Leftover Turkey

Using leftover turkey not only reduces food waste, but it also saves you time in the kitchen. After preparing a large turkey for a holiday feast, you’ll often have plenty of meat left over. Instead of letting it go to waste, repurpose it into a delicious soup. Leftover turkey has a mild, tender flavor that works wonderfully in soups and stews, where it can absorb the flavors of the broth and other ingredients. It’s a smart and efficient way to make the most of your leftovers while creating a meal that feels fresh and satisfying.

Ingredients for Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

Here’s what you’ll need to make a delicious batch of turkey and wild rice soup. This recipe serves approximately 6-8 people, depending on serving sizes.

For the Soup Base:

  • 2 tablespoons butter – for sautéing the vegetables and creating a rich base.
  • 1 medium onion – finely chopped. The onion adds depth to the soup’s flavor.
  • 2 carrots – peeled and diced. Carrots add natural sweetness and color to the soup.
  • 2 celery stalks – diced. Celery adds a subtle crunch and earthiness.
  • 3 cloves garlic – minced. Garlic enhances the soup’s savory flavor profile.
  • 6 cups turkey broth – or chicken broth, if turkey broth is unavailable. This will form the base of your soup.
  • 1 1/2 cups cooked wild rice – Wild rice brings a chewy texture and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with turkey.
  • 3 cups cooked turkey – shredded or chopped. Leftover turkey works best here, but you can also use rotisserie chicken.
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme – for a fragrant, herbaceous note.
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage – sage is a classic pairing with turkey and adds a warm, earthy flavor.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt – adjust to taste.
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper – freshly ground for the best flavor.
  • 1 bay leaf – adds a subtle, aromatic flavor to the broth.
  • 1 cup half-and-half or heavy cream – to add richness and creaminess to the soup.

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1 cup frozen peas – for a pop of color and added texture.
  • 1 cup corn kernels – frozen or fresh, adding sweetness and crunch.
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika – for a mild smoky flavor.
  • Fresh parsley – chopped, for garnish and a fresh burst of flavor.

For Garnish:

  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese – adds a salty, umami flavor when sprinkled on top of the soup.
  • Crusty bread or crackers – for serving alongside the soup to make the meal more filling.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making turkey and wild rice soup is simple and requires just a few steps. Here’s how to make this comforting dish from start to finish:

Step 1: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large pot or Dutch oven, melt the butter over medium heat. Once the butter is melted and bubbly, add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, or until they begin to soften. Stir occasionally to ensure they cook evenly.

Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Garlic can burn quickly, so keep an eye on it and stir frequently.

Step 2: Add the Broth and Seasonings

Once the vegetables are softened and aromatic, pour in the turkey broth (or chicken broth if you’re using that), and stir to combine. Add the dried thyme, dried sage, salt, black pepper, and bay leaf to the broth. Stir well to distribute the seasonings throughout the soup.

Bring the broth to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and infuse the broth with savory goodness.

Step 3: Add the Turkey and Wild Rice

After the broth has simmered and developed flavor, add the cooked turkey and wild rice to the pot. Stir the ingredients together and allow the soup to continue simmering for an additional 10-15 minutes. This gives the turkey and wild rice time to absorb the broth’s flavors.

Step 4: Stir in the Cream

Once the soup has simmered and the flavors are well blended, stir in the half-and-half or heavy cream. This adds a creamy texture and richness to the soup, making it feel indulgent and comforting. Allow the soup to heat through for another 2-3 minutes, ensuring that the cream is fully incorporated.

Step 5: Optional Add-ins

If you’re adding frozen peas, corn, or other vegetables to your soup, now is the time to stir them in. Add the frozen peas and corn, and continue cooking for 5-7 minutes, until they are heated through and tender.

If you’d like to add a touch of smokiness, sprinkle in the paprika and stir to combine. This will give the soup a deeper flavor with a slight hint of smokiness that complements the turkey beautifully.

Step 6: Taste and Adjust Seasonings

Before serving, taste the soup and adjust the seasonings if needed. Add more salt or pepper if desired, or a dash of extra thyme or sage for additional flavor. The soup should be well-seasoned but not overpowering, allowing the delicate flavor of the turkey and the earthiness of the wild rice to shine.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the hot turkey and wild rice soup into bowls. Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped parsley. Serve the soup with crusty bread or crackers for dipping, and enjoy the comforting, hearty flavors.

Tips for Customizing Your Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

One of the great things about turkey and wild rice soup is how adaptable it is. You can easily customize the ingredients to suit your preferences or what you have available in your kitchen. Here are a few ideas to make this soup your own:

Make it Vegetarian

If you want to make this soup vegetarian, you can easily swap out the turkey for mushrooms or tofu. Use vegetable broth instead of turkey or chicken broth, and add extra vegetables like zucchini, potatoes, or sweet potatoes for a filling, plant-based version.

Add More Veggies

If you’re a fan of vegetables, consider adding more to the soup. You can throw in extra vegetables like bell peppers, green beans, or spinach. Just be sure to chop them into bite-sized pieces so they cook evenly and blend well with the other ingredients.

Spice it Up

If you like a bit of heat, add some red pepper flakes or a chopped jalapeño pepper to the soup. The heat will balance out the richness of the cream and add another layer of flavor to the soup.

Storing Leftovers

This turkey and wild rice soup stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. Make sure to let the soup cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. Reheat the soup on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally until it’s heated through.

Freezing Leftovers

If you want to store the soup for a longer period, you can freeze it. Allow the soup to cool completely, then transfer it to freezer-safe containers or bags. It will keep in the freezer for up to 3 months. When you’re ready to enjoy it, thaw the soup in the refrigerator overnight and reheat on the stovetop.

Conclusion

Turkey and Wild Rice Soup is a satisfying, nutritious, and flavorful way to use up your leftover turkey. This hearty soup is easy to make, customizable, and packed with ingredients that bring comfort to every spoonful. Whether you’re enjoying it right after the holidays or making it on a chilly evening, this soup is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

The next time you find yourself with leftover turkey, turn it into a delicious and filling meal with this turkey and wild rice soup. It’s a wonderful way to reduce food waste while enjoying a nourishing and hearty dish that’s perfect for any time of the year. Enjoy!
