Authentic Italian Easter Pie Recipe - Traditional Holiday Treat


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to embark on a culinary journey to Italy! In this blog post, we’ll be sharing with you an authentic Italian Easter Pie recipe, a traditional holiday treat that will surely delight your taste buds. So, let’s dive right in and discover the flavors of this mouthwatering dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1 pound ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage, cooked and crumbled
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup diced ham
  • 1/2 cup diced salami
  • 1/2 cup diced provolone cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use your fingers to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and ice water. Gradually pour the egg mixture into the flour mixture, stirring until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9-inch pie dish and set it aside.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, cooked Italian sausage, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, beaten eggs, salt, black pepper, and nutmeg. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly incorporated.

  5. Take the chilled dough out of the refrigerator and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out one portion to fit the bottom of the greased pie dish. Press the dough into the dish, making sure it covers the bottom and sides completely.

  6. Spread the diced ham, salami, and provolone cheese evenly over the dough in the pie dish. Pour the ricotta mixture over the top, smoothing it out with a spatula.

  7. Roll out the remaining portion of dough and place it on top of the ricotta mixture. Pinch the edges of the dough together to seal the pie.

  8. Use a sharp knife to cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  9. Bake the Easter Pie in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set.

  10. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

  • This Authentic Italian Easter Pie is a perfect addition to your Easter brunch or as a delightful centerpiece for your holiday table.
  • Serve it warm or at room temperature, accompanied by a fresh green salad dressed with lemon vinaigrette for a refreshing contrast.
  • Pair it with a glass of red wine, such as Chianti or Sangiovese, to complement the rich flavors of the pie.

Conclusion

There you have it, a tantalizing recipe for Authentic Italian Easter Pie. This traditional holiday treat is sure to impress your family and friends with its heavenly combination of flavors. So, why not bring a taste of Italy to your Easter celebration this year? Give this recipe a try and let the aromas of the Italian countryside fill your kitchen. Buon appetito!
