How to Make Authentic Spanish Beef Empanadas


Empanadas are a beloved staple in Spanish cuisine, known for their flaky crust and savory filling. These handheld pastries are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a party, enjoying a family meal, or simply looking for a delicious snack. In this guide, we’ll explore how to make authentic Spanish beef empanadas from scratch, taking you step by step through the process. Let’s dive into the flavorful world of empanadas!

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (cold, cut into small cubes)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup cold water (more as needed)

For the Beef Filling

  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably chuck)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup green olives (chopped, optional)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (chopped, optional)
  • Olive oil for cooking

For Assembly

  • 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)

Making the Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder. Whisk together until well combined.

  2. Incorporate the Butter
    Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add the Egg and Water
    Beat the egg and add it to the flour mixture. Gradually add cold water, mixing until a dough forms. You may need to adjust the amount of water depending on the humidity and the flour used.

  4. Knead and Chill
    Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow it to rest and firm up.

Preparing the Beef Filling

  1. Sauté the Aromatics
    In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, sautéing until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  2. Cook the Ground Beef
    Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until browned and fully cooked, about 7-10 minutes. Drain any excess fat if necessary.

  3. Season the Filling
    Stir in the smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. If using, add the chopped green olives and hard-boiled eggs. Mix well and remove from heat. Allow the filling to cool for a few minutes.

Assembling the Empanadas

  1. Roll Out the Dough
    On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to about 1/8-inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 4-5 inches in diameter) to cut out circles from the dough.

  2. Fill the Empanadas
    Place a tablespoon of the beef filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this may cause the empanadas to burst during baking.

  3. Seal the Empanadas
    Fold the dough over to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together to seal. For a decorative touch, crimp the edges with a fork or twist them with your fingers.

  4. Prepare for Baking
    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the assembled empanadas on the sheet, leaving space between each one.

  5. Apply Egg Wash
    Brush the tops of the empanadas with the beaten egg for a beautiful golden finish when baked.

Baking the Empanadas

  1. Bake
    Bake the empanadas in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crisp.

  2. Cool and Serve
    Once baked, remove the empanadas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes on a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature, accompanied by your favorite dipping sauce or salsa.

Tips for Perfect Empanadas

  • Make Ahead: Empanada dough can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. You can also freeze the assembled empanadas before baking. Just make sure to separate them on a baking sheet and freeze individually before transferring to a freezer bag.

  • Experiment with Fillings: While beef is traditional, don’t hesitate to get creative! Try chicken, pork, vegetables, or even sweet fillings like fruit or chocolate for dessert empanadas.

  • Serve with Dipping Sauces: Pair your empanadas with chimichurri, salsa, or a simple yogurt dip for an extra layer of flavor.

Conclusion

Making authentic Spanish beef empanadas is a delightful culinary experience that combines rich flavors and comforting textures. With a little patience and practice, you can master this classic dish that is sure to impress family and friends alike. Whether enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or main course, these empanadas bring a taste of Spain to your kitchen. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on this delicious journey of making empanadas at home! Enjoy every bite!
