10 Paleo Chicken Breast Recipes for Quick and Easy Meals


Chicken breast serves as the foundation for many versatile, delicious, and health-conscious meals. For those following a Paleo lifestyle, it’s an ideal protein choice—lean, adaptable, and packed with nutrients. Whether you’re meal prepping for a busy week or crafting a dinner for the family, these quick and easy Paleo chicken breast recipes are bound to inspire you. Each recipe is designed to complement the principles of Paleo eating while keeping meal prep simple and flavorful. Let’s dive into these wholesome creations.

1. Lemon Garlic Chicken Breasts

Bring brightness and zest to your table with lemon garlic chicken breasts. This recipe is perfect for a refreshing yet hearty dish that pairs wonderfully with roasted vegetables or a fresh salad.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large chicken breasts
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon oregano

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the lemon juice, zest, garlic, olive oil, salt, pepper, and oregano.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over them. Let them sit for 30 minutes (or longer if you have time).
  3. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the chicken breasts for 5-6 minutes per side or until fully cooked.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley or additional lemon zest before serving.

2. Creamy Coconut Chicken

This dish transforms plain chicken breasts into something luxurious yet wholesome. The rich coconut flavor pairs beautifully with Paleo-friendly sides like cauliflower rice or sautéed greens.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Slice chicken breasts into strips or cubes for easier cooking.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat and add the chicken pieces, cooking until golden on each side.
  3. In a bowl, mix the coconut milk, curry powder, turmeric, garlic powder, salt, and paprika.
  4. Pour the mixture over the chicken and simmer for 15-20 minutes until thickened.
  5. Serve hot and garnish with cilantro if desired.

3. Balsamic Glazed Chicken

For a recipe that balances tangy and sweet flavors, balsamic glazed chicken is a crowd pleaser. The glaze caramelizes beautifully as it cooks, infusing the chicken with rich flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey (or Paleo-approved sweetener)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix balsamic vinegar, honey, Dijon mustard, olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place chicken breasts in a baking dish and pour the glaze evenly over them.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes until cooked through, basting occasionally with the sauce.
  5. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving.

4. Spicy Grilled Chicken

This recipe brings a fiery twist and is perfect for your next Paleo-friendly barbecue. Adjust the spice level to your liking by modifying the chili powder or cayenne.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 tablespoons avocado oil
  • Juice of ½ a lime
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix chili powder, paprika, cumin, cayenne, oil, lime juice, and salt.
  2. Rub the mixture generously over the chicken breasts.
  3. Grill the chicken on medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes per side, or until cooked through.
  4. Serve with grilled vegetables or a side of guacamole.

5. Spinach-Stuffed Chicken Breast

This stuffed chicken recipe is as satisfying as it is nutritious. Packed with spinach and herbs, it brings layers of flavor to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Butterfly the chicken breasts by slicing them horizontally, being careful not to cut all the way through.
  2. In a skillet, sauté spinach and garlic in olive oil until wilted.
  3. Stuff the chicken breasts with the sautéed spinach mixture and secure with toothpicks.
  4. Sprinkle almond flour, salt, and pepper over the chicken.
  5. Bake for 25 minutes until fully cooked.

6. Herbed Chicken Tenders

For a quick snack or appetizer, these herbed chicken tenders are a hit. They’re easy to make and perfect for dipping in Paleo-friendly sauces.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced into strips
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Rub the chicken strips with thyme, rosemary, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook the tenders for 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown.
  3. Serve with a side of Paleo-friendly ranch or a tomato-based sauce.

7. Honey Mustard Chicken

Sweet and tangy honey mustard chicken is an all-time favorite. Pair this dish with roasted sweet potatoes or steamed broccoli for a satisfying Paleo dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk honey, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Coat the chicken breasts with the mixture.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet and cook the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side until caramelized and cooked through.
  4. Serve hot alongside your favorite Paleo sides.

8. Tomato Basil Chicken

This tomato basil chicken recipe brings comfort and classic Italian flavors to your plate. With minimal ingredients, it’s perfect for a quick and nourishing meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 handful fresh basil leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken breasts for 5 minutes per side.
  2. Add cherry tomatoes and garlic to the pan and cook for an additional 5 minutes until tomatoes soften.
  3. Add fresh basil leaves, letting them wilt slightly before serving.

9. Avocado Chicken Salad

This light and creamy salad uses avocado as a substitute for traditional mayonnaise, making it fully Paleo-approved.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cooked chicken breasts, shredded
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 teaspoon mustard (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mash the avocado with lemon juice, mustard, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  2. Fold the shredded chicken into the avocado mixture.
  3. Serve atop Paleo-friendly crackers, leafy greens, or enjoy it on its own.

10. Buffalo Chicken Bake

For a bold and spicy flavor, this buffalo chicken bake is perfect for lunch or dinner. Pair it with fresh celery sticks or Paleo-friendly dressings for an extra bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon melted ghee
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Mix hot sauce, ghee, garlic powder, and salt in a small bowl.
  2. Coat the chicken breasts with the mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  3. Bake for 20-25 minutes until cooked through.
  4. Serve hot and drizzle with extra sauce if desired.

These Paleo chicken breast recipes are crafted to cater to a variety of tastes and occasions. Whether you want something spicy, comforting, or refreshing, there’s an option here for everyone. You can mix and match side dishes like roasted vegetables, zoodles, or cauliflower rice to complement the flavors while sticking to your Paleo guidelines. Cooking wholesome meals doesn’t have to be complicated—with recipes like these, you can nourish your body and enjoy every bite.
