Greek Pastitsio Recipe: A Comfort Food Classic


Greek Pastitsio is a comforting and hearty dish that brings together rich, flavorful layers of pasta, meat sauce, and béchamel into one incredible, oven-baked masterpiece. A staple of Greek cuisine, this dish is widely loved for its creamy texture, bold flavors, and ability to feed a crowd with ease. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, serving Pastitsio is sure to impress your guests.

In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to create the perfect pan of Pastitsio from scratch. From selecting the right ingredients to mastering its multiple layers, this traditional recipe offers deep satisfaction both in the preparation and final result. Put on your apron, and let’s get started with this culinary classic!

Understanding Pastitsio

Pastitsio, often nicknamed the “Greek lasagna,” is a layered casserole-style dish that combines three distinct components: a tender pasta base, a savory meat sauce laced with Mediterranean spices, and a velvety béchamel sauce topping. While the preparation involves some effort, the result is well worth it—a golden, bubbling bake that tastes even better than it looks.

This dish traces its history back to Greece, though its roots have influences from other Mediterranean and even Italian cuisines. Traditionally served during holidays, special occasions, and family gatherings, Pastitsio remains one of the most beloved comfort foods in Greek households.

Ingredients for Greek Pastitsio

You’ll need a variety of ingredients to assemble this layered dish. Below, we’ve separated them into groups for better clarity.

For the Pasta Layer:

  • 1 pound (500g) long tubular pasta (ideally pastitsio pasta or bucatini)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup grated kefalotyri cheese (or Parmesan as a substitute)
  • Salt, for cooking pasta water

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pound (500g) ground beef or lamb (or a mix of both)
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes (or tomato puree)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional, but highly recommended)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and white pepper, to taste
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup grated kefalotyri cheese (or Parmesan, as above)

How to Make Greek Pastitsio

While Pastitsio may seem intimidating due to its multi-step nature, breaking the recipe down into manageable layers simplifies the process considerably. Follow these steps to master this iconic dish.

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

  1. Begin by cooking the tubular pasta in a large pot of salted boiling water according to the package instructions. Cook it to al dente, as it will continue to bake in the oven later.
  2. Drain the cooked pasta, return it to the pot, and toss with the butter, beaten eggs, and grated cheese. This step helps the pasta hold together as a base for the casserole.
  3. Spread the prepared pasta evenly into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Set it aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Meat Sauce

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Then add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute.
  2. Add the ground beef (and/or lamb) to the skillet, breaking it up with your spoon or spatula, and cook until browned all over.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for an additional minute to deepen the flavor before adding the crushed tomatoes and red wine (if using).
  4. Season the meat mixture with cinnamon, nutmeg, oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss in the bay leaf, reduce the heat to low, and let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Remove the bay leaf before assembling the dish.

Step 3: Make the Béchamel Sauce

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, to create a roux.
  2. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly, to ensure a smooth consistency. Let the mixture cook until it thickens into a creamy sauce.
  3. Add the nutmeg, along with salt and white pepper to taste.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat, and allow it to cool slightly before whisking in the beaten eggs. The eggs add richness and help the béchamel set during baking. Stir in the grated cheese for an extra layer of flavor.

Step 4: Assemble the Pastitsio

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Over the prepared pasta layer in the baking dish, spoon the meat sauce evenly, spreading it out to cover the entire surface.
  3. Pour the béchamel sauce over the meat layer, using a spatula to spread it smoothly and evenly to the edges.

Step 5: Bake

  1. Place the assembled dish into the preheated oven and bake for about 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling.
  2. Remove from the oven and allow the Pastitsio to rest for at least 20 minutes before slicing. This resting time ensures the layers set properly and are easier to cut.

Tips for the Perfect Pastitsio

  • Pasta Choice: While traditional Greek Pastitsio uses specially-produced Greek pastitsio pasta, bucatini or thick macaroni are great substitutes. Their hollow centers help hold the creamy sauce and meat mixture.
  • Cinnamon and Nutmeg: These warming spices are key to the dish’s signature flavor, so don’t skip them! However, use them sparingly for a balanced, subtle taste.
  • Make Ahead: Pastitsio can be prepared up to a day in advance. Assemble the dish, cover it tightly, and refrigerate. Add 10-15 minutes to the baking time when cooking directly from the fridge.
  • Storing Leftovers: Pastitsio stores beautifully! Keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to four days. Individual portions can also be frozen for up to three months.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Pastitsio is a complete dish on its own, thanks to its layers of pasta, meat, and creamy béchamel. However, consider serving it alongside a light, refreshing Greek salad with crisp cucumbers, ripe tomatoes, red onions, and a sprinkle of feta cheese, dressed with a bright lemon and olive oil vinaigrette. A side of crusty bread and a glass of dry red wine will also perfectly complement the meal.

Why You’ll Love Making Pastitsio

One of the most satisfying aspects of Pastitsio is its ability to bring people together. The fragrant spices, silky sauce, and comforting pasta make it a perfect dish for creating memories around the dinner table. Whether you’re introducing this dish to friends unfamiliar with Greek cuisine or passing down a family tradition, Pastitsio has a way of making everyone feel at home.

Roll up your sleeves, take your time, and enjoy both the cooking process and the warmth this hearty dish brings to your kitchen. Happy cooking!
