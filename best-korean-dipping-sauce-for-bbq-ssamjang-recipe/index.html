Best Korean Dipping Sauce for BBQ (Ssamjang) Recipe


Korean barbecue, or gogi-gui, is a beloved culinary tradition that has gained international popularity for its unique flavors and social, interactive dining style. Whether you’re grilling meat at home or enjoying a meal at a Korean BBQ restaurant, no dish is complete without the perfect dipping sauce. Among the many sauces that can accompany grilled meat, Ssamjang stands out as the quintessential choice for enhancing the flavors of your barbecue experience.

Ssamjang, which translates to “wrapped sauce” in Korean, is a thick, savory, and slightly spicy dipping sauce that pairs perfectly with a variety of grilled meats, especially when served with lettuce wraps. This rich sauce is a blend of miso, gochujang (Korean chili paste), garlic, sesame oil, and other flavorful ingredients, making it an indispensable condiment in Korean barbecue cuisine. If you’re looking to recreate this delicious sauce at home, you’ve come to the right place.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the simple steps to make your own Ssamjang, so you can bring the flavors of Korean BBQ right into your kitchen.

Ingredients Needed for Ssamjang

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the key ingredients that make Ssamjang so rich and flavorful:

  • Doenjang (Fermented Soybean Paste): This is the base of the sauce. Doenjang provides a deep umami flavor that forms the foundation of Ssamjang. It is a fermented paste made from soybeans and grains, and it has a slightly salty and earthy taste.

  • Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste): Gochujang is another essential ingredient. It adds spice, a touch of sweetness, and a rich depth of flavor to the sauce. It’s made from fermented soybeans, chili peppers, rice, and salt.

  • Sesame Oil: A few tablespoons of sesame oil provide a nutty, aromatic flavor to the sauce. It enhances the richness and depth of the Ssamjang.

  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a pungent and savory note that balances the other ingredients.

  • Sugar or Honey: A small amount of sugar or honey brings a subtle sweetness to the sauce, balancing out the heat from the gochujang.

  • Rice Vinegar: A splash of rice vinegar adds acidity and brightness to the sauce, making it more balanced.

  • Sesame Seeds: Toasted sesame seeds add a lovely crunch and nuttiness that complements the other ingredients.

  • Green Onion (Optional): Fresh green onions can be added for a mild, oniony flavor and a bit of color.

Now that you have all the ingredients ready, let’s get started on making this incredible dipping sauce.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Ssamjang

Step 1: Prepare the Base Sauces

The first step in making Ssamjang is to mix the two key bases: doenjang (fermented soybean paste) and gochujang (Korean chili paste). These pastes provide the deep umami flavor and the heat in the sauce.

  • In a bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of doenjang and 2 tablespoons of gochujang. Stir them together until you achieve a smooth and uniform paste.

Step 2: Add Aromatics and Seasonings

Next, it’s time to build upon the base by adding the additional flavors.

  • Grate or finely chop 2 cloves of garlic and add them to the bowl with the pastes. The garlic will infuse the sauce with a strong, savory taste.
  • Pour in 1 tablespoon of sesame oil to introduce the nutty aroma. The sesame oil is essential to give Ssamjang its signature richness.
  • Add 1 tablespoon of sugar (or honey, if you prefer) to balance out the spicy and salty flavors with a hint of sweetness.
  • Mix in 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar for acidity, which will help cut through the richness of the sauce and bring balance.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

Once all the ingredients are added, stir everything together until the mixture is smooth and well-combined. The consistency of Ssamjang should be thick, but spreadable. If the sauce seems too thick, you can add a few drops of water or additional sesame oil to thin it out to your desired consistency.

Step 4: Add Toasted Sesame Seeds and Green Onions

For extra texture and flavor, stir in 1 tablespoon of toasted sesame seeds. These seeds add a lovely crunch and extra nuttiness to the sauce. If you want a touch of freshness, finely chop 1-2 green onions and mix them into the sauce as well. Green onions provide a mild, oniony bite and color to the sauce.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Before serving, taste the Ssamjang and adjust the flavors if needed. You may want to add a pinch of salt if it needs more seasoning or an extra spoonful of sugar for sweetness. Keep in mind that Ssamjang is meant to be bold and rich, so don’t be afraid to make adjustments until it suits your personal taste.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once your Ssamjang is perfectly balanced, it’s ready to serve! Traditionally, Ssamjang is served as a dipping sauce for grilled meats like beef, pork, and chicken at Korean BBQ. To enjoy it in the traditional style, simply dip your grilled meat into the sauce, then wrap it in a leaf of lettuce with some rice and other accompaniments like garlic and chili peppers.

If you’re hosting a Korean BBQ at home, you can prepare a variety of side dishes, such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, and rice, to complement the dipping sauce and create a complete Korean meal experience.

Tips for Making the Best Ssamjang

While this recipe is straightforward, here are a few tips to help you perfect your Ssamjang:

1. Use High-Quality Doenjang and Gochujang

The flavor of your Ssamjang will heavily depend on the quality of the doenjang and gochujang you use. Look for authentic, high-quality brands of these ingredients, preferably from Korean or Asian grocery stores. Homemade doenjang and gochujang can also elevate the flavor of your sauce, but store-bought versions work just as well.

2. Adjust the Spice Level

If you prefer a milder sauce, you can reduce the amount of gochujang or add a bit more sugar to tone down the heat. Conversely, if you like it spicier, increase the gochujang or add chopped fresh chili peppers.

3. Customize with Additional Ingredients

Feel free to experiment by adding other ingredients to personalize your Ssamjang. Some variations include adding fermented shrimp paste for a deeper umami flavor or roasted garlic for an extra savory punch. Just keep in mind that the balance of flavors is key.

4. Make It Ahead of Time

Ssamjang can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator. In fact, the flavors will deepen and improve after a day or two. Just make sure to stir it well before serving to ensure that it’s smooth and creamy.

Health Benefits of Ssamjang

Not only is Ssamjang delicious, but it also has several health benefits. The main ingredients—doenjang and gochujang—are packed with probiotics and antioxidants that promote digestive health. The sesame oil provides healthy fats, while garlic is known for its immune-boosting properties. If you’re looking to enjoy a healthier dipping sauce with your barbecue, Ssamjang is a great option that offers both taste and nutrition.

Conclusion

Whether you’re hosting a Korean BBQ at home or just looking for a flavorful dip to accompany grilled meats, Ssamjang is the perfect sauce to elevate your meal. With its rich, savory, and slightly spicy flavor, it complements the smoky taste of grilled meats and enhances the overall dining experience. This recipe for Ssamjang is easy to make and can be customized to suit your tastes.

So the next time you fire up the grill, don’t forget to prepare this iconic Korean dipping sauce. Your taste buds will thank you for it!
