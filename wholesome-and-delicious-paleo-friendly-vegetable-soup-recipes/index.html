Wholesome and Delicious Paleo-Friendly Vegetable Soup Recipes


As the temperatures drop and the days grow shorter, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of vegetable soup to lift your spirits and nourish your body. When it comes to paleo-friendly cooking, incorporating a wide variety of vegetables into your meals is essential for ensuring you’re getting the nutrients you need to thrive. In this post, we’ll explore five wholesome and delicious paleo-friendly vegetable soup recipes that are perfect for a chilly winter’s day.

Recipe 1: Creamy Butternut Squash and Apple Soup

This creamy and comforting soup is a staple of paleo cooking, and for good reason. The sweetness of the butternut squash pairs perfectly with the tartness of the apple, creating a flavor profile that’s both familiar and exciting. To make this soup, simply sauté 1 large onion, 3 cloves of garlic, and 1 teaspoon of ground cumin in a large pot over medium heat. Add in 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed, 2 apples, peeled and cubed, and 4 cups of chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the squash is tender. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 2: Spicy Sweet Potato and Black Bean Soup

For a soup that’s a little more adventurous, look no further than this spicy sweet potato and black bean soup. The sweetness of the sweet potatoes pairs perfectly with the earthiness of the black beans, while the jalapeño adds a welcome kick. To make this soup, simply sauté 1 large onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 teaspoon of ground cumin in a large pot over medium heat. Add in 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed, 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed, and 4 cups of chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the sweet potatoes are tender. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of cayenne pepper to taste.

Recipe 3: Roasted Carrot and Ginger Soup

For a soup that’s a little more exotic, try this roasted carrot and ginger soup. The sweetness of the carrots pairs perfectly with the spiciness of the ginger, creating a flavor profile that’s both familiar and exciting. To make this soup, simply preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped, with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1 tablespoon of honey, and 1 teaspoon of ground ginger. Spread the carrots out in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender. Then, sauté 1 large onion and 2 cloves of garlic in a large pot over medium heat. Add in the roasted carrots, 4 cups of chicken broth, and 1/2 cup of coconut milk. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the soup is heated through. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 4: Creamy Kale and Potato Soup

For a soup that’s a little more comforting, try this creamy kale and potato soup. The earthiness of the kale pairs perfectly with the heartiness of the potatoes, creating a flavor profile that’s both familiar and exciting. To make this soup, simply sauté 1 large onion and 2 cloves of garlic in a large pot over medium heat. Add in 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed, and 4 cups of chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then stir in 1 cup of chopped kale and 1/2 cup of coconut cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 5: Butternut Squash and Lentil Soup

For a soup that’s a little more hearty, try this butternut squash and lentil soup. The earthiness of the lentils pairs perfectly with the sweetness of the butternut squash, creating a flavor profile that’s both familiar and exciting. To make this soup, simply sauté 1 large onion and 2 cloves of garlic in a large pot over medium heat. Add in 1 cup of red or green lentils, rinsed and drained, 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed, and 4 cups of chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the lentils are tender. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Conclusion

There you have it - five wholesome and delicious paleo-friendly vegetable soup recipes that are perfect for a chilly winter’s day. Whether you’re in the mood for something creamy and comforting, spicy and adventurous, or hearty and earthy, there’s something on this list for everyone. So go ahead, get cooking, and enjoy the warm, comforting goodness of these paleo-friendly vegetable soups.
