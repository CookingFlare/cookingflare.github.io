Creative Thanksgiving Vegetarian Main Courses: Delicious Plant-Based Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time of gratitude, family, and, of course, a delicious feast. For those who follow a vegetarian or plant-based diet, Thanksgiving can be a wonderful opportunity to showcase the versatility and creativity of plant-based cooking. Whether you are a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based options into your Thanksgiving menu, these recipes will leave your taste buds satisfied and your guests impressed.

1. Stuffed Acorn Squash with Quinoa and Cranberries

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the acorn squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash halves on a baking sheet, cut-side up, and brush with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast the squash in the oven for about 30-35 minutes, or until tender.
  5. While the squash is roasting, cook the quinoa. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, chopped parsley, olive oil, and maple syrup. Mix well and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Once the squash is cooked, remove it from the oven and fill each half with the quinoa mixture.
  8. Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the filling is heated through.
  9. Serve the stuffed acorn squash as a main course or a side dish. Enjoy!

2. Lentil and Mushroom Wellington

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked lentils
  • 2 cups finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup bread crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the chopped mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and become tender.
  4. Stir in the cooked lentils, chopped spinach, chopped parsley, bread crumbs, soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Mix well and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface.
  6. Spoon the lentil and mushroom mixture onto the center of the puff pastry sheet, leaving a border around the edges.
  7. Carefully fold the pastry over the filling, sealing the edges by pressing them together.
  8. Place the Wellington on a baking sheet and brush the top with a little bit of olive oil.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for approximately 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.
  10. Allow the Wellington to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

3. Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash to the pot and cook for about 5 minutes, until it starts to soften.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Pour in the white wine and cook until it has been absorbed by the rice.
  5. Begin adding the vegetable broth to the pot, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each ladleful to be absorbed before adding the next. Continue this process until the rice is cooked al dente and creamy.
  6. Stir in the nutritional yeast (if using) and chopped sage. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Remove the pot from the heat and let the risotto sit for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together.
  8. Serve the butternut squash and sage risotto as a hearty and satisfying Thanksgiving main course.

These creative Thanksgiving vegetarian main courses are sure to impress even the most devout meat-eaters at your holiday table. With their delicious flavors and plant-based ingredients, they showcase the beauty and versatility of vegetarian cooking. So, gather your loved ones, give thanks, and enjoy a Thanksgiving feast that celebrates the abundance of nature and the joy of good food. Happy cooking!
