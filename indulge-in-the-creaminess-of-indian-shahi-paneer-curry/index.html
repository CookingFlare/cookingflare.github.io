Indulge in the Creaminess of Indian Shahi Paneer Curry


If you’re a fan of rich, creamy curries with a touch of royal indulgence, then Shahi Paneer is a dish that deserves a special place in your recipe collection. This exquisite Indian dish boasts a luxurious blend of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) simmered in a velvety smooth gravy, enriched with aromatic spices and a hint of sweetness. Let’s delve into the world of Shahi Paneer and learn how to recreate this regal delicacy in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Paneer:

  • 500g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water for 20 minutes
  • 1 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 tablespoon melon seeds
  • 1 tablespoon green cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • Slivered almonds and pistachios

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Paneer

  1. In a bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them evenly.
  3. Let the paneer marinate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Gravy

  1. In a blender, grind the soaked cashew nuts, poppy seeds, and melon seeds into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and add the finely chopped onions. Sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil begins to separate.
  4. Pour in the cashew-nut paste and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Add the green cardamom powder, kasuri methi, and salt. Stir well.
  6. Pour in the heavy cream and sugar. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the gravy thickens.

Step 3: Fry the Marinated Paneer

  1. Heat oil in a separate pan and fry the marinated paneer cubes until they turn golden brown.
  2. Remove the fried paneer from the pan and set aside.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

  1. Gently add the fried paneer cubes to the prepared gravy and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, slivered almonds, and pistachios.

Serving Suggestions

Shahi Paneer pairs wonderfully with steamed basmati rice, naan bread, or even simple rotis. For a complete royal dining experience, serve it alongside fragrant saffron rice and a cooling cucumber raita.

Conclusion

The allure of Shahi Paneer lies in its luscious texture, harmonious blend of flavors, and the sheer indulgence it offers. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or simply craving a taste of royalty, this creamy Indian delicacy is sure to captivate your palate and leave a lasting impression on your guests. Embrace the art of Indian cooking, and let the velvety richness of Shahi Paneer elevate your culinary repertoire to a regal realm of gastronomic delight.
