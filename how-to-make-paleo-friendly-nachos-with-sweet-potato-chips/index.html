How to Make Paleo-Friendly Nachos with Sweet Potato Chips


If you’re craving a crunchy, savory snack but want to stick to a paleo lifestyle, you’re in the right place! Traditional nachos are loaded with cheese, corn chips, and processed toppings that don’t align with a paleo diet. But don’t worry – we’ve got a tasty, healthy alternative that uses sweet potato chips as the base, offering a naturally gluten-free, nutrient-dense option. This paleo-friendly nachos recipe is not only delicious but also simple to make. It’s perfect for game nights, casual get-togethers, or even as a satisfying snack when you’re in the mood for something indulgent yet wholesome.

In this guide, we’ll take you step-by-step through how to prepare sweet potato nachos that taste just as good, if not better, than the traditional version. The key to making these nachos paleo-approved lies in swapping out common non-paleo ingredients with whole, nutrient-rich foods.

Why Choose Paleo-Friendly Nachos?

The paleo diet emphasizes whole, unprocessed foods and eliminates grains, dairy, refined sugars, and legumes. By making paleo nachos with sweet potato chips, you’re choosing ingredients that are nutrient-packed and in line with the principles of the paleo diet. Sweet potatoes are rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals like vitamin A and potassium, making them a great alternative to traditional tortilla chips. Instead of cheese and sour cream, you’ll use healthier toppings like guacamole, fresh vegetables, and seasoned meat for a delicious and wholesome dish.

What You’ll Need

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need. We’ll break them into categories for easy reference:

Sweet Potato Chips

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (or avocado oil)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)

Toppings

  • 1 pound ground beef (or ground turkey or chicken)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (or olive oil)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1 cup fresh salsa (or homemade salsa)
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Jalapeño slices (optional for extra heat)

Optional Extras

  • Dairy-free cheese (made from nuts or coconut milk)
  • Olive tapenade (for an extra savory layer)

Step 1: Make Your Sweet Potato Chips

First, let’s start with the base – the sweet potato chips. These chips are the perfect crunchy foundation for your nachos.

  1. Preheat your oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C) to ensure the sweet potato chips bake evenly and become perfectly crisp.

  2. Prepare the sweet potatoes: Wash and peel the sweet potatoes. Using a sharp knife or mandolin slicer, cut the sweet potatoes into thin, uniform slices. The thinner the slices, the crisper the chips will be. Aim for around 1/8 inch thick slices.

  3. Toss with oil and seasoning: In a large bowl, toss the sweet potato slices with olive oil, sea salt, and any optional seasonings you’d like to add. A little paprika and garlic powder will give them an extra flavor boost, but these are completely optional.

  4. Arrange on a baking sheet: Lay the sweet potato slices in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Be sure not to overcrowd the slices, as this can prevent them from crisping up properly.

  5. Bake the chips: Bake the sweet potato slices for 20–25 minutes, flipping them halfway through. Keep a close eye on them toward the end to prevent burning. You’ll know they’re done when they’re golden and crispy, but not overly browned. Let them cool slightly before using them for your nachos.

Step 2: Cook the Ground Meat

While the sweet potato chips are baking, it’s time to prepare the savory topping.

  1. Sauté the onions and garlic: In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of coconut oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 2–3 minutes until it softens and becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Cook the ground meat: Add the ground beef (or turkey/chicken) to the skillet and break it up with a spatula. Cook until browned, about 5–7 minutes. Drain any excess fat if necessary.

  3. Season the meat: Once the meat is browned, stir in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), and a pinch of sea salt. Mix everything together until the spices are well incorporated and the meat is fully cooked. Set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Guacamole

A fresh guacamole topping is an essential part of these paleo nachos. It’s creamy, tangy, and provides a delicious contrast to the crunchy sweet potato chips.

  1. Mash the avocado: Cut the ripe avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop out the flesh into a bowl. Use a fork to mash it until smooth but still a little chunky.

  2. Add the lime and seasonings: Stir in the lime juice, a pinch of sea salt, and if desired, a pinch of garlic powder or onion powder. You can also mix in a little chopped cilantro for extra flavor.

  3. Taste and adjust: Taste your guacamole and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add more lime juice for acidity or salt for flavor.

Step 4: Assemble the Paleo Nachos

Now that all your components are ready, it’s time to assemble your paleo nachos.

  1. Layer the chips: Spread your homemade sweet potato chips out on a large serving platter or baking sheet. You want a generous base to hold all your toppings.

  2. Add the seasoned meat: Spoon the cooked ground meat evenly over the sweet potato chips. Spread it out so every chip gets a bit of flavor.

  3. Top with salsa: Spoon a generous amount of fresh salsa over the nachos, making sure to cover all the chips. You can use store-bought salsa or, if you’re feeling adventurous, make your own using fresh tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and lime juice.

  4. Add the guacamole: Top the nachos with your creamy guacamole, spreading it evenly across the plate. You can also serve the guacamole on the side for guests to add themselves.

  5. Garnish: Sprinkle fresh cilantro over the top for a burst of color and flavor. If you like a bit of heat, add some jalapeño slices to give your nachos an extra kick.

  6. Optional extras: For an even more decadent experience, top with dairy-free cheese or olive tapenade. Both are great ways to elevate your nachos while keeping them paleo-approved.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

Once your paleo-friendly nachos are assembled, they’re ready to serve! Enjoy them fresh out of the oven, while the sweet potato chips are still warm and crispy. These nachos are perfect for sharing, but you might just want to keep them all to yourself – they’re that good.

Tips and Variations

  • Spicy Kick: If you like your nachos spicy, add a few dashes of hot sauce to the meat or guacamole. You can also sprinkle some red pepper flakes over the top for an extra layer of heat.
  • Different Proteins: If you’re not a fan of beef, you can easily swap it out for ground turkey, chicken, or even ground lamb. Each option brings its own flavor to the dish, so feel free to get creative.
  • Add More Veggies: To make your nachos even more nutrient-packed, try adding other vegetables like diced bell peppers, zucchini, or olives. They’ll add color, texture, and additional flavor.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the sweet potato chips and meat mixture ahead of time and store them separately in the fridge. When you’re ready to serve, simply reheat and assemble the nachos.

Conclusion

These paleo-friendly nachos with sweet potato chips are a healthier, yet equally satisfying, alternative to traditional nachos. With their crispy base, savory meat, fresh salsa, and creamy guacamole, they’re sure to become your new go-to comfort food. Whether you’re sticking to a paleo diet or just looking for a delicious snack, this recipe proves that healthy and indulgent can go hand in hand.

Give this recipe a try at your next gathering, and get ready to impress your friends and family with a snack that’s both wholesome and delicious. Not only will you be fueling your body with nutritious ingredients, but you’ll also be enjoying a snack that’s full of flavor and texture. So grab your sweet potatoes, gather your toppings, and get ready to dig into the best paleo nachos ever!
