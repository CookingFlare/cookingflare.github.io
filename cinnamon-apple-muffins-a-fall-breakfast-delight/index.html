Cinnamon Apple Muffins: A Fall Breakfast Delight


As the leaves start to change and the air becomes crisp, there’s nothing quite like the comforting aroma of cinnamon and apples wafting through the kitchen. And what better way to embrace the flavors of fall than with a batch of homemade Cinnamon Apple Muffins? These moist, tender treats are the perfect way to start your day, whether enjoyed with a steaming cup of coffee or as a grab-and-go snack on a busy morning.

Ingredients

For the Muffins:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cups finely chopped apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)

For the Cinnamon Sugar Topping:

  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a standard 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or grease the cups with cooking spray.

  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg until well combined.

  3. Cream the Butter and Sugars: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.

  4. Incorporate the Eggs and Vanilla: Beat in the eggs, one at a time, and then add the vanilla extract, mixing until smooth.

  5. Alternate Adding Dry and Wet Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the sour cream and milk. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix.

  6. Fold in the Chopped Apples: Gently fold in the finely chopped apples until evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  7. Fill the Muffin Cups: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about two-thirds full.

  8. Create the Cinnamon Sugar Topping: In a small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar and cinnamon for the topping. In a separate bowl, melt the butter.

  9. Top the Muffins: Brush the tops of the muffins with the melted butter, then sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over each muffin.

  10. Bake and Enjoy: Bake the muffins for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Why Cinnamon Apple Muffins?

Cinnamon and apples are a classic flavor combination that evokes the warmth and comfort of autumn. The natural sweetness of the apples pairs beautifully with the warm spiciness of the cinnamon, creating a flavor profile that is both familiar and irresistible. These muffins are not only delicious but also a convenient and portable breakfast option for those busy fall mornings.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing the Right Apples: Select firm, slightly tart apples such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp for the best texture and flavor in your muffins.
  • Don’t Overmix: When incorporating the dry and wet ingredients, be sure to mix the batter until just combined. Overmixing can result in tough, dense muffins.
  • Testing for Doneness: Use a toothpick to test for doneness. If it comes out with moist crumbs rather than wet batter, the muffins are ready.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re preparing a cozy weekend brunch or looking for a delightful addition to your weekday breakfast routine, these Cinnamon Apple Muffins are sure to become a fall favorite. With their tender crumb, delicate sweetness, and comforting spices, they embody the essence of the season. So, preheat your oven, gather your ingredients, and let the scent of cinnamon and apples fill your home as you bake up a batch of these irresistible muffins. Enjoy the simple pleasures of fall with every delicious bite!
