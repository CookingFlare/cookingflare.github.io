How to Make Authentic Italian Cannoli at Home


As the aroma of freshly fried pastry wafts through the air, the anticipation builds. You’re about to indulge in a classic Italian treat that’s both crispy and creamy, sweet and savory – the beloved cannoli. But have you ever wondered how to make authentic Italian cannoli at home? The answer lies in mastering the art of filling and frying these Sicilian pastries. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process, sharing the secrets to creating the perfect cannoli that will transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed streets of Italy.

Ingredients

For the shell:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons lard or vegetable shortening
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the filling:

  • 1 cup sweetened ricotta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon confectioners’ sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped candied citron
  • 1/2 cup chopped candied orange peel
  • 1/2 cup chopped pistachios

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Shell

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, lard or shortening, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and pliable. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/16 inch (1.5 mm). Cut out circles of dough using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass. You should be able to get about 12-15 circles.

Step 2: Fry the Shell

Heat the vegetable oil in a deep frying pan to a temperature of about 375°F (190°C). Fry the dough circles in batches of 3-4, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the shells are golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the shells from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

In a medium-sized bowl, combine the sweetened ricotta cheese, confectioners’ sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix well until smooth. Stir in the chopped candied citron, orange peel, and pistachios.

Step 4: Assemble the Cannoli

Once the shells are cool enough to handle, use a pastry bag or a plastic bag with a corner cut off to fill the shells with the ricotta mixture. Pipe the filling into the shells, mounding it slightly in the center.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Serve the cannoli immediately, or store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. When ready to serve, allow the cannoli to come to room temperature before serving.

Tips and Variations:

  • To ensure the shells stay crispy, do not overfill them. Leave a small border around the edge of the shell to prevent the filling from spilling out.
  • Experiment with different flavor combinations by adding a pinch of cinnamon, nutmeg, or cardamom to the filling.
  • For a chocolate-dipped cannoli, melt 1 cup of chocolate chips and dip the ends of the filled shells in the chocolate. Let the chocolate set before serving.

With these simple steps and ingredients, you’ll be well on your way to creating authentic Italian cannoli at home. So go ahead, indulge in the sweet and savory goodness, and transport your taste buds to the charming streets of Italy. Buon appetito!
