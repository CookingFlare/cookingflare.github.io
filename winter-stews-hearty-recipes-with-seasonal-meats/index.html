Winter Stews: Hearty Recipes with Seasonal Meats


As the winter chill sets in, there’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of hearty stew to warm you from the inside out. With seasonal meats like beef, lamb, and venison coming into their prime, it’s the perfect time to whip up some comforting and delicious stews. Here are a few recipes that will satisfy your cravings and keep you cozy during the colder months.

Classic Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into chunks
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the beef chunks with flour, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches, then set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  4. Return the beef to the pot and add the beef broth, red wine, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 1/2 hours.
  5. Add the carrots and potatoes, then continue to simmer for another 30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Season with additional salt and pepper if needed, then serve hot.

Rustic Lamb and Root Vegetable Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds lamb shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 4 parsnips, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 turnips, peeled and cut into chunks

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown the lamb cubes in batches, then set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  3. Return the lamb to the pot and add the beef or vegetable broth, red wine, bay leaves, and rosemary. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 2 hours.
  4. Add the parsnips and turnips, then continue to simmer for another 30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, then serve piping hot.

Venison and Mushroom Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds venison stew meat, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 pound mushrooms, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium-high heat. Brown the venison chunks in batches, then set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  3. Return the venison to the pot and add the beef broth, red wine, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 1/2 hours.
  4. Add the mushrooms and continue to simmer for another 30 minutes or until the mushrooms are tender.
  5. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, then serve with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Winter stews are not only delicious and comforting, but they also make use of the best seasonal meats available. Whether you prefer the classic beef, the rustic lamb, or the unique flavor of venison, these recipes are sure to warm you up and satisfy your hunger on those cold winter nights. So, grab your pot, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the delightful aroma and flavors of these hearty winter stews.
