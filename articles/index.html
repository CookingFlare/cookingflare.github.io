Creamy and Dairy-Free Alfredo Sauce Recipe for Pasta Lovers

If you’re a pasta lover but have dietary restrictions that prevent you from enjoying traditional alfredo sauce, fear not! This creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce recipe is here to satisfy your cravings while accommodating your dietary needs. Made with simple ingredients and bursting with flavor, this sauce will take your pasta dishes to a whole new level. Ingredients 1 cup raw cashews, soaked in water for at least 4 hours 2 tablespoons olive oil 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup unsweetened almond milk 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Drain the soaked cashews and rinse them under cold water. In a blender, combine the cashews, olive oil, minced garlic, almond milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard. Blend until smooth and creamy. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and pour in the sauce from the blender. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool for a few minutes before serving. Pour the creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce over your favorite cooked pasta and toss until well coated. Serve hot and garnish with fresh parsley or basil for an extra burst of flavor. Tips and Variations To add some extra richness to the sauce, you can stir in 1/4 cup of coconut cream or a tablespoon of vegan butter during step 3. For a cheesy twist, sprinkle some dairy-free parmesan or grated vegan cheese on top before serving. If you prefer a thinner sauce, you can adjust the consistency by adding a little more almond milk. Feel free to get creative and customize this recipe to your liking. You can add sautéed mushrooms, roasted cherry tomatoes, or steamed broccoli to the sauce for added texture and flavor. The Benefits of Dairy-Free Alfredo Sauce Not only is this creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce delicious, but it also offers a range of health benefits. Homemade Nut-Free Apple Butter for a Sweet and Spiced Spread

Apple butter is a delightful and versatile spread that adds a burst of autumn flavors to your favorite dishes. While traditional recipes often include nuts, this homemade version is nut-free, making it perfect for those with allergies or dietary restrictions. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a delicious, sweet, and spiced apple butter that will elevate your breakfasts, snacks, and desserts. Let’s get started! Ingredients 5 pounds of apples (a mix of sweet and tart varieties) 1 cup of apple cider or apple juice 1 cup of granulated sugar 1 cup of brown sugar 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves 1/4 teaspoon of salt 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Instructions Wash, peel, and core the apples. Cut them into small chunks or slices. Make sure to remove any seeds or tough parts. In a large pot, combine the apples, apple cider, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Stir well to evenly distribute the spices and sugars. Place the pot over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally. The apples should become tender and start to break down. Once the apples are soft, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, carefully puree the apple mixture until smooth. If using a regular blender, blend in batches to avoid overflow. Return the blended mixture to the pot and place it back over low heat. Stir in the vanilla extract and let it simmer for an additional 30 minutes to thicken the apple butter. While the apple butter is simmering, prepare your canning jars by washing them thoroughly and sterilizing them in boiling water for a few minutes. Once the apple butter has thickened to your desired consistency, carefully ladle it into the prepared canning jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip tight. Process the filled jars in a boiling water canner for 10 minutes. Make sure the water covers the jars by at least 1 inch. After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and let them cool on a towel or wire rack. As they cool, you should hear the satisfying “pop” of the lids sealing. Once completely cooled, store the apple butter in a cool, dark place for up to one year. Once opened, refrigerate and use within a few weeks.
