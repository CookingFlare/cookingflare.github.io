Savory and Satisfying: Pulled Pork Sandwich with Coleslaw


Pulled pork sandwiches are a quintessential comfort food that brings together the rich flavors of slow-cooked pork and the crunch of fresh coleslaw. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, feeding a crowd, or simply craving something hearty, this dish is sure to satisfy. In this post, we will delve into the art of crafting the perfect pulled pork sandwich, complete with a homemade coleslaw that adds a refreshing contrast to the savory meat.

The Art of Pulled Pork

Choosing the Right Cut of Meat

To make a mouthwatering pulled pork sandwich, start with the right cut of meat. The most popular choice is the pork shoulder, also known as pork butt. This cut is ideal because it has a good amount of fat and connective tissue that breaks down during the long cooking process, resulting in tender, juicy meat that’s easy to shred. For a more flavorful experience, you can also consider using a combination of cuts, such as pork belly or a whole pork shoulder.

Seasoning: A Flavorful Rub

Creating a flavorful rub is crucial for enhancing the taste of your pulled pork. A well-balanced blend of spices can elevate the dish from ordinary to extraordinary. Here’s a simple recipe for a dry rub that you can easily customize to suit your taste:

Ingredients for the Dry Rub:

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a kick)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin

Preparation: Marinating the Meat

Once you have your rub ready, it’s time to season the pork. Pat the pork shoulder dry with paper towels, and then generously coat it with the dry rub, making sure to cover all sides. For the best flavor, wrap the seasoned meat in plastic wrap and refrigerate it overnight. This allows the spices to penetrate the meat, creating a depth of flavor that will shine through during cooking.

Cooking Methods: Slow and Low

When it comes to cooking pulled pork, the key is to go slow and low. There are several methods to achieve this, including using a slow cooker, an oven, or a smoker. Each method has its merits, so choose one that suits your preferences and equipment.

Slow Cooker Method:

  1. Preparation: Place the seasoned pork shoulder in the slow cooker. Add a splash of apple cider vinegar or chicken broth to keep it moist.
  2. Cooking: Cover and cook on low for 8-10 hours or until the meat is tender and shreds easily with a fork.
  3. Finishing Touches: Once cooked, remove the pork from the cooker and let it rest for a few minutes before shredding it with two forks. Mix in your favorite barbecue sauce to enhance the flavor.

Oven Method:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). Place the pork in a roasting pan and cover it with aluminum foil to retain moisture.
  2. Cooking: Roast for about 6-8 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender. Baste the meat with juices every hour to keep it moist.
  3. Finishing Touches: After cooking, let the meat rest, then shred and mix with barbecue sauce.

Smoker Method:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your smoker to 225°F (107°C). Use wood chips like hickory or applewood for added flavor.
  2. Cooking: Smoke the pork shoulder for about 10-12 hours, maintaining a consistent temperature. Spritz the meat with apple juice every hour to keep it moist.
  3. Finishing Touches: Once the internal temperature reaches 195°F (90°C), remove the pork from the smoker, let it rest, and shred as desired.

The Perfect Coleslaw

No pulled pork sandwich is complete without a crunchy, tangy coleslaw. The contrast between the savory pork and the fresh coleslaw elevates the flavor profile and adds texture to the sandwich.

Ingredients for Coleslaw:

  • 4 cups green cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, grated
  • 1/2 cup red cabbage, shredded (for color)
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Steps:

  1. Mix the Vegetables: In a large mixing bowl, combine the green cabbage, red cabbage, and carrots.
  2. Prepare the Dressing: In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Combine: Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss until evenly coated. For best results, let the coleslaw chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

Assembling the Sandwich

Now that you have your pulled pork and coleslaw ready, it’s time to assemble the sandwich. Choose your favorite buns—soft brioche, classic hamburger buns, or even whole grain for a healthier option. Here’s how to put it all together:

  1. Bottom Bun: Start with a bottom bun and place a generous portion of the pulled pork on top.
  2. Add Coleslaw: Pile on a hearty scoop of coleslaw. The crunchiness of the slaw complements the tenderness of the pork perfectly.
  3. Top Bun: Finish off with the top bun, and if desired, drizzle extra barbecue sauce for added flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Pulled pork sandwiches are incredibly versatile and can be served with various sides to complete your meal. Here are some classic suggestions:

  • Baked Beans: The sweetness and smokiness of baked beans pair beautifully with pulled pork.
  • Cornbread: A slice of moist cornbread makes for a comforting side that complements the sandwich.
  • Pickles: Add some tangy pickles on the side for a refreshing crunch.
  • Potato Salad: Creamy potato salad is another great option that balances the flavors of the meal.

Tips for the Best Pulled Pork Sandwich

  1. Rest the Meat: Allowing the pork to rest after cooking is crucial for retaining its juices. This will lead to a more flavorful and succulent sandwich.
  2. Experiment with Sauces: While traditional barbecue sauce is fantastic, don’t hesitate to try different sauces, such as mustard-based or vinegar-based, to put your unique spin on the dish.
  3. Make it Your Own: Feel free to customize your pulled pork sandwich with additional toppings like sliced jalapeños, avocado, or even fried onions for added texture.
  4. Store Leftovers Properly: If you have any leftover pulled pork, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to four days or freeze it for future meals.

Conclusion

Pulled pork sandwiches with coleslaw are more than just a meal; they are a celebration of flavors and textures that brings people together. Whether enjoyed at a summer cookout or a cozy family dinner, these sandwiches are sure to impress. With a little patience and the right techniques, you can master the art of making pulled pork and create a dish that your friends and family will rave about. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the mouthwatering goodness of pulled pork sandwiches with homemade coleslaw!
