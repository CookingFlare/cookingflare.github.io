Grilled Halloumi: The Cheesy Delight for Vegetarians


When it comes to vegetarian cooking, one ingredient that never fails to impress is halloumi cheese. This semi-hard cheese, originating from Cyprus, has gained immense popularity in kitchens around the world. Its unique texture and ability to withstand high temperatures make it a favorite for grilling. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of grilled halloumi, exploring its origins, health benefits, and a variety of delicious recipes that showcase this cheesy delight.

What is Halloumi?

Halloumi is a traditional cheese made from a mixture of goat and sheep milk, although cow’s milk varieties are now common. Its high melting point allows it to be grilled or fried, making it a versatile ingredient for a range of dishes. Halloumi has a distinct flavor that is both salty and slightly tangy, with a chewy texture that appeals to cheese lovers everywhere.

The History of Halloumi

Halloumi has been a staple in Cypriot cuisine for centuries. Its production dates back to the Middle Ages, and it has been an integral part of the local diet. Traditionally, halloumi was made during the summer months when the milk was plentiful, and it was often preserved in brine or olive oil to extend its shelf life. Today, halloumi is enjoyed worldwide, often served as part of mezze platters or grilled as a standalone dish.

Why Choose Halloumi?

For vegetarians looking for a hearty protein source, halloumi is a fantastic option. It contains about 20 grams of protein per 100 grams, making it a valuable addition to any meat-free meal. Furthermore, halloumi is rich in calcium, which is essential for bone health. It is also low in carbohydrates, making it suitable for those following low-carb diets.

Health Benefits of Grilled Halloumi

Grilling halloumi not only enhances its flavor but also makes it a healthier option compared to frying. Here are some of the health benefits of incorporating grilled halloumi into your diet:

1. Source of Protein

As mentioned, halloumi is packed with protein, which is crucial for muscle repair and growth. This makes it an excellent choice for vegetarians and active individuals.

2. Rich in Calcium

Halloumi is an excellent source of calcium, which is vital for maintaining strong bones and teeth. Grilling it retains most of its nutritional value, making it a smart choice for health-conscious eaters.

3. Low in Carbohydrates

Grilled halloumi is low in carbohydrates, making it a suitable ingredient for those who are managing their carb intake or following ketogenic diets.

4. Versatile Ingredient

Grilled halloumi can be used in a variety of dishes, from salads to wraps, making it a versatile ingredient that can suit different palates and dietary needs.

How to Grill Halloumi

Grilling halloumi is a simple process, but there are a few tips to ensure you get the best results:

1. Choose the Right Cheese

Opt for high-quality halloumi cheese. Look for brands that use natural ingredients and avoid additives. Fresh halloumi will have a more pleasant flavor and texture.

2. Prepare the Cheese

Slice the halloumi into thick pieces, about 1 cm thick. Thicker slices will hold up better on the grill and will have a satisfying texture when cooked.

3. Preheat the Grill

Make sure your grill is hot before placing the halloumi on it. A preheated grill will create those beautiful grill marks and enhance the flavor through caramelization.

4. Use Oil

Brush the halloumi slices lightly with olive oil to prevent sticking and to add flavor. You can also add spices or herbs to the oil for an extra kick.

5. Grill to Perfection

Place the halloumi slices on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and have grill marks. Be careful not to overcook them, as they can become rubbery.

Delicious Recipes Featuring Grilled Halloumi

Now that you know how to grill halloumi, let’s explore some delightful recipes that highlight this cheesy delight.

1. Grilled Halloumi Salad

Ingredients:

  • 200g halloumi cheese, sliced
  • 4 cups mixed greens (spinach, arugula, and romaine)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Brush halloumi slices with olive oil and grill until golden brown on both sides.
  3. In a large bowl, combine mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Top the salad with grilled halloumi and serve immediately.

2. Grilled Halloumi Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 200g halloumi cheese, cut into cubes
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • Olive oil
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Thread halloumi cubes, bell pepper, zucchini, and red onion onto skewers.
  3. Brush with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and halloumi is golden.
  5. Serve with a side of tzatziki sauce for dipping.

3. Halloumi and Vegetable Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 200g halloumi cheese, sliced
  • 4 whole grain wraps
  • 1 cup mixed veggies (spinach, carrots, bell peppers)
  • Hummus
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (basil or parsley)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Grill the halloumi slices until golden brown.
  3. Spread hummus on each wrap, add grilled halloumi, mixed veggies, and fresh herbs.
  4. Roll up the wraps tightly and serve with a side salad.

4. Grilled Halloumi Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 200g halloumi cheese, sliced
  • 4 burger buns
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Grill the halloumi slices until they are golden brown.
  3. Toast the burger buns on the grill for a minute.
  4. Assemble the burgers by placing lettuce, grilled halloumi, avocado, and tomato on each bun.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic glaze if desired, and serve warm.

5. Mediterranean Quinoa Bowl with Grilled Halloumi

Ingredients:

  • 200g halloumi cheese, sliced
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup olives, pitted and sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Grill the halloumi slices until golden brown.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, olives, and parsley.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Top with grilled halloumi and serve.

Tips for Pairing Grilled Halloumi

Grilled halloumi pairs beautifully with a variety of ingredients. Here are some ideas to enhance your halloumi dishes:

  • Fruits: Grilled peaches, figs, and pineapple add a sweet contrast to the salty cheese.
  • Herbs: Fresh herbs like mint, basil, and parsley elevate the flavor profile.
  • Nuts: Toasted almonds or walnuts provide a crunchy texture that complements the creaminess of halloumi.
  • Dressings: A drizzle of balsamic glaze or a squeeze of lemon adds brightness to grilled halloumi dishes.

Conclusion

Grilled halloumi is more than just a cheese; it’s a versatile ingredient that can elevate any vegetarian dish. Whether you’re preparing a refreshing salad, hearty wraps, or skewers for a summer barbecue, halloumi’s unique flavor and texture make it a star. With its numerous health benefits and endless possibilities in the kitchen, it’s no wonder grilled halloumi has earned a special place in the hearts of food lovers everywhere. So fire up the grill, get creative, and enjoy the cheesy delight that is grilled halloumi!
