Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu Over Fettuccine


If you’re looking to elevate your pasta game with something both savory and refreshing, this Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu over Fettuccine is the perfect dish to try. The rich flavors of lamb, combined with the cooling and aromatic mint, create a beautifully balanced sauce that complements the delicate fettuccine. This dish offers an incredible blend of savory, herbal, and slightly sweet notes, making it a crowd-pleaser for family dinners or special occasions.

Ingredients for the Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu

For the Lamb Ragu:

  • 1.5 lbs ground lamb (or lamb shoulder, finely chopped)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine (optional but highly recommended for depth of flavor)
  • 1/2 cup chicken or beef broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint leaves, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Fettuccine:

  • 12 oz fresh fettuccine (or dried if fresh is unavailable)
  • Salt, for the pasta water

For Serving:

  • Freshly grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese (optional)
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves (for garnish)

Instructions: How to Prepare Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu Over Fettuccine

Step 1: Brown the Lamb

To create a rich and flavorful ragu, begin by heating a tablespoon of olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the ground lamb (or finely chopped lamb shoulder) and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and no longer pink. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Once browned, transfer the lamb to a bowl and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pan, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery. Sauté for 5-6 minutes, or until the vegetables soften and the onions become translucent. The mixture should smell sweet and fragrant by now.

Step 3: Add the Garlic and Spices

Add the minced garlic to the pan and sauté for another 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent the garlic from burning. Next, incorporate the cumin, cinnamon, coriander, and cloves. The warmth of these spices will add a wonderful depth of flavor to the ragu. Stir everything together until the spices become fragrant, which should take about 30 seconds.

Step 4: Deglaze the Pan

To deglaze the pan and scrape up all the caramelized bits from the bottom of the pot, pour in the red wine (if using) and allow it to simmer for about 3-4 minutes. This step will intensify the flavors and provide a rich base for the ragu. If you prefer not to use wine, you can substitute it with additional broth.

Step 5: Build the Ragu Sauce

Once the wine has reduced, add the tomato paste, followed by the crushed tomatoes and broth. Stir everything together until well combined. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and return the browned lamb to the pan. Let the ragu simmer on low heat for about 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Stir occasionally, ensuring the sauce does not stick to the bottom. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add a bit more broth or water to reach the desired consistency.

Step 6: Add Fresh Mint

Once the ragu has cooked down and thickened to your liking, stir in the chopped fresh mint. The mint will infuse the ragu with its aromatic, refreshing flavor, which contrasts wonderfully with the savory lamb. Taste the ragu and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to your preference.

Step 7: Prepare the Fettuccine

While the lamb and mint ragu is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine and cook according to the package instructions until al dente, typically around 2-3 minutes for fresh pasta or 8-10 minutes for dried. Once cooked, drain the pasta, reserving about a cup of pasta water.

Step 8: Combine the Ragu and Fettuccine

Once the pasta is cooked and drained, add it directly to the pan with the lamb ragu. Toss the pasta in the ragu, making sure the fettuccine is well-coated with the flavorful sauce. If needed, add a little pasta water to help the sauce cling to the noodles.

Step 9: Serve and Garnish

To serve, plate the fettuccine with a generous portion of lamb and mint ragu on top. Garnish with additional fresh mint leaves and a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese, if desired. The richness of the cheese complements the savory lamb and fresh mint beautifully.

Tips for the Best Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu Over Fettuccine

1. Choosing the Right Cut of Lamb

For a more tender ragu, lamb shoulder is an excellent choice because it has a higher fat content and becomes incredibly tender when slow-cooked. Ground lamb is convenient and still offers that deep, savory flavor, but using whole cuts like shoulder and then shredding the meat provides a more rustic texture.

2. Fresh Herbs Make a Difference

While dried mint can be used in a pinch, fresh mint really brings the dish to life. The herbal, slightly sweet flavor of mint adds a beautiful contrast to the richness of the lamb. Don’t skip the fresh mint garnish – it will elevate the entire dish.

3. Don’t Rush the Simmering

The longer the ragu simmers, the more developed and complex the flavors will become. Aim for at least 30-40 minutes of simmering, but if you have the time, let it go for an hour or longer. Low and slow is the key to a deeply flavorful sauce.

4. Wine Alternatives

If you prefer not to use red wine in the recipe, consider using balsamic vinegar for a touch of acidity and richness. You can also substitute the wine with broth if you’re looking for a non-alcoholic version of the dish.

5. Make Ahead for the Best Flavor

This lamb and mint ragu can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. In fact, the flavors will only improve after a day or two. Simply reheat the ragu on the stovetop before serving and cook fresh fettuccine.

Pairing Suggestions

This savory lamb and mint ragu pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes and beverages. For a well-rounded meal, consider serving it with a crisp green salad dressed with a simple lemon vinaigrette to balance the richness of the ragu. Roasted vegetables, such as carrots, parsnips, or Brussels sprouts, are also a great choice, adding sweetness and texture to the meal.

In terms of drinks, a medium-bodied red wine like Chianti or a Cabernet Sauvignon would complement the lamb’s rich flavors. If you prefer something non-alcoholic, a sparkling water with a wedge of lemon can provide a refreshing contrast to the savory sauce.

Conclusion

Savory Lamb and Mint Ragu over Fettuccine is a wonderful dish that perfectly balances rich and aromatic flavors. The tender lamb, combined with the fragrant mint and warm spices, creates a comforting, yet vibrant, sauce that coats each strand of pasta. This dish is perfect for any occasion where you want to impress your guests with something special and memorable. Whether it’s a cozy family dinner or a special celebration, this lamb and mint ragu will surely become a new favorite in your recipe rotation.
