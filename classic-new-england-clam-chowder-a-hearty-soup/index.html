Classic New England Clam Chowder - A Hearty Soup


Clam chowder is a classic New England dish that warms the soul and delights the taste buds. With its rich and creamy base, tender clams, and hearty chunks of potatoes, this soup is a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting bowl of goodness, this recipe is a must-try. So, let’s dive in!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh clams
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups clam juice
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by cleaning the clams. Place them in a large bowl filled with cold water and let them soak for about 20 minutes. This will help remove any sand or grit. Rinse and scrub the clams under running water to ensure they are clean.

  2. In a large pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the bacon grease in the pot.

  3. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and diced celery to the pot with the bacon grease. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the cubed potatoes, clam juice, and bay leaf to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes.

  5. While the potatoes are cooking, prepare the clams. Discard any clams with broken shells or that do not close when tapped. In a separate pot, steam the clams over high heat until they open, about 5 minutes. Remove the clams from the pot and set them aside. Discard any clams that did not open during cooking.

  6. Once the potatoes are tender, remove the bay leaf from the pot and add the cooked clams. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Ladle the clam chowder into bowls and garnish with the crispy bacon and fresh parsley. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe even more flavorful, consider adding a splash of white wine or a sprinkle of Old Bay seasoning.
  • If you prefer a thicker chowder, you can mash some of the potatoes against the side of the pot to thicken the soup.
  • For a lighter version of this classic chowder, you can substitute half-and-half or whole milk for the heavy cream.
  • If fresh clams are not readily available, you can use canned clams. Just be sure to drain the clams before adding them to the soup.

Conclusion

Classic New England Clam Chowder is a timeless recipe that never fails to impress. With its creamy base, tender clams, and comforting flavors, it’s a soup that will warm your heart and satisfy your cravings. Whether you’re a seafood lover or simply looking for a delicious and hearty meal, this chowder is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, get cooking, and indulge in the goodness of this classic New England dish. Enjoy!
