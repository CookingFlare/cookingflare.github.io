Korean Style Spicy Pork Stir-Fry (Dwaeji Bulgogi)


Korean cuisine is celebrated globally for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and irresistible aromas. One dish that perfectly encapsulates these qualities is Dwaeji Bulgogi, or Korean Style Spicy Pork Stir-Fry. This dish is a flavor-packed delight that combines tender slices of pork with a rich and spicy marinade, perfectly balanced by hints of sweetness and umami. Whether you’re a seasoned Korean food enthusiast or a home cook looking to experience something new, this recipe will bring a burst of authentic flavors to your table. Let’s dive into the details of making Dwaeji Bulgogi at home.

What is Dwaeji Bulgogi?

Dwaeji Bulgogi (돼지 불고기) directly translates to “pork fire meat” in Korean. While its beef counterpart, simply called Bulgogi, tends to lean towards a sweeter marinade, Dwaeji Bulgogi is all about heat and spice, making it an excellent choice for those who enjoy bolder, more robust flavors.

The dish is characterized by marinated pork slices stir-fried over high heat until they develop deep caramelization. With the combination of gochujang (fermented Korean chili paste), gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and sugar, the result is a spicy, savory, and slightly sweet dish that pairs wonderfully with rice and an array of Korean side dishes.

Ingredients for Korean Style Spicy Pork Stir-Fry

To make this dish, you’ll need a mix of fresh ingredients and pantry staples often found in Korean cooking. Each ingredient plays an important role in achieving the complex layering of flavors that Dwaeji Bulgogi is famous for.

For the Marinade:

  • Pork: 500g (about 1 pound) of pork shoulder or pork belly, thinly sliced
  • Gochujang: 3 tablespoons (adds heat and depth)
  • Gochugaru: 2 tablespoons (for extra spiciness)
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons (for saltiness and umami)
  • Sesame oil: 2 tablespoons (adds nutty richness)
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon (balances the heat and acidity)
  • Honey: 1 tablespoon (optional, for a gentler sweetness)
  • Rice wine or mirin: 2 tablespoons (adds a mild tang and helps tenderize the meat)
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced (essential for savory undertones)
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, grated (for subtle warmth)
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, finely chopped
  • Black pepper: ½ teaspoon (for a touch of heat)

For Stir-Frying:

  • Cooking oil: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Vegetables (optional): Sliced onions, green bell peppers, carrots, and mushrooms
  • Sesame seeds: 1 tablespoon (for garnish)
  • Chopped green onions: For garnish

For Serving:

  • Steamed white rice
  • Korean side dishes such as kimchi, pickled radishes, or japchae (sweet potato noodles)

Preparing the Dish

Step 1: Marinate the Pork

The key to tender and flavorful Dwaeji Bulgogi lies in the marinade. Start by preparing the pork. Thin slices work best for this dish as they absorb the flavors beautifully and cook quickly. If your pork isn’t pre-sliced, you can place it in the freezer for 20-30 minutes to make slicing easier.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, honey (if using), rice wine, minced garlic, grated ginger, chopped green onions, and black pepper. Mix the marinade thoroughly until it forms a smooth paste.

Add the sliced pork into the bowl, ensuring every piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or transfer the pork into a ziplock bag. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

Step 2: Prepare Your Ingredients

While the pork marinates, prepare the vegetables and garnishes. Slice your onions, bell peppers, carrots, and mushrooms thinly to ensure they cook evenly in the stir-fry. Keep your sesame seeds and chopped green onions handy for garnish.

Step 3: Stir-Frying

Heat a large skillet, wok, or frying pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the cooking oil and swirl it around the pan. Start by adding the marinated pork in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Overcrowding can lead to steaming instead of stir-frying, resulting in less caramelization.

Let the pork cook undisturbed for 2-3 minutes, allowing the sugars from the marinade to caramelize and form a golden-brown crust. Stir and toss the pork around to cook evenly. If you’re adding vegetables, toss them in once the pork is mostly cooked. Stir-fry everything together for an additional 3-4 minutes or until the vegetables are tender yet crispy and the pork is fully cooked.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Once cooked, transfer the Dwaeji Bulgogi to a serving plate. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and fresh chopped green onions for an added crunch and burst of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Dwaeji Bulgogi is best enjoyed fresh and hot off the skillet. Pair it with steamed rice to soak up the flavorful sauce. For an authentic Korean dining experience, serve Dwaeji Bulgogi as part of a larger meal with several side dishes, collectively known as banchan. Kimchi, pickled radish, and seasoned spinach complement this dish perfectly.

Additionally, if you’re feeling adventurous, wrap the pork in lettuce leaves or perilla leaves with a dollop of ssamjang (Korean wrap sauce). This traditional way of eating Korean barbecue keeps the meal fresh and adds texture to every bite.

Tips for Success

  • Choose the right cut of pork: Pork shoulder and pork belly work best for this recipe due to their balance of lean meat and fat. Pork loin can also be used but may be less tender.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of gochugaru or replace it with paprika. For more heat, add sliced fresh chili peppers to the stir-fry.
  • Preheat your pan: Stir-frying requires high heat for quick cooking, so make sure your skillet or wok is hot before adding the pork. This helps achieve caramelization and locks in the flavor.
  • Don’t skimp on marination time: Overnight marination yields the best results, but even an hour can make a noticeable difference in flavor.

Culinary Variations

While the traditional Dwaeji Bulgogi recipe is delightful as is, there’s plenty of room for creativity. Consider these variations:

  • Add rice cakes (tteokbokki): For a chewy texture, toss in Korean rice cakes during the stir-fry process.
  • Make it saucier: If you prefer a saucier dish, add a splash of chicken broth or water while stir-frying and let it reduce slightly.
  • Use different proteins: Although pork is traditional, you can substitute chicken, beef, or even tofu for a vegetarian twist.
  • Try it with noodles: Mix the stir-fried pork with cooked sweet potato noodles (japchae-style) for a hearty one-pot meal.

Wrapping It Up

Dwaeji Bulgogi is a quintessential Korean dish that’s bursting with flavor and vibrancy. Its rich marinade and quick cooking method make it accessible to home cooks while providing a restaurant-quality meal that’s sure to impress family and friends. Whether you enjoy it with rice, wrapped in lettuce, or as part of a Korean feast, this spicy pork stir-fry will undoubtedly become a cherished addition to your kitchen repertoire.
