Healthy BBQ Options: Light and Delicious Grilled Fare


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious BBQ. But if you’re trying to eat healthy, traditional BBQ fare might not be the best choice. Luckily, there are plenty of light and delicious options that you can grill up without sacrificing flavor. In this blog post, we’ll explore some healthy BBQ options that will leave your taste buds satisfied and your waistline happy.

Grilled Chicken Skewers with Lemon and Herbs

Chicken is a lean protein that is perfect for grilling. To make these delicious skewers, start by marinating your chicken in a mixture of lemon juice, garlic, and a variety of fresh herbs such as rosemary, thyme, and parsley. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat. Then, thread the chicken onto skewers and grill them over medium heat until they are cooked through and slightly charred. The result is tender, juicy chicken with a burst of fresh flavors.

Grilled Vegetable Medley

Who says BBQ has to be all about meat? Grilling vegetables not only brings out their natural sweetness but also adds a smoky flavor that is hard to resist. To make a grilled vegetable medley, choose a variety of colorful vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, eggplant, and cherry tomatoes. Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces and toss them with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Grill the vegetables in a grill basket or skewer them for easy flipping. The result is a colorful and flavorful side dish that pairs perfectly with any grilled protein.

Grilled Salmon with Mango Salsa

Salmon is not only packed with heart-healthy omega-3 fatty acids but also incredibly delicious when grilled. To make this dish, season your salmon fillets with a sprinkle of salt, pepper, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Grill the salmon over medium-high heat until it is cooked to your desired level of doneness. While the salmon is grilling, prepare a refreshing mango salsa by combining diced mango, red onion, jalapeno, cilantro, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Serve the grilled salmon topped with the vibrant mango salsa for a burst of tropical flavors.

Grilled Shrimp and Pineapple Skewers

Shrimp is another lean protein that is perfect for grilling. To make these flavorful skewers, marinate your shrimp in a mixture of soy sauce, honey, garlic, and ginger for about 15 minutes. Thread the shrimp onto skewers alternating with chunks of pineapple. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the shrimp turns pink and the pineapple caramelizes. The combination of sweet and savory flavors in these skewers will have you coming back for seconds.

Grilled Fruit Kabobs

Who says the grill is only for savory dishes? Grilling fruit can take it to a whole new level. Choose your favorite fruits such as pineapple, peaches, and strawberries, and thread them onto skewers. Grill the fruit over medium-high heat until it is slightly charred and caramelized. The heat intensifies the natural sweetness of the fruit, creating a delightful contrast of flavors. Serve the grilled fruit kabobs on their own or with a dollop of Greek yogurt for a healthy and satisfying dessert.

Conclusion

With these healthy BBQ options, you can enjoy the delicious flavors of summer without feeling guilty. Whether you’re grilling up chicken skewers, a medley of grilled vegetables, or even fruit kabobs, there are plenty of light and delicious options to choose from. So fire up the grill, invite your friends and family, and enjoy a BBQ feast that’s both healthy and satisfying. Happy grilling!
