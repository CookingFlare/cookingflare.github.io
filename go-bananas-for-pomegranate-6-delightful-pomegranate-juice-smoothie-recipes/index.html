Go Bananas for Pomegranate: 6 Delightful Pomegranate Juice & Smoothie Recipes


Ah, the pomegranate – that jewel-toned awesomeness that just refuses to go out of style. A thing of beauty and deliciousness, it’s a fruit that offers a burst of refreshing sweet and tangy goodness. Whether it’s winter or summer, the pomegranate packs a powerful punch of vitamins and nutrients, making it the perfect ingredient to have around. So let’s go bananas for this superfruit and check out these delightful juice and smoothie recipes that are guaranteed to make your taste buds sing.

1. Pomegranate Orange Pineapple Cooler

Who isn’t a fan of the classic orange pineapple combo? Now let’s kick it up a notch by adding the mighty pomegranate. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • 1 cup sparkling water
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A dash of agave nectar or honey, to taste

Blend all of the ingredients together until smooth, and you’ve got yourself a refreshing tropical spritzer that’s light but oh-so-packed with the goodness of pomegranates.

2. Pomegranate Berry Blitz

This one’s for all you berry lovers out there.

  • 1 cup mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • A handful of baby spinach leaves
  • Optional: a swirl of Greek yogurt for added creaminess

Throw everything into the blender and give it a whirl to create a vibrant concoction full of fiber and antioxidants.

3. Pomegranate Banana Green Smoothie

Is your typical smoothie regimen in dire need of some revamping? Introducing the perfect green smoothie to satiate your taste buds and keep you feeling satisfied. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 ripe banana
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • 1 cup fresh baby spinach
  • ½ avocado
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • A big handful of fresh kale leaves

You may be surprised at how delicious this combination is. With the creamy pomegranate and banana, you won’t even taste the greens!

4. Pomegranate Ginger Twist

Looking to add some spice to your life? Let’s heat things up with the addition of ginger in this recipe:

  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, peeled
  • 1 ripe banana
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • ½ cup fresh baby spinach

A dash of cinnamon and a drizzle of honey could take this smoothie to the next level.

5. Pomegranate Matcha Mojito

Time to try something out of the ordinary. This matcha mojito with a pomegranate twist will make you feel as if you’re on a tropical vacation.

  • ½ cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 cup ice
  • 1 ripe banana
  • ½ cup matcha green tea powder
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • Soda water
  • Fresh mint leaves

Blend everything except soda water and mint leaves, then pour it into glasses filled with ice, top off with soda water, and garnish with mint leaves for that authentic mojito touch.

6. Spicy Pomegranate Chai Smoothie

Looking to ignite your senses while nourishing your body? Then give this spicy chai pomegranate smoothie a try.

  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • ½ teaspoon chai tea powder
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup roasted daliya (broken cereal) or rolled oats
  • Optional: a splash of unsweetened vanilla almond milk or regular milk to thin it out

Blend all your spices, daliya (or oats), banana, and almond milk, and then stir in the pomegranate arils – that’s it! You’ve got yourself a tasty and nutrient-packed breakfast or snack.

The Final Verdict

Whether you’re searching for a sweet treat or something on the healthier side, incorporating pomegranate juice or smoothies into your daily routine can’t be beat. With its pleasant, refreshing taste and an abundance of nutrients, who wouldn’t want to “go bananas” for pomegranate juice and smoothies? Give these delightful recipes a try, and soon enough, you’ll be specifying your smoothie order as “hold the banana, extra pomegranate, please.” Enjoy!
