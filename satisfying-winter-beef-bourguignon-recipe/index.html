Satisfying Winter Beef Bourguignon Recipe


Winter is the perfect time to indulge in comforting, hearty dishes that warm both the body and soul. And when it comes to classic winter recipes, few can rival the rich and flavorful Beef Bourguignon. This French dish, traditionally prepared with tender beef, aromatic vegetables, and a generous splash of red wine, is a true winter delight. Today, I’ll be sharing with you my recipe for a satisfying Beef Bourguignon that will surely become a staple in your winter cooking repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups red wine (such as Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 pound small white button mushrooms
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the bacon drippings in the pot.

  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper, then add it to the pot with the bacon drippings. Cook the beef until browned on all sides, then remove it from the pot and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the diced onion, sliced carrots, and minced garlic. Cook until the vegetables are softened and golden brown, about 5 minutes.

  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste.

  5. Slowly pour in the red wine while stirring, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. This will add depth and flavor to the dish.

  6. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, thyme leaves, and bay leaves to the pot. Stir to combine.

  7. Return the cooked beef and crispy bacon to the pot, along with any accumulated juices. Stir well to coat the meat and vegetables in the flavorful liquid.

  8. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  9. In the last 30 minutes of cooking, add the white button mushrooms to the pot. They will soak up the delicious flavors of the stew, becoming tender and flavorful.

  10. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Remove the bay leaves and discard.

  11. Serve the Beef Bourguignon hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley. This dish pairs perfectly with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or buttered egg noodles.

Enjoy the Satisfying Winter Beef Bourguignon

This Satisfying Winter Beef Bourguignon is a true showstopper. The tender beef, rich broth, and aromatic vegetables come together to create a dish that is both comforting and elegant. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to develop and intensify, resulting in a stew that is truly satisfying. So, when the weather turns cold and you’re in need of a hearty meal, give this Beef Bourguignon a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
