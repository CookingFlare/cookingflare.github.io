The Mystery of Braised Pork Shanks: Regional Chinese Recipes Worth Missing Out


Ah, braised pork shank—finger-licking, succulent, and an iconic dish in the realm of Chinese cuisine. While the passage of time may push these traditional recipes to the margins, there is still a buzz in the culinary world for the return of these braised pork shanks. Let us explore the heart of regional Chinese recipes worth missing out.

Lanzhou Braised Pork Shank

Lanzhou’s braised pork shank, or Lan Zhou La Mian Pork Knuckle, deserves its place on the list. This local dish is popular in Lanzhou, the bustling capital of Gansu Province. The stunning contrast between the tender and crispy texture of the pork shank and its rich flavor with slight sweetness is what gives this recipe its uniqueness. The slow cooking method, along with spices such as star anise, cinnamon, and ginger, sets it apart from other recipes.

To make this delicacy, choose large pork shanks to ensure a better bite and flavor. Begin by parboiling the shank, scraping the surface skin, then dredge in seasoned cornstarch to create that perfectly textured crust. The marvelous balance of flavors comes with slow-braising the pork shank in a potent, aromatic broth.

Chongqing Braised Pork Shank

Oh, Chongqing—what magic you weave with food! The Chongqing braised pork shank, or Chongqing La du, is a dish that blends fiery flavors with delightfully savory notes. Renowned for its famous hotpot, Chongqing also offers its unique spin on this braised pork shank. The dish boasts a rustic appearance, with a tomato-based sauce blending the flavors of chili and other spices.

Selecting pork shank with a good layer of fat is essential as it provides an ideal balance between crunchy and tender. Begin by cooking the pork shank until it’s tender, then mix together a blend of spices, including Sichuan pepper, star anise, and fennel seeds. Create a tangy and spicy sauce using tomatoes, wine, and various condiments, then drizzle it over the pork shank while keeping it warm in the oven.

Tianjin Braised Pork Shank

Now, let’s venture into the city of ‘100 Shops’—Tianjin. Famous for itsrich historical background and distinctive culture, Tianjin boasts a delightful spin on the braised pork shank, locally known as ‘Tianjin La Rou.’ The culinary marvel is the result of a marriage between the local’s love for pork and their fusion with Manchu culture.

The dish features tender pork shank bathing in a rich, dark sauce highlighting the flavors of a rich vitamin-C filled Tianjin red pickled cabbage, otherwise known as Tianjin purple-sprouting broccoli, and soybean paste. The stared anise, cinnamon, and fennel seeds add its charm to this tantalizing dish.

Suzhou Pork Knuckle Clarified

Suzhou, a city of classical Gardens and refine culture, graces our table with a silky and appetizing Pork Knuckle Clarified, strategically known as ‘Suzhou Pig Knuckle,’ which is an elegant and refined approach that offers an exclusive experience. Incorporate fresh ginger, garlic, and white pepper into the recipe as flavors grow with time, making this dish a comforting and delightful experience.

The richness in taste comes not just from the marination but also from its unique technique ‘clarifying’ the broth. Suzhou locals are stringent on using superior ingredients cautiously touching each flavor with care— a vital aspect of crafting ‘a dish that is well-balanced and full-bodied.’

Shandong Braised Pork Shanks

Shandong Province’s cuisine is famous for creativity, flavor, aroma, and rich ingredients. The Shandong braised pork shank offers variety through its rich usage of spices and sauces that complement the meat’s texture beautifully. This dish uses an insipid sauce made from a blend of soy sauce, sugar, and Shandong vinegar—one of China’s most exceptional culinary treasures.

Start by dredging the pork shank in cornstarch and pan-fry for a crackling outer crust. Follow this step by slow braising with the Shandong sauce, revealing the authentic flavor with a hint of spiciness. Finally, garnish with fresh scallions and crush some Sichuan peppercorns to accompany the dish.

From the central plains to the coastal cities—each region of China has its unique and unforgettable take on braised pork shank. Each dish provides a distinctive culinary journey, revealing layers of tradition, culture, and taste. Come, let the mystery of the braised pork shank commence, and let your taste buds be your guide to the authenticity of regional Chinese cuisine. Enjoy these mouth-watering dishes from China, and don’t forget to share the secret with your friends and family!
