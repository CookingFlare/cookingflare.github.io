A Spicy Concoction: How to Blend an Array of Spices in Cooking


Cooking with spices is a wonderful way to enhance the flavors of your dishes. A skilled chef knows that blending an array of spices can create unique flavor profiles, take your culinary creations to the next level, and surprise your taste buds. In this blog post, we will dive into the art of spice blending and share some tricks, tips, and recipes to get you started.

Why Blend Spices?

Think about walking through a spice market or dense herb garden. The diversity of smells and flavors is almost overwhelming. Each spice adds its own unique personality to a dish, which is why blending them can create a harmonious symphony of flavors. In addition, blending spices allows you to control the intensity, compatibility, and balance of flavors in your cooking. Pre-blended spices can be store-bought, but nothing beats the smell and taste of your custom-blended spice mix.

The World of Spices

Before we start blending, let’s explore the world of spices. By understanding their individual characteristics, you’ll be better equipped to create your perfect blend. Here are some common spices you might want to explore:

  • Cumin: Earthy with a slightly nutty taste, cumin pairs well with many staple ingredients including tomatoes, chilies, and onions.
  • Coriander: Offers a bright, citrusy note with warm, sweet undertones. It pairs beautifully with meat dishes and adds a kick to curries.
  • Paprika: Provides a sweet, almost smoky flavor, which can range from mild to hot depending on the type used. It’s versatile and shines in dishes like Spanish paella and Hungarian goulash.
  • Turmeric: Known for its golden color and mild, peppery flavor, turmeric adds both color and flavor to curries and rice dishes.
  • Cayenne Pepper: The king of heat, cayenne pepper can transform a dish from mild to mouth-numbingly hot. Use it sparingly but effectively.
  • Cinnamon: Often used in sweet dishes, its distinctive sweetness and delicate warmth also complement savory rice, lamb, and tomato-based recipes.
  • Ginger: Sharp and pungent with a hint of heat, ginger elevates a wide range of dishes, from stir-frys to baked goods.

These spices are just a starting point. There are hundreds—if not thousands—of unique spices from around the world, each with its own story and flavor profile.

Techniques for Spice Blending

1. Choosing Your Spices

Start by selecting the spices you think would work well together. Aim for a balance of flavors—sweet, savory, bitter, and hot. Consider which spices complement each other and enhance your dish’s natural flavors.

2. Measuring Your Spices

It’s time to start measuring. For each blend, use a ratio that works for your dish. Here are some ratios to get you started:

  • ** Indian Curry Powder**: 1 part turmeric, 1 part coriander, 1/2 part cumin, 1/4 part fenugreek, and 1/4 part red chili powder.

Remember, this is just a starting point. Feel free to experiment with the ratios until they suit your taste buds.

3. Grinding Your Spices

Whole spices offer more robust flavors and longer shelf life when compared to their ground counterparts. Use a spice grinder or a mortar and pestle to grind your whole spices. Be careful not to over-grind or you may produce a paste, which is not what we want.

4. Mixing It All Together

Combine your ground spices in a jar or mixing bowl. Whisk or shake until evenly distributed. The perfect spice blend should smell intoxicating and leave you eager to use it in your cooking.

Storage and Shelf Life

Store your homemade spice blends in a cool, dark, and airtight container. Opt for glass jars with tight-fitting lids, or sealed plastic bags. Proper storage keeps your blends fresh and prevents them from absorbing odors from your pantry.

Blended spices can last anywhere from a few months to a year, depending on the ingredients. As a rule of thumb, if your spice blend has been exposed to moisture, air, or light, it’s best to use it sooner rather than later. Trust your senses; if it looks or smells off, it’s time to toss it.

Incorporating Spice Blends in Your Cooking

Adding spice blends at different stages of cooking can significantly enhance the taste of your dishes. Here are some suggestions:

  • Dry rubs: Create your custom dry rub for meat by combining wood sugars, salts, and spices. Rub it into your protein hours before cooking for deeply flavored results.
  • Marinades: Incorporate your spice blends into homemade marinades for tender and flavorful meat and vegetables.
  • Sauces and gravies: Add a splash of flavor to dishes by stirring your spice blend into a sauce or gravy.
  • On the side: Serve a variety of spice blends for your guests to customize their dish. This can be a fun way to introduce new flavors to your diners.

Recipes to Get You Started

1. Spicy Chicken ChILI

  1. Combine 1 tablespoon cumin, 1 tablespoon smoked paprika, 2 teaspoons garlic powder, 2 teaspoons onion powder, and salt in a small bowl.
  2. Season 8 boneless, skinless chicken thighs with half of the spice mixture.
  3. Sauté chicken, then add it to a simmering pot of chili with your favorite ingredients and remaining spice mixture.
  4. E ncourage guests to add extra spice on top before serving.

2. Moroccan_style Lamb with Couscous

  1. Combine 1 tablespoon cumin, 1 tablespoon coriander, 1 tablespoon paprika, 1 teaspoon cinnamon, and salt in a small bowl.
  2. Season 1 pound of lamb stew pieces with the spice mixture, then brown in a pan.
  3. Add the lamb to a slow cooker with your favorite vegetables, dried apricots, and chickpeas. Cook on low for 6-8 hours.
  4. Serve over fluffy couscous and sprinkle with fresh cilantro.

3. Filipino Garlic_Soy Roasted Chicken

  1. Combine 1 tablespoon ground ginger, 1 tablespoon garlic powder, 1 tablespoon paprika, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and salt in a small bowl.
  2. Brush your roasting chicken with a mix of olive oil and the spice mixture; let it marinate for a few hours or overnight.
  3. Roast the chicken at 375°F (190°C) until golden and crispy.

These creative blends and recipes will get you started, but don’t be afraid to experiment. As your palate evolves, you’ll find your own unique blends to elevate your dishes. Happy cooking!
