Spanish Flan with Caramel Sauce: A Classic Dessert


If you’re a fan of creamy, indulgent desserts, then Spanish Flan with Caramel Sauce is a must-try. This classic dessert, also known as “Flan de Leche,” is a staple in Spanish and Latin American cuisine. Its smooth, velvety texture and rich caramel flavor make it a beloved treat for special occasions or simply to satisfy your sweet tooth. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the history of Spanish Flan, explore the key ingredients and steps to prepare it, and provide some tips for achieving the perfect flan every time.

The History of Spanish Flan

Flan has a rich history that dates back to ancient Rome, where it was known as “tyropatina” and made with honey and cheese. Over time, the recipe evolved, and the custard-based dessert we know today began to take shape. As culinary traditions spread across the globe, flan made its way to Spain, where it became a beloved part of the country’s dessert repertoire.

Ingredients

To make Spanish Flan with Caramel Sauce, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 3/4 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Caramel Sauce

  1. Start by making the caramel sauce. In a small, heavy-bottomed saucepan, melt the granulated sugar over medium heat. Swirl the pan occasionally to ensure even melting. Be careful not to stir the sugar, as this can cause it to crystallize.

  2. Once the sugar has melted and turned a deep amber color, carefully pour it into the bottom of your flan mold or individual ramekins. Swirl the mold or ramekins to evenly coat the bottom with the caramel. Set aside to cool and harden.

Step 2: Prepare the Flan Mixture

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, whole milk, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  3. Strain the flan mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to ensure a smooth, creamy texture.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Pour the flan mixture over the cooled caramel in the flan mold or ramekins.

  2. Place the flan mold or ramekins in a larger baking dish. Carefully pour hot water into the baking dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the flan mold or ramekins. This water bath, known as a bain-marie, will help the flan cook gently and evenly.

  3. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and carefully transfer it to the preheated oven.

  4. Bake for 50 to 60 minutes, or until the flan is set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center.

  5. Once the flan is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool to room temperature.

  6. Once cooled, transfer the flan to the refrigerator and chill for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow it to fully set.

Step 4: Unmold and Serve

  1. To unmold the flan, run a sharp knife around the edges to loosen it from the mold. Place a serving plate over the mold, then carefully invert it to release the flan and caramel sauce.

  2. Slice and serve the flan chilled, with a drizzle of the remaining caramel sauce over the top.

Tips for Success

  • When making the caramel sauce, be cautious as melted sugar can cause severe burns. Handle with care and keep a close eye on the melting process.
  • Straining the flan mixture is essential for achieving a smooth and silky texture, free from any lumps or air bubbles.
  • Allowing the flan to chill in the refrigerator for an extended period will help it set properly and develop its signature creamy consistency.

In Conclusion

Spanish Flan with Caramel Sauce is a timeless dessert that never fails to impress. Its luscious texture and decadent caramel flavor are sure to delight your taste buds and those of your guests. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet indulgence, this classic dessert is a perfect choice. So, why not try your hand at making this delightful treat and savor the creamy, caramel goodness of Spanish Flan?
