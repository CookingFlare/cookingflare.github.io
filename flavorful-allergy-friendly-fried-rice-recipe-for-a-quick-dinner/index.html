Flavorful Allergy-Friendly Fried Rice Recipe for a Quick Dinner


Are you tired of the same old dinner options? Looking for a quick and easy meal that is both flavorful and allergy-friendly? Look no further! This delicious fried rice recipe is packed with mouthwatering flavors and is suitable for those with food allergies. Whether you follow a gluten-free, dairy-free, or nut-free diet, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. Let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 tablespoons tamari sauce (gluten-free soy sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  2. Add the mixed vegetables to the skillet and stir-fry for a few minutes until they are tender-crisp. Make sure not to overcook them, as we want to retain their vibrant colors and crunchiness.

  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet, creating a space in the center. Crack the eggs into the center and scramble them until they are fully cooked.

  4. Now it’s time to add the cooked rice to the skillet. Break up any clumps of rice with a spatula and mix it with the vegetables and eggs.

  5. Drizzle the tamari sauce and sesame oil over the rice mixture. Stir everything together to ensure that the sauce is evenly distributed and coats the rice and vegetables.

  6. Continue to cook the fried rice for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is heated through and well combined.

  7. Finally, add the sliced green onions and season with salt and pepper according to your taste preferences. Give the fried rice a final toss to incorporate the green onions throughout the dish.

  8. Remove the skillet from the heat and serve the flavorful allergy-friendly fried rice immediately.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this dish even more allergy-friendly, you can customize it based on your specific dietary needs. For example, if you are avoiding soy, you can substitute tamari sauce with coconut aminos or a soy sauce alternative of your choice.

  • Feel free to add some protein to your fried rice by including cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu. Simply add your protein of choice during step 3, before adding the rice.

  • If you want to add an extra kick to your fried rice, you can sprinkle some red pepper flakes or drizzle some sriracha sauce over the finished dish.

  • Leftover rice works best for this recipe as it tends to be drier and less sticky. However, if you don’t have any leftover rice, you can cook a fresh batch and spread it out on a baking sheet to cool before using it in the recipe.

Now that you have this fantastic recipe in your arsenal, you can whip up a flavorful and allergy-friendly fried rice in no time. It’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a main course or as a side dish alongside your favorite Asian-inspired meals. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and get ready to impress your taste buds with this quick and delicious dinner option. Enjoy!
