Creamy Mashed Potatoes: The Perfect Side Dish for Any Comfort Meal


Mashed potatoes have always been a classic favorite, and for good reason. There’s something incredibly satisfying about a bowl of creamy, buttery potatoes that melt in your mouth. Whether you’re serving them alongside a roast dinner or as a comforting side dish for a weeknight meal, creamy mashed potatoes are always a crowd-pleaser. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make the perfect batch of mashed potatoes that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into evenly-sized chunks. This will ensure that they cook evenly and result in a smooth texture. Place the potato chunks in a large pot and cover them with cold water.

  2. Bring the pot of water to a boil over medium-high heat. Once the water reaches a rolling boil, reduce the heat to medium and let the potatoes cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are fork-tender. You should be able to easily pierce a potato chunk with a fork without any resistance.

  3. While the potatoes are boiling, prepare the creamy mixture. In a separate saucepan, heat the milk and butter over low heat until the butter has melted. Keep the mixture warm until the potatoes are ready.

  4. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them well and return them to the pot. Using a potato masher or a fork, mash the potatoes until they are smooth and free of lumps. Be careful not to over-mash, as this can result in a gluey texture.

  5. Slowly pour the warm milk and butter mixture into the mashed potatoes, stirring gently to incorporate. Add salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the seasoning as needed. The amount of salt will depend on your personal preference, so start with a small amount and add more if desired.

  6. Continue to stir the potatoes until they are creamy and well combined. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can use a hand mixer or a stand mixer on low speed to whip the potatoes until they reach your desired consistency.

  7. Once the mashed potatoes are smooth and creamy, transfer them to a serving dish and garnish with a pat of butter or a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley or chives. Serve immediately while still warm.

Tips and Variations

  • For extra flavor, you can add roasted garlic, grated cheese, or chopped fresh herbs to the mashed potatoes. Simply mix in these additional ingredients during step 5 for a delicious twist on the classic recipe.
  • If you prefer a dairy-free option, you can substitute the butter and milk with olive oil and vegetable broth. This will give the mashed potatoes a lighter, yet still creamy, texture.
  • To make the mashed potatoes ahead of time, prepare them as directed and transfer them to a heatproof dish. Cover with foil and keep them warm in a preheated oven at 200°F until ready to serve. Give them a quick stir before serving to ensure they are still creamy.

Creamy mashed potatoes are the ultimate comfort food. Their rich and velvety texture makes them the perfect side dish for any meal, from a holiday feast to a simple weeknight dinner. With just a few simple ingredients and some love and care, you can create a bowl of mashed potatoes that will have everyone at the table asking for seconds. So go ahead, whip up a batch of creamy mashed potatoes and enjoy the comfort and satisfaction they bring.
