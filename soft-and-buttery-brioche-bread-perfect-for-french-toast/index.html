Soft and Buttery Brioche Bread: Perfect for French Toast


There’s something truly magical about the taste and texture of a perfectly made brioche bread. Its soft, buttery richness makes it an ideal choice for creating the most decadent French toast you’ve ever tasted. If you’re a fan of indulgent breakfasts or simply enjoy the art of baking, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, dust off your apron, and let’s dive into the delightful world of brioche French toast.

Ingredients

For the Brioche Bread:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/3 cup warm milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups unsalted butter, softened and cut into pieces

For the French Toast:

  • 8 slices of brioche bread
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Butter, for cooking
  • Maple syrup, fresh berries, or powdered sugar, for serving

Instructions

Making the Brioche Bread:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm milk and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy.
  3. Add the yeast mixture to the dry ingredients and mix until a rough dough forms.
  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  5. Gradually add the softened butter, mixing until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  7. Once risen, gently punch down the dough and transfer it to a greased loaf pan.
  8. Cover the pan with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise again for 1 hour.
  9. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake the brioche for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  10. Allow the brioche to cool completely before slicing.

Making the French Toast:

  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon.
  2. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a knob of butter.
  3. Dip each slice of brioche into the egg mixture, allowing it to soak for a few seconds on each side.
  4. Place the soaked bread onto the hot skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Repeat with the remaining slices of brioche, adding more butter to the skillet as needed.
  6. Serve the French toast warm, topped with maple syrup, fresh berries, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra indulgent treat, try using chocolate brioche for your French toast.
  • Leftover brioche French toast can be stored in the refrigerator and reheated in a toaster for a quick and delicious breakfast on busy mornings.
  • Experiment with different toppings such as whipped cream, caramelized bananas, or a dollop of Greek yogurt for a personalized twist.

Conclusion

There’s no denying the allure of a slice of soft and buttery brioche, especially when it’s transformed into a delectable French toast. Whether you’re preparing a leisurely weekend brunch or simply want to elevate your breakfast game, this recipe is guaranteed to impress. So, gather your ingredients, savor the aroma of freshly baked brioche, and savor every delightful bite of this irresistible indulgence. French toast will never be the same again!
