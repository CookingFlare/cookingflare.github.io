IPA and Spice: Bold Pairings with IPA Beers


As the craft beer scene continues to flourish, both amateur and experienced home cooks are taking inspiration from bold, hoppy India Pale Ales (IPAs) to redefine and elevate their culinary creations. IPA’s distinctive bitterness and aromatic profile invite experimentation with various spices and flavors, making it the centerpiece for exciting, savory dishes.

Let’s dive into some innovative and delightful combinations that mesh the robust, hop-forward flavors of IPA with an array of spices.

Spicy Jalapeño Popper IPA Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 jalapeños, seeded and sliced
  • 2 cups IPAX beer
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 3 tablespoons chopped scallions (optional)

Directions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and cook until softened.
  2. Add the jalapeños and cook until slightly softened.
  3. Pour in IPAX beer and chicken stock. Stir in the heavy cream, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and cumin.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Stir in salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with grated cheddar cheese and chopped scallions, if desired.

IPA Braised Short Ribs with Ginger and Cardamom

Ingredients:

  • 2-2.5 lbs short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh ginger
  • 2 whole green cardamom pods
  • 2 cups IPAX beer
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the short ribs with salt and pepper and sear them on all sides.
  2. Add the ginger and cardamom pods and cook for a minute.
  3. Pour in IPAX beer, beef stock, soy sauce, and honey. Stir in the five-spice powder.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and braise the short ribs in the liquid for 2-2.5 hours, until tender.
  5. Remove the meat from the cooking liquid and serve with sides, such as steamed vegetables or creamy mashed potatoes.

IPA Beer Bread with Cinnamon and Brown Sugar

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup topsy IPA beer
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. Stir in the IPA beer and melted butter until the ingredients combine.
  4. Fold in the chopped rosemary, if using.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool the bread in the pan for 10 minutes before removing it from the pan and transferring it to a wire rack.
  7. Serve slices of the warm beer bread with softened butter or your favorite spread.

IPA and spice combinations offer a world of exciting flavor profiles, inviting home cooks to push the boundaries of traditional brewing and cuisine. These recipes highlight how the robust and refreshing contradictions of IPAs serve as an adaptable, versatile canvas for culinary creativity. Experiment with other spices, seasonings, and ingredients to further indulge your love for craft beer and gastronomy.
