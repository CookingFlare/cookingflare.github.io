Instant Pot Garlic Butter Shrimp: A Simple and Flavorful Dish


The Instant Pot has become a staple kitchen tool, turning complex recipes into quick, flavorful meals with minimal effort. If you’re a fan of seafood and crave a dish that’s both rich and simple to prepare, Instant Pot Garlic Butter Shrimp is the ultimate solution. Combining succulent shrimp with the golden goodness of garlic-infused butter, this recipe promises a restaurant-quality experience in the comfort of your own home. Ready in minutes, it’s perfect for weeknight dinners or to impress guests at a gathering.

Shrimp is a protein powerhouse with a naturally sweet flavor, and when paired with the aromatic, velvety notes of garlic and butter, it transforms into a culinary masterpiece that’s suitable for any occasion. In this blog, we’ll dive into everything you need to know to create this decadent dish, from selecting the best shrimp to perfecting the cooking process in your Instant Pot. Let’s get started!

Why Instant Pot Garlic Butter Shrimp?

This recipe is more than just delicious; it’s convenient. The Instant Pot reduces cooking time dramatically while preserving the texture and flavor of the ingredients. Shrimp, known for its quick cook time, pairs exceptionally well with the fast, pressure-steamed environment of the Instant Pot.

Additionally, garlic butter shrimp is a versatile dish. Serve it over pasta, rice, quinoa, or alongside roasted veggies for a complete meal. You can even enjoy it on its own as a luxurious appetizer. With simple ingredients that most home chefs already have on hand, this recipe is accessible to beginners and satisfying for seasoned cooks alike.

Choosing the Perfect Shrimp

The star of this dish is undeniably the shrimp. Selecting high-quality shrimp is essential to achieving that perfect taste and texture. Here’s a quick guide to help you choose the best option:

Fresh vs. Frozen

  • Fresh Shrimp: If you live near a coastal area, fresh shrimp is an outstanding choice. It’s typically more flavorful and has a firm texture. Just make sure to use it within a day or two of purchasing to maintain its freshness.
  • Frozen Shrimp: For most people, frozen shrimp is the more accessible option. Look for shrimp that’s flash-frozen shortly after being caught to preserve its quality. Frozen shrimp is available year-round, making this recipe feasible no matter the season.

Size Matters

Shrimp are categorized by size, such as jumbo, large, or medium. For this recipe, large shrimp (16–20 shrimp per pound) work best. They’re meaty enough to hold up against the bold flavors of garlic and butter without being overly delicate.

Peeled or Unpeeled

To save time, opt for peeled and deveined shrimp. However, if you prefer extra flavor, you can use shrimp with the shells intact, as they infuse the dish with a deeper seafood essence.

Ingredients You’ll Need

This recipe uses a handful of simple ingredients that come together to create layers of flavor. Here’s what you need:

  • Shrimp (large, peeled and deveined)
  • Garlic (minced for optimal flavor infusion)
  • Unsalted Butter (for richness and to control salt levels)
  • Olive Oil (enhances the butter and prevents burning)
  • Lemon Juice (brightens the dish and balances the richness)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)
  • Paprika (optional, for a hint of smokiness and color)
  • Fresh Parsley (chopped, as a garnish)
  • Chicken Broth or Water (for pressure cooking)

Now that you’ve got your ingredients lined up, it’s time to dive into the cooking process.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Start by preparing the shrimp. If using frozen shrimp, thaw them properly by placing them in a colander under cold running water for a few minutes. Pat the shrimp dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.

Mince the garlic finely to maximize its flavor release, and chop the parsley for garnish.

Step 2: Sauté Garlic

Begin by turning on the Instant Pot and selecting the “Sauté” function. Add olive oil and a tablespoon of butter to the pot, allowing the butter to melt completely. Toss in the minced garlic and stir until fragrant. This step ensures the garlic infuses the butter with its aroma, creating the base for your dish.

Step 3: Pressure Cooking

Once the garlic is lightly golden, add the shrimp to the Instant Pot. Season them with salt, black pepper, and paprika if desired. Pour in the chicken broth or water to provide the liquid necessary for pressure cooking.

Cancel the “Sauté” mode, then secure the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” setting, and set the timer for 1 minute at high pressure. Yes, 1 minute—shrimp cook incredibly fast, even in the Instant Pot.

Step 4: Quick Release

When the timer goes off, perform a quick release by carefully turning the steam valve to “Venting.” Once the pressure is fully released, open the lid.

Step 5: Incorporate Butter and Lemon

Switch the Instant Pot back to “Sauté” mode. Add the remaining butter and squeeze in fresh lemon juice. Stir gently until the butter has melted and fully coated the shrimp. Adjust seasoning as needed.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Sprinkle fresh parsley on top for an added layer of herbaceous flavor. Serve immediately over your choice of base: pasta, rice, or steamed vegetables.

Tips for Perfect Garlic Butter Shrimp

  1. Don’t Overcook the Shrimp
    Shrimp becomes rubbery and tough if overcooked. The Instant Pot offers precise control, but make sure to release the pressure promptly and avoid letting it sit in residual heat for too long.

  2. Butter Quality Matters
    Use high-quality unsalted butter—it’s the foundation for your sauce and greatly enhances the overall taste.

  3. Adjust to Your Preferences
    Feel free to customize this recipe. Add red pepper flakes for a touch of heat or white wine for extra depth.

Pairing Suggestions

Garlic butter shrimp pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes. Here are a few ideas to inspire your meal planning:

  • Pasta: Toss the shrimp with al dente spaghetti or linguine for a classic scampi-style experience.
  • Rice: Use fluffy white rice or fragrant jasmine rice to soak up the garlic butter sauce.
  • Vegetables: Serve alongside roasted asparagus or zucchini for a lighter option.
  • Bread: A crusty baguette or garlic bread is perfect for mopping up the sauce.
