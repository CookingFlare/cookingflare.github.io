Savory Mushroom Stroganoff Over Egg Noodles


Mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles is a delightful and satisfying dish that combines rich, savory flavors with the comforting texture of tender pasta. This recipe is perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner or a special weekend meal that will impress your family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this dish is simple to prepare and will fill your home with delicious aromas. Let’s dive into how to make this mushroom stroganoff and why it deserves a spot on your recipe list.

The Origins of Stroganoff

The origins of stroganoff can be traced back to Russia, where it was first created in the mid-19th century. Traditionally, the dish was made with beef, onions, and a creamy sauce, often served over rice or potatoes. Over time, the recipe has evolved, and variations using chicken, pork, and even mushrooms have become popular. Mushroom stroganoff, in particular, is a wonderful vegetarian alternative to the classic version, allowing the earthy flavors of mushrooms to shine in a rich, creamy sauce.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this savory mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles, you’ll need a few simple ingredients that come together to create a mouthwatering meal. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Egg noodles – The hearty, chewy texture of egg noodles makes them the perfect base for the creamy stroganoff sauce. You can also use other types of pasta, but egg noodles are the traditional choice for this dish.

  • Mushrooms – Fresh mushrooms, such as cremini, white button, or a mix of wild mushrooms, add deep, earthy flavors to the dish. The mushrooms are sautéed until golden brown, which helps enhance their natural umami flavor.

  • Onion – A medium onion adds a mild sweetness and depth of flavor when cooked down in the sauce.

  • Garlic – Fresh garlic adds a lovely aromatic element to the dish. Mince it finely to release its flavor.

  • Vegetable broth – This will serve as the base of the sauce. It helps create a savory, rich flavor without overpowering the mushrooms. If you prefer a meatier flavor, you can substitute with beef broth.

  • Sour cream – A creamy and tangy addition that gives the sauce a smooth texture and a hint of acidity to balance the richness.

  • Dijon mustard – Just a touch of Dijon mustard adds a subtle sharpness and depth of flavor to the sauce.

  • Flour – Used as a thickening agent, flour ensures the sauce reaches the perfect consistency without being too runny.

  • Butter – Used for sautéing the vegetables and adding richness to the dish.

  • Fresh parsley – For garnish, parsley adds a burst of color and a fresh herbal note that brightens up the dish.

  • Salt and pepper – To taste, these basic seasonings enhance the flavors and bring the dish together.

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s dive into how to prepare this mushroom stroganoff!

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Egg Noodles

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the egg noodles and cook according to the package instructions, usually about 7-9 minutes, until the noodles are al dente. Drain the noodles and set them aside. You can toss them with a little olive oil or butter to keep them from sticking together.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

While the noodles are cooking, heat a large skillet or sauté pan over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of butter to the pan. Once the butter has melted and starts to bubble, add the chopped onions and cook them for 3-4 minutes until they are softened and slightly caramelized.

Next, add the garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds until fragrant. Then, add the mushrooms to the pan. The mushrooms will release moisture as they cook, so continue sautéing them for 8-10 minutes, or until they are golden brown and the moisture has evaporated.

Step 3: Make the Sauce

Once the mushrooms are ready, sprinkle 2 tablespoons of flour over the mixture. Stir the flour into the mushrooms and onions, ensuring it is evenly distributed. This will help thicken the sauce as it cooks.

Now, slowly pour in the vegetable broth while stirring constantly. This will create a smooth, creamy base for the sauce. Bring the mixture to a simmer and allow it to cook for 4-5 minutes until the sauce has thickened.

Step 4: Add the Creaminess

Once the sauce has thickened, reduce the heat to low and stir in the sour cream and Dijon mustard. The sour cream adds a luscious creaminess and a tangy note that complements the earthiness of the mushrooms. The Dijon mustard provides a subtle sharpness that balances the richness of the sauce.

Stir everything together until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste. If you prefer a thinner sauce, you can add a little more vegetable broth until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 5: Combine the Noodles and Sauce

Add the cooked egg noodles directly into the pan with the mushroom sauce. Toss the noodles gently to coat them evenly with the sauce. Let everything cook together for another minute or two to allow the noodles to soak up the flavors of the sauce.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Once the noodles and sauce are well combined, remove the pan from the heat. Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the top for a pop of color and a fresh flavor. Serve the mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles immediately, and enjoy the comforting, savory goodness!

Tips for Perfect Mushroom Stroganoff

  • Use a mix of mushrooms: While white button mushrooms are delicious, you can elevate the dish by using a variety of mushrooms like cremini, shiitake, or portobello. Wild mushrooms add complexity and a deeper, earthier flavor to the sauce.

  • Adjust the creaminess: If you prefer a richer sauce, you can substitute half of the vegetable broth with heavy cream. This will give the sauce a velvety texture and a more luxurious mouthfeel.

  • Make it spicy: For a little heat, you can add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce. This will add a subtle kick without overpowering the dish.

  • Add a protein: While the dish is delicious on its own, you can add protein if you’d like. Grilled chicken, beef, or tofu would pair wonderfully with the mushroom stroganoff.

  • Make it ahead: Mushroom stroganoff is a great make-ahead meal. You can prepare the sauce a day or two in advance and store it in the refrigerator. When you’re ready to eat, just reheat the sauce and toss with freshly cooked egg noodles.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

There are several reasons why mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles is a dish worth making again and again. The combination of earthy mushrooms, rich sour cream, and savory broth creates a comforting sauce that perfectly complements the soft, chewy egg noodles. Whether you’re a mushroom lover or just looking for a hearty vegetarian meal, this dish is sure to please.

Additionally, this recipe is incredibly versatile. You can adjust the level of creaminess, add your favorite herbs, or include a protein to suit your taste. It’s also an ideal meal for any season, as mushrooms are available year-round, and the rich, creamy sauce is perfect for both cold winter nights and cozy summer dinners.

The Perfect Pairings

Mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles is a filling meal on its own, but it can be complemented with a few simple side dishes to round out your dinner. Here are some great ideas for sides to serve with this dish:

  • A fresh green salad: The crispness of greens like arugula, spinach, or mixed lettuce pairs beautifully with the creamy richness of the stroganoff. Add a simple vinaigrette for balance.

  • Roasted vegetables: Roasted Brussels sprouts, carrots, or broccoli are excellent choices. Their natural sweetness and slight char add a wonderful contrast to the savory dish.

  • Crusty bread: Serve with a warm loaf of sourdough or baguette to soak up the delicious mushroom sauce.

  • Steamed asparagus: Lightly steamed asparagus with a squeeze of lemon adds a refreshing touch and a bit of color to your plate.

Final Thoughts

Mushroom stroganoff over egg noodles is the epitome of comfort food. With its creamy, savory sauce, hearty mushrooms, and tender noodles, it’s a dish that brings warmth and satisfaction with every bite. Whether you’re enjoying it on a cozy evening at home or serving it to guests, this dish is sure to become a new favorite. With easy-to-find ingredients and simple steps, this recipe is perfect for anyone looking to enjoy a flavorful, comforting meal. Give it a try and let the savory goodness fill your kitchen and your heart!
