Tender and Juicy Herb-Roasted Beef Tenderloin Recipe for a Showstopping Thanksgiving

3 min · 567 words

Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in a feast fit for royalty. While turkey often takes center stage on this special occasion, why not switch things up this year and surprise your guests with a showstopping Herb-Roasted Beef Tenderloin? This mouthwatering recipe will leave everyone in awe of your culinary skills and craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef tenderloin, approximately 4 to 5 pounds
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, parsley, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful herb paste.

  3. Place the beef tenderloin on a cutting board and pat it dry with paper towels. This step is crucial to ensure a crispy exterior.

  4. Rub the herb paste all over the beef tenderloin, making sure to cover all sides evenly. The aromatic blend of herbs will infuse the meat with incredible flavors.

  5. Transfer the herb-coated beef tenderloin to a roasting pan and insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the meat. This will help you monitor the cooking process and ensure the perfect level of doneness.

  6. Roast the beef tenderloin in the preheated oven for approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the internal temperature reaches your desired level of doneness. For medium-rare, aim for an internal temperature of 135°F (57°C), while medium requires a temperature of around 145°F (63°C).

  7. Once the beef tenderloin has reached the desired temperature, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a tender and juicy roast.

  8. After the resting period, use a sharp carving knife to slice the beef tenderloin into thick, succulent pieces. Arrange the slices on a platter and serve them alongside your favorite Thanksgiving sides, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or cranberry sauce.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider marinating the beef tenderloin in the herb paste for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator. This will allow the flavors to penetrate the meat even more deeply.

  • If you prefer a crusty exterior, sear the herb-coated beef tenderloin in a hot skillet before transferring it to the oven. This will create a beautiful brown crust that adds both texture and taste.

  • Don’t forget to adjust the cooking time depending on the size of your beef tenderloin. A larger roast will require more time in the oven, while a smaller one will cook faster.

  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste preferences. Rosemary, thyme, and parsley work wonderfully together, but you can also add a touch of oregano, sage, or even a sprinkle of chili flakes for some heat.

Conclusion

This Herb-Roasted Beef Tenderloin recipe is guaranteed to be the star of your Thanksgiving table. Its tender and juicy texture, combined with the aromatic blend of herbs, will leave your guests impressed and craving for seconds. So, go ahead and embrace this showstopping alternative to the traditional turkey, and create a memorable Thanksgiving feast that will have everyone asking for your recipe. Happy cooking and happy holidays!
