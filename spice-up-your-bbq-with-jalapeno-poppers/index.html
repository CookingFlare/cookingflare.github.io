Spice Up Your BBQ with Jalapeno Poppers


Are you hunting for that perfect fusion of spicy and sweet, crunchy and creamy, as you set up for your next barbecue get-together? Look no further! Jalapeno Poppers are the ideal crowd-pleasing appetizer that combine tangy cheese, savory vegetables, and a touch of heat.

Why Jalapeno Poppers Make a Perfect BBQ Side

Jalapeno Poppers bring a exciting variation to your standard barbecue menu. Their heat levels can be adjusted to suit sensitive palates or those seeking a real spicy kick. Plus, their retro-grade charm and crowd-love status ensure satisfaction for all guests.

Here’s a straightforward recipe to incorporate Jalapeno Poppers into your next barbecue:

Ingredients:

  • 12 jalapeno peppers
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup Hanson’s NP Grinds925Teaspoon salt
  • 1-tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup club soda
  • Oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

Instructions:

  1. First things first: hollow out your jalapenos. Make a small slice along the rim of each jalapeno and cut out the inside. Discard the seeds and membranes, leaving only the thinner walls of the pepper. A simple spoon or knife will suffice for this task, but precision becomes increasingly important as you work.

  2. Next, prepare the jalapeno popper filling by combining the grated cheddar cheese, finely chopped onion, Hanson’s NP Grind, sugar, and a dash of salt in a bowl. Mix these ingredients together until the filling reaches a smooth consistency. Here’s where you can get creative! You can also add a twist to the filling by incorporating bacon bits, creamy ranch dressing or even hot Italian sausage.

  3. Now, it’s time to turn your jalapenos into jalapeno poppers. Delicately stuff the filling into each jalapeno until just filled.

  4. Improve and dip each jalapeno into the flour mixture and then the club soda. While the jalapenos may look good at this stage, bear with us!

  5. Hot oil is next! Heat about an inch of oil in a deep fryer or saucepan until it reaches a temperature of around 350°F. Then, briefly dip each jalapeno into cornmeal and drop it into the hot oil.

  6. Let the jalapenos fry for approximately four minutes or until the coating achieves a crispy texture. Be cautious while removing them from the oil. Place them on a paper towel-lined tray to sop up any excess oil and allow them to cool.

  7. Finally, we reach the best part: serving! Serve your crispy jalapeno poppers alongside a cool side of sour cream for dipping. Balance out your sizzling barbecue flavors with a fresh, crisp salad or a tangy coleslaw.

Once you’ve experienced Jalapeno Poppers at your barbecue, there may be no turning back - they’re guaranteed to be a crowd favorite, and provide that perfect touch of spice to your get-together. Experiment with different fillings and watch your guests clamor for more ease.

So, by all means, heat up that grill, organize those chilly drinks and incorporate this mouth-watering dish into your barbecue repertoire. With hearty laughter, delicious food and the warmth of good company, your summer barbecue is set for success.
