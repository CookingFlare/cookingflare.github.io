Flavorful Red Pork Tamales: A Mexican Holiday Tradition


Tamales, a staple of Mexican cuisine, are a beloved holiday tradition in many Latin American countries. Among the numerous types of tamales, the flavorful red pork tamale stands out as a crowd-pleaser. This recipe combines the rich flavors of slow-cooked pork with the vibrant color of ancho chile sauce, creating a dish that is both comforting and festive.

Ingredients

For the pork filling:

  • 2 pounds boneless pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups ancho chile sauce (see below for recipe)

For the ancho chile sauce:

  • 4 ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the tamales:

  • 2 cups masa harina
  • 1/2 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Corn husks (fresh or dried)

Instructions

Prepare the Pork Filling

  1. In a large Dutch oven, heat the lard or oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the pork, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the pork is browned on all sides, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and shreds easily with a fork.
  4. Stir in the ancho chile sauce and let it simmer for an additional 30 minutes.

Prepare the Ancho Chile Sauce

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the ancho chilies, chicken broth, lard or oil, garlic, and cumin. Blend until smooth.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assemble the Tamales

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the masa harina, lard or oil, and chicken broth. Mix until a dough forms, adding more broth as needed to achieve a smooth consistency.
  2. Knead the dough for 5-10 minutes, until it is pliable and easy to work with.
  3. Soak the corn husks in water for at least 30 minutes. This will make them pliable and easier to work with.
  4. To assemble the tamales, lay a corn husk flat on a work surface. Take a small piece of the dough and flatten it into a disk shape.
  5. Place a tablespoon or two of the pork filling in the center of the dough. Fold the sides of the corn husk over the filling, and then roll the tamale up, starting from the wide end.
  6. Repeat with the remaining ingredients.

Steam the Tamales

  1. Place a steamer basket in a large pot with a lid. Add enough water to the pot to come up to the bottom of the basket.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.
  3. Place the tamales in the steamer basket, with the folded side down.
  4. Cover the pot and steam the tamales for 1-2 hours, or until the dough is firm and the filling is hot.

Tips and Variations

  • To add extra flavor to the tamales, you can add a sprinkle of queso fresco or crumbled cotija cheese on top of the filling before steaming.
  • For a spicy kick, you can add diced jalapeños or serrano peppers to the pork filling.
  • To make the tamales more festive, you can add a few drops of orange or lemon zest to the dough for a bright, citrusy flavor.

Conclusion

Flavorful red pork tamales are a true delight, combining the rich flavors of slow-cooked pork with the vibrant color of ancho chile sauce. With this recipe, you can create a dish that is both comforting and festive, perfect for holiday gatherings and special occasions. So gather your family and friends, and get ready to indulge in the warm, comforting goodness of these delicious tamales.
