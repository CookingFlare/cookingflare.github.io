Wine and Dine: 5 Red Wine-Infused Marinades for Steaks


There’s something truly special about the combination of red wine and steak. The rich flavors of the wine, paired with the tender, juicy meat, create a culinary experience that is hard to beat. If you’re looking to take your steak game to the next level, why not try infusing your marinades with red wine? Not only will it add a depth of flavor to your steaks, but it will also tenderize the meat, making it even more delicious. Here are five red wine-infused marinades that will elevate your steak dinners to new heights.

1. Classic Cabernet Marinade

The classic cabernet marinade is a tried and true favorite. It combines the bold flavors of Cabernet Sauvignon with garlic, soy sauce, and Worcestershire sauce for a marinade that is both savory and robust. To make this marinade, simply mix together half a cup of Cabernet Sauvignon, three cloves of minced garlic, two tablespoons of soy sauce, and one tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce. Pour the marinade over your steaks and let them marinate for at least four hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.

2. Zesty Merlot Marinade

For a tangy twist on your steak marinade, try using a zesty Merlot. This marinade combines the fruity notes of Merlot with the freshness of lemon and the kick of crushed red pepper flakes. To make the zesty Merlot marinade, combine half a cup of Merlot, the juice of one lemon, one teaspoon of crushed red pepper flakes, and a pinch of salt. Let your steaks marinate in this flavorful mixture for at least two hours before grilling.

3. Bold Malbec Marinade

If you’re a fan of bold flavors, the Malbec marinade is the one for you. This marinade combines the deep, dark fruit flavors of Malbec with the smokiness of paprika and the warmth of ground cumin. To make the bold Malbec marinade, mix together half a cup of Malbec, one tablespoon of paprika, one tablespoon of ground cumin, and a pinch of salt. Let your steaks marinate in this bold mixture for at least one hour before cooking.

4. Herbaceous Pinot Noir Marinade

For a more herbaceous flavor profile, try using a Pinot Noir-based marinade. This marinade combines the earthy flavors of Pinot Noir with the freshness of herbs like rosemary and thyme. To make the herbaceous Pinot Noir marinade, combine half a cup of Pinot Noir, two tablespoons of olive oil, two cloves of minced garlic, one tablespoon of fresh rosemary, one tablespoon of fresh thyme, and a pinch of salt. Let your steaks marinate in this fragrant mixture for at least three hours before grilling.

5. Sweet and Spicy Shiraz Marinade

For a marinade with a kick, the sweet and spicy Shiraz marinade is the way to go. This marinade combines the sweet flavors of Shiraz with the heat of chili powder and the sweetness of honey. To make the sweet and spicy Shiraz marinade, mix together half a cup of Shiraz, two tablespoons of honey, one tablespoon of chili powder, and a pinch of salt. Let your steaks marinate in this flavorful concoction for at least four hours before cooking.

Whether you prefer a classic cabernet or a sweet and spicy Shiraz, these red wine-infused marinades are sure to take your steak dinners to a whole new level. Experiment with different wines and flavors to find the marinade that suits your taste buds. So, next time you fire up the grill, don’t forget to wine and dine your steaks with these delicious red wine-infused marinades. Cheers to a meal that is sure to impress!
