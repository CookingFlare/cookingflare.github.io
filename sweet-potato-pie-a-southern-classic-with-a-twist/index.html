Sweet Potato Pie: A Southern Classic with a Twist


The sweet potato pie is a beloved Southern dessert that has been enjoyed for generations. With its rich, creamy filling and flaky crust, this pie is the perfect combination of sweet and savory. But what if we told you that there’s a way to take this classic recipe to the next level? That’s right, we’re going to add a twist to the traditional sweet potato pie that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

The Secret Ingredient: Pecans

To give our sweet potato pie a unique twist, we’re going to incorporate the flavors of another Southern favorite: pecans. These crunchy and nutty gems will not only add a delightful texture to the pie but also enhance its overall taste.

The Crust: A Buttery Delight

No pie is complete without a buttery and flaky crust. To make the perfect crust for our sweet potato pie, you’ll need:

  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 4-6 tablespoons of ice-cold water

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together.

Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into a disk. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Once chilled, roll out the dough into a circle large enough to fit your pie dish. Carefully place the dough into the dish and trim any excess. Set aside.

The Filling: A Symphony of Flavors

Now it’s time to prepare the star of our sweet potato pie: the filling. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of cooked and mashed sweet potatoes
  • ¾ cup of granulated sugar
  • ½ cup of packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup of heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon of ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of chopped pecans

In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, granulated sugar, brown sugar, heavy cream, eggs, melted butter, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

Next, fold in the chopped pecans, reserving a handful for garnish. Pour the sweet potato filling into the prepared pie crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Sprinkle the reserved pecans on top for an extra touch of crunch.

The Baking Process: A Time for Patience

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and place the pie on the middle rack. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. To test for doneness, gently jiggle the pie. If the center is still wobbly, bake for a few more minutes.

Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. This will allow the filling to set and the flavors to meld together.

Serving Suggestions: A Slice of Heaven

Now that your sweet potato pie with a twist is ready, it’s time to indulge in a slice of heaven. Serve it at room temperature or slightly chilled, and feel free to top it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra touch of decadence.

This delightful dessert is perfect for any occasion, from family gatherings to holiday feasts. Its unique combination of flavors and textures will have everyone craving seconds.

So, why stick to the traditional sweet potato pie when you can elevate it with the addition of crunchy pecans? Give this Southern classic with a twist a try, and let your taste buds experience a whole new level of deliciousness. Happy baking!
