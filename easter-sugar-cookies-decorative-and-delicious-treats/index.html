Easter Sugar Cookies: Decorative and Delicious Treats


Easter is a time for celebration and indulgence, and what better way to do so than with a classic Easter treat: sugar cookies. These sweet and colorful treats are a staple of the holiday season, and with a few simple ingredients and some creative decorating, you can create a batch of Easter sugar cookies that will impress even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Colored sugar or sprinkles for decorating

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. Divide the dough into two equal parts, and on a lightly floured surface, roll out each half to a thickness of about 1/4 inch.
  7. Cut out desired shapes using a cookie cutter.
  8. Place the cookies on the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch of space between each cookie.
  9. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  10. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Decorating

  1. Once the cookies are completely cool, it’s time to get creative with the decorating. You can use colored sugar or sprinkles to add a pop of color to your cookies.
  2. To make a simple glaze, mix together 1 cup powdered sugar and 2 tbsp milk until smooth. Add a few drops of food coloring to tint the glaze.
  3. Dip the top of each cookie into the glaze, or use a spoon to drizzle the glaze over the top of the cookie.
  4. Add sprinkles or colored sugar to the top of the cookie for extra decoration.
  5. Allow the glaze to set before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • To add a little extra flavor to your cookies, try adding a teaspoon of lemon zest or a pinch of salt to the dough.
  • For a different texture, try using brown sugar instead of granulated sugar.
  • To make the cookies more festive, try using Easter-themed cookie cutters or adding a few drops of food coloring to the dough to create a pastel color.
  • If you’re short on time, you can also decorate the cookies with a simple powdered sugar glaze and a sprinkle of colored sugar.

Conclusion

Easter sugar cookies are a classic treat that is sure to impress. With a few simple ingredients and some creative decorating, you can create a batch of cookies that are both delicious and beautiful. Whether you’re making them for a special occasion or just as a sweet treat for the family, these cookies are sure to be a hit. So go ahead, get creative, and make some Easter sugar cookies that will be remembered for years to come.
