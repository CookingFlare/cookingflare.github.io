Making Pies with Kids: Fun Recipes for Family Time


Pie-making is one of the most delightful culinary adventures you can embark on with your children. The act of combining ingredients, rolling out dough, and shaping crusts not only ignites creativity but also creates lasting memories. Whether it’s a sweet dessert pie or a savory one, making pies together can be an enriching experience that teaches kids about baking while fostering family bonds. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of easy and fun pie recipes to make with your kids, as well as tips for a successful pie-making experience.

The Joy of Pie-Making

Before diving into recipes, let’s discuss the numerous benefits of baking pies with your kids. Cooking together encourages teamwork, boosts confidence, and enhances fine motor skills. Kids get the chance to measure ingredients, mix them, and see the transformation of simple components into a delicious treat. Plus, who can resist the delicious aroma of freshly baked pie wafting through the house?

Essential Tools for Pie-Making

To ensure a smooth pie-making experience, gather the following tools:

  • Mixing bowls: Various sizes for mixing ingredients.
  • Measuring cups and spoons: Accurate measurements are key in baking.
  • Rolling pin: Perfect for rolling out dough.
  • Pie dish: A standard 9-inch pie dish works well for most recipes.
  • Pastry brush: Great for applying egg wash or glaze.
  • Cutting board: A safe surface for chopping fruits or vegetables.

Tips for a Successful Pie-Making Session

  1. Choose the Right Time: Pick a day when you have plenty of time to bake without feeling rushed. Weekends or school holidays are perfect.

  2. Prep Together: Involve your kids in the prep work. They can wash fruits, measure ingredients, and even help with the rolling.

  3. Make It Fun: Play music, chat, and joke around while cooking. The more relaxed and enjoyable the atmosphere, the more your kids will want to participate.

  4. Embrace Messiness: Baking can get messy, and that’s part of the fun. Encourage your kids to enjoy the process, even if it means flour on the floor!

  5. Taste Test: Allow your kids to taste the filling before it goes into the pie. This not only makes them feel involved but also teaches them about flavors.

Fun Pie Recipes to Try with Kids

Here are some delightful pie recipes that are perfect for a fun family baking day. Each recipe includes simple steps that kids can easily follow, ensuring that everyone has a hand in the process.

1. Classic Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups sliced apples (Granny Smith and Honeycrisp work well)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts (or homemade crust)
  • Egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, mix sliced apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Let it sit for 10 minutes.

  3. Roll Out the Crust: Unroll one pie crust and fit it into the pie dish. Let the kids help press it into the edges.

  4. Fill the Pie: Pour the apple mixture into the crust, making sure it’s evenly distributed.

  5. Top with Crust: Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Seal the edges by crimping with a fork or your fingers. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  6. Brush with Egg Wash: Use a pastry brush to apply the egg wash on top for a golden finish.

  7. Bake: Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the crust is golden brown. Let it cool before serving.

2. Berry Burst Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts
  • Whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, combine the mixed berries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Gently toss to mix.

  3. Assemble the Pie: Place one pie crust in the pie dish. Fill it with the berry mixture.

  4. Top with Crust: Roll out the second pie crust, cut it into strips, and create a lattice pattern over the filling. Seal the edges.

  5. Bake: Bake for about 30-35 minutes or until the berries are bubbling and the crust is golden.

  6. Serve: Allow to cool slightly and serve with a dollop of whipped cream.

3. Mini Chicken Pot Pies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Make the Filling: In a saucepan, combine chicken, mixed vegetables, chicken broth, milk, flour, and garlic powder. Cook on medium heat until thickened.

  3. Prepare the Crusts: Roll out pie crusts and cut them into circles large enough to fit into a muffin tin.

  4. Assemble Mini Pies: Press the dough circles into the muffin tin, fill with the chicken mixture, and top with smaller dough circles.

  5. Bake: Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the crust is golden brown.

  6. Cool and Serve: Allow to cool for a few minutes before removing from the tin.

4. Chocolate Cream Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup chocolate pudding mix
  • 2 cups milk
  • Chocolate shavings for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, whisk together chocolate pudding mix and milk until thickened. Pour into the pre-baked pie crust.

  2. Make Whipped Cream: In a separate bowl, beat heavy whipping cream and powdered sugar until soft peaks form.

  3. Top the Pie: Spread the whipped cream over the chocolate filling.

  4. Garnish: Sprinkle chocolate shavings on top for decoration.

  5. Chill: Refrigerate for a few hours before serving.

Conclusion

Baking pies with your kids can be a joyful and rewarding family experience. Not only do you create delicious treats, but you also foster creativity, teamwork, and cherished memories that will last a lifetime. With these fun and easy pie recipes, you can embark on a delightful baking adventure that involves everyone in the kitchen. So grab your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start making some sweet (and savory) family memories today!
