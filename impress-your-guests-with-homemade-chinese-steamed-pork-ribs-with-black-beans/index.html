Impress Your Guests with Homemade Chinese Steamed Pork Ribs with Black Beans


Chinese cuisine is known for its rich flavors and aromatic dishes, and one classic dish that never fails to impress is Chinese steamed pork ribs with black beans. This savory and tender dish is a favorite at many Chinese restaurants, but did you know that you can easily recreate it at home? Impress your guests with this homemade delicacy that’s bursting with umami goodness and succulent pork flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork ribs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons fermented black beans
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1/4 cup chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork Ribs: Start by rinsing the pork ribs under cold water and patting them dry with paper towels. If the ribs are large, you can cut them into smaller, bite-sized pieces for easier steaming.

  2. Marinate the Ribs: In a large bowl, combine the pork ribs with the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, cornstarch, sesame oil, and ground white pepper. Mix well to ensure that the ribs are evenly coated with the marinade. Let the ribs marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  3. Soak the Black Beans: Rinse the fermented black beans under running water to remove excess salt. Then, soak the black beans in warm water for about 10 minutes to soften them slightly.

  4. Prepare the Steaming Setup: Fill a wok or a large pot with water and bring it to a simmer. Place a steaming rack or a heatproof plate inside the wok to hold the ribs.

  5. Assemble the Dish: Once the ribs have marinated, arrange them in a heatproof dish suitable for steaming. Sprinkle the minced garlic, ginger, and soaked black beans over the ribs. Pour the chicken stock and Shaoxing wine (if using) around the edges of the dish.

  6. Steam the Ribs: Carefully place the dish onto the steaming rack in the wok, ensuring that the water does not touch the bottom of the dish. Cover the wok with a lid and steam the ribs over medium heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the pork is tender and cooked through.

  7. Garnish and Serve: Once the ribs are done, sprinkle the chopped green onions over the top for a pop of color and freshness. Serve the steamed pork ribs hot, accompanied by steamed rice or your favorite side dishes.

Tips for Perfect Steamed Pork Ribs

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can add a few slices of red chili or a sprinkle of chili flakes for a touch of heat.
  • If you prefer a more intense black bean flavor, you can mash some of the soaked black beans and mix them into the marinade for the pork ribs.

Final Thoughts

Impress your guests with this homemade Chinese steamed pork ribs with black beans, and watch as they savor every tender, flavorful bite. This dish is a testament to the beauty of Chinese cuisine, and with a few simple ingredients and a steaming setup, you can recreate the magic in your own kitchen. So, the next time you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of authentic Chinese flavors, give this recipe a try and let the aromas of black beans and succulent pork take center stage on your dining table.
