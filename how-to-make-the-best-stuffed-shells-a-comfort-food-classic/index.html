How to Make the Best Stuffed Shells: A Comfort Food Classic


Stuffed shells are a beloved comfort food that brings warmth and joy to the dinner table. This classic Italian-American dish features large pasta shells filled with a rich and creamy mixture, topped with marinara sauce and melted cheese. Whether you’re preparing a family meal, hosting a gathering, or simply craving a hearty dish, stuffed shells are sure to satisfy. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of making the best stuffed shells, from selecting the right ingredients to perfecting the cooking technique.

The History of Stuffed Shells

Stuffed shells, or “conchiglie ripiene,” have their roots in Italian cuisine, where pasta is often filled with various ingredients. The dish gained popularity in the United States during the mid-20th century, particularly in Italian-American communities. It became a staple in many households, celebrated for its versatility and comforting flavors. Today, stuffed shells are enjoyed by people of all backgrounds, making it a true comfort food classic.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create the best stuffed shells, you’ll need a selection of fresh and flavorful ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pasta Shells

  • Jumbo pasta shells: These large shells are perfect for stuffing and hold the filling well.

For the Filling

  • Ricotta cheese: A creamy base that adds richness to the filling.
  • Mozzarella cheese: Shredded mozzarella provides a gooey texture and flavor.
  • Parmesan cheese: Grated Parmesan adds a nutty, salty flavor to the filling.
  • Egg: Helps bind the filling together.
  • Fresh herbs: Basil and parsley add freshness and depth of flavor.
  • Salt and pepper: Essential for seasoning.

For the Sauce

  • Marinara sauce: You can use store-bought or homemade sauce for a rich, tomato-based flavor.
  • Garlic: Fresh minced garlic enhances the sauce’s flavor.
  • Olive oil: For sautéing the garlic and adding richness to the sauce.
  • Red pepper flakes: Optional, for a touch of heat.

For Topping

  • Additional mozzarella cheese: For that perfect cheesy topping.
  • Fresh basil: For garnish and added flavor.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pasta Shells

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the jumbo pasta shells and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Be careful not to overcook them, as they will continue to cook in the oven. Drain the shells and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, half of the shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, egg, chopped basil, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. The filling should be creamy and flavorful.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

  1. Sauté the Garlic: In a saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.

  2. Add the Marinara Sauce: Pour in the marinara sauce and stir to combine. If you like a bit of heat, add red pepper flakes at this stage. Let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 4: Assemble the Stuffed Shells

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Spread Sauce in the Baking Dish: Pour a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. This will prevent the shells from sticking.

  3. Stuff the Shells: Using a spoon or a piping bag, carefully fill each cooked pasta shell with the ricotta mixture. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish, open side up.

  4. Top with Sauce: Once all the shells are stuffed, pour the remaining marinara sauce over the top. Make sure to cover all the shells to keep them moist during baking.

  5. Add Cheese: Sprinkle the remaining shredded mozzarella cheese over the top of the sauce.

Step 5: Bake the Stuffed Shells

  1. Cover and Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. This will help steam the shells and keep them moist.

  2. Uncover and Brown: After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  3. Garnish and Serve: Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
