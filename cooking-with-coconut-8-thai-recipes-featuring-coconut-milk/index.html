Cooking with Coconut: 8 Thai Recipes Featuring Coconut Milk


There’s something magical about coconut milk. Its rich, creamy texture and subtly sweet flavor add depth and complexity to dishes, making it one of the most cherished ingredients in Thai cuisine. Whether you’re an adventurous cook looking to explore new flavors or simply curious about Thai food, incorporating coconut milk into your recipes is a fantastic way to experience the vibrant culinary traditions of Thailand. From fragrant curries to decadent desserts, coconut milk can elevate your cooking in unexpected ways.

In this post, we’ll walk through eight mouthwatering Thai recipes that showcase the versatility and charm of coconut milk. Grab your apron, ignite your senses, and let’s bring the taste of Thailand into your kitchen!

1. Thai Green Curry

Thai Green Curry is a quintessential dish that highlights the warmth and creaminess of coconut milk while delivering a spicy kick. Traditionally made with chicken or shrimp, this curry is a perfect marriage of bold flavors and comforting textures.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken or shrimp
  • 2 tbsp Thai green curry paste
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup Thai eggplants, quartered
  • ½ cup bamboo shoots
  • Handful of Thai basil leaves
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Cooking oil (as needed)

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the curry paste until aromatic.
  2. Add coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  3. Stir in chicken or shrimp and cook until tender.
  4. Add fish sauce, brown sugar, eggplants, and bamboo shoots. Let everything simmer until the vegetables are soft.
  5. Toss in Thai basil leaves and squeeze lime juice over the curry.
  6. Serve hot with jasmine rice.

2. Tom Kha Gai (Coconut Chicken Soup)

A beloved Thai soup, Tom Kha Gai is known for its creamy coconut broth infused with fragrant ingredients like lemongrass, galangal, and lime.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 4 stalks lemongrass, sliced
  • 3 slices galangal or ginger
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 cup mushrooms (e.g., oyster, shiitake)
  • Fresh cilantro

Method:

  1. Boil chicken broth, lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  3. Add chicken slices and mushrooms, cooking until tender.
  4. Season with fish sauce and lime juice, adjusting to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.

3. Massaman Curry

Massaman Curry combines the creamy richness of coconut milk with the earthy, warming spices of cardamom and cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef or lamb
  • 2 tbsp Massaman curry paste
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup diced potatoes
  • 1 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce

Method:

  1. Sear beef or lamb in oil until browned.
  2. Add curry paste and cook until fragrant.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and simmer gently.
  4. Add potatoes and cook until tender.
  5. Mix in peanuts, tamarind paste, sugar, and fish sauce. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  6. Serve with steamed rice or roti.

4. Thai Coconut Iced Coffee

Coconut milk isn’t reserved just for savory dishes—it makes an incredible addition to drinks. This Thai-style iced coffee is refreshing and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brewed coffee
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • Ice cubes

Method:

  1. Brew your coffee and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Combine coconut milk and condensed milk, stirring to blend.
  3. Add ice to a glass, pour in the coffee, and top with the coconut milk mixture.
  4. Stir well and enjoy your refreshing iced coffee.

5. Thai Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niew Mamuang)

A Thai dessert classic, Mango Sticky Rice is a celebration of coconut milk’s sweet side. When paired with ripe mango and glutinous rice, this dish becomes an unforgettable treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, sliced

Method:

  1. Soak rice in water for several hours, then steam until cooked.
  2. In a pan, heat coconut milk, sugar, and salt until dissolved.
  3. Stir the warm coconut mixture into the steamed rice and let it absorb.
  4. Serve sticky rice alongside sliced mangoes, drizzling more coconut milk on top.

6. Thai Coconut Pancakes (Kanom Krok)

These bite-sized street food favorites are creamy on the inside and crispy on the outside, with coconut milk as the hero ingredient.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • ½ cup water
  • ½ cup sugar
  • Green onions or sweet toppings (optional)

Method:

  1. Mix coconut milk, rice flour, and water until smooth.
  2. Heat a kanom krok pan or regular skillet and pour in batter.
  3. Sprinkle green onions or sweet toppings over the pancakes.
  4. Cook until crispy on the edges and creamy in the center. Serve warm.

7. Thai Coconut Grilled Shrimp

Simple yet flavorful, this dish brings out the natural sweetness of shrimp while adding a creamy coconut twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Method:

  1. Marinate shrimp in coconut milk, fish sauce, lime juice, and garlic for 20 minutes.
  2. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  3. Grill over medium heat until cooked, turning occasionally for even charring.
  4. Serve with a side of Thai chili sauce.

8. Coconut Milk Ice Cream

End your Thai feast with homemade coconut milk ice cream—rich, refreshing, and entirely dairy-free!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (13.5 oz each) coconut milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Whisk coconut milk, sugar, and vanilla until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Pour into an ice cream maker and churn until thick.
  3. Freeze for at least 4 hours before serving. Add toppings like roasted coconut flakes or fresh fruits.

Thai cuisine’s love affair with coconut milk is something worth celebrating. Whether you’re savoring a spicy curry, sipping a refreshing drink, or indulging in a luscious dessert, these recipes offer a glimpse of Thailand’s rich culinary culture. Coconut milk’s versatility allows it to shine in every dish, bringing a distinct flavor and texture that’s utterly irresistible.

Incorporating these recipes into your repertoire not only adds variety but also introduces you to an essential aspect of Thai cooking. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and immerse yourself in the magic of cooking with coconut milk—it’s time to create something delicious!
