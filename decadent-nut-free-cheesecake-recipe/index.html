Decadent Nut-Free Cheesecake Recipe

5 min · 886 words

Are you a cheesecake lover, but have a nut allergy that prevents you from enjoying this delectable dessert? Fear not, because I have the perfect solution for you - a decadent nut-free cheesecake recipe that will satisfy your cheesecake cravings without compromising on flavor and texture. This recipe is not only delicious but also simple to make, making it a great option for any occasion. So, let’s dive into the details and get ready to indulge in this delightful treat.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1.5 cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt

For the Topping (Optional):

  • Fresh berries
  • Whipped cream

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until well combined.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, ensuring an even layer on the bottom and slightly up the sides.
  4. Place the pan in the oven and bake the crust for 10 minutes. Once done, remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the sour cream, heavy cream, eggs, vanilla extract, flour, and salt. Mix until everything is well combined and smooth.
  3. Pour the filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan.

Step 3: Baking the Cheesecake

  1. Place the springform pan on a baking sheet and then into the preheated oven.
  2. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center is still slightly jiggly.
  3. Once baked, turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven with the door slightly ajar for about an hour.
  4. Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to set completely.

Step 4: Adding the Topping (Optional)

  1. Once the cheesecake is set, you can add a topping of fresh berries or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra touch of flavor and presentation.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the cheesecake is fully chilled and set, carefully remove the sides of the springform pan. Slice the cheesecake into portions and serve it to your delighted guests or simply indulge in a slice yourself. This nut-free cheesecake is a creamy, indulgent delight that will leave everyone craving for more.

Conclusion

This nut-free cheesecake recipe is a game-changer for those with nut allergies who want to savor the rich and creamy goodness of a classic cheesecake. It’s a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed at any time of the year, and its simple preparation makes it an ideal choice for both novice and experienced bakers. So, go ahead and treat yourself and your loved ones to this heavenly dessert, and revel in the joy of a perfectly crafted nut-free cheesecake.
