Homemade Gnocchi for Two: Pillowy and Delicious


There’s something undeniably satisfying about making your own pasta from scratch, and homemade gnocchi is no exception. This Italian classic is a true labor of love, resulting in pillowy soft dumplings that are hard to resist. Whether you’re looking for a special date night dish or simply want to impress a loved one, homemade gnocchi for two is the perfect choice. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the process of making this delectable dish step by step. So, roll up your sleeves, tie on your apron, and let’s get started!

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound (about 2 large) Russet potatoes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

  • Start by washing the potatoes and poking them all over with a fork. This will allow steam to escape while they cook.
  • Place the potatoes on a baking sheet and bake them in a preheated 400°F oven for about 45-60 minutes, or until they are completely tender. You can also boil the potatoes, but baking them helps to keep the flesh dry, resulting in lighter gnocchi.

Step 2: Mash the Potatoes

  • Once the potatoes are cooked, let them cool for a few minutes, then peel off the skins. Using a potato ricer or a fork, mash the potatoes until no lumps remain.

Step 3: Add the Egg and Flour

  • Create a well in the center of the mashed potatoes and add the beaten egg and a pinch of salt.
  • Gradually add the flour, starting with 3/4 cup, and mix until a soft dough forms. Add more flour as needed, but be careful not to overwork the dough. It should be soft and slightly sticky.

Step 4: Shape the Gnocchi

  • Divide the dough into 4 equal parts and roll each part into a long rope, about 1/2 inch thick.
  • Using a sharp knife, cut the ropes into 3/4-inch pieces. If desired, you can use a fork to create ridges on the gnocchi, which will help them hold sauce later on.

Step 5: Cook the Gnocchi

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the gnocchi into the boiling water and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Use a slotted spoon to transfer them to a plate.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  • At this point, the gnocchi is ready to be enjoyed! You can toss them with your favorite sauce, such as a simple tomato basil, brown butter and sage, or creamy Alfredo. Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped herbs for an extra touch of flavor.

Tips for Success:

  • Use starchy potatoes like Russets for the best texture.
  • Be gentle when mixing the dough to avoid tough gnocchi.
  • Dust the work surface lightly with flour to prevent sticking while rolling the dough.

There you have it – a simple yet incredibly satisfying recipe for homemade gnocchi for two. The effort put into making these delicate dumplings is well worth it once you take that first bite. So, if you’re looking to create a memorable meal with your loved one, consider trying your hand at homemade gnocchi. The process is as rewarding as the end result, and the satisfaction of enjoying a dish made entirely from scratch is truly unparalleled. Happy cooking!
