Vegetarian Middle Eastern Recipes That Everyone Will Love


Middle Eastern cuisine is a celebration of bold flavors, vibrant spices, and healthful ingredients. With its rich heritage and versatile dishes, it offers a treasure trove of vegetarian recipes that are both satisfying and deeply nourishing. Whether you’re looking to expand your weekly meal rotation or impress guests at a dinner gathering, Middle Eastern vegetarian fare has plenty to offer. From creamy dips to hearty mains and sweet treats, let’s dive into a selection of vegetarian recipes that bring the rich culinary traditions of the Middle East into your kitchen.

1. Creamy Hummus With Paprika Oil Drizzle

Hummus is a classic Middle Eastern staple that transcends boundaries. This simple, protein-packed chickpea spread is as versatile as it is delicious and serves as the perfect appetizer with pita bread or vegetable sticks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas (or 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas)
  • 3 tbsp tahini (sesame paste)
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • Warm pita or sliced vegetables for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rinse and drain the canned chickpeas. For an extra creamy hummus, peel the chickpeas by gently squeezing each one until the skin comes off.
  2. Add chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, ground cumin, and a pinch of salt to a food processor.
  3. Blend the mixture until smooth, gradually adding olive oil and a bit of water (1 tablespoon at a time) until it reaches your desired consistency.
  4. Transfer to a serving dish and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle smoked paprika on top and garnish with parsley if desired.
  5. Serve with warm pita bread or fresh vegetables.

2. Smoky Baba Ghanoush

If you enjoy eggplant, baba ghanoush is a must-try dish. Creamy and slightly smoky, it pairs beautifully with pita or as a flavorful base in sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 3 tbsp tahini
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus more for garnish
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp ground sumac (optional, for garnish)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Char the eggplants over an open flame on a gas stove or grill until the skin is blackened and the flesh becomes tender. Alternatively, roast them in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 45 minutes, turning occasionally.
  2. Once cooled, peel off the charred skins and scoop out the flesh.
  3. In a food processor, combine the eggplant flesh, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and salt. Pulse until you achieve a smooth consistency.
  4. Transfer to a serving dish and drizzle with extra olive oil. Sprinkle with sumac and parsley before serving.

3. Falafel: Crispy Chickpea Fritters

Falafel is a beloved Middle Eastern street food that can be enjoyed in wraps, salads, or on its own. Making falafel from scratch ensures a crisp and savory fritter that’s second to none.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight (do not use canned chickpeas)
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • ½ cup fresh parsley
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Neutral oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and pat them dry. Transfer them to a food processor with the onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, baking soda, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pulse the mixture until it has the texture of coarse sand but holds together when pressed. Do not over-process.
  3. Form the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve immediately with tahini sauce and warm pita.

4. Lentil Mujadara: The Comfort Food of the Levant

Mujadara combines earthy lentils, caramelized onions, and fluffy rice into a hearty dish that’s both filling and budget-friendly. It’s a comforting, plant-forward meal packed with protein and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh yogurt or cucumber salad for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lentils in a pot of salted boiling water until tender but not mushy, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring regularly, until they turn golden brown and caramelized — this can take up to 30 minutes.
  3. Rinse the rice well and cook it in a separate pot according to package instructions. Once cooked, combine the rice and lentils in the pot and stir in the spices and some caramelized onions, reserving the rest for topping.
  4. Plate the mujadara, garnishing generously with the reserved caramelized onions.
  5. Add a dollop of yogurt or serve with a crisp cucumber and tomato salad to balance the savory flavors.

5. Herbaceous Tabbouleh Salad

Brimming with fresh herbs, tabbouleh is a refreshing salad that serves as a palate cleanser or a stand-alone light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely diced
  • 1 small cucumber, finely diced
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ½ bunch fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the bulgur in a large bowl and cover with boiling water. Let it sit for 15 minutes, then drain and press out any excess water.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bulgur, tomatoes, cucumber, parsley, and mint.
  3. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Toss well and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to let the flavors meld together.

6. Sweet and Nutty Baklava

Finally, no Middle Eastern feast is complete without a little sweetness. Baklava is a rich, flaky dessert made of layers of filo pastry filled with nuts and soaked in syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of filo dough
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, or almonds), finely chopped
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • ½ cup honey
  • 1 tsp rose water or orange blossom water (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish.
  2. Layer half the filo dough in the dish, brushing each sheet lightly with melted butter.
  3. Spread the chopped nuts evenly over the dough.
  4. Layer the remaining filo sheets on top, again brushing each layer with butter. Cut the assembled pastry into diamond or square shapes.
  5. Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until golden.
  6. While the baklava bakes, prepare the syrup by combining sugar, water, honey, and rose water in a saucepan. Simmer until slightly thickened.
  7. Pour the hot syrup over the baklava as soon as it comes out of the oven. Let it cool completely before serving.

Middle Eastern cuisine has something for everyone, especially those who prioritize vegetarian dishes. Each recipe above highlights the region’s extraordinary depth of flavor while being approachable for the home cook. From creamy dips and satisfying mains to a sweet finale, these dishes will add variety and vibrant taste to your table. Try them out, tweak to suit your preferences, and immerse yourself in the magic of Middle Eastern fare!
