The Best Sous Vide Recipe for Whole Chicken


Cooking a whole chicken can be a daunting task, but with the sous vide method, you can achieve juicy, tender, and flavorful results that will impress your family and friends. Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, allows you to cook food evenly by immersing it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This technique ensures that your chicken is cooked perfectly all the way through, while also retaining its natural juices.

In this blog post, we’ll explore the best sous vide recipe for whole chicken, including tips on seasoning, cooking times, and finishing techniques that will elevate your dish to a gourmet level. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients

To make the best sous vide whole chicken, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds)
  • Salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried)
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried)
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil or melted butter
  • Optional: fresh vegetables like carrots, potatoes, or onions for roasting

Equipment

Before we get started, here’s a list of equipment you’ll need for sous vide cooking:

  • Sous vide immersion circulator
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Vacuum sealer and bags or resealable freezer bags
  • Cast iron skillet or oven-safe pan for finishing
  • Tongs
  • Instant-read thermometer (optional but recommended)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

  1. Clean and Dry: Start by cleaning your whole chicken under cold running water. Pat it dry with paper towels. This step is crucial to achieve a nice sear later.

  2. Season: Generously season both the inside and outside of the chicken with salt and freshly ground black pepper. The seasoning will enhance the flavors and help form a delicious crust when you finish the chicken later.

  3. Add Aromatics: In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, chopped rosemary, and thyme with olive oil or melted butter. Rub this mixture all over the chicken, making sure to get under the skin for maximum flavor.

  4. Vacuum Seal: Place the seasoned chicken into a vacuum seal bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, a resealable freezer bag will work too. If using a freezer bag, use the water displacement method to remove air: submerge the bag in water until just the zip is above the surface, then seal.

Step 2: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat the Water Bath: Fill a large pot or container with water and set your sous vide immersion circulator to 165°F (74°C). This temperature will ensure the chicken is cooked safely and remains juicy.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Once the water has reached the desired temperature, submerge the sealed chicken in the water bath. Cook for 4 to 6 hours. Cooking for this time allows the chicken to become incredibly tender and flavorful.

Step 3: Finishing Touches

  1. Remove from Water Bath: After the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the chicken from the water bath. Use tongs to avoid burns.

  2. Dry the Chicken: Pat the chicken dry with paper towels. This step is essential for achieving a crispy skin when searing.

  3. Sear the Chicken: Heat a cast iron skillet or oven-safe pan over medium-high heat. Add a little olive oil or butter to the pan. Once the oil is shimmering, carefully place the chicken in the skillet, breast side down, and sear for about 4-5 minutes until golden brown.

  4. Flip and Finish: Using tongs, flip the chicken onto its back and sear for an additional 4-5 minutes. If you’re using an oven-safe pan, you can finish cooking in a preheated oven at 450°F (232°C) for about 10-15 minutes to ensure the skin is crispy.

  5. Rest: Once the chicken is nicely browned, remove it from the heat and let it rest for about 10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute for an even juicier chicken.

Serving Suggestions

Carve the chicken and serve it with your favorite sides. Roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh garden salad make excellent companions. You can also use the leftover carcass to make a flavorful chicken stock.

Tips for the Best Sous Vide Whole Chicken

  • Season Generously: Don’t skimp on the seasoning. This is your chance to infuse flavor throughout the chicken.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to experiment with different herbs, spices, or even citrus fruits. Thyme, sage, or even orange zest can add delightful flavor notes.

  • Cooking Time and Temperature: Cooking at 165°F will give you a traditional texture, but if you wish for a more tender result, you can cook at 145°F (63°C) for 2-4 hours. Just be aware that the texture will differ from the classic roast chicken.

  • Make it a Meal: To save time, you can add vegetables to the vacuum-sealed bag with the chicken before cooking. Carrots, potatoes, and onions cook beautifully in the sous vide and will absorb the chicken’s flavors.

Conclusion

The sous vide method for cooking a whole chicken is not only easy but also guarantees a moist and flavorful result every time. With just a few simple steps, you can elevate your chicken dish to gourmet status that will wow your dinner guests.

So, gather your ingredients, fire up your sous vide immersion circulator, and prepare to enjoy the most succulent chicken you’ve ever made. Bon appétit!
