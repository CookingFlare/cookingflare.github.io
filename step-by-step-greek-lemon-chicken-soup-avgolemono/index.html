Step-by-Step Greek Lemon Chicken Soup (Avgolemono)


Greek Lemon Chicken Soup, known as Avgolemono, is a classic Mediterranean dish that’s creamy, tangy, and comforting. Packed with bold citrus flavors and rich textures, this soup perfectly balances simplicity and sophistication, making it the ideal choice for a cozy night at home or a dinner party centerpiece. Whether you’re familiar with Greek cuisine or new to its vibrant flavors, Avgolemono is guaranteed to delight your palate and leave you craving more. This post will guide you through each step in creating this traditional dish, breaking it down so even beginners can master it with confidence.

What Makes Avgolemono Special?

Avgolemono derives its name from two Greek words: avgo (egg) and lemono (lemon). These key ingredients create a unique creamy consistency without relying on dairy. The soup’s velvety texture comes from the delicate emulsification of eggs and lemon juice into hot broth, resulting in a dish that’s both light and luscious. Beyond its irresistible flavor, Avgolemono is deeply rooted in Greek tradition, often prepared during cold months or as a comforting remedy for colds and fatigue. Moreover, its adaptable nature means you can tailor the recipe to suit various dietary needs or preferences.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s everything required to create authentic Avgolemono soup:

Core Ingredients:

  • Chicken (Bone-in, skin-on preferred): About 1.5 - 2 pounds for richness.
  • Carrots: 2 medium-sized, peeled and diced.
  • Celery: Two stalks, chopped.
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely diced.
  • Rice or Orzo: ½ cup (choose based on preference; rice will yield a denser texture while orzo tends to result in a lighter bite).
  • Eggs: 3 large eggs.
  • Fresh Lemon Juice: 3 tablespoons (adjust based on taste preference).
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: Optional, for added flavor.
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste.
  • Parsley or Dill: For garnish.

Optional Additions:

  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced (to enhance the broth’s depth).
  • Lemon Zest: For an extra citrus punch.
  • Chicken Stock: Use homemade if possible for the best flavor, but store-bought works fine.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

1. Prepare the Broth

Start by creating a flavorful base for the soup. In a large pot, place your chicken pieces, diced carrots, celery, and onion. Cover everything with water (approximately 10 cups) and bring to a gentle boil. If you’re using chicken stock, substitute part of the water with stock for a more robust taste.

Skim off any foam that arises as the chicken cooks. This ensures a clear broth. Lower the heat to a simmer and let it cook for about 40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and fully cooked. At this stage, the vegetables should also be soft, allowing their flavors to meld beautifully into the liquid.

2. Remove and Shred the Chicken

Using tongs or a slotted spoon, remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside to cool slightly. Once cool enough to handle, shred the meat off the bones. You can discard the skin and bones or save them for making stock later. Return the shredded chicken to the pot or keep it aside as a garnish.

3. Strain the Broth

For a smoother soup, strain out the vegetables from the broth using a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Though optional, this step elevates Avgolemono’s signature silky texture. If you prefer a heartier soup, leave the vegetables in the pot.

4. Cook the Rice or Orzo

Return the strained broth to the pot and bring it to a gentle simmer. Stir in the rice or orzo, cooking until tender. For rice, this typically takes 15–20 minutes, while orzo cooks faster, usually within 7–10 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

5. Prepare the Egg-Lemon Mixture

While the rice or orzo cooks, it’s time to prepare the hallmark of Avgolemono: the egg-lemon emulsion. In a mixing bowl, whisk the eggs until frothy. Gradually add the lemon juice, whisking continually to combine.

6. Temper the Egg Mixture

Tempering is a critical step to prevent the eggs from scrambling when added to the hot broth. Gradually ladle 1–2 cups of hot broth into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. This step gently warms the eggs without curdling them. When the mixture is well combined and feels warm to the touch, proceed to the next step.

7. Combine the Egg Mixture with the Soup

Slowly pour the tempered egg-lemon mixture back into the pot, stirring continuously. Reduce the heat to low; the soup should be hot but not boiling, as excessive heat can cause the eggs to separate.

At this point, the soup will transform into a creamy, rich texture without the need for heavy cream or other thickeners. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional lemon juice.

8. Add Optional Enhancements

If you set the shredded chicken aside earlier, you can now reincorporate it into the soup. For extra flavor, drizzle a touch of olive oil or sprinkle in fresh lemon zest. Finish with chopped parsley or dill for a pop of color and added freshness.

Serving Suggestions

Avgolemono is best enjoyed warm, served in generous bowls and garnished with freshly chopped herbs. Pair it with crusty bread or warm pita for a perfect meal. While delicious as is, you can also explore complementary sides such as a Greek salad or roasted vegetables to round out your menu. For special occasions, pair Avgolemono with a crisp white wine like Assyrtiko to complement its citrusy notes.

Tips for Perfect Avgolemono Every Time

  • Quality Matters: Use fresh, high-quality eggs, lemons, and chicken for optimal flavor. Organic products are ideal if available.
  • Master Egg-Tempering: This step is crucial for achieving the soup’s creamy consistency while avoiding scrambled eggs. Take your time and whisk diligently.
  • Balance the Lemon: Adjust the lemon juice to your taste. Some prefer a bold citrus kick, while others opt for a milder tang.
  • Reheating: Avgolemono is best enjoyed freshly made, but if you need to reheat leftovers, do so gently over low heat to preserve the emulsion.

Why You’ll Love Avgolemono

The beauty of Avgolemono lies in its simplicity and versatility. Whether you’re tackling a busy weekday dinner or hosting loved ones for a festive gathering, this Greek delight always delivers authentic flavors and warming comfort. Its unique preparation method, combining eggs and lemons for natural creaminess, also makes Avgolemono a healthier alternative to traditional creamy soups.

Furthermore, its Mediterranean origins bring a touch of culture and history to every bite, reminding us of the deep-rooted connection between food, tradition, and heritage. If you’re looking for a dish that’s as nurturing as it is flavorful, Avgolemono truly stands out as a timeless classic.

Closing Thoughts

Making Greek Lemon Chicken Soup (Avgolemono) at home doesn’t have to be intimidating. With this step-by-step guide, you can create a dish that’s both delicious and impressive. The combination of bright lemon, tender chicken, silky eggs, and aromatic herbs creates a comforting experience every time. So grab your ingredients, follow these steps, and enjoy the taste of Greece in your own kitchen!
