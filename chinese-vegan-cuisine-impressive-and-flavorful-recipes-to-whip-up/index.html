Chinese Vegan Cuisine: Impressive and Flavorful Recipes to Whip Up


Are you looking to experiment with vegan cuisine or seeking recipe ideas for a plant-based dinner party? Chinese vegan recipes are not only healthy and nutrient-packed but also feature a complex range of flavors that’ll leave you enchanted! From aromatic and savory dishes to delectable desserts, there’s no shortage of impressive vegan recipes straight from the heart of China. Let’s dive into a curated selection of some of the most flavorsome Chinese vegan dishes, perfect for both vegetarians and meat-eating individuals alike.

1. Mapo Tofu

A popular vegan classic from Sichuan province, Mapo Tofu is a spicy dish packed full of mouth-watering flavors. The tender tofu blocks are simmered in a savory sauce, with the warmth of Sichuan peppercorns and chili oil offering a delightful numbing sensation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 blocks of firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of Sichuan peppercorns, ground
  • 1 tablespoon of chili bean paste
  • 2 tablespoons of water
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu into cubes and dust them with cornstarch.
  2. In a skillet, heat a splash of oil over medium heat, and saut√© the garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, and chili bean paste until aromatic.
  3. Add the tofu and gently mix until the cubes are evenly coated with the sauce.
  4. Add the water and soy sauce, and cook on low heat for about 5 minutes until the tofu is fully cooked through. Season with salt as needed.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice.

2. Ma Jiang Bao (Pan Fried Buns with Fermented Bean Paste)

These delectable Chinese vegan buns feature a soft, fluffy dough encasing a luscious filling of vegetables and fermented bean paste. Pan-fried to perfection, they offer a textural contrast with a crispy bottom and a warm, doughy top.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable shortening
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup finely chopped bamboo shoots
  • 1 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green peas
  • 1 tablespoon fermented bean paste, mashed
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. To make the dough, combine the flour, warm water, shortening, yeast, and salt. Knead for about 10 minutes, until smooth and pliable.
  2. Let the dough rise for about an hour in a warm, draft-free place.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the bamboo shoots, mushrooms, green peas, fermented bean paste, and sesame oil, and mix until well combined.
  4. Roll out the dough into large circles, and place a spoonful of the filling mixture in the center of each. Fold the dough to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges to seal.
  5. Pan-fry the buns over medium heat, until golden brown and crisp on the bottom.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with sesame seeds, and a side of your favorite dipping sauce.

3. Veggie Lo Mein

A classic and scrumptious vegan noodle dish, veggie lo mein is a one-pan meal packed with an assortment of colorful vegetables, all nestled in a silky, savory sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of lo mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 cup of finely sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup of finely chopped bok choy or Chinese cabbage
  • 1/2 cup of shelled edamame
  • 1/2 cup of thinly sliced carrots
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lo mein noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the garlic and onion, cooking until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add in the bell peppers, bok choy, edamame, and carrots. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 5 minutes until just tender.
  5. Whisk together the soy sauce, cornstarch, and water in a small bowl, then pour over the vegetables.
  6. Add the cooked lo mein noodles, tossing gently to coat them with the sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve.

4. Deep-fried Tofu with Black Bean Sauce

This twist on the classic Chinese dish features tender, crispy fried tofu, smothered in a delectable black bean sauce, giving it a rich and savory flavor to tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • vegetable oil, for deep-frying
  • 1/2 cup fermented black beans, drained and mashed
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes, then dredge them in the flour, followed by the batter (made by combining the remaining flour and water, plus baking powder).
  2. Deep-fry the tofu in batches until golden brown and crisp, then drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the mashed black beans, soy sauce, water, sugar, and sesame oil to create the black bean sauce.
  4. Arrange the fried tofu on a plate and drizzle the sauce over the top, serving with steamed rice or noodles.

5. Sweet and Sour Vegetables

A quintessential Chinese takeout dish, Sweet and Sour Vegetables is a delightful and colorful dish brimming with a mixture of fresh vegetables and pineapple in a tangy and Sticky sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of finely diced bell peppers
  • 1 cup of finely sliced onions
  • 1 cup of pineapple chunks
  • 1 cup of finely sliced bamboo shoots
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of crushed red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat, and stir-fry the bell peppers, onions, pineapple, and bamboo shoots until just tender.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, water, sugar, cornstarch, and red pepper flakes to create the sweet and sour sauce.
  3. Pour the sauce over the vegetables, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce has thickened and the vegetables are fully coated.
  4. Serve hot alongside rice or noodles for a filling and satisfying meal.

Entering the world of Chinese vegan cuisine can be a gratifying and delicious experience. These recipes showcase just a small portion of the incredible diversity and wide range of flavors in Chinese cooking. Incorporating a variety of colorful, fresh ingredients and simple yet bold sauces, these dishes offer a wholesome meal, packed full of flavors that are sure to impress. Enjoy your cooking endeavors, and embrace the tantalizing taste of China.
