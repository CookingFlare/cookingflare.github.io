Dango is a Japanese dumpling made from rice flour, typically served on a skewer. It is popular during festivals and is often enjoyed with seasonal ingredients. A Brief History of Dango Dango has a long and rich history in Japan, dating back to ancient times. The word "dango" is believed to have originated from the ancient term "daigaku," referring to campus life and student culture during the Edo period. Historically, it was a food associated with festivals and has become popular in Japanese culture for its symbolism of unity and togetherness.
