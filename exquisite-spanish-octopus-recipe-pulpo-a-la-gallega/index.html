Exquisite Spanish Octopus Recipe: Pulpo a la Gallega


Welcome to our culinary journey through Spain! Today, we’re going to explore one of the most beloved and iconic dishes of Galicia: Pulpo a la Gallega. This exquisite Spanish octopus recipe showcases the simplicity and brilliance of Galician cuisine. With its tender octopus, flavorful paprika, and drizzle of olive oil, this dish is sure to transport your taste buds to the picturesque coastal region of Galicia.

Ingredients

To create this traditional Spanish dish, you will need:

  • 1 large octopus (approximately 3 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Crusty bread, to serve

Instructions

  1. Start by cleaning the octopus. Rinse it thoroughly under cold water, removing any impurities or debris.

  2. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add the sea salt to the water, ensuring it is well-dissolved.

  3. Gently lower the octopus into the pot, making sure it is fully submerged. Cover the pot and let the octopus cook over medium heat for approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until tender.

  4. While the octopus is cooking, prepare a large bowl of ice water. Once the octopus is done, carefully remove it from the pot and immediately submerge it in the ice water. This will help stop the cooking process and ensure the octopus remains tender.

  5. Let the octopus cool in the ice water for about 10 minutes, then remove it and place it on a cutting board.

  6. Using a sharp knife, cut the octopus into bite-sized pieces, aiming for uniform thickness.

  7. In a small bowl, mix together the paprika and cayenne pepper (if using). Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the octopus, ensuring each piece is coated with the flavorful spices.

  8. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the octopus pieces on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until slightly charred. This step will add a smoky flavor and enhance the overall texture of the dish.

  9. Once grilled, transfer the octopus pieces to a serving platter. Drizzle generously with extra virgin olive oil, allowing it to seep into the crevices of the octopus.

  10. Sprinkle the chopped parsley over the octopus for a fresh burst of flavor and vibrant color.

  11. Serve the Pulpo a la Gallega immediately, accompanied by crusty bread to soak up the delicious juices.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve successfully recreated the Exquisite Spanish Octopus Recipe: Pulpo a la Gallega. This dish is a true taste of Galicia, showcasing the region’s commitment to fresh seafood and simple yet impactful flavors. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to indulge in a culinary adventure, Pulpo a la Gallega is a dish that will impress and delight your guests. So grab your ingredients, fire up the grill, and savor every bite of this Spanish culinary masterpiece. ¡Buen provecho!
