Paleo-Friendly Barbecue Recipes: Grilling Goodness without the Guilt


The smoky aroma of a barbecue, the sizzle of the grill, and the promise of delicious food shared with friends and family – there’s something truly special about a summer cookout. But for those following a paleo lifestyle, traditional barbecue fare can present a challenge. The good news is that with a few simple swaps and creative twists, you can enjoy all the flavors of a classic barbecue while staying true to your paleo principles. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering paleo-friendly barbecue recipes that will have you firing up the grill with confidence and savoring every guilt-free bite.

Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • 2 lemons, juiced and zested
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken cubes onto skewers.
  4. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a beautiful char.

Serve with: A side of grilled vegetables or a fresh green salad.

Smoky BBQ Pulled Pork Lettuce Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup paleo-friendly barbecue sauce
  • Lettuce leaves for wrapping

Instructions:

  1. Season the pork shoulder with salt and pepper and place it in a slow cooker.
  2. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and chicken broth to the slow cooker.
  3. Cook on low for 8-10 hours until the pork is tender and easily pulls apart with a fork.
  4. Shred the cooked pork using two forks and mix in the paleo-friendly barbecue sauce.
  5. Spoon the smoky pulled pork into lettuce leaves and serve.

Serve with: Coleslaw or pickles for a refreshing crunch.

Grilled Pineapple and Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pineapple, cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, honey, lime juice, paprika, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Thread the shrimp, pineapple, bell pepper, and onion onto the soaked skewers and brush them with the marinade.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat and grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes, turning once, until the shrimp is pink and the pineapple has caramelized.

Serve with: Cauliflower rice for a complete meal.

Conclusion

With these paleo-friendly barbecue recipes, you can indulge in the joy of grilling without compromising your dietary preferences. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or simply craving the flavors of summer, these recipes are sure to satisfy your barbecue cravings while keeping you on track with your paleo lifestyle. So, fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the goodness of paleo-friendly barbecue – guilt-free and utterly delicious!
