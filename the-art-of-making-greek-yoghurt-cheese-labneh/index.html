The Art of Making Greek Yoghurt Cheese (Labneh)


Labneh, a creamy and tangy cheese made from strained Greek yogurt, has a special place in Middle Eastern cuisine. Known for its rich flavor and versatile uses, labneh can be enjoyed on its own, spread on bread, or incorporated into a variety of dishes. Making labneh at home is surprisingly simple and requires minimal ingredients. In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through the art of crafting labneh, exploring its history, health benefits, and various ways to enjoy this delicious cheese.

What is Labneh?

Labneh, also known as strained yogurt or yogurt cheese, is a traditional Middle Eastern dairy product. It is made by straining yogurt to remove the whey, resulting in a thick, creamy consistency that resembles cream cheese. Originating from regions like Lebanon and Syria, labneh has become popular worldwide due to its unique flavor and health benefits.

The History of Labneh

The history of labneh can be traced back thousands of years to the nomadic tribes of the Middle East who would ferment milk to create a more stable and flavorful product. The process of straining yogurt to make labneh allowed these communities to preserve dairy in a hot climate. Over time, labneh evolved into a staple in Middle Eastern households, often served with olive oil, herbs, and spices as part of a mezze platter.

Health Benefits of Labneh

Labneh is not just a delicious addition to your meals; it also boasts several health benefits:

  1. Rich in Probiotics: Labneh is made from fermented yogurt, which means it is packed with probiotics. These beneficial bacteria support gut health and digestion.

  2. High in Protein: Straining yogurt concentrates the protein content, making labneh an excellent source of protein for vegetarians and those looking to increase their protein intake.

  3. Lower in Lactose: The straining process reduces lactose content, making labneh easier to digest for individuals who are lactose intolerant.

  4. Nutrient Dense: Labneh contains essential vitamins and minerals, including calcium, potassium, and B vitamins, contributing to overall health.

  5. Versatile Ingredient: Labneh can be used in various dishes, from breakfast spreads to savory dips and desserts, making it a fantastic addition to a balanced diet.

Ingredients Needed for Labneh

To make labneh at home, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of Greek yogurt: Choose plain, full-fat Greek yogurt for the best flavor and texture.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt: This enhances the flavor and helps draw out moisture from the yogurt.
  • Cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel: This will be used to strain the yogurt.

Optional Additions:

  • Olive oil: For drizzling over the finished labneh.
  • Herbs and spices: Such as za’atar, dill, or crushed garlic for added flavor.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Labneh

Step 1: Prepare the Yogurt Mixture

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the Greek yogurt and salt. Stir until the salt is fully dissolved and the mixture is well combined.
  2. Taste the mixture and adjust the salt according to your preference. Remember that the salt can enhance the flavor, so don’t skip this step.

Step 2: Strain the Yogurt

  1. Place a fine-mesh strainer over a large bowl. If you don’t have a strainer, you can use a colander lined with several layers of cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Pour the yogurt mixture into the strainer, spreading it out evenly.
  3. Bring the edges of the cheesecloth together and tie them securely, creating a pouch. You can also use a rubber band to secure it if needed.

Step 3: Let It Drain

  1. Allow the yogurt to drain for about 12-24 hours, depending on how thick you want your labneh to be. For a creamier texture, drain for 12 hours; for a thicker consistency, let it drain for up to 24 hours.
  2. Place the bowl in the refrigerator during this process to keep the yogurt fresh.

Step 4: Collect and Store

  1. After the draining period, carefully untie the cheesecloth or towel and transfer the thickened labneh into a clean container.
  2. At this point, you can mix in any additional herbs or spices you desire for flavor.
  3. Drizzle a little olive oil over the top for added richness and to help preserve the labneh.
  4. Seal the container and store it in the refrigerator. Labneh can last for up to two weeks in the fridge.

Ways to Enjoy Labneh

Labneh is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in numerous ways:

1. As a Spread

Spread labneh on warm pita bread or crusty baguettes for a delightful appetizer. You can top it with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of za’atar for a Middle Eastern twist.

2. As a Dip

Serve labneh as a dip alongside fresh vegetables, olives, or crackers. It pairs wonderfully with roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, or herbs like dill and parsley.

3. In Salads

Incorporate labneh into salads for a creamy dressing. Mix it with lemon juice, garlic, and herbs to create a fresh and zesty salad dressing that complements greens, grains, and roasted vegetables.

4. With Breakfast

Labneh can elevate your breakfast routine. Use it as a topping for toast, pancakes, or waffles. Pair it with honey, fresh fruits, or nuts for a wholesome start to your day.

5. In Dishes

Add labneh to savory dishes like stews, curries, or grain bowls for a creamy finish. It can also be used as a replacement for sour cream in various recipes.

6. As a Dessert

For a unique dessert, mix labneh with honey and fresh fruits for a light and refreshing treat. You can also use it in place of ricotta in desserts like cannoli.

Pairing Labneh with Other Ingredients

Labneh’s tangy flavor profile makes it a great companion for various ingredients. Here are some popular pairings:

  • Fruits: Berries, figs, and pomegranate seeds add sweetness and color.
  • Nuts: Almonds, pistachios, or walnuts provide a crunchy texture.
  • Spices: Cumin, paprika, and sumac enhance the flavor and add depth.

Conclusion

Making labneh at home is a rewarding culinary adventure that yields a delicious and healthy cheese. With just a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create a creamy, tangy labneh that can enhance a multitude of dishes. Whether you enjoy it as a spread, dip, or ingredient in your favorite recipes, labneh is sure to impress your family and friends. Embrace the art of making labneh, and savor the delightful flavors of this Middle Eastern treasure!
