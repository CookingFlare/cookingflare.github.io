Garnishing with Seeds: Pumpkin, Sunflower, and More


Seeds are not just for planting in the garden or snacking on. They can also add a delightful crunch and a burst of flavor to your dishes. In this blog post, we will explore the art of garnishing with seeds, focusing on three popular varieties: pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, and more.

Pumpkin Seeds

Pumpkin seeds, also known as pepitas, are a versatile seed that can be used in both sweet and savory dishes. They have a mild nutty flavor and are packed with essential nutrients like protein, fiber, and magnesium.

Roasted Pumpkin Seeds

One of the most popular ways to enjoy pumpkin seeds is by roasting them. Here’s a simple recipe to make your own roasted pumpkin seeds:

  • Start by cleaning the pumpkin seeds. Remove any pulp or strings and rinse them under cold water.
  • Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).
  • Spread the cleaned pumpkin seeds on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  • Drizzle the seeds with olive oil and sprinkle them with salt or your favorite seasoning. You can get creative with flavors by adding spices like cinnamon, paprika, or garlic powder.
  • Toss the seeds to coat them evenly with the oil and seasoning.
  • Bake the seeds in the preheated oven for about 20-30 minutes or until they turn golden brown. Stir them occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  • Once done, remove the baking sheet from the oven and let the seeds cool completely before using them as a garnish.

Pumpkin Seed Pesto

Another delicious way to incorporate pumpkin seeds into your dishes is by making a pumpkin seed pesto. Here’s a quick recipe to get you started:

  • In a food processor, combine 1 cup of roasted pumpkin seeds, 2 cloves of garlic, 1 cup of fresh basil leaves, ¼ cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and ½ cup of olive oil.
  • Blend the ingredients until smooth and creamy.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Use the pumpkin seed pesto as a sauce for pasta, a spread for sandwiches, or a dip for vegetables.

Sunflower Seeds

Sunflower seeds are rich in healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals. They have a slightly nutty flavor and can be used in a variety of dishes, from salads to baked goods.

Sunflower Seed Crusted Chicken

Adding a crunchy sunflower seed crust to chicken breasts can take your dish to the next level. Here’s a simple recipe to try:

  • Start by pounding boneless, skinless chicken breasts to an even thickness.
  • In a shallow bowl, combine 1 cup of crushed sunflower seeds, ½ cup of breadcrumbs, and your preferred seasonings like salt, pepper, and dried herbs.
  • Dip each chicken breast into beaten eggs, then coat it with the sunflower seed mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  • Heat some oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  • Cook the chicken breasts for about 4-5 minutes on each side or until they are cooked through and the crust is golden brown.
  • Serve the sunflower seed crusted chicken with your favorite side dishes for a delicious and nutritious meal.

More Seeds to Explore

While pumpkin seeds and sunflower seeds are popular choices for garnishing, there are many other seeds that you can experiment with to add texture and flavor to your dishes. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Sesame seeds: These tiny seeds are commonly used in Asian cuisine and are a great addition to stir-fries, salads, and baked goods.
  • Chia seeds: Chia seeds are known for their high fiber content and can be sprinkled on top of yogurt, oatmeal, or smoothies for an added nutritional boost.
  • Flaxseeds: Rich in omega-3 fatty acids, flaxseeds can be ground and used as an egg substitute in vegan baking or sprinkled on top of cereal or salads.

Get creative with these seeds and let your culinary imagination run wild. Experiment with different combinations and find your favorite ways to garnish your dishes with seeds.

Remember, garnishing with seeds not only adds visual appeal but also enhances the taste and nutritional value of your meals. So go ahead and sprinkle some seeds on your next culinary creation. Happy cooking!
