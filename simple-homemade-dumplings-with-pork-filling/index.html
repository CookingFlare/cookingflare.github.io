Simple Homemade Dumplings with Pork Filling


Dumplings are a comforting and versatile dish that can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, from steamed to boiled, pan-fried to deep-fried. One of the most popular and beloved dumpling varieties is the pork-filled dumpling. Whether you’re preparing them for a family gathering, a weeknight dinner, or as part of a special occasion, homemade dumplings bring a sense of satisfaction that can’t be matched by store-bought options. The key to creating the perfect dumpling lies in the balance of flavors, texture, and technique. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through making simple, delicious homemade dumplings with pork filling from scratch.

The Perfect Pork Filling: Tender, Flavorful, and Juicy

The pork filling is the heart of the dumpling. The best dumplings feature a moist, tender filling that is packed with flavor. When choosing the pork for your filling, aim for a combination of lean and slightly fatty cuts. The fat adds moisture and flavor to the filling, helping it stay juicy while cooking. Ground pork shoulder or a mixture of pork belly and pork shoulder works best.

Ingredients for Pork Filling

  • 1 pound ground pork (preferably a mix of lean and fatty cuts for the best texture)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for umami and saltiness)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (adds a mild acidity and balance)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (adds a nutty aroma)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (balances the saltiness)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger (adds freshness and warmth)
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely minced (for depth of flavor)
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped (adds freshness and slight sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese rice wine (Shaoxing wine) (for depth of flavor, optional)
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped napa cabbage or shiitake mushrooms (optional, for added texture and flavor)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preparing the Pork Filling

  1. Mixing the Pork: In a large bowl, combine the ground pork with soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, grated ginger, minced garlic, and Chinese rice wine (if using). Stir everything together until the mixture is smooth and well combined.

  2. Add the Vegetables: If you’re using napa cabbage or mushrooms, stir them into the pork mixture. This adds a bit of texture and moisture to the filling, making the dumplings juicier and more flavorful.

  3. Season the Mixture: Taste the filling and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. The flavor should be rich, savory, and slightly aromatic, with a hint of sweetness.

  4. Rest the Filling: Once everything is thoroughly mixed, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the filling for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the filling helps the flavors meld together and improves the texture.

Making the Dumpling Dough from Scratch

The dough is just as important as the filling when it comes to dumplings. A perfect dough should be soft, elastic, and easy to work with. The key is to achieve the right balance of flour and water, which will create a dough that’s not too tough or too sticky.

Ingredients for Dumpling Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water (you may need a bit more, depending on the flour)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional, for added flexibility)

Making the Dough

  1. Combine the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Stir until evenly distributed.

  2. Add the Water: Gradually add the warm water to the flour mixture while stirring with a spoon or chopsticks. The warm water helps activate the gluten, making the dough more pliable. Add the water a little at a time, ensuring the dough comes together in small clumps.

  3. Knead the Dough: Once the dough starts to come together, transfer it to a clean, floured surface. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much. You want the dough to be soft but not sticky.

  4. Rest the Dough: After kneading, shape the dough into a ball and cover it with a damp towel or plastic wrap. Let it rest for 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax. This makes rolling the dough out easier.

Assembling the Dumplings

Once the filling and dough are ready, it’s time to assemble the dumplings. This step requires some practice to ensure the dumplings hold together and cook evenly.

  1. Roll Out the Dough: After the dough has rested, divide it into small portions (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it slightly with your hands. Using a rolling pin, roll each piece into a thin, circular wrapper. Aim for wrappers that are about 3 inches in diameter, but try to keep them even in thickness. The edges should be slightly thinner than the center.

  2. Fill the Dumplings: Place a spoonful of the pork filling in the center of each wrapper. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the dumplings to burst open while cooking. Use about 1 to 1.5 teaspoons of filling per dumpling.

  3. Seal the Dumplings: To seal the dumpling, fold the wrapper in half over the filling, creating a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges together to form a tight seal. For a more decorative touch, you can pleat the edges by folding the dough in small, neat pleats as you press the edges together.

  4. Repeat the Process: Continue assembling the dumplings until all the dough and filling are used. If you’re making a large batch, you can freeze any leftover dumplings by placing them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freezing them in a single layer. Once frozen, transfer the dumplings to a ziplock bag or airtight container for storage.

Cooking the Dumplings

Now that your dumplings are assembled, it’s time to cook them! Dumplings can be boiled, steamed, or pan-fried. Each method gives the dumplings a different texture, so you can choose the cooking style based on your preference.

Boiling Dumplings

  1. Boil Water: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt to the water for seasoning.

  2. Cook the Dumplings: Carefully add the dumplings to the boiling water, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Stir gently to prevent them from sticking to the bottom. Once the dumplings float to the surface, allow them to cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, until they are cooked through.

  3. Serve: Remove the dumplings from the water using a slotted spoon and serve hot with a dipping sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, and a dash of sesame oil.

Steaming Dumplings

  1. Prepare a Steamer: If you’re steaming the dumplings, line a bamboo steamer or metal steamer basket with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking. Make sure there’s enough space between each dumpling.

  2. Steam the Dumplings: Place the dumplings in the steamer and steam over simmering water for 15-20 minutes, or until the dough is fully cooked and the filling reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  3. Serve: Carefully remove the dumplings from the steamer and serve them with your favorite dipping sauce.

Pan-Frying Dumplings

  1. Heat a Pan: Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.

  2. Fry the Dumplings: Add the dumplings in a single layer, ensuring they’re not touching. Fry for 2-3 minutes until the bottoms are golden and crispy.

  3. Steam the Dumplings: Once the bottoms are crispy, add 1/4 cup of water to the pan (be careful, as it will splatter). Cover immediately with a lid and let the dumplings steam for another 4-5 minutes until the water evaporates.

  4. Serve: Remove the dumplings from the pan and serve hot with a dipping sauce.

Tips for Making Perfect Dumplings

  • Use Room Temperature Ingredients: When making the dough, use warm water and ensure your filling ingredients are at room temperature. This helps the dough come together more easily and ensures the filling is well-mixed.

  • Don’t Overfill the Dumplings: When adding the filling, be sure not to overstuff the dumplings. Too much filling can cause the dumplings to burst open while cooking.

  • Keep the Dough Covered: If you’re making dumplings in batches, keep the dough covered with a damp towel to prevent it from drying out.

  • Experiment with Fillings: While pork is the classic filling, you can easily mix it up by adding ingredients like shrimp, chicken, or vegetables. You can even make a vegetarian version using mushrooms, tofu, or cabbage.

Serving Your Dumplings

Dumplings can be served as a main dish or as a side dish. They pair wonderfully with a light soup, steamed vegetables, or a crisp salad. A simple dipping sauce made of soy sauce, rice vinegar, and a drizzle of sesame oil complements the dumplings perfectly.

Classic Dumpling Dipping Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • A pinch of sugar
  • A few drops of chili oil (optional)

Mix all ingredients together and serve alongside your freshly cooked dumplings. For added flavor, you can also garnish the dipping sauce with finely chopped garlic, green onions, or chili flakes.

Conclusion

Making homemade dumplings with pork filling is a rewarding and enjoyable experience. With the right balance of flavors, a perfect dough, and a little patience, you can create delicious dumplings that are sure to be a hit with friends and family. Whether you choose to boil, steam, or pan-fry them, these dumplings are sure to satisfy your cravings for a comforting, flavorful dish.

Now that you know how to make your own pork-filled dumplings, why not gather your loved ones and have a dumpling-making session? It’s a fun, interactive activity that will bring everyone together around the table. Happy cooking, and enjoy your homemade dumplings!
