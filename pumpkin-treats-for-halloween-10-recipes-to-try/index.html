Pumpkin Treats for Halloween: 10 Recipes to Try


When the crisp air of fall rolls in and Halloween decorations pop up everywhere, nothing captures the spirit of the season quite like pumpkin treats. The vibrant orange gourd isn’t just for carving jack-o’-lanterns; it’s a versatile ingredient that lends itself beautifully to a variety of sweet and savory dishes. From creamy pumpkin pies to decadent pumpkin-spiced snacks, pumpkin brings warmth, flavor, and festive cheer to every bite.

Whether you’re hosting a Halloween party, looking for cozy desserts to enjoy with family, or simply craving something pumpkin-flavored, these 10 pumpkin recipes will delight your taste buds and make your celebration extra special. Let’s dive in!

1. Classic Pumpkin Pie with a Twist

Forget the basic pumpkin pie; this version adds a subtle ginger and cardamom twist that amplifies the autumnal flavors. The crust is flaky and buttery, with a hint of cinnamon, while the filling is velvety smooth, blending pumpkin puree with brown sugar, warm spices, and a splash of vanilla extract.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust or homemade equivalent
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Fit the pie crust into a 9-inch pie pan. Trim and crimp edges as desired.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together pumpkin puree, brown sugar, eggs, cream, cinnamon, ginger, cardamom, salt, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Pour the filling into the crust and bake for 50-60 minutes or until the center is just set.
  5. Let cool before serving. Top with whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon for that extra touch.

2. Pumpkin Spice Latte Cupcakes

Imagine your favorite coffeehouse drink transformed into a cupcake. These pumpkin spice latte cupcakes offer a tender crumb infused with espresso and pumpkin spices, topped with a mocha frosting that’s both rich and creamy.

Ingredients:

Cupcakes:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup brewed espresso, cooled
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons brewed espresso, cooled
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk espresso, pumpkin puree, eggs, oil, and vanilla.
  4. Gradually add wet ingredients to dry, mixing until combined.
  5. Fill cupcake liners 2/3 full and bake for 18-20 minutes.
  6. Let cool completely before frosting.
  7. For the frosting, beat butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and cocoa powder alternating with espresso. Add vanilla and beat until fluffy.
  8. Pipe or spread frosting over cooled cupcakes.

3. Savory Pumpkin and Sage Soup

Not all pumpkin dishes are sweet. This creamy pumpkin soup, enhanced by earthy sage and a touch of garlic, delivers comfort in a bowl. Perfect as a starter or a light dinner on a chilly Halloween night.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups pumpkin puree
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 2 teaspoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Roasted pumpkin seeds and a drizzle of cream for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in garlic and sage; cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add pumpkin puree and vegetable broth, bring to a simmer.
  5. Let cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Blend the soup until smooth using an immersion blender or normal blender in batches.
  7. Stir in cream and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with roasted pumpkin seeds and a drizzle of cream.

4. Spiced Pumpkin Pancakes

Start your Halloween morning with these fluffy pumpkin pancakes loaded with warming spices. They’re perfect paired with maple syrup and a pat of butter for a festive breakfast that feels indulgent yet wholesome.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, pumpkin spice, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, mix pumpkin puree, milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed; do not overmix.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Grease lightly.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake and cook until bubbles appear on surface, 2-3 minutes.
  6. Flip and cook another 2 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Serve with maple syrup and your favorite toppings.

5. Pumpkin Cheesecake Bars

Blending the creamy tang of cheesecake with the spicy sweetness of pumpkin results in these irresistible bars. A gingersnap crust adds texture and a perfect kick that complements the smooth filling.

Ingredients:

  • Crust:

    • 1 1/2 cups gingersnap crumbs
    • 1/4 cup melted butter

  • Filling:

    • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
    • 1 cup pumpkin puree
    • 3/4 cup sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Mix gingersnap crumbs and melted butter; press into the bottom of the pan.
  3. Beat cream cheese until smooth.
  4. Add sugar, pumpkin, eggs, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla; blend until fully combined.
  5. Pour filling over the crust and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until set around edges but slightly jiggly in the center.
  7. Cool completely before cutting into bars and serving.

6. Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Muffins

These moist muffins burst with pumpkin flavor and studded with gooey chocolate chips, making them a favorite for quick breakfasts or afternoon treats. Easy to bake and perfect for sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line 12 muffin cups with liners.
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking soda, pumpkin pie spice, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine pumpkin puree, eggs, oil, and vanilla.
  4. Stir wet ingredients into dry until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Divide batter evenly into muffin cups and bake 18-22 minutes.
  7. Cool slightly before serving.

7. No-Bake Pumpkin Pie Energy Bites

For a healthy, on-the-go snack with all the flavors of pumpkin pie, these no-bake energy bites combine oats, pumpkin puree, and warm spices into bite-sized treasures. They’re naturally sweetened and perfect for lunchboxes or quick snacks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/4 cup almond butter or peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup mini chocolate chips or chopped nuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl until well mixed.
  2. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up.
  3. Roll mixture into 1-inch balls.
  4. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

8. Pumpkin Garlic Bread

A savory twist on traditional garlic bread, adding pumpkin puree to the dough creates a subtly sweet and moist loaf, perfect for pairing with soups or salads. Fresh rosemary and garlic heighten the flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, yeast, and salt.
  2. Mix pumpkin puree, warm water, and olive oil.
  3. Gradually add wet ingredients to dry, kneading until a smooth dough forms.
  4. Cover and let rise for 1 hour or until doubled.
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Punch dough down, form into a loaf, place on baking sheet.
  7. Mix garlic and rosemary with olive oil and brush over loaf.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden.
  9. Cool before slicing.

9. Pumpkin Cheesecake Dip

A quick, creamy, and crowd-pleasing dessert dip perfect for Halloween parties. Serve with graham crackers, apple slices, or ginger snaps for dunking.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream and chopped pecans for topping (optional)

Directions:

  1. Beat cream cheese until fluffy.
  2. Add pumpkin puree, powdered sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla.
  3. Mix until smooth and creamy.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl and top with whipped cream and pecans.
  5. Chill until ready to serve.

10. Spiced Pumpkin Oatmeal Cookies

Chewy, tender, and full of pumpkin and spice, these oatmeal cookies are perfect for a Halloween treat that’s both satisfying and nostalgic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup raisins or chocolate chips (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment.
  2. In a bowl, mix oats, flour, baking soda, pumpkin spice, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream butter and brown sugar until light.
  4. Add egg, pumpkin puree, and vanilla; mix well.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients.
  6. Stir in raisins or chocolate chips if using.
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto baking sheets.
  8. Bake 12-15 minutes or until edges are golden.
  9. Cool completely on wire racks.

Wrapping up

Pumpkin treats are synonymous with fall celebrations and Halloween fun, offering endless possibilities for both sweet and savory cravings. The warm spices and creamy texture of pumpkin elevate these dishes, making them memorable staples at any festive gathering.

From the classic pie with a twist to innovative snacks and comforting soups, these recipes invite you to explore the rich flavors of pumpkin in delightful ways. Let your kitchen fill with seasonal aromas, and enjoy sharing these pumpkin creations with friends and family as you celebrate the magic of Halloween together. Happy cooking!
