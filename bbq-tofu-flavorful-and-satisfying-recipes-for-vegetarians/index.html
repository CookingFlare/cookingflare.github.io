BBQ Tofu: Flavorful and Satisfying Recipes for Vegetarians


If you’re a vegetarian looking for a flavorful and satisfying BBQ option, look no further than BBQ tofu. Tofu, with its versatile and absorbent nature, is the perfect canvas for all those smoky, tangy, and mouthwatering flavors that we associate with barbecue. Whether you’re grilling outdoors or cooking indoors, these BBQ tofu recipes will surely impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

1. Grilled BBQ Tofu Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu block into 1-inch cubes and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the BBQ sauce, soy sauce, maple syrup, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the tofu cubes to the marinade and gently toss to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Thread the tofu cubes onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  6. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 5 minutes per side, or until the tofu is nicely charred and heated through.
  7. Remove from the grill and let the skewers cool for a few minutes before serving.

2. BBQ Tofu Sliders

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of extra firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1/2 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Slider buns
  • Toppings of your choice (lettuce, tomato, pickles, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the tofu block into thin slices, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the BBQ sauce, vegetable broth, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, smoked paprika, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Dip each tofu slice into the BBQ sauce mixture, making sure to coat both sides.
  5. Place the tofu slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, or until the tofu is golden and crispy around the edges.
  7. Toast the slider buns in the oven for a few minutes, if desired.
  8. Assemble the sliders by placing a BBQ tofu slice on each bun, and adding your favorite toppings.

3. BBQ Tofu Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or agave syrup
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu block into cubes and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the tofu cubes to the skillet and cook until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the bell pepper, onion, broccoli, and snap peas. Cook until the vegetables are tender-crisp, about 5 minutes.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the BBQ sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey or agave syrup, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  6. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the skillet and stir to coat.
  7. Add the cooked tofu back to the skillet and toss everything together until well combined and heated through.
  8. Serve the BBQ tofu stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles.

Note: Feel free to customize these recipes by adding your favorite BBQ spices, herbs, or additional vegetables. The key is to have fun and experiment with different flavors to find your perfect BBQ tofu dish.

Now that you have these flavorful and satisfying BBQ tofu recipes in your arsenal, you can enjoy the deliciousness of barbecue season all year round, vegetarian style. Fire up your grill or heat up your skillet, and get ready to savor the smoky goodness of BBQ tofu. Happy cooking!
