Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon: Irresistibly Sweet and Savory


If you’re searching for a culinary experience that perfectly balances sweetness and umami, look no further than sous vide teriyaki salmon. This dish not only tantalizes the taste buds but also showcases the versatility of sous vide cooking, which ensures that your salmon is cooked to perfection every single time. With its succulent texture and rich flavor, sous vide teriyaki salmon is a dish that will impress your family and friends while being simple enough to whip up on a weeknight.

Understanding Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a bag and immersing it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method allows for even cooking and exceptional flavor retention, making it a favorite among chefs and home cooks alike. The sous vide process makes it nearly impossible to overcook your salmon, resulting in a moist, tender fillet that melts in your mouth.

Benefits of Sous Vide Cooking

  • Precision: Sous vide cooking allows you to control the temperature with incredible accuracy, ensuring your salmon is cooked exactly how you like it.
  • Flavor Infusion: Cooking in a vacuum-sealed bag allows the flavors of your marinade to penetrate the fish deeply, enhancing the overall taste.
  • Convenience: Once your salmon is in the water bath, you can set a timer and focus on other tasks, making it an ideal cooking method for busy weeknights or special occasions.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather our ingredients. For this recipe, you will need:

For the Salmon:

  • 2 salmon fillets, skin-on (approximately 6 ounces each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Teriyaki Marinade:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 2 tablespoons honey (or maple syrup for a vegan option)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 tablespoon water (for thickening)

Optional Garnish:

  • Sliced green onions
  • Sesame seeds
  • Lemon wedges

Preparing Your Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon

Step 1: Make the Marinade

In a small mixing bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger until well combined. Taste the marinade and adjust the sweetness or saltiness according to your preference. If you like a thicker sauce, stir in the cornstarch mixture and set it aside for later use.

Step 2: Marinate the Salmon

Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper on both sides. Place the fillets in a zip-top bag or vacuum-seal bag, and pour the teriyaki marinade over the fish. Seal the bag, ensuring that as much air as possible is removed. If using a zip-top bag, you can use the water displacement method to help remove air: slowly lower the bag into a pot of water, allowing the pressure of the water to push the air out before sealing it completely.

Step 3: Sous Vide Setup

Fill your sous vide container or large pot with water and attach your immersion circulator. Preheat the water to 125°F (52°C) for a medium-rare finish, or 130°F (54°C) for medium doneness. The beauty of sous vide is that you can customize the temperature according to your personal preference.

Step 4: Cook the Salmon

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, submerge the sealed bag containing the salmon in the water bath. Cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour. The longer cooking time will give you more tender salmon, but remember that cooking it for too long might result in a mushy texture.

Step 5: Finish with a Glaze

While your salmon is cooking sous vide, you can prepare a quick teriyaki glaze to enhance the flavor. In a small saucepan, pour in the remaining teriyaki marinade (without the salmon) and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Allow it to cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until it thickens slightly. This will create a beautiful glaze to drizzle over your finished salmon.

Step 6: Sear (Optional)

For a beautiful presentation and added texture, you can finish your salmon with a quick sear. Heat a non-stick skillet or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat and add a small drizzle of oil. Once hot, carefully remove the salmon fillets from the bag and pat them dry with paper towels. Sear the salmon skin-side down for about 1-2 minutes until crispy, then flip and sear the other side for another minute. Be careful not to overcook it during this step.

Serving Your Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon

Once your salmon is perfectly cooked and beautifully glazed, it’s time to serve! Place the fillet on a plate and drizzle some of the thickened teriyaki sauce over the top. Garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds for an added crunch and flavor. A wedge of lemon on the side can add a refreshing touch when squeezed over the fish.

Suggested Pairings

Sous vide teriyaki salmon pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. Consider serving it with:

  • Steamed Rice: The classic pairing, white or brown rice absorbs the delicious teriyaki sauce beautifully.
  • Vegetable Stir-Fry: A medley of colorful vegetables can add texture and nutrition to your meal.
  • Quinoa: For a healthier grain option, quinoa is packed with protein and adds a nutty flavor that complements the salmon.
  • Salad: A fresh green salad with a light vinaigrette can balance the richness of the salmon.

Tips for Perfecting Sous Vide Teriyaki Salmon

  • Quality of Salmon: Always choose high-quality, fresh salmon. The better the fish, the better the dish!
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to play around with the marinade ingredients. Adding a touch of chili paste can give your teriyaki salmon a spicy kick, or you can include other herbs and spices that you enjoy.
  • Cook Time Variations: If you prefer your salmon cooked more thoroughly, you can increase the temperature to 135°F (57°C) for medium-well doneness, but be cautious as it may lose some of its moistness.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat gently in a skillet or in the microwave to avoid overcooking.

Conclusion

Sous vide teriyaki salmon is an irresistible dish that brings together the art of precision cooking and the rich flavors of Japanese cuisine. Whether you’re preparing a weeknight dinner or hosting a gathering, this recipe is sure to impress. With its tender, flaky texture and the delightful balance of sweet and savory flavors, it’s a meal that will leave everyone coming back for seconds.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and dive into the delicious world of sous vide cooking. Your taste buds will thank you!
