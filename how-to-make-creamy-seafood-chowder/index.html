How to Make Creamy Seafood Chowder


There’s something irresistibly comforting about a warm bowl of seafood chowder. The velvety broth, brimming with tender chunks of fresh seafood, potatoes, and vegetables, creates a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that satisfy both the heart and palate. This classic creamy seafood chowder recipe brings the ocean’s bounty right to your kitchen, offering a filling and indulgent dish perfect for cool evenings or any time you crave a bit of coastal charm.

In this comprehensive guide, you’ll discover step-by-step instructions to make a luscious, restaurant-quality creamy seafood chowder at home. From selecting the freshest seafood and building a deeply-flavored base, to achieving the perfect creamy consistency, each component is explained in detail to help you master this dish effortlessly.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Creamy Seafood Chowder

Before diving into cooking, let’s gather all the essentials for this chowder. This recipe balances rich dairy, savory aromatics, fresh seafood, and hearty vegetables to create a well-rounded and indulgent soup.

Fresh Seafood

  • 1/2 pound fresh cod or haddock, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1/2 pound fresh shrimp, peeled, deveined, and tails removed
  • 1/2 pound sea scallops, halved if large

Tip: You can substitute or add other seafood such as crab meat, clams, or lobster depending on availability and preference.

Vegetables and Aromatics

  • 4 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

Starch & Broth

  • 3 medium russet potatoes, peeled and diced into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 4 cups seafood stock or clam juice (or chicken stock if unavailable)
  • 1 cup water (optional for thinning)

Dairy and Flavoring Agents

  • 2 cups half-and-half or heavy cream (adjust based on desired richness)
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour (for thickening)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley and chives for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions to Achieve Perfect Creamy Seafood Chowder

Step 1: Prepare the Base with Bacon and Aromatics

Start by heating a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped bacon and cook it slowly until it renders out its fat and becomes crispy. This process can take about 8 to 10 minutes but is crucial because the bacon fat will infuse the chowder with smoky depth.

Remove the crisp bacon with a slotted spoon and set it aside. Leave the bacon fat in the pot.

Add the diced onion, celery, carrots, and a pinch of salt to the bacon fat. Sauté gently for 6-8 minutes until the vegetables soften and the onion becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant but not browned.

Step 2: Create a Roux and Build the Chowder Base

Melt the butter into the softened vegetable mixture. Sprinkle the flour evenly over the vegetables and butter, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or whisk for about 2-3 minutes. This step cooks out the raw flour taste and starts thickening the base.

Gradually pour in the seafood stock, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Add the bay leaves and thyme, then bring the liquid to a gentle simmer.

Add the diced potatoes and continue simmering uncovered for about 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.

Step 3: Add the Cream and Adjust Texture

Once the potatoes are tender, pour in the half-and-half or cream slowly, stirring to combine. Avoid bringing the chowder to a rapid boil after adding the dairy, as high heat can cause it to curdle. Maintain a low simmer to meld the flavors and gently thicken the soup.

If the chowder feels too thick, stir in a little water or additional stock to reach your preferred consistency.

Step 4: Incorporate the Seafood Gently

Lower the heat to medium-low, and add the seafood—cod chunks, shrimp, and scallops—to the chowder. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring gently, until the seafood is opaque and just cooked through.

Overcooking seafood will toughen it, so keep a keen eye here.

Step 5: Final Seasoning and Serve

Remove the bay leaves, then taste the chowder carefully. Adjust the seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper as needed.

Sprinkle the reserved crispy bacon bits back on top to reintroduce a crunchy texture contrast. Garnish generously with chopped fresh parsley and chives to brighten the rich flavors.

Serve hot with crusty bread or oyster crackers on the side. For an added touch of zest, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice can lift the chowder’s creaminess perfectly.

Tips and Variations

  • Stock choice: If you don’t have seafood stock or clam juice, a good-quality chicken stock works in a pinch. Making your own seafood stock with shrimp shells, fish bones, and aromatics yields the best depth of flavor.
  • Cream alternatives: Half-and-half produces a lighter chowder, while heavy cream delivers luxurious richness. For a dairy-free option, coconut milk can be used, but expect a slightly different flavor profile.
  • Seafood mix: Feel free to use pre-cooked shrimp or crab meat for a quicker meal. Just add them at the very end to warm through.
  • Thickening: If you’d prefer a thicker chowder, mix an additional tablespoon of flour with cold water to make a slurry and stir it in near the end, cooking for a few extra minutes.
  • Herbs and spices: Add a pinch of smoked paprika or a dash of cayenne pepper to introduce subtle heat and smoky tones.

Why This Chowder Stands Out

What makes this creamy seafood chowder so memorable is the harmony between smoky bacon, silky broth, fresh seafood, and tender vegetables. Each element is carefully layered to build complexity and texture.

The slow rendering of bacon fat combined with the roux-based thickening method ensures a luscious mouthfeel. Cooking seafood gently preserves its delicate texture, while fresh herbs add vibrancy to every spoonful.

Whether you’re making this for a special occasion or a cozy weeknight dinner, this chowder is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser that can become a staple in your kitchen repertoire.

Ready to enjoy a bowl of hearty comfort? Follow these steps, and you’ll be indulging in rich, creamy seafood chowder that tastes like it’s straight from a seaside bistro. The perfect blend of fresh ingredients and classic technique elevates this humble chowder to something truly special.

Happy cooking!
