Classic Chicken Fried Steak: A Southern Delicacy


If there’s one dish that screams Southern comfort food, it’s the classic Chicken Fried Steak. This mouthwatering delicacy is a true icon of Southern cuisine, combining tenderized steak, a crispy coating, and a rich, flavorful gravy. In this recipe, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of creating your very own Chicken Fried Steak masterpiece.

Ingredients

  • 4 pieces of cube steak (about 6 ounces each)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • For the gravy:
    • 2 tablespoons butter
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 2 cups milk
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Mix well to create a seasoned flour mixture.

  2. Dip each piece of cube steak into the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off. Then, dredge the steak in the seasoned flour mixture, ensuring it is well-coated on all sides. Set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated cube steaks, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook the steaks for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the skillet and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  4. To make the gravy, melt the butter in a separate saucepan over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour until smooth and bubbly. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Cook the gravy, stirring constantly, until thickened to your desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Serve the Chicken Fried Steak hot, smothered in the creamy gravy. It pairs perfectly with mashed potatoes, green beans, or any other classic Southern sides you prefer.

Tips and Tricks

  • Tenderize the cube steak using a meat mallet or the back of a heavy spoon. This will help break down the tough muscle fibers and ensure a tender, juicy result.
  • For an extra crispy coating, double-dip the steak in the buttermilk and seasoned flour mixture. This will create a thicker crust that fries up beautifully.
  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make your own by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 cup of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
  • To keep the Chicken Fried Steak warm while you’re frying the remaining pieces, place cooked steaks on a wire rack set over a baking sheet in a warm oven (around 200°F).

Conclusion

Classic Chicken Fried Steak is a true Southern delicacy that brings comfort and flavor to your table. With its crispy coating, tenderized steak, and creamy gravy, it’s no wonder this dish has become a staple in Southern households. So, next time you’re craving a taste of the South, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and prepare to indulge in this timeless comfort food classic.
