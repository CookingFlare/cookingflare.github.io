Making Crunchy Chinese Chicken Fingers at Home


There’s something irresistible about the crunch of perfectly fried chicken paired with flavors that dance between sweet, savory, and tangy. Chinese chicken fingers are a popular dish that delivers all of these qualities. Whether served as an appetizer or added to a full meal lineup, these golden strips are sure to impress anyone who takes a bite. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to make crunchy Chinese chicken fingers right in your kitchen. With the right ingredients, techniques, and tips, you’ll master this dish and make it a household favorite.

The Foundation of Crunchy Chinese Chicken Fingers

Chinese chicken fingers differ from classic fried chicken strips in their signature flavor profile and texture. The dish requires tender chicken, seasoned and coated delicately, then fried to perfection. The hallmark of this recipe is its thin, crispy batter, which encapsulates the juicy chicken within. Achieving that ideal crunch depends on precision in preparation — the marinade, coating, and frying techniques all contribute to the final product.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, gather everything you’ll need to ensure your process is smooth and efficient. For this recipe, you’ll be using high-quality, fresh ingredients to achieve authentic flavors.

For the Chicken:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken breast (or chicken tenders)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (light soy sauce is preferred for flavor balance)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste (freshly grated ginger works best)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, for initial coating

For the Batter:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 large egg
  • ⅓ cup cold water (or sparkling water for added crispiness)
  • Pinch of baking powder

For Frying:

  • Neutral oil (vegetable oil, canola oil, or peanut oil)

For Dipping Sauce:

Chinese chicken fingers are often paired with a dipping sauce that perfectly complements their crunch:

  • 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for a spicy kick)

Preparing the Chicken

  1. Select the Right Cuts: Start by choosing boneless, skinless chicken breast or pre-cut chicken tenders. Slice the chicken into thin, even strips about 4–5 inches long. Keeping them uniform in size ensures consistent cooking.

  2. Marinate for Flavor: In a large bowl, combine soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger paste, garlic paste, five-spice powder, and a pinch of salt. Add the chicken strips and toss until evenly coated. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes; however, if you have more time, allowing the chicken to sit for 2–3 hours will deepen the flavors.

  3. Starch Coating: After marinating, lightly toss the chicken strips in cornstarch to absorb excess moisture. This step helps your batter adhere well to the meat.

Crafting the Perfect Batter

The batter is key to achieving that signature crispiness. Follow these steps to create a light yet crunchy coating:

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add cold water and the egg, whisking until you achieve a smooth, lump-free batter. If you’re using sparkling water, the carbon dioxide bubbles will give the batter an airy texture.

  2. Consistency Matters: The batter should be thick enough to coat the chicken without dripping excessively, yet thin enough to create a delicate layer. If needed, adjust the water or add a sprinkle of flour to achieve the right texture.

Frying the Chicken Fingers

For that golden crunch, frying technique is essential. Be sure to take your time and follow these steps:

  1. Heat the Oil: Pour the frying oil into a deep skillet, pan, or wok and heat it to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to monitor the temperature; overheating the oil can cause the outer coating to burn while the chicken remains undercooked.

  2. Dip and Fry: Working in batches, dip the marinated chicken strips into the prepared batter, letting any excess drip off. Gently lower the chicken into the hot oil, taking care not to overcrowd the pan — this ensures the temperature stays consistent and all strips cook evenly.

  3. Cook Until Golden Brown: Fry the chicken for 4–5 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy. Once done, use tongs or a slotted spoon to remove the chicken from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Creating the Perfect Dipping Sauce

While the chicken fingers are cooling slightly, prepare the dipping sauce:

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together hoisin sauce, soy sauce, honey, rice wine vinegar, sesame oil, and chili flakes (optional).
  2. Balance the Flavors: Taste the sauce and adjust it according to preference. If you favor more sweetness, add a touch more honey. If saltiness is key for you, a dash of soy sauce will deepen the flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Your crunchy Chinese chicken fingers are best served fresh and warm. Plate them alongside a small bowl of dipping sauce or drizzle the sauce directly on top. For added flair, sprinkle sesame seeds or chopped green onions over the chicken fingers before serving.

These chicken strips pair wonderfully with side dishes like:

  • Fried rice or garlic noodles
  • Stir-fried vegetables
  • A simple cucumber salad for refreshing balance

Pro Tips for Success

  • Sparkling Water Trick: For extra airy batter, use sparkling water instead of plain cold water.
  • Double Fry Method: For even crispier chicken fingers, fry the strips for 3 minutes at 325°F, remove from the oil, increase the heat to 375°F, and fry them again for another 2 minutes.
  • Maintaining Oil Temperature: Avoid crowding your pan, as this can lower the oil temperature and result in soggy batter. Work in small batches for best results.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, store the fried chicken fingers in an airtight container lined with paper towels to absorb any residual moisture. Keep them refrigerated for up to 3 days. When reheating, use an air fryer or oven to restore the crispiness — microwaving tends to make the batter softer.

Wrapping It Up

Making crunchy Chinese chicken fingers at home is easier than you might think, and the payoff is truly worth the effort. With these detailed instructions and tips, you’ll be able to create restaurant-quality chicken fingers packed with flavor and crunch. Perfect for family dinners, game-day snacking, or as a standout appetizer at gatherings, this dish is bound to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Take the time to experiment with sauces and sides to customize your dish to your liking. Once you’ve mastered the basics, there’s no limit to how creative you can get with this recipe. Happy cooking!
