From Garden to Gut: Utilizing fermentation to make use of surplus produce


Fermentation is an age-old process that has been used for centuries as a means of preserving food and enhancing its flavor. Whether it be kimchi, sauerkraut, or homegrown wine, fermentation is a simple technique that brings a range of delicious results. In a world where gardens may produce more than what’s needed, turning to fermentation can become a game-changer.

Let’s dive into some easy-to-follow fermentation recipes that are perfect for utilizing all that surplus produce:

Fermented Vegetables

There’s nothing better than growing and consuming your own vegetables. However, dealing with too many zuke’s or bell peppers can be overwhelming. Turn to fermentation to preserve and enhance these vegetables. Taking advantage of some basic equipment and ingredients like salt and vinegar, you can transform a surplus into a tastefully tangy meal:

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 lbs of washed and chopped vegetables (cucumbers, bell peppers, or any other surplus)
  • 1 tbsp of pickling salt
  • 1 tsp of black pepper
  • Optional: 1/4 cup of vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your fermentation jars.
  2. Layer vegetables and salt in the jar, ensuring every vegetable is contact with salt.
  3. Leave some room for water weight.
  4. Add black pepper for seasoning.
  5. Optional - add vinegar to the jar for added preservation.

Keep the jars in a cool dark place for several days to a few weeks, depending on taste.

Immortal Ginger Beer with Kombu

Having more ginger than the recipes call for? Enter ginger beer - atermore refreshing beverage that you’ll love to have on hand. And, the bonus is you can make it immortal.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 lbs of fresh ginger
  • 3-4 kombu (seaweed)
  • 1/2 tsp of yeast
  • Spring water (tap or distilled)

Instructions:

  1. Chop fresh ginger into roughly bite-sized pieces.
  2. Soak your kombu in water overnight.
  3. Mix your ginger, kombu, and spring water in a container.
  4. Stir in yeast and cover.
  5. Keep at room temperature, stirring daily.
  6. When the ginger beer reaches your desired flavor, move it to the fridge. It will continue to ferment at a slower pace, making it immortal.

Microgreen-Mixed Salsa

Microgreens have become the trendy item in kitchens across the globe. Ferment them, and you’ve got yourself a unique condiment.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 cups of washed and chopped microgreens (including surplus)
  • 1 cup of chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of onions
  • 2 tbsp of spices (diced jalapeño, cilantro, garlic, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Combine all the ingredients in a mix.
  2. Add water and salt to create a salsa that can be fermented.
  3. Keep in a jar covered with a lid in the fridge for a few days before enjoying.

In conclusion, fermentation can be a revolutionary technique for our gardens and our taste buds. With the usual ingredients in your pantry, you can create tasty dishes while keeping food waste to a minimum. And once you get started, you’ll wonder how you ever went without these delectable treats.
