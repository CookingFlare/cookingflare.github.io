How to Make Delicious Mexican Stuffed Peppers


Are you looking for a flavorful and healthy meal option that's perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion? Look no further than Mexican stuffed peppers, a classic dish that combines the sweetness of roasted peppers with the bold flavors of Mexican cuisine. Ingredients To make this recipe, you'll need the following ingredients:

4 large bell peppers, any color 1 pound ground beef or ground turkey 1 medium onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon chili powder 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1 cup cooked rice 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed 1 cup shredded cheese (Monterey Jack or Cheddar work well) 1 tablespoon olive oil Optional: diced tomatoes, chopped fresh cilantro, sour cream, or salsa for topping

Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Peppers Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the peppers, removing the seeds and membranes. Place them in a baking dish and drizzle with a little olive oil. Roast the peppers in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until they're tender and slightly charred. Step 2: Cook the Filling While the peppers are roasting, cook the ground beef or turkey in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Once browned, add the diced onion and cook until it's translucent. Add the garlic, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly. Step 3: Add the Rice and Beans Stir in the cooked rice and black beans, and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the filling is well combined. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Step 4: Assemble the Peppers Once the peppers are done roasting, scoop out a little of the flesh to make room for the filling. Fill each pepper with the meat and rice mixture, and top with shredded cheese. Step 5: Bake the Peppers Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake the peppers for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Step 6: Top and Serve Remove the foil and top each pepper with any desired toppings, such as diced tomatoes, chopped cilantro, sour cream, or salsa. Serve the peppers hot, garnished with a sprinkle of cilantro or a dollop of sour cream. Tips and Variations

For an added boost of flavor, add some diced jalapeños or serrano peppers to the filling. If you prefer a vegetarian option, skip the ground meat and add some roasted vegetables or beans to the filling instead. Experiment with different types of cheese, such as Queso Fresco or Pepper Jack. For a fun twist, make individual mini stuffed peppers using small bell peppers.

Conclusion Delicious Mexican stuffed peppers are a flavorful and healthy meal option that's perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. With their combination of roasted peppers, savory meat and rice filling, and creamy cheese, they're sure to become a family favorite. Try them tonight and enjoy the fiesta! Ingredients
