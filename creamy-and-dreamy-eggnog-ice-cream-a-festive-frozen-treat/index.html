Creamy and Dreamy Eggnog Ice Cream: A Festive Frozen Treat


Winter is in the air, and with it comes the festive spirit of the holiday season. What better way to get into the holiday mood than with a creamy and dreamy eggnog ice cream that’s sure to become a new family favorite?

Eggnog is a classic holiday drink that’s rich in history and flavor. Made with milk, cream, sugar, eggs, and spices, it’s a delicious and comforting treat that’s perfect for sipping on its own or incorporating into recipes. And what better way to enjoy eggnog than in a cold and creamy ice cream?

In this recipe, we’ll be using a combination of heavy cream, whole milk, and eggnog to create a rich and creamy ice cream base. We’ll also be adding in some egg yolks to give the ice cream a smooth and velvety texture. And of course, we can’t forget the spices - a pinch of nutmeg and a dash of cinnamon will add a warm and comforting flavor to our eggnog ice cream.

So, without further ado, let’s get started on this festive frozen treat!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until well combined. Set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, eggnog, and kosher salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture reaches a temperature of 170°F.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and slowly pour the warm mixture into the bowl with the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  4. Return the saucepan to the heat and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the ground nutmeg and ground cinnamon.
  6. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  7. Once the mixture is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  8. Once the ice cream is churned, transfer it to an airtight container and cover with plastic wrap or aluminum foil. Place in the freezer to harden for at least 2 hours.

Tips and Variations:

  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can also place the chilled mixture in a 9x13 inch baking dish and freeze for about 2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes until the desired consistency is reached.
  • To add a little extra flavor to your eggnog ice cream, you can stir in some chopped nuts or chocolate chips after the ice cream has been churned.
  • If you want to make your eggnog ice cream even more festive, you can add some crushed candy canes or holiday sprinkles to the mixture before freezing.

And that’s it! With these simple steps and ingredients, you can create a creamy and dreamy eggnog ice cream that’s sure to be a hit at your holiday gatherings. So go ahead, get creative, and make this festive frozen treat a part of your holiday traditions. Happy holidays!
