30-Minute Mongolian Beef Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re a fan of Asian cuisine but don’t always have the time to prepare elaborate meals, then this 30-minute Mongolian beef stir-fry recipe is just what you need. Packed with savory flavors and tender beef, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your weeknight dinner rotation. Best of all, it’s simple to make and requires just a few key ingredients. Let’s dive into the recipe and get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • Cooked white or brown rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, coat the thinly sliced flank steak with cornstarch until all pieces are evenly coated. Set aside.

  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the coated beef slices in a single layer and cook until browned and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side. You may need to work in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Once cooked, transfer the beef to a plate and set aside.

  3. In the same pan, add the minced garlic and grated ginger, stirring constantly for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  4. Pour in the low-sodium soy sauce, water, and brown sugar, stirring to combine. Allow the sauce to come to a simmer and thicken slightly, about 2-3 minutes.

  5. Return the cooked beef to the pan, tossing to coat it evenly in the flavorful sauce. Let the beef and sauce simmer together for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  6. Once the beef is fully coated and the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency, remove the pan from the heat.

  7. Garnish the Mongolian beef stir-fry with chopped green onions for a fresh, zesty finish.

  8. Serve the stir-fry over a bed of steamed white or brown rice, allowing the tender beef and savory sauce to mingle with the fluffy grains.

Tips for Success

  • To ensure the beef stays tender, be sure not to overcook it during the initial browning stage. The cornstarch coating helps create a crispy exterior while locking in the beef’s natural juices.

  • Feel free to customize the level of sweetness in the sauce by adjusting the amount of brown sugar to suit your taste preferences.

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a drizzle of sriracha to the sauce mixture.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a pan or microwave for a quick and satisfying meal.

Final Thoughts

This 30-minute Mongolian beef stir-fry recipe offers a delightful balance of savory, sweet, and umami flavors, making it a perfect choice for busy weeknights or casual dinner gatherings. With its simplicity and bold taste, this dish is sure to impress your family and friends without requiring hours of preparation. So, the next time you’re craving the bold flavors of Asian cuisine but are short on time, give this recipe a try and savor every bite!
