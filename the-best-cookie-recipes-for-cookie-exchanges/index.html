The Best Cookie Recipes for Cookie Exchanges


Cookie exchanges are one of the sweetest traditions, especially around the holidays. They’re a chance to share your best baking efforts with friends, discover new favorites from other bakers, and fill your home with the unbeatable aroma of freshly baked cookies. Whether you’re preparing for a neighborhood gathering, a work party, or just a fun way to celebrate with friends, having a variety of delicious and impressive cookie recipes is key to making your contribution stand out.

In this blog post, I’ll share an assortment of the best cookie recipes for cookie exchanges — from timeless classics to inventive treats. These recipes are sure to spark joy and turn your cookie platter into the star of the party.

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

When it comes to cookie exchanges, starting with a crowd favorite is always a smart idea. Chocolate chip cookies are universally loved, and this recipe produces the perfect balance of chewy and gooey texture. The secret lies in the combination of melted butter and just the right proportion of brown sugar, giving these cookies a deep, buttery flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth. Stir in vanilla extract and eggs; mix until well blended.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until edges are golden.
  6. Cool on wire racks and pack them up for your exchange in decorative tins or boxes.

2. Snickerdoodles

Snickerdoodles are the kind of cookie that brings nostalgia to the table. Their cinnamon-sugar coating and tangy flavor make them a unique addition to any cookie exchange. They’re simple to make but endlessly satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for coating)
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare your baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the 1/4 cup granulated sugar and cinnamon. Set aside.
  3. In a medium bowl, sift together flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Using a hand or stand mixer, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla extract and mix until combined.
  5. Gradually mix the dry ingredients into the wet, forming a soft dough.
  6. Roll the dough into small balls, then coat each one with the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  7. Place balls on the baking sheet, leaving room between cookies. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Let cool completely before packaging for the exchange.

3. Peppermint White Chocolate Cookies

For a festive twist, peppermint white chocolate cookies pack a refreshing punch. These cookies are perfect for holiday-themed cookie exchanges and will stand out for their colorful and eye-catching appearance.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp peppermint extract
  • 1 cup white chocolate chips
  • 1 cup crushed candy canes (or peppermint candies)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. Cream the butter with granulated sugar and brown sugar until fluffy. Add eggs, vanilla extract, and peppermint extract. Mix well.
  4. Gradually combine the dry ingredients with the wet. Stir in the white chocolate chips and crushed candy canes.
  5. Drop rounded mounds onto the baking sheets and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  6. Allow cookies to cool before transferring to airtight containers for the exchange.

4. Thumbprint Cookies with Jam

Elegant and versatile, thumbprint cookies are a favorite for cookie exchanges. Fill them with your choice of fruit preserves, such as raspberry, apricot, or strawberry, for a touch of sweetness and texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup fruit preserves (or jam of choice)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine flour and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter and sugar in a large bowl. Mix in vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the flour mixture, mixing until a soft dough forms.
  5. Roll the dough into balls and place them on the baking sheets. Use your thumb or the back of a spoon to create an indentation in each cookie.
  6. Fill each indentation with a dab of jam. Bake for 12-15 minutes. Cool completely before storing them for the exchange.

5. Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Chocolate crinkle cookies are rich, fudgy, and visually stunning. Their powdered sugar coating gives them a “crackled” appearance that makes them irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. In another bowl, mix granulated sugar and oil. Add eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla extract.
  2. Gradually stir dry ingredients into the wet mixture until combined. Refrigerate dough for at least 3 hours.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  4. Roll dough into small balls and coat them thoroughly with confectioners’ sugar.
  5. Arrange cookies on sheets and bake for 10-12 minutes. Cool before serving or gifting.

Conclusion

Cookie exchanges are a delightful way to celebrate togetherness and enjoy an array of homemade treats. With recipes ranging from classic chocolate chip to seasonal peppermint white chocolate, you’ll have a cookie for every taste and occasion. The beauty of these recipes lies not only in their flavors but also in their ability to spark connection and joy.

Make every cookie exchange memorable by experimenting with flavors, presenting your cookies in unique packaging, and sharing the stories behind your creations. Happy baking!
