Decadent Chocolate Pumpkin Pie Recipe for Thanksgiving


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to take your Thanksgiving dessert game to a whole new level with a decadent chocolate pumpkin pie recipe. This indulgent twist on the classic pumpkin pie combines the rich flavors of chocolate with the creamy sweetness of pumpkin, resulting in a dessert that will have your guests coming back for seconds (and maybe even thirds!).

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups of chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 6 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar

For the Filling:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) of pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 6 ounces of semisweet chocolate, chopped

For the Whipped Cream Topping:

  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the chocolate cookie crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar. Mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie dish. Use the back of a spoon or a measuring cup to firmly press the crumbs into place.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, brown sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt until smooth.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the chopped chocolate and a splash of heavy cream over low heat, stirring constantly until the chocolate is melted and the mixture is smooth.
  3. Pour the melted chocolate into the pumpkin mixture and whisk until well combined.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Pour the chocolate pumpkin filling into the cooled crust, spreading it out evenly.
  2. Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center still has a slight jiggle.
  3. Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool completely at room temperature. Then, refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to set.

Step 4: Prepare the Whipped Cream Topping

  1. In a large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract together until soft peaks form.
  2. Spread or pipe the whipped cream over the chilled pie before serving.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the pie is chilled and the whipped cream is added, it’s time to slice and serve! This decadent chocolate pumpkin pie is best enjoyed with a cup of freshly brewed coffee or a dollop of whipped cream on top. The combination of rich chocolate and spicy pumpkin flavors is sure to be a hit at your Thanksgiving gathering.

So, this Thanksgiving, wow your family and friends with this irresistible dessert that perfectly balances the warmth of pumpkin with the indulgence of chocolate. Happy baking and happy Thanksgiving!
