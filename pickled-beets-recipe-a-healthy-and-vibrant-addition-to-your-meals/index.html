Pickled Beets Recipe: A Healthy and Vibrant Addition to Your Meals


Pickled beets are a versatile, delicious, and healthy addition to your meals. They’re packed with nutrients, full of flavor, and incredibly easy to make. If you’ve ever tried them, you know they can bring a burst of color and tang to almost any dish. Whether you add them to salads, sandwiches, or simply enjoy them as a side, pickled beets elevate your eating experience with their vibrant hue and bold taste.

In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to create the perfect pickled beets at home. From selecting the best beets to preparing and storing them, we’ve got all the details covered. Let’s dive into the world of pickled beets!

Why Pickled Beets Are a Must-Try

Beets, in general, are a powerhouse of nutrients. They’re rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals such as folate, manganese, and potassium. When pickled, beets not only retain their nutritional value but also gain an irresistible tang that makes them an ideal accompaniment to a wide variety of dishes.

Pickling beets also adds an extra layer of health benefits. The process of pickling involves adding vinegar, which is known for its ability to balance the body’s pH levels and support digestion. Furthermore, pickled beets offer a natural source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them a great addition to any health-conscious meal plan.

Let’s take a look at the step-by-step guide on how to make your own pickled beets at home!

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we begin the process, here’s what you’ll need to make the perfect pickled beets:

For the Beets:

  • 6 medium-sized beets (choose firm, unblemished beets)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil (for roasting)

For the Pickling Solution:

  • 1 ½ cups of white vinegar (or apple cider vinegar for a milder taste)
  • ½ cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar (optional, depending on your sweetness preference)
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves of garlic (peeled and smashed)
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds (optional for a spicy kick)
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander seeds (optional for added flavor complexity)
  • Fresh herbs, such as dill or thyme (optional, for added aromatic layers)

Equipment:

  • A medium-sized pot for boiling the pickling liquid
  • A large bowl for the beets
  • A jar with a tight-fitting lid for storing the pickled beets

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Pickled Beets

Step 1: Prepare and Roast the Beets

The first step is to prepare the beets. If you’re using fresh beets, it’s best to roast them for maximum flavor and sweetness. Start by washing the beets thoroughly under cool running water to remove any dirt. Trim off the greens, leaving about an inch of the stem attached to avoid bleeding the color during the roasting process.

Next, drizzle the beets with olive oil and wrap them individually in aluminum foil. Place the foil-wrapped beets on a baking sheet and roast them in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 45 minutes to an hour. The beets are done when a fork easily pierces through them.

Once they’re roasted, remove the beets from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before handling them. To peel them, simply rub the skin off with your hands or use a paper towel. The skins should come off easily, leaving you with vibrant, tender beetroot.

Step 2: Prepare the Pickling Solution

While the beets are roasting, it’s time to prepare the pickling solution. In a medium-sized pot, combine the vinegar, water, sugar, salt, peppercorns, garlic, mustard seeds, and coriander seeds. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally to ensure the sugar and salt dissolve completely.

Let the pickling solution simmer for about 5-10 minutes. The aromas from the spices and herbs will begin to fill your kitchen, giving you a preview of the deliciously tangy flavor that awaits!

Once the solution is ready, remove the pot from the heat and set it aside to cool slightly. If you prefer a spicier or more robust flavor, you can allow the pickling liquid to steep for a longer period, even overnight, to let the flavors meld.

Step 3: Slice the Beets

After the beets have cooled, slice them into your desired shape. Some people prefer thin slices, while others enjoy chunky wedges or even matchsticks. The shape and thickness of the slices depend on how you plan to use the pickled beets—thin slices are perfect for salads, while wedges are ideal as a side dish or topping for a sandwich.

Feel free to experiment with different cuts to suit your culinary needs.

Step 4: Pack the Beets in Jars

Once the beets are sliced, it’s time to pack them into jars. Choose clean, sterilized jars that are large enough to hold the beet slices and the pickling solution. If you prefer a more concentrated flavor, you can pack the beets tightly, but ensure there’s enough room for the liquid to cover them completely.

Add any optional herbs like dill or thyme between the layers of beets to enhance the flavor. If you like your pickled beets to have an additional burst of flavor, consider adding some fresh chili peppers or a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes for heat.

Step 5: Pour the Pickling Solution

Pour the cooled pickling solution over the beets in the jar, making sure the beets are fully submerged. You can use a spoon or a small weight to press the beets down, ensuring that they remain submerged in the liquid. Seal the jars with airtight lids and let them sit at room temperature for about 24 hours to allow the flavors to develop.

Step 6: Refrigerate and Enjoy

Once the beets have had time to pickle, place the jars in the refrigerator to continue marinating. The pickled beets will be ready to enjoy within 48 hours, but for the best flavor, let them sit for at least 3-4 days. They can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2-3 weeks.

How to Enjoy Pickled Beets

Pickled beets are incredibly versatile and can be used in a variety of ways. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

1. Add Them to Salads

Pickled beets add a vibrant pop of color and tang to any salad. Their earthy flavor pairs well with greens like arugula, spinach, or mixed lettuce. For an extra touch, add some goat cheese, walnuts, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze. The contrast of flavors will elevate your salad and give it a more complex taste.

2. Layer Them on Sandwiches and Wraps

Pickled beets can be used as a unique addition to sandwiches and wraps. Whether you’re making a classic turkey sandwich or a vegetarian wrap, the sweet and tangy beets will add a burst of flavor that complements almost any filling. Try pairing them with cream cheese or hummus for an extra layer of creaminess.

3. Serve Them as a Side Dish

If you’re looking for a simple and flavorful side, pickled beets can stand on their own. Serve them chilled alongside roasted meats, grilled vegetables, or even alongside some fresh bread and cheese. The acidity from the vinegar can also help balance out richer, heavier dishes.

4. Enjoy Them as a Topping for Tacos or Bowls

For a fun twist on tacos or grain bowls, add pickled beets as a topping. Their bright color and tangy flavor will complement the spices and other fresh ingredients, making each bite more exciting and flavorful.

5. Mix Them Into a Grain Salad

Incorporating pickled beets into a grain salad like quinoa or couscous adds texture and color. Pair the beets with ingredients like feta cheese, olives, cucumbers, and fresh herbs to create a refreshing, nutrient-packed salad that can be served as a main dish or a side.

Tips for Perfect Pickled Beets Every Time

  • Freshness is Key: Always use fresh, firm beets for the best texture and flavor. Avoid beets that are soft or have blemishes.
  • Customize the Pickling Solution: Adjust the amount of sugar, salt, or vinegar based on your taste preferences. If you prefer a sweeter flavor, increase the sugar, or if you prefer a more sour profile, add a bit more vinegar.
  • Experiment with Spices and Herbs: While mustard seeds, coriander, and garlic are classic ingredients, feel free to get creative. Dill, star anise, or even cinnamon can be used to impart different flavors to your pickled beets.
  • Sterilize Your Jars: Properly sterilize the jars to ensure a longer shelf life and to avoid contamination. Boiling the jars in water for 10 minutes before filling them will do the trick.
  • Give It Time: Though pickled beets are edible right away, the flavors develop even more after a few days in the refrigerator. Be patient for the best results!

Conclusion

Pickled beets are a flavorful, healthy, and vibrant addition to any meal. They bring a perfect balance of sweetness and tang, and their deep red hue brightens up your plate. Whether you’re looking to add a zesty twist to salads, sandwiches, or grain bowls, homemade pickled beets can do it all. With just a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can make your own pickled beets at home and enjoy the taste of this nutritious, vibrant vegetable year-round.

Give this recipe a try and enjoy the delightful crunch and complex flavor of pickled beets!
