An Aphrodisiac Affair: Sensuous Valentine's Day Dinner Recipes


This Valentine’s Day, treat your loved one to a culinary experience that will leave them weak in the knees. Our aphrodisiac-inspired dinner recipes are designed to ignite the flames of passion and create a romantic atmosphere that will make the evening unforgettable.

Starter: Oysters Rockefeller

Oysters have long been considered an aphrodisiac, and for good reason. These succulent morsels are packed with zinc, which is essential for testosterone production and a healthy libido. To make Oysters Rockefeller, start by shucking 12 oysters and placing them on a bed of rock salt. Top each oyster with a spoonful of spinach and garlic butter, followed by a sprinkle of parmesan cheese. Bake in a preheated oven at 400°F for 10-12 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Main Course: Filet Mignon with Red Wine Reduction

Filet mignon is a classic aphrodisiac ingredient, and when paired with a rich red wine reduction, it’s sure to impress. To make this dish, start by seasoning 4 filet mignon steaks with salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika. Sear the steaks in a hot skillet for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness. Remove the steaks from the skillet and set them aside to rest. Reduce 1 cup of red wine by half, then add in 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard. Simmer the sauce until it thickens, then serve it over the filet mignon.

Side Dish: Asparagus with Lemon Butter

Asparagus is a natural aphrodisiac, and when paired with a tangy lemon butter, it’s the perfect side dish for our Valentine’s Day dinner. To make this dish, start by trimming 1 pound of fresh asparagus and placing it on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast in a preheated oven at 425°F for 12-15 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender. Meanwhile, mix together 2 tablespoons of softened butter, 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice, and 1 clove of minced garlic. Brush the lemon butter over the asparagus during the last 2 minutes of cooking.

Dessert: Chocolate Lava Cake

Chocolate is a classic aphrodisiac ingredient, and when paired with a rich, gooey center, it’s sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. To make these decadent lava cakes, start by preheating your oven to 425°F. In a medium bowl, whisk together 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, and 2 tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder. In a separate bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1/2 cup of whole milk, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Pour the batter into 4 ramekins and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are set and the centers are still slightly jiggly. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Wine Pairing Suggestions

  • Oysters Rockefeller: Sauvignon Blanc or Sparkling wine
  • Filet Mignon with Red Wine Reduction: Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot
  • Asparagus with Lemon Butter: Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc
  • Chocolate Lava Cake: Port wine or Tawny port

With these recipes, you’ll be sure to impress and leave a lasting impression on your loved one. Bon appétit!
