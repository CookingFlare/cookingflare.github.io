Greek Lamb Kofta: A Spicy and Flavorful Grilled Delight


When it comes to grilled meat, few options can rival the bold flavors and textures of Greek lamb kofta. These juicy, flavorful cylinders of ground lamb are a staple of Greek cuisine, and for good reason. With their perfect balance of spices, herbs, and richness, they’re sure to delight even the most discerning palates. In this recipe, we’ll explore the secrets to making the perfect Greek lamb kofta, and share some tips for grilling them to perfection.

The Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients we’ll need. For this dish, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice

The Preparation

To make the kofta, start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. While it’s heating up, combine the ground lamb, breadcrumbs, parsley, garlic, olive oil, paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Mix everything together with your hands or a wooden spoon until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the mixture, as this can make the kofta dense and tough.

Once the mixture is just combined, use your hands to shape it into small cylinders, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter and 3-4 inches long. You should end up with around 8-10 kofta.

The Grilling

Once the grill is hot, place the kofta on the grates, leaving about 1 inch of space between each one. Close the lid and cook for 4-5 minutes, or until the edges start to sear and the surface is nicely charred.

After 4-5 minutes, flip the kofta over and cook for an additional 4-5 minutes, or until they’re cooked through to your desired level of doneness.

The Finishing Touches

Once the kofta are cooked, remove them from the grill and brush them with the red wine vinegar and lemon juice mixture. This will add a bright, tangy flavor to the kofta and help to balance out the richness of the lamb.

Serving Suggestions

Greek lamb kofta are incredibly versatile, and can be served in a variety of ways. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Serve them as an appetizer or snack, with a side of tzatziki sauce or a simple green salad.
  • Add them to a Greek-inspired salad, with mixed greens, feta cheese, olives, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  • Serve them as a main course, with a side of roasted vegetables or a simple grain like quinoa or couscous.
  • Use them as a topping for a Greek-style pizza, with feta cheese, kalamata olives, and sun-dried tomatoes.

Conclusion

Greek lamb kofta are a delicious and flavorful addition to any meal, and with this recipe, you’ll be well on your way to making them like a pro. Whether you’re serving them as an appetizer or a main course, these spicy and flavorful grilled delights are sure to impress. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in the bold flavors of Greece!
