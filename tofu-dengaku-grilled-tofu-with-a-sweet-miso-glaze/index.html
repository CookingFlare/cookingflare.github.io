Tofu Dengaku: Grilled Tofu with a Sweet Miso Glaze


If you’re a fan of tofu and are looking for a flavorful and satisfying dish to add to your repertoire, look no further than Tofu Dengaku. This Japanese delicacy features grilled tofu with a delicious sweet miso glaze that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a tofu enthusiast or a curious foodie, this recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

To create this delectable dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 3 tablespoons of white miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons of mirin
  • 2 tablespoons of sake
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tofu. Gently remove the tofu from its packaging and drain any excess water. Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel and place it on a cutting board. Put a heavy object, such as a plate or a book, on top of the tofu to press out the remaining liquid. Let it sit for about 15 minutes to ensure a firmer texture.

  2. While the tofu is being pressed, it’s time to prepare the sweet miso glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the white miso paste, mirin, sake, and sugar. Stir the ingredients together over low heat until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture becomes smooth. Remove the saucepan from the heat and set it aside.

  3. Once the tofu has been pressed, cut it into rectangular slices, about ½ inch thick. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the tofu slices with vegetable oil to prevent sticking.

  4. Carefully place the tofu slices on the preheated grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until grill marks appear and the tofu is heated through.

  5. With the tofu nicely grilled, it’s time to add the sweet miso glaze. Using a brush or a spoon, generously coat each side of the tofu slices with the glaze. Return the tofu to the grill and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes on each side, allowing the glaze to caramelize slightly.

  6. Once the tofu is beautifully glazed, remove it from the grill and transfer it to a serving plate. Sprinkle the finely chopped green onion on top for a touch of freshness and color.

  7. Serve your Tofu Dengaku immediately while it’s still warm. It makes for a fantastic appetizer, a delightful addition to a bento box, or a flavorful main course when paired with a bowl of steamed rice and some crisp vegetables.

Final Thoughts

Tofu Dengaku is an elegant yet straightforward dish that showcases the versatility of tofu. The combination of the grilled tofu’s smoky flavor and the sweet umami notes of the miso glaze creates a harmonious balance that will leave you craving more. With just a few ingredients and simple steps, you can bring this traditional Japanese delight to your own kitchen.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something unique and delicious, give Tofu Dengaku a try. You’ll be pleasantly surprised by the complex flavors and the sheer enjoyment that this dish brings. Happy cooking!
