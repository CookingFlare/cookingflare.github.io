Sweets and Suds: Dessert Pairings with Craft Beer


Craft beer isn’t just for savory dishes and barbecues. It can also be a delightful companion to sweet treats and desserts. The complex flavors and aromas of craft beer can balance, enhance, or contrast with the sweetness of various desserts, creating a unique and memorable dining experience. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious dessert pairings with craft beer that will tantalize your taste buds and elevate your culinary adventures.

Chocolate Stout Cake with Raspberry Lambic

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup stout beer
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3/4 cup sour cream
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Fresh raspberries for garnish
  • Raspberry lambic beer for pairing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a cake pan.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the stout beer and butter until the butter is melted.
  3. Whisk in the cocoa powder and sugar until smooth.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the sour cream, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  5. Combine the beer mixture with the sour cream mixture.
  6. Sift in the flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix until just combined.
  7. Pour the batter into the cake pan and bake for 45-50 minutes.
  8. Let the cake cool before garnishing with fresh raspberries.
  9. Serve slices of chocolate stout cake with a chilled raspberry lambic beer for a decadent and fruity pairing.

Salted Caramel Brownies with Coffee Porter

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup caramel sauce
  • Flaky sea salt for sprinkling
  • Coffee porter beer for pairing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking pan.
  2. Melt the butter in a saucepan and stir in the sugar until combined.
  3. Remove from heat and whisk in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. Stir in the cocoa powder, salt, and flour until smooth.
  5. Pour half of the batter into the baking pan and drizzle caramel sauce over it.
  6. Top with the remaining batter and swirl with a knife to create a marbled effect.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, then sprinkle with flaky sea salt.
  8. Let the brownies cool before cutting into squares.
  9. Enjoy these indulgent salted caramel brownies with a rich and robust coffee porter for a perfect balance of sweetness and bitterness.

Lemon Ricotta Cheesecake with Belgian Tripel

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 16 oz cream cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • Zest and juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Belgian tripel beer for pairing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a springform pan.
  2. Mix graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter, then press into the bottom of the pan.
  3. In a bowl, beat cream cheese, ricotta cheese, and sugar until smooth.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then mix in lemon zest, juice, and vanilla extract.
  5. Pour the mixture over the crust and bake for 50-60 minutes.
  6. Let the cheesecake cool before refrigerating for at least 4 hours.
  7. Serve slices of lemon ricotta cheesecake with a Belgian tripel beer for a tangy and effervescent pairing.

Craft beer and desserts may seem like an unconventional combination, but when paired thoughtfully, they can create harmonious flavor profiles that elevate both the beer and the sweet treats. Next time you’re hosting a dinner party or simply indulging in a nightcap, consider exploring the delightful world of dessert pairings with craft beer. Cheers to delicious combinations and culinary adventures!
