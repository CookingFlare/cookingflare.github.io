Summer Grilling: Recipes with Seasonal Seafood


As the summer sun warms the air and invites us outdoors, there’s no better time to fire up the grill and indulge in the bounty of seasonal seafood. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a fresh and flavorful meal, grilling seafood brings out its natural sweetness and adds a delightful smoky char that enhances every bite. From succulent shrimp skewers to delicate fish fillets, here are some delectable recipes to inspire your summer grilling adventures.

Grilled Lemon Garlic Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • Fresh large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic, minced
  • Lemon zest and juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated shrimp onto soaked wooden skewers.
  5. Grill the shrimp skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are opaque and cooked through.
  6. Serve immediately with additional lemon wedges for squeezing over the shrimp.

These grilled lemon garlic shrimp skewers are perfect as an appetizer or main dish, boasting a burst of citrusy flavors complemented by the smoky notes from the grill.

Grilled Salmon with Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • Fresh salmon fillets
  • Butter, softened
  • Fresh herbs (such as dill, parsley, and chives), chopped
  • Lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together softened butter, chopped fresh herbs, lemon zest, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels and generously spread the herb butter mixture over the top of each fillet.
  4. Place the salmon fillets on the grill, skin-side down if applicable.
  5. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side, or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and is cooked to your desired doneness.
  6. Remove from the grill and let the salmon rest for a few minutes before serving.

Grilled salmon with herb butter is a classic summer dish that showcases the rich flavors of salmon enhanced by the aromatic herb butter, creating a dish that’s both elegant and satisfying.

Grilled Garlic Butter Lobster Tails

Ingredients:

  • Lobster tails, split in half lengthwise
  • Butter, melted
  • Garlic, minced
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Paprika
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine melted butter, minced garlic, fresh lemon juice, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the cut side of the lobster tails generously with the garlic butter mixture.
  4. Place the lobster tails, cut-side down, on the grill.
  5. Grill for 5-6 minutes, basting occasionally with the remaining garlic butter mixture, until the lobster meat is opaque and cooked through.
  6. Serve the grilled lobster tails with additional lemon wedges and melted garlic butter on the side.

Grilled garlic butter lobster tails are a luxurious treat that embodies the essence of summer grilling, offering tender lobster meat infused with the flavors of garlic and butter.

Grilled Swordfish Steaks with Citrus Salsa

Ingredients:

  • Swordfish steaks
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Citrus Salsa:

  • Oranges, segmented and chopped
  • Grapefruit, segmented and chopped
  • Red onion, finely chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced
  • Lime juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub swordfish steaks with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill swordfish steaks for 3-4 minutes per side, or until grill marks form and the fish is cooked through.
  4. While the swordfish is grilling, prepare the citrus salsa by combining oranges, grapefruit, red onion, cilantro, jalapeño pepper, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Serve grilled swordfish steaks topped with citrus salsa, offering a refreshing contrast to the smoky grilled fish.

Grilled swordfish steaks with citrus salsa offer a vibrant and zesty twist on traditional grilled seafood, celebrating the bright flavors of summer fruits paired with perfectly grilled swordfish.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Seafood

  • Preheat the Grill: Ensure your grill is properly preheated to the appropriate temperature to achieve those desirable grill marks and even cooking.
  • Oil Your Grill Grates: Prevent seafood from sticking by brushing the grill grates with oil before placing the fish or shrimp on the grill.
  • Use High-Quality Seafood: Fresh, high-quality seafood will deliver the best results when grilling, so visit your local fish market or seafood counter for the freshest catches.
  • Experiment with Marinades and Rubs: Enhance the flavors of seafood with marinades, rubs, and herb butters that complement rather than overpower the delicate taste of fish and shellfish.
  • Monitor Cooking Time: Seafood cooks quickly on the grill, so keep a close eye on it to prevent overcooking, which can result in dry or tough texture.

Embrace Summer with Grilled Seafood Delights

Summer grilling is synonymous with enjoying the great outdoors and savoring the flavors of the season. Whether you’re a seafood aficionado or new to grilling fish and shellfish, these recipes offer a delicious starting point for creating memorable meals. From succulent shrimp skewers to tender swordfish steaks, each dish celebrates the natural sweetness and versatility of seafood when paired with the smoky goodness of the grill. Gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and let the aromas of grilled seafood fill your summer gatherings with joy and satisfaction. Happy grilling!
