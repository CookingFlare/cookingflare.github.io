Farm to Table Desserts: 8 Sweet Recipes Using Fresh Ingredients


The farm-to-table movement has revolutionized the way we think about food, emphasizing the use of fresh, local, and sustainable ingredients. While farm-to-table is often associated with savory dishes, it’s also an incredible approach to desserts. By focusing on seasonally available fruits, fresh dairy, and locally sourced ingredients, you can elevate your desserts and bring the flavors of the farm right to your table. In this post, we’ll explore eight delicious farm-to-table dessert recipes that highlight the beauty and flavor of fresh ingredients. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner in the kitchen, these recipes will inspire you to create something sweet and wholesome.

1. Peach and Lavender Crumble

Nothing says summer quite like fresh peaches, and this Peach and Lavender Crumble recipe takes full advantage of that sweet, juicy fruit. Paired with the delicate fragrance of lavender, this dessert offers a beautiful balance of sweet and floral notes.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lavender flowers, chopped
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup cold butter, cubed
  • Pinch of sea salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, toss the sliced peaches with honey or maple syrup and chopped lavender. Set aside to marinate for 10 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine oats, flour, brown sugar, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Add the cold butter to the oat mixture and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Pour the marinated peaches into a baking dish and spread the oat mixture evenly over the top.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the topping is golden brown and the peaches are bubbling.
  7. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

2. Farm Fresh Strawberry Shortcake

Strawberries are one of the most beloved fruits of the summer, and a strawberry shortcake made with fresh, local berries is a timeless dessert. This recipe uses light, fluffy biscuits and homemade whipped cream to create a classic treat that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries with sugar and toss to coat. Let sit for 15-20 minutes to macerate.
  2. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  3. In a food processor, pulse the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add the milk and pulse just until the dough comes together.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead a few times. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick rectangle and use a biscuit cutter to cut out rounds.
  6. Place the biscuit rounds on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, until golden brown.
  7. While the biscuits bake, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  8. To assemble, split each biscuit in half and top with macerated strawberries and a dollop of whipped cream. Serve immediately.

3. Blueberry Lemon Cheesecake Bars

Blueberries are packed with antioxidants, and when combined with the tangy flavor of lemon and the creamy richness of cheesecake, they create a dessert that’s both refreshing and indulgent. These Blueberry Lemon Cheesecake Bars are the perfect balance of sweet and tart.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 2 cups cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan to form a crust.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese and sour cream together until smooth. Add the sugar, eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest, and beat until well combined.
  4. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust and smooth the top.
  5. Drop fresh blueberries evenly over the top of the cheesecake mixture, gently pressing them into the filling.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the cheesecake is set and slightly golden at the edges.
  7. Let the cheesecake bars cool completely before cutting into squares and serving. Chill in the refrigerator for best results.

4. Apple Cinnamon Galette

A galette is a rustic, free-form pastry that’s simple to make and always delicious. This Apple Cinnamon Galette uses fresh, crisp apples and a sprinkle of cinnamon to create a warm, comforting dessert that’s perfect for fall, but enjoyable year-round.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 4 large apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add ice water, stirring until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a 12-inch circle.
  4. In a bowl, combine the sliced apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, lemon juice, and cornstarch. Toss to coat the apples evenly.
  5. Pile the apple mixture in the center of the dough, leaving about 2 inches around the edges.
  6. Fold the edges of the dough over the apples, pleating as you go.
  7. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the crust is golden and the apples are tender.
  8. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce.

5. Farm Fresh Rhubarb Pie

Rhubarb is one of the first fruits to arrive in the spring, and its tart flavor makes it a perfect pairing with sweet fillings. This classic Rhubarb Pie uses fresh rhubarb to create a tangy, sweet treat that’s full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 prepared pie crust (top and bottom)
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, salt, vanilla, and lemon juice. Toss to combine and set aside.
  3. Roll out the bottom pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Pour the rhubarb mixture into the crust.
  4. Dot the filling with butter and cover with the top pie crust. Crimp the edges to seal the pie and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Let the pie cool for at least an hour before serving. It’s perfect with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

6. Pumpkin Spice Panna Cotta

Panna cotta is a silky, Italian dessert that’s incredibly easy to make and adaptable to many flavors. This Pumpkin Spice Panna Cotta uses fresh pumpkin puree and warming spices to create a comforting dessert perfect for fall and winter gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh pumpkin puree
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 packet unflavored gelatin

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over 1/4 cup cold water and let it bloom for 5 minutes.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, pumpkin puree, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Heat over medium heat until the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Add the bloomed gelatin to the saucepan and whisk until completely dissolved.
  5. Pour the mixture into serving glasses or ramekins and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until set.
  6. Serve with a dollop of whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon.

7. Mixed Berry Sorbet

If you’re looking for a refreshing, dairy-free dessert, this Mixed Berry Sorbet is the perfect option. Made with fresh berries, it’s naturally sweetened and incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups mixed fresh berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries, blackberries)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup water

Directions:

  1. In a blender, combine the berries, sugar, lemon juice, and water. Blend until smooth.
  2. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds.
  3. Pour the berry puree into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Once the sorbet is frozen, transfer it to a container and freeze for an additional 2 hours before serving.

8. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrots are a versatile farm-fresh ingredient that shines in both savory and sweet dishes. This moist Carrot Cake is packed with grated carrots, warm spices, and topped with a luscious cream cheese frosting. It’s the ultimate comfort dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

For the frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, flour, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, oil, and vanilla extract. Add the grated carrots and stir to combine.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined. Fold in the chopped nuts, if using.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the two cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. While the cake cools, prepare the frosting by beating the cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat until fluffy.
  7. Once the cake is completely cool, frost the top of one cake layer. Place the second layer on top and frost the entire cake.
  8. Slice and enjoy this rich and moist carrot cake as a delightful end to any meal.

Farm-to-table desserts bring the essence of fresh, local ingredients to your kitchen, allowing you to enjoy the true flavors of the seasons. With these eight recipes, you’ll be able to craft delicious, wholesome treats using farm-fresh fruits, vegetables, and dairy. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or just treating yourself, these desserts will bring joy to your tastebuds and warmth to your home.
