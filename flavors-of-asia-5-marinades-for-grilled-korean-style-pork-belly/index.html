Flavors of Asia: 5 Marinades for Grilled Korean-style Pork Belly


Grilled Korean-style pork belly is a mouthwatering dish that combines the succulent flavors of marinated pork with the smoky char of the grill. This dish is a staple in Korean cuisine and is known for its rich and bold flavors. In this blog post, we will explore five different marinades inspired by the diverse flavors of Asia that can take your grilled Korean-style pork belly to new heights. So fire up your grill and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure!

1. Soy Ginger Marinade

This marinade is a classic combination of savory soy sauce and zesty ginger. It adds a depth of flavor to the pork belly, making it incredibly delicious. To make the soy ginger marinade, combine the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Mix all the ingredients together and let the pork belly marinate in this flavorful concoction for at least two hours, or overnight for maximum flavor infusion.

2. Spicy Korean Gochujang Marinade

For those who love a bit of heat, this gochujang marinade is the perfect choice. Gochujang is a Korean chili paste that adds a spicy kick to any dish. To make the spicy Korean gochujang marinade, combine the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar

Mix all the ingredients together and let the pork belly marinate for at least two hours. The longer you marinate, the spicier and more flavorful the pork belly will be.

3. Sweet and Tangy Pineapple Marinade

If you’re looking for a marinade that balances sweetness and tanginess, this pineapple marinade is the perfect choice. The natural sweetness of pineapple complements the pork belly beautifully. To make the sweet and tangy pineapple marinade, combine the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Mix all the ingredients together and let the pork belly marinate for at least two hours. The pineapple juice tenderizes the meat, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

4. Hoisin Sesame Marinade

For a rich and flavorful marinade, look no further than the hoisin sesame marinade. Hoisin sauce is a thick and savory sauce commonly used in Chinese cuisine. Combined with the nutty flavor of sesame oil, it creates a marinade that is both sweet and savory. To make the hoisin sesame marinade, combine the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Chinese five-spice powder

Mix all the ingredients together and let the pork belly marinate for at least two hours. The hoisin sauce caramelizes on the grill, creating a sticky and irresistible glaze.

5. Lemongrass Coconut Marinade

For a taste of Southeast Asia, try this lemongrass coconut marinade. Lemongrass adds a refreshing citrusy flavor, while coconut milk brings a creamy and tropical touch to the dish. To make the lemongrass coconut marinade, combine the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 stalks of lemongrass, minced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Mix all the ingredients together and let the pork belly marinate for at least two hours. The lemongrass and coconut milk infuse the pork belly with a unique and exotic flavor.

Grilling Instructions

Once you have marinated your pork belly, it’s time to grill it to perfection. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat and grill the pork belly for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F. Let the pork belly rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

Serve your grilled Korean-style pork belly with a side of steamed rice, kimchi, and fresh lettuce leaves for wrapping. The combination of flavors and textures will create a culinary experience that is truly unforgettable.

Note: Remember to keep an eye on the pork belly as it grills, as the marinades contain sugars that can caramelize quickly and potentially burn. Adjust the heat accordingly to prevent any charring.

In conclusion, these five marinades inspired by the flavors of Asia will take your grilled Korean-style pork belly to new heights. Whether you prefer a spicy kick, a sweet and tangy taste, or a rich and savory profile, there’s a marinade for every palate. So next time you’re craving a taste of Korea, fire up the grill and experiment with these delicious marinades. Your taste buds will thank you!
