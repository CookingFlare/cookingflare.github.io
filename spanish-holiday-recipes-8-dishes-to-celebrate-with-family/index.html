Spanish Holiday Recipes: 8 Dishes to Celebrate with Family


When the holidays arrive, it’s time to gather loved ones around the table and celebrate with dishes that evoke warmth, tradition, and unforgettable flavors. In Spain, food plays a pivotal role in holiday festivities, bringing families and friends closer together as they share dishes rooted in centuries-old customs. From hearty stews to sweet pastries, Spanish holiday specialties offer the perfect combination of comfort and culinary artistry. Here are eight delectable recipes that will transform your celebrations into a flavorful feast.

1. Roscón de Reyes: A Sweet Start to the New Year

The highlight of Spain’s Epiphany celebrations, Roscón de Reyes is a festive crown-shaped cake adorned with candied fruits to symbolize precious gems. This dish is not just a dessert; it’s a tradition imbued with fun and surprises as it hides a small figurine and a dried bean inside the dough. Whoever finds the figurine is crowned “king” or “queen” for the day, while the unlucky bean finder pays for the next year’s Roscón.

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 120g sugar
  • 100g butter (softened)
  • 200ml warm milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 25g fresh yeast
  • Grated zest of 1 orange and 1 lemon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Candied fruits for decoration
  • Powdered sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Dissolve the yeast in the warm milk, letting it sit until bubbles form.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, citrus zest, and butter. Slowly pour in the milk and yeast mixture, then add the eggs one at a time. Knead until the dough becomes elastic and non-sticky.
  3. Shape the dough into a ball, cover, and let it rise for 2 hours or until doubled in size.
  4. After rising, form the dough into a ring on a baking sheet. Insert the figurine and the bean inside, carefully sealing them in.
  5. Decorate with candied fruits, brush the dough with egg wash, and bake at 180°C for 20-25 minutes. Dust with powdered sugar to finish.

2. Cochinillo Asado: Roast Suckling Pig

A timeless centerpiece for Spanish holiday meals, Cochinillo Asado is a tender, roasted suckling pig, known for its crispy golden skin and succulent meat. Whether served at Christmas or New Year’s Eve, this dish symbolizes abundance and celebration.

Ingredients:

  • Whole suckling pig (about 4-5kg)
  • 150ml olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 200ml white wine

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 190°C. Clean and pat the pig dry, seasoning generously inside and out with salt, pepper, rosemary, and thyme.
  2. Brush the pig with olive oil and place it belly-up on a roasting tray.
  3. Roast for approximately 3 hours, basting occasionally with white wine to enhance flavor and moisture.
  4. Once the skin is golden and crisp, carve and serve with roasted potatoes and a green salad.

3. Sopa de Almendras: Creamy Almond Soup

Often enjoyed as a starter during Christmas Eve dinner, Sopa de Almendras is a silky, comforting soup made from ground almonds and infused with cinnamon and lemon. Its delicate flavor and warm essence make it ideal for cozy winter evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 200g ground almonds
  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 piece of cinnamon stick
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 slices of stale bread (crumbled)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk along with the cinnamon stick and lemon zest until simmering.
  2. Stir in the sugar, salt, ground almonds, and crumbled bread.
  3. Simmer for 15 minutes, stirring frequently to thicken.
  4. Remove the cinnamon stick and serve warm with a sprinkle of sliced almonds or cinnamon powder.

4. Turrón de Jijona: Nougat Tradition

No Spanish holiday is complete without Turrón, a rich almond nougat that dates back to medieval Spain. There are many variations, but the soft and crumbly Turrón de Jijona is a holiday staple.

Ingredients:

  • 300g blanched almonds
  • 200g honey
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 egg white

Instructions:

  1. Toast the almonds in a dry pan until golden, then grind them finely.
  2. Heat the honey and sugar in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth.
  3. Beat the egg white until stiff peaks form, then fold it into the honey mixture.
  4. Stir in the ground almonds and cook until thickened.
  5. Pour into a loaf pan lined with parchment paper and let it cool completely before slicing.

5. Cordero Asado: Roasted Lamb

In parts of Spain, especially Castilla y León, Cordero Asado is a classic dish prepared for Christmas. The simplicity of this roasted lamb makes it a standout choice, combining tender meat with aromatic flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2kg leg of lamb
  • 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
  • 250ml olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • Fresh parsley and thyme

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Rub the lamb with garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, paprika, parsley, and thyme.
  2. Place the lamb in a roasting pan and cover tightly with foil. Roast for 2 hours, checking once halfway through.
  3. Remove the foil, increase the heat to 220°C, and roast for an additional 30 minutes to create a delicious crust.

6. Polvorones: Crumbly Holiday Cookies

These delicate almond-flavored cookies crumble in your mouth, making Polvorones a favorite in Spanish homes during the holidays. Packed with traditional ingredients, they embody the spirit of celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 100g ground almonds
  • 125g lard
  • 100g powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Toast the flour in a dry pan until lightly browned and fragrant. Allow it to cool.
  2. Combine cooled flour, ground almonds, powdered sugar, and cinnamon in a bowl. Work in the lard until a cohesive dough forms.
  3. Roll the dough into individual round balls and flatten slightly. Bake at 180°C for 15 minutes.
  4. Cool completely before serving or wrapping as gifts.

7. Gambas al Ajillo: Garlic Shrimp

For something light yet flavorful, Gambas al Ajillo offers a tasty seafood option perfect for holiday hors d’oeuvres. Prepared in minutes, this dish highlights the bold flavors of garlic, paprika, and fresh shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 500g peeled shrimp (raw)
  • 6 garlic cloves (thinly sliced)
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Pinch of chili flakes
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté garlic until golden, then add chili flakes and paprika.
  2. Add shrimp to the skillet, cooking for about 2-3 minutes per side until pink and tender.
  3. Garnish with parsley and serve with crusty bread for dipping.

8. Leche Frita: Fried Milk Dessert

A crowd-pleasing dessert that feels indulgent yet comforting, Leche Frita combines creamy milk custard coated in a crisp cinnamon-layered crust. This is the sweet finale to any Spanish holiday meal.

Ingredients:

  • 500ml whole milk
  • 100g sugar
  • 50g cornstarch
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Cinnamon powder
  • Flour (for coating)
  • 2 eggs (beaten)
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Heat milk, sugar, lemon zest, and cornstarch in a saucepan until thickened. Pour into a shallow dish and chill until firm.
  2. Cut into squares, coat each piece in flour and beaten egg, then fry until golden.
  3. Sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar before serving.

Let these eight exceptional dishes inspire your Spanish holiday celebrations. Whether you’re recreating beloved traditions or discovering new favorites, these recipes will fill your table with joy, love, and incredible flavors to share with family and friends.
