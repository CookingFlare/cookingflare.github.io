How to Make Authentic Mexican Chorizo


Housed in every Mexican household, chorizo has become an integral part of the country's culinary identity. This mouthwatering sausage is deep-rooted in tradition, and when done right, elevates any dish to new gastronomic heights. While it's easy to get it from your local butcher, making authentic Mexican chorizo from scratch is an adventure worth taking. The Story Behind Chorizo Chorizo originated in the 16th century in Spain and was later brought to Mexico by Spanish conquistadors. The word 'chorizo' is derived from the Latin word 'chirizo', meaning 'chopped pork'. Over time, Mexican cuisine evolved the recipe, incorporating locally-sourced ingredients like chili peppers, garlic, and spices. Mexico's incredibly diverse geographical and cultural landscape means every region boasts its unique take on this beloved sausage. Ingredients Before diving into the steps, gather the following:

Ingredient Quantity Ingredient Name

25 % Pork, finely ground (you can make this from scratch or purchase pre-ground pork butt or shoulder from a butcher)

4 ounces Onion, finely chopped

2 ounces Garlic, minced

4 habanero peppers, seeded and chopped (depending on your preference, adjust the heat level to your taste)

2 tablespoons Mexican oregano

1 teaspoon Mexican cumin (also known as cumin seed or craisira)

1 teaspoon Salt

½ teaspoon Black pepper

1 teaspoon Vinegar

The Process With your ingredients in order, prepare a flavor rich Mexican chorizo that will win over your family and friends: Step 1: Grind the Meat Start with your pork in a meat grinder or food processor to reduce it into fine pieces. Set this aside in a large bowl to add your spices and aromatics. Step 2: Sauté the Onion, Garlic, and Chilli Peppers In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of oil, adding the finely chopped onion and sautéing until translucent, followed by the minced garlic, and then your chopped habaneros. Be sure not to burn your onions; it brings the dress to the lowest notice. Let the peppers initially cook for about a minute, but be aware that overcooking them will be unwished. Step 3: Combine the Meat Mixture Once your onions, garlic, and chillies have been strained and completely translucent, mix in the ground pork, finishing with the Mexican oregano, cumin seeds, black pepper, salt, and vinegar. With your spatula, disturb all the combinations well so your ingredients intermingle the right measure. Store your homemade chorizo in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week or freeze for up to three months.
