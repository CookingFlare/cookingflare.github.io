Making Sweet Brioche Rolls at Home


Brioche, with its rich, buttery flavor and soft, airy texture, is a classic pastry that has been loved for generations. While you may have enjoyed it from bakeries or cafes, there’s something extra special about making sweet brioche rolls at home. Not only do you get to enjoy the freshness and warmth of homemade bread, but you also have complete control over the ingredients, ensuring that each roll is perfect. This step-by-step guide will walk you through the process of making sweet brioche rolls from scratch, with detailed instructions and helpful tips along the way.

What is Brioche?

Brioche is a French bread known for its luxurious texture, often referred to as a “buttery, egg-rich bread.” Unlike regular bread, which is made with flour, yeast, and water, brioche dough includes eggs, milk, and butter, which contribute to its soft and tender crumb. The addition of sugar gives brioche its signature sweetness, making it perfect for breakfast, desserts, or even as a side for savory dishes.

Sweet brioche rolls are often enjoyed as a breakfast item, paired with a cup of coffee or tea, or served as a treat for special occasions. Their rich, golden hue and melt-in-your-mouth texture make them irresistible.

Ingredients for Sweet Brioche Rolls

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to make the dough for your sweet brioche rolls:

  • 500 grams (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 60 grams (1/4 cup) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 10 grams (2 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 240 ml (1 cup) whole milk, at room temperature
  • 200 grams (3/4 cup + 2 tablespoons) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract (optional, but recommended for added flavor)
  • 1 egg, for egg wash (optional, for brushing)

Tools and Equipment

  • Stand mixer (with dough hook attachment)
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Baking sheet or cake pan
  • Parchment paper (for lining the baking sheet)
  • Plastic wrap or a damp towel (for covering the dough while it rises)
  • Pastry brush (for egg wash, if desired)

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

The first step in making brioche dough is to activate the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk (about 110°F or 43°C) and the sugar. Stir gently to dissolve the sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5 minutes. You should start to see the mixture become frothy and bubbly, which indicates that the yeast is alive and active.

If the yeast does not bubble or foam, it may be expired or the milk may have been too hot or too cold. In that case, discard the mixture and start again with fresh yeast and properly warm milk.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In the bowl of your stand mixer, combine the flour and salt. Use a whisk to stir the dry ingredients together. Once mixed, create a small well in the center of the flour mixture.

Pour the activated yeast mixture into the well, followed by the eggs. Use the dough hook attachment to mix the ingredients on low speed for about 2-3 minutes. As the dough begins to come together, increase the speed to medium and continue mixing for another 5-7 minutes, until the dough is smooth and slightly elastic.

Step 3: Add the Butter

Once your dough has formed, it’s time to add the butter. This part is what makes brioche so rich and buttery! Gradually add the softened butter, one tablespoon at a time, allowing each piece to incorporate fully before adding the next. This will take a few minutes, but be patient. The dough will become increasingly soft and sticky, and this is completely normal.

Continue to mix on medium speed until the dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl and forms a smooth, cohesive ball. You may need to scrape down the sides of the bowl to ensure everything is well incorporated.

Step 4: Knead the Dough

Once the butter has been fully incorporated into the dough, you can either continue mixing with the stand mixer for another 5-7 minutes or knead by hand. If kneading by hand, transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for 10-12 minutes. The dough should be soft, smooth, and slightly tacky to the touch, but not overly sticky.

When the dough reaches the right consistency, shape it into a ball.

Step 5: First Rise

Transfer the dough to a lightly greased bowl and cover it with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the dough has doubled in size.

This slow rise allows the yeast to work its magic, creating the airy, fluffy texture that brioche is known for. You can speed up the process by placing the dough in an oven with just the light on, creating a warm environment.

Step 6: Shape the Brioche Rolls

Once the dough has doubled in size, gently punch it down to release the air. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into 12 equal portions. You can use a kitchen scale for accuracy, weighing the dough to ensure each roll is the same size.

Shape each portion of dough into a round ball by folding the edges under and pinching them together. Place each ball onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving a little space between each one.

Alternatively, if you prefer a more cohesive look, arrange the rolls in a cake pan or a large baking dish so that they will touch each other as they bake, creating a pull-apart effect.

Step 7: Second Rise

Cover the shaped rolls with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for another hour, or until they have puffed up significantly. During this time, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C), so it’s ready when the rolls are finished rising.

Step 8: Egg Wash (Optional)

For a shiny, golden finish on your brioche rolls, you can brush them with an egg wash before baking. To make the egg wash, beat the extra egg with a tablespoon of water and use a pastry brush to lightly coat the tops of the rolls.

This step adds an extra layer of shine and richness, giving the rolls a professional look.

Step 9: Bake the Brioche Rolls

Once your rolls have risen and you’ve applied the egg wash, it’s time to bake them. Place the rolls in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Keep an eye on the rolls as they bake to prevent over-baking. If they are browning too quickly, you can loosely cover them with aluminum foil during the last few minutes of baking.

Step 10: Cool and Serve

Once the sweet brioche rolls are baked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for about 10 minutes. While the rolls are cooling, their texture will set, and you’ll be able to easily pull them apart.

Serve the rolls warm or at room temperature. Brioche rolls are perfect on their own, but you can also enjoy them with a dollop of jam, honey, or butter. These rolls also make fantastic French toast the next morning!

Optional Add-Ins and Variations

While these sweet brioche rolls are delicious on their own, feel free to get creative with your dough. You can add:

  • Cinnamon and sugar: After dividing the dough into portions, roll each ball into cinnamon sugar before shaping them into rolls for a sweet twist.
  • Chocolate chips or chunks: Fold some chocolate into the dough for a rich, decadent variation.
  • Orange zest: Add a touch of citrus zest to the dough for a fresh, aromatic flavor.

Tips for Success

  • Room temperature ingredients: Make sure your butter, milk, and eggs are at room temperature before starting. This will ensure a smoother dough and better incorporation of ingredients.
  • Use a scale: For the most accurate measurements, use a kitchen scale. This ensures consistency and helps prevent any mishaps with ingredient ratios.
  • Don’t rush the rise: Brioche dough benefits from a slow, steady rise. Be patient and give it time to double in size. Rushing the process can result in denser rolls.
  • Butter is key: Brioche is all about butter, so don’t skimp on it. The more butter you incorporate, the richer and fluffier your rolls will be.

Conclusion

Making sweet brioche rolls at home is a rewarding and delicious experience. The process might take some time, but the results are well worth the effort. With soft, buttery, and slightly sweet rolls fresh from your oven, you’ll have a bakery-quality treat to enjoy whenever you like. Whether you serve them for breakfast, as a snack, or as part of a special meal, homemade brioche rolls are sure to impress.

Give it a try, and enjoy the art of making this classic French pastry right in your own kitchen.
