Discover Italy's Best-Kept-Secret Dishes: Regional Treasures to Savor


Italy, known for its boot-shape, has been a culinary heaven since centuries. It is possibly the most well-acknowledged destinations in terms of gastronomic delights, but do you know about Italy’s best-kept-secret dishes? Dishes that come from regions lesser known or at times overshadowed by their glorious neighbors? Allow us to take a culinary trip through these hidden regional treasures that fill every palate with sheer delight.

1. Friulano Wine & Arancini - Friuli Venezia Giulia

Just north of Venice lies Friuli Venezia Giulia, a region not often credited for its gastronomy but has some truly incredible culinary offerings. One such precious gem is Friulano, a light and floral dry white wine that truly brings out the flavors of the region. Next up is Arancini - think savory fried rice stuffed inside a crunchy ball of rice, coated with crispy breadcrumbs - is a perfect example of Friulano’s popularity. Traditionally tri-colored risotto is used, and the mighty cheese “Montasio” gives a cheesy burst with every bite.

2. Pecorino Cheese Crostini - Marche

If your idea of an exquisite Italian meal doesn’t include a heaping plate of simple yet scrumptious appetizers, then you don’t know Marche - a region along the eastern coast, a blend of Tuscany and Umbria’s richness. The very essence of Marche lies in its use of ‘pecorino’, a firm cheese made from sheep’s milk. Pecorino cheese crostini are a staple of the evening aperitivos - a heavenly marriage of toasted bread, flavorful cheese, and a zesty drizzle of olive oil.

3. Sarde in Saor - Venice

Venice is synonymous with gondolas, canals, and, most importantly, exquisite seafood dishes. Prepare yourself for Sarde in Saor - literally translated as ‘sardines in sauce’, it’s a culinary masterpiece from Venice. It consists of layers of marinated sardines, warm onions, pine nuts, and a dash of cinnamon. Stacked and refrigerated, this complex yet simple dish is a great way to experience Venetian food at its best.

4. Cacio e Pepe - Lazio

The heart of Italy, Lazio is undoubtedly known for its eternal city, Rome, and not to mention the Roman pasta dishes that originated here. Cacio e Pepe is a simple, refined pasta dish - “Cacio” refers to Pecorino Romano cheese, while “Pepe” stands for freshly cracked black pepper. This Roman classic combines the two with spaghetti for a flavor-packed low maintenance dish that Roman natives have cherished for generations.

5. Amatriciana Sauce - Abruzzo

On the doorstep of Rome lies Abruzzo, a region aspiring to maintain a unique culinary identity with sharp flavors and coarse textures. The enchants of this region lies in its homemade Amatriciana Sauce. A luxurious tomato-based pasta sauce laced with Guanciale (cured pork cheek), pecorino cheese, and red chili, this vibrant sauce has imperial origins, a delicious witness to the passion and creativity of Abruzzo’s grand tradition.

6. Timballo di Maccheroni - Sicily

Stepping off the tip of the Italian peninsula into the Mediterranean stretches the magical island of Sicily, a culinary wonderland. The alluring Catanese Timballo di Maccheroni reveals Sicily’s love for pasta. Pasta is layered with mince meat sauce, béchamel, and cheese, then baked into a flavorful timballo - a baked pastry pie that is the perfect blend of Sicilian flavors.
