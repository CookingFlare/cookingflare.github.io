10 Classic Cookie Recipes Everyone Should Try


Cookies are more than just a sweet treat; they are a universal symbol of comfort and nostalgia. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion, a holiday gathering, or simply to enjoy with a cup of coffee or tea, classic cookie recipes have a way of bringing people together. In this post, we’ll explore ten timeless cookie recipes that everyone should try. From chewy chocolate chip to delicate butter cookies, these classics will surely delight your taste buds and become favorites in your household.

1. Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and baking soda; set aside.
  3. Cream Butter and Sugars: In a large bowl, cream the butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and salt until the mixture is light and fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add Eggs and Vanilla: Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually blend in the flour mixture until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  6. Fold in Chocolate Chips: Stir in the chocolate chips using a spatula or wooden spoon.
  7. Scoop and Bake: Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto ungreased baking sheets. Bake for 9-11 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.
  8. Cool: Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

Tips:

  • For even chewier cookies, let the dough rest in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before baking.
  • Experiment with different types of chocolate chips or add nuts for added texture.

2. Classic Sugar Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons buttermilk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a small bowl, stir together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt; set aside.
  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract.
  4. Combine Mixtures: Gradually blend the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. If the dough is too dry, add buttermilk one tablespoon at a time until the dough is workable.
  5. Shape Cookies: Roll rounded teaspoonfuls of dough into balls, and place them on ungreased cookie sheets. Flatten each ball slightly with the bottom of a glass.
  6. Bake: Bake 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven until golden. Let cool on wire racks.

Tips:

  • For festive occasions, roll the dough in sugar before baking for a sparkling finish.
  • Use cookie cutters to create shapes and decorate with icing for a fun twist.

3. Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cream Butter and Sugars: In a large bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  3. Mix Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Combine All Ingredients: Gradually blend the dry ingredients into the creamed mixture. Stir in the oats and raisins.
  5. Scoop and Bake: Drop by rounded spoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Cool: Let cool on wire racks.

Tips:

  • For a chewy texture, do not overbake.
  • Substitute chocolate chips for raisins if you prefer a different flavor.

4. Peanut Butter Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, combine peanut butter, sugar, egg, baking soda, and vanilla extract. Mix well until smooth.
  3. Shape Cookies: Roll the dough into balls and place them on a baking sheet. Flatten each ball with a fork, creating a crisscross pattern.
  4. Bake: Bake for 10-12 minutes until edges are lightly browned. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Tips:

  • For added crunch, mix in chopped peanuts or use crunchy peanut butter.
  • Drizzle melted chocolate over the cooled cookies for an indulgent touch.

5. Snickerdoodles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar (for rolling)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon (for rolling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy. Beat in the eggs.
  3. Combine Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, combine flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add to the creamed mixture.
  4. Mix Sugar and Cinnamon: In a small bowl, mix the sugar and cinnamon for rolling.
  5. Shape Cookies: Roll dough into balls, then roll in the sugar-cinnamon mixture. Place on ungreased baking sheets.
  6. Bake: Bake for 8-10 minutes, until set but still soft. Let cool on the sheets for a few minutes before transferring.

Tips:

  • For a fun twist, add a pinch of nutmeg to the dough.
  • Ensure not to overbake for a soft and chewy texture.

6. Butter Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a bowl, cream together the butter and powdered sugar until fluffy. Stir in the vanilla.
  3. Add Flour and Salt: Gradually add the flour and salt, mixing until a dough forms.
  4. Shape Cookies: Roll the dough into small balls and place them on ungreased baking sheets. Flatten slightly with a fork.
  5. Bake: Bake for 12-15 minutes until the edges are lightly golden. Cool on wire racks.

Tips:

  • For added flavor, mix in lemon or almond extract.
  • These cookies can be easily decorated with icing or sprinkles for special occasions.
