Wine Pairing for Game Day: Recipes and Drink Suggestions


When you think of game day, chances are wings, nachos, and beer come to mind. But what if you elevated your celebration? Whether you’re hosting a crowd of die-hard fans or enjoying the game with a small group, pairing the right wine with classic game day snacks can turn a casual event into a culinary experience. Let’s explore how wine can complement your favorite game day dishes and discover recipes that make these pairings unforgettable.

Why Wine for Game Day?

Wine’s complexity, versatility, and ability to enhance flavors make it a fantastic—yet often overlooked—option for game day. Unlike beer, with its relatively uniform flavor profile, wine offers a wide range of tastes and textures. Subtle white wines, robust reds, or sparkling wines can each elevate your appetizers, mains, and even desserts. Plus, offering wine options besides the traditional beer creates a more inclusive experience for a diverse crowd of guests.

Recipe Pairing #1: Spicy Buffalo Wings with Dry Riesling

Buffalo wings are a staple at any game day gathering. Their tangy, spicy kick can be a challenge to pair with beverages, but a crisp, slightly sweet wine like a dry Riesling makes for a surprising yet harmonious choice. The vibrant acidity of the wine cuts through the heat while its fruity undertones balance the spice.

Recipe: Spicy Buffalo Wings

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. chicken wings
  • 1/2 cup hot sauce (e.g., Frank’s RedHot)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack on top.
  2. Toss the chicken wings with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them on the rack in a single layer.
  3. Bake for 40-50 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the wings are crispy and fully cooked.
  4. In a saucepan, combine hot sauce, melted butter, white vinegar, and garlic powder. Heat over medium-low until the mixture is smooth.
  5. Toss the cooked wings in the sauce and serve with celery sticks, blue cheese dressing, and your chilled Riesling.

Recipe Pairing #2: Loaded Nachos with a Medium-Bodied Tempranillo

Nachos are the ultimate shareable dish for game day, offering layers of cheese, salsa, sour cream, guacamole, and protein. To avoid overpowering the dish, go for a medium-bodied red like Tempranillo. The wine’s balanced tannins and red berry notes work well with the smoky, spicy, and creamy elements of loaded nachos.

Recipe: Loaded Nachos

Ingredients:

  • Tortilla chips (1 large bag)
  • 1 lb. ground beef or shredded chicken
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup black beans
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup jalapeño slices
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup guacamole
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup salsa

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the ground beef or chicken in a skillet over medium heat. Add the taco seasoning and cook until well combined.
  3. On a large baking sheet or oven-safe platter, layer half the tortilla chips, half the cheese, and the seasoned meat. Repeat with another layer of chips, cheese, and toppings like black beans, tomatoes, and jalapeños.
  4. Bake for 10-15 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Remove from the oven and garnish with cilantro. Serve with guacamole, sour cream, salsa, and a glass of Tempranillo.

Recipe Pairing #3: Classic Chili with a Full-Bodied Syrah

A steaming pot of chili is the ultimate comfort food for game day. The bold flavors of chili—savory meat, sweet tomatoes, and earthy spices—call for an equally bold wine. Enter Syrah. Its dark fruit notes, peppery undertones, and sturdy body complement the richness of chili without overpowering its depth.

Recipe: Classic Game Day Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 lb. ground pork
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz.) kidney beans, drained
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, cook the ground beef and pork until browned. Remove excess grease as needed.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes, kidney beans, beef broth, and all the spices. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Cook on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chili thickens and flavors meld.
  5. Serve hot with shredded cheese, chopped onions, a dollop of sour cream, and a glass of Syrah.

Recipe Pairing #4: BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders with Zinfandel

Sweet and smoky BBQ pulled pork sliders are bite-sized indulgences perfect for game day. A bold Zinfandel, with its jammy fruit flavors and hints of spice, complements the sweetness of the BBQ sauce and enhances the savory pork.

Recipe: BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. pork shoulder
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 12 slider buns
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the pork shoulder in a slow cooker. Add chicken broth and 1/2 cup of BBQ sauce.
  2. Cook on low for 8 hours or until the pork is tender and easy to shred.
  3. Shred the pork with two forks and mix it with the remaining BBQ sauce.
  4. Serve the pulled pork on slider buns, topped with coleslaw if desired. Pair with a glass of Zinfandel to elevate the smoky, tangy flavors.

Recipe Pairing #5: Veggie Pizza with Sauvignon Blanc

If you’re catering to vegetarian guests—or just want a lighter option—veggie pizza is a vibrant and satisfying choice. Pair this dish with Sauvignon Blanc, known for its crisp acidity and herbal notes that complement the fresh vegetables and cheesy goodness of the pizza.

Recipe: Veggie Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza crust
  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced red bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup sliced red onions
  • 1/4 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup black olives
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh basil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven according to the instructions for your pizza crust.
  2. Spread marinara sauce evenly over the crust. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese on top.
  3. Arrange the vegetables evenly over the cheese.
  4. Bake until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve with a chilled Sauvignon Blanc.

Wine Pairing Tips for Game Day Success

While these recipes offer specific pairings, understanding the basics of wine pairing will help you adapt these suggestions to your own menu. Here are a few general tips to keep in mind:

  • Match intensity: Pair bold, flavorful dishes with equally bold wines to prevent either from being overshadowed.
  • Balance flavors: A touch of sweetness in your wine can balance spicy dishes, while acidity cuts through rich, fatty foods.
  • Complement or contrast: Either complement your dish with similar flavors or contrast it to add complexity.
  • Experiment: Don’t be afraid to try new combinations. Wine pairing is as much about personal preference as it is about guidelines.

Break out the corkscrew for your next game day gathering and surprise your guests with thoughtfully chosen wine pairings. From classic wings to indulgent nachos, these recipes and wine suggestions prove that combining great food with the perfect glass of wine can score big on flavor and fun. Cheers to an elevated game day experience!
