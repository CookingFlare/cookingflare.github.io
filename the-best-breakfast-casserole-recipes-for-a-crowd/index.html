The Best Breakfast Casserole Recipes for a Crowd


If you’re planning a gathering or family brunch, there’s nothing quite like a hearty breakfast casserole to satisfy a large group of hungry guests. Breakfast casseroles offer the perfect combination of convenience, taste, and ease, making them a go-to option when you need to feed a crowd. Whether you’re hosting a holiday breakfast, a weekend get-together, or just a family morning meal, these breakfast casserole recipes will ensure everyone leaves the table full and satisfied. From savory options to a sweet twist, there’s a casserole for every taste preference.

Why Choose a Breakfast Casserole?

Breakfast casseroles are ideal for several reasons:

  1. Easy to Prepare: Many casseroles can be assembled the night before and simply baked in the morning. This makes them a great option for busy hosts who don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen.

  2. Feeds a Crowd: A casserole can serve many people at once, which makes it perfect for large groups or gatherings. No need to prepare individual servings – simply cut into squares or portions, and you’re good to go.

  3. Customizable: You can adapt a breakfast casserole to fit your guests’ preferences, dietary restrictions, and the ingredients you have on hand. Whether you prefer vegetarian, meaty, or gluten-free options, there’s a casserole recipe that can meet those needs.

  4. Versatile Ingredients: Breakfast casseroles typically include eggs, cheese, bread, and a variety of vegetables and meats, making them flexible for a range of flavor profiles. You can easily mix and match ingredients to create a dish that suits your guests.

Now, let’s explore some of the best breakfast casserole recipes that are guaranteed to wow your crowd.

Classic Breakfast Casserole with Sausage

A classic breakfast casserole is a crowd-pleaser that combines savory sausage, eggs, cheese, and potatoes for a satisfying and filling dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. breakfast sausage (crumbled)
  • 10 large eggs
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 4 cups frozen hash browns (thawed)
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and crumbled, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the onions and bell pepper (if using) until softened, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and oregano.
  5. Add the sausage, onions, bell peppers, and hash browns to the bowl, stirring to combine.
  6. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and top with shredded cheddar cheese.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the casserole is set in the center and the top is golden brown.
  8. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy your delicious classic breakfast casserole!

Veggie-Packed Breakfast Casserole (Vegetarian Option)

If you have guests who prefer a vegetarian breakfast, this veggie-packed breakfast casserole is a wonderful option. It’s loaded with fresh vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, and tomatoes, making it light yet satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 10 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk (or dairy-free alternative)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup cooked and crumbled veggie sausage (optional)
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 6 slices whole wheat bread, cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the mushrooms, bell pepper, onion, and spinach until tender, about 7-8 minutes.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, basil, and garlic powder.
  4. Add the sautéed vegetables, bread cubes, and cheese to the bowl, stirring gently to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the casserole is firm and golden brown on top.
  7. Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve warm.

This veggie-packed casserole is full of nutrients and flavor while being light on the calories, making it a great choice for those seeking a healthier option.

Bacon and Egg Breakfast Casserole

Bacon lovers will appreciate this rich and indulgent breakfast casserole. With crispy bacon, eggs, and layers of gooey cheese, this dish is the ultimate comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 12 slices bacon
  • 10 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 6 cups cubed bread (day-old bread works best)
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp paprika

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Cook the bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Once cooked, drain on paper towels and crumble.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and onion powder.
  4. Add the bread cubes, crumbled bacon, and half of the cheddar cheese to the egg mixture. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and top with the remaining cheddar cheese and chopped green onions.
  6. Sprinkle paprika on top for added color and flavor.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the casserole is set and golden brown.
  8. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving. This bacon and egg casserole will be a hit at your brunch table.

Sweet Potato and Ham Breakfast Casserole

This breakfast casserole features the natural sweetness of roasted sweet potatoes, combined with savory ham, eggs, and cheese. It’s the perfect balance of sweet and salty.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 lb. cooked ham, diced
  • 10 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Roast the sweet potato cubes on a baking sheet at 375°F (190°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly browned. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the diced onion and ham for 5-7 minutes, until the onion is translucent.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, paprika, and garlic powder.
  5. Add the roasted sweet potatoes, sautéed onion and ham, and shredded cheddar cheese to the egg mixture. Stir gently to combine.
  6. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  7. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the casserole is set and golden on top.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired and slice into squares for serving.

Tips for a Perfect Breakfast Casserole

  • Use Stale Bread: Stale or day-old bread works best for casseroles because it absorbs the egg mixture more efficiently, preventing the casserole from becoming soggy.

  • Prep Ahead: Many of these casseroles can be assembled the night before and refrigerated overnight. This makes your morning a breeze as you simply have to pop the casserole in the oven.

  • Add Fresh Herbs: Fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, or chives can elevate the flavor and presentation of your casserole.

  • Choose the Right Baking Dish: For even cooking, make sure to use a baking dish that fits the amount of ingredients you’re using. A 9x13-inch dish is standard, but if you’re scaling up or down, adjust accordingly.

  • Check for Doneness: The casserole should be firm in the center and golden brown on top. You can also insert a toothpick to ensure the center is fully cooked.

Conclusion

Breakfast casseroles are a perfect solution when feeding a crowd. With so many variations to choose from, you can customize each dish to suit the tastes of your guests. From classic sausage casseroles to vegetarian and even sweet potato options, these breakfast casseroles are guaranteed to impress. They’re easy to prepare, and the leftovers (if there are any) are just as delicious the next day. So next time you have a crowd to feed, try one of these breakfast casserole recipes and enjoy the smiles around the breakfast table.
