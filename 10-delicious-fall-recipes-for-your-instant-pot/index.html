10 Delicious Fall Recipes for Your Instant Pot


Fall is the season of cozy flavors, vibrant colors, and comforting meals. The crisp air and the golden hues of autumn inspire hearty, rich, and warming dishes that are perfect for the season. With the Instant Pot, you can bring these flavors to life quickly and easily, whether you’re craving a pumpkin soup or a savory stew. Below are 10 fall recipes for your Instant Pot that will help you embrace the flavors of the season and make your autumn meals even more enjoyable.

1. Instant Pot Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash is one of the quintessential fall vegetables, and it makes a creamy, comforting soup perfect for a chilly day. With the Instant Pot, you can have a delicious, velvety soup in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional for extra creaminess)

Instructions:

  1. Select the “Sauté” function on your Instant Pot. Add a bit of olive oil and sauté the onions and garlic until softened, about 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to combine.
  3. Secure the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 10 minutes.
  4. Once the cooking time is complete, release the pressure manually. Use an immersion blender to purée the soup until smooth and creamy.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk (if using), and season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve warm.

2. Instant Pot Beef Stew

Beef stew is a classic fall dish, perfect for a hearty meal. The Instant Pot makes this comforting dish even easier by tenderizing the beef quickly and infusing it with rich flavors in a fraction of the time it takes to make on the stove.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Heat the olive oil and brown the beef stew meat in batches. Remove the beef and set it aside.
  2. Add the onions, garlic, and tomato paste to the Instant Pot. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant.
  3. Return the beef to the pot along with the carrots, potatoes, beef broth, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 35 minutes.
  5. When the timer goes off, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  6. For a thicker stew, mix the cornstarch with a little water to make a slurry, then add it to the pot. Stir until the stew thickens. Serve hot.

3. Instant Pot Apple Cider Chicken

Fall and apples go hand in hand, and this savory dish combines the sweetness of apple cider with tender chicken, making it the perfect autumn dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 apples, cored and sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add the olive oil and sear the chicken thighs for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add the onions and apples to the Instant Pot and sauté for 3 minutes until they start to soften.
  3. Stir in the apple cider, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, thyme, salt, and pepper. Scrape any browned bits off the bottom of the pot.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot, placing it on top of the apples and onions.
  5. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 10 minutes.
  6. After cooking, perform a quick release of the pressure. Serve the chicken with the apple-cider sauce and enjoy the flavors of fall.

4. Instant Pot Pumpkin Risotto

Pumpkin risotto is a creamy and flavorful fall dish that’s easy to make with your Instant Pot. The subtle sweetness of pumpkin pairs beautifully with Parmesan and a touch of sage, making it a perfect side or main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add butter and sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes until lightly toasted.
  3. Pour in the white wine and cook until the liquid is mostly absorbed.
  4. Add the pumpkin puree, broth, sage, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  5. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 6 minutes.
  6. After cooking, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  7. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, adjust seasoning if necessary, and serve warm.

5. Instant Pot Sweet Potato Chili

Sweet potatoes add a lovely sweetness to chili, making this version of the classic fall dish a little different, but equally satisfying. The Instant Pot ensures the flavors meld perfectly in a short time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup vegetable broth

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Brown the ground turkey or beef, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot, cooking until softened, about 3 minutes.
  3. Stir in the sweet potatoes, diced tomatoes, beans, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, pepper, and broth.
  4. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 15 minutes.
  5. After the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes before performing a quick release.
  6. Stir and serve hot. Top with sour cream, shredded cheese, or fresh cilantro if desired.

6. Instant Pot Maple Pecan Oatmeal

Start your fall mornings with a warm, comforting bowl of oatmeal. The addition of maple syrup and toasted pecans gives this breakfast a true fall flair, making it the perfect cozy meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups steel-cut oats
  • 4 cups water or milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans, toasted
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add the oats, water or milk, maple syrup, cinnamon, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt to the Instant Pot.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 4 minutes.
  3. After the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Stir the oatmeal and top with toasted pecans before serving. Add more maple syrup if you prefer a sweeter flavor.

7. Instant Pot Sausage and Apple Stuffing

Stuffing is a must-have side dish for fall dinners, and this sausage and apple stuffing is a hearty, flavorful twist. The Instant Pot ensures it cooks up quickly while keeping all the delicious flavors intact.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb sausage, casing removed
  • 2 apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cups cubed bread (preferably day-old)
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Cook the sausage until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  2. Add the onions and apples, cooking for 3-5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the cubed bread, chicken broth, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 5 minutes.
  5. Allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  6. Fluff the stuffing with a fork and serve.

8. Instant Pot Fall Harvest Soup

A mixture of root vegetables, lentils, and spices creates a nourishing soup that’s perfect for fall. This recipe is both filling and healthy, offering a great way to enjoy the season’s flavors in a wholesome way.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups diced butternut squash
  • 2 cups diced sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup green lentils
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add all ingredients to the Instant Pot, stirring to combine.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 15 minutes.
  3. Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Stir the soup and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot.

9. Instant Pot Cranberry Sauce

No fall meal is complete without a bowl of homemade cranberry sauce. This recipe takes just minutes in the Instant Pot and adds a vibrant, tangy side to your Thanksgiving or fall dinner table.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Add cranberries, sugar, orange juice, orange zest, and cinnamon to the Instant Pot.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 4 minutes.
  3. After the cooking time is complete, perform a quick release.
  4. Stir the cranberry sauce and let it cool before serving.

10. Instant Pot Maple-Glazed Carrots

These sweet, tender carrots are glazed with maple syrup and a touch of butter, making them a perfect side dish for any fall meal. They’re quick and easy to make in the Instant Pot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb baby carrots
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the carrots, maple syrup, butter, salt, and pepper to the Instant Pot.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 3 minutes.
  3. After the cooking time is complete, perform a quick release.
  4. Stir the carrots to coat them in the glaze and serve hot.

These 10 Instant Pot fall recipes offer a wide range of options to celebrate the flavors of autumn. Whether you’re craving something savory, sweet, or comforting, your Instant Pot will make the process fast and easy, giving you more time to enjoy the season. Try these recipes this fall, and let the Instant Pot take your autumn meals to the next level.
