Fig Newton Bars: Homestyle Comfort with a Twist

3 min · 494 words

Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re always cooking up something delicious and exciting! Today, we’re taking a classic treat and giving it a modern twist. Get ready to dive into the world of Fig Newton Bars: Homestyle Comfort with a Twist.

Introduction

Fig Newton Bars have been a beloved snack for generations. The combination of sweet figs and tender cookie dough has made them a staple in many households. But what if we could take this classic treat and elevate it to new heights? That’s exactly what we’re going to do today.

The Dough

First things first, let’s start with the dough. To give our Fig Newton Bars a modern twist, we’re going to infuse the dough with a hint of orange zest. This citrusy addition will add a refreshing burst of flavor that perfectly complements the sweetness of the fig filling.

To make the dough, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, vanilla extract, and orange zest, and mix until well combined. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a soft dough forms.

The Filling

Now that our dough is ready, let’s move on to the star of the show - the fig filling. While traditional Fig Newton Bars use a simple fig jam, we’re going to take it up a notch by adding a touch of cinnamon and a splash of brandy. These additions will give our bars a warm and cozy flavor that will transport you straight to a homestyle kitchen.

For the filling, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup dried figs, stems removed
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup brandy
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

In a small saucepan, combine the dried figs, water, brandy, sugar, and cinnamon. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the figs have softened and the liquid has reduced to a jam-like consistency. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Putting it Together

Now comes the fun part - assembling our Fig Newton Bars. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thickness. Cut the dough into rectangular strips, approximately 2 inches wide and 3 inches long. Spoon a dollop of the fig filling onto the center of each dough strip, leaving a small border around the edges. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges to seal.

Place the filled bars onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a small gap between each one. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Remove from the oven and let them cool completely on a wire rack.

Enjoying the Twist

Once your Fig Newton Bars have cooled, it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor. These Homestyle Comfort with a Twist treats are perfect for any occasion. Serve them as a delightful snack with a cup of tea, or impress your guests by pairing them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a unique dessert.

The combination of the citrus-infused dough, the warm and spiced fig filling, and the tender cookie exterior will have your taste buds dancing with joy. These Fig Newton Bars are a delightful twist on a classic treat, bringing comfort and excitement to every bite.
