Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp: A Classic Summer Dessert


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in the sweet and tangy flavors of strawberry rhubarb crisp. This classic dessert combines the vibrant taste of ripe strawberries with the tartness of rhubarb, all topped with a crunchy and buttery crumble. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a delicious and comforting treat that will impress your family and friends. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 cups fresh rhubarb, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Crumble Topping

  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish or a similar-sized oven-safe dish.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced strawberries, rhubarb, granulated sugar, all-purpose flour, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss the mixture until the fruit is coated evenly.

  3. Transfer the fruit mixture into the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, all-purpose flour, brown sugar, cold cubed butter, salt, and ground cinnamon. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to mix the ingredients together until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  5. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the fruit mixture in the baking dish.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 40-45 minutes, or until the fruit is bubbling and the crumble topping is golden brown.

  7. Remove the crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

  8. Serve the strawberry rhubarb crisp warm, either on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • You can use either fresh or frozen strawberries and rhubarb for this recipe. If using frozen fruit, make sure to thaw and drain it before using.

  • Adjust the amount of sugar according to your taste preferences and the sweetness of the fruit. If your strawberries are particularly sweet, you might want to reduce the sugar slightly.

  • Experiment with adding different spices to the crumble topping, such as nutmeg or ginger, to enhance the flavor profile of the dessert.

  • If you want to make the crisp in advance, you can prepare the fruit mixture and the crumble topping separately, then store them in the refrigerator. When ready to bake, assemble the dish and follow the baking instructions.

Strawberry rhubarb crisp is a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of summer. The combination of sweet strawberries and tangy rhubarb, paired with a crispy and buttery topping, is a match made in dessert heaven. Whether you’re serving it at a family gathering or simply treating yourself after a long day, this classic summer dessert is sure to please. So grab your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in the deliciousness of strawberry rhubarb crisp!
