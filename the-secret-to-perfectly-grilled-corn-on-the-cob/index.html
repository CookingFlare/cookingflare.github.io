The Secret to Perfectly Grilled Corn on the Cob


Ever wondered how to achieve that perfect, char-grilled flavor on your corn on the cob? Grilled corn is a favorite summer time dish that many of us love to enjoy, especially at barbecues and picnics. But how do the professionals get that delicious, slightly smokey taste that keeps us coming back for more? In this blog post, we’ll uncover the secret to perfectly grilled corn on the cob and take you step-by-step through the process.

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 ears fresh corn on the cob
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • Salt and pepper for seasoning

Preparation:

It all starts with selecting the right corn. Look for sweet, fresh corn with bright green husks that still have some flexibility. Before getting started, remove the husks and silks from the corn - this is where many people go wrong. Leaving the husk on while cooking not only impedes even cooking but also makes removing the silk a messy affair. Removing the husks and silks beforehand makes for a cleaner, more enjoyable grilling experience.

If you’re concerned about the corn drying out before grilling, soak the corn in cold water for 30 minutes. While this isn’t absolutely necessary, it can help prevent the corn from burning in areas.

Grilling Process:

  1. Preheat the grill: Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. This is the perfect temperature for achieving that characteristic char-grilled flavor. While your grill is heating, oil the grates to prevent sticking. We recommend using a high smoke point oil like vegetable or peanut oil.

  2. Ensure even heating: The key to perfectly grilled corn is to cook it evenly. You can achieve this by either placing the corn on the grill in a grid pattern or use a grill basket, which effectively organizes your corn for an even cook through.

  3. Add a bit of butter or seasonings: While traditional butter is a delicious addition to corn on the cob, you can also experiment with seasonings to add a unique twist to your dish. Consider paprika, garlic powder, or even a squeeze of lemon or lime juice.

  4. Stoke the fire as needed: As the corn cooks, keep an eye on the flames. No one wants burnt corn, so carefully monitor the heat and adjust the toppings to prevent any charring.

  5. Turn often and rotate: To achieve evenly cooked corn, make sure to turn it regularly. Every few minutes, rotate the corn to ensure even cooking on all sides. Keep this routine up until the corn is uniformly cooked to perfection

  6. Remove and serve: Once your corn is cooked to your liking, remove it from the grill and let it rest a couple of minutes. This ensures that the flavors meld together. Serve your grilled corn as a perfect addition to any backyard barbecue or picnic - and impress your family and friends!

Tips for The Perfect Grilled Corn:

Grilled corn is all about the timing. Cook your corn for about 10-12 minutes, rotating and turning it every few minutes, over medium-high heat. Test the corn for doneness by taking a knife or fork and lightly piercing the cob. If it’s easily penetrated, it’s perfectly grilled. Remember, it’s always better to undercook your corn slightly rather than risk it becoming too charred or burnt.

Feel free to experiment with different seasoning options like chili powder, cilantro, or Parmesan cheese. The Tuscan-inspired corn with pesto and chopped tomatoes or Mexican corn with queso fresco and chili Powder are an excellent option for a twist on a classic.

The secret to perfectly grilled corn is simple: preparation, time, and Patience. Once you master the combination of carefully removing the husks and silks, preheating the grill, and monitoring the heat, you’ll be enjoying perfectly grilled corn as often as you like! Happy grilling!
