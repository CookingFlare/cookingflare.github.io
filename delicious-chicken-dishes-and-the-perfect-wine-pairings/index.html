Delicious Chicken Dishes and the Perfect Wine Pairings


Chicken is a versatile and delicious protein that can be prepared in countless ways. Whether you prefer it grilled, roasted, or sautéed, there’s no denying the appeal of a perfectly cooked chicken dish. But what if you could take your chicken dinner to the next level by pairing it with the perfect wine? In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering chicken recipes and the ideal wine pairings to elevate your dining experience.

Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken

One classic chicken dish that never disappoints is lemon herb roasted chicken. The tangy citrus flavors combined with fragrant herbs create a symphony of taste that is hard to resist. To make this dish, start by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C).

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 2 lemons, sliced
  • Fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, and parsley)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the chicken under cold water and pat it dry with a paper towel.
  2. Season the chicken generously with salt and pepper, both inside and out.
  3. Stuff the cavity of the chicken with sliced lemons and fresh herbs.
  4. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine to hold everything in place.
  5. Place the chicken on a rack in a roasting pan and roast for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  6. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before carving and serving.

Wine Pairing: The bright and zesty flavors of lemon herb roasted chicken pair beautifully with a crisp and refreshing Sauvignon Blanc. The wine’s citrus notes complement the dish perfectly, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

Spicy Chicken Curry

If you’re in the mood for something with a kick, a spicy chicken curry is just what you need. This aromatic and flavorful dish is a crowd-pleaser and a great way to spice up your dinner routine. Here’s how you can make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) chicken pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until fragrant and golden brown.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Sprinkle the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and coriander over the chicken. Stir well to coat the chicken evenly with the spices.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover the pot and let it cook for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  5. Season with salt to taste and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Wine Pairing: The bold and spicy flavors of chicken curry call for a wine with enough depth to stand up to the heat. A medium-bodied red wine like Syrah or Malbec complements the richness of the curry while adding a touch of earthiness to the overall experience.

Crispy Fried Chicken

Who can resist the allure of crispy fried chicken? This indulgent comfort food is a guilty pleasure that never fails to satisfy. With a crunchy exterior and juicy interior, it’s a crowd favorite for a reason. Here’s how you can make it at home:

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 chicken pieces (such as drumsticks and thighs)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Dredge the chicken pieces in the flour mixture, making sure they are evenly coated.
  3. In a large skillet or deep fryer, heat enough vegetable oil to fully submerge the chicken pieces.
  4. Carefully place the chicken in the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  5. Fry the chicken for about 10-12 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Remove the chicken from the oil and let it drain on a paper towel-lined plate.

Wine Pairing: When it comes to fried chicken, a sparkling wine like Champagne or Prosecco is an excellent choice. The effervescence and acidity of the wine help cut through the richness of the fried chicken, creating a delightful contrast that enhances the overall experience.

Conclusion

Chicken dishes offer a world of possibilities, and when paired with the right wine, they can become culinary masterpieces. Whether you’re in the mood for a classic roast, a spicy curry, or some crispy fried goodness, these recipes and wine pairings are sure to impress. So next time you’re planning a chicken dinner, don’t forget to consider the perfect wine to elevate your meal to new heights. Cheers to delicious chicken dishes and unforgettable wine pairings!
