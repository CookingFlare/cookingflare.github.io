Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble: A Sweet and Tart Dessert


Who doesn’t love a delicious dessert that combines the sweetness of strawberries with the tartness of rhubarb? If you’re looking for a simple yet indulgent treat, this Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble is just what you need. With its buttery crumbly topping and juicy fruit filling, it’s the perfect balance of flavors and textures. So, let’s dive right in and discover how to make this mouthwatering dessert.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 4 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 cups of rhubarb, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract

For the Crumble Topping:

  • 1 and 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 3/4 cup of unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries, chopped rhubarb, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss everything together until the fruit is evenly coated. Set aside and let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. In another bowl, prepare the crumble topping. Mix together the all-purpose flour, oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the cold butter pieces and use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to incorporate the butter into the dry ingredients. Continue mixing until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Pour the strawberry-rhubarb filling into the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly. Take handfuls of the crumble topping and sprinkle it over the fruit, covering the entire surface.

  5. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 40-45 minutes, or until the filling is bubbling and the crumble topping is golden brown.

  6. Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, straight out of the oven. You can serve it as is or pair it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra touch of indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a sweeter dessert, you can increase the amount of sugar in the filling to suit your taste.

  • Feel free to add a handful of chopped nuts, such as almonds or walnuts, to the crumble topping for some added crunch.

  • This recipe works well with frozen strawberries and rhubarb too. Just make sure to thaw and drain the fruit before using it in the filling.

  • For a gluten-free version, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend of your choice. Be sure to check the packaging for any additional instructions or measurements.

  • If you don’t have a 9x9-inch baking dish, you can use a similar-sized dish or even individual ramekins for individual servings.

Conclusion

There you have it—a simple yet delectable Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble that will satisfy your cravings for something sweet and tart. This dessert is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a casual family gathering or a special dinner party. The combination of juicy strawberries, tangy rhubarb, and buttery crumble topping is simply irresistible. So, why not give this recipe a try and indulge in a slice of heaven? Your taste buds will thank you!
