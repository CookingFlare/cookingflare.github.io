The Best Middle Eastern Grilled Vegetable Recipes


Middle Eastern cuisine is celebrated for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and creative use of fresh ingredients. Grilled vegetables are a staple in many Middle Eastern households, serving as a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a side, a main course, or even a light snack. The combination of earthy spices, smoky char, and simple preparation elevates these dishes to a whole new level. Let’s dive into some of the best Middle Eastern-inspired grilled vegetable recipes that are sure to delight your taste buds and bring bold, authentic flavors to your table.

Grilled Eggplant with Tahini Drizzle

Eggplant is a beloved ingredient in Middle Eastern cuisine, renowned for its ability to absorb flavors and transform into something magical when grilled. In this recipe, the smoky flavor of charred eggplant is complemented by a nutty tahini drizzle.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced lengthwise
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2–3 tablespoons water (to thin the tahini sauce if needed)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice the eggplants lengthwise into 1/2 inch-thick slices. Brush both sides with olive oil and sprinkle with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the slices on the grill and cook for 4–5 minutes per side until grill marks form and the eggplant is tender.
  4. In a bowl, whisk the tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, and a few tablespoons of water until a smooth, pourable sauce forms.
  5. Arrange the grilled eggplant on a platter and drizzle the tahini sauce over the top.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

Halloumi and Vegetable Skewers with Zaatar

Halloumi cheese paired with grilled vegetables is a quintessential Middle Eastern barbecue item, and adding zaatar—a flavorful blend of herbs, sumac, sesame seeds, and salt—makes it even better.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz halloumi cheese, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 zucchini, cubed
  • 1 bell pepper (red or yellow), cubed
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon zaatar
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Skewers (soaked in water if wooden)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine olive oil, zaatar, and lemon juice. Toss the halloumi, zucchini, bell pepper, onion, and cherry tomatoes in the mixture to coat thoroughly.
  3. Thread the halloumi and vegetables onto skewers, alternating for a colorful presentation.
  4. Grill the skewers for 3–4 minutes per side, or until the vegetables are tender and the halloumi develops golden-brown grill marks.
  5. Serve hot, and enjoy alongside hummus or pita bread.

Smoky Grilled Cauliflower Steaks

Grilled cauliflower steaks are hearty, flavorful, and perfect for vegetarians seeking a substantial dish. Infused with Middle Eastern spices, this recipe transforms the humble cauliflower into a vibrant main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower, sliced into thick “steaks”
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Pomegranate seeds, for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the olive oil with coriander, turmeric, smoked paprika, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Brush the cauliflower steaks generously with the spice-infused olive oil on both sides.
  4. Grill the steaks for 5–6 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred.
  5. Serve the cauliflower steaks with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and garnish with pomegranate seeds and mint leaves for a burst of flavor.

Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Garlic-Lemon Herb Sauce

Hearty and flavorful, grilled portobello mushrooms take on a bold Middle Eastern twist in this recipe, thanks to a zesty garlic and herb sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sumac
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon freshly chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon freshly chopped dill
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Remove the stems from the portobello mushrooms and brush both sides with 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Sprinkle with sumac, salt, and chili flakes for heat.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium heat and grill the mushrooms for 4–5 minutes per side, pressing gently to ensure even cooking.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, cilantro, and dill to create the garlic herb sauce.
  4. Serve the grilled mushrooms warm with a drizzle of the garlic herb sauce on top.

Charred Okra with Harissa

Okra, though often overlooked, makes a fantastic grilled vegetable. Harissa—a spicy chili paste widely used in Middle Eastern and North African cuisines—adds depth and heat to this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh okra
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons harissa paste
  • Fresh lemon zest, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Toss the okra with olive oil, coriander, cumin, salt, and pepper in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Preheat the grill over high heat. Grill the okra for 3–4 minutes per side until lightly charred and tender.
  3. Brush the okra with harissa paste while still warm.
  4. Garnish with fresh lemon zest for a citrusy finish, and serve.

Tips for Perfectly Grilled Middle Eastern Vegetables

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Middle Eastern recipes rely on the natural flavors of vegetables, so fresh, high-quality produce is essential.
  • Marinate for Maximum Flavor: Allow the vegetables to sit in their seasoning or marinade for at least 15 minutes before grilling.
  • Control the Heat: Medium-high heat is ideal for charring vegetables without burning them.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Grill: Leave space between vegetables to allow even heat distribution.

These Middle Eastern grilled vegetable recipes are loaded with flavor, vibrant in color, and incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re planning a casual family dinner or hosting a summer barbecue, these dishes are sure to impress. They celebrate the best of Middle Eastern culinary traditions while making vegetables the star of the show. Enjoy these recipes with warm pita bread, creamy dips like baba ghanoush or hummus, or your favorite grains for a complete meal.
