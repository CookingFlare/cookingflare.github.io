Flatbread with Garlic and Rosemary: A Simple Recipe


Flatbread with garlic and rosemary is a classic, flavorful dish that’s both simple to prepare and utterly delicious. This recipe combines a soft, warm flatbread with the aromatic flavors of fresh garlic and rosemary, creating a perfect side dish or snack that will elevate any meal. Whether served with dips, as a wrap, or just on its own with a drizzle of olive oil, this flatbread will surely become a favorite in your kitchen.

In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making your own flatbread from scratch, giving you step-by-step instructions and tips on how to achieve the best texture and flavor. The addition of garlic and rosemary transforms a simple flatbread into an incredibly aromatic treat that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this aromatic flatbread, gather the following ingredients:

  • For the Dough:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
    • 1 cup warm water (about 110°F or 45°C)
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil

  • For the Topping:

    • 4 cloves garlic, finely minced
    • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried rosemary)
    • 3 tablespoons olive oil (for brushing)
    • Optional: Sea salt, for finishing

With these simple ingredients, you can make an irresistibly fragrant and fluffy flatbread, perfect for pairing with a variety of meals. The combination of rosemary and garlic adds a lovely depth of flavor, and the olive oil brings richness to the dough.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and instant yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5 minutes. The yeast should become foamy and bubbly, signaling that it’s activated and ready to use. This process is crucial, as it ensures that your dough will rise properly and achieve a light texture.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the activated yeast mixture and the olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients together until a dough starts to form.

Once the dough is combined, transfer it to a floured surface and knead it for about 5 to 8 minutes. The goal is to develop the gluten, which will give the flatbread its structure. The dough should be smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky to the touch. If the dough feels too sticky, you can add a little more flour, but be careful not to overdo it. The dough should remain soft and pliable.

2. Let the Dough Rise

After kneading, place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Allow the dough to rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size. This resting period is essential for achieving a light and fluffy texture in your flatbread.

While the dough is rising, it’s the perfect time to prepare the garlic and rosemary topping. Mince the garlic finely, and chop the fresh rosemary into small pieces. If you’re using dried rosemary, just break it up with your fingers to release its natural oils.

3. Preheat the Oven

Once the dough has doubled in size, preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats. A pizza stone helps achieve a crispy bottom crust, but it’s optional. If you don’t have a pizza stone, a baking sheet works perfectly fine.

4. Shape the Flatbreads

Once your dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and divide it into 4 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and let them rest for 5 to 10 minutes. This short resting period makes it easier to roll the dough out into thin rounds.

Now, take each ball of dough and roll it out into a flat, round shape about 6 to 8 inches in diameter. Don’t worry if the shape isn’t perfectly round – rustic, uneven edges give flatbread its charm. The dough should be thin but not so thin that it tears easily.

5. Add the Garlic and Rosemary Topping

Before baking, it’s time to add the garlic and rosemary topping. Brush each rolled-out flatbread with a thin layer of olive oil. Sprinkle a generous amount of minced garlic and rosemary on top, pressing them lightly into the dough to help them stick. If you’d like, sprinkle a little sea salt over the top to enhance the flavors further.

6. Bake the Flatbread

Place the prepared flatbreads on your preheated pizza stone or baking sheet. Bake for about 8 to 10 minutes or until the flatbreads are golden brown with slightly crispy edges. The aroma of garlic and rosemary will fill your kitchen, signaling that the flatbread is almost ready.

If you prefer your flatbread to be extra crispy, you can bake it for an additional 2 minutes. Keep an eye on it to prevent over-baking.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Once the flatbread is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and brush it with a little more olive oil for an extra shine and flavor boost. Serve it warm, either sliced into wedges or left whole, and enjoy with your favorite dips or as a side dish to your main course.

This flatbread pairs wonderfully with a variety of dishes, from hummus and tzatziki to a simple salad or a bowl of soup. It also makes a great accompaniment to grilled meats or vegetables.

Tips for Perfect Flatbread

  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Knead the dough until it’s smooth, but don’t overdo it. Overworking the dough can result in a dense, tough flatbread. The goal is to develop just enough gluten to make the bread soft and airy.

  • Adjust the Thickness: You can adjust the thickness of the flatbread based on your preferences. Thinner flatbreads will be crispier, while slightly thicker ones will be softer and more pillowy. Just keep an eye on the baking time, as thinner flatbreads will cook faster.

  • Use Fresh Garlic: For the best flavor, use fresh garlic instead of pre-minced garlic from a jar. Fresh garlic will give your flatbread a much more intense and aromatic flavor.

  • Experiment with Herbs: While rosemary is a classic pairing with garlic, feel free to experiment with other herbs. Thyme, oregano, or basil can also be used to create different flavor profiles.

  • Make Ahead: You can make the dough ahead of time and let it rise in the fridge overnight. This can help develop a deeper flavor. Just let it come to room temperature before shaping and baking.

Variations to Try

While garlic and rosemary are a classic flavor combination for flatbread, you can easily customize this recipe with different toppings to suit your tastes. Here are some ideas:

1. Cheese and Garlic Flatbread

Add a sprinkle of grated parmesan or mozzarella to the flatbread before baking. The cheese will melt beautifully and add a savory richness to the flatbread.

2. Olive and Rosemary Flatbread

For a Mediterranean twist, add sliced black or green olives to the topping. The salty olives pair wonderfully with the fresh rosemary and garlic.

3. Sun-Dried Tomato and Garlic Flatbread

Top the flatbread with chopped sun-dried tomatoes and garlic for a burst of tangy flavor. The sweet, concentrated tomato flavor balances the savory garlic and rosemary perfectly.

4. Spicy Flatbread

If you like a little heat, sprinkle some red pepper flakes or chili powder over the garlic and rosemary topping. This will add a spicy kick that complements the garlic’s bold flavor.

Conclusion

Flatbread with garlic and rosemary is an incredibly versatile and easy-to-make dish that can be served in so many ways. Whether you enjoy it as a simple snack, a side dish, or as a base for toppings, this flatbread recipe will surely become a favorite in your kitchen. With just a few basic ingredients, you can create a warm, aromatic flatbread that will enhance any meal.

By following this simple recipe and incorporating your own variations, you can enjoy the fresh, fragrant flavors of garlic and rosemary in every bite. Happy baking, and don’t forget to share your delicious creations with family and friends!
