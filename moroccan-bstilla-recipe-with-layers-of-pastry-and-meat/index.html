Moroccan B'stilla Recipe with Layers of Pastry and Meat


B’stilla, also known as pastilla, is a traditional Moroccan savory pie that is both a delight to the taste buds and a feast for the eyes. The combination of flaky pastry, tender meat, and aromatic spices is a true reflection of Moroccan cuisine’s rich cultural heritage. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating a classic Moroccan B’stilla with layers of pastry and meat that will impress your family and friends.

ingredients

  • 1 package of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 egg, beaten (for brushing pastry)

instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper, and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add the chicken to the skillet and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked chicken mixture, chopped parsley, chopped cilantro, and lemon juice. Mix well to combine.
  5. Roll out the puff pastry to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Cut the pastry into a rectangle that is slightly larger than the diameter of a 9-inch (23 cm) pie plate.
  6. Spoon the chicken mixture onto one half of the pastry, leaving a 1-inch (2.5 cm) border around the edges. Brush the edges with the beaten egg.
  7. Fold the other half of the pastry over the filling, pressing the edges to seal. Use a knife to trim any excess pastry and create a decorative edge.
  8. Place the B’stilla on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and brush the top with the beaten egg.
  9. Bake the B’stilla for 35-40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the filling is hot and bubbly.
  10. Remove the B’stilla from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes before serving.

tips and variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle some chopped almonds or pine nuts on top of the pastry before baking.
  • For a vegetarian option, substitute the chicken with cooked and mashed eggplant, zucchini, or mushrooms.
  • To make individual servings, cut the pastry into squares and fill with the chicken mixture. Fold the pastry in half and press the edges to seal. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

With this Moroccan B’stilla recipe, you will be able to create a delicious and authentic Moroccan dish that is sure to impress your family and friends. The combination of flaky pastry, tender meat, and aromatic spices is a true reflection of Moroccan cuisine’s rich cultural heritage. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the flavors of Morocco in the comfort of your own home.
