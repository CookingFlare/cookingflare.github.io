10 Fresh Herb Recipes for Summer Cooking


Summer is the perfect time to embrace fresh herbs in your cooking. The season brings a bounty of vibrant flavors that can elevate any dish, adding brightness, fragrance, and a touch of nature’s goodness. Whether you’re grilling, roasting, or preparing fresh salads, herbs can turn ordinary meals into extraordinary experiences. Here, we’ll explore 10 delicious and creative recipes that use fresh herbs, perfect for bringing summer to your table.

1. Lemon Herb Grilled Chicken

Grilled chicken is a summertime staple, and when combined with fresh herbs, it’s taken to a whole new level of flavor. This recipe uses a blend of lemon, thyme, and rosemary to infuse the chicken with aromatic goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Coat the chicken breasts in the marinade and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, until fully cooked.
  5. Serve with a side of grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.

The combination of lemon and herbs creates a bright, refreshing dish that’s perfect for a summer barbecue or weeknight dinner.

2. Mint and Cucumber Salad

This refreshing salad is perfect for hot summer days. The coolness of cucumber combined with the fresh aroma of mint creates a crisp and hydrating side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large cucumbers (sliced thin)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cucumbers thinly and place them in a large bowl.
  2. Add the chopped mint leaves.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the dressing over the cucumbers and mint, tossing gently to coat.
  5. Let the salad sit for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld before serving.

This salad is a delightful addition to any summer meal, offering a burst of freshness that pairs perfectly with grilled meats or seafood.

3. Herb-Infused Olive Oil

Herb-infused olive oil is a versatile condiment that can be used to enhance everything from pasta to grilled vegetables. It’s easy to make and lasts for weeks when stored properly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh oregano
  • 1 clove garlic (crushed)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over low heat.
  2. Add the rosemary, thyme, oregano, and garlic to the oil.
  3. Let the herbs simmer in the oil for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool.
  5. Strain the oil into a bottle or jar, discarding the herbs and garlic.

This herb-infused olive oil is perfect for drizzling over salads, roasted vegetables, or fresh bread. You can also use it as a marinade for meats or seafood.

4. Basil Pesto Pasta

No summer meal is complete without a fresh basil pesto. This classic Italian sauce brings a punch of flavor with its blend of basil, garlic, and parmesan.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (packed)
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (toasted)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/2 cup parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb pasta of your choice

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, and parmesan. Pulse until finely chopped.
  2. With the processor running, slowly add olive oil until the pesto is smooth and creamy.
  3. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions.
  4. Toss the hot pasta with the pesto until well-coated.
  5. Garnish with additional parmesan and fresh basil leaves.

This fresh, vibrant pasta dish is a true celebration of summer flavors. The basil pesto can also be used as a dip, spread, or topping for pizza.

5. Herbed Goat Cheese Crostini

This easy appetizer is a perfect way to showcase fresh herbs. The creamy goat cheese paired with fresh herbs and toasted bread creates a flavorful bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette (sliced into 1/2-inch rounds)
  • 8 oz goat cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh chives (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet and toast in the oven for 10-12 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  3. In a bowl, combine the goat cheese, parsley, chives, thyme, honey, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the herbed goat cheese mixture over the toasted baguette slices.
  5. Serve immediately as an appetizer or snack.

This herbed goat cheese crostini is a delicious, bite-sized treat that pairs well with a chilled glass of white wine.

6. Rosemary Lemon Roasted Potatoes

Roasted potatoes are a classic comfort food, and when paired with rosemary and lemon, they become something extraordinary. The crisp edges and aromatic flavors make these potatoes the perfect side dish for any summer meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs baby potatoes (halved)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary (chopped)
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the halved potatoes in olive oil, rosemary, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the potatoes out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 30-35 minutes, or until golden and crispy, stirring halfway through.
  5. Serve as a side dish to your favorite summer meals.

The combination of rosemary and lemon adds a zesty twist to these roasted potatoes, making them the perfect accompaniment to grilled meats or fish.

7. Herb-Infused Watermelon Salad

This fresh and fruity salad is perfect for a hot summer day. The sweetness of watermelon is balanced by the savory notes of fresh herbs, creating a harmonious dish that’s both refreshing and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon (cubed)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cubed watermelon, mint, and basil.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, honey, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the watermelon and herbs, tossing gently to combine.
  4. Let the salad sit for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld before serving.

This watermelon salad is an ideal addition to any summer picnic or barbecue, offering a burst of flavor and color.

8. Chive and Dill Cream Cheese Spread

This easy-to-make spread is perfect for summer brunches or picnics. The creamy texture paired with the fresh, tangy flavor of chives and dill is a match made in heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz cream cheese (softened)
  • 2 tbsp fresh chives (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese, chives, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix until smooth and well-combined.
  3. Serve the cream cheese spread with crackers, fresh vegetables, or on toasted bread.

This herb-packed spread is great for adding a burst of flavor to your favorite snacks or as a savory topping for bagels.

9. Thyme-Infused Grilled Peaches

Grilled peaches are a summer dessert that combines the natural sweetness of fruit with the earthy, aromatic flavor of thyme. This simple dish is an ideal way to end a summer meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches (halved and pitted)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the peach halves with melted butter and drizzle with honey.
  3. Sprinkle with fresh thyme.
  4. Grill the peaches for 4-5 minutes on each side, until grill marks appear and the peaches are softened.
  5. Serve warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

These grilled peaches are a decadent and aromatic dessert that captures the essence of summer.

10. Garlic and Herb Shrimp Skewers

Shrimp is a fantastic summer protein that pairs wonderfully with a variety of fresh herbs. These garlic and herb shrimp skewers are packed with flavor and perfect for grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh basil (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the shrimp, garlic, parsley, basil, olive oil, salt, pepper, and lemon juice.
  2. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side, until pink and cooked through.
  5. Serve the shrimp skewers with a side of grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.

These garlic and herb shrimp skewers are an easy, flavorful dish that will be the star of your summer cookout.

Conclusion

Herbs are an essential part of summer cooking, bringing vibrant flavors and aromas to any meal. Whether you’re grilling, roasting, or preparing fresh salads, fresh herbs can add a delightful twist to your dishes. From simple herb-infused oils to fresh pesto pasta, these 10 recipes will help you make the most of the herbs growing in your garden or at your local farmer’s market. Embrace the season, and enjoy these herb-filled dishes that will make your summer cooking truly unforgettable!
