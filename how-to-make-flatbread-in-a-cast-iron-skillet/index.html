How to Make Flatbread in a Cast Iron Skillet


Making homemade flatbread in a cast iron skillet is a delightful and satisfying cooking experience. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting to explore the joys of baking, this recipe offers a simple and delicious way to create soft, warm flatbreads that pair perfectly with a variety of dishes. From dipping in sauces to wrapping around grilled meats or fresh vegetables, flatbread is versatile and easy to make in your own kitchen.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to make your very own flatbread in a cast iron skillet. We’ll also share tips and tricks to ensure your flatbreads turn out perfectly every time, offering a chewy texture with a crispy exterior.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To get started, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (or whole wheat flour for a healthier version)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or melted butter for a richer flavor)
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast (optional for a fluffier texture)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, helps activate yeast if you’re using it)
  • 1 tablespoon yogurt (optional for added softness)

With these basic ingredients, you can make the dough that will turn into the perfect flatbread. If you choose not to use yeast, you’ll still end up with deliciously soft and pliable flatbread, but yeast will provide an extra layer of fluffiness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Mixing the Dough

Begin by mixing your dry ingredients in a large mixing bowl. Combine the flour and salt. If you’re using yeast, add the sugar as well, and then sprinkle in the instant yeast. If you’re skipping the yeast, you can go ahead and add the olive oil and yogurt directly.

  • Tip: Use a whisk to ensure the dry ingredients are well-combined and any lumps are broken up. This step is crucial for achieving smooth dough.

Add the warm water little by little, stirring constantly until the dough starts to come together. You can use your hands to knead the dough once it’s mostly combined.

  • Tip: Make sure the water is warm, not hot, to prevent killing the yeast if you’re using it. The ideal temperature should be around 110°F (43°C).

If you’re using yogurt, it will help make the dough a bit more pliable and give it a slightly tangy taste, which can complement savory toppings. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic.

2. Let the Dough Rest (Optional)

If you’re using yeast, you’ll need to let the dough rest for about an hour in a warm place to rise. This resting time allows the dough to become airy and soft, creating the ideal texture. If you choose not to use yeast, you can skip this step and move on to the next part.

Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap to prevent the dough from drying out. After about an hour, you’ll notice that the dough has doubled in size.

3. Divide the Dough into Balls

Once your dough is ready, whether after rising or immediately after mixing, turn it out onto a floured surface. Divide the dough into 6 to 8 equal portions, depending on how large or small you want your flatbreads to be.

Roll each portion into a small ball. If you’re making smaller flatbreads, aim for about a golf ball-sized portion. Larger ones might need more dough. Let these dough balls rest for a few minutes while you prepare your skillet.

4. Roll the Dough Into Flatbreads

Now, it’s time to roll out the dough. Take one ball of dough at a time and use a rolling pin to flatten it out on a lightly floured surface. Roll it into a thin, round shape—aim for a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm).

  • Tip: If the dough sticks to the rolling pin, lightly dust it with more flour. You can also turn the dough a quarter turn as you roll it out to ensure it maintains a round shape.

Don’t worry about perfect shapes. Part of the charm of homemade flatbread is its rustic appearance. The uneven edges just add to the homemade vibe.

5. Heat Your Cast Iron Skillet

Place your cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. You want the skillet to be very hot before adding the flatbread, so give it a few minutes to heat up. A hot skillet will give your flatbread the perfect crispy texture on the outside, while keeping it soft inside.

  • Tip: You don’t need to add any oil to the skillet for cooking. The dry heat from the cast iron will be enough to cook the flatbread. However, if you prefer a bit of extra flavor, you can brush the skillet with a thin layer of olive oil or butter before cooking.

6. Cooking the Flatbread

Once the skillet is hot, carefully place your rolled-out flatbread into the pan. It should immediately sizzle when it hits the skillet.

Cook the flatbread for about 1-2 minutes on the first side. You’ll notice bubbles start to form on the surface as it cooks. When the edges start to lift from the skillet, flip the flatbread over with tongs or a spatula. Cook the second side for about 1-2 minutes as well, until golden brown spots appear on both sides.

  • Tip: If the flatbread starts to puff up too much, use the back of the spatula to gently press it down, ensuring even cooking.

Repeat the process with each dough ball, placing them one by one into the skillet. Don’t overcrowd the skillet; cook the flatbreads in batches if needed.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Once all your flatbreads are cooked, stack them together and cover them with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm. Serve immediately with your favorite dip, like hummus or tzatziki, or use them to wrap around grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or cheese.

If you want to elevate the flavor, you can brush the warm flatbreads with a little melted butter or olive oil, and sprinkle them with herbs such as rosemary, thyme, or garlic for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for Perfect Flatbread

  • Consistency of the Dough: If your dough feels too sticky, add a bit more flour, but be careful not to overdo it. The dough should be soft and slightly tacky, but not sticky.
  • Don’t Skip the Resting Time: If you’re using yeast, allowing the dough to rise will make your flatbreads fluffier and softer. If you skip the rise, the flatbreads will still be delicious, but they might be denser.
  • Control the Heat: Keep the skillet at medium-high heat. Too high, and the flatbread might burn; too low, and it won’t cook through properly. Adjust the heat as needed.
  • Flavor Variations: For a unique twist, try adding spices to your dough, such as cumin, coriander, or smoked paprika. Fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro can also be mixed into the dough for a fresh, aromatic flavor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Use Whole Wheat Flour?

Yes, whole wheat flour can be used to make a healthier version of flatbread. The texture will be slightly denser, but it will still be delicious. If you’re using whole wheat flour, you may need to add a little more water to achieve the right dough consistency.

Can I Freeze Flatbread?

Yes! Flatbreads can be easily frozen. After cooking and cooling, stack the flatbreads with parchment paper between each one to prevent them from sticking together. Wrap the stack in plastic wrap or foil and store in a freezer bag. When ready to eat, simply reheat them in a skillet or microwave.

Can I Add Toppings to the Flatbread While Cooking?

While the dough is still in the skillet, it can be difficult to add toppings directly, as the flatbread cooks quickly. However, you can brush the cooked flatbread with olive oil, sprinkle with herbs, or add cheese and vegetables immediately after cooking for a delicious, custom flatbread.

Conclusion

Making flatbread in a cast iron skillet is an incredibly rewarding and simple process. Whether you’re making it to serve alongside your favorite curry, as a base for a wrap, or simply to enjoy with a light spread of butter, homemade flatbread always tastes better. With just a few basic ingredients and a bit of practice, you can master this flatbread recipe and impress your friends and family with your culinary skills.

The next time you’re craving something warm and comforting, skip the store-bought options and whip up a batch of flatbread in your trusty cast iron skillet. The combination of crispy edges and soft center will have you coming back for more, time and time again. Happy cooking!
