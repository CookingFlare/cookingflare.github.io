Indulge in Traditional Greek Feta Cheese Recipes


Feta cheese, the crumbly and tangy delight of Greece, is more than just a delicious topping for salads. It’s a versatile ingredient that can elevate a wide array of dishes, thanks to its rich flavor and creamy texture. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a culinary novice, incorporating feta into your meals can transform your dishes from ordinary to extraordinary. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of traditional Greek recipes that highlight the beauty of feta cheese. From savory pies to refreshing salads, let’s dive into the world of Greek cuisine and indulge in the deliciousness of feta!

1. Classic Greek Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 100g Kalamata olives
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh oregano for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and bell pepper.
  2. Add the crumbled feta cheese and Kalamata olives on top.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and red wine vinegar and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss gently to combine, being careful not to break the feta too much.
  5. Garnish with fresh oregano before serving.

This classic Greek salad is refreshing and perfect for a summer day. The saltiness of the feta pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the tomatoes, creating a balanced and delicious dish that’s sure to impress.

2. Spanakopita (Spinach and Feta Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 package of phyllo pastry
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooled spinach mixture, crumbled feta, beaten eggs, salt, and pepper.
  5. Lay one sheet of phyllo pastry in a greased baking dish, brushing it lightly with olive oil. Repeat this step with several layers (about 5 sheets).
  6. Spoon the spinach and feta filling onto the pastry and spread it evenly.
  7. Cover with more phyllo sheets, brushing each layer with olive oil.
  8. Once assembled, sprinkle sesame seeds on top.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until golden brown.

Spanakopita is a beloved Greek dish that showcases the combination of spinach and feta beautifully. The flaky layers of phyllo pastry cradling the savory filling create a delightful texture that will leave your guests craving more.

3. Feta and Herb Stuffed Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or rice
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds.
  3. In a bowl, combine the crumbled feta, cooked quinoa, cherry tomatoes, parsley, basil, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff the mixture into each bell pepper, pressing down gently to pack it in.
  5. Place the stuffed peppers upright in a baking dish and add a splash of water to the bottom of the dish.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the peppers are tender.

These feta and herb stuffed peppers are not only vibrant but also healthy and satisfying. The combination of fresh herbs and the creamy feta adds a delightful flavor that makes for a perfect main course or side dish.

4. Feta and Olive Tapenade

Ingredients:

  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 100g Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 tablespoons capers
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the feta cheese, Kalamata olives, capers, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Pulse until you achieve a chunky paste. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, if needed.
  3. Transfer the tapenade to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh thyme.

Serve this feta and olive tapenade with crusty bread or pita chips as a flavorful appetizer. The saltiness of the olives and the creaminess of the feta create a delectable spread that’s perfect for sharing.

5. Baked Feta with Honey and Thyme

Ingredients:

  • 200g feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Place the block of feta cheese in a small baking dish.
  3. Drizzle the feta with honey and olive oil, and sprinkle with fresh thyme and black pepper.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the feta is warm and starting to soften.

This baked feta with honey and thyme is a simple yet indulgent dish that makes for an excellent appetizer. The sweet honey complements the saltiness of the feta beautifully, and the fresh thyme adds an aromatic touch that will tantalize your taste buds.

6. Feta and Spinach Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 250g pasta of your choice (penne, fusilli, etc.)
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 300g fresh spinach
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and drain the rest.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes, sautéing until fragrant.
  3. Add the fresh spinach and cook until wilted.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet along with the reserved pasta water and crumbled feta.
  5. Toss everything together until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.

The feta and spinach pasta dish is a quick and easy weeknight meal that doesn’t skimp on flavor. The creamy feta melts into the pasta, creating a luscious sauce that coats every bite.

7. Feta and Beetroot Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized beetroots, roasted and diced
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
  • 2 cups arugula or mixed greens
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the roasted beetroot, crumbled feta, walnuts, and arugula.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

This feta and beetroot salad is a feast for the eyes and the palate. The earthy sweetness of the beets pairs perfectly with the tangy feta, while the walnuts add a satisfying crunch.

Conclusion

Greek feta cheese is a culinary treasure that can enhance a multitude of dishes. From fresh salads to comforting baked goods, its versatility makes it a staple in kitchens around the world. The recipes highlighted in this blog post are just the beginning of what you can create with feta cheese. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking for a quick weeknight meal, these traditional Greek feta cheese recipes will surely delight your taste buds and impress your guests. So, roll up your sleeves and indulge in the rich flavors of Greek cuisine!
