Picnic Skillets: Easy and Flavorful One-Pot Meals


When it comes to picnics, the last thing you want to worry about is a complicated meal prep. That’s where picnic skillets come in – these easy and flavorful one-pot meals are perfect for outdoor dining. Whether you’re planning a family picnic, a romantic outing, or a casual get-together with friends, these picnic skillet recipes are sure to impress. Let’s dive into some delicious and fuss-free recipes that will elevate your picnic experience.

Chicken and Vegetable Picnic Skillet

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and dried oregano.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the seasoned chicken breasts and cook until golden brown on both sides, about 5-6 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the cherry tomatoes, bell peppers, zucchini, red onion, and minced garlic. Sauté for 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, nestling it among the vegetables. Cover the skillet and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve directly from the skillet for a rustic and charming picnic meal.

Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya Skillet

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz Andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the sliced Andouille sausage over medium heat until browned. Remove the sausage from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the diced bell pepper, celery, onion, and minced garlic. Sauté for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
  3. Add the long-grain rice to the skillet and stir to coat the rice with the vegetable mixture.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, and Cajun seasoning. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, covered, for 20 minutes.
  6. Stir in the shrimp and the cooked Andouille sausage. Cover and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the shrimp are pink and opaque.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve this zesty jambalaya directly from the skillet for a flavorful picnic feast.

Veggie Pesto Pasta Skillet

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz fusilli pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 3 tablespoons pesto
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fusilli pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, combine the cherry tomatoes, broccoli florets, and sliced mushrooms. Sauté over medium heat for 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and stir in the pesto until everything is well coated.
  4. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the pasta and vegetables, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve this vibrant and satisfying veggie pesto pasta directly from the skillet for a delightful picnic lunch.

These picnic skillets not only make for easy and convenient meals, but they also bring a touch of rustic charm to your outdoor dining experience. So, pack your favorite skillet, gather your loved ones, and head out for a memorable picnic with these delicious one-pot wonders. Happy picnicking!
