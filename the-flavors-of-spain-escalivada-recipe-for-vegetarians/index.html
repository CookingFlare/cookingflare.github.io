The Flavors of Spain: Escalivada Recipe for Vegetarians


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine, you’re likely familiar with the rich, robust flavors that are characteristic of the region. One dish that perfectly encapsulates these flavors is Escalivada, a traditional Catalan dish that celebrates the bounty of fresh vegetables. While the original recipe typically includes fish, we’re going to put a vegetarian spin on this classic dish, making it accessible to all.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 yellow bell peppers
  • 2 red onions
  • 4 ripe tomatoes
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

  • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  • Place the eggplants, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes directly on the grill grates.
  • Grill the vegetables, turning occasionally, until the skins are charred and the flesh is tender. This should take about 10-15 minutes for the peppers and tomatoes and 20-25 minutes for the eggplants and onions.

Step 2: Let the Vegetables Cool

  • Once the vegetables are charred and tender, remove them from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes. This will make them easier to handle.

Step 3: Peel and Chop

  • After the vegetables have cooled, peel off the charred skins from the peppers, eggplants, and tomatoes.
  • Cut the vegetables into long strips and arrange them on a serving platter.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

  • In a small bowl, whisk together the minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Drizzle the garlic-infused olive oil over the grilled vegetables.
  • Allow the flavors to meld together for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

  • Before serving, garnish the Escalivada with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness.
  • This dish can be served warm, at room temperature, or even cold, making it a versatile option for any occasion.

Serving Suggestions

Escalivada is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Serve it as a standalone appetizer, accompanied by crusty bread or crackers.
  • Use it as a topping for crostini or bruschetta for a delightful Spanish twist on a classic dish.
  • Incorporate it into a salad by tossing it with mixed greens and a simple vinaigrette for a light and refreshing meal.

Final Thoughts

The beauty of Escalivada lies in its simplicity and the incredible flavors that are coaxed out of just a few humble ingredients. This vegetarian twist on a classic Spanish dish allows everyone to experience the vibrant tastes of Catalonia. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply looking for a new way to enjoy your favorite vegetables, Escalivada is sure to delight your taste buds and transport you to the sun-soaked shores of Spain. So, fire up the grill and get ready to savor the essence of Spanish cuisine with this delightful vegetarian Escalivada recipe!
