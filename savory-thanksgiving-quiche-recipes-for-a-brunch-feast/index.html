Savory Thanksgiving Quiche Recipes for a Brunch Feast


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, indulging in delicious food. While the traditional Thanksgiving dinner is a highlight for many, why not switch things up this year and surprise your loved ones with a savory quiche feast for brunch? Quiches are versatile, easy to make, and can be customized to suit everyone’s tastes. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering savory Thanksgiving quiche recipes that will elevate your brunch spread and leave your guests wanting more.

1. Turkey and Cranberry Quiche

This quiche combines the classic flavors of Thanksgiving with a twist. Start by sautéing leftover turkey, onions, and garlic in a skillet until golden brown. In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, shredded cheddar cheese, dried cranberries, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the mixture over a pre-baked pie crust, then top with the sautéed turkey mixture. Bake at 350°F for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden brown. Serve warm and garnish with fresh cranberries for an extra festive touch.

2. Sweet Potato and Sage Quiche

This quiche is a delightful combination of sweet and savory flavors. Start by roasting diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper until tender. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, cream, chopped fresh sage, grated Parmesan cheese, and a dash of nutmeg. Place the roasted sweet potatoes in a pre-baked pie crust, then pour the egg mixture over them. Bake at 375°F for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is puffed and golden. Let it cool slightly before serving, and garnish with additional sage leaves for a pop of color.

3. Mushroom and Gruyère Quiche

For the mushroom lovers at your Thanksgiving brunch, this quiche is a must-try. Start by sautéing sliced mushrooms and shallots in butter until they release their moisture and turn golden brown. In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, heavy cream, grated Gruyère cheese, chopped fresh thyme, salt, and pepper. Spread the sautéed mushrooms and shallots evenly over a pre-baked pie crust, then pour the egg mixture over them. Bake at 375°F for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly browned. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

4. Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Quiche

Brussels sprouts are a quintessential Thanksgiving vegetable, and they shine in this delicious quiche. Start by blanching Brussels sprouts in boiling water for a few minutes, then drain and chop them into bite-sized pieces. Cook bacon in a skillet until crispy, then remove and crumble it. In a mixing bowl, combine eggs, milk, shredded Gruyère cheese, chopped cooked bacon, chopped Brussels sprouts, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake at 375°F for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden. Let it cool slightly before serving.

Conclusion

These savory Thanksgiving quiche recipes are sure to impress your guests and add a unique twist to your holiday brunch feast. From the traditional flavors of turkey and cranberry to the earthy combination of mushrooms and Gruyère, each quiche offers a delightful explosion of flavors that will make your taste buds dance. So this Thanksgiving, break away from tradition and treat your loved ones to a memorable and delicious brunch. Cheers to a happy and flavorful Thanksgiving!
