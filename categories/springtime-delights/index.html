Elegant Entertaining: Spring Pea and Prosciutto Crostini

When it comes to elegant entertaining, nothing quite sets the tone like a beautifully presented appetizer. Spring pea and prosciutto crostini is a delightful and sophisticated dish that perfectly captures the fresh flavors of the season. This recipe is not only visually stunning but also incredibly flavorful, making it the ideal choice for any springtime gathering, from garden parties to bridal showers. Ingredients 1 baguette, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds 2 cups fresh or frozen peas 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil 1 garlic clove, minced Salt and pepper to taste 8 slices prosciutto 4 ounces goat cheese Zest of 1 lemon Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Pea Puree In a pot of boiling salted water, cook the peas until tender, about 3-4 minutes. If using frozen peas, follow the package instructions. Drain the peas and transfer them to a food processor. Add the chopped mint, half of the olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to the peas. Pulse the mixture until smooth, adding more olive oil if necessary to achieve a creamy consistency. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Step 2: Make the Crostini Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet and brush both sides with olive oil. Season with a pinch of salt. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crostini are golden and crisp. Remove from the oven and cool slightly. Step 3: Assemble the Crostini Spread a generous layer of pea puree onto each crostini. Tear the prosciutto slices into smaller pieces and drape them over the pea puree. Crumble the goat cheese over the prosciutto. Finish with a sprinkle of lemon zest and a drizzle of olive oil. Step 4: Serve and Enjoy Arrange the spring pea and prosciutto crostini on a platter and serve immediately. These delightful bites are best enjoyed fresh, allowing the vibrant flavors to shine. ...
