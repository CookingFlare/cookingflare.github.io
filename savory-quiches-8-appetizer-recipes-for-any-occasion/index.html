Savory Quiches: 8 Appetizer Recipes for Any Occasion


Savory quiches are an excellent choice for any occasion, whether it’s a casual gathering or a formal event. These versatile and flavorful dishes are perfect for serving as an appetizer or even as a main course. In this article, we’ll explore 8 savory quiche recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

Quiche Lorraine

Quiche Lorraine is a classic French dish that originated in the Lorraine region. It’s a simple yet elegant quiche filled with bacon, onions, and cheese. To make Quiche Lorraine, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, cook 6 slices of bacon until crispy, then chop them into small pieces. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of grated Gruyère cheese. Add the cooked bacon, 1/2 cup of chopped onion, and a pinch of salt and pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Spinach and Feta Quiche

For a healthier quiche option, try the Spinach and Feta Quiche. This quiche is filled with fresh spinach, crumbled feta cheese, and a hint of garlic. To make Spinach and Feta Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1/2 cup of chopped onion and cook until softened, then add 2 cups of fresh spinach leaves and cook until wilted. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese. Add the cooked spinach mixture, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Mushroom and Leek Quiche

For a savory and earthy quiche option, try the Mushroom and Leek Quiche. This quiche is filled with sautéed mushrooms and leeks, and a hint of thyme. To make Mushroom and Leek Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms and 1/2 cup of sliced leeks, and cook until softened. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of grated cheddar cheese. Add the cooked mushroom and leek mixture, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Broccoli and Cheddar Quiche

For a vegetarian quiche option, try the Broccoli and Cheddar Quiche. This quiche is filled with steamed broccoli, cheddar cheese, and a hint of nutmeg. To make Broccoli and Cheddar Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, steam 1 cup of broccoli florets until tender. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of grated cheddar cheese. Add the cooked broccoli, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche

For a seafood-inspired quiche option, try the Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche. This quiche is filled with smoked salmon, fresh dill, and a hint of lemon. To make Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of grated cheddar cheese. Add 6 ounces of smoked salmon, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh dill, and a squeeze of lemon juice to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Asparagus and Goat Cheese Quiche

For a light and refreshing quiche option, try the Asparagus and Goat Cheese Quiche. This quiche is filled with blanched asparagus, goat cheese, and a hint of parsley. To make Asparagus and Goat Cheese Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of crumbled goat cheese. Add 1 cup of blanched asparagus spears, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Roasted Red Pepper and Manchego Quiche

For a sweet and savory quiche option, try the Roasted Red Pepper and Manchego Quiche. This quiche is filled with roasted red peppers, Manchego cheese, and a hint of paprika. To make Roasted Red Pepper and Manchego Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of grated Manchego cheese. Add 1/2 cup of roasted red pepper, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Caramelized Onion and Blue Cheese Quiche

For a savory and pungent quiche option, try the Caramelized Onion and Blue Cheese Quiche. This quiche is filled with caramelized onions, blue cheese, and a hint of thyme. To make Caramelized Onion and Blue Cheese Quiche, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, cook 1 large onion over low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until caramelized. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of crumbled blue cheese. Add the cooked onion, a pinch of salt, and a few grinds of black pepper to the bowl. Pour the mixture into a pre-baked pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown.

Conclusion

=====================================

Savory quiches are a versatile and delicious option for any occasion. Whether you’re looking for a classic Quiche Lorraine or a more adventurous option like the Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche, there’s a quiche recipe here for you. With their rich flavors and flaky crusts, quiches are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. So go ahead and get creative in the kitchen, and experiment with different ingredients and flavors to create your own unique quiche recipes.
