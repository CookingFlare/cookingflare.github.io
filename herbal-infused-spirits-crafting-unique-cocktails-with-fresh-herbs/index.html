Herbal Infused Spirits: Crafting Unique Cocktails with Fresh Herbs


In the world of mixology, spirits are the foundation upon which a drink’s flavor profile is built. While traditional spirits like gin, vodka, and whiskey have their own unique characteristics, they can also be elevated by the addition of fresh herbs. Herbal infused spirits are a growing trend in the cocktail world, offering a new level of complexity and depth to classic drinks. In this post, we’ll explore the art of crafting unique cocktails with fresh herbs and provide a few recipes to get you started.

What are Herbal Infused Spirits?

Herbal infused spirits are spirits that have been infused with the flavors and aromas of fresh herbs. This can be achieved through a variety of methods, including steeping herbs in the spirit, adding herbs to the spirit during distillation, or even using herbs as a flavor component in the fermentation process.

The Benefits of Herbal Infused Spirits

Herbal infused spirits offer a number of benefits over traditional spirits. For one, they can add a level of complexity and depth to a drink that would be difficult to achieve with traditional ingredients. Additionally, herbal infused spirits can provide a number of health benefits, as many herbs have been shown to have medicinal properties.

How to Make Herbal Infused Spirits

Making herbal infused spirits is a relatively simple process. To get started, you’ll need a few basic ingredients, including the spirit of your choice, fresh herbs, and a clean glass jar or container. Here’s a basic recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup spirit (vodka, gin, or whiskey work well)
  • 1/4 cup fresh herbs (such as mint, basil, or lemongrass)
  • 1 glass jar or container with a lid

Instructions:

  1. Combine the spirit and herbs in the glass jar or container.
  2. Seal the jar and let it sit at room temperature for 2-3 weeks, shaking the jar every day or two.
  3. After the infusion period, strain the spirit through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle.
  4. Store the infused spirit in the refrigerator and use within a few weeks.

Recipes Using Herbal Infused Spirits

Now that you have your herbal infused spirit, it’s time to start experimenting with recipes. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

Minty Fresh Gimlet

  • 1 1/2 oz herbal infused vodka
  • 1/2 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • 1/2 oz mint leaves
  • Ice
  • Lime wheel, for garnish

Combine the herbal infused vodka, lime juice, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Add a handful of mint leaves and muddle gently. Strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with a lime wheel.

Basil Gimlet

  • 1 1/2 oz herbal infused gin
  • 1/2 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • 1/2 oz basil leaves
  • Ice
  • Lime wheel, for garnish

Combine the herbal infused gin, lime juice, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Add a handful of basil leaves and muddle gently. Strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with a lime wheel.

Lemongrass Collins

  • 2 oz herbal infused whiskey
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • 1/2 oz lemongrass syrup (see below)
  • Splash of soda water
  • Ice
  • Lemon wheel, for garnish

Combine the herbal infused whiskey, lemon juice, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Add a splash of lemongrass syrup and shake gently. Strain into a chilled Collins glass filled with ice and top with a splash of soda water. Garnish with a lemon wheel.

Lemongrass Syrup

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemongrass leaves

Combine the water and sugar in a saucepan and bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and add the lemongrass leaves. Let steep for at least 30 minutes, or until the syrup has cooled. Strain the syrup through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle and store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Conclusion

Herbal infused spirits offer a new level of complexity and depth to classic cocktails. By infusing spirits with fresh herbs, you can create unique and delicious drinks that are sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or just starting out, we hope this guide has inspired you to experiment with herbal infused spirits and create your own unique cocktails. Happy mixing!
