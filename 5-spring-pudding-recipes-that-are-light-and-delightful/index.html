5 Spring Pudding Recipes That Are Light and Delightful


Spring is the perfect time to indulge in light and refreshing desserts. With the weather warming up and nature coming back to life, there’s no better way to celebrate than with a delicious spring pudding. Whether you’re hosting a garden party or simply want to treat yourself, these five spring pudding recipes are guaranteed to satisfy your sweet tooth and leave you feeling delighted.

1. Lemon Berry Trifle

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of ladyfingers
  • 1 cup of fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup of fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup of fresh raspberries
  • 2 cups of vanilla pudding
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Layer the bottom of a trifle dish with ladyfingers.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the vanilla pudding with the lemon zest.
  3. Spread a layer of the lemon-infused pudding over the ladyfingers.
  4. Add a layer of mixed berries - strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries.
  5. Repeat the layers until the dish is filled, ending with a layer of berries on top.
  6. Garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

2. Mango Coconut Chia Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup of chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • Sliced almonds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blend the diced mango until smooth.
  2. In a bowl, combine the mango puree, coconut milk, chia seeds, and honey. Mix well.
  3. Pour the mixture into individual serving glasses or jars.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the chia seeds to thicken.
  5. Garnish with sliced almonds before serving.

3. Strawberry Rhubarb Bread Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of stale bread, cubed
  • 2 cups of fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup of rhubarb, chopped
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt.
  3. Place the cubed bread in the greased baking dish, followed by the sliced strawberries and chopped rhubarb.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the bread, strawberries, and rhubarb, making sure everything is evenly coated.
  5. Let the bread pudding sit for 10 minutes to allow the bread to absorb the liquid.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set.
  7. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

4. Coconut Lime Rice Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cooked jasmine rice
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • Zest and juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup of honey
  • Shredded coconut for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the cooked rice, coconut milk, lime zest, lime juice, and honey.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and the rice is tender.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Divide the rice pudding into individual serving bowls or glasses.
  5. Garnish with shredded coconut.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

5. Raspberry Matcha Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh or frozen raspberries
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of matcha powder
  • 1 cup of Greek yogurt
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the raspberries, water, honey, and matcha powder.
  2. Cook over medium heat until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens slightly.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. In a bowl, mix the raspberry mixture with Greek yogurt until well combined.
  5. Divide the pudding into serving glasses or bowls.
  6. Garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

Enjoy these delightful spring pudding recipes and let your taste buds dance with joy! Each recipe offers a unique combination of flavors and textures that are perfect for the season. Whether you prefer the zesty tang of lemon, the tropical sweetness of mango, or the classic pairing of strawberries and rhubarb, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So go ahead, embrace the flavors of spring, and treat yourself to a light and refreshing dessert that will leave you wanting more.
