The Secret to Making Perfect Meringue Cookies


Meringue cookies are a delight for the senses with their crisp outer shell and soft, marshmallow-like interior. These simple, yet sophisticated treats present a great challenge to many home bakers, as the key ingredient – the meringue – is temperamental and requires a gentle hand. Here’s my best trick for achieving perfect meringue cookies every time!

Firstly, ensure that your egg whites are at room temperature. Freshness is key, and cold egg whites will cause your meringue to weep during baking. Beat the egg whites in a stainless steel or glass bowl using a mixer with a well-fitted balloon whip attachment. Avoid plastic bowls, as they interfere with the air incorporation process. Gradually add 1/4 teaspoon of salt to the egg whites. Since your meringue cookie batter will be mostly sugar, it all evens out taste-wise and you won’t notice the salt in the finished product.

Once the egg whites are frothy, start adding sugar. Not too fast, not too slow – just right. Here I suggest you use superfine sugar as it dissolves quickly, resulting in a smoother meringue. Whisk your dry mixture (powdered sugar, cream of tartar, and your flavoring of choice) together and begin adding it to the egg whites in small amounts as you continue beating. This process will take approximately 6-8 minutes to achieve stiff peaks. Too fast, and you’ll incorporate air too quickly, leading to a weak and short cookie that may not hold its shape. Too slow, and you risk deflating the meringue.

At the peak stage, carefully add your ground flavoring of your choice – vanilla bean or almond – to the egg whites. If mixing sounds too daunting, consider using an electric mixer. This step will bring out the natural flavor and heighten your meringue’s aroma.

With your stiff peaks achieved, fold in the reserved dry mixture. Consider using a separate dish or bowl for the dry mixture to aid in aeration. Another crucial area of advancement is to avoid overmixing the meringue. Overdoing it here will result in a flat and heavy cookie. Once all ingredients are smoothly incorporated, prepare your piping bag, fitted with your favorite tip, or a simple zip-top plastic bag with a corner snipped.

Line your baking sheets with parchment paper, and write down your measurements for each meringue cookie, as the size will impact the bake time. Don’t forget to give it space on the tray to maintain flaunt, or they’ll flatten into one another.

Finally, bake at a low-medium temperature, around 200 degrees Fahrenheit, for an extended period, about two hours. The temperature and time combination will cook through the meringue without browning the exterior too quickly. For best results, keep the cookies in the oven after it’s turned off, known as “baking” the meringues in a turned-off oven overnight. This cool-down process will prevent any excess moisture from entering your delicate cookies.

If done correctly, these cookies should be crisp on the outside and chewy on the inside. Try this recipe for your next get-together and surprise your friends and family with your meringue mastery!
