Easy Blueberry Cheesecake Bars: A Quick and Delicious Dessert


If you’re a fan of cheesecake and blueberries, then you’re in for a treat with this easy recipe for Blueberry Cheesecake Bars. These bars are the perfect combination of creamy, tangy cheesecake and sweet, juicy blueberries, all nestled on a buttery graham cracker crust. Not only are they absolutely delicious, but they are also incredibly simple to make, making them the ideal dessert for any occasion.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the cheesecake filling:

  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup sour cream

For the blueberry topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.

  2. Make the Crust: In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside.

  3. Prepare the Cheesecake Filling: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract and sour cream until fully combined.

  4. Assemble and Bake: Pour the cheesecake filling over the baked crust, spreading it into an even layer. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly. Remove from the oven and let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 3 hours or until completely chilled.

  5. Make the Blueberry Topping: In a small saucepan, combine the blueberries, sugar, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the blueberries start to burst and release their juices. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry, then add it to the blueberry mixture. Continue to cook for a few more minutes, until the mixture has thickened. Remove from the heat and let it cool to room temperature.

  6. Finish and Serve: Once the cheesecake bars are chilled and the blueberry topping has cooled, spread the blueberry mixture over the top of the cheesecake. Use the parchment paper overhang to lift the cheesecake out of the pan and onto a cutting board. Cut into bars and serve.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, try using other berries such as raspberries or blackberries for the topping.
  • You can add a hint of lemon zest to the cheesecake filling for a refreshing citrus flavor.
  • To make these bars in advance, you can freeze them (without the topping) for up to a month. Simply add the blueberry topping before serving.

These Easy Blueberry Cheesecake Bars are sure to be a hit with anyone who tries them. The creamy cheesecake, the burst of blueberry flavor, and the buttery graham cracker crust all come together to create a delightful dessert that’s perfect for summer gatherings, potlucks, or simply as a sweet treat to enjoy at home. Give this recipe a try and watch as it becomes a new favorite in your dessert repertoire!
