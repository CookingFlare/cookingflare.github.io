Escalivada: A Colorful and Flavorful Spanish Roasted Vegetable Dish


If you’re in the mood for a dish that bursts with color and flavor, escalivada is an excellent choice. This Spanish roasted vegetable medley is not only a feast for the eyes but also a delightful combination of smoky, savory, and tangy flavors. Traditionally hailing from the Catalonia region of Spain, escalivada has become a beloved dish across the country and beyond. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice cook, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests. Let’s dive into the world of escalivada and learn how to recreate this vibrant dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To start your escalivada adventure, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 yellow bell peppers
  • 2 onions
  • 3-4 ripe tomatoes
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Preparation

Begin by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is warming up, wash all the vegetables thoroughly. Then, place the whole eggplants, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Don’t forget to leave the skins on – they will help to infuse the vegetables with a delicious smoky flavor as they roast.

Once the oven reaches the desired temperature, pop the baking sheet in and let the vegetables roast for about an hour. You’ll want to turn them occasionally to ensure they cook evenly and develop a nice char on the outside. Keep a close eye on the garlic cloves, as they may roast faster than the other vegetables. Remove them from the oven when they are soft and golden.

Roasting and Assembling

After an hour, the vegetables should be tender and beautifully charred. Take them out of the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes. This step not only makes the vegetables easier to handle but also intensifies their flavors.

While the vegetables are cooling, peel the skins off the bell peppers and tomatoes. The eggplants and onions should be easy to peel as well. Once all the vegetables are peeled, cut them into long strips and arrange them on a serving platter. The vibrant colors of the roasted vegetables will create a stunning visual display.

Flavor Infusion

To bring the dish together, drizzle a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil over the vegetables. Then, finely chop the roasted garlic and sprinkle it on top. Season the escalivada with salt, black pepper, and a splash of red wine vinegar for a tangy kick. The acidity of the vinegar pairs perfectly with the smoky sweetness of the roasted vegetables, elevating the dish to a whole new level of flavor.

Allow the escalivada to sit for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting period allows the flavors to meld and develop, resulting in a truly harmonious blend of tastes.

Serving Suggestions

Escalivada is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various ways. It makes a delightful appetizer when served with crusty bread, allowing guests to create their own bite-sized masterpieces. Alternatively, it can accompany grilled meats or fish, adding a burst of color and taste to the main course. You can also toss it with pasta or add it to sandwiches for a delightful twist.

Final Thoughts

Escalivada is not just a dish – it’s a celebration of the Mediterranean’s bounty and a testament to the art of simple, yet flavorful cooking. By using the finest vegetables and a few key ingredients, you can create a masterpiece that will transport you to the sun-drenched coasts of Spain with every bite.

So, the next time you’re looking to add a pop of color and a burst of flavor to your table, consider making escalivada. Your taste buds will thank you, and your guests will be impressed by your culinary prowess. Embrace the vibrant world of Spanish cuisine and savor the magic of escalivada!
