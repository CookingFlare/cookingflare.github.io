Easy Peach Cobbler Recipe: A Sweet Southern Treat


Peach cobbler, a classic Southern dessert that’s as warm and welcoming as a hug from your grandmother. This easy peach cobbler recipe is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for potlucks, family gatherings, or just a quiet evening at home with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh or frozen peaches, sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pie crust (homemade or store-bought)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix until the peaches are evenly coated with the dry ingredients.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, heavy cream, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients over the peach mixture and stir until everything is well combined.
  5. Roll out the pie crust to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Place it in a 9x13-inch (23x33 cm) baking dish.
  6. Pour the peach mixture into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  7. Roll out the remaining pie crust to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Use a pastry cutter or a knife to cut the crust into strips, about 1 inch (2.5 cm) wide.
  8. Place the strips on top of the peach mixture, weaving them into a lattice pattern. Trim any excess crust from the edges.
  9. Brush the top crust with a little bit of water and sprinkle with granulated sugar.
  10. Bake the cobbler for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  11. Remove the cobbler from the oven and let it cool for at least 30 minutes before serving. Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream if desired.

Tips and Variations:

  • Use fresh peaches in the summer when they’re in season, or frozen peaches during the rest of the year.
  • Substitute other fruits like blueberries, strawberries, or blackberries for a different flavor profile.
  • Add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg to the peach mixture for extra spice.
  • Top the cobbler with a crumb topping instead of a lattice crust for a crunchy texture.
  • Make individual cobblers in ramekins for a fun and easy dessert.

Why This Recipe Works:

This peach cobbler recipe is a classic for a reason. The combination of sweet peaches, flaky crust, and creamy topping is a match made in heaven. The best part? It’s ridiculously easy to make, even for those who aren’t confident in their baking skills.

Conclusion:

Peach cobbler, the ultimate Southern dessert that’s sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. With this easy recipe, you’ll be enjoying a warm and comforting slice (or two) in no time. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the warm and fuzzy feeling that comes with a delicious homemade dessert.
