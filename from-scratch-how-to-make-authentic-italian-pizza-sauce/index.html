From Scratch: How to Make Authentic Italian Pizza Sauce


Are you tired of store-bought pizza sauce that lacks the authentic flavors of Italy? Look no further! In this recipe, I will guide you through the process of making your very own homemade Italian pizza sauce from scratch. Get ready to elevate your pizza game with this flavorful and aromatic sauce that will transport you straight to the streets of Napoli.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.

  2. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir well to combine. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Add the dried oregano, dried basil, salt, black pepper, sugar, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir to incorporate all the spices into the sauce.

  4. Continue simmering the sauce on low heat for another 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

  5. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. If you prefer a sweeter sauce, add a pinch more sugar. For a spicier sauce, increase the amount of red pepper flakes.

  6. Remove the sauce from the heat and let it cool slightly. For a smoother texture, transfer the sauce to a blender or use an immersion blender to puree until smooth. However, if you prefer a chunkier sauce, leave it as is.

  7. Once the sauce has cooled, it is ready to be used. Spread a generous amount onto your pizza dough, add your favorite toppings, and bake according to your pizza recipe.

  8. For an extra burst of freshness and flavor, garnish your pizza with torn fresh basil leaves before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • Use high-quality crushed tomatoes for the best results. San Marzano tomatoes, known for their sweet flavor and low acidity, are highly recommended for an authentic taste.
  • If you prefer a smoother sauce but don’t have a blender, you can strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any chunky bits.
  • Experiment with additional herbs and spices to customize the sauce to your liking. Freshly chopped parsley, thyme, or even a pinch of dried fennel seeds can add interesting layers of flavor.
  • This sauce can also be used as a base for other Italian dishes, such as pasta, lasagna, or meatball subs. Simply adjust the seasoning according to the recipe.

Now that you have learned how to make authentic Italian pizza sauce from scratch, say goodbye to bland and boring store-bought options. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of love, you can create a sauce that will take your homemade pizzas to a whole new level of deliciousness. So get creative, have fun, and enjoy the taste of Italy right in your own kitchen! Buon appetito!
