Thai Spring Rolls Recipe with Shrimp and Vegetables


When it comes to Thai cuisine, the flavors are vibrant, the ingredients are fresh, and the dishes are a celebration of taste and texture. One of the most beloved Thai dishes that perfectly encapsulates these qualities is the Thai Spring Rolls. These delightful rolls are a harmonious blend of fresh vegetables, succulent shrimp, and aromatic herbs, all wrapped in delicate rice paper. Today, I’m excited to share with you my take on this classic Thai dish, featuring a tantalizing combination of flavors and a step-by-step guide to creating these delectable spring rolls in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Spring Rolls:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 16 medium-sized cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped roasted peanuts

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 small red chili, finely chopped

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

  • Begin by preparing all the ingredients for the spring rolls. Wash and dry the vegetables and herbs thoroughly. Arrange the shrimp, lettuce, bean sprouts, carrot, cucumber, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, and chopped peanuts on a clean, dry surface for easy assembly.

Step 2: Prepare the Dipping Sauce

  • In a small bowl, combine the fish sauce, lime juice, water, rice vinegar, sugar, minced garlic, and chopped red chili. Stir the mixture until the sugar dissolves completely. Set the dipping sauce aside for later use.

Step 3: Soften the Rice Paper

  • Fill a large shallow dish with warm water. Working with one rice paper wrapper at a time, immerse the wrapper in the water for about 5-10 seconds, or until it becomes pliable but still slightly firm. Carefully remove the rice paper and place it on a clean, damp kitchen towel.

Step 4: Assemble the Spring Rolls

  • Place two shrimp in a row, leaving space on either side of the shrimp. On top of the shrimp, add a small handful of lettuce, bean sprouts, julienned carrot, cucumber, and a few leaves of each herb. Sprinkle a pinch of chopped peanuts over the filling.

Step 5: Roll the Spring Rolls

  • Gently lift the bottom edge of the rice paper over the filling and tuck it in. Fold in the sides of the rice paper, then continue to roll until the spring roll is tightly sealed. Repeat the process with the remaining rice paper wrappers and filling.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  • Arrange the prepared spring rolls on a serving platter and serve with the prepared dipping sauce. These Thai spring rolls are best enjoyed fresh, so be sure to savor them as soon as they are ready.

Tips for Perfect Thai Spring Rolls

  • To prevent the rice paper from sticking or tearing, ensure that your work surface is damp but not wet when assembling the spring rolls.
  • Customize the filling according to your preference by adding ingredients such as vermicelli noodles, bell peppers, or even tofu for a vegetarian option.
  • Experiment with the dipping sauce by adjusting the level of spiciness, sweetness, or acidity to suit your taste.

Final Thoughts

Thai Spring Rolls with Shrimp and Vegetables are a delightful combination of fresh, crunchy, and flavorful ingredients wrapped in delicate rice paper. With a burst of refreshing herbs and succulent shrimp, these spring rolls are a perfect appetizer or light meal for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of Thailand, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy your culinary cravings. Embrace the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine with these homemade spring rolls and elevate your dining experience with every delightful bite.
