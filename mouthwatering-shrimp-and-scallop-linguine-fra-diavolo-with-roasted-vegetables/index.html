Mouthwatering Shrimp and Scallop Linguine Fra Diavolo with Roasted Vegetables


As the sun sets on a warm summer evening, there’s nothing quite like a dish that combines the freshness of the sea with the warmth of the season. Linguine Fra Diavolo, a classic Italian recipe, is a staple of the Mediterranean diet, and when paired with succulent shrimp and scallops, it becomes a culinary masterpiece. In this recipe, we’ll take the traditional dish to new heights by adding a medley of roasted vegetables, elevating it to a satisfying and flavorful meal that’s perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) linguine
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound (450g) large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound (450g) scallops, rinsed and patted dry
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups (475ml) white wine
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1 cup (115g) chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cups (220g) roasted vegetables (see below)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Roasted Vegetables

  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 large yellow bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 large zucchini, sliced
  • 1 large yellow squash, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Roasted Vegetables

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, toss together the sliced bell peppers, zucchini, and yellow squash with the minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and black pepper until they’re evenly coated. Spread the vegetables out in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re tender and lightly caramelized.

Step 2: Cook the Linguine

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the linguine according to the package instructions until it’s al dente. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water and then drain the linguine.

Step 3: Prepare the Shrimp and Scallop Mixture

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant. Add the shrimp and scallops and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they’re pink and cooked through. Remove the seafood from the skillet and set it aside.

Step 4: Make the Fra Diavolo Sauce

In the same skillet, add the red pepper flakes and paprika and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly. Add the white wine and heavy cream and bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 5: Combine the Linguine, Shrimp and Scallop Mixture, and Fra Diavolo Sauce

Add the cooked linguine to the skillet with the Fra Diavolo sauce and toss it to combine, adding some of the reserved pasta cooking water if the sauce seems too thick. Add the cooked shrimp and scallops back into the skillet and toss everything together until the seafood is well coated with the sauce.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Divide the linguine among individual plates and top it with the roasted vegetables. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and grated Parmesan cheese, if desired. Serve immediately and enjoy the flavors of the Mediterranean.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add some chopped fresh parsley and lemon wedges to the dish.
  • Substitute the shrimp and scallops with other types of seafood, such as mussels or clams, for a different twist.
  • Serve the dish with some crusty bread or garlic bread for a satisfying meal.
  • Make the Fra Diavolo sauce ahead of time and refrigerate it for up to 3 days or freeze it for up to 2 months. Reheat it before using.

By combining the classic Italian flavors of linguine with the freshness of the sea and the warmth of roasted vegetables, this dish is sure to become a new favorite. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the taste of the Mediterranean in every bite.
