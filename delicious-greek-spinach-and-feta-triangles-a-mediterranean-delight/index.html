Delicious Greek Spinach and Feta Triangles: A Mediterranean Delight


If you’re a fan of Mediterranean cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Greek Spinach and Feta Triangles. These delightful pastries, also known as Spanakopita, are a popular Greek appetizer that combines the earthy flavors of spinach and the rich creaminess of feta cheese, all encased in flaky, golden phyllo pastry. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, these savory triangles are sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

For the assembly:

  • 1 package phyllo pastry sheets, thawed according to package instructions
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a large skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
    • Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the sautéed spinach, crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, beaten eggs, chopped dill, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Set the filling aside.

  2. Assemble the Triangles:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    • Lay one sheet of phyllo pastry on a clean work surface and brush it lightly with melted butter. Place another sheet of phyllo on top and repeat the process until you have a stack of 5-6 sheets.
    • Cut the stacked phyllo sheets lengthwise into 3 equal strips.
    • Place a spoonful of the spinach and feta filling at the bottom of each strip. Fold the bottom corner of the strip over the filling to form a triangle, then continue folding as you would a flag, until the entire strip is folded into a neat triangle. Place the completed triangle on the prepared baking sheet, seam side down. Repeat with the remaining phyllo and filling.

  3. Bake to Perfection:

    • Once all the triangles are assembled, brush the tops with a little more melted butter to help them achieve a beautiful golden color during baking.
    • Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the triangles for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Once baked, remove the triangles from the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes before serving.
    • These Greek Spinach and Feta Triangles are best enjoyed warm, and they make a fantastic appetizer or snack. Serve them with a dollop of tzatziki sauce or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for an extra burst of flavor.

Conclusion

With their crispy layers of phyllo pastry and the savory, creamy filling, these Greek Spinach and Feta Triangles are a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or looking to expand your culinary horizons, this recipe is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to the Mediterranean with these delectable triangles. Your taste buds will thank you!
