Easter Recipes for the Instant Pot: Fast and Flavorful Meals


Easter is a time for family gatherings, festive celebrations, and, of course, delicious meals. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, dinner, or an intimate family get-together, preparing a meal for Easter can often feel overwhelming. However, the Instant Pot can transform your Easter feast into an easy, stress-free experience, allowing you to serve a range of flavorful dishes in record time.

In this blog post, we’ll explore several Easter recipes that you can prepare using your Instant Pot. These recipes are perfect for those looking to spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the company of family and friends. From tender lamb to fluffy mashed potatoes, we’ve got a variety of dishes that will impress your guests and satisfy their appetites.

Why Use the Instant Pot for Easter?

The Instant Pot is a versatile kitchen appliance that combines the functions of a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, steamer, and yogurt maker. For busy cooks, it’s a game-changer, especially when preparing large meals for special occasions like Easter. It significantly cuts down cooking times, making it easier to serve up a delicious spread without spending hours in the kitchen.

Here are just a few reasons why the Instant Pot is your best friend when preparing Easter meals:

  1. Speed: What would normally take hours can be done in less than an hour.
  2. Tenderness: The pressure cooking function ensures meats, like lamb or ham, are incredibly tender.
  3. Flavor retention: The sealed environment of the Instant Pot helps retain flavors, making dishes richer and more aromatic.
  4. Convenience: You can set it and forget it, leaving you time to relax, entertain, or prepare other dishes.

Let’s dive into some mouth-watering Easter recipes you can whip up in no time with your Instant Pot.

Instant Pot Leg of Lamb

Lamb is a traditional Easter dish, and it’s perfect for cooking in the Instant Pot. The pressure cooking function ensures that the meat becomes tender and juicy without drying out.

Ingredients:

  • 3 to 4-pound leg of lamb, boneless or bone-in
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Lamb: Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting and heat the olive oil. Once hot, sear the lamb on all sides for 4-5 minutes until a golden crust forms. Remove the lamb and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Seasoning: In a small bowl, mix together garlic, rosemary, thyme, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Season the Lamb: Rub the seasoning mixture all over the lamb, covering it evenly.
  4. Pressure Cook: Add the chicken broth and balsamic vinegar to the Instant Pot. Place the lamb back into the pot, locking the lid. Set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 25 minutes (for a medium-rare roast) or 30 minutes for medium doneness.
  5. Release the Pressure: After cooking, let the pressure naturally release for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the lamb rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing. Serve with your favorite side dishes.

Tips:

  • Adjust the cooking time depending on the size of your lamb and your preferred doneness.
  • If you prefer a crispy exterior, you can broil the lamb for 2-3 minutes after pressure cooking.

Instant Pot Scalloped Potatoes

A creamy and cheesy side dish, scalloped potatoes are a classic addition to any Easter meal. The Instant Pot makes it easy to create a velvety sauce while ensuring the potatoes are perfectly tender.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes: Slice the potatoes as thinly as possible using a mandolin or sharp knife. Set aside.
  2. Make the Sauce: Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” mode and melt the butter. Add the flour and cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly to create a roux. Slowly whisk in the milk and heavy cream, ensuring there are no lumps.
  3. Add the Cheese and Seasoning: Once the sauce has thickened slightly, stir in the cheddar cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Continue to stir until the cheese is fully melted.
  4. Layer the Potatoes: Layer the sliced potatoes in the Instant Pot, pouring the cheese sauce over each layer. You may need to press down to ensure the potatoes are fully coated.
  5. Pressure Cook: Secure the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 10 minutes.
  6. Release the Pressure: After cooking, quick-release the pressure and carefully remove the lid.
  7. Finish with Parmesan: Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top and let it melt before serving.

Tips:

  • For a more indulgent dish, add a little extra cheddar cheese on top after pressure cooking and broil for 2-3 minutes for a golden, bubbly crust.
  • You can also add chopped onions or herbs for extra flavor.

Instant Pot Honey Glazed Carrots

These honey glazed carrots are the perfect balance of sweet and savory, and they cook up incredibly quickly in the Instant Pot, making them a fantastic side for your Easter feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby carrots, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Carrots: Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” and melt the butter. Add the baby carrots and sauté for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
  2. Add the Glaze: Stir in the honey, brown sugar, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Pressure Cook: Add ½ cup of water to the Instant Pot, ensuring the carrots are not fully submerged. Lock the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 4 minutes.
  4. Release the Pressure: When the cooking time is up, quick-release the pressure.
  5. Serve: Stir the carrots to coat them in the glaze, then serve warm.

Tips:

  • For a more savory version, try adding a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the glaze.
  • If you prefer a little crunch, reduce the cooking time by a minute or two.

Instant Pot Easter Ham

A glazed ham is a beloved Easter dish, and cooking it in the Instant Pot makes it even easier. With just a few ingredients, you can create a flavorful ham that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 3 to 5-pound fully cooked ham (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ham: Place the ham in the Instant Pot. If it’s too large to fit, trim the ham so that it fits comfortably inside the pot.
  2. Make the Glaze: In a small bowl, mix together pineapple juice, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, maple syrup, ground cloves, and cinnamon.
  3. Pressure Cook: Pour the glaze over the ham and secure the Instant Pot lid. Set it to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 15 minutes per pound.
  4. Release the Pressure: Once the cooking time is complete, let the pressure naturally release for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  5. Finish and Serve: Remove the ham from the Instant Pot, cover it with foil, and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with extra glaze from the pot.

Tips:

  • For an extra touch, after cooking, glaze the ham with additional syrup or honey and broil it for a few minutes to caramelize the exterior.
  • Add a handful of whole cloves into the ham for an aromatic flavor.

Instant Pot Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs are a classic appetizer for Easter, and making them in the Instant Pot is a breeze. You can cook the eggs quickly, and the peeling process is easier than ever.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Eggs: Add 1 cup of water to the Instant Pot and place the eggs on the trivet. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 5 minutes.
  2. Release the Pressure: Once the cooking time is complete, quick-release the pressure and transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water to cool.
  3. Peel the Eggs: Peel the eggs once they’ve cooled. Slice them in half and remove the yolks.
  4. Make the Filling: Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  5. Fill the Eggs: Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites. Garnish with paprika.

Tips:

  • Add some chopped pickles or chives to the filling for extra flavor.
  • You can prepare the eggs a day ahead for easy entertaining.

Conclusion

With these Instant Pot recipes, your Easter meal will be stress-free and full of flavor. Whether you’re making a tender leg of lamb, creamy mashed potatoes, or sweet honey-glazed carrots, the Instant Pot helps you prepare meals quickly without compromising on taste. By embracing the convenience of the Instant Pot, you can enjoy a festive and delicious Easter celebration with less time spent in the kitchen. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
