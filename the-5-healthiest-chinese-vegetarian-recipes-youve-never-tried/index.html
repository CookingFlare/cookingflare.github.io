The 5 Healthiest Chinese Vegetarian Recipes You've Never Tried


Chinese cuisine is famous for its bold flavors, health benefits, and delectable dishes. However, not everyone is aware of the vegetarian gems hidden within this rich culinary world. As you’ll see, these dishes aren’t just healthy—they’re also surprisingly delicious and very beautiful. Let’s dive into five healthy Chinese vegetarian recipes that you’ve probably never tried before!

1. Stuffed Tofu Pockets

Tofu pockets, or “白肉包” in Chinese, are vegan versions of pork-stuffed buns, featuring a tofu-based “meat” filling wrapped in soft, steamed bun dough. The tofu can be seasoned with soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, and a variety of spices for an additional depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz. extra-firm tofu
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Green onion and ginger for stuffing
  • Homemade or store-bought steamed bun dough

Instructions:

  1. Drain and crumble the tofu into a bowl. Drizzle with sesame oil and mix thoroughly.
  2. Add the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, finely chopped green onion, and ginger to the tofu mixture. Mix well until combined and set aside.
  3. Prepare the steamed bun dough or purchase pre-made dough and portion out the mixture into equal sizes.
  4. Stuff the dough with the tofu mixture and steam or pan-fry the stuffed pockets until cooked through.
  5. Serve hot, garnishing with sesame seeds or chopped green onions for extra flavor.

2. Stewed Chinese Cabbage and Tofu

This savory and delicate dish, known as “油菜豆腐煲” (yóu cǎi dòu fu cāo), entails braising a delicious mixture of Chinese cabbage (also known as bok choy), tofu, and mushrooms in a fragrant broth flavored with garlic, ginger, and soy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of Chinese cabbage, chopped
  • 14 oz. firm tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup mushrooms (shiitake or oyster), sliced
  • 1 stalk of leek, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 inch piece of ginger
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil (like vegetable or canola)

Instructions:

  1. Mince the garlic and ginger and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat and sauté the garlic, ginger, leek, and mushrooms for 2-3 minutes or until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped Chinese cabbage, tofu cubes, and soy sauce. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth or water, making sure all ingredients are submerged.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for around 20 minutes or until the cabbage and tofu are tender.
  6. Adjust seasoning with extra soy sauce or a pinch of salt if needed and serve piping hot.

3. Pan-fried Pumpkin Dumplings

Known as " позиющие гигош" in Chinese, pan-fried pumpkin dumplings give a delightful sweet and salty twist to traditional potstickers. The pleasantly chewy texture of the pumpkin combined with savory seasonings like garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil creates an unforgettable dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium pumpkin
  • 4-5 store-bought dumpling wrappers or homemade
  • 3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Neutral oil (like vegetable or canola) for pan-frying

Instructions:

  1. Peel, de-seed and grate the pumpkin. Wrap it in a damp cloth and squeeze out excess moisture.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated pumpkin with the green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt to taste. Mix well until combined.
  3. Lay out the dumpling wrappers on a flat surface. Place a spoonful of the pumpkin filling in the center of each wrapper, wet the edges with water, fold in half, and press the edges together to seal.
  4. Place a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour a little neutral oil. Once hot, add as many dumplings as can fit comfortably, pleats-side-up, without overcrowding the pan. Pan-fry for about 2-3 minutes or until the bottoms are golden brown.
  5. Add ¼ cup of water to the pan, cover, and cook on low heat for about 6-8 minutes, allowing the dumplings to steam.
  6. Carefully transfer the pan-fried pumpkin dumplings to a plate and serve immediately with soy sauce or your preferred dipping sauce.

4. Spinach and Tofu Soup

Also known as “菠菜豆腐汤” (bō cǎi dòu fu tāng), this healthful Chinese soup combines protein-rich tofu and nutrient-dense spinach for an nutritious meal that’s both satisfying and easy to make. Don’t forget to garnish with a sprinkle of sesame oil for additional flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 bunch of spinach, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 5 cups of vegetable broth or water
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Neutral oil (like vegetable or canola)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the neutral oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the tofu cubes, stirring gently until they’re lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth or water, making sure all ingredients are submerged, then add the soy sauce.
  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for around 10 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped spinach and cook for an additional 2 minutes or until wilted. Adjust seasoning to taste if necessary.
  6. Drizzle with sesame oil before serving, and feel free to add a squeeze of lime if you’d like a hint of tanginess.

5. Buddhist Lo Mein

Derived from traditional Chinese Buddhist cuisine, this dish, also known as “佛家炒面” (fó jiā chǎo miàn), presents a delicious, healthy alternative to the traditional noodle dishes. By incorporating vegetables, tofu, and noodles, Buddhist Lo Mein offers a fantastic balance of flavors, textures, and nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. of noodles (lo mein or rice noodles)
  • 14 oz. of firm tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup of chopped vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, carrots, and onion)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoonsneutral oil (like vegetable or canola)

Instructions:

1.Boil a pot of water and cook the noodles as per package instructions. Drain and set aside. 2.In a wok or large pan, heat the neutral oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and ginger for 30 seconds or until fragrant. 3.Add the tofu cubes and sauté until lightly browned on all sides. Remove tofu from the pan and set aside. 4.In the same pan, add the chopped vegetables, and stir-fry until tender-crisp. 5.Return the tofu to the pan and add the cooked noodles, soy sauce, and oyster sauce (optional). Stir well to coat and heat through. 6.Drizzle with sesame oil before serving.

These unique and healthy Chinese vegetarian recipes bring a fresh take on traditional dishes, proving that not only are they delicious, but they offer various health benefits. Give them a try and indulge your taste buds while being good to your body. Enjoy!
