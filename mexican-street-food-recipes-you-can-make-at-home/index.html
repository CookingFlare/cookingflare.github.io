Mexican Street Food Recipes You Can Make at Home


Mexican street food is a delicious and vibrant representation of the country's diverse culture. The unique combination of indigenous, European, and African influences creates a mouth-watering culinary experience that's easy to recreate at home. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this guide will walk you through the steps to make some of the popular Mexican street foods, and provide a deeper understanding of the flavors and ingredients that bring them to life. Grilled Corn with Cotija Cheese, Chili Powder, and Lime

A staple at Mexican street markets, Elote is the perfect side dish to accompany any meal. It's also a simple and flavorful snack when finished with a sprinkle of Cotija cheese and a sprinkle of chili powder. Ingredients

4 ears of corn 1/4 cup Cotija cheese, crumbled 1 tsp chili powder 1/2 tsp ground cumin 2 tbsp vegetable oil Juice of 2 limes Salt, to taste Mayonnaise (optional)

Instructions

Preheat your grill to 375°F (190°C). Grill the corn on the cob for about 10-15 minutes per side, or until slightly charred. Rub the grilled corn with vegetable oil, ground cumin, and chili powder. Squeeze lime juice over the corn while it's still warm. Sprinkle with Cotija cheese and salt to taste. For a richer flavor, drizzle with mayonnaise. Serve warm.

Carnitas Tacos with Salsa Roja and Pico de Gallo

Carnitas is a popular Mexican dish consisting of slow-cooked pork shoulder. It's then shredded and fried until crispy, served with a side of fresh salsa and a sprinkle of cilantro. Ingredients For the Carnitas

2 pounds pork shoulder 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil 1/4 cup orange juice 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp dried oregano Salt, to taste

For the Salsa Roja

1 cup chopped tomatoes 1/2 cup chopped onion 1 jalapeno pepper, diced 1/4 cup vegetable oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp cumin 1 tsp smoked paprika Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Pico de Gallo

1 cup diced tomatoes 1/2 cup diced onion 1 jalapeno pepper, diced 1/4 cup chopped cilantro Juice of 1 lime Salt, to taste

Instructions

In a large Dutch oven or heavy saucepan, combine the pork, lard or oil, orange juice, garlic, and oregano. Braise the pork over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until it's tender and easily shreds. Shred the pork and fry it in a skillet until crispy and golden brown. Meanwhile, make the Salsa Roja. In a separate skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and jalapeno pepper, and sauté until the onion is translucent. Stir in the cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for 1 minute. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for another 5-10 minutes, or until the Salsa Roja reaches the desired consistency. To make the Pico de Gallo, combine the diced tomatoes, onion, jalapeno pepper, and cilantro in a bowl. Squeeze lime juice and season with salt to taste. Serve the carnitas with Salsa Roja, Pico de Gallo, and warm tortillas for a classic Mexican meal.

Churros with Sugar and Cinnamon

Churros are a traditional Mexican dessert consisting of fried dough pastry coated in sugar and cinnamon. They're often enjoyed with a side of chocolate or coffee. Ingredients

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1/2 tsp salt 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon 1/2 cup vegetable oil 2 eggs 2 tsp active dry yeast 1 cup warm milk Confectioners' sugar, for dusting

Instructions

In a saucepan, combine the sugar, 1/4 cup water, and 1 tbsp vegetable oil. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat. Remove from the heat and let it cool slightly. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, cinnamon, and yeast. Make a well in the center and pour in the eggs, warm milk, and sugar mixture. Mix the dough until it's smooth and elastic. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep frying pan to 375°F (190°C). Pipe the dough through a star tip to create the churros. Fry the churros until they're golden brown and puffed up. Dust with confectioners' sugar and cinnamon.

Tacos de Carnitas with Salsa Verde and Pickled Onions

Tacos are a staple in Mexican cuisine, and this variation features slow-cooked carnitas served with a creamy Salsa Verde and crunchy pickled onions. Ingredients For the Carnitas

3 pounds pork shoulder 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil 1 cup orange juice 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp dried oregano Salt, to taste

For the Salsa Verde

1 cup chopped tomatillos 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Juice of 2 limes Salt, to taste

For the pickled onions

1 red onion, thinly sliced 1/4 cup red wine vinegar 1/4 cup water 1 tsp sugar Salt, to taste

Instructions

In a large Dutch oven or heavy saucepan, combine the pork, lard or oil, orange juice, garlic, and oregano. Braise the pork over low heat for 2-3 hours, or until it's tender and easily shreds. Shred the pork and fry it in a skillet until crispy and golden brown. Meanwhile, make the Salsa Verde. In a blender or food processor, combine the tomatillos, garlic, jalapeno pepper, and cilantro. Process until smooth. Season with lime juice and salt to taste. For the pickled onions, combine the sliced onions, red wine vinegar, water, and sugar in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes or until the onions reach the desired level of tenderness. Serve the carnitas with Salsa Verde, pickled onions, and warm tortillas.

Mexican Chocolate with Coconut Whipped Cream

Mexican chocolate is a rich and decadent dessert often enjoyed with a dollop of whipped cream. Ingredients

1 cup dark chocolate chips 1 cup coconut milk 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon 1/4 tsp ground cayenne pepper (optional) Whipped cream, for serving

Instructions

In a double boiler or a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, melt the chocolate chips. In a separate saucepan, heat the coconut milk over low heat until it's steaming. Whisk the sugar, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper (if using) into the melted chocolate. Whisk the coconut milk into the chocolate mixture until smooth. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Serve with whipped cream.
