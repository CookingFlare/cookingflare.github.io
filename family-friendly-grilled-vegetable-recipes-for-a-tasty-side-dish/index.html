Family-Friendly Grilled Vegetable Recipes for a Tasty Side Dish


Are you looking for a delicious and healthy side dish that the whole family will love? Look no further than these family-friendly grilled vegetable recipes! Grilling vegetables not only brings out their natural flavors but also adds a delightful smoky touch that will leave everyone wanting more. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply want to add some pizzazz to your weeknight dinners, these recipes are sure to impress.

Grilled Zucchini and Yellow Squash Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, sliced into 1/4-inch rounds
  • 2 yellow squashes, sliced into 1/4-inch rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, red onion, olive oil, minced garlic, dried Italian herbs, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to coat the vegetables evenly.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between different colors for a vibrant presentation.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and serve hot.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herbed Butter

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh herbs (such as parsley, chives, or basil), finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, fresh herbs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are incorporated.
  3. Spread the herbed butter evenly over each ear of corn.
  4. Place the corn directly on the grill grates and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn is tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

Grilled Vegetable Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 2 red bell peppers, halved and seeded
  • 2 yellow bell peppers, halved and seeded
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 red onion, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mixed salad greens
  • Balsamic glaze, for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the bell peppers, eggplant, zucchinis, and red onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the vegetables directly on the grill grates and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side until they are tender and have grill marks.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the grill and let them cool slightly. Cut the bell peppers into thin strips.
  5. Arrange the mixed salad greens on a platter and top with the grilled vegetables.
  6. Drizzle balsamic glaze over the salad and serve.

Grilled Vegetable Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into 1/4-inch rounds
  • 1 red onion, sliced into 1/4-inch rounds
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (such as cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • Sour cream and salsa, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the bell peppers, zucchini, and red onion until they are tender and lightly charred, about 5-7 minutes per side.
  3. Remove the grilled vegetables from the grill and let them cool slightly. Slice the bell peppers into thin strips.
  4. Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Place a tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle half of it with shredded cheese. Top with grilled vegetables and fold the tortilla in half.
  5. Cook the quesadilla for about 2-3 minutes per side until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is crispy.
  6. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling.
  7. Slice the quesadillas into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa.

These grilled vegetable recipes are not only tasty but also a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your family’s diet. They are versatile, customizable, and perfect for any occasion. So fire up the grill and get ready to enjoy these mouthwatering dishes with your loved ones. Happy grilling!
