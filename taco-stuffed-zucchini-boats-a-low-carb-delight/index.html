Taco Stuffed Zucchini Boats: A Low-Carb Delight


As the summer season approaches, many of us are looking for light and refreshing meal options that still pack a punch of flavor. One such recipe that fits the bill is the Taco Stuffed Zucchini Boats. This creative twist on traditional tacos uses zucchini boats as the “tortillas” and packs them with a flavorful mixture of ground beef, cheese, and spices. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make this delicious and healthy dish that’s perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a summer barbecue.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized zucchinis
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet of taco seasoning
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray or oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the zucchinis: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the insides, leaving a shell about 1/4 inch thick. Place the zucchinis on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and spray with cooking spray or brush with oil. Roast the zucchinis in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. Cook the ground beef: In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium-high heat until it’s browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Drain the excess fat and add the diced onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the taco seasoning: Add the taco seasoning to the ground beef mixture and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the flavors have melded together.
  4. Assemble the stuffed zucchinis: Once the zucchinis are done roasting, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly. Spoon the ground beef mixture into the zucchinis, filling them to the top. Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the top of the beef mixture.
  5. Top with cilantro and bake: Sprinkle the chopped cilantro over the top of the cheese and bake the stuffed zucchinis in the oven for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve and enjoy: Remove the stuffed zucchinis from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve hot, garnished with additional cilantro if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe more substantial, you can add some cooked black beans, diced tomatoes, or sliced bell peppers to the ground beef mixture.
  • If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, you can substitute the ground beef with cooked black beans, roasted vegetables, or a combination of the two.
  • To make this recipe more portable, you can serve the stuffed zucchinis in individual boats or wrap them in foil and take them on the go.

Conclusion

The Taco Stuffed Zucchini Boats are a delicious and innovative twist on traditional tacos. By using zucchinis as the “tortillas,” you can enjoy a low-carb and healthy meal that’s perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a summer barbecue. With its flavorful ground beef mixture and melted cheese, this recipe is sure to become a new favorite in your household. So go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds will thank you!
