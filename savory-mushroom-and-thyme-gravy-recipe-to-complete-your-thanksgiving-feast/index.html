Savory Mushroom and Thyme Gravy Recipe to Complete Your Thanksgiving Feast


A delectable Thanksgiving feast is incomplete without a rich and flavorful gravy to complement the main course. This Savory Mushroom and Thyme Gravy recipe is a perfect addition to your holiday table. With its earthy mushrooms and fragrant thyme, this gravy will elevate your meal to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté until they become tender and golden brown. This will take about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Sprinkle the flour over the mushrooms and stir well to coat. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring constantly, to cook off the raw flour taste.

  3. Slowly pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, while continuously whisking to prevent any lumps from forming. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle boil.

  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the gravy for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the gravy to thicken further.

  5. Add the heavy cream and fresh thyme leaves to the skillet. Stir well to combine and simmer for another 5 minutes, until the gravy reaches a smooth and creamy consistency.

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to taste as you go and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  7. Remove the skillet from heat and let the gravy cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This delectable Savory Mushroom and Thyme Gravy pairs perfectly with roasted turkey, chicken, or even mashed potatoes. Pour it generously over your favorite protein or side dishes to enhance their flavors. The earthy aroma of mushrooms and the herbal notes of thyme will add depth and richness to your Thanksgiving feast.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, you can use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, you can strain the gravy before serving to remove any mushroom bits.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as cremini or shiitake, to add more complexity to the gravy.
  • If you want to make the gravy ahead of time, you can store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat it gently on the stovetop, whisking occasionally, until warmed through.
  • If you want to make the gravy gluten-free, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend or cornstarch. Just be sure to adjust the quantities accordingly.

With its savory flavors and creamy texture, this Mushroom and Thyme Gravy is sure to become a Thanksgiving favorite. It adds an extra layer of indulgence to your holiday meal, making it truly memorable. So, go ahead and give this recipe a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
