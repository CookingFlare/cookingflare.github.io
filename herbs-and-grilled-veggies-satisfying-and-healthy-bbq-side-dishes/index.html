Herbs and Grilled Veggies: Satisfying and Healthy BBQ Side Dishes


When it comes to BBQ, most of us focus on the main course, whether it’s juicy burgers, succulent ribs, or tender chicken. However, the sides can make or break the entire meal. In this post, we’ll explore the world of herbs and grilled veggies, showcasing the perfect combination to elevate your BBQ game.

The Power of Herbs

Herbs are the unsung heroes of the culinary world. They add depth, complexity, and freshness to any dish. When it comes to BBQ, herbs can transform even the simplest of vegetables into a flavorful masterpiece. From classic rosemary and thyme to more exotic options like lemongrass and cilantro, the possibilities are endless.

Grilled Veggie Delights

Grilled veggies are a staple of any BBQ, and for good reason. They’re easy to prepare, packed with nutrients, and can be seasoned to perfection. Here are some of our favorite grilled veggie recipes:

Roasted Red Pepper and Garlic Grilled Zucchini

  • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 roasted red peppers, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Brush zucchini slices with olive oil and season with garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred. Serve with roasted red peppers on top.

Lemongrass and Chili Flake Grilled Asparagus

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Brush asparagus with olive oil and season with lemongrass, chili flakes, salt, and pepper. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred. Serve as a side dish or add to sandwiches.

Thyme and Lemon Grilled Bell Peppers

  • 2 large bell peppers, any color
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Brush bell peppers with olive oil and season with thyme, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Grill for 5-7 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred. Serve as a side dish or add to salads.

Tips and Tricks

When it comes to grilling veggies, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Preheat the grill: Make sure the grill is hot before adding the veggies. This will help prevent them from sticking and ensure even cooking.
  • Don’t overcrowd: Grill veggies in batches if necessary, to prevent them from steaming instead of grilling.
  • Use the right oil: Choose an oil with a high smoke point, like olive or avocado oil, to prevent it from burning or smoking.
  • Don’t forget the seasoning: Herbs and spices can make or break the flavor of your grilled veggies. Experiment with different combinations to find your favorite.

Conclusion

Herbs and grilled veggies are the perfect combination for a satisfying and healthy BBQ side dish. With a little creativity and experimentation, you can elevate your BBQ game and impress your friends and family. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a beginner, these recipes are sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in the flavors of the season!
