Savory and Creamy Scalloped Potatoes Au Gratin: A Cheesy Delight


If you’re a fan of comfort food and cheesy delights, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for savory and creamy scalloped potatoes au gratin. This classic dish is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy family dinner or a holiday feast. With layers of tender potatoes, velvety cream, and a generous amount of melted cheese, this dish is sure to satisfy even the most discerning taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Gruyere cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a medium saucepan, heat the cream over medium heat until it simmers. Add the minced garlic, thyme leaves, salt, and black pepper. Stir well to combine and let it simmer for 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.

  3. Arrange a layer of sliced potatoes in the greased baking dish, slightly overlapping each other. Pour a small amount of the cream mixture over the potatoes, making sure to cover them evenly. Sprinkle a handful of Gruyere and Parmesan cheese over the potatoes.

  4. Repeat the layering process until all the potatoes and cream mixture are used, finishing with a layer of cheese on top. Dot the surface with small cubes of butter to help create a golden and crispy crust.

  5. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown and bubbling.

  6. Once the scalloped potatoes are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the dish to set and make it easier to cut into portions.

  7. Serve the scalloped potatoes au gratin as a side dish with roasted chicken, grilled steak, or enjoy it on its own as a comforting main course. The creamy texture and rich flavors will surely make it a crowd-pleaser.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add layers of caramelized onions or sautéed mushrooms between the potato slices.
  • Experiment with different types of cheese, such as cheddar, fontina, or even blue cheese, to customize the flavor profile of your scalloped potatoes au gratin.
  • If you prefer a lighter version of this dish, you can substitute half of the heavy cream with chicken or vegetable broth.
  • To enhance the presentation, sprinkle some fresh parsley or chives on top of the baked dish before serving.

So there you have it - a recipe for savory and creamy scalloped potatoes au gratin that is sure to become a family favorite. Indulge in the layers of tender potatoes, velvety cream, and melty cheese for a truly satisfying and comforting meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving some cheesy goodness, this dish is a surefire way to impress your guests and your taste buds. Enjoy!
