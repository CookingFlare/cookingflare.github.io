Korean Sweet Potato Stew Recipe (Goguma Jorim)


Korean cuisine is known for its variety of flavors and unique dishes that combine sweet, savory, and spicy elements in delightful harmony. One such dish that stands out for its comforting and naturally sweet flavor is Goguma Jorim, or Korean Sweet Potato Stew. This dish is a traditional Korean side dish, often enjoyed as part of a larger meal, and features tender sweet potatoes simmered in a rich, flavorful sauce. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or an experienced cook looking to add a warm and hearty dish to your repertoire, this recipe will show you how to prepare Goguma Jorim with simple ingredients and easy-to-follow instructions.

What Is Goguma Jorim?

Goguma Jorim is a Korean side dish made by braising sweet potatoes in a savory-sweet soy sauce-based broth. The stew is not overly complex but is incredibly satisfying due to the natural sweetness of the sweet potatoes paired with the umami flavors from the soy sauce and other ingredients. This dish can be served hot or at room temperature and is a favorite during Korean holidays, special family gatherings, or as a comforting dish on a chilly evening.

Unlike the traditional stews that feature meat, Goguma Jorim is entirely plant-based, making it an ideal option for vegetarians and vegans. The sweet potatoes absorb the sauce as they cook, resulting in soft, tender pieces of sweet potato that practically melt in your mouth.

Ingredients for Goguma Jorim

To prepare a traditional Korean Sweet Potato Stew, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Main Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes (Korean sweet potatoes or similar varieties work best)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce (for a gluten-free version, use tamari)
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar or honey (for a natural sweet flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar (optional, for a tangy flavor)
  • 2 cups of water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 2 green onions (sliced, for garnish)

Optional Ingredients for Extra Flavor

  • 1 teaspoon of ginger (grated)
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin (for extra sweetness and depth)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) for a hint of spice
  • A pinch of salt (to taste)

These ingredients are easy to find at most local supermarkets, and many are staples in Korean pantries. The beauty of this dish is its flexibility – you can adjust the sweetness, saltiness, or richness based on your preferences.

How to Make Goguma Jorim: Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Sweet Potatoes

The first step in making Goguma Jorim is preparing the sweet potatoes. Since the skin of Korean sweet potatoes is tender and flavorful, it’s not necessary to peel them. Simply wash the sweet potatoes thoroughly to remove any dirt. Then, cut them into bite-sized chunks. The size of the pieces can vary depending on your personal preference, but they should be small enough to cook evenly and absorb the flavors of the stew.

Step 2: Stir-Fry the Garlic and Soy Sauce

In a medium-sized pot, heat up the sesame oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the minced garlic and sauté it until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter. Once the garlic has softened and turned golden, add the soy sauce, brown sugar (or honey), and rice vinegar. Stir well to combine the ingredients.

At this stage, you can also add any optional ingredients you’d like to incorporate into the stew. If you’re using ginger, mirin, or gochugaru, now is the time to add them into the pot. Stir everything together until the sugar dissolves completely, and the mixture begins to bubble.

Step 3: Add the Sweet Potatoes and Liquid

Now, it’s time to add the prepared sweet potatoes to the pot. Gently toss the sweet potatoes in the sauce, ensuring each piece is coated well. Pour in the water or vegetable broth to the pot, just enough to cover the sweet potatoes. The liquid will help create the stew base, so make sure the sweet potatoes are mostly submerged.

Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium-high heat. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a lid. Let the sweet potatoes simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until they become tender and easily pierced with a fork. During this time, the sweet potatoes will absorb the flavors of the sauce, and the sauce will reduce to a thick, flavorful glaze.

Step 4: Check the Consistency

As the sweet potatoes cook, the sauce will thicken. If you find that the sauce is getting too thick and sticky before the potatoes are done, feel free to add a little more water or broth to adjust the consistency. If you’d like a richer, more concentrated sauce, let it reduce for a few extra minutes.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the sweet potatoes are fully cooked and tender, remove the pot from the heat. At this point, you can taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning, adding a pinch of salt or more sugar if necessary. Transfer the stew to a serving bowl and garnish with toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions for a fresh, crunchy contrast.

Goguma Jorim can be served immediately while it’s warm, or it can be allowed to cool and served at room temperature. It pairs beautifully with steamed rice, Korean kimchi, or as a side dish with any Korean meal.

Tips and Variations for Perfect Goguma Jorim

  • Choosing the Right Sweet Potatoes: If you’re new to Korean sweet potatoes, it’s important to note that they have a distinct texture compared to regular sweet potatoes. They’re firmer, drier, and less starchy, making them perfect for braising. Look for sweet potatoes with deep purple skin and light orange flesh for the best results.

  • Adjusting Sweetness and Savory Flavor: Goguma Jorim is all about balancing sweetness and savory flavors. You can adjust the sugar or honey based on how sweet you want the stew to be. Similarly, feel free to add more soy sauce if you prefer a saltier, more umami-rich dish.

  • Vegan-Friendly Options: This recipe is naturally vegan when you use plant-based ingredients. If you’re looking to make it more robust, you could add tofu cubes or tempeh for a protein boost.

  • For a Spicy Kick: If you enjoy a little spice, adding gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) can bring a nice heat to the dish. Start with a small amount and increase according to your preference.

  • Add More Vegetables: While sweet potatoes are the star of this dish, you can also throw in some carrots, onions, or even mushrooms to enhance the stew’s flavor and texture.

Why You Should Try Goguma Jorim

Goguma Jorim is more than just a side dish; it’s a perfect example of how Korean cuisine brings out the natural flavors of ingredients through simple yet effective cooking techniques. The combination of sweet, savory, and earthy flavors is comforting, satisfying, and balanced. It’s a fantastic option for a busy weeknight meal or as a hearty dish for gatherings. Plus, it’s naturally gluten-free and can be made vegan, making it suitable for various dietary preferences.

The best part about making Goguma Jorim is that it requires only a few basic ingredients that you likely already have in your pantry. The cooking process is straightforward, with minimal prep time and a simple cooking method. Once you try this dish, it’s sure to become a favorite in your cooking rotation.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just beginning your journey into Korean cuisine, Goguma Jorim offers an approachable and rewarding experience. Its deep flavors, nutritious ingredients, and comforting texture will make it a dish that you return to time and time again. So, grab a few sweet potatoes, gather the ingredients, and start cooking this delightful Korean stew today. Your taste buds will thank you!
