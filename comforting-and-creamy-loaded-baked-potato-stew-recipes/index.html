Comforting and Creamy Loaded Baked Potato Stew Recipes


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the hearty, satisfying nature of a loaded baked potato stew. This creamy, flavorful stew is a perfect choice for a cozy night in or a gathering with loved ones. The combination of tender potatoes, savory bacon, and gooey cheese makes this dish a true crowd-pleaser. In this blog post, we’ll explore two delightful variations of this classic recipe that are sure to become staples in your cooking repertoire.

Classic Loaded Baked Potato Stew

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the bacon over medium heat until it’s crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pot.
  2. Add the chopped onion to the pot and sauté until it’s soft and translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add the diced potatoes and chicken or vegetable broth to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Using a potato masher, partially mash the potatoes in the pot to thicken the stew.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and shredded cheddar cheese, allowing the cheese to melt and the stew to thicken further.
  6. Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the stew hot, garnished with the crispy bacon pieces and chopped chives.

This classic loaded baked potato stew is a comforting and indulgent dish that will warm you from the inside out. The combination of creamy potatoes, smoky bacon, and rich cheese creates a truly irresistible flavor profile that is sure to delight your taste buds.

Vegetarian Loaded Baked Potato Stew

For those who prefer a meatless option, the vegetarian loaded baked potato stew is a wonderful alternative that doesn’t skimp on flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup shredded sharp white cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it’s soft and translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  3. Using a potato masher, partially mash the potatoes in the pot to thicken the stew.
  4. Stir in the whole milk and shredded cheddar cheese, allowing the stew to thicken further.
  5. Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the stew hot, garnished with sliced green onions for a pop of color and freshness.

This vegetarian variation of the loaded baked potato stew is just as creamy and satisfying as its meaty counterpart. The creamy potatoes and rich cheese create a luscious base, while the green onions add a bright, fresh finish to the dish.

Whether you opt for the classic version with crispy bacon or the vegetarian rendition with sharp cheddar, these loaded baked potato stew recipes are guaranteed to fill your home with warmth and your belly with contentment. So, grab your ingredients, don your apron, and get ready to whip up a pot of pure comfort. Cheers to good food and even better company!
