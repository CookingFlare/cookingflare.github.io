Vegan and Vegetarian Casserole Recipes for the Whole Family


Casseroles are the ultimate comfort food. They bring the whole family together with their rich flavors, hearty textures, and ability to be made in large batches. Whether you’re a vegan or a vegetarian, casseroles offer a versatile foundation for creating healthy, satisfying meals. These dishes are perfect for feeding the family, meal prepping for the week, or bringing to a potluck. The best part? You can customize casseroles to suit various dietary preferences while still delivering on taste and nutrition.

If you’re looking to create a wholesome, plant-based casserole that everyone will love, here are several delicious recipes to try. From cheesy veggie bakes to savory lentil and mushroom mixes, you’ll find a variety of flavors and textures to keep your family coming back for more. Let’s dive into these mouthwatering vegan and vegetarian casserole recipes.

1. Vegan Sweet Potato and Black Bean Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup vegan shredded cheese (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the diced sweet potatoes in a large pot for about 10 minutes or until soft. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the onion, garlic, and bell pepper in a little olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper to the skillet and stir to combine.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked sweet potatoes, black beans, corn, sautéed onion mixture, and tomato sauce. Mix everything together until well combined.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased 9x13-inch casserole dish and spread it out evenly. Top with vegan shredded cheese, if desired.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the casserole is hot and bubbly and the cheese is melted.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This vibrant casserole offers a mix of sweet, savory, and smoky flavors. The black beans and sweet potatoes provide a healthy, hearty base, while the spices give it a kick. It’s an easy, filling dish that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner.

2. Vegetarian Mushroom and Spinach Lasagna Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked and drained
  • 1 pound mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese (or plant-based ricotta)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (or vegan mozzarella)
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook for about 5 minutes, until the mushrooms release their moisture and become soft.
  4. Stir in the spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt and pepper. Add the diced tomatoes and marinara sauce, and let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese and shredded mozzarella (reserve a bit of mozzarella for topping). Set aside.
  6. To assemble the casserole, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top.
  7. Add half of the mushroom and spinach mixture over the noodles, followed by half of the ricotta mixture. Repeat with another layer of noodles, sauce, mushrooms, and ricotta.
  8. Top the casserole with the remaining mozzarella cheese and bake for 30 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top.
  9. Let it cool for a few minutes before garnishing with fresh basil.

This vegetarian lasagna casserole is a wonderful way to enjoy all the flavors of lasagna without the traditional layers. The combination of mushrooms and spinach makes it a wholesome, satisfying dish that’s perfect for any vegetarian dinner.

3. Vegan Lentil and Vegetable Shepherd’s Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup peas (frozen or fresh)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ¼ cup unsweetened almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 3 tablespoons vegan butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the lentils and reduce the heat. Simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  3. While the lentils are cooking, boil the diced potatoes in a separate pot for about 10-15 minutes, or until fork-tender. Drain and mash with almond milk, vegan butter, salt, and pepper.
  4. In a large skillet, sauté the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery in a little oil over medium heat for about 7 minutes until softened.
  5. Add the cooked lentils, tomato paste, peas, thyme, rosemary, and salt and pepper to the skillet. Stir everything together and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  6. Transfer the lentil and vegetable mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Spread the mashed potatoes evenly over the top, smoothing it out with a spatula.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the top is slightly golden and crispy.
  8. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving.

This vegan shepherd’s pie is a true comfort food that’s filling and hearty. The lentils and vegetables provide a savory base, and the mashed potatoes create a creamy, indulgent topping. It’s a family-friendly dish that’s sure to satisfy everyone.

4. Vegan Cauliflower and Broccoli Mac and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 12 ounces elbow macaroni (gluten-free if preferred)
  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked in water for at least 2 hours
  • 1 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Steam the cauliflower and broccoli florets until tender, about 10 minutes.
  4. While the vegetables are steaming, blend the soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, onion powder, lemon juice, water, salt, and pepper in a high-speed blender until smooth and creamy.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked macaroni, steamed cauliflower, steamed broccoli, and cashew cheese sauce. Stir everything together until well coated.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased 9x13-inch casserole dish and spread it out evenly.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

This creamy, cheesy vegan mac and cheese casserole is a comforting dish that will please even the most discerning eaters. The cauliflower and broccoli add extra nutrition, while the cashew-based cheese sauce creates a rich, creamy texture. It’s the perfect casserole for a family meal or a gathering.

5. Vegan Zucchini and Tomato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 4 zucchinis, sliced
  • 3 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup bread crumbs (gluten-free if needed)
  • 1 cup vegan Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and onion, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer the zucchini, tomatoes, and sautéed onion mixture in an alternating pattern. Season with salt, pepper, oregano, and basil as you layer.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the breadcrumbs with vegan Parmesan cheese. Sprinkle the mixture over the top of the casserole.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the top is golden.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

This zucchini and tomato casserole is a light and fresh dish that’s bursting with flavor. The combination of roasted vegetables and crispy breadcrumbs makes it a perfect side dish or a main course for a lighter meal.

These vegan and vegetarian casserole recipes are packed with flavor, nutrition, and wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re looking for a hearty shepherd’s pie or a creamy mac and cheese, these dishes offer something for everyone. Best of all, they’re all family-friendly, making them perfect for busy weeknights or casual gatherings. Try one today and enjoy a delicious, plant-based meal that everyone can feel good about!
