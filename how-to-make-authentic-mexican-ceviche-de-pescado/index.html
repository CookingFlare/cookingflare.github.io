How to Make Authentic Mexican Ceviche de Pescado


Ceviche de Pescado, a quintessential Mexican dish that has captivated the senses of people worldwide with its unique blend of flavors and textures. This refreshing appetizer or snack, originating from the coastal regions of Mexico, offers a delightful combination of succulent seafood, tangy lime juice, and aromatic spices. In this recipe, we'll guide you through the preparation of an authentic Mexican Ceviche de Pescado that will transport you to the sun-kissed coasts of Mexico. History and Significance of Ceviche de Pescado Ceviche, also known as cahuahue or cevich, has been a staple in Mexican cuisine for centuries, particularly in coastal areas where fresh seafood was abundant. This traditional dish is made with raw fish marinated in citrus juices, such as lime or orange, which "cooks" the fish without heat, preserving its delicate flavor and texture. Ceviche de Pescado is a beloved dish that showcases the innovative and resourceful approach of Mexican cuisine in utilizing the freshest ingredients. Essential Ingredients for Authentic Ceviche de Pescado Before diving into the recipe, ensure you have the following key ingredients:

1 pound of firm white fish, such as halibut, snapper, or tilapia, cut into small dice 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice (6-8 limes) 1/4 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 1 minced jalapeño pepper (optional) 1 teaspoon ground cumin Salt and pepper to taste 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 ripe mango, diced (optional) Tortilla chips or tostadas for serving

Preparing the Ceviche de Pescado To start, carefully select your fish, ensuring it is fresh and of high quality. Cleaning and cutting the fish is crucial, as any imperfections or residual blood can affect the presentation and flavor. Cut the fish into small dice, taking care not to create any inconsistencies in size. This even cutting also helps to ensure that the ingredients are evenly coated with the marinade. In a large glass or ceramic bowl, combine the lime juice, chopped onion, cilantro, jalapeño (if using), and cumin. Stir to combine and add the garlic, mixing well to avoid creating a pungent flavor profile. Marinating the Fish To assemble the ceviche, gently add the fish dice to the marinade, taking care not to unbalance the bowl. Make sure the fish is fully submerged in the marinade to prevent any acidity from affecting the fish. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the fish to "cook" in the lime juice. Curing and Seasoning After the fish has been marinated, check if it has reached the preferred level of doneness. Remove the fish from the marinade, ensuring not to break apart the delicate flesh. Gently stir in the diced mango (if using) for added sweetness and texture. Taste and adjust the seasoning as necessary by adding salt and pepper to taste. Serving and Garnishing Transfer the ceviche to individual serving dishes or bowls, garnished with additional cilantro and a slice of lime. Serve the ceviche with tortilla chips or tostadas to scoop up the mixture. Traditionally, Ceviche de Pescado is also paired with other Mexican staples such as tortillas, roasted vegetables, or creamy avocado slices. Authenticity and Tips for Success Authentic Ceviche de Pescado requires a delicate balance of fresh seafood, acidity, and spices. To achieve this harmony, adhere to the following guidelines:

Utilize only the freshest ingredients Use a ratio of 1 cup of fish to 1/2 cup of lime juice for each pound of fish Avoid over-marinating, as this can create a bitter flavor Trust your senses, adjusting seasoning and acidity levels accordingly Serve immediately, preferably within 15-20 minutes of marinating to ensure the optimal flavor profile

Trying it Out: Where to Begin? Try experimenting with different combinations of fresh fish, citrus juices, and spices to tailor the flavor profile to your taste preferences. Feel free to substitute ingredients with regional favorites, but maintain the essence of traditional Mexican flavors.
