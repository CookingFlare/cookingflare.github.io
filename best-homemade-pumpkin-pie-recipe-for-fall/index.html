Best Homemade Pumpkin Pie Recipe for Fall


As the days grow shorter and the crispness of autumn fills the air, there’s no dessert more iconic for the season than a warm slice of homemade pumpkin pie. Its silky, spiced filling nestled in a flaky crust brings a comforting sense of nostalgia, making it the perfect treat for Thanksgiving, family gatherings, or even a quiet evening at home. If you’re searching for the best pumpkin pie recipe to truly capture the essence of fall, look no further. This step-by-step guide will show you how to make a pumpkin pie from scratch that will become a favorite in your household for years to come.

The Perfect Pumpkin Pie: Key Ingredients

Before diving into the recipe, let’s talk about the essential ingredients you’ll need to create a pumpkin pie that is bursting with flavor. While there are many variations, these basic ingredients will ensure a rich, spiced filling and a perfect, buttery crust.

For the Pie Crust:

  1. All-purpose flour – This will be the base of your crust, providing structure and texture.
  2. Salt – A pinch of salt helps balance out the sweetness in the filling.
  3. Butter – The key to a flaky, tender crust. You’ll want cold butter that’s cut into cubes.
  4. Ice water – To bring the dough together without warming it up too much.

For the Pumpkin Pie Filling:

  1. Pure pumpkin puree – Always choose pure pumpkin puree rather than pumpkin pie filling, which often contains added sugar and spices.
  2. Brown sugar – Adds a deeper, molasses-like sweetness that pairs wonderfully with the pumpkin.
  3. Granulated sugar – For the perfect balance of sweetness.
  4. Eggs – Essential for setting the filling and giving it the perfect texture.
  5. Heavy cream – This helps create a smooth, rich filling.
  6. Milk – A combination of milk and cream ensures a velvety smooth texture.
  7. Ground cinnamon – The classic spice that gives pumpkin pie its signature warmth.
  8. Ground ginger – Adds a subtle, zesty kick that complements the pumpkin’s earthiness.
  9. Ground nutmeg – For a touch of aromatic flavor that pairs beautifully with cinnamon and ginger.
  10. Ground cloves – A tiny amount adds an extra layer of depth and warmth.
  11. Vanilla extract – Enhances the overall flavor of the pie.
  12. Salt – A pinch to balance out the sweetness and spices.

With these ingredients, you’ll create a pumpkin pie that’s both flavorful and comforting—truly the perfect dessert for any fall occasion.

Step-by-Step Instructions for the Best Pumpkin Pie

1. Preparing the Pie Crust

First, let’s tackle the pie crust. While store-bought crusts can be convenient, homemade crusts have a superior texture and flavor. Making your own crust is easier than you might think, and the results are well worth the effort.

Make the Dough:

  1. Combine dry ingredients – In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 1 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
  2. Cut in the butter – Add 1/2 cup (1 stick) of cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or two forks, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. You should still have pea-sized bits of butter visible.
  3. Add ice water – Slowly add about 4-6 tablespoons of ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork after each addition. When the dough starts to come together, stop adding water. You don’t want the dough to be too wet, just enough to hold together when pressed.
  4. Form the dough – Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently a few times until it forms a ball. Flatten it into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Roll Out the Dough:

Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes. Lightly flour your work surface and roll the dough out into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges and crimp the edges to create a decorative border. Set the pie crust aside.

2. Making the Pumpkin Pie Filling

While your dough chills, it’s time to prepare the filling. This step is straightforward, and you’ll love the aroma of the spices as they blend with the pumpkin.

  1. Combine the wet ingredients – In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 1 1/2 cups of pure pumpkin puree, 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, and 3/4 cup of brown sugar.
  2. Add eggs – Beat in 2 large eggs, one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next.
  3. Add dairy – Pour in 1 cup of heavy cream and 1/2 cup of milk. Whisk until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  4. Spices and flavorings – Stir in 1 1/2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon, 1/2 teaspoon of ground ginger, 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg, 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves, and a pinch of salt. Finally, add 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract and whisk to combine.

3. Assembling the Pie

Now that your crust is prepared and the filling is mixed, it’s time to assemble the pie.

  1. Preheat your oven – Set the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Pour the filling – Carefully pour the prepared filling into the chilled pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  3. Bake the pie – Place the pie on the center rack of the preheated oven and bake for 15 minutes. This initial high heat helps the crust set and prevents it from becoming soggy. After 15 minutes, reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 40-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean. The edges of the crust should be golden brown, and the filling should have a slight wobble in the center.
  4. Cool the pie – Once the pie is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours. This allows the filling to set and develop its full flavor.

4. Serving and Storing the Pumpkin Pie

After your pumpkin pie has cooled, it’s time to enjoy it! For the best experience, serve your pie with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The rich, spiced filling pairs perfectly with the cool, creamy toppings.

Storing the pie: If you have any leftovers (though this is unlikely!), store the pie in the refrigerator. Cover it loosely with plastic wrap or foil, and it will keep for up to 3 days. For longer storage, you can freeze the pie. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and foil, and it will stay fresh in the freezer for up to 1 month. To thaw, simply place it in the refrigerator overnight before serving.

Tips for the Best Pumpkin Pie

  1. Use pure pumpkin puree – Always opt for pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling, which contains added spices and sugar. You can find pure pumpkin puree in cans at most grocery stores, or you can roast a fresh pumpkin and puree it yourself.
  2. Chill your dough – Don’t skip the chilling step for your pie crust. This helps the butter stay cold, creating a flaky, tender crust.
  3. Adjust spices to taste – The amount of spice in your pumpkin pie can be adjusted based on personal preference. If you love cinnamon, feel free to add a little more. Likewise, if you enjoy a spicier pie, add extra ginger or cloves.
  4. Blind bake the crust – If you want a crispier crust, you can blind bake the pie crust before adding the filling. Simply line the crust with parchment paper, fill it with pie weights or dried beans, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10-15 minutes. Remove the weights and parchment, then fill the crust and bake as usual.
  5. Don’t overbake – Overbaking can lead to a dry filling. When the pie is done, the center should still have a slight wobble. It will firm up as it cools.

Why This Pumpkin Pie Recipe is the Best

What sets this recipe apart from others is its balance of flavors and textures. The crust is perfectly flaky, the filling is rich and creamy with just the right amount of spice, and the combination of milk and cream creates an incredibly smooth texture. Whether you’re making this pumpkin pie for a special occasion or simply as a fall treat, it’s sure to impress your friends and family.

With this recipe, you’ll create a pumpkin pie that embodies the flavors of fall and becomes a staple in your dessert repertoire. It’s simple enough for even beginner bakers to tackle, yet impressive enough for seasoned bakers to enjoy. If you’ve been looking for the ultimate pumpkin pie recipe, this is it—one that will leave everyone asking for seconds. Happy baking, and enjoy every delicious bite!
