Classic British Pies: Savory and Sweet Recipes to Enjoy


British pies have a long history dating back to the 12th century, and they remain a beloved staple of British cuisine to this day. Whether you’re looking for a comforting savory pie or a sweet treat, British pies have something for everyone. In this article, we’ll explore the world of classic British pies, from traditional savory pies to sweet and indulgent treats.

Savory Pies

Savory pies are a staple of British cuisine, and for good reason. They’re hearty, comforting, and perfect for a cold winter’s night. Here are a few classic British savory pies that are sure to become a favorite:

Steak and Kidney Pie

Steak and kidney pie is a classic British dish that’s been a staple of pub menus for generations. The filling is made with slow-cooked beef, kidney, and vegetables, topped with a puff pastry crust. To make it, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound beef, cut into small pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pie crust

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, cook the beef and onion over medium-high heat until the beef is browned. Add the garlic, broth, wine, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 1/2 hours. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the beef mixture and dot the top with butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

Chicken and Mushroom Pie

Chicken and mushroom pie is another classic British dish that’s perfect for a cold winter’s night. The filling is made with sautéed chicken, mushrooms, and vegetables, topped with a puff pastry crust. To make it, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (such as button, cremini, and shiitake), sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pie crust

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, cook the chicken and mushrooms over medium-high heat until the chicken is browned. Add the onion and garlic and cook until the onion is translucent. Add the broth and cream and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the chicken mixture and dot the top with butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

Sweet Pies

Sweet pies are a delicious and indulgent treat that’s perfect for dessert. Here are a few classic British sweet pies that are sure to become a favorite:

Apple Pie

Apple pie is a classic British dessert that’s perfect for a cold winter’s night. The filling is made with sliced apples, sugar, and spices, topped with a puff pastry crust. To make it, you’ll need:

  • 2 pounds Granny Smith apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 pie crust

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until the apples are evenly coated with the dry ingredients. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture and dot the top with butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

Treacle Tart

Treacle tart is a classic British dessert that’s perfect for a special occasion. The filling is made with golden syrup, sugar, and eggs, topped with a pastry crust. To make it, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup golden syrup
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 pie crust

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the golden syrup, sugar, eggs, salt, and baking soda. Mix until smooth. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the treacle mixture and dot the top with butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.

Conclusion

Classic British pies are a delicious and comforting treat that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking for a savory pie to warm your belly or a sweet pie to satisfy your sweet tooth, there’s a British pie out there for you. With their rich history and delicious flavors, it’s no wonder that British pies remain a beloved staple of British cuisine. So go ahead, give one of these recipes a try, and experience the warmth and comfort of a classic British pie for yourself.
