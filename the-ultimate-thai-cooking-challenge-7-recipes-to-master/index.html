The Ultimate Thai Cooking Challenge: 7 Recipes to Master


Thai cuisine is a harmonious blend of bold flavors, vibrant colors, and aromatic herbs. Whether you’re a novice in the kitchen or a seasoned chef, mastering a few essential Thai dishes can elevate your cooking skills and introduce you to a whole new world of culinary possibilities. In this post, we’ll walk you through seven iconic Thai recipes that will challenge your cooking abilities and help you develop a deeper understanding of Thai food.

Get ready to embrace the challenge! From tangy curries to zesty salads, here are the seven Thai recipes that will transform you into a pro.

1. Pad Thai: The Quintessential Thai Noodle Dish

Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Pad Thai is often the first dish that comes to mind when we think of Thai food. This flavorful stir-fried noodle dish combines rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, tofu, peanuts, bean sprouts, and a tangy tamarind sauce. It’s a perfect balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy, which are the hallmark flavors of Thai cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp or chicken breast (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • ¼ cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1-2 red chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-2 spring onions, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, lime juice, and red chili to make the sauce.
  3. Heat the oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. If using shrimp or chicken, add it to the wok and cook until fully done.
  5. Push the protein to one side of the wok and pour the beaten egg into the other side. Scramble the egg until cooked.
  6. Add the cooked noodles and sauce to the wok, tossing everything together to coat the noodles.
  7. Stir in the bean sprouts and spring onions. Cook for another minute or two.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped peanuts, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges on the side.

2. Tom Yum Goong: The Famous Thai Hot and Sour Soup

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 20 minutes
Serves: 4

Tom Yum Goong is a spicy, tangy, and aromatic soup that’s a staple in Thai cuisine. It’s known for its distinct flavor profile, achieved by using ingredients like lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and fish sauce. The soup is commonly made with shrimp, but you can easily substitute it with chicken or tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 400g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, smashed and cut into pieces
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 4-5 slices galangal
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 handful fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Thai chili paste (optional, for added heat)

Instructions:

  1. Bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil in a large pot.
  2. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and bird’s eye chilies to the broth. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. Add the mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the shrimp to the soup and cook until they turn pink (about 2-3 minutes).
  5. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Taste and adjust the seasoning to your liking.
  6. Remove the soup from heat and discard the lemongrass, galangal, and lime leaves.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot.

3. Green Curry: A Creamy, Spicy Coconut Delight

Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 30 minutes
Serves: 4

Thai green curry is known for its rich and creamy coconut base, infused with aromatic herbs and spices. The green curry paste is the star ingredient, offering a spicy, herbaceous kick. This curry is typically made with chicken, eggplant, and bamboo shoots, but you can adapt it to your tastes by adding your favorite vegetables or protein.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 200g eggplant, cubed
  • 100g bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1-2 Thai bird’s eye chilies, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves, for garnish
  • Rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pan over medium heat and add the green curry paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes until it becomes aromatic.
  2. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, stirring to combine. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the chicken slices and cook for 5-7 minutes until they are fully cooked.
  4. Add the eggplant, bamboo shoots, and bell pepper. Continue to simmer for another 10 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce, palm sugar, and lime juice. Adjust the flavors to your liking.
  6. Garnish with fresh Thai basil and chopped chilies for extra heat, if desired.
  7. Serve the curry hot with steamed rice.

4. Som Tum: A Refreshing Thai Green Papaya Salad

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cook time: 5 minutes
Serves: 4

Som Tum is a popular Thai salad made with shredded green papaya, tomatoes, green beans, peanuts, and a tangy, spicy dressing. The salad is a perfect balance of sweetness, sourness, saltiness, and spice. It’s typically served with sticky rice or grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup green beans, chopped
  • ¼ cup roasted peanuts
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste

Instructions:

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies until finely mashed.
  2. Add the tamarind paste, fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar to the mortar. Mix well until the sugar dissolves.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, and green beans.
  4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss everything together.
  5. Top with roasted peanuts and serve immediately with sticky rice or grilled meats.

5. Massaman Curry: A Rich, Mildly Spicy Beef Curry

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cook time: 45 minutes
Serves: 4

Massaman curry is a unique Thai curry that combines traditional Thai ingredients with Persian and Indian influences. It has a mild yet aromatic flavor profile, thanks to the addition of ingredients like cinnamon, cardamom, and roasted peanuts. This dish is perfect for anyone who enjoys a less spicy but still flavorful curry.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef stew meat, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • ¼ cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add the Massaman curry paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes until it becomes fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk and beef cubes, stirring to coat the beef with the curry paste.
  3. Add the potatoes, onion, cinnamon stick, and cardamom pods. Stir well to combine.
  4. Simmer the curry on low heat for 30-40 minutes, or until the beef is tender and the potatoes are cooked through.
  5. Season with fish sauce, sugar, and tamarind paste. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with roasted peanuts and fresh cilantro.

6. Khao Pad: The Famous Thai Fried Rice

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 15 minutes
Serves: 4

Thai fried rice, or Khao Pad, is a versatile dish that can be made with a variety of ingredients. It’s quick, easy, and perfect for using up leftovers. The key to great fried rice is day-old rice, which is drier and fries better than freshly cooked rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 200g shrimp or chicken, cooked and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 spring onion, chopped
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add the eggs and scramble them until fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same wok, add the remaining oil and sauté the onion, garlic, and carrots for 2-3 minutes until soft.
  3. Add the shrimp or chicken, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked rice, soy sauce, fish sauce, and oyster sauce to the wok. Stir well to combine, breaking up any clumps of rice.
  5. Stir in the scrambled eggs and spring onions. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Serve the fried rice with lime wedges on the side.

7. Thai Iced Tea: A Sweet and Creamy Beverage

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 5 minutes
Serves: 2

No Thai meal is complete without a refreshing Thai iced tea. This sweet, creamy beverage is made from strong brewed black tea, sugar, and condensed milk, creating a perfect balance of sweetness and richness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons Thai tea leaves
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup ice

Instructions:

  1. Boil 2 cups of water and steep the Thai tea leaves for about 5 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and stir in the sugar while the tea is still hot.
  3. Let the tea cool down to room temperature or chill it in the fridge for quicker results.
  4. Fill a glass with ice and pour the cooled tea over it.
  5. Add the sweetened condensed milk, stirring until well combined.
  6. Serve cold and enjoy!

Conclusion

These seven recipes will test and expand your Thai cooking skills while allowing you to experience the full range of flavors that make Thai food so beloved worldwide. Whether you’re making a fragrant soup, a rich curry, or a refreshing salad, each dish provides an opportunity to explore new ingredients and cooking techniques.

Don’t be intimidated by the bold flavors and intricate combinations—embrace the challenge, and with each dish you master, you’ll find yourself more confident in the kitchen and more connected to the heart of Thai cuisine. Happy cooking!
