Soft and Chewy Gingersnaps: A Spicy and Sweet Cookie


Gingersnaps are a classic cookie that combines the warm and spicy flavors of ginger with the sweetness of molasses. These soft and chewy gingersnaps are the perfect treat to enjoy with a cup of tea or as a dessert after a delicious meal. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making these delightful cookies from scratch. So let’s dig in!

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ cup molasses
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar (for rolling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Set aside.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, ground ginger, baking soda, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and 1 cup of granulated sugar until light and fluffy. You can use an electric mixer or do it by hand if you prefer.

  4. Add the egg to the butter and sugar mixture and beat until well combined. Then, add the molasses and mix until fully incorporated.

  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, mixing well after each addition. The dough will be thick and slightly sticky.

  6. Take tablespoon-sized portions of the dough and roll them into balls using the palms of your hands. Roll each ball in the remaining ⅓ cup of granulated sugar until coated.

  7. Place the sugar-coated dough balls onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading.

  8. Bake the gingersnaps in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still slightly soft. Keep in mind that the cookies will firm up as they cool.

  9. Remove the baking sheet from the oven and let the gingersnaps cool on the sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  10. Once the gingersnaps are completely cooled, store them in an airtight container to maintain their soft and chewy texture.

Serving Suggestions

These soft and chewy gingersnaps are delicious on their own, but you can also get creative with how you serve them. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Enjoy a gingersnap alongside a hot cup of tea or coffee for a cozy afternoon treat.
  • Create an indulgent dessert by sandwiching a scoop of your favorite ice cream between two gingersnap cookies.
  • Crumble gingersnaps over a bowl of vanilla ice cream or yogurt for added texture and flavor.
  • Serve gingersnaps alongside a warm and comforting bowl of apple or pumpkin soup for a delightful contrast of flavors.

Conclusion

Soft and Chewy Gingersnaps are a delightful combination of spicy and sweet flavors that will satisfy your cookie cravings. With their soft and chewy texture, these cookies are sure to become a favorite among family and friends. So why not give this recipe a try and experience the joy of baking these delicious treats in your own kitchen? Happy baking!
