Tempting Toffee Pudding Recipe for a Sweet Christmas Treat


‘Tis the season to indulge in delightful desserts, and what better way to spread Christmas cheer than with a mouthwatering Toffee Pudding. This rich and decadent treat is a perfect addition to your holiday menu, guaranteed to leave your guests craving for more. So, let’s dive into the tantalizing world of Toffee Pudding and learn how to create this irresistible dessert that will surely become a family favorite.

Ingredients

For the Pudding:

  • 1 cup Medjool dates, pitted and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Toffee Sauce:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9 inch baking dish.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the chopped dates and baking soda. Pour the boiling water over the dates and let it sit for 5 minutes.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  4. Using an electric mixer, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the creamed butter and sugar, mixing until just combined.

  6. Fold in the soaked dates and their liquid until evenly distributed.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula.

  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the pudding is set and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  9. While the pudding is baking, prepare the toffee sauce. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, butter, and dark brown sugar. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce is smooth and slightly thickened. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

  10. Once the pudding is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.

  11. Using a skewer or toothpick, poke holes all over the top of the warm pudding.

  12. Pour half of the warm toffee sauce over the pudding, allowing it to seep into the holes.

  13. Serve the Toffee Pudding warm, drizzled with the remaining toffee sauce. For an extra indulgence, pair it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a festive twist, add a splash of rum or brandy to the toffee sauce for a boozy kick.
  • Substitute the all-purpose flour with almond flour for a gluten-free version of this delectable dessert.
  • Sprinkle chopped pecans or walnuts on top of the toffee sauce for added crunch and nutty flavor.

Now, you’re ready to impress your loved ones with this irresistible Toffee Pudding recipe. Let the warm, buttery aroma fill your home and create lasting memories with this sweet Christmas treat. Happy baking and Merry Christmas!
