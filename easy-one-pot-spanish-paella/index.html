Easy One Pot Spanish Paella


If you’re looking for a delicious and impressive dish to serve your family and friends, look no further than this easy one-pot Spanish paella recipe. Paella is a traditional Spanish rice dish that is packed full of flavor and is perfect for a special occasion or a cozy family dinner. The best part? It’s surprisingly simple to make, and with this recipe, you can recreate the authentic flavors of Spain right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this mouthwatering paella:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon saffron threads
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 lb chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 lb chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by prepping all your ingredients. Chop the onion, slice the bell peppers, mince the garlic, and cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces.

  2. Sear the Chicken and Chorizo: In a large, deep skillet or paella pan, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and chorizo and cook until browned, then transfer to a plate and set aside.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the onion, garlic, and bell peppers. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant.

  4. Add the Rice and Spices: Stir in the Arborio rice, smoked paprika, and saffron threads. Cook for a minute to toast the rice and infuse it with the spices.

  5. Simmer with Broth and Tomatoes: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and add the diced tomatoes. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  6. Incorporate the Protein: Nestle the seared chicken and chorizo back into the pan, along with the frozen peas. Let everything cook together for another 10 minutes, or until the rice is almost tender.

  7. Add the Shrimp: Gently place the shrimp on top of the paella and let it cook for a few more minutes until the shrimp turns pink and opaque.

  8. Finish and Serve: Once the rice is tender and the shrimp is cooked through, remove the pan from the heat. Season the paella with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Tips for Success

  • Use a wide, shallow pan if you don’t have a traditional paella pan. This will help the rice cook evenly and develop that coveted socarrat, the crispy layer of rice at the bottom of the pan.
  • Feel free to customize the protein in your paella. You can use a combination of chicken, chorizo, seafood, or even make it vegetarian with an assortment of vegetables and beans.
  • Don’t stir the paella once the rice has been added. This will help form that desirable crust on the bottom.

Final Thoughts

Spanish paella is a vibrant and comforting dish that brings people together around the table. Its rich flavors and colorful presentation make it a standout addition to any gathering. With this easy one-pot recipe, you can capture the essence of Spain and delight your loved ones with a memorable meal. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and let the aromas of saffron and smoked paprika transport you to the sunny shores of Valencia. Enjoy your culinary adventure!
