Baking with Citrus: Bright and Zesty Desserts


Citrus fruits not only add a burst of refreshing flavor to desserts but also bring a vibrant touch of color to your creations. From tangy lemons to juicy oranges and zesty limes, the world of baking with citrus is a journey filled with delightful surprises. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of citrus-infused desserts that are sure to brighten up your day. So, let’s grab our aprons and dive into the world of zesty treats!

Lemon Bars

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 40 minutes
Total time: 55 minutes
Servings: 12 bars

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the flour and powdered sugar. Cut in the softened butter using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  4. Bake the crust for 15-20 minutes or until lightly golden.
  5. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest until well combined.
  6. Add the flour and baking powder to the egg mixture and whisk until smooth.
  7. Pour the filling over the baked crust and return to the oven.
  8. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes until the filling is set and lightly golden.
  9. Allow the lemon bars to cool completely before cutting into squares.
  10. Dust the bars with powdered sugar before serving.

Orange and Almond Cake

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 45 minutes
Total time: 1 hour 5 minutes
Servings: 8-10 slices

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup fresh orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons orange zest
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • ½ cup sliced almonds
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the sugar and softened butter until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, until well incorporated.
  5. Stir in the orange juice, orange zest, and almond extract.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  8. Sprinkle the sliced almonds evenly over the batter.
  9. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  11. Dust the cake with powdered sugar before serving.

Lime Coconut Bars

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 25 minutes
Total time: 45 minutes
Servings: 12 bars

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons lime zest
  • 1 cup shredded coconut

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8-inch baking dish.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, lime juice, and lime zest until well combined.
  4. Pour the lime mixture over the crust and spread it evenly.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded coconut on top, covering the lime mixture completely.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the coconut turns golden brown.
  7. Allow the bars to cool completely before cutting into squares.

Final Thoughts

Baking with citrus fruits adds a burst of tanginess and brightness to your desserts. Whether you choose to make lemon bars, orange and almond cake, or lime coconut bars, these zesty treats are bound to impress your friends and family. The vibrant flavors of citrus combined with the sweetness of these desserts create a harmonious balance that will keep you coming back for more. So, don’t hesitate to explore the world of baking with citrus and let your taste buds dance with joy!
