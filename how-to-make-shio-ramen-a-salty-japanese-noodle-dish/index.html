How to Make Shio Ramen: A Salty Japanese Noodle Dish


Ramen has become a beloved dish around the world, offering endless variations that entice the palate. Among these, Shio ramen, characterized by its salty broth, stands out with its light, delicate flavors and appealing simplicity. Originating from Japan, Shio ramen often showcases a clear broth flavored with salt, making it a favorite for those who enjoy a mildly seasoned yet nutritious meal. This blog post will guide you through the step-by-step process of making authentic Shio ramen at home. Not only will you learn how to prepare this delectable dish, but you will also discover useful tips and tricks to elevate your ramen game. Understanding Shio Ramen Before diving into the cooking process, it's essential to understand exactly what Shio ramen entails. In the context of ramen, "Shio" translates to "salt," which indicates the primary seasoning in this variety. The broth is typically made from a combination of ingredients such as chicken, seafood, or vegetables, resulting in a lighter flavor profile compared to soy sauce ramen or miso ramen. The beauty of Shio ramen lies in its simplicity, allowing the fresh ingredients to shine through. The Essential Components of Shio Ramen To create a delightful bowl of Shio ramen, you will need to focus on three primary components:

Broth: The foundation of any ramen dish. For Shio ramen, a clear, salty broth is essential. Noodles: Fresh or dried ramen noodles that complement the broth. Toppings: Various garnishes that add texture and flavor. Common toppings include sliced pork, green onions, nori (seaweed), and menma (bamboo shoots).

Ingredients for Shio Ramen Broth Ingredients

Chicken carcasses (or a mix of chicken and pork bones): 1-2 pounds Water: 8 cups Ginger: 1 knob, sliced Garlic: 4 cloves, crushed Green onions: 2, roughly chopped Dried kombu (kelp): 1 piece (about 10cm x 10cm) Bonito flakes: 1 cup (optional for umami) Salt: 2-3 tablespoons, adjust to taste

Noodle Ingredients

Ramen noodles: 2 servings (fresh or dried, any type you prefer)

Toppings

Chashu (braised pork belly): 200 grams Menma (bamboo shoots): 100 grams Green onions: 2, finely sliced Nori (seaweed): 2 sheets, cut into strips Soft-boiled eggs: 2 (optional) Sesame seeds: for garnish (optional)

Equipment Needed

Large pot for the broth Saucepan for boiling noodles Strainer for broth Bowls for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Shio Ramen Step 1: Prepare the Broth

Clean and blanch the bones: Start by cleaning the chicken carcasses and bones under cold running water. To remove impurities, place the bones in a large pot filled with water and bring it to a boil for about 5 minutes. Drain and rinse them under cold water.

Combine ingredients: In a clean large pot, add the blanched bones, 8 cups of water, sliced ginger, crushed garlic, chopped green onions, and dried kombu. If using bonito flakes, add them as well.

Simmer the broth: Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for at least 2 hours. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface for a clearer broth. You can simmer it longer (up to 6 hours) for a richer flavor.

Strain the broth: Once the broth is done simmering, strain out the solids using a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth. Discard the solids and return the clear broth to the pot. Season the broth with salt to taste.

Step 2: Prepare the Noodles

Cook the noodles: Bring a separate pot of water to a boil. Add the ramen noodles and cook according to the package instructions (usually around 2-4 minutes for fresh noodles and 4-6 minutes for dried noodles). Ensure the noodles are al dente since they will continue to cook slightly in the hot broth.

Drain and rinse: Once cooked, drain the noodles and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside for assembly.

Step 3: Prepare the Toppings

Prepare chashu: If you’re using chashu, slice it thinly. You can refer to any traditional chashu recipe, which usually involves braising the pork belly in soy sauce, mirin, and other seasonings until tender.

Prepare soft-boiled eggs: If using, boil the eggs for about 6-7 minutes for a soft yolk, then transfer them to an ice bath to stop the cooking. Peel and cut in half before serving.

Step 4: Assembling the Ramen Bowl

Noodles first: In a large serving bowl, place a portion of noodles at the bottom.

Add the broth: Gently ladle the hot, salted broth over the noodles, enough to cover them.

Add toppings: Artfully arrange the sliced chashu, menma, green onions, egg halves (if using), and nori strips on top of the noodles.

Final touches: If desired, sprinkle sesame seeds over the ramen for an added touch.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy Serve the Shio ramen immediately while hot, and enjoy the symphony of flavors and textures in your homemade bowl. Tips for a Perfect Shio Ramen

Quality ingredients: Use fresh and quality ingredients for the best flavor. The quality of the broth is paramount in ramen, making fresh bones and vegetables key.

Adjust seasoning: Everyone’s taste is different. Adjust the salt in the broth gradually, tasting as you go, to ensure the perfect level of seasoning.

Noodle sauce: Often, a small amount of yuzu or lemon juice can add a refreshing note to your ramen. Drizzle a few drops before serving.

Experiment with toppings: Don’t hesitate to customize your toppings. Other great options include corn, spinach, and bean sprouts. They can enhance both the flavor and the nutritional profile of your dish.

Conclusion Crafting a bowl of Shio ramen at home can be a fulfilling culinary adventure. The process might take time, especially when simmering the broth, but the end result—a warm, savory bowl of noodles—is worth the effort. By paying close attention to each component, from the thoughtfully prepared broth to the perfectly cooked noodles, you’ll be able to savor an authentic Shio ramen experience right in your kitchen. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on this flavorful journey. Enjoy the process, and don't forget to share your ramen creations with friends and family. Happy cooking!
