Preserving the Flavor: Recipes for Canning Fresh Meats


In the world of food preservation, canning is a time-honored technique that allows us to enjoy the flavors of fresh produce long after their peak season. While canning fruits and vegetables is quite common, canning meats might not be as well-known. However, canning fresh meats is a fantastic way to preserve their flavor and tenderness, ensuring you have a delicious protein source on hand whenever you need it. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering recipes for canning fresh meats that will elevate your pantry game to a whole new level.

The Benefits of Canning Fresh Meats

Canning fresh meats offers several advantages. Firstly, it allows you to lock in the flavor of the meat at its prime, ensuring that every bite is as succulent and delicious as the day it was canned. Secondly, canning meats provides convenience. You can prepare large batches of canned meats and store them in your pantry, eliminating the need for frequent grocery store trips. Lastly, canning fresh meats can be a cost-effective way to take advantage of sales or deals on bulk meat purchases.

Recipe 1: Savory Canned Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • 4 cups of chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large pot.
  2. Season the chicken with salt, black pepper, and dried thyme.
  3. Pour the chicken broth over the seasoned chicken.
  4. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cooked chicken into sterilized canning jars, leaving about an inch of headspace.
  6. Ladle the hot broth into the jars, ensuring the chicken is fully covered.
  7. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip tight.
  8. Process the jars in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for pint-sized jars or 90 minutes for quart-sized jars.
  9. Allow the jars to cool and check for proper seals before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Recipe 2: Tender Canned Pork

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds of pork shoulder, trimmed of excess fat and cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 4 cups of pork broth

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine salt, brown sugar, paprika, and garlic powder.
  2. Rub the seasoning mixture onto the chunks of pork shoulder, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  3. Place the seasoned pork shoulder into a large pot and pour the pork broth over it.
  4. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 2 hours or until the pork is tender.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cooked pork into sterilized canning jars, leaving about an inch of headspace.
  6. Ladle the hot broth into the jars, covering the pork completely.
  7. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip tight.
  8. Process the jars in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for pint-sized jars or 90 minutes for quart-sized jars.
  9. Allow the jars to cool and check for proper seals before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Recipe 3: Flavorful Canned Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 4 cups of diced potatoes
  • 2 cups of sliced carrots
  • 2 cups of chopped onions

Instructions:

  1. Season the beef stew meat with salt, black pepper, and Worcestershire sauce.
  2. In a large pot, combine the seasoned beef, beef broth, diced potatoes, sliced carrots, and chopped onions.
  3. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 hour or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  4. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the beef stew into sterilized canning jars, leaving about an inch of headspace.
  5. Ladle the hot broth and vegetables into the jars, ensuring the beef is fully covered.
  6. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip tight.
  7. Process the jars in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for pint-sized jars or 90 minutes for quart-sized jars.
  8. Allow the jars to cool and check for proper seals before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Enjoy the Fruits of Your Labor

Canning fresh meats allows you to enjoy the flavors of your favorite proteins long after their season has passed. Whether it’s savory canned chicken, tender canned pork, or flavorful canned beef stew, these recipes will ensure you always have a delicious and convenient protein source at your fingertips. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on your journey to preserve the flavor of fresh meats through the wonderful art of canning!

Remember, when canning meats, it’s important to follow proper canning procedures and guidelines to ensure food safety. Take the time to sterilize your jars, process them in a pressure canner, and store them in a cool, dark place. With a little effort and patience, you’ll be rewarded with jars of mouthwatering goodness that will make your meals truly unforgettable. Happy canning!
