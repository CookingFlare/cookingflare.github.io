Delicious Grilled Vegetable Recipes for Your Picnic


Are you planning a picnic and looking for some delicious and healthy recipes to take along? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some mouthwatering grilled vegetable recipes that are perfect for your outdoor gathering. Grilling vegetables not only brings out their natural flavors but also adds a smoky and charred touch that will tantalize your taste buds. So, let’s fire up the grill and get cooking!

Grilled Zucchini and Eggplant Skewers

If you’re a fan of zucchini and eggplant, you’ll love this simple yet flavorful recipe. Start by cutting the zucchini and eggplant into thick slices. In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Toss the vegetables in the marinade and let them sit for about 15 minutes.

While the vegetables marinate, preheat the grill to medium heat. Thread the zucchini and eggplant slices onto skewers and place them on the grill. Cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred. Serve the skewers hot and enjoy the smoky goodness!

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Corn on the cob is a classic summertime favorite, and grilling it takes it to a whole new level. Start by peeling back the husks of the corn, leaving them attached at the base. Remove the silk strands and then pull the husks back up to cover the corn. Soak the corn in water for about 15 minutes to prevent it from drying out on the grill.

Meanwhile, prepare the herb butter by combining softened butter, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, thyme, and chives), salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the soaked corn on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and lightly charred. Carefully peel back the husks and brush the corn with the herb butter. Serve hot and savor the irresistible flavors!

Grilled Bell Pepper and Halloumi Salad

For a refreshing and satisfying salad option, try this grilled bell pepper and halloumi combination. Start by halving and deseeding bell peppers, then brush them with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.

Preheat the grill to medium heat and place the bell peppers on the grates. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the peppers are soft and slightly charred. Remove from the grill and let them cool.

In the meantime, slice the halloumi cheese and grill it for about 2-3 minutes per side until it develops a golden-brown crust. Cut the grilled bell peppers into thin strips and combine them with the grilled halloumi in a bowl.

To make the dressing, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the bell pepper and halloumi mixture and toss gently to coat. Serve the salad at room temperature and revel in the deliciousness!

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers

Who says burgers have to be meat-based? These grilled portobello mushroom burgers are a fantastic vegetarian option that will satisfy even the most dedicated carnivores. Start by cleaning the portobello mushrooms and removing the stems.

In a bowl, combine olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Brush the mushroom caps with the marinade, making sure to coat both sides. Let them marinate for about 30 minutes.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the marinated mushrooms on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and juicy.

To assemble the burger, spread a dollop of mayo or your favorite sauce on a bun. Place the grilled portobello mushroom on the bottom bun and top with your favorite toppings, such as lettuce, tomato, onion, and cheese. Finish with the top bun and sink your teeth into this scrumptious creation!

Conclusion

Grilled vegetables are a fantastic addition to any picnic menu. They offer a healthy and flavorful alternative to traditional picnic fare. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more veggies into your diet, these grilled vegetable recipes will surely impress your family and friends. So, fire up the grill, pack your picnic basket, and get ready to indulge in the deliciousness of grilled vegetables!
