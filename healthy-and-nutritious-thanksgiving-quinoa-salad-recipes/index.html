Healthy and Nutritious Thanksgiving Quinoa Salad Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time of indulgence, with rich and decadent dishes taking center stage on the table. But why not balance out the feast with some healthy and nutritious options? Quinoa salad is a perfect choice for those looking to add a wholesome touch to their Thanksgiving spread. Packed with protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, quinoa is a versatile grain that can be used in a variety of dishes. In this blog post, we will explore three delicious and easy-to-make Thanksgiving quinoa salad recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

1. Roasted Vegetable Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cook the quinoa according to package instructions and set aside to cool.
  3. In a large baking dish, toss the mixed vegetables with olive oil, dried herbs, salt, and pepper.
  4. Roast the vegetables in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly charred.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, lemon juice, parsley, and feta cheese (if using).
  6. Toss everything together until well combined. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  7. Serve the salad at room temperature or chilled.

2. Cranberry and Pecan Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water and drain.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, and green onions.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the quinoa mixture and toss until well coated.
  6. Serve the salad at room temperature or chilled.

3. Apple and Kale Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups chopped kale
  • 1 apple, diced
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa in vegetable broth according to package instructions and set aside to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chopped kale, diced apple, dried cranberries, and chopped walnuts.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the quinoa mixture and toss until well coated.
  5. Let the salad sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Serve the salad at room temperature or chilled.

These healthy and nutritious Thanksgiving quinoa salad recipes are a wonderful addition to any holiday table. They provide a refreshing balance to the richness of traditional Thanksgiving dishes, while still delivering on flavor and satisfaction. Whether you choose the roasted vegetable, cranberry and pecan, or apple and kale variation, your guests are sure to be impressed with these wholesome and delicious salads. Give them a try this Thanksgiving and enjoy the benefits of a nutritious and flavorful feast.
