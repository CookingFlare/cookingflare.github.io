Authentic Neapolitan Pizza: History and Recipe


If there’s one dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people worldwide, it’s the authentic Neapolitan pizza. This iconic Italian creation has a rich history and a distinct recipe that sets it apart from other pizza variations. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the fascinating history of Neapolitan pizza and provide you with a traditional recipe that will transport your senses to the streets of Naples.

The History of Neapolitan Pizza

Origins and Tradition

Neapolitan pizza, or “pizza Napoletana” as it’s known in Italian, traces its roots back to the vibrant city of Naples in the 18th century. Initially, it was a simple and humble dish, consisting of basic ingredients such as dough, tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, olive oil, and basil. The pizza was a favorite among the working class due to its affordability and delicious flavors.

The Art of Pizza Making

The craft of making Neapolitan pizza is deeply ingrained in tradition and culture. In fact, the art of pizza making in Naples is so revered that it has been safeguarded by the True Neapolitan Pizza Association (Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana, VPN), which ensures that pizzerias around the world adhere to the authentic Neapolitan pizza-making process.

UNESCO Recognition

In 2017, the traditional art of Neapolitan pizza-making was inscribed on the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. This recognition not only celebrates the culinary significance of Neapolitan pizza but also acknowledges the cultural heritage and expertise that goes into crafting this beloved dish.

The Authentic Neapolitan Pizza Recipe

Now that we’ve explored the captivating history of Neapolitan pizza, it’s time to bring the flavors of Naples into your kitchen with an authentic recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams of “00” flour
  • 300 milliliters of water
  • 2 grams of fresh yeast
  • 25 grams of sea salt
  • 400 grams of San Marzano tomatoes
  • 200 grams of buffalo mozzarella
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in the water.
    • Gradually pour the yeast mixture into the flour and mix until a dough forms.
    • Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
    • Place the dough in a bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 6-8 hours.

  2. Shape the Pizza:

    • After the dough has risen, divide it into four equal parts and shape each portion into a ball.
    • Cover the dough balls with a damp cloth and let them rest for an additional 2 hours.

  3. Assemble the Pizza:

    • Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, ideally around 500°F (260°C).
    • On a floured surface, flatten each dough ball into a round pizza base, leaving a slightly thicker crust around the edges.
    • Place the San Marzano tomatoes on the dough and spread them evenly, leaving a border around the edges.
    • Tear the buffalo mozzarella into pieces and distribute it over the tomatoes.
    • Drizzle some extra virgin olive oil over the pizza and season with a pinch of salt.

  4. Bake the Pizza:

    • Transfer the pizza to a preheated pizza stone or baking sheet and bake for 90 seconds to 2 minutes, or until the crust is puffed and charred in spots.
    • Once the pizza is out of the oven, scatter fresh basil leaves over the top.

  5. Enjoy the Authentic Neapolitan Pizza:

    • Slice the pizza and savor the authentic flavors of Naples right in your home.

Final Thoughts

The authentic Neapolitan pizza is not just a dish; it’s a culinary journey that encapsulates the history, tradition, and artistry of Naples. By following this recipe, you can experience the time-honored flavors of Neapolitan pizza and share in its cultural significance. Whether you’re a pizza enthusiast or a cooking aficionado, mastering the art of Neapolitan pizza will undoubtedly elevate your culinary repertoire and transport you to the bustling streets of Naples with every bite. Buon appetito!
