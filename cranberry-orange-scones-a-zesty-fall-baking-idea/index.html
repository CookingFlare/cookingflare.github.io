Cranberry Orange Scones: A Zesty Fall Baking Idea


As the leaves start to turn shades of crimson and gold and the air becomes crisp, it’s time to embrace the flavors of fall. One delightful way to usher in the season is by baking a batch of Cranberry Orange Scones. These tender, buttery scones are bursting with the tangy sweetness of cranberries and the bright, citrusy zing of oranges, making them the perfect treat for cozy autumn mornings or afternoon tea. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create these delightful confections in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter cubes to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gently stir in the dried cranberries and orange zest, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, egg, and vanilla extract. Pour this wet mixture into the dry ingredients and gently stir until the dough starts to come together.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it a few times until it holds together. Pat the dough into a circle about 1 inch thick.

  6. Use a sharp knife to cut the dough circle into 8 wedges. Transfer the scones to the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each one.

  7. Brush the tops of the scones with a little extra cream and sprinkle with a touch of sugar for a golden, sweet crust.

  8. Bake the scones for 15-18 minutes, or until they are golden brown and baked through. Transfer them to a wire rack to cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These Cranberry Orange Scones are delightful on their own, but you can take them to the next level by serving them with a dollop of clotted cream and a spoonful of tangy orange marmalade. Pair them with a steaming cup of Earl Grey tea for a truly luxurious treat.

Storage

If you have any scones left over (a rare occurrence!), you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. For longer storage, wrap them tightly and freeze for up to one month. Simply thaw at room temperature or reheat in a low oven before enjoying.

Final Thoughts

Baking a batch of Cranberry Orange Scones is a wonderful way to embrace the flavors of fall and add a touch of warmth to your kitchen. The combination of tart cranberries and zesty orange is a match made in culinary heaven, and these scones are sure to become a seasonal favorite. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let the aroma of these delightful treats fill your home with the comforting essence of autumn. Happy baking!
