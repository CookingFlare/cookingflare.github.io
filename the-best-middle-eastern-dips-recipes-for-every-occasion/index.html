The Best Middle Eastern Dips: Recipes for Every Occasion


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its bold flavors, rich heritage, and traditions that celebrate communal dining. Among its most cherished contributions to global cuisine are its dips—vibrant, versatile, and brimming with flavor. Perfect as appetizers, side dishes, or even the main event in a spread, Middle Eastern dips offer something for every palate. From creamy classics to zesty innovations, we’ve compiled a collection of the best Middle Eastern dips that will elevate any dining occasion.

Hummus: The Timeless Classic

Hummus is, without a doubt, the crown jewel of Middle Eastern dips. With its creamy texture and garlicky undertones, it pairs beautifully with warm pita bread, fresh veggies, or even as a decadent spread on sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup tahini (sesame seed paste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Paprika and more olive oil for garnishing

Method:

  1. Blend the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin in a food processor.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while blending, achieving that silky, creamy consistency.
  3. Adjust seasoning with salt to taste.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle paprika on top, and serve.

Hummus is endlessly customizable. Add roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, or even caramelized onions to create unique versions suited to your taste.

Baba Ghanoush: Smoky and Sublime

Baba Ghanoush is a bold and smoky eggplant dip that is both indulgent and surprisingly healthy. It’s perfect for those who crave roasted flavors with a tangy twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley and pomegranate seeds for garnishing

Method:

  1. Preheat your grill or oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pierce the eggplant with a fork in several places and roast until soft with charred skin (about 30-40 minutes).
  3. Allow the eggplant to cool, then peel off the charred skin.
  4. In a food processor, blend the roasted eggplant with tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then garnish with parsley and pomegranate seeds.

Serve Baba Ghanoush alongside pita chips or vegetable sticks for an irresistible smoky bite.

Labneh: Creamy Yogurt Perfection

Labneh, a strained yogurt dip, is a refreshing addition to any table. Its tangy creaminess makes it a crowd favorite, especially when paired with soft, fresh flatbreads.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups full-fat Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zaatar spice for garnishing

Method:

  1. Line a colander with a cheesecloth and place it over a bowl.
  2. Mix the Greek yogurt with salt, then pour it into the cheesecloth.
  3. Allow the yogurt to strain overnight in the refrigerator to achieve a thick, rich consistency.
  4. Transfer the labneh to a bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with zaatar.

For added excitement, you can mix in crushed garlic, chopped herbs, or diced cucumber before serving.

Muhammara: The Bold and Nutty Option

Muhammara is a lesser-known Middle Eastern dip that deserves the spotlight. Made from roasted red peppers and walnuts, it’s packed with spicy, nutty, and slightly sweet flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and red pepper flakes to taste

Method:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted peppers, walnuts, garlic, breadcrumbs, cumin, pomegranate molasses, and olive oil.
  2. Blend until smooth, then season with salt and red pepper flakes to taste.
  3. Serve Muhammara with fresh bread or crackers.

This dip is an excellent accompaniment to grilled meats or as an enriching condiment in sandwiches.

Toum: A Garlicky Dream

Toum is a fluffy Middle Eastern garlic sauce loved for its pungent garlic flavor and creamy consistency. It’s a must-try for garlic enthusiasts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peeled garlic cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups neutral oil (like canola or grapeseed oil)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons cold water

Method:

  1. In a food processor, process the garlic with salt until finely minced.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the oil while the processor runs, alternating with small amounts of lemon juice and water to emulsify.
  3. Continue blending until the mixture is thick and fluffy.

Toum pairs fabulously with grilled kebabs, shawarma wraps, or even as a dip for fries.

Tzatziki: Refreshing Elegance

Although often associated with Greek cuisine, versions of Tzatziki are also enjoyed throughout the Middle East. This cucumber and yogurt dip is light, cooling, and versatile.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, finely grated and drained
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, garlic, dill, olive oil, and lemon juice in a bowl.
  2. Mix well and season with salt to taste.
  3. Chill for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Tzatziki is an excellent accompaniment to grilled veggies, falafel, or lamb dishes.

Foul Mudammas: Heartiness in a Dip

Foul Mudammas, made from hearty fava beans, is a protein-rich dish that doubles as a dip. Traditionally served for breakfast, it’s also great for any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned fava beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped parsley and tomatoes for garnishing

Method:

  1. In a small pot, heat the fava beans with a splash of water until warmed through.
  2. Mash the beans slightly with a fork, leaving some texture.
  3. Stir in the garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and cumin.
  4. Season with salt and garnish with parsley and diced tomatoes.

Serve Foul Mudammas with warm flatbreads for a comforting treat.

Elevate Your Spread with Middle Eastern Dips

Middle Eastern dips are not just recipes—they’re an invitation to gather, share, and celebrate flavors rooted in tradition. Each dip brings its own unique character to the table, whether it’s the creaminess of hummus or the bold smokiness of Baba Ghanoush. With these recipes in hand, you’re ready to create a delicious spread suitable for any occasion, from casual get-togethers to elegant dinner parties.

Experiment with these recipes, add your twists, and enjoy the timeless joy of Middle Eastern cuisine. Your taste buds—and your guests—will thank you!
