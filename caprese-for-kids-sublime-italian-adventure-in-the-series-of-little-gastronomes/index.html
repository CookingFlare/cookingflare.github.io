Caprese' for Kids: Sublime Italian Adventure in the Series of Little Gastronomes


Welcome to our little gastronomical journey, where we get to embrace the tantalizing world of Italian cuisine with our kids! If you’re looking for a fun meal that not only packs a flavor punch but also comes with an educational twist, why not introduce your children to the exquisite Caprese?

Caprese is an elegant and refreshing salad that’s simple yet versatile enough to be enjoyed by people of all ages. Making it with kids is a brilliant way to introduce them to the delightful tastes of Italy while teaching them about various culinary techniques. In this blog post, we’ll share how to create your Caprese adventure with kids as the dedicated little gastronomes!

The Classic Caprese:

The classic Caprese is composed of slices of fresh mozzarella cheese, ripe tomatoes, and basil leaves. These ingredients are often drizzled with a touch of extra virgin olive oil, seasoned with salt and pepper, and topped with a balsamic glaze.

To make it more visually appealing and appetizing for kids, try the following steps:

Step 1: Gathering Ingredients

First, let’s get all the items you’ll need. Ask your kids to help you gather the following ingredients:

  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Ripe tomatoes
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper

Don’t forget to let them pick the ripest, reddest tomatoes and the freshest basil leaves for that extra sensory learning experience!

Step 2: Preparing the Caprese

Now, for the fun part - let the kids assemble the Caprese! For younger kids, you might want to cut and slice the tomatoes and mozzarella into bite-sized pieces, ensuring that they’re safe while handling the knife. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

a. Make Layers:

Have them start by laying down a slice of tomato on a plate. This will be the base of their Caprese.

b. Add Mozzarella:

Next, place a slice of mozzarella cheese on top of the tomato. Make sure they press it gently onto the tomato slice so that it sticks.

c. Sprinkle the Basil:

Incorporate a vibrant pop of color and flavor by adding a couple of fresh basil leaves on top of the mozzarella. Encourage them to smell the basil leaves, as it’s an excellent opportunity to teach them about the various smells found in nature.

d. Drizzle the Olive Oil:

Now that everything is in place, it’s time to drizzle some extra virgin olive oil. Give your kids a small bottle or a spoon to practice their pouring skills!

e. Add Balsamic Glaze:

To complete this Italian masterpiece, apply a balsamic glaze over the basil leaves using a teaspoon or pipette for the youngest chefs.

Step 3: Season and Taste:

Now that the Caprese is ready, encourage your kids to season the dish with a pinch of salt and pepper to their preference. This makes them feel involved in the process and confident in their culinary skills.

Step 4: Time to Enjoy!

The final step is to sit back, relax, and enjoy the fruit of their labor! Ask your kids questions about their experience and encourage them to share their thoughts on the taste, texture, and appearance of their Caprese.

This delightful adventure not only cultivates an appreciation for Italian cuisine but also equips your little ones with essential culinary skills. Now that you’ve embarked on this gastronomical adventure with Caprese, you can continue taking them through other fun Italian dishes, such as Spaghetti alla Carbonara or Risotto Milanese. Who knows? You might just have a future Masterchef or Michelin-starred chef on your hands. Buon appetito, and happy cooking!
