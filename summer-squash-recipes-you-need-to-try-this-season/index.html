Summer Squash Recipes You Need to Try This Season


Summer squash is a vibrant, versatile vegetable that often shines in seasonal cooking. Lightweight, mild, and full of natural sweetness, it’s a favorite ingredient for everything from quick snacks to hearty meals. Whether you’re a home cook looking to shake up your routine or simply want to celebrate the flavors of the season, summer squash brings color, texture, and nutrition to the table.

Let’s dive into some delicious recipes you can whip up with summer squash, exploring inventive ways to incorporate this vegetable into lunches, dinners, and even snacks. From zesty grilled dishes to comforting casseroles and fresh salads, these recipes bring out the best of summer squash without overwhelming its delicate flavor.

1. Grilled Summer Squash Medley with Garlic Herb Dressing

Grilling summer squash intensifies its natural sweetness and adds a delightful smoky note. This simple yet flavorful medley is perfect as a side dish or a light main course.

Ingredients:

  • 3 summer squash (zucchini, yellow squash, or pattypan), sliced lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Juice of half a lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the summer squash slices with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the slices for about 3-4 minutes per side, until tender and slightly charred.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, parsley, thyme, lemon juice, and a bit of olive oil.
  5. Drizzle the garlic herb dressing over the grilled squash and serve warm.

Tip: Add some crumbled feta or shaved Parmesan on top for a creamy contrast.

2. Summer Squash and Tomato Gratin

This layered gratin combines tender summer squash and ripe tomatoes, baked with a crispy topping that brings out the best of summer produce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium summer squash, thinly sliced
  • 3 medium tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 cup grated Gruyere or mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a baking dish, arrange alternating layers of squash and tomatoes.
  3. Sprinkle minced garlic evenly and season with salt and pepper between layers.
  4. Drizzle olive oil over the top.
  5. Mix the panko breadcrumbs with the grated cheese, then sprinkle over the vegetables.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until the top is golden brown and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

This gratin pairs wonderfully with grilled chicken or fish for a refined summer dinner.

3. Summer Squash Noodles with Lemon Basil Pesto

Spiralizing summer squash is a fantastic low-carb alternative to pasta. Toss it with a fresh lemon basil pesto for a zesty and refreshing meal.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large summer squashes (zucchini or yellow squash), spiralized
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts or walnuts
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Zest and juice of one lemon
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend basil, nuts, garlic, lemon zest, and juice until finely chopped.
  2. Slowly add olive oil while the processor runs until a smooth pesto forms.
  3. Toss summer squash noodles with the lemon pesto, ensuring an even coating.
  4. Add grated Parmesan and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with extra nuts or Parmesan if desired.

Pro Tip: Lightly sautéing the squash noodles for 1-2 minutes keeps them tender but still crisp.

4. Stuffed Summer Squash Boats

Hollowed summer squash halves provide a perfect vessel for stuffing with a hearty mix of grains, vegetables, and cheese. This recipe makes for an attractive and substantial vegetarian entrée.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium summer squash (zucchini or pattypan)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or couscous
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/3 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds, creating boats.
  3. Lightly brush the inside with olive oil and place on a baking sheet.
  4. In a skillet, sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until softened.
  5. Add cherry tomatoes, cooked quinoa, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for 3-4 more minutes.
  6. Stir in half of the feta cheese.
  7. Fill each squash boat with the quinoa mixture, then sprinkle remaining feta on top.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the squash is tender and the filling is golden.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Stuffed squash boats are excellent served with a crisp green salad or roasted vegetables.

5. Summer Squash Fritters with Yogurt Dill Sauce

Crunchy on the outside and soft within, summer squash fritters are an irresistible appetizer or snack. The cool, tangy dill sauce complements their crispy texture perfectly.

Ingredients:

Fritters:

  • 2 large summer squash, grated
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (or chickpea flour for gluten-free)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Squeeze excess water from grated squash using a clean towel.
  2. In a large bowl, combine squash, flour, Parmesan, eggs, green onions, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Spoon batter into the pan, flattening slightly to form fritters.
  5. Cook for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and crisp.
  6. Mix yogurt, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to make the sauce.
  7. Serve fritters hot with a dollop of dill yogurt sauce.

These fritters work beautifully as finger food at summer gatherings or a quick weeknight treat.

6. Summer Squash and Chickpea Stew

A warming stew packed with summer squash, chickpeas, and tomatoes, this dish is both nutritious and comforting. Its subtle spices highlight the freshness of the squash.

Ingredients:

  • 3 summer squash, chopped
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add cumin and smoked paprika, toasting briefly until fragrant.
  4. Add summer squash, chickpeas, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth.
  5. Bring stew to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes, until squash is tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with crusty bread or over rice.

This stew is perfect for a cozy evening or for batch cooking to enjoy throughout the week.

7. Summer Squash and Corn Tacos with Avocado Salsa

Take your taco night to fresh heights with a colorful mix of sautéed summer squash and sweet corn, topped with vibrant avocado salsa.

Ingredients:

Tacos:

  • 2 summer squash, diced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Small corn or flour tortillas

Avocado Salsa:

  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add summer squash and corn, seasoning with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook for 6-8 minutes until vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. In a bowl, gently combine avocado, tomatoes, red onion, lime juice, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  5. Warm tortillas on a griddle or in a pan.
  6. Fill each tortilla with the summer squash and corn mixture, then top with avocado salsa.
  7. Serve with lime wedges.

The mix of warm, seasoned veggies and cool, creamy avocado makes these tacos an instant hit.

Conclusion

Summer squash lends itself to an inspiring range of dishes that are light, fresh, and satisfying. From rustic grills and comforting bakes to crisp salads and flavorful stews, the options are endless. By incorporating herbs, spices, and complementary vegetables, you can elevate the simplicity of summer squash and create dishes worthy of every season’s table.

Give these recipes a try to experience the delightful textures and subtle sweetness that only summer squash can bring. With vibrant colors and healthful benefits, summer squash is your secret ingredient for cooking up memorable meals this season and beyond. Happy cooking!
