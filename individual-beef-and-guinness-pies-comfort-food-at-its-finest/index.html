Individual Beef and Guinness Pies: Comfort Food at its Finest


There’s something incredibly satisfying about digging into a warm, flaky individual pie, filled with succulent beef and rich Guinness gravy. These Individual Beef and Guinness Pies are the epitome of comfort food, perfect for cozy nights in or for impressing guests at a dinner party. The combination of tender beef, hearty Guinness, and buttery pastry creates a dish that is truly irresistible.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 cup (250ml) Guinness stout
  • 1 cup (250ml) beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf

For the pastry:

  • 2 sheets of store-bought puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large bowl, coat the beef stew meat with flour, salt, and pepper. Make sure each piece is well coated.

  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.

  4. In the same skillet, add the diced onion, minced garlic, carrot, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are softened and the onion is translucent.

  5. Return the beef to the skillet and add the Guinness stout, beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, and bay leaf. Stir well to combine.

  6. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it cook for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  7. While the filling is simmering, prepare the pastry. Roll out the thawed puff pastry sheets on a lightly floured surface until they are about 1/8 inch (3mm) thick. Using a round cutter, cut out circles that are slightly larger than the top of your individual pie dishes.

  8. Once the filling is ready, remove the bay leaf and divide the mixture evenly among your individual pie dishes.

  9. Brush the edges of the pie dishes with a little water, then place the pastry circles on top, pressing the edges to seal. Cut a small slit in the center of each pie to allow steam to escape.

  10. Brush the pastry with beaten egg wash, which will give it a beautiful golden color when baked.

  11. Place the pies on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is puffed up and golden brown.

  12. Remove the pies from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. These pies are best enjoyed warm, with a side of creamy mashed potatoes and steamed greens.

Conclusion

Individual Beef and Guinness Pies are the ultimate comfort food. The tender beef, combined with the rich and robust flavors of Guinness, create a pie that is truly a delight to eat. Whether you’re looking for a hearty weeknight dinner or a dish to impress your guests, these pies are sure to satisfy. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy a taste of comfort food at its finest.
