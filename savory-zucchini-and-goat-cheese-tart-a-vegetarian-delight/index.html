Savory Zucchini and Goat Cheese Tart: A Vegetarian Delight


As the summer months approach, our gardens and markets are filled with an abundance of fresh zucchini. This versatile and nutritious vegetable can be used in a variety of dishes, from savory tarts to sweet breads. In this recipe, we’ll explore the combination of zucchini and goat cheese in a savory tart that’s perfect for a light and refreshing meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

Make the Pastry Crust

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and cold butter. Use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced zucchinis and cook, stirring occasionally, until they’re tender and lightly browned, about 10-12 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assemble the Tart

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the excess dough and press the dough into the corners of the pan.

In a small bowl, whisk together the goat cheese, Parmesan cheese, and eggs. Spread the cheese mixture evenly over the tart crust, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.

Arrange the cooked zucchini slices on top of the cheese mixture, overlapping them slightly to cover the entire surface.

Bake the Tart

Bake the tart for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 10-15 minutes.

Serve and Enjoy

Slice the tart into wedges and serve warm or at room temperature. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired. This savory tart is perfect for a light lunch or dinner, and it’s also a great side dish for a summer barbecue or picnic.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor to the tart, sprinkle some chopped fresh herbs, such as thyme or rosemary, over the cheese mixture before baking.
  • If you prefer a crisper crust, bake the tart for an additional 5-10 minutes. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning.
  • This tart is also delicious with other types of cheese, such as feta or ricotta. Feel free to experiment and find your favorite combination.

With its flaky pastry crust, creamy cheese filling, and tender zucchini, this savory tart is a perfect way to enjoy the flavors of summer. So go ahead, give it a try, and indulge in the deliciousness!
