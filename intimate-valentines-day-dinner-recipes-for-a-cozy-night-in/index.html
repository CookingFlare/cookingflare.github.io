Intimate Valentine's Day Dinner Recipes for a Cozy Night In


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and you’re probably thinking of ways to make it a special and romantic evening with your loved one. Instead of going out to a crowded restaurant, why not cook a delicious and intimate dinner at home? In this post, we’ll share some romantic Valentine’s Day dinner recipes that will make your evening truly unforgettable.

Appetizer: Oysters Rockefeller Start your evening with a luxurious and decadent appetizer - Oysters Rockefeller. This classic dish is a staple of fine dining, and with our simple recipe, you can recreate it at home.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oysters
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup of Pernod or other anise-flavored liqueur

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Shuck the oysters and place them on a baking sheet lined with rock salt.
  3. Dot the top of each oyster with butter, then sprinkle with garlic, spinach, and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Bake the oysters in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and the tops are golden brown.
  5. Remove the oysters from the oven and drizzle with Pernod.
  6. Serve immediately.

Main Course: Filet Mignon with Red Wine Reduction For the main course, we recommend a tender and juicy filet mignon, served with a rich and flavorful red wine reduction. This dish is sure to impress your loved one and satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 4 filet mignon steaks, 6 ounces each
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the filet mignon steaks with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the filet mignon steaks for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they’re browned.
  4. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake the filet mignon steaks for 8-10 minutes, or until they’re cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  5. While the filet mignon steaks are cooking, prepare the red wine reduction. In a small saucepan, combine the red wine, beef broth, and butter. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  6. Stir in the Dijon mustard and season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the filet mignon steaks with the red wine reduction spooned over the top. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves.

Side Dish: Roasted Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan For a delicious and healthy side dish, we recommend roasted asparagus with lemon and Parmesan. This dish is easy to make and adds a burst of flavor to your Valentine’s Day dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Place the asparagus spears on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the asparagus, then sprinkle with garlic, lemon juice, and Parmesan cheese.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Roast the asparagus in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until it’s tender and slightly caramelized.
  7. Remove the asparagus from the oven and garnish with fresh parsley leaves. Serve hot.

Dessert: Chocolate Lava Cake For dessert, we recommend a rich and decadent chocolate lava cake. This classic dessert is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and impress your loved one.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of high-quality dark chocolate, broken into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Fresh raspberries or strawberries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the melted butter to the milk mixture and whisk until smooth.
  5. Pour the milk mixture into the flour mixture and whisk until just combined.
  6. Melt the chocolate in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  7. Pour the chocolate into the batter and whisk until just combined.
  8. Pour the batter into 4 (6-ounce) ramekins or small cups.
  9. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  10. Bake the cakes in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are set and the centers are still slightly jiggly.
  11. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the ramekins for 1-2 minutes.
  12. Run a knife around the edges of each cake to release it from the ramekin.
  13. Invert the cakes onto plates and garnish with fresh raspberries or strawberries. Serve immediately.

Conclusion With these romantic Valentine’s Day dinner recipes, you’ll be able to create a truly unforgettable evening with your loved one. From the luxurious Oysters Rockefeller appetizer to the decadent Chocolate Lava Cake dessert, each dish is sure to impress and satisfy your taste buds. So why not stay in and cook a delicious dinner at home this Valentine’s Day? With a little creativity and some simple recipes, you can create a romantic and memorable evening that you’ll both cherish forever.
