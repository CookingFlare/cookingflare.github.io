Easy Does It: Beginner's Oatmeal Raisin Cookie Recipe


As the aroma of freshly baked cookies wafts through the air, it’s hard not to feel a sense of warmth and comfort. Oatmeal raisin cookies, in particular, evoke a sense of nostalgia and familiarity, reminding us of childhood memories and cozy afternoons spent baking with loved ones. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating the perfect oatmeal raisin cookie, simple enough for beginners to master yet delicious enough to satisfy even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prep

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat to ensure easy cleanup and prevent cookies from sticking.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, oats, baking soda, and salt. Set aside for later use.

Step 3: Cream the Butter and Sugars

In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to cream together the butter and sugars until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes.

Step 4: Add the Eggs and Vanilla

Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Beat in the vanilla extract until fully incorporated.

Step 5: Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Do not overmix.

Step 6: Stir in the Raisins

Add the raisins to the dough and stir until they’re evenly distributed throughout.

Step 7: Scoop and Bake

Use a cookie scoop or spoon to drop rounded balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 2 inches of space between each cookie. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.

Step 8: Cool and Enjoy

Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days.

Tips and Variations

  • For a chewier cookie, bake for 8-10 minutes. For a crisper cookie, bake for 12-14 minutes.
  • Substitute chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or dried cranberries for the raisins to create unique flavor combinations.
  • Use old-fashioned oats for a slightly denser cookie or quick-cooking oats for a lighter texture.
  • Add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg for an extra boost of flavor.

Conclusion

With this easy-to-follow recipe, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious oatmeal raisin cookies that are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is the perfect starting point for exploring the world of cookie baking. So go ahead, get baking, and indulge in the warm, comforting aroma of freshly baked cookies wafting from your oven.
