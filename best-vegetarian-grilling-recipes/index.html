Best Vegetarian Grilling Recipes


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the outdoors. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or just cooking for a small group, incorporating vegetarian options into your grilling lineup can create a well-rounded and satisfying meal. Here are some of our favorite vegetarian grilling recipes that will delight your taste buds and impress your guests.

1. Portobello Mushroom Burgers

This delicious and hearty Portobello Mushroom Burger is a surefire crowd-pleaser. The rich, savory flavor of the mushroom pairs perfectly with a variety of condiments like guacamole, thousand island dressing, or a simple balsamic vinaigrette. To make, simply brush both sides of the mushroom cap with olive oil and grill for about 5-7 minutes over medium-high heat. Then top with your favorite fixings and enjoy!

2. Grilled Eggplant “Steaks”

Eggplant can easily be confused with meat when grilled. To prepare these Grilled Eggplant “Steaks,” slice the eggplant into 1-inch thick rounds, salt lightly, and let sit for 15-30 minutes to release excess moisture. Then, brush both sides with a flavorful marinade consisting of olive oil, garlic, and your choice of herbs (such as rosemary or thyme). Grill for about 6-8 minutes on each side until tender and slightly charred.

3. Halloumi and Vegetable Skewers

Halloumi is a semi-hard, Greek brined cheese that has a high melting point, making it perfect for grilling. To make Halloumi and Vegetable Skewers, thread the halloumi slices and colorful vegetables such as bell peppers, onions, and cherry tomatoes onto skewers. Brush them with olive oil and grill until the cheese is slightly browned and the vegetables are tender. These tasty skewers can easily be adjusted to include your favorite vegetables for a customizable treat.

4. Grilled Parmesan Potatoes

There’s nothing quite like crispy, grilled potatoes. Slice the potatoes into 1/4-inch thick rounds and place them on a baking sheet. Toss them with a mixture of olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Let them marinate for at least an hour and up to 4 hours. Preheat your grill and toss the potatoes in additional olive oil before grilling. Place the potatoes on the hot grate and use a reserved potato slice or bean to press them into the grill. This method will give the potatoes unique grill marks and maximize flavor.

5. Grilled Pineapple

Fruit is an unexpected and refreshing addition to any grilled menu. Simply slice a ripe pineapple into rounds and brush them with a mixture of brown sugar and melted butter. Place the pineapple slices on the grill and cook for around 5-7 minutes on each side, or until caramelized. Serve these slightly sweet Grilled Pineapple slices as a side dish or topping.

Starting your summer grilling with vegetarian options not only accommodates dietary restrictions but also provides a delicious and exciting variation to your classic grilling lineup. These vegetarian grilling recipes will elevate your barbecue game and ensure a memorable meal for you and your guests. So fire up that grill and enjoy a meatless grilling experience!
