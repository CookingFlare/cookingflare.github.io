Fermented Pickled Corn: A Unique Twist on a Summer Favorite


Summer is here, and with it comes an abundance of fresh corn on the cob. While traditional grilled or boiled corn is always a crowd-pleaser, why not try something new and exciting? Fermented pickled corn is a game-changer, and we’re excited to share this unique twist on a summer favorite.

What is Fermented Pickled Corn?

Fermented pickled corn, also known as kimchi corn, is a Korean-inspired dish that combines the sweetness of corn with the tanginess of fermented vegetables. The process of fermentation breaks down the natural sugars in the corn, creating a rich, umami flavor that’s both addictive and refreshing. This dish is perfect for hot summer days when a light, cool snack is just what you need.

The Benefits of Fermentation

Fermentation is a natural process that not only adds flavor to your food but also preserves it. By fermenting the corn, you’re creating a probiotic-rich snack that’s packed with vitamins and minerals. Fermentation also helps to break down the starches in the corn, making it easier to digest. Plus, the lactic acid produced during fermentation acts as a natural preservative, allowing you to store the pickled corn in the fridge for weeks to come.

The Recipe

To make fermented pickled corn, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked and silked
  • 1/4 cup of Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 cup of chopped green onions for garnish
  • 1/4 cup of toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the corn off the cob and place it in a large bowl. Sprinkle the Korean chili flakes, brown sugar, rice vinegar, water, salt, and black pepper over the corn. Mix well to combine.
  2. Pack the corn mixture into a clean glass jar, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top. Press down on the corn to remove any air pockets.
  3. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit at room temperature (around 70-75°F) for 3-5 days. You’ll start to see bubbles forming and the corn will begin to soften.
  4. After 3-5 days, transfer the jar to the fridge to slow down the fermentation process. Let it sit for at least 2 weeks before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • To speed up the fermentation process, you can add a small amount of active dry yeast to the mixture.
  • If you prefer a milder flavor, you can reduce the amount of Korean chili flakes or omit them altogether.
  • To add some extra flavor, you can add 1-2 cloves of minced garlic or 1 tablespoon of grated ginger to the mixture.
  • Fermented pickled corn is a great addition to salads, sandwiches, and soups. Try using it as a topping for your favorite tacos or grilled meats.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled corn is a game-changing snack that’s perfect for hot summer days. With its unique combination of sweet and tangy flavors, it’s sure to become a new favorite. By following this simple recipe, you’ll be enjoying a delicious and healthy snack in no time. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
