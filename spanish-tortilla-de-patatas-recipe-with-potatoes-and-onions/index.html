Spanish Tortilla de Patatas Recipe with Potatoes and Onions


If you’re looking for a delicious and authentic Spanish dish to impress your friends and family, look no further than the classic Spanish Tortilla de Patatas. This simple yet flavorful dish is a staple in Spanish cuisine and is loved by people all over the world. Made with just a few basic ingredients, it’s easy to make and incredibly satisfying to eat. So let’s dive right in and learn how to make this traditional Spanish delicacy!

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 6 large eggs
  • Salt, to taste
  • Olive oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the potatoes and slicing them into thin rounds. You want them to be about 1/4 inch thick. Peel and thinly slice the onion as well.

  2. Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onions to the pan, making sure they are well coated in oil. Sprinkle some salt over the potatoes and onions and stir gently to combine. Cook them slowly, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and lightly golden brown. This can take about 15-20 minutes.

  3. While the potatoes and onions are cooking, crack the eggs into a large bowl and beat them lightly. Season the eggs with a pinch of salt.

  4. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, remove them from the pan using a slotted spoon, allowing any excess oil to drain back into the pan. Transfer the potatoes and onions to the bowl with the beaten eggs and gently mix everything together.

  5. Clean the frying pan and heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-low heat. Pour the potato and egg mixture into the pan, spreading it out evenly. Cook the tortilla for about 5 minutes, or until the edges start to set.

  6. Now comes the tricky part - flipping the tortilla! Take a large plate that’s slightly larger than the frying pan and place it upside down on top of the pan. Using oven mitts or a kitchen towel to protect your hands, carefully flip the pan over so that the tortilla lands on the plate. The uncooked side of the tortilla should now be facing down.

  7. Slide the tortilla back into the pan and continue cooking for another 4-5 minutes, or until the center is set and the tortilla is golden brown on both sides. You can adjust the cooking time depending on how runny or firm you like the center of your tortilla to be.

  8. Once the tortilla is cooked to your liking, remove it from the pan and let it cool slightly. Cut it into wedges or squares and serve warm or at room temperature. It’s perfect for brunch, lunch, or even as a tapas dish for a party.

Conclusion

The Spanish Tortilla de Patatas is a true classic that embodies the essence of Spanish cuisine. With its simple yet satisfying combination of potatoes, onions, and eggs, it’s a dish that can be enjoyed any time of the day. Whether you’re serving it as a main course, a side dish, or even as part of a tapas spread, this traditional Spanish delicacy is sure to impress. So why not give it a try and transport yourself to the sunny streets of Spain with every bite? ¡Buen provecho!
