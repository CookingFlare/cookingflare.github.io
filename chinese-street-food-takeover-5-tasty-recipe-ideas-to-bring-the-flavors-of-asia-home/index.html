Chinese Street Food Takeover: 5 Tasty Recipe Ideas to Bring the Flavors of Asia Home


Chinese street food is an explosion of flavors, colors, textures, and aromas. No trip to Asia can go by without indulging in the local street food scene. With scorching hot stir-fries, steaming bowls of noodles, and crisp veggies, Chinese street food is famous all around the world. Here are five delicious recipe ideas to bring that essence of Asia to your kitchen to whip up an authentic Chinese Street Food feast at home.

1. Crispy Five-Spice Pork Belly

Our first recipe is a juicy and tender piece of pork belly seasoned with a harmonious blend of Chinese five-spice powder. The unique combination of star anise, cloves, cinnamon, Sichuan pepper, and fennel seeds delivers a mouth-watering aroma and distinct flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1kg pork belly
  • 2 tbsp five-spice powder
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 230°C.
  2. Mix the five-spice powder, soy sauce, salt, ginger, and garlic in a roasting pan.
  3. Add the pork belly to the pan, making sure every cut is well-coated in the seasoning mix.
  4. Drizzle the cooking oil on top and cover the pan with aluminum foil.
  5. Roast in the oven for one hour. Then turn the heat down to 180°C and roast for an additional 30 minutes until crisp and golden brown.

2. Szechuan Dan Dan Noodles

These spicy, numbing, and tangy noodles hail from Sichuan Province and are traditionally served by carrying them in a bowl from the kitchen to the dining table. The sour pickled cabbage, peanut sauce, and chili oil make this dish the ultimate wake-up call for your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 200g noodles
  • 100g pickled cabbage
  • 2 tbsp peanut sauce
  • 2 tbsp chili oil
  • 1 tbsp sesame paste
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
  • 100g boiled and crumbled Chinese sausage
  • 50g chopped green onions
  • 2 tbsp crushed peanuts

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions, drain, and rinse with cold water.
  2. In a bowl, mix the peanut sauce, sesame paste, and chili oil.
  3. In another skillet, heat up some cooking oil and add the Sichuan peppercorns to infuse the oil.
  4. Toss in the noodles, pickled cabbage, Chinese sausage, and green onions. Stir-Fry and mix well with the seasonings.
  5. Finally, mix in the crushed peanuts for a delightful crunch.

3. Miso Glazed Eggplant

This grill-friendly Asian street food-inspired dish brings together the rich, salty, umami flavor of the miso with the smoky flavor of the charcoal grill – a match made in Flavor Heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large Japanese eggplants
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onion
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Mix the miso paste, mirin, sake, and brown sugar together in a bowl to create a marinade.
  2. Cut the eggplants into long diagonal slices, and toss them in the marinade. Make sure each slice is well-coated.
  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the eggplants for 3 minutes per side, or until they’re tender and slightly charred.
  5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving.

4. Pan-Fried Cumin Lamb

Cumin lamb is a patronized street food throughout China that perfectly balances the luscious, tender meat with the fragrant Indian ammouting taste of cumin – an aromatic and savory delight.

Ingredients:

  • 500g lamb, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh ginger
  • 1 tsp Chinese five-spice powder
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp dried red chilies flakes
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of sunflower oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the cumin seeds, garlic, ginger, five-spice powder, ground cumin, and dried red chilies flakes, sautéing until aromatic, around 2-3 minutes.
  3. Increase the heat to high and add the lamb to the skillet.
  4. Cook the lamb, stirring occasionally, until browned on the outside and cooked through – about 6-7 minutes.
  5. Drizzle in the soy sauce and season with salt to taste before serving.

5. Shengjianbao (Pan-Fried Pork Buns)

These delectable, juicy pork-filled buns have a delightful crispy crust that gives way to a soft, fluffy inside packed with exhilarating, savory Pork Soup Dumpling filling. A famous Chinese street food, Shengjianbao takes a unique approach by frying the buns to give them a crunchy outer layer.

Ingredients:

For the Pork filling:

  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped scallions
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt and white pepper to taste

For the Buns:

  • 300g all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Start by preparing the filling: Mix all the filling ingredients in a bowl and set aside for at least 30 minutes for the flavors to meld.
  2. For the dough, mix the warm water, yeast, sugar, and oil in a bowl. Let it sit until it becomes frothy – around 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add the flour and salt to the yeast mixtures, and knead until you have a smooth, elastic dough. Knead for around 10 minutes, or until smooth.
  4. Cover the dough and leave it to rise in a warm spot for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, roll it into a long rope.
  6. Cut the rope into 5 equal pieces, and roll these out into round discs.
  7. Place 1 tbsp of the pork filling into the center of each disc and seal the edges, pleating to create a round, smooth bun.
  8. Heat the oil for frying in a wok or deep pot. To test the temperature, place a wooden paddle in the oil – it’s hot enough when small bubbles form around the paddle.
  9. Carefully place the buns into the hot oil, frying until golden brown. Turn them over to fry the other side until the same color.
  10. Remove from the oil and serve immediately with pickled vegetables for the ultimate Shengjianbao street food experience.

These five classic street-food-inspired recipes from China not only bring the taste of the bustling streets of Asia to your home but also provide an opportunity to recreate some of the most beloved cuisines in the world. So, go ahead and put on that chef’s hat, and have a ball cooking your way through the flavors of the East.
