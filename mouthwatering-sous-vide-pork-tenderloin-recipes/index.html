Mouthwatering Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin Recipes

4 min · 694 words

If you’re looking for a mouthwatering and tender pork dish, look no further than sous vide pork tenderloin. Sous vide cooking is a technique that involves sealing the food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method ensures that the pork tenderloin cooks evenly and retains its moisture, resulting in a succulent and flavorful dish.

Classic Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C).
  2. Season the pork tenderloin with salt and pepper on all sides.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the pork tenderloin until browned on all sides.
  4. Transfer the pork tenderloin to a vacuum-sealed bag and add minced garlic and a sprig of fresh rosemary.
  5. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or a zip-top bag with the water displacement method.
  6. Place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours.
  7. Once cooked, remove the pork tenderloin from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  8. Heat a clean skillet over high heat and sear the pork tenderloin for a minute on each side to achieve a golden crust.
  9. Let the pork tenderloin rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Honey Glazed Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C).
  2. Season the pork tenderloin with salt and pepper on all sides.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic.
  4. Transfer the pork tenderloin to a vacuum-sealed bag and pour the honey glaze mixture over it.
  5. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or a zip-top bag with the water displacement method.
  6. Place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours.
  7. Once cooked, remove the pork tenderloin from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  8. Preheat a grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat.
  9. Brush the pork tenderloin with the remaining honey glaze mixture and grill for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until nicely charred.
  10. Let the pork tenderloin rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Balsamic Glazed Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C).
  2. Season the pork tenderloin with salt and pepper on all sides.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, brown sugar, and dried thyme.
  4. Transfer the pork tenderloin to a vacuum-sealed bag and pour the balsamic glaze mixture over it.
  5. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or a zip-top bag with the water displacement method.
  6. Place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours.
  7. Once cooked, remove the pork tenderloin from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  8. Preheat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the pork tenderloin for a minute on each side to caramelize the glaze.
  9. Let the pork tenderloin rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork tenderloin is a game-changer when it comes to cooking pork. The precise temperature control ensures a perfectly cooked and juicy tenderloin every time. Whether you prefer a classic preparation, a honey glaze, or a balsamic glaze, these recipes will satisfy your taste buds and impress your guests. Give sous vide pork tenderloin a try and elevate your cooking to new heights!
