Mastering the Art of Middle Eastern Flatbreads: Saj, Laffa, and More


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its rich flavors, aromatic spices, and diverse array of breads. Among these, flatbreads hold a special place, offering a delightful combination of soft texture and subtle smokiness. In this guide, we’ll delve into the art of mastering Middle Eastern flatbreads, focusing on the traditional Saj and Laffa breads, as well as exploring some lesser-known variations.

Understanding the Basics

Saj Bread

Saj bread, also known as Markook or Shrak, is a paper-thin flatbread that’s cooked on a domed griddle called a saj. This traditional bread is popular across the Levant region, and its preparation involves stretching the dough thinly and then quickly cooking it on the hot surface of the saj. The result is a delicate, slightly charred bread that’s perfect for wrapping around grilled meats, fresh vegetables, and flavorful dips.

Laffa Bread

Laffa bread, on the other hand, hails from the Middle East and is characterized by its soft, chewy texture and slightly blistered appearance. It’s typically cooked in a tandoor oven, but can also be prepared on a stovetop or grill. Laffa is versatile and can be used for wrapping shawarma, falafel, or as a base for creating delectable flatbread pizzas.

The Art of Preparation

Saj Bread Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt together.
  2. Gradually add the warm water and olive oil, kneading the dough until it’s smooth and elastic.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into thin circles.
  4. Cook the circles on a hot saj or griddle for about 1-2 minutes on each side, until they puff up and develop a golden-brown color.

Laffa Bread Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups bread flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the sugar, yeast, and warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Knead the dough until it’s smooth and elastic, then let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  4. Divide the dough into balls and roll them out into thin rounds. Cook the rounds on a hot griddle or stovetop until they puff up and develop a light char.

Exploring Variations

Mana’eesh

Mana’eesh is a popular Levantine flatbread that’s often topped with a variety of ingredients such as za’atar, cheese, or ground meats. It’s an iconic street food that’s perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Barbari Bread

Barbari bread is a Persian flatbread that’s characterized by its elongated shape and dimpled surface. It’s often sprinkled with sesame seeds and makes a wonderful accompaniment to kebabs and stews.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of Middle Eastern flatbreads is a rewarding endeavor that opens up a world of culinary possibilities. Whether you’re savoring the delicate crunch of Saj bread or indulging in the pillowy softness of Laffa, these flatbreads are a testament to the rich and diverse tapestry of Middle Eastern cuisine. Experiment with different toppings, fillings, and cooking methods to truly make these flatbreads your own, and let your creativity soar in the kitchen as you embark on this flavorful journey.
