Szechuan Mapo Eggplant: A Spicy and Flavorful Delight


If you’re a fan of bold, spicy flavors, then Szechuan Mapo Eggplant is a dish that should definitely be on your radar. This classic Szechuan dish combines tender eggplant, savory ground pork, and a mouthwatering spicy sauce that will leave your taste buds tingling with delight. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting to explore the world of Szechuan cuisine, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy your craving for flavorful, spicy food.

Ingredients

For the Eggplant:

  • 2 large Chinese eggplants, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Mapo Sauce:

  • 200g ground pork
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Szechuan peppercorns, toasted and ground
  • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (Szechuan broad bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon chili bean paste
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Eggplant:

    • In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch. Toss the eggplant pieces in the mixture until well coated.
    • Heat vegetable oil in a wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the eggplant in batches and fry until golden brown and tender. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  2. Make the Mapo Sauce:

    • In the same wok or skillet, add a little more oil if needed and cook the ground pork until browned.
    • Add the minced garlic, ginger, and green onions, and cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
    • Stir in the Szechuan peppercorns, doubanjiang, and chili bean paste, and cook for another minute.
    • Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, sugar, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for a few minutes until slightly thickened.

  3. Combine and Serve:

    • Gently add the fried eggplant to the wok with the Mapo sauce, tossing to coat the eggplant evenly.
    • Once everything is well combined and heated through, transfer the Szechuan Mapo Eggplant to a serving dish and garnish with additional chopped green onions if desired.

Serving Suggestions

Szechuan Mapo Eggplant is best enjoyed with steamed white rice, which helps balance out the heat of the dish. The fluffy texture of the rice complements the rich, spicy flavors of the eggplant and pork, creating a perfectly satisfying meal.

Conclusion

With its vibrant flavors and spicy kick, Szechuan Mapo Eggplant is a dish that never fails to excite the palate. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to a special homemade meal, this recipe is a fantastic way to explore the bold and robust flavors of Szechuan cuisine.
