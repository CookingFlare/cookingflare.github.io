Delicious Thanksgiving Stuffed Bell Pepper Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gratitude, family, and of course, delicious food. One dish that perfectly captures the essence of Thanksgiving is stuffed bell peppers. These vibrant and flavorful peppers are not only a feast for the eyes but also for the taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering Thanksgiving stuffed bell pepper recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

1. Traditional Turkey Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground turkey until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the diced onion, celery, carrots, garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Stir in the cooked quinoa and remove from heat.
  6. Stuff each bell pepper with the turkey and quinoa mixture.
  7. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  8. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese on top.
  9. Bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

2. Vegetarian Quinoa and Mushroom Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup diced mushrooms (any variety)
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced zucchini
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the diced mushrooms, onion, zucchini, red bell pepper, and garlic until tender.
  4. Stir in the cooked quinoa, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stuff each bell pepper with the quinoa and mushroom mixture.
  6. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  7. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
  8. Bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

3. Southwest Style Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded pepper jack cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the diced onion, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, jalapeño pepper, garlic, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Stir in the cooked rice and black beans.
  6. Stuff each bell pepper with the beef and rice mixture.
  7. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  8. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and sprinkle shredded pepper jack cheese on top.
  9. Bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

These three delicious Thanksgiving stuffed bell pepper recipes are guaranteed to add a burst of flavor and color to your holiday table. Whether you prefer the traditional turkey stuffing, a vegetarian option with quinoa and mushrooms, or a Southwest-inspired filling, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So gather your loved ones, celebrate the season of gratitude, and savor these scrumptious stuffed bell peppers. Happy Thanksgiving!
