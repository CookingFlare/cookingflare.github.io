Satisfying Mediterranean Chickpea and Spinach Stew


A hearty, comforting bowl of Mediterranean Chickpea and Spinach Stew is the perfect remedy for a chilly evening. This mouthwatering stew is a delightful blend of tender chickpeas, rich flavorful tomatoes, boosted with aromatic herbs and spices, and finished with a burst of freshness from spinach. Let's learn how to prepare this delicious, nutritious, and satisfying dish that will keep you warm throughout the winter months. The Key Ingredients Before we dive into the cooking process, let's first review the essential ingredients you will need for this scrumptious stew: Produce

1 large onion, diced 3 cloves of garlic, minced 2 medium tomatoes, diced 2 cups of fresh spinach leaves 2 medium zucchinis, diced (optional) 2 medium red bell peppers, diced 1 large eggplant, diced (optional)

Provisions

1 (15 oz) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 can of crushed tomatoes (28 oz) 2 tbsp olive oil Salt and pepper, to taste 1 tsp dried oregano 1 tsp Spanish paprika 1/2 tsp cumin powder 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional) 1 bay leaf 1 lemon, juiced

Step 1: Searing the Onions and Garlic In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat 1 tbsp of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until it caramelizes and becomes golden brown. The onions are raw, releasing their pungency, so the caramelization process is crucial to minimize the initial acidity. Keep stirring the onions occasionally until they become translucent. Add minced garlic and sauté for 2 minutes or until fragrant. Step 2: Softening the Vegetables Next, add the diced zucchinis, red bell peppers, and eggplant to the pot and sauté them for about 5 minutes, allowing them to release their moisture. Remove the bay leaf and lower the heat to a medium-low setting. Step 3: Adding the Liquid Ingredients In the same pot, pour the crushed tomatoes, followed by the chickpeas, water to cover, salt, black pepper, oregano, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using), stirring occasionally to stop the mixture from sticking to the iron bottom of the pot. Step 4: Tomato-Caramelizing Now, quickly heat the mixture over high for 5 minutes, causing the liquid to boil rapidly, allowing the tomatoes to evaporate their excess moisture while softening the ingredients. This aggressive boiling process creates the unique mix of flavors in the stew. Lower the heat and cover the pot. As it boils gently, reduced heat, attention will be paid to prevent sticking. Step 5: Achieving Depth of Flavor Transfer the stew to a 325°F preheated oven and allow it to simmer and wiggle for approximately 20 minutes to create layered richness and distinct flavor. After 20 minutes, transfer the pot back to the stovetop, leaving the lid on. Season with salt as needed, then stir in the lemon juice. Step 6: Finishing Touches Next, wilt the fresh spinach into the stew by piling the fresh spinach on top of the chickpea stew then letting it wilt, the steam should soften spinach for you. Satisfying Mediterranean Chickpea and Spinach Stew Recipe Introduction A hearty, comforting bowl of Mediterranean Chickpea and Spinach Stew is a delightful blend of flavors and textures that's perfect for a chilly evening. This mouthwatering stew is a staple in the Mediterranean diet, and for good reason. The Key Ingredients To make this recipe, you'll need the following ingredients: Produce

1 large onion, diced 3 cloves of garlic, minced 2 medium tomatoes, diced 2 cups of fresh spinach leaves 2 medium zucchinis, diced (optional) 2 medium red bell peppers, diced 1 large eggplant, diced (optional)

Provisions

1 (15 oz) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 can of crushed tomatoes (28 oz) 2 tbsp olive oil Salt and pepper, to taste 1 tsp dried oregano 1 tsp Spanish paprika 1/2 tsp cumin powder 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional) 1 bay leaf 1 lemon, juiced

Step 1: Searing the Onions and Garlic Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 8-10 minutes, or until it caramelizes and becomes golden brown. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 2 minutes. Step 2: Softening the Vegetables Add the diced zucchinis, red bell peppers, and eggplant to the pot and sauté them for 5 minutes, allowing them to release their moisture. Remove the bay leaf and lower the heat to medium-low. Step 3: Adding the Liquid Ingredients Pour the crushed tomatoes, followed by the chickpeas, water to cover, salt, black pepper, oregano, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using), stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Step 4: Tomato-Caramelizing Quickly heat the mixture over high for 5 minutes, causing the liquid to boil rapidly and the tomatoes to evaporate their excess moisture. This process creates the unique flavor of the stew. Lower the heat and cover the pot. Step 5: Achieving Depth of Flavor Transfer the stew to a 325°F preheated oven and let it simmer and reduce for approximately 20 minutes to create a rich, layered flavor. After 20 minutes, transfer the pot back to the stovetop, leaving the lid on. Step 6: Finishing Touches Stir in the lemon juice and wilt the fresh spinach into the stew. Season with salt as needed. Tasting and Serving Serve the stew in bowls and enjoy! Nutritional Content and Shelf Life Per serving (2 cups):

Calories: 550 - 650 Protein: 15g - 18g Fat: 12g - 18g Saturated Fat: 2g - 3g Carbohydrates: 70g - 85g Fiber: 10g - 12g Sugar: 6g - 8g Sodium: 1500 - 2000mg Cholesterol: 4g Potassium: 4000mg

Consume the stew within a day or two, or freeze to store and reheat.
