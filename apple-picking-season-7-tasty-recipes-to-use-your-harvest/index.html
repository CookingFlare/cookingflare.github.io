Apple Picking Season: 7 Tasty Recipes to Use Your Harvest


As the cool breeze of autumn sets in and the leaves start to change their colors, it’s the perfect time for apple picking. This beloved fall tradition is more than just a fun outdoor activity; it’s an opportunity to harvest the fruits of the season and create delicious dishes that celebrate the crisp, sweet, and tart flavors of freshly picked apples. Whether you’ve ventured to an orchard to pick your own apples or have been gifted a bounty of them, you’re likely wondering what to do with this wonderful harvest.

Here are seven delicious and creative recipes that will make the most of your apple harvest. These recipes are perfect for breakfast, snacks, or dessert, and will fill your home with the warm, comforting smells of fall. From classic pies to savory dishes, these ideas will ensure that your apple picking experience results in tasty meals all season long.

1. Classic Apple Pie

You can’t go wrong with a traditional apple pie. This all-time favorite is a must for anyone who has freshly picked apples in their kitchen. The combination of sweet apples, a hint of cinnamon, and a perfectly flaky crust is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of peeled, cored, and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp apples work well)
  • ¾ cup of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts (or homemade if you prefer)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon of milk (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cornstarch. Stir until the apples are evenly coated.
  3. Unroll one pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Press gently to fit the bottom and sides of the dish.
  4. Pour the apple mixture into the pie crust, ensuring it’s evenly distributed. Dot the filling with butter.
  5. Unroll the second pie crust and place it over the apple filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges together to seal the pie.
  6. Cut a few slits in the top of the pie to allow steam to escape. For a golden finish, brush the top crust with an egg wash (1 egg mixed with 1 tablespoon of milk).
  7. Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  8. Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

2. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

For a quick and easy breakfast treat, these apple cinnamon muffins are a great way to use your harvest. They are soft, fluffy, and full of cozy autumn flavors. Perfect with a hot cup of coffee or tea!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup of unsweetened applesauce
  • ½ cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 medium apple, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the sugar and egg until light and fluffy. Add the applesauce, milk, vanilla, and melted butter. Mix until combined.
  4. Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Fold in the diced apples.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about two-thirds of the way.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before serving. These are perfect for breakfast, brunch, or a midday snack!

3. Apple and Cheddar Grilled Cheese Sandwiches

For a savory twist on the classic grilled cheese sandwich, add slices of crisp apples and sharp cheddar. The combination of the sweet apples and tangy cheese is a delightful surprise and makes for a satisfying lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of sourdough bread
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 slices of sharp cheddar cheese
  • ½ apple, thinly sliced (Granny Smith or Gala apples work great)
  • 1 tablespoon of honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Butter one side of each slice of bread.
  3. Place one slice of bread, butter side down, on the skillet. Top with one slice of cheddar cheese.
  4. Layer the apple slices on top of the cheese, and drizzle with a little honey if desired.
  5. Place the second slice of bread, butter side up, on top.
  6. Grill the sandwich for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  7. Slice and serve warm. Enjoy the sweet and savory combo that’s perfect for a cozy fall meal!

4. Apple Cider Donuts

What’s better than a freshly baked donut? A warm, homemade apple cider donut! These donuts are flavored with apple cider, cinnamon, and nutmeg, giving them a delicious fall twist. They’re perfect for breakfast or a sweet treat with your afternoon coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of apple cider
  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, bring the apple cider to a simmer over medium heat. Cook for about 10 minutes, until the cider is reduced to ¾ cup. Set aside to cool.
  2. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a donut pan.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  4. In a separate bowl, beat together the sugar, eggs, melted butter, vanilla extract, and reduced apple cider.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Spoon the batter into the donut pan, filling each cavity about two-thirds of the way full.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the donuts comes out clean.
  8. Let the donuts cool for a few minutes before removing them from the pan. While they’re still warm, dip them in a mixture of cinnamon and brown sugar for an extra layer of sweetness.
  9. Serve and enjoy these warm, fragrant donuts that will make your kitchen smell like fall!

5. Apple Slaw with Cinnamon Vinaigrette

This apple slaw is a refreshing and crunchy side dish that pairs perfectly with fall meals. The cinnamon vinaigrette adds a unique twist, making this slaw stand out at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of shredded green cabbage
  • 1 cup of shredded red cabbage
  • 1 large apple, julienned
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • ¼ cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded green and red cabbage, julienned apple, and carrot.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the apple cider vinegar, honey, cinnamon, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the slaw mixture and toss to combine.
  4. Let the slaw sit for at least 15 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve chilled as a refreshing side dish for your next autumn meal!

6. Apple Crisp

For a simple yet incredibly satisfying dessert, apple crisp is a winner. With a buttery crumble topping and a warm, sweet apple filling, it’s comfort food at its finest.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • ¾ cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup of old-fashioned rolled oats
  • ¼ cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup of brown sugar
  • ½ teaspoon of ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, cold and cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with granulated sugar, lemon juice, and cinnamon. Spread the apple mixture into a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, ginger, and salt. Add the cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are bubbling.
  6. Let the apple crisp cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

7. Apple and Bacon Stuffed Pork Tenderloin

For a savory dish that will impress your guests, try making apple and bacon stuffed pork tenderloin. The sweetness of the apples and the smoky flavor of bacon perfectly complement the richness of the pork.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin (about 1 ½ pounds)
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • ½ onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the bacon until crisp. Remove the bacon from the pan and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the skillet.
  3. Add the onions and garlic to the skillet and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes. Add the diced apples and sage, and cook for another 3 minutes until the apples begin to soften.
  4. Stir in the cooked bacon and season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. Cut a slit down the center of the pork tenderloin to create a pocket for the stuffing.
  6. Stuff the pork tenderloin with the apple-bacon mixture, then secure with kitchen twine or toothpicks.
  7. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Brown the stuffed pork tenderloin on all sides.
  8. Transfer the pork to a baking dish and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  9. Let the pork rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Apple picking season offers an abundance of opportunities to create delicious dishes. Whether you’re in the mood for sweet desserts, savory meals, or refreshing salads, the versatility of apples means there’s a recipe for every occasion. Try these seven dishes this fall, and let your apple harvest shine in your kitchen!
