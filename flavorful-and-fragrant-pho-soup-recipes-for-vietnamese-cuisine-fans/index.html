Flavorful and Fragrant Pho Soup Recipes for Vietnamese Cuisine Fans


Pho soup is a beloved dish in Vietnamese cuisine, known for its rich flavors and fragrant aromas. This traditional Vietnamese soup is made with a savory broth, tender rice noodles, and an array of fresh herbs and toppings. If you’re a fan of Vietnamese cuisine and want to recreate the authentic flavors of pho in your own kitchen, you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’ll explore three delightful pho soup recipes that will transport you to the bustling streets of Vietnam.

Classic Beef Pho

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef bones
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 4 star anise
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • Bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, char the onion and ginger over an open flame until lightly blackened. This step adds a smoky flavor to the broth.
  2. In a dry skillet, toast the star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves, coriander seeds, and fennel seeds until fragrant.
  3. Add the beef bones, charred onion, ginger, toasted spices, beef broth, fish sauce, and sugar to a stockpot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for at least 2 hours.
  4. While the broth simmers, prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Strain the broth and discard the solids. Return the broth to the pot and bring to a boil.
  6. Place a handful of cooked rice noodles in each serving bowl. Top with sliced beef sirloin.
  7. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef, ensuring they are completely submerged.
  8. Serve the pho with bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers on the side. Let your guests customize their pho to their liking.

Chicken Pho

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 4 star anise
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • Bean sprouts, cilantro, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers for serving

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken in a large pot and add enough water to cover it. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Remove the chicken from the pot and discard the water. Rinse the chicken under cold water to remove any impurities.
  3. In the same pot, char the onion and ginger over an open flame until lightly blackened.
  4. Toast the star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves, coriander seeds, and fennel seeds in a dry skillet until fragrant.
  5. Add the chicken, charred onion, ginger, toasted spices, chicken broth, fish sauce, and sugar to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for at least 1 hour.
  6. While the broth simmers, shred the cooked chicken meat and set aside.
  7. Prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  8. Strain the broth and discard the solids. Return the broth to the pot and bring to a boil.
  9. Place a handful of cooked rice noodles in each serving bowl. Top with shredded chicken.
  10. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and chicken.
  11. Serve the pho with bean sprouts, cilantro, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers on the side. Enjoy the comforting flavors of chicken pho!

Vegetarian Pho

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 4 star anise
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • Tofu, sliced mushrooms, bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, char the onion and ginger over an open flame until lightly blackened.
  2. Toast the star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves, coriander seeds, and fennel seeds in a dry skillet until fragrant.
  3. Add the charred onion, ginger, toasted spices, vegetable broth, soy sauce, and sugar to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for at least 1 hour.
  4. While the broth simmers, prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Strain the broth and discard the solids. Return the broth to the pot and bring to a boil.
  6. Place a handful of cooked rice noodles in each serving bowl. Top with tofu and sliced mushrooms.
  7. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, tofu, and mushrooms.
  8. Serve the vegetarian pho with bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers on the side. Enjoy the delightful flavors of this meatless pho.

Pho soup is a delightful dish that brings the flavors of Vietnam to your table. Whether you prefer the classic beef version, the comforting chicken variation, or the flavorful vegetarian option, pho is sure to satisfy your cravings for a hearty and aromatic soup. Experiment with different toppings and herbs to create your own unique pho experience. So, gather your ingredients, immerse yourself in the fragrant aromas, and indulge in the deliciousness of pho soup!
