How to Make Mouthwatering Tandoori Chicken at Home


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, then you’ve probably had your fair share of tandoori chicken. The smoky, flavorful dish is a crowd favorite, and now you can recreate it in the comfort of your own kitchen. In this recipe, I’ll walk you through the steps to make mouthwatering tandoori chicken at home. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

To make tandoori chicken, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds of chicken (preferably bone-in, skinless)
  • 1 cup of plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of tandoori masala
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, tandoori masala, paprika, ginger paste, garlic paste, ground cumin, ground coriander, salt, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  2. Add the chicken to the bowl and coat it evenly with the marinade. Make sure to rub the marinade into the chicken to ensure maximum flavor. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight for the best results.

  3. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). If you have a grill, you can also cook the chicken over medium-high heat.

  4. If you’re using an oven, place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle the chicken with oil and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.

  5. If you’re using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for about 10-12 minutes per side or until fully cooked and slightly charred.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the oven or grill and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy chicken.

  7. Garnish the tandoori chicken with fresh cilantro and serve it hot with naan bread, rice, or your favorite side dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra smoky flavor, you can add a few drops of liquid smoke to the marinade.
  • If you don’t have tandoori masala, you can make your own by combining equal parts of ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika, turmeric powder, and red chili powder.
  • If you want a spicier version, you can increase the amount of red chili powder or add some chopped green chilies to the marinade.
  • Tandoori chicken is traditionally cooked in a tandoor, a clay oven. If you have access to one, feel free to use it for an authentic taste.

Final Thoughts

Making mouthwatering tandoori chicken at home is easier than you might think. With the right ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can enjoy the flavors of this classic Indian dish without leaving your kitchen. So, gather your spices, marinate the chicken, and get ready to indulge in a delicious meal that will impress your family and friends. Happy cooking!
