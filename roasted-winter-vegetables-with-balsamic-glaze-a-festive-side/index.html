Roasted Winter Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze: A Festive Side


Winter is the time for hearty meals and cozy gatherings, and what better way to complement your main dish than with a beautiful and flavorful side of roasted winter vegetables? This recipe for Roasted Winter Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze is the perfect addition to any festive feast. The combination of colorful vegetables, aromatic herbs, and a tangy balsamic glaze will leave your taste buds dancing with delight.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 fennel bulb, trimmed and cut into wedges
  • 1 small head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 4-5 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Balsamic Glaze

  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey (or maple syrup for a vegan option)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cubed butternut squash, carrot rounds, parsnip rounds, onion wedges, fennel wedges, and cauliflower florets.

  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the vegetables and season generously with salt and pepper. Toss everything together until the vegetables are well coated.

  4. Spread the vegetables evenly on the prepared baking sheet. Scatter the fresh thyme sprigs on top.

  5. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast for 30-35 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and caramelized, stirring once halfway through.

  6. While the vegetables are roasting, prepare the balsamic glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar and honey (or maple syrup). Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the glaze has thickened slightly. Remove from heat and set aside.

  7. Once the vegetables are done roasting, remove them from the oven and transfer to a serving dish. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the top, using as much or as little as you prefer.

  8. Serve the Roasted Winter Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze as a festive side dish alongside your favorite main course. The vibrant colors and rich flavors will impress your guests and add a touch of elegance to your meal.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences and what’s available. Brussels sprouts, sweet potatoes, and beets are also great options for roasting.
  • Add a sprinkle of crumbled feta cheese or toasted nuts, such as pecans or walnuts, for an extra layer of flavor and texture.
  • You can make the balsamic glaze ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to a week. Simply reheat gently before drizzling over the roasted vegetables.
  • This dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Winter vegetables are rich in vitamins and antioxidants that support overall health and well-being.

So, there you have it – a simple yet impressive recipe for Roasted Winter Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze. This festive side dish will elevate any winter gathering and leave your guests craving for more. Enjoy the warmth, flavors, and colors of the season with this delightful recipe. Happy cooking!
