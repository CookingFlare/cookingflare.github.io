Ricotta and Spinach Stuffed Shells: A Cheesy Italian Comfort


If you’re looking for a comforting and delicious Italian dish, look no further than Ricotta and Spinach Stuffed Shells. These cheesy pasta shells are filled with a creamy ricotta and spinach mixture, baked to perfection, and topped with a flavorful marinara sauce. This recipe is the perfect combination of flavors and textures, making it a crowd-pleaser for both weeknight dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup of chopped spinach, cooked and drained
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions. Make sure to cook them until they are al dente, as they will continue to cook in the oven. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, cooked spinach, minced garlic, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  4. Take each cooked pasta shell and stuff it generously with the ricotta and spinach mixture. Place the stuffed shells in a 9x13-inch baking dish, arranging them in a single layer.

  5. Pour the marinara sauce evenly over the stuffed shells, making sure they are all covered. Sprinkle the remaining 1 cup of mozzarella cheese on top.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  7. Once baked, remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired.

  8. Serve the Ricotta and Spinach Stuffed Shells warm and enjoy the cheesy, gooey goodness!

Tips and Variations:

  • You can add cooked ground meat such as ground beef, sausage, or turkey to the ricotta and spinach mixture for an extra protein boost and a heartier flavor.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese like fontina or provolone to add more depth to the dish.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, you can add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the ricotta and spinach mixture or to the marinara sauce.
  • This recipe can be prepared in advance and refrigerated until ready to bake. Just make sure to increase the baking time slightly to ensure the shells are heated through.
  • Serve the stuffed shells alongside a fresh green salad and crusty garlic bread for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion:

Ricotta and Spinach Stuffed Shells are a classic Italian comfort food that never fails to impress. With its creamy and cheesy filling, paired with the tangy marinara sauce, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re cooking for your family or entertaining guests, these stuffed shells are a delicious and satisfying option that will leave everyone asking for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and indulge in this cheesy Italian comfort!
