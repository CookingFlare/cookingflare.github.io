Irresistible Blackberry Cobbler Recipe


If you’re looking for a classic dessert that’s both comforting and bursting with flavor, then this irresistible blackberry cobbler recipe is just what you need. This timeless dessert is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a casual family dinner or a special gathering with friends. The combination of sweet, juicy blackberries and a buttery, biscuit-like topping is sure to win over everyone’s taste buds. So, let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create this delightful treat.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 6 cups fresh blackberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

For the Topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/3 cup boiling water

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, gently toss together the blackberries, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and cornstarch until the blackberries are evenly coated. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out into an even layer.

  3. Make the Topping: In another bowl, combine the flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the blackberry filling.

  4. Bake the Cobbler: Pour the boiling water evenly over the topping. This will create a beautiful, golden crust. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the blackberry filling is bubbling.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the cobbler is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve the cobbler warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, try adding a hint of cinnamon or nutmeg to the blackberry filling for an extra layer of flavor.
  • You can substitute fresh blackberries with frozen ones, especially if they’re not in season. Just be sure to adjust the sugar and cornstarch accordingly based on the sweetness and juiciness of the berries.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This blackberry cobbler recipe is a delightful balance of sweet and tart flavors, with a comforting, biscuit-like topping that’s both tender and crispy. The contrast between the warm, juicy blackberries and the buttery topping creates a dessert that’s truly irresistible. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding, making it a go-to for any dessert lover.

So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of fresh blackberries, or simply craving a classic dessert that’s sure to impress, give this irresistible blackberry cobbler recipe a try. It’s a timeless treat that never fails to bring joy to the table.
