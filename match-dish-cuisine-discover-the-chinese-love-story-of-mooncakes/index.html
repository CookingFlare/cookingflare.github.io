Match-Dish Cuisine: Discover the Chinese Love Story of Mooncakes


Mooncakes hold a special place in Chinese culture, symbolizing unity and togetherness, especially during the Mid-Autumn Festival when family members gather to share a moonlit feast. Within these delicate pastries lies a match-like essence, with each bite connecting to the tale of passionate love between two famous figures - Zheng Wei and Tie Zhong.

Culinary Love Affair of Zheng Wei and Tie Zhong

Zheng Wei, the devoted scholar, and Tie Zhong, the beautiful Taoist priestess, concealed their love under the cloak of piety and discipline in a time when their love was considered a taboo. Their passionate correspondence was secreted within mooncakes, which they shared when they could be together during midnight moonlit rendezvous.

One of the earliest accounts of mooncakes, documented in the “Treatise on Food and Drink” written by Huizing in the Song Dynasty, highlights this passionate story. These date back to several centuries ago, showcasing a long-standing tradition that developed through the centuries.

Crafting Mooncakes: The Match of Culture and Culinary Arts

Mooncakes feature a dense, sweet filling made of lotus seed paste or red bean paste, studded with fragrant melon seeds, encased in a delicate, thin, crisp pastry shell. This centuries-old culinary technique represents the harmonious pairing of sweet and savory, symbolizing the love that Zheng Wei and Tie Zhong shared.

Creating mooncakes at home can be a fun and exciting culinary adventure that will deepen your understanding of the story and tradition behind this iconic dish. Here’s a simple and easy recipe to make these beloved treats:

Match-Dish Mooncake Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lotus seed paste
  • 2 tablespoons melon seeds
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 cup wheat starch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 6 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • Lotus or pumpkin seed paste filling
  • Melon seeds, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough: To make the pastry, combine water, wheat starch, all-purpose flour, and sugar in a large mixing bowl. Set aside for 10-15 minutes. This will help hydrate the starch and allow the dough to form.

  2. Mix the Dough: After the rest period, slowly pour the vegetable oil into the bowl while continuously stirring the mixture. Combine until the dough forms into a sticky mass. If the dough feels too wet, add more flour gradually until it becomes less sticky.

  3. Form the Dough: Place the dough on a lightly floured surface and knead for a few minutes. The dough should be smooth and elastic. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  4. Preheat the Filling: Briefly warm the lotus seed paste in the microwave on medium heat for about 30 seconds. Stir the paste and set it aside to cool to room temperature.

  5. Mix the Filling: When the paste is cool, mix in 2 tablespoons of melon seeds. If the paste is too dense or dry, add a little water or syrup to adjust the consistency.

  6. Combine the Pastry and Filling: Roll the dough into a long log, cut into nine equal pieces, and then shape each piece into a flat round. Place about two tablespoons of filling into the center of each flattened dough piece. Wrap the dough around the filling, making sure to seal the edges. If desired, garnish with a few melon seeds on top.

  7. Cook the Mooncakes: Heat about 1 inch of oil in a deep pot or wok over medium heat. Once the oil reaches 325°F, carefully drop each mooncake into the oil.

  8. Bake to Perfection: Fry the mooncakes until they float to the surface and turn golden brown. This should take about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove the mooncakes with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.

  9. Serve Warm: Enjoy warm or cool, with a steaming cup of tea, just like how Zheng Wei and Tie Zhong shared them centuries ago.

Mooncakes have evolved through time, growing from a symbol of hidden love between two dedicated souls to beloved treats celebrating the Mid-Autumn Festival. By crafting these pastries, we connect with their enduring tale and share the enduring flavors of love, hope, and unity with our loved ones.

Dive into Chinese Match-Dish Cuisine, explore the legacy of Zheng Wei and Tie Zhong, and reinvent this culinary love story in your kitchen. With their stories woven in your very own mooncakes, you’ll be embracing a piece of tradition that has withstood the sands of time.
