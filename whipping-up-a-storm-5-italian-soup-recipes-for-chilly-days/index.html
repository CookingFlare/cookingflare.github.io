Whipping Up a Storm: 5 Italian Soup Recipes for Chilly Days


When the temperature drops, there’s nothing quite like a hot bowl of soup to warm up your insides and chase away the chill. Soups are not only nurturing; they also offer an excellent opportunity to showcase your culinary skills. This article presents five Italian soup recipes that will take your cooking to the next level. Time to get your apron on and whip up a storm!

1. Minestrone

Minestrone is an Italian vegetable soup that has it all – vegetables, beans, and pasta. It is a hearty, crowd-pleasing soup that makes a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup slashed green cabbage
  • 2 small zucchini, diced
  • 1 small eggplant, diced
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 cup short-cut pastina pasta
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until it becomes tender and translucent.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute or two, stirring constantly to keep the garlic from burning.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes, and bring the liquid to a simmer.
  4. Add the remaining vegetables – green beans, cabbage, zucchini, and eggplant – and the water. Season everything with a generous pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Let the soup cook for approximately 30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Finally, add the short-cut pastina pasta and cook for another 10 minutes or until the pasta is al dente.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese.

2. Tomato and Basil Soup

This tomato and basil soup recipe is a classic Italian treat that tastes just like summer in a bowl.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups fresh tomato juice (blended fresh tomatoes and their juices)
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until it reaches a tender state.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Pour in the tomato juice and vegetable or chicken broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Add the chopped basil and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Simmer the soup for about 15 minutes.
  6. Stir in a tablespoon of balsamic vinegar just before serving.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese.

3. Pasta e Fagioli

This Italian bean and pasta soup is a hearty and satisfying dish that winners.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup small pasta (like ditalini)
  • 2 cups salvaguarda (Italian sausage with tomato sauce), optional
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until tender.
  2. Stir in the sliced carrots and chopped celery and cook until they start to soften.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and bring everything to a simmer.
  5. Stir in the drained and rinsed red kidney beans, and if using, the salvaguarda.
  6. Season everything with salt and pepper.
  7. Finally, add the small pasta and cook for about 10 minutes, or until it’s al dente.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese.

4. Zuppa Toscana

If you love Italian soups, zuppa Toscana won’t disappoint. It is a luscious potato, spinach, and sausage soup that packs plenty of heartiness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 cups leftover cooked diced potatoes
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup Italian sausage, cooked and crumbled
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until tender.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, and bring everything to a simmer.
  4. Add the cooked diced potatoes, fresh spinach, and cooked crumbled Italian sausage.
  5. Simmer the soup for about 10 minutes or until the spinach wilts.
  6. Before serving, stir in the heavy cream and season everything with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese.

5. Cioppino

This seafood Cioppino is a stew-like soup loaded with seafood and flavored with white wine and tomato. It’s a flavorful, rich soup perfect for chilly days.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 4 cups fish broth or seafood stock
  • 1 cup chopped green bell pepper
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup chopped AI (artichoke hearts)
  • 2 cups mixed seafood (shrimp, clams, or calamari)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they become tender.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes (with their juice) and white wine, and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Pour in the fish broth or seafood stock.
  5. Add the chopped green bell pepper, chopped celery, and chopped artichoke hearts.
  6. Let the soup simmer for about 15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Finally, stir in the mixed seafood and cook until the seafood is cooked through.
  8. Season everything with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve hot, with lemon wedges on the side.

Don’t let the cold weather get you down. Whip up any of these Italian soup recipes, and let their warm and hearty flavors chase away the chill. Buon appettito!
