Sous Vide Cabbage: A Twist on a Classic Side Dish


Cabbage is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that has been a staple in kitchens around the world for centuries. From sautéing to pickling, there are countless ways to prepare cabbage. But have you ever considered cooking it sous vide? This innovative cooking method takes cabbage to a whole new level, enhancing its flavor and texture. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a delicious sous vide cabbage dish that will surely impress your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 1 head of cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. Begin by preparing the cabbage. Remove the outer leaves and cut the head into quarters, removing the core. Rinse the cabbage quarters under cold water to remove any dirt or debris.

  3. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. This flavorful mixture will infuse the cabbage with a delicious taste.

  4. Place each cabbage quarter on a separate piece of aluminum foil, ensuring that each piece is large enough to fully enclose the cabbage. Drizzle the olive oil mixture evenly over each cabbage quarter, making sure to coat all sides.

  5. Carefully wrap each cabbage quarter in the aluminum foil, creating a tight seal to prevent any moisture from escaping during the sous vide cooking process.

  6. Gently place the wrapped cabbage quarters into a sous vide bag, ensuring that they are in a single layer. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  7. Submerge the sealed bag into the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours. This prolonged cooking time allows the cabbage to cook evenly and develop a tender texture.

  8. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Be cautious as the bag and cabbage will be hot.

  9. Unwrap the cabbage quarters from the aluminum foil and transfer them to a serving dish. The cabbage should be soft, yet still retain its shape.

  10. Serve the sous vide cabbage as a side dish alongside your favorite protein or incorporate it into other recipes. Its tender texture and enhanced flavor will elevate any meal.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different seasonings to customize the flavor of the sous vide cabbage. Try adding smoked paprika or chili flakes for a spicy kick, or sprinkle some Parmesan cheese over the cabbage before serving for a savory twist.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add a splash of balsamic vinegar or lemon juice to the olive oil mixture before drizzling it over the cabbage.

  • Sous vide cabbage can also be used as a filling for sandwiches, wraps, or tacos. Its tender texture and savory taste make it a versatile ingredient in various dishes.

  • If you prefer a bit of crunch in your cabbage, you can briefly sear the sous vide cabbage quarters in a hot skillet for a caramelized exterior.

Sous vide cabbage offers a unique and delicious way to enjoy this humble vegetable. By cooking it slowly and precisely, the cabbage becomes tender and flavorful, making it an excellent addition to any meal. So why not give this twist on a classic side dish a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
