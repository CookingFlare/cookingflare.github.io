Easy Burrito Bowl Recipe: A Healthier Spin on a Classic Favorite


Are you looking for a tasty new way to enjoy an old favorite? Then let’s dive into a revitalized version of the classic burrito bowl! This healthier spin on the traditional dish offers the same satisfying taste and texture, but with fewer calories and less sodium.

Burrito bowls originated in the southwestern United States, where families and friends would gather around a big bowl of rice and an array of enticing fillings. They quickly gained popularity for their customizable nature, allowing individuals to incorporate their favorite ingredients and flavors to customize their perfect dish.

Our healthier burrito bowl recipe prioritizes the use of fresh, nutrient-dense produce, whole grains, and lean proteins. By exchanging some traditional components with more nutritious options, we maintain the essence of the dish while providing our bodies with the nourishment they need to thrive.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts

Instructions:

  1. Begin by cooking the brown rice according to the package instructions.

  2. While the rice is cooking, prepare the chicken. In a small bowl, mix together the lime juice, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag, pour the seasoning mixture over them, and toss gently to coat. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. When the rice is finished cooking, fluff it with a fork and set it aside.

  4. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Take the chicken out of the marinade and lightly dust both sides with the remaining spice mixture. Heat a large, oven-safe skillet over medium heat, then add the olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken breasts and cook them for about 6 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and no longer pink in the center.

  5. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the veggies. Dice your avocado, tomato, red onion, and cilantro, setting them aside in separate containers to maintain their respective flavors and textures.

  6. When the chicken is done, remove it from the skillet and set it aside to rest. Add your black beans to the same skillet and cook for a few minutes until heated through.

  7. Serving time! Divide the cooked brown rice between four bowls. Top each bowl with the black beans, diced avocado, tomato, red onion, and cilantro. Once the chicken has rested for a few minutes, slice it into strips and evenly distribute it over the bowls.

That’s it! Your healthier burrito bowl recipe is now ready to be devoured. Don’t forget to drizzle each bowl with an extra splash of lime juice for added flavor.

This dish stores well for cafeteria or meal prep days. Simply divide the components into individual containers and refrigerate for up to four days. When ready to enjoy the meal, warm up the chicken and beans and toss the colorful veggies over the rice for the perfect bowl every time.

So, gather your friends and family and enjoy a warm and satisfying meal, elevated with a healthier twist! Who’s ready to dive into their first burrito bowl?
