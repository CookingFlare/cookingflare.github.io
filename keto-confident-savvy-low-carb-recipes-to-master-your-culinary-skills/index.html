Keto & Confident: Savvy Low-Carb Recipes to Master Your Culinary Skills


Are you tired of struggling with carb-heavy diets? Do you want to dive headfirst into a new eating style that boosts your energy, clears your mind, and promotes weight loss? Look no further than the ketogenic (keto) diet! Embrace the power of fats, the beauty of meats, the joy of vegetables, and much more in this deliciously relaxing guide, Keto & Confident. Ready to take on culinary mastery while maintaining a low-carb lifestyle? Let’s get started then!

Seamless and Silky Butternut Squash Risotto

Maybe you think risotto is reserved only for fatter times, well think again! With a fantastic low-carb tweak, it’s possible to indulge in this creamy, dreamy delight while remaining keto-friendly. Let’s put your culinary prowess to the test with this easy, yet elegant dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • Coconut oil, for cooking
  • 2 cups chorizo, casings removed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup cauliflower rice
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or thyme, chopped (optional)
  • 2 lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss your butternut squash cubes in coconut oil and spread them evenly on a lined baking sheet. Roast for about 30-35 minutes, or until tender and golden. Set aside.
  2. Cook the chorizo in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat until fully browned. Remove from the pot with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add some more coconut oil and sauté the diced onion until translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the arborio rice, making sure each grain is coated in oil and onion-garlic mixture. Slowly pour in the chicken or vegetable broth one ladle at a time, allowing each ladle to be fully absorbed before adding the next.
  5. At the same time, cook the cauliflower rice according to the package or by sautéing it in a separate pan. Once done, set it aside for now.
  6. Once the arborio rice is al dente and the broth has been fully absorbed, add the roasted butternut squash and the cooked cauliflower rice. Mix well to combine.
  7. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese, cooked chorizo, salt, and pepper. Continue to cook until the cheese has melted, and the flavors have been well incorporated.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary. If you’re feeling fancy, sprinkle some fresh parsley or thyme on top.
  9. Serve warm with lemon wedges on the side for a zesty touch.

The Best Ever Bacon and Cheddar Quiche

Quiche is a scrumptious, versatile dish, perfect for any meal of the day! Here’s a low-carb version that’s sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 premade low-carb pie crust
  • Coconut oil, for cooking
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 10-12 eggs
  • 1 lb thick-cut bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Carefully place the low-carb pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and gently prick the bottom with a fork. Brush with coconut oil and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, milk, eggs, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Scatter half of the shredded cheddar cheese on the bottom of the pie crust, followed by the cooked bacon and the remaining cheese.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the cheese and bacon, ensuring it’s evenly distributed. Give it a gentle shake or tap on the counter to release any air bubbles.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the center is set and doesn’t jiggle when the quiche is gently shaken.
  6. Allow the quiche to cool for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing. Optional: garnish with fresh chives on top.

Peppery Steaks with Sautéed Greens

Let’s take a moment to appreciate a deceptively simple steak recipe that delivers full-bodied flavors and tends to please the palate—steak and greens with a peppery kick!

Ingredients:

  • 4 ribeye or sirloin steaks, about 1-inch thick
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or grass-fed butter
  • 1 sprig rosemary, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bunches of green vegetables (like kale, spinach, or Swiss chard), destemmed and torn
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Red pepper flakes, optional

Instructions:

  1. Season both sides of the steaks generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a large cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add ghee or grass-fed butter and allow it to melt and foam.
  3. Once the butter has stopped foaming, sear the steaks for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they reach your desired doneness. Remove the steaks from the pan and set them aside on a plate, loosely tented with foil to keep warm.
  4. In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add the chopped rosemary and minced garlic. Sauté for about 1 minute, or until fragrant.
  5. Toss in the green vegetables, cook for 5-7 minutes, or until just wilted and tender. Stir them occasionally to make sure they cook evenly.
  6. Serve the peppery steaks alongside the greens, with lemon wedges on the side. For an extra kick, sprinkle some red pepper flakes on top.
