Easter Slow Cooker Recipes: Set It and Forget It


As the Easter holiday approaches, many of us are looking for ways to make the most of our time with family and friends. With busy schedules and a never-ending to-do list, it’s easy to get overwhelmed. That’s where the slow cooker comes in – a kitchen essential that allows you to prepare delicious meals with minimal effort and maximum flavor.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best Easter slow cooker recipes that are perfect for your holiday gathering. From classic ham and side dishes to sweet treats and desserts, we’ve got you covered. So, let’s get started!

Classic Easter Ham

There’s nothing more iconic than a slow-cooked Easter ham. This recipe is a crowd-pleaser, and for good reason – it’s easy to make, tender, and full of flavor.

  • 1 (4-6 pound) bone-in ham, trimmed of excess fat
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup orange juice

Combine the brown sugar, honey, mustard, vinegar, cloves, cinnamon, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Rub the mixture all over the ham, making sure to get some under the skin as well. Place the ham in the slow cooker and pour in the pineapple and orange juice. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours.

Slow Cooker Mashed Potatoes

These creamy mashed potatoes are the perfect side dish to accompany your Easter ham. They’re easy to make and can be customized to your taste with a variety of add-ins.

  • 3-4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: garlic powder, chives, or grated cheese for added flavor

Place the potatoes, butter, heavy cream, milk, salt, and pepper in the slow cooker. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. Mash the potatoes with a fork or potato masher, adding any desired add-ins.

Slow Cooker Green Beans

These tender green beans are a delicious and healthy side dish that’s perfect for Easter. They’re easy to make and can be customized with your favorite seasonings.

  • 1 pound fresh or frozen green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth

Place the green beans, olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, lemon zest, and lemon juice in the slow cooker. Cook on low for 4-6 hours or on high for 2-3 hours. Add the chicken broth during the last 30 minutes of cooking.

Slow Cooker Peach Cobbler

This delicious peach cobbler is the perfect dessert for your Easter gathering. It’s easy to make and requires minimal effort – simply mix the ingredients, pour into the slow cooker, and cook.

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peaches, sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Combine the peaches, sugar, flour, baking powder, and salt in the slow cooker. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients over the peach mixture, followed by the chopped pecans if using. Cook on low for 4-6 hours or on high for 2-3 hours.

Slow Cooker Cinnamon Rolls

These sweet cinnamon rolls are a delicious breakfast or brunch option for your Easter gathering. They’re easy to make and require minimal effort – simply mix the ingredients, pour into the slow cooker, and cook.

  • 1 can refrigerated cinnamon roll dough
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Combine the cinnamon roll dough, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in the slow cooker. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, milk, egg, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients over the dough mixture. Cook on low for 2-3 hours or on high for 1-2 hours.

In conclusion, these Easter slow cooker recipes are perfect for your holiday gathering. With minimal effort and maximum flavor, you’ll be able to enjoy a delicious and stress-free meal with your loved ones. Whether you’re serving a classic ham or a sweet dessert, these recipes are sure to impress. So, go ahead and set it and forget it – your taste buds will thank you!
