Farm to Table Cooking: 5 Recipes for a Light Summer Dinner


When summer hits, there’s no better time to embrace fresh, seasonal produce. The farm-to-table approach emphasizes using locally sourced ingredients to create meals that are both nutritious and delicious. For those warm, sun-kissed evenings, light and refreshing meals are ideal. In this blog post, we’ll explore five farm-to-table recipes perfect for a light summer dinner. These dishes feature vibrant vegetables, seasonal fruits, and a variety of herbs, ensuring every bite bursts with flavor.

1. Grilled Peach and Arugula Salad with Goat Cheese

A summer salad should be crisp, refreshing, and flavorful. This grilled peach and arugula salad is a perfect example of a dish that highlights the season’s best produce while offering a delightful combination of sweet and savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 4 cups fresh arugula, washed and dried
  • ½ cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • ¼ cup toasted almonds
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grill the Peaches: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the peach halves with a little olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place them on the grill, cut side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes or until grill marks appear. Flip the peaches and cook for an additional 3 minutes. Remove from heat and let them cool slightly.

  2. Prepare the Salad: In a large mixing bowl, combine the fresh arugula with the toasted almonds.

  3. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, and honey until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Assemble the Salad: Slice the grilled peaches into wedges and arrange them over the arugula. Sprinkle with crumbled goat cheese and drizzle with the dressing.

  5. Serve: Toss everything gently and serve immediately, enjoying the balance of sweetness from the peaches and the tanginess of the goat cheese.

This salad brings together the sweetness of summer fruit with the peppery bite of arugula and the richness of goat cheese, making it a perfect dish for a light summer dinner.

2. Zucchini Noodles with Fresh Pesto

For those who are looking for a lighter alternative to pasta, zucchini noodles (also known as “zoodles”) are an excellent choice. Paired with a vibrant basil pesto, this dish is bursting with fresh flavors while keeping things light and healthy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, packed
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Zoodles: Using a spiralizer, turn the zucchinis into noodles. If you don’t have a spiralizer, you can use a vegetable peeler to make long ribbons or buy pre-made zucchini noodles from your local market.

  2. Make the Pesto: In a food processor, combine the basil, garlic, pine nuts, and Parmesan cheese. Pulse until everything is finely chopped. With the processor running, slowly stream in the olive oil until the pesto reaches your desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Toss the Zoodles: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add the zucchini noodles. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes, just until they’re slightly tender but still crisp.

  4. Combine and Serve: Toss the sautéed zoodles with the fresh pesto, ensuring the noodles are well-coated. If desired, top with halved cherry tomatoes for added color and flavor.

This dish is perfect for those who crave a pasta alternative but still want all the fresh, herbaceous flavors of a summer meal. It’s quick, easy, and an absolute crowd-pleaser.

3. Grilled Chicken with Mango Salsa

Grilled chicken is always a great option for a light summer dinner, and when paired with a refreshing mango salsa, it becomes a flavorful, vibrant dish perfect for warm evenings. The combination of smoky grilled chicken and sweet, tangy salsa offers a great balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Mango Salsa:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • ½ red onion, finely diced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the chicken breasts and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours in the fridge.

  2. Grill the Chicken: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for 5-6 minutes per side, or until fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). Remove from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.

  3. Make the Mango Salsa: While the chicken is grilling, combine the diced mango, red bell pepper, red onion, and cilantro in a bowl. Squeeze the juice of one lime over the salsa and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Serve: Slice the grilled chicken breasts and top with the fresh mango salsa. Serve alongside a light salad or grilled vegetables for a complete summer meal.

The combination of smoky, charred chicken and sweet, tangy mango salsa will make your taste buds dance, making this dish an excellent choice for a light yet satisfying dinner.

4. Roasted Vegetable and Quinoa Bowls

This dish combines the earthy flavors of roasted vegetables with the protein-packed goodness of quinoa. It’s a simple yet filling option for a light summer dinner, offering a variety of textures and flavors that work together beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 medium eggplant, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce the heat, and cover. Simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the water is absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork.

  2. Roast the Vegetables: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the zucchini, eggplant, red bell pepper, and onion with olive oil, dried oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly browned.

  3. Assemble the Bowls: Divide the cooked quinoa into bowls and top with the roasted vegetables. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

This wholesome dish is perfect for a light but filling summer dinner. The roasted vegetables provide a savory richness that pairs perfectly with the light, fluffy quinoa.

5. Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze

Caprese skewers are a fun and simple way to enjoy the flavors of a classic Caprese salad in a bite-sized form. The combination of fresh mozzarella, juicy tomatoes, and aromatic basil makes for a light, satisfying appetizer or main dish when served with a drizzle of balsamic glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 package fresh mozzarella balls (ciliegine)
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Skewers: On small wooden or metal skewers, alternate threading cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves.

  2. Make the Balsamic Glaze: In a small saucepan, bring the balsamic vinegar and honey to a simmer over medium heat. Let it cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the glaze thickens to a syrupy consistency.

  3. Assemble and Serve: Arrange the skewers on a platter and drizzle with the balsamic glaze. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper before serving.

These bite-sized Caprese skewers are bursting with fresh, summer flavors and make for a light, refreshing dinner option. They also make for a great appetizer for any summer gathering.

Conclusion

Farm-to-table cooking not only supports local farmers but also ensures that you’re using the freshest ingredients in your meals. These five light summer dinner recipes are perfect for showcasing the season’s best produce, creating meals that are both satisfying and nutritious. Whether you’re looking for a crisp salad, grilled chicken, or a savory roasted vegetable bowl, these dishes highlight the vibrant flavors of summer, making your dinners both light and delicious.

By embracing seasonal ingredients, you’ll enjoy more vibrant, flavorful meals while supporting sustainable agriculture. Try these recipes the next time you’re planning a light, farm-fresh summer dinner and enjoy the fruits (and veggies!) of the season.
