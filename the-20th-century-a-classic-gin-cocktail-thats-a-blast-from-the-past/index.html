The 20th Century: A Classic Gin Cocktail That's a Blast from the Past


Are you a fan of classic cocktails? Do you enjoy the elegance and sophistication of drinks from a bygone era? If so, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re diving into the depths of cocktail history to bring you a gem from the 20th century: The 20th Century cocktail. This gin-based drink is a true blast from the past, and it’s guaranteed to transport you to a time when jazz was king and flapper dresses were all the rage.

What Makes the 20th Century Cocktail Special?

The 20th Century cocktail was first introduced in 1937 by a British bartender named C.A. Tuck. It gained popularity during the golden age of cocktails, and its unique combination of ingredients sets it apart from other drinks of its time.

The Ingredients

To make the 20th Century cocktail, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 ½ ounces of gin
  • ¾ ounce of Lillet Blanc (a French aperitif wine)
  • ¾ ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • ¼ ounce of crème de cacao (preferably white)

The Preparation

Now that you have all the ingredients, it’s time to shake things up! Here’s how you can make the 20th Century cocktail:

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice cubes.
  2. Add the gin, Lillet Blanc, lemon juice, and crème de cacao to the shaker.
  3. Shake vigorously for about 15 seconds to chill the ingredients and blend the flavors together.
  4. Strain the mixture into a chilled cocktail glass.
  5. Garnish with a lemon twist or a sprinkle of grated chocolate, if desired.

The Taste

The 20th Century cocktail is a harmonious blend of flavors. The gin provides a strong backbone, while the Lillet Blanc adds a touch of sweetness and complexity. The lemon juice adds a refreshing tartness, and the crème de cacao brings a subtle hint of chocolate to the mix. The end result is a smooth and well-balanced cocktail that’s both timeless and delicious.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips to help you perfect your 20th Century cocktail:

  • Use a good quality gin to ensure the best flavor. London dry gin works particularly well in this cocktail.
  • If you can’t find Lillet Blanc, you can substitute it with dry vermouth, although the taste will be slightly different.
  • Experiment with different garnishes to add your own personal touch to the drink. A lemon twist or a sprinkle of grated chocolate are classic choices, but feel free to get creative!
  • If you prefer a sweeter cocktail, you can increase the amount of crème de cacao. Just be careful not to overpower the other flavors.

In Conclusion

The 20th Century cocktail is a true classic that has stood the test of time. Its unique combination of ingredients and flavors makes it a must-try for any cocktail enthusiast. Whether you’re hosting a 1920s-themed party or simply looking to expand your cocktail repertoire, this blast from the past is sure to impress.

So, why not channel your inner Gatsby and indulge in a taste of the past? Shake up a 20th Century cocktail, sit back, and let the elegance of the golden age wash over you. Cheers!
