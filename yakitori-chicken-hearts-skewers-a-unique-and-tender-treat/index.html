Yakitori Chicken Hearts Skewers: A Unique and Tender Treat


If you’re looking to venture into the realms of unconventional grilling, then look no further than Yakitori Chicken Hearts Skewers. This unique dish takes a delicacy often overlooked and transforms it into a mouthwatering treat that will surprise and delight your taste buds. Don’t be intimidated by the idea of cooking chicken hearts; their tender texture and rich flavor make them a perfect candidate for the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of chicken hearts
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. This flavorful marinade will infuse the chicken hearts with a delectable combination of sweet, savory, and aromatic notes.

  2. Add the chicken hearts to the marinade and toss gently to ensure they are well coated. Let the hearts marinate for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat.

  3. While the hearts are marinating, prepare your grill. Preheat it to medium-high heat, ensuring the grates are clean and lightly oiled to prevent sticking.

  4. Thread the marinated chicken hearts onto the soaked bamboo skewers, leaving a small space between each heart to ensure even cooking.

  5. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for approximately 4-5 minutes per side, or until the hearts are cooked through and slightly charred. Be careful not to overcook them, as chicken hearts can become tough if cooked for too long.

  6. Once cooked, remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a couple of minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a tender and juicy bite.

  7. Serve the Yakitori Chicken Hearts Skewers as an appetizer or main course. They pair wonderfully with a side of steamed rice and a drizzle of the remaining marinade for added flavor.

Tips:

  • If you’re unable to find chicken hearts at your local grocery store, don’t hesitate to ask the butcher. They may be able to source them for you or suggest an alternative cut of meat with a similar texture and flavor.
  • Soaking the bamboo skewers in water before grilling helps prevent them from burning during cooking. This step is crucial to ensure the skewers are sturdy and safe to handle.
  • Feel free to experiment with the marinade by adding a touch of heat with some chili flakes or a squeeze of lime for a citrusy twist.

Yakitori Chicken Hearts Skewers are a unique and tender treat that will surprise and impress your guests. Don’t be afraid to step out of your comfort zone and try something a little different. The combination of the rich marinade and the succulent chicken hearts will leave you craving for more. So fire up the grill and embark on a culinary adventure that will take your taste buds to new heights. Enjoy!
