Grilled Ratatouille: A Colorful and Healthy Dish


Ratatouille, a well-known French dish originating from the province of Nice, combines a medley of vibrant vegetables with aromatic herbs and spices to create an enticing, aromatic, and delicious meal. While traditionally prepared by simmering the vegetables slowly, we’re introducing a more modern and uniquely cholesterol-free way to prepare this delightful dish - by grilling! This technique brings out the natural sweetness of the vegetables and imparts a smoky, slightly caramelized flavor.

To kick off, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 large eggplant, cubed
  • 1 large zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 2 bell peppers of various colors, seeds removed and sliced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • A dash of dried oregano
  • A pinch of cayenne pepper
  • A handful of cherry tomatoes, halved

To begin, preheat your grill to medium heat with a slightly divided space - one side with no coals, ensuring the other side is well-lit. This two-temperature system will create the base for our flavorful grilled ratatouille.

Prepare a seasoned, separate zone for vegetables, and a slightly hotter area for searing meats or fish if you’d like to round out the meal.

Over the cooler section of the grill, lay down the onion and garlic. Season with olive oil, salt, pepper, and dried oregano. This will bring out the aroma without burning and will form the foundation for our ratatouille.

Next, add the zucchini, yellow squash, and various bell peppers to the mix. The cool temperature will allow them to soften gently without torching, preserving their freshness and sweetness. Remember to season with another spoonful of olive oil, salt, black pepper, and a touch of oregano. The cayenne pepper will heat things up, adding an underlying, persistent warmth without being too flaming hot.

Once the vegetables have cooked for about 15 minutes in the cooler section, gather the remaining high heat to grill the eggplant and cherry tomatoes. Season the eggplant with salt and pepper, and cook them for about 5 minutes, until tender but still firm to the touch.

In the last few minutes, introduce the cherry tomatoes to the high heat side for added smokiness and color.

Combine the fully-grilled vegetables on the cooler side, in the order listed above. Use this mix to mélange all the different colors together, getting the various flavors to marry and create a dish of vivacious color, reminiscent of a summer sunset.

Finally, let’s present our delightful dish. Plate the ratatouille on a bed of pasta, or on its own atop a lightly dressed leafy greens salad. This unique twist adds a smoky depth not usually found in the traditional oven-baked preparation, with less fat and more flair. Feast your eyes and your palate!

In these grilling days, take that time to relish every minute outdoors. Uncork a bottle of wine, find your summer rhythm, and put your spin on the classic French ratatouille through grilling. By trying out this one-of-a-kind preparation, you’ll create an endearing and rustic dish that’s as sunny and warm as the season that inspires it. Bon appétit!
