Find Your Personal Slice of China: Explore Domestic Chinese Cuisine in Your Own Kitchen


Delight your taste buds and kindle your adventurous spirit by diving into the diverse and delicious world of domestic Chinese cuisine. From the fiery spices of Sichuan Province to the sweet and sour flavors of Guangdong, let your kitchen be your portal to the fascinating realm of Chinese gastronomy. With a blend of traditional techniques and fresh ingredients, you can create your own slice of China with these tantalizing recipes.

1. Ma Po Tofu (麻婆豆腐)

Regarded as one of China’s iconic dishes hailing from Sichuan Province, Ma Po Tofu is a glorious reincarnation of traditionally bold and spicy flavors. This dish’s beauty lies in its simplicity; yet maintains an enchanting dance of textures and colors to captivate your senses.

To prepare Ma Po Tofu, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 200g Soft tofu
  • 100g Ground pork
  • 2 Chilies, thinly sliced
  • 1 Spring onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Szechuan peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon fermented bean paste
  • 1 tablespoon Doubanjiang (Chili bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon Soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon Cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon Sesame oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or a large frying pan, heat some oil, then add the Szechuan peppercorns to infuse the oil with their enticing aroma.
  2. Stir-fry the ground pork until fully cooked, breaking up any clusters. Reserve this in a bowl.
  3. In the same wok, use a little more oil if necessary, then stir-fry the chili and fermented bean pastes until fragrant.
  4. Add a splash of rice wine to release the alcohol and clutter the sensory banquet.
  5. Return the pork to the pan, then mix in the Doubanjiang, soy sauce, cornstarch, and sesame oil. Allow it to simmer and thicken.
  6. Lightly crumble the tofu and place it on top of the saucy pork layer, allowing the heat to evenly distribute throughout.
  7. Boil for a few minutes until the tender tofu soaks up the rich flavors, then taste and adjust with salt if needed.
  8. Finally, garnish with the chopped spring onion and a sprinkle of Szechuan peppercorns. Serve over steamed rice for a perfect blend of textures and flavors.

2. Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)

Kung Pao Chicken, originating from the Shaanxi province, is a classic duck-out-of-water tale that has conquered taste buds all around the globe. The striking contrast between sweet, salty, spicy, and numbing flavors, melded together in a savory sauce, makes this dish a culinary masterpiece.

To make Kung Pao Chicken, gather these ingredients:

  • 500g Chicken, cubed
  • 10 Cashew nuts
  • 2 Green onions, chopped
  • 2 Dry red chilies, deseeded
  • 1 tablespoon Ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Peanuts
  • 1 teaspoon Szechuan peppercorns
  • 3 tablespoons Hunt’s Manchurian Sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon Cornstarch
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Drench the chicken cubes in cornstarch, then deep-fry them until golden brown. Set them aside.
  2. In the same oil, stir-fry the ginger, garlic, green onions, and dry chilies until fragrant.
  3. Add the fried chicken, cashews, peanuts, and Szechuan peppercorns to mix into the delightful aroma.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the Manchurian sauce and soy sauce, then pour it over the ingredients in the wok.
  5. Toss and coat until all pieces are drenched in the tangy sauce, ensuring a harmonious balance of flavors.
  6. Plate up the Kung Pao Chicken and sprinkle sesame seeds and chopped peanuts for a touch of elegance. Bon Appétit!

3. Hotpot (火锅)

Hotpot is a popular all-you-can-eat style of meal that encourages social bonding and close interactions through cooking. Savor the charms of different provinces, all blending into an epic feast at your fingertips.

Ingredients for Taiwanese Beef Hotpot (牛肉炖豆腐):

  • 200g Sliced beef
  • 1 block of Bean curd
  • A handful of Enoki mushrooms
  • 1 Daikon radish
  • 1 bunch of Leafy greens, e.g., bok choy
  • 1 Pineapple chunk
  • 1 tablespoon Potato starch
  • 2 cups of Broth (beef or chicken)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the broth by bringing it to a gentle simmer.
  2. Dip each slice of beef in potato starch, then add to the broth for a mouthwatering sear. Collect the cooked meat and place them on skewers or in a soup bowl. Repeat until all slices are cooked.
  3. Dice the daikon radish and add them into the simmering broth, allowing their flavors to infuse.
  4. Carefully drop the enoki mushrooms, bean curd, and pineapple chunks into the broth, cooking for a few minutes until tender.
  5. Lastly, add the leafy greens for just over a minute, carefully stirring them into the aromatic broth.
  6. Plate up your hotpot with a little bit of everything, sip on the soothing broth, and indulge your senses into a delightful feast.

These flavorsome dishes offer but a glimpse of the culinary pleasures China has to offer, all in the comforts of your own kitchen. Each bite unravels an uncharted feel, aroma, and taste, allowing you to find and relish in your personal slice of China. So, grab your steaming wok, ignite your spirit, and let the adventure unfold on this scrumptious voyage!
