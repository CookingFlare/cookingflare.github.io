Slow Cooker Balsamic Glazed Pork Tenderloin: Sweet and Tangy


Are you looking for a delicious and easy-to-make meal that will impress your friends and family? Look no further than this Slow Cooker Balsamic Glazed Pork Tenderloin recipe! With its sweet and tangy flavors, tender meat, and effortless preparation, this dish is sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork tenderloin
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, dried thyme, black pepper, and salt. Set aside.

  2. Trim any excess fat from the pork tenderloin and place it in the slow cooker. Pour the balsamic glaze mixture over the pork, ensuring it is evenly coated.

  3. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or on high heat for 3-4 hours. The pork should be tender and easily shredded with a fork.

  4. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the pork tenderloin from the slow cooker and place it on a cutting board. Let it rest for a few minutes.

  5. While the pork is resting, pour the remaining liquid from the slow cooker into a saucepan. Bring the liquid to a boil over medium-high heat and let it simmer until it reduces and thickens into a glaze-like consistency, about 5-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

  6. While the glaze is simmering, use two forks to shred the pork tenderloin into bite-sized pieces. Place the shredded pork back into the slow cooker and pour the thickened glaze over it. Stir to coat the pork evenly.

  7. Serve the Slow Cooker Balsamic Glazed Pork Tenderloin hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. This dish pairs well with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a side salad.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, marinate the pork tenderloin in the balsamic glaze mixture for 1-2 hours before cooking. This will enhance the taste and tenderness of the meat.
  • If you prefer a thicker glaze, you can mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water and add it to the simmering liquid. Stir well and continue simmering until the glaze reaches the desired consistency.
  • Feel free to experiment with additional spices and herbs to customize the flavor profile of the dish. Rosemary, thyme, or even a pinch of red pepper flakes can add a unique twist to the glaze.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The pork can be reheated in the microwave or on the stovetop, and the glaze can be reheated separately and poured over the pork before serving.

Enjoy this Slow Cooker Balsamic Glazed Pork Tenderloin recipe for a mouthwatering and hassle-free meal that will leave everyone craving for more. Its sweet and tangy flavors, combined with the tender and juicy pork, make it a standout dish that is perfect for any occasion.
