Savory Pozole: A Hearty Mexican Soup for the Soul


Pozole, a traditional Mexican soup, is a staple in many Mexican households, particularly during special occasions and celebrations. This hearty, comforting dish is a perfect remedy for a chilly day, and its rich flavors and aromas will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico. In this recipe, we’ll explore the authentic flavors of pozole, a savory and satisfying soup that’s sure to become a favorite in your household.

The Essence of Pozole

Pozole is a slow-cooked soup made with hominy, a type of corn that’s been soaked in limewater to remove the hulls, leaving behind a tender and slightly sweet corn. The soup is typically flavored with a combination of spices, including oregano, garlic, and chili peppers, which give it a depth of flavor that’s unmatched. The addition of shredded chicken or pork adds protein and texture, making it a filling and satisfying meal.

Recipe: Savory Pozole

** Servings: 6-8 **

** Ingredients: **

  • 2 cups dried hominy, rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup diced radishes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Tortilla chips, for serving
  • Crumbly queso fresco, for serving (optional)

** Instructions: **

  1. Rinse the hominy and soak it in water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Drain and rinse the hominy again.
  2. In a large pot, combine the hominy, chicken broth, chicken, onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 1 1/2 hours, or until the hominy is tender.
  3. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Use two forks to shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Return the pot to the stovetop and simmer for an additional 30 minutes, or until the soup has thickened slightly.
  4. Stir in the vegetable oil, then taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  5. Ladle the pozole into bowls and top with shredded cabbage, diced radishes, and chopped cilantro. Serve with lime wedges, tortilla chips, and crumbly queso fresco, if desired.

Variations and Tips

  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the chicken with roasted sweet potatoes or carrots.
  • To add extra flavor, add 1-2 tablespoons of tomato paste or 1-2 cups of diced tomatoes during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  • Pozole can be made ahead and refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 2 months. Reheat the soup over low heat, adding more broth if necessary to achieve the desired consistency.
  • Experiment with different types of hominy, such as white or yellow, for varying levels of sweetness and texture.
  • Pozole is a versatile soup that can be served as a main course, side dish, or even as a base for other soups and stews.

With its rich flavors and comforting warmth, pozole is a dish that’s sure to become a staple in your household. Whether you’re looking for a hearty meal to warm your belly or a flavorful soup to share with friends and family, this savory Mexican soup is the perfect choice. So gather your ingredients, simmer the pot, and let the aromas of this delicious pozole transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico.
