Spring Flavors: Bright and Fresh Farm to Table Recipes


Spring is a time of renewal, and what better way to celebrate the season than by embracing the bright and fresh flavors that come straight from the farm to your table. As the weather warms and the earth awakens, a bounty of seasonal produce becomes available, inspiring a whole new array of culinary possibilities. From vibrant salads to light and flavorful main dishes, here are some farm to table recipes that will bring the essence of spring to your kitchen.

1. Asparagus and Arugula Salad

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 5 minutes Total Time: 20 minutes Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 4 cups fresh arugula
  • 1/4 cup shaved Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot of salted boiling water, blanch the asparagus for 2-3 minutes, then transfer to an ice bath to stop the cooking process.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the arugula, shaved asparagus, Parmesan cheese, and pine nuts.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon zest, lemon juice, and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat. Serve immediately.

2. Lemon Herb Roast Chicken

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 1 hour Total Time: 1 hour and 10 minutes Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Rinse the chicken and pat dry with paper towels. Season the cavity with salt and pepper, then stuff it with the lemon slices and half of the minced garlic.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the remaining garlic, thyme, rosemary, and olive oil. Rub the mixture all over the chicken, including under the skin.
  4. Place the chicken on a roasting pan and roast for 15 minutes. Reduce the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue roasting for another 45 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Let the chicken rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve with your favorite spring vegetables.

3. Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 40 minutes Total Time: 55 minutes Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 2 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the strawberries, rhubarb, granulated sugar, and cornstarch. Toss to coat the fruit evenly, then transfer to a baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, mix the oats, flour, brown sugar, melted butter, and cinnamon until crumbly. Sprinkle the mixture over the fruit.
  4. Bake for 40 minutes, or until the fruit is bubbly and the topping is golden brown.
  5. Let the crisp cool for a few minutes before serving. Top with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream if desired.

These recipes capture the essence of spring with their use of fresh, seasonal ingredients straight from the farm. Embrace the flavors of the season and elevate your cooking with these bright and delicious farm to table dishes. Whether you’re hosting a spring gathering or simply enjoying a meal at home, these recipes are sure to bring the essence of spring to your table. Happy cooking!
