Best BBQ Recipes for Your Gas Grill


There’s something truly magical about firing up a gas grill, feeling the heat, and smelling the tantalizing aroma of smoky, perfectly cooked meat and veggies. Whether you’re hosting a weekend backyard party or just craving a flavorful meal, mastering the best BBQ recipes on your gas grill will elevate your cooking game and impress your friends and family.

In this post, we’ll dive deep into an array of mouthwatering recipes tailored specifically for gas grilling, covering everything from juicy steaks and tender chicken to vibrant veggie options and irresistible sides. Get ready to turn your gas grill into the ultimate culinary playground.

Why Gas Grilling?

Before jumping into the recipes, let’s acknowledge why gas grills are such a popular choice. They provide:

  • Convenience: Instant heat with the push of a button.
  • Temperature Control: Precise flame adjustments mean perfect doneness.
  • Clean Burning: Less smoke compared to charcoal, but you can add wood chips for flavor.

This makes gas grills ideal for both beginners and seasoned grill masters wanting consistent results.

Getting Started: Prep Tips for Best Results

Great BBQ starts with proper preparation:

  • Preheat Your Grill: Aim for around 400–450°F (or higher for searing). This ensures a good char without sticking.
  • Oil the Grates: Brush lightly with oil to prevent sticking.
  • Marinate or Season: Marinating adds flavor and can tenderize; dry rubs create fabulous crusts.
  • Let Meat Rest: Post-grilling rest of 5–10 minutes locks in juices.

Alright, now onto the recipes!

1. Perfectly Grilled Ribeye Steak with Garlic-Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1-inch thick)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Season the Steaks: Pat dry ribeyes, rub both sides with olive oil, then generously season with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat the Grill: Set burners to high and close the lid until hot.
  3. Grill the Steaks: Place steaks on the grill; cook 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare. Avoid pressing down on the steaks.
  4. Make Garlic-Herb Butter: In a bowl, mix softened butter, garlic, parsley, and thyme.
  5. Rest and Serve: Remove steaks, tent loosely with foil, and let rest 5 minutes. Top with a dollop of herb butter before serving.

Tip: Use a meat thermometer to check for 130°F internal temperature for medium-rare.

2. Sweet and Spicy BBQ Chicken Thighs

Ingredients:

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite kind)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust for spice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season Chicken: Combine paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper; rub into chicken thighs.
  2. Preheat Grill: Medium-high heat, around 375–400°F.
  3. Grill Thighs: Place skin side down, cook 7 minutes, then flip.
  4. Apply Sauce Mix: Mix BBQ sauce and honey; brush over chicken after flipping.
  5. Continue Cooking: Grill for an additional 7-10 minutes, basting occasionally until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Rest Before Serving: Let rest for 5 minutes for juiciness.

A great combination of sweet, smoky, and heat that pairs well with cold slaw or grilled corn.

3. Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers (Vegetarian Favorite)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional toppings: Swiss cheese, avocado, lettuce, tomato, burger buns

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Marinade: Whisk balsamic vinegar, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate Mushrooms: Place mushroom caps in a shallow dish, pour marinade over, and let sit 20-30 minutes.
  3. Preheat Grill: Medium heat, about 350–375°F.
  4. Grill Mushrooms: Cook mushrooms caps 5-6 minutes per side, brushing with marinade occasionally.
  5. Assemble Burger: Place grilled mushrooms on buns with toppings of choice.

Juicy, flavorful, and a hearty vegetarian alternative that even meat eaters love.

4. Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon and Honey Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Glaze: Mix honey, cinnamon, and melted butter.
  2. Preheat Grill: Medium heat, 350°F.
  3. Grill Pineapple Rings: Place rings on grill, cook around 3 minutes per side until grill marks appear.
  4. Brush Glaze: During grilling, brush pineapple with honey glaze.
  5. Serve Warm: Great as a dessert or side dish.

Sweet and smoky fruit notes highlight summer grilling perfectly.

5. Classic Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears fresh corn, husks removed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix Butter: Combine softened butter, chili powder, lime zest, lime juice, and salt.
  2. Preheat Grill: Medium heat, 350°F.
  3. Grill Corn: Place corn directly on grill, turning every 3-4 minutes until charred in spots (about 15 minutes).
  4. Serve: Slather with chili lime butter right after grilling; garnish with chopped cilantro.

Zesty and buttery, these grilled cobs brighten any BBQ table.

6. Beer Can Chicken: Ultimate Juicy and Flavorful Whole Bird

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 lbs)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons BBQ dry rub (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can of beer (12 oz)

Instructions:

  1. Prep Chicken: Remove giblets, pat dry. Rub olive oil all over and then the BBQ rub.
  2. Open Beer Can: Pour out (or drink!) half the beer and place can on stable surface.
  3. Position Chicken: Sit the chicken upright on the beer can (the cavity fits over the can).
  4. Preheat Grill: Medium indirect heat, 375°F.
  5. Grill Chicken: Place chicken on grill away from direct flame, cover grill, and cook 1 to 1.5 hours or until internal temp reaches 165°F.
  6. Remove and Rest: Carefully take off beer can and let chicken rest 10 minutes before carving.

This method steams the bird internally while crisping the skin. A guaranteed wow at your next cookout.

7. Smoky Grilled Shrimp Skewers with Lemon-Garlic Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • Wooden skewers (soaked for 30 mins)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate Shrimp: Mix olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Toss shrimp and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat Grill: Medium-high heat, 400°F.
  3. Skewer Shrimp: Thread shrimp onto skewers.
  4. Grill: Cook shrimp 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  5. Serve Immediately: Garnish with fresh parsley or lemon wedges.

Quick, flavorful, and perfect as an appetizer or light entree.

Bonus Tips for Mastering BBQ on a Gas Grill

  • Use Wood Chips for Smoke: Wrap soaked wood chips (hickory, applewood) in foil with holes, place over burner to add smoky flavor.
  • Keep the Lid Closed: Retains heat and enhances smoky flavor.
  • Invest in a Good Meat Thermometer: Precision matters to avoid over or undercooking.
  • Clean Your Grill Grates: Prevents sticking and flare-ups, and ensures better grill marks.
  • Experiment with Rubs and Sauces: Customize flavor profiles to suit your taste buds.

Final Thoughts

The versatility of a gas grill opens up countless opportunities to create delicious BBQ dishes effortlessly. From the smoky notes of perfectly seared steaks to the tangy sweetness of grilled pineapple, these recipes embrace the essence of outdoor cooking while showcasing how accessible and rewarding gas grilling can be.

Bookmark this collection, grab your apron, and get ready to impress your guests with incredible flavors that only the grill can achieve.

Happy grilling!
