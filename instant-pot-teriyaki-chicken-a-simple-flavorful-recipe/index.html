Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken: A Simple, Flavorful Recipe


If you’re looking for a quick, delicious, and savory meal that the entire family will enjoy, look no further than Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken. This dish brings together the perfect balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors, all thanks to a rich and flavorful homemade teriyaki sauce. The beauty of making this dish in an Instant Pot is that it’s incredibly easy and quick—ideal for busy weeknights, meal prepping, or any time you’re craving a comforting, flavorful meal. Whether you’re an experienced cook or just starting to explore the world of pressure cooking, this recipe is foolproof and guaranteed to impress.

Why Choose Instant Pot for Teriyaki Chicken?

The Instant Pot is a kitchen game-changer, providing the convenience of cooking in a fraction of the time it would take using traditional methods. With its multi-functionality, you can sauté, pressure cook, steam, and slow cook all in one pot, making it incredibly versatile. When it comes to dishes like Teriyaki Chicken, pressure cooking ensures the chicken is perfectly tender and juicy, while the flavors from the sauce have enough time to infuse every bite.

By making the sauce and chicken together in the Instant Pot, you cut down on cleanup and time spent in the kitchen, all while getting a delicious, flavorful meal. Plus, the Instant Pot locks in moisture, ensuring that your chicken turns out incredibly tender and infused with the perfect amount of savory sweetness.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken recipe, you’ll need a few essential ingredients that work together to create that classic, beloved teriyaki flavor. Here’s what you need to get started:

For the Chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs if you prefer dark meat)
  • Salt and pepper (to season the chicken)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or another cooking oil of your choice)

For the Teriyaki Sauce:

  • ½ cup soy sauce (for a gluten-free version, use tamari or coconut aminos)
  • ¼ cup brown sugar (light or dark, depending on your preference)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar (adds a slight tang)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (for extra sweetness and richness)
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (for flavor depth)
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger (gives the sauce that signature sharp warmth)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (to thicken the sauce)
  • 1 cup chicken broth (or water, but broth adds more flavor)
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil (for an added layer of depth and aroma)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 2-3 green onions (for garnish)

Optional Sides (to serve):

  • Cooked white rice or brown rice
  • Steamed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, or snow peas work great)

How to Make Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken

Now that you’ve gathered all your ingredients, it’s time to cook! Follow these easy steps to prepare your Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken:

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by seasoning your chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides. This will enhance the natural flavor of the chicken and allow the teriyaki sauce to shine. You can also choose to cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces, which will allow the sauce to coat the chicken more thoroughly, but it’s completely optional.

Step 2: Sauté the Chicken

Turn your Instant Pot to the ‘Sauté’ mode and allow it to heat up. Add one tablespoon of olive oil to the pot, and once it’s hot, add your chicken breasts. Sauté the chicken for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown. You don’t need to cook the chicken all the way through at this point, as it will finish cooking in the pressure cooking process.

Once the chicken is browned, remove it from the Instant Pot and set it aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Teriyaki Sauce

In a separate bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, honey, garlic, ginger, and chicken broth until everything is well combined. The combination of sweet, salty, and tangy flavors is what makes the teriyaki sauce so irresistible.

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Chicken

Place the chicken breasts back into the Instant Pot, and pour the prepared teriyaki sauce over the top of the chicken. Close the lid of the Instant Pot and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select the ‘Manual’ or ‘Pressure Cook’ function and set the timer for 8 minutes on high pressure.

While the chicken cooks, the sauce will infuse the chicken, making it tender and flavorful.

Step 5: Quick Release and Thicken the Sauce

Once the Instant Pot finishes cooking, carefully do a quick release by turning the valve to “Venting.” This will release the steam quickly and safely.

After releasing the pressure, remove the chicken breasts from the pot and set them aside. To thicken the sauce, switch the Instant Pot back to the ‘Sauté’ mode. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and a few tablespoons of cold water until it forms a slurry. Add the slurry to the pot and stir the sauce until it thickens, which should only take a few minutes.

Step 6: Shred the Chicken (Optional)

Once the sauce has thickened, you can choose to shred the chicken breasts with two forks or slice them into strips. If you decide to shred the chicken, it will absorb more of the flavorful teriyaki sauce, making each bite more flavorful.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

To serve, place the chicken on a plate or bowl over a bed of freshly cooked rice, and pour the thickened teriyaki sauce over the top. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an extra touch of flavor and texture.

If you’re serving with vegetables, add steamed broccoli, carrots, or snow peas as a side for a well-rounded, nutritious meal.

Tips for the Perfect Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken

1. Use Chicken Thighs for Extra Flavor:

While chicken breasts work wonderfully in this recipe, chicken thighs are another great option if you prefer dark meat. Thighs are a bit more forgiving and will stay moist and tender during the cooking process.

2. Adjust the Sweetness:

If you prefer a sweeter teriyaki sauce, you can add extra honey or brown sugar to taste. On the flip side, if you like your sauce to be less sweet, reduce the amount of sugar or honey in the recipe.

3. Add More Vegetables:

This recipe is versatile, and you can easily add vegetables like bell peppers, mushrooms, or onions to the Instant Pot along with the chicken. This adds even more flavor and makes the dish more filling.

4. Meal Prep with Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken:

This recipe is perfect for meal prepping. Make a large batch of Teriyaki Chicken and divide it into meal prep containers. It stores well in the refrigerator for 3-4 days, and you can quickly reheat it for a quick lunch or dinner throughout the week.

5. Gluten-Free Option:

For those with dietary restrictions, it’s easy to make this recipe gluten-free by swapping the soy sauce with tamari or coconut aminos. These alternatives provide a similar salty and umami flavor without the gluten.

6. How to Store Leftovers:

If you have leftovers, simply store the chicken and sauce in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The flavor actually improves as it sits! You can reheat the chicken in the microwave or on the stovetop.

7. Freezing for Later:

This Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken also freezes well. Simply let the chicken cool completely, then store it in a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. To reheat, thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop.

Why You’ll Love Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken

1. Quick and Easy:

This recipe takes only about 30 minutes from start to finish. The pressure cooking process allows the chicken to cook quickly and remain tender without hours of simmering.

2. Flavorful:

The homemade teriyaki sauce gives the chicken an authentic flavor that’s far superior to store-bought options. The combination of soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and honey creates a perfectly balanced sauce that’s both savory and slightly sweet.

3. Versatile:

Whether you’re serving it over rice, with noodles, or alongside roasted vegetables, this dish is incredibly versatile and can be adapted to suit any meal.

4. One-Pot Meal:

One of the biggest perks of using the Instant Pot is that it’s a one-pot meal. You don’t have to worry about dirtying multiple pans or baking dishes—everything is made in the Instant Pot, cutting down on cleanup time.

5. Healthy Option:

Chicken is a lean source of protein, and when paired with vegetables and rice, it creates a well-balanced meal. You can even opt for brown rice or cauliflower rice to make the dish even healthier.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Teriyaki Chicken is a perfect dish for busy weeknights when you want a flavorful, comforting meal without spending hours in the kitchen. The tender chicken, savory-sweet sauce, and simple preparation make this recipe a family favorite. Plus, with the Instant Pot, the entire dish comes together quickly, leaving you with more time to relax or enjoy other activities. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or meal prepping for the week, this dish is guaranteed to be a hit. Give it a try and enjoy a bowl of delicious, homemade teriyaki chicken tonight!
