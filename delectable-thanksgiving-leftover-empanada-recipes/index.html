Delectable Thanksgiving Leftover Empanada Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time of indulgence, with tables groaning under the weight of roasted turkeys, creamy mashed potatoes, and rich pumpkin pies. But what happens when the feast is over, and you find yourself with an abundance of Thanksgiving leftovers? Fear not! I have just the solution for you: Delectable Thanksgiving Leftover Empanada Recipes.

Why Empanadas?

Empanadas are a versatile and delicious way to repurpose your Thanksgiving leftovers. These savory turnovers, with their crispy crusts and flavorful fillings, can be made with just about any ingredient you have on hand. Plus, they’re easy to make and perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Turkey and Cranberry Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1/2 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1 cup stuffing
  • 1/2 cup gravy
  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the shredded turkey, cranberry sauce, stuffing, and gravy. Mix well.
  3. Roll out the pie crusts and cut out circles using a round cookie cutter or a glass.
  4. Spoon a generous amount of the turkey mixture onto one half of each circle.
  5. Fold the other half of the circle over the filling and crimp the edges with a fork to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy!

Sweet Potato and Marshmallow Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the mashed sweet potatoes, mini marshmallows, brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Roll out the pie crusts and cut out circles using a round cookie cutter or a glass.
  4. Spoon a dollop of the sweet potato mixture onto each circle.
  5. Fold the other half of the circle over the filling and crimp the edges with a fork to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the marshmallows are gooey.
  8. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Green Bean Casserole Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover green bean casserole
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the leftover green bean casserole and shredded cheddar cheese.
  3. Roll out the pie crusts and cut out circles using a round cookie cutter or a glass.
  4. Spoon a generous amount of the green bean mixture onto one half of each circle.
  5. Fold the other half of the circle over the filling and crimp the edges with a fork to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy!

Get Creative!

These are just a few ideas to get you started, but don’t be afraid to get creative and come up with your own Thanksgiving leftover empanada recipes. Whether you’re using leftover mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or even pumpkin pie filling, there’s no limit to the delicious combinations you can create.

So, next time you find yourself with a fridge full of Thanksgiving leftovers, don’t let them go to waste. Whip up a batch of delectable Thanksgiving leftover empanadas and enjoy a whole new meal that’s sure to delight your taste buds. Your Thanksgiving feast doesn’t have to end on Thanksgiving Day – it can continue with every savory bite of these scrumptious empanadas. Happy cooking!
