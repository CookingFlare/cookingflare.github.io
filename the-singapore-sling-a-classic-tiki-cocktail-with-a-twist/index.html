The Singapore Sling: A Classic Tiki Cocktail with a Twist


If you’re a fan of tropical flavors and refreshing cocktails, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to dive into the world of tiki cocktails and explore a classic favorite with a unique twist – The Singapore Sling.

Introduction to the Singapore Sling

The Singapore Sling is a classic cocktail that originated in the early 20th century at the Raffles Hotel in Singapore. It was created by bartender Ngiam Tong Boon and quickly gained popularity for its vibrant flavors and exotic appeal. Traditionally, the Singapore Sling is made with gin, cherry brandy, Cointreau, pineapple juice, lime juice, and grenadine, all mixed together to create a harmonious blend of sweet, sour, and fruity notes.

Adding a Twist to the Classic

While the original Singapore Sling is undeniably delicious, we’re going to put a unique twist on this classic recipe to give it an even more exciting flavor profile. Instead of using traditional grenadine, we’ll be incorporating a homemade hibiscus syrup. This addition will not only add a stunning pink hue to the cocktail but also infuse it with a delicate floral note that complements the tropical flavors perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz gin
  • 1 oz cherry brandy
  • 1 oz Cointreau
  • 4 oz pineapple juice
  • 1 oz lime juice
  • 1 oz homemade hibiscus syrup (recipe below)
  • Fresh pineapple slices and maraschino cherries, for garnish
  • Ice cubes

Homemade Hibiscus Syrup

To prepare the homemade hibiscus syrup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup dried hibiscus flowers

In a small saucepan, combine the water, sugar, and dried hibiscus flowers. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar has dissolved completely. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and let the syrup cool before straining out the hibiscus flowers.

Instructions

  1. In a cocktail shaker, combine the gin, cherry brandy, Cointreau, pineapple juice, lime juice, and homemade hibiscus syrup.
  2. Fill the shaker with ice cubes and shake vigorously for about 15-20 seconds to chill the ingredients and mix them thoroughly.
  3. Strain the mixture into a tall glass filled with ice cubes.
  4. Garnish the cocktail with fresh pineapple slices and maraschino cherries for an extra touch of tropical flair.
  5. Serve the Singapore Sling immediately and enjoy the refreshing burst of flavors.

Final Thoughts

The Singapore Sling is a true tropical delight that transports you to a sunny paradise with every sip. By adding the homemade hibiscus syrup, you elevate this classic tiki cocktail to a whole new level of sophistication and intrigue. The floral notes of the hibiscus perfectly complement the fruity flavors, creating a unique and unforgettable taste experience.

So why not gather your friends, put on some island music, and mix up a batch of Singapore Slings? Cheers to the perfect blend of tradition and innovation, and enjoy the refreshing taste of this classic tiki cocktail with a delightful twist!
