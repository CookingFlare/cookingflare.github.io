Grilled Margherita Pizza: Simple and Flavorful


If you’re a fan of classic Italian flavors and love the idea of firing up the grill for a delicious meal, then this Grilled Margherita Pizza recipe is perfect for you. This simple yet flavorful dish combines the smoky char of the grill with the fresh and vibrant flavors of tomatoes, basil, and mozzarella. Whether you’re hosting a summer get-together or simply craving a homemade pizza with a twist, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and your guests.

Ingredients

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 3 1/2 - 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For the Toppings:

  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 1-2 large tomatoes, thinly sliced
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 1/2 cups of flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a dough begins to form.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, adding more flour as needed until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

Step 2: Preheat the Grill

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, around 400-450°F (200-230°C).

Step 3: Shape the Dough

  1. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions.
  2. On a floured surface, roll each portion of dough into a circle, about 1/4 inch thick, and transfer to a lightly floured baking sheet.

Step 4: Grill the Dough

  1. Brush one side of each dough circle with olive oil and place them, oiled side down, directly onto the grill grates.
  2. Grill for 2-3 minutes, or until the dough begins to bubble and grill marks appear.
  3. Brush the tops with olive oil, then carefully flip the dough over using tongs.

Step 5: Add the Toppings

  1. Spread marinara sauce over the grilled side of the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  2. Arrange the sliced tomatoes and mozzarella on top of the sauce. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Close the grill and cook for an additional 4-5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted, and the crust is golden brown.

Step 6: Finish and Serve

  1. Remove the pizzas from the grill and sprinkle with fresh basil leaves.
  2. Let them cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a crispy crust, you can grill the dough for a few minutes on each side before adding the toppings.
  • Feel free to add other toppings such as prosciutto, arugula, or a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a personalized touch.
  • If you don’t have access to a grill, you can also bake the pizzas in a preheated oven at 475°F (245°C) for 10-12 minutes.

Final Thoughts

Grilled Margherita Pizza is a delightful twist on the classic pizza, and its simplicity allows the fresh flavors to shine through. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is approachable and rewarding. Impress your family and friends, or simply treat yourself to a taste of Italy right in your backyard. Enjoy the smoky, savory, and satisfying experience of Grilled Margherita Pizza!
