Mouthwatering Cacio e Pepe with Grilled Vegetables: A Simple and Elegant Dish


If there’s one dish that perfectly encapsulates the beauty of Italian cuisine, it’s Cacio e Pepe. This simple yet elegant pasta dish has been delighting taste buds for generations with its rich, creamy sauce and bold, peppery flavor. Today, we’re going to take this classic recipe to the next level by adding a delightful twist – grilled vegetables. This mouthwatering Cacio e Pepe with grilled vegetables is a celebration of flavors and textures, offering a satisfying and wholesome dining experience.

Ingredients

For the Cacio e Pepe:

  • 12 oz (340g) of spaghetti
  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) of finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) of finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 2-3 tablespoons of freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter

For the Grilled Vegetables:

  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 medium yellow squash, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and deseeded
  • 1 red onion, cut into thick slices
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Grill: Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat, ensuring it’s clean and lightly oiled to prevent sticking.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, toss the zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, and red onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Ensure the vegetables are evenly coated.

  3. Grill the Vegetables: Place the prepared vegetables on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and have beautiful grill marks. Once done, transfer the grilled vegetables to a plate.

  4. Cook the Spaghetti: While the vegetables are grilling, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining the spaghetti.

  5. Make the Cacio e Pepe Sauce: In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the freshly ground black pepper and toast it for about 1 minute until fragrant. Then, add 3/4 cup of the reserved pasta water to the pan and bring it to a simmer.

  6. Combine the Ingredients: Add the cooked spaghetti to the pan and toss to coat it in the pepper-infused water. Reduce the heat to low and add the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheeses. Continuously toss the pasta until the cheeses melt and the sauce becomes creamy, adding more pasta water if needed.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Divide the Cacio e Pepe into serving bowls and top each portion with the grilled vegetables. Finish with an extra sprinkle of cheese and black pepper if desired.

Why Cacio e Pepe with Grilled Vegetables?

The marriage of the creamy, peppery Cacio e Pepe with the smoky, charred flavors of grilled vegetables creates a symphony of taste and texture. The tender-crisp vegetables add a delightful contrast to the velvety pasta, elevating the dish to new heights. Not only does this combination tantalize the palate, but it also provides a vibrant and colorful presentation that’s sure to impress your guests.

Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or a stunning dish to serve at a dinner party, this mouthwatering Cacio e Pepe with grilled vegetables is a versatile and delightful option. It’s a celebration of simplicity and sophistication, showcasing the best of Italian flavors and the bountiful harvest of the grill.

So, the next time you’re craving a comforting yet refined pasta dish, consider infusing it with the smoky allure of grilled vegetables. It’s a simple and elegant way to elevate a beloved classic and create a memorable dining experience for yourself and your loved ones.

With the combination of the timeless allure of Cacio e Pepe and the vibrant charm of grilled vegetables, this dish is a testament to the artistry of Italian cooking. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just beginning your culinary journey, this recipe invites you to savor the harmonious interplay of flavors and textures. Bon appétit!
