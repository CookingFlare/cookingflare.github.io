Afternoon Tea Treats: 9 British Scone Recipes


Few British traditions are as iconic and beloved as afternoon tea, and no spread would be complete without freshly baked scones. Whether you prefer them classic with cream and jam or filled with creative flavors, scones capture the essence of comfort and indulgence. Prepare to elevate your afternoon tea experience with these nine unique and delicious scone recipes. Perfectly soft, crumbly, and irresistible, these treats are sure to impress everyone at your table.

1. Classic All-Butter Scones

No afternoon tea menu is complete without these quintessential classics. Traditional all-butter scones are simple to prepare and highlight the buttery richness that defines the perfect bake. Flour, butter, milk, sugar, and baking powder come together harmoniously for scones that rise with promising golden edges.

Ingredients:

  • 225g self-raising flour
  • 55g cold butter, cubed
  • 150ml milk
  • 25g caster sugar
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 220°C (428°F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. Rub the butter into the flour until you achieve a fine breadcrumb texture.
  3. Stir in sugar and salt before gradually adding milk to form a soft dough.
  4. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to 2cm thickness and cut using a round cutter.
  5. Bake for 12 minutes and enjoy warm with clotted cream and strawberry jam.

2. Cheddar & Chive Savory Scones

Break tradition and opt for a savory twist with these delightful cheddar and chive scones. Perfect for those who prefer a savory bite with their tea, these scones pair wonderfully with fresh herb butter or tangy chutney.

Ingredients:

  • 250g plain flour
  • 50g cold butter, grated
  • 150ml milk
  • 75g mature cheddar, grated
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh chives
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add grated butter and rub until crumbly. Stir in cheddar and chives.
  3. Add milk gradually to form a soft dough.
  4. Shape and cut the dough, then bake at 200°C (392°F) for 15 minutes.

3. Lemon & Poppy Seed Scones

Brighten your afternoon tea with zesty lemon and poppy seed scones. Their refreshing flavor makes them the perfect accompaniment to a piping hot cup of Earl Grey tea.

Ingredients:

  • 250g self-raising flour
  • 50g cold butter
  • 3 tbsp caster sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tsp poppy seeds
  • 150ml milk
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, sugar, lemon zest, and poppy seeds in a bowl.
  2. Rub in the butter until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Mix milk and lemon juice, then combine with dry ingredients to form a dough.
  4. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 12–15 minutes.

4. Triple Chocolate Indulgence Scones

Indulge your sweet tooth with these decadent scones packed with white, milk, and dark chocolate chips. Pair them with a rich hot chocolate for the ultimate treat.

Ingredients:

  • 250g plain flour
  • 50g cocoa powder
  • 75g butter
  • 75g sugar
  • 100g mixed chocolate chips
  • 150ml milk

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, cocoa powder, and sugar before rubbing in the butter.
  2. Fold in chocolate chips and gradually add milk to form a soft dough.
  3. Bake at 180°C (356°F) for 20 minutes.

5. Fruit-packed Cranberry & Orange Scones

Dried cranberries and fresh orange zest create a magical symphony of flavors in this recipe. With their vibrant color and tangy sweetness, these scones are perfect for festive occasions or any time of year.

Ingredients:

  • 250g self-raising flour
  • 50g butter
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 100g dried cranberries
  • 150ml milk

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, baking powder, sugar, and orange zest.
  2. Rub butter into the mix, then fold in cranberries and milk.
  3. Shape, cut, and bake at 200°C (392°F) for 15 minutes.

6. Maple Walnut Scones

Rich, nutty, and slightly sweet, these maple walnut scones offer depth in flavor and pair beautifully with tea, coffee, or even milk. Try drizzling them with a touch of maple syrup for an extra burst of sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 250g plain flour
  • 50g butter
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 75g chopped walnuts
  • 4 tbsp maple syrup
  • 100ml milk

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour and sugar, then rub in butter until crumbly.
  2. Add walnuts, maple syrup, and milk to form dough.
  3. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 12–14 minutes.

7. Rich Toffee Date Scones

For a caramelized sweetness, these toffee and date scones are unparalleled. The chewy texture of dates alongside chunks of buttery toffee creates layers of indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 250g plain flour
  • 50g butter
  • 75g soft brown sugar
  • 50g chopped dates
  • 50g toffee pieces
  • 150ml milk

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour and sugar before rubbing in butter.
  2. Fold in dates, toffee, and milk to create soft dough.
  3. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 15 minutes.

8. Lavender & Honey Scones

Delicate lavender and the sweetness of honey make these scones sophisticated and fragrant. They’re a great choice for elevating your tea party to gourmet status.

Ingredients:

  • 250g plain flour
  • 50g butter
  • 1 tbsp dried lavender
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 150ml milk
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, baking powder, and lavender.
  2. Rub in butter and stir in honey and milk to bind dough.
  3. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 12 minutes.

9. Spiced Apple & Cinnamon Scones

Warm and inviting, these apple and cinnamon scones are perfect for cozy afternoons. The tender chunks of apple and aromatic spices add seasonal charm to any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 250g self-raising flour
  • 50g butter
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 small apple, peeled and diced
  • 150ml milk

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, cinnamon, and sugar, then rub in butter.
  2. Add diced apple and stir in milk to form dough.
  3. Bake at 200°C (392°F) for 15–18 minutes.

Scones are a versatile canvas for flavor exploration, whether you lean towards sweet, savory, classic, or inventive profiles. Each of these nine recipes offers its own charm, making them ideal treats for hosting guests or enjoying a quiet moment of indulgence. Baking your own scones allows you to embrace the time-honored tradition of afternoon tea with a personal touch—and these recipes are your key to baking perfection.
