Mastering the Art of Middle Eastern Grilled Halloumi


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, then you’ve probably encountered halloumi - the delicious, salty cheese that’s perfect for grilling. Halloumi has become increasingly popular in the culinary world, and for good reason. Its unique texture and robust flavor make it a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes. In this blog post, we’re going to delve into the art of grilling halloumi, Middle Eastern style.

Understanding Halloumi

Before we jump into the grilling process, let’s take a moment to understand what halloumi is all about. This semi-hard, brined cheese originated in Cyprus and is traditionally made from a mixture of goat’s and sheep’s milk. It has a high melting point, which makes it perfect for grilling or frying without losing its shape. Halloumi has a distinct salty flavor and a satisfyingly squeaky texture when eaten, making it a unique addition to any dish.

Choosing the Right Halloumi

When it comes to grilling halloumi, the first step is to choose the right kind of cheese. Look for a good quality halloumi that is firm to the touch and has a slightly springy texture. Opt for halloumi that is made from a blend of goat’s and sheep’s milk for an authentic taste.

Preparing the Halloumi

To prepare the halloumi for grilling, start by cutting it into slices, about half an inch thick. You can also cut it into cubes if you prefer. Once sliced, pat the halloumi dry with a paper towel to remove any excess moisture. This will help the halloumi achieve a beautiful golden crust when grilled.

Marinating the Halloumi

While halloumi is delicious on its own, marinating it can take its flavor to the next level. A simple marinade of olive oil, fresh lemon juice, and a pinch of za’atar or sumac can add depth and complexity to the cheese. Let the halloumi marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the cheese.

Grilling the Halloumi

Now, it’s time to fire up the grill. Brush the halloumi slices with a bit of olive oil to prevent them from sticking to the grill grates. Place the halloumi slices on the grill over medium-high heat and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they develop a beautiful golden crust.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled halloumi is incredibly versatile and can be served in a variety of ways. You can enjoy it as a standalone appetizer, drizzled with a bit of honey and sprinkled with fresh herbs. Alternatively, you can incorporate grilled halloumi into salads, sandwiches, or wraps for a delicious and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Grilling halloumi is a delightful way to enjoy this unique cheese, and mastering the art of grilling it Middle Eastern style can elevate your culinary skills to new heights. With its robust flavor and satisfying texture, grilled halloumi is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, fire up the grill, grab some halloumi, and get ready to impress your friends and family with this Middle Eastern culinary masterpiece.
