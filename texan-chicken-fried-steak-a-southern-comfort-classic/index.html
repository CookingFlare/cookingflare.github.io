Texan Chicken Fried Steak: A Southern Comfort Classic


If you’re a fan of Southern comfort food, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to dive into the delicious world of Texan Chicken Fried Steak. This classic dish is a staple in Texas cuisine and is loved for its crispy exterior, tender meat, and flavorful gravy. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the steak:

  • 4 beef cube steaks
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the steak. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  2. Take each cube steak and dredge it in the flour mixture, pressing it firmly to ensure the steak is fully coated. Set the coated steaks aside on a plate.

  3. In a large skillet, heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. You want the oil to be hot enough to sizzle when you add the steak, but not smoking.

  4. Carefully place the coated steaks into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the skillet. Cook the steaks for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. You may need to cook them in batches, depending on the size of your skillet.

  5. Once cooked, transfer the steaks to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Keep the steaks warm while you prepare the gravy.

  6. To make the gravy, melt the butter in a separate saucepan over medium heat. Once melted, sprinkle in the flour and whisk continuously for about 1-2 minutes, or until the mixture turns golden brown.

  7. Slowly pour in the milk while whisking constantly to avoid any lumps. Continue whisking until the gravy thickens and reaches your desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  8. Serve the chicken fried steak hot, topped with a generous ladle of creamy gravy. Pair it with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, green beans, or cornbread for the ultimate Southern comfort meal!

Tips and Tricks

  • For extra tender steak, you can use a meat mallet to pound the cube steaks before coating them in the flour mixture.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, you can increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the flour mixture or add a pinch of chili powder.
  • Don’t rush the frying process. Cooking the steaks on medium-high heat allows them to develop a crispy crust while ensuring the meat stays tender and juicy.
  • Leftover chicken fried steak can be refrigerated and reheated in the oven or toaster oven to retain its crispy texture.

Now that you have this fantastic Texan Chicken Fried Steak recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to gather your ingredients and start cooking. This classic Southern comfort dish is bound to become a family favorite. Enjoy the crispy, flavorful goodness and experience a taste of Texas right in your own kitchen!

Note: This recipe serves four people. Adjust the quantities accordingly for smaller or larger portions.
