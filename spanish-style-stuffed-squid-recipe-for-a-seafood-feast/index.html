Spanish Style Stuffed Squid: Recipe for a Seafood Feast


If you’re a seafood lover looking to impress your guests or simply indulge in a delicious dish, Spanish Style Stuffed Squid is the recipe for you. This mouthwatering dish combines the flavors of tender squid, aromatic herbs, and a rich tomato sauce to create a seafood feast that will leave everyone craving for more. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s dive into this exquisite culinary adventure!

Ingredients

  • 6 large squid tubes
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh oregano
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (14 ounces)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the squid tubes. Rinse them thoroughly under cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel. Set them aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, chopped onion, bell pepper, garlic, parsley, basil, oregano, and Parmesan cheese. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Stuff each squid tube with the rice mixture, filling them about three-quarters full. Be careful not to overstuff, as the rice will expand during cooking.

  4. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the stuffed squid tubes and cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until they turn golden brown. This will add a delicious caramelized flavor to the dish.

  5. Once the squid tubes are browned, remove them from the skillet and set them aside. In the same skillet, add the crushed tomatoes and season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Carefully place the stuffed squid tubes back into the skillet, making sure they are submerged in the tomato sauce. Cover the skillet with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Let the squid simmer in the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are tender and cooked through.

  7. Once the squid is cooked, remove them from the skillet and transfer them to a serving platter. Spoon the tomato sauce over the squid, covering them generously. Garnish with some fresh herbs for an extra burst of flavor and visual appeal.

  8. Serve the Spanish Style Stuffed Squid as a main dish, accompanied by a side of crusty bread or a refreshing salad. This dish pairs perfectly with a glass of chilled white wine, enhancing the flavors of the seafood and herbs.

Conclusion

Spanish Style Stuffed Squid is a seafood lover’s dream come true. The tender squid, combined with the flavorful rice stuffing and rich tomato sauce, creates a harmony of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Spain. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a special meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So, go ahead and embark on this seafood feast, and savor every bite of this delectable dish!
