Korean Stir-Fried Kimchi with Pork: A Tangy and Spicy Delight


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just looking to explore new flavors, then you’re in for a treat with this Korean Stir-Fried Kimchi with Pork recipe. This dish is a perfect blend of tangy, spicy, and savory flavors that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Kimchi, a staple in Korean cuisine, is a fermented vegetable dish, usually made with cabbage and a variety of seasonings. When stir-fried with pork, it creates a mouthwatering dish that is both comforting and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of kimchi, chopped
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by preparing the ingredients. Thinly slice the pork belly and chop the kimchi into bite-sized pieces. Slice the onion, mince the garlic, and chop the green onions.

  2. Marinate the Pork: In a bowl, combine the sliced pork belly with the gochujang, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well to ensure the pork is coated evenly. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  3. Stir-Fry: Heat a tablespoon of cooking oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated pork and stir-fry until it’s cooked through and slightly caramelized. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Add Aromatics: Once the pork is cooked, add the minced garlic and sliced onion to the skillet. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the onions are translucent and fragrant.

  5. Incorporate Kimchi: Now, it’s time to add the star ingredient – the chopped kimchi. Stir-fry the kimchi along with the pork and aromatics for about 5-7 minutes. The kimchi will soften and release its tangy, spicy flavors, creating a delightful aroma.

  6. Seasoning: Season the stir-fry with a teaspoon of sugar, salt, and pepper to taste. The sugar helps to balance the tanginess of the kimchi, while the salt and pepper enhance the overall flavor profile.

  7. Finish with Green Onions and Sesame Seeds: Once the stir-fry is well-combined and the flavors have melded together, sprinkle chopped green onions and sesame seeds over the dish. This adds a fresh, vibrant finish to the stir-fry.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Transfer the Korean Stir-Fried Kimchi with Pork to a serving dish and garnish with extra green onions and sesame seeds if desired. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed rice, providing a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Conclusion

Korean Stir-Fried Kimchi with Pork is a wonderful representation of the bold and robust flavors found in Korean cuisine. The combination of tender pork belly, piquant kimchi, and aromatic seasonings creates a dish that is both comforting and exciting. Whether you’re a kimchi enthusiast or new to Korean flavors, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, grab your wok, and embark on a culinary adventure with this tantalizing dish. Happy cooking and even happier eating!
