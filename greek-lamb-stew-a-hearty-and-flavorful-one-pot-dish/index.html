Greek Lamb Stew: A Hearty and Flavorful One-pot Dish


Are you craving a delicious and comforting meal that will transport you straight to the sunny shores of Greece? Look no further than Greek Lamb Stew! This hearty and flavorful one-pot dish is a true crowd-pleaser, perfect for sharing with family and friends. Packed with tender lamb, aromatic herbs, and a medley of vegetables, this stew is a celebration of Mediterranean flavors. So, let’s dive in and learn how to create this mouthwatering dish!

Ingredients

To make the Greek Lamb Stew, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the lamb chunks and cook until browned on all sides. This step will help to develop rich flavors in the stew. Remove the lamb from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onions become translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the carrots, celery, and red bell pepper to the pot. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables begin to soften.

  4. Return the lamb to the pot and add the diced tomatoes, chicken or vegetable broth, red wine (if using), tomato paste, bay leaves, dried oregano, and dried thyme. Stir everything together to combine.

  5. Season the stew with salt and black pepper to taste. Remember, you can always adjust the seasoning later, so start with a little and add more if needed.

  6. Bring the stew to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for about 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the lamb is tender and the flavors have melded together beautifully.

  7. Once the stew is ready, remove the bay leaves and discard them. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  8. Serve the Greek Lamb Stew hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. This stew pairs perfectly with crusty bread or fluffy rice, allowing you to soak up every last drop of the flavorful broth.

Enjoy a Taste of Greece

With its tender chunks of lamb, vibrant vegetables, and aromatic herbs, Greek Lamb Stew is a true delight for the senses. The long simmering process allows the flavors to develop and intensify, resulting in a dish that will warm both your heart and your belly. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, this stew is sure to impress. So, gather your loved ones, pour a glass of wine, and savor the flavors of Greece with every spoonful. Opa!

Note: This recipe serves 4 to 6 people, but feel free to adjust the quantities based on your needs. Leftovers can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day, as the flavors often deepen and become even more delicious over time.
