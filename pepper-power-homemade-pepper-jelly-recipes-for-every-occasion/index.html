Pepper Power: Homemade Pepper Jelly Recipes for Every Occasion


In the world of homemade preserves, pepper jelly stands out as a versatile and vibrant addition to any pantry. The perfect balance of sweet and spicy, pepper jelly can elevate a simple cracker to a gourmet treat or add a zesty kick to your favorite sandwich. Whether you prefer a mild heat or a fiery kick, there’s a pepper jelly recipe out there for you. Here, we explore three delightful pepper jelly recipes that are sure to impress your taste buds and your guests.

1. Classic Red Pepper Jelly

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups red bell peppers, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  • 6 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1 pouch liquid pectin

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the red bell peppers, jalapeño peppers, sugar, and apple cider vinegar.
  2. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil over high heat, stirring constantly.
  3. Stir in the liquid pectin and return to a boil for exactly 1 minute.
  4. Remove the pot from the heat and skim off any foam.
  5. Pour the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Seal the jars and process them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.

Enjoy this classic red pepper jelly spread on toast or paired with cream cheese and crackers for a delightful appetizer.

2. Mango Habanero Pepper Jelly

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup habanero peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 pouch liquid pectin

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, puree the diced mangoes and habanero peppers until smooth.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the mango-habanero puree, sugar, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring frequently.
  4. Stir in the liquid pectin and boil for an additional minute.
  5. Remove the jelly from the heat and skim off any foam.
  6. Ladle the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes before cooling.

This mango habanero pepper jelly is a tropical delight with a spicy kick. Serve it alongside grilled meats or as a glaze for seafood dishes.

3. Cranberry Jalapeño Pepper Jelly

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cranberries
  • 1/2 cup jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 pouch liquid pectin

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, pulse the cranberries until finely chopped.
  2. In a large pot, combine the chopped cranberries, jalapeño peppers, sugar, and white vinegar.
  3. Bring the mixture to a rapid boil over high heat, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the liquid pectin and boil for 1 minute, continuing to stir.
  5. Skim off any foam from the top of the jelly.
  6. Transfer the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving a small amount of headspace.
  7. Process the jars in a water bath canner for 10 minutes before cooling.

This cranberry jalapeño pepper jelly is a festive addition to holiday gatherings. Pair it with roast turkey or enjoy it as a condiment with cheese platters for a burst of flavor.

Pepper jelly is a delightful way to add a touch of heat and sweetness to your dishes. Whether you prefer the classic red pepper jelly, the tropical twist of mango habanero, or the festive cranberry jalapeño variation, these homemade recipes are sure to impress. Get creative with your pairings and enjoy the flavorful versatility of pepper jelly on every occasion!

Remember, homemade pepper jelly makes a wonderful gift for friends and family, so don’t hesitate to share the love (and the heat) with those around you. Experiment with different pepper combinations and spice levels to find your perfect balance of flavors. Happy jelly-making!
