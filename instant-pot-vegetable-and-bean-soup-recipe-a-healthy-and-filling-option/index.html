Instant Pot Vegetable and Bean Soup Recipe: A Healthy and Filling Option


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing like a warm and comforting bowl of soup to satisfy our cravings. And what’s better than a vegetable and bean soup that’s packed with nutrients and flavor? This Instant Pot recipe is a game-changer, and we’re excited to share it with you.

The Benefits of Vegetable and Bean Soup

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about the benefits of vegetable and bean soup. This type of soup is an excellent source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. The vegetables provide a boost of antioxidants, while the beans offer a good source of plant-based protein. This soup is also low in calories and fat, making it an excellent option for those looking to manage their weight.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Now, let’s get started!

Step 1: Saute the Onion and Garlic

Press the “Saute” button on your Instant Pot and heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Step 2: Add the Vegetables

Add the chopped carrots and celery to the pot and cook until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Canned Goods and Broth

Add the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, black beans, vegetable broth, and thyme to the pot. Stir to combine.

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Soup

Close the lid of the Instant Pot and make sure the valve is set to “Sealing”. Press the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” button and set the cooking time to 20 minutes at high pressure.

Step 5: Let the Pressure Release

When the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.

Step 6: Season and Serve

Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Use any combination of vegetables you like or have on hand. This recipe is very versatile!
  • Add some heat with a diced jalapeno or serrano pepper.
  • Use different types of beans, such as pinto or cannellini.
  • Add some creaminess with a splash of heavy cream or Greek yogurt.
  • Experiment with different spices, such as cumin or paprika.

Conclusion

This Instant Pot vegetable and bean soup recipe is a delicious and nutritious option for a quick and easy meal. With its high fiber and protein content, it’s perfect for a filling and satisfying lunch or dinner. And the best part? It’s ready in under 30 minutes! So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the warm and comforting bowl of goodness.
