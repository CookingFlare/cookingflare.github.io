Vegan Desserts Inspired by International Cuisines


Exploring the world through food is one of the most delightful experiences, and dessert is often the sweet finale to any meal. For those who are vegan or looking to incorporate more plant-based options into their diets, there’s a treasure trove of delicious international desserts that can be adapted to meet vegan standards. In this post, we will embark on a journey through various countries, showcasing vegan dessert recipes that celebrate the rich tapestry of flavors, textures, and traditions from around the globe.

1. Coconut Sticky Rice with Mango (Thailand)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, sliced
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then soak for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the rice and steam it in a bamboo steamer or a steaming basket lined with cheesecloth for about 30 minutes until tender.
  3. While the rice is steaming, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Do not bring it to a boil.
  4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl and pour about 3/4 of the coconut milk mixture over it, mixing well. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  5. To serve, place a portion of the sticky rice on a plate, drizzle with the remaining coconut milk mixture, and top with sliced mango. Garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans.

2. Chia Pudding with Tropical Fruits (Hawaii)

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 2 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup diced tropical fruits (pineapple, mango, banana)
  • Toasted coconut flakes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined.
  2. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight until it thickens.
  3. When ready to serve, give the pudding a good stir and layer it with diced tropical fruits in serving cups.
  4. Top with toasted coconut flakes for added crunch and flavor.

3. Chocolate Avocado Mousse (Mexico)

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup agave syrup or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine ripe avocados, cocoa powder, agave syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Adjust sweetness to your preference by adding more syrup if desired.
  3. Spoon the mousse into serving dishes and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

4. Baklava with Vegan Honey (Turkey)

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (vegan-friendly)
  • 2 cups walnuts, finely chopped
  • 1 cup almonds, finely chopped
  • 1 cup melted coconut oil (or vegan butter)
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish with some melted coconut oil.
  2. In a bowl, mix the chopped walnuts, almonds, cinnamon, and cloves.
  3. Lay one sheet of phyllo dough in the baking dish and brush with melted coconut oil. Repeat this process, layering about 8 sheets.
  4. Sprinkle a layer of the nut mixture over the phyllo. Continue layering with 4 sheets of phyllo and another layer of nuts until all ingredients are used, finishing with phyllo sheets on top.
  5. Cut the baklava into diamond shapes before baking. Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden brown.
  6. In a saucepan, combine maple syrup and water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes. Pour this syrup over the hot baklava as soon as it comes out of the oven and let it soak for a few hours.

5. Gulab Jamun (India)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • Oil for frying
  • For the syrup:
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1 cup water
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • A few drops of rose water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine almond flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and coconut milk to form a dough. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. For the syrup, combine sugar, water, cardamom powder, and rose water in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes, then set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Shape the dough into small balls and fry until golden brown.
  4. Remove the fried balls and immediately soak them in the warm syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.

6. Saffron and Cardamom Rice Pudding (Persia)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups almond milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped pistachios for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the arborio rice under cold water until it runs clear.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, almond milk, sugar, saffron threads, and cardamom powder. Bring to a gentle boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is creamy and cooked through (about 30 minutes).
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving. Garnish with chopped pistachios.

7. Tiramisu (Italy)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup agave syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegan ladyfinger cookies (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine coconut cream, cocoa powder, agave syrup, and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth.
  2. Dip each vegan ladyfinger in the brewed coffee briefly and layer them in a serving dish.
  3. Spread a layer of the coconut cream mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat the layers until you run out of ingredients, ending with the cream on top.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight before serving.

8. Mochi Ice Cream (Japan)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup water
  • Vegan ice cream (your choice of flavor)
  • Cornstarch for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water until smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl with a damp paper towel and microwave for 1 minute. Stir and microwave for another minute. Repeat this step until the mixture is thick and translucent (about 3-4 minutes total).
  3. Dust a clean surface with cornstarch and transfer the mochi dough onto it. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Roll out the dough and cut it into circles, about 3 inches in diameter. Place a small scoop of vegan ice cream in the center of each circle and fold the mochi around it, sealing it well.
  5. Freeze the mochi ice cream for at least 2 hours before serving.

9. Panna Cotta (Italy)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon agar-agar powder
  • Fresh berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, almond milk, sugar, and vanilla extract. Stir and heat until warm.
  2. Sprinkle agar-agar powder over the mixture and whisk to dissolve.
  3. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then remove from heat. Pour into molds and let cool at room temperature.
  4. Once set, refrigerate for at least 2 hours. Serve with fresh berries on top.

10. Aloo Tikki with Sweet Tamarind Chutney (India)

Ingredients:

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • For the chutney:
    • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the mashed potatoes, green peas, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Form small patties (tikkis).
  2. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the tikkis until golden brown on both sides. Remove and place on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
