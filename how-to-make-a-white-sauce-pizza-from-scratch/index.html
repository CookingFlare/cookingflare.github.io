How to Make a White Sauce Pizza from Scratch


Making a white sauce pizza from scratch is a wonderful way to enjoy a fresh and delicious pizza that stands apart from the traditional red sauce variety. This creamy, rich sauce enhances the flavors of various toppings, creating a unique and flavorful dish. Whether you’re a pizza enthusiast or simply looking for something different, this recipe is perfect for those who love experimenting with new flavors.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through each step of making your very own white sauce pizza, from preparing the dough to crafting the creamy white sauce and assembling the pizza. This homemade pizza will not only satisfy your cravings but will also impress your friends and family.

What Is White Sauce Pizza?

White sauce pizza, also known as “bianca pizza,” is a pizza that skips the traditional tomato sauce and uses a creamy, garlicky sauce instead. This sauce is typically made from ingredients like heavy cream, butter, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. The result is a smooth, rich base that pairs beautifully with a variety of toppings, including vegetables, meat, and herbs.

White sauce pizza offers a lighter, more subtle taste compared to its red-sauce counterpart. Its versatility means you can customize it to suit your personal taste and preferences.

Key Ingredients for a White Sauce Pizza

Before we dive into the process of making white sauce pizza, let’s go over the key ingredients you’ll need for both the dough and the white sauce:

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F or 45°C)
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for greasing)

For the White Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional, for extra flavor)

For the Toppings:

  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese (optional, for added creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Sliced mushrooms, onions, spinach, or any vegetables of your choice
  • Cooked chicken, bacon, or other meats (optional)
  • Olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese (for topping)

Step 1: Prepare the Pizza Dough

The first step in making a homemade white sauce pizza is preparing the pizza dough. If you’re using store-bought dough, you can skip this step, but nothing beats the flavor and texture of homemade dough.

  1. Activate the Yeast
    Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves, then sprinkle the yeast over the water. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy and bubbly. If the yeast doesn’t bubble, it may be inactive, and you’ll need to try with fresh yeast.

  2. Mix the Dough
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough
    Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 8-10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much, as this can make the dough too stiff.

  4. Let the Dough Rise
    Grease a large bowl with olive oil and place the dough inside. Cover the bowl with a clean towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm area for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Preheat the Oven
    While the dough is rising, preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you’re using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat as well.

  6. Shape the Dough
    Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Transfer it to a floured surface and roll it out into a 12-inch round shape or your desired size. If you like a thicker crust, leave it slightly thicker. Transfer the shaped dough to a parchment paper or a pizza peel if you’re using a stone.

Step 2: Make the White Sauce

The white sauce is what gives this pizza its distinctive creamy, rich flavor. Making this sauce from scratch is easy and quick, and it adds an incredible depth of flavor to the pizza.

  1. Melt the Butter
    In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once the butter has melted, add the minced garlic and sauté it for about 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Be careful not to let the garlic brown, as this can cause it to become bitter.

  2. Add the Cream and Milk
    Slowly pour in the heavy cream and milk, stirring constantly to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, and let it cook for about 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should begin to thicken slightly.

  3. Stir in the Parmesan
    Gradually add the grated Parmesan cheese to the sauce, stirring continuously until the cheese has fully melted and the sauce is smooth.

  4. Season the Sauce
    Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and nutmeg, if using. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Once it’s to your liking, remove the sauce from the heat.

Step 3: Assemble the White Sauce Pizza

Now that the dough is ready and the white sauce is prepared, it’s time to assemble your pizza.

  1. Spread the White Sauce
    Using a spoon or a small ladle, spread an even layer of the white sauce over the prepared pizza dough. Be sure to leave a small border around the edges for the crust.

  2. Add the Cheese
    Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese over the white sauce. You can also add a few dollops of ricotta cheese for extra creaminess and texture.

  3. Add the Toppings
    Choose your favorite toppings, such as fresh basil, sautéed mushrooms, spinach, onions, and cooked meats. Feel free to get creative and experiment with different combinations. For example, a white sauce pizza with grilled chicken, spinach, and mushrooms is a classic favorite.

  4. Drizzle with Olive Oil
    For an extra layer of flavor, drizzle a small amount of olive oil over the top of the pizza before baking. This will help the crust become golden and crispy while adding a hint of richness.

Step 4: Bake the Pizza

Now that your pizza is assembled, it’s time to bake it.

  1. Bake the Pizza
    Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (or pizza stone if you’re using one). Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly. If you like a crispier crust, bake the pizza a little longer.

  2. Finish with Fresh Parmesan and Basil
    Once the pizza is baked, remove it from the oven and sprinkle it with freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Add a few fresh basil leaves for a burst of freshness and color.

  3. Slice and Serve
    Slice the pizza into wedges and serve immediately. The combination of the rich white sauce, creamy cheese, and flavorful toppings is sure to impress.

Tips for Making the Perfect White Sauce Pizza

  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh mozzarella and high-quality Parmesan cheese make a significant difference in the flavor of your pizza.
  • Experiment with toppings: White sauce pizza is incredibly versatile. Try different combinations of vegetables, meats, and herbs to find your favorite.
  • Use a pizza stone: If you want a crispy crust, use a preheated pizza stone. It helps evenly distribute the heat, resulting in a perfectly baked pizza.
  • Add garlic and herbs to the dough: For an extra burst of flavor, you can mix minced garlic or dried herbs like oregano into the pizza dough before letting it rise.
  • Make extra white sauce: If you’re a fan of creamy sauces, you can make extra white sauce and use it for dipping or as a topping for other dishes.

Why You Should Try White Sauce Pizza

White sauce pizza offers a break from the typical tomato-based pizza, and its creamy texture provides a unique and indulgent experience. Whether you prefer vegetables, meats, or a combination of both, the white sauce enhances the flavors and elevates the toppings. Plus, making this pizza from scratch allows you to control the quality and quantity of each ingredient, ensuring a delicious and satisfying meal.

If you haven’t tried white sauce pizza yet, it’s time to give it a go. With its rich, comforting flavors and endless possibilities for customization, this pizza is sure to become a favorite in your home. Happy baking!
