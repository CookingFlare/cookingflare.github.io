One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta: A Quick and Easy Italian-Inspired Meal


If you’re searching for a delightful dinner option that combines the classic flavors of Italian cuisine with the convenience of a one-pot meal, look no further than One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta. This recipe is not only incredibly easy to prepare but also packs a punch of flavor, making it a perfect weeknight dinner that the whole family will love. With tender chicken, al dente pasta, and a rich, cheesy sauce, this dish is sure to become a staple in your home.

Why One-Pot Meals?

One-pot meals have gained immense popularity for good reason. They save time, reduce cleanup, and allow flavors to meld beautifully as they cook together. In this recipe, the chicken and pasta absorb the savory broth and spices, resulting in a dish that is both hearty and satisfying. Plus, who doesn’t love a meal that can be made in a single pot?

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this mouthwatering One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for a little heat)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 12 oz (340g) penne pasta (or your choice of pasta)
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Step-by-Step Preparation

Step 1: Sauté the Chicken

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the bite-sized chicken pieces and season them with salt and black pepper. Sauté the chicken until it is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

Step 2: Cook the Aromatics

In the same pot, add the finely chopped onion and garlic. Sauté them for about 2-3 minutes, or until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant. This step is crucial as it builds the flavor base for the dish.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

Once the onion and garlic are ready, return the cooked chicken to the pot. Sprinkle in the Italian seasoning and red pepper flakes if you’re using them. Stir everything together to ensure the chicken is well-coated with the spices.

Step 4: Add the Liquids

Pour in the chicken broth and crushed tomatoes, stirring to combine. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil. This is where the magic begins; the chicken will infuse the broth with flavor, and the tomatoes will add a lovely acidity that balances the richness of the cheese.

Step 5: Cook the Pasta

Once boiling, add the penne pasta to the pot. Stir well to ensure that the pasta is submerged in the liquid. Reduce the heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and cook for about 12-15 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

Step 6: Add the Cheese

When the pasta is cooked, remove the pot from heat. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until it melts into the sauce, creating a creamy texture. Then, sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese on top. Cover the pot for a few minutes to allow the mozzarella to melt beautifully.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Once the cheese is melted, give the pasta a gentle stir to combine everything. Serve it hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and freshness. This dish pairs wonderfully with a simple side salad or some garlic bread for a complete Italian feast.

Tips for the Perfect One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta

Choose the Right Pasta

While penne pasta works beautifully in this recipe, feel free to experiment with other types like fusilli, rotini, or even spaghetti. Just be mindful of the cooking times, as some pasta varieties may require adjustments.

Customize Your Dish

One of the best aspects of this one-pot meal is its versatility. You can easily add vegetables like spinach, bell peppers, or zucchini to enhance the nutritional value and add more flavor. Simply toss them in when you add the chicken back to the pot.

Spice It Up

If you enjoy a bit of heat, increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add a dash of your favorite hot sauce. Alternatively, for a milder dish, omit the red pepper flakes altogether.

Make It Ahead

This recipe can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat it gently on the stove, adding a splash of chicken broth to loosen the sauce if needed.

Freezing Leftovers

If you have leftovers, you can freeze them for up to three months. Store the pasta in an airtight container, and when you’re ready to enjoy it again, thaw it overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Nutritional Information

This One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta is not only delicious but also provides a balanced meal. Each serving contains a healthy dose of protein from the chicken, carbohydrates from the pasta, and essential vitamins from the tomatoes and herbs.

Estimated Nutritional Breakdown (per serving):

  • Calories: 550
  • Protein: 35g
  • Carbohydrates: 65g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 3g

Final Thoughts

One-Pot Chicken Parmesan Pasta is the epitome of comfort food, combining the ease of preparation with the rich, satisfying flavors of Italian cuisine. It’s perfect for busy weeknights or when you want to impress guests without spending hours in the kitchen. With just one pot to clean up, you’ll have more time to enjoy your meal and the company of your loved ones.

So, the next time you’re looking for a quick and easy dinner option, remember this recipe. It’s bound to become a favorite in your household, and who knows—maybe you’ll even find yourself making it on repeat! Enjoy this delightful dish, and buon appetito!
