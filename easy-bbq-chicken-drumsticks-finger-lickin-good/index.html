Easy BBQ Chicken Drumsticks: Finger-Lickin' Good


Who doesn’t love the tantalizing, smoky, finger-lickin’ goodness of BBQ chicken drumsticks? Contrary to popular belief, mastering this classic summer cookout staple doesn’t require a dripping wet smoker. All you need are a few simple ingredients and a little know-how. Get ready to delight your tastebuds, as we share our go-to easy BBQ chicken drumsticks recipe.

Ingredients:

  1. 24 - 30 chicken drumsticks
  2. Salt
  3. Smoked paprika
  4. Chicken seasoning of your choice
  5. Olive oil
  6. Your favorite BBQ sauce
  7. Optional: pineapple juice

Instructions:

Step 1: Preparing the Chicken

We start by Pattying our chicken drumsticks with a meat tenderizer to help the seasoning adhere better. Season both sides of the drumsticks with salt, smoked paprika, and chicken seasoning of your choice. Make sure to rub the spices into the meat well to maximize flavor.

Step 2: Olive Oil Bath

Next, we give our seasoned chicken drumsticks a healthy dusting of olive oil for even cooking. Place the seasoned chicken in a large bowl or Ziplock bag and add enough olive oil to lightly coat the drumsticks. Make sure every piece is evenly coated.

Step 3: Resting The Chicken

Let the seasoned drumsticks sit for a while (at least 30 minutes) - pros call this “wetting the meat.” This step allows the salt and spices to be absorbed into the chicken, yielding juicier and more flavorful results.

Step 4: Preheat The Grill

While the chicken is resting, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. We like to use a gas grill with one side set up for smoking with a mixture of wood chips and charcoal. If you don’t have these, no problem - we’ll get to the foil packet trick in a moment. Just make sure the temperature is right; you want it hot enough to cook the chicken through but not so high that it burns.

Step 5: Grill To Perfection

Once the grill is hot, place the olive oil-coated drumsticks directly onto the grates. Be sure the chicken isn’t touching, as this prevents even cooking. Quit fussing and let your chicken grill for about 45 minutes, turning it every 10-15 minutes to encourage even cooking. Ensure the internal temperature reaches at least 165°F (74°C) to kill bacteria and keep your guests safe.

Step 6: Sauce It Up

Now that your drumsticks are cooked, it’s time to glaze them with your favorite BBQ sauce. Feel free to use a store-bought sauce or whip up your own homemade version. You can also mix pineapple juice into your BBQ sauce for a tropical twist. Remember to reserve a portion of your BBQ sauce for later - this doubles as a marinade for future use.

Step 7: Serve It Up

You’ve reached the home stretch, my friends. Remove the drumsticks from the grill, slather them in BBQ sauce, and serve up these finger-lickin’ favorites. Remember to bring extra napkins - this dish is famous for leaving a mess behind.

Give this recipe a go at your next cookout, and watch your friends and family rejoice! From first-timers to seasoned grillmasters, everyone can enjoy the savory delight of homemade BBQ chicken drumsticks. Just remember that practice makes perfect, so don’t be dismayed by any rookie mistakes. Happy grilling!
