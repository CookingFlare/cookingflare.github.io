The Best Spanish Cooking Shows to Watch for Inspiration


Spain is a country synonymous with passion, tradition, and bold flavors that come to life in its culinary heritage. From the vibrant markets of Barcelona to the sun-soaked kitchens of Andalusia, Spanish cuisine represents an extraordinary fusion of history and innovation. Whether you’re a seasoned chef, a culinary enthusiast, or someone simply looking for fresh inspiration, Spanish cooking shows have a way of transporting you straight to the heart of the Mediterranean. Packed with cultural insights, tantalizing recipes, and entertaining hosts, these shows can inspire your next kitchen masterpiece.

Here’s a carefully curated list of the best Spanish cooking shows to binge-watch, guaranteed to expand your horizons and ignite your love for one of the world’s most celebrated cuisines.

1. MasterChef España

An international sensation with Spanish flair, MasterChef España has captivated food lovers for years. The show takes the beloved format of the original series and infuses it with Spain’s unique culinary traditions. Aspiring chefs face off in a series of intense competitions, from recreating tapas classics to modernizing regional dishes like paella or Galician octopus.

The beauty of MasterChef España lies not just in the competitive element but in the knowledge shared along the way. Contestants often collaborate with some of Spain’s top chefs and use ingredients intrinsic to local culture. If you’re looking to learn both technique and how to incorporate authentic Spanish flavors into your dishes, this show is a treasure trove of inspiration.

Pro tip: Pay attention to the behind-the-scenes clips and judges’ critiques—they offer valuable insights into Spanish cooking techniques, ingredient pairings, and plating styles.

2. Cocina Abierta de Karlos Arguiñano

Karlos Arguiñano is a household name in Spain, and his cooking show Cocina Abierta de Karlos Arguiñano is perfect for home cooks looking to master authentic recipes. Unlike more elaborate or competition-based shows, this program emphasizes simple, achievable dishes that don’t skimp on flavor.

Karlos brings a sense of humor and approachability to his demonstrations, making each episode feel welcoming. Whether he’s showcasing techniques for perfect sofrito or revealing the secrets to traditional Basque desserts, Karlos ensures that viewers of every skill level can follow along. His cheerful energy is infectious, and watching his show will inspire you to experiment with ingredients like pimentón, manchego cheese, and sherry vinegar.

Diving into Karlos’s creations is also an excellent way to appreciate the vast culinary diversity of Spain, as he dedicates time to various regional cuisines. On one day, he might be cooking a hearty stew from Asturias, while the next, he’s whipping up a Valencia-inspired dessert.

3. El Xef

Bold, daring, and unrelentingly creative, El Xef is a cooking show that follows the adventures of chef Dabiz Muñoz, a leading figure in Spain’s avant-garde culinary scene. If you’re fascinated by innovative techniques and dramatic presentation, this show will keep you hooked.

Dabiz brings viewers behind the scenes of his three-Michelin-starred restaurant, DiverXO, located in Madrid. However, El Xef also dives into the backstory of Dabiz’s culinary journey, showcasing the struggles and triumphs of running one of the most creative kitchens in the world.

While the focus of the show leans heavily on modern, experimental cuisine, there’s no shortage of inspiration for home cooks. Dabiz’s fearless approach to flavor combinations—such as incorporating Asian influences into traditional Spanish recipes—brings fresh perspectives to the culinary table.

Watching El Xef will inspire you to think outside the box and experiment with ingredients and techniques that you wouldn’t typically consider. It’s an invitation to break the rules of traditional cooking and to celebrate art on a plate.

4. España Directo: Cocina Segment

While not exclusively a cooking show, España Directo dedicates a significant portion of its runtime to culinary explorations, particularly through its “Cocina” segment. Broadcast on RTVE, this show highlights local food traditions and connects viewers with Spain’s rich gastronomic culture.

The “Cocina” segments often feature local chefs preparing seasonal dishes, offering viewers a glimpse into Spain’s deep respect for fresh, high-quality ingredients. Additionally, these segments often travel to different regions of Spain, giving you a window into the specialties of Catalonia, Andalusia, Galicia, and the Basque Country. This regional flair is invaluable if you’re interested in understanding the cultural nuances of Spain’s cuisine.

The recipes featured in España Directo are typically accessible to home cooks, with a focus on using ingredients you can find at most markets. It’s an ideal show for anyone looking to bring the authentic flavors of Spain into their kitchen with minimal fuss.

5. Un País para Comerlo

Un País para Comerlo takes you on an unforgettable journey through Spain’s culinary landscape. The show is as much about food as it is about storytelling—each episode dives deeply into the agricultural, cultural, and historical contexts of Spanish dishes.

Hosted by a rotating cast of celebrity chefs and food experts, the show travels to small villages, bustling cities, and remote countrysides to uncover the traditions behind Spain’s most beloved foods. From family-owned olive groves to artisan cheesemakers, each region is celebrated for its unique contributions to Spanish cuisine.

What sets Un País para Comerlo apart is its focus on sustainability and local sourcing. You’ll learn how traditional Spanish recipes are intrinsically tied to the land and the seasons, offering a more holistic approach to cooking. Watching this show can inspire you to think beyond recipes, encouraging you to explore the origins of your ingredients and connect with local farmers.

6. Robin Food: Atracón a Mano Armada

Looking for a cooking show that’s both entertaining and practical? Robin Food: Atracón a Mano Armada is a delightful program hosted by popular Basque chef David de Jorge. With humor and wit, David makes cooking approachable, emphasizing bold flavors and hearty portions.

The recipes featured on Robin Food are often a blend of traditional and contemporary Spanish dishes with an emphasis on simplicity. From classic seafood paellas to modern tapas ideas, this show is packed with reliable recipes that can easily be recreated at home.

David’s personable style adds an extra layer of engagement, and his anecdotes about the history and culture behind the dishes make the learning process all the more enjoyable. If you love cooking shows that feel like a conversation with an old friend, Robin Food is the perfect choice.

7. La Cocina de Nicola

La Cocina de Nicola is a unique Spanish cooking show that merges traditional techniques with Mediterranean flair. Hosted by chef Nicola Poltronieri, this program showcases how to create restaurant-quality dishes in a home kitchen.

Nicola focuses on fresh ingredients and vibrant presentation, making it an excellent resource for those who want to impress guests with flavorful yet visually stunning creations. The recipes often incorporate staples like olive oil, garlic, tomatoes, and fresh seafood—hallmarks of Spanish and Mediterranean cuisine.

What makes La Cocina de Nicola stand out is its artistic approach to cooking, with an emphasis on balance, flavor, and style. Watching Nicola work his magic in the kitchen will leave you inspired to elevate your own cooking skills.

Why These Shows Matter

Spanish cuisine is more than just food—it’s a gateway to culture, history, and tradition. What makes these cooking shows so valuable is their ability to immerse you in the textures, aromas, and stories behind Spanish gastronomy. More than just recipes, they inspire creativity, ignite a passion for fresh ingredients, and promote a deep appreciation for the art of cooking.

Whether you’re perfecting a classic tortilla española, experimenting with molecular gastronomy, or simply looking to bring new life to your weeknight meals, these Spanish cooking shows will guide you every step of the way. So, grab your apron, tune in, and let the vibrant world of Spanish cuisine inspire your next culinary adventure!
