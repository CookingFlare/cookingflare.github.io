Creating Your Own Hiroshima-Style Okonomiyaki


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve probably heard of okonomiyaki. It’s a savory pancake that’s popular throughout Japan, with each region putting its own spin on the dish. Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is a unique variation that features layers of ingredients, creating a delightful and filling meal. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make your very own Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki right in your kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cabbage, thinly shredded
  • 4 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup tenkasu (tempura scraps)
  • 1/4 cup pickled red ginger (beni shoga)

Additional Toppings:

  • 4 cups cooked yakisoba noodles
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced pork belly
  • 4 eggs
  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Aonori (dried seaweed flakes)
  • Katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, dashi, and eggs. Mix until smooth. Then, add the thinly shredded cabbage, green onions, tenkasu, and pickled red ginger. Mix all the ingredients until well combined.

  2. Cook the Pancake: Heat a large non-stick skillet or teppanyaki grill over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of the batter onto the skillet and spread it into a circle. Cook for a few minutes until the bottom is golden brown.

  3. Layer the Ingredients: Push the pancake to one side of the skillet and add a handful of cooked yakisoba noodles and bean sprouts to the empty side. Place a few slices of pork belly on top of the noodles. Once the noodles are heated through and the pork is cooked, carefully flip the pancake over the ingredients.

  4. Add the Toppings: While the bottom of the pancake finishes cooking, crack an egg directly on top of the pancake and spread it out slightly. Let it cook until the egg is set.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the pancake is cooked through and the egg is set, transfer it to a plate. Drizzle the top with okonomiyaki sauce and Japanese mayonnaise. Sprinkle aonori and katsuobushi on top as a finishing touch.

Tips and Tricks

  • Customize Your Toppings: Feel free to add or substitute any ingredients to suit your taste. Some popular additions include cheese, shrimp, and kimchi.
  • Cooking Technique: Use a spatula to press down on the layers of the pancake as it cooks to ensure everything sticks together.
  • Presentation: The visual appeal is part of the charm of this dish. Arrange the toppings in a visually pleasing manner for an Instagram-worthy okonomiyaki.

Creating your own Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is not only a delicious culinary adventure but also a fun and interactive cooking experience. The layers of flavors and textures make each bite a delightful surprise. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a journey to create this iconic Japanese dish in the comfort of your own kitchen. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy your cravings for a taste of Japan. Enjoy!
