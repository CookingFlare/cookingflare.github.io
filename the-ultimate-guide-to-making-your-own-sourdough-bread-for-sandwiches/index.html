The Ultimate Guide to Making Your Own Sourdough Bread for Sandwiches


Are you tired of store-bought bread that lacks flavor and doesn’t quite satisfy your sandwich cravings? Look no further! In this ultimate guide, we will take you through the step-by-step process of making your very own sourdough bread that is perfect for sandwiches. Get ready to elevate your sandwich game to a whole new level!

Ingredients

Before we dive into the process, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 500g bread flour
  • 350g water
  • 100g sourdough starter
  • 10g salt

Equipment

To make the sourdough bread, you’ll need the following equipment:

  • Mixing bowl
  • Kitchen scale
  • Dough scraper
  • Dutch oven or baking stone
  • Parchment paper
  • Kitchen towel or proofing basket

Step 1: Preparing the Sourdough Starter

The first and most crucial step in making sourdough bread is preparing the starter. If you already have an active starter, skip to step 2. If not, follow these instructions to create your own:

  1. In a clean jar, mix equal parts of flour and water. For example, combine 50g of flour with 50g of water.

  2. Cover the jar loosely with a clean cloth and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours.

  3. After 24 hours, discard half of the mixture and feed the starter with equal parts of flour and water. Repeat this process daily for about a week, or until your starter becomes active and bubbly.

Step 2: Mixing the Dough

Now that you have an active sourdough starter, it’s time to mix the dough. Follow these steps:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, water, and sourdough starter. Mix until all the ingredients are well incorporated.

  2. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes. This allows the flour to hydrate and the gluten to develop.

  3. After 30 minutes, sprinkle the salt over the dough and knead it for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can use a dough scraper to help with the kneading process.

Step 3: Fermentation

Fermentation is a crucial step in sourdough bread-making as it develops the flavor and texture of the bread. Follow these steps:

  1. Place the dough back into the mixing bowl and cover it with a kitchen towel. Let it ferment at room temperature for about 4-6 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  2. Every hour during the fermentation process, perform a series of “stretch and fold” techniques. This helps strengthen the dough and improve its structure.

Step 4: Shaping and Proofing

Once the dough has finished fermenting, it’s time to shape and proof it. Follow these steps:

  1. Dust your work surface with flour and gently transfer the dough onto it. Be careful not to deflate the dough.

  2. Fold the dough in thirds, both horizontally and vertically, to create a tight ball. Use the dough scraper to help with the shaping.

  3. Place the shaped dough into a proofing basket or on a parchment paper-lined baking sheet. Cover it with a kitchen towel and let it proof at room temperature for 2-4 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Baking

The final step is baking the sourdough bread to perfection. Follow these steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). If using a Dutch oven, place it in the oven while preheating.

  2. Once the oven is preheated, carefully transfer the proofed dough onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet or into the preheated Dutch oven.

  3. Score the top of the dough with a sharp knife or razor blade to allow for expansion during baking.

  4. If using a baking sheet, place it in the oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the bread has a golden crust and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. If using a Dutch oven, place the lid on and bake for 20 minutes, then remove the lid and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes.

  5. Once baked, remove the bread from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 1 hour before slicing.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully made your own sourdough bread for sandwiches. The process may seem time-consuming, but the end result is definitely worth it. Slice the bread, add your favorite sandwich fillings, and enjoy the incredible flavors and textures that only homemade sourdough bread can offer. Get creative with your sandwich combinations and elevate your lunchtime experience. Happy baking!
