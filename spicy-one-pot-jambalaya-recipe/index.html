Spicy One Pot Jambalaya Recipe


Looking for a flavorful and satisfying one-pot meal? Look no further than this Spicy One Pot Jambalaya recipe. Bursting with a mix of savory flavors and just the right amount of heat, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or new to the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow and perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound Andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 pound chicken breast, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the Andouille sausage and cook until browned. Remove the sausage from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced chicken breast and cook until browned and no longer pink. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery to the pot. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes and chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Stir in the rice, Cajun seasoning, paprika, thyme, oregano, and cayenne pepper. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and most of the liquid is absorbed.

  6. Once the rice is cooked, return the Andouille sausage and chicken to the pot. Stir to combine and let it heat through for a few minutes.

  7. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you like it extra spicy, you can add more cayenne pepper at this point.

  8. Serve the Spicy One Pot Jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Tips and Variations

  • For a seafood twist, you can add shrimp or crawfish to the jambalaya during the last few minutes of cooking.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply omit the meat and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors of the jambalaya will develop even more with time, making it a great make-ahead meal.

Final Thoughts

This Spicy One Pot Jambalaya is a hearty and satisfying dish that brings the flavors of the Louisiana bayou right to your kitchen. It’s perfect for a cozy dinner at home or for entertaining a crowd. The combination of savory Andouille sausage, tender chicken, and aromatic spices creates a symphony of flavors that will leave everyone coming back for seconds. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or just looking to spice up your weeknight meals, this recipe is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to enjoy a taste of the South with this delicious and comforting Spicy One Pot Jambalaya.
