Comforting and Creamy Loaded Baked Potato and Ham Chowder Recipes


When the chill of autumn settles in or winter’s frost starts to bite, there’s nothing quite as comforting as a warm bowl of chowder. This Loaded Baked Potato and Ham Chowder combines the rich, creamy flavors of baked potatoes with the savory goodness of ham, creating a dish that feels like a hug in a bowl. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the delightful world of chowder, explore its history, and provide you with two fantastic recipes that are sure to satisfy your cravings.

The History of Chowder

Chowder has a rich history that dates back to the 18th century. Originating from the coastal regions of France, the word “chowder” is believed to be derived from the French term “chaudière,” which refers to a cauldron used for cooking. Early chowders were often made with fish or seafood, but as the dish made its way to America, it evolved into a hearty meal with various ingredients.

Today, chowder is a staple in many households, with variations that include clam chowder, corn chowder, and, of course, potato chowder. Each recipe brings its own unique flavor profile, but the creamy texture and comforting warmth remain constant. Now, let’s turn our attention to a recipe that encapsulates the essence of comfort food: Loaded Baked Potato and Ham Chowder.

Ingredients: The Comfort Components

For our Loaded Baked Potato and Ham Chowder, we will start with a list of ingredients that will create layers of flavor and richness.

For the Chowder Base:

  • 4 large russet potatoes (about 2 pounds), peeled and diced into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or half-and-half for a lighter option)
  • 2 cups cooked ham, diced (smoked ham adds a wonderful flavor)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese (plus extra for garnish)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions or chives for garnish

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1 cup corn (fresh, canned, or frozen)
  • 1 cup diced carrots for added sweetness and color
  • Crumbled bacon for a smoky crunch

Preparing the Chowder: Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s get cooking! This chowder is not only easy to prepare but also offers a chance to get creative with flavors and textures.

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Step 2: Cook the Potatoes

Add the diced potatoes to the pot and pour in the chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover the pot and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and easily pierced with a fork.

Step 3: Blend for Creaminess

Once the potatoes are tender, use an immersion blender to puree about half of the soup. This will give the chowder a creamy texture while still maintaining some chunky potato pieces. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can transfer half of the soup to a blender, puree it, and then return it to the pot.

Step 4: Add the Creamy Goodness

Stir in the heavy cream, sour cream, and shredded cheddar cheese. Mix well and allow the chowder to simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cheese has melted and the chowder is heated through.

Step 5: Incorporate the Ham and Season

Add the diced ham, thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot. If you’re using corn or carrots, now is the time to add them. Allow the chowder to simmer for another 5 minutes to let the flavors meld together.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the chowder into bowls and garnish with chopped green onions or chives, and extra shredded cheese if desired. Serve hot with crusty bread or crackers on the side for a complete meal.

Variations: Customize Your Chowder

One of the best aspects of chowder is its versatility. Here are a few variations to consider:

1. Vegetarian Option

For a vegetarian twist, omit the ham and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. You can add more vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, and mushrooms to enhance the flavor and nutrition.

2. Spicy Kick

If you prefer a bit of heat, add diced jalapeños or a splash of hot sauce to the chowder during cooking. It will elevate the flavor profile and provide a delightful kick.

3. Seafood Delight

For a seafood version, replace the ham with shrimp or crab meat. Adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure the seafood is cooked perfectly.

4. Cheesy Broccoli Chowder

Incorporate steamed broccoli florets into the chowder for a cheesy broccoli twist. This adds color and nutrition, making it a great option for families.

Storing and Reheating Chowder

If you find yourself with leftovers (which is unlikely, given how delicious this chowder is!), storing it is simple. Allow the chowder to cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days.

When you’re ready to enjoy it again, reheat the chowder on the stove over low heat, stirring occasionally until warmed through. You may need to add a splash of broth or cream to loosen the consistency, as chowder can thicken when stored.

Conclusion: A Cozy Bowl of Comfort

This Loaded Baked Potato and Ham Chowder is the epitome of comfort food. With its creamy texture, rich flavors, and heartwarming ingredients, it’s a dish that invites you to slow down and savor every spoonful. Perfect for chilly evenings or family gatherings, this chowder is sure to become a family favorite.

So, gather your ingredients, bring your loved ones to the kitchen, and enjoy the process of creating this delightful dish. Whether you’re following the classic recipe or putting your own spin on it, one thing is for sure: each bowl of chowder will be filled with warmth and love. Happy cooking!
