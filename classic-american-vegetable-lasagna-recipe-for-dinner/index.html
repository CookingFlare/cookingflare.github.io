Classic American Vegetable Lasagna: Recipe for Dinner


Lasagna is a beloved staple of American cuisine, known for its hearty layers of flavor and comforting texture. While traditional lasagna often features rich meats and cheeses, a classic vegetable variation offers a delightful, wholesome alternative that doesn’t skimp on taste. This Classic American Vegetable Lasagna recipe is perfect for a family dinner, a gathering with friends, or even a cozy night in. Packed with vibrant vegetables, creamy ricotta, and topped with a bubbling layer of mozzarella, this dish is sure to please everyone at the table.

Ingredients

To create a mouthwatering Classic American Vegetable Lasagna, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Vegetables:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 medium bell pepper (red or yellow), diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Ricotta Mixture:

  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

For Assembly:

  • 9-12 lasagna noodles (depending on the size of your dish)
  • 3 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

  2. Cook the Vegetables: Add the diced zucchini, bell pepper, and sliced mushrooms to the skillet. Sauté for about 7-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Season with oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Finally, mix in the drained diced tomatoes and remove from heat. Allow the vegetable mixture to cool slightly.

Step 2: Prepare the Ricotta Mixture

In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, black pepper, and nutmeg (if using). Mix until smooth and well combined. This creamy mixture adds richness to your lasagna and balances the flavors of the vegetables.

Step 3: Cook the Lasagna Noodles

While your vegetable mixture is cooling, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the lasagna noodles and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the noodles and lay them flat on a clean kitchen towel or parchment paper to prevent sticking.

Step 4: Assemble the Lasagna

Now that all components are ready, it’s time to layer your lasagna:

  1. Start with Marinara Sauce: Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. This prevents the noodles from sticking.

  2. Layer the Noodles: Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce, covering the bottom of the dish.

  3. Add the Ricotta Mixture: Spread about one-third of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, using a spatula to ensure even coverage.

  4. Layer the Vegetables: Spoon half of the vegetable mixture over the ricotta layer, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.

  5. Repeat: Add another layer of noodles, followed by another third of the ricotta mixture, the remaining vegetable mixture, and then another layer of noodles.

  6. Top It Off: For the final layer, add the remaining ricotta mixture, followed by marinara sauce, and finish with a generous layer of shredded mozzarella cheese.

Step 5: Bake the Lasagna

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil to prevent the cheese from burning. Bake for 30 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 6: Let It Rest

Once the lasagna is done baking, remove it from the oven and allow it to rest for about 15-20 minutes. This resting time helps the layers set, making it easier to cut and serve.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Slice the lasagna into squares and serve warm. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired. This Classic American Vegetable Lasagna pairs beautifully with a crisp green salad and a side of garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for the Best Vegetable Lasagna

  1. Vegetable Variations: Feel free to mix and match your favorite vegetables. Broccoli, carrots, and eggplant are excellent additions to this lasagna. Just be sure to cook any firmer vegetables until tender before layering.

  2. Homemade Marinara Sauce: If you have the time, consider making your own marinara sauce. A simple blend of sautéed garlic, canned tomatoes, and fresh herbs can elevate the flavors of your lasagna.

  3. Make Ahead: This vegetable lasagna can be assembled ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours before baking. Just remember to add a few extra minutes to the baking time if baking from cold.

  4. Freezing: Unbaked lasagna can be frozen for up to 3 months. Wrap it tightly with plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing. When ready to bake, thaw in the refrigerator overnight before baking as usual.

  5. Cheese Choices: Experiment with different cheeses for added flavor. A blend of mozzarella and provolone adds a nice twist, while ricotta can be replaced with cottage cheese for a lighter option.

Nutritional Benefits

This Classic American Vegetable Lasagna is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here are some of the health benefits of the key ingredients:

  • Vegetables: The medley of zucchini, bell peppers, spinach, and tomatoes provides a wealth of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that support overall health.

  • Ricotta Cheese: A good source of protein and calcium, ricotta contributes to healthy bones and muscles.

  • Whole Wheat Noodles: Opting for whole wheat lasagna noodles increases fiber content, which is essential for digestive health.

Conclusion

Classic American Vegetable Lasagna is a versatile, comforting dish that brings families together at the dinner table. With its layers of rich flavors and nutritious ingredients, it’s a recipe that can easily become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re enjoying it as a hearty weeknight dinner or serving it at a special occasion, this lasagna is bound to impress.

Now it’s time to gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and create a delightful vegetable lasagna that will warm hearts and satisfy appetites. Happy cooking!
