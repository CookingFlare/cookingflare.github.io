Delicious Christmas Desserts: 15 Recipes to Try This Year


The holiday season is a time of joy, celebration, and indulgence, and what better way to embrace the festive spirit than by treating yourself and your loved ones to some mouth-watering Christmas desserts? Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or enjoying a quiet evening by the fire, a delicious dessert can elevate any occasion. If you’re looking for new and exciting ways to end your holiday meals, we’ve got you covered with 15 incredible Christmas dessert recipes that are sure to leave everyone asking for seconds.

1. Classic Christmas Yule Log (Bûche de Noël)

A traditional French Christmas dessert, the Yule Log is a beautiful and delicious way to celebrate the season. Made with a rich chocolate sponge cake rolled with a creamy buttercream filling, this dessert is often decorated to resemble a log, complete with meringue mushrooms and powdered sugar “snow.” It’s a showstopper on any Christmas table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 8 oz semisweet chocolate, melted

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a jelly roll pan with parchment paper.
  2. Beat the eggs and sugar together until light and fluffy. Sift in the cocoa powder, flour, and baking powder, then gently fold them in.
  3. Spread the batter evenly in the pan and bake for 12-15 minutes.
  4. While the cake is baking, prepare a damp kitchen towel on your counter.
  5. Once baked, immediately invert the cake onto the towel and remove the parchment paper. Roll it up with the towel and let it cool completely.
  6. For the filling, whip the heavy cream and powdered sugar until stiff peaks form. In a separate bowl, beat the butter and melted chocolate together until smooth. Fold in the whipped cream.
  7. Unroll the cooled cake, spread the filling, and roll it back up. Frost the outside with more chocolate buttercream and decorate as desired.

2. Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread cookies are a timeless classic during the Christmas season. Their fragrant blend of spices makes them perfect for holiday baking, and they can be decorated in a variety of fun and festive ways. Whether you prefer them soft or crisp, these cookies are a holiday favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup unsulfured molasses

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the butter and sugar until fluffy. Add the egg and molasses and beat until combined.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms. Divide the dough into two portions, wrap them in plastic wrap, and chill for at least an hour.
  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  5. Roll the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thick, then cut out your desired shapes with cookie cutters.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes, let them cool, and decorate with royal icing or sprinkles.

3. Peppermint Bark

Peppermint bark is a simple yet indulgent Christmas treat that combines the rich flavors of chocolate and refreshing peppermint. It’s a perfect dessert for when you need something quick and easy to make, and it’s always a hit with guests.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz dark chocolate
  • 12 oz white chocolate
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup crushed candy canes

Directions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl over simmering water. Once melted, pour the chocolate onto the prepared baking sheet and spread it evenly.
  3. Chill the dark chocolate layer in the fridge for 20 minutes.
  4. Melt the white chocolate in the same way and stir in the peppermint extract.
  5. Pour the white chocolate over the chilled dark chocolate layer and spread evenly. Sprinkle the crushed candy canes on top.
  6. Chill for an additional 30 minutes, then break into pieces.

4. Eggnog Cheesecake Bars

For those who love the creamy, spiced flavor of eggnog, these cheesecake bars are the ultimate holiday dessert. With a smooth, creamy filling and a hint of nutmeg and rum, they’re the perfect combination of festive and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter, then press it into the bottom of the prepared dish.
  3. Beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the sour cream, eggs, eggnog, vanilla, and nutmeg, and mix until combined.
  4. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust and bake for 45-50 minutes or until the center is set.
  5. Let the bars cool, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours before cutting into squares.

5. Pecan Pie Bars

If you love the rich, nutty goodness of pecan pie but want something easier to serve at a holiday party, these pecan pie bars are the perfect solution. They feature a buttery crust and a gooey, nut-filled topping that’s simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled
  • 2 cups chopped pecans
  • 3/4 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a food processor, combine flour, powdered sugar, and butter. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the dough into the bottom of the prepared dish and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, vanilla, and salt. Stir in the chopped pecans.
  5. Pour the pecan mixture over the crust and bake for another 25-30 minutes until the topping is set.

6. Cinnamon Roll Casserole

This cinnamon roll casserole combines the best parts of cinnamon rolls with the ease of a breakfast casserole. It’s a delicious, crowd-pleasing dessert that’s perfect for Christmas morning or a cozy holiday brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans refrigerated cinnamon rolls with icing
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish.
  2. Cut the cinnamon rolls into quarters and layer them in the casserole dish.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, maple syrup, and cinnamon. Pour the mixture over the cinnamon rolls.
  4. Top with chopped pecans if desired and bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown.
  5. Drizzle with the icing that came with the cinnamon rolls and serve warm.

7. Christmas Trifle

A trifle is an elegant and easy-to-make dessert that looks as beautiful as it tastes. Layers of sponge cake, custard, whipped cream, and fruit come together in a festive and delicious treat perfect for holiday celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cake or angel food cake, cubed
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups vanilla custard (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries)

Directions:

  1. In a large trifle dish, layer half of the cubed cake on the bottom.
  2. Spread a layer of vanilla custard over the cake.
  3. Add a layer of mixed berries and repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up.
  4. Whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until stiff peaks form.
  5. Spread the whipped cream on top of the trifle and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

8. Chocolate Peppermint Cupcakes

For those who love the combination of chocolate and peppermint, these cupcakes are the perfect Christmas treat. With a rich chocolate base and a refreshing peppermint frosting, they’re festive, flavorful, and fun.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and sugar together. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients, alternating with the milk. Stir in the peppermint extract.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes.
  6. For the frosting, beat together butter, powdered sugar, peppermint extract, and a splash of milk. Frost the cooled cupcakes and garnish with crushed candy canes.

9. Mince Pies

Mince pies are a beloved treat in many parts of the world during the Christmas season. Filled with a rich mixture of dried fruits, spices, and brandy, these little pies are the perfect bite-sized indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package pre-made shortcrust pastry
  • 2 cups mincemeat filling
  • 1 egg, beaten for egg wash

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. Roll out the pastry and cut out circles to fit the muffin tin cups.
  3. Fill each pastry cup with mincemeat filling and top with a smaller pastry circle.
  4. Brush with the beaten egg wash and bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.

10. Snowball Cookies

Snowball cookies, also known as Russian tea cakes, are buttery, melt-in-your-mouth cookies rolled in powdered sugar. They’re a holiday classic and are as delightful to eat as they are to share.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped pecans

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and powdered sugar together until light and fluffy. Add vanilla extract and mix.
  3. Gradually add the flour and mix until combined. Stir in the chopped pecans.
  4. Roll the dough into 1-inch balls and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  5. While still warm, roll the cookies in powdered sugar and allow to cool.

11. Rugelach

These buttery, flaky pastries filled with cinnamon, sugar, and jam are a Jewish tradition during the holidays. Rugelach is perfect for serving at a Christmas gathering, as they offer a beautiful mix of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup fruit preserves (apricot, raspberry, or strawberry)
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/4 cup cinnamon-sugar

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, butter, cream cheese, sugar, and salt to form a dough.
  3. Divide the dough into 4 equal parts and roll each into a 1/4-inch thick circle.
  4. Spread the fruit preserves over each circle, sprinkle with walnuts, and then cut the dough into wedges.
  5. Roll each wedge tightly, brush with egg wash, and bake for 20 minutes or until golden.

12. Frosted Sugar Cookies

Sugar cookies are another quintessential holiday treat, and this version includes a sweet, soft base with a festive touch of frosting and sprinkles. Perfect for cookie exchanges or simply enjoying with a cup of hot cocoa.

Ingredients:

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup colored sprinkles (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, and baking powder.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and sugar together until fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients and mix until combined.
  5. Roll the dough into 1-inch balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet. Flatten slightly and add sprinkles.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes, then cool completely before frosting with your favorite icing.

13. Cranberry Orange Loaf Cake

This moist loaf cake combines the tartness of cranberries with the bright flavor of oranges, making it a refreshing yet festive treat for your holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon orange zest
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs, orange juice, and zest and mix until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients, then fold in the cranberries.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 55-60 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

14. Christmas Pudding

Christmas pudding is a British classic that is both rich and comforting. Packed with dried fruits, nuts, and spices, this steamed pudding is often served with brandy butter or cream and is a symbol of holiday tradition.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup suet or butter
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup rum or brandy
  • 1 cup mixed dried fruits
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts

Directions:

  1. Grease a pudding basin and set it aside.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the suet, breadcrumbs, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and baking powder.
  3. Stir in the sugar, milk, and rum or brandy, followed by the dried fruits and nuts.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared pudding basin, cover with parchment paper and foil, and steam for 3 hours.
  5. Serve with brandy butter or heavy cream.

15. Toffee Apple Crumble

This dessert combines the flavors of toffee and apple in a warm, comforting crumble. The caramelized apple filling is topped with a buttery crumble that’s perfect for a cold winter’s night.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup toffee bits
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the sliced apples, brown sugar, toffee bits, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. For the crumble, combine the flour, oats, and butter until it forms a crumbly mixture.
  4. Pour the apple mixture into the prepared dish and top with the crumble.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until golden brown and bubbly.

These 15 Christmas desserts are sure to bring joy and flavor to your holiday celebrations. Whether you’re baking for a crowd or preparing a special treat for yourself, these festive desserts will make your Christmas even more memorable. Happy baking!
