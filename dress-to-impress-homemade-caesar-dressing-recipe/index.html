Dress to Impress: Homemade Caesar Dressing Recipe


When it comes to dressing up a salad, nothing beats the classic Caesar dressing. The tangy, savory, and slightly bitter flavors are a perfect match for crisp romaine lettuce, croutons, and parmesan cheese. But have you ever stopped to think about what’s really in that store-bought bottle? Artificial preservatives, additives, and a laundry list of unpronounceable ingredients are not exactly the epitome of culinary excellence.

That’s why we’re going to show you how to make your own homemade Caesar dressing from scratch. With just a few simple ingredients and some basic kitchen equipment, you’ll be whipping up a bottle of this deliciousness in no time.

The Magic of Homemade Caesar Dressing

Homemade Caesar dressing is a game-changer for several reasons. First and foremost, you get to control the ingredients. No more worrying about artificial preservatives or high-fructose corn syrup. You can use fresh, high-quality ingredients that will elevate the flavor of your dressing to new heights.

Secondly, homemade Caesar dressing is incredibly versatile. Want a lighter, more delicate flavor? Use more lemon juice and less garlic. Prefer a richer, creamier dressing? Add a bit more mayonnaise and Parmesan cheese.

Finally, making your own Caesar dressing is surprisingly easy. With just a few minutes of prep work and some basic kitchen equipment, you’ll be enjoying a delicious, homemade bottle of dressing in no time.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need to make your own homemade Caesar dressing:

  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise

Now, let’s get started!

Step 1: Make the Garlic-Anchovy Paste

In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic and anchovy fillets. Use a fork to mash the mixture until it forms a smooth, paste-like consistency.

Step 2: Whisk Together the Lemon Juice, Oil, and Mustard

In a large bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and black pepper until well combined.

Step 3: Add the Garlic-Anchovy Paste and Worcestershire Sauce

Add the garlic-anchovy paste and Worcestershire sauce to the lemon juice mixture. Whisk until smooth.

Step 4: Add the Parmesan Cheese and Mayonnaise

Add the grated Parmesan cheese and mayonnaise to the dressing mixture. Whisk until well combined.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed. If it’s too thick, add a bit more lemon juice. If it’s too thin, add a bit more mayonnaise.

Step 6: Store and Enjoy

Transfer the dressing to an airtight container and store it in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. Use it to dress your favorite salads, or as a dip for crudités and crackers.

And that’s it! With this simple recipe, you’ll be enjoying a delicious, homemade bottle of Caesar dressing in no time. Say goodbye to store-bought dressing and hello to a world of flavor and flexibility. Your taste buds will thank you!
