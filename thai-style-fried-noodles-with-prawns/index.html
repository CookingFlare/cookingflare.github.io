Thai Style Fried Noodles with Prawns


When it comes to vibrant and tantalizing street food, Thai cuisine offers an incredible array of dishes that blend savory, sweet, sour, and spicy flavors effortlessly. One iconic dish that has captured the hearts of food lovers worldwide is Thai Style Fried Noodles with Prawns. This dish, often known as Pad Thai, is a harmonious medley of noodles, succulent prawns, crisp vegetables, and a distinctive sauce that ties everything together beautifully.

If you’re craving an authentic Thai experience at home, this recipe will guide you step-by-step, ensuring you get the perfect balance of texture and taste. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a kitchen novice, this guide will bring the spirit of Thai street food right to your table.

Origins of Thai Stir-Fried Noodles with Prawns

Before diving into the recipe, it’s worth appreciating the story behind this dish. Fried noodles with prawns, commonly known as Pad Thai, gained popularity in Thailand during the mid-20th century. It was promoted by the government to boost nationalism and help reduce rice consumption. Today, it is one of the most recognizable Thai dishes globally.

The beauty of this dish lies in its layers of flavors: the tang from tamarind pulp, the saltiness of fish sauce, the sweetness of palm sugar, and the peanuts’ crunch, all complemented by tender prawns and perfectly wok-tossed rice noodles.

Ingredients Needed for Perfect Thai Fried Noodles with Prawns

Here is what you will need for this recipe, broken down by component:

For the Noodles and Protein:

  • 200g (7oz) flat rice noodles (soaked in warm water for 20-30 minutes if dry)
  • 250g (9oz) fresh prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 2 large eggs

Vegetables and Aromatics:

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 spring onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots (optional, for extra crunch and color)

Sauce Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste (you can use tamarind concentrate or fresh pulp soaked in water)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tbsp palm sugar (or light brown sugar as substitute)
  • 1 tsp chili powder (adjust to spice preference)

Garnishes:

  • Roasted crushed peanuts
  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves
  • Additional chili flakes (optional)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Let’s dig into the technique that will bring this dish to life, mimicking that fiery wok taste that makes Thai noodles irresistible.

1. Preparing the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, soy sauce, palm sugar, and chili powder. The sauce should be balanced – tangy, salty, and sweet with a mild kick of heat. Taste it; you can adjust it by adding more tamarind for sourness, sugar for sweetness, or chili for spice.

2. Soaking the Rice Noodles

If using dried rice noodles, soak them in warm water until they are pliable but still a bit firm (around 20-30 minutes). They will finish cooking in the wok so avoid over-soaking, which results in mushy noodles.

3. Stir Frying the Prawns

Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the minced garlic and the white parts of the spring onions. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.

Add the prawns and stir-fry until they just turn pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from the wok and set aside.

4. Cooking the Eggs

In the same wok, add a splash more oil if necessary. Crack the eggs in and scramble quickly, trying to keep the curds fluffy and soft. This adds richness to the dish.

5. Adding the Noodles and Sauce

Next, add the drained rice noodles to the wok. Pour the prepared sauce over and toss everything vigorously to coat the noodles evenly.

Return the cooked prawns to the wok, mixing them with the noodles. Toss in the bean sprouts (and shredded carrots if using) for the final minute of cooking, letting them warm through but retain their crunch.

Tips for Achieving Authentic Thai Fried Noodles at Home

  • Use a Wok or a Large Skillet: High heat and quick cooking are essential for that slightly charred, smoky aroma called “wok hei.”
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Cook in batches if needed. Crowding reduces heat and can cause steaming instead of stir-frying.
  • Balance Your Sauce: Thai cooking thrives on flavor layers. Adjust tamarind, sugar, and fish sauce gradually to suit your palate.
  • Add Freshness at the End: Garnishes of lime, peanuts, herbs, and extra chili elevate the dish right before serving.
  • Soak Noodles Properly: Undercooked or overcooked noodles can make or break the dish.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

While prawns are the star protein here, you can also switch them out for chicken, tofu, or even mixed seafood. For a vegetarian version, skip fish sauce and use mushroom soy sauce with an added splash of lime and tamarind.

Serve your fried noodles with a crisp fresh cucumber salad or pickled vegetables to contrast the rich flavors. A refreshing Thai iced tea or a cold beer can be the perfect beverage to accompany your meal.

Nutritional Highlights

This Thai fried noodle dish is more than just delicious; it packs a nutritional punch. Prawns offer a low-fat source of high-quality protein and are rich in selenium, vitamin B12, and iodine. The bean sprouts add fiber and vitamins, while peanuts contribute healthy fats and additional protein.

To lighten the dish, use less oil and load it up with fresh vegetables. It’s a well-rounded dish that fits nicely into a balanced diet when eaten in moderation.

Wrapping Up: Your Ticket to Thai Flavors

Making Thai style fried noodles with prawns at home is an immersive culinary adventure. The combination of textures—the chewy rice noodles, crunchy peanuts, tender prawns, and crisp bean sprouts—paired with the vibrant sauce creates a dish that’s dynamic and satisfying.

This recipe invites you to explore authentic Thai cooking methods while allowing room for personal twists. Whether cooking for family or impressing friends, these fragrant, colorful noodles will transport everyone to the bustling streets of Bangkok.

So, grab your wok, gather your ingredients, and dive into cooking this quintessential Thai street food classic. Prepare for an explosion of flavor with every bite!
