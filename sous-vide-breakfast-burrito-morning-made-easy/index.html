Sous Vide Breakfast Burrito: Morning Made Easy


Who doesn’t love the simplicity of a breakfast burrito? Wrapping all your favorite morning flavors into one handheld package is a genius way to make breakfast portable, satisfying, and absolutely delicious. But what if you could take your classic breakfast burrito to the next level with precision cooking? Enter the sous vide breakfast burrito—a culinary twist that guarantees perfect textures, deep flavors, and an unparalleled morning meal experience. Whether you’re feeding a crowd or meal-prepping for the week ahead, this sous vide recipe is about to revolutionize your mornings.

Why Sous Vide is Perfect for Breakfast Burritos

Sous vide, a French cooking method that involves vacuum-sealing food and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature, isn’t just for Michelin-starred chefs. This technique allows you to bring out the best in your ingredients, ensuring your eggs are creamy, proteins are cooked to perfection, and all the components retain their flavor and textures.

For breakfast burritos, sous vide eliminates the guesswork involved in cooking. It’s all about consistency—eggs won’t overcook or turn rubbery, meats retain their juiciness, and even vegetables will taste fresher thanks to precise temperature control. Plus, sous vide is a hands-off approach, leaving you free to get ready for your day while breakfast practically makes itself.

Ingredients for the Ultimate Sous Vide Breakfast Burrito

To transform your mornings into a gourmet experience, here’s what you’ll need:

Main Ingredients:

  • Eggs (6 large) – The foundation of your burrito, cooked to creamy perfection.
  • Cheddar Cheese (1 cup, shredded) – For that classic, melty breakfast burrito cheesiness.
  • Breakfast Sausage (8 oz or 4 links) – Adds flavor and protein.
  • Bell Peppers (1 medium) – Use a mix of colors for sweetness and crunch.
  • Onions (1 small) – For a touch of savory depth.
  • Hash Browns (1 cup) – Optional, but adds a delightful crispy contrast if incorporated after sous vide cooking.
  • Tortillas (4 large) – Soft flour tortillas work beautifully for rolling.
  • Salt and Pepper – To season your egg mixture.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Hot Sauce or Salsa – For some extra spice.
  • Avocado (sliced) – Creamy textures to complement the eggs.
  • Cilantro (chopped) – For a fresh herbal note.
  • Black Beans – A hearty addition for increased fiber.

Preparing Your Sous Vide Setup

Before we dive in, you’ll need a sous vide machine, vacuum-sealed bags, or reusable silicone sous vide pouches. If you don’t already have one, this affordable kitchen appliance will unlock endless culinary possibilities in your home.

  1. Set the Temperature
    For the eggs, set your sous vide machine to 172°F (78°C). This temperature is ideal for incredibly creamy scrambled eggs without reaching a rubbery texture.

  2. Prep Ingredients
    Chop your bell peppers and onions into bite-size pieces. Cook and crumble the sausage ahead of time to add into the egg mixture.

Step-by-Step Instructions: How to Make Sous Vide Breakfast Burritos

1. Vacuum-Sealing the Egg Mix

Crack the eggs into a large mixing bowl, add shredded cheddar cheese, cooked sausage, bell peppers, onions, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well combined. Pour the mixture into vacuum-sealed bags or sous vide pouches, ensuring the contents are spread out in an even layer. Remove excess air before sealing.

2. Cooking Eggs Sous Vide

Place the sealed egg mixture into the preheated water bath at 172°F (78°C). Cook for 1 hour. While it may seem like a long time compared to stovetop scrambled eggs, the sous vide method ensures consistent results every single time.

3. Cooking Optional Hash Browns

If you want crispy hash browns in your burrito, prepare them on the stovetop or in the oven while the sous vide egg mixture cooks. Set them aside for later assembling.

4. Preparing Tortillas

Warm your tortillas on a dry skillet for about 15–30 seconds on each side. This softens them and makes them pliable for rolling your burritos.

5. Final Touches

Once the eggs finish cooking, carefully open the sous vide bags. They will have an incredibly silky texture. Transfer the eggs to a large bowl and gently break them up with a spatula.

If desired, toss in cooked hash browns, black beans, or avocado slices. Don’t forget additional toppings like hot sauce or cilantro before rolling your burrito.

6. Assembling Burritos

Divide the egg mixture equally among the tortillas. Roll them tightly, tucking in the edges as you go for a neat burrito that won’t unravel.

7. Optional: Quick Toasting

For an added layer of texture, toast your rolled burritos on a skillet for 1–2 minutes per side. The tortilla gets golden and slightly crispy, adding even more flavor.

Meal Prep Made Easy with Sous Vide Burritos

One of the best things about sous vide breakfast burritos is their versatility. These burritos are tailor-made for meal prep, helping you start your mornings off right without the rush. Here’s how you can save time:

  • Batch Cooking: Make a large batch of sous vide egg mixture ahead of time. Assemble and roll burritos, then individually wrap them in aluminum foil or plastic wrap. Store them in the fridge or freezer.

  • Reheating: For refrigerated burritos, simply heat them for 1–2 minutes in the microwave, or toast them in a skillet for a fresher finish. For frozen ones, microwave for 3–4 minutes, flipping halfway.

Pro Tip: Sous vide-cooked fillings retain their texture even after freezing and reheating, so you’ll enjoy burritos that still taste freshly made.

Creative Variations to Try

Once you’ve mastered the classic sous vide breakfast burrito, you can experiment with flavors and ingredients to keep mornings exciting. Here are some ideas to inspire you:

  • Southwestern Style: Add black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, and plenty of salsa for a Tex-Mex twist.

  • Vegetarian Option: Swap the sausage for sautéed zucchini or mushrooms. Load up on veggies and use a smoky cheese like gouda for extra flavor.

  • Low-Carb Keto Burrito: Use low-carb tortillas, skip the hash browns, and fill with eggs, cheese, avocado, and bacon.

  • Mediterranean Breakfast Wrap: Include sun-dried tomatoes, feta cheese, spinach, and olives for a bold Mediterranean breakfast burrito.

A Gourmet Breakfast Without the Stress

Making breakfast burritos with sous vide isn’t just about creating the most consistent and flavorful meal. It’s about finding ways to simplify your mornings without compromising on quality. Whether you’re meal-prepping for the week or indulging yourself on a weekend morning, sous vide breakfast burritos are a game-changer.

By combining the precision cooking of sous vide with the hearty satisfaction of burritos, you’ll find yourself looking forward to breakfast every day. Experiment with flavors, plan for the week, or serve them to family and friends—they truly are the perfect morning creation.
