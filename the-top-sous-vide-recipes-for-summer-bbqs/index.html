The Top Sous Vide Recipes for Summer BBQs


As the sun shines brighter and temperatures rise, summer beckons us to gather with family and friends for backyard barbecues filled with delicious food and laughter. This year, elevate your grilling game with sous vide cooking, a technique that promises perfectly cooked meats and veggies every time. This guide will take you through some of the top sous vide recipes that will make your summer BBQs unforgettable.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing food in a plastic pouch and cooking it to a very precise temperature in a water bath. This technique allows for even cooking and retains moisture, flavors, and nutrients, making it ideal for meats and vegetables alike. The result? Tender and juicy dishes that will impress your guests.

Preparing for Your Sous Vide BBQ

Before diving into the recipes, ensure you have the right equipment:

  • Sous Vide Immersion Circulator: This device controls the temperature of the water bath to ensure consistent cooking.
  • Vacuum Sealer or Ziplock Bags: For sealing your food. If using Ziplock bags, the water displacement method can be used to remove air.
  • Large Pot or Container: To hold the water bath.

With everything ready, let’s explore our top sous vide recipes perfect for summer BBQs!

1. Sous Vide Steak

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1.5 inches thick)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (rosemary or thyme)
  • Garlic cloves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Season the Steaks: Generously season the ribeye steaks with salt and pepper. Add fresh herbs and garlic if desired.

  2. Seal the Steaks: Place the steaks in vacuum-seal bags or Ziplock bags. If using Ziplock bags, use the water displacement method to remove air.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat your sous vide immersion circulator to 129°F (54°C) for medium-rare. For medium, set it to 135°F (57°C).

  4. Cook: Submerge the sealed bags in the water bath and cook for 1 to 3 hours. The beauty of sous vide is that you can leave it for longer without overcooking.

  5. Sear the Steaks: Once cooked, remove the steaks from the bags and pat them dry. Heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat and sear each steak for about 1 minute on each side for that perfect crust.

  6. Serve: Let the steaks rest for a few minutes, slice, and serve with your favorite BBQ sides.

2. Sous Vide BBQ Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: dry rub (paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ribs: Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs. Season with salt, pepper, and any dry rub you prefer.

  2. Seal the Ribs: Place each rack in its own vacuum-seal bag. Pour in a half-cup of BBQ sauce for flavor.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat the sous vide to 165°F (74°C) for tender ribs. For fall-off-the-bone texture, cook at 185°F (85°C) for 12 to 24 hours.

  4. Cook: Submerge the bags in the water bath, making sure they are fully immersed.

  5. Finish on the Grill: Once cooked, remove the ribs from the bags and discard the liquid. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the ribs with additional BBQ sauce and grill for 5-10 minutes, turning occasionally until caramelized.

  6. Serve: Cut into individual ribs and serve with extra BBQ sauce on the side.

3. Sous Vide Chicken Breast

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (basil, thyme, or parsley)
  • Lemon slices

Instructions:

  1. Season the Chicken: Season the chicken breasts generously with salt and pepper. Add herbs and lemon slices for extra flavor.

  2. Seal: Place the chicken in vacuum-seal bags and drizzle with olive oil. Seal tightly.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat to 145°F (63°C) for juicy and tender chicken. Cook for 1 to 2 hours.

  4. Sear the Chicken: After cooking, remove the chicken from the bags and pat dry. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear each breast for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown.

  5. Serve: Slice and serve with grilled vegetables or a light summer salad.

4. Sous Vide Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of your choice of vegetables (asparagus, carrots, zucchini, bell peppers)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and cut vegetables into uniform sizes. Season with salt, pepper, and olive oil.

  2. Seal: Place the vegetables in a vacuum-seal bag or Ziplock bag with herbs if desired. Seal tightly.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat the sous vide to 183°F (84°C) for perfectly cooked veggies. Cook for 30 to 60 minutes depending on the type of vegetable.

  4. Finish on the Grill: Once done, remove from the bags and grill for a few minutes to add some smoky flavor.

  5. Serve: Enjoy your vibrant and flavorful grilled vegetables alongside your main dishes.

5. Sous Vide Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • Butter
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Corn: Husk the corn and break it into halves if necessary. Season with salt, pepper, and a pat of butter.

  2. Seal: Place corn in a vacuum-seal bag. You can also use individual bags for each ear.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat to 183°F (84°C) and cook for 45 minutes.

  4. Grill for a Finish: Once cooked, remove from the bags and place on the hot grill for a few minutes to get those lovely char marks.

  5. Serve: Brush with more butter and serve immediately.

6. Sous Vide Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 blend for juiciness)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional toppings: cheese, lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Patties: Form ground beef into patties and season each side with salt and pepper. Make a small indentation in the center to prevent puffing during cooking.

  2. Seal: Place the patties in a single layer in a vacuum-seal bag.

  3. Set the Sous Vide: Preheat to 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare, or 160°F (71°C) for well done. Cook for 1 hour.

  4. Sear the Burgers: After cooking, heat a skillet or grill to high heat. Sear each patty for 1-2 minutes on each side until browned.

  5. Assemble and Serve: Build your burgers with your favorite toppings and enjoy!

Conclusion

This summer, let sous vide cooking transform your BBQ experience. With these recipes, you can enjoy perfectly cooked meats and vibrant vegetables that are sure to impress your guests. The flexibility and precision of sous vide cooking will allow you to spend less time worrying about food and more time enjoying the company of friends and family. So fire up that sous vide immersion circulator, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of summer in every bite!

Embrace the art of sous vide cooking and make your summer BBQs the talk of the town!
