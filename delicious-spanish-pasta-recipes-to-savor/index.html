Delicious Spanish Pasta Recipes to Savor


When it comes to Mediterranean cuisine, Spanish food often takes center stage with its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and rich history. While Spain is famous for its tapas, paella, and churros, the country also boasts a delightful array of pasta dishes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious Spanish pasta recipes that you can easily recreate at home. From traditional dishes to modern interpretations, these recipes are perfect for any occasion. Let’s dive into the world of Spanish pasta!

1. Fideuà: The Coastal Gem

Fideuà is a traditional dish from Valencia, similar to paella but made with short noodles instead of rice. This coastal dish is often prepared with seafood and flavored with saffron, garlic, and a variety of spices.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fideuà noodles (or thin spaghetti if unavailable)
  • 500g mixed seafood (such as shrimp, mussels, and calamari)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, grated
  • 750ml fish stock
  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large paellera or wide skillet, heat a generous drizzle of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent. Then, add the garlic and bell pepper, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the grated tomatoes, saffron, and smoked paprika. Cook for about 5 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Add the mixed seafood and sauté for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in the fish stock and bring to a simmer. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Once the stock is bubbling, add the fideuà noodles. Stir gently to combine and ensure the noodles are evenly distributed.
  7. Allow the dish to cook without stirring for about 10-12 minutes, until the noodles have absorbed the liquid and are cooked through.
  8. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

2. Espaguetis a la Putanesca: A Spanish Twist

This Spanish version of the classic Italian puttanesca is a quick and flavorful dish, perfect for busy weeknights. With its rich tomato sauce, olives, and anchovies, it’s sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can of crushed tomatoes)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 anchovy fillets, chopped
  • 100g black olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Stir in the chopped anchovies and cook until they dissolve into the oil.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, olives, red pepper flakes, and oregano. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Toss in the cooked spaghetti, ensuring it is well coated with the sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil.

3. Pasta con Chorizo y Pimientos: A Hearty Delight

Chorizo is a staple in Spanish cuisine, and its smoky flavor adds depth to any dish. This pasta recipe combines chorizo with colorful bell peppers for a hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 400g penne pasta
  • 200g Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers (red and green), sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 100g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the penne pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the sliced chorizo and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the onion, garlic, and bell peppers. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and mix until melted and creamy.
  6. Add the cooked penne to the skillet, tossing to combine.
  7. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve garnished with fresh basil and additional Parmesan if desired.

4. Pesto de Espinacas: A Unique Spinach Pesto Pasta

Pesto is not only delicious but also versatile. This spinach-based pesto brings a Spanish twist to the classic basil sauce and pairs wonderfully with any pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 300g pasta of your choice (fusilli works well)
  • 150g fresh spinach
  • 50g walnuts
  • 50g grated Manchego cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 100ml olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions and drain.
  2. In a food processor, combine spinach, walnuts, Manchego cheese, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Pulse until finely chopped.
  3. With the food processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture becomes a smooth paste.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the spinach pesto until evenly coated.
  5. Garnish with halved cherry tomatoes and serve immediately.

5. Pasta al Horno: Spanish Baked Pasta

Baked pasta is a comforting dish that combines flavors, textures, and the joy of sharing a meal. This Spanish version features a medley of ingredients baked to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 400g macaroni or any pasta
  • 200g ground beef or pork
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 200g frozen peas
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce
  • 100g grated cheese (such as Manchego or mozzarella)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Cook the pasta until al dente and drain.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  4. Add the ground meat and cook until browned. Stir in the diced tomatoes and peas, simmering for about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. In a large baking dish, combine the cooked pasta with the meat mixture. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top and mix well.
  6. Sprinkle grated cheese on top and bake for 25-30 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  7. Remove from the oven, let it cool slightly, and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Conclusion

These delicious Spanish pasta recipes highlight the rich flavors and culinary traditions of Spain. Whether you choose to make the seafood-infused fideuà, the robust espaguetis a la putanesca, or the comforting pasta al horno, each dish is sure to impress. With a focus on fresh ingredients and bold flavors, these recipes invite you to explore the delightful world of Spanish cuisine. So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the aroma of these mouthwatering dishes fill your kitchen. Enjoy your culinary adventure!
