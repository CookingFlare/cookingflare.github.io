Easy Slow Cooker Cuban Mojo Pork Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious, easy-to-make dish that brings the vibrant flavors of Cuba to your dinner table, look no further than this Easy Slow Cooker Cuban Mojo Pork recipe. This dish is perfect for busy weeknights or for serving a crowd during a gathering. The slow cooker does all the heavy lifting, allowing the pork to become tender and infused with the zesty, citrusy flavors of the mojo marinade. Let’s dive into the recipe and explore how to create this flavorful dish that will leave your family and friends craving more.

What is Mojo Pork?

Mojo pork is a traditional Cuban dish that features marinated pork, typically marinated in a blend known as “mojo.” This marinade is a vibrant mix of garlic, citrus juices (like orange and lime), and spices. The name “mojo” comes from the Spanish word for “to cast a spell,” which is fitting because this marinade truly transforms the meat into something magical. It’s often served with rice, black beans, and fried plantains, making it a hearty, satisfying meal.

Ingredients: What You’ll Need

To make this Easy Slow Cooker Cuban Mojo Pork, gather the following ingredients:

For the Pork:

  • 3-4 pounds of pork shoulder (also known as pork butt), trimmed of excess fat
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Mojo Marinade:

  • 1 cup fresh orange juice (about 3-4 oranges)
  • 1/2 cup fresh lime juice (about 3-4 limes)
  • 1 head of garlic, peeled and minced (approximately 10-12 cloves)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced (optional, for heat)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

  1. Trim the Pork: Start by trimming any excess fat from the pork shoulder. While some fat adds flavor, too much can make the dish greasy.

  2. Season the Meat: Rub the pork shoulder with olive oil, and season generously with salt and pepper. This step enhances the flavor of the meat as it cooks.

Step 2: Make the Mojo Marinade

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the fresh orange juice, lime juice, minced garlic, ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, black pepper, and salt. If you’re using cilantro and jalapeño, add those as well.

  2. Mix Well: Whisk the marinade until all ingredients are well combined. The vibrant aroma of the garlic and citrus will make your mouth water!

Step 3: Marinate the Pork

  1. Marinate: Place the seasoned pork shoulder in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the mojo marinade over the pork, ensuring it’s well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. This allows the pork to absorb all those incredible flavors.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Pork

  1. Place in Slow Cooker: After marinating, remove the pork from the refrigerator and place it in the slow cooker. Pour the remaining marinade over the pork.

  2. Cook: Set the slow cooker to low and cook for 8-10 hours, or until the pork is fork-tender. If you’re short on time, you can cook it on high for 4-5 hours, but low and slow is the way to go for the best results.

Step 5: Shred the Pork

  1. Remove and Shred: Once the pork is cooked, carefully remove it from the slow cooker and place it on a cutting board. Use two forks to shred the meat into bite-sized pieces.

  2. Mix with Sauce: Return the shredded pork to the slow cooker, mixing it with the remaining juices in the pot. This step enhances the flavor and keeps the meat moist.

Serving Suggestions

This Easy Slow Cooker Cuban Mojo Pork is incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways:

1. Classic Cuban Plate

Serve the shredded mojo pork alongside white rice, black beans, and fried plantains. This combination is a staple in Cuban cuisine and will transport your taste buds straight to the Caribbean.

2. Tacos

Use the mojo pork as a filling for soft corn tortillas. Top with diced onions, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime for a refreshing twist.

3. Sandwiches

Create delicious Cuban sandwiches by layering the mojo pork with Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread. Press the sandwich in a panini maker or on a skillet for a crispy, melty treat.

4. Salad

For a lighter option, serve the shredded pork over a bed of greens with avocado, cherry tomatoes, and a citrus vinaigrette. This makes for a refreshing and satisfying meal.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you’re lucky enough to have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, simply place the pork in a saucepan over medium heat, adding a splash of orange juice or broth to keep it moist. Stir occasionally until heated through.

Freezing

You can also freeze the mojo pork for longer storage. Place it in a freezer-safe container or resealable bag, and it will keep well for up to 3 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Tips for the Best Mojo Pork

  • Choose Quality Meat: The best results come from using a good quality pork shoulder. Look for meat that has a good amount of marbling, as the fat will keep the meat juicy during cooking.

  • Don’t Rush the Marinade: While marinating for a couple of hours can work, letting it sit overnight or even 24 hours will result in a more flavorful dish.

  • Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a spicier dish, leave the seeds in the jalapeño or add a pinch of cayenne pepper to the marinade.

  • Experiment with Citrus: While orange and lime are traditional, you can experiment with other citrus fruits like grapefruit or blood orange for a unique twist.

Conclusion

This Easy Slow Cooker Cuban Mojo Pork recipe is not just a meal; it’s an experience that brings the flavors of Cuba into your home. With minimal prep and the convenience of a slow cooker, you can serve up a dish that’s rich in flavor and history. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a cozy night in, this dish promises to satisfy your cravings and impress your guests. So gather your ingredients, fire up the slow cooker, and get ready to enjoy a taste of the Caribbean!
