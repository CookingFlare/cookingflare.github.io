Flavors of Hunan Cuisine: Spicy and Savory Recipes


Hunan cuisine, also known as Xiang cuisine, is one of the eight great traditions of Chinese cuisine and is known for its bold flavors, particularly its use of chili peppers and garlic. This style of cooking hails from the western Hunan province in China and is characterized by its spicy, smoky, and savory flavors. Hunan cuisine is a celebration of bold and vibrant tastes, and today, we’re going to explore some classic Hunan recipes that will transport your taste buds to the heart of China.

Hunan Spicy Crispy Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns
  • 5-6 dried red chili peppers
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • Cooking oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with 2 tbsp of soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and 1 tbsp of cornstarch. Set aside and let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated chicken pieces and fry until golden and crispy. Remove from the oil and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, add a little more oil if needed, then add the Sichuan peppercorns and dried red chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant.
  4. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green onions to the wok. Stir-fry for a minute or until aromatic.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the sugar, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and cornstarch. Pour this mixture into the wok and stir until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add the fried chicken back into the wok and toss to coat the pieces in the spicy and savory sauce.
  7. Serve the Hunan spicy crispy chicken hot with steamed rice.

Hunan Style Hot and Sour Eggplant

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized eggplants, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp black vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp chili bean paste
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Cooking oil, for frying
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Heat some cooking oil in a wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the eggplant pieces and fry until golden and tender. Remove from the oil and set aside.
  2. In the same wok, add a little more oil if needed, then add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green onions. Stir-fry for a minute or until aromatic.
  3. Add the chili bean paste to the wok and stir-fry for another minute.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, black vinegar, sugar, chicken or vegetable broth, and cornstarch. Pour this mixture into the wok and stir until the sauce thickens.
  5. Add the fried eggplant back into the wok and toss to coat the pieces in the hot and sour sauce.
  6. Serve the Hunan style hot and sour eggplant hot with steamed rice.

Conclusion

Hunan cuisine is a delightful journey for the taste buds, offering a perfect harmony of spicy, smoky, and savory flavors. These two recipes are just a glimpse into the rich culinary heritage of the Hunan province. Whether you’re a fan of bold flavors or looking to explore new taste sensations, these Hunan dishes are sure to leave a lasting impression. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your wok, and let the magical flavors of Hunan cuisine take center stage in your kitchen!
