Savory Chicken Tetrazzini: A Family Comfort Dish


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes evoke the same sense of nostalgia as Chicken Tetrazzini. This creamy, savory casserole is a family favorite, perfect for cozy dinners or potluck gatherings. With its rich blend of chicken, pasta, and a luscious sauce, Chicken Tetrazzini delivers warmth and satisfaction in every bite. In this post, we will explore the history of this beloved dish, provide a step-by-step recipe, and share tips for customization and serving that will make your Chicken Tetrazzini truly unforgettable.

The History of Chicken Tetrazzini

Chicken Tetrazzini is named after the famous Italian opera singer Luisa Tetrazzini, who was known for her powerful voice and flamboyant personality during the early 20th century. The dish originated in the United States in the 1900s, with various culinary legends attributing its creation to different chefs in San Francisco. It quickly gained popularity, becoming a staple in American households.

Traditionally, Chicken Tetrazzini features cooked chicken, pasta (usually spaghetti or fettuccine), and a creamy sauce made with mushrooms, cheese, and often a splash of sherry. Over the years, cooks have adapted the recipe to fit their tastes and ingredients on hand, leading to a variety of delicious interpretations.

Ingredients for Savory Chicken Tetrazzini

Let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need to create a mouthwatering Chicken Tetrazzini that your family will love:

For the Pasta and Chicken:

  • 12 ounces of spaghetti or fettuccine (uncooked)
  • 3 cups of cooked chicken, shredded or diced (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 1 cup of frozen peas (optional, for added color and sweetness)

For the Creamy Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced (button or cremini mushrooms work well)
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese

For Topping:

  • 1/2 cup of breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti or fettuccine and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot. Set aside while you prepare the sauce.

Step 2: Prepare the Creamy Sauce

  1. In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms, cooking for an additional 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and have released their moisture.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir well, cooking for about 1 minute to eliminate the raw flour taste.
  4. Gradually whisk in the chicken broth, ensuring there are no lumps. Continue to whisk until the sauce thickens and bubbles.
  5. Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream, dried thyme, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Allow the sauce to simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Remove from heat and mix in the cooked chicken and frozen peas (if using). Add the cooked pasta to the sauce, stirring until everything is well combined. Finally, fold in the grated Parmesan and shredded mozzarella cheeses for extra creaminess.

Step 3: Assemble the Casserole

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Transfer the Chicken Tetrazzini mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, spreading it evenly.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, melted butter, and additional Parmesan cheese. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the casserole.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

  1. Bake the Chicken Tetrazzini in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is bubbly.
  2. Remove from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. This will help the casserole set and make it easier to serve.

Tips for Customization

One of the best aspects of Chicken Tetrazzini is its versatility. Here are some ideas to put your unique spin on this classic dish:

Vegetable Additions

Feel free to incorporate additional vegetables to enhance the dish’s flavor and nutrition. Some great options include:

  • Spinach: Add fresh or frozen spinach to the sauce for a pop of color.
  • Bell Peppers: Sauté diced bell peppers with the onions for added sweetness.
  • Zucchini: Grate or dice zucchini and mix it in for extra moisture and texture.

Protein Variations

While chicken is the traditional protein, you can easily switch it up:

  • Turkey: Use leftover turkey from holiday meals for a delightful twist.
  • Seafood: Incorporate cooked shrimp or crab for a luxurious seafood version.
  • Vegetarian: Replace the chicken with cooked chickpeas or tofu for a vegetarian-friendly alternative.

Spice it Up

For those who enjoy a little heat, consider adding some spices:

  • Red Pepper Flakes: Sprinkle in some red pepper flakes for a spicy kick.
  • Smoked Paprika: Add a teaspoon of smoked paprika for a depth of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Chicken Tetrazzini is a complete meal on its own, but you can always enhance your dining experience with some simple sides:

Fresh Salad

A crisp, fresh salad pairs beautifully with the creamy richness of Tetrazzini. Consider a mixed greens salad with a tangy vinaigrette or a classic Caesar salad for a delightful contrast.

Garlic Bread

Serve warm garlic bread on the side to soak up any leftover sauce. The combination of crunchy, buttery bread with the creamy casserole is simply irresistible.

Wine Pairing

If you’re looking to elevate your dinner, consider pairing Chicken Tetrazzini with a light white wine, such as Chardonnay or Sauvignon Blanc. The wine’s acidity complements the creaminess of the dish, making for a harmonious dining experience.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers (which is often the case with this hearty dish), storing them properly is key to maintaining flavor and texture.

Storing

  1. Allow the Chicken Tetrazzini to cool completely before storing.
  2. Transfer leftovers to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 3-4 days.

Reheating

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Place the leftovers in an oven-safe dish, cover with foil, and bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until heated through. You can also microwave individual portions for a quick meal.

Conclusion

Savory Chicken Tetrazzini is more than just a dish; it’s a comforting hug on a plate. Whether you’re enjoying it on a chilly evening or sharing it with loved ones at a gathering, this casserole brings warmth and joy to the table. With its rich, creamy sauce, tender chicken, and flavorful pasta, it’s no wonder that Chicken Tetrazzini has become a cherished family favorite.

By following the steps outlined in this post and personalizing the recipe to fit your tastes, you can create a Chicken Tetrazzini that will delight your family and friends. Embrace the comforting nature of this dish, and let it become a staple in your home for years to come. Happy cooking!
