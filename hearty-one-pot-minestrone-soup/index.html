Hearty One Pot Minestrone Soup


Are you in the mood for a comforting and nutritious meal that’s easy to make? Look no further than this hearty one pot minestrone soup recipe. Packed with vegetables, beans, and savory flavors, this Italian classic is the perfect dish to warm you up on a chilly evening or to enjoy with family and friends. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create this delicious and satisfying soup in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup small pasta (such as ditalini or elbow macaroni)
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds, until fragrant.

  3. Add the diced carrots, celery, zucchini, and green beans to the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables begin to soften.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.

  5. Add the cannellini beans, kidney beans, and pasta to the pot. Continue to simmer for an additional 10-12 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente.

  6. Season the soup with dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  7. Ladle the minestrone soup into bowls, garnish with fresh basil, and serve with grated Parmesan cheese on the side, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a heartier version, you can add cooked and crumbled Italian sausage to the soup.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on what’s in season or what you have on hand.
  • Leftover minestrone soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, making it a convenient option for meal prep.

Why We Love This Soup

Minestrone soup is beloved for its versatility and ability to accommodate a wide range of ingredients. It’s a great way to incorporate an assortment of vegetables into your diet while enjoying a comforting and flavorful meal. The combination of beans and pasta provides a satisfying texture, making this soup a filling and nutritious option for lunch or dinner.

Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this one pot minestrone soup recipe is a fantastic addition to your repertoire. Its simplicity, robust flavors, and wholesome ingredients make it a standout dish that’s sure to become a favorite in your household.

So, next time you’re looking for a satisfying and nourishing meal that’s easy to prepare, give this hearty one pot minestrone soup a try. It’s a classic Italian delight that never fails to bring joy to the table. Enjoy!
