Mouthwatering Thanksgiving Side Dishes for Every Table


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and of course, delicious food. While the turkey usually steals the spotlight, Thanksgiving side dishes are the unsung heroes of the holiday feast. These dishes not only complement the main course but also provide an opportunity to add variety and flavor to the table. Whether you’re hosting the big meal or bringing a dish to share, there’s a side for every taste and dietary preference.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best Thanksgiving side dishes that will delight your guests and make your holiday meal unforgettable. From creamy mashed potatoes to vibrant vegetable medleys, these recipes are sure to enhance your Thanksgiving spread and leave everyone asking for seconds.

Classic Mashed Potatoes

You can’t go wrong with mashed potatoes at any Thanksgiving table. Creamy, buttery, and comforting, they’re the perfect accompaniment to turkey and gravy. While traditional mashed potatoes are always a hit, you can elevate this dish with a few simple twists.

Ingredients:

  • 5 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes (or Russet potatoes)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives or parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Peel and cut the potatoes into uniform chunks. Place them in a large pot and cover with cold water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and let the potatoes simmer until fork-tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  3. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Mash using a potato masher or a hand mixer until smooth.
  4. In a separate saucepan, heat the cream and butter together until the butter is fully melted.
  5. Pour the cream mixture over the mashed potatoes, and stir until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Garnish with chopped chives or parsley before serving.

Variations:

  • Garlic Mashed Potatoes: Add roasted garlic to the potatoes before mashing for a flavorful twist.
  • Cheddar Mashed Potatoes: Stir in shredded cheddar cheese for a cheesy version of the classic.

Green Bean Almondine

This elegant side dish is a simple yet flavorful way to prepare green beans. The crunchy almonds and bright lemon zest pair beautifully with the tender beans, creating a side dish that’s as delicious as it is visually appealing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup sliced almonds
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the green beans and cook for 3-4 minutes, until tender but still crisp.
  2. Drain the beans and immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Drain again.
  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced almonds and toast them for 2-3 minutes until golden brown.
  4. Add the green beans to the skillet and toss to coat in the butter and almonds. Stir in the lemon juice and zest.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.

Tip:

For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a pinch of red pepper flakes for a bit of heat.

Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallows

Sweet potatoes are a staple at any Thanksgiving meal, and this sweet potato casserole with marshmallows is a crowd-pleaser. It combines the natural sweetness of the potatoes with a gooey, toasted marshmallow topping that’s simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • ¼ cup chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the sweet potatoes in a large pot of water and bring to a boil. Cook until the sweet potatoes are fork-tender (about 15-20 minutes). Drain and mash the potatoes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, butter, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture to a greased baking dish. Top with mini marshmallows and chopped pecans (if using).
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden brown. Serve hot.

Variations:

  • Nut-Free Version: Leave out the pecans or replace them with crushed graham crackers for an added crunch.
  • Spiced Sweet Potatoes: Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or paprika to give the casserole a spicy kick.

Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze

If you’re looking for a dish that’s both savory and sweet, roasted Brussels sprouts with bacon and balsamic glaze are a perfect choice. The crispy bacon adds richness, while the balsamic vinegar provides a sweet and tangy contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread them out on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the Brussels sprouts for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  4. While the Brussels sprouts are roasting, cook the chopped bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy.
  5. Drain the bacon on paper towels and set aside.
  6. Once the Brussels sprouts are done, drizzle them with balsamic vinegar and top with the crispy bacon.
  7. Toss to combine and serve immediately.

Tip:

For a more complex flavor, add a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese over the Brussels sprouts before roasting.

Cornbread Stuffing

Stuffing is another Thanksgiving classic, and cornbread stuffing is a delicious variation of the traditional bread stuffing. The slightly sweet flavor of the cornbread pairs wonderfully with savory herbs and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of cornbread, cubed (about 4 cups)
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, and garlic, and sauté until softened (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cornbread cubes, sautéed vegetables, chicken broth, sage, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish and cover with aluminum foil.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, removing the foil during the last 10 minutes to allow the top to crisp up. Serve warm.

Variations:

  • Sausage Cornbread Stuffing: Add cooked sausage to the stuffing mixture for added flavor and texture.
  • Vegetarian Version: Use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth to make it vegetarian-friendly.

Cranberry Sauce with Orange Zest

Cranberry sauce is an essential Thanksgiving side, and adding a bit of orange zest can elevate the flavor. The citrusy note complements the tartness of the cranberries, creating a perfect balance of sweet and sour.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, and orange juice. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cranberries have burst and the sauce has thickened.
  3. Stir in the orange zest and let the sauce cool to room temperature before serving.

Tip:

For a spiced version, add a cinnamon stick or a pinch of ground cinnamon while simmering the sauce.

Conclusion

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without a table full of delicious side dishes that complement the star of the show, the turkey. From creamy mashed potatoes and savory Brussels sprouts to sweet and tangy cranberry sauce, these side dishes offer something for every palate. Whether you stick to the classics or try a few new twists, these recipes are guaranteed to impress your guests and make your Thanksgiving feast memorable.

Remember, side dishes are the perfect opportunity to get creative and try new flavors. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different herbs, spices, and textures to make each dish uniquely yours. Enjoy your Thanksgiving celebration with these mouthwatering side dishes that will leave everyone asking for the recipe!
