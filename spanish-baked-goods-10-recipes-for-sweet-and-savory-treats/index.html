Spanish Baked Goods: 10 Recipes for Sweet and Savory Treats


Spanish cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, colors, and textures, renowned worldwide for its rich traditions and deep cultural roots. Among the many delights it offers, Spanish baked goods hold a special place, blending simplicity with exquisite taste. From flaky pastries infused with citrus zest to hearty savory pies bursting with local ingredients, these treats reveal the diversity and passion embedded in Spain’s culinary heritage. In this post, we explore ten authentic and lesser-known Spanish baked goods recipes that will transport your kitchen straight to the Iberian Peninsula.

1. Ensaimada: The Spiral Sweet Pastry from Mallorca

Ensaimada is a light, airy, and slightly sweet spiral pastry that originated in Mallorca. It is traditionally dusted with powdered sugar and flaky in texture thanks to its unique preparation method involving lard — or ensaïm, from which it gets its name.

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 100g sugar
  • 10g dry yeast
  • 150ml warm water
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100ml vegetable oil
  • 100g pork lard (or substitute with softened butter)
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Directions:

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water with a teaspoon of sugar, let it activate for about 10 minutes.
  2. Mix flour and sugar in a bowl, create a well in the center.
  3. Add eggs and oil to the flour mixture, then pour in the yeast mixture gradually. Knead until smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover dough and let it rise for 1.5 hours.
  5. Divide dough into portions, roll each into a large thin rectangle, brush with pork lard evenly.
  6. Roll the dough into a log, then coil it into a spiral shape on a baking sheet. Repeat for all portions.
  7. Let the shaped pastries rest for an hour to rise again.
  8. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes until golden.
  9. Dust generously with powdered sugar once cooled.

2. Tarta de Santiago: Almond Cake of Galicia

This iconic almond cake is a symbol of Galicia and the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage. It uses ground almonds and simple ingredients to create a moist, dense cake topped traditionally with the cross of Saint James made with powdered sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 250g ground almonds
  • 200g sugar
  • 5 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Powdered sugar for decorating

Directions:

  1. Beat eggs and sugar until fluffy.
  2. Fold in ground almonds, lemon zest, and cinnamon until combined well.
  3. Pour batter into a greased cake tin lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake at 170°C (340°F) for 30-40 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
  5. Let cool completely, then dust with powdered sugar using a stencil to mark the St. James cross.

3. Coca de Recapte: Savory Flatbread from Catalonia

Coca de Recapte is a rustic flatbread topped with roasted vegetables and savory ingredients like anchovies, sausage, or peppers. It’s Catalonia’s answer to pizza with local flair.

Ingredients:

  • 400g all-purpose flour
  • 200ml warm water
  • 10g dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 roasted red pepper, sliced
  • 1 roasted green pepper, sliced
  • 100g local sausage (chorizo or botifarra), thinly sliced
  • 4 anchovy fillets (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water. Mix flour, salt, and olive oil, then add yeast mixture slowly, knead to a smooth dough.
  2. Let the dough rise for about 1 hour.
  3. Roll dough out onto a baking sheet, brush with olive oil.
  4. Arrange roasted peppers, sausage, and anchovies evenly over the dough.
  5. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  6. Bake at 200°C (390°F) for 20-25 minutes until crust is golden and crisp.

4. Magdalenas: Spanish Lemon-Scented Muffins

Magdalenas are Spanish treats similar to muffins but lighter and fluffy with a subtle lemon aroma, perfect for breakfast or tea.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 150g sugar
  • 150ml olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Beat eggs and sugar together until pale and creamy.
  2. Add oil, lemon zest, and vanilla extract, mixing gently.
  3. Sift flour and baking powder, fold it carefully into wet ingredients.
  4. Pour batter into muffin tins lined with paper cups filling three-quarters full.
  5. Let batter rest for about 30 minutes to enhance fluffiness.
  6. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes until puffed and golden.
  7. Cool before serving.

5. Empanadas Gallegas: Galician Meat or Tuna Pies

Empanadas Gallegas are delicious hand pies filled with savory mixtures like tuna in tomato sauce or spiced pork, encased in flaky dough.

Ingredients for Dough:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 200ml warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 50ml olive oil
  • 10g dry yeast

Ingredients for Filling (Tuna Version):

  • 2 cans of tuna in olive oil, drained
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200ml tomato sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Directions:

  1. Prepare dough by dissolving yeast in water, mixing with flour, salt, and olive oil. Knead until elastic and let rise 1 hour.
  2. For filling, sauté onion, bell pepper, and garlic in olive oil until soft. Add tomato sauce, simmer 10 minutes. Stir in tuna, season to taste.
  3. Roll dough into two thin rounds.
  4. Place filling on one dough round, leaving edges free. Cover with the second round and seal edges.
  5. Bake at 200°C (390°F) for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Cool slightly before slicing.

6. Panellets: Catalan Marzipan Cookies

Panellets are bite-sized almond marzipan cookies traditionally eaten during the All Saints Day holiday but enjoyed year-round for their nutty, sweet flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 250g ground almonds
  • 250g powdered sugar
  • 2 egg whites
  • Zest of 1 lemon and 1 orange
  • Pine nuts for coating
  • Whole cloves (optional)

Directions:

  1. Mix almond flour, powdered sugar, citrus zest and egg whites to form a pliable dough.
  2. Shape dough into small balls, then roll in pine nuts, pressing gently to coat completely.
  3. Place on parchment paper and insert a clove into the center of each cookie if desired.
  4. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 10-12 minutes until pine nuts are lightly toasted.
  5. Cool before serving.

7. Rosquillas: Madrid-Style Fried and Baked Doughnuts

Rosquillas are traditional Spanish doughnuts that vary by region. The baked ones from Madrid are soft, subtly sweet, and often flavored with anise.

Ingredients:

  • 300g all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground anise seed
  • 3 eggs
  • 150g sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Vegetable oil for frying (if frying)

Directions:

  1. Beat eggs with sugar until fluffy.
  2. Add lemon zest and anise seeds.
  3. Gradually mix in flour and baking powder to form dough.
  4. Shape dough into rings about 2 inches in diameter.
  5. For baking: Place on a parchment-lined tray and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15 minutes.
  6. For frying: Heat oil and fry rings until golden, drain on paper towels.
  7. Dust with sugar before serving.

8. Bizcocho de Yogur: Spanish Yogurt Cake

Bizcocho de yogur is a moist, simple sponge cake that can be flavored with lemon or vanilla—quintessentially Spanish and often dubbed the “easy cake.”

Ingredients:

  • 1 natural yogurt (125g, use the pot as a measure)
  • 3 pots sugar
  • 3 pots flour
  • 1/2 pot olive oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 baking powder packet (about 15g)
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a bowl, beat eggs and sugar until combined.
  3. Add yogurt, olive oil, and lemon zest; mix well.
  4. Sift flour and baking powder, fold into wet ingredients carefully.
  5. Pour batter into a greased cake pan.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until golden and set.
  7. Let cool completely before slicing.

9. Mantecados: Crumbly Almond Shortbread

Mantecados are crumbly, buttery shortbread cookies spiced with cinnamon and sometimes anise, a seasonal staple of Andalusia especially at Christmas.

Ingredients:

  • 300g all-purpose flour
  • 200g lard or softened butter
  • 200g powdered sugar
  • 100g ground almonds
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Directions:

  1. Mix lard or butter with powdered sugar until creamy.
  2. Add ground almonds, cinnamon, and lemon zest.
  3. Gradually incorporate flour until dough forms.
  4. Shape dough into small discs and place on a baking tray.
  5. Refrigerate for 30 minutes before baking.
  6. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes until set but not browned.
  7. Cool before serving.

10. Torrijas: Spanish-Style French Toast

Torrijas are slices of day-old bread soaked in milk and egg, fried to golden perfection and sweetened with sugar and cinnamon syrup or honey—a beloved Easter treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of stale white bread, sliced about 2 cm thick
  • 500ml milk
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Ground cinnamon and sugar for dusting

Directions:

  1. Heat milk with sugar, cinnamon stick, and lemon zest until just boiling. Let cool.
  2. Soak bread slices in the flavored milk until saturated but not falling apart.
  3. Dip soaked bread in beaten eggs, coat thoroughly.
  4. Fry in hot olive oil until golden on both sides. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Dust generously with sugar and ground cinnamon. Serve warm.

Final Thoughts

These ten Spanish baked goods recipes offer a window into Spain’s rich culinary past and vibrant present. From the delicate sweetness of Ensaimadas and Magdalenas to hearty empanadas and savory Coca de Recapte, they showcase the variety and versatility of Spanish baking traditions. Whether you’re craving something sweet or a savory bite, these authentic treats provide fantastic inspiration to explore the flavors of Spain in your kitchen.

Cooking these recipes will not only fill your home with enchanting aromas but also bring you closer to the heart of Spain’s diverse and delicious food culture. Enjoy baking, sharing, and tasting these wonderful treats that have delighted generations. ¡Buen provecho!
