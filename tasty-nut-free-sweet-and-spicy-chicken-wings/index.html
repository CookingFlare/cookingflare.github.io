Tasty Nut-Free Sweet and Spicy Chicken Wings


If you’re looking for a delicious and crowd-pleasing appetizer or main dish, these nut-free sweet and spicy chicken wings are a must-try. They are perfect for game nights, parties, or simply as a flavorful addition to your weeknight dinner. This recipe combines the perfect balance of sweetness and heat, making it a hit with both kids and adults. The best part? You don’t need to worry about any nut ingredients, making it safe for those with nut allergies. Let’s dive into the tantalizing details of this scrumptious dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of chicken wings
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • Green onions, thinly sliced (for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil for easy cleanup.

  2. Marinade: In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, sriracha sauce, minced garlic, grated ginger, paprika, black pepper, olive oil, and apple cider vinegar until well combined. This flavorful marinade will be the key to the irresistible taste of the chicken wings.

  3. Coating the Wings: Add the chicken wings to the bowl with the marinade and toss until they are evenly coated. Let the wings marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse into the meat.

  4. Baking: Arrange the marinated chicken wings on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, turning them over halfway through the cooking time. The wings should be golden brown and crispy.

  5. Glazing: While the wings are baking, transfer the remaining marinade to a small saucepan. Simmer over medium heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens into a glaze.

  6. Finishing Touches: Once the wings are cooked through and crispy, brush them with the prepared glaze. Return the wings to the oven for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the glaze to caramelize slightly.

  7. Serve: Remove the wings from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes. Garnish with thinly sliced green onions and sesame seeds for a pop of color and extra flavor.

Serving Suggestions

These nut-free sweet and spicy chicken wings are incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways. You can enjoy them as an appetizer with your favorite dipping sauce, such as ranch or blue cheese dressing. They also make a fantastic main course when paired with steamed rice and a side of crunchy Asian slaw. For a more casual setting, these wings are perfect for a game day spread, alongside other finger foods and snacks.

Conclusion

The combination of flavors and the simplicity of preparation make them a go-to option for any occasion.
