Easy-to-Make Picnic Pies and Quiches


When it comes to outdoor dining, there’s nothing quite like a picnic. The combination of delicious food and beautiful surroundings creates a magical experience that’s hard to beat. And what better way to elevate your picnic than with easy-to-make pies and quiches? These handheld treats are not only flavorful but also convenient to pack and enjoy on the go. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering recipes for picnic pies and quiches that are sure to impress.

1. Classic Quiche Lorraine

Let’s start with a timeless favorite: Quiche Lorraine. This French dish is known for its rich and creamy filling, flavored with bacon, cheese, and onions. To make this picnic-friendly, we’ll transform it into individual-sized quiches.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup of shredded Swiss cheese
  • 1/2 cup of diced onions
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and cut it into individual circles that will fit into your muffin tin.
  3. Press the crust circles into the muffin tin, ensuring they cover the bottom and sides.
  4. In a skillet, sauté the diced onions until they become translucent.
  5. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  6. Place a tablespoon of crumbled bacon, shredded Swiss cheese, and sautéed onions into each crust.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the fillings, filling each crust about 3/4 full.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the quiches are golden brown and set in the center.
  9. Allow them to cool before packing them for your picnic.

2. Mini Chicken and Mushroom Pies

Next up, we have a savory and comforting option: Mini Chicken and Mushroom Pies. These handheld pies are packed with tender chicken, earthy mushrooms, and a creamy sauce. They are guaranteed to be a hit at your picnic!

Ingredients:

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts
  • 2 cups of cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of diced onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crusts and cut them into circles that will fit into your muffin tin.
  3. Press the crust circles into the muffin tin, ensuring they cover the bottom and sides.
  4. In a skillet, melt the butter and sauté the diced onions, garlic, and mushrooms until they are softened.
  5. Stir in the flour and cook for an additional minute.
  6. Slowly whisk in the chicken broth and heavy cream, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens.
  7. Add the shredded chicken to the sauce and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Spoon the chicken and mushroom filling into each crust.
  9. Place a second crust circle on top and seal the edges by pressing them together with a fork.
  10. Cut a small slit in the top of each pie to allow steam to escape.
  11. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the pies are golden brown and bubbling.
  12. Let them cool slightly before packing them for your picnic.

3. Spinach and Feta Hand Pies

For a vegetarian option that’s bursting with flavor, try these Spinach and Feta Hand Pies. Packed with nutritious spinach and tangy feta cheese, these pies are an excellent choice for picnics.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts
  • 4 cups of fresh spinach
  • 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup of diced onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crusts and cut them into circles that will fit into your muffin tin.
  3. Press the crust circles into the muffin tin, ensuring they cover the bottom and sides.
  4. In a skillet, heat the olive oil and sauté the diced onions and garlic until they become translucent.
  5. Add the spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
  7. Stir in the crumbled feta cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Spoon the spinach and feta filling into each crust.
  9. Place a second crust circle on top and seal the edges by pressing them together with a fork.
  10. Cut a small slit in the top of each pie to allow steam to escape.
  11. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the pies are golden brown and crispy.
  12. Allow them to cool before packing them for your picnic.

Conclusion

Picnic pies and quiches are the perfect addition to any outdoor gathering. With their delicious fillings and portable nature, they are a convenient and flavorful option for enjoying a meal in nature. Whether you choose the classic Quiche Lorraine, the comforting Mini Chicken and Mushroom Pies, or the vegetarian Spinach and Feta Hand Pies, these recipes are sure to impress your picnic companions. So pack your basket, grab a blanket, and get ready to indulge in these easy-to-make treats on your next outdoor adventure!
