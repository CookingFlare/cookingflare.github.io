Crisp Crunch of Fall: Unbelievable Seasonal Squash Recipes to Warm Your Soul


As the leaves begin to change, the air gets crisp, and the days shorten, there’s something comforting about diving into fall-inspired recipes. The star of this season’s menu? Squash. From its vibrant hues to its diverse flavors, squash embodies everything that’s beautiful about autumn. Whether you’re roasting, baking, or simmering it into soups, squash brings a warmth to the table like no other vegetable.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of squash recipes that capture the essence of fall and will surely warm your soul. These dishes will not only fill your kitchen with irresistible aromas but also nourish your body with the nutrients and flavors that fall offers. So, let’s dive into these unbelievable squash recipes!

1. Roasted Butternut Squash with Maple and Thyme

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 tsp dried thyme)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, maple syrup, thyme, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the squash cubes out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the squash is golden brown and tender.
  5. Remove from the oven and serve warm. This dish is perfect as a side or even on top of a salad.

The natural sweetness of butternut squash pairs wonderfully with the earthy thyme and rich maple syrup, creating a comforting dish that is just perfect for chilly fall evenings.

2. Spaghetti Squash Primavera

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Parmesan cheese, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise, remove the seeds, and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the squash halves cut side down on a baking sheet and roast for 35-40 minutes, or until tender. Once cooked, use a fork to scrape out the “spaghetti” strands.
  4. While the squash roasts, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add garlic, bell peppers, zucchini, and tomatoes. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add the spaghetti squash strands to the pan, toss everything together, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Stir in fresh basil, and serve with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

Spaghetti squash is an excellent low-carb alternative to pasta and works beautifully with the seasonal vegetables of fall. The slight crunch of the squash pairs well with the sautéed veggies, making this a light yet satisfying dish.

3. Acorn Squash Soup with Coconut Milk

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the acorn squash halves cut side down on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes until soft.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and carrot, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Once the squash is roasted, scoop the flesh into the pot with the sautéed vegetables.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, coconut milk, cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring the soup to a simmer, then use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, transfer the soup to a blender in batches.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary before serving.

This acorn squash soup with coconut milk is the epitome of fall comfort. The smooth texture of the squash pairs beautifully with the richness of coconut milk, while the spices add a warming, earthy flavor to each spoonful.

4. Delicata Squash and Kale Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 delicata squashes, sliced into half moons
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups kale, chopped
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup roasted sunflower seeds
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the delicata squash slices on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast the squash for 20-25 minutes, turning halfway through, until tender and lightly browned.
  3. While the squash roasts, massage the chopped kale with a little olive oil to soften it. You can also use a pinch of salt to help with this process.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar and honey to create a dressing.
  5. Once the squash is roasted, combine it with the kale in a large bowl. Add the cranberries, sunflower seeds, and goat cheese.
  6. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently. Serve immediately for a fresh, vibrant fall salad.

Delicata squash is a delightfully sweet and tender variety that’s perfect for roasting. Combined with the earthy kale and the sweet-tart cranberries, this salad is a hearty and nourishing option for any fall meal.

5. Pumpkin Squash Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 small sugar pumpkin or 2 cups canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. If using fresh pumpkin, roast it in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for 30-40 minutes, or until soft. Scoop out the flesh and mash it. If using canned puree, skip this step.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir to toast it lightly for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Slowly pour in the white wine, stirring until absorbed. Then, begin adding the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Allow the liquid to absorb before adding more broth.
  5. After about 20-25 minutes, once the rice is cooked and creamy, stir in the pumpkin puree, Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with additional cheese if desired.

This pumpkin squash risotto is a creamy, comforting dish that highlights the essence of fall. The natural sweetness of pumpkin and the richness of Parmesan create an indulgent meal that pairs wonderfully with a glass of white wine.

6. Stuffed Kabocha Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 small kabocha squashes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup black beans, cooked
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Slice the tops off the kabocha squashes and scoop out the seeds. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Place the squashes on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  3. While the squash roasts, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, cilantro, cumin, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Once the squashes are tender, remove them from the oven and carefully stuff them with the quinoa mixture. Top with shredded cheese if desired.
  5. Return the stuffed squashes to the oven for an additional 10 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve warm as a delicious main dish or a hearty side.

Kabocha squash has a deep, nutty flavor and soft texture that makes it the perfect vessel for stuffing. This dish combines the wholesome goodness of quinoa and beans, making it a filling and flavorful meal that embodies the spirit of autumn.

Final Thoughts on Fall Squash Recipes

As the fall season envelops you with its cool embrace, these squash recipes are the perfect way to celebrate the harvest. From roasted dishes to creamy soups and fresh salads, squash offers an incredible range of textures and flavors that cater to any meal or occasion. The versatility of this seasonal ingredient means that you can experiment with it in a variety of ways, all while staying grounded in the heartwarming comfort of fall.

Whether you’re looking to create a cozy dinner for yourself or an impressive spread for guests, these squash recipes will certainly make the season brighter, tastier, and full of warmth. So, next time you’re at the market, be sure to grab a squash or two—your soul will thank you!
