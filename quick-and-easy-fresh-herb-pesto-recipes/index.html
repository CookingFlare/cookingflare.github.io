Quick and Easy Fresh Herb Pesto Recipes


Pesto is a versatile and delicious sauce that can elevate any dish, from pasta to sandwiches, and even as a dip for fresh veggies. The traditional basil pesto is well-loved, but experimenting with different fresh herbs can unlock a world of flavors. In this post, we will explore several quick and easy fresh herb pesto recipes that are perfect for any occasion. These recipes not only highlight the vibrant flavors of fresh herbs but also offer a healthy dose of nutrients.

What is Pesto?

Pesto is an Italian sauce that originated in Genoa. The classic recipe consists of fresh basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil, all blended together to create a smooth, vibrant green paste. While basil pesto is the most popular variety, the beauty of pesto lies in its adaptability. You can use a range of herbs and nuts, allowing you to customize the pesto to suit your taste preferences or what you have on hand.

Essential Ingredients for Fresh Herb Pesto

Regardless of the type of pesto you’re making, there are some foundational ingredients that you’ll need:

  1. Fresh Herbs: The star of the show. Basil is traditional, but you can also use parsley, cilantro, dill, mint, or even arugula.
  2. Nuts: Pine nuts are classic, but walnuts, almonds, or pecans can add a unique flavor.
  3. Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a pungent kick to your pesto. Adjust the amount based on your preference.
  4. Cheese: Parmesan is commonly used, but you can experiment with other hard cheeses or even nutritional yeast for a vegan option.
  5. Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil provides richness and helps blend the ingredients smoothly.
  6. Acid: Lemon juice or vinegar can brighten the flavors and balance the richness.

Basic Method for Making Pesto

The process for making fresh herb pesto is simple and can be done in a few steps:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Rinse the herbs and dry them thoroughly. Gather the nuts, garlic, cheese, and olive oil.
  2. Blend: In a food processor, combine the herbs, nuts, and garlic. Pulse until finely chopped.
  3. Add Cheese and Oil: Sprinkle in the cheese and drizzle in the olive oil. Blend until you reach your desired consistency.
  4. Adjust to Taste: Taste your pesto and add salt, pepper, or more lemon juice as needed.

Now that you know the basics, let’s dive into some quick and easy fresh herb pesto recipes!

1. Classic Basil Pesto

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the basil leaves, Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, and garlic. Pulse until finely chopped.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while blending until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This classic basil pesto is perfect for pasta dishes, spreading on sandwiches, or drizzling over grilled vegetables.

2. Cilantro Lime Pesto

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup walnuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Combine the cilantro, walnuts, garlic, and lime juice in a food processor. Pulse until finely chopped.
  2. With the processor running, slowly add the olive oil until well combined.
  3. Season with salt to taste.

Cilantro lime pesto adds a zesty twist to tacos, grilled fish, or fresh salads.

3. Parsley and Almond Pesto

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, blend the parsley, almonds, garlic, and Parmesan cheese until finely chopped.
  2. Gradually add in the olive oil while blending until creamy.
  3. Season with salt and pepper as desired.

This parsley and almond pesto works wonderfully as a dressing for pasta salad or as a dip for bread.

4. Mint and Pea Pesto

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup cooked peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and lemon juice to taste

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the mint, peas, pine nuts, and garlic. Pulse until smooth.
  2. While blending, drizzle in the olive oil until you achieve a creamy consistency.
  3. Season with salt and a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness.

Mint and pea pesto is a refreshing option that pairs beautifully with grilled meats or as a spread on crostini.

5. Arugula and Walnut Pesto

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh arugula
  • 1/2 cup walnuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend the arugula, walnuts, garlic, and Pecorino cheese in a food processor until finely chopped.
  2. Slowly pour in the olive oil while blending until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper.

This peppery arugula and walnut pesto adds a delicious kick to pasta, pizza, or as a topping for grilled vegetables.

Tips for Perfect Pesto

1. Use Fresh Ingredients

The quality of your ingredients will directly affect the flavor of your pesto. Use fresh, vibrant herbs and high-quality olive oil for the best results.

2. Adjust Texture

If you prefer a chunkier pesto, pulse the ingredients less. For a smoother sauce, blend longer and add more olive oil as needed.

3. Store Properly

Pesto can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. To maintain its vibrant color, drizzle a thin layer of olive oil on top before sealing.

4. Freeze for Later

Pesto freezes well! Consider portioning it into ice cube trays and freezing. Once frozen, transfer the cubes to a resealable bag for easy use in the future.

Creative Uses for Pesto

While pesto is often associated with pasta, there are countless ways to enjoy this delicious sauce:

  • Spread on Sandwiches: Use pesto as a flavorful spread on sandwiches or wraps for an extra kick.
  • Pizza Sauce: Substitute traditional tomato sauce with pesto for a unique pizza experience.
  • Marinade for Proteins: Use pesto as a marinade for chicken, fish, or tofu before grilling or roasting.
  • Dipping Sauce: Serve pesto as a dip for fresh vegetables, breadsticks, or crackers.

Conclusion

Fresh herb pesto is a delightful and easy way to incorporate vibrant flavors into your meals. With just a few simple ingredients and a food processor, you can create a variety of delicious pestos that will elevate your culinary creations. Whether you stick with the classic basil or venture into the world of cilantro, mint, or arugula, your taste buds will thank you. So grab your fresh herbs and start blending today!
