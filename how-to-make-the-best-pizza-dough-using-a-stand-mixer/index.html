How to Make the Best Pizza Dough Using a Stand Mixer


Making homemade pizza is a rewarding experience. From choosing your toppings to stretching out the dough, the process is a fun, creative, and satisfying journey. One of the key elements to crafting an excellent pizza is the dough. Achieving the perfect pizza dough can seem daunting, but with the help of a stand mixer, it’s easier than ever to get a consistent, smooth, and elastic dough that will bake up beautifully in your oven.

In this guide, we will walk you through the steps of making the best pizza dough using a stand mixer. We’ll cover the ingredients you need, the proper mixing techniques, tips for kneading, and how to achieve that perfect texture every time.

Why Use a Stand Mixer for Pizza Dough?

While it’s possible to make pizza dough by hand, using a stand mixer can save you time and energy while ensuring your dough comes out perfectly every time. Stand mixers, such as those from KitchenAid, are designed to knead dough effectively. The dough hook attachment mimics the motion of hand-kneading, ensuring the dough is properly developed without tiring out your arms.

Using a stand mixer also allows for better consistency, as the mixer does the heavy lifting, ensuring the ingredients are evenly distributed and the dough texture is smooth and elastic. Plus, you don’t have to worry about overworking the dough with your hands, which can happen when kneading by hand.

Ingredients for the Perfect Pizza Dough

To get started, you’ll need a few basic ingredients. For the best results, measure everything accurately and use high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Flour: The foundation of any pizza dough is flour. Use high-protein bread flour for a chewier, more elastic dough. You can also use all-purpose flour, but bread flour tends to yield better results.
  • Water: Warm water helps activate the yeast, ensuring your dough rises properly. The ideal water temperature is around 110°F (43°C). Too hot or too cold water can affect yeast activation.
  • Yeast: Active dry yeast or instant yeast can both be used. Instant yeast is often preferred for stand mixer pizza dough, as it doesn’t require proofing.
  • Salt: Salt adds flavor to the dough and helps control yeast activity, ensuring the dough doesn’t rise too quickly.
  • Olive oil: Olive oil adds richness and flavor to the dough, contributing to a tender crust.
  • Sugar (optional): A small amount of sugar can help feed the yeast and improve the dough’s rise, but it’s optional depending on your preference.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Pizza Dough in a Stand Mixer

1. Prepare the Dry Ingredients

Start by adding the flour and salt to the bowl of your stand mixer. Use the paddle attachment to gently mix the ingredients together. This step ensures the salt is evenly distributed throughout the flour, which helps create an even dough.

2. Activate the Yeast

In a separate bowl, combine the warm water and sugar (if using). Sprinkle the yeast over the surface of the water and stir gently to combine. Allow the mixture to sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy and bubbly. This indicates that the yeast is active and ready to work.

If you’re using instant yeast, you can skip this proofing step and directly add the yeast to the flour mixture along with the dry ingredients.

3. Add the Wet Ingredients

Once the yeast is activated, pour it into the stand mixer bowl with the flour and salt mixture. Add the olive oil to the mixture as well. Switch your stand mixer attachment to the dough hook.

4. Mix the Dough

Start the mixer on low speed to prevent flour from flying out of the bowl. Gradually increase the speed to medium-low as the ingredients begin to combine. Mix the dough for about 2-3 minutes, or until all the ingredients are fully incorporated and the dough starts to come together into a ball.

At this point, the dough may appear slightly sticky, but it should be pulling away from the sides of the bowl. If it’s too wet and sticky, add flour a tablespoon at a time until it reaches the desired consistency. Conversely, if it’s too dry, add water a teaspoon at a time.

5. Knead the Dough

Once the dough has come together, continue mixing it on medium speed with the dough hook for 6-8 minutes. The dough should become smooth and elastic, with a slightly tacky texture. You can test the dough by performing the “windowpane test”: pinch off a small piece of dough and stretch it between your fingers. If it forms a thin, translucent membrane without tearing, it’s ready.

If the dough still feels dense or sticky, continue mixing and kneading for another 1-2 minutes.

6. Let the Dough Rise

Once the dough is properly kneaded, it’s time to let it rise. Lightly oil a clean bowl and place the dough inside, turning it once to coat it in oil. Cover the bowl with a damp towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. If you’re short on time, you can speed up the rising process by placing the bowl in a warm oven with the light on.

Tips for Perfect Pizza Dough Every Time

  • Use the right flour: While all-purpose flour can work, bread flour provides more protein and results in a chewier, airier dough.
  • Proper yeast activation: Be sure your yeast is fresh and active. If you see no bubbles or foam after proofing, the yeast may be expired, and you’ll need to use a fresh packet.
  • Water temperature matters: Too hot water can kill the yeast, while too cold water will not activate it properly. Always use water that’s around 110°F (43°C).
  • Don’t over-knead: Over-kneading can make your dough tough. Once the dough is smooth and elastic, stop kneading.
  • Let the dough rise slowly: For the best flavor and texture, allow the dough to rise slowly. You can even refrigerate it for 24 hours for enhanced flavor.
  • Use olive oil: Olive oil not only adds flavor but also keeps the dough from becoming too dry. It helps with the dough’s elasticity and flavor development.

Shaping and Baking the Pizza Dough

After the dough has risen, it’s time to shape it into a pizza crust. Punch down the dough gently to remove any large air bubbles. Then, divide the dough into individual portions if making multiple pizzas.

Roll the dough out on a floured surface or stretch it by hand to your desired thickness. If you prefer a thicker crust, keep it a bit thicker when shaping. If you like a thin and crispy crust, roll it out thinner.

Once shaped, transfer the dough to a pizza stone or baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Add your favorite toppings and bake at a high temperature, typically 475°F (245°C), for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy.

Storing and Freezing Pizza Dough

If you don’t plan on using all of your pizza dough immediately, you can store it in the fridge or freezer for later use.

  • Refrigerator: After the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into portions. Store it in an airtight container or wrap it tightly with plastic wrap. The dough can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. When ready to use, let it come to room temperature before shaping.
  • Freezer: For longer storage, you can freeze pizza dough. After the initial rise, divide the dough into portions and wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or place them in freezer bags. When ready to use, let the dough thaw in the fridge overnight, then bring it to room temperature before shaping and baking.

Troubleshooting Common Pizza Dough Issues

  • Dough is too sticky: If your dough is too sticky, add small amounts of flour, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches the right consistency. It should be smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky, but not wet.
  • Dough is too dry: If your dough feels too dry and crumbly, add water one teaspoon at a time until it comes together.
  • The dough isn’t rising: If your dough isn’t rising, check the temperature of your water. If it’s too cold, the yeast won’t activate. Ensure your yeast is fresh and active, and give the dough enough time to rise.

Conclusion

Making pizza dough with a stand mixer is a game-changer for home bakers. It simplifies the process while ensuring consistent, high-quality results. With the right ingredients, a few simple techniques, and a little patience, you’ll be able to make pizza dough that’s better than any takeout.

Whether you’re making a classic Margherita, a hearty meat-lover’s pizza, or a veggie-packed masterpiece, the perfect pizza dough is the key to an unforgettable homemade pizza experience. The stand mixer does all the hard work, leaving you to focus on creating your favorite pizza creations.

Now that you know how to make the best pizza dough using a stand mixer, it’s time to get started! Enjoy the process, and most importantly, enjoy the delicious pizza you’ll create.
