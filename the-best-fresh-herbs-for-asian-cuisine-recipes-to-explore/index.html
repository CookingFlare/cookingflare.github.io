The Best Fresh Herbs for Asian Cuisine: Recipes to Explore


Asian cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and the artistic use of fresh herbs. Each herb brings its unique character to dishes, enhancing taste and adding depth to the culinary experience. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best fresh herbs commonly used in Asian cooking, along with delicious recipes that feature these aromatic ingredients. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a culinary novice, these herbs will elevate your dishes and introduce you to the delightful world of Asian flavors.

1. Cilantro (Coriander)

Cilantro, also known as coriander, is a staple herb in many Asian dishes. Its bright, citrusy flavor adds freshness to curries, salads, and salsas. In Southeast Asian cuisine, cilantro is often used as a garnish, adding both flavor and a pop of color.

Recipe: Vietnamese Pho with Cilantro

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 lb rice noodles
  • 1/2 lb beef, thinly sliced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Bean sprouts, for garnish
  • Sliced jalapeños, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine the beef broth, water, star anise, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a simmer and let it infuse for about 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate pot, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. To serve, place the cooked noodles in a bowl, top with raw sliced beef, and ladle the hot broth over the noodles.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro, bean sprouts, jalapeños, and a squeeze of lime juice. Enjoy your aromatic Pho!

2. Thai Basil

Thai basil has a distinct flavor profile that is slightly spicier and more aromatic than sweet basil. Its peppery notes make it an ideal herb for stir-fries and curries, especially in Thai cuisine.

Recipe: Thai Basil Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 cups Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add sliced chicken and cook until browned. Stir in the red bell pepper and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce. Pour the sauce over the chicken and bell pepper, stirring to combine.
  4. Finally, add the Thai basil leaves and cook until wilted. Serve hot over jasmine rice for a flavorful meal.

3. Mint

Mint is widely used in various Asian cuisines, providing a refreshing burst of flavor. It complements both savory and sweet dishes, making it a versatile herb.

Recipe: Mint and Cucumber Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Add the sliced cucumber and chopped mint to the bowl, tossing to coat in the dressing.
  3. Let the salad marinate for about 15 minutes before serving. Sprinkle with sesame seeds for added texture.

4. Lemongrass

Lemongrass is a fragrant herb that imparts a citrusy flavor to dishes. It is commonly used in Thai and Vietnamese cooking, perfect for soups, curries, and marinades.

Recipe: Lemongrass Coconut Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, trimmed and smashed
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup tofu, cubed
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine coconut milk, vegetable broth, lemongrass, and ginger. Bring to a simmer.
  2. Add mushrooms and tofu, cooking for about 5-7 minutes until heated through.
  3. Remove lemongrass and ginger slices before serving. Garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges for a zesty finish.

5. Green Onions (Scallions)

Green onions, or scallions, are essential in Asian cuisine. They add a mild onion flavor and freshness to various dishes, from soups to stir-fries.

Recipe: Scallion Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup boiling water
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add boiling water, mixing until a dough forms. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out the dough into a thin circle. Brush with sesame oil and sprinkle chopped green onions over the surface.
  3. Roll the dough into a log, then coil it into a round shape. Flatten gently to form a pancake.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium heat and cook the pancake for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown. Cut into wedges and serve with soy sauce for dipping.

6. Shiso

Shiso, also known as perilla, is an herb with a unique flavor that combines elements of mint, basil, and anise. It is a popular ingredient in Japanese cuisine, used in salads, sushi, and as a garnish.

Recipe: Shiso-Infused Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup fresh shiso leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Combine rice and water in a rice cooker and cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved.
  3. Once the rice is cooked, let it cool slightly, then gently fold in the chopped shiso leaves and vinegar mixture. Serve as a side dish or as a base for sushi.

7. Garlic Chives

Garlic chives are a mild variety of chives with a subtle garlic flavor. They are often used in dumplings, stir-fries, and as a garnish.

Recipe: Garlic Chive Dumplings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
  • 1 cup garlic chives, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour with boiling water until a dough forms. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine garlic chives, mushrooms, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roll out the dough and cut into small circles. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, fold, and seal the edges.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry dumplings until golden brown on both sides. Serve with soy sauce for dipping.

Conclusion

Fresh herbs are the backbone of Asian cuisine, providing layers of flavor, aroma, and visual appeal to dishes. From the bright notes of cilantro to the aromatic presence of lemongrass, incorporating these herbs into your cooking can enhance your culinary creations. Experiment with the recipes provided, and let your taste buds explore the vibrant world of Asian flavors. The next time you cook an Asian dish, remember the power of fresh herbs, and don’t hesitate to get creative in the kitchen!
