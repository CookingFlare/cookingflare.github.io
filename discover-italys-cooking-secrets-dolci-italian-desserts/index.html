Discover Italy's Cooking Secrets: Dolci (Italian Desserts)


Italian desserts, or “dolci,” are renowned for their delicate flavors, rich textures, and refined elegance. When it comes to Italy’s sweet culinary traditions, there is more to explore than just gelato and tiramisu. Today, we are diving into the heart of Italian dessert culture by sharing some distinctive recipes and uncovering some lesser-known treats that are sure to delight your palate.

Torta di Ricotta

This simple, yet sophisticated ricotta cake is an Italian staple. It is delectable, soft, and creamy, with a lovely, delicate texture. To prepare it, you will need the following ingredients:

Ingredients:

  • 15 ounces (420g) of ricotta cheese
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 cups (400g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • ¾ cup (100g) of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup (120ml) of vegetable oil
  • Zest of 1 large lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the ricotta, eggs, 1 cup (200g) of granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until evenly incorporated.
  3. In another bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients and vegetable oil to the wet ingredients while mixing continuously.
  5. Fold in the lemon zest.
  6. Pour the batter into a greased 9-inch (23-cm) round cake pan.
  7. Bake for about 50 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cake to cool for an hour before dusting with the remaining ¼ cup (50g) of granulated sugar.

Carmelito

This carmello-themed dessert combines the best of both worlds: caramel anditto — two of Italy’s most loved sweets. Ingredients and instructions below:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (200g) of granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (120ml) of water
  • ¼ cup (60ml) of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup (240g) of heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the sugar and water over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Once the sugar dissolves, stop stirring and allow the caramel to cook until it reaches a deep golden brown color.
  3. Carefully mix in the lemon juice, vanilla extract, and salt.
  4. Whip the cream until soft peaks form and fold it into the caramel mixture.
  5. Pour the caramelito into individual serving dishes or one large serving dish and chill for at least two hours.
  6. Garnish with additional whipped cream and serve cold.

Sfogliatelle

Sfogliatelle are crisp, layered pastry shells filled with sweet ricotta and flavored with candied orange peel. To recreate this Italian delicacy, follow these steps:

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups (500g) of all-purpose flour
  • 8 tablespoons (115g) of unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) of whole milk
  • ½ cup (100g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 15 ounces (420g) of ricotta cheese
  • ¼ cup (60ml) of Marsala wine
  • ⅓ cup (75g) of chopped candied orange peel

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt. Cut the butter into small pieces and add to the mixture. Work the butter into the flour mixture using your fingertips until it forms pea-sized crumbs.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, sugar, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, followed by the milk mixture. Stir until everything is evenly incorporated.
  4. Roll the dough into a long log shape and cut it into equal parts, eight to ten pieces.
  5. Roll out each piece of dough into a circle about ¼ inch (6 mm) thick. Place a tablespoon of ricotta mixture wrapped in the center of each dough circle.
  6. Fold the sides of the dough over the filling, rotating the pastry while keeping the seams together to form a ring shape.
  7. Set the sfogliatelle on a lined baking sheet, arranging them with the folded sides facing down.
  8. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  9. Allow the sfogliatelle to cool slightly before serving.

Each of these delectable Italian desserts offers a unique taste of Italy’s wonderful culinary heritage. Experiment with them in your kitchen and satisfy your cravings for authentic Italian dolci. Buon appetito!
