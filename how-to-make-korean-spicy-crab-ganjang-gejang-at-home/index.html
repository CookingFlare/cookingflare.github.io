How to Make Korean Spicy Crab (Ganjang Gejang) at Home


Korean cuisine has captured the hearts of food lovers worldwide with its bold flavors and rich traditions. One standout dish often viewed as both a delicacy and a labor of love is Korean Spicy Crab, known as Ganjang Gejang (or Yangnyeom Gejang when made spicy). This dish is famed for its unique combination of raw crab bathed in a complex, spicy marinade that highlights the best of Korean seasoning. It’s a true testament to the deep umami flavors for which Korean cooking is known.

Although making Korean Spicy Crab at home can seem intimidating, it’s entirely achievable with the right ingredients, preparation, and patience. This blog will walk you through each step of crafting this culinary masterpiece from scratch, ensuring your homemade Yangnyeom Gejang rivals the flavors of a traditional Korean seafood restaurant. By the end of this post, you’ll have a flavorful creation that’s as exciting to eat as it is satisfying to make.

Ingredients for Korean Spicy Crab

Before you start, it’s crucial to gather all the ingredients. Freshness and authenticity in the components are key to recreating the flavors of Korean Spicy Crab. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Crab:

  • 4 medium-sized live or fresh raw blue crabs (or another variety of fresh crabs such as flower crabs)
  • 2–3 cups cold water with ice (for soaking)

For the Marinade:

  • 4 tablespoons Korean gochugaru (red chili pepper flakes)
  • 3 tablespoons Korean gochujang (red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons corn syrup or honey
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon roasted sesame seeds

For Aromatics and Vegetables:

  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned (optional)
  • 1 small chili pepper (red or green), thinly sliced, for extra heat

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Korean Spicy Crab

Step 1: Preparing the Crab

It all begins with the crab. Freshness plays a significant role in the success of the dish, so opt for live crabs if possible or very fresh raw crabs from a trusted seafood market.

  1. Start by thoroughly cleaning the crabs under cold running water. Use a small brush or toothbrush to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Next, humanely prepare the crab. If alive, consider placing the crabs in the freezer for 15–20 minutes to numb them before cleaning further. Then, remove the shell (carapace), gills, and rinse out any internal organs. Split each crab into halves or quarters, leaving the meat intact in the shell.
  3. Place the prepared crabs in a bowl of ice water to maintain their freshness while you prepare the marinade.

Step 2: Making the Spicy Marinade

The heart and soul of Korean Spicy Crab lie in its marinade. A balance of spice, sweetness, and umami is key.

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the gochugaru, gochujang, soy sauce, fish sauce, rice vinegar, and corn syrup or honey. This combination serves as the flavor base for the marinade.
  2. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil, mixing thoroughly to combine.
  3. Stir in the thinly sliced vegetables (onion, carrot, and green onion) along with the chili pepper slices. These aromatics add texture, color, and layers of flavor.
  4. Finish the marinade by sprinkling roasted sesame seeds over the mixture. This nutty element ties the marinade together beautifully.

Step 3: Marinating the Crab

Once the crabs are prepared and the marinade is ready, it’s time to bring everything together.

  1. Remove the crab from the ice water and pat dry with a paper towel to prevent excess water from diluting the marinade.
  2. Place the crab pieces in a shallow, non-reactive container (a glass or ceramic container works best). Pour the spicy marinade over the crabs, ensuring each piece is generously coated.
  3. Gently massage the marinade into the crabs using gloved hands. This step helps the marinade penetrate the shells and infuse the meat with flavor.
  4. Cover the container with an airtight lid or plastic wrap and refrigerate the marinating crabs for 12 to 24 hours. The longer the crab marinates, the deeper the flavor becomes.

Step 4: Serving Your Korean Spicy Crab

After the marinating period, your Korean Spicy Crab is ready to enjoy! This dish is typically served as part of a classic Korean table spread, but it can easily shine as a standalone star.

  1. Transfer the marinated crab to a serving platter, garnishing with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and a few extra slices of chili if desired.
  2. Serve with steamed white rice, which helps balance the strong flavors of the crab marinade. A side of Korean seaweed (gim), kimchi, or pickled radish makes a perfect accompaniment.
  3. To eat, suck the meat and marinade directly from the shell. The roe inside the shell is considered a delicacy and is not to be missed.

Tips and Tricks for Success

  1. Select High-Quality Crabs: Freshness is the foundation of this dish. Raw crab has a delicate texture that thrives in the spicy marinade, but only if it’s impeccably fresh. Talk to your seafood vendor and choose crabs with a firm texture and no off-smell.

  2. Balance the Heat Level: If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of gochugaru or use a mild variety. Conversely, for more heat, add extra chili pepper slices or include a dash of red chili oil.

  3. Prepare in Advance: This dish requires a significant marinating period to develop its full flavor. Plan ahead so the crabs have time to soak up the spicy goodness.

  4. Pair Strategically: Pair Korean Spicy Crab with neutral dishes like steamed rice to highlight its explosive flavors. A light Korean soup or broth-based side dish also works wonderfully.

Storing and Leftovers

If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 days. However, Ganjang Gejang is best when consumed fresh to enjoy the full integrity of the flavors and textures.

Why Make Korean Spicy Crab At Home?

Making Yangnyeom Gejang at home allows you to enjoy this authentic Korean specialty at your convenience, tailored to your exact taste preferences. Restaurants often reserve this dish for special occasions due to its laborious preparation, but by mastering the art at home, you can savor it anytime—and without the hefty price tag.

Moreover, crafting this dish yourself lets you control the quality of ingredients and cater to dietary specifications if needed. It’s a rewarding process that connects you to the heart of Korean culinary tradition.

By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll be well on your way to creating an unforgettable Korean Spicy Crab dish that rivals what you’d find in the bustling streets of Seoul. With just a little care and patience, you can transform raw crab into a masterpiece of bold, spicy, umami-packed delight. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a home cook craving something new, this recipe is sure to impress your palate and your guests.
