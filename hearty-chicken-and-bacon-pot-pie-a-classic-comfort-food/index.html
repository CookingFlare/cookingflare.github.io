Hearty Chicken and Bacon Pot Pie: A Classic Comfort Food


There’s something about a warm and delicious pot pie that brings comfort to the soul. And when it’s filled with tender chunks of chicken, smoky bacon, and hearty vegetables, it becomes a true classic. Today, I’m going to share with you my recipe for a Hearty Chicken and Bacon Pot Pie that will leave you feeling satisfied and content.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup of frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 sheets of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a 9-inch pie dish and set it aside.

  2. In a large skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set it aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess grease. Once cooled, crumble the bacon into small pieces.

  3. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  4. Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and add them to the skillet. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink in the center.

  5. Sprinkle the flour over the chicken and vegetables, stirring continuously to coat everything evenly. Cook for an additional minute to cook off the raw flour taste.

  6. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer, allowing it to thicken slightly.

  7. Add the frozen peas and carrots, dried thyme, and crumbled bacon to the skillet. Stir well to incorporate all the ingredients. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  8. Transfer the mixture to the greased pie dish, spreading it evenly.

  9. Unroll one sheet of puff pastry and place it over the top of the pie dish, tucking in the edges. Cut a few slits in the pastry to allow steam to escape during baking.

  10. In a small bowl, beat the egg. Brush the beaten egg over the top of the pastry to give it a golden shine.

  11. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet to catch any drips, and then transfer it to the preheated oven.

  12. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is puffed and golden brown.

  13. Remove the pot pie from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. The filling will be piping hot, so be careful when taking your first bite.

Serving Suggestions

This Hearty Chicken and Bacon Pot Pie is a complete meal in itself, but you can always serve it with a side salad or some crusty bread to make it even more satisfying. It’s the perfect dish for a cozy night in or when you’re craving a taste of nostalgia.

Final Thoughts

The aroma of this Chicken and Bacon Pot Pie as it bakes in the oven is enough to make your mouth water. With its flaky puff pastry crust and rich, flavorful filling, it’s a classic comfort food that never fails to impress. Give this recipe a try, and let the warmth and goodness of this dish envelop you in pure culinary bliss.
