Apple Cider Donuts: A Fall-Inspired Fruit Dessert


Fall is here, and what better way to celebrate the season than with some warm and delicious apple cider donuts? These delightful treats are the perfect combination of fruity sweetness and cozy autumn flavors. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create these fall-inspired delights that are sure to impress your family and friends. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups apple cider
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the apple cider to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the cider has reduced to 1/2 cup. Set aside to cool.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Whisk together until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter and eggs. Add the cooled apple cider reduction and whisk again until fully incorporated.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense donuts.

  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a donut pan with non-stick cooking spray.

  6. Spoon the batter into a piping bag or a large ziplock bag with the corner cut off. Pipe the batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about 2/3 full.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the donuts are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Remove the donuts from the oven and let them cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Once the donuts have cooled, dust them generously with powdered sugar. Feel free to get creative and add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg on top for an extra burst of flavor.

  10. Serve your apple cider donuts alongside a cup of hot apple cider or a warm mug of coffee. Enjoy the cozy flavors of fall!

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a donut pan, you can make these donuts in a muffin tin. Simply adjust the baking time accordingly.
  • For an extra indulgent treat, you can dip the cooled donuts in a glaze made with powdered sugar, apple cider, and a touch of vanilla extract.
  • Add a twist to your apple cider donuts by incorporating chopped apples or a sprinkle of apple pie spice into the batter.
  • These donuts are best enjoyed on the day they are made, but you can store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Warm them up in the oven or microwave before serving to restore their freshness.

There you have it – a simple yet delightful recipe for apple cider donuts. These fall-inspired treats are the perfect way to embrace the flavors of autumn and satisfy your sweet tooth. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to enjoy a cozy and delicious dessert that will surely become a seasonal favorite. Happy baking!
