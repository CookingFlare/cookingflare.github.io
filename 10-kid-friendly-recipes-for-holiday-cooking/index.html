10 Kid-Friendly Recipes for Holiday Cooking


The holidays are an exciting time filled with festivities, warmth, and lots of delicious food. Cooking during this season becomes more special when you include the little ones in kitchen activities. Not only do kid-friendly recipes make meal preparation more enjoyable, but they also help children develop basic cooking skills, boost creativity, and foster family bonding.

Here’s a collection of 10 enticing recipes perfect for holiday cooking with kids. These dishes range from sweet treats to savory delights, simple enough to engage young helpers but tasty enough to impress the entire family. Each recipe focuses on easy-to-find ingredients, straightforward methods, and festive flavors that inspired cooks of all ages to gather around the table.

1. Festive Snowman Pancakes

Start the holiday morning with a charming twist on a classic breakfast. Snowman pancakes delight children with their cute design and warm flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 ¼ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • Blueberries (for eyes and buttons)
  • Sliced strawberries or carrot sticks (for nose)
  • Whipped cream (for scarf)

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  2. In another bowl, whisk milk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients; mix until smooth.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  5. For each snowman, pour three different sizes of pancakes on the pan – a large one for the body, medium for the torso, and small for the head.
  6. Once bubbles form on top, flip and cook the other side until golden.
  7. Stack pancakes in descending size order on a plate.
  8. Decorate with blueberries for eyes and buttons, a strawberry slice or carrot stick for the nose, and whipped cream as a scarf.
  9. Serve warm with syrup or honey.

This interactive breakfast turns a simple meal into a festive art project that kids love.

2. Holiday Veggie and Cheese Pinwheels

Pinwheels are fun to eat and perfect for holiday parties or snacks. The colorful veggies inside make these as visually appealing as they are tasty.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • ½ cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ¼ cup chopped spinach
  • ¼ cup finely chopped bell peppers (red and green)
  • 2 tablespoons cream cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a floured surface.
  3. Spread cream cheese evenly over the pastry.
  4. Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese, spinach, chopped bell peppers, and oregano on top.
  5. Roll the pastry tightly into a log.
  6. Slice into ½-inch thick rounds and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake 12–15 minutes or until golden and puffed.
  8. Let cool slightly before serving.

Kids enjoy rolling up this savory snack, and it’s a great way to sneak in some veggies.

3. Cheesy Stuffed Mini Meatloaves

Individual mini meatloaves are perfect comfort food with a cheesy surprise inside. They’re great for holidays as they can be made ahead and baked when ready.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • ½ cup bread crumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup ketchup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix ground meat, bread crumbs, onion, egg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Divide the mixture into 4 equal parts. Flatten each piece and place a spoonful of shredded mozzarella in the center.
  4. Wrap meat around the cheese to create mini loaves, sealing the cheese inside.
  5. Place on a baking sheet and brush tops with ketchup.
  6. Bake for 25–30 minutes or until cooked through.
  7. Let cool for a few minutes, then serve.

This recipe offers a delightful surprise when kids cut into their mini meatloaves to discover the melty cheese inside.

4. Cinnamon Sugar Holiday Pretzel Bites

Pretzel bites covered in cinnamon sugar are an addictive holiday treat that kids will enjoy helping prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tube refrigerated biscuit dough
  • 4 cups water
  • ⅓ cup baking soda
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Mix sugar and cinnamon in a bowl.
  3. Cut each biscuit into quarters to make small pieces.
  4. Bring water and baking soda to a boil in a large pot.
  5. Drop biscuit pieces into the boiling water for 30 seconds, then remove with a slotted spoon.
  6. Place the pieces on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 8–10 minutes or until golden.
  8. Brush warm pretzels with melted butter and toss in cinnamon sugar mixture.
  9. Serve warm.

This recipe introduces children to the fun science of boiling dough and creates an irresistible snack.

5. Gingerbread Man Pancake Stacks

A festive variation on pancakes that looks like gingerbread cookies but tastes fluffy and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons molasses
  • Butter for cooking
  • Chocolate chips for eyes and buttons

Directions:

  1. Combine dry ingredients in a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk milk, egg, and molasses.
  3. Stir wet ingredients into dry until combined.
  4. Heat a griddle or pan over medium heat; grease with butter.
  5. Pour batter into gingerbread man shapes using a squeeze bottle or spoon.
  6. Add chocolate chips for eyes and buttons while batter cooks.
  7. Flip carefully when bubbles form, cooking until golden on both sides.
  8. Stack pancakes with a dab of whipped cream or syrup between each.

This recipe turns breakfast into an exciting holiday event.

6. Easy Holiday Fruit Kabobs with Dip

Fruit kabobs are colorful, healthy, and hands-on, making them an excellent holiday snack.

Ingredients:

  • Seedless grapes
  • Strawberries, hulled
  • Pineapple chunks
  • Kiwi slices
  • Wooden skewers
  • Greek yogurt
  • Honey
  • Ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Thread fruit pieces alternately onto skewers.
  2. Mix Greek yogurt with honey and a pinch of cinnamon to make a dip.
  3. Serve kabobs with the dip on the side.

Kids can play with color patterns while preparing these and get a serving of vitamins in a fun way.

7. Holiday-Themed Rice Krispie Treats

These treats are a holiday staple that kids can make with minimal help.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 package (10 oz) marshmallows
  • Food coloring (red and green)
  • Holiday sprinkles

Directions:

  1. Melt butter in a large saucepan over low heat.
  2. Add marshmallows and stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Divide the melted marshmallow mixture into three bowls.
  4. Add red food coloring to one, green to another, and leave one plain. Stir well.
  5. Mix each colored marshmallow portion with 2 cups Rice Krispies cereal.
  6. Press each colored mixture into separate pans lined with parchment paper.
  7. Let cool, then cut into squares.
  8. Decorate with holiday sprinkles or arrange the colors in fun patterns.

This recipe allows kids to experiment with colors and makes for festive, sweet snacks.

8. Mini Holiday Pizza Faces

Create personalized pizzas shaped with bells, stars, or circles and decorated to look like holiday characters.

Ingredients:

  • Pre-made pizza dough or flatbread
  • Tomato sauce
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Pepperoni or sliced olives
  • Bell pepper strips
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Spinach or basil leaves

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Roll out pizza dough into small rounds or cut into holiday shapes with cookie cutters.
  3. Spread a spoonful of tomato sauce on each base.
  4. Sprinkle shredded cheese on top.
  5. Use pepperoni, olives, bell peppers, tomatoes, and greens to create faces or festive designs.
  6. Bake for 10–12 minutes until cheese melts and crust is golden.

Kids’ creativity shines as they assemble their own edible artwork.

9. Holiday Hot Chocolate with Marshmallow Snowflakes

A cozy drink to warm up after a busy day of holiday activities, topped with homemade marshmallow snowflakes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • ¼ cup cocoa powder
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Powdered sugar (for decorating marshmallows)
  • Toothpicks

Directions:

  1. Heat milk in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Whisk in cocoa powder and sugar until fully blended.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Pour into mugs.
  5. To make marshmallow snowflakes, carefully pierce mini marshmallows with toothpicks in snowflake shapes.
  6. Dust snowflakes with powdered sugar.
  7. Place snowflakes on top of hot chocolate.

This drink is a magical treat that kids enjoy sipping and snacking on.

10. Holiday Fruit and Nut Energy Bites

An easy no-bake recipe that’s perfect for a quick holiday boost.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup peanut butter or almond butter
  • ⅓ cup honey
  • ½ cup dried cranberries or raisins
  • ½ cup chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, mix all ingredients until well combined.
  2. Roll mixture into 1-inch balls.
  3. Chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving.

These wholesome bites are convenient for kids to make and carry during holiday outings.

Bringing kids into the kitchen with these engaging recipes turns ordinary holiday meals into memorable moments. Whether it’s building a snowman pancake tower, decorating their own pizza, or crafting colorful fruit kabobs, the joys of food and family come alive in every bite and every shared step. Happy holiday cooking!
