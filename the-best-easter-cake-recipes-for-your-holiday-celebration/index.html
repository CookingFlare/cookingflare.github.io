The Best Easter Cake Recipes for Your Holiday Celebration


Easter is a time of joy, renewal, and, of course, delicious food. Among the festive dishes, cakes hold a special place on the holiday table. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to treat your family to something sweet, an Easter cake can be the centerpiece of your celebration. From light and airy to rich and indulgent, Easter cakes come in a wonderful variety of flavors and styles that capture the spirit of spring.

In this post, you’ll find a collection of exceptional Easter cake recipes, each bringing its own charm and a burst of flavor. These recipes are designed for home bakers of different skill levels, with helpful tips to make your baking experience smooth and enjoyable. Let’s dive into the world of delightful cakes perfect for Easter!

1. Classic Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is synonymous with Easter for many people. Its moist texture, combined with the spices and the subtle sweetness of carrots, makes it a perfect springtime dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 cups finely grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)
  • 1 cup crushed pineapple, drained

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the sugar, oil, and eggs until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring gently until combined.
  5. Fold in the carrots, nuts, and pineapple until evenly distributed.
  6. Divide batter between pans and bake for 30–35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow cakes to cool for 10 minutes before removing from pans; cool completely on wire racks.
  8. Beat cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla; beat until fluffy.
  9. Frost the cooled cakes, layering the frosting between and on top.

Why it works: The pineapple adds moisture and a subtle tropical hint, while freshly grated carrots ensure the classic flavor and texture. The cream cheese frosting complements the spice with its tangy sweetness.

2. Lemon Blueberry Layer Cake

Bright and zesty, this lemon blueberry cake is perfect for an Easter meal, mirroring fresh spring flavors and colors.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups fresh blueberries (tossed with 1 tablespoon flour)

Lemon Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 5 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 8-inch round cake pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Mix in vanilla and lemon zest.
  5. Alternately add flour mixture and milk, starting and ending with the flour mixture; mix just until combined.
  6. Gently fold in blueberries.
  7. Divide batter evenly into pans and bake for 25–30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool cakes in pans for 10 minutes; turn out onto wire racks to cool completely.
  9. For frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Add powdered sugar, lemon juice, and zest; beat until fluffy.
  10. Frost the cooled cakes in layers.

Why it works: The lemon zest and juice brighten the flavor, while the blueberries add natural bursts of sweetness and juiciness. A tender crumb balances the slight tartness of the frosting perfectly.

3. Almond and Raspberry Bundt Cake

For a slightly more sophisticated Easter dessert, this almond and raspberry bundt cake boasts a delicate nutty flavor paired with tart berries.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons almond extract
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries

Glaze:

  • 1 ½ cups powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons milk
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C); grease and flour a 10-cup bundt pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, almond flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugar together until fluffy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in almond extract.
  5. Alternate adding dry ingredients and sour cream to the butter mixture, starting and finishing with dry ingredients.
  6. Gently fold in raspberries.
  7. Pour batter into the bundt pan, smoothing the top.
  8. Bake 50–60 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Let cool 15 minutes in the pan, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Mix glaze ingredients until smooth and drizzle over cooled cake.

Why it works: The almond flour adds moistness and a subtle crunch, and the sour cream ensures a tender crumb. Raspberries provide natural color and a balanced tartness against the sweet almond glaze.

4. Classic Simnel Cake – A Traditional Easter Favorite

Simnel cake is a traditional British Easter cake with a rich fruit base and a marzipan topping. It’s dense, flavorful, and carries historical significance for the holiday.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried mixed fruit (currants, raisins, sultanas)
  • 1 cup chopped glacé cherries
  • 1 cup mixed peel
  • ½ cup orange juice
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon mixed spice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • 1 cup marzipan (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons apricot jam

Directions:

  1. Soak dried fruits and mixed peel in orange juice overnight.
  2. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and line a 9-inch round cake tin.
  3. Sift flour, baking powder, and spices.
  4. Cream butter and sugar until light. Add eggs one at a time.
  5. Stir in the ground almonds.
  6. Fold in the soaked fruits and any remaining juice.
  7. Pour half the mixture into the cake tin.
  8. Roll out half the marzipan to form a disk the size of the cake tin. Place it over the batter.
  9. Cover with remaining cake mixture, smoothing the surface.
  10. Bake for 1 hour and 45 minutes, covering the top if it browns too quickly. Test doneness before removing.
  11. Cool cake in tin for 20 minutes, then turn out.
  12. Roll out remaining marzipan to make a thin disk and brush the top of cooled cake with apricot jam. Place marzipan on top and lightly score the surface into 11 sections (representing the apostles—minus Judas).
  13. Optionally, lightly grill or brown the marzipan under a hot grill for a golden finish.

Why it works: The marzipan adds a sweet almond flavor that enhances the rich fruit cake, which gains more depth from soaking the dried fruits in citrus juice. This cake’s historical layers and textures make it uniquely special for Easter.

5. Coconut and Pineapple Upside-Down Cake

This tropical-inspired cake is a fresh twist on a classic upside-down cake, bringing a lush, sunny vibe to the Easter feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup light brown sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 1 can (20 oz) pineapple slices, drained
  • 1 cup flaked coconut
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Melt butter in a 9-inch round cake pan on the stovetop. Sprinkle the brown sugar evenly over butter. Arrange pineapple slices on top of sugar, then sprinkle with coconut.
  3. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt.
  4. In another bowl, beat sugar, oil, eggs, buttermilk, and vanilla.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients to wet, mixing until smooth.
  6. Pour batter over pineapple mixture.
  7. Bake for 40–45 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Let cool for 10 minutes; invert carefully onto a serving plate. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Why it works: The caramelized brown sugar and pineapple create a sticky, flavorful top, while the cake underneath remains moist and tender. The toasted coconut adds texture and aromatic depth, making it perfect for a spring celebration.

Tips for Baking the Perfect Easter Cake

  • Use room temperature ingredients. Butter, eggs, and dairy mix more uniformly when not cold, giving better texture.
  • Measure flour carefully. Overpacking flour can lead to dense cakes; spoon into measuring cup and level off.
  • Don’t overmix batter. Stir just until combined to avoid tough cakes.
  • Check oven temperature. Ovens often run hot or cold; use an oven thermometer for accuracy.
  • Let cakes cool completely before frosting. Warm cakes can cause frosting to melt and slide off.
  • Decorate with seasonal flair: Fresh flowers, edible pastel sprinkles, or tiny chocolate eggs can add festive charm.
  • Make ahead. Many cakes taste better after resting a day or two.

Conclusion

Easter cake is more than just a dessert — it is a symbol of celebration, warmth, and gathering around loved ones. Whether you opt for the spice of a classic carrot cake, the citrus brightness of lemon-blueberry layers, the rich tradition of simnel cake, or something more adventurous like almonds and raspberries, these recipes offer a delightful way to honor the holiday. Baking any of these cakes is sure to fill your home with the welcoming aromas of spring and sweet anticipation for the feast to come.

Gather your ingredients, pick a recipe that excites your taste buds, and create a beautiful Easter cake that will be remembered long after the holiday ends. Happy baking and joyful Easter celebrations!
