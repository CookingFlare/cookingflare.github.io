Savory Argentinian Asado: 6 Recipes for Meat Lovers


Welcome to our savory Argentinian Asado recipe collection! If you’re a meat lover, you’re in for a treat. Asado, the traditional Argentinian barbecue, is a culinary delight that brings people together over deliciously grilled meats. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing six mouthwatering recipes that will take your taste buds on a journey to Argentina. So fire up your grill and let’s get started!

1. Classic Asado

Ingredients:

  • Beef ribs
  • Salt
  • Chimichurri sauce

Instructions:

  1. Start by seasoning the beef ribs generously with salt.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 20-30 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  4. Serve the ribs hot, accompanied by chimichurri sauce for a burst of tangy flavor.

2. Vacío (Flank Steak)

Ingredients:

  • Flank steak
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Season the flank steak with salt, black pepper, and garlic powder.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Grill the steak for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness.
  4. Let the steak rest for a few minutes before slicing it against the grain.
  5. Serve the vacío with a side of chimichurri sauce and enjoy the tender and flavorful meat.

3. Choripán (Grilled Sausage Sandwich)

Ingredients:

  • Argentine chorizo sausages
  • Baguettes or crusty rolls
  • Chimichurri sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the chorizo sausages for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are nicely browned and cooked through.
  3. While the sausages are grilling, slice the baguettes or rolls in half lengthwise.
  4. Once the sausages are ready, place them inside the baguettes or rolls.
  5. Serve the choripán hot, topped with chimichurri sauce for an extra kick of flavor.

4. Mollejas (Sweetbreads)

Ingredients:

  • Lamb sweetbreads
  • Salt
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Soak the lamb sweetbreads in cold water for about 1 hour to remove any excess blood.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Season the sweetbreads with salt.
  4. Grill the sweetbreads for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the grilled sweetbreads before serving.

5. Matambre (Stuffed Flank Steak)

Ingredients:

  • Flank steak
  • Spinach
  • Carrots
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Red bell pepper
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Butterfly the flank steak by slicing it horizontally, leaving one edge intact.
  2. Season the inside of the steak with salt and black pepper.
  3. Layer the spinach, carrots, hard-boiled eggs, and red bell pepper on top of the steak.
  4. Roll the steak tightly and tie it with kitchen twine to secure the filling.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Brush the outside of the rolled steak with olive oil and season with salt and black pepper.
  7. Grill the matambre for about 10-15 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness.
  8. Let the matambre rest for a few minutes before slicing it into pinwheels.

6. Provoleta (Grilled Provolone Cheese)

Ingredients:

  • Provolone cheese slices
  • Oregano
  • Red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the provolone cheese slices directly on the grill and sprinkle with oregano and red pepper flakes.
  3. Grill the cheese for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until it is melted and golden brown.
  4. Serve the provoleta hot and gooey for a deliciously cheesy treat.

We hope you enjoy these authentic Argentinian Asado recipes! Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or simply craving some flavorful grilled meats, these recipes will surely satisfy your meat-loving cravings. So gather your friends and family, embrace the Argentinian spirit, and indulge in the mouthwatering flavors of an Asado feast. ¡Buen provecho!
