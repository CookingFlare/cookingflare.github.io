Fourth of July BBQ Recipes That Will Impress Your Guests


The Fourth of July is one of the most anticipated holidays of the year, a time for fireworks, family, and, of course, delicious food. Whether you’re hosting a backyard bash or heading to a community potluck, the heart of any Fourth of July celebration is the BBQ. With the right mix of dishes, you can make your gathering the talk of the neighborhood. If you’re looking to up your grilling game, or want to wow your guests with unforgettable flavors, these Fourth of July BBQ recipes will have everyone asking for seconds.

The Perfect Grilled Burger

No Fourth of July cookout is complete without burgers. But instead of the same old patty with ketchup, let’s elevate it to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs of ground chuck or ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio for juiciness)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Slices of sharp cheddar cheese
  • Brioche buns
  • Lettuce, sliced tomato, red onion, and your favorite condiments

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine ground beef, egg, breadcrumbs, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined—don’t overwork the meat.
  2. Divide the meat into equal portions and shape into patties, about ¾ inch thick. Use your thumb to make a small indentation in the center of each patty to prevent puffing up during cooking.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the patties on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side (or until your desired doneness).
  4. A minute before they are done, add cheddar cheese to each patty and allow it to melt.
  5. Toast your brioche buns on the grill for around 30 seconds.
  6. Assemble your burger with lettuce, tomato, onion, and condiments of choice. Serve hot!

Zesty Grilled Chicken Wings

Take chicken wings to the next level with a smoky, zesty marinade that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken wings
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, lime juice, garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken wings and toss to coat. Cover and let marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Grill the wings for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with ranch or blue cheese dip.

Smoky BBQ Ribs That Fall Off the Bone

Few things scream BBQ more than slow-cooked, smoky ribs. These ribs are guaranteed to be the centerpiece of your spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks pork baby back ribs
  • 1/4 cup yellow mustard
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup apple juice
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce

Instructions:

  1. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. Rub a thin layer of mustard on both sides of the ribs. In a small bowl, mix brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Generously coat both sides of the ribs with the rub.
  3. Preheat your grill to 250°F for indirect cooking.
  4. Place the ribs on the grill, bone side down, and smoke slowly for 2.5 hours.
  5. After 2.5 hours, wrap the ribs in aluminum foil and pour 1/2 cup of apple juice into each packet. Seal tightly and return to the grill for another hour.
  6. Remove ribs from the foil and brush with BBQ sauce. Cook uncovered for 20-30 minutes, or until the sauce caramelizes.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Add a fresh touch to your Fourth of July spread with grilled corn slathered in a delicious herb butter.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1 tbsp chopped chives
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, parsley, chives, garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well and set aside.
  3. Grill the corn directly on the grates, turning occasionally, until slightly charred and tender (about 10-12 minutes).
  4. Slather each ear with herb butter before serving.

Watermelon and Feta Salad

Balance out the decadent BBQ flavors with this refreshing and vibrant summer salad.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cubed watermelon
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss together watermelon cubes, feta cheese, and mint leaves.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  3. Chill in the fridge until ready to serve.

Classic Peach Cobbler

End your Fourth of July BBQ on a sweet note with a warm, classic peach cobbler.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill for indirect heat at around 375°F.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the peaches with granulated sugar. Set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, milk, baking powder, and melted butter to form a batter.
  4. Grease a cast-iron skillet and pour half the batter into the skillet. Layer the peaches on top, followed by the remaining batter.
  5. Sprinkle brown sugar and cinnamon over the top.
  6. Place the skillet on the grill and cook for about 40-45 minutes, or until golden and bubbling.

Conclusion

Hosting a memorable Fourth of July BBQ doesn’t have to be stressful. With these flavorful recipes, you’ll be well on your way to creating a festive and delicious feast that will impress your guests. From drool-worthy ribs to refreshing salads and indulgent desserts, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So fire up the grill, crank up some tunes, and get ready for a Fourth of July celebration overflowing with good food, good company, and great memories!
