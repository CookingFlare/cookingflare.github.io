The Best Chocolate Ice Cream Recipes for Summer


When the sun is blazing and the temperatures soar, few things can provide the sweet relief and satisfaction of a delicious, cold treat like chocolate ice cream. Whether it’s the creamy texture, the rich cocoa flavor, or the nostalgic joy it brings, chocolate ice cream is a classic favorite for many. This summer, why not take your dessert game to the next level with some indulgent homemade chocolate ice cream recipes that are perfect for cooling off?

In this post, we will dive into a variety of chocolate ice cream recipes, each offering something a little different to suit every taste and preference. From rich, dark chocolate ice cream to lighter, more refreshing options, you’ll discover all the techniques, tips, and ingredients needed to make ice cream at home that’s just as good as, if not better than, what you’d find in any shop.

Classic Chocolate Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the base: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and cocoa powder. Whisk until smooth and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Once the mixture starts to simmer, remove it from the heat.

  2. Make the egg mixture: In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with a pinch of salt until they become pale and slightly thickened.

  3. Temper the eggs: Gradually pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, stirring constantly to prevent curdling. Once combined, return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon (about 5 minutes).

  4. Chill the mixture: Strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any coagulated egg bits. Allow it to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Churn: Once the mixture has chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once done, transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze until firm, usually 2-3 hours.

  6. Serve and enjoy! After freezing, scoop out the velvety chocolate ice cream and serve. You can top it with chocolate syrup, whipped cream, or even chocolate shavings for an extra indulgence.

This recipe gives you a rich and velvety chocolate ice cream that’s perfect for those who enjoy the smooth, deep flavor of traditional chocolate.

Dark Chocolate Ice Cream

If you’re a fan of bittersweet chocolate, this recipe will satisfy your cravings for a decadent, dark chocolate ice cream that’s not too sweet but full of robust cocoa flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher), chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: Begin by melting the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl over a double boiler or in the microwave, stirring until smooth.

  2. Heat the cream mixture: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and cocoa powder. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to simmer, stirring frequently.

  3. Whisk the egg yolks: While the cream is heating, whisk the egg yolks and salt in a separate bowl until pale and thickened.

  4. Combine the hot cream and eggs: Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking continuously. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.

  5. Combine chocolate and custard: Pour the melted dark chocolate into the custard mixture, stirring until fully incorporated. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve and refrigerate until chilled, preferably overnight.

  6. Churn the ice cream: Once chilled, churn the mixture in your ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  7. Freeze: Transfer the churned ice cream into a container and freeze until firm.

With its deep and slightly bitter flavor, this dark chocolate ice cream will appeal to those who appreciate the complexity of high-quality chocolate.

Chocolate Peanut Butter Ice Cream

If you love the combination of chocolate and peanut butter, this recipe is for you. The creamy peanut butter swirls throughout the rich chocolate ice cream, creating an irresistible dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup smooth peanut butter
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Make the base: In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and cocoa powder over medium heat, whisking constantly. When the mixture begins to simmer, remove it from the heat.

  2. Whisk the eggs: In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with a pinch of salt until smooth and pale.

  3. Temper the eggs: Gradually pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the heat and cook for a few more minutes until it thickens.

  4. Add peanut butter: Once the custard has thickened, remove from heat and stir in the peanut butter until fully incorporated.

  5. Chill and churn: Strain the mixture and refrigerate until chilled. Once it has cooled, churn in your ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  6. Freeze: After churning, transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze until firm.

The swirl of peanut butter in this chocolate ice cream creates a creamy, nutty, and indulgent treat that’s perfect for chocolate and peanut butter lovers alike.

No-Churn Chocolate Ice Cream

If you don’t have an ice cream maker, don’t worry! You can still enjoy homemade chocolate ice cream with this no-churn recipe, which requires just a few ingredients and minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Whip the cream: In a large mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream with a hand mixer or stand mixer until stiff peaks form.

  2. Combine the other ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk the sweetened condensed milk, cocoa powder, and vanilla extract together until smooth.

  3. Fold together: Gently fold the cocoa mixture into the whipped cream, being careful not to deflate the whipped cream. Stir until well combined.

  4. Freeze: Pour the mixture into a loaf pan or another freezer-safe container. Cover with plastic wrap and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.

This no-churn chocolate ice cream is incredibly easy to make and doesn’t require any special equipment. The sweetened condensed milk adds richness, making it a creamy and satisfying dessert.

Chocolate Mint Ice Cream

For those who enjoy the refreshing flavor of mint paired with chocolate, this recipe combines the best of both worlds in a refreshing ice cream that’s perfect for summer evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Green food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the milk and cream: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and cocoa powder. Heat over medium heat, whisking frequently, until it starts to simmer.

  2. Whisk the eggs: While the milk mixture is heating, whisk the egg yolks and a pinch of salt in a separate bowl.

  3. Temper the eggs: Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the heat and cook until it thickens.

  4. Add mint and vanilla: Remove from heat and stir in the peppermint extract, vanilla extract, and a few drops of green food coloring (if desired). Let the mixture cool, then refrigerate it for several hours.

  5. Churn and freeze: Once the mixture is chilled, churn it in your ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Transfer it to a container and freeze until firm.

This chocolate mint ice cream is a perfect balance of cool mint and rich chocolate, making it the ideal treat for a hot summer day.

Final Thoughts

Chocolate ice cream is a timeless treat that never fails to impress. Whether you prefer a classic recipe or enjoy experimenting with unique flavors like peanut butter or mint, there’s a chocolate ice cream recipe for everyone. The key to making the best chocolate ice cream at home lies in the quality of your ingredients and the care you put into preparing the mixture.

From using the best cocoa powder to experimenting with different add-ins, you can create an ice cream that’s customized to your taste. So, grab your ingredients, churn away, and enjoy the best chocolate ice cream summer has to offer!
