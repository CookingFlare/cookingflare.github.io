Korean Spicy Stew with Pork and Kimchi (Kimchi Jjigae)


Warm, comforting, and bursting with bold flavors, kimchi jjigae (pronounced “kim-chee jee-gay”) is an iconic Korean dish that exemplifies the magic of fermentation and the depth of Korean culinary tradition. This spicy pork and kimchi stew is a favorite family meal across Korea, a dish that transcends time with its hearty ingredients and rich, tangy broth. Whether you’re whipping it up for a quick lunch or savoring it as the center of a shared dinner with friends, kimchi jjigae is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

This recipe takes the traditional flavors and techniques to create an authentic, satisfying stew while being accessible to beginner cooks. Perfect for chilly nights or when you’re craving something soulful, kimchi jjigae is the epitome of comfort food that showcases the harmonious balance of spice, umami, and fermented goodness.

What Makes Kimchi Jjigae So Special?

At its core, kimchi jjigae highlights the transformative power of kimchi, Korea’s beloved fermented cabbage. The aged kimchi lends the stew its signature tang and depth, amplified by the addition of savory pork and a robust broth spiked with gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) and gochujang (Korean chili paste). These ingredients come together to create a dish that is simultaneously spicy, sour, and deeply satisfying.

Another reason kimchi jjigae stands out is its flexibility. While pork is commonly used, you can make variations with beef, tuna, or even tofu for a vegetarian-friendly approach. The stew is a quintessential Korean comfort food that can evolve based on the ingredients you have on hand or dietary preferences.

Gathering the Ingredients

Before diving into the cooking process, gather all the necessary ingredients to ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience. Here’s what you’ll need:

Key Ingredients:

  • Kimchi (aged is best): 2 cups, chopped
    Older kimchi provides a stronger, tangy flavor when used in jjigae. If your kimchi is relatively fresh, you might want to incorporate a dash of vinegar to deepen the sour notes.

  • Pork belly or shoulder: 8 oz, sliced thinly
    Pork lends a rich, meaty flavor to the broth. Ensure you slice it thinly for faster cooking and better integration into the stew.

  • Firm tofu: 7 oz, sliced into cubes
    Tofu absorbs the flavors of the stew beautifully and adds an irresistible creamy texture to the dish.

  • Onion: 1 medium, sliced
    Contributes sweetness and texture, complementing the heat of the stew.

  • Scallions: 2 stalks, chopped
    Adds freshness and a subtle onion-like flavor.

  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
    Essential for depth and complexity.

  • Gochugaru: 2 tablespoons
    Provides the characteristic smoky heat that makes this stew shine.

  • Gochujang: 1 tablespoon
    A thick chili paste that enhances both spice and depth.

  • Vegetable or pork stock: 4 cups
    The base liquid for the stew. You can use water in a pinch, but stock adds flavor.

  • Sesame oil: 1 tablespoon
    For sautéing pork and aromatics.

  • Soy sauce or salt: To taste
    Balances the dish’s seasoning.

Preparing Kimchi Jjigae

Now that you’ve assembled the ingredients, it’s time to bring them together into a delicious stew:

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics and Pork

In a large pot or Korean earthenware pot (ttukbaegi), heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, stirring until fragrant, about 1 minute. Next, toss in the sliced pork and cook until the meat turns white and begins to brown slightly. This step builds a flavorful base for the stew.

Step 2: Add Kimchi and Spices

To the pot, add the chopped kimchi and stir well to incorporate with the pork mixture. Let the kimchi cook for 2-3 minutes to enhance its flavor. Sprinkle in the gochugaru and add gochujang, stirring to coat the ingredients evenly. This will create a beautiful reddish hue and an aromatic, spicy foundation for the broth.

Step 3: Pour in the Stock

Carefully pour the vegetable or pork stock into the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce it to a simmer. Let the stew bubble gently for 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. During this time, the kimchi softens, the pork absorbs the spice, and the broth reduces slightly for a more concentrated taste.

Step 4: Add Tofu and Soy Sauce

Add the tofu cubes to the pot, stirring gently to ensure they don’t break. Season the soup with soy sauce or salt according to your taste. Continue simmering for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the vibrant flavors.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Turn off the heat and add chopped scallions as a garnish, reserving some for presentation. Serve the hot kimchi jjigae immediately, ideally with a side of steamed white rice. The rice balances the spice and complements the stew’s depth.

Tips for Perfecting Kimchi Jjigae

Here are a few helpful tips to ensure your kimchi jjigae turns out flawless every time:

  1. Use well-aged kimchi: Older kimchi gives the stew its signature flavor. The longer your kimchi has fermented, the deeper the tangy undertones.

  2. Don’t rush the simmering process: Allow time for the flavors to meld fully. The longer the stew simmers, the more cohesive and robust the taste becomes.

  3. Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder stew, reduce the amount of gochugaru and gochujang. Not a fan of extreme heat? Serve the dish alongside cold banchan (side dishes) or extra rice for balance.

Why Kimchi Jjigae Is the Ultimate Comfort Food

Kimchi jjigae embodies the spirit of Korean home-cooking—simple yet impactful, hearty yet wholesome. It’s a dish that’s rooted in tradition yet adaptive to modern kitchens worldwide. Each spoonful of the spicy, tangy broth carries an explosion of flavor, making it a deeply satisfying meal capable of warming both body and soul.

Lastly, this dish is proof that a handful of humble ingredients can create culinary magic. From the fermented cabbage of kimchi to the rich umami of pork and the creamy tofu nestled in the broth, there’s something undeniably magical about the way these flavors fuse together. With kimchi jjigae, you’re not just cooking a meal—you’re connecting with a centuries-long tradition of Korean food culture.

Make It Your Own

The beauty of kimchi jjigae is that it welcomes personalization. Try adding mushrooms for extra earthiness, potatoes for heartiness, or zucchini for a subtle sweetness. You can even swap the pork for seafood like shrimp or canned tuna for a completely different but equally delicious stew.

Bringing Korea to Your Table

As you enjoy your homemade kimchi jjigae, you’ll find yourself embracing the cultural richness of Korea and exploring the bold, unapologetic flavors that have become beloved across the globe. The beauty of this dish lies beyond its complexity—it’s a celebration of simplicity, where thoughtful cooking and quality ingredients shine.

For those seeking to expand their culinary horizons or simply craving something comforting and flavorful, kimchi jjigae is an unforgettable addition to your repertoire. Cook it once, and we’re certain you’ll find yourself revisiting this spicy, tangy stew time and time again.
