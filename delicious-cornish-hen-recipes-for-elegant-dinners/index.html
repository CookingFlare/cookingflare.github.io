Delicious Cornish Hen Recipes for Elegant Dinners


Cornish hens, with their petite size and exquisite flavor, are a perfect choice for creating an elegant dining experience. These birds are not only visually appealing but also versatile in terms of flavor profiles, making them an ideal centerpiece for dinner parties, special occasions, or intimate gatherings. In this blog post, we will explore several delicious Cornish hen recipes that will elevate your dining experience and impress your guests.

What are Cornish Hens?

Cornish hens, also known as Cornish game hens, are a breed of chicken that is notably smaller and more tender than regular chickens. They typically weigh around 1 to 2 pounds, making them perfect for single servings. The meat is succulent and can be prepared in various ways, from roasting to grilling, braising, or even frying. Their mild flavor pairs beautifully with a range of herbs, spices, and side dishes, allowing for endless culinary creativity.

Why Choose Cornish Hens for Elegant Dinners?

  1. Presentation: Cornish hens have an elegant appearance that can enhance the visual appeal of your dinner table. Their petite size allows for beautiful plating and presentation.

  2. Flavor: The meat is tender and moist, offering a delightful taste that appeals to a wide range of palates.

  3. Portion Control: Serving individual hens ensures that each guest gets their own perfectly cooked bird, making it easier to control portion sizes and cater to dietary preferences.

  4. Versatility: Cornish hens can be seasoned and stuffed in numerous ways, allowing you to customize your dish to match the theme of your dinner.

Now, let’s dive into some mouth-watering recipes featuring Cornish hens that will make your next dinner party unforgettable!

Herb-Roasted Cornish Hens

Ingredients

  • 2 Cornish hens (about 1.5 pounds each)
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Hens: Rinse the Cornish hens under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Place them in a roasting pan.

  3. Make the Marinade: In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  4. Marinate the Hens: Rub the marinade all over the hens, ensuring they are well coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor).

  5. Roast: Pour the chicken broth into the roasting pan and place it in the preheated oven. Roast the hens for about 1 hour, basting them with the pan juices every 20 minutes, until the skin is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  6. Serve: Remove the hens from the oven and let them rest for 10 minutes. Garnish with fresh rosemary sprigs and serve with your favorite sides.

Stuffed Cornish Hens with Wild Rice and Cranberries

Ingredients

  • 2 Cornish hens
  • 1 cup wild rice, cooked
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup celery, diced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Make the Stuffing: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onion and celery until softened. In a large bowl, combine the sautéed vegetables with cooked wild rice, cranberries, pecans, parsley, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

  3. Prepare the Hens: Rinse and dry the Cornish hens. Season the inside and outside with salt and pepper.

  4. Stuff the Hens: Fill each hen with the wild rice mixture. Secure the openings with kitchen twine or toothpicks.

  5. Roast: Place the stuffed hens in a roasting pan and pour chicken broth into the bottom of the pan. Cover with foil and roast for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and roast for an additional 15 minutes until the skin is golden brown.

  6. Serve: Let the hens rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve with a side of seasonal vegetables.

Glazed Cornish Hens with Orange and Ginger

Ingredients

  • 2 Cornish hens
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 orange, sliced for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine orange juice, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and let cook for 5 minutes until slightly thickened.

  3. Prepare the Hens: Rinse and dry the Cornish hens. Place them in a roasting pan and brush the glaze generously over the hens.

  4. Roast: Roast the hens for about 45 minutes, basting with the glaze every 15 minutes, until the skin is caramelized and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  5. Serve: Garnish with orange slices and fresh cilantro. This dish pairs wonderfully with jasmine rice or a fresh salad.

Cornish Hens with Pomegranate Molasses

Ingredients

  • 2 Cornish hens
  • 1/4 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds for garnish
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Marinate the Hens: In a bowl, combine pomegranate molasses, olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper. Coat the Cornish hens with the mixture and let marinate for at least 1 hour.

  3. Roast: Place the hens in a roasting pan and roast for about 1 hour, basting with the marinade every 20 minutes until the skin is crispy and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh pomegranate seeds and mint leaves. Serve with couscous or roasted vegetables for a complete meal.

Honey-Balsamic Cornish Hens

Ingredients

  • 2 Cornish hens
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Make the Marinade: Whisk together honey, balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper in a bowl.

  3. Marinate the Hens: Place the hens in a large resealable bag and pour in the marinade. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

  4. Roast: Transfer the hens to a roasting pan and pour the marinade over them. Roast for about 1 hour, basting occasionally, until the hens are golden brown and cooked through.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh thyme before serving. This dish pairs beautifully with roasted potatoes or a seasonal vegetable medley.

Tips for Cooking Perfect Cornish Hens

  • Brining: Consider brining your hens for a few hours before cooking to enhance moisture and flavor.
  • Use a Meat Thermometer: To ensure the hens are perfectly cooked, use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C).
  • Resting Time: Allow your hens to rest for at least 10 minutes after cooking. This helps the juices redistribute, resulting in tender meat.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to try different marinades and stuffings. Cornish hens pair well with sweet, savory, and spicy ingredients.

Conclusion

Cornish hens are an elegant and delicious option for your next dinner gathering. With their tender meat and ability to absorb a variety of flavors, they are sure to be a hit with your guests. Whether you choose to roast them simply with herbs, stuff them with savory fillings, or glaze them with sweet sauces, these recipes are bound to impress. Explore the versatility of Cornish hens and enjoy creating memorable dining experiences that will leave a lasting impression. Happy cooking!
