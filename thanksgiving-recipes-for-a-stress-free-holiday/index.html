Thanksgiving Recipes for a Stress-Free Holiday


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and, of course, fantastic food. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a novice in the kitchen, creating a stress-free meal can be a challenge. To help you out, we’ve put together some easy, delicious recipes that will leave your guests coming back for seconds – and maybe even thirds.

  1. Creamy Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a Thanksgiving staple, and this creamy version will be the star of your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lbs. russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15 minutes.

  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.

  3. Add warm milk, butter, sour cream, buttermilk, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

  4. Mash the ingredients together until smooth.

  5. Serve hot and enjoy!

  6. Herb Roasted Turkey

A perfectly cooked turkey is the centerpiece of any Thanksgiving feast. This herb-roasted turkey recipe is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 14-15 lb. whole turkey
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup fresh bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp. fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp. fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tbsp. fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F.

  2. Rinse turkey inside and out with cold water; pat dry.

  3. In a small bowl, mix together softened butter, bread crumbs, Parmesan cheese, herbs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

  4. Rub the mixture all over the turkey, inside and out.

  5. Place the turkey on a roasting rack inside a roasting pan.

  6. Cover the turkey loosely with aluminum foil.

  7. Cook the turkey for about 3 hours or until internal temperature reaches 165°F.

  8. Remove the foil and let the turkey rest for about 15 minutes before carving.

  9. Serve and enjoy!

  10. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel Topping

Sweet potato casserole is a must-have at any Thanksgiving gathering. This decadent recipe features a crunchy pecan streusel topping that your guests won’t be able to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/3 cup packed brown sugar
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp. ground ginger
  • Pecan Streusel Topping (see below)

Pecan Streusel Topping Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large bowl, combine mashed sweet potatoes, softened butter, brown sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, salt, cinnamon, and ginger.
  3. Spread the sweet potato mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  4. Prepare the pecan streusel topping by mixing together flour, brown sugar, softened butter, chopped pecans, and vanilla extract.
  5. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the sweet potato mixture.
  6. Cook for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

Conclusion:

These stress-free Thanksgiving recipes will have your family and friends thanking you for the incredible feast. With creamy mashed potatoes, a perfectly roasted turkey, and a decadent sweet potato casserole, your holiday gathering will be unforgettable. Happy cooking!
