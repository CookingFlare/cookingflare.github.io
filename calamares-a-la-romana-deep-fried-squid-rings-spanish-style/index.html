Calamares a la Romana: Deep-Fried Squid Rings, Spanish-Style


If you’re a seafood lover, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to dive into the delicious world of Calamares a la Romana, also known as Deep-Fried Squid Rings, Spanish-Style. This traditional Spanish dish is a popular tapas option and is loved for its crispy texture and flavorful taste. So, let’s get started and learn how to make this mouthwatering dish right at home!

Ingredients

To make Calamares a la Romana, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the squid. Clean the squid thoroughly and remove the skin, head, and tentacles. Cut the squid into rings and set aside.

  2. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, paprika, and black pepper. Mix well to evenly distribute the spices.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to a temperature of 350°F (175°C).

  4. Take a handful of squid rings and coat them in the flour mixture, making sure to shake off any excess. Repeat this process until all the squid rings are coated.

  5. Carefully place the coated squid rings into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them for about 2-3 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy.

  6. Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried squid rings from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels. This will help absorb any excess oil.

  7. Repeat the frying process with the remaining squid rings until they are all cooked.

  8. Serve the Calamares a la Romana hot with lemon wedges on the side. Squeezing some lemon juice over the fried squid rings adds a refreshing tanginess that perfectly complements the dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can add some garlic powder or cayenne pepper to the flour mixture.

  • Calamares a la Romana is often served with aioli, a garlic mayonnaise sauce. You can make your own aioli by mixing mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.

  • If you want to make this dish healthier, you can try baking the squid rings instead of deep-frying them. Simply coat the rings in the flour mixture, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and bake in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for about 12-15 minutes or until they are crispy and golden brown.

  • Experiment with different dipping sauces like tartar sauce or spicy marinara sauce to find your favorite flavor combination.

Conclusion

Calamares a la Romana is a classic Spanish dish that brings a taste of the Mediterranean right to your table. With its crispy exterior and tender squid rings, it’s a delightfully addictive dish that is perfect for sharing with friends and family. Whether you’re hosting a tapas party or simply craving some seafood, this recipe is sure to impress. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy this Spanish delicacy in the comfort of your own home. Buen provecho!
