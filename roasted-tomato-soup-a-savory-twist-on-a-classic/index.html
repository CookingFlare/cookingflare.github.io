Roasted Tomato Soup: A Savory Twist on a Classic


Roasted Tomato Soup is a classic comfort food that warms the soul with its rich flavors and velvety texture. This recipe takes the traditional tomato soup to new heights by adding a savory twist through the process of roasting the tomatoes. The result is a soup that bursts with deep, caramelized flavors, making it the perfect dish to savor on a cozy evening.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of ripe tomatoes
  • 1 onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the tomatoes in half and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Drizzle the tomatoes with olive oil and sprinkle them with salt and black pepper.
  4. Roast the tomatoes in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes, or until they are soft and slightly charred.
  5. In a large pot, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic, and sauté until they are translucent and fragrant.
  6. Add the roasted tomatoes, dried basil, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir everything together and let it cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the soup to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop further.
  8. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy. Be careful when blending hot liquids, and if using a regular blender, blend in batches.
  9. If desired, stir in the heavy cream to add richness and creaminess to the soup. This step is optional, and the soup is equally delicious without it.
  10. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. Add more salt and pepper if needed.
  11. Serve the roasted tomato soup hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves for an extra burst of freshness.

Why Roasting?

Roasting the tomatoes before making the soup is what sets this recipe apart from the traditional method. The process of roasting intensifies the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and adds a depth of flavor that takes the soup to a whole new level. As the tomatoes cook in the oven, their natural sugars caramelize, resulting in a rich and robust taste that is hard to resist.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

While this recipe for roasted tomato soup is delicious on its own, you can always get creative and add your own twist. Here are a few ideas to inspire you:

  • Grilled Cheese Croutons: Cut your favorite cheese into small cubes and place them between two slices of bread. Grill the sandwiches until the cheese is melted and gooey. Cut the grilled cheese into crouton-sized pieces and serve them on top of the soup for a delightful crunch and cheesy goodness.
  • Herb Infusion: Add a handful of fresh herbs, such as basil, thyme, or rosemary, to the soup while it simmers. The herbs will infuse their aromatic flavors into the soup, enhancing its overall taste.
  • Spicy Kick: For those who enjoy a bit of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the soup. The subtle spiciness will add an extra layer of complexity to the flavors.
  • Garnish Galore: Get creative with your garnishes. Try topping your soup with a dollop of sour cream, a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, or a drizzle of balsamic glaze. These additions will not only enhance the presentation but also add extra flavor dimensions.

Final Thoughts

Roasted Tomato Soup is a comforting classic that can be easily elevated with a few simple steps. Whether enjoyed on a chilly night or served as an appetizer at a dinner party, this savory twist on a timeless favorite is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the oven, and let the roasting begin. Indulge in the rich flavors and warmth of this soup, and savor every spoonful.
