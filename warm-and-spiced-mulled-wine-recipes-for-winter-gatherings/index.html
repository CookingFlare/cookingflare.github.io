Warm and Spiced Mulled Wine Recipes for Winter Gatherings


As the winter months approach, there’s nothing quite like gathering around the fireplace with a warm and spicy mulled wine to get everyone in the holiday spirit. Mulled wine, also known as spiced wine, is a traditional European beverage that’s been enjoyed for centuries. It’s a perfect drink for winter gatherings, as it’s both soothing and festive.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the most popular warm and spiced mulled wine recipes that you can serve at your next winter gathering. From classic spices like cinnamon and cloves to more unique ingredients like star anise and citrus, we’ll cover it all.

Classic Mulled Wine Recipe

This is a simple and traditional mulled wine recipe that’s perfect for beginners. It’s made with just a few ingredients, including red wine, sugar, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of red wine (such as Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 6-8 whole cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup of orange juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the red wine, sugar, cinnamon sticks, cloves, nutmeg, and ginger.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the wine is hot and fragrant.
  3. Remove the pot from the heat and let it steep for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Strain the wine into a large pitcher or jug and discard the spices.
  5. Serve the mulled wine hot, with or without a splash of orange juice.

Spiced Apple Mulled Wine Recipe

This recipe adds a delicious twist to the classic mulled wine recipe by incorporating apple cider and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of red wine (such as Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 6-8 whole cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup of apple cider
  • 1/4 cup of sliced apples (such as Granny Smith or Golden Delicious)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the red wine, sugar, cinnamon sticks, cloves, nutmeg, and ginger.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the wine is hot and fragrant.
  3. Add the apple cider and sliced apples to the pot and stir to combine.
  4. Remove the pot from the heat and let it steep for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Strain the wine into a large pitcher or jug and discard the spices and apples.
  6. Serve the mulled wine hot, garnished with a slice of apple.
