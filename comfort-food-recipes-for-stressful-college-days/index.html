Comfort Food Recipes for Stressful College Days


College life is a whirlwind of lectures, assignments, and social events, often accompanied by a fair share of stress. When stress levels rise, so does the craving for comfort food—those warm, nostalgic dishes that make everything feel a little bit better. This blog post is dedicated to easy and delightful comfort food recipes that will soothe your soul and help you power through those stressful college days.

1. Creamy Macaroni and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil and add the macaroni. Cook according to package instructions until al dente, then drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In the same pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and whisk for about 1-2 minutes until it forms a roux. Gradually add the milk while continuously whisking to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens.

  3. Add Cheese: Remove the pot from heat and stir in the cheddar cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper until the cheese is melted and the sauce is creamy.

  4. Combine: Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and mix well. If desired, transfer to a baking dish, sprinkle breadcrumbs on top, and broil for a few minutes until golden brown.

  5. Serve: Enjoy your creamy mac and cheese as a comforting meal that feels like a warm hug.

2. Hearty Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into pieces

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until soft and fragrant.

  2. Add Remaining Vegetables: Stir in the carrots, celery, zucchini, and green beans. Cook for about 5 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.

  3. Simmer the Soup: Add the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes.

  4. Serve Warm: Ladle the hearty vegetable soup into bowls and enjoy a nourishing meal packed with vitamins and flavor.

3. Classic Grilled Cheese Sandwich

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bread (white, whole grain, or sourdough)
  • 4 slices of cheese (cheddar, American, or your favorite)
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Butter the Bread: Spread butter on one side of each slice of bread.

  2. Assemble the Sandwich: Place two slices of bread butter-side down in a skillet. Layer the cheese on top, then cover with the remaining bread slices, butter-side up.

  3. Grill the Sandwich: Cook over medium heat for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and the cheese is melted.

  4. Serve: Cut the grilled cheese in half and pair it with your favorite soup for the ultimate comfort food combo.

4. Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons milk (optional, for extra creaminess)

Instructions:

  1. Cream the Butter and Sugars: In a mixing bowl, beat together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until creamy. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  2. Add Flour and Salt: Gradually mix in the flour and salt until combined. If desired, add milk for a creamier texture.

  3. Fold in Chocolate Chips: Stir in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed.

  4. Chill: For food safety, consider chilling the dough for about 30 minutes before enjoying. Scoop out small amounts of dough and indulge in this sweet treat.

5. One-Pan Chicken and Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, skinless and boneless
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Chicken: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and paprika. Add to the skillet and cook until browned on both sides. Remove and set aside.

  2. Cook Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the onion and garlic. Sauté until softened.

  3. Add Rice and Broth: Stir in the rice, then add the chicken broth. Bring to a boil.

  4. Combine and Simmer: Return the chicken to the skillet, cover, and reduce heat to low. Cook for about 20 minutes or until the rice is tender and has absorbed the liquid. Add the frozen peas and carrots during the last 5 minutes of cooking.

  5. Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot. This one-pan dish is perfect for busy students needing a quick yet satisfying meal.

6. Banana Oatmeal Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Maple syrup and fresh fruit for serving

Instructions:

  1. Blend Ingredients: In a blender, combine the oats, milk, banana, baking powder, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  2. Cook Pancakes: Preheat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of the batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown.

  3. Serve: Stack the pancakes on a plate, drizzle with maple syrup, and top with fresh fruit for a wholesome breakfast.

7. Easy Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Meat: In a large pot, brown the ground meat over medium heat. Drain excess fat if necessary.

  2. Add Vegetables and Spices: Stir in the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Add the chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Pour in the beans and diced tomatoes. Mix well, bring to a simmer, and let cook for 20-30 minutes.

  4. Serve: Enjoy your chili hot, garnished with cheese, sour cream, or green onions if desired. This dish is perfect for meal prep and can be stored for easy reheating.

Conclusion

Comfort food is an essential part of navigating the ups and downs of college life. The recipes shared in this blog post are not only simple to prepare but also bring a sense of warmth and nostalgia to your table. Whether you’re studying late into the night or coming home after a long day, these dishes will provide the comfort you need. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the therapeutic nature of cooking. Happy eating!
