Classic Korean Noodle Dish: Bibim Guksu Recipe


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or looking to expand your culinary horizons, Bibim Guksu is a must-try dish. This classic Korean noodle dish is a refreshing and flavorful cold noodle salad that’s perfect for hot summer days. The combination of chewy noodles, crisp vegetables, and a tangy, spicy sauce makes Bibim Guksu a delightful and satisfying meal. In this blog post, you’ll learn how to make this delicious dish at home and impress your family and friends with your Korean cooking skills.

Ingredients

To make Bibim Guksu, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of somen or wheat noodles
  • 1/2 cup of julienned cucumber
  • 1/2 cup of julienned carrot
  • 1/2 cup of julienned radish
  • 1/2 cup of julienned Korean pear
  • 1/4 cup of julienned red cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons of toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of Korean red pepper paste (Gochujang)
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • Crushed roasted peanuts for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Noodles: Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and rinse them under cold water. This will stop the cooking process and ensure that the noodles remain firm and chewy.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Julienne the cucumber, carrot, radish, Korean pear, and red cabbage. Place the julienned vegetables in a bowl of cold water for a few minutes to crisp them up.

  3. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, Korean red pepper paste (Gochujang), rice vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, and minced garlic. Adjust the seasoning to your taste by adding more sugar or vinegar if desired.

  4. Assemble the Dish: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked and cooled noodles with the prepared vegetables. Pour the sauce over the noodles and vegetables, then toss everything together until well coated.

  5. Serve: Divide the Bibim Guksu into individual serving bowls and garnish with toasted sesame seeds, chopped green onions, and crushed roasted peanuts if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a protein boost, you can add sliced boiled egg, grilled chicken, or pan-seared tofu to the Bibim Guksu.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, adjust the amount of Korean red pepper paste (Gochujang) in the sauce to suit your heat preference.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables based on your preferences or what’s in season.

Bibim Guksu is best enjoyed fresh, but you can store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a day. Just be sure to give it a good toss before serving again to redistribute the sauce.

Final Thoughts

Bibim Guksu is a versatile dish that allows for creativity and personalization. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply enjoy exploring new flavors, this classic noodle dish is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. The combination of textures and flavors in Bibim Guksu makes it a delightful and satisfying meal that’s perfect for any occasion. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure to create this mouthwatering Korean delicacy in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
