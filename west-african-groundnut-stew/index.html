West African Groundnut Stew


Welcome to our flavorful journey to West Africa! Today, we’ll be exploring the vibrant and aromatic world of West African cuisine with a classic recipe – West African Groundnut Stew. This delicious and hearty stew is a beloved dish in many West African countries, known for its rich flavors and unique combination of ingredients. So, let’s dive right in and discover the secrets to mastering this culinary masterpiece!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for extra heat)
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup natural peanut butter
  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook for another minute, stirring frequently to release their fragrant aromas.

  3. Stir in the ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for an additional minute to toast the spices, enhancing their flavors.

  4. Add the sweet potatoes and diced red bell pepper to the pot, stirring well to coat them with the aromatic spice mixture. Cook for 5 minutes to slightly soften the vegetables.

  5. Pour in the chopped tomatoes, coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the stew for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and cooked through.

  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter and a ladleful of the hot stew liquid until smooth. This will help to prevent lumps when adding the peanut butter to the pot.

  8. Slowly add the peanut butter mixture to the stew, stirring continuously to incorporate it evenly. This will thicken the stew and give it a rich, creamy texture.

  9. Add the cooked chickpeas to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well and simmer for another 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  10. Once the stew has reached your desired consistency, remove it from the heat. Serve the West African Groundnut Stew hot, garnished with fresh cilantro for a burst of freshness.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve successfully prepared a tantalizing West African Groundnut Stew that is sure to impress your taste buds and transport you to the vibrant culinary landscape of West Africa. This hearty and flavorful stew combines the earthiness of sweet potatoes, the creaminess of coconut milk, the richness of peanut butter, and the warmth of aromatic spices to create a truly unforgettable dining experience. So, gather your loved ones, dive into this West African delicacy, and savor every spoonful of this savory masterpiece. Enjoy!
