Simple and Delicious Meatball Sub Recipes


There’s something truly comforting about a hearty meatball sub. It combines flavorful meatballs nestled in a crusty roll, topped with rich marinara sauce and gooey melted cheese. Perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a casual gathering, the meatball sub is both satisfying and versatile. Whether you’re feeding a crowd or just craving a homemade comfort food classic, these simple recipes will help you create a meatball sub masterpiece in your own kitchen.

The Essentials of a Great Meatball Sub

A meatball sub’s success lies in its combination of textures and flavors. There are four key components to crafting the perfect sandwich: the bread, the meatballs, the sauce, and the cheese. Let’s dive into why each element is important before moving on to the recipes.

Fresh, Quality Bread

For a meatball sub, the bread serves as the foundation. It should be sturdy enough to hold the meatballs and sauce without falling apart. Hoagie rolls, French baguettes, or Italian sub rolls work beautifully. A light toasting adds extra texture and helps prevent sogginess, especially if the sauce is generous.

Juicy, Flavorful Meatballs

The heart of the dish—the meatballs—should be tender, flavorful, and well-seasoned. Ground beef is the classic choice, but you can switch it up with pork, turkey, or a combination. Binding ingredients like breadcrumbs and eggs help keep the meatballs moist and ensure they don’t crumble.

Rich, Savory Sauce

A good marinara or tomato-based sauce complements the meatballs’ richness. The sauce should strike a balance between acidity and sweetness. Homemade versions allow you to customize the flavor, but quality store-bought options can work too.

Delicious Cheese

Melty cheese adds a finishing touch to a meatball sub. Mozzarella is traditional for its stretchiness, but Parmesan, provolone, or even cheddar can bring a unique flair. Broiling the assembled subs briefly ensures the cheese melts evenly and turns golden.

Meatball Sub Recipe #1: Classic Italian Style

Ingredients:

  • For the Meatballs:

    • 1 pound ground beef
    • ½ pound ground pork
    • ½ cup breadcrumbs
    • 1 egg
    • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • ½ teaspoon black pepper

  • For the Sauce:

    • 2 cups marinara sauce (homemade or store-bought)
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 shallot, diced
    • 1 clove garlic, minced

  • For Assembly:

    • 4 hoagie rolls
    • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
    • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Meatballs:
    Combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan cheese, parsley, garlic, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix well but avoid overhandling the meat to keep it tender. Roll the mixture into golf-ball-sized meatballs.

  2. Cook the Meatballs:
    Heat a skillet over medium heat with a drizzle of olive oil. Sear the meatballs until browned on all sides. Remove them from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Make the Sauce:
    In the same skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the shallot and garlic until fragrant. Pour in the marinara sauce and bring to a gentle simmer. Add the meatballs back into the sauce and cook until they’re fully cooked, about 15 minutes.

  4. Assemble the Subs:
    Toast the hoagie rolls lightly. Spoon a generous amount of sauce and meatballs into each roll. Top with shredded mozzarella cheese and broil briefly until the cheese melts.

  5. Optional Garnishes:
    Add fresh basil leaves before serving.

Meatball Sub Recipe #2: Spicy and Savory Twist

Ingredients:

  • For the Meatballs:

    • 1 pound ground turkey
    • ¼ cup breadcrumbs
    • 1 egg
    • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • 1 teaspoon chili powder

  • For the Sauce:

    • 2 cups chipotle tomato sauce
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 jalapeño, finely diced

  • For Assembly:

    • 4 French baguette slices, split lengthwise
    • 1 cup shredded pepper jack cheese
    • Pickled jalapeños for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Meatballs:
    Mix the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan, cilantro, smoked paprika, and chili powder in a bowl. Shape into small meatballs and set aside.

  2. Cook the Meatballs:
    Brown the meatballs in a pan with olive oil. Remove once browned and set aside.

  3. Make the Sauce:
    Sauté the finely diced jalapeño in olive oil. Add the chipotle tomato sauce and let it simmer for a few minutes. Place the cooked meatballs back into the pan and simmer for 10 minutes.

  4. Build the Subs:
    Slightly toast the baguettes. Fill them with meatballs and sauce, sprinkle with pepper jack cheese, and broil until melted.

  5. Add Spice:
    Garnish with pickled jalapeños for extra heat.

Meatball Sub Recipe #3: Vegetarian Option

Sometimes you want the indulgence of a meatball sub without the meat. This vegetarian recipe delivers on flavor and texture.

Ingredients:

  • For the Meatballs:

    • 1 cup cooked quinoa
    • ½ cup breadcrumbs
    • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 egg
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning

  • For the Sauce:

    • 2 cups tomato basil sauce
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil

  • For Assembly:

    • 4 ciabatta rolls
    • 1 cup provolone cheese

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Meatballs:
    Mix the quinoa, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, egg, garlic powder, and Italian seasoning in a bowl. Form into small meatballs and chill in the fridge for 30 minutes to firm up.

  2. Cook the Meatballs:
    Bake the quinoa meatballs in a 400°F preheated oven for 20 minutes or until firm.

  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    Heat olive oil in a saucepan, add the tomato basil sauce, and bring to a simmer. Carefully add the quinoa meatballs into the sauce.

  4. Assemble the Subs:
    Toast the ciabatta rolls. Spoon meatballs and sauce onto the bread and top with provolone cheese. Broil until cheese is golden and bubbly.

Tips for Meatball Sub Success

  • Prep in Advance: Make the meatballs ahead and freeze them for easy weeknight dinners.
  • Customizations: Add roasted vegetables, fresh greens, or hot sauces for extra flavor.
  • Serving Suggestions: Pair your meatball sub with a fresh salad or chips for a complete meal.

These foolproof, simple recipes make it easy to create mouthwatering meatball subs for any occasion. Whether you stick to the classic Italian approach, explore bolder flavors, or opt for a vegetarian twist, you’ll have a satisfying sandwich to enjoy. With quality ingredients and a little preparation, the meatball sub might just become a new favorite in your recipe collection.
