Classic Cocktails from the Prohibition Era: Recipes to Discover


The Prohibition era was a time of secrecy and rebellion, and one of the most iconic ways people expressed their defiance was through the creation of classic cocktails. These timeless concoctions have stood the test of time and continue to be beloved by cocktail enthusiasts around the world. In this blog post, we’ll take a journey back in time to explore some of the most popular and enduring cocktails from the Prohibition era, and we’ll provide you with the recipes to recreate these historic libations in the comfort of your own home.

The Sidecar

The Sidecar is a classic cocktail that is believed to have originated in Paris during World War I. This timeless drink is a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and strong, making it a favorite among cocktail connoisseurs. To make a traditional Sidecar, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz Cognac
  • 1 oz Cointreau
  • 1 oz Fresh Lemon Juice
  • Sugar for Rim (Optional)

To prepare the Sidecar, start by moistening the rim of a cocktail glass and dipping it in sugar to coat the rim. In a shaker, combine the Cognac, Cointreau, and lemon juice with ice. Shake well and strain the mixture into the prepared cocktail glass. Garnish with a twist of lemon, and enjoy this timeless classic.

The Bee’s Knees

The Bee’s Knees is a Prohibition-era cocktail that gained popularity as a way to mask the harsh flavor of bathtub gin. This refreshing and aromatic drink is a delightful blend of gin, honey, and lemon, creating a harmonious symphony of flavors. To craft your own Bee’s Knees cocktail, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz Gin
  • 3/4 oz Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 3/4 oz Honey Syrup (Equal Parts Honey and Water)

To make the Bee’s Knees, combine the gin, lemon juice, and honey syrup in a shaker with ice. Shake vigorously and strain the mixture into a chilled coupe glass. The Bee’s Knees is a delightfully simple yet sophisticated cocktail that is sure to impress your guests.

The Mary Pickford

Named after the silent film star, the Mary Pickford cocktail is a delightful blend of rum, pineapple juice, grenadine, and maraschino liqueur. This sweet and fruity concoction exudes elegance and charm, making it a perfect choice for any occasion. To create your own Mary Pickford cocktail, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz Light Rum
  • 2 oz Pineapple Juice
  • 1/4 oz Grenadine
  • 1/4 oz Maraschino Liqueur

To prepare the Mary Pickford, simply combine all the ingredients in a shaker with ice. Shake well and strain the mixture into a chilled cocktail glass. Garnish with a maraschino cherry, and transport yourself back to the glamour of the Roaring Twenties with this delightful libation.

The Manhattan

The Manhattan is a timeless classic that has been enjoyed by cocktail enthusiasts for generations. This sophisticated and robust drink is a perfect balance of whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters, creating a complex and satisfying flavor profile. To craft your own Manhattan, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz Rye Whiskey
  • 1 oz Sweet Vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura Bitters
  • Maraschino Cherry for Garnish

To make the Manhattan, simply stir the rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters with ice until well-chilled. Strain the mixture into a chilled cocktail glass and garnish with a maraschino cherry. The Manhattan is a timeless classic that exudes elegance and sophistication with every sip.

Conclusion

The Prohibition era may have been a time of secrecy and restriction, but it also gave birth to some of the most iconic and enduring cocktails in history. These classic libations continue to capture the imagination of cocktail enthusiasts and serve as a testament to the creativity and resilience of the human spirit. By recreating these historic cocktails in your own home, you can experience a taste of the past and pay homage to the ingenuity of those who defied the odds to enjoy a good drink. So, gather your ingredients, raise a toast to the past, and savor the timeless flavors of the Prohibition era. Cheers!
