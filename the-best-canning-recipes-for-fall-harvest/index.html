The Best Canning Recipes for Fall Harvest


As the crisp air of fall sets in and the leaves begin to change, the bounty of the fall harvest provides the perfect opportunity to preserve the flavors of the season for later enjoyment. Canning is an age-old tradition that not only helps you make the most of the abundance of fresh produce but also offers a way to enjoy the tastes of fall long after the harvest is over.

Whether you’re an experienced canner or just getting started, these canning recipes will guide you through some of the best ways to preserve the flavors of autumn. From sweet jams to savory sauces, these recipes capture the essence of fall and ensure you have delicious homemade pantry staples all year round.

1. Classic Apple Butter

Apple butter is a quintessential fall preserve. Its rich, spiced flavor captures everything we love about autumn. This spread is perfect for slathering on toast, pancakes, or as a topping for oatmeal. It’s easy to make and requires just a handful of ingredients, most of which are found in your fall harvest basket.

Ingredients:

  • 10 to 12 medium apples (such as Fuji, Gala, or Granny Smith)
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup water

Directions:

  1. Peel, core, and chop the apples into small pieces.
  2. In a large pot, add the apples, water, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat until the apples break down and become soft, about 20 minutes.
  3. Use an immersion blender to purée the mixture until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can transfer the apples to a regular blender, blend, and return the mixture to the pot.
  4. Stir in the sugars, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.
  5. Continue to cook the mixture on low heat, stirring occasionally, for 1 to 2 hours until it thickens to a spreadable consistency. Be patient, as the flavors will develop as it reduces.
  6. Once thickened, ladle the apple butter into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  7. Process the jars in a water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing.
  8. Let the jars cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place. Apple butter can last for up to a year when properly sealed.

2. Roasted Butternut Squash Soup (For Canning)

Fall’s star vegetable, the butternut squash, is perfect for soups, and when canned, it can be enjoyed throughout the colder months. This roasted butternut squash soup is flavorful and creamy, with a touch of warmth from ginger and garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium butternut squashes, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional for creaminess)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the cubed squash on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast the squash in the oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the squash is tender and lightly caramelized.
  3. In a large pot, heat a bit of olive oil over medium heat and sauté the onion until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic and ginger to the onions and sauté for another 2 minutes.
  5. Once the squash is done, add it to the pot with the sautéed onion mixture. Add vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.
  6. Use an immersion blender to purée the soup until smooth (or transfer to a blender).
  7. Stir in coconut milk if desired for a creamy texture, and adjust salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Ladle the soup into sterilized canning jars, leaving about an inch of headspace.
  9. Process the jars in a pressure canner for 90 minutes at 10 pounds of pressure.
  10. Let the jars cool completely before storing. Canned butternut squash soup can last for up to a year.

3. Cranberry Sauce

Fresh cranberries are a fall favorite, and homemade cranberry sauce is a must-have at every fall meal, especially around Thanksgiving. This cranberry sauce recipe adds a hint of orange and cinnamon for a festive touch.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Directions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, orange juice, and orange zest. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cranberries pop and the sauce thickens.
  3. Add the cinnamon stick and continue to cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat, discard the cinnamon stick, and pour the sauce into sterilized canning jars.
  5. Process the jars in a water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing.
  6. Once cooled, store the jars in a cool, dark place. Homemade cranberry sauce can last for up to one year.

4. Spicy Pickled Jalapeños

If you’re looking to add a bit of heat to your fall pantry, spicy pickled jalapeños are the way to go. These vibrant and flavorful peppers are perfect for adding to tacos, sandwiches, and even salads.

Ingredients:

  • 12 to 15 fresh jalapeños
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds

Directions:

  1. Slice the jalapeños into rounds, discarding the stems.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, salt, sugar, garlic, mustard seeds, cumin, and coriander. Bring to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let the brine simmer for 5-7 minutes to allow the spices to infuse.
  4. Pack the sliced jalapeños into sterilized canning jars.
  5. Pour the hot brine over the jalapeños, ensuring they are fully submerged. Leave about 1/2 inch of headspace in the jar.
  6. Process the jars in a water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing.
  7. Let the jars cool and store them in a cool, dark place. Pickled jalapeños can last for up to a year and become more flavorful over time.

5. Pear Jam

Pear jam is an elegant, yet simple preserve that’s perfect for canning in the fall. It has a delicate sweetness with a slight tang from lemon juice, making it a perfect pairing for cheese boards or as a topping for desserts.

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups peeled, cored, and chopped pears
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 package pectin (optional, for faster setting)

Directions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the pears and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, for about 15 minutes until the pears soften.
  2. Mash the pears with a potato masher or use an immersion blender for a smoother texture.
  3. Stir in the sugar and bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. If you’re using pectin, add it according to the instructions on the package. Continue to cook, stirring frequently, until the mixture reaches the desired consistency. You can test this by placing a spoonful of jam on a cold plate and seeing if it sets.
  5. Ladle the hot pear jam into sterilized canning jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Process the jars in a water bath for 10 minutes.
  7. Once the jars have cooled, check the seals. Store in a cool, dark place for up to a year.

6. Pumpkin Puree

One of the best things about fall is the arrival of pumpkins. Pumpkin puree is a versatile ingredient, great for baking, soups, or even smoothies. Canning it allows you to enjoy pumpkin-flavored dishes all year long.

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 small sugar pumpkins

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cut the pumpkins in half, remove the seeds, and place them cut-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Roast the pumpkins for about 45-60 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and easily pierced with a fork.
  4. Scoop out the flesh and place it in a food processor or blender. Purée until smooth.
  5. Pour the pumpkin puree into sterilized canning jars, leaving 1 inch of headspace.
  6. Process the jars in a pressure canner for 90 minutes at 10 pounds of pressure.
  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing. Canned pumpkin puree can last up to a year.

Conclusion

The fall harvest offers an abundance of delicious produce that can be preserved for future enjoyment. Canning is a wonderful way to extend the flavors of the season, and with these recipes, you can create a variety of pantry staples that will bring the warmth of fall into your home all year long. Whether you’re savoring a spoonful of apple butter on a chilly morning or warming up with a bowl of roasted butternut squash soup, these fall canning recipes will ensure that your autumn harvest isn’t just a fleeting moment—it will be enjoyed well into the months ahead.
