Slow Cooker Asian-Inspired Dishes You Need to Try


As the temperatures drop and the days get shorter, there's nothing like a hearty and aromatic slow cooker meal to warm the heart and soul. In this post, we'll take you on a culinary journey to explore the rich flavors of Asia, where spices, herbs, and marinades come together in perfect harmony. From the bold and spicy to the delicate and sweet, slow cooker Asian-inspired dishes offer a world of possibilities for the adventurous home cook. Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken Imagine a symphony of flavors, with crunchy peanuts and tender chicken creating a delightful harmony. Our Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken recipe is a slow cooker version of the iconic Chinese dish, with the added bonus of minimal effort and hands-on time. Here's what you'll need: Ingredients:

1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs 2 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns 1 tsp ground ginger 1 tsp soy sauce 1 tsp rice vinegar 1 tsp cornstarch 2 tbsp vegetable oil 4-6 dried red chilies 1/2 cup roasted peanuts 1 tsp sesame oil 1 tsp garlic, minced Salt and pepper, to taste Scallions, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

In a small bowl, whisk together Szechuan peppercorns, ginger, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Add the mixture to the slow cooker and stir to combine. Add chicken thighs, vegetable oil, dried red chilies, peanuts, sesame oil, garlic, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Serve hot, garnished with chopped scallions.

Thai Coconut Chicken Curry The warmth and creaminess of Thai curries are a perfect match for the slow cooker's magic. Our Thai Coconut Chicken Curry recipe is an explosion of flavors, with the added bonus of being dairy-free. Here's what you'll need: Ingredients:

1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts 2 cups coconut milk 1 cup vegetable broth 2 tbsp Thai red curry paste 2 tbsp fish sauce 1 tsp honey 1 tsp grated ginger 1 tsp ground cumin 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper, to taste Crushed red pepper flakes, for serving

Instructions:

In a small bowl, whisk together coconut milk, vegetable broth, Thai red curry paste, fish sauce, honey, ginger, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add the mixture to the slow cooker and stir to combine. Add chicken breasts, chopped basil, and chopped cilantro to the slow cooker. Stir to combine. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Serve hot, garnished with crushed red pepper flakes and additional cilantro, if desired.

Japanese Teriyaki Beef Japanese cuisine is all about balance and harmony, with flavors and textures coming together in perfect sync. Our Japanese Teriyaki Beef recipe is a slow cooker masterpiece, with a sweet and savory teriyaki sauce that elevates the star of the show: the beef. Here's what you'll need: Ingredients:

1 lb beef brisket 1/4 cup soy sauce 1/4 cup sake or mirin 2 tbsp brown sugar 2 tbsp rice vinegar 2 tbsp vegetable oil 4 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp grated ginger 1 tsp sesame oil Salt and pepper, to taste Sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sake or mirin, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and vegetable oil. Add the mixture to the slow cooker and stir to combine. Add beef brisket, minced garlic, grated ginger, sesame oil, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. Serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped scallions, if desired.

Malaysian Beef Rendang Malaysian cuisine is a fascinating fusion of flavors, with a focus on spice, fragrance, and freshness. Our Malaysian Beef Rendang recipe is a slow cooker masterpiece, with a complex curry-flavored stew that showcases the star of the show: the beef. Here's what you'll need: Ingredients:

1 lb beef short ribs 2 cups coconut milk 1/4 cup vegetable oil 4-6 dried red chilies 2 tbsp grated ginger 2 tbsp ground cumin 1 tsp coriander powder 1 tsp garam masala powder 1 tsp salt 1/2 tsp black pepper 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Scallions, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

In a small bowl, whisk together coconut milk, vegetable oil, dried red chilies, grated ginger, cumin, coriander powder, garam masala powder, salt, and black pepper. Add the mixture to the slow cooker and stir to combine. Add beef short ribs and chopped cilantro to the slow cooker. Stir to combine. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-6 hours. Serve hot, garnished with chopped scallions and additional cilantro, if desired.

Tips and Variations

For a thicker sauce, whisk together 1 tablespoon cornstarch and 2 tablespoons water, then add it to the slow cooker during the last 30 minutes of cooking. Add your favorite vegetables, such as bell peppers, carrots, or green beans, to any of these recipes for added flavor and nutrition. Experiment with different types of protein, such as chicken thighs, pork shoulder, or lamb shanks, for a new twist on these classic Asian-inspired dishes.

Conclusion The magic of slow cooker Asian-inspired dishes lies in their potential for endless creativity and experimentation. By combining bold and aromatic spices, herbs, and marinades with the convenience of a slow cooker, you'll be transported to a world of flavors and cuisines. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner, these recipes will guide you through the journey, with tips, variations, and inspiration to take your cooking to the next level. So go ahead, explore, experiment, and taste the magic of slow cooker Asian-inspired dishes for yourself!
