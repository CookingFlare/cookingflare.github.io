The Best Wines for Pairing with Savory Pies and Tarts


There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm, flaky savory pie or tart fresh from the oven. Whether it’s a buttery quiche loaded with garden vegetables or a rustic meat pie bursting with hearty flavors, these dishes have a way of satisfying both the stomach and the soul. But as much as a savory pie stands beautifully on its own, it becomes an extraordinary experience when paired with the perfect wine. The right bottle can elevate the flavors, enhance the ingredients, and turn a simple meal into a culinary masterpiece.

Selecting wines to accompany savory pies and tarts requires more than just pulling whatever bottle is closest. It’s about understanding the layers of flavors in the dish, considering the richness of the crust, and balancing the filling’s ingredients with the complexities of the wine. While it might sound daunting at first, mastering these pairings is more about exploring and experimenting than following strict rules. In this post, we’ll dive into some exceptional wine options that pair beautifully with popular styles of savory pies and tarts, giving you the confidence and inspiration to create an unforgettable dining experience.

The Key to Pairing Wine with Savory Pies

Before diving into specific combinations, it’s important to understand the foundational principles of pairing wine with food. The goal is to create harmony between the wine and the dish—letting the elements complement each other without any single component overpowering the experience. Here are a few factors to consider when pairing wine with savory pies or tarts:

  1. Richness of the Crust: The buttery, flaky crusts of pies and tarts are naturally rich, so wines with good acidity work well to cut through the fat and refresh the palate.

  2. Ingredients of the Filling: The filling is the star of the dish and dictates the primary direction of the wine pairing. Hearty meat pies pair best with robust reds, while vegetable or cheese-based tarts often shine alongside whites or lighter-bodied reds.

  3. Balance of Flavors: Sweet, salty, earthy, or spicy—the key is finding a wine that either mirrors the flavors or contrasts them in a complementary way. For example, earthy mushrooms pair beautifully with earthy Pinot Noir, while tangy goat cheese tarts benefit from vibrant, citrusy whites.

  4. Temperature and Seasonings: If the pie is served warm with bold spices, you might lean toward more structured wines. If it’s a chilled tart with light herbs, crisp and refreshing wines are ideal.

Pairing Ideas for Popular Types of Savory Pies and Tarts

Let’s explore some popular styles of savory pies and tarts and the wines that can make them truly shine.

1. Quiche Lorraine

This classic tart boasts a creamy custard base filled with smoky bacon and nutty Gruyère cheese, nestled in a buttery crust. The flavors are rich, indulgent, and slightly salty, calling for a wine that can balance the fatness of the cheese and bacon.

  • Wine Pairing: A well-chilled Chardonnay is the perfect match here, especially one with subtle oak aging. An unoaked option also works well, as its crisp apple and citrus notes can brighten the richness of the dish. Alternatively, consider a light-bodied Pinot Noir for a red wine pairing; its earthy undertones complement the Gruyère, while its acid structure balances the fatty richness of the filling.

2. Mushroom and Herb Pie

Earthy mushrooms, hearty herbs like thyme or rosemary, and a deep brown gravy-style sauce create a dish that is comforting and profound. The umami flavor dominates here, lending itself to wines that are equally earthy and nuanced.

  • Wine Pairing: A medium-bodied Pinot Noir is a foolproof choice for mushroom-based pies. Its cherry and berry notes provide a touch of fruitiness, while its earthy, forest-floor characteristics mirror the flavors of the mushrooms. If you’re leaning toward white wine, a Chenin Blanc or Dry Riesling with subtle minerality can offer a refreshing contrast to the earthy tones.

3. Chicken Pot Pie

Few dishes scream comfort food quite like a classic chicken pot pie, with its creamy interior, tender chunks of chicken, and a golden crust that flakes beautifully with every bite.

  • Wine Pairing: Opt for a Viognier, known for its creamy texture and stone fruit flavors, which can hold up to the richness of the sauce while complementing the savory chicken. If you prefer red, a supple Merlot with soft tannins and ripe plum notes will pair wonderfully without overwhelming the delicate flavors.

4. Vegetable and Goat Cheese Tart

Light, refreshing, and bursting with the flavors of fresh vegetables like zucchini, tomatoes, or spinach, this tart often leans on tangy goat cheese to unify the flavors. The acidity and herbal notes of the dish make it a natural partner for crisp, aromatic wines.

  • Wine Pairing: A classic Sauvignon Blanc is a go-to option here. Its bright acidity can cut through the creaminess of the goat cheese, while its herbaceous qualities echo the freshness of the vegetables. For a sparkling wine option, consider a Prosecco, whose effervescence brings a playful element to the pairing.

5. Steak and Ale Pie

Bold and hearty, this robust pie is often filled with tender chunks of beef, thick gravy, and perhaps a touch of caramelized onion. It’s a winter warmer that calls for an equally robust wine.

  • Wine Pairing: A full-bodied red is the ideal choice for matching the weight of this dish. Cabernet Sauvignon, with its firm tannins and dark fruit flavors, is a classic pairing. If you prefer something a bit softer, a Malbec offers similar depth while delivering a smoother finish.

6. Mediterranean Tarts (Feta, Olives, and Sundried Tomatoes)

These tarts burst with salty and tangy Mediterranean flavors, often featuring a flaky pastry base layered with feta cheese, briny olives, basil, and sweet sundried tomatoes. The combination of saltiness and acidity begs for a wine with bright and fragrant qualities.

  • Wine Pairing: A Rosé is a match made in heaven for these flavors, particularly a dry Provençal rosé. Its subtle red berry notes and crisp finish offer just the right amount of contrast to the pronounced saltiness of the ingredients. Pinot Grigio or a bright Assyrtiko from Greece are also excellent white wine options.

7. Lamb and Mint Pie

Rich lamb encased in a flaky crust and paired with mint has an elegant and slightly gamey flavor profile that can handle wines with both structure and subtlety.

  • Wine Pairing: A Syrah/Shiraz is a natural choice here. Its savory spice and dark fruit qualities complement the lamb, while its structure stands up to the richness of the pie. For a white wine pairing, try a Marsanne or Roussanne, which can provide weight and nuance without overshadowing the dish.

Tips for Serving Wine with Savory Pies and Tarts

  • Temperature Matters: Serve whites chilled and reds slightly below room temperature for the best experience. Too warm, and the reds can taste overly alcoholic; too cold, and the subtleties of the wine can get muted.

  • Experiment and Share: Don’t hesitate to try an unconventional wine pairing—sometimes the best pairings come from happy accidents! Share your discoveries with friends and family.

  • Match the Mood: Think about the overall ambiance of the meal. If it’s a casual brunch with friends, choose playful wines like sparkling options or rosé. For an elegant dinner, go for structured reds or complex whites.

Pairing wine with savory pies and tarts isn’t an exact science, but it is an art form that evolves with every meal. Whether you’re opting for a bold red to match a rich steak pie or a crisp white to balance a light vegetable tart, the key is to let the wine enhance the flavors of the dish and your overall dining experience.

So, the next time you prepare a flaky pastry crust filled with layers of flavor, take the time to select a wine that will make it truly special. Pour yourself a glass, sit back, and savor every bite—because life is better with pie and wine. Cheers!
