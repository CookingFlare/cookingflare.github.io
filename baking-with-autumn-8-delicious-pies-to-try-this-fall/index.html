Baking with Autumn: 8 Delicious Pies to Try This Fall


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, autumn invites us to embrace the warmth of our kitchens. One of the most delightful ways to celebrate this season is through baking, and what better way to do that than with a selection of delicious pies? From classic flavors to innovative twists, these eight pie recipes will not only fill your home with enticing aromas but also create cherished memories around the table. Let’s dive into the world of autumn baking!

1. Classic Pumpkin Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (fresh or canned)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, brown sugar, granulated sugar, eggs, spices, salt, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Pour the filling into the unbaked pie crust.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 40-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean.
  5. Allow the pie to cool before serving. Top with whipped cream for an extra touch of indulgence.

Why It’s a Must-Try:

Pumpkin pie is a quintessential autumn dessert that captures the essence of the season. Its creamy texture and warm spices make it a favorite at Thanksgiving gatherings and fall festivities.

2. Apple Cinnamon Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 6 cups thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, lemon juice, flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Toss until the apples are well coated.
  3. Pour the apple mixture into the unbaked pie crust and dot with butter.
  4. Cover with a second pie crust, sealing the edges. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  5. Brush the top crust with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender.

Why It’s a Must-Try:

Apple cinnamon pie is a comforting dessert that evokes memories of family gatherings and cozy evenings. The combination of sweet apples and warm spices is a true celebration of fall.

3. Pear and Ginger Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 5 cups sliced ripe pears
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the sliced pears, sugar, ginger, lemon juice, cornstarch, cinnamon, and salt. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Pour the pear mixture into the unbaked pie crust.
  4. Cover with a second pie crust, sealing the edges and cutting slits for steam.
  5. Brush the top with beaten egg.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbly.

Why It’s a Must-Try:

The combination of sweet pears and zesty ginger creates a unique flavor profile that is both refreshing and comforting. This pie is perfect for those looking to try something a little different this fall.
