Cooking for Two: Flavorful and Easy Indian Recipes


Cooking for two can often feel like a challenge, especially when you’re trying to keep things interesting and flavorful. When it comes to Indian cuisine, the possibilities are endless. Rich with spices, herbs, and bold flavors, Indian food offers an array of dishes that can be customized for any palate. Whether you’re craving a comforting curry, a zesty chutney, or a vibrant rice dish, Indian cooking for two is both achievable and delightful.

This blog will guide you through a variety of Indian recipes that are perfect for two servings. Each recipe is designed to be easy, quick, and full of the authentic flavors that make Indian cuisine so beloved around the world.

1. Paneer Butter Masala

One of the most popular Indian dishes, Paneer Butter Masala, is the ultimate comfort food. It’s rich, creamy, and packed with flavor. Paneer (Indian cottage cheese) is the star ingredient in this dish, which is cooked in a luscious tomato-based gravy with aromatic spices. It’s perfect for a romantic dinner or a cozy weeknight meal for two.

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat the butter over medium heat and sauté the chopped onions until golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add the pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the tomato mixture.
  4. Add the paneer cubes and stir gently to coat them in the masala.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream and garam masala. Stir well, and let the curry simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with naan or steamed rice.

Tip: If you prefer a spicier dish, increase the amount of red chili powder or add a green chili while cooking the onions.

2. Aloo Gobi (Potato and Cauliflower Curry)

This classic vegetarian curry, Aloo Gobi, features potatoes and cauliflower cooked in a blend of spices. It’s a simple yet delicious dish that pairs well with roti or rice. It’s also a great option for those looking for a low-carb meal as it’s light but satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add the cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add the chopped onion and sauté until golden.
  2. Add the chopped tomato, turmeric, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  3. Add the diced potato and cauliflower florets, stirring gently to mix with the spices.
  4. Add salt, a splash of water, and cover. Cook for 10-15 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Sprinkle garam masala on top and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Tip: For an extra layer of flavor, you can add a teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste while cooking the onions.

3. Chana Masala (Chickpea Curry)

Chana Masala is a hearty, flavorful curry made with chickpeas and an array of spices. It’s perfect for a quick weeknight meal that’s filling, nutritious, and full of zest. The combination of tomatoes, onions, and spices in this dish creates a deliciously tangy base that complements the chickpeas wonderfully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add the cumin seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the chopped onion and sauté until golden.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the chopped tomato, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate.
  4. Stir in the chickpeas and a little water to achieve your desired consistency. Let the curry simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Finish by adding garam masala and lemon juice. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with rice or naan.

Tip: For extra flavor, add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) along with the cumin seeds.

4. Dal Tadka (Lentil Soup)

Dal Tadka is a quintessential Indian comfort food, made from yellow lentils cooked with spices and then tempered with ghee or oil. It’s a staple in many Indian households and is a perfect accompaniment to rice or flatbreads.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (yellow lentils)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash the dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker or pot until soft and mushy (about 3-4 whistles in a pressure cooker).
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a separate pan and add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomato, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Add the cooked dal to this tempering and mix well. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, adjusting the consistency with water as needed.
  5. Sprinkle garam masala on top and garnish with fresh coriander.

Tip: For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a squeeze of lemon juice just before serving.

5. Tandoori Chicken (Oven-Baked)

Tandoori Chicken is a classic Indian dish that is typically cooked in a clay tandoor oven. However, it’s easy to recreate the same smoky flavor in your regular oven. This recipe uses yogurt and a blend of spices to marinate the chicken, making it tender, flavorful, and perfect for a cozy dinner for two.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken thighs or breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, cumin powder, red chili powder, lemon juice, and salt to make the marinade.
  2. Coat the chicken pieces with the marinade and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  3. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred at the edges.
  5. Serve with a side of naan or a fresh salad.

Tip: For extra smokiness, you can place a small piece of charcoal on the baking sheet while the chicken bakes and cover it with a lid for a few minutes.

6. Vegetable Pulao

This Vegetable Pulao is a fragrant rice dish made with mixed vegetables and spices. It’s perfect when you’re looking for a light yet satisfying meal. The recipe is simple to make, and you can adjust the spice level according to your preference.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, rinsed
  • 1 small carrot, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot and add the cumin seeds, cloves, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften.
  4. Add the rinsed rice, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Add 1.5 cups of water, bring to a boil, then lower the heat and cover. Let it cook for 10-15 minutes until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.

Tip: You can add a few strands of saffron soaked in warm milk for a luxurious touch.

Final Thoughts

Indian cuisine offers a rich tapestry of flavors, textures, and spices, making it an exciting culinary journey for anyone looking to cook for two. From rich curries to fragrant rice dishes, there’s something for every taste and occasion. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced cook, these recipes are simple to follow and guaranteed to bring authentic Indian flavors to your home.

Happy cooking, and enjoy the incredible flavors of India in your own kitchen!
