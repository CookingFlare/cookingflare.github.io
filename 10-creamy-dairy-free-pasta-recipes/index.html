10 Creamy Dairy-Free Pasta Recipes


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the creamy, luxurious appeal of a bowl of pasta. But for those who are dairy-free, finding rich and satisfying pasta recipes can sometimes feel like a challenge. The good news is that with a bit of creativity and the right ingredients, it’s absolutely possible to enjoy indulgent, dairy-free versions of creamy pasta dishes without compromising on taste. Whether you’re lactose-intolerant, vegan, or just looking to try something new, these 10 creamy dairy-free pasta recipes are sure to hit the spot.

1. Creamy Cashew Alfredo

A delicious alternative to traditional Alfredo sauce, this recipe uses soaked cashews to create a naturally creamy consistency.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cashews (soaked for 4–6 hours)
  • 1 ½ cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked fettuccine pasta

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and add them to a blender along with almond milk, garlic, nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Toss the sauce with freshly cooked fettuccine.
  3. Garnish with fresh parsley or cracked black pepper. Serve immediately.

The cashews add a velvety smooth base, while the nutritional yeast packs that cheesy, umami punch you’d expect from Alfredo sauce.

2. Avocado Pesto Pasta

Adding ripe avocado to pesto creates a lush, creamy texture that’s entirely dairy-free and packed with healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large ripe avocado
  • 3 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Your choice of pasta

Instructions:

  1. Combine avocado, basil, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice in a food processor. Blend until smooth.
  2. Season with salt and toss with hot, cooked pasta until evenly coated.
  3. Top with cherry tomatoes or roasted pine nuts for extra flavor and crunch.

This dish is refreshing, vibrant, and perfect for a quick weeknight meal.

3. Coconut Milk Mushroom Stroganoff

Coconut milk is an excellent dairy-free choice for making creamy sauces. It works beautifully in this mushroom stroganoff.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 12 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup canned coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • Cooked wide noodles or tagliatelle

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onions and mushrooms in olive oil until the mushrooms are soft and browned. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Add coconut milk and Dijon mustard and simmer until the sauce thickens slightly.
  3. Toss with freshly cooked noodles and serve topped with chopped parsley.

The creaminess of the coconut milk beautifully complements the earthy flavor of mushrooms.

4. Dairy-Free Butternut Squash Carbonara

For a fall-inspired twist, this carbonara swaps eggs and cream for a luscious butternut squash purée.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1/3 cup cashew cream (blend soaked cashews with water until smooth)
  • 2 garlic cloves, roasted
  • Cooked spaghetti or linguine

Instructions:

  1. Cook the butternut squash in vegetable broth until tender, then purée with roasted garlic and cashew cream.
  2. Toss the creamy mixture with cooked spaghetti. Add salt, pepper, and a touch of nutmeg for warmth.
  3. Top with crispy coconut “bacon” for added flavor.

This recipe has a slightly sweet undertone, balanced perfectly with savory toppings.

5. Silken Tofu Vodka Sauce

Silken tofu creates a luscious, dairy-free version of the classic vodka sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block silken tofu
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 2 tbsp vodka (optional, for cooking depth)
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • Red pepper flakes, to taste
  • Penne or rigatoni pasta

Instructions:

  1. Blend silken tofu, marinara sauce, vodka, and nutritional yeast until smooth.
  2. Heat the sauce in a pan, adding red pepper flakes for a gentle kick. Simmer for a few minutes.
  3. Toss with cooked pasta and garnish with fresh basil.

This recipe is creamy, tangy, and surprisingly rich, considering how light the tofu is.

6. Lemon Tahini Pasta

Tahini isn’t just for hummus — it makes an incredibly creamy pasta sauce with a rich, nutty flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Your choice of pasta

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together tahini, warm water, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper until a smooth sauce forms.
  2. Toss with your favorite pasta, making sure each strand is well-coated.
  3. Serve with steamed broccoli or sautéed spinach for added color and nutrients.

Simple yet satisfying, this dish is perfect for tahini lovers.

7. Sun-Dried Tomato Cream Pasta

Sun-dried tomatoes bring a concentrated, tangy flavor to this cashew-based cream sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed, drained)
  • 1 cup cashew cream
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 cup reserved pasta water
  • Pasta of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Blend the sun-dried tomatoes, garlic, cashew cream, and pasta water until smooth.
  2. Heat the sauce gently on the stovetop, then toss with cooked pasta.
  3. Sprinkle with fresh basil for garnish and serve.

The rich, tangy flavor of the sun-dried tomatoes shines through, making this dish irresistible.

8. Roasted Cauliflower Alfredo

Roasted cauliflower is surprisingly creamy when blended, making it a fantastic base for an Alfredo-inspired sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups almond milk
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • A pinch of nutmeg
  • Fettuccine pasta

Instructions:

  1. Roast the cauliflower with olive oil until tender and golden, about 25 minutes.
  2. Blend the roasted cauliflower, almond milk, garlic, and nutmeg until smooth.
  3. Heat the sauce and toss with cooked pasta.

This dairy-free Alfredo is light yet rich, with a depth of flavor added by the roasted cauliflower.

9. Pumpkin Sage Pasta

Pumpkin purée creates a creamy, earthy, and comforting sauce that’s perfect for colder months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pumpkin purée
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp sage, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • Pasta of choice

Instructions:

  1. Heat the pumpkin purée in a skillet and stir in coconut milk and sage.
  2. Simmer for a few minutes to meld the flavors. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Toss with cooked pasta and garnish with crispy sage leaves.

This recipe is cozy, aromatic, and makes for a great fall or winter dinner.

10. Dairy-Free Spinach Artichoke Pasta

This recipe takes inspiration from creamy spinach artichoke dip and turns it into a satisfying pasta dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsweetened oat milk
  • 1/2 cup cashew cream
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 can artichoke hearts, chopped
  • Penne pasta

Instructions:

  1. Heat oat milk and cashew cream in a skillet until warm. Add fresh spinach and chopped artichokes.
  2. Stir until the spinach wilts and the sauce thickens slightly.
  3. Toss with cooked penne pasta and serve.

This pasta is indulgent yet light, with plenty of greens to balance out the creamy sauce.

With these 10 creamy dairy-free pasta recipes in your arsenal, you’ll never feel like you’re missing out on creamy comfort food again. Each dish is packed with flavor and made with wholesome, plant-based ingredients, making them perfect for everyone, whether you follow a dairy-free diet or not. So grab your favorite pasta and get cooking — indulgent, dairy-free meals are just a few ingredients away!
