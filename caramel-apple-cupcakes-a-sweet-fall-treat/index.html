Caramel Apple Cupcakes: A Sweet Fall Treat


As the leaves start to change and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to indulge in the flavors of fall. One of the most beloved autumn treats is the classic caramel apple, and what better way to enjoy it than in the form of a delicious cupcake? These Caramel Apple Cupcakes are a delightful blend of moist apple cake, creamy caramel frosting, and a drizzle of rich caramel sauce. Whether you’re hosting a fall gathering or simply craving a sweet treat, these cupcakes are sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Apple Cupcakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 1/2 cups finely chopped apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)

For the Caramel Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 cups powdered sugar

For the Caramel Drizzle:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.

  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the sour cream, and mix until just combined. Fold in the chopped apples.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely.

  6. To make the caramel frosting, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the brown sugar and milk, and bring the mixture to a boil. Remove from the heat and let it cool slightly. Gradually beat in the powdered sugar until the frosting reaches a spreadable consistency.

  7. Once the cupcakes are cooled, frost them with the caramel frosting using a piping bag or a butter knife.

  8. For the caramel drizzle, heat the granulated sugar in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly. Once the sugar has melted and turned amber in color, add the butter and stir until melted. Carefully pour in the heavy cream while stirring constantly. Continue to cook for another 2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

  9. Drizzle the caramel sauce over the frosted cupcakes, allowing it to cascade down the sides.

  10. Serve and enjoy these delectable Caramel Apple Cupcakes with a warm mug of apple cider or a cozy cup of coffee.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, toss the chopped apples in a tablespoon of lemon juice before adding them to the batter.
  • Add a sprinkle of sea salt to the caramel drizzle for a delightful sweet and salty contrast.
  • For a time-saving option, use store-bought caramel sauce instead of making your own.

These Caramel Apple Cupcakes are a delightful fusion of the beloved fall flavors of apple and caramel, creating a treat that is perfect for any autumn occasion. Whether you’re baking for a gathering or simply treating yourself, these cupcakes are a must-try for the fall season. The warm spices, tender apples, and luscious caramel come together to create a truly irresistible dessert that captures the essence of fall in every bite. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of fall with these Caramel Apple Cupcakes.
