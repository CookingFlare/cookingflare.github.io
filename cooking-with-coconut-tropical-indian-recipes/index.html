Cooking with Coconut: Tropical Indian Recipes


Coconut is a staple ingredient in many tropical cuisines, and Indian cooking is no exception. The versatility of coconut makes it a popular choice for a wide range of dishes, from sweet treats to savory curries. In this blog post, we will explore the world of tropical Indian recipes that feature coconut as a main ingredient.

Coconut Ladoo

Ladoo is a popular Indian sweet that is typically made with gram flour, sugar, and ghee. However, adding coconut to the mixture gives it a unique flavor and texture. Here’s a recipe for coconut ladoo that you can try at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gram flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup coconut flakes
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads, soaked in 1 tablespoon hot water
  • 1/4 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon chopped pistachios (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Dry roast the gram flour in a pan over medium heat until it is lightly browned.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the roasted gram flour, sugar, coconut flakes, ghee, milk, cardamom powder, saffron threads, and rose water (if using).
  3. Mix the ingredients together until they form a smooth dough.
  4. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and pliable.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls and shape into ladoos.
  6. Garnish with chopped pistachios (if using) and serve.

Coconut Chutney

Coconut chutney is a popular condiment in Indian cuisine that is made with coconut, chilies, and spices. It is a great accompaniment to dosas, idlis, and vadas. Here’s a recipe for coconut chutney that you can try at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut flakes
  • 1/2 cup green chilies
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup tamarind paste
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Dry roast the cumin seeds and coriander seeds in a pan over medium heat until they are lightly browned.
  2. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted coconut flakes, green chilies, onion, garlic, ginger, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, cayenne pepper, and salt.
  3. Blend the ingredients together until they form a smooth paste.
  4. Heat the coconut oil in a pan over medium heat and add the tamarind paste.
  5. Cook the tamarind paste for 1-2 minutes until it is lightly browned.
  6. Add the blended coconut mixture to the pan and stir well.
  7. Cook the chutney for 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  8. Serve the coconut chutney with dosas, idlis, and vadas.

Coconut Rice

Coconut rice is a popular side dish in Indian cuisine that is made with coconut milk, rice, and spices. It is a great accompaniment to many Indian dishes. Here’s a recipe for coconut rice that you can try at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ghee
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice in a fine mesh sieve until the water runs clear.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, salt, ghee, ginger, cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid.
  5. Cook the rice for 15-20 minutes until it is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  6. Serve the coconut rice hot with your favorite Indian dishes.

Coconut Curry

Coconut curry is a popular dish in Indian cuisine that is made with coconut milk, curry powder, and spices. It is a great accompaniment to many Indian dishes. Here’s a recipe for coconut curry that you can try at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup curry powder
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger to the saucepan and cook until the onion is lightly browned.
  3. Add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper to the saucepan and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the curry powder to the saucepan and cook for 1-2 minutes until it is lightly browned.
  5. Add the coconut milk to the saucepan and stir well.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the curry for 10-15 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  8. Serve the coconut curry hot with your favorite Indian dishes. Garnish with chopped cilantro (if using).

Coconut Kheer

Coconut kheer is a popular dessert in Indian cuisine that is made with coconut milk, rice, and spices. It is a great way to end a meal. Here’s a recipe for coconut kheer that you can try at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ghee
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads, soaked in 1 tablespoon hot water
  • 1/4 teaspoon chopped pistachios (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice in a fine mesh sieve until the water runs clear.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, salt, ghee, ginger, cardamom powder, and saffron threads.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid.
  5. Cook the rice for 15-20 minutes until it is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  6. Serve the coconut kheer hot or at room temperature. Garnish with chopped pistachios (if using).

In conclusion, coconut is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a wide range of dishes, from sweet treats to savory curries. With its rich flavor and creamy texture, coconut is a great addition to many Indian recipes. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a beginner, experimenting with coconut recipes is a great way to add some variety to your cooking repertoire.
