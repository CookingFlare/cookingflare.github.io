Classic Spaghetti Bolognese: A Timeless Pasta Recipe


When it comes to classic comfort food, few dishes can rival the timeless appeal of spaghetti bolognese. This iconic Italian pasta dish has been a favorite in households around the world for generations, and for good reason. The rich, savory flavors of the meaty bolognese sauce combined with the satisfying twirl of perfectly cooked spaghetti make it a go-to meal for busy weeknights and special occasions alike.

The History of Spaghetti Bolognese

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the history of this beloved dish. Spaghetti bolognese, also known as spaghetti alla bolognese, hails from the northern Italian city of Bologna. While the traditional Bolognese sauce, known as ragù, has a history dating back centuries, the combination of the sauce with spaghetti as we know it today is a more recent development. In fact, the original ragù alla bolognese was typically paired with tagliatelle, a wide, flat pasta, but over time, the dish evolved to include spaghetti, which has become the more popular choice outside of Italy.

The Perfect Bolognese Sauce

The key to a truly exceptional spaghetti bolognese lies in the bolognese sauce itself. This hearty, meat-based sauce is a labor of love, requiring a slow simmer to develop its deep, complex flavors. To start, you’ll need a combination of ground beef and pork, along with aromatic vegetables such as onions, carrots, and celery. The addition of tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, and a splash of red wine adds layers of richness to the sauce, while a hint of nutmeg and a bay leaf infuse it with warmth and depth.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • 1 bay leaf

Cooking Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrot, and celery, and sauté until the vegetables are soft and the onions are translucent.

  2. Brown the Meat: Push the sautéed vegetables to one side of the pot and add the ground beef and pork. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until it’s browned and no longer pink.

  3. Add the Flavor: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for a few minutes to caramelize it, then pour in the crushed tomatoes and red wine. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, nutmeg, and add the bay leaf.

  4. Simmer to Perfection: Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat and let it bubble away for at least an hour, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the more the flavors will meld together, so feel free to let it go for up to three hours if you have the time.

Serving Suggestion

Once your bolognese sauce is ready, it’s time to bring it all together. Cook your favorite spaghetti according to the package instructions until it’s al dente, then toss it with a generous ladleful of the bolognese sauce. Serve it up in warm bowls, perhaps with a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprig of basil for a touch of freshness.

The Final Word

Classic spaghetti bolognese is a dish that’s stood the test of time, and for good reason. Its rich, hearty flavors and simple yet satisfying preparation make it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this timeless pasta recipe is sure to be a hit. So, the next time you’re in the mood for a comforting meal that never goes out of style, give spaghetti bolognese a try. You won’t be disappointed.
