Christmas Food Ideas for a Festive Afternoon Tea


The holidays are the perfect time to gather with friends and family over shared meals, and what better way to celebrate than with an enchanting Christmas-themed afternoon tea? Combining the charm of traditional tea-time delicacies with a festive twist can elevate the experience for everyone around the table. Whether you’re hosting a small get-together or a large holiday party, these Christmas food ideas for a festive afternoon tea will impress your guests and create lasting memories.

In this post, you’ll discover a collection of savory bites, sweet treats, and warm beverages that blend seasonal flavors with classic afternoon tea elegance. This guide also includes tips on presentation and table setting to make your festive tea party truly magical and merry.

Setting the Scene: Creating a Festive Atmosphere

Before diving into the delicious recipes, it’s important to think about how you want your afternoon tea to look and feel. The experience goes beyond just the food – it’s about creating an inviting ambiance that mirrors the warmth and joy of Christmas.

  • Table Setting: Use a crisp white or deep red tablecloth as the base. Accent with gold or silver cutlery and festive napkins adorned with holly or mistletoe patterns.
  • Centerpieces: Fresh pine branches, red berries, and candles can create a cozy, traditional feel. You can also add fairy lights for a touch of sparkle.
  • Teapots and Cups: Choose delicate china or vintage tea sets if possible. Patterned cups with gold rims can add a luxurious touch.
  • Music: Play classic Christmas carols softly in the background to set a joyful tone.

With that ambiance in place, your guests will be ready to enjoy a splendid afternoon of tasty delights.

Savory Bites: The Heart of Your Afternoon Tea

Afternoon tea often conjures images of sweet cakes and scones, but savory finger foods are just as vital. For a Christmas-themed tea, think about incorporating flavors like smoked salmon, roasted vegetables, cranberries, and warm spices into your savory selection.

Mini Brie and Cranberry Tartlets

A perfect balance of creamy, sweet, and flaky, these tartlets are elegant yet easy to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • 100g brie cheese, cut into small cubes
  • 4 tablespoons cranberry sauce
  • Fresh rosemary, chopped finely
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Method:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Roll out puff pastry and cut into small squares or circles.
  3. Place onto a baking tray lined with parchment, brush edges with beaten egg.
  4. Add a cube of brie into the center of each pastry piece.
  5. Top with a teaspoon of cranberry sauce and sprinkle chopped rosemary.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes until puff pastry is golden and risen.
  7. Serve warm or at room temperature.

These bite-sized tartlets offer a burst of Christmas flavor with every mouthful.

Smoked Salmon and Dill Tea Sandwiches

Nothing says refined teatime quite like dainty sandwiches. Smoked salmon adds a luxurious, festive note.

Ingredients:

  • 8 slices white or rye bread, crusts removed
  • 150g smoked salmon slices
  • 150g cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Black pepper, freshly cracked

Method:

  1. Mix cream cheese with dill, lemon juice, and cracked black pepper.
  2. Spread the cream cheese mixture evenly on each bread slice.
  3. Top half with smoked salmon, then sandwich with the remaining slices.
  4. Cut each sandwich into small rectangles or triangles.
  5. Optional: Garnish with a sprig of dill or a thin lemon slice.

These sandwiches are light, creamy, and perfectly festive.

Roast Turkey and Cranberry Mini Rolls

Capture the essence of Christmas dinner in a convenient finger food.

Ingredients:

  • 200g cooked turkey breast, thinly sliced
  • 100g cranberry sauce
  • 8 mini tortilla wraps or flatbreads
  • 100g cream cheese

Method:

  1. Spread cream cheese evenly over the wraps.
  2. Layer turkey slices on top followed by a spoonful of cranberry sauce.
  3. Roll up tightly and slice into bite-sized pinwheels.
  4. Secure with a cocktail stick if needed.

These mini rolls are a festive alternative to traditional tea sandwiches and great for heartier appetites.

Sweet Treats: A Delicate Dessert Selection

No Christmas afternoon tea is complete without an array of indulgent sweets. Incorporate classic flavors like cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves to evoke the holiday spirit.

Gingerbread Scones with Clotted Cream and Jam

Bring warmth and spice into your scones for a cozy Christmas vibe.

Ingredients:

  • 350g self-raising flour
  • 75g cold butter, diced
  • 75g soft brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 150ml milk
  • 2 tablespoons golden syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon mixed spice
  • Clotted cream and raspberry or strawberry jam to serve

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).
  2. Combine flour, sugar, ground ginger, and mixed spice in a bowl.
  3. Rub in cold butter until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Beat egg, milk, and golden syrup together and add to the bowl.
  5. Mix to a soft dough, then turn onto floured surface and knead lightly.
  6. Roll out to 3cm thick and cut into rounds.
  7. Place on a baking tray and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden.
  8. Serve warm with clotted cream and jam.

The spicy notes in these scones evoke the classic taste of gingerbread, making them perfect for Christmas teatime.

Cranberry and White Chocolate Mini Muffins

These petite muffins pack a punch of tart sweetness and smooth chocolate.

Ingredients:

  • 150g self-raising flour
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 50g white chocolate chips
  • 75g dried cranberries
  • 1 egg
  • 100ml milk
  • 75g melted butter
  • Zest of 1 orange

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F) and line mini muffin tins.
  2. Mix flour, sugar, chocolate chips, cranberries, and orange zest.
  3. Whisk egg, milk, and melted butter separately and combine with dry ingredients.
  4. Spoon mixture into tins and bake for 15-18 minutes until golden and risen.
  5. Cool before serving.

The orange zest enhances the Christmas feel, while cranberries add bright color and zing.

Spiced Mulled Wine Jelly

A grown-up delicacy that adds elegance and festive cheer.

Ingredients:

  • 500ml mulled wine (store-bought or homemade)
  • 4 gelatin leaves or 1 tablespoon powdered gelatin
  • Redcurrants and cinnamon sticks (optional for garnish)

Method:

  1. Soak gelatin leaves in cold water for 5 minutes (if using powdered gelatin, sprinkle over 2 tablespoons cold water).
  2. Warm the mulled wine gently (do not boil).
  3. Remove from heat and stir in softened gelatin until dissolved.
  4. Pour into small molds or glasses and refrigerate for at least 4 hours until set.
  5. Garnish with redcurrants and a cinnamon stick.

This jelly captures the flavors of festive mulled wine, providing a light and aromatic dessert.

Teas and Warm Beverages: Enhancing the Festive Mood

The choice of drinks can make or break an afternoon tea. Alongside traditional black and green teas, introducing flavored and spiced options adds a cozy feel to your Christmas tea.

  • Classic English Breakfast Tea: A robust choice that pairs well with all the savory and sweet treats.
  • Cinnamon-Spiced Black Tea: Brew black tea with a cinnamon stick and a slice of orange for holiday cheer.
  • Peppermint Tea: Refreshing and cool, balancing rich foods.
  • Chai Latte: Warm, spiced, and creamy—perfect for winter afternoons.
  • Hot Chocolate with Marshmallows: Make your tea party kid-friendly and indulgent.
  • Mulled Apple Cider: Serve warm with cloves and star anise for a non-alcoholic festive drink option.

Providing a variety of beverages means guests can customize their tea experience and enhances the welcoming vibe.

Presentation Tips: Serving Your Festive Afternoon Tea

How you present your Christmas food ideas elevates the visual appeal and heightens anticipation.

  • Tiered Stands: Showcase savory items on the bottom tier, scones in the middle, and sweets on the top tier.
  • Plates and Platters: Use festive-themed or elegant white platters to create contrast with colorful foods.
  • Garnishing: Pomegranate seeds, rosemary sprigs, edible gold dust, and fresh berries add sparkle to your dishes.
  • Serving Utensils: Provide small tongs for sandwiches and cake forks with scones.

A well-thought-out presentation invites guests to explore and enjoy each item, making the experience more memorable.

Final Thoughts

Hosting a Christmas afternoon tea filled with delightful food and drink can create a warm, festive atmosphere your guests will cherish. From savory bites like smoked salmon sandwiches and cranberry tartlets to sweet indulgences like gingerbread scones and spiced mulled wine jelly, this menu celebrates the flavors of the season with elegance and warmth.

Pair the food with a thoughtfully curated selection of teas and beverages, and surround your table with holiday cheer through decorations and music. With these Christmas food ideas, your afternoon tea will be a heartwarming highlight of the festive season, perfect for sharing laughter, stories, and delicious moments around the teapot.

Enjoy your celebration, and may your Christmas afternoon tea be as joyful and flavorful as the season itself!
