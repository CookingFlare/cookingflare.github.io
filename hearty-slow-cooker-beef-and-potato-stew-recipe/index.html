Hearty Slow Cooker Beef and Potato Stew Recipe


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a hearty beef and potato stew simmering in the slow cooker on a chilly day. This classic comfort food is perfect for feeding a crowd, and it’s a dish that’s sure to warm both body and soul. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a mouthwatering beef and potato stew that’s rich, flavorful, and oh-so-satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a large bowl, season the beef stew meat with salt, pepper, and coat with flour.

  2. Sear the Beef: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches, then transfer to the slow cooker.

  3. Saute the Vegetables: In the same skillet, sauté the onions and garlic until softened. Add the carrots and cook for a few minutes more.

  4. Combine Ingredients in Slow Cooker: Transfer the sautéed vegetables to the slow cooker with the browned beef. Add the diced potatoes, bay leaves, thyme, rosemary, diced tomatoes, and tomato paste.

  5. Add Liquids: Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Stir to combine all the ingredients.

  6. Slow Cook: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 8 hours or on high for 4 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stew is ready, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve hot, and enjoy with a slice of crusty bread or a side salad.

Tips for Success:

  • For a deeper flavor, you can sear the beef in the same skillet you used to sauté the vegetables, allowing the meat to soak up those delicious caramelized bits.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix a tablespoon of cornstarch with a tablespoon of water and stir it into the stew during the last hour of cooking.

Why Choose a Slow Cooker for This Recipe?

Using a slow cooker for this beef and potato stew recipe allows the flavors to meld together beautifully over several hours of gentle cooking. The low, slow heat tenderizes the beef and allows the potatoes and carrots to soak up all the savory goodness of the broth and seasonings. Plus, the convenience of being able to set it and forget it makes this recipe a winner for busy days.

Final Thoughts

This hearty slow cooker beef and potato stew is a timeless classic that’s perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or cozy nights in. The rich flavors and tender chunks of beef will make this stew a favorite in your recipe collection. So, next time you’re craving a comforting, filling meal, give this recipe a try and savor every satisfying spoonful!
