Homemade Apple Pie for Cooking for Two: Classic and Comforting

There’s something undeniably comforting about the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the kitchen. It’s a classic dessert that brings warmth and nostalgia with every bite. If you’re cooking for two and craving a taste of tradition, look no further than this homemade apple pie recipe. Ingredients For the Crust: 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes 2-4 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 3-4 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled, cored, and thinly sliced 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup light brown sugar 1 tablespoon lemon juice 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg A pinch of salt 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces Instructions Making the Crust: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough with a fork until it just holds together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Preparing the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, lemon juice, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss until the apples are evenly coated. Assembling the Pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disc of the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish, gently press it into the bottom and up the sides of the dish, and trim any excess overhang. Pour the prepared apple filling into the pie crust, and dot the top with small pieces of butter. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim, fold, and crimp the edges to seal the pie. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape. Baking the Pie: Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips, and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil. Once done, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 1 hour before slicing. Serving Suggestion This homemade apple pie is best enjoyed warm, perhaps with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.
