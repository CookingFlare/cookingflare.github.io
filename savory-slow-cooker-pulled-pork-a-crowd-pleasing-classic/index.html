Savory Slow Cooker Pulled Pork: A Crowd-Pleasing Classic


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the tender, juicy, and flavorful pulled pork that emerges from a slow cooker. With its rich, savory aroma wafting through the air, this beloved classic is sure to become a staple in your household. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of slow-cooking pork shoulder to perfection, resulting in a dish that’s both easy to make and sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

The Magic of Slow Cooking

Slow cooking is a game-changer when it comes to cooking pork shoulder. By cooking the meat low and slow, you’re able to break down the connective tissues, rendering the meat tender and fall-apart. This method also allows for the infusion of flavors, as the aromatics and spices have ample time to meld with the pork. Whether you’re serving this dish at a backyard barbecue or a weeknight dinner, slow-cooked pulled pork is sure to impress.

The Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless pork shoulder
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your slow cooker to low heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, smoked paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using).
  3. Rub the spice mixture all over the pork shoulder, making sure to coat it evenly.
  4. Place the sliced onion at the bottom of the slow cooker.
  5. Place the pork shoulder on top of the onion.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the barbecue sauce and chicken broth. Pour the mixture over the pork shoulder.
  7. Cover the slow cooker and cook for 8-10 hours or overnight.
  8. After 8-10 hours, remove the pork shoulder from the slow cooker and use two forks to shred it into bite-sized pieces.
  9. Return the shredded pork to the slow cooker and stir to combine with the juices.
  10. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro or scallions, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro or scallions to the slow cooker during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  • If you prefer a spicier pulled pork, increase the amount of cayenne pepper to 1/2 teaspoon or add in some diced jalapeños.
  • For a sweet and tangy twist, add 1/4 cup of honey or maple syrup to the barbecue sauce mixture.
  • Experiment with different types of barbecue sauce, such as Kansas City-style or Texas-style, to give the pulled pork a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

Savory slow cooker pulled pork is a crowd-pleasing classic that’s sure to become a staple in your household. With its tender, juicy texture and rich, savory flavor, this dish is perfect for casual gatherings or family dinners. By following this simple recipe and experimenting with different variations, you’ll be well on your way to becoming the ultimate pulled pork master. So go ahead, grab your slow cooker, and get ready to indulge in a culinary experience that’s sure to delight!
