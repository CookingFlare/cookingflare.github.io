Savor the Creaminess of Malai Kofta Curry


Malai Kofta Curry is a delicious and creamy Indian dish that combines soft and succulent koftas (dumplings) with a rich and flavorful curry. This vegetarian dish is a crowd-pleaser and will leave you craving for more. The koftas are made with a mixture of mashed potatoes, paneer (Indian cottage cheese), and a blend of aromatic spices. They are deep-fried until golden brown and then simmered in a velvety tomato-based curry. The result is a dish that is bursting with flavors and textures, making it the perfect choice for a special occasion or a comforting meal at home.

Ingredients

For the Koftas:

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 200g paneer, grated
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

For the Curry:

  • 3 tablespoons ghee or clarified butter
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, grated paneer, cornflour, ginger paste, garlic paste, green chili, garam masala, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  2. Shape the mixture into small round balls, about the size of a walnut. Ensure that the koftas are smooth and free from cracks.

  3. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully drop the koftas into the oil and fry them until they turn golden brown. Remove the koftas from the oil and set them aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil.

  4. In a separate pan, heat ghee or clarified butter over medium heat. Add the bay leaves, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for a minute until they release their aroma.

  5. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Then, add the ginger paste and garlic paste. Cook for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and add the coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Stir well to coat the onions with the spices. Cook for a minute to let the spices release their flavors.

  7. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook the mixture until the oil separates from the sides. This will take approximately 10-15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent the curry from sticking to the pan.

  8. Once the oil has separated, add the heavy cream and salt. Stir well to combine. Simmer the curry for another 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  9. Gently place the fried koftas into the curry and let them simmer for 5 minutes. This will allow the koftas to absorb the flavors of the curry.

  10. Garnish the Malai Kofta Curry with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with naan, rice, or roti.

Final Thoughts

Indulge in the creamy and aromatic flavors of Malai Kofta Curry. This dish is a true representation of the rich and diverse culinary heritage of India. The soft and melt-in-your-mouth koftas, combined with the luscious tomato-based curry, create a symphony of flavors that will leave you wanting more. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to explore new flavors, this dish is a must-try. So gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and savor the creaminess of Malai Kofta Curry. Enjoy!
