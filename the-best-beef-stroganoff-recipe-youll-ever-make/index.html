The Best Beef Stroganoff Recipe You'll Ever Make


Beef Stroganoff is a classic dish that has stood the test of time, delighting taste buds across generations. This creamy, savory dish, with its tender strips of beef and rich mushroom sauce, is perfect for a cozy family dinner or an impressive meal for guests. In this post, we will guide you through the best Beef Stroganoff recipe you’ll ever make, ensuring that you achieve a dish that is not only delicious but also easy to prepare.

Ingredients

To create the best Beef Stroganoff, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Beef

  • 1.5 pounds of beef sirloin or tenderloin: Cut into thin strips against the grain for tenderness.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour: For dusting the beef.
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil: For searing the beef.
  • 2 tablespoons of butter: For added flavor.

For the Sauce

  • 1 medium onion: Finely chopped.
  • 3 cloves of garlic: Minced.
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms: Sliced (cremini or button mushrooms work well).
  • 1 cup of beef broth: For a rich flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce: Adds depth to the sauce.
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard: For a subtle tang.
  • 1 cup of sour cream: For creaminess.
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh parsley: Chopped, for garnish.

For Serving

  • Egg noodles or rice: Cooked according to package instructions.
  • Additional parsley: For garnish.

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

  1. Trim and Slice: Start by trimming any excess fat from the beef sirloin or tenderloin. Slice the beef into thin strips, about 1/4 inch thick. Cutting against the grain will ensure that the meat remains tender.

  2. Season and Dust: Season the beef strips generously with salt and pepper. Dust them lightly with flour, shaking off any excess. This will help create a nice crust when searing and will also thicken the sauce later.

Step 2: Sear the Beef

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the butter and let it melt.

  2. Sear the Beef: Add the beef strips in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Sear for about 2-3 minutes on each side until browned. You may need to do this in batches. Once browned, remove the beef from the pan and set it aside on a plate.

Step 3: Cook the Vegetables

  1. Sauté the Onions and Garlic: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the Mushrooms: Toss in the sliced mushrooms and cook for about 5-7 minutes until they are browned and have released their moisture. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.

Step 4: Create the Sauce

  1. Deglaze the Pan: Pour in the beef broth, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any flavorful bits stuck to the surface. This step is crucial for building flavor in your sauce.

  2. Add Seasonings: Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and Dijon mustard. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5 minutes, letting it reduce slightly.

  3. Incorporate the Sour Cream: Lower the heat to medium-low and stir in the sour cream until well combined. This will give the sauce its creamy texture. Be careful not to let it boil after adding the sour cream, as it can curdle.

Step 5: Combine and Serve

  1. Return the Beef: Add the seared beef back into the skillet, along with any juices that have accumulated on the plate. Stir to coat the beef in the sauce and heat through for about 2-3 minutes.

  2. Taste and Adjust: Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.

  3. Serve: Serve the Beef Stroganoff over cooked egg noodles or rice. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Beef Stroganoff

  • Choose the Right Cut of Beef: For the best results, use tender cuts of beef like sirloin or tenderloin. These cuts are more tender and will cook quickly without becoming tough.

  • Don’t Overcook the Beef: Searing the beef quickly at high heat ensures it remains tender. Overcooking can lead to chewy meat, so be mindful of cooking times.
