Delightful Allergy-Friendly Raspberry Bars for a Tangy Dessert


Are you looking for a delicious and allergy-friendly dessert recipe that will wow your taste buds? Look no further! These delightful raspberry bars are the perfect combination of tangy and sweet, and the best part is, they are allergy-friendly, making them suitable for almost everyone. Whether you have food sensitivities or just want to enjoy a guilt-free treat, these raspberry bars are sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chia seeds

For the Crumble Topping:

  • 1/2 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons coconut sugar
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.

  2. Make the Crust: In a large bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and salt. Mix until a dough forms. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.

  3. Bake the crust for 12-15 minutes, or until lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

  4. Prepare the Raspberry Filling: In a saucepan, combine the raspberries, maple syrup, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir in the chia seeds. Let the mixture cool for 10 minutes.

  5. Spread the raspberry filling over the cooled crust, spreading it out evenly.

  6. Make the Crumble Topping: In a small bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, melted coconut oil, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Sprinkle the crumble topping over the raspberry filling.

  7. Bake the raspberry bars for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown. Remove from the oven and let the bars cool completely in the pan.

  8. Once cooled, lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang. Place them on a cutting board and cut into squares.

  9. Serve and enjoy these delightful allergy-friendly raspberry bars with a cup of tea or your favorite beverage!

Tips and Substitutions

  • If you’re allergic to almonds, you can substitute the almond flour with sunflower seed flour or a gluten-free flour blend.
  • Feel free to use frozen raspberries if fresh ones are not available. Just be sure to thaw and drain them before using in the recipe.
  • For a sweeter filling, you can adjust the amount of maple syrup according to your taste preferences.

These allergy-friendly raspberry bars are not only a delightful treat but also a healthier alternative to traditional desserts. They are packed with the goodness of raspberries, chia seeds, and wholesome ingredients, making them a guilt-free indulgence. Share this recipe with friends and family, and let everyone enjoy the tangy sweetness of these delightful raspberry bars!
