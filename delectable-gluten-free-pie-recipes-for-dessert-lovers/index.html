Delectable Gluten-Free Pie Recipes for Dessert Lovers


As the sweet and savory aroma of freshly baked pies wafts through the air, it’s hard to resist the temptation to indulge in a slice - or two. But for those with gluten intolerance or sensitivity, the classic pie recipes often contain wheat flour, making it a challenge to enjoy this beloved dessert. Fear not, dear dessert lovers! We’ve got you covered with these delectable gluten-free pie recipes that will satisfy your sweet tooth without compromising on taste.

Gluten-Free Pie Crust Recipe

A crucial component of any pie is the crust, and we’ve got a game-changing recipe that’s easy to make and gluten-free. This crust is made with almond flour, coconut oil, and a hint of salt, providing a flaky and buttery texture that pairs perfectly with your favorite fillings.

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cold water

Combine the almond flour, salt, and coconut oil in a bowl. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the coconut oil into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before rolling it out.

Apple Pie Recipe

There’s no denying the classic combination of tender apples and flaky crust, and this gluten-free apple pie recipe is a must-try. With a blend of Granny Smith and Gala apples, this pie is both sweet and tangy, perfect for snacking or serving at your next gathering.

  • 2 cups gluten-free pie crust dough (above)
  • 6-8 medium-sized apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the gluten-free pie crust dough and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until the apples are evenly coated. Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture and dot the top with the melted butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust dough and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender.

Pumpkin Pie Recipe

As the seasons change and the weather cools, there’s nothing like a warm slice of pumpkin pie to satisfy your sweet tooth. This gluten-free pumpkin pie recipe is a twist on the classic, using coconut milk and a hint of spice to create a creamy and comforting dessert.

  • 2 cups gluten-free pie crust dough (above)
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out the gluten-free pie crust dough and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, coconut milk, granulated sugar, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger until smooth. Beat in the eggs until well combined. Pour the filling into the pie crust and dot the top with the melted butter. Roll out the remaining pie crust dough and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (180°C) and continue baking for an additional 30-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

Lemon Meringue Pie Recipe

Brighten up your dessert menu with this refreshing lemon meringue pie recipe, featuring a gluten-free crust and a tangy lemon curd. The perfect combination of sweet and tart, this pie is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

  • 2 cups gluten-free pie crust dough (above)
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1 cup meringue topping (see below)

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the gluten-free pie crust dough and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. In a large bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, granulated sugar, and softened butter until smooth. Beat in the egg yolks until well combined. Stir in the grated lemon zest. Pour the filling into the pie crust and bake for 15 minutes. While the filling is baking, prepare the meringue topping (see below). Remove the pie from the oven and spread the meringue topping over the filling. Return the pie to the oven and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown.

Meringue Topping Recipe

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened

Preheat the oven to 200°F (90°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, egg whites, cream of tartar, and salt until stiff peaks form. Beat in the softened butter until well combined. Spread the meringue topping over the filling and bake for 5-10 minutes, or until golden brown.

Chocolate Cream Pie Recipe

For those with a sweet tooth, this rich and decadent chocolate cream pie recipe is a dream come true. Featuring a gluten-free crust and a creamy chocolate filling, this pie is perfect for special occasions or everyday indulgence.

  • 2 cups gluten-free pie crust dough (above)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large egg yolks

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the gluten-free pie crust dough and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. In a large bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, softened butter, and semisweet chocolate chips until smooth. Beat in the vanilla extract and salt until well combined. Beat in the egg yolks until smooth. Pour the filling into the pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

These delectable gluten-free pie recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth without compromising on taste. Whether you’re a classic apple pie fan or a chocolate cream pie devotee, there’s something for everyone in this collection of gluten-free pie recipes. So go ahead, indulge in a slice (or two) and enjoy the sweet and satisfying taste of gluten-free pie!
