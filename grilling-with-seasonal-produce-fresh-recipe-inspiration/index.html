Grilling with Seasonal Produce: Fresh Recipe Inspiration


Grilling season is here, and what better way to celebrate than by incorporating fresh, seasonal produce into your outdoor cooking adventures? The abundance of flavors and textures offered by nature’s bounty can take your grilling game to a whole new level. From juicy fruits to vibrant vegetables, there are endless possibilities to explore. In this blog post, we’ll inspire you with some mouthwatering recipes that highlight the best of the season.

Grilled Watermelon Salad

Watermelon is a summer favorite, and grilling it adds a delightful smoky twist to its refreshing sweetness. To make this grilled watermelon salad, start by slicing a watermelon into thick rounds. Brush the slices with a mixture of olive oil, honey, and lime juice. Place them on a preheated grill for a couple of minutes on each side, just until grill marks appear.

Once grilled, cut the watermelon into cubes and transfer them to a serving bowl. Add crumbled feta cheese, fresh mint leaves, and a sprinkle of sea salt. Toss gently to combine, and your vibrant and flavorful grilled watermelon salad is ready to be enjoyed.

Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Take advantage of the variety of vegetables available during the summer months by creating colorful and delicious grilled vegetable skewers. Start by selecting an assortment of veggies such as bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and mushrooms. Cut them into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto skewers.

To infuse the vegetables with even more flavor, prepare a marinade using olive oil, minced garlic, balsamic vinegar, and a pinch of dried herbs. Brush the marinade onto the skewered vegetables, ensuring they are evenly coated. Grill the skewers over medium heat, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.

Grilled Stone Fruit Dessert

Stone fruits like peaches, plums, and nectarines are at their peak during the summer, and grilling them brings out their natural sweetness and caramelizes their sugars. To make a simple yet impressive grilled stone fruit dessert, start by halving the fruits and removing the pits. Brush the cut sides with a mixture of melted butter and honey.

Place the fruits on the grill, cut side down, and cook for a few minutes until they develop beautiful grill marks. Serve the grilled stone fruits with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and a drizzle of honey. The combination of warm, smoky fruit with cool, creamy ice cream is a match made in heaven.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

No summer grilling session is complete without some corn on the cob. Elevate this classic dish by grilling the corn and slathering it with flavorful herb butter. Start by peeling back the husks of the corn, without removing them completely. Remove the silk and then fold the husks back into place.

Soak the corn in cold water for about 15 minutes before grilling. This will help prevent the husks from burning. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and place the corn directly on the grates. Grill for about 15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn is tender and lightly charred.

While the corn is grilling, prepare the herb butter by combining softened butter, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs like parsley and chives, salt, and pepper. Once the corn is done, carefully peel back the husks and brush the kernels with the herb butter. The result is a deliciously smoky and flavorful grilled corn on the cob.

Grilled Pineapple Salsa

Pineapple brings a tropical twist to any dish, and grilling it takes its sweetness to new heights. For a refreshing and tangy grilled pineapple salsa, start by peeling and slicing a fresh pineapple into rings. Grill the pineapple slices until they develop grill marks on each side.

Chop the grilled pineapple into small pieces and transfer them to a bowl. Add diced red bell pepper, finely chopped red onion, minced jalapeño, chopped cilantro, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Mix everything together, and your vibrant and zesty grilled pineapple salsa is ready to be enjoyed as a topping for grilled meats, tacos, or simply as a dip with tortilla chips.

Wrapping Up

Grilling with seasonal produce is a fantastic way to make the most of nature’s offerings during the summer months. From grilled watermelon salad to flavorful vegetable skewers, these recipes showcase the versatility and deliciousness of fresh, seasonal ingredients. So fire up your grill, embrace the flavors of the season, and enjoy the incredible taste and aroma of grilled dishes that celebrate the best of nature’s harvest. Happy grilling!
