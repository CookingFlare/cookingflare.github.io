Freezer-friendly Feast: How to Make and Store Pizza and Flatbreads for Easy Weeknight Dinners


Who doesn’t love a hot and cheesy pizza or a crispy flatbread straight from the oven? But busy weeks often leave us with minimal time for meal preparations, leading to takeout or, more often than not, a hankering for something fast and convenient. Fear not, as we bring to you a recipe to conquer your weeknight woes – Freezer-friendly Feast: How to Make and Store Pizza and Flatbreads. With this foolproof guide, you’ll be able to whip up a fresh meal any day of the week, even on those hectic evenings.

Why Freeze?

Before we jump right into the nitty-gritty of making and storing these frozen delights, let’s explore the benefits of our freezer-friendly feast:

  1. Prep Ahead: Schedule batch cooking sessions on the weekends, or on those days when you have more time, to save yourself from the cooking conundrum during a busy week.

  2. No Soggy Base: Proper storage technique ensures that your pizza or flatbread retains its tasty crunch even after being defrosted and reheated.

  3. Customize: Since you are preparing your pizzas/flatbreads at home, the possibilities for toppings are endless. Fill your plate with your favorite veggies, meats, and cheeses.

  4. Less Cleanup: A bonus, but with all your food prep done in one go, there’s less clean-up and more time for you to kick back and relax.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

Aside from pizza dough or flatbread bases and your favorite toppings, here’s a list of items you’ll need for your freezer-busting feast:

  • Aluminum foil or parchment paper
  • Plastic wrap
  • Freezable containers or bags (Ziploc offers great, leak-proof options; just remember to label yours!)
  • A large baking sheet or tray
  • A cooling rack

How to Make Your Pizza and Flatbreads

Part 1: Prepping the Base

  1. Preheat your oven: The temperature shouldn’t be too high – 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) will work just fine.

  2. Stretch it out: Stretch your pizza dough or flatbread base to the required size, shaping it on a piece of parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. Consider using a bit of flour to prevent any sticking.

  3. Apply the Sauce: After stretching your base, apply your sauce of choice (tomato sauce, barbecue sauce, pesto, etc.) leaving a small area around its edges to allow for crisping.

Part 2: Laying on the Toppings

  1. Load up on Toppings: Be generous, but don’t go overboard. Heaps of toppings are not just a delight for your taste buds, but also prevent soggy bases after defrosting.

  2. Bake Before Freezing: Bake your pizza or flatbread for 5-10 minutes to partially cook the dough and partially bake the crust before freezing. This ensures that you won’t have to deal with a doughy, undercooked crust, while keeping it still pliable enough for the freeze-and-reheat process.

Part 3: Wrapping and Storing

  1. Cool Before Freezing: Allow your pizza or flatbread to cool down, and then transfer it onto a cooling rack. This will ensure thorough cooling, eliminating any steam inside the packaging – a critical step to prevent a soggy mess after reheating.

  2. Wrap It Up: Wrap your cooled pizzas or flatbreads in a layer of plastic wrap, followed by aluminum foil or parchment paper. This double protection is crucial for preservation.

  3. Store Properly: Transfer your wrapped goodies into airtight containers or freezer bags and remove all air before sealing. Remember those labels? Now’s the time to put them to use!

Part 4: Reheating Your Creation

  1. Defrost: When you’re ready for your meal, move your pizza or flatbread from the freezer to your fridge. Defrost for at least 24 hours, giving ample time for safe consumption.

  2. Reheat: Preheat your oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C), and transfer your pizza or flatbread onto a baking sheet or a new sheet of parchment paper (if you didn’t wrap it in foil initially). Bake for 10-15 minutes or until it’s toasty and bubbly.

Et voila! Your freezer-friendly feast is ready to serve. Gather your friends, family, or Netflix and enjoy the fruits of your labor. Happy cooking and freezing!

Feel free to experiment with different flavor combinations and let your creativity run wild. In no time, you’ll be able to whip up a batch of your favorite pizzas, all ready to go in your freezer for those busy nights. Here’s to making meal prepping a cinch, and to enjoying freshly cooked meals anytime you want!
