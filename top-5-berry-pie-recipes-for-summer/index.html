Top 5 Berry Pie Recipes for Summer


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in light, fruity desserts, and no treat epitomizes the season’s bounty better than berry pies. Packed with fresh and vibrant flavors, these luscious creations are a delightful way to showcase summer’s juiciest offerings. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, celebrating the Fourth of July, or craving something sweet on a weekend afternoon, berry pies are a delicious solution. Below, we’ll explore five mouthwatering berry pie recipes that will elevate your summer dessert game.

1. Classic Mixed Berry Pie

Bring together all the best of summer’s berries in one unforgettable pie. A mixed berry pie combines the sweet and tart essence of strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries, making each bite a true celebration of summer flavors.

Ingredients

  • For the Filling:

    • 1 ½ cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
    • 1 cup fresh blueberries
    • 1 cup fresh raspberries
    • ½ cup fresh blackberries
    • ¾ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For the Crust:

    • 1 sheet premade pie dough or your favorite homemade crust
    • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss all the berries together in a large bowl. Sprinkle in sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract, then mix gently to combine.
  3. Roll out your pie dough and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the crust evenly with the berry mixture.
  4. Add a decorative lattice top or cover with an additional sheet of dough. Brush with egg wash for a golden finish.
  5. Bake for 50–60 minutes or until the filling bubbles and the crust is golden brown.
  6. Cool before serving for best results.

2. Strawberry Basil Pie

This unconventional combination marries the sweetness of strawberries with the aromatic flair of fresh basil, creating a refreshing summertime dessert that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • For the Filling:

    • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon lemon zest

  • For the Crust:

    • 1 sheet premade pie dough or homemade crust
    • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix strawberries with sugar, cornstarch, basil, and lemon zest. Let sit for 10 minutes to allow the strawberries to release their juices.
  3. Roll out the pie dough and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Fill with the strawberry-basil mixture.
  4. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter before sealing with another layer of crust. Crimp the edges to seal, then cut small slits on the top crust for steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 45–55 minutes or until the crust is golden and the filling bubbles. Let the pie cool completely to allow the flavors to meld together.

3. Blueberry Lemon Pie

This zesty, tangy pie combines sweet blueberries with bright lemon notes. The vibrant filling pairs perfectly with a flaky crust, delivering a refreshing bite every time.

Ingredients

  • For the Filling:

    • 5 cups fresh blueberries
    • ¾ cup granulated sugar
    • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
    • Zest of one lemon

  • For the Crust:

    • 1 sheet premade pie dough
    • 1 teaspoon sugar for garnish

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Stir gently to coat the blueberries evenly.
  3. Roll out the pie dough and transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish. Add the blueberry filling, leveling it with a spatula.
  4. Add a lattice crust or a full crust with decorative cutouts. Sprinkle with sugar for extra crunch and sweetness.
  5. Bake for 50–60 minutes or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling. Cool completely before slicing.

4. Raspberry Chocolate Pie

For those who love a decadent twist, raspberry chocolate pie combines rich cocoa tones with the tartness of juicy raspberries. Perfect for chocoholics!

Ingredients

  • For the Filling:

    • 4 cups fresh raspberries
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder

  • For the Chocolate Crust:

    • 1 premade chocolate pie crust or regular crust
    • Melted dark chocolate (optional garnish)

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Stir together raspberries, sugar, cornstarch, and cocoa powder in a medium-sized bowl to make the filling.
  3. Spread the filling evenly in the pie crust.
  4. Bake for 40–50 minutes or until the filling is set and the crust is firm.
  5. For extra indulgence, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the cooled pie before serving.

5. Blackberry Ginger Pie

This pie’s bold flavor profile highlights the juicy sweetness of blackberries while incorporating the warm, spicy notes of fresh ginger—a sophisticated upgrade to the traditional blackberry pie.

Ingredients

  • For the Filling:

    • 4 cups fresh blackberries
    • ¾ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

  • For the Crust:

    • 1 premade pie dough
    • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix blackberries, sugar, cornstarch, ginger, and cinnamon. Stir gently until combined.
  3. Place pie dough into a 9-inch pie dish. Fill with the blackberry mixture.
  4. Add a top crust or keep it simple with an open-faced pie. Brush with egg wash.
  5. Bake for 50–60 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the berry mixture bubbles. Cool completely before serving to allow the ginger’s warmth to shine.

Tips for Baking the Perfect Berry Pie

  • Use Fresh, Ripe Berries: Summer berries are the juiciest and sweetest, so take advantage of what’s fresh and in season.
  • Don’t Skip the Cornstarch: Cornstarch thickens the pie filling beautifully, preventing soggy slices.
  • Cool Before Cutting: Allow the pie to cool for at least two hours to ensure the filling sets properly.

Berry pies capture the essence of summer in every slice, and with these five recipes, you’ll never run out of ideas for turning fresh fruit into unforgettable desserts. Whether you prefer classic combinations or adventurous twists, each recipe offers a delectable way to celebrate the season. Grab your favorite berries and get ready to savor the flavors of summer!
