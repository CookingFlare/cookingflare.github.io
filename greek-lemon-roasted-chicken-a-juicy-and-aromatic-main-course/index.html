Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken: A Juicy and Aromatic Main Course


When it comes to Greek cuisine, one cannot help but be captivated by the vibrant flavors and aromatic herbs that make each dish a culinary masterpiece. Among the many beloved Greek recipes, Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken stands out as a timeless classic. This dish is a harmonious blend of succulent chicken, tangy lemon, and fragrant herbs that will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Greece. In this blog post, we will delve into the secrets of creating a juicy and aromatic Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken that will leave your guests craving for more.

Ingredients

To begin our culinary adventure, let’s gather the ingredients that will bring this delectable dish to life:

  • 1 whole chicken, approximately 4 pounds
  • 2 lemons
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Preparation

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and prepare a roasting pan by lining it with aluminum foil.

  2. Start by preparing the marinade for your chicken. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix them well to create a fragrant and flavorful blend.

  3. Take your whole chicken and rinse it thoroughly under cold water. Pat it dry using paper towels, ensuring that the skin is completely dry. This step is crucial to achieving a crispy and golden skin.

  4. Now, it’s time to infuse your chicken with the flavors of Greece. Cut one of the lemons in half and squeeze the juice over the chicken, ensuring that it is evenly distributed. Take the remaining lemon and slice it into thin rounds.

  5. Carefully loosen the skin of the chicken by gently sliding your fingers between the skin and the meat, being careful not to tear the skin. This will create a pocket for the marinade and allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

  6. Rub the marinade mixture all over the chicken, making sure to coat both the skin and the meat. Don’t forget to rub some of the marinade under the loosened skin as well, ensuring that the flavors reach every bite.

  7. Place the lemon slices under the loosened skin of the chicken, evenly distributing them to infuse each bite with a burst of citrusy goodness.

  8. Drizzle a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil over the chicken, ensuring that it is coated evenly.

  9. Transfer the chicken to the prepared roasting pan and place it in the preheated oven. Roast the chicken for approximately 1 hour and 20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Baste the chicken with the pan juices every 30 minutes to ensure a moist and succulent result.

  10. Once the chicken is cooked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute and ensure a tender and juicy bite.

  11. Serve your Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken with a side of roasted potatoes, a crisp Greek salad, and some warm pita bread. The combination of flavors will transport you to the Mediterranean in an instant.

Conclusion

Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken is a dish that embodies the essence of Greek cuisine. The succulent chicken, infused with the tangy citrus flavors of lemon and the aromatic blend of herbs, creates a symphony of tastes that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Greece, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to the sun-drenched shores of Greece with this juicy and aromatic Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken.
