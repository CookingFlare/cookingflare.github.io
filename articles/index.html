Healthy Low Carb Eggplant Lasagna Recipe

Are you looking for a delicious and healthy twist on traditional lasagna? Look no further! This Healthy Low Carb Eggplant Lasagna Recipe is a perfect alternative for those looking to cut back on carbs without sacrificing flavor. Packed with nutritious ingredients and bursting with savory flavors, this dish is sure to become a new family favorite. Ingredients 2 medium-sized eggplants, sliced lengthwise 1 pound ground turkey or chicken 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 bell pepper, diced 1 cup spinach, chopped 1 can (24 oz) marinara sauce 1 cup ricotta cheese 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 teaspoon dried basil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the eggplant slices on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the eggplant is tender. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the ground turkey or chicken to the skillet and cook until browned. Stir in the diced bell pepper and chopped spinach, cooking until the vegetables are tender. Pour the marinara sauce into the skillet with the meat and vegetable mixture. Add the dried oregano and basil, along with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, half of the shredded mozzarella, and half of the grated Parmesan. To assemble the lasagna, spread a thin layer of the meat and marinara sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Place a layer of roasted eggplant slices on top, followed by a layer of the cheese mixture. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of the meat sauce on top. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the top. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Benefits of Eggplant Lasagna Eggplant is a versatile and nutritious vegetable that serves as an excellent low-carb substitute for traditional lasagna noodles. Rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, eggplant not only adds a unique texture to the dish but also offers numerous health benefits. Additionally, by using lean ground turkey or chicken, this recipe provides a good source of protein while keeping the fat content low. ...
