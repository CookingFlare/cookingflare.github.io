Grilled Shrimp with Spicy Mango Salsa: A Paleo-Approved Summer Recipe


Summer is here, and it’s the perfect time to fire up the grill and indulge in some delicious and refreshing recipes. In this post, we’ll explore a mouth-watering combination that’s sure to become a staple in your summer cooking repertoire: grilled shrimp with spicy mango salsa. This paleo-approved recipe is not only easy to make but also packed with nutrients and flavor.

Grilled Shrimp

To start, we’ll need some fresh and succulent shrimp. For this recipe, we recommend using wild-caught or sustainably farmed shrimp, as they tend to be more flavorful and nutritious. Rinse the shrimp under cold water, pat them dry with a paper towel, and season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, and brush the grates with a little bit of oil to prevent sticking. Place the shrimp on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they’re pink and cooked through. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp, as they can quickly become tough and rubbery.

Spicy Mango Salsa

While the shrimp are grilling, let’s move on to the spicy mango salsa. This sweet and tangy condiment is the perfect accompaniment to the grilled shrimp, and it’s incredibly easy to make. Simply chop up 2 ripe mangos, 1/2 red onion, 1 jalapeño pepper, 1/4 cup of cilantro, and 2 tablespoons of lime juice.

Add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of fresh lime juice to taste, and stir everything together until well combined. The heat level of the salsa can be adjusted to your liking by adding more or less jalapeño pepper. If you prefer a milder salsa, start with half of the pepper and add more to taste.

Assembly and Serving

Once the shrimp are cooked and the salsa is prepared, it’s time to assemble the dish. Place a few grilled shrimp on a plate or platter, spoon some of the spicy mango salsa over the top, and garnish with a sprinkle of cilantro and a lime wedge.

Serve immediately, and enjoy the combination of flavors and textures. This recipe is perfect for a summer dinner party or a quick and easy weeknight meal. The spicy mango salsa adds a burst of flavor and heat, while the grilled shrimp provides a satisfying and protein-packed main course.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added crunch, top the shrimp with some toasted almonds or pumpkin seeds.
  • Substitute the mango with pineapple or papaya for a different flavor profile.
  • Add some diced bell peppers or zucchini to the salsa for extra nutrients and flavor.
  • Use this recipe as a base and add your own favorite toppings or seasonings.

In conclusion, grilled shrimp with spicy mango salsa is a paleo-approved summer recipe that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. With its combination of fresh flavors and spicy heat, this dish is perfect for warm weather gatherings and outdoor cookouts. So fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in a culinary delight that’s sure to satisfy your taste buds and keep you coming back for more.
