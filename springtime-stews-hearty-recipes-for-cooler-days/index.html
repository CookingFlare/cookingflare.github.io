Springtime Stews: Hearty Recipes for Cooler Days


As the weather begins to transition from the cold winter months to the warmer days of spring, there’s still a chill in the air that calls for comforting and hearty meals. What better way to embrace the changing seasons than with a delicious and nourishing springtime stew? In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering stew recipes that are perfect for those cooler spring days.

1. Spring Vegetable Stew

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Cooking time: 45 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add the carrots and celery, and cook for an additional 5 minutes, until slightly softened.
  3. Stir in the peas, asparagus, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Serve hot and enjoy this vibrant and nutritious spring vegetable stew!

2. Lemon Chicken Stew

Preparation time: 20 minutes | Cooking time: 1 hour | Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1.5 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1 cup frozen green beans
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken thighs and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the carrots, celery, corn, green beans, chicken broth, lemon juice, lemon zest, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  4. Serve hot with a side of crusty bread for a satisfying and tangy lemon chicken stew!

3. Beef and Mushroom Stew

Preparation time: 20 minutes | Cooking time: 2 hours | Serves: 8

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds stewing beef, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 pound mushrooms, quartered
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the beef chunks and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the carrots, celery, mushrooms, beef broth, red wine, tomato paste, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  4. Serve hot with mashed potatoes or crusty bread for a rich and satisfying beef and mushroom stew!

Conclusion

Springtime stews are the perfect way to warm up during those cooler days while celebrating the fresh produce that the season brings. Whether you prefer a vibrant vegetable stew, a tangy lemon chicken stew, or a rich beef and mushroom stew, these recipes will surely satisfy your cravings and bring comfort to your table. So grab your favorite pot, gather the ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the heartiness of springtime stews!
