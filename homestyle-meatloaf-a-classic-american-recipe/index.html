Homestyle Meatloaf: A Classic American Recipe


If there’s one dish that embodies the essence of classic American comfort food, it’s the timeless meatloaf. This hearty dish has been a staple in kitchens across the country for generations, and for good reason. Its savory flavors, moist texture, and simple preparation make it a go-to for busy weeknights or cozy family gatherings. In this recipe, I’ll share with you the secrets to creating the perfect homestyle meatloaf that will have your loved ones coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a loaf pan.

  2. Mix the Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, chopped onion, ketchup, eggs, Worcestershire sauce, garlic powder, thyme, oregano, salt, pepper, and milk. Use your hands to gently mix everything together until well combined.

  3. Shape the Meatloaf: Transfer the meat mixture to the prepared loaf pan and shape it into a loaf, making sure it’s evenly packed and smooth on top.

  4. Bake the Meatloaf: Place the loaf pan in the preheated oven and bake for 1 hour, or until the meatloaf is cooked through and the top is nicely browned.

  5. Rest and Serve: Once the meatloaf is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful meatloaf. Slice and serve with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, green beans, or a simple garden salad.

Tips for Success

  • Use Lean Ground Beef: Opt for lean ground beef to prevent the meatloaf from becoming too greasy.
  • Don’t Overmix: Be careful not to overmix the meat mixture, as this can result in a dense and tough meatloaf.
  • Customize the Flavors: Feel free to customize the seasonings to suit your taste. Add a bit of heat with some cayenne pepper or incorporate finely chopped bell peppers for extra texture and flavor.

Why We Love Homestyle Meatloaf

Homestyle meatloaf holds a special place in American cuisine for its simplicity and versatility. It’s a dish that brings people together around the dinner table, evoking feelings of warmth and nostalgia. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting out, mastering the art of the perfect meatloaf is a rewarding experience that will surely become a favorite in your recipe collection.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of classic American comfort, whip up a homestyle meatloaf and savor the timeless flavors that have stood the test of time. From its humble origins to its enduring popularity, this dish is a true testament to the power of good, old-fashioned cooking.

In conclusion, homestyle meatloaf is more than just a recipe; it’s a cherished tradition that has been passed down through the generations. Its simple yet satisfying nature makes it a beloved dish in countless households, and with this foolproof recipe, you can carry on the tradition in your own kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to create a homestyle meatloaf that will have everyone asking for seconds. Happy cooking!
