Stout Stews: Hearty Recipes with Stout Beer Pairings


When it comes to comfort food, nothing beats a warm, hearty stew on a chilly evening. And what better way to elevate the flavors of your stew than by incorporating rich and robust stout beer? Stout stews are not only delicious and satisfying but also bring a depth of flavor that is truly unique. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering stout stew recipes that are perfect for cozy nights in.

Classic Beef and Stout Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs stewing beef, cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 can stout beer
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides.
  2. Add the onions and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the carrots and celery, cooking for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in the stout beer, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Cover and let it simmer on low heat for 2-3 hours until the beef is tender.
  6. Serve hot with crusty bread and enjoy the rich flavors of this classic stew.

Guinness Lamb Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs lamb shoulder, cubed
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can Guinness stout
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss the lamb cubes in flour, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the lamb in batches.
  3. Add the onions and garlic, cooking until softened.
  4. Pour in the Guinness stout, chicken broth, potatoes, and Worcestershire sauce. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 1.5-2 hours until the lamb is tender.
  6. Stir in the peas and cook for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. Serve this hearty Guinness lamb stew with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Vegetarian Stout Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 1 can black beans, drained
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 can stout beer
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add the carrots, chickpeas, black beans, and sweet potato. Cook for a few minutes.
  3. Pour in the stout beer and vegetable broth. Add cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer for 30-40 minutes until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  5. Adjust seasoning as needed and serve this flavorful vegetarian stout stew with a side of crusty bread.

Whether you’re a meat lover or prefer a vegetarian option, stout stews offer a delicious and comforting meal for any occasion. The rich and malty flavors of stout beer add a unique twist to these traditional recipes, making them a must-try for all food enthusiasts. So, grab a can of stout beer and get cooking – your taste buds will thank you!

Remember, the key to a good stew is patience and allowing the flavors to meld together over time. Enjoy the process of slow cooking and savor every spoonful of these hearty stout stew creations. Cheers to good food and good company!
