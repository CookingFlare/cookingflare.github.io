How to Make Homemade Chicken Broth: A Step-by-Step Guide


Chicken broth is a staple in many kitchens, often used as the base for soups, stews, gravies, sauces, and even to enhance the flavor of grains like rice or quinoa. While you can easily buy it from the store, making homemade chicken broth brings an extra layer of depth to your dishes, and it’s surprisingly easy to make. Whether you’re a novice cook or an experienced chef, this guide will walk you through every step, ensuring your homemade chicken broth is packed with flavor and nutrients.

The Benefits of Making Your Own Chicken Broth

Making homemade chicken broth offers numerous benefits. When you make your broth, you control the ingredients, allowing you to adjust for specific tastes or dietary restrictions. Additionally, homemade chicken broth can be more nutrient-dense than store-bought versions since it retains vitamins and minerals from the bones and vegetables used. Homemade broth also tends to be free from preservatives, artificial flavorings, and excess sodium, making it a healthier option.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make a flavorful and rich chicken broth, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds) or chicken parts like wings, necks, or backs. Using a whole chicken adds more flavor and richness, but parts are also great for broths.
  • 2 medium onions, quartered (no need to peel)
  • 2-3 carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 3-4 celery stalks, cut into large chunks
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic, smashed (optional, but it adds depth to the flavor)
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 10-12 peppercorns
  • 1-2 teaspoons of sea salt (adjust to taste)
  • A few sprigs of fresh herbs such as thyme, parsley, and rosemary, or 1-2 teaspoons of dried herbs
  • Water (enough to cover the ingredients, usually 10-12 cups)

Optional Add-ins for Enhanced Flavor:

  • Ginger (a few slices, for a subtle heat)
  • Mushrooms (for earthy undertones)
  • Lemon (a few slices, for a fresh, bright note)
  • Tomatoes (1-2 for a richer, slightly tangy broth)

These ingredients can be adjusted based on personal preference or what you have in your pantry. The beauty of making your own chicken broth is that you can experiment with different flavors and find the perfect combination that suits your tastes.

Equipment You Will Need

Making chicken broth requires a few basic tools, which you likely already have in your kitchen. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • A large stockpot (at least 6-8 quarts in size)
  • A fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth
  • A large spoon for stirring
  • A ladle for skimming foam
  • A large bowl for straining the broth

With these tools, you’ll be able to make the perfect chicken broth with ease.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Make Homemade Chicken Broth

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken and Vegetables

Start by preparing your ingredients. If you’re using a whole chicken, rinse it under cold water and remove any giblets or excess fat from the cavity. If you’re using chicken parts, you can skip this step. Place the chicken or chicken parts in your large stockpot.

Next, roughly chop the vegetables. You don’t need to be precise with the cuts since you’ll be straining the broth later. The goal is to release the flavors, so large chunks are fine. Place the carrots, celery, and onions in the pot, along with the garlic (if using), bay leaves, and peppercorns.

Step 2: Fill the Pot with Water

Pour cold water into the pot until all the ingredients are covered. You’ll typically need about 10-12 cups of water, depending on the size of your chicken and pot. Make sure everything is fully submerged to ensure a rich broth.

Step 3: Bring to a Boil, Then Simmer

Turn the heat to high and bring the water to a boil. As the water heats up, you’ll notice some foam or scum rising to the top. This is natural and is often called “impurities.” Use a ladle or spoon to skim off this foam to help keep the broth clear.

Once the broth begins to boil, reduce the heat to a low simmer. The key here is to simmer, not boil, as boiling will make the broth cloudy and can make the chicken and vegetables break down too much. Cover the pot partially with a lid, leaving a small gap for steam to escape. Let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours for a full-bodied, flavorful broth.

Step 4: Add Fresh Herbs and Salt

About halfway through the simmering process, add your fresh herbs and salt. Herbs like thyme, parsley, and rosemary will infuse the broth with fragrant, earthy notes, while the salt will begin to bring out the natural flavors. Taste your broth periodically and adjust the seasoning as needed, remembering that you can always add more salt later.

Step 5: Skim the Broth Regularly

During the simmering process, make sure to skim the broth every 20-30 minutes. This will help remove impurities and excess fat, resulting in a cleaner and more refined broth. You’ll notice the liquid becoming darker and more flavorful as the chicken and vegetables break down.

Step 6: Strain the Broth

Once the broth has simmered for 1.5-2 hours, it’s time to strain it. Carefully remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside. You can use the meat from the chicken for soups, salads, or sandwiches. Place a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth over a large bowl, and pour the broth through to remove the vegetables and herbs.

If you’d like a more concentrated broth, you can return the strained liquid to the pot and let it simmer for another 30 minutes to reduce. This will intensify the flavors.

Step 7: Let the Broth Cool

Once the broth has been strained and reduced to your liking, allow it to cool. You can speed up this process by letting the broth sit at room temperature for a while before transferring it to the refrigerator.

Step 8: Skim the Fat (Optional)

After the broth has cooled in the refrigerator, you may notice a layer of fat on top. If you prefer a leaner broth, you can easily skim off this fat. Alternatively, leave the fat in for a richer flavor, as it adds depth and smoothness to the broth.

Step 9: Store the Broth

Your homemade chicken broth is now ready to use! You can store it in several ways:

  • In the fridge: Transfer the broth to airtight containers and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days.
  • In the freezer: If you want to store the broth for a longer period, pour it into freezer-safe containers or ice cube trays. Homemade chicken broth can last for 3-4 months in the freezer.

Step 10: Use the Broth in Your Favorite Recipes

Now that you have a batch of homemade chicken broth, the possibilities are endless! Use it as the base for soups, stews, risottos, or gravies. You can also use it to cook grains or add it to sauces for extra flavor. The depth and richness of homemade chicken broth will elevate any dish you make.

Tips for Making the Best Chicken Broth

  1. Use a whole chicken for richer broth: While chicken parts like wings and necks work well, using a whole chicken gives you a more flavorful and richer broth.

  2. Use cold water: Starting with cold water helps to draw out the flavors from the chicken and vegetables slowly, resulting in a clearer and more flavorful broth.

  3. Simmer gently: Keep the heat low and allow the broth to simmer, not boil. Boiling can cause the broth to become cloudy and bitter.

  4. Add vinegar: A splash of apple cider vinegar or white vinegar (1-2 teaspoons) can help draw out minerals from the bones, making the broth even more nutritious.

  5. Flavor variations: Experiment with adding different herbs, vegetables, or spices to create a broth that suits your specific tastes.

  6. Strain well: Make sure to strain the broth thoroughly to remove any solids that could make the broth cloudy or gritty.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade chicken broth is a simple, rewarding process that can bring comfort and flavor to a variety of dishes. By taking the time to make your own broth, you ensure that it’s packed with natural flavors, nutrients, and love. Whether you’re making soup for a cold day, preparing a hearty stew, or simply looking for a flavorful base for a recipe, this homemade chicken broth is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Try it out today and enjoy the difference it makes in your cooking!
