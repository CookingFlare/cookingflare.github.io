From Sweet to Spicy: 6 Marinades for Grilled Seafood


Grilled seafood is a staple of many summer gatherings, and for good reason. The smoky flavor of the grill pairs perfectly with the delicate taste of seafood, and the textures of the different seafood options add a nice variety to the dish. But to take your grilled seafood to the next level, you need to add some flavor. That’s where marinades come in. In this post, we’ll explore six different marinades that will take your grilled seafood from sweet to spicy.

1. Honey and Soy Glaze

This marinade is perfect for those who like a little sweetness in their seafood. The honey and soy glaze adds a rich, caramel-like flavor to the seafood, and the acidity of the soy sauce helps to break down the proteins and tenderize the meat. Combine 1/2 cup of honey with 1/4 cup of soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 cloves of minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger. Place your seafood of choice (shrimp, scallops, or salmon work well) in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

2. Lemon and Herb

For a lighter, brighter flavor, try this lemon and herb marinade. The acidity of the lemon juice helps to cut through the richness of the seafood, while the herbs add a fresh, floral note. Combine 1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice, 1/4 cup of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh dill, 1 clove of minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Place your seafood of choice in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

3. Spicy Mango Salsa

For those who like a little heat in their seafood, this spicy mango salsa marinade is the perfect choice. The sweetness of the mango pairs perfectly with the spiciness of the jalapenos, and the acidity of the lime juice helps to balance out the flavors. Combine 1/2 cup of diced mango, 1/4 cup of diced red onion, 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped, 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lime juice, 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 clove of minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Place your seafood of choice in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

4. Asian-Style Peanut Sauce

This marinade is perfect for those who like a little nuttiness in their seafood. The peanut sauce adds a rich, creamy flavor to the seafood, and the soy sauce and ginger add a savory, umami note. Combine 1/2 cup of creamy peanut butter, 1/4 cup of soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Place your seafood of choice in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

5. Indian-Style Yogurt and Spice

For a creamy, exotic flavor, try this Indian-style yogurt and spice marinade. The yogurt adds a tangy, cooling flavor to the seafood, while the spices add a warm, aromatic note. Combine 1/2 cup of plain yogurt, 1/4 cup of lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of ground cumin, 1 teaspoon of ground coriander, 1/2 teaspoon of ground cayenne pepper, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Place your seafood of choice in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

6. Garlic and Chili Flake

For a bold, savory flavor, try this garlic and chili flake marinade. The garlic adds a pungent, aromatic flavor to the seafood, while the chili flakes add a spicy kick. Combine 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1/4 cup of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of chili flakes, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper. Place your seafood of choice in a zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

In conclusion, these six marinades offer a variety of flavors and options for grilled seafood. Whether you prefer sweet, spicy, or savory, there’s a marinade on this list that’s sure to please. So next time you’re grilling seafood, try one of these marinades and take your dish to the next level.
