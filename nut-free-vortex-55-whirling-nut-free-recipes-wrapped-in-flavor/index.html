1. Coconutty Cauliflower Wings

Do you crave the finger-lickin’, crunchy goodness of chicken wings but follow a plant-based diet? Fear not! Our Coconutty Cauliflower Wings recipe is here to save the day. These zesty delights are coated in a fragrant, nut-free coating that includes a blend of flavorful spices and shredded coconut, offering a tropical twist on this popular dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets
  • 1/4 cup plant-based milk
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup hot sauce of your choice
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste (from a tube)
  • 1/4 cup plant-based butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the cauliflower florets with plant-based milk and cornstarch until evenly coated.
  3. Place the shredded coconut, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, salt, and pepper in a separate shallow dish and mix well.
  4. Dip each cauliflower floret into the mixture, fully coating it in the spices and coconut. Arrange the coated florets on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake the wings for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, mix the hot sauce, maple syrup, tomato paste, and plant-based butter in a saucepan over medium heat until combined and slightly thickened.
  6. Remove the wings from the oven and brush them generously with the spicy sauce. Return them to the oven and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until crispy and golden.
  7. Indulge in a retro-style feast by serving these scrumptious Coconutty Cauliflower Wings with ranch or blue cheese dressing, celery sticks, and carrots.

2. Spectacular Spinach Stuffed Mushrooms

Who can resist the irresistible allure of stuffed mushrooms? The savory, earthy flavors combined with the soft, succulent texture make for an unforgettable gourmet experience. Stuffed mushrooms have never been more appetizing or nutritious, thanks to our Nut-Free Vortex twist.

Ingredients:

  • 24 large white mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup plant-based parmesan cheese (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the mushrooms, stem side up, on the prepared baking sheet.
  3. Drizzle olive oil over the mushrooms and season with salt, pepper, thyme, oregano, and smoked paprika. Bake for 15 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and soft. Add the chopped spinach and sun-dried tomatoes, and continue to cook until the spinach has wilted.
  5. Remove the mushrooms from the oven and fill each cap with the spinach mixture. Sprinkle plant-based parmesan cheese on top.
  6. Return the mushrooms to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and slightly golden.
  7. Ready, set, devour! These Spectacular Spinach Stuffed Mushrooms are the perfect party appetizer or side dish. Serve them with toothpicks for a fanciful touch.

3. Quinoa and Black Bean Stuffed Bell Peppers

Fill flavorful bell peppers with an irresistible quinoa and black bean mixture that’s wholesome and satisfying. This entrée offers a symphony of textures and flavors, courtesy of the Nut-Free Vortex.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 cup dairy-free shredded cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup dairy-free sour cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish with olive oil.
  2. Place the quinoa and vegetable broth in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is fluffy and liquid is absorbed.
  3. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and soft. Stir in corn, black beans, cooked quinoa, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Arrange the bell pepper halves in the prepared baking dish, facing upright. Stuff the cavities generously with the quinoa and black bean mixture, and sprinkle dairy-free shredded cheese on top (optional).
  5. Bake the peppers for 30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted.
  6. Delight in the bounty of flavors and textures with a dollop of dairy-free sour cream and a sprinkling of fresh cilantro.
