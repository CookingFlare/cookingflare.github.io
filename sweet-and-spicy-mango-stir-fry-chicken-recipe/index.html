Sweet and Spicy Mango Stir Fry Chicken Recipe


When it comes to innovative dinner ideas that transform everyday ingredients into a symphony of flavors, this Sweet and Spicy Mango Stir Fry Chicken recipe stands out. Combining tender chicken breast with the natural sweetness of fresh mango, bright vegetables, and a kick of spice, this dish is a festival for the taste buds. It’s quick to prepare, packed with vibrant colors, and balances sweet, savory, and spicy elements beautifully.

Why This Sweet and Spicy Mango Stir Fry is a Must-Try

Chicken stir-fries are a staple in many households because of their versatility and speed. But what elevates this dish is the effective use of mango—not just for sweetness, but also for its juicy, tropical aroma and texture contrast. The interplay of tender chicken, crisp veggies, and luscious mango pieces gives every bite a perfect balance.

This recipe will appeal to those who love experimenting with bold flavors without spending hours in the kitchen. It’s perfect for weeknight dinners, yet impressive enough to serve to guests. Plus, since it’s made in one pan, cleanup is a breeze.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To recreate this vibrant stir fry, gather the following:

  • Chicken Breasts – 1 lb, thinly sliced for quick cooking.
  • Ripe Mango – 1 large, diced. Sweet and juicy varieties like Ataulfo or Tommy Atkins work best.
  • Bell Peppers – 1 red, 1 yellow, sliced into thin strips for color and crunch.
  • Red Onion – 1 small, sliced thinly.
  • Garlic – 3 cloves, minced.
  • Ginger – 1-inch piece, minced or grated.
  • Fresh Red Chili – 1 small, finely chopped (adjust to heat preference).
  • Soy Sauce – 3 tbsp, for umami depth.
  • Honey – 2 tbsp, adds a natural sweetness.
  • Rice Vinegar – 1 tbsp, a splash of tang.
  • Sesame Oil – 1 tsp, for toasty aroma.
  • Vegetable Oil – 2 tbsp, for stir-frying.
  • Green Onions – 2 stalks, sliced for garnish.
  • Sesame Seeds – 1 tbsp, toasted, optional enhancement.
  • Salt and Pepper – to taste.

Step-by-Step Preparation Guide

1. Prep Your Workspace

Organize and prep all ingredients before heating your pan. Slice the chicken thinly against the grain for tenderness. Dice mango into bite-size cubes, ensuring they’re ripe but firm to avoid mushiness during cooking. Slice bell peppers and onions evenly for consistent cooking.

2. Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped red chili. This sweet and spicy blend forms the backbone of your stir fry, so balance the honey and chili to suit your taste buds.

3. Cook the Chicken

Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken, seasoning lightly with salt and pepper. Stir-fry until the chicken is just cooked through—about 4-5 minutes.

4. Stir Fry the Veggies

Push the chicken to one side of the pan. Add a little more oil if needed, then toss in bell peppers and red onion. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until vegetables are just tender but still crisp—this keeps the texture lively.

5. Combine and Caramelize

Pour the prepared sauce over chicken and vegetables. Toss to coat everything evenly. Allow it to cook for another 2 minutes, letting the sauce thicken slightly and form a glossy coat over the mixture.

6. Add the Mango

Gently fold in the mango cubes, stirring carefully to avoid mashing them. Heat through for 1 minute—enough to warm the mango without compromising its juicy texture.

7. Final Touches

Remove from heat. Drizzle sesame oil over the stir fry and garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds for an extra layer of flavor and crunch.

Serving Suggestions

This Sweet and Spicy Mango Stir Fry Chicken pairs beautifully with:

  • Steamed Jasmine Rice – Its delicate aroma complements the bold stir fry.
  • Quinoa or Brown Rice – For a nutritional boost.
  • Noodles – Toss with egg noodles or rice noodles for a different texture experience.
  • Cauliflower Rice – A low-carb option maintaining the dish’s vibrant notes.

Variations to Suit Every Palate

  • Protein Swap: Try shrimp, tofu, or pork instead of chicken. Adapt cooking times accordingly.
  • Veggie Add-Ons: Incorporate snap peas, baby corn, or broccoli florets for additional veggie power.
  • Heat Control: Adjust the amount of chili or substitute fresh chili with chili flakes or even sriracha.
  • Sauce Twist: Use tamari instead of soy sauce for gluten-free or add a splash of orange juice for citrus complexity.

Tips for Perfecting Your Stir Fry

  • Heat is Key: Use high heat when stir-frying to achieve that slight caramelization without steaming the ingredients.
  • Prep in Advance: Since stir-frying is fast, chopping everything beforehand ensures smooth cooking.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Cook in batches if needed, to prevent steaming.
  • Balance Flavors: Taste the sauce before adding it to the pan and tweak sweetness or heat to your liking.
  • Mango Selection: Firm ripe mangoes hold up better during cooking, avoid overly soft or underripe fruits.

Nutritional Highlights

This dish balances lean protein with fresh fruit and vegetables, delivering a dose of vitamins A and C from bell peppers and mango, along with protein’s muscle-repair benefits. Honey and mango provide natural sugars, while the spice from chili can boost metabolism and add antioxidants.

Wrapping Up

Whether you’re craving something vibrant and tropical or looking to experiment with familiar ingredients, this Sweet and Spicy Mango Stir Fry Chicken recipe hits all the right notes. It’s a celebration of color, texture, and flavor that can enliven any dinner table, backed by simple steps and accessible ingredients.

So next time the midweek meal slump hits, whip up this tropical stir fry for a delightful and nourishing escape to a sweet and spicy paradise.

Feel free to share your variations or questions in the comments. Happy cooking!
