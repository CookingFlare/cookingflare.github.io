Vegan Cherry Pie: A Summery Delight


There’s something truly magical about a perfect slice of cherry pie. The sweet, tangy filling nestled inside a flaky, golden crust is the epitome of summertime indulgence. And the best part? This vegan version is just as delicious as the traditional one, but without any animal products. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to add more plant-based options to your diet, this vegan cherry pie recipe is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into the delightful world of vegan baking.

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup vegan butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 6-8 tbsp ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions:

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add the cold, cubed vegan butter to the flour mixture.
  3. Use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough starts to come together.
  5. Divide the dough into two equal parts, shape them into discs, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, gently toss the pitted cherries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, almond extract, cinnamon, and salt until well combined.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of the chilled dough into a circle large enough to line a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish and trim any excess dough from the edges.
  4. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared pie crust.
  5. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim, fold, and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  6. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  7. Optional: brush the top crust with a little non-dairy milk and sprinkle with sugar for a golden, crispy finish.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with a pie shield or strips of aluminum foil.
  3. Once done, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions:

This vegan cherry pie is a delightful treat on its own, but a scoop of dairy-free vanilla ice cream or a dollop of coconut whipped cream takes it to the next level. Enjoy a slice (or two) of this summery delight with your favorite warm beverage or a refreshing glass of iced tea.

Final Thoughts:

With its buttery, flaky crust and luscious cherry filling, this vegan cherry pie is a testament to the fact that plant-based desserts can be just as indulgent and satisfying as their non-vegan counterparts. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, celebrating a special occasion, or simply craving a taste of nostalgia, this pie is sure to impress vegans and non-vegans alike. So, embrace the joy of vegan baking and savor every blissful bite of this summery delight.

So, what are you waiting for? It’s time to channel your inner pastry chef and bring this Vegan Cherry Pie to life in your kitchen. Happy baking!
