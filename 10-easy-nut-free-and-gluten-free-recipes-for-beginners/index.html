10 Easy Nut-Free and Gluten-Free Recipes for Beginners


Whether you’re dealing with food allergies or simply following a specific dietary regimen, finding easy and delicious recipes that are both nut-free and gluten-free can feel like a challenge. But fear not! This post will provide you with 10 simple, tasty, and healthy recipes that require minimal ingredients and effort, making them perfect for beginners in the kitchen. These recipes are all designed to be free from common allergens and are perfect for anyone looking to enjoy flavorful meals without compromising on taste or nutrition.

1. Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

This light and nutritious quinoa salad is perfect for a quick lunch or dinner. Packed with fiber and plant-based protein, it’s a meal that will keep you full and energized without causing any food sensitivities.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, rinse the quinoa under cold water. Add it to the pot with the water or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook for 15 minutes or until the quinoa has absorbed all the liquid.
  3. While the quinoa is cooking, spread the diced bell pepper, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the vegetables evenly.
  4. Roast the vegetables for 20 minutes or until tender and slightly charred.
  5. Once the quinoa and vegetables are ready, combine them in a large bowl. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired and serve warm or cold.

2. Zucchini Noodles with Tomato Basil Sauce

Zucchini noodles (or zoodles) are a fantastic gluten-free alternative to traditional pasta. Paired with a simple tomato basil sauce, this dish is light, refreshing, and bursting with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the spiralized zucchini and cook for 2-3 minutes until tender. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the crushed tomatoes, dried basil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Let the sauce cook for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  3. Return the zucchini noodles to the pan with the tomato basil sauce and toss to coat. Cook for another 1-2 minutes to heat everything through.
  4. Serve the zucchini noodles topped with fresh basil leaves.

3. Chickpea Salad with Lemon Tahini Dressing

This chickpea salad is a filling, protein-packed option for those looking for a satisfying meal without gluten or nuts. The tahini dressing adds a creamy texture, while the lemon gives it a zesty kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and red onion.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, tahini, maple syrup, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve chilled or at room temperature.

4. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

These gluten-free and nut-free tacos are bursting with flavor and packed with nutrients. The combination of roasted sweet potatoes and black beans makes for a filling and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Gluten-free tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Salsa, for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed sweet potatoes with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the sweet potatoes for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are tender and slightly crispy on the edges.
  4. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, warm the black beans in a small pan over low heat.
  5. Once the sweet potatoes are done, assemble the tacos by placing the roasted sweet potatoes and black beans on the gluten-free tortillas. Top with fresh cilantro and salsa if desired.

5. Cauliflower Fried Rice

This cauliflower fried rice is a healthy, low-carb alternative to traditional fried rice. It’s quick, easy, and perfect for meal prep or a last-minute dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, grated or processed into rice-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons tamari or gluten-free soy sauce
  • Green onions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
  2. Push the garlic to the side and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble the eggs until cooked through, then set aside.
  3. Add the frozen peas and carrots to the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes until heated through.
  4. Add the cauliflower rice to the pan, stirring to combine. Cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the cauliflower rice to soften and slightly brown.
  5. Stir in the tamari or soy sauce and the cooked eggs. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve.

6. Baked Chicken Drumsticks with Roasted Potatoes

This simple, comforting dish of baked chicken drumsticks paired with crispy roasted potatoes is a crowd-pleaser that’s naturally gluten-free and nut-free.

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken drumsticks
  • 4 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the chicken drumsticks and cubed potatoes on a baking sheet.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly.
  4. Roast in the oven for 35-40 minutes, flipping the drumsticks and potatoes halfway through, until the chicken is cooked through and the potatoes are golden and crispy.
  5. Garnish with fresh thyme or rosemary before serving.

7. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Rice

This quick vegetable stir-fry with rice is a delicious and versatile dish that can be customized based on whatever vegetables you have on hand. It’s a perfect choice for a busy weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 tablespoons tamari or gluten-free soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the bell pepper, zucchini, broccoli, and snap peas. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  2. Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir to combine with the vegetables.
  3. Stir in the tamari, rice vinegar, and sesame oil, cooking for another 2-3 minutes until the rice is heated through.
  4. Serve the stir-fry warm, garnished with sesame seeds if desired.

8. Spaghetti Squash with Garlic and Olive Oil

Spaghetti squash is a great gluten-free and low-carb alternative to traditional pasta. When tossed with garlic and olive oil, it makes for a light, flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 35-40 minutes, until tender.
  4. Once cooked, use a fork to scrape the flesh of the squash into strands.
  5. In a pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat and sauté the garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  6. Add the spaghetti squash strands to the pan and toss to combine. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

9. Avocado Toast on Gluten-Free Bread

A quick and easy breakfast or snack, this avocado toast is made on gluten-free bread, and it’s packed with healthy fats and nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices gluten-free bread
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the gluten-free bread slices until crispy.
  2. In a small bowl, mash the avocado with lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mashed avocado evenly over the toasted bread.
  4. Sprinkle with red pepper flakes if desired and serve immediately.

10. Coconut Flour Pancakes

These fluffy pancakes are made with coconut flour, making them both gluten-free and nut-free. They’re a great way to start the day, and you can top them with your favorite fruits and syrups.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the coconut flour, baking powder, eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  3. Pour the pancake batter onto the pan in small circles. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  4. Serve warm with your favorite toppings.

These 10 nut-free and gluten-free recipes are perfect for beginners looking to create simple, satisfying meals without worrying about allergens. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or your family, these dishes offer a wide variety of flavors and textures that are sure to please everyone at the table. With minimal ingredients and easy-to-follow instructions, you’ll be able to enjoy delicious, safe meals in no time. Enjoy!
