Delicious Malaysian Laksa: 7 Recipes for a Spicy Kick


Laksa is a beloved dish in Malaysia, renowned for its vibrant flavors, fragrant spices, and comforting warmth. This spicy noodle soup comes in various regional variations, each offering a unique twist on this classic dish. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven delicious Malaysian Laksa recipes that promise to deliver a spicy kick to your palate. From the creamy coconut-based Laksa Lemak to the fiery Asam Laksa, there’s a bowl of Laksa waiting for every spice lover. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s dive into the world of Laksa!

1. Laksa Lemak: The Creamy Coconut Delight

Ingredients:

  • 400g rice noodles
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • 750ml chicken stock
  • 200g cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons laksa paste
  • 2 boiled eggs, halved
  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Lime wedges
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Broth: In a pot, combine chicken stock and laksa paste. Bring to a gentle simmer for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and let it simmer for another 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  4. Assemble the Bowl: Place a portion of noodles in a bowl, pour the Laksa broth over them, and top with shredded chicken, boiled eggs, bean sprouts, and coriander leaves.
  5. Serve: Offer lime wedges on the side for an extra zing.

Flavor Profile:

Laksa Lemak is rich and creamy, with a perfectly balanced sweetness from the coconut milk and a kick from the spices. The combination of tender chicken and fresh herbs makes every spoonful a delight!

2. Asam Laksa: Tangy and Spicy

Ingredients:

  • 400g thick rice noodles
  • 500g mackerel fish
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 750ml water
  • 2 tablespoons Asam Laksa paste
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Sliced red onions
  • Chilli paste to taste
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: Boil mackerel fish in water until cooked. Remove fish, and reserve the broth. Flake the fish and set aside.
  2. Make the Broth: In the reserved broth, add Asam Laksa paste and tamarind paste. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Cook the Noodles: Boil the rice noodles in a separate pot. Drain and place them in serving bowls.
  4. Assemble: Pour the hot broth over the noodles, add flaked fish, and top with cucumber, mint leaves, and red onions.
  5. Serve: Add chilli paste for those who like it hot!

Flavor Profile:

Asam Laksa is a tangy and spicy dish, characterized by the use of tamarind and the richness of mackerel. It’s a refreshing take on Laksa that awakens the senses!

3. Curry Laksa: A Fusion of Flavors

Ingredients:

  • 300g egg noodles
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 150g tofu puffs, sliced
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 750ml chicken stock
  • Bean sprouts
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Lime wedges
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a pot, heat chicken stock and curry powder. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Add Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  3. Cook the Noodles: In a separate pot, cook egg noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Bowl: Place noodles in a bowl, pour the curry broth over, and top with shrimp, tofu puffs, and bean sprouts.
  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil leaves and lime wedges.

Flavor Profile:

Curry Laksa offers a delightful fusion of flavors, combining the aromatic spices of curry with the richness of coconut milk. The addition of shrimp and tofu adds both texture and taste!

4. Penang Laksa: A Hearty Twist

Ingredients:

  • 400g rice vermicelli
  • 500g sardines, canned or fresh
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 750ml water
  • 2 tablespoons Penang Laksa paste
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Fresh mint and coriander
  • Chopped peanuts for garnish
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a pot, combine water, sardines, shrimp paste, and Penang Laksa paste. Bring to a boil and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the Broth: Remove sardines and strain the broth, then return to the pot.
  3. Cook the Noodles: Prepare rice vermicelli according to package instructions.
  4. Assemble: Place vermicelli in bowls, pour the strained broth over, and add sardines back in.
  5. Serve: Top with sliced cucumbers, mint, coriander, and chopped peanuts. Serve with lime wedges.

Flavor Profile:

Penang Laksa is hearty and flavorful, with the richness of sardines complemented by the tanginess of the broth. Each bite is a delightful blend of textures and tastes!

5. Sarawak Laksa: A Unique Spicy Treat

Ingredients:

  • 400g rice noodles
  • 200g shredded chicken
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon Sarawak Laksa paste
  • 750ml chicken stock
  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Chopped spring onions
  • Lime wedges
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Broth: In a pot, combine chicken stock and Sarawak Laksa paste. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Add Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and simmer for another 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  3. Cook the Noodles: Prepare rice noodles as per package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Bowl: Place noodles in a bowl, pour over the hot broth, and top with shredded chicken and bean sprouts.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped spring onions and lime wedges.

Flavor Profile:

Sarawak Laksa is known for its unique blend of spices, offering a delightful balance of creaminess and heat. The shredded chicken adds a heartiness that makes it a complete meal!

6. Vegetarian Laksa: A Plant-Based Delight

Ingredients:

  • 400g rice noodles
  • 200g mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, broccoli)
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon laksa paste
  • 750ml vegetable broth
  • Fresh basil and coriander
  • Lime wedges
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a pot, heat vegetable broth and laksa paste, simmering for 5 minutes.
  2. Add Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and simmer for another 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  3. Cook the Noodles: Prepare rice noodles according to package instructions.
  4. Blanch Vegetables: Quickly blanch mixed vegetables in boiling water, then drain.
  5. Assemble the Bowl: Place noodles in a bowl, pour the broth over, and top with blanched vegetables.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil and coriander, and serve with lime wedges.

Flavor Profile:

This vegetarian Laksa is fresh, light, and packed with flavors. The vibrant vegetables add crunch, while the creamy broth delivers a satisfying warmth.

7. Spicy Seafood Laksa: A Taste of the Ocean

Ingredients:

  • 300g rice noodles
  • 200g prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 200g squid, sliced
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 750ml fish stock
  • 2 tablespoons laksa paste
  • Fresh coriander and lime wedges
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a pot, heat fish stock and laksa paste. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Add Coconut Milk: Stir in coconut milk and let it simmer for another 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  3. Cook the Noodles: Prepare rice noodles according to package instructions.
  4. Cook Seafood: Add prawns and squid to the broth, cooking until they turn opaque.
  5. Assemble the Bowl: Place noodles in bowls, pour the seafood broth over, and garnish with fresh coriander and lime wedges.

Flavor Profile:

Spicy Seafood Laksa captures the essence of the ocean, with tender seafood swimming in a rich and spicy broth. Each bite is a celebration of flavors that transports you straight to the shores of Malaysia!

Conclusion

Malaysian Laksa is a diverse and flavorful dish that can cater to every palate. Whether you prefer the creamy richness of Laksa Lemak, the tangy punch of Asam Laksa, or the hearty goodness of Penang Laksa, there’s a recipe here for everyone. These seven Laksa recipes not only offer a spicy kick but also showcase the rich culinary heritage of Malaysia.

So gather your ingredients, invite some friends over, and prepare for a delightful Laksa feast that’s sure to impress. Happy cooking!
