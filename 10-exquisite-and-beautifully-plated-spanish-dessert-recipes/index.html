10 Exquisite and Beautifully Plated Spanish Dessert Recipes


Spanish desserts are renowned for their rich flavors, vibrant colors, and exquisite presentation. From the classic crema catalana to the innovative fusion desserts, Spanish patisserie has something to offer every palate. In this post, we’ll explore 10 exquisite and beautifully plated Spanish dessert recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

1. Crema Catalana

Crema catalana, also known as Catalan cream, is a classic Spanish dessert that’s similar to crème brûlée. This rich dessert consists of creamy custard base topped with a layer of caramelized sugar. To make crema catalana, you’ll need:

  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup caramelized sugar

Combine the egg yolks, heavy cream, sugar, and salt in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Pour the mixture into individual serving cups and refrigerate until chilled. Just before serving, sprinkle a layer of caramelized sugar on top.

2. Torta de Aceite

Torta de aceite is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with olive oil, flour, and sugar. This moist and flavorful cake is perfect for serving with a cup of coffee or tea. To make torta de aceite, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch (23cm) round cake pan. In a large mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Churros con Chocolate

Churros con chocolate is a classic Spanish dessert that consists of fried dough pastry sticks coated in sugar, served with a rich and creamy chocolate dipping sauce. To make churros con chocolate, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1 cup heavy cream

To make the churros, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and cinnamon in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add the milk, beating until a smooth dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes elastic. Cut the dough into 1-inch (2.5cm) pieces and fry in hot oil until golden brown. Drain the churros on paper towels and coat in sugar. To make the chocolate sauce, melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval. Stir in the heavy cream until smooth. Serve the churros with the chocolate sauce for dipping.

4. Flan de Leche

Flan de leche is a classic Spanish dessert that’s made with caramelized sugar, eggs, and cream. This creamy and smooth dessert is perfect for serving at a dinner party. To make flan de leche, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup caramelized sugar

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease six 1/2-cup ramekins and place them in a large baking dish. In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar and 1/4 cup of water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar dissolves. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 5 minutes, or until the caramel turns a deep amber color. Remove from heat and stir in the heavy cream, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Pour the mixture into the prepared ramekins and smooth the tops. Place the ramekins in the baking dish and add hot water to come halfway up the sides of the ramekins. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the edges are set and the centers are still slightly jiggly. Let the flan cool in the ramekins for 10 minutes before serving.

5. Torta de Tres Reyes

Torta de tres reyes is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with almonds, sugar, and egg whites. This moist and flavorful cake is perfect for serving with a cup of coffee or tea. To make torta de tres reyes, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch (23cm) round cake pan. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, sugar, egg whites, salt, baking powder, cinnamon, and cloves. Beat in the melted butter and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

6. Arroz con Leche

Arroz con leche is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with rice, milk, and sugar. This creamy and comforting dessert is perfect for serving at a family gathering. To make arroz con leche, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Combine the rice, milk, sugar, salt, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture comes to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the mixture is creamy. Remove from heat and stir in the butter and vanilla extract until melted. Serve the arroz con leche warm or at room temperature.

7. Mazapán de Alicante

Mazapán de alicante is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with ground almonds, sugar, and egg whites. This moist and flavorful cake is perfect for serving with a cup of coffee or tea. To make mazapán de alicante, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch (23cm) round cake pan. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, sugar, egg whites, salt, baking powder, cinnamon, and cloves. Beat in the melted butter and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

8. Polvorones

Polvorones are traditional Spanish cookies that are made with ground almonds, sugar, and egg whites. These crispy and flavorful cookies are perfect for serving with a cup of coffee or tea. To make polvorones, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, sugar, egg whites, salt, baking powder, cinnamon, and cloves. Beat in the melted butter and vanilla extract until well combined. Drop the dough by tablespoonfuls onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch (2.5cm) of space between each cookie. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

9. Roscón de Reyes

Roscón de reyes is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with a sweet bread dough, candied fruit, and nuts. This sweet and festive dessert is perfect for serving at a holiday gathering. To make roscón de reyes, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup candied fruit, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Combine the warm water and yeast in a small bowl. Let the mixture sit for 5-7 minutes, or until the yeast is activated. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sugar, flour, and salt. Beat in the melted butter, yeast mixture, candied fruit, nuts, egg, and vanilla extract until a smooth dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Punch down the dough and shape it into a ring. Place the ring on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 30-40 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown. Let the roscón de reyes cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before serving.

10. Crema de Navidad

Crema de navidad is a traditional Spanish dessert that’s made with a creamy custard base, topped with a layer of caramelized sugar. This rich and decadent dessert is perfect for serving at a holiday gathering. To make crema de navidad, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup caramelized sugar

Combine the heavy cream, sugar, egg yolks, vanilla extract, and salt in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and stir in the caramelized sugar until melted. Pour the crema de navidad into individual serving cups and refrigerate until chilled. Serve the crema de navidad chilled, garnished with a sprinkle of caramelized sugar if desired.

These 10 exquisite and beautifully plated Spanish dessert recipes are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth. From the classic crema catalana to the innovative fusion desserts, Spanish patisserie has something to offer every palate. So go ahead, indulge in these delicious and visually stunning desserts, and experience the rich flavors and traditions of Spanish cuisine.
