How to Make Delicious Pumpkin Spice Cookies


When the leaves turn golden and there’s a crisp chill in the air, few things embody the warmth of fall better than the flavors of pumpkin and spice. Pumpkin spice cookies are the ultimate treat for cozy evenings, family gatherings, or just when you’re in need of a little autumnal comfort. These soft, chewy cookies are packed with seasonal spices and a touch of natural sweetness that will have everyone reaching for seconds. If you’re ready to fill your kitchen with the enticing aroma of fall, let’s dive into making the perfect batch of pumpkin spice cookies.

Ingredients for Pumpkin Spice Cookies

Before we start whipping up these fall-inspired treats, it’s important to gather all the ingredients you’ll need. Using the right combination of pantry staples and seasonal ingredients is the key to achieving the perfect flavor and texture. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Cookies:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree (not pumpkin pie filling)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the Glaze (Optional, but Recommended):

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2–3 tablespoons milk (or your milk substitute of choice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of cinnamon (to enhance the glaze flavor)

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Perfect Pumpkin Spice Cookies

1. Prepare for Baking

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining your baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. This step ensures even baking and prevents the cookies from sticking. Then take a moment to measure out all your ingredients—this makes the entire process seamless and stress-free.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, ginger, and salt. This blend of spices is what gives the cookies their iconic pumpkin spice flavor, so make sure to mix everything evenly. Set the bowl aside for later use.

3. Cream the Butter and Sugars

In a large mixing bowl, use an electric mixer or stand mixer to beat the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until the mixture becomes light and creamy. This step is essential for achieving soft and luscious cookies. Depending on the speed of your mixer, this process should take about 2–3 minutes.

4. Add the Wet Ingredients

Once the butter and sugars are creamed, beat in the pumpkin puree, egg, and vanilla extract. These ingredients add moisture to the cookies, resulting in a soft and chewy texture. The mixture may look slightly curdled at this stage, but don’t worry—that’s perfectly normal.

5. Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredient mixture into the wet ingredients. Mix on low speed or gently fold using a spatula to avoid overworking the dough. The final consistency will be thick and slightly sticky. Resist the urge to overmix, as this can lead to dense cookies instead of the desired soft texture.

6. Shape the Dough

Using a small cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop rounded mounds of dough onto your prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Slightly flatten the tops with the back of your spoon for an even bake.

7. Bake to Perfection

Place the cookie sheets in the preheated oven and bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the edges feel firm but the centers remain soft. Be cautious not to overbake, as pumpkin cookies can dry out quickly. Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Optional: Add a Sweet Cinnamon Glaze

If you want to take your pumpkin spice cookies to another level, consider drizzling them with a simple cinnamon glaze. While the cookies cool, prepare the glaze by whisking powdered sugar, milk, vanilla extract, and a pinch of cinnamon until smooth. Start with 2 tablespoons of milk and add more as needed to reach your desired consistency.

Once the cookies are fully cooled, drizzle the glaze over the tops using a spoon or piping bag. Let the glaze set for about 15 minutes before serving. The added sweetness of the glaze perfectly complements the earthy spices in the cookies.

Tips for Baking the Best Pumpkin Spice Cookies

Use Fresh Spices

The quality of your spices plays a crucial role in the final flavor of your cookies. If the spices in your pantry have been sitting there for years, it’s worth investing in fresh ones. This small step will enhance the warm, robust flavors of your cookies.

Choose the Right Pumpkin Puree

Be sure to use canned pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling. Pie filling often contains added sugars and spices, which can throw off the balance of this recipe. If you prefer to make your own puree from fresh pumpkin, make sure to strain it well to remove any excess moisture.

Don’t Skip the Cooling Process

While the cookies smell irresistible straight out of the oven, allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. This step helps them set properly and prevents them from breaking apart when you transfer them.

Adjust Sweetness with the Glaze

If you prefer less sweetness, you can skip the glaze entirely or use it sparingly for a hint of flavor without overwhelming the cookie itself. On the other hand, for a more indulgent dessert, feel free to layer on the glaze generously.

How to Store Pumpkin Spice Cookies

Once completely cooled, store your pumpkin spice cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. If you’d like to keep them fresh longer, you can refrigerate them for up to a week. For long-term storage, freeze undecorated cookies in a sealed bag for up to 3 months. When ready to serve, thaw at room temperature and drizzle on fresh glaze after defrosting.

Perfect Pairings for Pumpkin Spice Cookies

These cookies are a treat on their own, but pairing them with the right beverage or side can elevate the experience. Here are a few ideas:

  • Hot Apple Cider: The warm, spiced flavor of apple cider complements the pumpkin spice profile beautifully.
  • Chai Latte: If you’re a tea lover, the spiced creaminess of a chai latte makes a fantastic companion to these cookies.
  • Vanilla Ice Cream: For a dessert option, try sandwiching two cookies with a scoop of vanilla ice cream in the middle for a fall-inspired ice cream sandwich.

Pumpkin spice cookies are more than just dessert—they’re a celebration of the cozy, nostalgic flavors that make fall so special. Whether you’re baking for a gathering, a festive treat, or just because you want to fill your home with warm, seasonal aromas, these cookies are sure to be a hit.
