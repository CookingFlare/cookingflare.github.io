Easter Recipes for a Beachside Celebration


Easter is a time of celebration, family gatherings, and indulging in delicious food. While many people associate this holiday with indoor feasts, it’s also the perfect opportunity to enjoy the outdoors, especially if you’re lucky enough to celebrate by the beach. A beachside Easter celebration combines the freshness of sea breezes with the joy of sharing a meal under the sun. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of Easter recipes that are perfect for a laid-back beach celebration. From light salads to hearty mains and sweet treats, there’s something for everyone to enjoy by the ocean.

1. Chilled Spring Vegetable Salad

A refreshing salad is a must for any Easter gathering, especially when celebrating by the beach. You’ll want something light and colorful to match the vibe of the beach, and a chilled spring vegetable salad is just the ticket.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced thinly
  • 1 cup of radishes, sliced
  • 1 cup of sweet corn kernels (fresh or thawed from frozen)
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 2 cups of mixed greens (arugula, spinach, and watercress)
  • 1/4 cup of fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of crumbled feta cheese

Dressing:

  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all the vegetables and greens.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper until the dressing emulsifies.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently.
  4. Top with fresh basil and crumbled feta cheese just before serving.

This salad is not only visually appealing, but the freshness of the vegetables pairs perfectly with the salty air of the beach. It’s a great side dish that can easily be enjoyed on a picnic blanket.

2. Lobster Rolls with Lemon Aioli

If you’re by the beach, why not indulge in some local seafood? Lobster rolls are a classic coastal treat and make for a perfect, upscale Easter lunch. The buttery rolls and the rich lobster meat make for a dish that feels both indulgent and refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh lobster meat (steamed or boiled)
  • 4 soft New England-style rolls
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • Lemon wedges, for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the lobster meat with mayonnaise, lemon juice, chives, parsley, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  2. Lightly toast the rolls with a little melted butter on a skillet until golden brown.
  3. Fill each toasted roll with the lobster mixture.
  4. Garnish with additional chives and parsley, and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

These lobster rolls are perfect for enjoying on the sand, offering a mix of luxury and simplicity. The lemon aioli adds a zingy freshness that complements the sweet lobster meat. Pair this dish with a chilled white wine or a refreshing iced tea for the ideal beachside experience.

3. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Grilled chicken is a fantastic addition to any Easter celebration, and when you throw in some fresh herbs and lemon, you get a dish that’s perfect for the outdoors. These chicken skewers are simple to prepare and great for a beachside BBQ or picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and toss to coat. Let it marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours).
  3. Thread the marinated chicken onto the skewers.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and cook the skewers for 4-5 minutes on each side, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred on the outside.
  5. Serve with a side of grilled vegetables or a light dipping sauce.

The charred, smoky flavor from grilling makes these chicken skewers a crowd favorite. Their bright, citrusy notes are perfect for a laid-back beachside meal. Plus, they’re easy to serve and eat, making them ideal for eating by the sea.

4. Spring Berry Tarts

For dessert, you can’t go wrong with a beautiful and delicious spring berry tart. Bursting with fresh berries, these tarts are not only stunning to look at, but they also provide a sweet, tangy contrast to the savory dishes.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into cubes
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the topping:

  • 1/2 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1/2 cup blueberries
  • 1/2 cup raspberries
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Directions:

  1. For the crust, pulse the flour, sugar, and salt together in a food processor. Add the chilled butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add the egg yolk and ice water, pulsing until the dough comes together. Press the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface and fit it into tart pans. Prick the bottoms with a fork and bake for 12-15 minutes, until golden brown. Let cool.
  3. For the filling, whisk together the mascarpone cheese, heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Once the tart crusts have cooled, spread the mascarpone filling evenly into each crust.
  5. Arrange the fresh berries on top of the filling and garnish with mint leaves.

These spring berry tarts are a wonderful way to end your beachside Easter meal. The sweet mascarpone filling paired with the fresh fruit provides the perfect balance of flavors. Plus, they’re light and refreshing – just what you need after a day spent in the sun.

5. Tropical Fruit Punch

No beachside celebration is complete without a refreshing drink to cool you off, and a tropical fruit punch is the perfect beverage for the occasion. This vibrant punch is made with fresh fruit juices, making it both hydrating and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pineapple juice
  • 2 cups orange juice
  • 1 cup coconut water
  • 1/2 cup lime juice
  • 1/4 cup grenadine
  • Fresh fruit for garnish (pineapple slices, orange slices, cherries)

Directions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the pineapple juice, orange juice, coconut water, lime juice, and grenadine. Stir well to combine.
  2. Fill glasses with ice and pour the punch over.
  3. Garnish with fresh fruit slices and a cherry on top.

This tropical fruit punch is as vibrant as it is refreshing. The combination of pineapple, orange, and coconut flavors offers a taste of paradise, and the bright, tropical colors will have you feeling like you’re on vacation. This is a drink that both kids and adults will love.

Conclusion

An Easter celebration by the beach offers a unique opportunity to enjoy both delicious food and the great outdoors. With these recipes, you’ll have a variety of dishes that are not only delicious but also perfect for a laid-back, beachy atmosphere. Whether you’re savoring lobster rolls, digging into grilled chicken skewers, or enjoying a slice of berry tart, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So pack up your picnic basket, grab your beach towel, and get ready for an unforgettable Easter by the sea.
