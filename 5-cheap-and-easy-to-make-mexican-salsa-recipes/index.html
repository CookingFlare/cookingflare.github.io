5 Cheap and Easy-to-Make Mexican Salsa Recipes


Salsas are a staple in Mexican cuisine, adding a burst of flavor and spice to any dish. Whether you’re hosting a party, looking for a quick snack, or simply want to elevate your meals, these 5 cheap and easy-to-make Mexican salsa recipes are a must-try. From tangy to fiery, there’s a salsa for every taste bud. So, grab your ingredients, put on your apron, and let’s get started!

1. Classic Tomato Salsa

This classic tomato salsa is a crowd pleaser. It’s vibrant, tangy, and pairs well with tacos, nachos, or simply as a dip for tortilla chips. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

In a bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, onion, jalapeno pepper, garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it sit for at least 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Just before serving, sprinkle with fresh cilantro. Enjoy!

2. Spicy Mango Salsa

This spicy mango salsa adds a tropical twist to your Mexican dishes. It’s sweet, tangy, and has a kick of heat. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

In a bowl, combine the diced mangoes, red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeno pepper, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it sit for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to develop. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro before serving. It’s a perfect accompaniment to grilled chicken or fish.

3. Roasted Tomatillo Salsa

Roasted tomatillos give this salsa a smoky and slightly tangy flavor. It’s great for enchiladas, tacos, or as a topping for grilled meats. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 6 tomatillos, husked and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the tomatillos, onion, garlic, and jalapeno pepper on a baking sheet. Roast for about 15-20 minutes until they are slightly charred. Let them cool before transferring to a blender or food processor. Add the lime juice, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth. Stir in the fresh cilantro. It’s ready to be enjoyed!

4. Creamy Avocado Salsa

This creamy avocado salsa is rich, smooth, and absolutely delicious. It’s perfect for dipping, spreading on sandwiches, or as a topping for grilled meats. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons of sour cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

In a blender or food processor, combine the avocados, jalapeno pepper, onion, lime juice, sour cream, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy. Stir in the fresh cilantro. It’s ready to be enjoyed! Keep it refrigerated until serving to maintain its freshness.

5. Pico de Gallo Salsa

Pico de Gallo is a fresh and vibrant salsa that adds a burst of flavor to any dish. It’s simple, yet incredibly delicious. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

In a bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, onion, jalapeno pepper, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it sit for at least 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro before serving. It’s a versatile salsa that complements tacos, grilled meats, or can be enjoyed as a refreshing side dish.

There you have it - 5 cheap and easy-to-make Mexican salsa recipes to spice up your meals. Whether you prefer the classic tomato salsa or want to try something different with the creamy avocado salsa, these recipes are sure to impress. So, grab your ingredients, get chopping, and let the salsa party begin! Enjoy!
