You Had Me at Mole: A Valentine's Day Chocolate Mole Recipe


Valentine’s Day is the perfect occasion to indulge in a little culinary romance. And what better way to spice things up than with a tantalizing and unconventional recipe? Forget the traditional Valentine’s Day desserts; today, we’re going to take a journey to Mexico and explore the magical world of mole. But not just any mole – a chocolate mole that will leave you and your special someone utterly enchanted.

The Mystery of Mole

Mole is a traditional Mexican sauce that has been passed down through generations. It’s a complex blend of flavors and aromas that combines savory, sweet, and spicy elements in perfect harmony. While mole can be made in various ways, the addition of chocolate adds a unique twist that takes it to a whole new level of decadence.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried mulato chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried pasilla chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 2 stale corn tortillas, torn into pieces
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons smooth peanut butter
  • 2 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent.

  2. While the onion and garlic are cooking, place the dried chilies in a bowl of hot water and let them soak for about 15 minutes until they become soft and pliable.

  3. Remove the hydrated chilies from the water and transfer them to a blender or food processor. Add the softened onion and garlic, along with the ground cinnamon, cumin, coriander, dried oregano, raisins, almonds, sesame seeds, and torn corn tortillas. Blend until you have a smooth paste, adding a splash of the soaking liquid if needed.

  4. Return the skillet to the heat and pour in the chili paste. Cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly, to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Slowly pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, stirring continuously to incorporate it into the sauce. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to deepen and the sauce to thicken slightly.

  6. Add the smooth peanut butter and chopped dark chocolate to the skillet, stirring until they melt and blend into the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the flavors as needed.

  7. Simmer the mole for an additional 10 minutes, allowing all the ingredients to come together harmoniously.

Serving Suggestions:

Serve your delectable chocolate mole over grilled chicken, turkey, or even roasted vegetables. The sauce’s rich, velvety texture and robust flavors will elevate any dish to new heights of culinary bliss. Accompany it with a side of fluffy rice and warm tortillas to soak up every last drop of this tantalizing sauce.

The Perfect Valentine’s Day Surprise

This Valentine’s Day, surprise your loved one with a dish unlike any other. The chocolate mole’s exotic flavors and seductive aroma will ignite the senses and create a truly unforgettable dining experience. It’s a testament to the power of love and the joy of exploring new culinary horizons.

So, light some candles, set the mood, and let this delicious chocolate mole be the centerpiece of your Valentine’s Day celebration. The journey to Mexico awaits, and with every bite, you’ll find yourself falling deeper in love – with the flavors, the experience, and most importantly, with each other.

Remember, love is a celebration, and what better way to celebrate than with a dish that will leave you saying, “You had me at mole.”

Enjoy!
