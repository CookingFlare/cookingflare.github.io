10 Easy Instant Pot Recipes for Beginners


The Instant Pot has become a kitchen essential for many, offering an incredible amount of convenience and versatility. Whether you’re a beginner or just looking for new ideas to make meal prep faster and easier, the Instant Pot can significantly reduce cooking times while enhancing flavors. This post will guide you through 10 easy Instant Pot recipes that are perfect for beginners. From hearty stews to creamy desserts, these recipes will make you feel like a kitchen pro in no time.

1. Instant Pot Chicken Alfredo

If you’re craving comfort food but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen, Chicken Alfredo made in the Instant Pot is the way to go. This creamy, cheesy dish can be prepared in just 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth
  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and heat the olive oil. Add the chicken breasts and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side until browned.
  2. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside. Add minced garlic to the pot and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and heavy cream, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the pot.
  4. Break the fettuccine in half and add it to the pot, ensuring the pasta is submerged in the liquid.
  5. Place the chicken back on top of the pasta and close the Instant Pot lid. Set it to “Pressure Cook” on high for 7 minutes.
  6. Once done, carefully release the pressure and shred the chicken with two forks.
  7. Stir in Parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

2. Instant Pot Beef Stew

Beef stew is a classic comfort dish, and making it in the Instant Pot speeds up the process while keeping all the flavors intact. This beef stew recipe is perfect for beginners because it’s simple and requires minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 potatoes, diced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and add the beef chunks, cooking until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the onions and garlic powder, sautéing for another 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, dried thyme, and bay leaf.
  4. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  5. Add the carrots and potatoes to the pot. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 35 minutes.
  6. Once the cooking time is up, let the pressure release naturally for about 10 minutes, then quick-release the remaining pressure.
  7. Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot.

3. Instant Pot Rice Pudding

Instant Pot rice pudding is a sweet treat that’s perfect for dessert or breakfast. The Instant Pot makes it quick and easy to prepare, and the result is a creamy, comforting dish that the whole family will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups milk (whole milk or almond milk)
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt
  • Raisins (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Add the rice, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon stick, and salt to the Instant Pot.
  2. Stir everything together, making sure the rice is submerged in the milk.
  3. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 10 minutes.
  4. Once done, let the pressure release naturally for about 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  5. Stir in the ground cinnamon and raisins (if using).
  6. Serve warm or chilled. Garnish with additional cinnamon or fruit, if desired.

4. Instant Pot Pulled Pork

Pulled pork is a crowd-pleasing dish that’s full of flavor and perfect for feeding a family or hosting a gathering. The Instant Pot cuts down on the usual cooking time for pulled pork while ensuring tender, juicy meat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • BBQ sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and add the pork chunks. Brown the pork on all sides, then remove from the pot.
  2. Add the onions and garlic to the pot, sautéing for 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the apple cider vinegar, chicken broth, brown sugar, smoked paprika, and cumin.
  4. Add the pork back to the pot, ensuring it is submerged in the liquid.
  5. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 60 minutes.
  6. Once done, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release the remaining pressure.
  7. Shred the pork with two forks and stir in BBQ sauce, if desired.
  8. Serve on buns, with coleslaw, or on its own.

5. Instant Pot Spaghetti

A one-pot meal that’s perfect for a busy weeknight, Instant Pot spaghetti is easy, quick, and full of flavor. Plus, it’s a dish that can be customized to your taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar marinara sauce
  • 4 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 12 oz spaghetti pasta
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and brown the ground meat. Add the chopped onions and garlic and cook for another 2 minutes until softened.
  2. Stir in the marinara sauce and broth, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the pot.
  3. Break the spaghetti in half and add it to the pot. Press the pasta down so it’s submerged in the liquid.
  4. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 8 minutes.
  5. Once done, carefully quick-release the pressure.
  6. Stir the pasta and meat mixture, season with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh basil if desired.

6. Instant Pot Chicken and Rice

Chicken and rice is a staple comfort meal that’s simple to make and always satisfying. With the Instant Pot, this dish comes together quickly, and the chicken is incredibly tender.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and brown the chicken thighs on both sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add the chopped onions and garlic to the pot, sautéing for 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the rice, paprika, and chicken broth, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the pot.
  4. Place the chicken thighs on top of the rice. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 12 minutes.
  5. Once done, let the pressure release naturally for 5 minutes, then quick-release the remaining pressure.
  6. Add frozen peas if desired, stir, and serve.

7. Instant Pot Lentil Soup

Lentil soup is a hearty, nutritious, and budget-friendly meal that can be easily made in the Instant Pot. This recipe is perfect for beginners because it’s low-maintenance but packs a ton of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups dried lentils
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and cook the onions, carrots, and celery for about 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the garlic, cumin, and turmeric, cooking for another minute.
  3. Stir in the lentils, vegetable broth, and diced tomatoes.
  4. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 20 minutes.
  5. Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

8. Instant Pot Hard Boiled Eggs

Hard-boiled eggs are an easy snack, and the Instant Pot makes them even easier to prepare. This method results in eggs with perfect yolks every time.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Place the trivet in the Instant Pot and add the water.
  2. Gently place the eggs on the trivet.
  3. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 5 minutes.
  4. Once done, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes before transferring the eggs to an ice bath to cool.
  5. Peel the eggs and enjoy!

9. Instant Pot Chicken Tacos

Chicken tacos are a quick and easy meal, and the Instant Pot makes them even simpler to prepare. With tender shredded chicken and minimal ingredients, you can have a delicious dinner ready in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the chicken breasts, taco seasoning, and chicken broth to the Instant Pot.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 15 minutes.
  3. Once done, release the pressure and shred the chicken with two forks.
  4. Serve the chicken in taco shells with your favorite toppings.

10. Instant Pot Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal

Starting your day with a warm bowl of oatmeal is comforting and nutritious. The Instant Pot makes preparing oatmeal so easy, and the addition of apples and cinnamon takes it to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup steel-cut oats
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 apple, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Add the oats, water, apple, cinnamon, and vanilla extract to the Instant Pot.
  2. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 4 minutes.
  3. Once the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in brown sugar if desired and serve warm.

The Instant Pot is a powerful kitchen tool that can transform the way you cook. These 10 easy recipes for beginners are just the beginning of what you can achieve with this versatile appliance. Whether you’re craving comfort food, a quick snack, or a sweet treat, there’s a recipe here for every taste. Happy cooking!
