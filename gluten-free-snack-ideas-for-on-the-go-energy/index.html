Gluten-Free Snack Ideas for On-the-Go Energy


Are you tired of the usual snacks that provide a quick boost of energy but leave you feeling guilty about getting? Do you need a break from the endless trail mixes and granola bars? If you’re searching for delicious, gluten-free snacks that will keep you energized throughout your day, look no further! We have gathered three fantastic snack ideas that are perfect for when you’re on-the-go.

1. Homemade Energy Balls

Who needs Rolos and Snackwells when you can make these bite-sized treats at home? You’ll need:

  • 1 cup rolled oats (use gluten-free oats if needed)
  • 1/2 cup sunflower seed butter (such as sunbutter)
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup mixed seeds (such as pumpkin, sunflower, and chia seeds)

Instructions:

Start by combining rolled oats, sunflower seed butter, honey, and applesauce in a medium bowl. Mix thoroughly. Once well combined, add the cinnamon, shredded coconut, and mixed seeds. Mix again until everything is evenly distributed.

Using your hands, roll rounded tablespoonfuls of the mixture into balls. If the mixture is too sticky, refrigerate it for 20-30 minutes before rolling. Place the balls on a plate and refrigerate for at least an hour until firm.

When you’re ready to snack, grab an energy ball and enjoy! Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

2. Roasted Chickpeos

Roasted chickpeos are a twist on classic popcorn, and they’re much healthier! You’ll need:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C). Dry the chickpeas thoroughly by spreading them on a clean dish towel or a few layers of paper towels. Once completely dry, toss them in a bowl with olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and kosher salt.

Spread the chickpeas evenly on a baking sheet and roast in your preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until golden and slightly crispy. Make sure to stir them halfway through the roasting process to ensure even crispiness.

For an extra boost of flavor, sprinkle roasted chickpeos with additional spices like cayenne pepper, curry powder, or chili powder. Store the leftover chickpeos in an airtight container for up to five days.

3. Seaweed Snack Sheets

No, we’re not talking about the kind you find in aitty boats at the bottom of a tide pool. These seaweed snack sheets are specially cultivated for snacking and come in a variety of flavors, including pink Himalayan salt, soy sauce, and wasabi. If you’re unfamiliar with edible seaweed snacks, like nori sheets, they’re a satisfying, crunchy, and nutrient-dense bite that’s a fun twist on potato chips.

So next time you’re looking for a grab-and-go snack, consider one of these gluten-free treats. Be sure to keep little ones handy as well since they can also reap the delicious health benefits! Whether it’s a quick break from work or an energy boost mid-workout, these snacks will keep you going.
