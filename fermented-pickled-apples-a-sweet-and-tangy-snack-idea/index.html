Fermented Pickled Apples: A Sweet and Tangy Snack Idea


Fermented pickled apples are a unique and delicious snack that combines the sweetness of apples with the tanginess of fermentation. This recipe is perfect for those who love the combination of sweet and sour flavors. The process of fermentation also adds a host of health benefits to the apples, making them a nutritious and tasty snack.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 apples, sliced into 1/4 inch thick wedges
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup fermented brine (see note)

Note on Fermented Brine

For this recipe, you will need a fermented brine that has been made with a combination of water, salt, and sugar. The brine should be fermented for at least 24 hours before using it in this recipe. You can make your own fermented brine by combining 1 cup of water, 1/2 cup of salt, and 1/4 cup of sugar in a jar. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours. After 24 hours, strain the brine through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean jar. Discard the solids and use the brine in this recipe.

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, water, white vinegar, sugar, salt, black pepper, cinnamon, and ginger. Mix well to combine.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours. This will allow the apples to soften and the flavors to meld together.
  3. After 24 hours, stir the mixture and add the fermented brine. Mix well to combine.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid. Press the apples down to remove any air pockets and make sure they are submerged in the liquid.
  5. Cover the jar with a lid and let it sit at room temperature for 7-10 days. This will allow the apples to ferment and become tangy.
  6. After 7-10 days, check the apples for fermentation by tasting them. If they are still too sweet, let them ferment for a few more days. If they are too tangy, you can stop the fermentation process by transferring the apples to the refrigerator.
  7. Once the apples have fermented to your liking, transfer them to the refrigerator to chill. They will keep for several weeks in the refrigerator.

Tips and Variations

  • Use a variety of apples that hold their shape well, such as Granny Smith or Fuji.
  • Add a few cloves of garlic to the mixture for an extra boost of flavor.
  • Use a different type of vinegar, such as apple cider vinegar or balsamic vinegar, for a different flavor profile.
  • Add a few sprigs of fresh herbs, such as thyme or rosemary, to the mixture for extra flavor.
  • Experiment with different spices, such as nutmeg or allspice, for a different flavor profile.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled apples are a unique and delicious snack that combines the sweetness of apples with the tanginess of fermentation. This recipe is perfect for those who love the combination of sweet and sour flavors and are looking for a healthy and tasty snack option. With its tangy flavor and crunchy texture, this snack is sure to become a favorite.
