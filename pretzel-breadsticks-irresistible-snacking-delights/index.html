Pretzel Breadsticks: Irresistible Snacking Delights


There’s something about the combination of soft, warm bread and the savory tang of pretzels that makes pretzel breadsticks an irresistible snacking option. Whether you’re hosting a party, looking for a delicious appetizer, or just craving a tasty snack, pretzel breadsticks are a versatile and crowd-pleasing choice. In this blog post, we’ll take you through a step-by-step guide to creating your own homemade pretzel breadsticks that will have everyone coming back for more.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) instant yeast
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted

For the Pretzel Coating:

  • ½ cup baking soda
  • 9 cups water
  • Coarse salt for sprinkling

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Add the flour, salt, and melted butter to the yeast mixture. Mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Shape the Breadsticks

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into equal portions. Roll each portion into a long, thin breadstick shape and place them on the prepared baking sheet.

Step 3: Pretzel Coating

  1. In a large pot, bring the 9 cups of water to a boil. Once boiling, add the baking soda (be careful, it will fizz up).
  2. Gently place the breadsticks, a few at a time, into the boiling water for about 30 seconds. Remove them with a slotted spoon and place them back on the baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle the breadsticks with coarse salt while they are still wet from the water.

Step 4: Bake and Enjoy

  1. Bake the pretzel breadsticks in the preheated oven for 12-14 minutes or until they are golden brown.
  2. Remove from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These pretzel breadsticks are delicious on their own, but you can take them to the next level by serving them with a variety of dipping sauces such as mustard, cheese sauce, or marinara. They also make a great accompaniment to soups, salads, or charcuterie boards.

Tips for Success

  • Make sure the water is boiling before adding the baking soda for the pretzel coating.
  • Don’t skip the step of sprinkling the breadsticks with coarse salt, as it adds that classic pretzel flavor.
  • For a twist, try brushing the breadsticks with garlic butter or adding different seasonings to the dough before baking.

Final Thoughts

Pretzel breadsticks are a delightful and versatile snack that will please any crowd. By following this simple recipe, you can bring the irresistible flavors of soft pretzels into your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply enjoying a cozy night in, these homemade pretzel breadsticks are sure to be a hit. So, roll up your sleeves, get baking, and treat yourself to these irresistible snacking delights!
