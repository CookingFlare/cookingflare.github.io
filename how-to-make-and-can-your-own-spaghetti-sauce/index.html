How to Make and Can Your Own Spaghetti Sauce


Making homemade spaghetti sauce is a rewarding and satisfying process that transforms simple ingredients into a rich, flavorful sauce that can elevate any pasta dish. It’s a wonderful way to use up ripe tomatoes in the summer or preserve the essence of fresh, in-season produce for year-round enjoyment. Canning your spaghetti sauce allows you to stock up on this culinary staple and enjoy it at your convenience, whether you’re preparing a quick weeknight meal or hosting a dinner party.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to make your own homemade spaghetti sauce from scratch and provide you with the necessary information to safely can it so that you can enjoy it all year long.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Spaghetti Sauce

To start, gather the following ingredients:

Base Ingredients:

  • Roma tomatoes (also known as plum tomatoes) – These are the best choice for spaghetti sauce due to their low moisture content and rich flavor. You’ll need about 10-12 pounds to yield approximately 4 quarts of sauce.
  • Olive oil – 2-3 tablespoons for sautéing.
  • Garlic – 6-8 cloves, minced. Fresh garlic gives the sauce a deep and aromatic flavor.
  • Onions – 2 medium-sized, finely chopped. Yellow or white onions work best for this sauce.
  • Carrots – 2 medium-sized, peeled and finely chopped. Carrots add a touch of sweetness that balances the acidity of tomatoes.
  • Celery – 2 stalks, finely chopped. Celery provides depth to the flavor.
  • Tomato paste – 1 small can (about 6 ounces). This ingredient helps to thicken the sauce and intensify the tomato flavor.
  • Crushed tomatoes – 1 large can (28 ounces). These tomatoes serve as a base to create a smooth texture.
  • Herbs – A mix of fresh or dried oregano, basil, and thyme. Fresh herbs provide a vibrant flavor, but dried herbs work well too.
  • Bay leaves – 2 leaves to infuse the sauce with subtle earthy notes.
  • Salt – To taste. You’ll need to add salt to bring out the flavors of the sauce.
  • Sugar – A teaspoon or two to balance acidity.
  • Black pepper – To taste.

Optional Additions:

  • Red wine – A splash of red wine can add depth and richness to the sauce.
  • Red pepper flakes – For those who prefer a spicy kick, red pepper flakes add a nice heat to the sauce.
  • Parmesan cheese – Freshly grated cheese can be stirred into the sauce for an extra burst of flavor.

How to Make Your Homemade Spaghetti Sauce

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

To make your spaghetti sauce, start by preparing the tomatoes. Since we’re using Roma tomatoes, which have a thicker skin, you’ll want to peel them first.

  1. Score the tomatoes: Using a sharp knife, make a small “X” incision on the bottom of each tomato. This will make it easier to peel the skin off after blanching.

  2. Blanch the tomatoes: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Once boiling, gently drop the tomatoes in and let them cook for 30 seconds or until the skins start to peel away. Remove them quickly with a slotted spoon and place them in a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.

  3. Peel and chop: Once the tomatoes are cool enough to handle, peel off the skins. Cut them in half and remove the seeds. Chop the flesh into small chunks and set aside. Reserve the juice as well, as it will be used in the sauce.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large saucepan or stockpot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions, carrots, and celery to the pot. Sauté them for about 8-10 minutes, or until they soften and develop a golden color. This step adds sweetness and complexity to the flavor of your spaghetti sauce.

Next, add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent the garlic from burning. The garlic will release its aroma and infuse the sauce with its rich flavor.

Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Tomato Paste

Now it’s time to incorporate the tomatoes into the sauce. Add the prepared fresh Roma tomatoes, crushed tomatoes, and tomato paste into the pot. Stir everything together to combine the ingredients and create a uniform base for the sauce. The tomato paste will help thicken the sauce and concentrate the tomato flavor, while the crushed tomatoes provide a smooth, chunky texture.

Step 4: Season the Sauce

Season the sauce with the following ingredients:

  • Herbs: Add a tablespoon of dried basil, oregano, and thyme, or a few sprigs of fresh herbs.
  • Bay leaves: Toss in two bay leaves to infuse the sauce with subtle depth.
  • Salt and pepper: Add salt to taste, being mindful not to overdo it. Freshly ground black pepper adds a gentle heat.
  • Sugar: Add a teaspoon of sugar to help balance the acidity of the tomatoes.

At this point, if you like, you can also add a splash of red wine and a pinch of red pepper flakes for extra flavor. Stir everything together and bring the sauce to a simmer.

Step 5: Simmer the Sauce

Let the sauce simmer on low heat for at least 45 minutes, preferably up to two hours if you have time. The longer it simmers, the more the flavors will develop and meld together. Stir occasionally to prevent the sauce from sticking to the bottom of the pot. You’ll know it’s ready when the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency and the flavors are rich and well-balanced.

Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Add more salt, sugar, or herbs depending on your preferences.

Step 6: Blend (Optional)

If you prefer a smoother spaghetti sauce, you can blend it using an immersion blender or transfer it to a blender in batches. Blending the sauce will give it a silky texture that’s perfect for those who enjoy a creamy sauce. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can skip this step.

Step 7: Prepare for Canning

Now that your homemade spaghetti sauce is ready, it’s time to prepare it for canning. Ensure that your canning jars and lids are clean and sterilized before use. To sterilize the jars, place them in a pot of boiling water for 10 minutes and then let them air dry.

Once your jars are ready, use a ladle to pour the hot spaghetti sauce into each jar, leaving about 1-inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to ensure there’s no residue, then seal the jars with sterilized lids.

Step 8: Process the Jars

To safely preserve the spaghetti sauce, you’ll need to process the jars in a water bath canner. Fill a large pot or canner with enough water to cover the jars by at least 1-2 inches. Bring the water to a boil, then carefully place the jars into the canner using a jar lifter. Process the jars for 35-40 minutes to ensure they are sealed and safe for long-term storage.

After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and let them cool on a clean towel or wire rack. You’ll hear a satisfying “pop” as the lids seal, indicating that the canning process was successful.

Once the jars are completely cool, check the seals. If the center of the lid is concave and doesn’t move when pressed, the jar is sealed properly. Store the sealed jars in a cool, dark place for up to one year.

Tips for Making and Canning Spaghetti Sauce

  • Tomato Variety: While Roma tomatoes are ideal for sauce due to their low moisture content, you can use other tomato varieties like San Marzano or even a mix of different types for unique flavors.
  • Herb Substitutes: Fresh herbs can add a more vibrant flavor, but dried herbs are easier to store and handle. Adjust the quantities based on whether you’re using fresh or dried herbs.
  • Acidity Adjustment: Tomatoes are naturally acidic, which is why it’s important to add sugar to balance the flavors. If you find the sauce too acidic, add a little extra sugar or even a teaspoon of baking soda to neutralize the acidity.
  • Canning Safety: Always ensure your jars and lids are sterilized, and follow proper canning procedures to avoid the risk of botulism or other foodborne illnesses. The acidity of the spaghetti sauce is critical for safe canning, so make sure to follow the correct ratios of tomatoes, vinegar, and seasoning.

Conclusion

Making and canning your own spaghetti sauce is not only a great way to preserve the taste of summer tomatoes, but it also allows you to control the ingredients and flavors in your sauce. By following the steps in this guide, you’ll have homemade, flavorful spaghetti sauce that you can use for months to come. Whether you’re tossing it with pasta, using it as a base for other recipes, or gifting it to friends and family, homemade spaghetti sauce is a versatile and delicious pantry staple that adds depth to any meal. Happy canning!
