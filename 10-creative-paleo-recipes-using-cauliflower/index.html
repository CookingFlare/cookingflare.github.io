10 Creative Paleo Recipes Using Cauliflower


Cauliflower is one of those versatile vegetables that has earned its place in the kitchens of Paleo enthusiasts worldwide. Not only is it nutritious and low in carbs, but it can also be transformed into a variety of dishes that are both delicious and satisfying. From cauliflower rice to cauliflower pizza crusts, this humble vegetable can take on many roles in your Paleo meals.

If you’re following a Paleo lifestyle, then you’ll be happy to know that cauliflower fits perfectly into the guidelines of this dietary approach. It’s gluten-free, dairy-free, and full of fiber and vitamins, making it an ideal ingredient for any healthy recipe. Here are 10 creative Paleo recipes using cauliflower that will help you make the most of this powerhouse vegetable.

1. Cauliflower Rice Stir-Fry

Cauliflower rice is a popular alternative to regular rice, and it’s a perfect fit for anyone following a Paleo diet. It’s a great way to enjoy stir-fried rice without the added carbs, and it can be prepared in just minutes. To make cauliflower rice stir-fry, start by ricing the cauliflower. You can do this by pulsing it in a food processor until it resembles rice grains, or by grating it by hand.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers)
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos (a Paleo-friendly soy sauce alternative)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for another 3-4 minutes, until tender.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet, then add the cauliflower rice to the other side. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. In a separate pan, scramble the eggs and then add them to the skillet with the cauliflower rice.
  5. Add the coconut aminos, salt, and pepper, and mix everything together. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, and serve hot!

This cauliflower rice stir-fry is a quick, healthy, and filling meal that can be enjoyed for lunch or dinner.

2. Paleo Cauliflower Pizza Crust

One of the best ways to enjoy pizza while sticking to a Paleo diet is by using a cauliflower-based pizza crust. Cauliflower pizza crust is grain-free, gluten-free, and has a similar texture to traditional crust, making it the perfect base for your favorite Paleo-friendly toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cut the cauliflower into florets and pulse in a food processor until it resembles rice-sized pieces.
  3. Place the cauliflower rice in a microwave-safe bowl and microwave for 5-6 minutes, or until soft. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Once cooled, transfer the cauliflower to a clean dish towel and squeeze out as much moisture as possible.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the cauliflower, egg, almond flour, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  6. Spread the cauliflower mixture onto the prepared baking sheet into a round or rectangular shape, about 1/4-inch thick.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and firm.
  8. Remove from the oven and top with your favorite Paleo-friendly pizza toppings such as tomato sauce, vegetables, and lean meats.
  9. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes until the toppings are cooked.

This cauliflower pizza crust will give you that pizza experience without compromising your Paleo principles.

3. Cauliflower Buffalo Wings

If you love buffalo wings but want a healthier, plant-based alternative, cauliflower buffalo wings are a must-try! These crispy cauliflower florets are coated in a flavorful buffalo sauce and then baked to perfection. They’re a great snack or appetizer for a party or game day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup hot sauce (make sure it’s Paleo-friendly)
  • 2 tablespoons melted ghee or coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond flour, almond milk, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Whisk until smooth.
  3. Dip each cauliflower floret into the almond flour mixture, coating it evenly. Place the coated cauliflower on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cauliflower is crispy and golden brown.
  5. While the cauliflower is baking, mix the hot sauce and melted ghee in a bowl.
  6. Once the cauliflower is done, toss the florets in the buffalo sauce mixture and serve immediately.

These cauliflower buffalo wings are an excellent appetizer or side dish, and they’re full of bold flavors that everyone will love.

4. Creamy Cauliflower Soup

Cauliflower makes an excellent base for a creamy, comforting soup. This Paleo cauliflower soup is dairy-free but still rich and velvety thanks to the cauliflower and coconut milk. It’s perfect for chilly days when you’re craving something warm and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegan version)
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a tablespoon of coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the cauliflower florets and cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the cauliflower is tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the soup until smooth and creamy.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

This creamy cauliflower soup is perfect for lunch or dinner and can be enjoyed as a light meal or paired with a side salad for a more filling dish.

5. Cauliflower Mash

If you’re missing mashed potatoes, cauliflower mash is the perfect substitute. It’s creamy, buttery, and a great side dish for any Paleo meal. Plus, it’s lower in carbs and packed with nutrients!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets until they are tender, about 10-12 minutes.
  2. Drain the cauliflower and transfer it to a food processor or blender.
  3. Add the ghee or olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Process until smooth and creamy.
  4. Transfer the mashed cauliflower to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh chives.
  5. Serve as a side dish to your favorite Paleo entrée.

Cauliflower mash makes for a great substitute for mashed potatoes while still delivering that creamy texture and rich flavor.

6. Cauliflower Tacos

Tacos don’t have to be loaded with tortillas or beans to be delicious. Using cauliflower as the filling is a fun and creative way to enjoy tacos while sticking to your Paleo diet. These cauliflower tacos are full of flavor and are an excellent meatless option for taco night.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Paleo-friendly tortillas (or lettuce leaves for a low-carb option)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the cauliflower florets with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the cauliflower on the baking sheet in a single layer. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and tender.
  4. Warm the tortillas (or lettuce leaves) and fill with roasted cauliflower.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges.

These cauliflower tacos are a healthy, flavorful option for Taco Tuesday or any time you want a tasty, Paleo-friendly meal.

7. Cauliflower “Mac” and Cheese

Cauliflower can be used to create a creamy, cheese-like sauce that makes a perfect substitute for mac and cheese. This recipe combines cauliflower with a creamy, dairy-free sauce that satisfies your craving for comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, about 10-12 minutes.
  2. In a blender, combine the steamed cauliflower, almond milk, nutritional yeast, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Toss the cauliflower with the sauce until well coated. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This cauliflower “mac” and cheese is a great Paleo-friendly alternative to traditional mac and cheese.

8. Cauliflower Fritters

Cauliflower fritters are a delicious and crispy snack or side dish. These fritters are made with cauliflower, almond flour, and egg, then pan-fried until golden brown.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small head of cauliflower, grated
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Coconut oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Grate the cauliflower using a box grater or food processor.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the grated cauliflower, almond flour, eggs, green onions, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Spoon the cauliflower mixture into the skillet and flatten into small patties. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
  5. Serve with a dipping sauce or enjoy on their own.

These cauliflower fritters are a fun way to incorporate more vegetables into your meals.

9. Cauliflower and Ground Turkey Casserole

This hearty casserole is made with cauliflower and ground turkey, topped with a savory tomato sauce. It’s an easy, one-pan meal that’s both filling and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish.
  2. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, then mash them in a bowl.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground turkey with the onion and garlic until browned.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes and Italian seasoning to the skillet, and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Spread the mashed cauliflower into the baking dish, then top with the turkey mixture.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, and serve hot.

This cauliflower and ground turkey casserole is a great dinner option that combines protein and veggies in a hearty dish.

10. Cauliflower Salad

A simple and refreshing cauliflower salad is a perfect addition to any Paleo meal. This salad combines roasted cauliflower with fresh vegetables and a tangy lemon dressing for a light, healthy dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Roast the cauliflower florets for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the roasted cauliflower with the cucumber, bell pepper, and red onion.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss to combine and serve immediately.

This cauliflower salad is a light, refreshing dish that can be served as a side or a light lunch.

With these 10 creative Paleo recipes using cauliflower, you can make the most out of this versatile vegetable and enjoy a variety of healthy and delicious meals. Whether you’re looking for a cauliflower pizza crust, a creamy soup, or a satisfying side dish, these recipes are sure to satisfy your Paleo cravings. Enjoy!
