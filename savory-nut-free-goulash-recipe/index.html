Savory Nut-Free Goulash Recipe


Are you looking for a comforting and hearty meal that’s nut-free and bursting with savory flavors? Look no further than this delectable Savory Nut-Free Goulash recipe! Goulash is a traditional Hungarian dish that has gained popularity around the world for its rich taste and simple preparation. In this recipe, we’ll take a nut-free approach to this classic dish, ensuring that everyone can enjoy its deliciousness without any concerns. Let’s dive into the details of this mouthwatering recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Sear the Beef: In a large Dutch oven or pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and sear them until browned on all sides. This will take about 5-7 minutes. Once browned, remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Add the Flavors: Stir in the tomato paste, paprika, caraway seeds, and dried thyme, and cook for another 2 minutes. These ingredients will infuse the dish with deep, rich flavors.

  4. Simmer with Broth and Tomatoes: Return the seared beef to the pot, and add the diced tomatoes, red and green bell peppers, and beef broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Cook the Pasta: In a separate pot, cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta and set it aside.

  6. Combine and Serve: Once the beef is tender, stir in the cooked elbow macaroni, ensuring it’s well coated with the savory goulash mixture. Let it simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Garnish and Enjoy: Serve the savory nut-free goulash in bowls, garnished with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 430
  • Fat: 14g
  • Carbohydrates: 38g
  • Protein: 36g

Conclusion

This savory nut-free goulash recipe is a wonderful way to bring warmth and satisfaction to the dinner table. Its rich flavors and tender beef will surely make it a family favorite. Best of all, it’s nut-free, making it a safe and delicious option for those with nut allergies. Whether you’re craving a cozy meal on a chilly evening or simply want to indulge in a bowl of comforting goodness, this goulash recipe is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to savor every spoonful of this delightful dish!
