Classic New England Clam Chowder Recipe to Warm Your Soul


There’s something about a steaming bowl of clam chowder that just warms you from the inside out. The rich, creamy broth, tender potatoes, and succulent clams come together to create a dish that’s as comforting as it is delicious. If you’re looking to recreate that cozy feeling at home, look no further than this classic New England clam chowder recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 dozen littleneck clams, scrubbed
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups clam juice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pound Yukon Gold potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, place the clams and add enough water to cover them. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to medium and cover the pot. Let the clams steam for about 5-10 minutes, or until they open. Discard any clams that do not open.

  2. Remove the clams from the pot and set them aside to cool. Once cool enough to handle, remove the clams from their shells and chop them into bite-sized pieces. Strain the clam cooking liquid through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any sand or grit, and set the liquid aside.

  3. In the same pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until it’s crisp and the fat has rendered. Add the chopped onion, celery, and garlic to the pot and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are soft and translucent.

  4. Sprinkle the flour over the bacon and vegetables, stirring to coat everything evenly. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the flour is lightly golden.

  5. Gradually pour in the reserved clam juice, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Add the bay leaf and diced potatoes, then bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook for about 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  6. Stir in the chopped clams and heavy cream, then season the chowder with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Remove the bay leaf from the pot and discard it. Ladle the clam chowder into bowls, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve hot.

Tips for the Perfect Chowder

  • Freshness is Key: When it comes to seafood, freshness is crucial. Look for the freshest clams you can find to ensure the best flavor in your chowder.
  • Creamy Consistency: If you prefer a thicker chowder, you can mash some of the cooked potatoes against the side of the pot to release their starch, which will help to naturally thicken the broth.
  • Make it Ahead: Clam chowder actually tastes even better the next day, as the flavors have more time to meld. Consider making it ahead for a stress-free meal.

In Conclusion

This classic New England clam chowder recipe is a timeless favorite that never fails to delight. Whether you’re enjoying it on a chilly evening or serving it at a gathering with loved ones, the warmth and comfort it brings are undeniable. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor a bowl of pure coziness.
