The Best of Southern BBQ: 7 Mouthwatering Recipes


Southern BBQ is a culinary delight that has been perfected over centuries, with each region adding its own unique twist to the traditional recipes. From tender pork ribs to smoky beef brisket, the South is famous for its slow-cooked meats that are falling off the bone. In this post, we'll take you on a culinary journey through the best of Southern BBQ, with 7 mouthwatering recipes that will make your taste buds dance with joy. Recipe 1: Classic Southern Pulled Pork There's no doubt that pulled pork is a staple of Southern BBQ. This recipe is a masterclass in tenderizing pork shoulder to perfection, infusing it with a rich, tangy flavor that will leave you wanting more. To make classic Southern pulled pork, you'll need the following ingredients:

2 pounds pork shoulder 1/4 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons smoked paprika 1 tablespoon salt 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 cup barbecue sauce 1 cup wood chips (hickory or apple)

Preheat your smoker to 225°F, then season the pork shoulder with brown sugar, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Place the meat in the smoker and cook for 8 hours, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 190°F. Finish the dish by brushing it with barbecue sauce and serving it on a bun with your favorite toppings. Recipe 2: Memphis-Style Dry Ribs Memphis-style dry ribs are a Southern BBQ classic, known for their tender, finger-lickin' good texture and flavor. To make this recipe, you'll need:

2 racks beef back ribs 1 cup kosher salt 1 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons black pepper 2 tablespoons paprika 2 tablespoons garlic powder 2 tablespoons onion powder

Preheat your oven to 200°F, then season the ribs with kosher salt, brown sugar, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder. Place the ribs on a baking sheet lined with foil and cook for 4 hours, or until they reach an internal temperature of 160°F. Finish the recipe by basting the ribs with your favorite barbecue sauce and serving them with a side of coleslaw. Recipe 3: Kansas City-Style Burnt Ends Kansas City-style BBQ is famous for its rich, smoky flavors, and burnt ends are the perfect example of this. This recipe is a masterclass in slow-cooking beef brisket to perfection, then cutting it into bite-sized pieces and finishing it with a sweet, tangy barbecue sauce.

1 beef brisket (10 pounds) 1 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons smoked paprika 1 tablespoon salt 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 cup barbecue sauce

Preheat your smoker to 225°F, then season the beef brisket with brown sugar, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook the brisket for 8 hours, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F. Finish the recipe by cutting the brisket into bite-sized pieces, then tossing it with barbecue sauce and serving it in a bun. Recipe 4: Texas-Style Beef Brisket Texas-style beef brisket is a slow-cooked masterpiece that's been perfected over centuries. This recipe is a masterclass in tenderizing the beef to perfection, then finishing it with a sweet, tangy barbecue sauce.

1 beef brisket (10 pounds) 1 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons smoked paprika 1 tablespoon salt 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 cup barbecue sauce

Preheat your smoker to 225°F, then season the beef brisket with brown sugar, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook the brisket for 8 hours, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F. Finish the recipe by brushing the brisket with barbecue sauce and serving it with a side of coleslaw. Recipe 5: Alabama-Style White BBQ Sauce Alabama-style white BBQ sauce is a unique twist on the traditional tomato-based sauce, made with a combination of mayonnaise, vinegar, and spices. This recipe is a masterclass in creating a creamy, tangy sauce that pairs perfectly with smoked chicken or pork.

1 cup mayonnaise 1/2 cup white vinegar 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce 1 tablespoon garlic powder 1 tablespoon onion powder 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Mix all the ingredients together in a saucepan, then cook over low heat until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Brush the sauce over smoked chicken or pork during the last 10 minutes of cooking, then serve it on a bun with your favorite toppings. Recipe 6: South Carolina-Style Mustard BBQ Sauce South Carolina-style mustard BBQ sauce is a tangy, spicy sauce that's made with a combination of yellow mustard, vinegar, and spices. This recipe is a masterclass in creating a bold, flavorful sauce that pairs perfectly with smoked chicken or pork.

1 cup yellow mustard 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Mix all the ingredients together in a saucepan, then cook over low heat until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Brush the sauce over smoked chicken or pork during the last 10 minutes of cooking, then serve it on a bun with your favorite toppings. Recipe 7: Georgia-Style Vinegar BBQ Sauce Georgia-style vinegar BBQ sauce is a classic Southern sauce that's made with a combination of vinegar, spices, and a hint of sweetness. This recipe is a masterclass in creating a tangy, flavorful sauce that pairs perfectly with smoked chicken or pork.

1 cup apple cider vinegar 1/4 cup water 2 tablespoons brown sugar 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Mix all the ingredients together in a saucepan, then cook over low heat until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Brush the sauce over smoked chicken or pork during the last 10 minutes of cooking, then serve it on a bun with your favorite toppings. In conclusion, Southern BBQ is a culinary delight that's been perfected over centuries, with each region adding its own unique twist to the traditional recipes. Whether you're a seasoned pitmaster or a BBQ newbie, these 7 mouthwatering recipes will take you on a journey through the best of Southern BBQ, with tender pork ribs, smoky beef brisket, and tangy, flavorful sauces that will leave you wanting more. So go ahead, grab your apron, and get ready to experience the richness, complexity, and flavor of Southern BBQ!
