Mouthwatering Greek Moussaka Stuffed Peppers Recipe

If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine and love the rich flavors of moussaka, then you’re in for a treat with this mouthwatering Greek moussaka stuffed peppers recipe. This dish combines the classic flavors of moussaka with the vibrant, colorful appeal of stuffed peppers, resulting in a delightful culinary experience that will tantalize your taste buds. Ingredients For the Stuffed Peppers: 6 large bell peppers (assorted colors for visual appeal) 1 pound ground lamb or beef 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup cooked rice 1 can diced tomatoes 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley for garnish For the Moussaka Filling: 1 large eggplant, diced 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 cup red wine 1 cup tomato sauce 1 cup crumbled feta cheese Bechamel sauce (see below) For the Bechamel Sauce: 3 tablespoons butter 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour 2 cups milk Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 2 egg yolks Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Peppers Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside. Place the peppers in a baking dish and set aside. Step 2: Make the Moussaka Filling Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced eggplant and sauté until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Sprinkle the cinnamon and nutmeg over the eggplant and stir to combine. Pour in the red wine and cook until it evaporates. Stir in the tomato sauce and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the crumbled feta cheese. Step 3: Prepare the Bechamel Sauce In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until golden. Gradually whisk in the milk, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Remove the saucepan from the heat and whisk in the egg yolks. Step 4: Assemble the Stuffed Peppers In a separate skillet, brown the ground lamb or beef with the chopped onion and garlic. Add the cooked rice, diced tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper to the skillet and stir to combine. Fill each bell pepper with the meat and rice mixture, then spoon the moussaka filling on top. Pour the bechamel sauce over the moussaka filling, covering the tops of the peppers. Step 5: Bake and Serve Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the tops are golden. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving. Savor the Flavors of Greece with Greek Lemon Garlic Roasted Shrimp

Are you ready to embark on a culinary journey to Greece? Get ready to savor the flavors of the Mediterranean with this mouthwatering recipe for Greek Lemon Garlic Roasted Shrimp. Bursting with tangy lemon, fragrant garlic, and a medley of aromatic herbs, this dish will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Greece. Ingredients 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined 4 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil 1 lemon, juiced and zested 1 teaspoon of dried oregano 1 teaspoon of dried thyme 1/2 teaspoon of dried rosemary Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish) Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, dried oregano, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful marinade. Add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the bowl, making sure they are coated evenly with the marinade. Let the shrimp marinate for at least 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Once the shrimp has marinated, transfer them to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Arrange the shrimp in a single layer to ensure even cooking. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast the shrimp for 8-10 minutes, or until they turn pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp, as they can become rubbery. Once the shrimp are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley for an extra pop of color and flavor. Serve the Greek Lemon Garlic Roasted Shrimp as an appetizer, main course, or even as a topping for a vibrant Greek salad. The possibilities are endless!
