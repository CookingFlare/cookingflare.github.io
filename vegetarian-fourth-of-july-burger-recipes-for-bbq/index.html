Vegetarian Fourth of July Burger Recipes for BBQ


Summer is in full swing, and what better way to celebrate the Fourth of July than with a delicious vegetarian burger? Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply looking to add some plant-based options to your barbecue menu, these burger recipes are sure to impress your guests. So fire up the grill and get ready to enjoy some mouthwatering burgers!

1. Black Bean Burger

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your favorite toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the black beans with a fork or potato masher.
  2. Add the bread crumbs, bell pepper, onion, garlic, cilantro, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well until everything is combined.
  3. Divide the mixture into patties and place them on a greased grill.
  4. Grill for about 5-6 minutes on each side, or until they are heated through and have grill marks.
  5. Serve on burger buns with your favorite toppings such as lettuce, tomato, avocado, and a dollop of vegan mayo.

2. Portobello Mushroom Burger

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your favorite toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, flipping them halfway through.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the mushrooms for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and juicy.
  5. Serve on burger buns with your favorite toppings such as caramelized onions, melted cheese, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze.

3. Quinoa Burger

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup grated zucchini
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your favorite toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, zucchini, carrot, onion, garlic, bread crumbs, parsley, feta cheese, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix well until everything is evenly distributed.
  3. Shape the mixture into patties and place them on a greased grill.
  4. Grill for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serve on burger buns with your favorite toppings such as tzatziki sauce, cucumber slices, and fresh spinach leaves.

4. Lentil Burger

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1/2 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup bread crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your favorite toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the cooked lentils with a fork or potato masher.
  2. Add the brown rice, onion, bell pepper, garlic, bread crumbs, parsley, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well until everything is combined.
  3. Divide the mixture into patties and place them on a greased grill.
  4. Grill for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are crispy on the outside and heated through.
  5. Serve on burger buns with your favorite toppings such as caramelized onions, BBQ sauce, and pickles.

These vegetarian burger recipes are not only delicious but also packed with protein and nutrients. They are perfect for celebrating the Fourth of July or any summer barbecue. So gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and enjoy these mouthwatering burgers. Happy cooking and happy Fourth of July!
