Exploring the Flavors of the Balkans: 5 Recipes to Try


The Balkans, a region in southeastern Europe, is rich in history, culture, and, of course, flavor. The culinary traditions of this diverse area blend influences from the Mediterranean, Ottoman Empire, and Central Europe, creating a vibrant and unique gastronomic landscape. Whether you’ve visited the region or are simply curious about its food, exploring Balkan cuisine through its most popular dishes is an exciting adventure.

From savory meats to fragrant herbs, from fresh vegetables to bold spices, the Balkans offer a delightful mix of ingredients and cooking techniques that are sure to leave an impression. In this post, we will explore five recipes that capture the essence of Balkan cuisine. These dishes are not only delicious but also simple enough to recreate in your own kitchen.

1. Ćevapi: Grilled Balkan Sausages

Ćevapi, or ćevapčići, are small grilled sausages made from a mixture of minced meat, typically beef and lamb, seasoned with garlic, onion, and spices. These juicy, flavorful sausages are popular across the Balkans and are often served with flatbread, onions, and a dollop of creamy yogurt or ajvar (a roasted red pepper spread). Perfect for grilling, ćevapi are a staple at barbecues and outdoor gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground beef
  • 500g ground lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil for grilling

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef and lamb. Add the chopped onion, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  2. Sprinkle the baking soda over the mixture and knead it into the meat for about 5-10 minutes. This will help make the sausages tender and juicy.
  3. Once the mixture is ready, shape it into small, finger-like sausages, about 3-4 inches long. If the mixture is too sticky, lightly grease your hands with olive oil.
  4. Preheat the grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the ćevapi with a little olive oil and place them on the grill. Cook for about 5-6 minutes on each side, or until browned and cooked through.
  5. Serve the ćevapi hot with flatbread, chopped onions, and a side of ajvar or yogurt.

2. Burek: Savory Pastry with Ground Meat

Burek is a beloved pastry found throughout the Balkans, and it’s as much a part of the region’s identity as its landscape. This flaky, golden pastry can be filled with various ingredients, but one of the most popular fillings is a savory mixture of ground meat. The dough is rolled thin and layered with the filling, creating a deliciously crunchy yet tender bite.

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground beef (or lamb)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 10 sheets of filo pastry (store-bought)
  • 100g melted butter
  • 1 egg (optional, for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the ground beef (or lamb) to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up into small pieces with a spoon. Season with paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Once the meat is cooked through, remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a round or square baking dish.
  5. Lay one sheet of filo pastry in the dish, brushing it with melted butter. Place another sheet on top and brush again. Repeat this process until you have used about half of the filo pastry.
  6. Spread the cooled meat mixture evenly over the filo.
  7. Layer the remaining filo sheets on top of the meat, brushing each with butter as you go. Fold in the edges of the filo and brush the top with a beaten egg for a golden finish.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and crisp.
  9. Let the burek cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy it with a side of yogurt or a fresh salad.

3. Sarma: Stuffed Cabbage Rolls

Sarma, a traditional Balkan dish, consists of pickled cabbage leaves stuffed with a mixture of minced meat, rice, and various seasonings. The rolls are slow-cooked in a pot, creating a rich and hearty dish perfect for cold winter days. This dish has deep roots in Balkan culture, and it’s often enjoyed during holidays or family gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of sauerkraut (pickled cabbage)
  • 500g ground pork (or beef, or a mix)
  • 1 cup rice, rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried dill (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Start by carefully separating the pickled cabbage leaves. If the leaves are too thick, you can blanch them in hot water to soften them.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, rice, chopped onion, garlic, paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  3. Take each cabbage leaf and place a spoonful of the meat mixture in the center. Roll the leaf tightly around the filling, folding in the sides as you go.
  4. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Begin layering the stuffed cabbage rolls in the pot, packing them tightly together.
  5. Once all the rolls are in the pot, pour the tomato sauce over them. Add enough water to cover the rolls, then place the bay leaves on top.
  6. Cover the pot and simmer the sarma over low heat for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the cabbage is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  7. Serve the sarma hot with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of fresh dill (if using).

4. Ajvar: Roasted Red Pepper and Eggplant Spread

Ajvar is a staple in the Balkans, especially during the harvest season when peppers and eggplants are abundant. This roasted red pepper and eggplant spread is a versatile condiment that can be enjoyed with meats, bread, or even as a dip. The rich, smoky flavor of the roasted vegetables combined with garlic and olive oil makes ajvar an irresistible addition to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large red bell peppers
  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp paprika (optional, for extra smokiness)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the red bell peppers and eggplants on a baking sheet. Roast for 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the vegetables are softened.
  2. Once roasted, remove the vegetables from the oven and let them cool. Peel off the skins from the peppers and eggplants, discarding the seeds and stems.
  3. In a food processor, combine the roasted peppers, eggplants, minced garlic, olive oil, vinegar, and paprika (if using). Blend until smooth, adding salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Transfer the ajvar to a jar and refrigerate. It will keep for about a week.
  5. Serve ajvar as a spread on bread, as a dip for vegetables, or alongside grilled meats.

5. Pita: Sweet Cheese and Fruit Pastry

While savory dishes dominate much of Balkan cuisine, the region also boasts a wide array of delicious desserts. Pita, a sweet pastry filled with cheese and fruits, is a popular treat in the Balkans. The combination of rich cheese and the sweetness of fruit encased in thin, flaky filo pastry creates a delightful contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 8 sheets of filo pastry
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, or strawberries)
  • 1 tbsp butter, melted
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with a little butter.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth.
  3. Lay one sheet of filo pastry in the baking dish and brush with melted butter. Add another sheet on top, brushing it with butter again. Repeat until you’ve used half of the filo sheets.
  4. Spread the cheese mixture evenly over the filo pastry, then scatter the mixed berries on top.
  5. Continue layering the remaining filo sheets, brushing each one with butter as you go. Fold in the edges of the filo pastry and brush the top with more butter.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crisp.
  7. Let the pita cool slightly before dusting with powdered sugar and serving.

These five Balkan recipes are just the beginning of what the region’s culinary traditions have to offer. With their vibrant flavors and unique combinations, each dish is a reflection of the rich cultural history of the Balkans. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a summer barbecue, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and transport them to the heart of southeastern Europe.

Try these dishes today and explore the unforgettable tastes of the Balkans!
