Agedashi Tofu: Crispy Fried Tofu in a Flavorful Sauce


If you’re a fan of tofu, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious and satisfying dish. Agedashi Tofu is a traditional Japanese recipe that takes soft tofu, coats it in a crispy batter, and serves it with a flavorful sauce. This dish is not only a delight for your taste buds but also a feast for your eyes with its contrasting textures and vibrant flavors.

Ingredients

For the Tofu:

  • 1 block of soft or silken tofu
  • Cornstarch, for dusting
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Batter:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup ice-cold water

For the Sauce:

  • 1 cup dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For Garnish:

  • Grated daikon (Japanese radish)
  • Grated ginger
  • Chopped green onions

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tofu. Drain the tofu and pat it dry with a paper towel. Cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes, about 1 inch thick.

  2. Dust the tofu cubes with cornstarch, ensuring that they are evenly coated. This will help create a crispy exterior when fried.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat. You’ll need enough oil to submerge the tofu cubes. Test the oil temperature by dropping a small piece of tofu into the oil. If it sizzles and floats to the top, the oil is ready for frying.

  4. In the meantime, prepare the batter. In a bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the ice-cold water while whisking until a smooth batter forms.

  5. Once the oil is hot, dip each tofu cube into the batter, ensuring it is evenly coated. Carefully place the battered tofu cubes into the hot oil, frying them in batches. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, usually around 3-4 minutes per side.

  6. While the tofu is frying, prepare the sauce. In a saucepan, combine the dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Once the tofu cubes are crispy and golden brown, remove them from the oil using a slotted spoon or tongs. Place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  8. To serve, place a few pieces of crispy tofu in a bowl or plate. Pour the flavorful sauce over the tofu, allowing it to seep into the cracks and crevices. Garnish with grated daikon, grated ginger, and chopped green onions for an added burst of freshness and flavor.

  9. Agedashi Tofu is best enjoyed immediately while it’s still hot and crispy. Serve it as an appetizer or pair it with steamed rice and a side of vegetables for a satisfying main course.

Conclusion

Agedashi Tofu is a classic Japanese dish that showcases the versatility of tofu. The contrast between the crispy fried exterior and the soft, silky interior creates a delightful texture, while the flavorful sauce adds depth and complexity to every bite. Whether you’re a tofu aficionado or just looking to explore new flavors, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try and discover the wonders of Agedashi Tofu in your own kitchen.
