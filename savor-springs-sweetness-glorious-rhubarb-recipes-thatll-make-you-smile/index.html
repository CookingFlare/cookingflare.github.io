Savor Spring's Sweetness: Glorious Rhubarb Recipes that'll Make You Smile


Spring is a time of renewal, where nature’s vibrant colors and fresh flavors return to our tables. One of the most anticipated ingredients of the season is rhubarb. With its tart yet sweet profile, rhubarb is an incredible vegetable (yes, it’s technically not a fruit) that can be transformed into an endless variety of delicious dishes. Whether you’re a long-time fan or new to the world of rhubarb, it’s time to embrace this unique springtime gem. From classic desserts to savory creations, rhubarb can elevate any dish, adding a zesty twist that will make your taste buds sing.

In this post, we’ll dive deep into some of the most mouth-watering rhubarb recipes. From pies and crumbles to refreshing drinks and savory sauces, let’s explore the versatile ways rhubarb can shine in your kitchen.

The Magic of Rhubarb

Before we dive into the recipes, it’s important to understand what makes rhubarb so special. Rhubarb is a perennial vegetable known for its vibrant red stalks and sour flavor. Despite its tartness, rhubarb pairs wonderfully with sweetness, making it perfect for a variety of desserts, jams, and even savory dishes.

Rich in vitamin C, potassium, and fiber, rhubarb is also low in calories, making it a great addition to a balanced diet. However, the leaves of rhubarb should be avoided as they are toxic. Only the stems should be consumed, so be sure to trim them before use.

Now that we have a bit of rhubarb knowledge under our belt, let’s jump into some fantastic recipes!

1. Classic Rhubarb Pie

What better way to start than with a classic rhubarb pie? This dessert is a staple of springtime baking and will fill your home with the sweet aroma of fresh rhubarb. This pie features a tender, flaky crust and a rich, tangy filling that’s the perfect balance of sweet and sour.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 double-crust pie pastry

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, flour, cinnamon, and salt until well combined. Let the mixture sit for about 15 minutes to allow the juices to release from the rhubarb.
  3. Roll out the pie pastry and line a pie dish with the bottom crust. Pour the rhubarb mixture into the prepared crust.
  4. Dot the filling with butter and cover with the top pie crust. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal.
  5. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Bake the pie for 40-45 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  7. Let the pie cool before serving, and enjoy this nostalgic treat with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

2. Rhubarb and Strawberry Crisp

If you’re looking for something a little different but just as comforting, a rhubarb and strawberry crisp is a delicious alternative. The combination of tart rhubarb and sweet strawberries, topped with a buttery, oat crumble, creates a dessert that’s hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 2 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rhubarb, strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and cinnamon. Stir until everything is well-coated and the mixture is slightly juicy.
  3. Transfer the fruit mixture to a greased 9x9-inch baking dish.
  4. In another bowl, mix the oats, flour, brown sugar, and melted butter. Stir until the mixture is crumbly.
  5. Sprinkle the oat mixture evenly over the fruit.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the fruit is bubbling.
  7. Serve warm with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a decadent finish.

3. Rhubarb Sorbet

When the weather warms up, a refreshing sorbet is the perfect way to cool off, and rhubarb is ideal for creating a tangy yet sweet frozen treat. Rhubarb sorbet is light, flavorful, and a delightful way to enjoy the vibrant taste of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the rhubarb and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, until the rhubarb has softened and broken down.
  2. Remove from heat and strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve, pressing down to extract as much liquid as possible. Discard the solids.
  3. Stir the sugar and lemon juice into the rhubarb juice until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or until it’s fully cooled.
  5. Once chilled, pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Transfer the sorbet to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours to firm up.
  7. Serve in bowls or cones and enjoy a light, refreshing dessert that’s packed with spring flavor.

4. Rhubarb Compote

Rhubarb compote is a versatile dish that can be used as a topping for yogurt, pancakes, waffles, or even savory dishes like roasted meats. The sweet-tart flavor of rhubarb is balanced with sugar and a hint of citrus, making it the perfect condiment for a variety of dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the rhubarb, sugar, water, orange zest, and lemon juice.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rhubarb has softened and the compote has thickened slightly.
  4. Remove from heat and let the compote cool before using.
  5. Serve the rhubarb compote warm or chilled over your favorite breakfast items or as a savory accompaniment.

5. Rhubarb BBQ Sauce

Yes, you read that right—rhubarb can also be used in savory dishes! This tangy rhubarb BBQ sauce is the perfect way to add a twist to your grilled meats. The acidity of rhubarb combines beautifully with the smoky sweetness of BBQ flavors, making it a must-try for your next cookout.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for 5-7 minutes, until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the rhubarb, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, ketchup, mustard, smoked paprika, and cumin. Stir to combine.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rhubarb breaks down and the sauce thickens.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Let the sauce cool slightly before using it to glaze grilled chicken, ribs, or vegetables. The tangy-sweet flavor will elevate your BBQ experience like never before.

6. Rhubarb Muffins

For a breakfast treat that celebrates the flavors of spring, these rhubarb muffins are the perfect choice. With their tender crumb and slight tang from the rhubarb, these muffins are great for a quick snack or to accompany your morning coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • ¾ cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • ¼ cup brown sugar, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, buttermilk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Gently fold in the rhubarb.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, and sprinkle the tops with brown sugar.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool slightly before enjoying with a cup of tea or coffee.

Conclusion

Rhubarb is truly one of spring’s most delightful gifts. With its unique flavor, it adds a refreshing twist to both sweet and savory dishes. Whether you’re baking a classic rhubarb pie, whipping up a tangy sorbet, or exploring savory BBQ sauces, rhubarb can be a versatile ingredient in any kitchen. Its vibrant color and bold flavor will brighten your meals, bringing a taste of spring to your table year-round.

So, next time you see fresh rhubarb at the market, grab a bunch and get creative! You won’t regret it.
