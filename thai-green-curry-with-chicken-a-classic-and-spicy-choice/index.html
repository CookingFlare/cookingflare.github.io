Thai Green Curry with Chicken: A Classic and Spicy Choice


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors and aromatic spices, and one dish that perfectly encapsulates these qualities is Thai Green Curry with Chicken. This classic Thai dish brings together the perfect balance of heat, sweetness, and creaminess, making it a favorite among curry lovers worldwide. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating an authentic and flavorful Thai Green Curry with Chicken right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make this delicious Thai Green Curry with Chicken, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons Thai green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 cup Thai eggplant, quartered
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for a minute or until fragrant.

  2. Add the chicken to the pan and cook until it is no longer pink in the center.

  3. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, stirring well to combine. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  4. Add the fish sauce and palm sugar to the pan, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.

  5. Add the Thai eggplant, bamboo shoots, and bell peppers to the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  6. Finally, add the basil leaves and torn kaffir lime leaves to the pan, stirring them into the curry.

  7. Simmer for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  8. Remove the pan from heat and let the curry rest for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Thai Green Curry with Chicken is best served hot with steamed jasmine rice. You can also pair it with a side of freshly sliced cucumbers to add a refreshing crunch to the dish. For those who enjoy a little extra heat, feel free to serve the curry with a side of Thai chili peppers or a drizzle of sriracha sauce.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder curry, you can adjust the amount of green curry paste to your taste.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as green beans, zucchini, or carrots to the curry for added color and texture.
  • For a vegetarian version, you can substitute the chicken with tofu or a medley of your favorite vegetables.
  • If you can’t find Thai eggplant or bamboo shoots, you can use regular eggplant and water chestnuts as substitutes.

In Conclusion

Thai Green Curry with Chicken is a classic and spicy choice that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Thailand. The combination of fragrant green curry paste, creamy coconut milk, and tender chicken, along with the assortment of vegetables, creates a harmonious and satisfying dish. So, why not bring a taste of Thailand to your own kitchen and indulge in the flavors of this beloved Thai curry?
