Budget-Friendly BBQ Recipes: Grilling on a Shoestring Budget


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill! But who says you need to break the bank to enjoy delicious BBQ meals? With a little creativity and smart shopping, you can create mouthwatering dishes that won’t empty your wallet. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of budget-friendly BBQ recipes that are not only easy to make but also perfect for gatherings, picnics, or a simple family dinner. Let’s dive into the world of grilling on a shoestring budget!

1. Savory BBQ Chicken Thighs

Ingredients:

  • 8 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on for flavor)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Start by preheating your grill to medium heat. While the grill is heating, pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels. This will help achieve that crispy skin we all love.

  2. Marination: In a bowl, combine the olive oil, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the chicken thighs. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or longer if you have the time.

  3. Grilling: Place the chicken thighs skin-side down on the grill. Cook for about 7-10 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). During the last few minutes, brush on your favorite BBQ sauce for that sticky, caramelized finish.

  4. Serving: Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. Pair with a simple coleslaw or grilled veggies for a complete meal.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Chicken thighs are often much cheaper than breasts, and they stay juicy and flavorful when grilled. Buying in bulk can save even more!

2. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), chopped
  • 1 onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and your choice of herbs (oregano, basil, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While it’s heating, soak wooden skewers in water for about 30 minutes to prevent burning.

  2. Assembling Skewers: In a bowl, toss all the veggies with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers alternating between each type.

  3. Grilling: Grill the skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally. The veggies should be tender and slightly charred.

  4. Serving: Serve these skewers as a side dish or over a bed of rice or quinoa for a light, healthy meal.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Utilize seasonal vegetables for the best prices and flavors. You can also use leftovers from your fridge!

3. BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwiches

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder (butt)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 hamburger buns

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Rub the pork shoulder with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Place it in a slow cooker with the sliced onion, and pour the BBQ sauce over the top.

  2. Cooking: Cook on low for 8-10 hours or until the meat is tender and shreds easily. If you prefer grilling, wrap the seasoned pork in foil and cook over indirect heat for about 4-5 hours.

  3. Shredding: Once cooked, shred the pork with two forks and mix it with the remaining BBQ sauce in the slow cooker or foil.

  4. Serving: Serve on hamburger buns with coleslaw for that classic BBQ experience.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Pork shoulder is often one of the cheapest cuts of meat and it’s perfect for slow cooking.

4. Classic Grilled Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Husk the corn, removing all silk and excess leaves.

  2. Grilling: Place the corn directly on the grill. Grill for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until the corn is tender and slightly charred.

  3. Serving: Spread the softened butter over the corn and season with salt and pepper. Serve hot as a delicious side.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Corn is very affordable, especially in season. Buy it in bulk for even more savings!

5. Affordable BBQ Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your grill for indirect heat. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and season with salt, pepper, paprika, and brown sugar.

  2. Cooking: Place the ribs on the cooler side of the grill. Cover and cook for about 2-3 hours, basting with BBQ sauce during the last 30 minutes.

  3. Serving: Cut into sections and serve with additional BBQ sauce on the side.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Look for sales or buy in bulk at wholesale clubs to take advantage of lower prices on ribs.

6. Grilled Pineapple Dessert

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Honey for drizzling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat the grill to medium heat. In a bowl, mix the brown sugar and cinnamon together.

  2. Grilling: Dip each pineapple slice into the sugar mixture and place them on the grill. Grill for about 3-4 minutes on each side until grill marks appear.

  3. Serving: Drizzle with honey if desired and serve warm. This makes for a refreshing and sweet end to your BBQ meal.

Cost-Effective Tip:

Pineapples are often on sale during summer months, making this dessert both delicious and budget-friendly.

Tips for Budget-Friendly BBQing

  1. Plan Ahead: Create a menu before shopping. This helps avoid impulse buys and reduces food waste.

  2. Shop Seasonal: Fruits and vegetables that are in season are usually cheaper and taste better.

  3. Buy in Bulk: If you can, buy meats and staples in larger quantities to save money over time.

  4. DIY Sauces and Marinades: Making your own BBQ sauce can save you money and allow you to customize flavors.

  5. Utilize Leftovers: Turn leftovers from one meal into another delicious dish. For example, leftover pulled pork can be used in tacos or omelets.

  6. Use Your Oven: If the weather doesn’t allow for grilling, many of these recipes can also be made in the oven or on the stovetop.

  7. Invite Friends: Consider hosting a potluck BBQ where guests bring a dish to share. This way, everyone gets to enjoy a variety of foods without one person shouldering the entire cost.

Conclusion

Grilling on a budget doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or variety in your meals. With these budget-friendly BBQ recipes, you can enjoy delicious dishes that are easy to prepare and won’t strain your wallet. Whether you’re hosting a backyard party or enjoying a quiet dinner with family, these recipes will impress and satisfy.

Fire up that grill, gather your friends and family, and enjoy a summer filled with mouthwatering BBQ goodness without breaking the bank! Happy grilling!
