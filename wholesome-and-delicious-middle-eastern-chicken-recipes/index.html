Wholesome and Delicious Middle Eastern Chicken Recipes


Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and wholesome ingredients. When it comes to chicken dishes, this culinary tradition offers a wealth of options that are not only delicious but also packed with nutrition. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of Middle Eastern chicken recipes that are both wholesome and delightful, perfect for any occasion.

1. Chicken Shawarma

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pita bread or flatbreads, for serving
  • Fresh vegetables (lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers) for topping
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine olive oil, yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken thighs and coat them well in the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Preheat your grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side until fully cooked and slightly charred. Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

  3. Assemble: Serve the chicken in pita bread or flatbreads, topped with fresh vegetables and a drizzle of tahini or garlic sauce.

Tips:

For an extra kick, add pickled vegetables or hot sauce to your shawarma. This dish is perfect for meal prep and can be stored in the refrigerator for several days.

2. Moroccan Chicken Tagine

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Chicken: In a large tagine or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then add them to the pot, skin-side down. Sear until golden brown, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken and set aside.

  2. Saut√© the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic, cumin, ginger, cinnamon, and turmeric, cooking for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Simmer: Return the chicken to the pot and add the chicken broth, chickpeas, and dried apricots. Bring to a simmer, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Cook for about 45 minutes until the chicken is tender and fully cooked.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Before serving, sprinkle with toasted almonds and fresh herbs. Pair this tagine with couscous or crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

Tips:

Feel free to add other vegetables such as carrots or bell peppers to the tagine for added nutrition and flavor. The combination of sweet apricots and savory spices creates a beautifully balanced dish.

3. Lebanese Chicken and Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant. Season the chicken cubes with salt, pepper, allspice, cinnamon, and cumin. Add the chicken to the pot and cook until browned.

  2. Add Rice and Broth: Stir in the rice, making sure to coat it in the oil and spices. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 20 minutes until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  3. Finish with Nuts and Raisins: Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork, then gently fold in the toasted pine nuts and raisins. Serve warm with a side salad or yogurt.

Tips:

For an extra layer of flavor, consider marinating the chicken in yogurt and spices for a few hours before cooking. This dish is not only comforting but also brings a taste of Lebanon to your dinner table.

4. Spicy Grilled Chicken Kebabs

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bell peppers, onions, and cherry tomatoes (for skewering)
  • Pita bread or flatbreads, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken cubes and coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers, alternating with bell peppers, onions, and cherry tomatoes.

  3. Grill the Kebabs: Grill the skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks.

  4. Serve: Serve the kebabs with warm pita bread and a side of tzatziki or garlic sauce for dipping.

Tips:

These kebabs are perfect for summer barbecues. You can also add other vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms for variety.

5. Chicken Fatteh

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cooked and shredded
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups plain yogurt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 4 pita breads, toasted and torn into pieces
  • Pine nuts, toasted, for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: In a pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the shredded chicken and simmer for about 10 minutes. Remove the chicken and set aside.

  2. Mix the Yogurt Sauce: In a bowl, combine yogurt, minced garlic, cumin, paprika, and a pinch of salt. Mix until smooth.

  3. Assemble the Fatteh: In a serving dish, layer the torn pita pieces at the bottom. Spoon the hot chicken broth over the pita to soften it. Top with the shredded chicken and drizzle the yogurt sauce on top.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with toasted pine nuts and fresh parsley. Serve warm as a comforting dish.
