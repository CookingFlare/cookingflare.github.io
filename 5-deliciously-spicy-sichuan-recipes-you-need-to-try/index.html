5 Deliciously Spicy Sichuan Recipes You Need to Try


Sichuan cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and numbing, explosive heat. This culinary gem from the southwestern province of China offers a world of possibilities for adventurous foodies. From the famous Kung Pao chicken to the rich, savory Mapo tofu, Sichuan recipes have the power to transport you to a far-off land, where spices, textures, and aromas blend in a delightful symphony. In this post, we'll delve into five mouth-watering Sichuan recipes that will ignite your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Recipe 1: Spicy Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork Belly The twice-cooked pork belly, or Huiguo Rou, is a staple in Sichuan cuisine. This dish showcases the region's love for bold flavors and generous use of spices. To make this recipe, you'll need the following ingredients:

2 pounds pork belly 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns 2 cloves garlic 1 Scotch bonnet pepper 1 tablespoon Sichuan chili bean paste 2 tablespoons soy sauce 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine 1 tablespoon sugar 1 teaspoon cornstarch 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

Slice the pork belly into thick pieces and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight. In a wok or large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and sauté the garlic and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the marinated pork belly and cook until browned on all sides. Add the Shaoxing wine, soy sauce, sugar, and cornstarch mixture. Stir until combined and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove the pork belly from the wok and set aside. In the same wok, combine the chili bean paste and Scotch bonnet pepper. Stir until the paste is fragrant. Add the cooked pork belly back into the wok and stir-fry until well combined. Serve the dish garnished with cilantro and steamed bok choy.

Recipe 2: Sichuan Eggplant Stir-Fry with Garlic and Chili Peppers This recipe is a masterclass in balancing flavors and textures. The tender eggplant pairs perfectly with the numbing Sichuan peppercorns and the spicy kick from the chili peppers.

2 large eggplants 4 cloves garlic 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns 2 Scotch bonnet peppers 1 tablespoon soy sauce 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 tablespoon cornstarch Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Slice the eggplant into thin strips and set aside. In a wok or large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and sauté the garlic and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the eggplant and cook until tender. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch mixture. Stir until the cornstarch has dissolved. Add the cooked eggplant back into the wok and stir-fry until the sauce has thickened. Add the chili peppers and stir-fry for an additional 2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 3: Spicy Sichuan Kung Pao Chicken Kung Pao chicken is a beloved Sichuan dish that has gained global popularity. This recipe is a light and airy take on the classic, with the addition of tiger nuts for added crunch.

1 pound chicken breast 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns 2 cloves garlic 1/2 cup tiger nuts 1/4 cup soy sauce 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine 1 tablespoon cornstarch 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 2 Scotch bonnet peppers Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Slice the chicken into bite-sized pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes. In a wok or large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and sauté the garlic and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch mixture. Stir until the cornstarch has dissolved. Add the cooked chicken back into the wok and stir-fry until the sauce has thickened. Add the tiger nuts and stir-fry for an additional 2 minutes. Add the Scotch bonnet peppers and stir-fry for an additional minute. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 4: Sichuan Braised Tofu with Chili Peppers and Szechuan Peppercorns This recipe showcases the comforting, warming flavors of Sichuan cuisine. The braised tofu absorbs the rich, savory flavors of the sauce, creating a dish perfect for a chilly evening.

1 block firm tofu 2 cloves garlic 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns 1/4 cup chili peppers 1 tablespoon soy sauce 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 2 tablespoons cornstarch Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Cut the tofu into large cubes and set aside. In a wok or large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and sauté the garlic and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch mixture. Stir until the cornstarch has dissolved. Add the cooked tofu back into the wok and stir-fry until the sauce has thickened. Add the chili peppers and stir-fry for an additional 2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 5: Spicy Sichuan Wonton Soup This recipe is a comforting, soothing take on traditional wonton soup. The spicy kick from the chili peppers adds a delightful twist to this classic dish.

1 package wonton wrappers 1 pound ground pork 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns 2 cloves garlic 1/4 cup soy sauce 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine 1 tablespoon cornstarch 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 2 Scotch bonnet peppers Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns. Mix well. Lay a wonton wrapper on a flat surface and place a small spoonful of the pork mixture in the center. Dip your finger in water and run it along the edges of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges to seal. In a large pot, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch mixture. Stir until the cornstarch has dissolved. Add the wontons to the pot and simmer until cooked through. Add the Scotch bonnet peppers and stir-fry for an additional minute. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

These five recipes offer a glimpse into the world of Sichuan cuisine, where bold flavors, textures, and spices come together to create a culinary experience like no other. Whether you're in the mood for something spicy, comforting, or light and airy, these dishes are sure to tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more.
