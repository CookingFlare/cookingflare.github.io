Stout Stews: Hearty Recipes and Stout Beer Pairings


Stout stews are the epitome of comfort food – hearty, flavorful, and perfect for chilly days. Pairing these robust stews with a rich stout beer can elevate the dining experience to a whole new level. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering stout stew recipes and suggest the perfect stout beer pairings to complement each dish.

Beef and Guinness Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs stewing beef
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 can of Guinness stout
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 4 potatoes, cubed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat and brown the beef in batches. Set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for a minute.
  4. Pour in the Guinness and beef broth, then add the bay leaves.
  5. Return the beef to the pot, cover, and simmer for 1.5 hours.
  6. Add the carrots and potatoes, and simmer for another 30 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Beer Pairing:

  • Pair this rich stew with a classic Guinness stout to complement the deep flavors of the dish. The roasted malt and coffee notes of the stout will enhance the savory beef and root vegetables.

Stout Lamb Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs lamb, cubed
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of stout beer
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 tsp rosemary
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 cups diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a Dutch oven, melt the butter over medium heat and brown the lamb in batches. Set aside.
  2. Sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  3. Return the lamb to the pot and add the stout beer, beef broth, rosemary, and thyme.
  4. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook for 1.5 hours.
  5. Add the diced tomatoes and continue simmering for another 30 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Beer Pairing:

  • Pair this flavorful lamb stew with a robust stout like an Imperial Stout. The dark chocolate and dried fruit notes in the beer will complement the tender lamb and aromatic herbs.

Vegetarian Stout Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained
  • 1 large sweet potato, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of stout beer
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add the sweet potato, bell pepper, and chickpeas, and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Pour in the stout beer and vegetable broth, then add the cumin and paprika.
  4. Simmer the stew for 30-40 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Beer Pairing:

  • Pair this hearty vegetarian stew with a creamy oatmeal stout to complement the earthy flavors of the chickpeas and sweet potato. The smooth mouthfeel of the stout will enhance the overall dining experience.

Stout stews are versatile, comforting, and perfect for sharing with family and friends. Whether you choose a traditional beef stew, a flavorful lamb stew, or a vegetarian option, pairing them with the right stout beer can take your meal to the next level. Experiment with different combinations to find your perfect match and enjoy the hearty goodness of stout stews!
