Homemade Cranberry Orange Bars: A Festive Flavor Combo


As the holiday season approaches, the aroma of freshly baked treats wafts through the air, filling our homes with warmth and joy. Among the many festive flavors, one combination stands out for its tangy sweetness and refreshing zest: cranberry and orange. In this recipe, we’ll explore the magic of homemade cranberry orange bars, a perfect treat to delight your family and friends during the holidays.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon grated orange zest
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare the Pan

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line an 8-inch square baking dish with parchment paper or aluminum foil, leaving some overhang for easy removal.

Step 2: Make the Shortbread Crust

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. Add the softened butter and mix until the dough comes together in a ball. Press the dough evenly into the prepared baking dish.

Step 3: Prepare the Cranberry Filling

In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, orange zest, orange juice, and salt. Add the cranberries and stir until they’re evenly coated with the egg mixture.

Step 4: Assemble the Bars

Pour the cranberry filling over the shortbread crust, spreading it evenly to the edges of the dish.

Step 5: Top with a Crumbly Topping

In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Add the brown sugar and mix until well combined. Sprinkle the crumbly topping evenly over the cranberry filling.

Step 6: Bake and Cool

Bake the bars for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. Remove the dish from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

Step 7: Dust with Confectioners’ Sugar

Once the bars have cooled, dust them with confectioners’ sugar for a touch of elegance.

Tips and Variations

  • To ensure the cranberries retain their color and texture, don’t overmix the filling.
  • If using frozen cranberries, thaw them first and pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  • Substitute the orange zest with lemon or lime zest for a different twist on the flavor combination.
  • Add a sprinkle of chopped pecans or walnuts to the crumbly topping for added texture and crunch.
  • These bars freeze beautifully, so feel free to wrap them individually and store them in an airtight container for up to 2 months.

Conclusion

Homemade cranberry orange bars are a delightful addition to any holiday spread, with their sweet and tangy flavors complemented by the crunch of the shortbread crust and crumbly topping. With this recipe, you’ll be sure to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills. So go ahead, get baking, and make this festive flavor combo a part of your holiday traditions!
