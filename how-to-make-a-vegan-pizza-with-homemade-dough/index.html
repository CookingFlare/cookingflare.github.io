How to Make a Vegan Pizza with Homemade Dough


Making a delicious vegan pizza from scratch is an incredibly satisfying experience. With a soft, crispy crust, rich tomato sauce, and a variety of colorful toppings, vegan pizza can rival any traditional pizza out there. Not only does making your own pizza give you control over the ingredients, but it also ensures that everything is fresh, flavorful, and made exactly to your liking. Plus, by creating a homemade dough, you can take your pizza to the next level and enjoy a healthier, preservative-free alternative.

In this guide, we will walk you through the entire process of making a vegan pizza with homemade dough, from creating the dough to assembling the pizza and baking it to perfection. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients for Homemade Pizza Dough

The first step in making the perfect vegan pizza is crafting the dough. While you can purchase pre-made dough from the store, nothing beats the taste and texture of fresh, homemade dough. Here’s what you’ll need for a basic vegan pizza dough:

For the Dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour (you can also use whole wheat flour for a healthier option)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (you can use maple syrup or agave syrup for a natural sweetener)
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water (around 110°F)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (extra virgin is best for flavor)

For the Sauce and Toppings (Optional):

  • 1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes (look for a brand that doesn’t have added sugar or preservatives)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ cups vegan mozzarella cheese (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh vegetables (such as bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, spinach, or zucchini)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Pizza from Scratch

Step 1: Prepare the Pizza Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, mix the warm water with the sugar and active dry yeast. Stir gently and allow the mixture to sit for about 5 minutes. You should see the yeast foam up and become frothy, which means it’s active and ready to use. If it doesn’t foam, your yeast may be expired or the water too hot, and you’ll need to start over.

Next, in a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, begin to mix the ingredients until a dough starts to form.

Once the dough has come together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and begin kneading it. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky to the touch. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle in a little more flour. Conversely, if it’s too dry, add a small amount of water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together.

Step 2: Let the Dough Rise

Once your dough is ready, place it in a lightly oiled bowl, turning it once to coat all sides in oil. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and place it in a warm area to rise for about 1 to 1 ½ hours. The dough should double in size during this time. If you’re in a hurry, you can also let it rise in the fridge for 24 hours for a deeper, more complex flavor.

Step 3: Prepare the Pizza Sauce

While the dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the sauce. In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and cook for 1-2 minutes to bring out the flavors. Then, add the tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes to the pan. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.

Step 4: Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Pizza Base

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently roll it into your desired shape, whether round or rectangular. Use a rolling pin or your hands to stretch it out, making sure to leave a thicker border around the edges for the crust.

Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a pizza stone or baking sheet inside while it heats up. This ensures that your crust will get crispy and golden once it’s in the oven.

Step 5: Assemble the Vegan Pizza

Once your pizza dough is ready, it’s time to assemble your pizza. Lightly flour a pizza peel or a piece of parchment paper and place the rolled-out dough onto it. Then, spread a generous amount of the prepared pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.

Next, add your vegan mozzarella cheese. You can use store-bought vegan cheese or make your own, depending on your preference. Spread it out evenly over the sauce.

Now, get creative with the toppings! Choose from your favorite vegetables, such as bell peppers, onions, spinach, zucchini, mushrooms, or olives. If you like a bit of heat, sprinkle some red pepper flakes over the top.

Step 6: Bake the Pizza

Carefully slide the prepared pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven. Bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crispy and the cheese has melted and bubbly.

If you like a crispier crust, you can bake the pizza for an extra 2-3 minutes. Keep an eye on the pizza as oven times can vary. Once the pizza is ready, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once your pizza has cooled slightly, use a pizza cutter to slice it into wedges. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and flavor. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips for the Perfect Vegan Pizza

  • Flour Choices: While all-purpose flour works well, you can use whole wheat flour for a more nutritious dough. For a gluten-free option, consider using a gluten-free pizza flour blend.
  • Dough Texture: The texture of your pizza dough is crucial for a crispy crust. If the dough is too sticky, add more flour. If it’s too dry, add more water.
  • Vegan Cheese Alternatives: If you prefer homemade vegan cheese, you can make cashew cheese or almond-based cheese for a more flavorful topping. There are also various store-bought vegan cheese brands that melt beautifully.
  • Preheat the Oven: Make sure to preheat your oven thoroughly before placing the pizza inside. A preheated oven will ensure a perfectly cooked crust.
  • Toppings: Don’t hesitate to get creative with your toppings. From roasted garlic to artichoke hearts, the possibilities are endless. Just be mindful not to overload the pizza with too many toppings, as this can make the crust soggy.

Variations on Vegan Pizza

White Vegan Pizza

If you’re not in the mood for a classic tomato sauce pizza, consider making a white vegan pizza. For this variation, simply skip the tomato sauce and use a garlic-infused olive oil base instead. Top with vegan mozzarella, fresh herbs, spinach, and roasted vegetables. The garlic oil adds a rich, aromatic flavor that pairs wonderfully with the creamy cheese and earthy vegetables.

BBQ Vegan Pizza

For a tangy twist, try making a BBQ vegan pizza. Swap the traditional tomato sauce for barbecue sauce. Top with sautéed mushrooms, red onions, and bell peppers for a smoky, flavorful pie. You can also add vegan protein like tempeh or jackfruit to give it a meaty texture.

Mediterranean Vegan Pizza

A Mediterranean-inspired vegan pizza is full of bright flavors and healthy ingredients. Use hummus or a simple olive tapenade as the base, then top with kalamata olives, artichoke hearts, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and spinach. Finish with a sprinkle of oregano and some fresh lemon juice for extra zing.

Margherita Vegan Pizza

For a classic vegan Margherita pizza, start with a base of tomato sauce, and top with vegan mozzarella and fresh basil leaves. It’s simple, but the fresh ingredients shine through with every bite. You can even drizzle a bit of balsamic reduction on top for added flavor.

Conclusion

Making vegan pizza with homemade dough is an incredibly rewarding process that results in a delicious meal perfect for any occasion. With this step-by-step guide, you can create your own customized pizza that is not only vegan but also free from preservatives and artificial ingredients. Whether you prefer a classic Margherita, a Mediterranean-inspired creation, or something entirely different, the possibilities are endless.

So, roll up your sleeves, get creative, and enjoy the experience of making your very own vegan pizza from scratch. Whether you’re feeding a family, hosting a pizza night with friends, or simply indulging in a personal pizza craving, homemade vegan pizza is sure to impress!
