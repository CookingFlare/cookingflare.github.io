Korean Potato Stew with Pork (Gamja Jorim) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, unique ingredients, and healthy preparations. One dish that truly captures the essence of Korean comfort food is Gamja Jorim – a savory, hearty potato stew with tender pork. This dish combines the rich, umami-packed flavor of pork with the comforting, starchy goodness of potatoes, all simmered in a delicious broth. Perfect for cozy family meals, Gamja Jorim is a staple in Korean households.

In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making this beloved Korean dish from scratch. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a beginner, you’ll find this recipe easy to follow. Let’s dive in!

What Is Gamja Jorim?

Gamja Jorim (감자조림) translates to “braised potatoes” in Korean. It’s a simple yet flavorful dish that showcases the versatility of potatoes. While potatoes are often considered a humble ingredient, when prepared correctly, they can transform into something extraordinary. Gamja Jorim is typically served as a side dish, but it can also be the main event, especially when paired with a bowl of steamed rice.

In this recipe, we will add pork, a common protein in Korean stews, to enhance the depth of the dish. The pork, often pork belly or pork shoulder, provides a rich and savory base for the dish, while the potatoes absorb the flavors during the simmering process, creating a perfectly balanced meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a traditional and flavorful Korean Potato Stew with Pork, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Stew:

  • 4 medium potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes work best)
  • 1 lb (450g) pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium onion, sliced thinly
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cups water (or low-sodium chicken broth for extra flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional, but adds depth of flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or mirin
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

For the Marinade (for the Pork):

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Gamja Jorim

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Start by marinating the pork to allow the flavors to infuse before cooking. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, rice wine or mirin, sesame oil, garlic, ginger (if using), and black pepper. Stir well and toss the pork pieces into the marinade. Let the pork sit for at least 30 minutes, or ideally, overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor absorption.

Step 2: Peel and Slice the Potatoes

While the pork is marinating, peel the potatoes and cut them into bite-sized chunks. You can cut them into rounds, cubes, or wedges, depending on your preference. Just make sure the pieces are uniform in size so that they cook evenly. Once cut, place them in a bowl of water to prevent them from browning.

Step 3: Sauté the Pork

Once the pork has marinated, it’s time to cook it. Heat a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat and add the vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork and cook until it’s browned on all sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes. The goal here is to develop a rich, caramelized exterior on the pork, which will add flavor to the stew.

Once the pork is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside for now.

Step 4: Sauté the Aromatics

In the same pot, add the sesame oil and sliced onions. Sauté the onions for about 3-4 minutes until they become soft and translucent. Add the minced garlic and continue to sauté for another minute until fragrant.

Step 5: Add the Potatoes

Now, add the drained potatoes into the pot with the onions and garlic. Stir to coat the potatoes in the oils and aromatics. Allow them to cook for about 3-4 minutes, giving them a chance to brown slightly and absorb the flavors from the onions and garlic.

Step 6: Build the Stew’s Flavor Base

Once the potatoes have browned a bit, it’s time to add the liquids and seasonings. Pour in the water (or chicken broth for extra flavor), soy sauce, fish sauce (if using), gochugaru, brown sugar, rice wine or mirin, black pepper, and salt. Stir everything together, making sure the potatoes are well-coated in the seasoning mix.

Step 7: Simmer the Stew

Return the browned pork to the pot and stir to combine with the potatoes and the seasoning mixture. Bring the stew to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a lid and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the pork is cooked through.

As the stew simmers, the flavors will meld together, and the potatoes will become infused with the savory broth. Stir occasionally to make sure the potatoes don’t stick to the bottom of the pot.

Step 8: Final Touches

Once the potatoes are soft and the pork is tender, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a slightly thicker broth, you can cook the stew uncovered for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow some of the liquid to evaporate.

Before serving, garnish with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for extra flavor and texture. Serve the stew hot with a bowl of steamed white rice, and enjoy the comforting, savory goodness of this Korean classic.

Tips and Variations

  • Pork Alternatives: While pork belly or pork shoulder is commonly used in this dish, you can also use other cuts of pork, such as pork loin or even ground pork. Each will give the stew a slightly different texture and flavor.

  • Vegetable Additions: Feel free to add other vegetables to the stew for added texture and flavor. Carrots, mushrooms, or even zucchini can be a great addition to the dish.

  • Spice Level: If you prefer a spicier version of Gamja Jorim, you can add more gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) or even some fresh green chilies for a kick of heat.

  • Shortening the Cooking Time: If you’re in a rush, you can also prepare this dish in a pressure cooker or Instant Pot to reduce cooking time. Simply follow the same steps, but cook the dish under high pressure for 10-15 minutes instead of simmering for 30-40 minutes.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

Gamja Jorim is the epitome of Korean comfort food – rich, savory, and satisfying. The tender pork and soft potatoes soak up the flavors of the broth, creating a deeply flavorful stew that will warm you from the inside out. It’s a great dish to prepare for family dinners or even meal prep for the week ahead. And the best part? It’s so easy to make!

Whether you’re a Korean food enthusiast or new to the cuisine, this Korean Potato Stew with Pork is bound to become a regular in your recipe rotation. Give it a try, and let the delicious aroma fill your kitchen!

Serving Suggestions

  • With Steamed Rice: As with many Korean stews, Gamja Jorim is best served with a side of steamed rice. The rice helps balance the flavors and soak up the rich broth.
  • Kimchi on the Side: Korean meals are often served with kimchi, which adds a perfect tangy, spicy contrast to the hearty stew. A side of kimchi will elevate the flavors and provide a balance of textures.
  • Pickled Vegetables: For an additional side dish, try serving pickled vegetables like pickled radish (mu) or cucumber kimchi to add a refreshing crunch to the meal.

Conclusion

Gamja Jorim is a deliciously savory, hearty Korean dish that combines tender pork, soft potatoes, and a flavorful broth. Whether you are new to Korean cuisine or a seasoned cook, this easy-to-follow recipe is sure to become a family favorite. The best part about this dish is that it brings together simple ingredients to create a complex, satisfying meal that warms the soul.

With just a few basic ingredients, you can prepare this comforting stew and experience the heart of Korean home-cooked meals. Enjoy this tasty treat with your loved ones, and embrace the comforting flavors of Korean Potato Stew with Pork.
