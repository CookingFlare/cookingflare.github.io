Simple Chinese Ground Pork and Green Bean Stir-Fry


Chinese cuisine has always had a reputation for transforming humble ingredients into something spectacular. One of the many gems of Chinese home cooking is the ground pork and green bean stir-fry. It’s a quick, savory, and utterly satisfying dish that’s perfect for busy weeknights or laid-back dinners. Made with fresh green beans, flavorful ground pork, and aromatic seasonings, this stir-fry proves that simplicity in the kitchen can lead to culinary excellence.

Ground pork is a versatile protein, pairing effortlessly with a variety of vegetables and seasonings. When combined with tender-crisp green beans, rich soy sauce, and fragrant garlic, it creates a dish that feels both wholesome and indulgent. Here’s everything you need to know about crafting your own simple Chinese ground pork and green bean stir-fry at home.

Why Ground Pork and Green Beans Are a Perfect Pair

At the heart of this dish is the harmonious marriage of texture and flavor. Ground pork, when cooked properly, offers a robust umami taste and a slightly chewy texture, while green beans provide a refreshing crunch and natural sweetness. Together, they create a delightful contrast that’s full-bodied yet light on the palate.

Aside from their complementary flavors, these ingredients are also widely accessible. Ground pork is readily available in most supermarkets and tends to be budget-friendly. Green beans, on the other hand, are a nutrient-packed vegetable that promotes balance in this protein-heavy dish. Both are easy to prepare, making this recipe ideal for beginner cooks or those short on time.

Ingredients You’ll Need

This recipe uses straightforward ingredients that are staples of Chinese cooking. They’re the building blocks of umami-rich dishes, and together, they produce a stir-fry that’s bursting with flavor. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 250g ground pork: The main protein, tender and flavorful.
  • 200g green beans: Trim off the ends and cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil: Neutral oils like canola or sunflower are best for stir-frying.
  • 1 medium onion: Thinly sliced for added sweetness.
  • 3 cloves garlic: Minced to infuse the dish with aromatic depth.
  • 1 small piece of ginger (about 1 inch): Grated or minced for a zingy note.

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce: Adds saltiness and depth.
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce: Provides a richer, slightly sweet flavor.
  • 1 tsp sesame oil: A small touch to enhance the fragrance.
  • 1 tsp sugar: Balances the savory elements.
  • 1 pinch of white pepper: Optional, but highly recommended for subtle warmth.

Optional Garnishes:

  • Thinly sliced scallions: For a pop of freshness.
  • Sesame seeds: Sprinkle for a bit of crunch.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep Your Ingredients

Start by preparing the green beans. Rinse them under cold water, trim off the ends, and cut them into bite-sized pieces. Thinly slice your onion and mince the garlic and ginger. Ground pork doesn’t require much preparation, but ensure it’s at room temperature for even cooking.

For the sauce, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and white pepper in a small bowl. Stir well and set it aside. Having the sauce ready before you start cooking will make the process seamless and prevent last-minute scrambling.

2. Preheat Your Pan or Wok

Heat a large pan or wok on medium-high heat. Once hot, add the vegetable oil and swirl it around to coat the surface. Woks are particularly great for stir-frying since their high sides distribute heat evenly, but any large skillet will work just fine.

3. Cook the Green Beans

Throw the green beans into the hot pan and stir-fry them for 3–5 minutes until they’re tender-crisp. You’ll want a slight char on them to enhance their natural sweetness. Once cooked, remove the beans from the pan and set them aside.

4. Brown the Ground Pork

Add another drizzle of vegetable oil to the pan if needed and toss in the ground pork. Break it apart with a wooden spoon or spatula as it cooks to prevent clumping. Stir-fry for about 5–7 minutes or until the pork is fully browned and starts developing crisp edges. Browning the meat properly is key to maximizing its flavor.

5. Incorporate Aromatics

Lower the heat slightly and add the minced garlic, ginger, and sliced onions to the pan with the cooked pork. Stir everything together for about 1–2 minutes, allowing the aromatics to bloom and infuse the dish with their rich fragrance.

6. Combine Pork, Green Beans, and Sauce

Return the green beans to the pan and pour the prepared sauce over everything. Toss well to coat each piece in the savory mixture. Let the stir-fry simmer for another minute to allow the flavors to meld.

7. Garnish and Serve

Transfer the stir-fry to a serving dish and garnish with scallions and sesame seeds if desired. Serve hot alongside steamed white rice for a wholesome, complete meal.

Tips for Perfect Stir-Fry Execution

High Heat, Fast Cooking

Stir-frying is all about quick cooking at high heat. Make sure your pan or wok is preheated before you add the oil. This ensures your ingredients cook evenly without steaming.

Mise en Place is Your Friend

Have all your ingredients ready to go before you start cooking. Stir-frying moves fast, and you won’t have time to chop or prepare mid-process.

Adjust Seasonings to Taste

Every palate is different, so feel free to tweak the sauce. If you prefer a sweeter profile, add a touch more sugar. For extra savoriness, a dash more soy sauce will do the trick.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

What makes this simple Chinese ground pork and green bean stir-fry a standout is its versatility. It’s a perfect one-pan dish, minimizing kitchen cleanup while delivering bold, layered flavors. The combination of tender pork, crisp green beans, and savory sauce is universally pleasing, making it suitable for family meals or solo dinners.

You can easily customize the recipe by swapping proteins or vegetables. Ground chicken or turkey offer lighter alternatives to pork, while vegetables like broccoli, snap peas, or bell peppers can substitute for green beans. No matter how you adjust it, the essence of the dish remains intact: Fresh ingredients cooked fast with bold seasonings.

Wrap-Up

The joy of cooking lies in transforming simple ingredients into something extraordinary, and this Chinese ground pork and green bean stir-fry exemplifies that philosophy. Whether you’re an experienced chef or a novice in the kitchen, this dish is approachable, satisfying, and endlessly adaptable. It’s a flavor-packed reminder that you don’t need a long list of ingredients or hours of preparation to create a memorable meal.

Try this recipe tonight and see how easy it is to bring the magic of Chinese home cooking to your dinner table. You’ll quickly discover why ground pork and green bean stir-fry is a beloved dish you’ll want to make again and again.
