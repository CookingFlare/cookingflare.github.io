Easy and Delicious Christmas Cookie Recipes


The holiday season is synonymous with warmth, joy, and the irresistible aroma of freshly baked cookies. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just getting started, Christmas offers the perfect excuse to dive into the world of festive treats. This post shares some of the easiest and most delicious Christmas cookie recipes that will fill your kitchen with festive cheer and satisfy every sweet tooth. From classic buttery shortbreads to spiced delights, these recipes are designed to be manageable, flavorful, and perfect for sharing—with family, friends, or even the occasional unexpected guest!

The Magic of Christmas Cookies: More Than Just a Recipe

Before diving into the recipes, it’s worth appreciating what makes Christmas cookies so special. Beyond their delightful taste, they’re a tradition steeped in nostalgia and community. Each cookie carries the story of holidays past and the laughter of family gatherings. The best cookies not only taste amazing but bring people together, creating lasting memories.

Let’s explore some incredible cookie recipes whose simplicity and flavor will make them staples in your holiday baking repertoire.

1. Classic Melt-in-Your-Mouth Shortbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, cream the softened butter until smooth and fluffy. Add the powdered sugar and continue beating until well combined. Mix in vanilla extract.
  2. Incorporate dry ingredients: Gradually add flour and salt, mixing just until a soft dough forms. Avoid overmixing or the cookies may become tough.
  3. Shape and chill: Shape dough into a log, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for an hour. This step makes slicing easier.
  4. Slice and bake: Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C). Slice the dough into ¼-inch thick rounds and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Bake: Bake for 15-18 minutes or until edges are lightly golden. Allow cookies to cool on the baking sheet briefly before transferring to a wire rack.

Why try this recipe? These buttery cookies are a crowd-pleaser with their tender crumb and simple flavor. You can dress them up with a dusting of powdered sugar, a drizzle of melted chocolate, or even dip half in festive sprinkles.

2. Gingerbread Men: Spiced and Playful

Ingredients:

  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup molasses
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter and sugar: Beat together butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg and molasses, mixing well.
  2. Combine dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, sift flour, baking soda, spices, and salt together.
  3. Form the dough: Gradually add dry ingredients to the wet mixture until you form a stiff dough. Divide into two parts and wrap in plastic wrap to chill for at least two hours.
  4. Roll and cut shapes: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out dough on a floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness. Use gingerbread men cutters to shape.
  5. Bake: Place onto baking sheets and bake for 8-10 minutes. Let cool completely.
  6. Decorate: Use icing, candies, or edible markers to bring your gingerbread men to life.

Fun fact: Crafting gingerbread men is often a family affair, allowing creativity and storytelling to flow alongside baking.

3. Snickerdoodles with Cinnamon Sugar Crust

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Cinnamon Sugar Coating:

  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough: Cream butter with sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Combine: Gradually add dry ingredients to wet, mixing until combined.
  4. Shape and coat: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Mix cinnamon and sugar in a shallow bowl. Roll dough into 1-inch balls and then roll each ball in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
  5. Bake: Place balls on baking sheet, spaced apart, and bake for 8-10 minutes. Let cool completely.

This recipe’s combination of tangy cream of tartar and cinnamon sugar coating gives a signature tang and delightful crunch.

4. Peppermint Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • ½ cup powdered sugar for rolling

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: Combine flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Combine wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk sugar, oil, eggs, and peppermint extract until smooth.
  3. Form dough: Gradually add dry ingredients to wet, mixing to form a thick batter. Cover and chill for at least 2 hours.
  4. Prepare to bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll dough into small balls, then coat thoroughly in powdered sugar.
  5. Bake: Place on baking sheets and bake for 10-12 minutes. The cookies will crackle, revealing the chocolate beneath.

These crinkle cookies perfectly blend rich chocolate with refreshing peppermint—ideal for a festive touch.

5. Easy No-Bake Snowball Cookies

For those with limited oven time or who want an extra treat for cookie exchanges, no-bake snowball cookies are a lifesaver.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar, divided
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (pecans or walnuts)

Instructions:

  1. Mix butter and sugar: Cream butter with 1 cup powdered sugar until fluffy. Stir in vanilla.
  2. Add flour and nuts: Gradually add flour and mix until combined, then fold in nuts.
  3. Shape cookies: Roll dough into 1-inch balls.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate balls for 30 minutes to firm up.
  5. Coat: Roll chilled balls in remaining powdered sugar until well coated.

These melt-in-your-mouth bites are called snowballs because their powdered sugar coating resembles freshly fallen snow.

Tips for Perfect Christmas Cookies Every Time

  • Measure precisely: Baking is a science; using a kitchen scale or leveling cups ensures consistent results.
  • Room temperature ingredients: Butter and eggs mix better when not chilled, providing better dough texture.
  • Don’t overbake: Cookies continue to cook slightly on the hot tray after removal. The moment they turn golden on the edges is ideal.
  • Use parchment paper: Prevent cookies from sticking, reducing cleanup and preserving delicate shapes.
  • Chill dough when needed: Many holiday cookie recipes benefit from chilling to improve texture and handling.

Wrapping Up Your Holiday Baking Adventure

Trying your hand at these easy and delicious Christmas cookie recipes guarantees a batch of festive happiness delivered fresh from your oven. Whether you’re wrapping them up as gifts, enjoying a quiet afternoon with a warm drink, or creating fun memories with loved ones, there’s something magical about homemade cookies during the holidays.

With these recipes in your baking arsenal, you can enjoy a varied spread—from classic buttery shortbread to peppermint-infused chocolaty bites. The best part? These cookies are forgiving enough for beginners but satisfying enough for experienced bakers aiming to impress.

So, preheat your oven, line your trays, and step into a world where butter, sugar, and spice become holiday joy on a plate. Ready, set, bake!

Happy holidays and happy baking!
