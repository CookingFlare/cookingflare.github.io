The Best Seafood Appetizers: 10 Recipes for Ocean Lovers


When it comes to seafood, there’s nothing quite like the thrill of biting into a succulent piece of fish, a juicy shrimp, or a tender scallop. But let’s face it, sometimes the main event can be a bit overwhelming, especially for those who are new to the world of seafood. That’s where appetizers come in – they’re the perfect way to ease your way into a seafood-filled meal. In this post, we’ll explore the best seafood appetizers that are sure to delight even the most discerning palates.

Recipe 1: Steamed Mussels with Garlic and Lemon

Mussels are a classic seafood staple, and when steamed with garlic and lemon, they become a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the mussels under cold water and remove any broken shells.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the mussels, lemon juice, white wine, and chicken broth to the pot. Cover with a lid and steam for 3-5 minutes, or until the mussels are open.
  4. Serve the mussels in bowls, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.

Recipe 2: Grilled Shrimp Skewers with Spicy Mango Salsa

Shrimp is another popular seafood option that’s easy to grill and pair with a variety of flavors. Here’s a recipe that combines succulent shrimp with spicy mango salsa:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup red onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10 bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the shrimp onto the skewers, leaving a small space between each shrimp.
  3. Brush the shrimp with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, combine the mango, jalapeño, red onion, and cilantro in a bowl. Squeeze the lime juice over the top and stir to combine.
  6. Serve the grilled shrimp skewers with the spicy mango salsa spooned over the top.

Recipe 3: Crab and Avocado Bruschetta

Crab and avocado are a match made in heaven, and when combined with toasted bread and a squeeze of lemon, they become a true indulgence. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1/2 cup lump crab meat, picked over for shells
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 baguette, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the avocado, crab meat, lemon juice, garlic, and parsley. Stir to combine.
  3. Arrange the bread slices on a baking sheet and toast for 5-7 minutes, or until lightly browned.
  4. Spoon the crab and avocado mixture over the toasted bread and top with Parmesan cheese if desired.

Recipe 4: Pan-Seared Scallops with Lemon Butter

Scallops are a delicate seafood option that can be easily overcooked, but when pan-seared with lemon butter, they become a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the scallops under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. In a large skillet, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the scallops to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and remaining 1 tablespoon of butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the scallops hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.

Recipe 5: Oysters Rockefeller

Oysters are a classic seafood appetizer that can be easily dressed up with a variety of flavors. Here’s a recipe for Oysters Rockefeller that combines the brininess of oysters with the richness of spinach and garlic:

Ingredients:

  • 1 dozen oysters, scrubbed and patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the butter, garlic, and spinach. Stir to combine.
  3. Arrange the oysters on a baking sheet and spoon the spinach butter mixture over the top of each oyster.
  4. Sprinkle the Parmesan cheese over the oysters and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Bake the oysters for 10-12 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve the oysters hot, garnished with a sprinkle of parsley if desired.

Recipe 6: Grilled Octopus with Chimichurri Sauce

Octopus is a seafood option that’s often overlooked, but when grilled with a tangy chimichurri sauce, it becomes a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound octopus, cleaned and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh oregano, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the octopus pieces onto skewers, leaving a small space between each piece.
  3. Brush the octopus with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill the octopus for 2-3 minutes per side, or until charred and cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, combine the garlic, parsley, oregano, and red wine vinegar in a bowl. Stir to combine.
  6. Serve the grilled octopus with the chimichurri sauce spooned over the top.

Recipe 7: Crispy Fried Calamari with Marinara Sauce

Calamari is a seafood option that’s often overlooked, but when fried crispy and served with a tangy marinara sauce, it becomes a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound calamari, cleaned and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, and Parmesan cheese. Stir to combine.
  2. Dredge the calamari pieces in the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  3. Fry the calamari in hot oil for 2-3 minutes, or until crispy and golden brown.
  4. Drain the calamari on paper towels and serve with marinara sauce spooned over the top.

Recipe 8: Seared Tuna with Soy-Ginger Glaze

Tuna is a seafood option that’s often overlooked, but when seared and glazed with a sweet and savory soy-ginger glaze, it becomes a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound tuna, cut into 1-inch thick slices
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and ginger. Stir to combine.
  3. Brush the tuna slices with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Heat a skillet over high heat and sear the tuna for 1-2 minutes per side, or until cooked through.
  5. Brush the soy-ginger glaze over the tuna and serve hot.

Recipe 9: Steamed Clams with White Wine and Garlic

Clams are a seafood option that’s often overlooked, but when steamed with white wine and garlic, they become a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds clams, scrubbed and debearded
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  2. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the clams, white wine, and chicken broth to the pot. Cover with a lid and steam for 3-5 minutes, or until the clams are open.

Recipe 10: Lobster and Shrimp Cakes with Remoulade Sauce

Lobster and shrimp cakes are a seafood option that’s often overlooked, but when paired with a tangy remoulade sauce, they become a true delight. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lobster meat, diced
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup remoulade sauce (see below)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Remoulade Sauce:

  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the lobster meat, shrimp, panko breadcrumbs, and Parmesan cheese. Stir to combine.
  2. Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and shape each portion into a patty.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium heat and add the butter. Cook the patties for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  4. Serve the lobster and shrimp cakes with remoulade sauce spooned over the top.

These seafood appetizers are sure to delight even the most discerning palates. Whether you’re a seafood lover or just looking to try something new, these recipes are sure to impress. So go ahead, give them a try, and discover the world of seafood in a whole new way.
