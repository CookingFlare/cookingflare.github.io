Savory Spinach and Feta Pie: A Crowd-Pleasing Easter Dish


Easter is just around the corner, and you’re probably looking for a show-stopping dish to impress your family and friends. Look no further! Today, we’re going to share a recipe for a savory spinach and feta pie that’s sure to become a new Easter tradition.

This pie is a masterclass in simplicity and elegance. With a flaky pastry crust, a creamy spinach and feta filling, and a sprinkle of fresh herbs on top, it’s a dish that’s sure to impress even the most discerning palate. And the best part? It’s incredibly easy to make!

To start, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). While that’s heating up, let’s get started on the filling. In a large skillet, heat a couple of tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add in a couple of cloves of minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Next, add in a couple of cups of fresh spinach leaves and cook until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg, then set aside to cool.

In a large bowl, combine the cooled spinach mixture with a block of crumbled feta cheese, a couple of tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley, and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese. Mix until well combined, then set aside.

Now it’s time to make the pastry crust. In a large bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter, and a pinch of salt. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Gradually add in 1/4 cup of cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Once the dough has chilled, roll it out to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Transfer the dough to a 9-inch (23 cm) pie dish and trim the edges to fit.

Fill the pie crust with the spinach and feta mixture, then sprinkle with a bit more feta cheese and a sprinkle of fresh parsley. Roll out the remaining dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm), then use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

Brush the top of the pie with a beaten egg and sprinkle with a bit more grated Parmesan cheese. Bake the pie for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is hot and bubbly.

And that’s it! Your savory spinach and feta pie is ready to impress. Slice it up and serve it with a green salad or a side of roasted vegetables for a delicious and satisfying Easter dinner.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the pie more substantial, add some cooked chicken or bacon to the filling.
  • Use different types of cheese, such as goat cheese or ricotta, for a unique flavor.
  • Add some dried herbs, such as thyme or oregano, to the filling for extra depth of flavor.
  • Make the pie ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze it until ready to bake.

With its flaky pastry crust, creamy spinach and feta filling, and fresh herbs on top, this savory spinach and feta pie is sure to become a new Easter tradition. So why not give it a try? Your family and friends will thank you!
