Authentic Korean Kimchi Pancake (Kimchijeon) Recipe


Kimchi Pancake, known as “Kimchijeon” in Korea, is a beloved savory dish that combines the tangy flavors of kimchi with a crispy golden crust. It’s a staple in Korean cuisine that’s perfect as a snack, appetizer, or side dish to complement a hearty meal. The recipe is straightforward and uses ingredients readily found in most kitchens, making it an excellent introduction to Korean cooking.

Kimchijeon is packed with bold flavors, thanks to its main ingredient—fermented napa cabbage kimchi. The natural umami and spiciness of kimchi balance beautifully with the crispy texture of the pancake. Whether you’re a dedicated fan of Korean food or new to these tantalizing flavors, this recipe ensures you can recreate an authentic taste experience at home.

What Makes Kimchi Pancake Special?

Kimchi Pancake isn’t just a delicious dish—it’s part of Korea’s vibrant culinary history. Traditionally, it was a practical way to use up old, extra-fermented kimchi that might be too pungent for eating as-is. In the Korean household, nothing goes to waste, and kimchi pancakes transform aged kimchi into something utterly magical.

Perfect for both casual meals and celebrations, Kimchijeon is quick to prepare while delivering impressive flavors. Best served hot and crispy straight from the pan, it’s loved by many for its versatility, simplicity, and bold, addictive taste.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Kimchi Pancakes

You don’t need a long list of ingredients to make authentic Kimchijeon. With the essentials listed below, you can whip up a batch in no time:

Core Ingredients

  • 1 cup well-fermented napa cabbage kimchi (preferably homemade or store-bought varieties)
  • 2 tablespoons kimchi juice (optional but enhances flavor)
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup potato starch or cornstarch (for extra crispiness)
  • ½ cup ice-cold water
  • 1 scallion (green onion), finely sliced
  • ½ teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Neutral cooking oil (e.g., vegetable oil or grapeseed oil) for pan-frying

Optional Garnishes or Enhancements

  • Thinly sliced chili peppers for extra heat
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Soy-based dipping sauce

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Kimchi

Start by chopping your fermented napa cabbage kimchi into bite-sized pieces. This allows the savory juices to mingle well with the pancake batter when it cooks, ensuring every bite is packed with flavor. If your kimchi comes with additional vegetables like radish or scallion, include them for extra texture.

For the best results, use kimchi that has been fermented for several weeks. The deeper the fermentation, the richer the taste of your pancake.

Step 2: Mix the Batter

In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and potato starch (or cornstarch). Starches help create a crisp exterior while the flour provides structure to the batter.

Add water and stir gently until you reach a smooth batter. The texture should be neither too runny nor too thick—it needs to barely coat the kimchi pieces evenly. Incorporate the chopped kimchi and any kimchi juice at this stage, stirring lightly to distribute the flavors.

Throw in the sliced scallion and half a teaspoon of sugar to counteract the sourness of the kimchi, if desired. For a spicier kick, mix in thinly sliced chili peppers.

Step 3: Heat the Pan

The secret to achieving a crispy Kimchijeon lies in using a well-heated skillet. Place a non-stick pan or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Pour in enough neutral oil to coat the bottom of the pan generously.

Wait until the oil is heated and shimmering—it should be hot enough that a small test droplet of batter sizzles immediately.

Step 4: Cook the Pancake

Pour the batter onto the center of the pan, allowing it to spread outward naturally. Use the back of a ladle or spatula to gently even out the thickness, aiming for a pancake roughly ¼ to ½ inch thick.

Let it cook undisturbed for 3-5 minutes or until the bottom turns golden and crisp. Flip the pancake carefully—using two spatulas might make it easier to maintain its structure. Cook the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes.

For extra crispiness, consider flipping it back and forth once more for 1-2 minutes on each side.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once cooked, transfer the Kimchijeon to a cutting board and slice it into wedges if you’re sharing with friends or family. For individual servings, leave it whole for a visually striking presentation.

Serve with a simple soy dipping sauce made of soy sauce, rice vinegar, a touch of sesame oil, and mirin, combined with minced garlic, scallions, or chili flakes. Pair your crispy Kimchijeon with a side of fresh salad or even rice for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfect Kimchi Pancakes

  1. Cold Water Is Key: Cold water in the batter helps create a lighter pancake that crisps nicely around the edges.

  2. Get the Right Consistency: Balance the liquid and dry ingredients for a batter that’s not overly thick—this ensures the pancake cooks evenly while crisping around the edges.

  3. Use Well-Fermented Kimchi: Fresh kimchi will lack the deep flavors needed for an authentic taste. Look for kimchi that’s tangy and deeply fermented.

  4. Be Generous with Oil: A good amount of oil is essential for achieving the crisp, golden exterior. You’re pan-frying, not deep-frying, so don’t skimp.

  5. Experiment with Add-Ins: While scallions and chili peppers are great classics, you can make it your own by adding shrimp, squid, or even grated vegetables like zucchini and carrots.

Why Make Kimchijeon at Home?

Making Kimchi Pancakes at home is a rewarding culinary experience that gives full control over the ingredients. You can adjust the spice level, crispness, and additional toppings to fit your personal preferences. Plus, it’s an affordable dish using pantry staples and leftover kimchi.

Whether it’s your first attempt or your hundredth batch, Kimchijeon is a dish that brings comfort, flavor, and a touch of Korean tradition directly to your kitchen.

Quick Troubleshooting for Kimchijeon

  • Pancake Sticking to the Pan: If the pancake sticks, ensure your skillet is preheated and greased sufficiently. Use a non-stick pan or a seasoned cast-iron skillet for optimal results.
  • Pancake is Too Soft or Soggy: A soggy or limp pancake can be due to excess liquid in the batter. Aim for a balance between dry and wet ingredients, and ensure the batter isn’t overly runny.
  • Edges Aren’t Crispy Enough: Increase the cooking temperature slightly and use cold water in the batter. Don’t be afraid to use a bit more oil.

Kimchi Pancakes are not just a recipe but a celebration of Korean food culture. By perfecting this dish at home, you connect with centuries of tradition while delighting your taste buds—and maybe even sparking curiosity for other Korean dishes. Now, grab your apron and a jar of kimchi, and enjoy bringing this authentic recipe to life!
