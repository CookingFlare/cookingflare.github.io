Classic Apple Pie Recipe with a Perfect Flaky Crust

  2. Gradually add the ice water, starting with ¼ cup, and mix until the dough comes together. Add more water if needed, but be careful not to overmix.
  3. Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape each into a disc. Wrap them in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of the dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to a 9-inch pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides.
  2. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust, making sure to distribute it evenly. Dot the top of the filling with small pieces of butter.
  3. Roll out the second disc of dough into a 12-inch circle. Place it over the filling and seal the edges by crimping or pressing them together with a fork.
  4. Optional: Cut decorative vents or a lattice pattern on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

Step 4: Bake the Pie

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  2. Reduce the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue baking for another 40 to 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  3. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil to prevent burning.
  4. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before serving.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Slice the classic apple pie and serve it warm or at room temperature.
  2. For an extra treat, serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different apple varieties such as Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Golden Delicious for a unique flavor profile.
  • Add a sprinkle of cinnamon sugar on top of the pie crust before baking to give it a sweet and crunchy finish.
  • For a gluten-free version, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend and ensure all other ingredients are gluten-free.

So there you have it, a classic apple pie recipe with a perfect flaky crust. Whether you’re baking it for a special occasion or just to satisfy your sweet tooth, this recipe is sure to impress. Enjoy the warm, comforting flavors of tender apples and buttery crust with every bite. Happy baking!
