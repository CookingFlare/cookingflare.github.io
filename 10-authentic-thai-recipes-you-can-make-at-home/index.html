10 Authentic Thai Recipes You Can Make at Home


Thai cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, colors, and textures, celebrated for its balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, these ten authentic Thai recipes will transport you to the bustling streets of Thailand. Each recipe is designed to be approachable, allowing you to recreate the magic of Thai cooking in your own home.

1. Pad Thai

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes or until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown.
  3. Push the tofu to one side of the pan and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble until cooked, then mix with the tofu.
  4. Add the soaked noodles, fish sauce, tamarind paste, and sugar. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until everything is well combined.
  5. Toss in the bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for an additional minute.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with crushed peanuts and lime wedges.

2. Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger)
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the broth to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Simmer for 5-10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook for another 3 minutes.
  3. Stir in the shrimp and chilies, cooking until the shrimp turns pink.
  4. Season with fish sauce and lime juice. Adjust to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

3. Green Curry Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup eggplant, diced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook until no longer pink.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, stirring to combine.
  4. Add the eggplant and bell peppers, simmering until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce and sugar. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

4. Som Tum (Green Papaya Salad)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups green papaya, shredded
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup green beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • Crushed peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, and green beans.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies, fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar until well mixed.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Serve chilled, garnished with crushed peanuts.
