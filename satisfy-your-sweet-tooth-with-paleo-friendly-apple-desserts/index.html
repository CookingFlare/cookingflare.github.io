Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth with Paleo-Friendly Apple Desserts


When it comes to satisfying your sweet tooth, there’s nothing quite like indulging in a delicious dessert. But what if you’re following a paleo diet and trying to avoid refined sugars and grains? Fear not, because we’ve got you covered with these delectable paleo-friendly apple desserts that will have you coming back for more.

Apple Crisp Delight

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, chopped pecans, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix well until a crumbly texture forms.
  3. Place the sliced apples in a baking dish and evenly sprinkle the crumbly mixture on top.
  4. Bake for 30 minutes or until the apples are tender and the top is golden brown.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  6. Serve warm and enjoy the delectable flavors of this paleo-friendly apple crisp!

Cinnamon Apple Muffins

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes | Servings: 12

Ingredients

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 medium-sized apples, peeled and grated

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, baking soda, ground cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until well combined.
  5. Fold in the grated apples until evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each liner about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the muffins cool for a few minutes before serving.
  9. Enjoy these moist and flavorful cinnamon apple muffins as a delightful paleo-friendly treat!

Apple Pie Energy Balls

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 10 minutes | Servings: 12

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pitted dates
  • 1 cup dried apples
  • 1 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup almond butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the pitted dates, dried apples, almonds, almond butter, ground cinnamon, vanilla extract, and salt.
  2. Pulse until all the ingredients are well combined and the mixture forms a sticky dough.
  3. Roll the dough into bite-sized balls, about 1 inch in diameter.
  4. Place the energy balls in an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to firm up.
  5. Serve chilled and enjoy these apple pie energy balls as a delicious and nutritious paleo-friendly snack!

Conclusion

There you have it, three mouthwatering paleo-friendly apple desserts that will satisfy your sweet tooth without derailing your dietary goals. Whether you’re a fan of warm apple crisp, fluffy cinnamon apple muffins, or energy-packed apple pie energy balls, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds. So go ahead, indulge in these guilt-free treats and enjoy the natural sweetness of apples in all their delectable forms!
