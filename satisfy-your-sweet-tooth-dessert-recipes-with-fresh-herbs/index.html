Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth: Dessert Recipes with Fresh Herbs


Who says herbs are only for savory dishes? The vibrant flavors and aromatic scents of fresh herbs can also take your desserts to a whole new level. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious dessert recipes that incorporate the goodness of fresh herbs. Get ready to satisfy your sweet tooth with these unique and flavorful treats!

1. Basil Lemon Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, finely chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Additional powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, softened butter, and powdered sugar. Mix until the ingredients are well incorporated and form a crumbly texture.
  3. Press the mixture into the greased baking dish to form an even crust layer. Bake for about 15-18 minutes or until golden brown.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, lemon juice, basil, lemon zest, and salt until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture over the baked crust and return the dish to the oven. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes or until the filling is set.
  6. Remove from the oven and allow the bars to cool completely in the dish. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  7. Cut into squares and dust with powdered sugar before serving. Enjoy the refreshing combination of basil and lemon in every bite!

2. Lavender Honey Panna Cotta

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 teaspoon dried culinary lavender
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons gelatin powder
  • 2 tablespoons cold water

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream, honey, dried lavender, and vanilla extract over medium heat. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, then remove from heat.
  2. Let the cream mixture infuse for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  3. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin powder over the cold water. Let it sit for a few minutes to bloom.
  4. After the cream mixture has infused, strain out the lavender using a fine-mesh sieve, and return the mixture to the saucepan.
  5. Heat the cream mixture again over low heat until hot, but not boiling. Add the bloomed gelatin and stir until fully dissolved.
  6. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Pour the mixture into serving glasses or ramekins. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until set.
  8. Serve chilled and garnish with a sprig of fresh lavender, if desired. The delicate blend of lavender and honey will leave you wanting more!

3. Rosemary Olive Oil Cake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and fresh rosemary.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the granulated sugar and eggs until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the milk, olive oil, vanilla extract, and lemon zest to the sugar and egg mixture. Mix until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing just until incorporated.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. Dust with powdered sugar before serving, if desired. The subtle hint of rosemary adds a unique twist to this classic cake.

Conclusion

Fresh herbs can elevate your dessert game by adding unexpected flavors and delightful aromas. From the zesty Basil Lemon Bars to the delicate Lavender Honey Panna Cotta, and the aromatic Rosemary Olive Oil Cake, these dessert recipes will satisfy your sweet tooth while showcasing the versatility of herbs in baking. Give them a try and indulge in these herb-infused delights that are sure to impress your family and friends. Enjoy the perfect balance of sweetness and herbal goodness in every bite!

Note: Remember to always use fresh herbs for the best results in these recipes.
