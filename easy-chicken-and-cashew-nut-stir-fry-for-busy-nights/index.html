Easy Chicken and Cashew Nut Stir Fry for Busy Nights


When the clock is ticking and you need a meal that’s both quick and satisfying, an easy chicken and cashew nut stir fry can become your go-to dinner. This dish is packed with protein, nutrition, vibrant colors, and a satisfying crunch that will keep you coming back for more. What’s more, it’s perfect for busy weeknights because it comes together in under 30 minutes without any complicated steps or hard-to-find ingredients.

In this post, I’m going to walk you through a flavorful stir fry recipe that balances tender chicken pieces with crisp vegetables and the rich, buttery texture of cashew nuts. You don’t need to be a wok master to pull this off. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, a family dinner, or meal prepping for the week, this dish is approachable, versatile, and downright delicious.

Why Chicken and Cashew Nut Stir Fry Works

Cooking a balanced stir fry like this one works well for several reasons:

  • Protein plus crunch: Chicken breast is lean and cooks quickly, while cashew nuts add a satisfying texture and healthy fats.
  • Vibrant vegetables: A variety of colorful veggies not only enhance the dish’s visual appeal but also boost its nutritional profile with vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
  • Simple sauces: A straightforward sauce with soy, oyster sauce, and a hint of sweetness keeps the flavor exciting without overwhelming the palate.
  • Speed: The ingredients cook fast, making it feasible for weeknight dinners.
  • Customizable: Easily adjust vegetables, protein type, and spice level according to your preferences or what you have on hand.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s what to gather before you start. The quantities can be adjusted depending on how many servings you want.

For the Stir Fry

  • 2 medium chicken breasts (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup raw cashew nuts (unsalted preferred)
  • 1 medium red bell pepper (sliced thinly)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot (julienned or sliced thin)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (grated or finely chopped)
  • 3 spring onions (cut into 2-inch segments)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (or any neutral oil suitable for high heat)

For the Sauce

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce (use low sodium for a healthier option)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth or water
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp chili flakes (if you like it spicy)

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Marinade the Chicken

Lightly salt the chicken pieces and let them sit while preparing the other ingredients. You can add a splash of soy sauce or a pinch of white pepper if you want a bit more flavor upfront, but keep it simple.

2. Make the Sauce Mixture

In a small mixing bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, vinegar, honey, sesame oil, and chicken broth. Stir well until the honey dissolves. Set aside.

3. Prep the Veggies and Nuts

Wash and cut your vegetables as described above. Toast the cashew nuts in a dry pan over medium heat for about 3–4 minutes until they turn golden and fragrant. Remove and set aside to prevent burning.

4. Stir Fry Time

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add the grated ginger and garlic, stir for 15 seconds to release the aroma.
  • Quickly add the chicken pieces and stir fry until they start turning opaque and golden on the edges (3–5 minutes).
  • Toss in the carrots and broccoli, stir for 2 minutes.
  • Add the bell pepper and spring onions next, and mix well.
  • Pour in the sauce mixture, give it a good stir to coat everything evenly.
  • Cook for another 2-3 minutes, letting the sauce thicken slightly and coat the ingredients.
  • Finally, stir in the toasted cashew nuts, mix thoroughly.

5. Serve

Serve the chicken and cashew nut stir fry immediately over steamed jasmine rice, brown rice, or even noodles. Garnish with a sprinkle of extra spring onions or freshly chopped coriander for added freshness.

Tips for the Best Stir Fry

  • High heat is key: To get that characteristic stir fry flavor, have your pan hot enough before the chicken goes in. This helps achieve a slight caramelization.
  • Prep ahead: Since stir frying is fast, chopping all your vegetables and meats beforehand (mise en place) can save precious time.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan: Cook in batches if needed; overcrowding causes steaming rather than sautéing.
  • Customize your veggies: Snow peas, baby corn, or mushrooms are excellent additions or substitutes.
  • Cashews vs. peanuts: While this recipe shines with cashews, peanuts are a suitable alternative if you prefer their crunch and flavor.
  • Add aromatics: For an extra layer of aroma, try adding a few drops of toasted sesame oil at the end or a handful of fresh basil leaves.

Healthier Variations

If you want to keep this meal on the lighter side without sacrificing flavor, consider these tweaks:

  • Use skinless chicken breast for lean protein.
  • Substitute vegetable oil with avocado oil or light olive oil for healthier fats.
  • Increase the veggie-to-protein ratio by adding extra non-starchy vegetables.
  • Swap oyster sauce for a mushroom-based vegetarian sauce to make it meatless.
  • Serve with cauliflower rice or a mixed green salad if you want to reduce carbs.

Why This Dish Is Perfect for Busy Nights

Cooking from scratch can feel overwhelming when you’re juggling a busy schedule. That’s what makes this chicken and cashew nut stir fry so invaluable — it meets critical criteria for a go-to meal:

  • Quick: From start to finish, it requires about 25–30 minutes.
  • Simple: Basic cooking skills, a wok or large frying pan, and common pantry staples suffice.
  • Nutritious: Balanced mix of lean protein, healthy fats from nuts, and fresh vegetables.
  • Delicious: A well-rounded taste profile that is savory, mildly sweet, and nutty.
  • Satisfying: The cashew nuts provide crunch and satiety, preventing post-dinner snack cravings.

Serving Suggestions and Pairings

This dish pairs well with a range of sides and complements:

  • Rice: Jasmine or brown rice are traditional and soak up the sauce perfectly.
  • Noodles: Egg noodles or rice noodles tossed with a little soy sauce are great alternatives.
  • Salad: A fresh cucumber salad with a light dressing adds contrast and freshness.
  • Soup: A simple miso soup or clear broth-based soup can round out the meal for a fuller dining experience.

Storing Leftovers and Meal Prep

If you make extra, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove or microwave. When meal prepping for the week, keep the sauce separate and add it while reheating to maintain freshness and texture.

Final Thoughts

Mastering a quick, balanced, and flavorful stir fry is a fundamental kitchen skill that pays off during those hectic evenings. An easy chicken and cashew nut stir fry fits neatly into a busy lifestyle while delivering on taste and nutrition. Once you try it, you’ll find it’s not only a reliable weeknight meal but also an opportunity to experiment with different vegetables and seasonings.

So next time you’re staring at the clock and wondering what to cook, reach for your stir fry ingredients and whip up this fulfilling dish—you might just create a new favorite!
