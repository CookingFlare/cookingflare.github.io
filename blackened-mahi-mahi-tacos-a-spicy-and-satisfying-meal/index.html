Blackened Mahi Mahi Tacos: A Spicy and Satisfying Meal


If you’re a fan of tacos and love a kick of spice in your meals, then you’re in for a treat with these Blackened Mahi Mahi Tacos. This recipe combines the bold flavors of blackened seasoning with the delicate and flaky texture of mahi mahi, resulting in a mouthwatering dish that’s perfect for a casual dinner or a weekend gathering with friends and family. Let’s dive into the details of this irresistible recipe.

Ingredients

For the Blackened Seasoning:

  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Mahi Mahi Tacos:

  • 4 mahi mahi fillets
  • 8 small flour or corn tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded red cabbage
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Lime Crema:

  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon lime zest
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Blackened Seasoning:

    • In a small bowl, mix together the paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, thyme, cayenne pepper, black pepper, and salt. This flavorful seasoning will be the star of your mahi mahi tacos, so make sure to coat the fillets generously for that irresistible blackened crust.

  2. Season the Mahi Mahi:

    • Pat the mahi mahi fillets dry with paper towels and then generously coat both sides with the prepared blackened seasoning. Make sure to press the seasoning onto the fillets to ensure it adheres well.

  3. Prepare the Lime Crema:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the sour cream, lime juice, lime zest, and a pinch of salt. This zesty lime crema will add a refreshing and tangy element to your tacos.

  4. Cook the Mahi Mahi:

    • Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Once the skillet is hot, add the seasoned mahi mahi fillets and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the fish is opaque and easily flakes with a fork.

  5. Warm the Tortillas:

    • While the mahi mahi is cooking, warm the tortillas in a separate skillet or directly over a gas flame for a few seconds on each side. This step will ensure that your tacos are served with warm and pliable tortillas.

  6. Assemble the Tacos:

    • To assemble the tacos, place a portion of the blackened mahi mahi on each tortilla. Top with shredded red cabbage, sliced avocado, and a drizzle of the zesty lime crema. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve with lime wedges on the side.

Serving Suggestions

These Blackened Mahi Mahi Tacos are best enjoyed fresh and hot, straight from the skillet to your plate. Pair them with a side of Mexican street corn, a refreshing mango salsa, or a simple side salad for a complete and satisfying meal. For a burst of flavor, consider adding a dash of hot sauce or a sprinkle of crumbled queso fresco on top of your tacos.

Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of the coast, these Blackened Mahi Mahi Tacos are sure to impress with their bold flavors and vibrant colors. Embrace the spicy and satisfying experience of savoring each bite, and don’t be surprised if these tacos become a regular feature on your dinner menu.

So, gather your ingredients, embrace the sizzle of the skillet, and get ready to indulge in a fiesta of flavors with these Blackened Mahi Mahi Tacos!
