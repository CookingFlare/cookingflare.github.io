Classic Mint Julep Cupcakes: A Boozy Southern Treat

If you’re a fan of the classic mint julep cocktail and have a sweet tooth, then these Classic Mint Julep Cupcakes are the perfect treat for you. These delightful little cakes combine the refreshing flavors of mint and bourbon with the sweetness of a moist cupcake, creating a truly indulgent dessert that’s perfect for any occasion. Ingredients To make these delicious cupcakes, you will need the following ingredients: For the Cupcakes: 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/4 tsp salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 1 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 1 tsp vanilla extract 1/2 cup whole milk 2 tbsp bourbon 1/2 tsp mint extract Green food coloring (optional) For the Mint Julep Buttercream: 1 cup unsalted butter, softened 4 cups powdered sugar 2 tbsp bourbon 1/2 tsp mint extract 1-2 tbsp heavy cream Instructions For the Cupcakes: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake tin with paper liners. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract. In a small bowl, combine the milk, bourbon, mint extract, and a few drops of green food coloring if desired. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk mixture, and mix until just combined. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before frosting. For the Mint Julep Buttercream: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, and beat until well combined. Stir in the bourbon and mint extract. Add the heavy cream, 1 tablespoon at a time, until the buttercream reaches your desired consistency. Once the cupcakes are completely cool, frost them with the mint julep buttercream using a piping bag or offset spatula. Serving Suggestion These Classic Mint Julep Cupcakes are best enjoyed with a glass of iced tea or, for an extra indulgent treat, a mint julep cocktail. The Classic Manhattan Tart: A Timeless Whiskey Dessert

If you’re a fan of the classic Manhattan cocktail, then you’re in for a treat with this unique dessert twist. The Classic Manhattan Tart combines the rich flavors of whiskey, vermouth, and aromatic bitters in a delightful, boozy dessert that’s perfect for special occasions or simply indulging in a sophisticated sweet treat. Ingredients: For the Crust: 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup powdered sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed 1 large egg yolk 1-2 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 3/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted 3 large eggs 1/2 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 cup whiskey 2 tablespoons sweet vermouth 1/2 teaspoon aromatic bitters Maraschino cherries for garnish (optional) Instructions: Step 1: Prepare the Crust In a food processor, combine the flour, powdered sugar, and salt. Pulse to mix. Add the chilled, cubed butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add the egg yolk and 1 tablespoon of ice water. Pulse until the dough comes together, adding more ice water if needed. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead briefly to bring it together. Flatten the dough into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Step 2: Preheat the Oven Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and place a rack in the center position. Step 3: Roll Out the Dough Once the dough has chilled, roll it out on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the tart pan, trimming any excess. Prick the bottom with a fork and place the pan in the freezer for 15 minutes. Step 4: Blind Bake the Crust Line the chilled tart crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the parchment and weights and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Set aside to cool. Step 5: Prepare the Filling In a mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and melted butter. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. Stir in the flour and salt until just combined. Add the whiskey, sweet vermouth, and aromatic bitters, and mix until smooth. Step 6: Assemble and Bake Pour the filling into the cooled tart crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Place the tart on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are golden brown. Allow the tart to cool completely before slicing. Step 7: Garnish and Serve Once the tart has cooled, garnish with maraschino cherries if desired. Slice and serve the Classic Manhattan Tart, and savor the delightful combination of whiskey, vermouth, and aromatic bitters in every decadent bite. Conclusion: The Classic Manhattan Tart offers a delightful way to enjoy the flavors of the iconic cocktail in dessert form. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to impress your guests with a unique sweet treat, this timeless whiskey dessert is sure to be a hit. So, channel your inner mixologist and pastry chef, and indulge in the rich, sophisticated flavors of the Classic Manhattan Tart. Cheers to a dessert that’s truly one of a kind! ...
