A Seafood Lover's Paradise: Mouthwatering Recipes from Coast to Coast


Welcome, seafood aficionados! Is there anything more delightful than sinking your teeth into a succulent piece of fish, a luscious morsel of shellfish, or a tender bite of fresh, savory seafood? We’ve scoured the coastlines of America to bring you the most mouthwatering, tantalizing recipes that are guaranteed to transport you to seafood heaven. Here are sixteen delightful dishes that capture the flavors of the sea and are sure to have you hooked.

1. Maryland Crab Cakes

Indulge in the rich taste of Maryland with these delectable crab cakes:

  • 1 1/2 pounds jumbo lump crabmeat
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup mustard
  • 2 tablespoons Parsley
  • 2 tablespoons Old Bay Seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying
  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the crabmeat, breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, mustard, parsley, Old Bay Seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  3. Gently mix the ingredients without breaking up the lumps of crabmeat.
  4. Mound generous portions of the crab mixture into six patties.
  5. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  6. Cook crab cakes, in batches if necessary, until golden brown, 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Transfer the cooked crab cakes to a baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes, until heated through.

2. New England Clam Chowder

A classic dish, this rich and creamy chowder is a staple on many seaside menus:

  • 4 slices bacon
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 cup chopped clams (with juice)
  • 3 cups potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Cook bacon in a large pot over medium heat until crisp. Remove bacon and reserve.
  2. In the same pot, sauté onion, celery, and garlic until softened.
  3. Add butter and melt before stirring in flour to create a roux.
  4. Gradually add the chopped clams and their juice, followed by the diced potatoes, milk, and heavy cream.
  5. Stir the mixture until well combined and bring it to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat and let simmer until potatoes are tender, approximately 20-25 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir in the chopped bacon just before serving.

3. South Carolina Shrimp and Grits

South Carolina’s favorite breakfast entrée is crispy shrimp served on a bed of creamy grits:

  • 1 lb fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups cooked grits
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  1. In a large skillet, melt butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  2. Season the shrimp with salt, pepper, and a splash of hot sauce. Add shrimp to the skillet and cook until pink and crispy, turning once.
  3. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and lemon juice, let cook for 1 minute.
  4. Evenly distribution the grits on plates and top with the sautéed shrimp and garlic.
  5. Garnish with grated cheese and serve immediately.

4. Gulf Coast Louisiana Cajun Crawfish Boil

A quintessential Louisiana celebration, this dish is perfect for impressing all your seafood-loving friends:

  • 4 pounds crawfish
  • 2 pounds red potatoes
  • 8 ears corn on the cob
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 lemons
  • 1 head garlic
  • 1 pound andouille sausage
  • Crab boil seasoning (Zatarain’s or similar)
  • Water
  1. In a large pot, combine water and crab boil seasoning, bring to a boil.
  2. Add the crawfish, red potatoes, corn on the cob, onion, whole lemon, garlic, and andouille sausage to the boiling pot.
  3. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, until the shrimp are a vibrant pink and everything is cooked through.
  4. Serve in large bowls or on newspaper-lined tables for a true Louisiana experience.

5. San Francisco Dungeness Crab Salad

Enjoy a touch of San Francisco in this refreshing yet satisfying salad:

  • 2 Dungeness crabs, yielding about 2-3 pounds of meat
  • 2 avocados, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Cook the crabs using your preferred method, reserving the meat.
  2. Prepare the dressing by blending lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  3. In a large serving bowl, combine the Dungeness crab, sliced avocado, cucumber, sliced onion, cherry tomatoes, and chives.
  4. Toss the ingredients with enough dressing to coat, seasoning to taste.
  5. Chill for at least an hour for the flavors to meld before serving.

6. Pacific Northwest Alaskan Salmon with Lemon Dill Sauce

Fresh, robust Alaska salmon gets added flavor and elegance from a lemon dill sauce:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  1. Season salmon fillets with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add fillets, skin-side down, and cook until crisp and golden.
  3. Flip fillets and add butter to the pan. Reduce heat to medium-low, spoon the melted butter over the fillets, and cook until desired doneness is reached.
  4. For the sauce, blend dill, garlic, lemon juice, cold water, and butter in a small saucepan.
  5. Bring to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Pour sauce over cooked salmon and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

7. Maine Lobster Rolls

A must-try dish from Maine, these lobster rolls are a true taste of summer:

  • 2 cups cooked lobster meat
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 hot dog buns, split
  • 4 tablespoons melted butter
  • Chopped chives for garnish
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine lobster meat, mayonnaise, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix gently to incorporate.
  2. Brush the split buns with melted butter and griddle or toast until golden.
  3. Fill the buns with the lobster mixture, garnish with chives, and serve immediately.

8. Florida Stone Crab Claws

A delectable taste of Florida, stone crab claws are sweet, meaty, and require little effort to prepare:

  • 1 dozen fresh stone crab claws
  • Boiled water
  • Lemon wedges and melted butter for serving
  1. To cook stone crab claws, bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Immerse the claws in the boiling water for 1 minute.
  2. Remove claws using tongs, drain, and immediately place in an ice bath to cool.
  3. Serve with lemon wedges and melted butter on the side.

9. Rhode Island Calamari Sandwich

Combining tender squid rings with a homemade marinara sauce, this sandwich is pure Rhode Island:

  • 2 calamari rings, dredged in flour and deep-fried
  • 2 slices Italian bread
  • 1/4 pound provolone cheese
  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  1. In a skillet, heat marinara sauce until simmering.
  2. Layer the fried calamari on one slice of bread.
  3. Top with the heated marinara sauce, followed by a slice of provolone cheese.
  4. Cover with the other slice of bread and press lightly.
  5. Cook the sandwich in a skillet or on a griddle until the cheese is melted and the outside is toasty.
  6. Slice and garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

10. Texas Gulf Shrimp Tacos

Bold, zesty, and oh-so-Texas, try this masterpiece with generous portions of Gulf shrimp:

  • 1 pound medium Gulf shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup sliced jalapeños
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Season the shrimp with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  2. Grill the shrimp until pink and opaque.
  3. Warm the corn tortillas over an open flame.
  4. Assemble the tacos by layering shrimp, jalapeños, and cilantro on each tortilla.
  5. Top with sliced avocado and serve with lime wedges on the side.

11. Washington Dungeness Crab Mac and Cheese

This rich, creamy, and indulgent dish elevates the classic comfort food:

  • 1 pound macaroni pasta
  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1 cup grated parmesan
  • 4 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups cooked Dungeness crab meat
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Panko bread crumbs for top
  1. Cook macaroni according to packet instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add flour and stir until smooth, creating a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in milk, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat to simmer, stirring until thickened.
  4. Stir in the parmesan and half of the cheddar cheese, until melted and smooth.
  5. Fold in cooked macaroni, Dungeness crab meat, salt, and pepper.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish.
  7. Sprinkle remaining cheddar cheese, then the panko bread crumbs on top.
  8. Bake at 375°F until bubbly and golden on top, about 30 minutes.

12. Massachusetts White New England Fish Chowder

A creamy, hearty chowder made with generous chunks of fresh fish:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups fish stock
  • 1 pound white fish (haddock, cod, or halibut), diced
  • 1/2 cup half-and-half
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley for garnish
  1. Sauté onion, potatoes, and celery in butter until softened in a large pot.
  2. Stir in flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to create a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in fish stock, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat to simmer.
  4. Add diced fish and cook until tender, about 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in half-and-half, salt, and pepper. Cook until heated through.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning, garnish with chopped parsley, and serve hot.

13. North Carolina Shrimp Dip

A crowd-pleaser and perfect for parties, this dip features shrimp, cream cheese, and spices:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan
  • 1/2 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  • 1 pound boiled shrimp, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced scallions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a bowl, combine cream cheese, mayonnaise, sour cream, parmesan, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix until well combined, then fold in chopped shrimp, bell pepper, and scallions.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a serving bowl and serve with crackers or toasted bread.

14. California Seafood Paella

A gorgeous, colorful, and bold dish, this paella showcases the West Coast’s love for fresh seafood:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 2 cups seafood stock
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound mussels
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 littleneck clams
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, diced
  1. In a large paella pan or skillet, sauté onion and garlic until softened over medium heat.
  2. Stir in rice, cooking until lightly toasted.
  3. Add seafood stock, white wine, saffron, salt, and pepper, bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat to simmer, stirring occasionally, until rice is tender, about 20 minutes.
  5. Stir in mussels, shrimp, clams, and chicken. Cover and cook until the mussels open and shrimp are pink.
  6. Sprinkle with parsley, serve hot, and savor this delightful Coastal feast.

15. Oregon Dungeness Crab Ceviche

This light, refreshing ceviche features Dungeness crab and a zesty avocado puree:

  • 2 cups cooked Dungeness crab meat
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/2 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 jalapeño, seeds removed, minced
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped cilantro for garnish
  1. In a food processor, blend avocado, lime juice, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine crab, diced onion, tomatoes, jalapeño, and avocado puree.
  3. Gently toss the ingredients, seasoning to taste.
  4. Chill for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  5. Serve cold, garnished with fresh cilantro.

16. Hawaii Poke Bowl

Bright, vibrant, and full of umami flavors, this poke bowl is a celebration of Hawaiian cuisine:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 1 cooked tuna steak, diced
  • 1/2 cup sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup sliced avocado
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Sriracha mayonnaise
  • Sesame seeds for topping
  1. Cook sushi rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a bowl, mix together diced tuna, sliced cucumber, avocado, red onion, and Sriracha mayonnaise.
  3. Divide sushi rice into serving bowls.
  4. Spoon the tuna mixture on top of the rice.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds, and serve chilled with chopsticks.

From the laid-back vibes of California to the hardworking spirit of New England, this coast-to-coast culinary tour has something to offer every seafood lover. Share these dishes with friends and family, and let the incredible flavors of America’s shores transport you to a seafood lover’s paradise. Happy cooking!
