Mediterranean Wrap Recipes to Add Variety to Your Meals


When it comes to vibrant, wholesome meals packed with fresh ingredients, Mediterranean cuisine consistently stands out. Its use of olive oil, fresh vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins offers not just flavor but also nutritional benefits that support a balanced lifestyle. A fantastic and versatile way to enjoy the Mediterranean palette is by making wraps. These handheld delights bring Mediterranean flavors into a convenient, portable format perfect for lunch, dinner, or even a light snack.

In this post, we will dive into a variety of Mediterranean wrap recipes that are easy to prepare, bursting with flavor, and can breathe new life into your meal rotation. Whether you prefer vegetarian options or are a fan of hearty meats and seafood, you’ll find something to excite your palate.

Why Choose Mediterranean Wraps?

Mediterranean wraps are not only delicious but also incredibly flexible. Their core ingredients—fresh veggies, herbs, tangy cheeses, and protein—lend themselves to endless combinations. Plus, they’re usually quick to assemble, making them perfect for busy weekdays or casual weekend meals.

Using whole grain or flatbread wraps increases fiber intake, and the abundance of veggies and lean proteins create a balanced meal with complex flavors. Adding traditional Mediterranean staples like tzatziki, hummus, or olives intensifies the taste and authenticity.

Essential Ingredients for Mediterranean Wraps

Before we jump into the recipes, here’s a list of go-to ingredients that give Mediterranean wraps their signature taste:

  • Flatbreads and Wraps: Pita bread, lavash, whole wheat tortillas
  • Proteins: Grilled chicken, lamb, falafel, shrimp, tuna
  • Fresh Vegetables: Cucumber, tomato, red onion, bell pepper, kalamata olives, spinach, arugula
  • Cheeses: Feta cheese, halloumi, goat cheese
  • Herbs and Aromatics: Fresh parsley, mint, oregano, dill, garlic
  • Condiments: Tzatziki, hummus, tahini, baba ganoush, lemon juice, olive oil, red wine vinegar
  • Spices: Cumin, smoked paprika, sumac, za’atar

Having a few of these staples on hand will make creating Mediterranean wraps simple and fun.

1. Classic Greek Chicken Wrap with Tzatziki

This wrap balances juicy, herb-marinated grilled chicken with the cool, creamy tang of tzatziki. It’s a classic for a reason.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large whole wheat wraps
  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 small cucumber, peeled and finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large tomato, sliced
  • ½ cup kalamata olives, pitted & halved
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add chicken slices and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare Tzatziki: Mix Greek yogurt, cucumber, garlic, dill, and a pinch of salt in another bowl. Chill in the fridge.
  3. Cook Chicken: Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium heat and cook chicken slices until fully cooked and slightly charred.
  4. Assemble the Wrap: Lay the wrap flat, spread a generous amount of tzatziki, add spinach, grilled chicken, tomato slices, red onion, and kalamata olives.
  5. Wrap it up: Fold in the sides and roll tightly. Slice in half and serve immediately.

This wrap is a burst of refreshment and savory spices — perfect for a satisfying meal any time.

2. Vegetarian Falafel & Hummus Wrap

Falafel is a beloved Mediterranean staple made from ground chickpeas and herbs, fried to crispy perfection. Paired with creamy hummus, it makes for a filling and nutrient-rich vegetarian meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large pita pockets or flatbreads
  • 8-10 falafel balls (store-bought or homemade)
  • ½ cup hummus
  • ½ cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • ¼ cup pickled turnips or pickled red cabbage for tang
  • Fresh parsley leaves
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce or mixed greens
  • 1 tbsp tahini sauce (optional)
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Directions:

  1. Prepare Falafel: If using store-bought, heat falafel according to package instructions. For homemade, prepare a simple falafel mix with chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cumin, and coriander—form into balls and fry or bake.
  2. Spread Hummus: Open the pita or lay the flatbread, spreading hummus evenly.
  3. Add Fillings: Layer falafel balls, cucumber, tomato, pickled vegetables, lettuce, and parsley.
  4. Drizzle: Add tahini sauce for an extra nutty flavor if desired.
  5. Roll and Serve: Wrap it up tightly and serve with lemon wedges.

This wrap is not only vibrant and colorful but also packed with plant-based protein and fiber.

3. Spiced Lamb & Feta Wrap with Mint Yogurt Sauce

Lamb is rich and flavorful, often associated with traditional Mediterranean and Middle Eastern meals. This wrap balances the intensity of lamb with fresh herbs and creamy sauces.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large flatbreads or lavash
  • 250 g ground lamb
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup mixed salad greens
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Olive oil for cooking

Directions:

  1. Cook the Lamb: Heat olive oil in a skillet and sauté onion and garlic until translucent. Add ground lamb, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Cook until browned and cooked through.
  2. Make Mint Yogurt Sauce: Combine yogurt, mint, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Assemble the Wrap: Spread some of the mint yogurt sauce over the flatbread, add cooked lamb, sprinkle feta cheese, add salad greens, and drizzle more yogurt sauce on top.
  4. Wrap and Enjoy: Fold and roll tightly. Slice in half if desired.

This wrap features complex spices balanced by cool and creamy elements creating a sophisticated flavor profile.

4. Grilled Shrimp & Avocado Wrap with Lemon Garlic Dressing

For seafood enthusiasts, this fresh and zesty wrap feels like a Mediterranean breeze in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole wheat tortillas or wraps
  • 200 g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ripe avocado, mashed
  • 1 cup shredded romaine lettuce
  • ½ red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • ½ small red onion, thinly sliced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper

Directions:

  1. Marinate Shrimp: In a bowl, toss shrimp with half the olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Let marinate for 15 minutes.
  2. Grill Shrimp: Heat grill pan or skillet and cook shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque.
  3. Prepare Avocado Spread: Mash avocado and spread it evenly over the wraps.
  4. Add Vegetables: Layer romaine, bell pepper, and onion slices on top of the avocado.
  5. Add Shrimp: Place grilled shrimp over the vegetables, sprinkle parsley, and drizzle remaining lemon garlic dressing.
  6. Wrap and Serve: Roll up tightly and slice if preferred.

The bright acidity and garlic make this wrap refreshing while the grilled shrimp add a satisfying protein boost.

5. Roasted Vegetable & Halloumi Wrap with Za’atar Drizzle

Vegetable lovers rejoice! Roasted veggies and salty halloumi cheese paired with za’atar seasoning create a wrap full of aroma and depth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large flatbreads or whole wheat wraps
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small eggplant, cubed
  • 150 g halloumi cheese, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp za’atar spice
  • 1 cup fresh arugula
  • 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (optional)
  • Salt and pepper

Directions:

  1. Roast Vegetables: Toss zucchini, bell pepper, and eggplant with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 200°C (390°F) for 20-25 minutes until tender and slightly charred.
  2. Cook Halloumi: In a non-stick pan, grill halloumi slices until golden brown on both sides.
  3. Assemble Wrap: Spread Greek yogurt on wrap if using, layer roasted vegetables, halloumi slices, and fresh arugula.
  4. Add Za’atar: Sprinkle za’atar spice over the top.
  5. Wrap and Serve: Fold and roll wraps tightly.

The combination of smoky roasted vegetables and crispy halloumi is irresistible, with za’atar adding a herbal, citrusy note.

Tips for Perfect Mediterranean Wraps Every Time

  • Warm Your Wraps: Lightly heating flatbreads or wraps makes them pliable and helps prevent tearing.
  • Balance Flavors: Mediterranean cuisine thrives on balance—contrast creamy elements with acidic and crunchy ingredients to keep each bite lively.
  • Use Fresh Herbs Generously: Parsley, mint, dill, and oregano can instantly lift a simple wrap to new heights.
  • Customize Protein Portions: Adjust the amount of meat or plant-based protein according to your appetite.
  • Layer Thoughtfully: Place wetter ingredients like sauces and juicy tomatoes away from the wrap’s edges to avoid sogginess.
  • Go Beyond the Usual: Experiment with less common additions like roasted beets, preserved lemon, or fresh pomegranate seeds for creative twists.

Wrapping Up

Mediterranean wraps offer an exciting way to diversify your meals without compromising on health or flavor. With fresh ingredients, fragrant herbs, and a variety of proteins and cheeses, you can enjoy countless variations to suit any dietary preference or craving.

Whether it’s a quick weekday lunch or a weekend gathering with family and friends, these Mediterranean wraps are sure to impress and satisfy. So next time you wonder what to make, reach for the flatbread and dive into these fabulous, flavor-packed recipes. Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you.

If you try any of these recipes or come up with your own Mediterranean wrap combinations, feel free to share your experiences! Cooking is all about creativity and joy, and the Mediterranean way makes it a celebration of wholesome, delightful food.
