10 Must-Try Thanksgiving Turkey Recipes for a Perfect Feast


Adding a twist to the classic Thanksgiving dinner, these innovative turkey recipes are sure to impress your guests and make your holiday table shine. The star of the show is, without a doubt, the majestic roasted turkey. The centerpiece of countless Thanksgiving celebrations, this dish is more than just a meal, it's an experience. Over the years, many of us have stuck to traditional recipes, reinventing the same recipes year after year. But, why settle for the same old when you can give your Thanksgiving feast a flavor boost? We're about to shake things up with 10 mouthwatering turkey recipes that are sure to revolutionize the way you think about the holidays.

  1. Herb Crusted Turkey with Caramelized Onions Level up your roasted turkey with a fragrant herb crust and a hint of sweetness from perfectly caramelized onions.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1/4 cup chopped fresh rosemary 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 tablespoons honey 1 large onion, thinly sliced 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss together rosemary, thyme, and olive oil. Rub the mixture all over the turkey. Mix together honey, olive oil, and thyme, and brush over the turkey. In a separate pan, caramelize onions. Season turkey with salt and pepper and transfer to oven. Top with caramelized onions and Parmesan cheese. Roast for about 45 minutes or until golden brown. 2. Smoked Paprika Glazed Turkey Bring the heat with a rich, smoky glaze that's guaranteed to leave your taste buds wanting more.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 2 tablespoons smoked paprika 2 tablespoons honey 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a small bowl, mix together smoked paprika, honey, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Brush the mixture all over the turkey. Transfer to oven and roast for about 45 minutes or until golden brown. 3. Korean-Style Fried Turkey Eat like a Seoulite with this crispy, bold Korean-inspired fried turkey that's about to take the RC recipe files to the next level.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1 cup all-purpose flour 1 cup cornstarch 1 cup panko breadcrumbs 1/2 cup Korean chili flakes 1 egg 1 cup buttermilk Vegetable oil for frying Optional: Spicy Korean-style sauce for serving

In a deep frying pan, combine flour, cornstarch, and panko breadcrumbs. Dredge turkey in buttermilk and then coat with the flour mixture on all sides, shaking off excess. Heat enough vegetable oil to deep frying in a large pot. Fry turkey until golden brown and crispy, about 10-12 minutes per side. 4. Braised Turkey in Red Wine with Mushrooms and Onions Fall in love with this indulgent, slow-cooked turkey dish, drenched in rich red wine and the sweetness of caramelized onions.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 2 cups red wine 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 large onion, thinly sliced 2 cups sliced mushrooms 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 cups turkey broth 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1 teaspoon dried rosemary 1/2 cup all-purpose flour Salt and pepper to taste

In a large Dutch oven, combine red wine, olive oil, and sliced onions. Braise in the oven over low heat for 30 minutes. Remove onions and set aside. In the same pot, brown the turkey in the remaining oil, then set aside. Add mushrooms, garlic, turkey broth, thyme, rosemary, and flour to the pot. Baste the turkey with the sauce and return it to the pot. Cover and cook in the oven until golden brown. 5. Maple-Glazed Turkey with Roasted Sweet Potatoes Add a touch of sweetness to your Thanksgiving feast with this stunning maple-glazed turkey paired with crispy roasted sweet potatoes.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1/4 cup pure maple syrup 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard 1 teaspoon ground mustard 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed Vegetable oil for frying

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a small bowl, mix together maple syrup, olive oil, Dijon mustard, ground mustard, salt, and black pepper. Brush the mixture all over the turkey. Toss sweet potatoes with olive oil and salt. Spread on baking sheet and roast in oven for about 25 minutes. Roast turkey for about 45 minutes or until golden brown. 6. Turkey En Croute with Mushroom Cream Sauce Container-style turkey gets a sophisticated twist with a delicate herb and mushroom filling, topped with a creamy bechamel sauce.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1 cup mushroom duxelles 1 cup grated cheddar cheese 1 cup milk 2 tablespoons butter 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Unroll puff pastry and use to line a 12 by 18 inch baking sheet. Fill pastry-lined sheet with mushroom duxelles, grated cheddar cheese, and chopped parsley. Roll up tightly and press edges to seal. Cut longitudinal slits to create compartments for cheese and turkey. Mix together milk, butter, flour, thyme, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Brush mixture all over turkey and transfer to oven. Roast for about 25 minutes or until golden brown. 7. Consommé-Style Turkey with Licorice and Lemon Living la dolce vita with this novel take on the classic roasted turkey infused with Italy's sweetness and full-bodied flavors.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1 cup licorice-spiced consommé (clear broth) 1/4 cup chop fresh parsley 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon dried basil

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Mix together licorice-spiced consommé, chopped parsley, lemon juice, dried oregano, and dried basil in a small bowl. Brush the mixture all over the turkey. Transfer to oven and roast for about 45 minutes or until golden brown. 8. K-Town-Style BBQ Turkey with Gochujang and Soy Sauce Wrap your taste buds in spices of Seoul with this BBQ-inspired turkey, deeply colored with the richness of soy sauce and the flush of fiery gochujang.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1 cup gochujang (Korean chili paste) 1 cup soy sauce 1/4 cup chopped scallions 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons brown sugar 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a small bowl, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, chopped scallions, garlic, brown sugar, vegetable oil, and chopped cilantro. Brush the mixture all over the turkey. Roast in oven for about 25 minutes or until crispy on the outside. 9. Spicy Chipotle-Crusted Turkey with Avocado Slaw Brighten up your Thanksgiving table with the freshness of a zesty avocado slaw, partnered with a bold smoky chipotle crunch.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1/4 cup chopped chipotle peppers in adobo sauce 1 cup corn tortilla crumbs 1 cup grated cheddar cheese 1 avocado, diced 1 cup shredded cabbage 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon lime juice Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a small bowl, mix together chipotle peppers, corn tortilla crumbs, grated cheddar cheese, diced avocado, shredded cabbage, olive oil, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Brush the mixture all over the turkey. Transfer to oven and roast for about 25 minutes or until golden brown. 10. Foie Gras-Stuffed and Herb-Crusted Turkey Indulge your guests with this downright decadent, make-ahead masterpiece that's sure to generate gasps at your holiday table.

1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey, rinsed and patted dry 1 cup (6 oz.) foie gras 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme 2 tablespoons butter 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Unroll puff pastry and use to line a 12 by 18 inch baking sheet. Fill pastry-lined sheet with foie gras, chopped parsley, chopped thyme, butter, flour, salt, and pepper. Roll up tightly and press edges to seal. Cut longitudinal slits to create compartments for cheese and turkey. Mix together herb seasonings and butter in a small bowl. Brush mixture all over turkey. Transfer to oven and roast for about 25 minutes or until golden brown.
