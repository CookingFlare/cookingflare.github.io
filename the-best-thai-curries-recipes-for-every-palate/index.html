The Best Thai Curries: Recipes for Every Palate


Thai cuisine is renowned for its complex flavors, vibrant colors, and aromatic ingredients. Among the many incredible dishes Thailand offers, curry holds a special place. The balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors in Thai curries makes them a favorite worldwide. Whether you prefer something mild and creamy or intensely spicy, there’s a Thai curry for everyone. In this blog post, we will explore the best Thai curries, offering recipes that cater to every palate, from beginner cooks to seasoned chefs.

What Makes Thai Curries Unique?

Before diving into the recipes, it’s essential to understand what sets Thai curries apart from other types of curry. Thai curries are characterized by their use of fresh herbs and spices, often blended into pastes, that create bold and aromatic flavors. These pastes are the backbone of most curries, incorporating ingredients like lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, garlic, and chilies. Coconut milk is frequently used as a base, adding richness and creaminess to the dish while balancing out the heat from the spices.

Thai curries are divided into two broad categories: red curries and green curries, though there are also other types like yellow curries, Massaman curry, and Panang curry. Each of these varieties is made using different combinations of ingredients, which influence the color, flavor, and heat level of the curry.

1. Green Curry (Gaeng Keow Wan)

Green curry is one of the most beloved Thai dishes, and for good reason. This curry is known for its vibrant color, which comes from the green chilies and fresh herbs used in the paste. The heat from the chilies is balanced by the richness of coconut milk, creating a harmonious and fragrant dish. Green curry is typically made with chicken, beef, or tofu, but it can be customized with your choice of protein or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon green curry paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 1-2 Thai bird’s eye chilies, sliced (adjust to heat preference)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (palm sugar is preferred)
  • 1 cup sliced eggplant
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 500g chicken breast (or tofu for a vegetarian version)
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the curry paste: If you are making your green curry paste from scratch, blend together green chilies, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, galangal, and kaffir lime zest until smooth.

  2. Sauté the curry paste: In a large saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add coconut milk: Pour in the coconut milk and stir to combine with the curry paste. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  4. Add vegetables and protein: Add the sliced eggplant, red bell pepper, bamboo shoots, and your choice of chicken or tofu. Let everything cook for about 10-15 minutes until the protein is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

  5. Season the curry: Add fish sauce, sugar, and sliced kaffir lime leaves. Stir to combine. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more fish sauce or sugar if needed.

  6. Finish the curry: Once the curry is ready, garnish with fresh basil leaves and sliced bird’s eye chilies. Serve with steamed jasmine rice and enjoy.

Tips:

  • For an extra layer of flavor, you can add Thai eggplant or zucchini.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by increasing or decreasing the amount of bird’s eye chilies used in the curry paste.

2. Red Curry (Gaeng Phed)

Red curry is another classic Thai dish with a deep, rich flavor that comes from the red chilies used in the curry paste. It is slightly milder than green curry but still packs a punch of spice and flavor. Red curry is often made with chicken, beef, or pork and is a great choice for those who enjoy a hearty, warming dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup baby corn, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 500g chicken or pork (sliced thinly)
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the curry paste: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the red curry paste and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  2. Add coconut milk: Pour in the coconut milk, mixing it with the curry paste, and bring to a simmer.

  3. Add vegetables and protein: Add your choice of protein and vegetables, such as baby corn, red bell peppers, and bamboo shoots. Let it cook for about 10 minutes until everything is tender.

  4. Season the curry: Add fish sauce, sugar, and kaffir lime leaves. Stir and taste, adjusting seasoning as needed.

  5. Finish and serve: Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve with steamed jasmine rice.

Tips:

  • For extra depth, add a squeeze of lime juice right before serving.
  • If you like your curry a bit sweeter, you can increase the amount of sugar.

3. Massaman Curry

Massaman curry is a unique Thai curry that is influenced by Indian and Persian cuisine, featuring aromatic spices such as cinnamon, cloves, and cumin. It is often made with beef or chicken and is known for its slightly sweet and nutty flavor due to the addition of peanuts and potatoes. Massaman curry is perfect for those who prefer a milder curry with a deeper, more complex flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 500g beef (or chicken), cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup onions, chopped
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the curry paste: If using homemade Massaman curry paste, blend together dried red chilies, garlic, lemongrass, shallots, cumin, cinnamon, and other spices until smooth.

  2. Cook the curry paste: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the Massaman curry paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add coconut milk and spices: Pour in the coconut milk and stir well. Add fish sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  4. Add meat and potatoes: Add the beef or chicken and cubed potatoes to the pot. Cook for about 30-40 minutes, until the meat is tender and the potatoes are cooked through.

  5. Finish the curry: Add the roasted peanuts and stir well. Serve with steamed rice or roti.

Tips:

  • Massaman curry can be made ahead of time and even tastes better the next day as the flavors continue to develop.
  • You can substitute the beef with lamb for a richer flavor, or use tofu for a vegetarian version.

4. Panang Curry

Panang curry is a rich, creamy Thai curry known for its mild heat and unique flavors, thanks to the use of ingredients like peanuts and lime zest. Unlike red or green curry, Panang curry is thicker and more concentrated, making it perfect for those who enjoy a creamy, subtly spicy dish. Traditionally, Panang curry is made with beef or chicken, though it can be easily adapted to suit other proteins or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Panang curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 500g chicken or beef, sliced thinly
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lime zest
  • 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the curry paste: Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat and sauté the Panang curry paste for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  2. Add coconut milk and simmer: Pour in the coconut milk, stir, and bring the mixture to a simmer.

  3. Add protein and vegetables: Add your chicken or beef, bell peppers, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, until the meat is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

  4. Finish the curry: Add fish sauce, sugar, and crushed peanuts. Stir to combine. Garnish with fresh lime zest and Thai basil leaves.

  5. Serve: Serve hot with jasmine rice.

Tips:

  • For an extra creamy texture, you can add a bit more coconut milk.
  • The curry paste can be adjusted to make it as mild or spicy as you prefer.

Conclusion

Thai curries offer a beautiful balance of flavors that appeal to a wide range of tastes. Whether you prefer the fiery heat of green curry or the rich, nutty notes of Massaman curry, there’s a Thai curry recipe for everyone. These dishes not only showcase the depth of Thai culinary traditions but also invite you to experiment with ingredients, textures, and spice levels to suit your palate. So, grab your ingredients, and enjoy the art of Thai cooking at home. Happy cooking!
