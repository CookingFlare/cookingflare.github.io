The Best Korean Soup Recipes for Every Season


Korean cuisine offers a rich variety of soups, each with its own unique flavors and ingredients that cater to different seasons of the year. From the comforting, hearty stews enjoyed during cold winter months to light and refreshing broths perfect for warmer days, Korean soups have something to offer all year round. Whether you’re a seasoned Korean food lover or new to the cuisine, this post will introduce you to some of the best Korean soup recipes that you can enjoy throughout the seasons.

Winter: Warm Up with Hearty Korean Soups

When the temperatures drop, there’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of Korean soup to keep you warm and satisfied. These soups are designed to provide comfort and nourishment, using ingredients that promote warmth and energy. Let’s dive into some of the most popular Korean winter soups.

1. Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개) - Kimchi Stew

Kimchi Jjigae is perhaps the most beloved and iconic Korean winter soup. It’s made with fermented kimchi, tofu, pork (or tuna), and various seasonings like garlic, onions, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). The rich, tangy flavor of the aged kimchi creates a depth of taste that’s perfect for cold weather.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of well-fermented kimchi (chopped)
  • 200g of pork belly or tuna (optional)
  • 1 block of tofu (firm)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 4 cups of water or anchovy stock
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a pot and sauté the garlic and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the kimchi and continue sautéing for 5 minutes to enhance its flavor.
  3. Pour in the water or anchovy stock and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the pork or tuna, gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  5. Let it simmer for 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Add the tofu and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with a bowl of steamed rice.

2. Banchan Jjigae (반찬찌개) - Leftover Side Dish Soup

Banchan Jjigae is the perfect dish for using up leftover banchan (side dishes). This hearty soup is often made with a mix of whatever vegetables, meats, or fermented dishes you have on hand, making it both flexible and comforting during winter.

Ingredients:

  • Leftover kimchi
  • Leftover vegetables (spinach, cabbage, etc.)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang
  • 2 cups of beef or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 1/2 cup of tofu (cubed)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the sesame oil in a large pot and sauté the garlic.
  2. Add in the leftover kimchi and vegetables, stirring for a few minutes.
  3. Pour in the broth and soy sauce, then bring to a boil.
  4. Add tofu and let it simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  5. Adjust seasoning to taste, and serve hot.

Spring: Lighter and Refreshing Soups

As the weather starts to warm up, Korean soups also begin to shift from rich and hearty to lighter and more refreshing. These soups use seasonal ingredients like fresh greens, seafood, and lighter broths.

3. Miyeok Guk (미역국) - Seaweed Soup

Miyeok Guk is traditionally eaten on birthdays in Korea, but it’s also a great spring soup thanks to its light, nutritious broth. Made with seaweed, this soup is known for its health benefits, particularly for its iodine and minerals that promote vitality.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup dried miyeok (seaweed)
  • 200g of beef (or seafood like clams or fish)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried seaweed in water for about 20 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.
  2. Cut the beef into thin strips and sauté in sesame oil until browned.
  3. Add the garlic, soy sauce, and water, and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the seaweed and simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and serve hot.

4. Dongchimi Guk (동치미국) - Radish Water Kimchi Soup

Dongchimi Guk is a refreshing, mildly tangy soup made with water kimchi, a popular Korean side dish. It’s particularly enjoyed in the spring and summer months, providing a cool, rejuvenating taste.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of dongchimi (water kimchi)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (optional for spice)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cucumber (sliced thinly)
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine the dongchimi, water, soy sauce, and salt.
  2. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes to blend the flavors.
  3. Add the cucumber, gochugaru, and garlic, and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Summer: Light and Refreshing Broths

During the hot summer months, Korean soups are often served cold or with light broths that help cool the body and refresh the spirit. These soups typically use ingredients like chilled noodles, cooling vegetables, and clean broths.

5. Samgyetang (삼계탕) - Ginseng Chicken Soup

Samgyetang is a beloved summer dish in Korea, particularly enjoyed during the hottest days of the year. It’s made with a whole chicken stuffed with garlic, ginseng, jujube, and sticky rice. This nourishing soup is believed to help boost stamina and energy during the sweltering summer heat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1kg)
  • 2 tablespoons of ginseng (dried or fresh)
  • 10 jujubes (dried dates)
  • 1/4 cup of glutinous rice
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 4 cups of water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the glutinous rice for an hour before stuffing it into the chicken’s cavity.
  2. Place the chicken, ginseng, jujubes, garlic, and rice in a large pot, then cover with water.
  3. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 1–2 hours.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, and serve with steamed rice and kimchi.

6. Naengguk (냉국) - Cold Cucumber Soup

This chilled cucumber soup is a classic summer recipe, offering a refreshing and light taste perfect for cooling off. It’s served cold, often with thin slices of cucumber floating in a tangy, vinegary broth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large cucumbers (thinly sliced)
  • 4 cups of cold water
  • 1 tablespoon of vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the cold water, vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil in a bowl.
  2. Add the cucumber slices and let it sit in the refrigerator for at least an hour to chill and marinate.
  3. Adjust seasoning with salt, then serve cold.

Fall: Comforting Stews to Transition Into Cooler Weather

As the leaves begin to change color and the air grows crisp, fall is the perfect time for heartier soups that aren’t as heavy as winter stews but still provide a comforting warmth. Korean fall soups often incorporate seasonal vegetables and lighter meats, making them ideal for the in-between weather.

7. Sundubu Jjigae (순두부찌개) - Soft Tofu Stew

Sundubu Jjigae is a versatile and comforting dish that can be enjoyed year-round, but it shines especially during fall. Made with silky tofu, vegetables, and a choice of meats, this spicy stew is a perfect dish for chilly evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of soft tofu
  • 200g of beef, pork, or seafood
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru
  • 2 cups of broth (beef or vegetable)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 1/4 cup of sliced green onions
  • 1 egg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a pot and sauté the garlic and meat until browned.
  2. Add the gochujang, gochugaru, and broth, and bring it to a boil.
  3. Gently add the soft tofu and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Optional: crack an egg into the soup for a richer texture.
  5. Garnish with green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

8. Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개) - Fermented Soybean Paste Stew

Doenjang Jjigae is a staple in Korean households, offering a savory, earthy flavor from fermented soybean paste. It’s a dish that uses ingredients available in fall, such as root vegetables, mushrooms, and zucchini.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of doenjang (fermented soybean paste)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 potato (cubed)
  • 200g of tofu (cubed)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang
  • 4 cups of water or stock
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • Green onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Dissolve the doenjang in water or stock in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add the zucchini, onion, potato, and garlic, and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Add the tofu and gochujang, then simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Garnish with green onions and serve with steamed rice.

Conclusion

Korean soups are incredibly versatile, offering something for every season. Whether you’re craving the warmth and comfort of kimchi jjigae in the winter, the cool refreshment of dongchimi guk in the summer, or the hearty and nourishing samgyetang in the fall, these dishes are sure to satisfy your culinary cravings throughout the year. By using a variety of seasonal ingredients and traditional Korean techniques, these soups not only offer great flavors but also celebrate the harmony of Korean food culture. So, gather your ingredients and give these recipes a try – your taste buds will thank you!
