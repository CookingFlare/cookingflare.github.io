10 Easy Fruit-Based Dessert Recipes for Summer


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in fresh, juicy fruits, and what better way to celebrate the season than by turning these vibrant ingredients into refreshing desserts? Whether you’re hosting a picnic, enjoying a backyard barbecue, or simply craving something sweet, these fruit-based treats are sure to bring a burst of flavor and sunny vibes to your table. From classics with a twist to quick and creative ideas, here are ten irresistible fruit-based dessert recipes you can whip up with ease this summer.

1. Watermelon Mint Granita

Watermelon is a summer staple, and this icy granita highlights its hydrating sweetness. This recipe combines watermelon with zesty lime and fragrant mint for a refreshing dessert that’s perfect for hot days.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon chunks (seeded or seedless)
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 2 tablespoons sugar or honey (optional)
  • 6-8 fresh mint leaves

Directions:

  1. Blend the watermelon, lime juice, sugar (if using), and mint leaves until you get a smooth puree.
  2. Pour the mixture into a shallow baking dish and freeze for one hour.
  3. After the first hour, scrape the mixture with a fork to break up ice crystals. Repeat every hour for about 3 hours, until fluffy and textured.
  4. Serve in chilled cups and garnish with fresh mint leaves.

2. Grilled Pineapple with Honey and Cinnamon

Grilling transforms pineapple into a caramelized masterpiece. It’s sweet, smoky, and loaded with flavor, making it the perfect summer dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Combine honey and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  3. Brush the pineapple rings generously with the honey mixture.
  4. Grill the pineapple for 2-3 minutes per side until caramelized.
  5. Serve warm as-is or add a scoop of vanilla ice cream!

3. Peach and Raspberry Cobbler

Peaches and raspberries create a juicy, crowd-pleasing combination in this easy cobbler recipe. It’s the quintessential summer dessert for any occasion.

Ingredients:

For the filling:

  • 4 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

For the topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 1/3 cup milk

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Toss the peaches, raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice together and spread evenly in the dish.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs, then stir in milk to form a dough.
  4. Drop spoonfuls of the dough over the fruit.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the topping is golden and the fruit is bubbling generously. Let cool slightly before serving.

4. Strawberry Basil Sorbet

This unexpected pairing of strawberries with subtle hints of basil creates a refined, refreshing sorbet that’s perfect for summer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 6 basil leaves, loosely packed

Directions:

  1. Blend strawberries, sugar, lemon juice, and basil leaves until smooth.
  2. Pour the puree into an ice cream maker and follow manufacturer’s instructions, or freeze in a baking dish, stirring every 30 minutes until set.
  3. Serve scoops with a sprig of basil for garnish.

5. Mango Coconut Ice Cream (No Churn)

No ice cream machine? No problem! This creamy mango coconut ice cream is made with just a few ingredients and is as easy as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Puree the mangoes in a blender until smooth.
  2. Stir in coconut cream, condensed milk, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the mixture into a loaf pan or freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 4-6 hours.
  4. Scoop into bowls and serve topped with shredded coconut.

6. Blueberry Lemon Cake Bars

Bright blueberries and tangy lemon juice come together in these easy cake bars, an ideal treat for snacking or summer gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line an 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
  2. Beat butter and sugar together until creamy. Add eggs and lemon zest, mixing well.
  3. Combine flour, baking powder, and salt in a bowl, then alternately mix into the wet ingredients with buttermilk.
  4. Gently fold in blueberries, pour the batter into the pan, and bake for 25-30 minutes.
  5. Cool, cut into bars, and serve.

7. Cherry Vanilla Parfaits

These layered cherry vanilla parfaits are elegant yet quick to assemble, perfect for impressing summer guests without a hassle.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1 cup whipped cream or vanilla yogurt
  • 1/2 cup granola or crushed cookies

Directions:

  1. In serving glasses, layer cherries, whipped cream (or yogurt), and granola.
  2. Repeat until glasses are full and garnish with fresh cherries.
  3. Serve immediately or refrigerate briefly before serving.

8. Orange Creamsicle Smoothie

Capture the nostalgia of a classic creamsicle in smoothie form with fresh oranges and creamy goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large oranges, peeled and segmented
  • 1/2 cup vanilla yogurt
  • 1/2 cup milk (regular or plant-based)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Directions:

  1. Blend all the ingredients until smooth. Adjust sweetness if needed.
  2. Pour into chilled glasses, and enjoy as a dessert or mid-afternoon snack.

9. Plum and Almond Galette

Rustic and delicious, this plum galette is a simple way to showcase the flavor of ripe summer plums.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 4 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 medium plums, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons almond flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Directions:

  1. Mix flour, sugar, and salt, then cut in butter until crumbly. Add ice water gradually until a dough forms. Chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out dough on parchment paper, about 12 inches across. Sprinkle almond flour over the center, leaving a border.
  3. Toss plums with sugar and lemon juice, then pile onto the almond flour. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 40-45 minutes. Cool slightly before slicing.

10. Kiwi Lime Popsicles

These tangy homemade popsicles are an easy, no-fuss way to beat the summer heat.

Ingredients:

  • 6 kiwis, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup water

Directions:

  1. Blend kiwi slices, honey, lime juice, and water until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds and freeze for at least 4 hours.
  3. Pop them out and enjoy the tangy, icy goodness!

Transform your summer desserts with fresh fruit and simple steps. Not only are these recipes delicious, but they’re versatile and perfect for highlighting seasonal ingredients. Whether you’re entertaining guests or treating yourself, these fruit-based creations promise to elevate your summer sweets game while keeping things easy and fuss-free. Give them a try and embrace the bright, flavorful joys of summer to the fullest!
