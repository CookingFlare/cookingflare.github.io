10 Flavorful Vegetarian Recipes on a Budget


As the world becomes increasingly health-conscious, vegetarianism has become a popular choice for many. However, it’s often assumed that a vegetarian diet is expensive and inaccessible. But what if you could enjoy a flavorful and nutritious vegetarian meal without breaking the bank? In this blog post, we’ll explore 10 delicious and budget-friendly vegetarian recipes that will tantalize your taste buds and ease your wallet.

1. Lentil Soup

Lentils are an affordable and nutritious legume that can be used in a variety of dishes. This hearty soup is a staple in many vegetarian households and can be made for under $1.50 per serving.

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Combine all ingredients in a large pot and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes or until lentils are tender. Serve with a swirl of olive oil and a side of crusty bread.

2. Roasted Vegetable Quinoa Bowl

Quinoa is a protein-rich grain that’s packed with nutrients and can be cooked for under $1 per serving. This roasted vegetable quinoa bowl is a colorful and flavorful dish that’s perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Cook quinoa according to package instructions. Serve quinoa in a bowl and top with roasted vegetables.

3. Black Bean and Sweet Potato Tacos

Sweet potatoes are a nutritious and affordable root vegetable that can be used in a variety of dishes. These tacos are a flavorful and filling vegetarian option that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 6 tacos shells
  • Shredded cheese, lettuce, and any other desired toppings

Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss sweet potatoes with olive oil and roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender. In a large skillet, cook black beans, onion, and bell pepper over medium heat. Add taco seasoning and cook according to package instructions. Serve sweet potatoes and black bean mixture in tacos shells with desired toppings.

4. Spinach and Feta Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

Portobello mushrooms are a nutritious and affordable vegetarian option that can be used in a variety of dishes. These stuffed mushrooms are a flavorful and impressive vegetarian main course that can be made for under $3 per serving.

  • 4 Portobello mushrooms, stems removed and caps cleaned
  • 1 package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat oven to 375°F. In a bowl, combine spinach, feta cheese, and garlic. Stuff each mushroom cap with spinach mixture and drizzle with olive oil. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until mushrooms are tender and filling is heated through.

5. Butternut Squash and Kale Curry

Butternut squash is a nutritious and affordable winter squash that can be used in a variety of dishes. This curry is a flavorful and comforting vegetarian option that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups kale, stems removed and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

In a large pot, sauté onion and garlic over medium heat until tender. Add butternut squash and cook until tender. Add kale and cook until wilted. Stir in coconut milk, curry powder, and cumin. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve over rice or with naan bread.

6. Quinoa and Black Bean Salad

Quinoa and black beans are a protein-rich and affordable vegetarian combination that can be used in a variety of dishes. This salad is a refreshing and flavorful vegetarian option that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Cook quinoa according to package instructions. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil and lime juice. Pour dressing over quinoa mixture and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

7. Roasted Eggplant Parmesan

Eggplant is a nutritious and affordable vegetarian option that can be used in a variety of dishes. This roasted eggplant parmesan is a flavorful and impressive vegetarian main course that can be made for under $3 per serving.

  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped

Preheat oven to 400°F. In a bowl, combine breadcrumbs and Parmesan cheese. Dip each eggplant slice in beaten egg and then coat with breadcrumb mixture. Place on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Top with marinara sauce and shredded mozzarella cheese. Sprinkle with chopped basil leaves.

8. Chickpea and Spinach Curry

Chickpeas are a nutritious and affordable vegetarian option that can be used in a variety of dishes. This curry is a flavorful and comforting vegetarian option that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups spinach, stems removed and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

In a large pot, sauté onion and garlic over medium heat until tender. Add chickpeas and cook until heated through. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Stir in coconut milk, curry powder, and cumin. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve over rice or with naan bread.

9. Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Vegetables are a nutritious and affordable vegetarian option that can be used in a variety of dishes. These grilled vegetable skewers are a flavorful and impressive vegetarian main course that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Thread vegetables onto skewers. Brush with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill for 10-12 minutes or until tender. Serve hot.

10. Vegetable and Bean Chili

Beans are a nutritious and affordable vegetarian option that can be used in a variety of dishes. This chili is a flavorful and comforting vegetarian option that can be made for under $2 per serving.

  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

In a large pot, sauté onion and garlic over medium heat until tender. Add bell pepper and cook until tender. Add black beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Stir in chili powder. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with crusty bread or over rice.

In conclusion, these 10 delicious and budget-friendly vegetarian recipes are perfect for anyone looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet without breaking the bank. With a little creativity and planning, you can enjoy a flavorful and nutritious vegetarian meal without sacrificing taste or quality.
