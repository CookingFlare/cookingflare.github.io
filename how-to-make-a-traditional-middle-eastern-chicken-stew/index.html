How to Make a Traditional Middle Eastern Chicken Stew


If you’re looking to expand your culinary horizons, a Traditional Middle Eastern Chicken Stew is a perfect dish to explore. This hearty and flavorful stew is rich in spices, tender chicken, and aromatic vegetables, making it a crowd-pleasing meal that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of the Middle East. Packed with history, this dish combines age-old cooking techniques with fresh ingredients to create a comforting and satisfying meal for any occasion.

In this post, we’ll dive into the step-by-step process of creating an authentic Middle Eastern chicken stew that brings together diverse flavors, from fragrant cinnamon and cumin to the savory depth of garlic and onions. Whether you’re an experienced cook or a novice, this recipe is easy to follow and incredibly rewarding.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before diving into the cooking process, it’s essential to gather the right ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need for this traditional Middle Eastern chicken stew:

  • Chicken: 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs (or use a whole chicken cut into pieces)
  • Onions: 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
  • Tomatoes: 3 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • Carrots: 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • Potatoes: 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • Chickpeas: 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed (or use dried chickpeas that have been soaked overnight)
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Spices:
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
    • 1 teaspoon ground allspice (optional)
    • Salt to taste
  • Herbs:
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 small bunch of fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon: 1 lemon, juiced
  • Water or Chicken Broth: 5 cups (you can substitute water with chicken broth for more flavor)

Preparing the Chicken

The chicken is the star of this Middle Eastern stew, and it’s essential to properly prepare it to enhance the flavors. Start by washing your chicken pieces under cold water and patting them dry with a paper towel. This will help ensure the chicken browns evenly when seared.

Next, season the chicken generously with salt, pepper, and some of the ground cumin. If you’re using a whole chicken, cut it into pieces to make the cooking process easier.

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken pieces skin-side down (if using bone-in thighs or a whole chicken) and brown them on both sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes per side. Browning the chicken adds a depth of flavor to the stew, making it more complex and savory. After browning, remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

Cooking the Vegetables

Now that the chicken is browned, it’s time to bring in the vegetables. In the same pot, add a bit more olive oil if necessary. Begin by sautéing the finely chopped onions over medium heat. Stir occasionally, cooking them until they become soft and golden brown, which will take about 8 minutes. The caramelization of the onions is crucial for developing a sweet and rich base for the stew.

Once the onions are soft and aromatic, add the minced garlic to the pot. Stir frequently to prevent burning, and cook for about 1 minute until the garlic becomes fragrant. At this point, it’s time to introduce the spices. Add the cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, paprika, black pepper, ginger, and allspice (if using) to the pot. Stir the spices into the onions and garlic, allowing them to bloom in the hot oil. This step will release the oils from the spices, intensifying their flavors.

After the spices have been toasted, add the chopped tomatoes to the pot. Stir the mixture and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the tomatoes to break down and create a sauce. The tomatoes will provide a tangy balance to the rich, earthy spices.

Adding the Vegetables and Liquid

With the tomato mixture cooking away, it’s time to add the hearty vegetables to the stew. Start by adding the sliced carrots and cubed potatoes. These vegetables will soak up the flavors from the spices and chicken, making them tender and flavorful. Add the chickpeas to the pot as well. If you’re using canned chickpeas, make sure they are well-drained and rinsed to remove any excess salt or preservatives.

Now, return the browned chicken to the pot, nestling the pieces among the vegetables. Pour in the water or chicken broth, ensuring that the chicken and vegetables are fully submerged. Add the bay leaves at this stage for extra depth of flavor. Bring the stew to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let everything simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.

Adjusting the Seasoning

Once the stew has simmered, it’s time to taste and adjust the seasoning. This is an important step because the flavors from the spices, chicken, and vegetables will meld together during cooking, and you may find that the dish needs a little more salt, pepper, or lemon juice to brighten it up.

Add more salt to taste and a generous squeeze of lemon juice to balance the richness of the dish. The acidity from the lemon will lift the flavors and bring out the best in the stew.

Serving the Stew

Once the stew is done, carefully remove the chicken pieces from the pot. If you used bone-in chicken, remove the bones, or simply serve the pieces whole for a more rustic presentation. Return the chicken to the pot and give everything one final stir. The stew should be thick, flavorful, and perfectly seasoned.

Serve the stew hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness. You can enjoy this Middle Eastern chicken stew with a side of steamed rice, couscous, or warm flatbread. The stew is hearty enough to stand on its own, but these sides complement the dish beautifully.

Tips for the Perfect Middle Eastern Chicken Stew

  1. Use bone-in chicken: Bone-in chicken provides more flavor and tenderness, making your stew extra rich. However, you can also use boneless skinless chicken thighs if preferred for a quicker cooking time.

  2. Adjust the spices to your liking: If you like your stew with a little more heat, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a couple of chopped green chilies when you sauté the onions and garlic.

  3. Add more vegetables: Feel free to experiment with additional vegetables such as zucchini, bell peppers, or even eggplant. The versatility of this dish allows you to tailor it to your preferences.

  4. Make it ahead of time: Like most stews, this dish tastes even better the next day when the flavors have had more time to meld together. You can make it ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to 3 days.

  5. Freezing for later: This stew freezes well! Simply let it cool completely, then store it in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 3 months. To reheat, thaw overnight in the fridge and warm on the stove.

Conclusion

This Traditional Middle Eastern Chicken Stew is a comforting, flavorful dish that brings together the best of Middle Eastern spices, tender chicken, and hearty vegetables. It’s the perfect meal to serve on a chilly evening or for a family gathering. With its vibrant colors, rich aromas, and satisfying flavors, this stew is sure to become a staple in your cooking repertoire. Enjoy the process, savor every bite, and share this recipe with friends and family for an unforgettable culinary experience.

Let this chicken stew take you on a journey through the Middle East with every mouthwatering spoonful. Happy cooking!
