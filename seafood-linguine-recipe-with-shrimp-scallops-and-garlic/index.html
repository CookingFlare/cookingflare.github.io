Seafood Linguine Recipe with Shrimp, Scallops, and Garlic


When it comes to indulgent and flavorful pasta dishes, seafood linguine with shrimp, scallops, and garlic is a classic favorite. This elegant dish brings together the briny goodness of seafood with the aromatic punch of garlic, all tossed with al dente linguine to create a harmonious and satisfying meal. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply craving a luxurious homemade meal, this seafood linguine recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces linguine
  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound sea scallops
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for a touch of heat)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Seafood: Begin by patting the shrimp and scallops dry with paper towels. Season them with a pinch of salt and pepper.

  2. Cook the Linguine: In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook the linguine until it is al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta water.

  3. Sauté the Seafood: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and scallops to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are golden and cooked through. Remove the seafood from the skillet and set it aside.

  4. Infuse the Garlic: In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and sauté for about a minute, allowing it to become fragrant but not browned.

  5. Deglaze with Wine: Pour the white wine into the skillet, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Allow the wine to simmer for a couple of minutes, reducing slightly.

  6. Combine the Ingredients: Return the cooked seafood to the skillet, along with the drained linguine. Toss everything together gently, allowing the flavors to meld.

  7. Add Finishing Touches: If you like a hint of heat, sprinkle in some red pepper flakes. Season the dish with additional salt and pepper to taste. If the pasta seems dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to moisten it slightly.

  8. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle the chopped parsley over the seafood linguine, giving it a vibrant pop of color and freshness. Serve the linguine hot, with crusty bread on the side to soak up the delicious juices.

Tips for Success:

  • Fresh Seafood: Opt for the freshest shrimp and scallops available to ensure the best flavor and texture in your dish.
  • Al Dente Linguine: Be mindful not to overcook the linguine; it should have a slight bite to it, known as al dente.
  • Wine Selection: Choose a dry white wine that you enjoy drinking, as its flavor will infuse the dish.
  • Customization: Feel free to personalize the dish with additional seafood such as mussels or clams for a varied seafood medley.

In Conclusion:

Seafood linguine with shrimp, scallops, and garlic is a delightful marriage of flavors that elevates the dining experience. The succulent seafood, aromatic garlic, and al dente linguine come together to create a dish that is both elegant and comforting. Whether you’re a seafood enthusiast or a pasta lover, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to savor the bounty of the sea in a delightful pasta presentation.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and prepare to delight your senses with this sumptuous seafood linguine creation. Buon appetito!
