5 Easy Muffin Recipes for Kids to Bake


Baking muffins with kids is a wonderful way to create lasting memories, teach valuable skills, and enjoy some delicious treats together. Muffins are perfect because they’re simple to make, adaptable to different tastes, and a great way to sneak in some healthy ingredients. Whether you’re looking for a quick breakfast, an after-school snack, or something fun to make on a rainy afternoon, these five easy muffin recipes will delight young bakers and their families alike. Each recipe includes clear, straightforward steps designed to keep things fun and safe in the kitchen, plus plenty of room for little chefs to experiment with their favorite flavors.

1. Classic Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins

Bananas add natural sweetness and moisture, making these muffins soft and flavorful. The addition of chocolate chips turns them into a kid-favorite treat perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup melted butter
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the mashed bananas and melted butter.
  3. Stir in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract until well mixed.
  4. Sprinkle the baking soda and salt over the mixture and stir to combine.
  5. Add the flour, mixing just until the dry ingredients are incorporated. Avoid over-mixing.
  6. Fold in the mini chocolate chips.
  7. Spoon the batter evenly into the muffin cups.
  8. Bake for about 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving.

This recipe is an excellent way to use leftover ripe bananas and get kids involved with measuring and mixing.

2. Blueberry Lemon Muffins

Bright and tangy, these blueberry lemon muffins feel like a burst of summertime. They’re packed with fresh blueberries and a little lemon zest, making them refreshing yet comforting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Directions:

  1. Heat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin pan with liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, salt, and baking powder.
  3. In a separate small bowl, combine oil, egg, milk, and lemon zest.
  4. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined.
  5. Carefully fold in the blueberries to avoid breaking them.
  6. Distribute batter evenly among muffin cups.
  7. Bake for approximately 18-20 minutes, or until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Cool before removing from the pan.

This recipe encourages kids to practice gentle folding techniques and learn about how zest changes flavor.

3. Cheesy Zucchini Muffins

Not all muffins have to be sweet! These cheesy zucchini muffins are savory, packed with veggies, and make a perfect side dish or snack for growing kids.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated zucchini (squeeze out excess water)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup milk

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat eggs with oil and milk.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients, stirring until just mixed.
  5. Fold in grated zucchini and shredded cheese.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin liners.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes until muffins are set and golden on top.
  8. Cool slightly before serving.

These muffins introduce kids to savory flavors and the concept of including vegetables in baked treats.

4. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

Sweet apples with warm cinnamon spice make these muffins a comforting choice any time of year. This recipe incorporates fresh diced apples, creating a moist and fragrant result.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk (or milk with 1 tbsp vinegar)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup peeled, diced apples (about 1 medium apple)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a muffin tin with liners.
  2. Mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, salt, and sugar in a bowl.
  3. In another bowl, whisk applesauce, buttermilk, egg, and vanilla.
  4. Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients, stirring gently.
  5. Fold in diced apples carefully.
  6. Fill muffin cups about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until tops spring back when touched.
  8. Let cool before eating.

These muffins are a delicious way to introduce kids to the warm, inviting spices of fall.

5. Peanut Butter and Jelly Muffins

This playful muffin takes inspiration from everyone’s favorite sandwich. It’s jam-packed with peanut butter flavor and a swirl of fruit jelly in the middle.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (creamy or chunky)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup fruit jelly or jam (your kid’s favorite)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a muffin pan.
  2. Mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  3. In another, beat together peanut butter and sugar until creamy.
  4. Stir in milk, egg, and vanilla until smooth.
  5. Add dry ingredients to wet, mixing just until combined.
  6. Spoon a bit of batter into each muffin cup, then add a small dollop of jelly.
  7. Cover with remaining batter.
  8. Bake for 18-20 minutes, keeping an eye so the jelly doesn’t bubble over.
  9. Cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring to a rack.

These muffins bring back nostalgic sandwich flavors and introduce kids to layering and swirling techniques in baking.

Tips for Baking Muffins with Kids

To make the experience enjoyable and safe for little bakers, it helps to have a few practices ready:

  • Pre-measure ingredients: This makes the process smoother and focuses on teaching mixing and pouring skills.
  • Use simple tools: Child-friendly mixing bowls, spoons, and measuring cups keep little hands engaged.
  • Supervise heat and knives: Adults should handle oven use and any cutting or grating.
  • Encourage creativity: Let kids add mix-ins or experiment with flavors to build confidence.
  • Teach patience: Waiting for the muffins to bake is a great chance to talk about timing and watching for changes in the batter and texture.
  • Highlight cleaning up: Baking can be messy but tidying up together is part of the process.

Each of these muffin recipes is a doorway to a fun and sensory kitchen adventure. Baking together not only results in tasty rewards but also nurtures important skills like following directions, measuring, and understanding ingredients. Whether you bake these muffins as a family project, in a classroom, or as a simple weekend activity, the joy of creating and sharing homemade treats is an experience kids will treasure—and remember.

Happy baking!
