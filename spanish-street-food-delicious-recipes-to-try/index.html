Spanish Street Food: Delicious Recipes to Try


When you think of Spanish cuisine, images of vibrant markets, bustling streets, and tantalizing aromas come to mind. Spanish street food captures the essence of this rich culinary culture, offering an array of flavors that reflect the country’s diverse traditions and regional specialties. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most delicious street food recipes from Spain, bringing a taste of the Iberian Peninsula straight to your kitchen. Prepare your taste buds for a delightful journey through the streets of Spain!

1. Patatas Bravas

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the sauce:
    • 1 cup mayonnaise
    • 2 tablespoons tomato sauce
    • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Fry the Potatoes: Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the diced potatoes and cook until golden brown and crispy, about 15-20 minutes. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a bowl, combine the mayonnaise, tomato sauce, hot sauce (if using), and smoked paprika. Mix well until smooth.

  3. Season the Potatoes: Once the potatoes are cooked, season them with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Toss to coat evenly.

  4. Serve: Plate the potatoes and drizzle with the sauce. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot. These crispy, spicy potatoes are perfect for sharing or enjoying as a snack!

2. Churros

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Oil for frying
  • For the sugar coating:
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Make the Dough: In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil. Once boiling, remove from heat and stir in the flour and vegetable oil until a dough forms.

  2. Pipe the Churros: Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Pipe strips of dough into the hot oil, cutting them to your desired length.

  3. Fry: Fry the churros until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove them from the oil and drain on paper towels.

  4. Coat with Sugar: In a shallow bowl, mix sugar and cinnamon. Roll the churros in the mixture while they are still warm.

  5. Serve with Chocolate: For an authentic experience, serve with a thick hot chocolate for dipping. These sweet treats are an irresistible accompaniment to any street food feast!

3. Tortilla Española

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 4 medium potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced potatoes (and onions, if using) and cook until soft, about 15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  2. Beat the Eggs: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and season with salt.

  3. Combine: Drain the potatoes and onions from the oil and let them cool slightly. Add them to the bowl with the beaten eggs and mix well.

  4. Cook the Tortilla: Wipe the skillet clean and add a bit more oil. Pour the mixture back in and cook over low heat for about 5-7 minutes, or until the bottom is set. Carefully flip the tortilla using a plate and cook the other side for another 5 minutes.

  5. Serve: Let it cool slightly before slicing. Serve it warm or at room temperature. This Spanish omelette is a staple in tapas bars and makes for a delicious street food option.

4. Pimientos de Padrón

Ingredients

  • 250 grams Padrón peppers
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Sea salt to taste
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Heat the Oil: In a frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.

  2. Cook the Peppers: Add the Padrón peppers to the pan and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until they are blistered and slightly charred. Keep them moving in the pan to ensure even cooking.

  3. Season: Once cooked, remove from heat and sprinkle with sea salt.

  4. Serve: Transfer to a serving plate and serve hot with lemon wedges. These small green peppers are usually mild, but occasionally you’ll find a spicy one—making them a fun surprise!

5. Empanadas

Ingredients

  • For the dough:
    • 3 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup butter, softened
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 1 egg
    • 1 teaspoon salt
  • For the filling:
    • 1 cup cooked and shredded chicken (or beef)
    • 1/2 cup onions, finely chopped
    • 1/2 cup bell peppers, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions

  1. Make the Dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add water and egg, and knead until a smooth dough forms. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté onions and bell peppers until soft. Add the shredded chicken (or beef), paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until heated through. Let cool.

  3. Assemble the Empanadas: Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thick. Cut into circles (about 4 inches in diameter). Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle. Fold over and crimp the edges to seal.

  4. Fry or Bake: You can either deep-fry the empanadas in hot oil until golden brown or bake them in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 20 minutes until golden.

  5. Serve: Serve warm, and enjoy the delightful combination of flaky pastry and savory filling!

6. Bocadillos

Ingredients

  • 1 crusty baguette or Spanish bread
  • 1/2 pound jamón ibérico or Spanish chorizo
  • 1/2 cup manchego cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh arugula or spinach (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bread: Slice the baguette into sandwiches, or cut it lengthwise.

  2. Layer the Ingredients: Drizzle olive oil on the bread, then layer with slices of jamón ibérico or chorizo and manchego cheese. Add fresh arugula or spinach for a peppery crunch.

  3. Press and Serve: If desired, press the sandwiches on a grill pan or sandwich press for a few minutes until the cheese melts. Cut into smaller pieces for easy eating on the go.

These bocadillos are a perfect street food option, providing rich flavors and textures in every bite!

7. Flamenquín

Ingredients

  • 4 slices of ham (Serrano or similar)
  • 4 slices of pork tenderloin
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Assemble the Flamenquín: Place a slice of ham on top of a slice of pork. Roll tightly and secure with toothpicks.

  2. Bread the Rolls: Dip each roll into the beaten egg, then coat with breadcrumbs, ensuring they are fully covered.

  3. Fry: Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the flamenquines until golden brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Serve: Drain on paper towels and remove toothpicks before serving. These crispy, meaty rolls are a delightful street food treat!

Conclusion

Spanish street food offers a wealth of flavors, textures, and experiences that are sure to please any palate. From the crispy Patatas Bravas to the sweet Churros and savory Tortilla Española, each dish tells a story of Spain’s rich culinary heritage. Whether you’re hosting a tapas night or simply craving a taste of Spain, these recipes are easy to make and perfect for sharing.

So gather your ingredients, fire up your stove, and embark on a delicious adventure through the streets of Spain. Enjoy the vibrant flavors and the joy of cooking authentic street food right in your own home!
