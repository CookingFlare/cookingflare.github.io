10 Classic Pie Recipes You Need to Try Today


Pies are the quintessential dessert, offering a perfect blend of rich fillings and flaky, golden crusts. From fruity favorites to creamy custards, there is a pie for every taste and occasion. Whether you’re baking for a holiday feast or looking to treat yourself on a quiet afternoon, these classic pie recipes are sure to impress. Let’s explore 10 timeless pies that deserve a spot in your kitchen.

1. Apple Pie

A slice of apple pie is like a warm hug in dessert form. The combination of sweet, tart apples, cinnamon, and a buttery, flaky crust makes it a perennial favorite in the world of baking.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of peeled and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work best)
  • ¾ cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of flour (for thickening)
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • Pie crust (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the apple slices with sugar, flour, cinnamon, and lemon juice until evenly coated.
  3. Roll out your pie crust and line a 9-inch pie dish with the bottom crust. Fill the crust with the apple mixture.
  4. Dot the apples with butter and place the top crust over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  5. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  7. Let the pie cool before serving, but it’s best enjoyed warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

2. Pumpkin Pie

Pumpkin pie is a classic holiday dessert, especially popular in the fall. Its smooth, spiced filling is perfectly complemented by a tender crust, making it a favorite at Thanksgiving and other family gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of pureed pumpkin
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • ¾ cup of brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon of ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon of nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pie crust (homemade or store-bought)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin, heavy cream, eggs, brown sugar, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt until smooth.
  3. Pour the filling into the pie crust and smooth the top.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 45-50 minutes, or until the center is set.
  5. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving. Top with a dollop of whipped cream for an extra touch of indulgence.

3. Pecan Pie

Pecan pie is a rich, sweet pie with a deep caramel flavor and a satisfying crunch from the toasted pecans. It’s perfect for those who love nutty desserts with a bit of sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of pecan halves
  • 1 cup of corn syrup
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, vanilla, and salt.
  3. Stir in the pecans and pour the filling into the pie crust.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly cracked on top.
  5. Let the pie cool completely before slicing. Serve with a scoop of ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra treat.

4. Cherry Pie

Made with sweet or tart cherries, this pie is bursting with flavor. The contrast between the juicy filling and the crisp crust is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of fresh or frozen cherries (pitted)
  • ¾ cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 pie crust (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the cherries release their juice, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Roll out the bottom crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Pour the cherry filling into the crust.
  4. Place the top crust over the filling and trim the edges. Crimp to seal and cut slits in the top for ventilation.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.

5. Lemon Meringue Pie

Lemon meringue pie combines the tartness of lemon with the sweetness of meringue. Its light, fluffy top contrasts beautifully with the zesty lemon filling, making it a refreshing choice for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of sugar
  • ¼ cup of cornstarch
  • 1 ½ cups of water
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/3 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 4 large egg whites
  • ½ teaspoon of cream of tartar
  • ¼ cup of sugar
  • 1 pie crust (pre-baked)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine sugar, cornstarch, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolks, then gradually add a little bit of the hot mixture to temper the eggs. Slowly pour the egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter. Stir until smooth.
  5. Pour the lemon filling into the pre-baked pie crust and set aside.
  6. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.
  7. Spread the meringue over the lemon filling, making sure to seal the edges to the crust.
  8. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the meringue is golden.
  9. Let the pie cool before serving. It’s best enjoyed chilled!

6. Key Lime Pie

Key lime pie is a tangy, creamy pie with a distinctive flavor from the small, tart key limes. This dessert is perfect for those who enjoy a zesty and refreshing treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) of sweetened condensed milk
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup of fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon of lime zest
  • 1 pre-baked graham cracker crust
  • Whipped cream for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, egg yolks, lime juice, and lime zest until smooth.
  3. Pour the filling into the graham cracker crust and spread evenly.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the filling is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.
  5. Allow the pie to cool before refrigerating for at least 2 hours.
  6. Top with whipped cream before serving. This pie is best served chilled.

7. Banoffee Pie

Banoffee pie is a delicious British dessert made with a toffee filling, bananas, and whipped cream. It’s indulgent, satisfying, and sure to satisfy any sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) of sweetened condensed milk (for toffee)
  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 ½ cups of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 pre-baked graham cracker crust
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the sweetened condensed milk over medium heat, stirring constantly, for about 10 minutes, until it thickens and turns a caramel color.
  2. Pour the toffee into the pre-baked graham cracker crust and spread evenly.
  3. Slice the bananas and arrange them on top of the toffee layer.
  4. Whip the heavy cream and sugar until stiff peaks form, then spread the whipped cream over the bananas.
  5. Top with dark chocolate shavings, if desired.
  6. Refrigerate for 2-3 hours before serving for the best texture.

8. Chocolate Cream Pie

Chocolate cream pie is a dessert lover’s dream, with a rich and velvety chocolate filling and a silky smooth whipped cream topping. It’s perfect for chocolate enthusiasts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of whole milk
  • ½ cup of sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • Whipped cream for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together the milk, sugar, cocoa powder, and cornstarch. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolks, then slowly temper them by adding a bit of the hot milk mixture. Gradually whisk the egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan and continue cooking until thickened.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the butter and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the chocolate filling into the pre-baked pie crust and smooth the top.
  5. Refrigerate the pie for at least 3 hours, or until set.
  6. Top with whipped cream before serving. Enjoy the chocolatey goodness!

9. Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

Strawberry rhubarb pie strikes the perfect balance between sweet and tart, thanks to the combination of ripe strawberries and tangy rhubarb. It’s a beloved spring and summer dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups of chopped rhubarb
  • 2 cups of sliced strawberries
  • 1 ¼ cups of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 pie crust (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the rhubarb, strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the filling into the bottom pie crust.
  4. Cover with the top crust, crimp the edges, and cut slits to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy the perfect balance of flavors.

10. Shoofly Pie

Shoofly pie is a traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dessert with a molasses-based filling and a crumbly topping. It’s a warm, comforting pie with a deep, rich flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of molasses
  • 1 cup of hot water
  • 1 tablespoon of baking soda
  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup of brown sugar
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ¼ cup of butter
  • 1 pre-baked pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the molasses, hot water, and baking soda.
  3. In another bowl, combine the flour, brown sugar, salt, and butter to create the crumb topping.
  4. Pour the molasses mixture into the pre-baked pie crust and top with the crumb mixture.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden and the filling is set.
  6. Let the pie cool before serving. It’s a cozy treat perfect for any occasion.

From fruity classics like apple and cherry pie to indulgent treats like pecan and chocolate cream pie, these 10 pie recipes offer a wide range of flavors and textures that will satisfy any craving. Each recipe is easy to follow and results in a homemade dessert that will wow your friends and family. Get baking and enjoy the comforting goodness of these classic pies!
