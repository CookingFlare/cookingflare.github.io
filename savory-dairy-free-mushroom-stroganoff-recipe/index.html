Savory Dairy-Free Mushroom Stroganoff Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes are as inviting as mushroom stroganoff. Traditionally creamy and rich, this beloved classic can be made entirely dairy-free without sacrificing flavor or texture. This savory dairy-free mushroom stroganoff is perfect for those who are lactose intolerant, vegan, or simply looking to cut back on dairy. With its hearty mushrooms, silky sauce, and satisfying pasta, this dish will make a delightful addition to your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

To create this delicious dairy-free mushroom stroganoff, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Stroganoff:

  • 12 oz (340g) wide pasta (like fettuccine or egg noodles)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 16 oz (450g) mushrooms (such as cremini or button), sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (for a gluten-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or arrowroot powder
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk or any other plant-based milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Optional Add-Ins:

  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast (for a cheesy flavor)
  • Fresh lemon juice (for brightness)

Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the wide pasta of your choice and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. Once cooked, drain the pasta and set it aside, reserving about 1 cup of pasta water. This starchy water can help thicken the sauce later if needed.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until the onion is translucent and fragrant.
  2. Add the minced garlic and continue sautéing for an additional minute, being careful not to let it burn. The aroma of the garlic will fill your kitchen with a delightful scent.

Step 3: Cook the Mushrooms

  1. Toss in the sliced mushrooms to the skillet. Sauté for about 8-10 minutes until they release their moisture and begin to brown.
  2. Stir frequently to ensure even cooking. The mushrooms will shrink in size and develop a deep, rich flavor, which is the essence of stroganoff.

Step 4: Add the Seasonings

  1. Sprinkle in the dried thyme and smoked paprika, mixing them into the mushrooms for about a minute. These spices will enhance the earthy flavors of the dish.
  2. Pour in the soy sauce or tamari, stirring to combine. This adds a savory depth that complements the mushrooms beautifully.

Step 5: Create the Creamy Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and vegetable broth until smooth. This mixture will help thicken the sauce.
  2. Slowly add the vegetable broth mixture to the skillet, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  3. Next, pour in the unsweetened almond milk, continuing to stir until the sauce thickens and becomes creamy. If the sauce is too thick, you can add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach the desired consistency.

Step 6: Combine with Pasta

  1. Once the sauce has thickened, add the cooked pasta to the skillet. Toss everything together until the pasta is well coated in the creamy mushroom sauce.
  2. If you’re including frozen peas, add them at this stage and cook for another 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a splash of fresh lemon juice or a tablespoon of nutritional yeast.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Once everything is combined, remove the skillet from heat.
  2. Serve immediately, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. The vibrant green of the parsley adds a lovely touch and freshness to the dish.
  3. Enjoy your savory dairy-free mushroom stroganoff warm, perhaps alongside a crisp green salad or crusty bread.

Tips for Success

  • Mushroom Varieties: Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms. Shiitake, portobello, or even a mix can add unique flavors and textures.
  • Pasta Alternatives: You can substitute traditional pasta with gluten-free options, zucchini noodles, or even quinoa for a healthier twist.
  • Make It Ahead: This dish can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of vegetable broth or almond milk to revive the creamy texture.
  • Freezing: If you wish to freeze this stroganoff, do so before adding the pasta. Thaw and reheat the sauce, then mix in freshly cooked pasta just before serving.

Nutritional Benefits

This savory dairy-free mushroom stroganoff is not only comforting but also packed with nutrients. Mushrooms are a great source of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. They provide essential nutrients such as vitamin D, B vitamins, and selenium. The use of whole-grain or legume-based pasta increases fiber content, making this meal more filling and nutritious.

Additionally, by using plant-based milk, you can enjoy a creamy texture without the saturated fats found in dairy products. This dish is a great option for those looking to maintain a balanced diet while enjoying a classic comfort meal.

Conclusion

This savory dairy-free mushroom stroganoff is a fantastic way to indulge in a classic dish without compromising dietary preferences. With its rich flavors, creamy texture, and wholesome ingredients, it’s sure to become a favorite among both vegan and non-vegan eaters alike. Perfect for weeknight dinners or special occasions, this recipe showcases how delicious dairy-free cooking can be.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and prepare to impress your family and friends with this delightful dish. Enjoy the process as much as the result, and don’t forget to share your creations and variations! Happy cooking!
