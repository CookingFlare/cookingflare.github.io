Mouthwatering Nut-Free Apple Crumble Recipe


There’s nothing quite like the comforting aroma of a freshly baked apple crumble wafting through the kitchen. This classic dessert is a crowd-pleaser, with its warm, cinnamon-spiced apples and crunchy crumble topping. However, for those with nut allergies, enjoying this delightful treat can be a challenge. That’s why I’ve created a nut-free version of the beloved apple crumble that’s just as delicious and satisfying. Whether you have a nut allergy or simply prefer to enjoy your apple crumble without nuts, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

For the Apple Filling:

  • 6 large apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Crumble Topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated, then transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out into an even layer.

  3. In a separate bowl, prepare the crumble topping. Combine the flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the softened butter and use your hands to mix everything together until large crumbs form.

  4. Sprinkle the crumble topping over the apple mixture in the baking dish, covering it completely.

  5. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crumble topping is golden brown and the apple filling is bubbling around the edges.

  6. Once baked, remove the apple crumble from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

  7. Serve the nut-free apple crumble warm, topped with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

Tips for Success

  • For the best texture, use a mix of sweet and tart apples such as Honeycrisp, Granny Smith, or Fuji.
  • If you prefer a thicker apple filling, you can increase the amount of flour used to coat the apples before baking.
  • Leftover apple crumble can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven before serving.

Enjoy Your Nut-Free Apple Crumble!

This nut-free apple crumble is a delightful dessert that’s perfect for any occasion, from casual family dinners to festive gatherings. The combination of tender, spiced apples and buttery, golden crumble topping is simply irresistible. Whether you’re accommodating nut allergies or simply looking for a new twist on a classic recipe, this nut-free apple crumble is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of this mouthwatering dessert!
