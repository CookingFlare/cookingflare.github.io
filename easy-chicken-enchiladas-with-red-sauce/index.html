Easy Chicken Enchiladas with Red Sauce


Enchiladas are a beloved staple in Mexican cuisine, and for good reason. These delicious tortillas wrapped around a savory filling, smothered in rich sauce, are sure to please any crowd. In this post, we’ll explore a simple yet flavorful recipe for easy chicken enchiladas with red sauce that you can whip up in no time. Perfect for weeknight dinners, gatherings, or meal prepping, this dish is both satisfying and delightful.

The Essentials for Making Chicken Enchiladas

Ingredients

To create mouthwatering chicken enchiladas, gather the following ingredients:

For the Enchiladas:

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • 1 cup chopped onion (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

For the Red Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) red enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (to thin the sauce, if needed)
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional for a smoky flavor)

Special Equipment

  • Baking dish (9x13 inches)
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Saucepan

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

Start by preparing the chicken filling for your enchiladas. In a mixing bowl, combine the shredded chicken, half of the shredded cheese, chopped onions (if using), garlic powder, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined. Set aside.

Step 2: Make the Red Sauce

In a saucepan over medium heat, pour in your red enchilada sauce. If you prefer a thinner consistency, add the chicken broth and whisk until smooth. Stir in the lime juice, oregano, and smoked paprika. Heat the sauce until it’s warm, but don’t bring it to a boil. This flavorful red sauce will enhance your enchiladas and infuse them with deliciousness.

Step 3: Prepare the Tortillas

To make rolling the tortillas easier, it’s essential to soften them. You can do this in one of two ways:

  1. Microwave Method: Wrap a stack of tortillas in a damp paper towel and microwave for about 30 seconds. This will steam them and make them pliable.

  2. Pan Method: Alternatively, heat a skillet over medium heat. Lightly toast each tortilla for about 10-15 seconds on each side until soft.

Step 4: Assemble the Enchiladas

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

Now it’s time to assemble the enchiladas. Take one tortilla and spoon a generous amount of the chicken filling into the center. Roll it tightly and place it seam-side down in your baking dish. Repeat this process with the remaining tortillas and filling.

Step 5: Pour the Red Sauce

Once all the enchiladas are in the baking dish, pour the remaining red sauce evenly over the top, ensuring that each enchilada is well-coated. Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese over the sauce for that gooey, cheesy finish.

Step 6: Bake the Enchiladas

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and slightly golden.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once baked, remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro if desired. Serve hot with your favorite sides, such as Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh salad.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Enchiladas

  1. Customize Your Filling: Feel free to add black beans, corn, or diced bell peppers to your filling for extra flavor and texture.

  2. Make It Spicy: If you enjoy a bit of heat, consider adding chopped jalapeños or a sprinkle of cayenne pepper to the filling or sauce.

  3. Use Leftovers: This recipe is a great way to use up leftover chicken or even turkey. Just shred it and mix it with your seasoning!

  4. Make-Ahead Option: You can assemble the enchiladas a day in advance and store them in the refrigerator. Just add a few extra minutes to the baking time if baking from cold.

  5. Freezing Enchiladas: If you want to prepare a batch for later, you can freeze the assembled but unbaked enchiladas. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and foil. When ready to eat, bake from frozen, adding about 15-20 minutes to the baking time.

Conclusion

These easy chicken enchiladas with red sauce are not only simple to make but also bursting with flavor. Perfect for a weeknight meal or a festive gathering, they are sure to impress your family and friends. With a few basic ingredients and a little time, you can create a dish that feels special and satisfying. Enjoy your culinary adventure, and don’t hesitate to experiment with different fillings and toppings to make this recipe your own. Happy cooking!
