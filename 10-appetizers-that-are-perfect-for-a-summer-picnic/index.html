10 Appetizers That Are Perfect for a Summer Picnic


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to gather your friends and family for a picnic. There’s something so carefree and joyful about spending a warm day outdoors, surrounded by good company and delicious food. And what’s a picnic without a few tasty appetizers to get the party started?

In this post, we’ll share 10 appetizers that are perfect for a summer picnic. From classic combinations to innovative twists, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and satisfy their cravings. So grab your picnic basket and let’s get started!

Grilled Peach and Prosciutto Skewers

Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the sweet, juicy flavor of peaches. Combine them with crispy prosciutto and a sprinkle of fresh mint, and you’ve got a sweet and savory appetizer that’s sure to be a hit.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, sliced
  • 6 slices of prosciutto
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread peach slices and prosciutto slices onto skewers.
  3. Brush with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until prosciutto is crispy.
  5. Garnish with fresh mint leaves.

Heirloom Tomato Bruschetta

There’s nothing more summery than a fresh heirloom tomato. Top toasted bread with a slice of tomato, a sprinkle of basil, and a drizzle of olive oil, and you’ve got a simple yet elegant appetizer that’s perfect for a picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 4 heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 baguette, sliced and toasted
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Slice baguette and toast until crispy.
  3. Top each slice with a slice of tomato and a sprinkle of basil.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

Spicy Shrimp Ceviche

Ceviche is a refreshing and light appetizer that’s perfect for a summer picnic. Marinate shrimp in lime juice, mixed with diced mango and red onion, and you’ve got a spicy and tangy treat that’s sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine shrimp, lime juice, mango, red onion, and jalapeño.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  3. Serve chilled, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

Caprese Salad Skewers

The classic Caprese salad gets a fun twist with these colorful skewers. Alternate cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves on skewers, and drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella balls
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Alternate cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves on skewers.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Aioli

Asparagus is at its peak in the summer months, and grilling it brings out its natural sweetness. Serve it with a tangy and creamy lemon aioli for a delicious and refreshing appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill asparagus for 2-3 minutes per side, or until tender.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, lemon juice, and garlic.
  4. Serve grilled asparagus with lemon aioli.

Mini Quiches with Caramelized Onions

Mini quiches are a great make-ahead appetizer for a picnic. Fill them with caramelized onions, goat cheese, and fresh thyme for a savory and delicious treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out puff pastry and cut into small squares.
  3. In a large skillet, cook onions over low heat for 30-40 minutes, or until caramelized.
  4. In a small bowl, beat eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Spoon a small amount of caramelized onions onto each square of puff pastry, followed by a sprinkle of goat cheese and thyme.
  6. Fold pastry squares in half and press edges to seal.
  7. Brush tops with egg wash and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

Smoked Salmon Canapés

Smoked salmon is a delicious and elegant appetizer that’s perfect for a summer picnic. Serve it on toasted bagels with cream cheese and capers for a tasty and refreshing treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package smoked salmon
  • 1 bagel, toasted
  • 1/4 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Spread cream cheese on toasted bagel.
  2. Top with smoked salmon and capers.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Grilled Halloumi with Honey and Thyme

Halloumi is a type of Cypriot cheese that’s perfect for grilling. Serve it with a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of thyme for a sweet and savory appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block halloumi cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill halloumi for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
  3. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle with thyme.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Spicy Pineapple and Prosciutto Skewers

Pineapple and prosciutto is a classic combination that’s perfect for a summer picnic. Add a spicy kick with some diced jalapeño peppers and you’ve got a sweet and savory appetizer that’s sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, cut into chunks
  • 6 slices prosciutto
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread pineapple chunks and prosciutto slices onto skewers.
  3. Brush with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until prosciutto is crispy.
  5. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and diced jalapeño peppers.

Mini Crab Cakes with Remoulade Sauce

Crab cakes are a delicious and indulgent appetizer that’s perfect for a summer picnic. Serve them with a tangy and creamy remoulade sauce for a tasty and refreshing treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jumbo lump crab meat
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup remoulade sauce (see below)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, gently combine crab meat, panko breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and parsley.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  3. Form into small patties and pan-fry until golden brown.
  4. Serve with remoulade sauce.

Remoulade Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, ketchup, parsley, dill, and lemon juice.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

These 10 appetizers are sure to impress your guests and satisfy their cravings. Whether you’re hosting a summer picnic or just want to enjoy some delicious food with friends, these recipes are perfect for any occasion. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and get ready for a summer of delicious food and good company!
