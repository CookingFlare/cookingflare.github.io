Warm and Spiced Apple Cider Pie: A Fall Delight


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, there’s nothing quite like the comforting aroma of warm spices and the sweet taste of apple cider to usher in the fall season. This Warm and Spiced Apple Cider Pie recipe is a delightful twist on the classic apple pie, infusing it with the rich flavors of spiced apple cider. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a cozy dessert at home, this pie is sure to become a new autumn favorite.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 cups apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup spiced apple cider
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually drizzle in the ice water, a little at a time, and mix the dough with a fork until it starts to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  3. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
  3. Sprinkle the sugar and spice mixture over the apples and toss to coat evenly. Then, pour in the spiced apple cider and gently stir until the apples are coated.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out one disc of the chilled dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully place the rolled-out dough into the pie dish.
  3. Pour the apple filling into the crust, then dot the top with small pieces of butter.
  4. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Cover the edges of the pie with foil to prevent excessive browning, then place the pie in the preheated oven.
  2. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 2 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion

This Warm and Spiced Apple Cider Pie is best enjoyed warm, perhaps with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The combination of tender, spiced apples nestled in a flaky, buttery crust is a true taste of fall in every bite.

Conclusion

With its aromatic spices and comforting flavors, this Warm and Spiced Apple Cider Pie is a delightful autumn dessert that captures the essence of the season. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply savoring a quiet moment at home, this pie is a wonderful way to embrace the beauty of fall. Give this recipe a try and let the warmth of spiced apple cider fill your kitchen with the spirit of the season.
