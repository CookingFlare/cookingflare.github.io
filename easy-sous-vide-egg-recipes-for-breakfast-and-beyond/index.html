Easy Sous Vide Egg Recipes for Breakfast and Beyond


When it comes to cooking eggs, sous vide is a game-changer. This precise cooking method ensures that your eggs turn out perfectly every time, with a creamy texture that’s hard to achieve through traditional cooking methods. Whether you’re a breakfast enthusiast or simply love incorporating eggs into your meals, sous vide egg recipes offer versatility and deliciousness that can elevate your culinary experience. In this blog post, we’ll explore easy sous vide egg recipes for breakfast and beyond, allowing you to unleash your creativity in the kitchen.

The Magic of Sous Vide Eggs

Sous vide cooking involves placing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This method is especially effective for cooking eggs, as it allows you to achieve consistent results with a custard-like texture that’s unmatched. Whether you prefer your eggs soft-boiled, hard-boiled, or somewhere in between, sous vide ensures that they come out exactly the way you like them, every time.

Sous Vide Soft-Boiled Eggs

Soft-boiled eggs are a delicacy that can be enjoyed on their own, with toast, or as a topping for salads and grain bowls. To make sous vide soft-boiled eggs, set your sous vide machine to 167°F (75°C). Gently place the eggs in the water bath and cook for 13 minutes for a runny yolk or 15 minutes for a slightly firmer yolk. Once cooked, carefully remove the eggs from the water bath and place them in an ice bath for a few minutes to stop the cooking process. Peel and enjoy these perfectly cooked soft-boiled eggs.

Sous Vide Hard-Boiled Eggs

For those who prefer hard-boiled eggs, sous vide offers a foolproof method for achieving consistent results. Set your sous vide machine to 167°F (75°C) and cook the eggs for 1 hour. After the hour is up, transfer the eggs to an ice bath to cool before peeling. The result? Creamy, evenly cooked hard-boiled eggs that are perfect for snacking, slicing onto salads, or making deviled eggs.

Sous Vide Egg Bites

Egg bites have gained popularity as a convenient, protein-packed breakfast option. By using sous vide, you can create velvety smooth egg bites that are infused with your favorite flavors. To make sous vide egg bites, whisk together eggs, milk, cheese, and your choice of fillings such as spinach, bacon, or sun-dried tomatoes. Pour the mixture into greased mason jars or silicone molds, and cook in the sous vide water bath at 172°F (77.8°C) for 1 hour. Once cooked, let the egg bites cool slightly before unmolding and serving. These versatile egg bites are perfect for meal prep and can be enjoyed on the go.

Sous Vide Poached Eggs

Poached eggs are notorious for being tricky to make, but sous vide simplifies the process and delivers consistently perfect results. To make sous vide poached eggs, set your sous vide machine to 167°F (75°C). Gently crack the eggs into individual small, heatproof containers or mason jars and lower them into the water bath. Cook for 13 minutes for a runny yolk or 15 minutes for a firmer yolk. Once cooked, carefully remove the eggs from the water bath and serve them over toast, salads, or alongside your favorite dishes.

Conclusion

Sous vide egg recipes offer a new level of precision and consistency to your egg-cooking endeavors. Whether you’re a fan of soft-boiled, hard-boiled, egg bites, or poached eggs, the sous vide method ensures that your eggs turn out perfectly every time. By incorporating these easy sous vide egg recipes into your cooking repertoire, you can elevate your breakfasts, brunches, and beyond with minimal effort and maximum flavor. Get ready to impress your taste buds and those of your loved ones with these delightful sous vide egg creations.
