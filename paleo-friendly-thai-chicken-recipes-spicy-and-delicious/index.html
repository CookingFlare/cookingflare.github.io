Paleo-Friendly Thai Chicken Recipes: Spicy and Delicious


If you’re looking for flavorful and healthy recipes that will satisfy your cravings for Thai cuisine while adhering to a paleo diet, you’ve come to the right place. Thai food is known for its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and vibrant colors, and with a few simple swaps and substitutions, you can enjoy these delicious dishes without compromising on your dietary goals.

Thai Red Curry Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Cauliflower rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  2. Add the bell peppers and zucchini, and cook for another 2 minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and add the chicken to the other side. Cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the Thai red curry paste and cook for another minute to coat the chicken and vegetables.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and fish sauce, and stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Remove from heat and stir in the lime juice. Serve the curry over cauliflower rice and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Thai Basil Chicken Stir-fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 bunch of Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cauliflower rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the garlic, chili pepper, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  3. Return the chicken to the skillet and add the Thai basil leaves. Stir-fry for another minute to wilt the basil.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, fish sauce, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, and toss to coat.
  5. Remove from heat and serve the stir-fry over cauliflower rice.

Thai Coconut Chicken Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 red chili pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, ginger, and chili pepper, and sauté until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken to the pot and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and coconut milk. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, lime juice, and coconut aminos. Taste and adjust the seasonings as needed.
  5. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

Enjoy these paleo-friendly Thai chicken recipes that are packed with flavor and perfect for satisfying your cravings. Whether you’re new to paleo or a seasoned pro, these dishes are sure to become favorites in your recipe rotation. So go ahead, spice up your mealtime with these delicious Thai-inspired creations!
