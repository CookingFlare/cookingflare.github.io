Fall-Inspired Grilled Recipes: 5 Dishes to Try


As the crisp autumn air sweeps in, it’s the perfect time to move your grilling outdoors and experiment with fall-inspired dishes that capture the flavors of the season. Whether you’re celebrating the harvest, enjoying a cozy evening with family, or hosting friends for a weekend gathering, grilling adds an extra layer of flavor to your meals. Fall grilling isn’t just about classic burgers and steaks; it’s about infusing hearty seasonal ingredients, smoky flavors, and warming spices into your dishes.

In this blog post, we’ll explore five unique fall-inspired grilled recipes that bring out the best of the season’s produce and flavors. From savory grilled vegetables to indulgent grilled desserts, these recipes are sure to become autumn staples. So, fire up your grill and let’s dive into these delicious fall recipes!

1. Grilled Butternut Squash with Maple Glaze and Sage

Butternut squash is one of the quintessential fall vegetables, and when it’s grilled, its natural sweetness comes to life. The combination of smoky char and the rich, earthy flavor of the squash pairs wonderfully with a maple glaze and fresh sage leaves.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized butternut squash
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 10-12 fresh sage leaves
  • Optional: Crumbled goat cheese or feta for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice the butternut squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Drizzle the squash halves with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  4. Grill the squash cut-side down for 10-12 minutes until you get grill marks, then flip it over.
  5. Brush the cut side of the squash with maple syrup, and continue grilling for an additional 8-10 minutes until the squash is tender.
  6. While grilling, add the sage leaves to the grill in a small grill-safe pan or directly onto the grate for a couple of minutes until crispy.
  7. Once the squash is cooked, serve it with crispy sage leaves and a drizzle of additional maple syrup. Optionally, sprinkle crumbled goat cheese or feta for extra flavor.

This dish captures the essence of fall—warm, sweet, and savory—all while showcasing the rich flavors of roasted squash and fresh herbs.

2. Grilled Apple and Sausage Skewers

Nothing says fall like the perfect combination of apples and sausages. The slight sweetness of apples perfectly complements the savory flavors of grilled sausages. When skewered and grilled, this dish brings together the best of both worlds—sweet and savory—with a smoky finish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 sausages (your choice of pork, chicken, or turkey)
  • 2 medium apples (such as Honeycrisp or Gala)
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the sausages into bite-sized chunks and set aside.
  3. Core and slice the apples into wedges, and cut the red onion into chunks.
  4. Assemble the skewers by alternating between pieces of sausage, apple wedges, and red onion chunks.
  5. Drizzle olive oil over the skewers and sprinkle with fresh thyme, salt, and pepper.
  6. Grill the skewers for about 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the sausages are cooked through, and the apples are slightly caramelized.
  7. Serve the skewers hot, drizzled with a bit of extra olive oil or a sweet balsamic glaze for added flavor.

These grilled apple and sausage skewers are the ultimate comfort food with a balance of textures and flavors—perfect for a fall cookout or a cozy dinner.

3. Grilled Harvest Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze

When it comes to fall grilling, it’s not just about the meat. The fall harvest is full of delicious vegetables that take on a whole new depth of flavor when grilled. A mix of root vegetables, squash, and hearty greens makes for a colorful and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, sliced into thick rounds
  • 2 small parsnips, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss the carrots, parsnips, zucchini, and bell peppers with olive oil, garlic powder, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the vegetables on the grill, turning occasionally, and cook for 10-15 minutes, until they are tender and slightly charred.
  4. While the vegetables are grilling, heat the balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Simmer for about 5 minutes until it reduces into a syrupy consistency.
  5. Once the vegetables are done, drizzle the balsamic glaze over them and serve immediately.

This vibrant dish is bursting with fall flavors and textures. The smoky grilled vegetables paired with the sweet tang of balsamic glaze create a perfect balance for any autumn meal.

4. Grilled Pear and Blue Cheese Salad

Pears are another fruit that comes into their own during fall, and when grilled, they develop a caramelized exterior with a juicy, tender interior. Combine that with tangy blue cheese and peppery arugula, and you’ve got a light yet satisfying dish that’s perfect for a fall evening.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe pears (such as Bartlett or Bosc)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Cut the pears in half and remove the cores. Brush the cut sides with olive oil and season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the pears cut-side down for 5-6 minutes, until grill marks appear and they soften.
  4. While grilling, prepare your salad by tossing the arugula with crumbled blue cheese and toasted walnuts.
  5. Once the pears are grilled, slice them into wedges and add them to the salad.
  6. Drizzle the salad with honey and apple cider vinegar for a sweet and tangy dressing.
  7. Serve immediately, either as a side dish or a light main course.

This grilled pear and blue cheese salad offers the perfect mix of sweetness, tang, and crunch, making it a must-try fall recipe.

5. Grilled Cinnamon Sugar Donuts

Let’s not forget about dessert! Fall is synonymous with warm spices, and a grilled donut glazed with cinnamon sugar is a perfect way to end your autumn meal. The grill adds a hint of smokiness that makes this dessert unique and irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 plain cake donuts
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Optional: Whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to low heat.
  2. Brush the donuts with melted butter on both sides.
  3. Grill the donuts for 1-2 minutes per side, just until they get a slight char and are warmed through.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the sugar and cinnamon together.
  5. Once the donuts are off the grill, immediately toss them in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
  6. Serve the donuts warm with whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream if desired.

These grilled cinnamon sugar donuts are a sweet and smoky treat that perfectly captures the essence of fall. They’re simple to make but pack a delightful punch of flavor.

Final Thoughts

Fall grilling is all about celebrating the season’s rich flavors and creating dishes that bring warmth and comfort to the table. Whether you’re grilling hearty vegetables, succulent sausages, or even sweet desserts, these five fall-inspired recipes will help you make the most of autumn’s bounty. So, grab your tongs, fire up the grill, and enjoy these delicious dishes with family and friends.

Happy grilling and enjoy the fall season to its fullest!
