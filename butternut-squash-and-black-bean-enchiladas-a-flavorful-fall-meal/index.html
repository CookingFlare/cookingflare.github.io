Butternut Squash and Black Bean Enchiladas: A Flavorful Fall Meal


Enchiladas are a beloved Mexican dish that can be filled with a variety of ingredients. One of my favorite variations is Butternut Squash and Black Bean Enchiladas, which combines the sweet and nutty flavors of butternut squash with the hearty and protein-packed black beans. This recipe is not only delicious but also perfect for the fall season when butternut squash is at its peak.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 8 tortillas
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and cook until they become soft and translucent.

  3. Add the cubed butternut squash to the skillet and cook until it becomes tender, about 10-15 minutes. Stir in the cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the spices to infuse the squash.

  4. In a separate bowl, mash half of the black beans with a fork or a potato masher. This will help bind the filling together.

  5. Add the mashed black beans and the remaining whole black beans to the skillet with the butternut squash mixture. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  6. Warm the tortillas in the microwave for a few seconds or on a hot skillet for a few seconds on each side to make them pliable.

  7. To assemble the enchiladas, place a spoonful of the butternut squash and black bean filling in the center of each tortilla. Roll the tortilla tightly and place it seam-side down in a greased baking dish.

  8. Repeat the process until all the tortillas and filling are used.

  9. Pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas, making sure to cover them completely.

  10. Sprinkle the shredded Monterey Jack cheese on top of the enchiladas.

  11. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  12. Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These Butternut Squash and Black Bean Enchiladas are delicious on their own, but you can take them to the next level with some tasty toppings. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Freshly chopped cilantro
  • Sliced avocado
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Squeeze of lime juice
  • Diced tomatoes

Serve the enchiladas with a side of rice and beans for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Butternut Squash and Black Bean Enchiladas are a delightful combination of flavors and textures that will satisfy your taste buds and warm your soul during the fall season. The sweetness of the butternut squash pairs perfectly with the earthy black beans, creating a filling and nutritious meal. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking for a meatless option, these enchiladas are sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. Enjoy!
