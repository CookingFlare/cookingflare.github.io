Easy Pho Soup: Vietnamese Street Food at Home


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes hit the mark quite like pho soup. This flavorful Vietnamese street food combines fragrant broth, tender noodles, and fresh herbs to create a bowl that’s both satisfying and nourishing. Whether it’s your first time making pho or you’re looking for a way to simplify the process, this recipe will guide you through creating an easy yet authentic version of the beloved dish in the comfort of your home.

What Is Pho?

Pho (pronounced “fuh”) is a traditional Vietnamese soup that has become an enduring culinary icon worldwide. At its core, pho consists of rice noodles, savory broth, and toppings such as thinly-sliced meat, bean sprouts, herbs, lime, and sauces for added depth of flavor.

Originating in the early 20th century, pho captures Vietnam’s rich culinary history with its unique blend of aromatics and comforting textures. Though often associated with long hours of preparation, this easy recipe will help you recreate the dish in record time, making it accessible to busy home cooks.

Ingredients for Easy Pho Soup

The beauty of pho lies in its adaptability and the option to use readily available ingredients without compromising flavor. Here’s what you’ll need for this simplified version of the Vietnamese classic:

For the Broth:

  • 6 cups beef or chicken stock (or vegetable stock for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 small onion, peeled and halved
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional for vegetarians)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For the Noodles and Toppings:

  • 200 grams rice noodles
  • Thinly sliced beef, chicken, shrimp, or tofu
  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Fresh herbs like cilantro, Thai basil, and mint
  • Sliced green onions
  • Thinly sliced red chili or jalapeño (optional)
  • Lime wedges
  • Hoisin sauce and sriracha for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Pho Broth

The heart and soul of any pho soup is the broth. While traditional broths simmer for hours, this recipe cuts down the time without sacrificing flavor.

  1. Toast the Aromatics
    Begin by heating a dry pan over medium heat. Add the sliced ginger, halved onion, star anise pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Toast the aromatics for 2-3 minutes, flipping occasionally, until fragrant and slightly charred.

  2. Simmer the Broth
    In a large pot, combine the toasted aromatics with your choice of stock (beef, chicken, or vegetable). Add garlic, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring the broth to a gentle boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes. Skim any impurities off the surface to keep the broth clear.

  3. Strain and Adjust
    After simmering, strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve into another pot, discarding the solids. Taste the broth and adjust seasoning as needed with more fish sauce, soy sauce, or sugar. Keep it warm over low heat.

Preparing the Rice Noodles

Rice noodles cook quickly, so timing is crucial.

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for 20 minutes or according to the package instructions.
  2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, then cook the noodles for 1-2 minutes until tender but firm.
  3. Drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

Cooking the Proteins

Pho is a versatile dish that can accommodate various proteins, from beef to tofu.

For Thinly Sliced Beef or Chicken

Place the raw slices of beef or chicken into individual serving bowls. The hot broth will cook the slices when poured over them.

For Shrimp

Cook shrimp in boiling water and set aside.

For Tofu

Cut firm tofu into cubes, then pan-fry or simply add directly into the bowls for a soft texture.

Assembling Your Pho Soup

Here’s where the magic happens—assembling the perfect bowl of pho.

  1. Divide the cooked rice noodles into bowls.
  2. Add your protein of choice (raw beef or chicken slices, cooked shrimp, or tofu).
  3. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and protein, ensuring it’s steaming hot to cook any raw meat.
  4. Garnish the pho with bean sprouts, fresh herbs, sliced green onions, chili slices, and lime wedges.
  5. Serve immediately alongside hoisin sauce and sriracha for customization.

Pro Tips to Enhance Flavor

  • Char Your Aromatics: Toasting ginger, onion, and spices helps achieve a deeper, smokier flavor in the broth.
  • Use High-Quality Stock: A rich, flavorful stock forms the foundation of good pho. If possible, opt for homemade stock.
  • Balance the Sweetness: A hint of sugar balances the savory and aromatic components of pho. Adjust it to suit your taste.
  • Fresh Herbs Are Essential: Thai basil, cilantro, and mint bring brightness and authenticity to the dish.

Why Make Pho at Home?

While grabbing a steaming bowl of pho from a bustling street vendor is a memorable experience, there’s something rewarding about recreating the dish at home. Making pho soup in your kitchen allows you to customize the ingredients, control the flavors, and embrace the process.

Plus, this easy pho recipe takes away the intimidation factor often associated with cooking Vietnamese cuisine. With just an hour of preparation, you can enjoy the taste of traditional pho without resorting to instant versions or dining out.

Perfect for Weeknight Dinners

Pho soup’s simplicity makes it an ideal dinner option for busy weeknights. The broth can be prepared in advance, and the assembly process is quick, allowing you to whip up a delicious meal without spending hours in the kitchen.

Feel free to experiment with the toppings—add cooked vegetables, a boiled egg, or even extra spice for variety. With a few tweaks, you can make this recipe uniquely yours while still preserving its authentic Vietnamese essence.

Conclusion

Pho soup is more than just a dish; it’s a sensory experience that combines aroma, flavor, and texture into one unforgettable bowl. By embracing this easy pho recipe, you can bring a taste of Vietnamese street food to your home with minimal effort.

Whether you’re in need of comfort, a light yet satisfying meal, or simply a way to dive into Vietnamese cuisine, this recipe will deliver. So gather your ingredients, ready your pot, and prepare to revel in the irresistible charm of homemade pho.
