Japanese Tempura Recipe with Shrimp and Vegetables


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve likely come across tempura – a delightful dish featuring crispy, light batter-coated seafood and vegetables. Tempura has become a popular choice in Japanese restaurants around the world, and its delicate, crispy texture and flavorful taste make it a favorite for many.

Today, we’re going to explore the art of making Japanese tempura with shrimp and vegetables right in your own kitchen. This recipe will guide you through the process of creating a light, crispy batter that perfectly complements the succulent shrimp and fresh vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Tempura Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 1/2 cups ice-cold water
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Ice cubes

For the Tempura:

  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined, leaving the tails intact
  • Assorted vegetables such as sweet potato, bell pepper, eggplant, and green beans, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by preparing the shrimp and vegetables. Ensure that the shrimp are deveined and the vegetables are cut into bite-sized pieces. Pat the ingredients dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture, which will help the batter adhere better.

  2. Make the Tempura Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and baking powder. Gradually add the ice-cold water while gently mixing the batter. Be careful not to overmix – it’s okay if there are a few lumps. Add the lightly beaten egg and a few ice cubes to keep the batter cold. Set the batter aside while you heat the oil.

  3. Heat the Oil: Pour vegetable oil into a deep, heavy-bottomed pot or a deep fryer. Heat the oil to 340-360°F (170-180°C). It’s important to maintain the right temperature for frying to achieve the perfect crispy texture.

  4. Coat the Shrimp and Vegetables: Lightly season the shrimp and vegetables with a pinch of salt. Dip each piece into the tempura batter, ensuring they are evenly coated.

  5. Fry the Tempura: Carefully place the coated shrimp and vegetables into the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot. Fry until the tempura is golden and crisp, which should take about 2-3 minutes for the shrimp and 1-2 minutes for the vegetables. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked tempura to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Arrange the freshly fried tempura on a serving platter and serve immediately. You can enjoy it with a side of tentsuyu, a dipping sauce made from dashi, soy sauce, and mirin, or simply with a sprinkle of salt.

Tips for Perfect Tempura

  • Keep the Batter Cold: Using ice-cold water and adding ice cubes to the batter helps create a light and crispy texture.
  • Don’t Overmix the Batter: A few lumps in the batter are okay and will contribute to the desired texture of the tempura.
  • Fry in Batches: Frying the shrimp and vegetables in batches ensures that the oil temperature remains consistent and allows the tempura to cook evenly.

Final Thoughts

Japanese tempura with shrimp and vegetables is a delightful dish that’s perfect for a special occasion or a fun cooking project at home. With its light, crispy texture and the natural sweetness of the seafood and vegetables, tempura is a treat for the senses. By following this recipe and tips, you can recreate the authentic flavors of Japanese tempura in your own kitchen and impress your family and friends with this classic dish. Enjoy your culinary adventure with this delightful Japanese tempura recipe!
