Dairy-Free Versions of Your Favorite Holiday Recipes


The holidays are a time for gathering, celebrating, and indulging in those classic dishes we’ve come to associate with the season. But for anyone navigating dairy sensitivities or seeking to embrace a plant-based lifestyle, holiday meals filled with butter, cream, and milk can present some challenges. Fortunately, there’s no need to miss out on the festive flavors you love. With a little creativity and the right substitutions, you can enjoy delicious dairy-free versions of your favorite holiday recipes that everyone—including friends and family who aren’t dairy-free—will love.

Here’s a guide to transforming some of the most beloved holiday dishes into fully dairy-free masterpieces without losing any of the rich flavors you cherish.

Dairy-Free Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are often considered one of the stars of any holiday spread, but traditional recipes lean heavily on butter and cream for their velvety texture. Going dairy-free doesn’t mean compromising on creaminess, though!

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of Yukon Gold or russet potatoes
  • 1/4 cup plant-based butter (such as avocado oil butter or vegan butter)
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk or oat milk
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel and cube the potatoes, then boil them in salted water until fork-tender.
  2. Drain the potatoes and mash them using a potato masher or an electric mixer.
  3. Add the plant-based butter and almond milk gradually while mashing, adjusting the amount of milk to achieve your desired texture.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder if desired.

The result is as light and creamy as the classic version, but completely dairy-free.

Dairy-Free Pumpkin Pie

Pumpkin pies are a holiday staple, so making them accessible for dairy-free guests is essential. The rich and warm spices paired with a creamy texture can be easily achieved with non-dairy alternatives.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil or plant-based butter
  • 3–5 tbsp cold water

For the filling:

  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream (scooped from full-fat canned coconut milk)
  • 2/3 cup brown sugar or coconut sugar
  • 2 eggs (or flax eggs for a vegan version)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare the crust by mixing flour and coconut oil until crumbly. Gradually add cold water until the dough forms a ball.
  2. Roll out the dough and gently place it in a pie pan. Trim the edges and press into the pan.
  3. For the filling, whisk together the pumpkin puree, coconut cream, sugar, eggs (or flax eggs), vanilla, and pumpkin pie spice.
  4. Pour the filling into the crust and bake for 50–60 minutes, or until the center is set.
  5. Let the pie cool for at least an hour before serving.

No one will guess this pie is dairy-free—it’s just as rich and indulgent as any traditional recipe.

Dairy-Free Green Bean Casserole

Green bean casserole often relies heavily on cream-based soups and cheese for its comforting flavor profile, but you can recreate this dish without dairy while maintaining its signature appeal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound green beans (trimmed and steamed)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or vegan butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk or cashew milk
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free vegetable broth
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional, for a cheesy flavor)
  • Dairy-free crispy fried onions for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Steam the green beans until tender.
  2. In a saucepan, sauté the diced onions in olive oil until softened. Sprinkle in the flour and stir to combine.
  3. Gradually whisk in almond milk and vegetable broth, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  4. Add garlic powder and nutritional yeast, stirring well.
  5. Toss the green beans in the sauce and transfer to a casserole dish. Top with dairy-free fried onions and bake for 15 minutes.

The dish is every bit as savory and satisfying as its traditional counterpart, with none of the dairy.

Dairy-Free Hot Chocolate

Warm up this holiday season with a dairy-free cup of creamy hot chocolate that rivals the classic version. Whether you’re hosting guests or indulging in some quiet time by the fire, this recipe is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk, oat milk, or cashew milk
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup or coconut sugar
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • Dairy-free mini marshmallows (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the almond milk over medium heat until warm.
  2. Whisk in the cocoa powder and sweetener until fully dissolved.
  3. Stir in the vanilla extract and pour into mugs.
  4. Top with dairy-free marshmallows for an extra festive touch.

This luscious hot chocolate will keep you cozy while complementing the holiday fun.

Dairy-Free Holiday Gravy

Gravy often relies on butter or cream for richness, but a dairy-free version can be just as flavorful. Pair this recipe with mashed potatoes, roasted turkey, or plant-based options.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil or plant-based butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable broth or turkey drippings
  • 1 tsp soy sauce (optional, for added umami flavor)
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat, then whisk in the flour to form a roux.
  2. Gradually pour in the vegetable broth, whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
  3. Add the soy sauce, onion powder, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Let the gravy simmer until thickened, then serve warm.

You’ll love how this dairy-free gravy ties together all the flavors on your holiday plate.

Dairy-Free Stuffing

Stuffing is an essential part of holiday feasts, and it’s surprisingly easy to make it dairy-free without compromising flavor or texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of dairy-free bread, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil or vegan butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2–3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread the bread cubes on a baking sheet and toast lightly.
  2. In a pan, sauté the onion and celery in olive oil until softened.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the bread cubes, sautéed vegetables, and seasonings.
  4. Gradually add vegetable broth, mixing until the bread is moist but not soggy.
  5. Transfer to a baking dish and bake for 25–30 minutes, or until golden brown on top.

This dairy-free stuffing is aromatic and comforting, just like the traditional version.

Adapting holiday recipes to be dairy-free doesn’t mean sacrificing the flavors and textures you love. In fact, these dishes prove that with the right substitutions, you can create holiday meals that are inclusive, satisfying, and delicious for everyone at the table. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or bringing a dish to share, these recipes will ensure a memorable celebration filled with dairy-free delights.
