Indian Spice Delight: 15 Traditional Curry Recipes


Indian cuisine is a symphony of flavors, with each dish offering a unique blend of spices, herbs, and ingredients. Curries, the heart and soul of Indian food, are a testament to this diversity. From the rich, creamy gravies of North India to the fiery, aromatic curries of the South, every region brings something special to the table. Whether you’re a seasoned curry enthusiast or a curious beginner, exploring the traditional curry recipes from India is a journey that will tantalize your taste buds.

In this post, we will take you through 15 traditional Indian curry recipes, each representing a different part of the country and showcasing the remarkable versatility of Indian spices. Whether you’re craving something mild and comforting or bold and spicy, there’s a curry for everyone.

1. Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is one of the most beloved Indian curries. Originating from Punjab, this dish features tender chicken pieces cooked in a rich and creamy tomato-based gravy, infused with an array of spices. It is a perfect balance of flavors—slightly sweet, mildly tangy, and utterly comforting. The magic lies in the butter and cream that give the curry its velvety texture.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Let it sit for at least an hour.
  2. In a pan, heat butter and sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the pureed tomatoes, garam masala, cumin powder, and chili powder. Cook the mixture until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is tender.
  6. Stir in heavy cream and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with naan or rice.

2. Rogan Josh

Rogan Josh is a signature dish of Kashmiri cuisine. This fragrant lamb curry is characterized by its red color, thanks to the use of Kashmiri red chili powder. The slow-cooked lamb absorbs all the spices, creating a rich, tender, and aromatic curry that’s perfect with steamed rice or flatbreads.

Ingredients:

  • 500g lamb (cut into pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 2 onions, finely sliced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon powder
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and sauté the sliced onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add lamb pieces and sear them on all sides.
  4. Add yogurt, Kashmiri chili powder, garam masala, cinnamon powder, cloves, and bay leaves. Mix well.
  5. Add water to cover the lamb and simmer on low heat for 1-1.5 hours until the lamb is tender.
  6. Adjust salt to taste and serve with rice or naan.

3. Chole (Chickpea Curry)

Chole, a popular dish from Punjab, is made with chickpeas cooked in a spicy, tangy gravy. It’s hearty, filling, and perfect for a vegetarian meal. This curry is a favorite in many Indian households, often enjoyed with bhature (fried bread) or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pressure cooker, cook the soaked chickpeas until tender.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds, followed by onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala until the oil separates.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas and some water to adjust the consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with rice or bread.

4. Tikka Masala

Tikka Masala is a popular dish where marinated chicken is cooked in a rich, spiced tomato-based gravy. It’s a deliciously fragrant curry that has captured hearts worldwide. The smoky flavor of grilled chicken pairs beautifully with the creamy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless, cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, lemon juice, cumin, garam masala, turmeric, and chili powder for at least an hour.
  2. Grill or cook the marinated chicken in a pan until it’s cooked through and slightly charred.
  3. In a separate pan, heat butter and sauté onions until golden brown.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes, garam masala, and chili powder. Cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add the grilled chicken pieces and cream. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with naan or rice.

5. Saag Aloo

Saag Aloo is a vegetarian curry made with spinach (saag) and potatoes (aloo). This comforting dish is rich in flavor and packed with nutrients. It’s a staple in many Indian homes and pairs beautifully with chapati or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and cubed
  • 2 cups spinach leaves (chopped)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds until fragrant.
  2. Add onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric, coriander powder, and chili powder. Stir well.
  4. Add chopped spinach and cook until wilted.
  5. Add the boiled potatoes and mix gently.
  6. Simmer for 5 minutes and adjust salt to taste. Serve with chapati or rice.

6. Malai Kofta

Malai Kofta is a rich and indulgent curry made with deep-fried balls of mashed vegetables or paneer (Indian cheese) in a creamy, spiced gravy. This dish is a favorite at special occasions and is often served with naan or pulao.

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer, grated
  • 2 potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mix grated paneer, mashed potatoes, garam masala, and salt. Shape the mixture into small balls and fry them until golden brown.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onions until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes, garam masala, and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add cream and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the fried koftas and let them soak in the gravy for a few minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with naan or rice.

7. Prawn Curry

Prawn curry is a delightful seafood dish from the coastal regions of India. The combination of fresh prawns and coconut milk in a spiced curry base creates a light, yet flavorful dish. It’s especially popular in Kerala and coastal Karnataka.

Ingredients:

  • 500g prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the masala until the oil separates.
  4. Add prawns and cook for 5-7 minutes until they turn pink.
  5. Add coconut milk and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with steamed rice.

8. Vindaloo

Vindaloo is a fiery, tangy curry originally from Goa. It’s known for its bold flavors, thanks to the use of vinegar, ginger, garlic, and spices. Traditionally made with pork, it’s also popular with chicken or beef.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork (cut into pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the pork with vinegar, chili powder, turmeric, and salt for at least an hour.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté mustard seeds until they pop.
  3. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  5. Add marinated pork and cook until browned.
  6. Add water to create a gravy and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the pork is tender.
  7. Serve with rice or bread.

9. Methi Malai Murg (Fenugreek Cream Chicken)

Methi Malai Murg is a rich and creamy chicken curry infused with the bitterness of fenugreek (methi). This dish is a wonderful balance of flavors, and the creaminess of the sauce makes it a luxurious treat.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless)
  • 2 tablespoons fenugreek leaves (methi), dried
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onions in oil until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add chicken pieces and cook until browned.
  4. Add garam masala and dried fenugreek leaves. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add cream and simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve with rice or naan.

10. Baingan Bharta

Baingan Bharta is a smoky-flavored eggplant curry from Punjab. The eggplant is roasted over an open flame, giving it a unique charred flavor that’s combined with a mix of spices to create a comforting curry.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the eggplants over an open flame or in the oven until the skin is charred.
  2. Peel off the skin and mash the roasted eggplant.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds, followed by onions until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates.
  6. Add the mashed eggplant and mix well.
  7. Simmer for 5 minutes and serve with chapati or rice.

11. Gobi Masala (Cauliflower Curry)

Gobi Masala is a flavorful vegetarian curry made with cauliflower, a vegetable that absorbs spices beautifully. The curry is hearty, aromatic, and perfect for a simple weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds until fragrant.
  2. Add onions and cook until golden.
  3. Add turmeric, coriander powder, and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add cauliflower florets and mix well.
  6. Add water to create a gravy and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Adjust salt to taste and serve with chapati or rice.

12. Dhansak

Dhansak is a Parsi-style curry made with lentils, vegetables, and meat. This dish is a perfect combination of spicy, sweet, and sour flavors. Traditionally made with lamb or chicken, it pairs well with brown rice.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken or lamb (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup lentils
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook lentils until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add tomatoes, cumin, coriander powder, turmeric, and salt. Cook the masala until the oil separates.
  5. Add chicken or lamb pieces and cook until browned.
  6. Add cooked lentils and water to create a thick gravy. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  7. Serve with brown rice.

13. Daal Tadka

Daal Tadka is a classic North Indian dish made with yellow lentils cooked in spices and tempered with ghee. It’s a comforting, protein-packed dish that’s perfect with rice or roti.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup yellow lentils (toor dal)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook lentils until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds, followed by onions until golden.
  3. Add tomatoes, turmeric, chili powder, and garam masala. Cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked lentils and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with rice or roti.

14. Aloo Gobi

Aloo Gobi is a simple, flavorful curry made with potatoes and cauliflower. It’s a popular vegetarian dish and is easy to make, yet packs a punch with its bold spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds until fragrant.
  2. Add onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Add turmeric, coriander powder, and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add potatoes and cauliflower, mix well.
  6. Add water and simmer for 15-20 minutes. Adjust salt and serve with rice or roti.

15. Chicken Korma

Chicken Korma is a rich and aromatic curry made with yogurt, cream, and ground nuts. It’s a Mughlai dish that is mild yet flavorful, with a luscious, silky gravy.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless, cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • 2 tablespoons ground almonds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, garam masala, and salt for an hour.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned.
  5. Add ground almonds and cream. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve with naan or rice.

These 15 traditional Indian curry recipes cover a wide range of flavors and cooking techniques, representing the diverse culinary traditions of India. From hearty vegetarian curries like Chole and Saag Aloo to rich and indulgent dishes like Malai Kofta and Butter Chicken, there is a curry for every palate. Each recipe reflects the essence of Indian cuisine: the perfect balance of spices, textures, and flavors. Whether you’re a novice or an experienced cook, these curry recipes will help you bring the taste of India to your kitchen.
