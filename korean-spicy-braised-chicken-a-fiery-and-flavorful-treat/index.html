Korean Spicy Braised Chicken: A Fiery and Flavorful Treat


If you’re a fan of spicy food and bold flavors, then Korean Spicy Braised Chicken is the dish for you. This fiery and flavorful treat is a popular dish in Korean cuisine, known for its mouthwatering combination of tender chicken, spicy sauce, and aromatic spices. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of chicken (preferably bone-in, skin-on thighs or drumsticks)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup of gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the minced garlic, grated ginger, gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Mix well to form a thick sauce.

  2. Heat a large, deep skillet or a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Place the chicken, skin side down, into the hot pan, and cook until the skin is golden brown and crispy, about 5 minutes. Flip the chicken and cook for an additional 3 minutes to sear the other side.

  3. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside. In the same pan, add the sliced onion and cook until it becomes translucent and slightly caramelized, about 3 minutes.

  4. Return the chicken to the pan, skin side up, and pour the prepared sauce over the chicken. Add the chicken broth, ensuring that the chicken is partially submerged in the sauce.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally to ensure the chicken is evenly coated with the spicy sauce.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pan and set it aside. Increase the heat to medium-high and simmer the sauce for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until it thickens and reduces slightly.

  7. To serve, place the braised chicken on a platter and garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds. Serve it hot with steamed rice or Korean-style noodles for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a teaspoon of Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru) to the sauce.
  • If you prefer boneless chicken, you can use boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breast. Adjust the cooking time accordingly as boneless chicken tends to cook faster.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use a pressure cooker or an Instant Pot to braise the chicken. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for cooking times.
  • Feel free to add your favorite vegetables to the dish, such as carrots, potatoes, or bell peppers. Simply chop them into bite-sized pieces and add them to the pan along with the onions.

Final Thoughts

Korean Spicy Braised Chicken is a dish that combines the best of both worlds - fiery flavors and tender, succulent chicken. The bold combination of gochujang, soy sauce, and aromatic spices creates a rich and complex sauce that coats the chicken, infusing it with a burst of flavors. With its vibrant colors and enticing aroma, this dish is not only a treat for your taste buds but also a feast for your senses.

So why wait? Gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure with this Korean Spicy Braised Chicken recipe. It’s time to spice up your kitchen and indulge in a fiery and flavorful treat that will surely leave you craving for more.
