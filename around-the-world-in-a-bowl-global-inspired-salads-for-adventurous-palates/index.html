Around the World in a Bowl: Global-Inspired Salads for Adventurous Palates


Salads need not be boring or predictable. With a little creativity, you can take a twist on the traditional side dish, and give your taste buds a delightful journey of flavors. To help you embark on this culinary adventure, we present you with three globe-trotting bowl recipes that will tickle your taste buds with their unique combinations of fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and vivid textures.

1. Japanese-Insipired Mt. Fuji Chicken & Avocado Salad

Let’s first take a trip to the vibrant streets of Tokyo and savor the beautiful union of Japanese flair and juicy chicken in this Mt. Fuji Chicken & Avocado Salad recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 medium-sized ripe avocados, sliced
  • 8 oz. gochujang sauce
  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 medium-sized red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Tenderly season your chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then toss them in one tablespoon of gochujang sauce. Refrigerate for at least one hour.

  2. Grill or pan-sear your marinated chicken breasts until thoroughly cooked. Allow cooling, then slice into thin strips.

  3. In a large salad bowl, combine mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber slices, red onion slices, and sliced chicken.

  4. Top each salad with a generous layer of sliced avocado and a drizzle of gochujang sauce.

  5. Garnish with sesame seeds, and serve for a satisfying meal.

2. West African Sweet Potato & Black-Eyed Pea Salad

Next, we’ll travel to West Africa to take a taste of the diverse flavors and spices that define its cuisine. This Sweet Potato & Black-Eyed Pea Salad is a delightful fusion of sweet, savory, and nutty elements.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized sweet potatoes, roasted and diced
  • 1 can black-eyed peas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cucumber, seeded and diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup dried unsweetened cranberries
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large pot with water and bring to a rolling boil. Carefully add sweet potatoes and cook until tender (about 20-25 minutes). Drain and allow to cool before dicing.

  2. Combine your cooled sweet potatoes, black-eyed peas, cucumber, parsley, cilantro, and dried cranberries in a large salad bowl.

  3. For the dressing, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a small mixing bowl.

  4. Pour the dressing over your salad, and gently mix to evenly distribute the flavors.

  5. This salad can be served immediately or chilled for a few hours to enhance flavors. Enjoy with your favorite grilled proteins or on its own.

3. Moroccan Quinoa & Roasted Vegetable Salad

For our final stop, we’ll journey to the colorful and fragrant streets of Morocco, a place where spices, nuts, and dried fruits gently intertwine to create unforgettable dishes. Our Moroccan Quinoa & Roasted Vegetable Salad gives you a taste of the rich flavors and textures characteristic of Moroccan cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 yellow squash, sliced
  • 2 red bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa according to package instructions, and let cool.

  2. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread your sliced vegetables onto a large baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, and toss to evenly coat.

  3. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes or until vegetables are tender and lightly browned.

  4. In a large salad bowl, combine quinoa, roasted vegetables, chopped apricots, toasted almonds, fresh mint, and fresh parsley.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, ground cumin, salt, and pepper to create a zesty dressing.

  6. Drizzle dressing over your salad and gently toss to blend flavors.

With these three global-inspired salads, you not only satisfy your hunger, but also create a sensory experience for your palate. So, tie on those culinary travel shoes and take your taste buds on an unforgettable adventure – right in your very own kitchen!
