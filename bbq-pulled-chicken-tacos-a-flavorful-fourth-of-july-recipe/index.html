BBQ Pulled Chicken Tacos: A Flavorful Fourth of July Recipe


Summer is in full swing, and what better way to celebrate the Fourth of July than with a mouthwatering BBQ pulled chicken tacos recipe? This crowd-pleasing dish combines the smoky flavors of barbecue with the fresh and zesty elements of tacos, making it the perfect addition to your holiday menu. So fire up the grill and get ready to impress your friends and family with this finger-licking good recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional, for an extra kick)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 12 small flour tortillas
  • 1 cup of shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper (if desired). Place the chicken on the grill and cook for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.

  2. While the chicken is grilling, prepare the BBQ sauce. In a small saucepan, combine the BBQ sauce, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, and a pinch of salt. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.

  3. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, using two forks, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken with the BBQ sauce, tossing until the chicken is evenly coated.

  5. Warm the flour tortillas on the grill for about 30 seconds per side, or until they are slightly charred and pliable.

  6. To assemble the tacos, spoon a generous amount of the BBQ pulled chicken onto each tortilla. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, diced red onion, and chopped cilantro. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top for an extra burst of flavor.

  7. Serve the BBQ pulled chicken tacos immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have access to a grill, you can also cook the chicken in the oven. Simply preheat your oven to 375°F, place the seasoned chicken breasts on a baking sheet, and bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.

  • Feel free to customize your tacos with additional toppings such as avocado slices, sour cream, or shredded cheese.

  • Don’t forget to serve these BBQ pulled chicken tacos with your favorite side dishes like coleslaw, corn on the cob, or grilled vegetables for a complete Fourth of July feast.

  • Leftover BBQ pulled chicken can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. You can reheat the chicken in the microwave or on the stovetop for a quick and delicious meal.

So there you have it, a flavorful and festive recipe for BBQ pulled chicken tacos that will surely impress your guests this Fourth of July. The smoky and tangy flavors of the BBQ sauce combined with the fresh and crisp toppings make for a truly irresistible combination. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy this delicious dish as you celebrate the holiday in style. Happy cooking and happy Fourth of July!
