Easy and Healthy Stir-Fry Recipes for a Quick Weeknight Meal


Are you looking for a delicious and nutritious meal that you can whip up in no time? Look no further than stir-fry recipes! Stir-frying is a versatile cooking technique that allows you to combine an array of fresh vegetables, protein, and flavorful sauces to create a satisfying and healthy meal. Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or a seasoned cook, these easy and healthy stir-fry recipes are sure to become your go-to weeknight dinner options.

1. Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add chicken strips and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add bell pepper, carrot, broccoli, snap peas, and garlic. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, or until vegetables are crisp-tender.
  5. Return chicken to the skillet and pour the teriyaki sauce over the ingredients. Stir to coat everything evenly.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and everything is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  8. Serve over cooked rice and enjoy!

2. Shrimp and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup sliced onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Green onions, for garnish
  • Cooked noodles, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, honey, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add shrimp and cook until pink and cooked through. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add bell pepper, zucchini, mushrooms, onions, and garlic. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, or until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Return shrimp to the skillet and pour the sauce over the ingredients. Stir to coat everything evenly.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and everything is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and sprinkle with green onions.
  8. Serve over cooked noodles and enjoy!

3. Tofu and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Crushed red pepper flakes, for garnish
  • Cooked quinoa, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add tofu cubes and cook until browned on all sides. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and garlic. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, or until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Return tofu to the skillet and pour the sauce over the ingredients. Stir to coat everything evenly.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and everything is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and sprinkle with crushed red pepper flakes.
  8. Serve over cooked quinoa and enjoy!

Stir-fry recipes are not only quick and easy to make, but they also offer a great way to incorporate a variety of vegetables into your diet. Feel free to customize these recipes by adding your favorite vegetables or protein options. With these delicious and healthy stir-fry recipes, you’ll be able to whip up a flavorful meal in no time, making weeknight dinners a breeze. So, grab your skillet and get ready to stir-fry your way to a satisfying and nutritious meal!
