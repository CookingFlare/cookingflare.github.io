Healthy Vegan Dessert Recipes Under 200 Calories


Desserts don’t have to be indulgent, heavy, or high in calories to be satisfying. With the right ingredients and creativity, you can whip up delicious vegan desserts that are both healthy and under 200 calories. Whether you’re looking to satisfy a sweet tooth or impress guests at a gathering, these recipes are a guilt-free way to enjoy something sweet. Below, we explore a variety of delightful vegan desserts that will keep your taste buds happy without compromising your health goals.

1. Avocado Chocolate Mousse

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup (or agave nectar)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • Optional: fresh berries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the ripe avocado, cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt.
  2. Blend until the mixture is smooth and creamy. Scrape down the sides as needed to ensure everything is well combined.
  3. Taste the mousse and adjust sweetness if necessary by adding more maple syrup.
  4. Transfer the mousse into serving bowls and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to chill.
  5. Serve chilled, topped with fresh berries if desired.

Nutritional Information (per serving):

Approximately 150 calories.

This avocado chocolate mousse is rich, creamy, and decadent, making it a perfect choice for a healthy dessert.

2. Banana Oat Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate chips (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas until smooth.
  3. Add the rolled oats, cinnamon, and salt to the mashed bananas. Mix until well combined.
  4. If using, fold in the dark chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of the dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the cookies are lightly golden on the edges.
  7. Allow to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Nutritional Information (per cookie, makes about 10):

Approximately 90 calories.

These banana oat cookies are chewy, delicious, and a great way to use overripe bananas. They make for a quick, healthy snack or dessert.

3. Chia Seed Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes, then whisk again to prevent clumping.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight until the mixture thickens.
  4. Serve chilled, topped with your choice of fresh fruit.

Nutritional Information (per serving):

Approximately 150 calories.

Chia seed pudding is not only simple to make but also packed with nutrients. It’s customizable with your favorite toppings, making it versatile for any occasion.

4. Coconut Macaroons

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the shredded coconut, almond flour, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.
  3. Using your hands, form small mounds of the mixture and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the macaroons are golden brown.
  5. Let them cool completely before serving.

Nutritional Information (per macaroon, makes about 12):

Approximately 90 calories.

These coconut macaroons are chewy and sweet, making them an excellent treat for coconut lovers. They’re simple to prepare and disappear quickly!

5. Berry Sorbet

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups frozen mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the frozen berries, maple syrup, and lemon juice.
  2. Pulse until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness if desired.
  4. Transfer the sorbet to a container and freeze for about 30 minutes to firm up.
  5. Serve in bowls or cones.

Nutritional Information (per serving):

Approximately 120 calories.

This refreshing berry sorbet is a perfect way to cool down on a hot day. With just a few ingredients, it’s incredibly easy to make and packed with flavor.

6. Almond Butter Energy Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup almond butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond butter, maple syrup, chocolate chips, and vanilla extract.
  2. Mix well until all ingredients are fully incorporated.
  3. Using your hands, roll the mixture into small balls, about one inch in diameter.
  4. Place the energy bites on a parchment-lined baking sheet and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.

Nutritional Information (per bite, makes about 12):

Approximately 100 calories.

These almond butter energy bites are perfect for a quick snack or dessert. They’re nutritious and provide a boost of energy, making them ideal for pre- or post-workout fuel.

7. Apple Nachos

Ingredients:

  • 1 large apple, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons almond butter or peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon mini dark chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon chopped nuts (walnuts or almonds)
  • A sprinkle of cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the apple slices on a serving plate.
  2. Drizzle almond butter or peanut butter over the apple slices.
  3. Sprinkle mini dark chocolate chips and chopped nuts on top.
  4. Finish with a light sprinkle of cinnamon.
  5. Serve immediately.

Nutritional Information (per serving):

Approximately 180 calories.

Apple nachos are a fun and interactive dessert that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. They’re crunchy, creamy, and full of flavor!

8. Lemon Coconut Bliss Balls

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pitted dates
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the pitted dates, shredded coconut, almond flour, lemon zest, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Process until the mixture is well combined and sticky.
  3. Roll the mixture into small balls, about one inch in diameter.
  4. Roll the bliss balls in additional shredded coconut for a finishing touch if desired.
  5. Store in an airtight container in the fridge.

Nutritional Information (per ball, makes about 12):

Approximately 100 calories.

These lemon coconut bliss balls are zesty and energizing, perfect for a refreshing treat that’s healthy and satisfying.

Final Thoughts

Enjoying dessert doesn’t have to mean sacrificing your health goals or exceeding your calorie limits. These healthy vegan dessert recipes under 200 calories are not only delicious but also simple to make. From creamy chocolate mousse to refreshing sorbet, the options are endless. You can indulge your sweet tooth while keeping things light and nutritious. So go ahead, treat yourself to something sweet without the guilt!

With these recipes, you can create a variety of desserts that are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just enjoying a cozy night in. Happy cooking!
