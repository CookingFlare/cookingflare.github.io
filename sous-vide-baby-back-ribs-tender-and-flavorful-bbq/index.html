Sous Vide Baby Back Ribs: Tender and Flavorful BBQ


There’s nothing quite like sinking your teeth into a juicy, fall-off-the-bone BBQ ribs. The smoky aroma, the tender meat, and the burst of flavor with every bite - it’s a true delight for the senses. And if you’re looking to take your BBQ game to the next level, then sous vide baby back ribs are the way to go.

What is Sous Vide?

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves sealing the food in an airtight bag and cooking it in a temperature-controlled water bath. This method ensures precise temperature control, resulting in perfectly cooked, tender meat every time.

Why Sous Vide Baby Back Ribs?

Sous vide is the secret to achieving ribs that are incredibly tender and flavorful. By cooking the ribs at a low and consistent temperature for an extended period, you allow the collagen in the meat to break down slowly, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture. The ribs also absorb all the flavors you add to the bag, creating a truly mouthwatering experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 rack of baby back ribs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • BBQ rub of your choice
  • BBQ sauce of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your water bath to 165°F (74°C).

  2. Season the rack of baby back ribs generously with salt and pepper. This will help enhance the flavor of the meat.

  3. Rub the BBQ rub of your choice all over the ribs, making sure to coat them evenly. This will add a delicious layer of flavor to the ribs.

  4. Place the seasoned ribs in a vacuum-sealed bag, making sure they are in a single layer and not overlapping. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  5. Submerge the bag of ribs into the preheated water bath, ensuring that they are fully submerged. Cook the ribs for 24 hours. Yes, you read that right! The long cooking time is what makes the magic happen.

  6. Once the cooking time is up, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully take out the ribs. Be gentle, as they will be incredibly tender at this point.

  7. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, brush the ribs with your favorite BBQ sauce. This will create a sticky and flavorful glaze on the ribs.

  8. Place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until you achieve a nice char and caramelization.

  9. Remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.

  10. Slice the ribs between the bones and serve with additional BBQ sauce on the side. Enjoy!

Tips and Tricks:

  • Experiment with different BBQ rubs and sauces to find your favorite flavor combination.
  • If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a zip-top bag and the water displacement method. Simply place the seasoned ribs in the bag, seal it most of the way, and then slowly lower the bag into the water bath, allowing the water to push out the air. Seal the bag completely once the air is removed.
  • Don’t skip the step of finishing the ribs on the grill. This will give them a delicious smoky flavor and a nice crispy exterior.
  • Sous vide ribs can be cooked ahead of time and refrigerated until you’re ready to grill them. Just make sure to bring them to room temperature before grilling.

Now that you know the secret to incredibly tender and flavorful BBQ ribs, it’s time to fire up that grill and impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. Enjoy the juicy goodness and savor every bite of these sous vide baby back ribs!
