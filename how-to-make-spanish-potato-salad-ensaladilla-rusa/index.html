How to Make Spanish Potato Salad: Ensaladilla Rusa


Ensaladilla Rusa, or Spanish Potato Salad, is a beloved dish that has become a staple in Spanish cuisine. This creamy, flavorful salad is perfect for summer gatherings, picnics, or as a side dish for any meal. With its origins tracing back to Russia, this dish has been adapted over the years to suit local tastes and ingredients, making it a versatile and delightful addition to any table. In this post, we will explore the history, ingredients, and step-by-step instructions to create your own delicious Ensaladilla Rusa.

A Brief History of Ensaladilla Rusa

The name “Ensaladilla Rusa” translates to “Russian Salad,” a nod to its origins in the 19th century when it was created by a Belgian chef named Lucien Olivier at the Hermitage restaurant in Moscow. The dish quickly gained popularity and made its way to Spain, where it was adapted to include local ingredients. Today, Ensaladilla Rusa is a quintessential part of Spanish tapas culture, often served in bars and restaurants across the country.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make a traditional Ensaladilla Rusa, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Potatoes: 500 grams (about 1 pound), preferably waxy potatoes for better texture.
  • Carrots: 2 medium-sized, peeled and diced.
  • Frozen peas: 100 grams (about 1 cup), thawed.
  • Canned tuna: 1 can (about 200 grams), drained.
  • Hard-boiled eggs: 2, peeled and chopped.
  • Mayonnaise: 200 grams (about 1 cup), homemade or store-bought.
  • Olives: A handful of green or black olives, pitted and sliced.
  • Roasted red peppers: 1 jar, drained and chopped (optional).
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Extra virgin olive oil: A drizzle for added flavor (optional).

Optional Ingredients

While the above ingredients are the traditional staples, you can customize your Ensaladilla Rusa with additional ingredients such as:

  • Capers: For a tangy kick.
  • Shrimp: For a seafood twist.
  • Pickles: For added crunch.
  • Apples or walnuts: For a sweet and nutty flavor.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Boil the Potatoes: Start by scrubbing the potatoes to remove any dirt. Place them in a large pot, cover with cold water, and add a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil and cook until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. Be careful not to overcook them, as they should hold their shape.

  2. Cook the Carrots and Peas: In the same pot, add the diced carrots to the boiling water for the last 10 minutes of cooking. After 5 minutes, add the thawed peas. Once everything is cooked, drain the vegetables and let them cool completely.

Step 2: Prepare the Dressing

While the vegetables are cooling, you can prepare the mayonnaise dressing. If you’re using store-bought mayonnaise, you can skip this step. However, making your own mayonnaise can elevate the flavor of your salad.

  1. Homemade Mayonnaise: In a tall measuring cup, combine 1 egg, 1 tablespoon of mustard, a pinch of salt, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Using an immersion blender, slowly drizzle in 250 ml (about 1 cup) of extra virgin olive oil while blending until the mixture thickens and emulsifies.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

  1. Mix the Vegetables: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled potatoes, carrots, peas, chopped hard-boiled eggs, and drained tuna. Gently fold the ingredients together to avoid breaking the potatoes.

  2. Add the Dressing: Pour the mayonnaise over the vegetable mixture and gently fold until everything is well coated. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Incorporate Optional Ingredients: If you’re using olives, roasted red peppers, or any other optional ingredients, fold them in at this stage.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

  1. Chill the Salad: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the Ensaladilla Rusa for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld together. This dish can be made a day in advance, making it perfect for meal prep.

  2. Serve: Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir. You can garnish it with additional olives, sliced hard-boiled eggs, or a drizzle of olive oil for presentation. Ensaladilla Rusa is best served at room temperature or slightly chilled.

Tips for the Perfect Ensaladilla Rusa

  • Choose the Right Potatoes: Waxy potatoes hold their shape better than starchy ones, making them ideal for salads.
  • Don’t Overcook: Keep an eye on the cooking time for the potatoes and vegetables to ensure they remain firm and don’t turn mushy.
  • Customize to Your Taste: Feel free to experiment with different ingredients based on your preferences or what you have on hand.
  • Make It Ahead: This salad tastes even better the next day, so consider making it in advance for gatherings.

Serving Suggestions

Ensaladilla Rusa is incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways:

  • As a Tapas Dish: Serve it alongside other Spanish tapas like patatas bravas, chorizo al vino, or tortilla española for a delightful spread.
  • As a Side Dish: Pair it with grilled meats or fish for a refreshing side.
  • On Bread: Spread it on crusty bread or toast for a delicious open-faced sandwich.

Conclusion

Ensaladilla Rusa is more than just a potato salad; it’s a dish that brings people together, evoking memories of summer gatherings and festive celebrations. With its creamy texture and vibrant flavors, this Spanish potato salad is sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or add your own twist, this dish is a must-try for anyone looking to explore the rich culinary heritage of Spain. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and enjoy a taste of Spain right in your kitchen!
