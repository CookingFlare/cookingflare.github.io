Thai Coconut Rice Pudding Recipe with Mango and Coconut Milk


In the heart of Southeast Asia, Thai cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and aromatic spices. Among the many delightful Thai desserts, coconut rice pudding, or “Khao Neow Mamuang” in Thai, is a beloved treat that never fails to satisfy the sweet tooth. This creamy, comforting dessert is a perfect blend of Thai flavors, textures, and presentation. Today, we’ll explore the art of making Thai coconut rice pudding with mango and coconut milk, a recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your dessert repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of Thai jasmine rice
  • 3 cups of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of pandan extract (optional)
  • 2 ripe mangos, diced
  • 1/4 cup of toasted coconut flakes
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of unsweetened shredded coconut

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain the water and cook the rice in a medium saucepan with 3 cups of fresh water and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  2. Make the coconut milk mixture: In a separate saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and pandan extract (if using). Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture comes to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 5-7 minutes or until the mixture thickens slightly.
  3. Combine the rice and coconut milk mixture: Once the rice is cooked, add it to the coconut milk mixture and stir until well combined. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the rice is well coated with the coconut milk mixture.
  4. Chill the pudding: Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool to room temperature. Cover the saucepan with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  5. Prepare the mango topping: Just before serving, dice the mangos into bite-sized pieces. In a small bowl, mix together the toasted coconut flakes, chopped fresh mint leaves, and unsweetened shredded coconut.
  6. Assemble the dessert: Spoon the chilled coconut rice pudding into individual serving cups or glasses. Top with the diced mango, toasted coconut flakes, and chopped fresh mint leaves.

Tips and Variations

  • For a creamier pudding, use full-fat coconut milk instead of low-fat or non-fat.
  • Substitute the mangos with other fruits, such as jackfruit, durian, or bananas, for a different flavor profile.
  • Add a sprinkle of cardamom powder or a pinch of salt to the pudding for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Experiment with different types of rice, such as brown rice or glutinous rice, for a unique texture and flavor.

Conclusion

Thai coconut rice pudding with mango and coconut milk is a delightful dessert that’s sure to impress your family and friends. With its creamy texture, sweet and tangy flavors, and vibrant colors, this dessert is a perfect representation of Thai cuisine’s beauty and charm. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and requires minimal ingredients. So go ahead, give it a try, and indulge in the sweet and creamy goodness of this beloved Thai dessert.
