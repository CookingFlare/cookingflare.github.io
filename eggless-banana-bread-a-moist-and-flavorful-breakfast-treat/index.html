Eggless Banana Bread: A Moist and Flavorful Breakfast Treat


Banana bread is a classic breakfast treat loved by many, but what if you’re looking for an eggless version? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a moist and flavorful eggless banana bread that will leave you wanting more. Whether you have dietary restrictions or just want to try something new, this recipe is perfect for you.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the mashed bananas, plain yogurt, and vanilla extract to the butter-sugar mixture. Mix until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense banana bread.
  6. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. Bake for approximately 60-70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The baking time may vary, so keep an eye on it after the 50-minute mark.
  8. Once baked, remove the banana bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added twist, you can mix in some chopped nuts, such as walnuts or pecans, into the batter before baking. The nuts will add a delightful crunch and extra flavor to your banana bread.
  • If you prefer a sweeter banana bread, you can sprinkle some brown sugar on top of the batter before baking. This will create a delicious caramelized crust.
  • To make this recipe vegan-friendly, simply substitute the butter with a plant-based butter alternative and use a dairy-free yogurt.

Serving and Storing

Once your eggless banana bread has cooled completely, it’s time to enjoy! Slice it up and serve it as is or spread a little butter or cream cheese on top for an extra indulgence. This banana bread also goes well with a cup of coffee or tea.

If you have any leftovers, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3-4 days. You can also freeze individual slices for longer storage. Just make sure to wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or place them in a freezer bag before freezing.

Conclusion

Eggless banana bread is a delightful and satisfying breakfast treat that can be enjoyed by everyone. With its moist texture and subtle sweetness, it’s the perfect way to start your day. Whether you have dietary restrictions or simply want to try something new, this recipe is a must-try. So grab those ripe bananas and get baking!
