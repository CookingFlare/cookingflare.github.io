Rise to the Occasion: Baking Chinese Steamed Custard Buns


There’s something truly magical about the soft, pillowy texture of Chinese steamed custard buns. The delicate balance of sweet custard filling encased in fluffy, steamed dough is a delight for the senses. If you’ve ever savored these delectable treats and wondered how to recreate them at home, you’re in for a delightful culinary adventure. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making Chinese steamed custard buns from scratch, ensuring that your kitchen becomes a haven of irresistible aromas and flavors.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

For the Custard Filling:

  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Dough:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar and yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and baking powder. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and vegetable oil.

  3. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix the ingredients until they come together to form a dough.

  4. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

Preparing the Custard Filling:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it is hot but not boiling.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch until smooth and creamy.

  3. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture while whisking continuously to prevent curdling.

  4. Transfer the mixture back to the saucepan and cook it over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens to a custard-like consistency.

  5. Remove the custard from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let it cool to room temperature.

Assembling the Buns:

  1. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a lightly floured surface.

  2. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.

  3. Flatten each ball into a disc and place a spoonful of the custard filling in the center.

  4. Gather the edges of the dough and pinch them together to seal the bun.

  5. Place each filled bun on a small square of parchment paper and transfer them to a bamboo steamer, leaving some space between each bun to allow for expansion.

  6. Cover the steamer with a lid and let the buns rest for another 15 minutes.

Steaming the Buns:

  1. Fill a wok or a large pot with water and bring it to a boil.

  2. Place the bamboo steamer over the boiling water and steam the buns for 12-15 minutes, ensuring that the water doesn’t touch the buns.

  3. Once the buns are puffed and cooked through, carefully remove the steamer from the heat.

  4. Let the buns cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving and Enjoying

Chinese steamed custard buns are best enjoyed fresh and warm. Serve them as a delightful treat for breakfast, brunch, or as a comforting snack with a cup of tea. The soft, airy texture of the bun combined with the luscious custard filling is a delightful indulgence that is sure to captivate your taste buds.

Conclusion

Baking Chinese steamed custard buns at home is a rewarding experience that allows you to savor the authentic flavors of this beloved Chinese delicacy. With a little patience and attention to detail, you can create these delightful buns that are bound to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe offers a delightful culinary journey that celebrates the art of baking and the joy of savoring homemade treats. Embrace the process, savor the aromas, and delight in the heavenly taste of freshly steamed custard buns.
