Mango Salad with Grilled Chicken: A Tropical Thai Salad


Imagine yourself on a sun-drenched beach, the gentle ocean breeze caressing your skin, and the aroma of exotic spices wafting through the air. Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1 head of lettuce or mixed greens, washed and torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1/4 cup fried shallots (optional)

Dressing:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1-2 Thai chili peppers, finely chopped (adjust to desired spice level)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken: Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Heat a grill pan or outdoor grill to medium-high heat. Brush the chicken with olive oil and grill until cooked through, about 6-8 minutes per side. Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes, then slice it into thin strips or bite-sized pieces.

  2. Make the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, sesame oil, and chopped chili peppers. Set aside.

  3. Assemble the salad: In a large bowl, combine the sliced mangoes, lettuce or mixed greens, sliced cucumber, red bell pepper, cilantro leaves, and mint leaves.

  4. Add the chicken and dressing: Add the sliced grilled chicken to the salad bowl. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

  5. Garnish and serve: Transfer the salad to a serving platter or individual plates. Garnish with roasted peanuts and fried shallots (if using). Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of tropical flavors!

Tips and Variations

  • Vegetarian option: Omit the chicken and add tofu or grilled mushrooms for a delicious vegetarian version.
  • Protein options: Instead of chicken, you can also use grilled shrimp, beef, or pork.
  • Fruit options: If mangoes are not in season, you can substitute them with other tropical fruits like papaya or pineapple.
  • Spice it up: Increase the amount of chili peppers in the dressing for an extra kick of heat.
  • Nutty twist: Instead of peanuts, try using toasted almonds or cashews for a different nutty crunch.
  • Meal prep: This salad can be prepared in advance by keeping the dressing separate and adding it just before serving.
