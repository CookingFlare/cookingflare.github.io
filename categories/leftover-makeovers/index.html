Leftover Love: How to Turn Yesterday's Turkey into Today's Stellar Dishes

Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, a delicious turkey feast. But what do you do with all that leftover turkey once the festivities are over? Don’t let it go to waste! With a little creativity and some pantry staples, you can transform yesterday’s turkey into today’s stellar dishes. Get ready to fall in love with your leftovers all over again! Creamy Turkey and Mushroom Pasta Ingredients: 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded 8 ounces pasta of your choice 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced 1 cup chicken broth 1 cup heavy cream Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh parsley, for garnish Instructions: Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Add the mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown. Stir in the shredded turkey and cook for another minute or two. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer for another 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss until well coated in the creamy turkey and mushroom sauce. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley. Turkey and Cranberry Quesadillas Ingredients: 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded 4 large flour tortillas 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/2 cup cranberry sauce 1/4 cup sliced green onions 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Instructions: Preheat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lay one tortilla flat on the skillet and sprinkle half of the shredded cheese evenly over the surface. Spread half of the cranberry sauce over the cheese, then top with half of the shredded turkey, green onions, and cilantro. Place another tortilla on top and press down gently. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom tortilla is golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip the quesadilla and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the other side is golden brown. Remove from the skillet and let it cool for a minute before slicing into wedges. Repeat the process with the remaining tortillas and ingredients. Serve the quesadillas warm with sour cream or additional cranberry sauce for dipping. Turkey Pot Pie Ingredients: 2 cups leftover turkey, diced 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn) 1/2 cup diced potatoes 1/4 cup diced onion 2 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tablespoons butter 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1 cup chicken broth 1/2 cup milk 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme Salt and pepper, to taste 1 package refrigerated pie crusts Instructions: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Stir in the diced potatoes and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat evenly. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Add the frozen mixed vegetables, diced turkey, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 5 minutes, until the sauce has thickened. Meanwhile, roll out one of the pie crusts and line a pie dish with it. Pour the turkey mixture into the pie dish and spread it out evenly. Roll out the second pie crust and place it on top of the filling. Seal the edges and cut a few slits in the crust to allow steam to escape. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Allow the pot pie to cool for a few minutes before serving. Conclusion Leftover turkey doesn’t have to be boring or repetitive.
