Italian Cooking for Two: Lovers, Friends, and Familes


Cooking for two can sometimes seem like a daunting task - restricted to the usual suspects of grilled cheese or pasta with marinara sauce. Fear not, for this guide will help you whip up an authentic Italian feast perfect for sharing with no more than one special someone or a cozy gathering with a few close friends and family.

Antipasto Platter

Ingredients:

  • Prosciutto di Parma
  • Fresh mozzarella
  • Artichoke hearts
  • Marinated mushrooms
  • Pepperoncini
  • Aged balsamic vinegar
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Spread a large wooden board with a cheesecloth or a round of parchment paper.
  2. Arrange the prosciutto slices around the board, making sure to leave space between each slice so guests can grab a piece easily.
  3. On top of the prosciutto, arrange the rest of the ingredients and drizzle with a generous amount of aged balsamic vinegar and extra-virgin olive oil. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.

Pasta alle Vongole (Clams Pasta)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 pounds littleneck clams, scrubbed
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons red pepper flakes
  • Grated Pecorino Romano cheese

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta according to package directions until al dente.
  2. In a large skillet, heat extra-virgin olive oil over medium heat. Add sliced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add clams and white wine, cover and cook until clams open, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Once pasta is cooked, drain and add to the skillet. Toss to combine with the clam mixture.
  5. Add parsley, red pepper flakes, and drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil. Toss to coat.
  6. Serve pasta with a generous sprinkling of grated Pecorino Romano cheese.

Caprese Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 large ball of fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Lay out tomato slices on a large serving platter.
  2. Arrange fresh mozzarella slices over the tomatoes.
  3. Sprinkle with fresh basil leaves, covering the tomatoes and mozzarella generously.
  4. Drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegar, creating a light dressing over the salad.
  5. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.

Tiramisu

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee, warm
  • 1/4 cup dark rum
  • 12 ounces ladyfingers
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Whisk in milk and set the bowl over a saucepan of simmering water, making sure the water does not touch the bottom of the bowl. Whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and reaches 160°F (71°C), 2-4 minutes.
  3. Remove the bowl from the heat and whisk in the mascarpone until smooth.
  4. In a small bowl, stir together coffee and rum and arrange ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of a 9-inch square baking dish.
  5. Spoon half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then drizzle with half the coffee mixture.
  6. Arrange another layer of ladyfingers on top and spoon the remaining mascarpone mixture.
  7. Drizzle in the remaining coffee mixture and dust the top with cocoa powder.
  8. Let tiramisu chill for at least 4 hours before serving.

By adding these delectable dishes to your Italian cooking repertoire, not only will you have a memorable time in the kitchen, but your guests will be left in awe. Whether you’re celebrating a romantic anniversary with your better half, or simply gathered with friends and family, these Italian favorites will surely be the highlight of any meal. Buon Appetito!
