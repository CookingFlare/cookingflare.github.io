Vegan Dessert Recipes for the Whole Family


When it comes to dessert, the joy of indulging in something sweet is universal, transcending age and dietary preferences. Vegan desserts have gained popularity not just for their health benefits, but also for their ability to satisfy that sweet tooth without compromising on flavor or texture. Whether you’re already familiar with plant-based cooking or just starting out, these vegan dessert recipes are perfect for the whole family. They are simple, delicious, and sure to impress even the pickiest eaters!

1. Decadent Vegan Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil (or melted coconut oil)
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together almond milk, oil, apple cider vinegar, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix!
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring to wire racks. Once cooled, frost with your favorite vegan chocolate frosting.

Tips

  • For an extra boost of flavor, add 1/2 cup of vegan chocolate chips to the batter.
  • Serve with fresh berries for a refreshing contrast.

2. Creamy Vegan Chocolate Mousse

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk, chilled overnight
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, melt the chocolate chips over low heat, stirring constantly until smooth.
  2. In a mixing bowl, scoop the solid part of the chilled coconut milk, leaving the liquid behind.
  3. Add the melted chocolate, maple syrup, and vanilla extract to the coconut cream.
  4. Using an electric mixer, whip the mixture until creamy and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Spoon into dessert cups and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

Tips

  • Garnish with fresh fruit or a sprinkle of cocoa powder for a gourmet touch.
  • This mousse can also be layered with crushed vegan cookies for a delightful parfait.

3. No-Bake Vegan Cheesecake

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs (or almond flour for gluten-free)
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup

For the filling:

  • 1 ½ cups soaked cashews (soak in water for 4 hours)
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted coconut oil, and maple syrup. Mix until well combined.
  2. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan to form the crust.
  3. In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, maple syrup, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Pour the filling over the crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until set. Serve chilled.

Tips

  • Top with fresh fruit, such as berries or sliced kiwi, for a colorful presentation.
  • This cheesecake can be flavored with cocoa powder or matcha for an extra twist.

4. Vegan Banana Bread

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup walnuts or chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mix together the mashed bananas and melted coconut oil.
  3. Stir in the brown sugar, almond milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add baking soda and salt, and mix well.
  5. Gradually add the flour, stirring until just combined. Fold in walnuts or chocolate chips if using.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let cool before slicing.

Tips

  • For added flavor, mix in spices like cinnamon or nutmeg.
  • This banana bread freezes well; slice it and store in an airtight container for later enjoyment.

5. Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine almond flour, oats, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together softened coconut oil, brown sugar, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them out.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Tips

  • Experiment with different mix-ins like dried fruits or nuts for a unique twist.
  • Store cookies in an airtight container for up to a week.

6. Fruit Sorbet

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of your favorite frozen fruit (mango, strawberries, or blueberries)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the frozen fruit, maple syrup, and lemon juice.
  2. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides as necessary.
  3. Scoop into bowls and serve immediately, or freeze for later use.

Tips

  • Use fresh fruit instead of frozen for a different texture.
  • Add herbs like mint or basil for a refreshing twist.

Conclusion

These vegan dessert recipes are not just for those following a plant-based diet; they are delightful treats that everyone in the family can enjoy. From rich chocolate cakes to creamy cheesecakes, these recipes are designed to bring joy and satisfaction to your dessert table. Easy to prepare and bursting with flavors, they promise to create cherished memories with every bite. So gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and dive into the world of delicious vegan desserts!
