Deliciously Sweet Mexican Flan Recipe


Are you in the mood for something sweet and indulgent? Look no further than a traditional Mexican flan recipe. This classic dessert has been a staple in Mexican cuisine for generations, and its creamy texture and caramelized topping make it a true delight. What is Flan? Flan is a type of custard dessert made with eggs, sugar, and cream, similar to crème brûlée. However, it's smoother and more velvety, with a rich, creamy texture that's sure to please even the pickiest eaters. The best part? It's incredibly easy to make at home, requiring only a few simple ingredients and minimal preparation. The History of Flan Flan has its roots in Spanish cuisine, dating back to the Roman Empire. The dessert was originally called "plato de huevo," or "egg dish," and was made with eggs, sugar, and milk. As the dessert spread throughout the world, it evolved into the creamy, caramel-topped delight we know and love today. Why Make Your Own Flan? While store-bought flan can be convenient, making your own at home ensures that you get to control the ingredients and the level of sweetness. Plus, it's a fun and rewarding dessert to make, especially for beginners. With a few simple steps and some basic kitchen equipment, you can create a delicious, creamy flan that's sure to impress your friends and family. Deliciously Sweet Mexican Flan Recipe Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups (190g) granulated sugar 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted 1 cup (235ml) heavy cream 1/2 cup (115ml) whole milk 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 4 large eggs 1/2 cup (65g) granulated sugar (for caramel topping)

Instructions:

Preheat the Oven and Caramelize the Sugar: Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Mix the sugar, melted butter, and 2 tablespoons of water in a small saucepan and heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar has dissolved. Bring to a boil and simmer for 2-3 minutes, or until the caramel turns a deep amber color. Remove from heat and carefully pour into the bottom of a 9-inch (23cm) round baking dish. Prepare the Custard: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, salt, and vanilla extract. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, until well combined. Pour in the sugar and whisk until well combined. Pour the Custard into the Baking Dish: Pour the custard mixture over the caramelized sugar in the baking dish. Bake the Flan: Place the baking dish in a large roasting pan and add hot water to come halfway up the sides. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is still slightly jiggly. Chill the Flan: Remove the baking dish from the oven and let cool to room temperature. Cover with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Caramelize the Topping: Just before serving, sprinkle the sugar over the top of the chilled flan and caramelize with a kitchen torch or under the broiler.

Tips and Variations

For a more stable flan, use a water bath to prevent the eggs from cooking too quickly. To add flavor, try substituting different extracts like almond, coconut, or coffee. For a blackberry or raspberry sauce, simply puree fresh or frozen berries with a tablespoon of sugar and a squeeze of lemon juice. To make individual flans, use 6 1/4-cup (150g) ramekins and adjust the baking time to 20-25 minutes.

Storage and Serving To store the flan, refrigerate it for up to 3 days in an airtight container. To serve, simply top with whipped cream, chopped nuts, or a drizzle of caramel sauce. Conclusion With this deliciously sweet Mexican flan recipe, you'll be sure to impress your friends and family with a creamy, caramel-topped dessert that's sure to please even the pickiest eaters. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned baker, this recipe is a fun and rewarding dessert to make. So go ahead, give it a try, and indulge in the rich, velvety goodness of homemade flan!
