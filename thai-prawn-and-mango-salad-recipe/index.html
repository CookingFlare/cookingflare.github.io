Thai Prawn and Mango Salad Recipe


When it comes to salads that burst with vibrant colors, distinct textures, and a harmonious blend of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors, Thai prawn and mango salad stands out as a true culinary gem. This refreshing dish combines succulent prawns, luscious ripe mangoes, crisp vegetables, and a zesty dressing, creating a sensory experience that transports you straight to the bustling streets of Thailand.

Whether you’re looking for a light lunch, an impressive appetizer for dinner parties, or a healthy yet indulgent meal option, this Thai prawn and mango salad recipe delivers on all fronts. Let’s dive into the details and explore how to prepare this delightful dish step by step.

Why Thai Prawn and Mango Salad?

This salad isn’t just a medley of ingredients thrown together. It’s a thoughtful composition that balances delicate seafood with the tropical sweetness of mango, the crunch of fresh vegetables, and the bright punch of Thai-inspired dressing ingredients like lime, fish sauce, and chili. Not only is it flavorful, but it also offers a nutritional boost with lean protein, fiber-rich produce, and a refreshing, light profile perfect for warm-weather dining.

Key Components:

  • Prawns: Juicy, tender, and packed with protein.
  • Mango: Sweet, fruity, and fragrant, adding a tropical flair.
  • Vegetables: Crisp cucumbers, crunchy red onions, and fresh herbs like cilantro and mint provide depth.
  • Dressing: A tangy, spicy concoction combining lime juice, fish sauce, palm sugar, and chilies that ties all the flavors together.

Ingredients

Here are the ingredients you’ll need to create this sensational Thai prawn and mango salad:

For the Salad

  • 300 grams of fresh prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 large ripe mango, peeled and cut into strips or cubes
  • 1 small cucumber, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • A handful of cherry tomatoes, halved (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Fresh Thai basil (optional)
  • 1-2 fresh red chilies, thinly sliced (adjust to your spice tolerance)
  • 2 tablespoons toasted peanuts, crushed (for garnish)

For the Dressing

  • 3 tablespoons lime juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 garlic clove, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes or finely chopped fresh chili
  • 1 tablespoon water (to adjust consistency)

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Prepare the Prawns

Start by bringing a pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add in the prawns and cook until pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes. Be careful not to overcook, as prawns can become tough. Once cooked, drain the prawns and submerge them in ice water immediately to stop the cooking process and keep them tender. Drain again and set aside.

2. Slice and Prepare the Fruits and Vegetables

Peel the mango and slice it into thin strips or bite-sized cubes depending on your preference. Thinly slice the cucumber and red onion. If you like a touch of sweetness and juiciness, halve the cherry tomatoes. Pick the herbs from the stems and roughly chop or tear large leaves.

3. Make the Dressing

In a small bowl, combine the lime juice, fish sauce, palm sugar, minced garlic, chili flakes, and water. Stir well until the sugar dissolves fully. Taste the dressing and adjust, balancing the sweet, sour, salty, and spicy notes as per your liking.

4. Assemble the Salad

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked prawns, mango strips, cucumber, red onion, cherry tomatoes (if using), and fresh herbs. Pour the dressing over the salad and gently toss everything to ensure an even coating.

5. Garnish and Serve

Transfer the salad onto a serving plate or bowl, and sprinkle the crushed toasted peanuts on top for a delightful crunch. Add extra slices of fresh chili or herbs if you wish to boost the flavor and make it visually appealing.

Tips for Perfecting Your Salad

  • Prawn Selection: If fresh prawns are not available, you can use frozen prawns as well. Just make sure to thaw them completely before cooking.
  • Ripeness of Mango: For the best flavor, choose a mango that is ripe but not overly soft. The ideal mango adds sweetness and vibrant color without becoming mushy.
  • Balancing the Dressing: This is the heart of the salad’s flavor profile. Slowly add palm sugar and lime juice to your taste, balancing between tangy and sweet with a salty kick from the fish sauce.
  • Crunch Matters: Don’t skip the toasted peanuts; their crunch contrasts beautifully with the soft mango and tender prawns.
  • Customize the Heat: If you’re sensitive to spice, reduce or remove the fresh chilies and chili flakes. For those who love heat, add more or even a dash of Thai bird’s eye chili.

Nutritional Benefits

This Thai prawn and mango salad is more than just a feast for the taste buds; it’s also a nutritional powerhouse:

  • Protein: Prawns provide essential proteins with low fat.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: Mangoes are rich in Vitamin C, A, and antioxidants, promoting healthy skin and immunity.
  • Digestive Health: Fresh herbs and vegetables contribute fiber and vitamins for digestion.
  • Low Calorie: This salad is light and perfect for those looking for a healthy yet fulfilling meal.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

Feel free to experiment and tweak this recipe to your liking. Some popular variations include:

  • Add Rice Noodles: For a heartier meal, toss in some cooked, cooled rice noodles.
  • Swap Protein: Instead of prawns, grilled chicken or tofu can be excellent substitutes.
  • Extra Crunch: Sliced roasted cashews or almonds can replace peanuts.
  • Fruit Twists: Add fruits like pineapple or papaya for more tropical vibes.
  • Make it a Wrap: Use large lettuce leaves or rice paper to wrap portions of the salad for a fresh, handheld snack.

Serving this salad chilled intensifies its refreshing qualities, making it an incredible summer dish.

Final Thoughts

The Thai prawn and mango salad is a splendid blend of exotic flavors and textures that’s simple enough for weeknight dinners but sophisticated enough to impress guests. Its balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy notes illustrates the beauty of Thai cuisine — bright, bold, and unforgettable.

Give this recipe a try and enjoy a taste of Thailand from your own kitchen. The marriage of prawns and mango with that zesty dressing is nothing short of magical. Each bite is a journey — colorful, refreshing, and utterly satisfying.

Ready to dive into this sensory delight? Gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and create your own vibrant bowl of Thai prawn and mango salad today!
