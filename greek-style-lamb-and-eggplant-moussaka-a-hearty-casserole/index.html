Greek-style Lamb and Eggplant Moussaka: A Hearty Casserole


Moussaka is a traditional Greek dish that combines layers of succulent lamb, creamy eggplant, and a rich tomato sauce, topped with a velvety béchamel sauce. This hearty casserole is a true comfort food, perfect for a cozy dinner on a chilly evening. The combination of flavors and textures in this dish is simply divine, making it a favorite among Greek cuisine enthusiasts.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds ground lamb
  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the ground lamb and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Remove the lamb from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add a little more olive oil if needed, then sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant and translucent.

  4. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well to combine the flavors and let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes to thicken slightly.

  5. While the sauce is simmering, heat another skillet with olive oil over medium-high heat. Fry the eggplant slices on both sides until golden brown. You may need to do this in batches.

  6. Grease a large baking dish with olive oil and arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices on the bottom. Top the eggplant with a layer of the lamb sauce, spreading it evenly.

  7. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of eggplant on top. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the final layer.

  8. To make the béchamel sauce, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the flour until well combined, then gradually whisk in the milk. Continue cooking and whisking until the sauce thickens.

  9. Remove the saucepan from the heat and season the béchamel sauce with ground nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Allow the sauce to cool slightly, then gradually whisk in the beaten eggs.

  10. Pour the béchamel sauce evenly over the top layer of eggplant in the baking dish.

  11. Place the moussaka in the preheated oven and bake for about 45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the dish is bubbly.

  12. Remove the moussaka from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to slice.

  13. Serve the moussaka warm, garnished with some fresh parsley if desired. It pairs perfectly with a crisp green salad and some crusty bread.

Conclusion

Greek-style Lamb and Eggplant Moussaka is a truly satisfying dish that brings the flavors of Greece straight to your table. With its layers of tender lamb, creamy eggplant, and aromatic spices, this casserole is a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving some comfort food, this moussaka recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and transport yourself to the Mediterranean with every delicious bite.
