The Perfect Side Dish: Mexican Rice Recipes for Tacos and Burritos


When it comes to Mexican cuisine, tacos and burritos are undoubtedly crowd favorites. The combination of flavorful fillings, crunchy shells, and a medley of toppings creates a delightful explosion of taste in every bite. But what makes these Mexican classics truly shine is the perfect side dish that accompanies them – Mexican rice.

Mexican rice, also known as “arroz rojo,” is a staple in Mexican cooking. This flavorful and aromatic rice dish is the ideal complement to tacos and burritos, adding a burst of color, texture, and taste to any meal. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering Mexican rice recipes that will take your taco and burrito game to the next level.

1. Classic Mexican Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, until it turns golden brown.
  4. Stir in the tomato puree, chicken or vegetable broth, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with fresh cilantro, and serve hot.

2. Spicy Mexican Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and jalapeño pepper, and sauté until fragrant and softened.
  3. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, until it turns golden brown.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken or vegetable broth, chili powder, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Serve the spicy Mexican rice with lime wedges on the side for an extra kick of flavor.

3. Mexican Rice with Corn and Black Beans

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 cup canned black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, until it turns golden brown.
  4. Stir in the frozen corn kernels, black beans, chicken or vegetable broth, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with fresh cilantro, and serve hot.

Conclusion

These three Mexican rice recipes are the perfect side dishes to elevate your tacos and burritos. Whether you prefer the classic version, the spicy kick of jalapeño, or the added goodness of corn and black beans, Mexican rice brings a vibrant and delicious element to any meal. So, next time you’re whipping up a batch of tacos or burritos, don’t forget to make one of these flavorful rice dishes. Your taste buds will thank you!

Remember, the key to cooking is experimentation and making each dish your own. Feel free to adjust the seasonings, add your favorite vegetables, or even throw in some protein to customize these recipes to your liking. Enjoy the culinary journey and savor the flavors of Mexico with these delectable rice recipes.
