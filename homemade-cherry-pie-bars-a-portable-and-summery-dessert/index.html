Homemade Cherry Pie Bars: A Portable and Summery Dessert


There’s nothing quite like the taste of fresh, juicy cherries in the summertime. The sweet and tart flavor of cherries is a perfect match for a buttery, flaky crust, making cherry pie a classic dessert that’s always a crowd-pleaser. However, when you’re on the go or hosting a picnic, bringing a whole cherry pie can be a bit cumbersome. That’s where Homemade Cherry Pie Bars come in – they’re a portable and summery twist on the traditional cherry pie, making them perfect for enjoying the delicious flavors of cherry pie on the move.

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes

For the Cherry Filling:

  • 4 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

For the Crumb Topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.

  2. Make the Crust: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt for the crust. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until lightly golden brown.

  3. Prepare the Cherry Filling: In a medium saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, almond extract, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens, about 10-12 minutes. Remove from heat and let the cherry filling cool slightly.

  4. Make the Crumb Topping: In a separate bowl, combine the flour and sugar for the crumb topping. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the dry ingredients until large crumbs form.

  5. Assemble and Bake: Spread the slightly cooled cherry filling over the baked crust. Sprinkle the crumb topping evenly over the cherry filling. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the crumb topping is golden brown.

  6. Cool and Serve: Allow the cherry pie bars to cool completely in the pan before using the parchment paper overhang to lift them out. Cut into bars and serve at room temperature or chilled.

Portable and Perfect for Summer

These Homemade Cherry Pie Bars are a delightful way to enjoy the flavors of cherry pie in a convenient and portable form. Whether you’re headed to a picnic, barbecue, or simply want to savor a taste of summer on the go, these bars are the ideal treat. The buttery crust, sweet cherry filling, and crumbly topping come together to create a dessert that’s sure to impress.

So, next time you find yourself craving the luscious taste of cherry pie, consider whipping up a batch of Homemade Cherry Pie Bars. They’re easy to make, transport, and share, making them a versatile and irresistible addition to your summer baking repertoire. Enjoy the taste of summer with every bite!

Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, these cherry pie bars are a great way to showcase your skills and treat your loved ones to a mouthwatering dessert. Get creative with presentation and enjoy the delightful flavors of homemade cherry pie in a portable form. Happy baking and savoring the taste of summer!
