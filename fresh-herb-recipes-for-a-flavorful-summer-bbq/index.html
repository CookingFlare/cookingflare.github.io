Fresh Herb Recipes for a Flavorful Summer BBQ


Summer is synonymous with outdoor gatherings, the sizzle of the grill, and the irresistible aromas of a well-prepared barbecue. If you’re looking to elevate your BBQ game, fresh herbs are the perfect way to bring bold, vibrant flavors to your dishes. They not only enhance the taste of your meats but can also add a refreshing twist to your sides and sauces. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of creative and delicious ways to use fresh herbs at your next summer BBQ, from marinades and rubs to garnishes and salads.

The Power of Fresh Herbs in BBQ Cooking

Herbs are an essential part of cooking, especially when grilling in the warmer months. Fresh herbs not only provide natural flavor but also have the added benefit of being aromatic, making your BBQ experience even more delightful. The combination of heat and fresh herbs results in aromatic, flavorful dishes that are sure to wow your guests.

Whether you’re using classic herbs like rosemary, thyme, and basil, or experimenting with more unusual varieties like tarragon or mint, fresh herbs can be used in a variety of ways. From infusing oils and vinegars to incorporating them into marinades, herb-based sauces, and even drinks, there’s no shortage of ways to enhance the flavor profile of your summer BBQ.

Tips for Using Fresh Herbs at Your BBQ

Before we dive into specific recipes, here are a few tips on how to make the most out of fresh herbs:

  • Use Fresh, Not Dried: When possible, always use fresh herbs for BBQ cooking. The flavors of fresh herbs are more vibrant and robust than dried ones.

  • Grill the Herbs: Grilling can bring out the natural oils in herbs, which intensifies their flavor. You can place whole sprigs of rosemary or thyme directly on the grill for an aromatic infusion in your grilled dishes.

  • Herb-Infused Oils and Butters: Consider creating herb-infused oils or butters for grilling. These can be used to brush onto meats and vegetables or serve as dips.

  • Chop Herbs for Salsas and Sauces: Freshly chopped herbs add a burst of flavor and color to salsas, dips, and BBQ sauces.

  • Pair Herbs with Complementary Ingredients: Some herbs pair beautifully with specific meats or vegetables. For example, rosemary and lamb, basil and tomatoes, and mint and lamb are classic pairings that never disappoint.

Now, let’s dive into some mouthwatering herb-infused recipes that will take your summer BBQ to the next level.

Herb-Marinated Grilled Chicken

Chicken is a BBQ staple, and marinating it with fresh herbs can infuse it with bold flavors that penetrate deep into the meat, creating a deliciously tender and juicy result.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts or thighs (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a medium bowl, combine the parsley, basil, thyme, garlic, and lemon zest. Add the olive oil and white wine vinegar, and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag or shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Grill the Chicken: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and place it on the grill. Grill for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  4. Serve: Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes. Serve with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a sprinkle of fresh herbs on top.

Grilled Veggie Skewers with Herb Dressing

Grilled vegetables are a fantastic side dish at any BBQ, and when paired with a herb-infused dressing, they become an irresistible offering. These skewers can be customized with your favorite veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, cut into thick slices
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup fresh oregano leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Skewers: Thread the bell peppers, zucchini, onion, and cherry tomatoes onto skewers. Brush the veggies lightly with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

  2. Grill the Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the veggie skewers for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are tender and slightly charred.

  3. Make the Herb Dressing: While the veggies are grilling, whisk together the oregano, parsley, lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.

  4. Serve: Once the skewers are off the grill, drizzle them with the fresh herb dressing. Garnish with extra herbs, if desired.

Mint and Basil Pesto for Grilled Meats

Pesto is a versatile sauce that can be used to elevate meats, vegetables, and even pasta. This refreshing version uses fresh mint and basil, creating a unique and fragrant topping for your grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (or walnuts)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pesto: In a food processor, combine the basil, mint, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. Pulse until the ingredients are finely chopped.

  2. Add Olive Oil: With the food processor running, slowly stream in the olive oil until the pesto reaches a smooth consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Serve: Drizzle the pesto over grilled chicken, lamb, or even grilled vegetables. The bright and fresh flavors of mint and basil will add a refreshing twist to any BBQ dish.

Herb-Infused BBQ Sauce

No BBQ is complete without a flavorful BBQ sauce to slather on your grilled meats. Adding fresh herbs to your BBQ sauce can take it from ordinary to extraordinary. This herb-infused BBQ sauce is perfect for brushing on ribs, burgers, or chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, honey (or brown sugar), and Dijon mustard. Stir well to combine.

  2. Add Fresh Herbs and Spices: Add the thyme, rosemary, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.

  3. Simmer the Sauce: Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the flavors meld together.

  4. Serve: Brush the sauce over your grilled meats during the last few minutes of cooking or serve it on the side for dipping.

Herb-Infused Water for Refreshing BBQ Beverages

While fresh herbs are often used in savory dishes, they can also elevate your beverages. Herb-infused water is a simple yet refreshing way to keep your guests hydrated during the hot summer BBQ months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 handful fresh mint leaves
  • 1 handful fresh basil leaves
  • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
  • 1 lime, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups water
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Infused Water: In a large pitcher, combine the cucumber, mint, basil, lemon, and lime slices. Add the water and stir gently.

  2. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve over ice for a cool, refreshing beverage.

Conclusion

Fresh herbs are a wonderful way to bring vibrant flavors to your summer BBQ spread. Whether you’re grilling meats, vegetables, or creating refreshing beverages, herbs can take your BBQ to the next level. From marinades and sauces to drinks and salads, there are endless ways to use fresh herbs to enhance your outdoor dining experience. With these recipes, you’ll be ready to impress your guests with a delicious, herb-filled BBQ celebration.
