How to Make Korean Spicy Tuna (Chamchi Bokkeum) at Home


Are you craving a dish that’s packed with bold flavors, easy to prepare, and versatile enough to serve as an entrée, side dish, or snack? Korean Spicy Tuna, also known as Chamchi Bokkeum, checks all these boxes. This delicious stir-fry combines tender canned tuna with a medley of Korean-inspired seasonings, creating a dish you simply won’t be able to resist. Whether you’re new to Korean cuisine or looking for a quick and crowd-pleasing recipe, Chamchi Bokkeum is a perfect choice.

In this guide, we’ll dive into every detail of making this flavorful dish at home, ensuring your cooking process is seamless, fun, and yields restaurant-quality results. Let’s get started.

Why You’ll Love Korean Spicy Tuna

Korean Spicy Tuna is a genius recipe that makes use of the most humble pantry staple: canned tuna. By adding vibrant gochujang (Korean red chili paste), sesame oil, soy sauce, and aromatic vegetables, this dish transforms into a savory, spicy masterpiece bursting with umami flavor. It’s not only quick to make but also highly nutritious, thanks to the protein-packed tuna and nutrient-dense seasonings.

One of the best parts? Its versatility. Serve it with steamed rice for a comforting meal, wrap it in seaweed for a quick snack, or enjoy it alongside other banchan (Korean side dishes). This recipe is also customizable to suit your spice tolerance and taste preferences.

Ingredients to Make Korean Spicy Tuna

Before you step into the kitchen, here’s everything you’ll need to gather. Most of these ingredients can be found in your local Asian market or ordered online. Let’s break it down:

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 can of tuna (5-6 oz): Choose a high-quality canned tuna in water or oil. Either works well in this recipe, but if you prefer a richer flavor, tuna in oil is a better option.
  • 1 small onion: Finely diced for a touch of sweetness and texture.
  • 2 to 3 garlic cloves: Minced, adding a fragrant note to the dish.
  • 1 green chili or red chili: Thinly sliced for some extra spice (optional).
  • 1 green onion: Chopped, with the white part cooked in the dish and the green part reserved for garnish.

For the Sauce:

  • 1 to 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste): The star ingredient that delivers heat and a depth of flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes): Boosts spiciness while adding a slightly smoky undertone.
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce: Adds saltiness and umami.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil: For a nutty aroma that enhances the overall flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon sugar or honey: Balances out the heat with a hint of sweetness.
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar (optional): Adds a touch of acidity for brightness.
  • 1 tablespoon water: To loosen up the sauce, making it easier to coat the tuna.

For Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds: Toasted, for an added crunch and nutty flavor.
  • Chopped green onions: For a pop of freshness.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Chamchi Bokkeum

Follow these simple steps to create your own Korean Spicy Tuna dish. This recipe takes less than 20 minutes from start to finish, making it perfect for weeknight dinners or last-minute cravings.

1. Prepare the Ingredients

Start by opening your can of tuna and draining any excess liquid. Use a fork to gently flake the tuna into smaller pieces. Set it aside.

Next, finely dice the onion, mince the garlic, slice the chili, and chop your green onion. Having all your ingredients prepped ahead of time will make the cooking process smoother.

2. Mix the Sauce

In a small bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar or honey, rice vinegar (if using), and a tablespoon of water. Stir well until the mixture is smooth and well combined. Taste the sauce and adjust the spice level or sweetness to your preference.

3. Cook the Aromatics

Heat a tablespoon of oil (vegetable or sesame) in a nonstick pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the diced onion and cook for 2-3 minutes until it turns translucent and begins to caramelize.

Add the minced garlic and sliced chili, and sauté for another 30 seconds to a minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter.

4. Stir in the Sauce

Lower the heat slightly and pour the sauce mixture into the pan with the aromatics. Stir constantly for about a minute to allow the flavors to meld and for the sauce to thicken slightly. This step is crucial for achieving that rich, flavorful base.

5. Add the Tuna

Gently fold the flaked tuna into the sauce mixture, making sure every piece is evenly coated. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tuna is heated through and the sauce is well absorbed. Be gentle to avoid breaking the tuna into overly small pieces—it’s nice to have some chunks for texture.

6. Garnish and Serve

Turn off the heat and sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions over the dish. Transfer your Chamchi Bokkeum to a serving plate or bowl and enjoy it warm.

Tips for Perfect Chamchi Bokkeum

  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of gochujang and omit the gochugaru. For those who love extra heat, add more chili flakes or drizzle in some Korean spicy chili oil.
  • Experiment with Add-Ins: Feel free to add diced bell peppers, carrots, or mushrooms for extra texture and nutrition. You can also throw in a handful of chopped kimchi for a tangy kick that complements the tuna.
  • Use Fresh Tuna: If you prefer, you can use fresh tuna steak instead of canned tuna. Simply cook the tuna in some oil before following the rest of the recipe.
  • Serving Ideas: Serve Chamchi Bokkeum over steamed white rice, with a side of roasted seaweed sheets (nori) for wrapping. You can also use it as a filling for rice balls or kimbap rolls.

Why Chamchi Bokkeum is a Pantry Hero

This humble dish is a testament to the magic of transforming simple ingredients into something extraordinary. Canned tuna may seem ordinary, but when paired with the boldness of Korean seasonings, it becomes a comforting, flavorful delight. Plus, this recipe is highly adaptable—whether you’re looking to clear out your pantry, prep a quick meal, or impress guests with minimal effort.

Storage and Reheating

Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it up in a skillet over low heat, adding a small splash of water to loosen the sauce if needed. Chamchi Bokkeum tastes just as good the next day, as the flavors deepen over time.
