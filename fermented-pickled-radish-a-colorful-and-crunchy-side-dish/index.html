Fermented Pickled Radish: A Colorful and Crunchy Side Dish


Fermented pickled radish is a popular condiment in many Asian cuisines, particularly in Korean and Chinese cooking. This side dish is not only delicious, but also packed with nutrients and health benefits. In this recipe, we will explore the process of making fermented pickled radish at home, and provide tips on how to incorporate it into your meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 large radishes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) sea salt
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) fish sauce (optional)
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) chopped scallions, for garnish

Equipment

  • Large bowl
  • Colander
  • Cheesecloth or a clean cotton cloth
  • Glass jar with a tight-fitting lid
  • Weight, such as a plate or a stone

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced radishes, Korean chili flakes, sea salt, and water. Mix well to ensure the radishes are evenly coated with the spice mixture.
  2. Cover the bowl with cheesecloth or a clean cotton cloth, and let it sit at room temperature for 24 hours. This step is called “curing” and helps to remove excess moisture from the radishes.
  3. After 24 hours, drain the radishes in a colander and rinse them with cold water to remove excess salt.
  4. In a large glass jar, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and fish sauce (if using). Stir until the sugar and salt are dissolved.
  5. Pack the radishes into the jar, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space at the top. Pour the vinegar mixture over the radishes, making sure they are completely covered.
  6. Cover the jar with a tight-fitting lid and weight it down with a plate or a stone. This will help to keep the radishes submerged under the brine.
  7. Let the pickled radish sit at room temperature for 3-5 days, or until it reaches your desired level of fermentation. You can check the fermentation by tasting the radish. When it is slightly sour and tangy, it is ready.
  8. Once the pickled radish is fermented, store it in the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. It will keep for several months.

Tips and Variations

  • To add more flavor to the pickled radish, you can add other ingredients such as garlic, ginger, or sesame oil to the brine.
  • If you prefer a milder flavor, you can reduce the amount of Korean chili flakes or omit them altogether.
  • Fermented pickled radish is a versatile condiment that can be used as a side dish, a topping for noodles or rice, or as an ingredient in salads and sandwiches.
  • Experiment with different types of radishes, such as daikon or black radish, for different flavors and textures.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled radish is a colorful and crunchy side dish that is easy to make and packed with nutrients. With its tangy and slightly sweet flavor, it is a great addition to many meals. By following this recipe and tips, you can create your own fermented pickled radish at home and enjoy its health benefits and delicious flavor.
