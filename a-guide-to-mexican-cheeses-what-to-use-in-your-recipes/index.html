A Guide to Mexican Cheeses: What to Use in Your Recipes


Mexican cuisine is world-renowned for its bold flavors and diverse use of ingredients. One of the key components that elevate Mexican dishes to a whole new level is its rich and varied array of cheeses. From creamy and mild to strong and tangy, Mexican cheeses offer endless possibilities for both traditional and modern recipes. In this comprehensive guide, we'll take you on a journey through the most popular Mexican cheeses, their unique characteristics, and how to incorporate them into your cooking. Queso Fresco This crumbly and fresh cheese is a staple in Mexican cuisine, made from cow's milk, goats milk, or a combination of both. Queso fresco has a bright, tangy flavor and a crumbly texture that works beautifully in both sweet and savory dishes. Use it in recipes like:

Grilled corn with queso fresco, chili powder, and lime juice for a light and refreshing side dish Stuffed peppers with queso fresco, ground beef, and tomatoes for a flavorful twist on a classic dish Queso fresco and chorizo-stuffed mushrooms for a burst of flavor in a vegetarian appetizer

Oaxaca Cheese Also known as "quesillo," Oaxaca cheese has a melting quality that makes it ideal for dishes like moles, soups, and casseroles. This semi-soft cheese is made from cow's milk and has a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Try:

Quesadillas filled with shredded Oaxaca cheese, chorizo, and served with a spicy salsa for a delicious breakfast dish Enchiladas rojas with Oaxaca cheese, shredded chicken, and topped with a rich tomato sauce Stuffed poblano peppers with Oaxaca cheese, ground beef, and served with a side of Mexican rice

Asadero Cheese Another semi-soft cheese from Mexico, Asadero has a mild, slightly sweet flavor and a smooth texture. Its high melting point makes it perfect for dishes like tacos, quesadillas, and grilled cheese sandwiches. Use it in recipes like:

Asadero cheese quesadillas with grilled chicken, served with a side of sour cream and salsa Tacos al pastor with Asadero cheese, marinated pork, and served with a side of pineapple and onion Grilled cheese sandwiches with Asadero cheese, sliced ham, and served with a side of tomato soup

Sobrante Cheese Sobrante, also known as "cow's milk queso," is a type of blue cheese made from cow's milk. Its strong, pungent flavor pairs perfectly with fruits, nuts, and sweet dishes like apple and pear tapenades. Try:

Manchego-Sobrante salad with mixed greens, apple slices, and a tangy vinaigrette Sobrante-stuffed figs with prosciutto and arugula for a creative appetizer Queso Sobrante con membrillo (quince paste) for a sweet and savory dessert

Other Popular Mexican Cheeses While the above cheeses are some of the most popular, there are many other types to explore, including:

Menonita Cheese: a mild and smooth cheese, perfect for snacking or using in sauces. Requesón Cheese: a tangy and crumbly cheese, great for salads and desserts. Queso Cotija: a dry and crumbly cheese, perfect for sprinkling on soups, salads, and tacos.

Tips and Tricks for Working with Mexican Cheeses

When melting Mexican cheeses, use a moderate heat to avoid separating the curds and transferring the cheese to a lower heat or using a double boiler. For best results, use fresh and high-quality Mexican cheeses, as they'll yield the most vibrant and interesting flavors. Experiment with different combinations of Mexican cheeses to create new and exciting flavor profiles. Consider using Mexican cheeses in other dishes beyond traditional recipes – their unique flavors will elevate even the simplest of meals.

Conclusion Mexican cheeses are a pantry staple that every cook should have in their repertoire. From creamy and mild to strong and tangy, these cheeses offer endless possibilities for both traditional and modern recipes. With this comprehensive guide, we hope to have introduced you to the rich and varied world of Mexican cheese, inspiring you to try new flavors and experiment with different cooking techniques.
