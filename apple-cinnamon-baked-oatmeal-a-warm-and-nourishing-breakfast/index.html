Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal: A Warm and Nourishing Breakfast


There’s something incredibly comforting about a warm breakfast, especially when it’s both nutritious and delicious. Enter Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal — the perfect dish to start your day on the right foot. This hearty breakfast combines the wholesome goodness of oats with the sweet, tart flavor of apples and the warming spice of cinnamon. It’s easy to prepare, can be made ahead of time, and offers a delightful way to enjoy the flavors of fall year-round.

Why Choose Baked Oatmeal?

Baked oatmeal stands out as a breakfast favorite for several reasons:

  1. Simplicity: It’s straightforward to make, using ingredients that you likely already have in your pantry.
  2. Versatility: You can customize it with different fruits, nuts, and spices to suit your taste.
  3. Convenience: Make it ahead, store in the fridge, and reheat portions throughout the week for a quick, nourishing breakfast.
  4. Health Benefits: Packed with fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, baked oatmeal supports digestion and provides sustained energy.

Ingredients

To make a delicious Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup or honey
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 medium apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)
  • Additional toppings: fresh apple slices, a drizzle of maple syrup, or a dollop of Greek yogurt

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease a 9x9-inch baking dish with cooking spray or a small amount of coconut oil.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, baking powder, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and salt. Stir well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed throughout the oats.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In another bowl, whisk together the milk, maple syrup (or honey), egg, melted coconut oil (or butter), and vanilla extract. Make sure the coconut oil or butter is slightly cooled before mixing to prevent scrambling the egg.

Step 4: Assemble the Oatmeal

Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients and stir until everything is well combined. Gently fold in the diced apples and chopped nuts, if using. The mixture should be well-coated and slightly thickened.

Step 5: Bake

Transfer the oatmeal mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly. For an extra touch, arrange a few thin apple slices on top for a decorative finish. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set. A toothpick inserted into the middle should come out clean.

Step 6: Serve

Allow the baked oatmeal to cool for a few minutes before serving. Cut into squares or scoop out individual portions. Top with additional fresh apple slices, a drizzle of maple syrup, or a dollop of Greek yogurt if desired.

Tips for the Best Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal

  1. Oats Matter: Use old-fashioned rolled oats for the best texture. Quick oats can become mushy, while steel-cut oats will require a longer cooking time and more liquid.
  2. Milk Options: Feel free to use your milk of choice. Almond milk, oat milk, or any plant-based milk works just as well as dairy milk in this recipe.
  3. Sweetness Adjustments: Adjust the sweetness to your preference by adding more or less maple syrup or honey. You can also try brown sugar or agave nectar.
  4. Apple Varieties: Choose apples that hold their shape well during baking, such as Honeycrisp, Granny Smith, or Fuji apples. These varieties add a nice balance of sweetness and tartness.
  5. Add-ins and Variations: Customize your baked oatmeal with different add-ins like raisins, dried cranberries, or shredded coconut. Experiment with spices by adding a pinch of ground ginger or cloves for an extra flavor boost.

Health Benefits of Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal

Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal is not only a comforting breakfast but also a nutritious one. Here’s how this dish benefits your health:

  1. Oats: Rich in fiber, particularly beta-glucan, oats help lower cholesterol levels, improve blood sugar control, and promote healthy gut bacteria.
  2. Apples: High in dietary fiber and vitamin C, apples support digestive health and boost the immune system.
  3. Cinnamon: Known for its anti-inflammatory properties, cinnamon helps regulate blood sugar levels and improve heart health.
  4. Nuts: If you include nuts, they add healthy fats, protein, and additional fiber, making this breakfast even more satisfying and nutrient-dense.
  5. Natural Sweeteners: Using maple syrup or honey provides a natural source of sweetness without the need for refined sugars.

Making Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal Ahead of Time

One of the best features of baked oatmeal is its suitability for meal prep. Here’s how to make it ahead of time:

  1. Prepare and Store: After baking, let the oatmeal cool completely. Cut into portions and store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to five days.
  2. Reheat: Reheat individual portions in the microwave for 1-2 minutes or in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10 minutes. Add a splash of milk before reheating to keep it moist.
  3. Freezing: Baked oatmeal also freezes well. Wrap individual portions in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag. Freeze for up to three months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Serving Suggestions

Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal is versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways:

  • Classic Style: Serve warm with a drizzle of maple syrup and a sprinkle of extra cinnamon.
  • On-the-Go: Cut into bars for a convenient on-the-go breakfast or snack.
  • With Toppings: Add a dollop of Greek yogurt, a handful of fresh berries, or a spoonful of nut butter for extra protein and flavor.
  • As Dessert: Top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a healthy dessert option.

Conclusion

Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal is a wonderfully cozy, healthy, and versatile breakfast option that can be enjoyed by the whole family. Its combination of wholesome oats, fresh apples, and warming cinnamon makes it a perfect dish to start your day. Whether you’re looking for a make-ahead breakfast, a nutritious meal prep option, or simply a delicious way to enjoy the flavors of fall, this baked oatmeal recipe has you covered.

Try making Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal part of your breakfast routine and enjoy the benefits of a warm, nourishing meal any day of the week.
