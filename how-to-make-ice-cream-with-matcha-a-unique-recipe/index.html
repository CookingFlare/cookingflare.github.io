How to Make Ice Cream with Matcha: A Unique Recipe


Matcha ice cream, with its vibrant green color and distinct flavor, has become a favorite treat for many. This Japanese-inspired dessert blends the smooth, creamy texture of traditional ice cream with the earthy, slightly bitter taste of matcha, creating a flavor profile that is both refreshing and indulgent. Whether you are a matcha enthusiast or new to the world of green tea-based desserts, this homemade matcha ice cream recipe is easy to follow and sure to impress.

In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making matcha ice cream from scratch, explaining everything from selecting the right ingredients to the steps needed to create the perfect batch of creamy matcha goodness.

Ingredients for Matcha Ice Cream

To make homemade matcha ice cream, you will need a few basic ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups heavy cream – The rich texture of heavy cream is essential to create that luxurious, smooth consistency that you expect from a good ice cream.
  • 1 cup whole milk – The milk helps balance the creaminess of the ice cream base while adding a lighter texture.
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar – This sweetener balances the slightly bitter flavor of matcha.
  • 4 large egg yolks – These add richness and help create the smooth, custardy texture that is typical of homemade ice cream.
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder – The key ingredient that gives this ice cream its signature flavor and color. Make sure to use high-quality ceremonial grade matcha for the best taste.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract – While matcha is the star of the show, a touch of vanilla enhances the overall flavor profile.
  • Pinch of salt – A small pinch of salt helps to round out the sweetness of the ice cream.

Optional:

  • 1 tablespoon of corn syrup – This can be added to help prevent ice crystals from forming in your ice cream, ensuring a smoother consistency.
  • Matcha powder for garnish – If you want to give your finished ice cream a more vibrant green look, a little extra matcha powder sprinkled on top can add an extra touch of flair.

Equipment You Will Need:

  • Ice cream maker – While you can make matcha ice cream without an ice cream maker, using one will yield better results. It ensures a smoother texture and less crystallization.
  • Whisk and mixing bowls – For blending the ingredients together.
  • Heatproof spatula – To stir the custard as it cooks.
  • Fine mesh sieve – To strain the custard, ensuring no lumps of matcha or egg solids remain.
  • Storage containers – For storing the ice cream once it’s made.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Matcha Ice Cream

Making homemade matcha ice cream involves a few simple steps, including preparing the matcha custard, cooling it, and churning it into creamy ice cream. Follow these steps to create a perfect batch of matcha ice cream every time.

1. Infuse the Matcha in Warm Milk

The first step in making matcha ice cream is infusing the matcha powder into warm milk. This process helps to release the delicate flavors of the matcha and prevent it from clumping.

  • Start by heating the milk in a small saucepan over medium heat. You want the milk to be warm, not boiling.
  • Once the milk is warm, add the matcha powder and whisk vigorously to dissolve the matcha into the milk. If there are any clumps, you can strain the milk through a fine-mesh sieve to ensure a smooth texture. Set this matcha-infused milk aside.

2. Prepare the Egg Custard Base

Next, you’ll need to prepare the custard base for your ice cream. This will give the ice cream its rich, creamy texture.

  • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the sugar until the mixture becomes pale and slightly thickened. This process, known as tempering, ensures the eggs don’t scramble when heated.
  • Gradually add the warm matcha-infused milk to the egg-sugar mixture while continuously whisking. This step is important because it gently heats the eggs, preventing them from cooking too quickly and forming lumps.

3. Cook the Custard

Transfer the egg and milk mixture back to the saucepan and cook over medium heat. Stir the custard constantly with a heatproof spatula, scraping the bottom to ensure it doesn’t stick. The custard is ready when it thickens enough to coat the back of the spatula.

  • Tip: To check if the custard has reached the right consistency, dip a spoon into the custard, then run your finger through the back of the spoon. If the line stays clear and doesn’t run, the custard is done.

4. Strain and Cool the Custard

Once the custard has thickened, remove it from the heat. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any bits of cooked egg or matcha clumps. Let the custard cool at room temperature for a few minutes.

  • Once the custard has cooled slightly, cover it with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the custard for at least 3-4 hours or overnight for best results. The custard must be completely chilled before churning.

5. Churn the Custard into Ice Cream

Once your custard has cooled, it’s time to churn it into ice cream.

  • Pour the chilled custard into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Typically, this will take about 20-25 minutes, depending on the model of your ice cream maker.
  • As the mixture churns, it will gradually thicken and take on the consistency of soft serve ice cream.

6. Freeze the Ice Cream

Once the ice cream has reached the desired consistency, transfer it into an airtight container. Use a spatula to smooth the top, and cover it with a piece of plastic wrap or parchment paper to prevent ice crystals from forming. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until it is firm enough to scoop.

  • Tip: If you want to add extra texture, such as pieces of chocolate or nuts, you can fold them into the ice cream after churning, just before freezing.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Once your matcha ice cream has fully frozen, it’s ready to serve! Scoop it into bowls or cones, and enjoy the delicate flavor and creamy texture of homemade matcha ice cream. For an added touch, dust with a little matcha powder on top.

Tips for Making the Best Matcha Ice Cream

Making matcha ice cream is relatively straightforward, but here are a few tips to ensure the best results:

1. Use High-Quality Matcha

The quality of the matcha you use will directly affect the flavor of your ice cream. Look for high-quality, ceremonial-grade matcha, as it has a smoother, more delicate flavor compared to lower-grade matcha. The best matcha should have a vibrant green color and a slightly sweet aroma.

2. Balance the Sweetness

Matcha has a slightly bitter taste, so balancing the sweetness in your ice cream is important. If you prefer a sweeter ice cream, feel free to adjust the sugar to your liking. However, be careful not to overpower the delicate matcha flavor.

3. Don’t Skip the Straining Step

Straining the custard is crucial to ensure a smooth, lump-free texture. Matcha powder can sometimes form clumps, and you don’t want these in your finished ice cream.

4. Let the Ice Cream Ripen

Homemade ice cream often has a softer texture than store-bought varieties. To achieve the perfect scoop, allow your ice cream to ripen in the freezer for several hours or overnight before serving.

Variations of Matcha Ice Cream

If you want to experiment with different flavors, here are a few variations of matcha ice cream that you can try:

1. Matcha and Red Bean Ice Cream

For a traditional Japanese twist, try adding sweetened red bean paste to your matcha ice cream. Red bean pairs beautifully with matcha, adding a subtle sweetness and earthy flavor that complements the green tea.

2. Matcha and Coconut Ice Cream

If you prefer a dairy-free version of matcha ice cream, substitute the heavy cream and milk with coconut milk and coconut cream. This variation gives the ice cream a tropical flavor that blends perfectly with the earthy matcha.

3. Matcha Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

For chocolate lovers, add dark chocolate chips to your matcha ice cream for a delicious contrast of flavors. The bitterness of the matcha pairs beautifully with the richness of the chocolate.

4. Matcha and Strawberry Swirl Ice Cream

For a fruity twist, swirl in some fresh strawberry puree after churning the matcha ice cream. The sweet-tart strawberry flavor contrasts wonderfully with the creamy matcha, creating a vibrant and refreshing dessert.

Final Thoughts

Making matcha ice cream from scratch is a fun and rewarding experience. The process may take a little time, but the result is a creamy, refreshing, and unique dessert that is sure to impress friends and family alike. Whether you enjoy it on its own, paired with other flavors, or as part of a dessert spread, matcha ice cream is a treat that will delight your taste buds. Try this recipe next time you’re craving something indulgent, and enjoy the delicious results of your hard work!
