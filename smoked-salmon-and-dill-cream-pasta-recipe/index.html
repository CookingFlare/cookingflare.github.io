Smoked Salmon and Dill Cream Pasta Recipe


Pasta dishes have an uncanny ability to weave themselves into our weekly meal rotations, offering versatility, ease, and comforting familiarity. Yet, there’s one particular recipe that stands out—not merely for its flavor but for its elegance: Smoked Salmon and Dill Cream Pasta. This dish marries the rich silkiness of cream with the aromatic freshness of dill, all complemented by the savory depth of smoked salmon. Perfect for a quiet weeknight dinner or an impressive crowd-pleaser, this recipe strikes just the right note of indulgence.

Why Smoked Salmon and Dill?

Smoked salmon is prized for its velvety texture and smoky profile, adding a layer of complexity to any dish it graces. Pairing it with dill—a fragrant herb that lends a hint of citrus—creates a harmonious balance between boldness and brightness. Dill’s subtle sweetness also rounds out the brininess of smoked salmon, enhancing the overall experience of the plate. Combined with the creamy base, we’ll create a dish that’s both comforting and refined.

Choosing Your Ingredients

To ensure the best outcome for this recipe, it’s important to understand what makes each ingredient tick. Here’s what to look for:

  1. Smoked Salmon: Opt for high-quality smoked salmon with just the right amount of salt. Pre-packaged versions work well, but you can also visit the fish counter to source fresh, hand-sliced smoked salmon for enhanced flavor. Avoid overly oily varieties, as they may overwhelm the cream sauce.

  2. Pasta: Fettuccine, linguine, or tagliatelle are excellent choices for this dish, as their long, flat shapes help scoop up the creamy sauce. However, for a unique twist, penne or rigatoni can also work. Make sure to cook the pasta just shy of al dente—it will finish cooking when mixed with the sauce.

  3. Fresh Dill: Fresh dill is non-negotiable for this recipe. It boasts a distinct flavor that dried dill simply can’t replicate. Chop it finely, ensuring uniform distribution throughout the sauce.

  4. Cream Base: Heavy cream will give you an ultra-luxurious sauce. You can also use half-and-half for a lighter version, but keep in mind the trade-off in richness.

  5. Supporting Cast: Garlic, shallots, lemon, butter, and white wine amplify the core ingredients while adding depth and brightness to both the sauce and the salmon.

Cooking Techniques

Cooking this dish is simple yet slightly nuanced, hinging on timing and layering flavors. Below, we’ll break it down step by step.

Ingredients

For the pasta:

  • 12 oz pasta (fettuccine, linguine, or tagliatelle)
  • A pinch of salt for boiling water

For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 medium shallot, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (optional, for depth)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or half-and-half)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice from 1/2 a lemon
  • 6 oz smoked salmon, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional garnish: grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook your pasta according to package instructions, stopping just shy of al dente. Reserve one cup of pasta water before draining. Set the pasta aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Aromatics

While the pasta cooks, start building the base of your sauce. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shallots and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and translucent—about 2–3 minutes.

Step 3: Deglaze and Introduce Acidity

If using white wine, pour it into the skillet to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits. Let it simmer for about 1–2 minutes until slightly reduced. Then, add the lemon zest and juice. The lemon will brighten the sauce and add a touch of acidity that harmonizes beautifully with the smoked salmon.

Step 4: Incorporate the Cream

Lower the heat to medium-low and slowly stir in the heavy cream. Allow the cream to warm through, thickening slightly as it simmers. Be sure not to let it boil, as this could cause the cream to curdle.

Step 5: Add the Smoked Salmon and Dill

Gently fold in the smoked salmon pieces, cooking just long enough for them to heat through—about 1–2 minutes. Finally, sprinkle the chopped dill into the sauce. Stir well to ensure the flavors meld together.

Step 6: Combine Pasta with Sauce

Transfer the drained pasta into the skillet, tossing to coat the noodles evenly with the cream sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen it. Season with salt and black pepper to taste, adjusting as needed.

Plating the Recipe

Place the pasta onto individual serving plates or bowls, twirling it into elegant nests for presentation. Garnish with a touch more dill for added color and freshness. If desired, sprinkle grated Parmesan on top for an extra layer of indulgence. Pair the dish with freshly baked bread or a crisp side salad to complete the meal.

Pro Tips for Success

  • Timing Is Key: Ensure that all your ingredients are prepped and ready before starting. This way, the sauce comes together seamlessly without overcooking any components.
  • Wine Pairings: A chilled glass of Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay complements the smoked salmon and cream perfectly.
  • Enhance Visual Appeal: A dash of paprika or fresh cracked pepper on top of the finished dish can elevate its presentation.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 days. To reheat, warm the pasta gently in a skillet over low heat, adding a splash of cream or milk to restore its creamy texture. Avoid microwaving, as it can dry out the salmon and sauce.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

What makes Smoked Salmon and Dill Cream Pasta undeniably special is its ability to feel luxurious while remaining incredibly simple to prepare. Every bite delivers a symphony of flavors—the smokiness of the salmon, the freshness of dill, and the richness of the cream sauce unite to create an unforgettable dish. Whether you’re a seasoned home chef or a beginner exploring new recipes, this exquisite pasta recipe deserves a place in your repertoire.

End your busy day or wow your dinner guests with this effortless yet gourmet preparation, and bask in the compliments that follow. Once you master it, you’ll be reaching for smoked salmon and dill at every opportunity.
