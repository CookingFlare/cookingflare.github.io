Authentic Middle Eastern Mansaf: A Dish Fit for Royalty


Mansaf, a traditional Middle Eastern dish, is a staple of Jordanian cuisine and a symbol of hospitality and generosity. This majestic dish is a culinary masterpiece that has been served to royalty and commoners alike for centuries. In this recipe, we will guide you through the preparation of an authentic Mansaf, fit for the most discerning palates.

The Story Behind Mansaf

Mansaf is a dish with a rich history and cultural significance. The name “Mansaf” is derived from the Arabic word for “crown,” which refers to the way the dish is served. A whole lamb is cooked in a special sauce made with yogurt, garlic, and spices, and then served with a mound of fluffy rice and a side of flatbread. The dish is typically served at special occasions, such as weddings and holidays, and is a symbol of the host’s generosity and hospitality.

Ingredients

For the lamb:

  • 1 whole lamb (about 10-12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups plain yogurt

For the rice:

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste

For the flatbread:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water

Preparing the Lamb

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). In a large bowl, mix together the olive oil, chopped onions, minced garlic, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, ground cardamom, salt, and black pepper. Add the lamb and rub the mixture all over the meat, making sure to coat it evenly.

Place the lamb in a large roasting pan and roast for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily. Remove the lamb from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

Preparing the Rice

Rinse the rice in a fine-mesh strainer and drain well. In a large saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it is translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.

Add the rice to the saucepan and stir to combine with the onion and garlic mixture. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the rice is lightly toasted.

Add the water to the saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.

Preparing the Flatbread

In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder. Add the olive oil and lukewarm water, and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Divide the dough into 4-6 equal pieces and shape each piece into a ball. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

Assembling the Mansaf

To assemble the Mansaf, place a mound of rice on a large serving platter or tray. Arrange the sliced lamb on top of the rice, leaving a small border around the edges.

Place a piece of flatbread on top of the lamb, and then spoon some of the yogurt sauce over the bread. Repeat this process, layering the lamb, rice, and flatbread, until you reach the top of the platter.

Yogurt Sauce

In a small bowl, mix together 2 cups of plain yogurt, 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 minced garlic clove, and 1 teaspoon of ground cumin. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Serving the Mansaf

Serve the Mansaf hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley and a side of chopped fresh mint. Traditionally, the Mansaf is served with a side of pickled vegetables, such as cucumbers and tomatoes.

Conclusion

Mansaf is a dish that is steeped in tradition and history, and is a true representation of Middle Eastern hospitality. With its tender lamb, fluffy rice, and crispy flatbread, this dish is sure to impress even the most discerning palates. Whether you are serving it at a special occasion or a family dinner, Mansaf is a dish that is sure to become a new favorite.

Note: This recipe serves 6-8 people. You can adjust the ingredients and preparation time to suit your needs.
