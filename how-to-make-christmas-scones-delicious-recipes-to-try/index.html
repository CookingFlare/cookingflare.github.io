How to Make Christmas Scones: Delicious Recipes to Try


When the holiday season approaches, there’s nothing more inviting than the aroma of freshly baked scones wafting through your home. Christmas scones are a delightful twist on the classic treat, perfect for festive gatherings, cozy moments by the fire, or as a thoughtful homemade gift. Combining the warm spices, sweet flavors, and iconic ingredients traditionally associated with Christmas, these scones are bound to be a favorite addition to your holiday table.

Below, you’ll find a comprehensive guide to crafting exquisite Christmas scones, from traditional recipes to more creative variations. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, follow this step-by-step recipe to make your holiday season unforgettable.

Classic Christmas Scones Recipe

Every great scone begins with a reliable base recipe, and this classic version combines subtle sweetness with traditional Christmas flavors like cinnamon, nutmeg, and dried fruits. Here’s how to make them.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups (350g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ⅓ cup (65g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter, cubed and chilled
  • ½ cup (70g) dried cranberries
  • ⅓ cup (50g) golden raisins or chopped dried apricots
  • ½ cup (120ml) buttermilk (plus more for brushing)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and sugar.
  3. Cut in the butter: Add the cold butter cubes into the flour mixture. Using your fingers or a pastry cutter, rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Incorporate the add-ins: Gently fold in the dried cranberries and golden raisins. These provide bursts of festive flavor and make each bite extra special.
  5. Prepare the wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  6. Bring the dough together: Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture. Mix gently with a spatula until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough to ensure your scones stay tender and flaky.
  7. Shape the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Pat it into a 1-inch thick circle or rectangle, then cut into 8 triangular wedges or use a round cutter for classic scone shapes.
  8. Bake: Transfer the scones to the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops with buttermilk for a golden finish. Bake for 15–18 minutes or until the tops are lightly golden and firm.
  9. Cool and serve: Allow the scones to cool on a wire rack before serving. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar for a snowy, festive touch.

Creative Christmas Scone Variations

Once you’ve mastered the classic recipe, it’s time to explore some fun and flavorful twists. From chocolate-infused options to savory surprises, these variations will keep your holiday baking fresh and exciting.

1. Chocolate Peppermint Scones

For those with a sweet tooth, chocolate peppermint scones are a decadent way to celebrate Christmas. Add 1/2 cup (85g) mini chocolate chips to the dough and replace the dried fruit with crushed peppermint candy. Drizzle with a simple chocolate glaze (melted chocolate mixed with a touch of cream) once they’ve cooled for an irresistible festive treat.

2. Gingerbread Scones

Capture the essence of Christmas with gingerbread-flavored scones. Add 1 teaspoon ground ginger, 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves, and 2 tablespoons molasses to the wet ingredients. For extra flair, top with a cream cheese glaze made of cream cheese, powdered sugar, and a splash of milk.

3. Orange Cranberry Scones

Citrusy flavors pair beautifully with Christmas, and this version is bright and refreshing. Swap out the golden raisins for an equal quantity of dried cranberries, and add zest from one orange to the dough. To elevate the presentation, prepare a simple orange glaze by mixing powdered sugar, orange juice, and a drop of orange zest.

4. Savory Cheese and Herb Scones

Not all Christmas treats have to be sweet! This savory variation is perfect as an appetizer or to accompany a warm bowl of soup. Omit the spices and dried fruit from the base recipe, and instead add 1 cup (120g) shredded sharp cheddar cheese and 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary or thyme. Brush the tops with egg wash and sprinkle with a pinch of flaky sea salt before baking.

Pro Tips for Perfect Christmas Scones

To ensure your scones are bakery-quality, follow these essential tips to elevate your baking game:

  1. Keep everything cold: Cold butter and chilled liquid ingredients create steam during baking, leading to light and flaky layers.
  2. Don’t overmix: Overworking the dough can make your scones tough. Mix until just combined to retain their tender texture.
  3. Use fresh leavening agents: Check your baking powder and baking soda for freshness, as they play a critical role in giving the scones their rise.
  4. Adjust for altitude: If baking at a high altitude, you may need to reduce the baking powder slightly to prevent overly domed scones.
  5. Experiment with toppings: From festive sprinkles to a dusting of spiced sugar, toppings can enhance the presentation of your Christmas scones.

Storage and Serving Suggestions

Storing Scones:

Freshly baked scones are best enjoyed the day they’re made, but they can be stored for later as well:

  • Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or place them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.
  • Alternatively, freeze unbaked scones for up to 3 months and bake directly from frozen, adding an extra 2–3 minutes to the baking time.

Serving Ideas:

  • Pair your scones with a warm cup of coffee, tea, or hot cocoa for a cozy winter morning.
  • Serve with clotted cream, whipped butter, or a selection of jams for an indulgent treat.
  • Use savory scones as a side dish for holiday dinners or brunches.

Why Christmas Scones Make the Perfect Holiday Treat

Scones embody the essence of the holiday season: they’re versatile, easy to make, and can be tailored to suit a variety of tastes. Whether you’re baking as a family tradition, preparing treats for a Christmas morning brunch, or crafting edible gifts for loved ones, scones are a heartwarming addition to your festivities. Their balance of simplicity and sophistication makes them a timeless choice.

Make your holiday season sweeter with these delightful Christmas scone recipes. With an array of flavors to choose from, every bite is a little taste of the magic that makes this time of year so special. So preheat your oven, gather your ingredients, and let the holiday baking adventures begin!
