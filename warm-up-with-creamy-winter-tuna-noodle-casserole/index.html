Warm Up with Creamy Winter Tuna Noodle Casserole


As the winter chill sets in, there’s nothing quite like a hearty and comforting casserole to warm you up from the inside out. This creamy winter tuna noodle casserole is the perfect dish to cozy up with on a cold evening. Packed with delicious flavors and simple ingredients, it’s a timeless classic that’s sure to please the whole family. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow and guaranteed to bring smiles to the dinner table.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz wide egg noodles
  • 2 cans (12 oz) tuna, drained
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. Cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, and mushrooms, and sauté until the vegetables are tender.

  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes to make a roux.

  5. Gradually whisk in the milk and chicken broth, stirring until the mixture is smooth and thickened.

  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and well combined.

  7. Gently fold in the cooked egg noodles, drained tuna, and frozen peas until everything is evenly coated with the creamy sauce.

  8. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste, then transfer it to the prepared baking dish.

  9. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs with a drizzle of melted butter and sprinkle the mixture over the top of the casserole.

  10. Bake the casserole in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and the filling is bubbly.

  11. Once done, remove the casserole from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

  12. Garnish with fresh parsley and enjoy the creamy, comforting goodness of this winter favorite!

Tips for Success

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables or using different types of cheese for a unique twist.
  • For an extra crunchy topping, consider using crushed potato chips or French-fried onions instead of breadcrumbs.
  • This casserole can be prepared in advance and refrigerated, then baked when ready to serve, making it a convenient option for busy days.

Conclusion

With its creamy texture, savory flavors, and satisfying warmth, this creamy winter tuna noodle casserole is a delightful way to embrace the season. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or simply craving a cozy homemade meal, this recipe is a winner. So, gather your ingredients, preheat the oven, and get ready to savor the delightful comfort of this timeless winter dish!
