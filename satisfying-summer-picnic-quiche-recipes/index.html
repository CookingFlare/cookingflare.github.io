Satisfying Summer Picnic Quiche Recipes


As the warm sun beckons us outdoors, there’s no better way to enjoy the season than by packing up a delightful picnic. One dish that captures the essence of summer picnics is the quiche. This versatile and satisfying dish comes in various flavors and can be served hot or cold, making it a perfect addition to any picnic basket. Whether you’re lounging at the park, enjoying beachside fun, or hosting a backyard gathering, these quiche recipes will be sure to impress.

What Makes Quiche Perfect for Picnics?

Quiche is incredibly adaptable, allowing you to customize it based on your favorite ingredients or what’s in season. Plus, it can be made ahead of time, which is a huge advantage for picnic planning. A well-made quiche can serve as a main course or a hearty side dish, and it’s easy to slice into portions for sharing. The flaky crust paired with a creamy filling is a delightful combination that pleases a crowd.

Essential Ingredients for Quiche

Before diving into the recipes, let’s explore the essential ingredients that make a great quiche:

  • Crust: You can use a store-bought pie crust for convenience, or whip up a homemade version with flour, butter, and a pinch of salt.
  • Eggs: The base of a quiche, eggs provide the structure and richness.
  • Dairy: Typically, heavy cream or milk is used to create a creamy texture. You can also experiment with different types of cheese for added flavor.
  • Fillings: This is where you can get creative! Vegetables, meats, and herbs can be added based on your preferences.

Classic Spinach and Feta Quiche

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Sauté Spinach: In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped spinach and sauté until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool.

  3. Prepare Filling: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir in the sautéed spinach and crumbled feta.

  4. Assemble Quiche: Pour the filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly.

  5. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the top is lightly golden. Let it cool slightly before slicing.

  6. Serve: This quiche can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, making it perfect for your picnic.

Tomato, Basil, and Mozzarella Quiche

Ingredients

  • 1 homemade or store-bought pie crust
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, half-and-half, salt, and pepper. Fold in the cherry tomatoes, basil, and mozzarella.

  3. Assemble Quiche: Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust.

  4. Bake: Bake for 30-35 minutes until the quiche is golden and fully set.

  5. Cool and Serve: Let it cool for a few minutes, then slice. This quiche is a refreshing option for summer picnics.

Smoked Salmon and Dill Quiche

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust
  • 6 oz smoked salmon, chopped
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Lemon zest from 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Mix Filling: In a bowl, beat the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Stir in the cream cheese until smooth, then add the dill, smoked salmon, and lemon zest.

  3. Assemble Quiche: Pour the mixture into the pie crust.

  4. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is puffed and golden.

  5. Cool and Enjoy: Allow it to cool slightly before slicing. This quiche brings a touch of elegance to your picnic spread.

Zucchini and Goat Cheese Quiche

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust
  • 2 cups zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Sauté Zucchini: In a skillet, sauté the zucchini until tender. Set aside to cool slightly.

  3. Prepare Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, thyme, salt, and pepper. Gently fold in the sautéed zucchini and goat cheese.

  4. Assemble Quiche: Pour the mixture into the pie crust.

  5. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes until set.

  6. Serve: This quiche is delicious warm or chilled, making it a versatile choice for your picnic.

Broccoli and Cheddar Quiche

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust
  • 2 cups broccoli florets, steamed
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Stir in the steamed broccoli and cheddar cheese.

  3. Assemble Quiche: Pour the broccoli mixture into the pie crust.

  4. Bake: Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the top is golden.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow it to cool slightly before slicing. This hearty quiche is sure to satisfy!

Tips for Perfect Picnic Quiche

  1. Make Ahead: Quiche can be made a day in advance, allowing flavors to meld beautifully. Just refrigerate and serve cold or at room temperature.

  2. Pack Smart: When transporting your quiche, slice it into portions and layer between parchment paper to keep slices intact.

  3. Variations: Feel free to mix and match ingredients based on your cravings. Incorporate seasonal vegetables, different cheeses, or even proteins like bacon or sausage.

Conclusion

Quiche is a wonderfully satisfying dish that embodies the spirit of summer picnics. With its flaky crust and endless filling possibilities, it’s a crowd-pleaser that you can customize to suit any palate. Whether you opt for the classic spinach and feta or try something unique like smoked salmon and dill, these quiche recipes will elevate your picnic experience. So, gather your friends and family, pack your picnic basket, and enjoy a delightful day outdoors with these satisfying summer quiches!
