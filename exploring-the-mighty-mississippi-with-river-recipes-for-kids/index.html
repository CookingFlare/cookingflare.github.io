Exploring the Mighty Mississippi with River Recipes for Kids


The Mighty Mississippi River stretches around 2,340 miles, from its source in Minnesota to its mouth in the Gulf of Mexico. This grand river has played a significant role in American history, providing a means of transportation, trade, and plenty of fresh water fish! In this blog post, we will explore the Mississippi River through the wonderful world of food and cooking. Let’s dive into some river-inspired recipes perfect for kids to make and enjoy!

1. Mississippi Mud Pie

Let’s start with a dessert that is as delicious as it is rich in Mississippi history. The Mississippi Mud Pie brings together the flavors of chocolate and vanilla in a delightful fusion.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 ounces unsweetened chocolate
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cold heavy cream
  • 1 cup whipped, sweetened cream
  • Mini chocolate chips or chopped candy bars for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350째F (175째C) and grease a 9-inch pie dish.
  2. In a saucepan, melt butter and unsweetened chocolate over low heat. Stir in cocoa powder and sugar.
  3. Beat eggs, one at a time, into the chocolate mixture. Stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Add the flour, mix until well combined, and pour the batter into the prepared pie dish.
  5. Bake for 20 minutes, then let it cool on a wire rack.
  6. Once cooled, whip heavy cream, add sweetened cream and spread it over the pie.
  7. Top with mini chocolate chips or chopped candy bars and chill in the refrigerator until served.

2. Crawfish Boil Spread

Nothing says “Mighty Mississippi” like the bountiful seafood that resides in its waters. Who doesn’t love a good old crawfish boil? Here’s a more kid-friendly version of the spread that you can serve with chips or veggies!

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound frozen crawfish tails
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup old bay seasoning
  • 4 boiled and peeled eggs, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped celery
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Cook frozen crawfish tails according to the package instructions, then drain and let them cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine mayonnaise and old bay seasoning.
  3. Add crawfish tails, chopped eggs, celery, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and lemon juice.
  4. Mix everything well and chill in the refrigerator for about an hour before serving.

3. Cornmeal Pancakes with River Blueberries

Take advantage of the sweet and tasty blueberries that grow wild along the Mississippi River by making some delicious and healthy cornmeal pancakes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups cornmeal
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Fresh river blueberries (fresh or frozen)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together cornmeal, flour, and baking powder.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients and stir in honey.
  4. Lightly grease a non-stick skillet or griddle and heat over medium heat.
  5. For each pancake, scoop 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet, followed by a handful of blueberries.
  6. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown.
  7. Serve hot with maple syrup or honey on the side.

4. Mississippi Mudpuppy Fritters

Last but not least, here is a fun recipe that sounds like a Mississippi swamp creature! These Mudpuppy Fritters are made using everyone’s favorite pantry staple - canned biscuits.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can buttermilk biscuits
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose baking mix
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped cooked crawfish or shrimp
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped green bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped onion
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200째F (90째C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cut each biscuit in half and place on a flat surface, pressing down lightly to flatten slightly.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together baking mix, cheese, crawfish tails, bell pepper, onion, egg, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the mixture onto the center of each biscuit half, then top with the remaining biscuit halves, pinching the edges to seal.
  5. Gently press down to flatten slightly, then place on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Once all fritters have been assembled, let them rest in the refrigerator for 10-15 minutes.
  7. After chilling, heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  8. Working in batches, carefully place a few fritters in the hot oil and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown.
  9. Transfer fried fritters to paper towels to drain excess oil, then keep warm in the oven while you fry the remaining batches.

Conclusion

There you have it, friends! Four delightful recipes that transcend the stories and marvels of the Mighty Mississippi River. Teach your kids about this historical river through these delicious, easy, and fun dishes! Who knows, they might just discover a newfound appreciation for not only these recipes but also for the great American River itself. Happy cooking and exploring with your young chefs!
