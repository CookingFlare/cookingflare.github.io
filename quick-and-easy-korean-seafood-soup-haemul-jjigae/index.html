Quick and Easy Korean Seafood Soup (Haemul Jjigae)


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, diverse ingredients, and comforting dishes that satisfy both the body and soul. One such dish that stands out is Haemul Jjigae, or Korean Seafood Soup. This dish is a staple in many Korean households, beloved for its rich broth, fresh seafood, and hearty ingredients. Whether you’re looking for a dish to warm you up on a cold day or seeking something flavorful to pair with steamed rice, Haemul Jjigae is the perfect solution.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make Haemul Jjigae from scratch using fresh ingredients and simple techniques. We’ll also discuss some variations you can try and tips for getting the most out of this delicious dish. So, grab your apron and let’s dive into this quick and easy recipe for Korean Seafood Soup!

What is Haemul Jjigae?

Haemul Jjigae, or Korean seafood stew, is a popular Korean dish that combines a variety of seafood such as shrimp, clams, mussels, and fish with tofu, vegetables, and a spicy, savory broth. The soup is typically served with a bowl of steamed rice, making it a satisfying and complete meal. It’s a dish that is both comforting and full of flavor, thanks to the combination of fresh seafood and the bold seasoning typical of Korean cuisine.

In Korea, Haemul Jjigae is often enjoyed as a family meal or served as a side dish with alcoholic beverages like soju. It’s a dish that’s versatile enough to suit a range of palates, from those who prefer their food spicy to those who like a more mild taste.

The best part? You don’t have to wait for a special occasion to enjoy this dish. With the right ingredients and a bit of time, you can create an incredible seafood stew right in your own kitchen.

Key Ingredients for Haemul Jjigae

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients that make Haemul Jjigae so special. While some ingredients are commonly found in most Korean kitchens, others might require a trip to your local Asian grocery store. Here’s what you’ll need:

Fresh Seafood

  • Shrimp: Large, peeled shrimp are ideal for this dish. They provide a sweet and succulent taste to the broth.
  • Clams or Mussels: Shellfish adds depth to the soup with its natural briny flavor.
  • White Fish Fillets: Firm white fish such as cod or halibut works best, as they won’t break apart easily during cooking.
  • Squid (optional): For an extra layer of texture, you can add squid, which gives the soup a unique chewiness.

Vegetables and Tofu

  • Tofu: Soft tofu absorbs the flavors of the soup, creating a smooth contrast to the texture of the seafood.
  • Zucchini: Adds a subtle sweetness and crunch.
  • Onion: Sliced onions help form the base of the soup’s flavor profile.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic is a must for any Korean dish. It gives the broth an aromatic richness.
  • Chili Peppers: Depending on your spice preference, you can add fresh chili peppers to bring heat to the soup.
  • Green Onions: For garnish and extra flavor.

Broth Base

  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Pepper Flakes): The key ingredient for the signature heat and color in Korean stews.
  • Gochujang (Korean Red Pepper Paste): Adds a depth of umami and a touch of sweetness.
  • Fish Sauce: This adds the essential umami that complements the seafood.
  • Soy Sauce: For savory richness.
  • Anchovy Broth (or Seafood Broth): If you want to get a more authentic taste, making or using an anchovy-based broth will enhance the dish’s overall flavor.

Seasonings

  • Sesame Oil: For finishing the dish with a fragrant, nutty flavor.
  • Salt: To taste, for seasoning.
  • Black Pepper: Freshly cracked, for additional flavor.

These ingredients may seem like a lot, but many of them are essential for achieving the signature Korean flavors. Most of these items are readily available at Korean or Asian grocery stores, so don’t be intimidated by the list.

Step-by-Step Recipe for Haemul Jjigae

Now that we’ve covered the key ingredients, let’s get cooking! This recipe for Haemul Jjigae is quick and simple, perfect for busy weeknights or any time you want to enjoy a delicious, homemade Korean meal.

Ingredients:

  • 200 grams of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 100 grams of clams (or mussels), cleaned
  • 150 grams of white fish fillets (like cod or halibut), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 block of soft tofu, cut into cubes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 fresh red chili peppers, sliced (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 3-4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 4 cups anchovy broth (or seafood broth)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    • In a large pot, pour in the anchovy broth (or seafood broth). If you’re using homemade anchovy broth, ensure it’s rich and flavorful for the best taste.
    • Bring the broth to a simmer over medium heat.

  2. Sauté Aromatics:

    • In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat.
    • Add the sliced onion and minced garlic to the pan. Sauté until the onions become translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

  3. Season the Broth:

    • Add the sautéed onions and garlic to the simmering broth.
    • Stir in the gochugaru (red pepper flakes) and gochujang (red pepper paste). Mix well to incorporate the spices into the broth, which will give the soup its characteristic rich, red color and spicy flavor.

  4. Add Vegetables:

    • Next, add the zucchini and fresh chili peppers to the pot. Let the vegetables cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the zucchini to soften and release its flavor into the broth.

  5. Add the Seafood:

    • Gently add the shrimp, clams (or mussels), and white fish fillets to the pot. Let them cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the shrimp turns pink and the shellfish opens up.

  6. Add Tofu:

    • Once the seafood is cooked, gently add the tofu cubes. Let the tofu heat through in the broth, soaking up all the delicious flavors.

  7. Season:

    • Stir in the soy sauce and fish sauce for a savory umami boost. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with salt and black pepper to your liking.

  8. Simmer:

    • Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for another 5 minutes to ensure all the flavors meld together perfectly.

  9. Garnish and Serve:

    • Just before serving, stir in the chopped green onions. Drizzle a little more sesame oil on top for added richness and fragrance.
    • Serve the Haemul Jjigae hot with steamed rice on the side, and enjoy!

Tips and Variations for Haemul Jjigae

While the recipe above is a classic version of Haemul Jjigae, there are plenty of ways to make it your own. Here are a few variations and tips for customization:

1. Add More Vegetables:

If you love vegetables, feel free to add more to the soup. Mushrooms, such as shiitake or enoki, work wonderfully in this dish, adding umami and texture. You could also include Korean radish (mu), which is commonly used in Korean stews for a slightly sweet flavor.

2. Adjust Spice Levels:

The amount of heat in Haemul Jjigae is entirely up to you. If you like your soup extra spicy, add more gochugaru or extra chili peppers. For a milder version, reduce the amount of chili peppers and gochugaru, and omit the gochujang altogether.

3. Use Different Seafood:

Experiment with different types of seafood based on what’s available to you. Scallops, octopus, and squid are all excellent additions to Haemul Jjigae. Just make sure to adjust the cooking times accordingly to avoid overcooking delicate seafood like scallops.

4. Vegan Version:

For a vegan or vegetarian version, omit the seafood and use mushrooms, tofu, and vegetables as the base. You can also replace the fish sauce with soy sauce and miso paste to create a savory, plant-based broth.

Conclusion

Haemul Jjigae is a dish that combines the best of Korean flavors – savory, spicy, and comforting. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or preparing a meal for friends and family, this quick and easy seafood soup is sure to be a hit. It’s a versatile recipe that can be adapted to suit any dietary preferences or spice tolerance, making it a perfect addition to your culinary repertoire.

Next time you’re craving something warm and flavorful, give Haemul Jjigae a try!
