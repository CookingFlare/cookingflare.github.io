Spanish Gazpacho with Tomatoes and Bread Recipe


If you’re looking for a refreshing and flavorful dish to beat the summer heat, then look no further than the classic Spanish gazpacho. This chilled soup is a perfect way to make the most of ripe, juicy tomatoes and is a delightful combination of fresh vegetables, olive oil, and tangy vinegar. To add a unique twist to this traditional recipe, we’ll also incorporate bread to give the gazpacho a wonderful texture and depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, cored and roughly chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
  • 1 small red onion, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of stale bread, torn into pieces
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons of red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • Cold water, as needed

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the chopped tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and minced garlic. Toss the vegetables together to mix well.

  2. Soak the Bread: Place the torn bread pieces in a separate bowl and sprinkle with a little water to moisten. Let the bread soak for a few minutes to soften.

  3. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender or food processor, add the soaked bread and the vegetable mixture. Blend until smooth and well combined. You may need to do this in batches, depending on the size of your blender.

  4. Season the Gazpacho: While blending, gradually drizzle in the olive oil and red wine vinegar. Add the salt and pepper to taste. Continue blending until the gazpacho reaches a smooth consistency. If the gazpacho is too thick, you can thin it out with cold water until you reach your desired consistency.

  5. Chill and Serve: Transfer the gazpacho to a large pitcher or bowl, cover, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld and the soup to chill thoroughly.

  6. Serve the Gazpacho: Once chilled, give the gazpacho a good stir, then ladle it into individual serving bowls. Garnish with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of freshly chopped herbs, such as parsley or chives, for a burst of freshness.

Tips for Serving:

  • Customize Your Toppings: Gazpacho is incredibly versatile when it comes to toppings. Consider adding diced avocado, croutons, or even a dollop of Greek yogurt for an extra layer of flavor and texture.

  • Pair with a Main Course: While gazpacho is a delightful appetizer on its own, it also pairs wonderfully with grilled shrimp, fish tacos, or a simple charcuterie board for a complete summer meal.

  • Make it in Advance: Gazpacho can be made a day ahead and stored in the refrigerator, allowing the flavors to develop even further.

With its vibrant color and refreshing taste, this Spanish gazpacho with tomatoes and bread is a celebration of summer’s bounty. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply looking for a light and satisfying meal, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. Enjoy the simplicity and deliciousness of this classic dish as you savor the best of the summer season. Cheers to good food and good company!
