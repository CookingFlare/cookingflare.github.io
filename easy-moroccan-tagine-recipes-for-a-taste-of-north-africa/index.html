Easy Moroccan Tagine Recipes for a Taste of North Africa


If you’re looking to transport your taste buds to the exotic flavors of North Africa, then Moroccan tagine recipes are the perfect culinary adventure. These aromatic and flavorful dishes are not only delicious but also surprisingly easy to prepare. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, these easy Moroccan tagine recipes will delight your senses and impress your guests. Let’s dive into the world of Moroccan cuisine and discover the magic of tagine cooking!

Classic Chicken Tagine

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. chicken thighs, bone-in
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup green olives
  • 1 preserved lemon, quartered
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a tagine or large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, cumin, ginger, turmeric, and cinnamon.
  4. Place the seasoned chicken in the tagine and brown on all sides.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth and add the green olives and preserved lemon.
  6. Cover the tagine and simmer for 45-50 minutes, or until the chicken is tender.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.

This classic chicken tagine is a delightful blend of savory and tangy flavors, with the tender chicken infused with the essence of Moroccan spices.

Vegetable Tagine with Chickpeas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chickpeas, cooked
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 large eggplant, cubed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a tagine or large pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the carrots, zucchinis, and eggplant, and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Sprinkle the vegetables with cumin, coriander, paprika, and cinnamon, and stir to coat evenly.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth, and bring to a gentle simmer.
  6. Cover the tagine and let it simmer for 30-35 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

This vibrant and hearty vegetable tagine is a celebration of the bountiful produce of North Africa, with the chickpeas adding a satisfying protein punch.

Lamb and Apricot Tagine

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. lamb shoulder, cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup dried apricots
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a tagine or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the cubed lamb shoulder and brown on all sides.
  4. Season the lamb with salt, pepper, ginger, cinnamon, cumin, and paprika.
  5. Stir in the dried apricots and pour in the chicken broth.
  6. Cover the tagine and simmer for 1.5-2 hours, or until the lamb is tender.
  7. Garnish with chopped mint before serving.

This aromatic lamb and apricot tagine is a delightful marriage of tender lamb, sweet apricots, and warm Moroccan spices, creating a symphony of flavors on the palate.

Moroccan tagine recipes offer a delightful culinary journey, rich in history and flavor. These easy recipes are a testament to the vibrant and diverse cuisine of North Africa, and they are sure to elevate your home cooking to new heights. So, gather your ingredients, dust off your tagine, and embark on a flavorful adventure to the heart of Morocco!
