Vegetarian Picnic Recipes for a Light and Flavorful Meal


Are you tired of the same old sandwiches and snacks for your picnic? Here are some delicious vegetarian recipes that will delight your taste buds and keep you energized throughout your picnic adventure!

  1. Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

Quinoa is a nutritious and tasty alternative to traditional picnic fare like pasta and sandwiches. This salad is loaded with colorful, flavorful roasted vegetables and dressed with a tangy vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp tamari (or soy sauce)
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 zucchini
  • 1 yellow squash
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 yellow bell pepper
  • 1 onion
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 oz feta cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa and combine with water, salt, black pepper, and garlic powder in a saucepan.

  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cover. Cook for 15 minutes, then remove from heat and let sit for 5 minutes.

  3. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 375°F. Cut vegetables into bite-sized pieces and toss with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, honey, thyme, oregano, and tamari.

  4. Spread vegetables on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through.

  5. Mix roasted vegetables with quinoa, cherry tomatoes, and feta cheese (if using). Serve warm or at room temperature.

  6. Hummus and Veggie Wrap

Hummus and veggies make for a delicious and easy-to-eat wrap. This recipe uses a whole-grain lava bread as the wrap, which is both flavorful and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole-grain lava bread
  • 3 tbsp hummus
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 2 oz goat cheese (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp olive oil (for the pan)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat. Cut lava bread in half and toast in the skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until lightly browned.

  2. Spread hummus generously on the toasted lava bread.

  3. Layer baby spinach, shredded carrots, shredded cabbage, and chopped red onion on top of the hummus.

  4. If desired, crumble goat cheese on top of the veggies.

  5. Drizzle with olive oil and wrap up tightly. Enjoy your tasty and nutritious picnic wrap!

  6. Tomato, Basil, and Mozzarella Salad

This simple and classic Italian combination will transport you to a sunny, vine-covered terrace in the heart of Tuscany.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup fresh mozzarella balls
  • 1/2 cup freshly chopped basil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. If desired, slice cherry tomatoes in half and arrange on a platter.
  2. Scatter fresh mozzarella balls and freshly chopped basil on top of tomatoes.
  3. Drizzle with freshly squeezed lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Finish with a sprinkle of balsamic vinegar. Enjoy your refreshing and flavorful treat at the park or by the lake!

Remember, with these vegetarian picnic recipes, you don’t have to compromise on taste, nutrition, or convenience. Happy picnicking!
