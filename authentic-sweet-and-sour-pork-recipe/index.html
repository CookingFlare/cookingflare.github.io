Authentic Sweet and Sour Pork Recipe


Sweet and sour pork is one of the most beloved dishes in Chinese cuisine. Known for its tangy, slightly sweet flavor and tender meat, this dish has earned a permanent spot on the menus of Chinese restaurants around the world. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a home cook looking to recreate an authentic restaurant-style dish, mastering the sweet and sour pork recipe is a rewarding experience.

In this post, we’ll walk you through the traditional methods of preparing sweet and sour pork, offering tips for achieving the perfect balance of flavors and textures. By the end of this guide, you’ll have everything you need to serve up this mouthwatering dish right in your own kitchen.

What is Sweet and Sour Pork?

Sweet and sour pork, or “gu lou yuk” in Cantonese, is a classic Chinese dish made from crispy pieces of pork, typically deep-fried, and tossed in a vibrant sauce made from vinegar, sugar, and ketchup. This sauce strikes a delicate balance between sweetness and acidity, creating a flavor profile that has universal appeal.

Although it originated in Cantonese cuisine, sweet and sour pork has become a staple in Chinese-American restaurants and is a favorite among many diners. While the ingredients can vary slightly depending on the region, the essence of this dish lies in the contrast between the crunchy, golden-brown pork and the tangy sauce.

Ingredients for Authentic Sweet and Sour Pork

To make an authentic sweet and sour pork recipe, you’ll need to source a few essential ingredients. Don’t be discouraged if some of them are unfamiliar — most can be found at your local Asian grocery store or online.

For the Pork:

  • Pork tenderloin or pork belly (about 1 pound): These cuts of meat are tender and juicy, perfect for this dish. Pork tenderloin is the most commonly used cut because of its mild flavor and lean texture.
  • Cornstarch (for coating): This helps give the pork its crispy, golden exterior when deep-fried.
  • Egg: Used in the batter to help bind the cornstarch and create a light, crispy coating.
  • Salt and pepper (to season): Just a pinch will suffice.

For the Sweet and Sour Sauce:

  • Ketchup (1/2 cup): This gives the sauce its signature red color and a mild sweetness.
  • Rice vinegar (1/4 cup): This adds the necessary acidity and balances the sweetness.
  • Sugar (1/4 cup): Granulated sugar is used to sweeten the sauce. You can adjust the sweetness based on your preference.
  • Pineapple juice (1/4 cup): Pineapple juice adds an extra layer of sweetness and a tropical touch.
  • Soy sauce (1 tablespoon): Adds a savory, umami flavor to the sauce.
  • Bell peppers (1 red and 1 green, chopped into chunks): These vegetables add texture, color, and a slight bitterness that pairs well with the sweet and sour sauce.
  • Onion (1 medium, cut into chunks): The onion brings a mild sweetness when cooked, complementing the sauce beautifully.
  • Pineapple chunks (1/2 cup, fresh or canned): The natural sweetness of the pineapple works in harmony with the sauce and gives the dish a refreshing twist.

For the Frying:

  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying): A neutral oil like vegetable or canola oil works best for frying.
  • Sesame oil (optional, for flavor): Just a few drops of sesame oil add a fragrant, nutty flavor to the dish, enhancing the overall profile.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Prepare Sweet and Sour Pork

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s begin the cooking process. Follow these simple steps to prepare an authentic sweet and sour pork dish.

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Start by cutting the pork tenderloin into bite-sized chunks, ensuring that each piece is roughly the same size. This will help them cook evenly. Season the pork with a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance its natural flavor.

Step 2: Make the Batter

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and the egg to form a smooth batter. The consistency should be thick enough to coat the pork but not too heavy. If the batter seems too thick, add a little bit of water to adjust it. The batter should be sticky but not runny, as it needs to adhere well to the pork.

Step 3: Deep-Fry the Pork

Heat the vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. You’ll want enough oil to fully submerge the pork pieces for even frying.

Once the oil is hot (about 350°F or 175°C), dip the seasoned pork pieces into the batter, ensuring they’re evenly coated. Carefully place them in the hot oil, frying in batches if necessary. Fry the pork until golden brown and crispy, which should take about 3-4 minutes per batch.

Once the pork is crispy and cooked through, remove it from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Set the fried pork aside.

Step 4: Make the Sweet and Sour Sauce

While the pork is frying, it’s time to prepare the sauce. In a small saucepan, combine the ketchup, rice vinegar, sugar, pineapple juice, and soy sauce. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved and the sauce begins to bubble.

Add the bell peppers, onions, and pineapple chunks to the saucepan, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables start to soften. The sauce should thicken slightly during this process.

If you’d like a thicker sauce, you can dissolve a teaspoon of cornstarch in a tablespoon of cold water and stir this slurry into the sauce. Allow the sauce to simmer for another minute until it reaches your desired consistency.

Step 5: Combine the Pork and Sauce

Once the sauce has thickened and the vegetables are tender, gently toss the fried pork into the sauce. Make sure each piece of pork is coated with the glossy, vibrant sauce. Stir-fry for another minute or two to allow the pork to absorb some of the flavors from the sauce.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Transfer the sweet and sour pork to a serving dish, garnishing it with sesame oil (if using) for added flavor. Serve the dish hot, ideally with steamed rice or noodles to soak up the delicious sauce. The contrast of crispy pork, tangy sauce, and fresh vegetables will surely make this a crowd-pleaser.

Tips for Perfect Sweet and Sour Pork

  • Choose the right cut of pork: For the best texture, opt for pork tenderloin or pork belly. These cuts are tender, juicy, and cook well when fried.
  • Adjust the sweetness and acidity: If you prefer a sweeter sauce, increase the amount of sugar or pineapple juice. For more acidity, add a bit more vinegar. Tailor the sauce to your taste!
  • Crispiness is key: To achieve the perfect crispy texture, ensure the oil is at the right temperature before frying. If the oil is too cold, the pork will absorb excess oil and become greasy. If it’s too hot, the pork will burn before it cooks through.
  • Use fresh vegetables: Fresh bell peppers, onions, and pineapple bring vibrant color and texture to the dish. Canned pineapple works too, but fresh pineapple gives a fresher, juicier bite.
  • Control the heat: If you’re cooking with a wok, make sure to control the heat while stir-frying. High heat helps create the signature crispy pork and caramelizes the sauce without burning the ingredients.

Variations of Sweet and Sour Pork

While the classic version of sweet and sour pork is already a hit, there are a few variations you can try depending on your preferences.

  • Vegetable variations: Feel free to add other vegetables like carrots, zucchini, or even mushrooms to the dish for additional flavor and texture.
  • Pineapple alternatives: If you’re not a fan of pineapple, try using other fruits like mango or apple slices. These fruits still provide the sweetness and acidity needed for the sauce.
  • Spicy twist: For those who enjoy heat, add a few slices of fresh chili or chili paste to the sauce. The spicy kick will balance beautifully with the sweet and sour flavors.

Conclusion

Authentic sweet and sour pork is a delightful dish that brings together a perfect blend of crispy, savory pork and a tangy, slightly sweet sauce. It’s a dish that never fails to impress, whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or enjoying a weeknight meal with family. With a few simple ingredients and these straightforward steps, you can recreate this classic Chinese dish in your own kitchen. So, grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy one of the most popular and satisfying dishes in Chinese cuisine.

Happy cooking!
