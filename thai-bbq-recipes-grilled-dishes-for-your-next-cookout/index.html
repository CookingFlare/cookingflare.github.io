Thai BBQ Recipes: Grilled Dishes for Your Next Cookout


When it comes to outdoor cooking, few cuisines can rival the vibrant flavors and aromatic spices of Thai BBQ. The combination of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements creates a symphony of taste that can elevate any cookout. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or a beachside gathering, Thai BBQ recipes are sure to impress your guests and tantalize their taste buds. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of Thai BBQ dishes, from marinated meats to grilled vegetables, and provide you with tips to make your next cookout unforgettable.

The Essence of Thai BBQ

Thai BBQ is not just about grilling; it’s about infusing ingredients with bold flavors. The key components of Thai cuisine—herbs, spices, and sauces—play a crucial role in creating the unique taste profile that defines Thai BBQ. Common ingredients include lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, fish sauce, and chili paste. These elements come together to create marinades and dipping sauces that enhance the natural flavors of grilled meats and vegetables.

Essential Ingredients for Thai BBQ

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a look at some essential ingredients that you’ll need for your Thai BBQ:

  • Fish Sauce: A staple in Thai cooking, fish sauce adds umami and depth to marinades and dipping sauces.
  • Coconut Sugar: This natural sweetener balances the savory and spicy elements in dishes.
  • Lemongrass: Adds a citrusy aroma and flavor, perfect for marinades.
  • Kaffir Lime Leaves: These leaves impart a unique fragrance and flavor to dishes.
  • Chili Paste: For those who love heat, chili paste is a must-have for marinades and sauces.
  • Fresh Herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, and mint are commonly used to garnish dishes and add freshness.

Thai BBQ Chicken Skewers (Gai Yang)

Ingredients

  • 1 pound chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons coconut sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemongrass, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine fish sauce, soy sauce, coconut sugar, vegetable oil, minced garlic, chopped lemongrass, and black pepper. Mix well until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces and add them to the marinade. Toss to coat the chicken evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.

  3. Skewer the Chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the soaked bamboo skewers.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken skewers for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until fully cooked and slightly charred.

  5. Serve: Remove from the grill and serve with a side of sweet chili sauce or a homemade peanut sauce for dipping.

Grilled Thai Pork Satay

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions

  1. Make the Marinade: In a bowl, whisk together coconut milk, curry powder, fish sauce, brown sugar, soy sauce, and turmeric powder.

  2. Marinate the Pork: Add the sliced pork to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  3. Skewer the Pork: Thread the marinated pork slices onto the soaked bamboo skewers.

  4. Grill the Satay: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Grill the pork satay for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until cooked through and slightly caramelized.

  5. Serve: Serve with a side of cucumber salad and peanut sauce for a delightful combination.
