The Best Pie Recipes for Thanksgiving Dessert


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and, most importantly, food. As the aroma of roasted turkey fills the air, it’s the sweet, decadent pies that often steal the show at the dessert table. If you’re looking to impress your guests this year, here are some of the best pie recipes that will have everyone reaching for seconds. From classic pumpkin to a twist on pecan, these desserts are sure to become family favorites.

Classic Pumpkin Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (fresh or canned)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Prepare the Crust: Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie plate. Trim and crimp the edges. Place the pie crust in the refrigerator while you prepare the filling.

  3. Mix the Filling: In a large bowl, whisk together pumpkin puree, heavy cream, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, and all the spices until smooth.

  4. Pour and Bake: Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared crust. Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 40-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing. Serve with whipped cream for that perfect finish.

Tips for Perfection

  • For a richer flavor, use fresh pumpkin instead of canned.
  • Ensure your spices are fresh for the best flavor impact.

Decadent Pecan Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Crust: Fit the unbaked pie crust into a 9-inch pie plate.

  3. Make the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine corn syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Whisk until well blended. Stir in the pecan halves.

  4. Pour and Bake: Pour the pecan filling into the prepared crust. Bake for 60-70 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly jiggly in the center.

  5. Cool and Enjoy: Let the pie cool for at least two hours before serving. This allows the filling to firm up.

Secret Tip

  • For a unique twist, add a splash of bourbon to the filling for a deeper flavor.

Apple Pie with a Twist

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 6-8 cups of thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour. Let it sit for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Prepare the Crust: Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie plate. Fill with the apple mixture, dotting with small pieces of butter on top.

  4. Top the Pie: Roll out the second pie crust and either lay it on top or cut into strips for a lattice design. Brush the top with a beaten egg for a golden finish.

  5. Bake: Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 35-45 minutes.

  6. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool before serving, preferably with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Pro Tip

  • To prevent a soggy bottom, sprinkle a thin layer of flour or breadcrumbs on the bottom of the crust before adding the apples.

Sweet Potato Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 2 cups mashed sweet potatoes (about 2 medium sweet potatoes)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Crust: Fit the unbaked pie crust into a 9-inch pie plate.

  3. Mix the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine mashed sweet potatoes, sugar, heavy cream, eggs, vanilla extract, and spices. Blend until smooth.

  4. Pour and Bake: Pour the sweet potato mixture into the prepared crust. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until the filling is set.

  5. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool before slicing. It’s delightful with whipped cream or a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Culinary Insight

  • Sweet potatoes can be boiled or baked; baking enhances their flavor.

Chocolate Cream Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup heavy cream (for whipping)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 4 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped

Instructions

  1. Make the Chocolate Filling: In a saucepan, whisk together sugar, cocoa powder, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually whisk in milk. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until it thickens and bubbles. Remove from heat and stir in chopped chocolate and vanilla extract.

  2. Cool the Filling: Pour the chocolate mixture into the pre-baked pie crust and let it cool for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Prepare Whipped Cream: In a bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. Add sugar and whip until stiff peaks form.

  4. Top and Serve: Spread the whipped cream over the cooled chocolate filling. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Delightful Addition

  • For added texture, sprinkle chocolate shavings on top of the whipped cream.

Tips for a Successful Pie

  1. Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your pies will significantly improve with fresh, high-quality ingredients.

  2. Don’t Overmix: When making pie crust, handle the dough as little as possible to avoid a tough crust.

  3. Chill Your Dough: If your dough becomes too warm while rolling, chill it for 15 minutes before continuing.

  4. Bake on a Sheet Pan: Place your pie on a baking sheet to catch any spills and make for easier handling.

  5. Let It Cool: Allow your pies to cool completely before slicing to ensure clean cuts and a firm filling.

Conclusion

With these pie recipes, your Thanksgiving dessert table will be the talk of the family gathering. Each pie carries its own tradition and flavor, making them perfect for any palate. Whether you stick to the classics or try something new, remember that the heart of Thanksgiving lies in sharing delicious food with loved ones. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create some pie magic this holiday season! Happy baking!
