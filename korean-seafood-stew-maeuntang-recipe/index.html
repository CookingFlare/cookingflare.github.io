Korean Seafood Stew (Maeuntang) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold and spicy flavors, and one dish that perfectly exemplifies this culinary tradition is Maeuntang, a rich and spicy seafood stew. Packed with fresh seafood, aromatic herbs, and a mix of savory and spicy flavors, Maeuntang is a beloved comfort food enjoyed across Korea. This hearty stew is not only comforting on cold days but also ideal for gatherings with family and friends.

In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the process of making an authentic Korean Seafood Stew from scratch, highlighting every essential ingredient and step. By the end, you’ll be able to make Maeuntang in your own kitchen, bringing a taste of Korea to your table. So, get ready to dive into the depths of this satisfying, bold dish that captures the essence of Korean comfort food.

What is Maeuntang?

Maeuntang (매운탕) is a traditional Korean seafood stew known for its vibrant red color, thanks to the spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste) and gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). The name “Maeuntang” literally translates to “spicy soup” or “spicy stew” in Korean, with “maeun” referring to spiciness and “tang” meaning soup or stew.

This dish typically features a variety of fresh seafood, such as fish, shellfish, squid, and shrimp, simmered in a savory broth infused with spicy, sour, and umami-rich flavors. It’s commonly enjoyed with a bowl of steamed rice and kimchi, making it a well-rounded, satisfying meal.

The Essential Ingredients for Maeuntang

To make Maeuntang, the quality of your ingredients plays a crucial role in creating the best flavor. Below are the key components you’ll need for this seafood stew:

Seafood

Maeuntang is a seafood-centric dish, and the variety of seafood you choose will impact the overall flavor. Common ingredients include:

  • White fish: Flounder, bass, or cod are perfect options. They provide a delicate texture that absorbs the broth’s flavors well.
  • Shrimp: Shrimp adds sweetness and a slightly chewy texture, balancing the spiciness of the dish.
  • Squid: Squid is a popular addition that adds both flavor and texture to the stew.
  • Clams and mussels: These shellfish lend a savory briny flavor to the broth.

Broth Base

The broth for Maeuntang is typically made from anchovy stock, which provides a deep, umami-packed foundation for the stew. You can also use dried kelp (kombu) or seafood stock to enhance the richness of the broth.

  • Anchovy stock: This is the most common base for Maeuntang. To make anchovy stock, simply simmer dried anchovies and kelp in water for about 20 minutes. The result is a savory, umami-filled broth that pairs beautifully with the seafood.
  • Vegetables: A few aromatic vegetables are essential for flavor. These include onions, garlic, and Korean radish (mu), which give the broth a subtle sweetness and complexity.

Seasonings and Condiments

Maeuntang’s signature flavor profile is built on a combination of spicy, sour, and salty elements. To achieve the perfect balance, use the following seasonings:

  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): This red chili paste is a must-have in any Korean spicy dish. It brings both heat and a touch of sweetness to the broth.
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): These flakes are the key to achieving the desired spiciness and are often used alongside gochujang to give the stew an extra kick.
  • Soy sauce: Soy sauce adds saltiness and depth to the broth, enhancing the umami flavors.
  • Sesame oil: A small drizzle of sesame oil adds richness and a nutty aroma to the finished dish.
  • Vinegar: A splash of vinegar (usually rice vinegar) is used to balance out the richness of the broth and give it a slight tang.

Herbs and Greens

For added fragrance and flavor, Maeuntang is garnished with fresh herbs and greens. These ingredients enhance both the visual appeal and the taste of the stew:

  • Green onions: Thinly sliced green onions provide a fresh, oniony kick.
  • Perilla leaves: These leaves, commonly used in Korean cooking, have a mild, minty flavor that complements the richness of the stew.
  • Chili peppers: If you like it extra spicy, adding some fresh chili peppers is a great way to kick up the heat.

How to Make Maeuntang (Korean Seafood Stew)

Step 1: Prepare the Stock

Start by making the anchovy stock, which forms the base of the Maeuntang broth. Here’s how:

  1. Prepare the anchovies and kelp: Take a handful of dried anchovies (around 10-12 pieces) and a small piece of dried kelp (about 10 cm long). Rinse both to remove any impurities.

  2. Simmer the stock: Place the anchovies and kelp in a pot with 6 cups of water. Bring it to a boil and let it simmer over medium heat for about 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, remove the anchovies and kelp from the pot. Your anchovy stock is now ready.

Step 2: Prepare the Seafood

While the stock is simmering, prepare your seafood:

  1. Clean and cut the fish: If using whole fish, remove the scales, gut it, and cut it into large chunks. You can use flounder, cod, or any white fish of your choice.

  2. Prepare the shrimp and squid: Peel and devein the shrimp, removing the shells and tails. For the squid, clean it and cut it into rings or strips.

  3. Clean the shellfish: If using clams or mussels, scrub the shells clean to remove any sand or debris. Discard any open shellfish that don’t close when tapped.

Step 3: Make the Broth

Now that the stock is ready, it’s time to build the flavorful broth:

  1. Add the vegetables: To the prepared anchovy stock, add a few slices of Korean radish (mu) and a whole onion (cut in half). Let these simmer for about 10-15 minutes until they become tender and fragrant.

  2. Season the broth: Add 2 tablespoons of gochujang, 1 tablespoon of gochugaru, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 teaspoon of sugar to the pot. Stir well to dissolve the gochujang and gochugaru into the broth.

  3. Adjust the flavor: Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning to your liking. If you prefer a tangy flavor, add a splash of rice vinegar. You can also add a pinch of salt if necessary.

Step 4: Add the Seafood

Once the broth is well-seasoned, it’s time to add the seafood:

  1. Add the fish: Carefully add the chunks of fish to the pot and allow them to cook for about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Add the shrimp, squid, and shellfish: After the fish has cooked through, add the shrimp, squid, and shellfish to the pot. Let them cook for another 5-7 minutes until the shrimp turn pink and the squid becomes tender.

Step 5: Final Touches

Once all the seafood is cooked, it’s time to bring everything together:

  1. Add the herbs and greens: Add a handful of sliced green onions, perilla leaves (if available), and a few slices of fresh chili peppers for extra heat.

  2. Finish with sesame oil: Drizzle a teaspoon of sesame oil over the stew to add richness and a subtle nutty flavor.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Maeuntang is best served hot, directly from the pot. Ladle the stew into bowls, making sure each serving gets a good portion of seafood, broth, and vegetables. Serve it with steamed white rice and a side of kimchi for a truly authentic Korean meal.

Tips for the Perfect Maeuntang

  • Use fresh seafood: Fresh seafood makes a big difference in the flavor of Maeuntang. If possible, buy your fish and shellfish from a local market or seafood store to ensure they’re as fresh as possible.

  • Adjust the spice level: Maeuntang can be as spicy as you like. If you prefer a milder stew, reduce the amount of gochujang and gochugaru. On the other hand, for those who enjoy intense heat, add more chili peppers or increase the amount of gochujang.

  • Use Korean radish (mu): Korean radish has a unique flavor that adds a subtle sweetness to the broth. It also absorbs the flavors of the soup, making it incredibly delicious when eaten with the seafood.

  • Experiment with different seafood: While fish, shrimp, and squid are typical choices, feel free to mix and match other seafood like octopus or crab to make the stew your own.

Conclusion

Korean Seafood Stew (Maeuntang) is a bold, flavorful dish that showcases the richness of Korean cuisine. With its spicy broth, fresh seafood, and a delightful balance of savory, sour, and sweet flavors, Maeuntang is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re enjoying it with family on a chilly evening or serving it at a gathering, Maeuntang is the perfect dish to bring people together over a shared love of great food.

Give this recipe a try, and savor the incredible taste of this traditional Korean comfort dish right at home.
