Grilled Turkey Burgers with Caramelized Onions: A Paleo-Approved Recipe for the 4th of July


Summer is finally here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and indulge in some delicious, juicy burgers. But why settle for the same old beef patties when you can try something new and exciting? Enter the grilled turkey burger, a paleo-approved alternative to traditional beef burgers that’s just as flavorful and satisfying.

The Magic of Caramelized Onions

The secret to making these grilled turkey burgers truly special lies in the caramelized onions. Slow-cooking the onions in a mixture of butter and oil brings out their natural sweetness, which perfectly complements the savory flavor of the turkey. And the best part? It’s incredibly easy to make. Simply slice the onions thinly, heat up a pan with some butter and oil, and let the onions cook for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they’re dark golden brown and caramelized.

The Grilled Turkey Burgers

While the onions are cooking, it’s time to prepare the turkey burgers. You’ll need:

  • 1 pound ground turkey breast
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika

In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, parsley, garlic, olive oil, salt, black pepper, and paprika. Use your hands or a spoon to mix everything together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the burgers tough.

Grilling the Burgers

Once the onions are caramelized, it’s time to fire up the grill. Preheat it to medium-high heat and shape the turkey mixture into 4-6 patties, depending on your desired burger size. Place the patties on the grill and cook for about 5-6 minutes per side, or until they’re cooked through and reach an internal temperature of 165°F.

Assembly and Serving

Once the burgers are cooked, it’s time to assemble the sandwiches. Place a cooked turkey patty on a bun, followed by a spoonful of those delicious caramelized onions, some lettuce, tomato, and your favorite condiments. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • To make these burgers more substantial, try adding some sliced avocado or a fried egg on top.
  • If you prefer a spicier burger, add some diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes to the turkey mixture.
  • For a vegetarian twist, substitute the turkey with a portobello mushroom cap and grill it until tender.

Conclusion

Grilled turkey burgers with caramelized onions are a game-changer for the 4th of July, and with this paleo-approved recipe, you can enjoy a delicious and healthy meal without sacrificing flavor. So fire up the grill, get creative with your toppings, and enjoy the perfect summer burger. Happy grilling!
