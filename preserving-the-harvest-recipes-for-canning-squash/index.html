Preserving the Harvest: Recipes for Canning Squash


As summer transitions into fall, the abundance of fresh produce in gardens and farmers’ markets presents a delightful challenge: how to preserve the harvest for enjoyment throughout the year. One versatile and nutritious vegetable that shines during this time is squash. Whether you have an abundance of zucchini, yellow squash, or butternut squash, canning these vegetables is a fantastic way to savor their flavors long after the season has passed.

Understanding the Basics of Canning Squash

Canning squash involves the process of preserving it in jars to maintain its freshness and flavor. It’s important to note that only certain types of squash are suitable for canning due to their texture and density. For instance, zucchini and yellow squash are excellent candidates for canning, while delicate summer squash varieties like pattypan may not hold up well during the canning process.

Canning Squash: The Recipes

1. Zesty Pickled Zucchini

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups zucchini, sliced
  • 1 ½ cups onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 ½ cups bell peppers, chopped
  • 3 ½ cups white vinegar
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon pickling salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the zucchini, onions, and bell peppers.
  2. In a separate pot, bring the vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, turmeric, nutmeg, black pepper, and pickling salt to a boil.
  3. Pour the hot mixture over the zucchini, onions, and bell peppers.
  4. Bring the entire mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Ladle the hot zesty pickled zucchini into sterilized jars, leaving ½ inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the jar rims, seal with lids, and process in a boiling water canner for 15 minutes.

2. Butternut Squash Soup Base

Ingredients:

  • 10 cups butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 3 cups onions, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the butternut squash, onions, garlic, and broth.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  3. Using a blender, puree the mixture until smooth.
  4. If adding heavy cream, stir it into the pureed soup.
  5. Ladle the hot butternut squash soup base into sterilized jars, leaving 1 inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the jar rims, seal with lids, and process in a pressure canner for 90 minutes.

Conclusion

Canning squash not only allows you to enjoy the flavors of summer well into the colder months but also provides a sense of accomplishment and self-sufficiency. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or just starting out, these recipes offer a delightful way to savor the vibrant flavors of squash throughout the year. Embrace the art of canning and fill your pantry with the wholesome goodness of freshly preserved squash!
