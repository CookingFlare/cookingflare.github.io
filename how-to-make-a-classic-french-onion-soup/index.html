How to Make a Classic French Onion Soup


French onion soup is a timeless classic that warms the soul and tantalizes the taste buds. With its rich, caramelized onions, savory broth, and gooey cheese topping, this dish is a staple in French cuisine and a favorite in many households. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to create an authentic French onion soup that rivals any restaurant version.

The History of French Onion Soup

French onion soup, or soupe à l’oignon, has humble beginnings. It originated as a peasant dish, made with simple ingredients like onions, stale bread, and water. Over time, it evolved into the luxurious version we know today, featuring rich beef broth and a decadent cheese topping. The soup gained popularity in the 18th century and has since become a beloved dish worldwide.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a classic French onion soup, gather the following ingredients:

  • Onions: About 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced.
  • Butter: 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter for sautéing.
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons to help with caramelization.
  • Beef Broth: 6 cups of high-quality beef broth or stock.
  • White Wine: 1 cup of dry white wine (optional, but recommended for depth of flavor).
  • Sherry: 1/4 cup of sherry or brandy (optional).
  • Thyme: Fresh thyme sprigs or 1 teaspoon of dried thyme.
  • Bay Leaf: 1 bay leaf for added flavor.
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste.
  • Bread: 4 slices of crusty French bread or baguette.
  • Cheese: 1 1/2 cups of grated Gruyère cheese (or a mix of Gruyère and Swiss).

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Caramelizing the Onions

The key to a flavorful French onion soup lies in the caramelization of the onions. This process takes time but is well worth the effort.

  1. Heat the Butter and Oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the Onions: Once the butter is melted and bubbling, add the sliced onions. Stir to coat them in the butter and oil.
  3. Cook Slowly: Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook the onions, stirring occasionally. This process will take about 30-40 minutes. You want the onions to become soft and golden brown, not burnt. If they start to stick, you can add a splash of water to deglaze the pot.
  4. Add Sugar (Optional): If you prefer a sweeter flavor, you can add a teaspoon of sugar to help with caramelization.

2. Deglazing the Pot

Once the onions are beautifully caramelized, it’s time to deglaze the pot.

  1. Add Wine and Sherry: Pour in the white wine and sherry (if using). Scrape the bottom of the pot to release any flavorful bits stuck to the bottom.
  2. Simmer: Allow the wine to simmer for about 5 minutes until it reduces slightly.

3. Adding the Broth

Now, it’s time to create the soup base.

  1. Pour in the Broth: Add the beef broth to the pot, along with the thyme and bay leaf.
  2. Season: Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Simmer: Bring the soup to a gentle simmer and let it cook for about 30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully.

4. Preparing the Bread and Cheese

While the soup is simmering, prepare the bread and cheese topping.

  1. Toast the Bread: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the slices of bread on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven until golden brown, about 10 minutes.
  2. Grate the Cheese: While the bread is toasting, grate the Gruyère cheese. You can also mix in some Swiss cheese for added flavor.

5. Assembling the Soup

Once the soup is ready, it’s time to assemble and broil.

  1. Preheat the Broiler: Set your oven’s broiler to high.
  2. Ladle the Soup: Divide the soup among oven-safe bowls or crocks.
  3. Add Bread: Place a slice of toasted bread on top of each bowl of soup.
  4. Top with Cheese: Generously sprinkle the grated cheese over the bread, ensuring it covers the entire surface.

6. Broiling

  1. Broil the Soup: Place the bowls on a baking sheet and broil in the oven for about 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown. Keep a close eye on it to prevent burning.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Carefully remove the bowls from the oven (they will be hot!) and let them cool for a minute. Serve your classic French onion soup with a sprinkle of fresh thyme or parsley for garnish.

Tips for the Perfect French Onion Soup

  • Quality Ingredients: Use high-quality beef broth for the best flavor. Homemade broth is ideal, but store-bought can work in a pinch.
  • Onion Variety: While yellow onions are traditional, you can experiment with sweet onions or even a mix for different flavor profiles.
  • Cheese Choices: Gruyère is the classic choice, but feel free to mix in other cheeses like mozzarella or Parmesan for a unique twist.
  • Make Ahead: French onion soup can be made ahead of time. Simply store the soup and toppings separately in the refrigerator. When ready to serve, reheat the soup, assemble, and broil.

Conclusion

Making a classic French onion soup is a rewarding culinary experience that brings warmth and comfort to any meal. With its rich flavors and satisfying textures, this soup is perfect for cozy nights in or impressing guests at dinner parties. Follow these steps, and you’ll have a delicious bowl of French onion soup that will transport you straight to a quaint Parisian bistro. Enjoy your cooking adventure!
