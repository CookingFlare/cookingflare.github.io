Best Pumpkin Pie Recipes for Thanksgiving Dessert


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and, of course, delicious food. Among the many traditional dishes that grace the holiday table, pumpkin pie stands out as one of the most beloved desserts. Its rich, spiced flavor and creamy texture make it the perfect finish to a hearty meal. If you’re looking to create the ultimate pumpkin pie for your Thanksgiving feast, you’re in the right place. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best pumpkin pie recipes that will surely impress your guests and leave them asking for seconds.

Why Pumpkin Pie is the Perfect Thanksgiving Dessert

Pumpkin pie has a long history as a Thanksgiving favorite, with its origins tracing back to early American settlers. The pumpkin, a symbol of autumn and harvest, pairs beautifully with warm spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. The combination of flavors creates a comforting, cozy dessert that complements the savory dishes of Thanksgiving dinner.

While pumpkin pie is a classic, there are many ways to put a fresh spin on this dessert. Whether you’re looking for a traditional recipe or want to experiment with a modern twist, we’ve got you covered. In this post, we’ll guide you through some of the best pumpkin pie recipes to try this Thanksgiving.

Classic Pumpkin Pie Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin puree (about one 15-ounce can)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-made 9-inch pie crust (or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust: If you’re using a store-bought crust, simply place it in a 9-inch pie pan. If you’re making your own pie crust, roll it out to fit your pan and crimp the edges. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Mix the Filling: In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, sugar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, and cloves. Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each is fully incorporated. Slowly add the heavy cream and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth and well combined.

  3. Pour into Crust: Pour the pumpkin mixture into the prepared pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.

  4. Bake: Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 15 minutes. After 15 minutes, reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for another 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool completely on a wire rack before slicing. Serve with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate indulgence.

This classic pumpkin pie recipe is a tried-and-true favorite that never disappoints. The smooth, spiced filling pairs perfectly with the buttery, flaky crust, making it an ideal dessert for your Thanksgiving celebration.

Cream Cheese Pumpkin Pie

For those who enjoy a little extra richness and tang in their dessert, this cream cheese pumpkin pie recipe adds a delightful twist to the traditional version. The cream cheese filling creates a smooth, velvety texture that complements the spiced pumpkin perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin puree
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-made 9-inch pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust: Place your pie crust in a 9-inch pie pan and set aside. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a medium bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy. Add the sugar, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and nutmeg, and mix until well combined. Add the pumpkin puree and beat until smooth. One at a time, add the eggs, mixing thoroughly after each addition. Gradually add the heavy cream and vanilla extract, stirring until the mixture is smooth and well blended.

  3. Fill the Crust: Pour the pumpkin cream cheese filling into the prepared pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.

  4. Bake: Bake the pie at 350°F (175°C) for 50-55 minutes, or until the center is set and a knife inserted into the filling comes out clean. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil to prevent burning.

  5. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This pie is especially delicious when served with a dollop of whipped cream or a sprinkle of cinnamon on top.

The creamy, tangy filling of this cream cheese pumpkin pie makes it a standout at any Thanksgiving gathering. It’s a great choice for anyone looking to add a bit of sophistication to their holiday dessert table.

Vegan Pumpkin Pie

For those following a vegan or dairy-free diet, this vegan pumpkin pie recipe is a perfect alternative. It uses coconut milk and a homemade date-sweetened crust for a wholesome, plant-based dessert that everyone can enjoy.

Ingredients for the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • ¼ cup coconut flour
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: In a medium bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt. Stir until the mixture forms a dough. Press the dough into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie pan. Use a fork to prick the bottom of the crust. Place the crust in the refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm up. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Make the Filling: In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves, and cornstarch. Mix until smooth and well combined.

  3. Assemble the Pie: Pour the pumpkin filling into the chilled pie crust and smooth the top with a spatula.

  4. Bake: Bake the pie for 50-55 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean. If the edges of the crust start to over-brown, cover them with aluminum foil.

  5. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool for at least 2 hours before slicing. For an extra touch, serve with whipped coconut cream or a drizzle of maple syrup.

This vegan pumpkin pie is a delicious, allergy-friendly dessert that doesn’t sacrifice flavor. The creamy coconut milk filling is rich and comforting, while the homemade crust adds a delightful texture.

Pumpkin Pie with Pecan Streusel Topping

If you’re looking for a way to elevate your pumpkin pie, adding a pecan streusel topping is a great option. The crunchy, nutty streusel contrasts beautifully with the smooth pumpkin filling and creates a dessert that’s both visually appealing and incredibly delicious.

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin puree
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-made 9-inch pie crust

Ingredients for the Streusel Topping:

  • ½ cup chopped pecans
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 4 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small cubes

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust: Place your pie crust into a 9-inch pie pan. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Make the Filling: In a medium bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, sugar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, and cloves. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then add the heavy cream and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.

  3. Make the Streusel Topping: In a small bowl, combine the chopped pecans, brown sugar, flour, and cinnamon. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Assemble the Pie: Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared pie crust. Sprinkle the streusel topping evenly over the filling.

  5. Bake: Bake the pie at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for another 40-45 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool completely before serving. This pie is amazing on its own, but it’s especially delicious when served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

The pecan streusel topping adds a delightful texture and sweetness to the pumpkin pie, creating a dessert that’s perfect for those who love a little crunch with their creamy fillings.

Conclusion

No Thanksgiving celebration is complete without a delicious pumpkin pie. Whether you prefer the traditional recipe, want to try something creamy and tangy with cream cheese, or need a vegan-friendly alternative, there’s a pumpkin pie recipe here for everyone. With the right ingredients and a little bit of love, you’ll create a dessert that your guests will remember long after the last crumb is gone.

This year, take your Thanksgiving dessert to the next level with these irresistible pumpkin pie recipes. Whether you go classic, creamy, or crunchy, these pies are sure to be the star of your holiday table. Happy baking and enjoy your Thanksgiving feast!
