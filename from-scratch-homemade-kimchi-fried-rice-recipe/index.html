From Scratch: Homemade Kimchi Fried Rice Recipe


Note: This recipe is perfect for those who want to try their hand at making a delicious and authentic Korean dish. Kimchi fried rice is a popular dish that combines the tangy flavors of kimchi with the savory goodness of fried rice. With this recipe, you can create your own homemade version from scratch, ensuring that every bite is bursting with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1/2 cup kimchi juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs, fried sunny-side-up
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  2. Add the diced carrot and frozen peas to the skillet, and continue cooking until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet, then add the cooked rice to the other side. Break up any clumps of rice with a spoon or spatula, and cook for a few minutes until the rice is heated through.

  4. Add the chopped kimchi and kimchi juice to the skillet, mixing it in with the rice and vegetables. Cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Pour the sauce over the rice mixture, and stir well to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Remove the skillet from heat, and garnish the kimchi fried rice with thinly sliced green onions. Mix well to distribute the onions throughout the dish.

  7. Serve the kimchi fried rice hot, topped with a fried sunny-side-up egg. The runny yolk adds a creamy richness to the dish, complementing the tangy and spicy flavors of the kimchi.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add some gochujang, a Korean chili paste, to the sauce mixture. Start with 1 teaspoon and adjust to your preferred spice level.

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding other vegetables or protein options. Some popular additions include mushrooms, bell peppers, tofu, or shredded chicken.

  • If you don’t have kimchi juice on hand, you can substitute it with a mixture of 1 tablespoon vinegar and 1 tablespoon water.

  • To make this recipe vegetarian or vegan, omit the fried egg or substitute it with a plant-based alternative.

  • Kimchi fried rice can be enjoyed as a main dish on its own, or as a side dish alongside other Korean favorites like bulgogi or Korean fried chicken.

Now that you have the recipe for homemade kimchi fried rice, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and start cooking! Gather the ingredients, follow the instructions, and savor the delicious results. Enjoy the flavors of Korea right in your own kitchen with this from-scratch recipe.
