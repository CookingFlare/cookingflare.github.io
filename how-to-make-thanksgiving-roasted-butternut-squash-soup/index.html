How to Make Thanksgiving Roasted Butternut Squash Soup


Thanksgiving is a time of celebration, family, and, of course, delicious food. If you’re looking for a new dish to add to your holiday spread, consider making a roasted butternut squash soup. This velvety-smooth, savory soup is perfect for the fall season, with its natural sweetness from the squash and its warm, comforting flavors.

Roasting the squash beforehand brings out the depth of flavor, enhancing the soup’s richness and making it a satisfying addition to your Thanksgiving feast. The combination of aromatic herbs, a touch of cream, and the nutty undertones of the butternut squash make this soup an unforgettable starter for your holiday meal.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through each step, from preparing the squash to blending the ingredients into a luscious, creamy soup. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow, and it’s sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need for this roasted butternut squash soup:

  • 1 large butternut squash – peeled, seeded, and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil – for roasting the squash and sautéing the vegetables
  • 1 medium onion – chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic – minced
  • 1 medium carrot – peeled and diced
  • 1 celery stalk – chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth – depending on your preference
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin – for a warm, earthy flavor
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon – for a touch of warmth
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg – enhances the soup’s fall flavors
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt – or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper – freshly ground
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream – for a rich, velvety texture
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup – optional, to enhance the sweetness of the squash
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage – finely chopped (or substitute with thyme)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley – for garnish

Preparation

Step 1: Roasting the Butternut Squash

To start, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roasting the squash brings out its natural sweetness and intensifies its flavor, so this step is essential.

  • Peel and cube the butternut squash: Begin by cutting off both ends of the squash. Stand the squash upright and carefully peel the skin using a vegetable peeler. Once peeled, cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds. Then, slice the squash into cubes, about 1 to 1.5 inches in size. This ensures the squash will roast evenly.
  • Toss with olive oil: Place the cubed squash onto a baking sheet, and drizzle with 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Toss the cubes to coat them evenly. This will help them caramelize and turn golden brown as they roast.
  • Season: Sprinkle a pinch of salt and black pepper over the squash to season it lightly.
  • Roast: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast the squash for 25–30 minutes, or until the cubes are tender and lightly browned. Stir halfway through to ensure even roasting.

Step 2: Sautéing the Vegetables

While the squash roasts, it’s time to prepare the aromatic vegetables that will form the base of the soup. These vegetables add flavor depth and balance out the sweetness of the squash.

  • Sauté the onions, garlic, and carrots: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until softened and translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and diced carrots, and cook for an additional 3–5 minutes, stirring occasionally. The carrots should begin to soften, and the garlic will release its aroma.
  • Add celery: Once the carrots are tender, add the chopped celery and continue to sauté for another 3 minutes. The celery will add a subtle crunch and flavor to the soup.

Step 3: Blending the Soup

Once the squash is roasted to perfection, it’s time to combine everything into a smooth, comforting soup.

  • Add the roasted squash: Once the squash is out of the oven, carefully add the cubes into the pot with the sautéed vegetables. Stir to combine.
  • Add the spices: Sprinkle in the ground cumin, cinnamon, and nutmeg. These spices complement the butternut squash and bring warmth to the soup. Stir them into the mixture, letting the flavors meld for a minute or two.
  • Pour in the broth: Pour in 4 cups of vegetable or chicken broth, depending on your preference. The broth will help thin out the mixture and create the right consistency for the soup.
  • Simmer: Bring the soup to a simmer over medium heat. Let it simmer for 10–15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 4: Pureeing the Soup

Once the soup has simmered, it’s time to make it smooth and creamy. You can use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the soup.

  • Using an immersion blender: If you have an immersion blender, simply blend the soup directly in the pot. Move the blender around to ensure the soup is fully pureed, leaving no chunks behind.
  • Using a regular blender: If you don’t have an immersion blender, transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender. Be careful when blending hot liquids – allow the soup to cool slightly before blending, and cover the blender with a towel to prevent splashing. Puree the soup until it’s completely smooth and creamy.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Once the soup is pureed, it’s time to make it rich and velvety.

  • Add heavy cream: Stir in 1/2 cup of heavy cream to give the soup its creamy texture. This will make the soup luxuriously smooth and indulgent.
  • Adjust seasoning: Taste the soup and add more salt or pepper as needed. If you’d like the soup to be a touch sweeter, you can add 1 tablespoon of maple syrup, which will enhance the natural sweetness of the butternut squash.
  • Stir in fresh sage: Finally, stir in the freshly chopped sage for a burst of herbal flavor. If you prefer thyme, feel free to substitute it here.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Your Thanksgiving roasted butternut squash soup is now ready to serve! Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for color and a hint of freshness. You can also add a few extra sage leaves for an aromatic touch.

This soup pairs wonderfully with warm, crusty bread or even a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a tangy contrast to the sweetness of the squash.

Tips for the Perfect Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

  • Roast the squash longer for more caramelization: If you prefer a deeper, more caramelized flavor, feel free to roast the squash for a bit longer. Just make sure to check it regularly to avoid burning.
  • Make it ahead: This soup can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors actually develop and improve as they sit. Simply reheat before serving.
  • Add protein: For a heartier soup, you can add cooked chicken, turkey, or even a handful of beans for extra protein. This can turn the soup into a full meal.
  • Vegan option: To make the soup vegan, simply swap the heavy cream for coconut milk or a plant-based cream alternative. Use vegetable broth to keep it entirely plant-based.

Why This Roasted Butternut Squash Soup Is Perfect for Thanksgiving

Butternut squash soup is a great addition to your Thanksgiving menu for several reasons. The natural sweetness of the squash pairs wonderfully with savory herbs and spices, offering a balance of flavors that complement the rich and hearty dishes commonly served at Thanksgiving.

Moreover, it’s a wonderful way to showcase the best of fall produce. The orange hue of the roasted squash brings a festive touch to the table, and the soup’s creamy texture is comforting and satisfying. Whether served as a first course or alongside the main dishes, this soup is sure to become a Thanksgiving favorite.

By using fresh ingredients and taking the time to roast the squash, you’ll bring out the best flavors and create a dish that is both healthy and indulgent. And, with the option to make it in advance, you’ll have more time to spend with your loved ones on the big day.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, wow your guests with a homemade roasted butternut squash soup that combines the flavors of the season in every spoonful. The roasting process enhances the squash’s natural sweetness, while the addition of aromatic herbs and spices brings warmth and complexity to the dish. Whether you serve it as a starter or a side, this soup will undoubtedly be a hit at your Thanksgiving table. Enjoy!
