Easy Breakfast Recipes for Holiday Mornings


Holiday mornings are a special time—a break from the rush, an invitation to relax and enjoy some delicious food with loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a festive brunch or simply want to wake up to something delightful, easy breakfast recipes for holiday mornings can set the tone for the day. Let’s dive into a selection of crowd-pleasing, straightforward dishes that combine comfort, flavor, and festive flair.

1. Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes with Maple Butter

Nothing beats the classic pancakes, especially when they come out fluffy and tender. The trick to holiday this recipe up is to use buttermilk for richness and a dollop of homemade maple butter.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For Maple Butter:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tbsp pure maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the buttermilk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until combined.
  3. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; some lumps are fine.
  4. Preheat a non-stick griddle or skillet over medium heat and lightly grease it.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the griddle. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2-3 minutes. Flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Meanwhile, prepare maple butter by mixing softened butter with maple syrup until smooth.
  7. Serve pancakes warm topped with a generous spread of maple butter and a drizzle of syrup.

This recipe scales effortlessly, and the maple butter adds a touch of luxuriously sweet creaminess that transforms familiar pancakes into something holiday-worthy.

2. Savory Spinach and Feta Breakfast Casserole

If you want to get ahead and appreciate a no-fuss dish, this savory casserole is your answer. Minimal hands-on time, packed with veggies and cheese, and easy to slice for a gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk or cream
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano or mixed Italian herb blend
  • 4-6 slices of bread, cubed (day-old works well)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan, add onions and garlic, sauté until softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes. Stir in spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and oregano together until smooth.
  4. Spread the cubed bread evenly in the baking dish. Layer the sautéed spinach mixture over the bread.
  5. Sprinkle mozzarella and feta cheeses over the top.
  6. Pour the egg mixture evenly over everything, pressing down gently to soak the bread.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the casserole is set and golden on top.
  8. Let it cool slightly before cutting into squares.

This casserole is a feast for the eyes and palate, marrying salty feta with tender spinach and a comforting custard texture. It’s perfect for feeding a crowd with minimal babysitting required.

3. Quick Cranberry Orange Muffins

When cold weather hits, a cozy breakfast treat featuring fresh or frozen cranberries and bright bursts of orange can really feel like a holiday hug in food form.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt or sour cream
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease well.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Toss cranberries and orange zest into the dry ingredients to coat (this helps prevent berries from sinking).
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the orange juice, yogurt, oil, egg, and vanilla.
  5. Pour wet ingredients into the dry and gently fold just until combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-22 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool on a rack before serving.

These muffins balance tart and sweet beautifully and their vibrant color adds to the festive atmosphere.

4. One-Pan Sausage and Veggie Hash

Sometimes simplicity wins the morning. This one-pan sausage and veggie hash packs bright flavors and satisfying textures in fewer steps and less cleanup, an ideal recipe for relaxed holiday mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) breakfast sausage, sliced or crumbled
  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large eggs (optional)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika or chili powder (optional, for heat)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add sausage and cook until browned and cooked through, 5-7 minutes. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  3. Add diced sweet potato to the skillet, season with salt, pepper, and paprika, and sauté until beginning to soften, about 8 minutes.
  4. Stir in onion, bell pepper, and garlic, cooking another 5 minutes until tender.
  5. Return sausage to the pan and add spinach, cooking just until wilted.
  6. If using eggs, clear small spaces in the hash and crack an egg into each. Cover and cook until eggs are poached to your liking.
  7. Serve hot straight from the pan, ideal for a cozy family-style breakfast.

This dish presents a hearty balance of protein, vegetables, and a touch of spice, perfect for starting the day on a flavorful note without fuss.

5. Easy Cinnamon French Toast Bake

If you’re aiming for a make-ahead solution that feels indulgent without drama, this cinnamon French toast bake is the place to be. Its golden crust and soft, custardy center scream holiday mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf day-old challah or brioche bread, cut into cubes
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk (whole or 2%)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • Butter for greasing

Optional Topping:
Powdered sugar, maple syrup, fresh berries

Instructions:

  1. Butter a 9x13 inch baking dish and spread cubed bread evenly in it.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk eggs, milk, cream, brown sugar, cinnamon, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Pour the mixture over the bread cubes, pressing lightly with a spatula to soak.
  4. Cover the dish with foil and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight—this step lets the bread absorb the custard.
  5. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove foil and bake for 40-45 minutes until the top is golden and a knife inserted comes out clean.
  6. Let sit for 10 minutes before serving with powdered sugar, syrup, and berries.

This recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser and frees up your morning to relax as your oven does the work.

Final Thoughts

Holiday mornings don’t have to mean frantic cooking or bland cereals. With a few easy recipes, you can create a breakfast spread that feels special but isn’t a stress factory. From fluffy buttermilk pancakes with homemade maple butter to hearty sausage hash and elegant baked French toast, these dishes can be made ahead, cooked quickly, or simply whipped up with love.

Pair these recipes with freshly brewed coffee, fresh fruit, or a festive mimosa, and you’ve got a holiday morning that feels like a celebration at every bite. So next time the holidays come around, remember — breakfast can be both easy and extraordinary.

If you try any of these recipes or have your own simple holiday morning favorites, feel free to share! The best breakfasts are the ones shared around a table.
