Soft and Buttery Parker House Rolls Recipe


Indulge in the soft, buttery, and comforting experience that is the Parker House roll. Originating in the late 19th century at the Parker House Hotel in Boston, these iconic rolls continue to be a staple in the culinary world. Primarily associated with Thanksgiving and succulent turkey sandwiches, the Parker House roll’s versatility extends beyond the holiday season. Their delectable taste and texture can elevate any meal. Let’s dive into crafting these masterpieces.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 to 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk powder (or mashed potatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons vital wheat gluten (optional, but enhances texture)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • Extra flour and butter, for rolling and brushing

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix 1 1/4 cups of the flour, water, buttermilk powder, sugar, yeast, and vital wheat gluten (if using).
  2. Heat the milk in a saucepan until it reaches about 110°F (45°C) – the perfect temperature to activate yeast. Carefully pour the milk mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Stir well, and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter until it becomes fluffy and pale. Gradually add about 1/2 cup of the yeast mixture, stirring consistently.
  4. Add the remaining 1/4 cup of flour to the yeast mixture, followed by the egg. Combine until everything is well incorporated.
  5. Gradually mix the flour mixture into the yeast mixture until all ingredients are fully blended.
  6. On a floured surface, knead the dough for about 6-8 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  7. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  9. Divide the dough into 12-16 equal portions and roll each into a tight ball. Place the rolls in the prepared dish, allowing space for growth. Cover and let rise for another 20 minutes.
  10. Melt the remaining butter and brush it over the rolls’ tops. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  11. Remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

The Secret to their Softness

The Parker House roll’s secret lies within the shaping process. Give the rolls the perfect crescent shape by rolling out the dough thinly, then folding one side over the other. The innovative technique results in stunningly soft and warm rolls that melt in your mouth.

Final Thoughts

Concocting your own batch of Parker House rolls is far more achievable than you may have imagined. From start to finish, these comforting masterpieces are sure to become a staple in all of your culinary adventures. Upgrade your dinner gatherings, family meals, and even simple snacking with these delightful and versatile creations. The soft, buttery taste of tradition is only a few simple steps away. Happy baking!
