The Perfect Middle Eastern Comfort Food: Shish Barak (Lamb Dumplings in Yogurt Sauce)


If you’re looking for a comforting and flavorful dish that will transport your taste buds to the Middle East, then Shish Barak is the perfect choice. This traditional Middle Eastern delicacy consists of delicate lamb-filled dumplings served in a creamy yogurt sauce. With its rich flavors and satisfying textures, Shish Barak is the ultimate comfort food that will warm your heart and soul.

Ingredients:

For the Dumplings:

  • 500g ground lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup bread crumbs
  • 1 pack of dumpling wrappers

For the Yogurt Sauce:

  • 2 cups plain yogurt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

For the Garnish:

  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the ground lamb, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground cinnamon, ground allspice, ground black pepper, salt, chopped parsley, chopped mint, and bread crumbs. Combine well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  2. Take a dumpling wrapper and place a small teaspoonful of the lamb mixture in the center. Fold the wrapper in half to form a half-moon shape, then pinch the edges together to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining mixture and wrappers until all the dumplings are formed.

  3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the dumplings and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until the dumplings float to the surface. Remove the dumplings from the pot and set aside.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the plain yogurt, crushed garlic, cornstarch, salt, and ground black pepper until smooth and well combined.

  5. Transfer the yogurt mixture to a saucepan and heat over medium-low heat. Stir constantly until the sauce thickens and comes to a gentle simmer. Be careful not to let it boil to prevent curdling.

  6. Once the yogurt sauce has thickened, add the cooked dumplings to the saucepan. Gently stir to coat the dumplings with the creamy yogurt sauce. Simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. In a small skillet, melt the butter over low heat. Add the ground paprika and stir for a few seconds until fragrant.

  8. Serve the Shish Barak hot, garnished with a drizzle of melted butter and paprika mixture. Sprinkle with fresh chopped parsley for added freshness and color.

Tips and Variations:

  • Shish Barak is traditionally served as a main dish, accompanied by warm Arabic bread and a fresh salad.
  • For a vegetarian version, you can replace the ground lamb with a mixture of finely chopped mushrooms, onions, and garlic. The rest of the recipe remains the same.
  • You can also experiment with the spices used in the lamb filling. Consider adding a pinch of ground nutmeg or cardamom for a unique twist.
  • Leftover Shish Barak can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving.

Enjoy the delightful flavors of Shish Barak, a true Middle Eastern comfort food that will transport you to the bustling streets and vibrant markets of the region. With its tender lamb dumplings and creamy yogurt sauce, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. So gather your loved ones, savor each bite, and let the flavors of the Middle East enchant your palate.
