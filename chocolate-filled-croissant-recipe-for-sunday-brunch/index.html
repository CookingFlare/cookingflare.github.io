Chocolate Filled Croissant Recipe for Sunday Brunch


The mere thought of a flaky, buttery croissant filled with rich, velvety chocolate is enough to brighten anyone’s day. Croissants are such a delightful treat, and when filled with decadent chocolate, they transform into an indulgence perfect for a Sunday brunch. This recipe combines the magic of homemade croissant dough with the irresistible allure of chocolate. Whether you’re hosting a family brunch or treating yourself to something special, these chocolate-filled croissants are sure to become a favorite.

Why Make Homemade Chocolate Croissants?

There’s something genuinely satisfying about making croissants completely from scratch. While it might feel like a labor of love, the payoff is worth it: crisp layers, a delicate chew, and the sweetness of gooey chocolate nestled inside every bite. Store-bought croissants pale in comparison to the homemade variety, especially when filled with your favorite chocolate. Plus, using fresh ingredients ensures your Sunday morning treat is free of unnecessary preservatives or additives.

Tools You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, gather a few essential tools to make the process smooth:

  • A Rolling Pin: Crucial for creating those buttery layers in the dough.
  • Pastry Brush: Great for applying egg wash to achieve that perfect golden finish.
  • Mixing Bowls: A couple of good-sized mixing bowls for preparing and resting the dough.
  • Bench Scraper: Handy for cutting and shaping dough pieces.
  • Baking Sheet or Tray: Line it with parchment paper for easy cleanup.
  • Plastic Wrap or Clean Towel: Useful for covering the dough while it rests.

Ingredients

For the Croissant Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup whole milk, warmed (about 110°F)
  • 1/2 cup water, warmed (about 110°F)
  • 3 sticks (1 1/2 cups) unsalted butter, room temperature

For the Filling and Finishing:

  • 1 cup high-quality semisweet chocolate (chopped or use chocolate chips)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for the egg wash)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, warm water, and yeast. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until bubbles form on the surface—this indicates the yeast is active.

Meanwhile, in a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Gradually pour the yeast mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until a dough begins to form. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes, or until it becomes soft and elastic.

Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl and cover it with a clean towel or plastic wrap. Allow it to rise at room temperature for about 1.5 hours, until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Incorporate the Butter

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently and transfer it to a lightly floured surface. Roll the dough into a large rectangle, about 16 x 10 inches.

Take your room-temperature butter and spread it evenly across two-thirds of the dough rectangle, leaving one-third bare. Fold the unbuttered third over the middle section, then fold the remaining buttered section over the top—like folding a letter.

Seal the edges lightly with your fingers, then gently roll the dough back into a rectangle. Be careful not to press too hard; the goal is to maintain those buttery layers. Refrigerate the folded dough for 30 minutes.

Step 3: Laminate the Dough

To achieve the classic croissant layers, you’ll repeat the folding and rolling process several times. Remove the dough from the fridge, roll it into a large rectangle, fold it into thirds, and refrigerate again for 30 minutes.

Repeat this process two more times, chilling the dough between each fold. This lamination process creates distinct, flaky layers.

Step 4: Cut and Shape the Croissants

After the final chilling, roll the laminated dough into a rectangle about 16 x 10 inches. Using a knife or a bench scraper, cut the dough into triangles, roughly 4–5 inches wide at the base.

Place a small handful of chocolate (or chocolate chips) near the base of each triangle. Starting at the wide end, gently roll the dough up into the shape of a croissant. Ensure the tip of the triangle is tucked underneath, as this keeps the croissant from unrolling while baking.

Step 5: Proofing

Arrange your croissants on a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving space between them to allow for expansion. Cover with a clean towel and allow them to proof for 1–2 hours. This step ensures the croissants rise properly and develop their signature light, airy texture.

Step 6: Egg Wash

Preheat your oven to 375°F while the croissants finish proofing. Brush the tops and sides of each croissant with the beaten egg wash to give them a glossy, golden-brown finish.

Step 7: Bake

Bake the croissants for 20–25 minutes, or until puffed and golden. Rotate the baking sheet halfway through to ensure even cooking. Once done, transfer them to a cooling rack for at least 10 minutes before serving—they’ll be piping hot!

Serving Suggestions

Serve these chocolate-filled croissants warm for optimal gooeyness. Pair them with a cup of freshly brewed coffee or a glass of orange juice to complete your Sunday brunch spread. For an extra touch, dust the croissants with powdered sugar or drizzle them with melted chocolate before serving.

Storage and Reheating

If any croissants survive the brunch table, you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. To reheat, simply pop them into a preheated oven at 300°F for 5–8 minutes to restore their flakiness.

For longer storage, freeze the baked croissants by wrapping them in plastic wrap and placing them in an airtight freezer bag. When ready to enjoy, thaw them at room temperature and reheat briefly in an oven.

Tips for Success

  1. Plan Ahead: Croissant-making requires time and patience. If you’re hosting brunch, start the dough a day ahead to manage your schedule better.
  2. Use Quality Ingredients: The butter and chocolate are the stars of this recipe. Using high-quality, unsalted butter and rich chocolate makes a huge difference in flavor.
  3. Work Quickly with Butter: Butter melts quickly, so keep it chilled and work fast during the rolling process to ensure the laminated layers remain intact.
  4. Practice Makes Perfect: Croissants can seem tricky at first, but with practice, you’ll feel more confident managing the dough and shaping the final pastries.

Conclusion

A Sunday brunch is an occasion to celebrate the joy of good food, and these chocolate-filled croissants are the perfect centerpiece. From the buttery, flaky pastry to the indulgent chocolate core, each bite is a testament to the magic of homemade baking. While the process does require effort, the reward is undeniable—croissants so delicious, they rival those from your favorite bakery. So roll up your sleeves, embrace the art of lamination, and savor the satisfaction of your homemade creation!
