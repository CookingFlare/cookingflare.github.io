How to Make Vegan Cheesecake Without Cashews


Creating a delicious vegan cheesecake without cashews may seem like a challenge, but it’s entirely possible with the right ingredients and techniques. This guide will walk you through a simple, satisfying recipe that will impress both vegans and non-vegans alike. The smooth, creamy texture and rich flavor of this cheesecake will leave everyone asking for seconds. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour or ground oats
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil (melted)
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups silken tofu (drained)
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream (chilled)
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (or arrowroot powder)
  • 1/4 cup coconut yogurt (plain, unsweetened)
  • Zest of one lemon

For the Topping (Optional):

  • Fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Mint leaves
  • Vegan chocolate shavings

Equipment Needed

  • 9-inch springform pan
  • Mixing bowls
  • Food processor or blender
  • Spatula
  • Electric mixer (optional)
  • Serving plates

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, combine the almond flour (or ground oats), melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt. Stir until the mixture is well combined and resembles wet sand.

  2. Form the Crust: Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Use the back of a measuring cup or your fingers to compact it evenly. Ensure the base is flat and snug.

  3. Pre-bake the Crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the crust for about 10-12 minutes, or until it is lightly golden. Remove from the oven and allow it to cool completely while preparing the filling.

Step 2: Blend the Filling

  1. Prepare the Tofu: In a blender or food processor, add the silken tofu. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as necessary.

  2. Add Remaining Ingredients: To the blended tofu, add the chilled coconut cream, maple syrup, lemon juice, vanilla extract, cornstarch, coconut yogurt, and lemon zest. Blend until the mixture is completely smooth and well combined.

  3. Taste and Adjust: Give the filling a taste. Adjust sweetness or acidity by adding more maple syrup or lemon juice as desired.

Step 3: Assemble the Cheesecake

  1. Pour the Filling: Once the crust has cooled, pour the creamy filling over it. Use a spatula to smooth the top, ensuring an even layer.

  2. Bake the Cheesecake: Bake in the preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 45-50 minutes. The edges should be slightly firm while the center remains a bit jiggly.

  3. Cool and Chill: After baking, turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside for an additional hour. This helps prevent cracks. Once cooled, remove it from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Then, cover it with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time helps the cheesecake set properly.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Release the Cheesecake: Once chilled, carefully release the cheesecake from the springform pan. Run a knife along the edge before gently removing the sides.

  2. Add Toppings: Top with fresh berries, mint leaves, or vegan chocolate shavings if desired. These not only enhance the presentation but also add a burst of flavor.

  3. Slice and Enjoy: Use a sharp knife to slice the cheesecake into wedges. Serve chilled and enjoy the creamy, rich textures without the heaviness of traditional cheesecakes.

Storage Tips

  • Refrigerate: Store any leftover cheesecake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to one week.
  • Freeze: For longer storage, you can freeze slices of cheesecake. Wrap each slice in plastic wrap and place it in an airtight container. Thaw in the refrigerator before serving.

Variations

Feel free to experiment with flavors and ingredients to make this vegan cheesecake your own. Here are a few ideas:

  • Chocolate Vegan Cheesecake: Add 1/3 cup of cocoa powder to the filling for a rich chocolate flavor.
  • Fruit Swirls: Blend in some fruit puree (strawberry, raspberry, or blueberry) into the filling for a fruity twist or swirl it on top before baking.
  • Nutty Flavor: Incorporate almond extract instead of vanilla for a different flavor profile.

Conclusion

Making a vegan cheesecake without cashews is not only possible but also incredibly rewarding. This recipe provides a creamy, delightful dessert that pleases everyone at the table. Whether you’re following a vegan diet or just looking to reduce dairy consumption, this cheesecake is a fantastic option. With a simple ingredient list and easy steps, you can create a dessert that is both delicious and visually stunning.

So, gather your ingredients, invite some friends over, and indulge in a slice of this delightful vegan cheesecake. Your taste buds will thank you!
