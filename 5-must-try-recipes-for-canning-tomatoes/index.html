5 Must-Try Recipes for Canning Tomatoes


Tomatoes are the crown jewel of summer harvest. Bursting with juicy flavor, they can turn any dish into a vibrant culinary masterpiece. But why let their magic fade with the season? Canning tomatoes allows you to preserve their fresh taste and nutritional value for use year-round. From savory sauces to versatile bases, we’ve rounded up five must-try canning recipes that will elevate your pantry game. Below, you’ll find an assortment of ideas ranging from classic to creative that suit all skill levels.

1. Classic Crushed Tomatoes

If you’re new to canning, starting with classic crushed tomatoes is a great idea. This all-purpose recipe is versatile, perfect for soups, stews, pasta sauces, or even as a pizza base.

Ingredients:

  • 20 pounds of ripe tomatoes
  • 4 tablespoons bottled lemon juice (for safety)
  • 2 teaspoons salt (optional but enhances flavor)
  • Sterilized glass canning jars

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Rinse the tomatoes under cold water and remove the stems. Place them in boiling water for 30-60 seconds until skins loosen. Transfer to an ice bath and peel off the skins.
  2. Chop and Cook: Cut the tomatoes into chunks. Place them in a large stockpot, crushing them slightly with a masher as they heat. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Fill Jars: Add 1 tablespoon of bottled lemon juice to each pint jar and 2 tablespoons for quarts. This ensures the acidity is safe for canning. Ladle the hot tomatoes into jars, leaving ½-inch headspace. Add salt if desired.
  4. Seal and Process: Wipe jar rims, apply lids, and process in a boiling water bath for 35 minutes for pints and 45 minutes for quarts. Allow jars to cool before checking the seals.

Why Try It?

Crushed tomatoes are incredibly versatile and make homemade meals more convenient year-round. Plus, making your own crushed tomatoes ensures you skip artificial preservatives and additives.

2. Savory Tomato Basil Pasta Sauce

This tomato basil sauce is a must-have for busy weeknights. It’s perfect for pasta, meatballs, or as a dipping sauce for garlic bread. When you’ve got a shelf full of these jars, Italian-inspired dinners are just moments away.

Ingredients:

  • 15 pounds of tomatoes
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 1 tablespoon dried Italian seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 tablespoons bottled lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Blanch and Peel: Peel the tomatoes as described in the first recipe and remove the cores. Dice finely or run them through a food processor if you prefer a smoother sauce.
  2. Cook the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot and sauté onions and garlic until fragrant. Add the diced tomatoes, dried Italian seasoning, and basil leaves.
  3. Simmer: Let the mixture simmer on low for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally. Adjust seasoning to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. Prep for Canning: Add lemon juice to sterilized jars (1 tablespoon per jar for pints) and pour in the hot sauce. Leave ½-inch headspace.
  5. Boil Water Bath: Process pints for 35 minutes in a boiling water canner. Let the jars rest for 12-24 hours, then store in a cool, dark place.

Bonus Tip:

For added depth, roast some tomatoes in the oven with olive oil and garlic, then blend them into the sauce before canning.

3. Tomato Salsa with a Spicy Kick

Canning your own salsa is an excellent way to capture the flavors of summer. This zesty tomato salsa pairs well with tortilla chips or as a topping for tacos and burrito bowls.

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds ripe tomatoes
  • 3 cups chopped onions
  • 2 cups chopped jalapeños (adjust to desired heat level)
  • 1½ cups chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 6 minced garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Produce: Remove tomato skins as in the previous recipes, then dice them into small chunks. Chop onions, jalapeños, and cilantro. Keep everything uniform for even consistency.
  2. Cook the Salsa: Combine all ingredients in a large pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Canning Process: Carefully ladle the salsa into hot, sterilized jars, leaving ½-inch headspace. Wipe rims and apply lids.
  4. Water-Bath Canning: Process in a boiling water canner for 15 minutes. Adjust for altitude if necessary.

Serving Idea:

Your homemade salsa also doubles as a marinade for grilled chicken or pork, infusing your meats with bold flavor.

4. Sweet & Tangy Tomato Jam

Tomato jam might sound unconventional, but its balance of sweetness and tanginess makes it a standout condiment. Spread it on toast, serve over cream cheese with crackers, or use it as a glaze for meats.

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 3 cups sugar
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon chili flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Tomatoes: Remove tomato skins using the blanching method and chop them finely.
  2. Cook the Jam: In a large saucepan, combine tomatoes, sugar, vinegar, ginger, cinnamon, and chili flakes. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens (about 1.5-2 hours).
  3. Fill Jars: Spoon the jam into clean, warm jars, leaving ¼-inch headspace. Wipe rims and seal with lids.
  4. Process in Water Bath: Process for 10 minutes. Allow to cool before storing.

Pro Tip:

For deeper flavor, let the jam sit for a week before opening. Serve it alongside a farmhouse cheese platter for a unique appetizer.

5. Homemade Tomato Juice

Tomato juice isn’t just a delicious drink—it’s also a kitchen staple for soups, casseroles, and cocktails like Bloody Marys. Preserving your own juice ensures you have a fresh-tasting, chemical-free option at your fingertips.

Ingredients:

  • 22 pounds of tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon salt (optional)
  • Bottled lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Extract Juice: Remove tomato stems and cores. Quarter the tomatoes and simmer them in a stockpot until they release their juices (about 10-15 minutes). Run them through a food mill or fine sieve to remove seeds and skins.
  2. Heat the Juice: Bring the strained juice back to a boil, stirring occasionally to prevent scorching.
  3. Prepare Jars: Add 1 tablespoon bottled lemon juice per pint or 2 tablespoons per quart to sterilized jars. Ladle the hot juice into jars, leaving ½-inch headspace.
  4. Process for Preservation: Process pints for 35 minutes and quarts for 40 minutes in a boiling water bath.

Variations:

For a spicy twist, add a dash of hot sauce or cayenne pepper to the juice while boiling.

Final Thoughts

Canning tomatoes is a rewarding way to bring the flavors of summer into your kitchen year-round. These five recipes prove that canned tomatoes are not just practical but can also be an opportunity to experiment with flavors. From the simplicity of crushed tomatoes to the boldness of spicy salsa, there’s a recipe to suit everyone’s taste. With a little effort and the right preparation techniques, your pantry can become a treasure trove of culinary delights.

Start exploring these recipes today, and you’ll never look at store-bought canned tomatoes the same way again! What’s your favorite way to use canned tomatoes? Share your ideas and tips for canning success in the comments below!
