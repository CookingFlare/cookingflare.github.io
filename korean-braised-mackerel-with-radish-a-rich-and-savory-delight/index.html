Korean Braised Mackerel with Radish: A Rich and Savory Delight


韩式braised Mackerel with Radish, also known as Jangjorim (), is a traditional Korean dish that has been a staple in many Korean households for generations. This hearty and flavorful dish is made by slow-cooking mackerel in a rich and savory sauce with radish, garlic, ginger, and other aromatics. The result is a tender and fall-apart mackerel that is infused with the deep and complex flavors of the sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 mackerel (about 1 pound), cleaned and gutted
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup Korean soy sauce (jeotgal)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds, for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons grated Korean radish (mu), for serving
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger and cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant and slightly caramelized, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the mackerel to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until browned. Remove the mackerel from the pot and set aside.
  3. Add the water, chili flakes, soy sauce, Korean soy sauce, sugar, and rice vinegar to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and reduced slightly.
  4. Add the browned mackerel back to the pot and simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is tender and falls apart easily.
  5. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, discarding the solids.
  6. Slice the mackerel into thick pieces and serve with the braising liquid spooned over the top. Garnish with green onions, sesame seeds, and grated radish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier dish, add more Korean chili flakes or use hotter peppers like habanero or ghost peppers.
  • For a richer sauce, add 1-2 tablespoons of Korean sesame oil or toasted sesame oil.
  • Serve with steamed rice or noodles for a filling and satisfying meal.
  • Experiment with different types of fish, such as cod or tilapia, for a milder flavor.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the mackerel with extra-firm tofu or tempeh and adjust the cooking time accordingly.

History and Cultural Significance

Jangjorim has a long history in Korean cuisine, dating back to the Joseon Dynasty (1392-1910). The dish was originally made with salted mackerel, which was a staple protein in many Korean households. The addition of radish and other aromatics was a way to add flavor and texture to the dish. Today, Jangjorim is still a beloved dish in Korea, often served at special occasions and celebrations.

Conclusion

Korean Braised Mackerel with Radish is a rich and savory delight that is sure to become a staple in your household. With its complex flavors and tender texture, this dish is perfect for special occasions or everyday meals. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and requires minimal ingredients. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the bold and aromatic flavors of Korean cuisine!
