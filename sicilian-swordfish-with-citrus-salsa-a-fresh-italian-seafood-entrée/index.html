Sicilian Swordfish with Citrus Salsa: A Fresh Italian Seafood Entrée


If you’re in the mood for a light and refreshing seafood dish that will transport you to the sunny shores of Sicily, look no further than this Sicilian Swordfish with Citrus Salsa recipe. Bursting with vibrant flavors and colors, this dish is a celebration of the Mediterranean cuisine that Italy is famous for. The succulent swordfish, grilled to perfection, is complemented by a zesty and tangy citrus salsa that adds a delightful contrast. It’s a dish that will impress your guests and leave them craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 4 swordfish fillets (about 6 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For the citrus salsa:
    • 2 oranges, peeled and segmented
    • 1 grapefruit, peeled and segmented
    • 1 lemon, peeled and segmented
    • 1 lime, peeled and segmented
    • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh basil leaves, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. Season the swordfish fillets with salt and pepper, and drizzle them with olive oil. Make sure the fillets are evenly coated.

  3. Place the swordfish fillets on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Be careful not to overcook the fish, as it can become dry.

  4. While the swordfish is cooking, prepare the citrus salsa. In a bowl, combine the orange, grapefruit, lemon, and lime segments. Add the finely chopped red onion, mint leaves, and basil leaves. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Gently toss the ingredients together until well combined.

  5. Once the swordfish is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.

  6. Serve the grilled swordfish fillets with a generous spoonful of citrus salsa on top. Garnish with a sprig of fresh mint or basil, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have access to a grill, you can also cook the swordfish fillets in a hot skillet or under the broiler. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • Feel free to customize the citrus salsa based on your preferences. You can add diced avocado, chopped cucumber, or even a sprinkle of chili flakes for a hint of heat.

  • This dish pairs well with a side of roasted potatoes or a simple green salad. You can also serve it with a side of crusty Italian bread to soak up the delicious citrus salsa.

  • For a complete Sicilian-inspired meal, start with an antipasto platter featuring cured meats, olives, and marinated vegetables. Follow it up with this Sicilian Swordfish with Citrus Salsa, and finish with a traditional cannoli for dessert.

So there you have it - a delightful Sicilian Swordfish with Citrus Salsa recipe that will transport you to the sunny shores of Italy. With its fresh flavors and vibrant presentation, this dish is perfect for a summer gathering or a special dinner at home. Give it a try and let your taste buds embark on a Mediterranean adventure!
