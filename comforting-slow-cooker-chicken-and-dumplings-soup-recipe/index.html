Comforting Slow Cooker Chicken and Dumplings Soup Recipe


When the weather turns chilly and the days grow shorter, there’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of chicken and dumplings soup to bring comfort and joy. This slow cooker chicken and dumplings recipe is a timeless classic that transforms simple ingredients into a hearty, soul-satisfying dish. The slow cooker allows the flavors to meld beautifully, resulting in a rich, creamy broth and tender chicken that practically falls apart.

Why Choose Slow Cooker Chicken and Dumplings?

Utilizing a slow cooker for this chicken and dumplings recipe not only makes it easier to prepare but also enhances the flavors. The slow cooking process allows the chicken to soak up the broth’s richness while keeping it moist and juicy. Plus, the dumplings become fluffy and light, soaking up all the delicious flavors of the soup.

Ingredients for the Perfect Comfort Dish

To create this comforting dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Soup:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs (or breasts)
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half (optional for creaminess)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter

For the Dumplings:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken and Vegetables

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a skillet over medium heat, add the olive oil or butter. Once melted, sauté the diced onion, sliced carrots, and celery until they begin to soften, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Add to Slow Cooker: Transfer the sautéed vegetables to the slow cooker.

  3. Season the Chicken: Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, thyme, rosemary, onion powder, and garlic powder. Place the seasoned chicken on top of the vegetables in the slow cooker.

Step 2: Create the Broth

  1. Add Broth: Pour the chicken broth over the chicken and vegetables. Ensure that the chicken is fully submerged in the broth.

  2. Cook: Cover the slow cooker and set it to cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. The chicken should be tender and easily shredded.

Step 3: Make the Dumplings

  1. Prepare the Dumpling Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually stir in the milk until a soft dough forms. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

Step 4: Shred the Chicken and Add Dumplings

  1. Shred the Chicken: Once the cooking time is complete, remove the chicken from the slow cooker and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the slow cooker.

  2. Add Cream (Optional): For a creamier soup, stir in the heavy cream or half-and-half at this point. If you prefer a lighter soup, you can skip this step.

  3. Add Frozen Peas: Stir in the frozen peas and mix well.

  4. Dumpling Time: Using a tablespoon, scoop the dumpling dough and drop it onto the surface of the soup, spacing them evenly. Cover the slow cooker again and cook on high for an additional 30 minutes, or until the dumplings are cooked through and fluffy.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Garnish: Once the dumplings are ready, ladle the chicken and dumpling soup into bowls. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and flavor.

  2. Serve: Enjoy this comforting dish with crusty bread or biscuits on the side for a complete meal.

Tips for Success

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of the soup largely depends on the quality of the chicken broth and the chicken itself. Opt for low-sodium broth to control the saltiness of your dish.

  • Customize Your Vegetables: While the classic mirepoix (onions, carrots, and celery) works beautifully, feel free to add other vegetables like corn, green beans, or bell peppers to your soup.

  • Dumpling Variations: For a twist on the traditional dumpling, you can add herbs or spices to the dough, such as dried dill or paprika, for an extra layer of flavor.

  • Make Ahead: This soup can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors deepen as it sits, making it even more delicious the next day.

Storing and Reheating

If you happen to have leftovers (which is unlikely because this soup is so good), store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it on the stove or in the microwave, adding a splash of broth or water if it thickens too much.

Conclusion

This comforting slow cooker chicken and dumplings soup recipe is the epitome of home-cooked goodness. It’s perfect for cozy family dinners or as a quick weeknight meal. With minimal effort and a few simple ingredients, you can create a dish that warms the heart and nourishes the soul.

So, the next time the cold weather hits or you just need a bowl of comfort, pull out your slow cooker and make this delightful chicken and dumplings soup. Your family will thank you, and you’ll savor every spoonful of this deliciously hearty meal. Enjoy!
