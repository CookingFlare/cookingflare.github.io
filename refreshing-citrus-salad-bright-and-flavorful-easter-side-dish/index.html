Refreshing Citrus Salad: Bright and Flavorful Easter Side Dish


Easter is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate the arrival of spring than with a refreshing and vibrant citrus salad? Packed with the zesty flavors of oranges, grapefruits, and lemons, this salad is a burst of sunshine on your plate. Whether you’re hosting a festive Easter brunch or looking for a light and healthy side dish, this citrus salad is sure to impress your guests and tantalize their taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 oranges
  • 2 grapefruits
  • 2 lemons
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of mixed salad greens
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint leaves, torn
  • 1/4 cup of roasted almonds, roughly chopped

Dressing

  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the oranges, grapefruits, and lemons. Remove the pith and cut them into thin slices. Set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced citrus fruits, red onion, salad greens, and torn mint leaves.

  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, orange juice, lemon juice, salt, and pepper until well combined. This tangy dressing adds a zingy kick to the salad.

  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and gently toss to coat all the ingredients evenly. Be careful not to crush the delicate citrus slices.

  5. Once the salad is dressed, sprinkle the roasted almonds on top for an added crunch and nutty flavor.

  6. Allow the salad to sit for a few minutes to let the flavors meld together. The citrus juices will infuse the salad, making it even more vibrant and refreshing.

  7. Serve the citrus salad chilled and enjoy the bright and invigorating flavors. It pairs perfectly with roasted lamb, grilled chicken, or even as a standalone dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a sweeter twist, you can add a drizzle of honey or a sprinkle of brown sugar to the dressing.

  • Experiment with different citrus varieties like blood oranges or tangerines to add more complexity to the salad.

  • If you’re not a fan of raw onions, you can soak the sliced red onion in cold water for 10 minutes before adding it to the salad. This will mellow out the strong flavor.

  • Feel free to get creative with your toppings. Add some crumbled feta cheese, sliced avocado, or even a handful of pomegranate seeds for an extra burst of color and flavor.

  • Make sure to use fresh and ripe citrus fruits for the best results. The juiciness and acidity of the fruits are what make this salad so refreshing.

So, this Easter, embrace the flavors of the season with a bright and flavorful citrus salad. Its vibrant colors and zesty taste will bring a burst of sunshine to your table. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or simply looking for a refreshing side dish, this salad is a guaranteed hit. So go ahead, indulge in the tangy goodness of this citrus delight and celebrate the arrival of spring in style!
