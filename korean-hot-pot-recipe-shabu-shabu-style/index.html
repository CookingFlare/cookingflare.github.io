Korean Hot Pot Recipe (Shabu Shabu Style)


Korean Hot Pot, also known as “Shabu Shabu,” is a delightful and flavorful dish that brings a fusion of savory broths, tender meats, fresh vegetables, and tasty dipping sauces. This dish is perfect for family gatherings, intimate dinners, or even casual get-togethers with friends. The beauty of this meal lies in the communal dining experience where everyone dips their ingredients into a simmering pot of broth, cooking them to perfection in front of their eyes. This hot pot style offers the perfect balance of heat, savory richness, and fresh ingredients that will have you coming back for more.

In this recipe, we’ll explore the step-by-step process of creating the perfect Korean Hot Pot with a Shabu Shabu twist. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, you’ll find that assembling this dish is easier than it seems, and the end result is nothing short of spectacular. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients for Korean Hot Pot (Shabu Shabu Style)

For the Broth:

  • 5 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of Korean fish sauce (or anchovy paste for a deeper umami flavor)
  • 2 teaspoons of sesame oil
  • 3 slices of ginger (thinly sliced)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (crushed)
  • 1 dried kelp sheet (about 2-3 inches long)
  • 1 teaspoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes, optional for a mild spicy kick)
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin (optional for a touch of sweetness)

For the Protein:

  • 200g of thinly sliced rib-eye beef (or any preferred beef cut like sirloin)
  • 200g of thinly sliced pork belly or pork loin
  • 200g of chicken breast or thighs (thinly sliced)

For Vegetables:

  • 1 medium-sized napa cabbage (cut into chunks)
  • 1 bunch of bok choy (cut into halves)
  • 200g of shiitake mushrooms (sliced)
  • 200g of enoki mushrooms
  • 1 medium-sized zucchini (cut into half-moons)
  • 1 carrot (julienned or sliced into thin rounds)
  • 1 onion (sliced thinly)
  • 1 handful of spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup of tofu (cut into cubes, optional)

For Dipping Sauces:

  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds
  • A pinch of sugar (optional)

Preparing the Broth

The first step to making an incredible Korean Hot Pot is to prepare the broth. The broth is the heart and soul of this dish, so taking the time to make it flavorful will set the stage for a delicious meal.

  1. In a large pot, pour in the water and add the dried kelp sheet. Let it soak for about 10 minutes to extract all the natural flavors from the seaweed. Afterward, bring the water to a simmer over medium heat.

  2. Once the broth begins to simmer, remove the kelp sheet and discard it. Add the ginger slices, garlic, soy sauce, fish sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  3. Allow the broth to simmer gently for an additional 15-20 minutes. The broth should have a clear, umami-rich flavor, with a subtle hint of sweetness and depth. At this point, taste the broth, and adjust the seasoning by adding more soy sauce, fish sauce, or sugar if desired.

  4. If you prefer a spicier broth, add a teaspoon of gochugaru, or adjust the amount based on your spice preference.

  5. Optional: Add a tablespoon of mirin for a hint of sweetness that complements the salty and umami flavors of the broth.

Once the broth is prepared, keep it simmering on low heat while you prepare the ingredients for the hot pot.

Preparing the Ingredients

The beauty of Korean Hot Pot is the variety of fresh ingredients that get cooked in the simmering broth. Each person will dip the ingredients into the pot, allowing them to cook to their liking. To ensure everything is ready for the hot pot, follow these steps:

  1. Slice the proteins: Begin by thinly slicing your chosen meats. Thinly sliced beef, pork, and chicken are ideal because they cook quickly and absorb the flavors of the broth. Arrange each protein separately on individual plates, ready for dipping.

  2. Prepare the vegetables: Wash all your vegetables thoroughly and cut them into bite-sized pieces. The napa cabbage, bok choy, and spinach should be cut into manageable pieces. Slice the zucchini and carrot into thin rounds or half-moons, and slice the onion thinly. For the mushrooms, slice the shiitake mushrooms and separate the enoki mushrooms into small bundles.

  3. Prepare the tofu: If you’re including tofu, cut it into cubes and set it aside in a separate bowl.

  4. Set up your dipping sauces: In small dipping bowls, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and gochujang (if using). Garnish each bowl with sesame seeds and a pinch of sugar for a balanced dipping sauce.

Assembling the Hot Pot

Now that you’ve prepared everything, it’s time to assemble the Korean Hot Pot. This is where the fun begins, as everyone gathers around the table to dip their ingredients into the simmering broth.

  1. Heat the broth: Make sure the broth is at a gentle simmer on the stovetop. It should be hot enough to cook the ingredients quickly but not boiling vigorously.

  2. Set up the table: Arrange the sliced meats, vegetables, tofu, and dipping sauces around the hot pot. Each person should have their own set of chopsticks or a small mesh strainer to dip the ingredients into the broth.

  3. Start cooking: Begin by adding the vegetables to the simmering broth, as they take longer to cook than the proteins. Start with the napa cabbage, bok choy, zucchini, and carrots. Once the vegetables are tender, you can start dipping the meats and tofu into the broth.

  4. Dipping the meats: Each person can take thin slices of beef, pork, or chicken and dip them into the simmering broth for just a few seconds until they are cooked through. The key to perfect hot pot is not to overcook the meat—just a quick dip is all it takes for the proteins to cook through and remain tender.

  5. Enjoy with dipping sauces: After cooking the ingredients, dip them into the flavorful dipping sauces for an extra burst of flavor. The balance of the salty soy sauce, tangy rice vinegar, and aromatic sesame oil complements the tender meats and fresh vegetables perfectly.

Tips for the Best Korean Hot Pot

  • Choose fresh, high-quality ingredients: Since the broth and dipping sauces are simple, the quality of the ingredients really shines in this dish. Opt for the freshest vegetables, meats, and tofu you can find to ensure the best flavor.

  • Customize the spice level: Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) to the broth or incorporating more gochujang (Korean chili paste) into your dipping sauces. For a milder version, you can skip the spicy elements altogether.

  • Be mindful of cooking times: Thinly sliced meats cook incredibly quickly in the hot broth. Be sure not to overcook the ingredients, as they can lose their tenderness and become tough. A quick dip is all it takes to cook each ingredient perfectly.

  • Add variety with noodles: To make the meal more filling, consider adding some noodles to the broth. Thin udon noodles, rice noodles, or even instant ramen can be added at the end of the meal to soak up the remaining flavors in the broth.

  • Replenish the broth: As you continue to cook, the broth may reduce in volume. Keep an eye on it and add more water if necessary to maintain the right level of liquid for cooking.

Serving and Enjoying Korean Hot Pot

Korean Hot Pot is a communal and interactive meal, which makes it an enjoyable and social dining experience. The combination of savory broth, tender meats, fresh vegetables, and flavorful dipping sauces creates a symphony of textures and tastes that will leave everyone satisfied.

Once the hot pot is ready, encourage your guests to dip their favorite ingredients into the broth, savoring the flavors and enjoying the warmth of the meal. Serve the hot pot with steamed rice or noodles for a complete, hearty meal.

This dish is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy winter evening, a weekend gathering with friends, or a special family meal. The flavors are versatile, and you can adjust the ingredients to suit your preferences. Whether you’re a fan of spicy dishes or prefer something milder, Korean Hot Pot offers something for everyone to enjoy.

Conclusion

Korean Hot Pot (Shabu Shabu Style) is more than just a meal—it’s a memorable experience that brings people together. The combination of fresh ingredients, flavorful broth, and dipping sauces creates a warm, comforting, and satisfying dish that is perfect for any occasion. By following this recipe, you can recreate the delicious flavors of Korean Hot Pot in your own home and enjoy a wonderful communal meal with your loved ones.

So, gather your ingredients, prepare your broth, and enjoy a delicious Korean Hot Pot experience that will leave everyone coming back for more!
