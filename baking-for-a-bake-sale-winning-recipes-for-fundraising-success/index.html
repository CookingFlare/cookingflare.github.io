Baking for a Bake Sale: Winning Recipes for Fundraising Success


Are you getting ready to host a bake sale for a good cause? Whether you’re raising funds for a local charity, school event, or community project, the key to a successful bake sale lies in the irresistible aroma and delicious taste of your baked goods. To help you make your bake sale a hit, here are some winning recipes that are sure to fly off the table and into the hands of eager supporters.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  4. Combine the flour and baking soda, then gradually stir into the creamed mixture.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips and nuts, if desired.
  6. Drop rounded spoonfuls onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Lemon Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, cream together the butter and 1/2 cup powdered sugar.
  3. Gradually add 2 cups of flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Press the mixture into the prepared pan.
  5. Bake for 20 minutes in the preheated oven, until firm and golden.
  6. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, 1/3 cup of flour, lemon juice, and lemon zest.
  7. Pour the lemon mixture over the baked crust.
  8. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 20 minutes, or until the lemon topping is set.
  9. Allow to cool in the pan on a wire rack. Once cooled, dust the top with powdered sugar and cut into squares.

Red Velvet Cupcakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons red food coloring
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Cream cheese frosting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, baking soda, cocoa powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the oil, buttermilk, eggs, food coloring, vinegar, and vanilla.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until smooth.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 20 to 22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, frost the cupcakes with cream cheese frosting.

Conclusion

With these delectable recipes in your bake sale arsenal, you’re sure to attract a crowd and raise significant funds for your cause. Remember to package your treats attractively, label them clearly, and consider offering a variety of options to cater to different tastes. Happy baking, and best of luck with your fundraising bake sale!
