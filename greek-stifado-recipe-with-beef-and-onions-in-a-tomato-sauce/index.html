Greek Stifado Recipe with Beef and Onions in a Tomato Sauce


Stifado, a classic Greek dish, is a hearty and flavorful stew made with tender beef, caramelized onions, and a rich tomato sauce. This beloved recipe has been passed down through generations, and its popularity endures to this day. In this article, we’ll explore the origins and nuances of stifado, as well as provide a recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

** Origins of Stifado **

Stifado, which translates to “stew” in Greek, has its roots in the 16th century. The dish originated in the Peloponnese region, where it was cooked over an open flame in a large cauldron. The original recipe consisted of beef, onions, garlic, and a sweet and sour sauce made with honey and vinegar. Over time, the recipe evolved, and the addition of tomatoes, a staple in Greek cuisine, became a defining characteristic of stifado.

** The Magic of Caramelized Onions **

At the heart of stifado is the caramelized onion, which provides a depth of flavor and sweetness that’s unmatched by any other ingredient. To achieve the perfect caramelization, it’s essential to cook the onions slowly over low heat, stirring occasionally, until they reach a deep golden brown. This process can take up to 30 minutes, but the end result is well worth the effort.

** The Recipe **

For this recipe, we’ll use 500g of beef, cut into 2.5cm cubes, and 3 large onions, thinly sliced. We’ll also need 2 cloves of garlic, minced, 1 can of crushed tomatoes, 1 tablespoon of tomato paste, 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, 1/2 teaspoon of ground allspice, salt, and black pepper. Finally, we’ll add 2 tablespoons of olive oil and 1/4 cup of red wine vinegar to the pot.

Cooking the Beef and Onions

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside. Add the sliced onions to the pot and cook until they’re caramelized, stirring occasionally, about 20-25 minutes.

Adding the Aromatics and Tomato Sauce

Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, cinnamon, allspice, salt, and black pepper. Stir to combine, then add the browned beef back to the pot. Stir to coat the beef with the tomato sauce.

Simmering the Stifado

Bring the stifado to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 1 1/2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir in the red wine vinegar and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Serving Suggestions

Stifado is a versatile dish that can be served with a variety of sides, including mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or crusty bread. For a more authentic experience, serve the stifado with a side of Greek salad and a dollop of creamy yogurt.

** Conclusion **

Stifado is a dish that’s sure to warm the hearts and bellies of anyone who tries it. With its rich flavors and tender beef, it’s a recipe that’s worth sharing with friends and family. By following this recipe and cooking the onions to perfection, you’ll be rewarded with a dish that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead, give stifado a try, and experience the magic of Greek cuisine for yourself.
