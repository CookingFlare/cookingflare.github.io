Tantalizing Gluten-Free Lemon Bars: A Tangy Sweet Treat


Are you ready to tantalize your taste buds with a delightful gluten-free dessert? Look no further than these tantalizing gluten-free lemon bars! Bursting with tangy, zesty flavors and a buttery, crumbly crust, these lemon bars are the perfect combination of sweet and sour. Whether you follow a gluten-free diet or simply love a good lemony treat, these bars are sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup melted butter or coconut oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 3/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, honey or maple syrup, melted butter or coconut oil, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Mix until the ingredients come together to form a dough.
  3. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

Preparing the Lemon Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, and honey or maple syrup until well combined.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the coconut flour, almond flour, and baking powder. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, whisking until smooth and thick.
  3. Pour the lemon filling over the cooled crust, spreading it out evenly. Return the pan to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set.

Finishing Touches:

  1. Once the lemon bars are done baking, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool to room temperature. Then, refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.
  2. Once chilled, use the parchment paper to lift the bars out of the pan. Cut into squares or bars and dust with powdered sugar if desired.
  3. Serve and enjoy the tantalizing gluten-free lemon bars!

Tips for Success

  • For the best results, use fresh lemon juice and zest for a vibrant citrus flavor.
  • If you prefer a sweeter filling, you can adjust the amount of honey or maple syrup to suit your taste.
  • Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days for optimal freshness.

Conclusion

These tantalizing gluten-free lemon bars are a delightful way to satisfy your sweet tooth while embracing the bright and refreshing flavors of lemon. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself to a special dessert, these bars are a perfect choice. The buttery almond flour crust paired with the tangy lemon filling creates a harmonious balance of textures and tastes that will leave you craving for more. Give this recipe a try and indulge in the joy of a gluten-free dessert that’s anything but ordinary!
