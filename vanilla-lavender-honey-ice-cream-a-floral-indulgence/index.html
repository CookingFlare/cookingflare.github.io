Vanilla Lavender Honey Ice Cream: A Floral Indulgence


Ice cream has always been a delightful treat, but have you ever thought of adding a touch of floral elegance to this classic dessert? If not, then it’s time to try something new and indulge in the exquisite flavors of Vanilla Lavender Honey Ice Cream. This unique combination will transport your taste buds to a serene garden filled with fragrant lavender fields. So, let’s dive into this floral indulgence!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise and seeds scraped
  • 2 tablespoons of dried culinary lavender
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup of honey

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, vanilla bean, and dried lavender. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Stir occasionally to ensure the sugar dissolves completely.

  2. Once the mixture reaches a simmer, remove it from the heat and allow it to steep for 15-20 minutes. This will allow the flavors of the vanilla bean and lavender to infuse into the cream.

  3. While the mixture is steeping, whisk the egg yolks in a separate bowl until they become smooth and pale in color.

  4. After the steeping time has elapsed, strain the cream mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the vanilla bean and lavender. Discard the solids.

  5. Slowly pour the strained cream mixture into the bowl with the whisked egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling. Once combined, return the mixture to the saucepan.

  6. Place the saucepan over low heat and cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This process, called tempering, ensures the eggs are cooked and the ice cream base achieves the desired consistency.

  7. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature. Once cooled, cover the saucepan and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the mixture to chill thoroughly.

  8. After the chilling time, remove the saucepan from the refrigerator. Stir in the honey, ensuring it is fully incorporated into the ice cream base.

  9. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.

  10. Transfer the churned ice cream to a lidded container and freeze for an additional 2-4 hours, or until firm.

  11. Serve the Vanilla Lavender Honey Ice Cream in bowls or cones and garnish with a sprinkle of dried lavender for a touch of elegance. Enjoy the creamy, floral flavors that will transport you to a blissful oasis of taste.

This Vanilla Lavender Honey Ice Cream recipe is a perfect choice for those looking to elevate their dessert experience. The delicate floral notes of lavender, combined with the richness of vanilla and the sweetness of honey, create a truly indulgent and unique treat. Whether enjoyed on a hot summer day or as a delightful ending to a special meal, this ice cream is sure to impress. So, grab your ingredients, embrace the floral elegance, and savor every spoonful of this heavenly delight!
