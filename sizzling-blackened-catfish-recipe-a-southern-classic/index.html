Sizzling Blackened Catfish Recipe: A Southern Classic


If you’re looking for a taste of the South that’s packed with flavor and a little heat, look no further than this sizzling blackened catfish recipe. This dish is a true Southern classic, known for its bold seasoning and rich, smoky flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress and delight your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 4 catfish fillets
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Seasoning Mixture: In a small bowl, combine the paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper, black pepper, white pepper, and salt. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  2. Preheat the Cast Iron Skillet: Place a cast iron skillet over medium-high heat and allow it to get hot. It’s important to use a cast iron skillet for this recipe as it helps to achieve that perfect blackened crust on the catfish fillets.

  3. Coat the Catfish Fillets: Brush both sides of each catfish fillet with the melted butter. Then, generously coat each fillet with the seasoning mixture, ensuring that the fillets are evenly covered with the flavorful spices.

  4. Sear the Catfish: Carefully place the seasoned catfish fillets in the hot cast iron skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the fillets are blackened and crispy on the outside, and tender and flaky on the inside.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the catfish fillets are cooked to perfection, remove them from the skillet and transfer them to a serving platter. Garnish with lemon wedges and serve hot.

Tips for Success:

  • Ensure that your cast iron skillet is properly preheated before adding the catfish fillets to achieve that signature blackened crust.
  • Adjust the amount of cayenne pepper to suit your heat preference. Feel free to add a little extra if you like it spicy, or reduce the amount for a milder flavor.
  • Pair this sizzling blackened catfish with classic Southern sides like creamy coleslaw, buttery cornbread, or collard greens for a complete and satisfying meal.

There you have it - a sizzling blackened catfish recipe that’s perfect for bringing a taste of the South to your table. Whether you’re hosting a summer cookout or simply craving a flavorful and hearty meal, this Southern classic is sure to hit the spot. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every sizzling bite of this mouthwatering dish. Happy cooking!
