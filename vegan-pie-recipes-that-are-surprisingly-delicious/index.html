Vegan Pie Recipes That Are Surprisingly Delicious


Pie has long been a cherished comfort food, from golden crusts to irresistible fillings that delight taste buds. While many people associate pies with butter-laden crusts and cream-based fillings, there’s a whole world of plant-based possibilities that prove vegan pies can be just as indulgent—and even more creative. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just exploring the possibilities, these recipes offer a fresh take on this classic dish.

Why Vegan Pies Deserve a Spot at Your Table

Switching to plant-based pies doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or texture. In fact, it opens new doors to experiment with vibrant fruits, hearty vegetables, alternative plant-based ingredients, and guilt-free decadence. Vegan pie recipes cater to every occasion—holiday feasts, special celebrations, or a quiet evening at home. Plus, the rich flavors of these pies rival their traditional counterparts, making them perfect for satisfying cravings and impressing guests. Let’s dive into these surprisingly delicious recipes and discover the magic of plant-based pies!

1. Sweet Potato Pie With Coconut Cream

Sweet potato pie is a nostalgic favorite for many, and this vegan rendition keeps the spirit alive while introducing a tropical twist. The hearty sweet potatoes create a rich filling complemented by the smooth, velvety texture of coconut cream.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 cups almond flour
    • ¼ cup coconut oil
    • Pinch of salt
    • 2-3 tablespoons maple syrup or agave

  • For the filling:

    • 2 large sweet potatoes (roasted and mashed)
    • ½ cup coconut cream
    • ½ cup brown sugar or coconut sugar
    • Pinch of ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix almond flour, coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt until a crumbly dough forms. Press this into a pie pan and bake for 10 minutes.
  3. Blend roasted sweet potatoes, coconut cream, brown sugar, spices, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Pour the filling into the pre-baked crust, smoothing it evenly.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set. Let the pie cool completely before serving.

The sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes interacts beautifully with the tropical notes of coconut cream, providing a dessert that’s both wholesome and luxurious.

2. Classic Vegan Apple Pie

Apple pie is the epitome of comfort, and this vegan recipe ensures everyone can enjoy its warming spiced flavors. The plant-based version swaps out butter for a dairy-free option but retains its flaky crust and gooey apple filling.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose or whole wheat flour
    • ½ cup coconut oil or vegan butter (cold and solid)
    • 6 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 6 medium apples, chopped (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
    • ½ cup coconut sugar or maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust by mixing flour and coconut oil until it resembles coarse crumbs. Slowly add ice water until it forms a dough. Divide into two balls, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll one ball of dough and press it into a pie pan.
  3. Toss apples with sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Add the mixture to the crust.
  4. Roll the second dough ball into a circle and lay it over the pie, sealing edges and cutting slits for ventilation.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

The combination of tart apples and warming spices makes this pie irresistible—and a favorite for events year-round.

3. Savory Mushroom and Leek Pie

Vegan pies aren’t just about dessert; savory versions deliver satisfying dinners that leave you comfortably full. This mushroom and leek pie uses earthy ingredients for robust flavors paired with a flaky, golden crust.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 cups whole wheat or spelt flour
    • ½ cup olive oil
    • 6 tablespoons cold water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 2 cups sliced mushrooms (button, cremini, or shiitake)
    • 1 medium leek, thinly sliced
    • 1 cup unsweetened plant-based milk
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or arrowroot powder
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Prepare the crust by mixing flour and oil, then slowly add water to form a dough. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pan, sauté mushrooms and leeks with olive oil until tender. Add plant-based milk, cornstarch, thyme, salt, and pepper, cooking until thickened.
  3. Roll out the dough and press into a pie pan. Pour the mushroom-leek filling inside and cover with another rolled dough for the top crust.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is crisp and golden.

This hearty pie makes a perfect centerpiece for dinner, creating a dish that is both comforting and elegant.

4. Chocolate Avocado Cream Pie

Decadent chocolate desserts often rely on heavy creams, but this vegan chocolate avocado cream pie achieves the same luscious effect using nutrient-packed avocados. The result is creamy, rich, and indulgent—without dairy in sight!

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups vegan graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup melted coconut oil

  • For the filling:

    • 2 ripe avocados
    • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • ¾ cup maple syrup or agave
    • Pinch of salt
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Mix graham cracker crumbs and coconut oil, pressing them into a pie pan. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm.
  2. Blend avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, salt, and vanilla until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the filling into the chilled crust, spreading evenly. Refrigerate for 1-2 hours before serving.

This dessert surprises everyone with its combination of decadent flavor and healthy ingredients, proving that avocados are incredibly versatile.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Pies

Successfully making vegan pies requires just a few simple tips to elevate the results:

  1. Choose the right plant-based fats for the crust: Coconut oil and vegan butter help replicate the flakiness of traditional crusts. Keep them cold to achieve ideal texture.
  2. Be mindful of thickeners in your filling: Cornstarch, arrowroot powder, or even blended nuts like cashews help create creamy textures in both sweet and savory fillings.
  3. Don’t forget the spices: Flavorful spices, extracts, and fresh herbs enhance every pie, ensuring layers of complexity.

Celebrate Vegan Pies

With these recipes, vegan pies prove themselves as versatile, flavorful, and satisfying dishes that can cater to any occasion. Whether you’re indulging in a velvety chocolate avocado cream pie or delighting in the savory richness of mushroom and leek fillings, plant-based pies celebrate creativity and taste. Give these recipes a try, and prepare to be amazed at how wonderfully delicious vegan pies can be!
