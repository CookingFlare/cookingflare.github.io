Creepy Crawly Bug Cookies: A Delectable Halloween Treat


Halloween is right around the corner, and nothing says spooky season like a batch of creepy crawly bug cookies! These delightful treats are sure to elicit gasps and giggles from your guests, so get ready to bake up some frightful fun. With just a few simple ingredients and some creative decorating skills, you can create a plate of malicious maggots, venomous spiders, and oozy slime trail ants. Brace yourself for a baking adventure like no other!

Ingredients and Equipment

IngredientsEquipment
2 cups all-purpose flourBaking sheets
1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powderPiping bags
1 teaspoon baking powderSmall paintbrush
1/2 teaspoon saltFood coloring (black, red)
1 cup unsalted butter, softRolling pin
1 cup granulated sugarPrep surface (floured)
2 large eggsPastry brush (for egg wash)
2 teaspoons vanilla extractScissors
1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips(Optional) candy eyes

Directions

Step 1: Prepare your baking environment

Begin by gathering all your ingredients and equipment. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line your baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats.

Step 2: Mix the cookie dough

In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

In another bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, combining until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Fold in the chocolate chips.

Step 3: Roll and shape the cookies

Place a generous amount of flour on your prep surface and use your hands or a spatula to shape the dough into a large rectangle. Using a floured rolling pin, roll the dough out to about 1/4 inch thickness. Transfer the dough to a cutting mat and use assorted cookie cutters to create your bug shapes.

Transfer your cookie shapes to the prepared baking sheet using a spatula and place them at least 1 inch apart. Using a pastry brush, gently brush each cookie with the beaten egg to give it shine.

Step 4: Bake the cookies

Place the cookies in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until they’re firm to the touch. Carefully remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for at least 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 5: Decorate your creepy crawly bug cookies

Once your cookies are cool, it’s time for the fun part - decorating!

To create a spider, thin out some black icing and use a piping bag or a plastic bag with a small corner snipped off to pipe spindly legs and a big, round body. Add candy eyes if desired.

To make maggots or worms, pipe elongated curved shapes using black icing.

For ants with a slimy trail, pipe small ant shapes and then zig-zag lines of red icing connecting the ants, forming a trail as if they’re marching.

Feel free to be as creative as you like. You can also use small candies, sprinkles, or edible glitter to give your bug cookies extra flair.

Food Safety Tips

When handling raw dough, make sure to keep your work surfaces clean and sanitized, as well as your utensils, to avoid any cross-contamination or foodborne illnesses. Once your cookies are baked and cooled, they can be stored in an airtight container for up to a week.

Conclusion

Creepy Crawly Bug Cookies will have you and your little ghouls giggling while indulging in the sweet taste of Halloween. With their dark chocolate appearance and deliciously tangy flavor, these adorable bug-shaped treats are the perfect accompaniment to your spooky feast. Get ready to add this delectable little trinity of insects to your menu, and watch as your friends and family are delighted by their unexpected - yet absolutely delightful - taste!

Remember to keep things creepy and kooky, mysterious and spooky, with a touch of altogether ooky – happy baking!
