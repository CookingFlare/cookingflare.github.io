One Pot Vegetable Lo Mein


Lo Mein is a popular Chinese dish that combines noodles with various vegetables and a savory sauce. It’s a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. In this recipe, we will be making a delicious and nutritious One Pot Vegetable Lo Mein that is not only easy to prepare but also requires minimal clean-up. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 8 oz lo mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional for a non-vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce (optional for some spice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the lo mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Add the bell pepper, carrot, broccoli, snap peas, and mushrooms to the pot. Stir-fry for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sesame oil, and sriracha sauce (if using).

  5. Pour the sauce mixture over the vegetables in the pot, and stir well to coat everything evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

  6. Add the cooked lo mein noodles to the pot, and toss gently to combine with the vegetables and sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the noodles are heated through.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning and sauce according to your preference.

  8. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped green onions.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to add other vegetables like bok choy, cabbage, or snow peas to make it even more colorful and nutritious.

  • For a protein-packed version, you can add tofu, shrimp, chicken, or beef. Simply cook the protein separately and add it to the pot along with the noodles.

  • If you prefer a gluten-free option, use gluten-free soy sauce and rice noodles instead of lo mein noodles.

  • Customize the level of spiciness by adjusting the amount of sriracha sauce or adding crushed red pepper flakes.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop before serving.

Conclusion

This One Pot Vegetable Lo Mein recipe is a quick and easy way to enjoy a flavorful and satisfying meal packed with colorful vegetables. With minimal prep and clean-up, it’s perfect for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. Feel free to experiment with different vegetables and seasonings to make it your own. So go ahead, whip up a batch of this delicious lo mein and indulge in a taste of Asia right in your own kitchen!
