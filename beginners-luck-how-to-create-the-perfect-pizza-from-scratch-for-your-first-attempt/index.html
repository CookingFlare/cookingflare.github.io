Beginner's Luck: How to Create the Perfect Pizza from Scratch for Your First Attempt


Are you eager to rustle up a delicious homemade pizza on your first attempt? You’ve come to the right place! I shall reveal a simple, yet effective method in creating the perfect pizza from scratch. Owning a pizza stone and a pizza peel may give you that professional edge, but don’t fret if you don’t have these handy. With a regular baking sheet, you’ll still create a drool-worthy pie in no time.

Before we begin, let’s gather some essential ingredients for our homemade pizza dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 package (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

And for the sauce, gather the following:

  • 1 can (14.5 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the toppings, be creative! Opt for any combinations of the following:

  • Grilled chicken
  • Crumbled sausage
  • Pepperoni
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Sliced bell peppers
  • Sliced mushrooms
  • Olives
  • Sliced onions
  • Sliced black olives
  • Pineapple chunks

Now, let’s begin our pizza-making journey!

  1. Mix the Dough

First, let’s start by making the dough. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Once well mixed, slowly pour in the warm water and olive oil. Stir the mixture using a wooden spoon until it forms into a shaggy dough. Using your hands, knead the dough on a lightly-floured surface for approximately 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels a bit sticky, sprinkle in some extra flour and continue kneading.

  1. First Rise

After kneading, place your precious dough in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover it with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise in a warm place for about one hour or until it doubles in size. Now’s the time to prep the ingredients for the sauce and toppings.

  1. Prepare the Sauce

In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for a minute until it becomes fragrant. Toss in the diced tomatoes and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the sauce reaches your preferred consistency, add fresh basil, salt, and pepper. Let it cool before applying it to your dough.

  1. Assemble the Pizza

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a lightly-floured surface. Using your hands, gently stretch or roll the dough into a 12-inch circle. Transfer the pizza dough onto your pizza peel or a baking sheet. Brush the outer edges of the dough with a little olive oil.

  1. The Final Touches

Now, spread an even layer of your delicious sauce on the dough, leaving a 1-inch gap around the edges. Add your favorite toppings, ensuring that the cheese goes on last. Be generous with it, as nothing beats a bubbly, melted layer of cheese draping off the sides of a freshly baked pizza.

  1. The Final Stretch

If you have a pizza stone, place it in your preheated oven (at 425°F) for at least 30 minutes. This allows it to heat up adequately. Slide your beautifully-assembled pizza directly onto the hot stone, or simply transfer the baking sheet into the oven. Bake the pizza for 15-20 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown, and the cheese bubbles gently.

  1. Cooling and Cutting

Carefully remove your masterpiece from the oven. Using a spatula or pizza cutter, slice the pizza into your preferred sizes, and let it rest for a few minutes to allow it to settle. Now, dig in and relish the flavor of your homemade pizza creation!

Remember, practice makes perfect. Each pizza you create will only improve, and soon, you’ll be hailed as the next pizza-making wizard! Enjoy your cheesy endeavor and share your delicious creations with friends and family. Good luck and happy cooking!
