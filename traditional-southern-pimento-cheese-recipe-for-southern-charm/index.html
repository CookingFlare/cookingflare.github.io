Traditional Southern Pimento Cheese Recipe for Southern Charm


If there’s one dish that embodies the heart and soul of Southern cuisine, it’s pimento cheese. This classic Southern staple is a versatile and beloved spread that can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. Whether it’s slathered on crackers, stuffed into a grilled cheese sandwich, or simply enjoyed on its own, pimento cheese is a true Southern comfort food that never fails to delight.

The History of Pimento Cheese

Pimento cheese has deep roots in Southern culinary traditions. The combination of sharp cheddar cheese, sweet pimentos, and creamy mayonnaise is a testament to the simple yet delicious flavors that have been cherished in the South for generations. Originally, pimento cheese was a way for Southern homemakers to make use of surplus cheese and pimentos, creating a spread that was both economical and delicious.

The Ingredients

To make a traditional Southern pimento cheese, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, freshly grated
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 4-ounce jar of diced pimentos, drained
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

The Preparation

  1. Start by grating the sharp cheddar cheese. Freshly grated cheese is essential for achieving the perfect texture and flavor in your pimento cheese.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated cheddar cheese, diced pimentos, mayonnaise, onion powder, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper. Mix the ingredients together until they are well combined and the cheese is evenly coated with the mayonnaise and seasonings.

  3. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Keep in mind that the sharp cheddar cheese already adds a salty quality, so be sure to taste as you season.

  4. Once the pimento cheese is well mixed and seasoned to your liking, cover the bowl and refrigerate the mixture for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld together.

Ways to Enjoy Pimento Cheese

Now that you’ve made a batch of traditional Southern pimento cheese, it’s time to savor its deliciousness. Here are a few classic ways to enjoy this beloved spread:

Pimento Cheese Sandwich

Spread a generous amount of pimento cheese between two slices of fresh white bread, then grill the sandwich until the cheese is melted and the bread is golden brown. The result is a gooey, savory delight that’s perfect for lunch or a quick and comforting dinner.

Pimento Cheese Dip

For a crowd-pleasing party appetizer, serve the pimento cheese as a dip alongside crispy crackers, crunchy vegetables, or warm, toasted baguette slices. The creamy, tangy flavors of the pimento cheese pair beautifully with the crunch of the dippers.

Pimento Cheese Stuffed Celery

For a lighter, more refreshing way to enjoy pimento cheese, fill crisp celery sticks with the creamy spread. The combination of the savory pimento cheese and the fresh, crisp celery is a match made in Southern heaven.

In Conclusion

The appeal of traditional Southern pimento cheese lies in its simple yet irresistible flavors. This beloved Southern staple has stood the test of time and continues to be a cherished part of Southern culinary heritage. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of Southern comfort, pimento cheese is sure to bring a touch of Southern charm to any occasion. So, grab your ingredients, get mixing, and savor the delightful flavors of this classic Southern favorite.
