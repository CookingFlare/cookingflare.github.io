Seafood Skewers Perfect for BBQ


Few things capture the spirit of summer better than the sizzle of skewers on a beachside grill. When it comes to outdoor cooking, seafood skewers are a standout choice, offering vibrant flavors, quick grilling times, and an undeniable touch of elegance to your BBQ spread. Whether you’re a seasoned griller or a passionate beginner, this comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know to create seafood skewers that impress, delight, and satisfy.

Why Seafood Skewers?

Seafood is a versatile ingredient that lends itself beautifully to the skewer format. The natural sweetness of shrimp, the tender flakiness of fish, and the softness of scallops all combine with the crispy char of the grill, creating a symphony of textures and flavors. Skewering seafood also makes portioning simple and lends itself well to marinades and seasonings soaking in evenly.

Grilled skewers are perfect for gatherings because they’re easy to serve, visually appealing, and customizable to diverse palates. Let’s dive into crafting these culinary gems.

Choosing the Right Seafood for Skewers

The foundation of any great seafood skewer is the selection of seafood. Not all seafood performs equally well on the grill, so picking the right ingredients ensures delicious and safe results.

Best Seafood Choices for Skewers

  • Shrimp: Firm and slightly sweet, shrimp grill quickly and hold up well on skewers. Opt for medium to large sizes with shells removed and deveined.
  • Scallops: Their delicate sweetness pairs wonderfully with smoky char. Choose dry-packed sea scallops (not treated with phosphates) for the best texture.
  • Firm Fish Fillets: Fish like salmon, swordfish, halibut, tuna, and mahi-mahi hold together well when skewered. Avoid flaky fish like cod or tilapia as they tend to fall apart.
  • Calamari (Squid): When cooked properly, tender calamari tubes and tentacles add a beautiful chewiness and take marinades well.
  • Lobster or Crab Meat: For a luxurious twist, bite-sized chunks of lobster tail or cooked crab meat can be threaded onto skewers.

Where to Source Quality Seafood

For optimal flavor and freshness, buy your seafood at a reputable fishmonger. Ask for previously frozen products that have been thawed properly, as freshly caught seafood often spoils rapidly unless consumed immediately.

Preparing Your Seafood for Skewers

Cleaning and Cutting

  • Shrimp: Peel and devein, leaving tails on for presentation if desired.
  • Scallops: Rinse and pat dry.
  • Fish: Cut into uniform 1-inch cubes to allow even grilling.
  • Calamari: Slice tubes into rings or use small tentacles.
  • Lobster/Crab: Use pre-cooked bite-sized chunks.

Skewering Tips

  • Soak wooden skewers in water for at least 30 minutes before grilling to prevent burning.
  • For metal skewers, no soaking is necessary.
  • Space pieces loosely to promote even heat circulation and prevent overcrowding.

Marinades and Seasonings: Elevating Your Seafood Skewers

Marinades not only enhance flavor but also protect delicate seafood from drying out on the grill. Remember that seafood requires shorter marinating times compared to other proteins to maintain firmness.

Classic Marinade Components

  • Acid: Lemon juice, lime juice, or vinegar help tenderize and add brightness.
  • Oil: Olive oil or avocado oil provide moisture and help distribute flavors.
  • Aromatics & Herbs: Garlic, ginger, parsley, cilantro, dill, thyme, and basil add complexity.
  • Spices: Paprika, cayenne, black pepper, and cumin introduce subtle heat and smokiness.
  • Sweetness: Honey, maple syrup, or brown sugar balance acidity.

Sample Marinade Recipes

Mediterranean Citrus Marinade

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Asian-Inspired Soy-Ginger Glaze

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)

Marinating Tips

  • Marinate seafood for 15-30 minutes. Over-marinating can lead to mushy textures.
  • For scallops and firm fish, 20 minutes is usually plenty.
  • Keep marinating seafood refrigerated to maintain freshness.

The Grilling Process: Mastering BBQ Seafood Skewers

Setting Up Your Grill

  • Preheat the grill to medium-high heat (around 375–450°F / 190–232°C).
  • Use clean grates to prevent sticking.
  • Oil the grill grates lightly with a cloth dipped in oil held by tongs.

Cooking Times and Techniques

  • Shrimp: Grill for 2-3 minutes per side until opaque and pink.
  • Scallops: Around 3 minutes per side until firm and opaque.
  • Fish Cubes: 3-4 minutes per side until grill marks appear and fish flakes easily.
  • Calamari: 1-2 minutes per side to avoid toughness.
  • Lobster/Crab: If pre-cooked, heat through for 1-2 minutes per side.

Tips for Perfect Grill Marks and Doneness

  • Resist turning too frequently; flip only once to prevent breaking.
  • Use a grill thermometer if possible to monitor temperature.
  • Avoid overcooking — seafood becomes rubbery fast.
  • Basting with leftover marinade or melted butter while grilling adds extra flavor and moisture.

Serving Suggestions and Pairings

Seafood skewers are a versatile dish that pairs well with a variety of sides and sauces.

Recommended Side Dishes

  • Grilled Vegetables: Bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, and red onions complement seafood beautifully when skewered alongside.
  • Fresh Salads: Crisp mixed greens, cucumber and tomato salad, or a grain salad with quinoa or farro balance the rich flavors.
  • Rice or Couscous: Lightly herbed rice pilaf or fluffy couscous absorb drippings and add substance.
  • Crusty Bread: A fresh baguette or garlic bread helps mop up juices.

Sauces to Elevate

  • Tzatziki: Creamy cucumber-yogurt sauce for a cool contrast.
  • Chimichurri: Herbaceous and tangy Argentine sauce pairs well with grilled seafood.
  • Spicy Aioli: Garlic mayonnaise with a kick of cayenne or smoked paprika.
  • Mango Salsa: Sweet and tangy fruit salsa introduces refreshing tropical notes.

Frequently Asked Questions About Seafood Skewers

Can I prepare seafood skewers in advance?

You can marinate seafood skewers a few hours ahead and keep them refrigerated. However, for best grilling results, cook them fresh to avoid sogginess.

How to prevent seafood from sticking to the grill?

Make sure the grill is well oiled and clean. Coat seafood lightly with oil before grilling, and avoid flipping too early.

Are metal skewers better than wooden skewers?

Metal skewers heat up more quickly and can help cook seafood from the inside, but they get hot and require oven mitts to handle. Wooden skewers are safer to handle but need soaking to prevent burning.

Can I mix different seafood on one skewer?

Yes, but try to pair items with similar cooking times to avoid some pieces overcooking while others are underdone.

A Few Creative Seafood Skewers Recipes to Try

1. Lemon Herb Garlic Shrimp Skewers

  • Shrimp marinated in lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, parsley, and thyme.
  • Serve with a side of grilled asparagus and tzatziki.

2. Spicy Cajun Fish and Veggie Skewers

  • Firm fish cubes seasoned with Cajun spice mix, threaded with bell peppers and onions.
  • Serve alongside cilantro-lime rice and a dollop of spicy aioli.

3. Asian-Style Scallop and Calamari Skewers

  • Marinate scallops and calamari rings in soy-ginger glaze.
  • Grill quickly and finish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds.
  • Serve with coconut rice and mango salsa.

Final Thoughts

Mastering seafood skewers opens up a world of BBQ possibilities full of fresh, vibrant flavors and an appealing presentation. With the right choice of seafood, balanced marinades, and attentive grilling, these skewers can be the crown jewel of any cookout or dinner party.

Fire up the grill, savor the aromas, and delight your friends and family with a delicious seafood feast that’s as fun to make as it is to eat!

Ready to fire up the grill? Try out these techniques and personalize your seafood skewers with your favorite herbs, spices, and sides. Let the smoky, succulent goodness begin!
