Homemade Rosemary Olive Oil Bread: Aromatic and Delicious


There’s something truly special about the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the kitchen. It’s a scent that evokes warmth, comfort, and the promise of something delicious. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making Homemade Rosemary Olive Oil Bread, a delightful and aromatic loaf that’s perfect for sharing with loved ones or savoring on your own.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This indicates that the yeast is active and ready to be used.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and chopped rosemary. Create a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook for this step.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Loaf

Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Shape it into a round or oval loaf, depending on your preference. Place the shaped dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Step 6: Second Rise

Cover the shaped dough with a kitchen towel and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) during this time.

Step 7: Bake the Bread

Before baking, you can make shallow slashes on the surface of the dough to create a decorative pattern. This also helps the bread expand while baking. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 8: Cool and Enjoy

Once the bread is baked, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Allow it to cool for at least 30 minutes before slicing. This allows the crumb structure to set, ensuring a perfect texture. Serve the Homemade Rosemary Olive Oil Bread with a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, or alongside your favorite soups and salads.

Conclusion

The process of making Homemade Rosemary Olive Oil Bread is a truly rewarding experience. From the moment you activate the yeast to the final golden-brown loaf, the aromas and flavors that fill your kitchen are simply irresistible. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a novice, this recipe offers a wonderful opportunity to explore the art of breadmaking and savor the delightful results. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a journey to create a loaf of aromatic and delicious Homemade Rosemary Olive Oil Bread. Your senses will thank you.
