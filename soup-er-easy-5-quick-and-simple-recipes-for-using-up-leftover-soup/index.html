Soup-er Easy: 5 Quick and Simple Recipes for Using Up Leftover Soup


Are you tired of having leftover soup sitting in your fridge, waiting to be eaten before it goes bad? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this post, we’ll share five quick and simple recipes that will transform your leftover soup into delicious and satisfying meals. So grab your ladle and let’s get cooking!

1. Soup-Stuffed Bell Peppers

Turn your leftover soup into a hearty and flavorful filling for bell peppers. Start by slicing off the tops of the bell peppers and removing the seeds and membranes. Fill each pepper with your leftover soup, making sure to pack it in tightly. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and bake at 375°F for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender. Serve with a sprinkle of shredded cheese on top for an extra touch of indulgence.

2. Soup and Pasta

If you have a creamy or tomato-based soup, why not turn it into a pasta dish? Cook your favorite pasta according to package instructions. Drain the pasta and add it to a pan along with your leftover soup. Heat everything together until the soup coats the pasta evenly. You can also add some cooked vegetables or protein like chicken or shrimp for a complete meal. Top with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs for added flavor.

3. Soup Dumplings

Transform your leftover soup into savory dumplings that will impress your family and friends. Start by making or buying dumpling wrappers. Place a small spoonful of soup in the center of each wrapper and fold it in half, sealing the edges tightly. Steam the dumplings for 10-12 minutes until they are cooked through. Serve with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and sesame oil. These bite-sized treats are perfect for snacking or as an appetizer.

4. Soup Bread Bowl

Give your leftover soup a fun twist by serving it in a bread bowl. Choose a round loaf of bread, such as a sourdough or Italian loaf. Cut off the top and hollow out the center, leaving a thick shell. Pour your reheated soup into the bread bowl and sprinkle some grated cheese on top. Place the bread bowl on a baking sheet and bake at 350°F for 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Scoop out the soup along with the bread for a satisfying and delicious meal.

5. Soup Croquettes

Turn your leftover soup into crispy and flavorful croquettes. Start by chilling your soup in the refrigerator until it becomes firm. Using a spoon or an ice cream scoop, portion out the soup and shape it into small balls or cylinders. Roll each croquette in breadcrumbs and refrigerate for 30 minutes to set. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the croquettes until golden brown and crispy. Serve with a dipping sauce of your choice, such as ranch dressing or marinara sauce.

And there you have it! Five creative and delicious ways to use up your leftover soup. Don’t let that precious liquid go to waste. With a little creativity, you can transform it into completely new and exciting dishes. So the next time you find yourself with leftover soup, don’t fret. Instead, turn it into something extraordinary and enjoy the satisfaction of a wonderful meal. Happy cooking!
