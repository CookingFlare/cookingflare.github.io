How to Make Decadent Nutella Tart from Scratch


Tartes and tarts have been a staple in French patisserie for centuries, and for good reason. The combination of a flaky crust, a rich filling, and a delicate balance of flavors is a match made in heaven. In this recipe, we’re going to take the classic tart to the next level by incorporating the rich, chocolatey goodness of Nutella. The result is a decadent Nutella tart that’s sure to impress even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water

For the Nutella filling:

  • 1 cup Nutella
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped hazelnuts (optional)

Instructions

Crust

To make the crust, combine the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt in a large bowl. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it a few times until it becomes smooth and pliable. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes.

Nutella Filling

To make the Nutella filling, combine the Nutella, heavy cream, softened butter, vanilla extract, granulated sugar, and chopped hazelnuts (if using) in a medium bowl. Whisk until smooth and creamy.

Assembly and Baking

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom and press it into the corners of the pan.

Trim the excess dough from the edges and prick the bottom of the crust with a fork to prevent it from bubbling up during baking. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans.

Bake the crust for 15 minutes, then remove the parchment paper and pie weights or beans. Continue baking for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

Remove the crust from the oven and let it cool slightly. Pour the Nutella filling into the cooled crust and smooth the top with an offset spatula.

Return the tart to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

Serving

Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate the tart for at least 2 hours or overnight to allow the filling to set.

When ready to serve, remove the tart from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes. Slice and serve with a sprinkle of confectioners’ sugar and a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To ensure a flaky crust, make sure to keep the butter cold and the water ice-cold.
  • If you don’t have hazelnuts, you can omit them or substitute with chopped almonds or pecans.
  • To make the tart more decadent, drizzle with chocolate sauce or caramel before serving.
  • Consider using different flavors of Nutella, such as peanut butter or caramel, to create a unique twist on the classic recipe.

Conclusion

With this recipe, you’ll be able to create a decadent Nutella tart that’s sure to impress your friends and family. The combination of a flaky crust, a rich Nutella filling, and a delicate balance of flavors is a match made in heaven. So go ahead, indulge in this rich and creamy treat, and enjoy every bite of this delicious Nutella tart.
