Caramel Apple Hand Pies: A Fun and Festive Dessert


Who doesn’t love the combination of sweet caramel and tart apples? When these flavors come together in a handheld pie, it’s pure magic. Caramel apple hand pies are the perfect treat for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy autumn evening or a festive holiday gathering. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make these delectable treats from scratch.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • ½ cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 large apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ cup caramel sauce

For the topping:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, and wrap in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Make the filling: In a medium bowl, combine the diced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Stir until the apples are evenly coated. Set aside.

  3. Roll out the dough: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disk of dough to a thickness of about ⅛ inch. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles, about 4 inches in diameter.

  4. Assemble the hand pies: Place about 1 tablespoon of the apple filling in the center of each dough circle. Drizzle a teaspoon of caramel sauce over the filling. Fold the dough in half, sealing the edges with a fork. Repeat this process with the remaining dough and filling.

  5. Bake the hand pies: Transfer the assembled hand pies to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops of the pies with beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar. Make a small slit on the top of each pie to allow steam to escape during baking. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pies are golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  6. Serve and enjoy: Allow the hand pies to cool for a few minutes before serving. These caramel apple hand pies are delicious on their own, but you can also serve them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra treat.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different types of apples for different flavor profiles. Granny Smith apples provide a tartness that pairs well with the sweetness of the caramel, while Honeycrisp apples add a natural sweetness to the filling.
  • Drizzle additional caramel sauce over the baked hand pies for an extra indulgent touch.
  • If you prefer a more rustic look, you can fold the edges of the dough over the filling instead of using a fork to seal the pies.
  • These hand pies can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat them in the oven before serving.

So there you have it – a recipe for caramel apple hand pies that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. These handheld treats are perfect for any occasion, and their sweet, tart, and buttery flavors will leave you craving more. Give this recipe a try and let us know how it turns out. Happy baking!
