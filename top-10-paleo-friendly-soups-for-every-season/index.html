Top 10 Paleo-Friendly Soups for Every Season


Soup is one of the most versatile and comforting dishes, and when it comes to the paleo diet, it’s easy to create flavorful and nutritious recipes. The paleo diet, focused on whole foods like meats, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds while excluding grains, dairy, and processed foods, aligns perfectly with the comforting nature of soups. Whether you’re in the middle of a hot summer day or curling up by the fire on a chilly winter evening, there’s a paleo-friendly soup for every season.

Here, we’ve compiled a list of the top 10 paleo-friendly soups that are perfect for all year round. From hearty winter meals to refreshing summer broths, each recipe is designed to fuel your body with nutrient-dense ingredients while keeping your taste buds satisfied.

1. Hearty Butternut Squash Soup (Fall Favorite)

Butternut squash soup is a crowd-pleaser that brings the flavors of fall right to your kitchen. This velvety-smooth soup is naturally sweet and creamy, making it perfect for those who crave comfort without the dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 4 cups chicken broth (make sure it’s paleo-friendly)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash and cook for about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes until the squash is tender.
  4. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can transfer the soup to a blender in batches.
  5. Stir in cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper to taste. Serve hot.

This paleo soup is perfect for autumn, when butternut squash is in season and the temperatures begin to drop.

2. Creamy Chicken and Mushroom Soup (Winter Comfort)

During the colder months, nothing beats the warmth of a creamy soup. This paleo-friendly creamy chicken and mushroom soup is made without dairy but still delivers a rich and indulgent texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth (paleo-approved)
  • 1/2 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and mushrooms. Sauté until the mushrooms release their moisture and soften.
  2. Add the chicken broth, shredded chicken, and thyme. Bring the soup to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

This creamy, savory soup will keep you warm and full through the chilly winter months.

3. Tomato Basil Soup (Spring Refresh)

Nothing says spring like the bright and refreshing flavor of tomatoes. This paleo tomato basil soup is light, flavorful, and packed with fresh ingredients, making it perfect for the transitional weather of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cups vegetable broth (paleo-approved)
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook for 5 minutes, allowing them to break down.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Add the fresh basil and season with salt and pepper. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with additional basil leaves.

This simple yet delicious soup is perfect for a light lunch or dinner during the warmer months of spring.

4. Paleo Beef and Vegetable Soup (Autumn Hearty Meal)

When the weather starts to cool down, a hearty and filling soup is just what you need. This paleo beef and vegetable soup is packed with protein and a variety of colorful vegetables, making it an excellent choice for fall.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound grass-fed ground beef
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth (paleo-friendly)
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  2. Add the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables begin to soften.
  3. Add the zucchini, beef broth, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  4. Let the soup simmer for 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve hot with fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish.

This soup is a filling and nourishing meal, perfect for when you’re craving a substantial dish on a crisp fall day.

5. Paleo Pumpkin Soup (Fall/Winter Comfort)

Pumpkin soup is another seasonal favorite that fits perfectly into the fall and winter months. The sweet and savory flavors make this soup incredibly satisfying, and it’s an easy recipe to whip up on a chilly day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (fresh or canned, ensure it’s just pumpkin)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth (paleo-friendly)
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the pumpkin puree, chicken broth, and spices to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  3. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or transfer to a blender until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

This paleo-friendly pumpkin soup is a great choice for those seeking a warm, comforting dish during the fall and winter months.

6. Zucchini Noodle Soup (Spring and Summer Freshness)

This zucchini noodle soup is a perfect way to enjoy a refreshing soup during the warmer months. The zucchini noodles take the place of traditional pasta, making it a light yet satisfying option that’s perfect for spring and summer.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth (paleo-friendly)
  • 1 cup chopped kale or spinach
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, and carrots and sauté for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken or vegetable broth to the pot and bring it to a simmer. Let it cook for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the zucchini noodles and kale to the soup and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.

This is a light, nutritious soup that’s perfect for enjoying during the warmer months when you want something fresh and vibrant.

7. Coconut Curry Soup (Winter Indulgence)

If you’re craving something warm and spicy, this coconut curry soup is perfect for the colder months. The creamy coconut milk combined with the bold flavors of curry spices creates a rich and comforting dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 4 cups chicken broth (paleo-approved)
  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Add the curry powder and stir for another minute.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and coconut milk, then add the cauliflower florets. Bring the soup to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

This coconut curry soup is rich, flavorful, and perfect for warming up on a cold winter evening.

8. Paleo Chicken and Vegetable Soup (Year-Round Comfort)

This chicken and vegetable soup is a classic comfort food that’s easy to make and perfect for any time of the year. Packed with lean protein and vegetables, it’s a great way to fuel your body with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken broth (paleo-friendly)
  • 2 cups spinach or kale, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken broth, carrots, celery, and onions. Bring to a simmer and cook until the vegetables are tender.
  2. Add the shredded chicken and spinach or kale. Cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Season with salt and pepper and serve immediately.

This simple, yet satisfying soup is a great option for a quick lunch or dinner, no matter the season.

9. Paleo Carrot and Ginger Soup (Spring and Fall)

Carrot and ginger soup is a vibrant and flavorful option that’s perfect for both spring and fall. The sweetness of the carrots pairs beautifully with the warmth of ginger, creating a soup that’s both soothing and energizing.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (paleo-approved)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger, cooking until softened.
  2. Add the carrots and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 20-25 minutes, until the carrots are tender.
  3. Blend the soup using an immersion blender until smooth. Season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

This refreshing soup is perfect for spring but can also be a wonderful addition to your fall meal rotation.

10. Spicy Sweet Potato Soup (Winter Hearty)

Sweet potatoes are packed with nutrients and flavor, making them a great addition to any soup. This spicy sweet potato soup combines the natural sweetness of the potatoes with a kick of spice for a balanced, satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken broth (paleo-friendly)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onions and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the sweet potatoes, chicken broth, chili powder, and cayenne pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20-25 minutes.
  3. Blend the soup until smooth and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or a drizzle of lime juice.

This spicy sweet potato soup is an excellent choice for warming up during the colder months of winter.

These top 10 paleo-friendly soups are perfect for keeping you cozy and nourished all year round. Whether you’re craving a light, refreshing broth or a hearty, creamy dish, these recipes are packed with flavor and nutrients that fit perfectly into your paleo lifestyle. Enjoy!
