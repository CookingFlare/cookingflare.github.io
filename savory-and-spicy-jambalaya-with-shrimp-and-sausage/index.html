Savory and Spicy Jambalaya with Shrimp and Sausage


If you’re looking for a dish that is bursting with flavors, look no further than this savory and spicy Jambalaya with shrimp and sausage. Originating from the vibrant Cajun and Creole cuisine of Louisiana, Jambalaya is a one-pot wonder that combines rice, meat, and an array of aromatic spices. This recipe is sure to transport your taste buds to the heart of the bayou!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the sausage slices and cook until browned. Remove the sausage from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes (including the juice) and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Add the rice, Cajun seasoning, dried thyme, dried oregano, paprika, and cayenne pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the Jambalaya simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the liquid. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. While the Jambalaya is simmering, season the shrimp with salt and pepper. In a separate pan, cook the shrimp until pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  7. Once the rice is cooked, add the cooked sausage and shrimp to the pot. Stir gently to incorporate everything together.

  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and additional Cajun seasoning if desired.

  9. Serve the Jambalaya hot, garnished with chopped green onions for a fresh and vibrant finish.

Enjoy the Flavors of the Bayou!

This savory and spicy Jambalaya with shrimp and sausage is a delicious and satisfying meal that brings the vibrant flavors of Louisiana right to your plate. The combination of tender shrimp, smoky sausage, and aromatic spices creates a mouthwatering dish that will have your taste buds dancing with joy.

The beauty of Jambalaya lies in its versatility. Feel free to add your favorite vegetables, such as okra or tomatoes, to make it your own. You can also adjust the spice level to suit your taste buds by adding more or less cayenne pepper.

Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the South, this Jambalaya recipe is sure to impress. So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the bold and flavorful magic of Jambalaya!
