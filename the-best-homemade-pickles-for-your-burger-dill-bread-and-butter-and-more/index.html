The Best Homemade Pickles for Your Burger: Dill, Bread and Butter, and More


When it comes to a delicious burger, the toppings can make or break the experience. Among the myriad of toppings you can choose from, pickles hold a special place. Their crunch, tang, and flavor elevate any burger from ordinary to extraordinary. In this guide, we explore the world of homemade pickles, focusing on the most popular varieties that will take your burger game to the next level: Dill pickles, Bread and Butter pickles, and other delightful options.

Why Homemade Pickles?

Making pickles at home not only allows you to customize flavors to your liking, but it also guarantees freshness. Store-bought pickles can be loaded with preservatives and artificial flavors, while homemade versions are made from fresh ingredients, providing an authentic taste. Plus, the process of making pickles is simple and rewarding.

Essential Ingredients for Pickling

Before diving into specific recipes, let’s look at the essential ingredients you’ll need for pickling:

  • Cucumbers: The star of the show. Look for pickling cucumbers, which are shorter and have fewer seeds.
  • Vinegar: Distilled white vinegar or apple cider vinegar are popular choices.
  • Salt: Pickling salt or kosher salt is best as it dissolves easily and doesn’t contain additives.
  • Sugar: Often used in sweeter pickle recipes.
  • Spices: Black peppercorns, mustard seeds, dill seeds, garlic, and red pepper flakes are common picks.

Dill Pickles: A Classic Choice

Dill pickles are a timeless favorite—crunchy, tangy, and bursting with flavor. They complement burgers perfectly, adding a refreshing bite.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pickling cucumbers, quartered
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup pickling salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons dill seeds
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cucumbers: Wash the cucumbers and cut them into quarters or spears, depending on your preference.
  2. Make the Brine: In a pot, combine the water, vinegar, pickling salt, and sugar (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the salt and sugar dissolve.
  3. Add Spices: Remove the brine from heat. Add the garlic, dill seeds, mustard seeds, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Pack the Jars: Place the cucumber spears into sterilized jars. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers until they are fully submerged, leaving about half an inch of headspace.
  5. Seal and Store: Seal the jars with lids and let them cool to room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate the pickles for at least 24 hours before using. For optimal flavor, let them sit for a week!

Bread and Butter Pickles: A Sweet Twist

Bread and Butter pickles offer a delightful sweetness that contrasts beautifully with savory burgers. Their unique flavor profile makes them a must-try!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pickling cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup sliced onions (optional)
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup pickling salt
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric (for color)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cucumbers: In a large bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers and onions. Sprinkle with pickling salt and let sit for 1-2 hours to draw out moisture.
  2. Make the Brine: In a saucepan, mix the vinegar, sugar, celery seeds, mustard seeds, and turmeric. Bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Combine: Drain the cucumber mixture and rinse under cold water. Pack the cucumbers and onions into sterilized jars.
  4. Pour the Brine: Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, ensuring they are fully covered.
  5. Seal and Refrigerate: Seal the jars and let them cool. Refrigerate for at least 24 hours before enjoying, though a week is ideal for maximum flavor.

Spicy Pickles: Adding a Kick

If you love a little heat, spicy pickles can add an exciting dimension to your burgers.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pickling cucumbers, cut into spears
  • 1 cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup pickling salt
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2-3 fresh jalapeños, sliced (adjust to your heat preference)
  • 1 tablespoon dill seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cucumbers: Cut the cucumbers into spears and set aside.
  2. Make the Brine: In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, and pickling salt. Bring to a boil.
  3. Add Heat: Add garlic, jalapeños, dill seeds, peppercorns, and red pepper flakes to the brine.
  4. Pack the Jars: Place the cucumber spears in sterilized jars and pour the hot brine over them.
  5. Seal and Store: Seal the jars and let them cool before refrigerating. Allow them to sit for at least two days for the flavors to meld.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Pickles

  1. Use Fresh Produce: The better the cucumbers, the tastier your pickles will be. Choose firm, unblemished cucumbers.
  2. Experiment with Spices: Feel free to add different spices based on your taste preferences. Coriander seeds, allspice, and even bay leaves can enhance the flavor.
  3. Sterilize Jars: Always sterilize your jars to avoid any contamination. A simple wash in hot, soapy water followed by a rinse in boiling water will do the trick.
  4. Adjust Sweetness and Saltiness: If you prefer less sweetness or saltiness, adjust the sugar and salt in your recipes accordingly.

Storing Your Pickles

Homemade pickles can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a few months. The flavors will continue to develop over time, making them even tastier! Just ensure they remain submerged in the brine to prevent spoilage.

Serving Suggestions

Now that you have an array of homemade pickles, it’s time to elevate your burgers. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Classic Cheeseburger: Top your cheeseburger with dill pickles for that quintessential taste.
  • Spicy Chicken Burger: Pair spicy pickles with a fried or grilled chicken burger for an extra kick.
  • Veggie Burger: Add Bread and Butter pickles to a veggie burger to complement the earthy flavors.

Conclusion

Homemade pickles are not just a condiment; they’re a way to express your culinary creativity. Whether you prefer the classic crunch of dill pickles or the sweet tang of Bread and Butter varieties, making your own pickles allows you to customize flavors that perfectly match your burgers. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create the best homemade pickles that will have your friends and family asking for seconds. Happy pickling!
