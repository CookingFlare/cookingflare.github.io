Delicious Greek Spanakopita: A Spinach and Feta Delight


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious recipe for Greek Spanakopita. Spanakopita is a traditional Greek dish made with layers of flaky phyllo pastry, a rich spinach and feta cheese filling, and a hint of aromatic herbs. It’s a classic dish that is loved by many and is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

To make this delectable Greek Spanakopita, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 package of frozen phyllo pastry sheets, thawed
  • 2 pounds of fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup of ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Now that you have gathered all the necessary ingredients, let’s dive into the step-by-step instructions for making this scrumptious Greek Spanakopita:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet in batches, stirring until it wilts down. Cook for about 5 minutes until the excess moisture has evaporated.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked spinach, crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, chopped dill, chopped parsley, and salt and pepper to taste. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  5. Take a sheet of phyllo pastry and brush it with olive oil. Place another sheet on top and repeat the process until you have a stack of 5 to 7 sheets.

  6. Spread a generous amount of the spinach and cheese filling onto the phyllo pastry stack, leaving about an inch of space around the edges.

  7. Carefully roll the phyllo pastry into a log, tucking in the edges as you go. Place the rolled spanakopita seam-side down into the greased baking dish.

  8. Repeat the process with the remaining phyllo pastry sheets and filling until you have used up all the ingredients. You should have several rolls of spanakopita in the baking dish.

  9. Brush the top of the spanakopita rolls with olive oil, ensuring they are well coated.

  10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 35-40 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and crisp.

  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Spanakopita is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed as an appetizer, side dish, or even a main course. It pairs well with a fresh Greek salad, tzatziki sauce, or a dollop of Greek yogurt. Serve it warm or at room temperature for the best flavor.

Enjoy!

There you have it, a mouthwatering recipe for Delicious Greek Spanakopita. This traditional Greek dish is a true delight, with its flaky phyllo pastry, savory spinach and feta filling, and aromatic herbs. It’s a crowd-pleaser that will transport your taste buds to the beautiful shores of Greece. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and enjoy the flavors of the Mediterranean in your own kitchen!
