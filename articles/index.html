Hearty Beef Barbacoa: A Slow-Cooked Mexican Marvel

Barbacoa, a traditional Mexican dish, has been a staple in many Latin American households for generations. This slow-cooked beef recipe is a true delight, with tender chunks of beef simmered in a rich, flavorful broth, accompanied by a medley of aromatic spices and ingredients. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making Hearty Beef Barbacoa, a dish that’s sure to become a family favorite. Ingredients 2 pounds beef brisket or beef shank, cut into 2-inch pieces 1 large onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 medium tomatoes, diced 1 jalapeño pepper, diced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 cups beef broth 2 cups water 2 tablespoons lard or vegetable oil 2 bay leaves 2 sprigs fresh oregano 2 sprigs fresh cilantro Instructions In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the lard or oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside. Add the onion to the pot and cook until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 8 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the diced tomatoes, jalapeño, cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, salt, and pepper to the pot. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly. Add the beef broth, water, bay leaves, oregano, and cilantro to the pot. Stir to combine, then add the browned beef back to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, or until the beef is tender and falls apart easily. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Remove the bay leaves and oregano sprigs from the pot. Shred the beef with two forks and return it to the pot. Simmer the mixture over low heat for an additional 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve the Hearty Beef Barbacoa hot, garnished with fresh cilantro, onion, and lime wedges. Tips and Variations For a more intense flavor, use beef short ribs or beef shank instead of brisket. Add some acidity to the dish by squeezing a lime wedge over the top before serving. Serve the Barbacoa with warm flour tortillas, fresh cilantro, and a dollop of sour cream for a delicious and satisfying meal. Experiment with different types of peppers, such as Anaheim or Poblano, for a unique flavor profile. Make the dish ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze it for up to 3 days. Reheat it over low heat, adding more broth or water if necessary, until the beef is tender and the flavors are rich and full-bodied. Conclusion Hearty Beef Barbacoa is a slow-cooked Mexican marvel that’s sure to become a family favorite. With its tender chunks of beef, rich broth, and aromatic spices, this dish is perfect for a cold winter’s night or a special occasion. By following these simple steps and experimenting with different ingredients and variations, you’ll be able to create a dish that’s truly unique and delicious.
