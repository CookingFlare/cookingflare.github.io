Homemade Baguettes: Crusty and Irresistible


There’s something truly magical about the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the kitchen. And when it comes to bread, few things can compare to the classic French baguette. With its golden, crusty exterior and soft, chewy interior, a homemade baguette is a true delight for the senses. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating your very own baguettes that are crusty on the outside and irresistibly delicious on the inside.

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 5g instant yeast
  • 350ml lukewarm water

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, and instant yeast. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the lukewarm water. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix everything together until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Once the dough has come together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Begin kneading the dough by pushing it away from you with the heel of your hand and then folding it back over itself. Repeat this process for about 10 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Shape the dough into a ball and place it back into the mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise for 1 to 2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it by pressing down on it with your fingertips. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into three equal portions.

  6. Take one portion of the dough and flatten it into a rectangle. Starting from the long edge, roll the dough up tightly, like a jelly roll. Pinch the seam closed and tuck the ends under to create a baguette shape. Repeat this process with the remaining two portions of dough.

  7. Place the shaped baguettes onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover them loosely with a kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes to 1 hour, or until they have doubled in size.

  8. While the baguettes are rising, preheat your oven to 230°C (450°F). Place a shallow pan on the bottom rack of the oven and fill it with hot water. This will create steam, which helps to give the baguettes their crusty exterior.

  9. Once the baguettes have finished their final rise, use a sharp knife to make diagonal slashes across the tops. This will allow the bread to expand while baking.

  10. Carefully transfer the baking sheet with the baguettes into the preheated oven. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the baguettes are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  11. Remove the baguettes from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving. Enjoy them fresh with butter, or use them as the base for delicious sandwiches and bruschetta.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra crusty baguette, spritz the oven with water a few times during the first 10 minutes of baking.

  • To achieve a chewier texture, place a small oven-safe dish filled with water on the bottom rack of the oven while baking.

  • Experiment with different toppings such as sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or sea salt to add a unique twist to your baguettes.

  • Leftover baguettes can be sliced and frozen for future use. Simply thaw them at room temperature or pop them in the oven for a few minutes to warm them up.

Now that you have this simple and straightforward recipe, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! There’s truly nothing quite like the satisfaction of pulling a fresh batch of homemade baguettes out of the oven. So go ahead, embrace your inner baker, and indulge in the crusty, irresistible goodness of homemade baguettes. Happy baking!
