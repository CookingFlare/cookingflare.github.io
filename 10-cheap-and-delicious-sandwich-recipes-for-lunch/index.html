10 Cheap and Delicious Sandwich Recipes for Lunch


When you’re on a budget, lunch can often feel like a challenge. You want something tasty, filling, and affordable, but it can be hard to find the right balance. Sandwiches are the perfect solution. They are versatile, easy to make, and can be customized to your tastes and the ingredients you have on hand. Here are 10 cheap and delicious sandwich recipes that are perfect for lunch, whether you’re at home, at school, or at work.

1. Classic Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

You can’t go wrong with a peanut butter and jelly sandwich. It’s an affordable classic that satisfies both hunger and your sweet tooth. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 tablespoons of peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons of jelly or jam (your favorite flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Spread the peanut butter on one slice of bread.
  2. Spread the jelly or jam on the other slice of bread.
  3. Press the two slices together, peanut butter side facing jelly side.
  4. Cut into halves or quarters, and enjoy!

This sandwich is not only delicious but also rich in protein and provides an instant energy boost. You can easily make a large batch, and it stores well for a few hours, making it perfect for school or work lunches.

2. Grilled Cheese Sandwich

A grilled cheese sandwich is a comforting, budget-friendly lunch that never goes out of style. With just a few ingredients, you can enjoy a warm and gooey meal in minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1-2 slices of cheese (cheddar, American, or any other cheese you prefer)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Butter one side of each slice of bread.
  3. Place a slice of cheese between the unbuttered sides of the bread.
  4. Place the sandwich in the skillet and cook until golden brown on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Remove from the skillet, slice, and serve.

Grilled cheese sandwiches can be paired with tomato soup for a classic combo or served with a side of crispy chips for extra crunch.

3. Egg Salad Sandwich

Egg salad sandwiches are another inexpensive and filling option for lunch. Eggs are a cheap source of protein, and the creamy filling in this sandwich makes it a favorite for many.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the eggs for 8-10 minutes, then peel and chop them.
  2. In a bowl, combine the chopped eggs, mayonnaise, mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix well until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Spread the egg salad mixture on one slice of bread.
  5. Top with lettuce if desired, then place the second slice of bread on top.
  6. Cut into halves and serve.

Egg salad sandwiches are not only affordable but also highly customizable. You can add extras like pickles, onions, or fresh herbs to jazz it up.

4. Hummus and Veggie Sandwich

For a healthy, vegetarian option, a hummus and veggie sandwich is a great choice. Hummus is a flavorful and filling spread, while fresh vegetables provide crunch and nutrition.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of whole wheat or multigrain bread
  • 3-4 tablespoons of hummus
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • ½ cucumber, sliced
  • A handful of spinach or lettuce
  • 1 small avocado, sliced (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Spread hummus generously on both slices of bread.
  2. Layer the vegetables on one slice of bread.
  3. Add spinach or lettuce and top with avocado slices if using.
  4. Close the sandwich with the second slice of bread.
  5. Cut and enjoy!

This sandwich is perfect for those who want a light, yet filling, lunch. It’s full of vitamins, fiber, and healthy fats.

5. Turkey and Cheese Sandwich

Turkey and cheese sandwiches are a classic lunch option that’s both easy to make and packed with protein. You can use any type of bread and cheese you prefer, making it a customizable option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2-3 slices of turkey breast (deli meat or homemade)
  • 1-2 slices of cheese (Swiss, cheddar, or provolone)
  • Lettuce and tomato (optional)
  • Mayonnaise or mustard (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Lay the turkey slices on one slice of bread.
  2. Place the cheese on top of the turkey.
  3. Add lettuce and tomato if desired, and spread mayonnaise or mustard on the other slice of bread.
  4. Close the sandwich and cut it into halves.

This sandwich is ideal for when you want something hearty and satisfying without spending too much. It’s also great for meal prepping ahead of time.

6. Tuna Salad Sandwich

Tuna salad sandwiches are a timeless favorite for lunch. They are incredibly filling and can be made in minutes using canned tuna, which is an inexpensive protein source.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 1 can of tuna (in water or oil), drained
  • 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped onion (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the tuna, mayonnaise, and onion.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Spread the tuna mixture on one slice of bread.
  4. Add lettuce if desired, then top with the second slice of bread.
  5. Cut and serve.

Tuna salad sandwiches can be enjoyed as is or with extra toppings like pickles or tomato slices. This sandwich is a great option for those looking for a budget-friendly, high-protein meal.

7. Caprese Sandwich

The Caprese sandwich brings together the flavors of Italy in a simple yet elegant way. Fresh mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil are the star ingredients, making it a refreshing choice for lunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of baguette or Italian bread
  • 1-2 slices of fresh mozzarella
  • 2-3 slices of tomato
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil and balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Slice the baguette or Italian bread and place the mozzarella, tomato slices, and fresh basil on one slice of bread.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Top with the second slice of bread, press gently, and slice.

This sandwich is a great way to enjoy fresh, seasonal ingredients on a budget. It’s light yet full of flavor and perfect for a midday meal.

8. Chicken Caesar Sandwich

If you have leftover chicken from dinner or a rotisserie chicken from the store, this sandwich is a great way to use it up. A chicken Caesar sandwich combines the classic Caesar salad with the heartiness of chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread (preferably a sturdy type like sourdough)
  • 1 cup of cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons of Caesar dressing
  • 1 tablespoon of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Romaine lettuce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toss the shredded chicken in Caesar dressing until well-coated.
  2. Place the chicken mixture on one slice of bread.
  3. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese over the chicken, and top with romaine lettuce if desired.
  4. Close the sandwich with the second slice of bread and cut in half.

This sandwich offers all the delicious flavors of a Caesar salad, making it a filling and flavorful choice for lunch.

9. Veggie and Cream Cheese Sandwich

For a light and refreshing lunch, a veggie and cream cheese sandwich can hit the spot. The creamy texture of the cream cheese pairs wonderfully with fresh veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 3 tablespoons of cream cheese
  • 1 small cucumber, sliced thin
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • Lettuce or spinach

Instructions:

  1. Spread the cream cheese evenly on both slices of bread.
  2. Layer the cucumber, grated carrot, tomato slices, and leafy greens on one slice of bread.
  3. Top with the other slice of bread, press gently, and cut into halves.

This sandwich is low in calories and packed with vitamins, making it a healthy option for lunch.

10. Avocado and Bacon Sandwich

For a more indulgent sandwich, an avocado and bacon combination is hard to beat. The creamy avocado contrasts nicely with the crispy bacon, making for a satisfying and tasty lunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 2-3 slices of cooked bacon
  • Lettuce or spinach
  • Tomato slices (optional)
  • Mayonnaise (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Spread mayonnaise on one slice of bread if desired.
  2. Layer the avocado slices, bacon, lettuce, and tomato on one slice of bread.
  3. Top with the second slice of bread and cut the sandwich into halves.

This sandwich is a bit more decadent but still affordable, especially if you already have bacon and avocado on hand.

Conclusion

Sandwiches are the ultimate lunchtime solution: quick, simple, and budget-friendly. Whether you’re in the mood for a classic peanut butter and jelly sandwich, a fresh veggie-filled option, or something heartier like a turkey and cheese or chicken Caesar sandwich, there’s something for everyone on this list. These recipes are designed to be both affordable and delicious, so you don’t have to break the bank to enjoy a satisfying lunch.

Experiment with different fillings, breads, and spreads to create your own perfect sandwich. Happy lunching!
