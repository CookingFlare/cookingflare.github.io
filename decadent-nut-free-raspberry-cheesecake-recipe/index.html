Decadent Nut-Free Raspberry Cheesecake Recipe


If you’re a fan of cheesecake but have a nut allergy, fear not! This decadent nut-free raspberry cheesecake recipe is here to satisfy your sweet cravings without any worries. The combination of creamy cheesecake and tangy raspberries makes for a delightful dessert that’s perfect for any occasion. So, let’s dive into this delectable recipe and create a dessert that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour

For the Raspberry Swirl:

  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, ensuring an even layer.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. Using a stand mixer or handheld mixer, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the sour cream, heavy cream, eggs, and vanilla extract, and mix until well combined.
  3. Gradually add the flour and mix until the filling is smooth and free of lumps.

Step 3: Creating the Raspberry Swirl

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, and water.
  2. Cook over medium heat, mashing the raspberries with a fork, until the mixture thickens and becomes syrupy.
  3. Remove from heat and strain the mixture to remove the seeds, leaving you with a smooth raspberry sauce.

Step 4: Assembling the Cheesecake

  1. Pour the cream cheese filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan.
  2. Drizzle the raspberry sauce over the filling.
  3. Use a knife or toothpick to gently swirl the raspberry sauce into the cream cheese filling, creating a marbled effect.

Step 5: Baking and Chilling

  1. Place the springform pan on a large baking sheet to catch any potential leaks.
  2. Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for 55-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  3. Once baked, turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.
  4. Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow it to set completely.

Serving

When you’re ready to serve, carefully remove the sides of the springform pan and slice the cheesecake into portions. For an extra touch, garnish each slice with fresh raspberries or a dollop of whipped cream.

Conclusion

This nut-free raspberry cheesecake is a delightful treat for anyone with a love for creamy, fruity desserts. The smooth and rich cream cheese filling, combined with the tartness of the raspberry swirl, creates a flavor explosion in every bite. Whether it’s for a special occasion or simply to satisfy your sweet tooth, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. Enjoy creating and savoring this indulgent cheesecake with your loved ones!
