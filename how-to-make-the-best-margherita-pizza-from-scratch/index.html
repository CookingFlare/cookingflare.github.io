How to Make the Best Margherita Pizza from Scratch


Few dishes embody simplicity and culinary perfection quite like the classic Margherita pizza. A combination of crispy, thin-crust dough, tangy tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil leaves, this Italian masterpiece is a celebration of natural flavors. Making the perfect Margherita pizza in your own kitchen may sound like a challenge, but with the right technique and fresh ingredients, you’ll have a pizza that rivals even the most authentic Italian pizzerias.

In this guide, you’ll learn everything you need to know to craft the best Margherita pizza from scratch: from selecting ingredients, preparing your dough, making a rich tomato sauce, and the art of assembling and baking the pizza to perfection. Let’s dive into the details and start transforming your kitchen into an Italian trattoria!

The Ingredients: Quality Matters

The key to making a Margherita pizza that stands out is using high-quality ingredients. Each element complements the other, so freshness and authenticity are non-negotiable.

For the Dough:

  • Flour: Use high-protein bread flour or Italian “00” flour for elasticity and a delicate crispness.
  • Water: Lukewarm, purified water works best.
  • Yeast: Active dry yeast or instant yeast for the leavening.
  • Salt: Enhances flavor and strengthens the structure of the dough.
  • Sugar: Feeds the yeast to assist fermentation.
  • Olive Oil: Adds a subtle flavor and helps make the dough pliable.

For the Sauce:

  • Tomatoes: Use San Marzano tomatoes for their balanced acidity and robust flavor.
  • Garlic: A single clove will suffice for adding depth.
  • Salt: Draws out the sweetness in the tomatoes.
  • Olive Oil: Adds richness to the sauce.
  • Fresh Basil: Infuses the sauce with a hint of herbal aroma.

For the Toppings:

  • Mozzarella Cheese: Opt for fresh mozzarella or Fior di Latte for that creamy texture.
  • Fresh Basil Leaves: The soul of a Margherita pizza, adding brightness and fragrance.
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: For drizzling, to enhance the crisp crust and harmonize the flavors.

Step One: Preparing the Dough

A light, airy crust is essential to achieving the perfect Margherita pizza. The trick lies in patience: allowing the dough to ferment for optimal flavor.

Ingredients for the Dough

  • 3 ½ cups (500g) of flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 packet (7g) of active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cup of lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast: Add the yeast and sugar to the lukewarm water, stirring gently. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the yeast mixture and olive oil, stirring until incorporated.
  3. Knead: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. First Rise: Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch Down & Rest: Punch the dough to release air bubbles, divide it into two portions, and let it rest for 20 minutes.

Step Two: Crafting the Tomato Sauce

A great sauce is the heart of a Margherita pizza. Authentic flavors come from using whole tomatoes and minimal seasoning to let their natural taste shine.

Ingredients for the Sauce

  • 1 can (400g) of San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 clove of garlic, finely minced
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1-2 fresh basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Crush the Tomatoes: Use your hands or a potato masher to crush the San Marzano tomatoes into a bowl.
  2. Cook the Sauce: In a saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté just until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  3. Simmer: Add the tomatoes and salt to the saucepan. Stir gently and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, until thickened.
  4. Add Basil: Stir in a fresh basil leaf at the end of cooking for an aromatic touch.
  5. Cool: Remove from heat and let the sauce cool before assembling the pizza.

Step Three: Stretching and Shaping the Dough

Achieving the perfect pizza crust takes practice, but this step is crucial for that thin, yet puffed edge result.

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, ideally around 475-500°F (245-260°C). Place a pizza stone or inverted baking tray inside to heat up.
  2. Stretch the Dough: Take one portion of rested dough, place it on a floured surface, and gently press it into a flat disc. Using your knuckles and fingertips, stretch the dough outward, rotating it until you have a 12-inch circle.
  3. Transfer the Dough: Carefully transfer the stretched dough to a piece of parchment paper if you’re using a baking tray, or a pizza peel if you’re using a stone.

Step Four: Assembling the Pizza

The magic happens when all the components come together in harmony. Assembly should be delicate to avoid weighing down the crust.

  1. Spread the Sauce: Use a ladle or the back of a spoon to spread a thin, even layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a slight border for the crust.
  2. Add Mozzarella: Tear the fresh mozzarella into small chunks and distribute them evenly over the sauce.
  3. Top with Basil: Scatter whole basil leaves across the pizza.
  4. Drizzle with Olive Oil: Finish with a light drizzle of extra virgin olive oil for a golden finish.

Step Five: Baking the Pizza

Timing and temperature are crucial here. To replicate the results of a wood-fired oven, you’ll need high heat and a watchful eye.

  1. Bake: Slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or tray. Bake for 6-8 minutes, or until the crust is golden and puffed, the cheese has melted, and the edges have charred slightly.
  2. Cool: Remove the pizza and let it cool for a minute or two to stabilize the toppings.

Final Touches and Serving

Your Margherita pizza is now ready to impress! Slice it into wedges and serve immediately while the cheese is still gooey and the crust crisp. Pair it with a light, refreshing beverage, and enjoy the fruits of your labor.

Tips for Perfecting Your Margherita Pizza

  • Invest in a Pizza Stone: A stone retains heat evenly and creates a crispy base.
  • Don’t Overload: Less is more when it comes to Margherita toppings; keep it simple and minimalist.
  • Experiment with Fermentation: Longer fermentation (up to 24 hours in the fridge) enhances the dough’s flavor.
  • Use Fresh Mozzarella: Dry the mozzarella slightly before using to prevent excess moisture.

Making the best Margherita pizza from scratch is a delicious and rewarding experience. With practice, your pizzas will soon rival restaurant-quality pies, and you’ll gain a newfound appreciation for this timeless Italian classic. So gather your ingredients, follow these steps, and let the culinary adventure begin! Buon Appetito!
