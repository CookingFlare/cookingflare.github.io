Savor the Holidays with These Flavorful Stuffing Recipes


The holidays are a time for warmth, family, and delicious food. One of the most iconic dishes that screams of the season is homemade stuffing, a mixture of bread, vegetables, herbs, and seasonings baked inside a roasting turkey or a separate dish. Whether you call it dressing or stuffing, this traditional family recipe has got some surprising variations to make your holiday gatherings truly unforgettable. Herbed Challah Stuffing Start by using high-quality challah bread, the perfect base for an aromatic stuffing. Tear the bread into 1-inch cubes and spread them on a baking sheet, allowing the bread to dry for a while. This step ensures the bread browns nicely during baking without turning into mush. Essential ingredients for this herbed challah stuffing include

4 cups cubed challah bread 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened 1 onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 celery stalks, diced 2 sprigs of fresh sage 2 sprigs of fresh thyme 1 teaspoon poultry seasoning

Melt the butter in a skillet and sauté the onion, garlic, and celery until softened. Combine the cooked vegetables with the softened bread, fresh herbs, and spices. Mix well to coat the bread evenly. Add the mixture to a greased 9x13 baking dish or to the cavity of a roasted turkey. Before adding any liquid, be sure to see if there is enough moisture from the ingredients. Add a splash of broth if you prefer your stuffing saucier. Cornbread and Sausage Stuffing Inspired by the rustic simplicity of country cooking, this cornbread and sausage stuffing brings out the grandeur of Southern flavors. Moist cornbread crumbs complement spicy sausage and vegetables, filling every bite with a deep, savory taste.

8 cups crumbled cornbread 1 pound fresh sausage, removed from the casing 1 onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 stalk celery, diced 2 eggs, beaten salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat your oven to 350°F. Cook the sausage in a skillet until browned, then set it aside. Chop the cooled sausage coarsely. Add the diced onion, garlic, and celery to the same skillet. Cook until the onions turn translucent. Combine the crumbled cornbread with cooked sausage, the cooked vegetable mixture, beaten eggs, salt, and pepper. Italian Sausage and Provolone Stuffing The possibilities are endless in pulling off this intuitive pasta-stuffed side dish appetizer. Italian sausage, gooey cheese, and lip-smacking plum tomatoes put together an exquisite combination that seems to dance in your mouth. Use the same method of browning the Italian sausage. Use shoulder or finocchiona work well for optimal flavor. Diced tomatoes make up for delicious juice, ensuring the perfect cheesy texture throughout the treat.

8 cups cubed white bread 1 pound cooked Italian sausage 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 cups diced tomatoes 1 cup grated provolone cheese 2 tablespoons chopped Italian flat-leaf parsley 2 tablespoons panko breadcrumbs Salt and pepper, to taste

Combine the Italian sausage, crumbled provolone cheese, diced tomatoes, chopped parsley, and breadcrumbs. Add a layer of melted cheese to the bread for a delightfully affected appetizer.
