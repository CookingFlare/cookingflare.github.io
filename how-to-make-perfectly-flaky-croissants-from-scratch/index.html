How to Make Perfectly Flaky Croissants from Scratch


Welcome to this delightful recipe where we will dive into the art of creating perfectly flaky croissants from scratch. Croissants are a beloved pastry enjoyed by many around the world, known for their buttery layers and delicate texture. While making croissants may seem intimidating, with a little patience and practice, you can achieve bakery-worthy results in the comfort of your own kitchen. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

Before we begin, let’s gather all the ingredients you will need:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 10g salt
  • 50g granulated sugar
  • 10g instant yeast
  • 250ml cold water
  • 250g unsalted butter, chilled and cut into thin slices

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Mix well to ensure even distribution of the dry ingredients.
  2. Gradually add the cold water to the dry mixture. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to bring the dough together. It will be sticky at first, but continue to knead until it forms a smooth and elastic dough.
  3. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly greased bowl. Cover with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.
  4. Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it by pressing down with your fingertips. Transfer it to a lightly floured surface and roll it out into a rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.
  5. Arrange the chilled butter slices on two-thirds of the dough, leaving one-third uncovered. Fold the uncovered third over the middle third, then fold the remaining third on top. This creates three layers of dough with butter in between.
  6. Turn the dough 90 degrees and roll it out again into a rectangle. Repeat the folding process by folding the dough into thirds. This is known as a “turn.” Repeat the rolling and folding process three more times, for a total of four turns. This will create the beautiful layers that make croissants so flaky.
  7. After the final turn, wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. This allows the butter to solidify and the dough to relax.
  8. Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and roll it out into a rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick. Trim the edges to create clean lines.
  9. Cut the dough into triangles, approximately 5 inches wide at the base. Gently stretch each triangle to elongate it slightly.
  10. Starting from the base, roll each triangle up tightly towards the tip, shaping it into a crescent. Place the shaped croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving enough space between them to allow for expansion.
  11. Cover the croissants with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until they have doubled in size. This second rise will give the croissants their light and airy texture.
  12. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Brush the risen croissants with an egg wash made from one beaten egg and a splash of water. This will give them a beautiful golden color when baked.
  13. Bake the croissants for 15-20 minutes or until they are puffed up and golden brown. Keep a close eye on them to prevent over-browning.
  14. Once baked, transfer the croissants to a wire rack and let them cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them warm or at room temperature.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully made perfectly flaky croissants from scratch. The process may take some time and practice, but the end result is well worth the effort. These homemade croissants will impress your family and friends, and they make for a delightful breakfast or a special treat any time of the day. So go ahead, indulge in the buttery layers and delicate texture of your own homemade croissants. Bon appétit!

Note: While this recipe can be made in one day, for the best results, it is recommended to prepare the dough the day before and let it rest overnight in the refrigerator for optimal flavor and texture.
