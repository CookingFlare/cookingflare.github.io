Vegan Matcha Cupcakes: Green Tea Flavored Perfection


Matcha, the vibrant green powder derived from finely ground Japanese green tea leaves, has captured the hearts of many for its unique flavor and health benefits. If you’re a fan of matcha, you’re in for a treat with these Vegan Matcha Cupcakes. These cupcakes are the perfect balance of rich green tea flavor, subtle sweetness, and a light, fluffy texture that everyone will love – vegan or not. Whether you’re hosting a party, enjoying a quiet afternoon snack, or simply craving something sweet and green, these cupcakes are the answer.

In this post, we’ll explore how to create the perfect vegan matcha cupcakes, why matcha is such a great ingredient to bake with, and some tips for achieving the most delicious and aesthetically pleasing cupcakes.

Why Choose Vegan Matcha Cupcakes?

Many people are now turning to plant-based alternatives for a variety of reasons: health, sustainability, ethical concerns, or dietary preferences. Fortunately, it’s easier than ever to create delicious vegan treats that don’t compromise on taste or texture. When it comes to matcha, its distinct flavor and antioxidant properties make it a standout in any dessert. Vegan matcha cupcakes offer all the goodness of matcha, with a cruelty-free and dairy-free twist.

Health Benefits of Matcha

One of the reasons matcha has become so popular in the health and wellness community is its impressive list of health benefits. Unlike regular green tea, which is steeped and then discarded, matcha involves consuming the whole tea leaf in powdered form, making it a more concentrated source of nutrients.

Here are a few health benefits of matcha:

  • Rich in antioxidants: Matcha is packed with catechins, which are powerful antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and protect your body from harmful free radicals.
  • Boosts metabolism: Matcha has been shown to increase thermogenesis, the process by which your body burns calories, potentially aiding in weight loss.
  • Enhances focus and energy: The combination of caffeine and L-theanine in matcha provides a gentle energy boost without the jitters, promoting calm focus.
  • Improves digestion: Matcha may help improve digestion by promoting healthy gut bacteria.

These benefits make matcha an ideal addition to any recipe, especially one like these vegan cupcakes, which combine indulgence with a hint of health-consciousness.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the recipe itself, let’s take a look at what you’ll need to create these matcha cupcakes. Fortunately, most of these ingredients are easy to find, and you likely already have some of them in your kitchen.

For the Cupcakes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour: This is the base of your cupcakes. You can use whole wheat flour if you prefer a denser, heartier texture, but all-purpose flour gives the lightest, fluffiest results.
  • 2 tablespoons matcha powder: High-quality matcha powder is essential to give your cupcakes that vibrant green color and signature taste. Look for ceremonial-grade matcha for the best flavor.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda: This helps the cupcakes rise and gives them their airy texture.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder: A leavening agent that works with the baking soda to ensure a light and fluffy cupcake.
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt: A small amount of salt enhances the flavor of the matcha and balances out the sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar: Coconut sugar is a great natural sweetener that works perfectly in vegan baking. It has a mild caramel flavor that complements the matcha well.
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce: Applesauce replaces the eggs in this vegan recipe, providing moisture while also binding the ingredients together.
  • 1/2 cup non-dairy milk (such as almond or oat milk): The non-dairy milk adds moisture to the batter and helps dissolve the dry ingredients.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: Vanilla adds depth of flavor and enhances the sweetness of the cupcakes.
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil: The oil ensures the cupcakes remain moist and tender.

For the Vegan Matcha Frosting:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar: This sweetens and thickens the frosting.
  • 2 tablespoons vegan butter: For a creamy, rich frosting base. Make sure the vegan butter is softened for easy mixing.
  • 1 tablespoon non-dairy milk: This helps loosen the frosting to your desired consistency.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: Adds flavor to the frosting.
  • 1 teaspoon matcha powder: A little extra matcha for the frosting brings that gorgeous green color and boosts the flavor.

How to Make Vegan Matcha Cupcakes

Let’s jump into the process! Making these cupcakes is easy and straightforward, even for beginner bakers. You’ll love how simple it is to whip up these matcha-flavored treats.

Step 1: Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Cupcake Pan

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-cup muffin pan with cupcake liners, or lightly grease the pan if you prefer not to use liners.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, matcha powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. It’s important to sift the matcha powder beforehand to avoid any lumps and to ensure it mixes evenly into the dry ingredients. Once everything is combined, set the bowl aside.

Step 3: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In another large bowl, combine the coconut sugar, applesauce, non-dairy milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Use a whisk or a hand mixer to mix everything until smooth and well-combined.

Step 4: Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring gently with a spatula or spoon. Be careful not to overmix, as that can lead to dense cupcakes. Stir until everything is just combined, and no dry flour remains. The batter will be a little thick, but that’s perfectly fine.

Step 5: Fill the Cupcake Liners

Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each one about 2/3 of the way full. This will give the cupcakes plenty of room to rise without overflowing.

Step 6: Bake

Place the muffin pan in the preheated oven and bake for about 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Every oven is different, so be sure to check the cupcakes a few minutes before the suggested baking time.

Once done, remove the cupcakes from the oven and let them cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 7: Prepare the Matcha Frosting

While the cupcakes cool, you can prepare the matcha frosting. In a medium bowl, beat together the powdered sugar, vegan butter, non-dairy milk, vanilla extract, and matcha powder. If the frosting is too thick, add a little more non-dairy milk to reach your desired consistency. If it’s too runny, add more powdered sugar to thicken it up.

Taste the frosting and adjust the sweetness or matcha flavor if needed.

Step 8: Frost the Cupcakes

Once the cupcakes have cooled completely, use a spatula or piping bag to generously frost each cupcake with the matcha frosting. If you want to get extra fancy, you can decorate the tops of the cupcakes with a light dusting of matcha powder, sprinkles, or edible flowers for an elegant touch.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Matcha Cupcakes

  1. Use high-quality matcha: The quality of your matcha powder will significantly affect the flavor and color of your cupcakes. Opt for ceremonial-grade matcha for the best results.
  2. Don’t overmix the batter: Overmixing the batter can lead to dense cupcakes. Mix only until combined for a light and fluffy texture.
  3. Cool the cupcakes before frosting: Always allow your cupcakes to cool completely before applying frosting. If the cupcakes are too warm, the frosting will melt and slide off.
  4. Adjust sweetness: Depending on your preference, you can adjust the amount of coconut sugar and powdered sugar to suit your taste. If you prefer less sweetness, you can reduce both.

How to Store Vegan Matcha Cupcakes

These vegan matcha cupcakes can be stored at room temperature for up to 2-3 days in an airtight container. If you want to keep them fresh for longer, you can store them in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Make sure to allow the cupcakes to come to room temperature before serving for the best flavor and texture.

If you have leftovers (though that’s unlikely!), you can freeze the cupcakes for up to 3 months. Just wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag or container. Thaw at room temperature when ready to enjoy.

Conclusion: A Flavorful, Vibrant Treat

Vegan matcha cupcakes are not only a delicious treat but also a visually stunning dessert. With their vibrant green color and light, fluffy texture, they’re perfect for any occasion. The subtle flavor of matcha paired with the creamy, sweet frosting makes them a standout dessert for those looking for something a little different from the typical cupcake offerings.

Whether you’re new to vegan baking or a seasoned pro, these cupcakes are a breeze to make and will surely impress your friends and family. Enjoy the natural sweetness, the health benefits of matcha, and the knowledge that you’ve made a plant-based dessert that’s as tasty as it is beautiful. Happy baking!
