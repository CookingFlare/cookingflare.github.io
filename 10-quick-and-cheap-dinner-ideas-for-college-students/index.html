10 Quick and Cheap Dinner Ideas for College Students


When you’re in college, juggling classes, assignments, and exams can be overwhelming. But that doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice a good meal for the sake of convenience or cost. With limited time and a tight budget, preparing tasty, nutritious dinners is absolutely possible. Whether you’re cooking for one or sharing with roommates, these 10 quick and cheap dinner ideas will help you stay energized without breaking the bank.

1. One-Pot Pasta

If you’re looking for a hassle-free, filling dinner, a one-pot pasta dish is the way to go. This meal requires minimal effort, and the best part is that you don’t have to worry about washing multiple pots and pans afterward. Here’s how you can make a simple one-pot pasta:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pasta (spaghetti, penne, or fusilli)
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: Grated cheese, fresh basil

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Pour in the diced tomatoes and broth, and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the pasta and season with oregano, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stir frequently, ensuring the pasta is cooked evenly. The broth will reduce and thicken into a delicious sauce.
  6. Once the pasta is al dente, serve with your favorite toppings like grated cheese or fresh herbs.

This meal is both cost-effective and satisfying, as you get a warm, flavorful dish without needing an arsenal of ingredients.

2. Vegetarian Stir-Fry with Rice

A stir-fry is an excellent option for a quick, healthy, and budget-friendly dinner. With just a few vegetables, some rice, and a simple sauce, you’ll have a filling meal that can be easily customized.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (white, brown, or jasmine)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • Optional: Tofu or chicken for added protein

Directions:

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chopped vegetables and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar to make a quick stir-fry sauce.
  5. Add the garlic and sauce to the pan and stir to combine, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Serve the stir-fry over rice, and add tofu or chicken for extra protein if desired.

This stir-fry is great for using up leftover vegetables and can be easily altered to suit whatever ingredients you have on hand. Plus, the flavors are versatile enough to suit any taste.

3. Egg Fried Rice

Egg fried rice is a classic dish that’s not only delicious but also extremely affordable. If you have leftover rice, this is a perfect option to transform it into something savory and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas or mixed vegetables
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Green onions for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  2. Scramble the eggs in the skillet and cook until set, then remove and set aside.
  3. Add the chopped onion to the pan and sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the peas (or mixed vegetables) and cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Stir in the rice, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring frequently.
  6. Add the scrambled eggs back into the pan and mix everything together.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot.

This dish is great for using up leftover rice and is packed with flavor. You can also add other ingredients like shrimp, chicken, or even some leftover meat to give it a protein boost.

4. Chili Mac

Chili mac combines the comforting flavors of chili and macaroni and cheese in one dish. It’s a great budget-friendly meal that’s easy to prepare, especially when you’re craving something hearty and filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box macaroni and cheese
  • 1 can chili (with or without beans)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Optional: Jalapeños, sour cream, or green onions for garnish

Directions:

  1. Prepare the macaroni and cheese according to the instructions on the box.
  2. While the mac and cheese is cooking, heat the chili in a separate pan.
  3. Once the mac and cheese is ready, stir in the chili and combine until fully mixed.
  4. Add shredded cheddar cheese and stir until it melts into the dish.
  5. Serve with optional toppings like jalapeños, sour cream, or green onions for extra flavor.

This is a super simple meal that will leave you feeling full and satisfied, especially on those days when you’re short on time or energy.

5. Taco Salad

A taco salad is a light and fresh option that’s quick to assemble and easy on the wallet. Packed with veggies, protein, and flavor, it’s a perfect meal for busy college nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey (or black beans for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 4 cups lettuce, chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Tortilla chips for crunch

Directions:

  1. Brown the ground meat in a pan and drain excess fat.
  2. Add taco seasoning and follow the package instructions to season the meat.
  3. While the meat is cooking, chop the lettuce, tomato, and any other toppings you prefer.
  4. Once the meat is ready, assemble the salad by layering the lettuce, cooked meat, diced tomatoes, and shredded cheese.
  5. Top with salsa, sour cream, and a handful of crushed tortilla chips for crunch.

This meal is incredibly versatile—you can make it as spicy as you want and add toppings like avocado, jalapeños, or even black olives for extra flavor.

6. Simple Quesadillas

Quesadillas are an easy and fun dinner that’s perfect for using up leftover ingredients. With just a few items, you can have a delicious, cheesy meal that will satisfy your hunger.

Ingredients:

  • 2 flour tortillas
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix)
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken, beans, or veggies (optional)
  • Salsa and sour cream for dipping

Directions:

  1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  2. Place one tortilla in the pan and sprinkle cheese over the top.
  3. Add any optional fillings, such as cooked chicken, beans, or veggies.
  4. Top with the second tortilla and cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom tortilla is golden brown.
  5. Flip the quesadilla and cook for another 2-3 minutes until both sides are crispy.
  6. Cut into wedges and serve with salsa and sour cream.

Quesadillas are incredibly customizable, allowing you to get creative with your fillings based on what you have in the fridge.

7. Baked Potatoes with Toppings

Sometimes, all you need is a simple, comforting meal that you can prepare with little effort. Baked potatoes are a versatile base that can be topped with anything you like.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large russet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Chopped green onions, bacon bits, or any other toppings of your choice

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Wash and scrub the potatoes, then poke a few holes in them with a fork.
  3. Bake the potatoes directly on the oven rack for 45 minutes to an hour, or until they’re soft when pierced with a fork.
  4. Slice the potatoes open and fluff the insides with a fork.
  5. Top with cheese, sour cream, butter, and any other toppings you desire.

Baked potatoes are a filling and easy dinner idea that can be adapted to suit your cravings, whether you want a classic version or something more adventurous with toppings like chili or sautéed veggies.

8. Chicken and Vegetable Sheet Pan Dinner

For a no-fuss dinner that requires minimal cleanup, a sheet pan meal is perfect. Simply toss your ingredients onto a baking sheet, bake, and enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small potato, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chicken, vegetables, and potatoes with olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mixture onto a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve the chicken with the roasted vegetables and enjoy!

This sheet pan dinner is an easy way to prepare a healthy, filling meal with minimal effort. You can swap out vegetables depending on what’s in season or what you have available.

9. Ramen with a Twist

Instant ramen is a staple for college students due to its affordability, but it doesn’t have to be bland. With a few simple additions, you can turn this basic dish into something more exciting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 packet instant ramen
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup vegetables (spinach, peas, corn)
  • Soy sauce or sesame oil for flavor

Directions:

  1. Cook the ramen according to package instructions, but don’t add the seasoning packet just yet.
  2. In the last 2 minutes of cooking, crack an egg into the pot and let it cook in the broth.
  3. Add vegetables to the ramen and cook until tender.
  4. Stir in a splash of soy sauce or sesame oil for added flavor.
  5. Serve hot, and enjoy your upgraded ramen.

Ramen is a quick and cheap meal, and with just a few extra ingredients, you can create a more balanced and delicious dish.

10. Peanut Butter and Banana Sandwiches

For a no-cook option that still provides nutrition and comfort, peanut butter and banana sandwiches are a perfect choice. This is especially ideal for a late-night snack or when you don’t have much time to cook.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices whole-grain bread
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 1 banana, sliced

Directions:

  1. Spread peanut butter on one slice of bread.
  2. Top with banana slices and close the sandwich.
  3. For extra flavor, toast the sandwich lightly for a warm and crispy treat.

This simple sandwich provides a mix of protein, fiber, and healthy fats, making it an ideal quick meal or snack when you’re craving something sweet yet substantial.

These 10 quick and cheap dinner ideas are perfect for college students who need to make the most of their time and money. With just a few basic ingredients, you can enjoy a variety of delicious meals that are easy to prepare and satisfying. Whether you prefer comfort food, something lighter, or a quick stir-fry, these meals offer something for every taste and budget. So, next time you’re looking for a simple and affordable dinner, turn to one of these recipes for inspiration!
