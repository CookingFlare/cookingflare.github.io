Delicious and Versatile: Canning Homemade Tomato Sauce


There’s something incredibly satisfying about making your own homemade tomato sauce. The rich aroma of tomatoes simmering with herbs and garlic, the vibrant red color, and the incredible versatility of this staple make it an essential recipe in any kitchen. Canning homemade tomato sauce not only allows you to preserve the vibrant flavors of summer but also provides a convenient base for a wide range of dishes throughout the year. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or a beginner looking to explore the world of home canning, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating a delicious and versatile homemade tomato sauce that you can enjoy for months to come.

Ingredients

  • 25 lbs of fresh, ripe tomatoes
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of dried oregano
  • 2 tablespoons of dried basil
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Equipment

  • Large stockpot
  • Canning jars with lids and bands
  • Water bath canner
  • Canning funnel
  • Jar lifter
  • Ladle
  • Wooden spoon
  • Cutting board and knife

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

Start by washing the tomatoes thoroughly and removing the stems. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and blanch the tomatoes for 1-2 minutes. Transfer the tomatoes to an ice water bath to stop the cooking process. Once cooled, peel the skins off and remove the cores. Chop the tomatoes into quarters and set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Sauce

In a large stockpot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they are soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add the chopped tomatoes, dried oregano, and dried basil to the pot, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let the sauce cook for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until it has thickened and the flavors have melded together. Season the sauce with sugar, salt, and pepper to taste.

Step 3: Prepare the Canning Jars

While the sauce is cooking, prepare your canning jars by washing them in hot, soapy water and sterilizing them in a water bath canner. Keep the jars hot until ready to use.

Step 4: Fill the Jars

Using a canning funnel, ladle the hot tomato sauce into the prepared jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace at the top. Run a wooden spoon around the inside of the jar to remove any air bubbles. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to ensure a proper seal, then place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip tight.

Step 5: Process the Jars

Once all the jars are filled, place them in the water bath canner, making sure they are covered by at least 1 inch of water. Bring the water to a gentle boil and process the jars for 35 minutes. After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner using a jar lifter and place them on a towel-lined countertop to cool.

Step 6: Store and Enjoy

Allow the jars to cool completely before checking the seals. Any unsealed jars should be refrigerated and used within a few days. Properly sealed jars can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to a year. Use your homemade tomato sauce in pasta dishes, soups, stews, and more for a taste of summer all year round.

Conclusion

Canning homemade tomato sauce is a rewarding and practical way to preserve the flavors of the season. By following this simple recipe and the basic principles of home canning, you can enjoy the delicious taste and versatility of homemade tomato sauce throughout the year. Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the sauce to your liking, and savor the satisfaction of knowing that you’ve created a kitchen essential from scratch. Happy canning!
