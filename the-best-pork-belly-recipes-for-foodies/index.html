The Best Pork Belly Recipes for Foodies


Pork belly is a cut of meat that many food enthusiasts swear by, and for good reason. Known for its rich marbling of fat and layers of tender meat, it’s a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes, from crispy roasted pork belly to slow-braised creations. Whether you’re a seasoned home chef or someone looking to add a flavorful dish to your weekly rotation, pork belly offers an opportunity to experiment with both classic and modern cooking techniques.

In this post, we’ll explore the best pork belly recipes that are sure to satisfy your culinary cravings, from simple weeknight meals to extravagant feasts. Get ready to dive into a world of flavor, texture, and creativity as we take pork belly to new heights!

1. Classic Crispy Roasted Pork Belly

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 pounds of pork belly (skin on)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Pork Belly: Start by scoring the skin of the pork belly in a crisscross pattern. Be careful not to cut too deep into the meat—just the skin. This will allow the fat to render out and help create a crispier skin.

  2. Season: Rub the pork belly with olive oil, sea salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and smoked paprika. Make sure the seasoning gets into all the nooks and crannies, especially the scored skin.

  3. Roast: Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Place the pork belly on a roasting rack or a baking tray lined with foil, skin side up. Roast the pork belly for about 30 minutes, or until the skin starts to crisp up.

  4. Reduce Heat: Lower the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue roasting for an additional 1-1.5 hours, or until the pork belly is tender and the skin is crackling.

  5. Rest: Once roasted, remove the pork belly from the oven and let it rest for at least 10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a moist bite.

  6. Serve: Slice the pork belly into bite-sized pieces and drizzle with a little apple cider vinegar or soy sauce for an extra burst of flavor. Serve with your favorite side dishes, such as mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad.

2. Braised Pork Belly in Soy Sauce and Five-Spice

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of pork belly, cut into 1.5-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine or Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar (preferably rock sugar)
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cups of water (or enough to cover the pork)
  • 1 teaspoon of five-spice powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Scallions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Pork Belly: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the cubed pork belly and sear all sides until golden brown. Remove the pork belly from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Aromatic Base: In the same pot, add minced garlic and sliced ginger. Sauté for about 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.

  3. Build the Sauce: Add soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, star anise, cinnamon stick, and five-spice powder to the pot. Stir to combine and bring it to a simmer.

  4. Add Pork Belly: Return the seared pork belly to the pot. Add enough water to cover the meat, then bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the pork belly braise for about 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened. Check occasionally to ensure the meat remains covered with liquid, adding more water as necessary.

  6. Final Touches: Season with salt to taste. Before serving, garnish with freshly chopped scallions for a burst of color and freshness.

  7. Serve: This braised pork belly dish is perfect served over steamed rice or alongside sautéed greens for a comforting meal.

3. Pork Belly Tacos with Pickled Onions

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup of red onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup of white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Pickle the Onions: In a small bowl, combine the red onions, white vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let this sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the onions to pickle and soften.

  2. Prepare the Pork Belly: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced pork belly and season with chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for 6-8 minutes, flipping occasionally, until the pork belly is crispy and fully cooked.

  3. Warm the Tortillas: While the pork belly cooks, warm the corn tortillas on a separate pan over low heat for 1-2 minutes on each side.

  4. Assemble the Tacos: Place a few pieces of crispy pork belly on each tortilla. Top with the pickled onions and fresh cilantro.

  5. Serve: Squeeze fresh lime juice over the tacos before serving for a zesty kick. These pork belly tacos are perfect for a casual meal with friends or family.

4. Slow-Cooked Pork Belly with Apple and Thyme

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 pounds of pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large apple, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Pork Belly: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season the pork belly with salt and pepper and sear it on all sides until browned. Remove the pork belly from the pan and set it aside.

  2. Prepare the Braising Liquid: In the same skillet, add the sliced apple, onion, and thyme. Sauté for a few minutes until the onion softens. Add the chicken broth, apple cider, and honey. Bring it to a simmer.

  3. Slow Cook the Pork Belly: Transfer the seared pork belly to a slow cooker and pour the apple mixture over the top. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or until the pork belly is tender and falls apart easily.

  4. Finish the Dish: Once the pork belly is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Drizzle some of the braising liquid over the slices for extra flavor.

  5. Serve: This slow-cooked pork belly is perfect served alongside mashed potatoes, roasted carrots, or a fresh green salad.

5. Pork Belly and Kimchi Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup of kimchi, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds
  • 2 scallions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pork Belly: Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the pork belly slices and cook for 4-5 minutes, or until browned and crispy.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and gochujang.

  3. Combine Pork and Kimchi: Add the garlic and chopped kimchi to the skillet with the pork belly. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the kimchi is heated through.

  4. Add Sauce: Pour the prepared sauce over the pork and kimchi. Stir to coat everything evenly and cook for an additional 2 minutes.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle sesame seeds and chopped scallions over the stir-fry before serving. Serve with steamed rice or noodles for a complete meal.

Conclusion

Pork belly is one of those ingredients that has the ability to elevate any dish with its rich flavor and satisfying texture. From crispy roasted versions to slow-cooked, tender cuts, the possibilities are endless. Whether you prefer the savory depth of soy-braised pork belly, the kick of a kimchi stir-fry, or the simplicity of roasted pork with crackling skin, there’s a recipe here for every food lover.

Don’t be afraid to get creative in the kitchen. With its tender, flavorful meat and crispy skin, pork belly can take on a variety of cooking methods and flavor profiles. So, get ready to impress your friends and family with these incredible pork belly recipes!
