Beginner's Baking: Decadent Chocolate Brownie Recipe


If you’re new to baking and looking for a delicious yet simple recipe to impress your friends and family, look no further than this decadent chocolate brownie recipe. Brownies are a classic crowd-pleaser, and with this foolproof recipe, you’ll be able to whip up a batch of rich, fudgy brownies in no time.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups (400g) granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup (75g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (175g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13 inch (23x33 cm) baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.

  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter in 30-second intervals until it is completely melted. Alternatively, you can melt the butter on the stovetop over low heat. Allow the melted butter to cool slightly.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter and granulated sugar. Mix well until the sugar is fully incorporated into the butter.

  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, to the butter and sugar mixture, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. In a separate bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, unsweetened cocoa powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Do not overmix the batter.

  6. Gently fold in the semi-sweet chocolate chips, reserving a handful to sprinkle on top of the batter.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Sprinkle the remaining chocolate chips over the top of the batter.

  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs attached. Be careful not to overbake the brownies, as you want them to be fudgy and moist.

  9. Once baked, remove the brownies from the oven and allow them to cool completely in the pan. Once cooled, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the brownies out of the pan and onto a cutting board.

  10. Cut the brownies into squares of your desired size and serve. These brownies are delicious on their own, or you can top them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra special treat.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra indulgent twist, you can add chopped nuts, caramel swirls, or even a sprinkle of sea salt to the brownie batter before baking.
  • Make sure not to overmix the batter once you add the dry ingredients, as this can result in tough brownies.
  • Store any leftover brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

With this simple yet indulgent chocolate brownie recipe, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a baking pro. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or just craving a sweet treat, these brownies are sure to hit the spot. Enjoy the process and the delicious results!
