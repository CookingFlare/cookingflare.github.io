Easy and Tasty Naan Bread Recipes for Indian Cuisine Lovers


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, then you know that naan bread is an essential part of any meal. Its fluffy texture and delectable flavors make it the perfect accompaniment to curries, kebabs, and other Indian dishes. While naan bread may seem daunting to make at home, I’m here to share with you some easy and tasty recipes that will have you baking your own naan bread in no time!

Classic Homemade Naan Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Butter, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and baking powder.
  3. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the yeast mixture, yogurt, and vegetable oil.
  4. Mix everything together until a soft dough forms.
  5. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  7. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles.
  8. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  9. On a floured surface, roll out each ball into an oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  10. Heat a skillet or a griddle over medium heat.
  11. Place the rolled-out naan bread onto the hot skillet and cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until golden brown spots appear.
  12. Remove the naan bread from the skillet and brush it with melted butter.
  13. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  14. Serve the naan bread warm and enjoy it with your favorite Indian dishes!

Garlic and Herb Naan Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • Butter, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Follow the same instructions as the Classic Homemade Naan Bread recipe until step 7.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, chopped cilantro, and melted butter.
  3. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  5. On a floured surface, roll out each ball into an oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  6. Spread a small amount of the garlic and herb mixture onto one side of the rolled-out naan bread.
  7. Heat a skillet or a griddle over medium heat.
  8. Place the naan bread onto the hot skillet, garlic and herb side down, and cook for about 2 minutes, or until golden brown spots appear.
  9. Brush the uncooked side of the naan bread with melted butter.
  10. Flip the naan bread and cook for another 2 minutes, or until golden brown spots appear on the other side.
  11. Remove the naan bread from the skillet and brush it with melted butter.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  13. Serve the garlic and herb naan bread warm and savor the aromatic flavors!

Cheese-Stuffed Naan Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix)
  • Butter, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Follow the same instructions as the Classic Homemade Naan Bread recipe until step 7.
  2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  4. On a floured surface, roll out each ball into an oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  5. Place a spoonful of grated cheese onto one side of the rolled-out naan bread.
  6. Fold the other half of the naan bread over the cheese and pinch the edges to seal it.
  7. Gently roll out the stuffed naan bread to ensure the cheese is evenly distributed.
  8. Heat a skillet or a griddle over medium heat.
  9. Place the stuffed naan bread onto the hot skillet and cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until golden brown spots appear.
  10. Remove the naan bread from the skillet and brush it with melted butter.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  12. Serve the cheese-stuffed naan bread warm and relish the gooey cheese filling!

Now that you have these easy and tasty naan bread recipes, you can elevate your Indian cuisine experience right in your own kitchen. Whether you choose the classic version, the garlic and herb variation, or the cheese-stuffed option, you’ll be amazed at how simple it is to make naan bread from scratch. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and enjoy the delightful flavors of homemade naan bread with your favorite Indian dishes!
