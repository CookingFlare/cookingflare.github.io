Homemade Classic American Chocolate Cake Recipe


There’s something timeless about a classic American chocolate cake. The rich, moist layers of chocolate sponge paired with velvety frosting create a dessert that resonates with nostalgia and indulgence. Whether it’s a birthday celebration, a family gathering, or just a sweet craving, a homemade chocolate cake can elevate any occasion. In this post, we’ll guide you step-by-step through crafting the ultimate homemade classic American chocolate cake, complete with tips to ensure your cake turns out perfect every time.

Ingredients

For the Cake

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

For the Chocolate Frosting

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (or milk, if preferred)

Equipment Needed

  • Two 9-inch round cake pans
  • Parchment paper
  • Mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer (or whisk)
  • Rubber spatula
  • Cooling rack
  • Sifter (for cocoa powder and powdered sugar)
  • Offset spatula (for frosting)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Cake Pans

Before you start mixing your ingredients, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While the oven is warming up, prepare your cake pans. Grease the bottom and sides of two 9-inch round cake pans with butter or cooking spray. For easy removal, you can also line the bottoms with parchment paper. This step is crucial to ensure your cakes come out easily without sticking.

Step 2: Combine Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, unsweetened cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Sifting helps to aerate the flour and cocoa, which will give your cake a lighter texture. Use a whisk to mix the dry ingredients thoroughly until well combined.

Step 3: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, combine the eggs, whole milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Whisk these ingredients together until they are well blended. The combination of milk and oil adds moisture to the cake, while the eggs provide structure.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, mixing on medium speed with an electric mixer until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cake. The batter will be quite thick at this stage.

Step 5: Add Boiling Water

This is where the magic happens! Carefully stir in the boiling water. The batter will become thin, which is perfectly normal. This step ensures that your chocolate cake is incredibly moist and rich.

Step 6: Bake the Cakes

Pour the batter evenly into the prepared cake pans. Tap the pans gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles. Place them in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Keep a close eye on the cakes as baking times can vary depending on your oven.

Step 7: Cool the Cakes

Once baked, remove the pans from the oven and let them cool in the pans for about 10 minutes. Afterward, carefully transfer the cakes to a cooling rack to cool completely. This step is essential to prevent the frosting from melting when you assemble the cake.

Step 8: Prepare the Chocolate Frosting

While the cakes are cooling, it’s time to make the frosting. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened unsalted butter with an electric mixer until creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar and cocoa powder, mixing until combined. Pour in the vanilla extract and heavy cream, and continue to mix until the frosting is smooth and fluffy. Adjust the consistency by adding more cream or sugar as needed.

Step 9: Assemble the Cake

Once the cakes are completely cool, it’s time to assemble. Place one cake layer on a serving platter. Spread a generous amount of chocolate frosting on top. Place the second cake layer on top of the frosted layer. Use the offset spatula to spread frosting on the top and sides of the cake. Feel free to create swirls or decorative patterns as you frost.

Step 10: Decorate and Serve

For a finishing touch, you can sprinkle chocolate shavings, chocolate chips, or even fresh berries on top of the cake. Slice into generous pieces and serve to family and friends. Prepare for the smiles and satisfied sighs that follow!

Tips for the Perfect Chocolate Cake

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your cake will be influenced by the quality of your ingredients. Opt for high-quality cocoa powder and vanilla extract for the best results.

  • Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure your eggs and milk are at room temperature before mixing. This helps to create a more uniform batter.

  • Check for Doneness: Ovens can vary, so start checking your cakes a few minutes before the suggested baking time.

  • Cool Completely: Allow your cakes to cool completely before frosting. This prevents the frosting from melting and sliding off.

Storage

If you have any leftovers (though it’s hard to imagine!), store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to three days. For longer storage, you can refrigerate it for up to a week. The cake can also be frozen for up to three months. Just wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn.

Conclusion

A homemade classic American chocolate cake is not just a dessert; it’s a celebration of flavors and memories. With this recipe, you can create a cake that’s not only delicious but also a centerpiece for any gathering. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply indulging your sweet tooth, this chocolate cake is sure to impress. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake your way to chocolate bliss! Enjoy every bite of your creation, and remember that the best cakes are made with love.
