Charming Mini Christmas Pies Recipe for a Cute Dessert Display

The holiday season is all about spreading joy, spending time with loved ones, and indulging in delicious treats. If you’re looking to add a touch of charm to your Christmas dessert table, these delightful mini Christmas pies are the perfect choice. Not only are they visually appealing, but they also boast a delectable combination of flavors that will leave your guests craving for more. Ingredients For the Pie Crust: 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon granulated sugar 6-8 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 3 cups assorted fresh berries (such as raspberries, blueberries, and blackberries) 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 tablespoons cornstarch 1 teaspoon lemon zest 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice For the Egg Wash: 1 egg 1 tablespoon water Instructions Making the Pie Crust: In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually drizzle in the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough with a fork until it starts to come together. Avoid overworking the dough. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Preparing the Filling: In a medium bowl, gently toss the fresh berries with granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon zest, and lemon juice until well combined. Set aside. Assembling the Mini Pies: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one of the chilled dough disks to a 1/8-inch thickness. Use a round cookie cutter to cut out circles, then gently press each circle into the cups of a mini muffin tin. Spoon the prepared berry filling into each pie crust, filling them to the top. Baking and Finishing: Roll out the second chilled dough disk and use a small cookie cutter to cut out decorative shapes, such as stars or trees. Place the cutouts on top of the filled pies. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and water to create an egg wash. Lightly brush the tops of the pies with the egg wash. Bake the mini pies for 20-25 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Allow the pies to cool in the muffin tin for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Serving and Presentation To create a charming display, arrange the mini Christmas pies on a festive platter or cake stand. Dust them with powdered sugar for a snowy effect, and garnish with fresh mint leaves or edible gold glitter for an extra touch of elegance. These mini pies are not only a treat for the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes, making them a delightful addition to any Christmas gathering. ...
