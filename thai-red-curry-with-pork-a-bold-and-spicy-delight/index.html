Thai Red Curry with Pork: A Bold and Spicy Delight


If you’re a fan of bold and spicy flavors, then Thai Red Curry with Pork is the dish for you. This vibrant and aromatic curry is packed with layers of flavors that will surely tantalize your taste buds. From the rich and creamy coconut milk to the fiery red chili paste, every bite of this dish is an explosion of taste. So, let’s dive into the world of Thai cuisine and learn how to make this delicious and satisfying curry.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of pork, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 can (400 ml) of coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons of Thai red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 cup of Thai basil leaves
  • 1 cup of baby corn, halved
  • 1 cup of bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or wok over medium heat. Add the Thai red curry paste and stir-fry for a minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the pork slices to the pot and cook until browned on all sides. Make sure the pork is cooked through.

  3. Pour in the coconut milk, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Add the red bell pepper, zucchini, baby corn, and bamboo shoots to the pot. Stir to coat the vegetables with the curry sauce.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the curry simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  6. Stir in the Thai basil leaves and torn kaffir lime leaves. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed.

  8. Serve the Thai Red Curry with Pork hot with steamed rice or noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder curry, you can reduce the amount of Thai red curry paste or remove the seeds from the red chili peppers before adding them to the paste.

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables or proteins. Shrimp, chicken, or tofu are great alternatives to pork.

  • For an extra kick of flavor, garnish the curry with fresh cilantro, sliced red chili peppers, and a squeeze of lime juice.

  • If you can’t find Thai basil leaves, regular basil can be used as a substitute. However, Thai basil has a unique flavor that adds authenticity to the dish.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will develop even more overnight, making it even more delicious.

Now that you have the recipe for this bold and spicy Thai Red Curry with Pork, it’s time to unleash your culinary skills and indulge in the flavors of Thailand. This dish is perfect for a cozy night in or for impressing your friends and family at a dinner party. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the magic of Thai cuisine transport you to a world of exotic tastes and aromas. Enjoy!
