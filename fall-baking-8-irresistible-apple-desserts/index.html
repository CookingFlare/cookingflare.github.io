Fall Baking: 8 Irresistible Apple Desserts


When the crisp autumn air settles in and the leaves start to turn shades of amber and gold, there’s nothing quite as comforting as the aroma of freshly baked apple desserts filling your home. Fall baking holds a special place in our hearts, as it brings the essence of the season right into the kitchen. Apple desserts, with their sweet-tart flavor and warm, inviting scent, are the perfect way to celebrate fall.

Whether you’re hosting a cozy gathering or simply indulging in a solo treat, these eight apple-based desserts will surely satisfy your cravings and elevate your autumn baking game. From classic pies to innovative tarts and cakes, let’s dive into some delicious apple dessert ideas that will make your kitchen the ultimate fall haven.

1. Classic Apple Pie: A Timeless Fall Favorite

Apple pie is an iconic dessert that never goes out of style, especially when fall rolls around. It’s a dessert that feels like home, with its flaky crust and warm, spiced apple filling. To make the perfect apple pie, you’ll want to use a mix of tart and sweet apples for balance—Granny Smith apples combined with Honeycrisp apples are a winning duo.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium apples (preferably a mix of tart and sweet varieties)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crust (or homemade if you prefer)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Peel, core, and slice the apples, then toss them in a bowl with lemon juice, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour. Mix until well combined.
  3. Roll out one pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Add the apple mixture to the crust and dot with small pieces of butter.
  4. Roll out the second pie crust and place it on top of the apples. Crimp the edges to seal the pie, then make a few small slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling. Let it cool before serving.

2. Apple Crisp: A Cozy Dessert with a Crunchy Topping

Apple crisp is another fall classic that’s incredibly easy to prepare and packed with flavor. The warm apple filling is topped with a buttery, crunchy oatmeal topping that adds the perfect texture contrast. Serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an indulgent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup cold butter, cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, granulated sugar, and cinnamon. Spread the mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to incorporate the butter until the mixture forms a crumbly texture.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden and the apples are tender. Let it cool slightly before serving.

3. Apple Fritter Donuts: Fall’s Sweet Breakfast Treat

If you’re looking for a breakfast treat or a fun weekend project, apple fritter donuts are the way to go. These donuts are loaded with tender apple pieces and coated in a sugary glaze, making them an irresistible choice for apple lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 cup peeled and diced apples (Granny Smith or Fuji work well)
  • 1 cup vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk (for glaze)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and granulated sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, vanilla extract, and milk. Pour this wet mixture into the dry ingredients and stir to combine.
  3. Gently fold in the diced apples until evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  4. Heat the oil in a large pot or deep fryer to 350°F (175°C). Use a spoon or ice cream scoop to drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil. Fry in batches, turning the fritters to ensure they cook evenly, for about 2-3 minutes per side or until golden brown.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar and milk to make the glaze.
  6. Once the fritters are fried and drained, dip them in the glaze while still warm. Allow the glaze to set before serving.

4. Apple Cider Donuts: A Fall Festival Favorite

Apple cider donuts are a staple at fall festivals and farmers’ markets, and for good reason. The combination of apple cider and a rich, spiced dough makes these donuts soft, flavorful, and perfect for any fall occasion. Whether served fresh from the fryer or stored for a few days, these donuts will keep you coming back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups apple cider
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • ½ cup cinnamon-sugar (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, reduce the apple cider over medium heat until it’s about ½ cup. Set it aside to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and granulated sugar.
  3. Add the egg, melted butter, vanilla extract, and reduced apple cider to the dry ingredients. Mix until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it out to about ½ inch thickness. Use a donut cutter to cut out the donuts.
  5. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer to 350°F (175°C). Fry the donuts for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
  6. Drain the donuts on paper towels, then coat them in cinnamon-sugar while they are still warm.

5. Caramel Apple Cheesecake Bars: A Decadent Treat

For those who love the combination of rich, creamy cheesecake and the sweet flavor of apples, caramel apple cheesecake bars are a must-try. These bars combine a buttery crust, a smooth cheesecake filling, and a topping of cinnamon-spiced apples and caramel drizzle.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of graham crackers, crushed
  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 3 (8-ounce) packages cream cheese, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 medium apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup caramel sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, combine the crushed graham crackers, melted butter, and granulated sugar. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared dish to form the crust.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese with the sugar until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition, followed by the vanilla extract. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust.
  4. In a small bowl, toss the diced apples with cinnamon, then scatter them over the cheesecake batter.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the center is set. Let the bars cool completely, then drizzle with caramel sauce before serving.

6. Apple Tart with Almond Cream: A Sophisticated Treat

If you’re looking for a dessert that’s as beautiful as it is delicious, an apple tart with almond cream is the answer. The flaky pastry, sweet almond filling, and delicate apple slices make for a stunning dessert that’s perfect for fall gatherings or special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • ½ cup almond flour
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 medium apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon apricot jam (for glazing)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the puff pastry and fit it into a tart pan.
  2. In a bowl, mix the almond flour, granulated sugar, butter, egg, and vanilla extract to form the almond cream filling.
  3. Spread the almond cream evenly over the puff pastry.
  4. Arrange the thin apple slices in a spiral pattern over the almond cream.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and the apples are tender.
  6. Melt the apricot jam in a small saucepan and brush it over the apples for a shiny finish.

7. Apple Cinnamon Rolls: A Fall Breakfast Favorite

Apple cinnamon rolls are the perfect way to start your fall mornings. These soft, fluffy rolls are filled with spiced apples and drizzled with a sweet glaze, making them a delightful breakfast or dessert option.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 medium apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup powdered sugar (for glaze)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and granulated sugar in warm milk. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and melted butter. Add the eggs and yeast mixture and mix to form a dough. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Cover and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  3. Roll the dough out into a large rectangle and spread the diced apples, cinnamon, and brown sugar over it.
  4. Roll the dough tightly into a log and slice it into 12 rolls. Place the rolls in a greased baking dish.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the rolls for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Mix the powdered sugar with a little milk to make a glaze, then drizzle it over the warm rolls.

8. Apple Brown Betty: A Warm, Comforting Dessert

Apple brown betty is a simple yet comforting dessert that layers cinnamon-spiced apples with buttery breadcrumbs. It’s a dessert that feels like a warm hug on a chilly fall evening.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 cups fresh breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, toss the apple slices with granulated sugar and cinnamon. Place half of the apples in the bottom of the baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, mix the breadcrumbs, brown sugar, salt, and melted butter. Sprinkle half of the breadcrumb mixture over the apples.
  4. Repeat the layers with the remaining apples and breadcrumbs.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.

These eight apple desserts are a perfect way to embrace the flavors and warmth of fall. Whether you’re craving a slice of apple pie or a caramel-drizzled cheesecake bar, there’s something for everyone in this collection. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let the sweet smell of baked apples fill your home this autumn. Happy baking!
