Homestyle Chicken and Dumplings: A Southern Comfort Classic


There’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of homestyle chicken and dumplings to bring comfort to your soul. This Southern classic is a dish that has been passed down through generations, and for good reason. With its rich flavors, tender chicken, and fluffy dumplings, it’s a meal that warms both the body and the heart. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make this comforting dish from scratch, using simple ingredients that you probably already have in your pantry.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant.

  2. While the vegetables are cooking, season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Add the chicken to the pot and brown on all sides. This will help develop a deep flavor in the broth.

  3. Once the chicken is browned, add the chicken broth, bay leaf, and enough water to cover the chicken. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  4. While the chicken is simmering, prepare the dumplings. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Slowly add the milk, stirring until a soft dough forms.

  5. After the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and shred the meat using two forks. Discard the bones and return the shredded chicken to the pot.

  6. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the broth to a rolling boil. Drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough into the boiling broth, making sure to leave enough space between each dumpling. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the dumplings simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through and fluffy.

  7. Give the pot a gentle stir to combine the chicken, dumplings, and broth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  8. Ladle the chicken and dumplings into bowls, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve hot.

Tips and Variations

  • For a richer broth, you can add a splash of heavy cream or whole milk during the final minutes of cooking.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in the dish. You can add peas, corn, or even mushrooms for extra flavor and texture.
  • If you prefer a thicker broth, you can mix a small amount of cornstarch with cold water and stir it into the broth. Simmer for a few additional minutes until thickened.
  • Leftover chicken and dumplings can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a pot on the stove or in the microwave.

Enjoy the warmth and comfort of this homestyle chicken and dumplings recipe. With its hearty flavors and tender dumplings, it’s sure to become a family favorite. Serve it on a chilly evening or whenever you need a little taste of Southern comfort.
