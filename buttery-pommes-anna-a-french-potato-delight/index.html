Buttery Pommes Anna: A French Potato Delight


If you’re looking for a show-stopping side dish that will impress your guests, look no further than Buttery Pommes Anna. This classic French dish may sound fancy, but it’s surprisingly simple to make. With just a few ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a potato masterpiece that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a round baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. Start by peeling the potatoes and then slicing them into thin rounds, about 1/8 inch thick. You can use a mandolin or a sharp knife for this step, but be careful to keep the slices uniform in thickness.

  3. Once you’ve sliced all the potatoes, it’s time to start assembling the dish. Take one potato slice and brush it with melted butter on both sides. Place it in the center of the baking dish. Repeat this process, slightly overlapping the potato slices, until you’ve covered the entire bottom of the dish.

  4. Once you’ve formed the first layer, brush it with more melted butter and sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste. Repeat the layering process with the remaining potato slices, brushing each layer with butter and seasoning with salt and pepper as you go. Make sure to press down gently on each layer to create a tight, compact stack.

  5. Once you’ve used up all the potato slices, brush the top layer with more melted butter and sprinkle with a bit more salt and pepper. This will give your Pommes Anna a beautiful golden crust.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes. After the initial 45 minutes, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the potatoes are tender when pierced with a knife.

  7. Once the Pommes Anna is cooked through, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Carefully invert the dish onto a serving platter, allowing the golden crust to become the top.

  8. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves, if desired, and serve immediately. The crispy, buttery layers of potato will melt in your mouth, creating a truly indulgent experience.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add layers of thinly sliced onions or grated cheese between the potato slices. This will add an extra layer of flavor and texture to your Pommes Anna.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the dish to your liking. Rosemary, garlic, or paprika can all add a unique touch to the potatoes.
  • If you’re short on time, you can prepare the Pommes Anna ahead of time and refrigerate it until you’re ready to bake. Just make sure to bring it to room temperature before baking.

Conclusion

Buttery Pommes Anna is a truly elegant side dish that will elevate any meal. Its crispy, buttery layers and melt-in-your-mouth texture make it a favorite among both home cooks and professional chefs. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to something special, give this classic French potato delight a try. Your taste buds will thank you.
