Simple Rice Dishes for a Quick Weeknight Dinner


Weeknight dinners can sometimes feel like a challenge, especially when you’re pressed for time but still want something flavorful and satisfying. One of the most versatile and comforting ingredients you can always rely on is rice. It’s quick, easy to prepare, and pairs wonderfully with a variety of ingredients to create simple, delicious meals. If you’re looking for easy rice-based recipes to whip up after a busy day, this post is for you. Here are some fantastic simple rice dishes that are perfect for a quick weeknight dinner!

1. One-Pot Chicken and Rice

Chicken and rice is a classic combination that never goes out of style. The beauty of this one-pot dish is that it’s simple to make, uses minimal ingredients, and comes together quickly. All you need is some chicken, rice, a few veggies, and a few seasonings. You can also customize this dish based on what you have in your fridge, making it a perfect recipe to use up leftovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs)
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ cup frozen peas (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, paprika, and thyme.
  2. Cook the chicken breasts in the skillet for about 5-6 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the rice to the skillet and stir for about 1 minute to lightly toast the rice.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 15-18 minutes, until the rice is cooked through.
  6. Return the chicken to the skillet and place it on top of the rice. Cover and cook for an additional 5 minutes to ensure the chicken is heated through and the rice has absorbed all the flavors.
  7. If using peas, stir them in just before serving. Serve warm, and enjoy this one-pot wonder!

2. Vegetable Fried Rice

Fried rice is a great dish to make when you have leftover rice sitting in your fridge. It’s quick, easy, and the possibilities for customization are endless. Packed with vegetables and full of savory flavor, vegetable fried rice makes for a light yet filling meal. You can add protein like scrambled eggs, tofu, or even shrimp if you like.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the sesame oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion, carrot, and peas. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  2. If you’re using eggs, push the vegetables to one side of the pan. Crack the eggs into the empty space, scramble them, and cook until they are fully set. Mix the eggs with the vegetables.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the cold cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with a spoon or spatula. Stir everything together to combine.
  5. Pour in the soy sauce and rice vinegar. Stir well to coat the rice evenly.
  6. Cook for another 3-4 minutes until everything is heated through and slightly crispy. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.

3. Mushroom Risotto

Risotto might seem like a dish reserved for special occasions, but it can actually be a quick and satisfying weeknight dinner. With the right technique, you can prepare a creamy, flavorful mushroom risotto in just 30 minutes. The key is to add the broth gradually and stir constantly, which helps the rice release its natural starch and create that creamy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced (use any variety you prefer)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth (warm)
  • ½ cup white wine (optional)
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat the olive oil and butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes until they begin to release their moisture and turn golden brown.
  3. Stir in the garlic and cook for 30 seconds, allowing the garlic to become fragrant.
  4. Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir for 1-2 minutes to lightly toast the rice.
  5. Pour in the white wine (if using) and stir until it is mostly absorbed by the rice.
  6. Begin adding the warm broth, one ladleful at a time. Stir the rice frequently, allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and cooked through, about 18-20 minutes.
  7. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot and enjoy!

4. Lemon Garlic Shrimp and Rice

For a light yet flavorful dinner, try this lemon garlic shrimp and rice dish. The combination of shrimp, garlic, and fresh lemon creates a bright, refreshing flavor that pairs beautifully with rice. It’s perfect for a quick weeknight meal and can be ready in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions, using chicken broth for extra flavor. Once cooked, set it aside and keep warm.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet, seasoning with salt and pepper. Cook the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and fully cooked.
  4. Stir in the lemon zest and lemon juice, then remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.
  5. In the same skillet, add the cooked rice, stirring to absorb all the flavors. Season with additional salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss everything together to combine.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

5. Coconut Rice with Grilled Vegetables

Coconut rice is a fragrant, slightly sweet dish that pairs wonderfully with grilled vegetables. This dish is perfect for vegetarians or anyone looking for a plant-based meal. The creamy texture of the coconut milk in the rice is the perfect complement to the smoky, charred vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium pot, combine the jasmine rice, coconut milk, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once it begins to boil, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  3. While the rice is cooking, heat a grill pan or outdoor grill over medium-high heat. Toss the sliced zucchini, bell pepper, and onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Grill the vegetables for 4-5 minutes on each side until they are tender and have nice grill marks.
  5. Once the rice is ready, fluff it with a fork and serve it with the grilled vegetables on top. Garnish with fresh cilantro and enjoy!

Conclusion

Rice is a pantry staple that can be transformed into a wide variety of meals, whether you’re in the mood for something hearty, light, or full of flavor. These simple rice dishes are perfect for quick weeknight dinners, offering a range of flavors and textures to keep your meals exciting. Whether you’re making a comforting one-pot meal, a vibrant fried rice, or a creamy risotto, rice will never disappoint. So, next time you’re looking for a quick and satisfying dinner, turn to rice for an easy, tasty solution that everyone will love!
