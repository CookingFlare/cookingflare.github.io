Spring Blossoms in Your Kitchen: Floral Infused Recipes


Welcome to a delightful journey of flavors where we bring the essence of spring blossoms into your kitchen with enchanting floral-infused recipes. As nature comes alive with vibrant blooms, why not bring that freshness into your cooking? From delicate petals to fragrant blossoms, let’s explore how these floral ingredients can elevate your dishes to a whole new level.

Lavender Lemonade

Servings: 4 | Preparation time: 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup dried lavender flowers
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices and lavender sprigs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Steep the dried lavender flowers in hot water for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Strain the lavender-infused water and discard the flowers.
  3. In a pitcher, combine the lavender water, fresh lemon juice, honey, and cold water. Stir well.
  4. Chill the lavender lemonade in the refrigerator.
  5. Serve over ice cubes and garnish with lemon slices and lavender sprigs.

Rose Petal Jam

Yields: Approximately 2 cups | Cooking time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh rose petals (make sure they are pesticide-free)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups sugar
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rose petals gently and pat them dry.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the rose petals and water. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes.
  3. Add sugar and lemon juice to the rose petal mixture. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Continue cooking until the mixture thickens to a jam-like consistency.
  5. Let the rose petal jam cool before transferring it to sterilized jars.

Hibiscus Tacos with Mango Salsa

Servings: 2-3 | Preparation time: 30 minutes

Ingredients: For the hibiscus tacos:

  • 1 cup dried hibiscus flowers
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 small corn tortillas

For the mango salsa:

  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste

Instructions: For the hibiscus tacos:

  1. Boil the dried hibiscus flowers in water for 10-15 minutes until tender. Drain and chop the flowers.
  2. In a skillet, sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until translucent.
  3. Add the chopped hibiscus, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas and fill them with the hibiscus mixture.

For the mango salsa:

  1. In a bowl, combine diced mango, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Serve the hibiscus tacos with mango salsa on top.

Violet Infused Shortbread Cookies

Yields: 2 dozen cookies | Baking time: 12-15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup fresh violet petals
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream together butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Gradually add the flour, violet petals, vanilla extract, and salt. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Roll out the dough and cut into desired shapes using cookie cutters.
  5. Place the cookies on the baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes until lightly golden.
  6. Let the cookies cool before serving.
