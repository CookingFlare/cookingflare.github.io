Korean Noodle Stir-Fry: A Quick and Easy Recipe


In the heart of Korean cuisine, noodles play a vital role in many dishes. Among the various types of noodles, Japchae () stands out as a popular and iconic Korean dish made from stir-fried glass noodles. Japchae is a staple in Korean households and is often served as a side dish or a light meal. In this recipe, we will explore a quick and easy way to make a delicious Korean noodle stir-fry that will satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 200g glass noodles (dangmyeon)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mushrooms (button or cremini), sliced
  • 1 cup zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup carrots, peeled and grated
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Glass Noodles

Rinse the glass noodles in cold water, then soak them in hot water for about 5-7 minutes or according to the package instructions. Drain and cut the noodles into shorter lengths.

Step 2: Heat the Wok or Large Skillet

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the sliced onions and cook until they are translucent and starting to caramelize.

Step 3: Add the Garlic and Mushrooms

Add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms to the wok or skillet. Cook until the mushrooms release their moisture and start to brown.

Step 4: Add the Zucchini and Carrots

Add the sliced zucchini and grated carrots to the wok or skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender-crisp, stirring occasionally.

Step 5: Add the Glass Noodles and Seasonings

Add the cooked glass noodles to the wok or skillet. Stir to combine with the vegetables. Add the soy sauce, Gochujang, and sesame oil. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the noodles are well coated with the sauce.

Step 6: Season and Serve

Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Transfer the Korean noodle stir-fry to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier version, add more Gochujang or use Korean chili flakes (gochugaru).
  • To add protein, stir-fry cooked chicken, beef, or tofu with the vegetables.
  • Experiment with different vegetables, such as bell peppers, snow peas, or bok choy.
  • For a vegan version, omit the sesame oil and use a neutral-tasting oil instead.

Conclusion

Korean noodle stir-fry is a quick and easy recipe that can be prepared in under 30 minutes. This dish is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a light meal. With its combination of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors, it’s sure to satisfy your cravings and leave you wanting more. So go ahead and give this recipe a try, and enjoy the delicious flavors of Korea!
