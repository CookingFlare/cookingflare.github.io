Delightful Thanksgiving Stuffed Acorn Squash Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in delicious comfort foods. And what better way to celebrate this special occasion than with a delightful stuffed acorn squash dish? Acorn squash not only adds a touch of elegance to your Thanksgiving table, but its sweet and nutty flavor pairs perfectly with a variety of fillings. In this blog post, we will explore three unique and mouthwatering stuffed acorn squash recipes that are sure to impress your guests. So without further ado, let’s dive into the world of Thanksgiving flavors!

1. Quinoa and Cranberry Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up.
  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the squash halves and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast the squash in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until tender.
  5. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, fresh parsley, and crumbled feta cheese. Mix well.
  6. Once the squash is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it cool slightly.
  7. Spoon the quinoa mixture into each squash half, filling them generously.
  8. Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the filling is heated through and lightly browned.
  9. Serve the stuffed acorn squash as a main dish or a side dish alongside your Thanksgiving feast. Enjoy!

2. Sausage and Apple Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 pound ground sausage (your choice of mild or spicy)
  • 1 apple, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 1/2 cup diced mushrooms
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground sausage over medium heat until browned. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the diced apple, onion, celery, and mushrooms. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Return the cooked sausage to the skillet and stir in the dried sage, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  6. Spoon the sausage and apple mixture into each squash half, filling them generously.
  7. Cover the baking sheet with foil and roast the stuffed squash in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the squash is tender and the filling is heated through.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.
  9. Serve the sausage and apple stuffed acorn squash as a hearty and flavorful addition to your Thanksgiving meal. Enjoy!

3. Wild Rice and Mushroom Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked wild rice
  • 1 cup diced mushrooms (your choice of variety)
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up.
  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the diced mushrooms, onion, and minced garlic to the skillet. Cook until the mushrooms are tender and the onions are translucent.
  5. Stir in the cooked wild rice and chopped fresh parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well.
  6. Spoon the wild rice and mushroom mixture into each squash half, filling them generously.
  7. Cover the baking sheet with foil and roast the stuffed squash in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the squash is tender and the filling is heated through.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is lightly browned.
  9. Serve the wild rice and mushroom stuffed acorn squash as a wholesome and earthy dish that will tantalize your taste buds on Thanksgiving Day. Enjoy!

These delightful stuffed acorn squash recipes are just a taste of the endless possibilities that await in your Thanksgiving kitchen. Get creative and experiment with different fillings and flavors to make them your own. Whether you choose a vegetarian option, a meaty indulgence, or a combination of both, these stuffed acorn squash recipes are guaranteed to elevate your Thanksgiving feast to new heights. So gather your loved ones, give thanks, and savor every bite of these delicious creations. Happy Thanksgiving!
