Maple Glazed Ham: A Showstopping Fall Dinner Centerpiece


Fall is a season that brings with it an array of warm and comforting flavors. And what better way to celebrate the season than with a showstopping Maple Glazed Ham? This delicious centerpiece is not only visually stunning but also incredibly flavorful, making it the perfect choice for your next fall dinner gathering.

The Perfect Maple Glaze

The secret to a truly delicious Maple Glazed Ham lies in the glaze. The combination of sweet and savory flavors creates a harmonious balance that will leave your taste buds dancing. Here’s how to create the perfect maple glaze:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the maple syrup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cinnamon, and ground cloves.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar has dissolved and the glaze has thickened slightly.
  3. Remove the glaze from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.

Preparing the Ham

Now that you have your delicious maple glaze ready, it’s time to prepare the ham. Follow these simple steps to ensure a perfectly cooked and flavorful centerpiece:

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked spiral-cut ham (8-10 pounds)
  • Whole cloves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Place the ham on a rack in a shallow roasting pan.
  3. Score the surface of the ham with a sharp knife in a diamond pattern, being careful not to cut too deep.
  4. Insert whole cloves into the ham at the intersections of the score marks, if desired.
  5. Brush the ham generously with the maple glaze, making sure to coat all sides.
  6. Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil, ensuring that it is not tightly wrapped.
  7. Place the ham in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 12-15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  8. Every 30 minutes, remove the ham from the oven and brush it with more glaze to build up a beautiful, sticky crust.
  9. Once the ham is fully cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before carving.

Serving and Pairing

Your Maple Glazed Ham is now ready to be the star of your fall dinner table. Serve it alongside some traditional fall sides like roasted sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, or a warm apple and cranberry salad. The sweet and savory flavors of the ham will complement the earthy flavors of these dishes perfectly.

For a complete fall dinner experience, pair your Maple Glazed Ham with a glass of crisp apple cider or a full-bodied red wine. The combination of flavors will elevate your meal to a whole new level.

Final Thoughts

Maple Glazed Ham is not just a delicious and visually stunning centerpiece; it’s a testament to the flavors of fall. The sweet and savory combination of the maple glaze and tender ham is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving more. So, why wait? Bring the flavors of fall to your dinner table with this showstopping Maple Glazed Ham. Happy cooking!
