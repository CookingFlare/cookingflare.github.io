10 Delicious Fall Recipes for Your Next Potluck


Fall is a time for cozy gatherings, crisp air, and delicious comfort food that brings people together. When it comes to potlucks, the options for crowd-pleasing dishes are endless. Whether you’re attending a festive dinner or hosting a fun get-together with friends and family, the fall season offers an array of flavors that are perfect for sharing. From hearty soups to indulgent desserts, here are 10 fall recipes that will make your next potluck a hit.

1. Butternut Squash Soup

A creamy, rich butternut squash soup is the ultimate fall comfort food. The natural sweetness of the squash paired with savory herbs and spices makes for an irresistible dish that will warm everyone up.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic over medium heat until softened.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, vegetable broth, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the squash is tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a creamier texture, stir in the heavy cream.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This soup can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days, making it a great choice for a potluck.

2. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts often get a bad rap, but when roasted to perfection with crispy bacon and a drizzle of balsamic glaze, they become a savory, irresistible side dish. This recipe combines the earthy flavor of Brussels sprouts with the smoky richness of bacon, creating a winning combination.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes, tossing halfway through, until the Brussels sprouts are golden and crispy.
  4. While the sprouts are roasting, cook the bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside.
  5. In the same skillet, combine balsamic vinegar and honey. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the glaze thickens.
  6. Once the Brussels sprouts are done roasting, toss them with the crispy bacon and drizzle with the balsamic glaze before serving.

This dish is perfect for a fall potluck, offering a delightful balance of flavors and textures that everyone will love.

3. Apple Cider Glazed Chicken Wings

Chicken wings are always a crowd-pleaser, and this fall-inspired version features a sweet and tangy apple cider glaze. The glaze caramelizes as the wings cook, giving them a deliciously sticky coating that will have everyone reaching for more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken wings
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange the chicken wings on a baking sheet and season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a saucepan, combine apple cider, brown sugar, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, cinnamon, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, until the glaze thickens.
  4. Brush the glaze over the chicken wings and bake for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until the wings are crispy and golden.
  5. Serve with extra glaze on the side for dipping.

These sweet and savory wings are perfect for sharing at a potluck and will disappear quickly!

4. Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallow Topping

Sweet potato casserole is a beloved fall dish, and this version with a fluffy marshmallow topping adds a nostalgic touch to any potluck. The sweet potatoes are mashed with brown sugar and butter, then topped with toasted marshmallows for a creamy, sweet side dish everyone will enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 2 cups mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in a large pot for 15-20 minutes until they are fork-tender. Drain and mash them.
  3. Stir in the melted butter, brown sugar, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mashed sweet potatoes into a greased baking dish.
  5. Top with mini marshmallows and bake for 20-25 minutes until the marshmallows are golden brown.
  6. Let the casserole cool slightly before serving.

This sweet potato casserole is a perfect balance of sweetness and richness, making it an ideal choice for a fall potluck.

5. Pumpkin Pasta Salad

Pumpkin isn’t just for pies and soups—it also makes a great addition to pasta salad! This unique pumpkin pasta salad is packed with autumn flavors, from the savory roasted pumpkin to the tangy dressing. It’s a delightful dish that will stand out at your next potluck.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta (such as penne or fusilli)
  • 1 small pumpkin, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil

For the dressing:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed pumpkin with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread it in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, until tender.
  3. Cook the pasta according to package instructions, then drain and set aside to cool.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  5. In a large bowl, toss the cooled pasta with the roasted pumpkin, feta cheese, pumpkin seeds, and fresh basil. Drizzle with the dressing and toss to combine.
  6. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

This pumpkin pasta salad is a unique and flavorful dish that’s sure to be a conversation starter at your potluck.

6. Cranberry Pecan Bread

Bread is always a favorite at potlucks, and this cranberry pecan bread adds a fall twist with tart cranberries and crunchy pecans. It’s the perfect side to complement savory dishes or as a snack with a cup of cider.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a loaf pan and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Gently fold in the cranberries and pecans.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 60-70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

This cranberry pecan bread is a sweet and slightly tart treat that will complement any fall-themed potluck.

7. Maple Roasted Carrots

Roasted vegetables are always a hit, and these maple roasted carrots add a touch of sweetness and depth that makes them stand out. The maple syrup caramelizes in the oven, creating a delicious glaze that makes each bite irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the carrot sticks with olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper. Spread them out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes, turning halfway through, until the carrots are tender and caramelized.
  4. Garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

These sweet and savory roasted carrots are a perfect side dish for your fall potluck.

8. Spiced Pumpkin Cupcakes

Pumpkin cupcakes are the epitome of fall desserts. With a moist texture and warm spices, they’re topped with a rich cream cheese frosting that adds just the right amount of tanginess. These little treats are perfect for a potluck.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the oil, sugar, eggs, pumpkin puree, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Let the cupcakes cool completely before frosting.

To make the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then frost the cooled cupcakes.

These spiced pumpkin cupcakes are perfect for sharing at a potluck, offering a moist and flavorful dessert that everyone will enjoy.

9. Caramel Apple Dip

If you’re looking for a fun and interactive dish to bring to a potluck, a caramel apple dip is the way to go. This creamy, sweet dip pairs perfectly with crisp apple slices for a delicious fall snack.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup caramel sauce
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth.
  2. Add the brown sugar, caramel sauce, and vanilla extract, and beat until combined.
  3. Serve with sliced apples for dipping, and top with chopped pecans if desired.

This easy-to-make caramel apple dip is a fun and flavorful treat that will be the star of your potluck.

10. Spiced Pear Crisp

For a dessert that’s both comforting and seasonal, a spiced pear crisp is the perfect choice. The tender pears are combined with warm spices and topped with a buttery, oat-filled topping that bakes up golden and crispy.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe pears, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced pears with brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Transfer to a greased baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, butter, and salt. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat topping over the pears.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the topping is golden brown and the pears are tender.
  6. Let the crisp cool slightly before serving.

This spiced pear crisp is a warm and comforting fall dessert that’s perfect for a potluck gathering.

Fall is a season full of comforting flavors, and these 10 recipes are just the tip of the iceberg. Whether you’re looking for savory dishes, sweet treats, or something in between, these fall recipes are sure to impress at your next potluck.
