Farm to Table Poultry Recipes: From Farmyard to Fork


Welcome to our farm-inspired cooking adventure! In this blog post, we will explore a range of delicious poultry recipes that celebrate the concept of “farm to table.” From tender chicken to succulent turkey, these recipes will take you on a culinary journey that highlights the freshness and flavor of locally sourced ingredients. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get cooking!

1. Roasted Lemon Herb Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (approximately 4-5 pounds)
  • 2 lemons, sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Rinse the chicken thoroughly and pat it dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Rub the mixture all over the chicken, ensuring it is evenly coated.
  5. Place the sliced lemons inside the chicken cavity.
  6. Tie the chicken legs together with kitchen twine.
  7. Drizzle olive oil over the chicken skin.
  8. Place the chicken on a roasting rack in a roasting pan.
  9. Roast the chicken in the preheated oven for approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  10. Let the chicken rest for 10 minutes before carving. Enjoy!

2. Farmhouse Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, onion, garlic, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs, approximately 1 inch in diameter.
  4. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through and golden brown.
  6. Serve the meatballs with warm marinara sauce and enjoy!

3. Herbed Cornish Game Hens

Ingredients:

  • 2 Cornish game hens
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Rinse the Cornish game hens and pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, lemon juice, minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the herb mixture all over the Cornish game hens, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  5. Place the game hens on the grill, breast-side down, and cook for approximately 15 minutes.
  6. Flip the game hens and continue grilling for another 15-20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  7. Remove the game hens from the grill and let them rest for 5 minutes before serving. Enjoy!

4. Rustic Duck Breast with Cherry Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 2 duck breasts
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup pitted cherries
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Score the skin of the duck breasts in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to cut into the meat.
  3. Season the duck breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. Place the duck breasts, skin-side down, in a cold oven-safe skillet.
  5. Turn the heat to medium and cook the duck breasts for approximately 8-10 minutes, or until the skin is crispy and golden brown.
  6. Flip the duck breasts and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Cook for another 6-8 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare.
  7. Remove the duck breasts from the skillet and let them rest for 5 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, in a small saucepan, combine the pitted cherries, balsamic vinegar, honey, and butter. Cook over medium heat until the cherries are soft and the sauce has thickened slightly, about 5 minutes.
  9. Slice the duck breasts and drizzle the cherry glaze over the top. Serve and enjoy!

Conclusion

These farm-inspired poultry recipes offer a taste of freshness and quality that only farm-to-table cooking can provide. By using locally sourced ingredients, you not only support your local farmers but also enhance the flavors and nutritional value of your meals. So, whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply satisfying your culinary cravings, don’t hesitate to give these recipes a try. From the farmyard to your fork, enjoy the deliciousness of farm-to-table poultry dishes!
