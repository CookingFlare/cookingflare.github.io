How to Cook with a Slow Cooker in College


Cooking in college can be a challenge. Between studying for exams, attending lectures, and trying to maintain a social life, the idea of preparing home-cooked meals might seem like a distant dream. But what if there was a way to eat nutritious, delicious meals without spending hours in the kitchen? Enter the slow cooker.

A slow cooker, also known as a crockpot, is a versatile kitchen appliance that can help you create a wide variety of meals with minimal effort. Whether you’re living in a dorm, an apartment, or sharing a house with friends, a slow cooker is a game-changer. In this blog post, we’ll explore why slow cooking is perfect for college students and how you can make the most out of this appliance.

Why You Should Use a Slow Cooker in College

1. Minimal Effort for Maximum Reward

One of the best features of a slow cooker is that it allows you to make hearty, flavorful meals with little effort. You don’t need to spend hours chopping vegetables or stirring over a hot stove. Instead, you can simply toss your ingredients into the slow cooker, set it, and let it do all the work. This means you can focus on studying or relaxing while your meal cooks itself.

2. Time-Saving

College life is busy, and time is a precious commodity. Slow cookers work on a “set it and forget it” principle. Once you’ve assembled your ingredients, you can let the slow cooker do its magic. Whether you’re preparing a stew, soup, or casserole, the slow cooker will take care of the cooking process, leaving you with plenty of time to focus on other things.

3. Cost-Effective

As a college student, you’re probably looking for ways to save money on food. Cooking with a slow cooker is an excellent way to make affordable meals that are both filling and nutritious. You can buy ingredients in bulk, use less expensive cuts of meat, and stretch your ingredients to create multiple servings. Plus, slow cookers are energy-efficient compared to traditional ovens or stovetops, which can help you save on electricity bills.

4. Healthy Meals

In college, it can be easy to rely on takeout or processed foods, but these options aren’t always the healthiest. A slow cooker allows you to control the ingredients you use, which means you can make healthier meals. You can load your dishes with fresh vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains, ensuring that you’re getting the nutrients you need to stay energized and focused.

5. Batch Cooking for Leftovers

One of the greatest advantages of using a slow cooker is the ability to make large batches of food. This is perfect for college students who don’t have the time or energy to cook every day. You can prepare a big pot of soup, chili, or stew, and have leftovers for the next few days. Leftovers can be easily stored in the fridge or freezer, making it simple to reheat and enjoy a home-cooked meal later in the week.

How to Use a Slow Cooker in College

1. Start Simple: Choose Easy Recipes

If you’ve never used a slow cooker before, it’s important to start with simple recipes that don’t require too much prep. Dishes like chili, chicken stew, and vegetable soups are great beginner recipes. These meals often require basic ingredients and can be customized based on your preferences.

Here’s a basic recipe to get you started:

Slow Cooker Chicken and Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts (boneless, skinless)
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 cup frozen peas

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts at the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. Add all the vegetables, garlic, and spices.
  3. Pour the chicken broth over the ingredients.
  4. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours.
  5. About 30 minutes before serving, shred the chicken using two forks.
  6. Stir in the frozen peas and let the soup finish cooking.

This recipe is simple, filling, and nutritious. Plus, it’s easy to store leftovers for later.

2. Choose the Right Ingredients

When cooking with a slow cooker, it’s important to choose the right ingredients. Tough cuts of meat, such as beef chuck or pork shoulder, work well in slow cookers because they become tender and flavorful after hours of slow cooking. Similarly, root vegetables like potatoes, carrots, and parsnips do well in slow cookers since they take longer to cook than other vegetables.

Here are some other great ingredients to try:

  • Beans: Dried beans are an excellent, cost-effective choice. Just make sure to soak them overnight before cooking.
  • Rice and Grains: Brown rice, quinoa, and barley can all be cooked in a slow cooker and make great additions to your meals.
  • Canned Goods: Canned tomatoes, beans, and corn can help bulk up your slow cooker meals without adding much cost.

3. Adjust Cooking Times

Not all slow cookers are created equal. Some models cook faster than others, and the temperature settings can vary. It’s essential to adjust your cooking times based on your slow cooker. Most recipes provide a range for cooking times—either 4-6 hours on high or 6-8 hours on low—but be sure to check your food periodically to ensure it’s cooked to your liking.

4. Prep Ingredients in Advance

To make things even easier, consider prepping your ingredients ahead of time. You can chop vegetables, measure spices, and even portion out meats in advance. This can save you a lot of time during the week when you’re in a rush to get dinner ready. Another option is to assemble your ingredients in the slow cooker insert and store it in the fridge overnight. In the morning, all you need to do is plug in the slow cooker and turn it on.

5. Use Slow Cooker Liners

Cleaning up after cooking can be a hassle, but slow cooker liners make the process much easier. These disposable liners fit inside the slow cooker, allowing you to simply lift out the liner and discard it after cooking. This minimizes cleanup and makes using the slow cooker even more convenient.

6. Experiment with Flavor

The beauty of slow cooking is that you can experiment with a variety of flavors. Slow cookers allow the flavors of your ingredients to meld together over time, resulting in rich, complex dishes. Don’t be afraid to try different herbs, spices, and seasonings. You can make everything from savory stews to sweet desserts. Adding a little bit of extra seasoning or sauce can completely change the flavor profile of your dish.

Slow Cooker Meal Ideas for College Students

1. Chili

Chili is a classic slow cooker dish that’s hearty, filling, and perfect for meal prepping. You can make a simple beef chili with beans and tomatoes, or experiment with turkey or vegetarian versions. Chili is also a great way to use up leftover vegetables and grains.

2. Curry

Curries are another great option for slow cooking. Whether you’re making a chicken curry, vegetable curry, or beef curry, the slow cooker allows the spices to develop deep flavors over time. Serve it over rice or with naan bread for a satisfying meal.

3. Tacos

Making taco fillings in a slow cooker is a fantastic way to simplify dinner. Try slow-cooked beef, chicken, or pork, and use the meat for tacos, burritos, or taco bowls. Add toppings like salsa, avocado, and cheese for extra flavor.

4. Pulled Pork

Pulled pork is incredibly easy to make in a slow cooker. Simply season a pork shoulder, add a little liquid (like broth or barbecue sauce), and cook on low for 8-10 hours. Once the pork is tender, shred it with forks and serve on buns or in wraps.

5. Breakfast Options

Slow cookers are not just for dinner. You can also use them to make breakfast dishes like oatmeal, frittatas, and egg casseroles. Try cooking overnight oats or a savory egg bake for a simple, satisfying breakfast.

6. Desserts

Yes, you can even make desserts in your slow cooker! From chocolate lava cakes to fruit cobblers, the slow cooker is perfect for creating indulgent treats without the need for a traditional oven.

Tips for Using a Slow Cooker in College

  • Invest in a Small Slow Cooker: If you’re living in a dorm room with limited space, a smaller 3-4 quart slow cooker is ideal. It’s compact and easy to store, yet big enough to make meals for one or two people.

  • Share Meals with Roommates: One of the best things about slow cooking is that it allows you to make big batches of food. This is perfect for sharing meals with roommates, helping you save money and time. You can take turns cooking or each contribute ingredients to the meal.

  • Use Leftovers Wisely: Leftovers are a huge benefit of using a slow cooker. Don’t let them go to waste—use them in sandwiches, salads, or as ingredients for another dish.

  • Experiment with Different Cuisines: The slow cooker is a versatile tool, so don’t limit yourself to just one type of cuisine. Try making Asian-inspired dishes, Mediterranean stews, or even Italian pasta sauces.

Final Thoughts

Slow cooking is an excellent option for college students who want to save time, eat healthily, and enjoy home-cooked meals without the stress of spending hours in the kitchen. Whether you’re cooking a simple chicken soup or a hearty chili, the slow cooker will help you create flavorful, filling meals with minimal effort. So, next time you’re at the store, grab a slow cooker and start experimenting with new recipes—you’ll be surprised at just how easy and delicious college cooking can be!
