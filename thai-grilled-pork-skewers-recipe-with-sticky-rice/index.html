Thai Grilled Pork Skewers Recipe with Sticky Rice


If you’re looking to add some authentic Thai flavors to your grilling repertoire, look no further than this mouthwatering Thai grilled pork skewers recipe with sticky rice. This dish encapsulates the essence of Thai cuisine – bold, aromatic, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of Thailand, these grilled pork skewers are sure to impress your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok. Paired with fragrant sticky rice, this dish is a complete sensory experience that you won’t soon forget.

Ingredients

For the Pork Skewers:

  • 1.5 pounds of pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground white pepper
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

For the Sticky Rice:

  • 2 cups of glutinous rice
  • Water for soaking
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Banana leaves (optional)

Instructions

Marinating the Pork:

  1. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped shallots, minced lemongrass, fish sauce, oyster sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, and ground white pepper. Mix well to create a fragrant marinade.
  2. Add the thinly sliced pork shoulder to the marinade, ensuring that each piece is thoroughly coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to infuse into the pork.

Preparing the Sticky Rice:

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Transfer the rice to a bowl and cover with water, allowing it to soak for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, drain the rice and place it in a steamer lined with a clean cloth. Steam the rice for approximately 20-25 minutes, or until it is tender and cooked through.
  3. In a separate saucepan, gently heat the coconut milk and salt, stirring until the salt has dissolved. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl and slowly pour the coconut milk over the rice, gently folding it in to achieve a sticky, fragrant consistency. If using banana leaves, you can wrap the sticky rice in them for an added aromatic touch.

Grilling the Pork Skewers:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated pork slices onto the soaked bamboo skewers, ensuring an even distribution.
  2. Place the pork skewers on the grill, allowing them to cook for approximately 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are caramelized and cooked through. The lemongrass and garlic in the marinade will create a tantalizing aroma as the pork sizzles on the grill, enticing your senses with their fragrance.
  3. Once the pork skewers are grilled to perfection, remove them from the heat and let them rest for a few minutes.

Serving:

To serve, arrange the fragrant sticky rice on a platter, accompanied by the sizzling grilled pork skewers. Garnish with fresh cilantro, sliced cucumbers, and a wedge of lime for a burst of freshness. The combination of the savory, succulent pork skewers and the aromatic sticky rice creates a harmony of flavors that will leave you craving more. This dish pairs beautifully with a chilled Thai beer or a refreshing lemongrass-infused iced tea.

Conclusion

This Thai grilled pork skewers recipe with sticky rice is a celebration of bold flavors and aromatic ingredients that are synonymous with Thai cuisine. The marriage of lemongrass, garlic, and shallots in the marinade, combined with the fragrant sticky rice, creates a culinary experience that is both comforting and exciting. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe invites you to embark on a flavorful journey that will delight your palate and transport you to the vibrant streets of Thailand. So fire up your grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the irresistible allure of Thai cuisine right in your own home.
