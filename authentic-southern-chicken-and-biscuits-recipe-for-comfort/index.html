Authentic Southern Chicken and Biscuits Recipe for Comfort


If you’re looking for a comforting and delicious meal that will transport you straight to the heart of the South, then this Authentic Southern Chicken and Biscuits recipe is exactly what you need. With its flavorful fried chicken and flaky homemade biscuits, this dish is sure to satisfy your cravings and warm your soul. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the fried chicken:

  • 4 chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the biscuits:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions

Fried Chicken:

  1. Start by marinating the chicken breasts in buttermilk. Place the chicken in a shallow dish and pour the buttermilk over it. Make sure all the chicken is coated. Cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a seasoned flour mixture.

  3. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk and let any excess drip off. Dip the chicken breasts into the seasoned flour mixture, making sure to coat them evenly.

  4. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or deep fryer to about 350°F (175°C). Carefully place the coated chicken breasts into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes per side.

  5. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oil and transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Biscuits:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Pour in the milk and stir until a dough forms. Be careful not to overmix.

  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together. Roll the dough out to about 1/2 inch thick.

  5. Use a biscuit cutter or a round cookie cutter to cut out the biscuits. Place them on the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each biscuit.

  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the biscuits are golden brown and cooked through.

Serving Suggestions

Now that your Authentic Southern Chicken and Biscuits are ready, it’s time to serve them up and enjoy the comforting flavors of the South. You can serve the fried chicken with the biscuits on the side, or you can create a delicious sandwich by splitting the biscuits in half and adding a crispy chicken breast in between.

For a complete Southern meal, consider serving with collard greens, macaroni and cheese, or coleslaw. And don’t forget the hot sauce and honey for some extra flavor!

Conclusion

There’s something truly special about the combination of crispy fried chicken and flaky biscuits that makes it the ultimate comfort food. With this Authentic Southern Chicken and Biscuits recipe, you can bring a taste of the South to your own kitchen and enjoy a meal that’s both satisfying and nostalgic. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to indulge in a truly comforting Southern feast. Happy cooking!
