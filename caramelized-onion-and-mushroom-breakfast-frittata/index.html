Caramelized Onion and Mushroom Breakfast Frittata


There’s nothing quite like starting your day with a hearty and flavorful breakfast. If you’re looking to elevate your morning meal with a dish that’s both satisfying and packed with delicious flavors, then a Caramelized Onion and Mushroom Breakfast Frittata is just what you need. This savory egg-based dish is incredibly versatile, making it perfect for a leisurely weekend brunch or a quick weekday breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (such as Gruyere or cheddar)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Caramelize the Onions

Begin by caramelizing the onions to bring out their natural sweetness and depth of flavor. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they turn a rich golden brown, about 20-25 minutes.

Step 2: Sauté the Mushrooms

Once the onions are caramelized, add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet. Cook the mushrooms with the onions until they are tender and any liquid they release has evaporated, about 8-10 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.

Step 3: Prepare the Egg Mixture

In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, whole milk, salt, and black pepper until well combined. Pour the egg mixture over the caramelized onions and mushrooms in the skillet. Gently stir the mixture to distribute the vegetables evenly.

Step 4: Add the Cheese

Sprinkle the grated cheese over the top of the egg mixture. Allow the frittata to cook on the stovetop, without stirring, for 4-5 minutes, or until the edges start to set.

Step 5: Bake the Frittata

Preheat your oven’s broiler. Transfer the skillet to the oven and broil the frittata for 3-4 minutes, or until the top is set and golden brown. Keep a close eye on it to prevent overcooking.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Once the frittata is cooked to your liking, remove it from the oven and let it cool slightly. Garnish with fresh parsley, slice into wedges, and serve.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as cremini or shiitake, to add a unique flavor to your frittata.
  • Consider adding cooked and crumbled bacon or sausage for an extra boost of protein and flavor.
  • For a dairy-free option, substitute the whole milk and cheese with non-dairy alternatives like almond milk and dairy-free cheese.

Final Thoughts

The Caramelized Onion and Mushroom Breakfast Frittata is a delightful combination of savory flavors and creamy texture, making it a perfect centerpiece for any breakfast or brunch spread. This versatile dish allows for endless variations, so don’t be afraid to get creative with your favorite ingredients. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply treating yourself to a special morning meal, this frittata is sure to impress with its rich, satisfying taste and beautiful presentation.

So, the next time you’re craving a breakfast that’s both comforting and sophisticated, give this recipe a try and savor the delightful flavors of caramelized onions, earthy mushrooms, and creamy eggs all in one perfect bite.
