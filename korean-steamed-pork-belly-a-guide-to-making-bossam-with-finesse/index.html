Korean Steamed Pork Belly: A Guide to Making Bossam with Finesse


If you’re looking to add a touch of Korean culinary finesse to your cooking repertoire, then bossam, a traditional Korean steamed pork belly dish, might just be the perfect recipe to master. This beloved dish is not only delicious but also a wonderful example of the rich and flavorful Korean cuisine. Whether you’re hosting a special gathering or simply want to treat yourself to an exquisite meal, learning how to make bossam with finesse will undoubtedly elevate your culinary skills to new heights.

Understanding Bossam

Before delving into the nitty-gritty of preparing bossam, it’s essential to understand the dish itself. Bossam consists of thinly sliced, tender steamed pork belly, accompanied by an array of condiments and fresh vegetables. The key to perfecting bossam lies in achieving a melt-in-your-mouth texture for the pork belly while infusing it with a symphony of flavors. The dish is typically served with a variety of side dishes, including kimchi, pickled radishes, and a spicy dipping sauce, allowing for a customizable and delightful dining experience.

Ingredients

To get started on your bossam journey, let’s first gather the essential ingredients. For the pork belly, you’ll need:

  • 2.5 pounds of pork belly
  • 1 cup of Korean radish, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of ginger, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of green onions, chopped
  • 1 cup of onion, sliced
  • 1 cup of Korean chili pepper

For the steaming liquid and seasoning:

  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 cup of soybean paste
  • 1 cup of cooking wine
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper

For the condiments and sides:

  • Fresh lettuce leaves
  • Kimchi
  • Pickled radishes
  • Spicy dipping sauce

Preparation

  1. Prepare the Pork Belly: Rinse the pork belly under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Then, place the pork belly in a large pot.

  2. Prepare the Seasoning: In a separate bowl, mix together the soybean paste, cooking wine, soy sauce, sugar, and black pepper. Once combined, pour the mixture over the pork belly in the pot.

  3. Add Aromatics: Toss in the sliced Korean radish, minced garlic, ginger, green onions, sliced onion, and Korean chili pepper into the pot with the pork belly and seasoning.

  4. Steam the Pork Belly: Cover the pot and bring the contents to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and steam the pork belly for approximately 2 to 2.5 hours, or until it’s tender and easily pierced with a fork.

  5. Prepare the Condiments and Sides: While the pork belly is steaming, arrange the fresh lettuce leaves, kimchi, and pickled radishes on a serving platter. Additionally, prepare the spicy dipping sauce by mixing together soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and Korean red chili powder.

Serving

Once the pork belly is tender and succulent, carefully remove it from the pot and slice it thinly. Arrange the slices on a serving platter alongside the fresh lettuce leaves and side dishes. Encourage your guests to create their own bossam wraps by placing a slice of pork belly on a lettuce leaf, adding a dollop of spicy dipping sauce, and incorporating their preferred condiments. The interactive nature of assembling the wraps adds an element of fun to the dining experience and allows everyone to customize their bossam to their liking.

Conclusion

With its rich flavors and interactive serving style, bossam is a dish that embodies the essence of Korean cuisine. By mastering the art of making bossam with finesse, you’ll not only impress your guests but also embark on a culinary journey that celebrates tradition and creativity. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the art of steaming pork belly, and savor the delight of indulging in this exquisite Korean delicacy. Cheers to creating bossam that will leave a lasting impression and tantalize the taste buds of all who have the pleasure of partaking in this culinary masterpiece.
