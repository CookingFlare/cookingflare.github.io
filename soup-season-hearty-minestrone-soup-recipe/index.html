Soup Season: Hearty Minestrone Soup Recipe


As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, it’s the perfect time to embrace the warmth and comfort of a hearty bowl of soup. And what better way to welcome soup season than with a classic Italian favorite – Minestrone soup. This versatile dish is not only delicious and comforting but also incredibly nutritious, making it the ideal choice for a cozy family dinner or a hearty lunch.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup small pasta (such as ditalini or elbow macaroni)
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or kale
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.

  3. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15 minutes.

  4. Stir in the cannellini beans and pasta. Simmer until the pasta is al dente, following the package instructions.

  5. Add the chopped spinach or kale, dried oregano, and dried basil to the pot. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the greens to wilt and the flavors to meld.

  6. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Ladle the hot Minestrone soup into bowls and serve with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Tips for a Perfect Minestrone Soup

  • Don’t be afraid to customize your Minestrone with seasonal vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or even butternut squash.
  • For a heartier version, you can add cooked Italian sausage or diced chicken to the soup.
  • If you have leftover cooked vegetables, toss them into the pot for added flavor and texture.

The Versatility of Minestrone Soup

What makes Minestrone soup truly special is its versatility. This recipe can easily be adapted to suit your personal taste and dietary preferences. Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or a meat lover, there’s a version of Minestrone that will delight your taste buds. The combination of savory broth, tender vegetables, and wholesome beans makes it a satisfying and nourishing meal for everyone.

Nutritional Benefits

Minestrone soup is not just a feast for the senses; it’s also a powerhouse of nutrients. Packed with fiber, vitamins, and minerals from the variety of vegetables and beans, this soup is a fantastic way to boost your immune system and nourish your body during the colder months. The addition of leafy greens like spinach or kale provides an extra dose of iron and antioxidants, making it a wholesome choice for a well-rounded meal.

Embracing Soup Season with Minestrone

As the days grow shorter and the temperatures drop, there’s something deeply comforting about a steaming bowl of Minestrone soup. It’s a celebration of the season’s harvest, a testament to the art of slow cooking, and a warm embrace in a bowl. So, gather your ingredients, let the aromas fill your kitchen, and savor the goodness of a homemade Minestrone soup. It’s the perfect way to welcome soup season with open arms.

Whether you enjoy it as a starter, a main course, or a satisfying lunch, this Hearty Minestrone Soup is a timeless classic that embodies the essence of comfort and nourishment. So, as you gather around the table with your loved ones, let this beloved Italian soup be the centerpiece of your seasonal feasts, filling your home with warmth, love, and the timeless flavors of autumn.
