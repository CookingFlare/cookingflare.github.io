Ocean Ambassador: Seafood Recipes that Give Back to Our Oceans


Ladies and gentlemen, here is a savory proposition for you: imagine not only relishing in the succulent awesomeness of seafood recipes that tantalize your taste buds but also actively participating in safeguarding our precious oceans by giving back with every delicious bite! Feast your eyes on our mouthwatering assortment of sea-inspired dishes which go beyond restaurant-worthy delicacies - each one is a proud ambassador for the ocean’s conservation.

  1. Grilled Octopus with Roasted Red Pepper Sauce and Garlic Butter

Soak in the Mediterranean with this tantalizingly tender octopus dish, grilled to perfection with the subtle smokiness you simply wouldn’t resist. Accompanying it is a luscious roasted red pepper and garlic butter sauce that sends an explosion of flavors to your taste buds with every bite. It’s the very embodiment of what it means to do good and feel good. The organic ingredients you’ll find at your local community-supported fishery are supporting sustainable fishing practices, contributing to the well-being of our waters.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium octopus (cleaned and organized)
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups roasted red pepper, pureed
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the octopus in a mix of olive oil, salt, and pepper for at least 8 hours to infuse it, then roast at 350 degrees for about 90 minutes until tender. Cool, then cut into bite-sized pieces.

  2. In a small saucepan, melt butter and sauté garlic until fragrant. Add the pureed red peppers and simmer for 10 minutes.

  3. Grill the octopus segments over medium heat, brush with the sauce, and serve alongside.

  4. Sustainable Sushi Rolls with Local Fish and Farm-Fresh Vegetables

Go green and global with these delightful sushi rolls, packed with nutrient-rich, farm-raised fish coupled with locally sourced veggies that ensure your plate not only bursts with flavors but also spawns conservation efforts. Instead of importing fish from afar, supporting local sustainably farmed sources reduces the carbon footprint and aids ocean conservation. Here’s how you make it:

Ingredients:

  • 4 sheets of nori
  • 3 cups short-grain rice
  • 1 cup sushi rice seasoning (vinigrette)
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1 avocado
  • A variety of locally farmed fish (tuna, salmon, and your preference)
  • Pickled ginger

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice in a pot with water, let it simmer and stir occasionally to drive off excess moisture. Let it cool and combine with sushi rice seasoning.

  2. Cut veggies and fish into bite-sized portions.

  3. Layer your sushi mat with nori, spread rice evenly, and top with fish and vegetables. Roll tightly and trim the edges.

  4. Serve with pickled ginger and wasabi.

  5. Seared Scallops with Lemon Papaya Salsa and Spicy Polenta Square

Sink your teeth into these delightful seared scallops presented atop a spicy yet sweet polenta sunken in tangy papaya salsa. The best part? The key ingredients come from fisheries that advocate for sustainable practices, protecting the ocean from further harm. Let’s step by step dive into making this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 cups of cornmeal
  • 2 cups of water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 papaya, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onion
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare polenta by simmering cornmeal and water for 30 minutes. Add salt, pepper, and mash it to the desired consistency.
  2. Season and cold-sear scallops on both sides for approximately two minutes each side, top with lemon zest.
  3. Combine papaya, green onions, and lemon juice, stir in olive oil, adjust seasoning according to taste, and spoon atop the polenta and scallops.

So, let your palate indulge in these gastronomical ocean ambassadors while creating waves of positive change for our seas. Sharing these delightful dishes will not only satisfy your culinary cravings but will also spread awareness about sustainable seafood and its significant impact on ocean conservation. So go ahead, savor every bite, generously spread the good — and who knows? You might become an Ocean Ambassador yourself, making our world a better place one seafood serving at a time.
