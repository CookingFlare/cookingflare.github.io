Cherry Almond Biscotti: Nutty and Fruity Italian Treat


If you’re a fan of crunchy, nutty, and slightly sweet treats, then you’re in for a real treat with this Cherry Almond Biscotti recipe. Biscotti, also known as cantuccini, are Italian almond biscuits that are twice-baked, making them delightfully crunchy and perfect for dipping into a cup of coffee or tea. This particular recipe adds a fruity twist to the classic biscotti by incorporating dried cherries, adding a burst of tangy sweetness to each bite. The combination of almonds and cherries creates a delightful flavor profile that will have you reaching for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure almond extract
  • 1 cup whole almonds, toasted and coarsely chopped
  • 3/4 cup dried cherries

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and almond extract together.

  4. Mix Everything Together: Gradually add the egg mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until the dough starts to come together. Add the almonds and dried cherries, and continue to mix until everything is well incorporated.

  5. Shape the Dough: Divide the dough in half. On a lightly floured surface, shape each half into a log about 12 inches long and 2 inches wide. Place the logs on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them a few inches apart.

  6. First Bake: Bake the logs for 25-30 minutes, or until they are lightly golden and firm to the touch.

  7. Cooling Time: Remove the logs from the oven and let them cool for 10 minutes. Reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (165°C).

  8. Cutting the Biscotti: Using a serrated knife, carefully slice the logs on the diagonal into 1/2-inch thick slices.

  9. Second Bake: Arrange the biscotti cut side down on the baking sheet and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden and crisp.

  10. Cool and Enjoy: Let the biscotti cool completely on a wire rack before indulging in this delightful Italian treat.

Serving Suggestions

These Cherry Almond Biscotti are perfect for enjoying with a cup of coffee or tea, especially as an afternoon pick-me-up. You can also wrap them up in a decorative bag or box and give them as a thoughtful homemade gift to friends and family.

Final Thoughts

The combination of crunchy almonds and tangy cherries makes this Cherry Almond Biscotti a truly special treat. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to impress with its delightful flavors and elegant presentation. So, why not add a taste of Italy to your kitchen and try your hand at making these delightful biscotti? They’re perfect for any occasion and are sure to become a new favorite in your baking repertoire. Enjoy!
