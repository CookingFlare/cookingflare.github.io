Delicious Apple Cider Donuts: A Perfect Fall Treat


There’s nothing quite like the taste of a warm apple cider donut on a crisp fall day. The combination of sweet apple flavors, warm spices, and a tender, cake-like texture makes these donuts the perfect treat to enjoy with a cup of hot cider or a pumpkin spice latte. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make these delicious apple cider donuts from scratch, right in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a donut pan with non-stick cooking spray. If you don’t have a donut pan, you can use a muffin tin instead.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. These warm spices will give your donuts that quintessential fall flavor.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the apple cider, milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense donuts. The batter should be thick and slightly lumpy.

  5. Spoon the batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about 3/4 full. Smooth the tops with a spatula or the back of a spoon.

  6. Bake the donuts for 12-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The donuts should be golden brown and spring back when lightly touched.

  7. Remove the donuts from the oven and let them cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. While the donuts are cooling, you can prepare a simple glaze to drizzle over the top. In a small bowl, whisk together powdered sugar and a few tablespoons of apple cider until you reach a smooth and pourable consistency. Adjust the amount of cider or sugar as needed.

  9. Once the donuts have cooled, dip each one into the glaze, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the glazed donuts back on the wire rack to set.

  10. If desired, you can also sprinkle the donuts with a dusting of cinnamon or a sprinkle of crushed nuts for added flavor and texture.

Now that your apple cider donuts are ready, it’s time to sit back, relax, and savor the flavors of fall. These donuts are best enjoyed fresh, but they will keep for a day or two in an airtight container. Whether you’re hosting a fall brunch or simply treating yourself to a special dessert, these delicious apple cider donuts are sure to impress.

So, grab a cozy sweater, head to your local apple orchard, and let the aroma of cinnamon and apples fill your kitchen. Fall is here, and it’s time to indulge in the simple pleasures of the season. Enjoy!
