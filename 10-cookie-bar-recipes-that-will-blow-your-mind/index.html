10 Cookie Bar Recipes That Will Blow Your Mind


If you’re a cookie lover, you know the joy that a perfectly baked cookie can bring. But have you ever tried cookie bars? These treats take the essence of your favorite cookies and transform them into a delightful, chewy, and easily shareable dessert. Whether you’re hosting a party, bringing a dish to a potluck, or simply indulging in a sweet craving, these ten cookie bar recipes will surely blow your mind!

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this to the wet mixture.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips, ensuring they are evenly distributed.
  6. Spread the batter into the prepared baking pan and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the edges are golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Let cool before cutting into squares.

Why You’ll Love It:

These classic chocolate chip cookie bars are soft, chewy, and loaded with chocolate, making them the ultimate comfort dessert.

2. Salted Caramel Pretzel Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup crushed pretzels
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup salted caramel sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. Cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually mix this into the wet ingredients.
  5. Fold in the crushed pretzels and chocolate chips.
  6. Spread half of the dough into the baking pan. Drizzle the salted caramel sauce over it. Top with the remaining dough.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until golden brown. Allow to cool before slicing.

Why You’ll Love It:

The combination of sweet and salty from the caramel and pretzels creates an irresistible flavor contrast.

3. Peanut Butter Swirl Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the butter, peanut butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. In another bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually blend this into the wet mixture.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and create swirls on the top with extra peanut butter.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, then cool before cutting.

Why You’ll Love It:

These cookie bars are rich and decadent, with the peanut butter and chocolate pairing perfectly for a satisfying treat.

4. Oatmeal Raisin Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. Cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. In another bowl, combine the flour, oats, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Gradually mix this into the wet ingredients.
  5. Fold in the raisins.
  6. Spread the batter into the prepared baking pan and bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden. Cool before slicing.

Why You’ll Love It:

These oatmeal raisin cookie bars are chewy and wholesome, offering a nostalgic flavor that’s perfect for any time of the day.

5. S’mores Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1 cup milk chocolate chips
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. Cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract.
  4. In another bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually blend into the wet ingredients.
  5. Fold in the marshmallows, chocolate chips, and graham cracker crumbs.
  6. Spread the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 25-30 minutes. Allow to cool before cutting.

Why You’ll Love It:

These s’mores cookie bars capture the essence of a campfire treat, combining chocolate, marshmallows, and graham crackers into one delicious dessert.

6. Lemon Blueberry Cookie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. Cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs, vanilla, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
  4. In another bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually mix into the wet ingredients.
  5. Gently fold in the blueberries.
  6. Spread the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 25-30 minutes. Cool before slicing.

Why You’ll Love It:

These lemon blueberry cookie bars are refreshing and tangy, making them a perfect summer treat.
