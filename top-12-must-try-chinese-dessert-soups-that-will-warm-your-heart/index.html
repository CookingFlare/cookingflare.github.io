Top 12 Must-Try Chinese Dessert Soups That Will Warm Your Heart


Chinese dessert soups, or tangshui, are a delightful way to end a delicious meal. Known for their sweet and soothing flavors, these soups are not only calming to the tastebuds but also believed to provide various health benefits. In this post, we’ll explore a list of must-try Chinese dessert soups and invite you to warm your heart with these unique sweet soups.

  1. Red Bean Soup

Starting off our list, red bean soup is a classic and popular dessert soup in Chinese cuisine. Packed with red beans’ natural sweetness, this soup is often simmered with rock sugar or ginger for a comforting taste. An excellent choice for those looking for a cozy dessert to bond over with family and friends.

  1. Osmanthus Sweet Potato Soup

Sweet potatoes are not only loaded with nutrition but also versatile for sweet treats. In this version, sweet potato is simmered with osmanthus flowers and rock sugar to create a soothing and aromatic soup. It’s an unequivocal delight that brings an herbal fragrance while taming the sweetness with the natural attributes of sweet potatoes.

  1. Goji Berries and Longan Soup

The Goji berries and Longan soup combines the power of antioxidant-rich goji berries with the tender sweetness of longan. The soup is simple, flexible, and can sometimes include other ingredients like dried dates, peanut, or beans to make it even heartier while adding a delightful variety to flavors.

  1. Walnut Coconut Milk Soup

A dash of exotic flair, the walnut coconut milk soup is a perfect balance of nuttiness and creaminess. This dessert soup combines the rich flavor of fresh walnuts with creamy coconut milk and sweetened with honey or rock sugar.

  1. Pomelo and Lily Bulb Soup

Citrus meets floral in this refreshing and invigorating dessert soup. The pomelo and lily bulb soup make use of the zesty pomelo alongside the floral, slightly sweet taste of lily bulbs, resulting in a soup both delightful and invigorating.

  1. Lotus Seed and Barley Soup

Soothing and uplifting, the Lotus Seed and Barley soup combines the delicate flavor of lotus seeds with barley’s texture and earthiness. Sweetness is brought in through rock sugar, creating a light and comforting soup.

  1. White Fungus Dessert Soup

This multi-faceted dessert soup uses white fungus, a type of edible fungus that’s tender and chewy, bringing a subtle, unique taste to the dish. Combined with sweet ingredients like red dates and sweet potatoes, the white fungus dessert soup balances chewy, crunchy, and moist textures.

  1. Oats Dessert Soup

The oats dessert soup offers heartiness while staying smooth and creamy. Using old-fashioned oats as the main ingredient, this soup gains sweetness from dates and a slight crunch from almonds or peanuts.

  1. Black Sesame and Tofu Soup

The Black Sesame and Tofu soup might seem unusual, but it’s an excellent pick for those seeking a twist. Combining the rich, nutty taste of black sesame with smooth, silky tofu, this dessert soup encompasses complexity and depth in flavors.

  1. Taro Coconut Milk Soup

The Taro Coconut Milk Soup is a striking balance of creaminess and earthiness. Featuring the delightfully earthy taste of taro combined with the creaminess of coconut milk, this dessert soup is rich and indulgent.

  1. Lemon and Chrysanthemum Tea Soup

A refreshing take on a classic tea, the Lemon and Chrysanthemum Tea Soup combines the zesty notes of lemon with the subtle flavor of chrysanthemum tea. This soup is simple, refreshing, and perfect for those seeking something light and floral.

  1. Snow Fungus and Gingko Nut Soup

Lastly, the Snow Fungus and Gingko Nut soup elevates simplicity with a delightful medley. The snow fungus and gingko nuts contribute a unique contrast of texture while giving a fresh and mild flavor. Combine it with red dates and goji berries for added health benefits.

Embrace the delicious world of Chinese dessert soups, and be sure to explore these twelve must-try recipes that guarantee to make your heart warm with every sip. Remember to adapt ingredients to personal preferences and dietary needs, and don’t shy away from experimentation. Happy tasting!
