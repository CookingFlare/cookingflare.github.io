Apple Cider Donuts for a Festive Breakfast Treat


The arrival of fall brings with it a bounty of seasonal delights, and one of the most beloved treats is the apple cider donut. These delightful, fluffy pastries are a perfect way to start your day with a touch of festive flavor. Whether you’re sipping on a warm cup of coffee or enjoying a brisk morning walk, apple cider donuts are the ideal companion to kickstart your autumn mornings.

The Magic of Apple Cider Donuts

What sets apple cider donuts apart from their traditional counterparts is the incorporation of apple cider into the batter. This addition not only infuses the donuts with a subtle sweetness but also imparts a hint of tanginess that perfectly complements the warm spices typically associated with fall.

Ingredients for Apple Cider Donuts

To embark on the journey of creating these delectable treats, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups apple cider
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions for Apple Cider Donuts

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, reduce the apple cider to about 1/2 cup. This process will take approximately 15-20 minutes. Once reduced, set it aside to cool.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then, stir in the reduced apple cider, buttermilk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.
  5. Transfer the batter to a pastry bag fitted with a large round tip.
  6. Heat oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F (190°C). Pipe the batter in a circular motion into the hot oil, cutting the dough with scissors to form donuts.
  7. Fry the donuts for about 2 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  8. Once fried, transfer the donuts to a paper towel-lined plate to drain and cool slightly.

Glazing and Serving Apple Cider Donuts

To add an extra layer of indulgence to your apple cider donuts, consider glazing them with a mixture of confectioners’ sugar and a splash of apple cider. Alternatively, a simple dusting of cinnamon sugar can enhance their flavor.

Pair your freshly made apple cider donuts with a steaming mug of spiced chai or a hot cup of freshly brewed coffee for the ultimate fall breakfast experience. These delicious treats are best enjoyed warm, straight from the fryer, but they will still bring joy to your taste buds even when enjoyed later in the day.

Embrace the Warmth of Fall with Apple Cider Donuts

As the days grow shorter and the air becomes crisper, indulge in the comforting flavors of fall with homemade apple cider donuts. Whether you’re hosting a festive brunch or simply treating yourself to a cozy morning at home, these delightful pastries are a delightful way to celebrate the season. Enjoy the process of creating these treats, and savor the heartwarming aromas that will fill your kitchen as you bake. Happy fall baking!
