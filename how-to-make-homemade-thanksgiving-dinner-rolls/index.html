How to Make Homemade Thanksgiving Dinner Rolls


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering around the table, enjoying hearty meals, and sharing gratitude with loved ones. A classic staple at any Thanksgiving dinner is the basket of warm, fluffy dinner rolls. Homemade rolls bring a sense of comfort and tradition to the table that store-bought varieties simply can’t match. Whether you pair them with butter, dip them into gravy, or use them to make mini turkey sandwiches with leftovers, Thanksgiving dinner rolls are the perfect addition to your holiday spread.

In this guide, I’m sharing step-by-step instructions on how to make perfectly soft and golden homemade Thanksgiving dinner rolls from scratch. These rolls are easy to prepare, full of flavor, and guaranteed to impress your guests. Bring that aroma of freshly baked bread into your kitchen and let’s dive into the magic of making these irresistible dinner rolls.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of homemade dinner rolls is that they rely on simple pantry staples. Here’s a list of what you’ll need before you begin:

For the dough:

  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for rolling and shaping)
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature

For the topping:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted (for brushing)
  • A sprinkle of flaky sea salt (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Thanksgiving Dinner Rolls

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by preparing your yeast for the dough. In a small bowl, combine the warm water and the granulated sugar. Stir gently until the sugar is dissolved. Sprinkle the active dry yeast over the water mixture and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it becomes foamy. This step is critical for ensuring your rolls rise properly, as it activates the yeast.

Step 2: Mix the Dough Base

In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over low heat until it’s just warm—not boiling. You want it around 100°F to 110°F (lukewarm to the touch). Pour the warm milk and melted butter into a large mixing bowl. Add the eggs and whisk until everything is well combined.

Step 3: Combine Dry and Wet Ingredients

In the same mixing bowl, add the all-purpose flour, salt, and foamy yeast mixture. Stir with a wooden spoon or a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook until the dough starts coming together. If using a stand mixer, set it to low speed to incorporate the ingredients evenly.

Step 4: Knead the Dough

Once the dough has formed, transfer it to a lightly floured surface. Knead it for about 8–10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. If you’re using a stand mixer, continue kneading with the dough hook for about 5 minutes. The dough should spring back slightly when pressed with a fingertip but remain soft.

Step 5: Let the Dough Rise

Shape the kneaded dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm place for 1–2 hours until it’s doubled in size. This is when the yeast works its magic, creating the light and fluffy texture of the rolls.

Step 6: Shape the Rolls

After the dough has risen, punch it down to release excess air. Turn it onto a floured surface and divide it into 16 equal portions (you can weigh them for consistency). Roll each portion into a smooth ball by pulling the edges under and pinching them at the base. Arrange the rolls in a lightly greased 9x13-inch baking dish or on a parchment-lined baking sheet, ensuring they’re spaced slightly apart.

Step 7: Second Rise

Cover the shaped rolls with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise again for 30–45 minutes. This second rise ensures that your rolls are extra fluffy and tender. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Step 8: Bake

Once the rolls have puffed up during the second rise, place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 18–22 minutes. Keep an eye on them as they bake; they should turn golden brown on top. If you’d like a softer crust, tent the rolls loosely with aluminum foil in the last 5 minutes of baking.

Step 9: Brush and Serve

Immediately after taking the rolls out of the oven, brush the tops generously with melted butter. For a finishing touch, sprinkle a pinch of flaky sea salt over the rolls. Let them cool slightly before serving, but don’t wait too long—there’s nothing better than enjoying these while they’re still warm!

Tips for Perfect Thanksgiving Dinner Rolls

  1. Control the Temperature: Yeast is sensitive to temperature, so make sure your water and milk are warm but not hot. Using liquid that’s too hot can kill the yeast, while cold liquids will prevent proper activation.

  2. Room Temperature Ingredients: Eggs and butter mix better if they’re at room temperature. Take them out of the fridge a couple of hours before you start baking.

  3. Use Fresh Yeast: Make sure your yeast is active and fresh. Expired yeast may not cause the dough to rise, resulting in dense rolls.

  4. Avoid Overkneading: Kneading is essential, but overdoing it can make your rolls chewy rather than fluffy. Stop kneading when the dough becomes soft and elastic.

  5. Plan Ahead: Time management is key for Thanksgiving meal prep. These rolls require multiple rises, so start the process early to ensure they’ll be ready when dinner begins.

Storage and Reheating

If you’re preparing these rolls ahead of time, allow them to cool completely before storing them in an airtight container. They’ll keep fresh at room temperature for up to two days or in the fridge for up to five days.

To reheat, wrap the rolls in aluminum foil and place them in a 300°F oven for 10–12 minutes. Brush with a little melted butter before serving to restore their soft, buttery flavor.

Making These Rolls Ahead of Time

For convenience during the busy Thanksgiving holiday, you can prepare these dinner rolls in advance. After shaping the rolls and placing them in the baking dish, cover them with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight instead of letting them rise the second time. The next day, allow them to come to room temperature and rise for about an hour before baking as directed.

A Memorable Addition to Your Thanksgiving Table

Homemade Thanksgiving dinner rolls are more than just bread—they’re a tradition that brings people together over a shared joy of good food. As the rolls bake and their soft, buttery aroma fills your kitchen, you’ll know you’re creating something special that your family and friends will savor.

This recipe is perfect for both beginner bakers and seasoned chefs, offering a mix of simplicity and reward. Whether you’re hosting a large feast or a cozy gathering, these rolls will undoubtedly earn a permanent spot on your Thanksgiving menu. Warm, golden, and irresistibly fluffy, they’re the kind of side dish that elevates your holiday meal into a memorable experience for all. Happy baking!
