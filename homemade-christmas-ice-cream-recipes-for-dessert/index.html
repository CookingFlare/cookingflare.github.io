Homemade Christmas Ice Cream Recipes for Dessert


Holiday celebrations often mean gathering around the table with family and friends, indulging in festive flavors that bring warmth and joy. While traditional Christmas desserts like pies and cookies have their special place, homemade ice cream adds a delightful twist that cools down and captivates. Whether you’re hosting a festive dinner or want to surprise your loved ones with unique sweet treats, making your own Christmas-themed ice cream can be a showstopper.

Below, you’ll find a collection of delicious, easy-to-make homemade Christmas ice cream recipes that blend seasonal spices, rich ingredients, and festive flair. These recipes span from classic peppermint and eggnog flavors to more inventive ideas with gingerbread and cranberries. Each recipe is designed to be simple enough for home cooks while delivering an impressive depth of flavor.

Why Make Homemade Christmas Ice Cream?

Before diving into the recipes, let’s explore why crafting your own ice cream for the holidays is such a treat:

  • Customization: Control the level of sweetness, richness, and ingredients, making your ice cream exactly how you like it.
  • Festive Flair: Incorporate fresh, seasonal ingredients and holiday spices that aren’t found in store-bought varieties.
  • Impression Factor: Homemade ice cream adds a personal touch, showing extra care and creativity.
  • Fun Activity: Making ice cream from scratch is enjoyable and a great way to engage kids and family in holiday cooking.

Now, roll up your sleeves, and let’s scoop into these festive flavors.

1. Classic Peppermint Bark Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup crushed peppermint candies or candy canes
  • 1/2 cup chopped white chocolate bark or white chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Mix Base: In a mixing bowl, whisk together sugar, salt, milk, and peppermint extract until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add Cream & Vanilla: Stir in heavy cream and vanilla extract.
  3. Chill Mixture: Cover and chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Churn: Pour the chilled mixture into an ice cream maker, and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.
  5. Add Mix-ins: During the last minutes of churning, add crushed peppermint candies and white chocolate pieces.
  6. Freeze: Transfer ice cream to a container, smooth the top, and freeze for an additional 3-4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Serve this peppermint bark ice cream with a drizzle of hot fudge or alongside warm brownies. Garnish with fresh mint leaves or extra candy cane pieces for a vibrant holiday look.

2. Creamy Eggnog Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg, plus extra for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup eggnog (store-bought or homemade)
  • Optional: 2 tablespoons rum or bourbon (if you want an adult version)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Custard: Warm milk, heavy cream, and half the sugar in a saucepan over medium heat until hot but not boiling.
  2. Whisk Egg Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks and remaining sugar until pale.
  3. Temper Yolks: Slowly pour about 1 cup of the warm milk mixture into the egg yolks while whisking vigorously to avoid curdling.
  4. Combine & Cook: Pour the yolk mixture back into the saucepan and cook on low heat, stirring constantly until the custard thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon (around 170°F).
  5. Strain & Flavor: Strain custard into a bowl to remove any lumps. Stir in nutmeg, vanilla extract, eggnog, and alcohol if desired.
  6. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  7. Churn: Process in an ice cream maker until thick and creamy.
  8. Freeze: Transfer to a container and freeze for several hours to set fully.

Serving Tips

Dust the top of each serving with freshly grated nutmeg. Pair with gingerbread cookies or fruitcake for an authentic holiday experience.

3. Gingerbread Ice Cream with Molasses Swirl

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon molasses
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Molasses Swirl

  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

  1. Ice Cream Base: Combine sugar, spices, and salt in heated milk and heavy cream until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add Molasses & Vanilla: Stir in molasses and vanilla extract for rich flavor.
  3. Chill: Refrigerate mixture until very cold.
  4. Make Swirl: Mix molasses and water in a small saucepan and warm slightly until runny. Let cool.
  5. Churn Ice Cream: Process ice cream base in maker until thickened.
  6. Add Swirl: Transfer half the ice cream to a container, drizzle half of the molasses swirl, then add remaining ice cream and swirl the rest on top. Use a knife to create marble patterns.
  7. Freeze: Freeze for at least 4 hours to allow textures to meld.

Serving Notes

Top with whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon. This gingerbread ice cream pairs beautifully with warm apple pie or spiced nuts.

4. Cranberry Orange Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • Zest of 1 large orange
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for cranberry compote)
  • 2 tablespoons water

Instructions

  1. Prepare Cranberry Compote: In a small saucepan, combine cranberries, sugar, and water. Cook over medium heat until cranberries burst and mixture thickens (about 10 minutes). Cool completely.
  2. Mix Ice Cream Base: Whisk sugar, milk, orange zest, orange juice, and vanilla in a bowl until sugar dissolves. Stir in heavy cream.
  3. Chill: Refrigerate for several hours for flavors to meld.
  4. Churn: Process mixture in ice cream maker until creamy.
  5. Add Cranberry Swirl: Layer ice cream and cranberry compote alternately into a container to create swirls.
  6. Freeze: Let freeze for 3-4 hours.

Serving Inspiration

Garnish with candied orange peel or a sprig of rosemary for a fragrant, festive touch. This bright and tangy ice cream cuts through rich holiday dishes wonderfully.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Ice Cream

  • Chill Your Ingredients: Starting with cold ingredients helps your ice cream freeze smoother and quicker.
  • Use Full-Fat Dairy: Cream and whole milk yield creamier textures.
  • Don’t Skip Chilling the Mixture: It enhances flavor and ensures even freezing.
  • Add Mix-Ins Late: Incorporate candies, nuts, or swirls during the final minutes of churning.
  • Patience is Key: Allow your ice cream to freeze solid in the freezer to develop body and scoopability.

Creative Serving Ideas for Christmas Ice Cream

Elevate your ice cream experience with these serving suggestions:

  • Ice Cream Sandwiches: Use gingerbread or sugar cookies as the “bread” for ice cream sandwiches.
  • Affogato Style: Pour hot espresso or coffee over a scoop of your eggnog or peppermint ice cream.
  • Spiked Sundaes: Add a splash of festive liquor (rum, bourbon, or peppermint schnapps) before topping with whipped cream and sprinkles.
  • Topped with Festive Bites: Sprinkle crushed candy canes, toasted nuts, cinnamon sticks, or holiday sprinkles on top.
  • Accompanied by Fruit and Nuts: Warm fruit compotes or roasted nuts add texture and seasonal flavor.

Homemade Christmas ice cream can brighten up any holiday gathering by introducing playful and nostalgic tastes that evoke the spirit of the season. Whether you lean toward the cool and refreshing peppermint or the warm and spiced gingerbread, there’s a recipe here for every palate. So gather your ingredients, invite some helpers into the kitchen, and treat your guests to a truly special holiday dessert.

May your holidays be sweet, smooth, and sprinkled with joy!
