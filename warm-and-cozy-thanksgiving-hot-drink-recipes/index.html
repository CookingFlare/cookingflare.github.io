Warm and Cozy Thanksgiving Hot Drink Recipes


As the chilly autumn breeze fills the air and the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and gold, it’s time to gather around a crackling fire and indulge in some heartwarming Thanksgiving hot drink recipes. These delightful concoctions will not only keep you cozy but also add a touch of festive cheer to your holiday celebrations. So, grab your favorite mug and let’s dive into the world of warm and flavorful Thanksgiving drinks!

1. Spiced Apple Cider

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups apple cider
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 orange, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the apple cider, cinnamon stick, cloves, and orange slices.
  2. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the cider infuse with the flavors for about 15 minutes.
  4. Remove the spices and orange slices from the cider using a slotted spoon.
  5. If desired, stir in honey to sweeten the cider.
  6. Pour the spiced apple cider into mugs and garnish with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon. Serve warm.

2. Pumpkin Spice Latte

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons pumpkin puree
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup hot brewed coffee
  • Whipped cream for topping (optional)
  • Pumpkin pie spice for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the milk, pumpkin puree, maple syrup, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla extract over medium heat. Whisk until hot and frothy.
  2. Pour the hot brewed coffee into a mug.
  3. Slowly pour the pumpkin spice milk mixture over the coffee, stirring gently to combine.
  4. Top with whipped cream, if desired, and sprinkle pumpkin pie spice on top. Serve warm.

3. Cranberry Orange Hot Toddy

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cranberry juice
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup bourbon or whiskey (optional)
  • Orange slices for garnish
  • Fresh cranberries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the cranberry juice, orange juice, honey, and lemon juice.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until hot but not boiling.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the bourbon or whiskey, if using.
  4. Pour the cranberry orange hot toddy into mugs and garnish with orange slices and fresh cranberries. Serve warm.

4. Maple Cinnamon Hot Chocolate

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream for topping (optional)
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until hot but not boiling.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the cocoa powder, maple syrup, cinnamon, and vanilla extract.
  3. Slowly whisk the cocoa mixture into the hot milk until well combined.
  4. Pour the maple cinnamon hot chocolate into mugs and top with whipped cream, if desired.
  5. Sprinkle ground cinnamon on top for an extra touch of warmth. Serve warm.

5. Gingerbread Chai Latte

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 chai tea bags
  • 2 tablespoons molasses
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Whipped cream for topping (optional)
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until hot but not boiling.
  2. Add the chai tea bags to the milk and let them steep for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea bags and stir in the molasses, ground ginger, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg.
  4. Heat the mixture for another minute, stirring occasionally.
  5. Pour the gingerbread chai latte into mugs and top with whipped cream, if desired.
  6. Sprinkle ground cinnamon on top for a festive finishing touch. Serve warm.

These warm and cozy Thanksgiving hot drink recipes are sure to create a delightful atmosphere during your holiday gatherings. Whether you prefer the comforting flavors of spiced apple cider or the indulgent richness of maple cinnamon hot chocolate, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, sip away and savor the flavors of the season. Happy Thanksgiving!
