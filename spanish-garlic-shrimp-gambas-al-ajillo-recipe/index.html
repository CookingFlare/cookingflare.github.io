Spanish Garlic Shrimp (Gambas al Ajillo) Recipe


If you’re a fan of seafood and bold flavors, then the Spanish Garlic Shrimp, or Gambas al Ajillo, is a recipe you must try. This classic Spanish dish is simple yet packed with flavor, making it a perfect appetizer or main course for any occasion. With just a few key ingredients, you can create a dish that will transport you to the sunny shores of Spain.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly chopped parsley
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, and sauté until the garlic turns golden brown and becomes fragrant.

  2. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the shrimp to the skillet. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until the shrimp turns pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp, as they can become rubbery.

  3. Once the shrimp are cooked, remove them from the skillet and set aside. Leave the garlic and oil in the skillet.

  4. Reduce the heat to low and add the freshly chopped parsley to the skillet. Stir for about 30 seconds to a minute until the parsley is slightly wilted.

  5. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and toss them in the garlic-infused oil and parsley. Make sure each shrimp is coated with the flavorful mixture.

  6. Season with salt to taste and give it a final toss to ensure the flavors are well-distributed.

  7. Transfer the Spanish Garlic Shrimp to a serving dish, and garnish with some additional parsley for a pop of color.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the Gambas al Ajillo as an appetizer with a side of crusty bread. The bread is perfect for soaking up the delicious garlic-infused oil.
  • Alternatively, serve the Spanish Garlic Shrimp as a main course over a bed of rice or pasta. The flavors of the shrimp complement the mild flavors of rice or pasta perfectly.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can add a splash of dry white wine to the skillet after sautéing the garlic. Let it simmer for a minute or two before adding the shrimp.
  • If you prefer a milder spice level, you can reduce or omit the red pepper flakes altogether.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the dish to your taste. Some popular additions include paprika, bay leaves, or even a squeeze of lemon juice for a hint of acidity.

Conclusion

The Spanish Garlic Shrimp, or Gambas al Ajillo, is a dish that showcases the simplicity and excellence of Spanish cuisine. With just a handful of ingredients, you can create a flavorful and satisfying dish that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Spain. Whether you serve it as an appetizer or a main course, this recipe is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in the rich flavors of the Spanish Garlic Shrimp!
