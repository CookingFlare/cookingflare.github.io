Instant Pot Shrimp Fried Rice: Quick and Tasty


If you’re a fan of quick and delicious meals, then you’re in for a treat with this Instant Pot Shrimp Fried Rice recipe. Not only is it easy to make, but it’s also packed with flavor and can be customized to suit your taste preferences. The convenience of using an Instant Pot makes this dish a perfect option for busy weeknights when you want something satisfying without spending hours in the kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cups of cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Shrimp: Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and heat the vegetable oil. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink. Remove the shrimp from the Instant Pot and set aside.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for a few minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add Vegetables and Rice: Stir in the mixed vegetables and cooked rice. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Cook the Eggs: Push the rice mixture to the side of the pot and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are fully cooked.

  5. Season the Fried Rice: Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil to the pot. Stir everything together to evenly distribute the sauces.

  6. Add Shrimp and Garnish: Gently fold in the cooked shrimp and allow them to heat through. Season the fried rice with salt and pepper according to your taste. Top with chopped green onions for a burst of freshness.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once everything is heated through, your Instant Pot Shrimp Fried Rice is ready to be served. Scoop generous portions onto plates and savor the delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Tips for Success

  • Use day-old rice if possible, as freshly cooked rice can become too sticky when pressure cooked.
  • Customize the vegetables to your liking. Bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas are great additions to this dish.
  • For an extra kick of heat, add a dash of Sriracha or red pepper flakes.

Final Thoughts

The Instant Pot Shrimp Fried Rice is a versatile and satisfying dish that can be prepared in no time, thanks to the wonders of pressure cooking. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a casual dinner with friends, this recipe is sure to impress with its vibrant colors and delectable flavors. Embrace the convenience of the Instant Pot and treat yourself to a delightful meal that will leave everyone wanting seconds. Happy cooking!
