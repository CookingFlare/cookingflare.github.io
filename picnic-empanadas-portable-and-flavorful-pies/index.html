Picnic Empanadas: Portable and Flavorful Pies


When it comes to picnics, a perfect addition to your basket is the humble empanada. These delightful hand pies are not only delicious but also incredibly portable, making them an ideal choice for an outdoor feast. Whether you’re picnicking in the park, at the beach, or even in your backyard, empanadas are a versatile and convenient option. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making picnic empanadas and share some mouthwatering filling ideas to elevate your outdoor dining experience.

The Empanada Dough

The foundation of a great empanada lies in its dough. You can opt for a traditional dough made with flour, butter, and a touch of salt, or you can experiment with variations such as using puff pastry for a flakier texture. Whichever dough you choose, ensure that it’s sturdy enough to hold the filling and withstand the journey to your picnic spot. When making the dough, consider adding a hint of paprika or turmeric for an extra pop of color and flavor.

Filling Ideas

1. Classic Beef and Onion

  • Start by browning ground beef with onions, garlic, and a blend of smoky spices like cumin and chili powder. Add some diced potatoes and green olives for a traditional touch. This savory filling is a crowd-pleaser and pairs beautifully with a buttery, golden crust.

2. Spicy Chicken and Pepper

  • Shredded chicken mixed with sautéed bell peppers, onions, and a kick of cayenne pepper creates a zesty and vibrant filling. Consider adding a sprinkle of fresh cilantro or a dollop of sour cream to balance the heat.

3. Vegetarian Delight

  • For a meatless option, sauté a medley of mushrooms, spinach, and feta cheese. Season with a hint of thyme and a splash of lemon juice for a refreshing and flavorful vegetarian filling.

Assembly and Baking

Once you have prepared the dough and the filling, it’s time to assemble your empanadas. Roll out the dough, cut it into circles, spoon the filling onto one half of each circle, fold the dough over the filling, and crimp the edges to seal. Brush the empanadas with an egg wash for a glossy finish, and then bake them until golden brown and fragrant. For added convenience, you can also prepare the empanadas in advance and bake them at your picnic site using a portable oven or grill.

Packing and Serving

To ensure that your picnic empanadas stay fresh and delicious, pack them in a secure, airtight container with a layer of parchment paper between each layer. This will prevent them from sticking together and preserve their crispy exterior. Consider serving them with a side of chimichurri sauce, salsa, or a simple green salad for a complete and satisfying picnic meal.

Conclusion

In conclusion, picnic empanadas are a delightful and practical addition to any outdoor gathering. Their portability, versatility, and tantalizing flavors make them a standout choice for al fresco dining. Whether you opt for a classic meat filling or get creative with vegetarian options, these handheld pies are sure to impress. So, the next time you plan a picnic, consider adding homemade empanadas to your menu for a memorable and savory experience. Happy picnicking!
