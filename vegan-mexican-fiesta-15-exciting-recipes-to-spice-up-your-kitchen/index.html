Vegan Mexican Fiesta: 15 Exciting Recipes to Spice Up Your Kitchen


Are you craving the warm flavors and zest of Mexican cuisine but looking to go vegan? Look no further! Here’s a vibrant collection of 15 exciting vegan Mexican recipes to spice up your kitchen, making your taste buds dance with joy and excitement. So grab your apron, and let’s fiesta!

1. Easy Vegan Queso Blanco

Start your fiesta with this velvety smooth queso blanco that’s creamy, spicy and without a hint of dairy! It’s perfect for dipping or pouring over tacos.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours or overnight
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 small head of garlic, divided
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded
  • 1/2 tsp.celtic salt
  • Juice of 1/2 a lemon

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and add them to the blender along with the water, a head of garlic, the jalapeño, the salt, and the lemon juice. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Scrape the sides if necessary and blend again until you get a smooth, guilt-free queso.

2. Street Corn Taco Salad

This colorful salad is a fun take on the popular Mexican street food, elote. It’s full of flavor, texture, and color.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of fresh corn, husked
  • 2 tbsp. vegan mayo
  • 1 tbsp. chili powder
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  • Lime juice from half a lime
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups of mixed salad greens
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup of pickled red onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium heat. Rub the corn with the the vegan mayo then sprinkle the chili powder, garlic powder, and salt. Grill turning often, until the corn starts to brown, 10 to 15 minutes.
  2. Remove from heat, let it cool and carefully cut the kernels off the cob.
  3. In a large salad bowl, mix the salad greens, black beans, cherry tomatoes, pickled red onions and grilled corn.
  4. Drizzle with the lime juice and add the remaining vegan mayo. Mix well and serve.

3. Smoky Chipotle Sweet Potato Tacos

Learn to make tacos that are overflowing with smoky chipotle flavor and packed to the brink with sweet potato.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and cut into small cubes
  • 1 tbsp. oil
  • 1/2 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt to taste
  • 6 corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Avocado slices

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C). Toss the sweet potato with the oil, chipotle pepper, garlic, and salt. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast until tender and lightly caramelized, about 25 to 30 minutes.
  2. Warm the corn tortillas on a griddle or in a pan. Fill with the roasted sweet potato and top with a slice of avocado and fresh cilantro.

4. Avocado Cilantro Lime Dip

A vibrant, creamy, and zesty avocado dip that’s ready to be devoured with chips or served as a spread for sandwiches and burgers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. fresh ground pepper

Instructions:

  1. Scoop the avocado flesh into a food processor. Add the lime juice, cilantro, onion, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pulse until creamy but still slightly chunky. Serve immediately or cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.
