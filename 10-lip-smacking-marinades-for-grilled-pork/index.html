10 Lip-Smacking Marinades for Grilled Pork


Grilled pork is a delicious and versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or used as a base for various recipes. One way to elevate the flavor of grilled pork is by marinating it with lip-smacking marinades. Marinades not only infuse the meat with incredible flavors but also help to tenderize it, resulting in juicy and succulent pork. In this blog post, we will share with you 10 mouthwatering marinades that will take your grilled pork to the next level. Let’s dive in!

1. Honey Garlic Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, sesame oil, and grated ginger.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it, ensuring the meat is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Grill the marinated pork until cooked through, brushing with the remaining marinade occasionally.

2. Teriyaki Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, mirin, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Let the teriyaki marinade cool completely before using.
  4. Marinate the pork for at least 1 hour before grilling, brushing with the marinade during cooking.

3. Lemon Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or basil)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine lemon juice, lemon zest, olive oil, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over it.
  3. Turn the meat to coat both sides and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Grill the pork until nicely charred and cooked to your desired doneness, basting with the marinade.

4. Spicy Chipotle Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine minced chipotle peppers, lime juice, olive oil, honey, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  4. Grill the marinated pork until nicely charred and cooked through, brushing with the remaining marinade.

5. Asian Sesame Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, minced garlic, grated ginger, and toasted sesame seeds.
  2. Place the pork in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over it, ensuring the meat is fully coated.
  3. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour before grilling.
  4. Grill the pork until cooked through, brushing with the marinade during cooking.

6. Pineapple Ginger Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine pineapple juice, soy sauce, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, and sesame oil.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Grill the marinated pork until nicely caramelized and cooked through.

7. Balsamic Rosemary Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, chopped fresh rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over it.
  3. Turn the meat to coat both sides and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Grill the pork until nicely charred and cooked to your desired doneness.

8. Korean BBQ Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and gochujang.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it, ensuring the meat is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Grill the marinated pork until nicely caramelized and cooked through, brushing with the remaining marinade.

9. Italian Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs (such as basil, oregano, or parsley)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over it.
  3. Turn the meat to coat both sides and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Grill the pork until nicely charred and cooked to your desired doneness.

10. Smoky Maple Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together maple syrup, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, minced garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it, ensuring the meat is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Grill the marinated pork until nicely caramelized and cooked through, brushing with the remaining marinade.

Now that you have 10 lip-smacking marinades for grilled pork, it’s time to fire up the grill and get cooking! Whether you prefer sweet, spicy, or tangy flavors, there’s a marinade on this list that will satisfy your taste buds. Experiment with different combinations and discover your favorite. Happy grilling!
