How to Make Korean Seafood Pancakes (Haemul Pajeon)


Korean cuisine is celebrated for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and unique combinations of ingredients. Among its many delights, Korean Seafood Pancakes, or Haemul Pajeon, stand out as a crowd favorite. This savory pancake, packed with fresh seafood and green onions, boasts a crispy exterior and a soft, flavorful interior. Perfect as an appetizer, snack, or main dish, Haemul Pajeon is a must-try recipe for anyone looking to expand their culinary repertoire. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to make authentic Korean Seafood Pancakes from scratch, bringing a taste of Korea to your kitchen.

Why You’ll Love Haemul Pajeon

Haemul Pajeon is more than just a pancake; it’s a balance of textures and flavors. The crunch from the pan-fried batter complements the tender seafood, while the mild sweetness of green onions cuts through the rich, savory notes. Served with a tangy dipping sauce, this dish is a melting pot of tastes that leaves you craving more. Plus, it’s versatile—you can customize the seafood and vegetables to suit your preferences or whatever you have on hand.

Ingredients for Haemul Pajeon

To achieve the authentic taste of Haemul Pajeon, using fresh and high-quality ingredients is essential. Here’s everything you’ll need:

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup potato starch (or cornstarch) – This ensures extra crispiness.
  • 1½ cups cold water – Cold water is key to achieving the batter’s perfect texture.
  • 1 egg
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • ½ cup squid, cleaned and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ½ cup mussels or clams, shelled – Optional, but adds a briny depth.
  • 6-8 stalks green onions, cut into 2-3 inch pieces
  • 1 small red chili, thinly sliced – Optional, for a hint of spice.

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ½ teaspoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – Optional, for added heat.
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s crucial to prepare all your ingredients. Clean and slice the seafood as noted above. If using frozen seafood, make sure it’s completely thawed and patted dry to avoid excess moisture. Wash and cut the green onions and chili, setting them aside in separate bowls for easy access while cooking.

2. Make the Batter

In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, potato starch, salt, and cold water. Stir until smooth and lump-free. Add the egg and whisk it into the batter until fully incorporated. The consistency should be slightly runny but not watery—adjust with a bit more water or flour if needed.

3. Prepare the Dipping Sauce

To enhance the pancake’s flavor, pair it with a tangy dipping sauce. Mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, gochugaru, and chopped green onions in a small bowl. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Set the sauce aside while you cook the pancakes.

4. Cook the Pancakes

Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat. Add 2-3 tablespoons of vegetable oil and allow it to heat until shimmering.

Once the oil is hot, pour about half a cup of pancake batter into the pan, spreading it out into a thin layer using the back of a ladle or spoon. Layer the green onions evenly across the batter, followed by the seafood and sliced chili. Then, pour just enough additional batter over the toppings to lightly cover them.

Let the pancake cook for 3-4 minutes or until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip the pancake using a spatula and cook the other side for another 3-4 minutes. Add a little more oil around the edges, if necessary, to ensure a crispy finish.

5. Repeat the Process

Once the pancake is done, transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil. Repeat the process with the remaining batter and toppings. Adjust the heat as needed to avoid burning—medium heat is your best bet for even cooking.

6. Serve

Slice the pancakes into bite-sized pieces or wedges using a knife or pizza cutter. Serve hot with the dipping sauce you prepared earlier. Garnish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds for an extra touch.

Pro Tips for Creating the Perfect Haemul Pajeon

  1. Use a Non-Stick Pan or Cast-Iron Skillet
    A non-stick surface or well-seasoned cast-iron skillet is key to cooking pancakes evenly without sticking.

  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan
    Cook one pancake at a time to ensure it crisp up properly. Too much batter or filling can lead to uneven cooking.

  3. Chill the Batter
    Cold batter helps create a crispier pancake. If you’re cooking in batches, store the batter in the fridge to keep it cold.

  4. Get Creative with Seafood
    Feel free to swap the shrimp, squid, and mussels for other seafood like scallops, crab, or oysters. A mix of flavors adds complexity to the dish.

  5. Adjust the Spice Level
    For those who enjoy heat, add more chili slices to the pancake or spice up the dipping sauce with extra gochugaru.

When to Enjoy Haemul Pajeon

Korean Seafood Pancakes are incredibly versatile and can be served at various occasions. They’re a popular dish at Korean gatherings or celebrations, often paired with makgeolli (Korean rice wine). Whether you’re hosting friends for dinner, preparing a cozy weekend lunch, or cooking up a quick snack, Haemul Pajeon fits the bill. Its warm, savory flavors also make it a comforting choice on a chilly day.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have extra pancakes, don’t worry—they store and reheat well! Wrap the pancakes in plastic wrap or place them in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to two days. To reheat, simply pan-fry them on a lightly oiled skillet over medium heat until they regain their crispy texture. Avoid microwaving, as it can make the pancakes soggy.

A Taste of Korea in Every Bite

Making Haemul Pajeon at home is a rewarding culinary adventure. From assembling the ingredients to savoring the final bite, every step allows you to connect with the rich tapestry of Korean flavors. With its inviting aroma, crisp edges, and tender seafood, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your cooking rotation. Try your hand at making these iconic Korean Seafood Pancakes and enjoy a delightful meal that feels like a celebration. Happy cooking!
