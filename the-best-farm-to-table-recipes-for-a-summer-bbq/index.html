The Best Farm to Table Recipes for a Summer BBQ


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and indulge in fresh, locally sourced produce. With longer days, warm weather, and the scents of smoky grills filling the air, there’s no better way to enjoy a weekend afternoon with family and friends than hosting a summer BBQ. But what makes a BBQ truly special is the use of fresh, farm-to-table ingredients that bring out the natural flavors of the season.

Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just a few close friends, farm-to-table recipes bring a unique touch to your BBQ spread, showcasing the best produce and meats that the summer season has to offer. In this post, we’ll explore a collection of mouth-watering recipes that will elevate your summer BBQ to new heights.

Why Choose Farm-to-Table Ingredients?

Farm-to-table eating is all about sourcing fresh ingredients directly from local farms. This practice not only supports small-scale agriculture but also ensures that you’re consuming the freshest, most flavorful produce available. By incorporating local vegetables, meats, and dairy into your BBQ, you can enjoy a more sustainable and health-conscious meal while celebrating the bounty of the season.

For the perfect summer BBQ, farm-to-table ingredients offer several key benefits:

  • Freshness: Produce harvested at its peak season delivers superior taste and texture.
  • Sustainability: By supporting local farmers, you’re reducing the carbon footprint of your meal and promoting eco-friendly practices.
  • Healthier Meals: Locally grown food is often free from pesticides and preservatives, making it a healthier option.
  • Variety: Each season offers a unique range of ingredients, giving you the opportunity to try new and exciting flavors each year.

Grilled Corn with Herb Butter

One of the stars of any summer BBQ is corn on the cob. Corn, when picked at its peak, has a natural sweetness and richness that pairs perfectly with a smoky grill. For this recipe, we’ll take things up a notch with a homemade herb butter that adds layers of flavor to this classic side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of fresh corn on the cob, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for grilling)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Butter: In a small bowl, combine the softened butter with garlic, parsley, thyme, and lemon zest. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well and set aside.
  2. Grill the Corn: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the corn with olive oil and season with a little salt and pepper. Place the corn on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning every few minutes, until the kernels are charred and tender.
  3. Serve: Once the corn is grilled, slather each ear with the herb butter. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Farm-Fresh Tomato and Mozzarella Salad

A summer BBQ wouldn’t be complete without a vibrant salad that highlights the best of the season’s produce. The combination of juicy tomatoes and creamy mozzarella is a classic pairing that can be enhanced with a simple vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 ripe heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 ball of fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Assemble the Salad: Arrange the tomato slices on a platter, alternating with the mozzarella slices. Sprinkle fresh basil leaves on top.
  2. Dress the Salad: Drizzle the olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the tomatoes and mozzarella. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Serve: Serve immediately as a refreshing side to your BBQ. The burst of freshness from the tomatoes paired with the creamy mozzarella is sure to be a hit!

Grilled Veggie Skewers with Lemon Tahini Sauce

Vegetable skewers are a fun and delicious way to add color and variety to your BBQ spread. Grilled vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and onions have a smoky flavor that pairs beautifully with a tangy lemon tahini sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 2 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 8 oz mushrooms, cleaned and halved
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 garlic clove, minced

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Thread the bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and onion onto skewers. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly.
  2. Grill the Veggies: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the vegetable skewers for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are tender and lightly charred.
  3. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, water, and garlic until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve: Drizzle the tahini sauce over the grilled veggie skewers and serve warm.

Grilled Chicken Thighs with Peach Glaze

Chicken thighs are an excellent choice for grilling because they remain tender and juicy while absorbing delicious smoky flavors. This recipe features a sweet and tangy peach glaze, perfect for summer BBQs.

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 ripe peaches, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for grilling

Instructions:

  1. Make the Peach Glaze: In a saucepan, combine the diced peaches, honey, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook over medium heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the peaches break down and the glaze thickens. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Prep the Chicken: Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Grill the Chicken: Place the chicken thighs on the grill, skin-side down. Grill for 6-7 minutes on each side, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Glaze the Chicken: During the last few minutes of grilling, brush the peach glaze over the chicken thighs, allowing the glaze to caramelize slightly.
  5. Serve: Remove from the grill and let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. The juicy chicken, paired with the sweet peach glaze, will be a standout dish at your BBQ.

Fresh Berry and Mint Salad

For a light and refreshing dessert, a fresh berry and mint salad is the perfect way to wrap up your summer BBQ. The combination of sweet berries with a hint of mint and a touch of honey creates a light, delicious finish to your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup raspberries
  • 1/2 cup blackberries
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fruit: In a large bowl, combine all of the fresh berries.
  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together honey and lemon juice until smooth. Pour the dressing over the berries and toss gently to combine.
  3. Serve: Sprinkle the chopped mint leaves on top and serve immediately. This light and refreshing dessert is perfect for summer BBQs and pairs beautifully with the savory dishes you’ve prepared.

Tips for a Successful Farm-to-Table BBQ

  • Plan Ahead: Shop at your local farmers’ market or directly from a farm to get the freshest ingredients. Many farmers’ markets offer seasonal produce and locally raised meats, so take advantage of these resources.
  • Keep It Simple: Farm-to-table cooking is all about celebrating the natural flavors of the ingredients, so avoid overcomplicating your recipes. Use simple seasonings and let the fresh produce shine.
  • Grill with Care: Grilling enhances the flavors of many ingredients, but be careful not to overcook delicate vegetables or proteins. Use direct and indirect heat to control the cooking process and achieve the best results.
  • Use Seasonal Ingredients: Make the most of what’s in season. Summer is the perfect time for tomatoes, peppers, zucchini, peaches, and berries. By incorporating seasonal produce into your BBQ, you’ll ensure that your meal is bursting with flavor.

Conclusion

Farm-to-table BBQs are all about embracing the season’s freshest ingredients and enjoying the vibrant flavors of summer. From grilled corn with herb butter to succulent chicken thighs glazed with peaches, each recipe brings a unique, farm-fresh touch to your BBQ spread. So, gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and enjoy a summer BBQ that celebrates the beauty of locally sourced, fresh ingredients.
