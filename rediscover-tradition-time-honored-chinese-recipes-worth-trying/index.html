Rediscover Tradition: Time-Honored Chinese Recipes Worth Trying


Chinese cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, textures, and aromas, with a rich history that spans thousands of years. From the spicy Sichuan hot pots to the delicate Cantonese dim sum, each region has its own unique cooking techniques, ingredients, and traditions. In this article, we will delve into the world of traditional Chinese recipes, exploring the secrets behind some of the most iconic dishes that have been passed down through generations.

The Art of Braising

Braising is a cooking technique that involves cooking ingredients in liquid over low heat for an extended period of time. This method is often used to cook tougher cuts of meat, such as beef shank or pork belly, until they become tender and fall-apart. One of the most famous braised dishes in Chinese cuisine is the Cantonese-style braised pork belly, also known as “siu yuk”.

To make siu yuk, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 1 star anise
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fermented bean paste (doubanjiang)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 275°F (135°C).
  2. Cut the pork belly into a large piece and score the fat layer in a crisscross pattern.
  3. In a large saucepan, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, Shaoxing wine, and vegetable oil. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic, ginger, star anise, cloves, and cinnamon stick to the saucepan. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Add the pork belly to the saucepan and braise for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and the fat is caramelized.
  6. Remove the pork belly from the saucepan and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing it into thin strips.
  7. Strain the braising liquid and discard the solids. Skim off any excess fat and serve the pork belly with the braising liquid spooned over the top.

The Art of Stir-Frying

Stir-frying is a quick and versatile cooking technique that involves cooking ingredients in a wok or large skillet over high heat. This method is often used to cook vegetables, meat, and noodles, and is a staple of Chinese cuisine. One of the most famous stir-fried dishes in Chinese cuisine is the Sichuan-style Kung Pao chicken.

To make Kung Pao chicken, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into small pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Scallions, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the wok or large skillet over high heat and add the vegetable oil. Swirl the oil to coat the bottom of the pan.
  2. Add the chicken to the pan and cook until it is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Add the garlic and onion to the pan and stir-fry until they are softened, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the peanuts, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch to the pan and stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the chicken back to the pan and stir-fry until it is well coated with the sauce. Season with Sichuan peppercorns, salt, and black pepper.
  6. Garnish with chopped scallions, if desired. Serve immediately.

The Art of Steaming

Steaming is a gentle cooking technique that involves cooking ingredients in steam, often using a steamer basket or a metal steamer. This method is often used to cook delicate ingredients, such as fish and vegetables, and is a staple of Cantonese cuisine. One of the most famous steamed dishes in Chinese cuisine is the Cantonese-style steamed fish with ginger and scallions.

To make steamed fish with ginger and scallions, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound fish fillets (such as sole or cod), skin removed
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the fish fillets under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and vegetable oil. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Place the fish fillets in a steamer basket and pour the soy sauce mixture over the top.
  4. Steam the fish for 8-10 minutes, or until it is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Garnish with sliced ginger and chopped scallions. Serve immediately.

Conclusion

In this article, we have explored the world of traditional Chinese recipes, delving into the secrets behind some of the most iconic dishes that have been passed down through generations. From the art of braising to the art of stir-frying, and from the art of steaming to the art of cooking with woks, we have seen how these techniques can be used to create delicious and authentic Chinese dishes. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, we hope that this article has inspired you to try new recipes and explore the rich and diverse world of Chinese cuisine.
