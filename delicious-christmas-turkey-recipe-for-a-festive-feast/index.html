Delicious Christmas Turkey Recipe for a Festive Feast


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to celebrate than with a delicious Christmas turkey? This mouthwatering recipe will leave your guests in awe and have them coming back for seconds. With a perfect blend of flavors and a juicy, tender texture, this turkey is sure to be the star of your festive feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (12-15 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 3 cups chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the turkey on a rack in a roasting pan, breast side up.

  2. In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, chopped rosemary, thyme, sage, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. This aromatic herb butter will infuse the turkey with incredible flavors.

  3. Carefully loosen the skin of the turkey by gently sliding your fingers between the skin and the meat. Be careful not to tear the skin. Spread the herb butter mixture evenly under the skin, covering as much of the turkey as possible.

  4. Squeeze one half of the lemon over the turkey, then place both lemon halves inside the cavity of the bird. Add the quartered onion as well.

  5. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the roasting pan. This will keep the turkey moist and flavorful throughout the cooking process.

  6. Tent the turkey loosely with aluminum foil, ensuring that the foil does not touch the skin. This will prevent the skin from becoming overly browned and crispy.

  7. Place the turkey in the preheated oven and roast for approximately 15 minutes per pound. A 12-pound turkey will take around 3 hours to cook, while a 15-pound turkey will take closer to 3 hours and 45 minutes. Remember to baste the turkey with the pan juices every 30 minutes to ensure a moist and succulent result.

  8. About 30 minutes before the turkey is done, remove the foil to allow the skin to brown and crisp up. This will give the turkey an irresistible golden finish.

  9. To check if the turkey is cooked, insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). If the turkey needs more time, continue cooking and checking the temperature every 15 minutes until it reaches the desired doneness.

  10. Once cooked, remove the turkey from the oven and let it rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a moist and flavorful turkey.

  11. Carve the turkey and serve it with your favorite holiday sides, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, and cranberry sauce. Enjoy the delicious flavors of Christmas!

Conclusion

This delicious Christmas turkey recipe is a surefire way to impress your guests and create a memorable festive feast. The herb butter infusion, combined with the juicy and tender meat, will leave everyone craving more. Don’t forget to pair this mouthwatering turkey with all your favorite holiday sides for a truly delectable meal. So, gather your loved ones, embrace the holiday spirit, and indulge in this festive turkey delight. Happy cooking and Merry Christmas!
