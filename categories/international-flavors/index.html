Peruvian Ceviche de Camarones Recipe with Shrimp and Lime

Ceviche, the popular dish from Peru, is a refreshing and tangy seafood dish that is perfect for warm summer days. One of the most beloved variations of ceviche is the Peruvian Ceviche de Camarones, which features succulent shrimp and zesty lime juice. In this recipe, we will explore the traditional method of preparing this delightful dish. Ingredients 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 red onion, thinly sliced 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped 1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lime juice Salt and pepper to taste 1 sweet potato, boiled and sliced 1 ear of corn, boiled and cut into rounds Lettuce leaves for serving Instructions Start by preparing the shrimp. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and add the shrimp. Cook for about 2-3 minutes until the shrimp turn pink and opaque. Remove from heat and drain. Once the shrimp have cooled, chop them into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large mixing bowl. Add the thinly sliced red onion, minced jalapeno pepper, and chopped cilantro to the bowl with the shrimp. Pour the freshly squeezed lime juice over the mixture, ensuring that all the ingredients are well coated. The acidity of the lime juice will cook the shrimp, giving them a tender and flavorful texture. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to start with a small amount and adjust as needed. Gently toss all the ingredients together, making sure that the flavors are evenly distributed. Allow the ceviche to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. While the ceviche is marinating, prepare the accompaniments. Peel and boil the sweet potato until tender. Slice it into rounds and set aside. Boil the corn until cooked, then cut it into rounds as well. To serve, line a serving dish with lettuce leaves. Arrange the marinated ceviche in the center of the dish, and place the sweet potato and corn rounds around it as garnish. Serve the Peruvian Ceviche de Camarones immediately, along with some crispy corn tortilla chips or toasted bread for dipping. Tips for Success Freshness is key when it comes to ceviche. Ensure that your shrimp is of high quality and smells fresh before cooking. Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less jalapeno pepper. You can also leave the seeds in for an extra kick. For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding diced avocado or mango to the ceviche. Serve the ceviche immediately after marinating to enjoy the vibrant flavors at their best. Peruvian Ceviche de Camarones is a dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of Peru. The tangy lime juice, combined with the succulent shrimp, creates a refreshing and light dish that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply craving a taste of Peru, this ceviche recipe is sure to impress. Give it a try and savor the flavors of this delightful dish! ...
