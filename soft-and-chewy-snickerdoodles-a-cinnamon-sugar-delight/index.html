Soft and Chewy Snickerdoodles: A Cinnamon-Sugar Delight


Are you craving a classic cookie that is soft, chewy, and packed with the comforting flavors of cinnamon and sugar? Look no further than these delightful Snickerdoodles! This timeless recipe is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and leave you wanting more. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is perfect for all skill levels. So, let’s dive right into creating these delectable treats!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for rolling)
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon (for rolling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and 1 1/2 cups of granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This step is crucial as it creates the perfect texture for our Snickerdoodles.

  3. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. This will ensure a smooth and cohesive dough.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a tougher cookie.

  5. In a small bowl, combine the remaining 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar and 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon. This will be used for rolling the cookie dough.

  6. Scoop out about 2 tablespoons of dough and roll it into a ball using your hands. Roll each dough ball in the cinnamon-sugar mixture until fully coated. Place the coated dough balls onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.

  7. Bake the Snickerdoodles in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. The centers may still appear slightly undercooked, but they will firm up as they cool.

  8. Remove the baking sheet from the oven and allow the cookies to cool on the sheet for 5 minutes. Transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before enjoying.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra soft and chewy texture, slightly underbake the cookies by reducing the baking time by a minute or two.

  • If you prefer a more pronounced cinnamon flavor, you can increase the amount of ground cinnamon in the dough or in the rolling mixture.

  • Add a touch of vanilla extract to the dough for an additional layer of flavor.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, consider adding a sprinkle of coarse sea salt on top of each cookie before baking. The combination of sweet and salty is simply irresistible!

  • These Snickerdoodles can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. However, we doubt they’ll last that long!

Now that you have this fantastic Snickerdoodle recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to get baking! Whip up a batch of these soft and chewy delights and watch them disappear before your eyes. Whether you’re sharing them with loved ones or indulging in a solo treat, these cinnamon-sugar cookies are sure to bring joy to your taste buds. Happy baking!
