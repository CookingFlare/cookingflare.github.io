Homemade Sourdough Baguettes: Crusty and Delicious


Making your own sourdough baguettes at home is a rewarding and exciting experience. With a few simple ingredients and steps, you can create crusty, delicious, and authentic-looking baguettes that will impress your friends and family. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making these delectable breads, so let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 250 g active sourdough starter
  • 800 g bread flour
  • 100 g whole wheat flour
  • 40 g fined salt
  • 10 g honey
  • 350 ml filtered water, at room temperature

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Begin by mixing your active sourdough starter with the whole wheat flour in a large mixing bowl. This will create a thick batter that will serve as the base for your baguette dough.

  2. Autolyse: Add the bread flour, salt, and honey to the mixture. Mix everything together with the filtered water until combined. Cover the bowl with a damp towel or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This process called autolyse allows the dough to absorb the water and flour, making it easier to handle and knead later.

  3. Kneading: After the autolyse period, uncover the bowl and use your hands to knead the dough thoroughly. Be sure to scrape the sides and bottom of the bowl, incorporating any loose flour or dough particles. Knead for about 10 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. First Proof: Once the dough is properly kneaded, place it back in the covered bowl and allow it to rise for about 3 hours at room temperature. This first proofing period will help the dough develop its characteristic sour flavor and improve its texture.

  5. Shaping: After the first proof, remove the dough from the bowl and cut it into two equal pieces for the baguettes. Shape each piece into a long, skinny log, rounding the edges and tapering the ends. Use a flour-dusted cloth or ruler to help you achieve a uniform shape.

  6. Second Proof: Transfer the shaped baguettes to a proofing basket or a specially designed baguette basket. Place them in a cool area for about 1-2 hours, allowing them to rise a little more before baking. You can also refrigerate the baguettes overnight, giving them a more pronounced flavor.

  7. Preheating: Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) with a baking stone or a pizza peel inside. Prepare a steam source by placing a large pan with water on the bottom rack or adding a water-filled dish in the oven.

  8. Baking: Once the oven reaches the desired temperature, remove the shaped baguettes from the proofing basket and transfer them to a piece of parchment paper. Use a sharp blade or razor to score the baguettes, creating 1-2 cm deep slashes on the top. This helps control the expansion of the dough during baking.

  9. Final Touches: Dust the baguettes lightly with flour, creating that classic baguette look.

  10. Final Steam: Place the baguettes with the parchment paper directly on the hot baking stone or pizza peel into the preheated oven. Quickly pour hot water into the steam source or create steam by spraying water using a misting bottle. Close the oven door to trap the steam and allow it to circulate inside.

  11. Baking Time: Bake your homemade sourdough baguettes for about 20-25 minutes. When finished, they should have a dark, beautiful crust and a crisp texture.

That’s it! You now have delicious, crusty, and homemade sourdough baguettes. These breads are perfect for sandwiches, as a side dish, or just to enjoy on their own. Happy baking!
