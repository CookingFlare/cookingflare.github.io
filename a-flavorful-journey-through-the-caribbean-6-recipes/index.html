A Flavorful Journey Through the Caribbean: 6 Recipes


As we embark on a culinary journey through the Caribbean, we're transported to a world of vibrant flavors, rich traditions, and decadent delights. The region's diverse past and present come alive in every dish, each one a testament to the indigenous, African, European, and Asian influences that have shaped its cuisine. Our flavorful journey takes us to six distinct islands, each with its unique twists on traditional cooking. We'll explore the bold flavors of Jamaica, the picturesque charm of Barbados, the exotic flair of Trinidad and Tobago, the spicy zest of St. Lucia, the rich history of Haiti, and the culinary innovations of the Dominican Republic.

  1. Jerk Chicken with Caribbean Rice and Beans Imagine plucking juicy chicken from a spit, emitting the unmistakable aroma of allspice and thyme. Our Jerk Chicken is marinated in a secret blend of spices and herbs, smoked to perfection on a grill or stovetop. Serve it with a flavorful Caribbean Rice and Beans, seasoned with a pinch of salt, a dash of pepper, and the warmth of scotch bonnet peppers. Jerk Chicken:

1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts 1/4 cup jerk seasoning 2 tablespoons lime juice 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1/4 cup chopped scotch bonnet peppers 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Caribbean Rice and Beans:

1 cup uncooked white rice 1 cup uncooked kidney beans 2 cups chicken broth 1 tablespoon lima beans 1 tablespoon chopped scallions Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Preheat grill or stovetop to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, combine jerk seasoning, lime juice, soy sauce, scotch bonnet peppers, garlic, brown sugar, and black pepper. Add chicken to marinade, refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight. Grill or pan-fry chicken, about 5-6 minutes per side. Meanwhile, cook Caribbean Rice and Beans according to package instructions. Serve jerk chicken with Caribbean rice and beans, and enjoy the bold flavors of the Caribbean.

  1. Conch Fritters with Mango Salsa Imagine heading to the beachside bar in Nassau, where delectable Conch Fritters are served with a tropical twist. These crispy fritters showcase the tender conch meat, bathed in a sweet and spicy mango salsa. Don't be surprised if you devour an entire plate – these scrumptious bites are addictive! Conch Fritters:

1 pound conch meat (canned or fresh) 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup cornstarch 1/4 cup grated cheddar cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/2 cup buttermilk Vegetable oil, for frying

Mango Salsa:

2 ripe mangos, diced 1/4 cup red onion, diced 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped 1 tablespoon lime juice 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine conch meat, flour, cornstarch, cheddar cheese, parsley, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Add buttermilk and mix well. Shape mixture into small patties. Fry fritters in hot oil, about 2-3 minutes per side. Meanwhile, mix mango, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, and cilantro for the salsa. Serve conch fritters with mango salsa and enjoy the delightful tropical flavors.

  1. Barbados-Style Beef and Vegetable Stew Embark on a culinary adventure to the beautiful island of Barbados, where Beef and Vegetable Stew reign supreme. This hearty, comforting dish is a staple of the region's cuisine, blending tender beef with a medley of colorful vegetables and aromatic spices. Barbados-Style Beef and Vegetable Stew:

1 pound beef stew meat 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 eggplant, diced 2 bell peppers (any color), diced 1 cup beef broth 1 tablespoon tomato paste 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Add beef, cooking until browned, about 5 minutes. Add eggplant, bell peppers, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Cook, covered, until beef and vegetables are tender, about 1-2 hours. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.

  1. Crispy Fried Plantains with Feta Cheese Geographically, Trinidad and Tobago sits at the south of the Caribbean Island chain. Culturally and gastronomically, these islands share a unique flavor profile, showcased in Crispy Fried Plantains with Feta Cheese. Thin slices of fried plantain take on a golden crunch, while creamy feta cheese melts in the warmth of island spices. Crispy Fried Plantains:

2 ripe plantains 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup cornstarch 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/2 teaspoon paprika 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper Vegetable oil, for frying

Feta Cheese:

1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese 1 tablespoon chopped fresh mint

Instructions:

Peel and slice plantains into 1/4-inch rounds. In a shallow dish, combine flour, cornstarch, Parmesan cheese, parsley, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Dip each plantain slice into flour mixture, shaking off excess. Fry plantains in hot oil, about 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown and crispy. Meanwhile, mix feta cheese and chopped mint. Serve crispy fry plantains with feta cheese, this flavor-packed delight ensuring a delightful culinary experience.

  1. St. Lucian Conch Cakes with Tropical Mango Salsa Travel with us to the enchanting island of St. Lucia, renowned for its vibrant culture and mouthwatering cuisine. In the bustling streets of St. Lucia, immerse yourselves in the passion-fueled rhythm of live music and tantalize your taste buds with St. Lucian Conch Cakes. These fusion wonders pair crispy conch meat patties with the depth of walnut crust, on a smooth bed of Mango Salsa. St. Lucian Conch Cakes:

1 pound conch meat (canned or fresh) 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup cornstarch 1/4 cup grated cheddar cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/2 teaspoon paprika 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/2 cup buttermilk Vegetable oil, for frying 2 cups walnuts, chopped

Mango Salsa:

2 ripe mangos, diced 1/4 cup red onion, diced 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped 1 tablespoon lime juice 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine conch meat, flour, cornstarch, cheddar cheese, parsley, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Add buttermilk and mix well. Shape mixture into small patties. Coat each patty in walnut crust mixture. Heat oil in a deep pot over medium heat. Fry Conch Cakes, about 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown. Meanwhile, combine mango, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, and cilantro for the salsa. Serve Conch Cakes with Tropical Mango Salsa.

Haitian-Style Chicken and Rice Bowl with Dried Fruit and Almonds Travel to the beautiful island of Haiti, which boasts rich culinary traditions grounded in African, French, and Spanish influences. Enjoy the sought-after Haitian-Style Chicken and Rice Bowl with Dried Fruit and Almonds. This delectable bowl overflows with vibrant flavors: soft rice is combined with rich beef broth, decadent chicken, sumptuous dried fruit, crunchy almonds, and boisterous Haiti's heart-stopping aromatic spices. Haitian-Style Chicken and Rice Bowl:

1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts 2 cups cooked white rice 2 cups chicken broth 1/4 cup chopped scallions 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1/4 cup chopped dried fruit (dates, apricots, pineapple) 1/4 cup chopped almonds Salt and pepper, to taste 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon Haitian atmosphere with habanero peppers, very efficient

Instructions:

Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chicken to skillet and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-6 minutes. Add cooked rice to skillet, stir to combine, breaking up any clumps. Add chicken broth, scallions, cilantro, dried fruit, almonds, salt, pepper, cumin, and habanero peppers to skillet. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve reddish-brown chicken bowls, garnished with further aromatic thyme.
