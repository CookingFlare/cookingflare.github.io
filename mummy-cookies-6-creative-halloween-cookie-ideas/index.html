Mummy Cookies: 6 Creative Halloween Cookie Ideas


When it comes to Halloween treats, cookies topped the list of go-to snacks for both kids and adults. But let’s face it — your average cookie just doesn’t cut it once October rolls around. Enter Mummy Cookies, an exciting twist on the classic baked good that’s perfect for any Halloween party, classroom treat, or cozy night in with spooky vibes.

Mummy-themed cookies blend visual appeal with tasty ingredients, offering an impressive but approachable way to transform your kitchen into a Halloween bakery. These six creative mummy cookie ideas bring fun and flavor together—each with its own unique flair and twist on the undead theme.

1. Classic White Chocolate Mummy Cookies

Starting simple never hurt anyone. The classic white chocolate mummy cookies combine the creamy sweetness of white chocolate with the soft chewiness of sugar cookies, adorned with strips of melted white chocolate ramen to mimic mummy wrappings.

Ingredients:

  • Sugar cookie dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • White chocolate chips or melting wafers
  • Mini chocolate chips or edible candy eyes
  • A little coconut oil (for melting white chocolate smoothly)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare your cookie dough. Roll into evenly sized balls and place on parchment-lined baking sheets.
  2. Bake for 8-10 minutes until the edges slightly golden. Remove and cool on wire racks.
  3. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt white chocolate and a dab of coconut oil for smooth consistency.
  4. Using a piping bag or a small ziplock with the corner cut, drizzle thin lines across the cookies to create the mummy wrap effect.
  5. Before the chocolate hardens, quickly add mini chocolate chips or candy eyes to give your mummy its eerie gaze.

This recipe focuses on the contrast between the white chocolate wraps and the soft cookie base, enhancing both flavor and texture. These cookies are light, sweet, and perfect for decorating with messy, drippy “bandages.”

2. Spiced Pumpkin Mummy Cookies

With fall’s favorite flavor starring here, pumpkin mummy cookies offer a seasonal twist. Infused with warm spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice, this recipe brings comfort and festivity to your Halloween lineup.

Ingredients:

  • Pumpkin puree (1/2 cup)
  • All-purpose flour (2 cups)
  • Brown sugar (3/4 cup)
  • Butter (1/2 cup, softened)
  • Egg (1 large)
  • Baking powder (1 tsp)
  • Pumpkin pie spice (1 tbsp)
  • White chocolate or cream cheese frosting for decoration
  • Candy eyes

Directions:

  1. Cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add the egg and pumpkin puree; mix well.
  2. In a separate bowl, sift the flour, baking powder, and pumpkin pie spice.
  3. Gradually add dry ingredients into the wet mixture until a smooth dough forms.
  4. Chill dough for 30 minutes to make shaping easier.
  5. Roll dough and cut into rounds or ovals; bake at 350°F for 10-12 minutes until set but soft.
  6. Once cooled, apply thin lines of white frosting to mimic mummy wraps and add candy eyes while the frosting is still wet.

This moist, spiced treat combines a soft, tender crumb with bright orange pumpkin hues masked under playful mummy bandages—perfect for festive gatherings and afternoon tea.

3. Chocolate Mummy Monster Cookies

For chocolate lovers seeking a bold Halloween experience, chocolate mummy monster cookies are loaded with cocoa intensity and fun monster vibes. Think chewy dark chocolate cookies wrapped in white drizzle with quirky candy eyes.

Ingredients:

  • Dark cocoa powder (3/4 cup)
  • Flour (1 3/4 cups)
  • Baking soda (1 tsp)
  • Salt (1/2 tsp)
  • Brown sugar (1 cup)
  • Granulated sugar (1/2 cup)
  • Butter (1 cup, softened)
  • Eggs (2 large)
  • Vanilla extract (1 tsp)
  • White candy melts or white chocolate
  • Edible googly eyes (you can find them in baking stores)

Directions:

  1. Cream butter and sugars until light and fluffy.
  2. Beat in eggs one at a time, then vanilla extract.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine cocoa, flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients to wet, mixing until just combined.
  5. Scoop spoonfuls onto a cookie sheet lined with parchment, spacing evenly.
  6. Bake at 350°F for approximately 10 minutes.
  7. After cooling, melt white candy melts and use a piping bag to create the mummy bandage effect.
  8. Place a few edible googly eyes onto the cookies while the drizzle is still wet.

These cookies pack a depth of rich chocolate flavor and look playful with their “monster” personalities. Ideal for chocolate enthusiasts and Halloween fanatics alike.

4. No-Bake Peanut Butter Mummy Cookie Bites

Not in the mood to bake? No problem. These no-bake peanut butter mummy cookie bites bring a quick, nutty delight with minimal prep.

Ingredients:

  • Creamy peanut butter (1 cup)
  • Honey (1/2 cup)
  • Rolled oats (2 cups)
  • Crushed graham crackers (1 cup)
  • White chocolate chips or yogurt chips
  • Mini chocolate chips or candy eyes

Directions:

  1. Mix peanut butter and honey in a bowl until smooth.
  2. Stir in oats and graham cracker crumbs to form a thick dough.
  3. Roll into small bite-sized balls and chill for about 30 minutes.
  4. Melt white chocolate chips and drizzle thin lines over the chilled bites to create mummy wraps.
  5. Immediately add mini chocolate chips or candy eyes.

These bites combine chewy oats with the richness of peanut butter and the crunch of graham crackers—perfect for a quick snack or an easy Halloween treat station.

5. Ghostly Mummy Meringue Cookies

For a light and airy twist, ghostly mummy meringue cookies bring ethereal sweetness to your Halloween spread. These are perfect for those who want a crisp, melt-in-your-mouth experience.

Ingredients:

  • Egg whites (4 large)
  • Granulated sugar (1 cup)
  • Cream of tartar (1/4 tsp)
  • Vanilla extract (1 tsp)
  • Mini chocolate chips or black icing for eyes

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 225°F (110°C).
  2. Beat egg whites and cream of tartar on medium speed until foamy.
  3. Gradually add sugar, one tablespoon at a time, while beating until stiff peaks form.
  4. Fold in vanilla extract gently.
  5. Pipe small rounded shapes onto parchment paper to mimic mummy heads.
  6. Bake for 1 hour and then turn off the oven, leaving meringues inside to dry for another hour.
  7. When cooled, draw mummy eyes using mini chocolate chips or a dab of black edible icing.

This recipe produces light-as-air meringues that look ghostly but play with the mummy theme through their shape and decoration. A refined, delicate treat for Halloween afternoon tea or party favors.

6. Chewy Caramel Mummy Oatmeal Cookies

For a heartier cookie, caramel mummy oatmeal cookies deliver layers of texture and a sweet caramel kick, wrapped in festive mummy decoration.

Ingredients:

  • Rolled oats (2 cups)
  • Flour (1 cup)
  • Baking soda (1/2 tsp)
  • Salt (1/4 tsp)
  • Cinnamon (1 tsp)
  • Butter (3/4 cup, softened)
  • Brown sugar (3/4 cup)
  • Granulated sugar (1/4 cup)
  • Eggs (2 large)
  • Vanilla extract (1 tsp)
  • Caramel bits (1 cup)
  • White chocolate or vanilla icing for wraps
  • Candy eyes

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cream butter and sugars until fluffy.
  3. Add eggs and vanilla extract, mix well.
  4. In another bowl, combine oats, flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  5. Slowly mix dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
  6. Fold in caramel bits for surprise chewiness.
  7. Drop spoonfuls on baking sheets spaced apart.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden and set.
  9. Let cool and decorate with white chocolate or icing mummy wraps and candy eyes.

This chewy cookie embraces the warmth of oatmeal with caramel sweetness and the spooky delight of mummy decoration, making it a crowd-pleaser.

Wrapping Up Your Mummy Cookie Adventure

Creating mummy cookies is an enjoyable and versatile way to celebrate the spooky season. Whether you’re baking from scratch, assembling no-bake bites, or experimenting with flavors, these six ideas present options for every taste, skill level, and occasion.

You can customize your mummy cookies with different eyes (candy, icing, or chocolate), vary the drizzle technique for more realistic bandaging, or add a sprinkle of edible glitter or colored sugar for that extra festive effect.

Whip these cookies up for Halloween parties, trick-or-treat giveaways, or simple dessert platters that bring some ghostly charm to life. Each recipe offers a different take on the mummy concept — from sweet and soft to crunchy and spiced — so you have the ultimate lineup ready for the haunting season.

Happy baking, and may your cookie bandages never unravel!
