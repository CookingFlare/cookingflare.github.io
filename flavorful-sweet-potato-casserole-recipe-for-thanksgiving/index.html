Flavorful Sweet Potato Casserole Recipe for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones and indulge in a feast that celebrates the bounties of the season. And what better way to complement your turkey than with a delicious and flavorful sweet potato casserole? This classic dish is a crowd-pleaser that never fails to impress. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to take your sweet potato casserole to the next level, creating a dish that will have your guests coming back for seconds (and maybe even thirds!).

Ingredients

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and set aside.

  2. Place the sweet potato cubes in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes. Drain and return the potatoes to the pot.

  3. Using a potato masher or fork, mash the sweet potatoes until smooth. Add the melted butter, brown sugar, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix well to combine.

  4. Add the beaten eggs to the sweet potato mixture and stir until fully incorporated.

  5. Pour the sweet potato mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it evenly.

  6. In a small bowl, combine the chopped pecans with a tablespoon of melted butter and a sprinkle of brown sugar. Mix well to coat the pecans.

  7. Sprinkle the pecan mixture evenly over the sweet potato mixture in the baking dish.

  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the casserole is set and the pecans are golden brown.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This flavorful sweet potato casserole is a fantastic addition to your Thanksgiving menu. The creamy and sweet potato base paired with the crunchy and caramelized pecan topping creates a delightful contrast of textures and flavors. Serve it alongside your perfectly roasted turkey, and watch as your guests swoon over this delicious creation.

For an extra touch of indulgence, you can top the casserole with mini marshmallows during the last 5 minutes of baking. The marshmallows will melt and form a gooey, sweet layer that adds another dimension of deliciousness to the dish.

Make-Ahead Tips

If you’re looking to save time on Thanksgiving day, you can prepare the sweet potato casserole in advance. After step 7, cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When you’re ready to serve, simply remove the foil, let the casserole come to room temperature for about 30 minutes, and then bake as directed.

Conclusion

This flavorful sweet potato casserole recipe is a must-have for your Thanksgiving feast. It’s a dish that combines classic flavors with a modern twist, making it a standout side dish that will have everyone asking for the recipe. So, this Thanksgiving, elevate your menu with this crowd-pleasing sweet potato casserole and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Enjoy and Happy Thanksgiving!
