Mouthwatering Shrimp and Scallop Linguine Fra Diavolo


If you’re craving a dish that combines the richness of seafood with a spicy kick, look no further than this delectable Shrimp and Scallop Linguine Fra Diavolo recipe. Fra Diavolo, which translates to “Brother Devil” in Italian, is a fitting name for this dish known for its fiery tomato sauce and succulent seafood. It’s a classic Italian-American favorite that promises to tantalize your taste buds and impress your dinner guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound linguine pasta
  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound sea scallops, tough muscles removed
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pasta:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the linguine according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Cook the Seafood:

    • In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and scallops in a single layer. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are just cooked through and have a nice golden color. Remove from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Make the Fra Diavolo Sauce:

    • In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Saute the minced garlic, chopped onion, and red pepper flakes for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  4. Deglaze the Pan:

    • Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes, allowing the alcohol to evaporate and the flavors to meld.

  5. Add the Tomatoes:

    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.

  6. Combine Pasta and Seafood:

    • Add the cooked linguine to the skillet with the Fra Diavolo sauce. Toss well to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.

  7. Add the Seafood Back In:

    • Gently fold in the cooked shrimp and scallops, taking care not to break them apart. Allow everything to heat through for another 2-3 minutes.

  8. Season and Serve:

    • Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Stir in the chopped fresh basil.

  9. Serve Hot:

    • Divide the Shrimp and Scallop Linguine Fra Diavolo among serving plates. Garnish with chopped parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Fresh Seafood: Use the highest quality shrimp and scallops you can find for the best flavor and texture.
  • Spice Level: Adjust the amount of red pepper flakes to suit your preference for spiciness.
  • Wine Selection: A dry white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio works well for deglazing the pan and adds depth to the sauce.
  • Garnish: Fresh herbs like basil and parsley not only enhance the flavor but also add a pop of color to your dish.

Enjoy Your Meal!

This Shrimp and Scallop Linguine Fra Diavolo is perfect for a special dinner occasion or whenever you’re in the mood for something indulgent yet satisfying. The combination of tender seafood, spicy tomato sauce, and perfectly cooked pasta creates a harmony of flavors that will leave everyone at the table asking for seconds. Serve with a side of crusty bread to mop up the delicious sauce, and enjoy with a glass of your favorite wine. Buon appetito!
