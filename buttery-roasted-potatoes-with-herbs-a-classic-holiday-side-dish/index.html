Buttery Roasted Potatoes with Herbs: A Classic Holiday Side Dish


When the holidays roll around, the kitchen becomes a bustling hub of activity, filled with the enticing aromas of festive dishes being prepared. Among the array of culinary delights that grace our tables during this season, buttery roasted potatoes with herbs stand out as a timeless classic. Their golden, crispy exterior combined with a soft, fluffy interior makes them the perfect side dish to complement any holiday feast. In this post, we’ll explore everything you need to know to create this delightful dish, including tips on selecting the right potatoes, techniques for achieving the perfect texture, and the best herbs to enhance the flavor.

The Allure of Roasted Potatoes

Roasted potatoes hold a special place in holiday gatherings. They’re not just a side dish; they bring warmth, comfort, and a touch of nostalgia. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity. With just a handful of ingredients, you can create a mouthwatering addition to your holiday menu that appeals to both the young and old. The combination of butter and herbs creates a rich, savory flavor that elevates the humble potato into a gourmet experience.

Choosing the Right Potatoes

The first step in crafting the perfect buttery roasted potatoes is selecting the right type of potato. While there are many varieties available, not all are suited for roasting. Here are some of the best options:

  • Yukon Gold Potatoes: Known for their creamy texture and buttery flavor, Yukon Golds are ideal for roasting. Their thin skin crisps up beautifully, while the flesh remains tender.

  • Red Potatoes: These have a slightly waxy texture, which helps them hold their shape during cooking. Their vibrant color adds visual appeal to your dish.

  • Russet Potatoes: Also known as Idaho potatoes, russets are starchy and fluffy when cooked. However, they can become too soft if overcooked, so they require careful attention during roasting.

Choosing the right potato will significantly impact the final outcome of your dish. For a harmonious blend of flavors and textures, consider mixing different varieties for a more complex taste experience.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare buttery roasted potatoes with herbs, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold, red, or Russet)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Preparing the Potatoes

Once you’ve chosen your potatoes, it’s time to prepare them for roasting. Follow these steps for optimal results:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C). A hot oven is crucial for achieving that crispy exterior.

  2. Wash and Cut the Potatoes: Rinse the potatoes under cold water to remove any dirt. Depending on the size, you can leave them whole (for smaller potatoes) or cut them into quarters (for larger potatoes). Aim for uniform pieces to ensure even cooking.

  3. Parboil the Potatoes: To enhance the creaminess of the potatoes, consider parboiling them. Place the cut potatoes in a large pot of salted water and bring it to a boil. Let them cook for about 5-7 minutes until they are just tender but not fully cooked. Drain and allow them to steam dry for a few minutes.

The Flavor Base

While the potatoes are drying, prepare the flavor base. In a large mixing bowl, combine melted butter and olive oil. The combination of these fats provides a rich flavor and helps the potatoes crisp up beautifully.

  • Add Garlic and Herbs: Mix in the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, and thyme. The aromatic herbs will infuse the potatoes with an irresistible fragrance.

  • Season Well: Generously season the mixture with salt and freshly ground black pepper. The seasoning will be absorbed by the potatoes, enhancing their overall flavor.

Roasting the Potatoes

Now that your potatoes are prepped and your flavor base is ready, it’s time to combine everything and roast them to perfection.

  1. Toss the Potatoes: Add the parboiled potatoes to the bowl with the butter and herb mixture. Gently toss until all the potatoes are evenly coated. Ensure that each piece is covered in the buttery goodness.

  2. Arrange on a Baking Sheet: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup. Spread the coated potatoes in a single layer, ensuring they have enough space to roast evenly. Crowding the pan can lead to steaming rather than roasting.

  3. Roast: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven. Roast the potatoes for 30-35 minutes, flipping them halfway through to ensure even browning. You want them to develop a beautiful golden color and crispy texture.

The Finishing Touch

As the potatoes near the end of their roasting time, the kitchen will fill with a delightful aroma that signals a feast is near. Once they are golden and crispy, remove them from the oven and transfer them to a serving dish. For an extra touch, sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the top for a burst of color and freshness.

Serving Suggestions

Buttery roasted potatoes with herbs are versatile and can complement a variety of main courses. Here are some ideas for pairing:

  • Roasted Turkey: A classic pairing for holidays, the savory flavor of roasted potatoes enhances the rich taste of turkey.

  • Honey Glazed Ham: The sweetness of the ham contrasts beautifully with the herby potatoes, creating a delightful balance on your plate.

  • Prime Rib: These roasted potatoes make an excellent side for a hearty cut of beef, soaking up the flavors of the meat.

Tips for Success

To ensure your buttery roasted potatoes are a hit at your holiday gathering, consider the following tips:

  • Don’t Skip the Parboiling: This step is crucial for fluffy interiors. It helps to create that perfect contrast between the crispy exterior and soft inside.

  • Experiment with Herbs: While rosemary and thyme are traditional, feel free to experiment with other herbs like sage, oregano, or even dill to customize the flavor to your liking.

  • Monitor Cooking Time: Oven temperatures can vary, so keep an eye on your potatoes towards the end of the cooking time. You want them to be golden brown and crispy without burning.

  • Serve Immediately: Roasted potatoes are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven. If you need to keep them warm, place them in a low-temperature oven (around 200°F or 93°C) until ready to serve.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have any leftovers (which is rare with this dish!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, spread them out on a baking sheet and place them in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) until warmed through and crispy again.

Conclusion

Buttery roasted potatoes with herbs are a quintessential holiday side dish that elevates any festive meal. Their delightful combination of flavors, textures, and aromas creates a dish that brings everyone to the table. With simple ingredients and straightforward preparation, this recipe is accessible for cooks of all skill levels. So this holiday season, make sure to add this classic dish to your menu and enjoy the warmth and joy it brings to your celebrations. Happy cooking!
