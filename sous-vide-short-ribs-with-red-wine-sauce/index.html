Sous Vide Short Ribs with Red Wine Sauce


Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we approach meals. It’s all about precision, control, and delivering flavors that melt in your mouth. When it comes to cooking tough cuts of meat like short ribs, sous vide is a game-changer. The result is a juicy, fall-off-the-bone masterpiece with deep, rich flavors, all enhanced by a decadent red wine sauce.

What Is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide is a French term that translates to “under vacuum.” It refers to the process of sealing food in a vacuum bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise, low temperature for an extended period of time. Unlike traditional cooking methods, where temperatures fluctuate wildly, sous vide provides consistent heat, ensuring the food is cooked evenly throughout. This technique allows you to achieve perfect doneness, especially with tougher cuts of meat like short ribs, which can be notoriously challenging to cook.

The beauty of sous vide is that it allows the meat to break down slowly, making it tender without sacrificing flavor. For dishes like short ribs, which benefit from a long cooking time, sous vide is an ideal method.

In this recipe, we’ll use the sous vide technique to create short ribs that are tender, flavorful, and perfectly cooked. To elevate the dish, we’ll finish the ribs with a rich red wine sauce that complements the meat beautifully. Ready to dive in? Let’s get started!

Ingredients for Sous Vide Short Ribs

For the short ribs:

  • 4 bone-in beef short ribs (about 2–3 pounds)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter for searing

For the red wine sauce:

  • 1 cup red wine (a medium-bodied wine like Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot works best)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparing the Short Ribs for Sous Vide Cooking

Before you start, you’ll want to prepare your short ribs. The first step is to season the ribs generously with salt and pepper on all sides. This basic seasoning ensures that the flavor of the meat shines through during cooking.

For added flavor, you can also place a sprig of fresh rosemary and a couple of smashed garlic cloves in the bag with the ribs. These aromatics will infuse the meat during the cooking process, resulting in a richer, more complex flavor.

Once seasoned, place each short rib into a vacuum-seal bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a resealable zip-top bag and the water displacement method (just lower the bag into the water bath slowly to push out the air before sealing).

Sous Vide Cooking Time and Temperature

Set your sous vide immersion circulator to 165°F (74°C) for a tender and juicy result. If you prefer your ribs to be a little more well-done and pull apart easily, you can increase the temperature to 175°F (79°C).

Now, place the sealed bag of short ribs in the water bath. Allow them to cook for 24 to 36 hours. Yes, you read that right—this long cook time is the secret to achieving the fall-apart texture that makes sous vide short ribs so incredible.

While the ribs are cooking, you can take care of other tasks, like preparing the sauce or getting the rest of your meal ready. The great thing about sous vide is that it requires minimal attention during the cooking process, leaving you with more time to focus on other parts of your meal.

Searing the Short Ribs

After your ribs have cooked to perfection in the sous vide bath, it’s time to finish them off with a quick sear. This step is essential for developing a crispy, flavorful crust on the outside of the meat.

To sear the ribs, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil or butter. Once the oil is hot, carefully remove the short ribs from the vacuum bags and pat them dry with paper towels. This will help the meat develop a beautiful sear.

Place the short ribs in the skillet and sear them for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they’re golden brown and crispy. Don’t overcrowd the pan—sear the ribs in batches if necessary. Once seared, set the ribs aside and let them rest while you prepare the red wine sauce.

Making the Red Wine Sauce

The red wine sauce is what really takes this dish to the next level. It’s rich, savory, and perfectly complements the tender short ribs. Plus, the sauce is quick and easy to make once your ribs are done.

Start by heating a tablespoon of olive oil or butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-5 minutes, or until the onion becomes soft and translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to avoid burning.

Next, pour in the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any flavorful bits (called fond) that have stuck to the pan during the cooking process. Let the wine reduce by about half, which should take 4-5 minutes. The reduction will concentrate the flavors and make the sauce richer.

Once the wine has reduced, add the beef stock, balsamic vinegar, and thyme. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. The sauce should thicken slightly as it cooks.

Season with salt and pepper to taste, and then strain the sauce to remove the onions and thyme leaves. This step ensures a smooth, velvety sauce that coats the short ribs beautifully.

Plating and Serving

Now that your sous vide short ribs and red wine sauce are ready, it’s time to plate everything and enjoy your masterpiece.

Start by placing the seared short ribs on a serving platter or individual plates. Spoon a generous amount of the red wine sauce over the top of the ribs, allowing the sauce to pool around the meat. You can garnish with fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs for a touch of color and flavor.

This dish pairs wonderfully with a variety of sides. Some great options include creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a simple green salad. The rich, tender ribs and the savory red wine sauce will be the star of the meal, so choose sides that complement the flavors without overpowering them.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Short Ribs

  • Flavor Variations: Experiment with different herbs and spices in the vacuum-seal bag. You could try adding a sprig of thyme, a bay leaf, or even some crushed black peppercorns for a different flavor profile.

  • Searing: The searing step is important for developing a rich, crispy exterior. If you have a blowtorch, you can also use it to sear the ribs for a more even and controlled crust.

  • Wine Pairing: Since the red wine sauce plays such an important role in this dish, consider serving a glass of the same red wine used in the sauce. A full-bodied red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, or Syrah pairs beautifully with the rich flavors of short ribs.

  • Storage: If you have leftovers, store the ribs and sauce separately in airtight containers. The short ribs will keep in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or can be frozen for up to 3 months. To reheat, you can sous vide them again or warm them gently in the oven.

Why Sous Vide Is the Best Way to Cook Short Ribs

Sous vide cooking takes the guesswork out of preparing short ribs. By cooking them at a precise temperature for an extended period of time, you’re able to achieve a perfect balance between tenderness and juiciness. Traditional methods of cooking short ribs, such as braising, can sometimes result in unevenly cooked meat, but sous vide guarantees that every bite is consistently flavorful.

Moreover, sous vide cooking is hands-off and virtually foolproof. Once the ribs are in the water bath, you can forget about them until they’re ready to be seared and served. This makes it a fantastic option for dinner parties or special occasions when you want to impress without spending all day in the kitchen.

Conclusion

Sous vide short ribs with red wine sauce is the ultimate indulgence for any meat lover. With tender, juicy ribs and a rich, flavorful sauce, this dish is sure to impress. By utilizing the precision of sous vide cooking, you’ll achieve perfect results every time, and the added touch of the red wine sauce makes this meal truly unforgettable.

Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply treating yourself to a delicious dinner, sous vide short ribs with red wine sauce will deliver an experience that’s both comforting and sophisticated. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the sous vide, and get ready to enjoy a dish that will leave everyone asking for seconds.
