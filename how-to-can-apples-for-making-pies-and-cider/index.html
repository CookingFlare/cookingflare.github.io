How to Can Apples for Making Pies and Cider


Canning apples is one of the most rewarding and practical ways to preserve this beloved fruit. Whether you’re making homemade apple pies, crafting your own apple cider, or simply enjoying the apples throughout the year, canning allows you to capture the taste of fall and store it for months. This guide will walk you through the process of canning apples, from selecting the best varieties to ensuring the preservation of their flavor and texture for later use.

Why Canning Apples is Worth the Effort

Canning is a tried-and-true method of preserving food that has been passed down through generations. It’s not just about convenience – canning apples can enhance the flavor of pies, sauces, and ciders when you want the sweet, tart taste of apples year-round.

One of the main reasons to can apples is that it allows you to stockpile them for use in pies, cider, and other apple-based dishes when apples are out of season. Instead of buying costly fresh apples that may not have the same flavor and texture, you can open a jar of your own preserved apples and enjoy their peak flavor, no matter the time of year.

What You’ll Need for Canning Apples

Before diving into the canning process, it’s essential to gather the right equipment. The tools required for canning apples are relatively simple, but having everything in place beforehand can make the process smoother.

Essential Equipment:

  • Canning Jars: Mason jars (quart or pint sizes) are most commonly used for canning apples. Ensure that the jars are clean and free of any chips or cracks.
  • Lids and Bands: You’ll need canning lids and rings (bands) to secure the jars. These should be new or in excellent condition to ensure a proper seal.
  • Canning Pot or Water Bath: A large pot is required for water bath canning, which is the method used for preserving apples. It should be deep enough to cover the jars with at least an inch of water.
  • Jar Lifter: A jar lifter helps you safely remove hot jars from the canning pot without burning yourself.
  • Canning Funnel: This is an optional but helpful tool that allows you to easily fill the jars without spilling.
  • Peeler, Corer, and Slicer: A tool that helps you peel, core, and slice apples quickly will save you time and effort.
  • Large Bowl: A large bowl to hold the apples as you peel and slice them.
  • Lemon Juice: This will help prevent browning and preserve the apples’ color.
  • Syrup or Juice: You’ll need a sugar syrup or apple juice to pack the apples in. You can make a light syrup, medium syrup, or use apple juice, depending on your preference for sweetness.

Selecting Apples for Canning

The choice of apple variety plays a significant role in the final result. Different apple varieties have distinct characteristics, so it’s essential to choose the right type for canning. When looking for apples to can, it’s important to select firm, ripe apples. Overripe or soft apples may not hold up well during the canning process and can affect the texture of your pies and cider.

Best Apple Varieties for Canning:

  • Granny Smith: Known for their tartness and firm texture, Granny Smith apples hold up well during canning. They are excellent for pies and cider.
  • Fuji: These apples are sweet, crisp, and slightly tart, making them great for pies.
  • Golden Delicious: These apples are sweet and soft, which makes them ideal for applesauce or cider.
  • Jonagold: A hybrid of Jonathan and Golden Delicious apples, Jonagold apples are both sweet and tart, perfect for making apple pies.
  • Honeycrisp: Known for their juicy, sweet flavor and crisp texture, Honeycrisp apples are perfect for both pies and cider.

If you are unsure which variety to choose, a mix of tart and sweet apples will create a balanced flavor for pies and cider.

Preparing the Apples

Once you’ve selected your apples, it’s time to prepare them for canning. This step is critical, as it ensures that your apples retain their flavor and texture after processing.

Steps to Prepare Apples:

  1. Wash and Peel: Wash your apples thoroughly to remove any dirt or pesticides. Once washed, peel the apples using a vegetable peeler or paring knife.

  2. Core the Apples: Use an apple corer to remove the cores of the apples. If you don’t have a corer, you can cut the apples in half and manually remove the core.

  3. Slice the Apples: Slice the apples into wedges or cubes, depending on your preference for pies and cider. If you plan to use the apples for pies, thin slices work best.

  4. Prevent Browning: Apples can brown quickly after being cut. To prevent this, place the sliced apples in a large bowl of water mixed with lemon juice (about 1 tablespoon of lemon juice per quart of water). This will help keep the apples looking fresh and appealing in the jars.

Optional: Pre-Cook the Apples

While you can can raw apples, some people prefer to pre-cook them slightly to enhance the flavor and make them easier to pack into jars. To do this, simply simmer the apple slices in a light syrup or apple juice for about 5-10 minutes. This process softens the apples slightly and infuses them with extra flavor.

Preparing the Syrup or Juice

The liquid you pack your apples in helps preserve them and adds to the overall taste. There are a few options for this:

  • Light Syrup: A mixture of sugar and water, this syrup provides a delicate sweetness that enhances the apples without overpowering their natural flavor.
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 ¼ cups sugar
  • Medium Syrup: A sweeter syrup for a more pronounced flavor.
    • 4 cups water
    • 2 ½ cups sugar
  • Apple Juice: If you prefer to keep the flavor of the apples pure, apple juice is an excellent choice.

Heat the syrup or juice in a large pot until it reaches a simmer. This will help the apples stay tender when they’re packed into the jars.

Canning the Apples

Now that your apples are prepared and your syrup or juice is ready, it’s time to start the canning process. This involves placing the apples into jars, sealing them, and processing them in a water bath.

Steps for Canning Apples:

  1. Sterilize Jars: Before filling the jars, sterilize them by placing them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove the jars carefully with the jar lifter and allow them to cool slightly.

  2. Pack the Apples: Fill each jar with the prepared apples, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top. Gently tap the jars on the counter to remove air bubbles. If needed, add more syrup or juice to cover the apples, ensuring they’re fully submerged.

  3. Wipe the Rims: Before sealing the jars, wipe the rims with a clean, damp cloth to ensure there’s no residue. This step is essential for creating a proper seal.

  4. Apply the Lids and Bands: Place the sterilized lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they’re fingertip-tight. Do not overtighten the bands, as this can prevent the jars from sealing properly.

  5. Process in a Water Bath: Place the filled jars into the canning pot, ensuring they’re fully submerged in water. Bring the water to a boil and process the jars for 20-25 minutes. The boiling water helps create the vacuum seal necessary for long-term storage.

  6. Cool and Check the Seal: After processing, carefully remove the jars from the water bath and allow them to cool on a clean towel or countertop. Once the jars have cooled completely, check the seals. The lids should be concave, and there should be no popping sound when pressed in the center.

Storing Canned Apples

Once your apples are safely canned and sealed, store them in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard. Properly sealed jars can last for up to one year, although they are best enjoyed within 6-9 months for optimal flavor and texture.

If a jar fails to seal properly, it’s essential to refrigerate and use the apples immediately.

Using Your Canned Apples

Now that your apples are preserved, it’s time to put them to good use! Here are a few ways to enjoy your canned apples:

  • Apple Pies: Simply open a jar of your canned apples, and they’re ready to be used in pies. You can use the liquid as a base for your pie filling or drain it before adding the apples to your crust.
  • Apple Cider: Use your canned apples to make homemade apple cider by heating them with spices like cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.
  • Apple Sauce: Blend your canned apples to make applesauce – a perfect treat for all ages.
  • Apple Jam or Jelly: If you prefer, you can even use your canned apples to make jams or jellies, adding sugar, pectin, and other fruits for additional flavor.

Conclusion

Canning apples is an excellent way to preserve the sweet and tart flavors of autumn for use throughout the year. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you’ll ensure that your apples remain flavorful and vibrant, ready for use in pies, cider, sauces, or any of your favorite apple dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or just getting started, this process will help you enjoy the taste of homemade apple treats whenever you desire. Happy canning!
