Traditional Nepalese Dishes: 6 Recipes for Culinary Adventurers


Nepalese cuisine is a unique blend of Indian, Tibetan, and Chinese flavors, with a focus on fresh vegetables, meats, and spices. For culinary adventurers, exploring the traditional dishes of Nepal can be a thrilling experience. Here are six recipes that showcase the diversity and richness of Nepalese cuisine.

1. Dal Bhat

Dal Bhat is a staple dish in Nepal, consisting of lentil soup (dal), steamed rice (bhat), and a variety of vegetables and pickles. To make Dal Bhat, start by cooking split red lentils (masoor dal) with onions, garlic, ginger, and spices. Serve the dal with steamed rice and a side of saag paneer (spinach and cheese curry), aloo tamatar (potato and tomato curry), and raita (yogurt and cucumber sauce).

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup split red lentils
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • Steamed rice
  • Saag paneer
  • Aloo tamatar
  • Raita

2. Momo

Momo is a popular Nepalese snack or appetizer, consisting of steamed or fried dumplings filled with meat or vegetables. To make Momo, start by mixing ground meat (beef, chicken, or buffalo) with onions, garlic, ginger, and spices. Then, wrap the mixture in a dough made from flour, water, and salt. Steam the momos for 15-20 minutes or fry them until golden brown.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground meat
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

3. Gorkhali Lamb

Gorkhali Lamb is a traditional Nepalese dish made with lamb or goat meat cooked in a spicy tomato-based sauce. To make Gorkhali Lamb, start by marinating the lamb in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, and spices. Then, cook the lamb in a pan with onions, garlic, ginger, and spices until it’s tender. Add the tomato sauce and simmer for 10-15 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lamb or goat meat
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • Vegetable oil, for cooking

4. Gundruk

Gundruk is a traditional Nepalese fermented green leafy vegetable dish made with mustard greens or other leafy greens. To make Gundruk, start by blanching the leafy greens in boiling water until they’re tender. Then, mix the greens with onions, garlic, ginger, and spices, and let it ferment for 2-3 days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mustard greens or other leafy greens
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/4 cup water

5. Sel Roti

Sel Roti is a traditional Nepalese sweet bread made with rice flour and sugar. To make Sel Roti, start by mixing rice flour, sugar, and water to form a dough. Then, shape the dough into small balls and fry them until golden brown. Dust the sel roti with powdered sugar and serve with a cup of tea or coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

6. Juju Dhau

Juju Dhau is a traditional Nepalese yogurt-based dessert made with sweetened yogurt, sugar, and spices. To make Juju Dhau, start by mixing plain yogurt with sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron threads. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld. Serve the Juju Dhau chilled, garnished with chopped nuts or dried fruits.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • Chopped nuts or dried fruits, for garnish

These six recipes showcase the diversity and richness of Nepalese cuisine, with its unique blend of Indian, Tibetan, and Chinese flavors. For culinary adventurers, exploring the traditional dishes of Nepal can be a thrilling experience. Bon appétit!
