Comforting and Rich: Beef and Red Wine Stew


There’s something truly comforting about a hearty beef stew simmering on the stove, filling the house with the rich aroma of savory goodness. This classic dish has stood the test of time for good reason – it’s delicious, satisfying, and perfect for warming you up on a chilly day. One of the best variations of this timeless favorite is the Beef and Red Wine Stew. This recipe combines tender beef, aromatic vegetables, and the deep, robust flavor of red wine to create a dish that’s both luxurious and comforting. Let’s dive into how to make this soul-warming stew that will surely become a staple in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of stewing beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup of red wine (such as Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: Pat the beef dry with paper towels to ensure it browns properly. Season the beef with salt and pepper, then lightly coat it with flour, shaking off any excess.

  2. Sear the Beef: In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot, and sear until deeply browned on all sides. Transfer the seared beef to a plate and set aside.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables until they begin to soften and the onion turns translucent, about 5 minutes.

  4. Deglaze with Red Wine: Pour the red wine into the pot, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes to reduce slightly.

  5. Simmer the Stew: Return the seared beef to the pot. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring the stew to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  6. Adjust the Seasoning: Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If the stew is too thin, you can thicken it by mixing 1 tablespoon of flour with 1 tablespoon of softened butter and whisking it into the stew. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes to allow the stew to thicken.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Discard the bay leaves and ladle the beef and red wine stew into bowls. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness. This stew is best enjoyed with a crusty baguette or over a bed of creamy mashed potatoes.

Conclusion

The Beef and Red Wine Stew is a true classic that never fails to impress. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, this stew is sure to hit the spot. The rich depth of flavor from the red wine, combined with the tender beef and aromatic vegetables, creates a dish that’s both elegant and deeply satisfying. So, next time you’re in the mood for a comforting and rich meal, give this recipe a try – it might just become a beloved staple in your home-cooking repertoire.
