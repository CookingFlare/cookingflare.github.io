How to Make a Middle Eastern Feast: Recipes for Every Course


Middle Eastern cuisine is a tantalizing mosaic of vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and time-honored traditions. This post will guide you through crafting an authentic Middle Eastern feast, offering recipes for every course: appetizers, main dishes, sides, and desserts. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or simply exploring new culinary horizons, this guide will infuse your table with the warmth and soul of Middle Eastern cooking.

Appetizer: Classic Hummus

No Middle Eastern feast is complete without a bowl of hummus to kick things off. Creamy, tangy, and utterly satisfying, this dish pairs beautifully with warm pita bread or fresh vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups cooked chickpeas (or one 15-oz can, drained)
  • ¼ cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (plus more for garnish)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste
  • Paprika and fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, cumin, and a pinch of salt until smooth. Add a splash of water if the texture is too thick.
  2. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  3. Transfer to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Garnish with paprika and parsley.
  4. Serve with pita bread, cucumber slices, or carrot sticks.

Appetizer: Falafel

Falafel is another staple of Middle Eastern cuisine. These golden, crispy chickpea fritters are perfect as an appetizer or part of a mezze spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • ½ onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1½ tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and pat them dry.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, coriander, cumin, and baking powder. Pulse until the mixture holds together but is not entirely smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. Form the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet to about 350°F (180°C). Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with tahini sauce or yogurt dip.

Main Dish: Chicken Shawarma

Shawarma is one of the Middle East’s most beloved street foods. This recipe brings tender, spiced chicken to your home kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • ¼ cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground paprika
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and spices.
  2. Add the chicken thighs, ensuring they’re fully coated. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) or warm an outdoor grill.
  4. Roast the chicken on a baking sheet or grill until fully cooked, about 25 minutes, turning halfway through.
  5. Slice into thin strips and serve in pita wraps with tahini sauce, shredded lettuce, and pickled vegetables.

Side Dish: Tabbouleh

Tabbouleh is a vibrant herb and bulgur salad that adds freshness to your feast.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ½ bunch fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely diced
  • ½ cucumber, diced
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, pour the boiling water over the bulgur and let it soak for 15 minutes until it softens. Drain any excess water.
  2. Add parsley, mint, tomatoes, cucumber, and green onions to the bulgur.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss well. Adjust seasoning as needed. Chill before serving for optimal flavor.

Dessert: Baklava

To end your Middle Eastern feast on a sweet note, baklava is the perfect choice. Layers of flaky phyllo pastry, a spiced nut filling, and honey syrup make this dessert irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (thawed according to package instructions)
  • 2 cups walnuts or pistachios, finely chopped
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¾ cup water
  • ½ cup honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Combine the chopped nuts and cinnamon in a bowl.
  3. Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish. Layer 10 sheets of phyllo in the dish, brushing each one with melted butter.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the nut mixture over the phyllo. Add 5 more buttered sheets on top, followed by another layer of nuts. Repeat until you’ve used up the nuts. Finish with 10 buttered sheets of phyllo on top.
  5. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  6. While the baklava bakes, combine sugar, water, honey, and vanilla in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Pour the syrup over the hot baklava and let it soak for several hours before serving.

Beverage Option: Mint Lemonade

Pair your meal with a refreshing drink. Mint lemonade, or “limonana,” is a cooling and aromatic complement to the bold flavors of the feast.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large lemons
  • ½ cup fresh mint leaves
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Squeeze the juice from the lemons and strain to remove seeds.
  2. In a blender, combine the lemon juice, mint leaves, sugar, and a small amount of water. Blend until smooth.
  3. Pour the mixture into a pitcher and add the remaining water. Stir well.
  4. Serve over ice for a refreshing finish to your feast.

Final Thoughts

Creating a Middle Eastern feast at home is not just preparing a meal—it’s a journey into a rich and flavorful culinary tradition. The balance of spices, textures, and aromas in these recipes delivers an unforgettable dining experience. By following these recipes for every course, you’re weaving the warmth and hospitality of the Middle East into every bite.

Embrace the art of Middle Eastern cooking, and gather your loved ones to share in this feast. The delicious flavors and vibrant dishes will undoubtedly leave everyone asking for seconds.
