Want to Dig into Dumplings? Master These 9 Chinese Dumpling Wrappers Recipes


Embarking on a culinary adventure into the realm of Chinese dumplings? It’s a journey of taste, texture, and tradition! Biting into a steaming, tender dumpling filled with succulent, unexpected flavors, surrounded by a multitude of mouthwatering wrappers, is like taking a deliciously flavorful journey across the vast and diverse landscape of China.

In this post, we will deeply explore and decode the enigmatic world of Chinese dumpling wrappers recipes. Get ready to dive into the secrets and nuances of crafting these delicate culinary treasures. Buckle up, as we’re about to reveal a treasure trove of unique and diverse dumpling wrappers recipes for your gastronomic pleasure!

  1. Homemade Egg Wrapper First up, we have the classic egg wrapper – a staple in countless Chinese dumpling dishes. With only two ingredients and an unbelievably simple method, this recipe will have you on your way to dumpling dominance.
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs

Directions:

  • Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl and whisk until well beaten.
  • Slowly sift in the flour while continuously whisking the eggs. Make sure the mixture is well combined without any lumps.
  • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with a small amount of oil and wipe it clean with a paper towel.
  • Pour about 1 tablespoon of the mixture into the pan, swirling the pan to make a thin round wrapper.
  • Cook for about 30 seconds or until the bottom begins to turn gold-brown, place a flat spatula under the wrapper, and flip it over.
  • Cook for another 20 to 30 seconds, then lift the wrapper with the spatula, allowing the excess liquid to drip back into the pan.
  • Transfer to a plate and repeat the process for the remaining batter.
  1. Potato Wrapper For a gluten-free dumpling journey, the potato wrapper reigns supreme. Adding a twist to the ordinary world of dumplings, this recipe provides an incredible taste and texture.
  • 1 large potato
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  • Boil the potato until cooked, slice it in half, and scoop out the flesh into a mixing bowl. Allow it to cool.
  • Mash the potato thoroughly, then add salt and flour. Mix until a rough dough forms.
  • Knead the dough on a floured surface until it becomes smooth and pliable.
  • Roll the dough into a long log, then cut into smaller portions. Roll each portion into a round wrapper, about 2mm thick.
  1. Wheat Wrapper Traditionally known as the ‘Scallion Pancake’ wrapper or ‘Liang pian wrapper,’ this wheat-based option offers a harmonious blend of flavors.
  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 150ml warm water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Pinch of salt
  • A handful of chopped scallions

Directions:

  • Combine the flour, water, and salt in a mixing bowl. Mix until smooth, then cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  • Add the vegetable oil and scallions to the dough. Knead until the oil is well-incorporated and the scallions are evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  • Rest the dough once more for another 30 minutes, then roll and stretch it to a similar thickness as your desired wrappers.
  • Cut portions from the dough and roll each portion into a thin, round wrapper.
  1. Colored Wrapper Feeling festival-inspired and wanting to make a statement? Let’s dive into the colorful world of dumpling wrappers! Incorporating food coloring, these eye-catching creations will impress and delight your senses.
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • Food coloring of your choice

Directions:

  • For each color, whisk the egg and flour together. Divide the mixture into separate bowls for each color.
  • Color each bowl with the respective food coloring and mix until the desired shade is achieved.
  • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with a small amount of oil, then follow the standard egg wrapper direction.
  1. Purple Sweet Potato Wrapper Swap the classic egg wrapper for a more exotic choice – the purple sweet potato wrapper. Not only are they visually stunning but they also provide a unique, sweet-tasting twist to your dumplings.
  • 150g purple sweet potato
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  • Steam or boil the sweet potato until tender and easily mashed. Allow it to cool briefly.
  • Mash the sweet potato thoroughly, then add flour and salt. Mix until a rough dough forms.
  • Follow the wheat wrapper directions for rolling and stretching the dough into thin wrappers.
  1. Fresh Spring Onion Wrapper This delicate, onion-infused wrapper will bring a delightful aromatic quality to your dumplings, elevating your culinary masterpiece to a whole new level.
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • A handful of finely chopped fresh spring onions
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons water

Directions:

  • Mix the flour and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Add the chopped spring onions.
  • Gradually add water, until the dough begins to come together.
  • Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth.
  • Roll and stretch each dough portion into thin wrappers.
  1. Sticky Rice Wrapper Dubbed as ‘Dumpings Dumplings,’ these sticky rice wrappers are traditionally used in Vietnamese cuisine. Nonetheless, they make for a fantastic gluten-free alternative to the wheat-based wrappers.
  • 200g sticky rice flour
  • 200g boiling water
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  • Combine the rice flour and boiling water in a bowl. Stir until smooth, then cover and let it sit for about 20 minutes.
  • Lightly oil your hands and the surface you’ll be working on. Divide the dough and roll each portion into a round wrapper.
  1. Chives and Bacon Wrapper Bacon brings such a phenomenal rich flavor to the dumplings, and chives complement the aromatic taste. This modification will create a dumpling that you’ll want to eat on repeat.
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 2-3 slices of bacon
  • A hand of finely chopped chives
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  • Cook the bacon until crispy, then chop into small pieces. Finely chop the chives.
  • Whisk the eggs and flour in a mixing bowl, then stir in the bacon and chives.
  • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with a small amount of oil, then follow the standard egg wrapper direction.
  1. Lemongrass and Kaffir Lime Leaf Wrapper For a Southeast Asian twist, this aromatic and colorful wrapper will bring an exotic taste and fragrance to your dumplings, reminiscent of a vibrant, bustling Asian street market.
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • A stalk of finely chopped lemongrass
  • A handful of finely chopped kaffir lime leaves
  • Pinch of salt
  • Saffron colorant

Directions:

  • Whisk eggs, flour, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, salt, and saffron in a mixing bowl.
  • Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat with a small amount of oil, then follow the standard egg wrapper direction.

Embarking on a culinary journey with these 9 Chinese dumpling wrappers will undeniably enrich your dumpling-making experience! The world of dumplings is vast and inviting, with countless ways to spin tradition and personalize your dumpling creations. So, step into the kitchen, unleash your creativity, and let your taste buds rejoice in the tantalizing adventure that awaits!
