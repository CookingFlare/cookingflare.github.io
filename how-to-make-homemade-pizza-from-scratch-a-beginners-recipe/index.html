How to Make Homemade Pizza from Scratch: A Beginner's Recipe


Making homemade pizza from scratch can be one of the most rewarding cooking experiences. Not only do you get to customize every aspect of your pizza, from the crust to the toppings, but you also enjoy the delicious aroma wafting through your kitchen. This guide will take you through each step of the process, allowing you to create a perfect homemade pizza that impresses family and friends.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Dough:

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water (about 110°F or 43°C)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Toppings (Customize to Your Liking):

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Pepperoni slices
  • Sliced bell peppers
  • Sliced mushrooms
  • Red onion rings
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Any other favorite toppings

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Activating the Yeast

  1. Combine the yeast and sugar: In a small bowl, mix the warm water with the sugar and sprinkle the yeast on top. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, or until it becomes foamy. This step is crucial as it ensures your yeast is active, which is what helps the dough rise.

  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the olive oil.

  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Once the yeast is foamy, pour it into the well in the flour mixture. Stir with a wooden spoon or your hands until a rough dough forms.

Kneading the Dough

  1. Knead the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes, adding flour as necessary, until it becomes smooth and elastic. You should be able to stretch it without it tearing.

  2. Let the dough rise: Place the kneaded dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

While your dough is rising, you can prepare the pizza sauce.

  1. Sauté the garlic: In a medium saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.

  2. Add the tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine.

  3. Season the sauce: Add oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken slightly and meld the flavors together.

Step 3: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to its highest setting, usually around 475°F (245°C) to 500°F (260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat.

Step 4: Shape the Dough

After the dough has risen:

  1. Punch down the dough: Gently punch down the dough to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a floured surface.

  2. Divide the dough: If you want to make more than one pizza, divide the dough into two equal parts.

  3. Shape the pizza: Roll out one portion of the dough with a rolling pin to your desired thickness. If you prefer a thicker crust, roll it out less. Transfer the dough to a floured pizza peel or a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Step 5: Assemble Your Pizza

  1. Spread the sauce: Spoon a generous amount of sauce onto the pizza dough and spread it evenly, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.

  2. Add cheese and toppings: Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce. Add your desired toppings, such as pepperoni, bell peppers, mushrooms, and onions. If you’re feeling adventurous, try out unique toppings like arugula, feta, or even pineapple.

Step 6: Bake the Pizza

  1. Transfer to the oven: If using a pizza stone, carefully slide the pizza onto the hot stone. If you’re using a baking sheet, simply place it in the oven.

  2. Bake: Bake the pizza for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.

  3. Check for doneness: Keep an eye on your pizza as it bakes. Every oven is different, so baking times may vary.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

  1. Remove from the oven: Once your pizza is done, carefully remove it from the oven using the pizza peel or a spatula.

  2. Let it cool: Allow the pizza to cool for a couple of minutes before slicing. This helps the cheese set and makes for cleaner cuts.

  3. Slice and enjoy: Use a pizza cutter to slice your pizza into wedges. Serve hot and enjoy with your favorite dipping sauces or a fresh salad on the side.

Tips for the Perfect Homemade Pizza

  • Use high-quality ingredients: The quality of your ingredients can make a significant difference in the overall taste of your pizza. Choose fresh vegetables, high-quality cheese, and good olive oil.
  • Experiment with flavors: Feel free to experiment with different sauces, cheeses, and toppings to discover your perfect combination. Try BBQ sauce instead of tomato sauce, or mix different types of cheese for a gourmet touch.
  • Make ahead: You can prepare the dough in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Just make sure to let it come to room temperature before shaping.
  • Try different cooking methods: If you don’t have a pizza stone, you can use a cast-iron skillet or even grill your pizza for a unique flavor.
  • Store leftovers properly: If you have leftover pizza, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Reheat in the oven for the best results.

Conclusion

Making homemade pizza from scratch is not only fun, but it also allows you to create a meal that is entirely tailored to your tastes. With this beginner-friendly recipe, you can easily impress your friends and family with your culinary skills. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a delicious homemade pizza that’s made with love. Whether it’s a classic Margherita or a loaded meat feast, the possibilities are endless. Happy cooking!
