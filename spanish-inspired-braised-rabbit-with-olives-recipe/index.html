Spanish Inspired Braised Rabbit with Olives Recipe


If you’re looking to add a touch of Spanish flair to your dinner table, this Spanish-inspired braised rabbit with olives recipe is just the thing. Rabbit meat is lean, tender, and has a delicate flavor that pairs beautifully with the rich, briny taste of olives. This dish is a classic example of Spanish cuisine, known for its bold flavors and simple preparation. Let’s dive into the recipe and bring a taste of Spain to your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole rabbit, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup green olives, pitted
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Prepping the Rabbit: Start by cutting the rabbit into pieces. If you’re purchasing a whole rabbit, you can ask your butcher to do this for you. Rinse the rabbit pieces under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Season the pieces with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.

  2. Searing the Rabbit: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat a few tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the rabbit pieces in batches and brown them on all sides. This step is crucial for developing flavor, so take your time with it. Once browned, remove the rabbit pieces and set them aside.

  3. Sauteing the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced red bell pepper. Cook until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Deglazing the Pot: Pour in the white wine and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes to reduce slightly.

  5. Braising the Rabbit: Return the rabbit pieces to the pot. Add the chicken broth, tomato paste, bay leaves, and dried oregano. Bring the liquid to a gentle simmer, then cover the pot and reduce the heat. Let the rabbit braise for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

  6. Adding the Olives: About 15 minutes before the rabbit is done, stir in the pitted green olives. The olives will infuse the dish with their briny flavor and add a wonderful contrast to the richness of the rabbit.

  7. Finishing Touches: Once the rabbit is tender, taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If the sauce is too thin, you can simmer it uncovered for a few more minutes to thicken.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the braised rabbit with olives in shallow bowls, spooning the flavorful sauce over the meat. This dish pairs beautifully with crusty bread or a side of creamy mashed potatoes.

Final Thoughts

This Spanish-inspired braised rabbit with olives recipe is a celebration of robust flavors and simple, rustic cooking. It’s a dish that showcases the essence of Spanish cuisine – using quality ingredients to create a memorable dining experience. The tender rabbit, enriched with the deep flavors of wine, olives, and aromatic spices, is a true delight for the senses.

So, whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply craving a taste of Spain, this recipe is sure to impress. Gather your ingredients, embrace the spirit of Spanish cooking, and savor every delicious bite of this braised rabbit with olives. Buena provecho!
