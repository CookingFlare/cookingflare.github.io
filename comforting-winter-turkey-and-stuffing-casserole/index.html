Comforting Winter Turkey and Stuffing Casserole


winter is here, and with it comes the craving for warm, comforting, and hearty dishes that can help us shake off the chill. one such dish that fits the bill perfectly is a classic winter turkey and stuffing casserole. this recipe is a masterclass in layering flavors and textures, with tender turkey, savory stuffing, and a crispy, golden-brown crust.

ingredients

  • 1 (4-6 pound) whole turkey breast, boned and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup homemade or store-bought stuffing
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans or walnuts (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

instructions

  1. preheat your oven to 375°f (190°c).
  2. in a large skillet, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. in a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked turkey, chicken broth, white wine (if using), and seasonings. stir until the turkey is well coated.
  4. add the cooked onion and garlic mixture to the turkey mixture and stir until combined.
  5. in a separate mixing bowl, combine the stuffing, chopped parsley, grated cheddar cheese, and chopped nuts (if using). stir until the stuffing is well combined.
  6. in a 9x13-inch baking dish, create a layer of the turkey mixture. top with a layer of the stuffing mixture, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  7. dot the top of the stuffing layer with the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter.
  8. cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  9. remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is heated through.
  10. remove the dish from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.

tips and variations

  • to make the casserole more substantial, add some sautéed mushrooms or diced bell peppers to the turkey mixture.
  • for an extra boost of flavor, add some chopped fresh sage or thyme to the stuffing mixture.
  • if you prefer a crisper top, broil the casserole for an additional 2-3 minutes after removing the foil. keep an eye on it to avoid burning.
  • to make the recipe more festive, add some diced cranberries or chopped fresh rosemary to the turkey mixture.

nutrition information

per serving (serves 6-8):

  • calories: 420
  • fat: 24g
  • saturated fat: 8g
  • cholesterol: 60mg
  • sodium: 450mg
  • carbohydrates: 20g
  • fiber: 2g
  • sugar: 2g
  • protein: 35g

conclusion

this winter turkey and stuffing casserole is a true comfort food classic. with its tender turkey, savory stuffing, and crispy crust, it’s sure to become a staple in your household. whether you’re serving it for a special occasion or just a cozy night in, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings and leave you feeling warm and fuzzy inside. so go ahead, give it a try, and let the comforting aromas of this casserole fill your home this winter.
