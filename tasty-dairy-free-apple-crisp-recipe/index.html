Tasty Dairy-Free Apple Crisp Recipe


When the autumn leaves start to fall and the air turns crisp, there’s nothing quite like the comforting aroma of a freshly baked apple crisp wafting through your home. This delightful dessert, filled with warm, tender apples and a crunchy topping, is not only delicious but also incredibly easy to make. And the best part? This recipe is completely dairy-free, making it a perfect treat for those with dietary restrictions or anyone looking to enjoy a lighter dessert. Let’s dive into this tasty dairy-free apple crisp recipe that will surely impress your family and friends!

Ingredients

To create your apple crisp, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 6 to 8 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Fuji)
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or arrowroot powder (to thicken)

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup almond flour (or any nut flour of your choice)
  • 1/2 cup almond butter (or your favorite nut or seed butter)
  • 1/3 cup coconut sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (such as walnuts or pecans, optional)
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil (or other dairy-free butter)

Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let’s get started with the preparation!

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This allows the apple crisp to cook evenly and develop a lovely golden brown color.

Step 2: Prepare the Apples

  1. Peel and Slice: Peel the apples and remove the core. Slice them into thin wedges or chunks, depending on your preference.
  2. Mix the Filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples with coconut sugar, lemon juice, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and cornstarch. Stir until the apples are well coated. The cornstarch will help thicken the juices as the apples cook.

Step 3: Make the Topping

  1. Combine Ingredients: In another bowl, mix together the rolled oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, salt, and ground cinnamon. Stir in the almond butter and melted coconut oil until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. If you like a bit of crunch, fold in the chopped nuts at this stage.

Step 4: Assemble the Crisp

  1. Layer the Filling: In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, spread the apple filling evenly across the bottom.
  2. Add the Topping: Sprinkle the oat topping over the apples, ensuring it’s evenly distributed. Don’t worry about covering every inch; some gaps allow the apples to peek through and create a rustic look.

Step 5: Bake

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown. You’ll know it’s ready when the kitchen is filled with a heavenly aroma, inviting everyone to gather around the table.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the apple crisp from the oven and let it cool for about 10-15 minutes. This cooling period allows the juices to thicken slightly, making for a more enjoyable serving experience. Serve your dairy-free apple crisp warm, and for an extra treat, consider pairing it with a scoop of dairy-free ice cream or a dollop of coconut whipped cream.

Tips for the Perfect Dairy-Free Apple Crisp

  • Choosing Apples: The key to a fantastic apple crisp lies in the apples you choose. Opt for a combination of sweet and tart apples for depth of flavor. Granny Smith apples add the perfect tartness, while Honeycrisp or Fuji apples bring sweetness.
  • Sweetness Levels: Adjust the sweetness of your filling based on your taste preferences and the natural sweetness of the apples. You can always start with less sugar and add more if needed.
  • Make it Your Own: Feel free to experiment with spices! Adding a pinch of ginger or allspice can add an exciting twist to the flavor profile.
  • Storage: Leftover apple crisp can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Nutritional Benefits

This dairy-free apple crisp not only satisfies your sweet tooth but also offers nutritional benefits. Apples are a great source of dietary fiber, vitamin C, and various antioxidants. The rolled oats contribute to heart health and provide a good source of energy, while almond flour is packed with healthy fats and protein. By using almond butter instead of traditional butter, you’re adding beneficial nutrients while keeping the recipe dairy-free.

Conclusion

There you have it—a simple yet delicious dairy-free apple crisp recipe that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a fall gathering, enjoying a family dinner, or simply craving something sweet, this dessert is sure to please. It’s warm, comforting, and packed with flavor, making it a delightful addition to your recipe repertoire. Don’t forget to share this recipe with friends and family, as they too will love the comforting taste of this dairy-free apple crisp.

Enjoy your baking, and may your kitchen be filled with the delightful scent of apples and spices!
