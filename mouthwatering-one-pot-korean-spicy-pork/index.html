Mouthwatering One Pot Korean Spicy Pork


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this mouthwatering one-pot Korean spicy pork recipe. This dish boasts the perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Not only is it incredibly delicious, but it’s also a convenient one-pot meal, making it perfect for a cozy night in or a gathering with friends and family.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to prepare this delectable dish:

  • 2 pounds of pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of water
  • 4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 teaspoons of toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Cooking Instructions

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s get cooking!

  1. Prepare the Pork: In a large bowl, combine the sliced pork, minced garlic, grated ginger, and soy sauce. Toss the ingredients together, ensuring that the pork is well-coated. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a large, deep skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become translucent and slightly caramelized. This should take about 5 minutes.

  3. Cook the Pork: Add the marinated pork to the skillet and cook until it’s no longer pink, stirring occasionally. This should take around 5-7 minutes.

  4. Add the Gochujang and Water: Once the pork is cooked through, add the gochujang to the skillet, stirring to coat the pork and onions evenly. Then, pour in the water and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  5. Simmer and Garnish: Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the pork simmer in the flavorful sauce for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Once the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened slightly, stir in the brown sugar, sesame oil, and green onions. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Transfer the Korean spicy pork to a serving dish and sprinkle it with toasted sesame seeds for a delightful nutty flavor. Serve the dish with steamed rice to soak up the delicious sauce.

Tips for Success

  • Thinly slicing the pork is crucial for ensuring quick and even cooking.
  • Gochujang can vary in spiciness, so adjust the quantity based on your heat preference.
  • If you don’t have gochujang on hand, you can substitute it with a combination of red chili paste and miso paste for a similar flavor profile.

Final Thoughts

This one-pot Korean spicy pork dish is a fantastic way to experience the bold and vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine right in your own kitchen. Whether you’re a spice enthusiast or simply enjoy exploring new culinary delights, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the sizzle of the skillet, and treat yourself to a truly satisfying dining experience with this irresistible Korean spicy pork creation.
