Quick Seafood Recipes Under 20 Minutes


When you’re in the mood for a delicious seafood meal but are pressed for time, quick seafood recipes can come to the rescue. Seafood is not only flavorful but also packed with nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids, making it an excellent choice for healthy meals. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or preparing a dinner for friends and family, there are numerous ways to prepare seafood in less than 20 minutes.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of quick seafood recipes that you can whip up in under 20 minutes. These dishes are perfect for busy weekdays when you need something satisfying yet fast. From shrimp to salmon, we’ll share simple yet tasty recipes that will leave you craving more.

1. Garlic Butter Shrimp

Garlic butter shrimp is a classic and one of the easiest seafood recipes you can make. This dish features succulent shrimp cooked in a rich and aromatic garlic butter sauce that takes only minutes to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and melt the butter.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they turn pink and opaque.
  4. Season with paprika, salt, and pepper. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the shrimp.
  5. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.

Tips:

  • Pair garlic butter shrimp with pasta, rice, or a fresh salad for a complete meal.
  • If you prefer a bit of heat, add red pepper flakes to the garlic butter sauce.

2. Lemon Grilled Salmon

Grilled salmon is one of the healthiest and quickest ways to prepare fish. In just 15 minutes, you can enjoy a perfectly cooked piece of salmon with a zesty lemon flavor that is sure to delight your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Drizzle olive oil over the salmon fillets and season with salt, pepper, thyme, and lemon zest.
  3. Place the salmon on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets, until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  4. Drizzle fresh lemon juice over the salmon before serving.

Tips:

  • Serve this lemon grilled salmon with steamed vegetables or quinoa for a light and healthy meal.
  • For a smoky flavor, add a little bit of smoked paprika or grill the salmon with lemon slices on top.

3. Quick Shrimp Tacos

Tacos are always a crowd-pleaser, and shrimp tacos are no exception. With fresh shrimp and zesty toppings, this quick recipe is the perfect way to satisfy your taco cravings in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa or crema (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, until pink and opaque.
  3. Warm the tortillas in a separate pan or microwave.
  4. Assemble the tacos by placing the shrimp on each tortilla, followed by shredded cabbage, avocado slices, and fresh cilantro.
  5. Squeeze lime juice over the tacos and add salsa or crema if desired.

Tips:

  • For an extra kick, drizzle your tacos with a spicy hot sauce or sprinkle some jalapeños.
  • If you’re looking for a low-carb option, you can replace the tortillas with lettuce wraps.

4. Spicy Tuna Poke Bowl

Poke bowls are a trendy and healthy dish that you can prepare in a flash. This spicy tuna poke bowl combines fresh tuna, rice, and spicy sauce for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb fresh tuna, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1/4 cup cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup edamame beans (cooked)
  • 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, sesame oil, sriracha, and rice vinegar.
  2. Add the tuna cubes and toss gently to coat them in the marinade. Let them sit for a few minutes to absorb the flavors.
  3. In a bowl, arrange a portion of cooked rice as the base.
  4. Top the rice with marinated tuna, cucumber slices, edamame beans, avocado, sesame seeds, and chopped green onions.
  5. Serve immediately with additional sriracha sauce if desired.

Tips:

  • For a variation, you can add seaweed salad, radishes, or pickled ginger to your poke bowl.
  • If you don’t have fresh tuna, you can use cooked shrimp or salmon as an alternative.

5. Crispy Fish Tenders

If you’re craving something crispy and satisfying, these fish tenders are perfect for a quick seafood snack or dinner. They’re easy to make and pair well with your favorite dipping sauces.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb white fish fillets (such as cod or haddock), cut into strips
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine breadcrumbs, Parmesan, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Dip each fish strip into the beaten egg, then coat with the breadcrumb mixture.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Fry the fish tenders for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with a side of tartar sauce or lemon wedges.

Tips:

  • You can bake the fish tenders in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for a healthier option, flipping them halfway through the cooking time.
  • Serve with a side of coleslaw or roasted potatoes for a complete meal.

6. Creamy Shrimp Pasta

Creamy shrimp pasta is a comforting dish that can be prepared in no time. The rich, creamy sauce paired with tender shrimp and pasta creates a satisfying meal that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz pasta (linguine or spaghetti works best)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté the garlic for 1 minute.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until they turn pink.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and bring it to a simmer. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss the cooked pasta into the creamy shrimp sauce and stir to combine.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

Tips:

  • For a lighter version, you can substitute the heavy cream with half-and-half or milk.
  • Add a squeeze of lemon juice for a bright, zesty finish.

7. Mussels in White Wine Sauce

Mussels in white wine sauce is a luxurious dish that’s surprisingly quick to prepare. The white wine and garlic sauce infuse the mussels with flavor, making for a delicious and elegant meal in under 20 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs fresh mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the white wine and bring it to a boil. Let it cook for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  3. Add the mussels to the pot, cover with a lid, and steam for about 5-7 minutes, or until the mussels open.
  4. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

Tips:

  • Make sure to discard any mussels that remain closed after cooking.
  • You can add a touch of lemon zest for an extra layer of flavor.

Conclusion

Seafood doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. With these quick seafood recipes, you can enjoy delicious and nutritious meals in less than 20 minutes. Whether you’re craving shrimp, salmon, or mussels, these recipes offer a variety of flavors and options to suit your taste.

So the next time you’re looking for a fast and flavorful dinner, turn to these quick seafood dishes. You’ll have a satisfying meal on the table in no time, and your taste buds will thank you!
