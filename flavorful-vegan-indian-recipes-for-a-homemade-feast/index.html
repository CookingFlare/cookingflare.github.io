Flavorful Vegan Indian Recipes for a Homemade Feast


Indian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, diverse ingredients, and vibrant colors. The beauty of Indian cooking lies in its ability to cater to a variety of dietary preferences, including veganism. For those looking to create a homemade feast that is both delightful and plant-based, this blog post will explore a selection of flavorful vegan Indian recipes. From aromatic curries to hearty rice dishes and delectable snacks, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and tantalize their taste buds.

1. Chana Masala

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chickpeas (cooked or canned)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cooking for another minute.
  4. Add the pureed tomatoes along with turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas and a cup of water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Stir in the garam masala and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or flatbreads.

2. Aloo Gobi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cauliflower florets
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan and add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  2. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes along with turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Add diced potatoes and cauliflower florets, mixing well to coat them in the spices.
  5. Pour in a little water, cover, and cook on low heat until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Once cooked, sprinkle garam masala and stir to combine.
  7. Garnish with cilantro and serve with roti or paratha.

3. Palak Tofu

Ingredients

  • 2 cups spinach leaves, blanched and pureed
  • 200 grams firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions

  1. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add the chopped onion and sauté until golden.
  2. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cooking for a minute.
  3. Add the spinach puree along with turmeric and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes to enhance the flavors.
  4. Gently fold in the cubed tofu and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle garam masala before serving. This dish pairs wonderfully with rice or naan.

4. Vegetable Biryani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh mint and cilantro, for garnish
  • 3 tablespoons oil

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute before adding chopped tomatoes, turmeric, and salt.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5 minutes, then add soaked basmati rice.
  4. Pour in vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  5. Fluff the biryani and garnish with fresh mint and cilantro before serving.

5. Vegan Samosas

Ingredients

  • For the dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon ajwain seeds
    • A pinch of salt
    • Water, as needed

  • For the filling:

    • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1/2 cup green peas
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    • Salt, to taste
    • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, ajwain seeds, and salt. Add oil and mix until crumbly. Gradually add water to form a smooth dough. Cover and set aside.
  2. For the filling, heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, stir in the boiled potatoes and green peas.
  3. Add garam masala, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Allow to cool.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin circle. Cut it in half to form two semi-circles.
  5. Take one semi-circle and form a cone by folding and sealing the edges. Fill with a spoonful of the potato mixture and seal the top edge.
  6. Heat oil for frying in a deep pan. Fry the samosas until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels before serving.

6. Coconut Chutney

Ingredients

  • 1 cup grated fresh coconut
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tablespoon ginger
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, and salt. Blend until smooth, adding a little water if needed.
  2. In a small pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, pour this tempering over the chutney.
  3. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with dosas, idlis, or as a dip for snacks.

7. Vegan Kheer (Rice Pudding)

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/2 cup jaggery or coconut sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A handful of chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon raisins

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pot, bring almond milk to a boil. Add the soaked rice and cook on medium heat until the rice is soft and the mixture thickens (about 20-25 minutes).
  3. Stir in jaggery, cardamom powder, saffron, chopped nuts, and raisins. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Serve warm or chilled in bowls, garnished with additional nuts.

Conclusion

Creating a flavorful vegan feast inspired by Indian cuisine is not only rewarding but also a way to explore a world of spices and ingredients that can elevate your meals. Each of these recipes offers a unique taste experience, showcasing the diversity and richness of plant-based Indian cooking. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply wanting to enjoy a delicious homemade meal, these vegan dishes will surely impress and satisfy everyone at the table.

By preparing these delightful recipes, you will not only enjoy the process of cooking but also indulge in the aromatic flavors that Indian cuisine has to offer. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that celebrates the vibrant and wholesome world of vegan Indian food. Happy cooking!
