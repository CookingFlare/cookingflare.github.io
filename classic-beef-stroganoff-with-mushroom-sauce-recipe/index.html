Classic Beef Stroganoff with Mushroom Sauce Recipe


Beef Stroganoff is a beloved classic in the world of comfort food, known for its tender beef, rich and creamy sauce, and savory flavors. While many modern variations of the dish exist, there’s something truly special about the classic Beef Stroganoff recipe. This dish, originally created in Russia, has evolved over the years and become a favorite in kitchens worldwide. Whether you’re new to cooking or an experienced home chef, this recipe will guide you through creating an authentic Beef Stroganoff with mushroom sauce that’s sure to impress.

In this post, we will delve into the history of Beef Stroganoff, break down the ingredients, and provide a step-by-step guide on how to prepare this timeless dish. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner or a special occasion, the comforting flavors of this meal will undoubtedly become a staple in your recipe collection.

The History of Beef Stroganoff

Beef Stroganoff is believed to have originated in Russia during the 19th century, named after the noble Stroganov family. While there are several theories about its exact creation, one popular story is that it was first made by a French chef working for the Stroganov family, who prepared it for Count Pavel Stroganov, a prominent figure of the time. The dish gained international fame in the 20th century, especially after it was introduced in the United States and Europe, where it was adapted with various regional twists.

Though the dish has undergone numerous transformations, it has retained its signature elements: tender strips of beef, a creamy sauce, and mushrooms. These ingredients come together to create a comforting and luxurious dish that’s perfect for both weeknight dinners and festive occasions.

Ingredients You Will Need

The classic Beef Stroganoff recipe relies on a few simple yet essential ingredients. Each component plays a crucial role in developing the dish’s signature flavors. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Beef Stroganoff:

  • Beef: The best cuts for Beef Stroganoff are tender cuts like beef tenderloin, sirloin, or ribeye. These cuts cook quickly and remain tender in the sauce.
  • Mushrooms: Fresh white button mushrooms or cremini mushrooms are commonly used in the recipe. These mushrooms add earthiness and depth to the dish.
  • Onion: A small yellow or white onion is typically used. The onion is sautéed until soft, adding sweetness and richness to the sauce.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds an aromatic layer of flavor that perfectly complements the beef and mushrooms.
  • Beef Broth: A rich beef broth is essential for creating the base of the sauce. If you prefer a more intense beef flavor, you can use demi-glace or a beef bouillon cube dissolved in water.
  • Sour Cream: Sour cream is what gives Beef Stroganoff its signature creamy texture and tangy flavor. It balances the richness of the beef and the sauce.
  • Dijon Mustard: A small amount of Dijon mustard adds a touch of acidity and spice, brightening up the overall flavor profile of the dish.
  • Flour: A little flour is used to help thicken the sauce and give it a velvety smooth consistency.
  • Butter and Olive Oil: These fats are used to sear the beef and sauté the vegetables, giving the dish a rich base.

For Serving:

  • Egg Noodles: Traditionally, Beef Stroganoff is served with egg noodles, but you can also serve it over mashed potatoes, rice, or even crusty bread.
  • Fresh Parsley: Chopped fresh parsley is a common garnish that adds a pop of color and freshness to the final dish.

How to Make Classic Beef Stroganoff with Mushroom Sauce

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Before you begin cooking, gather all your ingredients and have them ready to go. This will make the cooking process smoother and faster. Slice the beef into thin strips against the grain. If you’re using larger mushrooms, slice them into thick pieces. Mince the garlic and chop the onion finely.

Step 2: Sear the Beef

To start, heat a large skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of butter and a drizzle of olive oil. Once the butter has melted and the oil is hot, add the beef strips in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Sear the beef for about 2-3 minutes per side, until it develops a nice brown crust. Remove the beef from the pan and set it aside on a plate.

This step is crucial for developing the deep flavors of the dish. The caramelization that occurs when searing the beef adds richness to the overall flavor of the stroganoff.

Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pan, add a little more butter if needed and lower the heat to medium. Add the onions and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until they soften and become translucent. Add the garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds, allowing it to become fragrant.

Next, add the sliced mushrooms to the pan. Mushrooms tend to release moisture when they cook, so allow them to cook for about 5-7 minutes until they become golden brown and tender. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.

Step 4: Make the Sauce

Once the mushrooms are cooked, sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to combine. This will help thicken the sauce. Continue cooking for another 1-2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste.

Now, pour in the beef broth, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits. These bits contain a lot of flavor, so be sure to incorporate them into the sauce. Stir in the Dijon mustard and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

Step 5: Add the Sour Cream

Once the sauce has thickened, reduce the heat to low and stir in the sour cream. The sour cream will give the sauce its signature creamy texture and tang. Stir until the sauce is smooth and well combined. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 6: Return the Beef to the Pan

Add the seared beef back into the pan with the sauce. Stir to combine, ensuring the beef is coated in the creamy mushroom sauce. Allow the beef to warm through, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the beef, as it can become tough.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

While the beef is warming through, cook your egg noodles according to the package instructions. Once the noodles are done, drain them and divide them among plates. Spoon the beef Stroganoff over the noodles, making sure to drizzle plenty of the creamy mushroom sauce on top.

Finish by garnishing with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and a hint of freshness. Serve immediately, and enjoy this classic Beef Stroganoff with mushroom sauce!

Tips for Making the Perfect Beef Stroganoff

  1. Choose the Right Cut of Beef: The key to a tender Beef Stroganoff is using the right cut of meat. Tender cuts like tenderloin, sirloin, or ribeye are ideal because they cook quickly and stay tender in the sauce. Avoid using tougher cuts of beef, as they can become chewy.

  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: When searing the beef, make sure not to overcrowd the pan. This allows the beef to sear properly, creating a nice crust and preventing it from steaming.

  3. Use Fresh Mushrooms: Fresh mushrooms add the best flavor to the sauce. While you can use dried mushrooms, they require rehydration and may not have the same texture or flavor as fresh mushrooms.

  4. Adjust the Sauce Consistency: If the sauce is too thick, you can add a bit more beef broth or water to reach your desired consistency. If it’s too thin, simmer it for a few more minutes until it thickens.

  5. Garnish for Extra Flavor: While parsley is the traditional garnish, you can also add a sprinkle of grated Parmesan or a dash of paprika for added flavor and visual appeal.

Variations of Beef Stroganoff

While the classic version of Beef Stroganoff is a favorite, there are many variations you can try based on your preferences and dietary needs:

  • Vegetarian Stroganoff: For a meatless version, swap the beef for plant-based proteins like tempeh, tofu, or even lentils. Use vegetable broth instead of beef broth and make the same creamy mushroom sauce.

  • Chicken Stroganoff: You can substitute chicken breast or thighs for the beef for a lighter version of this dish. Chicken breasts will work best if sliced thinly.

  • Pasta Substitutes: While egg noodles are traditional, you can serve Beef Stroganoff over other types of pasta, mashed potatoes, rice, or even a bed of steamed vegetables for a low-carb option.

Final Thoughts

Classic Beef Stroganoff with mushroom sauce is a rich, comforting, and delicious dish that’s sure to satisfy your cravings. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion, this recipe will undoubtedly be a crowd-pleaser. The combination of tender beef, earthy mushrooms, and a creamy sauce over a bed of noodles creates a meal that’s both luxurious and comforting at the same time.

With the tips and steps provided in this post, you’re well on your way to mastering this classic recipe. Don’t be afraid to get creative with your ingredients, and enjoy the process of making this timeless dish from scratch. Happy cooking!
