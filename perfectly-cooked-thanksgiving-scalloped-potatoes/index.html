Perfectly Cooked Thanksgiving Scalloped Potatoes


The holidays are a time for delicious comfort food and togetherness, and one dish that never fails to bring those warm, cozy vibes is scalloped potatoes. Layers of tender, creamy potatoes cooked to golden perfection can elevate any Thanksgiving menu to new heights. Whether you’re hosting a feast or bringing a dish to share, this recipe for perfectly cooked scalloped potatoes will quickly become a staple for Thanksgiving and beyond.

Why Scalloped Potatoes Deserve a Spot on Your Thanksgiving Table

Scalloped potatoes are more than just a side; they’re a celebration of rich, indulgent flavors. Each bite delivers tender slices of potatoes bathed in a velvety cream sauce, enhanced with layers of melted cheese and subtle seasonings. The dish pairs beautifully with turkey, roasted vegetables, or fall-inspired salads, making it a versatile addition to your Thanksgiving spread.

What makes scalloped potatoes stand out is their ability to transform simple, budget-friendly ingredients into a luxurious, crowd-pleasing dish. It’s made with pantry staples, yet it feels special enough for the holidays. Plus, the aroma alone—cheesy, creamy, comforting—will entice everyone to gather around the table, ready to dig in.

Ingredients for Perfect Scalloped Potatoes

Creating the ultimate scalloped potatoes starts with fresh, quality ingredients and a balanced approach to flavor. It’s important to use the right type of potato and pay attention to layering in order to achieve the quintessential creamy texture.

Ingredients You’ll Need:

  • 4 pounds of Russet or Yukon Gold potatoes (peeled and sliced thinly, about 1/8 inch thick)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 medium-sized yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese (or a blend with Swiss or Fontina for added depth)
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Perfect Scalloped Potatoes

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes
Peel and slice the potatoes thinly for even cooking. Using a mandoline slicer ensures uniform thickness, but a sharp knife works just as well. Place the sliced potatoes in a large bowl of cold water to prevent discoloration as you prepare the other ingredients.

Step 2: Make the Creamy Base
In a medium saucepan, heat the butter on medium heat until melted. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes, until softened and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds.

Slowly pour in the heavy cream and whole milk, stirring to combine, and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Lower the heat and stir in the shredded Gruyere and Parmesan cheeses, along with the thyme, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Continue stirring until the sauce is smooth and luscious. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 3: Assemble the Layers
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with a kitchen towel. Begin layering them in the prepared dish, overlapping the slices slightly for an attractive presentation. After the first layer, pour a portion of the cheese sauce evenly over the potatoes. Repeat this process until all the potatoes and sauce have been used, ending with a generous layer of sauce on top.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection
Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil and bake the scalloped potatoes in the preheated oven for 60 minutes. This locks in the moisture and ensures the potatoes cook all the way through.

After 60 minutes, remove the foil, sprinkle a bit more Parmesan or Gruyere cheese on top, and return to the oven. Bake uncovered for an additional 20-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly.

Step 5: Rest and Serve
Once baked, allow the scalloped potatoes to rest for 10 minutes before serving. This helps the layers set and ensures every slice is clean and picture-perfect. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and extra flavor.

Tips for Elevating Your Scalloped Potatoes

  1. Select the Best Potatoes: Yukon Golds are ideal for a creamier texture, while Russets lend themselves to a fluffy and tender finish. Avoid waxy potatoes like red or fingerling varieties, as they don’t absorb the creamy sauce as effectively.

  2. Don’t Skip the Cheese: Gruyere adds a nutty, savory note that makes scalloped potatoes irresistible, while Parmesan offers salty depth. If you prefer other cheeses, experiment with sharp Cheddar, Monterey Jack, or creamy Havarti for unique twists.

  3. Generous Seasoning: Potatoes can be bland without proper seasoning. Taste your sauce and adjust salt, pepper, and spices as needed to ensure a robust flavor profile.

  4. Use a Mandoline: Achieving uniform slices is key to even cooking. A mandoline slicer ensures perfectly thin potato slices while saving time.

  5. Let It Rest: Resting your scalloped potatoes post-baking allows the sauce to thicken slightly, ensuring cleaner servings and more cohesive flavors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Make Scalloped Potatoes Ahead of Time?
Absolutely! Prepare the dish up to the point of layering and assembling in the baking dish, then cover tightly and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to bake, add 10-15 minutes to the covered baking time to ensure the potatoes heat through completely.

Do Scalloped Potatoes Reheat Well?
Yes, they reheating beautifully! For leftovers, cover the dish with foil and warm in a 350°F oven until heated through. You can also microwave single portions for convenience.

Can I Freeze Scalloped Potatoes?
If you plan to freeze the dish, it’s best to do so before baking. Assemble the layers, then wrap well with plastic wrap and aluminum foil. When ready to bake, thaw overnight in the fridge and bake according to the original directions.

Perfect Pairings for Thanksgiving Scalloped Potatoes

Scalloped potatoes pair wonderfully with many Thanksgiving classics, offering a rich counterpoint to dry or lean dishes. Here are a few pairing ideas:

  • Maple-glazed carrots or candied yams
  • Garlic green beans or sautéed Brussels sprouts
  • Herb stuffing or cornbread casserole
