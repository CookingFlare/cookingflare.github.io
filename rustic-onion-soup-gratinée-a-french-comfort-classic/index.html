Rustic Onion Soup Gratinée: A French Comfort Classic


On a cold winter day, there’s nothing quite like a bowl of warm and comforting soup. And when it comes to classic comfort food, French cuisine never disappoints. In this blog post, we will be exploring the delicious and soul-warming Rustic Onion Soup Gratinée, a French classic that has been enjoyed for centuries. With its rich flavors and hearty texture, this soup is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, let’s dive right into the recipe and discover how to create this culinary masterpiece!

Ingredients

To make Rustic Onion Soup Gratinée, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Baguette, sliced
  • Gruyere cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown and caramelized, stirring occasionally. This process can take up to 30 minutes, so be patient.

  2. Once the onions are caramelized, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

  3. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and garlic, and stir well to combine. This will help thicken the soup.

  4. Slowly pour in the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pot to deglaze it and release all the flavorful bits.

  5. Add the beef broth and dried thyme to the pot. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. While the soup is simmering, preheat your broiler.

  7. Season the soup with salt and pepper, adjusting the amounts to suit your taste.

  8. Ladle the soup into individual oven-safe bowls or crocks, filling them about 3/4 full.

  9. Place a few slices of baguette on top of each bowl, covering the surface of the soup.

  10. Sprinkle a generous amount of grated Gruyere cheese over the baguette slices, making sure to cover them completely.

  11. Carefully transfer the bowls to a baking sheet and place them under the broiler. Keep a close eye on them, as the cheese can quickly turn golden brown and bubbly.

  12. Once the cheese is melted and bubbling, remove the bowls from the broiler and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Conclusion

And there you have it — a steaming bowl of Rustic Onion Soup Gratinée, ready to warm your body and soul. The combination of caramelized onions, rich broth, and gooey melted cheese creates a flavor profile that is both comforting and satisfying. Serve this soup as a starter or as a main course with a side salad or crusty bread. Either way, you can’t go wrong with this French classic.

So, why not give this recipe a try and bring a taste of France into your home? It’s sure to impress your family and friends, and become a go-to recipe for those chilly evenings. Bon appétit!
