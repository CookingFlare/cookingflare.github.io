10 Exquisite and Beautifully Plated Thai Dessert Recipes


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you know that it’s not just about the savory dishes. Thai desserts are equally incredible, with their unique flavors, vibrant colors, and exquisite presentation. In this blog post, we will explore 10 Thai dessert recipes that are not only delicious but also beautifully plated. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds and impress your guests with these stunning creations.

1. Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niao Mamuang)

Let’s start with a classic Thai dessert that needs no introduction – Mango Sticky Rice. This heavenly dessert combines the sweetness of ripe mangoes with the creamy texture of coconut-infused sticky rice. To elevate the presentation, serve the dish on a banana leaf and garnish with a sprinkle of roasted sesame seeds.

2. Water Chestnut Rubies in Coconut Milk (Tub Tim Grob)

Tub Tim Grob is a visually stunning dessert that resembles precious rubies floating in a sea of coconut milk. The red water chestnut rubies are made by coating diced water chestnuts in tapioca flour and coloring them with red food dye. Serve this dessert in a glass bowl with crushed ice and drizzle it with coconut milk for an impressive presentation.

3. Thai Tea Panna Cotta

For a fusion twist on a classic Italian dessert, try making Thai Tea Panna Cotta. This creamy and silky dessert is infused with the flavors of Thai tea, giving it a unique and aromatic taste. Serve the panna cotta in individual glasses, and top it with a sprinkle of crushed pistachios for added texture and visual appeal.

4. Coconut Ice Cream (I Tim Kati)

Cool down on a hot day with a scoop of homemade Coconut Ice Cream. This refreshing dessert is made with coconut milk, sugar, and a touch of pandan essence for added fragrance. To make it visually appealing, serve the ice cream in a coconut shell and garnish it with a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes.

5. Thai Pumpkin Custard (Sangkhaya Fak Thong)

Sangkhaya Fak Thong is a delightful Thai dessert that combines the smoothness of custard with the sweetness of pumpkin. The custard is traditionally steamed inside a hollowed-out pumpkin, which not only adds a beautiful touch but also infuses the custard with a subtle pumpkin flavor. Serve slices of this custard on a decorative plate for an elegant presentation.

6. Thai Coconut Pancakes (Khanom Krok)

Khanom Krok is a popular street food dessert in Thailand. These bite-sized coconut pancakes are crispy on the outside and soft and creamy on the inside. To make them visually appealing, use a special Khanom Krok pan to create perfectly round pancakes. Serve them on a banana leaf with a sprinkle of shredded coconut for an authentic touch.

7. Thai Tapioca Pudding (Saku Sai Sai)

Saku Sai Sai is a delightful tapioca pudding dessert that is both creamy and refreshing. This colorful dessert is made by cooking tapioca pearls in coconut milk and pandan juice, giving it a vibrant green color. Serve the pudding in a clear glass to showcase the layers of tapioca pearls and top it with a drizzle of coconut cream for a visually appealing finish.

8. Thai Banana Fritters (Gluay Tod)

Gluay Tod, or Thai Banana Fritters, are a popular street food dessert in Thailand. These crispy and sweet fritters are made by coating ripe bananas in a light batter and deep-frying them until golden brown. Serve the fritters on a platter with a sprinkle of powdered sugar and a side of coconut ice cream for a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

9. Thai Jelly (Wun Gati)

Wun Gati is a refreshing and visually appealing Thai dessert made with agar-agar jelly and coconut milk. The jelly is typically molded into intricate shapes and served in individual bowls with a drizzle of sweetened coconut milk. Experiment with different molds and colors to create stunning jelly creations that will impress your guests.

10. Thai Sweet Sticky Rice with Durian (Khao Niao Durian)

Last but not least, we have Khao Niao Durian, a dessert that is not for the faint of heart. This unique combination of sticky rice and the pungent fruit durian is a favorite among durian lovers. Serve the sticky rice in a banana leaf cone and top it with creamy durian flesh for a dessert that is as visually striking as it is flavorful.

There you have it – 10 exquisite and beautifully plated Thai dessert recipes that will take your culinary skills to new heights. Whether you’re a fan of sweet mangoes, coconut-infused treats, or unique flavor combinations, there’s something for everyone in this list. So, gather your ingredients, put on your chef’s hat, and get ready to create stunning desserts that will leave a lasting impression on your taste buds and your guests. Happy cooking!
