Savor the Harvest: Seafood Recipes for Seasonal Eaters


Embrace the bounty of the season by diving into these scrumptious seafood recipes created with locally-sourced ingredients. Delight your tastebuds by enjoying fresh flavors as the seasons change. Savoring the harvest has never been more rewarding, especially when you indulge in mouth-watering dishes like the ones below. These recipes bring to life the freshness of the ocean, ensuring that each bite is as refreshing as the first.

Seasonal Catch: Seafood Selections

Scoring the freshest seafood is crucial to ensuring the best flavors shine through, and here are some must-try seasonal picks:

  1. Spring: Soft-shell crab, hiramasa, and spot prawns
  2. Summer: Bluefish, scallops, and flounder
  3. Fall: Oysters, mackerel, and halibut
  4. Winter: Monkfish, black sea bass, and cod

Let’s dive into some seasonally-inspired seafood dishes that will make you crave more!

Springtime Soft-shell Crab Fritters

Indulge in the sweet flavors of spring with these crispy crab fritters - the perfect snack or side dish for your seasonal celebrations.

Ingredients - Serves 4

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 pound soft-shell crab, cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, combine flour, cornmeal, baking powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs.

  2. Take the crab pieces, and dip them into the egg mixture, coating each piece evenly. Subsequently, coat each piece in the seasoned flour mixture with panko breadcrumbs.

  3. In a large non-stick skillet, heat oil on medium-high until hot but not smoking. Once hot, add fritters - 3 or 4 at a time, ensuring not to overcrowd the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until crunchy and golden brown.

  4. Drain fritters on paper towels, then keep warm in the oven while you fry the remaining batches.

  5. Serve hot, with a side of spicy aioli for dipping.

Summer Bluefish Ceviche

Channel the heat of the summer sun with this vibrant, zesty ceviche that features the distinct, robust taste of bluefish.

Ingredients - Serves 4

  • 2 pounds sashimi-grade bluefish
  • 1 cup lime juice
  • 1/2 cup finely diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup finely diced jalapeño pepper
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped cilantro
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Corn or plantain chips, for serving

Instructions

  1. Cut the bluefish into small, bite-sized pieces, then place them in a large glass or stainless steel bowl. Pour the lime juice over the fish, ensuring that each piece is fully submerged, cover, and refrigerate for 30-40 minutes, until cooked.

  2. Add the diced onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and avocado to the bowl, gently stirring to combine. Season to taste with salt and pepper, then chill for another 10-15 minutes.

  3. Serve in small, decorative bowls with corn or plantain chips on the side.

Fall Oyster Po’ Boy Sandwiches

Sink your teeth into warmth with this intriguing take on a classic sandwich. The briny taste of fresh oysters meets the soothing flavors of autumn, perfect for chilly nights.

Ingredients - Makes 4 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 4 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup hot sauce
  • 1/2 cup Sriracha mayo
  • 8 New Orleans-style po’ boy rolls
  • 2 pounds oysters, shucked
  • Lettuce, tomato slices, and pickles

Instructions

  1. Place the flour on a large plate. In a shallow bowl, mix the beaten eggs with 1/4 cup hot sauce. Set up the panko breadcrumbs on another large plate, and place the remaining hot sauce in a small bowl.

2.Dip each oyster into the flour, then the egg mixture (allowing excess to drip off) and finally coat it in breadcrumbs. Submerge each in the hot sauce to coat, and place on a baking sheet. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to an hour, allowing the breading to set.

  1. In a separate bowl, mix together the Sriracha mayo and remaining hot sauce for a spicy spread.

  2. In a large, non-stick skillet, heat oil on medium-high until hot but not smoking. Cook the oysters for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and crisp.

  3. Toast the po’ boy rolls, then spread the spicy mayo on both sides. Fill each sandwich with the cooked oysters, lettuce, tomato slices, and pickles. Enjoy while warm and spicy!

Winter Monkfish Sliders

After those chilly winter days, satisfy your taste buds with these savory sliders featuring the hearty monkfish. Its meaty texture makes it a true winter warmer.

Ingredients - Makes 12 sliders

  • 1 pound monkfish fillet, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 12 small brioche buns
  • 1/4 cup garlic aioli
  • 1/4 cup Mascarpone cheese
  • Micro greens, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large, non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Season the monkfish with salt and pepper, and add to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side for even cooking.

  2. As the monkfish cooks, prepare the buns by spreading a layer of garlic aioli on the top and bottom halves. Top the bottom half with a dollop of Mascarpone cheese.

  3. Once cooked, place the monkfish on top of the cheese, and garnish with micro greens. Close the slider, and serve immediately.

Conclusion

Savoring the harvest truly brings the best out of seafood. From the tangy flavors of spring to the spicy hits of summer, the robust tastes of autumn, to the comforting warmth of winter, our seasonal recipes guarantee a culinary adventure worth taking. Happy cooking!
