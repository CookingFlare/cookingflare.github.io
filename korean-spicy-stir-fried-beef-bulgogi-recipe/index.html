Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Beef (Bulgogi) Recipe


Korean cuisine has captivated food lovers worldwide with its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and intricate layers of seasoning. Among Korea’s many culinary treasures, Bulgogi, or “fire meat,” stands out as an irresistible dish that pleases all palates. Traditionally made with tender slices of beef marinated in a mix of savory, sweet, and spicy ingredients, Bulgogi is a quintessential example of Korean BBQ that has earned global acclaim.

While classic Bulgogi is known for its sweetness, spicy variations of the dish take things up a notch by introducing fiery elements that add even more depth to its flavor profile. With this recipe for Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Beef, you’ll master the art of creating a perfect fusion of heat, sweetness, and umami—all from the comfort of your kitchen. Whether you’re new to Korean food or already a devoted fan, this dish is sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create authentic Korean Spicy Bulgogi at home, it’s essential to use fresh ingredients and traditional flavors. Below are the components you’ll need for this recipe:

For the Marinade:

  • 1 pound (450g) beef tenderloin or sirloin, thinly sliced (or pre-sliced beef for Bulgogi, available at Asian grocery stores)
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (brown sugar works best for a richer flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional, for an extra touch of sweetness)
  • 3 tablespoons rice wine (mirin) or Korean cooking wine
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced or grated
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 Korean pear or Asian pear, finely grated (can substitute with apple)
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black ground pepper

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 medium red bell pepper, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 scallion, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, for stir-frying
  • Optional garnish: fresh chopped scallions, sesame seeds

For Serving:

  • Steamed white rice or sticky rice
  • Lettuce leaves (to make wraps)
  • Banchan (Korean side dishes like kimchi or pickled radish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Beef

When making Bulgogi, the beef’s texture and thinness are key to achieving that iconic melt-in-your-mouth feel. Purchase thinly sliced beef labeled “bulgogi-cut” at Asian stores or slice your own. To make slicing easier, freeze your beef for 15–20 minutes before cutting. Use a sharp knife to slice the meat against the grain into thin strips, about 1/8-inch thick.

Place the sliced beef into a large bowl and set aside for marination.

2. Make the Marinade

Mix the marinade ingredients in a separate bowl to create your spicy base. Begin with the soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and honey, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Add the garlic, ginger, gochugaru, and gochujang, ensuring no clumps remain in your mixture. Grate the pear (or apple) and add it into the blend, followed by the rice wine, ground pepper, and toasted sesame seeds.

Pour the marinade over your beef slices, taking care to coat each piece thoroughly. A well-massaged marinade will infuse the meat with layers of flavor. Cover and refrigerate for at least one hour, though marinating overnight will yield the best results.

3. Prep the Vegetables

While the beef marinates, slice the onion, carrot, and bell pepper. The vibrant mix of vegetables adds texture and complements the robust flavors of the stir-fried beef. For convenience, prep your scallions ahead of time as well.

4. Cook the Bulgogi

Heat your wok or a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil to prevent sticking, then toss in the marinated beef. Stir and cook for 4–5 minutes until the beef begins to caramelize. Avoid crowding the pan, as this can lead to steaming instead of searing—the goal is to achieve a slight char for maximum flavor.

Once the beef is cooked, add the onions, carrot, bell peppers, and scallions to the skillet. Stir-fry everything together until the vegetables soften but still retain their crunch, about 3–4 minutes. Additional sesame oil can be drizzled for a fragrant finishing touch.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Transfer the spicy Bulgogi to a large serving plate and garnish with chopped scallions and sesame seeds for visual appeal. Pair the dish with steamed rice and share it family-style around the table. For a traditional Korean dining experience, wrap the beef in crisp lettuce leaves and add a pinch of ssamjang (Korean dipping sauce).

Pro Tips for Perfect Spicy Bulgogi

1. Use Premium Meat:

Choose tender cuts of beef, such as ribeye, sirloin, or tenderloin. High-quality meat ensures that your Bulgogi retains a buttery texture even after stir-frying.

2. Adjust the Spice Level:

Gochugaru and gochujang are the powerhouses behind the heat in this recipe. If you’re new to spicy food or prefer milder flavors, reduce the quantity of gochugaru and use sweeter, less spicy varieties of gochujang.

3. Marinate for Maximum Flavor:

Marinate the beef for at least 1 hour, but overnight is ideal. The longer the meat absorbs the marinade, the more flavorful and tender it becomes.

4. Pair It Right:

Balance the spicy Bulgogi with mild, refreshing side dishes like cucumber salad, pickled radish, or egg rolls. Adding crunchy banchan to your meal spreads the flavors in an enjoyable way.

5. Experiment with Toppings:

Feel free to customize your Bulgogi with additional toppings, such as a fried egg, crushed roasted peanuts, or even shredded cheese for a fusion twist.

Why This Dish Stands Out

Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Beef Bulgogi isn’t just a meal—it’s an experience that brings friends and family together around a table filled with laughter and delicious aromas. The interplay of fiery gochugaru, tangy soy sauce, and sweet pear creates a dynamic flavor profile that dances on your palate.

In addition to its vibrant taste, Bulgogi is remarkably versatile. It can be used as a filling for Korean-style tacos, added atop a bed of noodles, or even paired with crispy kimchi pancakes. Serving this dish at get-togethers guarantees an unforgettable dining moment that bridges cultures and sparks conversations.

Final Thoughts

Mastering this Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Beef Bulgogi recipe will level up your cooking repertoire and broaden your appreciation for authentic Asian flavors. The secret lies in the balance—each ingredient plays a pivotal role in creating the harmony of sweet, savory, and spicy notes.

So roll up your sleeves, fire up your skillet, and bring the authentic taste of Korea into your home. With this easy-to-follow guide, you’ll soon be sharing the joy of Bulgogi with friends and family, bite after flavorful bite!
