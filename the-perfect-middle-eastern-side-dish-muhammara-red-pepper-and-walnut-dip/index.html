The Perfect Middle Eastern Side Dish: Muhammara (Red Pepper and Walnut Dip)


Muhammara, a vibrant and flavorful Middle Eastern dip, is a true delight for the taste buds. Made with roasted red peppers, walnuts, and a blend of aromatic spices, this dip is guaranteed to elevate any dish it accompanies. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply looking to add some excitement to your meals, Muhammara is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

To make this delicious dip, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 3 large red bell peppers
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Olive oil, for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the red bell peppers on a baking sheet and roast them for about 20-25 minutes, or until the skin is charred and blistered.

  2. Remove the roasted peppers from the oven and transfer them to a heatproof bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the peppers steam for about 10 minutes. This will make it easier to remove the skin.

  3. Once the peppers have cooled slightly, peel off the charred skin. Cut off the stems and remove the seeds. Cut the peppers into smaller pieces for easier blending.

  4. In a food processor or blender, combine the roasted red peppers, walnuts, breadcrumbs, minced garlic, pomegranate molasses, lemon juice, cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and a pinch of salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  5. Taste the Muhammara and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more salt or spices to suit your preferences.

  6. Transfer the Muhammara to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil. You can garnish it with some crushed walnuts, a sprinkle of smoked paprika, or a few pomegranate seeds for an extra touch of elegance.

  7. Serve the Muhammara with warm pita bread, toasted flatbread, or fresh vegetable sticks. It also pairs well with grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or as a spread for sandwiches and wraps.

Storage and Tips

  • Muhammara can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Just make sure to give it a good stir before serving.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, you can strain the Muhammara through a sieve to remove any larger walnut pieces.
  • Feel free to customize the spice level by adjusting the amount of cayenne pepper or adding a pinch of chili flakes for an extra kick.
  • For a nut-free version, you can substitute the walnuts with roasted sunflower seeds or add extra breadcrumbs for texture.

Final Thoughts

Muhammara is a versatile and flavorful dip that brings the distinct flavors of the Middle East to your table. Its rich and smoky taste, combined with the crunch of walnuts, makes it a crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or looking to add some excitement to your daily meals, Muhammara is the perfect choice. So why not give this vibrant and delicious dip a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
