Easter Muffin Recipes: Sweet and Savory Ideas for Breakfast


Easter morning brings with it a special kind of joy—a fresh start, vibrant colors, and the scent of delicious treats wafting through the air. One of the best ways to celebrate this festive holiday is with a batch of freshly baked muffins that can satisfy both sweet and savory cravings. Whether you’re planning a family gathering, brunch with friends, or just a quiet morning delight, these Easter muffin recipes offer something for everyone. From fluffy, fruit-studded bites perfect with coffee to hearty, cheese-laden morsels that pair beautifully with eggs, this collection will inspire your holiday breakfast table.

In this post, you’ll find a range of muffin recipes balanced between sweet and savory, bursting with spring flavors like lemon, berries, herbs, and fresh veggies. Let’s dive in!

Sweet Easter Muffin Recipes

1. Lemon Blueberry Muffins with a Zesty Glaze

These muffins are bursting with juicy blueberries and the bright tang of fresh lemon zest. The tender crumb is complemented by a simple glaze that adds a sweet, glossy finish—perfect for Easter brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup plain yogurt or sour cream
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries (or frozen, thawed)
  • For the glaze:
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 2-3 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the yogurt, melted butter, eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
  4. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined. Don’t overmix.
  5. Carefully fold in the blueberries.
  6. Divide the batter evenly into the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-22 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool on a wire rack.
  9. Meanwhile, make the glaze by mixing powdered sugar and lemon juice until it reaches a drizzle-able consistency.
  10. Drizzle the glaze over the warm muffins and let set before serving.

Why this recipe works: The combination of yogurt or sour cream ensures moist, tender muffins while the lemon cut through the sweetness with lively brightness. Blueberries add bursts of natural sugar with natural antioxidant power.

2. Carrot Cake Muffins with Cream Cheese Swirl

Celebrate spring with these carrot cake muffins that combine shredded carrots, warm spices, and a swirl of creamy cheese—a delicious nod to the classic Easter cake, but perfectly portable and muffin-sized.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1 1/4 cups grated carrots (about 3 medium carrots)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt or buttermilk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the Cream Cheese Swirl:

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease or line a muffin tin.
  2. In a medium bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together sugars, eggs, oil, yogurt, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients to the wet mixture until just combined.
  5. Fold in grated carrots.
  6. For cream cheese swirl, beat together cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla until smooth.
  7. Fill muffin cups halfway with batter, add a dollop of cream cheese mixture, then top with more batter.
  8. Use a knife to gently swirl the cream cheese into the muffin batter.
  9. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  10. Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

This recipe is perfect for those who want the spice and moisture of carrot cake without the hassle of layers—the cream cheese swirl adds the lusciousness of frosting in a single bite.

3. Raspberry Almond Muffins

The combination of tart raspberries with the warm nuttiness of almonds creates a sophisticated flavor profile. The almond extract enhances the natural flavors and adds a delightful depth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp almond extract
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen raspberries
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) and line muffin pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine buttermilk, oil, eggs, and almond extract.
  4. Add wet ingredients to dry, stirring gently until just combined.
  5. Fold in raspberries and 1/4 cup sliced almonds.
  6. Divide batter into muffin cups and sprinkle remaining almonds on top.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes or until golden and toothpick clean.
  8. Let cool for 5 minutes before removing.

Savory Easter Muffin Recipes

1. Ham, Cheddar & Chive Breakfast Muffins

For a savory kick on Easter morning, these muffins combine salty ham, sharp cheddar, and fresh chives for a satisfying, protein-packed start.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked ham
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir in cheddar, ham, and chives.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk milk, oil, and egg.
  5. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  6. Spoon batter into greased muffin tin and bake for 18-20 minutes.
  7. Cool slightly and serve warm.

Tip: These muffins pair exceptionally well with scrambled eggs or a side of sautéed greens.

2. Spinach and Feta Muffins

Rich, flaky, and bursting with fresh flavors, spinach and feta muffins are a light yet filling option perfect for Easter breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chopped fresh spinach, lightly sautéed and drained
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Stir in spinach, feta, and sun-dried tomatoes.
  4. In a bowl, whisk yogurt, olive oil, and eggs.
  5. Fold wet ingredients into dry until combined.
  6. Pour into muffin cups and bake for 20-22 minutes.
  7. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

The creamy tang of Greek yogurt and feta combines perfectly with the savory veggies, exemplifying Mediterranean flair in a muffin.

3. Roasted Red Pepper and Goat Cheese Muffins

These muffins boast the sweet-nutty flavor of roasted red peppers paired with tangy goat cheese and fresh thyme, making them a sophisticated savory option for your Easter breakfast spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup diced roasted red peppers (drained)
  • 3/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Fold in red peppers, goat cheese, and thyme.
  4. In a medium bowl, beat milk, oil, and eggs.
  5. Gently combine wet and dry mixtures.
  6. Fill muffin cups and bake 18-22 minutes until golden.

Tips for Perfect Easter Muffins

  • Don’t overmix batter: Muffins require gentle mixing to maintain tenderness.
  • Use fresh ingredients: Especially for herbs, fruits, and zests to ensure vibrant flavors.
  • Room temperature eggs and dairy: These mix more evenly into batter, helping to create fluffier muffins.
  • Test doneness with a toothpick: Insert into the center. If it comes out clean or with few crumbs, muffins are done.
  • Customize toppings: Add streusel on sweet muffins or extra cheese on savory ones before baking.
  • Storage: Keep muffins in an airtight container. Sweet muffins last 2-3 days at room temperature; savory muffins are best eaten within 24 hours or refrigerated and warmed before serving.

Final Thoughts

Easter is a wonderful time to enjoy bright, fresh flavors that celebrate the renewal of spring. Muffins are versatile, easy to prepare, and fit beautifully into a holiday breakfast or brunch spread. These sweet and savory recipes make it easy to please a crowd with varying tastes. Whether you prefer the fruit-and-zest combo of lemon blueberry muffins or the comforting saltiness of ham and cheddar, these ideas bring something special to the table.

The key is to embrace seasonal produce and a little creativity, transforming the humble muffin into a festive sensation. So grab your mixing bowl, preheat your oven, and bake up a batch—or two—of these delicious Easter muffins. Your table (and your guests) will thank you.

Enjoy your Easter morning with warmth, flavor, and the simple joy of homemade muffins!
