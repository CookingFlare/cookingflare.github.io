Paleo Pizza Recipes: 5 Ways to Make a Grain-Free Pie


Are you following a Paleo diet but missing your favorite comfort food, pizza? Well, you’re in luck! In this blog post, we will explore five delicious and grain-free Paleo pizza recipes that will satisfy your cravings and keep you on track with your healthy lifestyle. So, let’s dive right in and discover these mouthwatering creations!

1. Cauliflower Crust Pizza

Cauliflower is a versatile vegetable that can be transformed into a delicious pizza crust. To make this Paleo-friendly crust, start by finely grating a head of cauliflower. Squeeze out any excess moisture using a clean kitchen towel. In a bowl, combine the grated cauliflower with almond flour, eggs, and your favorite herbs and spices. Mix well and press the mixture onto a lined baking sheet. Bake in a preheated oven at 400°F for about 20 minutes or until golden brown. Remove from the oven and add your favorite pizza toppings. Return to the oven for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Slice and enjoy!

2. Portobello Mushroom Pizza

For a unique twist on traditional pizza, try using portobello mushrooms as your base. Remove the stems and gills from the mushrooms and brush them with olive oil. Place them on a baking sheet and bake at 375°F for about 10 minutes to soften them slightly. Remove from the oven and top each mushroom cap with marinara sauce, shredded chicken, vegetables, and your favorite Paleo-friendly cheese. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Serve hot and savor the earthy flavors of this grain-free pizza alternative.

3. Zucchini Pizza Boats

Zucchini is another fantastic vegetable that can be transformed into a delicious pizza boat. Cut zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create a hollowed-out boat shape. Brush the zucchini boats with olive oil and place them on a baking sheet. Bake at 400°F for about 10 minutes to soften them slightly. Remove from the oven and fill each boat with marinara sauce, cooked ground meat, sliced olives, and your favorite pizza toppings. Sprinkle with Paleo-friendly cheese and return to the oven for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Serve these zucchini pizza boats as a fun and nutritious alternative to traditional pizza!

4. Sweet Potato Crust Pizza

If you’re craving a slightly sweeter pizza crust, try using sweet potatoes. Start by grating a peeled sweet potato and combine it with almond flour, eggs, and a pinch of salt. Mix well and press the mixture onto a lined baking sheet. Bake in a preheated oven at 400°F for about 20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown. Remove from the oven and add your favorite pizza toppings. Return to the oven for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. The combination of sweet and savory flavors in this Paleo pizza will leave your taste buds wanting more!

5. Eggplant Pizza Slices

Eggplant slices make for a delicious and hearty grain-free pizza crust. Start by slicing an eggplant into rounds, about ¼ inch thick. Brush each slice with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Grill the eggplant slices on a hot grill or grill pan for about 3-4 minutes on each side until they are tender and slightly charred. Remove from the grill and top each slice with marinara sauce, grilled chicken, sliced tomatoes, and your favorite Paleo-friendly cheese. Return to the grill for an additional 3-4 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. These eggplant pizza slices are a perfect low-carb alternative to traditional pizza!

Now that you have these five fantastic grain-free Paleo pizza recipes in your repertoire, you can enjoy a guilt-free indulgence anytime you want. Get creative with your toppings and experiment with different flavors to find your favorite combination. So, gather your ingredients, preheat that oven or grill, and let’s get cooking! It’s time to satisfy your pizza cravings the Paleo way.
