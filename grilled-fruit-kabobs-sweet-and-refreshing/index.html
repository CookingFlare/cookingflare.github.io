Grilled Fruit Kabobs: Sweet and Refreshing


Fruit kabobs are a delightful and nutritious treat, especially during warm summer days when you’re craving something sweet yet refreshing. While they are popularly enjoyed on barbecues, grilled fruit kabobs are an underappreciated and versatile culinary option. In this post, we will share our three favorite fruit kabob recipes that are sure to impress everyone at your next gathering.

Recipe #1: Classic Tropical Fruit Kabobs

Ingredients:

  • Pineapple
  • Mango
  • Papaya
  • Kiwi
  • Banana
  • Yakimeshi skewers (optional)

Instructions:

  1. First, make sure all your fruits are ripe and washed thoroughly.
  2. Slice the fruits into bite-sized pieces, removing any inedible skin or seeds.
  3. If you prefer, soak the fruit slices in water for 30 minutes to prevent them from sticking to the skewers.
  4. Thread the fruit slices onto skewers. A combination of colors and flavors ensures a visually appealing and tasty kabob.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium heat, around 350°F (180°C).
  6. Place the fruit kabobs on the grill, ensuring they don’t stick to the grate. Be sure to turn them often to avoid burning.
  7. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, until the fruit is tender but not mushy.
  8. Once done, remove the kabobs from the grill and let them cool slightly before serving.

Recipe #2: Savory Fruit Kabobs with Nutty Dip

Ingredients:

  • Apple
  • Banana
  • Pear
  • Bored Person Nut Butter (almond, peanut, cashew, your choice)
  • Vanilla yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the fruits as mentioned in Recipe #1.
  2. Make a batch of your favorite nut butter – homemade or store-bought.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the nut butter and vanilla yogurt, creating a creamy dip.
  4. Assemble the fruit kabobs and skewer them.
  5. Grill the kabobs for 3-5 minutes, as described in Recipe #1.
  6. Serve the fruit kabobs with the nutty dip on the side. Enjoy the mixture of sweet and savory flavors!

Recipe #3: Fruit Kabobs with Fiery Glaze

Ingredients:

  • Watermelon
  • Cantaloupe
  • Honeydew
  • Passion fruit
  • Chili pepper (or pepper flakes)
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Honey

Instructions:

  1. Slice the watermelon, cantaloupe, and honeydew into bite-sized pieces and remove any seeds.
  2. Add a small chopped chili pepper or a pinch of pepper flakes to the fruit mixture.
  3. Whisk together 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon of honey to create a unique glaze.
  4. Assemble and skewer the fruit.
  5. Grill the fruit kabobs for about 3-4 minutes, ensuring they don’t stick to the grill.
  6. While the kabobs are grilling, heat a small saucepan with the glaze on medium heat. Stir the glaze until the honey has dissolved and it thickens slightly.
  7. Carefully brush the glaze onto the kabobs as they come off the grill.
  8. Drizzle extra glaze over the kabobs as you serve, and watch the flavors intertwine.

Regardless of your choice of recipe, grilled fruit kabobs are an effortless and flavorful addition to any barbecue or outdoor gathering. They can be prepared in a matter of minutes, while offering endless possibilities for creativity in combining and topping flavors. So, embrace the fun and sizzling world of fruit kabobs, and let your grill be the key to unlocking their taste potential.
