Bring on the Heat: Homemade Scotch Bonnet Hot Sauce


If you’re a fan of spicy food, then you’re in for a treat with this homemade Scotch Bonnet hot sauce recipe. Known for its fiery heat and fruity flavor, Scotch Bonnet peppers are a staple in Caribbean cuisine and can add a punch of flavor to any dish. Making your own hot sauce allows you to customize the heat level and ingredients to suit your taste buds. So, let’s dive into the world of heat and spice with this delicious homemade Scotch Bonnet hot sauce recipe!

Ingredients

  • 10 Scotch Bonnet peppers
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 medium-sized onion
  • 1 cup of white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • Juice of 2 limes

Instructions

  1. Begin by wearing gloves to handle the Scotch Bonnet peppers. These peppers are incredibly hot, so it’s important to protect your hands from the spicy oils.
  2. Roughly chop the Scotch Bonnet peppers, garlic, and onion. The size of the chop will depend on how chunky or smooth you want your hot sauce to be.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the chopped peppers, garlic, onion, white vinegar, sugar, and salt. Stir well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.
  4. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, or until the peppers and onions have softened.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly.
  6. Using a blender or food processor, blend the mixture until smooth. Be careful when blending hot ingredients as they can splatter. Start at a low speed and gradually increase to achieve your desired consistency.
  7. Once blended, pour the hot sauce back into the saucepan and return it to low heat.
  8. Stir in the lime juice and let the sauce simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  9. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the hot sauce cool completely.
  10. Transfer the hot sauce to sterilized glass bottles or jars. Seal tightly and store in the refrigerator.

Tips and Variations

  • Adjust the heat level by adding or reducing the number of Scotch Bonnet peppers. Remember, the more peppers you use, the hotter the sauce will be.
  • If you prefer a milder hot sauce, remove the seeds and membranes from the Scotch Bonnet peppers before chopping them.
  • Experiment with different ingredients to customize the flavor of your hot sauce. You can add spices like cumin or paprika, or even fruits like mango or pineapple for a tropical twist.
  • Be sure to label and date your hot sauce bottles, as they can last for several months when stored properly in the refrigerator.

Final Thoughts

Homemade Scotch Bonnet hot sauce is not only a delicious addition to your meals, but it also allows you to control the heat and flavor of your sauce. Whether you’re a spice lover or just looking to add a kick to your dishes, this hot sauce recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. So, get ready to bring on the heat and enjoy the fiery goodness of homemade Scotch Bonnet hot sauce!
