Creamy Seafood Chowder: A Hearty and Comforting Soup


There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm, creamy bowl of seafood chowder. The rich flavors of the sea combined with the smooth, velvety texture of the chowder make it a perfect dish for a cozy night in or a gathering with loved ones. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making the perfect creamy seafood chowder, packed with fresh seafood and delicious flavors that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of mixed seafood (such as shrimp, scallops, and white fish)
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups of fish or seafood stock
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish
  • Crusty bread for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, cook the chopped bacon until it’s crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

  2. Add the diced onion and celery to the pot and sauté until they are soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for 2-3 minutes to get rid of the raw flour taste.

  4. Slowly pour in the fish or seafood stock, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Add the potatoes, bay leaves, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes.

  5. Once the potatoes are tender, stir in the milk and heavy cream. Add the mixed seafood and simmer gently until the seafood is cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the seafood, as it can become tough.

  6. Season the chowder with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme, adjusting the seasoning to your taste. Remove the bay leaves from the chowder.

  7. Ladle the creamy seafood chowder into bowls and garnish with the crispy bacon and chopped fresh parsley. Serve with crusty bread for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips for the Perfect Chowder

  • Use a mix of seafood for a variety of flavors and textures. Shrimp, scallops, and white fish work well together, but feel free to experiment with your favorite seafood.
  • Don’t skimp on the bacon! The smoky flavor it adds to the chowder is simply irresistible.
  • Fresh seafood stock will give your chowder a deep, rich flavor, but if you can’t find it, a good-quality fish stock will work just fine.
  • Be sure not to let the chowder boil after adding the seafood, as this can cause the seafood to become tough and rubbery.

In Conclusion

Creamy seafood chowder is a versatile and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed year-round. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, this hearty soup is sure to delight. With its creamy texture, flavorful seafood, and hearty ingredients, this chowder is a true crowd-pleaser. So, gather your ingredients, get cooking, and savor the warmth and comfort of this delightful seafood chowder.
