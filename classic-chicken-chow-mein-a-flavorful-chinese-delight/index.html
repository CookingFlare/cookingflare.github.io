Classic Chicken Chow Mein: A Flavorful Chinese Delight


If you are a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you are probably familiar with the delectable flavors of Chicken Chow Mein. This classic dish is a staple in Chinese restaurants around the world, and for good reason! The combination of tender chicken, crunchy vegetables, and flavorful sauce is simply irresistible. In this blog post, we will explore how to make the perfect Classic Chicken Chow Mein right in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Classic Chicken Chow Mein, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces of chow mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the chow mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the mushrooms, bell peppers, carrots, and bean sprouts. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for another minute, until fragrant.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the skillet and stir to coat evenly.

  6. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss to combine with the vegetables and sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until everything is heated through.

  7. Add the cooked chow mein noodles to the skillet and toss to coat with the chicken, vegetables, and sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the noodles are warmed through.

  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Serve the Classic Chicken Chow Mein hot, garnished with sliced green onions or sesame seeds if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version of this dish, simply omit the chicken and add more vegetables such as broccoli, snow peas, or bok choy.

  • Feel free to customize the sauce to your taste by adding more soy sauce or oyster sauce for a saltier flavor, or a touch of honey or brown sugar for a hint of sweetness.

  • If you prefer a crispier texture, you can pan-fry the noodles separately until golden and crispy before adding them to the skillet.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved to suit your needs.

Conclusion

Classic Chicken Chow Mein is a versatile and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed for lunch or dinner. With its vibrant colors, contrasting textures, and bold flavors, it is sure to impress even the most discerning taste buds. So why not bring a taste of China into your own kitchen and give this recipe a try? You won’t be disappointed!
