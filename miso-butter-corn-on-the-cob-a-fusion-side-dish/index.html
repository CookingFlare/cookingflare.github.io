Miso Butter Corn on the Cob: A Fusion Side Dish

5 min · 902 words

If you’re looking to add a unique twist to your summer cookouts, then this Miso Butter Corn on the Cob recipe is just what you need. Combining the rich umami flavor of miso with the creamy goodness of butter, this fusion side dish will elevate your corn on the cob game to a whole new level. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or simply craving a flavorful snack, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Chopped green onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Corn: Start by shucking the corn and removing the silk. Rinse the ears under cold water to remove any remaining silk.

  2. Make the Miso Butter: In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, white miso paste, honey, soy sauce, and sesame seeds. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  3. Coat the Corn: Using a butter knife, generously spread the miso butter mixture over each ear of corn, ensuring that the corn is evenly coated on all sides.

  4. Wrap in Foil: Individually wrap each ear of corn in aluminum foil, making sure to seal the edges tightly to trap the flavors inside.

  5. Grill the Corn: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the foil-wrapped corn on the grill and cook for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. The corn is ready when it’s tender and has developed a slight char.

  6. Serve and Garnish: Carefully unwrap the foil from each ear of corn and transfer them to a serving platter. Sprinkle freshly ground black pepper over the corn, garnish with chopped green onions, and serve immediately.

Tips and Variations:

  • If you don’t have a grill, you can also roast the foil-wrapped corn in the oven at 400°F for 20-25 minutes.
  • For a spicy kick, add a pinch of cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes to the miso butter mixture.
  • Experiment with different types of miso paste for unique flavor profiles, such as red miso or mixed miso.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe:

The marriage of savory miso and creamy butter creates a mouthwatering glaze that perfectly complements the natural sweetness of the corn. The addition of honey lends a subtle sweetness, while the soy sauce and sesame seeds provide depth and complexity to the flavor profile. The result is a harmonious blend of savory, sweet, and nutty notes that will tantalize your taste buds with every bite.

This Miso Butter Corn on the Cob is not only delicious but also visually stunning, making it a show-stopping side dish for any occasion. The vibrant yellow corn coated in a glistening miso butter glaze is a feast for the eyes, adding a touch of elegance to your table.

Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is easy to make and requires minimal prep time, allowing you to spend more time enjoying the company of your guests. Plus, it’s a great way to introduce your family and friends to the delightful flavors of Japanese cuisine in a familiar and comforting form.
