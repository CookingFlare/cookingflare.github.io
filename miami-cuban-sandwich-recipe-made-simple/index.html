Miami Cuban Sandwich Recipe Made Simple


When it comes to iconic sandwiches, the Miami Cuban Sandwich stands in a league of its own. Perfectly balanced with savory roasted pork, sweet ham, gooey Swiss cheese, tangy pickles, and a touch of mustard, all pressed together between slices of Cuban bread, this sandwich is a flavor-packed bite from the heart of Miami’s vibrant culinary scene. Crafting the perfect Cuban sandwich at home might seem daunting, but with the right ingredients and a few simple steps, you can recreate this masterpiece in your own kitchen.

What Makes a Cuban Sandwich Unique?

The Cuban sandwich, or “Cubano,” is rooted in Cuban-American cuisine and is a staple in Miami eateries. Its origins trace back to the working-class communities in Tampa and Miami, where Cuban immigrants brought their rich culinary traditions. While variations abound, the Miami version is celebrated for its simplicity and reliance on authentic, high-quality ingredients that work harmoniously together. The key to making this sandwich exceptional lies in balance—each component complements the others, creating an irresistible combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients for the Perfect Miami Cuban Sandwich

To ensure your homemade Cuban sandwich stays true to its origins, it’s important to use the right ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

Main Ingredients:

  • Cuban bread: Authentic Cuban bread is crucial for this sandwich. Soft and slightly crusty, it’s robust enough to hold up to pressing while maintaining its unique texture. If you can’t find Cuban bread, you can substitute with French bread or an Italian loaf.
  • Roast pork: Slow-roasted pork shoulder is the star of the Cuban sandwich. Marinated in citrus and spices, it’s tender and flavorful. If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought roasted pork, but homemade is always better!
  • Ham: Sweet butter ham or smoked ham adds a delicate flavor that plays beautifully off the pork.
  • Swiss cheese: Preferably thinly sliced, it contributes a creamy and slightly nutty taste.
  • Dill pickles: Crunchy and tangy, they cut through the richness of the meats and cheese.
  • Yellow mustard: Spicy, tangy mustard ties everything together and adds a bold kick.

Optional Ingredients:

Some versions of the sandwich include additional spices or spreads, but simplicity is key here. Stick to the basics to fully embrace the traditional Miami-style Cuban sandwich.

Extras:

  • Butter: To brush the bread for pressing.

Step-by-Step Recipe

1. Prepare the Roast Pork

One of the most defining elements of a great Cuban sandwich is the roast pork. While it may take extra preparation, the effort is always worth it. To make the pork:

Ingredients for Roast Pork:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder
  • Juice of 2 oranges
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the orange juice, lime juice, minced garlic, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well blended.
  2. Place the pork shoulder in a large container or zip-seal bag, pour the marinade over it, and ensure the pork is completely coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or, for deeper flavor, overnight.
  3. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Remove the pork from the marinade and pat it dry.
  4. Heat olive oil in a large skillet. Once hot, sear the pork on all sides until golden brown.
  5. Transfer the pork to a roasting pan, cover tightly with foil, and roast for approximately 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and you can shred it easily with a fork. Let it cool slightly, then shred into thin strips for sandwich assembly.

2. Slice and Prepare Cuban Bread

Cut the Cuban bread into sandwich-sized portions. Then, slice each portion lengthwise to create a top and bottom half. Be careful to avoid tearing the bread so the structure holds up during pressing.

3. Assemble the Cuban Sandwich

Start building your sandwich layer by layer:

  • Spread a thin layer of yellow mustard onto the bottom slice of bread.
  • Add a layer of roast pork, making sure it’s evenly distributed.
  • Top the pork with slices of ham, followed by Swiss cheese for that melt-in-your-mouth texture.
  • Finally, add dill pickle slices—be generous to ensure a satisfying crunch in every bite.
  • Place the top slice of bread on the stack to complete the sandwich.

4. Press the Sandwich

Traditional Cuban sandwiches are pressed using a plancha, a flat grill press. If you don’t have one, a panini press or a heavy skillet will work perfectly.

Instructions for Pressing:

  1. Preheat your press or skillet to medium heat. If you’re using a skillet, place a heavy object (such as a second skillet weighted with cans) on top.
  2. Brush the outside of the sandwich with butter to achieve a golden, crispy crust.
  3. Place the sandwich onto the press or skillet and cook until the bread is firmly pressed, golden, and crunchy, and the cheese is melted—about 3–5 minutes per side.
  4. Remember to press firmly but gently—you don’t want the contents spilling out.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Once your sandwich is pressed to perfection, remove it from the heat and allow it to cool slightly. Cut the sandwich in half diagonally for the classic Cuban presentation, and serve warm. Pair it with a side of crispy plantain chips or a refreshing mojito for a true Miami experience.

Tips for Success

  • Choose Quality Ingredients: For an authentic Cuban sandwich, quality is everything. Opt for freshly baked bread, high-quality meats, and premium cheese to maximize flavor.
  • Don’t Overload: Balance is key. While it’s tempting to add more meat or cheese, overstuffing the sandwich will make pressing difficult and could compromise the harmony of flavors.
  • Preheat Your Press: Ensure your press or skillet is properly preheated to get that satisfying crunch on the bread’s exterior without drying out the interior.
  • Experiment (But Stay Authentic): While it’s best to stay true to the classic recipe, you can experiment with adding extra seasonings or tweaking the roast pork marinade for a personal touch.

A Bite of Miami in Every Sandwich

There’s nothing quite like a homemade Miami Cuban sandwich to transport you straight to the bustling streets of Little Havana. Each bite tells a story of tradition, community, and bold flavors. Whether you’re serving up these sandwiches for a family lunch or bringing a touch of Miami to your next gathering, this recipe is sure to impress. From the tangy mustard to the melt-in-your-mouth pork, you’ll love how easy it is to recreate this culinary icon right at home.

So fire up your grill, gather your ingredients, and enjoy the process of bringing one of Miami’s most beloved dishes to life. With the right techniques and a little patience, you’ll be savoring your very own Cuban sandwich—fresh, hot, and packed with the flavors that make Miami cuisine unforgettable.
