Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole Recipe


Greek cuisine is known for its bold flavors and wholesome ingredients, and this Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole is no exception. Combining savory chicken, nutrient-packed spinach, aromatic herbs, and a creamy, cheesy topping, this dish is a tribute to the hearty and healthy cooking traditions of Greece. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or meal prepping for the week, this casserole is destined to become a favorite that blends simplicity, taste, and nourishment.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

The Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole offers an irresistible balance of flavors and textures. The tender chunks of chicken are perfectly seasoned, the spinach adds a vibrant note and a wealth of vitamins, while the cheesy topping infused with Mediterranean herbs ties everything together in one delicious bite. It’s a versatile dish that pairs beautifully with fresh salads, crusty bread, or roasted vegetables. Plus, it’s easy to prepare ahead of time, making it ideal for busy weekdays or special occasions.

Ingredients for Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole

To prepare this mouthwatering casserole, you’ll need a variety of Mediterranean-inspired ingredients that work harmoniously to create a comforting and satisfying dish. Here’s what you need:

For the Main Casserole:

  • 2 lbs (900 g) chicken breasts, diced into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (extra virgin preferred)
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1½ cups cooked quinoa or rice (optional, for added texture)
  • ½ cup Kalamata olives, sliced
  • ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Creamy Topping:

  • 1½ cups plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • ½ cup shredded mozzarella or Parmesan
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg (optional, for a warm undertone)

Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preheat and Prep

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a large casserole dish with olive oil or cooking spray to prevent sticking.

2. Cook the Chicken

Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Add the diced chicken and season lightly with salt and pepper. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown on the outside and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

3. Sauté the Aromatics

Using the same skillet, add the diced onion and garlic. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until softened and fragrant. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Stir in the sun-dried tomatoes and Kalamata olives, mixing well.

4. Combine the Layers

Place the cooked chicken in the prepared casserole dish, followed by an even layer of the sautéed spinach mixture. If you’re adding cooked quinoa or rice for texture, spread it evenly over the spinach layer.

5. Prepare the Creamy Topping

In a mixing bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, crumbled feta, shredded mozzarella or Parmesan, egg, dried oregano, thyme, and nutmeg. Stir until the mixture is smooth and well-combined.

6. Finish the Casserole

Pour the creamy topping evenly over the chicken mixture in the casserole dish. Use a spatula or spoon to spread it out for full coverage.

7. Bake to Perfection

Place the casserole dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25–30 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbling. If desired, you can broil for the final 2–3 minutes to achieve a slightly crisp topping.

8. Garnish and Serve

Remove the casserole from the oven and let it rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side. The citrusy brightness complements the savory Mediterranean flavors of the dish perfectly.

Serving Suggestions

This Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole can stand alone as a complete meal, but pairing it with other Mediterranean-inspired sides can take your dining experience to the next level. Consider serving it with:

  • A crisp Greek salad featuring cucumber, tomato, red onion, and more feta.
  • Warm pita bread or rustic whole-grain bread to soak up the creamy topping.
  • Roasted vegetables, such as zucchini, eggplant, or asparagus, seasoned simply with olive oil and herbs.

For wine enthusiasts, a dry white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio complements the dish beautifully, while a light red wine like Pinot Noir can also work well.

Make-Ahead Tips and Storage

Meal Prep-Friendly

This casserole is excellent for meal prep thanks to its layered construction and baked finish. You can assemble the entire dish up to 24 hours in advance and store it in the refrigerator until ready to bake.

Storing Leftovers

Once baked, allow the casserole to cool completely before storing. Cover it tightly or transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 4 days. Reheat portions in the oven or microwave for a quick and delicious meal.

Freezing Option

To freeze, assemble the casserole but skip the baking step. Wrap it tightly with plastic wrap and then aluminum foil, and store in the freezer for up to 3 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before baking.

Health Benefits of This Recipe

This Greek-inspired casserole is packed with ingredients that offer a variety of health benefits:

  • Chicken: A lean protein source that helps build and repair muscle tissues while keeping you satiated.
  • Spinach: Rich in iron, calcium, and vitamins A, C, and K, spinach supports bone health and boosts immunity.
  • Greek Yogurt: Loaded with probiotics and protein, this ingredient adds creaminess without excessive fat.
  • Feta Cheese: Lower in fat compared to other cheeses, feta brings bold flavor with fewer calories.
  • Olive Oil: A cornerstone of the Mediterranean diet, olive oil is high in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats.

This recipe is a perfect option for those looking to embrace a wholesome, balanced eating plan while savoring the vibrant flavors of Greek cuisine.

Final Thoughts

The Greek Chicken and Spinach Casserole embodies everything we love about Mediterranean cooking: simple ingredients, bold flavors, and nutritious comfort food. This recipe is a celebration of natural ingredients coming together to create a dish that’s both nourishing and indulgent. Perfectly seasoned chicken, hearty spinach, zesty herbs, and creamy feta yogurt topping make every bite unforgettable. Whether you’re serving it for a weeknight dinner or impressing guests at a weekend gathering, this casserole is a winner.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and treat yourself to this home-cooked Mediterranean masterpiece. It’s a dish that brings people together, and ultimately, that’s the heart of Greek cuisine. Enjoy!
