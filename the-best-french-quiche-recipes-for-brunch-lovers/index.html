The Best French Quiche Recipes for Brunch Lovers


If you’re a brunch lover like me, then you know that a good quiche can make all the difference. The combination of a buttery crust, creamy custard, and flavorful fillings creates a delightful dish that is perfect for any occasion. In this blog post, I’ll be sharing some of the best French quiche recipes that will take your brunch game to the next level. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Classic Quiche Lorraine

Let’s start with the classic – Quiche Lorraine. This timeless recipe originated in the Lorraine region of France and has become a staple in French cuisine. The key ingredients are bacon, cheese, and onions, which create a rich and savory flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove from the skillet and let it cool before crumbling it into small pieces.
  4. In the same skillet, sauté the chopped onions until they become translucent. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Spread the crumbled bacon, sautéed onions, and shredded Gruyere cheese evenly over the pie crust.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the filling, making sure it covers everything.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Spinach and Gruyere Quiche

For a lighter and vegetarian-friendly option, this Spinach and Gruyere Quiche is a must-try. The combination of earthy spinach and nutty Gruyere cheese creates a harmonious flavor that will delight your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 ½ cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the chopped onions until they become translucent. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Spread the sautéed spinach and onions evenly over the pie crust.
  6. Sprinkle the shredded Gruyere cheese on top of the spinach and onions.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the filling, ensuring it covers everything.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top.
  9. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Mushroom and Goat Cheese Quiche

For those who love the earthy taste of mushrooms and the tanginess of goat cheese, this Mushroom and Goat Cheese Quiche is a must-try. The combination of flavors in this recipe is truly divine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the sliced mushrooms until they release their moisture and become golden brown. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Spread the sautéed mushrooms evenly over the pie crust.
  6. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese on top of the mushrooms.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the filling, making sure it covers everything.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top.
  9. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Conclusion

Quiches are a versatile and delicious dish that can be enjoyed for brunch, lunch, or dinner. The recipes shared in this blog post are just a few examples of the endless possibilities when it comes to French quiches. So whether you prefer the classic Quiche Lorraine, a vegetarian Spinach and Gruyere Quiche, or the earthy Mushroom and Goat Cheese Quiche, there’s a recipe here for every brunch lover. So go ahead, try them out, and let your taste buds go on a French culinary adventure!
