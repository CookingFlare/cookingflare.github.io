Farm to Table Grilling: 5 Recipes for Your Next BBQ


There’s something truly special about grilling outdoors, especially when you can enjoy the fruits of the earth, fresh and vibrant, straight from the farm to your table. A farm-to-table BBQ combines the best of both worlds—sustainability and flavor—by utilizing seasonal, locally sourced ingredients for a memorable grilling experience. This approach to cooking not only supports local farmers but also encourages eating fresh, nutrient-rich foods that are bursting with flavor.

In this post, we’ll explore five farm-to-table grilling recipes that will take your BBQ to the next level. These dishes are perfect for a sunny afternoon with friends, a family get-together, or simply enjoying a relaxing evening in your backyard. So fire up the grill and let’s dive into some mouthwatering recipes that showcase the very best of what’s in season.

1. Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz button mushrooms, whole
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Veggies: Begin by cutting all the vegetables into uniform chunks or slices. This ensures that they cook evenly on the grill.

  2. Make the Marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, chopped oregano, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper until well combined. This simple herb marinade will bring out the natural flavors of the vegetables.

  3. Marinate the Vegetables: Place all the chopped veggies in a large bowl. Pour the marinade over the vegetables and toss them until evenly coated. Allow them to marinate for 20-30 minutes for maximum flavor absorption.

  4. Prepare the Skewers: Thread the marinated vegetables onto the soaked wooden skewers, alternating between different types for color and variety. You can mix and match your favorite seasonal veggies.

  5. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are charred and tender. Be sure to turn them regularly to prevent burning.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the vegetables are perfectly grilled, remove them from the skewers and arrange them on a platter. These veggie skewers are a great accompaniment to any grilled meats and are also perfect for vegetarians and vegans alike.

Pro Tip:

Add a drizzle of balsamic glaze or a sprinkle of goat cheese after grilling to elevate the flavor even further.

2. Farm-Fresh Corn on the Cob with Cilantro Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of fresh corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp lime zest
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grill the Corn: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the husked corn directly on the grill grates, turning every 3-4 minutes, until the kernels are golden brown and slightly charred, about 12-15 minutes.

  2. Make the Cilantro Lime Butter: While the corn is grilling, prepare the butter. In a small bowl, mix together softened butter, fresh cilantro, lime juice, lime zest, and chili powder (if using). Add salt to taste.

  3. Coat the Corn: Once the corn is fully grilled, remove it from the grill and immediately brush the cilantro lime butter over the hot corn. The heat from the corn will melt the butter, allowing it to soak into the kernels.

  4. Serve: Garnish with extra cilantro and lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor. This corn on the cob recipe is a refreshing and vibrant side dish to any BBQ.

Pro Tip:

For added smoky flavor, try grilling the corn in its husks or wrapping the ears in foil for a more steamed effect.

3. Herb-Marinated Chicken Thighs

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, rosemary, parsley, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until fully mixed.

  2. Prepare the Chicken: Place the chicken thighs in a shallow dish or zip-lock bag. Pour the marinade over the chicken, making sure to coat it evenly. Let the chicken marinate for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator, or up to 4 hours for more intense flavor.

  3. Grill the Chicken: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the chicken thighs on the grill, skin-side down. Grill for 7-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F and the skin is crispy and golden brown.

  4. Serve: Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Pair this flavorful herb-marinated chicken with your favorite grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.

Pro Tip:

For extra crispiness, finish grilling the chicken skin-side down for the last couple of minutes, ensuring the skin crisps up perfectly.

4. Grilled Peach Salad with Goat Cheese and Arugula

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 4 cups arugula, washed and dried
  • 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup pecans, toasted
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Olive oil for grilling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grill the Peaches: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Lightly brush the peach halves with olive oil and place them cut-side down on the grill. Grill for 3-4 minutes until grill marks appear and the peaches are softened.

  2. Prepare the Salad: While the peaches are grilling, assemble the salad by placing arugula in a large bowl. Add the crumbled goat cheese and toasted pecans.

  3. Dress the Salad: In a small bowl, whisk together honey and balsamic vinegar to create a simple, tangy dressing. Drizzle this over the arugula and toss gently to combine.

  4. Assemble the Salad: Once the peaches are grilled, slice them into wedges and arrange them on top of the salad. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Serve: This grilled peach salad is a light and refreshing side dish that pairs wonderfully with rich meats and grilled fish. The sweetness of the peaches complements the tanginess of the goat cheese, creating a perfect balance of flavors.

Pro Tip:

For a vegan version, replace the goat cheese with dairy-free feta or omit the cheese entirely.

5. Grilled BBQ Ribs with Homemade Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups homemade BBQ sauce (see recipe below)

BBQ Sauce Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Ribs: Remove the silver skin from the ribs by sliding a knife under the membrane on the back of the ribs and pulling it off. Rub both sides of the ribs with olive oil, and season generously with salt and pepper.

  2. Make the BBQ Sauce: In a saucepan over medium heat, combine ketchup, apple cider vinegar, honey, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat and let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens.

  3. Grill the Ribs: Preheat your grill to low heat, about 250°F. Place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down, and cook with indirect heat for 2-2.5 hours, basting with the BBQ sauce every 30 minutes. The ribs should be tender and slightly caramelized by the end.

  4. Serve: Slice the ribs into individual portions and serve with extra BBQ sauce on the side. These grilled BBQ ribs are juicy, flavorful, and perfect for any BBQ spread.

Pro Tip:

For a smokier flavor, add some wood chips to the grill for a truly authentic BBQ taste.

Conclusion

Farm-to-table grilling isn’t just about cooking; it’s about connecting with the land and enjoying food at its peak. By sourcing fresh, seasonal ingredients and embracing local produce, you can elevate your BBQ and create memorable meals that not only taste incredible but also support sustainable practices. Whether it’s fresh veggies, juicy peaches, or tender ribs, each of these recipes celebrates the best that nature has to offer. So gather your ingredients, fire up your grill, and enjoy a farm-to-table feast that everyone will love!
