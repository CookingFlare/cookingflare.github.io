Spicy One-Pot Cajun Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya: A Southern Treat


If you’re looking for a taste of the South, look no further than this Spicy One-Pot Cajun Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya recipe. Bursting with flavor and heat, this dish is a perfect blend of spices, seafood, and savory sausage. Best of all, it’s all cooked in just one pot, making cleanup a breeze. So grab your apron and get ready to embark on a culinary journey through the heart of Louisiana.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound of smoked sausage, sliced into rounds
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons of Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery, and cook until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

  3. Add the smoked sausage to the pot and cook until it starts to brown, about 5 minutes.

  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, dried thyme, dried oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Once the mixture is boiling, stir in the rice. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and most of the liquid has been absorbed.

  6. While the rice is cooking, season the shrimp with salt and pepper. In a separate skillet, cook the shrimp over medium-high heat until they turn pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

  7. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the cooked shrimp. Cover the pot and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  8. Serve the Spicy One-Pot Cajun Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Notes

  • Feel free to adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less cayenne pepper. Cajun cuisine is known for its bold flavors, so don’t be afraid to turn up the heat if you’re feeling adventurous.

  • This recipe is perfect for feeding a crowd or for meal prepping. It can easily be doubled or tripled to accommodate larger gatherings or to have leftovers for the week.

  • If you prefer a different type of protein, such as chicken or crawfish, feel free to substitute it for the shrimp. The key is to use ingredients that are readily available and suit your taste preferences.

  • Jambalaya is a versatile dish that can be customized to your liking. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different vegetables, spices, or even add some hot sauce for an extra kick.

So there you have it, a taste of the South in the form of Spicy One-Pot Cajun Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya. This flavorful dish is sure to transport you to the vibrant streets of New Orleans with every bite. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the rich, spicy flavors of this Southern treat. Enjoy!
