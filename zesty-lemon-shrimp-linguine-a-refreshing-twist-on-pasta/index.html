Zesty Lemon Shrimp Linguine: A Refreshing Twist on Pasta


If you’re looking for a light and refreshing pasta dish that’s bursting with flavor, then look no further than this zesty lemon shrimp linguine recipe. This dish is the perfect combination of tangy citrus, succulent shrimp, and al dente linguine, creating a symphony of flavors that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a gourmet meal at home, this recipe is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the details of this delightful dish and learn how to bring a touch of elegance to your dinner table.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of linguine
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Linguine: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the linguine and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the linguine, reserving a cup of the pasta water, and set it aside.

  2. Cook the Shrimp: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and season with salt and pepper. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

  3. Make the Sauce: In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the white wine and chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Allow the mixture to simmer for 3-4 minutes to reduce slightly.

  4. Combine the Ingredients: Stir in the lemon juice and zest, then add the heavy cream. Allow the sauce to simmer for another 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet, tossing them in the sauce to coat evenly.

  5. Bring It All Together: Add the cooked linguine to the skillet, tossing it with the shrimp and sauce. If the sauce is too thick, you can add some of the reserved pasta water to achieve the desired consistency. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

  6. Serve and Garnish: Once everything is well combined, transfer the zesty lemon shrimp linguine to a serving dish. Garnish with fresh parsley and an extra sprinkle of lemon zest for a vibrant finish.

Enjoy Your Culinary Masterpiece

Now that you’ve mastered the art of creating zesty lemon shrimp linguine, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. This dish is best enjoyed with a crisp white wine to complement the citrusy flavors and delicate shrimp. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or treating yourself to a gourmet meal, this recipe is a surefire way to elevate your culinary repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and prepare to embark on a culinary adventure that will leave you craving more. Cheers to indulging in the simple pleasures of good food and great company!
