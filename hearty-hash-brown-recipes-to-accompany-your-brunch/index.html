Hearty Hash Brown Recipes to Accompany Your Brunch


Brunch is a beloved mealtime tradition, offering the perfect blend of breakfast and lunch. And what better way to elevate your brunch experience than with some delicious and hearty hash browns? These crispy and golden treats are the perfect accompaniment to any brunch spread, providing a satisfying crunch and a burst of flavor. Whether you’re hosting a brunch for friends or simply treating yourself to a leisurely weekend meal, these hash brown recipes will surely impress. So roll up your sleeves, grab your potatoes, and let’s get cooking!

Classic Hash Browns

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium russet potatoes
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Start by peeling and grating the potatoes. Rinse them under cold water to remove excess starch and then drain well. This step will help achieve crispier hash browns.
  2. Place the grated potatoes in a clean kitchen towel and squeeze out any remaining moisture.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the grated potatoes, chopped onion, flour, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.
  4. Heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Once the oil is hot, spoon the potato mixture into the skillet, pressing it down lightly with the back of a spatula to form a flat layer.
  6. Cook the hash browns for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the hash browns from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  8. Serve the classic hash browns hot alongside your favorite brunch dishes.

Cheesy Bacon Hash Browns

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium russet potatoes
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Follow the same steps for grating and preparing the potatoes as in the classic hash browns recipe.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the grated potatoes, crumbled bacon, shredded cheddar cheese, green onions, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Spoon the potato mixture into the skillet, pressing it down lightly to form a flat layer.
  5. Cook the hash browns for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and the cheese has melted.
  6. Remove the hash browns from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  7. Serve the cheesy bacon hash browns hot, and watch them disappear in no time!

Sweet Potato Hash Browns

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1 small red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by peeling and grating the sweet potatoes. Rinse them under cold water and drain well.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced red bell pepper, red onion, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the grated sweet potatoes to the skillet and season with paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Cook the sweet potato hash browns for about 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are cooked through and crispy.
  5. Remove from heat and serve the sweet potato hash browns as a delightful side dish to complement your brunch.

Conclusion

Hash browns are a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. From the classic version to the cheesy bacon twist or the sweet potato variation, there’s a hash brown recipe to suit every taste. So the next time you’re planning a brunch gathering or simply craving a delicious and filling meal, give these hearty hash brown recipes a try. They’re sure to add a touch of warmth and flavor to your brunch table. Enjoy!
