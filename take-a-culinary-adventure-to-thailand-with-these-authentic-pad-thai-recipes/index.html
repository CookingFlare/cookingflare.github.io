Take a Culinary Adventure to Thailand with These Authentic Pad Thai Recipes


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, then you know that one dish stands out above the rest - Pad Thai. This flavorful and aromatic stir-fried noodle dish has become synonymous with Thai food around the world. But if you’ve only ever tried Pad Thai at your local Thai restaurant, you’re missing out on the true flavors and textures of this iconic dish. So why not take a culinary adventure to Thailand and try these authentic Pad Thai recipes for yourself?

Classic Pad Thai

Let’s start with the classic Pad Thai recipe that has been enjoyed by generations of Thai people. To make this dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
  • 100g tofu, diced
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons crushed peanuts
  • Lime wedges, for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 15 minutes or until they are soft and pliable. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a wok or large frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and shallot and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the tofu and stir-fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar until well combined. Pour the sauce into the wok and stir-fry for another minute.
  5. Push the ingredients to one side of the wok and crack the egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg until it is almost cooked, then mix it with the other ingredients in the wok.
  6. Add the shrimp and stir-fry until they turn pink and are cooked through, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add the soaked rice noodles to the wok and toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce and heated through, about 2-3 minutes.
  8. Remove the wok from the heat and add the bean sprouts. Toss everything together one last time.
  9. Serve the Pad Thai on a plate, garnished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh cilantro.

Vegetarian Pad Thai

For those who prefer a vegetarian version of Pad Thai, this recipe is packed with flavor and texture. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
  • 100g tofu, diced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons chopped peanuts
  • Lime wedges, for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 15 minutes or until they are soft and pliable. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a wok or large frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and shallot and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the tofu, carrot, and bell pepper to the wok and stir-fry until the vegetables are tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, soy sauce, and brown sugar until well combined. Pour the sauce into the wok and stir-fry for another minute.
  5. Push the ingredients to one side of the wok and pour the beaten egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg until it is almost cooked, then mix it with the other ingredients in the wok.
  6. Add the soaked rice noodles to the wok and toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce and heated through, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Remove the wok from the heat and add the bean sprouts. Toss everything together one last time.
  8. Serve the Pad Thai on a plate, garnished with chopped peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh cilantro.

Conclusion

By trying these authentic Pad Thai recipes, you can truly experience the flavors and aromas of Thailand. Whether you prefer the classic version with shrimp or a vegetarian alternative, these recipes will transport you to the streets of Bangkok or the beaches of Phuket. So grab your wok and embark on a culinary adventure to Thailand with Pad Thai as your guide. Happy cooking!
