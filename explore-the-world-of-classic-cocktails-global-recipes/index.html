Explore the World of Classic Cocktails: Global Recipes


The world of cocktails is a vast and wondrous place, full of flavors, aromas, and stories from around the globe. From the classic daiquiri to the sophisticated negroni, each drink has its own unique history, ingredients, and cultural significance. In this recipe collection, we’ll take you on a journey to explore the world of classic cocktails, with a focus on global recipes that showcase the diversity and richness of this beloved drink.

The Classic Daiquiri

The daiquiri is a classic cocktail that originated in Cuba in the late 19th century. Made with rum, lime juice, and simple syrup, this refreshing drink is a staple of tropical getaways and summer parties. To make a classic daiquiri, combine 2 ounces of white rum, 1 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice, and 1/2 ounce of simple syrup in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lime wheel and enjoy!

The Negroni

The negroni is a sophisticated cocktail that originated in Italy in the early 20th century. Made with gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth, this bitter drink is a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts. To make a negroni, combine 1 1/2 ounces of gin, 1/2 ounce of Campari, and 1/2 ounce of sweet vermouth in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with an orange twist and enjoy!

The Mai Tai

The Mai Tai is a classic cocktail that originated in Tahiti in the 1940s. Made with rum, lime juice, orange curaçao, and orgeat syrup, this tropical drink is a staple of tiki bars and beachside vacations. To make a Mai Tai, combine 2 ounces of dark rum, 1 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice, 1/2 ounce of orange curaçao, and 1/2 ounce of orgeat syrup in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lime wheel and enjoy!

The Margarita

The margarita is a classic cocktail that originated in Mexico in the 1940s. Made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, this refreshing drink is a staple of Mexican cuisine and celebrations. To make a margarita, combine 2 ounces of tequila, 1 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice, and 1/2 ounce of triple sec in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lime wheel and enjoy!

The Moscow Mule

The Moscow Mule is a classic cocktail that originated in Russia in the 1940s. Made with vodka, ginger beer, and lime juice, this spicy drink is a staple of bars and restaurants around the world. To make a Moscow Mule, combine 1 1/2 ounces of vodka, 4 ounces of ginger beer, and 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice in a copper mug filled with ice. Stir gently for 5-10 seconds, then garnish with a lime wheel and enjoy!

The Whiskey Sour

The Whiskey Sour is a classic cocktail that originated in the United States in the late 19th century. Made with whiskey, lemon juice, and simple syrup, this tangy drink is a staple of bars and restaurants around the world. To make a Whiskey Sour, combine 2 ounces of whiskey, 3/4 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice, and 1/2 ounce of simple syrup in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lemon wheel and enjoy!

The French 75

The French 75 is a classic cocktail that originated in France in the early 20th century. Made with gin, lemon juice, sugar, and champagne, this elegant drink is a staple of fine dining and special occasions. To make a French 75, combine 1 1/2 ounces of gin, 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice, 1/2 ounce of simple syrup, and 2 ounces of champagne in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lemon twist and enjoy!

The Sidecar

The Sidecar is a classic cocktail that originated in France in the early 20th century. Made with cognac, Cointreau, and lemon juice, this elegant drink is a staple of fine dining and special occasions. To make a Sidecar, combine 1 1/2 ounces of cognac, 1/2 ounce of Cointreau, and 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lemon juice in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lemon twist and enjoy!

The Gin Rickey

The Gin Rickey is a classic cocktail that originated in the United States in the late 19th century. Made with gin, lime juice, and soda water, this refreshing drink is a staple of bars and restaurants around the world. To make a Gin Rickey, combine 1 1/2 ounces of gin and 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds, then strain into a chilled glass filled with ice. Top with soda water and garnish with a lime wheel.

In this collection of global recipes, you’ve explored the world of classic cocktails, from the tropical flavors of the Mai Tai to the sophisticated elegance of the Sidecar. Each drink has its own unique ingredients, techniques, and cultural significance, making the world of cocktails a rich and diverse place. Whether you’re a seasoned cocktail enthusiast or just starting your journey, we hope you’ve enjoyed this exploration of the world’s classic cocktails. Cheers!
