Taste the South: 5 Southern-inspired Rubs for Flavorful Grilled Ribs


Summer is here, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and indulge in some mouthwatering barbecue. If you’re looking to add a touch of Southern charm to your grilled ribs, we’ve got you covered. In this post, we’ll be sharing five delicious and easy-to-make Southern-inspired rubs that will take your grilled ribs to the next level. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds and impress your friends and family with these flavorful creations.

1. Smoky Memphis Rub

If you’re a fan of rich, smoky flavors, this Memphis-style rub is a must-try. In a bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of paprika, 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of chili powder, 1 tablespoon of garlic powder, 1 tablespoon of onion powder, 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper, 1 teaspoon of black pepper, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix well to ensure all the spices are evenly distributed. Generously rub this mixture onto your ribs, making sure to coat them thoroughly. Let the flavors meld for at least 30 minutes before grilling.

2. Sweet and Spicy Carolina Rub

For those who prefer a balance of sweetness and heat, this Carolina-style rub is a winner. In a small bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of paprika, 1 tablespoon of chili powder, 1 tablespoon of black pepper, 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, and 1 teaspoon of onion powder. Mix well to create a harmonious blend of flavors. Massage this rub onto your ribs, ensuring every nook and cranny is covered. Allow the flavors to infuse for at least 1 hour before grilling.

3. Tangy Alabama White Sauce Rub

If you’re in the mood for something unique and tangy, look no further than this Alabama-inspired white sauce rub. In a bowl, combine 1 cup of mayonnaise, 1/4 cup of apple cider vinegar, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, 1 tablespoon of black pepper, 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, and 1 teaspoon of onion powder. Whisk until smooth and creamy. Slather this sauce onto your ribs, making sure to coat them liberally. Allow the flavors to penetrate the meat for at least 2 hours before grilling.

4. Savory Texas Rub

For a classic, savory flavor, this Texas-style rub hits the mark. In a small bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of paprika, 1 tablespoon of chili powder, 1 tablespoon of black pepper, 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, 1 teaspoon of onion powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper. Mix well to create a robust and flavorful rub. Massage this mixture onto your ribs, ensuring an even coating. Let the flavors intensify for at least 1 hour before grilling.

5. Spicy Cajun Rub

If you’re a fan of bold and spicy flavors, this Cajun-style rub is a must-try. In a bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of paprika, 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 tablespoon of garlic powder, 1 tablespoon of onion powder, 1 tablespoon of dried thyme, 1 tablespoon of dried oregano, 1 tablespoon of cayenne pepper, and 1 tablespoon of black pepper. Mix well to create a fiery and aromatic rub. Massage this mixture onto your ribs, ensuring every inch is covered. Let the flavors marinate for at least 30 minutes before grilling.

Now that you have these five delicious Southern-inspired rubs, it’s time to fire up the grill and get cooking. Whether you prefer smoky, sweet and spicy, tangy, savory, or spicy Cajun flavors, these rubs will elevate your grilled ribs to new heights. So gather your friends and family, embrace the flavors of the South, and enjoy a memorable barbecue experience. Happy grilling!
