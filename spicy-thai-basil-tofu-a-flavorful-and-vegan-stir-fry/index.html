Spicy Thai Basil Tofu: A Flavorful and Vegan Stir-Fry


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and plant-based cuisine, this Spicy Thai Basil Tofu stir-fry is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Inspired by the vibrant and aromatic flavors of Thai cuisine, this dish is a delightful fusion of crispy tofu, fragrant basil, and a fiery sauce that will transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok with every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz (400g) firm tofu, drained and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch or arrowroot powder
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, plus more for garnish
  • 2 green onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (or more if you prefer extra heat)

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tofu: Pat the tofu dry with paper towels and cut it into 1-inch cubes. Place the cubes in a bowl and toss with 2 tablespoons of cornstarch or arrowroot powder until evenly coated. This step will help create a crispy exterior on the tofu.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, sesame oil, water, and 1 teaspoon of cornstarch until well combined. Set aside.

  3. Cook the Tofu: Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the coated tofu cubes in a single layer and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. Work in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pan. Transfer the cooked tofu to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

  4. Stir-Fry the Aromatics: In the same skillet or wok, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced white parts of the green onions. Sauté for 30 seconds, or until fragrant.

  5. Add the Vegetables: Toss in the sliced red bell pepper and continue stir-frying for 2-3 minutes, or until the pepper starts to soften slightly.

  6. Combine and Toss: Return the cooked tofu to the skillet or wok and add the fresh basil leaves and red pepper flakes. Give everything a good toss to combine.

  7. Sauce it Up: Pour the prepared sauce over the stir-fry and continue tossing until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the tofu and vegetables, about 1-2 minutes.

  8. Garnish and Serve: Remove the stir-fry from heat and garnish with sliced green parts of the green onions and extra fresh basil leaves. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

Tips and Variations:

  • Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of red pepper flakes or omit them altogether. For an extra kick of heat, add a teaspoon or two of sambal oelek or sriracha sauce to the stir-fry.

  • Protein Options: While tofu is the star of this dish, you can also substitute it with tempeh, seitan, or even cubed extra-firm tofu for a meatier texture.

  • Vegetable Variety: Feel free to experiment with different vegetables in this stir-fry. Sliced mushrooms, snow peas, baby corn, or broccoli florets would make excellent additions.

  • Nutty Twist: For an extra crunch and nutty flavor, consider sprinkling some toasted cashews or peanuts over the finished dish.

  • Gluten-Free Option: To make this dish gluten-free, use tamari or coconut aminos instead of soy sauce, and ensure that your cornstarch or arrowroot powder is gluten-free.

This Spicy Thai Basil Tofu stir-fry is a delightful blend of flavors and textures that will leave you craving more. With its perfect balance of spicy, savory, and aromatic notes, it’s a dish that will transport your senses to the vibrant streets of Thailand. Serve it as a flavorful main course or a side dish to complement your favorite Asian-inspired meals. Prepare to be captivated by the alluring aroma and taste of this vegan delight!
