10 Fall Recipes That Are Perfect for Meal Prep


As the crisp air of fall settles in, the allure of hearty, nourishing meals becomes irresistible. This season is the ideal time to make the most of cozy flavors and warming ingredients, and what better way to enjoy them than with meal prep? Planning your weekly meals not only saves time but can also help you savor the essence of autumn in every bite. Below are ten fall-inspired recipes that are perfect for meal prep. These dishes are flavor-forward, nutrient-packed, and designed to hold up beautifully in the fridge or freezer.

1. Roasted Butternut Squash and Quinoa Salad

Nothing screams fall quite like butternut squash. Packed with vitamins and subtly sweet, this squash is the star of a wholesome salad that’s perfect for lunches or side dishes. Start by roasting cubes of butternut squash in olive oil, salt, and paprika. While they roast, cook a batch of fluffy quinoa and prepare a dressing of maple syrup, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, and olive oil. Toss the squash and quinoa with the dressing, along with dried cranberries, crumbled goat cheese, and a handful of chopped pecans. This dish keeps well in the fridge for up to five days and gets better as the flavors meld together over time.

2. Turkey and Sweet Potato Chili

Chili is a meal prep favorite, and this fall-inspired version adds a unique seasonal twist. In a large pot, sauté onions, garlic, and bell peppers until fragrant, then add ground turkey and cook until browned. Toss in cubed sweet potatoes, canned diced tomatoes, black beans, and a blend of spices like chili powder, cumin, and smoked paprika. Simmer until the sweet potatoes are tender and everything is infused with a rich, spicy aroma. Divide into individual portions and freeze for quick, comforting meals later in the week.

3. Pumpkin Sage Risotto

Ideal for dinner and perhaps even better as leftovers, pumpkin sage risotto is creamy, aromatic, and luxurious. Begin by sautéing finely chopped onions and garlic in butter. Add Arborio rice and toast it lightly, then deglaze with white wine or vegetable stock. Gradually ladle in hot stock, stirring continuously until the rice is cooked and creamy. Mix in pumpkin puree, a splash of heavy cream, grated Parmesan cheese, and freshly chopped sage. This dish can be portioned out for lunches, and its velvety texture reheats beautifully.

4. Maple-Glazed Carrot and Lentil Bowls

Perfect for vegetarians or anyone looking for a nutrient-packed option, these lentil bowls highlight roasted carrots drizzled with a sweet maple glaze. Roast fresh carrots in a mixture of olive oil, maple syrup, and a hint of cinnamon until caramelized. In the meantime, prepare a batch of green or black lentils and toss them with lemon juice, fresh parsley, and a pinch of salt. Layer the lentils and carrots over a bed of baby spinach or arugula, and drizzle each portion with tahini dressing before serving. These bowls are vibrant, nutrient-dense, and perfect for meal prep.

5. Stuffed Bell Peppers with Wild Rice and Sausage

Stuffed bell peppers are a meal prep classic, and during fall, you can put an autumnal spin on them by using wild rice and your favorite sausage. Hollow out bell peppers, then sauté crumbled sausage with onions, garlic, and spinach. Combine the mixture with cooked wild rice and spoon it into the peppers. Bake in the oven with a sprinkle of cheese on top until the peppers are tender and the cheese is golden. Store in airtight containers for an easy grab-and-go meal any day of the week.

6. Apple Cinnamon Overnight Oats

Breakfast meal prep options don’t get better than overnight oats, and apple-cinnamon flavors are an autumn staple. Combine rolled oats, almond milk, Greek yogurt, grated apple, cinnamon, and a touch of maple syrup in a mason jar or small container. Stir well, screw on the lid, and refrigerate overnight. These oats are ready to eat in the morning and are perfect for busy fall mornings. Top with chopped pecans or walnuts just before eating for added crunch.

7. Sheet Pan Harvest Chicken and Vegetables

Sheet pan dinners are a lifesaver for meal prep, and this fall version delivers all the flavors of the season. On a large baking sheet, arrange boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cubed sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, and quartered red onions. Toss everything with olive oil, rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Bake until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are caramelized. This one-pan wonder can be portioned out for multiple meals and reheats perfectly.

8. Creamy Cauliflower Soup

A steaming bowl of creamy cauliflower soup is just what’s needed to warm up on fall days. Roast cauliflower florets and peeled garlic cloves in the oven until golden. Blend them with chicken or vegetable broth, a splash of cream, and a pinch of nutmeg until smooth. For added flavor, drizzle each bowl with truffle oil or sprinkle crispy bacon bits on top. Store the soup in jars or containers, and you’ll have a comforting meal ready to enjoy all week long.

9. Autumn Farro Pilaf

Farro, a nutty ancient grain, is the perfect canvas for fall ingredients. In a large skillet, sauté diced onions and garlic in olive oil, then toss in cooked farro. Mix in roasted butternut squash cubes, dried cranberries, toasted almonds, and a sprinkle of feta or goat cheese. Finish with a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a touch of tangy sweetness. This pilaf can be served as a side or a main dish and keeps well in the fridge for reheating.

10. Spiced Pear and Walnut Muffins

Fall isn’t complete without baked goods to enjoy alongside a hot drink, and these spiced pear and walnut muffins fit the bill. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, baking powder, warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, and a pinch of salt. In a separate bowl, mix mashed ripe pears, eggs, Greek yogurt, and a touch of maple syrup. Combine wet and dry ingredients, fold in chopped walnuts, and bake in a muffin tin until golden. These muffins make for a perfect grab-and-go breakfast or snack and can be frozen for longer storage.

Meal Prep Tips for Fall Recipes

  • Invest in Quality Containers: Opt for airtight, microwave-safe containers to keep your meals fresh and easy to reheat.
  • Plan for Variety: Use seasonal produce and spices to keep your meals interesting all week long.
  • Batch Cooking: Reserve a few hours over the weekend to prepare multiple recipes and save time during the week.

With these ten fall-inspired recipes, you can embrace the season’s flavors while staying organized with your meals. From hearty soups to sweet treats, these dishes are packed with the essence of autumn and are perfect for weeks when you want delicious, home-cooked meals without the daily hassle of cooking.
