Classic Spanish Flan Recipe for a Decadent Dessert


Flan, the quintessential Spanish dessert, is a silky and indulgent treat that will leave you craving for more. With its smooth custard-like texture and luscious caramel sauce, it’s no wonder that flan has been a favorite dessert in Spain for centuries. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create a classic Spanish flan that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place a deep baking dish inside the oven, filled halfway with water. This will create a water bath for baking the flan.

  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly until the sugar melts and turns a deep golden brown color. Be careful not to burn the sugar, as it can happen quickly. Remove the saucepan from heat and carefully pour the caramel into a 9-inch round baking dish. Tilt the dish to evenly coat the bottom with the caramel.

  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the mixture over the caramel in the baking dish.

  4. Carefully place the baking dish into the preheated oven, making sure it is sitting in the water bath. Bake for approximately 45-50 minutes, or until the flan is set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center.

  5. Once the flan is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Then, cover it with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow it to fully set.

  6. To serve, run a knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it from the dish. Place a serving plate upside down over the flan and carefully flip it over, allowing the caramel to drizzle over the top. Gently lift the baking dish off the flan.

  7. Slice the flan into individual portions and serve chilled. The creamy texture and rich caramel flavor make it a perfect ending to any meal.

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize your flan by adding a touch of orange zest or a sprinkle of cinnamon to the custard mixture for an extra layer of flavor.
  • For a more decadent twist, you can serve the flan with a dollop of whipped cream or a sprinkle of shaved chocolate on top.
  • If you prefer a lighter version of flan, you can use low-fat milk or even substitute part of the milk with heavy cream for a creamier texture.
  • Experiment with different shapes and sizes of baking dishes to create individual flan servings or to showcase your creativity.

So there you have it, a classic Spanish flan recipe that will transport you to the streets of Spain with its rich flavors and smooth texture. Whether you’re serving it at a dinner party or enjoying it as a special treat for yourself, this decadent dessert is sure to impress. Give it a try and savor the taste of Spain in every bite.

Note: This recipe serves approximately 6-8 people, depending on portion sizes.
