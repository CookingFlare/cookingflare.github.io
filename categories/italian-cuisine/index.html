Italian Cooking for Two: Lovers, Friends, and Familes

Cooking for two can sometimes seem like a daunting task - restricted to the usual suspects of grilled cheese or pasta with marinara sauce. Fear not, for this guide will help you whip up an authentic Italian feast perfect for sharing with no more than one special someone or a cozy gathering with a few close friends and family. Antipasto Platter Ingredients: Prosciutto di Parma Fresh mozzarella Artichoke hearts Marinated mushrooms Pepperoncini Aged balsamic vinegar Extra-virgin olive oil Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper Instructions: Spread a large wooden board with a cheesecloth or a round of parchment paper. Arrange the prosciutto slices around the board, making sure to leave space between each slice so guests can grab a piece easily. On top of the prosciutto, arrange the rest of the ingredients and drizzle with a generous amount of aged balsamic vinegar and extra-virgin olive oil. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Pasta alle Vongole (Clams Pasta) Ingredients: 1 pound spaghetti 2 pounds littleneck clams, scrubbed Extra-virgin olive oil 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced 1/2 cup dry white wine 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1 1/2 tablespoons red pepper flakes Grated Pecorino Romano cheese Instructions: In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. In a large skillet, heat extra-virgin olive oil over medium heat. Add sliced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add clams and white wine, cover and cook until clams open, about 5-7 minutes. Once pasta is cooked, drain and add to the skillet. Toss to combine with the clam mixture. Add parsley, red pepper flakes, and drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil. Toss to coat. Serve pasta with a generous sprinkling of grated Pecorino Romano cheese. Caprese Salad Ingredients: 2 large tomatoes, sliced 1 large ball of fresh mozzarella, sliced Fresh basil leaves Extra-virgin olive oil Balsamic vinegar Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper Instructions: Lay out tomato slices on a large serving platter. Arrange fresh mozzarella slices over the tomatoes. Sprinkle with fresh basil leaves, covering the tomatoes and mozzarella generously. Drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegar, creating a light dressing over the salad. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Tiramisu Ingredients: 1 pound mascarpone cheese, room temperature 1/4 cup granulated sugar 4 large egg yolks 1/4 cup whole milk 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee, warm 1/4 cup dark rum 12 ounces ladyfingers 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder Instructions: In a medium bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale, about 2-3 minutes. Whisk in milk and set the bowl over a saucepan of simmering water, making sure the water does not touch the bottom of the bowl. Whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and reaches 160°F (71°C), 2-4 minutes. Remove the bowl from the heat and whisk in the mascarpone until smooth. In a small bowl, stir together coffee and rum and arrange ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of a 9-inch square baking dish. Spoon half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then drizzle with half the coffee mixture. Arrange another layer of ladyfingers on top and spoon the remaining mascarpone mixture. Drizzle in the remaining coffee mixture and dust the top with cocoa powder. Let tiramisu chill for at least 4 hours before serving.
