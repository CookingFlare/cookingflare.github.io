How to Make Spanish Croquetas: A Creamy and Crunchy Snack


Spain is known for many mouthwatering dishes, but few are as beloved and universally enjoyed as croquetas. These creamy, crunchy delights are the epitome of comfort food and are a staple at tapas bars across the country. Whether you’re planning a Spanish-inspired dinner party or simply looking for a satisfying bite-sized treat, learning how to make croquetas will elevate your culinary skills while impressing your guests.

What Are Spanish Croquetas?

Croquetas are small, breaded, and fried bites featuring a luscious, creamy interior encased in a crisp exterior. Traditionally made from a béchamel base, Spanish croquetas often include fillings such as ham (jamón), chicken, cod, or even spinach and cheese. The beauty of croquetas lies in their versatility; you can incorporate any leftovers or ingredients you have at hand, creating unique variations to suit your taste.

Perfect as an appetizer, snack, or paired with wine and cocktails, these bite-sized wonders are truly irresistible. Let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making Spanish croquetas from scratch.

Ingredients for Classic Spanish Croquetas

To make approximately 25 croquetas, you’ll need:

For the béchamel base:

  • 1/4 cup (60g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (70g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (500ml) whole milk (warm)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Ground nutmeg, a pinch
  • Black pepper, to taste

For the filling:

  • 3/4 cup (140g) finely diced Serrano ham or cooked chicken
    (You can substitute with cod, spinach, or cheese for variations.)
  • Optional: 1/4 cup (50g) grated Manchego cheese for a richer flavor.

For the coating:

  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup (100g) breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, for flavoring the breadcrumbs)
  • Vegetable oil for frying (enough to submerge the croquetas)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Spanish Croquetas

Step 1: Preparing the Béchamel Base

The béchamel sauce is the heart of any croqueta recipe. It gives the croquetas their creamy texture and ensures they hold together during frying.

  1. Melt the Butter
    In a medium-sized saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Be careful not to let it brown—just melt it completely.

  2. Make the Roux
    Gradually sprinkle the flour into the melted butter, whisking constantly until the mixture forms a thick paste. This is called a roux, and it’s the foundation of the béchamel sauce.

  3. Add the Milk
    Slowly pour the warm milk into the roux, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Keep stirring as the mixture thickens; this will take around 7–10 minutes. The béchamel should be silky smooth.

  4. Season
    Stir in salt, nutmeg, and black pepper to taste. If you’re using a particularly salty filling (like Serrano ham), go light on the salt.

Step 2: Incorporating the Filling

Once your béchamel has reached a thick consistency, it’s time to incorporate the filling to flavor your croquetas.

  1. Add the Filling
    Fold your chosen filling—Serrano ham, cooked chicken, or other options—into the béchamel sauce, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.

  2. Chill the Mixture
    Transfer the mixture to a shallow dish and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, cover it with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 3 hours, or overnight. This step is crucial, as it firms up the béchamel mixture, making it easier to shape and roll later.

Step 3: Shaping the Croquetas

The chilled béchamel mixture becomes firm enough to mold into classic croqueta shapes.

  1. Portion and Shape
    Using a spoon or small ice cream scoop, take portions of the chilled mixture and form them into elongated oval shapes or small balls. The shape is up to you, but oval croquetas are traditional in Spain.

  2. Set Aside
    Place the formed croquetas on a tray lined with parchment paper. Refrigerate them again briefly while you prepare the coating.

Step 4: Coating the Croquetas

The crunchy coating is what makes croquetas so iconic. Here’s how to get it just right:

  1. Prepare the Coating Stations
    Place the beaten eggs in one shallow bowl and the breadcrumbs in another. For extra flavor, you can mix olive oil, salt, or spices into the breadcrumbs.

  2. Dip and Coat
    Roll each croqueta first in the beaten egg, letting any excess drip off, and then into the breadcrumbs, ensuring they’re fully coated. For a thicker crust, you can repeat this process for a second layer of egg and breadcrumbs.

Step 5: Frying the Croquetas

Frying is the final step that transforms croquetas into golden, crispy snacks.

  1. Heat the Oil
    In a large pan or deep fryer, heat vegetable oil to around 350°F (180°C). Ensure there’s enough oil to submerge the croquetas completely.

  2. Fry the Croquetas
    Fry the croquetas in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for 2–3 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy on the outside.

  3. Drain and Serve
    Use a slotted spoon to remove the croquetas and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Tips for Perfect Croquetas

  1. Consistency Is Key
    The béchamel mixture should be thick but smooth. If it’s too loose, the croquetas will fall apart during frying.

  2. Chilling Is Crucial
    Don’t rush the process. Adequate chilling ensures the croquetas hold their shape.

  3. Breadcrumbs Matter
    Use fresh breadcrumbs or panko for the best results. Stale breadcrumbs can compromise the crispiness.

  4. Experiment with Fillings
    While ham and chicken are classics, don’t shy away from experimenting. Try combinations like spinach and goat cheese, smoked salmon, or caramelized onions.

Serving and Pairing Suggestions

Croquetas are best enjoyed freshly fried and hot, but they’re versatile enough to pair with various accompaniments. Serve them as part of a tapas spread with olives, empanadas, and patatas bravas. Pair them with a glass of Spanish wine or beer for the ultimate indulgent snack.

Additionally, croquetas can work as a side dish for a larger meal. You can plate them alongside a crisp salad or drizzle them with aioli or homemade tomato sauce for added flavor.

Storing and Reheating

If you don’t plan to serve all your croquetas at once, they can be stored and reheated with great results:

  • Storing Uncooked Croquetas
    Once formed and coated, uncooked croquetas can be frozen in a single layer on a tray, then transferred to a sealed container. This way, you’ll have croquetas ready to fry anytime.

  • Reheating Cooked Croquetas
    If you have leftover croquetas, reheat them in the oven at 350°F (180°C) for 8–10 minutes. Avoid microwaving, as it can make them soggy.

Conclusion

Spanish croquetas are a testament to the country’s culinary ingenuity—a small dish that delivers big flavor. With their creamy filling and crispy exterior, they’re a must-try for anyone who loves fried comfort food. Once you’ve mastered the base recipe, the possibilities for customization are endless. Whether enjoyed as a quick snack or a highlight at your next festive gathering, croquetas are sure to delight everyone who takes a bite.

So roll up your sleeves, head to the kitchen, and experience the joy of making Spanish croquetas from scratch. Your taste buds—and your friends and family—will thank you!
