The Best Spanish Desserts: 8 Recipes to Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth


Spain is famous for its rich culture, history, and, of course, its vibrant cuisine. Spanish food is not only savory but also offers an abundance of sweet treats that will transport your taste buds to another world. Whether you’re strolling through the streets of Madrid, relaxing on a beach in Barcelona, or simply dreaming of Spain from afar, these eight iconic Spanish desserts will leave you craving more. From indulgent cakes to crispy pastries, the diversity of Spanish desserts is truly spectacular.

In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the best Spanish desserts, with recipes that are easy to follow and sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. Whether you’re a baking pro or just starting, these recipes will inspire you to recreate the delightful flavors of Spain in your own kitchen.

1. Churros with Chocolate Sauce

Churros are one of Spain’s most beloved treats, and for good reason! These crispy, golden-brown dough sticks are the perfect balance of sweetness and crunch. Traditionally served with thick hot chocolate for dipping, churros make an irresistible dessert or breakfast snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Cinnamon and sugar (for coating)

For the Chocolate Sauce:

  • 100g dark chocolate
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and salt. Bring it to a boil, then remove from heat.
  2. Stir in the flour until the dough forms a thick consistency.
  3. Allow the dough to cool for a few minutes before adding the eggs one at a time. Mix until smooth.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. While the oil is heating, transfer the dough into a piping bag with a star-shaped nozzle.
  5. Once the oil is hot, pipe the dough into the pan, cutting the churros to your desired length. Fry until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  6. In a small bowl, mix cinnamon and sugar together. Coat the churros with the mixture while they are still warm.
  7. For the chocolate sauce, melt the chocolate in a small saucepan with milk and sugar over low heat, stirring constantly until smooth and thick.
  8. Serve the churros warm with the decadent chocolate sauce for dipping.

2. Tarta de Santiago (Santiago Cake)

This almond cake is a classic Spanish dessert from the region of Galicia. It is traditionally served during the Feast of St. James and is decorated with a simple cross of St. James on top. Tarta de Santiago has a delightful almond flavor and a soft, moist texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups of ground almonds
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder
  • Powdered sugar (for decoration)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a round cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, beat the eggs and sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  3. Add the ground almonds, lemon zest, cinnamon, and baking powder. Stir until the mixture is fully combined.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  5. Allow the cake to cool completely before removing it from the pan.
  6. To decorate, dust the top of the cake with powdered sugar. You can use a stencil of the Santiago Cross or freehand it for a traditional touch.

3. Flan (Spanish Caramel Custard)

Flan is a creamy, smooth custard dessert with a luscious caramel topping. This classic Spanish dessert is simple to make and features ingredients you likely already have in your kitchen. It’s a great choice for anyone who loves velvety textures and rich flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of sugar (for the caramel)
  • 1 can of sweetened condensed milk (14 oz)
  • 1 can of evaporated milk (12 oz)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium saucepan, heat the sugar over medium heat, stirring constantly until it melts and turns into a golden caramel. Carefully pour the caramel into a flan dish or individual ramekins, swirling it to coat the bottom.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the condensed milk, evaporated milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the custard mixture over the caramel in the dish.
  5. Place the flan dish in a larger baking pan and add hot water to the outer pan, creating a water bath (about halfway up the sides of the flan dish).
  6. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the flan is set and a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Allow the flan to cool completely before refrigerating for at least 2 hours. To serve, run a knife around the edges and invert the flan onto a plate.

4. Tarta de Queso (Spanish Cheesecake)

The Spanish cheesecake is a simple yet indulgent dessert that stands out from the traditional American cheesecake. It’s made with cream cheese, sour cream, and a few other pantry staples. The result is a rich, creamy texture that melts in your mouth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup of sour cream
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup of flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C) and grease a springform pan.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cream cheese, sour cream, and sugar. Mix until smooth and creamy.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the lemon juice, vanilla extract, and flour.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top.
  5. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until the cheesecake is set and slightly golden on top.
  6. Let the cheesecake cool to room temperature before transferring it to the fridge. Chill for at least 4 hours before serving.

5. Polvorones (Spanish Almond Cookies)

Polvorones are traditional Spanish shortbread cookies that are typically made during the holiday season. These crumbly, melt-in-your-mouth cookies come in various flavors, but the classic almond version is the most popular.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of butter, softened
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of ground almonds
  • 1/2 cup of powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the softened butter, flour, ground almonds, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Mix until the dough comes together. If the dough is too sticky, add a little extra flour until it reaches the right consistency.
  4. Roll the dough into small balls or shape it into rounds using your hands.
  5. Place the cookies on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until lightly golden around the edges.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool completely before dusting with powdered sugar.

6. Arroz con Leche (Rice Pudding)

Arroz con leche is a comforting Spanish dessert made with rice, milk, sugar, and a dash of cinnamon. It’s simple to make, yet incredibly satisfying, with a rich, creamy texture that’s perfect for a cozy treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of short-grain rice
  • 4 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the rice, milk, sugar, cinnamon stick, and lemon zest.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the mixture thickens.
  3. Remove the cinnamon stick and discard. Spoon the rice pudding into individual bowls and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Serve with a sprinkle of cinnamon on top.

7. Cinnamon and Sugar “Torrijas” (Spanish French Toast)

Torrijas are a traditional Spanish dessert that’s especially popular during Lent and Easter. They are made by soaking slices of bread in milk and eggs, frying them until golden, and then coating them in cinnamon and sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of stale bread (preferably baguette)
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the milk and eggs. Whisk together until well combined.
  2. Soak each slice of bread in the milk and egg mixture for a few seconds, allowing the bread to absorb the liquid but not become too soggy.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the bread slices until golden brown on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the sugar and cinnamon together.
  5. Immediately coat the warm torrijas in the cinnamon sugar mixture and serve.

8. Bizcocho (Spanish Sponge Cake)

Bizcocho is a simple yet delicious Spanish sponge cake that can be enjoyed on its own or used as a base for layered desserts. It’s soft, airy, and lightly sweetened, making it a versatile dessert option for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a round cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Sift the flour, baking powder, and salt together, then fold them into the egg mixture gently.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Let the cake cool before serving or using as a base for other desserts.

These 8 Spanish desserts are a true testament to the country’s love for sweet treats. Whether you’re a fan of rich cakes, crispy pastries, or creamy custards, there’s something for every dessert lover. Try these recipes and bring a little taste of Spain into your kitchen!
