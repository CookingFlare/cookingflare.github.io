A Greek Sweet Tradition: How to Make Kourabiedes Cookies


If there’s one thing that Greeks take seriously, it’s their sweet treats. Among the many delightful desserts in Greek cuisine, Kourabiedes cookies hold a special place. These buttery, almond-filled cookies are a staple during festive occasions and celebrations, especially during Christmas and weddings. The irresistible aroma of butter and almonds wafting from the oven as these cookies bake is enough to make anyone’s mouth water. If you’re ready to embark on a culinary adventure to Greece from the comfort of your kitchen, let’s dive into the art of making Kourabiedes cookies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons brandy
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup blanched almonds, finely chopped
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

Instructions:

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and confectioners’ sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Add the egg yolk, brandy, and vanilla extract to the butter mixture, and beat until well combined.
  3. Gradually add the chopped almonds, flour, and baking powder to the mixture, stirring until a soft dough forms.

Step 2: Shaping the Cookies

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls, approximately 1 inch in diameter.
  3. Place the dough balls on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart.
  4. Use the tines of a fork to gently press down on the top of each cookie, creating a subtle pattern.

Step 3: Baking

  1. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the bottoms are lightly golden.
  2. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

  1. Once the cookies have cooled, sift a generous amount of confectioners’ sugar over the top of each cookie, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  2. Gently shake off any excess sugar, and your Kourabiedes cookies are ready to be enjoyed!

Serving and Storing

These delightful Kourabiedes cookies are best enjoyed with a cup of Greek coffee or a hot mug of tea. They also make a wonderful gift for friends and family. To store them, place the cookies in an airtight container with layers of parchment paper in between to prevent them from sticking together. Stored at room temperature, they will keep well for up to two weeks.

Final Thoughts

The process of making Kourabiedes cookies is not just about creating a sweet treat; it’s about carrying on a cherished Greek tradition that has been passed down through generations. As you savor the delicate, buttery flavor of these cookies, you’ll be transported to the sun-drenched landscapes of Greece, where hospitality and a love for good food are deeply rooted in the culture.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the spirit of Greek tradition infuse your kitchen as you bake these delectable Kourabiedes cookies. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply craving a taste of Greece, these cookies are sure to bring joy to your table and warmth to your heart. Opa!
