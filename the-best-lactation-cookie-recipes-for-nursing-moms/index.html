The Best Lactation Cookie Recipes for Nursing Moms


As a nursing mom, you’re likely no stranger to the challenges that come with breastfeeding. From managing engorgement to soothing sore nipples, it can be a daunting experience. But one of the most important things you can do to support your breastfeeding journey is to fuel your body with the right foods. And what better way to do that than with a delicious and nutritious lactation cookie?

In this post, we’ll explore the best lactation cookie recipes for nursing moms, complete with tips and tricks for making them at home. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or just starting out, these recipes are sure to become a staple in your breastfeeding routine.

The Science Behind Lactation Cookies

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s take a quick look at the science behind lactation cookies. You see, these cookies are designed to support lactation by providing a boost of nutrients that can help increase milk production. The key ingredients in lactation cookies are galactagogues, which are substances that stimulate milk production.

Some of the most common galactagogues found in lactation cookies include oatmeal, brewer’s yeast, and fenugreek. Oatmeal is a rich source of fiber, which can help regulate blood sugar levels and support milk production. Brewer’s yeast is a natural source of B vitamins, which are essential for milk production. And fenugreek is a natural herb that has been used for centuries to support lactation.

Recipe 1: Classic Lactation Cookies

Here’s a simple recipe to get you started. These classic lactation cookies are a staple for a reason - they’re easy to make and packed with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup brewer’s yeast
  • 1/4 cup fenugreek powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the oats, flour, brown sugar, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the honey, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until a dough forms.
  5. Fold in the chopped walnuts and dried cranberries.
  6. Drop the dough by tablespoonfuls onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind when making lactation cookies:

  • Make sure to use high-quality ingredients, including fresh brewer’s yeast and fenugreek powder.
  • Don’t overbake the cookies - they should be lightly golden and still slightly soft in the center.
  • Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days.
  • Freeze the cookies for up to 2 months and thaw at room temperature when you’re ready to enjoy them.

Conclusion

Lactation cookies are a delicious and effective way to support your breastfeeding journey. With these three recipes, you’ll be well on your way to making a batch of cookies that will become a staple in your household. Remember to always use high-quality ingredients and to store the cookies properly to ensure they stay fresh and effective. Happy baking!
