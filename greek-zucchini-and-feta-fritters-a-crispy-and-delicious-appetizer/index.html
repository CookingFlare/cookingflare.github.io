Greek Zucchini and Feta Fritters: A Crispy and Delicious Appetizer

3 min · 540 words

If you’re looking for a tasty appetizer that combines the freshness of zucchini with the richness of feta cheese, then look no further than Greek Zucchini and Feta Fritters. These crispy little bites are packed with flavor and make the perfect start to any meal or party.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium zucchinis, grated
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Olive oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. Start by grating the zucchinis and placing them in a colander. Sprinkle with salt and let them sit for about 10 minutes to draw out the excess moisture. After 10 minutes, squeeze out as much liquid as possible from the grated zucchinis using your hands or a clean kitchen towel.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated zucchinis, crumbled feta cheese, chopped dill, chopped mint, sliced green onions, and minced garlic. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Add the all-purpose flour, beaten eggs, and black pepper to the bowl. Stir everything together until a thick batter forms. The batter should be sticky but still manageable.

  4. Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, drop spoonfuls of the zucchini and feta batter into the skillet, flattening them slightly with the back of the spoon. Cook the fritters for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they turn golden brown and crispy.

  5. Once the fritters are cooked, transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, adding more oil to the skillet as needed.

  6. Serve the Greek Zucchini and Feta Fritters warm as an appetizer or a side dish. They can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a tangy tzatziki sauce for dipping. The fritters are best served immediately while still hot and crispy.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier alternative, you can bake the fritters instead of frying them. Simply preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and place the fritters on a lined baking sheet. Bake for about 15-20 minutes, flipping them halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor of the fritters. Some popular additions include parsley, oregano, or even a pinch of chili flakes for a hint of heat.
  • If you’re not a fan of feta cheese, you can substitute it with grated Parmesan or crumbled goat cheese for a different twist on the fritters.
  • These fritters can also be enjoyed as a vegetarian main course. Serve them on a bed of fresh greens or alongside a Greek salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

So there you have it, a simple and delicious recipe for Greek Zucchini and Feta Fritters. These crispy appetizers are sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more. Enjoy the combination of the mild zucchini and the tangy feta cheese, perfectly balanced with the freshness of herbs. Whether you’re hosting a party or just looking for a quick snack, give these fritters a try and let your taste buds dance with delight!
