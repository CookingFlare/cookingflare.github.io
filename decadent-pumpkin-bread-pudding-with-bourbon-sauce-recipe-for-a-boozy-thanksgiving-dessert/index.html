Decadent Pumpkin Bread Pudding with Bourbon Sauce Recipe for a Boozy Thanksgiving Dessert


Thanksgiving is a time for indulgence, and what better way to satisfy your sweet tooth than with a decadent pumpkin bread pudding? This boozy dessert is the perfect addition to your holiday spread, combining the warm flavors of pumpkin, cinnamon, and nutmeg with a rich and creamy bourbon sauce. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or simply craving a festive treat, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of day-old French bread, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of canned pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of raisins (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole milk, heavy cream, canned pumpkin puree, granulated sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and salt. Whisk until well combined.

  3. Add the cubed French bread to the bowl and gently toss until every piece is coated with the pumpkin mixture. Allow the bread to soak for about 15 minutes, so it absorbs the flavors.

  4. If desired, fold in the raisins for an extra burst of sweetness and texture.

  5. Transfer the bread mixture into the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the pudding is set and golden brown on top.

  7. While the bread pudding is baking, prepare the bourbon sauce.

Bourbon Sauce

  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup of packed light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of bourbon whiskey
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • Pinch of salt

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the heavy cream, packed light brown sugar, bourbon whiskey, unsalted butter, and a pinch of salt. Stir continuously until the sugar has dissolved and the sauce is smooth and creamy.

  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the sauce cool slightly before serving.

Serving

Once the bread pudding is out of the oven, allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing it into generous portions. Drizzle each serving with a generous amount of the bourbon sauce, allowing it to seep into the warm bread pudding.

For an extra touch of indulgence, you can serve the bread pudding with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The creamy, spiced pumpkin bread pudding combined with the rich and boozy bourbon sauce is a match made in dessert heaven.

So, this Thanksgiving, treat yourself and your loved ones to a truly decadent dessert. The pumpkin bread pudding with bourbon sauce will not only satisfy your sweet cravings but also add a touch of warmth and festivity to your holiday celebration. Enjoy!

Note: This recipe serves approximately 8-10 people, so adjust the quantities accordingly if you’re serving a larger or smaller crowd.
