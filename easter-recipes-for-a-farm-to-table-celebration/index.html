Easter Recipes for a Farm-to-Table Celebration


Easter is a time for renewal, rebirth, and gathering around the table with family and friends. It’s also an excellent opportunity to celebrate the changing season with fresh, local ingredients that offer vibrant flavors and nourishing meals. This year, why not host a farm-to-table Easter celebration that highlights the best of spring’s bounty? From savory dishes to indulgent desserts, here’s a curated collection of Easter recipes that bring the farm to your table in the most delicious way possible.

The Essence of Farm-to-Table Cooking

Farm-to-table cooking is all about connecting with the land and the seasons. It’s about using fresh, seasonal ingredients that are sourced directly from local farms, ensuring the highest quality and flavor in every bite. When you bring farm-to-table dishes into your Easter celebration, you’re not just making a meal – you’re making a connection to the land, the people who grow our food, and the cycles of nature.

A Fresh, Flavorful Easter Feast

Appetizers to Start the Celebration

Easter brunch or dinner can be elevated with fresh, seasonal appetizers. Here are a few ideas to kick off the feast.

1. Spring Vegetable Tart with Herb Goat Cheese

This light and savory tart is the perfect way to showcase the fresh vegetables that arrive with spring. Use a crisp, buttery pie crust or puff pastry as the base, and layer it with a creamy herb goat cheese filling. Add roasted spring vegetables like asparagus, peas, and radishes for a colorful, flavorful topping. Bake until golden and serve warm or at room temperature. It’s an impressive yet simple dish that highlights the season’s vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry or pie dough
  • 1 cup goat cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 4-5 radishes, thinly sliced
  • Olive oil for roasting
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry or pie dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. In a bowl, combine goat cheese, cream cheese, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Spread this mixture evenly on the pastry.
  4. Toss asparagus, peas, and radishes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then arrange the vegetables on top of the cheese mixture.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the pastry is golden and the vegetables are tender.

2. Deviled Eggs with Pickled Shallots

Deviled eggs are a quintessential Easter appetizer, but adding a tangy twist with pickled shallots takes this classic to the next level. The sharpness of the pickled shallots pairs wonderfully with the creamy filling of the eggs.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon pickle juice
  • 2 tablespoons pickled shallots
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the eggs for about 10 minutes, then cool and peel them.
  2. Slice the eggs in half and scoop out the yolks. Place the yolks in a bowl and mash them.
  3. Mix the mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, pickle juice, and pickled shallots with the mashed yolks until smooth.
  4. Spoon or pipe the filling into the egg whites.
  5. Sprinkle with paprika and garnish with additional pickled shallots.

Main Course: Celebrating the Heart of Easter

The main course of your Easter farm-to-table celebration should feature ingredients that honor the season. Whether you prefer lamb, ham, or a vegetarian alternative, these recipes will offer something to suit every palate.

3. Roasted Spring Lamb with Herb and Garlic Crust

Lamb is a traditional Easter dish, and when roasted with fresh herbs and garlic, it becomes a showstopper. The rich flavor of the lamb pairs perfectly with a side of roasted root vegetables or a fresh spring salad.

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless leg of lamb (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine the garlic, rosemary, thyme, lemon zest, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub this herb mixture all over the lamb.
  4. Place the lamb on a rack in a roasting pan and roast for 45-60 minutes, depending on the desired level of doneness.
  5. Let the lamb rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

4. Honey-Glazed Ham with Mustard and Herbs

A perfectly glazed ham is a crowd-pleaser for Easter dinner. This recipe uses a blend of honey, Dijon mustard, and fresh herbs to create a sweet and tangy glaze that complements the savory flavor of the ham.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in ham (about 6-8 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a small saucepan, combine honey, Dijon mustard, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Heat over medium heat until warmed and slightly thickened.
  3. Place the ham on a rack in a roasting pan. Brush half of the honey mustard glaze over the ham.
  4. Roast the ham for about 1.5-2 hours, basting with the remaining glaze every 30 minutes.
  5. Allow the ham to rest for 15 minutes before carving.

Side Dishes to Complement Your Easter Feast

No Easter meal is complete without delicious side dishes that complement the main course and highlight fresh seasonal produce. These dishes are perfect for a farm-to-table celebration.

5. Spring Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan

Asparagus is in full bloom during the spring, making it a perfect side dish for Easter. Roasting the asparagus with a drizzle of olive oil, lemon, and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese enhances its natural flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 10-12 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender.
  4. Remove from the oven, squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top, and sprinkle with lemon zest and Parmesan cheese before serving.

6. Farmhouse Mashed Potatoes with Chives and Cream

These creamy mashed potatoes are made with locally grown potatoes and enhanced with the flavor of fresh chives. The addition of cream gives the dish an indulgent richness that pairs beautifully with the rest of your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Mash with a potato masher or hand mixer.
  3. Heat the cream and butter in a small saucepan until melted, then add it to the mashed potatoes.
  4. Stir in the chives, salt, and pepper, and serve warm.

7. Spring Pea and Mint Salad

A refreshing, light salad is a perfect complement to the richness of lamb or ham. This pea and mint salad is bursting with fresh flavors, and the mint adds a surprising brightness to the sweet peas.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh peas (or frozen peas, thawed)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the peas in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then transfer them to ice water to stop the cooking process.
  2. Drain and place the peas in a bowl. Toss with olive oil, lemon juice, chopped mint, salt, and pepper.
  3. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Desserts to End the Meal on a Sweet Note

Easter is the perfect time for indulging in sweet treats. Here are a couple of farm-to-table dessert recipes that will satisfy your guests’ sweet tooth.

8. Lemon Ricotta Cheesecake with Fresh Berries

This light and tangy cheesecake is made with ricotta cheese, which gives it a creamy texture without being overly heavy. Top it with fresh, seasonal berries for a refreshing finish to your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • Zest and juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a food processor, blend ricotta, cream cheese, sugar, eggs, lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Pour the mixture into a greased springform pan and bake for 45-50 minutes, until the center is set.
  4. Allow the cheesecake to cool, then top with fresh berries before serving.

9. Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp

This warm, comforting dessert is a celebration of spring’s bounty. The tart rhubarb and sweet strawberries are perfectly balanced by a buttery oat topping. Serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 3 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, cold and cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine strawberries, rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla extract. Pour into a baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine oats, flour, brown sugar, and butter. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the dry ingredients until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the fruit and bake for 40-45 minutes, until the topping is golden and the fruit is bubbling.

Conclusion

A farm-to-table Easter celebration is a wonderful way to embrace the season and create lasting memories with loved ones. By incorporating fresh, locally sourced ingredients, you not only support sustainable farming practices but also enjoy the finest flavors that nature has to offer. Whether you’re serving a succulent roast lamb, a hearty glazed ham, or vibrant spring vegetables, these recipes will help make your Easter gathering a true celebration of the season.
