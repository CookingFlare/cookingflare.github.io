Spinach and Feta Flatbread: A Greek-inspired Delight

Flatbreads are a staple in many cuisines around the world, and the combination of crispy, crunchy texture and savory flavors is a winning combination. In this recipe, we’re taking inspiration from Greek cuisine to create a delicious Spinach and Feta Flatbread that’s perfect for a quick snack or a satisfying meal. Ingredients 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon sugar 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast 1 cup lukewarm water 2 tablespoons olive oil 1/2 cup fresh spinach leaves, chopped 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese 1 clove garlic, minced 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1/4 teaspoon black pepper Instructions Make the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Gradually add the lukewarm water, mixing until a shaggy dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size. Prepare the toppings: In a small bowl, mix together the chopped spinach, crumbled feta cheese, minced garlic, lemon juice, and black pepper. Shape the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 2-3 equal portions, depending on the size of flatbread you prefer. Roll out each portion into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick. Assemble the flatbreads: Place the dough circles on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the edges with a little water, then spoon the spinach and feta mixture onto one half of each flatbread, leaving a 1/2 inch border around the edges. Fold the other half of the flatbread over the filling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges gently to seal. Bake the flatbreads: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Bake the flatbreads for 15-20 minutes, or until they’re golden brown and crispy. Serve and enjoy: Remove the flatbreads from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve warm, perhaps with a side of hummus or tzatziki sauce for dipping. Tips and Variations For an extra crispy crust, bake the flatbreads for an additional 2-3 minutes. Substitute other types of cheese, such as ricotta or goat cheese, for the feta. Add some heat to your flatbreads by sprinkling a pinch of red pepper flakes over the spinach and feta mixture. Experiment with different herbs, such as oregano or thyme, to give the flatbreads an extra boost of flavor. Conclusion Spinach and Feta Flatbread is a delicious and easy-to-make recipe that’s perfect for a quick snack or a satisfying meal. With its crispy crust, savory filling, and tangy feta cheese, it’s a flavor combination that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the taste of Greece in the comfort of your own home! ...
