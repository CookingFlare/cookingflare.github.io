Easy Vietnamese Stir Fry Recipes to Try Tonight


When it comes to quick, flavorful, and satisfying meals, Vietnamese stir fries stand out for their vibrant ingredients, balanced flavors, and effortless preparation. Whether you’re a busy professional, a culinary enthusiast, or someone just looking for weeknight dinner ideas, these easy Vietnamese stir fry recipes can bring the zest and freshness of Southeast Asia right into your kitchen tonight.

Vietnamese cuisine is notable for its harmony of sweet, sour, salty, and umami notes, often enhanced by fresh herbs, crisp vegetables, and aromatic sauces. The stir fry technique, with its fast cooking over high heat, preserves the texture and brightness of each element while melding them into a cohesive dish.

In this post, you’ll discover several approachable Vietnamese stir fry recipes that range from classic to creative, incorporating essential ingredients like fish sauce, fresh herbs, garlic, lemongrass, and of course, the perfect protein. Prepare to be inspired for tonight’s dinner with meals that require minimal fuss but deliver maximum impact.

1. Vietnamese Lemongrass Chicken Stir Fry

Overview: Lemongrass is one of the signature flavors in Vietnamese cooking. This fragrant, slightly citrusy spice pairs beautifully with tender chicken, garlic, chili, and a touch of sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks fresh lemongrass, finely minced (white part only)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small red chili, sliced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers (mixed colors)
  • 1/2 cup sliced onions
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice or vermicelli noodles, to serve

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken slices with lemongrass, garlic, 1 tablespoon fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, and black pepper. Set aside for 15-20 minutes to marinate.

  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until browned and cooked through.

  3. Add the bell peppers, onions, and sliced chili (if using). Continue stir-frying for 2-3 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Splash in the remaining fish sauce and toss everything well. Adjust seasoning if needed.

  5. Serve right away over steamed jasmine rice or vermicelli noodles. Garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges for an added burst of brightness.

2. Classic Vietnamese Beef and Basil Stir Fry (Bò Xào Húng Quế)

Overview: This dish capitalizes on the earthy, peppery tones of Thai basil, which complements savory marinated beef perfectly. Quick to cook, it’s a fantastic weeknight meal that bursts with authentic flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 400g flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small shallot, thinly sliced
  • 1 red chili, sliced (optional)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Cooked rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Toss the beef slices with soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and cornstarch. Let marinate for about 20 minutes.

  2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add garlic, shallot, and chili; stir-fry until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  3. Add the beef in one layer and sear without stirring for 1-2 minutes, then stir-fry for another 2 minutes until cooked through.

  4. Remove from heat, stir in fresh Thai basil leaves and toss until wilted.

  5. Serve immediately with steamed rice.

3. Quick Tofu and Vegetable Vietnamese Stir Fry

Overview: This vegetarian-friendly recipe infuses all the bright, tangy flavors typical of Vietnamese food into a tofu-based dish that’s filling and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 400g firm tofu, drained and cut into cubes
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • Fresh mint and chopped peanuts for garnish
  • Cooked rice or rice noodles, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add cubed tofu and pan-fry until golden brown on all sides. Remove and set aside.

  2. In the same pan, add garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.

  3. Add broccoli, carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are crisp-tender.

  4. Mix soy sauce, hoisin, rice vinegar, and brown sugar in a small bowl. Pour over vegetables and add tofu back into the skillet.

  5. Stir well to coat everything evenly and cook for another 2 minutes.

  6. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and chopped peanuts before serving over rice or noodles.

4. Vietnamese Shrimp and Pineapple Stir Fry

Overview: This stir fry plays with sweet, tangy pineapple and succulent shrimp, balanced by fish sauce and chili for a vibrant, tropical taste experience.

Ingredients:

  • 400g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 red chili, chopped (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp lime juice
  • Fresh basil or cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat. Add garlic and chili, stir-fry until fragrant.

  2. Toss in shrimp and cook until pink, about 2 minutes per side.

  3. Add onions and pineapple chunks, stir-fry for another 2 minutes.

  4. Pour in fish sauce, brown sugar, and lime juice. Toss everything well and cook for 1 more minute.

  5. Remove from heat, garnish with herbs and serve with hot jasmine rice.

Tips for Perfect Vietnamese Stir Fries Every Time

  • Prep Ahead: Vietnamese stir fries cook fast, so make sure all your ingredients are prepared before you start cooking.
  • High Heat: Use a wok or large skillet over high heat for quick searing, which ensures crisp textures and caramelized flavors.
  • Fresh Herbs: Don’t skip fresh herbs like cilantro, mint, or Thai basil; they are the finishing touch that elevates the dish.
  • Balancing Flavors: Never be afraid to adjust the balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy to suit your taste buds.
  • Rice or Noodles: Most Vietnamese stir fries shine when served with simple jasmine rice or delicate rice vermicelli, soaking up the flavorful sauces.

Wrapping Up

Vietnamese stir fry recipes offer an impressive array of tastes and textures while requiring relatively little time and skill to prepare. From lemongrass chicken to tofu with crisp vegetables, these dishes bring a taste of Vietnam’s culinary magic to your dinner table tonight.

Each recipe captures the essence of Vietnamese cooking: a delightful balance of fresh herbs, vibrant produce, and bold, harmonious flavors. Try these recipes for an easy, tasty, and authentic dinner that can quickly become a regular in your meal rotation. Bon appétit, or should we say, chúc ngon miệng!
