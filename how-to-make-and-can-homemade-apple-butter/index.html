How to Make and Can Homemade Apple Butter


Apple butter is a classic autumn treat that embodies the essence of fall. With its rich, spiced flavor and smooth, velvety texture, it’s the perfect accompaniment to a wide range of foods—spread it on toast, swirl it into oatmeal, or even use it as a glaze for meats. Making apple butter at home allows you to customize the sweetness and spice to your liking, and canning it ensures you can enjoy this delicious spread all year long.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the process of making homemade apple butter from scratch and show you how to can it so you can store it for the months ahead. Whether you’re new to canning or an experienced home cook, this guide will provide you with all the information you need.

Why Make Homemade Apple Butter?

Before we dive into the recipe, you might be wondering why you should make apple butter at home. Here are a few reasons to consider:

  1. Freshness and Quality: Homemade apple butter allows you to choose the best apples in season, ensuring a fresh, flavorful product free from any artificial preservatives or unnecessary additives.

  2. Customization: When making apple butter yourself, you have complete control over the spices and sweetness level. Adjust the cinnamon, nutmeg, or sugar to your taste.

  3. Long Shelf Life: Canning your homemade apple butter allows you to store it for up to a year, providing you with a convenient and delicious treat long after apple season ends.

Now that we’ve established why homemade apple butter is worth making, let’s dive into the steps.

Ingredients for Homemade Apple Butter

The beauty of apple butter is its simplicity. You don’t need a lot of ingredients to make a great batch. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 8 cups of apples (approximately 3 to 4 pounds) – A mix of tart and sweet apples works best. Consider using varieties like Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Jonagold.
  • 1 cup of water – To help get the apples cooking down into a soft consistency.
  • 1 cup of sugar – You can adjust the sweetness depending on your preference. You can also substitute this with honey or maple syrup for a more natural sweetener.
  • 1 tablespoon of cinnamon – The star spice that gives apple butter its comforting, autumnal flavor.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves – This adds a warm, aromatic depth.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg – A dash of nutmeg brings a lovely, subtle warmth to the butter.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract – This is optional, but it can add a hint of sweetness and complexity to the flavor.

Optional Variations:

If you want to add a twist to your apple butter, consider some of these optional ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice – This helps balance the sweetness and adds a little tartness to the flavor.
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground allspice – For an additional layer of warmth and spice.
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar – For a richer, deeper sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup of apple cider – If you prefer a more intense apple flavor, you can substitute the water with apple cider.

Equipment You’ll Need

Before starting, make sure you have the following equipment on hand:

  • Large pot or Dutch oven – For cooking the apples.
  • Food processor or immersion blender – To puree the apples into a smooth consistency.
  • Canning jars and lids – To store the finished apple butter.
  • Large canning pot – For the water bath canning process.
  • Jar lifter or tongs – To safely remove the hot jars from the canning pot.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Apple Butter

Step 1: Prepare the Apples

Start by washing the apples thoroughly. Next, peel, core, and chop them into small chunks. You can use a combination of apple varieties to achieve the desired balance of sweet and tart flavors. If you’re using a food mill later, you don’t need to worry about peeling the apples, as the mill will separate the skins from the flesh.

Step 2: Cook the Apples

Place the chopped apples into a large pot or Dutch oven and add 1 cup of water. Cook the apples over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until they begin to soften. This should take about 10 to 15 minutes. Once the apples are soft and tender, you can proceed to the next step.

Step 3: Puree the Apples

Once the apples are fully softened, it’s time to puree them. You can either use an immersion blender directly in the pot or transfer the apples to a food processor. Blend the apples until they form a smooth, thick puree.

If you used an immersion blender, be sure to scrape the bottom of the pot to avoid any chunks of apples that didn’t break down. A smooth consistency is key to creating that signature apple butter texture.

Step 4: Add the Sweeteners and Spices

Now that you have your apple puree, it’s time to season it. Add 1 cup of sugar (or your chosen sweetener), 1 tablespoon of cinnamon, 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves, 1/2 teaspoon of nutmeg, and any optional ingredients you’d like to include (such as vanilla or lemon juice).

Stir well to incorporate the spices evenly into the mixture. If you want your apple butter to be sweeter, feel free to adjust the sugar. You can always taste and add more as needed.

Step 5: Simmer the Apple Butter

Once all the ingredients are mixed in, bring the apple butter to a simmer over low heat. Continue to cook the mixture, stirring occasionally, for 1 to 2 hours. This slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together and the apple butter to thicken to the desired consistency.

During this time, keep an eye on the apple butter and stir it frequently to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot. If the mixture becomes too thick and starts to scorch, you can add a little more water or apple cider to loosen it up.

Step 6: Test the Consistency

To test if your apple butter has reached the right consistency, drop a small spoonful onto a plate. Let it cool for a minute, then run your finger through it. If the apple butter leaves a clean line and doesn’t immediately run back together, it’s ready for canning.

If it’s still too thin, continue cooking for a little longer. If it’s too thick, add a splash of water or cider to thin it out.

Step 7: Can the Apple Butter

Once the apple butter has reached the perfect consistency, it’s time to can it. Start by sterilizing your canning jars and lids. To do this, place them in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes. Make sure the jars are heated through before filling them with the hot apple butter.

Use a ladle to fill each jar with the apple butter, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue that could interfere with the sealing process.

Place the sterilized lids on top of each jar and screw on the metal bands, ensuring they’re snug but not too tight.

Step 8: Process the Jars in a Water Bath

Fill a large canning pot with water, ensuring that the jars are fully submerged once placed in the pot. Bring the water to a boil, then process the jars for 10 to 15 minutes. The water should be boiling vigorously during this time.

Once the jars have been processed, use a jar lifter or tongs to carefully remove them from the water. Place them on a clean towel and let them cool completely. You should hear a “pop” as the lids seal. If any jars don’t seal, you can refrigerate those and use them within a few weeks.

Step 9: Store and Enjoy

Once your jars are sealed and cooled, they’re ready for storage. Store your canned apple butter in a cool, dark place for up to one year. Once opened, keep the jar in the refrigerator and consume it within a few weeks.

Apple butter is incredibly versatile. Spread it on toast, swirl it into yogurt or oatmeal, or use it as a glaze for meats like pork or chicken. The possibilities are endless!

Tips for Perfect Apple Butter Every Time

  • Choose the Right Apples: The type of apples you use will affect the flavor of your apple butter. A combination of sweet and tart apples is ideal. Try varieties like Honeycrisp, Granny Smith, or Golden Delicious.

  • Cook Low and Slow: The key to great apple butter is slow cooking. This helps the flavors develop and the apples break down into a smooth, spreadable texture. Be patient and let the mixture simmer for a couple of hours.

  • Adjust the Sweetness: Depending on the sweetness of your apples, you may need to adjust the sugar. Taste the apple butter as it cooks and add more sweetener if necessary.

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Jars: When filling your jars, be sure to leave about 1/4 inch of headspace to allow for expansion during the canning process.

Final Thoughts

Making and canning your own homemade apple butter is a rewarding and simple process that yields delicious results. With the right apples, a bit of patience, and a little creativity, you can make a batch of apple butter that is perfectly spiced and perfectly sweet. Canning it ensures that you can enjoy the flavors of fall all year long.

Whether you’re a canning novice or a seasoned expert, this homemade apple butter recipe will become a cherished staple in your kitchen. Happy canning!
