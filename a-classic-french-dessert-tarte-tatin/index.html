A Classic French Dessert: Tarte Tatin


If you’re a fan of classic French desserts, you’re in for a treat with this delightful Tarte Tatin recipe. Originating from the heart of France, this apple upside-down tart has become a timeless favorite around the world. Its buttery, caramelized apples and flaky pastry make it a truly irresistible dessert. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the steps to create your very own Tarte Tatin that will impress your guests and leave them craving for more.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6-8 large apples, such as Granny Smith or Braeburn
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pastry

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or fork to blend the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Form it into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Prepare the Apples

  1. Peel, core, and slice the apples into quarters. You can also choose to leave the skin on for added texture and color.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Add the granulated sugar and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sugar caramelizes and turns a deep amber color.
  3. Add the vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and a pinch of salt to the caramel, then carefully arrange the apple slices on top in a circular pattern. Cook for about 10-15 minutes until the apples have softened but still hold their shape.

Step 3: Assemble the Tarte Tatin

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled pastry dough on a lightly floured surface to fit the size of your skillet. Carefully place the pastry over the caramelized apples and tuck in the edges around the apples.
  3. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crisp.
  4. Remove the skillet from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before inverting it onto a serving plate. Be cautious as the caramel will be very hot.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Slice the Tarte Tatin into wedges and serve it warm, preferably with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The combination of the buttery pastry, sweet caramelized apples, and a hint of cinnamon will surely delight your taste buds.

Conclusion

Tarte Tatin is a timeless dessert that embodies the elegance and simplicity of French cuisine. Its rustic charm and rich flavors make it a perfect way to end any meal, whether you’re entertaining guests or simply indulging in a sweet treat for yourself. With this recipe, you can master the art of creating this classic dessert and savor the joy of French baking in your own kitchen.

So, why not channel your inner pastry chef and whip up a Tarte Tatin? Bon appétit!
