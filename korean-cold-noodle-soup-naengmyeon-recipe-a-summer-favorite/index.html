Korean Cold Noodle Soup (Naengmyeon) Recipe: A Summer Favorite


When the sun blazes and the air feels heavy with summer heat, there’s nothing more refreshing than a chilled bowl of Korean Cold Noodle Soup, or Naengmyeon. This traditional dish has long been a staple of Korean cuisine, a beloved meal that combines flavor, texture, and a much-needed cooling effect on a hot day. Originating in North Korea, particularly in the regions of Pyongyang and Hamhung, Naengmyeon has grown to become a popular dish worldwide. Let’s dive into its unique flavors and learn how you can make this iconic dish at home.

What Makes Naengmyeon So Special?

Naengmyeon is more than just a dish—it’s a celebration of balance. Its star ingredient is the noodles, commonly made of buckwheat, sweet potato starch, or arrowroot powder. These noodles provide a chewy, elastic texture that holds up well in the icy broth. While traditional Naengmyeon uses a tangy, slightly acidic base flavored with vinegar and mustard, variations abound to suit different tastes.

There are two primary styles of Naengmyeon: Mul Naengmyeon (in a cold broth) and Bibim Naengmyeon (mixed with a spicy red chile paste). The former, often served with an icy, beef-based broth, is perfect for beating the summer heat. Bibim Naengmyeon, on the other hand, is a fiery version that offers a spicier kick.

Today, we’ll be focusing on crafting a bowl of Mul Naengmyeon. The chilled broth made with beef and radish complements the slurp-worthy noodles, while toppings like crunchy cucumbers, pickled radish, and half a boiled egg add flavor and depth.

Ingredients at a Glance

Here’s what you’ll need to prepare a classic bowl of Naengmyeon. While the recipe requires a few specialty ingredients, many can easily be found at your local Asian grocery store or purchased online.

Noodles:

  • 1 package of Naengmyeon noodles (buckwheat or sweet potato starch)

Broth:

  • 8 cups of water
  • 350 grams (¾ pound) beef brisket or shank
  • 1 Korean radish (mu), peeled and sliced into large pieces
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 piece of ginger (2-inch), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • Salt to taste
  • Ice cubes for serving

Garnishes and Toppings:

  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 Korean pear (or Asian pear), thinly sliced
  • 2 boiled eggs, halved
  • Pickled radish (optional, for tanginess)
  • Yellow mustard paste (optional, for a kick)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Recipe for Korean Cold Noodle Soup

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

The broth forms the foundation of this dish, so it’s important to give it some care.

  1. Fill a large pot with the 8 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Add the beef brisket or shank, sliced radish, onion, garlic, and ginger. Simmer gently on low heat for about 90 minutes. Skim off any impure foam that rises to the surface.

  2. After 90 minutes, remove the beef and set it aside to cool. Strain the broth through a fine sieve into a large bowl, discarding the solids. Let the broth cool to room temperature, then season it with soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, and salt to taste.

  3. Refrigerate the broth for at least 2-3 hours or until thoroughly cold. Alternatively, you can place it in the freezer for about 30-40 minutes if you’re short on time.

Step 2: Cook the Noodles

Naengmyeon noodles can be somewhat tricky to cook, given their unique texture. Here’s how to nail it.

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the Naengmyeon noodles, stirring gently to prevent them from clumping together. Cook for about 2-3 minutes or as instructed on the package.

  2. Once the noodles are tender yet chewy, drain them immediately and shock them in cold water. Rinse the noodles several times in icy water, using your hands to rub away excess starch. This step ensures the noodles are cold and springy.

  3. Drain thoroughly and portion the noodles into serving bowls, forming neat mounds in the center.

Step 3: Assemble the Dish

Now it’s time to bring it all together.

  1. Retrieve your chilled broth from the refrigerator. Add a few ice cubes to keep it extra cold.

  2. Pour the cold broth over the noodles, adding just enough to submerge them.

  3. Arrange your toppings. Place a few slices of boiled beef on one side of the noodles, julienned cucumber on another, and pickled radish (if using) on a third.

  4. Gently place the halved boiled egg on top of the noodles. Add a few slices of Korean pear for a sweet crunch. For an extra flourish, sprinkle sesame seeds or serve with a dollop of mustard paste on the side.

Tips for Perfecting Your Naengmyeon

  • Customize the flavor: Mul Naengmyeon is all about balance, so feel free to adjust the vinegar, sugar, and soy sauce to suit your taste preferences. Some people prefer extra tang, while others enjoy a sweeter profile.

  • Experiment with toppings: The classic toppings are beef, cucumber, pear, and egg, but you can also add watermelon slices for a summery twist or additional pickled vegetables for extra boldness.

  • Use quality noodles: Authentic Naengmyeon noodles boast a chewy texture that’s hard to replicate with substitutes. Look for brands that specialize in Korean noodles for the best results.

  • Pre-chill everything: Cold noodles and broth are only as good as their temperature. Keep your serving bowls in the fridge and ensure all ingredients are thoroughly cold before assembling.

Why Naengmyeon is the Ultimate Summer Dish

There’s no denying that this chilled noodle soup is a summertime savior. Its tart, sweet, and savory broth is invigorating and light, offering a perfect contrast to the rich, meaty flavors of Korean barbecue or grilled dishes. The cold temperature not only refreshes the palate but also keeps you cool and hydrated during scorching days.

Moreover, Naengmyeon is a meal that invites creativity. From swapping out beef for seafood to playing with spice levels, there’s room to make it your own. Whether you’re introducing it to family or friends, it’s a show-stopping dish that highlights the vibrant, fresh flavors of Korean cuisine.

Make Naengmyeon Your Go-To Summer Recipe

With its zesty flavors and icy broth, Naengmyeon is like no other dish. While it may seem a bit intimidating at first, the recipe becomes manageable once you break it down into its components. The reward? A restaurant-quality Korean Cold Noodle Soup right in your home.

Give yourself a cooling treat this summer with a bowl of homemade Naengmyeon. Share it with loved ones, or enjoy it as a refreshing solo meal. If you’ve never tried making it before, now is the perfect time to turn this iconic dish into your next culinary triumph!
