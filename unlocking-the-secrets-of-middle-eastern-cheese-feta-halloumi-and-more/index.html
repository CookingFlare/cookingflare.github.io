Unlocking the Secrets of Middle Eastern Cheese: Feta, Halloumi, and More


If you’re a cheese lover, then Middle Eastern cheeses should definitely be on your radar. These cheeses are not only delicious but also have a rich history and a unique flavor profile that sets them apart from their Western counterparts. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the secrets of Middle Eastern cheese, focusing on two popular varieties: Feta and Halloumi. So, let’s jump right in and unlock the secrets of these tantalizing cheeses!

Feta: The Pride of Greece

Feta cheese, a staple in Greek cuisine, is known for its crumbly yet creamy texture and tangy flavor. Made from sheep’s milk or a blend of sheep’s and goat’s milk, feta cheese has a distinct and robust taste that can elevate any dish it’s added to.

How to Make Feta Cheese at Home

Making feta cheese at home is easier than you might think. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 gallon of sheep’s milk or a blend of sheep’s and goat’s milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon of mesophilic starter culture
  • 1/4 teaspoon of liquid calcium chloride (if using pasteurized milk)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of liquid rennet
  • Cheese salt

Instructions:

  1. Heat the milk in a large pot over medium heat until it reaches 86째F (30째C).
  2. Sprinkle the mesophilic starter culture over the milk and let it dissolve for a minute.
  3. If using pasteurized milk, add the liquid calcium chloride and stir well.
  4. Cover the pot and let the milk ripen for 1 hour.
  5. Dilute the liquid rennet in 1/4 cup of cool, non-chlorinated water.
  6. Stir the rennet solution into the milk gently for 1 minute.
  7. Cover the pot and let the milk set for 1-2 hours or until it forms a clean break.
  8. Cut the curds into 1/2 inch (1.3 cm) cubes.
  9. Let the curds rest for 5 minutes, then stir them gently for 10 minutes.
  10. Slowly heat the curds to 105째F (40째C) over 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  11. Maintain the temperature for 30 minutes, stirring gently.
  12. Ladle the curds into a cheesecloth-lined colander and let them drain for 6-8 hours.
  13. Sprinkle cheese salt over the drained curds and mix gently.
  14. Transfer the curds to a cheese mold and press them at 10 pounds (4.5 kg) for 12 hours.
  15. Remove the cheese from the mold, slice, and enjoy!

Delicious Ways to Enjoy Feta Cheese

Feta cheese is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in various dishes. Here are a few ideas to get your creative juices flowing:

  • Crumble feta over a Greek salad for a burst of tangy flavor.
  • Stuff grilled bell peppers with feta and herbs for a tasty appetizer.
  • Bake feta with tomatoes, olives, and herbs for a warm and comforting dip.
  • Add feta to your favorite pasta dish for a creamy and savory twist.

Halloumi: The Grilling Champion

Halloumi cheese, originating from Cyprus, is a true delight for cheese enthusiasts. What makes halloumi unique is its high melting point, which allows it to be grilled or fried without losing its shape. This makes it perfect for summer barbecues and adds a delightful charred flavor to your dishes.

Grilled Halloumi Skewers with Vegetables

For a simple yet delicious halloumi dish, try these grilled halloumi skewers with vegetables:

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225 grams) of halloumi cheese, cut into cubes
  • Assorted vegetables of your choice (bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, etc.)
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh herbs (such as mint or basil)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the halloumi cheese and vegetables onto skewers, alternating between them.
  3. Drizzle the skewers with olive oil and lemon juice.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and your choice of fresh herbs.
  5. Grill the skewers for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the cheese is golden and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Remove from the grill and serve immediately.
