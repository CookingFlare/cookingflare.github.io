Turkish Lokum Delights: 7 Easy Recipes to Try


Turkish Lokum, also known as Turkish delight, is a beloved sweet treat that has been enchanting taste buds for centuries. Originating from Turkey, this gelatinous confection is known for its soft, chewy texture and vibrant flavors. Whether enjoyed during special occasions, shared with family and friends, or simply savored with a cup of Turkish tea, lokum holds a special place in the hearts of many around the world.

While the traditional rose-flavored Turkish delight is the most iconic version, there are endless variations to explore. These recipes are not only delicious but also easy to make with ingredients that are simple to find. Let’s dive into the sweet world of Turkish lokum and discover how you can bring this iconic dessert to your home.

1. Classic Turkish Lokum (Rose-Flavored)

The classic Turkish delight is fragrant and delicately flavored with rosewater. This is the recipe most people think of when they imagine Turkish lokum.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid
  • 1 tablespoon rosewater
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves completely. Once the sugar is dissolved, bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the cornstarch and 1 1/2 cups of cold water, ensuring there are no lumps.
  3. Slowly add the cornstarch mixture to the sugar syrup, stirring continuously. Once combined, cook the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly until it thickens, which should take about 30 minutes.
  4. Stir in the citric acid, rosewater, and lemon juice. Continue stirring until the mixture is smooth and thick, almost like a gelatinous consistency.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased square or rectangular mold, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  6. Let the lokum set at room temperature for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  7. Once the lokum has set, dust it generously with powdered sugar. Cut it into small cubes and enjoy.

This classic rose-flavored Turkish delight is perfect for those who love a floral, aromatic treat. Its melt-in-the-mouth texture is sure to impress anyone who tries it.

2. Pomegranate and Walnut Turkish Lokum

Pomegranate and walnut Turkish lokum adds a rich, tangy twist to the traditional recipe. The combination of the sweet and tart pomegranate with the crunchy texture of walnuts creates a unique and satisfying treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Stir over medium heat until the sugar dissolves, then bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, mix the cornstarch with 1 1/2 cups of cold water.
  3. Add the cornstarch mixture to the syrup slowly, stirring continuously. Cook the mixture over low heat until it thickens, which should take around 25-30 minutes.
  4. Once thickened, stir in the citric acid, pomegranate juice, and lemon juice.
  5. Add the chopped walnuts and stir to distribute evenly throughout the mixture.
  6. Pour the lokum mixture into a greased mold, smoothing the surface with a spatula.
  7. Let it set for 6-8 hours or overnight at room temperature.
  8. Once set, dust the lokum with powdered sugar, cut it into pieces, and enjoy the combination of pomegranate and walnuts.

This variation provides a wonderful balance of sweetness and tanginess, with the added crunch from the walnuts making each bite a delightful experience.

3. Lemon and Mint Turkish Lokum

If you prefer refreshing flavors, lemon and mint Turkish lokum is the perfect treat for you. The zesty lemon pairs beautifully with the fresh mint, creating a crisp and invigorating flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Stir over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved, then bring the mixture to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix cornstarch with 1 1/2 cups of cold water until smooth.
  3. Slowly pour the cornstarch mixture into the syrup while stirring continuously. Continue to cook over low heat until the mixture thickens (about 30 minutes).
  4. Add the citric acid, lemon juice, and chopped mint leaves to the thickened mixture. Stir well to incorporate.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased mold and smooth the top.
  6. Allow the lokum to set at room temperature for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  7. Once it has set, dust with powdered sugar, cut into cubes, and enjoy the refreshing lemon and mint flavor.

This recipe is perfect for summer and offers a light, refreshing twist on the classic Turkish delight.

4. Chocolate Turkish Lokum

For those who canâ€™t resist the allure of chocolate, this Turkish delight recipe is for you. Rich, decadent, and smooth, chocolate Turkish lokum is a delightful treat for chocolate lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid

  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  2. In a bowl, mix the cornstarch with 1 1/2 cups of cold water.
  3. Gradually add the cornstarch mixture to the syrup, stirring continuously until the mixture thickens, about 25 minutes.
  4. Add the citric acid, cocoa powder, and vanilla extract, stirring until everything is well combined.
  5. Pour the thickened mixture into a greased mold and smooth the surface.
  6. Let the lokum set for 6-8 hours or overnight at room temperature.
  7. Once set, dust with powdered sugar and cut it into small squares.

The rich cocoa flavor combined with the traditional lokum texture makes for a luxurious and indulgent treat.

5. Pistachio Turkish Lokum

Pistachios are a popular ingredient in Turkish desserts, and they make a fantastic addition to Turkish delight. This recipe offers the perfect balance of creamy sweetness and nutty crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved, then bring it to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Mix the cornstarch with 1 1/2 cups of cold water in a bowl.
  3. Slowly add the cornstarch mixture to the sugar syrup while stirring constantly. Continue to cook on low heat for about 30 minutes, until the mixture thickens.
  4. Stir in the citric acid and chopped pistachios, mixing well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased mold and smooth the top.
  6. Allow the lokum to set for 6-8 hours or overnight at room temperature.
  7. Once set, dust with powdered sugar, cut into squares, and enjoy the nutty goodness of pistachio lokum.

This pistachio version is a delightful treat with its earthy, nutty flavor that complements the sweetness of the Turkish delight.

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water

  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid

  4. Stir in the citric acid and orange blossom water.

  6. Allow it to set for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  7. Dust with powdered sugar, cut into cubes, and enjoy the fragrant orange blossom flavor.

The citrusy fragrance and flavor of orange blossom water add a refreshing note to this traditional sweet.

7. Saffron and Almond Turkish Lokum

For an exotic twist, saffron and almond Turkish delight offers a luxurious combination of spices and flavors. Saffron adds a touch of warmth, while almonds provide a delightful crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon citric acid
  • 1 tablespoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, dissolve the sugar in water over medium heat. Bring to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Soak the saffron threads in a tablespoon of warm water for about 10 minutes, then add the saffron infusion to the sugar syrup.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix cornstarch with 1 1/2 cups of cold water.
  4. Slowly add the cornstarch mixture to the syrup, stirring continuously until the mixture thickens.
  5. Stir in the citric acid and chopped almonds.
  6. Pour the mixture into a greased mold and smooth the top.
  7. Let it set for 6-8 hours or overnight at room temperature.
  8. Dust with powdered sugar, cut into cubes, and enjoy the luxurious saffron and almond flavors.

The vibrant color of saffron combined with the richness of almonds creates a unique and indulgent Turkish delight.
