Hearty Split Pea Soup: A Timeless Favorite


There’s something comforting about a warm bowl of soup on a chilly day. And when it comes to hearty and satisfying soups, few can beat the classic split pea soup. This timeless favorite has been enjoyed by generations, and for good reason. Packed with nutrients and bursting with flavor, it’s a dish that never fails to please.

Ingredients

To make this delicious split pea soup, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups dried split peas
  • 1 ham bone or 2 cups diced ham
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by rinsing the split peas under cold water. This will help remove any dirt or debris.

  2. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant.

  3. Add the ham bone or diced ham to the pot. This will give the soup a rich and savory flavor. If using a ham bone, make sure to remove any excess fat.

  4. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, along with the rinsed split peas. Stir well to combine.

  5. Add the bay leaves and dried thyme to the pot. These herbs will infuse the soup with a wonderful aroma.

  6. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the split peas are soft and tender.

  7. Once the soup is cooked, remove the bay leaves and ham bone (if using). If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use an immersion blender to puree the soup partially. This step is optional, as some people prefer a chunkier texture.

  8. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to taste as you go and adjust the seasoning accordingly.

  9. Serve the split pea soup hot, garnished with some fresh herbs or a dollop of sour cream.

Tips and Variations

  • If you’re vegetarian or prefer a meatless version, you can omit the ham bone and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. The soup will still be flavorful and delicious.

  • Feel free to customize the soup by adding other vegetables such as potatoes or bell peppers. You can also experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste.

  • Leftover split pea soup can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well, so you can make a big batch and save some for later.

  • To make the soup even heartier, you can serve it with a side of crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich.

Conclusion

Hearty split pea soup is a classic dish that stands the test of time. With its rich flavors and nourishing ingredients, it’s the perfect comfort food for any occasion. Whether you’re craving a warm bowl of soup on a cold winter night or simply want to enjoy a wholesome and satisfying meal, this timeless favorite won’t disappoint. So gather your ingredients, get cooking, and savor the goodness of homemade split pea soup.
