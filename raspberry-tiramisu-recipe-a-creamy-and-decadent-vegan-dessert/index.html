Raspberry Tiramisu Recipe: A Creamy and Decadent Vegan Dessert


Are you a fan of the classic Italian dessert, Tiramisu, but follow a vegan lifestyle? Well, this Raspberry Tiramisu recipe is here to satisfy your cravings! With its creamy, luscious layers and the burst of tartness from fresh raspberries, this dessert is sure to become a favorite among vegans and non-vegans alike.

Ingredients

For the Raspberry Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Cashew Cream:

  • 2 cups raw cashews, soaked overnight
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Coffee-Soaked Ladyfingers:

  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 12 vegan ladyfingers

For Assembly:

  • Fresh raspberries, for garnish
  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Start by making the raspberry sauce. In a blender, combine the fresh raspberries, maple syrup, and lemon juice. Blend until smooth. Set aside.

  2. Drain and rinse the soaked cashews. In a blender, combine the cashews, coconut cream, maple syrup, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth and creamy. Set aside.

  3. In a shallow dish, combine the brewed coffee and maple syrup. Dip each vegan ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, making sure to coat both sides. Place the soaked ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of a rectangular or square dish.

  4. Spread half of the cashew cream over the soaked ladyfingers, ensuring an even layer. Drizzle half of the raspberry sauce over the cream.

  5. Repeat the layers with the remaining soaked ladyfingers, cashew cream, and raspberry sauce.

  6. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to meld together and the dessert to set.

  7. When ready to serve, remove the plastic wrap and garnish the top of the tiramisu with fresh raspberries. Dust with cocoa powder for an extra touch of indulgence.

  8. Slice and serve chilled, savoring each decadent bite of this vegan twist on the classic Tiramisu.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have fresh raspberries, you can use frozen raspberries instead. Just thaw them before making the sauce.
  • Feel free to experiment with different fruit sauces. Strawberry or blueberry sauce would be equally delicious in this recipe.
  • For a nut-free version, you can substitute the cashews with silken tofu or coconut cream.
  • If you prefer a sweeter dessert, you can increase the amount of maple syrup in both the cashew cream and the raspberry sauce.

Enjoy this Raspberry Tiramisu as a delightful ending to any meal or as a special treat for a celebration. Its creamy texture, the tangy sweetness of raspberries, and the rich flavors will have you coming back for seconds. And the best part? It’s entirely vegan, so you can indulge guilt-free!

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients and get ready to create a show-stopping dessert that will impress everyone, regardless of their dietary preferences. Let the Raspberry Tiramisu be the star of your next gathering and leave your guests wanting more!
