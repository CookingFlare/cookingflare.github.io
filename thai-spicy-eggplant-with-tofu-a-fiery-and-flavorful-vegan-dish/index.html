Thai Spicy Eggplant with Tofu: A Fiery and Flavorful Vegan Dish


If you’re looking to add some heat and excitement to your vegan cooking repertoire, look no further than this Thai Spicy Eggplant with Tofu recipe. Bursting with bold flavors and vibrant colors, this dish is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized eggplants, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 red chili peppers, thinly sliced (adjust according to your spice tolerance)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamari
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon coconut sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, mixed with 2 tablespoons of water
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced chili peppers, and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the tofu cubes to the skillet and cook until they turn golden brown on all sides. This should take around 5-7 minutes. Remove the tofu from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the eggplant pieces and stir-fry for a few minutes until they start to soften. If needed, you can add a splash of water to help cook the eggplant evenly.

  4. Add the sliced bell peppers and onions to the skillet, and continue stir-frying for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, tamari, rice vinegar, and coconut sugar. Pour the sauce mixture into the skillet with the vegetables.

  6. Return the tofu cubes to the skillet and gently toss everything together, ensuring that the sauce coats all the ingredients evenly. Allow the dish to simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  7. In a separate bowl, mix the cornstarch with water to create a slurry. Slowly pour the slurry into the skillet, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens slightly.

  8. Remove the skillet from the heat and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

  9. Serve the Thai Spicy Eggplant with Tofu over steamed jasmine rice or noodles of your choice. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the dish for an extra burst of citrusy flavor.

The Perfect Vegan Delight

This Thai Spicy Eggplant with Tofu is a vegan delight that delivers on both taste and texture. The combination of spicy chili peppers, tender eggplant, and crispy tofu creates a harmonious balance that keeps you coming back for more. The soy sauce, tamari, and rice vinegar add depth and tang to the dish, while the coconut sugar brings a subtle hint of sweetness. The cornstarch slurry helps create a luscious sauce that beautifully coats the ingredients.

Not only is this dish bursting with flavor, but it’s also packed with nutrients. Eggplants are a great source of dietary fiber, while tofu provides a healthy dose of plant-based protein. The colorful bell peppers add a vibrant touch and are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

So, if you’re in the mood for a fiery and flavorful vegan dish that will tantalize your taste buds, give this Thai Spicy Eggplant with Tofu recipe a try. It’s the perfect balance of heat, spice, and everything nice.
