Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies: Chewy and Sweet Vegan Desserts


As the autumn leaves begin to fall, the aroma of freshly baked cookies wafts through the air, filling our senses with warmth and comfort. Among the classic cookie flavors, oatmeal raisin stands out for its unique combination of textures and tastes. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a vegan version of this beloved treat, using wholesome ingredients and clever substitutions to achieve a chewy, sweet, and utterly irresistible cookie.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter (such as Earth Balance), softened
  • 2 large eggs, replaced by 2 flax eggs*
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the oats, flour, brown sugar, and granulated sugar.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the vegan butter, flax eggs, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until a dough forms. Fold in the raisins and walnuts (if using).
  5. Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 2 inches of space between each cookie.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  7. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips and Variations

  • To enhance the oatmeal flavor, use rolled oats with a high fiber content or add a pinch of cinnamon to the dough.
  • For a crisper cookie, bake for 12-14 minutes. For a chewier cookie, bake for 8-10 minutes.
  • Substitute chopped pecans or hazelnuts for the walnuts for a different texture and flavor.
  • Add a handful of chocolate chips to the dough for a sweet surprise.

The Science Behind the Chew

The secret to achieving chewy cookies lies in the combination of ingredients and the way they interact during the baking process. The rolled oats provide a natural chewiness, while the flax eggs help to bind the dough together without adding excess moisture. The vegan butter, with its higher water content than traditional butter, contributes to a tender and soft texture. Finally, the baking time and temperature allow the cookies to retain their moisture while developing a crispy exterior.

Conclusion

Vegan oatmeal raisin cookies are a delightful treat that can be enjoyed by anyone, regardless of dietary restrictions. With their chewy texture and sweet flavor, they’re perfect for snacking on the go or serving at your next gathering. By following this recipe and experimenting with different variations, you’ll discover a world of plant-based baking possibilities that will leave you feeling satisfied and inspired. Happy baking!
