Spicy One-Pot Jambalaya: A Flavorful Weeknight Dinner


Jambalaya is a classic dish that originated in Louisiana and is known for its bold flavors and colorful presentation. This one-pot wonder is packed with a combination of meats, vegetables, and rice, creating a tantalizing blend of spices that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of the South.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 pound (450g) smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 pound (450g) chicken breast, diced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional, adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 1 pound (450g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, bell peppers, and celery, and sauté until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.

  2. Push the vegetables to one side of the pot and add the sliced sausage. Cook until browned, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the diced chicken breast to the pot and cook until no longer pink, about 5 minutes.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, dried thyme, paprika, and hot sauce (if using). Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Stir in the rice and return the pot to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 20 minutes or until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid.

  7. Add the shrimp to the pot and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until they turn pink and opaque.

  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Serve the jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

A Taste of Louisiana

Spicy One-Pot Jambalaya is a perfect dish for busy weeknights when you want to bring some excitement to your dinner table. The combination of smoky sausage, tender chicken, succulent shrimp, and aromatic vegetables creates a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving for more. The Cajun seasoning adds just the right amount of heat and depth, while the rice soaks up all the goodness, making each bite a delight.

Jambalaya is a versatile dish, allowing you to get creative with the ingredients. You can swap the proteins to suit your taste, adding in ham, Andouille sausage, or even crawfish. If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply omit the meat and increase the amount of vegetables.

This one-pot wonder not only delivers on taste but also saves you time on cleanup. With just a single pot, you can whip up a hearty meal that feeds a crowd or leaves you with leftovers for the next day.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a flavorful and satisfying dinner, give Spicy One-Pot Jambalaya a try. Your taste buds will thank you, and your family and friends will be begging for seconds!
