Savory and Nutty Farro Pilaf with Grilled Vegetables


Farro pilaf is a wholesome, delicious dish that brings together the earthy flavors of farro, a nutty ancient grain, with a medley of colorful, grilled vegetables. This recipe not only showcases the versatility of farro but also highlights how simple ingredients can come together to create a satisfying meal that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking for a filling side dish or a hearty main course, this farro pilaf with grilled vegetables is sure to impress.

What is Farro?

Farro is an ancient grain that has been enjoyed for thousands of years, originating from the Fertile Crescent region. It is a type of wheat, typically available in three varieties: spelt (farro grande), emmer (farro medio), and einkorn (farro piccolo). Farro is known for its chewy texture and nutty flavor, making it a great base for salads, soups, and pilafs. Packed with fiber, protein, and essential nutrients, farro is not only tasty but also contributes to a balanced diet.

Why Choose Grilled Vegetables?

Grilling vegetables enhances their natural sweetness and adds a delightful smoky flavor. The charred edges and tender interiors created by grilling make vegetables more appealing, especially for those who may not typically enjoy them. In this recipe, we will use a variety of seasonal vegetables, but feel free to customize according to your preferences or what you have on hand. Grilled vegetables bring color, texture, and nutritional benefits to the farro pilaf, making it a well-rounded dish.

Ingredients

For the Farro Pilaf:

  • 1 cup farro (spelt, emmer, or einkorn)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or fresh if available)
  • 1 bay leaf

For the Grilled Vegetables:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar (optional)

For Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Cooking the Farro

  1. Rinse the Farro: Start by rinsing the farro under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. This step ensures a cleaner taste.

  2. Cook the Farro: In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed farro, vegetable broth (or water), salt, and bay leaf. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes or until the farro is tender but still chewy. If you’re using pearled farro, the cooking time will be shorter (about 15-20 minutes).

  3. Drain and Fluff: Once cooked, drain any excess liquid, remove the bay leaf, and fluff the farro with a fork. Set it aside while you prepare the vegetables.

Step 2: Grilling the Vegetables

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the sliced zucchini, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, and red onion. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Toss to coat the vegetables evenly.

  2. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. If using an outdoor grill, ensure the grates are clean and lightly oiled.

  3. Grill the Vegetables: Add the seasoned vegetables to the grill. Cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and have nice grill marks. Depending on the size of your grill, you may need to do this in batches.

  4. Add Balsamic Vinegar: If desired, drizzle the grilled vegetables with balsamic vinegar for an extra layer of flavor right before removing them from the grill.

Step 3: Combining Farro and Grilled Vegetables

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked farro with the grilled vegetables. Gently fold everything together, being careful not to mash the vegetables.

  2. Season to Taste: Taste the pilaf and adjust the seasoning if necessary. A sprinkle of fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon juice can add brightness.

  3. Serve: Transfer the farro pilaf to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped parsley and lemon wedges for a fresh touch. Enjoy warm or at room temperature.

Tips for the Perfect Farro Pilaf

  • Soak the Farro: If you have time, soaking the farro for a few hours or overnight can reduce cooking time and enhance its flavor.

  • Experiment with Vegetables: Use whatever vegetables are in season or your favorites. Asparagus, eggplant, or even mushrooms can make great additions.

  • Add Protein: To make this dish even heartier, consider adding chickpeas, beans, or grilled chicken for a protein boost.

  • Store Leftovers: This pilaf makes excellent leftovers! Store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stovetop with a splash of broth.

Nutritional Benefits

Farro is a nutritional powerhouse, offering a variety of health benefits:

  • High in Fiber: This ancient grain is packed with dietary fiber, which helps keep you feeling full and supports digestive health.

  • Rich in Protein: Farro contains more protein than many other grains, making it a great choice for vegetarians and those looking to increase their protein intake.

  • Packed with Nutrients: Farro is rich in vitamins and minerals, including magnesium, iron, and B vitamins, all of which are essential for maintaining overall health.

Grilled vegetables are also nutrient-dense, providing vitamins A, C, and K, along with antioxidants that contribute to a healthy diet.

Conclusion

Savory and nutty farro pilaf with grilled vegetables is a delightful dish that showcases the beauty of simple, wholesome ingredients. Whether served as a main course or a side dish, it’s a versatile and satisfying option that’s perfect for any meal. With its nutty flavor, chewy texture, and vibrant colors, this pilaf is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and prepare to enjoy a bowl of deliciousness that celebrates the flavors of the season!
