Fall Pies: Sweet and Savory Recipes with Seasonal Apples


Fall is the perfect time to cozy up with a warm slice of pie. And what better way to celebrate the season than with delicious fall pies made with seasonal apples? In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of sweet and savory pie recipes that are sure to delight your taste buds and impress your guests.

Sweet Apple Pie

Let’s start with the classic sweet apple pie. This timeless dessert is a staple at fall gatherings and is loved by all. Here’s a simple yet mouthwatering recipe to help you create the perfect sweet apple pie:

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6 tablespoons ice water
  • 6 cups peeled and thinly sliced apples (use a mix of sweet and tart varieties)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape them into discs. Wrap each disc in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In another bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of dough into a circle large enough to fit your pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish and press it gently into the bottom and sides.

  6. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust and dot with small pieces of butter.

  7. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it on top of the apple filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  8. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

  9. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  10. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  11. Allow the pie to cool for at least 1 hour before serving. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate fall dessert experience.

Savory Apple and Cheddar Pie

Looking for something a little different? Try this savory apple and cheddar pie. The combination of sweet apples and tangy cheddar cheese creates a unique and delicious flavor profile that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6 tablespoons ice water
  • 4 cups peeled and thinly sliced apples (use a mix of sweet and tart varieties)
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape them into discs. Wrap each disc in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and cook until soft and translucent.

  5. Add the sliced apples to the skillet and cook for a few minutes until slightly softened. Remove from heat and set aside.

  6. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for one minute, stirring constantly.

  7. Gradually whisk in the milk, Dijon mustard, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Continue cooking until the mixture thickens.

  8. Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and smooth.

  9. Roll out one disc of dough on a lightly floured surface and fit it into a pie dish. Trim any excess dough.

  10. Spread the apple and onion mixture over the bottom crust. Pour the cheese sauce over the apples.

  11. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it on top of the pie. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal.

  12. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg.

  13. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  14. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  15. Allow the pie to cool for a few minutes before serving. Slice and enjoy this unique and savory twist on traditional apple pie.

There you have it – two delicious fall pie recipes that showcase the natural sweetness and versatility of seasonal apples. Whether you prefer the classic sweet apple pie or want to try something a little different with the savory apple and cheddar pie, these recipes are sure to become fall favorites. So go ahead, grab some fresh apples, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to bake your way into the cozy, comforting flavors of autumn!
