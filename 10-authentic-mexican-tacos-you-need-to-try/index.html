10 Authentic Mexican Tacos You Need to Try


Tacos are more than just a popular street food; they are a vibrant expression of Mexican culture and culinary artistry. With origins deeply rooted in the rich landscape of Mexico, tacos have evolved into countless variations. From savory meats to fresh vegetables, these handheld delights offer a taste of tradition and innovation. Here, we dive into ten authentic Mexican tacos that you absolutely must try, each with its unique flavor profile and story.

1. Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al Pastor are a staple in Mexican cuisine, originating from the influence of Lebanese immigrants who brought their shawarma technique to Mexico. These tacos feature marinated pork cooked on a vertical spit, akin to shawarma. The meat is seasoned with a blend of spices, including achiote, which gives it a vibrant red color. It is typically served on small corn tortillas, topped with freshly chopped onions, cilantro, and a slice of pineapple, adding a sweet and tangy contrast to the savory meat.

Tip: Serve with a squeeze of lime for an extra flavor boost!

2. Tacos de Carnitas

Carnitas, meaning “little meats,” are made from pork that is braised and then shredded. The cooking process involves simmering the pork with spices like cumin, garlic, and bay leaves in its own fat until it becomes tender and flavorful. Once cooked, the meat is crisped up in a hot skillet for added texture.

These tacos are often garnished with diced onions, cilantro, and a drizzle of salsa verde. The rich flavor of carnitas combined with the freshness of toppings makes these tacos irresistible.

Tip: Pair with a refreshing agua fresca for a complete meal!

3. Tacos de Barbacoa

Barbacoa is a traditional method of cooking meat, typically lamb or beef, that is slow-cooked until tender. Originating from the central regions of Mexico, this dish is often prepared in a pit, allowing the meat to absorb smoky flavors. The meat is seasoned with spices and sometimes wrapped in banana leaves, enhancing its unique taste.

Served in warm corn tortillas with onions, cilantro, and a spicy salsa, barbacoa tacos are a true comfort food.

Tip: Enjoy with a side of pickled onions to cut through the richness of the meat!

4. Tacos de Pescado

Fried fish tacos are a coastal favorite, particularly in Baja California. Fresh white fish, typically battered and fried to golden perfection, is the star of this dish. The tacos are typically served on soft corn tortillas and topped with shredded cabbage, pico de gallo, and a zesty lime crema.

The crunch of the cabbage and the acidity of the lime juice complement the crispy fish beautifully, making this a refreshing option.

Tip: Add a slice of avocado for creaminess!

5. Tacos de Pollo

Chicken tacos, or tacos de pollo, are incredibly versatile and can be made using various cooking methods. Whether grilled, roasted, or simmered with spices, chicken tacos are a crowd-pleaser. Often marinated with lime juice, garlic, and chili powder, the chicken becomes flavorful and juicy.

These tacos are usually topped with fresh guacamole, pico de gallo, and a sprinkle of queso fresco for a satisfying meal.

Tip: Experiment with different marinades, like chipotle or adobo, to switch up the flavor profile!

6. Tacos de Nopales

For a vegetarian option, tacos de nopales feature tender cactus paddles, a traditional ingredient in Mexican cooking. Nopales are cleaned, cooked, and then sautéed with onions, tomatoes, and spices. Their slightly tangy flavor and unique texture make them an interesting filling for tacos.

These tacos are often topped with crumbled queso fresco, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. They are not only delicious but also nutritious!

Tip: Pair with a side of black beans for added protein!

7. Tacos de Chicharrón Prensado

Chicharrón prensado, or pressed pork cracklings, create a unique and flavorful taco filling. This dish is made by cooking pork skin until tender, then pressing it into a cake and frying until crispy. The result is a crunchy yet soft texture that’s packed with flavor.

When served in tacos, chicharrón is often accompanied by fresh avocado, onions, and a drizzle of salsa verde, making for a delightful combination of textures and flavors.

Tip: Serve with a side of tangy tomatillo salsa for extra zing!

8. Tacos de Lengua

Tacos de lengua, or beef tongue tacos, might sound adventurous to some, but they are a beloved delicacy in Mexico. The tongue is boiled, then simmered with spices until tender, making it easy to shred. The flavor is rich and savory, often described as a beefy delicacy.

These tacos are typically topped with diced onions, cilantro, and a spicy salsa. For those who appreciate unique cuts of meat, lengua tacos are a must-try.

Tip: Add a dollop of sour cream to balance the flavors!

9. Tacos de Sopes

While not a traditional taco in the classic sense, sopes are often enjoyed in a similar manner. With a thick masa base, these little discs are topped with various ingredients, including meats, beans, and vegetables. The edges are raised to hold all the delicious toppings, making for a hand-held delight.

Common fillings include spicy chorizo, refried beans, lettuce, and queso fresco, all drizzled with crema. They are perfect for sharing or as a hearty meal.

Tip: Experiment by creating a variety of toppings to please a crowd!

10. Tacos de Mole

For a truly authentic experience, try tacos de mole. Mole is a complex sauce made from a mixture of ingredients, including chili peppers, spices, chocolate, and nuts. Often served with chicken or turkey, the deep flavors of mole create a rich filling for tacos.

These tacos are typically garnished with pickled onions and fresh cilantro, enhancing the overall flavor experience. The combination of savory, sweet, and spicy notes makes mole tacos unforgettable.

Tip: Serve with a side of rice and beans to complete the meal!

Conclusion

Tacos are a celebration of flavor, tradition, and creativity. Each taco tells a story, whether it’s the influence of Lebanese immigrants on Tacos al Pastor or the use of local ingredients for Tacos de Nopales. With such a diverse range of fillings and preparations, there’s a taco for every palate.

As you explore these authentic Mexican tacos, don’t hesitate to personalize them with your favorite toppings and salsas. Dive into the world of tacos and discover the rich flavors and vibrant culture of Mexico, one tortilla at a time. Happy taco tasting!
