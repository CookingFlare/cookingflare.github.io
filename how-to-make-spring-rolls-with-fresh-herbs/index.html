How to Make Spring Rolls with Fresh Herbs


Spring rolls are a timeless culinary delight that blend fresh, crisp textures with vibrant, aromatic flavors. Unlike deep-fried versions, fresh spring rolls offer a lighter, refreshing alternative that is perfect for any time of the year. Incorporating a variety of fresh herbs elevates these rolls to an entirely new level, delivering bursts of flavor that delight the palate and invigorate the senses.

In this guide, you’ll discover everything you need to know to make spring rolls filled with fresh herbs, vegetables, and protein of your choice. Whether you’re a novice or a seasoned cook, these instructions and tips will help you master the art of rolling and sourcing the freshest ingredients.

Ingredients for Fresh Herb Spring Rolls

A spring roll is only as good as its ingredients. The beauty of fresh rolls is the light interplay between crisp textures, delicate herbs, and savory fillings. Here’s a simple foundational list to start with, and later, I’ll share variations to customize according to your taste.

Fresh Herbs

  • Mint leaves: Gives a cooling aroma and fresh pop of flavor.
  • Cilantro: Provides a citrusy, pungent note.
  • Thai basil: Adds a sweet, slightly peppery taste.
  • Chives or green onions: For mild sharpness and crunch.

Vegetables and Other Fresh Ingredients

  • Julienned carrots: Adds crunch and sweetness.
  • Cucumber strips: Refreshing and hydrating crunch.
  • Bean sprouts: Traditional texture and mild flavor.
  • Lettuce leaves (butter lettuce or green leaf): Acts as a crisp bed.
  • Avocado slices (optional): For a creamy texture contrast.

Protein Options (Choose one or mix)

  • Cooked shrimp: Sweet, tender, and iconic in many traditional spring rolls.
  • Grilled chicken strips: Adds hearty protein and a savory base.
  • Firm tofu (grilled or fried): Ideal for vegetarians, with a satisfying chew.
  • Thinly sliced pork or beef can also work wonderfully.

Rice Paper Wrappers

  • Thin, translucent sheets of rice paper—a key material used to encase all the fillings snugly.

Dipping Sauce

No spring roll is complete without dipping sauce. Popular choices include:

  • Peanut sauce: Creamy, nutty, and mildly spicy.
  • Nuoc cham: A Vietnamese tangy-sweet fish sauce base with lime, garlic, chilies, and sugar.
  • Soy sauce mix: For a simpler, savory dip alternative.

Prepping the Ingredients

Herb Preparation

Wash all herbs gently in cold water to remove any dirt. Herbs like cilantro and mint are delicate, so pat dry carefully using paper towels or a salad spinner.

Vegetable Preparation

Wash vegetables thoroughly. Use a mandoline slicer or a sharp knife to julienne carrots, cucumbers, and bell peppers uniformly. Bean sprouts should be rinsed and any brown bits removed. Tear lettuce leaves into pieces that fit comfortably inside the rice paper.

Protein Prep

If using shrimp, boil or steam them until just cooked and then slice them in half lengthwise. For chicken or pork, marinate briefly in a mix of garlic, soy sauce, and a touch of sugar before grilling to add flavor. Slice thinly once cooked. For tofu, press excess moisture out, then pan-fry or grill until golden before slicing thinly.

Step-by-Step Assembly of Fresh Herb Spring Rolls

1. Soften Rice Paper Wrappers

Fill a large shallow dish or pie plate with warm water (not too hot, about 90°F or 32°C), big enough to submerge a rice paper sheet.

Place one sheet in the water for about 10-15 seconds until it softens slightly but is still pliable (it will continue to soften once out of water).

Lay the softened sheet flat on a clean, damp kitchen towel or bamboo mat.

2. Layer the Ingredients

In the bottom third of the sheet (nearest you), start layering your ingredients.

  • Begin with a few lettuce leaves.
  • Add a small handful of herbs: mint, cilantro, and basil.
  • Place a few julienned carrots and cucumbers.
  • Add bean sprouts and avocado slices, if using.
  • Add the protein slices on top.

Don’t overload the wrapper—it’s better to have a modest amount to make rolling easier and prevent the roll from tearing.

3. Roll Tightly

Fold the bottom edge of the rice paper tightly over the filling.

Then fold in the left and right sides towards the center to encase the filling on both sides.

Finally, continue to roll upwards until sealed and you have a neat roll. The rice paper will stick to itself, sealing the roll.

4. Repeat with Remaining Wrappers

Continue until all fillings are used. Try to keep rolls uniform in size for consistent presentation.

Tips for Perfecting Your Spring Rolls

  • Don’t soak the rice paper too long: Over-soaked rice paper becomes fragile and difficult to handle, tearing easily.
  • Layer herbs directly on the leafy greens: This creates a moisture barrier, keeping the wrapper from getting soggy.
  • Use a damp towel to keep rolls fresh: Cover rolls with a damp cloth or plastic wrap if you’re not serving immediately.
  • Cut rolls diagonally to serve: It exposes the colorful filling and adds a professional touch.

Delicious Dipping Sauce Recipes to Complement Your Fresh Herb Spring Rolls

Classic Peanut Dipping Sauce

  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha (adjust for heat preference)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1/4 cup warm water to thin

Whisk all ingredients in a bowl until smooth. Adjust water if needed for desired consistency.

Nuoc Cham (Vietnamese Dipping Sauce)

  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 small chili, finely chopped (optional)

Mix until sugar dissolves. Taste and adjust balance between sweet, sour, and salty.

Variations and Creative Twists

Spring rolls are endlessly adaptable to your preferences and available ingredients.

  • Add fruit for a sweet pop: Mango, pineapple, or shredded apple work wonders.
  • Change up the protein: Smoked salmon or crab meat can be exciting alternatives.
  • Spice it up with chili or jalapeño slices for an unexpected kick.
  • Use gluten-free fillings: Stick with rice-based or vegetable proteins to keep it gluten-free.

Storing and Serving Suggestions

Fresh spring rolls are best enjoyed immediately, but if you need to store them, place them tightly covered in the refrigerator with a damp paper towel over the rolls to keep them moist. Consume within 24 hours for the best texture.

Serve alongside a bowl of dipping sauce, and for a complete meal, pair with a simple Asian-inspired salad or vermicelli noodles.

Final Thoughts

Making fresh herb spring rolls at home might seem intimidating at first, but with a little practice, the process is simple, fun, and incredibly rewarding. The interplay of fresh herbs, crisp vegetables, and savory protein wrapped delicately in rice paper creates a satisfying, light dish perfect for any occasion. Once you master the basics, experiment with different herbs, sauces, and fillings to find your signature combination.

Whether you serve them as an appetizer, a light lunch, or party finger food, these fresh herb spring rolls promise a burst of flavor that cheers the palate and nourishes the body. So grab some rice paper, fresh herbs, and your favorite fillings — and start rolling!
