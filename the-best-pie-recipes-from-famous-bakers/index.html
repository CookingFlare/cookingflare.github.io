The Best Pie Recipes from Famous Bakers


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the classic pie. From flaky crusts to luscious fillings, pies have a special place in our culinary hearts. Whether you’re a fan of fruit, custard, or savory varieties, there is a pie out there that can satisfy your cravings. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best pie recipes crafted by famous bakers, each offering a unique twist on this beloved dessert. Get ready to roll up your sleeves and embark on a delicious journey through the world of pies!

1. Classic Apple Pie by Martha Stewart

Martha Stewart, the queen of domesticity, has perfected the classic apple pie recipe. Her version is a staple that showcases the beauty of simple ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 cup (2 sticks) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 6 to 8 apples (a mix of Granny Smith and Honeycrisp)
    • ¾ cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, mixing until dough forms. Divide into two disks, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least one hour.

  2. Make the filling: Peel, core, and slice the apples. In a large bowl, toss the apples with sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disk of dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie pan. Place the crust in the pan and fill with the apple mixture. Roll out the second disk and place it over the filling. Cut slits in the top crust for steam to escape.

  4. Bake: Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Allow to cool before serving.

2. Chocolate Cream Pie by David Lebovitz

Renowned pastry chef David Lebovitz brings a touch of elegance with his chocolate cream pie. This recipe is perfect for chocolate lovers and is sure to impress at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • ⅓ cup sugar
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 1 cup whole milk
    • ¾ cup sugar
    • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For the topping:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a mixing bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, cocoa powder, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let cool.

  2. Prepare the filling: In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, sugar, cocoa powder, and cornstarch. Cook over medium heat, whisking constantly until thickened. Remove from heat and stir in chopped chocolate and vanilla extract until smooth.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the chocolate filling into the cooled crust. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours until set.

  4. Make the topping: Whip heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until soft peaks form. Spread over the chilled chocolate filling and serve.

3. Key Lime Pie by Chris Bianco

Chef Chris Bianco, known for his amazing pizza, also makes a mean key lime pie. This refreshing dessert is the perfect balance of tart and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup sugar
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

  • For the filling:

    • 4 large egg yolks
    • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
    • ½ cup fresh key lime juice
    • Zest of 1 lime

  • For the topping:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • Lime slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter in a bowl. Press the mixture into a 9-inch pie pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let cool.

  2. Make the filling: In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, sweetened condensed milk, key lime juice, and lime zest until smooth. Pour the filling into the cooled crust.

  3. Bake: Bake for 15 minutes. Remove from the oven and let cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 3 hours.

  4. Top and serve: Whip heavy cream with sugar until soft peaks form. Spread over the chilled pie and garnish with lime slices.

4. Pecan Pie by Paula Deen

No pie collection would be complete without a classic pecan pie. Paula Deen’s recipe is rich, sweet, and has a delightful crunch that is hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup corn syrup
    • 1 cup brown sugar
    • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 4 large eggs
    • 2 cups pecan halves

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: In a bowl, combine flour and salt. Cut in diced butter until crumbly. Stir in ice water until dough forms. Shape into a disk, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for 1 hour.

  2. Prepare the filling: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, whisk together corn syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, vanilla, and eggs until smooth. Stir in pecans.

  3. Assemble the pie: Roll out the chilled dough and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Pour the pecan filling into the crust.

  4. Bake: Bake for 60-70 minutes, or until set. Let cool before slicing.

5. Lemon Meringue Pie by Nancy Silverton

Nancy Silverton, a celebrated baker and co-founder of La Brea Bakery, has a delightful lemon meringue pie recipe that strikes the perfect balance between tartness and sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • ¼ cup sugar
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup sugar
    • ¼ cup cornstarch
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1 ½ cups water
    • 3 large egg yolks, beaten
    • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 2 tablespoons butter

  • For the meringue:

    • 3 large egg whites
    • ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar
    • ½ cup sugar

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a bowl, combine flour, butter, and sugar until crumbly. Add egg yolk and ice water until a dough forms. Roll out and fit into a 9-inch pie pan. Bake for 20 minutes, then let cool.

  2. Make the filling: In a saucepan, whisk together sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add water and cook over medium heat until thickened. Stir in egg yolks, lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter until smooth.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the lemon filling into the baked crust.

  4. Make the meringue: In a clean bowl, beat egg whites and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar until stiff peaks form. Spread meringue over the lemon filling, sealing the edges.

  5. Bake: Bake for 10 minutes, or until the meringue is golden. Let cool before serving.

Conclusion

Pies are more than just desserts; they are a tradition, a comfort, and a way to share love through food. The recipes from these famous bakers represent a range of flavors and techniques, ensuring that there’s a perfect pie for every occasion. Whether you’re baking a classic apple pie or a refreshing key lime pie, these recipes are sure to impress your friends and family. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let the pie-making magic begin! Happy baking!
