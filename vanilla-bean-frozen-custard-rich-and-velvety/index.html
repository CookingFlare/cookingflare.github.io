Vanilla Bean Frozen Custard: Rich and Velvety


Are you craving a cool and creamy treat that will satisfy your sweet tooth? Look no further than this indulgent recipe for Vanilla Bean Frozen Custard. Unlike traditional ice cream, frozen custard is made with a higher proportion of egg yolks, resulting in a richer and silkier texture. With the addition of fragrant vanilla beans, this frozen custard takes on a whole new level of decadence.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise and seeds scraped
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and half of the granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to simmer, then remove from heat.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, remaining granulated sugar, vanilla bean seeds, and salt until well combined.

  3. Slowly pour a small amount of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling. Continue to gradually add the hot cream mixture, whisking constantly.

  4. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and return to medium heat. Cook, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until the custard thickens and coats the back of the spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any lumps or cooked egg bits. Discard the vanilla bean pod.

  6. Let the custard cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the custard to chill thoroughly.

  7. Once the custard is chilled, transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  8. When the custard reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to an airtight container and freeze for an additional 2-4 hours, or until firm.

  9. Serve the Vanilla Bean Frozen Custard in bowls or cones, and enjoy the rich and velvety goodness. You can also garnish it with fresh berries or a drizzle of chocolate sauce for an extra touch of indulgence.

Tips & Variations

  • For a more intense vanilla flavor, add an extra vanilla bean or a teaspoon of pure vanilla extract to the custard mixture.
  • Experiment with different mix-ins, such as crushed cookies, chocolate chips, or chopped nuts, to add texture and flavor to your frozen custard.
  • To prevent ice crystals from forming, press a piece of plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the custard before freezing.
  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make frozen custard by pouring the chilled custard into a shallow dish and placing it in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, take it out and stir vigorously to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process until the custard reaches the desired consistency.

Now that you have this incredible recipe for Vanilla Bean Frozen Custard, you can enjoy a luscious, homemade frozen treat whenever the craving strikes. Whether you savor it on a sunny afternoon or serve it at a special occasion, this rich and velvety dessert is sure to become a favorite among family and friends. So go ahead, indulge in the creamy goodness of Vanilla Bean Frozen Custard and experience a taste sensation like no other.
