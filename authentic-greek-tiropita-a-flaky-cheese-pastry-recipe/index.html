Authentic Greek Tiropita: A Flaky Cheese Pastry Recipe


Tiropita, a classic Greek savory pastry, is a staple in every Greek household. It’s a delightful combination of melted cheese, crispy phyllo, and a hint of fresh herbs, all wrapped up in a delicious package. Whether you’re a foodie, a Greek cuisine enthusiast, or simply looking for a new recipe to impress your family and friends, this authentic Greek tiropita recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients

For the phyllo dough:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (usually found in the freezer section of most supermarkets)
  • 1/2 cup of olive oil

For the filling:

  • 1 cup of grated Greek feta cheese (or other crumbly cheese)
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh dill
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 egg, beaten (for brushing phyllo)

Instructions

Preparing the Phyllo Dough

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Thaw the phyllo dough according to the package instructions.
  3. Cut the phyllo into four equal pieces.
  4. Brush each piece with olive oil, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  5. Stack the phyllo pieces, brushing each layer with olive oil, until you have a total of 8-10 layers.

Preparing the Filling

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the grated feta and Parmesan cheese.
  2. Add the chopped parsley, dill, garlic, salt, and pepper to the bowl and mix well.
  3. Beat the egg in a small bowl and add it to the cheese mixture. Mix until well combined.

Assembling the Tiropita

  1. Place one piece of the phyllo dough on a flat surface, with the oiled side facing down.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the cheese filling in the center of the phyllo.
  3. Fold the phyllo over the filling, forming a triangle, and press the edges to seal.
  4. Repeat the process with the remaining phyllo and filling ingredients.

Baking the Tiropita

  1. Place the tiropita triangles on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between each triangle.
  2. Brush the tops of the tiropita with the beaten egg.
  3. Bake the tiropita in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle some chopped kalamata olives or sun-dried tomatoes on top of the tiropita before baking.
  • For a spinach and feta twist, add some chopped spinach to the cheese filling.
  • To make individual servings, simply divide the phyllo and filling ingredients into smaller portions and assemble the tiropita as directed.

Conclusion

Authentic Greek tiropita is a delicious and easy-to-make pastry that’s sure to become a staple in your household. With its flaky phyllo crust, gooey cheese filling, and fresh herbs, this recipe is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just looking for a quick and easy snack, this tiropita recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the authentic taste of Greece in the comfort of your own home.
