8 Iconic Chinese Dishes to Cook Tonight


When it comes to culinary delight, few cuisines can rival the rich tapestry of flavors, textures, and aromas found in Chinese cooking. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, cooking these iconic Chinese dishes at home can be an adventure filled with creativity. 1. Kung Pao Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, diced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup unsalted peanuts
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine diced chicken, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, sesame oil, and cornstarch. Let it marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In another bowl, mix the remaining soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Stir-fry: Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add marinated chicken and cook until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add Aromatics: In the same wok, add dried chilies, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Combine Ingredients: Add bell pepper and cooked chicken back to the wok. Pour the sauce over and stir well. Toss in peanuts and green onions just before serving.

Kung Pao Chicken offers a delightful mix of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors, making it a perennial favorite.

2. Mapo Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block soft tofu, cubed
  • 1/2 lb ground pork (or beef)
  • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (spicy bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Tofu: Blanch the tofu cubes in boiling water for 2-3 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook Meat: In a wok, heat vegetable oil and add the ground pork. Stir-fry until browned.
  3. Add Seasoning: Incorporate garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir in the doubanjiang and continue cooking for another minute.
  4. Combine: Pour in chicken broth and soy sauce, bringing it to a simmer. Gently add the tofu and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions for brightness.

Mapo Tofu is a spicy, fragrant dish that beautifully balances the silkiness of tofu with the robust flavor of meat.

3. Sweet and Sour Pork

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers, diced
  • 1/2 cup pineapple, diced
  • 1/2 cup onion, diced

For the Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce

Instructions:

  1. Coat the Pork: Dip pork pieces in beaten egg and then coat with cornstarch.
  2. Fry the Pork: Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry pork in batches until golden. Drain on paper towels.
  3. Make the Sauce: In a bowl, mix ketchup, rice vinegar, sugar, and soy sauce.
  4. Stir-fry Vegetables: In the same wok, stir-fry bell peppers, pineapple, and onion for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Combine: Add fried pork and sauce to the vegetables, tossing until everything is well coated.

This dish offers a delightful balance of flavors that both children and adults adore, capturing the essence of Chinese cuisine.

4. Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Wok: Heat sesame oil in a wok over medium heat. Scramble the eggs until just set, then remove and set aside.
  2. Stir-fry Vegetables: Add a bit more oil if necessary, and stir-fry mixed vegetables until tender.
  3. Fry the Rice: Increase heat and add the day-old rice. Stir well, breaking up any clumps.
  4. Season: Pour in soy sauce, returning scrambled eggs to the wok. Mix in chopped green onions and season with salt and pepper.

Fried rice is a versatile dish that can easily adapt to whatever leftovers you have, making it a go-to for many home cooks.

5. Dumplings (Jiaozi)

Ingredients:

  • Dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup water
  • Filling:
    • 1 lb ground pork
    • 1 cup Napa cabbage, finely chopped
    • 2 green onions, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix flour and water until a dough forms. Knead for 5-10 minutes, then let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling: Combine all filling ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
  3. Form Dumplings: Roll dough into small circles, place a dollop of filling in the center, and pinch the edges to seal.
  4. Cook the Dumplings: Boil, steam, or pan-fry the dumplings until cooked through, usually about 6-8 minutes.

Dumplings are a fun, social dish, perfect for family gatherings or dinner parties, and they can be customized with various fillings.

6. Peking Duck

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole duck (about 5-6 lbs)
  • 1/4 cup maltose or honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • Scallions and hoisin sauce for serving
  • Mandarin pancakes or tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Duck: Clean the duck and pat it dry. Mix maltose, soy sauce, vinegar, and five-spice powder, then rub it all over the duck.
  2. Air-dry: Hang the duck in a cool place for 24 hours to dry out the skin.
  3. Roast the Duck: Preheat the oven to 375Â°F (190Â°C). Roast the duck for about 1.5 hours, basting occasionally until the skin is crispy and golden brown.
  4. Serve: Carve the duck and serve with scallions, hoisin sauce, and pancakes.

Peking Duck is a feast for the senses, celebrated for its crispy skin and succulent meat, making it an ideal centerpiece for any festive occasion.

7. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb flank steak, sliced thin against the grain
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, toss beef with soy sauce and cornstarch. Let it sit for 15 minutes.
  2. Blanch Broccoli: In boiling water, blanch broccoli for 2 minutes, then transfer to ice water to maintain color and crunch.
  3. Stir-fry: In a hot wok, add vegetable oil and stir-fry the beef until browned. Add garlic and ginger, cooking until fragrant.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Add broccoli and oyster sauce, tossing everything together until heated through.

This dish exemplifies the balance of flavors found in Chinese cuisine, showcasing tender beef and crisp broccoli in a savory sauce.

8. Hot and Sour Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup silken tofu, cubed
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 4 tablespoons water
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Simmer the Broth: In a pot, bring the broth to a simmer. Add mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and tofu.
  2. Season: Stir in soy sauce, rice vinegar, and white pepper.
  3. Thicken the Soup: Add the cornstarch mixture slowly, stirring until the soup thickens.
  4. Finish with Egg: Slowly pour in the beaten egg while stirring gently to create ribbons in the soup.
  5. Garnish: Serve hot, topped with chopped green onions.

Hot and Sour Soup is a comforting bowl of flavors, perfectly balancing spicy and sour notes, ideal for any chilly evening.

From the spicy zing of Kung Pao Chicken to the comforting warmth of Hot and Sour Soup, these recipes are sure to delight your palate. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a culinary journey tonight!
