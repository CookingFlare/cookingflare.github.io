Step-by-Step Guide to Making Mouthwatering Indian Butter Chicken


Indian Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a rich and flavorful dish that has become a favorite in many households around the world. This creamy curry combines tender chicken with a luscious tomato-based sauce, infused with aromatic spices. In this step-by-step guide, we will show you how to make this mouthwatering Indian Butter Chicken from scratch. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. This will take about 8-10 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies to the pan. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw aroma of the garlic disappears.

  3. Now, it’s time to add the spices. Sprinkle the Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala over the onions. Mix well to coat the onions evenly with the spices.

  4. Add the tomato puree to the pan and stir everything together. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Next, add the chicken pieces to the pan and mix them well with the tomato-spice mixture. Cook the chicken for about 10-12 minutes, or until it is cooked through and tender.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, reduce the heat to low and pour in the heavy cream. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  7. Crush the dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) between your palms and sprinkle them over the chicken. The kasuri methi adds a unique aroma to the dish and enhances the flavors.

  8. Season the butter chicken with salt according to your taste. Give it a final stir, cover the pan, and let it simmer for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.

  9. Once the butter chicken is ready, turn off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the sauce to thicken slightly.

  10. Garnish the Indian Butter Chicken with fresh cilantro leaves for a burst of freshness and bright flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Indian Butter Chicken pairs perfectly with steamed basmati rice or naan bread. You can also serve it with roti or paratha for a wholesome meal. For a complete Indian feast, consider adding some vegetable dishes like saag paneer (spinach and cheese curry) or aloo gobi (potato and cauliflower curry) to the menu.

Conclusion

Making Mouthwatering Indian Butter Chicken at home is easier than you think. With this step-by-step guide, you can recreate the flavors of your favorite Indian restaurant right in your own kitchen. The combination of tender chicken, creamy tomato sauce, and aromatic spices will surely impress your family and friends. So, give it a try and indulge in the deliciousness of this classic Indian dish. Enjoy!
