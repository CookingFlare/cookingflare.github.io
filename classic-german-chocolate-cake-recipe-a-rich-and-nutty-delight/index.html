Classic German Chocolate Cake Recipe: A Rich and Nutty Delight


German Chocolate Cake—despite the name—originates from the United States, named after an English-American chocolatier, Samuel German, who developed a dark-baking chocolate in 1852. This decadent cake has charmed dessert lovers for generations, combining layers of rich chocolate cake with a luscious coconut and pecan frosting. In this blog post, we’ll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to bake this classic cake, while also exploring its history, variations, and tips for the perfect bake.

The History of German Chocolate Cake

The story of German Chocolate Cake began in 1852 when Samuel German created a dark-baking chocolate for a British chocolate company. It wasn’t until the 1950s that the cake gained popularity in the U.S. A recipe for “German’s Chocolate Cake” was published in a Dallas newspaper, catching the attention of many. The cake quickly became a staple at family gatherings and celebrations, solidifying its place in American dessert culture.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create the perfect Classic German Chocolate Cake, gather the following ingredients:

For the Cake

  • 1 cup (240ml) of boiling water
  • 1 cup (90g) of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups (250g) of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (400g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) of buttermilk, at room temperature
  • 1 cup (240ml) of vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract

For the Coconut Pecan Frosting

  • 1 cup (240ml) of heavy cream
  • 1 cup (200g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (100g) of sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup (100g) of chopped pecans
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

For Garnishing (Optional)

  • Additional pecans
  • Chocolate shavings

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Cake

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. Mix Cocoa and Water: In a medium bowl, combine the boiling water and cocoa powder. Whisk until smooth and set aside to cool.

  3. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking soda, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

  4. Combine Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, blend the buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.

  5. Mix Everything Together: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Slowly pour in the cocoa mixture, stirring gently until fully incorporated.

  6. Bake the Cake: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cake pans. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Once done, remove from the oven and let the cakes cool in the pans for about 10 minutes before transferring them to wire racks to cool completely.

Step 2: Making the Coconut Pecan Frosting

  1. Prepare the Mixture: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine the heavy cream, sugar, and egg yolks. Whisk constantly until the mixture thickens, about 10-12 minutes.

  2. Add Coconut and Pecans: Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the shredded coconut, chopped pecans, and vanilla extract. Allow the frosting to cool to room temperature, which will help it thicken further.

Step 3: Assembling the Cake

  1. Layer the Cakes: Once the cakes have completely cooled, place one layer on a serving platter. Spread a generous amount of the coconut-pecan frosting on top.

  2. Add the Second Layer: Place the second cake layer on top and repeat the frosting process. Top with the final layer of cake.

  3. Frost the Top and Sides: Use the remaining frosting to cover the top and sides of the cake. For a decorative touch, you can sprinkle additional pecans and chocolate shavings on top.

Step 4: Let It Set

Allow the assembled cake to sit for at least 30 minutes before slicing. This helps the frosting to set and makes cutting easier.

Tips for the Perfect German Chocolate Cake

  • Use Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure all your ingredients are at room temperature for a smoother batter and even baking.

  • Don’t Overmix: When combining the wet and dry ingredients, mix until just combined. Overmixing can lead to a dense cake.

  • Cool Completely: Make sure the cakes are completely cool before frosting. This will prevent the frosting from melting and sliding off.

  • Storage: Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week. The flavors will meld and enhance over time.

Variations on Classic German Chocolate Cake

While the Classic German Chocolate Cake is a timeless favorite, you can also get creative with variations:

  • Chocolate Ganache: For a richer flavor, consider topping the cake with a chocolate ganache before adding the coconut-pecan frosting.

  • Flavored Layers: Experiment with different flavored layers, such as a mocha or raspberry cake, for a unique twist.

  • Gluten-Free Option: Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend to cater to dietary restrictions.

Conclusion

The Classic German Chocolate Cake is a delightful dessert that brings together the rich flavors of chocolate, coconut, and pecans in a beautifully layered cake. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this cake is sure to impress. Follow the steps outlined in this post, and you’ll create a rich and nutty delight that your family and friends will adore. Happy baking!
