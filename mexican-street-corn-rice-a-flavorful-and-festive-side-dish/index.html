Mexican Street Corn Rice: A Flavorful and Festive Side Dish


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor and a touch of fiesta to your dinner table, Mexican Street Corn Rice is the perfect side dish to elevate any meal. This vibrant and zesty recipe takes inspiration from the classic Mexican street food, elote, and combines it with fluffy rice to create a dish that’s both comforting and exciting. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a Cinco de Mayo celebration, or simply craving a taste of Mexico, this delightful side dish is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of sweet corn, drained
  • 1/2 cup of mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup of sour cream
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled cotija cheese
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the Rice: Begin by cooking the white rice according to the package instructions. Once cooked, set the rice aside and allow it to cool slightly.

  2. Prepare the Corn Mixture: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped red onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become fragrant and translucent. Then, add the sweet corn to the skillet, stirring occasionally until it starts to caramelize and develop a golden color.

  3. Create the Creamy Dressing: In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, sour cream, half of the crumbled cotija cheese, chopped cilantro, chili powder, smoked paprika, and the freshly squeezed lime juice. Mix the ingredients together until they form a creamy and tangy dressing.

  4. Combine the Ingredients: Once the rice has cooled slightly, gently fold it into the skillet with the caramelized corn mixture. Gradually add the creamy dressing to the rice and corn, ensuring that every grain is coated with the flavorful mixture.

  5. Serve and Garnish: Transfer the Mexican Street Corn Rice to a serving dish and garnish with the remaining crumbled cotija cheese and a sprinkle of fresh cilantro. For an extra pop of color and flavor, consider adding a dash of additional chili powder and a squeeze of lime juice.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smoky twist, you can grill the corn on the cob before cutting off the kernels and incorporating them into the dish.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, consider adding a finely diced jalapeño pepper to the corn mixture for an extra burst of heat.
  • To make this dish vegan-friendly, simply substitute the mayonnaise and sour cream with dairy-free alternatives, and use a plant-based cotija cheese.

In Conclusion

Mexican Street Corn Rice is a celebration of vibrant flavors and festive colors, making it an ideal accompaniment to a wide array of main dishes. Whether served alongside grilled meats, tacos, or as part of a colorful buffet spread, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser. With its creamy texture, smoky undertones, and zesty finish, it’s bound to become a beloved addition to your recipe collection. So, transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Mexico and savor the essence of elote with this delightful and versatile side dish. Cheers to the joy of cooking and the delight of savoring every flavorful bite!
