10 Authentic Italian Pasta Recipes You Need to Try


Italian cuisine is celebrated for its rich flavors, fresh ingredients, and comforting dishes. Among its most beloved contributions to the culinary world is pasta, a versatile staple that comes in numerous shapes and sizes. If you’re looking to elevate your cooking game, discover the vibrant tastes of Italy with these ten authentic Italian pasta recipes. Each recipe captures the essence of Italian cooking while offering a delightful exploration of flavors.

1. Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 100ml extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 red chili pepper, chopped (or 1 tsp red pepper flakes)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook the spaghetti until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red chili pepper, sautéing until the garlic is golden but not burnt.

  3. Combine: Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet, tossing to coat in the garlic oil. If the pasta seems dry, add a bit of the reserved pasta water.

  4. Finish: Stir in the chopped parsley, season with salt, and serve immediately. Top with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Tip:

For a twist, consider adding sautéed shrimp or cherry tomatoes for a pop of flavor.

2. Fettuccine Alfredo

Ingredients:

  • 300g fettuccine
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 200g freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Fettuccine: Cook the fettuccine in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve some pasta water before draining.

  2. Make the Alfredo Sauce: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the heavy cream, stirring until combined and heated through.

  3. Combine: Slowly whisk in the Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth. Add the cooked fettuccine, tossing to coat. If needed, add reserved pasta water to achieve your desired consistency.

  4. Season and Serve: Season with salt and black pepper, garnish with parsley, and serve immediately.

Tip:

For added depth, consider adding grilled chicken or sautéed mushrooms.

3. Penne Arrabbiata

Ingredients:

  • 400g penne pasta
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 800g canned tomatoes (crushed)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Penne: Boil the penne in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes, sautéing until fragrant.

  3. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes, season with salt, and let it simmer for about 15 minutes until the sauce thickens.

  4. Combine: Add the cooked penne and fresh basil to the sauce, tossing well to combine.

  5. Serve: Plate and finish with grated Parmesan cheese.

Tip:

For a smoky flavor, try adding pancetta or a touch of smoked paprika.

4. Lasagna alla Bolognese

Ingredients:

  • 400g lasagna sheets
  • 500g ground beef or pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g canned tomatoes (crushed)
  • 200ml béchamel sauce
  • 100g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bolognese Sauce: In a large skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until translucent. Add the ground meat, cooking until browned. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 30 minutes.

  2. Layer the Lasagna: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). In a baking dish, spread a layer of Bolognese sauce, followed by lasagna sheets, then béchamel sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with béchamel and a sprinkle of Parmesan.

  3. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until golden.

  4. Serve: Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Tip:

Experiment with adding spinach or mushrooms for extra flavor and nutrition.

5. Orecchiette with Broccoli Rabe

Ingredients:

  • 400g orecchiette pasta
  • 500g broccoli rabe, trimmed and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt, to taste
  • Grated Pecorino cheese (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Orecchiette: Boil the orecchiette in salted water until al dente, adding the broccoli rabe for the last 3 minutes of cooking. Drain.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, sautéing until fragrant.

  3. Combine: Add the drained orecchiette and broccoli rabe to the skillet, tossing to coat in the garlic oil. Season with salt.

  4. Serve: Plate and finish with grated Pecorino cheese.

Tip:

For a protein boost, add sautéed sausage or chickpeas.

6. Tagliatelle al Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 300g tagliatelle
  • 100g fresh basil leaves
  • 50g pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Tagliatelle: Boil the tagliatelle in salted water until al dente. Reserve some pasta water before draining.

  2. Make the Pesto: In a food processor, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan. With the processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until smooth. Season with salt.

  3. Combine: Toss the drained tagliatelle with the pesto, adding reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency.

  4. Serve: Garnish with extra Parmesan and pine nuts.

Tip:

For a variation, add sun-dried tomatoes or roasted red peppers to the pesto.

7. Ravioli di Ricotta e Spinaci

Ingredients:

  • 250g fresh ravioli (store-bought or homemade)
  • 200g ricotta cheese
  • 100g spinach, cooked and chopped
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 100g butter
  • Fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Ravioli: Boil the ravioli in salted water until they float to the surface. Drain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat and add sage leaves. Cook until the butter turns golden brown.

  3. Combine: Add cooked ravioli to the skillet, tossing gently to coat in the sage butter. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Serve: Plate and sprinkle with extra Parmesan cheese.

Tip:

For added flavor, consider adding a splash of lemon juice to brighten the dish.

8. Bucatini all’Amatriciana

Ingredients:

  • 400g bucatini pasta
  • 150g guanciale (or pancetta), diced
  • 400g canned tomatoes (crushed)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 100g grated Pecorino cheese
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Bucatini: Boil the bucatini in salted water until al dente. Drain and reserve some pasta water.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet, cook guanciale over medium heat until crispy. Add chopped onion and sauté until soft. Stir in crushed tomatoes and simmer for 15 minutes.

  3. Combine: Toss the cooked bucatini with the sauce, adding reserved pasta water as needed. Season with salt and black pepper.

  4. Serve: Plate and finish with grated Pecorino cheese.

Tip:

For a spicy kick, add red pepper flakes to the sauce.
