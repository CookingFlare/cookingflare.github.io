Homemade Paleo BBQ Sauce: A Simple and Delicious Recipe for Your Next Cookout


When it comes to BBQ sauce, many of us are accustomed to reaching for the bottle at the grocery store. But have you ever stopped to think about what’s really in that sauce? Preservatives, artificial flavorings, and refined sugars are just a few of the unwanted additives that can make your BBQ sauce less than ideal. The good news is that making your own BBQ sauce at home is easier than you think, and it allows you to control exactly what goes into it.

In this recipe, we’ll be making a delicious and healthy paleo BBQ sauce that’s perfect for your next cookout. This sauce is free from grains, dairy, and refined sugars, making it a great option for those following a paleo diet. But don’t worry, it still packs plenty of flavor and is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of fresh or canned pineapple juice
  • 1/2 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 cup of water

Instructions

To start, combine the pineapple juice, apple cider vinegar, coconut aminos, honey, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper in a medium-sized saucepan.

Whisk the mixture together until it’s smooth, then add in the water. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, whisking constantly.

Once the sauce has reached a simmer, reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, or until it’s thickened to your liking. You can test the thickness of the sauce by drizzling a small amount onto a plate and letting it cool to room temperature. If it’s too thin, continue to cook the sauce for a few more minutes. If it’s too thick, add in a little more water.

Once the sauce has reached the desired consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool to room temperature.

Tips and Variations

This paleo BBQ sauce recipe is incredibly versatile, and can be used on a variety of meats and vegetables. Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind:

  • For a spicier sauce, add in an additional 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes.
  • For a sweeter sauce, add in an additional 1-2 tablespoons of honey.
  • For a smokier sauce, add in an additional 1/4 teaspoon of liquid smoke.
  • This sauce is also delicious as a dip for vegetables, so feel free to serve it on the side.
  • You can also use this sauce as a marinade for your meats, simply by whisking it together with a little bit of oil and applying it to the meat before cooking.

Conclusion

Making your own BBQ sauce at home is a game-changer for anyone looking to avoid unwanted additives in their food. This paleo BBQ sauce recipe is not only delicious, but it’s also free from grains, dairy, and refined sugars, making it a great option for those following a paleo diet. With its tangy pineapple juice and smoky paprika, this sauce is sure to be a hit at your next cookout. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
