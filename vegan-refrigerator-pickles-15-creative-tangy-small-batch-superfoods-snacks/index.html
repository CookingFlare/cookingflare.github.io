Vegan Refrigerator Pickles: 15 Creative & Tangy Small Batch Superfoods Snacks


Preparing a homemade snack is always a delightful idea, especially when it involves healthier, plant-based ingredients. Today, let us explore the magical world of small batch vegan refrigerator pickles and how they can supplement your meals with an array of scrumptious superfood flavors. Dive into 15 unique recipes, and indulge in the tangy world of homemade vegan pickles!

1. Spicy Mango Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups thinly sliced mango
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp coconut sugar or maple syrup

Dressing:

  • 1 tbsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, mix sliced mango with water, apple cider vinegar, and sweetener.
  2. Prepare the dressing by combining red pepper flakes, sea salt, and cinnamon stick in a small bowl.
  3. Pour the dressing onto the mango slices, ensure they are fully coated.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a clean 1-quart jar, close the lid tightly, and refrigerate for at least 24 hours.

2. Roasted Garlic and Caramelized Onion Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves roasted garlic

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp dill seeds
  • 1 tsp sea salt

Method:

  1. In a pan over medium heat, saut√© sliced onions in coconut oil until caramelized.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix water, apple cider vinegar, and roasted garlic.
  3. Combine the caramelized onions with the remaining ingredients in a 1-quart jar.
  4. Refrigerate for 24 hours.

3. Turmeric-Kissed Cauliflower Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup small cauliflower florets
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp dill seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp sea salt

Method:

  1. Submerge cauliflower florets in water mixed with apple cider vinegar in a bowl.
  2. Prepare the dressing by combining turmeric powder, dill seeds, cumin seeds, and sea salt in a small bowl.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cauliflower florets.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a clean 1-quart jar, close the lid, and let it refrigerate for at least 24 hours.

4. Beetroot and Ginger Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded raw beetroots
  • 1 small ginger, sliced
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red chili flakes

Method:

  1. Place shredded beetroots and sliced ginger in a large bowl.
  2. Whisk water and apple cider vinegar together and pour over the beetroots and ginger.
  3. Mix salt, black peppercorns, and crushed red chili flakes in a small bowl, add it to the beetroots and ginger.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, close it and let it sit in the fridge for at least 24 hours.

5. Tangy Carrot and Fennel Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup small carrot chunks
  • 1 cup thinly sliced fennel
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp fennel seeds, crushed
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns

Method:

  1. Combine carrot chunks, sliced fennel, water, and apple cider vinegar in a large bowl.
  2. Mix crushed fennel seeds, sea salt, and black peppercorns in a small bowl, add it to the carrot-fennel mixture.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, let it sit in the fridge for at least 24 hours.

6. Zesty Lemon and Sweet Potato Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and julienned
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, mix julienned sweet potato with water, apple cider vinegar, and lemon juice.
  2. Prepare the dressing using sea salt and ground coriander.
  3. Add the dressing to the sweet potato mixture and combine well.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, let it sit in the fridge for at least 24 hours.

7. Purple Cabbage and Red Bell Pepper Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup thinly sliced purple cabbage
  • 1 cup thinly sliced red bell pepper
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns

Method:

  1. Mix sliced purple cabbage, red bell pepper, water, and apple cider vinegar in a large bowl.
  2. Prepare the dressing using caraway seeds, sea salt, and black peppercorns in a small bowl.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cabbage and bell pepper mixture.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, let it sit in the fridge for at least 24 hours.

8. Crunchy Jicama and Red Onion Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup small jicama chunks
  • 1 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Dressing:

  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns

Method:

  1. Mix jicama chunks, chopped red onion, water, and apple cider vinegar in a large bowl.
  2. Prepare the dressing using dried oregano, sea salt, and black peppercorns.
  3. Add dressing to the jicama-red onion mixture and combine well.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, refrigerate for at least 24 hours.

9. Roasted Cherry Tomato Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 400¬∞F and roast cherry tomatoes for 20 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix roasted cherry tomatoes, water, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Prepare the dressing by combining smoked paprika, sea salt, black peppercorns, and cumin seeds in a small bowl.
  4. Pour the dressing over the tomatoes and combine.
  5. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, let it sit in the fridge for at least 24 hours.

10. Radish and Microgreen Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup small radish chunks
  • 1 cup microgreens, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Dressing:

  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns

Method:

  1. Mix radish chunks, microgreens, water, and apple cider vinegar in a large bowl.
  2. Prepare the dressing by combining mustard seeds, sea salt, and black peppercorns.
  3. Add dressing to the radish and microgreen mixture and combine well.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a 1-quart jar, refrigerate for at least 24 hours.
