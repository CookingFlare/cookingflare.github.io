How to Grill Corn on the Cob: Best Methods


Grilling corn on the cob is one of the best ways to enjoy this versatile vegetable. The smoky flavor from the grill, combined with the natural sweetness of the corn, makes it a perfect addition to any summer BBQ or family meal. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a beginner, there are a few methods you can use to achieve perfectly grilled corn every time. In this guide, we’ll explore various techniques, tips, and tricks for grilling corn on the cob, ensuring it comes out tender, flavorful, and just the right amount of charred.

Why Grill Corn on the Cob?

Grilling corn on the cob not only brings out its natural sweetness but also imparts a deep, smoky flavor that can’t be matched by other cooking methods. The heat from the grill enhances the kernels, giving them a delightful caramelization on the outside while keeping the inside tender and juicy. The grill marks that form on the corn add an extra layer of texture, creating an irresistible visual appeal as well.

Moreover, grilling corn allows you to customize the flavors with various seasonings, herbs, and even cheeses. It’s a simple, yet elegant side dish that pairs well with almost any grilled meat, from burgers and steaks to grilled chicken and fish. So, let’s dive into the best methods for grilling corn on the cob!

How to Prepare Corn on the Cob for Grilling

Before you can start grilling corn, you need to prepare the ears properly. Whether you prefer to grill the corn with or without the husk, the preparation steps are quite simple. Here are some options for preparing your corn for grilling:

1. Removing the Husks and Silk (Husked Method)

If you want to achieve a charred, smoky flavor on the kernels, husking the corn is a great option. Follow these steps:

  • Peel off the outer husks, exposing the kernels.
  • Remove the silk (the thin threads inside the husk) by hand or with a soft brush.
  • Trim the stem of the corn so it is level with the bottom of the cob.

Once the corn is husked and cleaned, you’re ready to grill it.

2. Grilling with the Husk On (Husked Method)

If you prefer a more subtle flavor and want the corn to stay more moist, grilling the corn with the husk still on can be a great choice. The husks act as a natural barrier, keeping the kernels tender and juicy while still allowing the smoky flavor to penetrate.

  • Leave the husks on and peel them back slightly to remove the silk.
  • After removing the silk, fold the husks back over the corn.
  • Soak the corn in water for 10-15 minutes to prevent the husks from burning during grilling.

This method creates a gentler grilling process, preserving moisture while still giving a deliciously grilled flavor.

3. Peel Back the Husk and Grill with Butter (For a Richer Flavor)

Some people prefer grilling corn with the husk partially peeled back and using butter to enhance the flavor. If you’re going this route, the steps are simple:

  • Peel the husks back carefully, but don’t remove them entirely.
  • Spread a thin layer of butter or olive oil over the kernels and then fold the husks back around the corn.
  • This method infuses the corn with extra richness, thanks to the fat in the butter.

This method adds an extra layer of flavor and is perfect for those who love their corn with a bit more decadence.

Best Methods for Grilling Corn on the Cob

Now that you’ve prepared the corn, it’s time to get it on the grill. There are several different methods to grill corn on the cob, each offering unique flavors and textures. Here are some of the best ways to grill corn on the cob:

Method 1: Direct Grilling (High Heat)

Direct grilling is one of the most popular methods for grilling corn. This method allows the corn to develop a delicious charred exterior while maintaining a tender interior.

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the husked corn directly on the grill grates. You want the corn to cook over direct heat, which will give it that signature smoky flavor and beautiful grill marks.
  3. Turn the corn frequently to ensure all sides are evenly charred. This should take about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Once the corn is evenly charred with grill marks on all sides, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

For extra flavor, you can brush the corn with butter or oil, seasoning it with salt, pepper, or your favorite spices before grilling.

Method 2: Grilling with the Husk On (Indirect Heat)

Grilling corn with the husk on is ideal for those who want a more traditional, moist result. This method uses indirect heat to cook the corn, and the husk helps to steam the kernels inside.

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Place the corn with the husk on the grill, but instead of directly over the flames, position the corn on the cooler side of the grill, allowing for indirect heat.
  3. Cover the grill and cook for 20-25 minutes, turning the corn every 5 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  4. When the corn is done, carefully peel back the husks (they’ll be hot!) and serve. You can also add butter, salt, and other toppings for additional flavor.

This method is great for those who like their corn extra juicy and tender, with a subtle smokiness.

Method 3: Foil-Wrapped Corn

If you want to lock in the moisture and ensure that the corn is tender without much hassle, wrapping it in foil is a foolproof method.

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Peel back the husks slightly (or remove them completely), and remove the silk from the corn.
  3. Wrap the corn tightly in aluminum foil, ensuring it’s fully enclosed.
  4. Place the foil-wrapped corn on the grill over medium heat, cooking for 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally.
  5. After the corn has finished grilling, carefully remove the foil and check for tenderness. Serve with butter or seasoning of your choice.

This method results in beautifully moist corn, as the foil traps steam and allows the corn to cook evenly.

Method 4: Grilled Corn on the Cobb (Smoky Flavor with Grilled Char)

If you want a more smoky, charred result, try grilling the corn directly on the grill grates for that crisp, charred exterior. This method is ideal if you love that deep smoky flavor that grilling imparts.

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  2. Place the corn on the grill without husks or foil. It is recommended to lightly oil the corn or brush it with a layer of butter before grilling to prevent sticking.
  3. Grill the corn for about 10-12 minutes, turning it every few minutes to ensure an even char on all sides.
  4. Check for doneness—the corn should have a nice char and be tender inside.
  5. Serve immediately, and season to taste with your preferred toppings.

This method is perfect for getting that crispy, grilled flavor, with just the right amount of smokiness.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Corn on the Cob

  • Don’t overcook the corn: Overcooking can lead to dried-out kernels. Be sure to check your corn frequently while grilling to ensure it stays tender and juicy.
  • Season it right: Corn is naturally sweet, so pairing it with spicy, salty, or buttery seasonings can balance the flavor. Try chili powder, garlic butter, fresh herbs, or even grated Parmesan cheese.
  • Let it rest: After grilling, let the corn rest for a few minutes before serving. This allows the steam to finish cooking the corn while keeping it juicy.
  • Add a squeeze of lime: For a fresh and zesty twist, drizzle your grilled corn with fresh lime juice. It adds a refreshing contrast to the smoky flavor.
  • Try flavored butters: Experiment with compound butters, such as garlic butter, cilantro lime butter, or even a spicy Sriracha butter, to elevate the flavor of your grilled corn.

Conclusion

Grilling corn on the cob is an easy and rewarding way to enjoy one of summer’s most beloved vegetables. Whether you prefer a simple, no-fuss method or love experimenting with different flavors, there’s a grilling technique for everyone. By following the tips and methods outlined above, you’ll be able to create the perfect grilled corn every time. So, fire up the grill, prepare your favorite seasoning, and enjoy this delicious side dish at your next BBQ or family gathering!
