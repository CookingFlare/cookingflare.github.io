How to Make Homemade Oatmeal Cream Pies


Oatmeal cream pies are a classic treat that combines the wholesome flavors of oatmeal with the indulgence of a creamy filling. These soft, chewy, and perfectly spiced cookies are a nostalgic favorite for many, and making them from scratch is easier than you might think. Whether you’re craving a sweet snack or preparing for a special event, this homemade version of oatmeal cream pies will delight your taste buds and impress your friends and family.

In this post, we’ll walk you through every step of the process, from making the oatmeal cookies to assembling the delicious cream filling. We’ll also provide helpful tips and variations to make this treat uniquely yours.

Ingredients for Homemade Oatmeal Cookies

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to gather all the ingredients you’ll need for the oatmeal cookies. Here’s the list:

For the Oatmeal Cookies:

  • 1 ½ cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup raisins (optional, but they add a nice touch)
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional, for added texture)

For the Cream Filling:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons milk (or more to reach the desired consistency)
  • Pinch of salt

These ingredients will yield about 12 oatmeal cream pies, depending on the size of the cookies. Feel free to double or halve the recipe based on your needs.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Oatmeal Cream Pies

Now that we have everything ready, let’s dive into the preparation process. Follow these easy steps to make your homemade oatmeal cream pies.

1. Preheat Your Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This will give it enough time to heat up while you prepare the dough.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. This mixture will provide the dry ingredients needed for your cookie dough, ensuring everything is evenly combined. Set the bowl aside.

3. Cream the Butter and Sugars

In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to cream the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together. Beat on medium speed for 3-4 minutes until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial for achieving soft, chewy cookies.

4. Add Eggs and Vanilla Extract

Next, add the eggs to the creamed butter and sugar mixture, one at a time, making sure to fully incorporate each egg before adding the next. Stir in the vanilla extract. These ingredients will enhance the flavor and contribute to the moisture of the cookies.

5. Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough cookies. Once the dough is ready, it should be slightly sticky and thick.

6. Stir in the Oats and Optional Add-ins

Using a spatula or spoon, gently fold in the oats, raisins, and chopped nuts (if using). The oats will give the cookies their signature chewy texture, while the raisins and nuts provide additional flavor and crunch.

7. Scoop the Dough

To form the cookies, use a tablespoon or cookie scoop to measure out even portions of dough. Roll the dough into balls and place them onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spacing them about 2 inches apart. If you prefer uniform cookies, you can lightly flatten each dough ball with the back of a spoon.

8. Bake the Cookies

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are set. Keep in mind that the cookies will continue to cook a little after you take them out of the oven due to residual heat.

9. Cool the Cookies

Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for about 5 minutes. After that, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Allowing the cookies to cool ensures they won’t become too soft when you add the cream filling.

How to Make the Cream Filling

While the cookies are cooling, it’s time to prepare the creamy filling that will go between the cookies. The cream filling should be smooth, sweet, and slightly fluffy. Here’s how to make it:

1. Beat the Butter and Powdered Sugar

In a medium-sized bowl, beat the softened butter and powdered sugar together until smooth and creamy. This should take about 2-3 minutes using an electric mixer on medium speed.

2. Add Vanilla Extract and Milk

Next, stir in the vanilla extract and add the milk, one tablespoon at a time, until the filling reaches your desired consistency. If you like your cream filling thick, you can use less milk. For a softer filling, add a little more milk.

3. Add a Pinch of Salt

Add a small pinch of salt to balance the sweetness of the filling. The salt will enhance the flavor and make the cream taste even richer.

4. Mix Until Smooth

Once everything is combined, beat the mixture for another 1-2 minutes until it is light and fluffy. You want the filling to be spreadable but not too runny.

Assembling the Oatmeal Cream Pies

Now that both the oatmeal cookies and cream filling are ready, it’s time to assemble your homemade oatmeal cream pies!

1. Pair the Cookies

Choose two cookies that are of similar size and shape to create a perfect sandwich. If any cookies are slightly uneven in size, you can trim the edges to make them more uniform.

2. Spread the Cream Filling

Take a generous amount of the cream filling and spread it on the flat side of one cookie. You want enough filling to create a thick, satisfying layer but not so much that it spills out when you press the cookies together.

3. Sandwich the Cookies

Place the second cookie, flat side down, onto the cream-covered cookie, pressing gently to sandwich the two cookies together. The filling should squeeze out a little at the edges, creating a perfect oatmeal cream pie.

4. Repeat the Process

Continue assembling the remaining oatmeal cream pies, spreading the filling and sandwiching the cookies until all are completed. If there’s any extra filling, you can spread it on the cookies for an extra creamy touch.

Tips for the Best Oatmeal Cream Pies

Making oatmeal cream pies from scratch can be a fun and rewarding experience, but there are a few tips and tricks to ensure your cookies turn out perfectly every time:

  • Use Old-Fashioned Rolled Oats: Old-fashioned oats provide the best texture for oatmeal cookies. Avoid quick oats, as they can make the cookies too soft and mushy.
  • Chill the Dough: If you prefer thicker cookies, refrigerate the cookie dough for about 30 minutes before baking. This helps the dough firm up and prevents the cookies from spreading too much while baking.
  • Customize Your Filling: Feel free to get creative with the cream filling! You can add a little bit of marshmallow fluff for extra fluffiness or a splash of almond extract for a unique twist.
  • Storage: Homemade oatmeal cream pies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. They can also be refrigerated for a slightly firmer filling. If you want to keep them for longer, these treats freeze well too! Just wrap each pie individually in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag.

Variations of Oatmeal Cream Pies

While the classic oatmeal cream pie recipe is undeniably delicious, there are plenty of ways to put your spin on this dessert. Here are a few ideas:

  • Chocolate Oatmeal Cream Pies: Add a handful of mini chocolate chips to the cookie dough or drizzle melted chocolate on the finished cream pies for a rich chocolate twist.
  • Spicy Oatmeal Cream Pies: Amp up the flavor with a pinch of nutmeg, ground ginger, or cloves in the cookie dough for an extra layer of spice.
  • Peanut Butter Filling: Swap the traditional cream filling for a peanut butter filling made with creamy peanut butter, powdered sugar, and a little butter. The combination of oatmeal cookies and peanut butter is irresistible.
  • Fruit Additions: Stir dried cranberries, dried apricots, or chopped apples into the cookie dough for a fruity surprise in every bite.

Final Thoughts

Making oatmeal cream pies from scratch is an enjoyable and rewarding experience. Whether you’re baking them for a special occasion or simply as a sweet treat for yourself, these homemade pies are bound to impress. With a chewy oatmeal cookie base and a creamy, sweet filling, oatmeal cream pies are the perfect combination of textures and flavors. Plus, they’re easy to customize, so you can make them your own.

Once you’ve made your batch, be prepared for compliments and requests for the recipe. These homemade oatmeal cream pies are sure to become a favorite treat in your household!

Enjoy every bite and happy baking!
