Quick and Easy Middle Eastern Street Food Recipes


Middle Eastern cuisine is rich in flavor, spices, and textures, with street food playing a vital role in the culinary scene. Whether you’re in a bustling market in Cairo, a vibrant alley in Istanbul, or a lively street in Beirut, the aroma of freshly prepared street food fills the air. From savory kebabs to delicious pastries, Middle Eastern street food is diverse, offering something for everyone. Here, we’ll explore some quick and easy Middle Eastern street food recipes that you can recreate at home. These recipes not only capture the essence of Middle Eastern flavors but are also simple to make, ensuring that anyone can enjoy these delicious dishes.

1. Falafel

Falafel is one of the most popular Middle Eastern street foods, loved by vegetarians and meat-eaters alike. These crispy, deep-fried balls are made from chickpeas (or fava beans) mixed with herbs, spices, and garlic. They are often served in pita bread with salad, tahini sauce, and pickles, making them a satisfying snack or meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2-4 tablespoons flour (as needed)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is finely chopped but not completely smooth.
  2. Add the baking powder and 2 tablespoons of flour. Pulse again until the mixture is well combined. If the mixture is too wet, add a bit more flour until it holds together.
  3. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties, about the size of a golf ball.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the falafel balls and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Remove the falafel from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve in pita bread with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, pickles, and tahini sauce.

2. Shawarma

Shawarma is another iconic Middle Eastern street food that is loved worldwide. It consists of thinly sliced marinated meat (often lamb, chicken, or beef) that’s cooked on a vertical rotisserie. In this quick recipe, we replicate the flavors of shawarma by marinating chicken breasts and grilling them to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pita or flatbreads for serving

For the Garlic Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together olive oil, cumin, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, coriander, garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Rub the marinade all over the chicken breasts and let them sit for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator).
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan over medium heat. Cook the chicken breasts for 5-6 minutes per side until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the garlic sauce by combining yogurt, mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, and salt in a bowl. Stir until smooth and set aside.
  5. Once the chicken is done, slice it thinly against the grain.
  6. Serve the shawarma slices in pita or flatbreads, drizzling with the garlic sauce. Add your favorite toppings, such as pickled vegetables, cucumbers, tomatoes, and lettuce.

3. Samosa

Samosas are popular in many Middle Eastern countries, especially in street food markets. These savory pastries are filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat, then fried until crispy. They are perfect as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 10-12 samosa wrappers or filo pastry sheets
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a pot of boiling water, cook the diced potatoes until tender, about 10-12 minutes. Add the peas in the last 3 minutes of cooking. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, stirring until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the cooked potatoes and peas to the pan. Mash the mixture lightly and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper, then let it cool to room temperature.
  5. To assemble the samosas, place a samosa wrapper on a flat surface. Fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with a little water. Fill the cone with the potato mixture and seal the top to form a triangular shape.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the samosas in batches for 3-4 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with tamarind or mint chutney.

4. Hummus and Pita

Hummus is a classic Middle Eastern dip made from chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and olive oil. It’s often served as part of a mezze platter alongside pita bread, vegetables, or falafel. Making your own hummus at home is incredibly simple and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pita bread, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, and salt. Process until smooth and creamy, adding water a tablespoon at a time if needed to achieve the desired consistency.
  2. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more lemon juice, garlic, or salt if necessary.
  3. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Serve with warm pita bread, cut into wedges.

5. Manakish (Za’atar Flatbread)

Manakish is a beloved street food in Lebanon and other parts of the Middle East. It’s a flatbread topped with a mixture of za’atar (a blend of thyme, sesame seeds, and sumac) and olive oil. Sometimes it’s baked with cheese or meat, but the classic za’atar version is simple, fragrant, and utterly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons za’atar seasoning

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, and yeast. Gradually add warm water and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  2. Cover the dough with a cloth and let it rise for 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.
  3. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into 4 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and then flatten into a circle about 6 inches in diameter.
  4. Place the dough circles on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush each with olive oil and sprinkle generously with za’atar seasoning.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drizzle with a little more olive oil before serving. Enjoy the manakish as a snack or part of a larger meal.

6. Kebab Skewers

Kebabs are a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine, and they are often cooked over open flames or on a grill. These simple grilled skewers of marinated meat (usually lamb, chicken, or beef) are a great option for street food.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken, lamb, or beef, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Skewers

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, coriander, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the cubed meat to the marinade and mix well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  4. Grill the kebabs for 4-5 minutes per side until they are cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Serve the kebabs with pita bread, grilled vegetables, and a side of garlic sauce or yogurt.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern street food is a celebration of flavor, texture, and tradition. From the crispy falafel to the savory shawarma and aromatic manakish, these dishes are perfect for anyone looking to explore new tastes. The best part? These recipes are quick, easy, and accessible, so you can bring the vibrant flavors of the Middle East right into your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or enjoying a quiet evening at home, these street food recipes will surely impress. Enjoy these flavorful dishes and transport yourself to the bustling streets of the Middle East!
