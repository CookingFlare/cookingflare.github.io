Classic Tonkatsu: Crispy Breaded Pork Cutlet


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve probably encountered Tonkatsu at some point. This beloved dish features a crispy breaded pork cutlet that is both comforting and flavorful. The combination of tender pork and a crunchy coating makes Tonkatsu a favorite in both homes and restaurants. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to recreate this classic dish in your own kitchen, allowing you to enjoy the delightful taste of Tonkatsu anytime you want.

Ingredients

For this recipe, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 pork loin chops, about 1-inch thick
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Shredded cabbage, for serving
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork: Start by seasoning the pork loin chops with salt and pepper on both sides. This will enhance the natural flavor of the meat.

  2. Coat the Pork: Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes. Place the flour in the first dish, beat the eggs in the second dish, and put the panko breadcrumbs in the third dish. Take each pork chop and coat it in the flour, then dip it into the beaten eggs, and finally coat it with the panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the pork.

  3. Fry the Pork: In a large skillet, heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the breaded pork chops to the skillet. Cook for about 5-6 minutes on each side, or until the pork is fully cooked and the coating is golden brown and crispy.

  4. Slice and Serve: Once the pork chops are cooked, transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Let them rest for a few minutes before slicing them into strips. Serve the Tonkatsu with shredded cabbage, a side of rice, and a drizzle of Tonkatsu sauce.

Tips for Success

  • Pounding the Pork: If your pork loin chops are thicker than 1 inch, consider pounding them to an even thickness before breading. This will ensure that the pork cooks evenly and the coating doesn’t burn before the meat is fully cooked.

  • Choosing the Right Oil: When frying the Tonkatsu, use a neutral-flavored oil with a high smoke point, such as vegetable or canola oil. This will help achieve a crispy and golden-brown crust on the pork.

  • Resting the Pork: Allowing the cooked pork to rest for a few minutes before slicing will help retain its juiciness and tenderness.

Conclusion

Tonkatsu is a delightful dish that brings together the satisfying crunch of the breaded pork cutlet with the umami-rich Tonkatsu sauce. Whether you’re serving it as a main course with rice and cabbage or using it to make a flavorful sandwich, this classic Japanese favorite is sure to become a regular in your cooking repertoire. With this recipe, you can recreate the crispy goodness of Tonkatsu in your own kitchen and impress your family and friends with a taste of Japan. Enjoy!
