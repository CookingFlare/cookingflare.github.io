Haitian National Recipes You'll Love to Master


Haitian cuisine is a vibrant and flavorful fusion of African, French, and Caribbean influences. The rich history of Haiti is reflected in its food, which blends spices, fresh herbs, and local ingredients to create meals that are deeply satisfying and packed with bold flavors. Whether you’re looking to explore new dishes or bring a taste of the island to your kitchen, mastering Haitian recipes is an incredible culinary journey. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most beloved Haitian national dishes you’ll want to add to your cooking repertoire.

1. Griot (Fried Pork)

What is Griot?

Griot is one of Haiti’s most iconic dishes, often served during special occasions, parties, and holidays. It consists of marinated and fried pork, known for its tender, juicy interior and crispy exterior. The marination process involves a mix of citrus, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers, which imbue the pork with a distinct and robust flavor. Griot is often paired with a side of pikliz, a spicy pickled vegetable mixture that perfectly complements the savory pork.

How to Make Griot

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs of pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup of orange juice
  • 1/4 cup of lime juice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (or your preferred hot pepper), chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of thyme
  • 2 tablespoons of parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cups of vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Pork: In a large bowl, combine the pork cubes with orange juice, lime juice, vinegar, garlic, onion, scotch bonnet peppers, thyme, parsley, salt, and black pepper. Mix everything well, ensuring the pork is coated in the marinade. Let it sit in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours, preferably overnight for the best flavor infusion.
  2. Fry the Pork: Heat the vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the marinated pork cubes in batches, being mindful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 5-7 minutes until the pork is golden and crispy on the outside, but tender on the inside.
  3. Serve: Remove the pork from the oil and drain on paper towels. Serve with pikliz (Haitian spicy pickled vegetables) and fried plantains for a complete meal.

2. Joumou (Haitian Squash Soup)

The Significance of Joumou

Joumou is not just a delicious soup; it’s a dish of great historical and cultural significance. On January 1st, Haitians celebrate their independence from French colonial rule, and one of the symbols of this liberation is the preparation of Joumou. Traditionally, the French colonists forbade enslaved Africans from eating joumou squash, deeming it a food reserved for the elite. After Haiti’s independence, the newly freed people celebrated by making and eating this soup. Today, it remains a staple of Haitian cuisine, especially on Independence Day.

How to Make Joumou

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized joumou (yellow squash), peeled and chopped
  • 1 lb of beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, whole
  • 6 cups of beef or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup of lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup of pasta (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Squash: In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the chopped squash. Boil for about 15 minutes until soft. Once cooked, mash the squash and set it aside.
  2. Cook the Beef: In the same pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned. Remove the beef and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion, garlic, celery, carrots, and bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables begin to soften, about 5 minutes.
  4. Combine and Simmer: Return the beef to the pot along with the mashed squash. Add the broth, thyme, scotch bonnet pepper, tomato paste, and lime juice. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Add Potatoes and Pasta: Add the potatoes and pasta (if using) to the pot and cook until they are tender, about 15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot.

3. Marinad (Haitian Fritters)

What is Marinad?

Marinad are savory Haitian fritters that make for an irresistible snack or appetizer. They are typically served as a street food and are easy to make at home. These crispy, golden bites are filled with a combination of savory ingredients like salted cod, onions, and hot peppers, all wrapped up in a fluffy batter. Whether enjoyed on their own or as part of a larger meal, Marinad is a delicious and comforting treat.

How to Make Marinad

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb salted cod, soaked overnight and shredded
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cod: After soaking the salted cod overnight, drain and shred the fish into small pieces. Set aside.
  2. Make the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, and chopped parsley. Slowly add the water and mix until a smooth batter forms. Add the shredded cod, chopped onions, and scotch bonnet peppers to the batter and stir to combine.
  3. Fry the Marinad: Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Using a spoon, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil, forming small fritters. Fry for about 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Serve: Drain on paper towels and serve hot with a side of pikliz or a dipping sauce.

4. Akra (Haitian Malanga Fritters)

What is Akra?

Akra is another popular Haitian fritter, but instead of using flour and cod, this dish is made with malanga, a root vegetable that is similar to yam. Malanga is finely grated and mixed with a flavorful seasoning blend before being fried into crispy golden fritters. These are perfect as a snack, appetizer, or side dish.

How to Make Akra

Ingredients:

  • 2 large malangas, peeled and grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1/4 cup of parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Malanga: After peeling and grating the malanga, place it in a clean kitchen towel and squeeze out any excess moisture.
  2. Make the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine the grated malanga, onion, garlic, scotch bonnet pepper, thyme, parsley, salt, pepper, and flour. Mix everything until well combined.
  3. Fry the Akra: Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Using your hands, form small patties with the malanga mixture and carefully drop them into the hot oil. Fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve: Drain on paper towels and serve with a spicy sauce or pikliz for an extra kick.

5. Poisson Gros Sel (Haitian Salted Fish)

The Essence of Poisson Gros Sel

Poisson Gros Sel, or salted fish, is a traditional Haitian dish often served with rice and beans or fried plantains. The dish features fish that has been salted and preserved, imparting a rich and intense flavor. It’s a hearty and filling meal that’s perfect for any time of the day, and it highlights the Haitian love for seafood.

How to Make Poisson Gros Sel

Ingredients:

  • 2 salted fish fillets (such as cod or mackerel)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of lime juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1/2 cup of tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fish: Rinse the salted fish to remove excess salt. Soak it in water for a few hours, changing the water once or twice to reduce the saltiness. After soaking, shred the fish into small pieces.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In a pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, bell pepper, and scotch bonnet pepper. Sauté until softened.
  3. Cook the Fish: Add the shredded fish to the pan along with the vinegar, lime juice, thyme, tomatoes, and green onions. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is well combined and heated through.
  4. Serve: Serve with rice and beans or fried plantains for a complete Haitian meal.

Haitian cuisine is a treasure trove of bold, vibrant flavors and unforgettable dishes. From the crispy Griot to the hearty Joumou soup, each recipe tells a story of Haiti’s rich cultural heritage. Mastering these dishes will not only broaden your cooking skills but also connect you to the rich history and traditions of this Caribbean gem. Whether you’re cooking for family, friends, or just yourself, these recipes are sure to bring the warmth and joy of Haitian cuisine into your home.
