Thai Green Curry with Shrimp: A Creamy and Spicy Combination


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’re probably no stranger to the delightful flavors of Thai green curry. This vibrant and aromatic dish is a perfect balance of creamy coconut milk, fragrant herbs, and fiery spices. When combined with succulent shrimp, it creates a mouthwatering fusion of flavors that will leave your taste buds tingling with delight. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating this classic Thai dish right in your own kitchen. So, let’s dive into the world of Thai green curry with shrimp and learn how to bring this exotic culinary experience to your table.

Ingredients

For the Green Curry Paste:

  • 2-3 fresh green Thai chilies
  • 1 shallot, peeled and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, thinly sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of galangal or ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon shrimp paste
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves and stems

For the Curry:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Green Curry Paste:

    • In a food processor, combine all the green curry paste ingredients and process until it forms a smooth paste. Set aside.

  2. Cook the Shrimp:

    • In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Make the Green Curry:

    • In the same skillet, add the green curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until it becomes fragrant.
    • Pour in the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth, stirring to combine.
    • Add the red bell pepper and bamboo shoots, then bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  4. Add the Shrimp and Season:

    • Once the curry base has simmered for a few minutes, return the cooked shrimp to the skillet.
    • Stir in the fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting the seasoning to your taste.
    • Toss in the Thai basil and kaffir lime leaves, letting them infuse their flavors into the curry.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Allow the curry to simmer for another 5 minutes, ensuring the shrimp are fully cooked and the flavors are well blended.
    • Remove from heat and serve the Thai green curry with shrimp over steamed jasmine rice or with a side of warm, flaky roti.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra aromatic touch, lightly bruise the lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves before adding them to the curry.
  • Adjust the spiciness by adding more or fewer green Thai chilies to suit your preference.
  • If you can’t find fresh galangal, use ginger as a substitute for a similar flavor profile.

Final Thoughts

Thai green curry with shrimp is a culinary masterpiece that encapsulates the essence of Thai cuisine. The creamy coconut milk, fragrant herbs, and bold spices come together to create a dish that is both comforting and exhilarating. By following this recipe, you can embark on a culinary journey to Thailand right in your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, unleash your inner chef, and treat yourself to the delightful flavors of Thai green curry with shrimp. Your taste buds will thank you for this creamy and spicy adventure!
