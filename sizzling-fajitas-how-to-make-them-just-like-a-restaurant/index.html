Sizzling Fajitas: How to Make Them Just Like a Restaurant


There's something magical about a sizzling plate of fajitas arriving at your table, the aroma of sizzling onions and bell peppers filling the air as the chef juggles a sizzle pan with a flourish. The sound of metal clinking, the presentation, and the taste all come together to create an unforgettable dining experience. But did you know that you don't need to rely on a restaurant to satisfy your fajita cravings? With a few basic kitchen tools and ingredients, you can create a mouth-watering, restaurant-worthy sizzling fajita dish right in the comfort of your own home. In this recipe, we'll guide you through the steps to prepare a classic beef fajita recipe with an added twist of arrachera steak, a popular choice in Mexican cuisine. Feel free to substitute the beef with chicken or vegetables for a delicious vegetarian option. Ingredients

1 lb beef arrachera steak or flank steak, sliced into thin strips 1/2 cup fajita seasoning mix 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into thin strips 1 large onion, sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 large tomatoes, cored and chopped 1 lime, cut into wedges 8-10 small flour tortillas Optional toppings: shredded cheese, diced avocado, sour cream, cilantro, salsa

Equipment Needed

Large cast-iron skillet or comal Spatula Measuring cups and spoons Spices bottle with fajita seasoning mix Tortilla warmer (optional)

Step 1: Prepare the Fajita Seasoning Mix If using homemade fajita seasoning, combine all the spices in a small bowl and mix well. Otherwise, use store-bought fajita seasoning according to the package instructions. Step 2: Season the Steak In a shallow dish, mix the fajita seasoning mix with the olive oil and brush the mixture evenly on the steak. Set aside and let the meat marinate for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to penetrate. Step 3: Heat the Sizzle Pan Preheat the sizzle pan or comal over the stovetop to medium-high heat. The pan should be searing hot when adding the steak; we want those beautiful fajita brown spots. While the pan heats up, start slicing the onion, bell peppers, and tomatoes. Ensure these are all set to add to the pan. Step 4: Sear the Steak When the pan is hot, add the steak and sear for 3-4 minutes per side, depending on the desired level of doneness. Be careful not to press down on the steak, as this will squeeze out the juices. Remove the steak from the pan and set it aside to rest while you add the onion, bell peppers, and garlic. Step 5: Add the Aromatics Add the sliced onion to the hot pan and sauté until they start to caramelize, stirring occasionally. After 5 minutes, add the sliced bell peppers, stirring occasionally until the onion and peppers are almost tender. Reduce heat to low and simmer the mix. Step 6: Add the Tomatoes and Seasoning Add the chopped tomatoes and stir in the minced garlic into the pan. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Add fajita seasoning mix, salt, and pepper according to taste. Stir well and simmer for 1 minute. Step 7: Slice the Rested Steak Slice the rested steak into thin fajita-style strips. Step 8: Assemble the Fajitas Using the preheated sizzle pan, add the sliced steak to the onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes mixture. Toss the ingredients with the steak to combine. Step 9: Warm the Tortillas Warm your small flour tortillas according to your preference. If using a tortilla warmer, warm the tortillas for several minutes to soften them. Step 10: Assemble Your Fajita Plate Add the steak, pepper and onion mixture, and lime wedges on a serving plate or individual plate, according to your preference. Remember to let everyone assemble their own tacos for the best flavor and presentation. Tips to Make it Like a Restaurant To create a memorable restaurant-style dish at home:

The sound of metal clinking, the presentation, and the taste all come together to create an unforgettable dining experience. But did you know that you don't need to rely on a restaurant to satisfy your fajita cravings? With a few basic kitchen tools and ingredients, you can create a mouth-watering, restaurant-worthy sizzling fajita dish right in the comfort of your own home. Ingredients

1 lb beef arrachera steak or flank steak, sliced into thin strips 1/2 cup fajita seasoning mix 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into thin strips 1 large onion, sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 large tomatoes, cored and chopped 1 lime, cut into wedges 8-10 small flour tortillas Optional toppings: shredded cheese, diced avocado, sour cream, cilantro, salsa

Equipment Needed

Large cast-iron skillet or comal Spatula Measuring cups and spoons Spices bottle with fajita seasoning mix Tortilla warmer (optional)

Preparing the Fajita Seasoning Mix If using homemade fajita seasoning, combine all the spices in a small bowl and mix well. Otherwise, use store-bought fajita seasoning according to the package instructions. Step 1: Season the Steak In a shallow dish, mix the fajita seasoning mix with the olive oil and brush the mixture evenly on the steak. Set aside and let the meat marinate for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to penetrate. Step 2: Heat the Sizzle Pan Preheat the sizzle pan or comal over the stovetop to medium-high heat. The pan should be searing hot when adding the steak; we want those beautiful fajita brown spots. While the pan heats up, start slicing the onion, bell peppers, and tomatoes. Ensure these are all set to add to the pan. Step 3: Sear the Steak When the pan is hot, add the steak and sear for 3-4 minutes per side, depending on the desired level of doneness. Be careful not to press down on the steak, as this will squeeze out the juices. Remove the steak from the pan and set it aside to rest while you add the onion, bell peppers, and garlic. Step 4: Add the Aromatics Add the sliced onion to the hot pan and sauté until they start to caramelize, stirring occasionally. After 5 minutes, add the sliced bell peppers, stirring occasionally until the onion and peppers are almost tender. Reduce heat to low and simmer the mix. Step 5: Add the Tomatoes and Seasoning Add the chopped tomatoes and stir in the minced garlic into the pan. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Add fajita seasoning mix, salt, and pepper according to taste. Stir well and simmer for 1 minute. Step 6: Slice the Rested Steak Slice the rested steak into thin fajita-style strips. Step 7: Assemble the Fajitas Using the preheated sizzle pan, add the sliced steak to the onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes mixture. Toss the ingredients with the steak to combine. Step 8: Warm the Tortillas Warm your small flour tortillas according to your preference. If using a tortilla warmer, warm the tortillas for several minutes to soften them. Step 9: Assemble Your Fajita Plate Add the steak, pepper and onion mixture, and lime wedges on a serving plate or individual plate, according to your preference. Remember to let everyone assemble their own tacos for the best flavor and presentation. Tips to Make it Like a Restaurant To create a memorable restaurant-style dish at home:

Slice the finished dish right before serving. Freshness is paramount for the soft taste, crunchy texture, and the availability of the ingredients. Use a pre-cooked and seasoned salsa to save time in the kitchen.

With these simple steps, you can enjoy a delicious, sizzling fajita dish, just like at your favorite restaurant.
