Classic Vanilla Cupcakes with Fresh Blueberry Frosting

If you’re looking for a delightful treat that captures the essence of summer, look no further than classic vanilla cupcakes with a twist of fresh blueberry frosting. These cupcakes are a perfect blend of timeless flavors combined with the vibrant, sweet taste of blueberries. Whether you’re hosting a summer party, a birthday celebration, or just want to indulge in a delicious homemade dessert, these cupcakes are sure to impress. Ingredients For the Vanilla Cupcakes: 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder ¼ teaspoon salt ½ cup unsalted butter, softened 1 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract ½ cup whole milk For the Fresh Blueberry Frosting: 1 ½ cups fresh blueberries 1 cup unsalted butter, softened 4 cups confectioners’ sugar 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract 2-3 tablespoons heavy cream Instructions Vanilla Cupcakes: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate large bowl, cream the butter and sugar together until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients, alternating with the milk, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Pour the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely before frosting. Fresh Blueberry Frosting: In a small saucepan, heat the blueberries over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until they release their juices and soften. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Once the blueberries have cooled, puree them in a blender or food processor until smooth. In a large bowl, beat the butter until creamy, then gradually add the confectioners’ sugar, vanilla extract, and 2 tablespoons of heavy cream. Mix in the blueberry puree until the frosting is smooth and fluffy. If the frosting is too thick, add an additional tablespoon of heavy cream and mix until the desired consistency is reached. Assembling the Cupcakes: Once the cupcakes are completely cooled, generously frost each cupcake with the fresh blueberry frosting. If desired, top each cupcake with a fresh blueberry for an extra pop of color and flavor. Serve and enjoy these delightful classic vanilla cupcakes with fresh blueberry frosting! Tips and Variations For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a hint of lemon zest to the cupcake batter or the frosting. If fresh blueberries are not in season, frozen blueberries can be used to make the frosting. Experiment with different piping techniques to create beautiful designs with the frosting. Conclusion Classic vanilla cupcakes with fresh blueberry frosting are a delightful combination of comforting vanilla flavors and the bright, fruity taste of blueberries. These cupcakes are perfect for any occasion and are sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who tries them. With simple yet flavorful ingredients, this recipe is a wonderful way to celebrate the season and create delicious memories with friends and family.
