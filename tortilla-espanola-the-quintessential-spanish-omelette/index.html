Tortilla Espanola: The Quintessential Spanish Omelette


If you’re looking for a classic Spanish dish that is both delicious and versatile, look no further than the Tortilla Española, also known as the Spanish omelette. This iconic dish has been a staple in Spanish cuisine for centuries, and it’s no wonder why. With its simple yet flavorful ingredients, the Tortilla Española is a crowd-pleaser that can be enjoyed at any time of the day.

Ingredients

To make your own Tortilla Española, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 6 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Preparation

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into thin slices. Finely chop the onion.

  2. In a large frying pan, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onions to the pan, ensuring they are evenly coated in oil. Sprinkle some salt over the mixture.

  3. Cook the potatoes and onions over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and lightly golden. This usually takes around 15-20 minutes. Be careful not to let them brown too much.

  4. While the potatoes are cooking, crack the eggs into a large bowl. Beat the eggs until they are well combined.

  5. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, use a slotted spoon to transfer them to a separate bowl, leaving behind as much oil as possible. Let the mixture cool slightly before adding it to the beaten eggs. Mix the potatoes and onions with the eggs, making sure they are well coated.

  6. In the same frying pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Pour the egg, potato, and onion mixture into the pan, spreading it out evenly.

  7. Cook the tortilla over low to medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until the edges start to set. Using a spatula, gently lift the edges of the tortilla to allow any uncooked egg mixture to flow to the bottom of the pan.

  8. Once the edges are set, carefully flip the tortilla using a large plate or a lid. Slide the tortilla back into the pan, uncooked side down.

  9. Continue cooking the tortilla for another 4-5 minutes, or until it is cooked through and slightly golden on both sides.

  10. Once the tortilla is cooked, remove it from the pan and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing it into wedges.

Serving Suggestions

The Tortilla Española can be enjoyed hot or cold, making it a versatile dish for any occasion. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Serve the Tortilla Española as a tapas dish, alongside other Spanish favorites like patatas bravas and gambas al ajillo.
  • Enjoy a slice of Tortilla Española on a fresh baguette, drizzled with a bit of olive oil and topped with a sprinkle of sea salt.
  • Pack a slice of Tortilla Española in your lunchbox for a delicious and satisfying meal on the go.
  • Serve the Tortilla Española as part of a brunch spread, alongside crispy bacon, fresh fruit, and a glass of chilled Sangria.

Conclusion

The Tortilla Española truly is the quintessential Spanish omelette. With its humble yet flavorful ingredients, it captures the essence of Spanish cuisine in every bite. Whether you’re serving it as a tapas dish, enjoying it on a baguette, or savoring it as part of a brunch spread, the Tortilla Española is sure to delight your taste buds and transport you to the sunny streets of Spain. So why not give this classic recipe a try and experience the flavors of Spain in your own kitchen?

Note: This recipe serves 4-6 people.
