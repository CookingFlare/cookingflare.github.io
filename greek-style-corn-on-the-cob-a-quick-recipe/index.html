Greek Style Corn on the Cob: A Quick Recipe


Corn on the cob is a timeless classic. Whether it’s grilled, boiled, or roasted, it’s a versatile side dish that pairs well with nearly any meal. But if you want to elevate your corn game, why not try something a little different? This Greek-style corn on the cob recipe brings the vibrant and fresh flavors of the Mediterranean to this simple dish. Packed with aromatic herbs, tangy feta, and a drizzle of olive oil, this recipe is quick to prepare and will make your taste buds sing.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to prepare Greek-style corn on the cob. From the preparation and cooking process to the finishing touches that bring this dish to life, we’ll make sure you can easily recreate this flavorful and satisfying dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make Greek-style corn on the cob, you won’t need many ingredients. In fact, the simplicity of the dish is part of what makes it so delicious. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn on the cob (husked)
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano (Greek oregano is preferred, but any variety will do)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (or use fresh minced garlic for an extra punch)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (for depth and flavor)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Optional: Crushed red pepper flakes (for a touch of heat)

These simple yet aromatic ingredients will transform your corn on the cob into something out of the ordinary. Greek cuisine is known for its bold and fresh flavors, and this recipe encapsulates that perfectly.

How to Prepare Greek Style Corn on the Cob

Step 1: Prepare the Corn

Before we get into the seasoning and cooking, let’s start by prepping the corn. Husk the ears of corn, removing all the leaves and silks. If you like, you can break the corn into halves or thirds to make them easier to handle, especially if you’re working with smaller grill spaces or cooking in smaller batches.

Rinse the corn under cold water to make sure all the silk has been removed. Pat them dry with a towel and set them aside for the next steps.

Step 2: Make the Greek Style Dressing

While the corn is being prepared, let’s focus on the delicious Greek dressing that will bring everything together. In a small bowl, combine the extra virgin olive oil, freshly squeezed lemon juice, dried oregano, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper.

Whisk the mixture thoroughly to combine all the ingredients. This dressing will be the foundation of the Greek flavors on your corn. The olive oil will provide richness, while the lemon juice adds brightness, and the oregano infuses the dish with that quintessential Mediterranean flavor. Smoked paprika gives the corn a subtle smoky depth, and the garlic powder rounds out the savory profile.

Step 3: Cook the Corn

You have a few options when it comes to cooking corn on the cob, depending on your equipment and preferences. Below, we’ll discuss three common methods: grilling, boiling, and roasting.

Grilling Corn on the Cob

Grilling corn brings out its natural sweetness and imparts a smoky char that’s perfect for this recipe.

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the prepared corn directly on the grill. Grill the corn for about 10-15 minutes, rotating it every few minutes until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.
  3. Once done, remove the corn from the grill and let it cool for a minute or two before handling.

Boiling Corn on the Cob

Boiling is an excellent option if you want to quickly cook the corn without fuss.

  1. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil over high heat. Add a pinch of salt to the water to help season the corn as it cooks.
  2. Once the water is boiling, add the corn and let it cook for about 7-10 minutes, depending on the size of the ears.
  3. When the corn is tender, remove it from the pot using tongs, and let it drain for a few moments.

Roasting Corn in the Oven

Roasting corn is another great way to cook corn on the cob, especially if you’re cooking it in large batches.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the husked corn directly on a baking sheet and roast for about 25-30 minutes, turning the corn halfway through the cooking time for even roasting.
  3. When the corn is tender and lightly browned, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool slightly.

Step 4: Dress the Corn

Once your corn is cooked, it’s time to add the Greek-style dressing.

While the corn is still warm, drizzle the olive oil and lemon dressing generously over each ear. Use a brush or spoon to ensure the entire surface of the corn is coated with the flavorful mixture.

Next, sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese evenly over the top of each ear of corn. The salty, creamy feta adds a burst of Mediterranean flavor that pairs beautifully with the charred or boiled corn.

For a finishing touch, sprinkle a little extra dried oregano on top, along with a pinch of smoked paprika for added color and flavor. If you like a bit of heat, a dash of crushed red pepper flakes is a great addition.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Finally, garnish the Greek-style corn with freshly chopped parsley. The fresh, bright green parsley adds a nice contrast to the corn’s golden hue, as well as a burst of fresh flavor.

Serve the corn immediately while it’s still warm. It pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, Mediterranean-style salads, or even as a standalone dish for a light meal.

Tips for Perfect Greek-Style Corn on the Cob

  • Freshness Matters: For the best flavor, use the freshest corn you can find. Corn is a summer vegetable, so during peak season, you’ll enjoy the sweetest and most tender kernels.

  • Adjust to Taste: While feta is an essential ingredient in this recipe, feel free to adjust the amount depending on your preference for saltiness and creaminess. You can also substitute with crumbled goat cheese or a dairy-free alternative if necessary.

  • Customize Your Spices: The beauty of this recipe is its flexibility. If you love garlic, add a little more. If you’re into a more intense lemon flavor, feel free to increase the lemon juice. You can even add fresh herbs like dill or mint for extra freshness.

  • Charred Flavor: If you prefer a more charred flavor, leave the corn on the grill a little longer to achieve that deep smoky finish. Just make sure to check that the corn doesn’t burn.

  • Serving Suggestions: This Greek-style corn on the cob works wonderfully as a side dish for a summer BBQ, a Mediterranean-inspired dinner, or even a casual weeknight meal. Pair it with grilled chicken, lamb, or a light fish like grilled salmon for a perfect feast.

Why This Recipe Works

What makes this Greek-style corn on the cob so special is the way it blends the sweet, tender flavor of corn with the bold, zesty elements of Greek cuisine. The olive oil and lemon juice create a light and refreshing dressing, while the feta cheese adds creaminess and saltiness. The dried oregano and smoked paprika provide a depth of flavor that makes each bite more exciting than the last.

By using simple ingredients that are common in Greek cooking, like olive oil, feta, and oregano, this dish brings the authentic flavors of the Mediterranean into your kitchen without requiring any complicated techniques.

It’s a quick and easy way to elevate a simple vegetable into something extraordinary. Whether you’re grilling for a crowd or making a quick weeknight meal, Greek-style corn on the cob is a winner every time.

Final Thoughts

If you’re looking for a fresh, flavorful way to enjoy corn on the cob, this Greek-style recipe is sure to satisfy. It’s simple, vibrant, and full of the kinds of Mediterranean flavors that make Greek cuisine so beloved. Plus, it’s quick to prepare and easily adaptable to suit your taste preferences.

So, next time you find yourself with a few ears of corn, skip the butter and go for something a little more exciting. The combination of olive oil, lemon, feta, and herbs will transform your corn into a dish that everyone will rave about. Whether served as a side or as a star of your meal, Greek-style corn on the cob is bound to become a new favorite in your cooking repertoire.

Give it a try, and let your taste buds experience the magic of the Mediterranean with each bite!
