BBQ Chicken Quesadillas: A Cheesy Fourth of July Appetizer


As the Fourth of July approaches, it’s time to start planning the perfect menu for your celebration. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or attending a potluck, having a crowd-pleasing appetizer on hand is essential. That’s where these BBQ chicken quesadillas come in. They’re the perfect combination of smoky, savory, and cheesy, making them an ideal addition to any Independence Day gathering.

Ingredients

For the BBQ Chicken:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Quesadillas:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Cooking oil or butter for grilling

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the BBQ Chicken

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the seasoned chicken on the grill and cook for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  4. During the last few minutes of grilling, baste the chicken with BBQ sauce, allowing it to caramelize and create a flavorful glaze.
  5. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

Step 2: Assemble the Quesadillas

  1. Lay out two tortillas and divide the shredded cheddar cheese evenly between them.
  2. Arrange the sliced BBQ chicken on top of the cheese, followed by the diced red onion and chopped cilantro.
  3. Place the remaining tortillas on top to create two quesadilla “sandwiches.”

Step 3: Grill the Quesadillas

  1. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of cooking oil or butter.
  2. Carefully transfer the assembled quesadillas to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the tortillas are golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  3. Once cooked, remove the quesadillas from the skillet and let them cool for a minute before slicing them into wedges.

Serving Suggestions

These BBQ chicken quesadillas are delicious on their own, but you can take them to the next level by serving them with a side of sour cream, guacamole, or your favorite salsa. They also pair wonderfully with a crisp, refreshing salad or a cold beer. Whether you’re enjoying a casual gathering with friends or a festive family get-together, these quesadillas are sure to be a hit.

Conclusion

When it comes to Fourth of July appetizers, these BBQ chicken quesadillas are a winning choice. They’re easy to make, packed with flavor, and perfect for sharing. Plus, their cheesy, smoky goodness makes them an irresistible addition to any summer celebration. So, fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to impress your guests with this mouthwatering appetizer. Happy Independence Day!

I hope you enjoy making these BBQ chicken quesadillas as much as you’ll enjoy eating them. They’re the perfect way to kick off your Fourth of July festivities and are sure to become a new favorite in your recipe collection. If you have any variations or additional serving suggestions for this recipe, feel free to share them in the comments below. Happy cooking and happy Fourth of July!
