How to Make a Classic Coq au Vin


Coq au Vin, a traditional French dish, is a culinary masterpiece that has stood the test of time. This rustic dish, which translates to “rooster in wine,” is a celebration of rich flavors and hearty ingredients. While the original recipe calls for rooster, modern adaptations often use chicken, making it accessible for home cooks everywhere. In this post, we will guide you through the steps to create a classic Coq au Vin that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To prepare a delicious Coq au Vin, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 1 bottle of full-bodied red wine (Burgundy or Pinot Noir works well)
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces (or 4 chicken thighs and 4 drumsticks)

For the Coq au Vin:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 ounces of bacon or pancetta, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of pearl onions, peeled
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, quartered
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Equipment Needed

Before you start cooking, gather the following equipment:

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot
  • Wooden spoon
  • Slotted spoon
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Knife and cutting board

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a large mixing bowl, combine the red wine, chicken stock, quartered onion, sliced carrots, smashed garlic, bay leaves, thyme, and black peppercorns. Stir well to combine.

  2. Add the Chicken: Place the chicken pieces into the marinade, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This step is crucial as it infuses the chicken with deep flavors.

Step 2: Sear the Chicken

  1. Remove the Chicken: After marinating, remove the chicken from the marinade and pat it dry with paper towels. Reserve the marinade for later use.

  2. Heat the Oil: In a Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat.

  3. Cook the Bacon: Add the diced bacon or pancetta to the pot and cook until crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

  4. Sear the Chicken: In the same pot, add the chicken pieces skin-side down. Sear them for about 5-7 minutes until golden brown. Flip the pieces and sear the other side for an additional 5 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

Step 3: Build the Flavor Base

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and diced carrots. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the onions are translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Add Tomato Paste: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, allowing it to caramelize slightly.

  3. Thicken the Mixture: Sprinkle the flour over the vegetable mixture and stir well to combine. Cook for another 2 minutes to eliminate the raw flour taste.

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

  1. Return the Chicken: Place the seared chicken back into the pot, along with the reserved marinade. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  2. Add Pearl Onions and Mushrooms: Add the pearl onions and quartered mushrooms to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Simmer: Cover the pot and let it simmer on low heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours. The chicken should be tender and the flavors well-developed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

Step 5: Final Touches

  1. Adjust Seasoning: After simmering, taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.

  2. Serve: Once the chicken is tender, remove the pot from heat. Serve the Coq au Vin hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley.

Serving Suggestions

Coq au Vin is best served with crusty French bread or over a bed of creamy mashed potatoes. The rich sauce is perfect for soaking up with bread, making every bite a delight.
