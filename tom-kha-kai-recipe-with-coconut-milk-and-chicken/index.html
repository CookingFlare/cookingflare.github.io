Tom Kha Kai Recipe with Coconut Milk and Chicken


Tom Kha Kai, also known as Thai coconut chicken soup, is a delightful and aromatic dish that beautifully combines the flavors of coconut milk, chicken, and fragrant Thai herbs. This classic Thai soup is a perfect balance of creamy, spicy, and tangy flavors that will transport you to the bustling streets of Thailand with every spoonful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 1 lb of chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, bruised and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4-5 slices of galangal
  • 6-8 kaffir lime leaves
  • 8-10 fresh Thai bird’s eye chilies, lightly crushed
  • 1 cup of straw mushrooms, halved
  • 1 medium-sized tomato, cut into wedges
  • 3-4 shallots, finely sliced
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2-3 tablespoons of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Herbs and Aromatics:

    • Bruise the lemongrass by pressing down on it with the back of a knife and cut it into 2-inch pieces.
    • Slice the galangal into thin slices.
    • Lightly crush the Thai bird’s eye chilies.

  2. Simmer the Aromatics:

    • In a large pot, combine the coconut milk, chicken stock, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and Thai bird’s eye chilies.
    • Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat for 10-15 minutes to infuse the flavors of the aromatics into the soup base.

  3. Add the Chicken and Mushrooms:

    • Once the soup base is infused with the aromatics, add the sliced chicken breast and straw mushrooms to the pot.
    • Allow the chicken to cook through and the mushrooms to soften, which should take approximately 8-10 minutes.

  4. Season the Soup:

    • Stir in the sliced shallots, minced garlic, fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar.
    • Let the soup simmer for an additional 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Garnish and Serve:

    • Remove the pot from the heat and discard the lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and Thai bird’s eye chilies.
    • Add the tomato wedges to the soup and stir gently.
    • Ladle the fragrant Tom Kha Kai into serving bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For a vegetarian version, you can substitute the chicken with tofu and the chicken stock with vegetable stock.
  • Adjust the amount of Thai bird’s eye chilies based on your spice preference. Be cautious, as they can be extremely spicy!
  • If you can’t find galangal, you can use ginger as a substitute, but the flavor will be slightly different.
  • Serve Tom Kha Kai with steamed jasmine rice for a complete and satisfying meal.

Final Thoughts:

Tom Kha Kai is a versatile and comforting soup that is suitable for any season. Its rich and creamy texture, combined with the subtle heat from the chilies and the citrusy notes from the lime leaves, makes it a truly unforgettable dish. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or just looking to expand your culinary horizons, this recipe is a must-try. So, gather the ingredients and embark on a culinary journey to Thailand from the comfort of your own kitchen!
