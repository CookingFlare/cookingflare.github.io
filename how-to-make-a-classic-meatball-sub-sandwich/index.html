How to Make a Classic Meatball Sub Sandwich


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the classic meatball sub sandwich. This hearty meal combines tender, flavorful meatballs with rich marinara sauce, all nestled in a soft, crusty sub roll and topped with gooey melted cheese. Whether you’re preparing a family dinner, hosting a game day gathering, or simply craving a satisfying sandwich, mastering the art of the meatball sub is a culinary skill worth acquiring. In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of the process, from making the meatballs to assembling the perfect sub.

Ingredients

For the Meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for added flavor)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably Italian-style)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembly:

  • 4 sub rolls (preferably hoagie or Italian)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish, optional)

Step 1: Prepare the Meatballs

Mixing the Ingredients

  1. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, egg, minced garlic, onion powder, oregano, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Use your hands to mix everything together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough meatballs.

  2. Shape the Meatballs: Once the mixture is well combined, use your hands to form meatballs about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You should end up with approximately 12-16 meatballs, depending on the size you choose.

Cooking the Meatballs

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). This high temperature will help the meatballs develop a nice crust while keeping them juicy inside.

  2. Bake the Meatballs: Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or lightly greased. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are cooked through and have a nice golden-brown color. You can check for doneness by cutting one in half; it should be no longer pink in the center.

Step 2: Make the Marinara Sauce

Sautéing the Aromatics

  1. Heat the Olive Oil: In a large saucepan over medium heat, add the olive oil. Once hot, add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, or until the onion becomes translucent.

  2. Add Garlic: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, being careful not to let it burn.

Simmering the Sauce

  1. Add Tomatoes and Seasonings: Pour in the crushed tomatoes, sugar, dried basil, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir everything together and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer.

  2. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully.

Step 3: Combine Meatballs and Sauce

  1. Add Meatballs to Sauce: Once the meatballs are cooked, carefully add them to the marinara sauce. Gently stir to coat the meatballs in the sauce. Let them simmer together for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the meatballs to absorb some of the sauce’s flavor.

Step 4: Assemble the Meatball Sub Sandwiches

  1. Prepare the Sub Rolls: While the meatballs are simmering, slice the sub rolls in half lengthwise. If you prefer a toasted sub, you can lightly toast the rolls in the oven for a few minutes until they are golden and crispy.

  2. Add Meatballs and Sauce: Using tongs or a slotted spoon, place 3-4 meatballs on the bottom half of each sub roll. Spoon additional marinara sauce over the meatballs to ensure they are generously covered.

  3. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese over the meatballs and sauce.
