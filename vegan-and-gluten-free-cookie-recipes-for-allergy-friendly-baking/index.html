Vegan and Gluten-Free Cookie Recipes for Allergy-Friendly Baking


Baking cookies that cater to dietary needs can feel challenging, especially when trying to meet multiple restrictions such as vegan and gluten-free requirements. However, it’s easier than you might think to create mouthwatering, allergy-friendly treats. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of vegan and gluten-free cookie recipes that will not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also ensure that everyone, regardless of food allergies or sensitivities, can enjoy them.

Whether you’re making cookies for a special occasion, a family treat, or just for yourself, these recipes are sure to hit the spot without compromising on taste or texture. Let’s dive into some fantastic options!

1. Classic Vegan and Gluten-Free Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are a timeless favorite, and it’s possible to make them without any dairy or gluten. These cookies are soft, chewy, and packed with rich chocolate flavor, all while being free from animal products and gluten.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup coconut oil (melted)
  • ½ cup coconut sugar (or brown sugar)
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseeds mixed with 3 tbsp water (flax egg)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the ground flaxseeds with water and set aside for about 5 minutes to thicken.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. In another bowl, combine the melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Add the flax egg and mix well.
  5. Slowly combine the wet ingredients with the dry ingredients. Stir until a dough forms.
  6. Fold in the dairy-free chocolate chips.
  7. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them a couple of inches apart.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These cookies offer that perfect balance of crunch around the edges and chewy goodness in the center. The coconut oil and maple syrup create a depth of flavor that complements the chocolate chips beautifully.

2. Vegan and Gluten-Free Peanut Butter Cookies

Peanut butter lovers will rejoice at this simple, yet delicious, recipe for vegan and gluten-free peanut butter cookies. The combination of nutty peanut butter and just a few other ingredients makes for a treat that’s easy to prepare and satisfyingly rich.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup natural peanut butter (no added sugar or oils)
  • 1 cup coconut sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseeds (optional for added binding)
  • 1 cup gluten-free rolled oats
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the peanut butter, coconut sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  3. Add in the oats, baking powder, salt, and ground flaxseeds (if using). Stir until all the ingredients are well incorporated.
  4. Use a tablespoon or cookie scoop to form dough balls and place them on the baking sheet. Flatten each ball slightly with a fork to create a crisscross pattern on top.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges start to turn golden brown.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool on the sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

These cookies are rich and buttery, with the perfect amount of sweetness. They’re excellent for those who love the classic combination of peanut butter and cookies, and they’re a great source of protein and fiber as well.

3. Vegan and Gluten-Free Snickerdoodle Cookies

Snickerdoodles are a cinnamon-sugar favorite that brings a warm, comforting flavor to your cookie jar. The sweet and spicy aroma of cinnamon paired with the soft texture of these cookies makes them irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ cup coconut oil (softened)
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar (or brown sugar)
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water (flax egg)
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp coconut sugar (for rolling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, prepare the flax egg by mixing ground flaxseeds and water. Set aside for a few minutes to thicken.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking soda, cream of tartar, and salt.
  4. In another bowl, cream together the coconut oil and coconut sugar. Add the maple syrup and vanilla extract and mix until smooth. Stir in the flax egg.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. In a small bowl, mix the cinnamon and coconut sugar for rolling.
  7. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough, roll them into balls, and then roll each ball in the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  8. Place the dough balls on the baking sheet and slightly flatten them with the back of a spoon.
  9. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are golden and the centers are set.
  10. Let the cookies cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

These snickerdoodles are soft, chewy, and full of the warm, comforting flavors of cinnamon and sugar. The cream of tartar gives them that signature tangy taste, making them an instant hit for anyone craving this classic treat.

4. Vegan and Gluten-Free Almond Joy Cookies

For those who love the combination of chocolate, coconut, and almonds, these cookies are a dream come true. Inspired by the famous Almond Joy candy bar, these cookies combine all those beloved flavors in a gluten-free and vegan-friendly form.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond meal or almond flour
  • 1 ½ cups gluten-free rolled oats
  • ½ cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • ½ cup dairy-free chocolate chips
  • ½ cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup almond butter (smooth)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • A pinch of salt
  • Whole almonds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond meal, rolled oats, shredded coconut, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, almond butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until well combined.
  5. Fold in the dairy-free chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough and roll them into balls. Place them on the baking sheet, pressing down slightly to flatten each one.
  7. If desired, place an almond in the center of each cookie for an extra touch of flavor.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the cookies are firm to the touch.
  9. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These Almond Joy cookies are a delightful combination of chewy oats, crunchy almonds, and rich chocolate, all balanced with the sweetness of maple syrup and the richness of almond butter.

5. Vegan and Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Oatmeal raisin cookies are another classic that many people enjoy. These cookies are hearty and satisfying, with chewy oats and sweet raisins combining for the perfect bite. And the best part? They’re completely vegan and gluten-free!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups gluten-free rolled oats
  • ¾ cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ½ cup coconut oil (melted)
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup almond milk (or other plant-based milk)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseeds mixed with 3 tbsp water (flax egg)
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, prepare the flax egg by mixing ground flaxseeds and water. Set aside for about 5 minutes.
  3. In a medium bowl, combine the oats, gluten-free flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and flax egg.
  5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, mixing until well combined.
  6. Stir in the raisins.
  7. Drop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the baking sheet, spacing them a couple of inches apart.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are golden and the cookies are firm.
  9. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These oatmeal raisin cookies are soft, chewy, and packed with natural sweetness from the raisins. The cinnamon adds a lovely warmth, making them perfect for any time of year.

Conclusion

Creating delicious vegan and gluten-free cookies doesn’t have to be a complicated task. With the right ingredients and a little creativity, you can make allergy-friendly treats that everyone can enjoy. Whether you’re in the mood for classic chocolate chip, comforting snickerdoodles, or something more exotic like almond joy-inspired cookies, these recipes are sure to delight.

As you explore the world of allergy-friendly baking, feel free to experiment with different ingredients to suit your preferences. From using different nut butters to incorporating various spices, there’s always room to make these cookies your own.

Happy baking, and enjoy your allergy-friendly treats!
