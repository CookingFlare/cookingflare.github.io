Homemade Ravioli: Fillings, Sauces, and Tips


Ravioli is a beloved classic of Italian cuisine that has captured hearts and palates worldwide. These delicate pasta pockets, typically filled with a combination of savory ingredients, offer endless opportunities for customization. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned home cook, making homemade ravioli is a rewarding and creative experience that can elevate any meal.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the entire process of making ravioli from scratch, from crafting the perfect dough to preparing mouthwatering fillings and sauces. We’ll also share expert tips to ensure your ravioli turns out perfectly every time. Let’s dive in!

Making the Perfect Ravioli Dough

The foundation of any great ravioli begins with the dough. Homemade pasta dough is surprisingly simple to make, with just a few basic ingredients. When making ravioli dough, the goal is to create a smooth, elastic texture that can be rolled thin enough to encase your filling without falling apart.

Ingredients for the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water (as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Flour: On a clean surface, mound the flour and create a well in the center. This well will be where you mix the eggs and other ingredients.

  2. Add the Eggs: Crack the eggs into the well of flour and add a pinch of salt. Use a fork to gently whisk the eggs, slowly incorporating flour from the edges of the well.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Once the eggs are well mixed with the flour, drizzle in the olive oil. Continue mixing with your fork until a dough begins to form.

  4. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a clean, floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour. If it’s too dry, sprinkle a small amount of water.

  5. Rest the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This step allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll the dough thin.

  6. Roll the Dough: After resting, cut the dough into smaller portions. Use a rolling pin or pasta machine to roll each portion into a thin sheet, about 1/16-inch thick. Dust the dough lightly with flour to prevent sticking.

Pro Tip:

If you’re using a pasta machine, start on the thickest setting and gradually work your way down to the thinnest. This ensures an even, delicate dough that holds up well when filled.

Delicious Ravioli Fillings

The filling is the heart of any ravioli. From traditional ricotta and spinach to more adventurous combinations, the possibilities are virtually endless. Below are a few classic and creative fillings to inspire you.

Classic Ricotta and Spinach Filling

This is perhaps the most iconic ravioli filling. The creaminess of ricotta combined with the earthy flavors of spinach creates a balanced and comforting dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ricotta cheese (drained)
  • 1 cup cooked spinach, squeezed dry and chopped finely
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the ricotta, spinach, Parmesan, and egg. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  2. Stir until the mixture is smooth and well combined. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary.

Mushroom and Truffle Filling

For a more sophisticated option, consider a mushroom and truffle filling. The earthy depth of mushrooms paired with the luxurious flavor of truffle oil or truffle salt makes this filling a true indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, or portobello), finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon truffle oil or ½ teaspoon truffle salt

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook until the liquid evaporates and the mushrooms are browned, about 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the cream and cook for an additional 2 minutes. Remove from heat and season with salt, pepper, and truffle oil (or truffle salt).
  4. Allow the mixture to cool slightly before using as a filling.

Butternut Squash and Sage Filling

For a sweet and savory combination, try a roasted butternut squash filling, complemented by the aromatic flavor of fresh sage.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups roasted butternut squash, mashed
  • ½ cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mashed butternut squash with ricotta, Parmesan, and sage.
  2. Season with salt and pepper, and mix until the filling is smooth and well combined.

Pro Tip:

When making ravioli, always ensure the filling is not too runny. If it is, the filling may leak out during cooking. For cream-based fillings, you can mix in breadcrumbs or a small amount of Parmesan cheese to help firm it up.

Assembling the Ravioli

Now that your dough and filling are ready, it’s time to assemble the ravioli. This process involves cutting the dough, adding the filling, and sealing the edges to create individual pasta pockets.

Instructions for Assembly:

  1. Prepare the Work Surface: Lay out a clean, floured surface where you can work with the dough. Ensure your rolling pin or pasta machine is nearby.

  2. Cut the Dough Sheets: Roll out the dough into long, thin sheets. Using a knife or ravioli cutter, cut the dough into even squares or rectangles, depending on the desired shape of your ravioli.

  3. Add the Filling: Place a small spoonful of filling (about 1 to 2 teaspoons) in the center of each square. Be careful not to overfill the ravioli, as this can cause them to burst during cooking.

  4. Seal the Ravioli: Lightly dampen the edges of the dough with water and fold the dough over the filling to create a pocket. Press down the edges to seal, ensuring there are no air bubbles trapped inside. Use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch and extra seal.

  5. Rest the Ravioli: Once assembled, let the ravioli rest on a floured surface for about 15 minutes. This helps the dough firm up slightly and prevents the ravioli from falling apart during cooking.

Pro Tip:

To prevent your ravioli from sticking to each other during resting, dust them lightly with flour or semolina. You can also place them on parchment paper if you’re making a large batch.

Cooking Homemade Ravioli

Cooking ravioli is quick and easy. Once your ravioli are assembled, all they need is a brief dip in boiling salted water.

Instructions:

  1. Boil Water: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Make sure there’s enough water for the ravioli to move freely as they cook.

  2. Cook the Ravioli: Gently drop the ravioli into the boiling water, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Cook for 3-5 minutes or until they float to the surface. Fresh ravioli cooks quickly, so keep an eye on them.

  3. Drain and Serve: Use a slotted spoon to remove the ravioli from the water and place them on a plate. You can drizzle them with olive oil or serve them with your favorite sauce.

Pro Tip:

If you’re cooking a large batch of ravioli, you can freeze some for later use. Lay the assembled ravioli on a baking sheet and freeze them until solid. Then, transfer them to a freezer bag for storage. To cook frozen ravioli, simply add them directly to boiling water without thawing.

Delicious Sauces for Ravioli

While ravioli is wonderful on its own, a rich, flavorful sauce can take it to the next level. Below are some classic and creative sauce options to pair with your homemade ravioli.

Classic Tomato Sauce

A fresh, vibrant tomato sauce is a timeless choice. This simple, yet flavorful sauce is perfect for complementing the delicate texture of ravioli.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and stir in fresh basil just before serving.

Brown Butter and Sage Sauce

For a more indulgent sauce, consider brown butter and sage. The nutty flavor of browned butter pairs beautifully with the earthy sage and adds richness to the ravioli.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 8 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, continue cooking until the butter begins to brown and emit a nutty aroma, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the sage leaves and cook for an additional 2 minutes until crisp.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then pour over the cooked ravioli.

Creamy Alfredo Sauce

For those who love a rich and creamy sauce, a homemade Alfredo sauce is a perfect match for ravioli, especially when paired with fillings like spinach, mushrooms, or cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  2. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Cook for 5 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  3. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper, and cook for an additional 2 minutes until the sauce is smooth.

Final Thoughts: Tips for Perfect Ravioli

Making homemade ravioli is a labor of love, but the results are truly worth it. Here are a few tips to ensure your ravioli turns out perfectly every time:

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh herbs, quality cheese, and the best available vegetables will make a noticeable difference in the flavor of your ravioli.
  • Avoid Overfilling: A little filling goes a long way. Too much filling can cause the ravioli to burst during cooking.
  • Seal Well: Ensure the edges of the ravioli are properly sealed to avoid any leaks during cooking. A fork can help crimp the edges securely.
  • Practice Patience: Don’t rush the dough-making and resting process. Let the dough relax before rolling, and allow the ravioli to rest after assembling.

Whether you’re preparing a traditional dish or experimenting with creative fillings and sauces, homemade ravioli brings a personal touch to any meal. With these tips and recipes, you’ll be well on your way to mastering this delightful dish!
