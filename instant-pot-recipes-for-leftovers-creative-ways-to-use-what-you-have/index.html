Instant Pot Recipes for Leftovers: Creative Ways to Use What You Have


The beauty of cooking lies in its creativity, and there’s no greater canvas for culinary innovation than leftovers. Instead of letting yesterday’s dishes linger in your fridge, turning them into something delicious and fresh can be surprisingly easy—especially when you have an Instant Pot. This versatile kitchen gadget isn’t just for quick weeknight meals; it’s also your ultimate ally in transforming leftover ingredients into exciting new dishes. Let’s explore some simple and resourceful Instant Pot recipes to help reduce waste and breathe new life into your meals.

Why Leftovers Deserve a Second Chance

Before we jump into the recipes, it’s worth taking a moment to rethink how we view leftovers. Often brushed aside, leftovers are actually filled with potential. They save time, help reduce food waste, and make it easier to whip up flavorful dishes in less time. Whether it’s reimagining roast chicken into a hearty soup or turning last night’s baked potatoes into creamy mash, leftovers create a foundation for endless improvisation. With an Instant Pot, you can quickly transform these refrigerated treasures into comforting meals bursting with flavor.

Recipe 1: Hearty Leftover Meat and Vegetable Stew

If you have roasted chicken, beef, or even turkey lounging in your fridge, this recipe is a lifesaver. Combined with leftover roasted vegetables or even raw odds and ends like celery or carrots, you’ll have a stew that tastes like it cooked for hours—even though it takes less than 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked meat (shredded chicken, beef, or turkey)
  • 1 cup of leftover cooked vegetables (e.g., carrots, celery, potatoes, or green beans)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups chicken, beef, or vegetable broth (depending on the type of meat)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes (optional for a richer flavor)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano or thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté and build your base: Using the Sauté function on the Instant Pot, add a tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the diced onion until translucent. Stir in garlic and oregano for about one minute until fragrant.
  2. Combine everything: Add your leftover meat, cooked vegetables, broth, and canned tomatoes (if using). Stir to combine. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  3. Set and cook: Close the Instant Pot lid, seal the vent, and cook on High Pressure for 10 minutes. Allow the pressure to release naturally for about 5–10 minutes before carefully opening the lid.
  4. Serve and enjoy: Pour the stew into bowls, and serve it piping hot with a slice of crusty bread or over rice.

Recipe 2: Creamy Instant Pot Risotto with Leftover Vegetables

If you have leftover broccoli, mushrooms, spinach, or even mixed stir-fried vegetables, this creamy risotto will be your new go-to. Traditional risotto can be time-consuming, but the Instant Pot churns out perfectly creamy results without constant stirring.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 ½ cups leftover cooked vegetables, chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese (or any cheese of your choice)
  • ¼ cup white wine (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the base: Using the Sauté function, heat the olive oil or butter in the Instant Pot. Add the diced onion and cook until softened.
  2. Toast the rice: Add the Arborio rice to the pot, stirring to coat it in the oil. Toast for 1–2 minutes until it begins to smell slightly nutty.
  3. Deglaze with wine (optional): Pour the white wine into the pot, scraping up any bits stuck to the bottom. Let it simmer for a minute until it starts to reduce.
  4. Pressure cook: Add the chopped vegetables and broth to the pot. Stir to combine, then seal the lid and cook on High Pressure for 6 minutes.
  5. Finish the dish: Quick-release the pressure, then stir in the grated Parmesan cheese. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve immediately: Plate up and enjoy your creamy risotto, perfect as a main or side dish.

Recipe 3: Leftover Roast-to-Pulled Meat Tacos

Ever wondered what to do with leftover roast beef, pork, or chicken? In just a few minutes, your Instant Pot can turn them into savory pulled meat perfect for tacos, burritos, or even quesadillas.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover roast beef, chicken, or pork (shredded or cut into small chunks)
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 cup chicken, beef, or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp taco seasoning or a blend of cumin, paprika, and chili powder
  • Tortillas and toppings (shredded lettuce, salsa, cheese, avocado)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the meat: Place the shredded leftover meat into the Instant Pot, along with the sliced onion and taco seasoning.
  2. Add liquid: Pour the broth over the meat mixture to keep it moist during cooking.
  3. Cook quickly: Seal the Instant Pot lid, cook on High Pressure for 5 minutes, and perform a quick release when it’s done.
  4. Assemble the tacos: Remove the meat from the pot using a slotted spoon, then pile it onto warm tortillas with your favorite toppings.

Recipe 4: Instant Pot Fried Rice with Leftover Rice

We’ve all been there—ordering too much takeout rice or boiling an extra pot by accident. Instead of letting it dry out in your fridge, this fried rice recipe brings it back to life. The Instant Pot steams the rice and blends it with flavorful seasonings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover white or brown rice
  • 1 cup diced vegetables (leftover peas, carrots, or any stir-fry leftovers work well)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ginger and garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the aromatics: On the Instant Pot’s Sauté setting, heat the sesame oil and add garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Cook the egg: Push the aromatics to the side, pour in the beaten eggs, and scramble until mostly set.
  3. Stir-fry in the rice: Add the rice and vegetables, breaking up any clumps as you go. Pour in the soy sauce and stir well to distribute.
  4. Heat everything through: Cook for 2–3 minutes, tossing consistently until heated through. Serve hot as a side or standalone dish.

Recipe 5: Savory Bread Pudding with Leftover Bread

Leftover bread often becomes overlooked and unused before it goes stale. However, you can turn it into a hearty, savory breakfast, brunch, or even dinner dish with this Instant Pot recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 5–6 slices of stale bread, cubed
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 ½ cups milk or cream
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix)
  • 1 cup leftover cooked vegetables (spinach, broccoli, bell peppers, etc.)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dish: Grease an Instant Pot-safe baking dish or casserole. Place the bread cubes in the dish and scatter the leftover vegetables and cheese on top.
  2. Whisk the batter: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture over the bread cubes, pressing slightly so the bread absorbs the liquid.
  3. Steam the pudding: Add one cup of water to the Instant Pot. Place the baking dish on a trivet and lower it into the pot. Seal the lid and steam on High Pressure for 30 minutes, followed by a quick release.
  4. Serve warm: Remove carefully and let cool for a minute or two before serving.

Final Tips for Reimagining Leftovers with Your Instant Pot

  • Avoid mixing strong flavors improportionately: Be mindful when combining leftovers; bold flavors can overwhelm mild ones.
  • Adjust seasoning: Leftovers often need another dash of seasoning, as their original flavors may dull during storage.
  • Label and date your leftovers: Always keep track of what’s in your fridge to avoid food spoilage or waste.

Reusing leftovers doesn’t have to result in tired repeats of the same dishes. With your Instant Pot, you can quickly and easily transform yesterday’s meals into fresh, flavorful creations that your entire family will enjoy. Get creative, embrace your leftovers, and start experimenting in the kitchen today!
