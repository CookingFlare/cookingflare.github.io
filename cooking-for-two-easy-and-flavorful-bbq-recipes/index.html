Cooking for Two: Easy and Flavorful BBQ Recipes


When it’s just the two of you, mealtime can feel special, intimate, and downright rewarding. Whether it’s a cozy weeknight dinner or a weekend grilling session under the open sky, BBQ recipes are perfect for creating memorable moments. Cooking for two lends a unique opportunity to design dishes packed with flavor while keeping the preparation efficient and enjoyable. In this post, we’re diving into a lineup of easy, flavorful BBQ recipes that are tailor-made for two people to enjoy. From smoky entrees to perfectly charred vegetables and delicious sides, there’s something here for every palate.

Reasons to Love BBQ Cooking for Two

Cooking for a pair lets you embrace culinary creativity without the burden of large portions or leftovers. You can focus on maximizing flavors, accommodating personal preferences, and experimenting with different textures and marinades.

  • Quick preparation: Most BBQ recipes for two are speedy in both preparation and cooking time, making them ideal for an efficient yet satisfying meal.
  • Minimal waste: With just two servings, you can shop and cook with precision, cutting down on leftovers and food waste.
  • Room for indulgence: Whether it’s a premium cut of meat or experimenting with gourmet ingredients, cooking for two allows you to try something special without breaking the bank.
  • Perfect for bonding: BBQ cooking is inherently interactive. From marinating to grilling, it’s a culinary dance that you and your companion can enjoy together.

Now let’s fire up the grill — it’s time to dive into some scrumptious recipes perfect for two.

1. Grilled BBQ Bourbon-Glazed Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are juicy, flavorful, and cook beautifully on the grill. This bourbon glaze adds a smoky sweetness with just a hint of spice.

Ingredients:

  • 4 skin-on, bone-in chicken thighs
  • 1/4 cup bourbon
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce of your choice
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine bourbon, BBQ sauce, honey, soy sauce, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Simmer over medium heat for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside to cool.
  2. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then brush them generously with the bourbon glaze.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Place the thighs skin-side down and cook for 8-10 minutes until the skin is crispy. Flip and cook for an additional 7-9 minutes, brushing with glaze occasionally.
  4. Remove from the grill once the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). Let rest for 5 minutes before serving.
  5. Pair with grilled corn on the cob or a light coleslaw for a satisfying dinner.

2. Cedar-Plank Grilled Salmon with Honey Mustard

The subtle smokiness from the cedar enhances the natural richness of the salmon, while a honey mustard glaze adds tang and a hint of sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (skin-on, about 6 oz each)
  • 1 cedar plank (soaked in water for 1 hour)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Place the cedar plank on the grill until it starts to smoke, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. In a small bowl, mix Dijon mustard, honey, lemon juice, salt, and black pepper to create the glaze.
  3. Place the salmon fillets, skin-side down, on the cedar plank. Brush the top of the salmon generously with the honey mustard glaze.
  4. Close the grill lid and cook for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve with grilled asparagus and a side of wild rice for a balanced plate.

3. BBQ Pork Tenderloin Kebabs

Kebabs are a fun and versatile way to infuse flavor into grilled dishes. These pork tenderloin kebabs are marinated in a smoky, tangy BBQ-based sauce, perfect for a quick and hearty dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin (about 12 oz), cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine BBQ sauce, olive oil, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, onion powder, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper. Mix well, then add pork cubes, tossing to coat evenly. Let marinate for 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated pork onto the soaked skewers.
  3. Grill the kebabs for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the pork is cooked through and reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
  4. Serve with a tangy cucumber salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

4. Charred Veggie Medley with Herb Butter

Pair your grilled meats with a vibrant mix of fresh veggies cooked to perfection on the grill. This herb butter adds richness and depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into large chunks
  • 1/2 red onion, sliced
  • 8-10 button mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp butter, softened
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Toss the vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Place vegetables directly on the grates or use a grill basket. Cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until slightly charred and tender.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with parsley and thyme.
  4. Transfer the grilled vegetables to a serving platter and top with dollops of herb butter. Let it melt over the warm veggies for a delicious finish.

5. Grilled Peaches with Honey Drizzle

End your BBQ dinner with a simple yet decadent dessert. Grilled peaches offer a warm, caramelized flavor that pairs beautifully with a drizzle of honey.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • Optional: Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the cut sides of the peaches with olive oil.
  2. Place the peaches cut-side down on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes, until grill marks appear and the fruit softens slightly.
  3. Remove from the grill and drizzle with honey. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an indulgent treat.

Tips for Successful BBQ Cooking for Two

  • Invest in quality ingredients: When cooking for two, prioritize fresh, high-quality ingredients to elevate the flavors of your recipes.
  • Use a meat thermometer: Ensuring your proteins are cooked to safe temperatures is key for both safety and taste.
  • Focus on balance: Pair your grilled main dishes with complementary sides and fresh vegetables for a harmonious meal.
  • Experiment with marinades: Marinades and rubs are the secret to adding depth to your BBQ dishes. Feel free to tweak recipes to suit your personal preferences.

Cooking BBQ meals for two doesn’t have to be complicated. With the right recipes and a little creativity, you can turn any evening into a culinary adventure. So grab your apron, fire up the grill, and get ready to enjoy flavorful, intimate dining in the comfort of your home — or outdoors for an extra touch of magic. Happy grilling!
