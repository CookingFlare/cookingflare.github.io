Easy Nut-Free Stuffed Zucchini Boats Recipe


Zucchini boats are a delightful way to enjoy the fresh taste of summer vegetables while being creative in the kitchen. These Easy Nut-Free Stuffed Zucchini Boats are not only simple to make but also packed with flavor and nutrients. Whether you’re looking for a healthy weeknight dinner or a delicious side dish for a family gathering, this recipe is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the exciting world of stuffed zucchini boats!

Ingredients

To create these tasty zucchini boats, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Zucchini Boats:

  • 4 medium-sized zucchinis
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Stuffing:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa (or rice for a different texture)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (any color)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (or dairy-free cheese if preferred)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (omit for a dairy-free option)

Equipment Needed

Before we start cooking, ensure you have the following kitchen tools:

  • A sharp knife
  • A cutting board
  • A baking dish
  • A large mixing bowl
  • A skillet or frying pan
  • A spoon for scooping out the zucchini

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepping the Zucchini

  1. Wash the Zucchini: Rinse the zucchinis under cold water to remove any dirt. Pat them dry with a towel.
  2. Slice the Zucchini: Cut each zucchini in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scoop out the seeds and some flesh, creating a boat-like shape. Leave about 1/4 inch of the flesh intact to maintain structure.
  3. Season the Zucchini: Drizzle the zucchini halves with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place them cut-side up in a baking dish.

Step 2: Making the Stuffing

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until the onion is translucent (about 3-4 minutes).
  2. Add the Bell Pepper and Corn: Toss in the diced bell pepper and corn, cooking for another 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Combine with Quinoa: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, sautéed vegetables, halved cherry tomatoes, Italian seasoning, and half of the mozzarella cheese. Mix until everything is evenly combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add Fresh Basil: For an extra burst of flavor, fold in the chopped fresh basil. This step is optional but adds a fresh touch to your stuffing.

Step 3: Stuff the Zucchini Boats

  1. Fill the Zucchini: Spoon the stuffing mixture generously into each zucchini half, pressing down lightly to pack it in.
  2. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese on top of the stuffed zucchini boats. If you’re using Parmesan cheese, add it now for an extra cheesy finish.

Step 4: Bake the Zucchini Boats

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. This will help the zucchini soften without drying out.
  3. Uncover and Crisp: After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 5: Serving

Once the zucchini boats are cooked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve warm, garnished with additional fresh basil if desired. These stuffed zucchini boats make a vibrant and nutritious dish that is perfect for any occasion.

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Zucchini Boats

  • Choosing Zucchini: Look for zucchinis that are firm and smooth. Smaller zucchinis tend to be sweeter and more tender.
  • Experiment with Fillings: This recipe is versatile. Feel free to add cooked ground meat, beans, or other veggies to the stuffing mixture for variation.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the stuffed zucchini in advance. Just store them in the refrigerator, covered, and bake them when you’re ready to eat.
  • Leftovers: Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat them in the oven or microwave before serving.

Nutritional Benefits

Zucchini is a nutrient-dense vegetable, low in calories but high in essential vitamins and minerals. It is an excellent source of vitamin C, potassium, and antioxidants, making it a great addition to any diet. This stuffed zucchini recipe is also rich in protein and fiber, especially if you choose quinoa or whole grains as your stuffing base.

Conclusion

These Easy Nut-Free Stuffed Zucchini Boats are a delicious, healthy, and versatile dish that can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of dietary restrictions. They are perfect for meal prep, a quick weeknight dinner, or a fun gathering with friends. With their vibrant colors and mouthwatering flavors, zucchini boats are sure to become a favorite in your household. So grab your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let your culinary creativity shine with this delightful recipe! Enjoy your cooking adventure!
