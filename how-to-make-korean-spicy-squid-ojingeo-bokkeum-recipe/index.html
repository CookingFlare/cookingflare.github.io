How to Make Korean Spicy Squid (Ojingeo Bokkeum) Recipe


Korean cuisine is famous for its bold flavors, and one dish that stands out in both taste and vibrant color is Ojingeo Bokkeum — a flavorful, spicy stir-fried squid that is a beloved dish across Korea. The combination of tender squid, spicy gochujang (Korean red chili paste), and savory ingredients creates an irresistible dish that can be served with a bowl of steamed rice or enjoyed as a main course.

In this detailed guide, we’ll walk you through the process of making this popular Korean dish, Ojingeo Bokkeum, at home. With a few simple ingredients, you’ll be able to recreate the experience of a Korean meal right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Ojingeo Bokkeum

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients that will make this dish shine. Some may be familiar, while others might be new, but don’t worry – most of them are easily available at your local Asian grocery store or online.

Main Ingredients:

  • Fresh squid (Ojingeo): You will need around 500g (1 lb) of fresh squid. Opt for squid with a firm texture, as it holds up well during stir-frying.
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): The base of the spicy sauce is gochujang, a fermented chili paste with a combination of heat and umami.
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): These red chili flakes add a deeper level of heat and a nice smoky flavor.
  • Garlic: A few cloves of minced garlic add fragrance and depth to the dish.
  • Ginger: Fresh ginger, finely minced, gives the dish an aromatic freshness that complements the heat of the chili.
  • Soy sauce: Used for saltiness and umami flavor.
  • Sugar: A small amount of sugar balances out the spiciness of the chili paste and enhances the overall flavor profile.
  • Sesame oil: For an earthy, nutty finish, sesame oil brings an essential depth to the dish.
  • Rice wine or soju: A bit of rice wine or soju helps to tenderize the squid and adds a subtle sweetness.
  • Green onions (scallions): These add a bright, fresh crunch and contrast the heat in the dish.

Vegetables for Stir-Frying:

  • Onion: Half an onion, sliced thinly, will bring sweetness and crispness to the stir-fry.
  • Carrot: A small carrot, julienned, adds color and a slight crunch that pairs well with the tender squid.
  • Bell pepper: A red bell pepper (or any color you prefer) sliced thinly adds sweetness and extra vibrancy to the dish.

Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds: Toasted sesame seeds sprinkled over the finished dish add a delightful crunch and extra flavor.
  • Chili peppers (optional): If you like extra heat, you can add fresh sliced chili peppers as a garnish.

Preparation of Squid

To make your Ojingeo Bokkeum recipe a success, start by preparing the squid. Fresh squid is best for this dish because of its tender texture and natural sweetness. Here’s how to clean and prep the squid:

Step 1: Clean the Squid

  • Rinse the squid under cold running water.
  • Peel off the skin, which can be purple and slippery. It should come off easily, but you can use a paper towel to grip it and pull it away.
  • Remove the head and tentacles by cutting just behind the eyes. Don’t discard the tentacles, as they are edible and will be used in the dish.
  • Remove the ink sac if present, and discard it.
  • Cut off the fins (the wings) and set them aside.
  • Slice the squid body into rings (about 1/2-inch wide) or strips, depending on your preference.

Step 2: Prepare the Squid for Stir-Frying

Once cleaned and sliced, pat the squid dry with paper towels. This step is important to remove excess moisture, ensuring that the squid will sear and not steam in the pan.

Making the Sauce for Ojingeo Bokkeum

The secret to the bold, spicy flavor in Ojingeo Bokkeum lies in the sauce. The combination of gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and garlic creates a spicy, savory, and slightly sweet coating for the squid.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

In a small bowl, mix together the following ingredients to create the sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 teaspoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine or soju
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of sesame oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of water (to thin the sauce, if needed)

Stir the ingredients well until the gochujang dissolves into a smooth, thick sauce. Set it aside.

Cooking the Ojingeo Bokkeum

With all your ingredients prepped and the sauce ready, it’s time to cook the Ojingeo Bokkeum. The key to a successful stir-fry is cooking the ingredients quickly over high heat, which helps to preserve the squid’s tender texture while infusing it with the bold flavors of the sauce.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

  • Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil or sesame oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  • Add the sliced onions, carrots, and bell peppers to the pan. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 2-3 minutes, or until they begin to soften and become fragrant. You want the vegetables to retain a slight crunch, as they will add texture to the final dish.
  • Remove the vegetables from the pan and set them aside.

Step 5: Stir-Fry the Squid

  • In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed and heat it over medium-high heat.
  • Add the squid rings and tentacles to the pan, spreading them out in a single layer. Stir-fry the squid for 2-3 minutes, or until they turn opaque and start to curl up. Be careful not to overcook the squid, as it can become tough and rubbery. Once done, remove the squid from the pan and set it aside.

Step 6: Combine Everything

  • Add a bit more oil to the pan if necessary. Then, return the vegetables to the pan and pour in the prepared sauce.
  • Stir everything together, ensuring the vegetables are coated in the sauce. Let the sauce simmer for a minute to thicken slightly and bring out its flavors.
  • Now, add the squid back into the pan and toss everything together gently to coat the squid in the sauce. Stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes until the squid is heated through and fully coated with the sauce.

Final Touches

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

  • Transfer the stir-fried squid to a serving dish.
  • Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and garnish with chopped green onions and chili peppers if desired.
  • Serve immediately with a bowl of steamed rice for a complete meal.

Tips for the Perfect Ojingeo Bokkeum

To make sure your Ojingeo Bokkeum turns out perfect every time, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t overcook the squid: Squid cooks very quickly. Overcooking can cause it to become tough and rubbery, so be sure to stir-fry the squid for just 2-3 minutes.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of gochujang or gochugaru. You can always add more to taste after cooking.
  • Serve with sides: Ojingeo Bokkeum pairs wonderfully with simple side dishes such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, or a light soup.

Conclusion

Making Korean Ojingeo Bokkeum at home is easier than you might think, and with the right ingredients and techniques, you can recreate this delicious, spicy stir-fried squid in your own kitchen. The perfect balance of heat from the gochujang and gochugaru, the sweetness from the sugar, and the nutty aroma of sesame oil all come together to make a dish that’s both flavorful and satisfying. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or trying it for the first time, this recipe will surely become a staple in your cooking repertoire.

Try it out and enjoy the vibrant and bold flavors of Korea at home!
