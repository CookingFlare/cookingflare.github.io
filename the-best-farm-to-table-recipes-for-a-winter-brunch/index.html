The Best Farm to Table Recipes for a Winter Brunch


The crisp chill in the air coupled with the warm, earthy flavors of the season creates a beautiful opportunity for an inviting winter brunch. This is the season where farm-to-table cuisine truly shines with its hearty root vegetables, winter greens, and seasonal touches that reflect the richness of nature. The connection to fresh, locally sourced ingredients not only enhances flavor but also brings a sense of sustainability to your table. From savory to sweet, here’s how you can build the ultimate winter brunch experience with farm-to-table recipes.

1. Warm Root Vegetable Frittata

Start your brunch with a rustic root vegetable frittata, a hearty dish perfect for highlighting locally sourced produce. Root vegetables like sweet potatoes, parsnips, and carrots are at their peak flavor in winter, and they add depth and texture to this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs (free-range if possible)
  • 1 cup sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup parsnips, peeled and diced
  • ½ cup shallots, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ¼ cup freshly grated parmesan
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a cast iron skillet over medium heat. Sauté the shallots until translucent, then add the diced sweet potatoes and parsnips. Cook for 8-10 minutes until tender and lightly browned.
  3. Beat the eggs in a bowl, season with salt, pepper, and thyme, and pour the mixture over the vegetables. Let the edges start to set.
  4. Sprinkle parmesan over the top and transfer the skillet to the oven. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the frittata is puffed and golden brown.
  5. Serve warm, sliced into wedges. Pair it with a side of fresh winter greens for added vibrancy.

2. Citrus and Beet Salad with Farm Honey Vinaigrette

Brighten up your winter table with a citrus and beet salad, a dish that beautifully balances sweetness and acidity. Beets, with their rich and earthy flavor, combine wonderfully with the sweet zest of oranges and grapefruits.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium beets, roasted and sliced
  • 2 blood oranges, peeled and segmented
  • 1 grapefruit, peeled and segmented
  • 4 cups mixed winter greens (like kale and arugula)
  • ¼ cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons roasted sunflower seeds

Honey Vinaigrette:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon local raw honey
  • ½ teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. To roast the beets, wrap them in foil and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45-60 minutes or until tender. Peel and slice once cooled.
  2. Arrange the winter greens on a serving platter. Top with roasted beet slices, citrus segments, goat cheese, and sunflower seeds.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the vinaigrette ingredients until well combined.
  4. Drizzle the honey vinaigrette over the salad before serving. This dish is a celebration of winter’s vibrant produce.

3. Savory Herbed Biscuits with Whipped Butter

These savory biscuits take the concept of comfort food up a notch. Made with fresh herbs and locally sourced butter, they’re perfect for mopping up sauces or enjoyed as a standalone delight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and diced
  • ¾ cup whole milk or buttermilk
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as rosemary, chives, or parsley)
  • For whipped butter: ½ cup unsalted farm butter, softened and whipped with a drizzle of honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Add the cold butter and cut it into the flour using a pastry cutter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Stir in the chopped herbs and gradually add milk, mixing until the dough comes together.
  4. Turn the dough onto a floured surface, gently knead, and pat into a 1-inch-thick round. Use a biscuit cutter to cut out rounds.
  5. Place on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with whipped honey butter on the side.

4. Butternut Squash and Sage Hash

For a cozy and satisfying side dish, this butternut squash and sage hash is a must-have. Its caramelized goodness and aromatic sage pairing make it an excellent addition to your brunch spread.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or clarified butter
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the butternut squash, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the squash is tender and caramelized.
  3. Stir in the sage during the last few minutes of cooking.
  4. Serve as a warm and flavorful side dish that complements your frittata or biscuits.

5. Maple and Pear Galette

No brunch is complete without a touch of sweetness. This rustic maple and pear galette brings together the finest local winter pears with a hint of maple for a perfectly balanced dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of pre-made pie dough or homemade pastry
  • 3 ripe pears, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Powdered sugar for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the pie dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Arrange the pear slices in a spiral pattern in the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border.
  3. Drizzle the maple syrup over the pears and sprinkle with sugar and cinnamon.
  4. Fold the edges of the dough over the pears, overlapping as needed, to create a rustic galette.
  5. Brush the crust with an egg wash and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the pears are tender.
  6. Let the galette cool slightly before serving. Sprinkle with powdered sugar for an elegant touch.

Final Thoughts

Winter brings with it a wealth of farm-fresh ingredients that turn any meal into an authentic seasonal feast. When you incorporate these recipes into your brunch, you’re not just serving delicious dishes—you’re celebrating the bounty of local farms and supporting sustainable practices. Whether you’re entertaining guests or treating your family, these farm-to-table recipes will satisfy both the palate and the soul. So, tie on your apron and bring the best of the winter harvest straight to your table!
