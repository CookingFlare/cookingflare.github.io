Grilled Steak with Garlic and Rosemary: A Paleo-Approved Recipe for a Special Occasion


When it comes to cooking a steak, there are many ways to go about it, but one of the most classic and flavorful methods is grilling. Grilling a steak allows for a nice char on the outside while keeping the inside juicy and tender. In this recipe, we’ll be using garlic and rosemary to add a boost of flavor to our grilled steak.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5-2 pounds ribeye or strip loin steak
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges (optional)

Instructions:

First, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, season the steak with salt and pepper on both sides. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic and olive oil. Brush the garlic and olive oil mixture evenly onto both sides of the steak.

Once the grill is hot, place the steak on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature of the steak. For medium-rare, the internal temperature should be around 130-135°F (54-57°C), while medium should be around 140-145°F (60-63°C).

While the steak is cooking, sprinkle the chopped rosemary onto the steak during the last minute of cooking. This will allow the rosemary to infuse into the steak and add a nice flavor.

Once the steak is cooked to your liking, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. Slice the steak against the grain and serve with lemon wedges on the side, if desired.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a more well-done steak, cook for an additional 1-2 minutes per side.
  • If you prefer a more tender steak, cook for an additional 2-3 minutes per side.
  • You can also add other seasonings or spices to the garlic and olive oil mixture, such as paprika or thyme.
  • For a more intense rosemary flavor, you can increase the amount of rosemary used in the recipe.
  • If you’re looking for a more budget-friendly option, you can use a less expensive cut of steak, such as flank steak or skirt steak.

Paleo-Approved and Special Occasion-Worthy:

This grilled steak with garlic and rosemary recipe is not only delicious, but it’s also paleo-approved. The only ingredients used in this recipe are those that are naturally found in nature, making it a great option for those following a paleo diet. Additionally, this recipe is perfect for a special occasion, such as a birthday or anniversary dinner. The combination of garlic and rosemary adds a sophisticated flavor to the steak, making it a great choice for a special occasion.

Conclusion:

Grilled steak with garlic and rosemary is a classic recipe that’s easy to make and packed with flavor. By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a delicious and paleo-approved dish that’s perfect for a special occasion. With its rich flavor and tender texture, this steak is sure to impress your guests and leave them wanting more. So next time you’re looking for a recipe that’s both delicious and special, give this grilled steak with garlic and rosemary a try!
