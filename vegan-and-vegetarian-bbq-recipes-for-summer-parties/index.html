Vegan and Vegetarian BBQ Recipes for Summer Parties


Summer is the season of long, sunny days, refreshing drinks, and often, sizzling grills filled with delicious food. While barbecues are traditionally associated with meats, vegetarian and vegan BBQ dishes are quickly gaining popularity, providing vibrant, satisfying options that cater to everyone. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or bringing a dish to share, these cruelty-free recipes make everyone at the table feel included while making good use of seasonal produce and bold flavors.

Here’s a collection of irresistible vegan and vegetarian barbecue recipes that will turn your next summer party into a mouthwatering event.

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers

Say goodbye to boring veggie burgers and hello to the earthy, meaty goodness of grilled portobello mushrooms! Portobellos have a natural umami flavor and hearty texture that makes them perfect for grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns
  • Optional toppings: lettuce, tomato slices, vegan cheese, avocado, caramelized onions, mustard, or pesto

Directions:

  1. Clean the mushroom caps thoroughly and remove the stems.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush this marinade onto both sides of the mushrooms and let them sit for 15–20 minutes.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the mushrooms cap-side down and grill for about 5–6 minutes per side, basting them with any remaining marinade.
  5. Assemble your mushroom “burger” on buns with your favorite toppings and serve hot.

Sticky BBQ Jackfruit “Ribs”

Jackfruit has emerged as a superstar in the world of plant-based eating. Its tender, fibrous texture makes it a great substitute for meat, especially when coated in a flavorful barbecue sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans young green jackfruit (in water or brine, not syrup)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ cup vegan BBQ sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Drain and rinse the canned jackfruit well. Shred the pieces using your hands or a fork.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant and softened.
  3. Stir in the jackfruit, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Pour in the vegan BBQ sauce and mix well, ensuring the jackfruit is thoroughly coated. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Transfer the jackfruit to foil packets and place them on the hot grill for about 10 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  6. Serve as a side dish or use it to top tacos, wraps, or loaded by itself.

Charred Corn with Herb Coconut Butter

Grilled corn is a summer classic, but this recipe adds a vegan twist by slathering the cobs with herb-infused coconut butter. It’s smoky, buttery, and delightfully fresh.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 4 tablespoons coconut butter (softened)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to high heat. Brush the corn lightly with olive oil and grill, turning occasionally, until charred and tender, about 10–12 minutes.
  2. In a small bowl, mix coconut butter, lemon juice, parsley, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Once the corn is cooked, slather the herb coconut butter generously over each cob.
  4. Serve immediately for a fresh, smoky treat.

Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Sweet Soy Glaze

Colorful, nutrient-packed, and incredibly easy to make, grilled vegetable skewers are a party favorite. A sweet soy glaze brings out the natural sweetness of the veggies while adding an irresistible umami kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, cubed
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Directions:

  1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. Combine soy sauce, maple syrup, sesame oil, and minced garlic in a bowl to make the glaze.
  3. Arrange the vegetables on the skewers and brush the sweet soy glaze generously over them.
  4. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat, rotating occasionally, for 8–10 minutes or until charred and tender.
  5. Drizzle additional glaze over the skewers before serving.

BBQ Sweet Potato Wedges with Spicy Tahini Sauce

This recipe offers a satisfying mix of smoky sweet potato wedges paired with a creamy, tangy, and slightly spicy tahini sauce for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha or chili paste
  • 2 tablespoons water (to thin the sauce)

Directions:

  1. Toss the sweet potato wedges with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Grill the wedges over medium heat for 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and slightly charred.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk tahini, lemon juice, sriracha, and water until creamy, adding more water if necessary to reach your desired consistency.
  4. Serve the wedges hot with the tahini sauce on the side.

Grilled Peach and Arugula Salad

When people think of barbecues, salads often get overlooked. This grilled peach and arugula salad is the perfect blend of smoky, sweet, and tangy flavors, making it the ideal summer-side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 4 cups arugula
  • ¼ cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • ¼ cup vegan feta cheese (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Brush peach halves lightly with olive oil and place them on the grill over medium heat for 3–4 minutes per side, until softened and slightly caramelized.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Add arugula, sliced almonds, and grilled peaches to the bowl and toss gently with the dressing.
  4. Sprinkle vegan feta on top before serving.

Final Tips for Hosting a Vegan and Vegetarian BBQ

Creating a memorable summer barbecue that caters to vegans and vegetarians isn’t just about the food; it’s also about ensuring variety, color, and inclusivity. Here are a few additional tips to make your party a hit:

  • Keep a selection of condiments and sauces on hand for customization—think vegan mayo, avocado crema, homemade salsa, or chimichurri.
  • Build a vibrant fruit platter or grilled pineapple skewers for dessert.
  • Be mindful of grill cross-contamination if you’re preparing meat alongside plant-based options—consider using separate grill sections or pans for vegan and vegetarian dishes.

Summer parties are a time to celebrate good company, warm weather, and delicious food. With these plant-based BBQ recipes, there’s no doubt your guests will leave satisfied, inspired, and looking forward to the next grill session!
