Low Carb Grilling: 12 Recipes for a Summer BBQ


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and gather friends and family for an epic backyard barbecue. But if you’re aiming to keep your carb intake in check without sacrificing flavor, it might seem challenging to balance satisfying dishes with healthy options. Fortunately, low-carb grilling offers a world of mouthwatering possibilities that leave no room for compromise. From juicy proteins to crisp veggies, these 12 tasty recipes are designed to take your summer BBQ to new heights while keeping your plate wholesome and carb-conscious.

1. Smoky Grilled Bone-In Pork Chops

Bone-in pork chops are brimming with flavor and easy to prepare on the grill. To enhance their smokiness, rub them down generously with a mix of smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium heat for 4-5 minutes per side, ensuring a slight char for that irresistible smoky crust.

Serve these pork chops with a refreshing herb and lemon gremolata for a zesty finish. Pair them with grilled asparagus or a mixed green salad to create a balanced meal that keeps your carbs in check.

2. Citrus-Marinated Salmon Steaks

Salmon is naturally low in carbs and rich in heart-healthy fats, making it a fantastic choice for grilling. Marinate salmon steaks in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, orange zest, minced garlic, and fresh thyme for an hour before grilling.

Cook on medium heat for about 6-8 minutes, flipping halfway, until the salmon is tender yet firm. Complement this recipe with grilled zucchini ribbons or a light cucumber-dill salad.

3. Grilled Buffalo Chicken Wings

Skip the breading to make these classic wings unmistakably low-carb. Toss your chicken wings in a blend of melted butter, hot sauce, a dash of smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper for fiery flavor.

Grill them over direct heat for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until crisp and fully cooked. Serve with celery sticks and a creamy blue cheese dressing for a crowd-pleasing appetizer.

4. Garlicky Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Shrimp skewers are quick, easy, and perfect for summer grilling. Toss peeled and deveined shrimp in olive oil mixed with minced garlic, parsley, lemon juice, and chili flakes.

Thread the shrimp onto skewers and cook over medium-high heat for 2-3 minutes per side or until pink and slightly charred. These skewers shine when paired with grilled vegetables like bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, and onions.

5. Herb-Crusted Grilled Lamb Chops

Lamb chops are a hearty protein option that benefits from fresh herbs to amplify their flavor. Rub them with a blend of rosemary, thyme, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Grill lamb chops over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side or until cooked to your liking. Pair them with a low-carb cauliflower rice pilaf for a filling, flavorful dinner.

6. Mexican Grilled Flank Steak

Flank steak is a tender, versatile cut that’s perfect for marinating. Whisk together lime juice, cilantro, minced garlic, cumin, chili powder, and olive oil to create a bold marinade.

Let the steak soak for at least two hours before grilling it on high heat for about 6-8 minutes, flipping halfway. Thinly slice against the grain and serve in lettuce wraps with avocado slices and a dash of salsa verde.

7. Cheesy Portobello Mushroom Burgers

Portobello mushrooms are a hearty, low-carb bun alternative as well as excellent carriers of flavor. Remove the stems and marinate the caps with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, and Italian seasoning.

Grill the mushrooms cap-side down for 5 minutes, then flip and add mozzarella or cheddar cheese to the underside for melty goodness. Stack with your favorite burger toppings like lettuce, tomato, avocado, and bacon for a satisfying summer meal.

8. Grilled Cilantro-Lime Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are a juicy, flavorful option that pairs beautifully with bold spices. Create a marinade with lime juice, cilantro, garlic, olive oil, smoked paprika, and a pinch of salt. Let the chicken soak for at least an hour to absorb the flavors.

Grill the thighs for 6-8 minutes on each side, ensuring a crispy char on the outside. Serve with grilled bell peppers and onions or a side of sautéed spinach.

9. Charred Cauliflower Steaks with Chimichurri

Cauliflower steaks are an ideal veggie-forward dish that feels indulgent yet keeps your meal light. Slice large cauliflower heads into thick, steak-like pieces and brush them with olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Grill over medium heat for 4-5 minutes per side or until charred and tender. Finish with a drizzle of chimichurri sauce made from parsley, garlic, red pepper flakes, vinegar, and olive oil.

10. Stuffed Jalapeños Wrapped in Bacon

Stuffed jalapeños are a bite-sized BBQ favorite. Slice jalapeños in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. Fill them with a mixture of cream cheese, shredded cheddar, and a dash of smoked paprika.

Wrap each stuffed jalapeño half with a slice of bacon and secure with toothpicks. Grill over medium heat for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the bacon is crisp and the cheese bubbling.

11. Grilled Eggplant Slices with Tahini Dressing

Eggplant is a rich, meaty vegetable that becomes tender and smoky when grilled. Slice eggplants into rounds or long strips and brush liberally with olive oil, garlic powder, and a sprinkle of sea salt.

Grill on medium heat for 3-4 minutes per side until softened. Serve with a drizzle of tahini dressing and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds for a Mediterranean-inspired dish.

12. Grilled Sausage and Pepper Skewers

Sausage is a BBQ staple that pairs perfectly with colorful vegetables. Slice your favorite low-carb sausage into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto skewers alongside red and yellow bell peppers, zucchini, and onion wedges.

Brush everything with a mix of olive oil, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and a pinch of black pepper. Grill for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the sausage is heated through and the veggies are slightly charred.

Tips for Low-Carb BBQ Success

While grilling these delicious recipes, incorporate these tips to elevate your summer BBQ experience:

  • Choose Fresh Ingredients: Make sure your vegetables and proteins are fresh to maximize flavor.
  • Use Healthy Fats in Marinades: Olive oil, avocado oil, and butter bring great depth to marinades and dressings.
  • Embrace Bold Spices: Opt for robust seasonings like smoked paprika, garlic, chili flakes, and black pepper to add punch without adding carbs.
  • Keep It Simple: Don’t overcomplicate recipes; grilling is about enjoying pure flavors and simplicity.

Low-carb grilling is all about embracing lean proteins and vibrant vegetables, preparing them with bold flavors, and celebrating summer together. Try these 12 recipes at your next BBQ and experience how satisfying healthy eating can be, even during indulgent gatherings. Your guests won’t even miss the carbs!
