Mouthwatering Omelette Ideas to Upgrade Your Brunch Game


Brunch is one of the most beloved meals of the day, and omelettes are a staple of the morning. But let’s be honest, the classic fillings of cheese, ham, and bell peppers can get a bit tired after a while. It’s time to shake things up and try some new and exciting omelette ideas to upgrade your brunch game!

Smoked Salmon and Cream Cheese Omelette

Start by beating 2 large eggs with a fork and seasoning with salt and pepper. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter. Once melted, pour in the eggs and let them cook for about 30 seconds. Add 2 tablespoons of cream cheese and 1/4 cup of smoked salmon, diced. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelette and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked egg to flow to the top. Once the eggs are almost set, use the spatula to fold the omelette in half. Cook for an additional 30 seconds and serve hot.

Spinach and Feta Omelette

For this omelette, you’ll need 2 large eggs, 1/4 cup of fresh spinach leaves, chopped, and 1/4 cup of crumbled feta cheese. Beat the eggs with a fork and season with salt and pepper. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter. Once melted, pour in the eggs and let them cook for about 30 seconds. Add the chopped spinach and crumbled feta cheese to one half of the omelette. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelette and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked egg to flow to the top. Once the eggs are almost set, use the spatula to fold the omelette in half. Cook for an additional 30 seconds and serve hot.

Mushroom and Truffle Omelette

For this decadent omelette, you’ll need 2 large eggs, 1/2 cup of fresh mushrooms, sliced, and 1 teaspoon of truffle oil. Beat the eggs with a fork and season with salt and pepper. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter. Once melted, pour in the eggs and let them cook for about 30 seconds. Add the sliced mushrooms to one half of the omelette. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelette and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked egg to flow to the top. Once the eggs are almost set, use the spatula to fold the omelette in half. Drizzle with truffle oil and serve hot.

This colorful omelette is perfect for a summer brunch. You’ll need 2 large eggs, 1/2 cup of fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced, 1/4 cup of fresh basil leaves, chopped, and 1/4 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved. Beat the eggs with a fork and season with salt and pepper. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter. Once melted, pour in the eggs and let them cook for about 30 seconds. Add the sliced mozzarella cheese, chopped basil, and halved cherry tomatoes to one half of the omelette. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelette and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked egg to flow to the top. Once the eggs are almost set, use the spatula to fold the omelette in half. Cook for an additional 30 seconds and serve hot.
