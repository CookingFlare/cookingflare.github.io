Soy-Free Stir-Fried Rice: A Quick and Tasty Vegan Dish


Are you looking for a delicious vegan dish that’s quick to make and packed with flavor? Look no further than this soy-free stir-fried rice recipe! This dish is perfect for those who want to enjoy the goodness of stir-fried rice without the soy, whether due to dietary restrictions or personal preference.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably jasmine or basmati)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tamari sauce (or coconut aminos for a soy-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent.

  2. Add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional minute, until fragrant.

  3. Add the diced red bell pepper and carrot to the pan. Stir-fry for about 5 minutes, until the vegetables start to soften.

  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan, creating a space in the center. Crack the eggs into the center and scramble them until fully cooked.

  5. Add the frozen peas to the pan and stir-fry for another 2 minutes, until the peas are heated through.

  6. Add the cooked rice to the pan and mix well with the vegetables and scrambled eggs.

  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamari sauce (or coconut aminos), rice vinegar, maple syrup, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the rice and vegetables, stirring well to ensure everything is evenly coated.

  8. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is heated through and the flavors are well combined.

  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  10. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or green onions if desired.

Why Choose Soy-Free Stir-Fried Rice?

Soy-free stir-fried rice is a great option for those who have soy allergies or sensitivities, follow a soy-free diet, or simply want to try a new twist on a classic dish. By using tamari sauce or coconut aminos instead of soy sauce, you can still achieve that umami flavor without compromising your dietary needs.

Additionally, this recipe is vegan-friendly, making it a perfect choice for those following a plant-based lifestyle. It’s packed with colorful and nutritious vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, and peas, providing a satisfying and well-rounded meal.

Customizing Your Stir-Fried Rice

Feel free to get creative with this recipe and make it your own! Here are some ideas for customizing your soy-free stir-fried rice:

  • Add protein: If you’re looking to boost the protein content, you can add tofu, tempeh, or edamame to the dish. Simply cook them separately and toss them in with the rice and vegetables at the end.

  • Spice it up: If you like a bit of heat, add some crushed red pepper flakes or Sriracha sauce to the stir-fry.

  • Experiment with vegetables: Don’t be afraid to swap out or add different vegetables to suit your taste. Broccoli, mushrooms, and corn are all great options.

  • Try different grains: While this recipe calls for rice, you can also use other grains like quinoa or cauliflower rice for a healthier alternative.

In Conclusion

Soy-Free Stir-Fried Rice is a quick and tasty vegan dish that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner or a satisfying lunch. It’s easy to make, customizable, and packed with flavor. Whether you’re following a soy-free diet or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So grab your wok and get ready to stir-fry your way to a delicious meal!
