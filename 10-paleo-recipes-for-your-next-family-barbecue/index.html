10 Paleo Recipes for Your Next Family Barbecue


Barbecues are a perfect way to gather friends and family for good food, good conversation, and plenty of fun in the sun. However, if you’re following a Paleo diet or simply trying to embrace a healthier lifestyle, the traditional barbecue fare might not always align with your goals. But don’t worry – you can still enjoy a delicious, mouthwatering barbecue without compromising on your health or wellness.

The key to a successful Paleo barbecue is focusing on clean, whole foods that are free from processed sugars, grains, dairy, and legumes. By using fresh, natural ingredients, you can create flavorful and satisfying dishes that everyone, Paleo or not, will love. Whether you’re a seasoned Paleo enthusiast or a newcomer to this eating style, these 10 Paleo recipes are sure to elevate your next family barbecue and make it a memorable event.

1. Grilled Lemon Garlic Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are a great choice for grilling due to their rich flavor and juicy texture. These grilled lemon garlic chicken thighs will be a standout at your barbecue.

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Rub the marinade generously over the chicken thighs, ensuring each piece is coated evenly.
  3. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours for more flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Place the chicken thighs on the grill and cook for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  6. Remove from the grill and let rest for a few minutes before serving.

These chicken thighs are packed with flavor and are tender on the inside while crispy on the outside. Pair them with a side of grilled vegetables for a wholesome Paleo meal.

2. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

If you’re looking for a light and fresh side dish, zucchini noodles with pesto are an excellent choice. This dish is both refreshing and satisfying, making it a perfect complement to any grilled meat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Use a spiralizer to create zucchini noodles, or slice the zucchini into thin strips if you don’t have a spiralizer.
  2. To make the pesto, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a food processor. Pulse until smooth.
  3. Toss the zucchini noodles with the pesto until well coated.
  4. Serve immediately as a refreshing side dish or as a light main course.

Zucchini noodles are a great low-carb alternative to pasta, and the fresh pesto adds a burst of vibrant flavor that everyone will enjoy.

3. Grilled Shrimp Skewers with Mango Salsa

If you’re craving seafood, these grilled shrimp skewers paired with a fresh mango salsa are a winning combination. The sweet and spicy salsa perfectly complements the savory shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 mango (diced)
  • 1/2 red onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 jalapeño (seeded and minced)
  • 1 tablespoon cilantro (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Skewer the shrimp and place them on the grill. Grill for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they turn pink and opaque.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the diced mango, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and lime juice to make the salsa.
  5. Serve the shrimp skewers topped with the fresh mango salsa.

These shrimp skewers are bursting with tropical flavors and make for a light, refreshing option at any barbecue. They’re also quick and easy to prepare!

4. Grilled Veggie Platter

A vegetable platter on the grill is not only healthy but also visually stunning. This grilled veggie platter features a variety of vegetables with a smoky, charred flavor that perfectly complements any grilled meat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 bell peppers (sliced into large pieces)
  • 1 red onion (cut into wedges)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss all the vegetables in olive oil, salt, pepper, and dried oregano.
  3. Arrange the vegetables on the grill, cooking them for 3-5 minutes per side or until tender and slightly charred.
  4. Serve the grilled vegetables as a side dish or even as a main vegetarian option.

This grilled veggie platter is a delicious and nutritious way to add more plant-based goodness to your barbecue, and it’s easy to customize based on the vegetables you have on hand.

5. Paleo Burger Patties

What’s a barbecue without burgers? These Paleo burger patties are made with grass-fed beef and simple ingredients, making them a great option for anyone following a Paleo diet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 egg (optional for binding)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves for wrapping (instead of buns)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, egg, mustard, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Form the mixture into patties (about 4-5 patties depending on desired size).
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the patties for about 5-6 minutes per side or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  5. Serve the burgers wrapped in lettuce leaves instead of buns, or enjoy them alongside other Paleo-friendly sides.

These Paleo burger patties are juicy and flavorful, and they offer a great alternative to traditional buns. Add your favorite toppings like avocado, tomato, and onion for an extra boost of flavor.

6. Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon

For a sweet and tropical touch to your barbecue, grilled pineapple with a hint of cinnamon is an easy and healthy dessert option. The natural sugars in the pineapple caramelize on the grill, creating a delicious treat that’s perfect for summer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple (peeled, cored, and sliced into rings)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Sprinkle the pineapple slices with cinnamon on both sides.
  3. Grill the pineapple rings for about 2-3 minutes per side or until grill marks appear and the fruit softens.
  4. Drizzle with honey for extra sweetness if desired, and serve warm.

This grilled pineapple is a simple yet satisfying dessert that everyone will enjoy, and it’s naturally sweet, making it a perfect choice for those following the Paleo diet.

7. Paleo Sausages with Sauerkraut

Grilled sausages are a classic barbecue food, and this Paleo-friendly version features sausages made with clean ingredients and paired with tangy sauerkraut. It’s the perfect combination of savory and sour.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Paleo-friendly sausages (such as chicken, turkey, or pork)
  • 1 cup sauerkraut (homemade or store-bought with no added sugars)
  • 1 tablespoon mustard (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the sausages for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are browned and cooked through.
  3. Warm the sauerkraut in a small saucepan over low heat until heated through.
  4. Serve the sausages with a generous scoop of sauerkraut and a dollop of mustard if desired.

This dish is a great way to enjoy sausages while keeping things Paleo-friendly, and the sauerkraut adds a nice tangy contrast.

8. Paleo Coleslaw

Coleslaw is a traditional barbecue side dish, and this Paleo version uses a simple, creamy dressing that’s free from dairy and added sugars. It’s crunchy, tangy, and the perfect accompaniment to grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small head of cabbage (shredded)
  • 1 large carrot (grated)
  • 1/2 cup Paleo mayonnaise (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine shredded cabbage and grated carrot.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the Paleo mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

This creamy coleslaw is tangy and crunchy, and it’s a great way to add some texture to your barbecue spread without compromising on your dietary goals.

9. Paleo Grilled Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

A grilled steak is a classic barbecue favorite, and when paired with a zesty chimichurri sauce, it becomes something truly special. The fresh herbs in the chimichurri add a burst of flavor that complements the richness of the steak.

Ingredients:

  • 2 steaks (such as ribeye or sirloin)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Season the steaks with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  3. Grill the steaks for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on thickness and desired doneness.
  4. While the steaks are grilling, prepare the chimichurri sauce by combining parsley, red wine vinegar, olive oil, garlic, oregano, and red pepper flakes in a small bowl.
  5. Once the steaks are cooked, let them rest for a few minutes, then drizzle with chimichurri sauce and serve.

This dish is packed with bold flavors and is sure to impress your guests. The chimichurri sauce adds a refreshing, herbaceous element to the rich, smoky flavor of the steak.

10. Paleo Stuffed Mushrooms

These Paleo stuffed mushrooms are a great appetizer or side dish that’s full of flavor and perfect for the grill. The mushroom caps are stuffed with a savory mixture of ground meat and spices, then grilled until tender.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large mushroom caps
  • 1/2 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1/4 cup onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Remove the stems from the mushrooms and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground meat with onion and garlic over medium heat until browned and cooked through.
  4. Stir in almond flour and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Stuff the mushroom caps with the meat mixture and place them on the grill.
  6. Grill for 5-7 minutes or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is heated through.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

These stuffed mushrooms are an irresistible appetizer that will be a hit at your barbecue. They’re loaded with flavor and a great way to enjoy a Paleo-friendly dish with a satisfying texture.

Conclusion

With these 10 Paleo recipes, your next family barbecue will be full of flavor, fun, and healthy eating options. From juicy grilled chicken to fresh salads, seafood, and even delicious desserts, you’ll have everything you need to make your barbecue a success while staying true to your Paleo lifestyle. These dishes are not only nutritious, but they also showcase the vibrant flavors of fresh, whole foods. Enjoy your barbecue and share these mouthwatering Paleo recipes with family and friends – everyone will be asking for seconds!
