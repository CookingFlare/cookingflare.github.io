The Ultimate Guide to Middle Eastern Flatbread: Saj Bread


Flatbreads are a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine, and one of the most beloved varieties is Saj bread. This thin, round bread is cooked on a dome-shaped griddle known as a saj, which gives it its unique texture and flavor. In this ultimate guide, we will explore the history, ingredients, and step-by-step process of making delicious Saj bread at home.

History and Origins

Saj bread has a rich history that dates back centuries. It originated in the Levantine region, which includes countries like Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, and Palestine. Traditionally, Saj bread was made by rolling out dough into thin rounds and then cooking them on a hot saj griddle. The griddle was heated over an open fire, giving the bread a slightly smoky flavor.

Ingredients

To make Saj bread, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm water

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Mix well.

  2. Gradually add the warm water to the flour mixture, stirring with a spoon until a dough forms.

  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a clean bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Allow it to rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, divide it into small balls, roughly the size of a golf ball.

  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each ball of dough into a thin round shape, about 1/8 inch thick.

  7. Heat a saj griddle or a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.

  8. Carefully transfer one round of dough onto the hot griddle and cook for about 1-2 minutes, or until the bottom is golden brown and the top starts to bubble.

  9. Flip the bread using a spatula and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the other side is golden brown as well.

  10. Remove the cooked bread from the griddle and place it on a clean kitchen towel to keep it warm.

  11. Repeat the process with the remaining rounds of dough until all the bread is cooked.

  12. Serve the Saj bread warm, either as a standalone bread or as a delicious accompaniment to your favorite Middle Eastern dishes.

Serving Suggestions

Saj bread is extremely versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to inspire you:

  • Wrap it up: Use Saj bread as a wrap for your favorite fillings, such as grilled vegetables, falafel, or shawarma. It’s a delicious and convenient alternative to regular tortillas or pita bread.

  • Dip it: Tear the Saj bread into bite-sized pieces and serve it with a selection of Middle Eastern dips, such as hummus, baba ganoush, or tzatziki. The combination of the warm bread and flavorful dips is simply irresistible.

  • Top it: Spread a thin layer of olive oil on the Saj bread, sprinkle some za’atar (a Middle Eastern spice blend), and bake it until crispy. This makes for a delightful snack or appetizer.

Conclusion

Saj bread is a culinary gem of the Middle East, with its thin and slightly chewy texture. By following this ultimate guide, you can recreate the authentic taste of Saj bread in your own kitchen. Experiment with different fillings, dips, and toppings to discover your favorite way of enjoying this versatile flatbread. Whether you choose to wrap it, dip it, or top it, Saj bread is sure to impress your family and friends with its delicious flavor and unique cooking method.

So why wait? Get your saj griddle ready and start making your own Saj bread today!
