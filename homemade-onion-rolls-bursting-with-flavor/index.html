Homemade Onion Rolls: Bursting with Flavor


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through your kitchen. The warm, comforting scent draws you in, making your mouth water in anticipation. And when that bread happens to be homemade onion rolls, well, prepare to be blown away. These rolls are a flavor explosion, with the perfect balance of sweetness from the onions and the rich, savory notes of the dough. So, let’s dive right in and learn how to make these mouthwatering treats.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water (about 110°F)
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Mix well and set aside.

  2. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes frothy.

  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the milk until warm. Remove from heat and stir in the melted butter.

  4. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the yeast mixture and milk mixture. Stir until a soft dough forms.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

  7. While the dough is rising, prepare the onion filling. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the thyme, rosemary, black pepper, and salt. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool.

  8. Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll it into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick.

  9. Spread the cooled onion filling evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.

  10. Starting from one of the long sides, tightly roll the dough into a log. Pinch the edges to seal.

  11. Using a sharp knife, cut the log into 12 equal pieces. Place the rolls in a greased baking dish, leaving a little space between each roll.

  12. Cover the rolls with a clean towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes, or until they are puffy and have doubled in size.

  13. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  14. Bake the rolls for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped.

  15. Remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These homemade onion rolls are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. Serve them warm as a side dish to accompany your favorite soups or stews. They also make fantastic sliders for your next barbecue or picnic. Simply slice them in half, fill with your choice of protein and toppings, and enjoy!

Conclusion

Homemade onion rolls are a labor of love, but the results are more than worth it. These rolls are soft, fluffy, and bursting with the delicious flavors of caramelized onions and aromatic herbs. Whether you’re a seasoned bread baker or a beginner, this recipe is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, tie on your apron, and get ready to indulge in the irresistible taste of homemade onion rolls.
