Creole Cajun Shrimp Pasta Recipe for Spice Lovers


If you’re a spice lover looking to elevate your pasta game, this Creole Cajun Shrimp Pasta recipe is about to become your new favorite. Packed with bold flavors, plump shrimp, creamy sauce, and vibrant seasoning, this dish brings a touch of Louisiana’s finest culinary traditions to your dining table. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or treating yourself to something special, this recipe offers the perfect balance of heat, richness, and savory satisfaction.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Creole Cajun Shrimp Pasta is not just delicious; it’s a celebration of flavors. Creole and Cajun culinary traditions come together to create a blend of spices that wakes up the palate. Shrimp, one of the stars of this dish, absorbs the zesty marinade beautifully, while the cream-based sauce cuts through the heat for a harmonious finish. It’s easy enough to whip up on a weeknight while still impressive enough to serve for special occasions.

Ingredients

For the Shrimp:

  • 1 pound (450g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon Creole seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Juice of half a lemon

For the Pasta:

  • 12 ounces (340g) linguine or fettuccine pasta
  • 1 teaspoon salt for boiling water

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bell pepper (red, orange, or yellow), finely diced
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1 cup (240ml) chicken or vegetable broth
  • ½ cup (120ml) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 teaspoons Creole seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust heat level to your preference)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (plus extra for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

Start by seasoning the shrimp. Place your peeled and deveined shrimp into a medium-sized bowl. Drizzle olive oil over the shrimp and sprinkle on Cajun seasoning, Creole seasoning, smoked paprika, and lemon juice. Mix well until the shrimp are evenly coated. Cover the bowl and let the shrimp marinate in the refrigerator for at least 15–20 minutes while you prepare the rest of your ingredients.

Step 2: Cook the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add your pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining. This will be handy for adjusting the consistency of the sauce later. Set the pasta aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Shrimp

Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of olive oil or butter to the pan. Once hot, add the marinated shrimp in a single layer. Cook for 2–3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.

Step 4: Build the Sauce

In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant and translucent, which should take about 2 minutes. Then, add diced bell peppers and cook for another 4–5 minutes until tender.
Reduce the heat to low and stir in the chicken or vegetable broth, followed by heavy cream. Mix until smooth. Gradually add the Parmesan cheese, stirring constantly to prevent clumps. If you prefer a thicker sauce, sprinkle in one tablespoon of all-purpose flour and whisk thoroughly.

Step 5: Add the Cajun & Creole Flavors

To give the sauce its signature kick, stir in Creole seasoning, Cajun seasoning, cayenne pepper, and ground black pepper. Taste the sauce and adjust the spices to fit your heat tolerance. Let the sauce simmer gently for 4–5 minutes so the flavors meld together beautifully.

Step 6: Combine Pasta and Shrimp

Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet, stirring them into the creamy sauce. Next, add the cooked pasta to the mixture. Toss everything together until the pasta is fully coated in the sauce and the shrimp are evenly distributed. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to help loosen it up.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the dish for a pop of brightness and color. Serve the Creole Cajun Shrimp Pasta immediately, piping hot, with extra Parmesan cheese on the side. Pair with crusty garlic bread or a fresh green salad to complete the meal.

Expert Tips and Tricks

1. Marinate Longer for Extra Shrimp Flavor

If you have extra time, let your shrimp marinate for an hour or more. This ensures the spices penetrate deeply for maximum flavor.

2. Adjust the Heat Level

Spice lovers might want to add an extra pinch of cayenne or hot sauce to the sauce, while those sensitive to heat can reduce the cayenne or swap it for smoked paprika for a milder dish.

3. Experiment with Pasta Varieties

While linguine and fettuccine work beautifully, this recipe also pairs well with penne or rigatoni. The sauce clings nicely to tube-shaped pasta.

4. Use Freshly Grated Parmesan

Pre-shredded cheeses often contain anti-caking agents that can make your sauce gritty. Opt for a block of Parmesan and grate it yourself for a creamier texture.

5. Don’t Overcook the Shrimp

Shrimp can turn rubbery when overcooked, so keep a close eye as they cook. Two to three minutes per side is plenty for small to medium shrimp.
