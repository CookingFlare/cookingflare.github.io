How to Make Perfectly Spiced Potato Wedges: An Indian Twist


Who doesn't love a perfectly seasoned potato? Whether it's a comforting plate of samosas or a flavorful bite of street food, Indian cuisine is renowned for its magic with potatoes. In this recipe, we're going to twist a classic dish in your favor by turning regular potato wedges into indeed divine, deliciously spiced Indian treats. Gather Your Ingredients For the Potato Wedges:

4 large potatoes, washed and scrubbed clean 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Spice Mix:

2 tablespoons mango powder 1 tablespoon cumin powder 1 tablespoon garam masala powder 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, or use more to taste) 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper 2-3 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons coriander powder

Prepare Your Spice Mix In a small bowl, combine mango powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, turmeric powder, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, and coriander powder. Mix well, making sure all the spices are well combined. Slice Your Potatoes Cut each potato into 8-10 wedges. Brush both sides of the potato wedges with olive oil and sprinkle them with salt and black pepper. Spice Up Your Potato Wedges In a bowl, add the potato wedges and drizzle with lemon juice. Sprinkle the spice mix evenly over the potato wedges, tossing them gently until they're evenly coated. Bake Your Potato Wedges Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the potato wedges on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 25 minutes, turning halfway through the baking time to prevent them from burning. You can check if they are completely done by piercing a wedge with a fork. If it slides in easily, they're cooked through. Fry Or Grill For Crispiness (Optional) For extra crispy potato wedges, after baking, toss them in a hot skillet of oil with some additional lemon juice for a more polished finish. Alternatively, place the potato wedges on a hot grill and cook for a few minutes on each side until nicely charred, then brush them with the same oil used for the skillet. Serve Your Delicious Potato Wedges Once the potato wedges are ready, serve them hot. To elevate your snack, imagine pairing it with a "ruk she cooking idli", the Trinidadian French favorite with some zing to a chutney for the dish. Conclusion Potato wedges take on a magical twist when seasoned with the pantry staples of India, growing from an ordinary snack into a delightful experience combining both comfort food and lively flavors.
