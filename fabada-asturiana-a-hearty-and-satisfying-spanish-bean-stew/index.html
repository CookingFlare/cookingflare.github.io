Fabada Asturiana: A Hearty and Satisfying Spanish Bean Stew


If you’re a fan of hearty and comforting dishes, then Fabada Asturiana is a recipe that you need to try. Originating from the Asturias region in northern Spain, this traditional bean stew is packed with flavors and textures that will warm your soul. Made with simple ingredients that are easily accessible, Fabada Asturiana is perfect for a cozy family meal or a gathering with friends. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 500g dried white beans (such as faba asturiana or butter beans)
  • 200g smoked Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 200g pancetta or bacon, cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Water, as needed

Instructions

  1. Soak the beans: The first step in preparing Fabada Asturiana is to soak the dried beans. Place the beans in a large bowl and cover them with water. Allow them to soak overnight or for at least 8 hours. This will help soften the beans and reduce the cooking time.

  2. Cook the beans: Drain the soaked beans and transfer them to a large pot. Cover the beans with fresh water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the beans simmer for about 1 hour, or until they are tender but not mushy. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface during cooking.

  3. Prepare the flavor base: While the beans are cooking, heat a separate pan over medium heat. Add the sliced chorizo and pancetta (or bacon) and cook until they release their fat and become slightly crispy. Remove the chorizo and pancetta from the pan, leaving the fat behind.

  4. Sauté the aromatics: In the same pan with the remaining fat, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  5. Add the seasonings: Return the chorizo and pancetta to the pan with the onions and garlic. Stir in the bay leaf, sweet paprika, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Combine everything: Once the beans are tender, add the flavor base to the pot of beans. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. If needed, add more water to ensure the beans are fully covered. Simmer the stew for another 30 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.

  7. Serve and enjoy: Fabada Asturiana is traditionally served in clay bowls or deep plates. Ladle the stew into individual bowls and garnish with some freshly chopped parsley. Serve it with crusty bread to soak up all the delicious flavors. Enjoy this hearty and satisfying Spanish bean stew with your loved ones!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra layer of flavor, you can add a piece of bone-in pork, such as ham hock or pork shoulder, to the pot of beans during cooking. The slow cooking process will infuse the stew with a rich meaty taste.
  • Feel free to customize the recipe to your liking by adding other ingredients such as carrots, celery, or even a splash of white wine.
  • Fabada Asturiana tastes even better the next day, so don’t hesitate to make a larger batch and enjoy the leftovers for a quick and delicious meal.

Final Thoughts

Fabada Asturiana is a classic Spanish dish that showcases the simplicity and beauty of traditional cooking. The combination of tender beans, smoky chorizo, and savory pancetta creates a stew that is both comforting and satisfying. Whether you’re looking to explore new flavors or simply want to warm up on a chilly evening, this recipe is sure to hit the spot. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and savor the taste of Spain in every spoonful of Fabada Asturiana!
