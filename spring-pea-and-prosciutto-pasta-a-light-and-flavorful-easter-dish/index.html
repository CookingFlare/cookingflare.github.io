Spring Pea and Prosciutto Pasta: A Light and Flavorful Easter Dish


As we welcome the season of renewal and rebirth, what better way to celebrate Easter than with a light and flavorful dish that encapsulates the essence of spring? This Spring Pea and Prosciutto Pasta is a delightful marriage of fresh, vibrant flavors and creamy textures, making it the perfect centerpiece for your Easter gathering. Whether you’re hosting a festive brunch or an intimate family dinner, this dish is sure to impress and delight your guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of pasta (such as fettuccine or penne)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 ounces of prosciutto, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Freshly chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the cooking water, and set aside.

  2. Cook the Prosciutto and Peas: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the prosciutto and cook until it becomes slightly crispy, about 3-4 minutes. Add the peas and garlic, and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the peas are bright green and tender.

  3. Create the Sauce: Pour the heavy cream into the skillet with the prosciutto and peas. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, then stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and lemon zest. Allow the sauce to thicken slightly, about 2-3 minutes, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Combine the Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the sauce, tossing gently to coat the pasta evenly. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it up.

  5. Serve and Garnish: Transfer the Spring Pea and Prosciutto Pasta to a large serving dish or individual plates. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Pairing Suggestions

This delightful pasta dish pairs beautifully with a crisp, chilled glass of Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc. The bright acidity and fruity notes of these wines complement the richness of the prosciutto and cream sauce, while their refreshing qualities elevate the overall dining experience.

Final Thoughts

This Spring Pea and Prosciutto Pasta is a celebration of the season’s bounty, bringing together the delicate sweetness of peas, the savory saltiness of prosciutto, and the luxurious creaminess of the sauce. Its vibrant colors and fresh flavors make it a fitting addition to your Easter table, embodying the spirit of new beginnings and the joys of the spring season.

So, as you gather with loved ones to mark this special occasion, consider adding this delightful pasta dish to your Easter menu. It’s a simple yet elegant offering that captures the essence of the season, leaving a lasting impression on all who partake in its delicious allure. Happy Easter, and may your celebrations be filled with love, joy, and delectable culinary delights.
