Taco Tuesday: Mexican-inspired Recipes for Two


Taco Tuesday is a beloved tradition that brings a burst of flavor to your weeknight dinners. If you’re looking to spice up your Taco Tuesday routine, we’ve got you covered with these Mexican-inspired recipes designed for two. From mouthwatering fillings to zesty salsas, these recipes will transport you to the streets of Mexico with every bite. So grab your partner, roommate, or friend and get ready to indulge in a fiesta of flavors!

1. Chicken Tinga Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 small corn tortillas
  • Toppings: diced avocado, chopped cilantro, crumbled queso fresco

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until softened.
  2. Add the chopped chipotle pepper, dried oregano, and ground cumin to the skillet. Cook for another minute to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Add the chicken breasts to the skillet and cook until they are no longer pink in the center.
  4. Remove the chicken from the skillet and shred it using two forks.
  5. Return the shredded chicken to the skillet and stir in the tomato puree. Cook for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to come together.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat until pliable.
  8. Serve the chicken tinga on the warmed tortillas and top with diced avocado, chopped cilantro, and crumbled queso fresco.

2. Grilled Shrimp Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • Toppings: shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, chili powder, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss until they are evenly coated with the marinade.
  4. Thread the shrimp onto skewers and grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Remove the shrimp from the skewers and set aside.
  6. Warm the flour tortillas on the grill for about 30 seconds on each side.
  7. Fill each tortilla with a handful of shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, and a few grilled shrimp.
  8. Squeeze some fresh lime juice over the tacos before serving.

3. Vegetarian Black Bean Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • Toppings: sliced avocado, diced red onion, chopped cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, diced bell pepper, and minced garlic. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
  2. Add the drained and rinsed black beans to the skillet. Stir in the ground cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beans are heated through and the flavors have melded.
  4. Warm the flour tortillas on a dry skillet over medium heat until pliable.
  5. Fill each tortilla with a generous scoop of the black bean mixture.
  6. Top with sliced avocado, diced red onion, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

Conclusion

Taco Tuesday doesn’t have to be boring. With these Mexican-inspired recipes for two, you can elevate your weeknight dinner and satisfy your cravings for bold flavors. Whether you prefer tender chicken tinga, succulent grilled shrimp, or hearty vegetarian black beans, these tacos are sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So gather your loved one or a close friend, and enjoy a taste of Mexico right in your own home. Buen provecho!
