Quick and Easy Nut-Free Chicken Noodle Soup Recipe


There’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of chicken noodle soup to comfort the soul and nourish the body, especially on a chilly day or when you’re feeling under the weather. This quick and easy nut-free chicken noodle soup recipe is perfect for those who need a hearty meal in a short amount of time. Packed with tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and perfectly cooked noodles, this soup is both delicious and satisfying.

Ingredients

For the Soup Base:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 8 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Noodles:

  • 2 cups wide egg noodles (or your favorite noodle variety)

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

Start by prepping your vegetables. Dice the onion, mince the garlic, peel and slice the carrots, and slice the celery stalks. Having all your ingredients ready will make the cooking process much smoother.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Next, add the minced garlic and continue to sauté for another minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter.

Step 3: Add the Vegetables and Broth

Once the aromatics are ready, add the sliced carrots and celery to the pot. Stir to combine and let them cook for about 5 minutes to soften slightly. Pour in the chicken broth, ensuring all the vegetables are submerged. Increase the heat to high and bring the mixture to a boil.

Step 4: Simmer the Soup

Once the soup reaches a boil, reduce the heat to low to maintain a gentle simmer. Add the shredded cooked chicken to the pot. Season with dried thyme, dried parsley, salt, and pepper. Let the soup simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the vegetables to become tender.

Step 5: Cook the Noodles

While the soup is simmering, prepare the noodles according to the package instructions in a separate pot. Cooking the noodles separately prevents them from absorbing too much broth and becoming mushy. Once cooked, drain and set aside.

Step 6: Combine and Serve

After the soup has simmered and the vegetables are tender, it’s time to combine everything. Add the cooked noodles to the pot and stir gently to mix. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

Step 7: Garnish and Enjoy

Ladle the hot chicken noodle soup into bowls. For an extra burst of freshness, garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for those who enjoy a hint of citrus in their soup. Enjoy your comforting and delicious nut-free chicken noodle soup!

Tips and Variations

Tips:

  1. Use Rotisserie Chicken: Save time by using pre-cooked rotisserie chicken. Simply shred the meat and add it to the soup.
  2. Homemade Broth: For an even richer flavor, use homemade chicken broth if you have some on hand.
  3. Adjust Consistency: If you prefer a thicker soup, add a slurry of cornstarch and water towards the end of cooking. For a thinner soup, add more broth.

Variations:

  1. Vegetable Variety: Feel free to add other vegetables such as peas, corn, or green beans to the soup for added nutrition and color.
  2. Herb Substitutes: Fresh herbs like rosemary or dill can be used in place of dried herbs for a different flavor profile.
  3. Gluten-Free Option: Use gluten-free noodles or rice instead of traditional egg noodles for a gluten-free version of this soup.

Storage and Reheating

Storing:

Store any leftover soup in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Keep the noodles separate if possible to prevent them from getting too soft.

Freezing:

This chicken noodle soup freezes well minus the noodles. Freeze the broth and vegetable mixture in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. When ready to serve, thaw, reheat, and add freshly cooked noodles.

Reheating:

To reheat, simply warm the soup on the stovetop over medium heat until it reaches the desired temperature. If the soup has thickened too much, add a splash of chicken broth or water to reach the preferred consistency.

Conclusion

This quick and easy nut-free chicken noodle soup is a versatile and comforting dish that can be enjoyed any time of year. Whether you’re looking for a speedy weeknight dinner or a soothing remedy for cold days, this recipe delivers on flavor and ease. With simple ingredients and straightforward steps, you’ll have a delicious homemade soup ready in no time. So gather your ingredients, follow these steps, and enjoy a bowl of homemade goodness that warms from the inside out. Happy cooking!
