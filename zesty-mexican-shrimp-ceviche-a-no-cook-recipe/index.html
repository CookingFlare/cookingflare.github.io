Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche: A No-Cook Recipe


If you’re a fan of fresh, zesty flavors and love seafood, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche recipe. Ceviche is a popular dish in Mexico and other Latin American countries, known for its bright and tangy flavors that come together without any cooking required. It’s the perfect dish for a hot summer day when you want something refreshing and light. This recipe is not only easy to make but also a great way to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the preparation, let’s take a look at the key ingredients you’ll need for this mouthwatering Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche:

  • 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled, deveined, and chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup of fresh lime juice
  • 1 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips or tostadas for serving

Preparation

Now that you’ve gathered all the ingredients, it’s time to bring them together to create this delightful dish. Follow these simple steps to prepare your Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche:

  1. In a glass or ceramic bowl, combine the chopped shrimp and lime juice. Make sure the shrimp is completely submerged in the lime juice. This step is crucial as the acidity of the lime juice will “cook” the shrimp by curing it, giving it a tender and flavorful texture. Let the shrimp marinate in the lime juice for about 15-20 minutes until they turn opaque.

  2. While the shrimp is marinating, prepare the rest of the ingredients. In another bowl, mix together the diced tomatoes, chopped red onion, jalapeño, and cilantro. Stir well to combine all the flavors.

  3. Once the shrimp has turned opaque, drain off the excess lime juice. Add the marinated shrimp to the bowl of mixed vegetables and herbs. Gently fold the ingredients together, ensuring that the shrimp and vegetables are evenly coated with the zesty lime juice.

  4. Add the diced avocado to the mixture and carefully fold it in, being mindful not to mash the avocado pieces.

  5. Season the ceviche with salt and pepper to taste, and give it a final gentle stir to ensure all the flavors are well incorporated.

  6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the ceviche for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Serving

Once the Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche has chilled and the flavors have melded, it’s time to serve it up. This refreshing dish can be enjoyed in a variety of ways:

  • Spoon the ceviche into individual serving bowls and garnish with a sprig of fresh cilantro.
  • Serve the ceviche with tortilla chips or tostadas on the side for a satisfying crunch.
  • For a decorative presentation, consider serving the ceviche in martini glasses or small glass bowls, adding a touch of elegance to your presentation.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to consider when preparing this Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche:

  • For a spicier kick, leave the seeds in the jalapeño or add a pinch of red pepper flakes.
  • If you prefer a milder onion flavor, you can soak the chopped red onion in cold water for 10 minutes, then drain and pat dry before adding it to the ceviche.
  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding diced mango, pineapple, or cucumber for a fruity twist.
  • Experiment with different herbs such as mint or basil to add an unexpected flavor dimension to your ceviche.

Final Thoughts

Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche is a delightful no-cook recipe that’s bursting with fresh flavors and vibrant colors. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply craving a light and refreshing meal, this ceviche is sure to impress. With its zesty lime-marinated shrimp, crunchy vegetables, and creamy avocado, it’s a dish that embodies the essence of Mexican coastal cuisine. So, grab your ingredients, put on some lively music, and transport yourself to the sunny shores of Mexico with this mouthwatering Zesty Mexican Shrimp Ceviche. Buena provecho!
