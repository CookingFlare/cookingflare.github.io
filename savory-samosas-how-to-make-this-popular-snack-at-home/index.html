Savory Samosas: How to Make This Popular Snack at Home


Samosas are a popular Indian snack that has gained worldwide recognition for their delicious taste and crispy texture. These savory pastries are typically filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and onions, and are served as an appetizer or snack. In this recipe, we will guide you on how to make samosas at home, using a simple and easy-to-follow method.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • Filling ingredients (see below)

Filling Ingredients

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and diced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. To make the dough, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add the ghee or oil and mix until the dough comes together. Gradually add the lukewarm water and knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, until it becomes smooth and pliable. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. To make the filling, heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add the chopped onion and cook until it is translucent. Add the garlic, cumin, coriander, garam masala, and salt, and cook for 1 minute. Add the boiled potatoes, peas, lemon juice, and cilantro, and mix well.
  3. To assemble the samosas, divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 3-4 inches in diameter. Place a tablespoon or two of the filling in the center of the dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form a triangle or a cone shape, and press the edges together to seal the samosa. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  4. To fry the samosas, heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add a few samosas to the pan (do not overcrowd the pan) and fry until they are golden brown, about 3-4 minutes. Repeat with the remaining samosas.
  5. To bake the samosas, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the samosas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving some space between each samosa. Brush the tops with a little oil and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the samosas more crispy, you can add a little vinegar to the dough while kneading.
  • You can also add other ingredients to the filling, such as chopped carrots or cauliflower, to give it a different flavor and texture.
  • To make the samosas more spicy, you can add a little cayenne pepper or red chili powder to the filling.
  • You can also make samosas in advance and store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat them in the oven or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

Samosas are a delicious and popular snack that can be easily made at home. With this recipe, you can make samosas with a crispy exterior and a flavorful filling. Whether you prefer to fry or bake them, samosas are a great snack to enjoy with your favorite dipping sauce. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and experience the delicious taste of samosas at home!
