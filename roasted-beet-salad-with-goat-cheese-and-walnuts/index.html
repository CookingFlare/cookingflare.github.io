Roasted Beet Salad with Goat Cheese and Walnuts


As the winter winds howl outside, we often find ourselves craving hearty, comforting dishes that warm our bellies and soothe our souls. But what if I told you that there’s a salad that can satisfy your cravings and transport you to a world of flavors and textures, all while embracing the season’s bounty? Enter the Roasted Beet Salad with Goat Cheese and Walnuts, a symphony of sweet, earthy, and tangy notes that will leave you spellbound.

the stars of the show: roasted beets

The foundation of this salad is, of course, the roasted beets. These humble root vegetables are transformed into a sweet and earthy masterpiece when roasted to perfection. Simply preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C), scrub and chop three to four beets into wedges, and toss them with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them out on a baking sheet and roast for about 45 minutes, or until they’re tender and caramelized. Let them cool, then peel and slice them into thin wedges.

the supporting cast: creamy goat cheese

While the beets are the stars of the show, the creamy goat cheese is the perfect supporting actor. Crumbly and tangy, with a subtle sweetness, it adds a richness and depth to the salad that’s simply irresistible. You can use either a log or a crumbled goat cheese – whichever you prefer. If you’re feeling fancy, you can even make your own goat cheese at home. If not, a high-quality store-bought variety will do just fine.

the nutty crunch: toasted walnuts

And then there’s the walnuts – the unsung heroes of this salad. Toasted to perfection in a 350°F (180°C) oven for about 10 minutes, or until fragrant and lightly browned, they add a satisfying crunch and a nutty flavor that complements the beets and goat cheese perfectly. Chop them coarsely and sprinkle them over the top of the salad for a delightful textural contrast.

the finishing touches: a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of thyme

Finally, it’s time to bring all the elements together. Place a handful of mixed greens on a large plate or platter, then top with the roasted beets, crumbled goat cheese, and toasted walnuts. Drizzle a spoonful of honey over the top – its sweetness will balance out the earthiness of the beets – and sprinkle a few sprigs of fresh thyme for added depth and freshness. And that’s it! Your Roasted Beet Salad with Goat Cheese and Walnuts is ready to be devoured.

a winter’s night delight

As the snowflakes fall gently outside, gather your loved ones around the table and serve this salad as the perfect centerpiece for a cozy winter’s night. With its vibrant colors, enticing aromas, and symphony of flavors, it’s sure to become a new family favorite. So go ahead, take a bite, and let the warmth and comfort of this salad envelop you in its sweet, earthy embrace.

recipe

  • 3-4 beets, peeled and chopped into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 log or crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish
  • Mixed greens for serving

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss beets with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread out on a baking sheet. Roast for 45 minutes, or until tender and caramelized. Let cool, then peel and slice into thin wedges. Toast walnuts in a 350°F (180°C) oven for 10 minutes, or until fragrant and lightly browned. Assemble salad by placing mixed greens on a plate, topping with roasted beets, crumbled goat cheese, and toasted walnuts. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle with thyme leaves. Serve immediately.
