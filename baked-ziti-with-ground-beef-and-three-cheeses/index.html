Baked Ziti with Ground Beef and Three Cheeses


When it comes to hearty, delicious, and family-friendly meals, baked ziti with ground beef and three cheeses is a timeless favorite. This Italian-American comfort dish combines perfectly cooked ziti pasta with a savory meat sauce, rich cheeses, and a golden, bubbly top that is hard to resist. Whether you’re preparing a meal for a busy weeknight or planning for a special gathering, baked ziti is sure to be a crowd-pleaser. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity, yet the depth of flavor that comes from the combination of ingredients.

In this recipe, we’ll walk through every step to create a truly satisfying meal that will leave everyone asking for seconds. From the perfectly seasoned ground beef to the luxurious three-cheese blend, this baked ziti is the ultimate comfort food that is as easy to prepare as it is delicious.

Ingredients for Baked Ziti with Ground Beef and Three Cheeses

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s first gather all the ingredients you’ll need. The key to making this dish a success is using high-quality ingredients and following the steps carefully to achieve a perfectly baked ziti.

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 lb ground beef: Ground beef provides a rich, savory flavor that complements the tomato sauce perfectly.
  • 1 medium onion, diced: Onions add sweetness and depth to the sauce.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced: Garlic is essential for an aromatic, flavorful base.
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes: This forms the base of the sauce.
  • 1 (6 oz) can tomato paste: Tomato paste thickens the sauce and adds richness.
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional): Adds depth to the sauce, but you can leave it out if you prefer.
  • 1 tsp dried basil: For a subtle, fragrant Italian flavor.
  • 1 tsp dried oregano: A classic herb in Italian cooking.
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes: Optional, for a touch of heat.
  • Salt and pepper to taste: Enhances the flavor of the sauce.

For the Pasta:

  • 1 lb ziti pasta: Ziti is the traditional pasta for this dish, but you can substitute it with penne or rigatoni if needed.
  • Salt: For seasoning the pasta water.

For the Cheese Layer:

  • 1 1/2 cups ricotta cheese: Ricotta adds a creamy, mild flavor to the dish.
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese: Mozzarella melts beautifully, creating a gooey, stretchy texture that’s perfect for baked ziti.
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese: Parmesan provides a sharp, salty contrast to the mild mozzarella and ricotta.
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, chopped (optional): Fresh basil adds a burst of flavor and color, and it pairs wonderfully with the cheesy, savory sauce.

For Baking:

  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese (for topping): To finish off the baked ziti with a crispy, golden top.

Instructions for Preparing Baked Ziti with Ground Beef and Three Cheeses

Step 1: Prepare the Meat Sauce

Start by heating a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook it until browned, breaking it up with a spatula as it cooks. Once the beef is fully browned and no longer pink, drain any excess fat.

Add the diced onion to the pan with the beef and cook until the onions are soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.

Next, pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir everything together to combine. If you’re using red wine, add it now and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly. Add the dried basil, oregano, and red pepper flakes (if using), and stir well. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Let the sauce simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure that the flavors meld together. The sauce should thicken slightly and become rich in flavor. Once the sauce is ready, remove it from the heat and set it aside.

Step 2: Cook the Ziti Pasta

While your sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the ziti pasta and cook it according to the package instructions, usually around 8-10 minutes, until it’s al dente. Be careful not to overcook the pasta, as it will continue to cook when baked.

Once the pasta is cooked, drain it well and return it to the pot. Drizzle a little olive oil or butter over the pasta to prevent it from sticking, and set it aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Cheese Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Stir until all the cheeses are well incorporated and smooth. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. If you want an added burst of freshness, you can mix in some fresh chopped basil into the cheese mixture.

Step 4: Assemble the Baked Ziti

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a large 9x13-inch baking dish with a little olive oil or cooking spray to prevent the ziti from sticking.

Start by spreading a thin layer of the meat sauce at the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Then, layer half of the cooked ziti pasta over the sauce. Top the pasta with half of the cheese mixture, spreading it evenly across the noodles. Add another layer of meat sauce, followed by the remaining pasta and the rest of the cheese mixture.

Finish off by pouring the remaining meat sauce over the top of the pasta and cheese layers, ensuring everything is covered. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese on top for a golden, crispy finish when it bakes.

Step 5: Bake the Ziti

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.

Once the baked ziti is done, remove it from the oven and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to set and make it easier to serve.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Scoop generous portions of the baked ziti onto plates and top with fresh basil if desired. Serve with a side of garlic bread and a simple green salad for a complete meal that will leave everyone satisfied.

Tips and Variations for the Best Baked Ziti

While this recipe is simple and straightforward, there are many ways you can adjust it to suit your preferences or dietary needs.

1. Add Vegetables: For a more well-rounded meal, consider adding vegetables to the meat sauce. Chopped bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, or spinach can be sautéed with the onions and garlic to add more flavor and nutrition.

2. Use Italian Sausage: If you prefer a spicier or more flavorful meat sauce, try using Italian sausage instead of ground beef. The sausage will add an extra layer of savory flavor that pairs wonderfully with the cheesy layers.

3. Make it Lighter: If you want a lighter version of this dish, you can substitute low-fat ricotta and mozzarella cheeses. You can also use whole wheat or gluten-free ziti for a healthier alternative.

4. Double the Recipe: This recipe is perfect for feeding a crowd. Feel free to double the ingredients and use a larger baking dish to make enough for a party, family gathering, or meal prep for the week.

5. Freeze for Later: Baked ziti is an excellent dish for freezing. Once assembled, cover the unbaked ziti with plastic wrap and aluminum foil and store it in the freezer. When you’re ready to enjoy it, bake it straight from the freezer, adding an extra 10-15 minutes to the cooking time.

Final Thoughts

Baked ziti with ground beef and three cheeses is the kind of dish that brings comfort and joy to any table. With its layers of rich meat sauce, gooey melted cheese, and perfectly cooked pasta, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser that never goes out of style. Whether you’re preparing it for a family dinner or a special occasion, baked ziti is sure to satisfy your hunger and your taste buds.

Give this recipe a try, and enjoy the warm, delicious flavors of a homemade baked ziti that will quickly become a staple in your kitchen.
