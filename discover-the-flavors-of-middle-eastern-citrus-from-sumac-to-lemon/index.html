Discover the Flavors of Middle Eastern Citrus: From Sumac to Lemon


The flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine are as diverse as the region itself, with a rich tapestry of spices and ingredients that create a culinary experience like no other. One of the essential components of Middle Eastern cooking is citrus, which adds a bright, tangy dimension to many dishes. From the tartness of sumac to the zesty freshness of lemon, Middle Eastern citrus flavors bring a unique and refreshing twist to both savory and sweet dishes.

Sumac: The Tangy Spice

Sumac is a deep red spice derived from the dried and ground berries of the sumac bush. It has a tangy, lemony flavor with a slightly fruity and tart taste. Often used as a seasoning in Middle Eastern cuisine, sumac adds a bright, citrusy note to dishes, making it a versatile and essential ingredient.

Sumac Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sumac
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the olive oil, sumac, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and coat them evenly.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken onto the wooden skewers.
  4. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the chicken is cooked through and has a golden brown color.
  5. Serve the sumac chicken skewers with a fresh salad and a squeeze of lemon for an extra citrus kick.

Preserved Lemons: A Burst of Flavor

Preserved lemons are a staple in Middle Eastern cooking, adding a unique and intense citrus flavor to a variety of dishes. To make preserved lemons, whole lemons are pickled in salt and their own juice, resulting in a complex, tangy flavor that can elevate the taste of many dishes.

Preserved Lemon and Herb Couscous

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of couscous
  • 1 ½ cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 preserved lemon, flesh removed and peel finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh mint
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil.
  2. Stir in the couscous, cover the pan, and remove it from the heat. Let it stand for 5 minutes.
  3. Fluff the couscous with a fork and transfer it to a serving bowl.
  4. Add the preserved lemon peel, chopped parsley, chopped mint, and olive oil to the couscous. Season with salt and pepper, and toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve the preserved lemon and herb couscous as a refreshing side dish alongside grilled fish or roasted vegetables.

Citrus-Mint Salad with Pomegranate Molasses

This vibrant and refreshing salad combines the zing of citrus with the sweetness of pomegranate molasses, creating a delightful medley of flavors that is perfect for a light and healthy meal.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed salad greens
  • 2 oranges, peeled and segmented
  • 1 grapefruit, peeled and segmented
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 2 tablespoons of pomegranate molasses
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of finely chopped fresh mint
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, orange segments, and grapefruit segments.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, pomegranate molasses, olive oil, and chopped mint to make the dressing.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad, tossing gently to coat the ingredients.
  4. Season the salad with salt and pepper, and serve immediately as a refreshing accompaniment to grilled meats or as a standalone salad.

Conclusion

The citrus flavors of sumac, preserved lemons, and fresh citrus fruits play a vital role in Middle Eastern cuisine, adding a bright and tangy dimension to a wide range of dishes. Whether used in savory marinades, zesty salads, or as a unique pickled condiment, Middle Eastern citrus brings a burst of refreshing flavor that is sure to tantalize the taste buds. Embrace the vibrant world of Middle Eastern citrus, and elevate your cooking with these delightful and aromatic ingredients.
