Best Recipes for Holiday Leftovers: Creative Ideas


Holiday meals can be a real treat, but let's face it, the leftovers can be a bit of a challenge. While some people may view them as a necessary evil, we believe in making the most of a good thing. The key to successfully repurposing holiday leftovers is to think outside the box and come up with creative ways to transform last night's dinner into tomorrow's star dish. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a culinary novice, there are endless possibilities for turning holiday leftovers into something new and exciting. And the best part? You can use up to 75% of leftover food by incorporating it into your cooking. To get started, take stock of your fridge and pantry, and brainstorm ideas that showcase the flavors and ingredients of your holiday meal. Transforming Turkey into a Rich and Creamy Soup If you've got a turkey left over from Thursday's big feast, you're in luck. A simple turkey soup can be a comforting and satisfying meal that uses up all the extra meat and veggies from your holiday menu. To make this recipe, start by simmering the turkey carcass in a large pot of water to extract all the rich flavors. Then, add in some diced onions, carrots, and celery for added depth. Once the soup has cooled slightly, puree it in a blender or with an immersion blender until smooth. Add in some heavy cream or half-and-half to give the soup a rich and creamy texture, and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of paprika. Finally, serve hot with a swirl of cream and a sprinkle of chives. This soup is the perfect way to warm up on a chilly winter evening, and it's a great way to use up a whole turkey. Buffalo Chicken Stuffed Baked Potatoes For a fun twist on the classic baked potato, try using leftover chicken from your holiday meal to make buffalo chicken stuffed potatoes. Start by baking several large potatoes in the oven until tender, then slice them in half lengthwise. Scoop out some of the fluffy potato to make room for the filling. Meanwhile, chop up some leftover cooked chicken and mix it with shredded cheddar cheese, diced onions, and a dollop of buffalo sauce. Spread the mixture inside each potato shell and top with additional cheese, sour cream, and a sprinkle of chives. Bake in the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly, then serve hot alongside a side of celery sticks for a fun twist on the classic appetizer. Turkey and Mashed Potato Shepherd's Pie For a hearty and comforting casserole, try making a turkey and mashed potato shepherd's pie. Start by layering leftover mashed potatoes, turkey, and frozen peas in a large baking dish. Then, top with a layer of shredded cheddar cheese and a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs. Bake in the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the mashed potatoes are golden brown. Serve hot from the oven with a side of steamed kale or green beans. This classic comfort dish is a great way to use up leftover mashed potatoes and turkey, and it's a crowd-pleaser at any meal. Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Brown Butter Sage Sauce If you've got leftover sweet potatoes from your holiday meal, you can use them to make delicious homemade gnocchi. Simply mix cooked mashed sweet potatoes with flour, egg, and a pinch of salt to form a dough. Roll out the dough and cut into small pieces, then boil in a large pot of salted water until tender. While the gnocchi are cooking, make a brown butter sage sauce by melting butter in a skillet over medium heat until golden brown. Stir in chopped fresh sage leaves and a pinch of salt. Serve the gnocchi hot with a drizzle of the brown butter sauce and a sprinkle of parmesan cheese. This unique side dish is a perfect way to use up leftover sweet potatoes and showcase their natural sweetness. Cranberry BBQ Sauce Chicken Wings For a sweet and tangy appetizer, try using leftover cranberry sauce to make cranberry BBQ sauce chicken wings. Simply mix store-bought or homemade cranberry sauce with your favorite BBQ sauce, and stir in some chopped fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme. Brush the mixture onto chicken wings and bake in the oven until crispy. Serve hot with a side of ranch dressing or blue cheese dressing for dipping. This creative appetizer is a great way to use up leftover cranberry sauce and add a pop of color to your holiday menu. Conclusion As you can see, there are countless creative ways to use up leftover food from your holiday meal. From soups and stews to casseroles and appetizers, the possibilities are endless. And the best part? You can use up to 75% of leftover food by incorporating it into your cooking. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a culinary novice, we hope these ideas inspire you to think outside the box and come up with your own creative ways to use up leftovers. With a little creativity and some resourcefulness, you can turn last night's dinner into tomorrow's star dish. Happy cooking!
