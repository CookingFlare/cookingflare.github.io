Greek Spanakopita with Spinach and Feta Cheese Recipe


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or looking to explore new flavors, then you’re in for a treat with this traditional and mouthwatering Greek Spanakopita recipe. Spanakopita, a savory pastry filled with spinach and feta cheese, is a beloved dish in Greek culture, known for its flaky layers and rich, earthy flavors. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Phyllo Pastry:

  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed according to package instructions
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the spinach is cooked down, transfer it to a colander and press out any excess liquid.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked spinach, crumbled feta cheese, dried dill, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the flavors are evenly distributed.

Step 2: Assemble the Spanakopita

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Carefully unroll the phyllo dough and lay it flat on a clean, dry surface.
  3. Using a pastry brush, lightly coat a 9x13 inch baking dish with melted butter.
  4. Place one sheet of phyllo dough in the prepared baking dish, allowing the edges to hang over the sides. Lightly brush the sheet with melted butter.
  5. Continue layering the phyllo sheets, brushing each layer with butter, until you have used about half of the sheets.

Step 3: Add the Filling

  1. Spread the spinach and feta filling evenly over the layered phyllo sheets in the baking dish.
  2. Continue layering the remaining phyllo sheets on top of the filling, brushing each layer with melted butter.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

  1. Using a sharp knife, carefully score the top layer of the phyllo into squares or triangles, being careful not to cut all the way through to the bottom.
  2. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the Spanakopita is golden brown and crisp.
  3. Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before cutting into portions.

Serving Suggestions:

This Greek Spanakopita pairs beautifully with a refreshing Greek salad, featuring crisp cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, tangy olives, and a drizzle of olive oil and lemon juice. For a heartier meal, serve it alongside grilled lamb chops or roasted vegetables. Whether you’re enjoying it as an appetizer, side dish, or main course, this Spanakopita recipe is a delightful addition to any meal.

Conclusion:

Mastering the art of making Greek Spanakopita with spinach and feta cheese is a rewarding experience that will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Greece. With its flaky layers, flavorful filling, and rich cultural heritage, this dish is a testament to the beauty of Mediterranean cuisine. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that will leave you savoring every delicious bite. Opa!
