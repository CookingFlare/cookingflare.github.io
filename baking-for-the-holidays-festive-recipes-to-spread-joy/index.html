Baking for the Holidays: Festive Recipes to Spread Joy


‘Tis the season to bake and spread joy through delightful treats! Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or looking for the perfect homemade gift, holiday baking is a wonderful way to celebrate the spirit of the season. In this blog post, we’ll explore a collection of mouthwatering recipes that are sure to impress your loved ones. From classic cookies to decadent cakes, these recipes will fill your home with warmth and delicious aromas.

Gingerbread Cookies

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 24 cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, salt, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the molasses and egg, and mix well.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut out shapes using cookie cutters.
  6. Place the cookies on the baking sheet and bake for 10 minutes, or until set.
  7. Allow the cookies to cool before decorating with icing.

Peppermint Bark

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Chill Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 12 servings

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 16 oz white chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Crushed candy canes

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the semisweet chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, stirring until smooth.
  3. Pour the melted chocolate onto the prepared baking sheet and spread it into an even layer. Chill in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.
  4. Melt the white chocolate in the same manner, adding the peppermint extract.
  5. Pour the white chocolate over the semisweet chocolate layer and spread it evenly.
  6. Sprinkle crushed candy canes on top and chill for another 15 minutes.
  7. Once set, break the bark into pieces and enjoy!

Eggnog Cheesecake

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Bake Time: 50 minutes
Chill Time: 4 hours
Total Time: 5 hours 10 minutes
Servings: 10 slices

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C) and grease a 9-inch springform pan.
  2. In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press into the bottom of the pan.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Add the eggnog, flour, vanilla extract, and nutmeg, and mix until combined.
  5. Pour the batter over the crust and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 50 minutes, then turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour.
  7. Chill the cheesecake in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours before serving.

These festive recipes are guaranteed to add a touch of sweetness to your holiday celebrations. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these treats are perfect for sharing with family and friends. Get into the holiday spirit and spread joy with these delicious delights! Happy baking!
