Pumpkin Pie Cupcakes: Thanksgiving's Favorite in Mini Size


If you’ve ever been in love with the traditional taste of pumpkin pie, but wished for a more bite-sized and manageable approach to dessert at your Thanksgiving table, then Pumpkin Pie Cupcakes are the perfect solution for you! These mini marvels combine the richness of pumpkin pie with the convenience of cupcakes, resulting in a delicious explosion of fall-favorite flavors that the whole family can enjoy. This post will guide you through some lovely tips, tricks, and ingredients to ensure you create the best batch of these scrumptious Pumpkin Pie Cupcakes for your next Thanksgiving celebration.

Ingredients

Before you dive into the step-by-step process, gather these essential ingredients from your pantry and consider picking up some fresh produce and garnishes from your local farmers’ market.

  • 1 and ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 and ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ¼ teaspoon fine salt
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • ¾ cup canned pumpkin puree
  • ¼ cup whole milk
  • ½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 (16 oz) can cream cheese frosting

Instructions

Now, gather your tools and utensils, sift the flour, measure the ingredients accurately, preheat your oven, and let’s get baking!

  1. Preheat and Prep

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (177°C) and lining a 12-cup muffin tin with cupcake liners. Prepare yourself for a delightful afternoon of baking!

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together 1 and ¼ cups all-purpose flour, 1 and ½ teaspoons baking powder, ½ teaspoon baking soda, ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon, ½ teaspoon ground ginger, ¼ teaspoon ground cloves, and ¼ teaspoon fine salt. Set the mixture aside to allow the dry ingredients to “speak” to each other.

  1. Cream Butter and Sugar

In a separate, larger mixing bowl, beat ¾ cup granulated sugar, and ½ cup unsalted butter until light and fluffy, using a hand mixer or a stand mixer equipped with the paddle attachment. Creaming the butter and sugar together is crucial to creating a tender crumb, so don’t rush or skimp on this step.

  1. Beat in Eggs

Gradually add in 2 large, room-temperature eggs, one at a time, ensuring each egg is well incorporated before adding the next. This step will add flavor and moisture to your cupcakes.

  1. Combine Wet Ingredients

In a small bowl or measuring cup, whisk together ¾ cup canned pumpkin puree, ¼ cup whole milk, and ½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract. This mixture of wet ingredients will achieve a smooth and evenly distributed batter.

  1. Combine All Ingredients

Gently fold in the dry and wet mixtures alternately into the butter and sugar mixture, starting and ending with the flour mixture, until everything is well-combined and smooth. Be careful not to overmix the batter, as this may cause your cupcakes to become dense.

  1. Fill the Cupcake Liners

Using an ice cream scoop or a spoon, distribute the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 of the way full. The liners should be quite full, but you don’t want to overfill them – this can cause the batter to overflow during baking.

  1. Bake the Cupcakes

Place the muffin tin in the oven and bake the cupcakes for about 18 to 20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Baking times may vary slightly, so keep a close eye on your cupcakes – you’ll know they’re done when the edges appear golden and skillet-firm.

  1. Let Rest and Cool

Remove the cupcakes from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for about 5 minutes. Afterward, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling the cupcakes before frosting will make it easier to frost without breaking the delicate crumb.

  1. Frost the Cupcakes

Once cooled, spread a generous swirl of cream cheese frosting onto the top of each cupcake using a spatula or an icing bag with a piping tip. You can either pipe the frosting in a swirl pattern or spread it evenly with a spatula for a more simple look.

  1. Adorn with Garnishes

To add flair and extra pumpkin spice flavor, garnish your cupcakes with a dash of cinnamon, a dusting of nutmeg, or even a dollop of whipped cream to make them even more delectable.

Tasty twists and substitutions

Fear not, experimenters and improvisers! The world of pumpkin spice blends is vast and the sky’s the limit when it comes to creating your very own variations of this delightful dessert. From adding chopped pecans or shredded coconut to the batter to substituting the cream cheese frosting with a full-bodied caramel or a sticky maple syrup glaze, the personal touch of customization is up to you.

Freeze and Serve

For those who would like to prepare ahead – don’t hesitate to enjoy your Pumpkin Pie Cupcakes at a later time! Simply store cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or wrap them individually in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. When ready to serve, remove from the freezer and let them thaw at room temperature for a few hours.

So, why not transform your Thanksgiving this year into a more charming and intimate event with your guests by serving these delightful Pumpkin Pie Cupcakes? From their delightful aroma to the last bite, these scrumptious mini treats are sure to steal the show, and your Thanksgiving celebration will be remembered as one for the books. Enjoy your baking adventures and happy Thanksgiving!
