Irresistible Chocolate Babka: A Decadent Sweet Bread


If you have a weakness for chocolate and a love for freshly baked bread, then this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. Chocolate babka is a sweet, twisted bread that originated in Eastern Europe and has become a beloved treat around the world. With its rich, chocolatey filling and buttery, golden dough, it’s hard to resist the temptation of a freshly baked slice.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup whole milk, warmed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

For the chocolate filling:

  • 1 cup dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the streusel topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Mix well to ensure the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warmed milk, melted butter, and eggs. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

  4. While the dough is rising, prepare the chocolate filling. In a heatproof bowl set over a pan of simmering water, melt the chocolate and butter together, stirring until smooth. Remove from heat and whisk in the powdered sugar, cocoa powder, and ground cinnamon until well combined. Set aside.

  5. Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down to release the air. Transfer it to a floured surface and roll it out into a large rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.

  6. Spread the chocolate filling evenly over the rolled-out dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Starting from one long side, tightly roll up the dough into a log.

  7. Using a sharp knife, carefully slice the log in half lengthwise, exposing the layers of chocolate filling. Gently twist the two halves together, keeping the cut side facing up.

  8. Transfer the twisted dough to a greased loaf pan, tucking the ends under to fit. Cover with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise for another 30 minutes.

  9. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While the babka is rising, prepare the streusel topping by combining the flour, sugar, and chilled butter in a bowl. Use your fingers to mix until crumbly.

  10. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the risen babka, ensuring it covers the entire surface. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  11. Remove the babka from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Enjoy the Decadence

Indulge in the irresistible combination of chocolate and bread with this decadent chocolate babka recipe. Whether enjoyed as a breakfast treat or a dessert, the rich flavors and beautiful twisted shape will leave you wanting more. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a baking adventure that will surely impress your friends and family. Happy baking!
