Home-Cooked Comfort: Classic American Pancakes (with Secret Tips)


There’s something amazingly satisfying about waking up to the smell of homemade pancakes wafting through the house. The warmth, fluffiness, and taste of freshly-made American pancakes never fail to bring a smile to our faces. In this guide, we’ll share some secret tips to make sure your pancakes come out perfect every time. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

For the pancake batter, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 3 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • Oil or cooking spray (for cooking)

Secret Tip #1: Use Self-Rising Flour

Instead of mixing and measuring separate leavening agents, using self-rising flour simplifies the process, while still providing the perfect rise to your pancakes. It contains baking soda and salt, so you only need to add one ingredient – the sugar.

Secret Tip #2: Cream the Butter and Sugar

Whisking melted butter with sugar before adding other ingredients helps create a tender and light pancake. The sugar adds sweetness, while the creamed butter introduces air into the batter, resulting in a delicate texture.

Secret Tip #3: Use Room Temperature Ingredients

Ensure your milk and eggs are at room temperature before whisking them into the batter. This helps create a smoother and more uniform batter, resulting in evenly-cooked pancakes.

The Pancake Process

First, in a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the melted butter and sugar mixture, room temperature milk, and eggs. Gently whisk the ingredients until everything is just combined, and no more dry streaks of flour remain. Overmixing can toughen the pancakes; so, take care not to overwork your batter.

Secret Tip #4: Rest the Batter

Let your pancake batter rest for at least 30 minutes before cooking. This allows the flour to fully absorb the liquid ingredients, leading to a more tender pancake. Since the batter will thicken as it sits, add a little extra milk if the consistency is too thick after resting.

Cooking the Pancakes

Heat your skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of oil or coat with cooking spray. Using an ice cream scoop or 1/4 cup, pour the batter onto the prepared surface. Secret Tip #5: Don’t Crowd the Pan

Leave some space between each pancake to prevent them from sticking together. After about 2 minutes, or when the edges start to look set, flip the pancake and cook for another minute, until golden brown. Keep your cooked pancakes warm in a low-temperature oven while you finish cooking the rest.

Serving Your Classic American Pancakes

Now that your pancakes are ready, feel free to get creative with the toppings. Fresh fruit, whipped cream, and pure maple syrup are always delicious options. But, really, the possibilities are unlimited. Happy cooking and enjoy your perfect, home-cooked American pancakes!
