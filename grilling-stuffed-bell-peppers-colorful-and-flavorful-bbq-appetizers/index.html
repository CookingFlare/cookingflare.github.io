Grilling Stuffed Bell Peppers: Colorful and Flavorful BBQ Appetizers


If you’re looking for a vibrant and delicious appetizer to impress your guests at your next BBQ, look no further than grilled stuffed bell peppers. These colorful and flavorful treats are not only visually appealing but also packed with a medley of mouthwatering ingredients that will leave everyone wanting more. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create these delectable appetizers that are sure to be a hit at any gathering.

Ingredients

  • 6 bell peppers (choose a variety of colors for a visually stunning presentation)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend of your choice)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. Start by preparing the bell peppers. Cut off the tops and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside. Place the peppers upright on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil.

  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  4. Stir in the cooked rice, tomato sauce, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the flavors.

  5. Fill each bell pepper with the ground beef and rice mixture, packing it in tightly. Top each pepper with a generous amount of shredded cheese.

  6. Carefully transfer the stuffed bell peppers onto the preheated grill. Close the lid and cook for approximately 15-20 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Remove the stuffed bell peppers from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to handle.

  8. Serve the grilled stuffed bell peppers as a colorful and flavorful appetizer at your BBQ gathering. They pair perfectly with a refreshing summer salad or as a side dish to complement your main course.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different fillings to cater to different dietary preferences. You can substitute the ground beef with ground turkey, chicken, or even a vegetarian alternative like black beans or quinoa.

  • For an extra kick of flavor, add some chopped jalapeños or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes to the filling mixture.

  • If you prefer a smoky aroma, try grilling the bell peppers for a few minutes before stuffing them. This will add an additional layer of charred flavor to the dish.

  • To make this recipe ahead of time, you can prepare the filling mixture and stuff the peppers in advance. Keep them refrigerated until you’re ready to grill. This will save you time and allow you to focus on other aspects of your BBQ gathering.

So there you have it – a simple yet impressive recipe for grilling stuffed bell peppers. These colorful and flavorful BBQ appetizers are sure to be a hit at your next gathering. From the juicy ground beef to the melted cheese and the vibrant bell peppers, each bite is a burst of deliciousness. So fire up your grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to wow your guests with this delightful dish. Happy grilling!
