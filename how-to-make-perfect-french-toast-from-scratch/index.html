How to Make Perfect French Toast from Scratch


French toast is one of those classic dishes that transcends breakfast traditions and makes for a delicious morning treat or even a weekend brunch indulgence. If you’ve ever wondered how to make French toast that is golden-brown, delicately crispy on the outside, and irresistibly custardy on the inside, you’ve come to the right place. In this guide, we’ll take you step-by-step through the process of creating perfect French toast entirely from scratch. With a few pantry staples, thoughtful technique, and some extra flair, your homemade French toast will rival even the fanciest café-style servings.

What Makes French Toast Truly Perfect?

The key to making exceptional French toast lies in achieving the right balance of texture, flavor, and technique. The bread should soak up just the right amount of custard mixture without becoming soggy and should fry to a perfect golden color with a slight crunch. The combination of spices and ingredients in the batter should highlight the dish’s subtle sweetness and elevate its aroma.

Choosing the Right Bread

Before diving into the recipe, let’s talk about the foundation of French toast: the bread. While technically any bread may work, certain types can take your French toast to a whole new level.

  • Brioche: Brioche is a soft, rich bread with a slight sweetness, making it the ideal choice for French toast. Its fluffy texture absorbs the custard mixture beautifully, while its buttery flavor complements the dish perfectly.
  • Challah: Similar to brioche, challah has a tender crumb that soaks up the custard without falling apart. Its slightly denser structure makes it easier to handle during cooking.
  • Sourdough: For those who love complexity in flavor, sourdough delivers a tangy note that pairs well with the sweetness of the French toast batter.
  • French Baguette: A day-old baguette can also work wonders for French toast. Its firm texture prevents sogginess while still absorbing plenty of custard.

Opt for bread that’s slightly stale (1-2 days old), as it’ll have a firmer texture that holds up well in the liquid mixture.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s a detailed breakdown of the ingredients needed to prepare perfect French toast:

For the French Toast:

  • 6 slices of thick-cut bread (brioche or challah are highly recommended)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk (or substitute with half-and-half for extra richness)
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional, but adds an extra velvety texture to the custard)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon (freshly ground is ideal)
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional)
  • Unsalted butter for cooking

Optional Garnishes:

  • Maple syrup
  • Powdered sugar
  • Fresh fruit (e.g., strawberries, blueberries, or sliced bananas)
  • Whipped cream

Feel free to tailor the garnishes based on your preferences or the occasion.

Preparing the Custard

The heart of every perfect French toast recipe is the custard mixture. The goal is to create a smooth, aromatic, and flavorful base that thoroughly infuses into the bread slices.

  1. Start with Eggs: Crack the eggs into a large mixing bowl and whisk them until the yolks and whites are fully combined. The eggs give structure to the French toast and help achieve the golden crust while cooking.
  2. Add Dairy: Gradually whisk in milk and heavy cream. The milk-and-cream combination makes for a rich custard that enhances the toast’s flavor. For lighter French toast, you can use skim milk, but for optimum indulgence, stick to whole milk or half-and-half.
  3. Sweeten It: Sprinkle in the granulated sugar and whisk until it dissolves into the mixture.
  4. Flavor Boost: Stir in vanilla extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg. These aromatics add warmth and depth to the custard, turning it into a decadent base for your French toast.

Transfer the custard into a shallow dish (such as a pie pan) so that it’s easier to dip the bread evenly.

Soaking the Bread

This step is critical for achieving the perfect French toast texture. You want the bread to soak up enough custard to be moist and flavorful inside, but not so much that it becomes soggy and falls apart during cooking.

  1. Dip each slice of bread into the custard, one side at a time.
  2. Let the bread sit in the custard for about 15-20 seconds per side to ensure it absorbs the liquid thoroughly. For thicker bread like brioche or challah, feel free to soak each side for an extra 5-10 seconds.
  3. Lift the bread gently to allow any excess custard to drip off before transferring it to the skillet.

Cooking Your French Toast

The cooking stage is where the magic happens. To achieve that crisp exterior and creamy interior, pay close attention to heat control and timing.

  1. Preheat Your Skillet: Place a nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Allow it to warm up for a minute before adding the butter.
  2. Add Butter: Melt about 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter in the skillet. Swirl the butter around so that it evenly coats the surface.
  3. Cook the Bread: Place a slice of bread onto the skillet, making sure it lays flat. Depending on the size of your pan, you can cook 2-3 pieces at a time without overcrowding.
  4. Frying Time: Cook each slice for about 2-3 minutes on one side, or until golden brown and slightly crisp. Flip the bread gently with a spatula and repeat on the other side. Adjust the heat if necessary to prevent burning.
  5. Transfer and Keep Warm: Once cooked, transfer the French toast slices onto a plate lined with a paper towel or directly to a serving plate. If you’re making a larger batch, keep the finished slices warm in a 200°F (93°C) oven until ready to serve.

Tips for Making the Best French Toast

  • Don’t Overcrowd Your Pan: Cook the slices in small batches to ensure even heat distribution and avoid steaming the bread instead of frying it.
  • Get Creative with Mix-ins: If you enjoy experimenting, try adding orange zest, almond extract, or a splash of coffee to the custard mixture for a unique twist.
  • Serve Immediately: French toast tastes best when it’s hot and fresh off the pan.

Serving and Plating

Serve your French toast slices warm, accompanied by your favorite toppings. For a classic presentation, drizzle maple syrup generously and dust the slices with powdered sugar. Add a medley of fresh fruit for brightness and color, or top with whipped cream for extra indulgence. If preparing for a special occasion, decorative plating can add a touch of elegance.

Storing Leftovers

If you have leftovers, don’t worry—French toast can be stored and reheated for later enjoyment. Wrap each slice securely in plastic wrap or place them in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 3 days. When reheating, use a toaster oven or skillet to restore their crispness rather than microwaving them, which can cause them to become rubbery.

Why Homemade French Toast is Worth It

Crafting French toast from scratch is more than just cooking—it’s an experience. The process of whisking together fragrant ingredients, dipping slices of bread, and watching them sizzle and transform in the skillet is deeply satisfying. Moreover, homemade French toast puts you in control of the flavors, textures, and overall quality of the dish. Whether you’re treating yourself to a hearty breakfast or hosting loved ones for brunch, this simple yet elegant dish is sure to leave an impression.

Perfect French toast is achievable with the right ingredients, careful preparation, and a bit of patience. Now that you know the secrets to mastering this timeless recipe, it’s time to don your apron and put your culinary skills to the test.
