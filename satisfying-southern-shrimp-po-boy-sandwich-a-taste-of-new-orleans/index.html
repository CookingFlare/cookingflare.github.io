Satisfying Southern Shrimp Po' Boy Sandwich: A Taste of New Orleans


If you’re looking for a sandwich that packs a punch of flavor and transports you to the vibrant streets of New Orleans, look no further than the Satisfying Southern Shrimp Po’ Boy Sandwich. This iconic dish is a true taste of the South, featuring succulent shrimp, a crispy breaded coating, and a mouthwatering combination of flavors that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients

For the shrimp:

  • 1 pound fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the sandwich:

  • French bread or baguette, sliced lengthwise
  • Remoulade sauce (recipe below)
  • Lettuce, tomato, and pickles for garnish

For the remoulade sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sweet pickle relish
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the shrimp and buttermilk. Allow the shrimp to marinate in the buttermilk for at least 30 minutes. This will help tenderize the shrimp and infuse it with flavor.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. This will be the coating for the shrimp.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C).

  4. Remove the shrimp from the buttermilk, allowing any excess liquid to drip off. Dip each shrimp into the flour mixture, ensuring it is evenly coated. Shake off any excess flour before carefully placing the shrimp into the hot oil.

  5. Fry the shrimp in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the skillet. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy. Remove the shrimp from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  6. To make the remoulade sauce, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, hot sauce, sweet pickle relish, lemon juice, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

  7. Slice the French bread or baguette lengthwise and spread a generous amount of remoulade sauce on both sides. Layer lettuce, tomato, pickles, and the crispy shrimp onto the bread.

  8. Slice the sandwich into individual portions and serve immediately. The Satisfying Southern Shrimp Po’ Boy Sandwich is best enjoyed fresh, with its crispy shrimp, tangy remoulade sauce, and refreshing toppings coming together in perfect harmony.

Conclusion

Indulge in the flavors of New Orleans with the Satisfying Southern Shrimp Po’ Boy Sandwich. This iconic dish is a true crowd-pleaser, with its crispy and flavorful shrimp, creamy remoulade sauce, and classic sandwich fixings. Whether you’re hosting a summer cookout or simply craving a taste of the South, this recipe is sure to satisfy. So roll up your sleeves, grab some fresh shrimp, and get ready to experience the bold and delicious flavors of the Big Easy right at home.
