Buttermilk Honey Wheat Bread Recipe


Looking to whip up some delectable homemade bread that’ll make your kitchen smell divine and satisfy your cravings? Look no further! This buttermilk honey wheat bread recipe is an excellent choice. This sumptuous loaf showcases the versatility of wheat flour and adds a delightful twist to a classic bread recipe. Read on to learn more about this scrumptious dish and treat your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 2 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tablespoon Vital Wheat Gluten (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Additional honey for brushing

Instructions

Begin by dissolving the honey in the warm water and sprinkling the yeast on top. Allow the mixture to sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes foamy. This step will activate the yeast, ensuring your bread rises nicely.

Next, combine both the bread flour and whole wheat flour in a large mixing bowl. Add the vital wheat gluten, 2 teaspoons of salt, and mix well. You can omit the vital wheat gluten if you have none on hand.

Create a well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the honey-infused water mixture, the beaten egg, buttermilk, melted butter, and vegetable oil. Mix everything until thoroughly combined and a cohesive dough forms. If the dough seems too sticky, add more flour, little by little, until you achieve a manageable consistency.

Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise for approximately 1.5 to 2 hours in a warm area. The dough should double in size during this time.

After the dough has risen, punch it down and shape it into a loaf. If you prefer larger loaves, divide the dough into two equal portions and place each into greased loaf pans. Alternatively, you can use smaller loaf pans or simply create rolls. Allow the shaped bread to rise once more, covered, in a warm place for about 45 minutes.

Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). To adhere to proper kitchen hygiene, wash out the very best covered dish or baking dish that fits your loaf pan or loaf pans. Brush the dish with additional honey for an added touch of sweetness.

Once the bread has finished its second rise, remove the covering and place it in your preheated oven. Bake for approximately 35 to 40 minutes, or until golden brown and the center of the bread registers around 190°F (88°C) on an instant-read thermometer.

Remove your bread from the oven, and let it cool in the pan for about 5 minutes. After that, take it out of the pan, and set it onto a wire rack to cool completely. Delight in slicing your freshly baked buttermilk honey wheat bread and enjoying it with your favorite spread, soup, or sandwich.

This delicious homemade bread serves as an excellent source of fiber and offers an extraordinary texture that bread enthusiasts will appreciate. Not only is this recipe hearty, but it’s also adaptable to suit different dietary preferences. You can replace the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour completely to make a truly 100% wholesome bread. Furthermore, substituting the buttermilk with a non-dairy alternative, such as unsweetened almond milk, will cater to lactose-intolerant individuals.

Let this buttermilk honey wheat bread be the shining star of your next meal or family gathering! Share with friends and family, and don’t forget to save some for yourself – after all, the baker deserves a slice too!
