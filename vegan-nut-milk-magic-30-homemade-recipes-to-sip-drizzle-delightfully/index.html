Vegan Nut Milk Magic: 30 Homemade Recipes to Sip & Drizzle Delightfully


Are you ready to embark on a delicious and creamy adventure into the world of vegan nut milks? Look no further because today we’re diving deep into 30 incredible homemade recipes that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wondering why you ever relied on store-bought options. So, put on your apron, grab your favorite nuts, and let’s get blending!

  1. Almond Milk

Almonds are a classic choice when it comes to vegan milks, and for good reason. Their smooth, slightly sweet, and buttery flavor makes them a versatile addition to any drink or recipe. To make almond milk, simply blend one cup of almonds with four cups of fresh water, strain, and store in the fridge. The results are a creamy and dreamy milk that you can flavor with vanilla, cinnamon, or even a touch of honey for an extra touch of sweetness.

  1. Cashew Milk

Crafting cashew milk is a breeze. Combine one cup of raw cashews with four cups of water, whirl in a blender, and strain through a cheesecloth or nut milk bag. This versatile milk delivers a rich, smooth texture and a neutral flavor that makes it perfect for sweet and savory dishes alike. You can customize it with flavoring ingredients like cinnamon, nutmeg, or cocoa powder, for a unique twist.

  1. Walnut Milk

There’s something entirely satisfying about the nutty taste of walnut milk that truly distinguishes it from other nut milks. To create this rich, hearty milk, blend together one cup of walnuts with four cups of water and enjoy its delicious earthy taste in smoothies, coffee, or drizzled over baked goods.

  1. Hazelnut Milk

If you’re looking for a milk that imparts a bold and assertive taste, hazelnut milk is your go-to choice. Mix one cup of hazelnuts with four cups of water in a blender, and then strain the mixture to separate the pulp. The result: a full-bodied beverage that pairs majestically with coffee or hot chocolate.

  1. Pistachio Milk

With its exotic and slightly sweet flavor, pistachio milk really stands out from the crowd. Blend together one cup of shelled pistachios with four cups of water, pour the mixture through a strainer, and you’ll attain a milk that is ideal for making lattes, milkshakes, or even creamy desserts.

  1. Macadamia Milk

Macadamia nut milk provides a luxuriously creamy texture that can be an excellent ingredient in smoothies or a delightful beverage on its own. Blend one cup of macadamia nuts with four cups of water in a blender, strain the mixture using cheesecloth, and you’ll discover a unique and indulgent milk that’s perfect for desserts or turmeric lattes.

  1. Pecan Milk

Pecan milk offers a mellow richness that is both comforting and soothing. To make it, mix one cup of pecans with four cups of water and then strain with a cheesecloth. Let your creativity run wild by using it in recipes or simply enjoying it on its own.

  1. Brazil Nut Milk

Brazil nut milk brings an unconventional twist to your beverage collection. With their buttery and slightly sweet flavor, blending one cup of raw Brazil nuts with four cups of water creates a nutritious and delicious milk to enhance your favorite cocktails, lattes, or even a protein-packed smoothie.

  1. Pine Nut Milk

The subtle essence of pine nuts creates lighter-than-air milk with a boosted aroma and taste. Blend together one cup of pine nuts with four cups of water, and strain to achieve a delicate, fresh-tasting milk that’s perfect for salad dressings, various sauces, and smoothies.

  1. Hemp Milk

There’s more to hemp milk than its nutritious content. With a somewhat nutty flavor, blend one cup of hemp seeds with four cups of water to create a drumroll-worthy vegan milk. Rich in omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, hemp milk is versatile and can be used in place of dairy milk in a wide array of recipes.

  1. Coconut Milk

Coconut milk-turned-vegan is a serene and satisfying addition to any recipe. Blend together one cup of unsweetened shredded coconut with four cups of water, strain the mixture, and enjoy its tropical taste. This thicker milk is perfect for curries, smoothies, lattes, or simply sipping on its own.

  1. Cherrywood Chai Spice Almond Milk

For all you chai aficionados out there, this almond milk recipe is a dream come true. Start with almond milk as the base and then blend in two tablespoons of cherrywood-smoked salt, a half teaspoon of ground cardamom, and a quarter teaspoon of each of the following: ground black pepper, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg. Embrace the warmth of this versatile concoction by using it in your favorite lattes or hot chocolates.

  1. Vanilla Cinnamon Macadamia Milk

This milk takes macadamia’s creamy texture and adds a touch of sweetness and warmth. Begin with macadamia milk and stir in two tablespoons of*powdered vanilla bean and a half teaspoon of ground cinnamon. Your taste buds will dance joyfully with the combination of flavors.

  1. Mint Mocha Nut Milk

When you want something cool and invigorating with a hint of chocolate, this is the milk for you. Start with a nut milk base of your choice and blend in two tablespoons of cocoa powder, a teaspoon of peppermint extract, and a pinch of vanilla powder. You’ll have a revitalizing brew that’s perfect for those early mornings or midafternoon slump moments.

  1. Nutella Cashew Milk

Indulge in a delicious treat with this creamy and indulgent Nutella-inspired cashew milk. Using cashew milk as the base, add a tablespoon of raw cacao powder, a tablespoon of almond butter, a teaspoon of vanilla extract, and a quarter cup of dark brown sugar. For a truly decadent experience, use this milk to make hot cocoa or pour over your favorite dessert.

  1. Matcha Matcha Walnut Milk

Relish the flavors of fresh green tea with this vibrant matcha-infused walnut milk. Begin with classic walnut milk and blend in a tablespoon of high-quality matcha powder. This nourishing and soothing drink is ideal for starting your day or unwinding at the end of it.

  1. Chocolate Hazelnut Milk

A classic combination rediscovered with a twist, chocolate hazelnut milk delivers both rich flavors in every glass. Combine hazelnut milk with two tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder for a creamy, milky, and indulgent beverage.

  1. Banana Sesame Milk

For those who crave a hint of natural sweetness and a savory sheen, banana sesame milk is the one. Mix sesame seeds with water, add a ripe banana, and blend until smooth. Embrace the fusion of mild sweetness and savory nuttiness in a glass.

  1. Cucumber Lime Hemp Milk

Revitalize your senses with this refreshing cucumber and lime-infused hemp milk. Blend equal parts cucumber and lime juice, hemp seeds, and water until smooth. This invigorating brew is an exquisite choice for hydrating on a hot day or refreshing your palate.

  1. Turmeric Ginger Sunflower Milk

Give your immune system the boost it deserves with this turmeric ginger sunflower milk recipe. Start with sunflower seeds and add a tablespoon of ground turmeric, a half-inch knob of ginger, and a cup of fresh water. The anti-inflammatory and medicinal properties work wonders in a glass.

  1. Strawberry Banana Peanut Milk

After a fruity yet grounded flavor profile? This strawberry banana peanut milk combines the fruity flavors of strawberries and bananas with the earthiness of peanuts. Start with peanut milk as the base, blend in ripe bananas and strawberries for a blend of textures and flavors.

  1. Lavender Almond Milk

Breathe in the scent of romance with this floral yet delicate almond milk beverage. Add two tablespoons of dried edible lavender buds to your almond milk blend, giving it a dreamy aroma. Spoon this one into a warm cup and get ready for a unique and memorable experience.

  1. Spiced Pumpkin Walnut Milk

Embrace the fall and winter months with this heartwarming spiced pumpkin walnut milk. Blend pumpkin puree with spiced and ground cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and clove, and walnuts. This milk carries the essence of the season and is a perfect ingredient in your favorite baked goods or lattes.

  1. Pineapple Chia Seed Milk

A tropical twist to your usual milk, pineapple chia seed milk is a delight. Combine chia seeds, water, and pineapples, for that tropical taste experience - and to top it all off, chia seeds are packed with omega-3s and fiber!

  1. Classic Vanilla Coconut Milk

It’s time to know what real vanilla coconut milk tastes like. Simmer together one cup of shredded coconut with two cups of water and a vanilla pod. Strain and voila! Your classic vanilla coconut milk.

  1. Moringa Peanuts Milk

Keen on trying nutrient-dense milks? This moringa-peanut milk is a superfood powerhouse. Blend together peanuts, water, and moringa powder for a protein and nutrient-rich beverage that holds a savory-peanutty flavor.

  1. Maple Oat Milk

Bring that warm, comforting feeling of fall to your glass with maple oat milk. Combine oats, water, a touch of vanilla extract, and a spoonful of maple syrup into a luxurious, creamy milk that tastes like liquid gold.

  1. Orange Creamsicle Cashew Milk

Relive your childhood memories with this fresh and creamy orange creamsicle cashew milk. Blend orange juice, cashew milk, and a hint of vanilla for a nostalgic twist that’s light, citrusy, and opinion-changing.

  1. Green Tea Avocado Milk

Rich in healthy fats and antioxidants, green tea avocado milk is a dream. Follow the classic nut milk recipe, substituting cashews with avocados, and adding a touch of matcha. For the ultimate hydrating milk that’s thick, rich, and ever so green.

  1. Candy Cane Almond Milk

Bring on the nostalgia with this festive candy cane-infused almond milk. Add crushed candy canes or candy cane extract to your almond milk blend for a delightful jolt of minty coolness. Anthocyanin-rich and coolly refreshing.

There you have it! 30 incredibly scrumptious vegan nut milk recipes to satisfy your taste buds and fuel your creativity. So go ahead and experiment, enjoy, and most importantly, have fun!
