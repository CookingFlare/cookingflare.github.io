Budget-Friendly Minestrone Soup Recipes for a Hearty Meal


Minestrone soup is a classic Italian dish that is both comforting and nutritious. This hearty soup is made with a variety of vegetables, beans, and pasta, making it a filling and satisfying meal. The best part? It’s incredibly budget-friendly, making it a great option for families and individuals on a tight budget.

Why Minestrone Soup is a Great Option for Budget-Friendly Meals

Minestrone soup is a great option for budget-friendly meals because it’s made with affordable ingredients. You can use canned tomatoes and beans, which are often cheaper than fresh alternatives. Additionally, you can use leftover vegetables and pasta to make the soup even more budget-friendly.

Basic Minestrone Soup Recipe

To make a basic minestrone soup recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 can (14.5 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) of kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) of cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 can (6 oz) of tomato paste
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup of small pasta shapes (such as elbow macaroni or ditalini)

To make the soup, sauté the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery in a large pot until the vegetables are tender. Add the canned tomatoes, kidney beans, cannellini beans, tomato paste, vegetable broth, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes.

Variations to Make the Soup Your Own

While the basic minestrone soup recipe is delicious on its own, you can also make variations to make the soup your own. Here are a few ideas:

  • Add some heat to the soup by adding a diced jalapeño pepper or some red pepper flakes.
  • Use different types of beans, such as black beans or chickpeas, to change up the flavor and texture of the soup.
  • Add some protein to the soup by adding cooked chicken, turkey, or tofu.
  • Use different types of pasta, such as bowtie pasta or orzo, to change up the texture of the soup.
  • Add some extra vegetables, such as diced bell peppers or chopped spinach, to make the soup even more nutritious.

Tips for Making Minestrone Soup on a Budget

To make minestrone soup on a budget, here are a few tips:

  • Use canned tomatoes and beans, which are often cheaper than fresh alternatives.
  • Use leftover vegetables and pasta to make the soup even more budget-friendly.
  • Shop for ingredients in bulk to save money.
  • Use herbs and spices to add flavor to the soup, rather than expensive sauces or seasonings.
  • Make a large batch of the soup and freeze it for later, so you can enjoy it for several meals.

Conclusion

Minestrone soup is a delicious and budget-friendly meal option that is perfect for families and individuals on a tight budget. With its variety of vegetables, beans, and pasta, it’s a filling and satisfying meal that is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. By using canned tomatoes and beans, leftover vegetables and pasta, and herbs and spices to add flavor, you can make a delicious and budget-friendly minestrone soup that is sure to become a staple in your household.
