Golden Roast Turkey: A Classic Easter Dinner Option


Easter is a time for celebration, and what better way to gather with family and friends than around a beautifully roasted turkey? The golden roast turkey is a classic centerpiece for any Easter dinner, and it’s surprisingly easier to prepare than you might think. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a succulent and flavorful golden roast turkey that will impress everyone at your table.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (12-15 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 lemons, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 carrots, chopped
  • 4 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine

Instructions

Preparing the Turkey

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Remove the turkey from its packaging and pat it dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, chopped thyme, rosemary, sage, and minced garlic to create an herb butter mixture.
  4. Gently separate the skin from the turkey breast and rub half of the herb butter mixture under the skin, directly onto the meat.
  5. Rub the remaining herb butter all over the outside of the turkey.
  6. Squeeze the lemon halves over the turkey, then place the squeezed halves inside the turkey cavity.
  7. Season the turkey generously with salt and pepper.

Roasting the Turkey

  1. Place the quartered onion, chopped carrots, and celery in the bottom of a roasting pan.
  2. Pour the chicken or turkey broth and white wine into the roasting pan.
  3. Place the prepared turkey on a roasting rack and set it in the pan, on top of the vegetables.
  4. Tent the turkey loosely with aluminum foil.

Cooking the Turkey

  1. Transfer the roasting pan to the preheated oven and roast the turkey for about 3 to 3.5 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the pan juices.
  2. After 3 hours, remove the foil tent to allow the turkey to brown.
  3. Continue roasting until the internal temperature of the turkey reaches 165°F (75°C) in the thickest part of the thigh and the juices run clear when pierced with a fork.
  4. Once cooked, remove the turkey from the oven and let it rest for at least 20 minutes before carving.

Serving

Carve the golden roast turkey and arrange the slices on a platter. Serve with your favorite sides such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, and gravy. This classic Easter dinner option is sure to be a hit with your guests, and it’s a dish that will bring warmth and joy to your holiday table.

Conclusion

The golden roast turkey is a timeless symbol of celebration and togetherness, and it’s a dish that holds a special place in the hearts of many during Easter. By following this recipe, you can create a stunning centerpiece for your holiday feast that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Embrace the joy of cooking and sharing a delicious meal with your loved ones this Easter with this classic golden roast turkey recipe. Happy cooking, and happy Easter!
