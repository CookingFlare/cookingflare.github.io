Eggplant Parmesan Pasta: A Vegetarian Twist on a Classic


If you’re looking for a hearty and flavorful vegetarian dish that pays homage to the classic Eggplant Parmesan, then this Eggplant Parmesan Pasta recipe is for you. This dish takes all the delicious elements of traditional Eggplant Parmesan and combines them with al dente pasta to create a mouthwatering fusion of flavors and textures. This recipe is a great way to elevate your pasta game and impress both vegetarians and meat-eaters alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs
  • 1 pound spaghetti or your preferred pasta
  • 4 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Eggplant:

    • Place the eggplant slices in a colander and sprinkle them with salt. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out the bitterness. Rinse the eggplant slices and pat them dry with paper towels.
    • Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with breadcrumbs.
    • Dredge each eggplant slice in the flour, then dip it into the beaten eggs, and finally coat it with breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere. Place the breaded eggplant slices on a baking sheet.

  2. Fry the Eggplant:

    • In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Fry the breaded eggplant slices in batches until golden brown on both sides. Transfer the fried eggplant to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  3. Cook the Pasta:

    • Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  4. Assemble the Dish:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices over the sauce, followed by a layer of cooked pasta.
    • Spoon more marinara sauce over the pasta, then sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
    • Repeat the layers, finishing with a generous amount of cheese on top.

  5. Bake and Serve:

    • Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
    • Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a lighter version, you can bake the breaded eggplant slices instead of frying them.
  • Experiment with different types of pasta such as penne or rigatoni for a fun twist.
  • Add a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for a touch of heat.
  • Serve the Eggplant Parmesan Pasta with a side of garlic bread and a crisp green salad for a complete meal.

Eggplant Parmesan Pasta is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a comforting weeknight dinner or served at a special gathering. Its rich flavors and satisfying textures make it a standout vegetarian option that will delight even the most devoted carnivores. So, whether you’re a vegetarian looking for a new go-to recipe or a pasta lover seeking a fresh take on a classic, this Eggplant Parmesan Pasta is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy!
