Easy and Elegant Stuffed Pork Tenderloin: A Holiday Showstopper


Are you looking for a show-stopping dish to impress your guests during the holiday season? Look no further than this easy and elegant stuffed pork tenderloin recipe. With its succulent flavors and beautiful presentation, this dish is guaranteed to be a hit at any festive gathering. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 8 slices of prosciutto
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 1 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for searing

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Butterfly the pork tenderloins by making a lengthwise cut down the center of each tenderloin, without cutting all the way through. Open the tenderloins like a book, laying them flat on a cutting board.

  3. Season the inside of the tenderloins with salt and pepper.

  4. Layer the prosciutto slices over the pork tenderloins, making sure to cover the entire surface.

  5. Spread the baby spinach evenly over the prosciutto, followed by the crumbled goat cheese, dried cranberries, and chopped rosemary.

  6. Starting at one end, carefully roll up each tenderloin, securing the filling inside. Use kitchen twine to tie the rolled tenderloins at regular intervals to keep them together.

  7. Heat some olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.

  8. Sear the stuffed pork tenderloins on all sides until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  9. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast the tenderloins for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).

  10. Remove the skillet from the oven and let the tenderloins rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing.

  11. Carefully remove the kitchen twine from the tenderloins and slice them into medallions.

  12. Serve the stuffed pork tenderloin medallions on a platter, garnished with fresh rosemary sprigs.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the dish even more festive, drizzle a balsamic glaze over the sliced tenderloins before serving.

  • If you prefer a different kind of cheese, feel free to substitute the goat cheese with feta, blue cheese, or even brie.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding toasted pecans or walnuts to the filling mixture.

  • To save time on the day of your gathering, you can prepare the stuffed pork tenderloins in advance and refrigerate them until ready to cook.

With its tender and juicy pork, savory prosciutto, creamy goat cheese, and sweet cranberries, this easy and elegant stuffed pork tenderloin is sure to become a holiday favorite. Its impressive presentation will make your guests think you spent hours in the kitchen, while in reality, it’s a simple and straightforward recipe. So go ahead and wow your loved ones with this showstopper dish this holiday season!

Note: This recipe serves approximately 4-6 people, depending on portion sizes. Adjust the quantities accordingly to accommodate your guest list.
