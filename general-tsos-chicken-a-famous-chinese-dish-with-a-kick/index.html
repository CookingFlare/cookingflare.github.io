General Tso's Chicken: A Famous Chinese Dish with a Kick


General Tso’s Chicken is a beloved Chinese dish that has gained popularity around the world for its flavorful combination of sweet and spicy flavors. This iconic dish is often found on Chinese takeout menus, and its addictive taste and unique blend of ingredients make it a favorite among many. In this blog post, we will delve into the history of General Tso’s Chicken, explore its key ingredients, and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to recreate this delicious dish in your own kitchen.

The History of General Tso’s Chicken

General Tso’s Chicken is named after Zuo Zongtang, a Chinese military leader from the Qing Dynasty. However, contrary to popular belief, General Tso himself did not actually create this dish. In fact, the origins of General Tso’s Chicken can be traced back to the 1970s in New York City, where it was first introduced by Chinese immigrant chefs.

The dish quickly gained popularity in the United States, and its unique combination of sweet, tangy, and spicy flavors captured the hearts and taste buds of many. Over the years, General Tso’s Chicken has become a staple in Chinese-American cuisine and has even made its way back to China, where it is now enjoyed in various regions.

Key Ingredients

To make General Tso’s Chicken, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Chicken: Boneless, skinless chicken thighs are traditionally used in this dish. They offer a tender and juicy texture that pairs well with the sauce.
  • Cornstarch: Cornstarch is used to coat the chicken, giving it a crispy exterior when fried.
  • Soy Sauce: This provides a savory umami flavor to the dish.
  • Rice Vinegar: Rice vinegar adds a tangy and slightly sweet taste to balance out the richness of the sauce.
  • Hoisin Sauce: Hoisin sauce is a thick, dark sauce made from soybeans, garlic, vinegar, and various spices. It adds a depth of flavor to the dish.
  • Sugar: Sugar is used to balance the flavors and add a touch of sweetness.
  • Garlic and Ginger: These aromatic ingredients provide a fragrant base for the sauce.
  • Red Chili Pepper Flakes: For a spicy kick, red chili pepper flakes are added. Adjust the amount according to your preferred level of spiciness.

How to Make General Tso’s Chicken

Now that we know the history and key ingredients of General Tso’s Chicken, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this delicious dish:

  1. Start by cutting the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. In a bowl, combine the cornstarch and a pinch of salt. Toss the chicken pieces in the cornstarch mixture until they are well coated.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the chicken pieces in small batches and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  4. In a separate pan, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  5. Stir in the soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, sugar, and red chili pepper flakes. Allow the sauce to simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.
  6. Return the fried chicken to the pan and toss it gently in the sauce until each piece is coated evenly.
  7. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously to ensure the chicken is well coated and heated through.
  8. Once the chicken is ready, remove it from the heat and garnish with sliced green onions for a fresh and vibrant touch.
  9. Serve your homemade General Tso’s Chicken hot with steamed rice or noodles for a complete and satisfying meal.

Enjoy the Flavorful Delights of General Tso’s Chicken

General Tso’s Chicken is a dish that perfectly balances sweet, tangy, and spicy flavors, making it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine or simply looking to try something new, this iconic dish is sure to impress. By following our easy-to-follow recipe, you can recreate the magic of General Tso’s Chicken in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, put on your chef’s hat, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will leave your taste buds craving for more. Happy cooking!
