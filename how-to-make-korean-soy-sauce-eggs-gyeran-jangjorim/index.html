How to Make Korean Soy Sauce Eggs (Gyeran Jangjorim)


Korean Soy Sauce Eggs, or Gyeran Jangjorim, is a beloved traditional dish that combines the rich, savory flavors of soy sauce with the delicate texture of hard-boiled eggs. This dish, often served as a side or a banchan in Korean meals, is incredibly easy to make, yet packed with flavor. Whether you’re looking to elevate your regular meal routine or impress guests with an authentic Korean touch, this recipe is a must-try.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make Gyeran Jangjorim from scratch, discuss its origins, and dive into variations that can take this dish to new heights. Let’s get started!

What is Gyeran Jangjorim?

Gyeran Jangjorim is a Korean dish that features hard-boiled eggs simmered in a savory, slightly sweet, and umami-rich soy sauce broth. The word “Gyeran” means egg, and “Jangjorim” refers to a type of Korean braised dish. The eggs soak up the soy sauce mixture, absorbing its flavors and becoming tender, while the broth also infuses the eggs with aromatic spices and seasonings. This dish can be enjoyed as part of a Korean meal alongside rice, soup, or even as a snack.

Typically served cold or at room temperature, Gyeran Jangjorim is a popular banchan (side dish) in Korean households and restaurants. It’s a fantastic addition to any Korean meal, offering a contrast in texture and flavor, especially when paired with something spicy or crunchy.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s gather the ingredients that make this dish so flavorful. Luckily, the ingredients for Gyeran Jangjorim are easy to find and often common in most kitchens.

Essential Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs – Choose eggs that are a little older for easier peeling after boiling.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce – Opt for a high-quality soy sauce for the best flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon sugar – This adds a subtle sweetness to balance the saltiness of the soy sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil – Adds a lovely nutty flavor to the dish.
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed – Infuses the broth with aromatic depth.
  • 1 small piece of ginger (about 1-inch), sliced – Adds a hint of warmth and fragrance.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar – Helps balance out the rich, savory taste.
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional) – For a little heat.
  • 1/4 cup water – To dilute the soy sauce and create a flavorful broth.

Optional Garnishes:

  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds – Adds a nice crunch and extra flavor.
  • Chopped green onions – Adds color and a fresh, mild bite.
  • A few sprigs of cilantro – For an extra burst of freshness.

These simple ingredients come together to create a dish that’s perfect for any occasion. The beauty of Gyeran Jangjorim lies in its simplicity, allowing each element to shine through in the final dish.

How to Make Gyeran Jangjorim (Korean Soy Sauce Eggs)

Now that we have all the ingredients, let’s get cooking! Below is a step-by-step guide to making these delicious soy sauce eggs.

Step 1: Hard Boil the Eggs

Start by boiling the eggs. The key to perfect hard-boiled eggs is timing. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Place the eggs in a single layer in a medium-sized saucepan.
  2. Cover the eggs with cold water, ensuring that the water level is at least 1 inch above the eggs.
  3. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to a low simmer and cover the pan. Let the eggs cook for about 9-10 minutes for fully set yolks.
  5. After 9-10 minutes, remove the eggs from the pot and immediately place them in a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process and make peeling easier. Let them cool for about 5-10 minutes.

Peeling the eggs after they’ve cooled makes the next steps much easier, so be sure to take your time and remove the shells carefully.

Step 2: Prepare the Soy Sauce Mixture

While the eggs are cooling, it’s time to prepare the soy sauce mixture. In a small saucepan, combine the following ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 smashed garlic cloves
  • 1-inch piece of sliced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Crushed red pepper flakes (optional)

Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Stir occasionally to dissolve the sugar and allow the garlic and ginger to infuse the liquid. Let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. The broth should reduce slightly and become more aromatic.

Once the mixture has simmered, remove it from the heat and discard the garlic and ginger slices. You now have your savory, umami-packed braising liquid ready to go.

Step 3: Braise the Eggs

Once the eggs have cooled and peeled, it’s time to braise them in the soy sauce mixture. Place the peeled eggs into a small, shallow bowl or a container that is big enough to hold the eggs in a single layer. Pour the prepared soy sauce broth over the eggs, ensuring they are fully submerged.

If the eggs aren’t fully covered by the sauce, you can add a little more water or soy sauce to ensure they’re soaking in the liquid. For best results, let the eggs marinate for at least 2 hours at room temperature. However, for even better flavor, it’s recommended to refrigerate them for several hours or overnight. This allows the eggs to soak up the soy sauce and other flavors, making them even more flavorful.

Step 4: Serve and Garnish

Once the eggs have marinated to your desired level, they’re ready to serve! Arrange the eggs on a platter or in small individual bowls. Drizzle some of the soy sauce mixture over the eggs for extra flavor.

For an extra touch, garnish with toasted sesame seeds, chopped green onions, or fresh cilantro. These additions will add texture and color to the dish, making it even more appetizing.

Step 5: Enjoy Your Gyeran Jangjorim

Gyeran Jangjorim is now ready to be enjoyed! The eggs should be soft yet firm, with a rich flavor profile from the soy sauce and sesame oil. They pair beautifully with a bowl of steaming white rice, kimchi, or other banchan dishes. The soy sauce mixture can also be used as a dipping sauce for other side dishes or grilled meats.

This dish can be enjoyed cold, at room temperature, or even slightly warmed, depending on your preference.

Tips and Variations

While the basic recipe for Gyeran Jangjorim is simple and classic, there are a few variations and tips that can help you personalize the dish:

Add Some Vegetables

If you want to make the dish even heartier or add some freshness, you can add small vegetables like green beans, mushrooms, or even tiny potatoes to the braising liquid. These vegetables will absorb the soy sauce mixture and become flavorful additions to the dish.

Spice it Up

If you enjoy a bit of heat, increase the amount of crushed red pepper flakes or add some sliced fresh chili peppers to the braising liquid. The peppers will infuse the soy sauce broth with spice, giving the eggs a pleasant kick.

Make it Sweet and Savory

For a sweeter twist, you can add a bit more sugar or even some honey to the soy sauce mixture. This will balance the saltiness of the soy sauce and give the eggs a slightly sweeter, caramelized flavor.

Use Different Soy Sauces

While regular soy sauce works perfectly for this dish, you can experiment with different types of soy sauce, such as dark soy sauce or tamari (for a gluten-free version). Each variety will lend a different flavor profile, so feel free to try what you have on hand.

Marinate Longer

For a deeper, richer flavor, you can marinate the eggs for an extended period, even up to 24 hours. The longer the eggs soak in the soy sauce mixture, the more intense the flavor will be. Just be mindful of not letting them marinate too long, as the eggs may become too salty.

Why You’ll Love Gyeran Jangjorim

This dish is perfect for anyone who loves bold, umami-rich flavors with a touch of sweetness. Gyeran Jangjorim offers a simple yet deeply satisfying experience, and it can easily be adapted to suit your taste preferences. Whether you enjoy it as a side dish or a main meal, Gyeran Jangjorim is sure to become a staple in your cooking repertoire.

It’s also a versatile dish that works for meal prepping. You can prepare a batch and keep it in the fridge for several days, making it an easy and tasty addition to lunches or dinners throughout the week.

Final Thoughts

Making Korean Soy Sauce Eggs (Gyeran Jangjorim) is an easy and rewarding way to introduce Korean flavors into your kitchen. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a delicious and savory dish that’s both comforting and flavorful. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is one that everyone can enjoy.

So, grab your eggs, soy sauce, and sesame oil, and start cooking today! You’ll be amazed at how a few humble ingredients can come together to create something truly special. Happy cooking!
