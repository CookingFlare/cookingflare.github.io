Brunch Cocktails That Are Perfect for Spring Celebrations


As the season of renewal and blooming flowers unfolds, spring brings with it a delightful opportunity to gather friends and family for festive brunch celebrations. What better way to elevate your brunch than with a selection of refreshing cocktails that capture the essence of spring? The Classic Mimosa

No brunch is complete without the quintessential mimosa. This effervescent drink is a delightful combination of sparkling wine and fresh juice, typically orange juice, but feel free to get creative with other fruit juices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of chilled sparkling wine (Champagne, Prosecco, or Cava)
  • 2 cups of freshly squeezed orange juice
  • Fresh orange slices, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a champagne flute, pour in equal parts of sparkling wine and orange juice. The traditional ratio is 1:1, but you can adjust according to your taste preferences.
  2. Gently stir to combine, being careful not to lose the bubbles.
  3. Garnish with a fresh orange slice on the rim of the glass.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Variations:

  • Berry Mimosa: Substitute orange juice with a blend of strawberry and raspberry puree.
  • Tropical Mimosa: Use pineapple or mango juice for a tropical twist.

Lavender Lemonade Spritzer

Bring a touch of floral elegance to your brunch with a Lavender Lemonade Spritzer. This refreshing drink combines the sweetness of lemonade with the fragrant notes of lavender, perfect for a spring celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of dried culinary lavender
  • 2 cups of sparkling water
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices and lavender sprigs, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and dried lavender. Heat over medium until sugar is dissolved, then let it steep for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the lavender syrup into a pitcher, discarding the lavender.
  3. Add fresh lemon juice and sparkling water to the pitcher. Stir gently to combine.
  4. Serve over ice and garnish with lemon slices and lavender sprigs.

Notes:

  • Adjust the sweetness by altering the amount of sugar in the syrup.
  • For an adult version, add a splash of gin or vodka.

Strawberry Basil Bellini

Spring is the season for strawberries, and what better way to showcase their vibrant flavor than in a Strawberry Basil Bellini? This cocktail is a delightful mix of fresh fruit and herbal notes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of honey (optional)
  • 1 bottle of chilled Prosecco
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine strawberries, chopped basil, and honey. Blend until smooth.
  2. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove seeds, if desired.
  3. In a champagne flute, fill halfway with the strawberry-basil puree.
  4. Top with chilled Prosecco and gently stir to combine.
  5. Garnish with a fresh basil leaf.

Tip:

  • Use different berries or herbs for a personalized twist on this refreshing drink.

Cucumber Mint Cooler

For a light and invigorating option, the Cucumber Mint Cooler is a fantastic choice. This cocktail is not only refreshing but also incredibly hydrating, making it perfect for warm spring days.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cucumber, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 cup of fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons of agave syrup or honey
  • 1 cup of vodka or gin
  • 2 cups of soda water
  • Cucumber slices and mint sprigs, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a cocktail shaker, muddle chopped cucumber, mint leaves, and agave syrup until well combined.
  2. Add vodka or gin and shake well with ice.
  3. Strain into a glass filled with ice and top with soda water.
  4. Garnish with cucumber slices and mint sprigs.

Variations:

  • For a non-alcoholic version, simply omit the vodka or gin and enjoy a refreshing mocktail.

Citrus Sangria

Sangria is a crowd-pleaser, and this citrus version is ideal for spring celebrations. Bursting with flavor from a variety of fruits and a splash of wine, this drink is as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of white wine (Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio)
  • 1 cup of orange juice
  • 1 cup of lemon-lime soda or sparkling water
  • 1 orange, sliced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 1 lime, sliced
  • 1 cup of fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries)
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine white wine, orange juice, and lemon-lime soda.
  2. Add sliced fruits and fresh berries. Stir gently to mix.
  3. Refrigerate for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Serve over ice and garnish with fresh mint leaves.

Note:

  • Experiment with different types of fruit or even add a splash of brandy for an extra kick.

Peach Bellini

A classic Italian cocktail, the Peach Bellini is a simple yet sophisticated drink that brings the sweet taste of peaches to your brunch table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar (optional)
  • 1 bottle of chilled Prosecco
  • Fresh peach slices, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, puree the sliced peaches until smooth. If the peaches are not very sweet, add sugar to taste and blend again.
  2. Strain the peach puree through a fine mesh sieve to remove any pulp, if desired.
  3. In a champagne flute, fill halfway with the peach puree.
  4. Top with chilled Prosecco and stir gently.
  5. Garnish with fresh peach slices.

Tips:

  • Frozen peaches can be used for a chilled version of this drink.

Spicy Bloody Mary

For those who enjoy a savory cocktail, the Bloody Mary is a robust option that can be customized to suit every palate. This spicy drink is not only delicious but also known for its hangover-curing properties.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of vodka
  • 4 ounces of tomato juice
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 dashes of hot sauce (Tabasco or your choice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Celery salt (optional)
  • Garnish: celery stalk, olives, pickles, or cherry tomatoes

Instructions:

  1. In a shaker, combine vodka, tomato juice, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, salt, and pepper. Add celery salt if desired.
  2. Fill the shaker with ice and shake gently to combine.
  3. Strain into a tall glass filled with ice.
  4. Garnish with a celery stalk and any additional garnishes of your choice.

Customization:

  • Experiment with different spices and garnishes to create your own signature Bloody Mary.

Rosemary Greyhound

Elevate your cocktail game with the Rosemary Greyhound, a sophisticated twist on the classic Greyhound. The addition of fresh rosemary adds an aromatic touch that complements the grapefruit perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of gin or vodka
  • 4 ounces of fresh grapefruit juice
  • 1 sprig of fresh rosemary
  • Ice cubes
  • Grapefruit slices, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a cocktail shaker, muddle the rosemary sprig to release its oils.
  2. Add gin or vodka, grapefruit juice, and ice to the shaker.
  3. Shake well and strain into a glass filled with ice.
  4. Garnish with a grapefruit slice and a sprig of rosemary.

Note:

  • For a sweeter version, add a splash of simple syrup.

Sparkling Raspberry Lemonade

This Sparkling Raspberry Lemonade is the perfect balance of sweet and tart, making it an ideal cocktail for a sunny spring day. The addition of sparkling water gives it a refreshing fizz.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of fresh raspberries
  • 1 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup of sugar (or to taste)
  • 2 cups of sparkling water
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices and raspberries, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, puree fresh raspberries until smooth. Strain through a fine mesh sieve to remove seeds.
  2. In a pitcher, combine raspberry puree, fresh lemon juice, and sugar. Stir until sugar is dissolved.
  3. Add sparkling water and stir gently to combine.
  4. Serve over ice and garnish with lemon slices and whole raspberries.

Tip:

  • This drink can easily be made non-alcoholic by omitting any spirits.

Conclusion

Spring is a time for celebration, and brunch cocktails are the perfect way to enhance your gatherings with friends and family. Whether you prefer the classic mimosa, a refreshing cucumber mint cooler, or a robust Bloody Mary, these cocktails will surely impress your guests and add a festive touch to your brunch menu. So gather your ingredients, get creative, and enjoy the vibrant flavors of spring in every sip. Cheers to delicious cocktails and memorable moments!
