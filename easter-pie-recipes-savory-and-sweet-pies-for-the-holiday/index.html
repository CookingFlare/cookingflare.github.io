Easter Pie Recipes: Savory and Sweet Pies for the Holiday


Easter is a time of renewal, celebration, and, of course, delicious food. Among the many culinary delights that grace our tables during this holiday, pies hold a special place. Whether you prefer savory or sweet, Easter pies can elevate your holiday feast and bring a touch of tradition to your dining experience. In this post, we will explore a variety of Easter pie recipes that will surely impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. From creamy quiches to rich fruit tarts, there’s something for everyone in this flavorful collection.

The Tradition of Easter Pies

Pies have been a staple in many cultures for centuries, and Easter is no exception. In various regions, families prepare different types of pies to celebrate the holiday. In some European countries, savory pies are filled with meats, cheeses, and vegetables, while sweet pies often feature seasonal fruits and creamy fillings. The tradition of making pies for Easter symbolizes abundance, fertility, and the arrival of spring, making them the perfect dish for this joyous occasion.

Savory Easter Pies

1. Classic Quiche Lorraine

Quiche Lorraine is a French classic that combines creamy custard with crispy bacon and cheese. It’s perfect for brunch or as a light dinner option during the Easter holiday.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (9-inch)
  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove from heat and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, milk, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  4. Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and sprinkle the cooked bacon and shredded cheese evenly over the bottom.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the bacon and cheese, filling the crust.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden brown on top.
  7. Allow to cool for a few minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh chives before serving.

2. Spinach and Feta Pie

This delicious spinach and feta pie is a Greek-inspired dish that brings a burst of flavor to your Easter table. It’s perfect for vegetarians and can be served warm or at room temperature.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (thawed)
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped (or 10 oz frozen, thawed and drained)
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, cooking until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the spinach and cook until wilted (if using fresh spinach). Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the spinach mixture, feta cheese, ricotta cheese, and eggs. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Grease a pie dish with olive oil. Layer 5 sheets of phyllo dough in the dish, brushing each layer with olive oil.
  6. Pour the spinach and cheese filling into the phyllo crust. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling and brush with more olive oil.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the phyllo is golden brown and crispy. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top before serving.

3. Bacon and Egg Breakfast Pie

This hearty breakfast pie combines the classic flavors of bacon and eggs in a flaky crust, making it a perfect addition to your Easter brunch.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (9-inch)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup cooked and crumbled bacon
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Spread the crumbled bacon and cheddar cheese evenly in the pie crust.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the bacon and cheese.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the egg is set and the top is golden.
  6. Let cool briefly before garnishing with fresh parsley and slicing.

Sweet Easter Pies

1. Easter Egg Custard Pie

This delightful custard pie is a sweet treat for Easter, featuring a smooth and creamy filling reminiscent of traditional Easter eggs.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (9-inch)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Food coloring (for decoration)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until well combined.
  3. Slowly add the heavy cream and vanilla extract while whisking.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the pie crust. Sprinkle nutmeg on top.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the custard is set and the top is lightly golden.
  6. Allow to cool before decorating with food coloring to create colorful Easter egg designs.

2. Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

This classic flavor combination is perfect for spring, bringing together sweet strawberries and tart rhubarb in a delicious pie.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (9-inch)
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for dotting)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the strawberries, rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and cinnamon. Toss until well mixed.
  3. Pour the filling into the pie crust and dot with small pieces of butter.
  4. Cover with a second pie crust, sealing the edges. Cut slits in the top crust for ventilation.
  5. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is bubbling and the crust is golden brown.
  6. Let cool before serving with whipped cream.

3. Lemon Meringue Pie

Lemon meringue pie is a refreshing dessert that perfectly balances tangy lemon filling with a light and fluffy meringue topping, making it an ideal choice for Easter celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (9-inch)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 large egg yolks (beaten)
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine the sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add the water, whisking until smooth.
  3. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and bubbles.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the beaten egg yolks, lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter. Cook for an additional minute, then pour into the pre-baked pie crust.
  5. In a mixing bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar, beating until stiff peaks form.
  6. Spread the meringue over the lemon filling, sealing the edges to prevent shrinking.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown. Allow to cool before serving.

Conclusion

Easter pies, both savory and sweet, are a delightful way to celebrate the holiday and bring family and friends together. Whether you choose a classic quiche, a comforting spinach and feta pie, or indulge in a sweet strawberry rhubarb or lemon meringue pie, you’ll create lasting memories around the table. The versatility of pie recipes allows for creativity and personalization, so don’t hesitate to experiment with flavors and ingredients that resonate with your family traditions. This Easter, let the love of cooking shine through these delicious pies, making your holiday feast truly special. Enjoy the flavors of the season and happy baking!
