Mediterranean-Inspired Gnocchi: Soft and Pillowy


If you’re a fan of Mediterranean cuisine and looking for a delightful twist on traditional Italian gnocchi, then you’re in for a treat. Mediterranean-inspired gnocchi brings together the best of both worlds, combining the beloved soft and pillowy texture of gnocchi with the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean region. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create these delectable little dumplings from scratch, infusing them with the rich and aromatic flavors of the Mediterranean.

Ingredients

  • 2 large russet potatoes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh basil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Potatoes: Start by washing the potatoes and pricking them with a fork. Place them on a baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven at 400°F for about 45-60 minutes, or until they are tender. Once done, remove the potatoes from the oven and let them cool slightly.

  2. Make the Dough: Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, peel off the skins and mash them in a large bowl until smooth. Add the beaten egg, flour, crumbled feta cheese, chopped basil, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Gently mix the ingredients together until a soft dough forms.

  3. Shape the Gnocchi: Lightly flour a clean work surface and transfer the gnocchi dough onto it. Divide the dough into manageable portions and roll each portion into a long rope, about 1-inch thick. Use a sharp knife to cut the rope into bite-sized pieces. You can choose to leave the gnocchi in their classic shape or use a fork to create ridges, which will help hold the sauce.

  4. Cook the Gnocchi: Bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Carefully drop the gnocchi into the boiling water in batches, ensuring they have enough space to cook evenly. Once the gnocchi float to the surface, let them cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, then remove them with a slotted spoon and transfer them to a plate.

  5. Finish with Mediterranean Flavors: In a large pan, heat a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil over medium heat. Add the cooked gnocchi to the pan and gently toss them to coat in the oil. For an extra burst of flavor, you can add some fresh cherry tomatoes, spinach, and a sprinkle of additional feta cheese. Cook for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Plate the Mediterranean-inspired gnocchi, sprinkle with a touch of fresh basil, and serve hot. The soft and pillowy texture of the gnocchi combined with the savory notes of feta cheese, aromatic basil, and garlic will transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed shores of the Mediterranean.

Conclusion

Mediterranean-inspired gnocchi is a delightful departure from the traditional Italian version, infusing these beloved dumplings with the vibrant and robust flavors of the Mediterranean. Whether you’re looking to impress dinner guests or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean at home, this recipe is sure to delight. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the art of homemade gnocchi, and savor the flavors of the Mediterranean with every soft and pillowy bite.
