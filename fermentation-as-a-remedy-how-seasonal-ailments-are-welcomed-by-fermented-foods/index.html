Fermentation as a Remedy: How Seasonal Ailments are Welcomed by Fermented Foods


As seasons change and we expose our bodies to varying environments, our immune systems can become vulnerable to attack. Fermented foods offer a natural defense against these seasonal ailments.

Fermented foods encompass a diverse range of products such as sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, miso, and pickles, among others. These goodies harbor livingmicroorganisms that, in appropriate quantities, have the power to restore balance to your gut flora, often known as gut microbiome, which plays a pivotal role in your overall well-being.

During fermentation, sugars, fibers, and other substances are transformed into various beneficial compounds. Some of these compounds act as natural remedies for multiple ailments, often linked to seasonal changes. Let’s take a look at some examples.

1. Boosting Immunity

Fermented foods possess probiotics, the so-called ‘good’ bacteria that enhance the body’s natural defense system. By maintaining a regular intake of these probiotics, you can build resistance to illness-causing pathogens. Combating common seasonal ailments such as cold and flu has never been easier!

2. Easing Digestive Issues

Acidosis or low-grade food poisoning commonly affect us during spring and summer. Fermented foods, specifically those with sour flavors, contain enzymes breaking down proteins and promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria. These natural processes aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms.

3. Fighting Inflammation

Inflammation is a critical reaction to infections or injury but can become problematic when it persistently occurs. Fermented foods rich in vitamins and minerals have antioxidant properties filtering out free radicals and quell chronic inflammation.

4. Gaining Nutrients

As fermentation absorbs minerals, vitamins, and other essential nutrients, it concentrates these substances, making them more bioavailable for our bodies. Additionally, fermentation enables lactose-intolerant individuals to enjoy milk products!

5. Promoting Mental Well-being

Research has connected gut health to mental well-being. As fermented foods improve gut health, they can improve mood, enhance focus, and even contribute to a sense of well-being.

Easy Fermented Foods Recipes for Beginners

Experimenting with fermented foods requires just three basic ingredients: the food you want to ferment, water, and starter culture. For most of us, starting this journey with still-uncultured ingredients offers a better experience. Here are some simple recipes to create a welcoming environment for beneficial microorganisms within your kitchen:

1. Quick Fermented Cabbage

  • Prep Time: 10 mins
  • Fermentation time: 5-7 days

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of green or purple cabbage (around 2 pounds)
  • 2-3 tbsp sea salt
  • Starter culture or whey

Instructions:

  • Chop the cabbage finely and layer it with salt in a large jar or pot.
  • If using whey, pour it on top of the cabbage. Otherwise, add your chosen starter culture.
  • Keep the cabbage beneath the brine during fermentation by weighing it down with a plate.
  • Keep the jar in a cool spot, avoiding direct sunlight. Periodically check brine levels to ensure the cabbage remains submerged.

2. Yogurt Cheese

  • Prep time: 5 mins
  • Culturing time: 24 hrs

  • Ingredients:

  • 4 cups plain yogurt

  • 3-4 cups milk or non-dairy alternative

Instructions:

  • Heat milk gently, then cool to room temperature.
  • Blend yogurt with milk and strain through cheesecloth or sieve.
  • Keep yogurt cheese in a cheesecloth-lined colander, suspended over a bowl to drain excess whey.
  • Flip the cheese every few hours to achieve an even consistency.

3. Simple Fermented Carrots

  • Prep Time: 10 mins
  • Fermentation time: 2-4 weeks

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 medium carrots
  • 2 tbsp sea salt
  • Starter culture or whey

Instructions:

  • Slice or grate the carrots and layer them in a jar with salt.
  • Pour in enough water to barely cover the carrots, then add starter culture or whey.
  • Cover the carrots completely with brine and let sit for 2-4 weeks. Keep the jar in a cool, dark place.

Final Thoughts

Fermentation not only enriches our food with health-promoting compounds but encourages us to engage more intimately with our kitchen, allowing us to control our habits and diet to improve our well-being. Don’t shy away from experimenting with new recipes and concoctions. As fermentation becomes a regular part of your routine, you’ll come to understand the impact it has on your health and the world of microorganisms that make it possible.

Remember, the process of fermentation can be transformative, extending the life of your garden, transforming your pantry, and even your worldview. So, discover the wonders of fermentation and bring the probiotics to your plate today!
