Best Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp Recipe


Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp is one of the most beloved dishes from Chinese-American cuisine, combining the perfect balance of crispy shrimp, sweet and creamy sauce, and crunchy caramelized walnuts. The dish is often served at upscale Chinese restaurants, but it’s easy to recreate at home with just a few ingredients and steps. This recipe brings you the best version of this sweet and savory treat that is sure to wow your family and friends.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make the best Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp, starting from the very basics and working through each step. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is simple to follow and will leave you with a mouthwatering result. So, let’s dive into making the perfect Honey Walnut Shrimp!

What Makes Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp So Special?

Honey Walnut Shrimp is unique because of the flavor profile that blends sweet, savory, and nutty elements together in a delightful symphony. The shrimp are battered and fried to crispy perfection, providing a crunchy exterior that contrasts beautifully with the tender, juicy shrimp inside. The dish’s crowning glory is the honey sauce, which is rich, sweet, and slightly tangy, and the caramelized walnuts that add a satisfying crunch.

The sauce is typically made with mayonnaise, honey, condensed milk, and a touch of lemon juice. It creates a creamy, smooth coating for the shrimp that is irresistible. Meanwhile, the caramelized walnuts add both texture and a delicate sweetness, rounding out the dish.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need to prepare this mouthwatering dish.

For the Shrimp:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup rice flour (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Honey Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise (use full-fat for best results)
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp salt

For the Walnuts:

  • 1/2 cup walnuts, halved
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

Step-by-Step Instructions for the Perfect Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp

Step 1: Prepare the Walnuts

The key to great Honey Walnut Shrimp is the caramelized walnuts. These are what make the dish truly irresistible and set it apart from other shrimp dishes. Here’s how to prepare them:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the sugar and water over medium heat. Stir occasionally until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Once the sugar is dissolved, bring the mixture to a simmer. Add the walnuts and continue to cook for 3-4 minutes. The syrup will start to thicken, and the walnuts will begin to take on a glossy appearance.
  3. Once the syrup thickens and the walnuts are coated, remove the pan from the heat. Place the walnuts on a parchment paper-lined baking sheet to cool and harden. The walnuts will have a nice caramelized finish once they cool completely.

Step 2: Prepare the Shrimp

Next, we’ll prepare the shrimp. The shrimp should be fresh, juicy, and perfectly battered to achieve that crispy texture we all love. Follow these simple steps:

  1. Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps to achieve a crispy coating when frying.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the cornstarch, all-purpose flour, rice flour (optional), baking powder, salt, and white pepper. Stir the dry ingredients together to create a well-mixed batter.
  3. Add the beaten egg into the dry mixture and mix until a smooth batter forms. The consistency should be thick enough to coat the shrimp evenly.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. The oil should be around 350°F (175°C) for optimal frying.
  5. Dip each shrimp into the batter, ensuring it’s fully coated. Gently drop the shrimp into the hot oil and fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Don’t overcrowd the pan to ensure the shrimp fry evenly.
  6. Once the shrimp are cooked, remove them from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 3: Prepare the Honey Sauce

The honey sauce is the key to making the dish rich and flavorful. Here’s how to make it:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, honey, sweetened condensed milk, lemon juice, and salt. Whisk until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Adjust the sweetness by adding more honey if desired.
  2. Taste the sauce and add a bit more lemon juice if you like a touch of tang to balance the sweetness.

Step 4: Combine the Shrimp, Sauce, and Walnuts

Now that all the components are ready, it’s time to put them together!

  1. In a large mixing bowl, gently toss the fried shrimp with the honey sauce. Make sure each shrimp is evenly coated with the creamy, sweet sauce.
  2. Add the caramelized walnuts to the bowl and give everything a final gentle toss. The walnuts will add both crunch and sweetness, making the dish even more delicious.
  3. Serve the Honey Walnut Shrimp immediately while the shrimp is still crispy, and the walnuts are crunchy. It pairs wonderfully with steamed jasmine rice or stir-fried vegetables.

Tips for the Best Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp

  1. Use Fresh Shrimp: Fresh shrimp yield the best texture and flavor. If you’re using frozen shrimp, make sure to thaw them thoroughly and pat them dry before cooking.
  2. Get the Right Crispy Texture: For the crispiest shrimp, ensure that the batter is thick enough to coat the shrimp evenly, and avoid overcrowding the pan while frying.
  3. Caramelizing Walnuts: Keep an eye on the walnuts as they caramelize. The sugar syrup can burn quickly, so stir the mixture and remove it from the heat once it reaches the desired consistency.
  4. Adjust the Sauce to Your Taste: Feel free to tweak the honey sauce by adding more honey for sweetness or more lemon juice for a tangier finish. Some variations include adding a touch of Dijon mustard for a mild tang or a splash of soy sauce for extra depth.

Variations of Honey Walnut Shrimp

While the classic version of Honey Walnut Shrimp is always a crowd-pleaser, you can experiment with different variations. Here are a few ideas to personalize the dish:

  • Spicy Honey Walnut Shrimp: Add a little bit of chili garlic sauce or Sriracha to the honey sauce to give the dish a spicy kick. The heat from the sauce pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of the honey.
  • Honey Lemon Shrimp: For a lighter, more citrus-forward version, increase the lemon juice in the sauce. This will give the dish a refreshing, zesty flavor while maintaining its sweetness.
  • Add Vegetables: Incorporate vegetables like bell peppers, onions, or snow peas for a more balanced meal. Stir-fry the veggies in a separate pan and serve them alongside the shrimp.

Why You Should Make This Recipe

There are countless reasons why Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp has earned its spot as a favorite among Chinese-American dishes. The dish is not only delicious but also visually stunning, with the glossy sauce, golden shrimp, and glistening walnuts creating a dish that looks as good as it tastes. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just craving something sweet and savory, this dish is sure to impress.

It’s versatile enough to serve as an appetizer, main course, or even a special occasion dish. The balance of sweet and savory flavors, combined with the textural contrast between the crispy shrimp and crunchy walnuts, makes this a memorable dish that everyone will enjoy.

Conclusion

Making Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp at home may seem like a daunting task, but with this recipe, you’ll be able to achieve restaurant-quality results with ease. The combination of crispy shrimp, creamy honey sauce, and caramelized walnuts creates a flavor explosion in every bite.

This dish is perfect for impressing your guests or treating your family to something special. So gather your ingredients, follow these simple steps, and get ready to enjoy the best Chinese Honey Walnut Shrimp you’ve ever tasted! Happy cooking!
