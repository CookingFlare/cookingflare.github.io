No-Churn Mango Coconut Ice Cream Recipe


When the summer heat hits, nothing beats a refreshing scoop of ice cream. But what if you crave something creamy and tropical without the hassle of an ice cream machine? Enter the No-Churn Mango Coconut Ice Cream — a luscious, easy-to-make dessert that combines the sunny sweetness of mangoes with the creamy richness of coconut. This recipe will guide you through creating a dreamy frozen treat that’s perfect for warm days or anytime you need a tropical escape.

Why You’ll Love This No-Churn Mango Coconut Ice Cream

This ice cream recipe is a standout for several reasons:

  • No Ice Cream Maker Needed: No fancy equipment means anyone can whip up this treat.
  • Natural Ingredients: Fresh mangoes and coconut bring wholesome flavor without artificial additives.
  • Creamy Yet Dairy-Optional: Using coconut cream instead of dairy cream keeps it vegan-friendly and full of rich texture.
  • Quick and Easy: From start to freezer, it only takes about 15 minutes to prepare.
  • Perfectly Balanced Flavor: The natural sweetness of mango meets the subtle nuttiness of coconut in every bite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving in, gather the following ingredients for the best results:

  • 3 cups ripe mango chunks (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut cream (chilled overnight)
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk (use coconut condensed milk for vegan version)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional, adjust based on mango sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • Toasted shredded coconut (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step: How to Make No-Churn Mango Coconut Ice Cream

Step 1: Prepare the Mango Puree

Start by combining the mango chunks with a tablespoon of sweetened condensed milk in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth and creamy. If your mangoes are perfectly sweet, you can adjust or even skip the added sweetener here.

Step 2: Whip the Coconut Cream

Open your chilled can of coconut cream and scoop the thick cream into a large mixing bowl. Whip with an electric mixer until it becomes fluffy and forms soft peaks. It should resemble traditional whipped cream in texture.

Step 3: Combine Ingredients

Gently fold the mango puree into the whipped coconut cream. Add the remaining sweetened condensed milk (or coconut sweetener), vanilla extract, and salt. Mix carefully until the mixture is smooth and evenly colored — no streaks of white or yellow.

Step 4: Freeze the Mixture

Transfer the mixture into a loaf pan or airtight container. Smooth the surface with a spatula. Cover tightly with a lid or plastic wrap and freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight.

Step 5: Serve and Garnish

Before serving, let the ice cream sit at room temperature for about 5-10 minutes to soften slightly. Scoop into bowls or cones, and sprinkle toasted shredded coconut on top to enhance the tropical vibe.

Tips for the Best Mango Coconut Ice Cream

Creating the perfect texture and flavor involves a few tricks:

  • Chill Your Coconut Cream: Refrigerate the coconut cream overnight to separate the thick cream from the liquid. Use only the thick part for the fluffiest ice cream.
  • Ripe Mangoes Are Key: The syrupy, sweet taste of ripe mangoes is essential. Alphonso or Ataulfo mangos are excellent choices if available.
  • Adjust Sweetness to Taste: Depending on how sweet your mangoes are, tweak the amount of sweetener. Frozen mangoes sometimes need a little boost.
  • Mix Gently: Folding the puree into the whipped cream carefully preserves the airiness, resulting in smooth ice cream rather than a dense block.
  • Add Texture Variations: For extra flair, consider folding in chopped nuts, coconut flakes, or small mango chunks before freezing.

Variations and Serving Ideas

The versatility of this no-churn ice cream opens up creative options:

  • Mango Coconut Swirl: Keep some mango puree separate and swirl it into the coconut mixture to create a marbled effect.
  • Tropical Fruit Mix: Blend pineapple or passion fruit with the mango for a more complex flavor profile.
  • Chocolate Chip Delight: Stir in vegan chocolate chips or cocoa nibs for a surprise crunch.
  • Spiced Version: Add a pinch of cardamom or cinnamon to the mix for a subtle warming spice note.
  • Serving Alternatives: Try it as a topping on warm pineapple upside-down cake or alongside fresh tropical fruit salad.

How to Store Your Ice Cream

Since this ice cream is made with natural ingredients, it’s best enjoyed within one week. Keep it tightly covered in the freezer to prevent ice crystals from forming and preserve its creamy texture.

If the ice cream becomes a little too hard, allow it to soften for 10-15 minutes before scooping.

Nutritional Perspective

Compared to traditional dairy ice cream, this mango coconut treat offers a nice balance of natural sugars, healthy fats from coconut, and fiber from fruit. It’s a more wholesome and lighter indulgence, especially when you skip or lower extra sweeteners.

Final Thoughts

This No-Churn Mango Coconut Ice Cream is a delightful, tropical treat designed for ease and flavor. Its creamy texture, coupled with mango’s natural sweetness and coconut’s richness, transports every bite straight to sunny shores. Whether you’re entertaining guests or treating yourself, this recipe is sure to become a favorite go-to for homemade ice cream with minimal fuss.

So grab those mangoes and coconut cream, and give this refreshing no-churn ice cream a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
