Traditional Greek Spanakopita: A Spinach and Feta Delight


If there is one dish that truly captures the essence of Greek cuisine, it’s the classic Spanakopita. This savory pastry, filled with a delicious mixture of spinach, feta cheese, and herbs, is a staple in Greek households and a favorite at family gatherings and celebrations. The combination of flaky phyllo dough and the rich, earthy flavors of the filling make Spanakopita a truly irresistible treat. In this post, I will share with you the traditional recipe for making this delectable Greek dish, as well as some tips for achieving the perfect Spanakopita every time.

Ingredients

Before we delve into the process of making Spanakopita, let’s take a look at the ingredients you will need to bring this delightful dish to life:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed according to package instructions

Instructions

Preparing the Filling

  1. Begin by wilting the chopped spinach in a large skillet over medium heat. This can be achieved by cooking the spinach for a few minutes until it is wilted and most of the moisture has evaporated. Once wilted, transfer the spinach to a colander and press out any excess liquid.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the wilted spinach, crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, chopped onions, minced garlic, chopped dill, chopped parsley, olive oil, salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to ensure an even distribution of flavors.

Assembling the Spanakopita

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.

  2. Unroll the phyllo dough and place it on a clean, dry surface. Cover it with a slightly damp kitchen towel to prevent it from drying out.

  3. Carefully lay out one sheet of phyllo dough and brush it lightly with melted butter. Repeat this process with several more sheets of phyllo, layering them on top of each other. Be sure to brush each layer with melted butter.

  4. Once you have layered several sheets of phyllo, spread the spinach and cheese filling evenly over the top sheet, leaving a small border around the edges.

  5. Gently fold the edges of the phyllo over the filling, then carefully roll the phyllo into a log, tucking in the edges as you go.

  6. Place the assembled Spanakopita in the prepared baking dish and brush the top with any remaining melted butter.

  7. Using a sharp knife, score the top of the Spanakopita into portions.

Baking the Spanakopita

  1. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake the Spanakopita for 45-50 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crisp.

  2. Once baked, remove the Spanakopita from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

Spanakopita is best enjoyed warm, either as a main dish accompanied by a fresh Greek salad or as a delightful appetizer. This dish pairs beautifully with a glass of crisp white wine or a refreshing tzatziki sauce for dipping.

Conclusion

With its delicate layers of phyllo dough and a flavorful filling of spinach and feta, Spanakopita is a dish that perfectly encapsulates the essence of Greek cuisine. Whether you are hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this traditional Greek recipe is sure to delight your senses and transport you to the sun-drenched shores of Greece. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create your very own delicious Spanakopita. Opa!
