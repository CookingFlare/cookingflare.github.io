Tantalizing Gluten-Free Raspberry Bars: A Sweet and Tart Treat


If you’re a fan of the sweet and tangy combination of raspberries and the buttery goodness of a crumbly bar, then you’re in for a treat! These tantalizing gluten-free raspberry bars are the perfect balance of sweet and tart, making them an ideal dessert or snack for any occasion. Plus, they’re gluten-free, so everyone can enjoy them without worry. Let’s dive into the delightful world of these raspberry bars and learn how to make them from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Crust and Crumble:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 3 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust and Crumble

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Using a pastry cutter or your hands, cut in the chilled butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add the beaten egg and vanilla extract, and mix until the dough comes together.
  5. Set aside 1 1/2 cups of the mixture for the crumble topping, and press the rest into the bottom of the prepared pan.

Step 2: Make the Raspberry Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the fresh raspberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let the raspberry filling cool slightly.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Spread the raspberry filling evenly over the crust in the pan.
  2. Sprinkle the reserved crumble mixture over the raspberry filling.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.
  4. Allow the raspberry bars to cool completely in the pan before slicing into squares.

Serving and Storing

Once the tantalizing gluten-free raspberry bars have cooled, slice them into squares and serve. These bars are perfect for a sweet treat with a cup of tea or coffee, or as a delightful dessert after a meal. Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Conclusion

These gluten-free raspberry bars are a delightful combination of sweet and tart flavors, with a crumbly crust and a juicy raspberry filling. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to treat yourself to something special, these bars are sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and indulge in the joy of creating these tantalizing gluten-free raspberry bars. Enjoy!
