Satisfy Your Sudden Cravings: Quick Chinese Recipes for Late-Night Binges


Embarking on a late-night culinary adventure without compromising on taste or time is no easy feat. The temptation to reach for that bag of chips or box of cookies becomes even stronger when you’re tired and hungry. But fear not, fellow midnight snackers. We’ve got a scrumptious arsenal of delectable Chinese recipes that are quick, easy, and bound to satisfy your cravings. Perfect for those chilly nights when nothing else quite hits the spot, here’s a selection of irresistible dishes guaranteed to fix just about any sudden late-night craving you have. Here’s how to transform your kitchen into a miniature Chinese food paradise in no time.

  1. Pork and Cabbage Dumplings

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 24 dumpling wrappers
  • Water or egg wash for sealing or brushing

Directions:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, cabbage, green onions, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, white pepper, and a pinch of salt. Mix everything together well.
  • Place the dumpling wrappers one by one on a flat surface. Spoon a small amount of the pork mixture in the center of each wonton wrapper, and fold as desired (either into half-moon shapes or purse shapes with pleats).
  • Seal the edges by either moistening them with water or brushing egg wash.
  • Steam the dumplings for 10-12 minutes or cook in a frying pan over medium heat with some oil for about 3-5 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  • Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
  1. Mapo Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon dried red chilies
  • 1/2 cup fermented black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
  • 1 pound firm tofu, drained and cut into cubes
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons chili oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

Directions:

  • In a small pan, dry-roast the Sichuan peppercorns and dried red chilies for a few minutes until fragrant. Grind them into a coarse powder and set aside.
  • In a wok or large skillet, sauté the black beans, green onions, garlic, and ginger in chili oil until fragrant. Add in the ground pork and cook until it’s no longer pink.
  • Stir in the tofu, Sichuan peppercorn and chili powder mixture, soy sauce, sugar, and broth. Simmer the mixture over low heat for about 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  • Season with salt to taste and serve hot with steamy rice.
  1. Spicy Three-Cup Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1/2 cup black rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup sake or dry sherry
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 Sichuan peppercorn, crushed
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 pound Broccoli florets

Directions:

  • In a large bowl, marinate the chicken chunks with soy sauce, sake, salt, and pepper for at least 30 minutes.
  • In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Saute the garlic, ginger, and green onions with the crushed Sichuan peppercorn and star anise until fragrant.
  • Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it’s cooked through. Pour in the black rice vinegar and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  • Stir in the chicken broth, bring it to a gentle boil, reduce the heat to low, and let it simmer for five more minutes.
  • Serve the Spicy Three-Cup Chicken hot, topped with broccoli florets, and accompanied by rice.

Conclusion These tantalizing late-night Chinese treats not only deliver enormous taste satisfaction but also offer you a chance to flex your cooking muscles. Keep these quick, fun recipes in your back pocket for those late-night Chinese food desires without ever leaving the comfort of your home. Happy cooking!
