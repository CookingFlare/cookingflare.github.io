Simple Lemon Butter Seafood Pasta Recipe


Who can resist the allure of a perfectly cooked plate of seafood pasta? The delicate, briny flavors of fresh seafood paired with creamy lemon butter sauce bring life to a simple bowl of pasta, turning it into a restaurant-worthy dish. The best part? You can recreate this magic in less than an hour, all in the comfort of your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or seeking an elevated weeknight meal, this simple lemon butter seafood pasta recipe is bound to impress.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Building a flavorful seafood pasta starts with quality ingredients. This recipe makes approximately 4 servings.

For the Pasta

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine pasta
  • 1 tablespoon salt for the boiling water

For the Seafood Lemon Butter Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to preference)
  • ½ cup dry white wine (such as Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio)
  • 1 medium lemon, juiced (about 3 tablespoons)
  • Zest from 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup heavy cream (optional for a richer sauce)

Seafood Options

These can be mixed or tailored to your preference:

  • 8 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz scallops (pat dry for a better sear)
  • 4 oz clams or mussels (cleaned and scrubbed)

Fresh Garnishes

  • ¼ cup chopped parsley
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional but highly recommended)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Once boiling, add your choice of pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve ½ cup of the pasta water, then drain the rest and set the cooked pasta aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Seafood

Before tackling the sauce, prepare your seafood:

  • Shrimp: Season lightly with salt and pepper. Sauté over medium heat until they turn pink (about 2-3 minutes per side). Remove from the pan and set aside.
  • Scallops: Season with salt and sear in the same pan over high heat for 2 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  • Clams or Mussels: Add these to the pan alongside the wine and cover with a lid for about 5-7 minutes, or until all shells open. Remove any that stay closed and set the rest aside.

Step 3: Create the Lemon Butter Sauce

Once your seafood is done, it’s time to make the star of the dish—the lemon butter sauce:

  1. In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat and add olive oil.
  2. Toss in the minced garlic and crushed red pepper flakes, sautéing until fragrant (about 1 minute).
  3. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and lemon zest, letting the citrus aroma infuse. Add a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance the flavors.
  5. If you prefer a creamier sauce, add the heavy cream and let it simmer gently for another 3-4 minutes.

Step 4: Combine the Pasta and Sauce

Reduce the heat to low and return the shrimp, scallops, and shellfish to the skillet with the sauce. Toss them to coat evenly. Then, add the cooked pasta and mix everything together until the strands are well-coated. If needed, add the reserved pasta water a tablespoon at a time to loosen the sauce and help it cling to the noodles.

Final Touches

No seafood pasta dish is truly complete without the perfect finishing touches:

  1. Sprinkle chopped parsley generously over the pasta for a pop of color and freshness.
  2. Add extra lemon zest or juice for an intensified citrus kick.
  3. Serve with grated Parmesan cheese on top for added richness—though optional, it’s highly recommended.

Tips, Tricks, and Variations

Choosing the Pasta

While spaghetti or linguine is a popular choice for seafood pasta, feel free to experiment with fettuccine or angel hair pasta for a lighter texture. Fresh pasta works beautifully in this recipe, offering a delicate, absorbent base for the lemon butter sauce.

Selecting Fresh Seafood

Often, the success of any seafood recipe boils down to the freshness of your ingredients:

  • Look for shrimp with clean, firm shells and a slight oceanic aroma.
  • Scallops should have a sweet scent and feel plump.
  • For clams or mussels, ensure all shells are tightly closed before cooking. Discard any that remain open, as they may be unsafe to eat.

Wine Selection

Dry white wine adds depth to the sauce, helping balance the acidity of the lemon. If wine isn’t an option, substitute with chicken broth or vegetable stock for similar results.

Amp It Up

Want to add more dimension to your dish? Consider these variations:

  • Stir in a handful of fresh spinach or arugula while combining the pasta with the sauce for added greens.
  • Grill the seafood instead of pan-searing for a smoky twist.
  • Sprinkle chili oil or smoked paprika for a subtle heat upgrade.

Serving Suggestions

Seafood pasta is best enjoyed fresh off the stove, served in wide, shallow bowls that showcase the colorful presentation. Pair with a crusty baguette or garlic bread to soak up the luscious lemon butter sauce—because no drop should go to waste. A chilled glass of white wine or sparkling water with a slice of lemon complements the meal seamlessly.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This simple lemon butter seafood pasta strikes the perfect balance between indulgence and simplicity. The delicate seafood pairs beautifully with the tangy citrus notes of the sauce, which is rounded out by butter and cream for richness. Whether you’re feeding the family or impressing dinner guests, this recipe delivers bold flavors that echo seaside elegance.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

While this dish is best enjoyed fresh, it’s possible to store leftovers for up to 2 days in an airtight container in the refrigerator. To reheat, use a skillet over medium-low heat and add a splash of water or broth to revive the sauce without drying it out.

Final Thoughts

Seafood pasta is often regarded as a sophisticated dish reserved for special occasions or restaurant menus. However, this simple lemon butter seafood pasta recipe flips that notion on its head, proving that elegance doesn’t have to come at the cost of convenience. Armed with fresh ingredients and a handful of pantry staples, you can create a dish that’s bursting with flavor and ready to impress at your next meal. Dive into this recipe today—you won’t regret it!
