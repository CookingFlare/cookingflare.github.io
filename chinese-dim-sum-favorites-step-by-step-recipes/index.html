Chinese Dim Sum Favorites: Step-by-Step Recipes


Dim sum, the delightful array of bite-sized treats served in Chinese tea houses, is a beloved tradition that spans centuries. Whether you’re in Hong Kong or a bustling Chinese neighborhood, dim sum offers an exciting variety of flavors, textures, and fillings. The joy of dim sum is not just in its taste but also in the experience of sharing it with friends and family, often accompanied by tea.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the most popular dim sum dishes and walk you through step-by-step recipes, ensuring you can recreate these delicious bites in your own kitchen. From dumplings to buns and everything in between, we’ve got you covered. Let’s dive into the world of dim sum!

1. Shrimp Dumplings (Har Gao)

Shrimp dumplings, or Har Gao, are an essential part of any dim sum spread. These translucent dumplings are filled with plump shrimp, offering a delightful contrast between the delicate wrapper and savory filling.

Ingredients:

  • 300g fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 cup water (for wrapping dough)
  • 200g wheat starch
  • 50g tapioca starch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • A pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Shrimp Filling:

    • Chop the shrimp into small pieces. In a bowl, mix the shrimp with bamboo shoots, cornstarch, sesame oil, salt, sugar, and white pepper. Set this aside to marinate while you prepare the dough.

  2. Make the Dumpling Wrapper:

    • In a separate bowl, combine the wheat starch, tapioca starch, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add warm water to form a smooth dough. Knead for about 10 minutes until the dough is soft and elastic.
    • Divide the dough into small pieces and roll each into a ball. Flatten each ball into a round wrapper using a rolling pin.

  3. Assemble the Dumplings:

    • Place a spoonful of the shrimp filling in the center of each wrapper. Carefully fold the edges over the filling to create a half-moon shape, pinching the sides together to seal the dumpling. Repeat until all dumplings are formed.

  4. Steam the Dumplings:

    • Arrange the dumplings on a steaming tray lined with parchment paper. Steam over high heat for about 6-8 minutes, or until the shrimp are cooked through and the wrappers become translucent.

  5. Serve:

    • Serve your shrimp dumplings hot with soy sauce and chili oil for dipping.

2. Char Siu Bao (BBQ Pork Buns)

Char Siu Bao are fluffy steamed buns filled with sweet and savory barbecued pork. These buns are a dim sum favorite, loved for their light texture and rich filling.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 150ml warm water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • 300g Char Siu (Chinese BBQ pork), diced
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (mixed with 2 tablespoons water)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, yeast, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add warm water, mixing until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a bowl, combine diced Char Siu with hoisin sauce, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and cornstarch slurry. Cook over medium heat until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, about 3-4 minutes. Let it cool to room temperature.

  3. Shape the Buns:

    • Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into small portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it into a disc. Place a spoonful of filling in the center and pinch the edges together to seal. Ensure the bun is completely sealed.

  4. Steam the Buns:

    • Place the buns on a piece of parchment paper and arrange them in a steamer. Steam the buns for 15-20 minutes over high heat, until they are soft and fluffy.

  5. Serve:

    • Serve the Char Siu Bao hot. These buns are perfect with tea and make a great appetizer or snack.

3. Siu Mai (Pork and Shrimp Dumplings)

Siu Mai, or Shumai, are open-topped dumplings filled with a combination of ground pork and shrimp. Topped with a small piece of crab roe or mushroom, these dumplings are not only tasty but visually stunning.

Ingredients:

  • 200g ground pork
  • 100g shrimp, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup shiitake mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup water chestnuts, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (or dry sherry)
  • 12-16 wonton wrappers
  • Crab roe or chopped mushrooms for topping

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a bowl, combine ground pork, shrimp, shiitake mushrooms, and water chestnuts. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, and rice wine. Mix well until everything is evenly combined.

  2. Assemble the Dumplings:

    • Place a wonton wrapper in your hand and spoon a generous amount of filling in the center. Gather the edges of the wrapper around the filling, leaving the top open. Gently press the filling down so it stays in place, shaping the dumpling into a slight cup-like shape.

  3. Steam the Dumplings:

    • Place the assembled dumplings in a steamer lined with parchment paper. Steam for 6-8 minutes, or until the filling is cooked through and the wrappers are soft.

  4. Serve:

    • Top each dumpling with a small piece of crab roe or a mushroom slice for garnish. Serve hot with a soy-based dipping sauce.

4. Egg Tarts (Dan Tat)

Egg tarts are a beloved dim sum dessert. These silky custard tarts, nestled in a flaky pastry shell, offer the perfect balance of sweetness and richness.

Ingredients:

For the Pastry:

  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 50g powdered sugar
  • 100g unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons cold water

For the Filling:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Pastry:

    • In a food processor, combine flour, powdered sugar, and butter. Pulse until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Add the egg yolk and cold water, and pulse until a dough forms. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar until smooth. Add the milk, heavy cream, and vanilla extract, and whisk again. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve to remove any bubbles or impurities.

  3. Assemble the Tarts:

    • Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface and cut into rounds to fit your tart pans. Press the dough into the pans, trimming any excess. Fill each tart shell with the egg custard filling.

  4. Bake the Tarts:

    • Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Bake the tarts for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the filling has set. Let them cool slightly before serving.

  5. Serve:

    • Enjoy these sweet and creamy egg tarts as a perfect end to your dim sum meal.

Conclusion

Dim sum is a delightful culinary experience that offers something for everyone. Whether you prefer savory dumplings, fluffy buns, or sweet tarts, there’s a dish for every palate. The best part? You don’t have to travel far to enjoy these delicacies. With these step-by-step recipes, you can bring the flavors of dim sum into your own kitchen and enjoy them with your loved ones.

Dim sum is all about variety, creativity, and sharing good food with good company. The joy of making these dishes yourself adds an extra layer of satisfaction to the experience. So, gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and start making your favorite dim sum at home. Enjoy the process, and most importantly, enjoy the delicious results!
