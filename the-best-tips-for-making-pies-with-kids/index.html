The Best Tips for Making Pies with Kids


Making pies with kids can be one of the most memorable and rewarding experiences in the kitchen. Rolling out dough, sprinkling sugar, and layering fruit filling—it’s not just about baking a delicious dessert but also about creating treasured family moments. However, cooking with little ones presents unique challenges. By following these tips, you can turn pie-making into a fun, stress-free activity while fostering creativity, teaching valuable skills, and instilling a love for cooking in kids.

1. Choose Recipes That Are Kid-Friendly

The first step to making pies with kids is picking a recipe that aligns with their skill level and interests. Simple pies like apple, peach, or even no-bake chocolate pies are great starting points. Avoid recipes that involve overly complicated techniques or long prep times, as children tend to have shorter attention spans. Highlight recipes with colorful ingredients or fun designs like lattice tops or cookie-cutter shapes because they appeal to kids and keep them engaged.

Consider involving your children in the selection process. Show them pictures or tell them the options, allowing them to pick what excites them the most. Giving kids ownership over the recipe motivates them and heightens their enthusiasm to participate.

2. Prepare Your Space for Mess and Fun

Cooking with kids is wonderful, but it’s not the time to worry about maintaining spotless countertops. Embrace the inevitable flour clouds, sticky fingers, and smudges. Before diving into pie-making, prep your workspace for mess. Lay down a vinyl tablecloth, provide them with aprons, and place wet towels nearby for quick cleanups.

Also, make sure your ingredients and tools are organized ahead of time. Pre-measure items like flour, sugar, and spices so younger kids can simply pour them in without frustration. If you’re using delicate tools like rolling pins or mixing bowls, ensure they’re child-friendly and lightweight.

3. Break Tasks into Small Steps

Cooking with kids works best when the process is broken into manageable, bite-sized steps. Instead of expecting them to understand the entire recipe from start to finish, assign specific, easy-to-follow tasks. For instance:

  • Little hands can stir: Mixing flour, sugar, and spices lets kids feel involved without requiring a lot of dexterity.
  • Roll and pat: Let them roll out the dough, though don’t worry too much about perfection—imperfections give the pie character!
  • Decorate boldly: Encourage them to cut shapes from leftover dough or sprinkle sugar generously on top.

By dividing the recipe into tasks, you not only simplify the process for young helpers but also ensure each activity is short enough to keep their attention from waning.

4. Incorporate Learning Opportunities

Cooking presents an excellent opportunity for hands-on learning. While baking pies, you can introduce kids to basic concepts like measurements, fractions, and the science behind how ingredients interact. For example:

  • Math: Ask questions like, “Can you measure out two cups of flour for me?” or, “How much sugar do we need if we’re doubling the recipe?”
  • Science: Talk about why butter needs to be cold for flaky pie crust or explain how steam escapes through vents in the dough.
  • Creative thinking: Encourage artistic expression by letting kids design the crust or arrange fruit fillings in unique patterns.

These tiny lessons not only make pie-making educational but also give children skills and confidence they’ll carry beyond the kitchen.

5. Focus on the Process, Not the Outcome

Kids undoubtedly bring their own flair to cooking, which may not meet the aesthetic standards of a perfect pie. Crust edges might be uneven, designs might look lopsided, or fillings might spill over—but none of that matters. The priority should always be the experience and the joy of working together rather than the perfection of the finished product.

Celebrate every attempt and praise their contributions. Phrases like, “Wow, I love the design you made with the crust!” or “That looks like it’s going to taste amazing!” keep the mood positive and encourage kids to feel proud of their work.

6. Foster Teamwork and Patience

Making pies can be a wonderful exercise in teamwork. If you have multiple kids participating, assign them complementary tasks. While one rolls out the dough, another can press it into the pie pan, and someone else can sprinkle sugar on top. Collaborative efforts keep everyone involved while teaching kids the value of working together toward a common goal.

Moreover, pie-making is a great opportunity to practice patience. From chilling the dough to baking the pie, it’s important to explain that good things take time. Filling that waiting period with fun activities, such as decorating the pie pan or setting the table for dessert, keeps anticipation high without frustration.

7. Experiment and Get Creative

The beauty of pie-making lies in its versatility. Encourage kids to experiment and make the process their own. Maybe they want to mix fillings together—apple and cranberry, or peach and raspberry—for unique fruit combinations. Maybe they’d prefer using colorful sprinkles alongside sugar or cutting unusual shapes into the crust.

Giving kids room for creative expression turns baking into an interactive art project. Plus, it teaches them that cooking isn’t just about following instructions—it’s also about innovating and having fun.

8. Always Highlight Safety

While pie-making with kids is filled with laughter and fun, safety must always come first. Be mindful of hot ovens, sharp knives, and heavy items. Assign specific tasks based on your child’s age, allowing older kids to help with cutting or arranging while keeping younger ones focused on rolling dough or sprinkling toppings.

If an activity is momentarily unsafe for kids, create a “helper station” nearby so they can still feel involved. For example, if you’re cutting hot pie fresh from the oven, let them garnish with a dollop of whipped cream when serving.

9. Clean Up Together

After the pie is baked, cooling on the counter, and the flour dust has settled, make cleaning an enjoyable part of the activity. Play some upbeat music and turn cleanup into teamwork. This teaches kids the importance of cleaning as they go and prepares them for independent cooking in the future.

Let them help wipe the counters, rinse spoons, or put away ingredients. Small efforts go a long way in fostering lifelong habits of tidiness and responsibility.

10. Celebrate and Share the Pie Together

There’s nothing more rewarding for kids than seeing (and tasting) the fruits of their labor. Once the pie is ready, sit down together and enjoy it as a family. Take a moment to thank your helpers, compliment their work, and let them savor the results of their hard efforts.

If you’ve made multiple pies, consider sharing one with a neighbor, grandparent, or friend to teach kids the value of kindness and generosity.

Making pies with kids transforms an ordinary recipe into an extraordinary adventure. It’s about more than whisking eggs or layering fruit—it’s about creating memories, nurturing creativity, and building life skills. By following these tips, you can ensure your pie-making sessions are filled with laughter, learning, and plenty of delicious rewards.
