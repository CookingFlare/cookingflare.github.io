Easy-to-Make Summer Picnic Desserts Everyone Will Love


Summer picnics are the perfect way to enjoy the great outdoors, bask in the sunshine, and share delicious food with friends and family. While savory picnic foods often take the spotlight, it’s the desserts that leave the lasting impression. Whether you’re hosting a laid-back gathering or joining a potluck picnic, the right desserts can elevate the whole experience. The key to a successful summer picnic dessert is making it light, refreshing, and easy to serve, all while keeping the sweet factor high.

In this post, we’ve gathered a list of easy-to-make summer picnic desserts that are sure to impress everyone. These treats are not only delicious but are also simple to prepare and pack for a picnic outing. So, grab your picnic blanket, your favorite people, and let’s dive into these irresistible summer dessert ideas.

1. No-Bake Cheesecake Jars

Cheesecake is a crowd-pleaser, but making a full cheesecake can be a bit of a hassle, especially when you’re on the go. Enter no-bake cheesecake jars – a simple and fun way to enjoy this creamy, tangy dessert without turning on the oven. These little jars are portable, customizable, and can be made in advance, saving you time and effort.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries or fruit compote (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter until the mixture resembles wet sand. Press the mixture into the bottom of small mason jars or dessert cups to form the crust. Set aside.
  2. Make the cheesecake filling: Beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and beat again until well combined. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture until smooth and fluffy.
  3. Assemble the jars: Spoon or pipe the cheesecake filling over the graham cracker crust. Top with fresh berries or a drizzle of fruit compote for added flavor and color.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours before serving to allow the flavors to set and meld together.

2. Lemonade Cupcakes

Lemonade is a quintessential summer drink, and what better way to incorporate it into your picnic than by turning it into a light, refreshing cupcake? These lemonade cupcakes are bursting with citrus flavor and are the perfect balance of sweet and tart. Top them off with a tangy lemon glaze for an extra zesty kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup milk
  • ½ cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

Directions:

  1. Prepare the cupcakes: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In another bowl, beat the eggs, softened butter, milk, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  2. Mix the ingredients: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Do not overmix.
  3. Bake: Fill the cupcake liners about two-thirds full with the batter. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cupcakes cool completely on a wire rack.
  4. Make the glaze: While the cupcakes are cooling, mix the powdered sugar and lemon juice in a small bowl until smooth. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled cupcakes.
  5. Serve: These cupcakes are best enjoyed the same day but can be stored in an airtight container for a few days.

3. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

Nothing says summer like fresh, juicy fruit. This fruit salad with honey-lime dressing is the ultimate refreshing treat for a picnic. The natural sweetness of the fruit combined with the zesty honey-lime dressing creates a delicious balance that will leave your guests wanting more. You can mix and match fruits depending on what’s in season or what you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup pineapple, chopped
  • 1 cup watermelon, cubed
  • 1 cup kiwi, peeled and sliced
  • 1 orange, peeled and segmented

For the dressing:

  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp lime zest

Directions:

  1. Prepare the fruit: Wash and cut the fruit into bite-sized pieces, placing them in a large bowl.
  2. Make the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, lime juice, and lime zest until smooth.
  3. Toss and serve: Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit salad and gently toss to combine. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve. If making in advance, wait to add the dressing until just before serving to keep the fruit fresh.

4. Chocolate-Dipped Strawberries

Chocolate-dipped strawberries are an elegant and simple dessert that is always a hit at any gathering. The combination of juicy strawberries and rich chocolate is irresistible, and they can be easily made ahead of time for your picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint fresh strawberries, washed and dried
  • 8 oz dark chocolate (or milk chocolate, depending on preference)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil or vegetable oil (for smooth dipping)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the strawberries: Wash and dry the strawberries thoroughly. Make sure there’s no water left on them, as it can interfere with the chocolate coating.
  2. Melt the chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, heat the chocolate and coconut oil in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until smooth and fully melted.
  3. Dip the strawberries: Hold each strawberry by the stem and dip it into the melted chocolate, covering about two-thirds of the berry. Let any excess chocolate drip off before placing the dipped strawberry on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  4. Chill: Once all strawberries are dipped, place the baking sheet in the refrigerator to set the chocolate, about 30 minutes.
  5. Serve: These chocolate-dipped strawberries can be made ahead and stored in the refrigerator until ready to enjoy.

5. Ice Cream Sandwiches

If you’re looking for a nostalgic treat that will keep everyone cool during your summer picnic, ice cream sandwiches are the way to go. They’re fun to eat, customizable, and easy to make. The best part? You can use your favorite cookies and ice cream flavors to personalize them to your taste.

Ingredients:

  • 24 cookies (chocolate chip, oatmeal, or your favorite variety)
  • 2 cups vanilla ice cream (or any flavor you prefer)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the cookies: Bake your cookies according to your recipe or package instructions. Allow them to cool completely on a wire rack.
  2. Assemble the sandwiches: Once the cookies are cool, scoop a generous amount of ice cream onto the bottom side of one cookie. Top with a second cookie and press gently to sandwich the ice cream between them.
  3. Freeze: Place the ice cream sandwiches on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze for 1-2 hours until the ice cream is firm.
  4. Serve: Once frozen, the ice cream sandwiches are ready to enjoy. Wrap them in wax paper or parchment for easy serving and transport.

6. Peach Cobbler Bars

Peach cobbler is a classic summer dessert, but instead of making it in a pie dish, turn it into peach cobbler bars for easy serving and sharing at a picnic. These bars are sweet, buttery, and have that perfect crispy topping that pairs wonderfully with a scoop of ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or canned peaches, diced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. Prepare the peach filling: In a bowl, toss the diced peaches with lemon juice and set aside.
  3. Make the crust and topping: In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, salt, and baking soda. Add the melted butter and stir until a crumbly dough forms.
  4. Assemble the bars: Press half of the oat mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking dish to form a crust. Spread the peaches over the crust. Sprinkle the remaining oat mixture on top to cover the peaches.
  5. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Let it cool before cutting into bars.
  6. Serve: Enjoy these warm or at room temperature, paired with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra treat.

Conclusion

These easy-to-make summer picnic desserts are guaranteed to satisfy your sweet cravings and make your outdoor gatherings even more memorable. From no-bake cheesecake jars to fresh fruit salad and decadent chocolate-dipped strawberries, these treats are perfect for sharing with loved ones. The best part is that most of these desserts can be made ahead of time, so you can spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the sunshine.

No matter which dessert you choose, these recipes will add a touch of sweetness and a whole lot of joy to your next summer picnic. So gather your ingredients, get creative, and let the fun begin!
