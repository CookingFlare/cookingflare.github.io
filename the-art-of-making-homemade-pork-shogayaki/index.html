The Art of Making Homemade Pork Shogayaki


There’s something truly special about the combination of tender pork, sweet and savory sauce, and the aromatic punch of ginger in a dish of homemade Pork Shogayaki. This traditional Japanese recipe has been enjoyed for generations, and today, I’m excited to share with you the art of making this mouthwatering dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 500g thinly sliced pork loin
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Steamed rice, to serve
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Start by patting the pork slices dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture. This will help the pork brown evenly when cooked.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and grated ginger to create the flavorful marinade for the pork. Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved.

Step 3: Marinate the Pork

Place the pork slices in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Ensure that each slice is evenly coated with the marinade. Let the pork marinate for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

Step 4: Cook the Pork

Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork slices to the pan, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding. Cook the pork for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and cooked through. Remove the cooked pork from the pan and set it aside.

Step 5: Sauté the Onion

In the same pan, add the thinly sliced onion and sauté until it becomes translucent and slightly caramelized. This will take around 3-4 minutes.

Step 6: Combine the Pork and Onion

Return the cooked pork to the pan with the sautéed onion. Toss everything together to ensure the flavors are well combined.

Step 7: Final Touches

Pour any remaining marinade over the pork and onion in the pan. Allow the sauce to bubble and thicken slightly, coating the pork and onion beautifully.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Transfer the Pork Shogayaki to a serving platter and garnish with chopped green onions. Serve it alongside steamed rice for a complete and satisfying meal that’s bursting with flavor.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For a richer flavor, consider using pork belly instead of pork loin.
  • If you’re not a fan of pork, you can easily substitute it with thinly sliced chicken for a delicious Chicken Shogayaki.
  • Don’t skimp on the ginger – it’s the star of this dish and adds a wonderful depth of flavor.

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making Homemade Pork Shogayaki, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. This classic Japanese dish is a celebration of simple yet powerful flavors that come together to create a truly unforgettable culinary experience. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and embark on a delightful journey of culinary creation in your own kitchen. Happy cooking!
