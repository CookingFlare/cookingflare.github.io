Best Chinese Steamed Silken Tofu Recipe


Tofu is one of the most versatile ingredients in Chinese cuisine. It absorbs the flavors of whatever it’s paired with, making it an ideal base for a variety of dishes. Among the many ways to prepare tofu, steaming silken tofu is one of the most delicate and flavorful methods. This method keeps the tofu’s soft, silky texture intact while enhancing its flavor with savory seasonings. If you’ve ever wondered how to make a restaurant-quality steamed silken tofu dish at home, you’re in the right place.

In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the step-by-step process of making the best Chinese steamed silken tofu. Whether you are a tofu enthusiast or new to cooking with this humble ingredient, this recipe is perfect for any home cook looking to create a light, flavorful dish.

What is Silken Tofu?

Silken tofu is a type of tofu that has a smooth, custard-like texture. Unlike regular firm tofu, which holds its shape and can be sliced, silken tofu has a much softer consistency, making it perfect for dishes that require a delicate texture. It’s often used in soups, smoothies, desserts, and other Chinese recipes.

The beauty of silken tofu lies in its ability to absorb flavors. When steamed, the tofu’s creamy texture is enhanced, and it serves as a perfect base for rich, flavorful sauces. This recipe focuses on steaming the tofu and pairing it with a savory sauce that’s simple yet packs a punch.

Ingredients for Steamed Silken Tofu

To create a flavorful steamed silken tofu dish, you’ll need only a few ingredients. These ingredients come together to provide a delicious balance of savory, umami-rich flavors.

  • 1 block of silken tofu (around 300g)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 spring onions, chopped (white and green parts separated)
  • 1-2 red chilies, finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • A small handful of cilantro (optional for garnish)
  • A pinch of white pepper or black pepper (optional)

Equipment Needed

Before you begin cooking, ensure you have the following items on hand:

  • A steamer basket or a large pot with a lid
  • A small mixing bowl
  • A spoon for stirring
  • A knife and chopping board

How to Prepare Steamed Silken Tofu

Step 1: Preparing the Tofu

The first step in making steamed silken tofu is to prepare the tofu. Silken tofu is delicate, so you need to handle it gently to avoid breaking it. Here’s how:

  1. Remove the tofu from its packaging. Place it on a plate and carefully drain any excess liquid.
  2. Use a paper towel or cloth to gently blot the tofu and remove any additional moisture. This step will help the tofu absorb the flavors of the sauce more effectively.
  3. Cut the tofu into smaller pieces if desired, or leave it whole for a more dramatic presentation. If you’re using a traditional Chinese steaming dish, you can leave the tofu block intact.

Step 2: Prepare the Steaming Setup

Next, set up your steamer. You can use a bamboo steamer, a metal steamer insert, or even a heatproof dish placed in a regular pot. Here’s how to steam your tofu:

  1. Fill the steamer with water and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
  2. If you’re using a bamboo or metal steamer, place the tofu in the steamer basket, ensuring there’s enough space for steam to circulate around it. For a pot, place the tofu in a heatproof dish and set it in the pot, ensuring the tofu is raised above the water level. You can use a steam rack or a small bowl upside down to create a stable platform for the dish.

Step 3: Steaming the Tofu

Now that your tofu is ready and the steamer is set up, it’s time to steam the tofu.

  1. Gently place the tofu into the steamer and cover it with a lid. Steam the tofu for about 8-10 minutes, or until it’s heated through. Silken tofu cooks quickly, so be careful not to oversteam it, as it may become too soft and lose its delicate texture.

  2. Once steamed, carefully remove the tofu from the steamer. Be gentle as the tofu will be hot and soft.

Step 4: Preparing the Sauce

While the tofu is steaming, you can prepare the savory sauce that will accompany the tofu. This sauce is a simple blend of soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and a touch of sugar to balance the flavors. Here’s how to make it:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sugar. Stir well to dissolve the sugar and combine the ingredients.
  2. Heat a small pan over medium heat. Add a bit of oil and sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant, about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Be careful not to burn them.
  3. Pour the sauce into the pan with the garlic and ginger, and bring it to a gentle simmer. Let the sauce cook for about 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly and the flavors meld together.

Step 5: Combining the Tofu and Sauce

Once the tofu is steamed and the sauce is ready, it’s time to combine the two. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Carefully transfer the steamed tofu to a serving dish if you haven’t already done so.
  2. Pour the hot sauce over the tofu, making sure it’s evenly distributed. The sauce will seep into the tofu, infusing it with savory, umami flavors.
  3. Sprinkle the chopped spring onions over the top of the tofu, focusing on the white parts first. The green parts can be used for garnish.
  4. If you want a bit of heat, sprinkle finely chopped red chilies over the tofu. If you prefer a milder dish, you can skip this step.
  5. Optionally, garnish with fresh cilantro for added freshness and color.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Your steamed silken tofu is now ready to be served! This dish can be served as part of a larger meal or as a main dish with steamed rice. The tofu pairs wonderfully with stir-fried vegetables, rice, and other traditional Chinese dishes.

Tips for Perfect Steamed Silken Tofu

  • Choosing the right tofu: Make sure to use silken tofu for the best texture. Regular or firm tofu will not achieve the same soft, silky result.
  • Adjusting the sauce: If you prefer a sweeter sauce, you can add a bit more sugar or even a dash of honey. For a spicier kick, increase the amount of chili or add a dash of chili oil.
  • Steaming time: Be mindful of the steaming time. Oversteaming can cause the tofu to break apart and lose its delicate texture, while understeaming may leave it too cold.
  • Substitute the oyster sauce: If you’re vegetarian or vegan, you can replace the oyster sauce with a plant-based alternative like mushroom soy sauce or vegan oyster sauce.

Health Benefits of Steamed Silken Tofu

In addition to its versatility in the kitchen, silken tofu also provides several health benefits. It’s a great source of plant-based protein, making it an excellent option for vegetarians and vegans. Tofu is also low in calories, making it suitable for a healthy, balanced diet. The dish is rich in essential amino acids, calcium, and iron, all of which contribute to bone health, muscle repair, and overall well-being.

Additionally, the ingredients in this recipe—such as ginger and garlic—have their own health benefits. Ginger is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, while garlic has been shown to support heart health and boost the immune system.

Variations of Steamed Silken Tofu

While this recipe offers a classic savory preparation, there are many ways to adapt the dish to suit your preferences. Here are a few variations you can try:

  1. Mushroom Steamed Tofu: Add sautéed mushrooms to the dish for an earthy, umami-rich flavor. Shiitake mushrooms or oyster mushrooms work particularly well.
  2. Spicy Tofu: For an extra kick, drizzle chili oil or add extra fresh chilies to the sauce. You can also top the tofu with Sichuan peppercorns for a numbing, spicy flavor.
  3. Vegetable Tofu: Pair the tofu with steamed vegetables such as bok choy, spinach, or broccoli. These vegetables complement the tofu’s texture and add more nutrients to the dish.

Conclusion

Steamed silken tofu is an incredibly versatile dish that brings together delicate textures and bold flavors. With just a few ingredients and a simple cooking method, you can create a dish that is both satisfying and light. The savory sauce complements the tofu beautifully, making each bite a delightful experience.

Whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests, this Chinese steamed silken tofu recipe is sure to impress. Its simplicity, health benefits, and customizable options make it a perfect addition to any meal. Give it a try, and you’ll see just how easy it is to make this restaurant-quality dish in the comfort of your own home.
