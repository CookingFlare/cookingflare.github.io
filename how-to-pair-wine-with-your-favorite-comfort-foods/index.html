How to Pair Wine with Your Favorite Comfort Foods


There’s nothing quite like wrapping yourself in the warmth of comfort food after a long day. From creamy mac and cheese to hearty stews, these meals have a way of soothing our souls. But have you ever thought about elevating these comforting dishes with the perfect wine pairing? Choosing the right wine can unlock flavors you didn’t know were there and make the experience even more indulgent. Let’s explore how to pair some of your favorite comfort foods with wines that complement, enhance, and maybe even surprise you.

Mac and Cheese

Why It’s Comfort Food: Creamy, cheesy, and utterly satisfying, mac and cheese is the quintessential comfort food. Its richness begs for a drink that can cut through the heavy texture while balancing the flavors.

Best Wine Pairing: A crisp, refreshing Chardonnay or an unoaked white wine like Sauvignon Blanc works beautifully here. These wines have enough acidity to counterbalance the richness of the cheese sauce. Prefer a red? Try a light-bodied Pinot Noir, which won’t overwhelm but will add a touch of fruitiness.

Fried Chicken

Why It’s Comfort Food: The crunchy golden crust surrounding tender, juicy chicken is pure happiness on a plate. Whether you enjoy it Southern-style or Korean-fried, this dish is always a crowd-pleaser.

Best Wine Pairing: For fried chicken, sparkling wine is a match made in heaven. Champagne or Prosecco provides a lively acidity and carbonation that cut through the greasiness of the fried coating while enhancing the flavors. If you’re a fan of still wines, try a Chenin Blanc or a dry Riesling for a touch of brightness.

Spaghetti and Meatballs

Why It’s Comfort Food: Nothing says cozy family dinner quite like a hearty plate of spaghetti and meatballs. The combination of tomato sauce, tender meat, and al dente pasta is a classic for good reason.

Best Wine Pairing: The tangy tomato sauce is the star here, so you’ll want a red wine with good acidity, such as a Sangiovese (think Chianti). The wine’s natural acidity complements the tomatoes while its earthy warmth balances the meatiness of the dish. If you’re open to exploring whites, an off-dry Sauvignon Blanc can bring a fresh, herbaceous note.

Grilled Cheese and Tomato Soup

Why It’s Comfort Food: It’s simple, nostalgic, and comforting on the chilliest of days. The melty sandwich and warm soup pairing is a hug in culinary form.

Best Wine Pairing: The creamy texture and savory flavor of grilled cheese paired with the acidity of tomato soup call for something that bridges the gap. A medium-bodied rosé is a fantastic choice as it offers refreshing acidity while being versatile enough to match both components. A dry Riesling or even a light Gamay can also work well here.

Beef Stew

Why It’s Comfort Food: Chunky vegetables, tender beef, and a rich broth simmered to perfection—beef stew is a dish that feels like home.

Best Wine Pairing: Full-bodied reds like Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, or Malbec are ideal here as they match the heartiness of the stew. These wines offer rich, robust flavors that stand up well to the dish’s meat and herbs. For a slightly softer option, a Merlot can provide a velvety companion to each bite.

Pizza

Why It’s Comfort Food: Pizza is an endlessly customizable comfort food that satisfies cravings like no other. Whether you’re into classic Margherita, pepperoni, or veggie-loaded pies, there’s a wine to match.

Best Wine Pairing: For a classic Margherita pizza, a medium-bodied red like Sangiovese or Barbera complements the tomato sauce and mozzarella. A pepperoni pizza works well with Zinfandel, which enhances the dish’s spiciness. If you prefer white pizzas (no tomato sauce), a Chardonnay or Pinot Grigio might be your go-to.

Shepherd’s Pie

Why It’s Comfort Food: Golden mashed potatoes layered over savory ground meat and vegetables—it’s hearty, filling, and deeply satisfying.

Best Wine Pairing: Opt for a fruity Grenache or Pinot Noir to highlight the savory flavors without overpowering the dish’s subtlety. If you love white wines, a full-bodied Viognier can add a floral and slightly creamy dimension to the pairing.

Chili

Why It’s Comfort Food: Spicy, smoky, and comforting, chili is perfect on a cold day or anytime you need a little heat to warm you up.

Best Wine Pairing: A fruity red like Zinfandel or Shiraz/Syrah complements the spicy notes in chili without clashing with its heat. If your chili has a milder spice profile, a Beaujolais or Tempranillo can work wonderfully, lending bright fruitiness.

Mashed Potatoes and Gravy

Why It’s Comfort Food: Creamy, buttery potatoes paired with savory gravy—this is the hallmark of holiday meals and family gatherings.

Best Wine Pairing: The rich, starchy texture of mashed potatoes shines next to a light-bodied red such as Pinot Noir. For white wine lovers, look for a buttery Chardonnay to mirror the creamy richness of the dish.

Lasagna

Why It’s Comfort Food: Layers of pasta, cheese, meat, and savory tomato sauce baked into a bubbling casserole—it’s the ultimate comfort food for pasta lovers.

Best Wine Pairing: Italian reds like Chianti, Barbera, or Montepulciano d’Abruzzo are excellent choices. Their acidity cuts through the cheese while enhancing the tomato sauce. For white wine enthusiasts, a robust white like a Soave or Vermentino can offer an unexpected yet delightful pairing.

Chocolate Desserts

Why It’s Comfort Food: Whether it’s a warm brownie, chocolate chip cookies, or a slice of cake, chocolate desserts are the ultimate in indulgence.

Best Wine Pairing: Sweet and fortified wines like Port or a rich red like Zinfandel are delicious choices. The sweetness of the wine should match or exceed the sweetness of the dessert. For a less traditional pairing, try a sparkling Rosé or an Italian Brachetto.

Why Pair Wine with Comfort Foods?

Pairing wine with comfort food doesn’t just elevate the dining experience—it brings out nuances in both the dish and the wine that might otherwise go unnoticed. Whether you’re planning a cozy night in or hosting friends for a laid-back dinner, the right wine can make your favorite comfort foods taste even better.

Here’s to savoring every bite and every sip!
