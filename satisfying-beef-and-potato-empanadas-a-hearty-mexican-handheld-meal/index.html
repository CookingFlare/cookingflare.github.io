Satisfying Beef and Potato Empanadas: A Hearty Mexican Handheld Meal


If you’re in the mood for a delicious, hearty, and satisfying meal, look no further than beef and potato empanadas. These handheld treats are a staple in Mexican cuisine, loved for their crispy exterior, flavorful filling, and versatility. Whether you’re hosting a party, planning a picnic, or simply craving a comforting meal, these empanadas are sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup cold water

For the filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup beef broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the dough. Add the cold, cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the cold water, mixing until the dough comes together. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently until smooth. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until softened and fragrant.

  5. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Stir in the cumin, paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper, ensuring the spices are evenly distributed.

  6. Add the diced potatoes and beef broth to the skillet. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

  7. Remove the chilled dough from the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles, approximately 4-5 inches in diameter.

  8. Spoon a generous amount of the beef and potato filling onto one half of each dough circle, leaving a border around the edges. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling, creating a half-moon shape. Use a fork to crimp the edges and seal the empanadas.

  9. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.

  10. Remove from the oven and let the empanadas cool for a few minutes before serving. These handheld delights are perfect on their own or served with salsa, guacamole, or sour cream for dipping.

Enjoy the satisfying combination of tender beef, flavorful potatoes, and crispy dough in every bite. These beef and potato empanadas are a delicious and versatile meal that can be enjoyed any time of the day. Whether you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure with these savory empanadas. Bon appétit!
