Taste the Mediterranean: 5 Marinades for Grilled Swordfish Steaks


Are you looking to add some Mediterranean flavors to your next summer cookout? Look no further than these delicious marinades for grilled swordfish steaks. Swordfish is a firm and meaty fish that holds up well to grilling, and when paired with these vibrant and aromatic marinades, it takes on a whole new level of flavor. So fire up the grill, grab your swordfish steaks, and let’s dive into the world of Mediterranean cuisine.

1. Lemon and Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, parsley, basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them.
  3. Make sure each steak is coated with the marinade on both sides.
  4. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a stronger flavor.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the swordfish steaks from the marinade and discard the excess marinade.
  7. Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and have beautiful grill marks.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy the fresh and zesty flavors of the Mediterranean!

2. Garlic and Rosemary Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, making sure to coat each steak evenly.
  3. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a stronger flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the swordfish steaks from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.
  6. Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  7. The combination of garlic, rosemary, and lemon zest will create a mouthwatering aroma that will transport you to the Mediterranean with every bite.

3. Balsamic Glaze Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar, honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  2. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 5 minutes, or until it has thickened slightly.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the marinade cool completely.
  4. Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring that each steak is coated evenly.
  5. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.
  6. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  7. Remove the swordfish steaks from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.
  8. Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and have a beautiful caramelized glaze.
  9. The sweet and tangy flavors of the balsamic glaze will take your grilled swordfish to new heights of deliciousness.

4. Mediterranean Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring that each steak is coated evenly.
  3. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a stronger flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the swordfish steaks from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.
  6. Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are cooked to perfection.
  7. The combination of fresh herbs and tangy lemon juice will transport you straight to the sunny shores of the Mediterranean.

5. Spicy Harissa Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons harissa paste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, harissa paste, lemon juice, minced garlic, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, making sure to coat each steak evenly.
  3. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a bolder flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the swordfish steaks from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.
  6. Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and have a delightful spicy kick.
  7. The fiery flavors of harissa combined with the succulent swordfish will transport your taste buds straight to the Mediterranean spice markets.

Conclusion

There you have it - 5 tantalizing marinades that will elevate your grilled swordfish steaks to a whole new level. Whether you prefer the freshness of lemon and herbs, the robustness of garlic and rosemary, the tanginess of balsamic glaze, the Mediterranean charm of herbs, or the fiery kick of harissa, there’s a marinade for every palate. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and embark on a culinary journey through the flavors of the Mediterranean. Bon appétit!
