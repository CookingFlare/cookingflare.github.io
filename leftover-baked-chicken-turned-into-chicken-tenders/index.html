Leftover Baked Chicken Turned into Chicken Tenders


Leftover baked chicken can often end up as an afterthought in your fridge, a lonely remnant of a previous meal, waiting for someone to give it new life. If you find yourself with leftover baked chicken, fear not! With a little creativity and a few simple steps, you can transform that chicken into crispy, mouth-watering chicken tenders that will delight your taste buds and make your meals exciting again.

Why Chicken Tenders?

Chicken tenders are the ultimate comfort food. They are versatile, easy to prepare, and loved by both kids and adults. Plus, they can be enjoyed in various ways: served with dipping sauces, tossed in salads, or nestled in a sandwich. By using leftover baked chicken, you not only reduce food waste but also save time in the kitchen. Let’s dive into how to make this transformation.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s what you’ll need to create chicken tenders from your leftover baked chicken:

  • Leftover baked chicken: Approximately 2 cups, shredded or chopped.
  • Breadcrumbs: 1 cup (use panko for extra crunch).
  • All-purpose flour: 1 cup.
  • Eggs: 2 large, beaten.
  • Garlic powder: 1 teaspoon.
  • Onion powder: 1 teaspoon.
  • Paprika: 1 teaspoon (smoked paprika adds a nice flavor).
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Cooking oil: For frying (canola, vegetable, or olive oil).
  • Optional spices: You can add cayenne pepper for heat or Italian seasoning for a herbal touch.

Step-by-Step Guide to Make Chicken Tenders

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by taking your leftover baked chicken and shredding or chopping it into bite-sized pieces. The size will depend on how thick you want your tenders to be. Aim for uniform pieces to ensure even cooking.

Step 2: Set Up Your Breading Station

Prepare three shallow dishes for the breading process:

  1. Flour Mixture: In the first dish, combine the all-purpose flour with garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  2. Egg Wash: In the second dish, beat the eggs until they are well combined. You can add a splash of water or milk to make it a little lighter.

  3. Breadcrumbs: In the third dish, place the breadcrumbs. If you want extra flavor, consider mixing in additional spices here.

Step 3: Bread the Chicken

Now it’s time to coat the chicken pieces:

  1. Dredge in Flour: Take a piece of the shredded chicken and coat it in the seasoned flour mixture, shaking off any excess.

  2. Dip in Egg Wash: Next, dip the floured chicken into the egg wash, ensuring it’s fully coated.

  3. Coat with Breadcrumbs: Finally, roll the chicken in the breadcrumbs, pressing gently to ensure the coating sticks. Set aside on a plate and repeat with the remaining chicken.

Step 4: Fry the Chicken Tenders

In a large skillet, heat about 1/2 inch of cooking oil over medium heat. You can test if the oil is ready by dropping a breadcrumb into it; if it sizzles, you’re good to go.

  1. Fry in Batches: Carefully add a few chicken tenders to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side.

  2. Drain Excess Oil: Once cooked, use a slotted spoon to transfer the tenders to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Your chicken tenders are ready to be devoured! Serve them hot with your favorite dipping sauces such as honey mustard, barbecue sauce, or ranch dressing. You can also pair them with a side salad or sweet potato fries for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Tenders

  • Keep It Crispy: If you want to keep the tenders crispy while you finish frying the rest, place them on a baking sheet in a warm oven (around 200°F or 93°C).
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to experiment with different spices and flavors in your breadcrumb mixture. Parmesan cheese, herbs, or even a bit of lemon zest can elevate your chicken tenders.
  • Baking Option: If you prefer a healthier approach, you can bake the chicken tenders instead of frying. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C), place the breaded chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and lightly spray with cooking oil. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden and crispy.

Creative Serving Suggestions

While chicken tenders are delicious on their own, here are a few creative ways to serve them:

  • Chicken Tender Wrap: Use a tortilla to wrap the tenders along with lettuce, tomatoes, and a drizzle of ranch dressing for a quick and satisfying lunch.
  • Salad Topper: Slice the tenders and add them to a fresh garden salad for a protein boost.
  • Chicken Tender Sliders: Place the tenders on slider buns with your choice of toppings for a fun party appetizer.
  • Buffalo Chicken Tenders: Toss the cooked tenders in buffalo sauce for a spicy twist, and serve with celery sticks and blue cheese dressing.

Storing Leftover Chicken Tenders

If you happen to have any chicken tenders left over (which is rare!), you can store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, place them in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 10-15 minutes until heated through and crispy again.

Conclusion

Transforming leftover baked chicken into crispy chicken tenders is not only a practical solution for reducing food waste but also a delightful way to enjoy a familiar flavor in a new form. With minimal effort, you can create a dish that is sure to please everyone around the table. So, the next time you find yourself with leftover baked chicken, don’t let it go to waste. Instead, roll up your sleeves and whip up a batch of these delicious chicken tenders! Enjoy the satisfaction of creating a new meal from leftovers, and relish every bite of your culinary creation!
