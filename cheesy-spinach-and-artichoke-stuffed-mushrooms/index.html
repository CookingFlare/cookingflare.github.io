Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Stuffed Mushrooms


If you’re looking for a crowd-pleasing appetizer that combines the rich flavors of cheese, spinach, and artichoke, then look no further than these Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Stuffed Mushrooms. These bite-sized delights are bursting with savory goodness and are sure to impress your guests at any gathering.

Ingredients

  • 24 large white mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 1 cup canned artichoke hearts, drained and finely chopped
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. Clean the mushrooms by wiping them with a damp paper towel. Remove the stems and set aside. Place the mushroom caps on the prepared baking sheet.

  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  4. Add the spinach to the skillet and cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the spinach is wilted, remove the skillet from the heat and set aside.

  5. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped artichoke hearts, cream cheese, Parmesan cheese, and mozzarella cheese. Stir in the cooked spinach mixture and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Spoon the spinach and artichoke filling into each mushroom cap, packing it in tightly. Place the stuffed mushrooms back on the baking sheet.

  7. Bake the mushrooms in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes or until the filling is golden and bubbly.

  8. Remove the stuffed mushrooms from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Sprinkle with fresh chopped parsley for an extra burst of flavor and a touch of color.

  9. Serve the Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Stuffed Mushrooms warm and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as cremini or portobello, for a unique twist on this recipe.
  • Add a sprinkle of breadcrumbs on top of each stuffed mushroom before baking for a crispy finish.
  • For a touch of heat, add some red pepper flakes to the filling mixture.
  • These stuffed mushrooms can be made ahead of time and refrigerated until ready to bake. Simply follow the instructions up to step 6, cover with plastic wrap, and place in the refrigerator. When you’re ready to serve, just pop them in the oven and bake as directed.

Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking for a delicious appetizer to enjoy with your loved ones, these Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Stuffed Mushrooms are a perfect choice. With their irresistible combination of flavors and easy preparation, they are bound to become a new favorite in your recipe repertoire. So, grab some mushrooms and get ready to indulge in a mouthwatering culinary experience!
