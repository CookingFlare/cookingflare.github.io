How to Make Grilled Veggie Skewers: A Step-by-Step Guide


Grilled veggie skewers are a delicious and versatile dish perfect for any occasion, from backyard barbecues to cozy family dinners. These skewers are packed with a vibrant mix of vegetables, and they are naturally vegetarian, making them a great choice for plant-based eaters or anyone looking to incorporate more vegetables into their meals. Whether you’re new to grilling or a seasoned pro, this step-by-step guide will help you create perfect grilled veggie skewers every time.

Why Grilled Veggie Skewers?

Grilled veggie skewers offer a wonderful combination of smoky, caramelized flavors and tender textures. The vegetables are cooked to perfection, enhanced by the grill’s heat, and often seasoned with a variety of marinades or spices. They make for a healthy, satisfying dish on their own or can serve as a side dish to complement other grilled items, such as meats or grains.

Grilling vegetables on skewers has the added benefit of evenly cooking each piece and making flipping them a breeze. You can get creative with your vegetable choices, try different marinades, or switch up the seasonings to match your tastes.

The Essential Tools for Grilled Veggie Skewers

Before we dive into the recipe, it’s important to know what tools and equipment you’ll need to make grilled veggie skewers successfully. Here’s a list of the essentials:

  1. Skewers: You’ll need either metal or wooden skewers to thread the veggies. Metal skewers are durable and reusable, while wooden skewers offer a more traditional, rustic feel. If you choose wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes before grilling to prevent them from burning.

  2. Grill: Whether you have a charcoal, gas, or even an indoor grill, you’ll want a good heat source to cook your skewers. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat for optimal cooking.

  3. Bowl for Marinade: A large bowl for tossing the vegetables in your marinade or seasoning is key to evenly coating each piece.

  4. Tongs: You’ll need tongs for turning the skewers to ensure even cooking on all sides.

  5. Basting Brush (optional): If you’re using a marinade or sauce, a basting brush can be handy for adding more flavor while grilling.

Choosing Your Vegetables

The first step in making delicious grilled veggie skewers is selecting your vegetables. You want to pick vegetables that hold up well to the heat of the grill, with some that caramelize and others that maintain a satisfying crunch. Here’s a list of popular vegetables that work beautifully on skewers:

  • Bell Peppers: Sweet, crunchy, and juicy, bell peppers add a pop of color to your skewers. They grill beautifully and develop a smoky flavor.
  • Zucchini: Zucchini’s mild taste and tender texture make it a perfect choice for grilling.
  • Red Onions: Sweet and savory when grilled, red onions become soft and slightly charred, adding depth of flavor to your skewers.
  • Mushrooms: Button or cremini mushrooms absorb marinades well and become juicy and tender when grilled.
  • Cherry Tomatoes: These little bursts of sweetness add contrast to the other grilled vegetables, and their skins blister perfectly over the grill.
  • Eggplant: Eggplant soaks up marinades and becomes soft and smoky, making it a great addition to any skewer mix.
  • Asparagus: With its slightly earthy flavor, asparagus adds a crisp bite to grilled veggie skewers.
  • Squash: Summer squash varieties like yellow squash or pattypan add a delicate, sweet flavor when grilled.

While these vegetables are among the best choices, you can experiment with any other vegetables you love. Keep in mind that denser vegetables like carrots may need to be pre-cooked or sliced thinner to ensure they cook evenly with the others.

Prepping the Veggies

Before threading the vegetables onto the skewers, you’ll need to properly prep them for grilling. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash and Dry: Wash all vegetables thoroughly and pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel. Removing excess moisture helps them cook evenly on the grill.

  2. Peel (If Necessary): For vegetables like eggplant and zucchini, you can either peel the skin or leave it on. The skin adds texture and flavor, but if you prefer a more delicate texture, peel them before slicing.

  3. Cut into Uniform Pieces: To ensure even cooking, cut all vegetables into similar-sized pieces. Larger pieces may take longer to cook, while smaller ones may char too quickly.

  4. Seasoning: Seasoning the vegetables before grilling is key to building flavor. You can either marinate the vegetables or simply toss them with olive oil, salt, pepper, and your choice of herbs and spices.

Marinades and Seasoning for Grilled Veggie Skewers

A marinade is a fantastic way to infuse flavor into your grilled veggies, and there are endless possibilities. A basic marinade typically includes oil, acid (like vinegar or citrus juice), and seasonings. Here’s a simple marinade recipe you can use for your veggie skewers:

Basic Marinade for Veggie Skewers

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar (or lemon juice)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano (or thyme)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions for Marinading Vegetables

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, vinegar, garlic, and herbs.
  2. Add the chopped vegetables to the bowl, tossing to coat them evenly with the marinade.
  3. Let the vegetables sit in the marinade for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours, to allow the flavors to soak in. You can also marinate them overnight in the fridge for more intense flavor.

If you’re in a rush, you can skip the marinade and simply toss the veggies with olive oil, salt, and pepper for a quick and easy flavor boost.

Assembling the Skewers

Once your vegetables are prepped and seasoned, it’s time to thread them onto the skewers. Follow these simple steps:

  1. Alternate Vegetables: Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between different types to create a colorful and flavorful mix. Be sure to leave a small gap between each piece of vegetable so the heat can circulate evenly around them.

  2. Don’t Overcrowd: Avoid overcrowding the skewers, as this can make it harder to turn the vegetables and could lead to uneven cooking. It’s better to use more skewers if necessary.

  3. Use Two Skewers for Stability (Optional): If you’re using wooden skewers or your veggies tend to slide around, try using two skewers for each skewer. This will keep the vegetables in place and make it easier to turn them.

Grilling the Veggie Skewers

Now that the skewers are ready, it’s time to fire up the grill. Follow these steps to ensure your veggie skewers are perfectly grilled:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. You want the grill hot enough to sear the vegetables, but not so hot that they burn too quickly.

  2. Oil the Grill Grates: Lightly oil the grill grates using a paper towel dipped in oil or a grill spray. This helps prevent sticking and ensures even grilling.

  3. Grill the Skewers: Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning them every 2-3 minutes with tongs. Keep an eye on them to avoid burning, and make sure all sides of the vegetables get a nice char.

  4. Check for Doneness: The vegetables should be tender with a slight crispness, and they should have a nice, smoky char on the outside. You can test doneness by piercing the vegetables with a fork or skewer. If it goes in easily, they’re ready.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Veggie Skewers

  • Don’t Overcook: Overcooked vegetables can become mushy and lose their vibrant color. Aim for a tender yet firm texture.
  • Add a Glaze or Sauce: If you want to add extra flavor, brush your veggies with a glaze or sauce while grilling. Balsamic glaze, chimichurri, or a simple lemon and herb dressing are excellent choices.
  • Use Skewers with Different Lengths: For ease of cooking, consider using skewers of different lengths, depending on how many vegetables you plan to grill at once.

Serving Your Grilled Veggie Skewers

Once your grilled veggie skewers are ready, it’s time to serve and enjoy them. They are incredibly versatile and can be paired with many different dishes. Here are a few ideas:

  • As a Side Dish: Serve your skewers alongside grilled meats, grains like quinoa or couscous, or a refreshing salad.
  • As a Main Dish: Make the skewers the star of the meal by pairing them with a hearty grain and a flavorful dip, such as tzatziki or hummus.
  • On a Wrap: Place the grilled veggies in a tortilla wrap with some fresh greens, feta cheese, and a drizzle of your favorite sauce for a delicious veggie wrap.

Final Thoughts

Grilled veggie skewers are a fantastic addition to any meal, offering a healthy and flavorful way to enjoy fresh vegetables. With the right preparation and seasoning, you can elevate your grilled veggies into something truly special. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced griller, these skewers are a fun and satisfying way to enjoy the smoky goodness of grilled vegetables.

By choosing your vegetables carefully, marinating them with delicious flavors, and grilling them to perfection, you can create a dish that everyone will love. Plus, you can easily customize the recipe based on the vegetables and flavors you enjoy most. Grilled veggie skewers are a true crowd-pleaser, and they’re perfect for any occasion. Happy grilling!
