How to Make Chinese Chive Dumplings


Chinese Chive Dumplings, also known as chive pockets or chive dumplings (韭菜饺子), are a beloved dish in Chinese cuisine. With their flavorful filling made of fresh chives and a delightful, tender dough, these dumplings are often served during festive occasions or as a comforting meal any time of the year. This recipe will guide you step-by-step on how to make these delicious dumplings from scratch, with tips on preparing the dough, filling, and cooking method to ensure you create the perfect batch of Chinese chive dumplings.

What are Chinese Chive Dumplings?

Chinese chive dumplings are typically filled with a mixture of finely chopped Chinese chives (also called jiucai), ground pork, and sometimes shrimp. The chives provide a distinctive, fragrant flavor that pairs wonderfully with the savory filling. Unlike other types of dumplings, these often feature a softer, more delicate wrapper. These dumplings can be steamed, boiled, or pan-fried, depending on your preference.

Ingredients Needed

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup warm water (approximately)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Cornstarch (for dusting)

For the Filling:

  • 1 bunch Chinese chives (about 2 cups, finely chopped)
  • ½ lb ground pork (or chicken, if preferred)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Cooking:

  • Vegetable oil (for frying, if pan-frying)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

The dough for Chinese chive dumplings is relatively simple to make, and the key to getting it just right is to knead it until it’s smooth and elastic. This will ensure your dumplings have a tender and chewy texture.

  1. Mixing the Flour and Water
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add the warm water, stirring with chopsticks or a spoon until the mixture starts to come together.

  2. Kneading the Dough
    Once the dough begins to form, transfer it onto a clean surface and knead it for about 10 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle a bit of flour; if it’s too dry, add a tiny bit more water, just a teaspoon at a time.

  3. Resting the Dough
    Once kneaded, cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. Resting allows the gluten to relax, which makes the dough easier to work with when you’re ready to shape the dumplings.

Step 2: Preparing the Filling

The filling is the heart of these dumplings, so it’s important to use fresh ingredients to ensure the best flavor.

  1. Chopping the Chives
    Wash the chives thoroughly and chop them finely. It’s important to chop them evenly so that they mix well with the other ingredients and create a balanced filling.

  2. Mixing the Filling
    In a bowl, combine the chopped chives with the ground pork (or chicken). Add soy sauce, sesame oil, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Taste the filling and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

  3. Letting the Filling Rest
    Allow the filling to sit for a few minutes to let the flavors meld. This also helps the filling release any excess moisture, which is important for preventing the dumplings from becoming too soggy.

Step 3: Shaping the Dumplings

Now that the dough and filling are ready, it’s time to shape the dumplings. The process may take a little practice, but with patience, you’ll soon be able to form perfectly shaped dumplings.

  1. Dividing the Dough
    After the dough has rested, divide it into 12-16 equal pieces, depending on how large or small you want your dumplings to be. Roll each piece into a ball.

  2. Rolling Out the Dough
    On a floured surface, take each ball of dough and roll it into a small circle, about 3-4 inches in diameter. The goal is to create a thin, even wrapper, but be careful not to roll it too thin, as it could tear when folding.

  3. Filling the Dumplings
    Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle. Be sure not to overfill, as this can cause the dumpling to burst during cooking. Gently fold the dough in half over the filling, pinching the edges together to seal them tightly. You can create a pleated edge by folding and pinching the dough as you seal it, giving the dumpling a traditional appearance.

  4. Sealing the Dumplings
    Once all your dumplings are shaped, give them a quick check to ensure they’re well sealed. If there are any openings, gently pinch them closed to avoid any filling spilling out during cooking.

Step 4: Cooking Methods for Chinese Chive Dumplings

Chinese chive dumplings can be cooked in several different ways, depending on your preference. Here are three popular methods to try:

Method 1: Steamed Dumplings

  1. Prepare the Steamer
    Line a steamer basket with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent the dumplings from sticking. Place the dumplings inside, leaving a little space between each to ensure they don’t stick together.

  2. Steaming the Dumplings
    Bring a pot of water to a boil. Once the water is boiling, place the steamer over the pot and steam the dumplings for about 10-12 minutes, or until the dough is cooked through and the filling is piping hot.

Method 2: Boiled Dumplings

  1. Boiling the Dumplings
    Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Gently drop the dumplings into the boiling water, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Stir occasionally to prevent them from sticking to the bottom.

  2. Cooking the Dumplings
    Once the dumplings float to the top, allow them to cook for another 2-3 minutes. The dumplings should be tender and cooked through when ready.

Method 3: Pan-Fried Dumplings

  1. Heating the Pan
    Heat a non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of vegetable oil and let it heat up.

  2. Frying the Dumplings
    Carefully place the dumplings in the pan, flat side down. Fry them for 2-3 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown and crispy.

  3. Adding Water to Steam
    Add about ¼ cup of water to the pan and cover with a lid. Let the dumplings steam for an additional 5-6 minutes or until fully cooked through.

  4. Serving
    Once the dumplings are golden and crispy on the bottom, transfer them to a serving platter and enjoy immediately.

Step 5: Serving and Enjoying Chinese Chive Dumplings

Chinese chive dumplings are typically served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and a dash of sesame oil. You can also add chili oil for some extra heat if desired. Serve the dumplings hot, either as an appetizer or as part of a larger meal. They pair wonderfully with other Chinese dishes like stir-fried vegetables or a light soup.

Tips for Perfect Chinese Chive Dumplings

  • Fresh Chives are Key: The star of the filling is the Chinese chives, which provide a distinct flavor. Make sure to use fresh, tender chives for the best taste.
  • Don’t Overfill: It’s easy to get excited about the delicious filling, but overfilling the dumplings can lead to bursting. Stick to a small spoonful of filling for the perfect ratio of dough to stuffing.
  • Practice Makes Perfect: Shaping dumplings may take some practice, but don’t worry if they don’t look perfect the first time. The more you make, the better your technique will become.

Conclusion

Making Chinese chive dumplings at home is a fun and rewarding experience. By following this detailed recipe, you can enjoy the authentic taste of homemade dumplings, packed with fresh chives and a savory filling. Whether you choose to steam, boil, or pan-fry your dumplings, they are sure to be a hit with your family and friends. So gather your ingredients, get your hands in the dough, and start making these flavorful dumplings today!
