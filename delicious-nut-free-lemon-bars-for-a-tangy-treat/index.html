Delicious Nut-Free Lemon Bars for a Tangy Treat


If you’re craving a tangy and refreshing dessert, look no further than these delicious nut-free lemon bars. Bursting with zesty lemon flavor, these bars are the perfect balance of sweet and sour. Whether you have a nut allergy or simply prefer to avoid nuts, this recipe is the perfect solution.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • Pinch of salt

For the lemon filling:

  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, softened butter, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to mix the ingredients until they resemble coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Make sure to firmly press it down to create a solid base for the lemon filling.
  4. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until it turns a light golden brown.

Step 2: Making the Lemon Filling

  1. While the crust is baking, prepare the lemon filling. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and flour until well combined.
  2. Add the eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest to the sugar mixture. Whisk everything together until smooth and creamy.
  3. Once the crust is done baking, remove it from the oven and pour the lemon filling over the hot crust.
  4. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and no longer jiggles when gently shaken.

Step 3: Cooling and Serving

  1. Once the lemon bars are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan.
  2. Once cooled, carefully lift the bars out of the pan using the edges of the parchment paper. Place them on a cutting board and dust the top with powdered sugar.
  3. Using a sharp knife, cut the bars into squares or rectangles. The size is up to you, but smaller pieces are perfect for serving at parties or as bite-sized treats.
  4. Serve these tangy and delicious nut-free lemon bars as is, or with a dollop of whipped cream for added indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of lemon flavor, add a tablespoon of lemon zest to the crust mixture.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, strain the lemon filling before pouring it onto the crust to remove any pulp or zest.
  • For a decorative touch, dust the bars with powdered sugar in a fun pattern using a stencil or cookie cutter.
  • Feel free to experiment with different citrus fruits, such as lime or orange, to create unique variations of these bars.

Enjoy these scrumptious nut-free lemon bars for a tangy treat that will satisfy your sweet tooth. The combination of zesty lemon flavor and buttery crust is sure to delight your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply treating yourself, these bars are a crowd-pleaser. So go ahead, indulge in this tangy dessert and savor every bite!

Note: Always remember to check the ingredient labels of any store-bought products to ensure they are nut-free, as cross-contamination can occur.
