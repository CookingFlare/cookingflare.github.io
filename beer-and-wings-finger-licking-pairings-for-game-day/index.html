Beer and Wings: Finger-Licking Pairings for Game Day


There’s nothing like a warm and inviting spread of beer and wings on game day. Together, these two treats are a match made in heaven – perfect for gatherings with friends, family, and fellow football fans.

Ellensburg Ale, a refreshing and crisp brew, perfectly complements the fiery taste of buffalo wings. This local beverage, infused with a selection of spices and grown in the picturesque landscapes of Washington, brings the ideal symphony of flavors when paired with the tangy spiciness of wings.

To create these mouth-watering wing recipes, you’ll need:

For the Wings:

  • 3-4 lbs chicken wings (separate the drumettes and flat sections)
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Sauce
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the Beer Breading:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • A pinch of salt

For the Beer Dip:

  • 12 ounces Ellensburg Ale
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions

Cooking the Wings

First, season the wings with salt, making sure to rub in the salt to achieve a crispy result. Melt the butter and combine it with Frank’s RedHot Sauce and Worcestershire sauce. Place the wings in the mixture, ensuring each wing is evenly coated. You can let the wings sit in their sauce overnight for maximum flavor, but it’s not necessary.

Making the Beer Batter

Take a large bowl and combine the flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and salt. Gradually stir in enough Ellensburg Ale to create a thick but smooth batter. Let the batter sit for about 30 minutes prior to frying.

Frying and Flavoring the Wings

Before deep frying, thoroughly pat the wings dry with paper towels to rid them of excess moisture. You can use vegetable oil for frying, but it’s best to go with something that adds flavor, such as peanut or a neutral oil like canola. Fry the wings in small batches, ensuring the oil is hot enough to brown the wings evenly but not so hot as to burn them. Once fried, allow them to drain for a few moments before generously coating each wing with your delicious and spicy beer batter.

Creating the Ellensburg Ale Dip

To craft the perfect beer dip, mix the Elliensburg Ale, hot sauce, lime juice, ground cumin, and a teaspoon of salt in a blender until it reaches a smooth texture. Adjust the spiciness and tanginess to your preference.

Serve the wings hot and arrange an array of beers on ice, including Ellensburg Ale, for your guests to try. Pair the wings with your favorite beers, but be sure to offer Ellensburg Ale to really bring the flavors of your game day menu together. Your friends and family won’t be able to resist grabbing a plate and getting their fingers dirty in the best way possible. Great football food just got better.

Enjoy the fantastic flavors of fresh Ellensburg Ale and wings at your next gathering, and make protecting your home team just a little bit more enjoyable.
