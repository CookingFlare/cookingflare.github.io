Classic Pork Carnitas Burritos Recipe


Few dishes can match the comforting satisfaction of a well-made burrito. Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering, planning a weeknight dinner, or simply craving a hearty meal, pork carnitas burritos offer a symphony of flavors wrapped neatly in a soft flour tortilla. In this recipe post, you’ll discover how to craft classic pork carnitas burritos at home, infused with bold spices, rich textures, and the unmistakable tenderness of slow-cooked pork.

Why Choose Pork Carnitas for Burritos?

Pork carnitas, which translates to “little meats” in Spanish, is a traditional Mexican dish that’s beloved for its rich, juicy flavor and crispy edges. Cooking pork carnitas involves slow roasting or braising to achieve its tender texture, followed by a quick crisping process to add depth to every bite. The result? A star ingredient that stands out in burritos, complementing the other fresh fillings perfectly.

Whether you’re crafting a burrito for a quick dinner or elevating it for a weekend feast, pork carnitas burritos shine as a meal that’s both versatile and satisfying. Plus, they are the perfect way to introduce traditional Mexican flavors to your family or guests without adding complexity to your cooking time.

Ingredients for Pork Carnitas Burritos

The success of this recipe lies in preparing high-quality, flavorful ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to make the burritos:

For the Pork Carnitas:

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder (also called pork butt) trimmed of excess fat
  • 3 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Juice of 2 oranges
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • ½ cup chicken broth

For the Burrito Assembly:

  • Large flour tortillas (ensure they’re soft and pliable for easy rolling)
  • Cooked Mexican rice
  • Black or pinto beans
  • Fresh pico de gallo (or chopped tomatoes, onions, and cilantro)
  • Shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
  • Guacamole
  • Sour cream
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Pork Carnitas

The foundation of your burrito begins with perfectly cooked carnitas. This process takes time but guarantees an authentic and flavorful result.

  1. Season the Pork: Start by rubbing the pork shoulder with cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Ensure the spice mix is evenly spread across all sides of the pork for maximum flavor.

  2. Braise the Meat: Place the seasoned pork in a large Dutch oven or slow cooker. Add the orange juice, lime juice, garlic, onion, and chicken broth. Cover and cook on low heat for 6-8 hours if using a slow cooker, or simmer on the stovetop for 3-4 hours. The pork should become so tender that it easily falls apart when shredded.

  3. Crisp the Carnitas: Once the pork is fully cooked, shred it using two forks. To achieve the traditional crispy edges, spread the shredded pork onto a baking sheet and broil for 5-7 minutes. Alternatively, you can sear the meat on a hot skillet with a drizzle of oil until the edges become golden and crispy.

2. Prepare the Burrito Filling Components

While the pork is roasting, you can prepare the fillings for your burrito:

  • Cook the Mexican rice and beans until they’re tender. Set aside to cool slightly.
  • Chop fresh ingredients for your pico de gallo and guacamole, ensuring they’re vibrant and flavorful.
  • Finely shred the cheese and keep sour cream chilled until ready to serve.

3. Warm Up the Tortillas

One crucial step to ensuring your burritos are easy to assemble is warming up the tortillas. Heat them in a skillet over medium heat for 30 seconds per side or wrap them in foil and bake in a 350°F oven for about 5 minutes.

How to Assemble Your Burrito

  1. Layer the Ingredients: Lay a warm tortilla flat on your countertop. Start with a spoonful of Mexican rice in the center, followed by the beans. Add a generous helping of crispy pork carnitas on top. Sprinkle shredded cheese for a creamy touch, then add pico de gallo, guacamole, and sour cream for flavor-packed layers.

  2. Fold and Roll: To create the perfect burrito, fold in the sides of the tortilla over the fillings and roll it up tightly from the bottom. The goal is to create a seamless wrap that holds all the ingredients together.

  3. Serve Warm: Serve your burrito immediately or wrap it in foil to keep warm. Add a sprinkle of cilantro on top or pair the burritos with a side of salsa verde for dipping.

Pro Tips for Perfect Pork Carnitas Burritos

  1. Keep the Tortillas Fresh: Always use fresh tortillas for burritos that taste authentic. If possible, purchase handmade flour tortillas from a local bakery or make your own at home.

  2. Balance Textures: The secret to any great burrito is achieving balance—creamy guacamole, crispy pork, melty cheese, and soft rice create an irresistible combination of textures. Adjust the ratio of fillings based on your preferences.

  3. Customize the Heat Level: For burritos with more spice, add thin slices of jalapeños or drizzle hot sauce over the carnitas before rolling them up. Alternatively, use mild chili powders and peppers to keep the dish kid-friendly.

Serving and Storing Leftovers

Serve pork carnitas burritos as they are or alongside a simple side salad. If you’re hosting a party, consider setting up a DIY burrito station with toppings and fillings for a fun interactive meal.

Pork carnitas store beautifully in the fridge or freezer. Keep them in an airtight container for up to 4 days in the refrigerator, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat them gently on the stovetop with a splash of broth to maintain moisture.

Bringing It All Together

Classic pork carnitas burritos epitomize comfort food at its finest. The flavorful pork, paired with vibrant fillings, creates a meal that’s both hearty and customizable. Whether for lunch, dinner, or even a party centerpiece, these burritos are bound to impress. Make this recipe once, and it’ll quickly find its way into your regular meal rotation.

Elevate your cooking game by trying this tried-and-true recipe—it’s a celebration of bold Mexican flavors wrapped in a soft tortilla. Dig in and enjoy every bite!
