Vegan Break-Up Braises: 15 Hearty and Slow-Cooked Plant-Based Recipes


A breakup doesn’t have to mean subsisting on microwave popcorn and sad glasses of wine. It’s time to reclaim your power, embrace self-love, and turn a slow-cooker or Dutch oven into your new BFF with these luscious, vegan break-up braises. In this heart-healing collection of recipes, you’ll learn how to amp up comfort food while getting the love and compassion we all deserve. From hearty, satisfying meals to an intimate affair with your kitchen, stick with us and start healing one pot at a time.

  1. Lentil Shepherd’s Pie Spice up the classic shepherd’s pie with earthy lentils and lashings of fluffy mashed potato. This wholesome, plant-based preparation warms the soul while leaving carnivores green with envy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup green lentils
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • Vegan butter and plant-based milk, for the mash

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until the vegetables are soft and fragrant.

  2. Add the lentils, garlic, tomato paste, smoked paprika, cumin, and thyme. Stir to combine and cook for a minute.

  3. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until it is reduced by half.

  4. Add the vegetable broth and a pinch of salt and pepper. Bring it to a boil, then decrease the heat, cover, and let it simmer for about 45 minutes or until the lentils are tender.

  5. While that cooks, boil the potatoes until tender. Drain well and mash with vegan butter and a splash of plant-based milk. Season with salt and pepper.

  6. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Transfer the lentil mixture to a baking dish, top it with the mashed potatoes, and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.

  7. Crispy Tofu and Vegetable Stew

You’ll find yourself coming back to this stew for its addictive combination of textures and flavors. Crispy tofu chunks, tender vegetables, and a velvety broth - this one’s a hit at any table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block extra firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chopped mushrooms
  • 1 cup chopped potatoes
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped broccoli
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Sesame seeds and green onions, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes and pat dry with paper towels. Coat with cornstarch and soy sauce, shaking off the excess.

  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Sear the tofu on all sides until crispy and golden. Remove and set aside.

  3. In the same pan, sauté the onion and garlic until softened. Add the mushrooms, potatoes, and carrots. Cook for a few more minutes before adding the broccoli.

  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, tamari or soy sauce, and maple syrup. Bring it to a simmer, then return the tofu to the pan.

  5. Let it cook for another 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the tofu is fully cooked. Serve with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

  6. Hearty Tomato and Red Lentil Stew

A steaming bowl of this fragrant stew with aromatic spices, tender lentils, and juicy tomatoes will remind you that love and comfort know no bounds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup spinach
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Sliced green onions, for serving
  • Vegan yogurt, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened.
  2. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, cumin, coriander, turmeric, smoked paprika, and cinnamon. Bring it to a boil, then decrease the heat and cover. Let it simmer for about 25 minutes or until the lentils are tender.
  3. Stir in the spinach and cook until wilted. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper according to your taste.
  4. Serve hot with sliced green onions and a dollop of vegan yogurt if desired.
