Apple Cinnamon Bread Pudding: A Deliciously Comforting Dessert


When it comes to desserts that evoke feelings of warmth and nostalgia, few dishes can compete with apple cinnamon bread pudding. This delightful treat combines the rich flavors of sweet apples, warm cinnamon, and soft, buttery bread, creating a dessert that is both comforting and satisfying. Perfect for family gatherings, holiday celebrations, or simply as a sweet indulgence after dinner, apple cinnamon bread pudding is a dish that will leave your taste buds dancing.

The Origins of Bread Pudding

Bread pudding has a rich history that dates back to the 13th century, originating as a way to use up stale bread. This frugal yet delicious dessert has evolved over the centuries, with various cultures adding their unique twists. The basic premise remains the same: stale bread soaked in a custard mixture, baked until golden and set. However, the addition of fruits, spices, and other ingredients has transformed it into a beloved dessert worldwide.

Why Choose Apple Cinnamon Bread Pudding?

Apple cinnamon bread pudding is a celebration of flavors and textures. The sweet, tender apples paired with the warm, aromatic cinnamon create a comforting experience that evokes the essence of fall. Additionally, this dessert is incredibly versatile—ideal for breakfast, brunch, or dessert. It can be served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, drizzled with caramel sauce, or enjoyed plain for a lighter option.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this delicious apple cinnamon bread pudding, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Day-old bread: French bread, challah, or brioche work beautifully for this recipe. Using stale bread helps absorb the custard without becoming overly soggy.

  • Apples: Use a mix of sweet and tart apples, such as Granny Smith and Honeycrisp, for a balanced flavor. Make sure to peel, core, and chop them into bite-sized pieces.

  • Eggs: These act as the binding agent in the custard, creating a creamy texture.

  • Milk and cream: A combination of whole milk and heavy cream adds richness to the pudding.

  • Sugar: Granulated sugar sweetens the custard, while brown sugar can add an extra depth of flavor.

  • Cinnamon and nutmeg: These warm spices enhance the apple flavor and create a cozy aroma during baking.

  • Vanilla extract: A splash of vanilla elevates the overall taste of the pudding.

  • Butter: Melted butter adds richness and helps create a golden crust.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making apple cinnamon bread pudding.

Step 1: Prepare the Bread

Start by cutting your day-old bread into cubes, about 1-inch in size. If you don’t have stale bread, you can dry fresh bread in a low oven (around 250°F) for about 15-20 minutes until it’s slightly crispy but not browned. Place the bread cubes in a large mixing bowl and set aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Apples

In a skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped apples, along with a tablespoon of sugar and a sprinkle of cinnamon. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until the apples are tender but not mushy. Remove from heat and let them cool slightly before adding them to the bread.

Step 3: Make the Custard Mixture

In a separate bowl, whisk together 4 eggs, 2 cups of milk, and 1 cup of heavy cream until well combined. Add 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, 1 teaspoon of cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg. Mix until the sugar is dissolved and everything is well incorporated.

Step 4: Combine the Ingredients

Pour the custard mixture over the bread cubes, ensuring that all the bread is soaked evenly. Gently fold in the sautéed apples, being careful not to break them apart. Allow the mixture to sit for about 15-20 minutes, giving the bread time to absorb the custard.

Step 5: Bake the Pudding

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter. Pour the bread pudding mixture into the prepared dish, spreading it evenly. Drizzle the top with melted butter for a golden crust. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the pudding is set and the top is golden brown.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the bread pudding from the oven and allow it to cool for about 10 minutes before serving. This resting time helps the pudding set further, making it easier to cut into squares. Serve warm, topped with whipped cream, vanilla ice cream, or a drizzle of caramel sauce.

Tips for the Perfect Apple Cinnamon Bread Pudding

  1. Use the Right Bread: Stale bread is key to a perfect pudding. Look for a bread that is slightly dry and crusty. Avoid using soft sandwich bread, as it can become mushy.

  2. Customize Your Apples: Experiment with different apple varieties to find your perfect flavor combination. Mixing sweet and tart apples adds a delightful complexity.

  3. Add Nuts or Raisins: For extra texture and flavor, consider adding chopped pecans, walnuts, or raisins to the mixture before baking.

  4. Make It Ahead: This dessert can be assembled a day in advance and stored in the refrigerator. Just pop it in the oven when you’re ready to bake.

  5. Perfect Pairings: Pair your apple cinnamon bread pudding with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream for an indulgent treat.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have any leftovers (though they’re unlikely to last long), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat individual portions in the microwave or warm them in the oven at 350°F until heated through. Add a splash of milk or cream while reheating to restore some moisture.

Conclusion

Apple cinnamon bread pudding is more than just a dessert; it’s a warm embrace in a bowl that brings back fond memories and creates new ones. Perfect for any occasion, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite. Whether enjoyed on a chilly evening or as a sweet surprise after dinner, this comforting dessert is as delightful to make as it is to eat. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and indulge in the deliciousness of homemade apple cinnamon bread pudding—you won’t regret it!
