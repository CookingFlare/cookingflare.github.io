How to Make Korean Rice Cakes (Tteok) from Scratch


Tteok, or Korean rice cakes, are a cherished staple in Korean cuisine. These chewy, supple bites of joy are used in celebratory dishes and everyday meals alike. From savory tteokbokki to sweet tteok covered in powdered soybean flour, tteok plays a meaningful role in Korean cultural traditions and gastronomy. If you’ve ever marveled at its soft, chewy texture or enjoyed its mild, versatile taste, you’re not alone. The best part? You can make these delightful rice cakes right at home, from scratch. Let’s dive into the detailed step-by-step process so you can create authentic Korean tteok in your kitchen.

Understanding Korean Tteok

Before we get into the recipe, let’s first explore the basics of making rice cakes. Korean tteok can be categorized into several types depending on ingredients, preparation methods, and the occasion they’re associated with. The foundational ingredient, though, is almost always rice—either glutinous sweet rice (chapssal) or non-glutinous rice (mepssal). Glutinous rice produces softer, stickier tteok, while non-glutinous rice yields a firmer texture.

Traditionally, tteok was made using a millstone to grind soaked rice, then steamed and pounded for that signature chewy elasticity. Today, you can recreate the magic, even without the heavy stone equipment. Here, you’ll find a modernized yet authentic approach that will deliver incredible results.

Ingredients for Korean Rice Cakes

To make Korean rice cakes from scratch, you only need a handful of ingredients, but the quality of these ingredients will determine the texture and flavor of your tteok. Here’s what you’ll need:

Essential Ingredients:

  • Rice Flour (Sweet or Regular): Choose finely milled Korean rice flour (mepssal garu or chapssal garu) for the best outcome.
  • Water: Warm water works best for mixing the rice flour into dough.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt enhances flavor without overwhelming the natural taste of the rice.

Optional Ingredients for Variety:

  • Sugar or Ground Sweeteners (Optional for sweeter varieties of tteok)
  • Toppings or Fillings: Such as red bean paste, nuts, dried fruit, or sesame seeds.
  • Food Coloring or Natural Dyes: Like powdered mugwort (ssuk garu) for herbal green tteok or pumpkin puree for natural orange tones.

Kitchen Tools Needed

Traditional Korean families often steam their tteok using bamboo steamers, but modern-day tools suffice perfectly. For this process, gather the following:

  • Mixing Bowls: For combining rice flour and water.
  • Fine Mesh Sieve: To sift rice flour for a smoother texture.
  • Steamer with a Lid: A regular steamer works well, but a bamboo or stovetop-style steamer is ideal for authenticity.
  • Parchment Paper or Muslin Cloth: To line the steamer and prevent sticking.
  • Mixing Spoon or Rubber Spatula
  • Cutting Board or Flat Surface: For shaping rice cakes.
  • Knife or Dough Cutter: To cut tteok into desired shapes.

Optional tools include food processors (if milling rice flour at home) or a pestle for gentle pounding after steaming.

How to Make Korean Rice Cakes

This recipe will walk you through three essential steps: preparing the rice dough, steaming, and shaping the tteok. Follow these instructions, and you’ll have perfectly chewy and satisfying rice cakes.

Step 1: Preparing the Rice Dough

  1. Sift the Rice Flour
    Start by sifting your rice flour into a large mixing bowl. This removes clumps and ensures a fine consistency for a smoother dough.

  2. Hydrate the Flour
    Gradually add warm water to the flour while mixing continuously with a spoon or your hands. Begin with small amounts, incorporating more water as needed until the dough holds together when pinched. It should be moist but not dripping wet.

  3. Season Lightly
    Add a small pinch of salt to the mixture. If making sweet tteok, sprinkle in sugar or your chosen sweetener for a touch of sweetness. Knead gently until the dough reaches a pliable consistency.

  4. Allow Resting Time
    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes. This allows the rice flour to fully hydrate.

Step 2: Steaming the Dough

  1. Prepare Your Steamer
    Line the bottom of your steamer with parchment paper or a muslin cloth. If using parchment paper, poke a few small holes to allow steam to circulate.

  2. Transfer the Dough
    Place the kneaded rice dough onto the prepared steamer tray, flattening it into an even layer.

  3. Steam Thoroughly
    Cover the steamer with a lid and steam the dough over medium-high heat for 25–30 minutes. The dough should be cooked through, firm, and slightly translucent.

  4. Check Doneness
    To test if your dough is ready, poke it with a toothpick; it should come out clean. If necessary, add a few more minutes of steaming.

Step 3: Shaping and Finishing the Rice Cakes

  1. Pound the Dough (Optional)
    After steaming, some traditional Korean methods involve gently pounding the dough to enhance its elasticity. You can use a large pestle or a clean heavy object for this step.

  2. Shape the Tteok
    Sprinkle a flat surface with a little cornstarch or leftover rice flour to prevent sticking. Transfer the steamed dough and knead it while it’s still warm. Cut into small pieces and shape them into cylinders, discs, or other desired forms.

  3. Personalize the Toppings or Fillings
    If sweet tteok is your goal, consider filling pieces with red bean paste or rolling them in roasted soybean flour (kinako). For savory tteok, coat lightly with sesame oil for added richness.

Serving and Enjoying Homemade Tteok

Your freshly made tteok is now ready to enjoy! They’re incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways:

  • Use plain, unsweetened pieces for tteokbokki, the iconic spicy rice cake dish.
  • Serve sweet varieties as a snack or dessert with green tea.
  • Celebrate holidays like Seollal (Korean Lunar New Year) by using your tteok in tteokguk (rice cake soup).
  • Share with family and friends for birthdays or other festive occasions.

Tips for Success

  • Consistency is Key: If the dough feels too dry, add a minimal amount of water and knead again. If it’s overly sticky, incorporate a touch of extra rice flour.
  • Store Properly: Rice cakes can dry out quickly. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or store them in an airtight container. They freeze well and can be reheated by steaming.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Try adding fruits, cocoa powder, or matcha for unique variations of tteok.

Making Korean rice cakes from scratch is a rewarding process that connects you to the heart of Korean culinary tradition. With just a few simple ingredients and some hands-on effort, you can create tteok that rivals those served in Korean households and markets. Whether sweet, savory, or plain, these soft, chewy rice cakes are sure to delight your taste buds and bring a bit of Korea into your kitchen. So go ahead—get steaming and start shaping your way to authentic tteok today!
