Savory Carnitas: A Mexican Delicacy for Any Occasion


Carnitas, which translates to “little meats” in Spanish, is a beloved Mexican dish that has gained worldwide popularity for its tender, flavorful, and aromatic pork. This savory delicacy is often served at family gatherings, celebrations, and casual get-togethers, and is a staple in many Mexican households. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making authentic savory carnitas that will impress your friends and family.

The History of Carnitas

Carnitas has its roots in the colonial era, when Spanish conquistadors introduced pigs to Mexico. The indigenous people of Mexico, in turn, developed their own unique cooking techniques, incorporating local ingredients and spices to create a distinct flavor profile. Over time, carnitas became a staple in many Mexican regions, with each region adding its own twist to the recipe.

The Magic of Pork Shoulder

Pork shoulder, also known as pork butt or Boston butt, is the key to making tender and juicy carnitas. This cut of meat is rich in connective tissue, which breaks down during the cooking process, resulting in a tender and flavorful dish. The pork shoulder is typically slow-cooked in its own lard or oil, which adds a rich, savory flavor to the dish.

The Recipe

To make authentic savory carnitas, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
  • 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Warm tortillas, for serving
  • Sliced radishes, for serving
  • Chopped onions, for serving
  • Sliced avocado, for serving
  • Sour cream or Mexican crema, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the lard or oil over medium heat. Add the pork chunks and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the pork from the pot and set aside.
  2. Add the orange and lime juice, garlic, oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the browned pork back to the pot, cover, and transfer to the oven. Braise the pork for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, or until it’s tender and easily shreds with a fork.
  4. Remove the pot from the oven and let it cool slightly. Using two forks, shred the pork into bite-sized pieces.
  5. Warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds.
  6. Assemble the carnitas by spooning the shredded pork onto a warmed tortilla, followed by a sprinkle of cilantro, sliced radishes, chopped onions, sliced avocado, and a dollop of sour cream or Mexican crema.

Tips and Variations

  • For a crispy exterior, pan-fry the shredded pork in a little oil until golden brown.
  • Add some heat to your carnitas by incorporating diced jalapeños or serrano peppers into the cooking liquid.
  • Experiment with different types of citrus juice, such as grapefruit or lemon, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Serve the carnitas with a side of spicy salsa, pickled onions, or grilled vegetables for added flavor and texture.

Conclusion

Savory carnitas is a dish that’s sure to impress your friends and family with its tender, flavorful, and aromatic pork. By following this recipe and incorporating your own twists and variations, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a carnitas master. So go ahead, gather your loved ones around the table, and indulge in the rich flavors of Mexico’s beloved national dish.
