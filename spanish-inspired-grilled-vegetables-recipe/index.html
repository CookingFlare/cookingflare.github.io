Spanish Inspired Grilled Vegetables Recipe


Grilled vegetables are a delightful addition to any meal, bursting with flavor, color, and nutrients. This Spanish-inspired grilled vegetables recipe showcases the vibrant produce of the Mediterranean, bringing together textures and tastes that will tantalize your taste buds. Perfect for a summer barbecue or a cozy dinner at home, these grilled vegetables are a versatile side dish that pairs beautifully with various main courses. Let’s dive into the steps to create this delicious dish that embodies the spirit of Spanish cuisine.

Ingredients

To prepare the Spanish-inspired grilled vegetables, you will need the following ingredients:

Fresh Vegetables

  • 1 medium eggplant: Cut into thick slices, about 1 inch wide.
  • 1 zucchini: Sliced into rounds or half-moons.
  • 1 red bell pepper: Cored, seeded, and cut into large chunks.
  • 1 yellow bell pepper: Cored, seeded, and cut into large chunks.
  • 1 red onion: Cut into thick wedges.
  • 8 oz. cherry tomatoes: Leave them whole for roasting.

Marinade

  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil: This will enhance the flavor and help with grilling.
  • 3 cloves garlic: Minced for a robust flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika: Adds that signature Spanish flair.
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar: To balance the flavors.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano: A classic herb in Spanish cooking.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste, always essential for seasoning.

Garnish

  • Fresh parsley: Chopped, for a pop of color and freshness.
  • Lemon wedges: For serving, to add a zesty finish.

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Preparing the Vegetables

Start by washing all your vegetables thoroughly. The freshness of the produce is key to the success of this dish. After washing, dry them with a clean kitchen towel to remove excess moisture, which can interfere with grilling.

  1. Eggplant: Slice the eggplant into thick rounds. To reduce bitterness, you may choose to salt the slices and let them sit for about 30 minutes. Rinse and pat dry before cooking.

  2. Zucchini: Cut the zucchini into rounds or half-moons, ensuring even thickness for consistent cooking.

  3. Bell Peppers: Cut the red and yellow bell peppers into large pieces. This will make them easier to handle on the grill.

  4. Red Onion: Cut the onion into thick wedges, keeping the layers intact to avoid them falling apart during grilling.

  5. Cherry Tomatoes: These can remain whole, bringing a burst of sweetness to the dish.

Step 2: Making the Marinade

In a medium bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, smoked paprika, balsamic vinegar, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. This marinade not only adds flavor but also helps to prevent the vegetables from sticking to the grill.

Step 3: Marinating the Vegetables

Place all the sliced vegetables in a large mixing bowl or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the vegetables, ensuring they are well coated. Allow the vegetables to marinate for at least 30 minutes. For deeper flavor, consider marinating them in the refrigerator for a few hours or even overnight.

Step 4: Preheating the Grill

While the vegetables are marinating, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill, wait until the coals are covered with white ash. For a gas grill, give it about 10-15 minutes to heat up properly.

Step 5: Grilling the Vegetables

Once your grill is hot, it’s time to cook the vegetables. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Oil the Grill Grates: To prevent sticking, lightly oil the grill grates using a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil.

  2. Grilling: Start with the eggplant and onion, as they take longer to cook. Place them directly on the grill. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they have nice grill marks and are tender.

  3. Adding the Zucchini and Bell Peppers: After the eggplant and onion have cooked for a few minutes, add the zucchini and bell peppers to the grill. These vegetables will need about 2-3 minutes per side.

  4. Cherry Tomatoes: Lastly, add the cherry tomatoes to a grill basket or skewers to prevent them from falling through the grates. Grill for about 2-3 minutes, until they are blistered and soft.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

Once all the vegetables are grilled to perfection, remove them from the grill and transfer them to a serving platter. Drizzle a bit more olive oil on top if desired, and sprinkle with freshly chopped parsley for a touch of color. Serve with lemon wedges on the side to squeeze over the vegetables just before eating.

Tips for the Best Grilled Vegetables

  • Choose Seasonal Vegetables: The best grilled vegetables come from seasonal produce. Visit local farmers’ markets to find the freshest ingredients.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Grill: Give each vegetable enough space on the grill for even cooking. Overcrowding can lead to steaming rather than grilling.
  • Experiment with Vegetables: Feel free to mix in other vegetables like asparagus, mushrooms, or corn for variety.
  • Serve at Room Temperature: Grilled vegetables can be served warm or at room temperature, making them a great make-ahead option for gatherings.

Pairing Suggestions

These Spanish-inspired grilled vegetables are incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways. Here are some ideas for pairing:

  • As a Side Dish: Serve alongside grilled meats like chicken, pork, or fish for a complete meal.
  • In a Salad: Toss the grilled vegetables with mixed greens, olives, and feta cheese for a Mediterranean salad.
  • On a Sandwich: Layer them in a sandwich with hummus or aioli for a tasty vegetarian option.
  • With Quinoa or Couscous: Serve over a bed of quinoa or couscous for a hearty, healthy meal.

Conclusion

This Spanish-inspired grilled vegetables recipe is a celebration of flavor, color, and nutrition. By using fresh ingredients and a simple marinade, you can create a dish that not only enhances your meals but also brings a touch of Mediterranean flair to your table. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or looking for a healthy side dish, these grilled vegetables are sure to impress. Enjoy the delightful combination of tastes and the joy of cooking with fresh produce!
