Seafood Sandwiches: A Mouthwatering Lineup for Your Next Getaway


There’s something special about a delicious sandwich, especially when it’s filled with fresh, succulent seafood. Why not elevate your next getaway by preparing one or two of these mouthwatering seafood sandwiches for a delightful meal? From classic selections to unique twists, these dishes will leave your taste buds tingling with excitement. Here is a fantastic collection of seafood sandwiches that you won’t soon forget.

1. Classic Shrimp Po’ Boy

A staple in the realm of seafood sandwiches, the shrimp po’ boy from Louisiana, USA, features succulent, battered, and fried shrimp smothered in tangy remoulade sauce, then tucked between two crispy slices of French bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup beer
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 4 French rolls
  • Remoulade sauce, for serving
  • Lettuce, tomatoes, and pickles, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the shrimp by dredging in a mixture of flour, paprika, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, and salt. Then, shake off any excess and dip into a mixture of eggs and beer.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy pan, then carefully drop in the shrimp to cook until golden brown. Work in batches and set aside on paper towels to drain.
  3. Split French rolls and crisp them up in the fryer or oven to achieve a golden exterior.
  4. For the perfect balance of flavors, spread a generous amount of remoulade sauce onto the roll’s interior. Then, pack the shrimp sandwich with lettuce, tomatoes, pickles, and a few pieces of fried shrimp.
  5. Serve immediately with additional remoulade sauce on the side, and dig into this New Orleans-inspired delight.

2. Lobster Roll

The quintessential seafood sandwich from New England, the lobster roll, boasts generous chunks of succulent lobster meat, infused with a slight touch of mayonnaise and served on a warm, fresh bun.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cooked lobster meat (about 1-1.5 tails)
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 soft hamburger buns
  • Fresh lemon wedges and clarified butter, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the cooked lobster meat with mayonnaise and gently fold until well-coated.
  2. Split your hamburger buns and lightly toast them to provide a warm, fresh base for the lobster.
  3. Pile the lobster mixture onto the buns, and be sure to pack the sandwich tightly. Serve with lemon wedges for squeezing over the top and a side of melted, clarified butter for dipping the sandwich before indulging.

3. Crab Cake Sandwich

Light, crispy, and flavorful crab cakes are a magnificent way to indulge in the delicate taste of crab without breaking the bank. The crab cake sandwich, originating in Maryland, USA, combines these delights with fresh and tangy ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jumbo lump crabmeat, picked over
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 mini brioche buns
  • Tarter sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C) and preparing a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Combine the crabmeat, mayonnaise, breadcrumbs, egg, bell pepper, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, and seasonings to form six evenly-sized patties. Place patties on the parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes, then transfer to a cooling rack and let sit for a couple of minutes before assembling the sandwiches.
  4. For a warm and slightly crispy experience, briefly pan-fry the patties in a bit of butter or oil until golden brown.
  5. Generously spread tarter sauce onto the buns, then nestle the crab cake patties, and enjoy a taste of the Maryland coast right at home.

4. Grilled Tuna Melt

Upgrade your classic tuna sandwich with this delectable grilled version, bursting with smoky and savory flavors. The grilled tuna melt is an excellent choice for a quick weeknight meal or an effortless weekend affair.

Ingredients:

  • 1 (5-ounce) can tuna in water, drained (or cooked fresh tuna, broken into chunks)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 slices whole-grain bread
  • 2 slices of Swiss cheese
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped red onion
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Begin by heating a grill pan over medium heat. Drizzle with olive oil.
  2. Combine the tuna, capers, red onion, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl, then gently fold to mix. Fold in the parsley, giving it a burst of freshness.
  3. Place one slice of bread on the grill, then layer on the tuna mixture and Swiss cheese. Top with the second slice of bread, press down, and let cook until the bottom slice is toasted and golden brown. Flip the sandwich, continue cooking until the cheese has begun to melt, and the bread is crispy and golden.
  4. Transfer to a cutting board, and slice the sandwich in half. Serve with a side of chips or a crisp salad for a balanced meal.

So, fire up your grill, heat your oil, and embark on a culinary adventure creating any of these scrumptious seafood sandwiches. The perfect pairing of flavors and textures will make for a memorable dining experience – unforgettable even after your getaway has come to a close. Happy cooking, and remember to share the love!
