Japanese Ramen Recipe with Pork and Soft-Boiled Egg


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in a steaming bowl of authentic Japanese ramen at some point. This iconic dish is a comforting and flavorful meal that has gained popularity worldwide. While there are various types of ramen, one of the most beloved and satisfying versions is the classic pork ramen with a soft-boiled egg. In this post, we’ll explore the art of creating this delectable dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

For the Broth:

  • 2 pounds pork neck bones
  • 1 pound chicken carcasses
  • 1 onion, peeled and halved
  • 1 head of garlic, halved horizontally
  • 6 slices of ginger
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 cup sake
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons salt

For the Pork Chashu:

  • 1 pound pork belly
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup sugar

For the Soft-Boiled Eggs:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1 cup water

For the Ramen:

  • 4 servings of fresh ramen noodles
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 2 sheets of nori (seaweed), halved

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F.
  2. Place the pork bones and chicken carcasses on a baking sheet and roast for 30 minutes until browned.
  3. In a large pot, add the roasted bones, onion, garlic, ginger, water, sake, mirin, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 6-8 hours. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve and discard the solids. Season with salt and set aside.

Step 2: Make the Pork Chashu

  1. Roll the pork belly and tie it with kitchen twine.
  2. In a pot, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Add the pork belly and enough water to cover it. Simmer for 2-3 hours until tender.
  3. Remove the pork belly from the pot and let it cool. Once cooled, slice it into thin pieces.

Step 3: Prepare the Soft-Boiled Eggs

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  2. Gently lower the eggs into the boiling water and cook for 6 1/2 minutes.
  3. Transfer the eggs to an ice water bath to stop the cooking process.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, mirin, and water. Bring to a simmer and remove from heat.
  5. Peel the eggs and place them in the soy sauce mixture to marinate for at least 2 hours, turning occasionally.

Step 4: Assemble the Ramen

  1. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions and divide them among four bowls.
  2. Ladle the prepared broth into each bowl.
  3. Arrange the pork chashu, soft-boiled egg, green onions, and nori on top of the noodles.

Serve the ramen immediately and enjoy the rich flavors and textures of this beloved Japanese dish. It’s a labor of love, but the end result is well worth the effort. Whether you’re seeking comfort on a chilly evening or simply craving a taste of Japan, this pork ramen with a soft-boiled egg is sure to satisfy your culinary desires.

In conclusion, mastering the art of preparing authentic Japanese ramen with pork and soft-boiled egg allows you to savor the delightful flavors of this beloved dish in the comfort of your own home. From the rich, savory broth to the tender pork chashu and perfectly cooked soft-boiled egg, each element of this recipe contributes to a truly satisfying dining experience. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that will transport you to the bustling ramen shops of Japan. Your taste buds will thank you!
