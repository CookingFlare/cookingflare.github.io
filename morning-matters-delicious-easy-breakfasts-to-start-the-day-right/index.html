Morning Matters: Delicious & Easy Breakfasts to Start the Day Right


Rise and shine! There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a tantalizing breakfast wafting through the air each morning. Not only does a hearty breakfast fuel your body and mind for the day ahead, but it can also be an enjoyable bonding experience for you and your loved ones. If you’re tired of scrounging for ideas, look no further. Today, we’ll explore a variety of delectable breakfast recipes that are not only easy to make but also impressive in presentation. Let’s dive in!

1. Baked Avocado and Egg

There’s something soothing about cracking open a sun-side-up egg - imagine doing so in a baked, buttery avocado? Yes, that dreamy breakfast can come true!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large ripe avocado
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon thinly sliced green onions
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Chili flakes, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°.
  2. Cut the avocado in half, lengthwise. Remove the pip, then arrange the avocado halves in a baking dish. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Crack an egg into each avocado half, then sprinkle the cheese over the top, followed by the feta and cilantro.
  4. Drizzle the avocado and egg mixture with olive oil and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly, the egg is cooked to your preference, and the avocado is soft and warm.
  5. Once ready, dollop sour cream over the avocado and egg, then garnish with green onions, chili flakes, and a drizzle of lime juice.

2. ABC Overnight Oats

Overnight oats combine the convenience of instant oats with the soothing flavors and health benefits of natural ingredients. They’re perfect for those mornings when you’re craving something nutritious yet don’t have the time to linger over breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup almond milk or preferred milk
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Fresh fruit (berries, bananas, etc.)
  • Chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds, etc.)
  • A dollop of Greek yogurt

Instructions:

  1. In a mason jar or an airtight container, combine the oats, almond milk, chia seeds, maple syrup, vanilla, and cinnamon. Mix until everything is well combined.
  2. Cover the jar and leave your oats in the fridge overnight (or for at least 6 hours).
  3. In the morning, remove the jar from the fridge. Give the oats a quick stir, then top with fresh fruit, nuts, and Greek yogurt.

3. Sweet Potato Pancakes

Want to have your cake and eat it too? In the morning? Try these flavorful and fluffy sweet potato pancakes that taste sweet and comforting just like your favorite dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium sweet potato
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour OR gluten-free flour (for gluten-free version)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Butter or oil (for the pan)
  • Maple syrup, whipped cream, and additional cinnamon for serving

Instructions:

  1. Start by cooking your sweet potato. Prick the sweet potato a few times with a fork, then microwave for about 5 minutes or until tender. Allow the sweet potato to cool, then peel and mash it with a fork.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together your dry ingredients (flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt).
  3. In another mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, mashed sweet potato, vanilla, and milk until well combined.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, and stir until just combined. Don’t overmix!
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet (or a skillet with a light layer of butter or oil) over medium heat. Drop 1/4 cup measure portions of the batter onto the skillet to form pancakes. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until nicely browned and cooked through.
  6. Serve your sweet potato pancakes with a drizzle of maple syrup, a dollop of whipped cream, and an extra sprinkle of cinnamon.

Breakfast is the meal that can set the stage for the rest of your day. The delicious and easy recipes here will help you start every day on a positive note. Give them a try, and share your morning delights with your loved ones - cheers to a lovely start to each day!
