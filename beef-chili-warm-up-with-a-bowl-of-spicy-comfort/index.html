Beef Chili: Warm Up with a Bowl of Spicy Comfort


When the weather turns chilly and you’re in need of a hearty and comforting meal, there’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of beef chili. This classic dish is packed with flavor, warmth, and just the right amount of spice to satisfy your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering or simply craving some cozy comfort food, this beef chili recipe is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of ground beef
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 2 cans of kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 can of tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Break it up into small pieces as it cooks. Once the beef is cooked through, remove any excess fat.

  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot with the ground beef. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic becomes fragrant.

  3. Stir in the diced bell peppers and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften.

  4. Add the kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and tomato sauce to the pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Sprinkle in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Adjust the amount of spices according to your preference for heat and flavor.

  6. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the chili cook for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld together. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  7. After the chili has simmered for an hour, taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you prefer a thicker chili, you can continue to cook it uncovered for an additional 30 minutes to reduce the liquid.

  8. Serve the beef chili hot, garnished with your favorite toppings such as shredded cheese, sour cream, chopped green onions, or diced avocado. Pair it with warm cornbread or tortilla chips for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smokier flavor, you can add a dash of liquid smoke to the chili while it simmers.
  • If you prefer a milder chili, reduce the amount of chili powder or use a mild chili powder variety.
  • To add some extra depth of flavor, you can also incorporate a small amount of dark chocolate or a splash of Worcestershire sauce.
  • This beef chili can easily be adapted to a slow cooker. Simply brown the ground beef and sauté the onions and garlic before transferring everything to a slow cooker. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.
  • Leftover chili can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or frozen for future meals.

Conclusion

There’s something incredibly satisfying about a bowl of beef chili. The combination of tender ground beef, aromatic spices, and rich tomato-based sauce creates a dish that warms you from the inside out. Whether you’re enjoying it alone on a cozy night or sharing it with friends and family, this beef chili recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. So go ahead, whip up a batch, and let the spicy comfort of beef chili warm you up.
