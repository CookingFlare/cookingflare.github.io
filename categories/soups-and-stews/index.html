Flavorful and Fragrant Thai Lemongrass Soup Recipes

When it comes to Thai cuisine, few dishes are as beloved and comforting as a steaming bowl of lemongrass soup. The delicate balance of tangy lemongrass, spicy chilies, and savory broth creates a symphony of flavors that is both soothing and invigorating. In this blog post, we’ll explore two distinct yet equally delightful Thai lemongrass soup recipes that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Bangkok. Classic Tom Yum Soup Ingredients: 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and chopped into 2-inch pieces 3-4 kaffir lime leaves 3-4 slices galangal 4-6 Thai bird’s eye chilies, lightly crushed 200g shrimp, cleaned and deveined 200g oyster mushrooms, torn into bite-sized pieces 3 tablespoons fish sauce 2 tablespoons lime juice 1 tablespoon tamarind paste 1 teaspoon sugar Fresh cilantro for garnish Instructions: In a pot, bring the broth to a gentle boil over medium heat. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Let the broth simmer for 5-7 minutes to infuse the flavors. Toss in the chilies, shrimp, and mushrooms. Cook until the shrimp turns pink and opaque. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, tamarind paste, and sugar. Adjust the seasoning to achieve the perfect balance of sour, salty, and sweet. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot and enjoy the aromatic goodness of this classic Tom Yum soup. Creamy Lemongrass Coconut Soup Ingredients: 2 cans coconut milk 4 cups vegetable broth 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and chopped into 2-inch pieces 1 red onion, finely chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon grated ginger 200g chicken breast, thinly sliced 200g tofu, cubed 1 red bell pepper, julienned 1 tablespoon soy sauce 1 tablespoon lime juice 2 teaspoons brown sugar Salt and pepper to taste Fresh basil for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, combine the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Add the lemongrass, red onion, garlic, and ginger. Let the flavors meld for 10-15 minutes. Introduce the sliced chicken, tofu, and red bell pepper into the pot. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink. Season the soup with soy sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to achieve a harmonious blend of flavors. Ladle the creamy lemongrass coconut soup into bowls and garnish with fresh basil. Indulge in the luscious creaminess of this Thai-inspired delight. Both of these Thai lemongrass soup recipes offer a delightful interplay of aromatic lemongrass, zesty lime, and a tantalizing combination of spices. Whether you opt for the classic Tom Yum or the creamy coconut version, these soups are sure to elevate your culinary repertoire and bring the exotic flavors of Thailand to your dining table.
