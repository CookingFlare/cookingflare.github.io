Top 10 Vegan and Vegetarian Recipes for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. While traditional Thanksgiving menus are often centered around turkey and meat-heavy dishes, there’s no reason why those who follow a vegan or vegetarian diet can’t enjoy a full, festive spread. Whether you’re hosting a plant-based dinner or just looking to add some diverse options to your table, we’ve curated a list of the top 10 vegan and vegetarian recipes that will delight your guests and make your Thanksgiving feast memorable.

1. Vegan Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squashes
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the acorn squashes in half and scoop out the seeds. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the squashes cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 35-40 minutes, or until tender.
  4. While the squash is roasting, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until translucent. Add the cooked quinoa, chopped pecans, dried cranberries, and maple syrup. Stir to combine and heat through.
  5. Once the squash is done, fill the centers with the quinoa stuffing mixture and return to the oven for another 10 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

These stuffed acorn squashes are not only a feast for the eyes but also bursting with flavors. The sweet and savory filling makes for a hearty, satisfying dish perfect for the Thanksgiving table.

2. Creamy Vegan Mushroom Gravy

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms (cremini or button mushrooms work best)
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes, until they release their moisture and become golden brown.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing it to brown slightly.
  4. Gradually pour in the vegetable broth, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Add the soy sauce and dried thyme.
  5. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes until the gravy thickens. Stir in the almond milk and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve warm over mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or any dish that could use a little extra savory richness.

This vegan mushroom gravy is rich and creamy, the perfect accompaniment for your mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or any other dish that traditionally calls for gravy. It’s simple, hearty, and packed with flavor.

3. Vegan Pumpkin Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium pumpkin, peeled and diced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and carrots. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the diced pumpkin, vegetable broth, ginger, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer. Let it cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the pumpkin is tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender or a regular blender to purée the soup until smooth.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with roasted pumpkin seeds or fresh herbs.

This vegan pumpkin soup is the epitome of fall flavors. It’s creamy, comforting, and a wonderful starter for your Thanksgiving meal.

4. Vegan Sweet Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup mini marshmallows (vegan, if desired)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in a large pot for about 15 minutes, or until fork-tender. Drain and mash them in a large bowl.
  3. Stir in the maple syrup, coconut milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.
  4. Transfer the mashed sweet potatoes into a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the top with chopped pecans and vegan marshmallows (if using).
  5. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden and the casserole is heated through.

This vegan sweet potato casserole is a perfect combination of creamy sweetness and crunchy texture. It’s a delicious alternative to the traditional casserole topped with butter and eggs.

5. Vegan Lentil Loaf

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups cooked green or brown lentils
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (use gluten-free if necessary)
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mash the cooked lentils until most of them are broken up, but some whole lentils remain for texture.
  3. Add the breadcrumbs, grated carrots, onion, garlic, tomato paste, soy sauce, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a greased loaf pan and press it down to form a loaf shape.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden and slightly crispy.
  6. Allow the loaf to rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

This lentil loaf is a hearty and satisfying main dish, perfect for those who want a plant-based alternative to turkey. The tomato paste and soy sauce give it a savory flavor that is complemented by the earthy lentils.

6. Vegan Cranberry Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the cranberries, orange juice, maple syrup, cinnamon stick, ginger, and cloves.
  2. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  3. Remove the cinnamon stick and allow the cranberry sauce to cool before serving.

This simple vegan cranberry sauce is a tangy, sweet complement to your Thanksgiving meal. The spices add a nice depth of flavor, and the orange juice brings a fresh twist to the traditional recipe.

7. Vegan Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  4. While the Brussels sprouts are roasting, combine the balsamic vinegar and maple syrup in a small saucepan. Simmer over low heat for about 5 minutes, until it thickens slightly.
  5. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the roasted Brussels sprouts before serving.

These Brussels sprouts are crispy and caramelized, with a perfect balance of savory and sweet thanks to the balsamic glaze. They make a great side dish for any holiday table.

8. Vegan Mac and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni (use gluten-free if necessary)
  • 1/2 cup raw cashews, soaked
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, mustard, turmeric, garlic powder, almond milk, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the cheese sauce over the cooked macaroni and stir until well coated.
  4. Serve immediately, or for a baked version, transfer to a baking dish, sprinkle with breadcrumbs, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes.

This vegan mac and cheese is creamy, cheesy, and the perfect comfort food for Thanksgiving. It’s a crowd-pleaser that both vegans and non-vegans will love.

9. Vegan Cauliflower Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes and cauliflower in a large pot of water until tender, about 15 minutes.
  2. Drain the vegetables and return them to the pot. Add the garlic, olive oil, and almond milk.
  3. Mash the potatoes and cauliflower together until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

These cauliflower mashed potatoes are a lighter alternative to traditional mashed potatoes but just as creamy and delicious. The cauliflower adds a subtle depth of flavor that pairs perfectly with the garlic and olive oil.

10. Vegan Apple Crisp

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toss the apple slices with lemon juice and maple syrup, then transfer to a greased baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  4. Stir in the melted coconut oil until the mixture is crumbly.
  5. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the apples are tender.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream if desired.

This vegan apple crisp is the perfect dessert to end your Thanksgiving meal. It’s warm, comforting, and the perfect balance of sweet apples and crunchy topping.

These 10 vegan and vegetarian recipes will surely impress your guests and make your Thanksgiving meal deliciously inclusive. With a variety of dishes that range from savory to sweet, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or just looking to add more plant-based options to your celebration, these recipes are guaranteed to become new holiday favorites.
