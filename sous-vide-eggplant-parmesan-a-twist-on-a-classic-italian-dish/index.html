Sous Vide Eggplant Parmesan: A Twist on a Classic Italian Dish


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’ve probably enjoyed a traditional eggplant Parmesan dish at some point. This classic comfort food features layers of crispy eggplant, savory marinara sauce, and gooey melted cheese. However, have you ever considered using the sous vide cooking method to elevate this beloved recipe to new heights? Sous vide eggplant Parmesan offers a unique twist on the traditional dish, resulting in tender, flavorful eggplant that perfectly complements the rich marinara and cheese. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to use sous vide cooking to achieve a delicious and innovative take on a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

For the Sous Vide Eggplant

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Assembly

  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

1. Preparing the Eggplant for Sous Vide

  • Preheat the water bath of the sous vide machine to 185°F (85°C).
  • In a bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.
  • Brush both sides of the eggplant slices with the seasoned olive oil mixture.
  • Place the seasoned eggplant slices in a single layer in a vacuum-sealable bag, removing as much air as possible before sealing.

2. Sous Vide Cooking

  • Submerge the sealed bag of seasoned eggplant slices in the preheated water bath and cook for 90 minutes.

3. Assembling the Eggplant Parmesan

  • Preheat the broiler in your oven.
  • Remove the cooked eggplant slices from the bag and pat them dry with paper towels.
  • In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce.
  • Arrange a layer of the sous vide eggplant slices over the marinara sauce.
  • Top the eggplant with a portion of the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese.
  • Repeat the layers of marinara, eggplant, and cheese until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a generous layer of cheese on top.
  • Place the baking dish under the broiler for 3-5 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  • Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide eggplant Parmesan pairs wonderfully with a side of al dente spaghetti or a crisp green salad dressed with balsamic vinaigrette. This innovative twist on a classic Italian dish is sure to impress your family and friends at your next gathering.

Why Sous Vide?

Sous vide cooking involves sealing food in airtight bags and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This method allows for even cooking and retention of flavors and nutrients, resulting in tender and flavorful dishes. By using the sous vide technique for the eggplant in this recipe, you can achieve a perfectly cooked texture that enhances the overall dish.

Conclusion

Sous vide eggplant Parmesan offers a modern and sophisticated take on the traditional Italian favorite. By infusing the eggplant with flavor through the sous vide method, you can create a dish that is both tender and bursting with taste. Whether you’re a fan of sous vide cooking or simply looking to reimagine a classic recipe, this innovative twist on eggplant Parmesan is a must-try for any culinary enthusiast. Enjoy the process of preparing this dish and savor the delightful flavors it brings to your table. Buon appetito!
