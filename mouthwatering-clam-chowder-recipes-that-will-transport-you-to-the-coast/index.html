Mouthwatering Clam Chowder Recipes That Will Transport You to the Coast


There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm bowl of clam chowder. The creamy broth, the tender clams, and the hint of smoky bacon all come together to create a dish that is both hearty and satisfying. Whether you’re a fan of the classic New England-style chowder or prefer a lighter, broth-based version, we’ve got you covered with these mouthwatering clam chowder recipes that will transport you to the coast.

1. Classic New England Clam Chowder

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh clams, scrubbed and cleaned
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups clam juice
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pot.
  2. Add the onion, celery, and garlic to the pot and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the potatoes and clam juice to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  4. In the meantime, steam the clams in a separate pot until they open. Discard any clams that do not open.
  5. Remove the clams from their shells and chop them into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Add the chopped clams, bacon, and heavy cream to the pot with the potatoes. Simmer for a few minutes until heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

2. Manhattan Clam Chowder

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh clams, scrubbed and cleaned
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  2. Drain the clams, reserving the clam juice. Add the clams and diced tomatoes to the pot, and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Add the vegetable broth, reserved clam juice, thyme, and bay leaf to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the bay leaf and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

3. Corn and Bacon Clam Chowder

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh clams, scrubbed and cleaned
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups frozen corn kernels
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pot.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the potatoes, corn, and chicken broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  4. In the meantime, steam the clams in a separate pot until they open. Discard any clams that do not open.
  5. Remove the clams from their shells and chop them into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Add the chopped clams, bacon, and milk to the pot with the potatoes and corn. Simmer for a few minutes until heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh chives before serving.

Conclusion

These mouthwatering clam chowder recipes are sure to transport you to the coast with their delicious flavors and comforting aromas. Whether you prefer the creamy richness of New England-style chowder or the tangy tomato base of Manhattan chowder, there’s a recipe here for everyone. So grab a bowl, cozy up, and enjoy a taste of the coast right in your own kitchen.
