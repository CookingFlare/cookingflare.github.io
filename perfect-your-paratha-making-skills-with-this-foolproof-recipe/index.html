Perfect Your Paratha Making Skills with this Foolproof Recipe


Parathas are a popular Indian bread that can be enjoyed with a variety of dishes. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced cook, this foolproof recipe will help you perfect your paratha making skills. With just a few simple ingredients and some practice, you’ll be able to create delicious and flaky parathas that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • Water, as needed

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.

  2. Add the oil to the flour mixture and mix it in using your fingertips. This will help incorporate the oil into the flour and create a crumbly texture.

  3. Slowly add water to the flour mixture, a little at a time, and knead the dough until it comes together. The amount of water needed may vary depending on the humidity in your kitchen. The dough should be soft and pliable, but not sticky.

  4. Once the dough is well-kneaded, cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 15 minutes. This will allow the gluten in the dough to relax, resulting in softer and more manageable dough.

  5. After the resting period, divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball between your palms to create a smooth surface.

  6. Dust your working surface with some flour and roll out each ball into a thin, round disc. The disc should be approximately 6-7 inches in diameter.

  7. Heat a tawa or a flat skillet over medium-high heat. Place the rolled paratha on the heated tawa and cook it for about 30 seconds or until you see small bubbles forming on the surface.

  8. Flip the paratha and cook the other side for another 30 seconds. At this point, drizzle a little oil or ghee on the surface of the paratha and spread it evenly using a spatula.

  9. Flip the paratha once more and cook it for another 30 seconds or until both sides are golden brown and crispy.

  10. Remove the paratha from the tawa and place it on a plate lined with a paper towel. This will help absorb any excess oil.

  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls until all the parathas are cooked.

  12. Serve the parathas hot with your favorite curry, chutney, or pickle. They can also be enjoyed on their own with a cup of chai or yogurt.

Tips and Tricks

  • To make your parathas even more flavorful, you can add finely chopped cilantro, green chili, or grated cheese to the dough before kneading.
  • If you prefer a softer and flakier texture, you can brush the rolled paratha with melted butter or ghee before cooking.
  • Experiment with different fillings such as mashed potatoes, paneer, or minced meat to create stuffed parathas.

Now that you have the foolproof recipe for making perfect parathas, it’s time to get in the kitchen and start rolling! With a little practice, you’ll be able to make parathas that rival those from your favorite Indian restaurant. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and enjoy the deliciousness of homemade parathas. Happy cooking!
