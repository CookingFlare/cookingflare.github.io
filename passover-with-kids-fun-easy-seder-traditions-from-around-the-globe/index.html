Passover with Kids: Fun & Easy Seder Traditions from Around the Globe


Passover is a deeply meaningful holiday for Jewish families, commemorating the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in ancient Egypt. However, Passover can sometimes feel scaled to adults; which is what makes our adventure through Passover seder traditions from around the globe even more exciting. In this blog post, we will introduce you to several fun and easy ways to engage your kids in seder traditions, making this important holiday a memorable and joyful experience for the entire family!

  1. Passover Treasure Hunt - Spain

Kick off your Passover seder with a twist – a wholesome family activity in the form of a treasure hunt! Inspired by Spain’s tradition of telling the Passover story, you can create a scavenger hunt leading up to the beginning of your seder. Hide clues or objects symbolizing elements of the Exodus story for your children to find. This not only ignites curiosity but also helps them understand the significance of the Passover story in a fun way.

  1. The Passover Matzah Mug Painting - Argentina

Bring life to your Passover table through an activity that unites your children with art and creativity. In Argentina, kids often take part in a matzah mug painting project. Purchase plain white mugs, and pass out paint and brushes for a fun-filled paint session before the seder. While the paint dries, fill the mugs with the four cups of wine and use them throughout the seder. You can store the painted mugs in a safe place, and use them throughout the year to share samples of your family’s Passover joy.

  1. Let’s Build the Seder Plate - France

The Passover seder plate is integral to the seder experience, yet it can be difficult for young children to understand all the symbolic foods. To empower them, let them make their own seder plate! Gather the necessary items for the traditional seder plate and source them accordingly. In the days preceding Passover, work on assembling the plates together with your kids, explaining the symbolism of each item.

  1. Create a Matzah Pizza Bar - Greece

Matzah pizza is a beloved Passover staple worldwide, but what if you turned it into a fun, hands-on activity? Inspired by the tradition from Greece, create a matzah pizza bar and let your kids personalize their pizzas from a selection of toppings. From colorful vegetables to cheese and beef, let your children’s taste buds explore the different combinations they can come up with.

  1. Magical Charoset Cups - Morocco

Charoset, a sweet mixture of apples, nuts, and wine, is a staple during the Passover seder. Encourage your children to get creative with their charoset presentation by turning their portions into fun, small glasses or cups. Fill small paper cups (or play cups) with colorful charoset, incorporating your children’s favorite fruits and nuts. This simple yet cute activity will also give them a sense of ownership over Passover.

  1. Craft Your Own Afikoman Cup - Italy

Possibly the most engaging activity, this idea comes from Italian Passover traditions, where kids create their own afikoman cups. Hide the afikoman and encourage siblings to find it while undertaking a fun scavenger hunt. Once the afikoman is found, place it in their handcrafted cup and let them drink the wine contained within, signifying joy right after the Exodus story is concluded.

  1. Celebrate with a Seder Around the Globe - Beyond

Why limit your Passover celebration to the traditional Middle Eastern countries? Dive into Passover customs from around the globe – there are so many unique, fun, and engaging ways to celebrate this meaningful holiday, and your children will love the sense of discovery, too!

Embracing these diverse Passover seder traditions will create an elaborate tapestry of memories, experiences, and learning for your children, who will grow up to carry the spirit of Passover with them wherever they go. Happy Passover!
