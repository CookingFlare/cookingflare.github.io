Homemade Blueberry Pie: Bursting with Juicy Berries


There’s nothing quite like a homemade blueberry pie – the aroma of fresh blueberries and buttery crust baking in the oven is enough to make anyone’s mouth water. This classic dessert is a celebration of the sweet and tangy flavors of blueberries, encapsulated in a flaky, golden crust. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating your very own blueberry pie that’s bursting with juicy berries.

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Blueberry Filling:

  • 5 cups fresh blueberries, rinsed and drained
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Zest of one lemon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

For the Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pie Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough comes together.
  3. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Make the Blueberry Filling

  1. In a large bowl, gently toss the blueberries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, lemon zest, and ground cinnamon until the blueberries are evenly coated.
  2. Set the blueberry mixture aside to macerate for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disk of the chilled pie crust into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the crust to a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Pour the blueberry filling into the prepared pie crust. Dot the top with small pieces of butter.
  4. Roll out the second disk of pie crust and place it over the blueberry filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

Step 4: Bake the Pie

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and water to make the egg wash. Brush the top crust with the egg wash.
  2. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  3. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with foil and continue baking.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Allow the blueberry pie to cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow the filling to set.
  2. Serve slices of the blueberry pie with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful treat that perfectly complements the juicy berries.

Conclusion

Indulge in the irresistible allure of homemade blueberry pie with this simple yet sensational recipe. The combination of plump, juicy blueberries nestled in a flaky, buttery crust is a timeless delight that never fails to impress. Whether it’s for a special occasion or a casual gathering, this blueberry pie will always steal the show. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to savor the sweet taste of success with every mouthwatering bite.
