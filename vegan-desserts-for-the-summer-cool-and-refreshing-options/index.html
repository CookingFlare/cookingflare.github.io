Vegan Desserts for the Summer: Cool and Refreshing Options


As the summer sun blazes down, the last thing you want is to spend hours in a hot kitchen baking. Instead, why not cool off with some delightful vegan desserts that are not only refreshing but also easy to prepare? From luscious ice creams to fruity sorbets and creamy parfaits, there’s no shortage of options to satisfy your sweet tooth while keeping things light and vibrant. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of vegan dessert recipes perfect for summer gatherings, picnics, or simply enjoying by the poolside.

1. Coconut Mango Sorbet

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup coconut milk (full-fat for creaminess)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup (or agave syrup)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Mango: Place the diced mangoes in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. Mix Ingredients: Add the coconut milk, maple syrup, lime juice, and a pinch of salt to the blender. Blend again until all ingredients are well combined.
  3. Chill: Pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer for about 2 hours, or until it begins to freeze around the edges.
  4. Whisk: Remove from the freezer and whisk the mixture vigorously to break up any ice crystals. Return to the freezer for another 2-3 hours, whisking every hour until it reaches a smooth, scoopable consistency.
  5. Serve: Scoop the sorbet into bowls and enjoy your refreshing treat!

2. Watermelon Granita

Ingredients

  • 4 cups seedless watermelon, cubed
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons agave syrup (optional)
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions

  1. Blend Watermelon: In a blender, combine the cubed watermelon, lime juice, and agave syrup (if using). Blend until completely smooth.
  2. Freeze: Pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer.
  3. Scrape: After about an hour, use a fork to scrape the mixture, breaking up any ice that forms. Repeat this process every 30 minutes for about 3 hours, or until the granita is fluffy and has a snow-like texture.
  4. Serve: Scoop the granita into serving cups and garnish with fresh mint. Enjoy the refreshing taste of summer!

3. Berry Chia Pudding

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries for topping (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)

Instructions

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, whisk together the almond milk, chia seeds, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  2. Refrigerate: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, until the mixture thickens to a pudding-like consistency.
  3. Serve: Once set, stir the pudding and divide it into serving glasses. Top with fresh berries for a burst of flavor and color.

4. No-Bake Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Blend Avocados: In a blender or food processor, combine the ripe avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Chill: Spoon the mousse into individual serving dishes and refrigerate for about 30 minutes to set.
  3. Serve: Garnish with fresh berries or coconut whipped cream for an indulgent finish.

5. Tropical Fruit Salad with Lime Dressing

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pineapple, diced
  • 1 cup mango, diced
  • 1 cup kiwi, sliced
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon agave syrup (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Fruit: In a large bowl, combine the diced pineapple, mango, kiwi, and strawberries.
  2. Make Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the lime juice and agave syrup (if using).
  3. Combine: Drizzle the lime dressing over the fruit salad and gently toss to combine. Add the chopped mint and mix again.
  4. Serve: Chill for about 30 minutes before serving to let the flavors meld together. Enjoy this refreshing summer delight!

6. Vegan Lemon Bars

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
    • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
    • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup almond milk
    • 1/2 cup maple syrup
    • 1/4 cup cornstarch
    • 1/4 cup lemon juice
    • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Make Crust: In a mixing bowl, combine almond flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt. Mix until well combined. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  3. Bake Crust: Bake the crust for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.
  4. Prepare Filling: In a saucepan over medium heat, whisk together the almond milk, maple syrup, cornstarch, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens.
  5. Combine and Bake: Pour the lemon filling over the cooled crust and spread it evenly. Bake for another 20-25 minutes, until the filling is set.
  6. Cool and Serve: Let the bars cool completely before slicing. Dust with powdered sugar if desired and serve chilled.

7. Vegan Ice Cream Sandwiches

Ingredients

  • For the cookies:

    • 1 cup almond butter
    • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 cup rolled oats
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • A pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups coconut cream (chilled)
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Make Cookie Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine almond butter, coconut sugar, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth. Add rolled oats, baking soda, and salt, mixing until combined.
  3. Bake Cookies: Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet and flatten slightly. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown. Let cool completely.
  4. Prepare Ice Cream Filling: In a mixing bowl, whip together the chilled coconut cream, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until fluffy.
  5. Assemble Sandwiches: Take one cookie, add a generous dollop of the coconut cream filling, and top with another cookie. Press gently to create a sandwich. Repeat with remaining cookies.
  6. Chill and Enjoy: Place the sandwiches in the freezer for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Conclusion

Summer is the perfect time to embrace the vibrant flavors of fresh fruits and creamy textures found in these vegan desserts. Whether you’re enjoying a scoop of coconut mango sorbet on a hot day or indulging in rich chocolate avocado mousse, these recipes offer something for everyone. The best part? They’re simple to make and require minimal effort, leaving you more time to relax and enjoy the sunshine.

So, gather your ingredients and get ready to impress your friends and family with these delicious, cool, and refreshing vegan desserts. They’ll not only satisfy your cravings but also keep you feeling light and energized throughout the summer season. Happy cooking!
