Tasty Vegan and Vegetarian Picnic Recipes for Summer


Summer picnics are the perfect way to enjoy the outdoors, soak up the sunshine, and indulge in delicious food. Whether you are planning a small gathering with friends or a family outing, packing the right food is essential. Vegan and vegetarian options offer a refreshing and healthy alternative to traditional picnic fare, and the variety of flavors and textures can cater to any dietary preference.

In this post, we will explore a collection of vibrant, easy-to-make, and crowd-pleasing vegan and vegetarian picnic recipes that will elevate your summer picnic experience. From hearty salads to savory snacks and refreshing drinks, you’ll find something for everyone.

1. Vegan Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

This vibrant and refreshing salad combines the flavors of the Mediterranean in a colorful and nutrient-packed dish. It’s quick to prepare, highly customizable, and perfect for a summer picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives, parsley, and mint.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat all the ingredients evenly.
  4. Let the salad sit in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve chilled or at room temperature for a refreshing picnic treat.

This Mediterranean Chickpea Salad is rich in fiber, protein, and healthy fats, making it a filling and satisfying option for any picnic spread.

2. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Grilled veggie skewers are an excellent addition to any summer picnic, offering a smoky flavor that’s sure to impress. This recipe allows for plenty of customization, so you can mix and match your favorite vegetables to create a truly unique dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup mushrooms, halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss all the vegetables in the marinade until evenly coated.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between different types of vegetables.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Serve the skewers warm or at room temperature.

These grilled veggie skewers are a fantastic, flavorful dish that can be paired with a variety of dips or served as a side to other picnic treats.

3. Vegan BLT Sandwiches

The classic BLT sandwich gets a vegan makeover with plant-based ingredients that are just as tasty as the traditional version. This recipe is perfect for those who want a hearty and satisfying sandwich for their picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 8 slices of whole-grain bread
  • 1 cup vegan mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup fresh lettuce leaves
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup vegan bacon strips (store-bought or homemade)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread slices lightly until golden brown.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the vegan mayonnaise and Dijon mustard.
  3. Spread a generous amount of the mayo-mustard mixture on one side of each bread slice.
  4. Layer the lettuce, tomato, avocado, and vegan bacon on four slices of bread.
  5. Top with the remaining slices of bread to create sandwiches.
  6. Slice the sandwiches in half and serve.

These Vegan BLT sandwiches are incredibly satisfying, with a crunchy, smoky, and creamy combination of flavors. Perfect for a picnic or as a quick lunch option.

4. Veggie and Hummus Wraps

Wraps are a simple yet delicious picnic option that can be made ahead of time and are easy to eat on the go. These veggie and hummus wraps are filled with colorful vegetables and creamy hummus, offering a great balance of textures and flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 whole wheat wraps
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup spinach leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Lay each wrap flat on a clean surface.
  2. Spread a generous layer of hummus on each wrap.
  3. Arrange the cucumber, bell pepper, carrot, avocado, spinach, and cilantro on top of the hummus.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Roll up each wrap tightly and slice it in half diagonally.
  6. Wrap each sandwich in parchment paper or foil for easy transport.

These wraps are full of fresh veggies and the creamy goodness of hummus, providing a healthy and filling option for your picnic.

5. Vegan Caesar Salad with Crispy Chickpeas

A classic Caesar salad gets a vegan twist with roasted chickpeas for crunch and a dairy-free dressing that’s just as creamy and tangy as the original. This is a great choice for a picnic when you want a filling and light salad.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of Romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup vegan Caesar dressing (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional, for extra cheesy flavor)
  • Croutons (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chickpeas with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the chickpeas for 25-30 minutes, tossing them halfway through, until they are golden and crispy.
  4. In a large bowl, toss the chopped Romaine lettuce with the vegan Caesar dressing.
  5. Top the salad with the crispy chickpeas, croutons, and nutritional yeast.
  6. Serve immediately or store in the fridge for later.

This Vegan Caesar Salad is a crunchy, creamy delight that makes for a satisfying side dish or light meal during your picnic.

6. Watermelon Mint Salad

A refreshing fruit salad is an essential part of any summer picnic, and this watermelon mint salad is the perfect way to beat the heat. The sweetness of the watermelon, combined with the freshness of mint and a touch of lime, creates a light and hydrating dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp honey or agave syrup (optional)
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cubed watermelon and chopped mint.
  2. Drizzle the lime juice over the salad and toss to combine.
  3. If desired, add a teaspoon of honey or agave syrup for extra sweetness.
  4. Sprinkle a pinch of sea salt over the salad to enhance the flavors.
  5. Serve chilled and enjoy!

This watermelon mint salad is light, refreshing, and hydrating—making it the perfect addition to your summer picnic.

7. Vegan Ice Cream Sandwiches

For a sweet finish to your summer picnic, these vegan ice cream sandwiches are a fun and indulgent treat. You can use store-bought or homemade cookies and your favorite vegan ice cream for this recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 8 vegan cookies (chocolate chip, oatmeal, or your favorite variety)
  • 2 cups vegan ice cream (your favorite flavor)
  • Optional: Sprinkles or shredded coconut for rolling

Instructions:

  1. Allow the vegan ice cream to soften for a few minutes so it’s easier to spread.
  2. Place one cookie on a flat surface and top with a generous scoop of vegan ice cream.
  3. Place the second cookie on top and gently press down to sandwich the ice cream between the cookies.
  4. If desired, roll the edges of the sandwich in sprinkles or shredded coconut for extra flair.
  5. Freeze the sandwiches for at least 30 minutes to set the ice cream before serving.

These vegan ice cream sandwiches are the ultimate summer treat, combining creamy ice cream with chewy cookies for a nostalgic and satisfying dessert.

Final Thoughts

When planning your next summer picnic, don’t forget to pack a mix of vibrant, delicious, and satisfying vegan and vegetarian recipes. From light salads and wraps to savory skewers and indulgent desserts, there’s something for every taste and preference. These recipes are not only healthy and delicious but also easy to prepare in advance, allowing you to focus on enjoying your time outdoors.

So gather your ingredients, pack your picnic basket, and head to your favorite outdoor spot. With these tasty vegan and vegetarian picnic recipes, you’re sure to impress your guests and enjoy a summer full of great food and good company!
