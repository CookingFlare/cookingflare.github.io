Master the Art of Neapolitan Pizza Making with These Classic Italian Recipes


Pizza, the beloved Italian dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all over the world. And when it comes to pizza, Neapolitan pizza stands tall as the pinnacle of Italian culinary mastery. The perfect combination of a thin, crispy crust, fresh and flavorful toppings, and that unmistakable wood-fired taste. If you’ve always wanted to master the art of Neapolitan pizza making, then this is the blog post for you. We’ll be sharing classic Italian recipes that will help you create authentic Neapolitan pizzas right in your own kitchen.

The Foundation: Neapolitan Pizza Dough

The key to a delicious Neapolitan pizza lies in its dough. Traditional Neapolitan pizza dough is made with just a few simple ingredients: flour, water, salt, and yeast. The secret to achieving that perfect chewy yet crispy crust lies in the dough’s fermentation process. To get started, here’s a classic Neapolitan pizza dough recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 500g “00” flour
  • 325ml water
  • 10g salt
  • 3g active dry yeast

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture into the flour mixture and mix until the dough starts to come together.
  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise for at least 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  6. Once the dough has risen, divide it into individual portions and shape them into balls. Cover the balls with a damp cloth and let them rest for another hour.

Classic Margherita Pizza

The Margherita pizza is a true Neapolitan classic, named after Queen Margherita of Italy. It’s a simple yet incredibly flavorful pizza that showcases the freshness of its ingredients. Here’s how you can make your own Margherita pizza:

Ingredients:

  • Neapolitan pizza dough (from the previous recipe)
  • Tomato sauce (made from San Marzano tomatoes, salt, and a pinch of sugar)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, ideally with a pizza stone inside to mimic the effect of a wood-fired oven.
  2. Take one portion of the Neapolitan pizza dough and stretch it out into a thin, round shape using your hands or a rolling pin.
  3. Place the stretched dough on a pizza peel or a parchment paper-lined baking sheet.
  4. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Tear the mozzarella cheese into small pieces and distribute them evenly over the sauce.
  6. Scatter fresh basil leaves on top of the cheese.
  7. Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the pizza.
  8. Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven and bake for about 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a minute before slicing and serving.

Classic Marinara Pizza

Another classic Neapolitan pizza that deserves a spot in your repertoire is the Marinara pizza. This delightful pizza is a celebration of simplicity and showcases the vibrant flavors of garlic and oregano. Here’s how you can make your own Marinara pizza:

Ingredients:

  • Neapolitan pizza dough (from the previous recipe)
  • Tomato sauce (made from San Marzano tomatoes, salt, and a pinch of sugar)
  • Garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • Dried oregano
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, ideally with a pizza stone inside to mimic the effect of a wood-fired oven.
  2. Take one portion of the Neapolitan pizza dough and stretch it out into a thin, round shape using your hands or a rolling pin.
  3. Place the stretched dough on a pizza peel or a parchment paper-lined baking sheet.
  4. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Arrange the thinly sliced garlic cloves evenly over the sauce.
  6. Sprinkle dried oregano generously over the pizza.
  7. Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the pizza.
  8. Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven and bake for about 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a minute before slicing and serving.

Get Creative with Toppings

While the Margherita and Marinara pizzas are the classic Neapolitan choices, don’t be afraid to experiment with different toppings to create your own masterpiece. Some popular Neapolitan pizza toppings include fresh buffalo mozzarella, cherry tomatoes, prosciutto, arugula, and even anchovies. The key is to use ingredients that are fresh, high-quality, and complement each other well.

Conclusion

With these classic Italian recipes, you’re well on your way to mastering the art of Neapolitan pizza making. Remember to start with a good Neapolitan pizza dough as the foundation, and then let your creativity shine through with your choice of toppings. Whether you stick to the classic Margherita and Marinara pizzas or venture into new flavor territories, the key to a great Neapolitan pizza lies in the quality of your ingredients and the love you put into making it. So, roll up your sleeves, fire up the oven, and get ready to enjoy the taste of Italy right in your own home.

Note: The recipes provided are for guidance only. Feel free to adjust the quantities and techniques to suit your own preferences and equipment.
