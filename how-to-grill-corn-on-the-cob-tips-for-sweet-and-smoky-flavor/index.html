How to Grill Corn on the Cob: Tips for Sweet and Smoky Flavor


Grilling corn on the cob is one of the easiest yet most rewarding ways to enjoy this versatile vegetable. The smoky flavor from the grill combined with the natural sweetness of the corn creates a flavor-packed treat that’s perfect for summer cookouts, barbecues, or even just a simple weeknight dinner. If you’ve ever wondered how to grill corn to perfection, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know, from selecting the best corn to seasoning it just right and mastering the grill for a flawless cook. Let’s dive in and explore the secrets to grilling corn on the cob that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Choosing the Best Corn on the Cob

The first step in grilling corn is selecting the freshest, most flavorful ears of corn you can find. Not all corn is created equal, so here are some tips to make sure you pick the best ears:

1. Look for Fresh Husks

When shopping for corn, always inspect the husks. The husks should be green, tight, and free of brown spots. Avoid corn with dry, brown, or yellowing husks, as these may indicate older or less fresh corn.

2. Feel the Kernels

Gently squeeze the ear of corn through the husk. The kernels should feel plump and firm, not shriveled or soft. You can also peel back a small portion of the husk to check the kernels directly. The kernels should be milky and evenly spaced.

3. Choose Corn with Bright Silk

The silk at the top of the corn should be a golden brown or light yellow color. If the silk looks dried out or darkened, it could be a sign that the corn is past its prime. Fresh silk should be moist and vibrant.

4. Freshness is Key

If possible, purchase your corn the same day you plan to grill it. Freshly picked corn tastes the best, and grilling it as soon as possible after harvest preserves its sweetness.

Preparing Corn on the Cob for Grilling

Once you’ve chosen the best corn, it’s time to prepare it for grilling. There are a few different methods you can use to prep the corn, depending on your preference and the flavor profile you’re aiming for. Let’s explore the most popular options.

1. Grilling Corn in the Husk

Grilling corn in its husk is a simple and effective way to retain moisture, giving the corn a tender texture while allowing it to develop a delicious smoky flavor. Here’s how to do it:

Steps for Grilling Corn in the Husk:

  1. Soak the Corn: Start by soaking the corn in its husk for about 15 to 30 minutes. This will prevent the husks from burning on the grill while the corn cooks. Submerge the corn in water, making sure the husks are completely soaked.

  2. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium heat (about 350-400°F). You want a steady, moderate flame for this method to cook the corn evenly without burning it.

  3. Place Corn on the Grill: Once soaked, place the corn directly on the grill grates, leaving the husks on. Rotate the corn every 5 minutes to ensure it cooks evenly on all sides. Grill the corn for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.

  4. Remove and Serve: After grilling, carefully remove the corn from the grill using tongs (be cautious, it will be hot!). Let it cool for a minute, then peel back the husks and remove the silk. The corn will be tender, smoky, and full of flavor.

2. Grilling Corn without the Husk (Directly on the Grill)

If you prefer a more charred and smoky flavor, grilling the corn directly on the grill is the way to go. This method exposes the kernels directly to the heat, allowing for a more pronounced grill mark and smoky taste.

Steps for Grilling Corn without the Husk:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Get your grill ready by heating it to medium-high heat (about 400-450°F). A hotter grill will help achieve a nice char on the kernels.

  2. Peel the Husk and Silk: Remove the husk and silk from the corn. You can either snap the husk off completely or peel it back and leave it attached to the base for easier handling later.

  3. Oil the Corn (Optional): For added flavor and to prevent the corn from sticking to the grill, you can brush the kernels lightly with oil or butter. This step is optional, but it does help with creating a golden-brown char on the corn.

  4. Grill the Corn: Place the corn directly on the grill grates. Rotate the corn every 2-3 minutes to ensure it cooks evenly and gets a good char on all sides. Grill for about 10-15 minutes or until the kernels are nicely charred and tender.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the corn is grilled to your liking, remove it from the grill and allow it to cool for a minute or two. You can enjoy it as is, or brush it with some butter and seasoning for added flavor.

3. Grilling Corn in Foil

Another excellent method for grilling corn is to wrap it in foil. This method locks in moisture and infuses the corn with a more subtle smoky flavor. It’s ideal if you want to control the moisture level and ensure the corn doesn’t dry out on the grill.

Steps for Grilling Corn in Foil:

  1. Prepare the Corn: Start by peeling back the husk (but don’t remove it entirely), then remove the silk. Once the silk is gone, fold the husk back over the corn. You can also cut off the tip of the corn if you prefer a more manageable size.

  2. Season the Corn: Before wrapping the corn in foil, add seasonings of your choice. You can brush the corn with butter, sprinkle with salt, pepper, garlic powder, or any other seasoning mix you like. For an extra burst of flavor, you can even add a bit of lime zest or chili powder.

  3. Wrap the Corn: Wrap the corn tightly in aluminum foil, ensuring that the foil is sealed securely to keep the steam in. This will help cook the corn evenly and maintain moisture.

  4. Grill the Corn: Place the foil-wrapped corn on the grill and cook for about 15-20 minutes, rotating the corn halfway through. The foil will trap the heat, allowing the corn to steam in its own natural moisture.

  5. Serve: Once done, remove the corn from the grill and let it cool slightly before unwrapping. The corn will be incredibly tender and infused with all the seasonings you added.

Seasoning Your Grilled Corn

While grilled corn is delicious on its own, adding the right seasoning can elevate the flavor to new heights. Here are a few seasoning ideas to try:

1. Classic Butter and Salt

This is the simplest and most traditional way to season your grilled corn. After removing the corn from the grill, slather it with butter and sprinkle with salt. For added flavor, you can use flavored butters such as garlic butter or herb butter.

2. Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

One of the most popular ways to enjoy grilled corn is to make Mexican street corn, or elote. To prepare this, spread a layer of mayonnaise or sour cream on the grilled corn, then sprinkle with chili powder, Cotija cheese, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.

3. Cajun Spices

If you love bold flavors, consider seasoning your corn with Cajun spices. After grilling, sprinkle a mix of Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper over the corn for a spicy kick.

4. Herbed Parmesan

For a savory twist, top your grilled corn with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs like basil, parsley, or thyme. The saltiness of the cheese pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the corn.

5. Honey and Chili Butter

For a sweet and spicy flavor combination, try making honey and chili butter. Mix softened butter with honey, a pinch of chili powder, and a little salt. Slather this mixture onto the grilled corn for a unique flavor experience.

Tips for Perfectly Grilled Corn

To ensure that your grilled corn turns out perfectly every time, here are a few additional tips to keep in mind:

1. Don’t Overcook the Corn

Grilling corn for too long can lead to tough, chewy kernels. Keep a close eye on the corn while it’s on the grill and check for doneness after about 10-15 minutes. You want the kernels to be tender and slightly charred, not dry.

2. Use Tongs to Rotate the Corn

When grilling corn, use tongs to rotate the ears every few minutes. This helps ensure even cooking and gives you those nice grill marks all around.

3. Grill with the Lid Closed (for Husked Corn)

If you’re grilling corn in the husk, closing the lid helps trap the heat and smoke, which infuses the corn with a more intense flavor. Make sure to check the corn periodically to avoid burning.

4. Avoid High Heat for Soaked Husked Corn

When grilling corn in the husk, it’s best to use medium heat. High heat can cause the husk to burn before the corn is properly cooked.

5. Use Fresh Corn

Whenever possible, use fresh, in-season corn. The taste is unbeatable, and the texture will be tender and juicy, making all your grilling efforts worthwhile.

Final Thoughts

Grilling corn on the cob is a simple yet delicious way to elevate your summer meals. Whether you prefer it smoky and charred, tender and juicy, or with a delicious seasoning blend, there’s no wrong way to grill corn. With the right techniques and a bit of creativity, you can make grilled corn that will impress your family and friends at every cookout. Happy grilling!
