Authentic Asian Cuisine for Beginner Cooks: Easy Recipes


Asian cuisine has gained immense popularity around the world, with its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and diverse range of dishes. If you’re a beginner cook looking to explore the world of authentic Asian cooking, you’ve come to the right place! In this blog post, we’ll share some easy and delicious recipes that will help you embark on your culinary journey with confidence.

1. Chicken Pad Thai

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 15 minutes
Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • Crushed peanuts for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced chicken breasts to the wok and cook until they are no longer pink in the center.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the fish sauce, soy sauce, tamarind paste, and brown sugar. Pour the sauce over the chicken and stir well to combine.
  5. Push the chicken to one side of the wok and crack the egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg until it is cooked through.
  6. Add the cooked rice noodles to the wok and toss everything together until well coated with the sauce.
  7. Throw in the bean sprouts and spring onions, and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until they are slightly wilted.
  8. Remove from heat and garnish with crushed peanuts. Serve with lime wedges on the side for squeezing over the dish.

2. Vegetable Fried Rice

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and diced onion, and cook until they are softened and slightly golden.
  2. Add the diced carrot and frozen peas to the skillet, and cook for a few more minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are fully cooked.
  4. Add the cooked jasmine rice to the skillet and stir everything together until well combined.
  5. Stir in the soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using), and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until everything is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped spring onions.

3. Thai Green Curry

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 20 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas)
  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the coconut milk and stir well to combine with the curry paste.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and sliced chicken breasts to the saucepan, and simmer for about 10 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Adjust the seasonings according to your taste.
  5. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh basil leaves.
  6. Serve the Thai green curry over cooked jasmine rice.

Conclusion

With these easy and delicious recipes, you can now confidently embark on your journey to explore the flavors of authentic Asian cuisine. Whether you choose to indulge in the savory Chicken Pad Thai, the comforting Vegetable Fried Rice, or the aromatic Thai Green Curry, these dishes are sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. So put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the culinary adventure begin! Happy cooking!
