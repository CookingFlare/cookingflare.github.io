Lemon Blueberry Cake Recipe: Sunshine in a Muffin Tin


If you’re craving a burst of sunshine in your life, this lemon blueberry cake recipe is just the solution! Bursting with fresh blueberries and zesty lemon flavor, this scrumptious treat epitomizes summer – perfect for a warm day or any time you need a taste of the sun. With a tender crumb and irresistible glaze, these muffins are sure to satisfy every sweet tooth and brighten up your day.

Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (113g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (80g) fresh blueberries

For the lemon glaze:

  • 1 cup (85g) powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions

To start, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with 12 liners or grease them with butter or non-stick cooking spray.

Step 1: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt until well combined. Set the mixture aside for later use.

Step 2: Cream the Butter and Sugar

In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar on high speed with an electric mixer until creamy and pale in color (about 2-3 minutes). This step helps to create a light and fluffy cake texture.

Step 3: Combine the Wet Ingredients

Add the eggs one at a time, making sure each is fully incorporated before adding the next. Then, add the lemon juice, milk, and vanilla extract and mix until well combined.

Step 4: Combine the Wet and Dry Mixtures

Gradually add the dry ingredient mixture to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to dense muffins. A few lumps are okay and will make a lighter, more tender cake.

Step 5: Add the Blueberries

Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, being careful not to break them. Distribute the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.

Step 6: Prepare the Lemon Glaze

In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest until smooth and glossy. Set aside the glaze.

Step 7: Bake the Muffins

Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean and the tops are lightly golden.

Step 8: Glaze the Muffins

Once the muffins are completely cool, drizzle the lemon glaze generously over the tops of each muffin. The glaze will seep into the cracks, creating a delectable lemon sugary sweetness.

Step 9: Serve and Enjoy

Serve these muffins on their own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Enjoy the sunshine in a muffin tin!

The Secret Ingredient: Love

Don’t forget the secret ingredient: love! Sharing these scrumptious lemon blueberry cakes with friends and family will surely bring a smile to their faces and fill your home with warmth and laughter.

Now that you know how to make these delightful lemon blueberry cakes, share them with the people you love and spread some sunshine wherever you go!

And guess what? Feel free to customize this recipe by swapping blueberries with other tasty fruits like raspberries or strawberries, or even experimenting with different citrus flavors like lime or orange. The sky’s the limit when it comes to creating a perfect cake for your taste buds!
