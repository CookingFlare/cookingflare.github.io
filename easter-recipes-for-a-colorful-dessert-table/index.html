Easter Recipes for a Colorful Dessert Table


Easter is a time for family gatherings, celebrations, and of course, delicious food. While the main course often takes center stage, it’s the dessert table that steals the show with its array of colorful treats and festive designs. From pastel-colored cakes to playful cupcakes and vibrant fruit tarts, Easter desserts can be as beautiful as they are tasty. If you’re looking to create an unforgettable dessert table for Easter, this guide will walk you through some delightful recipes that will not only wow your guests but also brighten up your celebration.

In this post, we’ll cover a variety of desserts that will add color, joy, and flavor to your Easter gathering. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, dinner, or simply looking to create a showstopping dessert spread, these Easter recipes will help you build a table full of springtime cheer.

1. Easter Bunny Cupcakes

These adorable cupcakes are a must-have for any Easter dessert table. The fluffy vanilla cupcakes are topped with cream cheese frosting and decorated with bunny ears, faces, and little pink noses. They’re a fun, whimsical way to get into the Easter spirit.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of vanilla cake mix (or homemade vanilla cake recipe)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pink and white sprinkles (for decoration)
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Black and pink food coloring

Directions:

  1. Make the Cupcakes: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare the vanilla cake mix according to the package instructions, or use your own favorite vanilla cake recipe. Bake the cupcakes in a muffin tin and let them cool completely before decorating.

  2. Prepare the Frosting: Beat the softened butter and cream cheese together until smooth and creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla extract and continue to beat until fluffy and light.

  3. Decorate the Cupcakes: Frost each cooled cupcake generously with the cream cheese frosting. Use a piping bag with a round tip to create a fluffy base. Then, carefully arrange mini marshmallows at the top of each cupcake to form the bunny ears. Use pink food coloring to tint the frosting for the bunny nose and create cute little eyes and whiskers with black food coloring.

  4. Add Final Touches: Add some colorful sprinkles around the bunny to enhance the festive look. These cupcakes will bring smiles to everyone’s face!

2. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Nothing says Easter more than a rich, moist carrot cake. This traditional favorite is made even better with a smooth and creamy cream cheese frosting. It’s the perfect balance of sweetness and spice, making it an irresistible treat for any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups finely grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

For the Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Carrot Cake: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. In a separate large bowl, beat together eggs, sugar, and oil until smooth. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined. Stir in the grated carrots and walnuts (if using).

  2. Bake the Cake: Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  3. Make the Cream Cheese Frosting: Beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, continuing to beat until fluffy and creamy.

  4. Assemble the Cake: Once the cakes have cooled, spread a generous amount of cream cheese frosting between the layers and on top of the cake. Decorate with additional chopped walnuts or candied carrots for a festive touch.

3. Springtime Fruit Tart

A fruit tart is a stunning and fresh dessert that will complement the rich and indulgent flavors of other Easter treats. This tart features a buttery, flaky crust filled with a smooth custard and topped with a colorful array of fresh, in-season fruits.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet pre-made tart crust or homemade shortcrust pastry
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruits (such as strawberries, blueberries, kiwi, and raspberries)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Tart Crust: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the tart crust in a tart pan and bake according to the package instructions (if using pre-made dough). For homemade pastry, bake until golden brown and crisp.

  2. Make the Custard Filling: In a medium saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, and vanilla extract over medium heat. In a separate bowl, whisk together egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch. Slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, whisking continuously until the custard thickens. Remove from heat and let it cool.

  3. Assemble the Tart: Once the crust has cooled, fill it with the custard mixture. Arrange an assortment of fresh fruit on top in a beautiful pattern, using strawberries, blueberries, kiwi slices, raspberries, and any other colorful fruits you desire.

  4. Serve: Chill the tart in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving. This light, fruity dessert is the perfect way to end an Easter feast.

4. Lemon Meringue Pie

Lemon meringue pie is a classic dessert that’s both tart and sweet, making it an excellent addition to your Easter table. The combination of a tangy lemon filling and a fluffy, golden meringue topping is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 large egg whites
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • 6 tbsp granulated sugar (for the meringue)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Lemon Filling: In a medium saucepan, whisk together sugar, cornstarch, and water over medium heat. Bring the mixture to a boil while stirring constantly until thickened. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and gradually temper them with a small amount of the hot sugar mixture. Pour the egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly. Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice and butter.

  2. Make the Meringue: In a clean bowl, beat the egg whites with cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar, beating until stiff, glossy peaks form.

  3. Assemble the Pie: Pour the lemon filling into the pre-baked pie crust. Spoon the meringue over the top, spreading it all the way to the edges to seal in the filling.

  4. Bake: Bake the pie at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving.

5. Easter Egg Sugar Cookies

Sugar cookies shaped like Easter eggs are another fun addition to your dessert table. These soft, buttery cookies are simple to make and can be decorated in a rainbow of colors using royal icing and sprinkles.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp almond extract
  • Royal icing (for decorating)
  • Sprinkles, edible glitter, or colored sugar

Directions:

  1. Make the Cookie Dough: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and baking powder. In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract and beat until combined. Gradually add the dry ingredients, mixing until the dough forms.

  2. Cut Out the Cookies: Roll the dough out on a floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness. Use an Easter egg-shaped cookie cutter to cut out the cookies and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  3. Bake the Cookies: Bake the cookies for 8-10 minutes, or until they just begin to turn golden around the edges. Let them cool on a wire rack before decorating.

  4. Decorate: Once the cookies have cooled, use royal icing and your favorite colorful decorations to create intricate designs on each cookie. You can also sprinkle edible glitter or colored sugar to give them an extra festive touch.

Conclusion

An Easter dessert table is an opportunity to get creative with colorful, delicious treats that will be the centerpiece of your holiday celebrations. These recipes offer a variety of flavors, from the sweet and tangy lemon meringue pie to the rich and comforting carrot cake. Each dessert brings a burst of color and joy to your table, making it the perfect way to celebrate the season with friends and family.

Whether you’re baking for a large family gathering or a small get-together, these Easter recipes will ensure that your dessert table is both beautiful and mouthwatering. Enjoy making these treats, and most importantly, enjoy the smiles and laughter they bring to your Easter celebration. Happy baking!
