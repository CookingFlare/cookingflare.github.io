Grilled Veggie Skewers: Colorful and Delicious Fourth of July Side


Grilled veggie skewers are a fantastic addition to any Fourth of July celebration. Not only are they visually appealing with their vibrant colors, but they also offer a burst of flavors that will leave your taste buds craving for more. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more veggies into your diet, these skewers are a perfect choice. So, fire up the grill and get ready to impress your guests with this simple yet impressive dish.

Ingredients

To make these grilled veggie skewers, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis
  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  • 1 red onion
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes
  • 8-10 button mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Meanwhile, soak wooden skewers in water for about 15 minutes to prevent them from burning.
  2. Wash and prepare the vegetables. Cut the zucchinis, bell peppers, and red onion into bite-sized pieces. Remove the stems from the mushrooms. Keep the cherry tomatoes whole.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. This will be the marinade for the veggies.
  4. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between the different veggies. This will not only make the skewers look appealing but also ensure even cooking.
  5. Brush the marinade generously over the skewered vegetables, coating them on all sides.
  6. Carefully place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning them occasionally to ensure they cook evenly. The vegetables should be tender and slightly charred.
  7. Once the skewers are cooked to your desired doneness, remove them from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Serve the grilled veggie skewers as a side dish alongside your favorite main course. They pair well with grilled meats, burgers, or even as a standalone vegetarian option.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different vegetables and seasonings to create your own unique flavor combinations. Try adding chunks of pineapple or marinating the veggies in a spicy barbecue sauce for an extra kick.
  • If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook these skewers in the oven. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  • Feel free to adjust the cooking time based on your preference. If you like your veggies more crunchy, reduce the cooking time. For softer vegetables, increase the cooking time.
  • These skewers can be prepared in advance and refrigerated. Simply marinate the vegetables and skewer them, then cover and refrigerate until ready to grill. This makes them a convenient choice for hosting parties or cookouts.

Grilled veggie skewers are not only a delicious and healthy side dish, but they also add a pop of color to your Fourth of July spread. With their smoky flavor and tender, charred veggies, they are sure to please everyone at your gathering. So, fire up the grill, grab your skewers, and get ready to impress your friends and family with this colorful and flavorful dish. Happy grilling!
