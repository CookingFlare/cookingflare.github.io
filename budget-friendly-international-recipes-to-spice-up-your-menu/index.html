Budget-Friendly International Recipes to Spice Up Your Menu


Are you looking to bring exciting flavors to your kitchen without breaking the bank? Cooking from different parts of the world doesn’t have to be expensive. In fact, many traditional international dishes use simple, affordable ingredients that pack a punch in terms of flavor. Whether you’re craving a hearty comfort meal or something fresh and vibrant, there’s a world of options waiting for you to explore. In this blog post, we’ll delve into a variety of budget-friendly international recipes that can spice up your menu without costing you a fortune.

1. Mexican Street Tacos

Mexican cuisine is a favorite for many, and one of the most popular, affordable, and customizable dishes is the humble street taco. These tacos are often filled with savory meats, fresh vegetables, and flavorful salsas. Making them at home is simple and cheap.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs or ground beef (chicken tends to be more budget-friendly)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa (store-bought or homemade)
  • Optional toppings: diced onions, radishes, avocado

Instructions:

  1. Cook the meat: In a skillet over medium heat, cook the chicken thighs or ground beef with onion and garlic until browned and cooked through. Add the cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper, and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the spices to release their flavors.
  2. Warm the tortillas: Heat the corn tortillas in a dry skillet for about 30 seconds on each side until they’re warm and slightly crispy.
  3. Assemble the tacos: Spoon the meat mixture onto each tortilla. Top with chopped cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and your choice of salsa.
  4. Serve and enjoy: Serve immediately with extra toppings like diced onions, radishes, or avocado if desired.

Budget Tip:

Chicken thighs are an affordable alternative to pricier cuts of meat, and buying corn tortillas in bulk can save you money. Opting for homemade salsa is also cost-effective, especially if you have extra tomatoes and herbs on hand.

2. Indian Chickpea Curry (Chana Masala)

Indian cuisine is known for its rich flavors and use of spices. Chana Masala is a simple yet flavorful dish that features chickpeas in a spicy, aromatic tomato-based sauce. It’s a staple in many Indian households and a fantastic option for a budget-friendly meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed (or 1 ½ cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the spices: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Cook the onions: Add chopped onion and cook for 5-7 minutes until softened and golden brown.
  3. Add garlic and ginger: Stir in garlic and ginger and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add tomatoes and spices: Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and the sauce thickens.
  5. Add chickpeas: Stir in the chickpeas and about 1 cup of water. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, letting the flavors meld together.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with rice or flatbread.

Budget Tip:

Canned chickpeas are very affordable and can be used to quickly prepare this dish. If you prefer to use dried chickpeas, soak them overnight to save some money. Buying bulk spices and using them in multiple recipes also ensures great value for money.

3. Italian Pasta Aglio e Olio

Sometimes, the simplest dishes are the most satisfying. Pasta Aglio e Olio, an Italian classic, is made with just a few basic ingredients – garlic, olive oil, and pasta – but the result is a deliciously savory meal.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti or your favorite pasta
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta: Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic slices and cook for 1-2 minutes until golden and fragrant, but not burnt.
  3. Combine: Add red pepper flakes and a pinch of salt to the garlic oil, then toss the cooked pasta into the pan. Add a little bit of reserved pasta cooking water if needed to create a silky sauce.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Budget Tip:

This dish is perfect for a last-minute, budget-friendly meal. By using only pantry staples, it’s an inexpensive but flavorful option. Red pepper flakes and garlic go a long way, so buying them in bulk will save you money.

4. Thai Green Curry with Vegetables

Thai food is known for its balance of sweet, spicy, sour, and salty flavors. While traditional Thai curries can sometimes require a variety of exotic ingredients, this vegetable-based green curry can be made affordably with ingredients you likely already have in your pantry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp green curry paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the curry base: In a large pot, combine the coconut milk, vegetable broth, green curry paste, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add vegetables: Add carrots, bell pepper, zucchini, and snap peas. Cook for 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the curry sauce has thickened.
  3. Finish the curry: Stir in the baby spinach and cook for another 2 minutes until wilted.
  4. Serve: Serve the curry over rice and garnish with fresh basil.

Budget Tip:

Using a variety of seasonal vegetables ensures that you’re not only keeping the cost low, but you’ll also enjoy the freshest ingredients available. If you have extra vegetables, feel free to add them to the curry. Green curry paste is a flavorful investment that will go a long way in many dishes.

5. Middle Eastern Falafel

Falafel is a popular street food across the Middle East and is made from chickpeas, herbs, and spices. These crispy, golden balls are typically served in pita bread with fresh veggies and tahini sauce, making for a satisfying and filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed (or 1 ½ cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch parsley, chopped
  • 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2-4 tbsp flour (as needed)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Pita bread or flatbread
  • Tahini sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the mixture: In a food processor, pulse chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, turmeric, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Add flour gradually until the mixture holds together but is not too sticky.
  2. Form the falafel: Use your hands to form small balls or patties with the falafel mixture.
  3. Fry the falafel: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel in batches, cooking for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Serve: Serve the falafel in pita bread with fresh vegetables and tahini sauce.

Budget Tip:

Using dried chickpeas instead of canned ones can lower the cost even further. Additionally, making your own tahini sauce with sesame paste, lemon juice, and olive oil is a great budget-friendly option compared to store-bought versions.

Final Thoughts

Exploring international cuisines doesn’t have to be expensive. By focusing on simple ingredients and cooking techniques, you can create delicious and flavorful meals that transport you to different parts of the world. Whether you’re enjoying the heat of Mexican street tacos, the aromatic spices of Indian chickpea curry, or the comforting simplicity of Italian pasta, these recipes are a fantastic way to add variety to your menu while staying within budget. So, grab your apron and get ready to spice up your cooking with these affordable international dishes!
