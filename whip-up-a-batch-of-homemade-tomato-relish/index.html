Whip Up a Batch of Homemade Tomato Relish


If you’re a fan of tangy and sweet condiments, then homemade tomato relish is a must-try. This versatile relish goes well with everything from grilled meats to sandwiches and can add a burst of flavor to any dish. The best part? It’s surprisingly easy to make at home, and the results are far superior to store-bought versions. So, roll up your sleeves, grab some fresh tomatoes, and let’s dive into the delightful world of homemade tomato relish.

Ingredients

Before we get started, let’s gather the ingredients we’ll need to make this delicious tomato relish:

  • 2kg ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups white vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for a spicy kick)

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by preparing your tomatoes, onions, and garlic as per the ingredient list.

  2. Cooking the Base: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, combine the tomatoes, onions, and garlic. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes have softened and released their juices. This should take about 30 minutes.

  3. Adding Flavor: Once the tomatoes have broken down, add the white vinegar, brown sugar, mustard seeds, ground ginger, ground allspice, salt, black pepper, and chili flakes (if using). Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Simmering: Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the relish has thickened to your desired consistency. Keep an eye on the relish and adjust the heat as needed to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot.

  5. Testing for Doneness: To test if the relish is ready, place a small amount on a chilled plate. If it doesn’t run and holds its shape, it’s done. If it’s too runny, continue cooking for a few more minutes.

  6. Cooling and Storing: Once the relish has reached the desired consistency, remove the pot from the heat and allow the relish to cool. Once cooled, transfer it to sterilized jars and seal. Store the jars in a cool, dark place. The relish will continue to develop its flavors over the next few weeks.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade tomato relish is incredibly versatile and can be used in various ways. Here are some serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Condiment: Use it as a condiment for burgers, sausages, or grilled meats.
  • Sandwich Spread: Slather it on sandwiches, wraps, or paninis for an added burst of flavor.
  • Dip: Mix it with cream cheese or sour cream for a delicious dip for crackers or vegetable sticks.
  • Ingredient: Add it to savory tarts, quiches, or as a topping for savory pies.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade tomato relish is a labor of love that rewards you with a delicious condiment that can elevate any dish. Experiment with the flavors and spices to tailor it to your taste. Once you’ve mastered the art of making your own relish, you’ll never go back to the store-bought version. So, grab those tomatoes and get ready to impress your taste buds and those of your friends and family with this delightful homemade tomato relish!
