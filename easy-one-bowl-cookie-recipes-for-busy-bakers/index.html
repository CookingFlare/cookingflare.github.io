Easy One-Bowl Cookie Recipes for Busy Bakers


If you love the idea of freshly baked cookies but don’t have the time or patience for a sink full of dishes, one-bowl cookie recipes are your new best friend. These uncomplicated treats offer a perfect solution for busy bakers who crave homemade goodness without the hassle. Whether you’re baking for yourself, your family, or a gathering, these recipes help you save time without compromising on flavor or quality.

Why One-Bowl Cookie Recipes Are Ideal for Busy Bakers

One-bowl cookie recipes simplify the baking process in two major ways. First, they reduce prep and cleanup time. There’s no need to juggle multiple mixing bowls or sift through a long list of utensils. Everything comes together in one bowl. Second, these recipes are designed to be straightforward and quick, making them perfect for those moments when you need to whip up a batch of cookies on short notice. As an added bonus, the simplicity of these recipes means they’re great for baking with kids.

Tips for Mastering One-Bowl Cookie Recipes

Before diving into the recipes, here are a few helpful tips to make your baking effortless and enjoyable:

  • Choose the right mixing bowl: Opt for a bowl that’s large enough to hold all your ingredients and allow thorough mixing without spills.
  • Use room-temperature ingredients: Butter, eggs, and other refrigerated ingredients mix more evenly when they’re not straight out of the fridge.
  • Be mindful of overmixing: While one-bowl recipes are simple, it’s important to mix just until combined to avoid dense or tough cookies.
  • Double-check your baking times: Because ovens vary, keep a close eye on your cookies to ensure they bake to perfection without overbrowning.

5 One-Bowl Cookie Recipes to Try

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Few things beat a batch of warm chocolate chip cookies. This one-bowl recipe keeps things simple without sacrificing the chewy center or golden edges we all love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and sugars together in a mixing bowl until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, mixing until incorporated.
  4. Stir in the flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix just until combined.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips using a spatula.
  6. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

2. Peanut Butter Cookies

Rich, nutty, and irresistibly buttery, these cookies are perfect for peanut butter lovers and come together in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • ½ cup packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine all ingredients in a mixing bowl and stir until smooth.
  3. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the baking sheet. Flatten each scoop with a fork, creating a crisscross pattern.
  4. Bake for 8–10 minutes. Let the cookies cool for a couple of minutes on the baking sheet before transferring them to a wire rack.

3. Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

For a wholesome treat packed with flavor, oatmeal raisin cookies are always a winner. This one-bowl recipe delivers chewy, cinnamon-spiced goodness.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1½ cups rolled oats
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup raisins

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and brown sugar in a mixing bowl until smooth.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla extract, mixing until combined.
  4. Stir in the oats, flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Mix just until combined.
  5. Fold in the raisins.
  6. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 10–12 minutes. Allow to cool before serving.

4. Double Chocolate Cookies

These fudgy cookies are a chocolate lover’s dream, featuring both cocoa powder and chocolate chips for maximum indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Stir together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar in a mixing bowl until smooth.
  3. Add the egg, egg yolk, and vanilla extract, mixing until homogenous.
  4. Sift in the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt, stirring until combined.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop dough onto the baking sheet and bake for 8–10 minutes. Let cool slightly before transferring to a wire rack.

5. Sugar Cookies

For a simple yet versatile cookie, this one-bowl sugar cookie recipe is perfect. Dress them up with sprinkles or icing for a festive touch, or enjoy them plain.

Ingredients:

  • ¾ cup (1½ sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and sugar in a mixing bowl until fluffy.
  3. Mix in the egg and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the flour, baking powder, and salt, stirring just until the dough comes together.
  5. Roll into golf ball-sized portions and flatten slightly on the baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 8–10 minutes. Let cool completely on a wire rack.

Why These Recipes Stand Out

These one-bowl cookie recipes not only save you precious time, but they also yield consistently delicious results. Each recipe is customizable, allowing you to swap ingredients or add your favorite mix-ins like nuts, dried fruit, or candies. They’re great for occasions big and small, and they accommodate bakers of all skill levels.

Final Thoughts

With these easy one-bowl cookie recipes at your fingertips, homemade cookies are within reach no matter how busy your schedule is. The simplicity, minimal cleanup, and irresistible flavors are a game-changer for anyone who loves baking without the fuss. So grab your mixing bowl, preheat the oven, and let the comforting aroma of cookies fill your kitchen.
