Homemade Thanksgiving Rolls: Warm and Flaky Breads for Your Feast


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and indulging in a feast fit for kings. And what feast would be complete without warm, fluffy rolls straight from the oven? In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating homemade Thanksgiving rolls that are not only mouthwateringly delicious but also incredibly easy to make. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get baking!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare a baking sheet by lining it with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Mix well to ensure the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  4. Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the yeast mixture, melted butter, and eggs. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients together until a dough forms.

  5. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, add a little more flour as needed.

  6. Shape the dough into a ball and place it back in the mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.

  7. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a floured surface and divide it into 12 equal portions.

  8. With each portion, gently roll it into a ball, tucking the edges underneath to form a smooth top. Place the rolls onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each one.

  9. Cover the baking sheet with the kitchen towel and let the rolls rise for another 30 minutes.

  10. After the second rise, remove the towel and bake the rolls in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until they turn golden brown on top.

  11. Once baked, remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack.

  12. Serve these warm and flaky Thanksgiving rolls alongside your feast, and watch as your guests swoon over their homemade goodness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a savory twist, you can add herbs like rosemary, thyme, or garlic powder to the dough before kneading.

  • If you prefer a sweeter roll, sprinkle some cinnamon and sugar on top of the rolls before baking.

  • Experiment with different shapes for your rolls, such as knots or crescents, to add visual appeal to your Thanksgiving table.

  • These rolls can be made ahead of time and frozen. Simply allow them to cool completely, place them in a freezer bag, and store them in the freezer for up to 3 months. When ready to serve, thaw them at room temperature and reheat in the oven for a few minutes.

Now that you have the secret to baking perfect homemade Thanksgiving rolls, there’s no excuse not to impress your loved ones this holiday season. Enjoy the warm, flaky goodness and let the aroma fill your home with love and gratitude. Happy Thanksgiving!
