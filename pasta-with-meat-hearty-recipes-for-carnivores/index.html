Pasta with Meat: Hearty Recipes for Carnivores


Pasta with meat is a classic pairing that has delighted food lovers around the world for centuries. Whether it’s the comforting simplicity of a Bolognese sauce or the bold flavors of a meaty ragu, combining pasta with hearty meat creates the perfect meal for carnivores. This post explores several mouthwatering pasta and meat recipes that will satisfy your cravings for rich, savory dishes. From classic favorites to innovative twists, there’s something here for every meat lover to enjoy.

1. Classic Beef Bolognese

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 large carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb spaghetti or tagliatelle
  • Freshly grated Parmesan for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bolognese Sauce: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat. Add the chopped onions, garlic, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened, about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Build the Flavor Base: Pour in the red wine and let it simmer for 2 minutes, allowing the alcohol to cook off. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, to let the flavors meld.

  3. Cook the Pasta: While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  4. Combine: Toss the cooked pasta with the Bolognese sauce until well coated. Serve hot with a generous sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan.

This beef Bolognese is a true Italian comfort food, with rich flavors from the meat and tomatoes and an aromatic touch from the herbs. It’s perfect for a cozy dinner or feeding a crowd.

2. Spaghetti and Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1 cup fresh breadcrumbs
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Make the Meatballs: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, eggs, Parmesan, garlic, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Mix until everything is well incorporated. Roll the mixture into meatballs, about 1-1.5 inches in diameter.

  2. Brown the Meatballs: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the meatballs in batches, browning them on all sides. Once browned, transfer the meatballs to a plate and set aside.

  3. Prepare the Sauce: In the same skillet, add the crushed tomatoes and a pinch of salt. Stir well, scraping up any browned bits left from the meatballs. Return the meatballs to the skillet and simmer for 25-30 minutes, until the meatballs are cooked through and the sauce thickens.

  4. Cook the Pasta: While the sauce is cooking, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta and set aside.

  5. Serve: Toss the spaghetti with the meatball sauce and serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

This dish is a timeless favorite, perfect for families and special occasions. The combination of tender meatballs and rich tomato sauce is irresistible and pairs perfectly with al dente pasta.

3. Pork and Sausage Ragu

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 lb Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup red wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb rigatoni or pappardelle
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Meat: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground pork and Italian sausage. Cook until browned, breaking the meat into smaller pieces with a wooden spoon. Drain any excess fat.

  2. Build the Ragu: Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pan, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Stir in the tomato paste, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer for 2 minutes, reducing slightly.

  3. Add Tomatoes and Simmer: Add the diced tomatoes to the pan, followed by the heavy cream. Stir everything together and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the flavors develop.

  4. Cook the Pasta: While the ragu is simmering, cook the pasta according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.

  5. Serve: Toss the cooked pasta with the pork and sausage ragu. Garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

This hearty ragu is packed with flavor, with the richness of the pork and sausage complemented by the creamy tomato sauce. It’s an excellent dish for anyone who enjoys bold, savory flavors.

4. Beef and Mushroom Stroganoff

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb egg noodles
  • Fresh dill or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced beef and cook for 2-3 minutes, until browned. Remove the beef from the pan and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pan, add the chopped onion and sliced mushrooms. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Season with paprika, salt, and pepper.

  3. Prepare the Sauce: Add the beef broth to the skillet, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits. Stir in the sour cream and Dijon mustard. Bring the sauce to a simmer, letting it cook for a few minutes until it thickens.

  4. Combine: Return the cooked beef to the pan, stirring to coat the meat in the creamy sauce. Cook for another 3-5 minutes, allowing the beef to reheat and absorb the flavors.

  5. Cook the Pasta: While the sauce is simmering, cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  6. Serve: Toss the egg noodles with the beef and mushroom stroganoff sauce. Garnish with fresh dill or parsley.

This beef and mushroom stroganoff is a rich and creamy dish that combines tender beef with earthy mushrooms and a velvety sauce. It’s perfect for a special dinner or when you want to indulge in something luxurious.

5. Lamb Ragu with Pappardelle

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb pappardelle or fettuccine
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Lamb: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the ground lamb and cook, breaking it apart with a spoon, until browned. Drain any excess fat.

  2. Build the Ragu: Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pan, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the tomato paste, cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper, allowing the spices to bloom.

  3. Add Tomatoes and Simmer: Pour in the white wine and let it cook for 2 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes, stirring to combine. Let the sauce simmer for 30-40 minutes, until the flavors are rich and the sauce thickens.

  4. Cook the Pasta: While the ragu is simmering, cook the pappardelle according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  5. Serve: Toss the pappardelle with the lamb ragu and garnish with fresh mint.

The lamb ragu is a unique and flavorful twist on the classic meat sauce, with spices that add warmth and depth. The pappardelle, with its broad ribbons, perfectly holds the rich sauce, making each bite satisfying and full of flavor.

Conclusion

Pasta with meat is an incredibly versatile dish that can range from classic to contemporary, all while providing the hearty, satisfying flavors that meat lovers crave. Whether you prefer a simple beef Bolognese, a comforting plate of spaghetti and meatballs, or a more exotic lamb ragu, these recipes offer something for every taste. Each dish brings together tender meats, savory sauces, and perfectly cooked pasta to create a meal that will fill you up and leave you craving more. These recipes are perfect for carnivores who want to indulge in delicious, meaty goodness.
