Delicious and Easy Chicken Enchilada Recipes


Chicken enchiladas are a crowd-pleasing, comfort-food classic that easily fits into any meal plan. They combine tender chicken, savory spices, rich sauces, and warm tortillas into a dish that’s both hearty and satisfying. Perfect for a weeknight dinner, potluck, or even a casual weekend gathering, chicken enchiladas are as versatile as they are delicious.

This post will guide you through everything you need to know about making delicious chicken enchiladas at home. Whether you’re a busy cook looking for a quick and easy meal or you want to experiment with flavors to make a recipe truly your own, we’ve got you covered. From traditional, cheesy chicken enchiladas to healthier and spicier takes, these recipes are easy to prepare, bursting with flavor, and guaranteed to impress.

What Are Chicken Enchiladas?

For those new to enchiladas, this iconic dish hails from Mexican cuisine. Enchiladas are typically made by rolling fillings – in this case, shredded chicken – into soft tortillas and covering them with a vibrant, flavorful sauce. They’re then baked to perfection, often topped with melted cheese, fresh herbs, or additional condiments like sour cream or guacamole.

Chicken enchiladas have gained widespread popularity because they are adaptable to various tastes and dietary preferences. You can easily customize the sauce (red, green, or creamy), level of spice, and additional fillings to suit your preferences. Plus, they’re great for meal prep, as they reheat beautifully for leftovers!

Tips for Perfect Chicken Enchiladas

Before diving into the recipes, here are some key tips to ensure your chicken enchiladas turn out perfect every time:

  1. Use Rotisserie or Pre-Cooked Chicken for Convenience
    Shredded rotisserie chicken is a fantastic time-saver when making chicken enchiladas. Simply remove the skin, shred the meat, and mix it with your chosen seasonings.

  2. Warm the Tortillas
    To prevent tortillas from breaking when rolling, warm them for a few seconds in the microwave or on a hot skillet. This makes them more pliable and easier to work with.

  3. Choose the Right Sauce
    The sauce is key to the flavor of your enchiladas. Red enchilada sauce offers a robust, smoky flavor, while green tomatillo sauce provides a tangy and slightly spicy kick. For a creamier twist, consider using a sour cream-based sauce.

  4. Bake for Just the Right Amount of Time
    Over-baking can dry out the enchiladas, while under-baking might leave them too soggy. Aim for about 20-30 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  5. Don’t Skimp on Toppings
    Fresh toppings like chopped cilantro, diced onions, or a dollop of favorite sauces can make all the difference. These ingredients add vibrant flavors and textures that elevate the dish.

Classic Chicken Enchiladas Recipe

This classic chicken enchilada recipe is a must-try. It combines tender chicken with a rich and smoky red enchilada sauce and plenty of gooey cheese, resulting in a universally loved dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked, shredded chicken
  • 2 cups red enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
  • 8 small flour or corn tortillas
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (for garnish)
  • Guacamole, sour cream, or diced onions (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken Filling
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded chicken, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and 1/2 cup of red enchilada sauce. Toss to coat the chicken evenly.

  2. Warm the Tortillas
    Heat the tortillas in a microwave or on a dry skillet for a few seconds to soften them.

  3. Assemble the Enchiladas
    Spoon a portion of the chicken mixture onto each tortilla. Sprinkle some cheese over the filling, roll it up tightly, and place it seam-side down in a greased 9 x 13-inch baking dish.

  4. Add Sauce and Cheese
    Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas, ensuring they are coated. Sprinkle the rest of the cheese on top.

  5. Bake
    Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the enchiladas for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  6. Garnish and Serve
    Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them sit for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh cilantro and your favorite toppings.

Healthier Chicken Enchiladas with Green Sauce

Looking for a lighter option? Swap out some of the traditional ingredients for healthier alternatives without sacrificing flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded, cooked chicken breast
  • 2 cups green tomatillo enchilada sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt or low-fat sour cream
  • 8 whole-grain tortillas
  • 1 cup reduced-fat shredded cheese
  • 1 small jalapeño, thinly sliced (optional, for heat)
  • 1/4 cup diced avocado (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the shredded chicken, 1 cup of green enchilada sauce, and Greek yogurt until well combined.

  2. Warm the tortillas to make them pliable. Spoon the chicken mixture onto each tortilla, roll them tightly, and place seam-side down in a greased baking dish.

  3. Pour the remaining green enchilada sauce over the tortillas, sprinkle with cheese, and add jalapeño slices if using.

  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20 minutes. Garnish with avocado and enjoy a healthier twist on a classic!

Spicy Chipotle Chicken Enchiladas

For those who enjoy their food with a kick, these chipotle chicken enchiladas are the perfect recipe. The smoky heat of chipotle peppers paired with creamy cheese takes this dish to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded chicken
  • 1-2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, finely chopped
  • 1 cup red enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup shredded pepper jack cheese
  • 8 corn or flour tortillas
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Blend the shredded chicken with the red enchilada sauce and chipotle peppers until well coated.

  2. Assemble the enchiladas by placing chicken in warmed tortillas, rolling them up tightly, and arranging them in a baking dish.

  3. Cover with more enchilada sauce, sprinkle with shredded pepper jack cheese, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes.

  4. Garnish with diced red onions and serve with lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

Wrapping It Up

Chicken enchiladas offer endless possibilities for customization. Whether you stick to the classic recipe or get creative with healthier ingredients and bold flavors, these recipes are sure to become a staple in your kitchen. With simple preparation techniques and a wide range of topping options, chicken enchiladas strike the perfect balance between easy cooking and delicious results.

So grab your ingredients, preheat the oven, and get ready to whip up one of the best homemade meals you’ll ever try. Your taste buds – and your dinner guests – will thank you!
