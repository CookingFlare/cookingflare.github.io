10 Easy Fruit-Based Dessert Recipes for Summer


Summer is the perfect season to indulge in refreshing, vibrant fruit-based desserts. With an abundance of fresh produce available, there's never been a better time to take advantage of seasonal fruits. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, enjoying a picnic, or simply craving a sweet treat on a hot day, these ten easy fruit-based dessert recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth while keeping things light and refreshing.

  1. Classic Strawberry Shortcake When it comes to classic summer desserts, almost nothing can compare to a homemade strawberry shortcake. This delightful treat combines fluffy biscuits with sweet, juicy strawberries and whipped cream. Ingredients:

1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced 1/4 cup granulated sugar 2 cups all-purpose flour 1 tablespoon baking powder 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed 3/4 cup heavy cream (plus extra for whipping) 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

In a bowl, combine sliced strawberries and sugar. Mix well and let them sit for about 30 minutes to macerate. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add the heavy cream and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead gently. Roll out to about 1-inch thickness and cut into rounds. Place the rounds onto a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown. Once cooled, slice the biscuits in half, layer with strawberries and whipped cream, and enjoy!

  1. Peach Melba Sundaes A dessert that combines the sweetness of peaches with the coolness of ice cream, Peach Melba Sundaes are a summer favorite. Ingredients:

3 ripe peaches, blanched, peeled, and halved 1 cup raspberry sauce 4 cups vanilla ice cream Fresh raspberries for garnish Mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

In a saucepan, heat the raspberry sauce gently until warm. Place a peach half in a dessert bowl. Scoop a generous portion of vanilla ice cream on top. Drizzle warm raspberry sauce over the ice cream and peach. Garnish with fresh raspberries and mint leaves if desired. Serve immediately for a deliciously cool summer treat!

  1. Watermelon Sorbet Light, refreshing, and deliciously fruity, watermelon sorbet is the perfect way to beat the summer heat. Ingredients:

4 cups watermelon, cubed and seeded 1/4 cup lime juice 1/4 cup granulated sugar (adjust based on sweetness preference) Pinch of salt

Instructions:

Puree the watermelon in a blender until smooth. Pour the watermelon puree into a bowl and stir in lime juice, sugar, and salt. Freeze the mixture for about 4-5 hours, stirring every hour to break up ice crystals. Once frozen solid, allow the sorbet to sit at room temperature for a few minutes before scooping. Serve in chilled bowls, garnished with lime zest or fresh mint.

  1. Tropical Fruit Salad with Coconut Whipped Cream A vibrant fruit salad mixed with tropical flavors is perfect for any summer occasion. The coconut whipped cream adds a luscious, dairy-free touch. Ingredients:

1 cup diced pineapple 1 cup diced mango 1 cup sliced kiwi 1 cup sliced strawberries 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk, refrigerated overnight 2 tablespoons powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine pineapple, mango, kiwi, and strawberries. To make the coconut whipped cream, open the chilled can of coconut milk. Scoop out the solidified cream into a mixing bowl, discarding the liquid. Whip the coconut cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until fluffy. Serve the tropical fruit salad with a dollop of coconut whipped cream on top. Enjoy this colorful and refreshing dessert!

  1. Chocolate-Dipped Frozen Bananas Perfect for hot summer days, chocolate-dipped frozen bananas are both easy to make and decadent. Ingredients:

2 ripe bananas 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips 1 tablespoon coconut oil (optional) Sprinkles, nuts, or coconut flakes for topping

Instructions:

Slice bananas into thick, bite-sized pieces or leave them whole for a more indulgent treat. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and arrange the banana pieces on it. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine chocolate chips and coconut oil. Heat in 20-second intervals, stirring in between, until smooth. Dip the banana pieces into the melted chocolate and place them back on the baking sheet. Top with sprinkles, nuts, or coconut flakes, if desired. Freeze for at least 1-2 hours before serving. Enjoy these cool treats any time!

  1. Blueberry Lemon Bars These tangy lemon bars are made even better with the addition of fresh blueberries. They are tart, sweet, and utterly delicious. Ingredients:

1 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 cup powdered sugar 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened 2 eggs 1 cup granulated sugar Zest and juice of 2 lemons 1 teaspoon baking powder 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, mix together flour, powdered sugar, and butter until a dough forms. Press into the bottom of a greased 8x8 inch baking pan. Bake the crust for 15 minutes. In the meantime, whisk together eggs, granulated sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice, and baking powder. Fold in fresh blueberries gently before pouring the filling over the baked crust. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes or until set. Let cool before slicing into bars. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

  1. Pineapple Coconut Popsicles These homemade pineapple coconut popsicles are a tropical getaway in a frozen treat! Ingredients:

2 cups fresh pineapple, diced 1 cup coconut milk 2 tablespoons honey (or maple syrup) 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

In a blender, combine diced pineapple, coconut milk, honey, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds, leaving a small space at the top for expansion. Insert sticks and freeze for at least 4 hours or until solid. To remove popsicles, run warm water over the outside of the molds for a few seconds. Enjoy these fruity popsicles as a refreshing treat on a hot summer day!

  1. Raspberry Fool Raspberry fool is an elegant and easy dessert that showcases fresh raspberries beautifully. Ingredients:

2 cups fresh raspberries 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 cup heavy cream 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

In a bowl, mash half of the raspberries with the sugar until they are slightly pureed. Leave the other half whole. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream with the vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Gently fold the raspberry puree into the whipped cream, leaving swirls visible for a marbled effect. Spoon the raspberry fool into serving dishes and top with the remaining whole raspberries. Chill for about 30 minutes before serving for the best flavor and texture.

  1. Cherry Clafoutis A French classic, Cherry Clafoutis is a custardy dessert pie filled with sweet cherries—a must-try for cherry lovers. Ingredients:

2 cups fresh cherries, pitted 3 large eggs 1 cup milk 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 teaspoon salt Butter for greasing the baking dish

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a pie dish with butter. Arrange pitted cherries in the bottom of the dish. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, sugar, and vanilla until smooth. Gradually mix in flour and salt until well combined. Pour the batter over the cherries and bake for 35-40 minutes or until golden and set. Let it cool slightly and serve warm or at room temperature, dusted with powdered sugar.

  1. Mango Mousse This light and airy mango mousse is a tropical delight that will elevate any summer gathering. Ingredients:

2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced 1/2 cup heavy cream 1/4 cup sugar 1 teaspoon gelatin (optional, for added stability) 2 tablespoons water (if using gelatin)

Instructions:

If using gelatin, dissolve it in water and let it sit for 5 minutes to bloom. Puree the diced mangoes in a blender until smooth. If using gelatin, microwave it for a few seconds to dissolve and then add it to the mango puree. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream and sugar until soft peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mango puree until fully combined. Spoon into serving dishes and chill for at least an hour before serving.

Conclusion Summer is a splendid time to celebrate the abundance of fresh fruits available to us. These ten easy fruit-based dessert recipes not only taste fantastic but are simple to prepare, allowing you to spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the warm sunshine. From the classic strawberry shortcake to the tropical delight of mango mousse, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So gather your ingredients, invite friends over, or just treat yourself to a sweet summer indulgence. Happy cooking!
