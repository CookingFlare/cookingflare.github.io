Best Side Dishes for Your Taco Night


Taco night is more than just a meal—it’s an occasion filled with flavor, variety, and endless possibilities. Whether you’re hosting friends, preparing a family dinner, or simply treating yourself, tacos are the ultimate customizable dish. But let’s be honest: as much as we love tacos, a truly spectacular taco night isn’t complete without the right side dishes.

Carefully selected sides can complement your taco spread, elevate the overall experience, and even steal the show with bold flavors and textures. From creamy dips to zesty salads, crispy snacks to refreshing beverages, there’s a world of options to explore. Let’s dive into some of the best side dishes you can serve alongside your tacos to create a memorable taco night feast.

Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

A star of Mexican cuisine, elote is a must-have side dish for taco night. Grilled corn on the cob coated with a tantalizing mixture of mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, and spices, elote is a burst of smoky, creamy, and spicy goodness. Top it off with crumbled cotija cheese and a sprinkle of fresh cilantro, and you’ll have a crowd-pleaser that pairs beautifully with every taco recipe.

Pro tip: If you’re preparing for a large gathering or looking for something less messy, transform elote into “esquites”—a street corn salad served in cups. This variation offers the same delicious flavor but in a more scoopable form.

Refried Beans

No taco night is complete without refried beans. Their creamy texture and savory flavor make them the perfect complement to tacos. Traditionally made with pinto beans, refried beans are often seasoned with garlic, onion, cumin, and a dash of chili powder. Whether served as a side dish or used as a filling for vegetarian tacos, they’re guaranteed to satisfy.

For a healthier twist, consider using black beans. Black refried beans boast the same rich texture while adding a slightly earthier flavor profile to your meal. Drizzle them with a touch of olive oil or a sprinkle of queso fresco for added flair.

Spanish Rice

Spanish rice, also known as Mexican rice, is a colorful and flavorful dish that adds warmth to your taco night spread. Cooked with tomatoes, onions, and garlic, this rice dish is subtly spiced and infused with smoky goodness. The vibrant orange hue and hearty texture pair perfectly with any taco filling.

For a creative variation, try cilantro lime rice. The tangy, aromatic freshness of lime and cilantro makes this dish a lighter option, especially if you’re serving fish or vegetarian tacos. Its bright flavor serves as an excellent counterbalance to spicy ingredients.

Guacamole and Chips

It’s hard to imagine tacos without guacamole, but this creamy avocado dip truly shines when served as a standalone side. Rich, smooth, and packed with flavor, guacamole can be as simple or as elaborate as you like. Mash your avocados, mix in lime juice, salt, and diced onion, and fold in plenty of chopped cilantro; then let your guests scoop it up with crispy tortilla chips.

If you’re feeling adventurous, add ingredients like diced tomatoes, minced garlic, or roasted jalapeños for a unique twist. And if you want something lighter, stay traditional with a classic salsa verde or pico de gallo for dipping.

Fresh Mango Salsa

For a refreshing side dish that surprises and delights, look no further than mango salsa. The sweetness of ripe mango contrasts beautifully with the tangy lime juice, spicy jalapeños, and fresh cilantro. Toss in diced red onion and bell peppers for added crunch and flavor complexity.

This tropical salsa complements pork, fish, and shrimp tacos especially well, adding a touch of brightness to your plate. Serve it chilled for maximum refreshment—it’s the perfect antidote to any heat-heavy taco seasoning.

Tortilla Soup

On colder nights, why not warm things up with a bowl of tortilla soup? This comforting side dish is a vibrant medley of bold flavors—chicken or vegetable broth base infused with smoky tomatoes, onions, garlic, chili peppers, and cumin. Topped with crispy tortilla strips, sliced avocado, shredded cheese, and a dollop of sour cream, this soup is a hearty addition to taco night.

For a vegetarian option, skip the chicken and load your soup with black beans, hominy, and zucchini. It’s every bit as satisfying, and adds a depth of flavor that pairs wonderfully with any taco spread.

Grilled Vegetables

Add some charred, smoky goodness to your taco night with a platter of grilled vegetables. Zucchini, bell peppers, poblano peppers, onions, and portobello mushrooms are excellent choices. Toss them with olive oil, salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of chili powder before grilling to enhance their natural flavors.

These vegetables can be served on the side or as taco toppings, offering a versatile option for anyone at the table. Plus, they bring some extra nutrition to the spread without compromising on flavor.

Black Bean and Corn Salad

Black bean and corn salad is a crowd-pleasing side dish for any taco night. This colorful blend pairs sweet corn kernels with black beans, diced red onion, and fresh cilantro, all brought together with a zesty lime dressing. It’s incredibly versatile—serve it as a scoopable salad, a dip for chips, or even as a topping for tacos.

For added flair, mix in some diced avocado or crumbled feta cheese. The creamy texture creates a delightful contrast with the crisp ingredients, elevating this dish to a whole new level.

Pickled Vegetables

For a tangy bite that cuts through rich flavors, pickled vegetables are an ideal taco side dish. Quick-pickle carrots, radishes, onions, or jalapeños with vinegar, sugar, and spices for a vibrant addition to your spread. Their acidity provides a refreshing contrast to heavy or spicy taco fillings.

Not only are pickled vegetables flavorful, but they’re also versatile—serve them on their own, as a garnish, or incorporate them into dishes like salads for added texture. The bright colors and bold flavors are sure to stand out.

Cilantro Lime Slaw

Tired of the usual coleslaw? Mix things up with cilantro lime slaw. This fresh and light dish swaps out heavy mayonnaise for a vibrant dressing of lime juice, olive oil, and a touch of honey. Toss shredded cabbage, carrots, and thinly sliced red onion in the tangy dressing, and finish off with a sprinkle of chopped cilantro.

Cilantro lime slaw is not only a fantastic side dish but also works as a taco topping, adding crunch, color, and flavor without overpowering the main ingredients.

Jalapeño Poppers

Bring some heat to your taco night with jalapeño poppers. These spicy treats are ideal appetizers or accompaniments to tacos. Stuff halved jalapeños with cream cheese, shredded cheddar, and cooked bacon, then bake or grill them until crispy and golden.

For a healthier version, swap cream cheese for Greek yogurt or try vegetarian-friendly fillings like black beans and avocado. The kick of spice paired with creamy and savory filling makes jalapeño poppers irresistible.

Mexican Dessert Options

To round out your taco night, consider serving authentic Mexican desserts. Churros with a dipping sauce of chocolate or caramel are a popular winner. Alternatively, arrange a platter of fresh-cut tropical fruits like papaya, pineapple, and watermelon for a refreshing and light end to the meal. For those craving creamy indulgence, traditional flan or arroz con leche (Mexican rice pudding) make excellent choices to satisfy the sweet tooth.

Taco night is inherently festive, and the side dishes you choose to serve alongside your tacos set the tone for the entire meal. Whether you opt for vibrant salads, hearty beans, creamy dips, or spicy snacks, these dishes offer a variety of complementary textures, flavors, and aromas to elevate your spread. Plan your sides with care, and your taco night will transform into an unforgettable celebration of flavor. Let your creativity guide you, and don’t be afraid to explore. After all, the best meals are not just eaten—they’re experienced.
