Homemade Nut-Free Apple Butter for a Sweet and Spiced Spread


Apple butter is a delightful and versatile spread that adds a burst of autumn flavors to your favorite dishes. While traditional recipes often include nuts, this homemade version is nut-free, making it perfect for those with allergies or dietary restrictions. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a delicious, sweet, and spiced apple butter that will elevate your breakfasts, snacks, and desserts. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds of apples (a mix of sweet and tart varieties)
  • 1 cup of apple cider or apple juice
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Wash, peel, and core the apples. Cut them into small chunks or slices. Make sure to remove any seeds or tough parts.
  2. In a large pot, combine the apples, apple cider, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Stir well to evenly distribute the spices and sugars.
  3. Place the pot over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally. The apples should become tender and start to break down.
  4. Once the apples are soft, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  5. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, carefully puree the apple mixture until smooth. If using a regular blender, blend in batches to avoid overflow.
  6. Return the blended mixture to the pot and place it back over low heat. Stir in the vanilla extract and let it simmer for an additional 30 minutes to thicken the apple butter.
  7. While the apple butter is simmering, prepare your canning jars by washing them thoroughly and sterilizing them in boiling water for a few minutes.
  8. Once the apple butter has thickened to your desired consistency, carefully ladle it into the prepared canning jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top.
  9. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue. Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip tight.
  10. Process the filled jars in a boiling water canner for 10 minutes. Make sure the water covers the jars by at least 1 inch.
  11. After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and let them cool on a towel or wire rack. As they cool, you should hear the satisfying “pop” of the lids sealing.
  12. Once completely cooled, store the apple butter in a cool, dark place for up to one year. Once opened, refrigerate and use within a few weeks.

Serving Suggestions

Now that you have your homemade nut-free apple butter, let’s explore some delicious ways to enjoy it:

  • Spread it on toast, bagels, or English muffins for a quick and flavorful breakfast.
  • Use it as a filling for pastries, turnovers, or thumbprint cookies.
  • Swirl it into oatmeal or yogurt for a touch of sweetness and warmth.
  • Drizzle it over pancakes or waffles for a delightful twist on a classic breakfast.
  • Add a dollop to your favorite ice cream or yogurt for a decadent dessert.
  • Use it as a glaze for roasted meats, such as pork or chicken, for a unique and flavorful twist.

The possibilities are endless when it comes to homemade apple butter. Feel free to experiment and discover your favorite ways to enjoy this delicious spread.

So there you have it – a simple and nut-free recipe for homemade apple butter. With its sweet and spiced flavors, this spread is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Enjoy the taste of fall all year round with this delightful and versatile condiment. Happy cooking!
