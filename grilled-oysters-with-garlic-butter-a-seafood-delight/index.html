Grilled Oysters with Garlic Butter: A Seafood Delight


Grilled Oysters with Garlic Butter is a delectable seafood dish that you’ll want to savor every bite of. This tantalizing appetizer takes just minutes to prepare, yet delivers an explosion of complex flavors that is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 2 dozen fresh, medium-sized oysters, in the shell
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon horseradish
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tray oyster shells, or a baking sheet, for grilling

Preparation:

  1. Begin by preparing your oyster shells. If using oyster shells, rinse them thoroughly in cool water. Dry each shell and set aside. If using a baking sheet, lightly oil the surface to prevent sticking.
  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine the softened butter, minced garlic, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and horseradish. Mix well until all ingredients are incorporated.
  3. Season the garlic butter mixture with a pinch of salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Carefully open each oyster and gently remove the oyster meat from its shell. Be sure not to puncture the oyster as it will spill its juices.
  5. Gently place the oyster meat onto the prepared oyster shells or baking sheet, reserving the shells for later use. If using a baking sheet, arrange the oysters, slightly apart, to prevent them from sticking together.
  6. Generously spoon the garlic butter mixture over each oyster, covering the entire surface of the oyster meat.
  7. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  8. Once your grill is hot, place the oyster shells or baking sheet directly onto the grilling grates.
  9. Grill the oysters for approximately 8-10 minutes, or until the edges of the oyster meat start to curl and the garlic butter is slightly golden brown.
  10. Remove the oysters from the grill and allow them to cool for a few minutes.
  11. Place the cooked oysters back into their reserved shells, if using, or serve directly from the baking sheet.
  12. Garnish your grilled oysters with a dab of extra garlic butter or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Enjoy!

Tips:

Do you have an oyster shucking party coming up, or just want tips on shucking? Check out this guide on how to shuck oysters like a pro: Link to guide.

Oysters are best served fresh and on the day they’re shucked. To ensure your oysters stay fresh, rinse them in cold water and store them underwater, in the refrigerator, for up to two days.

Grilled Oysters with Garlic Butter is a versatile dish that can be served as a stunning appetizer or a delicious main course. Dress it up with some fresh greens or crusty bread for the perfect meal.

So, enhance your next gathering or cookout by adding this scrumptious and mouthwatering seafood delight to your menu. Grilled Oysters with Garlic Butter is not only simple to prepare, but it’s also guaranteed to impress your friends and family. Enjoy the flavors of the sea in every bite!
