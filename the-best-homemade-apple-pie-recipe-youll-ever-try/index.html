The Best Homemade Apple Pie Recipe You'll Ever Try


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the house. The combination of sweet, cinnamon-spiced apples and a flaky, buttery crust is a timeless classic that never fails to delight. If you’ve been searching for the perfect homemade apple pie recipe, look no further. This tried and true recipe is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 large apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced (use a mix of sweet and tart varieties like Granny Smith and Honeycrisp)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually drizzle in the ice water, stirring with a fork, until the dough begins to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  3. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, gently toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and vanilla extract. Add this mixture to the apples and toss until the apples are evenly coated.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one of the chilled dough disks into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides.

  3. Pour the apple filling into the prepared crust, mounding it slightly in the center.

  4. Roll out the second dough disk and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  5. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  2. If the edges of the crust begin to darken too quickly, cover them with foil halfway through baking.

  3. Allow the pie to cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow the filling to set.

Serving and Enjoying

This homemade apple pie is best enjoyed warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The tender, perfectly spiced apples encased in a flaky, golden crust are a true testament to the beauty of homemade baking.

So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of fresh apples, don’t hesitate to whip up this irresistible apple pie. Whether it’s for a special occasion or simply to indulge in a comforting treat, this recipe is guaranteed to deliver a slice of perfection with every bite.
