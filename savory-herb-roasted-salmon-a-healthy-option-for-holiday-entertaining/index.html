Savory Herb-Roasted Salmon: A Healthy Option for Holiday Entertaining


Holiday entertaining can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to pleasing the palates of your guests. With so many delicious yet unhealthy options available, it’s easy to get caught up in the temptation of rich, heavy dishes. However, this year, consider offering a healthier alternative that’s just as flavorful and satisfying: savory herb-roasted salmon.

This recipe is a game-changer for several reasons. First, salmon is an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, which have been shown to have numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving heart health. Additionally, this dish is incredibly easy to prepare, requiring minimal effort and cleanup. And, with the bold flavors of herbs like thyme and rosemary, you’ll be able to impress your guests without breaking the bank or sacrificing flavor.

To get started, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil or parchment paper, leaving a small border around the edges to prevent the fish from sticking.

Next, season a 6-ounce (170g) salmon fillet with salt and pepper. You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice if you like. Set the fillet aside for the moment.

In a small bowl, mix together 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh thyme, 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh rosemary, and 1 minced garlic clove. This herb mixture will provide the savory flavor that sets this dish apart from more traditional holiday fare.

Place the salmon fillet on the prepared baking sheet, skin side down (if it has skin). Brush the herb mixture evenly over the fish, making sure to cover every surface. You can also add some lemon slices or sprigs of fresh herbs for added visual appeal.

Roast the salmon in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C). You can check for doneness by inserting a fork into the thickest part of the fish; if it flakes easily, it’s cooked to perfection.

Once the salmon is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, making the fish even more tender and flavorful.

To serve, place the salmon fillet on a platter or individual plates, garnished with additional fresh herbs if desired. Serve with your choice of sides, such as roasted vegetables, quinoa, or a simple green salad.

This savory herb-roasted salmon is sure to impress your holiday guests, and it’s a great way to incorporate some healthier options into your menu. With its bold flavors and impressive presentation, this dish is sure to become a new holiday tradition.

Ingredients

  • 6-ounce (170g) salmon fillet
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 minced garlic clove
  • Lemon slices or sprigs of fresh herbs (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil or parchment paper.
  3. Season the salmon fillet with salt and pepper.
  4. Mix together the olive oil, thyme, rosemary, and garlic in a small bowl.
  5. Place the salmon fillet on the prepared baking sheet, skin side down.
  6. Brush the herb mixture evenly over the fish.
  7. Roast the salmon in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).
  8. Remove the salmon from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes.
  9. Serve the salmon on a platter or individual plates, garnished with additional fresh herbs if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra boost of flavor, add a tablespoon of Dijon mustard or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to the herb mixture.
  • Substitute other herbs like parsley, basil, or dill for the thyme and rosemary.
  • Serve the salmon with a side of roasted vegetables, quinoa, or a simple green salad.
  • Consider using other types of fish, such as cod or tilapia, if you prefer.
