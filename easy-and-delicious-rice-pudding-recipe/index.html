Easy and Delicious Rice Pudding Recipe


If you’re looking for a comforting and easy dessert that’s perfect for any occasion, then you’ve come to the right place. Rice pudding is a timeless classic that has been enjoyed by people all over the world for generations. This simple yet indulgent treat is made with basic ingredients that you probably already have in your kitchen, and it’s a great way to use up leftover rice. In this blog post, I’ll share with you my favorite rice pudding recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your dessert repertoire.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: Cinnamon powder, nutmeg, or fresh fruit

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: Start by rinsing the rice under cold water to remove any excess starch. Then, in a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice and 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water has been absorbed.

  2. Add the Milk and Sugar: Once the rice is cooked, pour in the whole milk and add the granulated sugar, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  3. Simmer the Pudding: Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom of the pan. Let the pudding simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until it reaches a creamy consistency. Make sure to keep an eye on it and stir frequently to ensure even cooking.

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Once the rice pudding has thickened to your desired consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. You can serve the rice pudding warm or chilled, according to your preference. If desired, sprinkle a little extra cinnamon powder or nutmeg on top, or add some fresh fruit for a pop of color and flavor.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Variations: Feel free to customize your rice pudding by adding different flavorings such as almond extract, orange zest, or a splash of rum for an adult twist.
  • Storage: Leftover rice pudding can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it gently on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving.
  • Texture: If you prefer a smoother pudding, you can blend a portion of the cooked rice and milk mixture before simmering to achieve a creamier consistency.

Rice pudding is a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with your favorite toppings. Whether you serve it warm on a cold winter’s night or chilled on a hot summer day, this easy and delicious rice pudding recipe is sure to delight your taste buds and bring a touch of nostalgia to your table.

So, why not give it a try and experience the comforting joy of homemade rice pudding? It’s a simple yet satisfying treat that’s perfect for sharing with family and friends. Let me know in the comments how your rice pudding turns out and if you have any other favorite variations to share. Happy cooking!
