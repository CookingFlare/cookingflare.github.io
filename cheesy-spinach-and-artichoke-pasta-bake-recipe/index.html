Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake Recipe


If you’re looking for a comforting, cheesy, and indulgent dish that combines rich flavors with the goodness of vegetables, this Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake is the perfect recipe to try. With tender pasta, creamy cheese sauce, sautéed spinach, and artichokes, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your weekly dinner rotation. It’s easy to prepare, perfect for family gatherings, or even to make ahead for a busy weeknight.

Why You’ll Love This Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake

The combination of creamy spinach, tangy artichokes, and melty cheese makes this pasta bake a satisfying meal. The flavors blend together beautifully, creating a hearty and filling dish that everyone will love. Whether you’re serving it for a special occasion or just as a quick weeknight dinner, this recipe is sure to impress. The best part? It’s easy to make and can be prepped ahead of time for convenience.

Here’s why this dish stands out:

  • Comforting and Cheesy: The combination of three kinds of cheese – cream cheese, mozzarella, and Parmesan – gives this pasta bake its signature creamy and cheesy texture.
  • Packed with Vegetables: Spinach and artichokes add fiber, vitamins, and nutrients to the dish while balancing out the richness of the cheese.
  • Versatile and Adaptable: You can easily tweak this recipe based on your preferences or what you have on hand. Add extra veggies, swap the pasta, or even add some chicken or sausage if you want a protein-packed dish.
  • Make-Ahead-Friendly: You can prep the ingredients ahead of time and bake it just before serving, making it perfect for meal planning.

Let’s dive into the details of making this delicious cheesy spinach and artichoke pasta bake.

Ingredients for Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake

For this recipe, you’ll need a variety of ingredients to bring together the perfect creamy, cheesy pasta bake. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pasta:

  • Pasta of Your Choice: You can use any pasta shape you like for this recipe. Penne, rotini, or fusilli work especially well because they hold the sauce nicely. Around 12 ounces (340 grams) is the ideal amount for 4-6 servings.

For the Spinach and Artichoke Filling:

  • Fresh Spinach: About 4 cups of fresh spinach leaves, chopped. If you prefer frozen spinach, make sure to thaw and drain it thoroughly.
  • Artichoke Hearts: 1 can (14 ounces) of artichoke hearts, drained and chopped. You can also use frozen artichokes, but be sure to thaw and drain them before using.
  • Cream Cheese: 8 ounces of softened cream cheese for that rich, creamy texture.
  • Sour Cream: ½ cup of sour cream to add tanginess and creaminess.
  • Garlic: 2 cloves of garlic, minced, for an aromatic base.
  • Onion: 1 small onion, finely chopped, to complement the flavors of the spinach and artichokes.

For the Cheese Sauce:

  • Mozzarella Cheese: 1 ½ cups of shredded mozzarella for that melty, gooey goodness.
  • Parmesan Cheese: ½ cup of freshly grated Parmesan for added depth of flavor.
  • Milk: 1 ½ cups of whole milk to help create the smooth, creamy sauce.
  • Butter: 3 tablespoons of butter for a silky sauce.
  • Flour: 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour to thicken the sauce.
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste.
  • Nutmeg: A pinch of ground nutmeg to bring a warm, comforting note to the dish (optional but highly recommended).

For the Topping:

  • Breadcrumbs: 1 cup of panko breadcrumbs for a crispy topping.
  • Extra Mozzarella: ½ cup of shredded mozzarella for sprinkling on top, to create a golden, bubbly crust.

How to Make Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by cooking your pasta according to the package instructions. Make sure to cook it just until al dente, as it will continue cooking in the oven. Once the pasta is done, drain it and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

While the pasta is cooking, heat a large pan over medium heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, sauté the chopped onion until it’s translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant.

Next, add the fresh spinach to the pan and cook it down for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until wilted. If you’re using frozen spinach, make sure it’s fully thawed and drained before adding it to the pan.

Once the spinach is cooked, add the chopped artichokes to the pan and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the pan from heat and set it aside.

Step 3: Make the Cheese Sauce

In a separate medium-sized saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, sprinkle in the flour and whisk it together to form a roux. Cook for about 1-2 minutes to eliminate the raw flour taste.

Gradually add the milk to the roux while whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Continue to cook the mixture until it begins to thicken, about 3-4 minutes. Once the sauce is thick and smooth, stir in the cream cheese, sour cream, and a pinch of nutmeg.

Now, stir in the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese, one handful at a time, allowing each to melt completely before adding more. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Once the cheese sauce is creamy and smooth, remove from heat.

Step 4: Combine Everything

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked pasta, sautéed spinach and artichoke mixture, and the cheese sauce. Stir everything together until the pasta is fully coated in the creamy sauce and the vegetables are evenly distributed.

Step 5: Assemble the Bake

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with a little butter or non-stick spray. Transfer the pasta and sauce mixture into the baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

In a small bowl, mix together the panko breadcrumbs and the remaining mozzarella cheese. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the pasta bake for a crispy, cheesy crust.

Step 6: Bake the Pasta

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling. If you prefer a more golden and crispy top, you can switch the oven to broil for the last 2-3 minutes, but be sure to keep a close eye on it to avoid burning.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the pasta bake is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired for a touch of color and flavor. Serve this cheesy spinach and artichoke pasta bake alongside a simple green salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfecting Your Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake

  • Make It Ahead: This pasta bake can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours before baking. Just cover the dish with plastic wrap or foil and bake as instructed when you’re ready to enjoy it.
  • Add Protein: If you’d like to add some protein to this dish, grilled chicken, sausage, or even shrimp would pair wonderfully with the spinach and artichoke flavors.
  • Customize the Cheese: Feel free to experiment with different cheeses. Gouda, cheddar, or fontina could add a unique twist to the sauce.
  • Add Extra Veggies: Try adding other vegetables, like mushrooms, zucchini, or bell peppers, for even more flavor and texture.
  • Use Different Pasta Shapes: While penne is ideal, any pasta shape like fusilli, rigatoni, or farfalle would work just as well in this dish.

Final Thoughts

This Cheesy Spinach and Artichoke Pasta Bake is a comforting and hearty dish that delivers big on flavor. The rich, creamy sauce, tender pasta, and savory spinach and artichokes make for a satisfying meal everyone will love. Whether you make it for a weeknight dinner or a weekend gathering, it’s sure to become a family favorite.

If you’re craving something that combines cheese, vegetables, and pasta in a warm, baked dish, give this recipe a try! It’s easy to prepare, customizable, and will surely become a go-to in your dinner rotation. Enjoy!
