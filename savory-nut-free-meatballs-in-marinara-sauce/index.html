Savory Nut-Free Meatballs in Marinara Sauce


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival a classic plate of meatballs in marinara sauce. This savory dish combines tender, flavorful meatballs with a rich and tangy tomato sauce, making it a favorite for family dinners or cozy nights in. What sets this recipe apart is that it’s completely nut-free, ensuring that everyone at the table can enjoy it without worry. Whether you serve it over pasta, in a sub sandwich, or on its own, these savory nut-free meatballs are sure to please.

Ingredients

For the Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for added flavor)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (ensure they are nut-free)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)

For the Marinara Sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp sugar (to balance the acidity)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meatballs

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, minced garlic, egg, salt, black pepper, dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes.

  2. Form the Meatballs: Using your hands, mix the ingredients until well combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the meatballs tough. Once combined, form the mixture into meatballs, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter.

  3. Cook the Meatballs: In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the meatballs in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are browned and cooked through. Remove them from the skillet and set aside.

Step 2: Make the Marinara Sauce

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of olive oil. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes, or until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute, stirring constantly to avoid burning.

  2. Combine the Sauce Ingredients: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and add the tomato paste, sugar, dried oregano, and dried basil. Stir the mixture until everything is well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Simmer the Sauce: Allow the sauce to simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will help develop the flavors and thicken the sauce.

Step 3: Combine Meatballs and Sauce

  1. Add Meatballs to Sauce: Once the marinara sauce has simmered, gently add the cooked meatballs back into the skillet. Make sure they are submerged in the sauce.

  2. Simmer Together: Let the meatballs simmer in the sauce for an additional 10-15 minutes. This will allow the meatballs to absorb the flavors of the marinara.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Plate the Dish: Serve the meatballs hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves if desired. You can serve them over cooked spaghetti, in a sub with melted cheese, or simply on their own with a side of crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

  2. Enjoy: Gather around the table and enjoy this nut-free comfort food classic with your family and friends!

Tips for Perfect Meatballs

  • Choose the Right Meat: Using a mix of ground beef and ground pork can enhance the flavor and moisture of your meatballs. If you prefer a leaner option, ground turkey or chicken can also work well, though they may require additional seasoning.

  • Don’t Overmix: When combining your ingredients, mix just until everything is incorporated. Overmixing can create a dense texture.

  • Baking Option: If you prefer a healthier cooking method, you can bake the meatballs in the oven at 400°F for about 20-25 minutes, turning them halfway through for even cooking.

  • Make Ahead: These meatballs can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or frozen for up to 3 months. Just reheat in the marinara sauce when ready to serve.

  • Sauce Variations: Feel free to customize the marinara sauce by adding vegetables like bell peppers, mushrooms, or spinach for added nutrition.

Nutritional Information

This nut-free meatball dish is not only delicious but also packed with protein and essential nutrients. Depending on your serving size and preparation methods, a serving of meatballs in marinara sauce can provide a well-rounded meal. Here’s a rough estimate of the nutritional content per serving (4 meatballs with sauce):

  • Calories: 350
  • Protein: 25g
  • Carbohydrates: 18g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 2g

Conclusion

Savory nut-free meatballs in marinara sauce are a timeless dish that brings comfort and satisfaction to any meal. With their tender texture and flavorful sauce, they are a perfect choice for family gatherings, weeknight dinners, or meal prep. By following this recipe, you can create a dish that is not only delicious but also inclusive for those with nut allergies.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every bite of these delightful meatballs that everyone will love. Enjoy the process, share the meal, and make wonderful memories around the table!
