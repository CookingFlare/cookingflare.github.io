Canning Beyond Jams: Savory Tomato Preserves


Welcome to our blog, where we explore the world of canning beyond the traditional jams and jellies. Today, we’re diving into the delicious world of savory tomato preserves. These preserves are not only a delightful addition to your pantry but also a versatile ingredient that can elevate your dishes to new heights.

The Art of Tomato Preserves

When we think of canning, tomatoes may not be the first thing that comes to mind. However, tomato preserves are a hidden gem in the canning world. They are a fantastic way to capture the vibrant flavors of fresh tomatoes and extend their shelf life, allowing you to enjoy that summer taste all year round.

Selecting the Perfect Tomatoes

To create the best savory tomato preserves, it’s crucial to start with high-quality tomatoes. Look for ripe, juicy tomatoes that are bursting with flavor. Heirloom tomatoes are an excellent choice for their unique and robust taste, but any variety will work just fine.

Recipe: Savory Tomato Preserves

Here’s a simple and delicious recipe for savory tomato preserves that you can easily make at home:

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds of fresh tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the tomatoes, sugar, vinegar, salt, black pepper, basil, oregano, thyme, and red pepper flakes (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally.

  2. Once the mixture reaches a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer uncovered for about 2 hours, or until the mixture thickens and reduces by about half. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. While the mixture is simmering, prepare your canning jars and lids by sterilizing them in boiling water. Follow proper canning procedures to ensure food safety.

  4. Once the tomato mixture has reached the desired consistency, remove it from the heat. Carefully ladle the hot preserves into the prepared jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims of the jars clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands until fingertip-tight.

  5. Process the jars in a boiling water canner for 15 minutes. Adjust the processing time according to your altitude if necessary.

  6. After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and let them cool undisturbed for 12 to 24 hours. Check that the lids have sealed properly before storing the jars in a cool, dark place.

Serving Ideas

Now that you have a batch of delicious savory tomato preserves, let’s explore some creative ways to enjoy them:

  • Spread them on a crusty baguette for a flavorful bruschetta.
  • Mix them into your favorite pasta sauce for an extra burst of tomato goodness.
  • Use them as a topping for grilled meats or sandwiches.
  • Add a spoonful to your favorite stews or soups for a rich and tangy flavor.

Conclusion

Canning beyond jams opens up a world of possibilities, and savory tomato preserves are a delightful addition to any pantry. With their vibrant flavor and versatility, they are sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So, grab some fresh tomatoes and get canning!
