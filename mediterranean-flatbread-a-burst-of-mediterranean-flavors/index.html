Mediterranean Flatbread: A Burst of Mediterranean Flavors


Mediterranean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, aromas, and vibrant colors. One of the most iconic and beloved dishes from this region is the Mediterranean flatbread, also known as “pita” or “focaccia”. This delectable flatbread is a staple in many Mediterranean countries, including Greece, Turkey, and Italy. In this recipe, we will explore the art of creating a Mediterranean flatbread that embodies the essence of this region’s culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh oregano
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped Kalamata olives (optional)
  • 1/4 cup artichoke hearts, canned or marinated (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Let it sit for 5-7 minutes until the yeast becomes frothy and bubbly. Add the olive oil, salt, and 2 cups of all-purpose flour. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 2: Add the Remaining Flours and Herbs

Gradually add the remaining 1 cup of all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, parsley, oregano, and garlic. Mix until a smooth and elastic dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Knead the dough for 10-12 minutes until it becomes smooth and develops a slight sheen. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm and draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 4: Shape the Dough

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into 2-3 equal portions. Roll out each portion into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

Step 5: Top and Bake

Place the flatbreads on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Top each flatbread with crumbled feta cheese, chopped olives, and artichoke hearts, if using. Drizzle with a little olive oil and sprinkle with salt.

Bake the flatbreads for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more authentic Mediterranean flavor, use a combination of olive oils and herbs like thyme, rosemary, and oregano.
  • Add some heat by sprinkling red pepper flakes on top of the flatbread.
  • Use different types of cheese, such as goat cheese or ricotta, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Add some chopped sun-dried tomatoes or roasted red peppers for added flavor and color.

Conclusion

Mediterranean flatbread is a delicious and versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a snack, side dish, or even as a base for various toppings. With its crispy crust, soft center, and burst of Mediterranean flavors, this flatbread is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So go ahead, experiment with different toppings and variations, and indulge in the flavors of the Mediterranean!
