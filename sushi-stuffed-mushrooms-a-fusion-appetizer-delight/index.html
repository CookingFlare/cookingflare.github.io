Sushi Stuffed Mushrooms: A Fusion Appetizer Delight


If you’re a fan of sushi and mushrooms, then you’re in for a treat with this unique fusion recipe. Sushi stuffed mushrooms bring together the earthy flavors of mushrooms with the fresh, vibrant taste of sushi. It’s a delightful appetizer that is sure to impress your guests at any gathering. The best part? It’s easy to make and can be customized to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large button mushrooms
  • 1 cup sushi rice
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 ounces sashimi-grade tuna, finely diced
  • 4 ounces sashimi-grade salmon, finely diced
  • 1 small cucumber, finely diced
  • 1 avocado, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Nori sheets, cut into small strips for garnish
  • Soy sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mushrooms:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with cooking spray.
    • Remove the stems from the mushrooms and set them aside. Place the mushroom caps in the prepared baking dish, cavity side up.

  2. Cook the Sushi Rice:

    • Rinse the sushi rice in a fine-mesh sieve until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.
    • In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice with 1 1/4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18 minutes.
    • In a small bowl, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Microwave for 30 seconds to dissolve the sugar. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Let the rice cool to room temperature.

  3. Prepare the Sushi Filling:

    • In a separate bowl, combine the diced tuna, salmon, cucumber, and avocado. In a small dish, mix the mayonnaise and sriracha. Add the spicy mayo to the sushi filling and gently toss to combine.

  4. Stuff the Mushrooms:

    • Fill each mushroom cap with a spoonful of sushi rice, pressing it gently into the cavity. Top the rice with a generous amount of the sushi filling, allowing it to mound slightly.

  5. Bake the Stuffed Mushrooms:

    • Bake the stuffed mushrooms in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is heated through.

  6. Garnish and Serve:

    • Sprinkle the baked mushrooms with sesame seeds and garnish with strips of nori. Serve the sushi stuffed mushrooms with a side of soy sauce for dipping.

Tips for Customization:

  • For a vegetarian version, omit the sashimi-grade fish and double up on the cucumber and avocado.
  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms such as portobello or shiitake for a variety of flavors and textures.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of sriracha in the spicy mayo or add a dash of wasabi to the sushi filling.

Sushi stuffed mushrooms are not only a delicious appetizer but also a creative way to combine two beloved dishes into one. This recipe offers a unique twist on traditional sushi and adds a fun element to your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to elevate your snacking experience, these fusion appetizers are a must-try. Enjoy the delightful blend of flavors and textures in every bite!
