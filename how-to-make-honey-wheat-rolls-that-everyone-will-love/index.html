How to Make Honey Wheat Rolls That Everyone Will Love


There’s something about the smell of freshly baked bread wafting through your home that instantly brings a sense of comfort and warmth. The soft, pillowy texture of a freshly baked roll, with just a hint of sweetness from the honey, makes it a perfect addition to any meal or a delightful treat on its own. Honey wheat rolls are a healthier alternative to traditional white bread rolls, combining the rich, nutty flavor of whole wheat flour with the natural sweetness of honey.

If you’re looking to bake a batch of honey wheat rolls that will impress your friends and family, then look no further. This recipe will guide you through each step, offering tips and tricks along the way to ensure perfect results. Whether you’re new to bread making or a seasoned baker, you’ll find this recipe easy to follow and incredibly rewarding.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this recipe, you’ll need a few basic ingredients that you likely already have in your pantry. Here’s the list:

  • 3 ½ cups whole wheat flour – This is the star ingredient, providing the rolls with a slightly nutty flavor and a denser texture compared to white bread.
  • 1 cup warm water – This will help activate the yeast. Make sure it’s not too hot, as it can kill the yeast.
  • 2 tablespoons honey – The honey adds natural sweetness and enhances the flavor of the rolls.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil – This will keep the rolls soft and add moisture.
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (approximately 2 ¼ teaspoons) – Yeast helps the dough rise and gives it the perfect texture.
  • 1 teaspoon salt – The salt brings balance to the sweetness of the honey.
  • 1 egg – The egg adds richness to the dough and helps with the structure.
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter – To brush on the rolls after baking for a golden, glossy finish.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Seeds or oats – Feel free to sprinkle seeds like sunflower or sesame seeds on top for a bit of crunch.
  • Herbs – A little dried rosemary or thyme can add a savory twist.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Honey Wheat Rolls

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water and the honey. Stir gently to dissolve the honey. Then, sprinkle the yeast over the top of the water. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, until the yeast becomes frothy. If it doesn’t froth, your yeast may be expired or the water may have been too hot, so start again with fresh ingredients.

Step 2: Mix the Wet Ingredients

Once your yeast is activated, it’s time to mix the rest of the wet ingredients. In a large mixing bowl, combine the yeast mixture with olive oil, salt, and the egg. Whisk it all together until it’s well combined. The olive oil will add richness to the dough, while the egg provides structure and helps with the rise.

Step 3: Gradually Add the Whole Wheat Flour

Start adding the whole wheat flour, a little bit at a time, mixing continuously with a wooden spoon or dough hook. As you continue to add flour, the dough will begin to come together. Once all the flour is added, switch to using your hands or a dough hook to knead the dough.

The dough should feel slightly sticky but manageable. If it’s too sticky, sprinkle in a little more flour. If it’s too dry, add a few more drops of water. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes, until it’s smooth and elastic.

Step 4: First Rise

Once the dough is ready, form it into a ball and place it into a lightly greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Rolls

After the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air bubbles. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball by cupping your hands around the dough and rolling it in a circular motion.

Once all the rolls are shaped, place them in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish or a round cake pan. The rolls should be placed close together, as this helps them rise higher and form a soft, pull-apart texture.

Step 6: Second Rise

Cover the rolls with a kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they’ve doubled in size. This second rise is essential for achieving light and fluffy rolls.

Step 7: Baking the Rolls

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) while the rolls are rising. Once the rolls are ready, place them in the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown and the rolls sound hollow when tapped. Keep an eye on them as ovens can vary in temperature. If the rolls start to brown too quickly, cover them loosely with aluminum foil to prevent burning.

Step 8: Finishing Touches

Once the rolls are out of the oven, brush them with the melted butter. This adds flavor and gives them a beautiful glossy finish. Let the rolls cool for a few minutes before serving.

Step 9: Enjoy!

Now that your honey wheat rolls are baked to perfection, it’s time to enjoy them! They’re soft, fluffy, and have just the right amount of sweetness. Serve them as a side dish with your favorite meal, or enjoy them as a snack with butter or jam. They’re sure to be a hit at any dinner table.

Tips for Perfect Honey Wheat Rolls

  • Let the dough rise properly: Don’t rush the rising process. The dough needs enough time to rise and develop flavor. If you’re in a rush, try using a proofing setting on your oven or place the dough in a warm area.

  • Use fresh yeast: Make sure your yeast is fresh to ensure a good rise. Expired yeast won’t activate properly, leading to dense rolls.

  • Don’t over-knead the dough: While kneading is important to develop the dough’s structure, over-kneading can make the rolls tough. Knead just until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  • Experiment with flours: If you want a lighter texture, try using half whole wheat flour and half all-purpose flour. You can adjust the ratio depending on your preference for texture.

  • Serve warm: Honey wheat rolls are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven when they’re still warm. You can also reheat them in the microwave or oven for a few minutes if needed.

Storing Honey Wheat Rolls

If you happen to have leftover rolls, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. You can also freeze them for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply place them in the oven for a few minutes at 350°F or microwave them for 15-20 seconds.

Variations You Can Try

Honey wheat rolls are versatile, and there are plenty of ways you can customize this recipe to suit your tastes.

  1. Cinnamon Honey Rolls: Add a teaspoon of ground cinnamon to the dough for a delicious cinnamon honey roll. You can also drizzle a cinnamon glaze over the top after baking.

  2. Herb-Infused Rolls: Add finely chopped fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, or oregano to the dough for an herbaceous twist that pairs perfectly with savory dishes.

  3. Garlic Honey Rolls: For a savory-sweet flavor combination, add minced garlic to the dough. Brush the rolls with garlic butter after baking for an extra burst of flavor.

  4. Seeded Rolls: Before baking, sprinkle sunflower, sesame, or poppy seeds on top of the rolls. They’ll add a delightful crunch and additional flavor.

  5. Whole Wheat and Oat Rolls: Add rolled oats to the dough for an even heartier, more rustic texture. You can also sprinkle oats on top for extra crunch.

Why Honey Wheat Rolls Are a Great Choice

Making your own rolls at home not only allows you to control the ingredients but also gives you the chance to create something wholesome and delicious. Honey wheat rolls are a great way to incorporate whole grains into your diet while still enjoying the comforting texture and sweetness of freshly baked bread.

These rolls are perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a family dinner, a holiday meal, or just a simple weeknight meal. They’re also a great addition to your breadbasket at gatherings, as their slightly sweet flavor is sure to appeal to everyone.

Final Thoughts

Baking honey wheat rolls is a rewarding experience, and once you taste the results, you’ll wonder why you ever settled for store-bought bread. With a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create soft, fluffy rolls that everyone will love. Whether you enjoy them with butter, jam, or as a side to your favorite dishes, these honey wheat rolls will surely become a staple in your baking repertoire.

Happy baking!
