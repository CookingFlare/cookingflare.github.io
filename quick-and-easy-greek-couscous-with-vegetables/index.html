Quick and Easy Greek Couscous with Vegetables


Couscous is one of those incredibly versatile ingredients that can easily take on a Mediterranean flair, especially when combined with fresh vegetables and vibrant flavors. In this blog post, we’re diving into a delightful recipe for Quick and Easy Greek Couscous with Vegetables. This dish is not only packed with nutrients but also comes together in a flash, making it perfect for weeknight dinners or lunch meal prep.

Why Choose Couscous?

Couscous is often mistaken for a grain, but it’s actually a type of pasta made from semolina flour. It’s a staple in North African cuisine and has gained popularity worldwide due to its light, fluffy texture and ability to absorb flavors. Here are a few reasons why couscous is an excellent choice for your meals:

  • Quick Cooking Time: Couscous cooks in mere minutes, making it an ideal option for busy individuals or families.
  • Versatility: It pairs well with a variety of ingredients, from vegetables to proteins, and works as a side dish or a main course.
  • Nutritional Benefits: Couscous provides carbohydrates for energy and is often enriched with vitamins and minerals.

Ingredients for Greek Couscous with Vegetables

To make this Quick and Easy Greek Couscous with Vegetables, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Couscous

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup vegetable broth or water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Vegetables

  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For the Toppings

  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Step-by-Step Preparation

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s get started on this delicious dish!

Step 1: Cook the Couscous

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth or water to a boil.
  2. Add the couscous, a pinch of salt, and olive oil. Stir to combine.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat, cover it, and let it sit for about 5 minutes. Once the couscous has absorbed the liquid, fluff it with a fork to separate the grains.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

  1. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped red onion and sauté for about 2-3 minutes until it becomes translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, being careful not to burn it.
  4. Add the diced zucchini and red bell pepper to the skillet. Sauté for about 5 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  5. Mix in the cherry tomatoes and spinach, along with the dried oregano and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the spinach has wilted and the tomatoes are slightly softened.

Step 3: Combine and Serve

  1. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the fluffed couscous to the skillet.
  2. Squeeze the juice of one lemon over the mixture and stir everything together until well combined.
  3. Remove the skillet from the heat and gently fold in the crumbled feta cheese and sliced Kalamata olives.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.

Step 4: Garnish and Enjoy

  1. Transfer the Greek Couscous with Vegetables to a serving dish or individual plates.
  2. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for an extra pop of color and flavor.
  3. Serve warm or at room temperature. This dish can be enjoyed on its own or as a side to grilled meats or fish.

Tips for Customization

One of the great things about this Greek Couscous with Vegetables is its versatility. Here are some tips for customizing the recipe to suit your taste preferences or dietary needs:

  • Add Proteins: For a more filling meal, add cooked chickpeas, grilled chicken, or shrimp to the dish.
  • Vary the Vegetables: Feel free to substitute or add other vegetables like eggplant, carrots, or peas based on what you have on hand.
  • Make it Vegan: Omit the feta cheese or swap it with a vegan cheese alternative to keep the dish vegan-friendly.
  • Spice it Up: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce for some heat.

Nutritional Information

Here’s a rough estimate of the nutritional content per serving (based on four servings):

  • Calories: 300
  • Protein: 10g
  • Carbohydrates: 40g
  • Dietary Fiber: 5g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Saturated Fat: 3g

Keep in mind that these numbers may vary based on specific ingredients and portion sizes.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, this Greek Couscous with Vegetables stores well in the refrigerator. Here’s how to keep it fresh:

  • Storage: Place the cooled couscous in an airtight container and refrigerate. It should last for up to 3 days.
  • Reheating: To reheat, you can microwave the couscous for 1-2 minutes or heat it in a skillet over medium heat, adding a splash of water or olive oil to prevent it from drying out.

Final Thoughts

Quick and Easy Greek Couscous with Vegetables is a delightful dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean. Not only is it simple and quick to prepare, but it’s also nutritious and satisfying. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a flavorful meal prep option, this recipe is sure to impress.

Give this recipe a try, and don’t forget to share your experience! Feel free to experiment with different vegetables and proteins to make it your own. Happy cooking!
