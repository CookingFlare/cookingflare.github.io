Grilled Lobster Tails: A Luxurious BBQ Treat


When it comes to luxury dining experiences, few dishes can match the decadence of grilled lobster tails. These succulent, tender morsels, nestled within their natural marine armor, are a culinary delight that elevates any barbecue gathering to new heights. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of preparing and grilling these mouth-watering delicacies, ensuring that your next BBQ is unforgettable.

Ingredients:

  • 4 live lobsters (1¼ to 1½ pounds each)
  • 1 cup clarified butter
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, dill, and chives)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Equipment:

  • Kitchen shears
  • Chef’s knife
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Bench scraper
  • Cutting board
  • Mixing bowl
  • Large cast-iron skillet or grilling pan
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil
  • Instant-read thermometer
  • Kitchen towels

How to Prepare Grilled Lobster Tails

Step 1: Kill the Lobsters Humanely

To ensure the lobsters are killed humanely, place them in a hydrating environment (such as a refrigerator or cooler with wet towels) overnight before cooking. This process is essential as the lobsters will be subjected to minimal stress and discomfort, leading to a more tender and flavorsome dish.

Step 2: Remove the Tails

Using kitchen shears and a chef’s knife, carefully remove the lobster tails from the body. Make sure to keep the tails intact, as they’re the part we’ll be grilling.

Step 3: Butterfly the Tails

Using a sharp knife, cut along the length of the lobster’s tail (on the underside) from the tail end to the first set of legs, almost but not completely cutting through the shell. Then, open the tail flat, and press down on the tail to flatten it for grilling.

Step 4: Prepare the Butter Herb Garlic Sauce

In a mixing bowl, combine clarified butter, chopped fresh herbs, garlic, salt, and pepper to create a flavorful butter herb garlic sauce. This will be used to baste your lobster tails while grilling.

How to Grill Grilled Lobster Tails

Step 1: Preheat the Grill

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (approximately 350-400°F).

Step 2: Prepare the Grill Pan

Place a large cast-iron skillet or grilling pan on the grill. This will help distribute heat evenly and prevent the lobster tails from burning or sticking to the grill.

Step 3: Cook the Tails

Place the butterflied lobster tails, shell side down, on the hot grill pan. Cook the tails for about 5-7 minutes until they reach an internal temperature of 130-136°F. This will ensure that the lobster remains tender and succulent.

Step 4: Baste and Flip

Halfway through the grilling process, baste the lobster tails with the butter herb garlic sauce and flip them over to cook on the other side. Liberally apply the sauce on both sides of the tails.

Step 5: Remove from the Grill

After achieving the desired internal temperature, remove the lobster tails from the grill and transfer them to heavy-duty aluminum foil. Fold the foil around the tails, creating a secure package that retains heat and moisture.

Step 6: Let it Rest

Allow the lobster tails to rest in their foil packets for about 5-10 minutes. This step is vital, as it allows the juices to redistribute and makes the lobster easier to cut.

Plate and Serve

Finally, plate up your perfectly grilled lobster tails with a side of your choice, such as a crisp salad or grilled vegetables. Your guests, or loved ones, will be delighted by the succulent and flavorful results of your culinary mastery.

As summer barbecue gatherings return, and backyard chefs seek to delight their guests, grilled lobster tails will undoubtedly be a star player in countless divine feasts. With this guide, you will elevate your grilling skills and confidence in the kitchen, ensuring success when crafting this luxurious and delectable dish.
