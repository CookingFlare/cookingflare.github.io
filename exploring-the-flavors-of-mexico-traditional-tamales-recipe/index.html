Exploring the Flavors of Mexico: Traditional Tamales Recipe


Tamales are a beloved traditional Mexican dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. Made from masa (corn dough) and filled with a variety of savory or sweet ingredients, these delicious packages are wrapped in banana leaves or corn husks and steamed to perfection. They are perfect for festive occasions, gatherings, or a hearty weeknight meal. Let’s dive into the culinary world of tamales and explore a classic Mexican recipe.

The History of Tamales

Tamales date back to the Mayan civilization around 600 AD. These versatile wrappers were valued by Aztecs and other ancient Mesoamerican cultures for their portability, easy storage, and ability to preserve food. Tamales were a staple food item for travelers and soldiers, as they could be easily prepared and eaten on the go. Today, tamales continue to be a delicious and integral part of Mexican culture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups masa harina (masa flour)
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 3/4 cup lard or vegetable shortening, melted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 12 corn husks or banana leaves
  • Filling of your choice

Luxurious Fillings

Tamales fillings can range from savory to sweet, allowing for limitless creativity in developing unique flavor combinations. Common savory fillings include:

  1. Carnitas – slow-cooked pork shoulder simmered in a flavorful chili broth.
  2. Chicken with Chipotle – juicy shredded chicken infused with smoky chipotle chilies.
  3. Cheese and Chorizo – a blend of gooey cheese and spiced Mexican sausage.
  4. Vegetarian Option – black beans, corn, and cheese a hearty option for all to enjoy.

For dessert, go ahead and indulge in sweet tamales filled with:

  1. Cajeta – a caramel-like sauce made from caramelized goat milk and sugar.
  2. Frutas – filled with a mixture of fruit, raisins, and nuts, offering a sweet and juicy taste.
  3. Mote con Huevo – a comforting vanilla-porcini mushroom custard.

Preparing the Masa Dough

To create the perfect tamales, the masa dough must be fluffy and flavorful. Here’s a simple recipe:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the masa harina, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Gently warm the melted lard or vegetable shortening and then combine with the warm water. Allow the mixture to cool slightly.
  3. Add the warm liquid to the dry ingredients and mix until the dough is smooth and pliable.
  4. Allow the masa dough to rest for about 30 minutes before using it to prepare tamales.

Wrapping the Tamales

  1. Select the corn husks or banana leaves, ensuring they are soft and pliable. Soak them in hot water briefly to soften further.
  2. Spread a thin layer of masa dough on the flattened husk or leaf, leaving about an inch border on one of the longer sides.
  3. Place a tablespoon or two of the chosen filling in the center of the masa layer.
  4. Fold the longer side of the husk or leaf over the filling, then fold the sides inwards to create a compact, tamale-shaped package.
  5. The packages may be stacked side by side in a steamer, with the folded ends touching the bottom dish to hold them upright.

Steaming to Perfection

  1. Place the tamales vertically in a collapsible steamer, with the folded ends on the steamer’s bottom tray.
  2. Steam the tamales for approximately one hour and 15 minutes. Add water to the steamer as needed.
  3. Let the tamales cool slightly before unwrapping, allowing the masa to fully set.
  4. Serve and enjoy warm.

Notes

  • To ensure the masa dough to be the perfect consistency, always use warm water and melted lard or shortening.
  • Fillings should be cooled before use to prevent the masa dough from becoming too soft.
  • When steaming, keep the tamales moist by occasionally adding warm water to the steamer.
  • Let the tamales cool slightly after steaming before enjoying or trying to unwrap.
  • Leftover tamales may be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week or frozen for up to three months.

Embark on a Culinary Journey

Tamales’ vibrant history and delicious combinations of flavors make them an exciting culinary experience. By trying different recipes and exploring various fillings, you can continuously create new and unique tastes. So why not take a leap into the culinary world of tamales and prepare your very own flavors of Mexico?
