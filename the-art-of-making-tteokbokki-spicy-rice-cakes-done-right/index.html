The Art of Making Tteokbokki: Spicy Rice Cakes Done Right

3 min · 627 words

If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’ve probably heard of tteokbokki. This popular street food dish, also known as spicy rice cakes, has gained worldwide recognition for its unique flavors and addictively spicy kick. In this post, we’ll dive into the art of making tteokbokki from scratch, ensuring that you get the perfect balance of flavors and textures every time.

Ingredients

To make tteokbokki, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of cylindrical rice cakes (tteok)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 4 dried anchovies
  • 3 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili powder)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice cakes in cold water for about 30 minutes. This will soften them slightly, making them easier to cook.

  2. In a pot, add the water, dried anchovies, and dried shiitake mushrooms. Bring it to a boil over medium heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. This will create a flavorful broth.

  3. Remove the anchovies and mushrooms from the broth and discard them. Keep the broth aside for later use.

  4. In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, and sauté until they become fragrant and slightly caramelized.

  5. Add the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, and sugar to the pan. Stir well to combine all the ingredients and cook for another minute or two. This will create a thick and spicy sauce.

  6. Pour the broth into the pan with the sauce, stirring continuously to ensure everything is well mixed. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  7. Drain the rice cakes and add them to the simmering sauce. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the rice cakes become soft and chewy.

  8. Add the chopped green onions to the pan and stir them into the tteokbokki. Cook for an additional minute to let the flavors meld together.

  9. Remove the pan from heat and transfer the tteokbokki to a serving dish. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top for garnish.

Serving Suggestions

Tteokbokki is traditionally served as a street food snack in Korea. However, you can enjoy it as a main course by adding additional ingredients like fish cakes, boiled eggs, or vegetables. Here are a few serving suggestions to elevate your tteokbokki experience:

  • Add sliced fish cakes to the simmering sauce along with the rice cakes.
  • Boil eggs separately, peel them, and add them to the finished tteokbokki for a protein boost.
  • Stir-fry some vegetables like carrots, cabbage, or bell peppers and add them to the tteokbokki for added texture and nutrition.

Conclusion

Making tteokbokki at home is a wonderful way to experience the vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine. With a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a dish that is both comforting and explosively spicy. So why not give it a try? Embrace the art of making tteokbokki and enjoy this delicious dish done right!
