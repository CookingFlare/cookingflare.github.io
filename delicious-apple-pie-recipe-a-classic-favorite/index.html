Delicious Apple Pie Recipe: A Classic Favorite


There’s something timeless about a warm slice of apple pie—its flaky crust, tender apples infused with cinnamon, and that irresistible aroma that fills the kitchen as it bakes. Whether it’s for a holiday gathering, a cozy Sunday afternoon, or simply to satisfy a craving, baking an apple pie is a delightful culinary adventure that brings joy to both the baker and those lucky enough to enjoy a slice.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6-7 large apples (a mix of tart and sweet varieties like Granny Smith and Honeycrisp), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For Assembly:

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Additional sugar for sprinkling

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Add Water: Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing gently with a fork after each addition. Stop adding water when the dough begins to clump together.

  3. Form Dough: Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a flat disc. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or up to overnight.

Making the Filling:

  1. Prepare Apples: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning.

  2. Mix Ingredients: In a separate bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and flour. Add this mixture to the apples and toss until the apples are evenly coated.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat as well.

  2. Roll Out Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides.

  3. Add Filling: Pour the apple filling into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter.

  4. Top with Second Crust: Roll out the second disc of dough to a similar size. Place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edge of the pie dish and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  5. Bake the Pie: Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar. Place the pie on the preheated baking sheet in the oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  6. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before slicing and serving. Serve plain or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a truly indulgent treat.

Tips for Success:

  • Choosing Apples: Opt for a mix of apple varieties for depth of flavor and texture.
  • Handling Dough: Keep the dough and butter cold to ensure a flaky crust.
  • Preventing Soggy Bottoms: Preheating the baking sheet helps ensure the bottom crust cooks evenly.

Conclusion

Baking a homemade apple pie is a labor of love that pays off with every delicious bite. From preparing the buttery, flaky crust to filling it with spiced apples that melt in your mouth, every step is an opportunity to create a masterpiece. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the kitchen, this classic apple pie recipe promises to delight and become a staple in your repertoire. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and embark on a culinary journey that celebrates tradition and the simple joy of homemade goodness.
