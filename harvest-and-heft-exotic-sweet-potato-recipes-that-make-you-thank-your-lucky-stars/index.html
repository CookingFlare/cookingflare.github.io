Harvest and Heft: Exotic Sweet Potato Recipes That Make You Thank Your Lucky Stars


Sweet potatoes—often seen as humble root vegetables—have a remarkable ability to transform into flavorful dishes that captivate our taste buds and nourish our bodies. Whether it’s for a holiday feast, a comforting weeknight dinner, or a creative snack, sweet potatoes offer endless possibilities. But did you know that exotic varieties of sweet potatoes exist that can elevate your culinary game? From the earthy flavors of Japanese satsumaimo to the vibrant colors of purple sweet potatoes, these tubers bring much more to the table than the traditional orange variety.

This blog post will explore the harvest and heft of sweet potatoes and introduce you to some of the most tantalizing exotic sweet potato recipes. These recipes will make you thankful for the bounty of nature and the rich flavors that sweet potatoes offer.

Understanding the Variety: Exotic Sweet Potatoes

Before diving into the recipes, it’s worth taking a moment to appreciate the variety of sweet potatoes available around the world. While most of us are familiar with the classic orange sweet potato, there are many other varieties with distinct flavors, colors, and textures.

Purple Sweet Potatoes (Okinawan and Stokes)

Purple sweet potatoes, such as the Okinawan and Stokes varieties, are known for their striking color and rich, nutty flavor. The Okinawan sweet potato, for instance, has a smooth, creamy texture and a slightly sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with both savory and sweet dishes.

Stokes purple sweet potatoes, on the other hand, have a denser texture and a slightly more intense sweetness. They can be roasted, mashed, or baked into pies for a unique twist on traditional recipes.

Japanese Satsumaimo

Satsumaimo is a Japanese variety of sweet potato that is often served in Japan during colder months. Its flesh is a deep golden color, with a naturally sweet flavor that becomes even more pronounced when roasted. The satsumaimo variety is known for its smooth texture and is often used in savory dishes like tempura or as a filling for pastries.

White Sweet Potatoes

White sweet potatoes have a lighter, drier texture compared to their orange counterparts. They are subtly sweet but often hold a more savory flavor profile. These sweet potatoes are commonly used in dishes where a delicate flavor is desired.

Garnet and Jewel Sweet Potatoes

These varieties of sweet potatoes have an orange-red skin with soft, deep orange flesh. Known for their creamy texture and natural sweetness, Garnet and Jewel sweet potatoes are the go-to choice for many traditional recipes, including sweet potato pies and casseroles.

With so many exotic varieties, there is no shortage of creative ways to enjoy these earthy delights. Now that we’ve established the different types of sweet potatoes, let’s dive into the recipes that bring out their best characteristics.

Recipe 1: Satsumaimo Tempura

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium satsumaimo sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into ½-inch slices
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water (or cold water with ice)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sweet potatoes: Peel the satsumaimo sweet potatoes and cut them into ½-inch slices. You can also slice them into wedges if you prefer. Set them aside.
  2. Make the tempura batter: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt. Slowly pour in the cold sparkling water, stirring gently to create a smooth batter. Be careful not to overmix—it’s okay if the batter has a few lumps.
  3. Heat the oil: In a deep frying pan or wok, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). You’ll want enough oil to submerge the sweet potato slices.
  4. Fry the sweet potatoes: Dip the sweet potato slices into the batter, allowing any excess batter to drip off, and carefully lower them into the hot oil. Fry the sweet potatoes in batches, turning them occasionally until golden brown and crisp, about 3–4 minutes per side.
  5. Drain and serve: Remove the tempura sweet potatoes from the oil and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Serve immediately with a small bowl of soy sauce for dipping.

This recipe is a perfect snack or appetizer to showcase the naturally sweet and creamy texture of satsumaimo. The light, crispy batter contrasts beautifully with the tender sweet potato, making it an unforgettable dish.

Recipe 2: Purple Sweet Potato Mash with Coconut Milk

Ingredients:

  • 3 large purple sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (or coconut oil for a dairy-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sweet potatoes: Place the purple sweet potatoes in a large pot and cover them with water. Bring to a boil and cook for about 15–20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.
  2. Mash the sweet potatoes: Drain the sweet potatoes and return them to the pot. Mash them with a potato masher or fork until smooth and creamy.
  3. Add the coconut milk and butter: Stir in the coconut milk, butter, maple syrup, and cinnamon. Continue mashing until everything is well combined and the mash reaches a velvety texture.
  4. Season and serve: Season the mash with salt and pepper to taste. Transfer the mash to a serving bowl and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro if desired.

This purple sweet potato mash is a tropical twist on the traditional mashed potatoes we all know and love. The coconut milk adds richness, while the natural sweetness of the purple sweet potato shines through. This side dish is perfect for holidays or special occasions when you want to add something unique to your table.

Recipe 3: Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium white sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cooked black beans (or 1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Salsa or hot sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roast the sweet potatoes: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the diced white sweet potatoes with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Roast the sweet potatoes for 20–25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until tender and golden brown.
  2. Warm the tortillas: While the sweet potatoes are roasting, heat the corn tortillas on a dry skillet over medium heat for 30 seconds per side until warm and pliable.
  3. Assemble the tacos: To assemble the tacos, layer each tortilla with roasted sweet potatoes, black beans, avocado slices, and a handful of fresh cilantro leaves.
  4. Serve and enjoy: Squeeze a lime wedge over the top and drizzle with salsa or hot sauce if desired. Serve immediately.

These sweet potato and black bean tacos are a flavorful and filling meal that brings together a wonderful mix of textures and tastes. The sweetness of the white sweet potatoes pairs perfectly with the savory black beans and the creamy avocado, creating a taco that’s both satisfying and wholesome.

Recipe 4: Sweet Potato and Coconut Curry Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 large Okinawan sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  2. Add the spices: Stir in the turmeric, cumin, and coriander, allowing the spices to bloom in the oil for about 1 minute.
  3. Cook the sweet potatoes: Add the cubed Okinawan sweet potatoes to the pot and stir to coat them with the spices. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20–25 minutes, until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Blend the soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth and creamy. Alternatively, you can transfer the soup in batches to a blender.
  5. Serve and garnish: Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

This sweet potato and coconut curry soup is a comforting and flavorful dish that’s perfect for cool evenings. The rich coconut milk balances the earthy sweetness of the Okinawan sweet potatoes, while the spices add a lovely warmth and depth of flavor.

Final Thoughts

Exotic sweet potatoes offer a world of culinary possibilities that go far beyond the traditional sweet potato casserole. Whether you’re roasting, mashing, frying, or blending them into soups, these unique varieties bring rich flavor, texture, and color to your plate. The diverse recipes shared in this post will help you explore the bounty of sweet potatoes and discover new favorite dishes that highlight their natural sweetness and versatility.

From crispy tempura to creamy mashed potatoes, these sweet potato dishes are guaranteed to make you appreciate these humble tubers in a whole new light. So go ahead—thank your lucky stars for these exotic sweet potatoes and get creative in the kitchen!
