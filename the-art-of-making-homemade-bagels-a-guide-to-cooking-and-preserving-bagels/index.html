The Art of Making Homemade Bagels: A Guide to Cooking and Preserving Bagels


Bagels, those chewy, crispy, and delicious rings of dough, have been a staple in many cultures for centuries. From traditional Jewish bakeries to modern-day artisanal shops, bagels are a beloved treat that’s hard to resist. But have you ever wondered how to make homemade bagels? In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the art of making homemade bagels, from mixing and shaping to cooking and preserving.

Step 1: Mix the Dough

To start, you’ll need to mix the dough. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of warm water, 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the yeast is activated and the mixture is frothy.

Next, add 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil to the bowl. Mix the dough until it comes together in a sticky ball.

Step 2: Knead the Dough

Now it’s time to knead the dough. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment to knead the dough.

Step 3: Shape the Dough

Once the dough is kneaded, it’s time to shape it into bagels. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal pieces and roll each piece into a ball. Use your thumbs to create a hole in the center of each ball, stretching the dough out to form a bagel shape.

Step 4: Let the Bagels Rise

Place the shaped bagels onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between each bagel. Cover the bagels with a clean towel and let them rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1 hour, or until they’ve doubled in size.

Step 5: Boil the Bagels

Preheat a large pot of water and add 1 tablespoon of sugar for every quart of water. Bring the water to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer. Gently add the bagels to the pot, 2-3 at a time, and cook for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until they’re golden brown.

Step 6: Bake the Bagels

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the boiled bagels onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until they’re golden brown.

Step 7: Cool and Preserve

Once the bagels are baked, let them cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before serving. You can also store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or freeze them for up to 2 months.

Tips and Variations:

  • To add flavor to your bagels, try adding different herbs and spices to the dough, such as garlic, sesame seeds, or poppy seeds.
  • For a crispy crust, try baking the bagels at a higher temperature for a shorter amount of time.
  • To make mini bagels, simply divide the dough into smaller pieces and shape them into smaller balls.
  • To make bagel chips, slice the bagels thinly and bake them in the oven until crispy.

Conclusion:

Making homemade bagels is a fun and rewarding process that requires a little patience and practice. With these steps and tips, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious, chewy, and crispy bagels that will impress your friends and family. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the art of making homemade bagels!
