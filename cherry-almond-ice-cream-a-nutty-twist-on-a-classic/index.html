Cherry Almond Ice Cream: A Nutty Twist on a Classic


Summer is here, and what better way to beat the heat than with a refreshing scoop of homemade ice cream? In this recipe, we’re giving the classic cherry ice cream a nutty twist by incorporating the sweetness of almonds. The result is a unique and delicious flavor combination that’s sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup almonds, toasted and chopped
  • 1/4 cup cherry jam or preserves
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup pitted cherries, fresh or frozen

Equipment

  • Ice cream maker (optional)
  • Blender or food processor
  • Mixing bowl
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Freezer-safe container with lid

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Cherry Jam

If using frozen cherries, thaw them first and pat dry with a paper towel. In a blender or food processor, puree the cherries with 1/4 cup of granulated sugar until smooth. Transfer the mixture to a saucepan and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the jam has thickened and reduced slightly. Let it cool to room temperature.

Step 2: Toast the Almonds

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Spread the almonds on a baking sheet and toast for 5-7 minutes, or until fragrant and lightly browned. Let them cool completely.

Step 3: Make the Ice Cream Base

In a mixing bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, and salt until the sugar is fully dissolved. Add the toasted almonds, cherry jam, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined.

Step 4: Chill the Ice Cream Base

Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the mixture for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Step 5: Churn the Ice Cream (Optional)

If you have an ice cream maker, remove the ice cream base from the refrigerator and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If not, proceed to the next step.

Step 6: Freeze the Ice Cream

Transfer the ice cream base to a freezer-safe container with a lid. Cover the container and place it in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, remove the container from the freezer and use a fork to scrape the mixture and break up any ice crystals that have formed. This is called “tempering” the mixture, and it will help the ice cream to be smooth and creamy. Repeat this process for 2-3 hours, or until the desired consistency is reached.

Step 7: Add the Cherries

Once the ice cream is almost fully set, stir in the pitted cherries. Cover the container and return it to the freezer for at least 30 minutes to allow the cherries to freeze and the ice cream to set.

Tips and Variations

  • For a creamier ice cream, use a higher ratio of heavy cream to whole milk.
  • If you prefer a stronger almond flavor, you can add an additional 1/4 cup of toasted almonds to the ice cream base.
  • Experiment with different types of cherries, such as tart cherries or cherry preserves with a hint of spice.
  • Consider adding a splash of cherry liqueur or amaretto for an adult twist on this recipe.

Conclusion

Cherry almond ice cream is a unique and delicious twist on a classic flavor combination. With its creamy texture and sweet-tart flavor, it’s sure to be a hit at your next summer gathering. By following these simple steps and using high-quality ingredients, you’ll be enjoying a scoop (or two) of this tasty ice cream in no time.
