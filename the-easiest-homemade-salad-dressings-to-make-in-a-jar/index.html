The Easiest Homemade Salad Dressings to Make in a Jar


Salads are a staple in many kitchens, offering a fresh and healthy option for meals. Whether you’re creating a simple side salad or a hearty main dish, the dressing can make all the difference. Store-bought dressings can be convenient, but they often contain preservatives, excessive sugars, and unnecessary additives. Making your own salad dressing at home not only ensures better flavor but also gives you control over the ingredients. The best part? You don’t need any fancy equipment. All you need is a jar!

In this post, we’ll dive into the easiest homemade salad dressings to make in a jar. These dressings are quick, simple, and require only a few ingredients. Plus, they can be customized to fit your taste preferences and dietary needs. Let’s get started with these foolproof recipes that will elevate any salad.

1. Classic Vinaigrette

A classic vinaigrette is one of the simplest and most versatile salad dressings to make at home. With just three basic ingredients—oil, vinegar, and mustard—this dressing is perfect for any leafy salad, roasted vegetables, or even as a marinade.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar (balsamic, red wine, or apple cider)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 garlic clove, minced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a jar, combine the olive oil, vinegar, and Dijon mustard.
  2. Add the minced garlic, if desired, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Seal the jar and shake vigorously until the dressing emulsifies and thickens slightly.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.

This vinaigrette is bright and tangy, with the mustard helping to bind the ingredients together for a smooth finish. You can experiment with different vinegars to find your preferred flavor profile—apple cider vinegar for a milder taste or balsamic for a sweet depth.

2. Creamy Caesar Dressing

If you’re craving a rich and indulgent dressing, a homemade Caesar dressing is a must-try. Unlike the store-bought version, which can be overly thick or artificial, this creamy dressing is made with real ingredients like garlic, Parmesan, and lemon juice.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon anchovy paste (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the mayonnaise, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and Dijon mustard to the jar.
  2. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and anchovy paste (if using).
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and shake well until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice or Parmesan if desired.

This Caesar dressing is creamy, tangy, and has the right amount of umami thanks to the Parmesan and optional anchovy paste. It pairs wonderfully with romaine lettuce, croutons, and a sprinkle of extra cheese.

3. Honey Mustard Dressing

Sweet, tangy, and creamy, honey mustard dressing is a favorite for both salads and dipping sauces. It’s easy to make and can be modified to suit your sweetness level. Whether you want it more honey-forward or mustard-forward, this dressing is a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In the jar, combine the Dijon mustard, honey, and apple cider vinegar.
  2. Add the olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Shake the jar until the dressing is smooth and emulsified.

This honey mustard dressing is a great balance of sweet and tangy, with a creamy texture that coats your greens perfectly. Use it on mixed greens, grilled chicken salads, or as a dip for veggies.

4. Avocado Cilantro Lime Dressing

For something a little more unique, try making an avocado cilantro lime dressing. This creamy dressing is a fantastic addition to Southwest-style salads, tacos, or grilled shrimp dishes. The combination of avocado and lime creates a fresh, bright flavor that’s perfect for warm weather.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • ¼ cup water (adjust for desired thickness)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Scoop the avocado into a jar and add the cilantro, lime juice, garlic, olive oil, and water.
  2. Blend the ingredients with an immersion blender or use a regular blender until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and shake well.

This dressing has a velvety texture, with a rich and creamy consistency from the avocado. It’s perfect on leafy greens, grilled chicken, or even as a topping for grilled vegetables.

5. Tahini Lemon Dressing

For a nutty, creamy, and vegan-friendly option, try tahini lemon dressing. This dressing is made with tahini, a paste made from sesame seeds, which gives it a rich and earthy flavor. The lemon juice adds a refreshing tartness, balancing the richness of the tahini.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or honey, if not vegan)
  • 1-2 tablespoons water (to thin out the dressing)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a jar, combine the tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, and maple syrup.
  2. Add water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency.
  3. Shake well to combine, and season with salt and pepper.

This tahini lemon dressing is creamy and slightly sweet with a deep, nutty flavor. It’s an excellent choice for salads that feature roasted vegetables, chickpeas, or quinoa.

6. Balsamic Vinaigrette

Another variation of vinaigrette, the balsamic vinaigrette, offers a richer and slightly sweeter profile compared to the classic. It pairs especially well with salads that include fruits, nuts, and tangy cheeses like goat cheese or blue cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, and honey (if using) to the jar.
  2. Pour in the olive oil and seal the jar.
  3. Shake vigorously until the dressing emulsifies, and season with salt and pepper.

This balsamic vinaigrette adds a tangy and slightly sweet kick to your salads. It works wonderfully with fresh greens, roasted beets, or even as a marinade for grilled vegetables.

7. Greek Yogurt Ranch Dressing

If you’re a fan of creamy ranch dressing but want a healthier alternative, Greek yogurt ranch dressing is a great option. Greek yogurt adds creaminess and tang without the need for buttermilk or mayonnaise. Plus, it’s packed with protein and probiotics.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a jar, combine the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic powder, dried dill, and onion powder.
  2. Shake the jar until the dressing is smooth and creamy.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

This homemade ranch dressing is creamy, tangy, and packed with flavor from the dill and garlic. It’s perfect for dipping veggies or drizzling over a crisp green salad.

8. Blue Cheese Dressing

If you enjoy the bold, tangy flavor of blue cheese, this creamy dressing is sure to satisfy your cravings. With just a few ingredients, you can whip up a rich and decadent blue cheese dressing in no time.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup sour cream
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons milk (or more for a thinner consistency)
  • ¼ cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a jar, combine the sour cream, mayonnaise, and milk.
  2. Stir in the crumbled blue cheese and lemon juice.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then shake well until combined.

This dressing is bold and creamy, with just the right amount of tang from the blue cheese. It pairs wonderfully with a wedge salad, grilled chicken, or crispy bacon.

Conclusion

Making homemade salad dressings in a jar is simple, affordable, and a great way to enhance the flavor of any salad. Whether you prefer creamy, tangy, or vinaigrette-style dressings, these recipes can be easily customized to your taste. Plus, making your own dressings allows you to control the ingredients and avoid unnecessary additives.

The best part is that all of these dressings can be stored in the fridge for up to a week, making them perfect for meal prep. So next time you want to jazz up your salad, skip the store-bought bottles and try one of these easy homemade dressing recipes. Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you!
