Preserving the Harvest: 8 Seasonal Recipes for Canning


Welcome to our latest blog post, where we explore the art of preserving the harvest through canning. As the seasons change, we find ourselves surrounded by an abundance of fresh fruits and vegetables. What better way to make the most of these bountiful harvests than by preserving them? In this post, we will share eight delicious seasonal recipes for canning that will allow you to enjoy the flavors of summer all year round. So, let’s get started!

1. Strawberry Jam

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring frequently.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the jam thickens.
  4. Ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4-inch headspace.
  5. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  6. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

2. Spicy Pickled Green Beans

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup pickling salt
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 4 teaspoons dill seeds
  • 2 teaspoons red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Pack the green beans into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, salt, garlic, dill seeds, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Carefully pour the hot brine over the green beans, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  5. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  6. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

3. Peach Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups ripe peaches, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 jalapeno peppers, seeded and minced
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the peaches, red onion, cilantro, jalapeno peppers, lime juice, vinegar, honey, and salt.
  2. Mix well and let the flavors meld for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Ladle the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  4. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

4. Apple Butter

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups apples, peeled, cored, and chopped
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the apples, sugar, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the apples are soft and the mixture has thickened.
  3. Puree the mixture using an immersion blender or food processor.
  4. Ladle the apple butter into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4-inch headspace.
  5. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  6. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

5. Tomato Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions and garlic, and cook until softened.
  3. Add the tomatoes, salt, sugar, basil, and oregano.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 1-2 hours, until thickened.
  5. Ladle the tomato sauce into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 35 minutes.
  7. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

6. Blueberry Jam

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh blueberries
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the blueberries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens.
  3. Ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4-inch headspace.
  4. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

7. Pickled Beets

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds beets, trimmed and peeled
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons whole cloves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the beets in boiling water until tender.
  2. Drain and let them cool.
  3. Slice the beets and pack them into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  4. In a saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and cloves.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Carefully pour the hot brine over the beets, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 30 minutes.
  8. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

8. Raspberry Jam

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh raspberries
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens.
  3. Ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4-inch headspace.
  4. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

Now that you have these eight delicious recipes for canning, you can start preserving the harvest and enjoying the flavors of each season throughout the year. Whether it’s strawberry jam, spicy pickled green beans, or raspberry jam, these recipes will surely delight your taste buds. So roll up your sleeves, put on your apron, and get ready to embark on a canning adventure! Happy preserving!
