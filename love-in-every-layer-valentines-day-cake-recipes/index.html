Love in Every Layer: Valentine's Day Cake Recipes


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate this special day than with a delicious and romantic cake? In this post, we’ll explore three unique cake recipes that are sure to impress your loved one. From classic chocolate to decadent red velvet, each recipe is designed to melt hearts and satisfy sweet tooth.

Classic Chocolate Layer Cake

This classic recipe is a timeless favorite, and for good reason. Moist, rich, and indulgent, this chocolate layer cake is the perfect way to show your love and appreciation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup whole milk, at room temperature
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch (23cm) round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together cocoa powder and milk until smooth.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. Whisk in vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients to cocoa mixture, whisking until smooth.
  6. Divide batter evenly among prepared pans and smooth tops.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow cakes to cool in pans for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Red Velvet Layer Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

This show-stopping cake is sure to impress with its vibrant red color and creamy frosting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons red food coloring
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch (23cm) round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat butter until creamy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Add red food coloring and beat until combined.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients to butter mixture, alternating with buttermilk, beginning and ending with dry ingredients. Beat just until combined.
  6. Divide batter evenly among prepared pans and smooth tops.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow cakes to cool in pans for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Beat cream cheese and butter until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add vanilla extract and beat until combined.
  3. Gradually add powdered sugar, beating until smooth and creamy.

Lemon Lavender Pound Cake

This refreshing and fragrant cake is the perfect way to brighten up your Valentine’s Day celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried lavender buds
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch (23x13cm) loaf pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat butter until creamy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Add lemon juice and beat until combined.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients to butter mixture, alternating with heavy cream, beginning and ending with dry ingredients. Beat just until combined.
  6. Stir in lavender buds.
  7. Pour batter into prepared pan and smooth top.
  8. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow cake to cool in pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

These three cakes are sure to impress your loved one on Valentine’s Day. Whether you’re a classic chocolate fan, a red velvet enthusiast, or a lemon lover, there’s something for everyone in this collection of recipes. So go ahead, get baking, and show your love and appreciation with a delicious and romantic cake.
