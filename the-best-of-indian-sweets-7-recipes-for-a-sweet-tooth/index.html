The Best of Indian Sweets: 7 Recipes for a Sweet Tooth


Indian sweets, known as “mithai,” are an integral part of the country’s cuisine, rich in history and culture. They tantalize the taste buds with their variety, intricate flavors, and textures. Whether you are celebrating a festival, a wedding, or simply indulging your sweet tooth, Indian sweets hold a special place in our hearts. In this post, we’ll explore seven delightful recipes that will transport you to the bustling streets of India. Grab your apron, and letâ€™s dive into the world of Indian confectionery!

1. Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:

  • For the dough:

    • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 tsp baking soda
    • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
    • 1 tbsp milk (as needed)

  • For the sugar syrup:

    • 1.5 cups sugar
    • 1.5 cups water
    • 2-3 cardamom pods
    • A few drops of rose water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and cardamom pods. Bring to a boil and simmer until the sugar dissolves completely. Remove from heat and add rose water. Let it cool.

  2. Make the dough: In a mixing bowl, combine khoya, flour, baking soda, and cardamom powder. Mix well, adding milk gradually until you form a soft, pliable dough.

  3. Shape the balls: Grease your palms with ghee and take small portions of the dough to form smooth balls, about the size of a marble.

  4. Fry the balls: Heat ghee in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the dough balls until golden brown, ensuring even cooking.

  5. Soak in syrup: Once fried, immediately soak the balls in the warm sugar syrup. Let them sit for at least 30 minutes before serving. Enjoy them warm or at room temperature!

2. Jalebi

Ingredients:

  • For the batter:

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 tbsp cornflour
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • Water (as needed)
    • A pinch of turmeric for color

  • For the sugar syrup:

    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
    • A few drops of lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and lemon juice. Cook until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add cardamom powder and remove from heat.

  2. Make the batter: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, yogurt, turmeric, and enough water to make a smooth batter. Let it ferment for about 3-4 hours.

  3. Shape the jalebi: Heat ghee in a kadhai. Pour the batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle. Squeeze the batter in concentric circles into the hot ghee, frying until golden and crisp.

  4. Dip in syrup: Once fried, dip the jalebi in the warm sugar syrup for a few seconds before removing. Serve hot for that crispy, sweet delight!

3. Barfi

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated coconut (fresh or dried)
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnishing (almonds, pistachios)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the mixture: In a pan, combine grated coconut, condensed milk, and milk over low heat. Stir continuously until the mixture thickens.

  2. Add flavor: Once it thickens, add cardamom powder and mix well. Cook for a few more minutes to release moisture.

  3. Set the barfi: Transfer the mixture to a greased plate or tray and spread evenly. Sprinkle chopped nuts on top for added texture.

  4. Cut and serve: Allow it to cool and set. Cut into squares or diamonds and enjoy a piece of this heavenly treat!

4. Rasgulla

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • A few drops of rose water (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make chenna: Boil milk and add lemon juice to curdle it. Once curdled, strain the mixture through a muslin cloth to separate the whey.

  2. Knead the chenna: Knead the chenna until smooth and soft. Make small, smooth balls from the chenna.

  3. Prepare syrup: In a pot, combine sugar and water and boil until sugar dissolves. Add rose water for fragrance.

  4. Cook the balls: Gently drop the chenna balls into the boiling syrup and cook for about 15 minutes, allowing them to expand and absorb the syrup.

  5. Cool and serve: Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup before serving. They are best enjoyed chilled!

5. Peda

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups khoya
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook khoya: In a pan, heat khoya and condensed milk. Stir continuously over low heat until the mixture thickens.

  2. Flavoring: Add cardamom powder to the mixture and continue to cook until it leaves the sides of the pan.

  3. Shape the pedas: Allow the mixture to cool slightly. Grease your palms and shape small portions into flat discs.

  4. Garnish and serve: Top each peda with a piece of chopped nut. Let them cool completely before serving.

6. Kheer

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios) for garnish
  • A handful of raisins

Instructions:

  1. Prepare rice: Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain.

  2. Cook the kheer: In a pot, bring the milk to a boil and add the soaked rice. Cook on low heat, stirring often, until the rice is cooked and the milk thickens.

  3. Sweeten the dish: Add sugar, cardamom powder, and mix well. Cook for another 5-10 minutes.

  4. Garnish and serve: Stir in the chopped nuts and raisins before serving. Kheer can be enjoyed warm or chilled.

7. Ladoo

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast the besan: In a pan, heat ghee and roast the besan on low heat until it turns golden brown and fragrant.

  2. Mix the ingredients: Remove from heat and let it cool slightly. Add powdered sugar and cardamom powder, mixing thoroughly.

  3. Shape the ladoos: Once cool enough to handle, shape the mixture into small balls, rolling them between your palms.

  4. Garnish and serve: Roll the ladoos in chopped nuts if desired. Let them cool completely before serving.
