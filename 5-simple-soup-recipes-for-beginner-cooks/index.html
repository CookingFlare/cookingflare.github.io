5 Simple Soup Recipes for Beginner Cooks


Making soup is a rewarding experience for beginner cooks. Not only does it offer a chance to hone basic cooking skills, but it also produces warm, comforting meals that can be incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re looking for something hearty to keep the chill at bay or a light soup to pair with a salad, these five simple soup recipes are perfect for anyone just starting their culinary journey. Each recipe is easy to follow, uses accessible ingredients, and requires minimal effort, making them ideal for those who want to quickly build confidence in the kitchen. Let’s dive right in!

1. Classic Tomato Soup

Tomato soup is a timeless favorite for many, and its simplicity makes it a perfect starting point for beginner cooks. Pair it with some crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for a truly comforting meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon dried basil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup heavy cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Pour in the canned tomatoes and broth. If you prefer a touch of sweetness, add the sugar and dried basil.
  3. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Use a blender or immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can skip this step.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream for a richer flavor, if desired, and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve hot and garnish with fresh basil or croutons for a delightful touch.

2. Hearty Lentil Soup

Lentil soup is packed with nutrients, easy to make, and incredibly filling. It’s an affordable way to whip up a healthy meal in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cups dried lentils, rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of ½ lemon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion until translucent, then stir in the carrots and celery and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  2. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, water, cumin, paprika, and bay leaf.
  3. Bring the soup to a boil, reduce the heat, and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  4. Remove the bay leaf and season the soup with salt, pepper, and lemon juice if desired.
  5. Serve warm with a slice of whole-grain bread or a side salad.

3. Chicken Noodle Soup

Chicken noodle soup is a classic comfort dish that’s surprisingly easy to make at home. This recipe is ideal for beginners who want to recreate the flavors of this beloved meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1½ cups egg noodles
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Cook the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Pour in the chicken broth and water, and place the chicken breast into the pot.
  3. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked. Remove the chicken and shred it using two forks.
  4. Add the egg noodles to the pot and simmer for another 8-10 minutes, or until the noodles are tender.
  5. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with chopped parsley for added freshness.

4. Creamy Potato Soup

If you’re a fan of creamy, rich soups, potato soup is the ultimate comfort food. It’s simple to prepare, yet so satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or heavy cream
  • ½ teaspoon thyme (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Crumbled bacon and shredded cheese for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened.
  2. Stir in the diced potatoes and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Use an immersion blender or regular blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you like a chunky texture, puree half of the soup and leave the rest as is.
  4. Stir in the milk or cream for added richness and flavor. Add thyme if desired, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve the soup hot, topped with crumbled bacon, shredded cheese, or chopped parsley.

5. Easy Vegetable Soup

Vegetable soup is a versatile, light meal that’s perfect for inexperienced cooks who want something healthy and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Salt, pepper, and herbs (e.g., thyme, rosemary) to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  2. Stir in the carrots, celery, zucchini, and green beans, cooking for 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and canned tomatoes. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Add the frozen peas in the last 5 minutes of cooking and season the soup with salt, pepper, and your preferred herbs.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy the wholesome freshness of the vegetables.

These five simple soup recipes prove that cooking doesn’t have to be intimidating. They rely on easy-to-find ingredients, straightforward techniques, and minimal equipment — perfect for anyone who’s just starting to explore their culinary skills. By following these recipes, you can create heartwarming soups that bring comfort and nourishment to your table. So grab your pot, roll up your sleeves, and start cooking!
