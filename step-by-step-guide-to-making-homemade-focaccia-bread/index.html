Step-by-Step Guide to Making Homemade Focaccia Bread


Focaccia bread is a beloved Italian flatbread that stands out for its rich flavor, soft texture, and versatile uses. Whether you’re dipping it in olive oil, using it as a sandwich base, or serving it alongside a hearty meal, homemade focaccia bread is a surefire way to impress your family and friends. While it’s simple to make, crafting the perfect focaccia requires attention to detail and an appreciation for the artistry behind bread-making. This step-by-step guide will walk you through every stage, from preparing the dough to savoring the first delicious slice.

Ingredients for Homemade Focaccia Bread

Before diving into the process, it’s essential to gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Dough:

  • 3 ¾ cups (500g) all-purpose flour or bread flour
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast or instant yeast
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 ½ cups (360ml) warm water (around 110°F, warm but not hot)
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) olive oil

For the Topping:

  • ¼ cup (60ml) olive oil (for drizzling)
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Coarse sea salt, for sprinkling
  • Optional: Thinly sliced garlic, olives, cherry tomatoes, or caramelized onions for added flavor

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by activating the yeast, a crucial step for ensuring your focaccia bread rises properly. Combine the warm water and yeast in a small bowl or measuring cup and let it sit for 5-10 minutes. The water temperature matters—too hot and it will kill the yeast, too cold and it won’t activate. You’ll know the yeast is ready when it becomes foamy and bubbly, signaling that it’s alive and thriving.

Step 2: Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Use a whisk to mix them evenly, ensuring the salt is fully incorporated. This will create a solid foundation for the dough and ensure the flavor is well balanced.

Step 3: Mix the Dough

Once your yeast is activated, pour the yeast mixture and olive oil into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Using a wooden spoon, mix everything until it forms a shaggy dough. At this point, you may need to use your hands to fully incorporate the ingredients.

If you have a stand mixer, feel free to use it for this step. Attach the dough hook and knead on medium speed for about 5 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. Otherwise, knead the dough by hand on a lightly floured surface for about 8-10 minutes, until it’s soft and slightly sticky without sticking to your hands.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the dough into a large, lightly oiled bowl. Turn the dough over to coat it entirely in oil, which will prevent it from drying out as it rises. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and leave it in a warm spot to rise for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

If your kitchen is cool, you can preheat your oven to 100°F (or its lowest setting), then turn it off before placing the bowl inside. This creates an ideal warm environment for the dough to rise.

Step 5: Prepare the Pan

Once your dough has doubled in size, it’s time to prepare your baking pan. Choose a large, rimmed baking sheet or a 9x13-inch pan for thicker focaccia. Grease the pan generously with olive oil, ensuring the entire surface is coated—this creates a golden crust and prevents sticking.

Step 6: Shape the Dough

Turn the risen dough out onto the prepared pan and gently stretch and press it to fit the pan’s dimensions. Be careful not to deflate it too much; the goal is to maintain its airy structure. If the dough resists stretching, let it rest for 10 minutes, then try again—this allows the gluten to relax.

Step 7: Second Rise

Cover the pan loosely with plastic wrap or a damp towel and let the dough rise again for another 30-45 minutes. During this time, it will become even softer and puffier, making it perfect for baking.

Step 8: Prepare the Topping

While the dough is completing its second rise, prepare your toppings. Fresh rosemary and coarse sea salt are classic choices, but feel free to get creative. Thinly sliced garlic, halved cherry tomatoes, or briny olives can add excitement and complexity to your focaccia.

Mix the olive oil for drizzling with your chosen herbs and any optional ingredients in a small bowl. This will ensure the toppings distribute evenly during baking.

Step 9: Dimple the Dough

When the dough finishes its second rise, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Use your fingers to create deep dimples across the surface of the dough. Press all the way down to the bottom of the pan—this step is signature to focaccia bread and helps create its unique texture.

Drizzle the olive oil mixture evenly across the surface of the dough, allowing it to pool in the dimples. Sprinkle on a generous amount of coarse sea salt, and add your additional toppings if desired.

Step 10: Bake the Focaccia

Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the focaccia is golden brown and crisp around the edges. You’ll know it’s done when the top is beautifully golden and the bread sounds hollow when tapped.

Step 11: Cool and Serve

Remove the focaccia from the oven and transfer it to a cooling rack. Allow it to cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing—this ensures the flavor develops fully and makes cutting easier.

Serve your focaccia bread warm or at room temperature. It pairs beautifully with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, soups, salads, or even a charcuterie board.

Tips for Success

  • Don’t rush the rise: Allowing the dough to rise properly is key to achieving a fluffy, airy texture.
  • Use quality olive oil: Since olive oil plays a central role in both the dough and the topping, opt for a high-quality variety with rich flavor.
  • Experiment with toppings: Focaccia is endlessly customizable. Try sun-dried tomatoes, parmesan cheese, or caramelized onions for unique combinations.
  • Storage: Store leftover focaccia in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. Reheat in the oven to revive its crispiness.

Conclusion

Making homemade focaccia bread is a fulfilling culinary adventure. Its combination of simplicity, versatility, and delicious flavor makes it a favorite for both seasoned bakers and beginners. By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll master the art of focaccia-making and discover why this Italian classic has earned its place on tables around the world. So roll up your sleeves, embrace the satisfying rhythm of kneading dough, and prepare to enjoy one of the most rewarding homemade breads you’ll ever taste.
