Smoky Ham and Bean Soup for Meal Prep


Soup is a timeless comfort food, and when you combine smoky ham with hearty beans, the result is a rich, flavorful dish that warms you up from the inside out. Whether you’re looking for a meal to feed the family, a dish to make ahead for busy days, or a protein-packed lunch to fuel your week, smoky ham and bean soup is the perfect solution.

This recipe not only offers a satisfying combination of tastes and textures, but it’s also ideal for meal prepping. It’s simple to make, freezes well, and can be customized with a variety of beans and vegetables to suit your tastes. Let’s dive into how to make this smoky ham and bean soup and how to incorporate it into your weekly meal prep routine.

Why Smoky Ham and Bean Soup?

The combination of smoky ham, tender beans, and a well-seasoned broth creates a deeply satisfying and nutrient-dense soup. Not only is this dish a great way to use up leftover ham, but it’s also packed with fiber and protein, making it filling and nourishing. Additionally, the smoky flavor from the ham adds depth and complexity, while the beans contribute a creamy texture that makes each spoonful comforting.

Meal prepping this soup ensures you have a healthy and hearty meal ready for busy days when cooking seems like too much effort. The soup can be stored in the fridge for up to five days or frozen for longer storage, making it an excellent choice for those who want to plan ahead.

Ingredients

To make smoky ham and bean soup, you’ll need the following ingredients. This recipe is designed to serve about 6-8 people, and it can easily be doubled or halved depending on your needs.

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 pound dried white beans (such as great northern or navy beans), soaked overnight and drained, or use 3 cans of white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups cooked ham, diced (preferably with a smoky flavor, such as leftover ham from a holiday roast)
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika (for that signature smoky flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for a touch of acidity)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by prepping your vegetables and ham. If you’re using dried beans, make sure to soak them overnight, or if you’re short on time, use the quick-soak method by bringing them to a boil for 2 minutes, then letting them sit, covered, for 1 hour before draining.

Dice your onion, carrots, and celery. Mince the garlic and cut the cooked ham into bite-sized cubes. If you’re using canned beans, drain and rinse them before adding them to the soup later.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, or until the onion becomes translucent and the vegetables begin to soften. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring frequently, until the garlic becomes fragrant.

Step 3: Add the Ham and Seasonings

Next, add the diced ham to the pot. The ham will add a lot of flavor and depth to the soup, especially if it’s smoked. Stir the ham into the vegetables and cook for about 3-4 minutes, allowing the ham to release its smoky aroma and start to brown slightly.

Now, add the smoked paprika, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf to the pot. Stir everything together, letting the spices toast for a minute or two to release their oils and bring out their flavors.

Step 4: Add the Beans and Liquid

Once the vegetables are softened and the ham is well combined with the seasonings, it’s time to add the beans and liquid. If you’re using soaked dried beans, add them to the pot along with the chicken broth and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 45-60 minutes, or until the beans are tender.

If you’re using canned beans, add them after the soup has simmered for 20-30 minutes, just to heat through. Simmering the soup for a longer time allows the flavors to meld together and the beans to absorb the smoky essence of the ham.

Step 5: Final Touches

Once the soup is finished cooking, remove the bay leaf and taste the soup. Season with salt and black pepper as needed, and for a touch of brightness, add a tablespoon of lemon juice. This step is optional but helps balance the richness of the soup and enhances the overall flavor.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the soup into bowls and serve it hot. This smoky ham and bean soup is perfect on its own, but you can serve it with crusty bread or a side salad for a complete meal. The hearty, smoky, and savory flavors are sure to satisfy your taste buds.

How to Make This Soup for Meal Prep

Meal prepping this soup is straightforward, and it can save you time during the week when you need a healthy, comforting meal on the go.

  1. Make a Double Batch: This recipe is easy to double, which will provide enough soup for multiple servings. You can portion it out into individual containers and store it in the fridge for up to five days.

  2. Freezing: Smoky ham and bean soup freezes wonderfully. If you want to store it for longer, ladle the soup into freezer-safe containers or Ziploc bags. Be sure to leave some space at the top of the container for expansion as the soup freezes. The soup will keep in the freezer for up to 3 months. When you’re ready to eat, simply thaw it overnight in the fridge and reheat on the stovetop.

  3. Portioning for Convenience: If you’re meal prepping, portion the soup into individual servings so it’s easy to grab and go. It makes for a perfect lunch or dinner, and you can even add some extra toppings like shredded cheese or fresh herbs when reheating.

Tips for Customizing Smoky Ham and Bean Soup

  • Beans: While the recipe calls for white beans like great northern or navy beans, feel free to use other beans that you enjoy, such as pinto beans or black beans. Each variety brings a unique texture and flavor to the soup.

  • Vegetables: If you have other vegetables on hand, such as potatoes, spinach, or bell peppers, feel free to add them in. Potatoes can be diced and added early in the cooking process, while spinach or kale can be added in the final stages of cooking for added nutrition.

  • Smokiness: For even more smoky flavor, you can add a splash of liquid smoke or use smoked turkey instead of ham. The combination of these ingredients will deepen the smoky profile of the soup.

  • Spices: If you like a little heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or cayenne pepper. You can also play around with other herbs, like oregano or sage, to adjust the flavor profile to your liking.

Health Benefits of Smoky Ham and Bean Soup

This smoky ham and bean soup is not only delicious but also packed with health benefits. Here are some reasons why it’s a great choice for meal prep:

  • High in Protein: The ham and beans provide a great source of protein, which is essential for muscle repair and overall health.

  • Rich in Fiber: Beans are an excellent source of dietary fiber, which helps improve digestion and keep you feeling full longer.

  • Low in Fat: This soup is relatively low in fat, especially if you use lean ham. It’s a great option for those looking to eat a balanced, heart-healthy meal.

  • Packed with Vitamins and Minerals: The vegetables in this soup provide a range of essential vitamins and minerals, including vitamin A from the carrots, vitamin C from the onions, and potassium from the celery.

Conclusion

Smoky ham and bean soup is a versatile, hearty, and satisfying dish that is perfect for meal prepping. With a rich blend of smoky ham, tender beans, and a variety of vegetables, this soup can be customized to suit your preferences while offering a nutritious and filling meal. Whether you’re looking to use up leftovers or create a dish that can be enjoyed throughout the week, this smoky ham and bean soup is a winning choice.

With easy steps for preparation, storage tips, and options for customization, it’s a recipe that fits seamlessly into a busy lifestyle. Enjoy a comforting bowl of this soup on chilly days, and know that you have a nutritious, homemade meal waiting for you throughout the week.
