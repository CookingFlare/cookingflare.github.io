5 Chicken Alfredo Recipes for a Creamy Delight


Chicken Alfredo is a timeless classic that brings together tender chicken, creamy sauce, and perfectly cooked pasta in one heavenly dish. If you’re a fan of rich, comforting meals, chicken alfredo recipes are always a good choice. With a few simple ingredients, you can create a dish that feels indulgent yet easy enough for a weeknight dinner.

In this post, we’re going to explore five unique and delicious takes on the traditional chicken alfredo recipe. Whether you’re looking for a lighter version, a quick one-pot meal, or something a little more gourmet, we’ve got you covered. These recipes are perfect for dinner parties, family meals, or any occasion where you want a creamy, flavorful dish that everyone will love.

1. Classic Chicken Alfredo: A Comforting Favorite

Let’s start with the tried-and-true classic chicken alfredo. This version features a velvety homemade Alfredo sauce paired with perfectly cooked chicken and pasta. Simple, rich, and absolutely satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat one tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chicken breasts and cook for about 6-7 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside. Once cooled, slice into thin strips.

  2. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving a cup of pasta water.

  3. Prepare the Alfredo Sauce: In the same skillet you used for the chicken, melt the remaining tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir occasionally.

  4. Finish the Sauce: Reduce the heat to low and stir in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy. If the sauce is too thick, add a little pasta water to reach the desired consistency.

  5. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked pasta and sliced chicken to the skillet, tossing everything together until the pasta is coated with the creamy sauce. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This classic chicken alfredo recipe is perfect for those who crave a rich and indulgent meal that never goes out of style.

2. Lightened-Up Chicken Alfredo with Cauliflower Cream Sauce

If you’re looking to cut down on calories without sacrificing flavor, this lighter version of chicken alfredo uses a cauliflower-based cream sauce. It’s a great option for those seeking a healthier alternative without compromising on taste.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 medium cauliflower head, chopped
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Heat one tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, until fully cooked and golden brown. Remove from the skillet, let it rest for a few minutes, and then slice into strips.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Cook the whole wheat pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving a bit of pasta water.

  3. Prepare the Cauliflower Sauce: In a large pot, steam the cauliflower florets until tender (about 10 minutes). Transfer the cauliflower to a blender or food processor. Add the chicken broth, olive oil, garlic, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  4. Combine and Serve: In the same skillet you used for the chicken, heat the cauliflower sauce over low heat. Stir in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce is creamy and well combined. Add the pasta and toss to coat. Add the sliced chicken and continue mixing until evenly distributed. Garnish with fresh basil.

This version is not only lighter but also packed with nutrients, making it a great option for those who want to enjoy their favorite pasta dish without the extra calories.

3. One-Pot Chicken Alfredo for a Quick Weeknight Dinner

Life can get busy, and sometimes you just need a meal that’s both quick and delicious. This one-pot chicken alfredo recipe combines the creamy sauce, chicken, and pasta all in one pan, reducing both cooking time and cleanup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 12 oz penne pasta
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the diced chicken and cook for about 5-6 minutes until browned and fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Cook the Pasta: In the same pot, add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Stir in the penne pasta and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente and most of the liquid has been absorbed.

  3. Finish the Sauce: Once the pasta is cooked, add the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese to the pot. Stir to combine and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens. If the sauce is too thick, add a little more chicken broth or water to reach the desired consistency.

  4. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked chicken back into the pot and stir until everything is well mixed. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

This one-pot chicken alfredo is perfect for those nights when you want a quick and easy meal without compromising on flavor.

4. Spicy Chicken Alfredo for a Flavor Kick

If you enjoy a little heat with your creamy pasta, this spicy chicken alfredo recipe is just what you need. By adding a touch of red pepper flakes and some chili powder, this version of chicken alfredo will give your taste buds a delightful kick.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, red pepper flakes, and chili powder. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side until fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the pan and let it rest before slicing it into thin strips.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Cook the fettuccine pasta according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside, reserving a bit of pasta water.

  3. Make the Alfredo Sauce: In the same skillet, add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the heavy cream, stirring constantly. Bring it to a simmer and cook for a few minutes until the sauce thickens. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and continue stirring until the sauce is smooth.

  4. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked pasta and chicken to the skillet. Toss everything together, ensuring the pasta is well-coated in the spicy Alfredo sauce. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This spicy chicken alfredo is perfect for those who like to turn up the heat on their favorite pasta dishes.

5. Chicken Alfredo with Broccoli for Added Freshness

For those looking to add some vegetables to their meal without compromising on flavor, this chicken alfredo with broccoli is an excellent choice. The fresh broccoli adds a pop of color, a bit of crunch, and some nutritional value to the creamy dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Heat butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side until golden brown and fully cooked. Set the chicken aside and slice it into strips.

  2. Cook the Pasta and Broccoli: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to the package instructions. In the last 3-4 minutes of cooking, add the broccoli florets to the water with the pasta. Drain both and set aside.

  3. Make the Alfredo Sauce: In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for about 1 minute. Pour in the heavy cream and bring it to a simmer. Stir in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce becomes thick and creamy.

  4. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked pasta, broccoli, and sliced chicken to the skillet. Toss everything together until well combined and coated in the Alfredo sauce. Serve immediately.

This chicken alfredo with broccoli is a great way to add some extra nutrition and freshness to your meal while still enjoying the creamy comfort of a classic alfredo.

Conclusion

These five chicken alfredo recipes bring variety to your table without compromising on the creamy, comforting goodness that makes this dish so beloved. Whether you’re in the mood for a classic version, a lighter alternative, or something with a bit of spice, there’s a chicken alfredo recipe here for everyone. Give one or more a try and discover the delicious ways you can enjoy this classic pasta dish!
