Deliciously Divine: 15 Must-Try Asian Fusion Recipes for Food Lovers


Welcome, food enthusiasts, to a gastronomic adventure that will transport your taste buds to the mesmerizing world of Asian Fusion cuisine! In this blog post, we’ve compiled a list of 15 mouthwatering recipes that artfully blend flavors andstyles from different Asian countries to create dazzling dishes that’ll tantalize your senses. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen beginner, these recipes are bound to inspire and amaze you.

1. Spicy Korean Fried Chicken

Ever craved an unforgettable Korean Fried Chicken like those found in popular food shows? Look no further! This incredibly crisp and spicy chicken dish will have you seriously craving more. It features gochujang, a spicy Korean red chile paste, along with a sweet and savory sauce. A must-try for any spicy food lover looking to take their culinary skills to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lbs. chicken pieces (legs and wings)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chile paste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil
  • Green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Soak chicken pieces in the buttermilk for 24 hours in the refrigerator.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, hot sauce, gochujang, and minced garlic.
  3. Coat chicken pieces in the flour mixture, then dredge in cornstarch to ensure a super crispy crust.
  4. In a large skillet or deep-fryer, heat oil to 375°F (190°C) and fry chicken, in batches, for 10-12 minutes or until golden and cooked through.
  5. For the sauce, mix gochujang and a small amount of the fried chicken’s cooking oil; adjust to your personal spice level.
  6. Toss the fried chicken in the sauce and garnish with chopped green onions.

Enjoy the heat and the sweet and savory flavors at the same time!

2. Japanese-Style Chicken Donburi

This Donburi dish will make you wonder why you haven’t made it sooner! A comforting one-bowl meal where tender chicken and aromatic veggies are served on a bed of fluffy rice and drizzled with a flavorful sauce, this Japanese-Style Chicken Donburi will surely make a lasting impression on your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (sushi rice or jasmine rice)
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat sesame oil and sauté chicken, onion, and broccoli until cooked.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, mirin, and sake.
  3. Pour the sauce over the cooked chicken and veggies and stir well to coat.
  4. Serve warm over cooked rice.
  5. Top with chopped green onions before serving.

This savory and scrumptious Chicken Donburi will leave you feeling satisfied and asking for more!

3. Thai Pumpkin CURRY

Introducing Thai Pumpkin Curry, a rich, creamy, and utterly divine dish featuring tender pumpkin, harmoniously blended spices, and aromatic Thai herbs. This flavorful curry is both vegetarian and gluten-free, making it a crowd-pleaser for everyone!

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium pumpkin, peeled and cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Crushed peanuts, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat oil and add red curry paste, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add pumpkin, bell pepper, and zucchini; stir well to coat in the curry paste.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk, bring to a simmer, and cook until the pumpkin is tender.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together cornstarch and water, then slowly add to the curry. Stir well to thicken the sauce.
  5. Add palm sugar and fish sauce; taste and adjust to your liking.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with crushed peanuts.

Devour the sumptuous flavors of this exotic Thai Pumpkin Curry with a side of steamed rice or your preferred grain!

4. Vietnamese Grilled Lemongrass Chicken

You’ll be dreaming of the cao lau streets of Hoi An after trying this flavorful, packed-full-of-flavors Vietnamese Grilled Lemongrass Chicken. Fresh herbs, lemongrass, garlic, fish sauce, and more combine to create a mouthwatering taste explosion that’ll leave you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lime zest (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, for grilling
  • Fresh herbs, chopped (cilantro, mint, Thai basil)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together fish sauce, sugar, lemongrass, garlic, lime zest (if using), and lime juice to create the marinade.
  2. Prepare the chicken by removing fat and pores - this makes it taste better and can help reduce odors while cooking.
  3. Marinate the chicken in the mixture for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  5. Lightly brush the chicken with vegetable oil before placing it on the grill.
  6. Grill for 3-4 minutes on each side until nicely charred and cooked through. Allow to rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  7. Serve with fresh herbs, on sticks or rice, and chopped veggies.

Take your taste buds on a flavorful journey you won’t forget!

5. Singapore Chili Crab

Singapore’s iconic dish, Chili Crab, will transport you to the bustling hawker centers of this incredible city-state. Plump, juicy crab doused in a rich, sweet, and fiery chili sauce that’ll have you begging for more!

Ingredients:

  • 2 Dungeness crabs (about 1 1/2 lbs each)
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons cracked black pepper
  • Juice from 3 limes
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Few dashes fish sauce
  • 1 pound small green onions, sliced
  • 1 pound fried mantou (Asian buns), for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cut crab in half lengthwise, removing the face (eyeballs), gills, and outside shell. Rinse the crab, and save the claw meat for another use.
  2. Heat oil in a large wok, pan-fry crab halves for 8-10 minutes; flip halfway through cooking. Remove and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together rice vinegar, chicken broth, cornstarch, black pepper, lime juice, sesame oil, sugar, and fish sauce.
  4. In the same oil used for frying crab, cook the sauce over medium heat for 3-4 minutes until thickened.
  5. Add the crab halves back into the wok, spooning chili sauce generously over each half.
  6. Garnish with green onions, and serve with fried mantou.

Uncover the secret to that lip-smacking good Singapore Chili Crab right in your own kitchen!
