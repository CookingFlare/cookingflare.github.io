Kids in the Kitchen: 5 Healthy Recipes to Try


Getting kids involved in the kitchen is not only a fun activity, but it also sets the stage for a lifetime of healthy eating habits. Cooking with kids can be a great way to bond, teach them about nutrition, and encourage them to try new foods. Here are 5 healthy and delicious recipes that are perfect for getting kids involved in the kitchen.

1. Rainbow Veggie Wraps

Ingredients:

  • Whole grain wraps
  • Red, orange, yellow, green, and purple bell peppers (sliced into strips)
  • Shredded carrots
  • Cucumber (sliced into strips)
  • Hummus or Greek yogurt dip

Instructions:

  1. Lay out the whole grain wraps on a clean surface.
  2. Let the kids arrange the colorful veggies in a rainbow pattern on the wraps.
  3. Spread a layer of hummus or Greek yogurt dip over the veggies.
  4. Roll up the wraps tightly and slice them into bite-sized pieces.

This recipe not only encourages kids to eat their veggies but also allows them to get creative with arranging the colors of the rainbow.

2. Fruit Kabobs with Yogurt Dip

Ingredients:

  • Assorted fruit (such as strawberries, grapes, pineapple, and melon)
  • Wooden skewers
  • Greek yogurt
  • Honey

Instructions:

  1. Wash and prepare the fruit by cutting them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Thread the fruit onto the wooden skewers, creating colorful fruit kabobs.
  3. Mix Greek yogurt with a drizzle of honey to create a delicious and healthy dip.
  4. Serve the fruit kabobs with the yogurt dip for a refreshing and nutritious snack.

This recipe is not only visually appealing but also a great way to encourage kids to eat more fruit while having fun assembling their own fruit kabobs.

3. Mini Veggie Pizzas

Ingredients:

  • Whole grain English muffins or pita bread
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Assorted veggies (such as bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, mushrooms, and spinach)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Split the English muffins or pita bread in half to create the pizza crusts.
  3. Let the kids spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on each crust.
  4. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  5. Allow the kids to arrange their favorite veggies on top of the cheese.
  6. Bake the mini pizzas in the oven for 10-12 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

This recipe allows kids to customize their own mini pizzas with an assortment of colorful and nutritious veggies.

4. Crunchy Baked Zucchini Fries

Ingredients:

  • Zucchini (cut into sticks)
  • Whole wheat breadcrumbs
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Garlic powder
  • Egg
  • Cooking spray

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a shallow bowl, mix the whole wheat breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, and garlic powder.
  3. In another bowl, beat the egg.
  4. Dip the zucchini sticks into the beaten egg, then coat them with the breadcrumb mixture.
  5. Place the coated zucchini sticks on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Lightly spray the zucchini sticks with cooking spray.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the zucchini fries are golden and crispy.

This recipe provides a healthier alternative to traditional fries while allowing kids to participate in the breading process.

5. Berry Smoothie Bowl

Ingredients:

  • Mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • Banana
  • Greek yogurt
  • Milk or almond milk
  • Toppings (such as granola, shredded coconut, and chia seeds)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the mixed berries, banana, Greek yogurt, and milk.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the smoothie into a bowl.
  4. Let the kids get creative with arranging the toppings on the smoothie bowl.

Smoothie bowls are not only a nutritious breakfast or snack option, but they also allow kids to express their creativity with colorful and tasty toppings.

Conclusion

Cooking with kids can be a rewarding experience for both parents and children. These 5 healthy recipes are designed to engage kids in the kitchen while introducing them to a variety of nutritious foods. By involving kids in the cooking process, they are more likely to develop an appreciation for healthy eating and a sense of accomplishment. So, gather your little chefs and have fun creating these delicious and wholesome dishes together!
