Korean Pork Stir-Fry with Vegetables Recipe (Dwaeji Bokkeum)


Korean cuisine is renowned worldwide for its bold flavors, vibrant ingredients, and enticing aromas. Among its vast array of dishes, Dwaeji Bokkeum, or Korean Pork Stir-Fry, stands out as a delightful crowd-pleaser. This sizzling stir-fry combines tender, marinated pork with an assortment of colorful vegetables, all coated in a spicy-sweet sauce that packs a punch of flavor. It’s a dish that’s quick to prepare, versatile for any occasion, and sure to become a favorite in your home.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

The beauty of Dwaeji Bokkeum lies in its simplicity and depth of flavor. This recipe strikes the perfect balance between spicy, sweet, and savory elements, making it a feast for your taste buds. You’ll also love how customizable it is—you can swap out vegetables depending on what’s in your fridge or adjust the spice level to suit your personal preference. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe delivers incredible results with minimal effort.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare this authentic dish, make sure you have the following ingredients handy:

For the Pork Marinade:

  • 1 pound (450g) pork belly or pork shoulder, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 teaspoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any neutral cooking oil)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced thinly
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper (red, green, or yellow), sliced
  • 1 zucchini, halved and sliced
  • 2-3 scallions, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • Optional: 1 cup cabbage, shredded

For Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds
  • Additional chopped scallions

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Begin by slicing the pork belly or shoulder into thin strips, about 1⁄4-inch thick. Smaller slices help the pork cook evenly and allow the marinade to soak into the meat more thoroughly.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a mixing bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir until the ingredients are well blended to create a smooth and flavorful marinade.

Place the pork into the bowl with the marinade, ensuring every piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and let the pork marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes—though, if you have time, allowing it to marinate for 2-3 hours will enhance the flavors even more!

Step 3: Prepare the Vegetables

While the pork is marinating, prep your vegetables. Slice the onion, julienne the carrot, and thinly slice the bell pepper. Cut the zucchini into even-sized pieces, and shred the cabbage if you’re including it in your stir-fry. Chop the scallions into bite-sized pieces, setting aside a portion for garnish later.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of neutral cooking oil, and once it’s shimmering, toss in the onion and carrot. Stir-fry for about 2 minutes until they begin to soften.

Add the bell pepper, zucchini, and cabbage, stirring continuously for another 3-4 minutes. The vegetables should be tender-crisp and vibrant by the time they’re cooked. Remove the stir-fried vegetables from the pan and set them aside while you cook the pork.

Step 5: Cook the Pork

In the same skillet or wok, add another tablespoon of oil and increase the heat to high. Add the marinated pork slices in a single layer, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Stir-fry the pork for 6-8 minutes, flipping occasionally until the meat is caramelized, slightly crisped on the edges, and fully cooked through.

As the pork cooks, the spicy-sweet marinade will reduce, glazing the meat with its rich flavors. Once the pork is cooked, return the vegetables to the pan and toss everything together for 1-2 minutes to combine. If the dish looks dry, feel free to add a splash of water to loosen the sauce.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the stir-fried pork and vegetables to a plate or serving bowl. Garnish generously with sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Serve hot over steamed rice or as part of a larger spread of Korean side dishes.

Tips for Perfecting Your Dwaeji Bokkeum

  1. Choosing the Right Pork: Pork belly offers a rich, fatty texture that is ideal for this dish, but if you prefer leaner cuts, pork shoulder or loin works equally well.
  2. Authentic Korean Ingredients: Gochujang and gochugaru are key to achieving authentic flavors in this dish. You can find these ingredients at most Asian grocery stores or online.
  3. Vegetable Variations: Feel free to use seasonal or favorite vegetables. Mushrooms, snap peas, or bok choy can be excellent additions.
  4. Adjusting Spice Levels: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of gochugaru or substitute it with paprika for color without added heat.

Why Make This Recipe Tonight?

Dwaeji Bokkeum is more than just a meal—it’s an experience. Every bite is a harmonious symphony of smoky pork, crisp-tender vegetables, and spicy-sweet sauce. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a dish to impress dinner guests, this stir-fry checks all the boxes.

Pair the stir-fry with a steaming bowl of rice or Korean-style ssam (lettuce wraps), and suddenly, your home transforms into a Korean restaurant with a comforting and satisfying feast. Plus, the leftovers reheat wonderfully, making it a great make-ahead option for busy days.

Wrapping Up

Dwaeji Bokkeum is a celebration of Korean flavors and culinary simplicity that anyone can master. With its marinated pork and colorful vegetables all stir-fried to perfection, this recipe promises bold tastes in every bite. It highlights the versatility and vibrancy that Korean cuisine is known for, and it can easily become a staple in your weekly rotation.

So gather your ingredients, fire up your wok, and let your kitchen be filled with the irresistible aromas of this Korean Pork Stir-Fry. Once you try it, you’ll wonder how you ever cooked without it. Happy cooking!
