BBQ Pork Tenderloin: A Lean and Flavorful Option


Just because you want a tasty, smoky BBQ meal doesn’t mean you have to indulge in a fatty cut of meat. Enter the pork tenderloin - a lean, flavorful option that won’t weigh you down. It’s time to elevate your BBQ game with some scrumptious, tender BBQ pork tenderloin.

What is BBQ Pork Tenderloin?

Pork tenderloin is a long, thin cut of meat from the center of the pork loin. It’s tender, lean, and quite versatile when it comes to flavoring and cooking methods. When prepared using a combination of marinade and BBQ techniques, it becomes irresistible.

Perfect Flavor Pairings for BBQ Pork Tenderloin

While the pork tenderloin itself is lean, it can still handle an array of strong flavors, including rubs and marinades. Depending on your mood or occasion, you can experiment with different flavors, ranging from sweet to spicy to savory.

For those who favor tangy flavors, a vinegar-based mop will bring just the right level of zing to your tenderloin. If you’re feeling adventurous, experiment with a miso-glazed version for delicious umami undertones.

For a touch of smokiness, infuse your tenderloin with hickory or applewood smoke for added depth. Or, for a simple and comforting flavor, opt for garlic and rosemary, which combines beautifully with pork.

How to BBQ Pork Tenderloin

  1. Prepare your tenderloin: Purchase a one-pound pork tenderloin and trim any excess fat and silverskin. Pat the tenderloin dry with paper towels to create a crisp, crisp crust.

  2. Choose your BBQ method: Choose your BBQ setup whether it’s a grill, smoker, or even a cast-iron skillet with a burning chunk of wood if you’re cooking inside. Remember, low and slow is the way to go for achieving tender, juicy results.

  3. Apply your flavor-enhancing elements: Whether it’s a rub, mop, or glaze, apply your chosen flavorings to your tenderloin. It’s time to fire up that grill and get cooking.

  4. Smoke it low and slow: Keep the temperature around 250°F (121°C) and cook the tenderloin until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C), which will take around 20 minutes per pound on average.

  5. Rest and slice: Let your BBQ pork tenderloin rest for at least ten minutes under a foil tent before slicing. This step is crucial to allowing the juices to redistribute, ensuring a tender texture.

Tips for Perfect BBQ Pork Tenderloin

  • Invest in a meat thermometer. It’s the only reliable way to know if your tenderloin is cooked to perfection.

  • Don’t skip the drying process. Drying the pork removes excess surface moisture that can impede the formation of a delicious crust.

  • Opt for a wood pellet smoker for hands-off smoking that yields perfect results every time.

  • Remember to rest your tenderloin. Skimping on resting time can lead to disappointing results.

Incorporate these tips into your next BBQ, and you’ll have a crowd-pleasing, juicy, and flavorful BBQ pork tenderloin every time.(point-free) It’s time to upgrade your BBQ game with this lean and flavorful option. Happy grilling!
