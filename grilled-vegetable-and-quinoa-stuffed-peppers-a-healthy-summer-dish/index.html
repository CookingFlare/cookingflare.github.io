Grilled Vegetable and Quinoa Stuffed Peppers: A Healthy Summer Dish


If you’re looking for a delicious and healthy dish to enjoy this summer, look no further than grilled vegetable and quinoa stuffed peppers. This vibrant and flavorful recipe combines the smoky goodness of grilled vegetables with the nutty goodness of quinoa, all packed into a colorful and nutritious pepper shell. It’s a dish that will satisfy your taste buds and leave you feeling satisfied and nourished.

Ingredients

  • 4 bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the diced zucchini, yellow squash, red onion, cherry tomatoes, and corn kernels. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Toss well to coat the vegetables evenly.

  4. Place the vegetable mixture on a grill pan or skewers, making sure the pieces are spread out to allow for even cooking. Grill the vegetables for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly charred, turning occasionally.

  5. While the vegetables are grilling, prepare the quinoa according to package instructions. Fluff it with a fork and set aside.

  6. Once the vegetables are grilled to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them cool slightly. Dice the grilled vegetables into smaller pieces and transfer them to a large mixing bowl.

  7. Add the cooked quinoa to the bowl with the grilled vegetables and toss gently to combine. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  8. Spoon the quinoa and vegetable mixture into the hollowed-out bell peppers, filling them to the top.

  9. Place the stuffed peppers on the grill, cover, and cook for an additional 10-12 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.

  10. Remove the stuffed peppers from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled vegetable and quinoa stuffed peppers make a fantastic main course for a vegetarian or vegan meal. They are also great as a side dish or can be enjoyed cold as leftovers for a quick and healthy lunch.

These stuffed peppers can be served as is, or you can get creative and serve them with a dollop of Greek yogurt or a sprinkle of crumbled feta cheese for added creaminess and tanginess. If you’re a fan of spice, you can also drizzle some hot sauce or sprinkle red pepper flakes on top.

Final Thoughts

Grilled vegetable and quinoa stuffed peppers are a versatile and nutritious dish that celebrates the flavors of summer. The combination of smoky grilled vegetables and hearty quinoa creates a filling and satisfying meal that is perfect for outdoor gatherings or a simple weeknight dinner. Plus, the vibrant colors of the peppers make this dish a feast for the eyes as well.

So, fire up your grill and give this healthy summer dish a try. Your taste buds and your body will thank you!
