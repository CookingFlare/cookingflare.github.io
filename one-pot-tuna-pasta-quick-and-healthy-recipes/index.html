One-Pot Tuna Pasta: Quick and Healthy Recipes


Looking for a delicious and nutritious meal that can be whipped up in no time? One-pot tuna pasta is the perfect solution! This easy-to-make dish combines the savory goodness of tuna with the satisfying comfort of pasta, all cooked in one pot for minimal cleanup. Whether you’re a busy parent, a college student on a budget, or simply someone who enjoys a hearty meal without spending hours in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your weekly rotation.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. whole wheat spaghetti
  • 2 cans (5 oz. each) of tuna in water, drained
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz.) of diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups of low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until they are soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the canned tomatoes, oregano, and basil to the pot, stirring to combine with the onions and garlic.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Break the spaghetti in half and add it to the pot, ensuring that it is submerged in the liquid.
  5. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  6. Once the pasta is almost al dente, add the drained tuna to the pot and stir gently to incorporate it into the dish.
  7. Continue cooking for another few minutes until the pasta is cooked to your liking and most of the liquid has been absorbed.
  8. Season the one-pot tuna pasta with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh parsley.

Tips:

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables such as spinach, cherry tomatoes, or bell peppers.
  • For an extra kick of flavor, sprinkle some red pepper flakes or a squeeze of lemon juice over the finished dish.
  • If you prefer a creamier sauce, you can stir in a splash of heavy cream or a dollop of cream cheese towards the end of cooking.

One-pot tuna pasta is not only a time-saving meal but also a nutritious option packed with protein, fiber, and essential vitamins. Whether you’re cooking for one, feeding a family, or meal prepping for the week ahead, this recipe ticks all the boxes for convenience, taste, and health benefits. Try it out for your next dinner and enjoy a satisfying dish that’s as easy to make as it is delicious!

Remember, good food doesn’t have to be complicated. With simple recipes like this one, you can enjoy a homemade meal that nourishes both your body and soul. Happy cooking!
