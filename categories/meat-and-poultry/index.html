Homestyle Turkey Pot Pie Recipes for Comforting Dinners

Nothing beats the comforting taste of a homestyle turkey pot pie. This classic dish is perfect for cozy family dinners or gatherings with friends. With its flaky crust and savory filling, it’s no wonder that turkey pot pie is a favorite among many. In this blog post, we will explore different variations of this delicious recipe that you can try at home. Classic Homestyle Turkey Pot Pie Ingredients: 2 cups cooked turkey, diced 1 cup carrots, diced 1 cup peas 1/2 cup celery, diced 1/2 cup onion, diced 3 tablespoons butter 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 2 cups chicken broth 1/2 cup milk Salt and pepper to taste 2 sheets of puff pastry Instructions: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the carrots, peas, celery, and onion. Cook until the vegetables are tender. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the sauce thickens. Stir in the cooked turkey and season with salt and pepper. Transfer the mixture to a deep-dish pie plate or individual ramekins. Place the puff pastry over the filling, trimming any excess. Cut a few slits in the pastry to allow steam to escape. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Turkey Pot Pie with Sweet Potato Crust Ingredients: 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded 1 cup sweet potato, grated 1 cup mushrooms, sliced 1/2 cup onion, diced 1/2 cup celery, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1 cup chicken broth 1/2 cup milk Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, mushrooms, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are tender. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the sauce thickens. Stir in the cooked turkey and season with salt and pepper. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish. In a separate bowl, combine the grated sweet potato with a pinch of salt and pepper. Spread the sweet potato mixture over the turkey filling. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the sweet potato crust is crispy and golden brown. Mini Turkey Pot Pies Ingredients: 2 cups cooked turkey, diced 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables 1/2 cup onion, diced 1/2 cup celery, diced 1/4 cup butter 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1 1/2 cups chicken broth 1/2 cup milk Salt and pepper to taste Refrigerated biscuit dough Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, and frozen mixed vegetables. Cook until the vegetables are tender. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the sauce thickens. Stir in the cooked turkey and season with salt and pepper. Divide the turkey mixture among individual ramekins. Flatten each biscuit dough and place it over the turkey filling, pressing the edges to seal. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the biscuit topping is golden brown.
