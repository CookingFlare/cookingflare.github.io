Savory and Smoky BBQ Baked Beans: A Classic Side Dish


There’s something undeniably comforting about a big scoop of hearty, flavorful baked beans alongside your favorite grilled meats. The combination of savory and smoky flavors in BBQ baked beans makes them a classic side dish for summer barbecues, potlucks, and family gatherings. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through creating a mouthwatering batch of savory and smoky BBQ baked beans that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried navy beans
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup dark molasses
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced (optional for added smokiness)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beans

  • Rinse the navy beans under cold water and remove any debris.
  • Place the beans in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 hour. Drain the beans and set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Aromatics

  • Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  • In a Dutch oven or a large oven-safe pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy. If not using bacon, heat 1 tablespoon of oil in the pot.
  • Add the chopped onion to the pot and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

  • In a bowl, combine the ketchup, dark molasses, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, smoked paprika, black pepper, and salt. Mix well to combine.

Step 4: Combine and Bake

  • Add the cooked beans to the pot with the onions and bacon.
  • Pour the prepared sauce over the beans and stir to coat evenly.
  • Add enough chicken or vegetable broth to just cover the beans.
  • Cover the pot with a lid and transfer to the preheated oven.
  • Bake for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the beans are tender and the sauce has thickened to your liking.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  • Once the beans are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
  • Serve the savory and smoky BBQ baked beans as a side dish to your favorite grilled meats, or as a flavorful addition to any gathering.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, omit the bacon and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • If you prefer a sweeter flavor, increase the amount of brown sugar or molasses in the sauce.
  • Experiment with different types of beans, such as pinto beans or black beans, for a unique twist on this classic dish.

Savory and smoky BBQ baked beans are a timeless favorite that never fails to impress. With a perfect balance of savory, sweet, and smoky flavors, this classic side dish is sure to become a staple at your gatherings. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a comforting dish, these BBQ baked beans will elevate any meal with their rich and satisfying taste. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a batch of irresistible savory and smoky BBQ baked beans that will have everyone asking for your secret recipe.
