Vegan Raspberry Thumbprint Cookies: Sweet and Jammy


If you’re craving a sweet and fruity treat that’s not only delicious but also vegan, then you’re in for a real treat with these Vegan Raspberry Thumbprint Cookies. These delightful cookies are bursting with the fresh, tangy flavor of raspberries, and they’re perfect for any occasion, from afternoon tea to a special dessert.

Ingredients

For the Cookie Dough:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 1/2 cup raspberry jam
  • 1/4 cup fresh raspberries, mashed
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Make the Cookie Dough: In a large bowl, cream together the vegan butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. Add the vanilla extract and mix until well combined. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, almond flour, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.

  3. Form the Cookies: Roll the cookie dough into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter. Place the balls on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Using your thumb or the back of a small spoon, make an indentation in the center of each cookie.

  4. Prepare the Filling: In a small bowl, mix together the raspberry jam, mashed raspberries, and maple syrup until well combined. Spoon a small amount of the raspberry filling into the indentation of each cookie.

  5. Bake the Cookies: Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  6. Enjoy: Once the cookies have cooled, they are ready to be enjoyed! Serve them with a cup of tea or coffee, and savor the sweet and jammy flavor of these delightful vegan treats.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, try using different flavors of jam, such as apricot or blackberry, to create a variety of thumbprint cookies.
  • If you prefer a nut-free version, you can substitute the almond flour with additional all-purpose flour.
  • These cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days, so feel free to make a batch ahead of time and enjoy them throughout the week.

Whether you’re a seasoned vegan baker or just starting to explore plant-based desserts, these Vegan Raspberry Thumbprint Cookies are sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. Their sweet and jammy goodness will win over anyone who takes a bite, and you can feel good about indulging in a treat that’s both cruelty-free and utterly delicious. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to bake up a batch of these delightful cookies!
