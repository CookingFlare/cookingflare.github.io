Spanish Holiday Recipes: 12 Dishes to Celebrate with Family


The Spanish holiday season is a vibrant, flavorful time when the aroma of rich, traditional dishes fills every home. Whether you’re gathering with family or seeking to bring a taste of Spain to your celebrations, these 12 iconic Spanish holiday recipes offer a festive and heartwarming way to connect through food. Each dish tells a story, steeped in culture and tradition, showcasing the diverse regions of Spain and their culinary heritage.

1. Turrón: The Classic Almond Nougat

No Spanish holiday table is complete without turrón. This chewy, sweet almond nougat is a beloved Christmas treat. Made from toasted almonds, honey, sugar, and egg whites, it comes in two styles: the hard, crunchy Alicante variety and the soft, melt-in-your-mouth Jijona. Crafting turrón at home is a rewarding challenge, filling your kitchen with the scent of toasted almonds and sweet honey.

Tips for Perfect Turrón:

  • Use high-quality Marcona almonds for authenticity.
  • Toast almonds lightly to bring out their nutty flavor.
  • Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form for a light, airy texture.

2. Roscón de Reyes: The Three Kings’ Cake

Traditionally eaten on January 6th to celebrate the Epiphany, Roscón de Reyes is a tender, sweet bread shaped into a ring, adorned with candied fruits symbolizing jewels. Hidden inside are small figurines and a dry bean, making each slice a surprise. The person who finds the figurine becomes “king” or “queen” for the day.

Making Roscón:

  • Use aromatic orange blossom water to infuse the dough.
  • Patience is key: the dough requires several rises to develop its light texture.
  • Frost with a sugar glaze and decorate with candied orange peel and cherries.

3. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-Style Octopus)

Originating from Galicia in northwest Spain, Pulpo a la Gallega is a simple yet elegant dish. Tender octopus is boiled, then sliced and sprinkled with coarse sea salt, smoked paprika, and drizzled with rich olive oil, served atop boiled potatoes.

Cooking Notes:

  • Boil octopus gently to keep it tender.
  • Use sweet and smoky Spanish paprika for authentic flavor.
  • Serve on wooden platters for a rustic touch.

4. Cordero Asado (Roast Lamb)

Roast lamb is a centerpiece of many Spanish holiday feasts, especially in regions like Castilla and Aragón. Slow-roasted with garlic, rosemary, and olive oil until golden and succulent, Cordero Asado is simple comfort food elevated by its perfectly caramelized crust.

Key Tips:

  • Marinate the lamb ahead for deeper flavor.
  • Roast at moderate temperature to keep meat juicy.
  • Serve with roasted potatoes and seasonal vegetables.

5. Sopa de Almendra (Almond Soup)

Sopa de Almendra is a traditional Andalusian soup blending toasted almonds, garlic, bread, and olive oil into a creamy, warm bowl perfect for winter celebrations. This dish exemplifies the Moorish influences in southern Spanish cuisine.

How to Prepare:

  • Soak bread in water or broth to soften before blending.
  • Grind almonds finely for a smooth, velvety consistency.
  • Garnish with fried bread cubes or slivered almonds.

6. Empanadas Gallegas (Galician Meat Pies)

These savory pastries, stuffed with tuna, onion, bell peppers, and tomato sauce, are perfect for holiday gatherings. Empanadas Gallegas are celebrated throughout Spain, but hail from Galicia.

Baking Advice:

  • Make a sturdy dough that holds fillings well.
  • Use a rich tomato sofrito to keep the filling moist.
  • Brush tops with egg wash for a glossy finish.

7. Bacalao a la Vizcaína (Basque-Style Salted Cod)

Salt cod is a staple in Spanish Christmas cooking, especially on Christmas Eve. Bacalao a la Vizcaína features cod stewed in a sauce of red peppers, tomatoes, onions, and garlic. The complex sauce balances the saltiness of the fish beautifully.

Preparation Tips:

  • Desalt cod properly by soaking it for 24-48 hours and changing water frequently.
  • Simmer sauce gently to meld flavors.
  • Serve with crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

8. Marcona Almond and Olive Tapas

A simple yet essential part of Spanish celebrations is the array of tapas. Marcona almonds, roasted and salted, pair beautifully with Spanish olives like Manzanilla or Arbequina. These snacks stimulate the appetite and complement any festive drink.

To Serve:

  • Warm almonds slightly before serving for extra aroma.
  • Offer a variety of olives to cater to different tastes.
  • Add slices of manchego cheese for a richer board.

9. Polvorones and Mantecados

These crumbly shortbread cookies are traditional Christmas sweets throughout Spain, especially in Andalusia. Polvorones contain ground almonds, while mantecados rely on pork lard (manteca) to achieve their melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Baking Notes:

  • Chill dough before shaping to avoid spreading.
  • Use powdered sugar for sweetness and dusting.
  • Store in airtight containers to keep cookies dry.

10. Escudella i Carn d’Olla (Catalan Winter Stew)

A hearty Catalan stew, Escudella i Carn d’Olla includes a medley of meats, sausages, vegetables, and sometimes pasta or rice, served in two stages: the broth as a soup and the meats with vegetables as a main dish. It’s a communal dish perfect for cold holiday nights.

Cooking Suggestions:

  • Use a variety of meats: pork, chicken, and beef for depth.
  • Simmer slowly to develop rich flavors.
  • Serve the broth first, then plate the meats and vegetables.

11. Crema Catalana

Similar to crème brûlée, Crema Catalana is a creamy custard flavored with lemon zest and cinnamon, topped with a caramelized sugar crust. Often enjoyed during holiday meals, it offers a light and fragrant finish to rich feasts.

Tips for Success:

  • Infuse milk with zest and cinnamon before mixing custard.
  • Use a kitchen torch or broiler to caramelize sugar quickly without warming custard.
  • Chill custard well before adding sugar for best texture contrast.

12. Churros con Chocolate

End your holiday celebrations with this beloved Spanish dessert. Freshly fried churros are dusted with sugar and served alongside thick, rich hot chocolate for dipping.

How to Make:

  • The dough is simple—flour, water, salt—but requires a steady hand for piping churros shapes.
  • Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy.
  • Serve immediately for the best texture paired with warm chocolate.

Bringing the Flavors of Spain to Your Holiday Table

Each of these recipes showcases the warmth and spirit of Spanish holiday cooking. From turrón’s sweet crunch to the comforting embrace of Escudella, these dishes invite you to celebrate family, tradition, and the joy of sharing good food.

When preparing Spanish holiday dishes, the magic lies not only in the ingredients but also in the gathering itself—standing around the kitchen, exchanging stories and laughter, savoring every bite and moment.

Whether you choose to make one dish or all twelve, these recipes are your gateway to an unforgettable Spanish Christmas feast.

Bonus: Tips for Shopping and Cooking Spanish Holiday Ingredients

  • Olive Oil: Spain produces some of the finest extra virgin olive oils. Keep a good quality bottle on hand.
  • Paprika: Sweet and smoked Spanish paprika adds depth. It’s worth investing in authentic varieties.
  • Sea Salt: Coarse sea salt, like Flor de Sal, enhances finishing touches.
  • Almonds: Marcona almonds differ in flavor and texture from regular almonds; they’re sweeter, softer, and buttery.
  • Saffron: A pinch of saffron threads can elevate dishes like rice or stews.
  • Fresh Seafood: If possible, get fresh octopus and cod from reliable sources for best results.

Celebrate the season with these delightful tastes of Spain and toast to family, friendship, and timeless traditions. ¡Felices Fiestas!
