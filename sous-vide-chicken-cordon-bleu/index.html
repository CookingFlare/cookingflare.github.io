Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu


Chicken Cordon Bleu is one of those timeless dishes that brings together a delicious combination of flavors: tender chicken, savory ham, and creamy cheese. When prepared correctly, this dish can be an absolute showstopper, but achieving the perfect balance of textures and flavors can be a challenge. That’s where the sous vide method comes in. Sous vide cooking allows you to precisely control the temperature, ensuring that the chicken remains juicy and tender while the cheese melts to perfection. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make the best Chicken Cordon Bleu using sous vide, turning a classic into a next-level culinary experience.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide (pronounced soo-veed) is a French term that translates to “under vacuum.” It’s a cooking method where food is vacuum-sealed in a bag and then slowly cooked in a water bath at a precise, low temperature. This method allows for perfectly cooked food that is juicy, tender, and flavorful. Because sous vide cooking ensures that food is cooked to the ideal doneness throughout, it eliminates the risk of overcooking, which is particularly important when preparing dishes like Chicken Cordon Bleu.

Why Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu?

Traditionally, Chicken Cordon Bleu is made by pounding chicken breasts thin, stuffing them with ham and cheese, then breading and frying them to achieve a crispy exterior. While this method is delicious, it can sometimes result in dry chicken or unevenly melted cheese. Sous vide cooking, on the other hand, ensures that the chicken is cooked evenly, and the cheese remains perfectly melted inside the chicken. Plus, the sous vide method locks in all the flavors, making every bite melt-in-your-mouth tender.

By using sous vide, you can create a chicken cordon bleu that’s more flavorful, more tender, and more impressive without the stress of worrying about overcooked chicken or under-melted cheese.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu

To make this sous vide version of Chicken Cordon Bleu, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 slices of ham (thinly sliced, preferably a good-quality deli ham)
  • 4 slices of Swiss cheese (Gruyère or Emmental are also excellent options)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard (optional, for an extra layer of flavor)
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs (for breading)
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter (for pan-frying, if desired)

For the breading, we’ll create a standard flour-egg-breadcrumb setup, ensuring a crunchy exterior once the chicken is finished cooking sous vide.

Equipment Needed for Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu

To get the most out of this sous vide chicken recipe, you’ll need a few essential kitchen tools:

  1. Sous Vide Precision Cooker: This is the most important piece of equipment. It maintains the precise water temperature needed for sous vide cooking.
  2. Vacuum Sealer: To ensure that your chicken breasts cook properly, you’ll need to vacuum-seal them in a bag.
  3. Water Bath Container: A large container or pot to hold the water while cooking.
  4. Cast Iron Skillet or Non-Stick Pan: To finish the chicken by searing it after sous vide cooking.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu

Step 1: Prepping the Chicken

Start by preparing the chicken breasts. If they are too thick, use a meat mallet to gently pound them out to about 1/2-inch thickness. This ensures that the chicken cooks evenly.

Once the chicken is properly prepared, season each breast generously with salt and pepper on both sides. If you want to add a bit of tanginess to the flavor profile, spread a small amount of Dijon mustard over the top of each chicken breast. This adds a bit of sharpness that complements the richness of the ham and cheese.

Step 2: Assembling the Chicken Cordon Bleu

Now comes the fun part – assembling the chicken. Place one slice of ham and one slice of cheese on top of each chicken breast. Gently fold the sides of the chicken over the filling to create a pocket, then roll the chicken up tightly. You can secure the chicken with toothpicks or simply use the natural fold to hold it in place.

Next, vacuum-seal each chicken roll individually. Be sure to remove as much air as possible to ensure even cooking.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

Fill your water bath container with water and set the sous vide precision cooker to 140°F (60°C). This temperature will cook the chicken perfectly without drying it out. Once the water has reached the desired temperature, submerge the vacuum-sealed chicken breasts in the water. Ensure that they are fully submerged and that the bags are not touching each other to allow the water to circulate freely.

Cook the chicken for 1.5 to 2 hours. This cooking time is enough to achieve tender, juicy chicken with perfectly melted cheese inside. Because the sous vide method is so gentle, the chicken will not overcook, even if you leave it in the water bath a little longer.

Step 4: Preparing the Breading

While the chicken is cooking, it’s time to prepare the breading. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes:

  1. Flour – Season it lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Egg wash – Whisk together two eggs in a separate dish.
  3. Breadcrumbs – For a crispy coating, use panko breadcrumbs. You can also season the breadcrumbs with a little garlic powder or grated Parmesan cheese for extra flavor.

Once the chicken has finished cooking in the sous vide bath, remove it from the bags and carefully pat it dry with paper towels. This is an important step, as excess moisture can prevent the breading from adhering properly.

Step 5: Bread and Sear the Chicken

Now that your chicken rolls are dry, it’s time to coat them in the breadcrumb mixture. Dredge each roll in flour first, then dip it into the egg wash, and finally coat it in breadcrumbs. Ensure that each roll is evenly coated.

In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the breaded chicken rolls to the skillet. Sear the chicken for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy. Since the chicken is already cooked through from the sous vide, this step is simply to create a crispy exterior.

Step 6: Serving the Chicken Cordon Bleu

Once the chicken is golden and crispy on all sides, remove it from the skillet and let it rest for a couple of minutes. Slice each chicken roll to reveal the melted cheese and ham inside. Serve with your favorite side dishes, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Tips for Perfecting Your Sous Vide Chicken Cordon Bleu

  • Use high-quality ingredients: Since the flavors in Chicken Cordon Bleu come from the ham, cheese, and chicken itself, using quality ingredients will make a big difference in the overall taste.
  • Do not skip the patting dry step: Excess moisture can cause the breading to become soggy and prevent it from crisping up properly.
  • Flavor variations: While traditional Chicken Cordon Bleu uses Swiss cheese and ham, you can experiment with other cheeses like cheddar or gouda, and even substitute turkey or prosciutto for the ham.
  • Add herbs: For a fresh twist, sprinkle some fresh thyme or rosemary on the chicken before vacuum-sealing it.

Why Sous Vide Cooking is Ideal for Chicken Cordon Bleu

Sous vide cooking gives you unparalleled control over the doneness of your food, and this is especially helpful when making a dish like Chicken Cordon Bleu. Chicken breasts are notoriously tricky to cook perfectly; they can go from tender and juicy to dry and overcooked in a matter of minutes. With sous vide, the chicken is cooked gently and evenly, ensuring that the meat stays moist and tender while the filling remains gooey and melted. Moreover, because sous vide cooking is done at lower temperatures, it prevents the chicken from overcooking and drying out, making it a foolproof way to prepare this dish.

Conclusion

Sous vide Chicken Cordon Bleu is a simple yet elegant dish that’s perfect for special occasions or a comforting meal at home. By using the sous vide method, you’re guaranteed a juicy and tender chicken breast with perfectly melted cheese and savory ham. The combination of precise temperature control and slow cooking yields a restaurant-quality result that’s sure to impress your family and guests. Whether you’re a sous vide enthusiast or new to this cooking technique, Chicken Cordon Bleu is a fantastic recipe to try out and perfect in your kitchen.
