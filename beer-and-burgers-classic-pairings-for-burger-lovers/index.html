Beer and Burgers: Classic Pairings for Burger Lovers


Bursting with flavors, juicy burgers and ice-cold beers have always been an essential part of American cuisine. Together, they create a match made in heaven. This post showcases the riveting world of beer and burgers, unraveling the tantalizing combinations that elevate your dining experience.

Opting for a burger, one instantly recognizes the variety of styles and flavors they can choose from. Meat-lovers, vegetarians, and even health-conscious individuals are often spoilt for choice. Much like a burger, beers come in a wide array of types, intensities, and preferences, making it a daunting task to decide which burger (or toppings) to pair with which beer.

Introduction to Beer Pairings

When it comes to beer, pairing the drink with the right burger can strengthen and modify the flavor profile, transforming a simple meal into a memorable gastronomic experience. No one wants a beverage that vies for attention or dampens the taste of their burger patty. In this guide, we explore some classic beer and burger pairings that would dazzle even the most discerning diner.

Meet Your Meat: Beef Burgers

There’s no denying that beef patties are the mothership of burgers, as they are the traditional old faithfuls. Pairing the beef patty with an ale of similar character can be divine. Craft beer, particularly amber ales and IPAs (India Pale Ales), provide the perfect contrast to the juicy everyman’s protein. These beers offer a slight bitterness and a robust mouthfeel that complements the richness of the beef patties.

The Veggie Kick

For the herbivores among us, cheese-stuffed portobello mushrooms, grain-based patties, or black bean concoctions bring some eco-friendly choices to the table. With their firm textures and earthy flavors, these vegetarian patties can be expertly paired with citrusy beers or rauchbiers (smoked beers). The added zest in the beers cuts through the creaminess of the cheese while enhancing the flavors in the patty.

Hold the Lettuce, Hold the Tomato

For those stringent fans of the simple white bread and beef patty combo, white lagers are an underappreciated gem. These polarizing beers express a subtle sweetness that harmoniously mirrors the unadulterated taste of the beef patty. Taking it a notch up, pilsners and helles lagers can also highlight the natural flavors and lighten the load on the palate, allowing for more bites and ales.

Breakfast Anyone?

The ultimate fusion of flavor, breakfast burgers have broken new ground in the culinary scene. With their rich blend of eggs, bacon, and cheese, these morning meals in a bun cry out for a strong companion. Porters or stouts, with their chocolatey and ale-like characteristics, evoke the rustic charm of a cozy Sunday morning. Their roasted flavors and undertones of bitter coffee fulfill and round off the hearty breakfast burger.

The Juicy Lucy

Mexico meets America in the Juicy Lucy, oozing with melty queso and jalapeño crescendoing in spiciness. For such a daring dish, Mexican-style lagers or amber ales take center stage. These beers, with their crisp refreshing qualities, provide a spicy palate cleanser to abate the burning sensation.

Shake It Up

Milkshakes are not just for childhood; even grownups craving a liquid dessert can incorporate creative malted beverages into their culinary escapades. Chocolate milkshakes pair excellently with robust stouts, brown ales, or even Mexican-style lagers. The chocolate milkshake dessert burger takes on an entirely new dimension with the darkness of the beer.

End on a High Note

Burger pairing etiquette extends beyond the beer. Before you sink your teeth into your patty, enjoy a pre-burger brew. Giddy up and down the food-and-beverage conveyor belt—with a hoppy IPA, pale ale, wheat beer, or pilsner—an easy-sipping beer opens your palate, ensuring a flawless start to your burger trio adventure.

In conclusion, beer and burger pairings ignite a dynamite culinary journey. With infinite ways to customize the patty, the bread, the toppings, and the sidekick, extensive exploration is encouraged.
