How to Make Perfect Vegan and Vegetarian Burgers


Burgers have long been a staple of the American diet, but as plant-based eating becomes more popular, the traditional beef patty is getting a healthy and delicious upgrade. Whether you’re a full-time vegan, a vegetarian looking for more plant-based options, or simply someone who loves exploring new flavors, making your own vegan and vegetarian burgers at home is not only fun but also a great way to enjoy a wholesome, flavorful meal.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through the steps to create perfect vegan and vegetarian burgers that are satisfying, full of flavor, and easy to prepare. From the basics of selecting the right ingredients to assembling and cooking your homemade patties, we’ll cover everything you need to know to master the art of plant-based burger making.

The Basics of Vegan and Vegetarian Burgers

Before you dive into creating your burger masterpiece, it’s important to understand the difference between vegan and vegetarian burgers.

  • Vegan Burgers: These are entirely plant-based and do not contain any animal-derived ingredients, including dairy, eggs, or honey. Vegan burgers are typically made with ingredients like beans, lentils, grains, and vegetables, with the use of plant-based binders to hold the patty together.

  • Vegetarian Burgers: These burgers can include dairy products and eggs, which means they might incorporate ingredients like cheese, egg-based binders, or other non-vegan items. Vegetarian burgers can be slightly more indulgent if you’re craving a cheesy, egg-rich patty.

Essential Ingredients for Vegan and Vegetarian Burgers

The beauty of making your own burgers is that you can experiment with a variety of ingredients to suit your tastes and dietary needs. Here are some basic ingredients to consider for both vegan and vegetarian burgers:

Base Ingredients

  1. Beans and Lentils
    Beans, like black beans, chickpeas, and kidney beans, along with lentils, are the foundation of many plant-based burger patties. They provide a hearty texture, natural protein, and fiber, making them perfect for creating a solid patty. Cooked beans and lentils can be mashed or processed to form a base for your burger.

  2. Vegetables
    Vegetables add flavor, moisture, and color to your patty. You can use ingredients like mushrooms, zucchini, carrots, onions, and spinach. For a more meaty texture, mushrooms (especially portobello) are an excellent choice as they have a rich umami flavor.

  3. Grains and Starches
    Grains like quinoa, oats, and rice can help bind your patty together and provide a chewy texture. Starchy ingredients like sweet potatoes, potatoes, and even cornmeal can be used to add moisture and act as a binding agent.

  4. Nuts and Seeds
    Nuts like walnuts, almonds, or sunflower seeds add texture and protein to your burger mix. They can be chopped finely and added to the mixture for an extra crunch. Seeds such as chia or flaxseeds can also help bind the patty together while adding essential fatty acids.

Binding Ingredients

  1. Flax or Chia Egg
    To bind your vegan burgers, flaxseed or chia seeds mixed with water create a gel-like consistency that holds the patty together. The ratio is generally 1 tablespoon of flax or chia seeds mixed with 3 tablespoons of water. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes until it thickens.

  2. Breadcrumbs or Oats
    Breadcrumbs or rolled oats are great for absorbing excess moisture and helping to bind the patty. They also give the burger a more satisfying texture. You can use gluten-free breadcrumbs or oats if you’re making a gluten-free burger.

  3. Cornstarch or Arrowroot Powder
    For added binding, cornstarch or arrowroot powder can help provide structure to your burger patty, ensuring that it doesn’t fall apart when cooking.

  4. Plant-Based Yogurt or Aquafaba
    For extra moisture, you can use plant-based yogurt (such as coconut or soy yogurt) or aquafaba (the liquid from canned chickpeas). Aquafaba works as an egg replacer, offering both binding and moisture retention.

Flavoring Ingredients

  1. Spices and Herbs
    A great way to flavor your patties is by using spices and herbs. Popular choices include garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, turmeric, oregano, thyme, and chili flakes. Fresh herbs like cilantro, basil, and parsley can elevate the freshness of your patty.

  2. Soy Sauce or Tamari
    For an added umami depth, soy sauce or tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) adds savory flavor and a little saltiness to your patty mixture.

  3. Liquid Smoke or Barbecue Sauce
    Liquid smoke adds a smoky flavor to the patty, mimicking the taste of a grilled meat burger. Barbecue sauce or a tangy dressing can also add complexity to the burger.

  4. Vegan Cheese
    If you like a cheesy burger, opt for a high-quality vegan cheese. Meltable options such as cashew-based cheese, almond cheese, or coconut oil-based cheese are fantastic for giving your burger a creamy, indulgent feel.

Steps to Make the Perfect Vegan and Vegetarian Burger Patties

1. Choose Your Base

The first step in creating a plant-based burger patty is selecting a hearty base. Whether you’re using beans, lentils, or vegetables, you’ll want to mash or pulse the ingredients in a food processor until they reach a consistency that holds together but isn’t too smooth.

Example Vegan Burger Base:

  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas or black beans (mashed or processed)
  • 1/2 cup grated zucchini or mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup cooked quinoa or rice

2. Add Vegetables and Grains

Once your base is ready, add in any vegetables or grains you want. Grated carrots, finely chopped spinach, or sautéed mushrooms can be added to enhance flavor and texture. Grains like quinoa or oats will provide structure and help absorb any excess moisture from the vegetables.

Example Additions:

  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/4 cup sautéed mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup cooked quinoa or rice

3. Incorporate the Binding Agents

To ensure your patties stick together, it’s essential to add a binding ingredient. Mix in a flax or chia egg, breadcrumbs, or oats, depending on what you have on hand.

Binding Agent Suggestions:

  • 2 tbsp ground flaxseeds (mixed with 6 tbsp water) or chia seeds
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs or rolled oats
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch or arrowroot powder

4. Season Your Patties

Now comes the fun part: adding flavor! Season your mixture with spices, herbs, and flavorings. Garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and fresh herbs will give your burger a satisfying depth. Soy sauce or tamari will provide saltiness, and a dash of liquid smoke will give your patty a grilled flavor.

Suggested Seasoning:

  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • Salt and pepper to taste

5. Shape the Patties

Once your mixture is well-seasoned and properly combined, it’s time to shape the patties. With clean hands, divide the mixture into equal portions and form them into round, even patties. Aim for a thickness that’s about 1/2 inch, as this ensures they cook evenly and stay together.

6. Cook the Patties

You have a few options for cooking your burger patties:

  • Pan-frying: Heat a little oil in a skillet over medium heat. Place the patties in the skillet and cook for about 3-5 minutes on each side, until they’re golden brown and crispy.

  • Grilling: For a smoky flavor, grill the patties over medium heat for 4-5 minutes per side. Make sure the grill grates are well-oiled to prevent sticking.

  • Baking: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake the patties on a parchment-lined baking sheet for 25-30 minutes, flipping them halfway through for even cooking.

7. Assemble Your Burger

Now that your patties are cooked, it’s time to build your burger! Toast the burger buns, add a dollop of vegan mayo, mustard, or ketchup, and top with fresh veggies like lettuce, tomato, and avocado. For extra indulgence, add a slice of vegan cheese or sautéed onions.

Topping Ideas:

  • Lettuce, tomato, onion, avocado
  • Vegan mayo or mustard
  • Vegan cheese slice
  • Pickles or jalapeños
  • Sautéed mushrooms or caramelized onions

8. Serve and Enjoy

Your homemade vegan or vegetarian burgers are now ready to be enjoyed! Pair them with a side of crispy fries, sweet potato wedges, or a refreshing salad for a complete meal.

Tips for the Best Vegan and Vegetarian Burgers

  • Moisture Control: If your patty mixture is too wet, add more breadcrumbs or oats to firm it up. If it’s too dry, incorporate a bit of aquafaba or plant-based yogurt.

  • Chill the Patties: Before cooking, chilling the patties in the fridge for 20-30 minutes can help them firm up and prevent them from falling apart during cooking.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to try different spices, herbs, or even mix in other ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes, olives, or roasted garlic for a unique twist.

  • Make Ahead and Freeze: Vegan and vegetarian burger patties freeze beautifully. Make a big batch, freeze them individually, and have a quick meal ready whenever you need it.

Final Thoughts

Making perfect vegan and vegetarian burgers is an art form, but it’s also incredibly rewarding. By using simple ingredients and a little creativity, you can make plant-based burgers that are just as satisfying—if not more so—than their meat-based counterparts. Whether you’re grilling them up for a summer barbecue, preparing a family meal, or just looking for a healthy lunch, these homemade burgers will undoubtedly become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.
