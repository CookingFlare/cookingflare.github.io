Cranberry Orange Scones: Fall Brunch Treat


Scones are a quintessential part of any brunch spread, and when it comes to fall flavors, Cranberry Orange Scones are a delightful choice. The combination of tangy cranberries and zesty orange is a match made in culinary heaven, perfect for welcoming the crisp air and colorful foliage of autumn. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a cozy treat, these scones are sure to impress. Let’s dive into the recipe and learn how to whip up these delectable Fall brunch delights!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 tablespoon orange juice (for glaze)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

  3. Incorporate Butter: Add the cold butter pieces to the dry ingredients. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, egg, vanilla extract, and orange zest.

  5. Bring the Dough Together: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients along with the dried cranberries. Gently mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix.

  6. Shape the Scones: Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Pat it into a circle about 1-inch thick. Cut the circle into 8 wedges.

  7. Bake the Scones: Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until they are golden brown.

  8. Prepare the Glaze: While the scones are baking, mix the orange juice with a bit of powdered sugar to create a simple glaze.

  9. Finish and Serve: Once the scones are out of the oven and slightly cooled, drizzle the glaze over the top. Allow it to set for a few minutes before serving.

Tips for Success:

  • Cold Ingredients: Ensure your butter and cream are cold as this helps create a flaky texture in the scones.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Be gentle when mixing the dough to avoid tough scones.
  • Customize: Feel free to add nuts or white chocolate chips for extra flavor and texture.
  • Storage: These scones are best enjoyed fresh but can be stored in an airtight container for up to 2 days.

Cranberry Orange Scones are a wonderful addition to your fall baking repertoire. Serve them warm with a cup of tea or coffee for a truly delightful brunch experience. The burst of cranberries, paired with the citrusy notes of orange, creates a harmony of flavors that embodies the essence of the season. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and treat yourself to these homemade scones that are bound to become a fall favorite. Happy baking!
