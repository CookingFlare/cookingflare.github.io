Mexican Chiles en Nogada Recipe with Poblano Peppers


If you’re looking for a dish that captures the vibrant flavors and rich culinary heritage of Mexico, look no further than Chiles en Nogada. This iconic Mexican dish is a celebration of the country’s independence and a true feast for the senses. The dish consists of roasted poblano peppers filled with a flavorful mixture of meat, fruits, and spices, topped with a creamy walnut sauce and garnished with pomegranate seeds – a perfect representation of the Mexican flag with its green, white, and red colors.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup almonds, chopped
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 apple, peeled and diced
  • 1 pear, peeled and diced
  • 1 plantain, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Poblano Peppers:

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt

For the Walnut Sauce:

  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 ounces queso fresco
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground pork until browned.
  2. Add the chopped almonds, raisins, diced onion, and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is soft and translucent.
  3. Stir in the diced apple, pear, and plantain, and season with cinnamon, cloves, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the fruits are tender. Set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Poblano Peppers

  1. Preheat the broiler in your oven.
  2. Place the poblano peppers on a baking sheet and broil, turning occasionally, until the skins are charred and blistered. This will take about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the peppers from the oven and place them in a plastic bag to steam for 10 minutes. This will make the skins easier to peel.
  4. Once cooled, carefully peel the skins off the peppers. Make a vertical slit down the side of each pepper and carefully remove the seeds and membranes, leaving the stems intact.

Step 3: Fill the Peppers

  1. Stuff each pepper with the prepared filling, being careful not to overfill.
  2. In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Carefully place the stuffed peppers in the skillet and fry until they are golden brown on all sides. Remove and place on a paper towel to drain excess oil.

Step 4: Prepare the Walnut Sauce

  1. In a blender, combine the walnuts, milk, queso fresco, and sugar. Blend until smooth.
  2. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and gently fold in the sour cream. Season with salt to taste.

Step 5: Assemble and Serve

  1. Place the fried stuffed peppers on a serving platter.
  2. Generously pour the walnut sauce over the peppers, allowing it to cascade down the sides.
  3. Sprinkle the pomegranate seeds and chopped parsley over the top for a burst of color and freshness.

Chiles en Nogada is traditionally served at room temperature, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully. This dish is a true labor of love, with each step adding layers of complexity and depth to the final presentation. The combination of savory, sweet, and creamy elements makes it a delightful experience for anyone fortunate enough to enjoy it.

Whether you’re celebrating Mexican Independence Day or simply craving a taste of Mexico, Chiles en Nogada with Poblano Peppers is a dish that will transport you to the heart of this vibrant and diverse cuisine. With its rich history and unforgettable flavors, this recipe is sure to become a treasured addition to your culinary repertoire.
