Old-Fashioned Chicken and Rice Casserole

3 min · 535 words

If there’s one dish that never goes out of style, it’s the old-fashioned chicken and rice casserole. This comforting and hearty meal has been a staple in many households for generations, and for good reason. It’s a versatile dish that can be customized to suit different tastes, and it’s perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers throughout the week. In this recipe, I’ll share my take on the classic old-fashioned chicken and rice casserole, along with some tips for making it your own.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather our ingredients. For this old-fashioned chicken and rice casserole, you’ll need:

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup of long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1 cup of sliced carrots
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup of breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then brown them on both sides in the skillet. Once browned, remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and cook until it’s translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  4. Stir in the rice, chicken broth, milk, peas, carrots, dried thyme, paprika, and some salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Pour the rice mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly. Place the browned chicken thighs on top of the rice.

  6. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the rice is tender.

  7. While the casserole is baking, mix the grated Parmesan cheese, breadcrumbs, and melted butter in a small bowl.

  8. After the casserole has baked for 35-40 minutes, remove the foil and sprinkle the breadcrumb mixture over the top. Return the casserole to the oven and bake, uncovered, for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and crispy.

  9. Once the casserole is done, let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together beautifully.

Customization Ideas

One of the great things about old-fashioned chicken and rice casserole is that it’s highly customizable. Here are a few ideas to make it your own:

  • Cheesy Variation: Add some shredded cheddar or mozzarella cheese to the rice mixture for an extra creamy and cheesy casserole.

  • Vegetable Medley: Feel free to mix and match your favorite vegetables in this casserole. Broccoli, bell peppers, or even asparagus can be delightful additions.

  • Spice it Up: If you like a bit of heat, consider adding some diced jalapenos or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes to the rice mixture.

  • Herb Infusion: Experiment with different herbs to infuse the dish with varying flavors. Rosemary, oregano, or even a touch of sage can add a unique twist.

Conclusion

Old-fashioned chicken and rice casserole is a timeless dish that never fails to satisfy. Its simple yet comforting flavors make it a go-to recipe for busy weeknights or leisurely weekend meals. Whether you stick to the classic version or put your own spin on it, this casserole is sure to become a favorite in your household. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to enjoy the heartwarming goodness of this beloved classic.
