Quick Asian-Inspired Recipes for Dinner


Looking to add some excitement to your weeknight dinner routine? Asian-inspired dishes are not only delicious but also quick and easy to prepare. From flavorful stir-fries to aromatic curries, the world of Asian cuisine offers a plethora of options to tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll explore some quick and easy Asian-inspired recipes that are perfect for a speedy and satisfying dinner.

1. Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, snap peas, carrots, broccoli)
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned and cooked through.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and garlic to the skillet and stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce and soy sauce over the chicken and vegetables, tossing to coat evenly.
  5. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  6. Serve the teriyaki chicken stir-fry over steamed rice and garnish with sliced green onions, if desired.

2. Shrimp Pad Thai

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until pink and opaque.
  4. Push the shrimp to one side of the skillet and pour the beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs until just set.
  5. Add the cooked rice noodles, bean sprouts, fish sauce, tamarind paste, and brown sugar to the skillet, tossing to combine.
  6. Continue to stir-fry for a few more minutes until everything is heated through.
  7. Serve the shrimp pad Thai garnished with chopped peanuts and lime wedges.

3. Beef and Broccoli

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and cornstarch.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over high heat.
  3. Add the sliced flank steak to the skillet and cook until browned.
  4. Toss in the broccoli florets and continue to stir-fry for a few minutes until the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  5. Pour the sauce over the beef and broccoli, tossing to coat evenly.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats the beef and broccoli.
  7. Serve the beef and broccoli over steamed rice.

Conclusion

These quick Asian-inspired recipes are perfect for those busy evenings when you want to whip up something delicious and satisfying without spending hours in the kitchen. Whether you’re a fan of chicken, shrimp, or beef, there’s something for everyone in the world of Asian cuisine. So, the next time you’re craving something with a touch of Asian flair, give these recipes a try and transport your taste buds to the vibrant and flavorful world of Asian cooking!
