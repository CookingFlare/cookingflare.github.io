Paleo Beef and Broccoli Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


If you’re following a paleo lifestyle or just looking to incorporate more grain-free options into your diet, beef and broccoli is a classic dish that can easily be adapted to fit your dietary preferences. In this post, we’ll explore five delicious and grain-free variations of this beloved recipe, each offering its own unique twist and flavor profile. From savory marinades to creative substitutions for traditional ingredients, these recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings for a hearty, wholesome meal.

1. Classic Paleo Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb grass-fed beef, thinly sliced
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp arrowroot powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together coconut aminos, minced garlic, and arrowroot powder to create the marinade.
  2. Add the sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring it’s coated evenly, and let it sit for 10 minutes.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef and cook until browned.
  4. Once the beef is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set it aside.
  5. In the same skillet, add the broccoli florets and sauté until tender-crisp.
  6. Return the beef to the skillet with the broccoli, toss everything together, and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Garnish with sesame seeds if desired and serve hot.

This classic stir-fry recipe stays true to the traditional flavors of beef and broccoli while omitting grains and soy-based ingredients.

2. Slow Cooker Paleo Beef and Broccoli

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 4 hours | Total Time: 4 hours 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs grass-fed beef stew meat, cubed
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp arrowroot powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the beef stew meat, sliced onion, and minced garlic in a slow cooker.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together beef broth, coconut aminos, honey, apple cider vinegar, grated ginger, and arrowroot powder to create the sauce.
  3. Pour the sauce over the beef in the slow cooker, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.
  4. Cover and cook on low for 4 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  5. Add the broccoli florets to the slow cooker during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.

This slow cooker adaptation of beef and broccoli is perfect for busy days when you want a hearty meal waiting for you at dinnertime.

3. Paleo Beef and Broccoli Cauliflower Rice Bowl

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb grass-fed ground beef
  • 4 cups riced cauliflower
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: sliced avocado for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned.
  2. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the skillet, and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  3. Push the beef to one side of the skillet and add the riced cauliflower to the empty space. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. In the same skillet, add the broccoli florets, coconut aminos, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes. Cook until the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  5. Combine the beef, cauliflower rice, and broccoli in the skillet, tossing everything together.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the beef and broccoli mixture in bowls, garnishing with sliced avocado if desired.

This cauliflower rice bowl variation offers a low-carb alternative to traditional rice, making it an excellent choice for those following a grain-free diet.
