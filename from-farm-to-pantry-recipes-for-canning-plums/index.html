From Farm to Pantry: Recipes for Canning Plums


Welcome to our guide on canning plums! If you’ve found yourself with an abundance of plums from your farm or local orchard, you’re in for a treat. Canning plums is a wonderful way to preserve their delicious flavor and enjoy them throughout the year. In this blog post, we’ll explore the process of canning plums, including step-by-step instructions and a variety of recipes to make the most of this delightful fruit.

Why Can Plums?

Plums are not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients. Canning plums allows you to capture their natural sweetness and tanginess, preserving them for the months ahead. Whether you’re a fan of sweet spreads or savory chutneys, canned plums can be a versatile addition to your pantry. Plus, knowing exactly where your plums come from – whether it’s your own farm or a local source – adds a special touch to your homemade goodies.

Selecting the Right Plums

Before we dive into the canning process, it’s crucial to choose the right plums. Look for plump, ripe, and slightly firm plums. The varieties of plums suitable for canning include Italian plums, Santa Rosa plums, and Damson plums. These varieties hold their shape well during the canning process and offer a delightful flavor that shines through in the finished products.

Canning Plums: Step by Step

Step 1: Prepare Your Plums

Start by washing your plums thoroughly. Remove any stems and blemishes, and then cut the plums in half, discarding the pits. You can choose to leave the skins on for added texture and color, or peel them if you prefer a smoother consistency.

Step 2: Syrup or No Syrup?

There are two popular methods for canning plums: with syrup or without. If you prefer a lighter touch, you can opt for canning plums without syrup. However, for a touch of sweetness, a light syrup made from sugar and water can enhance the plums’ natural flavor.

Step 3: Pack the Jars

Once your plums are prepared, pack them tightly into sterilized canning jars, leaving about half an inch of headspace at the top. If you’re using syrup, pour it over the plums, ensuring they are completely covered.

Step 4: Process the Jars

Process the jars in a boiling water bath for the specified time according to your recipe. This will seal the jars and ensure their contents are safe for long-term storage.

Step 5: Enjoy Your Canned Plums

After the jars have cooled and the seals have been checked, your canned plums are ready to be enjoyed! They can be used in a variety of recipes, from desserts and breakfast dishes to savory accompaniments for meat and cheese platters.

Recipes for Canned Plums

1. Spiced Plum Jam

This delightful jam combines the sweetness of plums with warm spices like cinnamon and cloves. Spread it on toast, swirl it into yogurt, or use it as a glaze for meats.

2. Plum Chutney

A savory and tangy option, plum chutney pairs well with cheese and meats. It’s a fantastic addition to a charcuterie board or as a condiment for sandwiches.

3. Canned Plums in Syrup

For a simple yet versatile option, can plums in a light syrup. These plums can be used in pies, tarts, or enjoyed on their own with a dollop of whipped cream.

Conclusion

Canning plums is a rewarding and practical way to make the most of this seasonal fruit. With a bit of effort, you can savor the taste of summer plums all year round. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or new to the process, we hope this guide has inspired you to try your hand at preserving plums from farm to pantry. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to savor the fruits of your labor!

We hope you found this guide helpful and inspiring. Happy canning!
