Deliciously Tart Key Lime Pie: A Taste of the Tropics


Key lime pie is a classic dessert that transports you to a tropical paradise with its tangy and refreshing flavors. Made from the juice of key limes, this pie is known for its distinctive tartness that balances perfectly with the sweetness of the creamy filling. If you’re looking for a dessert that will wow your taste buds and transport you to a beachside oasis, look no further than this deliciously tart key lime pie recipe.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup melted unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar

For the filling:

  • 1 ½ cups sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup key lime juice
  • 4 large egg yolks

For the whipped cream:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated with the butter and sugar.

  3. Press the crumb mixture into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish, forming an even crust. Use the back of a spoon or the bottom of a measuring cup to press the mixture firmly.

  4. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 10 minutes until it turns golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, key lime juice, and egg yolks until well combined. The mixture should be smooth and creamy.

  6. Pour the filling into the cooled graham cracker crust, spreading it evenly.

  7. Bake the pie in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. The filling should be set but still slightly jiggly in the center.

  8. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until chilled and set.

  9. To make the whipped cream, beat the heavy cream in a chilled bowl until soft peaks form. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue beating until stiff peaks form.

  10. Top the chilled key lime pie with the whipped cream, spreading it evenly over the filling.

  11. Serve and enjoy! The combination of the tangy key lime filling and the light and fluffy whipped cream is a match made in dessert heaven.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra tropical twist, try adding a tablespoon of freshly grated key lime zest to the filling mixture.

  • If you can’t find key limes, regular limes can be used as a substitute. However, keep in mind that the flavor may be slightly different.

  • To make individual key lime tartlets, use a muffin tin and line each cup with a small circle of graham cracker crust. Fill with the key lime filling and bake for a shorter time.

  • If you prefer a more pronounced tartness, increase the amount of key lime juice to your liking.

  • This key lime pie can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will intensify over time, making it even more delicious.

So there you have it – a recipe for a deliciously tart key lime pie that will transport you to a tropical paradise with every bite. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply want to treat yourself to a taste of the tropics, this pie is sure to impress. Give it a try and indulge in the tangy and creamy flavors that make key lime pie a beloved dessert around the world.
