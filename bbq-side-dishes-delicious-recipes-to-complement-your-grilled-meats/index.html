BBQ Side Dishes: Delicious Recipes to Complement Your Grilled Meats


When it comes to BBQ, the main event is often the star of the show - the grilled meats. But let’s be real, a great BBQ isn’t just about the main course. The side dishes are just as important, if not more so. After all, what’s a BBQ without some crispy, crunchy, flavorful sides to complement your grilled delights? In this post, we’ll explore some of the most delicious BBQ side dishes that are sure to elevate your next BBQ to the next level.

Classic Coleslaw

Coleslaw is a BBQ staple, and for good reason. It’s easy to make, refreshing, and pairs perfectly with grilled meats. This classic recipe is a simple one, requiring just a few ingredients: shredded cabbage, mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper.

Combine the shredded cabbage, mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix until the cabbage is coated with the dressing, then refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Baked Beans

Baked beans are another BBQ classic, and for good reason. They’re easy to make, flavorful, and pair perfectly with grilled meats. This recipe is a simple one, requiring just a few ingredients: canned beans, tomato sauce, brown sugar, mustard, vinegar, and spices.

Preheat your oven to 275°F (135°C). In a large bowl, combine the canned beans, tomato sauce, brown sugar, mustard, vinegar, and spices. Mix until the beans are well coated with the sauce, then transfer the mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 6-8 hours, or until the beans are tender and caramelized. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Grilled Corn on the Cob

Grilled corn on the cob is a summer staple, and for good reason. It’s easy to make, flavorful, and pairs perfectly with grilled meats. This recipe is a simple one, requiring just a few ingredients: corn on the cob, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, combine the corn on the cob, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss until the corn is well coated with the oil and seasonings, then place the corn on the grill. Cook for 10-15 minutes, turning frequently, or until the corn is lightly charred and tender. Serve hot, slathered with butter and sprinkled with salt and pepper if desired.

Potato Salad

Potato salad is a BBQ classic, and for good reason. It’s easy to make, flavorful, and pairs perfectly with grilled meats. This recipe is a simple one, requiring just a few ingredients: boiled potatoes, mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and spices.

Boil the potatoes until they’re tender, then drain and let them cool. In a large bowl, combine the cooled potatoes, mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and spices. Mix until the potatoes are well coated with the dressing, then refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Garlic and Herb Roasted Carrots

Roasted carrots are a delicious and easy side dish to make, and they pair perfectly with grilled meats. This recipe is a simple one, requiring just a few ingredients: carrots, olive oil, garlic, and herbs.

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, combine the carrots, olive oil, garlic, and herbs. Toss until the carrots are well coated with the oil and seasonings, then spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the carrots are tender and caramelized. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

BBQ Side Dishes: Conclusion

These BBQ side dishes are sure to elevate your next BBQ to the next level. Whether you’re looking for a classic coleslaw, a sweet and smoky baked beans, a grilled corn on the cob, a creamy potato salad, or a roasted carrot side dish, we’ve got you covered. So why wait? Fire up the grill, and get ready to impress your friends and family with these delicious BBQ side dishes.
