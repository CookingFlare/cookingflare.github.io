Classic Spaghetti Bolognese: A Hearty and Flavorful Pasta Dish


There’s nothing quite like a comforting plate of classic spaghetti bolognese. This hearty and flavorful pasta dish has been a favorite in households around the world for generations. The rich, meaty sauce combined with al dente spaghetti creates a perfect harmony of flavors and textures that is hard to resist. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the origins of this beloved dish, explore the key ingredients that make it so delicious, and walk you through a step-by-step recipe to create your own mouthwatering spaghetti bolognese at home.

The Origins of Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti bolognese, also known as spaghetti alla bolognese, hails from the northern Italian city of Bologna. While it has become a staple in Italian-American cuisine, the authentic version of this dish is quite different from what is commonly served in many restaurants outside of Italy. Traditional spaghetti bolognese in Bologna features a rich, slow-cooked meat sauce that is typically served with tagliatelle, a broad, flat pasta.

Key Ingredients

The key to a delicious spaghetti bolognese lies in the quality and combination of ingredients. Ground beef, onions, carrots, and celery form the flavor base of the sauce, while tomatoes, tomato paste, and a splash of red wine add depth and richness. Herbs such as oregano, basil, and bay leaves infuse the sauce with aromatic notes, and a touch of milk rounds out the flavors, creating a luscious and creamy texture. Of course, a generous grating of Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese to finish off the dish is essential for that perfect umami kick.

Cooking Spaghetti Bolognese at Home

Now, let’s get cooking! Follow this step-by-step recipe to create a mouthwatering pot of spaghetti bolognese in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup (240ml) red wine
  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound (450g) spaghetti
  • Grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the ground beef over medium heat until fully cooked. Remove any excess fat from the pot.

  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery to the pot, and cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste, stirring to combine with the meat and vegetables. Add the red wine, and allow the mixture to simmer for a few minutes to cook off the alcohol.

  4. Stir in the milk, oregano, basil, and bay leaves. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Lower the heat, cover the pot, and let the sauce simmer gently for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally.

  5. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water before draining the spaghetti.

  6. Once the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together, remove the bay leaves and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Toss the cooked spaghetti in the sauce, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce and help it adhere to the pasta.

  7. Serve the spaghetti bolognese in individual bowls, topping each portion with a generous amount of grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese.

In Conclusion

Classic spaghetti bolognese is a timeless dish that never fails to satisfy. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this recipe is sure to impress. The slow simmering of the sauce allows the flavors to develop and intensify, resulting in a truly irresistible dish that captures the essence of Italian comfort food. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create your own unforgettable spaghetti bolognese at home. Buon appetito!
