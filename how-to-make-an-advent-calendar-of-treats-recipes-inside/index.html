How to Make an Advent Calendar of Treats: Recipes Inside


The holiday season is a time for joy, celebration, and, of course, delicious treats! One delightful way to count down the days until Christmas is by creating an Advent calendar filled with homemade goodies. This blog post will guide you through the process of making your own Advent calendar of treats, complete with a variety of recipes that will tantalize your taste buds and bring festive cheer to your home.

What is an Advent Calendar?

An Advent calendar is a special calendar used to count the days of Advent in anticipation of Christmas. Traditionally, these calendars have small doors or windows that open to reveal a treat or a message for each day leading up to Christmas. In this post, we’ll focus on creating a delicious Advent calendar filled with homemade treats that you can enjoy with family and friends.

Choosing Your Calendar Design

Before diving into the recipes, it’s essential to decide on the design of your Advent calendar. Here are a few ideas to inspire you:

  • Traditional Paper Calendar: Create a paper calendar with numbered doors that can be opened each day. You can decorate it with festive colors and designs.
  • Gift Bags: Use small gift bags or boxes, each labeled with a number. Fill each bag with a different treat.
  • Mason Jars: For a rustic look, use mason jars. Label each jar with a number and fill them with various treats.
  • Hanging Pockets: Sew or craft small pockets that can be hung on a wall or a string. Each pocket can hold a different treat.

Once you’ve chosen your design, it’s time to fill it with delicious recipes!

Treat Recipes for Your Advent Calendar

1. Chocolate-Covered Pretzels

Ingredients:

  • 1 bag of pretzel rods
  • 1 cup of chocolate chips (milk, dark, or white)
  • Sprinkles or crushed nuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  2. Dip each pretzel rod into the melted chocolate, allowing excess to drip off.
  3. Place the dipped pretzels on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  4. While the chocolate is still wet, sprinkle with toppings if desired.
  5. Let the pretzels cool until the chocolate hardens.

2. Mini Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, cream together butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add molasses and egg, mixing well.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until combined.
  4. Chill the dough for at least 1 hour.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough and cut into desired shapes.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Let cool before decorating.

3. Peppermint Bark

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • 12 ounces white chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crushed peppermint candies

Instructions:

  1. Melt the dark chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl, stirring until smooth.
  2. Spread the melted chocolate onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Melt the white chocolate and pour it over the dark chocolate, swirling with a knife to create a marbled effect.
  4. Sprinkle crushed peppermint candies on top.
  5. Refrigerate until set, then break into pieces.

4. Spiced Nuts

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed nuts (almonds, pecans, walnuts)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix the nuts with olive oil, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, and maple syrup.
  3. Spread the nuts on a baking sheet and roast for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Let cool before packaging.

5. Hot Cocoa Mix

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup powdered milk
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • Mini marshmallows (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix cocoa powder, powdered sugar, and powdered milk until well combined.
  2. Add chocolate chips and stir.
  3. Package the mix in small jars or bags, and include instructions for making hot cocoa: “Mix 1/4 cup of cocoa mix with 1 cup of hot water or milk.”

6. Fruit and Nut Energy Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup nut butter (peanut, almond, or cashew)
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup chopped dried fruit (dates, apricots, cranberries)
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine all ingredients and mix until well combined.
  2. Roll the mixture into small balls and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to firm up before packaging.

7. Sugar Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, baking soda, and baking powder.
  3. In another bowl, cream together butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in egg and vanilla.
  4. Gradually blend in the dry ingredients.
  5. Roll rounded teaspoonfuls of dough into balls, and place them on ungreased cookie sheets.
  6. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes. Let cool before decorating.

Assembling Your Advent Calendar

Once you’ve prepared your treats, it’s time to assemble your Advent calendar. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Fill Each Compartment: Place one treat in each compartment of your calendar. If you’re using bags or jars, label them with the corresponding day.
  2. Decorate: Add festive decorations to your calendar. You can use ribbons, stickers, or even hand-drawn designs to make it visually appealing.
  3. Display: Find a prominent place in your home to display your Advent calendar. This could be on a mantel, kitchen counter, or even hanging on a wall.

Tips for a Successful Advent Calendar

  • Plan Ahead: Start preparing your treats early to avoid last-minute stress. Many of these recipes can be made in advance and stored.
  • Mix and Match: Feel free to swap out recipes based on your preferences or dietary restrictions. Consider including gluten-free or vegan options.
  • Get Creative: Personalize your calendar with family traditions or favorite flavors. You can even include small gifts or notes along with the treats.
  • Involve the Family: Making an Advent calendar can be a fun family activity. Get everyone involved in the baking and decorating process.

Conclusion

Creating an Advent calendar filled with homemade treats is a wonderful way to celebrate the holiday season. Not only does it provide a daily dose of sweetness, but it also brings family and friends together in the spirit of giving and joy. With these recipes and tips, you can craft a delightful countdown to Christmas that will be cherished for years to come. Enjoy the process, and happy holidays!
