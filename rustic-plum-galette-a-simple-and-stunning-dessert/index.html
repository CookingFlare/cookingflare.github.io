Rustic Plum Galette: A Simple and Stunning Dessert


There’s something undeniably charming about a rustic plum galette. With its flaky, buttery crust and juicy, tangy plum filling, this dessert captures the essence of home baking at its finest. It’s a perfect balance between simplicity and elegance, making it an ideal choice for both casual family dinners and more sophisticated gatherings. In this post, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a mouthwatering rustic plum galette that will impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6-8 ripe plums, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sugar (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

Start by making the dough for the galette crust. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and granulated sugar. Add the chilled butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough begins to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough; it should be slightly crumbly but hold together when pressed.

Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough ensures that the butter remains cold, resulting in a flakier crust.

Step 2: Preparing the Filling

While the dough is chilling, prepare the plum filling. In a large bowl, toss the sliced plums with granulated sugar, lemon juice, vanilla extract, cornstarch, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. The combination of these ingredients will enhance the natural sweetness and flavor of the plums while thickening the juices to prevent a soggy crust.

Let the plum mixture sit for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the juices to release. This step is crucial for achieving a perfectly balanced filling.

Step 3: Assembling the Galette

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a rough circle, approximately 12 inches in diameter. Don’t worry if the edges are uneven; the beauty of a rustic galette lies in its imperfect charm.

Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the prepared baking sheet. Spoon the plum filling onto the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border around the edges. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pleating as needed to create a rustic, free-form tart. This process doesn’t need to be precise; the goal is to encase the fruit while maintaining an open center.

Brush the folded edges of the dough with the beaten egg. This will give the crust a beautiful golden color as it bakes. If desired, sprinkle the edges with coarse sugar for added texture and sweetness.

Step 4: Baking the Galette

Bake the galette in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly and fragrant. The plums should be tender, and the juices should have thickened.

Once baked, remove the galette from the oven and let it cool on the baking sheet for about 10 minutes. This allows the filling to set slightly, making it easier to slice and serve.

Step 5: Serving the Galette

Transfer the cooled galette to a serving platter or cutting board. Serve it warm or at room temperature, either on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The contrast between the warm, juicy plums and the cool, creamy accompaniment creates a delightful sensory experience.

Tips and Variations

  • Choosing Plums: Select ripe, firm plums for the best flavor and texture. Varieties such as Santa Rosa, Black Amber, or Italian prune plums work particularly well in this recipe.
  • Make-Ahead Option: The dough can be prepared up to two days in advance and stored in the refrigerator. You can also freeze the dough for up to a month; just thaw it in the refrigerator before using.
  • Flavor Enhancements: Feel free to experiment with additional spices or flavorings. A touch of almond extract or a sprinkle of cardamom can add an interesting twist to the filling.
  • Alternative Fruits: While plums are the star of this galette, you can substitute them with other seasonal fruits like peaches, apricots, or berries. Adjust the sugar and cornstarch quantities based on the fruit’s sweetness and juiciness.

Conclusion

A rustic plum galette is more than just a dessert; it’s a celebration of simple ingredients and traditional baking techniques. With its flaky crust and luscious filling, this galette embodies the essence of comfort food while maintaining a touch of sophistication. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe offers a rewarding and delicious way to showcase the beauty of fresh, seasonal plums.

Try making this rustic plum galette for your next gathering, and watch as it disappears quickly, leaving smiles and satisfied palates in its wake. Happy baking!
