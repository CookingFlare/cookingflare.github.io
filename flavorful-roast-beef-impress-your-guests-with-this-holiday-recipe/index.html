Flavorful Roast Beef: Impress Your Guests with This Holiday Recipe


As the holiday season approaches, many of us are scrambling to come up with a show-stopping centerpiece for our gatherings. Look no further than a perfectly roasted beef, packed with flavor and sure to impress your guests. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of roasting a tender and juicy roast beef, complete with a rich au jus that will have everyone begging for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pound prime rib roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)

Instructions

To begin, preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). While the oven is warming up, season the roast beef with salt and black pepper, making sure to coat the entire surface evenly. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Rub the garlic-herb mixture all over the roast beef, making sure to get it into all the nooks and crannies.

Next, heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the roast beef on all sides until it’s nicely browned, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the roast beef from the pot and set it aside.

Add the bay leaf to the pot and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant. Then, add the beef broth and red wine (if using) to the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Bring the liquid to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, until it’s reduced by about half.

Return the roast beef to the pot and cover it with a lid. Transfer the pot to the preheated oven and roast the beef for 15 minutes per pound, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature of the beef. For medium-rare, the internal temperature should be around 130°F (54°C), while medium should be around 140°F (60°C).

Once the roast beef is cooked to your liking, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes. During this time, the juices will redistribute and the beef will retain its tenderness.

To serve, slice the roast beef thinly against the grain and serve it with the rich au jus spooned over the top. You can also serve it with your favorite sides, such as roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor to your roast beef, rub it with a mixture of mustard and herbs before roasting.
  • If you prefer a more tender roast beef, cook it to an internal temperature of 120°F (49°C) and then finish it with a few minutes of high heat in the oven to sear the outside.
  • To make the au jus even richer, add a few tablespoons of butter or cream to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  • Consider serving the roast beef with a horseradish sauce or a drizzle of truffle oil for added depth of flavor.

With this recipe, you’ll be sure to impress your guests with a show-stopping centerpiece that’s both flavorful and tender. So why not give it a try this holiday season and make it a meal to remember?
