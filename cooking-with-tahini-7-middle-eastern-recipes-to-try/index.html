Cooking with Tahini: 7 Middle Eastern Recipes to Try


Tahini, a gorgeous creamy paste made from ground sesame seeds, has its roots deep in Middle Eastern cuisine. Its nutty taste, smooth texture, and versatility make it a favorite ingredient for chefs and home cooks alike. Whether you’re drizzling it over roasted vegetables or crafting classic dips, tahini adds a unique depth of flavor to any dish. Here, we dive into seven Middle Eastern recipes where tahini shines as the star, transforming simple ingredients into culinary delights. Let’s explore how you can bring the flavors of the Middle East into your kitchen!

1. Classic Tahini Sauce

Tahini sauce is a staple across Middle Eastern cuisine. It’s a multipurpose condiment that pairs well with grilled meats, falafel, roasted veggies, or even as a tangy salad dressing. This easy-to-make recipe will ensure you always have the perfect accompaniment at hand.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup tahini
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • ⅓ cup water (adjust to desired consistency)
  • Salt to taste
  • A pinch of paprika or fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk the tahini to make it creamy.
  2. Add the minced garlic, lemon juice, cumin, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Gradually add water, whisking continuously, until you reach your desired consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice or salt as needed.
  5. Garnish with paprika or parsley before serving.

This basic tahini sauce can elevate simple dishes with its tangy, creamy profile. Plus, it’s super easy to customize. Want it spicy? Add a dash of cayenne or chili flakes!

2. Hummus with a Tahini Twist

No Middle Eastern spread is complete without hummus. The magic of this dish lies in its simplicity, and tahini is an essential element that gives hummus its signature richness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas (or one 15-oz can, drained and rinsed)
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2-3 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Smoked paprika and olive oil for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the chickpeas and garlic to create a coarse texture.
  2. Add tahini, lemon juice, and cumin, blending until everything is well-incorporated.
  3. Stream in olive oil while the processor runs, making the mixture smooth.
  4. Add water, tablespoon by tablespoon, to achieve the consistency you desire.
  5. Season with salt, blend again, and taste to adjust flavors.
  6. Serve in a shallow bowl, drizzled with olive oil and a sprinkle of paprika.

Homemade hummus is endlessly forgiving—add roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, or herbs for unique takes on this classic dip.

3. Baba Ghanoush with Tahini

Baba ghanoush is a smoky, creamy eggplant dip that relies heavily on tahini for its nutty base. Originating from the Levant, it’s a crowd-pleaser loaded with subtle layers of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Pomegranate seeds and parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and roast the eggplants whole until the skins are charred and the flesh is tender (about 40-50 minutes).
  2. Let the eggplants cool, then peel off the skins.
  3. Mash the eggplant flesh in a bowl or blend for a smoother texture.
  4. Mix in tahini, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Top with pomegranate seeds or parsley for an extra touch of freshness and color.

Baba ghanoush is best served with warm pita bread or colorful vegetable crudités.

4. Tahini Drizzled Falafel

Falafel might be the world’s most beloved street food. These crispy chickpea balls are even better when paired with a creamy tahini drizzle.

Ingredients for Falafel:

  • 1 ½ cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • ½ cup parsley
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Tahini Drizzle:

  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 1-2 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Blend soaked chickpeas with onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, spices, baking soda, and seasonings in a food processor until the mixture is coarse but holds together.
  2. Shape into small balls or patties.
  3. Heat oil in a deep skillet and fry the falafel until golden brown and crispy.
  4. For the tahini drizzle, whisk together the tahini, water, lemon juice, and salt until smooth.
  5. Serve falafel hot, topped with the fragrant tahini sauce.

This is a hands-down favorite for lunch or dinner that’s impressively satisfying.

5. Roasted Cauliflower with Tahini Dressing

Who knew vegetables could taste this good? Roasted cauliflower pairs wonderfully with a zesty tahini dressing for a nourishing and flavorful side or main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp Greek yogurt (optional)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss cauliflower florets with olive oil, turmeric, paprika, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Spread the florets on a baking sheet and roast for about 25-30 minutes or until golden and tender.
  4. In a bowl, whisk tahini, yogurt (if using), lemon juice, water, garlic, and salt until smooth.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the roasted cauliflower just before serving.

This dish is perfect as a side or a vegetarian main course.

6. Tahini Halva

This Middle Eastern sweet showcases tahini in its purest form, combined with sugar to create a dense, crumbly, and irresistibly nutty dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tahini
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: Pistachios or almonds for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat sugar and water in a saucepan over low-medium heat to create a syrup. Once it reaches 250°F (120°C) on a candy thermometer, remove from heat.
  2. Quickly stir in tahini and vanilla extract until fully combined.
  3. Pour into a greased pan or mold, smoothing the top.
  4. Press nuts on top, if desired, and let cool completely before cutting into slices.

Halva offers a unique and indulgent taste experience that’s perfect with coffee or tea.

7. Tahini Chocolate Cookies

Yes—you can bake with tahini! These chocolate cookies are rich, nutty, and have a chewy texture you’ll fall in love with.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup tahini
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ cup dark chocolate chunks
  • Pinch of sea salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix tahini, sugars, egg, and vanilla until creamy.
  3. Add baking soda and fold in chocolate chunks.
  4. Scoop dough onto the baking sheet, spacing enough apart for spreading.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes, until the edges are golden but the center remains soft.
  6. Once cooled, sprinkle with a touch of sea salt for balance.

These cookies are perfect for showcasing tahini’s caramel-like flavor in a surprising way.

Tahini’s versatility makes it an incredible pantry staple ripe for culinary exploration. These recipes offer just a glimpse of the creative ways you can incorporate this Middle Eastern treasure into your cooking, from savory spreads to sumptuous desserts. Try a few, experiment with flavors, and let tahini transform your dishes!
