Savory Osso Buco Recipe - Italian Slow-Cooked Comfort


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to embark on a culinary journey to Italy with a classic dish that exudes comfort and warmth – Osso Buco. This traditional Italian recipe is a true celebration of flavor, tenderness, and slow-cooked goodness. Whether you’re an experienced cook or just starting your culinary adventures, this savory Osso Buco recipe is sure to impress and delight your taste buds.

Embracing the Tradition

Osso Buco, which translates to “bone with a hole,” is a dish that hails from Milan, Italy. It’s typically made with cross-cut veal shanks that are slowly braised to perfection, resulting in meat that’s fall-off-the-bone tender and a rich, flavorful sauce. The key to this dish lies in the slow cooking process, allowing the flavors to meld together and the meat to become incredibly tender.

Ingredients for Osso Buco

Let’s start with the star of the show – the ingredients. For this Osso Buco recipe, you’ll need:

  • 4 veal shanks, about 2 inches thick
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Gremolata (optional for garnish):
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    • 1 garlic clove, minced

Cooking Instructions

Now, let’s dive into the cooking process. Follow these steps to create a delicious Osso Buco dish:

  1. Start by tying kitchen twine around the circumference of each veal shank to keep the meat from falling off the bone during cooking.
  2. Season the flour with salt and pepper in a shallow dish. Dredge the veal shanks in the seasoned flour, shaking off any excess.
  3. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the veal shanks on all sides, working in batches if necessary. This step is crucial for developing deep flavor in the dish.
  4. Once the veal shanks are nicely browned, transfer them to a plate and set aside.
  5. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables until they begin to soften, then add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  6. Deglaze the pot with the dry white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Allow the wine to reduce slightly.
  7. Add the diced tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, thyme, and rosemary to the pot, stirring to combine.
  8. Return the veal shanks to the pot, nestling them into the vegetable and liquid mixture.
  9. Cover the pot and reduce the heat to low. Allow the Osso Buco to simmer gently for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  10. While the Osso Buco is cooking, prepare the gremolata by combining the lemon zest, chopped parsley, and minced garlic in a small bowl.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the Osso Buco is ready, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. Serve the tender veal shanks on a bed of creamy risotto, mashed potatoes, or polenta, spooning the flavorful sauce over the top. Garnish with a sprinkle of gremolata for a burst of freshness and color.

Pair this hearty dish with a glass of robust red wine, and you’ve got yourself a meal fit for a special occasion or a comforting night in. The Osso Buco is a dish that brings people together, creating lasting memories around the dinner table.

In Conclusion

In the realm of Italian cuisine, Osso Buco holds a special place as a soul-warming, satisfying dish that captures the essence of slow-cooked comfort. With its rich flavors and tender meat, this recipe is a testament to the beauty of traditional cooking methods and the joy of sharing a meal with loved ones.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the aroma of Osso Buco fill your kitchen, transporting you to the heart of Italy with every savory bite. Buon appetito!
