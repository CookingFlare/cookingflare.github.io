Decadent Nut-Free Cheesecake Recipe

Are you a cheesecake lover but have to avoid nuts due to allergies or personal preference? Look no further! This decadent nut-free cheesecake recipe is here to satisfy your cravings. Creamy, luscious, and utterly indulgent, this cheesecake is a delightful treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special milestone, or simply craving a sweet and creamy dessert, this recipe is sure to impress. Ingredients For the Crust: 2 cups of graham cracker crumbs 1/3 cup of granulated sugar 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract For the Filling: 32 oz (4 packages) of cream cheese, at room temperature 1 cup of granulated sugar 4 large eggs, at room temperature 1 cup of sour cream 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Zest of 1 lemon For the Topping (Optional): Fresh berries of your choice (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries) Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Crust Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C). In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened. Press the crumb mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, using the back of a spoon to create an even layer. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool. Step 2: Making the Filling In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the sour cream, flour, vanilla extract, and lemon zest until the filling is velvety and well combined. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and spread it into an even layer. Step 3: Baking the Cheesecake Place the springform pan on a large baking sheet to catch any potential leaks. Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for 55-60 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour. This gradual cooling helps prevent cracks on the surface. After an hour, remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Then, refrigerate it for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight to set. Step 4: Adding the Topping (Optional) Once the cheesecake has chilled and set, carefully release the springform pan and transfer the cheesecake to a serving platter. If desired, top the cheesecake with fresh berries for a burst of color and freshness. Serving and Storing Slice the nut-free cheesecake into wedges and serve with a dollop of whipped cream or a drizzle of fruit sauce. Store any leftovers in the refrigerator, covered, for up to 5 days. This cheesecake also freezes well, so you can make it ahead of time for future indulgence. ...
