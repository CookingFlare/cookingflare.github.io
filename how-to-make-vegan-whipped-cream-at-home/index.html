How to Make Vegan Whipped Cream at Home


Vegan whipped cream is a delightful and versatile topping that can elevate a variety of desserts, from cakes to pies and even coffee. Making it at home is not only simple but also allows you to control the ingredients, ensuring a delicious and healthy alternative to traditional whipped cream. In this guide, we’ll explore different methods to create vegan whipped cream, focusing on coconut cream and aquafaba, two popular bases for this creamy delight.

Why Choose Vegan Whipped Cream?

Vegan whipped cream is an excellent choice for those who follow a plant-based diet or have lactose intolerance. It provides the same light and fluffy texture as traditional whipped cream without any dairy. Additionally, making your own whipped cream at home means you can customize the sweetness and flavor to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For Coconut Whipped Cream

  • 1 can of full-fat coconut milk or coconut cream: Ensure it’s chilled overnight in the refrigerator. This allows the cream to separate from the liquid.
  • 2-4 tablespoons of powdered sugar: Adjust based on your sweetness preference.
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract: For added flavor.
  • A pinch of salt: To enhance the overall taste.

For Aquafaba Whipped Cream

  • 1/2 cup of aquafaba: This is the liquid from a can of chickpeas. It acts as a fantastic egg white substitute.
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cream of tartar: Helps stabilize the whipped cream.
  • 2-4 tablespoons of powdered sugar: Again, adjust to your taste.
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract: For flavor.

Method 1: Making Coconut Whipped Cream

Step 1: Prepare the Coconut Cream

  1. Chill the Coconut Milk: Place a can of full-fat coconut milk in the refrigerator overnight. This step is crucial as it allows the cream to solidify and separate from the liquid.

  2. Scoop Out the Cream: After chilling, open the can and scoop out the solidified coconut cream into a mixing bowl. Leave the liquid behind, or save it for smoothies or soups.

Step 2: Whip the Cream

  1. Beat the Cream: Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the coconut cream on medium speed until it becomes fluffy and light. This should take about 2-3 minutes.

  2. Add Sweetener and Flavor: Gradually add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Continue to beat until everything is well combined and the cream holds stiff peaks.

Step 3: Serve or Store

  • Serve Immediately: Use your coconut whipped cream to top desserts like vegan cakes, pies, or even pancakes.
  • Store: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. The cream may firm up, so give it a quick whip before serving again.

Method 2: Making Aquafaba Whipped Cream

Step 1: Prepare the Aquafaba

  1. Measure the Aquafaba: Pour 1/2 cup of aquafaba into a mixing bowl. Ensure it’s at room temperature for the best results.

Step 2: Whip the Aquafaba

  1. Add Stabilizer: Add the cream of tartar to the aquafaba. This will help stabilize the whipped cream.

  2. Beat the Aquafaba: Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the aquafaba on high speed until it becomes frothy. This can take about 5-10 minutes. You’re looking for soft peaks to form.

  3. Incorporate Sweetener and Flavor: Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract while continuing to beat. Keep whipping until you achieve stiff peaks.

Step 3: Serve or Store

  • Serve Immediately: Aquafaba whipped cream is perfect for topping desserts or adding to beverages.
  • Store: It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. However, it’s best enjoyed fresh as it may lose its fluffiness over time.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Whipped Cream

  • Chill Your Equipment: For coconut whipped cream, chilling your mixing bowl and beaters can help achieve a better whip.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add different extracts like almond or peppermint for a unique twist. You can also incorporate cocoa powder for a chocolate version.
  • Adjust Sweetness: Taste your whipped cream as you go. You can always add more sugar if you prefer a sweeter topping.
  • Use High-Quality Ingredients: The quality of your coconut milk or aquafaba can significantly affect the final product. Opt for organic or high-fat coconut milk for the best results.

Creative Ways to Use Vegan Whipped Cream

Vegan whipped cream is incredibly versatile. Here are some delicious ideas to incorporate it into your meals:

  • Topping for Desserts: Use it on cakes, cupcakes, or brownies for a delightful finish.
  • Coffee Enhancer: Add a dollop to your morning coffee or hot chocolate for a creamy treat.
  • Fruit Dip: Serve it alongside fresh fruit for a light and refreshing dessert.
  • Pancake or Waffle Topping: Elevate your breakfast by adding whipped cream to pancakes or waffles.

Conclusion

Making vegan whipped cream at home is a simple and rewarding process. Whether you choose the rich and creamy coconut version or the light and airy aquafaba option, you’ll have a delicious topping that can enhance any dessert. With just a few ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a delightful whipped cream that’s perfect for any occasion. Enjoy experimenting with flavors and serving it in various ways to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills!
