Vegan Buffalo Bites: 15 Spicy, Tangy, Finger-Licking Good Plant-Based Snacks


What’s better than a dish soaked in hot sauce and smothered with tangy blue cheese? A bite-sized version of that very dish! Because who wants to waste their time with utensils when you can indulge in bursts of spicy and tangy goodness with your bare hands? Introducing 15 mouthwatering vegan buffalo bites, designed for those who know that hunger pangs don’t wait for any restrictions. So let’s grab our chopping boards and aprons and dive right in!

  1. Savory Oatmeal Buffalo Bites

Occasionally, it’s good to get your hands dirty. Oatmeal, while not the first thing that comes to mind when thinking about spicy Buffalo bites, offers a deliciously satisfying foundation with a soft texture and nutty flavor. Prepare to be astonished!

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water)
  • 1/2 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Seasoned salt (optional)
  • Pumpkin seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring the almond milk to a boil, then stir in the rolled oats. Reduce heat and cook for 5 minutes or until the water is absorbed.

  2. Combine the flax egg in a bowl and set aside for 5 minutes to form a gel.

  3. Once the oatmeal has cooked and slightly cooled, mix in the flax egg, breadcrumbs, Frank’s RedHot, and garlic powder. Form small balls and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Place the pumpkin seeds in a little dish and roll the balls in them for a crunchy surface if preferred. Bake these bad boys at 350°F for 20 minutes or until crispy.

  5. Toss them in a bowl with more Frank’s RedHot and cover them in vegan ranch dressing or vegan blue cheese sauce, sprinkle with seasoned salt if desired, and enjoy!

  6. Quinoa Buffalo Bites

Quinoa is a protein powerhouse and absorbs flavors effortlessly. These bites are perfect for satisfying that hunger and kickstarting your vegan protein intake without breaking a sweat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/4 cup finely diced sun-dried tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • Vegan mayonnaise (optional)
  • Seasoned salt (optional)
  • Crushed walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine the cooked quinoa, sun-dried tomatoes, green onions, and Frank’s RedHot in a bowl. Form small balls and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. If you want a crunchy exterior, roll the bites in crushed walnuts before placing them in a baking dish.

  3. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes or until thoroughly heated and golden on top.

  4. Toss the bites in vegan mayonnaise and add a sprinkle of seasoned salt for enhanced flavor.

  5. Chickpea Buffalo Bites

Chickpeas, also known as garbanzo beans, love to masquerade as components of an array of dishes. In this version, they stand out with their mealy texture and starchy goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup panko bread crumbs
  • Seasoned salt (optional)
  • Smoked paprika (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Blend the chickpeas, Frank’s RedHot, tahini, and green onions in a food processor or mash them with a fork until a chunky batter forms.

  2. Fold in the panko breadcrumbs, and then form the mixture into small balls. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  3. At 400°F, bake these crunchy delights for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

  4. Finally, mix them with more Frank’s RedHot and dust with seasoned salt and smoked paprika for an unforgettable kick.

  5. Cauliflower Buffalo Bites

Cauliflower is a superstar in the world of plant-based eating. This versatile veggie is equipped with the crucial enzymes to hold its shape, making it a perfect candidate for any buffalo bites recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into bite-size florets
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
  • Olive oil
  • Seasoned salt (optional)
  • Dried parsley (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F. In a bowl, mix the Frank’s RedHot, green onions, and breadcrumbs. Coat the cauliflower florets with olive oil and toss them in the mixture.

  2. On a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, place the florets in a single layer.

  3. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender, and the coating is golden brown.

  4. Toss the cauliflower bites in more Frank’s RedHot and give a light sprinkle of seasoned salt and dried parsley.

  5. Jackfruit Buffalo Bites

Jackfruit: the miracle fruit that can imitate shredded chicken or pork when tailored with the appropriate spices and toppings. Unbelievable, right?

Ingredients:

  • 1 can jackfruit in water, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Seasoned salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Shred the jackfruit using your fingers or a fork. Cook it over medium heat with the olive oil for about 5 minutes to remove excess water.
  2. Stir in the Frank’s RedHot, green onions, red bell pepper, gluten-free Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Heat until everything is fragrant and thoroughly mixed.
  3. Form small balls and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake at 400°F for 20-25 minutes or until browned.
  4. Sprinkle seasoned salt over the bites before serving. Time for some buffalo bliss!

There’s no shortage of creativity in the vegan world, and the number of options continues to grow as more people join the plant-powered movement. Experiment with these buffalo bites and let your creativity run wild. Don’t be afraid to substitute ingredients or add new elements based on your taste preferences – you never know what culinary masterpieces may emerge in the kitchen! Happy vegan Buffalo tasting, and may it bring you fiery joy.
