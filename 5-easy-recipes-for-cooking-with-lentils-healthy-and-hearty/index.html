5 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Lentils: Healthy and Hearty


Lentils are the unsung heroes of the culinary world. Packed with protein, fiber, and an array of vitamins and minerals, they are not only versatile and affordable but also incredibly delicious. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out, these five easy lentil recipes will help you create hearty meals that are both healthy and satisfying. Let’s dive in!

1. Lentil Soup with Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are softened. Stir in the garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  2. Add Lentils and Broth: Add the rinsed lentils, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, thyme, and cumin to the pot. Stir to combine, bringing the mixture to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 30-35 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. If the soup becomes too thick, feel free to add more broth or water to reach your desired consistency.

  4. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Why It’s Great:

This lentil soup is not only comforting but also loaded with nutrients. The combination of colorful vegetables adds flavor and texture, making it a perfect dish for chilly evenings or meal prep for the week ahead.

2. Spicy Lentil Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning (or a mix of cumin, chili powder, and paprika)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn or flour tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, salsa, cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Lentils: In a pot, combine the rinsed lentils and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the lentils are soft and absorb most of the liquid.

  2. Sauté the Onion and Garlic: In a skillet, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute.

  3. Combine and Season: Add the cooked lentils to the skillet with the onion and garlic. Stir in the taco seasoning, and cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Assemble Tacos: Warm the tortillas in a separate skillet or microwave. Spoon the spicy lentil mixture onto the tortillas and top with avocado, salsa, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

Why It’s Great:

These tacos are a fun and creative way to enjoy lentils. They are packed with flavor and can easily be customized with your favorite toppings. Plus, they make a fantastic meatless meal!

3. Lentil Salad with Feta and Spinach

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Lentils: Combine the rinsed lentils and water in a pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 20-25 minutes until tender but not mushy. Drain and let cool.

  2. Prepare the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.

  3. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the cooled lentils, chopped spinach, red onion, and feta cheese. Drizzle with the dressing and toss gently to combine.

  4. Serve: This salad can be served immediately or chilled in the refrigerator for an hour to let the flavors meld.

Why It’s Great:

This lentil salad is refreshing and nutrient-dense, making it an excellent choice for lunch or a light dinner. The creamy feta and tangy dressing enhance the earthy flavor of the lentils, creating a delightful balance.

4. Lentil and Quinoa Pilaf

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils (green or brown), rinsed
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 cup raisins or dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds or walnuts)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Lentils and Quinoa: In a pot, combine the lentils, quinoa, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook for about 15-20 minutes, until the lentils and quinoa are tender and the liquid is absorbed.

  2. Sauté the Onion and Garlic: In a separate skillet, heat a bit of oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until golden brown, then add the garlic, turmeric, and cumin, cooking until fragrant.

  3. Combine Everything: Once the lentils and quinoa are cooked, fluff with a fork and stir in the sautéed onion and garlic, raisins, and chopped nuts. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve warm as a main dish or side.

Why It’s Great:

This pilaf is a hearty and comforting dish that combines the nutty flavors of quinoa with the earthiness of lentils. The sweetness from the raisins and the crunch from the nuts add delightful texture and flavor, making it a satisfying meal.

5. Lentil Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, sautéing until the onion is translucent.

  2. Add Spices: Stir in the curry powder and cumin, cooking for about 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Cook the Lentils: Add the rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 20-25 minutes until the lentils are tender and the curry has thickened. Stir occasionally, and add more broth if needed.

  4. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper. Serve the lentil curry over rice or with naan, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Why It’s Great:

This lentil curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish that transports you to the heart of Indian cuisine. The creaminess of the coconut milk complements the spices beautifully, creating a comforting meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Final Thoughts

Cooking with lentils opens up a world of culinary possibilities. These five easy recipes are not only healthy and hearty but also showcase the versatility of lentils in various cuisines. From comforting soups to vibrant salads, lentils are a fantastic ingredient to incorporate into your weekly meal planning. Enjoy experimenting with these recipes and feel free to adapt them to your taste preferences! Happy cooking!
