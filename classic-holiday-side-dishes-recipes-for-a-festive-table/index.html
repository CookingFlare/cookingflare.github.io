Classic Holiday Side Dishes: Recipes for a Festive Table


The holiday season is upon us, and with it, the excitement of cooking and sharing delicious meals with loved ones. While the main course is often the star of the show, the side dishes play a vital role in rounding out the flavor and texture of the meal. In this post, we'll explore some classic holiday side dishes that will complement your festive table and leave your guests asking for seconds. Traditional Green Bean Casserole This iconic dish has been a staple of American holiday tables since the 1950s. Its simplicity and convenience make it a crowd-pleaser, and the addition of a crispy fried onion topping elevates the dish to new heights. Ingredients:

1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces 1 medium onion, thinly sliced 1 cup cream of mushroom soup 1/2 cup milk 1 teaspoon black pepper 1/2 teaspoon salt 2 tablespoons butter 1 cup French-fried onions

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large skillet, sauté the sliced onions in butter until caramelized and golden brown. In a separate pot, boil the green beans until tender. Drain and set aside. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream of mushroom soup, milk, black pepper, and salt. Add the cooked green beans and stir until well combined. Transfer the mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish and top with the caramelized onions and French-fried onions. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the onions are golden brown and the casserole is hot and bubbly.

Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallows This sweet and savory side dish is a Southern classic, and for good reason. The sweetness of the marshmallows pairs perfectly with the earthy sweetness of the sweet potatoes. Ingredients:

2 large sweet potatoes, cooked and mashed 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger 1/2 cup marshmallows 1/4 cup brown sugar

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, granulated sugar, flour, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Transfer the mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish and smooth out the top. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the top is lightly browned. Remove from the oven and top with marshmallows and brown sugar. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes or until the marshmallows are toasted golden brown.

Sautéed Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze Braising sprouts in a sweet and savory glaze elevates this often-maligned veggie to new heights. The smokiness of the bacon adds a depth of flavor that's hard to resist. Ingredients:

1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved 6 slices of bacon, diced 1/4 cup balsamic glaze 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

In a large skillet, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside. In the same skillet, add the olive oil and cook the Brussels sprouts until caramelized and tender. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic glaze and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the glaze over the sprouts and toss to coat. Return the cooked bacon to the skillet and toss to combine. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.

Roasted Root Vegetables Roasting brings out the natural sweetness in root vegetables, making them a perfect side dish during the winter months. Simply toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast until tender and caramelized. Ingredients:

2 carrots, peeled and chopped 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and chopped 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, toss the chopped vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper until evenly coated. Spread the vegetables out in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender and caramelized. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.

Maple Glazed Carrots Sweet, sticky, and addictively delicious, these carrots are the perfect side dish for a holiday meal. Ingredients:

2 pounds carrots, peeled and chopped 1/4 cup maple syrup 2 tablespoons butter 1/4 cup brown sugar 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, butter, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Add the chopped carrots and toss to coat evenly. Spread the carrots out in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender and caramelized.

These timeless side dishes will add a touch of classic elegance to your holiday table. Whether you're hosting a family dinner or a festive gathering, these recipes are sure to delight your guests and satisfy their palates. So why not give one (or two, or three!) a try and see why they've become staples of holiday cuisine?
