Butternut Bounty: Savory Butternut Squash Recipes for the Fall Season


As the crisp fall air settles in and the leaves start to change colors, it’s the perfect time to embrace the comforting flavors of the season. Butternut squash, with its vibrant orange hue and rich, slightly sweet taste, is one of the stars of fall cuisine. Whether roasted, pureed, or simmered in soups, butternut squash is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of savory dishes that warm the soul and excite the taste buds.

In this post, we’ll dive into some delicious and creative ways to incorporate butternut squash into your meals this fall. From savory roasts to rich soups and hearty pastas, there are endless ways to enjoy this autumn vegetable.

Why Butternut Squash is Perfect for Fall

Butternut squash, with its nutty flavor and creamy texture, is a fall favorite for many reasons. It is a type of winter squash that is harvested in the cooler months, making it a staple ingredient for cozy autumn meals. Butternut squash is also incredibly nutritious, packed with vitamins like vitamin A and C, fiber, and antioxidants. It’s a low-calorie option that is filling and satisfying, making it ideal for hearty, warming meals as the weather turns colder.

One of the best things about butternut squash is how adaptable it is. It pairs beautifully with other fall flavors, such as sage, thyme, garlic, and cinnamon, and can be incorporated into both savory and sweet dishes. In the following recipes, we’ll explore some of the best savory ways to enjoy butternut squash this fall.

1. Roasted Butternut Squash with Garlic and Sage

A simple, yet flavorful dish, roasted butternut squash with garlic and sage is the perfect side dish for any autumn meal. The roasting process brings out the natural sweetness of the squash, while the garlic and sage add savory, earthy notes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried sage)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: A sprinkle of Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, minced garlic, chopped sage, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the squash in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until the squash is tender and lightly browned.
  5. Optional: Before serving, sprinkle with freshly grated Parmesan cheese for an extra burst of flavor.

This roasted butternut squash is perfect as a side dish for roasted meats, grain bowls, or even a light salad.

2. Butternut Squash Soup with Coconut Milk

There’s nothing more comforting than a warm bowl of soup on a chilly fall evening. Butternut squash soup is a classic fall recipe, and adding coconut milk makes it velvety smooth and adds a subtle sweetness that complements the squash perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk (full-fat for a creamier texture)
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 5-7 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, ground ginger, salt, and pepper to the pot, and stir to combine.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  4. Once the squash is cooked through, use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can carefully transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk and adjust the seasoning to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley or cilantro.

This butternut squash soup is both filling and nourishing. It makes a great starter for a fall dinner or can be served as a main dish alongside some crusty bread.

3. Butternut Squash and Spinach Stuffed Shells

For a hearty and comforting fall dinner, try butternut squash and spinach stuffed shells. This dish combines the creamy, slightly sweet flavor of roasted butternut squash with the richness of ricotta cheese and fresh spinach, all wrapped up in pasta shells and topped with marinara sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 12 large pasta shells
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 ½ cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 ½ cups marinara sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roast the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  3. Cook the pasta shells according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside to cool.
  4. In a large bowl, mash the roasted butternut squash and mix it with the ricotta cheese, chopped spinach, and egg. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Stuff each pasta shell with the butternut squash mixture and place the shells in a baking dish.
  6. Pour marinara sauce over the stuffed shells and cover the dish with foil.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until heated through. Remove the foil during the last 5 minutes of baking to allow the tops to brown.
  8. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley before serving.

These stuffed shells make for a filling and satisfying main course, and they’re perfect for family gatherings or special fall meals.

4. Butternut Squash Risotto with Parmesan

Risotto is a classic Italian dish that pairs perfectly with the creamy texture of butternut squash. This butternut squash risotto is rich, flavorful, and full of autumn warmth. The addition of Parmesan gives the dish a salty, nutty finish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • ½ cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a large pot, heat a little olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Slowly add the white wine (if using) and cook until it’s mostly absorbed.
  6. Begin adding the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until most of the liquid has been absorbed before adding the next ladle of broth. Continue this process until the rice is tender and creamy, about 20-25 minutes.
  7. Stir in the roasted butternut squash and Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme.

This creamy butternut squash risotto is a cozy, elegant dish that pairs beautifully with a crisp salad or roasted vegetables.

5. Butternut Squash and Black Bean Tacos

For a plant-based fall meal, these butternut squash and black bean tacos are a delicious and satisfying option. The roasted butternut squash provides a slightly sweet contrast to the savory black beans, and the toppings add extra crunch and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Toppings: Avocado slices, cilantro, lime wedges, crumbled feta cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Spread the squash in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. While the squash is roasting, warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or on a griddle.
  5. Once the squash is done, assemble the tacos by placing a spoonful of roasted butternut squash and black beans on each tortilla.
  6. Top with avocado slices, cilantro, lime wedges, and crumbled feta cheese if desired.
  7. Serve immediately.

These tacos are a great option for Taco Tuesday or any casual fall meal. They’re fresh, filling, and bursting with seasonal flavor.

Conclusion

Butternut squash is the perfect vegetable to embrace during the fall season. Its versatility, sweet flavor, and creamy texture make it ideal for a variety of savory dishes, from simple roasted sides to comforting soups and hearty pasta dishes. Whether you’re making a classic butternut squash soup or getting creative with stuffed shells or tacos, there’s no shortage of ways to enjoy this fall favorite.

The next time you’re at the farmers’ market or grocery store, be sure to pick up a butternut squash and try one of these delicious recipes. They’ll bring warmth, comfort, and a touch of autumn to your dining table. Enjoy!
