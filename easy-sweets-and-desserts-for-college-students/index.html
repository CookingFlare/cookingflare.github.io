Easy Sweets and Desserts for College Students


College life can be a whirlwind of assignments, exams, and social events, but that doesn’t mean students can’t indulge in some sweet treats to unwind after a long day of studying or celebrating a small victory. The best part? Many of these desserts are simple to make, budget-friendly, and require minimal ingredients and equipment. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner in the kitchen, these easy sweets and desserts will satisfy your sweet tooth without adding stress to your already packed schedule.

1. Microwave Mug Cakes: Quick and Customizable

Mug cakes have gained popularity among busy college students, and for good reason! They’re quick, customizable, and require just a microwave and a mug. Whether you’re craving chocolate, vanilla, or something more adventurous like peanut butter or s’mores, mug cakes can be whipped up in minutes.

How to Make a Basic Mug Cake

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder (for chocolate flavor)
  • 3 tablespoons milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe mug, whisk together all the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add the milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth, ensuring there are no lumps.
  3. Microwave the mug on high for 1 minute and 30 seconds, or until the cake has risen and looks set in the middle.
  4. Let it cool for a minute, and enjoy your warm, freshly baked mug cake!

You can add a dollop of whipped cream, a scoop of ice cream, or a sprinkle of chocolate chips for extra flavor.

2. No-Bake Cheesecake Parfaits

Sometimes, the thought of turning on the oven is the last thing a student wants to do. Fortunately, no-bake cheesecakes are the perfect solution. These creamy, rich desserts are made with simple ingredients, and they can be customized with a variety of toppings to suit your taste.

How to Make No-Bake Cheesecake Parfaits

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs (or crushed cookies of your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Fruit (berries, mango, or any fruit you prefer)
  • Jam or syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue mixing until the mixture is creamy and well-combined.
  2. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture.
  3. In another bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated.
  4. In individual jars or glasses, layer the graham cracker crust at the bottom, followed by a layer of cheesecake filling, and then a layer of your choice of fruit.
  5. Repeat the layers as needed, finishing with a dollop of whipped cream or fruit on top.
  6. Refrigerate the parfaits for at least 1 hour before serving, allowing the flavors to meld together.

These parfaits are easy to make in large batches and can be stored in the fridge for a few days, making them a great option for meal prep or when you’re craving something sweet on short notice.

3. Cookie Dough Bites: A Safe and Sweet Treat

Cookie dough is a guilty pleasure for many, but eating raw dough can sometimes be risky due to raw eggs. Luckily, you can make edible cookie dough without the eggs, so you can enjoy the delicious dough without worrying about any health concerns. These no-bake cookie dough bites are perfect for late-night cravings or when you want a sweet treat in a hurry.

How to Make Cookie Dough Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Heat the flour in the microwave for 1 minute to kill any potential bacteria, then let it cool completely.
  2. In a mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the vanilla extract and milk, then mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the cooled flour and salt, mixing until the dough comes together.
  5. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  6. Roll the dough into small bite-sized balls and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Chill the cookie dough bites in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before eating.

These cookie dough bites are sweet, buttery, and packed with chocolatey goodness. You can also add your favorite mix-ins, such as chopped nuts or mini marshmallows, to change up the flavor.

4. Banana Ice Cream: The Healthier Sweet Treat

If you’re looking for a healthier alternative to traditional ice cream, banana ice cream is the perfect solution. With just one ingredient – frozen bananas – you can make a creamy, dairy-free dessert that tastes just like ice cream. Plus, it’s naturally sweet, making it a guilt-free indulgence.

How to Make Banana Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas (peeled and sliced)
  • Optional add-ins: cocoa powder, peanut butter, vanilla extract, or chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Freeze the banana slices for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Place the frozen banana slices in a food processor and blend until smooth. You may need to scrape down the sides occasionally.
  3. If you’d like to add extra flavors, add cocoa powder for chocolate banana ice cream, peanut butter for a creamy nutty taste, or vanilla extract for extra sweetness.
  4. Serve immediately for a soft-serve consistency, or freeze for another 30 minutes for a firmer texture.

Banana ice cream is an incredibly versatile treat, and the best part is that you can experiment with various flavor combinations to suit your taste.

5. Rice Krispies Treats: A Classic Favorite

Rice Krispies treats are a nostalgic dessert that takes just a few minutes to prepare. They’re sweet, chewy, and perfect for sharing with friends or saving for late-night snacks. You can even customize them with different cereals or add-ins like chocolate chips, M&Ms, or sprinkles for a colorful touch.

How to Make Rice Krispies Treats

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 package (10 oz) mini marshmallows
  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • Optional add-ins: chocolate chips, sprinkles, or candies

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir constantly until they are completely melted and smooth.
  2. Remove the pan from the heat and add the Rice Krispies cereal. Stir gently until the cereal is fully coated with the marshmallow mixture.
  3. If you want to add extra ingredients like chocolate chips or sprinkles, fold them in now.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking pan. Use a spatula or your hands (dusted with a little butter) to press the mixture evenly into the pan.
  5. Allow the treats to cool completely before cutting them into squares.

Rice Krispies treats are incredibly easy to make in large batches and can be stored in an airtight container for up to a week. They’re perfect for sharing with friends or taking to a potluck event.

6. Chocolate-Dipped Pretzels: Sweet and Salty Delight

Chocolate-dipped pretzels are a simple yet delicious treat that combines the perfect balance of sweet and salty. They’re great for a quick snack or a fun party treat, and the best part is you can make them in just a few minutes.

How to Make Chocolate-Dipped Pretzels

Ingredients:

  • 1 bag of pretzels (pretzel rods or mini pretzels)
  • 1 cup chocolate chips (milk, dark, or white chocolate)
  • Sprinkles, crushed nuts, or crushed candy for decoration (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until smooth and fully melted.
  2. Dip each pretzel into the melted chocolate, covering about two-thirds of the pretzel.
  3. Place the dipped pretzels on a parchment-lined baking sheet. If desired, sprinkle them with sprinkles, crushed nuts, or candy while the chocolate is still soft.
  4. Allow the pretzels to cool and harden for about 30 minutes before enjoying.

These chocolate-dipped pretzels are a fun treat to make with friends, and you can easily customize them with different types of chocolate or toppings to match your preferences.

7. Apple Nachos: A Fruity, Fun Dessert

Apple nachos are a refreshing, healthier alternative to traditional nachos, and they’re just as fun to make. This sweet dessert is perfect for when you want something light and fruity, but still indulgent.

How to Make Apple Nachos

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples (any variety)
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter (or almond butter)
  • 1/4 cup chocolate chips (or melted chocolate)
  • 2 tablespoons granola
  • Optional toppings: dried fruit, coconut flakes, chopped nuts

Instructions:

  1. Slice the apples into thin rounds or wedges and arrange them on a plate or baking sheet.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, warm the peanut butter until it’s soft and spreadable. Drizzle it over the apple slices.
  3. Melt the chocolate chips in the microwave and drizzle over the apples as well.
  4. Sprinkle granola, dried fruit, coconut flakes, or chopped nuts on top for extra texture and flavor.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the sweet, crunchy, and creamy combination!

Apple nachos are a fun and light dessert that combines the sweetness of apples with the rich flavors of peanut butter and chocolate.

Conclusion

College life doesn’t have to be all about instant noodles and late-night study sessions. With these easy sweets and desserts, you can indulge in a little treat without spending too much time in the kitchen. Whether you’re in the mood for a microwave mug cake, a no-bake cheesecake parfait, or a healthier option like banana ice cream, there’s something for every craving. So gather a few basic ingredients, experiment with your favorite flavors, and enjoy the process of making simple, sweet treats that will make your college experience even sweeter.
