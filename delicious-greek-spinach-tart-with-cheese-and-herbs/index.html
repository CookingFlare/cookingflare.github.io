Delicious Greek Spinach Tart with Cheese and Herbs


Greek cuisine is celebrated worldwide for its rich flavors, fresh ingredients, and artful simplicity, and few dishes embody these qualities as beautifully as a spinach tart infused with cheese and aromatic herbs. Featuring a flaky golden crust and a savory filling that brims with the vibrant tastes of Greece, this recipe is perfect for everything from casual family dinners to indulgent weekend brunches. The Greek spinach tart delivers hearty satisfaction in each bite while remaining light enough to let the individual flavors shine.

This classic Mediterranean dish is inspired by the famous Greek “spanakopita,” a savory spinach pie typically made with layers of buttery phyllo dough filled with greens, feta cheese, and a medley of herbs. In this recipe, we bring together all of those beloved flavors with some twists to create a tart that’s as simple to prepare as it is delicious to eat.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Making a Greek spinach tart requires simple, fresh ingredients that come together to create a dynamic, flavorful dish. Here’s a detailed breakdown of what you’ll need to get started:

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 large egg
  • 3–4 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups fresh spinach leaves, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¾ cup ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup grated kefalotyri or Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Optional Garnish:

  • Lemon zest
  • Fresh dill sprigs

How to Make a Greek Spinach Tart

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

Begin by creating a perfectly flaky crust. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and dried oregano. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in the egg and gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough just comes together.

Form the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough ensures a tender, flaky texture and allows the butter to solidify, which will help it puff up beautifully as it bakes.

Step 2: Make the Filling

While the crust chills, it’s time to focus on the star of the dish: the savory spinach filling. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and sauté the chopped onion until it’s soft and translucent, about 4–5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Next, toss in the chopped spinach leaves. Cook until they wilt and release their moisture, stirring often. This should take about 5–7 minutes. Remove the spinach mixture from the heat and transfer it to a strainer. Press gently with the back of a spoon to remove any excess liquid, as too much moisture could cause the tart to become soggy.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked spinach mixture with the parsley, dill, feta cheese, ricotta cheese, and kefalotyri (or Parmesan). Mix well. Add the eggs, lemon juice, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Season with salt and pepper, keeping in mind that the feta cheese already has a salty profile. Stir everything together until fully incorporated.

Step 3: Assemble the Tart

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough into the pan, pressing gently to cover the bottom and sides evenly. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edge.

Using a fork, prick the base of the crust all over to prevent bubbles as it bakes. Place the crust in the oven and blind bake it for about 10 minutes or until lightly golden. If you like, you can use pie weights or dried beans to keep the crust flat during blind baking.

Once the crust is partially baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Add the spinach filling, spreading it evenly across the surface of the tart. Smooth the top with a spatula or the back of a spoon to ensure an even layer.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

Place the assembled tart back into the oven and bake for 30–35 minutes, or until the filling is set and a golden crust has formed around the edges. You’ll know it’s ready when the center is firm, and the top is lightly golden brown.

Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing. This allows the filling to set and ensures clean, neat slices.

Delicious Serving Suggestions

A Greek spinach tart pairs wonderfully with an array of complementary dishes or can shine as the star of the table. Serve it warm or at room temperature alongside a crisp Greek salad, featuring cucumbers, tomatoes, Kalamata olives, and a sprinkle of oregano. A dollop of tzatziki sauce on the side provides a creamy, tangy counterpart to the tart’s flavors.

For a heartier meal, consider adding roasted Greek-style potatoes seasoned with lemon, garlic, and olive oil, or a bowl of lentil soup that enhances the earthy tones of the spinach filling.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Whether you’re already a fan of Greek cuisine or trying it for the first time, this spinach tart with cheese and herbs is bound to become a favorite. It’s customizable, easy to make, and packed with layers of flavor from the fresh greens and herbs combined with the creaminess of Greek-style cheeses. The homemade crust adds a rustic flare, making this dish ideal for hosting dinner parties or enjoying quiet moments of indulgence with loved ones.

The best part? You can prepare this tart in advance. It stores well covered in the fridge for up to three days, and reheating it in a low-temperature oven maintains the warm flavors and flaky texture.

Tips for Success

  1. Fresh Ingredients Are Key: Use fresh spinach and herbs for the best flavor. If you’re short on fresh ingredients, frozen spinach can be substituted, though make sure it’s fully defrosted and drained to remove any excess moisture.

  2. Make It Ahead: Cooking the filling and preparing the crust a day in advance can save time. Store each component separately and assemble them when you’re ready to bake.

  3. Experiment with Cheeses: While feta and ricotta are traditional choices, options like goat cheese or cream cheese can introduce a unique variation in flavor. Kefalotyri can also be substituted with Pecorino Romano or Gruyère if needed.

  4. Watch the Baking Time: Overbaking the tart can dry out the filling, so keep a close eye during the final minutes. You want the edges crisp and the filling set, but still moist and creamy.

A Mediterranean Classic

This Greek spinach tart with cheese and herbs is a true celebration of flavors that highlight the spirit of Mediterranean cooking. From the buttery crust to the vibrant filling, each bite is a testament to the beauty of simple, wholesome ingredients coming together in harmonious balance. Whether you serve it as an appetizer, main course, or side dish, it’s guaranteed to delight both you and your guests.
