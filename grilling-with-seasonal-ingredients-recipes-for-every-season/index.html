Grilling with Seasonal Ingredients: Recipes for Every Season


Grilling is much more than just a cooking method; it’s a celebration of flavors, textures, and the great outdoors. When you incorporate seasonal ingredients into your grilling repertoire, you elevate your dishes to new heights. This blog post will guide you through the best seasonal ingredients to use for grilling, along with mouth-watering recipes that celebrate the essence of each season. Prepare to fire up your grill and impress your friends and family with these delectable dishes!

Spring: Fresh and Vibrant

As winter melts away, spring brings a bounty of fresh vegetables and herbs that are perfect for grilling. The season is characterized by tender greens, vibrant colors, and a sense of renewal.

Grilled Asparagus with Lemon-Garlic Butter

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add minced garlic, lemon zest, and juice, stirring well to combine.
  3. Season the asparagus with salt and pepper. Place them directly on the grill grates.
  4. Grill the asparagus for 4-6 minutes, turning occasionally until tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and drizzle the lemon-garlic butter over the top. Sprinkle with grated Parmesan if desired.

Herb-Marinated Grilled Chicken

Spring is also the perfect time to enjoy lighter meats like chicken, enhanced with fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken breasts to the marinade, covering them completely. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for 6-7 minutes on each side or until fully cooked.
  4. Serve with a side of grilled spring vegetables for a refreshing meal.

Summer: Bold and Juicy

Summer is synonymous with grilling, and the season offers a cornucopia of ripe fruits and vegetables. From juicy tomatoes to sweet corn and succulent peaches, summer grilling is all about bold flavors.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili-Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with lime zest, lime juice, chili powder, and salt.
  3. Grill the corn for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until charred and tender.
  4. Spread the chili-lime butter over the hot corn and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Grilled Peach and Burrata Salad

For a refreshing summer salad, grilled peaches paired with creamy burrata offer a delightful contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 8 ounces burrata cheese
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the peach halves cut-side down on the grill.
  2. Grill for 3-4 minutes until grill marks appear. Remove from the grill and let cool slightly.
  3. On a platter, arrange the arugula and top with grilled peaches and torn burrata.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and olive oil, then season with salt and pepper.

Fall: Earthy and Comforting

As the weather cools, fall brings hearty ingredients that add warmth and comfort to your grilling. Root vegetables and squashes are perfect for this season.

Grilled Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh sage leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Toss butternut squash cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill the squash for 15-20 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  3. In a saucepan, heat vegetable broth and keep it warm. In another pot, saut√© the Arborio rice until slightly translucent.
  4. Add the white wine and cook until absorbed. Gradually add the broth, stirring constantly until creamy.
  5. Stir in the grilled squash and Parmesan cheese. Garnish with fresh sage before serving.

Grilled Apple and Sausage Skewers

Fall is the perfect time for hearty flavors, and these skewers are a great way to celebrate the season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Italian sausage, cut into chunks
  • 2 apples, cored and cut into wedges
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. In a bowl, toss sausage, apple wedges, and onion with olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
  2. Thread the mixture onto skewers, alternating between sausage, apple, and onion.
  3. Grill skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the sausage is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

Winter: Warm and Hearty

While many people may think of grilling as a summer activity, winter grilling can be just as delicious, especially with the right ingredients. Winter produce is often hearty and can withstand the grill’s heat.

Grilled Winter Root Vegetable Medley

Ingredients:

  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 parsnip, sliced
  • 1 beet, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and dried thyme to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. In a bowl, toss the root vegetables with olive oil, salt, pepper, and thyme.
  2. Place the vegetables in a grill basket and grill for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally until tender and charred.
  3. Serve warm, drizzled with balsamic reduction for added flavor.

Grilled Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

A hearty steak is perfect for winter grilling, especially when paired with a vibrant chimichurri sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Season the steaks with salt and pepper, letting them come to room temperature.
  2. Preheat the grill to high heat. Grill the steaks for 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare.
  3. For the chimichurri, mix parsley, olive oil, red wine vinegar, garlic, and red pepper flakes in a bowl.
  4. Let the steaks rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve topped with chimichurri sauce.
