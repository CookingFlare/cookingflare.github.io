No-Churn Peanut Butter Ice Cream Recipe


If you’ve ever found yourself craving homemade ice cream but without the hassle of pulling out the ice cream maker, you’re in for a treat. This no-churn peanut butter ice cream recipe is a celebration of creamy texture, rich flavor, and simplicity. With just a handful of ingredients and a little patience, you’ll create a luscious frozen treat that tastes like it came from a boutique creamery.

Why No-Churn Ice Cream?

Traditional ice cream making often requires an ice cream machine, which can be intimidating or inconvenient for many home cooks. No-churn ice cream cuts through the fuss. You skip the freezing bowl and churning mechanism, and instead rely on a clever balance of whipping cream and sweetened condensed milk to achieve the perfect creamy consistency. This method is not only straightforward but also adaptable for endless flavor variations.

Peanut butter adds a deliciously nutty, salty-sweet punch to the ice cream, providing richness without overpowering the palate. Whether you’re a peanut butter fanatic or looking for a crowd-pleaser dessert, this recipe ticks all boxes.

Ingredients Breakdown

  • Heavy Whipping Cream (2 cups): The cornerstone of no-churn ice cream. Whipping cream’s fat content ensures the ice cream stays smooth and creamy rather than icy.

  • Sweetened Condensed Milk (1 can, 14 oz): Acts as a natural sweetener and provides the necessary sugars that prevent the formation of large ice crystals, improving texture.

  • Peanut Butter (1 cup): Use creamy peanut butter for smoother ice cream. Natural peanut butter works well, but you can add a touch of honey or sugar if it’s less sweet.

  • Vanilla Extract (1 teaspoon): Enhances depth of flavor, subtly complementing the peanut butter.

  • Salt (a pinch): Balances sweetness and intensifies the nutty aroma.

  • Optional Mix-Ins: Mini chocolate chips, chopped peanuts, or swirls of fudge sauce can take this ice cream to the next level.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Whip the Cream

Start by chilling your mixing bowl and beaters for about 10 minutes—cold equipment whips cream faster and achieves better volume.

Pour the heavy whipping cream into the chilled bowl and whip on medium-high speed until you reach stiff peaks. This means when you lift the beater, the cream stands up firmly without falling over.

2. Incorporate Sweetened Condensed Milk

In a separate bowl, combine the sweetened condensed milk with peanut butter, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. For the peanut butter, if you use a thicker variety, it might help to microwave it for 15-20 seconds to loosen the texture for easier mixing.

Whisk until the mixture is smooth and homogenous.

3. Fold the Mixtures Together

Gently fold the whipped cream into the peanut butter mixture using a spatula. The folding technique is crucial here—combine without deflating the air incorporated into the cream. Use a slow and methodical motion, scraping around the bowl and folding over the mixture until fully integrated.

4. Add Mix-Ins (Optional)

At this stage, stir in any additional ingredients you’d like. Chocolate chips add bursts of richness, chopped peanuts bring crunchy texture, and a swirl of fudge brings decadence.

5. Freeze and Patience

Transfer the ice cream base to a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top. Seal tightly with a lid or plastic wrap to avoid freezer burn or ice crystallization.

Freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight. Patience is your best friend here, as the mixture firms up into creamy perfection.

6. Serving Suggestions

Before scooping, allow the ice cream to sit at room temperature for roughly 5-10 minutes. This softens it slightly and makes scooping easier.

Consider pairing your peanut butter ice cream with:

  • Warm chocolate brownies
  • Sliced bananas and caramel drizzle
  • A sprinkle of sea salt for contrast

Tips for Perfect No-Churn Peanut Butter Ice Cream

  • Choice of Peanut Butter: Creamy peanut butter yields smooth ice cream. Chunky peanut butter can create interesting texture but may interfere with cream folding.

  • Whipping the Cream: Achieving stiff peaks is non-negotiable. Under-whipped cream results in a dense, icy product.

  • Mix-Ins and Swirls: Customize based on preferences. Add chocolate chips for texture, or swirl caramel or fudge sauce for richness.

  • Storage: Keep ice cream in an airtight container. Between servings, place a piece of parchment paper on the surface to guard against crystals.

Variation Ideas for Peanut Butter Lovers

Chocolate-Peanut Butter Swirl

Split the base mixture into two bowls. Mix cocoa powder into one half to create chocolate ice cream base, then alternate layering chocolate and peanut butter mixtures in your container, swirling with a knife before freezing.

Peanut Butter and Jelly

Fold in spoonfuls of strawberry or raspberry jam during the folding stage, creating pockets of fruity brightness in every bite.

Vegan No-Churn Option

Substitute heavy cream with full-fat coconut cream whipped until fluffy, and use a dairy-free sweetened condensed milk or make your own homemade version with coconut milk and sweetener.

Nutrition Snapshot (per serving, roughly 1/2 cup)

  • Calories: ~270
  • Fat: 22g
  • Carbohydrates: 15g
  • Protein: 6g
  • Sugar: 14g

This treat is rich and satisfying, making it an excellent choice for dessert or a special snack. Portion control is recommended due to its dense richness.

Conclusion

No-churn peanut butter ice cream offers an unbeatable combination of creamy texture, rich peanut flavor, and effortless preparation. Perfect for summer days, celebrations, or unexpected guests, this homemade dessert will become a favorite in your kitchen. With simple ingredients and no need for fancy equipment, it democratizes the joy of ice cream making. Experiment with mix-ins, create your own swirls, and enjoy endless variations of this classic peanut butter delight.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your whisk, a can of sweetened condensed milk, and a jar of your favorite peanut butter, and dive into creamy heaven today!
