Italian-Style Baked Ziti Recipe with Meatballs and Ricotta


Italian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, aromas, and hearty portions. One of the most beloved dishes to come out of Italy is baked ziti, a classic comfort food that never fails to satisfy. In this recipe, we’re taking the traditional baked ziti to new heights by adding homemade meatballs and a creamy ricotta cheese sauce.

The Magic of Meatballs

Meatballs are a staple in Italian cuisine, and for good reason. They add a depth of flavor and texture to any dish. In this recipe, we’re using a combination of ground beef, pork, and breadcrumbs to create meatballs that are both tender and flavorful. To make the meatballs, simply combine the ground meats, breadcrumbs, egg, and seasonings in a bowl. Mix everything together until just combined, being careful not to overmix. Use your hands to shape the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for 18-20 minutes, or until cooked through.

The Power of Ricotta Cheese

Ricotta cheese is a staple in Italian cooking, and for good reason. It adds a creamy texture and a subtle flavor to any dish. In this recipe, we’re using ricotta cheese to create a creamy sauce that complements the meatballs and ziti perfectly. To make the ricotta sauce, simply combine the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, and seasonings in a bowl. Mix everything together until smooth and creamy.

Assembling the Dish

Now it’s time to assemble the dish. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the ziti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the ziti and set it aside. In a large skillet, combine the meatballs and marinara sauce. Simmer the meatballs in the sauce for about 10 minutes, or until they’re fully coated. In a large bowl, combine the cooked ziti, meatballs, and ricotta sauce. Mix everything together until the ziti is well coated with the sauce.

The Final Touches

Transfer the ziti mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish. Top the ziti with the shredded mozzarella cheese and bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

The Verdict

This Italian-Style Baked Ziti Recipe with Meatballs and Ricotta is a game-changer. The combination of homemade meatballs, creamy ricotta sauce, and al dente ziti is a match made in heaven. This dish is perfect for a family dinner, a special occasion, or even a cozy night in. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the magic of Italian cuisine for yourself.

Recipe Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 pound ziti pasta
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for greasing the baking dish

Recipe Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Make the meatballs: Combine the ground meats, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan cheese, parsley, garlic, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Mix everything together until just combined. Use your hands to shape the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until cooked through.
  3. Make the ricotta sauce: Combine the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, and seasonings in a bowl. Mix everything together until smooth and creamy.
  4. Cook the ziti: Cook the ziti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the ziti and set it aside.
  5. Assemble the dish: In a large skillet, combine the meatballs and marinara sauce. Simmer the meatballs in the sauce for about 10 minutes, or until they’re fully coated. In a large bowl, combine the cooked ziti, meatballs, and ricotta sauce. Mix everything together until the ziti is well coated with the sauce.
  6. Transfer the ziti mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish. Top the ziti with the shredded mozzarella cheese and bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations:

  • To make the dish more substantial, add some sautéed mushrooms or bell peppers to the meatballs and sauce.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the ground meats with roasted eggplant or zucchini.
  • To make the dish more spicy, add some red pepper flakes to the meatballs and sauce.
  • To make the dish more creamy, add some heavy cream or half-and-half to the ricotta sauce.
