Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Pork: A Fiery and Flavorful Dish


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’re probably no stranger to the bold and vibrant flavors that characterize many of its dishes. One such dish that perfectly embodies the essence of Korean cooking is the spicy stir-fried pork, also known as “dwaejigogi-bokkeum.” This fiery and flavorful dish is a staple in Korean households and is beloved for its intense heat and rich umami taste. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the world of Korean spicy stir-fried pork, exploring its history, key ingredients, and providing you with a step-by-step recipe to recreate this delectable dish in your own kitchen.

A Brief History of Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Pork

Korean spicy stir-fried pork has a long-standing history in Korean cuisine, with its roots tracing back to the Joseon Dynasty. Pork has been a significant part of Korean culinary culture for centuries, and the art of stir-frying meat with a spicy marinade has been perfected over generations. The dish gained popularity as a comfort food among Korean families and has since become a beloved classic in Korean cuisine. Its fiery and robust flavors have made it a favorite at both home-cooked meals and restaurants across Korea and beyond.

Essential Ingredients for Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Pork

To create an authentic and mouthwatering Korean spicy stir-fried pork, you’ll need a handful of essential ingredients that contribute to its signature taste and texture. The star of the dish is, of course, the pork, which is typically cut into thin slices to ensure quick and even cooking. For the marinade, you’ll need a combination of gochujang (Korean red chili paste), gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes), soy sauce, minced garlic, ginger, and a touch of sweetness from sugar or honey. Additionally, the dish often includes sliced onions, green onions, and sesame oil, which add depth and complexity to the flavor profile.

Recipe: Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Pork

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 4-5 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Cooking oil
  • Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the thinly sliced pork with the minced garlic, grated ginger, gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, and sugar (or honey). Mix well, ensuring that the pork is evenly coated with the marinade. Allow the pork to marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat and add a drizzle of cooking oil. Once the pan is hot, add the marinated pork and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the pork is cooked through and caramelized.

  3. Add the sliced onions to the pan and continue stir-frying for an additional 2-3 minutes until the onions are softened and slightly translucent.

  4. Toss in the green onions and drizzle the sesame oil over the pork. Stir-fry for another minute to combine all the ingredients and allow the flavors to meld.

  5. Transfer the spicy stir-fried pork to a serving dish and garnish with toasted sesame seeds.

  6. Serve the Korean spicy stir-fried pork hot with steamed rice and banchan (Korean side dishes) for a complete and satisfying meal.

In Conclusion

Korean spicy stir-fried pork, with its explosive flavors and hearty appeal, is a dish that captures the essence of Korean cuisine. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or looking to expand your culinary repertoire, this fiery and flavorful dish is sure to impress. By following this recipe and using high-quality ingredients, you can recreate the authentic taste of Korean spicy stir-fried pork in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the bold and irresistible flavors of Korean cuisine.
