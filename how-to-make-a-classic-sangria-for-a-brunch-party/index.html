How to Make a Classic Sangria for a Brunch Party


Brunch parties are the perfect excuse to indulge in delicious food, good conversation, and of course, refreshing drinks. One classic beverage that never fails to elevate any brunch gathering is sangria. Whether you’re hosting a casual get-together with friends or a more formal brunch, sangria is an ideal choice. Its vibrant color, fruity flavors, and versatility make it the perfect drink to serve alongside a variety of dishes. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make a classic sangria that will delight your guests and ensure your brunch party is a hit.

What is Sangria?

Sangria is a traditional Spanish punch that’s typically made with red wine, fresh fruit, and a variety of additional ingredients to give it a unique twist. It is perfect for gatherings because it can be prepared in advance and served in large batches, making it ideal for brunches, parties, and other social events.

The beauty of sangria lies in its flexibility. You can tailor it to suit your taste preferences or dietary needs by experimenting with different fruits, wines, and spirits. Though there are many variations of sangria, the classic version uses red wine as its base and is mixed with citrus fruits, berries, and sometimes a splash of brandy or liqueur for added depth.

Why Sangria is Perfect for a Brunch Party

Sangria is the ideal brunch drink for several reasons:

  • Ease of Preparation: Unlike cocktails that require mixing individual drinks for every guest, sangria can be made in large batches ahead of time. This means less time behind the bar and more time enjoying the party.
  • Versatility: You can make sangria to suit any brunch theme. Whether you’re serving light and fresh fare or heartier dishes, sangria complements a wide range of foods.
  • Refreshingly Fruity: Sangria offers a delightful balance of wine and fruit, making it a refreshing choice for daytime gatherings like brunch.
  • Customizable: There are no hard-and-fast rules when it comes to sangria. You can experiment with different fruits, wines, and liqueurs to match your personal tastes or seasonal ingredients.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a classic sangria, you’ll need just a few essential ingredients. Below is a list of what you’ll need to make enough sangria for about 6-8 servings:

For the Sangria Base:

  • 1 bottle of red wine (750 ml): Opt for a medium-bodied red wine such as Tempranillo, Merlot, or Cabernet Sauvignon. These wines provide a rich base that balances the sweetness of the fruit and any added spirits. Avoid wines that are too tannic or overly complex, as they can overwhelm the fruit flavors.
  • 1/4 cup brandy: Brandy adds a subtle depth and warmth to the sangria. It also helps to enhance the fruity notes in the wine. If you prefer a non-alcoholic version, you can skip the brandy or replace it with a fruit juice like orange juice.
  • 1/4 cup orange liqueur (optional): Adding orange liqueur, such as Cointreau or Triple Sec, can give your sangria an extra citrusy zing. This is an optional ingredient, but it can really elevate the flavor.

Fruits and Flavorings:

  • 1 orange (sliced): Oranges are a classic ingredient in sangria. Their citrusy brightness complements the richness of the red wine.
  • 1 lemon (sliced): Lemons add a tart and zesty element, balancing the sweetness of the other fruits.
  • 1 apple (cored and sliced): A crisp apple adds a touch of sweetness and texture to the sangria. Green apples are ideal for a slightly tart contrast.
  • 1 cup berries (strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries): Berries add vibrant color and a burst of sweetness. You can choose one type or a mix, depending on your preference.
  • 1 cinnamon stick: A cinnamon stick infuses the sangria with a subtle warmth that pairs perfectly with the fruit.

Additional Options:

  • Sparkling water or soda: If you prefer a fizzy sangria, you can top off your drink with sparkling water, soda, or lemon-lime soda just before serving.
  • Sweetener: If you like your sangria on the sweeter side, you can add a little honey, agave syrup, or simple syrup to taste. Start with a small amount and adjust to your liking.

How to Make Classic Sangria: Step-by-Step Instructions

Making sangria is incredibly simple and can be done in just a few easy steps. The key is to give the flavors enough time to meld together, so it’s best to prepare the sangria ahead of time. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Prepare the Fruit

Start by washing and slicing your fruits. You’ll want to cut the orange and lemon into thin slices or wedges, ensuring the pieces are large enough to infuse the sangria but not so large that they overpower the drink. For the apple, slice it into thin wedges or cubes. Be sure to core the apple before slicing. If you’re using berries, simply wash them and remove any stems.

Step 2: Combine the Wine, Brandy, and Liqueur

In a large pitcher or bowl, pour the entire bottle of red wine. Add the brandy and orange liqueur (if using). Stir gently to combine. These ingredients form the base of your sangria, so it’s important to ensure they’re well-mixed from the start.

Step 3: Add the Fruits

Once the base is prepared, it’s time to add your fruit. Gently place the orange, lemon, apple, and berries into the pitcher or bowl. The fruit should be fully submerged in the wine mixture. Stir gently to combine, being careful not to crush the fruit.

Step 4: Add the Cinnamon Stick

Toss in the cinnamon stick to infuse the sangria with a subtle spiced flavor. The cinnamon will add a comforting warmth that makes the sangria perfect for any season, whether it’s a spring brunch or a winter gathering.

Step 5: Refrigerate and Let it Chill

Cover the pitcher or bowl and place it in the refrigerator. Let the sangria chill for at least 4-6 hours, but ideally overnight. The longer it sits, the more the flavors will meld together, creating a harmonious blend of wine, fruit, and spice. If you’re in a rush, you can chill it for as little as an hour, but overnight refrigeration is recommended for the best results.

Step 6: Serve

Before serving, give the sangria a gentle stir. If you prefer your sangria to be sparkling, top it off with sparkling water or soda right before pouring it into glasses. To serve, fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the sangria into each glass. Be sure to include some of the fruit in each serving for a burst of flavor and a pretty presentation.

Optional Garnishes

To add an extra touch, garnish each glass with a fresh slice of orange or lemon, or even a sprig of mint. These garnishes not only make your sangria look more inviting but also enhance the refreshing citrus aroma.

Tips for the Best Classic Sangria

  • Use Good-Quality Wine: Since sangria is wine-based, the quality of the wine matters. While you don’t need to splurge on an expensive bottle, avoid using wines that are too cheap, as they might have harsh or overly sweet flavors.
  • Adjust the Sweetness: Taste your sangria before serving and adjust the sweetness if needed. If it’s too tart, you can add a sweetener like honey or simple syrup to taste. If it’s too sweet, balance it out with a little extra lemon juice.
  • Let the Sangria Sit: The key to great sangria is letting it sit and marinate for several hours. This gives the fruit time to release its juices and infuse the wine, making the drink even more flavorful.
  • Use Seasonal Fruits: For a fun twist, you can swap in seasonal fruits. In the summer, consider adding peaches, cherries, or watermelon for a fresh flavor, while autumn could bring in figs, pears, or pomegranates.
  • Make it Ahead: Sangria is best when made ahead, so plan to prepare it the day before your brunch. This allows the flavors to come together, making the drink even more delicious.

Pairing Sangria with Brunch Foods

Classic sangria pairs wonderfully with a variety of brunch dishes. Here are some ideas to consider:

  • Cheese Platters: The fruity notes in the sangria pair well with a range of cheeses, from soft brie to sharp cheddar.
  • Pastries and Muffins: Sweet pastries, croissants, and fruit-filled muffins complement the refreshing qualities of sangria.
  • Charcuterie: A selection of cured meats, olives, and pickles balances the sweetness of the sangria and adds a savory element to your brunch.
  • Egg Dishes: Whether it’s scrambled eggs, omelets, or a quiche, sangria’s light, fruity flavor is a refreshing contrast to the richness of egg-based dishes.
  • Fruit Salad: Since sangria is made with fresh fruit, serving a fruit salad on the side creates a delightful theme that your guests will appreciate.

Conclusion

Sangria is a fantastic addition to any brunch party. With its fruity, refreshing flavor and vibrant color, it’s a crowd-pleaser that’s easy to prepare and perfect for making in large batches. By following this simple recipe and using fresh, high-quality ingredients, you’ll be able to craft a classic sangria that will keep your guests coming back for more. So go ahead, get creative with the fruits and flavors you use, and enjoy a beautifully crafted beverage that complements your next brunch gathering. Cheers to good times, great food, and unforgettable sangria!
