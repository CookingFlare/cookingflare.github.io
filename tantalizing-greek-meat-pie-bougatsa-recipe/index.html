Tantalizing Greek Meat Pie (Bougatsa) Recipe


Greece is known for its rich culinary heritage, and one of the most beloved dishes in Greek cuisine is the Bougatsa. This delectable pastry is a savory meat pie that captures the essence of Greek flavors in every bite. Whether enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, Bougatsa is versatile, satisfying, and a true crowd-pleaser. In this blog post, we will take you through an authentic recipe for Tantalizing Greek Meat Pie (Bougatsa), including tips and tricks to make it perfect every time.

What is Bougatsa?

Bougatsa is a traditional Greek pastry that typically features a flaky phyllo dough enveloping various fillings, including cheese, custard, or meat. Originating from the northern regions of Greece, particularly Thessaloniki, Bougatsa has become popular throughout the country. The meat version, filled with ground beef or lamb and spices, is particularly loved for its hearty and comforting qualities.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather the ingredients you will need for this mouthwatering Bougatsa. The following list will serve approximately 6 to 8 servings.

For the Filling:

  • 500g ground beef (or lamb)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup chicken or beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

For the Phyllo Dough:

  • 500g phyllo dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 250g unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

For Garnish:

  • Powdered sugar (for sweet version, optional)
  • Ground cinnamon (for sweet version, optional)

Preparing the Filling

  1. Cooking the Meat: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until they become translucent and fragrant, about 3 to 4 minutes.

  2. Browning the Meat: Increase the heat to medium-high and add the ground beef (or lamb) to the skillet. Break the meat apart with a wooden spoon and cook until browned, which should take about 5 to 7 minutes.

  3. Adding Spices: Once the meat is browned, stir in the ground cinnamon, nutmeg, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine the spices evenly throughout the meat.

  4. Simmering: Pour in the chicken or beef broth and let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes until the liquid has reduced and thickened slightly. If you’d like to add some freshness, stir in the chopped parsley at this stage.

  5. Cooling the Filling: Remove the skillet from heat and allow the filling to cool completely. This step is crucial as it prevents the phyllo from becoming soggy when assembled.

Preparing the Phyllo Dough

If you are using store-bought phyllo dough, make sure to thaw it in the refrigerator overnight prior to use. If you prefer to make your own, follow a simple phyllo dough recipe, but keep in mind that it requires time and patience.

  1. Working with Phyllo: Once your filling is cool, preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Lay out your phyllo dough on a clean surface. Keep the sheets covered with a damp kitchen towel to prevent them from drying out.

  2. Layering the Phyllo: Take one sheet of phyllo and brush it generously with melted butter. Add another sheet on top and repeat the buttering process until you have about 5-6 layers. This will ensure a flaky and crispy texture.

  3. Adding the Filling: Spoon a line of the cooled meat filling along one edge of the layered phyllo, leaving some space on the sides for folding.

  4. Rolling the Dough: Carefully roll the phyllo over the filling to form a tight log. Once rolled, tuck the ends underneath to seal the filling inside. If you prefer individual servings, you can cut the rolled phyllo into smaller pieces before baking.

Baking the Bougatsa

  1. Egg Wash: Place the rolled Bougatsa on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the top with beaten egg for a golden finish.

  2. Baking Time: Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy. The aroma will fill your kitchen, making it hard to resist!

  3. Cooling: Once baked, remove the Bougatsa from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. This resting time allows the filling to set and makes it easier to serve.

Serving Suggestions

Bougatsa can be served as a main dish or as an appetizer. Here are a few ideas to elevate your serving experience:

  • With Tzatziki: Serve alongside a refreshing tzatziki sauce made with yogurt, cucumber, garlic, and herbs for a delicious contrast of flavors.

  • Garnish with Herbs: Sprinkle some fresh parsley or dill on top for a pop of color and freshness.

  • Sweet Twist: For a sweet variation, consider adding powdered sugar and a dash of cinnamon on top before serving. This creates a delightful contrast to the savory filling.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (though they are unlikely given how delicious Bougatsa is), they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, place them in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 10-15 minutes until heated through and crispy again.

Conclusion

Bougatsa is not just a meal; it’s an experience that brings the warmth of Greek hospitality to your table. The combination of flaky phyllo pastry and savory meat filling is simply irresistible. Making Tantalizing Greek Meat Pie (Bougatsa) from scratch may seem daunting, but with this comprehensive recipe and step-by-step instructions, you’ll find it to be a rewarding culinary adventure. Share this dish with family and friends and transport them to the sun-soaked streets of Greece with every bite. Enjoy the journey of cooking and the joy of sharing this timeless Greek delicacy!
