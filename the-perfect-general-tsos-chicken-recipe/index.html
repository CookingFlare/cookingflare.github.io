The Perfect General Tso's Chicken Recipe


General Tso’s Chicken is a beloved dish that has captured the hearts and appetites of food lovers around the world. This Chinese-American dish is known for its deliciously crispy chicken, paired with a flavorful sauce that strikes a perfect balance between sweet, savory, and spicy. While you can find General Tso’s Chicken in many Chinese restaurants, making it at home allows you to enjoy this indulgent dish fresh from your own kitchen.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create the perfect General Tso’s Chicken. We’ll cover everything from selecting the right ingredients to frying the chicken, preparing the sauce, and assembling it all together for a restaurant-quality meal that will wow your taste buds.

Ingredients for General Tso’s Chicken

For the Chicken:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken thighs (about 1 lb or 450 grams)
  • Cornstarch (for dredging the chicken)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs (2 large)
  • Baking powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (½ teaspoon)
  • Pepper (¼ teaspoon)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sauce:

  • Soy sauce (¼ cup)
  • Rice vinegar (3 tablespoons)
  • Brown sugar (2 tablespoons)
  • Hoison sauce (2 tablespoons)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Ginger (1 teaspoon, freshly grated or minced)
  • Chili flakes or dried red chili peppers (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Sesame oil (1 teaspoon)
  • Cornstarch (1 tablespoon mixed with 2 tablespoons water, to thicken the sauce)
  • Chicken broth (¼ cup)

For Garnishing:

  • Sesame seeds (optional)
  • Chopped green onions (optional)

Preparation: How to Make General Tso’s Chicken

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

To create the perfect texture for your General Tso’s Chicken, you’ll want to start with boneless, skinless chicken thighs. Thighs are preferred over chicken breasts because they stay moist and juicy even after frying. Cut the chicken into bite-sized chunks, ensuring uniform pieces to cook evenly.

Once cut, place the chicken pieces in a large bowl. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, cornstarch, flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper. This batter will provide the crispy coating we’re looking for. Toss the chicken pieces in this batter until they are fully coated. The batter should be thick enough to cling to the chicken but not too heavy.

Step 2: Heat the Oil for Frying

Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. The oil needs to reach a temperature of about 350°F (175°C) to fry the chicken to perfection. To check if the oil is ready, drop a small amount of batter into the oil; if it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is hot enough.

Fry the battered chicken pieces in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry each batch for 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. Remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

While the chicken is frying, it’s time to prepare the sweet and savory sauce that will coat the crispy chicken. In a separate bowl, mix together soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, hoisin sauce, and chicken broth. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely.

In a small pan, heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and ginger, and sauté them for about 30 seconds, or until fragrant. Add the soy sauce mixture to the pan and bring it to a simmer. Once simmering, mix the cornstarch with water to create a slurry, then slowly pour it into the sauce while stirring. This will thicken the sauce and give it that rich, glossy texture.

If you enjoy a spicy kick, now is the time to add chili flakes or dried red chili peppers to the sauce. Stir the sauce well and let it simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes until it reaches your desired consistency.

Step 4: Combine Chicken with Sauce

Now that your chicken is perfectly crispy and the sauce is simmering, it’s time to bring everything together. Add the fried chicken pieces into the sauce and toss them carefully to coat each piece evenly. The sauce should adhere to the chicken, giving it that shiny, mouthwatering finish.

Allow the chicken to sit in the sauce for 2-3 minutes to absorb the flavors, but be careful not to let the chicken become soggy. The goal is to coat each piece with sauce while maintaining its crispy texture.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken is perfectly coated in the sauce, it’s time to plate and garnish. Transfer the General Tso’s Chicken to a serving dish and sprinkle with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for added flavor and a pop of color.

Serve your General Tso’s Chicken hot with a side of steamed white rice or fried rice. The crispy chicken combined with the savory, tangy sauce creates a flavor profile that is truly irresistible.

Tips for Perfecting General Tso’s Chicken

1. Use Chicken Thighs: Chicken thighs are juicier and more flavorful than breasts, making them perfect for this dish. They hold up better when fried and provide the rich texture that makes General Tso’s Chicken so irresistible.

2. Double Fry for Extra Crispiness: For an even crunchier coating, consider double-frying the chicken. After frying the first batch, let the chicken rest for a few minutes, then fry it again for 1-2 minutes. This extra step will give the chicken an extra layer of crispiness.

3. Adjust the Spice Level: You can easily control the heat in your General Tso’s Chicken by adjusting the amount of chili flakes or red pepper flakes in the sauce. For a milder version, use fewer peppers or skip them entirely. For a spicier version, feel free to add extra chili flakes or use fresh chopped chilies.

4. Use Fresh Garlic and Ginger: Fresh garlic and ginger will provide a much more vibrant and aromatic flavor compared to their powdered or jarred counterparts. Don’t skip this step if you want to truly elevate your dish.

5. Don’t Overcook the Chicken: Overcooking the chicken can cause it to become tough and dry. Fry the chicken just until it turns golden brown and crispy. The chicken will continue to cook slightly once you add it to the sauce, so there’s no need to overdo it during frying.

6. Keep the Sauce Thick: The sauce for General Tso’s Chicken should be thick enough to coat the chicken pieces without running off. If it’s too thin, it won’t adhere properly. Adjust the cornstarch slurry to achieve the perfect consistency.

7. Serve Immediately: General Tso’s Chicken is best served fresh. The crispy texture of the chicken will start to soften the longer it sits in the sauce. If you’re making this dish ahead of time, it’s better to store the chicken and sauce separately and combine them just before serving.

Pairing Suggestions

1. Fried Rice: A classic pairing with General Tso’s Chicken is fried rice. The rich and savory flavors of the chicken sauce complement the simplicity of fried rice, making it the perfect side dish.

2. Steamed Vegetables: For a lighter side, steamed vegetables such as broccoli, bok choy, or snap peas are a great choice. Their freshness and crunch balance out the richness of the chicken.

3. Egg Rolls or Spring Rolls: If you’re craving something crispy on the side, egg rolls or spring rolls make a great addition to the meal. The crunchiness of the rolls pairs nicely with the tender chicken.

4. Hot and Sour Soup: For a complete Chinese feast, serve your General Tso’s Chicken with a bowl of hot and sour soup. The spicy and tangy broth will complement the sweetness of the chicken sauce.

Conclusion

Now that you have the perfect recipe for General Tso’s Chicken, it’s time to put it to the test! With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can recreate this iconic dish at home. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner or impressing guests with your cooking skills, this recipe will surely become a staple in your kitchen.

The key to a truly great General Tso’s Chicken lies in the balance of flavors and textures—the crispy, golden chicken combined with the sweet, tangy, and spicy sauce. Once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll have the confidence to make this restaurant favorite whenever the craving strikes.

Happy cooking!
