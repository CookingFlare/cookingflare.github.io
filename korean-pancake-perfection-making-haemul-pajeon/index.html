Korean Pancake Perfection: Making Haemul Pajeon


Haemul Pajeon, also known as Korean-style seafood pancake, is a beloved dish in Korea that has gained popularity worldwide. This savory pancake is made with a delicate batter of flour, water, and scallions, and is loaded with a variety of seafood such as clams, mussels, and shrimp. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making Haemul Pajeon from scratch, exploring the secrets to achieving the perfect balance of crispy edges and tender centers.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup ice-cold water
  • 1/4 cup scallions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup clams, mussels, and shrimp, cleaned and chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Optional: additional seasonings such as sesame oil, garlic, and ginger

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Batter

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add in the ice-cold water, stirring with a wooden spoon until the batter is smooth and free of lumps. Add the chopped scallions and mix well.

Step 2: Prepare the Seafood

In a separate bowl, combine the chopped clams, mussels, and shrimp. Rinse the seafood under cold running water to remove any impurities. Pat the seafood dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.

Step 3: Combine the Batter and Seafood

Add the seafood to the batter and mix until well combined. The batter should still be slightly lumpy, but the seafood should be evenly distributed.

Step 4: Heat the Pan

Heat a non-stick pan or a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of vegetable oil to the pan and swirl it around to coat the bottom. Once the oil is hot, pour in about 1/4 cup of the batter mixture.

Step 5: Cook the Pancake

Cook the pancake for about 2-3 minutes, until the edges start to curl and the surface is dry. Loosely lift the edges of the pancake with a spatula and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked batter to flow to the top. Repeat this process until the pancake is almost cooked through.

Step 6: Flip and Cook the Other Side

Flip the pancake over and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the other side is golden brown. Repeat the process with the remaining batter mixture.

Step 7: Serve

Serve the Haemul Pajeon hot with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar, or with a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

Tips and Variations

  • To achieve the perfect balance of crispy edges and tender centers, make sure to not overmix the batter and to cook the pancake over medium-high heat.
  • For an added layer of flavor, add a small amount of sesame oil, garlic, and ginger to the batter mixture.
  • Experiment with different types of seafood, such as squid or octopus, for a unique twist on the traditional recipe.
  • For a gluten-free version, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.

By following these steps and tips, you will be well on your way to making Haemul Pajeon that rivals those found in Korean restaurants. With its crispy edges, tender centers, and savory seafood filling, this dish is sure to become a new favorite in your household. So go ahead, get cooking, and indulge in the flavors of Korea!
