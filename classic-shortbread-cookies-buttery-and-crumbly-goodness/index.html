Classic Shortbread Cookies: Buttery and Crumbly Goodness


Shortbread cookies are a beloved classic that never fails to bring comfort and joy. With their buttery, crumbly texture and delicate sweetness, these cookies are perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to satisfy your sweet tooth or impress your friends and family, these Classic Shortbread Cookies are sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon vanilla extract or other flavorings (e.g., lemon zest, almond extract)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or silicone baking mat.

  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. You can use an electric mixer or do it by hand, but make sure the ingredients are well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add the dry mixture to the butter mixture, stirring until a crumbly dough forms. If desired, add in your choice of flavorings, such as vanilla extract or lemon zest.

  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it until it comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough, as this can result in tough cookies.

  5. Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/4 inch (0.6 cm). You can use a rolling pin or simply press the dough down with your hands. If the dough becomes too sticky, lightly dust it with flour.

  6. Cut the dough into desired shapes using cookie cutters or a sharp knife. Traditional shortbread cookies are often shaped into rectangles or rounds, but feel free to get creative with your shapes!

  7. Carefully transfer the cut-out cookies onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a small gap between each cookie. Shortbread cookies don’t spread much during baking, so you can place them relatively close together.

  8. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the edges turn a light golden brown. Keep a close eye on them to prevent overbaking, as shortbread cookies should remain pale in color.

  9. Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. Transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

  10. Enjoy your homemade Classic Shortbread Cookies with a hot cup of tea or coffee, or share them with loved ones for a delightful treat.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of sweetness, you can dust the cooled cookies with powdered sugar or drizzle them with melted chocolate.
  • Experiment with different flavorings, such as adding a teaspoon of almond extract or finely grated lemon zest to the dough.
  • Feel free to get creative with the cookie shapes! Use seasonal cookie cutters for festive treats or try your hand at free-hand designs.
  • If you prefer a crispier texture, you can bake the cookies for a few minutes longer until they are slightly golden all over.
  • These cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, making them perfect for gifting or snacking on the go.

So why wait? Indulge in the buttery and crumbly goodness of Classic Shortbread Cookies. They’re simple to make, delicious to eat, and guaranteed to become a household favorite. Enjoy the process of baking these timeless treats and savor every bite. Happy baking!
