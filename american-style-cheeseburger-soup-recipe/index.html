American-Style Cheeseburger Soup Recipe


If you’re craving the irresistible taste of a cheeseburger but want something a little more comforting and satisfying, look no further than this American-Style Cheeseburger Soup. Packed with all the flavors of a juicy cheeseburger, this creamy and hearty soup is the ultimate comfort food. Whether you’re looking for a cozy meal on a chilly evening or a dish that will impress your family and friends, this recipe is sure to hit the spot.

In this post, we’ll take you through every step of making this scrumptious soup. From browning the beef to creating that perfectly creamy texture, we’ve got all the tips and tricks to make sure your cheeseburger soup is a success. Let’s dive in!

Why You’ll Love This American-Style Cheeseburger Soup

American-Style Cheeseburger Soup is the perfect mashup of a classic cheeseburger and a delicious soup. Here are a few reasons why this dish will become a family favorite:

  1. Hearty and Filling: The combination of beef, potatoes, and cheese makes this soup incredibly filling. It’s the kind of meal that warms you up and keeps you satisfied for hours.

  2. Creamy Comfort: The creamy base of this soup gives you all the rich, comforting flavors of a cheeseburger, without the need for a bun.

  3. Simple Ingredients: With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a delicious and satisfying soup in no time. Plus, most of the ingredients are pantry staples!

  4. Kid-Friendly: The flavors of this soup are familiar and comforting for kids. Plus, it’s a great way to sneak in some veggies.

  5. Customizable: You can easily adjust this recipe to suit your tastes. Add extra cheese, swap out veggies, or even use different types of ground meat. The options are endless!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this American-Style Cheeseburger Soup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Soup:

  • 1 lb ground beef (you can use lean or regular beef, depending on your preference)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups potatoes, peeled and diced (Yukon gold potatoes work great)
  • 3 cups beef broth (for that rich, meaty flavor)
  • 1 cup whole milk (for a creamy consistency)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (or a mix of cheddar and mozzarella)
  • 1 cup sour cream (adds a tangy richness to the soup)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (for a bit of tang and depth)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for sautéing)
  • Optional garnishes: crumbled crispy bacon, chopped green onions, extra cheese, or pickles.

Optional Vegetables:

You can also add some veggies for extra nutrition and flavor. Consider adding:

  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup celery, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas, for a burst of color and sweetness

How to Make American-Style Cheeseburger Soup

Making this delicious soup is easy and requires just a few simple steps. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Brown the Ground Beef

Start by heating 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon as it cooks. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Once browned, remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté for 2-3 minutes, until softened and fragrant. If you’re using carrots, celery, or any other veggies, add them at this point and sauté for an additional 5 minutes. The vegetables should begin to soften.

Step 3: Add the Potatoes and Broth

Once the vegetables are tender, add the diced potatoes to the pot, followed by the beef broth. Bring everything to a simmer over medium heat. Let it cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.

Step 4: Stir in the Cream and Mustard

Once the potatoes are tender, it’s time to add the creamy elements to the soup. Reduce the heat to low and add the whole milk and sour cream. Stir well to combine. Then, stir in the Dijon mustard for an extra layer of flavor.

Step 5: Return the Beef to the Pot

Now, return the cooked ground beef to the pot. Stir to combine all the ingredients and let the soup simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 6: Add the Cheese

The final step is to add the cheese. Stir in the shredded cheddar (and mozzarella, if using) a little at a time, allowing it to melt into the soup. Keep stirring until the soup is creamy and smooth. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the soup is creamy, cheesy, and fully combined, ladle it into bowls. Top with any optional garnishes, like crispy bacon, chopped green onions, extra cheese, or pickles. Serve with crusty bread for dipping and enjoy the comforting, satisfying flavors of this American-Style Cheeseburger Soup.

Tips for the Perfect Cheeseburger Soup

While this recipe is fairly simple, there are a few tips to keep in mind to ensure your soup turns out perfectly every time:

1. Choose the Right Pot:

A heavy-bottomed pot, such as a Dutch oven, works best for this recipe. It ensures even heat distribution and helps prevent burning or sticking.

2. Use Fresh Ingredients:

Fresh garlic, onions, and potatoes can make a significant difference in the flavor of the soup. While you can use frozen veggies, fresh ones tend to add a more vibrant taste.

3. Customize Your Cheese:

Feel free to experiment with different cheeses. While sharp cheddar is the most common choice, adding a bit of mozzarella or even gouda can give the soup a unique flavor profile. Just make sure to shred the cheese yourself, as pre-shredded cheese can contain anti-caking agents that affect the texture of the soup.

4. Adjust the Creaminess:

If you prefer a creamier soup, you can increase the amount of sour cream or milk. Alternatively, for a lighter version, use half-and-half instead of whole milk or reduce the amount of sour cream.

5. Make it Spicy:

If you like a little heat, you can add crushed red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the soup. Alternatively, you can mix in some diced jalapeños for a more substantial kick.

6. Storage and Reheating:

This soup stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply store it in an airtight container and reheat it on the stovetop over low heat. You may need to add a splash of milk or broth to thin it out slightly, as the soup can thicken when stored.

7. Freezing the Soup:

If you’d like to make a larger batch for later, this soup can be frozen. Allow it to cool completely before transferring it to freezer-safe containers. It will keep for up to 3 months in the freezer. To reheat, simply thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and reheat it on the stove.

Pairing Suggestions

This American-Style Cheeseburger Soup is a meal on its own, but if you’re looking to round out the dish, here are a few side ideas:

1. Garlic Bread:

A side of warm, buttery garlic bread is always a crowd-pleaser. It’s perfect for dipping into the cheesy soup.

2. Salad:

A light, crisp salad with a tangy vinaigrette can provide a refreshing contrast to the rich and creamy soup.

3. Pickles:

Since cheeseburgers often come with pickles, why not serve some on the side? The acidity of the pickles pairs wonderfully with the richness of the soup.

4. French Fries:

For the ultimate indulgence, serve your soup with crispy, golden fries. They’re a great complement to the cheeseburger flavor.

Final Thoughts

This American-Style Cheeseburger Soup is the perfect way to enjoy all the familiar, comforting flavors of a cheeseburger in a warm, hearty soup form. With its creamy texture, rich beef flavor, and melty cheese, this soup is sure to become a staple in your recipe collection. Whether you’re serving it for a weeknight dinner or a weekend get-together, it’s guaranteed to satisfy.

So, grab your ingredients and get ready to make a bowl of this delicious cheeseburger soup that your whole family will love. Happy cooking!
