Step Up Your Italian Cooking Game with these Essential Tools


Italian cuisine is renowned worldwide for its simplicity, quality, and bold flavors. To truly master the art of Italian cooking, utilizing the proper tools is essential. While you can achieve success with just a handful of well-chosen pieces, investing in a few top-notch tools will make your culinary experience even more enjoyable. In this blog post, we introduce you to some essential Italian cooking tools that will elevate your dishes to new heights.

1. A Chef’s Knife

The cornerstone of any Italian kitchen, a damn good chef’s knife is crucial for slicing, dicing, and mincing a variety of ingredients. Look for one with a sharp, 6-8-inch blade made from high-carbon steel for the perfect balance and razor-sharp edge. Remember to sharpen your knife regularly, as a sharp knife is safer and more efficient than a dull one.

2. A Wooden Cutting Board

Nothing beats the look, feel, and durability of a quality wooden cutting board made from hard or soft woods like maple, cherry, or walnut. Besides offering a homely and rustic touch to your Italian cooking experience, a wooden cutting board prevents knife marks on your countertops and absorbs more wear and tear than plastic ones. Plus, they are more eco-friendly and biodegradable.

3. A Ladle

If you’re a fan of soups, stews, and sauces, a high-quality wooden or stainless steel ladle should be part of your Italian cooking utensil collection. With a ladle, you can easily serve soups, pasta water, and sauce without making a mess, and they come in different sizes to best fit your needs.

4. A Spaghetti Server

What’s Italian cuisine without pasta? No pasta, no Italian cooking! A spaghetti server will make serving your homemade Italian pasta a breeze. Designed with both comfort and style, a spaghetti server typically features a long, flat handle and a deep scoop to catch the noodles, preventing them from flying all over the place and ensuring that your guests’ plates look as good as they taste.

5. An Olive Wood Pizza Peel

A pizza peel is a must if you’re serious about recreating authentic Italian pizza in your own kitchen. Made from olive wood, this multi-purpose tool is perfect for sliding pizzas in and out of your oven. It can also be used for serving bread, roasted vegetables, and more.

6. A Copper Buzzerbeater

A copper buzzerbeater (or cuddy beater or impasti) is an essential tool for making homemade pasta. It’s used to ensure the air is worked out of the pasta dough, resulting in a smooth and elastic texture. This traditional tool will help elevate your pasta-making game, making your Italian dishes even more authentic.

7. A Pizza/Cooking stone

Italian cuisine is known for its flavorful crusts, and a pizza or cooking stone is an excellent investment if you want to achieve that authentic homemade taste and texture. Preheat it in the oven, place your cooked dough on it, and witness the transformative effect it has on your baked goods.

8. A Garlic Press

Garlic is a staple in Italian cuisine, often adding pungent and flavorful notes to sauces, share, and other dishes. A reliable garlic press will give you a consistent texture, ensuring the perfect amount of potency in every dish you create. Made of sturdy materials, this tool crushes garlic cloves effortlessly, making meal prep a breeze.

9. A Wooden Pasta Drying Rack

If you enjoy making homemade pasta, a wooden drying rack is a smart investment. Wooden racks help retain the pasta’s shape and allow airflow to dry evenly, extending its shelf life. Compared to paper towels or parchment paper, a wooden rack is a more sustainable and hygienic option.

10. A Rolling Pin

Nothing beats the sensation of a smooth, well-balanced wooden rolling pin when making fresh homemade pasta or kneading pizza dough. As with the cutting board and drying rack, wooden rolling pins offer an eco-friendly alternative that’s sturdier and more durable than their plastic counterparts.

By incorporating these essential Italian tools into your cooking repertoire, you’ll be well-equipped to create exceptional homemade Italian dishes. So go ahead, turn on the music, and embark on a culinary adventure that will impress even the most discerning of Italian Nonnas.

Happy cooking!
