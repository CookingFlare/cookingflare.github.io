Easy Asian-Inspired Recipes for Two


When the craving for bold flavors hits but you’re only cooking for two, it can be tricky to whip up something satisfying without ending up with multiple leftovers or spending hours in the kitchen. Asian-inspired dishes are perfect for small-scale meals—they’re rich in flavor, often quick to prepare, and beautifully balance fresh ingredients with savory sauces.

Below, you’ll find an engaging collection of easy Asian-inspired recipes for two. These dishes bring vibrant tastes from various Asian cuisines straight to your table without overwhelming you with complexity or volume. Each recipe offers step-by-step guidance tailored for two servings, meaning less waste and fresher meals.

1. Quick Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • For the teriyaki sauce:
    • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon mirin (or substitute dry sherry)
    • 1 tablespoon sake (optional)
    • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
    • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (slurry)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sauce first: In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, ginger, and garlic. Set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat in a large skillet or wok. Add the chicken slices and stir-fry until cooked through, about 4-5 minutes.

  3. Toss in the broccoli, carrot, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for another 3 minutes until vegetables are just tender but still crisp.

  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken and veggies. Stir well and let it come to a simmer.

  5. Add the cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce, stirring until glossy and coated well.

  6. Remove from heat, garnish with chopped green onions, and serve hot—ideally over steamed jasmine rice.

2. Sesame Ginger Noodle Salad with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 100g (3.5 oz) rice noodles or thin soba noodles
  • 200g (7 oz) firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup shredded carrots
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or agave
  • 1 teaspoon freshly grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions, drain and rinse under cold water to stop cooking. Set aside.

  2. While noodles are cooking, heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add a little oil and pan-fry tofu cubes until golden and crispy on all sides.

  3. Whisk together all dressing ingredients in a bowl.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the noodles, tofu, cucumber slices, shredded carrots, and chopped cilantro.

  5. Drizzle the dressing over the noodle mixture and toss gently to coat everything evenly.

  6. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top for extra crunch before serving. This dish is delightful chilled or at room temperature, perfect for a light yet satisfying meal.

3. Spicy Shrimp and Mango Lettuce Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 200g (7 oz) raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 small red chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 6-8 large butter lettuce leaves
  • Fresh mint leaves and chopped peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Remove from heat.

  2. In a small bowl, mix lime juice, fish sauce, honey, garlic, and chili to create a vibrant dressing.

  3. Toss cooked shrimp with diced mango and splash the dressing over the mixture to coat.

  4. Scoop the shrimp and mango mixture evenly into the butter lettuce leaves.

  5. Garnish each wrap with fresh mint and chopped peanuts for a refreshing crunch.

  6. Serve immediately as an appetizer or light meal that’s bursting with sweet, tangy, and spicy notes.

4. Beef and Bok Choy in Black Bean Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 250g (9 oz) flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 2 baby bok choy, halved lengthwise and washed
  • 2 tablespoons fermented black beans, rinsed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup water or beef broth
  • Cooked rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant.

  2. Increase heat to high and add sliced beef. Stir-fry until browned but still tender, about 3-4 minutes. Remove beef and set aside.

  3. Add black beans, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and water or broth to the wok. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.

  4. Add bok choy to the wok and cook for 2-3 minutes until just tender but still vibrant green.

  5. Return the beef to the wok and toss everything together, coating beef and vegetables evenly in the sauce.

  6. Serve hot with steamed rice for a hearty and deeply savory meal.

5. Coconut Curry Ramen for Two

Ingredients:

  • 2 portions fresh or instant ramen noodles (discard seasoning packets if instant)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 100g (3.5 oz) cooked chicken or tofu, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce or soy sauce (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a medium pot, sauté onion and red bell pepper in a bit of oil until soft.

  2. Stir in red curry paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.

  4. Add noodles, cooking according to package instructions (usually 3-4 minutes), stirring occasionally to separate strands.

  5. Stir in cooked chicken or tofu and baby spinach. Cook just until spinach wilts and proteins are heated through.

  6. Season with fish sauce or soy sauce as needed.

  7. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice to brighten the flavors.

Final Thoughts

Cooking flavorful Asian-inspired meals for two doesn’t have to demand hours of prep or unwieldy ingredient lists. These recipes emphasize simplicity, fresh ingredients, and straightforward techniques that anyone can master. Whether you’re in the mood for something quick like a noodle salad or craving the rich warmth of coconut curry ramen, these dishes are designed to satisfy your appetite and bring enjoyable variety to your weeknight dinners or relaxed weekend lunches.

By scaling down cooking portions and focusing on recipes that come together quickly, you not only minimize food waste but also make mealtime feel more special and intentional. Give these recipes a try and experiment by swapping in your favorite fresh vegetables or adjusting spice levels to match your taste.

Enjoy cooking, savor the aromas, and most importantly—relish every bite of your Asian-inspired culinary adventure for two!
