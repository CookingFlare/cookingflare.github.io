How to Make the Best Homemade Pizza Using Traditional Methods


Pizza, often considered the epitome of comfort food, has been enjoyed for centuries and remains a beloved dish in homes and restaurants worldwide. While modern convenience has made it easier to buy frozen or ready-made pizza, there’s something magical about making pizza from scratch using traditional methods. From the dough to the sauce, toppings, and baking process, every step in the process plays a crucial role in crafting the perfect pizza.

In this post, we will guide you through the steps for making the best homemade pizza using traditional methods, ensuring that each bite delivers a delicious and authentic taste that rivals even the best pizzerias.

Essential Ingredients for Homemade Pizza

Before diving into the preparation process, it’s important to understand the ingredients you’ll need to achieve an authentic and flavorful pizza. Using fresh, high-quality ingredients is key to making a pizza that stands out.

For the Pizza Dough:

  • Flour: The foundation of a good pizza dough is the flour. The most traditional choice is “00” flour, which is finely milled and has a high protein content, allowing for a chewy yet crisp crust. However, you can also use all-purpose flour if “00” flour is not available.
  • Water: The water should be lukewarm. Too hot or too cold water can affect the yeast’s ability to activate and rise.
  • Yeast: Use active dry yeast for the best rise. Yeast is responsible for creating the airy texture of the dough.
  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil adds flavor and richness to the dough.
  • Salt: Salt is essential to enhance the overall flavor of the dough.
  • Sugar: A small amount of sugar helps activate the yeast and promotes the rising process.

For the Pizza Sauce:

  • Tomatoes: Use high-quality canned tomatoes, preferably San Marzano tomatoes, which are known for their sweetness and low acidity.
  • Olive Oil: A good drizzle of extra virgin olive oil enhances the sauce’s flavor.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a fragrant depth of flavor.
  • Herbs: Basil, oregano, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for a subtle spice can bring the sauce to life.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt to taste.

For the Toppings:

  • Cheese: The most traditional cheese used for pizza is mozzarella, particularly fresh mozzarella, which gives the pizza a creamy texture and a delicate flavor.
  • Toppings: Toppings can vary greatly based on personal preference, but classic choices include pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, and olives. Fresh basil or arugula can be added after baking for an aromatic touch.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Traditional Homemade Pizza

Step 1: Prepare the Pizza Dough

The dough is arguably the most important part of any pizza. Traditional pizza dough requires patience and attention, but the reward is a chewy, crispy crust that can’t be beaten. Here’s how to prepare it:

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine 1 teaspoon of sugar and 1 cup of lukewarm water. Sprinkle the active dry yeast over the water and stir gently. Allow it to sit for 5–10 minutes until the yeast becomes frothy. If the yeast doesn’t bubble, it may be expired, and you’ll need to start over with fresh yeast.

  2. Mix the Dough Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, add 3 cups of flour and 1 teaspoon of salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Stir the mixture with a wooden spoon until it forms a rough dough.

  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a clean surface and knead it for about 8–10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, add a little flour at a time. Conversely, if it feels dry, add a bit of water.

  4. Let the Dough Rise: Place the kneaded dough into a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm area for 1–1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. The dough should be soft and slightly sticky to the touch.

Step 2: Prepare the Pizza Sauce

A great pizza sauce is simple yet bursting with flavor. Here’s how to prepare a traditional pizza sauce from scratch:

  1. Cook the Garlic: In a medium saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and sauté them until fragrant, about 1 minute. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can become bitter.

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Pour in 2 cups of canned tomatoes (preferably San Marzano) along with their juices. Stir to combine.

  3. Season the Sauce: Add 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional, for a little spice), and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Simmer the sauce over low heat for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. You can also use an immersion blender to achieve a smoother texture, but leaving it slightly chunky adds to the rustic appeal of a traditional pizza sauce.

  4. Finish the Sauce: Once the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together, remove it from the heat. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

Step 3: Shape the Pizza Dough

Once your dough has risen, it’s time to shape it into the perfect pizza base. The traditional method is by hand, and this is where the real magic happens.

  1. Preheat Your Oven: If you’re using a pizza stone, place it in the oven and preheat to the highest temperature possible, usually around 500°F (260°C). This will give your pizza the crispiness that traditional wood-fired ovens achieve. If you don’t have a pizza stone, a baking sheet will work fine.

  2. Divide the Dough: Punch down the dough to release the air and divide it into two equal portions. This will give you two medium-sized pizzas. You can freeze one portion if you’re only making one pizza.

  3. Shape the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, flatten one portion of dough with your hands. Gently stretch and shape the dough into a round shape by rotating it and using your fingers to push from the center outwards. Try to keep the edges slightly thicker to form a crust. Traditional pizza dough is often stretched by tossing it in the air, but this requires a bit of practice. If you’re new to pizza making, use your hands to press it into shape.

  4. Transfer the Dough: Once the dough is shaped to your desired thickness (thin crust or slightly thicker, depending on your preference), carefully transfer it to a floured pizza peel or the back of a baking sheet. This will make it easier to slide onto the pizza stone later.

Step 4: Assemble the Pizza

Now comes the fun part – assembling your pizza. Start by adding the base ingredients and then layer with your favorite toppings.

  1. Add the Sauce: Using a spoon, spread a thin layer of your homemade pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.

  2. Top with Cheese: Add a generous amount of fresh mozzarella cheese, tearing it into small pieces for an authentic look. You can also add a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

  3. Add the Toppings: Now, it’s time to add your toppings. Traditional pizzas like Margherita are simply topped with basil leaves, but you can get creative here. Popular choices include sliced pepperoni, sautéed mushrooms, fresh bell peppers, onions, and olives.

  4. Finish with Olive Oil: Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the pizza before baking. This will give it a beautiful sheen and enhance the flavors.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

With your pizza fully assembled, it’s time to bake it to perfection.

  1. Bake the Pizza: If you’re using a pizza stone, slide the pizza from the peel or baking sheet onto the hot stone in the oven. If you’re using a baking sheet, simply place it directly in the oven. Bake for 7–10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.

  2. Check for Doneness: Keep an eye on the pizza as it bakes. You want the crust to be crisp and the cheese to melt perfectly, with slight browning around the edges. If your pizza is on the thinner side, it may cook faster, so be sure to check it around the 7-minute mark.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once your pizza is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. This brief resting time allows the cheese to set and makes the pizza easier to slice. Add any finishing touches, such as fresh basil or a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, before serving.

Tips for the Best Homemade Pizza

  • Use a Pizza Stone or Steel: For the crispiest crust, a pizza stone or steel is your best option. It retains heat and creates a more authentic cooking environment.
  • Don’t Overload with Toppings: Less is more when it comes to pizza. Overloading your pizza with too many toppings can result in a soggy crust. Stick to 3-5 toppings for a balanced pizza.
  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Whether it’s the mozzarella or the basil, fresh ingredients elevate the flavor of your pizza.
  • Experiment with Different Flours: While “00” flour is traditional, feel free to experiment with different types of flour to adjust the texture of your dough.

Making pizza from scratch using traditional methods is a rewarding and enjoyable experience. With a little practice, you’ll be able to create the perfect homemade pizza that rivals your favorite pizzeria. So, grab your apron, get your ingredients together, and start making the best pizza you’ve ever tasted!
