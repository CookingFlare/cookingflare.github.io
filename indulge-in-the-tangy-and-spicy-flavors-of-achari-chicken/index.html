Indulge in the Tangy and Spicy Flavors of Achari Chicken


Are you tired of the same old chicken recipes? Do you crave a burst of tangy and spicy flavors that will leave your taste buds dancing? Look no further! In this recipe, we’re going to take your chicken game to the next level with the tantalizing flavors of Achari Chicken.

What is Achari Chicken?

Achari Chicken is a popular dish from the Indian subcontinent that is known for its rich and robust flavors. The word “achar” translates to pickle, and that’s exactly what this dish is all about – the tangy and spicy goodness of pickles infused into succulent chicken pieces.

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon nigella seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the mustard oil in a pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the cumin seeds, fennel seeds, nigella seeds, and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter for a few seconds until they release their aroma.

  2. Add the finely chopped onion to the pan and sauté until golden brown. This will take about 5-6 minutes.

  3. Now, add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pan. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell of ginger and garlic disappears.

  4. Add the pureed tomatoes to the pan and cook until the oil separates from the masala. This will take around 8-10 minutes.

  5. In a small bowl, mix together the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Add this spice mixture to the pan and cook for a minute to release the flavors.

  6. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and coat them well with the masala. Cook for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is sealed and lightly browned.

  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the chicken cook for about 15-20 minutes until it is tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  8. Once the chicken is cooked, remove the lid and cook on high heat for a couple of minutes to reduce any excess liquid.

  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan bread.

Tips and Variations:

  • You can adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences. If you like it extra spicy, add more red chili powder or green chilies.
  • For a richer flavor, marinate the chicken in yogurt and achari masala for a few hours before cooking. This will help to tenderize the meat and infuse it with the flavors.
  • You can also add a tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to enhance the tanginess of the dish.
  • Feel free to experiment with different cuts of chicken, such as bone-in or boneless, depending on your preference.

Now that you have the recipe for Achari Chicken, it’s time to unleash your culinary skills and indulge in the tangy and spicy flavors that this dish has to offer. Get ready to take your taste buds on a flavorful journey that you won’t forget. Happy cooking!
