Homemade Hot Cross Buns: A Classic Easter Treat


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious treats. One such treat that has become synonymous with Easter is the hot cross bun. These lightly spiced, sweet buns, adorned with a distinctive cross on top, have been enjoyed for centuries during the Easter season. There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked hot cross buns wafting through the kitchen, and the taste of a warm bun slathered in butter is pure bliss. If you’ve never tried making hot cross buns at home, you’re in for a real treat. In this blog post, I’ll share with you a delightful homemade hot cross buns recipe that will have you savoring the flavors of Easter in every bite.

Ingredients:

For the Buns:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 3/4 cup mixed dried fruits (such as raisins, currants, and candied citrus peel)

For the Cross:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup water

For the Glaze:

  • 1/4 cup apricot preserves
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Yeast Mixture: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and spices. Create a well in the center.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter and eggs. Pour this mixture and the yeast mixture into the well of the dry ingredients. Stir until a dough forms.

  4. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.

  5. Add Dried Fruits: Flatten the dough and sprinkle the dried fruits over it. Fold the dough over and knead gently to incorporate the fruits evenly.

  6. Rise and Rest: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

  7. Shape the Buns: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 12 equal portions. Shape each portion into a smooth round bun and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

  8. Make the Cross Mixture: In a small bowl, mix the flour and water to form a thick paste. Spoon the paste into a piping bag or a small plastic bag with a corner snipped off.

  9. Pipe the Cross: Pipe a cross over the top of each bun.

  10. Bake the Buns: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the buns for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

  11. Prepare the Glaze: In a small saucepan, heat the apricot preserves and water until melted.

  12. Glaze the Buns: Brush the warm buns with the apricot glaze for a beautiful shine.

  13. Enjoy! Allow the buns to cool slightly before serving. These homemade hot cross buns are best enjoyed fresh with a generous spread of butter.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a twist, try adding a hint of citrus zest to the dough for a refreshing flavor.
  • If you prefer a more traditional cross, you can use a simple water and powdered sugar mixture instead of flour paste.
  • These hot cross buns can be stored in an airtight container for up to 3 days or frozen for later enjoyment.

There you have it – a delightful recipe for homemade hot cross buns that will surely become a cherished tradition in your home. Whether you enjoy them for breakfast, brunch, or as a sweet snack, these buns are a delightful way to celebrate the Easter season. Happy baking!
