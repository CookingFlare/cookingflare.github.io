Delight Your Senses with These Mouthwatering Mexican Dessert Recipes


Mexican cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and rich aromas. While many people associate Mexican food with savory dishes like tacos and enchiladas, the country’s dessert scene is equally impressive. From creamy custards to crunchy cookies, Mexican desserts offer a world of flavors and textures to explore. In this post, we’ll delve into the world of Mexican desserts and share some mouthwatering recipes that are sure to delight your senses.

Chocoflan: A Twist on Traditional Flan

Chocoflan is a unique dessert that combines the richness of chocolate cake with the creamy texture of flan. This decadent treat is a staple in many Mexican households and is often served at special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for caramel topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together cocoa powder, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth.
  5. Pour the batter into a 9x13-inch baking dish and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. While the cake is baking, prepare the caramel topping by melting the sugar in a saucepan over medium heat.
  8. Once the cake is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  9. Drizzle the caramel topping over the cake and smooth it out with a spatula.
  10. Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours before serving.

Tres Leches Cake: A Classic Mexican Dessert

Tres Leches Cake is a moist and creamy dessert that’s perfect for warm weather. The name “Tres Leches” translates to “three milks,” which refers to the three types of milk used in the recipe: evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together butter, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth.
  5. Pour the batter into a 9x13-inch baking dish and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool for 10 minutes.
  8. Poke holes all over the top of the cake with a skewer or fork.
  9. Pour the evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream over the top of the cake.
  10. Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours before serving.

Churros con Cajeta: A Sweet and Crunchy Treat

Churros con Cajeta is a classic Mexican dessert that consists of crispy fried dough pastries coated in cinnamon sugar, served with a side of creamy cajeta sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Cajeta sauce (see below for recipe)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together milk, eggs, and cinnamon.
  3. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth.
  4. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Heat the oil in a deep frying pan to 375°F (190°C).
  7. Pipe the dough into long strips and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown.
  8. Drain the churros on paper towels and coat with cinnamon sugar.
  9. Serve the churros with cajeta sauce for dipping.

Cajeta Sauce Recipe

Cajeta sauce is a rich and creamy sauce made with goat’s milk, sugar, and vanilla. It’s the perfect accompaniment to churros and other sweet treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup goat’s milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine goat’s milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the sauce has thickened.
  4. Remove the sauce from the heat and stir in the heavy cream.
  5. Let the sauce cool to room temperature before serving.

Alfajores: A Crunchy and Chewy Cookie

Alfajores are a classic Mexican cookie that consists of two crispy cookies sandwiched together with a creamy filling. These cookies are perfect for dunking in hot chocolate or coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Filling ingredients (see below)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together butter, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth.
  5. Pour the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Roll the dough out to a thickness of 1/4 inch and cut into desired shapes.
  7. Place the cookies on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool completely before assembling the alfajores.

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together confectioners’ sugar, butter, and heavy cream until smooth.
  2. Add the vanilla extract and whisk until combined.

Conclusion

Mexican desserts are a world of flavors and textures waiting to be explored. From the rich and creamy chocoflan to the crunchy and chewy alfajores, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re a sweet tooth or just looking to try something new, these mouthwatering Mexican dessert recipes are sure to delight your senses. So go ahead, take a bite, and experience the sweet side of Mexico.
