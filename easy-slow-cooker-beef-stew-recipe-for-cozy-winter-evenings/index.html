Easy Slow Cooker Beef Stew Recipe for Cozy Winter Evenings


winter evenings are the perfect time to gather around the dinner table with loved ones and enjoy a warm, comforting meal. One of the most popular and delicious options for a cold winter’s night is a hearty beef stew, slow-cooked to perfection in a rich and flavorful broth. In this post, we’ll share a simple and easy-to-make slow cooker beef stew recipe that’s sure to become a new family favorite.

The Perfect Winter Comfort Food

There’s something special about a slow-cooked stew that just can’t be replicated with quick-cooking methods. The long, gentle heat of the slow cooker allows the tender chunks of beef to absorb all the rich flavors of the broth, resulting in a dish that’s both comforting and satisfying. And the best part? It’s incredibly easy to prepare, making it the perfect option for a busy winter evening when you don’t have a lot of time to spend in the kitchen.

The Ingredients

To make this easy slow cooker beef stew, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat (such as chuck or round)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium-sized carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves

The Instructions

To start, season the beef stew meat with salt and pepper. Then, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of oil. Sear the beef on all sides until it’s browned, then remove it from the skillet and set it aside.

Next, add the chopped onion to the skillet and cook until it’s softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Add the chopped carrots and potatoes to the skillet and cook for about 5 minutes, until they begin to soften. Then, add the beef broth, red wine (if using), tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper to the skillet, stirring to combine.

Transfer the mixture to the slow cooker and add the browned beef, bay leaves, and any additional seasonings you like. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours.

The Result

When the stew is done, the beef should be tender and falling apart, and the vegetables should be cooked through and tender. Remove the bay leaves and serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley or thyme if desired.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind when making this easy slow cooker beef stew recipe:

  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can add a little bit of cornstarch or flour to the broth before serving.
  • If you like a bit of heat in your stew, you can add a diced jalapeno pepper or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, you can substitute the beef with mushrooms or eggplant and add additional vegetables like bell peppers or zucchini.
  • If you want to make the stew more substantial, you can serve it with a side of crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.

Conclusion

This easy slow cooker beef stew recipe is the perfect way to warm up on a cold winter evening. With its rich, flavorful broth and tender chunks of beef, it’s sure to become a new family favorite. And the best part? It’s incredibly easy to make, making it the perfect option for a busy winter evening when you don’t have a lot of time to spend in the kitchen. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the cozy warmth of a slow-cooked stew on a cold winter’s night.
