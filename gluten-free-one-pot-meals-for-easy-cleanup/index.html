Gluten-Free One-Pot Meals for Easy Cleanup


If you’re looking for a quick and easy way to clean up after cooking, one-pot meals are the answer! Not only do they make cleanup a breeze, but they also happen to be gluten-free. In this article, we’ll explore three delicious one-pot meals that are perfect for busy weeknights or any time you want a stress-free meal. So, let’s get cooking!

  1. One-Pot Gluten-Free Pork Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin, sliced thinly
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 tbsp gluten-free soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tsp rice vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork and cook for about 4-5 minutes, or until browned on all sides.

  2. Remove the pork from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet, cooking for about 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant.

  4. Stir in the bell pepper, broccoli, and snap peas. Cook for another 4-5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the gluten-free soy sauce, honey, cornstarch, and rice vinegar. Pour the mixture over the vegetables and stir to combine.

  6. Return the pork to the skillet and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until everything is heated through.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately.

  8. One-Pot Gluten-Free Chicken Alfredo Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz gluten-free penne pasta
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions in a large pot of boiling, salted water until al dente.

  2. In a separate large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook for about 6-8 minutes, or until browned on all sides.

  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  4. Add the onion and garlic to the same skillet, cooking for about 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant.

  5. Stir in the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, garlic powder, and dried basil. Cook for another 3-4 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

  6. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot. Add the chicken and sauce, stirring to combine.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately.

  8. One-Pot Gluten-Free Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups gluten-free chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 oz fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. In a separate large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking for about 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the mushrooms and cook until they start to brown, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. Add the Arborio rice and stir until the rice is well-coated with the oil and onion mixture.
  5. Pour in the white wine and cook until it evaporates, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add 1 cup of the chicken broth and stir continuously until the liquid is absorbed by the rice. Repeat with another cup of broth, stirring each time until the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Once the rice has absorbed all the broth, remove the skillet from the heat. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately.

Conclusion:

One-pot meals are not only easy to clean up after but also convenient and budget-friendly. These three gluten-free one-pot meals are sure to please your taste buds and leave your kitchen clutter-free. It’s time to start cooking!
