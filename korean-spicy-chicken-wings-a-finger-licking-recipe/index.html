Korean Spicy Chicken Wings: A Finger-Licking Recipe


Korean cuisine has long been celebrated for its vibrant flavors and exceptional balance of spice, heat, sweetness, and savoriness. Among its many brilliant dishes, Korean spicy chicken wings reign as one of the most irresistible snacks or appetizers. Whether you’re planning a game night, hosting a casual party, or simply indulging in some well-deserved “me-time,” these finger-licking wings are guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

In this post, we’ll walk you through every step to make perfectly crispy, juicy, and delectable Korean spicy chicken wings at home. You’ll be surprised how easy it is to recreate this classic Korean dish in your own kitchen!

Why Korean Spicy Chicken Wings Should Be Your Next Dish

When it comes to chicken wings, there are countless variations from around the world. However, Korean spicy chicken wings stand out for their deep, layered flavors, crispy exterior, and tender interior. What makes them truly magical is the unique sauce—a seamless blend of garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and Korea’s beloved chili paste, gochujang. This flavor-packed combination delivers the perfect mix of heat and sweetness in every bite.

The method for preparing these wings maximizes texture and flavor. The chicken is first coated and fried (or baked for a healthier option), ensuring every piece is crunchy on the outside yet juicy on the inside. Tossed in that signature sauce, each wing is transformed into a spicy, sticky delight that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make these Korean spicy chicken wings, you’ll need two sets of ingredients: one for preparing the chicken itself and one for crafting the sauce. Here’s a clear breakdown for convenience:

For the Chicken Wings:

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) of chicken wings or drumettes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons potato starch or cornstarch (for extra crispiness in frying)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup water (add gradually)
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola oil works well)

For the Korean Spicy Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar (adjust sweetness as needed)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (adds tanginess)
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) for extra spice (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup (for balance and added flavor)
  • Sesame seeds and chopped green onions (as garnish)

These ingredients come together to create a layered, flavorful dish that’s effortless enough for a weeknight dinner yet impressive enough for a special gathering.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare and Season the Chicken

Start by washing and patting dry your chicken wings. In a large mixing bowl, season the wings with salt, black pepper, and garlic powder. Toss to ensure the seasoning is evenly distributed over each piece. Let the wings sit for 20-30 minutes (optional but recommended) to allow the flavors to work their way into the meat.

Step 2: Batter the Wings

To achieve that signature crunch, coat the wings in a light batter. Mix the all-purpose flour with potato starch or cornstarch in a separate bowl. Gradually add water while stirring until the mixture reaches a semi-thick consistency—thick enough to coat the wings but thin enough to drip off slightly. Dip each wing into the batter, making sure it’s fully covered.

Step 3: Fry (or Bake) the Wings

Frying:

Heat a deep pan or fryer with oil to approximately 350°F (175°C). If you don’t have a thermometer, you can test the oil by dropping in a small piece of batter—it should sizzle and float to the top. Fry the wings in batches to avoid overcrowding, cooking each batch for about 8-10 minutes until golden and crispy.

For an even crunchier exterior, consider double-frying the wings. After the initial frying, set them aside for a few minutes to cool slightly. Then, fry them again for an additional 1-2 minutes.

Baking (Healthier Option):

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper, then set a wire rack on top to allow for airflow. Arrange the wings in a single layer on the rack. Spray or brush them lightly with oil, then bake for 40-45 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy.

Step 4: Make the Signature Sauce

While the wings are cooking, prepare the sauce. Combine the gochujang, soy sauce, honey or brown sugar, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, gochugaru (if using), and ketchup in a small saucepan. Stir the mixture over medium heat until it begins to bubble gently. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld into a sticky, smooth sauce. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 5: Coat the Wings in Sauce

Once your wings are cooked to perfection, it’s time to coat them in the flavorful sauce. Place the wings in a large mixing bowl and pour the warm sauce over them. Using a spatula or tongs, gently toss the wings until they are evenly coated. The sauce should cling beautifully to the crispy exterior of the wings, creating a mouthwatering glaze.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the wings to a serving platter. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish. These simple additions not only enhance the flavors but also add an appealing visual touch to your dish. Serve immediately while the wings are hot and crispy.

Tips for Perfect Korean Spicy Chicken Wings

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic, ginger, and gochujang will elevate the authenticity and taste of your wings. Avoid substituting with other chili pastes, as gochujang gives the dish its unique Korean flavor.

  2. Adjust the Spice Level: If you prefer milder wings, use less gochujang and gochugaru. For more heat, increase the amount of chili paste or add sliced fresh chilies.

  3. Double-Fry for Extra Crunch: Double-frying is a common technique in Korean cooking that ensures the chicken stays crispy even after being coated in sauce.

  4. Make Ahead: You can prepare the sauce in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to a week. This makes it easier to whip up these wings at a moment’s notice.

  5. Pair With Sides: Serve these spicy chicken wings with refreshing sides like pickled radish, cucumber salad, or steamed rice to balance the heat.

Why Gochujang is the Star Ingredient

A quick note about gochujang: This fermented chili paste is a cornerstone of Korean cooking. Made from red chili powder, glutinous rice, soybeans, and salt, it imparts a sweet, savory, and spicy depth to any dish. Gochujang’s complex flavors are what set these chicken wings apart from standard buffalo wings or other variations.

Look for gochujang in the Asian section of your local grocery store, or at a Korean or international market. Once you have a tub in your pantry, you can use it for bibimbap, noodles, marinades, soups, and more!

Final Thoughts

Korean spicy chicken wings are a surefire way to impress your family and friends, whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself to a delicious snack. With their crispy exterior, juicy interior, and perfectly balanced spicy-sweet sauce, these wings deliver on every level. Plus, they’re surprisingly easy to make!

So, grab your ingredients, follow these steps, and dive into the world of Korean flavors. Your taste buds—and anyone you share these wings with—will thank you. Prepare to get messy, because with wings this good, using your hands is non-negotiable. Enjoy every finger-licking bite!
