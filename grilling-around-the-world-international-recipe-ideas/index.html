Grilling Around the World: International Recipe Ideas


Are you ready to take your grilling game to the next level? Grilling isn’t just about hot dogs and hamburgers – there’s a whole world of flavors waiting to be explored. From spicy Mexican street corn to succulent Korean BBQ, let’s embark on a global culinary journey and discover some international grilling recipes that will tantalize your taste buds.

Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

Ingredients:

  • Fresh corn on the cob
  • Mayonnaise
  • Cotija cheese
  • Chili powder
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Husk the corn and grill it, turning occasionally until charred in spots, about 10 minutes.
  3. Spread a thin layer of mayonnaise on the grilled corn.
  4. Roll the corn in crumbled Cotija cheese and sprinkle with chili powder.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges.

Brazilian Picanha

Ingredients:

  • Picanha beef cut
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Garlic cloves
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Rub the picanha with coarse sea salt and let it sit for 30 minutes at room temperature.
  2. Skewer the beef onto a rotisserie and cook over a medium-high heat grill, turning occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 130°F for medium-rare.
  3. Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  4. Crush the garlic cloves and mix with olive oil. Drizzle over the sliced picanha before serving.

Korean BBQ (Bulgogi)

Ingredients:

  • Thinly sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • Soy sauce
  • Brown sugar
  • Sesame oil
  • Garlic
  • Pear, grated
  • Green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, grated pear, and chopped green onions.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade and let it sit for at least 2 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill to high heat and grill the beef for 2-3 minutes per side until slightly charred.
  4. Sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.

Argentinean Chimichurri Chicken

Ingredients:

  • Chicken thighs
  • Olive oil
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Fresh oregano, chopped
  • Red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, red wine vinegar, minced garlic, chopped parsley, oregano, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then brush with the chimichurri sauce.
  3. Grill the chicken over medium heat, turning occasionally, until cooked through.
  4. Serve with extra chimichurri sauce on the side.

Lebanese Kafta Kebabs

Ingredients:

  • Ground lamb or beef
  • Onion, grated
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Ground cumin
  • Ground allspice
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Ground paprika
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the ground meat, grated onion, chopped parsley, cumin, allspice, cinnamon, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Divide the mixture and form into long, thin kebabs around metal skewers.
  3. Grill the kebabs over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until cooked through.
  4. Serve with pita bread and yogurt sauce.

Conclusion

Exploring international grilling recipes allows you to experience a diverse range of flavors and culinary techniques. Whether it’s the zesty elote from Mexico, the savory picanha from Brazil, or the aromatic kebabs from Lebanon, each dish tells a unique story of its cultural origins. So, fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and embark on a flavorful journey around the world right in your backyard. The world is your grill, so go ahead and savor the global flavors of outdoor cooking. Happy grilling!
