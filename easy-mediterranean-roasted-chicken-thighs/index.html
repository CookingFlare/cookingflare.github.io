Easy Mediterranean Roasted Chicken Thighs


Are you looking for a versatile and mouth-watering dish that's perfect for a quick weeknight meal, a weekend brunch, or even a family gathering? Look no further than easy Mediterranean roasted chicken thighs. This flavorful and aromatic dish is a cinch to prepare and is sure to become a staple in your kitchen repertoire. The Secret to Perfectly Roasted Chicken Thighs Roasting chicken thighs is a breeze, but achieving that perfectly caramelized, tender, and juicy texture can be a bit of an art. The key is to understand the importance of low and slow cooking, combined with the right blend of herbs and spices. Here's a simple yet effective recipe to achieve restaurant-quality results in the comfort of your own home. Gather Your Ingredients To make this Mediterranean roast, you'll need the following ingredients:

4-6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs 2 tablespoons of olive oil 1 tablespoon of freshly chopped oregano 1 teaspoon of dried thyme 1 teaspoon of paprika 1 teaspoon of salt 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional) 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 lemon, sliced 1 onion, sliced 2 cups of mixed bell peppers (any color) 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Rinse the chicken thighs and pat them dry with paper towels. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, oregano, thyme, paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using). Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and toss to coat evenly. Step 2: Roast the Chicken Transfer the chicken thighs to a large baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Arrange the slices of onion and lemon on the baking sheet around the chicken. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is browned and crispy on the outside. Step 3: Add the Veggies After 25-30 minutes of roasting, add the mixed bell peppers and cherry tomatoes to the baking sheet. Return the chicken to the oven and continue roasting for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly caramelized. Step 4: Finish with Garlic Remove the baking sheet from the oven and sprinkle the minced garlic over the top. Return to the oven for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the garlic is fragrant. The Result Once the chicken thighs are done, let them rest for a few minutes before serving. The resulting dish is a symphony of flavors and textures, with the fall-off-the-bone tenderness of the chicken paired with the sweetness of the roasted vegetables and the brightness of the lemon. Tips and Variations

To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle some crumbled feta cheese over the chicken before serving. Swap out the mixed bell peppers for zucchini, eggplant, or any other colorful vegetables you have on hand. If you prefer a crisper exterior, broil the chicken for an additional 2-3 minutes after roasting. To make this dish gluten-free, substitute the olive oil with coconut or avocado oil.

With these easy Mediterranean roasted chicken thighs, you'll be the star of your next dinner party or family gathering. This simple recipe yields a dish that's full of flavor, nutritious, and sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.
