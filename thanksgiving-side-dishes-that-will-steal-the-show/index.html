Thanksgiving Side Dishes That Will Steal the Show


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with family and friends, share laughter, and indulge in a grand feast. While the turkey may be the star of the show, the side dishes are what really make the meal unforgettable. From creamy mashed potatoes to tangy cranberry sauce, side dishes elevate the entire dining experience. If you want to impress your guests and elevate your Thanksgiving spread, here are some unique and flavorful side dishes that will steal the show.

1. Herb-Infused Roasted Potatoes

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without some kind of potato dish. These herb-infused roasted potatoes are the perfect balance of crispy and tender. Tossed in olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, and a sprinkle of sea salt, these potatoes are roasted until golden brown. The aroma alone will have everyone eager to dig in.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the potatoes, olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Toss until the potatoes are well coated.
  3. Spread the potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until golden and crispy, flipping halfway through for even cooking.
  5. Serve hot with a sprinkle of fresh herbs for added color and flavor.

2. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel

Sweet potatoes are a classic Thanksgiving side, but this dish takes them to the next level with a sweet, nutty streusel topping. The creamy base of mashed sweet potatoes, combined with the crunchy pecan streusel, makes for a perfect balance of textures. This casserole can be made ahead of time and baked just before serving, making it an easy and crowd-pleasing option.

Ingredients for the casserole:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt to taste

Ingredients for the streusel topping:

  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and mash the potatoes until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the mashed sweet potatoes with brown sugar, melted butter, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture to the prepared baking dish and smooth the top.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the chopped pecans, brown sugar, flour, butter, and cinnamon to make the streusel topping. Sprinkle it evenly over the mashed sweet potatoes.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden and bubbly.
  7. Serve warm, and enjoy the perfect blend of sweetness and crunch.

3. Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts are often a divisive dish, but this recipe with bacon and a balsamic glaze will convert even the most skeptical eaters. The crispy bacon adds a savory, smoky flavor, while the balsamic glaze brings a tangy sweetness that complements the Brussels sprouts perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the Brussels sprouts, cut side down, and cook for 5-7 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar and honey. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes until the glaze thickens.
  4. Return the bacon to the skillet with the Brussels sprouts, then pour the balsamic glaze over the mixture. Stir to combine, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve immediately as a savory, sweet, and smoky side dish.

4. Garlic and Parmesan Green Beans Almondine

Green beans are a staple side dish, but this garlic and Parmesan green beans almondine offers a fresh and flavorful twist. The green beans are sautéed in butter with garlic and almonds, then finished with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese. The combination of crunchy almonds and creamy Parmesan makes this dish irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil and blanch the green beans for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced almonds to the skillet and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently until the almonds are lightly toasted.
  4. Add the blanched green beans to the skillet, tossing them to coat in the butter and almond mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the green beans are tender and flavorful.
  5. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top, and season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

5. Cranberry Sauce with Orange and Ginger

Cranberry sauce is a Thanksgiving classic, but this version incorporates orange and ginger for a refreshing twist. The citrusy brightness from the orange and the spicy kick from the ginger add complexity to the sweet and tart cranberries, making this side dish the perfect complement to your turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, orange juice, orange zest, ginger, and cinnamon.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  3. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool to room temperature. It will continue to thicken as it cools.
  4. Serve the cranberry sauce alongside your turkey, and enjoy the perfect balance of tart and sweet flavors.

6. Buttermilk Cornbread with Jalapeño and Cheddar

Cornbread is a classic side dish that pairs well with almost any main course, but adding jalapeño and cheddar gives it a bold and savory twist. The subtle heat from the jalapeño is balanced by the creamy tang of buttermilk and the rich, melty cheddar cheese, making this cornbread a standout at your Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 jalapeño, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish or a cast-iron skillet.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk the eggs, buttermilk, and melted butter together until smooth.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Fold in the cheddar cheese and chopped jalapeño.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Serve warm, and enjoy the perfect combination of savory cornbread with a spicy kick.

7. Cauliflower Gratin

For those looking for a low-carb alternative to traditional potato gratin, cauliflower gratin is the perfect choice. This creamy, cheesy dish features tender cauliflower in a velvety béchamel sauce, topped with a crisp golden crust. It’s rich and comforting, making it an excellent addition to your Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with butter.
  2. Steam the cauliflower florets for 5-7 minutes until tender but not mushy. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to make a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in the milk and heavy cream, cooking until the sauce thickens and is smooth.
  5. Stir in the shredded Gruyère cheese until melted and the sauce is creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Combine the cauliflower with the cheese sauce in the baking dish, then sprinkle the breadcrumbs on top.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  8. Serve hot, and enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness.

These Thanksgiving side dishes are sure to steal the show and elevate your holiday meal. With a mix of classic flavors and new twists, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re a fan of savory, creamy, or sweet dishes, these recipes will have your guests raving. Happy cooking, and enjoy a delicious and memorable Thanksgiving!
