The Best Easter Sides to Compliment Your Main Course


Easter is a celebration filled with the warmth of family, the joy of renewal, and, of course, the delight of a sumptuous meal. While the centerpiece of your Easter table might be a perfectly roasted ham, glazed lamb, or succulent roast chicken, the sides are what truly elevate the feast. They bring balance, texture, and vibrant flavors to the plate, making each bite a memorable experience. Whether you’re going for traditional dishes or something with a modern twist, the sides you choose will complement your main course and impress your guests.

Let’s dive into a curated list of the best Easter side dishes that shine alongside classic mains. These recipes cater to various tastes and dietary preferences, ensuring that no one leaves the table hungry.

1. Garlic and Herb Roasted Baby Potatoes

Simple yet intoxicating, roasted baby potatoes bring a golden, crispy texture that pairs brilliantly with any meat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Wash and halve the baby potatoes for even roasting.
  3. Toss the potatoes in olive oil, garlic, rosemary, and thyme.
  4. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until golden and crisp.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and serve warm.

Tip: For a creamy touch, sprinkle freshly grated Parmesan cheese over the potatoes five minutes before pulling them from the oven.

2. Spring Vegetable Medley with Lemon Butter Glaze

Nothing signals spring more than a vibrant medley of fresh vegetables. This side is not only colorful but also downright delicious, adding a fresh contrast to heavier mains.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup asparagus tips
  • 1 cup baby carrots, peeled
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 1 cup baby zucchini, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Directions:

  1. Blanch the asparagus, carrots, snap peas, and zucchini in boiling salted water for 2-3 minutes. Immediately rinse with cold water to stop cooking; drain well.
  2. In a skillet, melt butter and stir in lemon zest and juice.
  3. Add the vegetables to the skillet and toss gently to coat.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and garnish with chopped parsley.
  5. Serve warm, allowing the lemon butter to infuse its bright flavor.

Why this side works: The lemon butter glaze highlights the natural sweetness of the spring veggies, offering a refreshing lightness to balance richer dishes.

3. Creamy Butternut Squash Gratin

For those craving a rich, comforting side, butternut squash gratin is a delightful option, offering a hint of sweetness paired with creamy decadence.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds butternut squash, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup Gruyère cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a greased baking dish, layer half the sliced butternut squash.
  3. Sprinkle garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper over the squash.
  4. Pour half the cream and sprinkle half the Gruyère cheese.
  5. Repeat layers with remaining squash, cream, and cheese.
  6. Top with breadcrumbs for a crunchy finish.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes until the squash is tender and the top is bubbly and golden.

Extra note: This dish can be prepared a day ahead, making it perfect for holiday meal prep.

4. Glazed Carrots with Honey and Ginger

Carrots glazed with honey and fresh ginger offer a sweet-spicy side that’s bright and aromatic, cutting through the richness of many Easter mains.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander or parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Steam the carrot sticks until just tender, about 6-8 minutes.
  2. In a pan, melt butter over medium heat, add honey and ginger.
  3. Toss in the steamed carrots and cook for 3-4 minutes, coating them in the glaze.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander or parsley before serving.

Fun fact: Ginger’s warmth complements traditional Easter spices and adds a zesty kick.

5. Classic Deviled Eggs with a Twist

No Easter table feels complete without deviled eggs. Adding a modern twist with fresh herbs and a dash of smoked paprika can make this simple side stand out.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, finely chopped
  • Smoked paprika, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Hard boil the eggs, then cool them in ice water.
  2. Peel and halve eggs lengthwise, remove yolks to a bowl.
  3. Mash yolks with mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, chives, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  4. Pipe or spoon the yolk mixture back into egg whites.
  5. Sprinkle with smoked paprika for a subtle smoky flavor.

Pro tip: For extra flair, finely chop a bit of crispy bacon or use avocado in the filling for a creamy twist.

6. Fresh Asparagus with Parmesan and Lemon

Simple, elegant, and quick to prepare, this asparagus side is an easy way to bring fresh, green vibrancy to your Easter spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange asparagus on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and lemon juice, sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast for 12-15 minutes, until tender but still crisp.
  4. Remove from oven and sprinkle with lemon zest and Parmesan cheese immediately.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Why this side is a hit: The nutty Parmesan and bright lemon juice highlight the natural flavor of asparagus, creating a sophisticated but effortless dish.

7. Spring Pea and Mint Salad with Feta

This refreshing salad combines sweet peas, fresh mint, and creamy feta, offering the perfect palate cleanser between richer courses.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas (thawed if frozen)
  • 1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Blanch peas in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then cool with ice water to maintain color and crunch.
  2. In a bowl, combine peas, feta, mint, and red onion.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice.
  4. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss gently and serve chilled or at room temperature.

An added bonus: This salad can easily be made vegan by substituting feta with a plant-based cheese or toasted nuts.

8. Hot Cross Buns Bread Pudding

Looking to incorporate the Easter classic into your sides? A savory bread pudding using hot cross buns is a clever way to bring festive sweetness and comforting textures.

Ingredients:

  • 6 hot cross buns, cut into cubes
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk or cream
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Sauté onion until translucent. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs, milk, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add bread cubes, cheese, and onions; mix thoroughly.
  5. Pour mixture into a greased baking dish, press bread cubes down.
  6. Bake 35-40 minutes, till puffed and golden on top.

Result: The subtle sweetness of hot cross buns mixed with savory ingredients makes a unique and decadent side dish.

Wrapping Up Your Easter Feast

The best Easter sides should cater to a variety of textures and flavors—from earthy roasted potatoes to bright, crisp salads, and creamy gratins to zesty vegetables. These sides don’t just complement your main course but create a harmonious, satisfying meal that captures the spirit of Easter—renewal, balance, and togetherness.

Remember to balance richness with freshness, and consider dietary preferences when planning your menu. These recipes are versatile, crowd-pleasing, and scalable for any size table. With this collection, your Easter feast will be as memorable for its sides as it is for its centerpiece.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a passionate beginner, incorporating these sides into your Easter menu will bring color, flavor, and joy to your celebration. Happy cooking, and may your family’s Easter table be filled with laughter and delicious food!
