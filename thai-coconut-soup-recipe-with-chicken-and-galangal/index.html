Thai Coconut Soup Recipe with Chicken and Galangal


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’ve probably indulged in the exquisite flavors of Tom Kha Gai, a traditional Thai coconut soup with chicken and galangal. This aromatic and creamy soup is a delightful blend of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors, making it a favorite among food enthusiasts. The combination of creamy coconut milk, tender chicken, and the unique citrusy flavor of galangal makes this soup a stand-out dish that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, bruised and chopped
  • 3-4 slices of galangal
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 lb. boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced
  • 1 cup of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2-3 tbsp. fish sauce
  • 2-3 tbsp. lime juice
  • 1-2 tbsp. palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2-3 Thai red chilies, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Directions:

  1. Begin by heating a large pot over medium heat. Pour in one can of coconut milk and let it simmer until it starts to release its aroma.

  2. Add the chopped lemongrass, galangal slices, and kaffir lime leaves to the pot. Allow the flavors to infuse the coconut milk for a few minutes.

  3. Once the aromatics have imparted their flavors, add the sliced chicken thighs to the pot. Stir gently and let the chicken cook until it is no longer pink.

  4. Pour in the chicken broth and let the soup simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.

  5. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and let them cook until they become tender.

  6. In a small bowl, mix the remaining can of coconut milk with fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is well combined.

  7. Pour this coconut milk mixture into the pot, stirring gently to incorporate it into the soup.

  8. Toss in the sliced Thai red chilies for a touch of heat and let the soup simmer for a few more minutes to allow all the flavors to meld together.

  9. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more fish sauce, lime juice, or sugar as needed. Add salt to taste.

  10. Once the flavors have developed to your liking, remove the pot from the heat and discard the lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.

  11. Ladle the Thai coconut soup into serving bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Serving Suggestion:

This Thai coconut soup is best enjoyed hot, either as a standalone meal or served alongside steamed jasmine rice. The creamy texture and complex flavors make it a perfect starter or a comforting main course. Pair it with your favorite Asian-inspired salad or stir-fried vegetables for a complete and satisfying meal.

Final Thoughts:

The Thai Coconut Soup with Chicken and Galangal is a true celebration of Thai cuisine. With its rich and creamy coconut base, fragrant galangal, and tender chicken, this soup captures the essence of traditional Thai flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned Thai food lover or a newcomer to this vibrant cuisine, this recipe is sure to impress with its robust and aromatic profile. Try this recipe at home and treat yourself to a taste of Thailand right in your own kitchen!
