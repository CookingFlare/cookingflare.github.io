Slow Cooker Moroccan Recipes for Exotic Flavors


Moroccan cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and exotic combinations. One way to experience the rich taste of Morocco is through slow cooker recipes. With the convenience of a slow cooker, you can effortlessly create delicious Moroccan dishes that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Marrakech. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering slow cooker Moroccan recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends.

1. Slow Cooker Moroccan Chicken Tagine

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of dried apricots, halved
  • 1/2 cup of pitted green olives
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Place chicken thighs in the slow cooker.
  2. In a bowl, mix together onions, garlic, cumin, coriander, ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the spice mixture over the chicken, ensuring it is evenly coated.
  4. Add chicken broth, dried apricots, and pitted green olives to the slow cooker.
  5. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Serve the chicken tagine over couscous or rice and garnish with fresh cilantro.

2. Slow Cooker Moroccan Lamb Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup of dried apricots, halved
  • 1/2 cup of raisins
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the lamb chunks on all sides and transfer them to the slow cooker.
  2. In the same skillet, sauté onions, garlic, and carrots until softened.
  3. Add cumin, coriander, ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well to coat the vegetables with the spices.
  4. Pour beef broth and diced tomatoes into the skillet, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Transfer the vegetable mixture to the slow cooker, along with dried apricots and raisins.
  6. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours, until the lamb is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  7. Serve the lamb stew with couscous or crusty bread and garnish with fresh parsley.

3. Slow Cooker Moroccan Chickpea Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of dried apricots, halved
  • 1/2 cup of chopped dates
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onions, garlic, carrots, and tomatoes until softened.
  2. Add cumin, coriander, ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well to coat the vegetables with the spices.
  3. Transfer the vegetable mixture to the slow cooker, along with chickpeas, vegetable broth, dried apricots, and chopped dates.
  4. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours, until the flavors have melded together.
  5. Serve the chickpea stew with crusty bread and garnish with fresh mint leaves.

These slow cooker Moroccan recipes are perfect for those who want to savor the exotic flavors of Morocco without spending hours in the kitchen. With the help of your trusty slow cooker, you can effortlessly create a Moroccan feast that will impress even the most discerning palates. So, gather your ingredients, set your slow cooker, and let the magic of Morocco unfold in your kitchen. Bon appétit!
