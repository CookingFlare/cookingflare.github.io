Chicken Katsu: Japanese Style Breaded Chicken Cutlet


Are you craving a delicious and crispy chicken dish that’s bursting with flavor? Look no further than Chicken Katsu, a beloved Japanese dish that combines tender chicken cutlets with a crispy breaded coating. This dish is not only easy to make but also incredibly satisfying, making it a perfect choice for a hearty meal. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create your very own Chicken Katsu masterpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce, for serving
  • Shredded cabbage, for serving
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Start by preparing the chicken breasts. Place them on a cutting board and cover with plastic wrap. Using a meat mallet or the flat side of a heavy knife, pound the chicken breasts until they are about ½ inch thick. This will ensure even cooking and a tender texture.

  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides. Set aside.

  3. In three separate shallow bowls, set up your breading station. Place the flour in the first bowl, beaten eggs in the second bowl, and panko breadcrumbs in the third bowl.

  4. Dredge each chicken breast in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip the chicken into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the chicken in the panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the chicken. Repeat this process with all the chicken breasts.

  5. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. You want enough oil to cover the bottom of the skillet to ensure the chicken cooks evenly. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the breaded chicken breasts to the skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy.

  6. Once the chicken cutlets are cooked, transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Repeat the frying process with the remaining chicken breasts.

  7. To serve, slice the Chicken Katsu into strips or leave them whole. Serve alongside a bowl of steamed rice, a generous portion of shredded cabbage, and tonkatsu sauce for dipping. The combination of the crispy chicken, fluffy rice, and refreshing cabbage creates a harmonious and satisfying meal.

Tips:

  • To achieve maximum crispiness, refrigerate the breaded chicken cutlets for at least 30 minutes before frying. This will help the breading adhere to the chicken and prevent it from falling off during cooking.

  • If you prefer a healthier alternative, you can bake the breaded chicken cutlets in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for about 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

  • Tonkatsu sauce is a sweet and tangy Japanese condiment that pairs perfectly with Chicken Katsu. You can find it in most Asian grocery stores or make your own by combining Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, soy sauce, sugar, and a touch of mustard.

  • Feel free to get creative with your toppings and sides. Some popular additions to Chicken Katsu include a drizzle of Japanese mayonnaise, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, or a side of pickled ginger.

Now that you have this incredible Chicken Katsu recipe in your arsenal, you can impress your family and friends with a taste of Japan in the comfort of your own home. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking to try something new, this dish is sure to become a favorite. So put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a crispy and flavorful adventure with Chicken Katsu!
