Family BBQ Recipes: Grilling Ideas for Summer Gatherings


As the summer sun shines bright, there’s nothing like gathering with family and friends to create unforgettable memories. And what better way to do so than with a mouth-watering BBQ feast? In this post, we’ll share a collection of family BBQ recipes and grilling ideas that will make your summer gatherings truly unforgettable.

Classic BBQ Favorites

Before diving into some new and exciting recipes, let’s start with some classic BBQ favorites that are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

Grilled Burgers

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, and any other toppings you like

Mix ground beef, breadcrumbs, onion, bell pepper, garlic, egg, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Form into patties and grill over medium-high heat for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Serve on a toasted bun with your favorite toppings.

Grilled Hot Dogs

  • 4 hot dogs
  • 4 hot dog buns
  • Condiments of your choice (ketchup, mustard, relish, etc.)

Grill hot dogs over medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes, or until nicely charred and cooked through. Serve on a toasted bun with your favorite condiments.

New Twists on Old Favorites

Now that we’ve covered some classic BBQ recipes, let’s get creative with some new twists on old favorites.

Korean BBQ Tacos

  • 1 lb flank steak
  • 2 tbsp Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 8 taco shells
  • Sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, and kimchi for garnish

Marinate flank steak in a mixture of Gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, garlic, and ginger for at least 2 hours. Grill over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Serve in taco shells with sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, and kimchi.

Grilled Veggie Skewers with Chimichurri Sauce

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, onions, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh oregano, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Alternate vegetables on skewers and brush with olive oil. Grill over medium-high heat for 10-12 minutes, or until tender and lightly charred. Meanwhile, combine parsley, oregano, garlic, olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Serve skewers with chimichurri sauce for dipping.

Global Inspirations

Why stick to traditional American BBQ when you can incorporate flavors and ingredients from around the world? Here are a few global inspirations to spice up your summer gatherings.

Japanese-Style Teriyaki Chicken

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup sake (or dry sherry)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Marinate chicken breasts in a mixture of teriyaki sauce, soy sauce, sake, brown sugar, ginger, and garlic for at least 2 hours. Grill over medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Serve with steamed vegetables and a side of sticky rice.

Indian-Style Naan Bread with Garlic Butter

  • 1 package naan bread
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Brush naan bread with garlic butter and grill over medium heat for 2-3 minutes per side, or until crispy and slightly charred. Serve with a side of chutney or raita for dipping.

Tips and Tricks

Before we wrap up, here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind when grilling for your summer gatherings.

Keep It Simple

Don’t feel like you need to make a million different dishes. Keep it simple with a few crowd-pleasing favorites and a few fresh sides.

Plan Ahead

Make a list of what you need to buy and prep ahead of time to ensure a smooth and stress-free grilling experience.

Don’t Forget the Extras

Don’t forget to have plenty of cold drinks, plates, and utensils on hand to make your gathering truly enjoyable.

In conclusion, with these family BBQ recipes and grilling ideas, you’ll be well on your way to hosting unforgettable summer gatherings that your friends and family will be talking about for years to come. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and get ready to make some unforgettable memories!
