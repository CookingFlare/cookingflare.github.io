Sweet and Tangy Maple-Roasted Acorn Squash Recipe for a Seasonal Side Dish


Acorn squash is a delightful seasonal vegetable that is often overlooked in the culinary world. Its sweet and nutty flavor profile pairs perfectly with a tangy maple glaze, creating a mouthwatering dish that is sure to impress your guests. In this recipe, we will show you how to roast acorn squash to perfection and elevate its flavors with a simple yet delicious maple glaze. Let’s get started!

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Place the acorn squash halves, cut side up, on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the acorn squash halves, making sure to coat them evenly. Sprinkle with salt and black pepper.

  4. Roast the acorn squash in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the flesh is fork-tender and caramelized around the edges.

  5. While the acorn squash is roasting, prepare the maple glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and apple cider vinegar until well combined.

  6. Remove the acorn squash from the oven and brush the maple glaze generously over the flesh of each squash half.

  7. Return the acorn squash to the oven and continue roasting for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the glaze is bubbly and slightly caramelized.

  8. Once the acorn squash is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.

  9. Serve the sweet and tangy maple-roasted acorn squash as a side dish to complement your favorite seasonal meals. Garnish with fresh parsley, if desired, for an added touch of freshness.

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle some cinnamon or nutmeg over the acorn squash before roasting.
  • Feel free to adjust the sweetness and tanginess of the maple glaze according to your taste preferences. Add more maple syrup for a sweeter glaze or increase the amount of apple cider vinegar for a tangier kick.
  • If you’re short on time, you can microwave the acorn squash halves for 8-10 minutes before roasting them in the oven. This will help speed up the cooking process.
  • This recipe works well with other types of winter squash, such as butternut or delicata squash. Feel free to experiment and find your favorite flavor combination.

Enjoy the delightful flavors of this sweet and tangy maple-roasted acorn squash recipe as a seasonal side dish. Its comforting taste and beautiful presentation make it a perfect addition to any fall or winter meal. Serve it alongside roasted meats, poultry, or even as a vegetarian main course. The possibilities are endless!

So, next time you come across acorn squash at your local market, don’t hesitate to grab a few and give this recipe a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
