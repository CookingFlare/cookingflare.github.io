Traditional Aligot: A Cheesy French Side Dish


If you’re a cheese lover looking for a delicious and indulgent side dish, then look no further than Traditional Aligot. This cheesy French delicacy is the perfect accompaniment to any meal, adding a creamy and rich element that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of Aligot, its key ingredients, and provide you with a step-by-step recipe to recreate this mouthwatering dish in your own kitchen.

The History of Aligot

Aligot originated in the picturesque region of Aubrac in southern France. It is believed to have been created by the monks of the Aubrac Monastery in the 19th century. The dish quickly gained popularity among locals and soon became a staple in the region. Today, Aligot is synonymous with the gastronomy of Aubrac and is enjoyed throughout France and beyond.

Key Ingredients

To make Traditional Aligot, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 pound of fresh Tomme cheese, preferably from Aubrac (substitute with mozzarella if unavailable)
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

The Recipe

  1. Start by boiling the potatoes in a large pot of salted water until they are tender and easily mashed with a fork. This should take about 15-20 minutes.

  2. While the potatoes are cooking, grate the Tomme cheese and set it aside.

  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Keep an eye on it to prevent it from boiling over.

  4. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them and return them to the pot. Mash them until smooth using a potato masher or a fork.

  5. Add the minced garlic, butter, and grated cheese to the mashed potatoes. Stir well until the cheese has melted and the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  6. Slowly pour in the heated cream while continuously stirring the potato mixture. This will help incorporate the cream and create a silky texture.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and continue stirring until the Aligot reaches a stringy and stretchy consistency.

  8. Serve the Aligot immediately while it’s still hot to fully enjoy its gooey goodness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the traditional recipe, you can add a touch of nutmeg or a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs such as parsley or thyme to enhance the flavors.
  • Aligot pairs exceptionally well with grilled sausages, roasted meats, or even as a standalone dish.
  • If you’re serving Aligot for a larger gathering, you can easily double or triple the recipe to accommodate more guests.

Conclusion

Traditional Aligot is a cheesy French side dish that is sure to impress both cheese enthusiasts and food lovers alike. Its creamy texture and rich flavors make it a perfect accompaniment to any meal. By following our simple recipe, you can recreate this delectable dish in your own kitchen and transport yourself to the beautiful region of Aubrac. So why wait? Gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in the cheesy goodness of Aligot. Bon appétit!
