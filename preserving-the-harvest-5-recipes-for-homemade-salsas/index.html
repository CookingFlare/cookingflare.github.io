Preserving the Harvest: 5 Recipes for Homemade Salsas


Salsas are a versatile and delicious way to preserve the flavors of the harvest season. Whether you have an abundance of tomatoes, peppers, or other fresh produce, making homemade salsas is a great way to enjoy the flavors of summer all year round. In this blog post, we will share five unique recipes for homemade salsas that you can easily prepare and preserve.

1. Classic Tomato Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup of diced onions
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 jalapeno peppers, seeded and diced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, onions, cilantro, minced garlic, and diced jalapeno peppers.
  2. Squeeze the lime juice over the mixture and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Mix well and let the flavors marinate for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  4. To preserve, pour the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes.

2. Roasted Corn and Black Bean Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of roasted corn kernels
  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup of diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the roasted corn kernels, black beans, diced red bell pepper, diced red onion, and chopped cilantro.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salsa ingredients and toss gently to combine.
  4. Allow the flavors to meld for at least 1 hour before serving.
  5. To preserve, transfer the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch headspace. Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes.

3. Mango-Pineapple Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangos, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup of diced pineapple
  • 1/2 cup of diced red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and diced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the diced mangos, pineapple, red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeno pepper, and chopped cilantro.
  2. Squeeze the lime juice over the mixture and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Allow the flavors to meld for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  5. To preserve, spoon the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes.

4. Green Tomatillo Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of tomatillos, husked and quartered
  • 1/2 cup of chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the tomatillos, chopped onion, garlic, jalapeno pepper, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Blend until smooth.
  3. Transfer the salsa to a saucepan and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  4. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. To preserve, pour the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes.

5. Avocado Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled, pitted, and diced
  • 1/2 cup of diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the diced avocados, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, and diced jalapeno pepper.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Gently toss the ingredients together until well combined.
  4. Allow the flavors to meld for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  5. To preserve, spoon the salsa into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace. Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes.

Enjoy the Flavors of Summer All Year Round!

These five homemade salsa recipes are perfect for preserving the bountiful harvest of the summer season. Whether you prefer a classic tomato salsa or want to explore the flavors of roasted corn, mango-pineapple, green tomatillo, or avocado, these recipes have got you covered. By following the preservation instructions, you can enjoy the vibrant and fresh flavors of homemade salsas long after the summer sun has faded. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and start preserving the harvest with these delightful salsa recipes.
