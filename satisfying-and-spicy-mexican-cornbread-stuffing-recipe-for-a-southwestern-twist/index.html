Satisfying and Spicy Mexican Cornbread Stuffing Recipe for a Southwestern Twist


If you’re looking to add a Southwestern twist to your next gathering or simply want to spice up your dinner table, this satisfying and spicy Mexican cornbread stuffing recipe is just what you need. Combining the flavors of traditional cornbread stuffing with the zesty kick of Mexican cuisine, this dish is sure to be a hit with your family and friends. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the Cornbread:

  • 1 1/2 cups yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Stuffing:

  • 1 pound chorizo sausage, casings removed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped

For the Broth:

  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs, beaten

Instructions

1. Prepare the Cornbread:

  • Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  • In a large bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  • In another bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, eggs, and melted butter.
  • Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  • Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown. Let the cornbread cool completely, then crumble it into a large mixing bowl.

2. Cook the Chorizo and Vegetables:

  • In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the chorizo, breaking it into smaller pieces with a spoon, until browned and cooked through.
  • Add the chopped onion, garlic, and red bell pepper to the skillet and sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  • Stir in the black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, and oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, then remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

3. Combine the Ingredients:

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the broth, melted butter, and beaten eggs.
  • Add the crumbled cornbread to the chorizo and vegetable mixture, then pour the broth mixture over the top. Gently toss to combine, ensuring the cornbread is well coated with the liquid.

4. Bake the Stuffing:

  • Transfer the stuffing mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  • Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden and crispy.

5. Serve and Enjoy:

  • Once the stuffing is golden and crispy, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  • Sprinkle the chopped cilantro over the top before serving.
  • This Mexican cornbread stuffing is perfect for accompanying roasted chicken, grilled meats, or as a standalone dish. The combination of flavors and textures will surely impress your guests and leave them craving more.

Final Thoughts

This delightful Mexican cornbread stuffing brings the vibrant and bold flavors of the Southwest to your table. Its satisfying and spicy profile will add an exciting twist to any meal, making it a standout dish for gatherings and special occasions. So, next time you’re in the mood for a culinary adventure, give this recipe a try and watch as it becomes a new family favorite. Happy cooking!
