Irresistible Classic New York Cheesecake Recipe


There’s something timeless and comforting about a classic New York cheesecake. The rich, creamy texture, the delicate balance of sweet and tangy flavors, and the buttery graham cracker crust all come together to create a truly irresistible dessert. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this New York cheesecake recipe is sure to become a staple in your repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling

  • 32 oz cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature

For the Topping

  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until the mixture resembles wet sand.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, using the back of a spoon to create an even layer.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the sour cream and vanilla extract, and beat until well combined.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.

Step 3: Assembling and Baking

  1. Pour the filling over the cooled crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  2. Place the springform pan on a large piece of aluminum foil and fold the foil up around the sides of the pan to prevent water from seeping into the cheesecake.
  3. Place the pan in a large roasting pan and fill the roasting pan with enough hot water to come halfway up the sides of the springform pan.
  4. Bake the cheesecake for 1 hour, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  5. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour, then remove from the roasting pan and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. Once cooled, cover the cheesecake with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.

Step 4: Adding the Topping

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the sour cream, sugar, and vanilla extract.
  2. Carefully spread the topping over the chilled cheesecake.
  3. Return the cheesecake to the refrigerator for another hour to allow the topping to set.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the cheesecake is fully chilled and set, carefully remove it from the springform pan and transfer it to a serving platter. Slice into generous portions and serve with fresh berries, a drizzle of caramel or chocolate sauce, or simply enjoy it as is.

This classic New York cheesecake is a labor of love, but the end result is absolutely worth it. The velvety smooth texture, the indulgent flavor, and the satisfaction of creating such a timeless dessert from scratch are truly rewarding. Whether it’s for a special occasion or just a treat-yourself moment, this New York cheesecake is sure to impress and delight anyone who takes a bite.

So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to embark on a cheesecake-making adventure. Your kitchen will be filled with the aroma of vanilla and cream cheese, and your efforts will be rewarded with the most irresistible classic New York cheesecake you’ve ever tasted. Happy baking!
