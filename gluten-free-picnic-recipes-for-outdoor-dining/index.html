Gluten-Free Picnic Recipes for Outdoor Dining


When planning a picnic or outdoor meal, sharing a delicious, nutritious, and visually appealing dish adds to the overall experience. For those with gluten intolerance, finding tasty gluten-free alternatives can be a challenge. This article highlights unique gluten-free picnic recipes that will delight your guests and enjoy a delightful outdoor dining experience.

Eggplant Caprese Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 Japanese eggplants
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Slice the eggplant lengthwise into 1/4 inch thick rounds and salt lightly.
  2. Layer the eggplant slices on a paper towel-lined tray, separating them with more paper towels, and place a weighted tray on top. Allow the eggplant to rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Rinse the eggplant and pat dry with paper towels.
  4. Prepare the tomatoes and mozzarella and slice the fresh basil.
  5. In a small bowl, mix extra-virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegar to taste.
  6. Arrange the eggplant slices on a serving dish. Top with sliced tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and chopped basil. Drizzle the olive oil and balsamic vinegar mixture on top. Enjoy!

Quinoa Sunshine Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the quinoa according to package instructions and allow it to cool.
  2. Combine quinoa, chickpeas, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and mint in a large bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the quinoa mixture and mix well.
  5. Chill the salad in the refrigerator. Serve cold.

Rosemary-Salted Cashews

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cashews
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Spread cashews on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together melted butter, fresh rosemary, and salt.
  5. Drizzle the butter mixture over the cashews and mix well to evenly coat.
  6. Bake the cashews in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  7. Cool and serve as a delightful snack or as a side dish to complement your gluten-free picnic meal.

Homemade Gluten-Free Hummus

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Salt and ground cumin to taste
  • Water (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine cooked chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and ground cumin in a food processor.
  2. Blend until the ingredients form a smooth paste, scraping down the sides of the food processor as necessary.
  3. If the hummus seems too thick, add water in small amounts until the desired consistency is achieved.
  4. Taste the hummus and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Chill the hummus in the refrigerator until ready to serve.
