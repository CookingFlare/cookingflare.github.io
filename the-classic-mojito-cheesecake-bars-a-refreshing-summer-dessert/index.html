The Classic Mojito Cheesecake Bars: A Refreshing Summer Dessert


The Classic Mojito Cheesecake Bars are the perfect fusion of a classic cocktail and a luscious dessert. With a refreshing hint of mint and a zesty kick of lime, these cheesecake bars are a delightful treat for the summer season. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a poolside party, or simply craving a cool and creamy dessert, these bars are sure to impress your guests and tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • 2 tbsp white rum
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves

For the Mojito Glaze:

  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tbsp white rum
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh mint leaves

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened.
  3. Press the crumb mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom of the prepared pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and allow to cool while preparing the filling.

Step 2: Making the Cheesecake Filling

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  3. Mix in the sour cream, lime juice, lime zest, rum, and vanilla extract until fully combined.
  4. Gently fold in the chopped mint leaves.
  5. Pour the cheesecake filling over the cooled crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.

Step 3: Baking and Chilling

  1. Bake the cheesecake bars for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  2. Remove from the oven and let the bars cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to set.

Step 4: Preparing the Mojito Glaze

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the lime juice, sugar, and water over medium heat. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Remove from the heat and stir in the white rum and chopped mint leaves. Allow the glaze to cool to room temperature.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

  1. Once the cheesecake bars are chilled and set, carefully lift them out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang.
  2. Drizzle the Mojito glaze over the top of the bars, spreading it evenly with a spatula.
  3. Return the bars to the refrigerator for 30 minutes to allow the glaze to set.

Serving and Enjoying

  • Use a sharp knife to cut the cheesecake bars into squares.
  • Garnish with additional fresh mint leaves and lime slices, if desired.
  • Serve chilled and savor the refreshing flavors of a Mojito in every bite.

These Classic Mojito Cheesecake Bars are a delightful way to indulge in the flavors of summer. The creamy texture of the cheesecake, combined with the zesty lime and the subtle kick of rum, makes for a truly irresistible dessert. Whether you’re lounging by the pool, hosting a summer soiree, or simply treating yourself to a sweet escape, these bars are guaranteed to be a hit. So, embrace the spirit of summer and whip up a batch of these delightful treats for a cool and refreshing indulgence!
