Jammin' Good Time: Homemade Fruit Jam Recipes to Preserve


Who doesn’t love the sweet, fruity goodness of homemade jam? Whether spread on toast, swirled into yogurt, or dolloped on scones, homemade fruit jams add a burst of flavor to any dish. Not only are they delicious, but they are also a fantastic way to preserve the fresh flavors of seasonal fruits to enjoy all year round. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delightful homemade fruit jam recipes that are easy to make and will have you “jammin’” in no time.

Strawberry Fields Forever Jam

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 packet powdered pectin

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Let it sit for about an hour until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring frequently.
  3. Stir in the powdered pectin and boil for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and skim off any foam.
  5. Ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4 inch headspace.
  6. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  8. Allow the jars to cool before storing them in a cool, dark place.

This vibrant Strawberry Fields Forever Jam is a taste of summer in a jar, perfect for spreading on warm biscuits or layering between cake layers.

Peachy Keen Peach Jam

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds ripe peaches, peeled, pitted, and chopped
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the peaches, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Increase the heat and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring frequently.
  4. Cook until the jam reaches the desired consistency, about 20-30 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  6. Pour the jam into sterilized jars and seal them.

Peachy Keen Peach Jam is a luscious and fragrant jam that captures the essence of ripe summer peaches. Spread it on warm toast or use it as a glaze for grilled chicken for a burst of flavor.

Berry Berry Good Mixed Berry Jam

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup raspberries
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil and cook until the jam thickens, about 20-25 minutes.
  4. Skim off any foam and ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars.
  5. Seal the jars and process them in a water bath for 10 minutes.

Berry Berry Good Mixed Berry Jam is a delightful blend of summer berries that is bursting with flavor. Enjoy it on pancakes, ice cream, or swirled into yogurt for a sweet treat.

Homemade fruit jams are not only delicious but also make thoughtful gifts for friends and family. Get creative with your favorite fruits, add a hint of spice or herbs for a unique twist, and enjoy the satisfaction of preserving the flavors of the season. Happy jammin'!
