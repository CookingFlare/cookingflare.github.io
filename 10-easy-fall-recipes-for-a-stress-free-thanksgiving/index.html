10 Easy Fall Recipes for a Stress-Free Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for sharing food, gratitude, and laughter with loved ones, but it’s easy to feel overwhelmed when planning the holiday meal. Instead of spending the entire day in the kitchen, why not opt for a selection of simple yet festive recipes? From the comforting classics to creative seasonal dishes, these 10 straightforward recipes will help you craft a delicious Thanksgiving feast without the stress.

1. Maple-Glazed Sweet Potatoes

Sweet potatoes are a quintessential fall ingredient, and this maple-glazed version brings out their natural sweetness with minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and chopped into thick slices
  • 3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the sweet potato slices with maple syrup, melted butter, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Spread the sweet potatoes out on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Roast for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and caramelized.

2. Cranberry Orange Relish

Ditch the canned sauce and try this fresh cranberry relish for a vibrant, zesty addition to your Thanksgiving spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh cranberries
  • 1 large orange, peeled and roughly chopped
  • ½ cup granulated sugar

Instructions:

  1. Place cranberries, orange pieces, and sugar in a food processor.
  2. Pulse until you reach your desired texture—chunky or smooth.
  3. Refrigerate for at least an hour before serving to let the flavors meld together.

3. Herb and Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes don’t have to be complicated to be indulgent. Adding fresh herbs and garlic takes this comforting side dish to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds russet potatoes, peeled and chopped into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ cup milk or cream
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in a large pot of salted water until fork-tender (about 15–20 minutes).
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  3. Add butter, milk, garlic, and parsley, then mash until creamy. Season with salt and pepper.

4. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze

This simple dish brings out the crispy, caramelized flavor of Brussels sprouts while adding the smoky richness of bacon and tanginess of balsamic glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ pounds Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C) and toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, chopped bacon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread onto a baking sheet and roast for about 20–25 minutes, stirring halfway.
  3. Drizzle with balsamic glaze before serving.

5. Classic Sausage and Sage Stuffing

Stuffing is a Thanksgiving must-have, and this easy sausage and sage version combines savory flavors with a fluffy, moist texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of bread, cubed and left out overnight to dry
  • ½ pound ground sausage
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons dried sage
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook sausage until browned, then transfer to a bowl.
  3. In the same skillet, heat butter and sauté onion and celery until softened. Add sage, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix the bread cubes, sausage, and sautéed vegetables in the bowl, then add chicken broth. Toss until the bread is moist but not soggy.
  5. Transfer to a greased baking dish and bake for 30 minutes.

6. Honey Butter Rolls

Freshly baked rolls with honey butter are the perfect addition to your Thanksgiving table—and they’re so easy to whip up.

Ingredients:

  • 1 batch store-bought frozen dinner rolls, thawed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1½ tablespoons honey

Instructions:

  1. Bake rolls according to package instructions.
  2. In a small bowl, mix softened butter and honey until well combined.
  3. Brush the honey butter onto the warm rolls before serving.

7. Pumpkin Soup

A warm bowl of pumpkin soup sets the tone for a cozy Thanksgiving meal. This recipe is a no-fuss option packed with autumn flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) pure pumpkin puree
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine pumpkin puree, broth, cream, garlic, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  2. Simmer over medium heat for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Serve hot with a drizzle of cream or a sprinkle of roasted pumpkin seeds.

8. Harvest Fruit Salad

Light and refreshing, this fruit salad brings a pop of color and brightness to your Thanksgiving dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chopped apples
  • 2 cups chopped pears
  • ½ cup dried cranberries
  • ½ cup pecans, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Instructions:

  1. Toss apples, pears, cranberries, and pecans in a large bowl.
  2. Mix orange juice and honey, then drizzle over the fruit. Stir gently to combine.

9. Sheet Pan Turkey Breast

Cooking an entire turkey can be daunting, but this sheet pan turkey breast gives you all the flavor without the hassle.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in turkey breast (about 4 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Rub the turkey breast with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place on a sheet pan and roast for 45–55 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  3. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

10. Spiced Apple Crisp

End the meal with a dessert that’s as simple as it is satisfying. This spiced apple crisp captures the essence of fall in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 5 cups thinly sliced apples (such as Granny Smith)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Toss apples with lemon juice, brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg, then spread into a greased baking dish.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix oats, flour, and melted butter to create a crumbly topping. Sprinkle over the apples.
  3. Bake for 35–40 minutes or until the topping is golden and the apples are tender.

Thanksgiving doesn’t have to be an all-day culinary marathon to be memorable. By featuring these easy fall recipes, you’ll keep the focus on what truly matters: spending quality time with your family and friends. Whether you’re hosting a big gathering or enjoying a cozy dinner at home, these dishes will ensure that your holiday feast is both delicious and stress-free. Celebrate autumn’s bounty while keeping things simple—and savor every moment of the holiday.
