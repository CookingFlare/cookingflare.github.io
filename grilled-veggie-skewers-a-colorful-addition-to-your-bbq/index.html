Grilled Veggie Skewers: A Colorful Addition to Your BBQ


Nothing screams summer like a vibrant and flavorful grill session with friends and family. Bringing a healthy twist to your barbecue, grilled veggie skewers will not only add an explosion of color but also infuse appetizing flavors to your cookout. This versatile dish allows your guests to participate in the culinary experience, making it an inviting option for all ages.

Getting Started

To bring the delightful idea of veggie skewers to life, you’ll need a variety of vegetables that can be easily threaded onto skewers. Our list includes:

  • Bell peppers
  • Zucchini
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Summer squash
  • Portobello mushrooms
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Sweet potatoes

Though this list offers a variety of options, feel free to explore and experiment with other ingredients that suit your taste. Just keep in mind that tougher vegetables should be skewered with softer ones to promote even cooking.

Creating Your Skewers

Now that the vegetables are chosen, let’s proceed to create enticing skewers. Here is a simple method to achieve the perfect balance of flavor and tenderness:

  1. Soak wooden or metal skewers in water for at least 30 minutes before grilling. This prevents the skewers from burning during grilling.
  2. For optimal flavor, drizzle a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and herbs of your choice over the vegetables before skewering.
  3. Load the skewers with alternating vegetables, ensuring the ingredients are evenly distributed and the stalks are lined up.

Grilling it Up

Once your skewers are ready, you can bring them to your barbecue. Follow these steps to achieve succulent and mouthwatering grilled veggie skewers:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place skewers on the grill, either directly onto the grates or on a vegetable-specific grilling tray. If you’re using wooden skewers, start by placing them with the ends facing the bars to ensure they don’t fall through.
  3. Cook the skewers for approximately 8-10 minutes, turning them frequently to prevent burning. It’s essential to keep your vegetables neither too soft nor too firm. If using tender vegetables like squash, expect them to cook quicker.
  4. Remove the grilled veggie skewers from the grill and let them cool slightly. This will allow you to better handle the skewers while serving.
  5. Gently remove the vegetables from the skewers and serve them in a colorful display, garnished with your choice of herbs or sauces.

Conclusion

Adding grilled veggie skewers to your barbecue menu brings an appealing colorful addition and a refreshing break from the typically heavy barbecue fare. Incorporating an assortment of vegetables allows you to benefit from their diverse nutrients as you relish the flavors and the outdoor cooking experience. Remember, experimenting with vegetables is half the fun of this colorful, delightful dish. Enjoy the goodness of veggie skewers as a wholesome and gratifying testimony to grill season.
