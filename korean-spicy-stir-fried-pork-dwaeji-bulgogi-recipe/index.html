Korean Spicy Stir-Fried Pork (Dwaeji Bulgogi) Recipe


When it comes to Korean food, few dishes can compete with the bold, fiery, and tantalizing flavors of Dwaeji Bulgogi, the famous spicy stir-fried pork that has captured the hearts and taste buds of food lovers everywhere. Packed with savory, spicy, and sweet notes, this dish is a showstopper that easily elevates any dinner table. If you’re looking to explore authentic Korean cooking, this recipe is a fantastic way to experience the rich culinary tradition of Korea without stepping outside your kitchen.

What Makes Dwaeji Bulgogi Special?

The name “Dwaeji Bulgogi” literally translates to “pork fire meat,” which perfectly encapsulates its essence—both spicy and sizzling. Unlike the beef version of Bulgogi, which tends to lean toward a sweet and mild flavor profile, Dwaeji Bulgogi is all about combining robust heat with complex layers of taste. The key lies in its marinade, crafted from a perfect blend of Korean chili paste (gochujang), soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and other pantry staples.

Not only is Dwaeji Bulgogi irresistibly delicious, but it’s also remarkably versatile. Serve it with a steaming bowl of rice, wrap it in crisp lettuce leaves for a handheld treat, or pair it with a variety of side dishes (banchan) for a feast fit for royalty.

Let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this iconic dish at home.

Ingredients

To create authentic Dwaeji Bulgogi, start by assembling fresh ingredients. Traditional Korean cooking emphasizes quality and balance, so using the proper ingredients ensures the best results.

Pork:

  • 1 lb (450 g) pork shoulder, thinly sliced
    (Alternatively, use pork belly or pork loin for different textures.)

Marinade:

  • 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili powder)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp honey or sugar
  • 1 tbsp rice wine (optional, or substitute with mirin)
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 small onion, grated (adds sweetness)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Vegetables (Optional but recommended for texture and color):

  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 small bell pepper (red or green), sliced

Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds, toasted
  • Additional chopped green onions

Side Suggestions:

  • Steamed white rice or sticky rice
  • Fresh lettuce leaves (such as romaine or green leaf) for wrapping

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Begin by slicing the pork. If your cut of meat is thick, freeze it partially for about 15–20 minutes to make slicing easier. Aim for thin, bite-sized pieces to ensure quick and even cooking. Once sliced, set the pork aside while you prepare the marinade.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

The marinade is the heart of Dwaeji Bulgogi and where all the magic happens. In a mixing bowl, combine gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey (or sugar), rice wine (if using), minced garlic, grated ginger, and onion. Mix the ingredients thoroughly until a smooth, reddish paste forms. Taste the marinade and adjust according to your preference. For added heat, sprinkle in more gochugaru. If you want a slightly sweeter dimension, increase the amount of honey or sugar.

Step 3: Marinate the Pork

Add the sliced pork to the marinade and toss until each piece is evenly coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator. For the most robust flavor, marinate it for 2–3 hours—or even overnight if time allows.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Pork

Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add vegetable oil to prevent sticking. Once the pan is hot, add the marinated pork, spreading it out in an even layer. Stir-fry the pork for about 5–7 minutes, allowing the marinade to caramelize and coat the meat beautifully.

If you’re adding vegetables, toss them into the pan about halfway through the cooking process. Stir-fry everything together until the vegetables are tender and crisp and the pork is cooked through with a slight char.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the stir-fried pork to a serving plate. Sprinkle generously with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions for a touch of freshness. Serve hot with steamed rice and fresh lettuce leaves for wrapping. The combination of spicy pork, fluffy rice, and crisp lettuce creates an explosion of textures and flavors in every bite.

Cooking Tips and Variations

  • Choose Your Cut of Pork: Pork belly is fatty and rich, while pork shoulder or loin is leaner. Select the cut that best suits your preferences.
  • Grill or Barbecue Option: For a smokier flavor, grill the marinated pork over charcoal or in a grilling pan. This method adds a delicious charred edge.
  • Vegetarian Twist: While Dwaeji Bulgogi traditionally uses pork, you can substitute the meat with firm tofu or mushrooms for a vegetarian version. Keep the marinade the same to retain the signature flavors.
  • Adjust Heat Levels: If you’re sensitive to spice, use less gochugaru or gochujang. Conversely, if you love fiery flavors, double the chili powder for added heat.
  • Pair with Banchan: Complement the meal with iconic Korean side dishes like kimchi, pickled radishes, or stir-fried spinach for added authenticity.

Why Make Dwaeji Bulgogi at Home?

Dwaeji Bulgogi offers a simple yet authentic way to explore Korean cuisine. Making it at home gives you complete control over the ingredients, spice levels, and portion sizes. Unlike restaurant meals, cooking at home allows you to experiment and adapt the recipe to suit your palate. Plus, homemade meals always come with a sense of accomplishment—especially when the dish turns out delicious enough to rival your favorite Korean spot!

Conclusion

Dwaeji Bulgogi is more than just a recipe—it’s a transformative culinary experience that brings the excitement of Korean flavors to your kitchen. With its spicy, savory marinade and caramelized finish, this dish encapsulates everything we love about Korean cooking: bold taste, vibrant color, and the perfect harmony of ingredients.

Whether you’re new to Korean cuisine or an enthusiastic fan looking for your next favorite recipe, Dwaeji Bulgogi is an excellent addition to your repertoire. Prepare it once, and you’ll find yourself coming back to it time and time again, savoring every fiery and flavorful bite. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to ignite your taste buds with this mouthwatering dish! Happy cooking!
