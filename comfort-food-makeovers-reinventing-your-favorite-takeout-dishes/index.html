Comfort Food Makeovers: Reinventing Your Favorite Takeout Dishes


As we all know, takeout can be a convenient and delicious solution for a quick meal. But let’s be honest, the convenience often comes at the cost of nutritional value and culinary creativity. What if you could take your favorite takeout dishes and give them a makeover, making them not only healthier but also more flavorful and satisfying? In this post, we’ll explore how to reinvent your favorite takeout dishes and make them a staple in your household.

Chinese Takeout Makeovers

Let’s start with Chinese takeout, which is often high in sodium and sugar. We’ll take a classic dish like General Tso’s chicken and give it a makeover.

  • Original recipe: deep-fried chicken pieces coated in a sweet and spicy sauce, served with steamed rice and stir-fried vegetables.
  • Makeover recipe: baked chicken breast coated in a mixture of cornstarch, egg whites, and spices, then tossed in a homemade General Tso’s sauce made with honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili flakes. Serve with brown rice and roasted vegetables.

Another Chinese takeout classic is Kung Pao chicken. Here’s how to give it a makeover:

  • Original recipe: deep-fried chicken pieces stir-fried with peanuts, vegetables, and chili peppers in a sweet and spicy sauce, served with steamed rice.
  • Makeover recipe: baked chicken breast stir-fried with roasted peanuts, bell peppers, and snow peas in a homemade Kung Pao sauce made with soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and chili flakes. Serve with brown rice and steamed broccoli.

Italian Takeout Makeovers

Italian takeout can be high in calories and fat. Let’s take a classic dish like lasagna and give it a makeover.

  • Original recipe: layered pasta sheets with ground beef ragù, ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella, served with garlic bread and a side salad.
  • Makeover recipe: layered zucchini noodles with ground turkey ragù, ricotta cheese, and melted part-skim mozzarella, served with a side salad and whole-grain garlic bread.

Another Italian takeout classic is chicken parmesan. Here’s how to give it a makeover:

  • Original recipe: breaded and fried chicken breast topped with marinara sauce and melted mozzarella, served with spaghetti and garlic bread.
  • Makeover recipe: baked chicken breast topped with homemade marinara sauce made with canned crushed tomatoes, garlic, and olive oil, and melted part-skim mozzarella. Serve with whole-grain spaghetti and a side salad.

Mexican Takeout Makeovers

Mexican takeout can be high in sodium and saturated fat. Let’s take a classic dish like nachos and give it a makeover.

  • Original recipe: tortilla chips smothered in melted cheese, refried beans, and ground beef, served with a side of salsa and sour cream.
  • Makeover recipe: baked tortilla chips topped with black beans, roasted vegetables, and shredded reduced-fat cheese, served with a side of homemade salsa made with diced tomatoes, onions, jalapeños, and cilantro.

Another Mexican takeout classic is fajitas. Here’s how to give it a makeover:

  • Original recipe: sizzling skillet of beef or chicken strips cooked with bell peppers and onions, served with warm flour tortillas, sour cream, and salsa.
  • Makeover recipe: grilled chicken breast or beef strips cooked with roasted bell peppers and onions, served with whole-grain flour tortillas, homemade salsa, and a side of sautéed spinach.

Conclusion

As you can see, giving your favorite takeout dishes a makeover can be a simple and delicious way to improve their nutritional value and flavor. By making a few simple substitutions and adjustments, you can create healthier and more satisfying meals that your whole family will love. So next time you’re craving takeout, try giving one of your favorite dishes a makeover and experience the difference for yourself.
