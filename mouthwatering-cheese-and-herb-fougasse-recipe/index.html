Mouthwatering Cheese and Herb Fougasse Recipe


If you’re looking to impress your friends and family with a delicious homemade bread that’s bursting with flavor, then this Cheese and Herb Fougasse recipe is perfect for you. Fougasse is a traditional French bread known for its leaf-like shape and versatility in terms of toppings. With a crispy crust, soft interior, and savory cheese and herb filling, this bread is sure to be a hit at any gathering or mealtime. Let’s dive into the recipe and bring the taste of France to your kitchen!

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (such as Gruyère or Parmesan)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh herbs (such as rosemary and thyme), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, mix the warm water with yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until frothy.

  2. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Slowly pour in the yeast mixture while stirring. Mix until a dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.

  4. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place a baking stone or an upside-down baking sheet in the oven to heat.

  5. Shape the Fougasse: Punch down the risen dough and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out each portion into an oval shape, then use a sharp knife to make leaf-like cuts on each bread.

  6. Add Toppings: Brush the dough with olive oil, then sprinkle the shredded cheese, fresh herbs, salt, and pepper over the top.

  7. Bake the Bread: Carefully transfer the shaped dough onto the preheated baking stone or sheet. Bake for about 20-25 minutes or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Once baked, let the Cheese and Herb Fougasse cool slightly before slicing. Serve it warm with extra olive oil for dipping or alongside your favorite soup or salad.

Tips:

  • Experiment with different cheese and herb combinations to suit your taste preferences.
  • You can add a sprinkle of sea salt flakes on top before baking for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Fougasse is best enjoyed fresh on the day it’s baked, but you can store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days.

With its rustic appearance and rich flavors, this Cheese and Herb Fougasse is a delightful addition to any meal. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to bread-making, this recipe is sure to elevate your culinary skills and bring a touch of French sophistication to your table. Give it a try and savor the joy of creating homemade bread that’s both delicious and impressive!

Remember, the key to a perfect Fougasse lies in the quality of your ingredients and the love you put into the preparation. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your kitchen tools, and embark on a culinary journey that will leave your taste buds singing with delight!
