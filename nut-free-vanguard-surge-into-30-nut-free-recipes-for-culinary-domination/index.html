Nut-Free Vanguard: Surge into 30 Nut-Free Recipes for Culinary Domination


Are you craving for delectable dishes but worried about nut allergies? Look no further! Experience the world of culinary domination without compromising your health, courtesy of the Nut-Free Vanguard. With 30 irresistible recipes, you’ll no longer have to shy away from indulging in tantalizing flavors and textures. Let us set sail on this nut-free adventure, and prepare to be amazed.

1. Nut-Free Guacamole

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, mashed
  • 1/4 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup black beans, mashed

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the mashed avocados, tomatoes, red onion, cilantro, and lime juice.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Mix in mashed black beans and give it a good stir.
  4. Serve with chips or veggie sticks, and voila!

2. Nut-Free Chai Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup soy milk
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine soy milk, cinnamon, ginger, cardamom, and nutmeg. Heat gently until fragrant.
  3. Allow mixture to cool slightly before adding mashed bananas and vegan butter.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together granulated sugar, all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Grease a loaf pan with some vegan butter, and pour the batter in.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the bread to cool before slicing.

3. Nut-Free Thai Basil Chickpea Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp. cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp. vegan brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp. fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 cups fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu into 1/2-inch cubes and then gently toss with soy sauce and cornstarch to coat.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the pan and set it aside.
  3. In the same pan, add garlic and ginger, sauteing until fragrant.
  4. Stir in vegetable broth and vegan brown sugar, and bring it to a simmer.
  5. Return the tofu to the pan and cook for a few more minutes.
  6. Add the bell peppers and Thai basil leaves to the pan, cooking until peppers are tender.
  7. Season with salt to taste and serve immediately on a bed of your favorite rice.

4. Nut-Free Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, halved lengthwise and seeded
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup corn
  • 1/2 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar-style vegan cheese
  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Arrange bell pepper halves on a baking sheet, cut-sides up. Bake for 15 minutes.
  3. In a bowl, combine quinoa, black beans, corn, onion, tomatoes, cilantro, vegan cheese, olive oil, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Remove bell peppers from the oven and fill each half with the quinoa mixture.
  5. Return to the oven and bake for another 20-25 minutes, until the vegan cheese is melted and the peppers are tender.
  6. Serve and enjoy your flavorful quinoa-stuffed bell peppers!
