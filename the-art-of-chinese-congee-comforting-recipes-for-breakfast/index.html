The Art of Chinese Congee: Comforting Recipes for Breakfast


Congee, also known as rice porridge, is a staple in Chinese cuisine, especially for breakfast. It is a simple yet satisfying dish that warms the body and nourishes the soul. In this blog post, we will explore the art of making Chinese congee and share some comforting recipes that will elevate your breakfast game. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

What is Congee?

Congee is a traditional Chinese dish made by simmering rice in a large quantity of water or broth until it breaks down and becomes a thick, porridge-like consistency. It is often enjoyed as a breakfast dish or as a comforting meal for those who are feeling under the weather. Congee can be made with various ingredients, such as meats, seafood, vegetables, and condiments, depending on personal preference and regional variations.

The Basic Congee Recipe

To make a basic congee, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup of rice (jasmine or short-grain rice works best)
  • 8 cups of water or broth (chicken or vegetable)
  • Salt, to taste
  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch and helps to achieve a smoother texture.
  2. In a large pot, bring the water or broth to a boil over high heat.
  3. Add the rinsed rice to the boiling liquid and stir well.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the rice simmer, partially covered, for about 1 to 1.5 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. As the rice cooks, it will break down and thicken. If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use a whisk or immersion blender to further break down the rice.
  6. Season with salt to taste and adjust the thickness by adding more water or broth if desired.
  7. Serve hot and garnish with your choice of toppings (more on this later!).

Variations and Toppings

One of the joys of congee is its versatility. You can customize it to your liking by adding various ingredients and toppings. Here are a few popular variations:

1. Century Egg and Pork Congee

  • 1 cup of cooked basic congee
  • 1 century egg, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of cooked minced pork
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish
  1. Heat the cooked basic congee in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced century egg and minced pork, stirring gently to combine.
  3. Cook for a few minutes until the ingredients are heated through.
  4. Ladle the congee into bowls and garnish with chopped green onions.

2. Fish and Ginger Congee

  • 1 cup of cooked basic congee
  • 1/2 cup of flaked cooked fish (such as cod or salmon)
  • 1 tablespoon of grated ginger
  • Soy sauce, to taste
  • Sesame oil, for drizzling
  • Cilantro leaves, for garnish
  1. Heat the cooked basic congee in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the flaked cooked fish and grated ginger, stirring gently to combine.
  3. Cook for a few minutes until the ingredients are heated through.
  4. Season with soy sauce to taste.
  5. Ladle the congee into bowls and drizzle with sesame oil.
  6. Garnish with cilantro leaves.

3. Vegetarian Congee

  • 1 cup of cooked basic congee
  • Assorted vegetables, thinly sliced (carrots, mushrooms, bok choy, etc.)
  • Tofu, diced
  • Soy sauce or tamari, to taste
  • Sesame oil, for drizzling
  • Chopped scallions, for garnish
  1. Heat the cooked basic congee in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the assorted vegetables and tofu, stirring gently to combine.
  3. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender and the tofu is heated through.
  4. Season with soy sauce or tamari to taste.
  5. Ladle the congee into bowls and drizzle with sesame oil.
  6. Garnish with chopped scallions.

Final Thoughts

Chinese congee is not just a breakfast dish; it is a culinary art that brings comfort and nourishment to those who enjoy it. With its versatility and endless possibilities for customization, congee can be tailored to suit any taste or dietary preference. So why not start your day with a bowl of warm and comforting congee? Whether you prefer it simple or loaded with toppings, one thing is for sure – Chinese congee will keep you coming back for more.
