Easy Nut-Free Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe

3 min · 583 words

Are you looking for a quick and delicious dinner idea that’s also nut-free? Look no further! This Easy Nut-Free Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe is perfect for those with nut allergies or anyone who wants to enjoy a flavorful stir-fry without the worry of nuts. With a mix of fresh vegetables, tender chicken, and a savory sauce, this stir-fry is sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 cup carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce and cornstarch. Add the sliced chicken and toss to coat. Set aside to marinate for 10 minutes.

  2. Heat one tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-6 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced bell peppers, onion, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.

  4. Add the broccoli, snow peas, and carrots to the skillet. Stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce into the skillet and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.

  6. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss to coat with the sauce. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to heat through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve the Easy Nut-Free Chicken Stir-Fry over cooked rice or noodles. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize this stir-fry recipe by adding your favorite vegetables. Try adding mushrooms, zucchini, or snap peas for extra flavor and texture.
  • For a spicy kick, add some red pepper flakes or sriracha sauce to the sauce mixture.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can substitute tofu or tempeh for the chicken.
  • To make this dish gluten-free, use tamari or gluten-free soy sauce instead of the regular soy sauce.
  • Feel free to garnish your stir-fry with fresh cilantro, green onions, or sesame seeds for added visual appeal.

Now you have a delicious and nut-free chicken stir-fry recipe that’s quick and easy to make. It’s a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste preferences. Enjoy this flavorful stir-fry for a satisfying weeknight dinner or impress your guests with its vibrant colors and delicious flavors. Bon appétit!
