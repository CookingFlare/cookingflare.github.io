How to Make Tamales: A Mexican Holiday Tradition


Tamales are a cherished staple in Mexican cuisine, especially during the holiday season. The soft, flavorful masa (corn dough) wrapped around a delicious filling has made tamales a beloved dish for generations. Making tamales is not only about the food; it’s a beautiful tradition that brings families together. In this guide, we will explore the history of tamales, the ingredients needed, and step-by-step instructions on how to make them, ensuring you can recreate this delightful dish in your own kitchen.

The Rich History of Tamales

Tamales have a long and storied history in Mexican culture, dating back to ancient Mesoamerican civilizations. The word “tamale” comes from the Nahuatl word “tamalli,” which means “wrapped.” Historically, tamales were portable meals for warriors, travelers, and laborers. They were made by wrapping masa around various fillings, such as meats, beans, and vegetables, then steaming them in corn husks or banana leaves.

Tamales are typically enjoyed during festive occasions, particularly during Christmas and the Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead). The process of making tamales is often a communal activity, where family and friends gather to prepare the ingredients, fill the tamales, and share stories. This sense of community and togetherness is what makes tamales so special.

Ingredients for Making Tamales

To make tamales, you will need a selection of ingredients that include masa, fillings, and cooking tools. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need:

For the Masa

  • 4 cups masa harina: This is a special corn flour used for tamales. Look for masa harina labeled specifically for tamales.
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder: This helps the masa rise slightly, giving it a light texture.
  • 1 teaspoon salt: Enhances the flavor of the masa.
  • 1 cup lard or vegetable shortening: Provides richness and moisture to the masa. For a vegetarian option, use vegetable shortening.
  • 2 to 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth: Adjust based on the desired consistency of the masa.

For the Filling

The filling can vary based on personal preference. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Traditional Pork Filling:

    • 2 pounds pork shoulder: Cooked and shredded.
    • 1 onion: Chopped.
    • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
    • 2 cups red enchilada sauce: Store-bought or homemade.

  2. Chicken Filling:

    • 2 pounds chicken breast: Cooked and shredded.
    • 1 can green salsa: For flavor.
    • 1 teaspoon cumin: Adds warmth to the filling.

  3. Vegetarian Filling:

    • 2 cups black beans: Cooked and mashed.
    • 1 cup corn: Fresh or canned.
    • 1 teaspoon chili powder: For spice.
    • 1 bell pepper: Diced.

Additional Ingredients

  • Corn husks: Soaked in warm water for softening.
  • Banana leaves (optional): For a unique flavor and presentation.
  • Steamer pot or large pot with a steaming rack: To steam the tamales.

Preparing the Ingredients

Step 1: Soak the Corn Husks

Start by soaking the corn husks in warm water for about 30 minutes. This will soften them, making them easier to fold and wrap around the masa. If using banana leaves, cut them into rectangles and quickly blanch them in hot water to make them pliable.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the husks soak, prepare your filling of choice. If you’re going with the traditional pork filling, simmer the pork shoulder with onions and garlic in water until tender (about 2 hours). Once cooked, shred the pork and mix it with the enchilada sauce. For chicken, follow the same method with shredded chicken and green salsa. For the vegetarian filling, simply mix the black beans, corn, diced bell pepper, and spices together in a bowl.

Step 3: Make the Masa

In a large mixing bowl, combine the masa harina, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, beat the lard or vegetable shortening until fluffy. Gradually add the masa mixture to the lard, alternating with the broth, until you achieve a smooth and spreadable consistency. The masa should be moist but not too wet.

Assembling the Tamales

Now that your masa and filling are ready, it’s time to assemble the tamales.

Step 4: Spread the Masa

Take a soaked corn husk and lay it flat with the wider end facing you. Using a spatula or the back of a spoon, spread about 2 tablespoons of masa onto the husk, leaving about an inch of space at the top and sides. Aim for an even layer that covers most of the husk.

Step 5: Add the Filling

Place about 1 tablespoon of your desired filling in the center of the masa. Be careful not to overfill, as this can make wrapping difficult.

Step 6: Fold and Wrap

Fold the sides of the husk in toward the center, covering the filling. Then, fold the bottom of the husk up over the masa to secure the filling inside. If using banana leaves, wrap similarly, securing the ends with kitchen twine if necessary.

Step 7: Repeat

Continue the process until all the masa and filling are used. You should have a lovely batch of tamales ready for steaming.

Steaming the Tamales

Step 8: Prepare the Steamer

Fill a large pot with water and place a steaming rack inside. The water level should be below the rack to avoid soaking the tamales. Bring the water to a simmer.

Step 9: Arrange the Tamales

Stand the tamales upright in the steamer, with the open end facing up. Pack them snugly to prevent them from falling over. If you have leftover corn husks, you can place them over the top to help retain steam.

Step 10: Steam

Cover the pot with a lid and steam the tamales for about 1 to 1.5 hours. Check the water level occasionally and add more if necessary to keep the steam going. The tamales are done when the masa pulls away easily from the husk.

Enjoying Your Tamales

Once your tamales are cooked, remove them from the steamer and let them cool slightly before serving. Tamales are best enjoyed fresh, but they can also be frozen for later use. To serve, unwrap the tamales and enjoy them with a side of salsa, guacamole, or sour cream.

Tamales are more than just a meal; they are a celebration of culture, tradition, and family. Whether you’re making them for a holiday feast or just for fun, the joy of preparing and sharing tamales is an experience to cherish.

Tips for Making Perfect Tamales

  1. Consistency is Key: The masa should be soft but not too wet. Adjust the amount of broth as needed.
  2. Experiment with Fillings: Don’t hesitate to get creative with your fillings. Try different meats, cheeses, or even sweet fillings for dessert tamales.
  3. Get the Family Involved: Making tamales is a perfect activity for family gatherings. Assign different tasks, and enjoy the process together.
  4. Store Properly: If freezing, wrap tamales tightly in plastic wrap and store them in an airtight container. Steam them directly from frozen when you’re ready to enjoy.

Conclusion

In conclusion, making tamales is a time-honored tradition that embodies the essence of Mexican culture. From the rich history to the communal cooking experience, tamales are a celebration of flavors and family. With this guide, you can confidently embark on your tamale-making journey. So gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and start creating a delicious batch of tamales that will warm your heart and home. Happy cooking!
