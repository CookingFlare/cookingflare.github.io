Taste of the Far East: 6 Delicious Recipes from Indonesia


Indonesia, an archipelago of over 17,000 islands, is a treasure trove of culinary delights. The country’s diverse culture and rich history have given rise to a vibrant food scene that reflects its unique blend of indigenous traditions and foreign influences. From the spicy flavors of Java to the sweet and savory dishes of Bali, Indonesian cuisine offers something for everyone. In this blog post, we will explore six delicious recipes that capture the essence of the Far East, allowing you to bring a taste of Indonesia into your kitchen.

1. Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 red chilies, sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced cucumber and tomatoes for garnish
  • Fried shallots for topping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: If using freshly cooked rice, spread it out on a tray to cool and dry slightly. Day-old rice works best as it is less sticky.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, sautéing until fragrant and translucent.

  3. Add Vegetables: Stir in the sliced chilies, diced carrot, and green peas. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  4. Incorporate Rice: Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps. Pour in the sweet soy sauce and regular soy sauce, mixing well to ensure the rice is evenly coated.

  5. Scramble Eggs: Push the rice mixture to one side of the skillet and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked, then mix them into the rice.

  6. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with sliced cucumber, tomatoes, and a sprinkle of fried shallots.

2. Satay Ayam (Chicken Satay)

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Skewers (soaked in water if wooden)

Peanut Sauce:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate Chicken: In a bowl, combine vegetable oil, soy sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, minced garlic, ground coriander, and turmeric powder. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare Peanut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine peanut butter, coconut milk, sweet soy sauce, lime juice, and chili paste. Heat over low heat, stirring until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste and set aside.

  3. Skewer Chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the soaked skewers.

  4. Grill Satay: Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks.

  5. Serve: Serve the chicken satay hot with the peanut sauce on the side for dipping.

3. Rendang Daging (Beef Rendang)

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef, cut into cubes
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 piece of galangal (or ginger), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste

Spice Paste:

  • 6 shallots
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 5 red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
