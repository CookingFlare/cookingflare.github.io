Master the Art of Japanese Tempura with These Authentic Recipes


Tempura is a beloved Japanese dish that has gained popularity all over the world. With its light and crispy batter coating, tempura offers a delightful contrast to the tender and flavorful ingredients it encases. If you are a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary skills, mastering the art of tempura is a must.

In this blog post, we will share with you some authentic and mouthwatering tempura recipes that will transport your taste buds straight to Japan. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Classic Shrimp Tempura

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined, leaving the tail intact
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by preparing the batter. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Gradually add the ice-cold water to the dry ingredients while whisking gently. Be careful not to overmix the batter; a few lumps are fine.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot to a temperature of around 350°F (180°C).
  4. Take a shrimp by the tail and dip it into the batter, making sure it is fully coated. Allow any excess batter to drip off.
  5. Carefully place the shrimp into the hot oil and fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until the batter turns golden brown and crispy. Repeat with the remaining shrimp, frying a few at a time to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  6. Once cooked, transfer the shrimp to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt while they are still hot.
  7. Serve the shrimp tempura immediately with a side of soy sauce or tempura dipping sauce.

Vegetable Tempura

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • Assorted vegetables such as sweet potatoes, bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the batter by combining the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and a pinch of salt in a large bowl.
  2. Gradually add the ice-cold water to the dry ingredients while whisking gently. Again, avoid overmixing the batter.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot to a temperature of around 350°F (180°C).
  4. Dip the vegetable slices into the batter, ensuring they are well-coated, and let any excess batter drip off.
  5. Carefully place the vegetables into the hot oil and fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until they become crispy and golden brown. Fry in batches to maintain the oil temperature.
  6. Once cooked, remove the vegetables from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Sprinkle with salt while still hot.
  7. Serve the vegetable tempura immediately with a side of soy sauce or tempura dipping sauce.

Tips for Perfect Tempura:

  • Use ice-cold water: The cold water helps to create a light and crispy batter.
  • Don’t overmix the batter: A few lumps in the batter will actually contribute to its texture.
  • Fry in small batches: This ensures that the temperature of the oil remains constant, resulting in evenly cooked tempura.
  • Serve immediately: Tempura is best enjoyed when it’s hot and crispy, so serve it right away.

Now that you have these authentic tempura recipes and some handy tips, you are ready to embark on your tempura-making journey. Whether you choose to indulge in shrimp tempura or explore the world of vegetable tempura, the art of tempura will surely elevate your cooking skills and impress your family and friends. So, put on your chef’s hat, gather your ingredients, and start frying up some delicious tempura!
