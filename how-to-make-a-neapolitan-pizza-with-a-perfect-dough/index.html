How to Make a Neapolitan Pizza with a Perfect Dough


Neapolitan pizza has an unmistakable charm and flavor that has captivated food lovers worldwide. With its pillowy, airy crust, vibrant tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and hints of fresh basil, this Italian classic is a celebration of simplicity and authenticity. While perfecting a Neapolitan pizza may seem daunting, crafting one at home is entirely achievable with the right recipe and techniques. Below, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about making Neapolitan pizza, starting from the all-important dough to assembling and baking it to perfection.

Crafting the Perfect Dough: The Foundation of Neapolitan Pizza

The dough is the backbone of any great Neapolitan pizza. To achieve its soft, chewy texture and characteristic bubbles, sticking to traditional methods is key. Neapolitan pizza dough requires simple ingredients, patience, and a careful kneading process. Here’s the detailed breakdown:

Ingredients for Neapolitan Pizza Dough

  • 500 g (approx. 4 cups) of fine Italian 00 flour (alternatively use all-purpose flour, but 00 flour yields optimal results).
  • 325 mL (1 ⅓ cups) of water, room temperature.
  • 2 g (½ tsp) of fine sea salt.
  • 1 g (¼ tsp) of fresh or active dry yeast.
  • 10 mL (2 tsp) of olive oil (optional, for flexibility in dough handling).

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making the Dough

1. Start by Activating the Yeast

In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in lukewarm water and allow it to sit for about 5 minutes until slightly foamy. This step ensures your yeast is alive and ready to start fermenting the dough.

2. Mix the Flour and Water

In a large mixing bowl, add the flour and gradually pour in the water while stirring slowly with a wooden spoon. Once the mixture begins to form a shaggy dough, it’s time to incorporate the yeast.

3. Add the Yeast and Salt

Pour the activated yeast into the dough mixture and knead gently with your hands or a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook. Add the salt in small increments near the end of this process (never add salt directly to the yeast at the beginning, as this can inhibit its activity).

4. Knead Until Smooth and Elastic

Turn the dough onto a clean surface dusted with flour and knead it for about 8–10 minutes until it achieves a smooth, elastic texture. If you’re using a stand mixer, knead on medium speed for approximately 6 minutes.

5. Let It Rest and Rise

Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a large bowl lightly brushed with olive oil. Cover the bowl loosely with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let the dough rise for 8–10 hours at room temperature. For a slower fermentation (which enhances flavor), refrigerate the dough for up to 24 hours, taking care to bring it back to room temperature before use.

Shaping the Dough: A Delicate Art

The beauty of Neapolitan pizza lies in its handcrafted crust. Shaping the dough requires finesse to retain its airy structure without deflating it. Here’s how:

  1. Divide the Dough
    Once the dough has completed its fermentation, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Divide it into roughly 250-gram portions (perfect for one pizza). Roll each portion into a smooth ball.

  2. Flatten with Gentle Pressure
    Gently press one ball into a disc shape using your fingertips—start from the center and work your way outward, leaving a slightly thicker edge to form the crust. Avoid using a rolling pin, as it can crush the delicate air pockets within the dough.

  3. Stretch the Dough
    Pick up the disc, holding it by the edge, and rotate it slowly while allowing the dough’s weight to stretch it naturally. Aim for a diameter of roughly 10–12 inches.

Creating the Perfect Sauce: Simplicity Reigns Supreme

Neapolitan pizza sauce is simplicity at its finest, made with just a handful of fresh, high-quality ingredients. Here’s the ideal recipe:

Ingredients for Neapolitan Pizza Sauce

  • 400 g (14 oz) of canned San Marzano tomatoes (or any high-quality plum tomatoes).
  • 1–2 cloves of garlic, finely minced.
  • A pinch of fine sea salt.
  • A drizzle of olive oil.
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn into small pieces (optional).

Instructions for the Sauce

  1. Blend the Tomatoes
    Use a blender to pulse the canned tomatoes until smooth but not overly processed. Alternatively, crush the tomatoes manually with your hands for a rustic texture.

  2. Season the Sauce
    Mix in minced garlic, a pinch of salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. Keep the flavors understated—the tomatoes should be the star of the show. Add a few torn basil leaves if desired.

  3. No Cooking Needed
    Neapolitan pizza sauce requires no pre-cooking. The intense heat of the pizza oven will cook the sauce atop the dough, preserving its bright flavor.

Building and Topping the Pizza

With the dough and sauce ready to go, it’s time to assemble your masterpiece.

Essential Ingredients for Toppings

  • Fresh mozzarella or buffalo mozzarella, torn into small chunks.
  • Fresh basil leaves, whole or torn.
  • Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling.

Assembling the Pizza

  1. Spread the Sauce
    Spoon a thin layer of tomato sauce onto the stretched pizza base, spreading it evenly but leaving a 1-inch border for the crust.

  2. Add Mozzarella
    Scatter chunks of fresh mozzarella evenly over the sauce. Avoid overloading the pizza with toppings—Neapolitan pizza relies on balance and simplicity.

  3. Top with Basil and Oil
    Place a few whole basil leaves on top, then drizzle the pizza lightly with extra virgin olive oil to enhance its flavor during baking.

Baking the Pizza: Reaching Perfect Crispness

Traditional Neapolitan pizzas are baked in wood-fired ovens at temperatures exceeding 900°F (480°C), but you can achieve impressive results in a home oven with the right techniques.

Baking Tips for Home Chefs

  1. Preheat Your Oven to Maximum Temperature
    Set your oven to its highest setting—typically 500°F (260°C). If you have a pizza stone or steel, place it on the top rack and allow it to preheat for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Transfer the Pizza to the Stone
    Carefully slide the assembled pizza onto the preheated stone or steel using a pizza peel. Work quickly to prevent heat loss.

  3. Bake Until Perfect
    Bake the pizza for 7–10 minutes, or until the crust is puffed and golden with slight charring along the edges. The mozzarella should melt and bubble beautifully atop the vibrant tomato sauce.

Serving and Enjoying Your Neapolitan Pizza

Once it’s out of the oven, let the pizza cool for a minute, then slice it into wedges and serve hot. The contrast between the airy crust, tangy tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil results in an experience that’s nothing short of extraordinary. Pair with a crisp wine or sparkling water to complete the meal.

Tips for Success: Fine-Tuning Your Pizza Game

  1. Invest in Quality Ingredients
    From the flour to the tomatoes, choosing high-quality, authentic ingredients is essential for achieving the true flavor of Neapolitan pizza.

  2. Patience is Key
    Rushing the dough’s fermentation process can compromise its texture and flavor. Allow ample time for the yeast to work its magic.

  3. Experiment with Ovens
    If you’re a true pizza enthusiast, consider investing in a portable pizza oven that can mimic the high temperatures of traditional wood-fired ovens.

Mastering how to make Neapolitan pizza at home is a rewarding experience, offering the satisfaction of creating an iconic dish while honing your culinary skills. Whether it’s your first attempt or a journey to perfect the craft, let this guide inspire you to embrace the art of pizza-making and bring a taste of Naples to your kitchen. Happy baking!
