Delicious Spanish Fish Recipes for Seafood Lovers


Spain, with its stunning coastlines and vibrant culinary traditions, is a paradise for seafood lovers. The country’s rich maritime history and diverse regional cuisines have led to an impressive array of fish dishes that tantalize the taste buds. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a novice in the kitchen, these delicious Spanish fish recipes will transport you straight to the sun-drenched shores of the Mediterranean. Let’s dive into these mouthwatering dishes that showcase the best of Spanish seafood.

1. Bacalao a la Vizcaína (Basque-Style Salted Cod)

Ingredients:

  • 500g salted cod (bacalao)
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1 can (400g) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (preferably smoked)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cod: Rinse the salted cod under cold water to remove excess salt. Soak it in water for 24 hours, changing the water a few times to desalinate it properly. After soaking, drain and pat dry.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Add Bell Peppers and Tomatoes: Blend the roasted bell peppers and incorporate them into the skillet. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, add the paprika, bay leaf, and season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Cook the Cod: Add the cod to the skillet and cover it with the sauce. Cook on low heat for 15–20 minutes, or until the cod is tender and flakes easily with a fork.

  5. Serve: Remove the bay leaf and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot, preferably with crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

2. Pescado a la Sal (Salt-Crusted Fish)

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole fish (such as sea bream or snapper), cleaned and scaled
  • 2 kg coarse sea salt
  • 1 egg white
  • Fresh herbs (like thyme or rosemary)
  • Lemon slices
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fish: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Stuff the cavity of the fish with fresh herbs and lemon slices.

  2. Mix the Salt: In a bowl, combine the coarse sea salt with the egg white, mixing well until it resembles wet sand.

  3. Coat the Fish: On a baking tray lined with parchment paper, create a bed of salt. Place the fish on top and cover it completely with the remaining salt mixture, pressing it down to form a crust.

  4. Bake: Cook the fish in the preheated oven for about 25–30 minutes, depending on its size. The salt crust will harden and form a protective shell.

  5. Serve: Once done, remove the tray from the oven and carefully crack open the salt crust. Serve the fish with a drizzle of olive oil and a side of roasted vegetables or a fresh salad.

3. Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

Ingredients:

  • 500g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Garlic: In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic slices and cook until golden but not burnt.

  2. Add Shrimp: Increase the heat to high and add the shrimp to the skillet. Sprinkle with red pepper flakes if desired. Cook for about 2–3 minutes on each side until the shrimp turn pink and opaque.

  3. Finish: Remove from heat and sprinkle with chopped parsley. Serve immediately with lemon wedges for squeezing over the shrimp.

4. Calamares a la Romana (Battered Fried Squid)

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Squid: Pat the squid rings dry with paper towels to ensure they fry well.

  2. Coat the Squid: In a bowl, mix the flour with paprika, salt, and pepper. Dredge the squid rings in the flour mixture, shaking off any excess flour.

  3. Fry: In a deep skillet or frying pan, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Fry the squid in batches for about 2–3 minutes until golden and crispy. Avoid overcrowding the pan.

  4. Drain: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the fried squid to a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess oil.

  5. Serve: Serve hot with lemon wedges and a side of aioli or garlic mayonnaise for dipping.

5. Sopa de Pescado (Fish Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 500g white fish fillets (such as cod or haddock)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 liter fish stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Fresh herbs (like parsley or cilantro)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and bell pepper. Sauté until softened.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Stock: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook for a few minutes. Add the fish stock and bay leaf, bringing the mixture to a simmer.

  3. Cook the Fish: Add the fish fillets to the pot and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily.

  4. Season and Garnish: Remove the bay leaf, season with salt and pepper, and sprinkle with fresh herbs before serving.

  5. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and serve with crusty bread for a hearty meal.

6. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-Style Octopus)

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg octopus, cleaned
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • Paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • Sea salt
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Octopus: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the octopus and reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for about 40–50 minutes, or until tender. Remove and let cool.

  2. Boil the Potatoes: In the same pot, add the sliced potatoes and cook until fork-tender. Drain and set aside.

  3. Slice the Octopus: Once the octopus has cooled, slice it into bite-sized pieces.

  4. Assemble: On a serving platter, arrange the potato slices and top with the octopus. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with paprika and sea salt, and garnish with chopped parsley.

  5. Serve: Enjoy this dish warm or at room temperature, perfect for sharing with friends.

7. Fish Tacos with Mango Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 500g white fish fillets (like tilapia or mahi-mahi)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Season the Fish: In a bowl, mix chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture onto the fish fillets.

  2. Cook the Fish: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the fish for about 3–4 minutes per side until cooked through. Remove and flake the fish with a fork.

  3. Prepare the Salsa: In a separate bowl, combine the diced mango, avocado, red onion, lime juice, and cilantro. Season with salt to taste.

  4. Assemble Tacos: Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet. Fill each tortilla with flaked fish and top with mango salsa.

  5. Serve: Serve immediately with extra lime wedges on the side.

Conclusion

These delicious Spanish fish recipes are perfect for seafood lovers looking to explore the vibrant flavors and culinary traditions of Spain. From the hearty Bacalao a la Vizcaína to the refreshing Fish Tacos with Mango Salsa, each dish offers a unique taste of the Mediterranean. Whether you are hosting a dinner party or simply enjoying a meal at home, these recipes are sure to impress and satisfy. Dive into the world of Spanish cuisine and savor the delights of the sea!
