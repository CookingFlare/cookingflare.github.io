Grilling Pineapple: Sweet and Smoky Recipes for a Tropical BBQ


Are you looking to add a burst of tropical flavor to your next BBQ? Look no further than grilling pineapple! This sweet and smoky fruit brings a unique twist to your favorite summer dishes, and it’s incredibly easy to prepare. Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or simply craving a taste of the tropics, these grilled pineapple recipes are sure to impress your family and friends.

Grilled Pineapple Skewers with Teriyaki Glaze

Preparation time: 10 minutes
Cooking time: 8 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled, cored, and cut into chunks
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the teriyaki sauce, honey, soy sauce, and grated ginger.

  3. Thread the pineapple chunks onto the soaked wooden skewers.

  4. Brush the teriyaki glaze onto the pineapple skewers, making sure to coat them evenly.

  5. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 4 minutes on each side, or until the pineapple is lightly charred and caramelized.

  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and serve hot.

These grilled pineapple skewers with teriyaki glaze are the perfect combination of sweet and savory. The teriyaki glaze adds a rich umami flavor to the naturally sweet pineapple, creating a mouthwatering treat that will leave your guests wanting more.

Grilled Pineapple Salsa

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 5 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled, cored, and cut into small dice
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.

  2. Place the pineapple chunks directly on the grill grates and cook for about 3 minutes on each side, or until lightly charred. Remove from the grill and let cool.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the grilled pineapple, red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeno pepper, lime juice, and chopped cilantro. Mix well.

  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Refrigerate the salsa for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

This grilled pineapple salsa is a refreshing and tangy accompaniment to grilled meats, tacos, or simply enjoyed with tortilla chips. The smoky flavor from grilling the pineapple adds depth to the salsa, while the combination of sweet and spicy ingredients creates a perfect balance of flavors.

Grilled Pineapple Upside-Down Cake

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 40 minutes
Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled, cored, and cut into rings
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.

  2. In a cast-iron skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Sprinkle the brown sugar evenly over the melted butter.

  3. Arrange the pineapple rings on top of the brown sugar mixture in a single layer.

  4. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  5. In a separate bowl, cream together the granulated sugar and softened butter until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the creamed mixture, alternating with the milk. Mix until just combined.

  7. Carefully pour the batter over the pineapple rings in the skillet, spreading it evenly.

  8. Place the skillet on the preheated grill and close the lid. Cook for about 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.

  9. Remove the skillet from the grill and let it cool for 10 minutes. Invert the cake onto a serving platter.

  10. Serve the grilled pineapple upside-down cake warm, with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream if desired.

This grilled pineapple upside-down cake is a delightful twist on a classic dessert. The caramelized pineapple rings add a burst of flavor and moisture to the cake, making it incredibly moist and irresistible. The smoky notes from grilling the pineapple take this dessert to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Conclusion

Grilling pineapple is a fantastic way to elevate your BBQ game and add a touch of tropical flair to your dishes. Whether you prefer the sweet and smoky flavor in skewers, salsa, or cake, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and make your next BBQ a memorable one. So fire up the grill, grab a fresh pineapple, and get ready to enjoy the perfect combination of sweet and smoky flavors in every bite.
