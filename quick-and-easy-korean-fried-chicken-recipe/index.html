Quick and Easy Korean Fried Chicken Recipe


If you’ve ever indulged in Korean fried chicken, you know it’s more than just food—it’s a celebration of crispiness, spice, and flavor. There’s something truly magical about the combination of succulent, juicy chicken wrapped in an impossibly crunchy shell, coated with mouthwatering sauces that are sweet, spicy, and savory all at once. The good news? You don’t have to travel to your nearest Korean restaurant to savor this dish. With this quick and easy Korean fried chicken recipe, you can make this crowd-pleaser from the comfort of your own kitchen, even on the busiest weekdays.

This recipe simplifies the traditional method, preserving all the iconic flavors, textures, and aromas while dramatically cutting down on prep time. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or new to the culinary world, this step-by-step guide ensures anyone can master the art of Korean fried chicken with minimal hassle.

Why Korean Fried Chicken?

Korean fried chicken stands out because it’s more than just deep-fried goodness. Cooked twice for unmatched crispiness, even when drenched in sauce, the chicken achieves a sublime balance of texture and flavor. The signature sauce, typically built on a base of gochujang (Korean chili paste), harmonizes sweetness, heat, and tang in a way that’s utterly addictive.

But perhaps the most beautiful thing about this dish is its versatility. It pairs perfectly with beer, soda, steamed rice, or even crunchy salads, making it ideal for casual dinners, celebratory gatherings, or as a snack to binge-watch your favorite show.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Chicken:

  • 2 lbs chicken wings, drumettes, or boneless thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch (for crispiness)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup cold water or soda water (for better batter texture)
  • Oil for frying

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Optional: 1 tsp grated ginger for added flavor

For Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds
  • Thinly sliced green onions

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by washing the chicken thoroughly and patting it dry with paper towels. Moist chicken makes it harder for the coating to adhere, so ensure it’s completely dry before proceeding. This step also helps the meat stay juicy during frying—a key element of Korean fried chicken.

In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Stir in cold water or soda water gradually, whisking until smooth. The batter should have a slightly thick, pancake-like consistency. If it’s too runny, add more flour; if it’s too thick, add a small amount of water.

Step 2: Coat and Pre-Fry

Heat a skillet or deep fryer with vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Tip: Use a thermometer to ensure the oil stays at a consistent temperature for even frying.

Dip each chicken piece into the batter, ensuring an even coating. Shake off the excess batter gently and carefully place the chicken in the hot oil. Fry the chicken in batches for 4-5 minutes or until it just starts turning pale golden. Remove and place on a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

This first fry sets up the crispiness, but don’t worry—this isn’t the end.

Step 3: The Second Fry for Maximum Crunch

Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Fry the partially cooked chicken pieces a second time for 2-3 minutes, or until they turn deep golden and achieve that unmistakable crunchy texture. This double-frying method ensures the chicken stays crispy even after you coat it in sauce.

Once fried, let the chicken rest on a wire rack or paper towels to cool while you make the sauce.

Step 4: Make the Sweet and Spicy Sauce

In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine gochujang, ketchup, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, sesame oil, and optional ginger. Stir gently to combine as the mixture heats up.

Allow the sauce to simmer for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. It should thicken slightly, creating a perfect coating consistency. Taste and adjust, adding more honey for sweetness or more gochujang for an extra kick.

Step 5: Toss the Chicken

Once the sauce is ready, toss the fried chicken pieces in the warm sauce, ensuring each piece is generously coated. You can also brush the sauce onto the chicken if you prefer less coverage.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the saucy, golden-red chicken to a serving plate, and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions for a pop of freshness.

Pair your Korean fried chicken with steamed rice, pickled radish (traditionally called “chikin mu”), or enjoy it on its own as a snack.

Tips for Success

  • The Right Oil Temperature: Maintaining oil temperature is critical. If the heat is too low, the chicken will absorb excess oil and turn soggy. Too high, and you risk burning the outer layer before the chicken cooks through.
  • Prep Ingredients First: This recipe moves fast, so have all your ingredients ready before you start frying or making the sauce.
  • Gochujang Variety: Depending on your spice tolerance, you can opt for mild or hot gochujang. This ingredient is the backbone of Korean fried chicken’s iconic flavor, so don’t skip it!
  • Serve Fresh: Korean fried chicken is best enjoyed right after cooking, while the texture and flavors are at their peak.

Variations and Customizations

  • Sauce Swap: Not in the mood for spice? Try a soy garlic glaze or honey butter sauce as an alternative—the process remains the same!
  • Air Fryer Option: For a lighter version, skip the batter and coat the chicken with starch alone. Cook in an air fryer at 375°F for 20 minutes, shaking halfway through. Apply the sauce afterward.
  • Add Crunch: Toss crushed nuts like peanuts or almonds into the garnish mix for added texture and flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This quick and easy Korean fried chicken recipe eliminates the complexity while retaining the bold, irresistible flavors you’d find at your favorite Korean restaurant. The double-fried technique guarantees perfect crispiness, and the vibrant sauce immediately elevates the dish to superstar status.

With simple ingredients, straightforward cooking methods, and unbeatable results, this dish is destined to become a go-to recipe in any home kitchen. Prepare it for dinner parties, family meals, or even as a late-night indulgence—you won’t regret it.

Let Korean fried chicken work its magic on your taste buds and add a new favorite to your cooking repertoire. Have fun, and happy frying!
