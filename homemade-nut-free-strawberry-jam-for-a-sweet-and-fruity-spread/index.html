Homemade Nut-Free Strawberry Jam for a Sweet and Fruity Spread


Looking for a delicious and nut-free homemade strawberry jam recipe? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of making your very own sweet and fruity strawberry jam, perfect for spreading on toast, scones, or even using as a filling for cakes and pastries. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you’ll have a jar of delectable jam that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh strawberries, hulled and quartered
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the strawberries, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Stir well to ensure all the strawberries are coated in the sugar.

  2. Place the pot over medium heat and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the strawberries have softened and the mixture has thickened slightly. Stir occasionally to prevent the jam from sticking to the bottom of the pot.

  3. While the jam is simmering, prepare your jars for canning. Wash them thoroughly with hot, soapy water and sterilize them by boiling them in a pot of water for a few minutes. Place the lids in a separate pot of boiling water to sterilize them as well.

  4. Once the jam has reached your desired consistency, remove the pot from the heat. Using a potato masher or a fork, mash the strawberries to your preferred texture. For a smoother jam, you can use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the mixture.

  5. Carefully ladle the hot jam into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims clean with a damp cloth to ensure a proper seal, then place the lids on top and screw on the bands until they are fingertip tight.

  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper preservation. Carefully remove the jars from the water bath and place them on a towel to cool. As they cool, you may hear a satisfying “pop” sound, indicating that the jars have sealed properly.

  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place. The jam will continue to thicken as it cools.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a smoother jam without any strawberry chunks, you can strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve before transferring it to the jars.

  • Feel free to customize your jam by adding other fruits or flavors. Try mixing in some chopped rhubarb for a tangy twist, or add a sprinkle of cinnamon for a warm and cozy flavor.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved depending on your needs. Just adjust the quantities of the ingredients accordingly.

  • Don’t limit yourself to just spreading this jam on toast! It can also be used as a filling for cakes, pastries, and even as a topping for ice cream.

There you have it – a simple and delicious recipe for homemade nut-free strawberry jam. Give it a try and enjoy the fresh, fruity flavors all year round. Whether you’re enjoying it on a lazy Sunday morning or giving it as a thoughtful homemade gift, this jam is sure to delight. Happy jam-making!
