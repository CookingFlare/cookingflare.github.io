Homemade Sangria Recipe: A Spanish Classic


Sangria, a beloved Spanish drink, is not only refreshing but also packs a flavorful punch. With its vibrant red color and fruity notes, it’s the perfect beverage to enjoy on a hot summer day or at a festive gathering. Making homemade sangria allows you to customize the flavors to your liking and impress your guests with your mixology skills. In this article, we’ll share a classic homemade sangria recipe that will transport you to the sun-kissed streets of Spain.

Ingredients

  • 1 bottle of red wine (preferably a medium-bodied red like Tempranillo or Rioja)
  • 1 cup of orange juice
  • 1/2 cup of brandy
  • 1/4 cup of triple sec
  • 1/4 cup of simple syrup (equal parts sugar and water, dissolved together)
  • 1 orange, sliced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 1 lime, sliced
  • 1 apple, cored and diced
  • 1 cup of strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • Ice cubes, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the red wine, orange juice, brandy, triple sec, and simple syrup. Stir well to combine and let the mixture sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  2. After the mixture has rested, add the sliced orange, lemon, lime, diced apple, and sliced strawberries to the pitcher. Stir gently to incorporate the fruit into the sangria.

  3. Cover the pitcher and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight if possible. This will allow the fruit to infuse its flavors into the sangria, making it even more delicious.

  4. When you’re ready to serve, fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the chilled sangria over the top. Make sure to include some fruit slices in each glass for an extra burst of flavor and a beautiful presentation.

  5. Sip and savor the refreshing taste of homemade sangria. The combination of the red wine, citrus juices, and fruit creates a harmonious blend that will tantalize your taste buds.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different fruits: While the recipe above includes oranges, lemons, limes, apples, and strawberries, feel free to get creative and add your favorite fruits. Raspberries, peaches, and grapes are excellent choices that can add depth and complexity to your sangria.

  • Adjust the sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter sangria, you can increase the amount of simple syrup or add a splash of fruit juice, such as pineapple or cranberry. On the other hand, if you like your sangria less sweet, reduce the amount of simple syrup or omit it altogether.

  • Chill, but don’t dilute: It’s essential to serve sangria chilled, but try to avoid adding too much ice to the pitcher. Ice cubes can dilute the flavors over time. Instead, opt for frozen fruit slices to keep the drink cold without compromising the taste.

  • Sparkling sangria: For a fizzy twist, you can replace a portion of the red wine with sparkling wine or champagne. This variation adds a lively effervescence to the drink, making it even more celebratory.

  • Non-alcoholic version: If you prefer a non-alcoholic version of sangria, you can substitute the red wine with grape juice or a blend of fruit juices. Follow the same recipe, omitting the brandy and triple sec.

Now that you have the recipe for a classic homemade sangria, it’s time to gather your ingredients, put on some Spanish music, and transport yourself to the vibrant streets of Barcelona or Madrid. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply want to unwind after a long day, a glass of homemade sangria is sure to make any occasion feel special. ¡Salud!
