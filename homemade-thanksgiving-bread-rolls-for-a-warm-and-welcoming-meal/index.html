Homemade Thanksgiving Bread Rolls for a Warm and Welcoming Meal


As Thanksgiving approaches, it’s time to start planning the perfect menu for a warm and welcoming gathering. One of the essential elements of any Thanksgiving feast is the bread, and what better way to elevate your meal than with homemade Thanksgiving bread rolls? These soft, fluffy, and aromatic rolls will fill your home with the irresistible scent of freshly baked bread, setting the stage for a memorable and heartwarming celebration. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through a step-by-step recipe for creating these delightful Thanksgiving bread rolls that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need to bring these delectable bread rolls to life:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

For the glaze:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of flour, sugar, salt, and instant yeast. Mix well.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until a soft dough forms.
  4. Slowly add the remaining 2 cups of flour, kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Step 2: Let the Dough Rise

  1. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

Step 3: Shape the Rolls

  1. After the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air.
  2. Divide the dough into 12 equal portions and shape each portion into a smooth ball.
  3. Place the rolls on a parchment-lined baking sheet, cover them, and let them rise for an additional 30 minutes.

Step 4: Bake the Rolls

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Brush the risen rolls with the glaze made from melted butter and honey.
  3. Bake the rolls for 15-20 minutes or until they turn golden brown and sound hollow when tapped.

Serving Suggestions

These homemade Thanksgiving bread rolls are best served warm, straight from the oven. They pair beautifully with a generous slather of butter or a drizzle of honey for an extra touch of sweetness. You can also use them to make mini turkey sandwiches with leftover Thanksgiving turkey for a delightful post-holiday treat.

Conclusion

There’s something truly special about the aroma of freshly baked bread filling the air, especially during the Thanksgiving season. These homemade Thanksgiving bread rolls will not only complement your holiday feast but also infuse your home with warmth and comfort. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to create a batch of these irresistible rolls that will undoubtedly become a cherished tradition at your Thanksgiving table. Happy baking, and may your Thanksgiving be filled with love, laughter, and delicious food!
