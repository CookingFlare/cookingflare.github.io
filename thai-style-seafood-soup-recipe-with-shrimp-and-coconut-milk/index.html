Thai-Style Seafood Soup Recipe with Shrimp and Coconut Milk


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this delectable Thai-style seafood soup recipe. Bursting with the flavors of the sea, fragrant herbs, and creamy coconut milk, this soup is a harmonious blend of savory, spicy, and slightly sweet elements. The star of the show is succulent shrimp, but feel free to add your favorite seafood to create a personalized culinary masterpiece. Let’s dive into the tantalizing world of Thai flavors and create a soup that will transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok.

Ingredients:

For the broth:

  • 4 cups of fish or seafood stock
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 2 stalks of lemongrass, bruised and chopped
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2-inch piece of galangal, sliced
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies, lightly crushed
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of tamarind paste
  • Salt, to taste

For the soup:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz of squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 8 oz of mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 1 cup of straw mushrooms, halved
  • 1 medium tomato, cut into wedges
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro and Thai basil for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    • In a large pot, combine the fish or seafood stock, coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and Thai bird’s eye chilies. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
    • Stir in the fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind paste. Allow the flavors to meld together for about 10-15 minutes. Season the broth with salt to taste.

  2. Cook the Seafood:

    • In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
    • Add the shrimp, squid, mussels, and mushrooms to the pan, and cook until the shrimp turn pink and the mussels open. Discard any mussels that do not open.
    • Once the seafood is cooked, transfer it to the pot of broth.

  3. Finish the Soup:

    • Gently simmer the seafood in the broth for an additional 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
    • Add the tomato wedges to the soup and cook for another 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.

  4. Serve and Garnish:

    • Ladle the fragrant Thai-style seafood soup into bowls, making sure to distribute an equal amount of seafood and broth.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and Thai basil for an aromatic touch.
    • Serve the soup with lime wedges on the side for a burst of citrusy flavor.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra kick of heat, you can add more Thai bird’s eye chilies or a drizzle of sriracha to the soup.
  • If you’re unable to find fresh lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, or galangal, many grocery stores offer these ingredients in their dried or paste forms.
  • Adjust the level of sweetness and tartness by adding more or less palm sugar and tamarind paste to suit your taste preferences.

This Thai-style seafood soup is a celebration of vibrant flavors and textures, making it the perfect dish to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned Thai cuisine enthusiast or a newcomer to the world of Southeast Asian flavors, this recipe is sure to transport your taste buds to the bustling markets of Thailand. Embrace the aromatic herbs, creamy coconut milk, and succulent seafood in this delightful soup that promises to be a culinary adventure in every spoonful. Enjoy!
