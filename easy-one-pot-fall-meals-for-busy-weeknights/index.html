Easy One-Pot Fall Meals for Busy Weeknights


As the fall season settles in, there’s nothing better than a hearty, comforting meal that can warm you up after a long day. Busy weeknights can make it challenging to prepare a meal from scratch, but with one-pot dishes, you can have a wholesome, delicious dinner ready in no time. The key is in simplicity and using ingredients that embody the essence of fall – think root vegetables, hearty grains, and warming spices. One-pot meals save you time on both preparation and cleanup, making them the perfect solution for hectic evenings. Below are some easy and comforting one-pot fall meals that will make you look forward to dinner all week long.

1. Autumn Chicken and Sweet Potato Stew

This chicken and sweet potato stew is a complete meal in one pot, offering the perfect blend of savory and slightly sweet flavors. With tender chicken, earthy sweet potatoes, and an aromatic broth, it’s the ultimate fall comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs (or breasts)
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken thighs and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side until browned. Remove from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, garlic, and carrots. Sauté for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  3. Add the diced sweet potatoes, thyme, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, then return the chicken thighs to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for 30-35 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the pot, stirring to combine.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy the hearty, flavorful stew that brings the cozy essence of fall to your table.

2. Hearty Fall Vegetable Risotto

Creamy risotto is a comforting meal on its own, but adding roasted fall vegetables makes it even more delicious. This one-pot vegetable risotto is rich, creamy, and packed with seasonal vegetables like butternut squash and Brussels sprouts. It’s a vegetarian dish, but you can add chicken or sausage if desired.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Olive oil for roasting
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the diced butternut squash and halved Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and lightly caramelized.
  2. While the vegetables roast, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the Arborio rice to the pot and stir it around for 1-2 minutes to toast it lightly.
  4. Pour in the white wine (if using) and stir until the wine has been absorbed by the rice.
  5. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and letting the liquid absorb before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is tender and creamy, about 20 minutes.
  6. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the roasted vegetables, Parmesan cheese, and butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme for an extra burst of flavor.

3. Pumpkin and Black Bean Chili

Pumpkin isn’t just for pie – it adds a lovely creamy texture and subtle sweetness to chili. This pumpkin and black bean chili is a comforting, healthy meal that’s perfect for chilly fall nights. It’s also vegan and gluten-free, making it suitable for a variety of dietary needs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, red bell pepper, and garlic, and sauté for 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and cinnamon. Cook for 1 minute to toast the spices.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, black beans, pumpkin puree, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir well to combine and bring to a simmer.
  4. Let the chili cook for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the chili to thicken slightly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the chili hot, garnished with fresh cilantro for an extra touch of freshness.

4. Fall Harvest Chicken and Rice

This one-pot chicken and rice dish is perfect for a busy weeknight. The combination of tender chicken, aromatic rice, and fall vegetables makes for a complete, satisfying meal that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup diced apples (preferably tart apples like Granny Smith)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then brown them in the pot for about 5-7 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and carrots. Sauté for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the rice to the pot, stirring it for a minute to toast it lightly.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth and add the diced apples and fresh sage. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Return the chicken thighs to the pot, skin-side up. Cover the pot and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.
  6. Serve hot, with the rice and vegetables beneath the chicken for a cozy, hearty meal.

5. Mushroom and Barley Soup

Mushroom and barley soup is the perfect fall dish. It’s comforting, earthy, and packed with nutritious ingredients. This one-pot soup is filling and can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a crusty piece of bread for a more substantial meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced (button mushrooms or a mix of wild mushrooms)
  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become tender, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté for another 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the barley, thyme, and bay leaf.
  4. Pour in the broth, bringing the soup to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the barley is tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve hot. This hearty soup will leave you feeling warm and satisfied.

Conclusion

One-pot fall meals are the perfect solution for busy weeknights when you want a delicious, hearty meal without spending hours in the kitchen. The beauty of these dishes lies in their simplicity, allowing you to enjoy the flavors of fall with minimal effort. Whether you’re in the mood for a comforting stew, a creamy risotto, or a hearty chili, these recipes will help you get dinner on the table in no time. The best part? They all come together in a single pot, making cleanup a breeze. So next time you’re looking for a fall-inspired meal, reach for these easy one-pot recipes and enjoy the cozy season with ease.
