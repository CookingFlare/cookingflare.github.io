Homemade Cherry Chocolate Chunk Ice Cream: A Classic Combination


There’s something undeniably delightful about the combination of cherries and chocolate. The sweet, juicy burst of cherries paired with the rich, velvety goodness of chocolate is a match made in dessert heaven. And what better way to enjoy this classic combination than in a homemade ice cream?

Making your own ice cream at home may sound daunting, but with a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a batch of Homemade Cherry Chocolate Chunk Ice Cream that will rival any store-bought treat. So, grab your ice cream maker and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup dark chocolate, chopped into chunks

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, heavy cream, and half of the sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it begins to steam. Remove from heat.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and the remaining sugar until pale and creamy.

  3. Slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling.

  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 10-15 minutes. Do not let it boil.

  5. Remove the mixture from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let it cool to room temperature.

  6. Once cooled, cover the mixture with plastic wrap, making sure the wrap is touching the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  7. While the custard chills, prepare the cherries. If using fresh cherries, rinse and pit them. If using frozen cherries, thaw them and halve them.

  8. Once the custard is chilled, pour it into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  9. When the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, add the cherries and chocolate chunks. Continue churning until the cherries and chocolate are evenly distributed throughout the ice cream.

  10. Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.

  11. When you’re ready to serve, scoop the Homemade Cherry Chocolate Chunk Ice Cream into bowls or cones, and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • To enhance the cherry flavor, you can macerate the cherries in sugar for an hour before adding them to the ice cream. This will soften the cherries and release their juices, creating a more intense flavor.

  • If you prefer a smoother texture, you can strain the custard before chilling it to remove any lumps or bits of cooked egg.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of chocolate. Milk chocolate or white chocolate can be substituted for the dark chocolate, depending on your preference.

  • For an extra indulgent treat, serve the ice cream with a drizzle of chocolate sauce or a sprinkle of chopped nuts.

  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make this recipe by pouring the custard into a shallow dish and freezing it. Every hour, remove the dish from the freezer and stir vigorously to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process until the ice cream reaches your desired consistency.

Now that you have the recipe for Homemade Cherry Chocolate Chunk Ice Cream, it’s time to put your culinary skills to the test and treat yourself to a scoop (or two) of this classic combination. Enjoy the creamy goodness and savor the sweet harmony of cherries and chocolate in every bite!
