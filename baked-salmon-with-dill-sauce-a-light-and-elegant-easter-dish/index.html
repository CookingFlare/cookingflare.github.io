Baked Salmon with Dill Sauce: A Light and Elegant Easter Dish


Are you looking for a delicious and elegant dish to impress your loved ones this Easter? Look no further! Baked Salmon with Dill Sauce is the perfect choice. This light and flavorful recipe will leave your taste buds begging for more. With its delicate balance of flavors and beautiful presentation, it’s guaranteed to be a showstopper at your Easter dinner table.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of fresh salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 cup of fresh dill, chopped

Dill Sauce

  • 1/2 cup of plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, and chopped dill. This flavorful marinade will infuse the salmon with a burst of freshness.

  3. Place the salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet, skin side down. Pour the marinade over the salmon, ensuring that each fillet is evenly coated. Set aside for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  4. While the salmon is marinating, prepare the dill sauce. In a separate bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, and chopped dill. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to combine all the ingredients, creating a creamy and tangy sauce.

  5. Once the salmon has marinated, transfer the baking sheet to the preheated oven. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. Cooking time may vary depending on the thickness of the fillets, so keep an eye on them to prevent overcooking.

  6. Remove the salmon from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to further develop and the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender result.

  7. Serve the baked salmon hot, garnished with fresh dill sprigs for an extra touch of elegance. Drizzle the dill sauce over the salmon or serve it on the side for dipping. The creamy dill sauce perfectly complements the delicate flavor of the salmon, adding a tangy and herbaceous element to every bite.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, you can sprinkle some lemon zest over the salmon before baking. The bright citrus notes will enhance the overall taste of the dish.

  • If you prefer a slightly crispy texture, you can broil the salmon for the last 1-2 minutes of cooking. This will give the top of the fillets a beautiful golden crust.

  • For a complete Easter meal, serve the baked salmon with a side of roasted asparagus and a refreshing citrus salad. The combination of flavors is sure to delight your guests.

  • If you have any leftovers, don’t worry! Baked salmon is just as delicious when served cold. Use it to make a scrumptious salmon salad or flake it into a creamy pasta dish.

Baked Salmon with Dill Sauce is not only a perfect Easter dish but also a versatile recipe that can be enjoyed all year round. Its simplicity and elegance make it a go-to option for any occasion. So why wait? Gather your ingredients and get ready to impress with this delectable and light salmon recipe. Happy cooking!
