How to Make a Classic Almond Tart


The classic almond tart is a beloved dessert that is both elegant and irresistibly delicious. Perfect for special occasions or even just a casual treat, the almond tart presents a perfect harmony of buttery crust, nutty filling, and subtle sweetness. Its combination of fragrant almonds and delicate texture makes it a standout dish that will leave your guests delighted. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just exploring new recipes, preparing an almond tart can be an enjoyable experience that rewards you with unforgettable flavors and aromas.

Let’s break down the process step by step, from crafting the perfect pastry crust to preparing the signature almond filling guaranteed to impress.

Ingredients Needed

Before diving into the steps, gather these ingredients to ensure seamless preparation. The tart requires a few simple components, each playing a key role in delivering its distinct taste and texture. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pastry Crust (Shortcrust Pastry):

  • 1 ¼ cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the Almond Filling:

  • ¾ cup (75g) almond flour (ground almonds)
  • ⅓ cup (75g) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Optional Garnish:

  • Sliced almonds for decoration
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Fresh fruit (like raspberries or strawberries) to serve

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Pastry Crust

The foundation of any tart is a well-made crust, and the shortcrust pastry gives this dessert its buttery base. Follow these steps to create the perfect crust:

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, and salt. This ensures even distribution of the dry ingredients.

  2. Incorporate Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the bowl. Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Be careful not to overwork the dough, as you want it to stay light and flaky.

  3. Add the Egg Yolks and Water: Create a small well in the center of the mixture and add the egg yolk. Gradually add 2-3 tablespoons of ice water while gently mixing until the dough comes together. If the dough feels too dry, add more water a teaspoon at a time.

  4. Form and Chill: Gather the dough into a ball, press it gently into a disk shape, and wrap it tightly in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the butter to firm up and the gluten to relax.

  5. Roll Out the Dough: Once chilled, roll the dough out on a lightly floured surface to fit an 8-10 inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough to the pan, pressing it into the edges and trimming any excess. Prick the base with a fork to prevent bubbles, then refrigerate for another 20 minutes.

  6. Blind Bake: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line the pastry with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment and bake for an additional 5 minutes until lightly golden. Set aside to cool.

2. Make the Almond Filling

The almond filling is what gives this tart its signature flavor and texture. Creating it is simpler than you think!

  1. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a medium bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy. This step is crucial for ensuring a smooth and creamy filling.

  2. Add Eggs and Extracts: One at a time, add the eggs, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla and almond extracts to infuse the mixture with a rich, aromatic essence.

  3. Incorporate Dry Ingredients: Gradually mix in the almond flour and all-purpose flour until fully combined. The result should be a thick yet spreadable filling.

3. Assemble the Tart

Now that the crust and filling are ready, it’s time to bring everything together.

  1. Fill the Crust: Spoon the almond filling into the cooled pastry crust, spreading it evenly with a spatula.

  2. Decorate: For an extra touch of elegance, sprinkle sliced almonds evenly over the top of the filling. They’ll toast beautifully during baking, adding a delightful crunch.

4. Bake to Perfection

The final baking step ensures the almond tart is cooked evenly, with a golden crust and tender filling.

  1. Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the assembled tart in the oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is lightly golden.

  2. Cool Completely: Remove the tart from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for at least 10 minutes. Once cooled, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

5. The Finishing Touches

Once the tart has cooled, it’s time to elevate its presentation.

  1. Dust with Powdered Sugar: Generously dust powdered sugar over the surface of the tart for an elegant appearance.

  2. Serve with Fresh Fruit: If desired, plate the tart alongside fresh fruit like raspberries or strawberries for a pop of color and complementary flavor.

  3. Slice and Enjoy: Cut the tart into wedges and serve as the perfect dessert after any meal. Its buttery crust and sweet, nutty filling make it a crowd-pleaser.

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Almond Tart

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of the tart shines through the almonds and butter, so it’s worth using high-quality ingredients.
  • Chill the Dough: Don’t skip the chilling steps for the pastry. Cold dough is easier to work with and ensures the crust stays crisp and flaky.
  • Don’t Overbake: Keep an eye on the tart during the final baking step. Overbaking can cause the filling to dry out or crack.
  • Experiment with Flavors: If you want to customize the tart, consider adding a layer of jam (like raspberry or apricot) beneath the almond filling for a fruity twist.

Why This Tart Stands Out

The classic almond tart is a versatile treat that pairs well with tea, coffee, or even a glass of dessert wine. Its rich flavors make it suitable for festive gatherings, while its simplicity allows it to shine as a comforting everyday indulgence. The combination of the nutty almond filling and buttery crust ensures that each bite is as satisfying as the last.

Whether you serve it warm or chilled, an almond tart will always be the star of the table. For bakers looking to expand their repertoire or impress with a unique dessert, this recipe is a must-try.

Indulge in the process of crafting this timeless dessert and share the joy of its delicate flavors with your loved ones. Happy baking!
