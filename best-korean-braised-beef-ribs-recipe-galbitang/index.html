Best Korean Braised Beef Ribs Recipe (Galbitang)


Korean cuisine offers a plethora of deeply flavorful and comforting dishes, and one that stands out for its heartiness and richness is Galbitang—a restorative beef rib soup typically served as a centerpiece in family meals or special occasions. This traditional Korean stew, brimming with tenderized beef ribs and a delicate yet robust broth, is a perfect dish for chilly days or when you’re seeking something wholesome and soul-warming. Below, you’ll find the ultimate guide to preparing the best Galbitang at home, ensuring every bite embodies the essence of its authentic Korean roots.

What Is Galbitang?

Galbitang, literally translated to “rib soup,” is made by slowly simmering beef short ribs (or galbi) with aromatic ingredients to achieve an umami-packed broth. Unlike other Korean soups, Galbitang is less spicy and focuses on the pure, clean flavors derived from the beef itself. The end result is a crystal-clear broth enriched with the tender essence of the meat, complemented by subtle seasoning and traditional garnishes.

This dish is popular not only for its taste but also for its nutritional value. Packed with protein, minerals, and vitamins, Galbitang is believed to replenish the body after exertion or illness, making it both satisfying and invigorating. While its preparation requires some patience, the results are unfailingly worth the effort.

Now, let’s dive into crafting the perfect Galbitang recipe step-by-step.

Ingredients for Galbitang

Before starting, gather the following ingredients to ensure the best possible flavor and authenticity:

For the Broth:

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) beef short ribs (trim off excess fat for a cleaner broth)
  • 5 cups water (adjust as needed)
  • 6 garlic cloves, peeled and lightly crushed
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 2 medium-sized green onions, segmented (white parts only; save the green parts for garnish)
  • 1 Korean radish (mu), peeled and cut into thick slices (optional, but adds depth)

Seasoning:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Garnishes and Optional Add-Ins:

  • Egg yolk strips (optional, for garnish)
  • Prepared glass noodles (dangmyeon), softened in warm water
  • Thinly sliced green onions (green parts)
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish

Step-By-Step Guide to Making Galbitang

Step 1: Prepare and Clean the Beef Ribs

Cleaning the ribs is a critical step to ensure a clear broth. Take the beef short ribs and soak them in cold water for approximately 30 minutes to an hour. This process helps remove blood and impurities that could cloud your soup later. You’ll notice the water gradually turning pink—this is normal.

After soaking:

  1. Drain the water and gently rinse the ribs under cold running water.
  2. Trim any unwanted fat or connective tissue if needed.

Blanching the ribs before adding them to the soup pot is also essential for removing excess residue. Place the ribs in a large pot of boiling water and blanch them for 5 minutes, skimming off any foam or impurities that rise to the surface. Then, drain the ribs and rinse once more under cold water.

Step 2: Build the Base Broth

For Galbitang’s distinctive flavors and silky texture, patience is key. While the cleaned ribs are the star, the supporting aromatics infuse their essence into the soup over time.

  1. Place the cleaned ribs into a large stockpot.
  2. Add garlic cloves, ginger slices, onion halves, and white parts of the green onions to the pot.
  3. Pour in 5 cups of water (or enough to adequately cover everything). Bring the pot to a rolling boil, then reduce the heat to simmer.
  4. Let it simmer for at least 90 minutes—the longer the simmer, the deeper the flavor. Skim the broth occasionally to remove any foam or impurities.

Optional: If you have Korean radish (mu), add thick slices during the simmering process. The radish dissolves slightly, giving the broth a subtly sweet undertone.

Step 3: Seasoning the Broth

Once the ribs are tender:

  1. Remove the cooked aromatics (onions, ginger, and white green onion) with a slotted spoon.
  2. Season the soup with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a pinch of salt. Adjust seasoning to perfection, balancing saltiness with the natural beefy richness of the broth.

If desired, you can strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve for absolute clarity, though this isn’t strictly necessary.

Step 4: Incorporate Garnishes and Add-Ins

Many Korean households add glass noodles (dangmyeon) to Galbitang for extra substance and a beautiful presentation. If using noodles:

  1. Soak them in warm water for 10–15 minutes until softened, then add them to the simmering broth just before serving.

Prepare any garnishes like egg yolk strips or sesame seeds. These add lovely pops of color and texture.

Step 5: Serve Your Galbitang

Finally, assemble your bowl to serve:

  1. Ladle the hot broth and tender beef ribs into serving bowls.
  2. Add optional noodles and garnish generously with sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, and egg yolk strips.

Galbitang is typically served with a bowl of steamed rice and banchan (Korean side dishes) like kimchi, pickled radish, or seasoned spinach.

Tips for Perfect Galbitang

  1. Choose the Best Ribs: Opt for meaty, high-quality beef short ribs for the most satisfying results. Look for marbling but trim excessive fat to avoid greasy broth.
  2. Simmer with Care: Don’t rush the simmering process—it takes time for the ribs to release their flavors and become melt-in-your-mouth tender.
  3. Balance Seasoning: Start with light seasoning and intensify as needed. Galbitang is supposed to highlight beef’s natural flavor rather than overpower it.
  4. Make in Advance: Like many soups and stews, Galbitang tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld. Prepare it ahead for maximum deliciousness.
  5. Customize Your Soup: Add vegetables or noodles flexibly, according to your preference—no two bowls of Galbitang need to be identical!

Why Make Galbitang?

This recipe isn’t just about food; it’s about experiencing the heart and soul of Korean home cooking. Galbitang showcases the elegance of simple yet powerful flavors that blend tradition and nourishment. Whether served for a lively family dinner or savored quietly on a cold evening, this beef rib soup is bound to leave an indelible impression.

The next time you’re looking to explore Korean cuisine or impress guests with a hearty and warming dish, remember this recipe for Galbitang. Once you master it, you’ll find yourself coming back to it again and again.

Happy cooking, and may your Galbitang bring warmth and comfort to your table!
