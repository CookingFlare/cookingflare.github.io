How to Prepare Crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken


Crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken is a beloved dish in Chinese-American cuisine, known for its crispy golden-brown coating, tender chicken, and tangy, sweet, and slightly zesty lemon sauce. It’s a dish that combines the perfect balance of flavors—salty, sweet, and sour—while providing that satisfying crunch in every bite. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner, a family gathering, or even entertaining guests, this dish will never disappoint. Follow these steps to prepare this delightful meal at home!

Ingredients for Crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you will need. Many of these ingredients are staples in Chinese cooking, and you may already have them in your pantry.

For the Chicken:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch (for the crispy coating)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Lemon Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons honey (for an extra layer of sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (use low-sodium if preferred)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (or water)
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch (to thicken the sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon water (for dissolving cornstarch)
  • 1 tablespoon grated lemon zest (optional, for added lemony fragrance)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced (optional, for a slight spicy warmth)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (for an aromatic base)

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Lemon slices
  • Chopped green onions or cilantro
  • Sesame seeds

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s begin the cooking process.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken

1. Preparing the Chicken

Start by cutting the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Aim for pieces that are about 1 to 1.5 inches in size. These will ensure a crispy coating and allow for even cooking. Pat the chicken pieces dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture. This step is important for achieving a crispy coating during the frying process.

2. Making the Coating

In a large bowl, combine the cornstarch, all-purpose flour, salt, black pepper, and baking powder (if using). The combination of cornstarch and flour will give the chicken that light, crisp coating that’s so iconic in Chinese fried dishes.

Next, dip each piece of chicken into the beaten eggs, making sure each piece is coated evenly. Then, dredge the chicken in the flour mixture. Press gently to ensure the coating sticks well. It’s important to coat each piece thoroughly for maximum crispiness.

3. Frying the Chicken

Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat and add about 1 inch of vegetable oil. The oil should be hot, but not smoking, before you start frying. You can check the oil temperature by dropping a small piece of bread or batter into the oil—if it sizzles and starts to brown quickly, it’s ready.

Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated chicken pieces to the pan in batches. Do not overcrowd the pan, as this will cause the temperature of the oil to drop and result in soggy chicken. Fry the chicken for 3 to 4 minutes on each side, or until it turns golden brown and crispy. Use tongs or a slotted spoon to remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

4. Preparing the Lemon Sauce

While the chicken is frying, it’s time to make the tangy lemon sauce that will give the dish its signature flavor.

In a saucepan, combine the fresh lemon juice, sugar, honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chicken broth (or water). Stir well to combine and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat. As the sauce begins to heat up, it will start to thicken.

In a small bowl, mix the 2 teaspoons of cornstarch with 1 tablespoon of water to form a slurry. Gradually pour this mixture into the simmering sauce while stirring constantly. This will help thicken the sauce into a smooth, glossy consistency. Continue to cook for another 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce has thickened.

If you like your sauce extra lemony, add the grated lemon zest at this stage, along with the minced ginger and garlic powder for an additional burst of flavor. Taste the sauce and adjust the sweetness or acidity by adding more sugar, honey, or lemon juice, depending on your preference.

5. Coating the Chicken

Once the chicken is fried and the sauce is ready, it’s time to bring everything together. You have two options for coating the chicken:

  1. Drizzle the sauce over the chicken: If you prefer a more subtle and crispy chicken texture, drizzle the lemon sauce over the chicken pieces just before serving. This method ensures that the chicken retains its crunch while still absorbing the tangy flavors of the sauce.

  2. Toss the chicken in the sauce: For a more traditional approach, place the fried chicken pieces in a large bowl and pour the lemon sauce over the top. Toss the chicken gently to coat each piece in the sauce. This will result in a sticky, sweet, and tangy coating.

6. Garnishing and Serving

Once the chicken is coated with the lemon sauce, transfer it to a serving platter. For an extra touch, garnish with thin lemon slices, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and a few chopped green onions or cilantro for color and freshness.

Crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken is best served immediately while the chicken is still crispy. Pair it with steamed jasmine rice or stir-fried vegetables for a complete meal.

Tips for the Perfect Crispy Chicken

  • Temperature of the Oil: One of the most important factors for achieving crispy chicken is ensuring the oil is hot enough. If the oil is too cool, the chicken will absorb too much oil and become greasy. A thermometer can help you monitor the oil’s temperature, which should be around 350°F (175°C).

  • Double Frying: For an extra crispy texture, you can double fry the chicken. After the first frying, let the chicken rest for a few minutes before frying it again. This will give the chicken an additional crunch.

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Fry the chicken in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan, which can cause the oil temperature to drop and result in soggy chicken.

  • Adjust the Sauce to Your Taste: If you prefer a more tangy sauce, add more lemon juice or vinegar. For a sweeter sauce, increase the sugar or honey. Adjusting the sauce to your personal preference can make a big difference in the final flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken is sure to become a favorite in your household. It combines the savory and crispy texture of fried chicken with the sweet and tangy flavors of lemon, creating a perfect balance of flavors. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or a group of friends, this dish is sure to impress.

The best part? It’s easy to make, and you don’t need to be an expert chef to master this recipe. By following these simple steps, you’ll be able to make a dish that tastes just as good—if not better—than what you would get at your favorite Chinese restaurant.

Variations of Lemon Chicken

While the classic crispy Chinese Lemon Chicken is delicious on its own, there are a few variations you can try to mix things up:

  • Spicy Lemon Chicken: If you love a little heat, try adding some red chili flakes or chopped fresh chilies to the lemon sauce. The spicy kick will balance perfectly with the sweetness of the lemon.

  • Lemon Chicken with Vegetables: For a more hearty meal, add some sautéed bell peppers, onions, and snap peas to the dish. These vegetables will add flavor and texture while complementing the lemony sauce.

  • Baked Lemon Chicken: For a lighter version, instead of frying the chicken, bake it at 400°F (200°C) for about 20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until it’s golden and crispy. Then toss the baked chicken in the lemon sauce for a healthier alternative.
