The Best Rice Recipes to Serve with Seafood


When it comes to pairing seafood with the perfect side dish, few choices are as versatile and satisfying as rice. Whether you’re cooking up a delicate white fish, flavorful shrimp, or indulgent lobster, rice can complement these dishes with its mild flavor, varied textures, and ability to soak up delicious sauces. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best rice recipes that will elevate your seafood dishes and bring out the best in your meals.

1. Classic Spanish Paella with Seafood

Paella is a traditional Spanish dish that brings together saffron-infused rice, a medley of seafood, and aromatic vegetables. Paella is often seen as the ultimate rice recipe for seafood lovers. The key to creating a great paella is to ensure that the rice absorbs all the flavors from the seafood and broth, resulting in a one-pan dish that feels indulgent yet comforting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Arborio or short-grain rice
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb mussels
  • 1 lb clams
  • 1/2 lb squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups seafood broth or chicken broth
  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon wedges
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, soak the saffron threads in a couple of tablespoons of warm water for about 10 minutes.
  2. In a large paella pan or wide skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, and soaked saffron with its water. Cook for another 2 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  4. Add the rice to the pan and stir to coat it in the spices and vegetables. Cook for 2-3 minutes to slightly toast the rice.
  5. Pour in the seafood broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover, and cook for about 10-12 minutes.
  6. Add the shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid on top of the rice. Cover and cook for another 8-10 minutes, or until the seafood is fully cooked and the rice is tender.
  7. Remove from heat, discard any mussels or clams that haven’t opened, and garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges. Serve hot.

The combination of saffron, seafood, and rice makes this paella recipe a showstopper. It’s perfect for special occasions or a casual seafood feast with friends and family.

2. Coconut Rice with Grilled Shrimp

Coconut rice is a flavorful and aromatic alternative to regular white rice. Its creamy texture and hint of sweetness make it an excellent match for grilled shrimp, which provides a smoky, savory contrast. This dish is ideal for a tropical-inspired seafood meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a medium saucepan, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  3. While the rice is cooking, prepare the shrimp. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, paprika, and garlic powder. Heat a grill pan or outdoor grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes per side until pink and slightly charred.
  5. Fluff the coconut rice with a fork and transfer it to serving plates. Top with the grilled shrimp and garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

The richness of coconut milk balances perfectly with the grilled shrimp, offering a delightful contrast of flavors and textures. This coconut rice is a great accompaniment to any seafood, adding a tropical flair to your meal.

3. Lemon Herb Rice with Seared Scallops

For a lighter, zesty rice dish, lemon herb rice is a perfect complement to seared scallops. The freshness of lemon and the aromatic herbs brighten up the rich, sweet taste of the scallops, making for a light yet satisfying seafood meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb scallops, cleaned and patted dry
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Olive oil for searing

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the rice, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-18 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the lemon zest, lemon juice, parsley, thyme, salt, and pepper. Once the rice is done, stir in the lemon herb mixture to infuse the rice with its refreshing flavors.
  3. While the rice is cooking, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season the scallops with salt and pepper.
  4. When the skillet is hot, add the scallops and sear for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serve the lemon herb rice on a plate and top with the seared scallops. Garnish with additional fresh parsley if desired.

This dish highlights the delicate flavor of the scallops, with the citrusy rice adding a burst of freshness to every bite. It’s a simple yet elegant recipe that’s perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a sophisticated dinner party.

4. Garlic Butter Rice with Crab Legs

Crab legs are an indulgent and flavorful seafood option, and pairing them with garlic butter rice creates a rich, comforting dish. The garlic butter sauce infuses the rice with savory flavors that complement the sweet, tender crab meat beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb snow crab legs or king crab legs, cooked and cracked
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a large saucepan, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the rice, reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is tender.
  2. While the rice is cooking, melt the butter in a separate skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Stir in the chili flakes if using.
  3. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork and stir in the garlic butter mixture, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve the garlic butter rice on a platter and arrange the crab legs on top. Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges for a bright contrast.

The garlic butter rice enhances the natural sweetness of the crab meat, creating a luxurious and satisfying dish that’s sure to impress. This recipe is perfect for a special occasion or a lavish seafood dinner at home.

5. Pineapple Fried Rice with Shrimp

For a playful twist on traditional fried rice, pineapple fried rice brings tropical sweetness and vibrant flavors to the table. The addition of shrimp adds a savory element, while the pineapple lends a juicy burst of flavor. This dish works beautifully as a side or a main course for seafood lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably cold)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup diced pineapple (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and garlic and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes.
  2. Push the onion and garlic to the side of the pan and add the shrimp. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until the shrimp turns pink and opaque. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the diced carrots and peas. Cook for 3-4 minutes until tender.
  4. Push the vegetables to the side of the pan and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble them until fully cooked, then mix them with the vegetables.
  5. Add the cold rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir in the pineapple, soy sauce, fish sauce, and cooked shrimp. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly to ensure the rice is heated through.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with lime wedges.

This vibrant fried rice brings together sweet, savory, and tangy elements, creating a fun and flavorful dish that pairs perfectly with shrimp and other seafood. It’s a fantastic choice for a quick dinner or to serve as a side at your next seafood feast.

Conclusion

Rice is one of the most versatile and comforting side dishes that pairs beautifully with seafood. Whether you’re looking for something light and zesty, like lemon herb rice, or something rich and indulgent, such as garlic butter rice, these recipes will help you create unforgettable meals. From the vibrant and hearty Spanish paella to the sweet and savory pineapple fried rice, each dish enhances the flavors of your seafood and brings something unique to the table. So, the next time you’re cooking seafood, consider one of these rice recipes for the perfect side dish to complete your meal!
