Springtime Quiches: Recipes That Combine Eggs and Fresh Flavors


When spring arrives, it brings with it a burst of vibrant colors and fresh flavors. What better way to celebrate this season than by indulging in delicious springtime quiches? These savory delights combine the richness of eggs with an array of fresh ingredients, creating a perfect harmony of taste and texture. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering quiche recipes that embrace the essence of spring.

1. Asparagus and Goat Cheese Quiche

Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cooking Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pie crust
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the asparagus to the skillet and sauté for an additional 3 minutes, or until it is bright green and slightly tender. Remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Spread the sautéed onion and asparagus evenly over the pie crust. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese on top.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables and cheese, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  7. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden and the center is set.
  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Spinach and Feta Quiche

Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pie crust
  • 6 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 ounces feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, sauté the chopped onion over medium heat until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, nutmeg, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  5. Spread the sautéed spinach and onion evenly over the pie crust. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese on top.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables and cheese, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  7. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden and the center is set.
  8. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

3. Tomato and Basil Quiche

Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pie crust
  • 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, torn into small pieces
  • 4 ounces mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  2. Arrange the tomato slices in a single layer on a paper towel-lined plate. Sprinkle them with salt and let them sit for 10 minutes to remove excess moisture.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  4. Spread the torn basil leaves evenly over the pie crust. Place the tomato slices on top of the basil.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the tomatoes.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables and cheese, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  7. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden and the center is set.
  8. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

These springtime quiche recipes offer a delightful combination of eggs and fresh flavors. Whether you choose the asparagus and goat cheese quiche, the spinach and feta quiche, or the tomato and basil quiche, each bite will transport you to a sunny garden bursting with the essence of spring. Enjoy these quiches as a light lunch, a brunch centerpiece, or even a picnic treat. Embrace the season’s bounty and savor the taste of spring with these delicious recipes.
