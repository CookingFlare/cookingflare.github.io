Picnic-Inspired Stuffed Bell Pepper Recipes


When it comes to picnics, there’s something special about enjoying delicious food in the great outdoors. One dish that perfectly captures the essence of a picnic is stuffed bell peppers. Not only are they easy to transport, but they also offer a burst of flavors and colors that are sure to elevate any outdoor gathering. In this blog post, we’ll explore three unique and mouthwatering stuffed bell pepper recipes that are perfect for a picnic.

Classic Italian Stuffed Bell Peppers

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 40 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground Italian sausage
  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the Italian sausage, breaking it apart with a spoon, until browned.
  4. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened.
  5. Stir in the cooked rice, marinara sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheeses.
  7. Spoon the sausage and rice mixture into the prepared bell peppers.
  8. Place the stuffed bell peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  9. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is bubbly.
  10. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

These classic Italian stuffed bell peppers are a crowd-pleaser and can be enjoyed hot or at room temperature, making them an ideal choice for a picnic.

Southwest Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Avocado slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, cherry tomatoes, red onion, cilantro, cumin, chili powder, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the quinoa mixture into the prepared bell peppers.
  5. Place the stuffed bell peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with avocado slices before serving.

These Southwest quinoa stuffed bell peppers are packed with vibrant flavors and are a great vegetarian option for a picnic.

Mediterranean Couscous Stuffed Bell Peppers

Prep Time: 25 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup couscous, cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 cup chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked couscous, chickpeas, Kalamata olives, sun-dried tomatoes, parsley, feta cheese, olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
