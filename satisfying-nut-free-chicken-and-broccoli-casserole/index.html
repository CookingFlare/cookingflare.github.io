Satisfying Nut-Free Chicken and Broccoli Casserole


If you’re looking for a comforting, satisfying dish that the whole family will love, look no further than this Nut-Free Chicken and Broccoli Casserole. This recipe combines tender chicken, vibrant broccoli, and a creamy sauce, all topped with a golden, cheesy crust. Not only is it delicious, but it’s also easy to prepare, making it the perfect weeknight dinner. Whether you’re cooking for picky eaters, someone with nut allergies, or just in need of a hearty meal, this casserole is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Casserole:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 4 cups fresh broccoli florets (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup (or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

For Homemade Cream of Chicken Soup (if desired):

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken and Broccoli

If you haven’t already cooked your chicken, you can do this by boiling, baking, or pan-searing it until fully cooked. Allow it to cool before shredding or dicing.

For the broccoli, if using fresh, steam the florets until they are bright green and tender but still crisp, about 4-5 minutes. If using frozen broccoli, simply thaw and drain any excess water.

Step 2: Make the Cream of Chicken Soup (Optional)

Although you can use a canned version, making your own cream of chicken soup can elevate the flavor of your casserole. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute until golden. Gradually add the chicken broth and milk, whisking continuously until the mixture thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and set aside.

Step 3: Combine Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, steamed broccoli, cooked rice, sour cream, cream of chicken soup (if using), garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, salt, and paprika. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly coated.

Step 4: Assemble the Casserole

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with cooking spray or butter. Pour the chicken and broccoli mixture into the prepared dish, spreading it evenly.

Step 5: Add Cheese and Breadcrumbs

Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the top of the casserole. If you prefer a crunchy topping, add the breadcrumbs evenly over the cheese. This will create a delightful texture contrast when baked.

Step 6: Bake

Cover the casserole with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and the top is golden brown.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once baked, remove the casserole from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or crusty bread for a complete meal.

Tips for Success

  • Customize Your Veggies: Feel free to add other vegetables like carrots, peas, or bell peppers for added nutrition and color.
  • Make It Ahead: This casserole can be prepared ahead of time. Simply assemble it, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to bake, remove it from the fridge and add an extra 10-15 minutes to the baking time.
  • Freeze for Later: This dish freezes well. To freeze, prepare the casserole as directed, but do not bake it. Cover tightly with foil and freeze. When you’re ready to eat, thaw overnight in the fridge and bake as directed.

Nutritional Benefits

This Nut-Free Chicken and Broccoli Casserole not only satisfies your taste buds but also offers several health benefits:

  • High in Protein: The chicken provides a good source of lean protein, essential for muscle repair and overall health.
  • Rich in Vitamins: Broccoli is loaded with vitamins C and K, fiber, and various antioxidants, making it a nutrient powerhouse.
  • Versatile: You can easily adapt this recipe to accommodate various dietary restrictions or preferences, ensuring everyone at the table can enjoy it.

Final Thoughts

This Nut-Free Chicken and Broccoli Casserole is a true crowd-pleaser that seamlessly combines flavors and textures into one delightful dish. Whether you’re feeding a family or prepping meals for the week, this casserole is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Not only is it easy to make, but it also saves well, making it perfect for leftovers. So, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a comforting, satisfying meal that’s sure to warm your heart!

Enjoy your cooking adventure, and don’t forget to share your experience with friends and family. Happy cooking!
