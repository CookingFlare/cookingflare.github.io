Classic German Chocolate Cake: Layers of Coconut Pecan Frosting


German Chocolate Cake is a classic dessert that has been loved by generations. With its rich, moist chocolate layers and decadent coconut pecan frosting, it’s no wonder why this cake has stood the test of time. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making this delicious cake from scratch, including the homemade coconut pecan frosting that takes it to the next level. So let’s get started!

Ingredients:

For the cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

For the coconut pecan frosting:

  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/3 cups shredded sweetened coconut
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat on medium speed for about 2 minutes, until the batter is smooth and well combined.

  4. Stir in the boiling water. The batter will be thin, but that’s what will give the cake its moist texture. Don’t worry, it will bake perfectly!

  5. Pour the batter equally into the prepared cake pans. Tap the pans gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean. Remove from the oven and let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. While the cakes are cooling, let’s make the coconut pecan frosting. In a medium saucepan, combine the evaporated milk, sugar, egg yolks, butter, and vanilla extract. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens, about 10-12 minutes.

  8. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the shredded coconut and chopped pecans. Let the frosting cool completely before using it to assemble the cake.

  9. Once the cakes and frosting are completely cooled, it’s time to assemble the German Chocolate Cake. Place one cake layer on a serving plate and spread a generous amount of the coconut pecan frosting on top. Repeat with the second and third cake layers, stacking them on top of each other and frosting in between.

  10. Use the remaining frosting to generously cover the sides and top of the cake. You can leave it smooth or create decorative swirls with a spatula.

  11. For the finishing touch, you can sprinkle some additional shredded coconut and pecan pieces on top of the cake for added texture and visual appeal.

  12. Slice and serve this Classic German Chocolate Cake with Layers of Coconut Pecan Frosting to your family and friends, and watch their faces light up with delight!

Enjoy this timeless dessert that combines the richness of chocolate with the sweetness of coconut and pecans. The moist cake layers paired with the indulgent coconut pecan frosting create a flavor combination that is hard to resist. Whether it’s a special occasion or just a craving for something delicious, this Classic German Chocolate Cake will always be a crowd-pleaser. Happy baking!
