Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth: Creating Chinese Egg Custard Tarts at Home


If you have a sweet tooth and a love for Chinese desserts, then you’re in for a treat. Chinese Egg Custard Tarts, also known as Dan Tat, are a delightful combination of flaky pastry and smooth, creamy custard. These tarts are a popular dim sum offering, but they’re also a beloved treat enjoyed at any time of day. If you’ve ever wanted to recreate the magic of these delectable tarts in your own kitchen, then you’re in the right place. In this blog post, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of making Chinese Egg Custard Tarts at home. Get ready to embark on a culinary adventure and satisfy your sweet cravings with these irresistible treats.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 50g powdered sugar
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • A pinch of salt

For the Custard Filling:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 250ml whole milk
  • 50ml heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Pastry:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Cut the unsalted butter into small cubes and add it to the flour mixture.
  3. Use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Add the lightly beaten egg to the mixture and gently knead it until it forms a smooth dough.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Custard Filling:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the whole milk and heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Gradually pour the warm milk and cream mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
  4. Strain the custard mixture through a fine sieve to ensure a smooth texture. Set aside.

Assembling the Tarts:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled pastry dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness.
  3. Use a round cookie cutter to cut out circles of dough that will fit into your tart molds.
  4. Gently press the dough into the tart molds, ensuring it covers the bottom and sides evenly.
  5. Carefully pour the custard filling into each tart shell, filling them about 80% full.

Baking the Tarts:

  1. Place the filled tart molds on a baking sheet and transfer them to the preheated oven.
  2. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the custard is set and the pastry is golden brown.
  3. Remove the tarts from the oven and let them cool in the molds for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the tarts have cooled, they are ready to be served and enjoyed. These Chinese Egg Custard Tarts are best enjoyed fresh, with a cup of hot tea or coffee. The contrast between the flaky, buttery pastry and the creamy, sweet custard is simply irresistible. Whether you’re treating yourself to a midday snack or impressing guests at a gathering, these homemade tarts are sure to be a hit.

Conclusion

Creating Chinese Egg Custard Tarts at home is a rewarding endeavor that allows you to indulge in the rich flavors of this classic Chinese dessert. With a few simple ingredients and a bit of patience, you can bring the essence of a traditional dim sum experience into your own kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the sweetness of homemade Chinese Egg Custard Tarts. Happy baking!
