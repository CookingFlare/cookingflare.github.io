Sous Vide Pastrami Recipe for Sandwich Lovers


For sandwich enthusiasts, few things rival the satisfaction of biting into a perfectly crafted pastrami sandwich. The smoky aroma, the bold spices, and the tender juiciness of the meat combine to create a culinary experience worth every effort. While traditional methods of making pastrami involve lengthy curing and smoking processes, the sous vide technique provides a modern, foolproof way to achieve those unparalleled flavors with precise control.

Below, you’ll find a complete guide to creating your own sous vide pastrami—perfect for layering into a thick sandwich with mustard, pickles, and a hearty rye bread. This recipe will ensure every bite is bursting with flavor. Be prepared to impress not just yourself, but anyone lucky enough to be gifted one of your homemade pastrami sandwiches.

The Secret to Incredible Pastrami

The hallmark of pastrami lies in three elements: the curing process, the spice rub, and the cooking technique. Sous vide cooking takes the guesswork out of achieving tender meat by maintaining a controlled temperature throughout the cooking process. The key advantage? You can produce delicately tender pastrami with robust flavors and perfect juiciness every single time.

This recipe will guide you through curing the meat to create pastrami’s characteristic tangy brine, applying the signature spice mix, using the sous vide immersion circulator to cook the meat, and finishing it in a smoker or oven to achieve that smokiness.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Cure:

  • 1 gallon water
  • 1 cup kosher salt
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Prague Powder #1 (pink curing salt)
  • 3 tablespoons ground coriander
  • 2 tablespoons cracked black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons mustard seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves

For the Spice Rub:

  • 3 tablespoons cracked black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder

For the Meat:

  • 4-5 pounds beef brisket (flat cut preferred)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Brine

Start by making the brine for the curing process. In a large pot, mix water, kosher salt, sugar, and pink curing salt until dissolved. Add coriander, cracked black pepper, mustard seeds, minced garlic, bay leaves, and ground cloves. Heat the brine to a simmer and allow the spices to infuse for 10-15 minutes. Let the brine cool completely before using. Cooling is essential to ensure the meat starts curing at a safe temperature.

Step 2: Cure the Brisket

Place the brisket into a large, resealable bag or airtight container. Pour the cooled brine over the meat, ensuring it’s fully submerged. Seal the container and refrigerate for 5-7 days. Flip the brisket every day to ensure even curing. This step is crucial for developing pastrami’s iconic flavor.

Step 3: Rinse and Dry

After curing, remove the brisket from the brine and rinse it thoroughly under cold water to remove excess salt and spices. Pat it dry with paper towels. At this stage, the brisket will have absorbed the flavors from the brine while staying tender for the next steps.

Step 4: Apply the Spice Rub

Mix all the spice rub ingredients in a small bowl. Generously coat the brisket with the spice mixture, pressing it into the meat’s surface. The spice rub is what creates the bold, peppery crust—an essential component of pastrami.

Step 5: Cook with Sous Vide

Heat your sous vide immersion circulator to 155°F (68°C). Place the seasoned brisket in a vacuum-sealed bag or a heavy-duty freezer bag using the water displacement method to remove air. Seal it tightly to prevent water intrusion during the cooking process.

Submerge the bag in your sous vide water bath and cook for 24-36 hours. The longer cooking time allows the collagen in the meat to break down, resulting in the melt-in-your-mouth texture that pastrami is known for.

Step 6: Chill and Rest

After the sous vide process is complete, immediately transfer the bagged brisket into an ice bath to halt cooking and cool the meat down. Let the brisket chill for at least one hour. This step makes the meat easier to slice and prevents overcooking during the next phase.

Step 7: Smoke or Oven-Finish

To achieve the signature smoky crust, you’ll need to finish the pastrami in a smoker or an oven.

For Smoking:

Preheat your smoker to 225°F (107°C). Remove the brisket from the bag and place it directly on the smoker racks. Smoke the meat for about 2 hours using wood chips like hickory or cherry for added depth of flavor.

For Oven-Finishing:

Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). Place the brisket on a baking rack set over a tray to collect any drippings. Roast it for 1-1.5 hours or until a fragrant, crispy crust has formed.

Step 8: Slice and Serve

Once the brisket is finished smoking or roasting, let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing. Pastrami should be sliced thinly against the grain for the best texture. At this point, you’ll find the meat tender, flavorful, and wonderfully aromatic.

How to Build the Perfect Pastrami Sandwich

A pastrami sandwich is an art form. Don’t just pile ingredients together—build it with care. Start with slices of fresh rye bread slathered with yellow mustard. Layer the thinly sliced pastrami generously, then add crunchy pickles for a tangy finish. You can add Swiss cheese and melt it for a twist, but many purists will tell you to keep it simple: bread, pastrami, mustard, and pickles. Pair the sandwich with potato chips or coleslaw for the ultimate deli-style meal.

Why Sous Vide Makes a Difference

Traditional pastrami recipes involve boiling the brisket before roasting or smoking it. While effective, this method can lead to uneven cooking and occasionally tough meat. Sous vide ensures the brisket is cooked evenly and gently, resulting in unmatched tenderness. It also provides flexibility, allowing you to start the cooking process one day and finish it later.

Additionally, sous vide enhances the flavors of the spice rub and curing brine, locking them into the meat for an exceptional end result. If you’re serious about creating the perfect pastrami sandwich, sous vide is your secret weapon.

Tips for Making the Best Pastrami

  1. Quality Meat Matters: Choose a high-quality brisket for the best results. Look for marbling but avoid cuts that are overly fatty.
  2. Don’t Skimp the Curing Time: Curing for at least 5 days guarantees that the flavors of the brine penetrate deeply into the brisket.
  3. Use a Smoker for Depth: While oven-finishing works well, using a smoker adds layers of complexity to the flavor profile.
  4. Warm Your Pastrami Before Serving: If assembling sandwiches later, gently steam the sliced pastrami or heat it in a skillet with some water to bring back that freshly cooked tenderness.
  5. Experiment With Toppings: Pastrami goes well with everything from slaw to spicy peppers, but balance the textures and flavors without overpowering the meat.

Perfectly tender, richly flavored, and delightfully seasoned, sous vide pastrami is the sandwich lover’s dream come true. Whether it’s a casual lunch or a special occasion, this recipe ensures you’ll have homemade pastrami that exceeds any deli version. Now, grab your apron, prep the brisket, and get ready to enjoy the ultimate pastrami sandwich experience!
