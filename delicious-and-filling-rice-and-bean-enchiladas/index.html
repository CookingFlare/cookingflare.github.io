Delicious and Filling Rice and Bean Enchiladas


Enchiladas are a beloved staple in Mexican cuisine, celebrated for their versatility and vibrant flavors. Among the many variations, rice and bean enchiladas stand out as a delicious, filling, and vegetarian-friendly option that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re preparing a weeknight dinner or hosting a festive gathering, these enchiladas are sure to please even the pickiest eaters. Let’s dive into this mouthwatering recipe that combines wholesome ingredients, savory spices, and a touch of love.

Ingredients

For the Enchiladas:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (white, brown, or a blend)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers (red, green, or a mix)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a vegan alternative)
  • 8-10 large corn tortillas

For the Enchilada Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • Salt, to taste

Garnishes:

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt (optional)
  • Sliced avocado or guacamole
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

Start by heating a large skillet over medium heat. Add a splash of olive oil and sauté the chopped onion until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and diced bell peppers, cooking for another 2-3 minutes until softened.

Incorporate the cooked rice, black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper into the skillet. Stir well to combine all the ingredients, allowing them to warm through. Taste the mixture and adjust seasoning if necessary. Once ready, remove from heat and stir in half of the shredded cheese.

Step 2: Make the Enchilada Sauce

In a separate saucepan, combine the enchilada sauce, diced tomatoes, oregano, cayenne pepper (if using), and a pinch of salt. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, allowing the flavors to meld for about 5 minutes. You can adjust the consistency by adding a little water if it’s too thick.

Step 3: Assemble the Enchiladas

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). To assemble the enchiladas, spread a thin layer of the enchilada sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.

Take a tortilla and add a generous scoop of the rice and bean filling to the center. Roll the tortilla tightly, placing it seam-side down in the baking dish. Repeat this process for the remaining tortillas and filling, arranging them in the dish closely together.

Step 4: Top with Sauce and Cheese

Once all the enchiladas are in the dish, pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the top, ensuring they are well covered. Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese evenly across the surface.

Step 5: Bake to Perfection

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once baked, remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro, a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt, sliced avocado, and lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Rice and Bean Enchiladas

  • Tortilla Choices: While corn tortillas are traditional, you can use whole wheat or gluten-free tortillas depending on your dietary needs.
  • Add Protein: For those who prefer meat, shredded chicken or ground turkey can easily be added to the filling mixture.
  • Make Ahead: These enchiladas can be assembled a day in advance. Just cover and refrigerate until you’re ready to bake them.
  • Freezing: Cooked and cooled enchiladas freeze well. Just wrap them tightly in foil and store them in an airtight container. To reheat, bake from frozen at 375°F for about 30-40 minutes.

Nutritional Benefits

Rice and bean enchiladas are not just delicious; they also pack a nutritious punch! Rice provides a good source of carbohydrates, while beans are rich in protein and fiber. This combination not only keeps you feeling full but also helps stabilize blood sugar levels. The addition of vegetables like bell peppers and corn adds vitamins and minerals, making this dish a well-rounded option for lunch or dinner.

Conclusion

Delicious and filling rice and bean enchiladas are a fantastic way to enjoy a comforting meal that’s both satisfying and nutritious. With minimal effort, you can create a dish that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re celebrating a special event or simply looking for a hearty weeknight dinner, these enchiladas are sure to impress. Gather your ingredients, put on your favorite music, and enjoy the process of creating this delightful dish. Remember, cooking is not just about feeding your body; it’s about feeding your soul. Happy cooking!
