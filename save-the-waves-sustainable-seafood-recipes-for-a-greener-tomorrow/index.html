Save the Waves: Sustainable Seafood Recipes for a Greener Tomorrow


As the world becomes more aware of the impact our daily actions have on the environment, it’s essential to heed the call of “sustainable living” and embrace it wholeheartedly. One such area where we can effect change is in our plates, specifically in the seafood we eat. In this blog post, we’ll explore three sustainable seafood recipes that will allow you to Save the Waves and create a greener tomorrow.

1. Pan-Roasted Wild-Caught Salmon with Citrus Salsa

Wild-caught salmon is more sustainable than farm-raised salmon, as it typically has a lower impact on the surrounding ecosystem. This recipe for pan-roasted wild-caught salmon with citrus salsa is not only delicious but also promotes a healthier lifestyle. Here’s how you can prepare it in a few easy steps:

Ingredients:

  • 4 skin-on wild-caught salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped shallots
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • AO ZIRONE Citrus Set of 4 – 3-Piece Reusable Citrus Juicer Lemon Lime Squeezer, Manual Citrus Press, Premium Stainless Steel Lemon Lime Orange Citrus Press Squeezer
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Finely grated zest of 1 lime
  • 2 oranges - peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • 1 avocado - peeled, pitted, and diced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Season salmon fillets with salt and pepper.

  2. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, shallots, lemon juice, and lime juice. Brush the mixture on both sides of the salmon fillets.

  3. Spread panko breadcrumbs on a plate, and press the salmon fillets onto the breadcrumbs on both sides.

  4. Heat a large ovenproof skillet over medium heat. Add the salmon fillets, skin-side up, and cook for 2 to 3 minutes, until the skin is crispy.

  5. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 5 to 7 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fillets.

  6. While the salmon is roasting, prepare the citrus salsa. In a medium bowl, combine chopped cilantro, lime zest, orange segments, avocado, lemon juice, and lime juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and set aside.

  7. Once the salmon is cooked, transfer the fillets to a plate, skin-side up, and top with the citrus salsa.

Enjoy this tasty and sustainable seafood dish!

2. Eco-Scallop Ceviche with Tostadas

The scallop industry has faced its fair share of controversy over the years, but the eco-friendly scallop -those harvested using sustainable and eco-friendly methods- offers a more responsible and delicious option. Fresh, crunchy, and packed with explosive flavors, this eco-scallop ceviche with tostadas is the perfect recipe to try at home.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound eco-friendly scallops, halved
  • 1 small red onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeds removed, finely diced
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 cup cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 dozen small corn tostadas

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine scallops and enough water to cover by 2 inches. Add 2 tablespoons lime juice and a pinch of salt. Allow to sit for 5 minutes, until scallops are opaque. Drain and pat dry.

  2. To the same bowl, add onion, jalapeño, avocado, and remaining lime juice. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Gently fold in cilantro and serve with tostadas.

Not only is this ceviche refreshing and flavorful, but it also helps preserve ocean ecosystems and the marine life that calls them home.

3. Ocean-Friendly Shrimp & Veggie Stir Fry with Quinoa

Sustainable shrimp farming practices are more eco-friendly and contribute less to coastal destruction. If you’re not already incorporating shrimp in your culinary adventures, the Ocean-Friendly Shrimp & Veggie Stir Fry with Quinoa is a great recipe to try. Packed with protein, iron, and loads of flavor, this dish is a must-try for seafood lovers!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 8 oz sustainable Ocean-Friendly shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon chili garlic sauce (optional)
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring quinoa and water to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18 minutes, or until tender and liquid reduced.

  2. While quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, and sauté until onion is translucent, about 3 minutes.

  3. Add red bell pepper and yellow squash to the skillet, and cook for 5 minutes, until vegetables are slightly softened.

  4. Push vegetables to the side of the skillet, and add shrimp. Season with salt and pepper, and cook until pink and opaque, about 3 minutes.

  5. Stir in sesame oil, soy sauce, and chili garlic sauce (if using). Mix well to combine all ingredients.

  6. Serve shrimp, vegetables, and quinoa together, garnished with sliced green onions.

This healthy and flavorful shrimp & veggie stir fry with quinoa not only satisfies your seafood craving but also supports sustainable shrimp farming practices.

Conclusion

Saving the waves and protecting our oceans is not an easy task. However, by making mindful choices in the foods we consume, we can make a positive impact on marine ecosystems. In addition to these recipes, consider opting for locally-caught fish, choosing seafood with eco-friendly certifications, and eating seafood lower on the food chain. Together, we can help preserve the astounding beauty and diversity of our oceans for generations to come. Happy cooking, and best wishes on your sustainable seafood journey!
