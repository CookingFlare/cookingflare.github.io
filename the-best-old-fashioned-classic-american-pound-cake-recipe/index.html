The Best Old-Fashioned Classic American Pound Cake Recipe


Are you craving a timeless, classic dessert that’s rich in history and flavor? Look no further than the beloved old-fashioned American pound cake. This iconic recipe has been a staple in American kitchens for generations, cherished for its buttery richness and dense, moist texture. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to treat yourself to a slice of nostalgia, this pound cake is sure to delight your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Pound Cake:

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk, at room temperature

For the Glaze (optional):

  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and flour a 10-inch bundt pan, ensuring that it’s thoroughly coated to prevent the cake from sticking.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This step is crucial for achieving the cake’s signature dense yet tender crumb.

  3. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Incorporate the pure vanilla extract for a fragrant, comforting aroma.

  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the creamed mixture, alternating with the room-temperature whole milk. Begin and end with the flour mixture, ensuring each addition is fully combined before adding the next.

  5. Once the batter is smooth and velvety, pour it into the prepared bundt pan, smoothing the top with a spatula to ensure an even bake.

  6. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 60 to 70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The cake should have a golden-brown crust and a firm, springy texture.

  7. Allow the pound cake to cool in the pan for 15 minutes before carefully transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. This gradual cooling process helps the flavors to develop and the texture to set.

  8. If desired, prepare a simple glaze by whisking together the confectioners’ sugar, milk, and pure vanilla extract until smooth. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled pound cake, allowing it to set before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This classic American pound cake is a versatile dessert that pairs beautifully with a variety of accompaniments. Serve it with a dollop of freshly whipped cream and a medley of seasonal berries for a refreshing, vibrant twist. For a touch of indulgence, consider topping each slice with a scoop of premium vanilla ice cream and a drizzle of warm caramel sauce. Whether enjoyed on its own or dressed up with decadent embellishments, this timeless recipe is a true crowd-pleaser.

Conclusion

In a world filled with fleeting food trends, the classic American pound cake remains a steadfast symbol of tradition and comfort. Its simple yet indulgent nature has secured its place in countless family recipe collections, where it continues to evoke cherished memories of gatherings and celebrations. By mastering this time-honored recipe, you’re not only savoring a delectable dessert but also preserving a piece of culinary heritage. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and embark on a delicious journey through the annals of American baking with this exceptional pound cake recipe.
