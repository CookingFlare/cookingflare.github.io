Delightful Nut-Free Blueberry Muffin Recipe


Who can resist the allure of freshly baked blueberry muffins? With their bursting berries and tender crumb, they’re a beloved treat for breakfast or any time of day. This recipe for nut-free blueberry muffins ensures everyone can enjoy these delightful goodies without worry.

Ingredients

  • Dry Ingredients:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • Wet Ingredients:

    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • Additional:

    • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries (you can use frozen, but fresh is preferred)
    • Optional: Turbinado sugar for sprinkling on top

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease each cup lightly with oil.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together the yogurt, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry: Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Use a spatula or wooden spoon to gently fold everything together. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

  5. Add Blueberries: Gently fold the fresh blueberries into the batter. If using frozen blueberries, toss them with a tablespoon of flour first to prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the muffins.

  6. Fill Muffin Cups: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full. If desired, sprinkle the tops with a pinch of turbinado sugar for a crunchy finish.

  7. Bake: Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 18-22 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Cool and Enjoy: Remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for 5 minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely or enjoy them warm.

Tips for Success

  • Fresh vs. Frozen Blueberries: Fresh blueberries add the best flavor and texture to these muffins. If using frozen, do not thaw them before adding to the batter.

  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing can lead to dense muffins. Stir the batter just until the ingredients are combined.

  • Storage: Store leftover muffins in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or freeze them for longer storage.

Why Nut-Free?

Many traditional blueberry muffin recipes contain nuts or are made in facilities where cross-contamination can occur, making them unsafe for those with nut allergies. By omitting nuts entirely and using simple pantry staples, these nut-free blueberry muffins are a safer and equally delicious option for everyone to enjoy.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re baking these nut-free blueberry muffins for a leisurely weekend breakfast, a quick snack, or a lunchbox treat, they’re sure to be a hit. Their moist interior, bursting blueberries, and slightly crisp top make them irresistible to anyone who takes a bite. Share them with family and friends, and savor the joy of homemade goodness in every bite.

Enjoy baking and indulging in these delightful nut-free blueberry muffins! They’re a perfect way to celebrate the simplicity and sweetness of homemade baking, suitable for any occasion where a little sweetness is in order.
