Grilled Vegetable Tacos: A Vegetarian Option for Fourth of July


Tacos are a crowd-pleaser, and what better way to celebrate the Fourth of July than with a delicious, vegetarian twist on this classic dish? Grilled vegetable tacos are a fantastic option for those looking for a meatless alternative that is bursting with flavor and perfect for summer gatherings. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create mouthwatering grilled vegetable tacos that will impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

To make these grilled vegetable tacos, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, sliced lengthwise
  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange), seeded and sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 ears of corn, husked
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the zucchini, bell peppers, red onion, and corn. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Toss until the vegetables are evenly coated with the seasonings.

  3. Place the vegetables on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until they are charred and tender. Flip the vegetables occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  4. While the vegetables are grilling, warm the flour tortillas on the grill or in a dry skillet over medium heat. Cook for about 30 seconds on each side, or until they are heated through and pliable.

  5. Once the vegetables are done grilling, remove them from the heat and let them cool slightly. Cut the kernels off the corn cobs.

  6. To assemble the tacos, place a spoonful of the grilled vegetables onto each warm tortilla. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, chopped cilantro, and crumbled feta cheese. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top for an extra burst of flavor.

  7. Serve the grilled vegetable tacos immediately and enjoy!

Tips for Success

  • Feel free to customize your grilled vegetable tacos with your favorite toppings. Add some sliced avocado, a dollop of sour cream, or a drizzle of hot sauce for an extra kick.

  • Don’t have a grill? No problem! You can achieve similar results by roasting the vegetables in a hot oven (425°F/220°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly charred.

  • If you’re hosting a Fourth of July party, consider setting up a taco bar with various fillings and toppings. Let your guests build their own tacos and have fun experimenting with different flavor combinations.

  • For a complete meal, serve your grilled vegetable tacos with a side of Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh summer salad.

Conclusion

Grilled vegetable tacos are a fantastic vegetarian option for your Fourth of July celebration. They are packed with smoky, charred flavors and loaded with colorful, nutritious vegetables. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to add more plant-based options to your diet, these tacos are sure to be a hit. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy a delicious and satisfying meal that showcases the best flavors of summer. Happy Fourth of July!
