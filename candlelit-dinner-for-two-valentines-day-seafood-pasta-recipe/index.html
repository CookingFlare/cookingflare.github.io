Candlelit Dinner for Two: Valentine's Day Seafood Pasta Recipe


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a romantic candlelit dinner for two? If you want to impress your significant other with a delicious and elegant meal, look no further than this exquisite seafood pasta recipe. Combining the richness of the sea with the warmth of a homemade pasta dish, this recipe is sure to set the stage for an unforgettable evening.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of linguine pasta
  • 6 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 pound of assorted seafood (shrimp, scallops, and/or lobster)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Seafood: Begin by cleaning and deveining the shrimp, if using. If using scallops, pat them dry with a paper towel. Season the seafood with a pinch of salt and pepper.

  2. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook the linguine according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta water, and set it aside.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped shallot, and sauté until fragrant, about 2 minutes.

  4. Cook the Seafood: Push the aromatics to one side of the skillet and add the remaining butter. Once melted, add the seafood to the skillet and cook until just opaque and slightly caramelized, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  5. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon. Let the wine reduce by half, about 2-3 minutes.

  6. Add the Cream: Lower the heat and pour in the heavy cream, stirring to combine with the seafood and aromatics. Simmer the sauce gently until it thickens slightly, about 5-7 minutes. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can thin it out with a bit of the reserved pasta water.

  7. Combine the Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked linguine to the skillet, tossing to coat the pasta evenly with the seafood and creamy sauce. If desired, sprinkle in a pinch of red pepper flakes for a hint of heat.

  8. Garnish and Serve: Season the dish with additional salt and pepper to taste. Sprinkle the chopped parsley over the pasta and toss once more. Divide the seafood pasta between two serving plates and garnish with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

Conclusion

There you have it – a sumptuous and indulgent seafood pasta dish that is perfect for a romantic Valentine’s Day dinner. The combination of tender seafood, creamy sauce, and al dente pasta is sure to impress your loved one and create a memorable dining experience. Pair this delectable dish with a bottle of your favorite wine, dim the lights, and let the magic of the evening unfold. Cheers to love, good food, and cherished moments shared with that special someone.

So, what are you waiting for? Get ready to treat your significant other to a delightful candlelit dinner featuring this divine seafood pasta recipe. Happy cooking, and even happier Valentine’s Day!
