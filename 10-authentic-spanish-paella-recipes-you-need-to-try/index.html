10 Authentic Spanish Paella Recipes You Need to Try


Paella is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of Spain’s rich culinary heritage. Originating from the region of Valencia, this iconic Spanish dish combines rice, saffron, and various fresh ingredients cooked in a wide, shallow pan to create a symphony of flavors. While traditional paella may immediately conjure images of seafood and golden rice, the diversity of paella recipes reflects the vast regions and cultures across Spain.

In this post, explore ten authentic Spanish paella recipes that go beyond the basic versions to bring you unique tastes from land and sea. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook eager to deepen your repertoire or a curious food lover ready to embark on an aromatic journey, these recipes will inspire your next kitchen adventure.

1. Classic Valencian Paella (Paella Valenciana)

The original paella hails from Valencia and is considered the traditional foundation of all other variations. This recipe features rabbit, chicken, green beans, garrofó (a type of large white bean), and sometimes snails, simmered with saffron-infused rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Bomba or Calasparra rice
  • 1/2 lb chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 lb rabbit, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup garrofó beans (or lima beans as a substitute)
  • 1 cup green beans (preferably flat green beans)
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, grated
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • A generous pinch of saffron threads
  • 5 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs (optional)

Method

  1. Heat olive oil in a wide paella pan over medium heat. Brown the rabbit and chicken pieces thoroughly.
  2. Add green beans and sauté until slightly tender.
  3. Stir in the grated tomatoes and garlic; cook until the mixture thickens.
  4. Pour in the garrofó beans and stir.
  5. Add rice evenly across the pan; sprinkle with saffron.
  6. Pour chicken broth in and simmer without stirring until rice is cooked and socarrat (crispy bottom layer) forms.
  7. Garnish with rosemary and serve warm.

The heart of this version lies in its balance of meats and vegetables along with the smokey undertone from the socarrat.

2. Seafood Paella (Paella de Mariscos)

For seafood lovers, this paella is a festival of ocean flavors. Fresh scallops, clams, mussels, shrimp, and squid join saffron rice for a vibrant dish reminiscent of coastal Spain.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb mussels, cleaned
  • 1 lb clams, cleaned
  • 1/2 lb squid rings
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups fish stock or water
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Method

  1. In the paella pan, heat olive oil and sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until soft.
  2. Add grated tomatoes and cook until thickened.
  3. Stir in rice and saffron, coating the grains well.
  4. Pour fish stock and bring to a boil.
  5. When simmering, arrange the seafood on top; cover and cook until the rice is tender and seafood is properly cooked.
  6. Garnish with parsley and lemon wedges before serving.

This paella offers a briny freshness that captures the spirit of the Spanish coastline.

3. Mixed Paella (Paella Mixta)

The mixed or “mixta” paella combines both meat and seafood, catering to a broad range of palates. This fusion brings together chicken, shrimp, mussels, and veggies with aromatic rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup bomba rice
  • 1 cup chicken thighs, diced
  • 1/2 lb shrimp
  • 1/2 lb mussels
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method

  1. Brown chicken in olive oil until golden.
  2. Saute onion, pepper, and tomatoes; add paprika for depth.
  3. Stir in rice and saffron to coat grains well.
  4. Pour in chicken stock and bring to a boil.
  5. Add mussels and shrimp on top and simmer until the rice is cooked and shellfish is open.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and serve hot.

Mixed paella offers a versatile, crowd-pleasing flavor profile with a balance of textures.

4. Black Paella (Arroz Negro)

This immense favorite from Catalonia and Valencia features squid ink, which gives the rice a striking black color and a rich umami flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 lb squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • Squid ink (available at seafood markets)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 3 tomatoes, grated
  • 4 cups fish stock
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon wedges for garnish
  • Fresh parsley

Method

  1. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  2. Add squid and cook briefly.
  3. Stir in grated tomatoes, then add rice and coat with olive oil.
  4. Pour fish stock infused with squid ink and bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer until rice is done and liquid absorbed.
  6. Garnish with parsley and lemon; serve immediately.

The dense flavor of squid ink pairs wonderfully with the tender rice for an impressive presentation and experience.

5. Rabbit and Snail Paella (Paella con Conejo y Caracoles)

An earthy paella from Valencia, this version uses rabbit and snails to build a unique and rustic flavor in the dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 lb rabbit, chopped
  • 1 cup snails, cleaned (or substitute with baby clams)
  • 1/2 cup flat green beans
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 onion, minced
  • Saffron threads
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Method

  1. Brown rabbit meat in olive oil.
  2. Add onions and green beans, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in tomatoes and snails.
  4. Add rice and saffron, coating well.
  5. Pour broth and simmer without stirring.
  6. Cook until rice is tender and socarrat forms.

This recipe gives you a taste of rural Spain with wild earthiness and richness.

6. Vegetable Paella (Paella de Verduras)

For vegetarians or those seeking a lighter option, vegetable paella is an explosion of colors and fresh produce from the Spanish countryside.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 3 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges

Method

  1. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper in olive oil until tender.
  2. Add artichokes, green beans, and zucchini; cook briefly.
  3. Stir in grated tomatoes and saffron.
  4. Add rice, coating grains well.
  5. Pour vegetable broth and simmer until rice is cooked.
  6. Garnish with parsley and serve with lemon.

This colorful paella is as nourishing as it is delicious, highlighting the simplicity and freshness of Spanish vegetables.

7. Chicken and Chorizo Paella

Combining the smoky spiciness of chorizo sausage with tender chicken pieces, this hearty paella introduces bold flavors to the traditional dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 cup chicken thigh, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 pinch smoked paprika
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil

Method

  1. Brown chicken and chorizo in olive oil.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and bell pepper; cook until softened.
  3. Stir in tomatoes, paprika, and saffron.
  4. Add rice, coating in the aromatic mix.
  5. Pour in chicken broth and simmer until rice is cooked.
  6. Serve warm, enjoying the mix of smoky and savory flavors.

Chorizo’s fiery tang complements the comforting chicken and saffron rice brilliantly.

8. Lobster Paella (Paella de Bogavante)

A luxury take on the seafood paella, lobster paella is perfect for special occasions with rich and succulent lobster enhancing the dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 lobster (about 1.5 lbs), cooked and cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups seafood broth
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges

Method

  1. Heat olive oil and sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper.
  2. Add grated tomatoes and saffron; cook until thickened.
  3. Stir in rice and coat with the tomato mixture.
  4. Pour seafood broth and bring to a simmer.
  5. Once simmering, arrange lobster pieces on top.
  6. Cook until rice is tender and lobster is infused.
  7. Garnish with parsley and lemon.

This paella elevates classic seafood flavors with a premium touch.

9. Duck and Wild Mushroom Paella

Bringing a forest-inspired twist, this version combines tender duck with earthy wild mushrooms to create a decadent paella.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1 lb duck breast, diced
  • 1 cup wild mushrooms, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh thyme sprigs

Method

  1. Brown duck pieces in olive oil until crisp.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and mushrooms; cook until soft.
  3. Stir in tomatoes and saffron.
  4. Add rice and coat in the mixture.
  5. Pour broth and simmer without stirring until rice is cooked.
  6. Garnish with fresh thyme.

The combination of gamey duck and mushrooms delivers a rich and sophisticated flavor profile.

10. Paella with Clams and Chorizo

A daring fusion combining smoky chorizo and briny clams makes for a bold and flavorful paella bursting with personality.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups bomba rice
  • 1/2 lb clams, cleaned
  • 1/2 cup Spanish chorizo, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley

Method

  1. Sauté chorizo until oils release flavors.
  2. Add onion and garlic; cook until soft.
  3. Stir in tomatoes and saffron.
  4. Add rice, coating well.
  5. Pour broth and bring to simmer.
  6. Arrange clams on top and cook until they open and rice is tender.
  7. Garnish with parsley.

This recipe highlights how contrasting ingredients can harmonize beautifully in paella.

Final Thoughts

Paella is truly Spain on a plate — a dish that encourages sharing, storytelling, and savoring every bite. Each recipe here celebrates regional ingredients and cultural traditions, offering something unique for every taste. From the rustic rabbit and snails of Valencia to the luxurious lobster of the Mediterranean coast, these paella recipes can transform your meal into a festive experience.

Whichever variant you choose, the key is to use quality ingredients and a proper paella pan for that distinct texture and the beloved socarrat crust. Ready the saffron, gather your fresh produce, and dive into these ten authentic Spanish paella recipes. Your kitchen is about to take a flavorful trip to Spain!
