The Best Thai Noodle Recipes: From Pad See Ew to Drunken Noodles


Thai cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, textures, and aromas, and one of its most beloved components is undoubtedly its noodle dishes. From the sweet and savory notes of Pad See Ew to the spicy kick of Drunken Noodles, Thai noodle recipes offer a delightful culinary experience that can transport you straight to the bustling streets of Bangkok. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best Thai noodle recipes, providing you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and tricks to recreate these dishes in your own kitchen.

1. Pad See Ew: Stir-Fried Noodles with Soy Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz wide rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in hot water for about 30 minutes or until they are soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the broccoli, carrots, and bell peppers, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they are tender-crisp.
  3. Add the Noodles: Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet. Crack the eggs into the empty side and scramble them until fully cooked. Then, add the drained noodles to the skillet.
  4. Season the Dish: Pour the dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar over the noodles. Toss everything together until the noodles are evenly coated and heated through.
  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges on the side.

Tips:

  • For added protein, consider adding chicken, beef, or tofu to the dish.
  • Adjust the soy sauce according to your taste preference; some may prefer a saltier flavor.

2. Drunken Noodles (Pad Kee Mao): Spicy Stir-Fried Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz flat rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 Thai bird chilies, sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 1 cup sliced chicken or shrimp (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the flat rice noodles in hot water for about 30 minutes until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Stir-Fry the Aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok over high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced chilies, stir-frying for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add Protein and Vegetables: If using chicken or shrimp, add it to the wok and cook until fully cooked. Then, add the bell peppers and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Combine with Noodles: Add the soaked noodles to the wok, followed by the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Toss everything together until well combined.
  5. Finish with Basil: Stir in the Thai basil leaves and cook for an additional minute until the basil is wilted.
  6. Serve: Plate the noodles and enjoy the spicy, aromatic flavors.

Tips:

  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the protein with tofu and use vegetarian oyster sauce.
  • Adjust the number of chilies based on your spice tolerance.
