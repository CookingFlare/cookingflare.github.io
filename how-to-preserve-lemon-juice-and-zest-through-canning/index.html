How to Preserve Lemon Juice and Zest Through Canning


Lemons are one of the most versatile fruits in the kitchen. From brightening up a glass of water to enhancing savory dishes, the refreshing citrus flavor of lemons is irreplaceable. Whether you’re making lemonade, adding a tangy twist to marinades, or using the zest to give a burst of aroma, lemons are an essential part of many culinary experiences. However, there are times when fresh lemons are not available or when you want to preserve that fresh flavor for a longer period.

One of the best ways to extend the shelf life of lemons and ensure their flavor stays vibrant is by preserving lemon juice and zest through canning. Canning is an excellent method for storing lemon juice and zest, allowing you to enjoy their bright, fresh flavor throughout the year. If you’ve never canned before, don’t worry – we’ll walk you through the process step by step.

Why Canning Lemon Juice and Zest is a Great Idea

Canning lemon juice and zest offers several benefits:

  1. Long Shelf Life: Properly canned lemon juice and zest can last for up to a year or more when stored in a cool, dark place.
  2. Preserves Freshness: Canning locks in the fresh taste of lemons, allowing you to enjoy their citrusy flavor in dishes and drinks when fresh lemons are not in season.
  3. Convenience: With pre-canned lemon juice and zest, you can easily add that refreshing zing to your recipes without needing to slice or zest a lemon each time.
  4. Zero Waste: Canning allows you to use every part of the lemon, including the juice and zest, minimizing waste and making the most of your produce.

Let’s dive into the step-by-step process of how to preserve lemon juice and zest through canning.

What You’ll Need

Before you start the canning process, gather all the necessary equipment and ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

Equipment:

  • Canning Jars: You’ll need sterilized glass jars with tight-fitting lids. Typically, pint or quart jars are best for canning lemon juice.
  • Lid Bands: The metal bands that screw onto the jars to secure the lids in place.
  • Large Pot for Water Bath: This pot should be large enough to submerge the canning jars.
  • Lemon Juicer: A citrus juicer or reamer is helpful for extracting juice efficiently.
  • Zester or Microplane Grater: For zesting the lemons.
  • Funnel: A canning funnel helps to avoid spills when pouring juice into jars.
  • Tongs: For removing hot jars from the water bath.
  • Canning Kit (Optional): Some canning kits include tools like jar lifters, bubble removers, and lid magnet lifters, which can be helpful for beginners.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh Lemons: Ideally, choose organic lemons to avoid chemicals or pesticides on the peel. You’ll need about 6-10 lemons depending on how much juice and zest you plan to preserve.
  • Water: For water bath canning.
  • Canning Salt (Optional): If you’re preserving lemon zest, a small amount of salt can help to keep the zest fresh during storage.

Preparing the Lemons

Before you start the canning process, it’s essential to prepare your lemons properly.

Step 1: Wash the Lemons

Start by washing the lemons thoroughly to remove any dirt, wax, or pesticides. Even if you’re using organic lemons, it’s always a good idea to clean them before using.

Step 2: Zesting the Lemons

Use a zester or a microplane grater to zest the lemons. You only want to zest the outer yellow skin of the lemon – avoid the white pith beneath the skin, as it can be bitter. Zesting your lemons gives you the concentrated oils that are packed with flavor and aroma. Depending on your preference, you can zest all the lemons at once or zest them as you juice them.

For the best results, try to zest about 2 to 3 lemons at a time, as zesting can be time-consuming. Once you’ve finished zesting, set the zest aside in a bowl. You can use it immediately for canning or store it in an airtight container in the fridge until you’re ready to preserve it.

Step 3: Juicing the Lemons

Once the lemons are zested, it’s time to juice them. Cut each lemon in half and use a citrus juicer or reamer to extract the juice. Make sure to strain the juice to remove any seeds and pulp.

Step 4: Preparing for Canning

Now that you have your lemon juice and zest prepared, it’s time to move on to the canning process. It’s important to work with sterilized jars to ensure that your preserved lemon juice and zest are safe to store for an extended period.

Sterilizing Canning Jars

Sterilizing the jars before canning is a crucial step in preventing bacterial contamination. You can sterilize your jars by either:

  • Boiling: Submerge the jars in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  • Oven Method: Place the jars on a baking sheet and heat them in the oven at 225°F (107°C) for 10-15 minutes.

Once sterilized, remove the jars and set them on a clean towel or cooling rack to dry.

The Canning Process

Step 1: Preparing the Water Bath

To properly preserve lemon juice and zest, you’ll use the water bath canning method. This involves immersing the jars of lemon juice and zest in boiling water to create a vacuum seal.

Fill your large pot with enough water to submerge the jars, leaving at least an inch of water above the jars when they are placed inside. Heat the water to a rolling boil.

Step 2: Filling the Jars

Carefully pour the lemon juice into the sterilized jars, leaving about ½ inch of headspace at the top. The headspace is necessary to allow for expansion during the canning process. Add a small amount of zest into each jar, adjusting the amount according to your preference for flavor strength.

If you’re using salt, you can add ½ teaspoon of canning salt to each jar before filling it with juice and zest. This step is optional but can help preserve the zest’s freshness.

Step 3: Sealing the Jars

Once the jars are filled, wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth or paper towel to remove any juice or residue. This ensures a proper seal. Place the sterilized lids onto the jars and screw the metal bands on tightly, but not too tight.

Step 4: Processing the Jars

Carefully place the jars into the boiling water bath, making sure they are fully submerged. The water should cover the jars by at least an inch. Process the jars for 10-15 minutes to ensure the lemon juice and zest are properly sealed.

During this time, the heat will create a vacuum seal inside the jars, preserving the contents. After processing, carefully remove the jars from the water using tongs and allow them to cool completely on a clean surface.

Step 5: Checking the Seals

Once the jars have cooled, check the seals by pressing down in the center of each lid. If the lid doesn’t pop back, it has sealed correctly. If a lid hasn’t sealed, you can refrigerate that jar and use it within a few weeks.

Storing Canned Lemon Juice and Zest

Once your jars are sealed and the contents have cooled, it’s time to store them. Place your jars in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard, where they will stay fresh for up to a year or more.

It’s important to check the seals periodically. If you notice any signs of spoilage, such as mold or an off smell, discard the contents. Always ensure that your jars remain tightly sealed and stored properly.

How to Use Canned Lemon Juice and Zest

Canned lemon juice and zest are incredibly versatile. Here are just a few ideas for how to use them:

  • Lemonade: Mix canned lemon juice with water and sweetener for a refreshing glass of lemonade.
  • Baking: Add canned lemon zest to cakes, muffins, and cookies for a burst of citrus flavor.
  • Cooking: Use the zest and juice in marinades, salad dressings, or as a tangy addition to sauces.
  • Drinks: Mix canned lemon juice into cocktails or mocktails for a refreshing zing.
  • Homemade Preserves: Incorporate lemon zest into jams or fruit preserves for an added depth of flavor.

Tips for Success

  • Always use fresh, high-quality lemons for the best flavor and results.
  • If you prefer more intense lemon flavor, add more zest to the jars.
  • Be cautious of over-processing your jars during the canning process, as this can lead to a loss of flavor.
  • If you’re new to canning, consider following step-by-step tutorials or watching videos to get more familiar with the process.

Final Thoughts

Preserving lemon juice and zest through canning is a simple yet effective way to enjoy the bright, tangy flavor of lemons year-round. By following these easy steps, you can create your own supply of lemon juice and zest that is ready to enhance your culinary creations. Whether you’re making lemon meringue pie, refreshing lemonade, or adding zest to a savory dish, your homemade canned lemon preserves will be a valuable pantry staple.
