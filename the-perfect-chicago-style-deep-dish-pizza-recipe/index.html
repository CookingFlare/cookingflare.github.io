The Perfect Chicago-Style Deep Dish Pizza Recipe


Chicago-style deep dish pizza is a culinary legend that stands out in the world of pizza. With its thick, buttery crust, rich tomato sauce, and gooey cheese, this pizza offers a delicious twist on the traditional flat pizza. Whether you’re a pizza connoisseur or a home cook looking to try something new, making the perfect Chicago-style deep dish pizza can be both fun and rewarding.

In this blog post, we’ll guide you step by step to create a deep dish pizza that rivals what you might find in Chicago’s finest pizzerias. We’ll go through the history, the ingredients, and of course, the cooking techniques needed to get that pizza just right.

The History of Chicago-Style Deep Dish Pizza

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to understand the origins of this pizza style. Chicago-style deep dish pizza was created in the early 1940s at Pizzeria Uno, which is considered the birthplace of this iconic dish. The goal was to create a pizza that had all the elements of a hearty, filling meal: a thick, buttery crust that could support a hefty layer of cheese, meat, and tomato sauce. Over the years, deep dish pizza has evolved, but the key features have remained the same.

The pizza’s crust is typically much thicker than its New York counterpart, and it’s often referred to as more of a “pie” due to its deep, pan-like nature. The layers of cheese, meat, vegetables, and sauce are arranged in reverse order compared to a traditional pizza. This unique arrangement ensures that the crust stays crispy while the toppings meld into a flavorful, hearty dish.

Now that we have some background, let’s dive into how you can recreate this masterpiece at home.

Ingredients You’ll Need for the Perfect Deep Dish Pizza

To make Chicago-style deep dish pizza, you’ll need to gather a few key ingredients. Here’s a breakdown:

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornmeal (for extra texture)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water (110°F/43°C)
  • 1/2 cup olive oil (for a rich, crispy crust)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (melted, for added flavor)

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes (preferably San Marzano for the best flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for a little heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

For the Toppings:

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (preferably whole milk mozzarella for the best melt)
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage (either mild or spicy, based on preference)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped bell peppers (optional, for a little crunch and sweetness)
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making the Perfect Chicago-Style Deep Dish Pizza

Step 1: Make the Pizza Dough

First, you’ll need to make the dough for your deep dish pizza. This is an essential step because the dough provides the structure for this iconic dish.

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and instant yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5 minutes, until the mixture becomes foamy.
  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, cornmeal, and salt.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: Once the yeast has activated, add the olive oil and melted butter to the dry ingredients. Then, pour in the yeast mixture and stir until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Let the Dough Rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

While your dough is rising, it’s the perfect time to make the pizza sauce. A good sauce can make all the difference in deep dish pizza.

  1. Cook the Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until soft and fragrant (about 5 minutes).
  2. Add the Tomatoes and Spices: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, red pepper flakes, salt, pepper, and sugar (if using).
  3. Simmer: Let the sauce simmer on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the flavors meld together. Set aside to cool slightly before using it on your pizza.

Step 3: Prepare the Toppings

Now that your dough is ready, it’s time to get your toppings together.

  1. Cook the Sausage: In a skillet, cook the Italian sausage over medium heat until it’s browned and crumbled. Remove excess fat and set it aside.
  2. Prepare Other Toppings: If you’re using vegetables like mushrooms or bell peppers, sauté them in a little olive oil until they’re tender.

Step 4: Assemble the Deep Dish Pizza

Once the dough has risen, it’s time to assemble your deep dish pizza. This is where you’ll build layers of cheesy goodness and flavorful toppings.

  1. Prepare the Pan: Grease a deep dish or cast-iron skillet (about 12 inches) with olive oil. The heavy pan will help achieve the crispy, golden crust you’re looking for.
  2. Shape the Dough: Punch down the dough and turn it out onto a floured surface. Roll it out into a large circle, about 14-16 inches in diameter. It should be large enough to cover the sides of the pan.
  3. Place the Dough in the Pan: Carefully lift the dough and place it into the greased skillet. Press it gently into the pan and up the sides to form a deep crust. Leave a small overhang around the edges.
  4. Layer the Cheese: Start by layering the shredded mozzarella cheese directly on top of the dough. This forms the foundation of your deep dish pizza and helps prevent the crust from becoming soggy.
  5. Add the Sausage: Sprinkle the cooked sausage evenly over the cheese layer.
  6. Add the Sauce: Spoon the simmered tomato sauce over the sausage, spreading it evenly. Be generous with the sauce as deep dish pizzas require a hearty amount.
  7. Add the Parmesan: Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the sauce for extra flavor.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C) and allow it to fully preheat before baking the pizza.
  2. Bake the Pizza: Place the pizza in the oven and bake for 35-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy, and the sauce is bubbling. You might want to check the pizza after 30 minutes, as oven temperatures can vary.
  3. Let It Rest: Once the pizza is baked, let it rest for about 10-15 minutes before cutting into it. This allows the cheese and sauce to set slightly, making it easier to slice.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

After letting the pizza rest, cut it into generous wedges and serve hot. If desired, you can garnish with fresh basil for a burst of color and aroma. The combination of crispy crust, gooey cheese, savory sausage, and rich tomato sauce is bound to impress your family and friends.

Tips for the Perfect Chicago-Style Deep Dish Pizza

  1. Don’t Skip the Cornmeal: Adding cornmeal to the dough helps create the classic texture of the deep dish crust. It gives the crust a little more bite and a subtle, nutty flavor.
  2. Use Whole Milk Mozzarella: For the best melt and texture, use whole milk mozzarella cheese. It will provide the perfect balance of creaminess and stretch.
  3. Layering is Key: Remember, the order of the layers is important in deep dish pizza. Cheese on the bottom, followed by meat, sauce, and finally Parmesan on top. This ensures the crust stays crispy and the toppings don’t become too soggy.
  4. Let the Pizza Rest: Patience is key! Letting the pizza rest before cutting ensures that all the layers set perfectly and makes it easier to slice.

Conclusion

There you have it—the perfect Chicago-style deep dish pizza recipe! This hearty and satisfying dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. With a golden, crispy crust, a savory filling of mozzarella, sausage, and fresh tomato sauce, this pizza captures all the flavors and textures that make Chicago-style pizza a beloved classic. Whether you’re making it for a special occasion or just a fun weekend meal, this deep dish pizza will undoubtedly impress. Enjoy!
