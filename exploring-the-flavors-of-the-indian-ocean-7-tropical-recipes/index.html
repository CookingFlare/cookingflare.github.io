Exploring the Flavors of the Indian Ocean: 7 Tropical Recipes


The Indian Ocean is home to some of the world’s most vibrant and diverse culinary traditions. Stretching from the shores of East Africa to Southeast Asia, and encompassing jewel-like islands like the Maldives, Mauritius, and Sri Lanka, its cuisines are a tantalizing blend of spices, tropical ingredients, and centuries-old influences from Arabian, Indian, and Southeast Asian cultures. Today, we’re embarking on a flavor-packed journey across this tropical paradise, bringing you seven recipes that capture the essence of the Indian Ocean’s culinary heritage.

1. Maldivian Mas Huni (Tuna Coconut Salad)

Mas Huni is a quintessential Maldivian breakfast dish that perfectly embodies simplicity and bold, fresh flavors. Traditionally served with freshly baked flatbreads called “roshi,” this dish is a celebration of the ocean’s bounty.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh tuna (cooked and flaked)
  • 1 cup grated fresh coconut (or unsweetened desiccated coconut)
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flaked tuna, grated coconut, chopped onion, and green chilies.
  2. Drizzle lime juice over the mixture and season with salt. Mix thoroughly.
  3. Serve with soft roshi flatbreads or warm pita bread for a delightful start to your day.

2. Mauritian Dholl Puri

A street food staple in Mauritius, Dholl Puri is a comforting flatbread stuffed with yellow split peas, traditionally served with spicy chutneys and vegetable curries. It’s a dish that speaks of the island’s Indian roots and love of tropical spices.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup yellow split peas
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the yellow split peas overnight, then boil until soft. Drain and blend into a coarse paste.
  2. Mix the turmeric powder, cumin seeds, and a pinch of salt with the paste.
  3. For the dough, combine flour, salt, and warm water until soft and pliable. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough and filling into equal portions. Roll the dough balls flat, place filling in the center, and seal it completely. Roll gently into a thin circle.
  5. Cook on a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden spots appear.
  6. Pair it with spicy chutneys or a bean curry for a satisfying meal.

3. Sri Lankan Fish Ambul Thiyal (Sour Fish Curry)

This iconic Sri Lankan dish is a must-try for seafood lovers. Known for its bold tamarind flavor, Fish Ambul Thiyal is a dry curry that pairs beautifully with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh fish (like tuna or mackerel), cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons goraka or tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 red onion, finely sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry leaves
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rub the fish chunks with turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Set aside.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium heat and sauté onions, garlic, and curry leaves until aromatic.
  3. Add the tamarind paste and black pepper powder, stirring well.
  4. Gradually add the fish pieces and cook over low heat, ensuring they are coated with the mixture. Add a splash of water if needed.
  5. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until the curry thickens and the fish absorbs the spicy, tangy flavors.

4. Marak Hanfouz (Comorian Coconut Stew)

The Comoros Islands bring us Marak Hanfouz, a rich and flavorful coconut milk stew featuring green bananas, cassava, and tender chicken. It’s the perfect melody of tropical and hearty flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken pieces
  • 2 green bananas, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups cassava, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onions and garlic until golden. Add the turmeric and coriander, cooking for another minute.
  2. Add the chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Stir in the green bananas, cassava, and coconut milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken and vegetables are tender. Serve with steamed rice or crusty bread.

5. Seychellois Ladob (Coconut Dessert)

Ladob is a unique Seychellois dish that can be served as either a dessert or a savory side. Its blend of tropical fruits cooked in sweetened coconut milk is the ideal example of island flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe plantains, sliced
  • 2 medium breadfruit chunks (or sweet potatoes)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add the plantain slices and breadfruit chunks to a pot. Pour in the coconut milk.
  2. Stir in brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Simmer on low heat for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the fruits are soft and the milk thickens into a syrupy glaze.
  4. Serve warm as a comforting dessert or side dish.

6. Zanzibari Zanzibar Mix (Urojo Soup)

From the spice-laden islands of Zanzibar comes Urojo, an aromatic soup formed from coconut, spices, and tangy chutneys. This dish reflects the Swahili culture with its vibrant flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, sauté onions until translucent. Add curry powder and turmeric, stirring well.
  2. Gradually pour in the coconut milk and mashed potatoes, stirring to achieve a creamy consistency.
  3. Season with lime juice and salt. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Serve hot, topped with crispy fritters or boiled eggs for an authentic experience.

7. Malagasy Akoho sy Voanio (Chicken in Coconut Sauce)

A slow-cooked delight from Madagascar, this dish highlights the lush blend of chicken, ginger, and coconut milk, finished with fragrant rice.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • Fresh cilantro, to garnish

Instructions:

  1. Brown the chicken thighs in a skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until aromatic. Add tomato paste and mix well.
  3. Add the chicken back to the skillet and pour in coconut milk.
  4. Cover and let simmer for 30-40 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve over steamed rice.

Wrapping Up

The cuisines of the Indian Ocean offer a symphony of tropical flavors that honor the region’s rich history, lush geography, and multicultural influences. Whether it’s the tangy spice of Sri Lanka, the soothing sweetness of a Seychellois dessert, or the robust flavors of Zanzibar, these recipes transport you to sun-kissed shores with every bite. Why just read about these dishes? Gather the ingredients, step into your kitchen, and let the essence of the Indian Ocean infuse your dining table. Happy cooking!
