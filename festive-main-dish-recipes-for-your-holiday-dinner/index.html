Festive Main Dish Recipes for Your Holiday Dinner


The holiday season is a time of joy, togetherness, and, of course, delicious food. As families and friends gather around the table, the main dish often takes center stage, becoming a highlight of the festive meal. This year, elevate your holiday dinner with these delectable main dish recipes that promise to impress your guests and leave lasting memories.

1. Herb-Crusted Rack of Lamb

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of lamb, frenched
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup fresh breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Prepare the Lamb: Season the racks of lamb generously with salt and pepper. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Sear the lamb for 2-3 minutes on each side until browned.

  3. Make the Herb Crust: In a bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, rosemary, thyme, garlic, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir in a little olive oil until the mixture resembles wet sand.

  4. Coat the Lamb: Brush the tops of the lamb racks with Dijon mustard, then press the herb mixture firmly onto the mustard-coated areas.

  5. Roast the Lamb: Place the lamb in a roasting pan and roast for 20-25 minutes for medium-rare. Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 130°F (54°C). Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

  6. Serve: Slice the lamb between the bones and serve with roasted vegetables and a rich red wine reduction.

2. Maple-Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Whole cloves for studding

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).

  2. Prepare the Ham: Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and stud with whole cloves at the intersections.

  3. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine maple syrup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, cinnamon, and cloves. Bring to a simmer and stir until the sugar dissolves.

  4. Glaze the Ham: Place the ham in a roasting pan and brush with the maple glaze. Reserve some for basting later.

  5. Bake the Ham: Cover loosely with foil and bake for about 15 minutes per pound, basting every 30 minutes with the reserved glaze. Remove the foil for the last 30 minutes to allow the glaze to caramelize.

  6. Serve: Let the ham rest for 20 minutes before slicing. Serve with buttery mashed potatoes and a tangy apple chutney.

3. Stuffed Butternut Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium butternut squashes, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Roast the Squash: Place the butternut squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes until tender.

  3. Cook the Quinoa: In a saucepan, bring vegetable broth to a boil. Add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes or until all the liquid is absorbed.

  4. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, bell pepper, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.

  5. Stuff the Squash: Once the squash is tender, remove from the oven and flip cut-side up. Fill each half with the quinoa mixture.

  6. Bake Again: Return to the oven and bake for an additional 20 minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

4. Beef Wellington

Ingredients:

  • 2 lb beef tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 4 oz pâté (optional)
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef: Season the beef tenderloin with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat and sear the beef on all sides until browned. Let it cool, then brush with Dijon mustard.

  2. Make the Mushroom Duxelles: In the same skillet, add the chopped mushrooms and cook until moisture evaporates. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme.

  3. Assemble the Wellington: Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. Spread the mushroom mixture over the pastry, place the beef in the center, and wrap the pastry around the beef.

  4. Bake the Wellington: Brush the pastry with beaten egg and place it seam-side down on a baking sheet. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.

  5. Serve: Allow the Wellington to rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with rich gravy and seasonal vegetables.

5. Vegan Lentil Shepherd’s Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked green or brown lentils
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 4 cups mashed potatoes (store-bought or homemade)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Cook the Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Stir in garlic, thyme, and rosemary.

  3. Combine with Lentils: Add cooked lentils and vegetable broth to the skillet. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Assemble the Pie: In a baking dish, spread the lentil mixture evenly and top with mashed potatoes, smoothing the surface.

  5. Bake the Shepherd’s Pie: Bake for 25-30 minutes, until the potatoes are golden and the filling is bubbling.

  6. Serve: Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs for a pop of color.

6. Spiced Roast Duck

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole duck (4-5 pounds)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 orange, quartered
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • Fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Duck: Rinse the duck and pat it dry. Season inside and out with salt and pepper. Stuff the cavity with orange, onion, garlic, and herbs.

  3. Make the Glaze: In a small saucepan, combine honey and five-spice powder. Heat until warm.

  4. Roast the Duck: Place the duck on a roasting rack in a pan. Brush with the honey glaze and roast for about 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes.

  5. Crisp the Skin: Increase the oven temperature to 400°F (200°C) for the last 20-30 minutes to crisp the skin.

  6. Serve: Let the duck rest for 15 minutes before carving. Serve with a side of wild rice and seasonal vegetables.

7. Vegetarian Mushroom and Spinach Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the Noodles: Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In a skillet, sauté mushrooms until tender, add spinach, cooking until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.

  4. Assemble the Lasagna: In a baking dish, spread a layer of marinara sauce. Layer noodles, ricotta cheese, vegetable mixture, and mozzarella. Repeat layers, finishing with noodles and sauce, topped with Parmesan.

  5. Bake the Lasagna: Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until bubbly and golden.

  6. Serve: Let it cool for 10 minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh basil for an aromatic finish.

Final Thoughts

These festive main dish recipes are sure to make your holiday dinner unforgettable. Whether you choose the succulent herb-crusted rack of lamb or the heartwarming vegan lentil shepherd’s pie, each dish brings a unique flair to your table. The key is to embrace the spirit of the season, share laughter, and create memories with loved ones over a beautifully prepared meal. Happy cooking, and may your holiday season be filled with joy and delicious food!
