The Best of Both Worlds: Croissant Bread Pudding


If you’re a fan of both croissants and bread pudding, then you’re in for a treat with this delightful fusion of two beloved classics. The croissant bread pudding combines the flakiness of buttery croissants with the rich, custard-like texture of traditional bread pudding, resulting in a decadent dessert that will leave your taste buds begging for more. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create this indulgent delight right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To start off, gather the following ingredients:

  • 6 large croissants, preferably a day old
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Croissants

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Slice the croissants in half lengthwise and arrange them in a single layer on a baking sheet. Toast them in the oven for about 10 minutes, or until they are slightly crisp. Once done, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool.

Step 2: Prepare the Custard Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, eggs, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt until well combined. This will create a luscious custard mixture that will soak into the croissants, infusing them with flavor and moisture.

Step 3: Assemble the Pudding

Grease a baking dish with butter and arrange the toasted croissant halves in the dish. If using raisins and pecans, sprinkle them over the croissants. Then, carefully pour the custard mixture over the croissants, ensuring that each piece is well coated. Gently press down on the croissants to help them absorb the custard.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

Place the baking dish in a larger roasting pan and fill the pan with enough hot water to reach halfway up the sides of the baking dish. This water bath will help ensure a smooth and creamy texture in the finished pudding. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and carefully transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 45 minutes, then uncover the dish and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the pudding is set and the top is golden brown.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the croissant bread pudding is done baking, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, so serve it up while it’s still deliciously fresh from the oven. You can also add a dollop of whipped cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce for an extra touch of indulgence.

Conclusion

The croissant bread pudding offers a delightful twist on the classic bread pudding, elevating it to a whole new level of luxury and flavor. Whether you serve it as a decadent dessert or a sumptuous brunch dish, this recipe is sure to impress your family and friends. With its buttery croissants, creamy custard, and optional add-ins like raisins and pecans, this dessert truly embodies the best of both worlds. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor every blissful bite of this irresistible croissant bread pudding.
