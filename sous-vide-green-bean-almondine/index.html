Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine


When it comes to cooking vegetables, it’s easy to fall into the trap of boiling or steaming them, leaving them bland and lackluster. However, one of the most effective ways to elevate a simple vegetable like green beans is by using the sous vide method. Sous vide cooking ensures that the beans remain tender with just the right amount of crispness while maintaining all their natural flavors. Combine that with the rich, nutty taste of toasted almonds, and you have a side dish that’s both sophisticated and easy to make. Welcome to the world of Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine — a dish that’s as flavorful as it is visually appealing.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, French for “under vacuum,” is a method of cooking where food is placed in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooked slowly in a water bath at a precise, controlled temperature. This method is known for preserving the natural flavors and textures of food, resulting in a more consistent and flavorful dish. Unlike traditional cooking methods, sous vide guarantees that the food is cooked evenly throughout, ensuring it is never overcooked.

Green beans, in particular, benefit from sous vide cooking. The low temperature helps to maintain their fresh, crisp texture while avoiding the sogginess that can occur from boiling. With the right seasoning and a little bit of patience, sous vide green beans turn into an extraordinary side dish that’s bursting with flavor.

Why Green Bean Almondine?

Green beans almondine (also known as “almondine”) is a classic French dish that features green beans cooked with butter, garlic, and toasted almonds. This dish is often served as a side to accompany meats like chicken, steak, or fish, adding a touch of elegance and a burst of flavor to the meal. The combination of the tender green beans, crunchy almonds, and rich butter is simply irresistible.

Sous vide cooking takes the traditional recipe to new heights by enhancing the natural flavors of the green beans while maintaining their perfect texture. Additionally, cooking the green beans sous vide allows you to control the temperature, ensuring that the beans stay crisp-tender without being overcooked. By pairing them with the toasted almonds and butter, you’re able to create a side dish that’s both refined and comforting.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you begin, gather the following ingredients for your Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine:

  • 1 pound of fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Vacuum-seal bags or resealable freezer bags (for sous vide)

Pro Tip: Make sure to choose fresh, high-quality green beans for the best results. Look for beans that are firm, crisp, and have a vibrant green color.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cooking Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine

Step 1: Prepare the Green Beans

Start by trimming the ends of the green beans. You can use a sharp knife or kitchen shears to cut off the tough ends. Make sure to give the beans a quick rinse to remove any dirt or debris. After that, dry them thoroughly with a paper towel.

Step 2: Set Up the Sous Vide Bath

Fill a large container with water and set up your sous vide precision cooker. You’ll want to set the temperature to 185°F (85°C) for green beans. This temperature is ideal for ensuring they are perfectly tender while retaining a slight crispness.

Once the water bath reaches the desired temperature, you can begin the next step.

Step 3: Vacuum Seal the Green Beans

Take your trimmed green beans and place them in a vacuum-seal bag. Ensure that the bag is big enough to hold the beans without crowding them. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can also use the water displacement method with a resealable freezer bag — simply seal the bag almost all the way, then slowly lower it into the water bath, letting the pressure push the air out. Seal the bag completely before immersing it in the water.

Step 4: Cook the Green Beans Sous Vide

Submerge the vacuum-sealed bag of green beans into the preheated water bath. Let the beans cook for 45 minutes. This time allows the beans to cook through while preserving their vibrant color and texture.

Step 5: Toast the Almonds

While the beans are cooking, it’s time to prepare the almond topping. Heat a skillet over medium heat and add the sliced almonds. Toast them lightly, stirring frequently, until they become golden brown and fragrant. Be careful not to burn them, as almonds can quickly go from golden to charred. This should only take 2-3 minutes. Remove the almonds from the skillet and set them aside.

Step 6: Sauté Garlic in Butter

Next, melt the unsalted butter in the same skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until it becomes fragrant. Be sure to stir frequently to avoid burning the garlic. Once the garlic is lightly golden, turn off the heat and set the skillet aside.

Step 7: Combine the Beans with Butter and Garlic

Once the green beans have finished cooking in the sous vide bath, carefully remove them from the bag. You can use tongs to lift the beans out, and be sure to discard any excess water from the bag.

Now, add the cooked green beans to the skillet with the garlic butter. Toss the beans gently to coat them with the butter and garlic mixture. If you prefer a bit of acidity, drizzle the beans with a teaspoon of fresh lemon juice. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 8: Garnish with Toasted Almonds and Serve

Once the green beans are well-coated in butter and garlic, sprinkle the toasted almonds over the top. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and a fresh, herbal flavor. The final touch adds both texture and visual appeal to your dish.

Step 9: Enjoy!

Your Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine is now ready to serve! Pair it with your favorite main dish, or enjoy it on its own as a light, healthy meal. The tender, crisp green beans with the buttery garlic sauce and toasted almonds will surely impress any dinner guest. It’s a simple yet elegant side that’s perfect for weeknight dinners, holiday meals, or special occasions.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Green Beans Almondine

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Bag: When vacuum-sealing the green beans, avoid overcrowding the bag. If necessary, cook the beans in batches to ensure even cooking.
  • Adjust the Seasoning: Taste the green beans after cooking and adjust the salt and pepper as needed. You can also experiment with adding a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes for a little heat or a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese for added richness.
  • Make It Ahead: Sous vide cooking is perfect for meal prep! You can cook the green beans in advance and store them in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. When ready to serve, simply reheat them in a pan with the garlic butter and almonds.
  • Vary the Nuts: While almonds are classic for this dish, you can experiment with other nuts, such as pecans, walnuts, or hazelnuts, for a unique twist.

Why Sous Vide Cooking is Ideal for Vegetables

Sous vide cooking is especially ideal for vegetables like green beans because it allows for precise temperature control. Vegetables are delicate and can easily be overcooked when using traditional methods, but sous vide cooking eliminates this risk. The beans will be cooked evenly and retain their perfect texture, unlike boiling or steaming, which can lead to mushy, waterlogged vegetables.

Another great benefit of sous vide cooking is the ability to infuse flavors directly into the food. By sealing the beans with seasonings and butter in the bag, the flavors become concentrated and penetrated deeply into the vegetables, ensuring every bite is flavorful.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Green Bean Almondine is a delightful and sophisticated dish that elevates the humble green bean to a whole new level. With its tender texture, vibrant color, and the richness of garlic butter and toasted almonds, this side dish is perfect for any occasion, from a casual family dinner to an elegant holiday spread. By using sous vide cooking, you’ll ensure that your green beans remain perfectly crisp and flavorful every time.

So, whether you’re a seasoned sous vide pro or just starting out, this recipe is a great way to showcase the versatility of sous vide cooking while enjoying a delicious, healthy side dish. Give it a try, and you’ll be hooked on the magic of sous vide green beans almondine!
