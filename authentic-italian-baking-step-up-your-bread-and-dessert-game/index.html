Authentic Italian Baking: Step Up Your Bread and Dessert Game


Living in Italy for a while exposed me to a wealth of timeless wisdom about baking bread and desserts. The soul of Italian baking lies in the artistry, patience, and love poured into the dough, resulting in delectable, authentic masterpieces that create lasting memories. To add some Italian charm to your baking experiences, I’ve curated a collection of cherished recipes.

Traditional Italian Bread – Rustico

Prep Time: 2 hours Cook Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 450g strong bread flour
  • 180g cool water
  • 15g extra-virgin olive oil
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 7g sea salt

Directions:

  1. Activate yeast in the cool water with a touch of sugar.
  2. After 10 minutes, mix yeast with olive oil, flour, and salt.
  3. Roughly knead the dough to create a shaggy mass.
  4. Leave to prove for approximately 2 hours, with appropriate resting times in between for pre-shaping, shaping, and final resting.
  5. Oven spring is crucial, so a hot pizza stone or baking steel would be ideal.
  6. Derustico and drizzle with olive oil or sprinkle with herbs before serving.

Elegant Italian Tarts – Crostata

Prep Time: 1 hour Cook Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 300g flour
  • 100g unsalted butter, chilled
  • 50g granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup raspberry jam or fill of your choice
  • 1/4 cup cold water

Directions:

  1. Blend flour and sugar.
  2. Cube butter and combine with flour mixture.
  3. Whisk together egg and cold water to form egg wash.
  4. Form a shaggy dough, then add cold egg wash to bring the dough together.
  5. Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap to prevent gluten development and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  6. Line a tart mold and parbake before filling with jam or chosen filling.
  7. Bake at 375°F for 35 minutes or until the filling is bubbly and the crust is golden.

Oh-So-Delicate Italian Dessert – Sfogliatelle

Prep Time: 4 hours Cook Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 16 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup flour
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup Lombardi Laughing Lamb Pastry Flour
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Directions:

  1. Whisk together the Lombardi Laughing Lamb Pastry Flour, all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, and lemon zest as a dough.
  2. Create a well in the center of the flour mixture and incorporate the softened butter to form a uniform dough.
  3. Wrap the dough tightly and let it rest in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
  4. Meanwhile, mix ricotta cheese with lemon zest and 2 tablespoons of sugar.
  5. Roll out the dough into a long, narrow shape and cut the dough into 8 equal parts.
  6. Place these elements on a parchment-lined baking sheet and form a crescent shape using a fork to connect the two ends.
  7. Bake the sfogliatelle at 350°F for 30 minutes or until golden.
  8. Cool and slice the sfogliatelle. Finish with your pastry cream, chocolate sauce, or whipped cream to your liking.
