Scrumptious Easter Desserts: Sweet Treats to End Your Meal


Easter is a time for celebration, family, and friends. What’s a better way to cap off a delicious meal than with a scrumptious dessert? In this post, we’ll explore some mouth-watering Easter dessert recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

Lemon Lavender Panna Cotta

Panna cotta is an Italian dessert made from sweetened cream that’s set with gelatin. This Easter, give it a spring twist by infusing it with the brightness of lemon and the subtlety of lavender.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup dried lavender buds
  • 2 lemons, zested and juiced
  • 1 cup unflavored gelatin

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cream, milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  2. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it steep for 10 minutes with the lavender buds. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the lavender.
  3. Whisk in the vanilla extract and lemon zest. Let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  4. Pour the mixture into individual serving cups or ramekins. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight until set.
  5. Just before serving, drizzle with lemon juice and garnish with fresh lavender buds, if desired.

Chocolate-Dipped Shortbread Cookies

Shortbread cookies are a classic Easter treat that can be dressed up with a chocolate dip. These buttery cookies are easy to make and are sure to be a hit with both kids and adults.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 300째F (150째C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt.
  3. Add the softened butter and granulated sugar to the bowl. Use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Press the mixture into a disk and wrap in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/4 inch. Cut into desired shapes using a cookie cutter.
  6. Place the cookies on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  7. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  9. Dip the cooled cookies into the melted chocolate, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the dipped cookies on a piece of parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  10. Dust with confectioners’ sugar and serve.

Spring Floral Cake

This show-stopping cake is perfect for Easter brunch or dinner. The combination of flavors and textures is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped fresh flowers (such as violas, pansies, or nasturtiums)
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350째F (175째C). Grease and flour three 8-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the softened butter and milk until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition.
  4. Whisk in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, whisking until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chopped fresh flowers.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared pans and smooth the tops.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of each cake comes out clean.
  9. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Once the cakes are cool, you can frost and decorate them as desired.

Carrot Cake Truffles

These bite-sized truffles are a playful twist on traditional carrot cake. They’re easy to make and are perfect for Easter dessert tables or as a sweet treat for kids.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the grated carrots, chopped walnuts, and granulated sugar.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the softened butter and eggs until smooth. Add the vanilla extract and whisk until combined.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, whisking until just combined.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  5. Use a small cookie scoop or your hands to form the mixture into balls, about 1 inch in diameter. Place the balls on a piece of parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  6. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  7. Dip each ball into the melted chocolate, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the dipped balls on a piece of parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  8. Dust with confectioners’ sugar and serve.

These Easter desserts are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth. Whether you’re looking for a classic dessert or something new and exciting, these recipes are sure to be a hit. Happy Easter!
