Coconut Curry Fish and Vegetables


There’s something truly magical about the combination of creamy coconut milk, aromatic curry spices, tender fish, and colorful vegetables. This Coconut Curry Fish and Vegetables recipe is a delightful fusion of flavors that will transport your taste buds to a tropical paradise. Whether you’re a seafood lover, a curry enthusiast, or simply looking to try something new, this dish is sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb firm white fish fillets (such as cod or tilapia), cut into chunks
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan bread for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fish: Season the fish chunks with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet or pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant.

  3. Add the Spices: Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, and cumin, and cook for another minute to toast the spices and release their flavors.

  4. Create the Curry Base: Pour in the coconut milk, stirring well to combine with the spices. Let the mixture simmer for a few minutes to infuse the flavors.

  5. Cook the Vegetables: Add the sliced red bell pepper, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes to the curry base. Cook until the vegetables are slightly softened but still vibrant.

  6. Add the Fish: Gently place the seasoned fish chunks into the simmering curry. Allow the fish to cook for about 5-7 minutes or until it is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.

  7. Season to Perfection: Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Remember, the flavors should be well-balanced.

  8. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle fresh cilantro over the Coconut Curry Fish and Vegetables for a burst of freshness. Serve hot over cooked rice or with warm naan bread on the side.

Tips for Success:

  • Use fresh fish for the best flavor and texture.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables based on your preferences or what’s in season.
  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of chili flakes or fresh chopped chili peppers.
  • Make a larger batch and store leftovers in the refrigerator for a delicious meal the next day.
