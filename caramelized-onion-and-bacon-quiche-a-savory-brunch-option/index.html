Caramelized Onion and Bacon Quiche: A Savory Brunch Option

4 min · 689 words

When it comes to brunch, a quiche is a versatile and delicious option that never fails to impress. The combination of caramelized onions and crispy bacon in a savory custard filling, all nestled in a buttery crust, makes for a delightful dish that is perfect for any mid-morning gathering. Whether you’re hosting a brunch with friends or simply looking to elevate your weekend breakfast, this caramelized onion and bacon quiche is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  3. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Caramelize the Onions

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are soft and golden brown, about 25-30 minutes. Set aside to cool.

Step 3: Cook the Bacon

  1. In the same skillet, cook the chopped bacon until it is browned and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the bacon to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.

Step 4: Prebake the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Press the dough into the dish and trim any excess. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork, then line it with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans.
  3. Bake the crust for 15 minutes. Remove the parchment paper and weights, then continue baking for another 10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Set aside to cool.

Step 5: Assemble the Quiche

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, milk, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.
  2. Sprinkle half of the shredded Gruyere cheese over the prebaked crust. Top with the caramelized onions and cooked bacon, then pour the egg mixture over the filling. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top.

Step 6: Bake the Quiche

  1. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.
  3. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This caramelized onion and bacon quiche pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette. For a complete brunch spread, consider serving it alongside fresh fruit, breakfast potatoes, and mimosas or freshly squeezed orange juice.

Quiche is a make-ahead friendly dish, so you can prepare it the day before and simply reheat it in the oven before serving. The flavors meld together beautifully, making it even more delicious the next day.

Conclusion

The combination of sweet, caramelized onions and smoky bacon in a rich, custardy filling makes this quiche a standout dish for any brunch occasion. Whether you’re a fan of savory breakfasts or you simply enjoy a good quiche, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your brunch repertoire. Impress your guests or treat yourself to a delightful morning indulgence with this caramelized onion and bacon quiche.

I hope you find this recipe for caramelized onion and bacon quiche inspiring and easy to follow. It’s a perfect addition to any brunch or weekend breakfast. Enjoy!
