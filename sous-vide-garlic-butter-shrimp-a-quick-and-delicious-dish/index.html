Sous Vide Garlic Butter Shrimp: A Quick and Delicious Dish


If you’re a seafood lover, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe. Sous vide garlic butter shrimp is a dish that combines the delicate flavors of shrimp with the rich and savory taste of garlic butter. With the help of sous vide cooking, you can achieve perfectly cooked, tender shrimp every time. So let’s dive in and discover how to create this quick and delicious dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C).
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Season the shrimp with salt and pepper, ensuring both sides are coated evenly.
  4. Place the seasoned shrimp in a single layer in a ziplock bag or vacuum-sealed bag. Add the garlic butter mixture and olive oil to the bag.
  5. Seal the bag using the water displacement method or a vacuum sealer, ensuring all the air is removed.
  6. Gently place the bag of shrimp into the preheated water bath and cook for 30 minutes.
  7. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and transfer the shrimp to a plate. Discard the garlic butter mixture.
  8. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of olive oil and swirl to coat the pan.
  9. Once the pan is hot, add the shrimp in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side until the shrimp turns pink and slightly opaque.
  10. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and transfer to a serving dish.
  11. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide garlic butter shrimp makes a fantastic appetizer or main course. Serve it with a side of lemon wedges for a refreshing citrus kick. You can also pair it with a light salad or steamed vegetables to create a well-balanced meal. Additionally, this dish goes well with rice or pasta, allowing you to customize it to your taste.

Why Sous Vide?

Sous vide cooking is a technique that involves cooking food in a temperature-controlled water bath. This method ensures precise and consistent results, as the food is cooked at a specific temperature for an extended period. When it comes to shrimp, sous vide cooking helps to retain the natural juiciness and tenderness of the seafood, resulting in perfectly cooked shrimp every time.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide garlic butter shrimp is a dish that combines simplicity and elegance. With just a handful of ingredients and the help of sous vide cooking, you can create a dish that will impress your family and friends. The tender shrimp, infused with the rich flavors of garlic butter, will undoubtedly become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So give this recipe a try and elevate your seafood cooking game to new heights!

Enjoy, and happy cooking!
