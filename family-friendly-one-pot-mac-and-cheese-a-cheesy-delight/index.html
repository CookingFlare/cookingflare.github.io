Family-Friendly One-Pot Mac and Cheese: A Cheesy Delight


Who doesn’t love a warm, gooey bowl of mac and cheese? It’s the ultimate comfort food that brings a smile to everyone’s face, regardless of age. And what’s even better? Making it in just one pot, saving you time and effort in the kitchen. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to create a family-friendly one-pot mac and cheese that is not only easy to make but also incredibly delicious. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups milk
  • 4 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk until smooth, creating a roux. Cook the roux for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid burning.

  3. Slowly pour in the milk, whisking continuously to combine with the roux. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.

  4. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, and mustard powder. Stir to incorporate the flavors into the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and gradually add the shredded cheddar cheese, mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Stir until the cheeses have melted and the sauce is smooth and creamy.

  6. Add the cooked macaroni back into the pot with the cheese sauce. Stir well to coat the pasta evenly.

  7. Cover the pot and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. This will also help the mac and cheese thicken up slightly.

  8. Serve the one-pot mac and cheese hot, garnished with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese or chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, try adding crispy bacon or diced jalapenos to the mac and cheese before serving.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese to customize the flavor. Gouda, fontina, or even blue cheese can add a unique twist to your mac and cheese.
  • If you prefer a breadcrumb topping, transfer the mac and cheese to a baking dish, sprinkle breadcrumbs over the top, and broil until golden brown and crispy.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it up in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a splash of milk to bring back the creaminess.

Conclusion

There you have it, a simple yet incredibly tasty one-pot mac and cheese recipe that will leave your family asking for seconds. With just a handful of ingredients and a single pot, you can create a dish that is sure to become a family favorite. So why not ditch the boxed mac and cheese and give this homemade version a try? Trust me, once you taste the creamy, cheesy goodness, you’ll never look back. Enjoy!
