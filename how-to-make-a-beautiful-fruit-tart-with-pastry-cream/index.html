How to Make a Beautiful Fruit Tart with Pastry Cream


Creating a stunning fruit tart filled with luscious pastry cream is a delightful way to showcase fresh seasonal fruits and impress your family and friends. Whether you’re preparing for a special event or simply indulging in a personal treat, this recipe will guide you through each step to achieve a beautiful and delicious fruit tart that is sure to be the centerpiece of any dessert table.

Ingredients

For the Tart Shell

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons cold water

For the Pastry Cream

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Fruit Topping

  • 2 cups fresh mixed fruits (strawberries, blueberries, kiwi, raspberries, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup apricot jam (optional, for glaze)
  • 1 tablespoon water (if using apricot jam)

Equipment Needed

  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Saucepan
  • Fine mesh sieve
  • Tart pan (9-inch)
  • Rolling pin
  • Plastic wrap
  • Parchment paper
  • Baking weights or dried beans

Step 1: Making the Tart Shell

1. Prepare the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, powdered sugar, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter. Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial as it creates the flaky texture of the tart shell.

2. Add Egg Yolk

In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolk with 2 tablespoons of cold water. Gradually add this mixture to the flour mixture, stirring until the dough starts to come together. If the dough is too crumbly, add an additional tablespoon of cold water.

3. Chill the Dough

Form the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This chilling period allows the gluten to relax, resulting in a tender crust.

4. Roll Out the Dough

Once chilled, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly flour your work surface and rolling pin. Roll out the dough to about 1/8-inch thickness. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan, pressing it into the edges and trimming any excess.

5. Blind Bake the Tart Shell

Line the tart shell with parchment paper and fill it with baking weights or dried beans to prevent it from puffing up. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper, and bake for an additional 10-12 minutes or until lightly golden. Allow the tart shell to cool completely.

Step 2: Making the Pastry Cream

1. Heat the Milk

In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it begins to steam, but do not let it boil.

2. Whisk Egg Yolks and Sugar

In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and granulated sugar until the mixture is pale and creamy. Add the cornstarch and mix until well combined.

3. Temper the Egg Mixture

Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking continuously to prevent the eggs from scrambling. Once combined, return the mixture to the saucepan.

4. Cook the Pastry Cream

Cook the mixture over medium heat, whisking constantly, until it thickens and begins to bubble. Once thickened, remove from heat and stir in the butter and vanilla extract.

5. Strain the Cream

For an ultra-smooth pastry cream, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Cover the surface with plastic wrap, pressing it directly onto the cream to prevent a skin from forming. Allow it to cool to room temperature before refrigerating it until ready to use.

Step 3: Assembling the Fruit Tart

1. Fill the Tart Shell

Once the pastry cream is chilled and thickened, spoon it into the cooled tart shell. Spread it evenly using a spatula, ensuring a smooth finish.

2. Arrange the Fruit

Wash and slice your fresh fruits as desired. Begin by placing larger pieces, like kiwi slices or strawberries, around the edges, and fill in the center with smaller fruits like blueberries and raspberries. Get creative with your arrangement to make it visually appealing.

3. Glaze the Tart (Optional)

If you desire a shiny finish, melt the apricot jam with a tablespoon of water over low heat. Once melted, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any fruit pieces. Brush the glaze over the arranged fruit to give it a beautiful sheen.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Chill the tart for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the pastry cream to set further. When ready to serve, cut slices with a sharp knife and enjoy the symphony of flavors and textures. The buttery tart shell, creamy pastry filling, and fresh fruit topping create a delightful dessert that is as pleasing to the palate as it is to the eye.

Tips for a Perfect Fruit Tart

  • Choose Seasonal Fruits: For the freshest and most flavorful tart, use fruits that are in season. This not only enhances taste but also adds vibrant colors to your tart.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to incorporate different flavorings into your pastry cream, such as almond extract or citrus zest, to customize your tart.
  • Storage: Store any leftover tart in the refrigerator, covered, for up to 2-3 days. However, keep in mind that the crust may become slightly softer over time.

Conclusion

A beautiful fruit tart with pastry cream is a delightful dessert that can elevate any occasion. With its flaky crust filled with velvety pastry cream and topped with an array of colorful fruits, it’s not just a feast for the taste buds but also a visual treat. Follow this comprehensive guide to create your own masterpiece, and enjoy the compliments that come your way. Happy baking!
