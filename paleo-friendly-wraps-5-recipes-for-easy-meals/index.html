Paleo-Friendly Wraps: 5 Recipes for Easy Meals


When it comes to eating paleo, finding creative ways to enjoy delicious meals while staying true to the principles of this diet can sometimes feel challenging. Wraps are an easy, versatile way to create meals that are both satisfying and portable, perfect for lunchboxes, picnics, or even quick dinners. By using wholesome paleo-friendly ingredients, you can indulge in flavorful wraps that are grain-free, nutrient-dense, and packed with delicious, healthy fillings.

In this post, we’ll share five paleo-friendly wrap recipes that strike the perfect balance between taste and simplicity. Each wrap utilizes unique bases and fillings, allowing you to mix up your meal routine and discover new combinations. Let’s dive into these delightful and wholesome options!

1. Crunchy Veggie Collard Wraps with Avocado-Lime Dressing

Collard greens are perfect for paleo wraps. Their sturdy leaves hold up well to fillings and provide a boost of nutrients. This refreshing wrap is packed with crisp vegetables and drizzled with a creamy avocado-lime dressing for a zing of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large collard green leaves (stem removed)
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup thinly sliced red bell peppers
  • 1 cup thinly sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves

For the avocado-lime dressing:

  • 1/2 avocado
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blend all avocado-lime dressing ingredients in a food processor until smooth.
  2. Lay out the collard green leaves on a flat surface. Spread a spoonful of the dressing onto each leaf.
  3. Layer the vegetables and sliced avocado in the center of the leaves.
  4. Fold the sides of the leaves over the filling, then roll tightly to form a wrap. Slice in half if desired and enjoy fresh!

Pro Tip: To make the collard leaves more pliable, blanch them briefly in hot water and pat dry before use.

2. Coconut Wraps with Thai-Inspired Chicken Filling

Coconut wraps are an excellent grain-free alternative and bring a subtle sweetness to your meal. This Thai-inspired chicken wrap will take your taste buds on a flavorful journey.

Ingredients:

  • 4 coconut wraps
  • 1 cup cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • 1/2 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons almond butter
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • Juice of 1 lime

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix almond butter, coconut aminos, sesame oil, ginger, and lime juice until smooth to create a savory sauce.
  2. Combine the chicken, cabbage, carrots, and green onions in a separate bowl. Pour the sauce over the mixture and toss to coat.
  3. Place a scoop of the filling in the center of each coconut wrap. Fold and roll to create a secure wrap. Serve immediately or pack for later.

Pro Tip: For extra protein, top the wraps with crushed cashews or sesame seeds.

3. Sweet Potato Tortilla Wraps with Spiced Ground Beef

Sweet potatoes are a paleo staple that can be transformed into soft tortillas, making them the ultimate base for a flavorful wrap. This recipe pairs them with spiced beef for a savory, satisfying meal.

Ingredients for sweet potato tortillas:

  • 1 medium sweet potato, cooked and mashed
  • 1/4 cup tapioca flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • A pinch of salt

For the filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • Lettuce leaves or spinach for extra greens

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the mashed sweet potato, tapioca flour, almond flour, and salt. Mix until a dough forms.
  2. Divide the dough into small portions; roll them out into thin rounds. Cook each tortilla in a hot, dry skillet for 1-2 minutes per side until pliable.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground beef with spices until browned. Add diced tomatoes and simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Assemble the wraps by layering the sweet potato tortilla with beef filling and optional greens. Roll and serve.

Pro Tip: Make extra tortillas and store them in the fridge for a quick meal option throughout the week.

4. Egg Wrap Breakfast Burritos

For a protein-packed breakfast or brunch option, egg wraps are unbeatable. They serve as the perfect base for savory breakfast burritos without the need for traditional grain-based tortillas.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • Coconut oil for cooking
  • 1/2 cup sautéed spinach
  • 1/2 cup cooked and crumbled sausage (sugar-free)
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • Salsa for topping

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together eggs and almond milk until frothy.
  2. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with coconut oil. Pour a small amount of the egg mixture into the skillet, swirling to create a thin, even layer. Cook for 1-2 minutes, then carefully flip and cook the other side. Repeat for remaining batter.
  3. Fill each egg wrap with sautéed spinach, sausage, and avocado slices. Add a spoonful of salsa for extra flavor. Fold and enjoy.

Pro Tip: These egg wraps can be made in advance and stored in the fridge for up to two days.

5. Zucchini Ribbon Wraps with Tuna Salad

Zucchini ribbons are a fresh, low-carb alternative to traditional wraps. Paired with a light and tangy tuna salad, they create a refreshing, no-cook meal option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis (sliced lengthwise into thin ribbons using a mandoline or peeler)
  • 1 can of tuna, drained
  • 2 tablespoons paleo mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (check ingredient list for compliance)
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • 1/4 cup diced celery
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine tuna, mayo, mustard, capers, celery, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well until fully combined.
  2. Lay the zucchini ribbons flat, slightly overlapping them to form a wider base.
  3. Place a scoop of tuna salad on one end of the zucchini ribbons. Gently roll them up into wraps.
  4. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve immediately.

Pro Tip: If you’re making these wraps ahead of time, pat the zucchini ribbons dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.

Wrapping It Up (Pun Intended!)

These paleo-friendly wraps are perfect examples of how eating clean and grain-free doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or variety. From nutrient-rich collard greens to creative egg and sweet potato tortillas, these wraps show the versatility of paleo-inspired ingredients. Whether you need a quick lunch, a wholesome snack, or an easy dinner, these recipes have you covered.

Experiment with these recipes and feel free to swap out fillings to suit your preferences. Whether you’re a longtime paleo devotee or new to clean eating, these wraps are bound to inspire your next meal prep session. Happy wrapping, and enjoy every bite!
