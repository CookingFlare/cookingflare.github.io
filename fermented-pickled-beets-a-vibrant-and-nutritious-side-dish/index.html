Fermented Pickled Beets: A Vibrant and Nutritious Side Dish


Fermented pickled beets are not just a colorful addition to your plate; they are a powerhouse of nutrition, flavor, and gut health benefits. This vibrant dish combines the earthy sweetness of beets with the tangy, complex flavors brought about by fermentation. Whether you’re a seasoned fermenter or a curious beginner, this guide will walk you through the art of making your own fermented pickled beets at home.

Why Ferment Beets?

Fermenting vegetables is an ancient preservation technique that enhances both flavor and nutritional value. When beets are fermented, beneficial bacteria called probiotics thrive, promoting a healthy gut microbiome. These tiny allies help with digestion, boost your immune system, and may even improve your mood. Moreover, the fermentation process increases the bioavailability of certain nutrients, making them easier for your body to absorb.

Nutritional Benefits of Beets

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the nutritional benefits of beets. This vibrant root vegetable is rich in:

  • Vitamins: Beets are a great source of vitamin C, which is essential for immune function, and folate, crucial for DNA synthesis and repair.
  • Minerals: They provide significant amounts of potassium, which is important for heart health, and manganese, which supports metabolism.
  • Antioxidants: Beets are packed with betalains, powerful antioxidants that help fight inflammation and oxidative stress in the body.
  • Dietary Fiber: High in fiber, beets promote digestive health and help maintain a healthy weight.

With such a robust nutritional profile, it’s no wonder that fermented pickled beets are a fantastic side dish to include in your meals.

Ingredients for Fermented Pickled Beets

To make your own fermented pickled beets, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized beets (organic if possible)
  • 2 cups filtered water
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt (non-iodized)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (organic cane sugar or honey)
  • 1-2 cloves garlic (optional, for added flavor)
  • A few sprigs of fresh dill or thyme (optional)
  • A clean glass jar with a lid (1 quart size works well)

Equipment Needed

  • A pot for boiling water
  • A mixing bowl
  • A whisk or spoon
  • A cutting board and knife
  • A peeler (optional)
  • A fermentation weight or a smaller jar (to keep the beets submerged)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Fermented Pickled Beets

Step 1: Prepare the Beets

Start by washing the beets thoroughly to remove any dirt. You can peel them if you prefer a milder flavor, but keeping the skin on will add extra nutrients. Cut the beets into thin slices or cubes, depending on your preference. The smaller the pieces, the faster they will ferment.

Step 2: Prepare the Brine

In a pot, combine the filtered water, sea salt, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, stirring to dissolve the salt and sugar completely. Once dissolved, remove the pot from heat and allow the brine to cool to room temperature.

Step 3: Pack the Jar

In a clean glass jar, layer the sliced or cubed beets. If you’re incorporating garlic and herbs, add them between the layers of beets for an even distribution of flavor. Pack the beets tightly but not so tightly that they get squished.

Step 4: Add the Brine

Once the brine has cooled, pour it over the beets in the jar. Ensure that the beets are completely submerged in the brine. If necessary, use a fermentation weight or a smaller jar to keep the beets below the liquid. This step is important to prevent mold from forming on the surface.

Step 5: Ferment the Beets

Seal the jar with a lid, but don’t screw it on too tightly. Fermentation produces gases, and you want to allow some of these gases to escape. Place the jar in a cool, dark spot in your kitchen, ideally between 60°F and 75°F (15°C - 24°C).

Step 6: Monitor the Fermentation Process

Let the beets ferment for about 5 to 10 days. The exact time will depend on your taste preference and the ambient temperature. Check the beets daily, and if you see any bubbles or a frothy surface, that’s a good sign that fermentation is happening. Taste the beets after about five days; they should have a tangy flavor and a pleasing crunch.

Step 7: Store the Fermented Beets

Once the beets have reached your desired level of fermentation, remove the weight if you used one and seal the jar tightly. Transfer the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. The cold will help preserve the flavor and crunchiness of the beets.

Tips for Success

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add other spices or flavorings to your pickled beets, such as mustard seeds, coriander seeds, or even a splash of apple cider vinegar for added tang.
  • Taste as You Go: Fermentation is a personal journey. Taste your beets every couple of days to find the perfect balance of tanginess that you enjoy.
  • Be Patient: The longer you let the beets ferment, the stronger their flavor will become. Don’t rush the process; good things take time.
  • Use High-Quality Ingredients: The quality of your beets and salt will significantly impact the final product. Opt for organic ingredients whenever possible.

Ways to Enjoy Fermented Pickled Beets

Now that you’ve made your own fermented pickled beets, it’s time to enjoy them! Here are some delicious ways to incorporate this nutritious side dish into your meals:

1. As a Side Dish

Serve fermented pickled beets alongside grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or fish. Their tangy flavor provides a perfect contrast to rich dishes, enhancing your meal with a refreshing crunch.

2. In Salads

Add pickled beets to your salads for a vibrant splash of color and flavor. They pair wonderfully with goat cheese, walnuts, and mixed greens. Drizzle a light vinaigrette over the top for a delightful combination.

3. On Sandwiches and Wraps

Enhance your sandwiches or wraps with a layer of fermented pickled beets. Their acidity cuts through creamy spreads like hummus or avocado, adding depth and complexity.

4. As a Topping for Bowls

Top grain bowls or buddha bowls with fermented beets for a gorgeous pop of color. Combine them with quinoa, roasted vegetables, and protein of your choice for a satisfying meal.

5. In Smoothies

For the adventurous, consider blending a small amount of pickled beets into your smoothies. Their sweetness and vibrant color can add a unique twist to your usual recipes.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled beets are more than just a side dish; they are a celebration of flavors, colors, and health benefits. By making your own, you not only gain control over the ingredients but also engage in a rewarding culinary experience. These tangy, vibrant beets can brighten up any meal while providing a wealth of nutrients that support your overall health.

So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on your fermentation journey. With a little patience and creativity, you’ll have a delicious and nutritious side dish that will impress family and friends alike. Embrace the vibrant world of fermented pickled beets and enjoy the many ways they can enhance your culinary adventures!
