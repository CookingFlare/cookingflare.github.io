Irresistible Cinnamon Sugar Donut Holes: A Bite-Sized Treat


If you’re a fan of sweet treats, then these irresistible cinnamon sugar donut holes are a must-try. These bite-sized delights are perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy morning at home or a gathering with friends. With their warm, fluffy centers and sweet cinnamon sugar coating, these donut holes are guaranteed to satisfy your cravings. So, without further ado, let’s dive into the recipe!

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make these delectable cinnamon sugar donut holes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar, for coating
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon, for coating

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, salt, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. Make sure all the dry ingredients are well combined.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough donuts.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Use a candy thermometer to ensure the oil reaches the correct temperature.

  4. While the oil is heating, prepare the cinnamon sugar coating. In a shallow bowl, combine the granulated sugar and ground cinnamon. Mix well until the cinnamon is evenly distributed throughout the sugar.

  5. Once the oil is hot, use a small cookie scoop or spoon to drop small portions of the donut dough into the hot oil. Fry the donut holes for about 2-3 minutes, or until they turn golden brown. Make sure to fry them in batches, so they don’t overcrowd the pot.

  6. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the fried donut holes from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  7. While the donut holes are still warm, roll them in the cinnamon sugar coating until they are fully coated. You can do this by placing a few donut holes at a time in the cinnamon sugar mixture and gently tossing them around until they are evenly coated.

  8. Repeat the frying and coating process with the remaining donut holes until all the dough is used.

  9. Once all the donut holes are coated, they are ready to be enjoyed! Serve them warm for the best experience.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a different coating, you can try powdered sugar, chocolate glaze, or even a caramel drizzle instead of the cinnamon sugar coating.
  • Feel free to experiment with different spices in the dough, such as cardamom or ginger, to add a unique twist to the flavor.
  • These donut holes are best enjoyed fresh on the day they are made. However, you can store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Just keep in mind that they might lose some of their crispness over time.

Now that you have the recipe for these irresistible cinnamon sugar donut holes, it’s time to get cooking! Whether you’re indulging in a sweet breakfast or treating yourself to a delightful snack, these bite-sized treats are sure to hit the spot. So gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and let’s make some delicious donut holes! Enjoy!
