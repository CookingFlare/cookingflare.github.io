Delicious Thanksgiving Apple Pie: Classic Desserts for the Season


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and indulging in mouthwatering dishes that warm the heart and soul. One classic dessert that never fails to impress is the beloved Thanksgiving Apple Pie. With its golden crust, tender apples, and warm spices, this timeless dessert is a symbol of comfort and tradition. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating a delicious apple pie that will leave your guests in awe.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the topping:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ¼ cup rolled oats
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Start by preparing the crust. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently with a fork after each addition. Continue adding water until the dough comes together and can be formed into a ball. Be careful not to overmix.

  4. Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a disk. Wrap each disk in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the dough to rest.

  5. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.

  6. Roll out one disk of dough on a lightly floured surface until it’s large enough to fit into a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully transfer the dough to the dish and gently press it into the bottom and sides.

  7. Pour the apple filling into the pie dish, making sure it’s evenly distributed.

  8. In a separate bowl, prepare the topping. Combine the flour, brown sugar, cold cubed butter, rolled oats, and cinnamon. Use your fingers to rub the mixture together until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  9. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the apple filling, covering the entire surface.

  10. Roll out the second disk of dough and place it on top of the pie, either as a full crust or in a lattice pattern. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  11. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips, and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  12. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set and ensure clean slices.

  13. Serve the Thanksgiving Apple Pie warm or at room temperature, and if desired, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Whether it’s your first time baking a pie or you’re a seasoned pro, this Thanksgiving Apple Pie recipe is sure to become a family favorite. The combination of buttery crust, tender apples, and warm spices will transport you to a cozy autumn wonderland with every bite. So, embrace the spirit of the season and treat your loved ones to this classic dessert that embodies the essence of Thanksgiving. Happy baking!
