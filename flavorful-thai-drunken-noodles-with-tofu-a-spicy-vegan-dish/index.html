Flavorful Thai Drunken Noodles with Tofu: A Spicy Vegan Dish


If you’re looking for a dish that packs a punch of flavor and offers a delightful twist on traditional Thai cuisine, then look no further than Thai Drunken Noodles with Tofu. This spicy, aromatic dish, known as “Pad Kee Mao” in Thai, is a favorite among food lovers for its bold flavors and satisfying texture. Perfect for vegans and those seeking a hearty meal, this recipe combines the chewy goodness of wide rice noodles with perfectly seasoned tofu and vibrant veggies. Let’s dive into the world of Thai cooking and learn how to create this delectable dish right at home.

The Origins of Drunken Noodles

Drunken Noodles is believed to have originated in Thailand and is a popular street food dish. The name “drunken” is said to refer to the dish’s ability to cure a hangover, or perhaps it’s a nod to the spicy flavors that pair well with beer or cocktails. Traditionally, the dish is made with chicken or seafood, but our vegan version features tofu as the star ingredient, providing a perfect source of protein while keeping the dish plant-based.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make a flavorful batch of Thai Drunken Noodles with Tofu, gather the following ingredients:

For the Noodles:

  • 8 oz wide rice noodles (preferably fresh)
  • Water (for boiling)

For the Tofu:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce (vegan alternative)
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, sliced (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup baby corn, sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Tofu

  1. Drain and Press Tofu: Begin by draining your firm tofu. Wrap it in a clean kitchen towel or paper towel and place a heavy object on top to press out excess moisture. Let it sit for about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Cube and Marinate: Once pressed, cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes. In a bowl, combine with 2 tablespoons of soy sauce and let it marinate for at least 10 minutes.

  3. Coat with Cornstarch: After marinating, sprinkle the cornstarch over the tofu and gently toss to coat. This will give the tofu a crispy texture when fried.

Step 2: Cook the Noodles

  1. Boil Noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the wide rice noodles and cook according to package instructions, usually around 3-5 minutes for fresh noodles. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

  1. Mix Sauce Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, vegan oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, chili paste, sugar, and lime juice. Adjust the chili paste according to your spice preference.

Step 4: Fry the Tofu

  1. Heat Oil: In a large skillet or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.

  2. Fry Tofu: Once the oil is hot, add the marinated tofu cubes. Fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the tofu from the skillet and set aside.

Step 5: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

  1. Sauté Garlic and Chilies: In the same skillet, add another 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and sliced bird’s eye chilies. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  2. Add Vegetables: Throw in the sliced bell pepper, broccoli florets, and baby corn. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

Step 6: Combine Everything

  1. Add Noodles and Sauce: Add the cooked noodles to the skillet along with the sauce mixture. Toss everything together, ensuring the noodles are well coated in the sauce.

  2. Return Tofu: Gently fold in the crispy tofu and allow everything to heat through for an additional 1-2 minutes.

  3. Finish with Basil: Turn off the heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves and chopped green onions for a fresh aroma and flavor.

Step 7: Serve

  1. Plate the Dish: Transfer the Drunken Noodles to serving plates or bowls. You can garnish with extra Thai basil leaves or sliced green onions if desired.

  2. Enjoy: Serve hot and enjoy the burst of flavors in every bite! Pair it with a refreshing beverage, and you have yourself a meal that is sure to impress.

Tips for the Perfect Drunken Noodles

  • Adjust the Spice: If you’re not a fan of too much heat, feel free to reduce the amount of bird’s eye chilies or chili paste. The beauty of this dish lies in its adaptability to your taste preferences.

  • Experiment with Vegetables: While this recipe includes bell peppers and broccoli, feel free to add or substitute with other vegetables such as carrots, zucchini, or snap peas.

  • Marinate Longer: For even more flavor, you can marinate the tofu for a longer period. Overnight marination will allow the flavors to penetrate deeply into the tofu.

  • Fresh Ingredients: Using fresh Thai basil makes a significant difference in flavor. If you can find it at your local market, it’s worth the effort!

Nutritional Benefits

This Thai Drunken Noodles with Tofu recipe not only delights the palate but also offers a plethora of health benefits:

  • High in Protein: Tofu is an excellent source of plant-based protein, making this dish great for vegans and vegetarians.

  • Rich in Nutrients: The variety of vegetables adds essential vitamins and minerals to your meal, keeping it nutritious and balanced.

  • Low in Saturated Fat: This dish is low in saturated fat, as it avoids animal products and uses vegetable oil for cooking.

Final Thoughts

Indulging in a plate of Thai Drunken Noodles with Tofu is like embarking on a flavorful journey through the vibrant streets of Thailand. The combination of spicy, savory, and aromatic elements creates a satisfying meal that can be enjoyed any day of the week. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting dish, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to whip up this spicy vegan delight. Your taste buds will thank you! Enjoy the experience of cooking and sharing this delicious dish with friends and family. Happy cooking!
