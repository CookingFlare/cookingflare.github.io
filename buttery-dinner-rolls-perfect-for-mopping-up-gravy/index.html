Buttery Dinner Rolls: Perfect for Mopping Up Gravy


There’s something special about a warm, fluffy dinner roll, fresh from the oven, slathered in butter and served alongside a perfectly roasted turkey or a hearty beef stew. The aroma alone is enough to make your stomach growl with anticipation. Buttery dinner rolls are a staple of many holiday meals, and for good reason. They’re easy to make, delicious, and pair perfectly with a variety of savory sauces and gravies.

In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make the perfect buttery dinner rolls, complete with a golden brown crust and a soft, airy interior. Whether you’re serving them alongside your holiday feast or just want a tasty side dish for a weeknight dinner, these rolls are sure to please.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

Instructions

To start, combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar in a large mixing bowl. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the yeast is activated and foamy.

Next, add the flour, salt, and melted butter to the bowl. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then beat in the egg until the dough is smooth and elastic.

Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into 8-10 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and then flatten it slightly into a disk shape.

Place the rolls onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between each roll. Cover the rolls with plastic wrap and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they have puffed up slightly.

Bake the rolls for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown. Remove them from the oven and brush them with melted butter while they’re still warm.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the rolls more golden brown, brush them with an egg wash (beaten egg mixed with a little water) before baking.
  • To add some extra flavor to the rolls, try adding some chopped herbs (such as rosemary or thyme) or grated cheese to the dough.
  • If you want to make the rolls ahead of time, you can prepare the dough through the first rise, then refrigerate it overnight and let it come to room temperature before baking.
  • To make the rolls more savory, try using less sugar and adding some salt to the dough.

Conclusion

Buttery dinner rolls are a classic comfort food that’s easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. With their soft, fluffy interior and golden brown crust, they’re the perfect accompaniment to a hearty meal. Whether you’re serving them alongside a holiday feast or just want a tasty side dish for a weeknight dinner, these rolls are sure to be a hit. So go ahead, give them a try, and see why they’re a staple of many holiday meals.
