Rustic Cornbread Stuffing Recipe for a Homely Christmas Side


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to bring warmth and comfort to your Christmas table than with a delicious rustic cornbread stuffing? This homely side dish is packed with flavor and will leave your guests asking for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 batch of homemade cornbread
  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Crumble the cornbread into a large mixing bowl and set aside.

  2. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the chopped bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet, leaving the rendered fat behind.

  3. In the same skillet, add the diced onion, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the cooked vegetables to the bowl with the crumbled cornbread. Sprinkle in the dried thyme, dried sage, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to combine.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken or vegetable broth, melted butter, and beaten eggs. Pour the mixture over the cornbread mixture and stir until everything is evenly coated.

  6. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly. Cover the dish with aluminum foil.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the top is golden brown and crisp.

  8. Once the stuffing is done, garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

  9. Serve this rustic cornbread stuffing alongside your Christmas turkey or roasted chicken for a truly comforting holiday meal. Your guests will be blown away by the delicious flavors and the cozy, homely feel of this side dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, omit the bacon and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • Feel free to add in some chopped walnuts, dried cranberries, or even diced apples for extra texture and flavor.
  • If you prefer a moister stuffing, add a bit more chicken or vegetable broth to the mixture before baking.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

So, there you have it – a delightful rustic cornbread stuffing recipe that will make your Christmas feast extra special. The combination of the savory cornbread, aromatic herbs, and the hint of bacon creates a dish that embodies the spirit of the holiday season. Don’t forget to savor each bite and enjoy the warmth it brings to your table. Happy cooking and happy holidays!
