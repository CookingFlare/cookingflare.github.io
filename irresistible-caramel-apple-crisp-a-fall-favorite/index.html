Irresistible Caramel Apple Crisp: A Fall Favorite


Fall is the perfect season for cozy sweaters, colorful leaves, and of course, delicious apple desserts. One dessert that never fails to impress is the mouthwatering caramel apple crisp. The combination of sweet and tart apples, warm cinnamon, and gooey caramel is simply irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a fall gathering or simply looking to satisfy your sweet tooth, this caramel apple crisp recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups of peeled, cored, and sliced apples (a mix of tart and sweet varieties works best)
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup of brown sugar, packed
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup of caramel sauce
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Toss until the apples are evenly coated with the sugar and spices.

  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, oats, and melted butter until crumbly. This will be the delicious streusel topping for your caramel apple crisp.

  4. Spread half of the streusel mixture evenly over the bottom of the greased baking dish. Press down gently to create a firm crust.

  5. Pour the caramel sauce over the apples, ensuring that every slice is generously coated. The caramel will add a delightful sweetness and gooey texture to the crisp.

  6. Sprinkle the remaining streusel mixture over the caramel-coated apples, creating a deliciously crumbly topping.

  7. Bake the caramel apple crisp in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown and crispy.

  8. Once baked, allow the caramel apple crisp to cool for a few minutes before serving. The aroma of warm apples and cinnamon will fill your kitchen, making it even more inviting.

  9. Serve the caramel apple crisp warm, straight from the oven, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top if desired. The contrast between the warm crisp and the cold, creamy ice cream is pure bliss.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, sprinkle some chopped pecans or walnuts over the streusel topping before baking. The added crunch and nutty flavor will take this dessert to a whole new level.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, try adding a sprinkle of sea salt on top of the caramel sauce before adding the streusel. The salt will enhance the sweetness and create a delightful salty-sweet contrast.

  • Don’t limit yourself to just apples. Feel free to experiment with other fruit combinations such as peaches and raspberries or pears and cranberries. The possibilities are endless!

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply microwave individual servings for a few seconds or warm in a preheated oven until heated through.

Fall is the time to indulge in comforting desserts, and this irresistible caramel apple crisp is the epitome of fall flavors. The combination of tender apples, warm spices, and gooey caramel will leave you craving for more. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and get ready to enjoy this fall favorite.
