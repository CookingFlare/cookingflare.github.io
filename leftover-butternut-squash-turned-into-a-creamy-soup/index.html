Leftover Butternut Squash Turned into a Creamy Soup


If you find yourself with leftover roasted butternut squash, don’t let it go to waste! Instead, turn it into a delicious and creamy soup that will warm you up and delight your taste buds. This simple recipe is a great way to repurpose leftovers into a whole new meal, and it’s perfect for those chilly autumn or winter days when you crave something comforting and nourishing.

Ingredients

  • Leftover roasted butternut squash
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil
  • Optional garnishes: a dollop of crème fraîche, a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds, a drizzle of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, then add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the Butternut Squash: Scoop the leftover roasted butternut squash flesh into the pot with the sautéed onions and garlic. Stir to combine and let it cook for a few minutes to develop the flavors.

  3. Season the Mixture: Sprinkle the ground cumin, ground coriander, and nutmeg over the butternut squash mixture. Stir well to evenly distribute the spices.

  4. Pour in the Broth: Add the vegetable or chicken broth to the pot, ensuring that the butternut squash mixture is fully covered. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  5. Blend the Soup: Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, purée the soup until smooth and creamy. If using a regular blender, work in batches and be careful when blending hot liquids.

  6. Adjust the Seasoning: Taste the soup and add salt and pepper as needed. Adjust the consistency with additional broth if it’s too thick for your liking.

  7. Serve and Garnish: Ladle the creamy butternut squash soup into bowls and garnish with a dollop of crème fraîche, a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds, and a drizzle of olive oil if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Roasting Butternut Squash: If you need to roast butternut squash specifically for this recipe, simply halve the squash lengthwise, scoop out the seeds, brush the flesh with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Place the squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for about 45-50 minutes or until tender.

  • Creamy Texture: For an even creamier texture, add a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk to the soup before blending.

  • Flavor Enhancements: Feel free to customize the soup with additional spices such as cinnamon, paprika, or a dash of hot sauce for a kick.

  • Serving Suggestions: This creamy butternut squash soup pairs beautifully with a crusty bread or a simple green salad for a satisfying meal.

Enjoy Your Leftover Butternut Squash Soup

With this recipe, you can turn your leftover roasted butternut squash into a velvety and flavorful soup that’s perfect for a cozy night in or a special gathering with friends and family. Embrace the versatility of this humble ingredient and enjoy the heartwarming experience of transforming leftovers into something new and delightful.

So, the next time you have leftover butternut squash sitting in your fridge, let it inspire you to create a comforting and nourishing dish that will warm both body and soul. Happy cooking!
