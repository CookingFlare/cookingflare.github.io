Global Fermentation Fest: Celebrating Dishes From Every Nation


Love the aroma of fresh bread wafting through the air? How about exploring the delightful world of fermented dishes from all around the globe? From India’s mouth-watering dosas to Japan’s savory onigiris, the Global Fermentation Fest is an amazing opportunity to discover the unique and diverse culinary delights of various nations.

This festival brings together traditional fermented recipes from every corner of the world. From artisanal Belgian beer to Ethiopian Injera bread, experience the vibrant and incredible tastes of international fermentation. Here, we will guide you through a scrumptious journey to create these tantalizing, handpicked dishes from around the globe.

Fermented Indian Delights

Let’s first embark on our journey to India, where we will learn how to make a scrumptious dosa, a fermented and crepe-like delicacy that serves as an excellent breakfast option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of fermented rice batter
  • 1 cup of urad dal (split black gram)
  • Water
  • 2-3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • For the filling, we can use potatoes, turmeric, ginger, garlic, onions, and grated coconut.

Directions:

  1. Rinse and soak 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of urad dal separately for a couple of hours.
  2. Grind the rice and dal separately with water to obtain a smooth batter.
  3. Mix both the batters and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a pan, drizzle some oil, and spread 2-3 tablespoons of batter evenly on the pan.
  5. Cook until brown. Add filling on one side of the dosa when it’s done cooking.
  6. Fold, transfer onto a plate, and serve hot.

Japanese-style Onigiri

Next, let’s visit Japan for a delicious onigiri-making adventure. Onigiris are savory rice balls that can be enjoyed hot or cold, making them the perfect snack for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of water
  • 5 nori sheets, cut into halves (optional)
  • Veggies (carrots, corn, pickled radish) or seasoned fish (tuna, salmon) for filling.

Directions:

  1. Rinse the rice and drain until the water runs clear.
  2. Combine the rice, water, and salt in a saucepan.
  3. Cook for about 20 minutes. Wait until cooked rice is cool enough to handle.
  4. Form small balls using the rice, and create a shallow indentation to stuff some filling inside.
  5. Insert veggies or fish, and wrap the onigiri with nori sheets if desired.
  6. Serve with a sprinkle of furikake or soy sauce.

Ethiopian Injera Bread

As we near the end of our global culinary journey, let’s explore the flavors of Ethiopia with an authentic injera recipe. Injera is a versatile, sourdough flatbread that doubles as a utensil, making it a staple in Ethiopian cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of teff flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Additional water for the batter

Directions:

  1. Mix teff flour and water in a bowl.
  2. Add a pinch of salt and mix well.
  3. Add more water if necessary, and whisk until smooth.
  4. Allow the mixture to ferment for 2-3 days.
  5. On a griddle, cook a ladleful of batter to make a thin round bread.
  6. Serve with spicy stews, curries, or as a side to wash foods off your plate.

Global cuisine offers endless flavors and unique dishes. By incorporating these recipes into your meal planning, you’ll not only expand your palate but also initiate a taste exploration into the diverse food cultures that the world has to offer. So, gather your ingredients and embark on your next culinary adventure with the Global Fermentation Fest.
