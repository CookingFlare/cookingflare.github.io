Sous Vide Cooking: A Guide to Sous Vide Soups


sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we cook, and its applications are not limited to just meat and vegetables. in this article, we will explore the world of sous vide soups, and show you how to cook perfect soups every time.

why sous vide soups?

sous vide cooking allows for unparalleled control over the cooking process, ensuring that your soups are cooked to the perfect temperature every time. this is especially important when it comes to soups, as they can quickly go from perfect to overcooked and unappetizing.

basic principles of sous vide soup cooking

before we dive into specific recipes, let’s cover the basic principles of sous vide soup cooking.

  • temperature control: sous vide cooking relies on precise temperature control, which is especially important when cooking soups. a temperature of 180-190°F (82-88°C) is ideal for most soups.
  • water bath: soups are cooked in a water bath, which is a container filled with water and heated to the desired temperature.
  • sealing: soups must be sealed in airtight containers to prevent contamination and ensure even cooking.

basic equipment needed

to get started with sous vide soups, you will need the following basic equipment:

  • sous vide machine: a sous vide machine is necessary for controlling the temperature of the water bath.
  • water bath container: a large container filled with water, such as a pot or a sink.
  • sealable containers: containers that can be sealed airtight, such as ziplock bags or glass jars with lids.
  • thermometer: a thermometer is necessary for monitoring the temperature of the water bath.

common mistakes to avoid

when cooking sous vide soups, there are a few common mistakes to avoid:

  • overcrowding: overcrowding the container with too much food can lead to uneven cooking and a lack of flavor.
  • insufficient sealing: failing to seal the container airtight can allow contamination and affect the texture of the soup.
  • inconsistent temperature: failing to maintain a consistent temperature can result in overcooked or undercooked soup.

basic sous vide soup recipe

here is a basic recipe for a delicious and easy-to-make sous vide soup:

creamy tomato soup

  • 2 cups of chopped tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream or half-and-half
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • salt and pepper to taste
  1. combine the chopped tomatoes, onion, garlic, broth, and basil in a sealable container.
  2. add salt and pepper to taste.
  3. seal the container airtight and place it in the water bath.
  4. set the sous vide machine to 180°F (82°C) and cook for 30-40 minutes.
  5. remove the container from the water bath and stir in the heavy cream or half-and-half.
  6. serve hot and enjoy!

advanced sous vide soup recipes

once you have mastered the basic recipe, you can experiment with more advanced recipes. here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • roasted vegetable soup: add roasted vegetables, such as carrots, celery, and onions, to the basic recipe for added depth of flavor.
  • minestrone soup: add canned beans, diced tomatoes, and pasta to the basic recipe for a hearty and comforting soup.
  • creamy broccoli soup: add steamed broccoli and heavy cream to the basic recipe for a creamy and nutritious soup.

tips and variations

here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind when cooking sous vide soups:

  • add aromatics: add aromatics, such as onions, garlic, and celery, to the water bath for added flavor.
  • use different broths: use different broths, such as chicken, beef, or vegetable broth, to change the flavor of the soup.
  • add spices and herbs: add spices and herbs, such as cumin, paprika, and thyme, to the soup for added flavor.

conclusion

sous vide soups offer a new level of control and precision in the kitchen, allowing you to cook perfect soups every time. with the basic principles and equipment outlined above, you can start experimenting with different recipes and variations. happy cooking!
