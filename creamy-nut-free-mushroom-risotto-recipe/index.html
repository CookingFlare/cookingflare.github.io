Creamy Nut-Free Mushroom Risotto Recipe


Risotto is a classic Italian dish known for its creamy texture and rich flavors. This recipe puts a twist on the traditional version by making it nut-free, catering to those with allergies or dietary restrictions. The star ingredient here is mushrooms, which add earthy depth and umami to the dish. Combined with Arborio rice, Parmesan cheese, and a few secret ingredients, this mushroom risotto becomes a comforting and satisfying meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup white button mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Mushrooms: Start by cleaning the mushrooms. Use a damp paper towel to wipe off any dirt from the mushrooms. Trim the stems and slice them into even pieces.

  2. Heat the Broth: In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable or chicken broth to a simmer over medium heat. Once it simmers, reduce the heat to low to keep it warm throughout the cooking process.

  3. Sauté the Mushrooms: In a large skillet or saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced mushrooms in batches so they can brown properly without overcrowding the pan. Sauté for about 5-6 minutes until they are golden brown and caramelized. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  4. Cook the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the remaining butter and olive oil. Sauté the chopped onion until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  5. Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet with the onions and garlic. Stir continuously for 1-2 minutes until the rice becomes lightly toasted and opaque.

  6. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine (if using) and stir until it is absorbed by the rice. This step adds a depth of flavor to the risotto.

  7. Cook the Risotto: Begin adding the warm broth to the rice mixture, one ladleful (about 1/2 cup) at a time. Stir frequently and allow each addition of broth to be absorbed before adding the next. This gradual process helps release the starch from the rice, creating the creamy texture typical of risotto. Continue cooking and stirring for about 18-20 minutes or until the rice is tender yet slightly firm to the bite (al dente).

  8. Incorporate Mushrooms and Cheese: During the last 5 minutes of cooking, fold in the sautéed mushrooms and fresh thyme leaves. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until it melts and combines smoothly into the risotto. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Finish and Serve: Remove the risotto from heat. Let it rest for a minute to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve warm, garnished with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips for Perfect Risotto

  • Use Arborio Rice: This short-grain rice is essential for creamy risotto due to its high starch content.
  • Keep Broth Warm: Adding warm broth gradually ensures even cooking and creaminess.
  • Stir Constantly: Stirring releases starch and helps achieve the desired creamy texture.
  • Experiment with Mushrooms: Feel free to use your favorite mushroom varieties or a mix for depth of flavor.

Conclusion

This nut-free mushroom risotto recipe offers a delightful twist on a classic Italian dish, making it accessible to those with nut allergies or dietary preferences. The combination of earthy mushrooms, creamy Arborio rice, and savory Parmesan cheese creates a dish that is both comforting and sophisticated. Whether served as a main course or a side dish, this risotto is sure to impress with its rich flavors and creamy texture. Enjoy experimenting with different mushroom varieties and making this recipe your own!
