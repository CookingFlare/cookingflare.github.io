Scrumptious Dairy-Free Chicken Pot Pie

Are you craving a comforting and hearty meal that’s also dairy-free? Look no further than this scrumptious dairy-free chicken pot pie recipe! Packed with tender chicken, flavorful vegetables, and a creamy dairy-free sauce, this dish is a perfect choice for a cozy dinner with family or friends. Let’s dive into the details of creating this delectable pot pie. Ingredients For the Pie Crust: 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1 cup vegetable shortening 6-8 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced 1 cup diced potatoes 1 cup diced carrots 1 cup diced celery 1/2 cup diced onion 3 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup dairy-free butter 1/3 cup all-purpose flour 2 1/2 cups chicken broth 1 cup unsweetened almond milk 1 teaspoon dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Making the Pie Crust: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in the vegetable shortening until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, tossing with a fork until a ball forms. Divide the dough in half and shape into two disks. Wrap in plastic and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Preparing the Filling: In a large skillet, cook the diced chicken until no longer pink. Remove from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, sauté the potatoes, carrots, celery, onion, and garlic until the vegetables are tender. Add the dairy-free butter to the skillet and melt. Stir in the flour until well combined to create a roux. Gradually add the chicken broth and almond milk, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens. Add the cooked chicken back into the skillet and season with thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer for a few minutes until the filling is creamy and well combined. Assembling the Pot Pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one of the chilled pie crust disks to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Pour the prepared chicken and vegetable filling into the pie crust. Roll out the second pie crust disk and place it over the filling, sealing the edges and trimming any excess dough. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape. Baking the Pot Pie: Place the assembled pot pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Serving and Enjoying Once the pot pie is baked to perfection, allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. The creamy, dairy-free filling combined with the flaky, golden crust is sure to delight your taste buds and leave you wanting more. This scrumptious dairy-free chicken pot pie is a wonderful option for a cozy dinner on a chilly evening or a special gathering with loved ones. ...

Delicious Dairy-Free Raspberry Chia Pudding

Looking for a delicious and healthy breakfast or dessert option? Look no further! This dairy-free raspberry chia pudding is not only easy to make, but it’s also packed with nutrients and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re following a dairy-free diet or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based foods into your meals, this recipe is perfect for you. Ingredients 1 cup almond milk (or any other dairy-free milk of your choice) 1/4 cup chia seeds 1/2 cup fresh or frozen raspberries 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract Optional toppings: fresh raspberries, sliced almonds, shredded coconut Instructions In a medium-sized bowl, combine the almond milk, chia seeds, maple syrup (or honey), and vanilla extract. Stir well to ensure the chia seeds are evenly distributed throughout the mixture. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes, then give it another stir to prevent clumping. This will allow the chia seeds to absorb the liquid and create a pudding-like consistency. Add the raspberries to the bowl and gently mash them with a fork. This will release their natural juices and give the pudding a vibrant pink color. Stir the mixture once more, making sure the raspberries are well incorporated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results. This will give the chia seeds enough time to fully absorb the liquid and thicken the pudding. Once the pudding has set, give it a good stir to break up any clumps that may have formed. You can adjust the consistency by adding more almond milk if desired. Divide the pudding into serving bowls or jars and top with fresh raspberries, sliced almonds, or shredded coconut for added texture and flavor. Enjoy your delicious dairy-free raspberry chia pudding straight away, or refrigerate for later consumption.
