5-Ingredient Caprese Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms Recipe


If you’re a fan of the classic Caprese salad, you’re going to love this delightful twist on the traditional dish - the 5-Ingredient Caprese Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms. This recipe takes the fresh flavors of ripe tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil and combines them with hearty portobello mushrooms for a satisfying and flavorful meal. The best part? It only requires five simple ingredients and minimal prep time, making it a perfect go-to recipe for busy weeknights or a quick and impressive appetizer for entertaining.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Portobello Mushrooms: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Clean the portobello mushrooms by gently wiping them with a damp paper towel to remove any dirt. Carefully remove the stems and gills from the mushrooms to create a hollow space for the filling.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and chopped basil. Gently toss the ingredients together until they are well mixed.

  3. Stuff the Mushrooms: Place the cleaned portobello mushrooms on a baking sheet, cap side down. Evenly distribute the filling mixture into the mushroom caps, pressing it gently to ensure it fills the cavity.

  4. Bake the Stuffed Mushrooms: Drizzle the stuffed mushrooms with balsamic glaze and place them in the preheated oven. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is heated through.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the mushrooms are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with additional fresh basil if desired, and serve the Caprese Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms as a delicious appetizer or a light main course.

Tips and Variations:

  • Grilled Option: If you prefer a smoky flavor, you can also grill the stuffed portobello mushrooms over medium heat for about 10-15 minutes instead of baking them.

  • Add Protein: For a heartier meal, consider adding cooked quinoa, diced chicken, or Italian sausage to the filling mixture before stuffing the mushrooms.

  • Vegan Variation: To make this recipe vegan-friendly, substitute the fresh mozzarella with a dairy-free alternative such as vegan mozzarella or marinated tofu.

  • Make It Ahead: You can prepare the filling in advance and store it in the refrigerator until you are ready to stuff and bake the mushrooms, making it a convenient option for meal prepping.

Final Thoughts:

The 5-Ingredient Caprese Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms recipe is a testament to the beauty of simplicity and quality ingredients. With just a handful of components, you can create a dish that is as visually stunning as it is delicious. Whether you’re looking for a quick and easy weeknight dinner or an elegant appetizer to impress your guests, these stuffed portobello mushrooms are sure to become a staple in your recipe repertoire. Enjoy the vibrant flavors of summer with this delightful dish!
