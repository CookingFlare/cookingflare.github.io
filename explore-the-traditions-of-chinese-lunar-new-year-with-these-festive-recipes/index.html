Explore the Traditions of Chinese Lunar New Year with These Festive Recipes


As the clock strikes midnight on the eve of Chinese Lunar New Year, families gather around the dinner table to feast on traditional dishes, symbolizing good fortune, prosperity, and unity. The Lunar New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is a time-honored celebration that marks the beginning of the new year on the traditional Chinese calendar. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the cultural significance of Chinese Lunar New Year and explore the festive recipes that make this holiday so special.

The Significance of Chinese Lunar New Year

The Chinese Lunar New Year is a 15-day celebration that originated over 3,000 years ago. It is based on the lunisolar calendar, which combines the cycles of the moon and the sun. The holiday is marked by the beginning of the new year, the reunion of families, and the renewal of life. It is a time for people to come together, share in traditional foods and customs, and welcome the arrival of good fortune and prosperity.

Traditional Chinese Lunar New Year Dishes

The Lunar New Year’s Eve dinner, also known as the “Reunion Dinner,” is a highlight of the celebration. It is a time for families to gather around the table and share in a variety of traditional dishes that symbolize good luck, prosperity, and unity. Some of the most popular dishes include:

  • Dumplings (): Steamed or pan-fried dumplings filled with meat, vegetables, or seafood are a staple of the Lunar New Year’s Eve dinner. They symbolize wealth and good fortune.
  • Fish (): A whole fish is served as the main course, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • Noodles (): Long noodles are served to represent longevity and a long, healthy life.
  • Niangao (): A sweet glutinous rice cake is served to symbolize progress and advancement.
  • Fa gao (): A sweet, fluffy steamed cake is served to symbolize good luck and prosperity.

Recipes for a Festive Lunar New Year

Here are some traditional Chinese Lunar New Year recipes that you can try at home:

Dumplings ()

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • Filling ingredients (e.g. ground pork, shredded cabbage, chopped scallions)

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the warm water and mix until a dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg.
  4. Roll out each ball into a thin circle.
  5. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape.
  7. Press the edges together to seal the dumpling.
  8. Steam the dumplings for 10-15 minutes or pan-fry them until golden brown.

Fish ()

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole fish (e.g. tilapia, cod), scaled and gutted
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Rinse the fish under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, vegetable oil, garlic, ginger, sugar, and sesame oil.
  4. Place the fish in a baking dish and brush the sauce all over the fish.
  5. Bake the fish for 12-15 minutes or until cooked through.

Niangao ()

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Gradually add the water and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours.
  5. Preheat the steamer and place the dough in the steamer basket.
  6. Steam the niangao for 30-40 minutes or until cooked through.

Fa Gao ()

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped scallions

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Gradually add the water and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours.
  5. Preheat the steamer and place the dough in the steamer basket.
  6. Steam the fa gao for 30-40 minutes or until cooked through.
  7. Brush the top of the fa gao with sesame oil and sprinkle with chopped scallions.

Conclusion

The Chinese Lunar New Year is a time-honored celebration that is steeped in tradition and cultural significance. The festive recipes shared in this blog post are just a few examples of the many delicious dishes that are enjoyed during this holiday. Whether you’re celebrating with family and friends or simply looking to try new recipes, these dishes are sure to bring a taste of the Lunar New Year to your table. Gong Xi Fa Cai!
