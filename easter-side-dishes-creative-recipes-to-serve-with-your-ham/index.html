Easter Side Dishes: Creative Recipes to Serve with Your Ham


Easter Sunday dinner is a time-honored tradition filled with vibrant flavors and family gathering around a lovingly prepared table. While the centerpiece ham often steals the show, the side dishes are equally essential in crafting a memorable meal. This year, instead of defaulting to the usual potatoes and green beans, why not explore creative, fresh, and flavorful sides that complement your Easter ham beautifully?

Here’s a collection of inventive and delicious Easter side dishes designed to elevate your holiday feast and impress your guests. From bright spring vegetables to comforting starches, these recipes offer a balance of textures, colors, and tastes—perfect companions for your juicy, glazed ham.

1. Roasted Carrots with Honey and Thyme

Carrots roasting to a caramelized golden brown enhance the sweetness of ham, while fresh thyme adds an aromatic earthiness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrots with olive oil, honey, and thyme leaves. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Spread the carrots in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve warm alongside your ham for a burst of sweet and savory goodness.

Why it works: The natural sweetness of roasted carrots mirrors the honey-glazed ham, creating a harmonious palette, while thyme’s herbal notes refresh the palate.

2. Creamy Scalloped Potatoes with Gruyère and Chives

Classic scalloped potatoes receive a decadent upgrade with nutty Gruyère cheese and the subtle tang of fresh chives—comfort food with a sophisticated twist.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 ½ cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a saucepan, warm the cream and garlic until just simmering. Remove from heat.
  3. Layer half the potatoes in the dish, season with salt and pepper, sprinkle with half the Gruyère cheese and half the chives.
  4. Repeat the layering, then pour the warm cream mixture over the potatoes.
  5. Dot the top with butter.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 30 minutes or until the top is golden and potatoes tender.
  7. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Why it works: Creamy and cheesy potatoes balance the salty, smoky ham with richness, while fresh chives add a light, oniony contrast that brightens the dish.

3. Spring Pea and Asparagus Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

Celebrate spring’s bounty with a crisp salad filled with tender asparagus, sweet peas, and peppery arugula, all dressed in a zesty lemon vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb asparagus spears, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 ½ cups fresh or thawed frozen peas
  • 4 cups arugula
  • ½ cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp toasted almonds (optional)

Method:

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to boil. Blanch asparagus for 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp. Add peas for the last minute. Drain and rinse under cold water to cool.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk olive oil, lemon juice, mustard, salt, and pepper to make the vinaigrette.
  3. In a large bowl, toss arugula, asparagus, peas, and radishes.
  4. Drizzle with vinaigrette and toss gently to coat.
  5. Sprinkle toasted almonds on top, if using.
  6. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Why it works: The freshness of spring vegetables cuts through the richness of ham, while the lemon vinaigrette introduces brightness and acidity that balances the meal.

4. Maple-Glazed Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Crunch

Sweet potatoes add warmth and a subtle sweetness that harmonizes with ham, and a crunchy pecan topping brings texture and nutty flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ¾ cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil sweet potatoes until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain.
  3. Mash sweet potatoes with butter, milk, maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  4. Spread mashed sweet potatoes into a greased baking dish.
  5. In a small bowl, mix pecans and brown sugar, then sprinkle evenly over the top.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes until topping is toasted and casserole is heated through.

Why it works: Sweet potatoes amplify the natural sweetness of ham without overwhelming it, while the pecan topping adds a delightful crunch and subtle deeper flavor notes.

5. Green Bean Almondine with Garlic and Lemon Zest

Elevate a simple green bean side with toasted almonds, garlic, and fresh lemon zest for a vibrant dish that adds crunch and brightness.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ lbs fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup sliced almonds
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook green beans until crisp-tender, about 4-5 minutes. Drain and plunge into ice water to stop cooking.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add garlic and almonds, sauté for 2-3 minutes until almonds are golden and garlic fragrant.
  3. Drain green beans and add them to the skillet, tossing to coat.
  4. Remove from heat, add lemon zest, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve immediately.

Why it works: The nuttiness of almonds and the brightness of lemon perfectly contrast the smoky sweetness of ham, while the garlic adds a savory warmth.

6. Creamy Spinach and Artichoke Gratin

This warm gratin blends rich cream, tender spinach, and earthy artichoke hearts into a bubbly, irresistible side dish that pairs wonderfully with ham.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 10 oz fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
  3. Add spinach and cook until wilted, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in chopped artichokes and heavy cream; simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat, stir in mozzarella and half the Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Pour mixture into a greased baking dish, top with remaining Parmesan.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes until golden and bubbly.

Why it works: The creamy gratin contrasts the texture of ham slices, while the artichokes provide a slightly tangy complexity to the flavor profile.

7. Parmesan and Herb Dinner Rolls

No Easter table is complete without fresh, pillowy rolls. These herbed Parmesan dinner rolls bring cheesy richness and subtle herbal notes perfect for sopping up ham glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp mixed fresh herbs (parsley, rosemary, thyme), finely chopped

Method:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm milk with sugar. Let sit 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, Parmesan, and herbs.
  3. Add melted butter and yeast mixture; stir until dough comes together.
  4. Knead on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place dough in a greased bowl and cover. Let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled.
  6. Punch down, form into 12 rolls, place on a greased baking sheet.
  7. Cover and let rise 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake rolls for 18-20 minutes until golden.
  9. Brush warm rolls with melted butter before serving.

Why it works: These rolls offer a savory, cheesy note with fragrant herbs that lift ham’s flavors while providing a soft, comforting bite.

Bringing It All Together

When planning your Easter meal, think about variety: combine creamy, crunchy, sweet, savory, and fresh flavors to create a balanced experience. These recipes enhance the natural flavor of your ham with complementary tastes and textures. Adding seasonal vegetables and herbs brings freshness and color, making your Easter table as visually appealing as it is delicious.

Whether you select one side or put together a full spread, these dishes promise to make your holiday meal not only traditional but excitingly new. Enjoy the process of cooking, the laughter around the table, and the shared joy of good food and company.

Happy Easter and happy cooking!
