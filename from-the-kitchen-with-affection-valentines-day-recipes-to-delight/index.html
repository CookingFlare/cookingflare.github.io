From the Kitchen with Affection: Valentine's Day Recipes to Delight


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and it’s time to cook up some love in the kitchen! Whether you’re celebrating with your significant other, family, or friends, these recipes are sure to delight and impress. In this post, we’ll share some of our favorite Valentine’s Day recipes that are sure to make your taste buds skip a beat.

Sweet Treats

Start your Valentine’s Day celebration off right with some sweet treats. These recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and set the tone for a romantic evening.

Chocolate-Dipped Strawberries

  • 1 pint fresh strawberries
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon shortening (such as Crisco)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth. Dip the strawberries in the melted chocolate, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the dipped strawberries on a parchment-lined baking sheet and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Dust with confectioners’ sugar before serving.

Raspberry Tiramisu

  • 12-16 ladyfingers
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar

In a large bowl, combine the coffee and mascarpone cheese. In a separate bowl, combine the granulated sugar and cocoa powder. Dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture for about 3-5 seconds on each side. They should be soft and pliable but not too wet. In a large serving dish, create a layer of ladyfingers. Sprinkle with half of the sugar and cocoa mixture, followed by half of the heavy cream and half of the raspberries. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of ladyfingers. Dust with confectioners’ sugar before serving.

Main Course

After indulging in some sweet treats, it’s time to move on to the main course. These recipes are sure to impress your loved one and satisfy your appetite.

Pan-Seared Filet Mignon with Red Wine Reduction

  • 4 filet mignon steaks (6 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine (such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish

Season the filet mignon steaks with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the filets for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness. Transfer the filets to a plate and cover with foil to keep warm. Reduce the heat to medium and add the garlic to the skillet. Cook for 1 minute, or until fragrant. Add the red wine and beef broth to the skillet, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until reduced by half, stirring occasionally. Stir in the butter until melted. Serve the filets with the red wine reduction spooned over the top. Garnish with fresh thyme.

Baked Salmon with Lemon-Dill Sauce

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup freshly chopped dill
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat the oven to 400째F (200째C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with garlic. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through. Meanwhile, combine the chopped dill, lemon juice, and heavy cream in a bowl. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the baked salmon with the lemon-dill sauce spooned over the top.

Desserts

No Valentine’s Day celebration would be complete without a romantic dessert. These recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and leave a lasting impression.

Chocolate Lava Cake

  • 2 ounces high-quality dark chocolate (at least 70% cocoa), broken into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Preheat the oven to 425째F (220째C). Butter four 6-ounce ramekins and dust with sugar. Melt the chocolate in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. In a large bowl, using an electric mixer, beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract. Melt the chocolate and let cool slightly. Beat the chocolate into the butter mixture until well combined. Stir in the flour mixture until just combined. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are set and the centers are still slightly jiggly. Remove from the oven and let cool in the ramekins for 1 minute. Run a knife around the edges of each cake to release. Invert onto plates and serve immediately, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if desired.

Fresh Fruit Tart

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups mixed fresh berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice

Preheat the oven to 400째F (200째C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the pastry to the prepared baking sheet. In a small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, flour, and salt. Sprinkle the mixture evenly over the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Drizzle the melted butter over the sugar mixture. Arrange the fresh berries on top of the sugar mixture, leaving a small border around the edges. In a small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Sprinkle the mixture evenly over the berries. Fold the edges of the pastry up over the filling, pressing gently to seal. Brush the pastry with a little bit of water and sprinkle with granulated sugar. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Let cool before serving.
