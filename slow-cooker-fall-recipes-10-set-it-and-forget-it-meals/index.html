Slow Cooker Fall Recipes: 10 Set-It-and-Forget-It Meals


As the air turns crisp and the leaves shift into shades of amber and gold, there’s nothing quite as comforting as a warm, hearty meal to end the day. With busy schedules and shorter daylight hours, achieving homecooked perfection might seem like a tall order. Fortunately, slow cookers are here to save the day. These kitchen heroes allow you to prepare soul-soothing meals with minimal effort—perfect for autumn nights when you’d rather wrap yourself in a cozy blanket than stand over a stove. Below are ten incredible slow cooker recipes that embrace the flavors of fall while giving you the freedom to set it and forget it.

1. Maple-Glazed Apple Cider Pork Roast

Celebrate the season’s apple harvest with a sweet and savory apple cider pork roast that practically cooks itself. Combine a pork shoulder, apple cider, fresh apple slices, and a bit of maple syrup in your slow cooker. Add cinnamon sticks and fresh rosemary to heighten the autumnal flavors. After slow-cooking for 6-8 hours, the pork becomes melt-in-your-mouth tender, infused with a perfect balance of sweetness and spice.

Pro Tip:

Sear the pork shoulder in a skillet before placing it in the slow cooker. The caramelized crust adds depth to the dish and locks in the juices.

2. Autumn Beef Stew with Root Vegetables

Nothing says fall like a hearty bowl of beef stew. This version elevates classic comfort food by adding seasonal root vegetables like parsnips, sweet potatoes, and carrots. Chunky beef cubes are coated in flour, quickly browned, and then slow-cooked with beef broth, onions, garlic, and a hint of red wine for richness. Allow it to simmer for 8 hours to develop a deep, savory flavor.

Pair It With:

Crusty bread or buttermilk biscuits to soak up all the delicious gravy.

3. Pumpkin and Black Bean Chili

This vegetarian-friendly chili is a delightful twist on a cold-weather classic. Black beans, diced tomatoes, and a dollop of puréed pumpkin come together to create a thick and hearty dish that’s loaded with nutritional benefits. Spices like cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder provide warmth, while a hint of cinnamon ties it all together with a unique fall flair.

Serving Suggestion:

Top with shredded cheddar, sour cream, or avocado for an added burst of creamy goodness.

4. Slow Cooker Chicken and Wild Rice Soup

When the weather turns chilly, nothing soothes the soul like a bowl of chicken soup. This creamy chicken and wild rice recipe incorporates mushrooms, celery, and carrots for a rustic, earthy flavor. The slow cooker does all the work, blending tender chunks of chicken with nutty wild rice and a perfectly seasoned broth. After 6-7 hours, stir in some heavy cream or coconut milk for a velvety finish.

5. Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Think risotto is only achievable by constantly stirring over a stovetop? Think again! The slow cooker turns this classic dish into a low-maintenance masterpiece. Combine arborio rice, cubed butternut squash, vegetable broth, a touch of garlic, and a generous sprinkle of dried sage. Cook for 2-3 hours on high or 5-6 hours on low. Finish it off with grated Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil.

Bonus Tip:

Use fresh sage leaves if you can—they add a peppery aroma that dried herbs just can’t match.

6. Honey Garlic Meatballs

These sweet and tangy meatballs make a great appetizer or main dish. Toss prepared meatballs (store-bought or homemade) into your slow cooker with a glaze made of honey, soy sauce, garlic, and a splash of apple cider vinegar. In 3-4 hours on low heat, you’ll have flavorful, sticky meatballs that are perfect for serving over mashed potatoes or with toothpicks for an easy party snack.

7. Turkey and Sweet Potato Chili

If you’re starting to plan for Thanksgiving—or just looking forward to all things turkey—this slow cooker chili is the ideal meal. Brown lean ground turkey and layer it in the cooker with sweet potatoes, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and your favorite chili spices. The sweetness of the potatoes balances the smoky heat, creating a dish that’s hearty but refreshingly light.

8. Cinnamon Spice Pear Cobbler

Fall desserts are all about spices and warm fruit, and this pear cobbler nails it. Slice ripe pears and coat them with cinnamon, nutmeg, and brown sugar before layering them in your slow cooker. Top with a biscuit mix or crumble topping and let the magic happen over the next few hours. The result is an ooey-gooey dessert that pairs beautifully with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Short on Time?

Use canned pears if fresh ones aren’t available—they work just as well!

9. Savory Pumpkin Risotto with Parmesan

This savory pumpkin risotto is another autumn-inspired dish that doesn’t require hours of stirring. Simply mix arborio rice, pumpkin purée, chicken broth, garlic, and onion in the slow cooker. In about 2 to 3 hours, you’ll have a creamy, flavorful risotto that tastes like a hug in a bowl. Stir in grated Parmesan and a dash of nutmeg for the final touch.

Make It Vegan:

Skip the Parmesan and use a plant-based broth for a vegan-friendly twist.

10. Balsamic Glazed Short Ribs

When you need a show-stopping dinner with minimal effort, balsamic glazed short ribs should be your go-to. The combination of balsamic vinegar, brown sugar, and garlic creates a rich glaze that coats slow-cooked beef short ribs perfectly. With 8 hours on low, the meat becomes fork-tender and pairs beautifully with creamy mashed potatoes or roasted root vegetables.

Why Fall is the Perfect Season for Slow Cookers

The slow cooker earns its keep year-round, but fall is when it truly shines. The season’s bounty of harvest vegetables, warm spices, and robust proteins lends itself perfectly to the type of meals the slow cooker does best. These ten recipes not only help you savor every bite of autumn’s flavors but also free up your time so you can enjoy the season’s other joys—apple picking, leaf peeping, or simply savoring a pumpkin spice latte.

A Few Pro Tips for Slow Cooking Success

  • Choose the Right Cut of Meat: Cuts like pork shoulder, chicken thighs, and beef chuck roast are ideal for slow-cooking because they become tender over time.
  • Avoid Overcrowding: For best results, fill your slow cooker about two-thirds full to allow for even cooking.
  • Don’t Peek Too Much: Lifting the lid releases heat and can add extra cooking time. Keep the lid sealed unless absolutely necessary.

With these slow cooker recipes, embracing autumn’s rich, comforting flavors has never been easier. From savory stews to decadent desserts, there’s something in here for every palate. Best of all, you can set it, forget it, and let your slow cooker do the heavy lifting—leaving you more time to enjoy everything else fall has to offer. Which recipe will you try first?
