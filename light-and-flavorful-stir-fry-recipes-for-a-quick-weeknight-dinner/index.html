Light and Flavorful Stir-Fry Recipes for a Quick Weeknight Dinner


When it comes to whipping up a quick and delicious weeknight dinner, stir-fry recipes are a game-changer. Not only are they incredibly versatile, but they also offer a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re a fan of chicken, beef, or tofu, there’s a stir-fry recipe out there that’s sure to tickle your taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll explore three light and flavorful stir-fry recipes that are perfect for a busy weeknight. Let’s dive in!

Tangy Orange Chicken Stir-Fry

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and cornstarch. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat.
  2. In another bowl, combine the orange juice, rice vinegar, honey, ginger, and garlic. Set aside.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned and cooked through.
  4. Add the bell pepper and snap peas to the skillet and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour the orange juice mixture over the chicken and vegetables. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens.
  6. Serve the tangy orange chicken stir-fry over cooked rice.

Sesame Ginger Beef Stir-Fry

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • Cooked noodles, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, ginger, and garlic. Add the sliced beef and let it marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the marinated beef and cook until browned. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add a bit more sesame oil if needed. Stir-fry the red onion, broccoli, and carrots until tender-crisp.
  4. Return the beef to the skillet and toss to combine with the vegetables.
  5. Serve the sesame ginger beef stir-fry over cooked noodles.

Tofu and Vegetable Stir-Fry with Peanut Sauce

Prep Time: 25 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 14 ounces firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • Cooked quinoa, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, peanut butter, rice vinegar, honey, and sriracha.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the cubed tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, stir-fry the red bell pepper, snow peas, and mushrooms until tender-crisp.
  4. Return the tofu to the skillet and pour the peanut sauce over the tofu and vegetables. Toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve the tofu and vegetable stir-fry over cooked quinoa.

These light and flavorful stir-fry recipes are not only quick and easy to make but also packed with vibrant flavors that are sure to satisfy your taste buds. Whether you’re in the mood for tangy orange chicken, sesame ginger beef, or a tofu and vegetable stir-fry with peanut sauce, these recipes are perfect for a busy weeknight. Give them a try and enjoy a delicious and satisfying meal in no time!
