How to Cook with Chickpeas: 7 Middle Eastern Recipes


Chickpeas, often referred to as “garbanzo beans,” are one of the world’s most versatile ingredients, especially in Middle Eastern cuisine. These humble legumes have been a staple for centuries, adding a hearty texture, nutty flavor, and a wealth of nutrients to countless dishes. Whether blended into creamy dips, simmered in fragrant stews, or baked into flour for delicate pastries, chickpeas are the star ingredient in some of the most iconic Middle Eastern recipes.

In this post, we’ll explore seven delicious ways to cook with chickpeas, with recipes that range from classic dishes to creative ideas that bring a modern twist to traditional Middle Eastern flavors. Each recipe highlights chickpeas’ ability to adapt to various techniques and flavor profiles, ensuring they’ll win a permanent spot in your pantry if they haven’t already. Let’s dive in.

1. Hummus: Creamy Chickpea Dip with a Twist

Hummus is synonymous with Middle Eastern cuisine, but why stop at the classic version? While traditional hummus has a smooth, creamy texture complemented with olive oil, tahini, garlic, and lemon juice, there’s room for some creative enhancements.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned or cooked chickpeas (peeled for extra creaminess)
  • 2–3 tablespoons tahini
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional toppings: roasted red pepper, paprika, caramelized onions, or fresh herbs

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the chickpeas, tahini, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, and salt until smooth. Add a few tablespoons of reserved chickpea liquid (or water) for the desired consistency.
  2. For a unique twist, divide the hummus into sections, adding roasted red peppers or caramelized onions in one half while keeping the other traditional. Serve drizzled with olive oil and sprinkled with paprika.
  3. Pair it with warm pita bread, fresh veggies, or even as a spread on sandwiches.

2. Falafel: A Crispy Chickpea Delight

Falafel is a beloved street food made from ground chickpeas or fava beans spiced with herbs and aromatics. Perfectly crispy on the outside and tender inside, it’s great in a pita pocket or as a crunchy snack on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • A handful of parsley and cilantro (roughly chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Neutral oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and blend them in a food processor with onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, coriander, cumin, baking soda, salt, and pepper until a coarse mixture forms.
  2. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the falafel until golden brown. Serve hot with tahini sauce, pickles, and fresh vegetables in a pita or on a bed of greens.

Pro tip: For a baked or air-fried version, lightly coat the falafel in olive oil before cooking to achieve a crisp exterior.

3. Chickpea and Lentil Soup (Harira)

Harira is a comforting, protein-packed Moroccan soup often enjoyed during Ramadan. Chickpeas join lentils and spices in this hearty meal that’s perfect for colder months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpeas (cooked or canned)
  • ½ cup red lentils
  • 1 small onion (diced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium carrot (diced)
  • 2 cups diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 4–5 cups vegetable broth
  • Fresh cilantro and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onions and garlic until fragrant. Add diced carrots and sauté for 3–5 minutes.
  2. Stir in tomatoes, cooked chickpeas, lentils, and spices. Cover with vegetable broth and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for about 25–30 minutes or until lentils are tender. Add water or extra broth if it gets too thick.
  4. Adjust seasoning to taste and serve with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice.

4. Baked Chickpea Kibbeh

Traditionally made with meat, bulgur, and spices, this vegetarian kibbeh recipe swaps the meat for mashed chickpeas. It’s a satisfying, plant-based alternative to the Middle Eastern classic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat (soaked in warm water for 20 minutes)
  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for brushing

Instructions:

  1. In a processor, blend the chickpeas, onion, garlic, and spices into a coarse paste.
  2. Add the soaked bulgur and mix thoroughly. Adjust seasoning.
  3. Spread the mixture into a greased baking dish or shape into patties on a parchment-lined sheet.
  4. Brush the top with olive oil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes or until golden brown. Serve with yogurt sauce or a cucumber-tomato salad.

5. Mujadara with Crispy Chickpeas

Mujadara is a fragrant dish of lentils and rice, topped with caramelized onions. Adding roasted chickpeas to the mix gives it a delightful crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • ¾ cup green or brown lentils (cooked)
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Olive oil and salt

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice and lentils separately, and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, caramelize the onions in olive oil with a pinch of salt, stirring until golden brown.
  3. Toss the cooked chickpeas with olive oil, cumin, coriander, and a dash of salt, then roast in a 400°F (200°C) oven for 15–20 minutes until crispy.
  4. Combine the lentils, rice, and caramelized onions. Top with the roasted chickpeas and serve warm.

6. Chickpea and Eggplant Stew (Maghmour)

Maghmour is a slow-cooked dish bursting with smoky flavors, featuring chickpeas and eggplant. It’s hearty enough to serve as a main dish yet light enough for a side.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 2 medium eggplants (cubed)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 cups diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Salt the cubed eggplant and let it sit for 15 minutes to draw out bitterness. Rinse and pat dry.
  2. Brown the eggplant cubes in olive oil until tender. Set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté onions and garlic until soft. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, smoked paprika, and a splash of water. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the chickpeas and eggplant. Cover and let the flavors meld for another 15–20 minutes. Adjust seasoning and serve with crusty bread or rice.

7. Chickpea Tahini Cookies

Did you know chickpeas can take center stage in dessert? These tahini-flavored cookies are lightly sweetened, nutty, and utterly irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas (mashed)
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • Optional: Chocolate chips or chopped nuts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the mashed chickpeas, tahini, maple syrup, vanilla, and baking powder until a smooth dough forms.
  3. Fold in chocolate chips or nuts if desired. Scoop onto the baking sheet and flatten slightly.
  4. Bake for 12–15 minutes or until edges are golden. Let cool before serving.

Final Thoughts

Cooking with chickpeas is an adventure in flavor, versatility, and nutrition. From creamy dips to crispy delights and even desserts, there’s no shortage of ways to incorporate this powerhouse legume into your meals. The recipes above offer a glimpse into the vibrant world of Middle Eastern cuisine, where chickpeas are cherished for their ability to transform simple ingredients into unforgettable dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, chickpeas are a staple worth exploring.
