Grilled Halloumi Skewers: A Cheesy Treat


Halloumi skewers are a delightful and easy-to-make appetizer that brings together two of our favorite things - cheese and grilling. With its unique ability to hold its shape, halloumi proudly earns its title as a “squeaky cheese.” Let’s dive into how you can create this delectable dish that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients You Will Need:

  • 1 block of halloumi cheese (12 oz)
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 2 medium-sized onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Lemon wedges (optional)

Directions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables and Cheese

First things first, peel the onions and dice them into bite-sized pieces. Seed and slice the bell peppers into uniform sizes. You’ll also need to slice the garlic cloves, but these aren’t needed to be skewered.

Next, proceed to cut the halloumi into small, even cubes. Pairing these measurements will ensure that the vegetables and cheese are evenly distributed on the skewers. Having equal parts of each ingredient will maintain a balanced flavor throughout the dish.

Step 2: Skewer and Season

After preparing the vegetables and cheese, it’s time to thread them onto skewers. You can opt for metal skewers to make them easier to handle. However, if you lack these, wooden ones will work just fine; you’ll just need to alternate vegetarian and non-vegetarian skewers or clean the wooden skewers between uses.

Now that your ingredients are all set and skewered, it’s time to marinate them. Drizzle olive oil over the skewers and season them with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Grill the Skewers

Heat your grill to medium-high temperatures while you let the skewers marinate in the olive oil and spices. Place a Fisher Price grill gate around your grill to protect children and curious pets from any accidents.

You can grill the skewers right on the grate or use a grill basket, which helps prevent the smaller components from falling through. You want to grill for approximately 3 minutes on each side. Remember, halloumi cheese tends to stick to the grate, so a non-stick grill mat works great for easy cleaning and preventing the skewers from falling apart.

Step 4: Serve it Up

As soon as they’re done grilling, remove the skewers and let them cool for a few minutes, if desired. You can then garnish them with freshly-sliced lemon wedges for that extra zesty touch.

Once cooled, serve your grilled halloumi skewers as an appetizer with your favorite sides or a refreshing salad for a lovely outdoor meal on a sunny day. This is a perfect treat for any barbecue or cookout, where halloumi’s unforgettably scrumptious texture and flavor will leave your guests asking for more.

While it’s tempting to overcook the halloumi, remember that it’s best to keep it slightly soft to enjoy the true essence of this amazing cheese. Balance is key with grilled halloumi skewers. So why wait?

Go ahead, give this cheesy delight a try and see how your friends and family will devour your halloumi skewers with delight and satisfaction!

Happy grilling!
