Paleo-Friendly Thai Beef Recipes: Spicy and Delicious


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine and follow a paleo diet, you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering and healthy Thai beef recipes that are both spicy and delicious. These recipes are packed with flavor and will satisfy your cravings while keeping you on track with your paleo lifestyle. Let’s dive right in!

Thai Beef Salad

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Total time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, torn
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, torn

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 small red chili, minced (adjust according to your spice preference)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the dressing ingredients until well combined. Set aside.
  2. Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the sliced flank steak for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the salad greens, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, cilantro, mint, and basil. Toss gently to mix.
  4. Add the sliced beef to the bowl and drizzle the dressing over the salad. Toss everything together until well coated.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing flavors of this Paleo Thai Beef Salad!

Thai Basil Beef Stir-Fry

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Total time: 25 minutes
Servings: 2-3

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sliced shallot to the skillet and sauté for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced beef to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until browned and cooked through.
  4. Push the beef to one side of the skillet and add the sliced bell peppers to the other side. Stir-fry the peppers for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, fish sauce, lime juice, honey, and red pepper flakes.
  6. Pour the sauce over the beef and bell peppers. Stir everything together to coat evenly.
  7. Remove the skillet from heat and add the Thai basil leaves. Toss gently until the leaves wilt slightly.
  8. Serve the Thai Basil Beef Stir-Fry hot with cauliflower rice or zucchini noodles for a complete paleo meal.

Thai Beef Curry

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 40 minutes
Total time: 55 minutes
Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the coconut oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pot and sauté until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the beef cubes to the pot and brown them on all sides.
  4. Stir in the Thai red curry paste and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk, beef broth, fish sauce, coconut aminos, lime juice, and honey. Stir well to combine.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the beef is tender.
  7. Add the sliced bell peppers and zucchini to the pot and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  8. Serve the Thai Beef Curry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. Pair it with cauliflower rice or enjoy it on its own for a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Final Thoughts

These paleo-friendly Thai beef recipes are a wonderful way to incorporate the bold and vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine into your paleo diet. Whether you prefer a refreshing salad, a quick stir-fry, or a comforting curry, these recipes have got you covered. So, grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy some spicy and delicious Thai beef dishes that will leave your taste buds wanting more!
