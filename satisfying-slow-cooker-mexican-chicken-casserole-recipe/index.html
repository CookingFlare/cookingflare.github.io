Satisfying Slow Cooker Mexican Chicken Casserole Recipe


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen preparing dinner? Look no further! This satisfying slow cooker Mexican chicken casserole recipe is here to save the day. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can have a delicious and flavorful meal on the table with minimal effort.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can (15 ounces) corn, drained
  • 1 can (10 ounces) diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies
  • 1 cup salsa
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Tortilla chips, for serving

Instructions

  1. Place the chicken breasts in the slow cooker and season with salt, pepper, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and onion powder. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated with the spices.

  2. Add the black beans, corn, diced tomatoes with green chilies, diced green chilies, salsa, and chicken broth to the slow cooker. Stir everything together to combine.

  3. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  4. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the slow cooker and mix it with the other ingredients.

  5. Sprinkle the shredded Mexican blend cheese over the top of the casserole. Cover the slow cooker again and let the cheese melt for about 10 minutes, or until it’s nice and bubbly.

  6. Serve the slow cooker Mexican chicken casserole hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. Pair it with tortilla chips for a satisfying crunch.

Tips

  • Customize your casserole by adding your favorite toppings such as sour cream, guacamole, or diced avocado.
  • If you prefer a spicier casserole, add some crushed red pepper flakes or a few dashes of hot sauce.
  • This recipe is perfect for meal prep. Store the leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days, or freeze them for up to 3 months.

Conclusion

This satisfying slow cooker Mexican chicken casserole recipe is a game-changer in the kitchen. It’s the perfect meal for busy weeknights when you don’t have much time to cook. By using the slow cooker, you can let the flavors meld together while you focus on other tasks. The result is a flavorful, tender, and satisfying meal that the whole family will love. So, grab your slow cooker and give this recipe a try. You’ll be amazed at how easy and delicious it is!
