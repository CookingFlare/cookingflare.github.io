Paleo-Friendly French Chicken Recipes: Classic Dishes Made Healthy


French cuisine is known for its rich flavors and indulgent dishes. However, if you’re following a Paleo diet, you might think that you have to miss out on these classic French chicken recipes. But fear not! With a few simple substitutions and adjustments, you can enjoy the delicious taste of French cuisine while staying true to your Paleo lifestyle. In this post, we’ll explore some Paleo-friendly French chicken recipes that will satisfy your cravings and keep you on track with your health goals. So, let’s dive in and discover these culinary delights!

Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a traditional French dish that combines chicken, red wine, and aromatic vegetables. To make it Paleo-friendly, we’ll replace the red wine with chicken broth or bone broth. This substitution still adds depth of flavor to the dish without compromising its nutritional value. Here’s how to make Paleo-friendly Coq au Vin:

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 slices of bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth or bone broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • Fresh thyme and rosemary, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the diced bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the skillet.
  2. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper, then brown them in the skillet with the bacon fat over medium heat. Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion, carrots, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Return the chicken and bacon to the skillet. Add the chicken broth, tomato paste, thyme, and rosemary. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cover.
  5. Let the dish simmer for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Serve hot with cauliflower rice or roasted vegetables.

Poulet à l’Estragon

Poulet à l’Estragon, or chicken with tarragon, is a classic French dish that features the distinct flavor of tarragon. To make it Paleo-friendly, we’ll use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. This substitution adds a creamy texture while keeping the dish dairy-free. Here’s how to make Paleo-friendly Poulet à l’Estragon:

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup coconut cream
  • 2 tablespoons fresh tarragon, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the skillet, skin-side down. Cook until the skin is crispy and golden brown, then flip the chicken and cook for another few minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and fragrant.
  4. Add the chicken broth and coconut cream to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  5. Return the chicken thighs to the skillet, along with any juices that have accumulated. Sprinkle the fresh tarragon over the chicken.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the dish simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  7. Serve hot with a side of steamed vegetables or a fresh salad.

Conclusion

With these Paleo-friendly French chicken recipes, you can enjoy the flavors of France while sticking to your dietary preferences. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests, these classic dishes made healthy will impress everyone at the table. So go ahead, embrace your inner chef, and savor the taste of French cuisine the Paleo way!

Remember, cooking is all about experimentation and making recipes your own. Feel free to adjust the ingredients and seasonings according to your taste and dietary needs. Bon appétit!
