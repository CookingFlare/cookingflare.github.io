How to Make Amazing Cinnamon Raisin Bread at Home


Few aromas can compare to the warm, comforting scent of cinnamon wafting through your kitchen. The combination of fragrant spices, tender bread, and bursts of sweetness from juicy raisins makes cinnamon raisin bread an irresistible treat that’s guaranteed to delight your taste buds. Whether you’re looking for the perfect breakfast loaf or a snack that feels like a hug in baked form, this homemade recipe will guide you through every step of creating the most amazing cinnamon raisin bread.

Why Make Cinnamon Raisin Bread at Home?

Homemade cinnamon raisin bread is far superior to the store-bought varieties for several reasons. First, you have complete control over the ingredients, so you can avoid preservatives and artificial flavors. Second, making fresh bread allows you to customize it according to your preferences — whether you want more raisins, a stronger cinnamon flavor, or even a gluten-free version. Most importantly, baking from scratch gives you that satisfying sense of accomplishment and fills your home with the rich aroma of warm spices and fresh bread dough.

If you’ve never baked bread before, don’t worry! This recipe is straightforward, beginner-friendly, and requires only basic ingredients. With patience and the right techniques, you’ll have a loaf of cinnamon raisin bread that rivals the offerings of any bakery.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before rolling up your sleeves, gather all the ingredients to ensure a smooth cooking process. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Bread Dough:

  • 3 ¾ cups (450g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm milk (around 110°F or 43°C)
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 4 tablespoons (60g) unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Cinnamon-Sugar Filling:

  • ⅓ cup (70g) packed light brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened

Add-Ins:

  • ⅔ cup (100g) raisins (use golden raisins for a sweeter taste if desired)
  • Optional: ½ cup chopped nuts (such as pecans or walnuts for added crunch)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Begin by activating your yeast — a crucial step in the bread-making process. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let the mixture sit for 5–10 minutes. This allows the yeast to bloom, forming a frothy layer on the surface. If the mixture doesn’t bubble, your yeast might be expired, so start over with fresh yeast.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center, then pour in the activated yeast mixture. Add the melted butter and egg to the bowl. Using a wooden spoon or spatula, begin mixing until the dough starts to come together. Once the mixture forms a shaggy dough, transfer it to a lightly floured surface.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Knead the dough by hand for about 8–10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Resist the urge to add too much additional flour — the dough should be soft but not sticky. If using a stand mixer, attach the dough hook and knead on medium speed for about 7 minutes. Once the dough reaches the desired consistency, gently fold the raisins into the dough, ensuring even distribution.

Step 4: Let the Dough Rise

Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, turning it to coat with oil. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and set it in a warm, draft-free spot to rise for 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until the dough has doubled in size.

Step 5: Prepare the Cinnamon-Sugar Filling

While the dough is rising, prepare the filling. In a small bowl, mix the brown sugar and cinnamon until well combined. Set aside the softened butter, as you’ll use it later when assembling the bread.

Step 6: Roll Out and Shape the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release the air. Transfer it to a floured surface and roll it out into a rectangle, approximately 9x14 inches. Spread the softened butter evenly across the surface of the dough. Sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture over the butter, leaving a slight border around the edges.

Starting from the shorter end, tightly roll the dough into a log. Pinch the seams closed to seal the roll.

Step 7: Second Rise

Place the rolled dough seam-side down into a lightly greased 9x5-inch loaf pan. Cover the loaf loosely and let it rise for another 30–45 minutes. During this time, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

Step 8: Bake the Bread

Once the dough has risen again, bake it in the preheated oven for 30–35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped. If the bread is browning too quickly, tent a piece of foil over it for the last 10 minutes of baking.

Step 9: Cool and Slice

Remove the bread from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for about 5 minutes. Then transfer the loaf to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling is essential to allow the loaf to set properly — slicing while hot may cause the bread to crumble.

Pro Tips for Perfect Cinnamon Raisin Bread

  • Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure your egg, butter, and other ingredients are at room temperature for better incorporation and a smoother dough consistency.
  • Soak Your Raisins: If you’re using dried raisins, soak them in warm water for 15 minutes before adding them to the dough. This makes them plump and juicy, preventing them from drying out during baking.
  • Don’t Overpack the Filling: While tempting, avoid adding too much cinnamon-sugar filling, as it might spill out during baking and make the bread messy.
  • Use Bread Flour: For a chewier texture, substitute all-purpose flour with bread flour. This provides additional gluten structure to support the roll.

How to Serve Cinnamon Raisin Bread

Cinnamon raisin bread is versatile and can be enjoyed anytime during the day. Here are some serving suggestions:

  1. Classic Toast: Slice the bread and toast it lightly. Add a pat of butter or a spoonful of jam for a simple breakfast treat.
  2. French Toast: This bread makes the most indulgent French toast. Dip slices in a mixture of eggs, milk, and vanilla extract before cooking them on a skillet.
  3. Bread Pudding: Stale cinnamon raisin bread can be transformed into a rich and decadent bread pudding — perfect for dessert lovers.
  4. Tea-Time Snack: Pair your bread with a hot cup of tea or coffee for an afternoon pick-me-up.

Storage Tips

To keep your bread fresh, store it in an airtight container or a tightly wrapped plastic bag at room temperature. Homemade bread doesn’t have preservatives, so it’s best consumed within 2–3 days. For longer storage, slice the loaf and freeze individual pieces in airtight freezer bags. Simply thaw and toast when ready to eat.

Conclusion

Making cinnamon raisin bread at home is not only rewarding but also ensures you’re indulging in a wholesome treat crafted from scratch. With its buttery-soft texture, sweet bursts of raisins, and spicy notes of cinnamon, this bread will quickly become a household favorite. Whether you’re baking it for yourself, your family, or a gathering of friends, this recipe promises a loaf that’s irresistibly delicious every time.

Step into your kitchen with confidence, follow these steps, and be prepared to impress everyone with your baking skills. Nothing says “home-cooked comfort” better than a warm, aromatic loaf of cinnamon raisin bread!
