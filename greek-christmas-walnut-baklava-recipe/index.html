Greek Christmas Walnut Baklava Recipe


Baklava is a timeless dessert that bridges the gap between tradition and indulgence, and the Greek version of this layered pastry takes on an even more magical dimension during the holiday season. Unlike its year-round counterparts, the festive Christmas Walnut Baklava incorporates seasonal spices, richer textures, and heartwarming flavors that make it a centerpiece of any holiday gathering.

The origins of baklava trace back centuries, with Greece being one of the regions that has perfected its preparation. Combining thin layers of phyllo pastry with sweetened nuts and aromatic spices, it’s finished off with a syrupy drizzle that permeates every flaky layer. For Christmas, the addition of cinnamon, cloves, and a hint of orange zest sets this specialty version apart from its traditional counterparts.

Below, we guide you through the step-by-step preparation of a Greek Christmas Walnut Baklava—the perfect treat to serve up while celebrating with family and friends.

Ingredients

For the Baklava:

  • Phyllo pastry sheets: 1 pound (thawed, if frozen)
  • Unsalted butter: 1 cup, melted
  • Walnuts: 3 cups, finely chopped
  • Ground cinnamon: 2 teaspoons
  • Ground cloves: 1 teaspoon
  • Granulated sugar: ¼ cup

For the Syrup:

  • Water: 1 cup
  • Granulated sugar: 2 cups
  • Honey: ½ cup
  • Orange zest: Zest from 1 orange
  • Fresh lemon juice: 2 tablespoons
  • Cinnamon stick: 1

Optional Garnish:

  • Crushed pistachios: ¼ cup
  • Ground nutmeg: A dash

Step-by-Step Recipe

Preparing the Syrup

The syrup is a vital component, infusing the baklava with sweetness and holiday flavor. Start with this step because it needs time to cool before being poured onto the freshly baked dessert.

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine water, sugar, honey, orange zest, lemon juice, and the cinnamon stick. Stir gently to mix.
  2. Heat over medium heat, bringing the mixture to a boil. After boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and allow the syrup to cool completely. Discard the cinnamon stick after the infusion is done.

Preparing the Nut Mixture

The rich blend of walnuts and spices provides the heart of this dessert, delivering the warmth and crunch synonymous with holiday treats.

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the finely chopped walnuts with cinnamon, ground cloves, and sugar. Stir well to ensure an even distribution of the spices.
  2. Taste a small pinch, adjusting the spice levels if necessary to suit your preferences.

Assembling the Baklava

Baklava is all about layers—and the holiday variation makes those layers even more flavorful with the nut mixture sandwiched between sheets of delicate phyllo.

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish to prevent sticking.
  2. Trim the phyllo sheets to fit your pan, as needed. Cover unused phyllo with a damp cloth while working to prevent them from drying out.
  3. Lay one sheet of phyllo on the bottom of the dish and brush with melted butter. Repeat this process with about 10 sheets to form the base.
  4. Sprinkle a generous portion of the nut mixture evenly over the phyllo base.
  5. Add another layer of phyllo sheets, brushing each one lightly with melted butter. Repeat, alternating with layers of the nut mixture, until you run out of nuts, topping the dish with an additional 10 sheets of buttered phyllo to create the final crust.
  6. Using a sharp knife, score the assembled baklava into diamond or square shapes. Be sure to cut through all the layers to make serving easier once baked.

Baking the Baklava

  1. Place the baking dish into the preheated oven and bake for about 50–60 minutes, or until the top layer is crisp and golden brown.
  2. Remove the baklava from the oven and let it sit for 5 minutes.

Adding the Syrup

The moment the aroma of baked baklava fills your home is a tantalizing one. To elevate its decadence, the cooled syrup is poured over the warm pastry.

  1. Carefully pour the cooled syrup evenly over the baklava, ensuring every piece is soaked.
  2. Allow the baklava to rest for several hours, or overnight, to fully absorb the syrup. This step ensures that each bite is rich and moist.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Christmas Walnut Baklava is best served slightly chilled or at room temperature. Arrange your diamond-shaped portions on a festive platter and sprinkle with crushed pistachios for added color and crunch. A light dusting of nutmeg is optional but adds another dimension of holiday spice.

Pair it with traditional Greek coffee or a steaming mug of mulled wine to complement the dessert’s complex flavors. It also makes an excellent gift for your loved ones—packaged neatly in holiday tins or decorative boxes.

Tips for the Perfect Baklava

  1. Quality Ingredients: The flavor of baklava largely depends on the quality of the nuts, butter, and spices. Opt for fresh walnuts and unsalted butter.
  2. Layering Precision: Take care while layering phyllo sheets; uneven layering can affect the texture.
  3. Syrup Timing: Pouring cool syrup onto hot baklava ensures it absorbs evenly without making the pastry soggy.
  4. Flavor Enhancements: Experiment with other additions like cardamom, vanilla extract, or lemon zest to tailor the taste.

Why Greek Walnut Baklava Belongs on Your Christmas Table

This dessert stands out not just for its exquisite taste but also for its role as a festive tradition. The warming spices and sugary syrup mirror the celebratory tones of the season, while the crunchy and flaky layers bring loved ones together. Making baklava is an act of patience and craftsmanship—perfectly aligned with the spirit of giving during Christmas.

Whether you’re a seasoned baker or trying your hand at this for the first time, a plate of beautifully crafted baklava will never fail to impress. Its ability to combine cultural heritage with universal appeal makes it an excellent dessert to share.

With its understated elegance and mouthwatering taste, Greek Christmas Walnut Baklava is more than a dish—it’s an experience. Whether you’re serving it for a holiday feast or as a cozy indulgence on a frosty evening, this dessert is sure to spread cheer and warmth throughout your home. By embracing the flavors and traditions of Greece, you’re creating a connection with history and a heartfelt moment of celebration that guests and family alike will appreciate. Happy holidays, and may your baklava be as sweet and memorable as the season itself!
