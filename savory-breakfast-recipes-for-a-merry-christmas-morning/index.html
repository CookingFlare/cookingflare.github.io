Savory Breakfast Recipes for a Merry Christmas Morning


Christmas morning is a time of joy, laughter, and of course, delicious food! As you gather with family and friends, nothing sets the stage for a festive day quite like a savory breakfast spread. This year, elevate your holiday brunch with a selection of mouth-watering, savory recipes that will delight everyone’s taste buds. From hearty casseroles to flavorful frittatas, these dishes will not only fill your kitchen with enticing aromas but also create lasting memories around the breakfast table.

1. Christmas Morning Breakfast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of crusty bread, cubed
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Gruyère)
  • 1 pound breakfast sausage, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers (red and green)
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the base: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the cubed bread with the cooked sausage, bell peppers, and onion. Toss well to distribute the ingredients evenly.

  2. Mix the custard: In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, oregano, salt, and pepper. Pour this mixture over the bread and sausage mixture. Gently fold everything together until the bread is soaked in the egg mixture.

  3. Add cheese: Fold in the shredded cheese, reserving a bit for topping.

  4. Bake: Transfer the mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Sprinkle the reserved cheese on top. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set.

  5. Serve: Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve warm.

This casserole is perfect for feeding a crowd and can be assembled the night before, making your Christmas morning stress-free!

2. Savory Spinach and Feta Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Sauté vegetables: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced red onion and sauté until translucent. Then, add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted.

  3. Prepare egg mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Pour this mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet.

  4. Add toppings: Sprinkle the crumbled feta and halved cherry tomatoes over the top of the egg mixture.

  5. Bake: Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the frittata is set and lightly golden on top.

  6. Slice and serve: Allow to cool for a few minutes before slicing. This frittata can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, making it an excellent make-ahead option.

3. Herb and Cheese Savory Scones

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or your choice)
  • 1/4 cup fresh herbs (chives, parsley, or thyme), chopped
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  3. Cut in butter: Add the cold butter to the flour mixture and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work it in until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Add cheese and herbs: Stir in the shredded cheese and chopped herbs.

  5. Combine wet ingredients: Slowly add the milk to the dry mixture, stirring until just combined. Do not overmix; the dough should be slightly sticky.

  6. Shape scones: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Pat it into a circle about 1 inch thick. Cut into wedges or use a biscuit cutter to form rounds.

  7. Bake: Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

  8. Enjoy: Serve warm with butter or your favorite jam for a delightful breakfast treat.

4. Smoked Salmon and Avocado Toast

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of whole-grain bread
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 4 ounces smoked salmon
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill or chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread: Begin by toasting the slices of bread until golden and crispy.

  2. Prepare the avocado: In a bowl, mash the avocado with lemon juice, salt, and pepper until creamy.

  3. Assemble the toast: Spread the avocado mixture generously over each slice of toasted bread. Top with slices of smoked salmon and sprinkle with capers.

  4. Garnish: Finish with fresh dill or chives for an extra burst of flavor.

This dish is not only visually stunning but also packed with healthy fats and proteins, making it a fantastic option for a nourishing breakfast.

5. Potato and Leek Hash

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, diced
  • 2 leeks, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large eggs (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the potatoes: In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced potatoes and cook until they begin to soften and brown, about 10-15 minutes.

  2. Add leeks and peppers: Stir in the sliced leeks and diced bell pepper. Season with salt and pepper. Continue to cook until the vegetables are tender and the potatoes are crispy, about 10 more minutes.

  3. Fry eggs (optional): If you’d like to add eggs, push the hash to the sides of the skillet and crack the eggs into the center. Cover the skillet and cook until the eggs are set to your liking.

  4. Serve: Garnish the hash with fresh parsley and serve hot. This hearty dish is perfect for a filling breakfast that will keep everyone satisfied.

Wrapping Up

Christmas morning is a special time filled with love and laughter, and what better way to celebrate than with a savory breakfast that warms the heart and soul? Each of these recipes offers a unique flavor profile that can accommodate various tastes and preferences. Whether you choose to make a comforting casserole, a light frittata, flaky scones, elegant avocado toast, or a hearty hash, your Christmas morning will be a deliciously memorable experience.

As you gather around the table, sharing stories and laughter, these savory breakfast recipes will create the perfect backdrop for a joyful celebration. By preparing these dishes, you’re not just feeding your loved ones; you’re crafting moments that will be cherished for years to come. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to whip up some holiday magic in the kitchen this Christmas!
