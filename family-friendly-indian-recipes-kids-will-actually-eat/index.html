Family-Friendly Indian Recipes Kids Will Actually Eat


Indian cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and diverse regional specialties. While many Indian dishes are often considered too complex or spicy for kids, there are numerous family-friendly recipes that can be easily adapted to suit young palates. In this post, we'll explore some delicious and kid-friendly Indian recipes that are sure to become a hit with your little ones. Recipe 1: Chicken Tikka Pizza Bake Inspired by the classic Indian dish Chicken Tikka Masala, this recipe is a fun twist on traditional pizza night. The creamy tomato sauce and crispy crust will have kids asking for more:

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1/2 cup tomato sauce 1/2 cup diced cooked chicken 1 tsp garam masala 1/2 tsp cumin powder Salt and pepper, to taste 1 egg, beaten Cooking oil, for brushing

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Mix the flour and spices, then add the cheeses and mix until a dough forms. Divide the dough into 2-3 portions and roll out each one into a thin circle. Brush the dough with cooking oil and top with tomato sauce, chicken, and a sprinkle of garam masala. Roll up the dough and place seam-side down in a baking dish. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown. Recipe 2: Vegetable Samosas Samosas are a popular Indian snack that can be enjoyed as an appetizer or side dish. This vegan-friendly recipe uses a simple mixture of vegetables and spices wrapped in a flaky pastry crust:

1 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 cup ghee or oil 1/2 cup water 1 tsp cumin powder 1 tsp coriander powder 1/2 tsp garam masala 1/2 tsp salt 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, and corn)

Mix the flour and spices, then add the ghee or oil and water to form a dough. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, until smooth and pliable. Divide the dough into 8-10 portions and roll out each one into a thin circle. Mix the vegetables and spices, then place a tablespoon of the mixture in the center of each pastry circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form a triangle or a cone shape, and press the edges to seal. Brush the tops with a little water and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown. Recipe 3: Quinoa and Sweet Potato Biryani Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice-based dish that's a staple in Indian cuisine. This vegetarian recipe uses quinoa and sweet potatoes to create a nutritious and satisfying meal:

1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained 2 cups water 1 large sweet potato, peeled and diced 1 tsp cumin seeds 1 tsp coriander seeds 1/2 tsp garam masala 1/2 tsp salt 2 tbsp coconut oil Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Cook the quinoa according to package instructions. In a separate pan, heat the oil and sauté the cumin and coriander seeds until fragrant. Add the sweet potatoes and cook for 5-7 minutes, until slightly tender. Mix the cooked quinoa, sweet potatoes, and spices, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the quinoa is coated in the flavorful mixture. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves. Recipe 4: Chili Cheese Naan Bread Naan bread is a staple in Indian cuisine, but traditional recipes can be quite time-consuming. This simplified recipe uses store-bought naan bread to create a fun and flavorful snack:

1 package of naan bread 1 cup chili cheese dip or salsa 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 1 tsp garam masala 1 tsp cumin powder Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread a layer of chili cheese dip or salsa on each naan bread, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese and mix the garam masala and cumin powder. Bake for 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Serve hot and enjoy! These family-friendly Indian recipes offer a taste of authentic Indian cuisine with a kid-friendly twist. From the creamy Chicken Tikka Pizza Bake to the crispy Vegetable Samosas, there's something for everyone in this collection. Experiment with these recipes and adapt them to suit your family's tastes and preferences. Happy cooking!
