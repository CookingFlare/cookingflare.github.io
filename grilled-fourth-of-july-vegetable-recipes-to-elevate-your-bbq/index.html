Grilled Fourth of July Vegetable Recipes to Elevate Your BBQ


Summer is in full swing, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and celebrate Independence Day with a flavorful and vibrant BBQ feast. While burgers and hot dogs often steal the spotlight, don’t forget about the incredible potential of grilled vegetables. Elevate your Fourth of July BBQ with these mouthwatering grilled vegetable recipes that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Grilled Corn with Chili Lime Butter

There’s something undeniably delicious about biting into a juicy ear of corn straight off the grill. This simple yet flavorful recipe takes grilled corn to the next level. Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. Peel back the husks of the corn, leaving them attached at the base. Remove the silk from the corn and fold the husks back over the ears. Soak the corn in water for about 10 minutes before grilling.

Once the grill is ready, place the corn on the grates and grill for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred. While the corn is grilling, prepare the chili lime butter by combining softened butter, lime juice, chili powder, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Mix well.

When the corn is ready, carefully peel back the husks and brush the chili lime butter all over the corn. Sprinkle with a dash of additional chili powder for an extra kick of flavor. Serve immediately and watch as your guests devour this irresistible summer side dish.

Grilled Zucchini and Squash Skewers

Take advantage of the abundance of summer squash with this colorful and tasty grilled vegetable skewer recipe. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Cut zucchini and yellow squash into thick slices, making sure they are similar in size to ensure even cooking. Toss the sliced squash in a bowl with olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.

Thread the squash slices onto skewers, alternating between zucchini and yellow squash. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the squash is tender and lightly charred.

To add an extra layer of flavor, brush the skewers with a balsamic glaze during the last few minutes of grilling. The tangy sweetness of the glaze pairs perfectly with the smoky grilled vegetables. Serve these vibrant skewers as a side dish or as a vegetarian main course option at your Fourth of July BBQ.

Grilled Stuffed Bell Peppers

Impress your guests with these show-stopping grilled stuffed bell peppers. Start by cutting the tops off the bell peppers and removing the seeds and membranes. In a bowl, mix together cooked quinoa, diced tomatoes, black beans, corn, diced red onion, minced garlic, chopped fresh cilantro, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.

Spoon the quinoa mixture into the hollowed-out bell peppers, packing it tightly. Place the stuffed bell peppers on the grill over medium heat and cook for about 15-20 minutes, until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.

For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle shredded cheese on top of the stuffed peppers during the last few minutes of grilling. The cheese will melt and create a deliciously gooey topping. Serve these grilled stuffed bell peppers as a main course or as a colorful and satisfying side dish.

Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Garlic Butter

Asparagus is a classic grilling vegetable that always delivers incredible taste. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Trim the tough ends off the asparagus spears and toss them in a bowl with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.

Place the asparagus spears on the grill and cook for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are tender and lightly charred. While the asparagus is grilling, prepare the lemon garlic butter by melting butter in a small saucepan and adding freshly squeezed lemon juice and minced garlic. Stir until well combined.

Once the asparagus is done, drizzle the lemon garlic butter over the spears and serve immediately. The bright citrusy flavors of the butter complement the smoky grilled asparagus, making it a standout side dish for your Fourth of July BBQ.

Conclusion

This Fourth of July, take your BBQ game to a whole new level with these grilled vegetable recipes. From the sweet and spicy grilled corn with chili lime butter to the vibrant and flavorful grilled stuffed bell peppers, these recipes are sure to impress. Don’t forget to pair your grilled veggies with your favorite meats and enjoy the colorful and delicious feast with your loved ones. Happy grilling and happy Independence Day!
