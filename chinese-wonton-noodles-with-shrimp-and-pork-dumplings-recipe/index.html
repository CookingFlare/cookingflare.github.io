Chinese Wonton Noodles with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings Recipe


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’re in for a treat! Today, I’m going to share with you an incredible recipe for Chinese Wonton Noodles with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings. This dish is bursting with flavors and textures that will leave your taste buds wanting more. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the dumplings:

  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 30-40 wonton wrappers

For the noodles:

  • 200g Chinese egg noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup bok choy, chopped
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions

Making the Dumplings

  1. In a food processor, combine the shrimp, ground pork, minced garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, grated ginger, sugar, salt, and white pepper. Pulse until well combined and the mixture becomes slightly sticky.
  2. Place a wonton wrapper on a clean surface. Spoon about 1 teaspoon of the filling onto the center of the wrapper. Wet the edges of the wrapper with water, then fold it in half to form a triangle. Press the edges firmly to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Carefully drop the dumplings into the boiling water and cook for about 5 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Remove the dumplings from the water with a slotted spoon and set aside.

Preparing the Noodles

  1. Cook the Chinese egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and stir-fry for another minute.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the noodles and stir to coat evenly.
  5. Add the chicken broth to the pan and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until the noodles have absorbed some of the broth.
  6. Add the bok choy and green onions to the pan and cook for another 2 minutes, until the bok choy is wilted.

Serving

  1. Divide the noodles among serving bowls. Top each bowl with several dumplings.
  2. Garnish with additional chopped green onions, if desired.
  3. Serve hot and enjoy!

Final Thoughts

Chinese Wonton Noodles with Shrimp and Pork Dumplings is a complete meal in itself. The combination of tender shrimp and pork dumplings with flavorful noodles and vegetables creates a dish that is both comforting and satisfying. The contrast of textures and the umami-rich sauce make every bite a delight.

This recipe allows for some flexibility, so feel free to customize it to your taste. You can add other vegetables like carrots or mushrooms, or even experiment with different sauces to create your own unique twist.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of China, give this recipe a try. It’s a crowd-pleaser that will transport you to the bustling streets of Beijing or Shanghai. Enjoy the delicious flavors and the joy of cooking a restaurant-quality dish right in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!

Note: Remember to adjust the ingredients and measurements according to your desired serving size.
