Garlic Butter Shrimp Scampi: A Quick and Elegant Pasta Dish


If you’re looking for a delicious and elegant pasta dish that’s quick and easy to prepare, look no further than garlic butter shrimp scampi. This classic Italian-American dish is bursting with flavor, combining succulent shrimp, fragrant garlic, and rich butter, all tossed together with al dente pasta. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a comforting yet sophisticated meal, garlic butter shrimp scampi is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create this delightful dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 ounces of linguine or spaghetti
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 cup of dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Shrimp: Begin by patting the shrimp dry with paper towels and seasoning them with a pinch of salt and black pepper.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the linguine or spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Once cooked, drain the pasta and set it aside.

  3. Sauté the Shrimp: In a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

  4. Prepare the Sauce: In the same skillet, add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes. Sauté the garlic for about a minute until it becomes fragrant, being careful not to let it brown.

  5. Deglaze the Skillet: Pour the white wine into the skillet, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Allow the wine to simmer for 2-3 minutes, reducing slightly.

  6. Bring It All Together: Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet, then add the cooked pasta and lemon juice. Toss everything together until the shrimp and pasta are evenly coated with the flavorful sauce.

  7. Finish and Serve: Season the shrimp scampi with additional salt and pepper to taste, then sprinkle the chopped parsley over the dish. Toss once more to combine. Serve the garlic butter shrimp scampi in individual bowls, garnished with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

Enjoy Your Creation

Now that you’ve mastered the art of preparing garlic butter shrimp scampi, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. Whether you’re enjoying this dish as a weekday indulgence or as the star of a special dinner, its delicate flavors and luxurious textures are sure to leave a lasting impression. Pair it with a crisp white wine and perhaps a side of crusty bread to soak up the remaining sauce.

This recipe is a testament to the fact that elegance and simplicity can go hand in hand in the kitchen. The marriage of garlic, butter, and perfectly cooked shrimp is a timeless combination that never fails to delight the senses. So, gather your ingredients, don your apron, and embark on a culinary journey that celebrates the wonders of garlic butter shrimp scampi.
