How to Make Thai Mango Sticky Rice: A Sweet Treat


Thai Mango Sticky Rice, or “Khao Niew Mamuang,” is one of Thailand’s most beloved desserts. It’s a harmonious blend of sweet, creamy, and slightly salty flavors with tender mango slices alongside sticky rice soaked in a luscious coconut sauce. Perfect for summer days or as a treat after any meal, this dish is as easy to make as it is delicious. In this blog post, we will take you step by step through the process of preparing this flavorful dessert in your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the preparation process, let’s cover what you’ll need to make this recipe a success. As with any classic dessert, the magic lies in using fresh ingredients and assembling them with care. Here’s everything you’ll need:

For the Sticky Rice:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (also called sticky rice; available at Asian grocery stores)
  • Water (enough to soak and steam the rice)

For the Coconut Sauce:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For Topping (Optional):

  • 1 tablespoon roasted sesame seeds or mung beans

For Serving:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced

If you don’t have all the ingredients in your pantry, substitutions can be used in some cases. For instance, coconut cream can be used for a richer texture, and other fruits like papaya or pineapple can substitute mangoes for a tropical twist.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Sticky Rice

Sticky rice is the foundation of this dish, and getting it right is crucial. To make perfect sticky rice, follow these steps:

  1. Rinse the Rice: Start by rinsing the glutinous rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming overly gummy.

  2. Soak: Place the rinsed rice in a bowl and cover it with water. Allow it to soak for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results. Soaking softens the grains and ensures they cook evenly.

  3. Steam: Once soaked, drain the water. Use a steamer or a bamboo basket lined with cheesecloth to steam the rice. Steam for 25–30 minutes, checking periodically to ensure it’s evenly cooked.

Step 2: Make the Coconut Sauce

While the rice is cooking, prepare the coconut sauce that will infuse the sticky rice with its signature flavor.

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, sugar, and salt over medium heat. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Simmer: Allow the mixture to come to a gentle simmer. Be careful not to boil—boiling can cause the coconut milk to separate and affect texture.

  3. Set Aside: Once the sauce has thickened slightly, remove it from the heat and keep it warm.

Step 3: Combine the Rice with Coconut Sauce

Now that the rice and sauce are ready, it’s time to combine the two:

  1. Transfer the Rice: Place the cooked sticky rice in a large bowl.

  2. Pour the Coconut Sauce: Gradually add three-quarters of the coconut sauce over the rice while it’s still warm. The warmth helps the sauce absorb into the grains. Gently fold the rice into the sauce using a spatula or spoon.

  3. Rest: Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and let the rice sit for at least 15 minutes so it can fully absorb the coconut sauce.

Step 4: Prepare the Mangoes

The mango is the star of the dish, adding freshness and fruity sweetness. Here’s how to prepare it:

  1. Peel and Slice: Carefully peel the mangoes. Slice them in halves, removing the seed. Then cut the mango halves into thin, even slices or cubes, depending on your preference.

  2. Arrangement: Arrange the sliced mangoes on a plate in an appealing way next to the sticky rice. Presentation is key!

Step 5: Assemble and Serve

To serve Thai Mango Sticky Rice, follow these steps for a restaurant-worthy presentation:

  1. Plate the Rice: Scoop a portion of the sticky rice onto a plate using a mold or an ice cream scoop for a polished touch.

  2. Add Mango: Place a generous amount of mango slices beside the rice.

  3. Drizzle the Sauce: Drizzle the remaining coconut sauce over the rice and mango slices for an extra infusion of flavor.

  4. Garnish: Sprinkle roasted sesame seeds or mung beans on top for texture and a touch of nuttiness.

Tips for Perfect Thai Mango Sticky Rice

  • Choose the Right Mangoes: Look for sweet, ripe mangoes with golden skin and a fragrant aroma. Varieties like Ataulfo, Nam Dok Mai, or Keo Savoy are ideal.

  • Adjust Sweetness: The balance of sweetness varies from person to person. Taste the sauce while cooking and adjust the sugar to your liking.

  • Keep Coconut Milk Warm: Warm coconut sauce blends better with the sticky rice, so avoid letting it cool before combining.

Variations to Try

While traditional mango sticky rice is perfection in itself, you can experiment with creative variations:

  • Fruit Medley: Combine mango with other fruits like kiwi, dragon fruit, or strawberries for a colorful platter.

  • Chocolate Drizzle: Add a light drizzle of melted chocolate over the mango slices for an exotic fusion.

  • Pandan Flavor: Infuse pandan leaves in the coconut sauce for a fragrant twist.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, store the sticky rice and mango separately in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. To reheat sticky rice, steam it again or warm it in the microwave with a splash of coconut sauce to restore its moisture. Mangoes are best enjoyed fresh, so slice them just before serving.

A Sweet Conclusion

Thai Mango Sticky Rice is more than just a dessert—it’s a cultural experience and a celebration of flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or just starting your culinary journey, mastering this recipe is sure to impress friends and family alike. With natural sweetness from the mangoes, creamy richness from the coconut sauce, and the texture of tender sticky rice, this dish brings the authentic essence of Thai cuisine straight to your table.

So gather your ingredients and give it a try—your taste buds will thank you!
