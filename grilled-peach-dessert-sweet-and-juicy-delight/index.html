Grilled Peach Dessert: Sweet and Juicy Delight


Summer time calls for refreshing desserts that taste as good as they look. Have you ever thought of grilling your peaches for a dessert? It’s a refreshing twist on the classic fruit dessert, highlighting the natural sweetness and juiciness of the peaches. In this post, we will share a unique way to prepare grilled peaches, and pair them with other delicious ingredients to create a truly delightful summer dessert.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe yet firm peaches
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 lemon
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 teaspoons honey
  • 4 scoops vanilla ice cream or frozen yogurt

Instructions

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill or a griddle to medium-high heat.

  2. While the grill is heating up, prepare the peaches. Cut them in half, removing the pits. Then, using a soft-bristled toothbrush or sponge, wash the peaches under running water.

  3. In a bowl, mix together the brown sugar and ground cinnamon. Dip the peach halves into the cinnamon-sugar mixture so that they are evenly coated.

  4. Use a lemon and lightly dust each peach half with lemon juice to help the sugar stick better and to prevent the peaches from browning too much.

  5. Once the grill is heated, melt 1 tablespoon of butter onto a piece of parchment paper. Place the peach halves, cut side down, onto the parchment paper. This will help prevent the peaches from sticking to the grill or griddle.

  6. Put the peaches on the grill or griddle, cut side down, and cook them for about 3-4 minutes, until they are caramelized and tender. Carefully flip the peaches and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until they are nicely grill-marked and cooked through. Be cautious, as the peaches will be delicate and may fall apart during the flipping process.

  7. Remove the peaches from the grill and gently brush off any excess cinnamon-sugar mixture.

  8. Drizzle each peach half with 1 teaspoon of honey.

  9. Serve the grilled peaches immediately with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or frozen yogurt. The warmth of the peaches will cause the ice cream to slightly melt, creating a luscious and flavorful dessert.

Substitutions and Additions

You can experiment with different toppings, sauces, and flavors to customize this dessert to your taste preferences. Consider adding a dollop of whipped cream, crumbled graham crackers, or even a drizzle of chocolate sauce for an extra touch of sweetness.

If you’re looking for more of a tart contrast, try pairing the grilled peaches with a tart fruit sauce, such as a raspberry or blueberry sauce, or a scoop of sorbet.

Remember that learning to trust your taste buds and experimenting in the kitchen is half the fun of cooking. Get creative and find what works best for you and your guests!

Conclusion

Grilled peaches may seem like a simple dessert, but their warm and caramelized sweetness, paired with cool and creamy ice cream, creates a delightful summertime treat. It’s a delightful surprise for family and friends, and a unique twist on the classic fruit-based dessert. So, fire up your grill and treat yourself to this sweet and juicy delight!
