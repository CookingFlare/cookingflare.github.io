Sweet and Tangy: Homemade Mayo Recipes for All


Have you ever wondered how to make homemade mayo? Or perhaps you’re tired of store-bought versions filled with preservatives and unwanted ingredients? Whatever your reason, I am thrilled to share three divine homemade mayo recipes that fit your needs and preferences.

Before jumping into recipes, let me share a few tips and tricks that will ensure success every single time.

** Ingredient 1: Egg Yolks**

Mayonnaise is essentially emulsified egg yolks, oil, and an acidic ingredient. To make it at home, you’ll need egg yolks, as they act as the base for this creamy sauce.

** Ingredient 2: Acid**

To help emulsify the oil and egg yolks, you’ll need an acid like vinegar or lemon juice. Choose your preferred one, but always remember to use the best quality you can find.

** Ingredient 3: Oil**

A good-quality oil is crucial for homemade mayo. Extra virgin olive oil, avocado oil, or a neutral oil like canola or grapeseed oil yields the best results. Make sure your oil is at room temperature to make blending easier.

Emulsifying Technique

When making homemade mayo, it’s essential to add the acid slowly while continuously blending, as this helps emulsify the ingredients. Easier said than done? Don’t worry - my recipes will guide you every step of the way.

Recipe 1: Classic Homemade Mayonnaise

Ingredients:

  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup neutral oil (such as vegetable or grapeseed oil)
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Begin by beating the egg yolks in a mixing bowl. Add the vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and mix until combined.
  2. Combine the neutral and extra-virgin olive oil in a separate container.
  3. While continuously blending at a low speed, slowly pour the oil mixture into the egg yolk mixture. You must do this gradually. Trust me, patience pays off.
  4. After all the oil has been incorporated, taste and adjust seasonings if needed.

Recipe 2: Spicy Homemade Mayonnaise

Ingredients:

  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes (to taste)
  • 1 cup neutral oil (such as vegetable or grapeseed oil)
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Instructions:

The same as the Classic Homemade Mayonnaise, except add the cayenne or red pepper flakes during the initial seasoning stage. Adjust the heat according to your preference.

Recipe 3: Sweet Potato and Roasted Garlic Homemade Mayonnaise

Ingredients:

  • 2 egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sweet potato puree
  • 1 small roasted garlic clove (or to taste)
  • 1 cup neutral oil (such as vegetable or grapeseed oil)
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Instructions:

The same as Classic Homemade Mayonnaise, except add the sweet potato puree and roasted garlic during the initial seasoning stage. Enjoy the unique and delectable twist on a classic condiment!

Homemade mayo is effortless to make, incredibly versatile, and provides a healthier alternative to its store-bought counterpart. Consequently, you’ll impress friends and family with these enticing mayo recipes! Happy cooking!
