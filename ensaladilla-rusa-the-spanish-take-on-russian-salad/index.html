Ensaladilla Rusa: The Spanish Take on Russian Salad


If you’re a fan of creamy, flavorful salads, then you’re in for a treat with Ensaladilla Rusa, the Spanish version of the classic Russian salad. This dish has made its way into the hearts and homes of many Spaniards, offering a delightful mix of textures and flavors that make it a perfect addition to any meal or gathering. In this blog post, we’ll take a closer look at the origins of Ensaladilla Rusa, its key ingredients, and a step-by-step guide on how to make this delectable dish in your own kitchen.

Origins of Ensaladilla Rusa

Ensaladilla Rusa, also known as Russian Salad or Olivier Salad, has an interesting history that spans across different cultures. Despite its name, this salad actually has its origins in the 19th century in Russia, where it was created by Lucien Olivier, a Belgian chef. The original recipe featured a combination of diced potatoes, carrots, peas, pickles, and mayonnaise, and it quickly gained popularity not only in Russia but also in other parts of Europe.

Over time, the recipe made its way to Spain, where it underwent some modifications to suit the local palate. The Spanish version of the salad, Ensaladilla Rusa, typically includes potatoes, carrots, peas, tuna, hard-boiled eggs, olives, and mayonnaise. This variation has become a staple in Spanish cuisine, often served as a tapa or a side dish alongside grilled meats or seafood.

Key Ingredients

Ensaladilla Rusa is a versatile dish that allows for some creativity when it comes to choosing the ingredients. However, there are a few key components that form the foundation of this salad:

  • Potatoes: The base of the salad, boiled potatoes provide a creamy texture and absorb the flavors of the other ingredients.

  • Carrots: Boiled or steamed carrots add a touch of sweetness and vibrant color to the salad.

  • Peas: Whether fresh or frozen, peas contribute a burst of freshness and a pop of green to the dish.

  • Tuna: This protein-rich addition brings a savory element to the salad and pairs perfectly with the other flavors.

  • Hard-Boiled Eggs: Sliced or chopped hard-boiled eggs add a velvety richness and protein to the salad.

  • Olives: Sliced or whole olives lend a briny, salty flavor that complements the creaminess of the mayonnaise.

  • Mayonnaise: The binding agent that brings all the ingredients together, mayonnaise contributes a luscious, creamy texture to the salad.

How to Make Ensaladilla Rusa

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making Ensaladilla Rusa in your own kitchen. Here’s a simple and delicious recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup of peas, fresh or frozen
  • 1 can of tuna, drained
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup of olives, sliced
  • 1 cup of mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Potatoes and Carrots: In a large pot of salted water, boil the diced potatoes and carrots until they are fork-tender. Drain and let them cool to room temperature.

  2. Cook the Peas: If using fresh peas, blanch them in boiling water for a few minutes until they are tender. If using frozen peas, simply thaw them.

  3. Assemble the Salad: In a large mixing bowl, combine the boiled potatoes, carrots, peas, tuna, chopped hard-boiled eggs, and sliced olives.

  4. Add the Mayonnaise: Gently fold in the mayonnaise until all the ingredients are well-coated. Add salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Chill and Serve: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the salad for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld together. Once chilled, serve the Ensaladilla Rusa as a side dish or a tapa.

In Conclusion

Ensaladilla Rusa is a delightful and versatile salad that showcases the wonderful flavors of Spanish cuisine. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, a potluck, or simply craving a refreshing dish, this Spanish take on the classic Russian salad is sure to please your taste buds. Experiment with the ingredients and make it your own, and enjoy the delightful blend of textures and flavors in every bite. Buen provecho!
