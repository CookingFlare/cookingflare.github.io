Grilled Stuffed Peppers: Colorful and Delicious BBQ Appetizers


When it comes to grilling, there’s nothing quite like the vibrant, smoky flavors of grilled stuffed peppers. These colorful and delicious BBQ appetizers are not only visually stunning but also incredibly versatile, allowing you to experiment with various fillings and flavors. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a summer get-together, or simply craving a tasty and healthy snack, grilled stuffed peppers are the perfect choice. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to prepare and grill these delightful appetizers to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large bell peppers (assorted colors for a visually appealing dish)
  • 1 pound ground turkey (or any ground meat of your choice)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, pepper jack, or your preferred melting cheese)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Olive oil
  • Optional: your favorite BBQ sauce for brushing

Instructions:

Preparing the Peppers

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Wash the bell peppers, then carefully slice off the tops and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside.
  3. Brush the outside of the peppers with olive oil to help them char and soften on the grill.

Making the Filling

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, add a drizzle of olive oil and sauté the chopped onion until translucent.
  2. Add the garlic and ground turkey to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon and cooking until browned.
  3. Stir in the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Allow the mixture to cook for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Stuffing and Grilling the Peppers

  1. Spoon the filling mixture into the prepared bell peppers, pressing down gently to pack the filling.
  2. Sprinkle the shredded cheese on top of each stuffed pepper.
  3. If desired, brush the outside of the peppers with your favorite BBQ sauce for an extra layer of flavor.
  4. Place the stuffed peppers directly on the grill grates and cover the grill. Cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are slightly charred and the filling is heated through.
  5. Carefully remove the stuffed peppers from the grill and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Serving

Grilled stuffed peppers are best served hot off the grill, allowing your guests to enjoy the smoky aroma and gooey melted cheese. These appetizers can be served as a standalone dish or as a delightful accompaniment to grilled meats, salads, or other BBQ favorites.

Conclusion

Grilled stuffed peppers are a fantastic addition to any barbecue spread, offering a burst of flavors and a pop of color that will impress your guests. Whether you’re a fan of spicy, savory, or cheesy flavors, these versatile appetizers can be customized to suit your taste preferences. So, fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the delightful experience of preparing and enjoying these colorful and delicious BBQ appetizers.
