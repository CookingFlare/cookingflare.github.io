5 Dairy-Free Recipes for a Summer BBQ


As the summer sun shines brightly and the days stretch longer, there’s nothing quite like gathering with friends and family for a delightful barbecue. But for those who are lactose intolerant or simply prefer to enjoy dairy-free options, the challenge can be finding recipes that are both delicious and satisfying. Fear not! We have compiled a list of five scrumptious dairy-free recipes that will elevate your summer BBQ experience. From zesty grilled vegetables to a decadent dessert, these dishes will please all palates. Let’s dive in!

1. Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Chimichurri Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8 cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup mushrooms, whole or halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Chimichurri Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Skewers: Thread the zucchini, bell pepper, red onion, cherry tomatoes, and mushrooms onto skewers. Brush with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

  2. Make the Chimichurri Sauce: In a bowl, combine the parsley, minced garlic, olive oil, red wine vinegar, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Mix well and set aside.

  3. Grill the Skewers: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the vegetable skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are tender and have nice grill marks.

  4. Serve: Drizzle the chimichurri sauce over the grilled vegetables and serve warm. This vibrant dish is not only dairy-free but also packed with flavor!

2. Zesty Lemon Garlic Grilled Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken breasts, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for more flavor.

  2. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high.

  3. Grill the Chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade and place it on the grill. Cook for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  4. Rest and Serve: Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve with a fresh salad or alongside the grilled vegetable skewers for a complete meal.

3. Quinoa Salad with Black Beans and Avocado

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Quinoa: In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth or water to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed. Let it cool.

  2. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, cherry tomatoes, avocado, and red onion.

  3. Dress the Salad: Drizzle with lime juice and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and mix gently.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Top with fresh cilantro and serve chilled or at room temperature. This refreshing salad is perfect as a side dish or a light main course.

4. Spicy Grilled Shrimp Tacos with Mango Salsa

Ingredients:

For the Shrimp:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Corn tortillas

For the Mango Salsa:

  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine shrimp, olive oil, chili powder, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat and let marinate for 15 minutes.

  2. Make the Mango Salsa: In another bowl, mix together the diced mango, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, cilantro, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Grill the Shrimp: Preheat your grill to medium-high. Skewer the shrimp or place them directly on the grill. Grill for about 2-3 minutes per side, until pink and cooked through.

  4. Assemble the Tacos: Warm the corn tortillas on the grill for a few seconds on each side. Fill each tortilla with grilled shrimp and top with mango salsa. Enjoy the burst of flavors, perfect for summer!

5. Coconut Chia Seed Pudding with Fresh Berries

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk (canned or carton)
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup (or sweetener of choice)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries) for topping

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, chia seeds, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  2. Chill: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. The chia seeds will absorb the liquid and create a pudding-like texture.

  3. Serve: Spoon the chia seed pudding into serving glasses or bowls. Top with fresh berries for a refreshing dessert that is dairy-free and absolutely delicious.

Conclusion

This summer, don’t let dietary restrictions hold you back from enjoying a fantastic BBQ. With these five dairy-free recipes, you can treat your guests to a feast that is bursting with flavor and creativity. From colorful grilled vegetable skewers to the sweetness of coconut chia seed pudding, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the tastes of summer with these delightful dishes. Happy grilling!
