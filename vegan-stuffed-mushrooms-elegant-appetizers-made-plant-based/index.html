Vegan Stuffed Mushrooms: Elegant Appetizers Made Plant-Based


Are you looking for a delicious and elegant appetizer that’s completely plant-based? Look no further than these Vegan Stuffed Mushrooms! Bursting with flavor and packed with nutritious ingredients, these bite-sized delights are sure to impress your guests. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to elevate your snacking game, these vegan stuffed mushrooms are the perfect choice.

Ingredients

  • 24 large cremini or button mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (use gluten-free breadcrumbs if desired)
  • 1/4 cup vegan parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it with oil.

  2. Start by gently removing the stems from the mushrooms, being careful not to break the caps. Set the caps aside and finely chop the stems.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Add the chopped mushroom stems to the skillet and cook for an additional 5 minutes, until they have softened and released their moisture.

  5. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for another 2 minutes, until wilted. Remove the skillet from heat and set aside.

  6. In a separate bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, vegan parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  7. Add the cooked mushroom stem and spinach mixture to the bowl with the breadcrumbs. Stir until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

  8. Take each mushroom cap and spoon a generous amount of the stuffing mixture into it. Place the stuffed mushrooms on the prepared baking sheet.

  9. Bake the stuffed mushrooms in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the mushroom caps are tender and the filling is golden brown.

  10. Once cooked, remove the stuffed mushrooms from the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes before serving.

  11. Garnish with additional chopped parsley, if desired, and serve warm.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as portobello or shiitake, for a more robust flavor.
  • If you prefer a cheesier filling, add some vegan mozzarella or cheddar cheese to the stuffing mixture.
  • For a touch of heat, sprinkle some red pepper flakes over the stuffed mushrooms before baking.
  • These vegan stuffed mushrooms can be prepared in advance. Simply stuff the mushrooms and refrigerate until ready to bake. You may need to increase the baking time slightly if the mushrooms are cold from the fridge.
  • Serve these elegant appetizers alongside a fresh green salad or as part of a larger vegan feast.

So there you have it – a simple yet elegant recipe for Vegan Stuffed Mushrooms. These plant-based appetizers are not only delicious and satisfying, but they also make a beautiful addition to any table. Whether you’re a vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these stuffed mushrooms are a must-try. Enjoy!
