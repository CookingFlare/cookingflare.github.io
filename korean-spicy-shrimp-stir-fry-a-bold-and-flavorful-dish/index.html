Korean Spicy Shrimp Stir-Fry: A Bold and Flavorful Dish


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and spicy kick. One dish that embodies these characteristics is the Korean Spicy Shrimp Stir-Fry, a mouth-numbing and taste-bud-tingling masterpiece that will leave you craving for more. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the preparation of this iconic dish, using a combination of fresh ingredients and clever techniques to create a truly unforgettable culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1/4 cup Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

To begin, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until it’s translucent and slightly caramelized, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Next, add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the skillet, and cook, stirring frequently, until they’re pink and fully cooked, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the shrimp from the skillet, and set them aside on a plate.

In the same skillet, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the Gochujang, soy sauce, Korean chili flakes, brown sugar, and water, and whisk until the sauce is smooth and well combined. Bring the sauce to a simmer, and cook, stirring occasionally, until it’s thickened and reduced slightly, about 2-3 minutes.

Add the cooked shrimp back into the skillet, and toss them in the spicy sauce to coat. Cook for an additional minute, until the shrimp are fully coated and the sauce has thickened to your liking.

To serve, divide the cooked rice onto individual plates, and top it with the spicy shrimp stir-fry. Garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds, and season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add 1-2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro or scallions to the sauce before serving.
  • If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of Korean chili flakes or omit them altogether.
  • To make the dish more substantial, add 1 cup of cooked vegetables, such as bell peppers, carrots, or broccoli, to the stir-fry along with the shrimp.
  • For a gluten-free option, replace the soy sauce with a gluten-free alternative, such as tamari or coconut aminos.

In conclusion, the Korean Spicy Shrimp Stir-Fry is a bold and flavorful dish that’s sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. With its combination of spicy Gochujang sauce, succulent shrimp, and crunchy vegetables, it’s a true celebration of Korean cuisine. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the bold and vibrant flavors of Korea for yourself.
