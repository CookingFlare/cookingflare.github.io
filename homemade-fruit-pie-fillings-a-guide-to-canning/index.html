Homemade Fruit Pie Fillings: A Guide to Canning


Are you tired of store-bought fruit pie fillings that lack the freshness and flavor of homemade ones? Look no further! In this guide, we will take you through the process of making and canning your very own fruit pie fillings. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this step-by-step guide will help you create delicious and versatile fillings that can be used in a variety of fruit pies.

Why Make Homemade Fruit Pie Fillings?

Making your own fruit pie fillings has numerous advantages. Firstly, you have complete control over the ingredients, ensuring that only the freshest and highest-quality fruits are used. Secondly, you can customize the sweetness and flavor to suit your taste preferences. Lastly, by canning your fillings, you can enjoy the taste of summer fruits all year round!

Choosing the Right Fruits

The first step in creating a delicious fruit pie filling is selecting the right fruits. While almost any fruit can be made into a pie filling, some fruits work better than others. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Apples: Apples are a classic choice for pie fillings. Varieties like Granny Smith and Honeycrisp provide a perfect balance of tartness and sweetness.

  2. Cherries: Whether you prefer sweet or sour cherries, both make excellent pie fillings. Sweet cherries, like Bing or Rainier, lend a delightful sweetness, while sour cherries give a tangy twist.

  3. Peaches: Juicy and fragrant peaches are a summer favorite. Their natural sweetness makes them an excellent choice for pie fillings.

  4. Berries: From strawberries and blueberries to raspberries and blackberries, berries add vibrant colors and flavors to your pie fillings. Mix and match different varieties for a burst of fruity goodness.

Preparing the Fruit

Once you’ve chosen your fruits, it’s time to prepare them for the filling. Start by washing and peeling the fruits, if necessary. Remove any pits, seeds, or stems. Cut the fruits into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large bowl.

Sweetening and Flavoring

Now comes the fun part – sweetening and flavoring your fruit pie fillings. The amount of sugar required depends on the sweetness of the fruit and your personal preference. Begin by adding a small amount of sugar, and gradually increase it until you achieve the desired level of sweetness.

To enhance the flavors, consider adding spices such as cinnamon, nutmeg, or vanilla extract. These additions will add depth and complexity to your fillings.

Thickening the Filling

To ensure your pie fillings have the perfect consistency, you’ll need to thicken them. This can be achieved by using either flour or cornstarch. Mix a small amount of your chosen thickener with water to create a slurry. Add the slurry to the fruit mixture and stir well. The heat from the cooking process will activate the thickening agent, giving your fillings a luscious texture.

Cooking and Canning

Now that your fruit pie filling is ready, it’s time to cook and can it for future use. Transfer the filling to a large pot and cook it over medium heat until it reaches a gentle boil. Stir continuously to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

While the filling is cooking, prepare your canning jars and lids by washing them thoroughly. Sterilize the jars by placing them in boiling water for a few minutes. Once the filling has reached a boil, carefully ladle it into the sterilized jars, leaving a small amount of headspace.

Seal the jars with the lids and process them in a water bath canner for the recommended time according to your altitude and the type of fruit being used. This process ensures that your fillings are properly preserved and safe to consume for an extended period.

Enjoying Your Homemade Fruit Pie Fillings

Now that you have a collection of delicious homemade fruit pie fillings, the possibilities are endless! Use them as a filling for traditional pies, tarts, turnovers, or even as a topping for pancakes or ice cream. The sweet aromas and flavors of your homemade fillings will surely impress your family and friends.

Remember to label and date your canned fruit pie fillings for easy identification. They can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to one year. However, once opened, be sure to refrigerate any leftovers and consume them within a week for optimal freshness.

So, why settle for store-bought when you can enjoy the satisfaction of creating your own homemade fruit pie fillings? Follow this guide and let your creativity shine as you experiment with different fruit combinations and flavors. Happy canning and baking!
