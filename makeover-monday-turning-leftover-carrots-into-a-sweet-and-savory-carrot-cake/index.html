Makeover Monday: Turning Leftover Carrots into a Sweet and Savory Carrot Cake


Carrot cake is a classic dessert loved by many, but did you know that you can transform leftover carrots into a delicious and moist carrot cake? It’s a fantastic way to reduce food waste while indulging in a sweet treat. In this Makeover Monday recipe, we’ll show you how to turn those forgotten carrots into a heavenly carrot cake that’s both sweet and savory.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of grated leftover carrots
  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup of crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1/2 cup of chopped walnuts (optional)
  • Cream cheese frosting (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan or line it with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated carrots, flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Mix well until all the dry ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. In another bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet mixture into the dry mixture and stir until just combined.

  4. Fold in the crushed pineapple and chopped walnuts (if using). The pineapple adds a delightful sweetness and moisture to the cake, while the walnuts provide a nice crunch.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake for approximately 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Once the cake is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. Once the cake is completely cooled, frost it with cream cheese frosting. You can either use store-bought frosting or make your own by beating together cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.

  8. Optionally, you can decorate the cake with additional grated carrots or a sprinkle of chopped walnuts for an extra touch of visual appeal.

Serving and Storage

Serve the carrot cake at room temperature and enjoy every moist and flavorful bite. This carrot cake can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The flavors will continue to develop, making it even more delicious over time.

Conclusion

Transforming leftover carrots into a sweet and savory carrot cake is a delightful way to minimize food waste while satisfying your cravings for a delectable dessert. This Makeover Monday recipe allows you to create a moist and flavorful cake that will impress your family and friends. So, the next time you find yourself with leftover carrots, don’t let them go to waste. Instead, turn them into a scrumptious carrot cake that everyone will adore. Happy baking!
