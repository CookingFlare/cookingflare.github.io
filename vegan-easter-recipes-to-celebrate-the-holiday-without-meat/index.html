Vegan Easter Recipes to Celebrate the Holiday Without Meat


Easter is a time of renewal, celebration, and gathering with family and friends. Traditionally, many Easter dishes center around meat and dairy products, but embracing a plant-based lifestyle doesn’t mean missing out on the festive spirit or the delicious food. Vegan Easter recipes have evolved beautifully, offering vibrant, flavorful options that honor the essence of the holiday while promoting compassion and health.

Whether you’re fully vegan, exploring plant-based options, or simply looking to diversify your holiday menu, this collection of recipes will inspire your Easter celebration. From savory mains to decadent desserts, these dishes promise to delight all guests, regardless of their dietary preferences.

Starter Ideas: Light and Inviting

Roasted Spring Vegetable Soup

Begin your Easter feast with a comforting, colorful soup that captures the essence of spring. This roasted spring vegetable soup features asparagus, peas, carrots, and herbs, delivering a fresh and earthy flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and chopped
  • 2 cups baby carrots, peeled and halved
  • 1 cup fresh peas (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill or parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Arrange the asparagus, carrots, onion, and garlic on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast for about 20-25 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and caramelized.
  4. Transfer the roasted vegetables to a large pot, add the vegetable broth, and bring to a simmer.
  5. Stir in the peas and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  6. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender in batches, puree the soup until smooth.
  7. Adjust seasoning, garnish with fresh dill or parsley, and serve warm.

This soup not only showcases the bounty of spring but also offers a nourishing start to the meal.

Deviled Potatoes with Mustard-Tahini Filling

For a finger-food option, deviled potatoes provide a vegan twist on deviled eggs, a classic Easter appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 20-25 small baby potatoes
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened plant-based yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender (15-20 minutes). Drain and let cool.
  2. Cut the potatoes in half and scoop out a small portion of the flesh, creating a hollow.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the tahini, mustard, vinegar, yogurt, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix the scooped potato flesh into the tahini mixture until creamy.
  5. Fill each potato half with the mixture, sprinkle with chopped chives, and serve chilled or at room temperature.

These deviled potatoes are creamy, tangy, and boast the festive look and feel of traditional deviled eggs without any animal products.

Main Courses: Celebrating Spring’s Harvest

Herbed Lentil and Mushroom Wellington

A centerpiece worthy of any holiday table, this Wellington brings hearty lentils and savory mushrooms encased in golden pastry, embodying comfort and sophistication.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed and cooked
  • 2 cups mushrooms, finely chopped (cremini, shiitake, or a mix)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 sheet vegan puff pastry (thawed if frozen)
  • 2 tbsp ground flaxseed + 5 tbsp water (flax egg)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add chopped mushrooms, thyme, rosemary, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Cook until mushrooms release moisture and it evaporates.
  4. Mix cooked lentils into the mushroom mixture, stir frequently until well combined and thickened. Remove from heat and allow to cool.
  5. Mix ground flaxseed and water in a small bowl to form a flax egg; let it rest for 5 minutes.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. Shape the lentil-mushroom mixture into a log and place it in the center of the pastry.
  7. Fold the pastry over the filling, sealing edges with flax egg.
  8. Brush the entire Wellington with flax egg for a golden finish.
  9. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the pastry is puffed and browned.
  10. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This dish combines robust, umami flavors and flaky texture, rivaling any traditional meat roast.

Spring Pea Risotto

Elevate your holiday table with a creamy, vibrant spring pea risotto that’s as stunning visually as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • ½ cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 5 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast (for cheesy flavor)
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Fresh basil or mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat olive oil. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add Arborio rice, stirring to coat grains with oil.
  3. Pour in white wine and cook until absorbed (if not using wine, skip this step).
  4. Start adding warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring gently and allowing liquid to absorb before adding more.
  5. When about halfway through the broth, stir in peas.
  6. Continue cooking and stirring until rice is creamy and tender (about 20-25 minutes total).
  7. Remove from heat. Stir in lemon juice, zest, and nutritional yeast.
  8. Season with salt, pepper, and fold in fresh herbs.

The risotto offers a luscious texture and bright notes, making it a sublime vegetarian highlight that fits seamlessly into Easter celebrations.

Side Dishes: Complement and Contrast

Maple-Glazed Carrots with Toasted Pecans

Sweet and nutty, this side celebrates natural sweetness enhanced with a delicate maple glaze and crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby carrots or sliced regular carrots
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup toasted pecans, roughly chopped
  • Fresh thyme or parsley for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss carrots with olive oil, maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Spread evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  4. Remove from oven, sprinkle with toasted pecans and herbs before serving.

This dish offers a gratifying combination of textures and flavors, pairing well with the main dishes without overwhelming them.

Simple Spring Green Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

Balance heavier dishes with a crisp salad showcasing the best greens of the season.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed spring greens (arugula, baby spinach, watercress)
  • 1 cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced fennel
  • 1/4 cup toasted pumpkin seeds
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a large salad bowl, combine greens, radishes, cucumber, and fennel.
  3. Toss with dressing right before serving.
  4. Sprinkle toasted pumpkin seeds on top for a pleasant crunch.

The bright, zesty salad cleanses the palate, keeping the meal fresh and light.

Desserts: Sweet Endings Vegan Style

Coconut-Lemon Bundt Cake

Celebrate with a beautifully moist and zesty vegan coconut-lemon bundt cake, perfect for sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • Juice and zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup shredded unsweetened coconut

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a bundt pan with coconut oil.
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine coconut milk, melted coconut oil, lemon juice, zest, vinegar, and vanilla.
  4. Pour wet ingredients into dry and mix until just combined.
  5. Fold in shredded coconut.
  6. Pour batter into the bundt pan and bake for 45-50 minutes, until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  7. Let cool before inverting onto a plate. Optionally drizzle with a simple glaze made of lemon juice and powdered sugar.

This cake combines fresh citrus brightness with tropical coconut, delivering a tender crumb that feels indulgent yet wholesome.

Berry Chia Seed Pudding Parfaits

Light, nutritious, and visually striking, these parfaits layer creamy chia pudding with fresh, juicy berries.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup almond or other plant-based milk
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or agave
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine chia seeds, plant milk, sweetener, and vanilla.
  2. Stir well and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight, until thickened.
  3. Layer chia pudding and berries in serving glasses or jars.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint and serve chilled.

These parfaits offer a fresh, lightly sweet finish that complements the richer dishes of your holiday feast.

Final Thoughts

Crafting a vegan Easter menu offers the perfect opportunity to celebrate the season’s freshest ingredients with vibrant, hearty dishes that nourish both body and soul. These recipes emphasize texture, color, and bold flavors—proving plant-based cooking can be both festive and deeply satisfying.

By incorporating seasonal vegetables, legumes, nuts, and naturally sweetened desserts, you create a meaningful, inclusive holiday table that invites both vegans and omnivores to gather, savor, and enjoy.

Whether you’re hosting a crowd or simply celebrating quietly, these recipes bring warmth and joy to your vegan Easter experience. Here’s to a joyful, compassionate, and delicious holiday!
