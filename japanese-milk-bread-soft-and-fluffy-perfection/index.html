Japanese Milk Bread: Soft and Fluffy Perfection


Japanese Milk Bread, also known as Hokkaido milk bread, is a light, fluffy, and incredibly soft bread that has gained immense popularity worldwide. Its unique texture and subtly sweet flavor make it a favorite among bread lovers. In this blog post, we’ll explore the secrets to creating the perfect Japanese Milk Bread in your own kitchen. From the basic ingredients to the intricate techniques, we’ll delve into everything you need to know to achieve bakery-quality results.

Ingredients

For the Tangzhong (Water Roux):

  • 50g bread flour
  • 250ml whole milk
  • 250ml water

For the Dough:

  • 540g bread flour
  • 80g granulated sugar
  • 8g salt
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 60ml whole milk
  • 2 large eggs

For the Milk Bread:

  • 60g unsalted butter, softened

Instructions

Making the Tangzhong (Water Roux):

  1. In a small saucepan, whisk together the bread flour, whole milk, and water until smooth.
  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens into a gel-like consistency. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Transfer the tangzhong to a clean bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it cool to room temperature.

Preparing the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, granulated sugar, salt, and active dry yeast.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the whole milk and eggs.
  3. Add the tangzhong and the milk-egg mixture to the dry ingredients.
  4. Using a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment, knead the dough on low speed for 15 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Gradually add the softened unsalted butter and continue kneading until it is fully incorporated into the dough.

Shaping and Proofing:

  1. Transfer the dough to a lightly greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  2. Gently deflate the dough and divide it into equal portions.
  3. Shape each portion into a tight ball and place them into a greased loaf pan.
  4. Cover the loaf pan with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough proof for another 1-2 hours, or until it rises just above the rim of the pan.

Baking the Bread:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Bake the bread for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the bottom sounds hollow when tapped.
  3. Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips for Success

  • Use a kitchen scale to measure the ingredients for accuracy.
  • Be patient with the kneading and proofing process to achieve the desired texture and flavor.
  • Store the bread in an airtight container to maintain its softness and freshness.

Japanese Milk Bread is best enjoyed fresh, whether it’s used for sandwiches, toast, or simply savored on its own. With its pillowy softness and buttery aroma, this bread is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire. Embrace the art of bread-making and indulge in the delightful experience of creating your own Japanese Milk Bread at home.
