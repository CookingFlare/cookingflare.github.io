Hearty One Pot Beef and Mushroom Stew


If you’re looking for a comforting and hearty meal that’s easy to make and perfect for a cozy night in, then this One Pot Beef and Mushroom Stew is just what you need. Packed with savory flavors and tender beef, this stew is a true crowd-pleaser and will leave everyone coming back for seconds. The best part? It’s all made in one pot, making clean-up a breeze. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into this delicious recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound cremini mushrooms, quartered
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef: In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef pieces in batches and brown them on all sides. Once browned, remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  3. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the red wine and scrape the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes to reduce slightly.

  4. Add the Broth and Seasonings: Return the browned beef to the pot. Pour in the beef broth and stir in the tomato paste, bay leaves, dried thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.

  5. Add the Veggies and Mushrooms: Once the beef is tender, add the quartered cremini mushrooms, chopped carrots, and celery to the pot. Let the stew simmer for an additional 30 minutes, allowing the vegetables to soften and the flavors to meld together.

  6. Thicken the Stew: In a small bowl, mix the all-purpose flour with a bit of water to create a slurry. Stir the slurry into the stew and let it simmer for a few more minutes until the stew thickens slightly.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Remove the bay leaves from the stew. Ladle the hearty beef and mushroom stew into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Serve with crusty bread or over a bed of mashed potatoes for a truly satisfying meal.

Tips for Success

  • Use a flavorful red wine for added depth of flavor in the stew.
  • Don’t rush the browning process of the beef; this step adds a rich, caramelized flavor to the stew.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables like potatoes or parsnips for extra heartiness.

Final Thoughts

This Hearty One Pot Beef and Mushroom Stew is a wonderful dish to warm both the body and the soul. The tender beef, earthy mushrooms, and aromatic vegetables come together in a rich and comforting broth that’s perfect for chilly evenings or lazy weekends. The best part is that it only requires one pot, so you can spend less time cleaning up and more time savoring each spoonful of this delightful stew.

So, whether you’re cooking for your family, hosting a gathering with friends, or simply craving a bowl of something comforting, give this recipe a try. It’s a surefire way to bring a smile to everyone’s face and fill your home with the delightful aroma of a homemade meal made with love. Cheers to good food, good company, and the joy of creating something truly special in the kitchen.
