Savory Middle Eastern Kebabs: 6 Recipes for Grilling Season

As grilling season approaches, there’s nothing quite like the smell of savory kebabs wafting through the air. Middle Eastern cuisine offers a delightful array of kebab recipes that are bursting with flavor and perfect for outdoor cooking. From marinated meats to vegetarian options, these six kebab recipes will elevate your grilling game and impress your family and friends. 1. Classic Shish Kebab Ingredients: 1 pound beef sirloin, cut into 1-inch cubes 1 large onion, finely chopped 4 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup olive oil 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper to taste Skewers (soaked in water if wooden) Instructions: In a large bowl, combine the chopped onion, minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the beef cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for the best flavor. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated beef onto the skewers. Grill the kebabs for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they reach your desired level of doneness. Serve hot with pita bread, hummus, and a fresh salad. 2. Chicken Kofta Kebabs Ingredients: 1 pound ground chicken 1 small onion, grated 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt and pepper to taste Skewers Instructions: In a mixing bowl, combine the ground chicken, grated onion, minced garlic, parsley, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined. Divide the mixture into 8 equal portions and shape them around the skewers into elongated patties. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Grill the kofta kebabs for about 10-15 minutes, turning until they are cooked through and have a nice char. Serve with a side of tzatziki sauce and warm pita. 3. Lamb Kebabs with Mint Yogurt Sauce Ingredients: 1 pound lamb shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes 4 tablespoons olive oil 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar 2 teaspoons ground cumin 2 teaspoons ground coriander 1 teaspoon cinnamon Salt and pepper to taste Fresh mint leaves for garnish For the Mint Yogurt Sauce: 1 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup fresh mint, finely chopped 1 teaspoon lemon juice Salt to taste Instructions: In a bowl, mix together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Add the lamb cubes and marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. For the mint yogurt sauce, combine the yogurt, chopped mint, lemon juice, and salt in a small bowl. Mix well and set aside. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated lamb onto skewers. Grill the kebabs for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are cooked to your liking. Serve the lamb kebabs hot, drizzled with mint yogurt sauce and garnished with fresh mint leaves. 4. Spicy Shrimp Kebabs Ingredients: 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined 1/4 cup olive oil 3 tablespoons Sriracha sauce 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon smoked paprika Juice of 1 lime Salt and pepper to taste Skewers Instructions: In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, Sriracha sauce, minced garlic, smoked paprika, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Add the shrimp to the marinade and toss to coat. Let marinate for 30 minutes. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the shrimp onto skewers. Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until they turn pink and opaque. Serve immediately with lime wedges and a side of rice or quinoa salad. 5. Grilled Vegetable Kebabs Ingredients: 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks 1 red onion, cut into wedges 8 cherry tomatoes 1/4 cup olive oil 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning Salt and pepper to taste Skewers Instructions: In a large bowl, toss the zucchini, bell pepper, red onion, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating the types for a colorful presentation. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Grill the vegetable kebabs for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally until they are tender and slightly charred. Serve warm as a side dish or over a bed of couscous or quinoa. 6. Falafel Kebabs Ingredients: 1 can (15 ounces) chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 small onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 1/4 cup flour 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander Salt and pepper to taste Skewers Instructions: In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, flour, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Pulse until combined but still slightly chunky. Form the mixture into small patties or logs that can be easily skewered. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Thread the falafel onto skewers. Grill the falafel kebabs for about 5-7 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and heated through. Serve with tahini sauce and a side salad. Conclusion As you prepare for grilling season, these savory Middle Eastern kebab recipes are sure to become favorites at your gatherings. With a variety of meats and vegetarian options, these kebabs cater to every palate. Enjoy the bold flavors, vibrant colors, and the joy of sharing delicious meals with loved ones. Vietnamese Pho Ga with Chicken and Rice Noodle Soup Recipe

If you’re a fan of Vietnamese cuisine, you’ve probably heard of the famous and comforting dish called Pho. Traditionally made with beef, Pho also has a chicken version known as Pho Ga, which is equally delicious and satisfying. This aromatic and flavorful soup is perfect for any occasion, whether you’re craving a warm and nourishing meal or looking to impress your dinner guests with an exotic culinary delight. Ingredients For the Broth: 1 whole chicken, about 3-4 pounds 1 large onion, peeled and halved 3-inch piece of fresh ginger, halved lengthwise 2 cinnamon sticks 3 star anise 4 cloves 1 cardamom pod 1 teaspoon coriander seeds 8 cups of chicken broth 2 tablespoons fish sauce 1 tablespoon sugar Salt, to taste For the Soup: 1 pound rice noodles 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (from the broth) 1 onion, thinly sliced 4 green onions, thinly sliced 1 cup bean sprouts Fresh cilantro, basil, and Thai basil for garnish Lime wedges Sriracha or chili sauce (optional) Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Broth Rinse the chicken under cold water and place it in a large pot. Add enough water to cover the chicken, then bring it to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Remove the chicken from the pot and discard the water. Rinse the pot and return the chicken to it. Add the onion, ginger, cinnamon sticks, star anise, cloves, cardamom, and coriander seeds to the pot. Pour in the chicken broth and add fish sauce and sugar. Bring the broth to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, uncovered, for about 1 hour. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface. Once the broth is done, carefully remove the chicken from the pot and let it cool. Shred the meat and set it aside. Step 2: Prepare the Rice Noodles While the broth is simmering, soak the rice noodles in hot water according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. Step 3: Assemble the Soup Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot, discarding the solids. Bring the strained broth to a boil and season with salt to taste. Divide the cooked rice noodles among serving bowls and top with shredded chicken, sliced onion, and green onions. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and chicken. The hot broth will cook the chicken and warm the noodles. Serve the Pho Ga hot, garnished with bean sprouts, cilantro, basil, Thai basil, lime wedges, and a side of Sriracha or chili sauce for those who like it spicy. Enjoy the Authentic Flavors of Pho Ga!
