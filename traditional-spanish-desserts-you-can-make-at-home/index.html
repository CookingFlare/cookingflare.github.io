Traditional Spanish Desserts You Can Make at Home


Spain is known for its rich culinary heritage, especially when it comes to desserts. From creamy flans to rich cakes and sweet pastries, traditional Spanish desserts are a delightful way to explore the country’s diverse flavors and textures. In this post, we’ll take a journey through some of the most beloved Spanish desserts that you can easily recreate in your own kitchen. Get ready to indulge your sweet tooth and impress your friends and family with these authentic recipes!

1. Flan

Flan, a classic Spanish dessert, is a creamy caramel custard that melts in your mouth. It’s simple to make and requires just a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) of evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Caramel: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the sugar until it turns golden brown. Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking dish, tilting the dish to coat the bottom evenly. Allow it to cool and harden.

  2. Make the Custard: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  3. Combine: Pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the baking dish.

  4. Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the flan dish in a larger baking pan and fill it with water to create a water bath. Bake for about 50-60 minutes, until the flan is set.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow it to cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours. To serve, run a knife around the edges and invert onto a serving plate. Enjoy the delicious caramel sauce that drips over the sides!

2. Churros with Chocolate

Churros are a beloved Spanish treat, often enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack. These crispy fried dough pastries are typically served with a thick, rich chocolate sauce for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For the Chocolate Sauce:

  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil. Remove from heat and stir in the flour until a dough forms. Allow it to cool slightly, then mix in the eggs one at a time until smooth.

  2. Fry the Churros: Heat oil in a deep skillet over medium-high heat. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip. Pipe 4-6 inch strips directly into the hot oil and fry until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  3. Prepare the Chocolate Sauce: In a saucepan, heat the milk. In a separate bowl, mix the cornstarch and sugar, then add to the milk. Stir in the chopped chocolate and cook over low heat until thickened.

  4. Serve: Dust the churros with sugar and serve warm with the chocolate sauce for dipping.

3. Tarta de Santiago

Originating from the region of Galicia, Tarta de Santiago is an almond cake that is both moist and flavorful. It’s often adorned with the cross of Saint James, making it a symbolic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of ground almonds
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a round cake pan.

  2. Mix Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, sugar, lemon zest, and baking powder. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition until the batter is smooth.

  3. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan. Bake for about 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

  4. Cool and Decorate: Allow the cake to cool completely before removing it from the pan. Once cooled, dust with powdered sugar and place a stencil of the cross of Saint James on top before dusting again.

4. Natillas

Natillas is a traditional Spanish custard dessert that is often flavored with cinnamon and lemon. This creamy treat is perfect for a comforting dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Milk: In a saucepan, combine the milk, sugar, cinnamon stick, and lemon zest. Heat over medium heat until it begins to simmer.

  2. Mix Egg Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and cornstarch until smooth.

  3. Combine: Slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking continuously to temper the eggs. Return the mixture to the saucepan.

  4. Thicken: Cook over low heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and strain to remove the cinnamon stick and lemon zest.

  5. Chill and Serve: Pour into individual serving cups and refrigerate until set. Before serving, sprinkle with ground cinnamon.

5. Panna Cotta de Café

This Spanish twist on the Italian classic is infused with coffee, making it a delightful dessert for coffee lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of powdered gelatin
  • 1/4 cup of strong brewed coffee
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Gelatin: In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over 2 tablespoons of cold water and let it bloom for about 5 minutes.

  2. Heat the Cream: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and stir in the bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved.

  3. Add Flavor: Stir in the brewed coffee and vanilla extract.

  4. Chill: Pour the mixture into individual molds or serving glasses. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours until set.

  5. Serve: To serve, gently run a knife around the edges of the molds to release the panna cotta. Invert onto plates or serve directly in the glasses.

6. Arroz con Leche

Arroz con leche, or rice pudding, is a comforting dessert made with rice, milk, and cinnamon. It’s a beloved treat across Spain and Latin America.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of short-grain rice
  • 4 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: In a saucepan, combine the rice, 1 cup of milk, and cinnamon stick. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender.

  2. Add Remaining Ingredients: Stir in the remaining milk, sugar, and lemon zest. Cook for an additional 15-20 minutes, stirring frequently until creamy.

  3. Cool and Serve: Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon.

7. Panellets

These delightful marzipan treats are typically made for All Saints’ Day in Catalonia. They come in various flavors and are often coated in pine nuts.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of almond flour
  • 1 cup of powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • Pine nuts for coating
  • Optional: cocoa powder or grated chocolate for chocolate panellets

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a bowl, combine the almond flour, powdered sugar, egg, and lemon zest. Mix until a dough forms.

  2. Shape the Panellets: Divide the dough into small pieces and shape them into balls. Roll some in pine nuts, and for chocolate panellets, mix cocoa powder into the dough before shaping.

  3. Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and place the panellets on a baking sheet. Bake for about 10-12 minutes, until lightly golden.

  4. Cool and Enjoy: Allow them to cool before serving. These treats are perfect with coffee or as a sweet snack any time of the day.

Conclusion

Exploring traditional Spanish desserts is a delightful way to experience the country’s rich culinary culture. From creamy flans to crispy churros, each dessert offers a unique taste of Spain’s heritage. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging your sweet tooth, these recipes are sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and bring a piece of Spain into your home kitchen. Enjoy the sweet journey!
