How to Make a Chocolate Tart with Ganache


A chocolate tart with ganache is one of the most indulgent desserts you can make. Rich, creamy, and deeply satisfying, this tart combines a buttery, crisp pastry crust with a velvety smooth chocolate ganache filling. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion, a dinner party, or just because you want to treat yourself, this recipe will guide you through every step to ensure your chocolate tart is perfect every time.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Tart Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water (as needed)

For the Chocolate Ganache Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 10 oz (280g) good-quality dark chocolate (preferably 60-70% cocoa)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Step 1: Preparing the Tart Crust

Make the Tart Dough

Start by making the tart dough, which will form the base of your chocolate tart. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. These dry ingredients are the foundation of your crust and will help to create a delicate, crispy texture. Next, add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized pieces of butter throughout. This process ensures that your tart crust will be tender and flaky.

Once the butter is incorporated, add the egg yolk. The yolk helps to bind the dough together, adding richness and flavor. Gradually add the ice water, starting with 2 tablespoons and adding more if necessary, until the dough just begins to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. If the mixture is too crumbly, add a bit more water until it holds together when pressed.

Chill the Dough

Shape the dough into a flat disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough helps the gluten relax and prevents the crust from shrinking during baking.

Roll Out the Dough

After chilling, remove the dough from the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly. Lightly flour your work surface and rolling pin, and roll the dough out to about 1/8 inch thick. Aim for a circle large enough to fit your tart pan, with some extra dough around the edges.

Fit the Dough Into the Tart Pan

Carefully transfer the rolled dough to a 9-inch tart pan. Gently press the dough into the edges and bottom of the pan, ensuring it’s evenly distributed. If there are any tears or cracks, you can patch them by pressing the dough together with your fingers. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges, leaving about 1/4 inch to account for shrinkage during baking.

Blind Bake the Crust

Before filling the crust, you’ll need to blind bake it to prevent it from becoming soggy once the ganache is added. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place a piece of parchment paper or aluminum foil over the tart crust and fill it with pie weights, dried beans, or uncooked rice to weigh it down. Bake the crust for about 15 minutes, then remove the parchment and weights and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until the crust is golden brown.

Once baked, remove the tart crust from the oven and let it cool completely before filling it with ganache.

Step 2: Making the Chocolate Ganache

Heat the Cream

For the ganache filling, start by heating the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir it occasionally and let it come to a simmer—be sure not to let it boil. You’ll know the cream is ready when small bubbles begin to form around the edges of the pan.

Melt the Chocolate

While the cream is heating, chop the dark chocolate into small, even pieces and place it in a heatproof bowl. Once the cream reaches a simmer, pour it over the chopped chocolate. Let it sit for a minute or two to allow the heat of the cream to melt the chocolate.

Stir the Ganache

After a minute, gently stir the chocolate and cream together with a spatula or whisk until smooth and glossy. The chocolate should fully melt into the cream, creating a rich, smooth ganache. At this point, add the butter, vanilla extract (if using), and a pinch of salt. Stir the mixture until the butter is fully incorporated, which will give the ganache a silky, velvety texture.

Let the Ganache Cool Slightly

Once the ganache is smooth and shiny, set it aside for a few minutes to cool slightly. This will make it easier to pour into the tart shell without disturbing the crust.

Step 3: Assembling the Chocolate Tart

Fill the Tart Crust

Carefully pour the cooled ganache into the prepared tart crust. Use a spatula to gently spread the ganache into an even layer, ensuring it reaches all the edges of the crust. The ganache should fill the tart shell nearly to the top, with just a small border around the edges.

Chill the Tart

Once the tart is filled, place it in the refrigerator to set. This will allow the ganache to firm up and achieve the perfect creamy consistency. Let it chill for at least 2 hours, but overnight is ideal for the best texture and flavor.

Step 4: Serving Your Chocolate Tart

Garnish (Optional)

For an extra touch of elegance, you can garnish your chocolate tart with a variety of toppings. Consider a light dusting of cocoa powder or powdered sugar for a classic look. Fresh berries, such as raspberries or strawberries, complement the richness of the ganache perfectly. You could also add whipped cream or a few shavings of dark chocolate for an added layer of flavor and visual appeal.

Slice and Enjoy

Once the tart is fully chilled and set, it’s time to serve. Use a sharp knife to slice the tart into wedges. When cutting, dip your knife in hot water and wipe it clean between slices to maintain neat edges. This decadent chocolate tart is best served chilled, but it can also be enjoyed at room temperature if you prefer a softer ganache.

Tips for Perfecting Your Chocolate Tart

  1. Use High-Quality Chocolate: The key to a truly indulgent ganache is using high-quality chocolate. Look for chocolate with a cocoa content of 60-70% for a deep, intense flavor that isn’t too sweet.

  2. Be Careful Not to Overbake the Crust: When blind baking the crust, be sure not to overbake it. A golden-brown crust provides a crisp texture that contrasts beautifully with the creamy ganache filling.

  3. Let the Ganache Cool Slightly Before Pouring: Pouring the ganache into the crust while it’s too hot may cause the crust to soften or break. Let the ganache cool to room temperature before filling the tart shell for the best results.

  4. Experiment with Flavors: While a classic chocolate ganache tart is delicious on its own, feel free to get creative with different flavor combinations. Add a splash of orange liqueur, a sprinkle of sea salt, or a bit of espresso powder to enhance the depth of the chocolate flavor.

  5. Make Ahead: This chocolate tart can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days. In fact, the flavor often improves after sitting for a day or two, making it an excellent option for entertaining or special occasions.

Conclusion

Making a chocolate tart with ganache is simpler than it seems, and the result is an irresistibly rich dessert that will leave a lasting impression on anyone who tastes it. From the buttery, flaky crust to the creamy, smooth ganache filling, every bite is a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, this chocolate tart recipe is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire.

By following these step-by-step instructions, you can create a stunning dessert that will impress your guests and satisfy your sweet cravings. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to the ultimate chocolate indulgence.
