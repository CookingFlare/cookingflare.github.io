Creating a Focal Point: Tips for Drawing Attention to Your Dish in Food Photography


When it comes to food photography, creating a focal point in your dish can make all the difference. A well-executed focal point not only draws the viewer’s attention but also tells a story about the dish. Whether you’re a professional food photographer or an amateur looking to up your Instagram game, here are some tips for drawing attention to your dish in food photography.

1. Select the Hero Ingredient

Every dish has that one standout ingredient that steals the show. It could be a vibrant red tomato in a salad, a perfectly seared piece of steak, or a luscious drizzle of chocolate on a dessert. Identify the hero ingredient of your dish and make it the star of the show. Position it strategically within the frame to capture the viewer’s eye.

2. Use Color Contrast

Color plays a crucial role in food photography. Leverage color contrast to make your focal point pop. If your hero ingredient is predominantly red, place it against a contrasting background such as a deep green plate or a blue linen napkin. The stark contrast will draw attention to the focal point and add visual interest to the composition.

3. Play with Depth of Field

Experiment with the depth of field to emphasize your focal point. A shallow depth of field, achieved by using a wide aperture, can create a dreamy, blurred background that makes the focal point stand out. Conversely, a deep depth of field can be used to showcase multiple elements of the dish while still drawing attention to the hero ingredient.

4. Incorporate Texture

Texture adds another dimension to your focal point. Whether it’s the glistening sheen of a glaze, the rough surface of a sprinkle of sea salt, or the delicate crumb of a freshly baked loaf of bread, texture can entice the viewer to engage with the image. Capture the details of the hero ingredient’s texture to create a tactile connection through the photograph.

5. Utilize Composition Techniques

Composition can make or break a food photograph. Experiment with various composition techniques such as the rule of thirds, leading lines, and negative space to direct the viewer’s gaze towards the focal point. Remember that simplicity often reigns supreme in food photography, so aim to create a clean, uncluttered composition that highlights the focal point.

6. Harness Natural Light

Lighting is a cornerstone of photography, and food photography is no exception. Natural light is a game-changer when it comes to highlighting the focal point of your dish. Position your setup near a window or in an outdoor setting where soft, diffused light can gently illuminate the hero ingredient, enhancing its appeal and drawing attention to its finer details.

7. Tell a Story

Lastly, use the focal point to tell a story about the dish. Consider the context in which the dish is being enjoyed and incorporate elements that convey its narrative. Whether it’s a scattering of fresh herbs to evoke a sense of farm-to-table freshness or a sprinkling of spices that hints at exotic flavors, contextualizing the focal point adds depth and intrigue to the photograph.

In conclusion, creating a focal point in food photography is a blend of artistry and technique. By selecting the hero ingredient, leveraging color contrast, playing with depth of field, incorporating texture, utilizing composition techniques, harnessing natural light, and telling a story, you can draw attention to your dish and captivate your audience through the lens. Embrace experimentation, trust your instincts, and let your creativity shine as you master the art of creating a compelling focal point in food photography.
