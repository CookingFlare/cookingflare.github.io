How to Make Perfect Homemade Classic American Pancakes


If you’re looking for a delicious and comforting breakfast option, look no further than classic American pancakes. These fluffy, golden pancakes are a staple in many households and are surprisingly easy to make. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just craving a cozy morning treat, this recipe will guide you through creating the perfect homemade classic American pancakes.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • Butter or oil for cooking
  • Maple syrup, fresh fruits, or whipped cream for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk and egg. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter as this can result in tough pancakes. Finally, stir in the melted butter until the batter is smooth.

  2. Heat the Griddle or Pan: Place a non-stick griddle or large skillet over medium heat and allow it to warm up. You can test if the surface is ready by sprinkling a few drops of water on it – if the water dances and sizzles, the griddle is hot enough.

  3. Cook the Pancakes: Using a ladle or measuring cup, pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake and the edges start to look set, about 2-3 minutes. Flip the pancakes with a spatula and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes, until both sides are golden brown and the pancake is cooked through.

  4. Keep Warm and Serve: As you cook the pancakes, you can keep them warm by placing them on a baking sheet in a 200°F (93°C) oven. This will ensure that they stay fluffy and warm until you’re ready to serve. Once all the pancakes are cooked, serve them with a pat of butter, a drizzle of maple syrup, fresh fruits, or a dollop of whipped cream if desired.

Tips for Perfect Pancakes

  • Don’t Overmix: It’s important to mix the pancake batter until just combined. Overmixing can lead to tough and dense pancakes.
  • Fluffy Pancakes: For extra fluffy pancakes, let the batter rest for a few minutes before cooking. This allows the baking powder to activate and create air pockets in the batter.
  • Even Heat: Maintain a consistent heat while cooking the pancakes to ensure even browning and cooking.
  • Customize: Get creative with your pancake toppings! Try adding chocolate chips, blueberries, or even a sprinkle of cinnamon to the batter for a fun twist.

Now that you have the perfect homemade classic American pancake recipe, you can enjoy a delightful breakfast or brunch right in the comfort of your own kitchen. These pancakes are sure to become a favorite, and you can impress your family and friends with your pancake-making skills. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to flip some fluffy, golden pancakes!
