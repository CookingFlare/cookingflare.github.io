Traditional Easter Dinner Recipes with a Modern Twist


Easter, a time of reflection and rejuvenation, calls for a celebratory feast. This year, bring a contemporary twist to your traditional Easter dinner with these flavorful, innovative recipes. From tender roasted meats to vibrant vegetable dishes, your table is sure to be a hit!

Savory Breakfast Casserole

Indulge in a hearty breakfast-inspired casserole that serves as the perfect start to your Easter brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf stale bread, cut into 1-inch cubes (about 6 cups)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 cups diced ham
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 1 cup grated potatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Layer bread cubes in the prepared dish.
  3. Whisk eggs, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract in a medium bowl. Pour over bread cubes, ensuring all are evenly soaked.
  4. Arrange ham on top of bread. Sprinkle Swiss cheese, potatoes, and scallions evenly over the ham.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until golden brown and set in the middle.
  7. Allow to rest for 5 minutes before serving, providing extra toothpicks for easy portioning.

Colorful Balsamic Glazed Vegetables

Introduce a rainbow of color to your plate with this bright and flavorful side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound asparagus, ends trimmed
  • 1 pound baby carrots, halved
  • 1 pound sugar snap peas, ends trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup balsamic glaze
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard

Directions:

  1. Fill a large pot with water; bring to a rolling boil. For asparagus – cook for 3-5 minutes, for carrots – cook for 5-7 minutes, and for sugar snap peas – cook for 2-4 minutes. Drain each vegetable grouping separately.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add vegetables to the skillet individually in the following order: asparagus, carrots, and then sugar snap peas. Cook until heated through and spices are fully absorbed.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic glaze, parsley, and Dijon mustard. Pour mixture over vegetables, stirring to evenly coat.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve warm.

Lamb Meatballs with Lemon Herb Sauce

Introducing a unique main course to your Easter dinner, these delicious lamb meatballs boast an irresistible citrus twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh mint
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon Herb Sauce:
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground lamb, panko breadcrumbs, onion, mint, dill, and cumin. Season with salt and pepper. Form into 1-inch meatballs and place onto the prepared baking sheet.
  3. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until well-browned and cooked through.
  4. While lamb meatballs are cooking, prepare Lemon Herb Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, parsley, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  5. Once meatballs have finished cooking, transfer to a bowl and drizzle Lemon Herb Sauce evenly over top.

Revamp your annual Easter dinner with these innovative recipes. From a scrumptious breakfast-inspired casserole to vibrant vegetables and citrus-infused lamb meatballs, your Easter feast is on the cusp of tradition and trend. Bon appétit and Happy Easter!
