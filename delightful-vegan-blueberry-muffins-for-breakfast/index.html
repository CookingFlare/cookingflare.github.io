Delightful Vegan Blueberry Muffins for Breakfast


Who doesn’t love a warm and delicious blueberry muffin in the morning? The combination of juicy blueberries and soft, fluffy muffin batter is simply irresistible. And guess what? You don’t need eggs or dairy to achieve that perfect texture and flavor. Today, I’m going to share with you my recipe for delightful vegan blueberry muffins that will make your breakfasts a whole lot happier!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly with cooking spray.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Make sure they are well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the almond milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract until well incorporated.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix as it can result in dense muffins.
  5. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, making sure they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Using a spoon or an ice cream scoop, divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each one about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  8. Remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Serving Suggestions

These delightful vegan blueberry muffins are perfect on their own, but if you want to take them to the next level, here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Serve them warm with a pat of vegan butter or a drizzle of maple syrup for extra sweetness.
  • Pair them with a cup of hot coffee or tea for a cozy breakfast experience.
  • Enjoy them as an afternoon snack or pack them in your lunchbox for a tasty treat on the go.

Storage Tips

If you have any leftovers (which is highly unlikely!), you can store these muffins in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, you can freeze them individually wrapped in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer-safe bag. Just thaw them at room temperature or pop them in the microwave for a quick and easy breakfast.

The Perfect Vegan Blueberry Muffins

With this recipe, you can enjoy the delightful goodness of blueberry muffins without any animal products. These vegan muffins are moist, flavorful, and bursting with juicy blueberries in every bite. Whether you’re vegan or not, these muffins will become a breakfast favorite in your household.

So, why not treat yourself to a batch of these delightful vegan blueberry muffins? They’re easy to make, require simple ingredients, and will surely brighten up your mornings. Give them a try and let me know how they turn out. Happy baking!
