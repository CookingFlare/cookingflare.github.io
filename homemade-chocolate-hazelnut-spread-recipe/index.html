Homemade Chocolate Hazelnut Spread Recipe


Chocolate hazelnut spread has become a beloved treat for many around the world, whether it’s slathered on toast, used as a dip for fruit, or simply enjoyed by the spoonful. Store-bought versions of this sweet, creamy spread are easy to find, but nothing compares to the taste and satisfaction of homemade chocolate hazelnut spread. By making it from scratch, you not only control the ingredients but also customize the flavor to your liking.

This easy-to-follow homemade chocolate hazelnut spread recipe uses only a few simple ingredients to create a rich, smooth, and indulgent spread. The result is a spread that’s perfect for breakfast, desserts, snacks, or even as a gift for friends and family.

Why Make Homemade Chocolate Hazelnut Spread?

The store-bought versions of chocolate hazelnut spread often contain preservatives, artificial flavors, and excessive amounts of sugar. When you make it yourself, you can skip the unnecessary additives and make a more natural, healthier version. Plus, homemade chocolate hazelnut spread tends to have a richer, more robust flavor thanks to the freshly roasted hazelnuts and high-quality chocolate used in the process.

Ingredients

To make your own chocolate hazelnut spread at home, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Hazelnut Base:

  • 2 cups of raw hazelnuts (about 300 grams)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil or vegetable oil (optional, to help blend the nuts)

For the Chocolate Mixture:

  • 3/4 cup of dark chocolate chips or chopped dark chocolate (preferably 70% cocoa)
  • 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • 1/4 cup of powdered sugar (adjust for sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt (to balance the sweetness)
  • 1/4 cup of honey or maple syrup (for sweetness)
  • 1/4 cup of milk (or a non-dairy alternative like almond milk or oat milk)

These ingredients combine to create the perfect balance of creamy hazelnut flavor and rich chocolate indulgence.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Toast the Hazelnuts

The first step in making homemade chocolate hazelnut spread is to toast the hazelnuts. Toasting enhances their natural flavors and makes it easier to remove the skins. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Spread the hazelnuts in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the hazelnuts for 10–12 minutes, shaking the baking sheet halfway through to ensure they toast evenly. Keep a close eye on them, as they can burn quickly.
  4. Once toasted, remove the hazelnuts from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. The skins should easily loosen.

Step 2: Remove the Hazelnut Skins

To make your chocolate hazelnut spread smooth and creamy, you’ll need to remove the skins from the hazelnuts. The skins can have a slightly bitter taste, so it’s best to get rid of them. Here’s how:

  1. Place the roasted hazelnuts into a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Rub the towel together to loosen and remove the skins. It’s okay if not all the skins come off, but try to remove as much as possible.
  3. After rubbing the hazelnuts, place them in a food processor or blender. If you want a smoother consistency, you can also add a tablespoon of oil to help the blending process.

Step 3: Process the Hazelnuts

Now that the skins are removed, it’s time to transform the hazelnuts into a creamy base. In the food processor:

  1. Process the hazelnuts for 3–5 minutes, stopping occasionally to scrape down the sides of the bowl. The hazelnuts will first break down into small pieces, and then they will begin to release their natural oils, turning into a smooth paste.
  2. Keep processing until you achieve a creamy hazelnut butter consistency. This will serve as the base of your chocolate hazelnut spread.

Step 4: Melt the Chocolate

While the hazelnut butter is being processed, you can melt the chocolate. This will allow the chocolate to smoothly incorporate into the hazelnut mixture without clumping. Here’s how:

  1. Place the dark chocolate chips or chopped dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
  2. Melt the chocolate over a double boiler (a pot of simmering water with a bowl placed on top, not touching the water) or in the microwave in 20–30 second intervals, stirring between each interval.
  3. Stir until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth.

Step 5: Combine the Chocolate and Hazelnut Butter

Once the hazelnut butter is smooth and the chocolate is melted, it’s time to combine them. Add the melted chocolate to the food processor with the hazelnut butter. Add the cocoa powder, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, sea salt, and honey or maple syrup as well.

  1. Process the mixture for 2–3 minutes, until all the ingredients are fully combined and the spread is creamy and smooth.
  2. If the mixture is too thick, add the milk a little at a time, blending between each addition, until you reach your desired consistency. Some people prefer a thicker spread, while others like a slightly runnier texture.

Step 6: Taste and Adjust

Now is the time to taste your homemade chocolate hazelnut spread. If you feel it needs more sweetness, you can add a little extra powdered sugar or honey. If you want it richer in chocolate flavor, add more cocoa powder or melted chocolate. Remember, this spread is customizable to your taste preferences, so feel free to experiment with the flavor!

Step 7: Store the Spread

Once your homemade chocolate hazelnut spread is perfectly blended and smooth, transfer it to a clean, airtight jar or container. It will keep in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 weeks. Make sure to stir the spread before each use, as it may separate slightly when stored.

Tips for Perfect Chocolate Hazelnut Spread

  • Quality Chocolate: The quality of chocolate you use will greatly affect the flavor of the spread. Choose high-quality dark chocolate with a cocoa content of at least 70%. You can adjust the sweetness by using chocolate with a lower cocoa percentage if you prefer a sweeter spread.
  • Nuts: Toasting the hazelnuts is crucial for bringing out their flavor. If you don’t have hazelnuts on hand, you can try substituting with other nuts like almonds or cashews, but the flavor will be different.
  • Consistency: The consistency of your spread will depend on how much milk or oil you add. For a firmer spread, use less liquid, and for a smoother, runnier texture, add more liquid. You can even adjust the amount of sweetener if you want a less sweet spread.
  • Add-Ins: Get creative! You can add extra flavor by mixing in some ground cinnamon, a splash of coffee, or a pinch of chili powder for a spicy twist. If you love hazelnuts, you can even fold in chopped hazelnuts for a little extra texture in the spread.

Ways to Use Chocolate Hazelnut Spread

Homemade chocolate hazelnut spread is incredibly versatile, and you can use it in a variety of ways. Here are just a few ideas:

  1. On Toast or Bread: This classic use is probably the most popular way to enjoy chocolate hazelnut spread. Spread a generous amount on a slice of toast, croissant, or pancake for a quick breakfast or snack.

  2. In Desserts: Chocolate hazelnut spread can be used as a filling in cakes, cupcakes, and pastries. It pairs wonderfully with vanilla cake or pastry dough, creating a rich and indulgent treat.

  3. Dip for Fruit: For a healthier option, use your spread as a dip for fresh fruit like strawberries, bananas, or apple slices. The sweetness of the fruit balances perfectly with the creamy, chocolaty spread.

  4. In Smoothies: Add a spoonful of chocolate hazelnut spread to your smoothie for an extra touch of flavor. It pairs especially well with banana, almond milk, and a bit of ice for a creamy smoothie.

  5. In Oatmeal: Stir a spoonful of chocolate hazelnut spread into your morning oatmeal for a delicious twist on a classic breakfast.

  6. As a Gift: Homemade chocolate hazelnut spread makes a thoughtful gift. Package it in a cute jar with a ribbon, and it’s perfect for birthdays, holidays, or just because.

Conclusion

Making homemade chocolate hazelnut spread is a fun and rewarding experience. By using simple ingredients like hazelnuts, chocolate, and a touch of sweetness, you can create a delicious spread that’s far superior to anything you can buy in a store. Not only is it customizable, but it’s also free from the artificial additives found in many commercial spreads. Whether you use it on toast, in baking, or as a dip, this homemade chocolate hazelnut spread will quickly become a staple in your kitchen.
