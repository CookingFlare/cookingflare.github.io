How to Make Classic Brioche Rolls at Home


In the world of baking, few breads are as indulgent, soft, and versatile as brioche rolls. Whether served as dinner rolls, transformed into slider buns, or simply enjoyed on their own, brioche rolls offer a buttery richness that elevates any meal. While they might seem intimidating to make at first glance, preparing these golden, pillowy treats at home is absolutely achievable with the right recipe and tips.

This step-by-step guide will walk you through exactly how to master homemade brioche rolls, ensuring you achieve a light texture, rich flavor, and perfect golden crust every time.

Ingredients for Classic Brioche Rolls

Making brioche rolls from scratch requires a handful of pantry staples combined with a few premium ingredients for the bread’s signature richness. For this recipe, you’ll need the following:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (or one packet)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (warm, about 100°F or 38°C)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened and cut into small pieces)

For the Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Activate the Yeast

To create the perfect brioche rolls, the first step is activating your yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and sugar, stirring gently to dissolve the sugar. Sprinkle the active dry yeast on top and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This ensures your yeast is alive and ready to give your rolls their signature airy texture.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook attachment, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. The addition of salt here is crucial for controlling the fermentation process and balancing the overall flavor of the brioche.

3. Combine Wet Ingredients

Once your yeast is activated, add it to the flour mixture along with the eggs. Start mixing on a low speed to roughly combine the ingredients. Gradually increase the speed to medium until a loose dough forms, which should take about 3-5 minutes.

4. Incorporate the Butter

Now comes the key to classic brioche: the butter. Slowly add the softened butter, one small piece at a time, allowing each piece to be fully incorporated before adding the next. This step can take about 7-10 minutes, so be patient. The dough will transform into a shiny, smooth, and slightly sticky consistency.

5. Knead and Rest the Dough

Once all the butter has been incorporated, continue kneading the dough with the mixer for another 5-7 minutes until it becomes elastic and smooth. If the dough is too sticky to handle, you can sprinkle a small amount of flour into the bowl, but avoid adding too much as this can toughen the rolls.

Form the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and allow it to proof in a warm, draft-free area for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Shaping and Second Rise

6. Divide the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any trapped air. Transfer it to a lightly floured surface and divide it into 12 equal portions for large rolls or 16 portions for smaller ones. Use a kitchen scale for accuracy if you prefer uniform pieces.

7. Shape the Rolls

Shape each portion of dough into a smooth ball by tucking the edges underneath and pinching them at the bottom. This creates surface tension, which helps the rolls maintain their round shape as they bake.

Place the shaped dough balls onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing them evenly to allow room for expansion. Cover loosely with plastic wrap or a towel and let them rise again for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until they have doubled in size.

Baking the Brioche Rolls

8. Prepare the Egg Wash

While the rolls are undergoing their second rise, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a small bowl, whisk together one egg and a tablespoon of milk to create an egg wash. This will give your brioche rolls their signature golden-brown glaze.

9. Brush and Bake

Gently brush the risen rolls with the egg wash using a pastry brush. Be careful not to deflate the dough as you work. For an extra shiny finish, you can apply two layers of egg wash, waiting a minute or two between coats.

Bake the rolls in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top and sound hollow when tapped. If you notice the tops browning too quickly, tent the rolls loosely with aluminum foil during the last few minutes of baking.

10. Cool and Serve

Once baked, transfer the brioche rolls to a wire rack to cool slightly. Serve warm for the ultimate experience, or store them in an airtight container for up to three days. Brioche rolls also freeze beautifully—simply reheat them in the oven to restore their soft texture.

Tips for a Perfect Bake

  • Temperature Control: Ensure the butter is soft but not melted, and use warm (not hot) milk to activate your yeast properly.
  • Timing is Everything: Don’t rush the proofs; proper rising times are what give brioche its tender texture.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Add a sprinkle of coarse sea salt on top before baking, or incorporate mix-ins like dried fruit or chocolate chips for a fun twist.

Ways to Enjoy Brioche Rolls

Once you’ve baked your classic brioche rolls, there are endless ways to enjoy them. Pair them with jam and butter for breakfast, use them as the base for gourmet sandwiches, or turn them into decadent bread pudding for dessert. Their slightly sweet, buttery flavor makes them the perfect complement to a variety of dishes.

Making homemade brioche rolls might take some time, but the results are well worth the effort. Not only will your kitchen smell amazing, but you’ll also have a batch of golden rolls that rival those of the best bakeries. Whether it’s your first attempt or you’re a seasoned baker, mastering brioche rolls is an achievement that will have friends and family coming back for more. Happy baking!
