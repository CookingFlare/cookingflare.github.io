Sneaky Chef: How to Hide Vegetables in Your Kids' Pizza and Flatbreads


Do your kids refuse to eat vegetables? Well, fret no more! Here’s a Sneaky Chef guide to hiding those greens in your little ones’ favorites - pizza and flatbreads. These easy-to-follow recipes are perfect for sneaking in nutrients, without compromising on taste.

Veggie-Packed Pizzas

Forget about the traditional pepperoni or plain cheese pizzas. We’re here to bring you some exciting veggie-loaded pizzas that your kids will love! Let’s kick things off with a lesson on how to get your children to eat their greens, one slice at a time.

Spinach-Pesto Base

Spinach is easily one of the most hated vegetables among kids. But did you know that a little spin on classic pesto can make it even more appetizing to them? Replace the usual basil in your pesto recipe with fresh spinach. You’ll barely notice the difference, but your kids will!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups of your preferred pizza dough
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine your spinach leaves, pine nuts, Parmesan, and garlic.
  2. Slowly add the olive oil into the mix, and blend until you achieve a smooth, creamy pesto.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste and set your spinach-pesto sauce aside.
  4. Roll out your pizza dough and spread a thin layer of the spinach-pesto sauce on top.
  5. Sprinkle your shredded mozzarella on top, and bake your veggie-loaded pizza in a preheated oven (450°F or 230°C) for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown.

Roasted Veggie Toppings

Roasted vegetables add such an intense flavor to your pizza that it’s impossible not to love them. Here’s a colorful combination of veggies that can enhance your pizza game to a whole new level.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella

Instructions:

  1. Toss your sliced zucchini, red and yellow bell peppers, and red onion in olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast your veggie mixture in a preheated oven (425°F or 220°C) for about 20-25 minutes, until they develop a slightly crispy exterior.
  3. Spread your spinach-pesto sauce over your pizza dough, followed by a generous helping of the roasted veggies, and sprinkle the shredded mozzarella over the top.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for another 5-10 minutes or until the cheese turns golden and bubbly.

Veggie-Infused Flatbreads

Now that you’ve figured out how to transform your regular pizza into a green delicacy, let’s move on to flatbreads. Flatbreads are incredibly versatile and pair deliciously with a variety of vegetables.

Pesto and Sun-Dried Tomato Flatbread

If your kids are fans of caprese salads, then they’ll definitely love this flatbread. This sun-dried tomato and pesto combination is not only flavorsome but also makes it easy to sneak in loads of nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh basil
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups of your preferred flatbread dough
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella

Instructions:

  1. Combine your basil, sun-dried tomatoes, pine nuts, Parmesan, and garlic in a food processor.
  2. Blend until you achieve a smooth paste, then slowly incorporate your olive oil.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste and set your sun-dried tomato-pesto sauce aside.
  4. Roll out your flatbread dough and spread a thin layer of your sun-dried tomato-pesto sauce.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella over your flatbread and bake them in a preheated oven (450°F or 230°C) for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust becomes golden brown.

Final Thoughts

Remember that hiding vegetables doesn’t have to mean sacrificing taste. You can still create mouthwatering meals that will have your kids asking for seconds. Don’t be afraid to experiment with your recipes and add more creative vegetable combinations in the future. The more colorful your dishes, the more likely your kids will enjoy them. Happy cooking!
