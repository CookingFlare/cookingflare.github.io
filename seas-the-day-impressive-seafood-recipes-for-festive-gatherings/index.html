Seas the Day: Impressive Seafood Recipes for Festive Gatherings


There’s a certain opulence about seafood that sets the mood for any festive gathering—be it a lavish Christmas’ eve dinner, an astounding New Year’s Eve party or a joyous summer barbecue. Not only does it tantalize the taste buds with its unique flavors, but the freshness and nutritional value of seafood ensure a gastronomical delight while keeping things light and healthy. Today, let’s explore some impressive seafood recipes that’ll surely impress your guests, and help you celebrate the occasion in style!

1. Cork ’n’ Shell Shrimp Ceviche

This vibrant, South American-inspired appetizer is an excellent demonstration of minimalism and taste. Refreshing and zesty, this dish boasts of the ocean’s bounty in a lip-smacking avatar.

Ingredients:

  • 750g of fresh shrimp, peeled, deveined, and chopped
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 Red Chili, Deseeded and Chopped
  • 2 tbsp Red Onion, Chopped
  • 1 Avocado, Cubed
  • ½ cup Cilantro Leaves, Chopped
  • ⅓ cup Ketchup
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemons, limes, or Tortilla chips for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the chopped shrimp with lime juice and sprinkle salt to taste.
  2. Allow the mixture to marinate for about 30 minutes. This allows the proteins in shrimp to cook under the acid of lime juice.
  3. Once marinated, add the chopped red chili, red onion, cubed avocado, and chopped coriander to the mix.
  4. Combine ketchup, salt, and pepper and pour over the ceviche. Mix everything well.
  5. Serve chilled in a wide-mouthed bowl or in individual glasses, accompanied by wedges of lemon, lime, or tortilla chips for scooping.

2. Seared Tuna Steaks with Mango Salsa

A heart-healthy yet indulgent dish, Seared Tuna Steaks paired with a sweet and spicy Mango Salsa, make for a satisfying main course.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tuna steaks, around 1-inch thick
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 1 Mango, Diced
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper, Diced
  • 1 Red Onion, Diced
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil
  • ¼ cup Fresh Cilantro, Chopped
  • 2 tbsp of red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Season the tuna steaks with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Sear the tuna steaks for 2-3 minutes on each side, depending on your preferred level of doneness.
  3. While the tuna steaks are searing, you can make the salsa. In a bowl, combine diced mango, bell pepper, red onion, lime juice, olive oil, cilantro, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Once the tuna is seared to your liking, remove the steaks from the pan, and let them sit for a few minutes to retain the juices.
  5. Plate the tuna steaks with a generous serving of mango salsa on top.

3. Butter Poached Lobster with Asparagus

A luxurious seafood feast on its own, this butter-poached lobster is paired with sautéed asparagus to create an exquisite combination of flavors that will undoubtedly leave your guests sighing with delight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole lobsters, about 1 ½ lbs each
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • 1 cup asparagus spears
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large pot halfway with water, add salt, and bring it to a boil. Add the whole lobsters and cook them for 6-7 minutes. Once cooked, drain the water, and allow the lobsters to cool down.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the butter in a separate saucepan. Add the thyme, lemon zest, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Cook on low heat for about 10 minutes.
  3. Once the lobsters are cool enough to handle, split them in half lengthwise. Remove the meat from the shells, and place them in the butter sauce, cooking them for 1-2 minutes.
  4. While the lobster is poaching, blanch the asparagus in boiling salted water for 2 minutes. Drain and pat dry.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat, add the asparagus spears, and cook until tender. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Plate the asparagus, and arrange the butter-poached lobster pieces on top.

4. Piri-Piri Grilled Salmon

A spicy-salty twist to your classic grilled salmon, this Piri-Piri Grilled Salmon is a bold and beautiful entrée that is sure to set your party apart.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets, skin on
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 1 Lime, Juiced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chili, minced (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. In a bowl, combine lime juice, garlic, cilantro, and bird’s eye chili. Add the olive oil and whisk well to emulsify.
  3. Coat the salmon fillets on both sides with the piri-piri marinade.
  4. Preheat your barbecue or grill pan, and place the salmon fillets, skin-side down, over medium high-heat.
  5. Allow the salmon to cook without flipping for about 5 minutes or until the skin is crispy.
  6. Flip the salmon and cook for another 4 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F.
  7. Remove the salmon from the heat, and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

No festive gathering is complete without satisfactory seafood recipes inspired by different corners of the globe. These impressive seafood recipes not only bring the element of surprise to your table but also provide the opportunity to connect with others through food while involving your guests in the joy of cooking. So, seas the day, and let the celebration begin! Happy cooking!
