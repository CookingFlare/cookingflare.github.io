How to Make Focaccia: A Fluffy Italian Flatbread


Focaccia, a beloved classic from Italian kitchens, is a simple yet richly satisfying bread with its soft, airy texture and irresistible golden crust. Its versatility makes it a crowd favorite—whether served as a standalone snack, a sandwich base, or a side to any meal. The best part? You don’t need to be a professional baker to craft a perfect tray of focaccia in your own kitchen. With a handful of pantry staples and a bit of patience, you’ll be able to master this art and enjoy homemade focaccia that’s perfectly fluffy, fragrant, and flavorful.

Let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this traditional Italian flatbread, peppered with tips to ensure success every time you bake.

The Key Ingredients for Focaccia

To begin, gather these essential ingredients:

  • Flour: All-purpose flour works wonders for focaccia, but bread flour can be used if you prefer a chewier texture.
  • Yeast: You’ll need active dry yeast or instant yeast to help your dough rise beautifully.
  • Water: Lukewarm water is crucial for activating the yeast, encouraging a proper rise.
  • Olive Oil: High-quality extra virgin olive oil elevates the flavor and adds richness to your focaccia.
  • Salt: Essential for balancing flavor.
  • Herbs and Toppings: Rosemary, sea salt, olives, cherry tomatoes, or caramelized onions are traditional favorites, but feel free to get creative.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Perfect Focaccia

1. Activate the Yeast

In a small bowl, combine 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) of active dry yeast with 1 ½ cups of lukewarm water. Add a teaspoon of sugar to feed the yeast and stir gently. Let this mixture rest for about 5-10 minutes, or until you see a frothy layer form on the surface. This step confirms your yeast is alive and ready to work its magic.

2. Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine 4 cups of flour and 2 teaspoons of salt. Create a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture, followed by 3 tablespoons of olive oil. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix until a shaggy dough forms. The dough will be sticky—that’s perfectly normal.

3. Knead and First Rise

Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Alternatively, you can knead the dough in a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment for about 5-6 minutes.

Place the kneaded dough into a lightly oiled bowl, turning it to coat the surface with oil. Cover with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm, draft-free spot for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

4. Prepare the Pan

Generously coat the bottom and sides of a 9x13-inch baking pan (or a similar size sheet pan) with olive oil. The oil not only prevents sticking but also creates the signature crispy crust.

5. Press the Dough into the Pan

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to the prepared pan and use your fingertips to stretch and press it evenly into the edges. Don’t worry if it doesn’t reach all corners immediately—give it a few minutes to relax, then stretch it again.

6. Dimple the Dough

Here’s the fun part! Use your fingertips to dimple the surface of the dough, pressing deeply without tearing through. These indentations will trap olive oil and any toppings, adding to the bread’s flavor and texture.

7. Add Olive Oil and Toppings

Drizzle the dough generously with olive oil, ensuring it pools into the dimples. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and fresh rosemary, or add any toppings of your choice. Some delicious variations include:

  • Olive and Sundried Tomato: Scatter sliced green or black olives alongside julienned sundried tomatoes.
  • Garlic and Parmesan: Sprinkle minced garlic and grated cheese for a savory twist.
  • Tomato and Basil: Add halved cherry tomatoes and a few whole basil leaves for a fresh, vibrant option.

8. Second Rise

Cover the pan loosely with plastic wrap or a kitchen towel and let the dough rise again for 30-45 minutes. This second rise ensures a soft, airy texture in the finished bread.

9. Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Bake the focaccia on the center rack for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the edges are crisp. Keep a close eye during the last few minutes to avoid overbrowning.

Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and allow the focaccia to cool for a few minutes. For an extra touch, drizzle a bit more olive oil over the surface while it’s still warm.

10. Slice and Serve

Use a sharp knife or pizza cutter to slice the focaccia into squares, rectangles, or wedges. Serve it warm or at room temperature, either on its own or alongside soups, salads, or antipasti.

Tips for Success

  • Be Patient with Rising: Good bread takes time. Allowing sufficient rising time is essential for achieving the fluffy, airy texture synonymous with focaccia.
  • Don’t Skimp on Olive Oil: Generous use of olive oil ensures a rich flavor and the signature crisp exterior.
  • Experiment with Toppings: Focaccia is the perfect canvas for culinary creativity. Try combinations like roasted garlic and thyme, or even sweet options like sliced figs and honey.
  • Storage: Store leftover focaccia in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. To reheat, place it in a warm oven for a few minutes to restore its freshness.

The Joy of Homemade Focaccia

Making focaccia from scratch is more than just crafting delicious bread—it’s a deeply rewarding experience. From the moment the yeast begins to bubble, to the aroma that fills your kitchen as it bakes, every step invites you to connect with the timeless tradition of Italian baking.

Whether you’re a seasoned baker or trying your hand at bread-making for the first time, focaccia’s forgiving process makes it an excellent recipe to master. So roll up your sleeves, embrace the olive oil, and get ready to enjoy a homemade Italian flatbread that’s destined to become a staple in your recipe repertoire.

Buon appetito!
