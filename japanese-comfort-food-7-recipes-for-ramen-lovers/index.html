Japanese Comfort Food: 7 Recipes for Ramen Lovers


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes rival the warmth and satisfaction of a steaming bowl of ramen. Originally from China, this noodle soup has evolved into a quintessential part of Japanese cuisine, with countless regional variations and personal twists. For ramen lovers, the quest for the perfect bowl is a delightful journey, filled with the rich flavors of broth, the springy texture of noodles, and the harmonious blend of toppings.

In this post, we’ll explore seven mouthwatering ramen recipes that celebrate the diversity of this beloved dish. Each recipe brings something unique to the table, whether it’s the simplicity of a classic Shoyu Ramen or the deep flavors of Tonkotsu. Let’s dive into these culinary creations that are sure to warm your heart and satisfy your cravings.

1. Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups chicken stock
    • 2 cups water
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon mirin
    • 1 tablespoon sake
    • 1 inch ginger, sliced
    • 2 cloves garlic, smashed

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Soft-boiled eggs (marinated in soy sauce)
    • Sliced green onions
    • Nori (seaweed)
    • Chashu (braised pork belly)
    • Bean sprouts

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, combine the chicken stock, water, soy sauce, mirin, sake, ginger, and garlic. Bring to a boil and then let it simmer for 30 minutes.

  2. Cook the Noodles: In another pot, boil water and cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Ramen: In a bowl, place the cooked noodles, ladle the broth over them, and top with soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, chashu, and bean sprouts. Serve hot.

Shoyu Ramen is a great introduction to the world of ramen. Its savory soy sauce base pairs perfectly with the rich toppings for a balanced and comforting bowl.

2. Tonkotsu Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 2 pounds pork bones (neck bones or trotters)
    • 1 onion, halved
    • 1 head garlic, halved
    • 1 inch ginger, sliced
    • 10 cups water

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Chashu (braised pork belly)
    • Soft-boiled eggs
    • Menma (bamboo shoots)
    • Sliced scallions
    • Black garlic oil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Broth: Place the pork bones in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes. Discard the water and rinse the bones to remove impurities. In the same pot, add the cleaned bones, onion, garlic, ginger, and 10 cups of water. Simmer on low for at least 12 hours, adding water as necessary to keep the bones submerged.

  2. Prepare the Noodles: Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  3. Serve: In a bowl, combine the cooked noodles and hot tonkotsu broth. Top with chashu, soft-boiled eggs, menma, scallions, and a drizzle of black garlic oil for an extra flavor kick.

Tonkotsu Ramen is known for its rich, creamy broth, achieved through hours of simmering pork bones. It’s a labor of love that results in a deeply satisfying bowl.

3. Miso Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups chicken stock
    • 2 cups water
    • 1/4 cup miso paste (red or white)
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Ground pork (pan-fried)
    • Corn
    • Bean sprouts
    • Sliced green onions
    • Soft-boiled eggs

Instructions:

  1. Make the Broth: In a pot, combine chicken stock, water, miso paste, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Heat over medium until miso has dissolved completely, then reduce to low.

  2. Cook the Noodles: Prepare the ramen noodles as per the package instructions and drain.

  3. Assemble the Bowl: Place the cooked noodles in a bowl, pour the miso broth over the top, and garnish with pan-fried ground pork, corn, bean sprouts, green onions, and soft-boiled eggs.

Miso Ramen has a unique umami flavor profile that’s both hearty and comforting, making it a favorite among ramen aficionados.

4. Shio Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups chicken stock
    • 2 cups water
    • 1/4 cup sea salt (adjust to taste)
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 inch ginger, sliced

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Sliced chicken breast (grilled or poached)
    • Kamaboko (fish cake)
    • Sliced green onions
    • Nori
    • Soft-boiled eggs

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, combine chicken stock, water, salt, soy sauce, and ginger. Bring to a simmer and allow to cook for 20 minutes.

  2. Noodle Preparation: Cook the ramen noodles as per the package instructions and set aside.

  3. Final Assembly: In each bowl, add the noodles, pour the shio broth over them, and top with sliced chicken, kamaboko, green onions, nori, and soft-boiled eggs.

Shio Ramen is the lightest of the ramen varieties, allowing the flavors of the sea salt and broth to shine, complemented beautifully by the toppings.

5. Curry Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups chicken stock
    • 1 tablespoon curry powder
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon miso paste

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Sliced pork or chicken
    • Carrots, julienned
    • Daikon radish, shredded
    • Soft-boiled eggs
    • Chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Make the Broth: In a pot, heat chicken stock and stir in curry powder, soy sauce, and miso paste. Simmer for 15 minutes.

  2. Cook Noodles: In a separate pot, cook the ramen noodles as per the package instructions.

  3. Combine: Place the noodles in bowls, pour the curry broth over, and top with sliced meat, carrots, daikon, soft-boiled eggs, and cilantro.

Curry Ramen takes a spicy twist on the traditional dish, offering a warm and invigorating flavor profile that’s perfect for a cold day.

6. Vegan Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups vegetable stock
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon miso paste
    • 1 inch ginger, sliced
    • 2 cloves garlic, smashed

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Tofu (cubed and pan-fried)
    • Seaweed
    • Bean sprouts
    • Sliced green onions
    • Corn

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: Combine vegetable stock, water, soy sauce, miso paste, ginger, and garlic in a pot. Bring to a simmer and allow to cook for 20 minutes.

  2. Noodle Cooking: Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions and drain.

  3. Serve: In bowls, add noodles, pour the broth over, and top with tofu, seaweed, bean sprouts, green onions, and corn.

Vegan Ramen is an excellent option for those looking for a meat-free alternative, packed with flavor and nutrients while being completely plant-based.

7. Spicy Ramen

Ingredients:

  • Broth:

    • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
    • 2 cups water
    • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (Chinese chili bean paste)
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

  • Noodles:

    • 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles

  • Toppings:

    • Sliced pork or chicken
    • Soft-boiled eggs
    • Sliced jalapeños
    • Sliced green onions
    • Nori

Instructions:

  1. Broth Preparation: In a pot, combine stock, water, doubanjiang, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Bring to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.

  2. Noodle Cooking: Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions and drain.

  3. Final Assembly: Place the noodles in bowls, ladle the spicy broth over, and top with meat, soft-boiled eggs, jalapeños, green onions, and nori.

Spicy Ramen caters to those who enjoy a kick in their meal, balancing heat with the savory flavors of the broth.

Conclusion

Ramen is more than just a dish; it’s an experience that brings people together. From the comforting warmth of Shoyu to the rich and creamy Tonkotsu, each recipe offers a unique taste of Japanese culinary culture. Whether you prefer your ramen spicy, vegan, or loaded with toppings, there’s a recipe here for every ramen lover. Grab your bowls, gather your ingredients, and embark on a delicious ramen journey right in your kitchen! Enjoy the process and let each slurp bring you closer to the heart of Japan.
