Slow Cooker Game Day Recipes for Football Fans


Game day is one of the most exciting times of the year for football fans. Whether you’re cheering for your local team or hosting a viewing party for the big game, having delicious food ready to keep everyone satisfied is a must. What could be easier than letting your slow cooker do the work for you? Here are some fantastic slow cooker game day recipes that will have your guests cheering for more than just the score!

1. Slow Cooker Chili: A Classic Choice

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds ground beef or turkey
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground meat over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Transfer the meat to the slow cooker and add the onion, garlic, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir to combine and cover. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
  4. Serve hot with your favorite toppings.

Why It Works:

Chili is hearty, filling, and can easily be customized with different beans or spice levels. Plus, it’s perfect for making ahead of time!

2. BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 pounds pork shoulder
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite brand)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Slider buns
  • Coleslaw (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Place the sliced onion at the bottom of the slow cooker. Season the pork shoulder with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper, then place it on top of the onions.
  2. Pour the BBQ sauce over the pork. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours or high for 4 hours until the meat is tender and easily shredded.
  3. Shred the pork in the slow cooker using two forks, mixing it with the BBQ sauce.
  4. Serve on slider buns topped with coleslaw.

Why It Works:

Pulled pork sliders are a crowd-pleaser. They’re easy to eat while cheering for your team and can be prepared well in advance.

3. Buffalo Chicken Dip

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie works great)
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup buffalo sauce
  • 1 cup ranch dressing
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Optional: diced green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In your slow cooker, combine the shredded chicken, cream cheese, buffalo sauce, ranch dressing, and half of the cheddar cheese.
  2. Stir until mixed well. Cover and cook on low for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
  3. Before serving, sprinkle the remaining cheese on top and let it melt for about 15 minutes.
  4. Garnish with diced green onions and serve with tortilla chips or celery sticks.

Why It Works:

Buffalo Chicken Dip combines the tangy flavors of buffalo sauce with the creaminess of cheese, making it a perfect game day snack that can be enjoyed throughout the game.

4. Hearty Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 3 large carrots, sliced
  • 3 potatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the beef with salt and pepper. Place it in the slow cooker along with carrots, potatoes, onion, beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and thyme.
  2. Stir to combine, cover, and cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-6 hours until the beef is tender.
  3. Serve hot with crusty bread for dipping.

Why It Works:

Beef stew is a comforting dish that warms everyone’s hearts and stomachs. It’s perfect for those colder game days and can easily be made a day ahead.

5. Spinach and Artichoke Dip

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In the slow cooker, combine artichokes, spinach, cream cheese, sour cream, mayonnaise, mozzarella, Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix thoroughly, cover, and cook on low for 2-3 hours until everything is heated through and cheese is melted.
  3. Serve with pita chips or fresh vegetables.

Why It Works:

This dip is a creamy, cheesy delight that balances the heartiness of other dishes. It’s a great vegetarian option that everyone will enjoy.

6. Sweet and Spicy Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds frozen meatballs (beef, turkey, or chicken)
  • 1 cup grape jelly
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. In the slow cooker, mix the grape jelly, BBQ sauce, and hot sauce until well combined.
  2. Add the frozen meatballs and stir to coat them in the sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 4-6 hours until heated through.
  4. Serve with toothpicks for easy snacking.

Why It Works:

These meatballs are a perfect blend of sweet and spicy, making them irresistible. They’re also incredibly easy to prepare, allowing you to focus on the game.

7. Ratatouille: A Vegetable Delight

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (14 oz each) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place all the vegetables in the slow cooker, followed by the diced tomatoes, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir to combine, cover, and cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Serve as a side dish or over rice for a hearty vegetarian option.

Why It Works:

Ratatouille is vibrant and healthy, offering a refreshing contrast to heavier game day foods. It’s also a great way to include more vegetables in your spread.

8. Cheesy Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup
  • ½ cup onion, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix the diced potatoes, cheddar cheese, sour cream, cream of chicken soup, onion, melted butter, salt, and pepper in the slow cooker.
  2. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the potatoes are tender and the cheese is bubbly.
  3. Serve hot, garnished with additional cheese or chives, if desired.

Why It Works:

This casserole is a comforting and rich side dish that’s perfect for pairing with meat-based mains. It’s creamy, cheesy, and always a hit!

Game Day Preparation Tips

  • Prep Ahead: Many of these recipes can be prepared the night before. Chop vegetables, measure out ingredients, and store them in your slow cooker insert in the refrigerator. In the morning, just place it in the slow cooker and turn it on.

  • Double Up: If you expect a large crowd, consider making two different recipes of the same dish (like chili or meatballs) in separate slow cookers. This way, you can cater to different tastes.

  • Diverse Options: Ensure you have a variety of dishes – something hearty, something light, and some snacks. This will keep everyone happy and satisfied.

  • Stay Hydrated: Don’t forget to provide drinks! Consider setting up a cooler with a variety of beverages, from sodas to flavored sparkling water, and maybe even a signature game day cocktail.

Conclusion

With these slow cooker game day recipes, you’ll be well on your way to hosting an unforgettable football viewing party. Each recipe offers something unique, catering to different tastes and preferences, ensuring that all your guests leave with full bellies and happy hearts. As the game unfolds, enjoy the celebration of food, friends, and football – all made easier with the magic of the slow cooker!
