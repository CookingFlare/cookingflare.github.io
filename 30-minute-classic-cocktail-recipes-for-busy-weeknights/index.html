30-Minute Classic Cocktail Recipes for Busy Weeknights


When it comes to a busy weeknight, it’s easy to get caught up in the hustle and bustle of daily life and forget to take a moment to unwind and relax. But, a classic cocktail can be just what you need to take the edge off and recharge for the rest of the evening. The problem is, who has the time to spend hours in the kitchen perfecting a fancy cocktail when you’re already running late for dinner?

That’s why we’ve put together this collection of 30-minute classic cocktail recipes that are perfect for busy weeknights. From timeless favorites to modern twists, these recipes are quick, easy, and guaranteed to impress.

Classic Gin Fizz

  • 1 1/2 oz gin
  • 1/2 oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • 1/2 oz club soda
  • Dash of Angostura bitters
  • Lemon wheel, for garnish

Combine gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a Collins glass filled with ice. Top with club soda and garnish with a lemon wheel. Add a dash of Angostura bitters for a classic touch.

Vodka Spritz

  • 1 1/2 oz vodka
  • 2 oz Prosecco
  • 1 oz Aperol
  • 1 oz soda water
  • Splash of orange juice
  • Slice of orange, for garnish

Combine vodka, Prosecco, Aperol, and soda water in a wine glass filled with ice. Stir gently to combine, then top with a splash of orange juice. Garnish with a slice of orange and serve immediately.

Whiskey Sour

  • 2 oz whiskey
  • 3/4 oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • Egg white
  • Dash of Angostura bitters
  • Lemon wheel, for garnish

Combine whiskey, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until frothy, then strain into a rocks glass filled with ice. Add a dash of Angostura bitters and garnish with a lemon wheel.

Moscow Mule

  • 1 1/2 oz vodka
  • 4 oz ginger beer
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Slice of lime, for garnish
  • Sprig of mint, for garnish

Combine vodka and ginger beer in a copper mug or highball glass filled with ice. Squeeze in the juice of 1 lime and drop the lime wheel into the glass. Stir gently to combine, then garnish with a sprig of mint.

Dark ‘N’ Stormy

  • 1 1/2 oz dark rum
  • 4 oz ginger beer
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Slice of lime, for garnish

Combine dark rum and ginger beer in a highball glass filled with ice. Squeeze in the juice of 1 lime and drop the lime wheel into the glass. Stir gently to combine, then garnish with a slice of lime.

Sidecar

  • 1 1/2 oz Cognac
  • 1/2 oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 oz triple sec
  • Sugar cube
  • Lemon twist, for garnish

Combine Cognac, lemon juice, and triple sec in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a sugar-rimmed coupe glass. Garnish with a lemon twist and serve immediately.

Mai Tai

  • 2 oz dark rum
  • 1 oz light rum
  • 1 oz freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 oz orange curaçao
  • 1/2 oz orgeat syrup
  • Splash of club soda
  • Lime wheel, for garnish
  • Mint sprig, for garnish

Combine dark rum, light rum, lime juice, orange curaçao, and orgeat syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a tiki glass filled with ice. Top with a splash of club soda and garnish with a lime wheel and mint sprig.

These classic cocktails are sure to become your new go-to’s for busy weeknights. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of creativity, you can create a delicious and refreshing drink that’s sure to impress. So, the next time you’re short on time but still want to enjoy a great cocktail, give one of these recipes a try. Your taste buds (and your sanity) will thank you!
