Mediterranean-Inspired Stuffed Calamari: A Seafood Delight


If you’re a seafood lover and looking for a delicious and elegant dish to impress your guests, look no further than Mediterranean-inspired stuffed calamari. This recipe combines the flavors of the Mediterranean with the tender and delicate texture of calamari, creating a truly delightful seafood experience.

Ingredients

  • 8 medium-sized calamari tubes
  • 1 cup cooked couscous
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked couscous, crumbled feta cheese, chopped Kalamata olives, parsley, lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. Take the calamari tubes and rinse them under cold water. Pat them dry with a paper towel.

  4. Gently stuff each calamari tube with the couscous mixture, being careful not to overfill. Leave a little space at the top to allow for expansion during cooking.

  5. Once all the calamari tubes are stuffed, secure the openings with toothpicks, ensuring that the filling stays intact.

  6. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Carefully place the stuffed calamari tubes in the skillet and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until lightly golden brown.

  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes until the calamari is fully cooked and tender.

  8. Remove the toothpicks before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley and a squeeze of lemon juice for an extra burst of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Mediterranean-inspired stuffed calamari pairs perfectly with a refreshing Greek salad and a side of roasted vegetables. It also makes a great appetizer or main course for a special dinner party. Serve with a glass of crisp white wine to complement the flavors of the dish.

Tips and Variations

  • If you can’t find fresh calamari tubes, you can use frozen ones. Just make sure to thaw them thoroughly before cooking.
  • Feel free to experiment with different fillings for the calamari. You can add chopped tomatoes, roasted red peppers, or even cooked shrimp for an extra seafood twist.
  • To add a bit of heat, sprinkle some crushed red pepper flakes over the stuffed calamari before baking.
  • Leftovers can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave until warmed through.

Conclusion

Mediterranean-inspired stuffed calamari is a seafood lover’s dream come true. The combination of tender calamari and flavorful couscous filling, infused with the vibrant aromas of the Mediterranean, creates a dish that is both elegant and satisfying. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the sea, this recipe is sure to impress. So, put on your chef’s hat and dive into the world of Mediterranean cuisine with this seafood delight. Bon appétit!
