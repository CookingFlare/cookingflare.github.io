Thanksgiving Recipes for a Family Reunion


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and fantastic food. Nothing brings people together quite like the smell of a delicious meal wafting from the kitchen. Here’s a collection of recipes perfect for bringing loved ones together at your family reunion, making memories, and creating a taste of togetherness.

The All-American Turkey

No Thanksgiving meal would be complete without the star of the show - the turkey. With this succulent and juicy recipe, your reunion attendees will be begging for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 (12-14 pound) whole turkey
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 teaspoons chopped fresh thyme
  • 4 teaspoons chopped fresh sage
  • 2 teaspoons grated lemon zest
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
  2. Rinse turkey, then pat dry. Place on a rack in a roasting pan.
  3. In a medium bowl, mix butter, thyme, sage, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Rub the mixture all over the turkey.
  4. Roast the turkey for about 3 hours or until it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F.
  5. Once done, let it rest for about 15 minutes before carving. Serve with chicken broth gravy for a true Thanksgiving indulgence.

A Heirloom Side: Grandmother’s Green Beans

This iconic side dish will take you straight back to your grandmother’s kitchen, with its simple, comforting charm.

Ingredients:

  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans green beans, drained
  • 1 (8 ounce) package sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 1 cup sliced onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped green bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon celery salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat.
  2. Cook mushrooms, onions, and green bell pepper in butter for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add green beans, parsley, celery salt, garlic powder, salt, and pepper to the pan, stirring well.
  4. Cook for another 10 minutes or until beans reach your desired tenderness.
  5. Serve and enjoy this delicious reminder of family tradition.

Homestyle Mashed Potatoes: The Comfort of Carbs

Nostalgic and indulgent, there’s nothing quite like homemade mashed potatoes. This recipe is an ode to the humble spud.

Ingredients:

  • 6 pounds russet potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup minced fresh chives
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water until tender, approximately 20 minutes.
  2. Drain well and return potatoes to the hot pot.
  3. Add milk, heavy cream, melted butter, and chives. Mash with a potato masher until the desired consistency is reached.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve smooth and creamy mashed potatoes as the perfect companion to your roasted turkey.

A Sweet Ending: Hometown Pecan Pie

What better way to end a Thanksgiving feast than with a slice of southern comfort in the form of a warm pecan pie?

Ingredients:

  • 1 (9 inch) unbaked pie crust
  • 2 cups chopped pecans
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together pecans, sugar, cinnamon, and a teaspoon of vanilla.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, and heavy cream. Then, stir in the pecan mixture.
  4. Pour filling into pie crust.
  5. Bake until filling is set and the crust is golden brown, approximately 50-60 minutes.
  6. Allow to cool slightly before serving. A dollop of whipped cream adds a delightful finishing touch.

From the heartiest of mains to the most delectable of sides and desserts, these recipes are sure to delight your family and friends at your next family reunion. With each bite, cherish the memories and love that fill your home and tables on Thanksgiving. Happy feasting!
