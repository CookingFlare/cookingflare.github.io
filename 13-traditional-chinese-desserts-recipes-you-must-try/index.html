13 Traditional Chinese Desserts Recipes You Must Try


Chinese cuisine is a rich tapestry of exquisite flavors, unique textures, and stunning visual appeal. Among the numerous delicious dishes, Chinese desserts stand out for their delightful sweetness, exquisite presentation, and exquisite ingredients. From traditional recipes passed down through generations to contemporary twists on old favorites, these thirteen traditional Chinese desserts are sure to satisfy even the most discerning palate.

  1. Red Bean Paste Moon Cakes

Mooncakes are an iconic Chinese dessert, particularly enjoyed during the Mid-Autumn Festival. Traditionally, the cakes are made from a rich, dense red bean paste encased in a delicate, flaky pastry crust. For an extra touch of festive indulgence, try filling your mooncakes with lotus seed paste or mashed tung-telephone-shaped fruit. Serve with a hot cup of Chinese tea for a delightful treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup red bean paste
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and butter. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, work the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together and forms a ball.

  3. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4-inch thickness.

  4. Cut the dough into 6-inch circles using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass.

  5. Place a spoonful of red bean paste in the center of each circle, then fold the dough over to form a crescent shape, sealing the edges with a little water.

  6. Bake at 375째F (190째C) for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  7. Stir-Fried Sesame Balls

These delectable treats feature a chewy exterior made of glutinous rice flour, surrounding a savory scoop of sweet red bean paste or lotus seed paste. Perfect for a chilly day or as a satisfying snack after a rich meal, these sesame balls are a must-try for any lover of Chinese desserts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup red bean paste or lotus seed paste
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour and water until a soft, pliable dough forms.

  2. Roll the dough into 1-inch balls, then flatten each ball slightly.

  3. Place a spoonful of red bean paste or lotus seed paste in the center of each flattened dough ball, then wrap and seal the filling completely, being sure that there are no gaps.

  4. Roll the filled balls in sesame seeds, pressing gently to ensure the seeds adhere.

  5. Heat 2 inches of oil in a deep pot or pan to 350째F (180째C). Fry the coated balls in batches until they are golden brown, about 5 minutes.

  6. Remove the sesame balls with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

  7. Snow Fungus & Gingko Nuts in Sweet Red Beverages

This refreshing dessert combines tender snow fungus and fragrant gingko nuts with sweet red beans, suspended in a lightly sweetened, chilled red-bean broth. Refreshingly light and aromatic, it’s a perfect treat to cool off with during the summer months.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup snow fungus (soaked in water for at least 2 hours)
  • 1/4 cup gingko nuts (soaked in water for at least 1 hour)
  • 1 cup red beans (soaked in water for at least 4 hours)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 10 dried dates
  • 2 cups water
  • Sweetener to taste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the snow fungus and gingko nuts under cool water, then set aside.

  2. In a large pot, bring water, soaked cinnamon stick and dried dates to a boil. Once boiling, add the soaked red beans and reduce heat to a simmer.

  3. Cook the beans for about 2 hours or until tender, skimming off any foam or debris that rises to the surface.

  4. Remove the cinnamon stick and dates, then add the snow fungus and gingko nuts. Cook for about 15 minutes more or until the fungus is tender and the nuts are cooked through.

  5. Strain the liquid, reserving it in a separate bowl, and transfer the beans, fungus, and nuts back to the pot.

  6. If needed, sweeten the broth with honey, maple syrup, or sugar to taste.

  7. Divide the fruit and fungus mixture between chilled bowls and pour the sweetened broth over the top to serve.

  8. Sweet Potato Chim Chim

Sweet potatoes are a staple ingredient in Traditional Chinese Medicine, recognized for their ability to boost digestion and balance blood sugar levels. This delicious, chewy dessert combines the sweetness of sweet potatoes with a soothing blend of sesame oil and a touch of brown sugar, creating a satisfying treat to finish off any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch thick slices
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the sesame seeds, brown sugar, and sesame oil.

  2. Brush the sweet potato slices with the sesame oil mixture, then sprinkle with the sesame seeds.

  3. Arrange the slices in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Bake at 375째F (190째C) for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and the sesame seeds are toasted.

  5. Serve with a cup of warm Chinese tea.

  6. Mango Pudding

This luscious, fruity dessert brings together the sweet, aromatic taste of ripe mangoes with the soothing creaminess of tapioca pearls for a refreshing treat. When mangoes are in season, try making this dessert with fresh mango puree for an unmatched burst of tropical flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup tapioca pearls
  • 1 ripe mango, pureed
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the water and tapioca pearls. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the tapioca pearls are clear and chewy.

  2. In a blender or food processor, blend the mango puree, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream until smooth.

  3. Divide the tapioca pearls between chilled glasses, then pour the mango mixture on top.

  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

  5. Pineapple Bread Pudding

Serve up a sweet end to a Chinese feast with this delectable pineapple bread pudding. Made with fresh, juicy pineapple pieces and delicate, eggy bread crumb, this dessert is the perfect combination of sweet and tart flavors to leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups bread, cubed
  • 1 pineapple, peeled, cored, and cubed
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350째F (180째C).

  2. In a large bowl, toss bread cubes with melted butter, then spread out in a single layer on a baking sheet.

  3. Bake until golden brown, stirring occasionally, for about 12 minutes.

  4. In another bowl, mix the pineapple, brown sugar, eggs, milk, and salt.

  5. Coat the bread cubes with the pineapple mixture and pour everything into a greased 9x13 baking dish.

  6. Bake at 350째F (180째C) for 45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set.

  7. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, if desired.

  8. Black Sesame Rice Pudding

This soothing, aromatic dessert is a delightful take on traditional rice pudding. The nutty, earthy flavor of black sesame paste is perfectly complemented by the delicate sweetness of cooked rice and infused milk.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup black sesame paste
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • Sweetener to taste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the cooked rice, milk, and black sesame paste.

  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 20 minutes.

  3. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and sweetener, if using.

  4. Divide the pudding between chilled bowls and sprinkle with shredded coconut.

  5. Guan Xing Dou Fu

This ancient Chinese dessert dates back to the Han Dynasty and is made from Cassia seeds, a bitter plant that has numerous health benefits, such as improving digestion and nourishing the blood. The delicate, sweet flavor of the Cassia seeds is perfectly balanced by a creamy layer of coconut milk in this traditional cold dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Cassia seeds
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine Cassia seeds and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 1 hour or until the seeds are tender and fully hydrated.

  2. In a separate saucepan, gently heat the coconut milk and sugar until the sugar dissolves.

  3. Strain the Cassia seeds, reserving both the liquid and the seeds separately.

  4. Mix the reserved liquid with the coconut milk and sugar mixture, then add the strained Cassia seeds.

  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

  6. Tang Yuan (Glutinous Rice Dumplings)

These delightful, chewy dumplings feature a delightful balance of sweet and savory flavors, with a filling of ground black sesame or red bean paste encased in a fluffy, glutinous rice dough. A beloved Chinese dessert, these dumplings are particularly enjoyed during the Lantern Festival and are often served in a fragrant ginger-infused broth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup ground black sesame or red bean paste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour and water until a soft, pliable dough forms.

  2. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes, then cover and let rest for 30 minutes.

  3. Roll the dough into small, 1-inch balls, then flatten each ball using your hands or a rolling pin.

  4. Place about 1/2 teaspoon of the black sesame or red bean paste in the center of each flattened dough ball, then enclose and seal the filling completely.

  5. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, then gently add the dumplings. Cook for about 6-7 minutes or until the dumplings float to the surface.

  6. Serve hot or chilled, accompanied by a ginger-infused broth, if desired.

  7. Osmanthus Jelly

Osmanthus flowers, with their delicate, sweet-scented aroma, have been revered in China for centuries, traditionally used to promote harmony, abundance, and longevity. In this traditional dessert, the aromatic flowers are used to create a light, refreshing jelly with a delicate floral sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 packet (1.5 oz) agar-agar flakes
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup osmanthus flowers, lightly crushed

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add the agar-agar flakes.

  2. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the agar-agar has completely dissolved.

  3. Remove from heat and add in sugar, stirring until dissolved.

  4. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve, then mix in the crushed osmanthus flowers.

  5. Pour the mixture into a lightly greased 8x8 baking dish and let cool.

  6. Refrigerate until firm, about 2 hours, then cut into squares and serve chilled.

  7. Sweet Lotus Seeds Soup

Popular in Southern China, this creamy confection features the delicate, nutty flavor of lotus seeds infused with sweet osmanthus flowers and luscious red dates. Perfect for a soothing dessert,-this velvety soup is a delightful treat to share with friends and family.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lotus seeds (soaked for at least 2 hours)
  • 5 red dates, pitted
  • 1 stem osmanthus flowers, lightly crushed (optional)
  • 5 cups water
  • Sweetener to taste (sugar, honey, or maple syrup)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the lotus seeds, red dates, osmanthus flowers, and water to a boil.

  2. Reduce heat to low and simmer for about 1 hour or until the lotus seeds are tender.

  3. Remove from heat and strain the liquid, reserving it and discarding the-solid ingredients.

  4. Sweeten the soup to taste with sugar, honey, or maple syrup, if desired.

  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving, or serve warm for a comforting and nourishing treat.

  6. White Fungus Pear Soup

This refreshing dessert combines silken white fungus with the natural sweetness of fresh pears for a rejuvenating and nourishing treat. The soothing broth is infused with the delicate, subtle flavors of pears and mild white fungus, creating a perfectly balanced dessert that is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup white fungus, soaked for at least 2 hours
  • 2 ripe pears, peeled and cored
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 dried dates
  • Sweetener to taste (sugar, honey, or maple syrup)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the white fungus, pears, cinnamon stick, dried dates, and water to a boil.

  2. Reduce heat to low and simmer for about 1 hour or until the pears and white fungus are tender.

  3. Remove from heat and discard the cinnamon stick and dried dates.

  4. Sweeten the soup to taste with sugar, honey, or maple syrup, if desired.

  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving, or serve warm for a comforting treat.

  6. Osmanthus and Saffron Jelly

This luxurious dessert combines delicate osmanthus flowers with the exotic, aromatic touch of saffron for a fragrant, floral delight. With a silken texture and a luscious, natural sweetness, this dessert is a real showstopper that is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 packet (1.5 oz) agar-agar flakes
  • 1 cup osmanthus flowers, lightly crushed
  • A pinch of saffron threads, soaked in 1 tablespoon warm water

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the water and sugar. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. In a separate saucepan, bring the remaining 1 cup of water to a boil, add the agar-agar flakes, and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until dissolved.
  3. Strain the sugar and agar-agar mixtures through a fine sieve, then add the crushed osmanthus flowers, saffron and water mixture, and stir to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into lightly greased 8x8 baking dish and refrigerate until set, about 2 hours.
  5. Cut into squares and serve chilled.
