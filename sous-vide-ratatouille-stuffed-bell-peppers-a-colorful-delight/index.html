Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Colorful Delight


Are you looking for a delicious and visually stunning dish to impress your guests? Look no further than Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Bell Peppers! This recipe combines the rich flavors of Ratatouille with the vibrant colors of bell peppers, resulting in a dish that is both tasty and eye-catching.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 180°F (82°C).

  2. Slice the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside the tops for later use.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the zucchini, eggplant, red onion, tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure all the vegetables are coated with the seasonings.

  4. Fill each bell pepper with the ratatouille mixture, packing it tightly to ensure even cooking.

  5. Place the filled bell peppers in a vacuum-sealed bag, ensuring they are arranged in a single layer. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a ziplock bag and remove as much air as possible by submerging it in water, leaving the seal slightly open, and then sealing it once the air is expelled.

  6. Carefully lower the bag into the preheated water bath, ensuring it is fully submerged. Cook for 1 hour, or until the vegetables are tender.

  7. Once cooked, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully transfer the bell peppers to a serving dish.

  8. Optional: If you prefer a slightly charred and smoky flavor, you can finish the stuffed bell peppers on a hot grill or under a broiler for a few minutes.

  9. Serve the Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Bell Peppers as a main course or as a side dish. They pair well with a fresh green salad or crusty bread.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra protein to this dish, you can include cooked quinoa or crumbled feta cheese in the ratatouille mixture before stuffing the bell peppers.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to personalize the flavor profile of the ratatouille. Basil, parsley, or even a pinch of red pepper flakes can add a unique twist.
  • If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can still make this recipe by baking the stuffed bell peppers in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 30-35 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Bell Peppers are not only a feast for the eyes but also a delight for the taste buds. The tender and flavorful vegetables combined with the sweetness of the bell peppers create a harmonious dish that is sure to impress. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply want to elevate your weeknight dinner, this recipe is a winner. Give it a try and let the flavors of the Mediterranean shine through in every bite.

Enjoy!
