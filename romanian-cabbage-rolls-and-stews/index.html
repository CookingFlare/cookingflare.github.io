Romanian Cabbage Rolls and Stews


When it comes to hearty and comforting meals, Romanian cuisine has a lot to offer. From savory stews to satisfying cabbage rolls, the flavors and aromas of these dishes are sure to transport you to the heart of Eastern Europe. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the art of making Romanian cabbage rolls and stews, exploring the traditional methods and ingredients that make these dishes truly special.

The Classic Cabbage Rolls (Sarmale)

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cabbage
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup sour cabbage juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cabbage Leaves: Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Carefully remove the core from the cabbage head and place it in the boiling water. Let it simmer for a few minutes until the leaves start to soften. Remove the leaves one by one and set them aside to cool.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, ground beef, rice, onion, garlic, thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to ensure an even distribution of flavors.

  3. Assemble the Cabbage Rolls: Take a cabbage leaf and place a small amount of the filling at the base. Roll the leaf, tucking in the sides as you go, to form a neat roll. Repeat this process until all the filling is used up.

  4. Cook the Cabbage Rolls: Line the bottom of a large pot with a few cabbage leaves. Place the cabbage rolls in the pot, layering them tightly. Mix the tomato paste with the sour cabbage juice and pour it over the rolls. Add water to cover the rolls, then bring the pot to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 hours until the rolls are tender and the flavors have melded together.

Hearty Romanian Stew (Tochitura)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1/2 lb smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Meat: In a large skillet or Dutch oven, brown the pork shoulder and smoked sausage over medium-high heat. Once the meat is golden brown, remove it from the pan and set it aside.

  2. Saute the Aromatics: In the same pan, sauté the chopped onion and garlic until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Deglaze the Pan: Pour the white wine into the pan, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine reduce slightly before adding the chicken broth.

  4. Simmer the Stew: Return the browned meat to the pan and add the dried thyme, sweet paprika, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Bring the stew to a simmer, then cover and let it cook for 1-2 hours until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded together.

Serving Suggestions

Both the cabbage rolls and the stew are often served with a dollop of sour cream and a side of mamaliga (polenta). The creamy texture of the sour cream complements the robust flavors of the dishes, while the mamaliga provides a comforting base for sopping up the delicious sauces.

Conclusion

Romanian cuisine is a celebration of rich flavors and time-honored traditions. Whether you’re indulging in the tender cabbage rolls or savoring the rich stew, each bite is a testament to the culinary heritage of Eastern Europe. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a culinary journey to discover the soul-warming delights of Romanian cabbage rolls and stews. Bon appétit!
