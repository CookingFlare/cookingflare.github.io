Grilling Desserts: Sweet Treats You Can Cook on the BBQ


When you think about barbecuing, your mind likely gravitates toward smoky ribs, juicy burgers, or grilled veggies. But have you ever considered finishing your cookout with something sweet, smoky, and utterly irresistible? Grilling desserts is an exciting and often overlooked way to elevate your BBQ experience. From caramelized fruits to decadent grilled cakes, these sweet treats will add a whole new dimension to outdoor cooking.

Let’s delve into the world of grilling desserts, exploring diverse recipes, grilling tips, and flavor pairings that will wow your taste buds and impress your guests at the next barbecue.

Why Grill Desserts?

Grilling desserts offers a unique interplay of heat and flavor that baking or chilling just can’t replicate. The direct heat caramelizes sugars, creating a beautiful crust and intensifying natural sweetness. The smoke infuses addictive, subtle notes that complement many fruits and confections perfectly. Plus, grilling desserts can be quick and requires minimal cleanup compared to other culinary methods.

Choosing to grill your sweet dishes is also a fantastic way to keep your kitchen cool during hot weather. Instead of warming the house with an oven or stovetop, your desserts cook outside, letting you enjoy the summer breeze and the company of friends and family.

Tips for Successful Grilling of Desserts

  1. Use Direct and Indirect Heat Wisely: Some desserts need quick, direct grilling (like fruit skewers), while others benefit from slower, indirect heat to cook through without burning (like cakes or pies).

  2. Choose the Right Fruits: Firm fruits with high natural sugars, such as peaches, pineapples, apples, bananas, figs, and plums, grill beautifully and develop irresistible caramelization.

  3. Oil Your Grill Grates: Prevent sticking by oiling the grill grates lightly before cooking. You can use a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil and tongs to rub the grate.

  4. Use Grill-Safe Cookware: Cast iron pans, grill-safe silicone mats, or disposable aluminum trays can help you cook more delicate desserts that might fall apart.

  5. Don’t Forget Foil Packs: Wrapping delicate or small items in foil can protect them from burning and make cleanup easier.

  6. Enhance with Spices and Sweeteners: Cinnamon, nutmeg, brown sugar, honey, and maple syrup added before or after grilling elevate flavors beautifully.

Let’s Get Sweet: 7 Grilling Dessert Recipes You’ll Love

1. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Mascarpone

This recipe exudes simplicity with elegance. Peaches caramelize on the grill, their natural sugars bubbling up to create rich, smoky sweetness. Topped with creamy mascarpone and a drizzle of honey, it’s a luscious, sophisticated treat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe but firm peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted butter
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Fresh mint leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Oil the grates lightly.
  2. Brush the peach halves with olive oil to prevent sticking.
  3. Place peaches cut side down on the grill for about 4-5 minutes until grill marks appear and peaches soften. Flip and grill the skin side for 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from the grill, let cool slightly.
  5. Spoon mascarpone over each peach half and drizzle generously with honey. Garnish with mint leaves if desired.
  6. Serve immediately.

2. Grilled Banana Boats with Chocolate and Marshmallows

This childhood favorite campfire treat gets an easy outdoor upgrade on the grill. The bananas soften to sweet perfection, while the chocolate and marshmallows melt into gooey, delicious pools.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large ripe bananas
  • 1/2 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chunks
  • Aluminum foil
  • Optional: chopped nuts, shredded coconut

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice each banana lengthwise without cutting fully through, creating a pocket.
  3. Stuff the pocket with chocolate chips and marshmallows. Sprinkle nuts or coconut if using.
  4. Wrap each banana in aluminum foil tightly.
  5. Place foil packets on the grill for about 8-10 minutes until the chocolate is melted and marshmallows are soft.
  6. Let cool for a minute before carefully unwrapping. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if desired.

3. Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon and Brown Sugar

The tropical sweetness of pineapple becomes incredibly aromatic when grilled. Brown sugar caramelizes, and cinnamon adds a warm spice note making it perfect for summer and beyond.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  2. Brush pineapple slices with melted butter.
  3. Sprinkle both sides with the brown sugar-cinnamon mixture.
  4. Place pineapple directly on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes per side until caramelized marks appear.
  5. Serve warm as is or over ice cream or yogurt.

4. Grilled Pound Cake with Fresh Berries and Whipped Cream

Grilling pound cake transforms it into a crispy, smokey-sweet base perfect for fresh berries and fluffy whipped cream. It’s an elegant but easy dessert that feels indulgent without fuss.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cake loaf, pre-sliced 1-inch thick
  • Butter for brushing
  • Mixed fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Whipped cream or mascarpone

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush pound cake slices lightly with butter on both sides.
  3. Grill cake slices for 2-3 minutes on each side until grill marks appear. Watch carefully to avoid burning.
  4. Plate the grilled pound cake with a generous heap of fresh berries and whipped cream or a dollop of mascarpone.
  5. Enjoy immediately.

5. Grilled Stuffed Figs with Goat Cheese and Honey

Figs grilled on the BBQ become sweet, tender, and infused with subtle smokiness. When filled with creamy goat cheese and drizzled with honey, they make an impressive yet effortless appetizer or dessert bite.

Ingredients:

  • 12 fresh figs, sliced halfway to create an opening
  • 4 ounces goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Chopped pistachios (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium heat.
  2. Stuff each fig with a small spoonful of goat cheese.
  3. Place figs on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and grill marks appear.
  4. Remove from grill, drizzle with honey, and sprinkle pistachios on top if desired.
  5. Serve warm.

6. Grilled Chocolate-Stuffed Strawberries

Elevate fresh strawberries by stuffing them with rich chocolate and letting them melt in the warmth of the grill. These are quick, impressive, and a definite crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 20 large strawberries, hulled but kept whole
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chocolate chunks
  • Toothpicks to seal

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice strawberries horizontally halfway through to create a pocket.
  3. Insert 1-2 pieces of chocolate inside each strawberry. Use a toothpick to close the strawberry pocket gently.
  4. Place strawberries on grill and cook for 2-3 minutes to melt chocolate and slightly soften the strawberry.
  5. Remove toothpicks and serve warm.

7. Grilled S’mores Quesadilla

Taking classic s’mores ingredients and putting a grilled tortilla twist on them, this dessert is melty, crunchy, smoky, and sweet all at once.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large flour tortillas
  • 1/2 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • Butter for grilling

Instructions:

  1. Heat the grill to medium.
  2. Butter one side of each tortilla. Place one tortilla butter-side-down on a baking sheet or grill-safe pan.
  3. Sprinkle half the marshmallows and chocolate chips evenly over the tortilla. Top with the second tortilla, butter side up.
  4. Transfer the pan or foil packet to the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until tortillas are golden brown and filling is melted.
  5. Slice into wedges and serve immediately.

Beyond Recipes: Flavor Pairings and Presentation Ideas

Grilled desserts shine best when paired thoughtfully:

  • Add a sprinkle of flaky sea salt over grilled chocolate or caramel desserts to balance sweetness.
  • Fresh herbs like mint, basil, or thyme can freshen up grilled fruits.
  • Drizzle luscious caramel sauce, fruit coulis, or balsamic glaze to add layers of flavor and visual appeal.
  • Accompany warm grilled desserts with cold ice cream, sorbet, or whipped cream for delightful contrast.
  • Use toasted nuts or granola for a crunchy texture contrast.

Wrapping It Up

Grilling desserts is a surprisingly versatile and fun way to round out your backyard cooking. Whether you’re craving something light like grilled fruit or a decadent treat like chocolate-stuffed strawberries or a s’mores quesadilla, the grill opens up a whole new flavor landscape.

Next time you fire up your BBQ, don’t forget the sweet finish. These grilled desserts are guaranteed to impress and satisfy, making your cookout memorable from start to sweet finish.

Happy grilling, and even happier sweet eating!
