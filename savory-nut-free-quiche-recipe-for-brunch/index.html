Savory Nut-Free Quiche Recipe for Brunch


Are you looking for a delicious and allergen-friendly dish to serve at your next brunch gathering? Look no further than this savory nut-free quiche recipe! Quiche is a classic brunch dish that can be customized with endless ingredient combinations, making it a versatile and crowd-pleasing option for any brunch spread. This particular recipe is not only nut-free but also packed with flavorful ingredients that will leave your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 4-6 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (such as Gruyere or Cheddar)
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing gently with a fork after each addition. Stop adding water when the dough begins to hold together.
  3. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and shape it into a disk. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Pre-bake the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Press the dough into the dish and trim any excess overhang. Use a fork to prick the bottom of the crust.
  3. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper. Return the crust to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and black pepper until well combined.

Step 4: Assemble and Bake

  1. Spread the sautéed vegetables, halved cherry tomatoes, and shredded cheese evenly over the pre-baked crust.
  2. Carefully pour the egg mixture over the filling.
  3. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is golden brown.
  4. Remove from the oven and allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  5. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Serving Suggestions

This savory nut-free quiche is best enjoyed warm, making it a perfect addition to any brunch menu. Pair it with a simple green salad or fresh fruit for a well-rounded meal that will satisfy your guests’ cravings. The leftovers (if there are any!) can be refrigerated and enjoyed for a quick and delicious breakfast throughout the week.

Whether you’re accommodating nut allergies or simply looking for a flavorful and satisfying brunch dish, this savory nut-free quiche is sure to become a staple in your recipe collection. Experiment with different vegetable and cheese combinations to create your own unique twist on this timeless dish. Happy brunching!
