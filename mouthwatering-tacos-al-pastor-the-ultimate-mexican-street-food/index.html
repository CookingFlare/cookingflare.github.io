Mouthwatering Tacos al Pastor: The Ultimate Mexican Street Food


If you’ve ever strolled through the streets of Mexico, you’ve likely been tantalized by the delicious aroma of Tacos al Pastor sizzling on the grill. This iconic Mexican street food is a tantalizing blend of flavors, textures, and spices that beckons food lovers from all corners of the globe. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a culinary novice, this recipe will guide you through the steps to recreate this mouthwatering dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 3 dried guajillo chilies
  • 3 dried ancho chilies
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Tacos:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • Corn tortillas
  • Diced pineapple
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Diced onion
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade:

    • Start by removing the stems and seeds from the dried chilies. Place them in a bowl and cover with hot water. Let them soak for about 15 minutes until softened.
    • In a blender, combine the softened chilies, pineapple juice, orange juice, garlic, onion, oregano, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, cloves, black pepper, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  2. Marinate the Pork:

    • Place the thinly sliced pork shoulder in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the pork, making sure it is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Preheat the Grill:

    • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you don’t have a grill, a cast-iron skillet or grill pan will work just as well.

  4. Cook the Pork:

    • Remove the pork from the marinade and thread it onto skewers. If using a grill, cook the pork over direct heat for 5-7 minutes per side, or until slightly charred and cooked through. If using a skillet, cook the pork in batches, ensuring it is evenly browned.

  5. Assemble the Tacos:

    • Warm the corn tortillas on the grill or in a skillet. Fill each tortilla with the grilled pork, diced pineapple, chopped cilantro, and diced onion. Squeeze a wedge of lime over the top for an extra burst of flavor.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Arrange the Tacos al Pastor on a platter and serve immediately. The combination of tender, flavorful pork, sweet pineapple, and zesty cilantro is sure to delight your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Mexico.

Conclusion

Tacos al Pastor is a dish that encapsulates the vibrant and diverse flavors of Mexico. With its rich history and tantalizing combination of ingredients, it has become a beloved staple of Mexican cuisine. By following this recipe, you can experience the joy of recreating this iconic street food at home, bringing a taste of Mexico to your dinner table. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and treat yourself to the ultimate Mexican street food experience with these mouthwatering Tacos al Pastor.
