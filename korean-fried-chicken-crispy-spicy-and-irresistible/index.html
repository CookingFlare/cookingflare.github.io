Korean Fried Chicken: Crispy, Spicy, and Irresistible


If there’s one dish that has taken the world by storm in recent years, it’s Korean fried chicken. Crispy, spicy, and utterly irresistible, this delectable dish has captured the hearts and taste buds of food lovers everywhere. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or simply can’t resist the allure of perfectly crispy chicken, this Korean fried chicken recipe is sure to become a staple in your cooking repertoire. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making the perfect Korean fried chicken, step by step.

Ingredients

To start your Korean fried chicken adventure, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 lbs of chicken wings
  • 1 cup of potato starch
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1/2 cup of rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of honey
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Chicken

  1. Start by washing the chicken wings and patting them dry with paper towels. This will help ensure that the chicken is nice and crispy once fried.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together the potato starch, all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.

  3. Gradually add the water to the dry ingredients, whisking until you have a smooth batter.

  4. Dip the chicken wings into the batter, making sure they are evenly coated.

  5. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 350°F.

  6. Carefully lower the battered chicken wings into the hot oil and fry for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Once done, transfer the fried chicken to a wire rack to drain any excess oil.

Step 2: Making the Spicy Glaze

  1. In a saucepan, combine the soy sauce, gochujang, rice vinegar, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger.

  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and stir the mixture until the honey has completely dissolved and the sauce is smooth.

  3. Allow the sauce to simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it has thickened slightly.

Step 3: Coating the Chicken

  1. In a large bowl, toss the fried chicken wings with the spicy glaze until they are evenly coated.

  2. If you prefer your Korean fried chicken extra spicy, you can sprinkle some additional gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) over the glazed chicken wings.

Step 4: Serving

  1. Transfer the saucy, spicy, and crispy Korean fried chicken to a serving platter and garnish with sesame seeds and chopped scallions for an extra pop of flavor and color.

  2. Serve your Korean fried chicken with pickled radishes, kimchi, and a cold beer for the ultimate Korean dining experience.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Korean fried chicken is a dish that perfectly balances crispy, succulent chicken with a spicy, tangy glaze that’s simply irresistible. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary horizons, this recipe is a must-try. The combination of textures and flavors in this dish is guaranteed to leave you wanting more. So, roll up your sleeves, get your ingredients ready, and embark on a Korean fried chicken adventure that will delight your taste buds and impress your friends and family. Enjoy!
