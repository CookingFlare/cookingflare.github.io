Easy-to-Make Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowl


If you’re craving bold flavors, tantalizing spices, and a satisfying meal that’s not only flavorful but also incredibly easy to prepare, then a Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowl may just be your new go-to recipe. This dish brings together the vibrant spices of shawarma, fresh herbs, crisp vegetables, and creamy sauces, all served atop a hearty base for a meal that’s both filling and delicious. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or meal prepping for the week ahead, this recipe will quickly find a permanent spot in your rotation.

Let’s dive into how you can whip up this delectable Chicken Shawarma Bowl in your own kitchen with simple ingredients and straightforward steps.

What Makes This Recipe Special?

Chicken Shawarma Bowls are a delightful fusion of flavors. The rich, spiced chicken pairs beautifully with the fresh and zesty accompaniments, creating a symphony of taste in every bite. This dish is also highly customizable—you can mix and match grains, veggies, and toppings to suit your preferences or dietary needs.

Unlike traditional shawarma, which is typically cooked on a rotisserie, this version is adapted for the home cook using a stovetop or oven. The dish retains all the classic flavors and textures associated with Middle Eastern cuisine while remaining accessible to the average cook.

Ingredients You’ll Need

One of the reasons this recipe is a breeze to make is its simple ingredient list. Most of what you’ll need can already be found in your pantry or fridge, with just a few fresh items required.

For the Chicken Shawarma:

  • 1 ½ pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs (you can substitute chicken breast, but thighs stay juicier)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper

For the Base:

  • 2 cups cooked basmati rice or couscous (quinoa or cauliflower rice for a low-carb option)
  • 1 cup mixed greens, spinach, or arugula (optional)

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup diced cucumbers
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup finely diced red onion
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • ½ cup chopped parsley or cilantro
  • ¼ cup pickled red onions or turnips (optional, for an authentic touch)

For the Sauce:

  • ½ cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  • Pinch of salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

This recipe is straightforward and beginner-friendly. You’ll have your Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowl ready in no time! Here’s how to make it:

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. Combine the olive oil, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, cinnamon, cardamom, garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix well to form a spice marinade.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and ensure they are evenly coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate. For the best flavor, marinate for at least 1 hour, but if you’re short on time, 20-30 minutes will still yield good results.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

  1. Heat a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Lightly grease with a drizzle of olive oil.
  2. Cook the chicken thighs for 5-6 minutes per side, or until they are browned and crispy on the outside and fully cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F).
  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and allow it to rest for 5 minutes. Once rested, slice the chicken into bite-sized strips or chunks.

Step 3: Prepare the Base and Toppings

  1. While the chicken is cooking, prepare your base by cooking your grain of choice according to package instructions. Fluff with a fork and set aside.
  2. Chop your cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and herbs. Arrange them in small bowls to make the assembly easier.

Step 4: Make the Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and a pinch of salt until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

Step 5: Assemble the Shawarma Bowl

  1. Using a bowl or a shallow dish, layer the base (rice, couscous, or greens) first.
  2. Add the chicken shawarma strips as the star of the dish.
  3. Arrange the diced cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and additional toppings of your choice (feta, pickled vegetables, or chopped parsley) around the bowl for a colorful presentation.
  4. Drizzle the Greek yogurt tahini sauce over the top, or serve it on the side. If desired, garnish the bowl with a sprinkle of paprika or a few dashes of hot sauce.

Tips for Success

  • Maximize Marination Time: The longer the chicken marinates, the deeper the flavor penetrates into the meat. If possible, let it sit overnight in the refrigerator.
  • Char for Flavor: Cooking the chicken on high heat will give it a lovely charred exterior, reminiscent of traditional shawarma cooked on a spit.
  • Batch Cooking: Double the recipe to have leftovers that can be easily repurposed for future meals like wraps, sandwiches, or salads.
  • Customize Your Bowl: Swap out ingredients based on your preferences. For example, add roasted vegetables like zucchini or eggplant for extra depth of flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Shawarma Bowl

This dish is a one-stop solution for anyone who loves vibrant, well-seasoned meals. It’s nutrient-packed, offering a balanced combination of proteins, healthy fats, and carbohydrates.

The flexibility of this recipe allows for creativity. You can easily adjust it to fit dietary restrictions or ingredient availability. For example, opting for cauliflower rice makes the dish keto-friendly, while skipping the yogurt tahini sauce can make it dairy-free.

Not only is this bowl packed with bold Middle Eastern flavors, but it’s also a meal prep champion. Simply store the various components in individual containers, and you’ll have ready-to-assemble lunches or dinners throughout the week.

Wrapping It Up

If you’ve been searching for a way to enjoy the beloved flavors of shawarma without needing a vertical rotisserie, this Easy-to-Make Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma Bowl is the perfect solution. It’s simple, delicious, and endlessly customizable, making it a recipe you’ll come back to time and time again.

Treat yourself and your loved ones to a cozy, flavorful meal that celebrates the vibrant spices and fresh ingredients of Middle Eastern cuisine. After just one bite, this shawarma bowl will earn a permanent spot in your recipe collection!

So why wait? Grab your ingredients, fire up the stove, and get ready to enjoy a homemade meal bursting with flavor.
