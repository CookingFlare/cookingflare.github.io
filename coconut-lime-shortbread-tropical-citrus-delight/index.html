Coconut Lime Shortbread: Tropical Citrus Delight


If you’re a fan of tropical flavors and buttery, melt-in-your-mouth shortbread, then this Coconut Lime Shortbread recipe is sure to become a new favorite. The combination of zesty lime and sweet coconut creates a delightful burst of flavor in every bite, making this treat perfect for any occasion, from afternoon tea to a summertime dessert. So, let’s dive into this tropical citrus delight and bring a taste of the tropics into your kitchen!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (60g) confectioners’ sugar
  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (50g) shredded coconut
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a 9-inch (23cm) square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on two opposite sides for easy removal.

  2. Creaming the Butter and Sugar: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and confectioners’ sugar together until light and fluffy. This step is crucial for achieving that tender, crumbly texture of shortbread.

  3. Incorporating the Flavors: Add the lime zest and shredded coconut to the butter-sugar mixture. Gently fold in these tropical flavors until evenly distributed.

  4. Adding the Flour: Gradually add the all-purpose flour and salt to the mixture, stirring until a soft dough forms. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the shortbread tough rather than delicate.

  5. Pressing the Dough: Transfer the dough to the prepared baking pan and press it evenly into the bottom using your fingertips or the back of a spoon. Smooth out the surface for an even bake.

  6. Baking: Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. The shortbread may still seem soft in the middle, but it will firm up as it cools.

  7. Cooling and Cutting: Allow the shortbread to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before using the parchment paper overhang to lift it out onto a cutting board. While it’s still warm, carefully cut the shortbread into squares or rectangles.

  8. Optional Glaze: For an extra burst of lime flavor, you can drizzle the cooled shortbread with a simple glaze made from lime juice and confectioners’ sugar. Let the glaze set before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Coconut Lime Shortbread is a versatile treat that pairs beautifully with a variety of beverages. Serve it alongside a cup of aromatic Earl Grey tea for a sophisticated afternoon indulgence, or alongside a refreshing glass of iced coconut milk for a tropical twist. You can also include it as part of a dessert platter with fresh fruit, such as pineapple or mango, for a vibrant and satisfying conclusion to any meal.

Storing

To keep your Coconut Lime Shortbread fresh and delicious, store it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. For longer storage, you can freeze the shortbread for up to three months. Just be sure to separate the layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

Final Thoughts

With its irresistible blend of coconut and lime, this Coconut Lime Shortbread is a delightful departure from traditional shortbread recipes. Whether you’re hosting a tropical-themed gathering or simply craving a taste of the tropics, this recipe is a perfect way to infuse a burst of sunshine into your culinary repertoire. So, why not bring a touch of the tropics into your home with this easy and flavorful treat? Enjoy the sunny flavors of Coconut Lime Shortbread and transport your taste buds to a tropical paradise!
