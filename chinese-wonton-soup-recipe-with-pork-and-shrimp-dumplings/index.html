Chinese Wonton Soup Recipe with Pork and Shrimp Dumplings


If you’re craving a taste of traditional Chinese cuisine, look no further than this delightful recipe for Wonton Soup with Pork and Shrimp Dumplings. Wontons, also known as “hun tun” in Cantonese, are versatile and delicious dumplings that can be served in a variety of dishes, but there’s nothing quite like the comfort of a steaming bowl of wonton soup. This recipe combines the savory flavors of pork and shrimp with the lightness of delicate wonton wrappers, all swimming in a flavorful broth. Let’s dive into the art of making this beloved Chinese dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Wonton Filling:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled, deveined, and finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

For the Wonton Wrappers:

  • 1 package of square wonton wrappers

For the Soup Broth:

  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

Making the Wonton Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, garlic, ginger, green onions, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and white pepper. Mix until well combined.

Wrapping the Wontons:

  1. Lay a wonton wrapper on a clean, dry surface.
  2. Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of the wrapper.
  3. Dip your finger in water and moisten the edges of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the wrapper in half to form a triangle, pressing the edges to seal.
  5. Bring the two opposite corners of the triangle together, moisten with water, and press to seal.

Making the Soup Broth:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth, smashed garlic, ginger, and green onions to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat and let the broth simmer for 15-20 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  3. Strain the broth and return it to the pot.
  4. Season the broth with soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt to taste.

Cooking the Wontons:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  2. Carefully drop the wontons into the boiling water and cook for 4-5 minutes, or until they float to the top and are cooked through.
  3. Remove the cooked wontons with a slotted spoon and place them in serving bowls.

Serving the Wonton Soup:

  1. Ladle the hot broth over the cooked wontons in the serving bowls.
  2. Garnish with additional chopped green onions or a sprinkle of white pepper, if desired.

Tips and Tricks

  • To make the process easier, you can make the wonton filling and wrap the wontons in advance and freeze them for later use.
  • Experiment with different dipping sauces for the wontons, such as chili oil or a simple soy sauce and vinegar mixture.

Conclusion

With its delicate wrappers and savory filling, Wonton Soup with Pork and Shrimp Dumplings is a culinary delight that’s sure to impress your family and friends. The art of making wontons may take a bit of practice, but the results are well worth the effort. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a flavorful journey through the heart of Chinese cuisine with this authentic recipe. Enjoy!
