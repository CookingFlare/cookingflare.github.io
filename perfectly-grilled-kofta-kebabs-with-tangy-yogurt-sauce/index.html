Perfectly Grilled Kofta Kebabs with Tangy Yogurt Sauce


If you’re looking for a dish that brings together rich flavors, vibrant spices, and an unforgettable aroma, then look no further than grilled kofta kebabs. These savory meatballs on skewers are a staple in many cuisines across the Middle East, South Asia, and North Africa. What makes kofta kebabs so special is not just the spiced meat, but the texture and the char you achieve by grilling them to perfection.

In this post, we will walk you through creating perfectly grilled kofta kebabs served with a tangy yogurt sauce that adds an extra layer of depth to every bite. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or looking to spice up your weeknight dinner, this recipe is sure to impress.

What Are Kofta Kebabs?

Kofta kebabs are a form of minced or ground meat, typically mixed with spices, onions, herbs, and sometimes vegetables. The mixture is formed into round or oblong balls and skewered before being grilled or fried. The choice of meat can vary, with lamb, beef, chicken, or a blend of meats being the most common options.

What distinguishes a kofta kebab is its spice blend and cooking method. The meat mixture is seasoned with aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cardamom, which deliver complex flavors and create a satisfying experience with every bite. The kebabs are then grilled, giving them a beautiful char while keeping the inside juicy and tender.

This recipe will focus on lamb koftas, as lamb brings a richness that pairs beautifully with the spices and the cooling yogurt sauce. However, you can easily swap the lamb with beef or chicken depending on your preference.

Ingredients for Kofta Kebabs

For the Kofta:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground lamb (or beef/chicken)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped or grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (for a bit of heat and color)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice (optional for extra warmth)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 egg (to help bind the mixture)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (optional, if the mixture needs binding)

For the Tangy Yogurt Sauce:

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • 1 clove garlic, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of ground cumin (optional)

To Serve:

  • Fresh pita bread or flatbread
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Tomato wedges

How to Make the Perfect Kofta Kebabs

Step 1: Prepare the Kofta Mixture

The first step in making kofta kebabs is preparing the meat mixture. Start by placing your ground lamb (or any other meat you’re using) in a large bowl. Add the finely chopped onion, minced garlic, and all of the spices: cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, paprika, allspice, black pepper, and salt.

Next, mix in the fresh parsley and cilantro to give the kebabs a fragrant, herbal note. Once the herbs and spices are thoroughly mixed, add the egg and olive oil. The egg helps bind the meat together while the olive oil contributes to the richness and moisture of the kebabs. If the mixture feels a bit too loose or sticky, you can add breadcrumbs, but it’s not always necessary with the right amount of binding from the egg and oil.

Step 2: Shape the Koftas

Now, it’s time to shape your koftas. Using your hands, take a small handful of the meat mixture and form it into an elongated shape around skewers. You can make them as thick or thin as you like, but traditionally, kofta kebabs are about 4 to 6 inches long and about 1 inch thick.

If you’re making koftas without skewers, you can simply roll them into meatball shapes, but skewers help create a more even cook and add a nice presentation.

Once all the koftas are shaped, place them on a tray or plate and refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes. This resting time helps the flavors meld together and ensures that the meat stays firm and holds its shape on the grill.

Step 3: Prepare the Grill

While the koftas are chilling in the fridge, it’s time to prepare your grill. Whether you’re using a charcoal or gas grill, you want to aim for medium-high heat. If you’re using charcoal, let the coals burn down until they are glowing red with a light layer of ash. If you’re using a gas grill, preheat it for about 10-15 minutes before cooking.

Oil the grill grates lightly with a paper towel soaked in vegetable oil, or spray them with a non-stick spray to prevent the koftas from sticking. This step is essential because kofta kebabs, being a bit soft, can easily fall apart on the grill if they stick to the grates.

Step 4: Grill the Koftas

Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, turning them gently to ensure they cook evenly. The koftas should have a nice char on the outside while staying juicy and tender on the inside. Depending on the thickness of the kebabs and the heat of your grill, the total grilling time should be around 12-15 minutes.

You can check for doneness by using a meat thermometer. The internal temperature should be 160°F (71°C) for lamb or beef. For chicken, it should reach 165°F (74°C).

Step 5: Make the Tangy Yogurt Sauce

While the koftas are grilling, you can prepare the tangy yogurt sauce that will complement the richness of the kebabs. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, honey (if using), garlic, and chopped cilantro. Stir until smooth, and season with salt and pepper to taste. If you want to add a little extra flavor, a pinch of ground cumin goes wonderfully with this sauce, bringing out the flavors of the spices in the koftas.

The yogurt sauce should be tangy and refreshing, providing the perfect contrast to the savory, spiced meat. If the sauce feels too thick, you can thin it with a small splash of water or olive oil until you achieve your desired consistency.

Step 6: Assemble and Serve

Once the koftas are grilled to perfection, it’s time to assemble your meal. Lay out some fresh pita bread or flatbread on a platter, then carefully slide the koftas off the skewers. Drizzle the tangy yogurt sauce generously over the koftas, or serve the sauce on the side for dipping.

You can also serve the kebabs with fresh vegetables like cucumber slices, tomato wedges, and a few sprigs of mint to enhance the freshness and balance the richness of the grilled meat. The yogurt sauce will offer a refreshing coolness that contrasts beautifully with the spices, while the bread allows you to scoop up every bit of this flavorful dish.

Tips for Making Perfect Kofta Kebabs

  • Choose the right meat: Lamb is the traditional choice for kofta kebabs because of its rich flavor and tender texture. However, if you prefer a leaner option, you can use beef or chicken. For chicken koftas, make sure to use a fattier part, like thighs, to keep them juicy.
  • Don’t overwork the meat: When mixing the meat and spices, avoid over-mixing. Overworking the meat can lead to tough kebabs. Mix just until everything is incorporated.
  • Chill the mixture: Refrigerating the kofta mixture helps the flavors meld together and makes it easier to shape the kebabs. It also helps them hold their shape on the grill.
  • Grill over medium-high heat: Grilling on high heat will give your koftas a nice char without drying them out. Be sure to check the internal temperature for doneness.
  • Customize the spices: While this recipe uses a traditional Middle Eastern spice blend, feel free to experiment with different spices to suit your preferences. Add a little chili powder for heat or swap out the cinnamon for nutmeg for a different flavor profile.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

These grilled kofta kebabs with tangy yogurt sauce are an absolute crowd-pleaser. They combine the best of both worlds: juicy, spiced meat with a cooling, creamy sauce. The smokiness from the grill pairs beautifully with the freshness of the yogurt, and the grilled vegetables bring a delightful crunch to the dish.

This recipe is not only easy to make but also incredibly versatile. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a dinner party, or just want to enjoy a flavorful weeknight meal, these kofta kebabs are a sure-fire hit. Plus, the tangy yogurt sauce can be made ahead of time, saving you time when it’s time to cook.

So, fire up the grill, get your skewers ready, and enjoy the perfect kofta kebabs tonight!
