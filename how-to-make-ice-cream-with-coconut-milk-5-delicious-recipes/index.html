How to Make Ice Cream with Coconut Milk: 5 Delicious Recipes


Ice cream has always been a favorite treat, beloved for its creamy texture and ability to be customized with countless flavors. For those who follow a dairy-free diet, or just want to try something unique, coconut milk acts as an excellent substitute for traditional cream. Its rich, velvety texture and natural sweetness make it the perfect base for homemade ice cream. Below, we’ll explore five irresistible recipes that prove coconut milk ice cream is just as indulgent—if not more—than its dairy-based counterpart.

The Basics of Making Coconut Milk Ice Cream

Before diving into the recipes, it’s useful to understand the fundamentals of crafting ice cream with coconut milk. Coconut milk, derived from the flesh of mature coconuts, brings a creamy consistency to non-dairy ice creams while contributing a subtle tropical flavor. For the best results, opt for full-fat coconut milk, as the fat content ensures the finished product is rich and smooth.

Additionally, most recipes call for a sweetener, such as sugar, maple syrup, or honey. Vanilla extract or other flavorings can help round out the flavor profile. While an ice cream maker simplifies the process, you can also use a freeze-and-stir method if you don’t have one on hand.

Now that you know the essentials, let’s dive into five mouthwatering recipes featuring coconut milk as the star ingredient.

1. Classic Vanilla Coconut Milk Ice Cream

Sometimes, simplicity is key, and this vanilla ice cream will win over anyone craving a timeless dessert. Creamy, fragrant, and versatile, vanilla ice cream with coconut milk is a great base for toppings or to serve alongside your favorite baked goods.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • ⅔ cup granulated sugar or maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut milk and sweetener. Heat over medium heat until the sweetener dissolves, stirring occasionally. Be careful not to boil the mixture.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.
  3. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for 2–3 hours until well-chilled.
  4. Pour into an ice cream maker, and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions, or transfer the mixture to a shallow container, freezing for 30 minutes. Stir vigorously to break up any ice crystals, then repeat every 30 minutes for 2–3 hours.
  5. Serve immediately for soft-serve consistency, or freeze for an additional 2 hours for firmer ice cream.

2. Chocolate Coconut Milk Ice Cream

For chocolate lovers, this recipe blends rich cocoa powder with the natural creaminess of coconut milk, creating a decadent dessert that’s hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • ¾ cup cocoa powder
  • ⅔ cup sweetener (sugar, agave nectar, or maple syrup)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Whisk the coconut milk, cocoa powder, sweetener, and salt in a medium saucepan over medium heat until well combined. Heat until the sweetener dissolves, avoiding boiling.
  2. Remove from heat, stir in vanilla extract, and let the mixture cool.
  3. Refrigerate for 2 hours until chilled.
  4. Churn the mixture in an ice cream maker or use the freeze-and-stir method described earlier.
  5. Freeze for at least 2 hours for perfect scoopable ice cream.

3. Mango Coconut Milk Ice Cream

Bring a refreshing tropical twist to your dessert with mango coconut milk ice cream. The natural sweetness of mango pairs beautifully with the creamy coconut milk, creating a bright, flavorful treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup ripe mango puree (fresh or frozen)
  • ½ cup sweetener (honey or agave nectar works best)
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Blend the mango puree with coconut milk, sweetener, and lime juice in a blender until smooth.
  2. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for several hours.
  3. Pour into your ice cream maker and churn until thickened, or use the freeze-and-stir method.
  4. Scoop into a container, and let the ice cream freeze for an additional two hours for a firmer texture.

4. Matcha Coconut Milk Ice Cream

Matcha lovers, rejoice! Infuse your ice cream with earthy, aromatic green tea powder for a dessert that’s as sophisticated as it is refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • ½ cup sweetener (maple syrup pairs well)
  • 1 tablespoon matcha green tea powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Carefully sift the matcha powder into a bowl to eliminate clumps.
  2. Add matcha, coconut milk, sweetener, vanilla, and salt to a blender. Blend until fully combined, ensuring the matcha is evenly distributed.
  3. Transfer the mixture to an ice cream maker and churn, or chill and use the freeze-and-stir method.
  4. Freeze for at least 2 additional hours for the best consistency, then scoop and serve.

5. Strawberry Coconut Milk Ice Cream

Combining the natural sweetness of strawberries with creamy coconut milk, this ice cream is wonderfully fruity, indulgent, and perfect for summer days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • 1½ cups fresh strawberries, hulled (or frozen and thawed)
  • ½ cup sweetener (such as sugar or agave nectar)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Blend strawberries, coconut milk, sweetener, and lemon juice together in a blender until smooth.
  2. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for 2–3 hours until cold.
  3. Churn the mixture in an ice cream maker or follow the freeze-and-stir method for a no-machine option.
  4. Allow the ice cream to freeze for an additional 2 hours to firm it up.

Tips for Perfect Coconut Milk Ice Cream

  • Pre-chill your ingredients: Starting with cold coconut milk ensures a smoother churn and quicker freezing time.
  • Experiment with toppings: Coconut milk ice cream pairs beautifully with everything from fresh fruit and shredded coconut to chocolate chips and caramel drizzle.
  • Store properly: To maintain the creamy texture, store your ice cream in an airtight container in the coldest part of the freezer.

Why Choose Coconut Milk?

Coconut milk offers a unique flavor while accommodating those with dairy allergies, lactose intolerance, or vegan preferences. Additionally, it adds a tropical element to desserts without overpowering other flavors. From indulgent chocolate to refreshing mango, these recipes highlight the versatility of coconut milk as an ice cream base.

Now it’s time to grab a few cans of coconut milk and get started! Whether you’re craving classic vanilla or looking to experiment with fruity flavors, these recipes are guaranteed to satisfy your sweet tooth. Try them out, share with friends, and discover why coconut milk ice cream deserves a place in your dessert repertoire!
