Easy Casserole Recipes That Are Budget-Friendly


When it comes to satisfying meals that won’t break the bank, casseroles are a fantastic option. They are the perfect combination of simplicity, heartiness, and versatility, making them ideal for busy weeknights or when you’re looking to feed a crowd on a budget. The beauty of casseroles lies in their ability to use inexpensive ingredients and turn them into delicious meals with minimal effort. Whether you’re cooking for a family, a group of friends, or just yourself, budget-friendly casserole recipes can be both filling and flavorful.

In this post, we’ll explore several easy casserole recipes that won’t hurt your wallet but will still leave everyone at the table full and happy. These casseroles are perfect for meal prepping, leftovers, or feeding a family without splurging on expensive ingredients.

1. Classic Tuna Casserole

Tuna casserole is a staple for many households because it’s quick, easy, and affordable. Made with canned tuna, pasta, and a creamy sauce, it’s a dish that checks all the boxes for a comfort meal. You can also add vegetables to make it more nutritious, and it can be customized based on your preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of tuna (packed in water or oil)
  • 2 cups of egg noodles (or any pasta of your choice)
  • 1 can of cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup of breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the egg noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained tuna, cream of mushroom soup, frozen peas, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the cooked egg noodles to the bowl and mix everything together.
  5. Transfer the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  6. Top with shredded cheddar cheese and breadcrumbs.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the top is golden brown and the casserole is bubbly.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!

Why This Recipe Is Budget-Friendly:

The beauty of this casserole is in its simplicity and the low cost of its ingredients. Canned tuna is an affordable source of protein, and the other ingredients like pasta, frozen peas, and cream of mushroom soup are inexpensive and can be found in most pantries. The dish can feed a family of four with minimal cost.

2. Cheesy Potato and Sausage Casserole

Potatoes are one of the most affordable and versatile ingredients you can have in your kitchen. Combined with sausage and a cheesy sauce, they make for a rich and satisfying casserole. This is a perfect dish for a hearty breakfast or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 lb of breakfast sausage (or any sausage of your choice)
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the diced potatoes in a pot of salted water until tender (about 10-15 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and crumbled. Remove any excess grease.
  4. Add the diced onion to the skillet with the sausage and cook for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the boiled potatoes, sausage and onion mixture, shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, milk, paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir until everything is well combined.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the casserole is hot and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!

Why This Recipe Is Budget-Friendly:

Potatoes and sausage are both affordable ingredients that can easily stretch to serve multiple people. The addition of sour cream and cheese adds richness without raising the cost too much. This casserole makes for a filling meal that won’t cost a fortune.

3. Vegetable and Rice Casserole

This vegetable and rice casserole is a healthy, budget-friendly option for those looking to enjoy a meal packed with veggies. You can use any vegetables you have on hand, making it a great dish for using up leftovers or produce that’s about to go bad.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of cooked rice (white, brown, or any variety you prefer)
  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (carrots, corn, peas, green beans, etc.)
  • 1 can of cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon of onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, mixed vegetables, cream of chicken soup, milk, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir until everything is evenly mixed.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the casserole is hot and the cheese is melted.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

Why This Recipe Is Budget-Friendly:

Rice is an incredibly inexpensive base for any casserole, and the addition of mixed vegetables, which can be bought frozen, helps keep costs low while still providing plenty of nutrients. The canned soup and cheese help bind everything together without needing more expensive ingredients.

4. Beef and Bean Casserole

For a protein-packed meal that’s both budget-friendly and filling, look no further than beef and bean casserole. Ground beef and beans are an affordable duo that creates a hearty meal that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb of ground beef
  • 2 cans of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1/2 cup of frozen corn
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of tortilla chips, crushed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir until everything is well combined and heated through.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese and crushed tortilla chips on top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Serve with a side of sour cream or avocado if desired.

Why This Recipe Is Budget-Friendly:

Ground beef is often on sale, and beans are one of the most affordable protein sources. This casserole also uses inexpensive pantry staples like canned tomatoes and spices, making it a very cost-effective dish that delivers on flavor.

5. Chicken and Broccoli Casserole

If you’re looking for a healthier option that’s still filling, chicken and broccoli casserole is a great choice. This dish is packed with lean protein and vegetables, and it’s creamy without being overly indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked chicken, shredded or chopped
  • 2 cups of broccoli florets (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 can of cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Steam the broccoli florets until they are tender (about 5 minutes if using fresh).
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, steamed broccoli, cream of chicken soup, sour cream, and shredded cheddar cheese. Stir until everything is well mixed.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. Top with breadcrumbs and an extra handful of cheese if desired.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the casserole is bubbling and the top is golden brown.
  7. Serve and enjoy!

Why This Recipe Is Budget-Friendly:

Chicken breast is an affordable protein that can be bought in bulk or on sale, and broccoli (whether frozen or fresh) is a low-cost vegetable that adds plenty of nutrition. The creamy sauce uses budget-friendly ingredients like soup and sour cream to give the casserole its richness without using expensive creams or cheeses.

Conclusion

Casseroles are a timeless and budget-friendly solution for feeding your family without spending a fortune. With these recipes, you can create hearty, delicious dishes that make the most of affordable ingredients like rice, pasta, beans, and canned goods. Not only will your wallet thank you, but your taste buds will too! Whether you’re in the mood for a classic tuna casserole, a cheesy potato bake, or a veggie-packed rice casserole, there’s a recipe here for everyone. Try them out and enjoy the satisfaction of a great meal that doesn’t cost a lot.
