Fluffy and Moist Zucchini Bread Recipe


If you’re looking for a delightful treat that combines the goodness of zucchini with the comforting flavors of homemade bread, then look no further! This fluffy and moist zucchini bread recipe is the perfect way to use up that surplus of zucchini from your garden or farmers market. With its tender crumb and subtle sweetness, this bread is sure to become a favorite among your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups grated zucchini
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Set aside.

  3. In another bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined and slightly frothy.

  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing just until the flour is fully incorporated. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread.

  5. Gently fold in the grated zucchini and chopped walnuts or pecans, if desired. The zucchini adds moisture and a subtle flavor, while the nuts add a delightful crunch.

  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it evenly with a spatula.

  7. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The bread should be golden brown and springy to the touch.

  8. Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

  9. Once cooled, slice and serve the zucchini bread on its own, or with a pat of butter or cream cheese. It’s also delicious when toasted and topped with your favorite jam or honey.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe even more indulgent, you can add a handful of chocolate chips to the batter before baking. The melted chocolate will create pockets of gooey deliciousness throughout the bread.

  • If you prefer a spicier flavor profile, you can add a pinch of ground cloves or ginger to the dry ingredients. This will give the bread a warm and aromatic twist.

  • Don’t worry if your zucchini bread sinks slightly in the middle. This is normal and happens due to the moisture content of the zucchini. It won’t affect the taste or texture of the bread.

  • This zucchini bread can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you want to keep it longer, you can freeze individual slices and thaw them as needed.

So there you have it - a fluffy and moist zucchini bread recipe that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you enjoy it for breakfast, as a snack, or as a dessert, this bread is guaranteed to satisfy your cravings. Give it a try and let the delightful aroma of freshly baked zucchini bread fill your kitchen!
