Paleo Turkey and Cranberry Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


The holiday season is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a delicious and healthy Paleo meal? If you’re looking for some tasty options that are both grain-free and full of flavor, look no further. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing five mouthwatering Paleo turkey and cranberry recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends. So, let’s get cooking!

1. Herb-Roasted Turkey with Cranberry Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the mixture all over the turkey, making sure to get into all the nooks and crannies.
  4. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a roasting pan and cover with foil.
  5. Roast the turkey for about 3 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  6. Remove the foil and brush the cranberry sauce over the turkey.
  7. Return the turkey to the oven and roast for an additional 30 minutes, or until the glaze is sticky and caramelized.
  8. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving. Serve with additional cranberry sauce on the side.

2. Paleo Turkey Meatballs with Cranberry Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, onion, garlic, parsley, cranberry sauce, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the meatballs are cooked through and golden brown.
  5. Serve the turkey meatballs with additional cranberry sauce for dipping.

3. Paleo Turkey and Cranberry Stuffed Sweet Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pierce the sweet potatoes several times with a fork and place them on a baking sheet.
  3. Bake the sweet potatoes for 45-60 minutes, or until they are tender.
  4. Let the sweet potatoes cool slightly, then slice them in half lengthwise.
  5. Scoop out some of the flesh from each sweet potato half and place it in a bowl.
  6. Add the cooked turkey, cranberry sauce, pecans, parsley, salt, and pepper to the bowl with the sweet potato flesh. Mix well.
  7. Spoon the turkey and cranberry mixture back into the sweet potato skins.
  8. Return the stuffed sweet potatoes to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until heated through.
  9. Serve the stuffed sweet potatoes as a main dish or as a side.

4. Paleo Turkey and Cranberry Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • Balsamic vinaigrette dressing

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the salad greens, cooked turkey, dried cranberries, sliced almonds, and red onion.
  2. Drizzle the salad with balsamic vinaigrette dressing and toss gently to coat.
  3. Serve the turkey and cranberry salad as a light and refreshing meal.

5. Paleo Turkey and Cranberry Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 4 large lettuce leaves
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup shredded carrots

Instructions:

  1. Lay the lettuce leaves flat on a clean surface.
  2. Divide the cooked turkey, cranberry sauce, sliced almonds, and shredded carrots evenly among the lettuce leaves.
  3. Roll up the lettuce leaves tightly, enclosing the filling.
  4. Secure the wraps with toothpicks if needed.
  5. Serve the turkey and cranberry wraps as a quick and easy handheld meal.

Conclusion

These five Paleo turkey and cranberry recipes are not only delicious and grain-free but also perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or simply looking for a healthy meal option, these recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds. So, why not give them a try and wow your loved ones with these flavorful and wholesome dishes? Happy cooking!
