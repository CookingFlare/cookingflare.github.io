How to Prepare Korean Grilled Mackerel (Godeungeo Gui)


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and healthy ingredients. One dish that stands out in this culinary landscape is Godeungeo Gui, or Korean grilled mackerel. This dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, making it a favorite among seafood lovers. In this blog post, we will explore the history of Godeungeo Gui, the health benefits of mackerel, and a step-by-step guide on how to prepare this delightful dish at home.

The History of Godeungeo Gui

Mackerel has been a staple in Korean diets for centuries, particularly in coastal regions where fresh fish is abundant. The word “Godeungeo” translates to mackerel in Korean, and “Gui” means grilled. Traditionally, Godeungeo Gui is enjoyed during the summer months, often served with a side of rice and various banchan (side dishes). The grilling process enhances the natural flavors of the fish, making it a beloved dish in Korean households.

Health Benefits of Mackerel

Mackerel is not only delicious but also incredibly nutritious. Here are some of the health benefits associated with this oily fish:

  1. Rich in Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Mackerel is an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, which are essential for heart health. These healthy fats can help reduce inflammation and lower the risk of heart disease.

  2. High in Protein: Mackerel is packed with high-quality protein, making it an ideal choice for those looking to build muscle or maintain a healthy weight.

  3. Vitamins and Minerals: This fish is rich in vitamins B12 and D, as well as minerals like selenium and phosphorus, which are vital for overall health.

  4. Low in Mercury: Compared to other fish, mackerel has lower levels of mercury, making it a safer option for regular consumption.

Ingredients for Godeungeo Gui

To prepare Godeungeo Gui, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 whole mackerel (cleaned and scaled)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 green onions (chopped)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Salt (to taste)

Step-by-Step Guide to Preparing Godeungeo Gui

Step 1: Preparing the Mackerel

  1. Clean the Fish: If your mackerel is not already cleaned, rinse it under cold water. Use a sharp knife to remove the head and tail if desired. Make a shallow cut along the belly to open it up and remove the innards. Rinse again and pat dry with paper towels.

  2. Score the Fish: Using a sharp knife, make diagonal cuts on both sides of the mackerel. This will help the marinade penetrate the fish and ensure even cooking.

Step 2: Making the Marinade

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sesame oil, gochugaru, minced garlic, minced ginger, honey (or sugar), and black pepper. Stir until well combined.

  2. Marinate the Fish: Place the cleaned mackerel in a shallow dish or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the fish, ensuring it is well coated. Add the chopped green onions for extra flavor. Cover the dish or seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.

Step 3: Grilling the Mackerel

  1. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If using a charcoal grill, ensure the coals are evenly distributed and glowing.

  2. Prepare the Grill Grate: Lightly oil the grill grate to prevent the fish from sticking. You can use a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil and tongs to wipe the grate.

  3. Grill the Mackerel: Remove the mackerel from the marinade and let any excess drip off. Place the fish on the grill, skin side down. Grill for about 5-7 minutes on one side, then carefully flip the fish using a spatula or tongs. Grill for an additional 5-7 minutes on the other side, or until the fish is cooked through and has nice grill marks.

Step 4: Serving Godeungeo Gui

  1. Plate the Fish: Once the mackerel is cooked, carefully transfer it to a serving platter. You can garnish it with additional chopped green onions and lemon wedges for a fresh touch.
