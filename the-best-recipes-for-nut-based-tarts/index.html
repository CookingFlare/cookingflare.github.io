The Best Recipes for Nut-Based Tarts


Nut-based tarts are a delightful fusion of crunch and creaminess, offering a rich and decadent treat that pleases both the sweet and savory palate. Whether you’re hosting a festive dinner party, indulging in a weekend baking project, or simply craving a dessert that goes beyond the ordinary, nut-based tarts deliver a depth of flavor and texture that’s truly distinctive.

From buttery pecan pies to sophisticated hazelnut custard tarts, nut-based recipes can cater to nearly every taste. With endless combinations of nuts and fillings, these tarts can transition seamlessly from season to season, making them a versatile dessert option for any occasion. Below, you’ll discover tried-and-true recipes for nut-based tarts that are as stunning as they are scrumptious.

1. Classic Pecan Tart

A timeless favorite, the pecan tart embodies everything you love about comfort desserts: warm, nutty flavors, sweet caramel-like undertones, and a flaky golden crust. This recipe captures traditional elements while adding a modern twist to elevate its flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled
    • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 2-3 tbsp cold water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup light corn syrup
    • ½ cup brown sugar
    • 3 large eggs
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 2 cups pecans, halved or chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, combine flour and sugar. Add cold butter and, using a pastry cutter, blend until coarse crumbs form. Add the egg yolk and water until the dough comes together. Wrap in plastic wrap and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface and transfer it to your tart pan. Trim excess dough and prick the base with a fork. Blind bake at 350°F for 15 minutes.
  3. Make the filling: In a medium bowl, whisk together corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla. Arrange pecans in the tart shell and pour the filling over them.
  4. Bake for 35–40 minutes at 350°F until the edges are set, but the center remains slightly wobbly. Cool before serving.

2. Chocolate Almond Tart

Take your nut-based tart game to the next level with this dreamy chocolate almond creation. The combination of bittersweet chocolate and roasted almonds provides a luxurious treat for any dessert lover.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ⅔ cups almond flour
    • 3 tbsp powdered sugar
    • 4 tbsp chilled butter
    • 1 large egg

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
    • ½ cup heavy cream
    • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
    • 2 large eggs
    • ½ cup slivered almonds, toasted

Instructions:

  1. Build the crust: Combine almond flour and powdered sugar. Cut in chilled butter until crumbly. Add the egg and mix to form a dough. Press the dough into a tart pan and chill for 20 minutes. Pre-bake at 350°F for 10 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Melt chocolate chips and heavy cream over low heat, stirring until smooth. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Whisk in cocoa powder and eggs until creamy.
  3. Pour the chocolate mixture into the crust and sprinkle evenly with toasted almonds. Bake for 25–30 minutes at 350°F. Allow to cool before slicing.

3. Walnut Maple Tart

Embrace fall flavors with this irresistible walnut maple tart. The earthy depth of walnuts pairs beautifully with the sweetness of pure maple syrup, making this recipe a guaranteed crowd-pleaser for the holiday season.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ⅓ cups whole wheat pastry flour
    • ½ cup olive oil
    • 2 tbsp maple syrup
    • 2 tbsp ice water

  • For the filling:

    • ¾ cup pure maple syrup
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 3 large eggs
    • ⅓ cup heavy cream
    • 1 ½ cups walnuts, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Mix flour, olive oil, and maple syrup in a bowl until combined. Add ice water until the dough comes together, then roll it out and shape in the tart pan. Blind bake for 10–12 minutes at 375°F.
  2. Mix the filling: Whisk maple syrup, sugar, eggs, and heavy cream in a bowl. Stir in walnuts and pour the mixture into the pre-baked tart shell.
  3. Bake for 35 minutes at 375°F, or until the filling sets. Cool before serving and top with whipped cream for extra indulgence.

4. Pistachio Rose Tart

For a sophisticated dessert, look no further than the pistachio rose tart. With its vibrant green filling and delicate floral notes, this tart offers a show-stopping blend of flavors ideal for elegant gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup salted butter
    • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
    • 1 large egg

  • For the filling:

    • ¾ cup shelled pistachios
    • ½ cup cream cheese, softened
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
    • ½ tsp rosewater
    • 1 large egg

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Blend flour, butter, and sugar until crumbly, then mix in the egg until dough forms. Roll out the crust and line a tart pan. Pre-bake at 350°F for 10–12 minutes.
  2. Make the filling: Pulse pistachios in a food processor until fine. Combine with cream cheese, sugar, rosewater, and egg until smooth. Spread the mixture into the pre-baked crust.
  3. Bake for 25–30 minutes at 350°F or until firm. Cool before garnishing with powdered sugar or crushed pistachios.

5. Hazelnut Espresso Tart

Rich and robust, the hazelnut espresso tart combines the nutty essence of roasted hazelnuts with the bold flavor of espresso for a dessert that’s perfect for coffee lovers.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
    • ⅓ cup hazelnut meal
    • 3 tbsp melted butter
    • 2 tbsp sugar

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup dark chocolate, melted
    • ½ cup condensed milk
    • 2 tsp instant espresso powder
    • ¾ cup hazelnuts, roasted and chopped

Instructions:

  1. Create the crust: Mix graham cracker crumbs, hazelnut meal, butter, and sugar. Press the mixture into your tart pan and chill for 30 minutes. Pre-bake at 350°F for 8–10 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Stir melted chocolate, condensed milk, and espresso powder together until smooth. Fold in roasted hazelnuts and spread evenly into the cooled crust.
  3. Chill in the refrigerator for 2–4 hours before serving for a silky, set texture.

Nut-based tarts offer something unique for every dessert enthusiast. With the ability to showcase a variety of flavors, textures, and presentations, these recipes are the perfect addition to your dessert repertoire. Whether you prefer classic sweetness or modern twists, nut-based tarts are worth every bite—and every experiment in the kitchen!
