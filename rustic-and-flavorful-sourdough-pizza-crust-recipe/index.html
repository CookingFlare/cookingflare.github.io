Rustic and Flavorful Sourdough Pizza Crust Recipe


Are you a fan of homemade pizza but looking to take it to the next level? Look no further than this rustic and flavorful sourdough pizza crust recipe. Sourdough adds a unique tang and depth of flavor to the crust, elevating your pizza experience to a whole new level. Whether you’re a seasoned sourdough enthusiast or new to the world of wild yeast, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients

For the Sourdough Starter

  • 1/2 cup active sourdough starter
  • 1 cup bread flour
  • 1/2 cup water

For the Pizza Dough

  • 2 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup olive oil

For Topping (Optional)

  • Tomato sauce
  • Fresh mozzarella
  • Fresh basil
  • Any of your favorite toppings

Instructions

Day 1: Prepare the Sourdough Starter

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, mix 1/2 cup of active sourdough starter, 1 cup of bread flour, and 1/2 cup of water. Stir until well combined.
  2. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let it sit at room temperature for 8-12 hours or until the mixture is bubbly and has doubled in size.

Day 2: Make the Pizza Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 1/2 cups of bread flour and 1 teaspoon of salt.
  2. Add the bubbly sourdough starter to the bowl, along with 1/2 cup of water and 1/2 cup of olive oil.
  3. Mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a kitchen towel, and let it rise at room temperature for 4-6 hours, or until doubled in size.

Day 2 (Later): Shape and Bake the Pizza

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can go, usually around 500-550°F (260-290°C).
  2. Divide the dough in half to make two pizzas or leave it whole for a larger pizza.
  3. On a lightly floured surface, shape the dough into your desired pizza shape.
  4. Transfer the shaped dough onto a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  5. Add your favorite toppings, such as tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and basil.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and melted.

Tips for Success

  • Use a kitchen scale to measure ingredients for accuracy.
  • Experiment with different toppings to create your own signature pizza.
  • Let the dough rise slowly in the refrigerator for a deeper flavor.

Conclusion

There’s something undeniably satisfying about making pizza from scratch, and this sourdough pizza crust takes it to a whole new level. The tangy flavor of the sourdough combined with the rustic texture of the crust creates a pizza experience that is unparalleled. Whether you’re hosting a pizza night with friends or craving a homemade slice, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, get your hands doughy, and savor the delicious results of this rustic and flavorful sourdough pizza crust recipe!
