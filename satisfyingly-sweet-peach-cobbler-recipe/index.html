Satisfyingly Sweet Peach Cobbler Recipe


There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting fruit cobbler, and when it comes to summer desserts, peach cobbler takes the crown. This satisfyingly sweet peach cobbler recipe will transport you to a sunny orchard with its juicy peaches and buttery, golden crust. So grab your apron and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 6 large ripe peaches, peeled, pitted, and sliced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)
  • Vanilla ice cream, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches with 1/4 cup of the granulated sugar. Toss gently to coat the peaches evenly. Set aside and let them macerate for about 15 minutes. This will release some of the peach juices and enhance the flavor.

  3. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, remaining 3/4 cup sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the melted butter, milk, and vanilla extract. Stir until just combined. The batter will be slightly lumpy, but that’s okay.

  4. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly. Now, spoon the peaches and their juices over the batter. Don’t worry if the peaches sink a little, they will rise to the top during baking.

  5. If desired, sprinkle the cinnamon evenly over the peaches. This adds a warm and cozy touch to the cobbler.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the peaches are bubbling. A toothpick inserted into the crust should come out clean.

  7. Once baked, remove the peach cobbler from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top for an extra indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic peach cobbler, try adding a handful of fresh berries like raspberries or blueberries to the peach mixture before baking. The combination of sweet peaches and tart berries is simply divine.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also add a sprinkle of chopped nuts, such as almonds or pecans, to the batter for some added crunch and flavor.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

  • This peach cobbler recipe is a crowd-pleaser, so feel free to double the ingredients and bake it in a larger baking dish if you’re serving a larger group.

Final Thoughts

Indulge in the delightful flavors of summer with this satisfyingly sweet peach cobbler recipe. The combination of juicy peaches, buttery crust, and a hint of cinnamon is sure to leave your taste buds craving for more. Whether you’re enjoying it on a warm summer evening or during a cozy winter night, this peach cobbler will always bring a smile to your face. So gather your loved ones, savor the joy of homemade desserts, and make unforgettable memories with this timeless classic.
