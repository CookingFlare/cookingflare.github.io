Paleo-Friendly French Seafood Recipes: Classic Dishes Made Healthy


Are you a seafood lover who is also following a Paleo diet? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore some delicious French seafood recipes that are not only packed with flavor but also conform to your Paleo lifestyle. Get ready to indulge in classic French dishes made healthy!

Bouillabaisse

Let’s start with a true gem of French cuisine – Bouillabaisse. Traditionally, this fish stew is made with a variety of Mediterranean fish and shellfish, but we’ll give it a Paleo twist. Instead of the usual white wine and tomatoes, we’ll use rich bone broth and aromatic herbs.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound white fish fillets (such as cod or halibut), cut into chunks
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups fish or vegetable bone broth
  • 1 fennel bulb, thinly sliced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 ounces)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add sliced fennel bulb and cook until softened.
  3. Pour in the bone broth, diced tomatoes, thyme, and oregano. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the fish chunks and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Gently add the shrimp and mussels, and continue cooking until the shrimp are pink and the mussels have opened.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Coquilles Saint-Jacques

Next up, we have a classic French appetizer – Coquilles Saint-Jacques, or seared scallops in a creamy sauce. To make it Paleo-friendly, we’ll replace the traditional butter and cream with coconut oil and coconut milk.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound sea scallops
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons arrowroot powder
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat the scallops dry with a paper towel and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the scallops and sear for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  3. Remove the scallops from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until translucent.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together coconut milk and arrowroot powder until smooth.
  6. Pour the coconut milk mixture into the skillet and bring to a simmer, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens.
  7. Stir in lemon juice and chopped parsley.
  8. Return the seared scallops to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes, until heated through.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Salade Niçoise

Last but not least, we have a refreshing and vibrant salad – Salade Niçoise. This Mediterranean-inspired dish is typically made with tuna, but we’ll substitute it with grilled salmon to make it Paleo-friendly.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound salmon fillet
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Season the salmon fillet with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the salmon for about 4-5 minutes on each side, until cooked through. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the salad greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber slices, red onion, Kalamata olives, and sliced hard-boiled eggs.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together extra virgin olive oil, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Break the grilled salmon into flakes and add it to the salad.
  6. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Conclusion

French cuisine is known for its rich flavors and elegant dishes, and with these Paleo-friendly recipes, you can savor the taste of France while maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Whether you’re a seafood aficionado or simply looking to try something new, these classic French dishes will surely impress your taste buds. Bon appétit!
