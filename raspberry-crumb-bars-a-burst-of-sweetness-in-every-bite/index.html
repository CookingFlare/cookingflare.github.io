Raspberry Crumb Bars: A Burst of Sweetness in Every Bite


There’s something truly delightful about the combination of sweet, tangy raspberries and buttery, crumbly crust. If you’re a fan of fruity desserts that are easy to make and even easier to devour, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Raspberry Crumb Bars.

The Perfect Balance of Sweet and Tart

Raspberry Crumb Bars are the perfect marriage of sweet and tart flavors. The natural tartness of fresh raspberries is complemented by the buttery richness of the crumbly crust, creating a delightful balance that keeps you coming back for more. Whether you’re hosting a summer BBQ, a brunch, or just looking for a delightful treat to enjoy with your afternoon tea, these bars are sure to be a hit.

Simple Ingredients, Spectacular Results

One of the best things about this recipe is its simplicity. With just a handful of basic ingredients, you can whip up a batch of these delectable bars in no time. Fresh raspberries, flour, sugar, and butter come together to create a dessert that looks and tastes like it came straight from a fancy bakery.

A Versatile Dessert for Any Occasion

These Raspberry Crumb Bars are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in a variety of settings. Pack them up for a picnic, serve them at a bridal shower, or simply indulge in a few as a sweet ending to a family dinner. The buttery crumb crust acts as the perfect base for the juicy raspberry filling, making these bars a delightful addition to any dessert spread.

How to Make Raspberry Crumb Bars

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 teaspoons cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, 1 cup of sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter to blend the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Beat the egg and vanilla extract together, then add it to the crumb mixture, stirring until combined.
  5. Press half of the crumb mixture into the prepared baking pan, creating an even layer.
  6. In another bowl, combine the raspberries, 1/2 cup of sugar, and cornstarch, gently mashing the raspberries as you mix.
  7. Spread the raspberry mixture over the crust in the pan.
  8. Crumble the remaining crumb mixture over the raspberry layer.
  9. Bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.
  10. Allow the bars to cool completely before slicing into squares and serving.

A Crowd-Pleasing Dessert

The beauty of Raspberry Crumb Bars lies in their ability to please just about anyone with a sweet tooth. The combination of buttery crumb and juicy raspberry filling is a crowd-pleaser, and the fact that they’re so easy to make is an added bonus.

So, whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, these Raspberry Crumb Bars are a must-try. Give this recipe a whirl, and get ready to savor the burst of sweetness in every bite!
