Fall-Inspired Burgers: Pumpkin and Spice Recipes to Try


As the leaves turn and the air grows crisp, it’s time to embrace everything that autumn offers. One of the most delightful things about fall is its bounty of seasonal flavors—think pumpkin, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. These ingredients can transform the humble burger into a festive feast that celebrates the spirit of the season. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a cozy family dinner, or simply craving a comforting meal, fall-inspired burgers are a delicious solution. Here, we’ll explore several pumpkin and spice burger recipes that you can easily prepare at home. The Allure of Fall Flavors Before diving into the recipes, let’s discuss why fall flavors are so popular. Pumpkin, a staple of the season, is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. It’s rich in beta-carotene, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals. Pairing pumpkin with warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg can evoke feelings of comfort and nostalgia, making every bite a taste of seasonal joy. Burgers, typically a summer staple, can be reimagined with these ingredients, resulting in something uniquely seasonal that tantalizes your taste buds. Classic Pumpkin Spice Burger Ingredients For the Burger Patty:

1 lb ground turkey or chicken 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree 1/4 cup breadcrumbs 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 tsp garlic powder 1 tsp onion powder 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1/2 tsp nutmeg 1/2 tsp salt 1/4 tsp black pepper 1 large egg, beaten

For the Toppings:

Whole grain burgers buns Arugula or spinach Cream cheese spread or herbed goat cheese Sliced apples or pears (for added sweetness)

Instructions

Prepare the Patties: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey or chicken, pumpkin puree, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Add the beaten egg and mix thoroughly until everything is well incorporated.

Form Burgers: Shape the mixture into patties (about 4-5 patties). Ensure they are thick enough to hold up during cooking.

Cook the Patties: Preheat a grill or a skillet over medium heat. Cook the patties for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they reach an internal temperature of 165°F. They should have a nice golden-brown exterior.

Assemble the Burger: Toast the whole grain buns lightly. Spread a generous layer of cream cheese or herbed goat cheese on the bottom bun. Place the patty on top, add a handful of arugula or spinach, and finish with sliced apples or pears for a touch of sweetness.

Serve and Enjoy: Close the burger with the top bun and serve with a side of sweet potato fries or a seasonal salad.

Maple Bacon Pumpkin Burger Ingredients For the Burger Patty:

1 lb ground beef 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree 1/4 cup breadcrumbs 1/4 cup diced cooked bacon 1 tsp maple syrup 1 tsp smoked paprika 1/2 tsp salt 1/4 tsp black pepper

For the Toppings:

Brioche burger buns Maple mayonnaise (mix mayonnaise with a splash of maple syrup) Fried crispy bacon strips Sliced cheddar cheese Caramelized onions

Instructions

Mix the Patty Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine ground beef with pumpkin puree, breadcrumbs, cooked bacon pieces, maple syrup, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined.

Form the Patties: Shape into 4 equal patties. Remember that they will shrink slightly while cooking.

Cooking The Patties: Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for about 5-6 minutes on each side. In the last minute of cooking, place a slice of cheddar cheese on each patty to melt.

Prepare the Buns: Toast the brioche buns until golden brown. Spread a thin layer of maple mayonnaise on each bun.

Assemble: Start with the bottom bun, add the bacon pumpkin patty, crispy bacon strips, and caramelized onions, then top with the other half of the bun.

Serve: This burger pairs wonderfully with a side of coleslaw or roasted butternut squash.

Sweet Potato and Pumpkin Veggie Burger Ingredients For the Burger Patty:

1 cup sweet potato, cooked and mashed 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree 1/2 cup black beans, rinsed and mashed 1 cup quinoa, cooked 1 tsp chili powder 1 tsp cumin 1/2 tsp garlic powder 1/2 tsp onion powder Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 cup flour or breadcrumbs (for binding)

For the Toppings:

Whole wheat burger buns Avocado slices Fresh cilantro Spicy mayo or chipotle sauce

Instructions

Prepare the Bean Mixture: In a bowl, mix together the sweet potato, pumpkin puree, and black beans. Add the cooked quinoa, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.

Bind the Mixture: Gradually add flour or breadcrumbs until the mixture holds together when formed into a patty.

Form the Patties: Shape the mixture into 4 patties.

Cook the Patties: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook each patty for about 5 minutes on each side until golden brown and heated through.

Assemble: Toast the whole wheat buns. Place an avocado slice on the bottom bun, add the sweet potato and pumpkin patty, drizzle with spicy mayo, and sprinkle with cilantro.

Serve and Enjoy: This colorful burger is perfect for a meatless meal and goes well with a side salad topped with seasonal veggies.

Pumpkin Portobello Burger Ingredients

4 large portobello mushroom caps 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp dried thyme 1/2 tsp salt 1/4 tsp pepper Olive oil

For the Toppings:

Whole-grain burger buns Pesto sauce Fresh mozzarella cheese Fresh basil leaves

Instructions

Marinate the Mushrooms: In a bowl, whisk together pumpkin puree, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper. Add portobello caps to the marinade and let sit for at least 30 minutes.

Cook the Mushrooms: Preheat a grill or skillet over medium heat. Brush the mushrooms with olive oil and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and cooked through.

Prepare the Buns: Toast whole-grain buns lightly if desired. Spread a layer of pesto on the bottom.

Assemble the Burger: Place the grilled portobello cap on the bun, add a slice of fresh mozzarella, and a few basil leaves.

Serve and Savor: Close with the top bun and serve alongside a crisp salad for a light yet satisfying meal.

Tips for Perfect Fall Burgers

Quality Ingredients: Using high-quality ingredients, especially seasonal produce, will elevate your burger's flavor.

Don’t Skip the Marinade: Marinating your ingredients, especially mushrooms and meats, can greatly enhance flavor and tenderness.

Creative Combinations: Feel free to mix flavors! Spicy elements like jalapeños or toppings like cranberry sauce can be delightful complements to fall burgers.

Serve with Seasonal Sides: Pair your burgers with sides that celebrate autumn too. Sweet potato fries, roasted vegetables, or a hearty grain salad work beautifully.

Experiment with Buns: While classic burger buns are great, trying out different types like pretzel buns, brioche, or whole wheat can add new dimensions to your meal.

Conclusion Embracing fall flavors in your cooking is a great way to celebrate the season. Pumpkin and spices are versatile ingredients that can bring warmth and festivity to your burgers. Whether you choose to whip up a classic pumpkin spice burger, indulge in a hearty maple bacon option, or create a delicious veggie alternative, there are endless ways to get creative in your kitchen. So pull out your aprons, gather your ingredients, and enjoy these cozy, savory creations that capture the essence of fall in every bite. Happy cooking!
