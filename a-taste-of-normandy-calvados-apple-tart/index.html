A Taste of Normandy: Calvados Apple Tart


If there’s one thing that evokes the essence of Normandy, it’s the delightful combination of sweet, tangy apples and the rich, robust flavor of Calvados, the region’s famous apple brandy. The Calvados Apple Tart is a classic French dessert that captures the essence of Normandy in every bite. This indulgent treat is the perfect blend of buttery pastry, caramelized apples, and a touch of Calvados, resulting in a heavenly dessert that’s sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Filling:

  • 4-5 large apples, such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons Calvados
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pastry:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and sugar. Add the chilled, cubed butter and use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs.
    • Add the egg yolk and 2 tablespoons of ice-cold water. Mix gently until the dough comes together, adding more water if needed. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced apples, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the apples are tender and caramelized, about 10-15 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the Calvados. Allow the filling to cool to room temperature.

  3. Assemble the Tart:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry into a circle large enough to line a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the pastry to the tart pan, pressing it gently into the corners and trimming any excess dough.
    • Arrange the cooled apple filling over the pastry in an even layer.
    • If desired, use any excess pastry to create a lattice or other decorative pattern on top of the tart.

  4. Bake and Serve:

    • Place the assembled tart on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and the apples are bubbling.
    • Once baked, remove the tart from the oven and allow it to cool slightly before slicing and serving. For an extra touch of indulgence, serve with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

The Flavors of Normandy in Every Bite

The Calvados Apple Tart is a celebration of the bountiful apple orchards and rich culinary traditions of Normandy. With its buttery, flaky pastry and the irresistible aroma of caramelized apples and Calvados, this dessert is a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply craving a taste of France, this recipe is sure to transport you to the charming countryside of Normandy with every delectable bite.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather the freshest apples you can find, and let the enchanting flavors of Normandy come to life in your kitchen. From the first forkful to the last, the Calvados Apple Tart promises to be a memorable culinary journey that pays homage to the culinary heritage of this picturesque region. Bon appétit!
