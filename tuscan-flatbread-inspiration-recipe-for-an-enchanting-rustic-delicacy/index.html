Tuscan Flatbread Inspiration: Recipe for an Enchanting Rustic Delicacy


Ah, the beautiful region of Tuscany in Italy is known for its rolling vineyards, charming hilltop towns, and of course, its scrumptious cuisine. With its mouth-watering flavors and rustic appeal, Tuscan food is truly a unique culinary delight. So, it’s no wonder that we’re taking inspiration from this charming area to create our very own Tuscan flatbread. This crowd-pleaser ditches the traditional toppings for a fresh and flavorful combination of ingredients that beautifully embody the essence of Tuscan flavors. Let’s create an enchanting rustic delicacy in your own kitchen!

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 2/3 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1/2 cup drained and chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced prosciutto
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling

Flatbread Dough:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Gradually pour in the warm water, and stir until the dough begins to come together. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil and knead the dough until smooth, about 5-7 minutes. Cover the dough with a damp towel and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  2. Preheat your oven to 500 degrees Fahrenheit (260 degrees Celsius) and prepare a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Roll the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface to form a rectangle, about 12x16 inches. Place the dough on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes, until lightly golden and crisp.

Tuscan Flatbread Toppings:

  1. While the flatbread is baking, combine the sun-dried tomatoes, basil, and the remaining tablespoon of olive oil in a bowl and set it aside.

  2. Once the flatbread is finished baking, remove it from the oven and spread the sun-dried tomato mixture evenly over the surface, leaving a small border around the edges.

  3. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese over the tomato mixture, followed by the prosciutto, onions, and walnuts. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil for that authentic Tuscan touch.

  4. Return the flatbread to the oven and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the ingredients on top are heated through and the crust is a deeper golden brown. Keep an eye on your flatbread to ensure that it doesn’t burn.

  5. Slice your Tuscan flatbread into small squares or rectangles and serve it up immediately so that the crust remains crisp and the toppings remain delightful. Garnish with a sprig of fresh basil if desired. Enjoy this enchanting Tuscan-inspired treat with a glass of robust red wine or refreshing Prosecco for a truly unforgettable culinary experience!

The next time you’re looking to impress dinner guests or just treat yourself to a scrumptious Italian-inspired delight, look no further than this Tuscan flatbread recipe. With its mouthwatering combination of flavors and rustic charm, it’s sure to become a hit at any gathering, as you transport your taste buds straight to the rolling vineyards and hilltop towns of the enchanting Tuscan region. Buon appetito!
