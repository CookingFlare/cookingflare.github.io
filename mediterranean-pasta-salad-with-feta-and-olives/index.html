Mediterranean Pasta Salad with Feta and Olives


Mediterranean cuisine has long been celebrated for its bold flavors, healthy ingredients, and vibrant colors. One dish that embodies these qualities perfectly is the Mediterranean pasta salad with feta and olives. This versatile, refreshing, and wholesome salad is perfect for summer picnics, backyard barbecues, or even as a light meal on a busy weeknight. Its combination of pasta, creamy feta cheese, briny olives, and a variety of fresh vegetables brings together the best elements of Mediterranean cooking in a single dish.

The Essence of Mediterranean Cooking

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to understand what makes Mediterranean cuisine so special. The Mediterranean diet is known for its emphasis on fresh vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Olive oil, in particular, is a cornerstone of Mediterranean cooking. It’s rich in antioxidants and healthy monounsaturated fats, making it a key ingredient in many Mediterranean dishes, including our pasta salad.

This Mediterranean pasta salad with feta and olives brings together the finest ingredients from the Mediterranean region, offering a dish that is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Fresh, vibrant vegetables like cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onions provide a crunchy texture, while creamy feta cheese adds richness. The olives, a staple in Mediterranean cuisine, give the dish a delightful briny kick that ties all the flavors together.

Why You’ll Love This Mediterranean Pasta Salad

  • Refreshing and Flavorful: With a variety of fresh vegetables, tangy feta, and briny olives, this pasta salad is bursting with Mediterranean flavors.
  • Customizable: You can easily modify this salad to suit your taste. Add more vegetables, switch the type of pasta, or adjust the seasoning to make it your own.
  • Healthy and Wholesome: Packed with fresh ingredients, this salad is a great source of fiber, healthy fats, and antioxidants.
  • Easy to Make: With minimal prep and simple ingredients, this salad can be whipped up in no time, making it perfect for busy days or last-minute gatherings.
  • Great for Meal Prep: It keeps well in the fridge, so you can enjoy it for several days, making it an excellent option for meal prep.

Mediterranean Pasta Salad with Feta and Olives: Recipe

Ingredients:

For the Salad:

  • 1 pound (450g) of your favorite pasta (penne, rotini, or fusilli work well)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of crushed red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)

Instructions:

1. Cook the Pasta

Start by cooking the pasta according to the package instructions. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta until it’s al dente, which usually takes around 8-10 minutes. Drain the pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, and rinse the pasta under cold water to stop the cooking process. This step ensures the pasta stays firm and doesn’t become mushy in the salad.

2. Prepare the Vegetables

While the pasta is cooking, prep the vegetables. Slice the cherry tomatoes in half, dice the cucumber and red bell pepper, and thinly slice the red onion. You can also halve the olives if you prefer smaller pieces. The combination of these fresh, crunchy vegetables will add texture and color to your salad, making it as visually appealing as it is flavorful.

3. Make the Dressing

In a small bowl, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, dried oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. If you like a little heat, add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes to the dressing for an extra kick. Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed. You want the dressing to have a nice balance of tanginess, saltiness, and a touch of heat.

4. Assemble the Salad

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked and cooled pasta with the prepared vegetables. Toss everything together gently to combine. Add the crumbled feta cheese and olives, mixing until the feta is evenly distributed throughout the salad.

5. Add the Dressing

Drizzle the dressing over the pasta and vegetable mixture, then toss everything again to ensure the pasta is coated evenly with the dressing. If the salad seems a little dry, you can add some of the reserved pasta cooking water to help loosen it up. This step also ensures the pasta absorbs the flavors of the dressing.

6. Garnish and Serve

Before serving, garnish the Mediterranean pasta salad with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness. This step is optional, but it adds an extra layer of flavor that elevates the dish.

Tips for the Best Mediterranean Pasta Salad

  • Use High-Quality Olive Oil: The quality of the olive oil can make a significant difference in the flavor of the dressing. Opt for extra virgin olive oil for the best taste and health benefits.
  • Let the Salad Chill: For the best flavor, allow the salad to chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This gives the ingredients time to marinate and meld together.
  • Feel Free to Customize: This salad is highly customizable. You can add other Mediterranean ingredients such as artichoke hearts, roasted red peppers, or even grilled chicken for a heartier meal. For a vegan version, simply omit the feta cheese or replace it with a plant-based alternative.
  • Use Fresh Herbs: While parsley is the most common herb used for garnish, you can also experiment with fresh basil or mint to add a different twist to the flavor profile.
  • Store Leftovers Properly: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. Just be sure to give it a quick stir before serving, as the dressing may settle at the bottom of the bowl.

Why This Salad is Perfect for Every Occasion

The Mediterranean pasta salad with feta and olives is perfect for nearly every occasion. Here are a few scenarios where this dish will shine:

  • Picnics and Outdoor Gatherings: This salad is light, refreshing, and easy to transport. It doesn’t require reheating and can be served cold, making it the perfect dish for a picnic or a backyard barbecue.
  • Meal Prep: Preparing meals for the week can be a challenge, but this salad is ideal for meal prep. The ingredients hold up well in the fridge, and it’s an easy, satisfying dish to have on hand for lunch or dinner throughout the week.
  • Barbecues and Potlucks: If you’re heading to a barbecue or potluck, this pasta salad is sure to be a hit. It pairs well with grilled meats, seafood, or even a vegetarian spread.
  • Light Lunch or Dinner: When you’re looking for a quick and light meal, this salad is perfect. It’s filling but not heavy, making it great for a lunch or dinner that won’t leave you feeling sluggish.

Health Benefits of Mediterranean Ingredients

The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its health benefits, and the ingredients in this pasta salad are no exception. Here’s why:

  • Olives: Olives are a great source of healthy fats, particularly monounsaturated fats, which can help reduce the risk of heart disease. They are also packed with antioxidants, which help protect your body from oxidative stress.
  • Feta Cheese: Feta cheese adds a burst of flavor and is lower in fat and calories compared to many other cheeses. It’s also a good source of calcium and protein.
  • Cucumbers and Tomatoes: These vegetables are low in calories and high in water content, making them hydrating and refreshing. They’re also rich in vitamins and minerals like vitamin C, potassium, and antioxidants.
  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil is the gold standard when it comes to heart-healthy fats. It’s rich in monounsaturated fats and has anti-inflammatory properties, making it a great addition to any dish.
  • Herbs and Spices: Mediterranean cuisine uses a variety of herbs and spices like oregano, garlic, and parsley. These not only add flavor but also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Conclusion

The Mediterranean pasta salad with feta and olives is a vibrant, flavorful dish that’s easy to prepare and perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a summer party, prepping meals for the week, or simply craving something light yet satisfying, this salad is the answer. Its combination of fresh vegetables, tangy feta, briny olives, and the richness of olive oil creates a harmonious blend of flavors that will transport your taste buds straight to the Mediterranean coast.

By following this simple recipe, you can create a dish that is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients and health benefits. So next time you’re looking for a refreshing and satisfying meal, give this Mediterranean pasta salad a try—you won’t be disappointed!
