Thai Pineapple Curry with Chicken: A Sweet and Spicy Poultry Delight


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this mouthwatering Thai Pineapple Curry with Chicken recipe. This dish is a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors that will tantalize your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Thailand. The combination of succulent chicken, aromatic herbs, creamy coconut milk, and juicy pineapple creates a culinary masterpiece that is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

For the Curry Paste:

  • 3-4 Thai red chilies, deseeded
  • 3 shallots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 lemongrass stalk, white part only, thinly sliced
  • 1-inch piece of galangal, peeled and sliced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon shrimp paste
  • Zest of 1 kaffir lime

For the Curry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 1-2 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Curry Paste:

    • In a mortar and pestle or a food processor, pound or blend the red chilies, shallots, garlic, lemongrass, galangal, ground coriander, ground cumin, shrimp paste, and kaffir lime zest into a smooth paste.

  2. Cook the Chicken:

    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the curry paste and sauté for a few minutes until fragrant.
    • Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink on the outside.

  3. Simmer the Curry:

    • Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
    • Add the pineapple chunks and sliced red bell pepper, stirring gently to combine.

  4. Season and Finish:

    • Stir in the palm sugar, fish sauce, and torn kaffir lime leaves.
    • Let the curry simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the flavors have melded together.
    • Taste and season with salt if needed.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Remove the curry from the heat and stir in the fresh Thai basil leaves.
    • Serve the Thai Pineapple Curry with Chicken over steamed jasmine rice, garnished with additional basil leaves for a burst of freshness.

Tips for Success

  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the chicken with tofu or mixed vegetables such as bell peppers, snap peas, and baby corn.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or fewer red chilies to the curry paste.
  • Fresh pineapple is recommended for this recipe, but canned pineapple can be used as a convenient alternative.
  • If you can’t find kaffir lime leaves, you can substitute with a few strips of lime zest for a similar citrusy aroma.

Final Thoughts

Thai Pineapple Curry with Chicken is a delightful fusion of flavors that will elevate your home cooking experience. The tender chicken, luscious coconut milk, and tropical sweetness of pineapple come together with an array of aromatic spices to create a memorable dining experience. Whether you’re preparing a special dinner for guests or simply treating yourself to a flavorful meal, this Thai-inspired dish is sure to impress and satisfy. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Thailand with this irresistible Pineapple Curry with Chicken recipe.
