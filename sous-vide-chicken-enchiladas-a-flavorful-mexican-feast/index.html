Sous Vide Chicken Enchiladas: A Flavorful Mexican Feast


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine and are looking to add a new twist to your dinner repertoire, then look no further than Sous Vide Chicken Enchiladas. This flavorful dish combines tender, juicy chicken with the rich flavors of enchilada sauce and gooey melted cheese, making it a true feast for the senses. With the help of sous vide cooking, you can achieve perfectly cooked chicken every time, ensuring a delicious and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

For the chicken:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the enchilada sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the enchiladas:

  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • Chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Sour cream, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C).

  2. Season the chicken breasts with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Place them in a vacuum-sealed bag or a resealable plastic bag, removing as much air as possible before sealing.

  3. Submerge the bag of chicken breasts in the preheated water bath and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  4. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the enchilada sauce. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat, then add diced onions and minced garlic. Sauté until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  5. Add chili powder, cumin, dried oregano, diced tomatoes, and chicken broth to the saucepan. Stir well to combine, then bring the mixture to a simmer. Allow the sauce to cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the sous vide water bath and pat it dry with paper towels. Shred the chicken using two forks, then set it aside.

  7. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  8. Dip each flour tortilla into the enchilada sauce, coating both sides. Place a portion of shredded chicken in the center of each tortilla, then roll it up tightly. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and chicken.

  9. Place the rolled enchiladas in a baking dish, seam-side down. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the top, making sure to cover all the enchiladas. Sprinkle the shredded Mexican cheese blend evenly over the sauce.

  10. Bake the enchiladas in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  11. Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and serve with a dollop of sour cream.

Final Thoughts

Sous Vide Chicken Enchiladas are a perfect example of how sous vide cooking can elevate a classic dish to new heights. The precise temperature control ensures that the chicken is cooked to perfection, while the enchilada sauce adds a burst of flavor that complements the tender meat. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a Mexican-themed party, these enchiladas are sure to be a hit. So fire up your sous vide cooker and get ready to impress your taste buds with this delicious and flavorful Mexican feast!
