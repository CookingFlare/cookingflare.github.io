How to Make Homemade Hummus and Dips


Hummus is a delicious and versatile dip that is enjoyed by many cultures around the world. Whether you’re hosting a party, looking for a healthy snack, or simply craving a flavorful spread, homemade hummus and dips are the way to go. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making homemade hummus and share some exciting dip variations that will take your taste buds on a delightful journey. So, let’s dive in!

Classic Hummus Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (15 ounces)
  • 3 tablespoons of tahini
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: paprika, chopped parsley, olive oil drizzle

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, minced garlic, and lemon juice.
  3. Blend the mixture until it reaches a smooth and creamy consistency.
  4. If the hummus is too thick, add a little water or olive oil to thin it out.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give it a final blend to incorporate the flavors.
  6. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl and garnish with paprika, chopped parsley, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  7. Serve with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or your favorite crackers.

Exciting Dip Variations to Try

1. Roasted Red Pepper Hummus

  • Follow the classic hummus recipe above, but add 1 roasted red pepper (skins removed) to the food processor.
  • Blend until well combined, and you’ll have a vibrant and flavorful dip that pairs perfectly with grilled veggies or toasted bread.

2. Spicy Jalapeno Hummus

  • For those who like a little kick, this dip is for you! Follow the classic hummus recipe and add 1-2 seeded and chopped jalapenos to the food processor.
  • Blend until smooth, and you’ll have a spicy hummus that goes well with tortilla chips or as a spread on sandwiches.

3. Sun-Dried Tomato and Basil Hummus

  • Elevate your hummus game by adding the rich flavors of sun-dried tomatoes and fresh basil. Follow the classic hummus recipe and add 1/4 cup of sun-dried tomatoes (soaked in warm water to soften) and a handful of fresh basil leaves to the food processor.
  • Blend until creamy, and enjoy this tangy and aromatic dip with breadsticks or as a sandwich spread.

4. Avocado and Lime Hummus

  • Avocado lovers, rejoice! Combine the creaminess of avocado with the zesty freshness of lime to create a unique twist on classic hummus. Follow the classic hummus recipe and add 1 ripe avocado (peeled and pitted) and the juice of 1 lime to the food processor.
  • Blend until silky smooth, and serve this vibrant dip with vegetable sticks or as a topping for tacos.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade hummus and dips opens up a world of possibilities. By experimenting with different ingredients and flavors, you can create dips that suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. Whether you stick to the classic recipe or venture into exciting variations, the process of making hummus is a rewarding culinary experience. So, grab your ingredients, fire up the food processor, and get ready to dip into deliciousness. Happy cooking!
