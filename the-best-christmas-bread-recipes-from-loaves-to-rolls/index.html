The Best Christmas Bread Recipes: From Loaves to Rolls


The holiday season is a time for warmth, joy, and, of course, delicious food. Among the many culinary delights that grace our tables during Christmas, bread holds a special place. From sweet to savory, the aroma of freshly baked bread can evoke memories of family gatherings and festive celebrations. In this post, we will explore some of the best Christmas bread recipes, ranging from traditional loaves to delightful rolls, ensuring your holiday feast is both memorable and delicious.

The Tradition of Christmas Bread

Bread has been a staple in many cultures during the Christmas season. Each region boasts its unique recipes, often steeped in tradition and history. For instance, in Scandinavian countries, Julekake is a sweet, spiced bread studded with candied fruits and nuts, while in Hungary, Beigli is a rolled pastry filled with poppy seeds or walnuts. These breads not only serve as a delicious accompaniment to holiday meals but also symbolize the warmth and togetherness of the season.

Classic Christmas Loaves

1. Julekake (Norwegian Christmas Bread)

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp cardamom
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mixed candied fruits and nuts

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the milk until warm (not boiling). Remove from heat and stir in the yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cardamom, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, softened butter, and eggs.
  3. Mix until a dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Incorporate the candied fruits and nuts into the dough. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and shape it into a loaf. Place it in a greased loaf pan and let it rise again for about 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool before slicing.

2. Panettone (Italian Christmas Bread)

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups bread flour
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mixed dried fruits (raisins, candied orange peel, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, eggs, butter, and vanilla.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes. Incorporate the dried fruits.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1-2 hours until doubled.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a panettone mold. Let it rise for another hour.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 40-50 minutes. Cool before serving.

Festive Rolls for Your Holiday Table

3. Soft Dinner Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, and salt. Add the yeast mixture, melted butter, and egg.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Shape into rolls and place in a greased baking dish.
  6. Let rise for another 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 15-20 minutes until golden.

4. Garlic Herb Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried herbs (oregano, thyme, or rosemary)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, garlic, and herbs. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour.
  5. Shape the dough into rolls and place them in a greased baking dish. Let rise for another 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.

Sweet Treats for the Holidays

5. Cinnamon Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt, and cinnamon. Add the yeast mixture, butter, and eggs.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes.
  4. Roll the dough into a rectangle, spread with softened butter, and sprinkle with brown sugar and cinnamon.
  5. Roll up tightly and slice into rolls. Place in a greased baking dish and let rise for 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 20-25 minutes. Drizzle with icing before serving.

6. Stollen (German Christmas Bread)

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mixed dried fruits and nuts
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the yeast mixture, butter, and knead until smooth.
  3. Incorporate the dried fruits and nuts. Let the dough rise for 1 hour.
  4. Shape the dough into a loaf and place it on a baking sheet. Let it rise for another 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 30-35 minutes. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Tips for Perfect Christmas Bread

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Always use fresh yeast and high-quality flour for the best results.
  2. Knead Properly: Kneading develops gluten, which gives bread its structure. Don’t skip this step!
  3. Allow for Rising: Patience is key. Allow your dough to rise fully for a light and airy texture.
  4. Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to add your favorite spices, nuts, or dried fruits to personalize your bread.
  5. Serve Warm: Bread is best enjoyed fresh out of the oven. Serve it warm for the ultimate holiday experience.

Conclusion

Baking bread for Christmas is not just about the food; it’s about creating memories and sharing love with family and friends. Whether you choose to make a traditional loaf like Julekake or opt for soft dinner rolls, each recipe brings its unique flavor and charm to your holiday table. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the spirit of Christmas fill your kitchen with the delightful aroma of freshly baked bread. Happy baking!
