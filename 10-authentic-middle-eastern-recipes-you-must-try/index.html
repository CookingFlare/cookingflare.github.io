10 Authentic Middle Eastern Recipes You Must Try


Middle Eastern cuisine is a delightful blend of rich, diverse flavors and ingredients. Spanning multiple countries, the food from this region showcases a wide array of spices, fresh herbs, and hearty grains. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just beginning your culinary journey, these 10 authentic Middle Eastern recipes will elevate your cooking skills and take your taste buds on an unforgettable journey.

1. Hummus: The Classic Middle Eastern Dip

Hummus is one of the most iconic dishes in Middle Eastern cuisine. This creamy, velvety dip made from chickpeas is often served with pita bread or vegetables. It’s not just a dip; it can also be a side dish or a spread for sandwiches. With its base of tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic, hummus strikes the perfect balance of richness and freshness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Paprika, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic. Process until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. If the dip is too thick, add a bit of water to adjust the consistency.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle paprika on top for garnish.

Tips:

  • For an extra smooth hummus, peel the chickpeas before blending.
  • You can customize the flavor by adding roasted red peppers, herbs, or spices like cumin.

2. Tabbouleh: A Refreshing Herb Salad

Tabbouleh is a vibrant and refreshing salad made primarily from parsley, tomatoes, and bulgur wheat. It’s the perfect balance of fresh, tangy, and slightly nutty flavors. This dish is often served as a side with grilled meats or alongside hummus and pita.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup mint, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the bulgur wheat in a bowl and pour hot water over it. Let it sit for about 15 minutes, then drain and fluff with a fork.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the bulgur with parsley, mint, tomatoes, and cucumber.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss to combine, and serve chilled.

Tips:

  • Use fresh herbs to get the best flavor in your tabbouleh.
  • To make this salad gluten-free, you can substitute quinoa for the bulgur wheat.

3. Shawarma: Middle Eastern Street Food at Its Best

Shawarma is a popular street food in the Middle East, and it’s no surprise why. Marinated meat, typically lamb, chicken, or beef, is cooked on a vertical rotisserie and served with fresh veggies and a tangy sauce. If you don’t have a rotisserie, a grill or pan will work just as well for a delicious homemade shawarma.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (or your choice of meat)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pita bread, for serving
  • Sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions, for garnish
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce, for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the olive oil, cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander, garlic, cinnamon, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Coat the chicken with the marinade and let it sit for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  3. Heat a grill or a pan over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  4. Slice the chicken and serve in pita bread with vegetables and tahini sauce or garlic sauce.

Tips:

  • For the best flavor, marinate the chicken overnight.
  • Shawarma is often served with pickled vegetables to add an extra zing.

4. Falafel: A Crispy Chickpea Patty

Falafel is another beloved dish in Middle Eastern cuisine. These crispy, deep-fried chickpea patties are packed with herbs and spices, making them a delicious vegetarian option. Serve falafel in pita bread, as part of a salad, or as an appetizer with hummus.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup flour (or more, if needed)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas in water for 12 hours. Drain well.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Process until you have a coarse mixture.
  3. Add the baking powder and flour, and pulse until everything is mixed together.
  4. Form the mixture into small balls or patties, about the size of a golf ball.
  5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pan over medium-high heat. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Tips:

  • If the falafel mixture is too sticky, add a little more flour.
  • Falafel can be served with tahini sauce, yogurt, or a squeeze of lemon.

5. Kebabs: Grilled Meat Skewers

Middle Eastern kebabs are a favorite for a reason. Tender, juicy meat is marinated with spices, skewered, and grilled to perfection. Lamb, chicken, or beef kebabs are commonly enjoyed, often paired with flatbreads and dips like hummus or baba ganoush.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs lamb or chicken, cut into chunks
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, yogurt, garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the meat to the marinade and toss to coat. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  5. Grill the kebabs for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the meat is cooked through.
  6. Serve the kebabs with rice or pita and a side of tzatziki or garlic sauce.

Tips:

  • Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes before grilling to prevent them from burning.
  • You can also add vegetables like bell peppers and onions to the skewers for added flavor.

6. Baba Ganoush: Smoky Eggplant Dip

Baba ganoush is another Middle Eastern favorite, offering a smoky, creamy dip made from roasted eggplant. Its velvety texture and smoky flavor make it a great alternative to hummus and perfect for dipping pita bread or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Paprika, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prick the eggplants with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Roast for 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred, and the flesh is soft.
  3. Once roasted, allow the eggplants to cool. Scoop out the flesh and discard the skin.
  4. In a bowl, combine the eggplant flesh, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  5. Mash the mixture with a fork or blend it in a food processor until smooth.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil and garnish with paprika before serving.

Tips:

  • You can also grill the eggplants over an open flame for a more intense smoky flavor.
  • For extra richness, add a little yogurt to the dip.

7. Fattoush: A Crispy Bread Salad

Fattoush is a traditional Lebanese salad that combines fresh vegetables with crispy pieces of fried or toasted pita bread. It’s dressed with a tangy lemony vinaigrette and topped with sumac for a distinctive Middle Eastern flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pita breads
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sumac
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Tear the pita bread into small pieces and toast or fry them until crispy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cucumber, tomatoes, onion, parsley, and mint.
  3. Add the crispy pita pieces to the salad.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, sumac, salt, and pepper.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently before serving.

Tips:

  • To add more flavor, sprinkle extra sumac or za’atar on top.
  • Serve immediately after dressing to maintain the crispiness of the pita bread.

8. Mujadara: A Hearty Lentil and Rice Dish

Mujadara is a comforting Middle Eastern dish made from lentils and rice, typically garnished with crispy fried onions. It’s a simple, nutritious, and hearty meal that can be served as a main or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lentils in a large pot of water for about 20 minutes, until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a separate pot, cook the rice according to package instructions.
  3. While the rice and lentils are cooking, heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the onions until golden brown and crispy.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils and rice. Season with cumin, salt, and pepper.
  5. Top the mixture with the crispy fried onions and serve.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, add a pinch of cinnamon to the rice while cooking.
  • You can also garnish with fresh herbs, such as parsley.

9. Kibbeh: A Middle Eastern Meatball

Kibbeh is a traditional dish that consists of ground meat, bulgur wheat, onions, and spices, shaped into balls or patties and then deep-fried or baked. It’s a delicious and satisfying dish often served as an appetizer or main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the bulgur wheat in warm water for about 30 minutes, then drain well.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, soaked bulgur, onion, spices, salt, and pepper. Knead the mixture until it’s well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the kibbeh for 3-4 minutes per side, until golden brown.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Tips:

  • You can stuff the kibbeh with minced meat, pine nuts, and spices for added flavor.
  • Kibbeh can also be baked for a healthier version.

10. Baklava: A Sweet Pastry Delight

No Middle Eastern meal is complete without a sweet treat. Baklava is a rich, flaky pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, chopped nuts, and honey syrup. It’s a popular dessert that’s sure to impress anyone with its intricate layers and sweet flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (pistachios, walnuts, almonds)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Finely chop the mixed nuts and combine them with ground cinnamon.
  3. Brush a baking dish with melted butter and layer 8 sheets of phyllo dough, brushing each sheet with butter.
  4. Sprinkle a thin layer of the nut mixture on top and cover with 2 more sheets of phyllo dough. Repeat the layering process, ending with phyllo dough.
  5. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until golden brown and crispy.
  7. While baking, prepare the syrup by combining honey, water, and lemon juice in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  8. Pour the hot syrup over the baked baklava as soon as it comes out of the oven. Let it cool completely before serving.

Tips:

  • Ensure you don’t skip the butter between layers, as it’s essential for the flaky texture.
  • Baklava can be stored for up to a week in an airtight container.

These 10 authentic Middle Eastern recipes showcase the vibrant and diverse flavors of the region. From hearty stews to crisp salads and sweet pastries, these dishes will transport you to the heart of the Middle East. Whether you’re making a simple hummus or indulging in the layers of baklava, you’re sure to enjoy every bite. Happy cooking!
