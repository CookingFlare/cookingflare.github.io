How to Make a Hawaiian Pizza with Homemade Ingredients


Pizza is a favorite for many food lovers around the world, and Hawaiian pizza is one of the most unique variations. Combining the sweetness of pineapple with the savory flavors of ham and melted cheese, this recipe creates a sensational meal that can be easily made at home with fresh, homemade ingredients. Whether you’re a fan of traditional cuisine or adventurous flavor combinations, crafting this crowd-pleaser from scratch offers both flavor and fun. Let’s dive headfirst into the process of making Hawaiian pizza that’s restaurant-quality right in your own kitchen!

The Foundation: Making Homemade Pizza Dough

A great Hawaiian pizza starts with a solid base—its crust. Preparing homemade pizza dough allows you to control the flavor, texture, and quality of the foundation. Here’s what you’ll need for the dough:

Ingredients for Pizza Dough

  • 3 ½ cups of all-purpose flour (or bread flour for added chewiness)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) of active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups warm water (about 110°F/45°C)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions for Making Pizza Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast
    Mix the active dry yeast with warm water and sugar in a small bowl. Allow it to sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step ensures the yeast is alive and ready to work its magic.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a small well in the center.

  3. Combine Wet and Dry
    Pour the yeast mixture into the well, followed by the olive oil. Gradually mix until a shaggy dough forms.

  4. Knead the Dough
    Transfer the dough to a clean surface and begin kneading for 8-10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add small amounts of flour as necessary.

  5. Let it Rise
    Lightly oil a bowl, place the dough inside, and cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rise for 1-2 hours in a warm area, or until it doubles in size.

Pro Tip: For an ultra-chewy crust, refrigerate the dough overnight after its initial rise. Let it come to room temperature before using.

Crafting the Perfect Pizza Sauce

While store-bought pizza sauces are convenient, making your own at home ensures you capture fresh, bold flavors that complement the sweet and salty Hawaiian topping.

Ingredients for Pizza Sauce

  • 6 oz tomato paste
  • 15 oz canned or fresh tomato puree
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 pinch sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Instructions for Making Pizza Sauce

  1. Blend the Base
    In a saucepan over medium heat, combine tomato paste and tomato puree until smooth and well-mixed.

  2. Add Flavor
    Stir in minced garlic, oregano, basil, onion powder, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Taste and Adjust
    Taste your sauce and add a pinch of sugar if needed to balance the acidity of the tomatoes. Adjust the seasoning as desired.

Allow the sauce to cool slightly while you prepare the toppings and dough.

Preparing Fresh Hawaiian Toppings

The toppings are the stars of the show for Hawaiian pizza, so using fresh, quality ingredients is key to creating a flavor-packed pie.

Ingredients for Hawaiian Pizza Toppings

  • 1 ½ cups mozzarella cheese (shredded or fresh, thinly sliced)
  • ½ cup cooked ham, cubed or thinly sliced
  • ½ cup fresh pineapple chunks (if using canned, ensure they are well-drained)
  • Optional: Red onion slices, bacon bits, or chopped bell peppers for added flavor

Pro Tip: If fresh pineapple is unavailable, opt for canned pineapple in juice rather than syrup for a more natural taste.

Assembling Your Homemade Hawaiian Pizza

With all components prepared, it’s time to combine them into a masterpiece.

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you own a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats up. Alternatively, prepare a baking sheet by lightly greasing it with olive oil or lining it with parchment paper.

  2. Roll Out the Dough
    On a lightly floured surface, roll out your pizza dough to your desired thickness. For a thin crust, aim for a 12-inch diameter. Thick crust lovers can make a smaller circle to retain the doughy texture.

  3. Transfer to the Baking Surface
    Carefully transfer the rolled dough onto your pizza stone or prepared baking sheet.

  4. Spread Pizza Sauce
    Using the back of a spoon, spread an even layer of pizza sauce over the dough. Leave about ½ inch of space around the edges for the crust.

  5. Add Cheese and Toppings
    Sprinkle mozzarella cheese over the sauce. Distribute ham slices and pineapple chunks evenly across the cheese layer. If you’re incorporating optional ingredients, add them now.

  6. Bake the Pizza
    Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling.

  7. Cool and Serve
    Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a minute or two before slicing. Serve hot and enjoy the perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors.

Enjoying Your Hawaiian Pizza

With every bite of your homemade Hawaiian pizza, you’ll taste the difference that fresh, quality ingredients can make. The light, chewy crust, tangy tomato sauce, melty mozzarella, salty ham, and sweet pineapple combine to deliver a harmonious flavor explosion. This recipe is perfect for family dinners, casual gatherings, or even a weekday treat.

Experiment with variations by swapping ham for prosciutto, adding jalapeños for a spicy kick, or using smoked gouda in place of mozzarella for a unique twist. The beauty of homemade pizza lies in its versatility and creativity—there’s no wrong way to customize it to your taste.

By taking the time to make a Hawaiian pizza from scratch, you’ve mastered one of the most polarizing yet delicious dishes in the pizza world. Share your creation with friends and family, and let them marvel at your culinary skills. Once you try this recipe, store-bought pizzas will pale in comparison. Happy cooking!
