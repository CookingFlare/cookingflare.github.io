Pesto, Balsamic, and More: Unique Dressing Ideas for 101 Salad Recipes


Salads are an essential part of a balanced diet, and with countless combinations of vegetables, proteins, and dressings, they never go out of style. Often underestimated, salad dressings can be the little extra magic that takes your salad from bland to brilliant. If you’re tired of the same old vinaigrettes and creamy dressings, then this blog post is for you. Let’s dive into some unique dressing ideas using three classic components - pesto, balsamic, and more!

Pesto Explosion

Pesto, originating from Italy, is typically made with fresh basil, olive oil, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. With its vibrant green color and intense flavors, pesto can take your salads to new heights. Here are a couple of ideas to get you started:

  1. Zucchini Noodle Salad with Avocado and Pesto: Whole wheat penne with caramelized onions and mushrooms drenched in homemade basil pesto. Mix in some grilled chicken breast, cherry tomatoes, and freshly chopped red onions. Substitute the pasta with zucchini noodles for a healthier twist.

  2. Pesto Tuna Salad: Creamy pesto dressing mixed with canned tuna, chopped egg, carrot, celery, lemon juice, and crushed red pepper. Serve it over fresh spinach for a light yet satisfying meal.

Balsamic Delights

Balsamic vinegar, an aged treat from Modena, Italy, is loaded with complex flavors that pair beautifully with various salad components. Keep reading for some fantastic balsamic dressing ideas:

  1. Balsamic Pear Salad: Combine mixed greens, crumbled blue cheese, toasted pecans, and thinly sliced pears. Whisk together balsamic vinegar, honey, olive oil, and Dijon mustard for the dressing. Drizzle over the salad and finish with a sprinkle of cinnamon.

  2. Beef and Balsamic Two-Bean Salad: Slice up some grilled flank steak, and combine it with black beans, red kidney beans, diced red onions, chopped parsley, and crumbled feta. Mix together a tangy balsamic vinaigrette with Dijon mustard, honey, garlic, and red wine vinegar.

Bold and Creative Combinations

Sometimes, experimentation yields surprising results. Mixing different dressings, ingredients, and flavors can lead to a masterpiece. Let’s navigate through a few extraordinary combinations:

  1. Citrus Pesto Quinoa Bowl: Cook quinoa and let it cool. Toss the quinoa with mixed greens, mandarin oranges, cubed avocado, and thinly sliced red onions. Drizzle with a combination of fresh citrus dressing and store-bought pesto.

  2. Antipasto-Style Salad: Mix together baby spinach, cherry tomatoes, roasted red peppers, Kalamata olives, artichoke hearts, cucumber, and red onions. For the dressing, blend together Stonewall Kitchen’s basil pesto, balsamic vinegar, and a dash of honey.

  3. Greek-Inspired Pesto Bowl: Base your salad on a pyramid of Greek yogurt seasoned with fresh dill and garlic. Combine it with cooked and cooled potatoes, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and a hard-boiled egg. Drizzle with a spicy piquillo pepper and pesto sauce.

Creating Your Unique Dressings

Remember, it’s not necessary to stick to traditional recipes when concocting salad dressings. Use the three base ingredients - pesto, balsamic, or other - to create magical combinations. Experiment with different flavors like tahini, peanuts, or goji berries and don’t be afraid to venture into uncharted territory.

With these ideas, you’re ready to create exceptional salad recipes with 101 unique dressings. Cheers to culinary exploration and the joy of experimenting in the kitchen!

Happy cooking!
