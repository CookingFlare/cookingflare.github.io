10 Easter Dessert Recipes That Will Make You Smile


Easter is a time of celebration, renewal, and, of course, indulgence in delightful sweets. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering, planning a brunch, or simply want a sweet treat that embodies the spirit of spring, these ten Easter dessert recipes are guaranteed to bring smiles all around. Each recipe combines seasonal flavors, festive presentation, and just the right amount of sweetness. Let’s dive into these creations that blend tradition with a fresh twist.

1. Classic Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is synonymous with Easter — the perfect way to bring the flavors of the season right to your dessert table. This recipe balances the moistness of shredded carrots with warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, topped with a luscious cream cheese frosting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • ½ cup shredded coconut (optional)

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preparation:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a medium bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.

  3. In a large bowl, beat eggs and sugar until pale and fluffy. Add in the oil and mix well.

  4. Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Fold in the grated carrots, pineapple, walnuts, and coconut if using.

  5. Divide the batter evenly between the pans and bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  6. Cool completely before frosting.

  7. For the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla, beating until light and creamy.

  8. Frost the cooled cakes and sprinkle some extra walnuts or carrot decorations for a festive look.

2. Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

Bright, tangy lemon paired with juicy blueberries creates a refreshing dessert perfect for Easter brunch or dessert after dinner. These cheesecake bars have a buttery graham cracker crust topped with creamy lemon-infused cheesecake dotted with fresh blueberries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • ¼ cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest
  • 2 cups fresh blueberries

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper.

  2. Combine graham cracker crumbs, ⅓ cup sugar, and melted butter. Press firmly into the pan’s bottom.

  3. Beat cream cheese and 1 cup sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.

  4. Stir in lemon juice and zest.

  5. Pour filling over crust and gently fold in blueberries.

  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the center is set but still slightly jiggly.

  7. Cool completely in the fridge before slicing into bars.

3. Bunny-Shaped Sugar Cookies with Royal Icing

Decorating Easter-themed sugar cookies is an activity that’s both fun and rewarding. The bunny shapes paired with colorful royal icing make these cookies a stunning centerpiece for any dessert table or a sweet gift.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional)

Royal Icing:

  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons meringue powder
  • 9-10 tablespoons warm water
  • Food coloring as desired

Preparation:

  1. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt.

  2. In a stand mixer, cream butter and sugar until fluffy. Add egg, vanilla, and almond extract.

  3. Gradually add flour mixture until combined.

  4. Divide dough, wrap, and chill for at least an hour.

  5. Roll out dough on a floured surface, cut into bunny shapes, and place on baking sheets.

  6. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes. Let cool.

  7. Prepare royal icing by beating powdered sugar, meringue powder, and water until stiff peaks form.

  8. Divide icing, add food coloring, and decorate cookies as desired.

4. Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp

Celebrate spring’s fresh flavors with a warm, cozy strawberry rhubarb crisp. The tartness of rhubarb balances the sweetness of strawberries, topped with a crumbly oat topping that delivers both texture and aroma.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 3 cups rhubarb, chopped
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Topping:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup flour
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, toss strawberries, rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla. Transfer to baking dish.

  3. In another bowl, mix oats, flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or fingers until crumbly.

  4. Spread topping evenly over fruit.

  5. Bake 40-45 minutes until bubbling and golden.

  6. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.

5. Chocolate Nest Cupcakes

These cupcakes bring a playful and festive touch to Easter celebrations, resembling bird nests full of chocolate eggs. Moist chocolate cupcakes topped with rich chocolate ganache and finished with edible “eggs” make this dessert both fun and delectable.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ cup boiling water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Ganache:

  • 8 oz semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • ½ cup heavy cream

Decoration:

  • Shredded coconut (toasted)
  • Chocolate candy eggs or jelly beans

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line cupcake pans.

  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  3. Add egg, milk, oil, and vanilla. Mix well.

  4. Stir in boiling water carefully — batter will be thin.

  5. Fill cupcake liners ⅔ full and bake 20-22 minutes.

  6. For ganache, heat cream until simmering, pour over chocolate, and stir until smooth.

  7. Cool cupcakes completely, spread ganache, then press toasted coconut into a nest shape.

  8. Place candy eggs on top to complete the nests.

6. Mini Pavlovas with Passionfruit and Kiwi

Pavlova is a light and airy meringue dessert that feels like a taste of spring itself. These mini pavlovas topped with tropical passionfruit pulp and fresh kiwi slices provide a refreshing contrast to heavier desserts.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup superfine sugar
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch

Topping:

  • 1 cup heavy cream, whipped
  • 4 passionfruits, pulp scooped out
  • 2 kiwis, peeled and sliced
  • Fresh mint leaves (optional)

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.

  2. Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form, gradually add sugar and beat until glossy.

  3. Fold in vinegar and cornstarch gently.

  4. Spoon meringue into six even nests on parchment, creating a slight well in the center.

  5. Bake 50-60 minutes; turn oven off and leave meringues inside to cool.

  6. Once cool, fill nests with whipped cream, passionfruit pulp, and kiwi slices.

  7. Garnish with mint if desired.

7. Almond Joy Easter Bark

Channel the iconic candy bar into a simple bark perfect for Easter gifting or snacking. Layers of milk chocolate, coconut, and almonds create a satisfying treat decorated with pastel candy-coated chocolates for an Easter twist.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz milk chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded sweetened coconut
  • ½ cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • ½ cup pastel candy-coated chocolates

Preparation:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Melt chocolate in a double boiler or microwave in intervals until smooth.

  3. Stir in shredded coconut and most of the almonds, reserving a few.

  4. Spread mixture in a thin layer on prepared sheet.

  5. Sprinkle remaining almonds and candy-coated chocolates on top, pressing lightly.

  6. Chill in the fridge until set, then break into pieces.

8. Creamy Coconut Panna Cotta with Mango Puree

This panna cotta recipe offers a rich, silky dessert that feels light thanks to fresh mango puree topping. Perfect to prepare in advance, it adds an elegant finish to any Easter feast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk (full fat)
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons gelatin powder
  • 3 tablespoons cold water

Mango Puree:

  • 2 ripe mangos, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Preparation:

  1. Sprinkle gelatin over cold water and let bloom for 5 minutes.

  2. Heat coconut milk, cream, and sugar until sugar dissolves, but do not boil.

  3. Remove from heat and stir in gelatin until fully dissolved.

  4. Pour into molds or serving glasses and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.

  5. Blend mango, lime juice, and honey until smooth.

  6. Spoon mango puree over panna cotta before serving.

9. Pistachio and Rosewater Macarons

Add a sophisticated edge to your Easter sweets with these delicate macarons flavored with pistachio and a hint of floral rosewater. They’re a perfect bite-sized treat for those who appreciate elegant desserts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 3 tablespoons ground pistachios
  • 3 large egg whites
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon rosewater
  • Green food coloring (optional)

Filling:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup pistachio paste
  • 1 teaspoon rosewater

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.

  2. Sift powdered sugar, almond flour, and ground pistachios into a bowl.

  3. Beat egg whites to soft peaks, gradually add granulated sugar until stiff and glossy peaks form.

  4. Fold dry ingredients and rosewater carefully into egg whites. Add food coloring if desired.

  5. Pipe small rounds onto baking sheets; rest 30 minutes.

  6. Bake 15-17 minutes. Cool completely.

  7. Beat butter, powdered sugar, pistachio paste, and rosewater until creamy.

  8. Sandwich the shells with filling.

10. Apricot and Almond Tart

This tart offers a stunning display of halved apricots nestled into an almond cream filling inside a crumbly pastry crust. The balance of sweet fruit and nutty richness makes it a standout centerpiece dessert.

Ingredients:

  • Pastry crust:

    • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
    • ⅓ cup powdered sugar
    • ½ cup cold butter, cubed
    • 1 egg yolk

  • Almond Cream:

    • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
    • ½ cup powdered sugar
    • 1 cup almond meal
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • 6-8 ripe apricots, halved and pitted

  • Almond slices for garnish

  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Preparation:

  1. Make crust by mixing flour and powdered sugar. Cut in butter until pea-sized crumbs form.

  2. Add egg yolk and knead lightly. Chill 30 minutes.

  3. Roll dough and fit into tart pan. Chill again.

  4. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread almond cream evenly over crust.

  5. Arrange apricot halves cut side up on almond cream.

  6. Bake 40-45 minutes until golden.

  7. Cool, garnish with almond slices and powdered sugar.

Wrapping Up Your Easter Dessert Experience

Each of these 10 recipes brings something special to the Easter table—from vibrant colors and fresh spring flavors to whimsical shapes and nostalgic favorites. Whip up a carrot cake or dive into the crisp sweetness of strawberry rhubarb. Whether you prefer chocolate, fruit, or a creamy indulgence, these desserts make celebrating the season extra memorable. Baking any of these shows off both your skills and your festive spirit, guaranteeing smiles from the first bite to the last crumb. Enjoy your Easter feast!
