How to Make a Rustic Country Loaf of Bread


There’s something uniquely satisfying about making a rustic country loaf of bread. With its hearty, crunchy crust and soft, airy crumb, this bread brings the feeling of comfort and tradition to every meal. Whether you’re new to baking or an experienced home baker, making a rustic country loaf is a great way to challenge yourself and create something truly special. In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making this classic bread from scratch, step by step, so you can enjoy the smell of freshly baked bread right from your oven.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the process, let’s gather the essential ingredients for a rustic country loaf. These ingredients are simple yet crucial for creating that perfect balance of flavors and textures.

  • 500g (4 cups) of strong white bread flour: Bread flour has a higher protein content than all-purpose flour, which helps the dough rise and results in a better texture.
  • 300ml (1 ¼ cups) of warm water: The water should be warm, but not too hot. If the water is too hot, it could kill the yeast.
  • 10g (1 tablespoon) of salt: Salt enhances the flavor of the bread and regulates the fermentation process.
  • 7g (1 packet) of active dry yeast: This is the key ingredient to help the dough rise. You can use instant yeast if you prefer, but active dry yeast works wonderfully for this recipe.
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil: Olive oil adds richness to the dough, helping it stay moist and soft. You can substitute this with other vegetable oils, but olive oil gives a lovely flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar: This small amount of sugar feeds the yeast, helping it activate and work its magic.
  • Optional: A handful of flour for dusting: This will be used for shaping and dusting the bread before baking.

Additional Tools

In addition to your ingredients, there are a few essential tools you’ll need for making your rustic country loaf of bread:

  • A large mixing bowl
  • A wooden spoon or a dough scraper
  • A bench scraper or sharp knife
  • A baking sheet or Dutch oven
  • A clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap
  • A thermometer (optional but helpful for checking the dough temperature)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Rustic Country Bread

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

To begin, you’ll need to activate your yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water with the sugar. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves. Then, sprinkle the yeast over the water. Let it sit for about 5 to 10 minutes, or until the yeast becomes frothy and bubbly. This step is crucial because it ensures that the yeast is alive and ready to help the dough rise. If the yeast doesn’t bubble, it may be expired or the water might have been too hot.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour and salt. Stir them together so that the salt is evenly distributed throughout the flour. It’s important to mix the salt with the flour first because adding it directly to the yeast mixture could hinder the yeast’s activation. You want the yeast to work without interference from the salt.

Step 3: Form the Dough

Once the yeast mixture is frothy, pour it into the dry ingredients, along with the olive oil. Stir everything together with a wooden spoon or a dough scraper until a shaggy dough starts to form. At this point, the dough will be quite sticky, and that’s perfectly fine. You want it to be tacky but not too wet.

Step 4: Knead the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a clean, lightly floured surface. Begin kneading the dough by folding it in half, pressing it down with the heel of your hand, and then turning it a quarter turn. Continue this process for about 10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If you’re unsure, perform the “windowpane test”: Take a small piece of dough and stretch it gently. If it forms a thin, translucent sheet without tearing, you’ve kneaded the dough enough.

If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle a little flour, but avoid adding too much. You want the dough to stay slightly tacky, which helps achieve the characteristic open crumb in the bread.

Step 5: First Rise

Once the dough is kneaded, place it into a clean bowl and cover it with a damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Leave it in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 to 1.5 hours or until it has doubled in size. The rise time will depend on the ambient temperature, so if your kitchen is cooler, it may take a little longer. If you have a proofing drawer or warm oven, that’s a great place to let the dough rise.

Step 6: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has doubled in size, gently punch it down to release the air bubbles. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Shape it into a round or oval loaf, depending on your preference. To shape, fold the dough’s edges toward the center and turn it over, sealing the seams underneath. Use your hands to gently round it into a smooth ball or elongated shape.

Step 7: Second Rise

Place the shaped dough onto a piece of parchment paper, dust it lightly with flour, and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise for about 30 to 45 minutes, or until it puffs up slightly. During this time, you can prepare your oven for baking.

Step 8: Preheat Your Oven

Preheat your oven to 220°C (430°F). If you have a baking stone, place it on the middle rack of the oven to heat up as well. A baking stone helps the bread bake evenly and gives it that extra-crisp crust. If you don’t have one, don’t worry! You can bake your loaf on a regular baking sheet.

Step 9: Score the Dough

Before baking, it’s important to score the dough. Use a sharp knife or a bread lame to make a few shallow slashes on the top of the loaf. This helps the bread expand while baking, and it creates a beautiful rustic appearance. The slashes also allow steam to escape, which contributes to the perfect crust.

Step 10: Bake the Bread

Place your dough (with the parchment paper) directly onto the hot baking stone or a preheated baking sheet. Bake for about 30 to 35 minutes, or until the bread has a deep golden-brown color and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. If you have a thermometer, the internal temperature should reach around 90°C (190°F).

If you want an extra-crusty exterior, place a small dish of water at the bottom of the oven during baking to create steam. This will help the crust develop that signature crunchy texture.

Step 11: Cooling

Once your rustic country loaf is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and place it on a wire rack to cool. Let the bread cool for at least 30 minutes before slicing. This cooling time allows the bread’s flavor to fully develop and makes it easier to cut without squashing the crumb.

Tips for Perfecting Your Rustic Country Loaf

  1. Flour Variations: While bread flour is ideal for this recipe, you can experiment with different types of flour, such as whole wheat or spelt, for a different flavor and texture. Keep in mind that these flours absorb more water, so you may need to adjust the amount of liquid.

  2. Autolyse Method: If you have time, you can try the autolyse method. Mix the flour and water, and let it rest for 30 minutes before adding the yeast and salt. This allows the flour to hydrate more fully and helps develop gluten more easily.

  3. Sourdough Variation: If you prefer a sourdough flavor, you can substitute a portion of the yeast with a sourdough starter. This will add a more complex, tangy flavor to the bread. However, keep in mind that the rise time will be longer with sourdough.

  4. Let the Dough Rest Longer: If you want an even more flavorful bread, you can let the dough rise more slowly in the fridge for a longer fermentation process (around 12 to 18 hours). This slow fermentation allows the dough to develop deeper flavors.

  5. Steam in the Oven: To enhance the crust, you can create steam in your oven by placing a pan of water in the bottom of the oven or by spritzing the dough with water before baking.

Conclusion

Making a rustic country loaf of bread at home is a rewarding process that’s both simple and full of flavor. With just a few basic ingredients, you can create a loaf that’s perfect for sandwiches, toast, or served alongside your favorite meals. Remember, the key to a perfect rustic loaf is patience and practice. Don’t be discouraged if your first loaf isn’t perfect – each time you bake, you’ll learn something new. The smell of fresh bread wafting through your kitchen is worth every minute. Enjoy the process, and most importantly, enjoy the bread!
