Simple and Tasty Eggplant Parmesan: Family Dinner Recipe


Are you looking for a delicious and hearty meal that the whole family will love? Look no further than this simple and tasty Eggplant Parmesan recipe! It’s a classic Italian dish that’s perfect for a cozy family dinner or for entertaining guests. The crispy breaded eggplant slices, layered with rich tomato sauce and gooey melted cheese, create a flavor explosion that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

Step 1: Preparing the Eggplant

  1. Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the eggplant slices in a colander and sprinkle them with salt. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out any bitterness. Then, rinse the slices and pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. Set up three shallow dishes for breading the eggplant: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with a mixture of breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Dredge each eggplant slice in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it in the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off before coating it in the breadcrumb mixture. Make sure to press the breadcrumbs onto the eggplant to help them adhere.

Step 2: Frying the Eggplant

  1. In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Working in batches, fry the breaded eggplant slices for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Transfer the fried slices to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 3: Assembling the Eggplant Parmesan

  1. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices over the sauce, slightly overlapping them.
  3. Spoon more marinara sauce over the eggplant, then sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
  4. Repeat the layers until all the eggplant slices are used, finishing with a final layer of marinara sauce and mozzarella cheese.

Step 4: Baking the Eggplant Parmesan

  1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  2. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  3. Let the Eggplant Parmesan rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and added flavor.

Serving Suggestions:

This Eggplant Parmesan pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with balsamic vinaigrette and a crusty loaf of garlic bread. It’s also a great dish to serve alongside pasta for a more substantial meal. For a complete Italian dinner experience, consider adding a bottle of your favorite red wine to the table.

Final Thoughts:

Eggplant Parmesan is a fantastic way to introduce more vegetables into your family’s diet without sacrificing flavor. The crispy texture of the eggplant, combined with the savory marinara sauce and creamy melted cheese, makes this dish a winner for both kids and adults. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting out, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite that you’ll turn to again and again. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a simple and tasty Eggplant Parmesan that will bring everyone together around the dinner table!
