Blueberry Pancakes with Maple Syrup: A Breakfast-Inspired Dessert


Who says pancakes are just for breakfast? Today, we’re going to shake things up and turn this classic morning dish into a delightful dessert that will have everyone asking for seconds. Blueberry pancakes with maple syrup are a match made in heaven, and when transformed into a dessert, they become even more irresistible. So, let’s dive into this unique and delicious recipe that will elevate your dessert game to a whole new level.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • Maple syrup, for serving
  • Whipped cream, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, combine the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix - a few lumps are okay. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries.

  2. Heat the Griddle: Preheat a non-stick griddle or skillet over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with butter or cooking spray.

  3. Cook the Pancakes: Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake and the edges begin to look set, then flip and cook until golden brown on the other side. Repeat with the remaining batter.

  4. Assemble the Dessert: Once all the pancakes are cooked, it’s time to assemble the dessert. Stack the pancakes on a serving plate, drizzle generously with maple syrup, and top with a dollop of whipped cream if desired.

Tips for Success

  • For extra flavor, consider adding a pinch of cinnamon or lemon zest to the pancake batter.
  • If you don’t have fresh blueberries, frozen blueberries can be used as well. Just be sure to thaw and drain them before adding to the batter.
  • To keep the pancakes warm while you finish cooking the batch, place them on a baking sheet in a 200°F oven.

Serving Suggestions

Blueberry pancakes with maple syrup make a delightful dessert on their own, but if you’re feeling extra indulgent, you can pair them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra special treat. The combination of warm, fluffy pancakes, sweet blueberries, and rich maple syrup is a surefire way to satisfy your sweet tooth.

So, the next time you’re craving something sweet and comforting, consider whipping up a batch of these blueberry pancakes with maple syrup. Whether you serve them for dessert or as a unique twist on breakfast, they are guaranteed to be a hit with family and friends alike.

There you have it - a simple yet indulgent dessert that will have everyone reaching for seconds. Enjoy the sweet and satisfying flavors of blueberry pancakes with maple syrup, and get ready to make this delightful dish a new favorite in your recipe collection.
