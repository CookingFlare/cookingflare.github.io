The Best Wines to Complement Your Favorite Curry Recipes


When it comes to pairing wine with food, the first things that often come to mind might be steak and a full-bodied Cabernet or seafood with a crisp Sauvignon Blanc. But what about the bold, intricate flavors of curries? Rich in spices, vibrant in color, and brimming with depth, curry dishes can transform any meal into an unforgettable experience. Yet, when it comes to enjoying curry alongside a good glass of wine, the challenge can feel daunting. With their myriad spices, heat levels, and sauces, curries might seem incompatible with wine. However, the truth is that the right bottle of wine can beautifully enhance your favorite curry recipe, elevating the meal to extraordinary heights.

In this guide, we’ll explore the art of pairing wines with curries, considering different curry styles and flavors, as well as the types of wine that work best with each. Whether you’re savoring a creamy chicken tikka masala, a fiery Thai green curry, or a delicate Japanese katsu curry, there’s a wine out there to complement it perfectly. Let’s dive in and make your next curry night something spectacular.

Understanding the Complexity of Curry Dishes

Curries are wonderfully diverse, varying significantly between cuisines, regions, and personal recipes. Indian, Thai, Japanese, Malaysian, and Caribbean curries all bring their own unique styles to the table—combining spices, herbs, and protein in distinct ways. As a result, no two curries taste the same, which is why understanding the ingredients in your chosen curry is essential before selecting a wine to pair with it.

Key elements to consider include the spice profile, richness, acidity, and sweetness of the dish.

1. Spice Levels

The heat in a curry can range from mild and creamy to fiery and intense. Wines with high alcohol content can amplify the burn of spicy dishes, so it’s often better to opt for wines with lower alcohol levels and a mild sweetness to offset the heat.

2. Richness and Creaminess

Dishes like butter chicken or korma feature creamy, velvety sauces made with yogurt, cream, or coconut milk. These richer curries pair well with wines that cut through their heaviness without overpowering their flavors.

3. Acidity

Acidity in certain curries, especially those made with tomatoes, tamarind, or lime juice, makes a vibrant pairing with crisp, acidic wines that match the refreshing tang.

4. Sweetness

Sweet curries, such as a Thai mango curry or a Caribbean-style coconut curry, often call for wines with a touch of residual sugar to harmonize with the natural sweetness of the dish.

Wine Pairing Basics for Curry Lovers

When thinking about wine pairings for curry, balance is the key. The goal is to ensure that neither the wine nor the curry overwhelms the other. Let’s explore some wine varietals that tend to make excellent partners for curries across cuisines.

1. Riesling – A Curry All-Rounder

Riesling often tops the list as a go-to wine for curry due to its versatility. With its crisp acidity and varying levels of sweetness, Riesling can manage a wide spectrum of heat and spice. For spicier curries like a Thai red curry or Indian vindaloo, an off-dry or semi-sweet Riesling offers just the right amount of sweetness to counterbalance the heat. Meanwhile, a dry Riesling works beautifully with lighter, herbaceous options like a Thai green curry or a Vietnamese lemongrass curry.

2. Gewürztraminer – The Aromatic Firefighter

Known for its floral, lychee-like aromas and slight sweetness, Gewürztraminer is a dream match for many curries, especially those with bold spices or strong coconut bases. Its natural sweetness can tame even the spiciest dishes, such as a fiery Goan fish curry or a Malaysian laksa. Gewürztraminer’s tropical fruit notes also make it a great partner for sweet and tangy curries.

3. Sauvignon Blanc – A Citrus-Lover’s Choice

For curries with a zesty, tangy flavor profile, such as Thai green curry or Goan prawn curry, Sauvignon Blanc is an excellent choice. The wine’s refreshing acidity and citrusy notes beautifully mirror the lime and lemongrass commonly found in such dishes. A New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc, with its bold herbaceous quality, can be a particularly exciting match for green curries.

4. Rosé – The Versatile Companion

When in doubt, a dry or off-dry rosé is a safe and versatile option for pairing with curry. Its light body and subtle fruitiness make it compatible with a variety of dishes, from creamy kormas to lighter lentil-based curries like dal makhani. A Provençal rosé, with its delicate minerality, can also serve as a brilliant palate cleanser between bites of spicier dishes.

5. Pinot Noir – For the Tomato-Lovers

For curries with a tomato-heavy base, like chicken tikka masala or lamb rogan josh, a light to medium-bodied Pinot Noir works surprisingly well. The wine’s red berry notes and soft tannins complement the acidity of the tomato-based sauces, creating a harmonious pairing. Opt for a cooler-climate Pinot Noir with bright fruit flavors and a touch of earthiness to balance the spices.

6. Sparkling Wine – For a Festive Touch

Delicate sparkling wines, like Prosecco or a dry Champagne, can pair wonderfully with milder, creamy curries. Their crisp bubbles act as a palate cleanser, cutting through the richness of the sauce while adding a celebratory element to your meal. For spicier curries, a slightly sweeter sparkling wine like Moscato d’Asti can work wonders.

Pairing by Curry Type

If you’d rather focus on specific dishes, here are some classic pairings for common curry recipes from around the globe:

Butter Chicken

  • Wine Pairing: Chardonnay (unoaked), Chenin Blanc, or a dry Riesling.
  • Why: The creaminess of butter chicken requires a wine with enough acidity to cut through the richness without overwhelming the dish’s mild spices.

Thai Green Curry

  • Wine Pairing: Sauvignon Blanc, Grüner Veltliner, or off-dry Riesling.
  • Why: The herbal and zesty elements of Thai green curry shine bright when matched with a wine that echoes its crisp and citrusy notes.

Chicken Tikka Masala

  • Wine Pairing: Pinot Noir, Sangiovese, or a medium-bodied rosé.
  • Why: The tomato-heavy base of tikka masala pairs well with lighter red wines or rosés that can handle its acidity and spice.

Lamb Rogan Josh

  • Wine Pairing: Syrah/Shiraz or Grenache.
  • Why: The richness and depth of the lamb are perfectly balanced by a bold red wine with a hint of spice.

Coconut Shrimp Curry

  • Wine Pairing: Gewürztraminer, Viognier, or Moscato.
  • Why: The sweetness and tropical nature of Gewürztraminer and Moscato echo the coconut and balance the mild spice.

Vegetarian Dal Curry

  • Wine Pairing: Sauvignon Blanc, Albariño, or Chenin Blanc.
  • Why: The freshness of these wines complements the subtle, earthy flavors of lentils.

Final Tips for Pairing Wine with Curry

While this guide provides a variety of recommendations, the art of pairing wine with curry ultimately comes down to personal taste. Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind:

  1. Consider the Spice Tolerance: For dishes with intense heat, opt for wines with sweetness or lower alcohol to soften the spice.

  2. Experiment: Don’t be afraid to try unconventional pairings! You might discover a surprising combination that delights your taste buds.

  3. Serve Wines Chilled: Heat and spice can overwhelm the palate, so serving your wine slightly cooler than usual helps keep the flavors balanced and refreshing.

Pairing wine with curry may seem intimidating, but it’s an adventure worth embarking on. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party centered around mouthwatering curries or indulging in a cozy meal at home, the right wine adds an unforgettable dimension to the experience. So, the next time you spoon your favorite curry onto a plate, don’t shy away from cracking open a bottle. You just might find your new favorite pairing.
