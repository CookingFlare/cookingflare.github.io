Spanish Pasta Recipes: 7 Dishes with a Twist


When you think of Spanish cuisine, the mind often wanders to paella, tapas, or rich stews like cocido. But pasta? Though not traditionally associated with Spanish cooking, pasta dishes here reveal a vibrant fusion, blending Italy’s beloved noodles with Spain’s bold, passionate flavors. In this post, we explore seven Spanish pasta recipes that bring a twist to the classic noodle bowl. These creative takes offer the best of both worlds—comforting and familiar textures with bright, aromatic, and soulful Spanish ingredients. Whether you crave smoky chorizo, earthy saffron, or the deep umami of jamón, your taste buds are in for a treat.

1. Chorizo and Roasted Red Pepper Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz penne pasta
  • 6 oz Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 2 large roasted red peppers, sliced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 handful fresh parsley, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

The Twist:

Spanish chorizo brings a smoky and slightly spicy punch that transforms an ordinary creamy pasta into a robust, full-bodied dish. Roasted red peppers add a slight sweetness balancing the heat and richness.

Instructions:

  1. Cook penne in salted boiling water until al dente, drain and set aside, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet, sauté onion until translucent, then add garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Add chorizo slices and pan-fry until just crisp, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Pour in the white wine to deglaze, scraping the bottom of the pan, and cook until reduced by half.
  5. Stir in roasted red peppers, then add cream and simmer 2-3 minutes until sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Toss in cooked penne, mixing to coat well. Add pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper, sprinkle with parsley, and serve warm.

This dish exemplifies the melding of spicy, creamy, and sweet Spanish flavors with hearty pasta texture.

2. Saffron-Infused Seafood Linguine

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz linguine
  • 1 tbsp saffron threads
  • 1 lb mixed seafood (shrimp, mussels, calamari)
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 cup fish or seafood stock
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper

A Flavorful Departure:

Saffron threads braided into the pasta give the dish both a golden hue and an unmistakably warm, floral fragrance that instantly evokes the essence of Spain’s maritime regions.

Instructions:

  1. Soak saffron in 1/4 cup warm water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Cook linguine in salted boiling water until just shy of al dente. Drain and keep warm.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and sauté onion and garlic until aromatic.
  4. Add seafood, cook until opaque (shrimp and calamari) and shells of mussels begin to open.
  5. Pour in wine, allow to reduce for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add saffron water and seafood stock, simmer gently until mussels fully open and flavors meld.
  7. Toss linguine into the skillet, coating with the saffron seafood sauce.
  8. Season with salt and pepper, garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges before serving.

This Spanish seafood pasta shines in its simplicity, with the luxurious touch of saffron elevating the dish.

3. Jamón Ibérico and Manchego Macaroni

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz elbow macaroni
  • 4 oz thinly sliced Jamón Ibérico, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Manchego cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 ½ cups milk
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh thyme (optional)

What Makes it Special:

Jamón Ibérico introduces an intense nutty and savory depth to a creamy macaroni and cheese, enhanced with smoked paprika for a characteristic Spanish warmth.

Method:

  1. Cook macaroni until al dente, drain and set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in flour and cook 2 minutes to form a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in milk, keeping the mixture smooth and thickening for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add smoked paprika, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  5. Remove from heat, stir in Manchego cheese until melted and velvety.
  6. Fold in chopped Jamón Ibérico and then the cooked macaroni.
  7. For a crisp top, you may bake it under a broiler for 3-5 minutes until golden and bubbling.
  8. Sprinkle fresh thyme for a herbal twist before serving.

This rich, indulgent plate is a Spanish spin on a universal comfort classic.

4. Garlic Shrimp with Angel Hair and Pimentón

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz angel hair pasta
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp pimentón (Spanish smoked paprika)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Salt and pepper

A Bold and Simple Combo:

Pimentón adds a smoky depth to the garlic shrimp that is both fiery and soulful, paired beautifully with the delicate angel hair pasta that catches every bit of flavor.

Steps:

  1. Cook angel hair until al dente, drain and toss with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet, sauté garlic and pimentón for 1 minute.
  3. Add shrimp, season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
  4. Cook shrimp quickly until pink, about 2 minutes per side.
  5. Mix in lemon juice, toss in pasta to combine thoroughly.
  6. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

A quick and vibrant meal that bursts with Spanish character in every bite.

5. Tomato and Olive Tagliatelle Andaluz

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz tagliatelle
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 cup black Spanish olives, pitted and sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar
  • Fresh basil and oregano
  • Salt and pepper

Southern Spanish Soul in Pasta:

Influenced by Andalusian flavors, this vegetarian-friendly pasta incorporates flaky tomatoes and briny olives balanced with a tangy splash of sherry vinegar.

Preparation:

  1. Boil tagliatelle until al dente, keeping some pasta water aside before draining.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan, sauté onion and garlic until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook gently for 10 minutes, creating a rustic sauce.
  4. Stir in olives, fresh herbs, and sherry vinegar. Season to taste.
  5. Toss with cooked pasta, adding reserved water if sauce needs loosening.

The combination evokes sun-drenched southern Spain’s simple, vibrant culinary essence.

6. Spinach and Pine Nut Fettuccine with Romesco Drizzle

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz fettuccine
  • 3 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup toasted pine nuts
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 batch homemade or store-bought Romesco sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Salt and pepper

Why This One Stands Out:

The nutty pine nuts and fresh spinach deliver earthiness while the Romesco sauce—a classic Catalan sauce made with roasted red peppers, almonds, garlic, and tomatoes—infuses smoky-sweet, vibrant flavors onto silky pasta ribbons.

Instructions:

  1. Cook fettuccine to al dente, drain.
  2. Sauté spinach quickly in olive oil with garlic until just wilted.
  3. Toss pasta with spinach and pine nuts.
  4. Plate and generously drizzle Romesco sauce atop.

This dish is a vegetable-forward ode to Catalonia’s rich flavors with a sophisticated twist.

7. Pulled Pork and Green Olive Pasta Bake

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz rigatoni
  • 2 cups cooked pulled pork (preferably slow-roasted with smoked spices)
  • 1 cup green Spanish olives, sliced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh oregano
  • Salt and pepper

An Unexpected Spanish-Italian Mashup:

Bringing tender pulled pork with briny, tart green olives gives this baked rigatoni dish a game-changing Spanish flavor alongside comforting melted cheese.

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook rigatoni until al dente. Drain.
  3. In a skillet, sauté garlic in olive oil, add pulled pork and green olives, warming through.
  4. Stir in tomato sauce and oregano; season.
  5. Combine pasta with pork mixture, spoon into a baking dish.
  6. Top with mozzarella and Parmesan.
  7. Bake 20 minutes until cheese is golden and bubbly.

With smoky pulled pork and the acidity of green olives, each baked bite captures rustic, hearty Spain.

Wrapping Up

Pasta may not evoke immediate Spanish culinary images, but these seven recipes showcase how Spanish ingredients—chorizo, saffron, jamón, pimentón, olives, Romesco, and more—can creatively transform Italian pasta into distinctively Spanish dishes. Whether it’s the creamy Jamón Ibérico mac and cheese or the saffron-laced seafood linguine, these dishes invite exploration beyond borders.

By experimenting with local Spanish flavors and embracing fresh, bold ingredients, pasta becomes a versatile canvas for rich storytelling on your plate—a delicious journey through Spain’s diverse food landscape flavored with tradition and innovation.

Try these recipes at home and find your own twist on Spanish pasta. ¡Buen provecho!
