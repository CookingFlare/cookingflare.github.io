Classic Italian Dishes for a Romantic Valentine's Evening


As the clock strikes 5 o'clock on a chilly winter evening, the perfect dinner setup for a romantic Valentine's evening begins to take shape. The thought of a candlelit dinner at home, complete with a homemade Italian feast, is sure to send shivers down the spine of your loved one. But, worry not, for the selection of Italian dishes I have prepared for you will not only transport your taste buds to the Tuscan countryside but also guarantee a memorable evening with your special someone. Bruchetta with Creamy Tomato-'Shrooms Start off with an amuse-bouche, so to speak. Bruschetta, the classic Italian appetizer, gets an Italian twist with the introduction of 'shrooms - as in shiitake. The textures and the flavors will not only intrigue but also appease even the pickiest of eaters. Ingredients:

1 baguette, sliced 1 pint shiitake mushroom 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tbsp olive oil 1/4 cup creamy tomato sauce Salt, pepper, and parsley for garnish

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Arrange the sliced baguette on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and bake for 5 minutes or until golden. In a pan, sauté garlic and 'shrooms in olive oil until softened. Remove the crusty bread from the oven and top with the mushroom and garlic mixture, followed by a spoonful of creamy tomato sauce. Garnish with parsley and serve immediately.

Creamy Lasagna with Mozzarella Lasagna, the quintessential Italian comfort food, will be reimagined in a rich, creamy form, guaranteed to reinforce your loved one's feelings for you. Ingredients:

8 sheets lasagna 2 cups ricotta cheese 2 cups shredded mozzarella 1 cup grated Parmesan 1/4 cup chopped parsley 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil Salt and pepper for taste

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread a layer of marinara sauce at the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Arrange 4 lasagna sheets on top of the sauce. In a separate bowl, combine ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan, parsley, and basil. Spread the ricotta mixture on top of the lasagna. Drizzle with marinara and spread another layer of mozzarella. Repeat the layers and finish with a layer of mozzarella. Bake in the oven for 30 minutes or until golden and bubbly.

Palermo-Style Cannoli A romantic Italian dinner would be incomplete without at least a mention of Italy's most popular pastry - the cannoli. Rich, sweet, and promised to make your taste buds dance. Ingredients:

20-30 cannoli shells 12 oz ricotta cheese 2 tbsp confectioners' sugar 1 tsp vanilla extract 1/4 cup pine nuts 1/4 cup chopped chocolate chips chopped pistachios or candied fruit pieces for garnish

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the ricotta, confectioners' sugar, and vanilla. Dip each shell in the ricotta mixture, filling about 2/3 full. Leave some ricotta made, for garnish. Garnish each bowl with pine nuts and chocolate chips.

Ze Striploins with Bottarga Butter While the Italian palate cannot be complete without some pasta, how about perfect, butter-tender striploins to spice up your dinner date? Accompanied by a rich and vibrant bottarga butter that leaves you and your date wanting more. Ingredients:

4 (6 oz) beef striploins 2 tbsp olive oil 1/4 cup butter, softened 1 tsp paprika Salt and pepper to taste 1/2 cup fresh parsley 2 lemons, sliced (optional)

Instructions:

Heat the olive oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Season the striploins with salt and pepper, then sear in the pan. Sear the beef until cooked to desired doneness. Remove from heat. Meanwhile, mix softened butter with paprika and a pinch of salt and pepper. In a separate bowl, sear the lemons until slightly caramelized and set aside.

Tiramisu To round off this delightful Italian feast, what about a table-centric dessert, sure to delight: Tiramisu. Ingredients:

12-16 ladyfingers 1 cup strong brewed coffee 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 cups mascarpone cheese 1/2 cup whipping cream 1 tsp vanilla extract Cinnamon powder for garnish

Instructions:

Dip each ladyfinger in the coffee mixture for about 3-5 seconds on each side. In a separate bowl combine the mascarpone, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract. Spoon a layer of mascarpone mixture in a large bowl. Alternate the ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours. Top with powdered cinnamon to serve.

This Italian meal for two can be enjoyed anytime and anywhere, and remember, it's not about the destinations, but the moments you spend with the people you love.
