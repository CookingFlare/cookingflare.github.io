Southern Shrimp and Grits: A Seafood Lover's Delight


If there’s one dish that perfectly captures the essence of Southern cuisine, it’s Southern Shrimp and Grits. This classic combination of succulent shrimp and creamy grits is a seafood lover’s delight. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a cooking enthusiast looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup stone-ground grits
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan, bring water to a boil. Slowly whisk in the grits and reduce heat to low. Cook the grits for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are thick and creamy.

  2. While the grits are cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped bacon and cook until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet, leaving the drippings in the pan.

  3. In the same skillet, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and add the shrimp to the other side. Cook the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until they are pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.

  5. In the skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until it forms a roux. Slowly whisk in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.

  6. Stir in the heavy cream and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce thickens. Add the shredded cheddar cheese and stir until melted and smooth.

  7. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and toss to coat them in the creamy sauce. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to heat the shrimp through.

  8. Divide the cooked grits among serving bowls. Top with the shrimp and creamy sauce. Garnish with crispy bacon and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier version, add some diced jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce to the skillet when sautéing the vegetables.
  • If you prefer a lighter sauce, substitute half-and-half or milk for the heavy cream.
  • Experiment with different cheeses for a unique twist. Gouda, Monterey Jack, or even blue cheese can add a new dimension of flavor to the dish.
  • Don’t rush the cooking process for the grits. Slowly simmering them allows them to absorb the water and develop their creamy texture.

In Conclusion

Southern Shrimp and Grits is a classic dish that brings together the best of Southern flavors. The combination of tender shrimp, creamy grits, and a flavorful sauce is simply irresistible. Whether you serve it for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, this dish is guaranteed to please. So grab your apron, head to the kitchen, and let your taste buds experience the true essence of Southern cuisine. Enjoy!
