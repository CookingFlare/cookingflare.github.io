The Best of Chinese Street Food: Easy & Authentic Recipes


Chinese street food is a cultural experience that tantalizes the taste buds and satisfies the soul. These delectable and savory dishes make you fall in love with the unique flavor combinations and textures found only in Chinese cuisine. From the fragrant smell of the wok to the harmonious blend of spices and the vibrant colors of the ingredients, Chinese street food is a joy to both cook and eat. Allow me to take you on a gastronomical journey and introduce you to some of the most popular and beloved street foods from China, with authentic and easy-to-follow recipes.

1. Xiao Long Bao – Steamed Dumplings

Xiao Long Bao translates to “small baked bun,” and these dumplings are famous for their delicate skins and flavor. These dumplings are filled with an aromatic and flavorful pork soup, ensuring that as they cook, the soup inside becomes a divine broth that mixes with the filling and the yuanyang (dumpling sauce).

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of Chinese noodles (also known as egg noodles)
  • 2 cups of boiling water
  • 1 pound pork belly
  • 1 pound pork shoulder
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 4 scallions
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup wooden pickle (Shaoxing wine)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1pkg of dumpling skins

Instructions:

  1. For the noodles, add flour and boiling water to a mixing bowl. Mix until combined and knead the dough for about five minutes. Let the dough rest for about forty-five minutes.
  2. In a pan, cook the pork belly, pork shoulder, and scallions, then grind the mixture and add ginger, garlic, pickle, soy sauce, white pepper, and salt. Mix everything well and let the filling cool down.
  3. On a floured surface, roll out the noodles and cut them into strips.
  4. For the dumpling skins, slightly stretch and thin the dough until it’s big enough to hold a spoonful of filling.
  5. Add a spoonful of filling into the middle of each dumpling skin. Fold and seal the dumplings.
  6. Steam the dumplings for about fifteen minutes. Once cooked, serve them with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, vinegar, and a touch of chili oil to taste.

2. Shaokao – Chinese BBQ Skewers

Also known as street barbecue, shaokao offers up a smorgasbord of meat, veggies, and more grilled to perfection! The umami taste of the barbecued meat with hints of sesame, chili oil, and soy sauce make these skewers irreplaceable in Chinese street food culture.

Ingredients:

  • assorted meats such as beef, pork, lamb, or chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • assorted vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1-2 teaspoons Sichuan peppercorns

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together garlic, ginger, soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, chili oil, rice wine vinegar, green onions, and Sichuan peppercorns to create your marinade.
  2. Thread your choice of meats and vegetables onto skewers, ensuring that they are evenly distributed and tightly packed.
  3. Place skewers on a hot barbecue or grill for 3-4 minutes on each side or until the meat is cooked and the vegetables are charred.
  4. Drizzle with a bit more sesame oil and chili oil for extra flavor, and serve immediately.

3. Dan Dan Noodles

The Dan Dan noodles have an invigorating twist of bold flavors, as the sauce consists of chili oil, black vinegar, and a touch of miso, making the dish a delight for your taste buds. Hailing from Sichuan, China, these noodles are a fiery delight that’s street-food served with a purpose.

Ingredients:

  • 4 servings dried Chinese noodles
  • 1/2 cup of black beans, cooked
  • 1/4 cup of chili oil
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons of black rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Soak the noodles in hot water for a couple of minutes and drain them.
  2. Mix together cooked black beans, chili oil, miso paste, black rice vinegar, sesame oil, green onions, ginger, Sichuan peppercorns, soy sauce, and sugar to make the sauce.
  3. Toss the sauce thoroughly with the noodles, ensuring that they are evenly coated.
  4. Serve while hot! Garnish with extra green onions, sesame seeds, and a dollop of picked radish (if desired).

Experience the allure and appeal of the vibrant tapestry of Chinese culinary wonders by trying these three delicious recipes. The umami flavors and textures differ from the conventional and will captivate your tastebuds, leaving you wanting more.
