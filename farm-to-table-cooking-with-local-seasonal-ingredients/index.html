Farm-to-Table: Cooking with Local Seasonal Ingredients


Farm-to-table cooking is a culinary approach that emphasizes the importance of using locally sourced, seasonal ingredients in our recipes. This movement not only supports local farmers and the local economy but also ensures that we are cooking with the freshest, most flavorful ingredients available. In this blog post, we will explore the benefits of farm-to-table cooking and provide you with some delicious and easy-to-follow recipes that showcase the best of what the season has to offer.

The Benefits of Farm-to-Table Cooking

There are many benefits to cooking with local, seasonal ingredients. For one, it ensures that we are getting the freshest possible ingredients, which means that our dishes will have more vibrant flavors and textures. Additionally, farm-to-table cooking supports local farmers and the local economy, which is essential for building strong, sustainable communities. Finally, cooking with seasonal ingredients encourages us to be more creative and flexible in the kitchen, as we work with what is available and in season.

Spring Recipes

Spring is a time of renewal and rebirth, and the ingredients that are available during this season reflect this. Here are a few of our favorite spring recipes that showcase the best of what the season has to offer:

Asparagus and Prosciutto Frittata

  • 1 cup fresh asparagus, chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 slices prosciutto, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped asparagus and cook until tender, about 5 minutes. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus and cook until the eggs are set. Top with chopped prosciutto and Parmesan cheese and transfer to the oven. Bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh rhubarb, sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and rhubarb. In a separate bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, flour, and salt. Add the sugar mixture to the fruit and toss to combine. Transfer the fruit mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish and set aside. In a separate bowl, mix together the rolled oats, brown sugar, chopped walnuts, and melted butter. Spread the topping evenly over the fruit and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the fruit is tender and the topping is golden brown.

Summer Recipes

Summer is a time of abundance and warmth, and the ingredients that are available during this season reflect this. Here are a few of our favorite summer recipes that showcase the best of what the season has to offer:

Heirloom Tomato Salad

  • 4 cups heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)

In a large bowl, combine the sliced heirloom tomatoes. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the dressing over the tomatoes and toss to combine. Top with chopped fresh basil and crumbled feta cheese, if desired.

Blueberry Lemon Bars

  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the fresh blueberries, granulated sugar, and flour. In a separate bowl, whisk together the softened butter, eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Pour the batter into a 9x9-inch baking dish and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center is set. Allow the bars to cool completely before dusting with confectioners’ sugar.

Fall Recipes

Fall is a time of harvest and abundance, and the ingredients that are available during this season reflect this. Here are a few of our favorite fall recipes that showcase the best of what the season has to offer:

Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, toss the cubed butternut squash with the unsalted butter, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Spread the squash mixture in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the squash is tender. Remove the squash from the oven and let cool slightly. In a blender or food processor, puree the roasted squash until smooth. In a large pot, combine the pureed squash, chicken broth, and heavy cream. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until heated through. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro if desired.

Apple Cider Donuts

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup apple cider
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Preheat the oil in a deep frying pan to 350°F (180°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, whole milk, apple cider, yeast, and salt. Add the melted butter, egg, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix until a smooth batter forms. Using a piping bag or a spoon, drop the batter into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the donuts for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Remove the donuts from the oil and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Dust with confectioners’ sugar and serve warm.

Winter Recipes

Winter is a time of coziness and warmth, and the ingredients that are available during this season reflect this. Here are a few of our favorite winter recipes that showcase the best of what the season has to offer:

Braised Short Ribs with Red Wine and Mushrooms

  • 4 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced

Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C). In a large Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant. Add the short ribs and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Add the red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover the pot and transfer it to the oven. Braise the short ribs for 2 1/2 hours, or until tender and falling off the bone. Remove the pot from the oven and add the sliced mushrooms. Simmer the mixture over low heat for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.

Cranberry Orange Upside-Down Cake

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup orange zest
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the softened butter, egg, whole milk, and a pinch of salt. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in the fresh or frozen cranberries, orange juice, orange zest, brown sugar, and chopped pecans, if using. Pour the batter into a 9x13-inch baking dish and smooth the top. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then invert onto a serving plate. Serve warm, topped with a dollop of whipped cream if desired.

In conclusion, farm-to-table cooking is a culinary approach that emphasizes the importance of using locally sourced, seasonal ingredients in our recipes. By cooking with the freshest, most flavorful ingredients available, we can create delicious and nutritious meals that support local farmers and the local economy. Whether you are a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, we hope that this blog post has inspired you to try new recipes and explore the world of farm-to-table cooking. Happy cooking!
