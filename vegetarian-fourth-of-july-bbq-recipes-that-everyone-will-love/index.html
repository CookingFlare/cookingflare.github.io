Vegetarian Fourth of July BBQ Recipes That Everyone Will Love


Are you ready to celebrate Independence Day with a bang? This Fourth of July, why not surprise your guests with a mouthwatering vegetarian BBQ feast? From smoky grilled veggies to flavorful plant-based burgers, these recipes will ensure that everyone at your party has a delicious and memorable meal. So fire up the grill, put on your apron, and get ready to impress!

Grilled Veggie Skewers with Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped basil, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between the different veggies.
  4. Brush the herb marinade onto the skewered vegetables, ensuring that each piece is coated.
  5. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred, turning occasionally.
  6. Remove from the grill and serve hot.

Black Bean and Quinoa Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the black beans using a fork or potato masher, leaving some whole beans for texture.
  2. Add the cooked quinoa, breadcrumbs, finely chopped red onion, minced garlic, chopped cilantro, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the bowl. Mix well until all the ingredients are combined.
  3. Form the mixture into patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  5. Place the patties on the preheated grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely browned and heated through.
  6. Toast the burger buns on the grill for a minute or two.
  7. Assemble your burgers with the black bean and quinoa patties, and your favorite toppings like lettuce, tomato, avocado, and vegan mayo.

Grilled Corn Salad with Lime Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Place the corn on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are lightly charred.
  3. Remove the corn from the grill and let it cool slightly.
  4. Using a sharp knife, remove the kernels from the cobs and transfer them to a large bowl.
  5. Add the diced red bell pepper, finely chopped red onion, minced jalapeño pepper, and chopped cilantro to the bowl.
  6. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  7. Pour the lime dressing over the corn mixture and toss until well coated.
  8. Let the salad marinate for at least 15 minutes before serving.

Grilled Pineapple with Coconut Whipped Cream

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, peeled and cored
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk, refrigerated overnight
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice the pineapple into rounds, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Place the pineapple slices on the preheated grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they have grill marks and are caramelized.
  4. While the pineapple is grilling, open the can of refrigerated coconut milk and scoop out the solid coconut cream into a mixing bowl. Save the coconut water for another use.
  5. Using an electric mixer, beat the coconut cream on high speed until it becomes fluffy and resembles whipped cream. Add maple syrup if desired for a touch of sweetness.
  6. Transfer the grilled pineapple slices to serving plates and top each with a dollop of coconut whipped cream.
  7. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve immediately.

Conclusion

This Fourth of July, embrace the flavors of summer and show that vegetarian BBQ can be just as tasty and satisfying as its meaty counterparts. With these mouthwatering recipes, you’ll have a spread that will impress even the most die-hard carnivores. So gather your friends and family, enjoy good food, and celebrate the red, white, and blue in style. Happy Independence Day!
