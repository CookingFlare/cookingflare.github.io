Easter Potluck Recipes: Share the Cooking with Friends and Family


Easter is a time for celebration, and what better way to celebrate than by gathering with friends and family for a potluck feast? Potluck gatherings are a wonderful opportunity to share the joy of cooking and eating together. Whether you’re hosting the potluck or attending as a guest, having a few standout recipes up your sleeve can make the event truly memorable. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delightful Easter potluck recipes that are perfect for sharing with your loved ones.

Deviled Egg Salad

Deviled eggs are a classic Easter appetizer, but this deviled egg salad puts a fresh twist on the traditional favorite. To make this dish, start by boiling a dozen eggs until they are hard-boiled. Once cooled, peel the eggs and chop them into small pieces. In a bowl, mix the chopped eggs with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, chopped chives, salt, and pepper. For a bit of crunch, add diced celery and red onion. This creamy and tangy egg salad can be served on a bed of lettuce or as a filling for mini croissants. It’s a delightful appetizer that will disappear quickly at any potluck.

Honey Glazed Ham

No Easter potluck is complete without a succulent honey glazed ham. This recipe is a crowd-pleaser and is surprisingly easy to prepare. Start with a pre-cooked spiral-cut ham and place it in a roasting pan. In a saucepan, mix honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and a splash of apple cider vinegar. Heat the glaze until it’s smooth and pour it over the ham, making sure to get the glaze in between the slices. Bake the ham in the oven until it’s heated through and the glaze has caramelized. The result is a beautifully glazed ham that’s sweet, savory, and irresistible.

Spring Pea and Asparagus Salad

For a fresh and vibrant side dish, consider making a spring pea and asparagus salad. This salad celebrates the essence of spring with its bright colors and crisp flavors. Begin by blanching fresh asparagus and peas in boiling water for a few minutes, then immediately transfer them to an ice bath to retain their vibrant color. Toss the blanched vegetables with arugula, crumbled feta cheese, toasted pine nuts, and a zesty lemon vinaigrette. The result is a refreshing salad that perfectly complements the richer dishes on the potluck table.

Lemon Berry Trifle

End the potluck on a sweet note with a show-stopping lemon berry trifle. This dessert is a delightful combination of tangy lemon curd, luscious whipped cream, and juicy mixed berries. Start by layering cubed pound cake at the bottom of a trifle dish, followed by a generous dollop of lemon curd and a layer of mixed berries. Repeat the layers until the dish is filled, then top it off with a fluffy cloud of whipped cream. The trifle can be assembled ahead of time and chilled until ready to serve, making it a convenient and impressive dessert for any Easter gathering.

Conclusion

Easter potlucks are a wonderful opportunity to come together and share the joy of cooking and eating with friends and family. These recipes are sure to add a special touch to your Easter potluck, creating lasting memories and bringing everyone closer through the love of food. So, gather your loved ones, head to the kitchen, and let the cooking and sharing begin! Happy Easter!
