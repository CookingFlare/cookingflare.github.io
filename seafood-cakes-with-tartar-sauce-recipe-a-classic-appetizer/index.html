Seafood Cakes with Tartar Sauce Recipe: A Classic Appetizer


There’s something undeniably satisfying about biting into a perfectly cooked seafood cake. The crisp outer layer gives way to a tender and flavorful interior, making it a delightful appetizer for any occasion. Today, I’m excited to share with you my take on the classic seafood cakes with tartar sauce recipe. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds with this irresistible dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of mixed seafood (such as shrimp, crab, and scallops), finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Tartar Sauce Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons pickles, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon capers, drained and finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chopped seafood, breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, parsley, green onions, garlic, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  2. Shape the mixture into small patties, approximately 2 inches in diameter. Place the patties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and refrigerate for 30 minutes to help them firm up.

  3. Meanwhile, prepare the tartar sauce by combining mayonnaise, pickles, capers, lemon juice, dill, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Stir well to combine and adjust the seasoning according to your taste. Set aside.

  4. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully place the seafood cakes into the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side or until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil.

  5. Serve the seafood cakes warm with a dollop of tartar sauce on top. You can also garnish with additional fresh parsley or dill for an extra pop of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize your seafood cakes by adding your favorite herbs and spices. Old Bay seasoning, cayenne pepper, or even a squeeze of lemon juice can take the flavor profile up a notch.
  • If you prefer a lighter version, you can bake the seafood cakes in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 15 minutes or until they are golden brown.
  • These seafood cakes are incredibly versatile. Serve them as an appetizer, a main course, or even in a sandwich with some fresh lettuce and tomato slices.

Now that you have this delectable seafood cakes with tartar sauce recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to bring it to life in your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the sea, these classic appetizers are sure to impress. So, grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a culinary delight that will transport you straight to the coast. Enjoy!
