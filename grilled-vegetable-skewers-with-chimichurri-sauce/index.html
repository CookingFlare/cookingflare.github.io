Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Chimichurri Sauce


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the flavors of the season. One of the most refreshing and easy-to-make dishes is grilled vegetable skewers with chimichurri sauce. This Argentinean-inspired recipe is a staple for outdoor gatherings and potlucks, and is sure to impress your friends and family.

The Magic of Chimichurri Sauce

Chimichurri sauce is the unsung hero of this recipe. It’s a tangy and herby condiment made with parsley, oregano, garlic, red pepper flakes, red wine vinegar, and olive oil. The combination of flavors is a match made in heaven, and it adds a depth of flavor to the grilled vegetables that’s hard to resist.

The Perfect Vegetables for Skewers

When it comes to choosing vegetables for skewers, you want to select a mix of colors and textures to create a visually appealing dish. Some of the best options include:

  • Bell peppers (any color)
  • Zucchini
  • Yellow squash
  • Red onion
  • Mushrooms
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Asparagus
  • Green beans

Grilling the Skewers

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, leaving a small space between each piece. Brush the vegetables with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.

The Chimichurri Sauce Recipe

While the skewers are grilling, you can prepare the chimichurri sauce. In a food processor, combine:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh oregano leaves
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Process the ingredients until they form a smooth and creamy sauce. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Assembly and Serving

Once the skewers are grilled and the chimichurri sauce is prepared, it’s time to assemble the dish. Thread the grilled skewers onto a platter or individual plates. Drizzle the chimichurri sauce over the top of the vegetables, making sure each piece is coated with the flavorful sauce. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • To add some heat to the dish, you can add some diced jalapeños or serrano peppers to the chimichurri sauce.
  • For a Mediterranean twist, you can add some crumbled feta cheese and chopped kalamata olives to the skewers.
  • If you prefer a lighter sauce, you can reduce the amount of olive oil used in the chimichurri recipe.
  • You can also use this recipe as a base and add your own favorite vegetables and herbs to create a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

Grilled vegetable skewers with chimichurri sauce is a dish that’s sure to become a staple in your household. The combination of flavors and textures is a match made in heaven, and it’s perfect for outdoor gatherings and potlucks. With a few simple ingredients and some basic cooking skills, you can create a dish that’s sure to impress your friends and family. So fire up the grill and get ready to enjoy the flavors of the season!
