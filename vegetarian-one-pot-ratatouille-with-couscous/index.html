Vegetarian One Pot Ratatouille with Couscous


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying vegetarian meal that’s easy to make and packed with flavor, then this Vegetarian One Pot Ratatouille with Couscous is the perfect recipe for you. This dish is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a visual delight with its vibrant colors and rich aroma. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups diced tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup couscous
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Grated parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ratatouille: Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant. Add the diced eggplant, sliced zucchinis, and chopped bell peppers to the pot. Cook the vegetables until they start to soften, stirring occasionally.

  2. Add the Tomatoes and Seasoning: Once the vegetables are slightly softened, add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Pour in the vegetable broth to create a flavorful base for the ratatouille. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Cook the Couscous: While the ratatouille is simmering, prepare the couscous according to the package instructions. Fluff the cooked couscous with a fork and set it aside.

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Once the ratatouille has cooked down and the vegetables are tender, it’s time to serve. Spoon a generous portion of the ratatouille over a bed of fluffy couscous. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a sprinkle of grated parmesan cheese if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegan version of this dish, simply omit the parmesan cheese or use a dairy-free alternative.
  • Feel free to customize the ratatouille with additional vegetables such as mushrooms, cherry tomatoes, or spinach.
  • Add a dash of red pepper flakes for a hint of spice, if desired.
  • This dish can be enjoyed hot or cold, making it a versatile option for meal prep and leftovers.

Final Thoughts

This Vegetarian One Pot Ratatouille with Couscous is a celebration of fresh, wholesome ingredients that come together to create a harmonious symphony of flavors. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this recipe is a surefire way to impress and satisfy even the most discerning palates. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity and the way it showcases the natural goodness of the vegetables. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the magic of this hearty ratatouille transport you to the sun-drenched fields of southern France. Bon appétit!
