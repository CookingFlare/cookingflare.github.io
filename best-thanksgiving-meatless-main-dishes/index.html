Best Thanksgiving Meatless Main Dishes


Thanksgiving is a time of togetherness, family, and, of course, incredible food. For those who follow a plant-based or vegetarian diet, or simply want to enjoy a meat-free meal without sacrificing flavor, there are plenty of fantastic Thanksgiving meatless main dish options. These dishes not only highlight the best of seasonal produce but also offer unique, delicious alternatives to traditional meat-based dishes. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most mouthwatering and creative Thanksgiving meatless main dishes that will leave everyone at the table satisfied, no matter their dietary preferences.

1. Vegan Mushroom Wellington

A plant-based twist on the classic Beef Wellington, the Vegan Mushroom Wellington brings together savory mushrooms, rich vegan butter, and flaky puff pastry. This elegant dish is perfect for a Thanksgiving centerpiece and will impress your guests with both its taste and presentation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups cremini or white mushrooms, chopped
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, chopped
  • 1/2 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cooked quinoa (or cooked rice)
  • 2 tablespoons vegan cream cheese
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 package puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 tablespoon non-dairy milk, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 8-10 minutes, until the mushrooms release their moisture and become golden brown.
  4. Stir in the spinach and cook for another 2 minutes, until wilted.
  5. Remove the pan from heat and add cooked quinoa, vegan cream cheese, nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper. Mix well until everything is combined.
  6. Roll out the puff pastry onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Spoon the mushroom mixture in the center of the pastry and fold the sides over, creating a log shape. Seal the edges well.
  7. Brush the top with non-dairy milk and bake in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. Let cool for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.

Why It’s Perfect for Thanksgiving:

This Vegan Mushroom Wellington serves as an impressive, hearty alternative to a traditional turkey. The earthy mushrooms combined with quinoa and spinach create a flavor-packed filling, while the golden, crispy puff pastry gives a satisfying crunch. It’s a crowd-pleaser that looks as good as it tastes!

2. Butternut Squash and Spinach Lasagna

For those who love comforting, rich, and cheesy dishes, butternut squash and spinach lasagna is an excellent Thanksgiving meatless main dish option. The sweetness of roasted butternut squash, paired with the creamy ricotta (or tofu ricotta) and hearty spinach, creates a flavorful dish that’s both filling and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 10 oz fresh spinach
  • 12 lasagna noodles (gluten-free, if needed)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese (or vegan ricotta)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella (or dairy-free mozzarella)
  • 1 jar marinara sauce (or homemade tomato sauce)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, until soft and caramelized.
  3. While the squash roasts, cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a large pan, sauté the minced garlic in olive oil for about 1 minute. Add the fresh spinach and cook until wilted, about 3 minutes.
  5. In a food processor, blend the roasted butternut squash until smooth. Add the ricotta (or tofu ricotta), nutmeg, and salt, blending again until creamy.
  6. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce at the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top. Spread a layer of the butternut squash mixture, followed by a layer of spinach. Repeat until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of marinara sauce and shredded mozzarella on top.
  7. Cover the lasagna with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 10 minutes, until bubbly and golden.
  8. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves.

Why It’s Perfect for Thanksgiving:

This lasagna is a beautiful blend of flavors, with the sweetness of the roasted squash balancing out the savory spinach and creamy ricotta. It’s a cozy, comforting dish that can easily feed a crowd. Plus, the layers of rich cheese and sauce make it an indulgent option for anyone craving a hearty main dish.

3. Stuffed Acorn Squash with Quinoa and Cranberries

Acorn squash is a quintessential fall vegetable, and its natural sweetness pairs wonderfully with savory fillings. Stuffed acorn squash with quinoa and cranberries is a bright, colorful dish that’s perfect for Thanksgiving. It’s easy to make and packed with flavor, making it a satisfying main dish for vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squashes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup toasted pecans, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the acorn squashes in half and remove the seeds. Place them cut-side up on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast the squashes in the oven for 30-40 minutes, until tender.
  4. While the squash is roasting, rinse the quinoa under cold water. In a medium pot, combine quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and stir in the dried cranberries, toasted pecans, maple syrup, and cinnamon. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Once the acorn squashes are roasted, stuff each half with the quinoa mixture.
  7. Return the stuffed squashes to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  8. Remove from the oven and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Why It’s Perfect for Thanksgiving:

This stuffed acorn squash dish is as beautiful as it is flavorful. The sweetness of the roasted squash complements the savory quinoa filling with cranberries and toasted pecans. It’s a colorful, nutritious dish that adds variety to the Thanksgiving table while still feeling indulgent.

4. Vegan Shepherd’s Pie with Lentils and Vegetables

Shepherd’s pie is a classic comfort food, but when made vegan, it becomes an even more wholesome dish packed with vegetables and hearty lentils. This dish is rich and satisfying, making it a great choice for Thanksgiving when you want something both nourishing and filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter
  • 1/4 cup plant-based milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, cook the lentils in vegetable broth for 25-30 minutes, until tender. Drain any excess liquid and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté for 8-10 minutes, until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Add the cooked lentils to the vegetable mixture along with thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir well and set aside.
  5. In a separate pot, boil the potatoes in salted water for 15-20 minutes, until fork-tender. Drain and return to the pot. Mash the potatoes with vegan butter and plant-based milk until creamy.
  6. In a baking dish, spread the lentil and vegetable mixture at the bottom. Top with the mashed potatoes, spreading evenly.
  7. Bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes, until the top is slightly golden.
  8. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.

Why It’s Perfect for Thanksgiving:

This vegan shepherd’s pie is hearty, comforting, and rich with earthy flavors. The combination of lentils, vegetables, and mashed potatoes creates a filling dish that can easily stand in as a main course for Thanksgiving. Plus, it’s a great way to pack in protein and vegetables while still enjoying a cozy, indulgent meal.

5. Spaghetti Squash with Tomato Basil Sauce

If you’re looking for a lighter, more refreshing main dish, spaghetti squash with tomato basil sauce is a fantastic option. The natural “noodles” of the spaghetti squash create a great base for a fresh, vibrant tomato sauce, making this dish both satisfying and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium spaghetti squashes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/4 cup vegan Parmesan, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Slice the spaghetti squashes in half and remove the seeds. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 40-45 minutes, until tender.
  4. Once the squash is roasted, use a fork to scrape the flesh, creating “noodles.”
  5. Heat marinara sauce in a saucepan over medium heat, then stir in fresh basil.
  6. Toss the spaghetti squash noodles with the tomato basil sauce.
  7. Serve with a sprinkle of vegan Parmesan and additional fresh basil.

Why It’s Perfect for Thanksgiving:

This spaghetti squash dish is a lighter option that still feels like a celebration of fall flavors. The fresh, vibrant sauce combined with the subtly sweet squash noodles creates a dish that feels both refreshing and satisfying.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving doesn’t have to be centered around turkey to be delicious. Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply seeking a meat-free alternative, these Thanksgiving meatless main dishes offer a wonderful variety of flavors, textures, and ingredients. From the comforting layers of butternut squash lasagna to the earthy mushrooms in a vegan Wellington, there is a plant-based dish for every palate. These meals are not only packed with seasonal produce but also provide nourishment and flavor, ensuring that your Thanksgiving table is full of variety and delight for all your guests. Enjoy a delicious, plant-based feast that everyone will love!
