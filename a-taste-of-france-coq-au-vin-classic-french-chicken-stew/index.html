A Taste of France: Coq au Vin - Classic French Chicken Stew


Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. This hearty chicken stew combines tender chicken, rich red wine, and aromatic herbs to create a flavorful and comforting meal. With its deep flavors and rustic charm, Coq au Vin is sure to transport you to the picturesque countryside of France.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 slices of thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups of red wine
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 bouquet garni (a bundle of fresh herbs tied together with kitchen twine; typically includes thyme, parsley, and bay leaf)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

  2. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper. In the same pot, brown the chicken in the bacon fat over medium-high heat until golden brown on all sides. Remove the chicken and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and cook until they have released their moisture and are browned, about 8 minutes.

  5. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and mushrooms, stirring well to coat. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste.

  6. Return the bacon and chicken to the pot. Pour in the red wine and chicken broth, making sure the liquid covers the chicken. Stir in the tomato paste and add the bouquet garni.

  7. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.

  8. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside. Increase the heat to medium-high and simmer the sauce uncovered for about 10 minutes, or until it has thickened slightly.

  9. While the sauce is simmering, remove the skin from the chicken pieces and discard. Shred the chicken meat into bite-sized pieces and return them to the pot.

  10. Stir in the butter to add richness and shine to the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  11. Serve the Coq au Vin hot, garnished with fresh parsley. This dish pairs perfectly with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or buttered egg noodles.

Bon Appétit!

Coq au Vin is a labor of love, but the end result is well worth the effort. The combination of tender chicken, savory bacon, and robust red wine creates a symphony of flavors that will transport you to the French countryside. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a taste of France, this classic dish is sure to impress. Enjoy the rich flavors, the warm aroma, and the satisfaction of mastering this timeless French recipe. Bon appétit!
