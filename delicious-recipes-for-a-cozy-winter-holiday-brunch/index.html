Delicious Recipes for a Cozy Winter Holiday Brunch


As the winter chill sets in and the holidays approach, there’s nothing quite like gathering friends and family for a cozy brunch that warms the heart and delights the palate. This holiday season, elevate your brunch game with a spread of comforting dishes that celebrate the flavors of winter. From rich, savory options to sweet, indulgent treats, this collection of recipes is designed to bring joy and warmth to your dining table.

Savory Winter Brunch Dishes

Herbed Mushroom and Spinach Quiche

A quiche is a classic brunch dish that fits perfectly into a winter holiday spread. This Herbed Mushroom and Spinach Quiche combines earthy flavors and vibrant greens, making it as delicious as it is visually appealing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, sauté the onions and mushrooms over medium heat until the onions are translucent and the mushrooms have released their moisture. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and cream. Stir in the cheese and the sautéed vegetables.
  4. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes or until the center is set and the top is golden. Allow to cool slightly before slicing.

Maple Glazed Sweet Potato Hash

This Maple Glazed Sweet Potato Hash is a hearty side dish that can be served alongside your main brunch items. Its combination of sweet and savory flavors makes it a standout.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sweet potatoes and cook until they start to soften, about 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in the onion and bell pepper; continue cooking until the vegetables are tender, about another 5-7 minutes.
  3. Drizzle with maple syrup, sprinkle with cinnamon, salt, and pepper, and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Savory Sausage and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

Perfect for feeding a crowd, this Savory Sausage and Cheese Breakfast Casserole can be prepared the night before and baked just before serving.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage
  • 8 slices of bread, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon dry mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage until browned. Drain excess fat.
  3. In a large bowl, combine bread cubes, sausage, and cheese.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, mustard, salt, and pepper. Pour over the bread mixture and stir gently to combine.
  5. Transfer to a greased baking dish and bake for 45 minutes or until the egg is set and the top is golden.

Sweet Treats for the Holiday Table

Cinnamon Roll Pancakes

Take your pancake game to new heights with these delightful Cinnamon Roll Pancakes. They’re fluffy, sweet, and perfect for a winter brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • For the cinnamon filling:
    • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
    • 1/4 cup brown sugar
    • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • For the glaze:
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 2 tablespoons milk

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, whisk together milk, egg, and melted butter. Combine wet and dry ingredients.
  2. For the cinnamon filling, mix softened butter, brown sugar, and cinnamon in a small bowl until combined.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter and drizzle some cinnamon filling on top. When bubbles form on the surface, flip and cook until golden.
  4. For the glaze, whisk powdered sugar and milk together until smooth. Drizzle over pancakes before serving.

Gingerbread French Toast

Infuse your brunch with the cozy flavors of gingerbread with this delightful Gingerbread French Toast recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 4 thick slices of gingerbread loaf
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon.
  2. Dip each slice of gingerbread into the egg mixture, ensuring both sides are coated.
  3. Cook in a heated skillet over medium heat until golden brown on each side.
  4. Serve warm with maple syrup for drizzling.

Peppermint Mocha Muffins

Perfect for adding a festive touch to your brunch table, these Peppermint Mocha Muffins are as delightful to look at as they are to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Crushed peppermint candies for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix sugars, coffee, oil, eggs, and peppermint extract. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  4. Divide batter among muffin cups and sprinkle with crushed peppermint candies. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

Warm Beverages to Sip and Enjoy

Spiced Apple Cider

Warm, spiced apple cider is the quintessential winter beverage that pairs beautifully with any brunch spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 gallon apple cider
  • 1 orange, sliced
  • 4 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 teaspoon whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon allspice berries

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine apple cider, orange slices, cinnamon sticks, cloves, and allspice.
  2. Simmer over low heat for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve warm in mugs.

Holiday Peppermint Hot Chocolate

Rich and creamy, this Holiday Peppermint Hot Chocolate is a festive treat that’s sure to please everyone.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Whipped cream and crushed peppermint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together milk, cream, cocoa powder, and sugar over medium heat until heated through and well combined.
  2. Stir in vanilla and peppermint extracts.
  3. Serve in mugs topped with whipped cream and crushed peppermint.

Setting the Scene for Your Brunch

Creating a cozy atmosphere for your winter holiday brunch is just as important as the food itself. Here are some tips to set the perfect scene:

  • Warm Lighting: Use candles or string lights to create a warm, inviting glow.
  • Festive Decor: Incorporate seasonal elements like pinecones, evergreen branches, or even small ornaments on the table.
  • Comfortable Seating: Ensure there’s ample seating, perhaps with blankets or throws available for added comfort.
  • Engaging Music: Create a playlist of soft holiday music to enhance the cozy atmosphere.

By combining these delicious recipes with a warm and inviting atmosphere, you’ll create a memorable winter holiday brunch that friends and family will cherish. Enjoy the flavors of the season, the company of loved ones, and the joy of sharing good food. Happy holidays and happy brunching!
