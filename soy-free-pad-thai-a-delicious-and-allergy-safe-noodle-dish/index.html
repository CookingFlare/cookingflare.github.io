Soy-Free Pad Thai: A Delicious and Allergy-Safe Noodle Dish


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine but have a soy allergy, you might think that enjoying a classic Pad Thai dish is out of the question. However, fear not! With a few simple ingredient swaps, you can create a mouthwatering soy-free Pad Thai right in your own kitchen. This recipe is not only allergy-safe but also bursting with the authentic flavors of Thailand.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup unsalted peanuts, crushed
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in hot water for about 8-10 minutes or until they are soft but still slightly firm. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and chili flakes until well combined. Set the sauce aside.

  3. Cook the Chicken: In a large skillet or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until it’s no longer pink. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

  4. Scramble the Eggs: In the same skillet, add the remaining 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Crack the eggs into the skillet and scramble them until they are just set. Push the eggs to one side of the skillet.

  5. Stir-Fry the Veggies: Add the minced garlic, red bell pepper, and half of the bean sprouts to the skillet. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

  6. Combine Everything: Add the soaked rice noodles, cooked chicken, and the prepared sauce to the skillet. Toss everything together until the noodles are well-coated with the sauce and heated through.

  7. Finish and Serve: Sprinkle half of the crushed peanuts and half of the chopped green onions over the Pad Thai and toss to combine.

  8. Serve: Divide the Pad Thai among plates, and garnish with the remaining bean sprouts, crushed peanuts, and green onions. Serve with lime wedges on the side for squeezing over the dish.

Tips and Substitutions

  • To make this dish vegetarian, swap the chicken for tofu or your favorite vegetables.
  • If you prefer a spicier Pad Thai, add more chili flakes or a dash of sriracha sauce to the sauce mixture.
  • For a gluten-free version, ensure that the tamarind paste and fish sauce you use are gluten-free certified.

Final Thoughts

This soy-free Pad Thai recipe is a testament to the fact that allergies should never restrict your culinary adventures. With a few adjustments, you can savor the vibrant and aromatic flavors of Pad Thai without compromising your dietary needs. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or hosting a gathering, this allergy-safe dish is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests. Enjoy the delightful fusion of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors in every satisfying bite of this homemade Pad Thai!
