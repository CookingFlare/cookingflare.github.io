Mediterranean Stuffed Acorn Squash for Fall


As the leaves begin to turn and the air gets crisp, nothing welcomes fall quite like the warmth of a hearty stuffed acorn squash. This culinary gem is not only visually stunning but also packs a punch in flavor and nutrition. This recipe combines the essence of Mediterranean cuisine with the comforting heartiness of fall produce, creating a dish that is perfect for family gatherings, potlucks, or a cozy dinner at home.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Acorn Squash:

  • 2 medium acorn squashes
  • 1 cup quinoa (or couscous for a different texture)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth (or chicken broth)
  • 1 cup chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (red or yellow)
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • ½ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Olive oil for drizzling

For Garnish:

  • Pomegranate seeds
  • Extra feta cheese
  • Fresh mint leaves

Instructions

1. Preparing the Acorn Squash

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Carefully slice the acorn squashes in half from top to bottom and scoop out the seeds. Lightly drizzle the insides with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place the squashes cut-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Roast for about 25-30 minutes, or until they are tender and lightly browned.

2. Cooking the Quinoa

While the squashes are roasting, rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove its bitter coating. In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed. Once done, remove from heat and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes.

3. Sautéing the Vegetables

In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, sautéing for about 5 minutes until they are translucent. Then, add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and cinnamon, stirring for another minute until fragrant.

4. Combining Ingredients

Add the cooked quinoa to the skillet along with the chickpeas, spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, and feta cheese. Stir gently until everything is well mixed and the spinach has wilted slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped parsley for a fresh finish.

5. Stuffing the Squash

Once the acorn squashes are done roasting, carefully flip them cut-side up. Spoon the quinoa mixture generously into each half, packing it slightly but not overstuffing.

6. Baking the Stuffed Squash

Return the stuffed squashes to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, until everything is heated through and the tops are golden.

7. Garnishing and Serving

Once out of the oven, let them rest for a few minutes. Before serving, sprinkle with pomegranate seeds, extra feta cheese, and fresh mint leaves for a burst of color and flavor.

Pairing Suggestions

This Mediterranean stuffed acorn squash pairs beautifully with a light arugula salad adorned with lemon vinaigrette or a warm, crusty piece of artisan bread. A glass of crisp white wine or a fruity sangria can elevate the dining experience, bringing out the dish’s vibrant Mediterranean flavors.

Nutritional Benefits

Acorn squash is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. It’s rich in vitamins A and C, both essential for healthy immune function and skin. The quinoa provides a complete protein source, making this dish suitable for vegetarians and vegans alike. The inclusion of chickpeas adds fiber and additional plant-based protein, while the assortment of vegetables brings antioxidants and essential vitamins to the table.

Variations

Feel free to customize this recipe to suit your tastes or dietary restrictions. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Protein Alternatives: Swap chickpeas for cooked ground turkey or lamb for a heartier version.
  2. Grains: Substitute quinoa with farro or barley for a different texture and flavor profile.
  3. Herbs and Spices: Experiment with fresh herbs like dill or thyme, or add spices like coriander or turmeric for a unique twist.
  4. Cheese: For a lactose-free option, replace feta with nutritional yeast or a dairy-free cheese alternative.

Storage Tips

Leftover stuffed acorn squash can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving. This dish can also be made ahead of time and baked just before your gathering for minimal stress.

Conclusion

Embracing the flavors of fall doesn’t have to be bland or boring. This Mediterranean stuffed acorn squash recipe brings together the best of seasonal produce with vibrant Mediterranean ingredients, creating a dish that not only delights the palate but also nourishes the body. Whether you’re hosting a holiday dinner or simply enjoying a quiet night in, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your fall cooking repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the aromas of autumn fill your kitchen!

Happy cooking!
