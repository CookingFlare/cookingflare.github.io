Best Seafood Recipes for Air Fryer


Seafood lovers rejoice! If you’ve been hesitant to try an air fryer for your fish and shellfish dishes, it’s time to dive in. The air fryer is transforming how we cook seafood—delivering crispy textures and tender insides without the mess and excess oil of traditional frying. Whether you crave crunchy shrimp, golden-fried fish fillets, or succulent crab cakes, these seafood recipes are designed to impress. Let’s explore the best seafood air fryer recipes that will turn simple ingredients into mouth-watering meals, perfect for quick weeknight dinners or weekend feasts.

Why Use an Air Fryer for Seafood?

Before we dive into recipes, it’s worth understanding why air frying seafood has become a favorite among home cooks. The air fryer circulates hot air rapidly to cook food, which means you get that perfect crispy coating similar to deep frying but with up to 80% less oil. This means healthier meals without sacrificing taste or texture.

For seafood, which tends to cook quickly and can dry out easily, the air fryer locks in moisture, keeping fish flaky and shellfish juicy. It also drastically reduces cooking times and cleanup, so you spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your food. Plus, the versatility is amazing—you can cook everything from battered cod to garlic butter scallops.

Essential Tips for Air Frying Seafood

  • Preheat the Air Fryer: Just like an oven, preheating ensures even cooking and crispier results.
  • Pat Dry: Moisture is an enemy of crispiness. Dry your seafood with paper towels before seasoning or breading.
  • Don’t Overcrowd: Air needs to circulate. Cook in batches to get optimal crunch.
  • Spritz With Oil: A light spray of oil on breaded seafood enhances the golden crust.
  • Adjust Time per Thickness: Thicker fillets need longer, while delicate shrimp or scallops cook quickly.
  • Use the Right Coating: Panko breadcrumbs or cornmeal add crunch, while marinades add flavor without sogginess.

Recipe 1: Crispy Air Fryer Breaded Fish Fillets

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb white fish fillets (cod, haddock, or tilapia)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray or olive oil spray

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the dredging station:
    • Place flour in one bowl.
    • Beat eggs in a second bowl.
    • Mix panko breadcrumbs with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a third bowl.
  3. Pat fish dry with paper towels and cut into uniform portions.
  4. Coat each piece by first dredging in flour, then dipping in eggs, and finally pressing into the seasoned panko mix.
  5. Spray each fillet lightly with cooking spray to promote browning.
  6. Place fillets in air fryer basket in a single layer.
  7. Cook for 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy.
  8. Serve with lemon wedges, tartar sauce, or a fresh herb dip.

Recipe 2: Garlic Butter Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine butter, garlic, paprika, red pepper flakes, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add shrimp and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Place shrimp in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
  5. Cook for 6–8 minutes, shaking basket halfway, until shrimp is opaque and edges start to crisp.
  6. Garnish with chopped parsley and extra lemon wedges before serving.

Recipe 3: Air Fryer Crab Cakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lump crabmeat, picked over for shells
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs (plus extra for coating)
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onions
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix crabmeat, 1/2 cup panko, mayonnaise, egg, mustard, Worcestershire, seasoning, green onions, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Form mixture into 6 equally sized crab cakes. Press lightly into extra panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Preheat air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Spray both sides of crab cakes with cooking spray and place in air fryer basket without overcrowding.
  5. Cook for 10–12 minutes, flipping once, until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve with tartar sauce or a spicy aioli.

Recipe 4: Lemon Herb Air Fryer Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, dill, parsley, lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pat salmon fillets dry and brush each with the herb mixture.
  4. Place skin-side down in the air fryer basket.
  5. Cook for 8-10 minutes, depending on thickness, until salmon flakes easily with fork.
  6. Serve with steamed vegetables or a fresh salad.

Recipe 5: Spicy Air Fryer Calamari Rings

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb squid rings, cleaned and patted dry
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Cooking spray
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, smoked paprika, cayenne, and garlic powder in a bowl.
  2. Dip squid rings first in flour mixture, then in beaten eggs, then coat with panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Preheat air fryer to 400°F (200°C).
  4. Spray squid rings lightly with cooking spray.
  5. Cook in batches for 6–8 minutes until golden and crispy, shaking the basket halfway.
  6. Serve hot with lemon wedges and a spicy dipping sauce.

Frequently Asked Questions About Air Frying Seafood

Can you cook frozen seafood in the air fryer?

Yes! Frozen shrimp, fish sticks, and other seafood can be cooked directly from the freezer. Just increase the cooking time by a few minutes and check for doneness more frequently.

How do you prevent seafood from sticking to the air fryer basket?

Spray a light coat of oil on the basket before adding seafood. Also, using parchment paper with holes or a silicone liner can help.

Is battered fish suitable for air frying?

Thin batter can work if it’s applied carefully. For best results, use a light coating or bread crumbs instead of thick wet batters that may drip and create a mess.

Conclusion

Air frying seafood offers an exciting new way to enjoy your favorite coastal delights with less fuss and guilt. From crispy fish to buttery shrimp and delicate crab cakes, the air fryer brings out flavors and textures that will make you rethink how you cook seafood at home. With a handful of staple ingredients and some handy tips, these recipes promise quick, delicious meals any night of the week. Experiment with seasonings, sauces, and sides to make each meal uniquely yours. Happy cooking and even happier eating!
