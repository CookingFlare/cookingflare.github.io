The Best Homemade Sauces for Pizza Night


Pizza night is one of those events that bring joy, creativity, and a sense of togetherness. Whether it’s a family gathering, a casual hangout with friends, or a special occasion, pizza has a way of making every moment memorable. But while the toppings and the crust are important, the sauce is where the magic truly happens. A great pizza sauce can elevate the flavors of your pie and transform an ordinary meal into an extraordinary one.

Instead of relying on store-bought sauces, which can often feel generic or too salty, why not take your pizza game to the next level with a homemade sauce? Here, we’ll explore some of the best homemade sauces for pizza night—each one packed with flavor and designed to suit a variety of taste preferences. From the classic tomato-based sauce to some innovative options, you’ll find plenty of inspiration for your next pizza-making adventure.

1. Classic Marinara Pizza Sauce

The classic marinara sauce is a staple in pizza making. It’s rich, tangy, and full of flavor, making it the perfect base for virtually any pizza. This traditional tomato-based sauce is quick and easy to make, and it brings the authentic pizzeria experience straight to your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, but not browned.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, red pepper flakes (if using), sugar, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring to a simmer, reduce heat, and cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Use an immersion blender for a smoother texture, or leave it chunky for a rustic feel.
  6. Allow the sauce to cool slightly before spreading it on your pizza dough.

This sauce is perfect for Margherita pizza or any other pizza with simple, fresh ingredients.

2. Roasted Garlic White Sauce

If you love the creamy, savory taste of garlic and want something different from the traditional red sauce, a roasted garlic white sauce could be the answer. This sauce is creamy, garlicky, and pairs beautifully with a variety of toppings, including spinach, mushrooms, and grilled chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup cream cheese (optional for extra creaminess)
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the top off the garlic head to expose the cloves, drizzle with olive oil, wrap in foil, and roast for about 30-35 minutes, or until the garlic is soft and caramelized.
  2. Squeeze the roasted garlic out of the skins and set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  4. Add the roasted garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Stir frequently until the cream begins to thicken.
  6. Add the Parmesan cheese and cream cheese, and stir until melted and smooth.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and remove from heat once the sauce reaches a creamy consistency.
  8. Spread this sauce over your pizza dough for a rich and indulgent flavor.

This sauce works wonderfully with white pizza recipes, where the sauce complements the toppings without overpowering them.

3. Pesto Sauce

Pesto is a vibrant, herbaceous sauce that adds a refreshing burst of flavor to pizza. While traditional pesto is made with basil, you can easily experiment with other herbs like spinach or arugula to create different variations. Pesto pizza offers a unique twist that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (or a mix of basil and spinach)
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (or walnuts for a more budget-friendly option)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor or blender, combine the basil, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan.
  2. With the motor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the pesto reaches your desired consistency. You can make it chunky or smooth, depending on your preference.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the pesto sauce over your pizza dough before adding your favorite toppings.

Pesto pairs wonderfully with roasted vegetables, mozzarella, or even sun-dried tomatoes, making it a great choice for a gourmet pizza.

4. BBQ Sauce for a Sweet and Smoky Flavor

For a more adventurous pizza, BBQ sauce is an excellent choice. The smoky, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor profile of BBQ sauce brings a whole new level of taste to your pizza. It’s especially great for meat-heavy pizzas like BBQ chicken pizza, bacon, or sausage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes until it thickens slightly and the flavors meld together.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Allow the sauce to cool slightly before spreading it on your pizza dough.

BBQ sauce is perfect for creating delicious BBQ chicken or pulled pork pizzas, and it pairs wonderfully with a combination of cheeses and fresh veggies.

5. Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce

For those who love a little heat with their pizza, arrabbiata sauce is the way to go. This Italian-style tomato sauce is packed with spicy red pepper flakes, garlic, and herbs, making it an ideal option for those who want to add some kick to their pie.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, red pepper flakes, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.
  5. Taste and adjust the heat by adding more red pepper flakes if desired.
  6. Let the sauce cool before spreading it over your pizza dough.

Arrabbiata sauce pairs well with spicy sausage, grilled chicken, or even a simple pepperoni pizza if you’re looking to add some extra heat.

6. Alfredo Sauce for a Creamy Delight

Alfredo sauce, with its rich and creamy texture, is a fantastic option for those who enjoy a luxurious pizza experience. Its mild flavor complements a variety of toppings, and it’s especially delicious when paired with chicken, spinach, or mushrooms.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the Parmesan cheese and whisk until the sauce is smooth and thickened.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Allow the sauce to cool before spreading it over your pizza dough.

Alfredo sauce is the perfect base for a creamy chicken Alfredo pizza, or you can add roasted vegetables for a rich, satisfying flavor.

7. Buffalo Sauce for a Bold Kick

For those who enjoy bold, spicy flavors, buffalo sauce is a fantastic pizza sauce. Its tangy and spicy taste adds a kick to any pizza, and it pairs especially well with chicken, blue cheese, or ranch dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup hot sauce
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the hot sauce, white vinegar, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before spreading it over your pizza dough.

Buffalo sauce is perfect for making a buffalo chicken pizza, or you can use it as a dipping sauce alongside your pizza for an extra punch.

Conclusion

When it comes to pizza night, the sauce is truly the star of the show. Whether you prefer something classic, creamy, spicy, or smoky, there’s a homemade pizza sauce out there to suit every taste. Each of these sauces is easy to make, full of flavor, and perfect for elevating your homemade pizza experience. So next time you’re making pizza at home, skip the jarred sauces and try these delicious homemade alternatives for a fresh, authentic, and memorable meal. Happy cooking!
