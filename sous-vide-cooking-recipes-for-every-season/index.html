Sous Vide Cooking: Recipes for Every Season


Sous vide cooking is a revolutionary technique that brings precision, flavor, and tenderness to your culinary adventures. Originating from the French term meaning “under vacuum,” this method involves sealing food in a bag and immersing it in a water bath at a controlled temperature. This technique allows for consistent results and retains moisture, making it ideal for various dishes throughout the year. In this blog post, we will explore an array of sous vide recipes that celebrate the flavors of each season, providing you with inspiration to elevate your cooking game year-round.

Spring: Fresh and Vibrant Flavors

Sous Vide Asparagus with Lemon Butter

As spring awakens the earth, fresh produce like asparagus comes into season. This sous vide asparagus dish is bright and flavorful, making it the perfect side for any springtime meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A few lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 183°F (84°C).
  2. Place the asparagus in a single layer in a vacuum-seal bag. Add the butter, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.
  4. Immerse the bag in the water bath and cook for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  5. Remove the asparagus from the bag, drizzle with any remaining butter, and garnish with lemon slices before serving.

Sous Vide Lemon Herb Chicken Breast

Spring is also the season for light, zesty flavors. This sous vide lemon herb chicken breast is juicy and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix olive oil, thyme, oregano, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the chicken breasts in a vacuum-seal bag and pour the marinade over.
  4. Seal the bag and immerse it in the water bath. Cook for 1.5 to 2 hours.
  5. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the bag, sear in a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes per side for a crispy exterior, and serve.

Summer: Savor the Season of Barbecues

Sous Vide BBQ Ribs

Nothing says summer like a barbecue, and these sous vide BBQ ribs are guaranteed to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite brand)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C).
  2. Season the ribs generously with salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  3. Place the ribs in a vacuum-seal bag and seal tightly.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 24 hours for tender, fall-off-the-bone ribs.
  5. Preheat your grill to high heat. Remove the ribs from the bag, brush with BBQ sauce, and grill for about 5 minutes per side until caramelized.

Sous Vide Corn on the Cob

Sweet corn is a summer staple, and sous vide cooking unlocks its natural sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).
  2. Place each ear of corn in a vacuum-seal bag, adding a tablespoon of butter and a pinch of salt to each.
  3. Seal the bags and cook for 1 hour in the water bath.
  4. Remove the corn from the bags, serve hot, and enjoy the buttery goodness.

Autumn: Embrace the Comfort of Hearty Dishes

Sous Vide Butternut Squash Soup

As the leaves change, so does our palate. This creamy butternut squash soup is a comforting dish perfect for autumn.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 cup heavy cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 183°F (84°C).
  2. Combine the butternut squash, onion, garlic, vegetable broth, salt, pepper, and nutmeg in a vacuum-seal bag.
  3. Seal the bag and immerse it in the water bath. Cook for 1.5 hours.
  4. Once cooked, blend the soup until smooth. Stir in heavy cream if desired and serve warm.

Sous Vide Beef Short Ribs

Autumn is the season for hearty meals, and these sous vide beef short ribs are a true delight.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon cracked black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • Fresh herbs (rosemary and thyme)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C).
  2. Season the short ribs with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Place the ribs in a vacuum-seal bag with red wine, beef broth, and herbs. Seal tightly.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 48 hours.
  5. When ready, remove from the bag, sear in a hot skillet for a crispy crust, and serve with your favorite sides.

Winter: Cozy Up with Comfort Foods

Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin with Apples

Winter calls for warm, comforting meals. This sous vide pork tenderloin with apples is a delightful dish for chilly evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin
  • 2 apples, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 140°F (60°C).
  2. Season the pork tenderloin with salt and pepper. Place it in a vacuum-seal bag with the apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Seal the bag and immerse it in the water bath. Cook for 2-3 hours.
  4. After cooking, sear the tenderloin in a hot skillet for about 2 minutes per side. Serve with the sautéed apples for a sweet and savory contrast.

Sous Vide Chocolate Lava Cake

No winter meal is complete without dessert, and this sous vide chocolate lava cake is the ultimate treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 176°F (80°C).
  2. In a bowl, melt the chocolate and butter together. Let it cool slightly.
  3. In another bowl, whisk the eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla extract until combined. Stir in the melted chocolate and then fold in the flour and salt.
  4. Pour the mixture into individual silicone molds and seal them in vacuum bags.
  5. Cook for 1 hour in the water bath. To serve, carefully invert the molds onto plates, and enjoy the molten center.

Conclusion

Sous vide cooking offers an incredible opportunity to explore the rich flavors of each season through a diverse array of recipes. Whether you’re celebrating the freshness of spring, the vibrant barbecues of summer, the heartiness of autumn, or the cozy warmth of winter, sous vide has a dish to elevate your culinary experience. With precise temperature control and the ability to infuse flavors deeply into your ingredients, this technique ensures that every meal is a memorable one.

Embrace the seasons and let sous vide cooking inspire your kitchen endeavors. Happy cooking!
