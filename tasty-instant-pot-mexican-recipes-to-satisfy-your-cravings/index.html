Tasty Instant Pot Mexican Recipes to Satisfy Your Cravings


When it comes to satisfying food cravings, few cuisines can rival the bold flavors and comforting dishes of Mexican cuisine. The beauty of cooking Mexican food is that it can be both accessible and exciting, especially when you use an Instant Pot. This versatile kitchen appliance allows you to prepare traditional Mexican dishes in a fraction of the time, without sacrificing flavor. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of mouth-watering Instant Pot Mexican recipes that will have your taste buds dancing and your heart happy.

Why Choose Instant Pot for Mexican Cooking?

The Instant Pot is a game-changer for home cooks. It combines the functions of a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, steamer, sauté pan, and even a yogurt maker. This multifunctionality makes it ideal for the bold and complex flavors found in Mexican cooking. The pressure cooking feature infuses flavors quickly, tenderizes meats, and allows for a more efficient cooking experience. Plus, it’s energy-efficient and reduces the time spent in the kitchen!

1. Instant Pot Chicken Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup salsa (your choice of mild or spicy)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Corn or flour tortillas
  • Optional toppings: diced onions, cilantro, avocado, lime wedges, cheese

Instructions:

  1. Sauté: Set your Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add olive oil and allow it to heat. Add the chicken breasts and sear for about 2-3 minutes on each side until lightly browned.
  2. Add Ingredients: Pour in the salsa and sprinkle the taco seasoning over the chicken. Pour in the chicken broth.
  3. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and ensure the valve is set to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. After the timer goes off, allow for a natural release for 5 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  4. Shred Chicken: Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and stir to coat with the sauce.
  5. Serve: Serve the chicken in tortillas with your choice of toppings.

2. Instant Pot Mexican Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté: Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add olive oil, diced onion, and garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add Rice: Stir in the rice and toast it for a couple of minutes, stirring frequently.
  3. Mix Ingredients: Add diced tomatoes, chicken broth, taco seasoning, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and ensure the valve is set to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 6 minutes. Allow for a natural release for 10 minutes before quick releasing any remaining pressure.
  5. Fluff and Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

3. Instant Pot Beef Barbacoa

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast, cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup beef broth

Instructions:

  1. Sauté: Turn the Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add olive oil and brown the beef chunks on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. Cook Onions: In the same pot, add onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the beef back to the pot along with vinegar, lime juice, chipotle peppers, cumin, oregano, salt, and broth. Stir to combine.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 60 minutes. Once done, allow a natural release.
  5. Shred Beef: Remove the beef and shred it with two forks. Return the shredded beef to the pot and mix it with the juices.
  6. Serve: Serve in tacos, burritos, or over rice.

4. Instant Pot Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken
  • 1 can (10 ounces) enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (frozen or canned)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, cilantro, sliced jalapeños

Instructions:

  1. Layer: Spread a small amount of enchilada sauce on the bottom of the Instant Pot. Layer tortillas, then add shredded chicken, black beans, corn, chili powder, and a sprinkle of cheese. Repeat layers until ingredients are used up, finishing with tortillas on top.
  2. Add Sauce: Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the top layer and sprinkle with cheese.
  3. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes with a quick release.
  4. Serve: Garnish with sour cream, cilantro, and jalapeños before serving.

5. Instant Pot Queso Dip

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Velveeta cheese, cubed
  • 1 can (10 ounces) diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 cup cooked chorizo (optional)
  • Tortilla chips for serving

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In the Instant Pot, combine the Velveeta cheese, diced tomatoes, heavy cream, cumin, chili powder, and chorizo (if using).
  2. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 5 minutes. Once done, quick release the pressure.
  3. Stir: Carefully stir the mixture until smooth and creamy.
  4. Serve: Serve warm with tortilla chips for dipping.

6. Instant Pot Tamales

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups masa harina
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable shortening
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Filling of choice (shredded chicken, cheese, or pork)
  • Corn husks, soaked in warm water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Dough: In a large bowl, mix masa harina, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, beat the shortening until fluffy, then add it to the masa mixture. Gradually add chicken broth until a soft dough forms.
  2. Assemble Tamales: Take a soaked corn husk, spread about 2 tablespoons of dough on it, add a spoonful of filling, and fold the sides over. Repeat until all dough is used.
  3. Set Up Instant Pot: Place a steamer basket in the Instant Pot and add water to the bottom. Stand the tamales upright in the basket.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 25 minutes. Allow for a natural release.
  5. Serve: Let cool slightly before serving. Enjoy with salsa or guacamole.

Conclusion

The Instant Pot is a remarkable tool for anyone who loves Mexican cuisine but might feel intimidated by the traditional cooking methods. These recipes are not only easy to follow, but they also deliver authentic flavors that will satisfy any craving. From mouth-watering chicken tacos to cheesy queso dip, the possibilities are endless with the Instant Pot. Gather your ingredients, fire up your Instant Pot, and prepare to impress your family and friends with your culinary skills! Happy cooking!
