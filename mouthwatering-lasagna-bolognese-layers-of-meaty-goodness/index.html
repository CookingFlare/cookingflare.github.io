Mouthwatering Lasagna Bolognese: Layers of Meaty Goodness


If there’s one dish that can instantly evoke a sense of comfort and satisfaction, it’s a hearty, indulgent lasagna. And when it comes to lasagna, the classic Bolognese version is a true standout. This timeless Italian favorite is a symphony of flavors, textures, and aromas, all layered together to create a culinary masterpiece. In this recipe, we’ll delve into the art of creating the ultimate mouthwatering Lasagna Bolognese, where each bite is a celebration of meaty goodness and rich, velvety sauce.

Ingredients

For the Bolognese Sauce

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound Italian sausage, casing removed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

For the Lasagna

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Making the Bolognese Sauce

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until browned.

  2. Add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened and the onions are translucent.

  3. Pour in the red wine and allow it to simmer for a few minutes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, beef broth, tomato paste, sugar, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally. The longer the sauce simmers, the richer the flavors will become.

Preparing the Lasagna

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and lay them out flat on a clean surface.

  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese with half of the Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the Bolognese sauce. Place a single layer of lasagna noodles on top, slightly overlapping them.

  5. Spread a third of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by a third of the mozzarella cheese and a ladle of Bolognese sauce.

  6. Repeat the layers two more times, ending with a final layer of noodles topped with remaining Bolognese sauce and mozzarella cheese. Sprinkle the remaining Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese over the top.

  7. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  8. Allow the lasagna to rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Final Thoughts

The aroma of simmering Bolognese sauce, the anticipation of layers of tender noodles and creamy cheese, the first heavenly bite that melts in your mouth – this is the magic of a well-crafted Lasagna Bolognese. Whether you’re hosting a special gathering or simply craving a comforting meal, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that culminates in a symphony of meaty goodness and pure indulgence. Buon appetito!
