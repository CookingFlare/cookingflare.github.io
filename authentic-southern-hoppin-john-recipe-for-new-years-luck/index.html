Authentic Southern Hoppin' John Recipe for New Year's Luck


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to embark on a culinary journey to the heart of the Southern United States. Today, we’ll be diving into the rich traditions and flavors of the Authentic Southern Hoppin’ John recipe. Passed down through generations, this dish is believed to bring good luck and prosperity in the New Year. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas, dried
  • 1 ham hock
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups cooked rice

Instructions

  1. Soak the black-eyed peas: Place the dried black-eyed peas in a large bowl and cover them with water. Allow them to soak overnight. Drain and rinse before using.

  2. Prepare the ham hock: In a large Dutch oven or pot, add the ham hock and cover it with water. Bring it to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1 hour until the meat is tender. Remove the ham hock from the pot and set it aside to cool. Once cooled, remove the meat from the bone and chop it into small pieces. Discard the bone.

  3. Cook the vegetables: In the same pot, add the diced onion, green bell pepper, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté them over medium heat until they become fragrant and tender, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the seasonings and black-eyed peas: Stir in the bay leaf, dried thyme, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Add the soaked black-eyed peas and cooked ham hock pieces to the pot. Mix well to combine all the flavors.

  5. Simmer and cook: Pour in the chicken broth, ensuring that the peas are fully submerged. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until the peas are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. Season to taste: As the Hoppin’ John cooks, taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. The flavors should be robust and well-balanced.

  7. Serve and enjoy: Once the black-eyed peas are tender and the flavors have melded together, remove the bay leaf and discard it. Serve the Hoppin’ John over a bed of cooked rice. Garnish with some freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color. Enjoy this hearty and comforting dish with your loved ones!

Final Thoughts

The Authentic Southern Hoppin’ John is more than just a delicious meal; it’s a symbol of hope, luck, and tradition. As you savor each bite, remember the rich history behind this dish and the hopes it carries for a prosperous year ahead. May your New Year be filled with joy, good fortune, and the warm embrace of family and friends.

We hope you enjoy this recipe and that it becomes a cherished part of your own New Year’s tradition. Happy cooking and cheers to a marvelous year!
