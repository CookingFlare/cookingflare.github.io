Paleo Fish Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


If you’re following a Paleo diet and looking for delicious and grain-free fish recipes, you’ve come to the right place! Fish is not only a great source of protein but also provides essential nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids. Whether you’re a seafood lover or just looking to incorporate more fish into your diet, these five Paleo fish recipes will satisfy your taste buds and keep you feeling nourished.

1. Baked Lemon Garlic Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • Fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the salmon fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Brush the olive oil mixture over the salmon fillets.
  5. Top each fillet with a few slices of lemon and sprinkle with fresh dill.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Spicy Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, paprika, chili powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  4. Brush the shrimp with the olive oil and spice mixture.
  5. Grill the shrimp skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are pink and cooked through.
  6. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the grilled shrimp before serving.

3. Seared Tuna Steaks with Avocado Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 2 tuna steaks
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the avocado salsa:
    • 1 ripe avocado, diced
    • 1 tomato, diced
    • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
    • Juice of 1 lime
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the tuna steaks with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Sear the tuna steaks for 2-3 minutes per side, or until desired doneness.
  4. Remove the tuna steaks from the skillet and let them rest for a few minutes.
  5. In a bowl, combine the diced avocado, tomato, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper to make the avocado salsa.
  6. Slice the tuna steaks and serve with the avocado salsa on top.

4. Coconut Curry Cod

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb cod fillets
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cauliflower rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the coconut milk over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the red curry paste, fish sauce, and lime juice.
  3. Add the cod fillets to the skillet and simmer for 8-10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with cauliflower rice.

5. Lemon Herb Baked Halibut

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb halibut fillets
  • 2 tablespoons ghee, melted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the halibut fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the melted ghee, lemon juice, parsley, dill, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the ghee mixture over the halibut fillets.
  5. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until the halibut is cooked through and flakes easily.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

These five Paleo fish recipes are not only delicious but also grain-free, making them perfect for those following a Paleo lifestyle. Whether you’re craving salmon, shrimp, tuna, cod, or halibut, there’s a recipe here to satisfy your taste buds. Give them a try and enjoy a healthy and flavorful meal that supports your Paleo journey.
