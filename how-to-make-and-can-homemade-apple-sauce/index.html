How to Make and Can Homemade Apple Sauce


The crisp days of fall are synonymous with apple season, a time perfect for creating your own batch of homemade applesauce. Not only is applesauce an incredibly versatile addition to your pantry, but the satisfaction of making and canning your own brings a sense of accomplishment and culinary pride. Whether you use it as a snack, a side dish, or an ingredient for baked goods, knowing how to make and preserve applesauce will ensure you enjoy the delicious flavors of the season throughout the year.

In this guide, you’ll learn how to make applesauce from scratch, customize it to suit your taste, and master the canning process to preserve it safely. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients for Homemade Applesauce

To begin, you’ll need a few simple ingredients:

  • Apples (about six pounds, a mix of sweet and tart varieties works best)
  • Water (around 1 cup, or as needed)
  • Sugar (optional, to taste)
  • Cinnamon (optional, for flavor enhancement)
  • Lemon Juice (optional, preserves freshness)

You can tailor these ingredients based on your preferences, like making unsweetened applesauce or adding extra spices like nutmeg.

Choosing the Right Apples for Applesauce

Selecting the right types of apples is essential for homemade applesauce. Combining sweet and tart varieties makes for a more balanced flavor. Apples like Fuji, Gala, or Honeycrisp add sweetness, while Granny Smith, Braeburn, or Pink Lady offer a tart contrast. Choosing soft-fleshed apples will make it easier to achieve the smooth texture that applesauce is known for.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Applesauce

Step 1: Prepare Your Apples

Wash, peel, core, and slice your apples into uniform pieces. While peeling is optional, removing the peels prevents any unwanted texture in your final product. If you don’t mind extra fiber and a slightly chunkier applesauce, you can leave the peels on.

Step 2: Cook the Apples

Place the apple pieces in a large stockpot with one cup of water. The water prevents the apples from sticking to the pot and helps them break down. Bring the apples to a boil, then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer. Cover the pot and cook, stirring occasionally, until the apples become soft and tender. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.

Step 3: Blend to Your Desired Consistency

Decide what kind of applesauce you want—chunky or smooth. If you prefer a chunkier sauce, simply mash the softened apples with a potato masher. For a smoother texture, use an immersion blender or pass the mixture through a food mill. Blend until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 4: Add Sweeteners and Spices

Taste your applesauce and adjust the flavor. If you’re avoiding sugar, you might skip this step, but for those who prefer sweeter sauce, add a bit of sugar to taste. A sprinkle of cinnamon and a dash of nutmeg complement the apples and bring warm, cozy notes to the dish. Stir well and allow these additions to mingle with the applesauce on a low simmer for just a few minutes.

Why Can Your Applesauce?

Making applesauce is the easy part, but preserving it is crucial. Canning ensures a longer shelf life while allowing you to enjoy homemade flavor year-round without relying on store-bought versions. The process is simple, safe, and produces a pantry stocked with jars of ready-to-use applesauce.

How to Can Homemade Applesauce

Step 1: Prep Your Materials

Before you begin canning, ensure you have the following tools prepared:

  • Canning jars (preferably pint or quart-sized)
  • Lids and bands
  • Large pot or water bath canner
  • Jar lifter
  • Funnel

Sterilize your jars and lids by washing them thoroughly, then boiling them in water for ten minutes. Let them dry before filling.

Step 2: Heat Your Applesauce

Reheat your applesauce until it reaches a boil. This step ensures the applesauce is hot enough for safe preservation and helps eliminate any bacteria.

Step 3: Fill the Jars

Using a funnel, ladle the hot applesauce into your sterilized jars, leaving about ½ inch of headspace at the top. Use a clean utensil or the handle of a spoon to remove air bubbles by stirring gently inside the jar. Wipe the jar rims to ensure they’re dry and clean before sealing with lids and bands.

Step 4: Process in a Water Bath Canner

Place your filled jars into the water bath canner. The water should cover the jars by at least an inch. Bring the water to a rolling boil and process the jars:

  • Pint jars: Process for 15 minutes.
  • Quart jars: Process for 20 minutes.

Adjust processing time based on your altitude, as higher elevations require longer times.

Step 5: Cool and Store

Carefully remove the jars from the canner using a jar lifter and place them on a towel. Allow them to cool completely for about 12-24 hours. After cooling, check the seals—press down on the center of each jar lid. If it doesn’t pop back, it’s properly sealed. Store sealed jars in a cool, dark place for up to one year.

Customizing Your Applesauce

One of the beauties of homemade applesauce is the ability to customize it according to your preferences. Here are a few ideas to get creative:

  • Spiced Applesauce: Add cloves, allspice, or vanilla extract for a unique flavor profile.
  • Berry-Infused Applesauce: Mix in fresh or frozen berries during the cooking process for a fruity twist.
  • Unsweetened Applesauce: Skip the sugar completely for a healthier alternative or if you plan to use it in baked goods.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Applesauce Is Too Thin

If your applesauce is too watery, simmer it uncovered to allow excess water to evaporate. Alternatively, blending more thoroughly or adding additional cooked apples can help thicken your sauce.

Lids Aren’t Sealing

If a lid hasn’t sealed, refrigerate the jar immediately rather than storing it at room temperature. Be diligent about wiping the rims before sealing and ensure you’re using properly sterilized equipment.

Enjoying Your Homemade Applesauce

Now that you’ve learned how to make and can applesauce, take a moment to enjoy the fruits of your labor. Homemade applesauce is excellent paired with roasted pork, stirred into oatmeal, or eaten straight out of the jar. It’s also a fantastic base for recipes such as muffins or cakes that call for applesauce instead of oil.

Tips for Long-Term Preservation

Proper storage techniques will ensure your applesauce stays fresh for months. Keep jars in a consistently cool and dry place, away from direct sunlight. Labeling your jars with the date ensures that you use them before their shelf life ends.

Final Thoughts

Canning homemade applesauce gives you the freedom to enjoy the flavors of fall long after the season ends. With minimal effort, a few simple tools, and the freshest apples you can find, you’ll be able to create a pantry full of this delightful treat. Whether you prefer your applesauce sweet or tart, chunky or smooth, the customizable nature of the recipe means there’s a version suited perfectly for your taste buds. So roll up your sleeves and get started—your jars of homemade applesauce are waiting!
