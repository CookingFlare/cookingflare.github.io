Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Recipe for Summer


Strawberry shortcake is the quintessential summer dessert, bursting with fresh flavors that evoke sunny skies, picnics, and backyard celebrations. This homemade version combines buttery, golden shortcakes, luscious whipped cream, and vibrant fresh strawberries for a dessert that’s simple yet indulgent. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, celebrating a special occasion, or simply craving a sweet treat, this recipe is the ultimate crowd-pleaser.

Why Make Strawberry Shortcake at Home?

Store-bought strawberry shortcakes might seem convenient, but they lack the freshness and flavor that homemade versions deliver. Making it from scratch gives you full control over the sweetness levels, textures, and overall presentation. Plus, it’s incredibly satisfying to create something so beautiful and delicious in your own kitchen. Freshly baked shortcakes paired with juicy, macerated strawberries and fluffy whipped cream are unmatched in taste.

This recipe features step-by-step instructions that ensure every component is perfectly balanced. With a few basic ingredients and a little time, you’ll have a delightful dessert that feels like summer on a plate.

Ingredients List

For the Shortcakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 2/3 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Strawberry Filling:

  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice

For the Whipped Cream:

  • 1 cup heavy cream, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Strawberries

Start with the strawberries so they have time to macerate and develop their sweet, syrupy flavor. This step is crucial for creating a vibrant strawberry topping that complements the other components beautifully. Here’s how:

  1. Wash, hull, and slice the strawberries into thin, uniform pieces.
  2. Place the strawberries in a large bowl, then sprinkle the granulated sugar and drizzle the lemon juice over them.
  3. Toss gently to combine. Cover the bowl and let the mixture sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This process allows the strawberries to release their natural juices, creating a flavorful syrup.

While the strawberries are macerating, you’ll have plenty of time to prepare the other elements.

Baking the Shortcakes

Shortcakes are the heart of this recipe—golden, flaky, and slightly sweet. Their texture acts as the perfect foundation for layering the strawberries and cream.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cubed butter, and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Cold butter is essential for achieving that signature flaky texture.
  4. In a separate small bowl, mix the heavy cream and vanilla extract. Pour this mixture into the dry ingredients and gently combine until a soft dough forms. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and pat it into a rectangle about ¾ inch thick. Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out rounds of dough, gathering and re-rolling scraps as necessary.
  6. Transfer the shortcakes to the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12–15 minutes, or until they turn golden brown. Allow them to cool completely on a wire rack before assembling the dessert.

Whipping the Cream

No strawberry shortcake is complete without a cloud-like layer of whipped cream. Making it fresh at home ensures a velvety texture and subtle sweetness.

  1. Place the chilled heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract into a large bowl. The cream whips better when cold, so don’t skip this step!
  2. Using a handheld mixer or a stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment, whip the cream on medium-high speed until peaks form. Be careful not to overbeat, as it can turn grainy.
  3. Once finished, cover and refrigerate until ready to use.

Assembling the Strawberry Shortcakes

The magic moment has arrived—bringing all the elements together. This step transforms simple ingredients into a layered masterpiece that’s sure to delight.

  1. Slice each cooled shortcake horizontally into two halves, creating a top and bottom layer.
  2. Spoon a generous amount of the macerated strawberries and their syrup onto the bottom half of the shortcake.
  3. Add a thick dollop of whipped cream on top of the strawberries. Feel free to make it as generous as you like!
  4. Gently place the top half of the shortcake over the whipped cream, completing the stack.
  5. For a picture-perfect finish, add a small spoonful of strawberries and a swirl of whipped cream on top.

Serve immediately to enjoy the shortcakes at their best—flaky, creamy, and bursting with fresh fruit flavor.

Tips for Success

  1. Use Fresh Strawberries: Ripe, in-season strawberries will give your dessert the sweetest taste. You can find local berries at farmer’s markets or grocery stores during summer months.
  2. Don’t Skip Maceration: Allowing the strawberries to sit with sugar and lemon juice enhances their natural flavor and creates a delightful syrup.
  3. Keep Ingredients Cold: For both the shortcakes and whipped cream, cold ingredients lead to better textures. Make sure your butter and heavy cream are properly chilled before using.
  4. Customize the Layers: Feel free to experiment with extras like a drizzle of honey, chocolate shavings, or mint for a unique twist.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Homemade strawberry shortcake is a celebration of summer’s best ingredients, brought together in an easy yet elegant dessert. The buttery shortcakes provide just the right amount of richness, the whipped cream adds an irresistible lightness, and the strawberries deliver their characteristic tart-sweet punch. The result is a dessert that’s balanced, satisfying, and perfect for any summer gathering.

Whether it’s your first time making strawberry shortcake or you’re looking for a recipe to impress friends and family, this dish is sure to become a seasonal favorite. Serve it al fresco with a tall glass of lemonade for the ultimate summer experience.

Bring some sunshine to your table and savor the simple joys of homemade dessert with this irresistible strawberry shortcake recipe. Fresh ingredients, approachable steps, and timeless flavors combine to make it a staple for sunny days. After all, what better way to celebrate summer than with strawberries and cream?
