Quick Vegan and Vegetarian Stir-Fry Recipes with Tempeh


Stir-fries are some of the most versatile and efficient dishes to prepare, making them perfect for busy weeknights or a quick, wholesome meal. Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or just someone who enjoys plant-based meals, stir-fries offer a wide range of possibilities to incorporate nutritious ingredients. One fantastic ingredient that works wonderfully in stir-fries is tempeh. Tempeh is a fermented soy product that is packed with protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making it an excellent addition to any stir-fry.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of quick vegan and vegetarian stir-fry recipes featuring tempeh. These recipes are designed to be simple, satisfying, and packed with flavor. So, let’s dive in and discover how you can create easy, nutritious, and delicious stir-fries with tempeh as the star ingredient.

Why Tempeh?

Tempeh is often overlooked in favor of tofu, but it’s a great alternative and has several distinct benefits. Made from fermented soybeans, tempeh has a nutty flavor and a firmer texture compared to tofu. It’s more substantial, making it a great meat substitute for those looking for a plant-based option with a satisfying bite.

Nutritional Benefits of Tempeh

Tempeh is an excellent source of protein, providing around 21 grams of protein per 3.5 ounces (100 grams). It is also rich in fiber, with about 7-9 grams per serving, helping to keep you full longer. Additionally, tempeh is a great source of probiotics due to the fermentation process, which promotes healthy digestion. It’s also packed with vitamins and minerals like magnesium, iron, calcium, and potassium.

Because of its firm texture, tempeh is easy to slice, crumble, or cube, making it ideal for stir-fries where you need a hearty, satisfying protein. Now, let’s explore some delicious vegan and vegetarian stir-fry recipes with tempeh that you can whip up in no time.

1. Classic Tempeh Stir-Fry with Vegetables

This simple yet flavorful stir-fry features tempeh, a variety of fresh vegetables, and a savory sauce that brings everything together. It’s a quick meal that doesn’t compromise on taste or nutrition.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, cut into cubes or strips
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (or any cooking oil of your choice)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 cup broccoli florets
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or agave syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tempeh: Steam the tempeh for about 10 minutes to soften it and reduce its bitterness. After steaming, slice or cube the tempeh into bite-sized pieces.

  2. Cook the tempeh: Heat a tablespoon of sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the tempeh and cook, stirring frequently, until golden brown and crispy on all sides (about 5-7 minutes). Remove the tempeh from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same skillet, add a little more oil if necessary. Add the garlic, ginger, and sliced vegetables (bell pepper, carrot, zucchini, and broccoli). Stir-fry the vegetables for 4-5 minutes, until they are just tender but still crisp.

  4. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and cornstarch-water mixture (if using). Pour the sauce over the vegetables and tempeh, and toss everything together to coat evenly.

  5. Finish and serve: Continue to stir-fry everything for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens. Garnish with sesame seeds and fresh cilantro before serving. Serve this stir-fry over rice or noodles for a complete meal.

2. Spicy Peanut Tempeh Stir-Fry

If you’re craving a little heat and richness, this spicy peanut tempeh stir-fry is just what you need. The peanut butter adds a creamy texture, while the chili flakes bring the heat, balancing the savory flavors perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, sliced into strips
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter (smooth or crunchy)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Chopped peanuts, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tempeh: Slice the tempeh into thin strips. If you prefer, you can steam it for 10 minutes to soften it, but this is optional.

  2. Cook the tempeh: Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tempeh strips and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are golden brown and slightly crispy (about 5-7 minutes). Remove the tempeh from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same skillet, add the sliced onion, bell pepper, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften.

  4. Prepare the peanut sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, and chili flakes. Add a tablespoon of warm water if the sauce seems too thick.

  5. Combine the tempeh and sauce: Return the tempeh to the skillet, then pour the peanut sauce over the tempeh and vegetables. Stir well to coat everything in the sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until everything is heated through and the sauce has thickened slightly.

  6. Serve: Garnish the stir-fry with fresh cilantro and chopped peanuts for extra crunch. Serve it over steamed rice, quinoa, or noodles for a filling meal.

3. Tempeh and Tofu Stir-Fry with Sweet and Sour Sauce

This stir-fry combines two excellent plant-based proteins—tempeh and tofu—along with a tangy and sweet sauce that complements both ingredients beautifully. The tofu adds a soft texture that contrasts nicely with the firm tempeh.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, cut into cubes
  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch mixed with 3 tablespoons water (for thickening)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tempeh and tofu: If you haven’t already, press the tofu to remove excess moisture. Cut both the tempeh and tofu into bite-sized cubes.

  2. Cook the tempeh and tofu: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tempeh cubes and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are golden and crispy on all sides (about 5-7 minutes). Remove them from the skillet and set them aside. In the same skillet, add the tofu cubes and cook until they are lightly golden and crisped on the outside (about 5 minutes). Remove the tofu and set it aside with the tempeh.

  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil to the skillet. Stir-fry the onion, bell pepper, and carrot for 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

  4. Make the sweet and sour sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and cornstarch-water mixture. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and bring it to a simmer. Let it cook for 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly.

  5. Combine and serve: Add the cooked tempeh, tofu, and pineapple chunks to the skillet. Stir to coat everything in the sauce, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through. Drizzle with sesame oil and garnish with green onions. Serve this flavorful stir-fry with rice or noodles.

4. Tempeh and Kale Stir-Fry with Lemon Tahini Sauce

If you’re looking for a lighter stir-fry that’s packed with greens, this tempeh and kale stir-fry with a tangy lemon tahini sauce is a great choice. The richness of the tahini sauce complements the earthy flavor of the kale and tempeh perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, sliced into strips
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups kale, chopped
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon water (to thin the sauce)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon zest, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tempeh: Slice the tempeh into strips or cubes. You can steam it beforehand to soften it, but it’s optional.

  2. Cook the tempeh: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the tempeh and cook, stirring frequently, until golden brown and crispy (about 5-7 minutes). Remove the tempeh from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same skillet, add the red onion, zucchini, and kale. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.

  4. Make the tahini sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, soy sauce, maple syrup, and water. Add more water if necessary to achieve your desired sauce consistency.

  5. Combine and serve: Add the cooked tempeh back into the skillet with the vegetables. Pour the tahini sauce over everything and stir well to coat. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, until everything is heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with lemon zest before serving.

Conclusion

Tempeh is an incredibly versatile and nutritious ingredient that can elevate any stir-fry. Whether you’re looking for a spicy peanut stir-fry, a tangy sweet and sour dish, or a lighter lemon tahini option, tempeh can be easily incorporated into your favorite stir-fry recipes. Not only does tempeh provide a hearty and satisfying protein, but it also pairs wonderfully with a wide variety of vegetables and sauces, allowing you to experiment with different flavors and textures.

The next time you’re looking for a quick, easy, and delicious meal, give one of these tempeh stir-fry recipes a try. They’re sure to become your go-to dishes for any night of the week!
