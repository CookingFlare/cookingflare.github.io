Refreshing and Tangy: How to Make Korean Cucumber Kimchi Like a Pro


Kimchi is a staple in Korean cuisine, known for its bold and tangy flavors. While most people are familiar with the traditional cabbage kimchi, there are many other variations to explore. One such variation is Korean Cucumber Kimchi, a refreshing and tangy side dish that is perfect for hot summer days. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making Korean Cucumber Kimchi like a pro.

Ingredients

  • 4 large cucumbers
  • 2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Start by washing the cucumbers thoroughly under cold water. Cut off the ends and slice them into bite-sized pieces. Place the cucumber slices in a large bowl.

  2. Sprinkle the sea salt over the cucumbers and gently toss them to ensure even coating. Let the cucumbers sit for about 30 minutes to draw out excess water.

  3. While the cucumbers are resting, prepare the kimchi paste. In a separate bowl, combine the minced garlic, grated ginger, Korean red pepper flakes, sugar, fish sauce, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sesame seeds. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  4. After 30 minutes, rinse the cucumbers under cold water to remove the excess salt. Gently squeeze the cucumbers to remove any remaining water.

  5. Add the cucumbers to the kimchi paste bowl and mix thoroughly to ensure all the cucumbers are coated with the flavorful paste.

  6. Finally, add the thinly sliced green onions to the bowl and mix them into the cucumber kimchi.

  7. Transfer the cucumber kimchi into a clean, airtight container. Press down on the kimchi to remove any air pockets and ensure the cucumbers are fully submerged in the kimchi paste.

  8. Allow the cucumber kimchi to ferment at room temperature for about 24 hours. After 24 hours, taste the kimchi to check if it has reached the desired level of tanginess. If it needs more time, let it ferment for another 12-24 hours, tasting periodically.

  9. Once the cucumber kimchi has reached the desired level of tanginess, transfer it to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. The cucumber kimchi will continue to develop its flavors over time.

Serving Suggestions

Korean Cucumber Kimchi can be enjoyed as a side dish or used as a topping in various dishes. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Serve it alongside Korean BBQ for a refreshing and tangy contrast to the rich flavors of the meat.
  • Add it to your favorite salad for an extra kick of flavor and crunch.
  • Use it as a topping for tacos, sandwiches, or burgers for a unique twist.

Conclusion

Making Korean Cucumber Kimchi is a simple and rewarding process. The combination of refreshing cucumbers and tangy kimchi paste creates a delightful burst of flavors that will leave your taste buds wanting more. Whether you’re a kimchi enthusiast or a curious foodie looking to explore new flavors, this recipe is a must-try. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to make Korean Cucumber Kimchi like a pro. Enjoy!
