Delectable Dan Dan Noodles: A Sichuan Specialty


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and spicy dishes, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Dan Dan Noodles. Originating from the Sichuan province of China, Dan Dan Noodles are a beloved street food known for their rich, savory sauce and fiery kick. This dish is a perfect blend of numbing Sichuan peppercorns, savory minced pork, and chewy noodles, creating a symphony of flavors and textures that will leave your taste buds tingling. So, let’s dive into the tantalizing world of Dan Dan Noodles and learn how to recreate this Sichuan specialty in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

For the Noodles:

  • 10 oz (300g) fresh Chinese wheat noodles or any similar egg noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Chinkiang vinegar (or rice vinegar as a substitute)
  • 2 tablespoons Chinese sesame paste (or tahini as a substitute)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorn powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 1/4 cup chicken stock

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup ground pork
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry as a substitute)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan chili oil
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon roasted peanuts, crushed

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles:

    • Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and rinse them under cold water. Toss the noodles with vegetable oil to prevent sticking, then set aside.

  2. Make the Sauce:

    • In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, vinegar, sesame paste, sugar, Sichuan peppercorn powder, five-spice powder, and chicken stock until well combined. Set the sauce aside.

  3. Cook the Topping:

    • In a pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the ground pork and cook until it’s no longer pink. Add the Shaoxing wine, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry for another minute. Finally, add the Sichuan chili oil, green onion, and crushed peanuts. Cook for an additional minute, then remove from heat.

  4. Assemble the Dish:

    • Divide the noodles into serving bowls. Pour the prepared sauce over the noodles, and then top with the cooked pork mixture. Drizzle a little extra chili oil on top if you like it extra spicy. Garnish with additional chopped green onions and crushed peanuts.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Toss the noodles thoroughly to coat them in the sauce and toppings. Then, dig in and enjoy the explosion of flavors in every bite!

Tips and Tricks:

  • If you can’t find Chinese sesame paste, tahini works as a great substitute. It adds a rich nuttiness to the sauce.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less Sichuan chili oil according to your preference.
  • For a vegetarian version, you can replace the ground pork with minced mushrooms or tofu for a delightful twist.

A Taste of Sichuan Tradition:

Dan Dan Noodles are not just a dish; they are a culinary journey through the vibrant flavors of Sichuan cuisine. The combination of savory, spicy, and nutty elements creates a truly unforgettable dining experience. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary adventures, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to explore the diverse and captivating world of Chinese cuisine. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the magic of Dan Dan Noodles right in your own kitchen!
