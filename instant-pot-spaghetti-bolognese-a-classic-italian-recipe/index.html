Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese: A Classic Italian Recipe


Spaghetti Bolognese is one of those timeless Italian dishes that has won hearts worldwide. The rich, savory combination of ground meat, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and a medley of herbs creates a satisfying meal perfect for any occasion. Traditionally, Bolognese sauce takes hours to simmer on the stovetop, allowing the flavors to meld together. But with the help of the Instant Pot, you can create this delicious, hearty dish in a fraction of the time. This recipe for Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese gives you the full depth of flavor you’d expect from a slow-cooked sauce, but in just about 30 minutes!

What is Bolognese Sauce?

Bolognese, or ragù alla Bolognese, is a meat-based sauce that originated in the city of Bologna in northern Italy. It’s known for its rich texture and complex flavors, which come from slowly simmering meat, vegetables, wine, and stock together to create a deeply savory sauce. Traditionally, it is served with tagliatelle, but spaghetti has become a popular choice worldwide. The key to a good Bolognese is allowing the meat to brown properly and taking the time to build layers of flavor. While it’s typically cooked low and slow, using an Instant Pot for this recipe cuts down on the cooking time significantly while still achieving a deliciously rich result.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese

To make this Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Bolognese Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil – Used for sautéing the vegetables and meat.
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped – Adds sweetness and depth to the sauce.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced – For an aromatic base.
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped – Adds sweetness and body to the sauce.
  • 2 stalks celery, finely chopped – Another vegetable that contributes to the depth of flavor.
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef – This is the heart of the sauce, but feel free to use a combination of pork and beef for extra richness.
  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground pork – Optional, but it adds more flavor and depth to the sauce.
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes – This provides the foundation for the sauce’s texture and acidity.
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste – Adds richness and helps thicken the sauce.
  • 1/2 cup dry red wine – Wine enhances the sauce’s flavor. If you prefer not to use wine, substitute with beef broth or stock.
  • 1 cup beef broth – To add richness and help deglaze the pot.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano – For an earthy, slightly bitter note that complements the sauce.
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil – Adds an aromatic, sweet flavor to the sauce.
  • 1 bay leaf – For a deep, fragrant flavor.
  • Salt and pepper, to taste – Enhances all the ingredients.
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional) – Adds a little kick to the sauce, though it’s not essential.

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz spaghetti – The perfect pairing for this rich sauce.
  • Water – To cook the pasta in the Instant Pot.

Garnish (optional):

  • Grated Parmesan cheese – To add richness and a salty kick.
  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped – For a fresh, herbaceous finish.

Why Use an Instant Pot for Bolognese?

The Instant Pot is a revolutionary kitchen tool that combines the functions of several appliances in one. It can sauté, pressure cook, steam, and slow cook, making it a perfect choice for dishes like Spaghetti Bolognese. Traditional Bolognese sauce requires slow cooking, which can take several hours to develop the deep flavors. However, the Instant Pot drastically reduces the cooking time by using pressure cooking, allowing the flavors to intensify quickly without compromising the richness and texture of the sauce.

The Instant Pot also offers the advantage of cooking the spaghetti directly in the pot, so there’s no need to boil the pasta separately. Everything comes together in one pot, saving you time and effort while still delivering a delicious, comforting meal.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese

Step 1: Sauté the Vegetables and Meat

Start by setting your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil to the pot. Once the oil is hot, add the finely chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, or until they soften and become fragrant.

Next, add the ground beef and ground pork (if using) to the pot. Use a wooden spoon or spatula to break up the meat as it cooks. Sauté the meat for 7-10 minutes, or until it is browned and no longer pink. This step is crucial because browning the meat adds layers of flavor to your sauce.

Step 2: Deglaze the Pot

Once the meat is browned, it’s time to deglaze the pot. Pour in 1/2 cup of dry red wine (or beef broth if you prefer). Use your spatula to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. This will ensure that all the flavors are incorporated into your sauce, and it prevents the Instant Pot from giving you a “burn” warning.

Step 3: Add the Tomatoes and Herbs

After deglazing, it’s time to add the canned diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and beef broth. Stir everything together until the tomato paste is fully incorporated into the sauce. Then, add the dried oregano, dried basil, and bay leaf. Season with salt, pepper, and crushed red pepper flakes (if using). Stir everything to combine.

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Sauce

Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 20 minutes. The Instant Pot will take a few minutes to come to pressure, and once it does, it will cook the sauce quickly, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 5: Cook the Spaghetti

While the sauce is pressure cooking, break the spaghetti in half to fit into the Instant Pot. Once the sauce has finished cooking, carefully do a quick release of pressure by turning the valve to “Venting.” When the pressure has fully released and the lid can be opened, stir the sauce to combine.

Next, add the broken spaghetti to the pot. Pour in enough water to just cover the pasta (about 4 cups). Stir the spaghetti into the sauce, making sure the noodles are submerged. Close the lid again and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 4 minutes. This will cook the spaghetti right in the sauce, allowing it to absorb all the flavors.

Step 6: Final Touches

Once the pasta has cooked, carefully perform another quick release of pressure. Open the Instant Pot and give everything a good stir. The pasta should be perfectly al dente, and the sauce should have thickened to a rich consistency. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little more beef broth or water to reach your desired consistency.

Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or herbs if needed. Remove the bay leaf before serving.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Serve the Spaghetti Bolognese hot, topped with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and chopped basil or parsley for added freshness. The combination of the savory sauce, tender pasta, and rich cheese creates a comforting, satisfying meal that’s perfect for any weeknight dinner or special occasion.

Tips for the Best Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese

  1. Use Lean Meat: While traditional Bolognese uses a mixture of meats, lean ground beef works perfectly in this Instant Pot version. You can mix it up with pork or turkey for a lighter option.

  2. Don’t Skip the Browning: The key to a rich Bolognese sauce is browning the meat and vegetables. This caramelization adds a depth of flavor that you won’t get if you skip this step.

  3. Add a Touch of Cream: For a creamier sauce, stir in a splash of heavy cream or milk at the end of cooking. This will give the sauce a velvety texture.

  4. Make it Ahead: Bolognese sauce actually tastes better the next day, so feel free to make a big batch and store the leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well for up to 3 months, so it’s great for meal prep.

  5. Try Different Herbs: While oregano and basil are classic in Bolognese, feel free to experiment with other herbs like thyme, rosemary, or a touch of sage for a unique twist.

Conclusion

This Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese is the perfect solution for those who crave the rich, comforting taste of a traditional Italian dish but don’t have the time to spend hours in the kitchen. With just a few simple ingredients and the help of your Instant Pot, you can enjoy a flavorful and hearty meal in under 30 minutes. The Instant Pot’s pressure cooking function ensures that the sauce is rich and deeply flavorful, while the pasta cooks directly in the sauce, soaking up all the savory goodness. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner or a special gathering, this Instant Pot Spaghetti Bolognese is sure to be a hit with everyone at the table.

Happy cooking!
