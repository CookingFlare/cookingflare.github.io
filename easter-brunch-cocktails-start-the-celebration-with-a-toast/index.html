Easter Brunch Cocktails: Start the Celebration with a Toast


Easter Sunday is just around the corner, and it’s time to start planning the perfect brunch celebration. But before you sit down to enjoy your delicious eggs, waffles, and quiche, why not start the day off right with a refreshing Easter brunch cocktail? In this post, we’ll explore some of the most unique and delicious Easter-themed cocktails that are sure to impress your guests.

Spring Fling

The first cocktail on our list is the Spring Fling, a sweet and tangy concoction that’s perfect for the season. Made with gin, lemon juice, simple syrup, and a splash of sparkling water, this cocktail is as refreshing as a spring morning.

Combine 1 1/2 ounces gin, 1/2 ounce freshly squeezed lemon juice, and 1/2 ounce simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a Collins glass filled with ice. Top with 2 ounces sparkling water and garnish with a lemon wheel and a sprig of rosemary.

Bunny’s Kiss

Next up is the Bunny’s Kiss, a fruity and bubbly cocktail that’s sure to put a smile on your face. Made with vodka, peach schnapps, cranberry juice, and a splash of grenadine, this cocktail is as sweet as a basket full of Easter eggs.

Combine 1 1/2 ounces vodka, 1/2 ounce peach schnapps, 2 ounces cranberry juice, and 1/2 ounce grenadine in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a highball glass filled with ice. Top with 2 ounces sparkling water and garnish with a peach slice and a cherry.

Easter Egg Martini

The Easter Egg Martini is a sophisticated and elegant cocktail that’s perfect for a fancy Easter brunch. Made with vodka, lime juice, and a splash of blue curaçao, this cocktail is as colorful as a basket full of Easter eggs.

Combine 1 1/2 ounces vodka, 1/2 ounce freshly squeezed lime juice, and 1/2 ounce blue curaçao in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a chilled martini glass. Garnish with a lime wheel and a sprig of rosemary.

Mimosa Bar

No Easter brunch is complete without a Mimosa bar, and we’ve got a few variations to try. First, you can make a classic Mimosa with champagne and orange juice. But for a twist, try adding a splash of grenadine for a fruity flavor or a splash of peach puree for a sweet and creamy flavor.

Combine 1/2 cup champagne and 1/2 cup orange juice in a champagne flute. Add a splash of grenadine or peach puree if desired. Garnish with a slice of orange and a sprig of rosemary.

Lavender Spritz

The Lavender Spritz is a unique and refreshing cocktail that’s perfect for the spring season. Made with gin, lemon juice, and a splash of lavender syrup, this cocktail is as soothing as a spring breeze.

Combine 1 1/2 ounces gin, 1/2 ounce freshly squeezed lemon juice, and 1/2 ounce lavender syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake until chilled, then strain into a wine glass filled with ice. Top with 2 ounces Prosecco and garnish with a lemon wheel and a sprig of lavender.

Conclusion

These Easter brunch cocktails are sure to impress your guests and add a touch of springtime magic to your celebration. Whether you’re looking for something sweet and fruity or sophisticated and elegant, we’ve got a cocktail recipe that’s sure to fit the bill. So go ahead, start the celebration with a toast, and make this Easter Sunday one to remember!
