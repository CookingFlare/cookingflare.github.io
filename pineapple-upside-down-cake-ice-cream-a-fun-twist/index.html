Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream: A Fun Twist


Have you ever wondered what it would be like to combine the classic flavors of pineapple upside-down cake with the creamy goodness of ice cream? Well, wonder no more because we have the perfect recipe for you! Get ready to embark on a delicious adventure with our Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream. This fun and unique twist on a traditional dessert will surely satisfy your cravings for something sweet and refreshing. So, let’s dive in and discover the magic of this delightful treat!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of almond extract
  • 1/2 cup of crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1/2 cup of maraschino cherries, halved
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of yellow cake mix

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to steam, but make sure it doesn’t boil.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until they are pale and smooth. Slowly pour the heated cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling.

  3. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes.

  4. Stir in the vanilla extract and almond extract into the custard mixture. Transfer the mixture to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  5. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a baking dish, combine the crushed pineapple, halved maraschino cherries, brown sugar, melted butter, salt, and yellow cake mix. Mix until well combined.

  6. Spread the pineapple mixture evenly in the baking dish and bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the cake is golden brown.

  7. Remove the baked pineapple cake from the oven and let it cool completely. Once cooled, cut it into small cubes.

  8. Once the custard mixture has chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. During the last few minutes of churning, add the pineapple cake cubes into the ice cream mixture.

  9. Transfer the churned ice cream into a lidded container, layering it with additional pineapple cake cubes, if desired. Freeze for at least 2-3 hours or until firm.

  10. Serve your Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream in bowls or cones, and garnish with a cherry on top for an extra pop of color and flavor.

Enjoy the Twist!

Indulge in the delightful combination of flavors and textures that Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream brings. The creamy and luscious ice cream, paired with the tangy sweetness of pineapples and the rich, moist cake chunks, is a match made in dessert heaven. This unique twist on a classic dessert is sure to impress your family and friends at any gathering or celebration.

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and get ready to embark on a delightful culinary adventure. Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream is the perfect treat for those hot summer days or anytime you crave something sweet and refreshing. Enjoy the twist and savor every spoonful of this delicious creation!

Note: For an extra touch, you can also drizzle caramel sauce or sprinkle toasted coconut on top of your Pineapple Upside-Down Cake Ice Cream. Let your creativity soar and make it your own!
