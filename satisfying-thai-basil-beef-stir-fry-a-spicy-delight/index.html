Satisfying Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry: A Spicy Delight


Thai cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and aromatic spices. One of the most popular and beloved dishes from this culinary tradition is the Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry. This mouth-watering dish is a staple in many Thai restaurants and has gained immense popularity worldwide. In this recipe, we will explore the secrets to creating a satisfying and spicy Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry that will tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 250g beef strips (sirloin or ribeye)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, onions, snow peas, and mushrooms)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Cut the beef into thin strips and season with salt. Cut the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Mince the garlic and grate the ginger.
  2. Heat the wok or frying pan: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove the beef from the wok and set aside.
  3. Cook the aromatics: Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil to the wok. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the curry paste: Add the Thai red curry paste to the wok and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, until the paste is fragrant and slightly darkened.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables: Add the mixed vegetables to the wok and cook until they are tender-crisp, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and palm sugar: Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and palm sugar to the wok. Stir to combine and cook for 1 minute.
  7. Add the beef and fish sauce: Add the cooked beef back to the wok and stir to combine with the sauce. Add the fish sauce and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  8. Add the Thai basil: Stir in the chopped Thai basil leaves and cook for an additional 30 seconds.
  9. Season with black pepper and salt: Season the Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry with ground black pepper and salt to taste.
  10. Serve: Serve the Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry immediately, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro leaves (if desired).

Tips and Variations:

  • Use high-quality ingredients: Fresh and fragrant ingredients are essential to creating a delicious and authentic Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of Thai red curry paste. If you prefer a spicier dish, add more curry paste or use hotter peppers like Thai chilies.
  • Add noodles: Cook noodles according to package instructions and add them to the stir-fry for a more filling and satisfying meal.
  • Experiment with different proteins: Substitute chicken, pork, or tofu for the beef for a different twist on the recipe.

Conclusion:

The Thai Basil Beef Stir Fry is a dish that is both easy to make and packed with flavor. By following this recipe, you will be able to create a satisfying and spicy dish that is sure to please even the most discerning palates. With its bold flavors and vibrant colors, this dish is a true representation of the beauty and complexity of Thai cuisine. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and experience the magic of Thai cooking for yourself.
