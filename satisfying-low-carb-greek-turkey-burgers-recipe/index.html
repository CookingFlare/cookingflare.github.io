Satisfying Low Carb Greek Turkey Burgers Recipe


If you’re on the hunt for a flavorful, healthy alternative to traditional burgers, look no further than these Satisfying Low Carb Greek Turkey Burgers. Juicy, savory, and packed with Mediterranean flavors, they offer a delicious twist that will keep you coming back for more. Perfectly suited for those watching their carb intake, these burgers are not only delicious but also quick and easy to prepare. Let’s dive into the ingredients, preparation steps, and tips to make the ultimate Greek turkey burger.

Ingredients

To create the perfect Greek turkey burger, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Turkey Burgers:

  • 1 pound ground turkey (preferably lean)
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (or 2 tablespoons dried parsley)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for cooking)
  • 1 large egg (optional, for binding)

For Serving:

  • Romaine lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Tzatziki sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Kalamata olives (optional)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Mix the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, crumbled feta cheese, chopped red onion, chopped parsley, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. If you’re looking for extra binding, you can add the egg at this stage. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly using your hands or a spatula, but be careful not to overwork the meat, as this can lead to tough burgers.

Step 2: Form the Patties

Once the mixture is well-combined, divide it into four equal portions. Shape each portion into a patty about 1 inch thick. Make a slight indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent them from puffing up during cooking.

Step 3: Cook the Burgers

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or grill pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the patties to the pan, making sure not to overcrowd them. Cook for about 5-6 minutes on one side until golden brown. Flip the burgers and continue cooking for another 5-6 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

If you prefer grilling, preheat your grill to medium heat and cook the patties for about the same duration, making sure to flip them halfway through.

Step 4: Assemble the Burgers

To serve, take a large romaine lettuce leaf and place a cooked turkey patty on it. Top with sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and a generous dollop of tzatziki sauce. If desired, add some Kalamata olives for an extra burst of flavor. Wrap the lettuce around the fillings and enjoy your satisfying low carb Greek turkey burger!

Tips for Perfect Greek Turkey Burgers

1. Choose Lean Ground Turkey

Using lean ground turkey helps to keep the burgers lower in fat while still providing a juicy texture. However, you can use a mix of dark and light meat for added flavor if desired.

2. Don’t Skip the Feta

The crumbled feta cheese adds a creamy, tangy flavor that is essential to the Mediterranean profile of these burgers. It also helps to keep the burgers moist.

3. Use Fresh Herbs

Whenever possible, opt for fresh herbs like parsley to enhance the flavor of the burgers. Dried herbs work in a pinch, but fresh herbs will always yield better results.

4. Experiment with Toppings

Feel free to get creative with the toppings! Consider adding sliced avocado for creaminess, pickled red onions for tang, or even a sprinkle of sumac for an extra layer of flavor.

Nutritional Information

These low carb Greek turkey burgers are not only delicious but also nutritious. Here’s a quick breakdown of the nutritional values per serving (one burger without the bun):

  • Calories: Approximately 250
  • Protein: 30 grams
  • Fat: 12 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 5 grams
  • Fiber: 1 gram

Health Benefits

  • Low in Carbs: Perfect for those following a ketogenic or low-carb lifestyle.
  • High in Protein: Beneficial for muscle building and repair.
  • Rich in Healthy Fats: The olive oil and feta cheese provide heart-healthy fats.
  • Packed with Nutrients: Fresh veggies and herbs add vitamins and minerals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Make These Burgers Ahead of Time?

Absolutely! You can prepare the patties in advance and store them in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours before cooking. You can also freeze them for longer storage. Just make sure to separate each patty with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

What Can I Substitute for Feta Cheese?

If you’re not a fan of feta cheese, consider using goat cheese or even shredded mozzarella for a different flavor profile.

Can I Bake These Burgers?

Yes! Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and place the patties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for about 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are cooked through.

Conclusion

These Satisfying Low Carb Greek Turkey Burgers are a delightful and healthy alternative to traditional burgers. With their Mediterranean flavors and easy preparation, they make a perfect weeknight dinner or a fun addition to a summer barbecue. Serve them with fresh veggies and your choice of toppings for a meal that is as nutritious as it is delicious. So, gather your ingredients, fire up that skillet or grill, and enjoy a guilt-free burger experience that will have you craving more!
