How to Make a Delicious Strawberry Rhubarb Pie


There’s something magical about the marriage of sweet, juicy strawberries with tart, tangy rhubarb. Strawberry rhubarb pie is a timeless classic that embodies the bright flavors of spring and summer. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion or just craving a flavorful dessert, this pie is guaranteed to delight your taste buds. Below, you’ll find a step-by-step guide to mastering a delicious strawberry rhubarb pie, complete with tips to ensure your pie is as visually impressive as it is mouthwatering.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the process, gather your ingredients. Freshness is the key to a perfect strawberry rhubarb pie, so whenever possible, source ripe, juicy strawberries and vibrant rhubarb stalks. Here’s everything you’ll need to make the filling, crust, and topping.

For the Pie Filling:

  • 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 ½ cups rhubarb, diced into ½-inch pieces
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup brown sugar (adds depth and complexity to the flavor)
  • ¼ cup cornstarch (a must for thickening the filling)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (balances the sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional but recommended for enhanced flavor)
  • Pinch of salt

For the Pie Crust (Double Crust):

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (optional for a lightly sweet crust)
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 6–8 tablespoons ice water

For the Egg Wash & Topping:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon milk or cream
  • Coarse sugar for sprinkling (adds a subtle crunch and sparkle)

Step-by-Step Guide to Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Start by preparing your strawberries and rhubarb. Hull the strawberries and slice them into even pieces. For the rhubarb, trim the ends and dice the stalks into small chunks, ensuring they’re uniform to promote even cooking. Toss the strawberries and rhubarb together in a large mixing bowl and set aside.

Step 2: Make the Pie Crust

A perfect pie starts with a flaky, buttery crust. Follow these steps to make one from scratch:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to incorporate it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Divide the dough into two equal portions. Shape them into discs, wrap each in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes while you prepare the filling.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

In the bowl of strawberries and rhubarb, sprinkle the granulated sugar, brown sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and salt. Gently mix until everything is well combined, ensuring the fruit is evenly coated. Set the filling aside to allow the flavors to meld and the sugars to draw juices from the fruit.

Step 4: Roll Out the Pie Dough

After chilling, take one dough disc out of the refrigerator. Roll it out on a lightly floured surface into a circle roughly 12 inches in diameter. Carefully lift the dough and lay it into a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently against the edges and trimming any excess overhang. Return the prepared pie crust to the fridge while you make the lattice or top crust.

For the second dough disc, roll it out into another circle; this will be used to create your pie topping. You can opt for a traditional lattice pattern or cover the pie completely with a vented top crust—more on this below!

Step 5: Fill and Assemble the Pie

Pour the prepared strawberry rhubarb mixture into the chilled pie shell, spreading it evenly. Watch out for excess liquid; draining some juice can prevent a soggy crust during baking.

If you’re making a lattice top, cut the rolled-out dough into long strips. Lay the strips over the pie in a crisscross pattern, weaving them if desired. If you’re using a solid top crust, place it carefully over the filling, then cut small vents to allow steam to escape during baking.

Seal the edges of the pie by crimping or pressing them down with a fork, creating a decorative border.

Step 6: Add Egg Wash and Sprinkle Sugar

For a golden finish, mix the beaten egg with milk or cream and brush it generously over the top crust or lattice. Sprinkle coarse sugar on top to add texture and visual appeal.

Step 7: Baking the Pie

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the assembled pie on a baking sheet to catch any overflow from the bubbling filling. Bake the pie on the lower rack of your oven for about 20 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue baking for another 30–40 minutes.

The pie is ready when the filling is bubbling and the crust is deep golden brown. If the edges brown too quickly, cover them with foil while finishing the bake.

Step 8: Cool Before Serving

Patience is key—allow the pie to cool on a wire rack for at least 2–3 hours before serving. As tempting as it may be to slice into it immediately, cooling allows the filling to thicken and prevents it from spilling out.

Serving Suggestions

Strawberry rhubarb pie is heavenly on its own, but you can take it to the next level with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. Pair it with coffee or a refreshing glass of lemonade for a delightful dessert experience.

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

  1. Choose Your Rhubarb Wisely: Opt for crisp, firm stalks with no signs of wilting. Though rhubarb is typically bright red, don’t fret if yours is more green; the flavor will still be exceptional.

  2. Prevent Soggy Crusts: Drain excess juice from the filling if your strawberries are especially ripe or juicy. You can also sprinkle the bottom crust with a thin layer of breadcrumbs or ground nuts to absorb any liquid.

  3. Experiment with Spice: A hint of cinnamon or nutmeg can add warmth and complexity to the filling.

  4. Freeze Leftovers: Strawberry rhubarb pie freezes well! Wrap individual slices and store them in an airtight container for up to 3 months.

Final Thoughts

Strawberry rhubarb pie is more than just a dessert—it’s a celebration of fresh ingredients, vibrant flavors, and cherished culinary traditions. By following these steps, you can create a pie that is both stunning and delicious, impressing family and friends with each slice. Whether it’s your first attempt or part of your regular baking repertoire, this recipe will deliver delightful results every single time.

So grab your rolling pin, mix up some fresh produce, and bake a pie that’s sure to become a favorite in your home.
