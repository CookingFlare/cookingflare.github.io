Savory and Spicy Shrimp Fra Diavolo: A Fiery Pasta Dish


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and a little kick in your meals, then Shrimp Fra Diavolo is a dish that should be on your radar. This classic Italian-American recipe features succulent shrimp cooked in a fiery tomato-based sauce, served over a bed of al dente pasta. The combination of savory seafood, spicy sauce, and perfectly cooked pasta makes this dish a standout favorite for anyone who loves bold and vibrant flavors.

Ingredients

For this tantalizing Shrimp Fra Diavolo recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound of spaghetti or linguine
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

Begin by patting the shrimp dry with paper towels and seasoning them with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Once cooked, remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

Using the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the minced garlic and red pepper flakes for about 1 minute until fragrant. Be careful not to let the garlic brown. Pour in the white wine and allow it to simmer for 2-3 minutes until it reduces slightly. Then, add the crushed tomatoes to the skillet, season with salt and pepper, and let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: Cook the Pasta

While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta according to the package instructions until it reaches al dente perfection. Once cooked, drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta water.

Step 4: Combine and Serve

Add the cooked shrimp to the simmering sauce, allowing them to heat through for a couple of minutes. Then, gently toss the cooked pasta in the sauce, adding a little pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce and help it coat the pasta evenly.

Step 5: Garnish and Enjoy

Once the pasta and shrimp are well-coated in the fiery sauce, it’s time to serve. Divide the Shrimp Fra Diavolo among serving plates, garnish with fresh basil leaves, and perhaps a sprinkle of extra red pepper flakes for those who like it extra spicy.

Final Thoughts

Shrimp Fra Diavolo is a dish that truly packs a flavorful punch. The combination of succulent shrimp, spicy tomato sauce, and perfectly cooked pasta is a winning trio that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. Whether you’re preparing a special dinner for guests or simply treating yourself to a culinary adventure, this fiery pasta dish is sure to impress.

So, the next time you’re craving a meal with a bit of heat, give Shrimp Fra Diavolo a try. It’s a dish that brings together the richness of seafood and the boldness of spicy flavors in a way that’s simply irresistible. Buon appetito!
