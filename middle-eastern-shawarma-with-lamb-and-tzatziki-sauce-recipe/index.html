Middle Eastern Shawarma with Lamb and Tzatziki Sauce Recipe


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve probably encountered the tantalizing aroma and flavors of shawarma. This iconic dish, with its roots in the Levant region, is a true celebration of savory, aromatic spices and tender, juicy meat. While the classic shawarma is often made with chicken or beef, today we’re putting a unique spin on it by preparing a mouthwatering Middle Eastern Shawarma with succulent lamb and a refreshing tzatziki sauce. So, let’s dive into this culinary adventure and create a dish that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of the Middle East.

Ingredients:

For the Shawarma:

  • 2 pounds of boneless lamb shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For the Tzatziki Sauce:

  • 1 English cucumber, grated and squeezed to remove excess moisture
  • 1 1/2 cups of Greek yogurt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Warm pita bread
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Lamb: In a large bowl, combine the thinly sliced lamb, red onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, ground coriander, ground turmeric, ground cinnamon, cayenne pepper, Greek yogurt, olive oil, salt, and black pepper. Mix everything thoroughly, ensuring that the lamb is well coated with the aromatic spices. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Prepare the Tzatziki Sauce: While the lamb is marinating, take this time to prepare the refreshing tzatziki sauce. In a bowl, combine the grated cucumber, Greek yogurt, minced garlic, lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, chopped fresh dill, salt, and black pepper. Stir the ingredients together until well combined. Cover the bowl and refrigerate until ready to serve.

  3. Cook the Lamb: Preheat your grill or broiler to medium-high heat. Once the lamb has marinated, thread the slices onto skewers. Grill or broil the lamb for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until cooked to your desired doneness. Remove the lamb from the skewers and let it rest for a few minutes.

  4. Assemble the Shawarma: Warm the pita bread and place a generous amount of the grilled lamb on each piece. Top the lamb with sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and a dollop of the prepared tzatziki sauce. Sprinkle some freshly chopped parsley over the top and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Enjoy the Middle Eastern Shawarma with Lamb and Tzatziki Sauce!

This Middle Eastern Shawarma with lamb and tzatziki sauce is a culinary masterpiece that brings together the robust flavors of the Middle East in every bite. The tender, spiced lamb, paired with the cool, creamy tzatziki sauce, creates a harmony of textures and tastes that is truly irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the exotic, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy.

So, gather your ingredients, embrace the aromatic spices, and embark on a flavorful journey to the heart of the Middle East with this delightful shawarma recipe. Your taste buds will thank you for the adventure!
