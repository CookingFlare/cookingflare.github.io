A Delightful Korean Street Food: Hotteok Recipe


If you’ve ever strolled through the bustling streets of Seoul, you’ve likely encountered the mouthwatering aroma of hotteok wafting through the air. This delectable Korean street food is a delightful treat that combines a fluffy dough with a sweet and gooey filling. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the world of hotteok and learn how to recreate this popular snack in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make hotteok, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the filling:

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or peanuts
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon powder

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Gradually add the warm water while stirring the mixture. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let it rest for about an hour, or until the dough doubles in size.

  2. While the dough is rising, prepare the filling. In a separate bowl, mix together the brown sugar, chopped walnuts or peanuts, sesame seeds, and cinnamon powder. Set aside.

  3. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Divide the dough into equal-sized portions and roll each portion into a ball.

  4. Take one ball of dough and flatten it with your hands. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of the dough circle. Gather the edges of the dough and pinch them together to seal the filling inside. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.

  5. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add the vegetable oil. Place a filled dough ball in the pan and gently flatten it with a spatula. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden brown. Flip the hotteok and cook the other side until it becomes crispy and golden brown as well.

  6. Remove the hotteok from the pan and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. Repeat the cooking process for the remaining dough balls.

  7. Serve the hotteok warm and enjoy the delightful combination of the crispy exterior and the sweet, gooey filling.

Variations

Hotteok can be customized to suit your taste preferences. Here are a few variations you can try:

  • Nutella Hotteok: Replace the traditional filling with Nutella for a chocolatey twist.
  • Cheese Hotteok: Add a slice of cheese in the filling for a gooey and savory surprise.
  • Matcha Hotteok: Mix matcha powder into the dough for a unique flavor and vibrant green color.

Conclusion

Hotteok is a beloved Korean street food that offers a delightful combination of textures and flavors. With this recipe, you can recreate the magic of hotteok in your own kitchen. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or experiment with different fillings, hotteok is sure to satisfy your cravings for a sweet and indulgent treat. So go ahead, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure to experience the wonders of hotteok.
