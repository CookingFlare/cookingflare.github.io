Spanish Seafood Recipes: 10 Dishes from the Mediterranean Coast


Spain’s Mediterranean coast is a culinary paradise, where the sun-kissed shores meet the fresh bounty of the sea. From Valencia to Catalonia and along the balmy shores of the Costa Brava, Spanish seafood recipes reflect the region’s rich maritime heritage and passion for fresh ingredients. The Mediterranean diet, with its emphasis on fresh fish, seafood, and vibrant vegetables, has long been celebrated for its flavor, variety, and health benefits. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a home cook, these 10 Spanish seafood recipes will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Spain.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best Mediterranean seafood dishes from Spain. Each dish is a celebration of the region’s coastal flavors, blending local ingredients with time-honored techniques to create unforgettable meals.

1. Paella de Mariscos (Seafood Paella)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 cup Arborio rice or Paella rice
  • 3 cups seafood stock
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g mussels, scrubbed
  • 200g squid, sliced into rings
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the bell pepper and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Stir in the chopped tomato and paprika, letting it cook for 5 minutes to soften.
  3. Add the rice to the pan, stirring well to coat it in the oils and spices. Pour in the wine and let it reduce for a minute.
  4. Add the seafood stock (and saffron, if using), bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Let the rice simmer for 10-12 minutes.
  5. Add the shrimp, mussels, and squid to the pan, arranging them evenly. Cover the pan with a lid or foil and cook for another 10 minutes, or until the seafood is cooked through and the rice is tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for a burst of citrus.

Paella is one of Spain’s most iconic dishes, and the seafood version, or “paella de mariscos,” is a favorite among both locals and tourists. The secret to a perfect paella lies in the balance of flavors, the right rice, and the freshest seafood available.

2. Gambas a la Plancha (Grilled Shrimp)

Ingredients:

  • 500g large shrimp, shells on
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Sea salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, lemon juice, smoked paprika, and sea salt.
  3. Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and slightly charred.
  4. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve with extra lemon wedges.

Grilled shrimp, or “gambas a la plancha,” is a simple yet flavorful Spanish seafood dish. The smoky char from grilling combined with the tangy citrus and paprika creates a perfect balance of flavors. This dish is often enjoyed as a tapa or appetizer during warm Mediterranean evenings.

3. Calamares a la Romana (Fried Squid Rings)

Ingredients:

  • 500g squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for frying
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the flour, paprika, salt, and pepper. Toss the squid rings in the seasoned flour until well coated.
  2. Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry the squid rings in batches for about 2-3 minutes, or until golden and crispy.
  4. Drain on paper towels and serve immediately with lemon wedges.

“Calamares a la romana” is a beloved Spanish tapas dish that features tender squid encased in a crispy, golden batter. Served with a squeeze of lemon, these fried squid rings are a perfect snack for any occasion.

4. Suquet de Peix (Catalan Fish Stew)

Ingredients:

  • 600g mixed fish fillets (such as cod, monkfish, or bass)
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 cups fish stock
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes and paprika, cooking for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down.
  3. Pour in the white wine and let it reduce by half. Add the fish stock and bring to a boil.
  4. Add the cubed potatoes and cook for 10-12 minutes, until the potatoes are almost tender.
  5. Add the fish fillets and shrimp, simmering for another 5-7 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

Suquet de peix is a hearty fish stew that hails from Catalonia. This dish is typically served with rustic bread and is perfect for colder months or any time you’re craving a comforting, flavorful meal.

5. Arroz Negro (Black Rice with Squid and Squid Ink)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 300g squid, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp squid ink
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups fish stock
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking until softened.
  2. Add the squid and smoked paprika, cooking for 2-3 minutes until the squid starts to release its juices.
  3. Stir in the squid ink and rice, coating the rice evenly with the ink and oil.
  4. Add the white wine, letting it cook off for a minute. Then add the fish stock, bringing it to a simmer.
  5. Cover the pan and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and serve with lemon wedges for a burst of citrus.

Arroz negro is a strikingly beautiful dish with its black rice, rich with the flavors of squid ink. This dish embodies the essence of Spain’s coastal cuisine, showcasing the depth of the sea’s flavors in every bite.

6. Piquillos Rellenos de Marisco (Piquillo Peppers Stuffed with Seafood)

Ingredients:

  • 12 piquillo peppers, jarred or fresh
  • 300g crab meat or mixed seafood (shrimp, scallops, etc.)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp brandy (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the seafood, cooking until just opaque. If using brandy, carefully add it to the pan, allowing it to cook off.
  3. Stir in the cream and let it thicken slightly. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff the piquillo peppers with the seafood mixture, arranging them in a serving dish.
  5. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve.

Piquillo peppers stuffed with seafood make for an elegant appetizer or tapa. The sweet and smoky piquillo peppers pair wonderfully with the creamy seafood filling, offering a luxurious bite that highlights Spain’s exceptional seafood.

7. Ensalada de Mariscos (Seafood Salad)

Ingredients:

  • 200g cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g cooked squid, sliced into rings
  • 100g mussels, cooked and shelled
  • 1/2 red onion, finely sliced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shrimp, squid, mussels, onion, cucumber, and bell pepper.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil, vinegar, and lemon juice. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Ensalada de mariscos is a refreshing and light dish that’s perfect for a summer day. The combination of tender seafood and crisp vegetables is dressed in a tangy vinaigrette, making it an ideal starter or light main course.

8. Chipirones Rellenos (Stuffed Baby Squid)

Ingredients:

  • 12 baby squid, cleaned and tentacles removed
  • 200g breadcrumbs
  • 100g cooked crab meat
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a bowl, mix the breadcrumbs, crab meat, garlic, and parsley. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff each baby squid with the breadcrumb mixture and secure the openings with toothpicks.
  4. Heat olive oil in a pan and sear the stuffed squid for 2-3 minutes on each side.
  5. Transfer to the oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, until cooked through.

Chipirones rellenos are a delicious treat, where tender baby squid is stuffed with a flavorful crab and breadcrumb filling. These stuffed squid are often served as a tapa, bringing rich, savory flavors to the table.

9. Tortilla de Camarones (Shrimp Omelette)

Ingredients:

  • 200g small shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 bell pepper, diced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, cooking until soft.
  2. Add the shrimp, cooking for 2-3 minutes until pink and opaque. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a bowl, whisk the eggs and pour them into the pan with the shrimp. Cook over low heat, stirring gently to create a soft omelette texture.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

Tortilla de camarones is a Spanish-style shrimp omelette that showcases the tender, flavorful shrimp paired with the richness of eggs and vegetables. It’s a simple yet satisfying dish that makes for a wonderful brunch or light meal.

10. Mariscos a la Marinera (Seafood in Marinera Sauce)

Ingredients:

  • 500g mixed seafood (mussels, clams, squid, shrimp)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 cup seafood broth
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and onion, cooking until softened.
  2. Add the mixed seafood, white wine, and seafood broth, bringing it to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, cooking until the seafood is cooked through.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

Mariscos a la marinera is a classic Spanish dish that combines a variety of seafood in a rich, flavorful marinera sauce. This dish highlights the natural sweetness of the seafood, making it a must-try for seafood lovers.

These 10 Spanish seafood recipes bring the flavors of the Mediterranean coast right to your kitchen. Whether you’re enjoying a paella with friends, savoring grilled shrimp, or indulging in a seafood stew, these dishes celebrate Spain’s deep connection to the sea and its culinary traditions. By using fresh, local ingredients and time-honored techniques, you can recreate the vibrant flavors of Spain’s coastal cuisine in your own home. So, next time you want to bring a taste of the Mediterranean to your table, look no further than these delicious Spanish seafood recipes.
