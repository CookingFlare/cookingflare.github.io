Homemade Courgette (Zucchini) Balls Greek-Style


Courgette balls, known for their light, crispy exterior and savory interior, are a delicious dish that can easily be prepared at home with simple ingredients. This homemade courgette balls recipe, inspired by Greek cuisine, brings a fresh and flavorful twist to your dinner table. Made with grated zucchini, fragrant herbs, and a touch of feta cheese, these Greek-style courgette balls are sure to become a family favorite.

Whether you serve them as a snack, appetizer, or part of a larger meal, courgette balls offer a healthy, vegetarian alternative that’s both satisfying and full of flavor. Their versatility means you can enjoy them warm, cold, or reheated the next day.

In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps of making these delightful Greek-style courgette balls from scratch. Let’s dive into the ingredients and the step-by-step process!

Ingredients for Homemade Courgette Balls (Greek-Style)

To make homemade courgette balls Greek-style, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 medium courgettes (zucchini), grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ cup fresh dill, chopped (optional, but adds great flavor)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (preferably whole wheat for a healthier option)
  • ½ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (plus extra for frying)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon paprika (optional, for a subtle smoky flavor)

Tips on Ingredients:

  • Zucchini (Courgette): Choose firm, fresh zucchinis for the best texture. It’s essential to grate the zucchini finely to ensure it incorporates well with the other ingredients.
  • Feta Cheese: This salty, tangy cheese is a signature element of Greek cuisine. It adds richness and depth of flavor to the courgette balls.
  • Fresh Herbs: The combination of parsley and dill is quintessential in Greek cooking, offering a fresh and aromatic flavor that complements the zucchini perfectly.
  • Breadcrumbs: Breadcrumbs help to bind the mixture together, making the courgette balls easier to form and fry.

Preparing the Zucchini (Courgette)

The first step in making courgette balls is preparing the zucchini. Zucchini contains a lot of water, which can make the balls soggy if not handled properly.

  1. Grate the Courgette: Using a box grater, grate the zucchini into fine shreds. This helps to evenly distribute the zucchini throughout the courgette ball mixture.

  2. Remove Excess Moisture: Place the grated zucchini in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. This is crucial because excess moisture will prevent the mixture from holding together and make frying difficult. You want the grated zucchini to be as dry as possible.

Preparing the Mixture

  1. Combine the Vegetables and Herbs: In a large bowl, mix the grated zucchini, chopped onion, minced garlic, fresh parsley, and fresh dill. The zucchini should be the star of the dish, but the herbs will bring a burst of freshness that gives the courgette balls their signature flavor.

  2. Add Cheese and Seasonings: Stir in the crumbled feta cheese, dried oregano, salt, pepper, and paprika. The feta cheese adds a rich, tangy flavor that complements the zucchini and herbs. Don’t forget to adjust the seasoning based on your personal taste preferences.

  3. Add Eggs and Breadcrumbs: Beat the eggs in a small bowl and add them to the zucchini mixture. The eggs act as a binder, helping to hold the courgette balls together while frying. Next, add the breadcrumbs to the mixture. The breadcrumbs help absorb any remaining moisture from the zucchini and give the balls their crisp texture once fried.

  4. Mix Everything Together: Using your hands or a spoon, mix all the ingredients together until fully combined. The mixture should be thick enough to hold its shape when you form it into balls. If it feels too wet, add more breadcrumbs a little at a time until the desired consistency is achieved.

Shaping the Courgette Balls

Once the mixture is well combined, it’s time to shape the courgette balls.

  1. Form the Balls: Wet your hands with a little water to prevent the mixture from sticking to your fingers. Take a small portion of the mixture and roll it into a ball, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You can also make them slightly larger if you prefer bigger bites. Aim for uniform sizes so they cook evenly.

  2. Arrange on a Tray: Place the formed courgette balls on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. This step is important for keeping everything organized and preventing the balls from sticking together.

Cooking the Courgette Balls

There are two main ways to cook these courgette balls: frying or baking. Both methods yield delicious results, but frying gives them an extra crispy texture, while baking is a healthier option.

1. Frying

Frying courgette balls gives them a deliciously crispy exterior while keeping the inside tender. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Heat the Oil: Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. You’ll want enough oil to cover the bottom of the pan, but the balls should not be submerged in oil. A few tablespoons should be sufficient.

  2. Fry the Balls: Once the oil is hot, carefully place the courgette balls in the pan, making sure they don’t overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary. Cook each side for about 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.

  3. Drain Excess Oil: Once the courgette balls are golden and crispy, use a slotted spoon to remove them from the pan. Place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

2. Baking (Healthier Option)

If you prefer a healthier alternative, you can bake the courgette balls instead of frying them. Here’s how:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Prepare the Baking Tray: Line a baking tray with parchment paper and lightly coat it with olive oil or cooking spray.

  3. Place the Balls on the Tray: Arrange the courgette balls in a single layer on the baking tray. Lightly drizzle or spray a bit of olive oil on top of the balls to help them crisp up while baking.

  4. Bake: Bake the courgette balls in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, flipping them halfway through for even cooking. They should turn golden brown and crisp on the outside.

Serving Your Greek-Style Courgette Balls

Once your homemade courgette balls are cooked, it’s time to serve them! They can be enjoyed in many different ways.

  • With a Yogurt Dip: Greek yogurt makes an excellent accompaniment to courgette balls. Simply mix some plain Greek yogurt with a drizzle of olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt for a quick and refreshing dip.

  • On a Salad: These courgette balls can be served on a bed of fresh greens, making for a light yet satisfying meal. Add some olives, cherry tomatoes, and a drizzle of olive oil for a Greek-inspired salad.

  • As Part of a Meze Platter: In Greece, a meze platter is a collection of small dishes served as appetizers. Courgette balls are a perfect addition to any meze spread alongside other popular Greek dishes like hummus, tzatziki, and spanakopita.

  • As a Snack: These courgette balls are perfect for an afternoon snack or a light appetizer. They can be enjoyed warm or cold, making them an ideal dish to prepare ahead of time.

Tips for Perfect Courgette Balls

  1. Ensure Proper Drainage: Zucchini can release a lot of water, so it’s crucial to squeeze out as much moisture as possible to prevent sogginess.

  2. Use Fresh Herbs: Fresh herbs give the balls a vibrant and aromatic flavor. Don’t substitute with dried herbs if you can avoid it!

  3. Make Ahead: You can prepare the courgette ball mixture ahead of time and refrigerate it for up to 24 hours before frying or baking. This allows the flavors to meld and makes cooking easier when you’re ready to serve.

  4. Freezing: If you want to make a large batch, you can freeze the uncooked courgette balls. Arrange them in a single layer on a baking sheet and freeze until solid. Then transfer them to a freezer-safe bag or container. When ready to cook, bake or fry them directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

Why You’ll Love Greek-Style Courgette Balls

These homemade courgette balls are packed with flavor and texture. The fresh herbs, feta cheese, and zucchini combine to create a dish that is light yet satisfying. Whether you fry or bake them, these courgette balls are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, offering the perfect balance of flavors. They are also easy to customize with your favorite ingredients, such as adding a little chili for some heat or using other herbs like mint or basil.

Not only are these Greek-style courgette balls delicious, but they also offer a healthy alternative to traditional meatballs. With zucchini as the main ingredient, they are packed with nutrients, fiber, and antioxidants. Whether you’re looking for a vegetarian meal, a healthy appetizer, or a flavorful side dish, these courgette balls are a perfect choice.

Conclusion

Homemade courgette balls Greek-style are a wonderful addition to any meal. With fresh zucchini, aromatic herbs, and the tangy richness of feta cheese, they bring a taste of Greece to your kitchen. Whether you choose to fry or bake them, these courgette balls are sure to impress with their deliciously crispy exterior and flavorful, tender inside.

Perfect for serving as an appetizer, snack, or part of a larger meal, these Greek-style courgette balls are sure to be a hit with everyone at the table. Enjoy the taste of Greece and indulge in this delightful, healthy, and flavorful dish today!
