10 Spanish Dishes You Can Make in Under 30 Minutes


Spanish cuisine is celebrated worldwide for its vibrant flavors, simple preparation techniques, and use of fresh, wholesome ingredients. If you’ve ever wanted to recreate these culinary delights in the comfort of your own kitchen, you’re in luck. Here are ten authentic Spanish dishes that you can whip up in less than 30 minutes—perfect for busy weeknights or a last-minute dinner party with friends.

1. Pan con Tomate (Tomato Bread)

Pan con tomate is a classic Spanish dish that embodies the essence of simplicity. To prepare it, start by slicing crusty bread like ciabatta or baguette and lightly toasting it on the grill or in a pan. Cut a ripe tomato in half and rub it directly onto the warm bread slices. Drizzle the bread with high-quality olive oil, sprinkle with sea salt, and—if you’re feeling adventurous—add some garlic for extra flavor. Serve as an appetizer or alongside cured meats and cheeses.

2. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

Spanish omelette is a go-to comfort food that can be prepared quickly yet feels indulgent. Thinly slice potatoes and onions, and sauté them in olive oil until soft. In a bowl, beat eggs, then gently mix in the cooked potatoes and onions. Pour the mixture back into the skillet and cook over medium heat until the edges set. Flip the tortilla (using a plate or lid) to cook the other side. In under 30 minutes, you’ll have a satisfying tortilla that pairs beautifully with a fresh salad or crusty bread.

3. Patatas Bravas

Patatas bravas are a favorite Spanish tapas dish. Start by dicing potatoes into bite-sized cubes and frying them in olive oil until golden and crispy. For the sauce, stir together tomato paste, garlic, smoky paprika, and a splash of vinegar. Drizzle the spicy, zesty sauce over the fried potatoes and serve warm. You’ll have a dish that’s packed with flavor in hardly any time at all.

4. Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

If you’re a seafood lover, gambas al ajillo is the dish for you. Heat olive oil in a pan and add finely sliced garlic, chili flakes, and raw peeled shrimp. Sauté until the shrimp turns pink and tender, then finish with fresh parsley and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve with toasted bread to mop up the garlicky oil—it’s a simple yet indulgent plate that’s ready in minutes.

5. Gazpacho

This chilled soup is a refreshing option for warmer days, and it requires no cooking at all. Blend ripe tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, garlic, olive oil, and a touch of vinegar until smooth. Season with salt and pepper, then chill in the refrigerator before serving. Gazpacho is a healthy and hydrating dish that showcases the natural flavors of Spain’s farm-fresh produce.

6. Huevos Rotos (Broken Eggs)

Huevos rotos is rustic comfort food at its finest—and remarkably easy to prepare. Fry potatoes until crispy and golden, then layer them on a serving dish. Top the potatoes with fried eggs, ensuring that the yolks remain runny. Tear apart slices of Iberian ham, chorizo, or even sautéed mushrooms and scatter them over the eggs. Let guests dig in immediately, breaking the yolk over the potatoes for a rich, hearty experience.

7. Ensalada de Pulpo (Octopus Salad)

This healthy seafood salad requires only minimal prep. If you have pre-cooked octopus, simply chop it into bite-sized pieces and toss with olive oil, lemon juice, parsley, and smoked paprika. Add diced potatoes or mixed greens for extra substance. Ensalada de pulpo can be served warm or chilled, making it highly versatile for any occasion.

8. Chorizo al Vino (Chorizo in Red Wine)

Chorizo al vino is one dish that will have everyone licking their plates clean—and it takes just minutes to make. Slice Spanish chorizo into chunks and simmer them in red wine alongside garlic and bay leaves. The chorizo absorbs the wine’s rich flavors while releasing its smoky aroma. You can serve it on its own or pair it with bread or rice for a more filling meal.

9. Pisto (Spanish Ratatouille)

Pisto is Spain’s colorful take on ratatouille. Dice zucchini, eggplant, onion, bell peppers, and tomatoes into small chunks. Sauté everything in olive oil until tender and seasoned with salt, pepper, and oregano. Serve as a side dish, over toast, or topped with a fried egg for an extra twist. Pisto is quick, healthy, and incredibly versatile.

10. Boquerones en Vinagre (Marinated Anchovies)

This no-cook dish is all about letting fresh ingredients shine. Arrange cleaned fresh anchovies in a dish and cover them with white wine vinegar and water. Let them marinate for 20-30 minutes, then drain and transfer to a platter. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with chopped garlic and parsley, and serve alongside bread for dipping. Boquerones en vinagre are light, flavorful, and ideal for entertaining.

Spanish cuisine proves that you don’t need to spend hours in the kitchen to create dishes that are bursting with flavor. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a quick culinary getaway, these ten recipes bring the spirit of Spain right to your table—without the wait.
