Sous Vide Lemon Dill Salmon: A Fresh and Citrusy Dish


When it comes to cooking salmon, the sous vide method is a game-changer. It allows you to achieve the perfect level of doneness while infusing the fish with incredible flavors. One of my favorite sous vide salmon recipes is the Sous Vide Lemon Dill Salmon – a dish that’s not only fresh and citrusy but also incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
  • Fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Salmon

Start by preparing the salmon fillets. Pat them dry with paper towels and season both sides with salt and pepper. This simple seasoning will enhance the natural flavor of the salmon and prepare it for the infusion of lemon and dill.

Step 2: Flavor Infusion

Now, it’s time to infuse the salmon with the bright flavors of lemon and dill. Take the thinly sliced lemon and place a few slices on top of each salmon fillet. Then, sprinkle a generous amount of fresh dill over the lemon slices. The combination of lemon and dill will impart a refreshing and aromatic essence to the salmon, making each bite a burst of fresh citrusy goodness.

Step 3: Vacuum Sealing

Once the salmon fillets are seasoned and infused with lemon and dill, it’s time to seal them in a vacuum-sealed bag. This step is crucial for the sous vide method, as it ensures that the flavors are locked in and the salmon cooks evenly. Carefully place each fillet in a separate vacuum-sealed bag, drizzle a touch of olive oil over the top, and then seal the bags, removing as much air as possible.

Step 4: Sous Vide Cooking

Now, it’s time for the magic of sous vide cooking. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 125°F (52°C) for a tender and moist result. Once the water reaches the desired temperature, gently lower the sealed salmon fillets into the water bath and let them cook for 45 minutes. This precise cooking method ensures that the salmon is perfectly cooked through while preserving its delicate texture and flavors.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

After 45 minutes, carefully remove the salmon fillets from the water bath. Gently open the vacuum-sealed bags and transfer the salmon to a serving platter. The salmon will be perfectly cooked and infused with the vibrant flavors of lemon and dill. For an extra pop of freshness, you can garnish the salmon with a sprinkle of freshly chopped dill and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Step 6: Serving

Now that your Sous Vide Lemon Dill Salmon is perfectly cooked and beautifully infused with citrusy flavors, it’s time to serve and enjoy. This dish pairs wonderfully with a side of steamed vegetables or a light salad, allowing the fresh and citrusy flavors of the salmon to take center stage.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Lemon Dill Salmon is a delightful dish that celebrates the natural flavors of salmon while infusing it with the bright and aromatic essence of lemon and dill. The sous vide method ensures that the salmon is perfectly cooked, tender, and bursting with citrusy freshness. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a light and flavorful meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So, fire up your sous vide machine, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to the delightful experience of Sous Vide Lemon Dill Salmon.
