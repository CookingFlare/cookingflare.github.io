Pumpkin Spice Everything: 7 Halloween Treats to Bake This Fall


Fall is synonymous with cozy sweaters, crisp air, and the irresistible aroma of pumpkin spice wafting through kitchens everywhere. If you’re ready to embrace the season fully, why not transform your home into a haven of warming flavors with some delightful Halloween-themed pumpkin spice treats? From classic pumpkin pies with a twist to inventive snacks that bring in just the right hint of autumn, this collection of seven baking recipes celebrates everything pumpkin spice. Each one promises to fill your home with fall magic and satisfy those seasonal cravings.

1. Classic Pumpkin Spice Muffins with a Spiced Sugar Crust

Muffins are a staple for breakfast or an anytime snack, and pumpkin spice infused ones are the ultimate fall treat. This recipe layers tender crumb with a crispy spiced sugar topping that makes every bite a perfect balance between soft and crunchy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Spiced Sugar Topping

  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, pumpkin pie spice, and cinnamon.
  3. In a separate large bowl, mix pumpkin puree, brown sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Gently combine the wet ingredients with the dry until just incorporated. Avoid overmixing.
  5. Fill each muffin cup about two-thirds full.
  6. Mix the sugar and pumpkin pie spice and sprinkle liberally over the tops.
  7. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool on a wire rack and enjoy with a hot cup of cider.

2. Pumpkin Spice Cheesecake Brownies

If you can’t decide between pumpkin pie and rich, chocolatey brownies, this recipe merges both into a single sumptuous treat. Creamy pumpkin cheesecake swirls weave through dense chocolate brownies, all accentuated with aromatic pumpkin spice.

Ingredients

Brownie Base

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Pumpkin Cheesecake Swirl

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Grease or line a 9x13-inch pan with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the brownie base: mix melted butter and sugar, then whisk in eggs and vanilla. Sift cocoa, flour, and salt into the batter and stir gently.
  3. For the cheesecake swirl, blend cream cheese, pumpkin puree, sugar, egg, and pumpkin spice until smooth.
  4. Pour half of the brownie batter into the pan, then add the cheesecake mixture in dollops. Top with remaining brownie batter.
  5. Use a knife to swirl the layers gently, creating marbled effects.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick inserted near the center has a few moist crumbs.
  7. Cool completely before slicing for the best flavor and texture.

3. Pumpkin Spice Snickerdoodles

Snickerdoodles are beloved for their crackly exterior and tender center rolled in cinnamon sugar. Giving them a pumpkin spice makeover unites classic comfort with seasonal flair.

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons pumpkin puree

Cinnamon-Sugar Mixture

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Whisk together flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.
  3. In a large bowl, beat butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then blend in pumpkin puree.
  4. Gradually incorporate dry ingredients until dough forms. Chill dough for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Combine sugar, cinnamon, and pumpkin pie spice for the coating.
  6. Roll dough into 1-inch balls, then coat in cinnamon sugar mixture. Place on baking sheet 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 10–12 minutes, edges set but centers soft. Let cool on wire racks.

4. Spiced Pumpkin Oatmeal Cookies with White Chocolate Chips

For a cookie with wholesome oats, just the right amount of pumpkin, and bursts of sweet white chocolate, this recipe caters to those who want balanced nutrition and indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup white chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment.
  2. Mix oats, flour, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice in a bowl.
  3. Cream butter and sugars until light and fluffy. Add egg, pumpkin puree, and vanilla; blend well.
  4. Stir dry ingredients into wet, then fold in white chocolate chips.
  5. Drop by rounded tablespoons onto the baking sheets.
  6. Bake 12–14 minutes or until edges are golden. Cool on wire racks.

5. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes with Maple Butter

Why limit pumpkin spice treats to baked goods? Embrace it at breakfast with these fluffy pancakes laced with pumpkin spices, perfect for weekend mornings or holiday brunches.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • Maple Butter (softened butter mixed with maple syrup and a pinch of cinnamon)

Instructions

  1. Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and pumpkin spice in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, combine milk, pumpkin puree, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir until just combined.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form and edges look set, then flip and cook 1–2 minutes more.
  6. Serve warm with maple butter spread and extra syrup on the side.

6. Pumpkin Spice Whoopie Pies with Cream Cheese Filling

These pillowy little sandwich cakes are both fun to make and eat. The mellow pumpkin spice flavor and creamy tangy filling promise an unforgettable bite.

Ingredients

Cakes

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Filling

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.
  3. Cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in egg, pumpkin puree, and vanilla.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients to wet and mix until combined.
  5. Drop batter by rounded tablespoons onto baking sheets (about 12–14 per sheet).
  6. Bake 12–15 minutes until crowns spring back lightly. Cool on wire racks.
  7. For filling, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually mix in powdered sugar and vanilla until fluffy.
  8. Spread filling on the flat side of a cake, sandwich with another. Chill before serving for easier slicing.

7. Pumpkin Spice Bread Pudding with Caramel Drizzle

For those who want a show-stopping dessert or a special brunch centerpiece, this pumpkin spiced bread pudding combines custardy richness with crisp edges and a luscious caramel sauce.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups cubed day-old bread (brioche or challah recommended)
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup pumpkin puree
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Caramel Drizzle

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Toss bread cubes into the baking dish evenly.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together milk, cream, pumpkin puree, eggs, brown sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla.
  4. Pour custard mixture over bread, pressing gently to soak all pieces. Let rest for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes until custard is set and top is golden brown.
  6. For caramel, heat sugar over medium heat in a dry pan, stirring occasionally until melted and amber. Add butter, stir until combined, then slowly whisk in cream. Cool slightly.
  7. Drizzle warm caramel sauce generously over warm bread pudding before serving.

Final Thoughts

The richness of pumpkin combined with the warm hug of spice is the ultimate hallmark of autumn. These seven recipes range from simple and quick to special and indulgent, so you can enjoy pumpkin spice in every way imaginable this Halloween and throughout the fall season. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or savoring a quiet moment in your kitchen, these treats bring comfort, joy, and the essence of fall into every bite.

So, dust off your mixing bowls, light a cinnamon-scented candle, and start baking your way through the pumpkin spice everything experience this year. Happy baking and happy fall!
