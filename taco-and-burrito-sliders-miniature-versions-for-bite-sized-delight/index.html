Taco and Burrito Sliders: Miniature Versions for Bite-Sized Delight


If you’re a fan of tacos and burritos, then you’re in for a treat with these delightful taco and burrito sliders! These miniature versions pack all the flavors of the beloved Mexican dishes into bite-sized portions, making them perfect for parties, gatherings, or simply as a fun twist on taco Tuesday. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through how to create these delicious sliders from scratch, including the fillings, toppings, and a couple of creative variations to suit different tastes.

Ingredients

For the Slider Buns:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour

For the Taco Filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Burrito Filling:

  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toppings:

  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Sliced jalapeños
  • Shredded cheese
  • Sour cream
  • Guacamole

Instructions

Making the Slider Buns:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a separate large bowl, mix together the sugar, butter, egg, and salt. Add the yeast mixture and flour, and stir until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 6-8 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour until it doubles in size.
  4. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into small portions, shaping them into slider-sized buns. Place them on a baking sheet and let them rise for another 15-20 minutes. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown.

Preparing the Taco Filling:

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the ground meat and onions until the meat is no longer pink. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Stir in the taco seasoning, salsa, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together.

Preparing the Burrito Filling:

  1. In a separate skillet, combine the cooked rice, black beans, corn, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes until heated through.

Assembling the Sliders:

  1. Slice the slider buns in half and layer the bottom halves with the taco filling or burrito filling.
  2. Top with your choice of toppings such as shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, sliced jalapeños, shredded cheese, sour cream, and guacamole.
  3. Place the top half of the slider bun and secure with a toothpick.

Creative Variations:

  • For a vegetarian option, swap the ground meat in the taco filling with sautéed bell peppers and onions.
  • Add a drizzle of hot sauce or a sprinkle of chopped cilantro for an extra kick of flavor.

Final Thoughts

These taco and burrito sliders are not only a fun and creative twist on traditional Mexican dishes, but they also make for a visually appealing and delicious addition to any gathering. The best part is that you can customize the fillings and toppings to suit your taste, making them versatile and crowd-pleasing. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply craving a bite-sized delight, these sliders are sure to impress and satisfy. So, gather your ingredients, get creative, and enjoy the flavorful journey of making and savoring these mini delights!
