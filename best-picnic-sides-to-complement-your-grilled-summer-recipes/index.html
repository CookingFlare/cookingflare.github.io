Best Picnic Sides to Complement Your Grilled Summer Recipes


As the sun shines brighter and the days grow longer, summer beckons us to embrace the great outdoors. What better way to celebrate the season than with a delightful picnic? Grilled recipes are often the star of the show, but the right sides can elevate your outdoor dining experience to new heights. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of picnic sides that perfectly complement your grilled summer recipes, ensuring your gathering is both delicious and memorable.

1. Classic Potato Salad

No picnic is complete without a classic potato salad. This creamy dish is a staple that pairs beautifully with grilled meats. To make a traditional potato salad, start with:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of baby potatoes
  • 1 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup of diced celery
  • 1/2 cup of chopped red onion
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the baby potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Once the potatoes are cool, cut them into quarters and add them to the bowl along with the celery and red onion.
  4. Gently fold the ingredients together until well combined. Garnish with fresh dill before serving.

This potato salad is creamy, tangy, and a perfect match for grilled chicken or burgers.

2. Refreshing Cucumber Salad

A refreshing cucumber salad is a light and crisp side that balances the richness of grilled foods. It’s easy to prepare and can be made ahead of time.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of sliced cucumbers
  • 1/2 cup of red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cucumbers, red onion, and dill.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cucumber mixture and toss to coat.
  4. Let the salad sit for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

This salad is not only refreshing but also adds a vibrant color to your picnic spread.

3. Grilled Corn on the Cob

Grilled corn on the cob is a summer favorite that’s easy to prepare and incredibly delicious. The smoky flavor from the grill enhances the natural sweetness of the corn.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup of butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: grated Parmesan cheese, chili powder, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the husked corn directly on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until charred and tender.
  3. Remove from the grill and brush with melted butter. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. For an extra kick, sprinkle with Parmesan cheese and chili powder, and serve with lime wedges.

Grilled corn is a fun and interactive side that everyone can enjoy.

4. Caprese Skewers

Caprese skewers are a delightful and visually appealing side that combines fresh ingredients. They are easy to assemble and perfect for picnics.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint of cherry tomatoes
  • 8 ounces of fresh mozzarella balls
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers or toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. On each skewer, thread a cherry tomato, a basil leaf, and a mozzarella ball. Repeat until the skewer is filled.
  2. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and sprinkle with salt and pepper before serving.

These skewers are not only delicious but also add a touch of elegance to your picnic.

5. Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

For a hearty and nutritious side, consider a quinoa salad with roasted vegetables. This dish is packed with flavor and can be served warm or cold.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of quinoa
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, carrots)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the mixed vegetables with olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Spread them on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes.
