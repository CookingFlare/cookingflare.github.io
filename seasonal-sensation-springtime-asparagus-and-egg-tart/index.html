Seasonal Sensation: Springtime Asparagus and Egg Tart


Spring is the time of year when fresh produce begins to make its appearance, and there’s no better way to showcase the season’s bounty than with a delightful Springtime Asparagus and Egg Tart. This savory dish is a celebration of the vibrant flavors of spring, featuring tender asparagus spears, creamy eggs, and a flaky pastry crust. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, preparing a light lunch, or simply looking for a dish to brighten up your dinner table, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Freshly chopped chives for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pastry:

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Gradually add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Asparagus:

    • Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the trimmed asparagus on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Roast for 10-12 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender but still crisp. Remove from the oven and let cool.

  3. Assemble the Tart:

    • Roll out the chilled pastry dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess. Prick the bottom of the pastry with a fork, then line with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Blind bake the crust for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  4. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a skillet, sauté the diced onion until soft and translucent. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and heavy cream. Stir in the sautéed onions, shredded Gruyère cheese, and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Assemble and Bake:

    • Arrange the roasted asparagus in the pre-baked tart crust. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the asparagus. Reduce the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C), then bake the tart for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Allow the tart to cool for a few minutes before slicing. Garnish with freshly chopped chives and serve warm or at room temperature.

Conclusion

This Springtime Asparagus and Egg Tart is a delightful way to embrace the flavors of the season. The tender asparagus, creamy egg filling, and buttery pastry crust come together to create a dish that is both elegant and comforting. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a taste of spring, this recipe is sure to become a seasonal favorite. So, gather the freshest asparagus you can find and let the flavors of spring shine in this delightful tart.
