Step-by-Step Guide to Baking French Baguettes at Home


If you’ve ever wanted to create your own French baguettes, now is the time to unleash your inner baker! Making your own bread at home is not only satisfying, but it can also save you a significant amount of money. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to make delicious French baguettes that rival even the finest bakeries.

Ingredients:

  • 500g bread flour
  • 100g all-purpose flour
  • 3g active dry yeast
  • 12g coarse salt
  • 360g water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Equipment:

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Stand mixer (optional)
  • Dough scraper
  • Proofing basket or banneton
  • Piping bag
  • Bench knife
  • Scale
  • Kitchen towel

Instructions:

  1. Start by combining the bread flour, all-purpose flour, yeast, and coarse salt in a large mixing bowl. You can also use a stand mixer and mix it on low-speed to save time.
  2. Next, add the water and olive oil, mixing to form a cohesive dough. If the dough seems too wet, add small amounts of additional flour to achieve a smoother consistency. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Use a dough scraper for this process to ensure that you don’t lift off too much flour.
  4. Place the kneaded dough in a clean bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Allow the dough to rest in a warm, draft-free area for about 2 hours or until it doubles in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, gently remove it from the bowl and transfer it onto a lightly floured surface. Divide the dough into three equal parts and round each piece of dough into a tight tvar.
  6. Place the dough rounds in the proofing basket or banneton, seam side up, and cover with a damp cloth. Again, let the dough rest for about 2 hours in a warm, draft-free area.
  7. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) with a baking stone or baking sheet inside. Prepare a piping bag with a 1/2-inch hole cut at the end.
  8. After the dough has finished proofing, remove it gently from the proofing basket and transfer it to a clean kitchen towel. Dust the towel lightly with flour.
  9. Using the piping bag, shape the baguette dough by running the bag over the dough and rolling it away from you. Transfer the shaped baguette to a parchment-lined baking sheet or directly onto the heated baking stone.
  10. Spray the baguette with water to help create that crispy, crusty exterior. Place a few drops of water on the baking stone as well to create steam during the baking process.
  11. Immediately after spraying the water, place the baking sheets or stone in the oven and close the door quickly.
  12. Bake for about 25 minutes, rotating the baguettes halfway through the baking time to ensure even cooking. The finished baguettes should be golden brown and sound hollow when tapped.
  13. Remove the baguettes from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack for about 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

In just a few hours of your time and with simple ingredients, you can create delicious French baguettes in the comfort of your own home. Impress your friends and family with these satisfying and versatile loaves that are perfect for sandwiches, appetizers, or served alongside your favorite dinner dishes.

Remember, practice makes perfect, so don’t be discouraged if your first batch doesn’t turn out exactly as you planned. With each attempt, you’ll become more experienced and develop your unique style of creating the perfect French baguette. Bon appétit!
