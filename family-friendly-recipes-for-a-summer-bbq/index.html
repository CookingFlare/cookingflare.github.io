Family-Friendly Recipes for a Summer BBQ


Summer is the perfect time to gather your loved ones and enjoy a fun and flavorful BBQ in the backyard. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a first-timer, hosting a summer BBQ is a great way to create lasting memories while enjoying delicious food. If you’re looking to impress your family and friends with a spread that’s both delicious and easy to prepare, this collection of family-friendly BBQ recipes is for you. From mouthwatering main dishes to refreshing sides and tempting desserts, these recipes will keep everyone satisfied and coming back for more.

1. Juicy Grilled Burgers

A classic BBQ isn’t complete without juicy, flavorful burgers. These burgers are easy to make, customizable, and perfect for grilling on a warm summer day. Here’s how to make the perfect grilled burger:

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (80% lean)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Toppings: lettuce, tomato, pickles, cheese, ketchup, mustard, mayo

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the ground beef with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined—don’t overwork the meat.
  3. Divide the mixture into four equal portions and shape them into patties.
  4. Place the patties on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  5. Toast the hamburger buns on the grill for about 1 minute, until they’re lightly crispy.
  6. Assemble the burgers by placing a patty on each bun and adding your favorite toppings.
  7. Serve with a side of crispy fries or a refreshing salad.

These burgers are not only easy to make but also versatile. Feel free to swap out the beef for turkey or veggie patties to cater to different dietary preferences.

2. Grilled Chicken Skewers

Grilled chicken skewers are a fantastic choice for a family BBQ. They’re flavorful, easy to eat, and can be marinated in a variety of ways to suit everyone’s tastes. Plus, they cook quickly, making them an excellent option for feeding a crowd.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, soy sauce, honey, lemon juice, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken onto the skewers, ensuring they’re evenly spaced.
  5. Grill the chicken skewers for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the chicken is fully cooked and reaches an internal temperature of 165°F.
  6. Serve with a fresh side salad or grilled vegetables for a complete meal.

You can easily customize these chicken skewers by adding vegetables like bell peppers, onions, or zucchini to the skewers. This recipe is a hit with both kids and adults, making it a perfect family-friendly option.

3. Veggie-Filled Grilled Corn on the Cob

Grilled corn on the cob is a BBQ favorite that’s both sweet and savory. It’s simple to prepare and can be dressed up in a variety of ways to suit everyone’s preferences. Here’s a basic recipe, but feel free to experiment with different seasonings.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked and cleaned
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh herbs, such as cilantro or parsley (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the ears of corn with melted butter, then sprinkle with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the corn on the grill and cook, turning occasionally, for about 10-12 minutes, or until the kernels are lightly charred and tender.
  4. Remove the corn from the grill and brush with more butter, if desired.
  5. Garnish with fresh herbs for an added touch of flavor.

Grilled corn is the perfect side dish to complement your BBQ spread. It’s versatile, healthy, and loved by kids and adults alike.

4. BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders

Pulled pork sliders are a crowd-pleasing dish that requires minimal effort and delivers maximum flavor. This recipe uses slow-cooked pork for a tender, juicy bite that pairs perfectly with BBQ sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder or pork butt
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 12 slider buns
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Season the pork shoulder with smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the pork in a slow cooker with the chopped onion, BBQ sauce, apple cider vinegar, and brown sugar.
  3. Cook on low for 8 hours, or until the pork is tender and easily shreds.
  4. Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and shred it with two forks.
  5. Toast the slider buns and assemble the sliders by adding the pulled pork and a scoop of coleslaw if desired.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

These pulled pork sliders are perfect for feeding a large crowd and can easily be adapted to fit your family’s taste preferences. Add a little more BBQ sauce or experiment with different types of slaw for a unique twist.

5. Grilled Vegetable Medley

Grilled vegetables are a must-have side for any BBQ. They’re light, healthy, and bring vibrant color to the plate. This vegetable medley is the perfect way to balance out all the heavier dishes and add some variety to your BBQ spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the vegetables with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Arrange the vegetables on the grill and cook for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and slightly charred.
  4. Remove from the grill and serve immediately.

Grilled vegetables are not only delicious but also provide a great way to sneak in extra nutrients. This recipe is a great option for vegetarians or anyone looking to eat lighter during the summer months.

6. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

A refreshing fruit salad is the perfect way to end a summer BBQ meal. This vibrant fruit salad is made with seasonal fruits and dressed with a simple honey-lime dressing that adds just the right amount of sweetness and tang.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 cups watermelon, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup pineapple, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 kiwi, peeled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • Juice of 1 lime

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all of the fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and lime juice until well combined.
  3. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit and gently toss to combine.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

This fruit salad is a refreshing, naturally sweet treat that’s perfect for hot summer days. It’s easy to make, full of flavor, and a healthy dessert option to finish your BBQ meal on a light note.

7. S’mores Dip

No BBQ is complete without dessert, and what better way to end the day than with a fun and interactive treat like s’mores dip? This gooey, marshmallow-filled dessert can be served with graham crackers, cookies, or fruit for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mini marshmallows
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Graham crackers, cookies, or fruit for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a cast-iron skillet or a heatproof dish, combine the chocolate chips and heavy cream. Place the skillet on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chocolate is melted and smooth.
  3. Top the melted chocolate with the mini marshmallows and close the grill lid. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden and slightly toasted.
  4. Remove from the grill and serve with graham crackers, cookies, or fruit for dipping.

This s’mores dip is the perfect way to bring everyone together for a fun and delicious ending to your BBQ. The gooey chocolate and toasted marshmallows will have everyone coming back for more.

Conclusion

A family-friendly BBQ is all about enjoying good food, great company, and creating lasting memories. These recipes are designed to cater to different tastes and dietary preferences, ensuring everyone at your gathering finds something they love. Whether you’re grilling up juicy burgers, tender chicken skewers, or indulging in a sweet fruit salad or s’mores dip, these recipes are sure to make your summer BBQ a hit. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and get ready to enjoy some amazing food in the sun!
