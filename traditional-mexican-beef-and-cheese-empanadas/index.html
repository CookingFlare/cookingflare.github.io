Traditional Mexican Beef and Cheese Empanadas


Empanadas have a special place in the hearts and kitchens of many cultures around the world, but there’s something particularly enticing about the traditional Mexican beef and cheese empanadas. These delightful pastries are not only a delicious snack or appetizer but also a versatile dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the day. In this blog post, we will delve into the origins, ingredients, preparation methods, variations, and even some serving suggestions for these mouthwatering treats.

The Origin of Empanadas

Empanadas trace their roots back to Spain, where they were originally filled with a variety of meats, vegetables, and spices, then baked or fried in dough. As Spanish colonists traveled to Latin America, they brought this culinary tradition with them, adapting it to local ingredients and tastes. In Mexico, empanadas have evolved into a beloved comfort food, often featuring regional ingredients and flavors. While you can find countless variations throughout Mexico, the traditional beef and cheese empanada remains a favorite across the country.

Ingredients for Beef and Cheese Empanadas

To make traditional Mexican beef and cheese empanadas, you’ll need a selection of fresh ingredients. Here’s a comprehensive list to help you get started:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup cold water (you may need a little more or less)

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a combination)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Flour for dusting

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s move on to the preparation of these delicious empanadas.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the diced butter.

  2. Combine: Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add Egg and Water: Beat the egg in a small bowl and add it to the flour mixture. Gradually add cold water, mixing until the dough comes together. You may need to adjust the amount of water.

  4. Knead: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. Cook the Beef: In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  2. Brown the Meat: Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until it’s browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Drain any excess fat.

  3. Season: Stir in the cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the chopped cilantro if using. Remove from heat and let cool for a few minutes.

  4. Add Cheese: Once the beef mixture is slightly cooled, fold in the shredded cheese until well combined.

Step 3: Assemble the Empanadas

  1. Roll Out the Dough: On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8-inch thickness. Use a round cutter or a glass to cut circles (about 4-6 inches in diameter) from the dough.

  2. Fill the Empanadas: Place a spoonful of the beef and cheese filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the empanadas to burst during cooking.

  3. Seal: Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly to seal. For added security and a decorative touch, you can crimp the edges with a fork.

  4. Prepare for Baking: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the assembled empanadas on the sheet.

  5. Egg Wash: Beat the remaining egg and brush it over the top of each empanada for a golden finish.

Step 4: Bake the Empanadas

  1. Bake: Bake the empanadas in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until they are golden brown.

  2. Cool: Once baked, remove them from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These traditional Mexican beef and cheese empanadas are delicious on their own, but you can elevate your dining experience with a few serving suggestions:

  • Salsa and Dips: Serve with a side of fresh salsa, guacamole, or sour cream for dipping. The contrast of flavors will enhance the overall taste.

  • Salad: Pair your empanadas with a fresh salad. A simple Mexican-style salad with tomatoes, avocados, and lime dressing complements the hearty filling perfectly.

  • Beverages: Consider serving your empanadas with traditional Mexican drinks like horchata, agua fresca, or even a chilled beer for a festive touch.

Variations of Empanadas

While the beef and cheese empanada is a classic, there are numerous variations you can explore. Here are a few ideas to inspire your creativity:

  • Vegetarian Version: Substitute the beef with black beans, sautéed vegetables, or a combination of mushrooms and spinach. Use cheese or a vegan alternative to keep the dish delicious.

  • Chicken and Cheese: Shredded cooked chicken mixed with cheese and spices can make for a lighter filling.

  • Sweet Empanadas: For a dessert twist, you can fill empanadas with sweetened fruits like apples, peaches, or berries, combined with cinnamon and sugar.

Conclusion

Making traditional Mexican beef and cheese empanadas at home is not only rewarding but also a fantastic way to bring a taste of Mexico to your kitchen. With their golden, flaky crust and flavorful filling, these empanadas are sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you serve them as an appetizer, snack, or main dish, they are bound to be a hit.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on this culinary adventure. Enjoy the process of making these delicious empanadas, and don’t forget to share them with your loved ones. Happy cooking!
