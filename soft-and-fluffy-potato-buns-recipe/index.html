Soft and Fluffy Potato Buns Recipe


Making homemade potato buns is an excellent way to satisfy your cravings for soft and fluffy buns. These potato buns are perfect for sandwiches, burger buns, or simply enjoying as is. They are tender, delicious, and versatile, making them a staple in any household.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water (110°F to 115°F)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium-sized potato, boiled and mashed
  • Extra flour for kneading

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a large bowl, combine 1 cup of warm water, 2 ¼ teaspoons of active dry yeast, and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Mix well, cover the bowl, and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the yeast is activated and the mixture becomes frothy.

Step 2: Combine the Dough Ingredients

Add 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon of salt to the yeast mixture. Mix well. Once combined, add 2 large eggs and 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Stir again. Finally, mix in 1 boiled and mashed medium-sized potato. Ensure the potato is well combined.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Using extra flour for your work surface, knead the dough for approximately 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You may need to add more flour while kneading, but not too much to prevent the dough from becoming too tough.

Step 4: First Proofing

Form the kneaded dough into a ball, place it in a lightly greased bowl, and cover it tightly with plastic wrap or damp cloth. Allow the dough to rest and rise in a warm place for approximately 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Buns

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into equal portions (depending on the size of the buns you prefer). Shape each portion into a round ball, then flatten it slightly to form a disc shape. Place the buns on a greased or lined baking sheet, ensuring they have enough space to expand while they rise.

Step 6: Second Proofing

Again, cover the shaped buns with a plastic wrap or damp cloth and let them rise for an additional 30-45 minutes in a warm place until they have doubled in size.

Step 7: Baking

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and optionally, prepare an egg wash by beating 1 egg and a tablespoon of water. Brush the egg wash over the buns to give them sheen. Bake the buns for approximately 20-25 minutes, until they are golden brown on top and sound hollow when tapped.

Step 8: Cooling and Storing

When the buns are done, remove them from the oven, and let them cool down on wire racks. These buns are best enjoyed fresh, but they can be stored in an airtight container or plastic bag at room temperature for up to three days or frozen for longer-term storage.

Tips

  • For a more flavorful potato bun, consider adding a teaspoon of garlic powder or onion powder to the dough.
  • When testing if the potato buns are done, tap their bottoms gently with your knuckles; if it sounds hollow, they’re ready.
  • If desired, utilize the leftover mashed potatoes creatively. Make potato soup or scalloped potatoes to complement the potato buns.
  • Don’t let the second rise dry out the buns! Cover them securely with plastic wrap or a damp cloth to retain moisture.

Conclusion

Soft and fluffy potato buns are worth the effort, and making them at home is both cost-effective and enjoyable. The aroma and the taste of homemade potato buns are unparalleled, making them a versatile and delicious addition to any meal. Try making this recipe, and add it to your list of homemade bread recipes!
