Greek Chicken Souvlaki: A Classic Street Food Delight


If you’ve ever strolled through the bustling streets of Greece, you’ve likely been tantalized by the irresistible aroma of sizzling meat and warm pita bread. One of the most beloved street foods in Greece is the classic Chicken Souvlaki – a dish that perfectly captures the essence of Greek cuisine with its vibrant flavors and simple yet satisfying preparation. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the world of Greek Chicken Souvlaki, exploring its history, ingredients, and a step-by-step recipe to recreate this delectable dish in your own kitchen.

The History of Souvlaki

The origins of souvlaki can be traced back to ancient Greece, where it was a popular dish among soldiers and common people alike. The word “souvlaki” itself is thought to have derived from the medieval Latin word “supliculum,” meaning “little bits” – a fitting description for the small, skewered pieces of meat that define this dish. Over the centuries, souvlaki has evolved into a quintessential Greek street food, cherished for its portability, bold flavors, and cultural significance.

The Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Chicken Souvlaki:

  • 1 1/2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large red onion, cut into chunks
  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or green), cut into chunks
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

For Serving:

  • Warm pita bread
  • Tzatziki sauce
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced red onions
  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Lemon wedges

The Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. This aromatic marinade is the key to infusing the chicken with the authentic Greek flavors that define Chicken Souvlaki.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

Place the chicken cubes in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring that each piece is well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish, then refrigerate for at least 1 hour, allowing the chicken to soak up the flavors.

Step 3: Assemble the Skewers

Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken, red onion chunks, and bell pepper chunks onto the soaked wooden skewers, alternating the ingredients for a visually appealing presentation.

Step 4: Grill the Souvlaki

Place the assembled skewers on the preheated grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Warm the pita bread on the grill for a minute on each side. Serve the Chicken Souvlaki on warm pita bread, accompanied by tzatziki sauce, sliced tomatoes, red onions, chopped parsley, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. The vibrant colors and vibrant flavors of this dish are sure to transport you to the lively streets of Greece with every bite.

Conclusion

Greek Chicken Souvlaki is more than just a delicious meal – it’s a culinary journey that captures the spirit of Greek street food culture. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this classic dish is a crowd-pleaser that never fails to impress. With its vibrant colors, bold flavors, and simple preparation, Chicken Souvlaki is a celebration of the rich culinary heritage of Greece, and now, with this recipe, you can bring a taste of Greece right to your own kitchen.

So, roll up your sleeves, fire up the grill, and embark on a flavorful adventure with Greek Chicken Souvlaki – a timeless classic that continues to delight food enthusiasts around the world. Opa!
