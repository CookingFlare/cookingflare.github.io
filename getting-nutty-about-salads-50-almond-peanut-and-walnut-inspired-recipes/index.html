Getting Nutty About Salads: 50 Almond-, Peanut-, and Walnut-Inspired Recipes


Imagine chef-created salads that delight the senses with an earthy, protein-packed punch. You no longer have to daydream while drooling over restaurant menus - we’ve collected 50 almond-, peanut-, and walnut-inspired recipes that will transform your salad game. Each mouthful will be a celebration of texture, flavor, and wholesome ingredients. Let’s descend into the nutty world of salad recipes!

  1. Spicy Peanut Ginger Salad Dressing

Say goodbye to boring salads by incorporating this tangy, zesty peanut ginger dressing. A staple in Vietnamese cuisine, this recipe is a playful mix of flavors and warmth that are too delightful to ignore. Plus, all you need are five ingredients!

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup of crunchy peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • 1/8 teaspoon of red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Blend all ingredients in a food processor or whisk together until smooth and emulsified.

  2. Toss with mixed greens, sliced cucumbers, julienned carrots, and top with crushed peanuts before serving.

  3. Black Bean and Almond Salad

This classic salad gets a twist with the addition of almonds. The contrast between the crunch of the almonds and the tenderness of the black beans make this dish truly remarkable. It’s not only refreshing, but it’s also high in protein and fiber.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup of sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Juice from 1 lime
  • Salt, pepper, and chili flakes to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine black beans, cherry tomatoes, almonds, cilantro, and avocado.

  2. Drizzle with lime juice and season with salt, pepper, and chili flakes to taste.

  3. Gently toss to combine and enjoy immediately.

  4. Arugula, Walnut, and Gorgonzola Salad

Here’s a salad that’s not afraid to be bold! Bold flavors, that is. The slightly bitter arugula, the decadent Gorgonzola, and the sweet, toasty walnuts create a delicious harmony in this dish - perfect for your gourmet dinner parties.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of baby arugula
  • 1/2 cup of walnut halves, toasted
  • 1/2 cup of Gorgonzola cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup of thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup of honey-balsamic vinaigrette (recipe below)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine arugula, toasted walnuts, Gorgonzola, and red onion.
  2. Drizzle with honey-balsamic vinaigrette and gently toss until evenly coated.
  3. Do not forget to serve immediately to preserve the delicious crunch!

Honey-Balsamic Vinaigrette:

  • 1/2 cup of balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard

Instructions:

  1. Combine ingredients in a jar with a tight-fitting lid and shake until well emulsified.

  2. Or, whisk together in a bowl and store in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

  3. Thai Tofu Salad with Peanuts

This Thai-inspired dish mimics the flavors of one of the favorite Thai street foods. The combination of fresh veggies, Tangy lemon-lime dressing, peanut topping, makes this salad a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 cup of julienned red cabbage
  • 1 cup of grated carrot
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of roasted peanuts, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of agave nectar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Crushed red pepper flakes, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat a thin layer of olive oil over medium heat.

  2. Cook cubed tofu until golden brown on all sides; set aside.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, soy sauce, agave nectar, and sesame oil.

  4. In a large bowl, combine tofu, red cabbage, carrot, cilantro, and peanuts.

  5. Toss with dressing and top with a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes for heat.

  6. Spinach Salad with Warm Bacon Dressing and Walnuts

The classic spinach salad receives an upgrade with a delightful warm bacon dressing and crisp walnuts. This hearty salad will satisfy your taste buds and keep you coming back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups of fresh baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup of sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled bacon
  • 1/2 cup of sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Warm bacon dressing (recipe below)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine spinach, almonds, bacon, red onion, and cherry tomatoes.
  2. Drizzle with warm bacon dressing and toss to combine.
  3. Feel free to serve with a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper for a sharp contrast to the sweet caramelization of the bacon.

Warm Bacon Dressing:

  • 1/2 cup of chopped bacon
  • 1/4 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium skillet, cook chopped bacon over medium heat, stirring occasionally until crispy, about 7-10 minutes.

  2. In a small bowl, mix apple cider vinegar, sugar, and paprika; stir until the sugar dissolves.

  3. Remove bacon from the skillet with a slotted spoon and mix into the sugar-vinegar mixture.

  4. Slowly whisk in vegetable oil until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Keep warm by placing the bowl in a pan of simmering water, or reheat in the microwave for a few seconds, if needed.

  6. Asian-inspired Sesame Peanut Noodle Salad

It’s time to take your noodle salads to a whole new level with this Asian-inspired recipe. The delightful contrast of creamy and crunchy textures will shock your taste buds in the most pleasant way imaginable.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. of rice noodles
  • 2 cups of shredded red cabbage
  • 1 cup of shredded carrots
  • 1 cup of sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup of chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup of chopped roasted peanuts

Sesame-Peanut Dressing:

  • 1/3 cup of creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-2 tablespoons of water, if needed

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice noodles according to package instructions, then rinse in cold water and drain.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked noodles, shredded cabbage, shredded carrots, sliced cucumber, green onions, and chopped cilantro.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and honey.
  4. If the dressing is too thick, add a small amount of water to thin it out.
  5. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat well.
  6. Finally, top with chopped roasted peanuts and serve.

Conclusion

And that wraps up our round of nutty-inspired salads. We hope you enjoyed exploring these new recipes and perhaps found a new favorite for your repertoire. Go ahead, let your creativity loose and be daring in the kitchen! Good luck and enjoy exploring this world of delightful flavors.
