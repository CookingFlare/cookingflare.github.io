Vegan Donuts Recipe: Light, Fluffy, and Baked


If you’ve been looking for a healthier, yet equally delicious, alternative to traditional fried donuts, you’re in for a treat. These vegan donuts are light, fluffy, and baked to perfection. You won’t miss the frying oil, and you won’t believe how easy it is to create donuts that are both indulgent and kind to your body and the environment.

Why Choose Vegan Donuts?

Vegan donuts are an excellent choice for several reasons. Not only are they free from dairy and eggs, but they also tend to be a bit lighter and less greasy than their fried counterparts. Baked donuts provide a healthier way to enjoy your favorite treat without compromising on taste. Whether you follow a vegan diet, have dairy or egg allergies, or just want to try something new, this recipe will satisfy your sweet cravings.

Ingredients for Vegan Donuts

Making vegan donuts is simpler than you might think. Here’s what you’ll need to make your own batch of soft, pillowy, and utterly delicious donuts:

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (you can substitute with whole wheat flour for a healthier version)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (or coconut sugar for a less refined option)
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional but highly recommended for flavor depth)

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk of your choice)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (coconut oil works well for added richness)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup (for natural sweetness)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar (helps the dough rise and adds a slight tang)

Optional Toppings:

  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
  • Vegan chocolate glaze (recipe below)
  • Cinnamon sugar (a mix of sugar and ground cinnamon)
  • Chopped nuts or sprinkles for added crunch and texture

Vegan Chocolate Glaze:

  • 1/4 cup dairy-free chocolate chips
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil (or any plant-based butter)
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup (or agave syrup)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Directions to Make Vegan Donuts

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare the Donut Pan

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). While the oven is heating up, prepare your donut pan by lightly greasing it with oil or using a non-stick spray. This will ensure that your donuts come out easily once baked.

Step 2: Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon (if using). Sifting the dry ingredients will help prevent lumps and ensure that everything is evenly distributed.

Step 3: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In another bowl, combine the almond milk, vegetable oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and apple cider vinegar. Whisk everything together until well combined. The apple cider vinegar may react with the baking powder, causing a slight fizz – don’t worry, this is exactly what you want!

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently as you go. Mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the dough – it should be smooth but slightly thick. If the batter seems too thick, you can add an extra tablespoon of almond milk to loosen it up.

Step 5: Fill the Donut Pan

Spoon the donut batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about 2/3 full. For even results, you can use a piping bag or a plastic sandwich bag with the tip cut off to pipe the batter into the pan. This method ensures a neat and uniform donut shape.

Step 6: Bake the Donuts

Place the donut pan in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the donuts are golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Every oven is different, so start checking around 12 minutes to ensure they don’t overbake.

Step 7: Let the Donuts Cool

Once the donuts are baked, remove them from the oven and let them cool in the pan for about 5 minutes. Then, carefully transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. This prevents them from becoming soggy.

Vegan Chocolate Glaze (Optional)

While the donuts are cooling, you can prepare the optional vegan chocolate glaze for dipping. It adds a rich, sweet layer of chocolate that perfectly complements the light, fluffy texture of the donuts.

To make the glaze:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the dairy-free chocolate chips and coconut oil over low heat, stirring constantly.
  2. Once melted and smooth, remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the maple syrup and vanilla extract.
  3. Let the glaze cool slightly before dipping the donuts into it.

Once the donuts have cooled completely, dip each donut into the glaze, ensuring an even coating. You can also add toppings like sprinkles or chopped nuts while the glaze is still soft.

Additional Topping Ideas

The beauty of these vegan donuts lies in their versatility. Once you have baked and glazed the donuts, you can get creative with different toppings to suit your tastes. Here are a few ideas to elevate your donut experience:

  • Cinnamon Sugar Coating: After glazing the donuts with maple syrup or simply dusting them with powdered sugar, toss them in a cinnamon-sugar mixture for a classic, cozy flavor.
  • Chopped Nuts: Top the glazed donuts with chopped walnuts, pistachios, or almonds for added texture and a nutty crunch.
  • Fruit Toppings: Fresh berries or even a berry glaze can add a burst of freshness and color to your donuts.
  • Shredded Coconut: For a tropical touch, sprinkle some shredded coconut on top of your glazed donuts.

Why Baked Donuts Are the Best Choice

Donuts are usually associated with deep frying, which can lead to greasy, heavy treats. However, baking them eliminates this issue, allowing you to indulge without the excess oil. Plus, baked donuts tend to be lighter and fluffier than fried ones, making them easier to enjoy without feeling weighed down.

Baked donuts also tend to hold their shape better, making them easier to frost and decorate. This recipe gives you the base for both a delicious treat and a canvas to customize according to your cravings.

Make-Ahead Tips for Vegan Donuts

These baked vegan donuts are delicious fresh out of the oven, but they can also be made ahead of time for convenience. Here are some tips to help you prepare them in advance:

  • Batter Storage: You can store the donut batter in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. When you’re ready to bake, just give it a gentle stir before filling the donut pan and baking.
  • Frozen Donuts: If you want to make a big batch, you can freeze the baked donuts. Simply place them in a single layer on a baking sheet to freeze for 1-2 hours. Once frozen, transfer them to an airtight container or a resealable bag and store in the freezer for up to 2-3 months. To reheat, bake at 350°F for 5-7 minutes until warmed through.

Common Donut-Making Mistakes to Avoid

Even though making donuts at home is easy and fun, there are a few common mistakes to avoid:

  1. Overmixing the Batter: Stir the wet and dry ingredients together until just combined. Overmixing can lead to dense and tough donuts.
  2. Underbaking the Donuts: Make sure to bake the donuts long enough. If you remove them too early, they might collapse or be too doughy in the center.
  3. Not Greasing the Pan: To ensure easy removal, be sure to grease the donut pan well or use a non-stick spray. This will prevent the donuts from sticking.

Final Thoughts

These vegan donuts are the perfect combination of light, fluffy, and satisfying. Whether you enjoy them fresh out of the oven or with a decadent chocolate glaze, they’re sure to become a favorite in your home. By baking instead of frying, you get a healthier option that doesn’t sacrifice flavor or texture. So, gather your ingredients and treat yourself (and your loved ones) to a batch of homemade vegan donuts today!

Enjoy experimenting with different toppings and flavors. With endless variations, you can create a donut experience that’s all your own.
