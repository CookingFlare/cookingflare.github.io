How to Make Korean Soft Tofu Soup (Sundubu Jjigae)


Sundubu Jjigae, or Korean soft tofu soup, is a popular and comforting dish in Korean cuisine. Known for its rich flavors and hearty ingredients, this dish has been a staple in many Korean households for centuries. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or looking to try something new in the kitchen, Sundubu Jjigae is a fantastic dish to add to your repertoire. It’s savory, spicy, and full of nourishing ingredients, making it perfect for chilly nights or any time you’re craving a warm, satisfying meal.

This guide will walk you through the step-by-step process of making Sundubu Jjigae from scratch. The recipe is designed to be easy to follow, whether you’re a seasoned cook or just getting started. The combination of soft tofu, rich broth, and various seasonings creates a dish that’s bursting with flavor. By the end of this post, you’ll have all the knowledge you need to prepare this incredible Korean soup right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a delicious pot of Sundubu Jjigae, you’ll need a few key ingredients. Some of them may be unfamiliar, but don’t worry! They are widely available in Asian grocery stores, or you can find them online. Here’s what you need:

  • Soft tofu (Sundubu): The star ingredient of this soup. Look for soft or silken tofu, which gives the dish its signature creamy texture.
  • Broth: You can use either beef or chicken broth as the base of your soup. Traditionally, anchovy broth is used in Korean kitchens, but the choice is yours.
  • Kimchi: A small amount of kimchi adds depth of flavor to the soup, enhancing its savory taste.
  • Gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes): This is essential for adding the spicy kick that Sundubu Jjigae is known for. Adjust the amount to your spice preference.
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): A bit of gochujang brings a rich, fermented flavor and extra heat to the soup.
  • Sesame oil: Adds a nutty fragrance and flavor to the soup.
  • Garlic: Freshly minced garlic enhances the overall flavor of the broth.
  • Onion: A small amount of onion adds sweetness and depth to the soup.
  • Zucchini: This vegetable brings a subtle sweetness and texture to the dish.
  • Green onions: Chopped green onions are used as a garnish, giving the soup a refreshing crunch.
  • Egg: A raw egg is cracked directly into the hot soup just before serving. It gently cooks in the broth, adding richness to the dish.

You can also add other proteins to customize your Sundubu Jjigae. Pork, beef, or seafood (such as shrimp or clams) are common variations.

Preparing the Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s important to have all of your ingredients prepped and ready. Here’s what you’ll need to do:

  1. Tofu: Drain the tofu and cut it into large, bite-sized chunks. Be careful not to break it too much since you want it to remain intact during cooking.
  2. Kimchi: Slice the kimchi into small pieces. If you have older, more fermented kimchi, it’s ideal for this dish because of its stronger flavor.
  3. Zucchini: Slice the zucchini into half-moons. You don’t want the pieces to be too thick, as they’ll need to soften quickly in the broth.
  4. Onion: Slice the onion into thin strips.
  5. Green onions: Slice the green onions into small pieces for garnishing later.
  6. Garlic: Mince the garlic finely, as it will add a lot of flavor to the soup.

Once your ingredients are prepared, you’re ready to begin cooking.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Sundubu Jjigae

Step 1: Sauté Aromatics and Kimchi

In a large pot or a Korean stone pot (ttukbaegi) if you have one, heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, sautéing them until fragrant and softened, about 2-3 minutes. The goal is to release the essential oils of the garlic and onion, which will create a rich base for the broth.

Next, add the sliced kimchi. Stir it in with the garlic and onion, cooking for an additional 3-4 minutes. This step allows the kimchi to cook down and deepen in flavor, making it an integral part of the broth.

Step 2: Add Broth and Seasonings

Once the kimchi has softened and caramelized slightly, it’s time to build the broth. Pour in the broth of your choice, whether it’s beef, chicken, or anchovy-based. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.

Now, it’s time to add the seasonings. Stir in the gochugaru, gochujang, and a pinch of salt. The gochugaru will add the characteristic spice and red color to the soup, while the gochujang contributes a depth of flavor and a mild heat. Taste the broth and adjust the seasonings to your liking. If you want a more intense flavor, feel free to add a little more gochujang or gochugaru.

Let the broth simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. You should be able to smell the fragrant aromas of the spices and broth, setting the stage for the tofu to shine.

Step 3: Add Tofu and Zucchini

Once the broth is ready, carefully add the cubed soft tofu. Gently stir the tofu into the broth, making sure not to break it up too much. The tofu will absorb the delicious flavors of the broth while maintaining its delicate texture.

Add the zucchini slices into the pot as well. The zucchini will cook quickly, so give it about 2-3 minutes to soften. The soup should still be bubbling gently, and the zucchini should start to soften but still have a slight crunch.

Step 4: Add the Egg

Now for the fun part! Crack a raw egg directly into the center of the bubbling soup. If you prefer your egg more cooked, stir it in gently, but if you like it runny, leave it as is and let it cook gently in the hot broth. The heat from the soup will cook the egg slowly, adding a silky richness to the dish.

Let the soup simmer for another 2-3 minutes, until the egg is cooked to your desired consistency. If you’re using a traditional stone pot, you can serve the soup immediately, as the pot will keep the soup hot for longer.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once your Sundubu Jjigae is cooked, it’s time to serve! Ladle the soup into individual bowls and garnish with freshly chopped green onions. The green onions add a fresh, crunchy element that contrasts beautifully with the soft tofu and rich broth.

Sundubu Jjigae is traditionally served with a bowl of steamed rice, which you can enjoy alongside the soup. The rice helps balance the heat and the richness of the soup, making for a well-rounded meal.

Additional Tips for the Best Sundubu Jjigae

  1. Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a milder soup, reduce the amount of gochugaru and gochujang. If you want more spice, feel free to add extra chili flakes or even fresh chopped chilies.

  2. Use a Stone Pot: If possible, cook and serve the soup in a traditional Korean stone pot. It helps retain the heat and adds an authentic touch to the presentation.

  3. Add Protein: You can easily customize Sundubu Jjigae by adding different proteins. Try adding shrimp, clams, beef, or even a bit of pork. These proteins cook quickly and pair wonderfully with the rich tofu and spicy broth.

  4. Serve with Banchan: In Korean cuisine, a variety of side dishes, called banchan, are often served with the main meal. Consider serving your Sundubu Jjigae with kimchi, pickled vegetables, or a simple green salad.

  5. Adjust the Texture: If you prefer your tofu softer, you can reduce the cooking time slightly. On the other hand, if you like a firmer texture, you can let the tofu cook a little longer to absorb more of the flavors from the broth.

Why You’ll Love Sundubu Jjigae

Sundubu Jjigae is one of those dishes that has everything: it’s warming, comforting, and full of bold flavors. The soft tofu melts in your mouth, while the spicy, savory broth warms you from the inside out. It’s also incredibly customizable, so you can adjust the ingredients and spice level to your preferences. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or looking for a quick weeknight meal, this dish is sure to satisfy.

The balance of textures – from the soft tofu and zucchini to the crunchy green onions – makes each spoonful a delightful experience. The depth of flavor in the broth, paired with the richness of the egg, creates a satisfying meal that will leave you craving more.

Final Thoughts

Making Sundubu Jjigae at home may seem like a challenge at first, but with the right ingredients and a little patience, you can recreate this iconic Korean dish with ease. Whether you’re serving it for a special occasion or as a comforting weeknight meal, it’s sure to impress. The combination of spicy, savory, and umami-rich flavors will transport you straight to the heart of Korean cuisine.

So, gather your ingredients, heat up your pot, and get ready to enjoy a bowl of delicious Korean soft tofu soup. Your taste buds will thank you!
