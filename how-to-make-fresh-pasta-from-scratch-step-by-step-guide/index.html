How to Make Fresh Pasta from Scratch: Step-by-Step Guide


Making fresh pasta from scratch is an incredibly rewarding and enjoyable experience that brings the taste of Italy straight into your kitchen. Unlike store-bought pasta, homemade pasta offers a rich, authentic flavor and a satisfying texture that will elevate any dish. Whether you’re making pasta for the first time or looking to refine your technique, this step-by-step guide will walk you through the entire process, from mixing the dough to creating your favorite shapes.

In this guide, we’ll cover all the essentials you need to know to make pasta at home: the ingredients, tools, techniques, and various pasta shapes you can try. By the end of this post, you’ll be well on your way to mastering the art of fresh pasta making.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of homemade pasta lies in its simplicity. The ingredients are basic, but the results are exceptional. Here’s what you’ll need:

1. Flour

The type of flour you use plays a significant role in the texture and consistency of your pasta. For authentic Italian pasta, semolina flour is often preferred because it adds a nice texture and bite. However, all-purpose flour can also be used for a softer dough. Some pasta makers even choose to combine both types of flour.

2. Eggs

Eggs are the binding agent in fresh pasta dough, helping to create the smooth, elastic texture that makes homemade pasta so special. The number of eggs needed will vary depending on the amount of flour you’re using, but generally, one egg per 100 grams of flour is a good starting point.

3. Olive Oil (Optional)

While not strictly necessary, a small amount of olive oil can be added to the dough for a bit of extra flavor and a slightly softer texture. It’s especially helpful when you’re working with semolina flour, which can be a bit coarser.

4. Salt

A pinch of salt is essential for enhancing the flavor of your pasta dough. It’s a simple ingredient, but it helps to bring out the natural taste of the pasta.

Tools You’ll Need

While you don’t need a ton of specialized equipment to make pasta at home, there are a few tools that can make the process easier and more efficient.

1. Mixing Bowl

You’ll need a large mixing bowl to combine your ingredients. A well-sized bowl will give you enough room to work with the dough without it spilling over the sides.

2. Rolling Pin or Pasta Machine

You have two options when it comes to rolling out your pasta dough. A traditional rolling pin can do the job, but a pasta machine offers more precision and ease, especially when it comes to getting the dough thin enough for delicate pasta types like fettuccine or tagliatelle.

3. Sharp Knife or Pasta Cutter

If you don’t have a pasta machine, you can use a sharp knife to cut the dough into your desired shapes. If you do have a pasta machine, you can simply use the cutting attachment for easy slicing.

4. Clean Surface or Work Table

You’ll need a clean, flat surface to work the dough on. It’s best to use a wooden table or countertop, as it provides enough friction to help you roll out the dough smoothly.

5. Fork or Bench Scraper

A fork or bench scraper helps you mix the ingredients and scrape the dough off your work surface when it gets too sticky.

Step-by-Step Process for Making Fresh Pasta

Now that you have your ingredients and tools ready, let’s get started! Follow these simple steps to create fresh, homemade pasta.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Measure the Flour
    Start by measuring your flour. If you’re using semolina, begin with 200 grams for a small batch, or 400 grams if you’re making a larger batch. Place it on your clean work surface and form a mound.

  2. Make a Well
    With your fingers, create a well in the center of the mound of flour. The well should be wide enough to hold the eggs without spilling over. Think of this step like creating a little “nest” for the eggs.

  3. Crack the Eggs
    Crack the eggs into the well. If you’re making a small batch, 2 eggs should be sufficient. If you want to increase the quantity, add more eggs at a ratio of one egg per 100 grams of flour.

  4. Whisk the Eggs
    Using a fork, whisk the eggs gently, slowly incorporating the flour from the edges of the well. As you mix, the dough will start to come together. Don’t rush; be patient and allow the flour to mix thoroughly with the eggs.

  5. Knead the Dough
    Once the dough begins to come together and there are no more dry bits of flour, use your hands to start kneading. Fold the dough in half, then press it down with the heels of your hands. Turn the dough slightly after each fold. Continue kneading for about 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too dry, you can add a tiny bit of water. If it’s too sticky, sprinkle a little flour.

  6. Rest the Dough
    After kneading, wrap your dough in plastic wrap or cover it with a damp towel. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. Resting helps the gluten relax, which makes the dough easier to roll out.

Step 2: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Divide the Dough
    Once the dough has rested, divide it into smaller portions. This will make it easier to roll out.

  2. Flatten with a Rolling Pin (or Use a Pasta Machine)
    If you’re using a rolling pin, flatten one portion of dough into a disk. Then, roll it out from the center, applying even pressure, until it becomes thin enough to see your hand through. If you’re using a pasta machine, start by feeding the dough through the widest setting and progressively narrow the setting until the dough is thin and smooth.

  3. Work in Batches
    Don’t try to roll out the entire batch at once. Work with one portion at a time, keeping the rest covered so it doesn’t dry out.

Step 3: Cut the Pasta

Once your dough is rolled out to your desired thickness, it’s time to cut it into shapes.

  1. For Fettuccine or Tagliatelle
    If you want to make fettuccine or tagliatelle, simply roll the dough sheet into a loose cylinder and use a sharp knife to slice it into thin strips. Unravel the strips and set them aside.

  2. For Pappardelle
    For pappardelle, use the same method but cut the dough into wider strips.

  3. For Ravioli or Tortellini
    If you’re making stuffed pasta like ravioli or tortellini, cut the dough into squares or circles and fill with your favorite filling (cheese, spinach, or meat). Press the edges tightly to seal.

Step 4: Cook the Pasta

Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried pasta, so keep an eye on it. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fresh pasta and cook for 2-4 minutes, depending on the thickness of the pasta. Fresh pasta floats to the surface when it’s ready. Taste a piece to check for doneness.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once your pasta is cooked, drain it and toss it with your favorite sauce. Whether it’s a classic marinara, a creamy Alfredo, or a simple drizzle of olive oil and parmesan, homemade pasta elevates any dish. Serve immediately and enjoy the fruits of your labor!

Tips and Tricks for Perfect Homemade Pasta

  • Use Room Temperature Eggs: Cold eggs can make the dough tough and harder to mix, so always use eggs that are at room temperature.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Knead until smooth, but don’t go overboard. Overworking the dough can make it tough.
  • Add Flour Gradually: If the dough is too sticky, gradually add more flour as you knead, but try to avoid adding too much at once.
  • Rest the Dough: Don’t skip the resting step. It’s essential for gluten relaxation and easier rolling.
  • Experiment with Shapes: Don’t be afraid to try new pasta shapes. Once you’re comfortable with the basics, play around with different types like orecchiette, farfalle, or even stuffed pasta like tortellini.
  • Make Extra and Freeze It: Fresh pasta can be frozen for later use. Simply lay the pasta out on a baking sheet to freeze, then transfer it to an airtight container or bag.

Conclusion

Making fresh pasta from scratch may take some time and effort, but the results are well worth it. There’s nothing quite like the taste of homemade pasta, and once you’ve mastered the process, you’ll be able to create a variety of pasta dishes to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re preparing a simple dinner or crafting a gourmet meal, homemade pasta is always a treat.

With these steps and tips, you’re ready to start your pasta-making journey. Happy cooking!
