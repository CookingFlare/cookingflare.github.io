Vampire Teeth Cookies: Best Halloween Dessert for Kids Parties


Halloween is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate this spooky and fun holiday than to whip up some delicious treats that the entire family will enjoy. If you’re looking for a unique Halloween dessert that’s sure to bring gasps and giggles to your kids’ parties, then look no further than Vampire Teeth Cookies. These delightfully creepy cookies are not only easy to make but also seriously scrumptious. So, get ready to channel your inner vampire, and let’s get baking!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup black or red icing gel
  • Toothpick or skewer
  • Cookie press or piping bag fitted with a small round tip (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper, set aside.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon baking soda, 1/4 teaspoon baking powder, and 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon, and 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg. Set aside.

  3. In a large bowl, cream together 1/2 cup softened unsalted butter, 3/4 cup granulated sugar, and 1/2 cup packed brown sugar until light and fluffy. This can be done using a stand mixer, hand mixer, or even an old-fashioned whisk.

  4. Beat in 2 large eggs, one at a time, and then add 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract and mix until well combined.

  5. Slowly add in the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients mixture, alternating with 1/2 cup milk, making sure to mix well after each addition.

  6. Once all ingredients are combined, the cookie dough is ready to be shaped into the iconic vampire teeth shape. You can choose to either use a cookie press or piping bag to shape the dough, or you can go the old-fashioned route of shaping them by hand. If using a cookie press or piping bag, fill them with the dough and pipe small teardrop shapes onto the prepared baking sheet.

  7. If shaping by hand, take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a ball. Place the ball between your hands and flatten it slightly. Using a toothpick or skewer, carefully press in the middle of the cookie from the top to the bottom to create the vampire teeth shape. Place the shaped cookies onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them evenly apart to account for spreading during baking.

  8. Once all the cookies are shaped, bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden brown. It’s crucial not to overbake these cookies, as you want them to remain soft and chewy.

  9. As the cookies are baking, prepare the black or red icing gel by placing it in a piping bag (or a ziplock back with a small corner snipped off). This will be used to create the “blood” effect later.

  10. Once the cookies have baked and cooled entirely, carefully pipe a small amount of icing on top of each cookie to resemble bloody teeth. If you prefer a slightly more subtle touch, you can also brush the icing onto the cookies using a clean toothpick or skewer.

  11. Allow the icing to set fully before serving your Vampire Teeth Cookies at the Halloween party. Then, sit back, watch as the kids (and perhaps some adults) marvel at your creation, and watch the smiles spread across their faces.

These Vampire Teeth Cookies are a deviously simple yet incredibly fun way to make your Halloween celebrations extra memorable. With just a few ingredients, your kitchen will be transformed into a vampire den, and your kids’ parties will be the talk of the town. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the oven, and get ready to sink your teeth into these eerie edibles! Happy Halloween, everyone!
