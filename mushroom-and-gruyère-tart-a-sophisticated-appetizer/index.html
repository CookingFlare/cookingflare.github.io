Mushroom and Gruyère Tart: A Sophisticated Appetizer

4 min · 689 words

If you’re looking for an elegant and flavorful appetizer to impress your guests, look no further than this Mushroom and Gruyère Tart recipe. With its buttery crust, savory mushrooms, and creamy Gruyère cheese, this tart is the perfect combination of sophistication and deliciousness. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to something special, this recipe is sure to please.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 pound mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup Gruyère cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Gather the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and bake for 10 minutes, or until lightly golden. Set aside.

  4. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms, minced garlic, thyme leaves, salt, and pepper. Sauté until the mushrooms are tender and any liquid has evaporated, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.

  5. In a medium bowl, whisk together the shredded Gruyère cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, heavy cream, and eggs. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Spread the sautéed mushrooms evenly over the pre-baked tart crust. Pour the cheese mixture over the mushrooms, ensuring it is evenly distributed.

  7. Bake the tart for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

  8. Slice and serve the Mushroom and Gruyère Tart warm or at room temperature. It pairs perfectly with a crisp green salad or a glass of white wine.

Enjoy this sophisticated appetizer with its rich flavors and buttery crust. Your guests will be impressed by the combination of earthy mushrooms and creamy Gruyère cheese. This Mushroom and Gruyère Tart is a true crowd-pleaser and is sure to elevate any occasion. Bon appétit!

Note: This recipe serves approximately 6-8 people, depending on portion sizes. Feel free to adjust the quantities according to your needs.
