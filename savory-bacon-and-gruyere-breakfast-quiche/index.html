Savory Bacon and Gruyere Breakfast Quiche


If you’re looking to elevate your breakfast game, there’s nothing quite like a slice of savory bacon and Gruyère breakfast quiche. This dish combines the rich flavors of crispy bacon, nutty Gruyère cheese, and a creamy custard in a flaky pastry crust. Perfect for a weekend brunch or a special occasion, this quiche is not only delicious but also incredibly versatile. You can serve it hot, warm, or even at room temperature, making it an ideal dish to prepare ahead of time.

The Magic of Quiche

Quiche is a classic French dish that has gained popularity around the world. The beauty of quiche lies in its adaptability; you can customize it with various fillings to suit your taste. While the traditional quiche Lorraine features bacon and cheese, adding Gruyère gives it a delightful twist. This semi-hard cheese is known for its rich, creamy flavor and melts beautifully, making it a perfect addition to any quiche.

Why Bacon and Gruyère?

Bacon adds a savory, smoky flavor that pairs exceptionally well with the creamy texture of the custard and the nutty richness of Gruyère cheese. Together, they create a harmonious blend that is both satisfying and indulgent. The saltiness of the bacon complements the subtle sweetness of the caramelized onions, while the Gruyère provides depth and complexity.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For a delicious savory bacon and Gruyère breakfast quiche, gather the following ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup Gruyère cheese, grated
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped (optional)

Making the Crust

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Combine the Flour and Salt: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt. This will ensure that the salt is evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  2. Add the Butter: Add the chilled, diced butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Incorporate the Ice Water: Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  4. Chill the Dough: Form the dough into a ball, flatten it into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough helps to prevent shrinking during baking.

Step 2: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Roll the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle about 1/8 inch thick. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges.

  3. Blind Bake the Crust: To prevent a soggy bottom, blind bake the crust. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool.

Preparing the Filling

Step 3: Cook the Bacon and Onions

  1. Cook the Bacon: In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the chopped bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and drain it on paper towels, leaving about 1 tablespoon of bacon fat in the pan.

  2. Sauté the Onions: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until the onions are soft and lightly caramelized. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

Step 4: Whisk the Eggs and Cream

  1. Mix the Custard: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, black pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.

  2. Add the Cheese and Bacon: Stir in the grated Gruyère cheese, cooked bacon, and sautéed onions. If you’re using chives, fold them in at this stage for an extra burst of flavor.

Assembling the Quiche

Step 5: Combine and Bake

  1. Pour the Filling into the Crust: Carefully pour the egg mixture into the pre-baked crust, ensuring that the filling is evenly distributed.

  2. Bake the Quiche: Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the custard is set and the top is golden brown. A knife inserted into the center should come out clean.

  3. Cool Before Serving: Once baked, remove the quiche from the oven and allow it to cool for about 10 minutes before slicing. This resting time helps the filling to firm up, making it easier to cut clean slices.

Serving Suggestions

Savory bacon and Gruyère breakfast quiche can be served in various ways. Here are a few suggestions to enhance your dining experience:

  • Pair with a Side Salad: A light green salad with a tangy vinaigrette complements the richness of the quiche beautifully.

  • Add Fresh Fruit: Serve the quiche alongside a platter of fresh seasonal fruit for a refreshing contrast.

  • Offer Beverages: Pair with coffee, tea, or a mimosa to create a festive brunch atmosphere.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (which is unlikely, but it happens), you can store the quiche in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes, or until heated through. You can also enjoy it cold or at room temperature, making it a great option for meal prep or picnics.

Variations to Try

While the classic bacon and Gruyère combination is hard to beat, there are many ways to customize your quiche:

  • Vegetarian Option: Skip the bacon and add sautéed vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, or bell peppers for a delicious meatless version.

  • Different Cheeses: Experiment with other cheeses like cheddar, feta, or goat cheese for a unique flavor profile.

  • Add Herbs: Fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley can elevate the flavor of your quiche and add a pop of color.

Conclusion

Savory bacon and Gruyère breakfast quiche is a crowd-pleaser that’s perfect for any occasion. Its creamy texture, combined with the salty crunch of bacon and the rich flavor of Gruyère, creates a dish that’s both satisfying and indulgent. Whether you’re hosting a brunch or looking for a comforting meal, this quiche is sure to impress your guests and become a beloved staple in your recipe collection. So roll up your sleeves and get ready to enjoy the delightful experience of making and savoring this delicious quiche!
