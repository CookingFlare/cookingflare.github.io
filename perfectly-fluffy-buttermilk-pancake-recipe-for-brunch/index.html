Perfectly Fluffy Buttermilk Pancake Recipe for Brunch


As the weekend approaches, many of us start craving a hearty and comforting breakfast that will set us up for a day of relaxation and indulgence. There’s no better way to start the day than with a stack of fluffy buttermilk pancakes, drizzled with maple syrup and topped with fresh fruit. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create the perfect buttermilk pancake, with a tender crumb and a delicate, slightly tangy flavor.

The Secret to Fluffy Pancakes

The key to making perfectly fluffy pancakes lies in the batter. Unlike other pancake recipes that rely on a mixture of all-purpose flour and baking powder, this recipe uses buttermilk to create a tender and moist crumb. The acidity in the buttermilk reacts with the baking soda to produce a light and airy texture, making these pancakes a cut above the rest.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit and maple syrup for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. The batter should still be slightly lumpy.
  4. Let the batter rest for 10-15 minutes to allow the flour to absorb the liquid and the baking powder to activate.
  5. Preheat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Grease the pan with a small amount of butter or cooking spray.
  6. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, scoop the batter onto the pan. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes, until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges start to dry.
  7. Flip the pancakes and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until golden brown.
  8. Serve the pancakes hot with your choice of toppings, such as fresh fruit, maple syrup, or whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra-fluffy pancake, try using a mixture of all-purpose flour and cake flour. The cake flour will add a tender and delicate texture to the pancakes.
  • If you don’t have buttermilk, you can make a substitute by mixing 1 cup of milk with 1 tablespoon of white vinegar or lemon juice. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes before using.
  • To make blueberry pancakes, simply fold 1/2 cup of fresh or frozen blueberries into the batter before cooking.
  • For a savory twist, try adding 1/2 cup of grated cheddar cheese or 1/4 cup of chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley or chives, to the batter.

Conclusion

With this recipe, you’ll be able to create a stack of fluffy buttermilk pancakes that will impress even the most discerning brunch guests. The key is to use high-quality ingredients and to not overmix the batter, allowing the flour to absorb the liquid and the baking powder to activate. Whether you’re serving these pancakes at a weekend brunch or a special occasion, they’re sure to be a hit. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and start your day off right with a stack of perfectly fluffy buttermilk pancakes.
