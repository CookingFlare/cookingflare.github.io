Delectable Allergy-Friendly Apple Pie for a Classic Dessert


Are you craving the comforting flavors of a classic apple pie but need to accommodate food allergies? Look no further! This delectable allergy-friendly apple pie recipe will satisfy your dessert cravings without compromising on flavor. Whether you have gluten, dairy, or nut allergies, this recipe is designed to be inclusive and delicious for all. Let’s dive into the details of creating this mouthwatering apple pie that will delight your taste buds and impress your guests.

Ingredients

For the Crust

  • 2 1/2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup dairy-free butter, chilled and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling

  • 6-7 medium apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 2 tablespoons gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour and salt.
  2. Add the chilled dairy-free butter cubes to the flour mixture.
  3. Use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough until it holds together.
  5. Divide the dough in half, shape into two discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, allspice, gluten-free flour, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.

Assembling the Pie

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out one disc of the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Transfer the rolled dough to the pie dish and gently press it against the bottom and sides.
  4. Pour the prepared apple filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly.
  5. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  6. Use a sharp knife to make a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

Baking the Pie

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. If the edges of the pie start to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil.
  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This allergy-friendly apple pie pairs beautifully with dairy-free vanilla ice cream or a dollop of coconut whipped cream. For an extra indulgent treat, drizzle caramel sauce over the pie before serving. Whether enjoyed warm or at room temperature, this apple pie is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Conclusion

With this allergy-friendly apple pie recipe, you can enjoy the nostalgic flavors of a classic dessert without worry. The combination of tender spiced apples encased in a flaky, buttery crust will leave you craving more. Share this delightful treat with friends and family, and watch as they savor every bite. Embrace the joy of baking and savoring a slice of this delectable pie, knowing that it’s allergy-friendly and utterly delicious.
