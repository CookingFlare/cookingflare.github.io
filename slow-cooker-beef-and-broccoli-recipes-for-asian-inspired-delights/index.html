Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli Recipes for Asian-Inspired Delights


If you’re a fan of Asian cuisine but don’t always have the time to whip up a complicated meal, slow cooker beef and broccoli recipes are here to save the day. These recipes offer the perfect blend of convenience and flavor, allowing you to enjoy a delicious Asian-inspired meal with minimal effort. With the help of your trusty slow cooker, you can come home to a fragrant and tender beef and broccoli dish that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. So let’s dive in and explore some mouthwatering recipes that will satisfy your Asian food cravings.

Classic Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds beef chuck roast, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the beef broth, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the slow cooker and coat it with the sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 4-6 hours or on high for 2-3 hours, until the beef is tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water to create a slurry.
  5. Stir the slurry into the slow cooker and add the broccoli florets. Mix well.
  6. Cover and cook on high for an additional 30 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  7. Serve the slow cooker beef and broccoli over cooked rice. Enjoy!

Spicy Korean-Style Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Green onions, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the beef broth, Korean chili paste, soy sauce, honey, garlic, and sesame oil. Mix well to combine.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the slow cooker and coat it with the sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 4-6 hours or on high for 2-3 hours, until the beef is tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water to create a slurry.
  5. Stir the slurry into the slow cooker and add the broccoli florets. Mix well.
  6. Cover and cook on high for an additional 30 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  7. Serve the spicy Korean-style slow cooker beef and broccoli over cooked rice. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for extra flavor and presentation.

Thai Coconut Curry Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds beef stew meat, cubed
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup Thai red curry paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the coconut milk, Thai red curry paste, fish sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger. Mix well to combine.
  2. Add the beef stew meat to the slow cooker and coat it with the curry sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 4-6 hours, until the beef is tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water to create a slurry.
  5. Stir the slurry into the slow cooker and add the broccoli florets. Mix well.
  6. Cover and cook on high for an additional 30 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  7. Serve the Thai coconut curry slow cooker beef and broccoli over cooked rice. Garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

Conclusion

Slow cooker beef and broccoli recipes offer a convenient and delicious way to enjoy Asian-inspired delights without spending hours in the kitchen. Whether you prefer the classic version, a spicy Korean twist, or a Thai coconut curry variation, these recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings. So dust off your slow cooker, gather the ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of Asia in the comfort of your own home. Happy cooking!
