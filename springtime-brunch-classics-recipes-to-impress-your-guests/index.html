Springtime Brunch Classics: Recipes to Impress Your Guests


As the seasons change and spring arrives, the desire to host a delightful brunch with friends and family becomes increasingly appealing. The idea of gathering around the table, sharing stories, and savoring delicious dishes is a perfect way to celebrate the renewal and rejuvenation that comes with this time of year. In this post, we will explore some springtime brunch classics that are sure to impress your guests and leave them feeling full and satisfied.

Lemon Ricotta Pancakes with Fresh Berries

There’s something special about starting the day with a stack of fluffy pancakes, especially when they’re infused with the brightness of lemon and the creaminess of ricotta. To make these pancakes, you’ll need:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • Fresh berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries)

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, milk, eggs, and lemon juice. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in the ricotta cheese and melted butter. The batter should still be slightly lumpy.

Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and drop the batter by 1/4 cupfuls. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes, until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges start to dry. Flip the pancakes and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until golden brown.

Serve the pancakes warm with a dollop of whipped cream and a sprinkle of fresh berries.

Asparagus and Prosciutto Quiche

Quiche is a classic brunch dish that’s perfect for springtime gatherings. This asparagus and prosciutto quiche is a delicious and elegant option that’s sure to impress your guests.

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 6 slices prosciutto, chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the puff pastry and place it in a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. In a large bowl, whisk together the melted butter, cheddar cheese, and Parmesan cheese. Add the asparagus and prosciutto, and stir until well combined.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus mixture and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Bake the quiche for 35-40 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with chopped parsley, if desired.

Springtime Brunch Salad with Strawberries and Feta

As the weather warms up, a fresh and refreshing salad is the perfect way to start the day. This springtime brunch salad is a delightful combination of sweet and savory flavors.

  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cup sliced strawberries
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup crumbled cooked bacon
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, sliced strawberries, crumbled feta cheese, chopped pecans, and crumbled bacon. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil and white wine vinegar. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Garnish with chopped fresh mint and serve immediately.

Springtime Brunch Classics to Impress Your Guests

These springtime brunch classics are sure to impress your guests and leave them feeling full and satisfied. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a large party, these recipes are perfect for any occasion. So why not give them a try and see what your guests think?

With these springtime brunch classics, you’ll be sure to impress your guests and leave them feeling full and satisfied. From lemon ricotta pancakes to asparagus and prosciutto quiche, and from springtime brunch salad to strawberries and feta, these recipes are sure to become new favorites. So go ahead, get cooking, and make this springtime brunch one to remember!
