Wine Pairings for Chinese Takeout: Elevating Your Favorites


When it comes to indulging in Chinese takeout, there’s no denying the incredible flavors and diverse range of dishes that leave our taste buds tingling with delight. From spicy Szechuan to savory Cantonese, Chinese cuisine offers a symphony of flavors that can be enjoyed in the comfort of your own home. But why limit your culinary experience to just the food? Elevate your Chinese takeout favorites by pairing them with the perfect wines. In this blog post, we’ll explore some wine pairing suggestions that will take your Chinese takeout experience to the next level.

General Guidelines for Wine Pairing with Chinese Takeout

Before we dive into specific wine pairings, let’s establish some general guidelines to keep in mind when selecting the perfect bottle to accompany your Chinese feast.

  1. Acidity: Chinese cuisine often incorporates vinegars and citrus flavors. Look for wines with vibrant acidity to complement these tangy elements and provide a refreshing contrast.

  2. Sweetness: Many Chinese dishes have a touch of sweetness, whether from sauces or caramelization. Consider wines with a hint of residual sugar to balance out the flavors.

  3. Spiciness: If you enjoy spicy Chinese dishes, opt for wines with lower alcohol content and some sweetness to cool down the heat.

  4. Versatility: Chinese takeout often includes a variety of dishes, so it’s essential to choose wines with versatility that can pair well with different flavors and textures.

Now that we have our general guidelines, let’s explore some specific wine pairings for popular Chinese takeout dishes.

Wine Pairings for Chinese Takeout

1. Sweet and Sour Chicken

The combination of tangy sweetness and crispy chicken calls for a wine that can match its vibrancy. A Riesling with its fruity flavors and zesty acidity is an excellent choice. Look for a slightly off-dry (semi-sweet) Riesling to complement the sweet and sour sauce while cutting through the richness of the chicken.

2. Kung Pao Shrimp

With its spicy and savory flavors, Kung Pao Shrimp requires a wine that can handle the heat. A Gewürztraminer with its aromatic intensity and slight sweetness is an ideal match. The wine’s floral and exotic fruit notes will enhance the dish’s flavors while providing a cooling effect.

3. Peking Duck

Peking Duck is a classic Chinese dish known for its succulent meat and crispy skin. To complement the richness of the duck, opt for a Pinot Noir. The wine’s light to medium body, red fruit flavors, and silky tannins will harmonize with the duck’s flavors without overpowering them.

4. Beef and Broccoli

A popular choice in Chinese takeout, Beef and Broccoli is a flavorful dish that calls for a versatile wine. A Merlot with its smooth tannins, ripe fruit flavors, and hints of herbaceousness will complement the dish’s savory notes while providing a velvety texture.

5. Vegetable Lo Mein

For those who prefer vegetarian options, Vegetable Lo Mein is a delightful choice. Pair this dish with a Sauvignon Blanc. The wine’s bright acidity and herbal notes will cut through the richness of the noodles and vegetables, creating a refreshing and balanced pairing.

6. General Tso’s Chicken

General Tso’s Chicken is a beloved dish with its sweet, spicy, and tangy flavors. Pair it with a Zinfandel to match its boldness. The wine’s ripe fruit flavors, peppery notes, and higher alcohol content will complement the dish while holding up to its powerful flavors.

Experiment and Discover

While the suggested wine pairings are a great starting point, don’t be afraid to experiment and discover your own combinations. Chinese takeout offers a vast array of flavors and textures, making it a playground for wine pairing exploration. Remember to consider the general guidelines of acidity, sweetness, spiciness, and versatility when selecting your wines, and let your taste buds guide you to the perfect match.

So, the next time you order Chinese takeout, don’t forget to grab a bottle of wine to elevate your dining experience. Cheers to a harmonious union of flavors and a memorable meal!
