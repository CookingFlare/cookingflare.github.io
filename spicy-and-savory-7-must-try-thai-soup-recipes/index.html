Spicy and Savory: 7 Must-Try Thai Soup Recipes


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, complex aromatics, and tantalizing balance of spicy, savory, sweet, and sour notes. Among its many culinary delights, Thai soups are exceptional dishes that showcase the profound depth of the country’s food culture. Whether you’re seeking comfort in a bowl or an adventurous culinary experience, Thai soups offer something for every palate. Here are seven must-try Thai soup recipes that will take your taste buds on a flavorful journey.

1. Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup)

Tom Yum Goong is one of Thailand’s most legendary dishes, known for its bold and tangy flavors. This classic soup is a harmonious blend of fragrant herbs, sour lime juice, fiery chili paste, and rich, umami-packed shrimp. The essence of Tom Yum lies in its aromatic ingredients like lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal, which create its signature depth.

Ingredients:

  • Shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • Lemongrass (sliced)
  • Galangal (sliced)
  • Kaffir lime leaves
  • Bird’s eye chilies
  • Lime juice
  • Fish sauce
  • Straw mushrooms
  • Chili paste

Instructions:

  1. Boil a pot of water and add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.
  2. Incorporate shrimp and mushrooms into the boiling broth. Cook until the shrimp turn pink.
  3. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and chili paste. Adjust the balance of sour, salty, and spicy flavors to your preference.

This refreshing yet fiery soup pairs beautifully with steamed jasmine rice or can be enjoyed on its own as a light appetizer.

2. Tom Kha Gai (Chicken Coconut Soup)

Tom Kha Gai is a creamy, mildly spicy soup that is perfect for those who prefer a gentler kick. The foundation of this dish is a rich coconut milk broth infused with lime leaf, galangal, and lemongrass. Tender chicken pieces add texture, while fresh mushrooms and cilantro finish the dish beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken breast (sliced)
  • Coconut milk
  • Lemongrass
  • Kaffir lime leaves
  • Galangal (thinly sliced)
  • Mushrooms
  • Lime juice
  • Fish sauce
  • Thai chilies

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut milk and water in a saucepan and add lemongrass, galangal, and lime leaves.
  2. Add chicken and cook until it’s fully done. Then, throw in mushrooms for a little extra texture.
  3. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and chilies to bring out its full flavor profile.

Tom Kha Gai is comforting, slightly tangy, and irresistibly aromaticâ€”an excellent introduction to Thai soups for first-timers.

3. Kaeng Som (Thai Sour Curry Soup)

Kaeng Som is a beautifully tangy soup that embraces sour tamarind as its primary flavoring component. Unlike other Thai soups, Kaeng Som has a fiery, reddish-orange broth due to the addition of red curry paste and turmeric. Often served with fish or shrimp, this soup is both hearty and healthy.

Ingredients:

  • Fish or shrimp
  • Tamarind paste
  • Chili paste
  • Turmeric powder
  • Vegetables (cabbage, carrots, green beans)
  • Fish sauce

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a large pot and incorporate red curry paste and turmeric powder.
  2. Add tamarind paste for a vibrant sour taste, followed by fish or shrimp.
  3. Toss in vegetables and simmer for 15 minutes until tender. Season with fish sauce for balance.

Kaeng Som is a bold dish perfect for brightening up a cold winter day or complementing a Thai-inspired dinner spread.

4. Tom Saap (Isaan-Style Hot and Sour Soup)

Tom Saap hails from Thailand’s northeastern region, Isaan, famed for its bold, rustic flavors. Unlike its creamy counterparts, Tom Saap is a clear soup packed with aromatic herbs and spices, delivering a spicy and sour punch. Pork or beef ribs are often used to enhance its rich flavor.

Ingredients:

  • Pork or beef ribs
  • Lime juice
  • Lemongrass
  • Shallots
  • Coriander
  • Kaffir lime leaves
  • Fish sauce
  • Dried chilies

Instructions:

  1. Simmer pork or beef ribs in water until tender, then add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and shallots.
  2. Season with lime juice, fish sauce, and dried chilies to achieve that bold hot-and-sour flavor.
  3. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.

If you’re craving an intensely aromatic soup that showcases authentic Isaan cuisine, Tom Saap is for you.

5. Kaeng Liang (Thai Herbal Vegetable Soup)

Kaeng Liang is a highly nutritious soup that emphasizes fresh vegetables and Thai herbs. Known for its earthy flavors and light broth, Kaeng Liang is an ideal dish for those opting for a healthful meal without compromising texture or taste.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed seasonal vegetables (zucchini, pumpkin, mushrooms, etc.)
  • Thai basil
  • Garlic
  • Shrimp paste
  • Peppercorns

Instructions:

  1. Create a paste using garlic, shrimp paste, and peppercorns, then dilute with water.
  2. Bring the broth to a boil, adding a mixture of chopped vegetables.
  3. Simmer until the vegetables are tender and garnish with Thai basil.

Kaeng Liang carries a unique herbal profile, making it a vital addition to any fan of light, wholesome soups.

6. Gaeng Jued (Clear Thai Vegetable Soup)

Gaeng Jued is the epitome of simplicity and balance, often served as a side dish in Thai meals. This clear soup is mild and subtle, often cooked with pork balls, tofu, and assorted vegetables. Itâ€™s perfect for cleansing your palate in between more flavorful dishes.

Ingredients:

  • Minced pork (formed into meatballs)
  • Silken tofu
  • Glass noodles
  • Carrots, cabbage, celery
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • White pepper

Instructions:

  1. Form pork into small meatballs and cook them in a pot of boiling water or chicken stock.
  2. Add tofu, noodles, and vegetables, then season with garlic, soy sauce, and white pepper.
  3. Once the vegetables are tender, serve piping hot.

This soup is a gentle, comforting option for those seeking something milder than typical Thai fare while still enjoying authentic flavors.

7. Tom Jeaw Moo (Spicy Pork Soup)

Tom Jeaw Moo is a rich, savory pork soup full of fiery flavors and heady spices. This rustic countryside dish embodies classic Thai cooking techniques with pork cuts simmered to perfection in a spicy broth.

Ingredients:

  • Pork belly or pork ribs
  • Toasted rice powder
  • Lime juice
  • Fish sauce
  • Chili flakes
  • Shallots
  • Coriander

Instructions:

  1. Boil pork belly or ribs slowly until tender, while preparing a base of lime juice, fish sauce, and chili flakes.
  2. Add shallots, toasted rice powder, and coriander for added texture and aroma.
  3. Simmer until the soup thickens slightly and serve warm.

Tom Jeaw Moo offers a rich umami taste, making it a fantastic choice for meat lovers craving a fiery soup.
