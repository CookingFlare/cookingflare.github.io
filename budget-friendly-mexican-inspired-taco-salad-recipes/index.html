Budget-Friendly Mexican-Inspired Taco Salad Recipes


Who doesn’t love a delicious taco salad? Packed with bold flavors, fresh ingredients, and a satisfying crunch, taco salads are a go-to meal for many. And the best part? They can be budget-friendly too! In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering Mexican-inspired taco salad recipes that won’t break the bank. So let’s dive right in!

Classic Beef Taco Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 head lettuce, shredded
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced black olives
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • Tortilla chips, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the taco seasoning packet and water according to the instructions on the packet. Stir well and simmer for a few minutes until the beef is well-coated and flavorful.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the shredded lettuce, cherry tomatoes, corn kernels, black beans, cheddar cheese, black olives, red onion, and cilantro.
  4. Add the cooked ground beef to the bowl and toss everything together until well-combined.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the sour cream and salsa to create the dressing.
  6. Drizzle the dressing over the taco salad and toss again to coat everything evenly.
  7. Serve the taco salad with a handful of tortilla chips on the side for an extra crunch.

Vegetarian Taco Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Tortilla strips, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the black beans, corn kernels, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and cilantro.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, olive oil, cumin, salt, and pepper to create the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the bean and corn mixture and toss well to coat everything evenly.
  4. In a separate bowl, place the mixed salad greens and top with the dressed bean and corn mixture.
  5. Add the diced avocado on top of the salad.
  6. Serve the vegetarian taco salad with a sprinkle of tortilla strips for added crunch.

Chicken Fajita Taco Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fajita seasoning
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced black olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • Tortilla strips, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breast slices and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the sliced bell peppers and onion. Cook until softened and slightly charred.
  3. Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and sprinkle the fajita seasoning over everything. Stir well to coat the chicken and vegetables with the seasoning.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, cherry tomatoes, shredded Monterey Jack cheese, black olives, and cilantro.
  5. Place the chicken and vegetable mixture on top of the salad greens.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together the sour cream and salsa to create the dressing.
  7. Drizzle the dressing over the taco salad and toss everything together until well-combined.
  8. Serve the chicken fajita taco salad with a handful of tortilla strips on the side.

Conclusion

Taco salads are a versatile and budget-friendly option for a quick and delicious meal. With these Mexican-inspired recipes, you can enjoy all the flavors and textures of a traditional taco in a refreshing salad form. Whether you prefer beef, vegetables, or chicken, these recipes will satisfy your cravings without breaking the bank. So go ahead and give them a try – your taste buds will thank you!

Remember, you can always get creative and customize these recipes to suit your preferences. Add some guacamole, change up the dressing, or swap ingredients to make it your own. The possibilities are endless when it comes to taco salads, so let your imagination run wild in the kitchen. Happy cooking!
