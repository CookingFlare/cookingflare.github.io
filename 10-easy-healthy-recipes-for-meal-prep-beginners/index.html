10 Easy Healthy Recipes for Meal Prep Beginners


Meal prepping is a fantastic way to save time, eat healthier, and ensure you always have delicious food ready to go. For beginners, the thought of meal prepping can be daunting, but it doesn’t have to be! With a few simple recipes and some planning, you can create a week’s worth of meals that are both nutritious and satisfying. Here are ten easy healthy recipes perfect for those just starting their meal prep journey.

1. Quinoa and Black Bean Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa under cold water. In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, bell pepper, corn, avocado, and cilantro.
  3. Drizzle with lime juice, season with salt and pepper, and toss to combine.
  4. Divide into meal prep containers and store in the fridge for up to five days.

2. Baked Chicken Breast with Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss vegetables with olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place chicken breasts on a baking sheet, surrounded by the seasoned vegetables.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Allow to cool, then slice the chicken and divide into containers with vegetables. Store in the refrigerator for up to four days.

3. Overnight Oats

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups almond milk (or any milk of choice)
  • 2 tablespoons chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit, nuts, or seeds for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine rolled oats, almond milk, chia seeds, honey, and vanilla. Stir well.
  2. Divide mixture into jars or containers and refrigerate overnight.
  3. In the morning, top with your favorite fruit, nuts, or seeds before enjoying.

4. Veggie Stir-Fry with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, carrots)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • Cooked brown rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute.
  3. Toss in mixed vegetables, cooking until tender. Stir in soy sauce and mix well.
  4. Serve over cooked brown rice in meal prep containers.

5. Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion, carrots, and celery until softened.
  2. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in lentils, diced tomatoes, broth, cumin, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat, cover, and let simmer for 30-40 minutes or until lentils are tender.
  5. Cool and store in containers for up to one week.

6. Greek Yogurt Parfait

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup granola
  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Honey for drizzling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In jars or containers, layer Greek yogurt, granola, and mixed berries.
  2. Drizzle with honey if desired.
  3. These parfaits can be stored in the refrigerator for up to three days.

7. Sweet Potato and Chickpea Bowls

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spinach or kale for serving
  • Tahini or dressing of choice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss sweet potatoes and chickpeas with olive oil, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, until sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Divide spinach or kale into containers, top with roasted sweet potatoes and chickpeas, and drizzle with tahini or your favorite dressing.

8. Egg Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup cheese (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together eggs and milk. Stir in spinach, bell pepper, cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour mixture evenly into the muffin tin.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the egg is set. Cool and store in containers for up to five days.

9. Zucchini Noodles with Marinara Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Grated parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add zucchini noodles and sauté for about 3-5 minutes until slightly softened.
  2. Stir in marinara sauce and Italian seasoning. Cook until heated through.
  3. Divide into meal prep containers and top with parmesan cheese if desired.

10. Chia Seed Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 2 cups almond milk (or any milk of choice)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit or nuts for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Stir well to combine.
  2. Let it sit for 10 minutes, then stir again to prevent clumping.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight until thickened.
  4. Serve with fresh fruit or nuts on top.

Tips for Successful Meal Prepping

  1. Plan Your Meals: Spend a little time each week planning your meals. Choose recipes that share ingredients to minimize waste and save money.

  2. Invest in Quality Containers: Use airtight containers to keep your meals fresh. Glass containers are great for reheating and easy to clean.

  3. Batch Cooking: Prepare large quantities of grains, proteins, and vegetables at once. This can significantly cut down your cooking time throughout the week.

  4. Label Your Meals: Use labels to mark the contents and date of your meals. This helps keep track of what to eat first and ensures freshness.

  5. Stay Organized: Keep your fridge and pantry organized so you can quickly access the items you need for meal prep.

By implementing these easy recipes and tips, meal prepping can become a simple and enjoyable part of your weekly routine. Not only will you save time, but you’ll also be setting yourself up for a week of healthy eating that fits your lifestyle. Happy meal prepping!
