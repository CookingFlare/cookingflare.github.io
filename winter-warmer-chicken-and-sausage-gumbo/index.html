Winter Warmer Chicken and Sausage Gumbo


As the cold winter months approach, there’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of hearty gumbo to warm you up from the inside out. This Winter Warmer Chicken and Sausage Gumbo recipe is a soul-soothing dish that combines bold flavors and comforting warmth, making it the perfect addition to your cold-weather recipe collection. Let’s dive into the details of this delicious and satisfying gumbo recipe that will surely become a family favorite.

Ingredients

For the Gumbo Base:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Gumbo:

  • 1 pound andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup okra, sliced (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • Hot sauce to taste
  • Cooked white rice for serving
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Roux

In a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven or large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Gradually whisk in the flour to create a smooth paste. Stir constantly for 20-30 minutes, or until the roux reaches a deep, rich brown color. Be careful not to let it burn.

Step 2: Prepare the Gumbo Base

Add the diced onion, bell pepper, celery, and minced garlic to the roux. Cook, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are softened, about 10 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Broth and Seasonings

Gradually pour in the chicken broth, stirring constantly to incorporate the roux into the liquid. Add the bay leaves, dried thyme, dried oregano, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 4: Brown the Sausage and Chicken

While the gumbo base is simmering, brown the sliced andouille sausage in a separate skillet over medium heat until it’s nicely browned. Remove the sausage and set it aside. In the same skillet, brown the chicken thigh pieces until they’re golden on all sides. Transfer the browned chicken to the simmering gumbo base.

Step 5: Finish the Gumbo

Add the sliced okra, diced tomatoes, and Worcestershire sauce to the gumbo. Let it simmer for an additional 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Adjust the seasoning with hot sauce, salt, and pepper according to your taste.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

To serve, ladle the Winter Warmer Chicken and Sausage Gumbo over a mound of cooked white rice in individual bowls. Garnish with chopped green onions for a pop of freshness and color.

Conclusion

This Winter Warmer Chicken and Sausage Gumbo is a labor of love that yields a deeply flavorful and comforting dish, perfect for cozy winter evenings. The rich, caramel-colored roux, tender chicken, smoky andouille sausage, and the earthy okra come together in a symphony of flavors that will warm both body and soul. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a bowl of something special, this gumbo is sure to satisfy.

So, embrace the winter chill with this heartwarming and flavor-packed recipe that will bring a taste of the south to your kitchen. Gather your ingredients, set aside some time for slow cooking, and get ready to savor the rich and soulful flavors of this Winter Warmer Chicken and Sausage Gumbo. Happy cooking!
