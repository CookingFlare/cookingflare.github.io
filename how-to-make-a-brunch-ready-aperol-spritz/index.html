How to Make a Brunch-Ready Aperol Spritz


Brunch is so much more than just a late breakfast or early lunch—it’s an experience, a moment to savor the weekend with friends, family, or even yourself. And what better way to elevate your brunch than with a cocktail that perfectly blends sweetness, bitterness, and effervescence? Enter the Aperol Spritz: an iconic, refreshing, and brilliantly orange drink that promises to add a touch of sophistication to your brunch spread.

Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering or just treating yourself, learning how to make a brunch-ready Aperol Spritz will open up new possibilities for pairing cocktails with mid-morning bites. Let’s dive into crafting the ideal Aperol Spritz—perfectly tailored for a brunch setting.

Why the Aperol Spritz is Perfect for Brunch

The beauty of the Aperol Spritz lies in its versatility and lightness. With its combination of Aperol—an Italian bitter aperitif—Prosecco, soda water, and a garnish of orange, it’s the ideal cocktail for daytime enjoyment. Not too heavy and delightfully fizzy, it complements a wide array of brunch staples, including savory dishes like eggs Benedict, creamy avocado toast, or sweet offerings such as fluffy pancakes and fruit-laden French toast.

The Aperol Spritz doesn’t overpower the meal; instead, it enhances the experience, striking a balance between refreshing and indulgent. Plus, its vibrant orange hue adds a pop of color to your dining table, making it as visually delightful as it is tasty.

Ingredients: What You’ll Need

To prepare a brunch-ready Aperol Spritz, gather the following essential ingredients:

  • 3 parts Prosecco: This sparkling Italian white wine brings bubbles and brightness to the cocktail. Choose a dry Prosecco for a balanced flavor.
  • 2 parts Aperol: Aperol’s unique blend of bitter orange, rhubarb, and herbs will act as the backbone of the drink.
  • 1 part soda water: A splash of soda water adds volume and extra fizz without overwhelming the drink with sweetness.
  • Ice cubes: Keep your Aperol Spritz chilled and refreshing by using quality ice cubes.
  • Orange slice: For garnish and an added burst of citrus flavor.

Optional variations might include edible flowers for a charming brunch-inspired aesthetic, or swapping Prosecco with another sparkling wine like Cava if you prefer a slightly different taste profile.

Step-by-Step Recipe

1. Choose the Right Glass

The right glass sets the stage for this sophisticated cocktail. A large wine glass or a stemmed balloon glass is the ideal choice. Its wide shape allows the effervescence to bubble gracefully while providing space for plenty of ice and garnishes.

2. Add Ice Cubes

Start by filling your glass generously with ice cubes. The ice not only keeps the cocktail chilled but also ensures the flavors stay crisp throughout your brunch.

3. Pour in the Aperol

Measure and pour two parts Aperol directly over the ice. The bitter orange aroma will start wafting as you pour, creating an immediate invitation to indulge.

4. Top with Prosecco

Add three parts Prosecco. Make sure to tilt the glass slightly as you pour to preserve the bubbles and avoid flattening the drink. The Prosecco is what gives the Aperol Spritz its signature fizz and flair.

5. Finish with Soda Water

Complete the cocktail with one part soda water. The soda water lightens the drink while enhancing its sparkling qualities, making it perfect for relaxed brunch sipping.

6. Garnish with Orange Slice

Finally, garnish the drink with a fresh orange slice. Add it to the rim or drop it into the glass for a burst of natural citrus. The orange slice doubles as both a visual treat and a subtle flavor enhancer.

Brunch Food Pairings

Pairing your Aperol Spritz with the right dishes can take your brunch to the next level. Here are some food ideas:

Savory Options

  • Smoked Salmon on Toast: The salty richness of smoked salmon matches beautifully with the slight bitterness of Aperol. Add cream cheese or avocado for a creamier texture.
  • Spinach and Mushroom Frittata: Light and full of flavor, a frittata complements the citrusy notes of the spritz.
  • Eggs Benedict: The buttery hollandaise sauce balances the vibrant taste of the cocktail.

Sweet Options

  • Berry Pancakes: The tangy sweetness of berries harmonizes with the orange undertones of Aperol.
  • Vanilla Bean Yogurt and Granola: Light and refreshing, this pairing mirrors the brightness of the spritz.

Cheese Board

For the ultimate brunch indulgence, assemble a cheese board featuring mild cheeses like brie, goat cheese, or mascarpone. Add fresh fruit, nuts, and crackers, and you’ll have a versatile spread that plays well with the drink’s complex flavors.

Tips for Elevating Your Aperol Spritz Game

1. Experiment with Sparkling Wines

While Prosecco is the classic choice, you can explore alternatives like sparkling rosé for a fruity twist or champagne for a slightly richer flavor profile.

2. Go Seasonal with Garnishes

Elevate your Aperol Spritz by adding seasonal garnishes such as edible flowers, sprigs of fresh mint, or thin slices of grapefruit. These accents not only enhance the drink’s appearance but also bring subtle fragrant notes.

3. Batch Preparation for Hosting

If you’re hosting a brunch and want to keep the Aperol Spritz flowing, consider preparing a jug or pitcher ahead of time. Mix Aperol and Prosecco in the right ratio and let guests add ice, soda water, and garnishes themselves. This hands-on approach is as fun as it is practical.

4. Perfecting the Ice

Believe it or not, ice can make or break your Aperol Spritz. Use crystal-clear ice cubes for a more professional presentation. If possible, store ice freshly made so it doesn’t absorb any odors from the freezer.

A Splash of History

The Aperol Spritz has its roots in Italian aperitivo culture—a tradition of enjoying light, refreshing drinks before meals. Aperol itself was created in 1919, and the spritz culture gained widespread popularity after World War II. Its combination of bright flavors and low alcohol content made it the perfect daytime drink. Over the decades, it has transcended its origins to become a global favorite, delighting brunch-goers from Milan to Manhattan.

Craft the Perfect Brunch Moment

By adding an Aperol Spritz to your brunch menu, you’re creating an opportunity to slow down, celebrate life’s little joys, and delight in your own culinary creations. The cocktail’s vibrancy, lightness, and easy preparation make it a no-brainer for those who want to host with style or enjoy a personal treat.

Next time you’re planning a weekend morning feast, let the Aperol Spritz take center stage. With a few simple ingredients and steps, you’ll create a show-stopping addition to your brunch table—and maybe even a new family or friend tradition. Cheers!
