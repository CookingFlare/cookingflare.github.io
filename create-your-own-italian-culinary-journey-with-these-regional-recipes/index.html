Create Your Own Italian Culinary Journey with these Regional Recipes


Italy is a country renowned worldwide for its mouthwatering cuisine that has delighted palates for generations. The beauty of Italian food lies in its regional diversity, with each area offering a distinct and unique way of preparing dishes with traditional recipes passed down through generations. This culinary adventure explores some scrumptious regional Italian recipes that you won’t want to miss.

1. Sardinian Malloreddus with Saffron Sauce

Sardinia is well-known for its rich and delectable cuisine based on fresh ingredients. Malloreddus are small, shell-shaped pasta hailing from the island, filled with a mixture of pork, sheep’s milk cheese, and traditionally served with a juicy ragù.

Ingredients:

  • 400g durum wheat flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g pork ground
  • 100g pecorino, grated
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 50g butter
  • 50g onion, finely chopped
  • 200g minced lamb or beef
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery rib, finely chopped
  • Saffron-infused broth or saffron cream sauce

Instructions:

  1. To make the Malloreddus, combine the flour and the eggs in a bowl, and gradually incorporate the water while kneading until a smooth dough forms. Let it rest for half an hour.
  2. Roll out the dough to 1/4 inch thickness, then cut it into the desired shell shapes.
  3. Combine the pork, pecorino, salt, and pepper in a separate bowl. Stuff each pasta piece with a small amount of the filling and seal the edges by pinching the dough.
  4. In a pan, cook the malloreddus in boiling salted water, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Heat the butter in a separate pan and gently fry the onions and the meat. Once browned, add the carrot and celery, and cook until they soften.
  6. Serve the malloreddus topped with the saffron-infused broth or saffron cream sauce for a Sardinian masterpiece.

2. Ligurian Pesto Genovese

A classic from the Ligurian coast, Pesto Genovese is an essential part of Italian culinary heritage. This iconic basil pesto sauce is made with pine nuts, garlic, and Pecorino Romano cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Coarsely chop the basil leaves and mix them with the pine nuts and garlic cloves, finely minced or processed, in a mortar or food processor.
  2. Gradually incorporate the grated Pecorino Romano and small amounts of olive oil while continuously mixing the pesto to create an emulsified, smooth sauce.
  3. Taste and season with salt if necessary.
  4. Serve the fragrant pesto with your favorite Ligurian-based pasta dish, such as trofie or corzetti, for an authentic Ligurian experience.

3. Tuscan Ribollita Soup

Tuscany boasts a deeply flavorful and heartwarming cuisine. One standout dish from this historic region is Ribollita, a vegetable soup packed full of nutritious ingredients like cannellini beans and kale.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g carrots, chopped
  • 200g potatoes, cubed
  • 400g canned cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • A handful of torn-up kale leaves, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup stale bread, cubed
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic on medium heat, until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the chopped carrots and potatoes, and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the drained beans, chopped kale, and vegetable broth. Let it simmer for 30 minutes, OR until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. To serve, ladle the Ribollita into bowls and top with the cubed bread, a sprinkle of Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.

4. Naples-Style Spaghetti with Meatballs

Naples, the birthplace of pizza and the ever-popular spaghetti and meatballs, has so much more to offer. This southern Italian dish combines the flavors of tender meatballs with robust tomato sauce over a bed of al dente spaghetti.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or pork
  • 1 cup large bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 (28-ounce) can of whole peeled San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 lb spaghetti

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, breadcrumbs, Parmigiano-Reggiano, parsley, milk, eggs, onion, garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well, and shape the mixture into 1-inch meatballs.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat, and brown the meatballs on all sides.
  3. Use a wooden spoon to squish the meatballs while cooking, until they release their juices and start to break down. Crush the San Marzano tomatoes using your hands or a potato masher, and stir them into the sauce.
  4. Bring to a simmer and let cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the meat is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  5. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  6. Plate the cooked spaghetti, ladle the sauce and meatballs over the top, and serve with a sprinkle of additional Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese for a taste of Naples straight to your table.
