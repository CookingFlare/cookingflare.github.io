How to Use Aquafaba in Vegan Baking


Aquafaba, the water leftover from cooking legumes, particularly chickpeas, has taken the vegan baking world by storm. This versatile ingredient has become a staple for those seeking to replace eggs in their recipes, offering a range of possibilities from meringues to cakes, and even mayonnaise. If you’re venturing into vegan baking or simply want to explore new ingredients, aquafaba is a fantastic choice. In this comprehensive guide, we will delve into what aquafaba is, how to use it, and share some delightful recipes to inspire your baking adventures.

What is Aquafaba?

Aquafaba is the viscous liquid that remains after cooking legumes, such as chickpeas, or the liquid found in canned chickpeas. It is rich in proteins, starches, and soluble fibers, making it an excellent emulsifier and foaming agent. This makes it particularly useful in vegan baking, where the absence of eggs can pose challenges in achieving the desired texture and structure in baked goods.

The Science Behind Aquafaba

The magic of aquafaba lies in its ability to mimic the properties of egg whites. When whipped, aquafaba can achieve a light, airy consistency similar to that of whipped egg whites. This is due to the proteins and starches forming a stable foam when air is incorporated, making it perfect for recipes that require volume and structure, such as meringues, mousses, and soufflés.

How to Use Aquafaba in Baking

Using aquafaba in your baking is relatively straightforward, but there are a few tips and tricks to ensure the best results. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

1. Choosing Aquafaba

You can either use homemade aquafaba or opt for the liquid from canned chickpeas. If you choose to make it yourself, simply cook dried chickpeas in water until soft, then strain the liquid. For a thicker aquafaba, reduce the cooking liquid by simmering it until it reaches the desired consistency.

2. Measuring Aquafaba

In most recipes, three tablespoons of aquafaba can replace one whole egg. For egg whites, two tablespoons of aquafaba is equivalent to one egg white. It’s important to measure accurately to achieve the right texture in your baked goods.

3. Whipping Aquafaba

To whip aquafaba, use a stand mixer or hand mixer. Start on low speed and gradually increase to high speed. Whip until soft peaks form, which usually takes about 5-10 minutes. If you wish to create stiffer peaks, continue whipping until you achieve the desired consistency. You can add a pinch of cream of tartar to stabilize the foam, similar to how you would with egg whites.

4. Incorporating Aquafaba into Recipes

When substituting aquafaba for eggs in your recipes, add it at the stage where you would normally add eggs. For recipes that require whipped aquafaba, such as meringues or mousses, fold it gently into the other ingredients to maintain the airiness.

Delicious Vegan Recipes Using Aquafaba

Now that you understand how to use aquafaba, let’s dive into some delicious vegan recipes that showcase this incredible ingredient.

1. Aquafaba Chocolate Mousse

Ingredients

  • 1 cup aquafaba (about 1 can of chickpeas)
  • 1 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Melt the dark chocolate chips in a heatproof bowl over simmering water or in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth. Allow to cool slightly.
  2. In a separate bowl, whip the aquafaba with a mixer until soft peaks form.
  3. Gently fold the melted chocolate into the whipped aquafaba, being careful not to deflate the mixture.
  4. Add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, folding until just combined.
  5. Spoon the mousse into serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving. Enjoy your rich and creamy chocolate mousse!

2. Aquafaba Vegan Meringues

Ingredients

  • 1 cup aquafaba
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°F (93°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine aquafaba and cream of tartar. Whip until soft peaks form.
  3. Gradually add sugar, a tablespoon at a time, continuing to whip until stiff peaks form and the mixture is glossy.
  4. Gently fold in the vanilla extract.
  5. Spoon or pipe the meringue mixture onto the prepared baking sheet, creating small mounds.
  6. Bake for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meringues are dry and crisp. Allow to cool before serving.

3. Aquafaba Pancakes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup non-dairy milk
  • 1/2 cup aquafaba
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, combine non-dairy milk, aquafaba, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Let the batter sit for 5 minutes.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form and the edges look set, then flip and cook until golden brown.
  5. Serve with maple syrup, fruits, or your favorite toppings.

4. Aquafaba Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup aquafaba
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup vegan chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix the melted coconut oil, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth. Stir in aquafaba and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden. Let cool before enjoying.

Tips for Success with Aquafaba

  1. Quality Matters: The quality of your aquafaba can affect your results. If using canned aquafaba, choose a brand that uses minimal additives.

  2. Consistency is Key: For recipes requiring whipped aquafaba, make sure it is at room temperature before whipping, as cold aquafaba may not whip as well.

  3. Experiment: Aquafaba works differently in various recipes, so don’t hesitate to experiment with different ratios and techniques to find what works best for you.

  4. Storage: If you have leftover aquafaba, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week or freeze it in ice cube trays for later use.

Conclusion

Aquafaba is a game-changer in vegan baking, providing a versatile and effective alternative to eggs. With its ability to whip into a stable foam and its emulsifying properties, it opens up a world of possibilities for creating delicious vegan treats. Whether you’re whipping up a light mousse, baking airy meringues, or creating fluffy pancakes, aquafaba can help you achieve the texture and flavor you desire.

By incorporating aquafaba into your baking repertoire, you not only enhance your culinary creations but also embrace a more sustainable and compassionate way of cooking. So grab a can of chickpeas or cook up some legumes, and start experimenting with aquafaba in your kitchen today! Happy baking!
