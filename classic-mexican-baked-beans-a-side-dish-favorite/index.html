Classic Mexican Baked Beans: A Side Dish Favorite


Baked beans are a classic side dish that adds a burst of flavor and texture to any meal. In this recipe, we will be exploring the delicious and vibrant world of Classic Mexican Baked Beans. Packed with bold flavors and spices, these beans are sure to become a favorite at your dinner table. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried pinto beans
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 jalapeno peppers, seeded and diced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by rinsing the dried pinto beans under cold water. Then, place them in a large bowl and cover with water. Let them soak overnight or for at least 8 hours. This step will help soften the beans and reduce their cooking time.

  2. Once the beans have soaked, drain and rinse them again. Set them aside.

  3. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and diced jalapeno peppers. Sauté until the onions are translucent and the peppers are tender.

  4. Add the drained beans to the pot and stir well to combine with the onion and pepper mixture.

  5. Pour in the can of diced tomatoes and their juices. Stir in the chili powder, ground cumin, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. These spices will infuse the beans with the authentic Mexican flavors we crave.

  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the beans simmer for about 2 hours, or until they are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  7. Once the beans are cooked to perfection, remove the pot from the heat. Use a fork or a potato masher to lightly mash some of the beans. This step will help thicken the sauce and create a creamy texture.

  8. Serve the Classic Mexican Baked Beans hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. They are a versatile side dish that pairs well with tacos, enchiladas, or simply enjoyed on their own.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier version of these beans, feel free to leave the seeds in the jalapeno peppers or add a pinch of cayenne pepper.
  • For a smoky flavor, you can also add a few dashes of liquid smoke to the bean mixture.
  • To save time, you can use canned pinto beans instead of dried. Just be sure to drain and rinse them before adding them to the pot.
  • Leftover beans can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. They also freeze well, so you can make a big batch and have them ready for future meals.

Conclusion

Classic Mexican Baked Beans are a side dish that brings a burst of flavor and a touch of tradition to any meal. With their rich and hearty texture, these beans are sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re hosting a Mexican-themed dinner party or simply looking to spice up your weeknight meals, these beans are a must-try. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to savor the authentic taste of Mexico right in your own kitchen!
