A Taste of Paris: French Onion Tart


Bonjour, food enthusiasts! Today, we’re bringing a taste of Paris to your kitchen with a delightful recipe for French Onion Tart. This savory and elegant tart is a perfect blend of caramelized onions, flavorful herbs, and creamy cheese, all nestled in a buttery, flaky crust. Whether you’re a fan of French cuisine or simply looking to impress your guests with a sophisticated yet approachable dish, this French Onion Tart is sure to delight your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup Gruyère cheese, grated

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a food processor, pulse the flour and salt together. Add the cold butter cubes and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and pulse until the dough comes together.
  3. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Caramelize the Onions

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are soft and golden brown, about 30-40 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle the sugar over the onions and continue to cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the fresh thyme leaves, salt, and black pepper. Allow the onions to cool slightly.

Step 3: Preheat the Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Step 4: Roll Out the Dough

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle large enough to line a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough to the tart pan and press it into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough.

Step 5: Assemble the Tart

  1. Spread the caramelized onions evenly over the tart crust.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and eggs. Pour this mixture over the onions.
  3. Sprinkle the grated Gruyère cheese over the top.

Step 6: Bake the Tart

  1. Place the tart on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is set.
  2. Allow the tart to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This French Onion Tart pairs beautifully with a crisp green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette. For a complete Parisian experience, consider serving it alongside a glass of your favorite French wine.

Final Thoughts

The French Onion Tart is a delightful combination of sweet, savory, and creamy flavors that will transport you to the charming streets of Paris with every bite. Whether it’s a cozy dinner at home or a gathering with friends, this tart is a sophisticated yet comforting addition to any menu. So, don your imaginary beret, embrace the essence of French cooking, and indulge in the irresistible allure of this classic dish.

Enjoy creating this culinary masterpiece, and savor the enchanting flavors of A Taste of Paris: French Onion Tart!
