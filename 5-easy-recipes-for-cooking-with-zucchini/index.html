5 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Zucchini


Zucchini, with its mild flavor and versatile texture, is a superstar vegetable that can elevate a variety of dishes. Whether you’re looking for a healthy side, a hearty main, or a delightful snack, zucchini is the perfect ingredient to incorporate into your meals. In this blog post, we’ll explore five easy and delicious recipes that showcase this fantastic summer squash. Get ready to enjoy the taste and health benefits of zucchini!

1. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Zucchini Noodles: Using a spiralizer or a vegetable peeler, create noodle-like strands from the zucchinis. If using a peeler, aim for thin, long strips. Set aside.

  2. Make the Pesto: In a food processor, combine basil leaves, Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, and garlic. Pulse until finely chopped. While the processor is running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture is smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Combine: In a large bowl, toss the zucchini noodles with the pesto until evenly coated.

  4. Serve: Top with halved cherry tomatoes for a burst of color and flavor. Enjoy immediately as a refreshing, low-carb meal!

Tips:

  • For a protein boost, consider adding grilled chicken or shrimp on top.
  • You can also sauté the zucchini noodles for a few minutes if you prefer a softer texture.

2. Stuffed Zucchini Boats

Ingredients:

  • 4 small zucchinis
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or rice
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare Zucchini: Slice the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create “boats.”

  3. Mix Filling: In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa or rice, diced tomatoes, black beans, cumin, chili powder, and half of the cheese. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Stuff Zucchini: Fill each zucchini boat with the mixture and place them on a baking dish. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top.

  5. Bake: Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the zucchini is tender and cheese is bubbly.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle with fresh cilantro before serving. These stuffed zucchini boats are a wholesome and filling meal!

Tips:

  • Feel free to customize the filling with your favorite ingredients such as corn, bell peppers, or ground turkey.
  • Serve with a side salad for a complete meal.

3. Zucchini Fritters

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, grated
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Zucchini: Grate the zucchinis and place them in a clean kitchen towel. Squeeze out excess moisture.

  2. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the grated zucchini, flour, Parmesan cheese, eggs, green onions, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Heat Oil: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.

  4. Form Fritters: Drop spoonfuls of the zucchini mixture into the skillet, flattening them slightly with the back of the spoon.

  5. Cook: Fry for 3–4 minutes on each side or until golden brown. Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.

  6. Serve: Serve warm with sour cream or yogurt for dipping. These fritters make a great appetizer or snack!

Tips:

  • Add herbs like dill or parsley for added flavor.
  • They can also be baked in the oven for a healthier option.

4. Zucchini and Corn Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, diced
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen corn
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine diced zucchini, corn, red onion, bell pepper, and parsley.

  2. Dress the Salad: In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Pour over the salad and toss to combine.

  3. Chill and Serve: For best flavor, let the salad chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This salad is refreshing and perfect for summer gatherings!

Tips:

  • For a protein boost, add grilled chicken or chickpeas.
  • This salad can be served as a side dish or a light main course.

5. Baked Zucchini Chips

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 225°F (110°C).

  2. Prepare Zucchini: Thinly slice the zucchinis using a mandoline or a sharp knife. The thinner the slices, the crispier the chips will be.

  3. Season: In a bowl, toss the zucchini slices with olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, and salt.

  4. Bake: Spread the zucchini slices in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for about 2 hours or until the chips are crisp and golden, flipping halfway through.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow the chips to cool before serving. Enjoy as a healthy snack or a crunchy side dish!

Tips:

  • Experiment with different seasonings like Italian herbs, chili powder, or nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor.
  • Store any leftovers in an airtight container for a few days.

Conclusion

Zucchini is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, making it a fantastic choice for healthy cooking. These five easy recipes demonstrate the versatility of zucchini, whether you’re in the mood for a light salad, hearty stuffed boats, or crispy snacks. Incorporate these recipes into your meal planning, and enjoy the delightful flavors of zucchini throughout the week. Happy cooking!
