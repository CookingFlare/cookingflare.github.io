Butternut Squash and Sage Ravioli

Who doesn’t love a good homemade ravioli? This one’s filled with a comforting yet elegant butternut squash and sage filling that will dazzle your taste buds and make your dinner guests beg for more. Ingredients 1 medium butternut squash 1 cup fresh sage leaves Salt and freshly ground black pepper ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 cup all-purpose flour 1 egg Additional flour, for dusting Water Instructions For the Filling Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the butternut squash in half lengthwise, put it onto a baking sheet lined with parchment, and roast it in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes, or until tender and browned at the edges. While the squash is roasting, strip the fresh sage leaves and finely chop them. Set aside. Once the squash is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Then lower the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C). Scoop out the squash flesh, and mash it in a big bowl with the chopped sage, a generous pinch of salt, and about half of the grated Parmesan. Set aside. For the Pasta Combine flour, the remaining grated Parmesan, and a pinch of salt in a food processor. Pulse a few times to combine. Combine the beaten egg and water in a separate bowl, and then gradually add it to the flour mixture while processing. The dough should begin to come together in a ball. Remove the dough from the food processor, knead it a bit if needed, and wrap it in plastic wrap. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes. To Assemble and Cook the Ravioli Roll out the dough to about 1/16" thickness using a rolling pin or pasta machine, and cut into 3" circles with a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass. Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle, following the circle’s edge with your finger to create a slight rim. Brush the edges of the dough with water, and fold the dough in half to create a semi-circle. Press down around the filling gently to make sure the two dough layers adhere, and then carefully, pin the edges together with a fork, leaving the ravioli on a flat surface. Heat a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil, and cook the ravioli for about 3-4 minutes or until they float to the surface. Carefully remove the ravioli with a slotted spoon, and serve immediately, topped with your favorite sauce. Tips and Tricks Remember to always use fresh ingredients and rotate your produce to ensure the best possible flavor. If you’re having trouble rolling out your pasta thin enough, let your dough rest for a while, or consider allowing your pasta machine to do the work for you. Fresh sage is a must here. It’s earthy, slightly sweet flavor complements the nutty squash surprisingly well. If you dread the idea of handling raw sage, you can always toast it and make a simple sage butter. This butternut squash and sage ravioli is a great dish for a cozy dinner at home, with the added bonus of impressing your friends and family.
