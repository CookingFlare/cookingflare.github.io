Korean Fried Fish (Saengseon Twigi) Recipe


Korean cuisine is renowned for its unique flavors and bold seasonings, and one dish that perfectly encapsulates this is Korean Fried Fish, also known as Saengseon Twigi. This dish features tender fish coated in a crispy batter, deep-fried to golden perfection, and served with a dipping sauce that adds an extra punch of flavor. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or simply looking to try something new, this recipe will bring a taste of Korea right into your kitchen.

In this article, we’ll dive into the details of how to make this beloved dish, providing step-by-step instructions, tips, and variations to ensure your Saengseon Twigi turns out perfectly every time.

What is Saengseon Twigi?

Saengseon Twigi (생선튀기) is a traditional Korean dish that consists of fish fried in a crispy batter. The term “twigi” refers to the process of deep-frying, and “saengseon” simply means fish. While the type of fish can vary, the most common choices include pollock, flounder, or mackerel due to their mild flavor and firm texture, which hold up well during frying.

The fish is typically served alongside a variety of accompaniments, such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, and a tangy dipping sauce that balances the richness of the fried fish. It is a dish that pairs perfectly with steamed rice, making it a popular choice for both casual meals and special occasions.

Ingredients for Korean Fried Fish (Saengseon Twigi)

To prepare the perfect Korean Fried Fish at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Fish:

  • 4 medium-sized white fish fillets (such as pollock, flounder, or mackerel)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt (for seasoning)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper (optional, for seasoning)
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine (or sake, optional, for marinating)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce (for marinating)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil (adds a rich, nutty flavor)

For the Batter:

  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch (for extra crispiness)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder (helps create a lighter, fluffier batter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt (for seasoning)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 cup of cold water (adjust as necessary for batter consistency)
  • 1 egg (optional, for added richness)

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil (or canola oil) for deep frying

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of finely minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of chopped green onions
  • 1/4 teaspoon of chili flakes (optional, for a spicy kick)

How to Make Korean Fried Fish (Saengseon Twigi)

Now that we’ve gathered all the ingredients, let’s dive into the cooking process. Follow these steps for a crispy, flavorful Saengseon Twigi:

Step 1: Prepare the Fish

  1. Clean the Fish Fillets: If using whole fish, make sure they are cleaned, gutted, and filleted. Remove the bones carefully, and cut the fish into manageable pieces if necessary. For convenience, you can also use pre-filleted fish from your local seafood market.

  2. Marinate the Fish: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, rice wine (if using), sesame oil, salt, and black pepper. Stir well until the ingredients are fully incorporated. Place the fish fillets into the marinade and gently toss them to coat. Let the fish sit for about 10–15 minutes to absorb the flavors. Marinating the fish enhances its flavor and helps tenderize it, making for a better-tasting final dish.

Step 2: Make the Batter

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and black pepper. These dry ingredients will create the base for the batter, giving the fried fish its light, crispy texture.

  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Crack an egg (if using) into the dry mixture and add cold water gradually. Whisk the batter until smooth and slightly thick, resembling pancake batter. The cold water is essential for achieving a crispy texture, so make sure the water is chilled. Adjust the amount of water based on the consistency of the batter; it should be thick enough to coat the fish but not too heavy.

Step 3: Fry the Fish

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large frying pan or deep fryer, pour enough vegetable oil to submerge the fish fillets about halfway. Heat the oil to 350°F (175°C). A good test to check if the oil is hot enough is to drop a small amount of batter into the oil – if it sizzles immediately and rises to the surface, the oil is ready for frying.

  2. Coat the Fish: Take each fish fillet and dip it into the batter, ensuring it is fully coated. Let the excess batter drip off to avoid clumping in the oil.

  3. Fry the Fish: Gently place the battered fish fillets into the hot oil. Fry them in batches, ensuring the fish is not overcrowded. Fry each side for about 3–4 minutes, or until the coating turns golden brown and crispy. Use tongs to flip the fillets carefully.

  4. Drain the Oil: Once the fish is cooked, remove it from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil. This step is crucial for keeping the fish crispy.

Step 4: Make the Dipping Sauce

While the fish is frying, you can prepare the dipping sauce. In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic, chopped green onions, and chili flakes (if using). Stir the ingredients until the sugar dissolves, and the sauce is well-mixed.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the fish fillets are fried to perfection, arrange them on a serving plate. Serve the crispy Saengseon Twigi with a bowl of steamed rice and a side of kimchi or pickled vegetables for a truly authentic Korean meal. Drizzle the dipping sauce over the fish or serve it on the side for dipping. The sauce’s tangy, savory flavors complement the crispy fish beautifully.

Tips for the Perfect Korean Fried Fish

  • Choose the Right Fish: While you can use any white fish, certain types like pollock, flounder, and mackerel work especially well for frying due to their firm texture. Avoid using fish with a very soft flesh, as it may fall apart during frying.

  • Use Cold Water for the Batter: The secret to crispy batter lies in the use of cold water. The cold temperature helps create steam during frying, which results in a light and crispy texture. You can even chill the batter in the fridge before using it.

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Frying the fish in small batches ensures the oil stays hot, which is key to achieving a crispy coating. Overcrowding the pan will lower the oil temperature, causing the batter to become soggy.

  • Serve Immediately: Saengseon Twigi is best enjoyed immediately after frying while it’s still crispy. If you need to keep it warm for a little while, place the fried fish on a baking sheet in a preheated oven at 200°F (93°C) to keep it crispy without overcooking it.

Variations of Saengseon Twigi

While the classic version of Saengseon Twigi is delicious on its own, there are a few variations you can try:

  • Spicy Saengseon Twigi: For a spicier twist, add gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) or gochujang (Korean chili paste) to the batter or dipping sauce. The heat from the chili will complement the crispy fish perfectly.

  • Vegetable-Filled Version: If you’re looking for added texture, consider adding finely chopped vegetables (like carrots, onions, and bell peppers) to the batter. These will give the fried fish a more substantial crunch and a slight sweetness.

  • Crispy Fish Tacos: For a fun fusion twist, use the crispy fish to make Korean-inspired fish tacos. Top the fried fish with shredded cabbage, spicy mayo, and a drizzle of the dipping sauce for a refreshing take on the dish.

Final Thoughts

Korean Fried Fish (Saengseon Twigi) is a wonderfully satisfying dish that combines crispy textures, savory flavors, and a delightful dipping sauce. With its simple yet delicious preparation, it’s a perfect addition to your Korean culinary repertoire. Whether you serve it as a main dish or as part of a Korean-style feast, this crispy fried fish is sure to impress your friends and family.

By following this recipe and applying a few simple tips, you’ll be able to make Saengseon Twigi that is just as good (if not better!) than what you’d find in a Korean restaurant. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy this delectable dish in the comfort of your own home.
