Homemade Pineapple Coconut Bars: A Tropical Escape

There’s something undeniably delightful about the combination of pineapple and coconut. The sweet, tangy flavor of pineapple paired with the tropical, nutty essence of coconut creates a sensation that transports you to a sun-drenched beach with every bite. If you’re looking for a dessert that embodies the spirit of a tropical escape, then these homemade pineapple coconut bars are exactly what you need. Ingredients For the Crust: 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour ½ cup confectioners’ sugar ¾ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed For the Pineapple Coconut Filling: 1 cup granulated sugar ¼ cup all-purpose flour ½ teaspoon baking powder ¼ teaspoon salt 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 ½ cups sweetened shredded coconut 1 cup crushed pineapple, well-drained For the Glaze: 1 cup confectioners’ sugar 1-2 tablespoons milk ¼ teaspoon vanilla extract Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Crust Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving a slight overhang for easy removal. In a food processor, pulse together the flour and confectioners’ sugar. Add the cold, cubed butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until lightly golden. Step 2: Make the Pineapple Coconut Filling In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and mix until well combined. Gently fold in the shredded coconut and crushed pineapple. Spread the filling over the baked crust and return to the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the center is set. Step 3: Prepare the Glaze In a small bowl, whisk together the confectioners’ sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Drizzle the glaze over the warm pineapple coconut bars. Step 4: Chill and Serve Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Once cooled, refrigerate the bars for at least 2 hours before cutting into squares. Serve and enjoy the tropical escape in every delicious bite! Tips and Variations For an extra layer of indulgence, sprinkle toasted coconut over the glaze before chilling. You can use fresh pineapple instead of canned, but make sure to drain it well to avoid excess moisture in the filling. These bars can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, making them a perfect make-ahead dessert for gatherings and parties. Conclusion These homemade pineapple coconut bars are a delightful tropical treat that brings the flavors of the tropics right to your kitchen. With a buttery crust, luscious pineapple coconut filling, and a sweet glaze, every bite is a blissful escape to a sunny paradise. Whether you’re hosting a summer soiree or simply craving a taste of the tropics, these bars are sure to bring a smile to your face and a touch of sunshine to your day. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary journey to a tropical paradise with these irresistible homemade pineapple coconut bars. ...
