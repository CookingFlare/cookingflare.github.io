How to Make Homemade Chicken Nuggets


Chicken nuggets are a classic comfort food that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. Whether served as a snack, part of a meal, or even as a fun party appetizer, chicken nuggets are beloved for their crispy exterior and tender, juicy interior. While it’s easy to grab a box of frozen nuggets from the store, there’s something truly special about making your own at home. Homemade chicken nuggets are not only fresher, but you also have complete control over the ingredients, allowing you to create a healthier, tastier version.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of making homemade chicken nuggets from scratch. From preparing the chicken to creating the perfect coating and frying or baking them to golden perfection, this recipe will ensure you create mouthwatering chicken nuggets every time.

Ingredients for Homemade Chicken Nuggets

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need for your homemade chicken nuggets.

For the Chicken:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breasts – 2 large breasts (you can also use chicken thighs for a juicier option).
  • Salt – To season the chicken.
  • Pepper – For extra flavor.
  • Garlic powder – Adds a subtle, savory depth.
  • Onion powder – A great complement to the garlic powder.
  • Paprika – For a mild smoky flavor.
  • Egg – To help bind the coating ingredients.

For the Coating:

  • All-purpose flour – This will form the base of the coating.
  • Breadcrumbs – You can use either plain breadcrumbs or panko breadcrumbs for extra crunch.
  • Parmesan cheese – Optional, but a little grated Parmesan in the breadcrumb mixture can add an extra layer of flavor.
  • Cornstarch – Helps create an extra crispy outer layer.
  • Milk – Used to help create a wet batter for dipping the chicken pieces.

For Frying or Baking:

  • Vegetable oil – If frying, you’ll need oil for deep frying. Alternatively, you can use olive oil if baking.
  • Spray oil – For baking, to lightly coat the nuggets to help them crisp up in the oven.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Homemade Chicken Nuggets

Now that we have all the ingredients, let’s start making homemade chicken nuggets. We’ll go through each step in detail, ensuring you have a perfect batch of chicken nuggets at the end.

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

The first step in making homemade chicken nuggets is to prepare the chicken breasts. You’ll want to slice the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces. Aim for pieces that are about 1 to 1.5 inches in size, so they cook evenly and quickly.

  1. Trim any excess fat from the chicken breasts and cut them into small chunks. If you prefer uniform pieces, feel free to use a meat mallet to gently flatten the chicken slightly, making them even in thickness.
  2. Season the chicken with a pinch of salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika. Toss the chicken well to ensure the seasonings coat every piece.

At this point, your chicken is ready to go into the breading process. But before you do that, let’s prepare the coating mixture.

Step 2: Prepare the Coating Station

To get that crispy, golden crust, you’ll need three separate bowls for the breading process. This technique is essential to ensure that each piece of chicken is evenly coated.

  1. Bowl 1 (Flour mixture): In the first bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of pepper. This mixture will help the egg adhere to the chicken pieces.
  2. Bowl 2 (Egg wash): In the second bowl, crack 2 eggs and beat them lightly. Add a splash of milk to make the egg wash smoother.
  3. Bowl 3 (Breadcrumb mixture): In the third bowl, combine 1 cup of breadcrumbs with 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese (optional). Add a teaspoon of garlic powder, a pinch of paprika, and a pinch of salt for extra flavor. You can adjust the seasoning in this bowl according to your taste preferences.

Once you’ve prepared the breading station, you’re ready to start the coating process.

Step 3: Coat the Chicken

Coating your chicken properly is key to achieving the perfect crispy texture. Follow these steps for optimal results:

  1. Dredge the chicken pieces in the flour mixture, making sure each piece is thoroughly coated. Shake off any excess flour.
  2. Dip the floured chicken into the egg wash. Let any excess egg drip off.
  3. Roll the chicken in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to ensure the crumbs adhere well to the chicken. This will give the nuggets that irresistible crunch.

Repeat this process until all of your chicken pieces are coated.

Step 4: Frying or Baking the Chicken Nuggets

At this point, you can choose whether to fry or bake your homemade chicken nuggets. Both methods yield delicious results, but they have different cooking times and textures.

Frying Method (Crispier Nuggets):

  1. Heat the oil: In a large, deep pan or skillet, heat about 1 to 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. The oil is ready when it reaches around 350°F (175°C). You can test it by dropping a small breadcrumb into the oil – if it sizzles, the oil is hot enough.
  2. Fry the nuggets: Carefully add the coated chicken pieces to the hot oil, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding. Fry for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and fully cooked through (an internal temperature of 165°F/74°C).
  3. Drain the excess oil: Once fried, transfer the chicken nuggets to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

Baking Method (Healthier Option):

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and lightly spray it with cooking oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Arrange the chicken nuggets: Place the breaded chicken pieces on the prepared baking sheet, making sure they aren’t touching each other.
  3. Bake the nuggets: Lightly spray the tops of the nuggets with cooking spray for a golden, crispy finish. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are golden brown and cooked through. If you want an extra crispy finish, you can broil them for an additional 2-3 minutes at the end.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once your homemade chicken nuggets are ready, it’s time to serve them up! Whether you’ve fried them to crispy perfection or baked them for a lighter version, these nuggets will surely be a hit.

Serve your chicken nuggets with a variety of dipping sauces such as:

  • Ketchup
  • Honey mustard
  • BBQ sauce
  • Ranch dressing
  • Buffalo sauce

Pair your homemade chicken nuggets with a side of crispy fries or a fresh salad for a complete meal. You can also serve them as a snack or appetizer at your next party or family gathering.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Chicken Nuggets

To ensure your chicken nuggets turn out perfectly every time, here are a few helpful tips:

  1. Use chicken breasts or thighs: For the best results, use boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs. Chicken thighs are juicier and can add more flavor, while chicken breasts tend to be leaner.
  2. Double breading: If you want an extra crispy coating, you can double dip your chicken. After the first round of coating, dip the chicken back into the egg wash and then the breadcrumbs again for a thicker crust.
  3. Experiment with seasonings: Feel free to get creative with your seasonings. You can add cayenne pepper for a bit of heat, dried herbs for a fresh flavor, or even Italian seasoning for a twist.
  4. Air fryer option: If you have an air fryer, it’s a great alternative to baking or frying. Spray the chicken nuggets lightly with oil and cook at 400°F (200°C) for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  5. Freeze for later: If you have leftovers or want to make a large batch for future meals, you can freeze the breaded chicken nuggets before cooking. Arrange the uncooked nuggets on a baking sheet in a single layer, freeze for a couple of hours, and then store them in a freezer-safe bag or container. When you’re ready to cook, you can bake or fry them directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

Conclusion

Making homemade chicken nuggets is a fun and rewarding experience that results in a tasty, customizable dish everyone will love. Whether you prefer them crispy from the fryer or healthier from the oven, these homemade chicken nuggets are far superior to store-bought versions. Plus, you can adjust the flavors to your liking and ensure you’re using the freshest, most wholesome ingredients.

By following this simple step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to create a batch of chicken nuggets that will have your family and friends coming back for more. So, next time you’re craving something crispy and delicious, skip the fast food and make your own homemade chicken nuggets. They’re easier than you think, and the results are sure to impress!
