Delicious and Healthy Paleo-Friendly Seafood Recipes


As the world becomes increasingly health-conscious, the demand for paleo-friendly recipes has skyrocketed. For those who follow a paleo diet, it can be challenging to find seafood recipes that are both delicious and compliant with the diet’s guidelines. In this post, we’ll explore some mouth-watering and nutritious paleo-friendly seafood recipes that are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Grilled Salmon with Lemon and Herbs

Salmon is an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, which are essential for heart health and brain function. This recipe is a simple and flavorful way to prepare salmon, perfect for a quick weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 lemons, sliced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon slices, rosemary, and thyme.
  3. Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish and brush the top of each fillet with the lemon-herb mixture.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Grill the salmon for 4-6 minutes per side, or until cooked through.
  6. Serve hot with a side of roasted vegetables.

Baked Cod with Garlic and Capers

Cod is a mild-flavored fish that pairs well with a variety of flavors. This recipe is a great way to add some excitement to your seafood dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cod fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp capers, rinsed and chopped
  • 2 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Place the cod fillets on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the cod, then sprinkle the garlic and capers on top.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until cooked through.
  7. Serve hot with a side of roasted asparagus.

Spaghetti Squash with Shrimp and Pesto

Shrimp is a great source of protein and can be cooked in a variety of ways. This recipe is a fun twist on traditional pasta, using spaghetti squash instead.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium spaghetti squash
  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup freshly made pesto
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash on a baking sheet, cut side up.
  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the squash, then sprinkle the garlic on top.
  5. Roast the squash for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  6. While the squash is cooking, heat the pesto in a large skillet over medium heat.
  7. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and cooked through.
  8. Serve the shrimp on top of the roasted spaghetti squash.

Thai-Style Fish Cakes with Avocado Slaw

Fish cakes are a popular dish in many Asian cultures, and this recipe is a great way to make them paleo-friendly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fish of your choice (such as cod or tilapia), flaked
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the fish, almond flour, coconut milk, garlic, ginger, and cilantro.
  2. Mix well until everything is fully incorporated.
  3. Divide the mixture into 4-6 portions, depending on the size you prefer.
  4. Shape each portion into a fish cake.
  5. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the fish cakes to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
  7. Serve the fish cakes with a side of avocado slaw, made by combining the diced avocado, chopped cilantro, and lime juice.

Conclusion

These paleo-friendly seafood recipes are perfect for those who follow a paleo diet or are looking for healthy and delicious meal options. With a variety of flavors and textures, you’re sure to find something that suits your taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, these recipes are easy to follow and require minimal ingredients. So go ahead and give them a try – your taste buds will thank you!
