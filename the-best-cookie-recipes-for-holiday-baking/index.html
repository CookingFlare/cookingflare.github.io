The Best Cookie Recipes for Holiday Baking


The holiday season is the time of year when kitchens transform into warm havens brimming with the sweet aroma of baked goods. Cookies, in particular, are a timeless favorite that brings joy to holiday gatherings, whether you’re preparing treats for a crowd, filling gift tins for loved ones, or indulging in a quiet moment at home with freshly baked goodies. From classic traditions to inventive flavors, there’s a cookie recipe for everyone to love during the festive season. Below, we’ll explore step-by-step instructions, expert tips, and variations for irresistible holiday cookie recipes to make your celebrations extra special.

1. Classic Sugar Cookies for Decorating

Sugar cookies have long been the foundation of holiday baking, loved for their crisp edges, tender centers, and versatility in decoration. Whether you prefer colorful icing designs or a sprinkle of sanding sugar, they offer a blank canvas for your creativity.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  2. Cream the butter and sugar: In a mixer, beat the softened butter and sugar together until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add egg and extracts: Mix in the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract until well incorporated.
  4. Combine dry with wet: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture until the dough forms.
  5. Chill the dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least one hour or until firm.
  6. Roll and cut: Roll out the dough to about ¼-inch thickness on a lightly floured surface. Use festive cookie cutters for shapes.
  7. Bake: Bake cookies at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes or until edges are lightly golden. Cool completely before decorating.

Decorating Tips:

  • Use royal icing for intricate designs and a glossy finish.
  • For a quick option, sprinkle with sanding sugar before baking.

2. Chewy Ginger Molasses Cookies

Spiced cookies are quintessential for the holidays, and ginger molasses cookies offer rich, warm flavors that evoke memories of fireplaces and frosty evenings. Their chewy texture and spiced aroma make them unforgettable.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ cup molasses
  • Granulated sugar for rolling

Instructions:

  1. Combine dry ingredients: Whisk together the flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, salt, and baking soda in a large bowl.
  2. Cream butter and sugar: Beat the butter and brown sugar until smooth. Add the egg and molasses, mixing until fully incorporated.
  3. Form dough: Gradually add dry ingredients, mixing until the dough comes together.
  4. Chill the dough: Wrap and refrigerate the dough for an hour or until firm.
  5. Shape and roll: Form dough into small balls and roll them in granulated sugar.
  6. Bake: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10 minutes. Let cool slightly before transferring to a wire rack.

Variation:

  • Add chopped crystallized ginger or a pinch of ground nutmeg for additional layers of flavor.

3. Peppermint Chocolate Chip Cookies

Craving a burst of holiday flavor that combines sweetness with minty freshness? Peppermint chocolate chip cookies are the answer. This perfect combination of chocolate and peppermint is guaranteed to become a seasonal favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • Crushed candy canes for topping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare dry mix: In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Cream butter and sugars: Beat softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth and creamy. Mix in eggs, vanilla, and peppermint extract.
  3. Combine ingredients: Gradually add the dry mix, stirring until well blended. Fold in chocolate chips.
  4. Scoop and bake: Drop dough onto lined baking sheets using a cookie scoop. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes.
  5. Finish with candy canes: While cookies are warm, sprinkle crushed candy canes over the top for a festive touch.

4. Italian Ricotta Cookies

Light, fluffy, and decadently soft, Italian ricotta cookies bring a unique twist to holiday baking. Their mild flavor pairs beautifully with sweet glazes and bright sprinkles.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese (whole milk preferred)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

For the Glaze:

  • 1 ½ cups powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Holiday sprinkles for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: Combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Beat butter and sugar: Cream the butter and granulated sugar until fluffy, then mix in ricotta, eggs, vanilla, and lemon zest.
  3. Combine and chill: Stir in the dry ingredients until a soft dough forms, then refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. Shape and bake: Drop heaping tablespoons of dough onto baking sheets lined with parchment. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes. Cool completely.
  5. Glaze and decorate: Mix powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla to create the glaze. Dip each cookie and top with sprinkles.

5. Thumbprint Cookies with Jam

No holiday cookie platter is complete without thumbprint cookies. These delightful treats feature a buttery shortbread base and a dollop of jam nestled in the center.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • ½ cup favorite holiday jam (like raspberry or apricot)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare dry mix: Whisk flour and salt in a bowl.
  2. Cream butter and sugar: Beat softened butter and sugar until smooth. Add vanilla and almond extract, mixing until fluffy.
  3. Form dough: Slowly incorporate dry ingredients until the dough comes together.
  4. Shape and indent: Roll dough into small balls and press your thumb into each center to form indentations.
  5. Fill and bake: Spoon jam into each indentation. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 12-14 minutes until cookies set. Cool before serving.

Pro Tip:

  • Dust with powdered sugar for a snowy holiday look.

Make the Holidays Memorable with Homemade Cookies

Holiday baking is more than just a tradition—it’s a way to create lasting memories with loved ones. Choosing a variety of cookies that reflect the season’s flavors ensures there’s something for everyone to enjoy, from classic sugar cookies to creative peppermint chocolate chips. Dive into these recipes with confidence, and let the holiday spirit shine through every batch!

The joy of holiday cookies lies not just in their taste but in the love and care poured into their creation. So, preheat your ovens, dust off your aprons, and savor the magic of the season with these delicious recipes.
