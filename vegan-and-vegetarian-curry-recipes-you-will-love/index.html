Vegan and Vegetarian Curry Recipes You Will Love


When it comes to dishes that bring warmth, aroma, and bold flavors to the table, curries are an undeniable favorite. Whether you’re embracing plant-based eating or simply looking to expand your culinary repertoire, vegan and vegetarian curry recipes offer an exciting opportunity to savor vibrant and healthful meals. These recipes aren’t just delicious; they showcase the versatility of vegetables, legumes, and spices in ways that will delight your taste buds. From creamy coconut-based curries to hearty lentil stews, this post is your ultimate guide to creating mouthwatering plant-based curries that are sure to impress.

The Magic of Curry: A Fusion of Flavors

Curry is not a singular dish—it’s a celebration of countless cuisines and flavors. Countries like India, Thailand, Malaysia, and Sri Lanka each contribute unique approaches to curry, making it a treasure trove of inspiration for anyone who loves cooking. For vegan and vegetarian enthusiasts, curry is a perfect canvas to experiment with a variety of ingredients, allowing the natural goodness of plant-based food to shine.

The key to phenomenal curry is balance. Bold spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala are perfectly complemented by creamy coconut milk, tangy tomatoes, and fresh herbs. Whether you prefer a milder, comforting blend or something fiery and bold, there’s a curry recipe for everyone.

Let’s dive into some recipes you’ll love!

1. Creamy Coconut and Vegetable Curry

When comfort is what you’re craving, a creamy coconut-and-vegetable curry brings rich flavors with every bite. This recipe is quick to make and highly customizable.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, zucchini, bell peppers)
  • 1 cup chickpeas (or tofu for added protein)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent. Stir in the garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute.
  2. Add the curry powder, turmeric, and cumin, allowing the spices to toast lightly for a fuller flavor.
  3. Stir in the mixed vegetables, coating them evenly in the spice mixture. Allow them to sauté for five minutes.
  4. Mix in the chickpeas (or tofu), then pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Allow the curry to simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Finish with lime juice and sprinkle fresh cilantro on top before serving. Pair with jasmine rice or naan bread for a complete meal!

2. Red Lentil & Sweet Potato Curry

This hearty, protein-packed recipe is ideal for chilly evenings. Red lentils cook quickly, while the natural sweetness of sweet potatoes balances the spices perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium sweet potato, cubed
  • 1 can (400ml) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 cup spinach (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until golden. Stir in the garlic and ginger and sauté until aromatic.
  2. Sprinkle in the coriander, smoked paprika, and garam masala, stirring well to combine with the onions.
  3. Add the red lentils and sweet potato to the pot, followed by the diced tomatoes and vegetable stock. Stir everything well and bring it to a simmer.
  4. Cover and let cook for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure ingredients don’t stick to the bottom. The lentils and sweet potatoes should soften during this time.
  5. Stir in the spinach if using, season with salt and pepper, and cook for an additional 3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with parsley or cilantro and serve warm with quinoa or crusty bread.

3. Thai Green Curry with Tofu

If you’re craving the herbal, creamy goodness of Thai cuisine, this vegan green curry will transport you straight to Southeast Asia. Featuring tofu and fresh veggies, it’s as wholesome as it is delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste (ensure it’s vegan)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby corn, halved
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves (optional)
  • Fresh Thai basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and stir until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, mixing until combined. Simmer gently for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the tofu cubes, green beans, baby corn, and mushrooms to the curry broth. Cook for 10 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in the lime juice and kaffir lime leaves, adjusting seasoning according to taste.
  5. Garnish with Thai basil and serve alongside steamed jasmine rice.

4. Spinach and Tomato Indian Curry (Saag Curry)

This Indian-inspired dish celebrates the earthy freshness of spinach paired with tangy tomatoes in a comforting curry sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon ghee (or vegan butter)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 3 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, washed
  • ½ cup coconut cream or cashew paste for creaminess (optional)
  • Salt and chili powder to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and sauté the onion until golden. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for one minute.
  2. Sprinkle in turmeric, garam masala, and cumin, letting the spices bloom.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until softened into a sauce, around 8–10 minutes.
  4. Add fresh spinach, stirring until wilted. If you prefer creamy saag curry, mix in coconut cream or cashew paste at this stage.
  5. Adjust seasoning with salt and chili powder. Serve with basmati rice or chapati.

5. Spicy Chickpea and Pumpkin Curry

Perfect for lovers of bold flavors, this spicy chickpea and pumpkin curry combines hearty textures with satisfying heat.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoons curry paste (red or yellow)
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 1½ cups fresh pumpkin, cubed
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pot. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until soft. Stir in the curry paste and turmeric, cooking for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Add the chickpeas and pumpkin, ensuring they are well-coated in the spices.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Add chili flakes and finish with lemon juice. Serve warm with couscous or flatbread.

Tips for Perfect Vegan & Vegetarian Curries

  1. Toast Your Spices: Heating spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric in oil before adding other ingredients can boost their flavor.
  2. Layer Flavors: Starting with aromatic bases like onions, garlic, and ginger provides a flavorful foundation that allows the spices to truly shine.
  3. Experiment with Plant-Based Proteins: Tempeh, tofu, chickpeas, and lentils are wonderful protein sources that absorb curry sauces beautifully.
  4. Customize to Your Preferences: Whether you prefer creamy coconut milk, tangy tomatoes, or a spicy profile, curries are endlessly adaptable!

Conclusion

Vegan and vegetarian curries are a delicious testament to the versatility and vibrancy of plant-based cooking. Packed with bold spices, fresh vegetables, and satisfying textures, these recipes are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining friends and family. With so many flavor profiles to choose from, there’s no shortage of inspiration for creating wholesome meals that everyone will love. Try these recipes and discover how easy it is to bring global curry flavors to your kitchen table!
