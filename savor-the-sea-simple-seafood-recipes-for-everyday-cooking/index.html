Savor the Sea: Simple Seafood Recipes for Everyday Cooking


Embark on a culinary adventure by the shore with these dish inspiration for those who love diving into the oceans of flavor. From the heart of the Mediterranean to the steamy heat of the Southeast Asian islands, and everything in between, we’ll take you on a flavor journey you won’t forget.

1. Lemon Herb Baked Cod

Nothing says summer like lemon herb baked cod, a recipe that brings beach vibes right to your dining room. This dish is a true crowd-pleaser and requires hardly any effort to create a masterpiece. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized cod fillets
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ lemon, juiced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C).
  2. Rinse the cod fillets with cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  3. In a bowl, mix olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Place the cod fillets in a baking dish and pour the mixture over the fish, making sure each fillet is well coated.
  5. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

2. Thai Green Curry Prawn Pasta

Embrace the heat of Thailand with Thai green curry prawn pasta that elevates your everyday pasta dish by a hundred notches. This hearty meal is a perfect balance of tanginess, creaminess, and spiciness. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 12 large shrimps, deveined and peeled
  • 8 oz spaghetti
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp green curry paste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a pot of salted boiling water according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat the coconut milk and green curry paste in a large skillet over medium heat. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and let it cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the shrimp turns pink.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, drain and add it to the skillet with the curry sauce. Toss well to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.
  5. Sprinkle with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

3. Grilled Salmon with Mango Salsa

Create a taste of the tropics with grilled salmon and a fiery mango salsa. Perfect for a warm summer evening by the grill, here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

Mango Salsa:

  • 1 large ripe mango, diced
  • ½ red onion, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • ¼ cup lime juice
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Salmon:

  • 4 pieces salmon fillets, skin on, about 6 oz each

Instructions:

Mango Salsa:

  1. Combine diced mango, chopped red onion, fresh cilantro, lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Mix gently and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.

Salmon:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the salmon fillets, skin-side down, on the grill and cook for 4-6 minutes.
  4. Flip the fillets and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  5. Serve the grilled salmon with a side of the sweet and tangy mango salsa.

4. Seared Scallops with Avocado Gazpacho

Buoyed up by the flavors of the sea, this elegant but simple dish is a celebration of seafood at its finest. Savor the delicate texture of perfectly seared scallops alongside the refreshing avocado gazpacho. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

Avocado Gazpacho:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • ½ cup cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • ¼ cup red bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cold water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Scallops:

  • 16 large sea scallops
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Avocado Gazpacho:

  1. Combine chopped avocado, cucumber, red bell pepper, cherry tomatoes, cold water, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

Scallops:

  1. Pat the scallops dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Once the oil is hot, add the scallops to the skillet, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  4. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until the scallops reach your desired level of doneness.
  5. Serve the seared scallops with a side of chilled avocado gazpacho.

These simple seafood recipes should inspire you to elevate your home cooking with the world’s bravest flavors. Reinvent your fish Fridays or create an impromptu beach night with these delectable dishes that bring the joy of the sea right to your table. Enjoy!
