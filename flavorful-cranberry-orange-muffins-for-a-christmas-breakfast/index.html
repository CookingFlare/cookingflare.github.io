Flavorful Cranberry Orange Muffins for a Christmas Breakfast


‘Tis the season for festive flavors, cozy mornings, and delicious treats. And what better way to start your Christmas day than with a batch of homemade cranberry orange muffins? These delightful muffins are bursting with the flavors of tart cranberries and zesty orange, making them the perfect addition to your holiday breakfast spread. Whether you’re enjoying a quiet morning with your family or hosting a festive brunch, these muffins are sure to bring warmth and cheer to your Christmas celebration.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries, coarsely chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease the cups with butter or cooking spray.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Stir the dry ingredients together until well mixed.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, eggs, buttermilk, vanilla extract, and orange zest until smooth.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough muffins.

  5. Add Cranberries: Gently fold in the chopped cranberries, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  6. Fill the Muffin Cups: Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin cups, filling each cup about two-thirds full.

  7. Bake to Perfection: Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Cool and Enjoy: Once baked, remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve the muffins warm or at room temperature and enjoy the delightful combination of cranberry and orange flavors.

Tips for Success

  • To make the muffins even more festive, sprinkle a little coarse sugar on top of the muffins before baking for a sweet, crunchy finish.
  • If you prefer a slightly sweeter muffin, you can increase the amount of sugar to 3/4 cup.
  • For a dairy-free option, you can use a non-dairy milk alternative, such as almond milk, in place of buttermilk.

Conclusion

These cranberry orange muffins are a wonderful way to infuse your Christmas morning with the flavors of the season. The tartness of the cranberries pairs beautifully with the bright citrus notes of the orange, creating a harmonious blend of flavors in every bite. Whether you enjoy them with a steaming cup of coffee, a glass of freshly squeezed orange juice, or alongside other holiday favorites, these muffins are sure to become a cherished part of your Christmas breakfast tradition. Embrace the joy of the season and treat yourself and your loved ones to the simple pleasure of freshly baked cranberry orange muffins this Christmas. Happy baking, and merry Christmas!
