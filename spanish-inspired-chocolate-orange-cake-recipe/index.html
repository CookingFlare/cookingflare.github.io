Spanish Inspired Chocolate Orange Cake Recipe


Welcome to a delightful culinary adventure that combines the rich flavors of chocolate with the vibrant zest of orange, all inspired by the enchanting essence of Spain. This Spanish Inspired Chocolate Orange Cake is not just a dessert; it’s a celebration of flavors, culture, and tradition. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself, this cake is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the ingredients, preparation, and some tips to make your baking experience as delightful as the cake itself.

Ingredients

For the Cake

  • 1 ¾ cups (220g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup (75g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk
  • ½ cup (120ml) vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240ml) boiling water
  • Zest of 2 large oranges

For the Orange Syrup

  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (120ml) fresh orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange

For the Chocolate Ganache

  • 8 oz (225g) bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

For Decoration

  • Candied orange slices (optional)
  • Chocolate shavings (optional)

Step-by-Step Preparation

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Before you begin mixing your ingredients, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that your cake starts baking at the right temperature, allowing it to rise beautifully.

Step 2: Prepare the Cake Batter

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. This not only removes lumps but also aerates the flour, giving your cake a lighter texture.

  2. Mix in Wet Ingredients: Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, vanilla extract, and orange zest to the dry ingredients. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed for about 2 minutes until the batter is smooth.

  3. Incorporate Boiling Water: Carefully stir in the boiling water. The batter will be thin, but this is crucial for a moist cake. Mix until fully combined.

Step 3: Bake the Cake

  1. Prepare the Pans: Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans. Alternatively, you can line the bottoms with parchment paper for easier removal.

  2. Pour the Batter: Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans.

  3. Bake: Place the pans in the preheated oven. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  4. Cool: Once baked, remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer them to wire racks to cool completely.

Step 4: Make the Orange Syrup

While the cake is cooling, you can prepare the orange syrup.

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, fresh orange juice, and orange zest.

  2. Heat: Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Allow it to simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened.

  3. Cool: Remove from heat and let it cool. This syrup will add a deliciously tangy flavor and moisture to your cake.

Step 5: Prepare the Chocolate Ganache

  1. Heat the Cream: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer.

  2. Add Chocolate: Remove from heat and add the chopped bittersweet chocolate. Allow it to sit for a few minutes, then stir until smooth.

  3. Incorporate Butter: Stir in the unsalted butter until fully melted and combined. This will give your ganache a beautiful sheen.

Step 6: Assemble the Cake

  1. Layer the Cakes: Once the cakes have cooled completely, place one layer on a serving plate.

  2. Soak with Syrup: Use a brush or spoon to soak the top of the first layer with the orange syrup. This will infuse it with moisture and flavor.

  3. Add the Second Layer: Place the second cake layer on top and repeat the soaking process.

  4. Frost with Ganache: Pour the chocolate ganache over the top of the cake, allowing it to drip down the sides. Use a spatula to spread it evenly if desired.

Step 7: Decorate and Serve

To elevate your cake’s presentation, consider adding candied orange slices and chocolate shavings on top. These not only look stunning but also enhance the flavor profile.

Serving Suggestions

This cake pairs beautifully with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. For a more traditional Spanish touch, serve it alongside a cup of rich coffee or a glass of dessert wine.

Tips for the Perfect Cake

  • Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure that your eggs and milk are at room temperature for a smoother batter.

  • Measure Ingredients Accurately: Baking is a science, so use precise measurements for the best results.

  • Don’t Overmix: Once you add the wet ingredients to the dry, mix just until combined to prevent a dense cake.

  • Check for Doneness: Ovens can vary, so start checking your cake a few minutes before the recommended baking time.

Conclusion

The Spanish Inspired Chocolate Orange Cake is a fantastic way to bring a taste of Spain to your kitchen. With its rich chocolate flavor complemented by the refreshing brightness of orange, this cake is a feast for the senses. Perfect for any occasion, it’s sure to become a favorite among family and friends.

So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to indulge in a slice of this heavenly creation. Happy baking!
