Spicy and Smoky Chipotle Black Bean Chili


If you’re craving a hearty and flavorful dish that’s perfect for any occasion, look no further than this Spicy and Smoky Chipotle Black Bean Chili recipe. Packed with bold spices, protein-rich black beans, and the smoky heat of chipotle peppers, this chili is guaranteed to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re hosting a game day gathering, a cozy family dinner, or simply want to meal prep for the week, this recipe has got you covered.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 2 (15-ounce) cans black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, lime wedges, shredded cheese, sour cream

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and diced bell peppers, and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, and ground coriander. Cook the spices for 1-2 minutes to release their flavors, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

  3. Add the minced chipotle peppers, black beans, crushed tomatoes, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to combine all the ingredients, then bring the chili to a gentle boil.

  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the chili simmer, uncovered, for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows the flavors to meld together and the chili to thicken to your desired consistency. If the chili becomes too thick, you can add a bit more vegetable broth as needed.

  5. Once the chili has simmered to perfection, season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember that the chipotle peppers add a spicy kick, so adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  6. Serve the Spicy and Smoky Chipotle Black Bean Chili in bowls, and let your family or guests customize their bowls with optional toppings such as sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, shredded cheese, or a dollop of sour cream.

  7. Enjoy this smoky, spicy, and satisfying chili with your favorite sides, whether it’s warm cornbread, a side of rice, or simply a sprinkle of tortilla chips for some added crunch.

Conclusion

This Spicy and Smoky Chipotle Black Bean Chili recipe is a celebration of bold flavors and comforting warmth. With its rich combination of spices, smokiness, and hearty black beans, it’s a dish that’s sure to please everyone around your table. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is a fantastic addition to your cooking repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to delight your taste buds with this unforgettable chili.
