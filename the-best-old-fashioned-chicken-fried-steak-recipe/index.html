The Best Old-Fashioned Chicken Fried Steak Recipe


If you’re craving a hearty and comforting meal that’s sure to satisfy your taste buds, look no further than the classic old-fashioned chicken fried steak. This beloved Southern dish features tenderized steak coated in a crispy, golden breading and smothered in a rich, flavorful gravy. Whether you’re serving it up for a weekend family dinner or looking to impress your guests, this recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 4 beef cube steaks
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

Step 1: Tenderize the Steak

Begin by tenderizing the cube steaks to ensure a tender and juicy result. Use a meat mallet to pound the steaks to about 1/4-inch thickness. Season each steak with salt and black pepper on both sides.

Step 2: Prepare the Breading

In a shallow dish, combine the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, and additional salt and pepper. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to create a well-seasoned breading for the steaks.

Step 3: Dredge the Steaks

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk to create an egg wash. Dip each seasoned steak into the egg wash, allowing any excess to drip off, and then coat it evenly with the seasoned flour mixture. Press the breading onto the steaks to ensure a good coating.

Step 4: Fry the Steaks

In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the breaded steaks into the skillet. Fry the steaks for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Transfer the fried steaks to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 5: Make the Gravy

After frying the steaks, reduce the heat to medium and carefully pour off all but 1/4 cup of the oil from the skillet. Sprinkle 1/4 cup of the seasoned flour mixture over the hot oil, stirring constantly to create a roux. Cook the roux for 2-3 minutes, or until it turns a light golden brown.

Step 6: Finish the Dish

Slowly pour in 3 cups of whole milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Continue to cook the gravy, stirring frequently, until it thickens to your desired consistency. Season the gravy with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Place the fried steaks on a serving platter and generously ladle the rich, creamy gravy over the top. Serve the chicken fried steaks alongside mashed potatoes, green beans, or your favorite sides for a complete and satisfying meal.

Final Thoughts

The old-fashioned chicken fried steak is a timeless classic that never fails to impress. With its crispy exterior, tender beef, and luscious gravy, this dish is a true embodiment of comfort food. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the unbeatable flavors of this beloved Southern favorite.
