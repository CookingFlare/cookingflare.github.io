Classic Thanksgiving Green Bean Casserole: A Comforting Side Dish


As Thanksgiving approaches, it’s time to start planning the perfect menu for your holiday feast. While the turkey takes center stage, no Thanksgiving meal is complete without its supporting acts. One such dish that has stood the test of time and continues to be a favorite among many is the classic green bean casserole. This timeless side dish brings a comforting and familiar flavor to the table, making it a must-have for any Thanksgiving spread.

The Origins of the Green Bean Casserole

The green bean casserole has a rich history that dates back to the 1950s when it was first introduced by the Campbell Soup Company. Created by home economist Dorcas Reilly, the original recipe featured just a few simple ingredients – green beans, cream of mushroom soup, milk, soy sauce, and crispy fried onions. Its ease of preparation and delicious flavor quickly made it a staple in American households, especially during the holiday season.

A Modern Twist on a Classic Favorite

While the traditional green bean casserole has undoubtedly earned its place on the Thanksgiving table, there’s always room for a modern twist. By adding a few unique ingredients and techniques, you can elevate this classic dish to new heights, impressing your guests with a fresh take on a beloved favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (14 oz each) of French-style green beans, drained
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1/8 tsp black pepper
  • 1 1/3 cups crispy fried onions
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced bacon
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup diced shallots
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 2-quart casserole dish.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the diced bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set it aside, leaving the bacon fat in the skillet.
  3. In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced shallots, minced garlic, and sliced mushrooms, and sauté until the mushrooms are golden brown.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
  5. Slowly pour in the milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Allow the mixture to thicken slightly.
  6. Stir in the condensed cream of mushroom soup, shredded cheddar cheese, and black pepper. Cook until the cheese has melted and the mixture is smooth.
  7. Add the drained green beans and crispy fried onions to the skillet, and gently fold them into the creamy mushroom sauce until well combined.
  8. Transfer the green bean mixture to the greased casserole dish, spreading it out evenly.
  9. Sprinkle the reserved crispy fried onions and crispy bacon on top of the green bean mixture.
  10. Bake the casserole in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is bubbling around the edges.
  11. Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

The green bean casserole pairs perfectly with traditional Thanksgiving dishes such as roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, and cranberry sauce. Its creamy texture and savory flavor make it a versatile side that complements the richness of the holiday meal.

Conclusion

The classic Thanksgiving green bean casserole holds a special place in the hearts of many, evoking feelings of warmth and nostalgia with every bite. By infusing a touch of creativity into the traditional recipe, you can breathe new life into this timeless dish and create a memorable dining experience for your loved ones. This Thanksgiving, let the green bean casserole take its well-deserved place at your holiday table, adding a comforting and familiar element to your celebration.
