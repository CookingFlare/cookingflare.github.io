Taste the South: 5 Southern-inspired Rubs for Grilled Ribs


If you’re looking for a flavor-packed twist on your grilled ribs, then look no further. In this blog post, we’re going to take you on a journey through the South with five delicious and easy-to-make Southern-inspired rubs that will elevate your grilling game to a whole new level. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds with the rich and smoky flavors of the South!

1. Carolina Mustard Rub

Let’s start our flavor adventure in the beautiful state of South Carolina, famous for its tangy mustard-based barbecue sauce. This rub combines the boldness of mustard with a touch of sweetness and a hint of heat. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1/4 cup yellow mustard
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Mix all the ingredients together in a bowl until well combined. Generously coat your ribs with the Carolina Mustard Rub, ensuring every inch is covered. Let the ribs marinate for at least 2 hours before grilling. Prepare your grill for indirect cooking and grill the ribs over medium heat until tender and caramelized.

2. Memphis Dry Rub

Next stop, Memphis, Tennessee, where dry rub reigns supreme. This classic rub is all about the perfect balance of flavors - sweet, smoky, and a little bit spicy. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Massage the Memphis Dry Rub onto your ribs, making sure to cover every inch. Let the flavors meld for at least 1 hour before grilling. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat and cook the ribs until they are tender and the dry rub has formed a beautiful crust.

3. Louisiana Cajun Rub

Now we’re heading down to the vibrant and spicy Cajun country of Louisiana. This rub is packed with bold and fiery flavors that will transport you straight to the heart of the South. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

In a bowl, combine all the ingredients and mix well. Generously coat your ribs with the Louisiana Cajun Rub, making sure to get into every nook and cranny. Let the flavors infuse for at least 30 minutes before grilling. Grill the ribs over medium heat until they are tender and the Cajun flavors have created a mouthwatering crust.

4. Georgia Peach Rub

Our next stop takes us to the peach state itself, Georgia. This rub combines the sweetness of peaches with a touch of warmth and a hint of smokiness. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup peach preserves

In a bowl, combine all the dry ingredients and mix well. Add the peach preserves and mix until everything is well incorporated. Slather the Georgia Peach Rub onto your ribs, ensuring they are evenly coated. Let the ribs marinate for at least 1 hour before grilling. Grill the ribs over medium heat until they are tender and the peach flavors have caramelized beautifully.

5. Texas Brisket Rub

Last but certainly not least, we arrive in the Lone Star State, Texas, where big flavors and big personalities dominate. This rub is perfect for those who love a bold and robust taste. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Rub the Texas Brisket Rub onto your ribs, making sure to get a generous amount on every inch. Let the flavors meld for at least 2 hours before grilling. Preheat your grill to medium heat and cook the ribs until they are fall-off-the-bone tender and the flavors have developed into a Texas-sized feast.

Conclusion

There you have it, five incredible Southern-inspired rubs that will take your grilled ribs to a whole new level. Whether you prefer the tangy kick of Carolina Mustard, the balanced flavors of Memphis Dry, the fiery heat of Louisiana Cajun, the sweet peachiness of Georgia Peach, or the boldness of Texas Brisket, there’s a rub for everyone to enjoy. So fire up your grill, gather your loved ones, and get ready to taste the South in every juicy bite!
