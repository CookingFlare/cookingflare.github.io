A Step-by-Step Guide to the Classic Singapore Sling Recipe


The Singapore Sling is one of the most iconic cocktails in the world, known for its vibrant color and refreshing taste. Originating from Singapore in the early 20th century, this cocktail has since become a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts worldwide. If you’re looking to recreate this classic drink at home or simply want to know more about its fascinating history and perfect preparation, you’ve come to the right place.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the history of the Singapore Sling, the essential ingredients, and a step-by-step method for crafting this delicious cocktail. Whether you’re an experienced bartender or a home enthusiast, this guide will help you master the art of the Singapore Sling.

The History of the Singapore Sling

Before we dive into the recipe, it’s essential to understand the origins of the Singapore Sling. The drink was first created by Ngiam Tong Boon, a bartender at the famous Raffles Hotel in Singapore, around 1915. Ngiam is believed to have crafted the drink for women patrons at the hotel’s Long Bar, as it was taboo for women to drink hard liquor in public at the time. The cocktail was designed to be a more elegant, fruit-forward option, making it an instant hit.

The original recipe for the Singapore Sling has been a subject of debate over the years, with many variations of the drink emerging. However, the version that we know and love today is a delicious blend of gin, cherry liqueur, pineapple juice, lime juice, and a hint of bitters.

The Essential Ingredients for a Singapore Sling

To make the perfect Singapore Sling, it’s crucial to use the right ingredients. While some variations of the recipe exist, sticking to the classic ingredients will give you the most authentic taste.

Here’s a breakdown of the essential ingredients:

  1. Gin - The base spirit of the Singapore Sling, gin provides the cocktail with a crisp and botanical flavor.
  2. Cherry Liqueur (or Cherry Heering) - This sweet, red liqueur is what gives the drink its distinctive pinkish color and fruity flavor.
  3. Pineapple Juice - The tropical sweetness of pineapple juice complements the other ingredients and provides a refreshing base for the drink.
  4. Lime Juice - Freshly squeezed lime juice adds a zesty, tart contrast to the sweetness of the other ingredients.
  5. Cointreau - A type of orange liqueur that brings a hint of citrusy sweetness and complexity.
  6. Angostura Bitters - Just a dash of bitters adds depth to the cocktail and balances the flavors.
  7. Soda Water - To give the cocktail a refreshing, effervescent finish.
  8. Garnish - A cherry and a slice of pineapple are typically used to garnish the Singapore Sling, adding both color and a tropical flair.

The Classic Singapore Sling Recipe

Now that we’ve covered the history and ingredients, it’s time to learn how to make the classic Singapore Sling. Follow the step-by-step instructions below to create this delicious and refreshing cocktail.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ oz (45 ml) Gin
  • ¾ oz (22.5 ml) Cherry Liqueur (Cherry Heering is preferred)
  • ½ oz (15 ml) Cointreau
  • ¼ oz (7.5 ml) Benedictine (optional, for added herbal notes)
  • ¾ oz (22.5 ml) Fresh Lime Juice
  • 4 oz (120 ml) Pineapple Juice
  • 1 dash of Angostura Bitters
  • Soda Water (to top off)
  • Ice
  • Garnish: Pineapple slice and cherry

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Glass

Start by preparing a highball glass or a tall Collins glass. Fill it with ice cubes to chill the glass while you prepare the cocktail. This will ensure your drink stays cold once it’s mixed.

2. Add the Gin and Cherry Liqueur

In a cocktail shaker, combine 1 ½ ounces of gin with ¾ ounce of cherry liqueur. The gin serves as the base of the cocktail, while the cherry liqueur provides the signature sweet and fruity flavor.

3. Add the Citrus and Liqueurs

Next, add ½ ounce of Cointreau to the shaker. Cointreau brings a burst of orange flavor that enhances the drink’s complexity. If you prefer a more herbal flavor, add a small amount (¼ ounce) of Benedictine, though this step is optional. Benedictine is a herbal liqueur that adds a unique touch to the drink.

4. Add the Lime Juice

Squeeze fresh lime juice into the shaker, approximately ¾ ounce. The tartness of the lime juice will help balance the sweetness of the cherry liqueur and pineapple juice, creating a harmonious flavor profile.

5. Add Pineapple Juice

Pour in 4 ounces of fresh pineapple juice. Pineapple juice is the key to the drink’s tropical, refreshing flavor. Make sure to use fresh juice for the best results.

6. Add Bitters

Add one dash of Angostura bitters to the mix. The bitters serve to balance the sweetness of the drink and add depth to the flavor profile.

7. Shake It Up

Secure the lid on your cocktail shaker and shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds. The goal is to chill the mixture and combine all the flavors. Shaking the cocktail also creates a slight froth, which enhances the drink’s texture.

8. Strain into the Glass

Remove the ice from your prepared glass and strain the shaken cocktail into the glass. If you prefer a smooth, chilled drink, you can use a fine mesh strainer to catch any ice shards and pulp from the lime juice.

9. Top with Soda Water

To finish the cocktail, top it off with a splash of soda water. This will give the Singapore Sling a light and effervescent texture, making it even more refreshing.

10. Garnish and Serve

Finally, garnish the Singapore Sling with a slice of pineapple and a maraschino cherry. The garnish not only adds visual appeal but also enhances the tropical experience of the drink.

Serve and Enjoy!

Your Singapore Sling is now ready to be served! This cocktail is perfect for a summer day, a festive gathering, or any occasion that calls for a refreshing and flavorful drink. The balance of sweet, tart, and herbal flavors will transport you to the tropical climes of Singapore with every sip.

Tips for the Perfect Singapore Sling

While the above recipe will yield an excellent cocktail, here are a few tips to elevate your Singapore Sling even further:

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh lime juice and freshly squeezed pineapple juice make a huge difference in the flavor of your drink. Opt for natural juices instead of store-bought varieties that might contain preservatives.
  2. Experiment with Proportions: While the classic recipe is a great starting point, don’t hesitate to experiment with the ratios to suit your personal taste. Some people prefer a stronger gin flavor, while others might want more pineapple juice for a fruitier profile.
  3. Chill the Glass Properly: A properly chilled glass will help keep your drink cold and refreshing. You can chill the glass in the freezer or by adding ice before preparing the cocktail.
  4. Make It a Mocktail: If you’re not in the mood for alcohol, you can create a non-alcoholic version of the Singapore Sling by omitting the gin and cherry liqueur. Use extra pineapple juice and a dash of grenadine for sweetness, and add soda water to finish.

Variations of the Singapore Sling

While the classic recipe is timeless, there are several variations of the Singapore Sling that you can try to put your unique spin on the cocktail. Here are a few popular variations:

1. The Modern Singapore Sling

Some bartenders prefer a more modern take on the Singapore Sling by adding more fruit juices, such as orange juice or passion fruit juice, for a richer tropical flavor. The addition of other liqueurs, like Aperol or even grenadine, can also change the cocktail’s taste profile.

2. The Royal Singapore Sling

For a more luxurious version, some bartenders use champagne or sparkling wine instead of soda water. This bubbly twist elevates the drink for special occasions and adds an extra layer of sophistication.

3. The Low-Calorie Singapore Sling

If you’re watching your calorie intake, you can lighten up your Singapore Sling by substituting the pineapple juice with a low-calorie option or using sugar-free soda water instead of regular soda water. You can also skip the cherry liqueur and use a small amount of cherry syrup for flavor.

Conclusion

The Singapore Sling is a timeless classic that never goes out of style. Whether you’re sipping it at a chic rooftop bar in Singapore or enjoying it in the comfort of your home, this cocktail offers a refreshing blend of flavors that are perfect for any occasion. By following the steps outlined in this guide and using high-quality ingredients, you can craft the perfect Singapore Sling and transport yourself to a tropical paradise with every sip.

Next time you’re hosting a gathering or just looking to indulge in a delicious cocktail, reach for the ingredients to make this classic drink, and let the flavors of the Singapore Sling take you on a journey of taste and history. Cheers!
