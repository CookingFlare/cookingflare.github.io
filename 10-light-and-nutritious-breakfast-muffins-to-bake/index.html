10 Light and Nutritious Breakfast Muffins to Bake


Muffins are the quintessential breakfast treat that can satisfy your cravings while also providing a nutritious start to your day. Not only are they easy to make, but they can also be packed with wholesome ingredients. In this post, we’ll explore ten delightful muffin recipes that are light yet nourishing, perfect for busy mornings or leisurely brunches. Whether you’re a fan of fruity flavors, nutty textures, or rich chocolate, there’s a muffin for everyone. So, let’s dive in!

1. Blueberry Oat Muffins

These blueberry oat muffins are bursting with juicy blueberries and made with hearty oats, providing a wonderful chewy texture and a boost of fiber.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup almond milk (or any milk of choice)
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, mix oats, flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together almond milk, honey, applesauce, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually combine the wet and dry ingredients, then fold in the blueberries.
  5. Pour the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Allow to cool before enjoying.

2. Spinach and Feta Muffins

Packed with greens and protein-rich feta, these savory muffins are perfect for a nutritious breakfast option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup oat flour (or ground oats)
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flours, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and oregano.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together Greek yogurt, milk, and eggs.
  4. Stir the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, then fold in spinach and feta.
  5. Spoon the batter into the muffin tin and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  6. Let cool before serving.

3. Banana Nut Muffins

Ripe bananas add natural sweetness and moisture to these nutty muffins, making them a delightful morning treat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup honey or brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, mix mashed bananas, coconut oil, honey, eggs, and vanilla.
  3. In another bowl, combine baking soda, salt, and flour.
  4. Gradually add dry ingredients to the wet mixture, then fold in nuts.
  5. Fill the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Allow to cool before indulging.

4. Carrot and Zucchini Muffins

These muffins are a great way to sneak in some veggies while enjoying a sweet, spiced flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup grated zucchini (about 1 medium zucchini)
  • 1 cup grated carrots (about 2 medium carrots)
  • 1/2 cup applesauce
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a muffin tin with liners.
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine grated zucchini, carrots, applesauce, honey, oil, and eggs.
  4. Mix the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the muffin tin and bake for 20-25 minutes until golden.
  6. Cool before savoring.

5. Chocolate Chip Protein Muffins

Indulge in these chocolate chip muffins that also provide a protein boost, making them perfect for a post-workout snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1/2 cup protein powder (vanilla or chocolate)
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together oat flour, protein powder, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix almond milk, honey, and applesauce.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, then fold in chocolate chips.
  5. Fill the muffin cups and bake for 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Cool before enjoying.

6. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

These apple cinnamon muffins are a delightful way to enjoy the flavors of fall any time of the year.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/3 cup honey or brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup diced apples (about 1 medium apple)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together applesauce, honey, almond milk, and egg.
  4. Stir the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and fold in diced apples.
  5. Pour the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Allow to cool before serving.

7. Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins

Bright and zesty, these lemon poppy seed muffins are a refreshing choice to kickstart your day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup poppy seeds
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, poppy seeds, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix Greek yogurt, honey, oil, eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  5. Fill the muffin cups and bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Let cool before enjoying.

8. Peanut Butter Banana Muffins

These muffins combine the rich flavor of peanut butter with the sweetness of bananas, creating a delicious and filling breakfast option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (smooth or crunchy)
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a muffin tin with liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix peanut butter, mashed bananas, honey, almond milk, and egg until smooth.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
