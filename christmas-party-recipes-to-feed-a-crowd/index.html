Christmas Party Recipes to Feed a Crowd


Throwing a Christmas party and feeding a crowd can be both exciting and daunting. Balancing variety with ease, flavor with festivity, and quantity with quality doesn’t have to mean spending all day in the kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a casual get-together or a more formal holiday feast, having a lineup of crowd-pleasing, easy-to-prepare dishes is essential.

This guide offers a collection of carefully curated Christmas party recipes designed to delight every guest. From hearty mains to irresistible appetizers, and from festive sides to indulgent desserts, these recipes will help you create an unforgettable holiday spread.

Appetizers to Kick Off the Celebration

Starting the party with a selection of flavorful appetizers sets the tone for the evening. Aim for finger foods that are easy to serve and perfect for mingling.

1. Bacon-Wrapped Dates Stuffed with Goat Cheese

A classic crowd-pleaser with a holiday twist, this recipe combines the sweetness of dates, the creaminess of goat cheese, and the savory crunch of bacon to create a perfect bite-sized treat.

Ingredients:

  • 24 Medjool dates, pitted
  • 4 ounces goat cheese
  • 12 strips of bacon, cut in half
  • Toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice dates lengthwise and stuff each with about a teaspoon of goat cheese.
  3. Wrap each date with a half slice of bacon and secure with a toothpick.
  4. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes until bacon is crisp. Let cool slightly before serving.

2. Mini Spinach and Artichoke Dip Cups

Put a spin on the traditional dip by serving individual portions in phyllo cups—easy to eat and elegantly festive.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chopped spinach (fresh or frozen, thawed and squeezed dry)
  • 1/2 cup artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 package mini phyllo cups

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine spinach, artichoke hearts, cream cheese, sour cream, Parmesan, and garlic powder.
  3. Stir until smooth and well combined.
  4. Fill each mini phyllo cup with the mixture.
  5. Bake for 15 minutes or until tops are lightly golden. Serve warm.

3. Cranberry-Brie Bites with Pecans

This appetizer marries the creaminess of brie with the tartness of cranberry sauce and the crunch of pecans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 8 ounces brie cheese, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 cup cranberry sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut puff pastry into 24 small squares and press into mini muffin tins.
  3. Place a cube of brie in each pastry cup.
  4. Top with a teaspoon of cranberry sauce and a sprinkle of pecans.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until pastry is puffed and golden. Serve immediately.

Main Dishes to Satisfy a Hungry Crowd

Main courses for a Christmas party need to be both delicious and manageable in large quantities. Consider dishes that can be prepared in advance or cook slowly during the party.

1. Herb-Roasted Turkey Breast with Garlic Butter

A well-seasoned turkey breast delivers all the festive appeal without requiring an entire bird. It’s perfect for smaller gatherings or as a secondary meat option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 turkey breast (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix butter with garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the garlic-herb butter under and over the skin of the turkey breast.
  4. Place turkey breast on a roasting pan and roast for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing.

2. Slow Cooker Honey-Glazed Ham

A sticky, sweet ham takes center stage on any holiday table. Using a slow cooker helps lock in moisture and flavor while freeing you up for other preparations.

Ingredients:

  • 6 to 8-pound fully cooked ham
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Cloves for studding (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place ham in slow cooker. Score the surface and stud with cloves, if using.
  2. In a bowl, whisk honey, brown sugar, mustard, and vinegar.
  3. Pour half the glaze over the ham and cook on low for 4-6 hours, basting occasionally with remaining glaze.
  4. For a caramelized finish, broil the ham for 3-5 minutes after slow cooking.

3. Vegetarian Nut Roast

Offering a hearty vegetarian main shows consideration for all guests and adds variety.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed nuts, chopped
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup finely diced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup grated carrot
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté onion, garlic, and mushrooms until soft.
  3. Mix all ingredients together in a bowl until well combined.
  4. Press mixture into a greased loaf pan.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes until firm and golden. Slice and serve with vegetarian gravy.

Side Dishes that Complement the Spread

Sides can elevate a meal and bring festive colors and flavors to the table.

1. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Cranberries and Pecans

This side offers a balance of bitter, sweet, and crunchy.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped toasted pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking tray and roast for 20-25 minutes until caramelized.
  4. Toss with cranberries and pecans before serving.

2. Creamy Mashed Potatoes with Chive Butter

Mashed potatoes are a holiday staple, and the chive butter adds a subtle freshness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water until fork tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Drain and return potatoes to pot over low heat to dry out.
  3. Warm cream and butter together until butter melts.
  4. Mash potatoes, gradually adding cream mixture until smooth and creamy.
  5. Stir in chives, salt, and pepper.

3. Honey-Roasted Carrots with Thyme

Simple, colorful, and sweet, these carrots add brightness to the plate.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss carrots with olive oil, honey, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and caramelized.

Decadent Desserts to End the Festivities

No Christmas party is complete without sweets that charm guests into lingering and relaxing.

1. Classic Buche de Noel (Yule Log Cake)

While this traditional dessert requires some effort, its appearance and flavor make it worth it.

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs separated
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Beat egg yolks with half the sugar until pale.
  3. Beat egg whites with cream of tartar until soft peaks, then gradually add remaining sugar to stiff peaks.
  4. Sift flour and cocoa over yolk mixture; fold carefully.
  5. Gently fold in egg whites.
  6. Spread batter on a parchment-lined jelly roll pan and bake 10-12 minutes.
  7. Turn cake onto a towel dusted with powdered sugar, remove parchment, and roll from short end while warm.
  8. Whip cream with powdered sugar and vanilla. Unroll cake, spread cream, re-roll. Refrigerate.
  9. Garnish with chocolate shavings before serving.

2. Gingerbread Trifle with Whipped Cream and Berries

Quick to assemble and packed with holiday flavors, this trifle serves well in large bowls or individual cups.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound gingerbread cake, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups mixed berries (fresh or thawed frozen)
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Whip cream with powdered sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon until soft peaks form.
  2. In a large trifle dish, layer gingerbread cubes, berries, and whipped cream, repeating layers.
  3. Top with a final layer of whipped cream and scatter extra berries. Refrigerate until serving.

3. Peppermint Bark

Easy to make ahead and always a hit, peppermint bark combines chocolate and festive candy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 8 ounces white chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup crushed candy canes

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt semi-sweet chocolate and spread evenly on baking sheet. Refrigerate until set.
  3. Melt white chocolate with peppermint extract; spread over the set dark chocolate.
  4. Immediately sprinkle crushed candy canes on top.
  5. Refrigerate until firm, then break into pieces.

Tips for Serving a Crowd Effortlessly

  • Prepare Ahead: Many of these recipes can be made or prepped the day before, freeing up your time to enjoy the party.
  • Serve Buffet Style: Lay out all dishes on a decorated table and let guests help themselves.
  • Label Dishes: Especially useful for dietary needs or just to add a festive touch with creative name cards.
  • Keep Food Warm: Use warming trays or chafing dishes to ensure everything stays hot and fresh.
  • Plan Quantity: Estimate about 1 to 1.5 pounds of food per adult guest across all courses to avoid shortages.

Gather your friends and family around with these Christmas party recipes designed to delight large groups with minimal stress. With a mix of classic flavors and clever twists, you’ll provide a festive feast that sparks joy and holiday cheer all evening long. Cheers to good food and great company!
