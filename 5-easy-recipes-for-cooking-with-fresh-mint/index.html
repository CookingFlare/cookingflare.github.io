5 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Fresh Mint


Mint is a versatile herb that brings a refreshing and aromatic flavor to a variety of dishes. Whether you’re making sweet desserts, savory entrees, or even refreshing drinks, mint can elevate any recipe with its distinct, invigorating taste. It’s also packed with antioxidants and other health benefits, making it a great addition to your meals.

If you have fresh mint growing in your garden or bought some from the store, you’re in the right place! Here are five easy and delicious recipes that make the most of fresh mint in your cooking.

1. Mint-Infused Lamb Chops

Lamb and mint are a classic pairing, and for good reason. The freshness of mint balances the rich and savory flavors of lamb, creating a mouthwatering dish that’s perfect for a special dinner or holiday gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lamb chops
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, combine the chopped mint, olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Stir everything together and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  2. Marinate the Lamb: Rub the mint marinade onto the lamb chops, ensuring each chop is coated evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, but if possible, marinate for up to 2 hours to allow the flavors to fully develop.

  3. Cook the Lamb: Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the lamb chops for 4-5 minutes on each side or until they reach your desired level of doneness. For medium-rare, aim for an internal temperature of 135°F (57°C).

  4. Rest and Serve: Let the lamb chops rest for a few minutes before serving to retain their juiciness. Garnish with extra fresh mint leaves if desired.

2. Mint and Cucumber Salad

This mint and cucumber salad is the perfect side dish for a hot summer day. The coolness of the cucumber, paired with the vibrant mint, creates a refreshing contrast that pairs beautifully with grilled meats, seafood, or as a standalone snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Thinly slice the cucumbers and red onion. If you prefer, you can remove the cucumber seeds before slicing for a smoother texture.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, and honey (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Toss the Salad: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cucumbers, red onion, and fresh mint. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat everything evenly.

  4. Chill and Serve: Let the salad sit in the fridge for 15-20 minutes before serving. The chill time allows the flavors to meld together, making it even more delicious.

3. Mint Chocolate Chip Smoothie

Mint chocolate chip ice cream is a beloved treat, and this smoothie captures that flavor in a healthier, more refreshing way. It’s packed with fresh mint and a hint of cocoa, making it the perfect morning pick-me-up or post-workout drink.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any milk of your choice)
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender, combine the fresh mint, banana, almond milk, cocoa powder, and honey or maple syrup. Add a handful of ice cubes and blend until smooth and creamy.

  2. Adjust the Consistency: If the smoothie is too thick, add more almond milk to reach your desired consistency.

  3. Serve and Enjoy: Pour the smoothie into a glass and garnish with a sprig of mint or some chocolate chips if you’re feeling indulgent. Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing mint-chocolate flavor.

4. Mint-Infused Watermelon Salad

Watermelon and mint are another fantastic combination. This salad is perfect for a light and refreshing snack or as an accompaniment to a summer barbecue. The sweetness of the watermelon is enhanced by the aromatic mint, creating a delightful contrast in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cubed watermelon
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, torn into pieces
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Watermelon: Cut the watermelon into cubes or bite-sized pieces. If you prefer, you can use a melon baller for a more decorative presentation.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, honey, and a pinch of sea salt.

  3. Toss the Salad: In a large bowl, gently toss the cubed watermelon with the torn mint leaves. Drizzle the lime and honey dressing over the salad and toss again to coat everything evenly.

  4. Chill and Serve: Let the salad chill in the fridge for about 15 minutes before serving. This salad is best enjoyed cold, especially on a hot summer day.

5. Minty Pea and Feta Pasta

For a quick and flavorful dinner, this minty pea and feta pasta is a delicious choice. The sweetness of the peas, combined with the saltiness of feta cheese, and the freshness of mint, creates a perfect harmony of flavors that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta of your choice (penne, fusilli, or spaghetti work well)
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to the package instructions. About 2 minutes before the pasta is finished, add the frozen peas to the pot and cook them together. Drain the pasta and peas, reserving a bit of the cooking water.

  2. Prepare the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt and pepper.

  3. Combine the Pasta and Peas: Return the pasta and peas to the pot. Add the crumbled feta cheese, fresh mint, and the olive oil dressing. Toss everything together until the pasta is evenly coated. If the mixture is too dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it up.

  4. Serve: Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or lemon juice if needed. Serve the pasta warm or at room temperature for a light, refreshing meal.

Conclusion

Fresh mint is an incredible herb to incorporate into your cooking, and these five recipes highlight its versatility. From savory lamb chops and refreshing salads to sweet smoothies and fruity desserts, mint can transform a simple dish into something extraordinary. The best part? These recipes are easy to prepare, and many of them use ingredients you likely already have in your kitchen.

Next time you have fresh mint on hand, don’t let it go to waste! Try one (or all) of these delicious dishes and enjoy the refreshing, aromatic taste that mint adds to every bite.

Whether you’re cooking for yourself or hosting a gathering, these mint-infused recipes are sure to impress. Try them out and experience the vibrant, fresh flavors that mint brings to the table!
