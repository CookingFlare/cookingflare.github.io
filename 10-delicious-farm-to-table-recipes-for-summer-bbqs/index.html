10 Delicious Farm to Table Recipes for Summer BBQs


Summer is finally here, and what better way to celebrate than with a backyard BBQ? The warm weather and long days make it the perfect season to gather with friends and family, and a delicious spread of farm-to-table dishes is the perfect way to make the occasion unforgettable. In this post, we’ll explore 10 mouth-watering recipes that showcase the best of summer’s bounty, from fresh vegetables to juicy meats and sweet treats.

Recipe 1: Heirloom Tomato Salad with Burrata Cheese

Summer’s sweetest tomatoes are the star of this show-stopping salad. Simply slice up a few heirloom tomatoes, add some creamy burrata cheese, a sprinkle of basil, and a drizzle of olive oil, and you’ve got a dish that’s sure to impress.

  • 3 heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 ball of burrata cheese
  • 1/4 cup of fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 2: Grilled Corn with Herbs and Lemon

Grilled corn is a summer staple, and adding some fresh herbs and a squeeze of lemon takes it to a whole new level. Simply brush the corn with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and grill until slightly charred. Then, sprinkle with chopped fresh herbs like parsley, chives, or dill, and squeeze with a sliver of lemon.

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp of pepper
  • 1/4 cup of fresh herbs, chopped
  • 1 lemon, sliced

Recipe 3: Zucchini Noodle Stir-Fry with Garlic Shrimp

Zucchini noodles, or “zoodles,” are a low-carb alternative to traditional pasta, and when paired with succulent garlic shrimp, they make for a quick and easy summer dinner. Simply spiralize the zucchini, saut√© with some garlic and olive oil, and then add in cooked shrimp and a splash of soy sauce.

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of cooked shrimp
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 4: Watermelon and Feta Salad with Mint

Watermelon and feta is a match made in heaven, and adding some fresh mint takes it to a whole new level. Simply cube up a watermelon, crumble some feta cheese, and sprinkle with chopped fresh mint. Drizzle with a little olive oil and balsamic vinegar for a refreshing summer salad.

  • 2 cups of watermelon, cubed
  • 1/2 cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 5: Grilled Chicken Skewers with Peach BBQ Sauce

Grilled chicken skewers are a summer classic, and when paired with a sweet and tangy peach BBQ sauce, they’re sure to be a hit. Simply thread chicken breast and vegetables like bell peppers and onions onto skewers, brush with olive oil, and grill until cooked through. Serve with a side of peach BBQ sauce made by blending together canned peaches, ketchup, brown sugar, and vinegar.

  • 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1/2 cup of mixed vegetables (bell peppers, onions, etc.)
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 cup of peach BBQ sauce (see below for recipe)

Peach BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 can of peaches in syrup
  • 1/2 cup of ketchup
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp of smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 6: Roasted Sweet Potato Salad with Harissa and Pine Nuts

Roasted sweet potatoes are a game-changer, and when paired with the spicy kick of harissa and the crunch of pine nuts, they make for a delicious and unique summer salad. Simply roast diced sweet potatoes in the oven with some olive oil and salt, then toss with chopped fresh parsley, crumbled feta cheese, and a drizzle of harissa.

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tbsp of harissa
  • 1/4 cup of pine nuts, toasted

Recipe 7: Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Aioli

Asparagus is a summer staple, and when grilled to perfection and served with a tangy lemon aioli, it’s a dish that’s sure to impress. Simply brush asparagus spears with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and grill until slightly charred. Serve with a side of lemon aioli made by blending together mayonnaise, lemon juice, garlic, and parsley.

  • 1 pound of asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp of pepper
  • 1/2 cup of lemon aioli (see below for recipe)

Lemon Aioli:

  • 1/2 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp of lemon juice
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 8: Blackberry BBQ Sauce Glazed Pork Chops

Pork chops are a summer classic, and when glazed with a sweet and tangy blackberry BBQ sauce, they’re sure to be a hit. Simply brush pork chops with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and grill until cooked through. Serve with a side of blackberry BBQ sauce made by blending together canned blackberries, ketchup, brown sugar, and vinegar.

  • 1 pound of pork chops
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 tsp of pepper
  • 1 cup of blackberry BBQ sauce (see below for recipe)

Blackberry BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 can of blackberries in syrup
  • 1/2 cup of ketchup
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp of smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 9: Zesty Corn and Black Bean Salad

This salad is a colorful and flavorful way to enjoy the flavors of summer. Simply saut√© diced corn and black beans with some olive oil and garlic, then toss with chopped fresh cilantro, crumbled feta cheese, and a squeeze of lime juice.

  • 1 cup of frozen corn kernels, thawed
  • 1 cup of cooked black beans
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tbsp of lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 10: Peach Cobbler with Vanilla Ice Cream

Summer is the perfect time to indulge in a warm and comforting peach cobbler, topped with a scoop of creamy vanilla ice cream. Simply peel and slice up some ripe peaches, mix with sugar and flour, and top with a crumbly biscuit dough. Bake until golden brown, then serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

  • 3 cups of ripe peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of biscuit dough (see below for recipe)

Biscuit Dough:

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp of baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 3/4 cup of heavy cream

These 10 delicious farm-to-table recipes are sure to make your summer BBQ a hit. From fresh vegetables to juicy meats and sweet treats, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in the flavors of summer.
