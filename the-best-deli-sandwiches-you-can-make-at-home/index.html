The Best Deli Sandwiches You Can Make at Home


When it comes to a satisfying meal, few things hit the spot like a well-crafted deli sandwich. Whether you’re craving something classic or looking to experiment with new flavors, deli sandwiches offer endless possibilities. What’s even better is that you don’t have to venture to your local deli to get a great sandwich. With a few simple ingredients and some creativity, you can recreate some of the best deli sandwiches right in the comfort of your home.

In this post, we’ll explore how you can elevate your deli sandwich game. From classic options like the Reuben to gourmet creations that will leave you coming back for more, this guide has everything you need to make the best deli sandwiches at home. Let’s dive in!

1. Classic Reuben Sandwich

There’s no denying that the Reuben sandwich is a staple in the deli world. With layers of corned beef, sauerkraut, Swiss cheese, and Russian dressing, all piled between two slices of rye bread, the Reuben is a perfect blend of savory, tangy, and creamy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of rye bread
  • 4 oz. corned beef (thinly sliced)
  • 1/4 cup sauerkraut (drained)
  • 2 slices Swiss cheese
  • 2 tbsp Russian dressing (or Thousand Island dressing)
  • 1 tbsp butter (for grilling)

Directions:

  1. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Spread butter on one side of each slice of rye bread.
  3. On the non-buttered side of one slice, spread the Russian dressing.
  4. Layer the corned beef, sauerkraut, and Swiss cheese.
  5. Place the second slice of bread on top, buttered side facing out.
  6. Grill the sandwich on each side until golden brown and the cheese is melted, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

This sandwich is rich, hearty, and perfect for any lunch or dinner. You can customize the level of sauerkraut or dressing to suit your tastes, but the core ingredients remain the same for a truly satisfying sandwich.

2. Turkey Club Sandwich

For a lighter, yet equally delicious option, a turkey club sandwich is the way to go. Packed with lean turkey, crispy bacon, fresh lettuce, and juicy tomato, the turkey club is a refreshing and satisfying meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 3 slices of whole wheat or white bread
  • 4 oz. sliced turkey breast
  • 2 slices of crispy bacon
  • 2 leaves of lettuce
  • 2 slices tomato
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard (optional)

Directions:

  1. Toast the slices of bread until they are golden brown and crispy.
  2. Spread mayonnaise (and Dijon mustard, if using) on one side of each slice of bread.
  3. On the first slice of bread, layer the turkey, bacon, lettuce, and tomato.
  4. Place the second slice of bread on top and add more turkey, bacon, lettuce, and tomato.
  5. Top with the third slice of bread to complete the sandwich.
  6. Cut into halves or quarters and serve immediately.

The turkey club sandwich is perfect for a family lunch or a picnic. The combination of fresh veggies, turkey, and crispy bacon makes it an irresistible choice for any sandwich lover.

3. The Classic BLT

Sometimes, the classics are the best. The BLT is a sandwich that never goes out of style. The simplicity of crispy bacon, fresh lettuce, and ripe tomato, all stacked on toasted bread with a spread of mayonnaise, is nothing short of perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of white or whole wheat bread
  • 4 slices of crispy bacon
  • 2 leaves of lettuce (romaine or iceberg)
  • 2 slices of ripe tomato
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise

Directions:

  1. Toast the bread slices until they’re golden and crispy.
  2. Spread mayonnaise on one side of each slice of bread.
  3. Layer the bacon, lettuce, and tomato on one slice of bread.
  4. Top with the other slice of bread.
  5. Slice the sandwich in half and serve immediately.

This sandwich is perfect for a quick lunch or even a late-night snack. You can switch up the bread type or add avocado for an extra boost of flavor and richness.

4. Italian Sub Sandwich

For fans of bold flavors, the Italian sub sandwich offers a satisfying combination of cured meats, cheese, and fresh vegetables. The robust flavors of salami, pepperoni, ham, and provolone cheese, paired with the crunch of fresh lettuce, tomatoes, and onions, make this sandwich an indulgent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 hoagie roll or sub sandwich bun
  • 2 oz. salami
  • 2 oz. pepperoni
  • 2 oz. ham
  • 2 slices provolone cheese
  • 2 leaves lettuce
  • 2 slices tomato
  • 1/4 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

Directions:

  1. Slice the hoagie roll or sub sandwich bun in half.
  2. Drizzle the olive oil and red wine vinegar over the inside of the bread.
  3. Layer the salami, pepperoni, ham, and provolone cheese on the bottom half of the sandwich.
  4. Add the lettuce, tomato, and onion slices.
  5. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and dried oregano for seasoning.
  6. Place the top half of the bread on the sandwich and press lightly.
  7. Serve with chips or a side salad.

The Italian sub is hearty, flavorful, and packed with all the deli goodness you’d expect. It’s perfect for serving at gatherings or just as a treat for yourself.

5. Veggie Delight Sandwich

For those who prefer a vegetarian option, the Veggie Delight sandwich is a great choice. Fresh, crisp vegetables layered together with your favorite condiments provide a light, refreshing alternative to traditional meat-based sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices whole grain or multigrain bread
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 small carrot, shredded
  • 2 leaves lettuce
  • 2 slices tomato
  • 2 tbsp hummus or cream cheese
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar (optional)

Directions:

  1. Spread hummus or cream cheese on one side of each slice of bread.
  2. Layer the avocado, cucumber, carrot, lettuce, and tomato on the bottom slice of bread.
  3. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar if desired.
  4. Top with the second slice of bread and press gently.
  5. Slice and serve immediately.

This sandwich is an excellent option for anyone looking for a light, healthy meal. The creamy avocado pairs wonderfully with the crunch of the cucumber and carrot, and the balsamic vinegar adds an extra layer of tanginess to the mix.

6. Pastrami on Rye

For those who love pastrami, the pastrami on rye sandwich is a classic that can easily be made at home. This sandwich offers a satisfying balance of savory, smoky pastrami with a bit of tangy mustard, all tucked into a soft, slightly chewy rye bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices rye bread
  • 4 oz. pastrami (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tbsp mustard (yellow or Dijon)
  • 2 slices Swiss cheese (optional)
  • 1 tbsp butter (for grilling)

Directions:

  1. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Spread mustard on one slice of rye bread.
  3. Layer the pastrami on top of the mustard, followed by the Swiss cheese (if using).
  4. Place the second slice of rye bread on top.
  5. Butter the outside of both slices of bread.
  6. Grill the sandwich on each side until golden brown and the cheese is melted, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Slice and enjoy!

The pastrami on rye is a savory, satisfying sandwich that’s perfect for anyone who loves rich, meaty flavors. It’s a true deli classic that you can make easily at home with minimal ingredients.

7. Chicken Caesar Sandwich

A twist on the traditional Caesar salad, the chicken Caesar sandwich is a hearty meal that combines grilled chicken, Caesar dressing, lettuce, and Parmesan cheese, all sandwiched between slices of fresh bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of ciabatta or whole wheat bread
  • 1 grilled chicken breast (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp Caesar dressing
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese (shredded)
  • 2 leaves Romaine lettuce

Directions:

  1. Spread Caesar dressing on both slices of bread.
  2. Layer the grilled chicken breast slices on one slice of bread.
  3. Add the lettuce and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
  4. Top with the other slice of bread and press gently.
  5. Serve immediately.

This sandwich is a great option for lunch or dinner and provides all the delicious flavors of a classic Caesar salad with the added bonus of grilled chicken. The creamy Caesar dressing pairs perfectly with the crispy lettuce and savory chicken.

Conclusion

Making deli sandwiches at home allows you to get creative with flavors while enjoying the satisfaction of a freshly made sandwich. Whether you prefer something traditional like the Reuben or want to try a new creation like the Veggie Delight, there’s a deli sandwich out there for every taste. The best part? You can control the quality of ingredients and tailor each sandwich to your liking.

Experiment with different breads, condiments, and fillings to create your own signature deli sandwich. The possibilities are endless, and with a little practice, you’ll be able to craft deli sandwiches that rival anything from your local deli. Happy sandwich making!
