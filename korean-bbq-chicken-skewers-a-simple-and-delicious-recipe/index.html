Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers: A Simple and Delicious Recipe


Korean BBQ chicken skewers are a perfect way to enjoy the rich, savory flavors of Korean cuisine without the need for a grill or complex preparation. These skewers are packed with flavor, thanks to the perfect blend of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil, making them tender and juicy. Whether you’re planning a summer cookout, hosting a family gathering, or simply craving a delicious and easy dinner, these Korean BBQ chicken skewers are sure to impress. This simple recipe is a wonderful introduction to the world of Korean flavors, and it’s easy enough for anyone to prepare at home.

Why You’ll Love Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

Korean BBQ has a special place in the hearts of food lovers worldwide. It’s not just about the flavors—it’s about the experience. Cooking meat over a grill, dipping it into a sweet yet savory sauce, and pairing it with refreshing side dishes like kimchi or rice creates a full meal that’s perfect for sharing. The marinade used in this recipe is a key element that makes these chicken skewers so irresistible. It’s sweet, salty, and savory all at once, delivering a complex yet balanced taste.

The beauty of these chicken skewers is that they are incredibly versatile. Whether you prefer boneless or bone-in chicken, you can use either, and the marinade will infuse your meat with the same bold flavors. Plus, they are easy to make and cook quickly, making them an excellent weeknight dinner option.

Ingredients for Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

Before we get into the cooking process, let’s go over the essential ingredients that will make these skewers so flavorful.

For the Marinade:

  • Chicken thighs (boneless and skinless): Chicken thighs are perfect for this recipe due to their tenderness and ability to absorb flavors better than chicken breasts. If you prefer, you can use chicken breasts as well, but thighs are recommended for maximum flavor.
  • Soy sauce: This provides the base of the marinade, contributing saltiness and umami.
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): Gochujang is a key ingredient in Korean BBQ recipes. It’s slightly sweet, spicy, and savory, which gives the chicken its signature depth of flavor.
  • Sesame oil: Adds a rich, nutty taste to the marinade that enhances the overall flavor profile.
  • Honey or brown sugar: This brings sweetness to balance the saltiness of the soy sauce and the heat from the gochujang.
  • Rice vinegar: A touch of acidity to brighten up the flavors and balance the richness of the sesame oil.
  • Garlic (minced): A necessary aromatic for any Korean BBQ dish, garlic gives a fragrant, savory element to the marinade.
  • Ginger (minced): Fresh ginger adds a subtle heat and freshness that complements the other ingredients in the marinade.
  • Sesame seeds (optional): Toasted sesame seeds can be sprinkled over the cooked skewers for added texture and flavor.
  • Green onions (optional): For garnish, chopped green onions can be added to bring a fresh, crisp element.

For the Skewers:

  • Wooden skewers: If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during grilling. You can also use metal skewers if you prefer.
  • Vegetables (optional): Feel free to add vegetables like bell peppers, onions, or zucchini to the skewers for added flavor and color.

How to Prepare Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by cutting your chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces, roughly 1 to 1.5 inches in size. Cutting the chicken into smaller pieces ensures that the marinade penetrates the meat more effectively, resulting in more flavorful skewers.

If you’re using bone-in chicken, remove the bones and cut the meat into smaller pieces. Bone-in chicken can add a more smoky flavor when grilled, but boneless pieces are easier to skewer and cook faster.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, gochujang, sesame oil, honey or brown sugar, rice vinegar, minced garlic, and ginger. Whisk the ingredients together until they are fully incorporated, and taste the marinade. If you want it to be spicier, add a bit more gochujang; for extra sweetness, a little more honey or sugar will do the trick.

Once you’re happy with the flavor, pour the marinade over the chicken pieces. Toss the chicken in the marinade, making sure each piece is well-coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the chicken marinate in the fridge for at least 1 hour, or up to 6 hours. The longer the chicken marinates, the more flavorful and tender it will become.

Step 3: Skewer the Chicken

While the chicken is marinating, soak your wooden skewers in water (if using) for at least 30 minutes. This prevents them from catching fire when grilling. Once the skewers are soaked and the chicken has marinated, it’s time to assemble the skewers.

Take each skewer and thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewer, leaving a small space between each piece to allow for even cooking. If you’re adding vegetables, alternate between chicken and veggies for a colorful and balanced skewer. Bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, and zucchini work great in this recipe.

Step 4: Grill the Chicken Skewers

Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. If you’re using a grill, make sure it’s properly heated before placing the skewers on the grates. You can also brush the grill with a small amount of oil to prevent the chicken from sticking.

Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is fully cooked through and has developed a nice char. The internal temperature of the chicken should reach 165°F (74°C). Depending on the size of the chicken pieces and the heat of your grill, the cooking time may vary, so be sure to keep an eye on the skewers.

If you like your skewers with a bit of extra glaze, brush them with more marinade during the last minute of grilling for added flavor and shine.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken skewers are cooked to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a couple of minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures the meat stays tender.

For an extra touch, sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over the chicken and garnish with finely chopped green onions. These garnishes not only enhance the flavor but also add visual appeal to your dish.

Serve the Korean BBQ chicken skewers with steamed rice, kimchi, or a refreshing cucumber salad for a complete meal. These skewers also pair wonderfully with a side of pickled vegetables or a light dipping sauce, like soy sauce mixed with a dash of sesame oil.

Tips for the Best Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

  1. Marinate Longer for More Flavor: The longer you let the chicken marinate, the more flavorful it will be. Aim for at least 1 hour, but if you can, marinate the chicken overnight for the best results.
  2. Use Chicken Thighs for Tender Meat: While chicken breasts can be used, chicken thighs are more tender and absorb flavors better. If you’re using breast meat, be sure not to overcook it, as it can dry out quickly.
  3. Don’t Skip the Gochujang: This key ingredient brings depth and complexity to the marinade. If you can’t find it at your local grocery store, check out Asian markets or order it online.
  4. Grill Over Medium Heat: Grilling over medium heat ensures that the chicken cooks evenly and develops a nice char without burning.
  5. Add Veggies to the Skewers: Vegetables like bell peppers, onions, and zucchini not only add color to your skewers but also absorb the flavors of the marinade, making every bite even more delicious.

Variations on the Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

  1. Spicy Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers: If you love spice, increase the amount of gochujang or add some red pepper flakes to the marinade for an extra kick.
  2. Korean BBQ Chicken with Pineapple: For a tropical twist, add chunks of pineapple to the skewers. The sweetness of the pineapple pairs beautifully with the savory chicken and tangy marinade.
  3. Honey Sesame Chicken Skewers: If you prefer a sweeter flavor profile, increase the amount of honey and sesame oil in the marinade, and add a touch of Dijon mustard for some tang.
  4. Vegetarian Korean BBQ Skewers: For a vegetarian version, replace the chicken with tofu or portobello mushrooms. Both absorb marinades well and make a great alternative for non-meat eaters.

Conclusion

Korean BBQ chicken skewers are a flavorful, easy-to-make dish that brings the taste of Korean cuisine straight to your home. The combination of savory soy sauce, spicy gochujang, and nutty sesame oil makes for a marinade that’s nothing short of delicious. These skewers are perfect for any occasion, from casual weeknight dinners to outdoor BBQs. By following the simple steps in this recipe, you’ll be able to make restaurant-quality Korean BBQ chicken skewers in no time. Whether you’re serving them with rice, kimchi, or a fresh salad, this dish is sure to become a new favorite in your recipe repertoire. Happy grilling!
