Comforting Chicken and Dumplings for Winter


Winter is the perfect time to cozy up with a warm and comforting bowl of chicken and dumplings. This classic dish is a crowd-pleaser, and its rich flavors and hearty texture make it the ultimate comfort food. In this recipe, I will share with you my tried and true method for making the most delicious chicken and dumplings you’ve ever tasted.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 stalks of celery, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 6 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of milk

Instructions

  1. Start by heating the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken thighs and cook until browned on both sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, sliced carrots, sliced celery, minced garlic, dried thyme, and bay leaf. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

  3. Return the chicken to the pot and add the chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  4. While the chicken is simmering, prepare the dumplings. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the milk, stirring until a soft dough forms.

  5. Remove the bay leaf from the pot and discard it. Using two forks, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Return the shredded chicken to the pot.

  6. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough into the pot, spacing them evenly. Cover the pot and let the dumplings cook for 10 minutes, or until they are cooked through and fluffy.

  7. Serve the chicken and dumplings hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy the warm and comforting flavors of this winter favorite!

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor, you can toss in a handful of frozen peas or corn during the last few minutes of cooking.
  • If you prefer a thicker broth, you can whisk together 1/4 cup of cornstarch with 1/4 cup of cold water and stir it into the pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  • For a twist on the traditional recipe, try using bone-in chicken thighs instead of boneless. The bones will add extra flavor to the broth.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought biscuit dough instead of making the dumplings from scratch. Just roll out the dough and cut it into small pieces before adding them to the pot.

There you have it - a delicious and comforting chicken and dumplings recipe to warm you up on those cold winter nights. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this dish is sure to impress. Give it a try and enjoy the cozy flavors of winter!
