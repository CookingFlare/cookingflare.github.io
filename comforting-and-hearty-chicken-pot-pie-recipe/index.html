Comforting and Hearty: Chicken Pot Pie Recipe


There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting chicken pot pie on a chilly evening. The combination of tender chicken, flavorful vegetables, and a flaky crust makes it the ultimate comfort food. Today, I’m sharing my family’s favorite chicken pot pie recipe that is guaranteed to satisfy your cravings. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or cubed
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 cup diced potatoes
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 sheets of puff pastry, thawed

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions, celery, carrots, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.

  3. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir until everything is coated. Continue cooking for another minute to cook off the raw flour taste.

  4. Slowly pour in the chicken broth while stirring constantly. This will help to avoid lumps in your sauce. Add the thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Stir in the cooked chicken, potatoes, and frozen mixed vegetables. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove from heat and stir in the heavy cream. Let the mixture cool slightly.

  6. Roll out one sheet of puff pastry and line a greased pie dish with it. Pour the chicken and vegetable filling into the pie dish. Cover the filling with the second sheet of puff pastry, sealing the edges.

  7. Cut a few slits in the top of the pastry to allow steam to escape. This will prevent the pie from becoming soggy. You can also brush the top of the pastry with an egg wash for a golden finish.

  8. Place the pot pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. The filling will be piping hot, so be careful while enjoying this comforting dish.

Serving Suggestions

Chicken pot pie is a meal in itself, but you can always serve it with a side salad or some steamed vegetables to add a fresh and nutritious element to your plate. This recipe is also great for leftovers, as it reheats beautifully and makes for a delicious lunch the next day.

Conclusion

There you have it, a comforting and hearty chicken pot pie recipe that will warm your soul and satisfy your hunger. This classic dish is perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or whenever you need a little extra comfort. The combination of tender chicken, flavorful vegetables, and a flaky crust is simply irresistible. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and let your taste buds thank you for the cozy goodness!
