Irresistible Raspberry Swirl Bread: Sweet and Tart

If you’re a fan of sweet and tart flavors, then this recipe for Irresistible Raspberry Swirl Bread is right up your alley. This bread is a perfect combination of soft, fluffy dough and a tangy raspberry filling that will leave you craving for more. With its vibrant colors and delightful taste, it’s sure to be a hit at any breakfast or brunch gathering. Ingredients 2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 1/2 cup buttermilk 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup fresh raspberries 1/4 cup powdered sugar Instructions Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined. Add the softened butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread. In a small bowl, gently mash the fresh raspberries with a fork until they are broken down but still have some texture. Pour half of the bread batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly. Spoon the mashed raspberries over the batter in the pan, leaving a small border around the edges. Pour the remaining batter over the raspberries and use a knife or skewer to gently swirl the two together. Don’t overmix, as you want to create a marbled effect. Bake the bread in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Once the bread has cooled, dust the top with powdered sugar for a finishing touch.
