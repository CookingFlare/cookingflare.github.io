Classic American Chicken Noodle Soup Recipe for Comfort


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can compete with a steaming bowl of classic American chicken noodle soup. This timeless recipe has been a staple in kitchens across the country, providing warmth and nourishment on chilly days or when someone is feeling under the weather. In this blog post, we will explore the history of chicken noodle soup, its comforting qualities, and a step-by-step guide to creating your very own pot of this beloved dish.

The Comfort of Chicken Noodle Soup

Chicken noodle soup is more than just a meal; it is a symbol of care and warmth. For generations, families have gathered around the dinner table to enjoy this hearty dish, often passed down through family traditions. It evokes memories of childhood and the soothing hands of a loved one serving a bowl of soup when feeling unwell. The simple combination of chicken, noodles, vegetables, and broth offers not just nourishment but also a sense of home.

A Brief History

The origins of chicken noodle soup can be traced back to ancient cultures that utilized broth for its healing properties. In the United States, the dish gained prominence in the early 20th century when it became a popular remedy for colds and flus. The iconic Campbell’s Chicken Noodle Soup, introduced in 1934, solidified its place in American cuisine and culture, making it readily available for families across the nation.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Soup Base

  • 1 Whole Chicken (about 3-4 pounds): Look for a fresh or frozen chicken for the best flavor. A whole chicken allows for a rich broth.
  • 8 cups of Chicken Broth: You can use homemade or store-bought broth, but homemade adds an extra depth of flavor.
  • 3 Carrots: Peeled and sliced into thin rounds for sweetness and color.
  • 3 Celery Stalks: Chopped to add a crunchy texture.
  • 1 Medium Onion: Diced for a flavorful base.
  • 3 Garlic Cloves: Minced for a hint of aromatic goodness.
  • 1 teaspoon of Dried Thyme: For an earthy flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of Dried Oregano: To enhance the overall taste.
  • Salt and Pepper: To season the soup to your liking.

For the Noodles

  • 8 ounces of Egg Noodles: These are traditional in chicken noodle soup, but you can also use other types of pasta if desired.

Optional Additions

  • Fresh Parsley: Chopped for garnish.
  • Lemon Juice: A splash adds brightness to the flavor.
  • Bay Leaves: For added depth in the broth.

Step-by-Step Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

  1. Clean and Rinse: Start by rinsing your whole chicken under cold water. Pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. Season: Rub the chicken with salt and pepper, both inside and out, to enhance its flavor.
  3. Combine in a Pot: Place the chicken in a large stockpot and cover it with 8 cups of chicken broth. If you prefer a richer flavor, you can use half broth and half water.

Step 2: Simmer the Broth

  1. Bring to a Boil: Place the pot over medium-high heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce Heat: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the chicken simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

Step 3: Add the Vegetables

  1. Remove the Chicken: Carefully take the chicken out of the pot and place it on a cutting board. Allow it to cool slightly.
  2. Add Vegetables: Add the diced onion, carrots, celery, garlic, thyme, and oregano to the broth. Stir and let them cook for about 10 minutes until softened.

Step 4: Shred the Chicken

  1. Shred the Chicken: While the vegetables are cooking, remove the skin and bones from the chicken. Shred the meat into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Return to the Pot: Add the shredded chicken back into the pot with the vegetables.

Step 5: Cook the Noodles

  1. Add Noodles: Stir in the egg noodles and let the soup simmer for another 8-10 minutes, or until the noodles are tender.
  2. Taste and Adjust: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. If you like, add a splash of lemon juice for brightness.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color.
  2. Serve Hot: Enjoy your classic American chicken noodle soup hot, paired with crusty bread or crackers for a complete meal.

Tips for the Perfect Chicken Noodle Soup

  • Homemade Broth: If time permits, consider making your own chicken broth. It enhances the flavor and offers a personal touch.
  • Vegetable Variations: Feel free to get creative with the vegetables. Adding peas, corn, or green beans can provide more color and nutrition.
  • Storage: This soup is perfect for meal prep. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat on the stovetop or in the microwave.
  • Freezing: If you want to store it for longer, freeze the soup without the noodles for up to three months. When you’re ready to enjoy it, reheat and add freshly cooked noodles.

Health Benefits of Chicken Noodle Soup

Chicken noodle soup is not only delicious but also has several health benefits:

  • Hydration: The broth helps keep you hydrated, especially when you’re feeling under the weather.
  • Nutrients: The combination of chicken and vegetables offers a good source of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Comforting: The warm soup can soothe sore throats and provide comfort during illness, making it a go-to remedy.

Variations to Try

While the classic chicken noodle soup is iconic, you can experiment with various variations to suit your taste:

Creamy Chicken Noodle Soup

Add a splash of heavy cream or a dollop of sour cream to your soup for a rich, creamy texture. This variation offers a different mouthfeel and flavor profile.

Spicy Chicken Noodle Soup

If you enjoy a kick of heat, add some red pepper flakes or diced jalapeños to your broth. This spicy twist will awaken your taste buds and warm you up even more.

Asian-Inspired Chicken Noodle Soup

Substitute egg noodles with rice noodles and add soy sauce, ginger, and green onions for an Asian-inspired version. This fusion recipe offers a delightful new take on the classic dish.

Final Thoughts

Classic American chicken noodle soup is a dish that brings comfort, warmth, and nostalgia. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or for loved ones, this recipe is sure to warm hearts and bellies alike. The simple ingredients come together to create a nourishing and satisfying meal that can be enjoyed any time of the year. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aromas of chicken noodle soup fill your kitchen. It’s time to enjoy a bowl of comfort that truly feels like home.
