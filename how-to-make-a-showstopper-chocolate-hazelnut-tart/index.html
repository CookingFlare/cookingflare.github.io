How to Make a Showstopper Chocolate Hazelnut Tart


Indulging in a rich, decadent dessert is one of life’s greatest pleasures, and this Chocolate Hazelnut Tart is the epitome of indulgence. With its velvety chocolate filling, buttery hazelnut crust, and a touch of elegance, this tart is sure to impress at any gathering. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or simply treating yourself, this showstopper dessert will steal the spotlight. Let’s dive into the art of creating a Chocolate Hazelnut Tart that not only tastes divine but also looks stunning on your dessert table.

Ingredients

For the Hazelnut Crust

  • 1 ½ cups hazelnuts, toasted and skins removed
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons ice water (more if needed)

For the Chocolate Filling

  • 8 ounces dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Hazelnut Topping

  • ½ cup hazelnuts, roughly chopped and toasted
  • 2 tablespoons chocolate shavings (optional)
  • Sea salt, for garnish

Equipment Needed

  • Food processor
  • 9-inch tart pan with removable bottom
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Saucepan
  • Rubber spatula
  • Oven

Step 1: Prepare the Hazelnut Crust

Toast the Hazelnuts

Start by toasting the hazelnuts to enhance their flavor. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the hazelnuts on a baking sheet and toast for about 10-12 minutes until they are golden brown and fragrant. Allow them to cool before removing the skins by rubbing them in a clean kitchen towel.

Make the Crust

  1. In a food processor, combine the toasted hazelnuts, all-purpose flour, powdered sugar, and salt. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter and pulse until the mixture becomes crumbly.
  3. Add the egg yolk and ice water, pulsing until the dough comes together. If the dough is too dry, add more ice water, one tablespoon at a time.
  4. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

Blind Bake the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit your tart pan. Press the dough into the pan and trim any excess.
  3. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes until lightly golden.
  4. Allow the crust to cool completely before filling it.

Step 2: Prepare the Chocolate Filling

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is heated through, but not boiling.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the chopped dark chocolate. Let it sit for 2-3 minutes until the chocolate softens, then whisk until smooth.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Gradually add the chocolate mixture to the eggs, whisking continuously to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  4. Pour the chocolate filling into the cooled hazelnut crust, smoothing the top with a rubber spatula.

Step 3: Bake the Tart

  1. Place the filled tart back into the oven and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes. The filling should be set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center.
  2. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool at room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

Prepare the Topping

  1. Before serving, toast the roughly chopped hazelnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden and fragrant. This step adds a delightful crunch to your tart.
  2. Once the tart is set, remove it from the refrigerator. Garnish the top with the toasted hazelnuts and chocolate shavings if desired. Finish with a sprinkle of sea salt for an extra touch of flavor.

Slice and Enjoy

Using a sharp knife, slice the tart into wedges. Serve the Chocolate Hazelnut Tart chilled or at room temperature, as it pairs beautifully with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips for the Perfect Chocolate Hazelnut Tart

  • Quality Ingredients: Use high-quality dark chocolate for a richer flavor. The cocoa content can be adjusted based on your preference for sweetness and depth.
  • Chill the Crust: Chilling the dough before baking helps to achieve a tender, flaky crust that holds its shape.
  • Don’t Rush the Cooling: Allowing the tart to cool and set properly ensures a perfect slice when serving.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add a hint of espresso to the chocolate filling for a mocha twist or a splash of orange liqueur for a citrusy note.

Conclusion

This Chocolate Hazelnut Tart is more than just a dessert; it’s an experience that will tantalize your taste buds and delight your guests. The combination of rich chocolate and nutty hazelnuts creates a symphony of flavors that is hard to resist. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create a showstopper that will have everyone asking for seconds. Enjoy!
