Tasty Turkey Meatball Recipe for a Healthy Twist


Are you tired of the same old meatball recipes that are high in fat and calories? Look no further! This tasty turkey meatball recipe is a game-changer for anyone looking for a healthier twist on a classic favorite.

Why Turkey Meatballs?

Turkey meatballs are a great alternative to traditional beef or pork meatballs for several reasons. First, they are lower in saturated fat and calories, making them a healthier option for those watching their weight or managing health conditions. Second, turkey meat is leaner and has a milder flavor than beef or pork, which allows the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine through. Finally, turkey meatballs are a great way to add protein to your diet without breaking the bank.

The Ingredients

To make this delicious turkey meatball recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

The Instructions

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, parsley, garlic, salt, black pepper, and Italian seasoning. Mix the ingredients together with your hands or a wooden spoon until they are just combined. Do not overmix.

Use your hands to shape the mixture into meatballs, about 1 1/2 inches (3.8 cm) in diameter. You should end up with around 20-25 meatballs.

Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and drizzle with olive oil. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through and lightly browned on the outside.

The Sauce

While the meatballs are baking, you can prepare the sauce. In a large saucepan, combine 2 cups of marinara sauce and 1 cup of chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.

The Finishing Touches

Once the meatballs are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Then, add them to the saucepan with the sauce and simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the meatballs are fully coated in the sauce.

Serve and Enjoy

These tasty turkey meatballs are perfect for serving with pasta, on a sub sandwich, or as an appetizer for a party. You can also freeze them for up to 3 months and reheat them in the oven or microwave when you’re ready.

Conclusion

This tasty turkey meatball recipe is a healthier twist on a classic favorite. With its lean turkey meat and flavorful sauce, it’s a great option for anyone looking for a delicious and nutritious meal. Give it a try and enjoy!
