Delicious Greek Meatballs (Keftedes) with Fresh Herbs


Greek cuisine is a celebration of vibrant flavors, wholesome ingredients, and time-honored traditions. Among the myriad of dishes that define this culinary heritage, Keftedes, or Greek meatballs, stand out as a beloved favorite. These perfectly seasoned meatballs are tender, juicy, and infused with fresh herbs that deliver a distinct Mediterranean profile. Whether served as an appetizer, nestled in pita bread, or alongside a hearty salad, Keftedes are a versatile dish that appeals to any palate.

The Origin of Keftedes

Keftedes have a rich history that traces back centuries, deeply rooted in Greek culture and family gatherings. As with many dishes from this region, the recipe varies slightly depending on the household. Many families pass this recipe down through generations like a treasured heirloom. What makes Keftedes truly special is the combination of simple ingredients elevated by fresh herbs like parsley and mint, along with well-chosen seasonings that deliver an unforgettable burst of flavor with each bite.

The Secret to Perfectly Tender Greek Meatballs

Achieving the ideal texture for Keftedes requires attention to detail. The balance of moisture and binding ingredients is key to keeping these meatballs tender and juicy. Traditional Greek recipes often use soaked bread or breadcrumbs to provide structure without making the meat dense. Additionally, the incorporation of grated onion and garlic ensures every bite is flavorful and aromatic.

Fresh herbs, particularly parsley and mint, are signature components that bring life to this dish. Mint, often underrated in savory recipes, adds a refreshing brightness that complements the rich, savory flavors of the meat and spices. Preparing Keftedes isnâ€™t complicated, but the preparation process highlights the importance of balance and precision. Let’s dive into how to make these delicious meatballs at home.

Ingredients for Greek Meatballs (Keftedes)

For the Meatballs:

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for extra flavor)
  • 1/2 cup stale bread, soaked in milk and crumbled (or breadcrumbs as a substitute)
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Optional Toppings or Accompaniments:

  • Crumbled feta cheese
  • Lemon wedges
  • Tzatziki sauce
  • Thinly sliced red onions and tomatoes
  • Greek pita bread or flatbread

Step-by-Step Instructions: How to Make Keftedes

1. Prepare the Ingredients

Begin by gathering all your ingredients and prepping the herbs. Finely chop parsley and mint, ensuring no large stems remain. Grate the onion either by hand or using a food processor, and mince the garlic for an even texture. For the bread, soak it briefly in milk and squeeze out the excess liquid before crumbling it into small bits.

2. Mix the Meatball Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat (you can use beef, pork, or a mix of both for extra flavor) with the grated onion, minced garlic, crumbled bread, egg, fresh parsley, mint, dried oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper. Using your hands, mix everything together gently until the ingredients are evenly distributed. Be careful not to overwork the mixture; this can make the meatballs dense.

3. Shape the Meatballs

Once the mixture is ready, scoop small portions and roll them into round meatballs, roughly the size of a golf ball. For best results, keep your hands lightly wet to prevent sticking and ensure smooth meatballs.

4. Chill the Meatballs

To help them retain their shape while cooking, place the rolled meatballs on a tray and refrigerate them for about 20-30 minutes. Chilling also allows the flavors to meld beautifully.

5. Cook the Meatballs

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chilled meatballs to the pan, ensuring they have space to cook evenly. Fry them until golden brown and cooked through, turning occasionally to cook all sides. This should take about 8-10 minutes. Remove the cooked meatballs from the skillet and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Serving Suggestions

Traditional Style

Keftedes are often served with a simple squeeze of lemon juice for a bright, zesty finish. Pair them with a creamy tzatziki sauce, which complements the herbs and spices beautifully. A side of crispy potatoes or a vibrant Greek salad featuring tomatoes, cucumbers, feta cheese, and olives is a great addition.

Wrapped in Pita

Transform your Keftedes into a satisfying sandwich by serving them in soft pita bread. Add a dollop of tzatziki, fresh vegetables like red onions and tomatoes, and a sprinkle of crumbled feta cheese. This is an easy way to turn the meatballs into a Greek-inspired street food dish.

Family-Style Dinner

For a comforting family-style meal, offer the meatballs alongside rice pilaf or roasted vegetables. You can also drizzle them with extra virgin olive oil and toss them in a light tomato sauce to create another layer of flavor.
