Creamy and Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for indulgence, and what better way to treat your loved ones than with a sumptuous dish of creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes? This classic side dish is a crowd-pleaser, and with a few extra special touches, it can become the star of your Thanksgiving feast. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying an intimate meal with family, these scalloped potatoes are sure to impress. So, let’s dive into the recipe and make this Thanksgiving memorable with a delightful culinary creation.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and butter a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. Prepare the Potatoes: In a large bowl, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Add the sliced potatoes to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the creamy mixture.

  3. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Once melted, whisk in the all-purpose flour to create a roux. Gradually pour in the remaining creamy mixture from the bowl, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in 1 1/2 cups of Gruyere cheese and 1/2 cup of Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth.

  4. Layer the Potatoes: Arrange a layer of the creamy potatoes in the prepared baking dish, then sprinkle some of the remaining Gruyere and Parmesan cheeses on top. Repeat the layering process until all the potatoes and cheese sauce are used up, ensuring that the top layer is generously covered with the remaining cheese.

  5. Bake to Perfection: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 45 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden and bubbly.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Once out of the oven, let the scalloped potatoes rest for a few minutes. Garnish with freshly chopped chives for a pop of color and fresh flavor. Serve these creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes hot alongside your Thanksgiving turkey or as a standalone delight.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a crispy top, broil the scalloped potatoes for a couple of minutes after baking.
  • Experiment with different cheese combinations, such as cheddar, fontina, or mozzarella, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Add a layer of thinly sliced onions or crispy bacon between the potato layers for extra depth of flavor.

Conclusion:

Creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are a timeless Thanksgiving classic that never fails to impress. The velvety texture of the potatoes combined with the richness of the cheese sauce creates a dish that is both comforting and luxurious. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding. So, this Thanksgiving, elevate your feast with these delectable scalloped potatoes, and watch as your guests savor every spoonful of this delightful creation. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
