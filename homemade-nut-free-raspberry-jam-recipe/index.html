Homemade Nut-Free Raspberry Jam Recipe

3 min · 583 words

Are you looking for a delicious and easy-to-make jam recipe that’s free from nuts? Look no further! This homemade nut-free raspberry jam is bursting with the fresh, sweet flavors of ripe raspberries and is perfect for spreading on toast, adding to yogurt, or using as a filling for cakes and pastries. The best part? You can make it right in your own kitchen with just a few simple ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 packet (about 1.75 oz) powdered fruit pectin
  • 1/2 teaspoon unsalted butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Raspberries: Start by thoroughly washing the raspberries under cold water. Once clean, gently pat them dry with a paper towel and remove any stems or leaves.

  2. Mash the Raspberries: Place the raspberries in a large bowl and use a potato masher or fork to mash them into a chunky puree. You can leave some small pieces for texture.

  3. Cook the Raspberries: Transfer the mashed raspberries to a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan. Add the lemon juice and butter, then bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.

  4. Add the Sugar: Once the raspberry mixture is simmering, gradually stir in the granulated sugar, making sure it dissolves completely. Continue to cook the mixture, stirring frequently, until it reaches a rolling boil.

  5. Incorporate Fruit Pectin: Sprinkle the powdered fruit pectin over the boiling raspberry mixture, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Allow the mixture to boil for exactly 1 minute, continuing to stir.

  6. Test for Gel Point: To check for the gel point, place a small amount of the jam on a chilled plate and let it sit for a minute. If it wrinkles when pushed with your finger, it’s ready. If not, continue to boil for another minute and test again.

  7. Transfer to Jars: Once the jam has reached the desired consistency, remove the saucepan from the heat. Ladle the hot jam into clean, sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4-inch space at the top. Wipe the jar rims clean, then seal the jars with lids and bands.

  8. Process the Jars: If you’re planning to store the jam for an extended period, you can process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to ensure a proper seal. Otherwise, simply allow the jars to cool at room temperature before refrigerating.

Enjoy Your Nut-Free Raspberry Jam!

Congratulations, you’ve just made your own batch of delicious homemade nut-free raspberry jam! This sweet and tangy jam is a delightful addition to any breakfast or dessert table. Make sure to store your jars in the refrigerator and use within a few weeks for the best flavor and quality.

Whether you’re a seasoned jam maker or trying it for the first time, this nut-free raspberry jam recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Experiment with different ways to enjoy it and share the joy of homemade goodness with your family and friends. Happy jam-making!
