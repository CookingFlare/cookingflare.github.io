Classic Caesar Salad: Recipe and Dressing Made from Scratch


Caesar salad is a timeless classic that has graced tables across the globe, revered for its bold flavors and satisfying texture. With its crisp romaine lettuce, crunchy croutons, and a creamy dressing that tantalizes the palate, this dish is a staple in both casual and fine dining settings. Making a Caesar salad from scratch not only elevates the dish but also allows you to customize it to your taste. Let’s dive into creating the perfect Caesar salad, complete with a homemade dressing that will make your taste buds dance.

The History of Caesar Salad

Before we get into the recipe, it’s fascinating to note that the Caesar salad is not named after Julius Caesar, but rather Caesar Cardini, an Italian-American restaurateur. Legend has it that Cardini invented the salad in the 1920s in his restaurant in Tijuana, Mexico. The original recipe was simple, using just romaine lettuce, croutons, Parmesan cheese, olive oil, and a unique dressing that has evolved over the years.

Ingredients for the Classic Caesar Salad

For the Salad

  • Romaine Lettuce: 2 large heads, washed and chopped
  • Croutons: 2 cups (homemade or store-bought)
  • Parmesan Cheese: 1 cup, freshly grated
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper: to taste
  • Lemon Wedges: for garnish (optional)

For the Dressing

  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  • Anchovy Fillets: 4, finely chopped (or 2 teaspoons of anchovy paste)
  • Egg Yolks: 2 (preferably coddled for safety)
  • Dijon Mustard: 1 teaspoon
  • Worcestershire Sauce: 1 teaspoon
  • Fresh Lemon Juice: 2 tablespoons
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: ½ cup
  • Parmesan Cheese: ¼ cup, grated
  • Salt: to taste
  • Black Pepper: to taste

Making the Homemade Croutons

Homemade croutons are a game-changer for your Caesar salad. They are easy to make and will add a crunchy texture that complements the creamy dressing.

Ingredients for Croutons

  • Bread: 4 slices (preferably a day or two old, any type you like)
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Garlic Powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Black Pepper: to taste

Instructions for Croutons

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Bread: Cut the bread into bite-sized cubes. You can remove the crust if you prefer.

  3. Season the Croutons: In a large bowl, combine the bread cubes with olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss until evenly coated.

  4. Bake: Spread the seasoned bread cubes on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake for about 10-15 minutes or until golden brown, tossing halfway through to ensure even browning.

  5. Cool and Store: Remove from the oven and let them cool. Use immediately or store in an airtight container for up to a week.

Preparing the Homemade Caesar Dressing

The dressing is the heart of a Caesar salad. Making it from scratch elevates the flavors and freshness of the dish.

Instructions for Dressing

  1. Mince the Garlic and Anchovies: In a medium bowl, combine the minced garlic and chopped anchovies. Use the back of a fork to mash them into a paste.

  2. Mix the Egg Yolks: Add the egg yolks, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and lemon juice to the garlic-anchovy mixture. Whisk until well combined.

  3. Emulsify: While whisking continuously, slowly drizzle in the extra virgin olive oil. This step is crucial as it creates a creamy emulsion. Keep whisking until the dressing thickens.

  4. Add Cheese and Season: Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Adjust lemon juice or cheese based on your preference.

  5. Taste and Adjust: Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a tangier dressing, add more lemon juice.

Assembling the Classic Caesar Salad

Now that you have all your components ready, it’s time to assemble your Classic Caesar Salad!

Instructions for Assembling the Salad

  1. Prepare the Lettuce: In a large bowl, add the chopped romaine lettuce. Ensure it is dry to help the dressing adhere better.

  2. Add the Dressing: Pour the homemade Caesar dressing over the lettuce. Toss gently to coat the leaves evenly.

  3. Incorporate Croutons: Add the homemade croutons to the salad and toss lightly to combine.

  4. Finish with Parmesan: Top the salad with freshly grated Parmesan cheese, and if desired, a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Serve the salad immediately, garnished with lemon wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Caesar Salad

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh romaine and high-quality Parmesan cheese make a significant difference in flavor.
  • Coddle the Eggs: If you’re concerned about using raw eggs, you can coddle them by placing them in boiling water for about 1 minute before using them in the dressing.
  • Customize Your Salad: Feel free to add proteins like grilled chicken, shrimp, or bacon to make it a heartier meal.
  • Store Leftovers Wisely: If you have leftover salad, store the dressing separately to prevent the lettuce from wilting. Combine before serving.

Variations of Caesar Salad

While the classic Caesar salad is delicious on its own, there are numerous variations you can explore to give this dish a personal twist:

  1. Grilled Chicken Caesar Salad: Top your salad with slices of grilled chicken breast for added protein.

  2. Kale Caesar Salad: Substitute romaine with kale for a nutrient-rich alternative. Massage the kale leaves with a bit of olive oil to soften them before adding the dressing.

  3. Vegan Caesar Salad: Replace the anchovies with capers and use a vegan mayonnaise instead of egg yolks in the dressing. Nutritional yeast can also be added for a cheesy flavor.

  4. Spicy Caesar Salad: Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a few dashes of hot sauce to the dressing for a spicy kick.

Conclusion

Creating a Classic Caesar Salad from scratch is a rewarding culinary experience that brings together fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and a touch of history. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet meal at home, this salad is sure to impress. By making your own dressing and croutons, you can control the flavors and ingredients, ensuring a delightful dish that will have everyone coming back for seconds. So grab your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the process of making this timeless salad from scratch!
