Vegan Pumpkin Pancakes: Fluffy and Spiced Morning Delights


Are you ready to start your day with a delicious and nutritious breakfast? Look no further than these Vegan Pumpkin Pancakes! Packed with the warm flavors of fall, these fluffy and spiced pancakes are the perfect way to kickstart your morning.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup almond milk (or any non-dairy milk)
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt. Ensure all the dry ingredients are well combined.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the almond milk, pumpkin puree, apple cider vinegar, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract. Make sure the wet ingredients are thoroughly mixed.

  3. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense pancakes.

  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with cooking spray or a small amount of coconut oil.

  5. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, pour the pancake batter onto the heated skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until bubbles start to form on the surface. Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.

  6. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, adding more oil to the skillet as needed. This recipe typically yields around 8 medium-sized pancakes.

  7. Serve the pancakes warm, topped with your favorite vegan butter, maple syrup, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar. You can also add a dollop of coconut whipped cream or a handful of toasted pecans for extra flavor and texture.

Tips and Variations

  • For a gluten-free option, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
  • If you prefer a sweeter pancake, increase the amount of brown sugar or add a drizzle of maple syrup to the batter.
  • Feel free to experiment with different spices, such as cloves or allspice, to customize the flavor profile of your pancakes.
  • To make these pancakes extra indulgent, you can fold in a handful of dairy-free chocolate chips or chopped nuts into the batter before cooking.
  • Leftover pancakes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat them in a toaster or microwave before enjoying.

Conclusion

These Vegan Pumpkin Pancakes are a delightful way to start your day. The combination of warm spices and creamy pumpkin creates a comforting and satisfying breakfast that will keep you fueled throughout the morning. Whether you’re a vegan or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these pancakes are sure to please your taste buds. So, go ahead and give this recipe a try—you won’t be disappointed!
