Baking with Vegetables: Surprising and Delicious Savory Bakes


Are you tired of the same old baking recipes? Looking to add a healthy twist to your baked goods? Well, look no further because today we’re going to explore the wonderful world of baking with vegetables! Yes, you heard it right – vegetables!

Vegetables are not only packed with essential nutrients, but they also add incredible flavor, moisture, and texture to baked goods. So, put on your apron and let’s get started on some surprising and delicious savory bakes that will leave you craving for more.

1. Zucchini and Feta Cheese Muffins

Who said muffins have to be sweet? These savory muffins are a delightful way to incorporate zucchini into your baking.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated zucchini
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the grated zucchini, feta cheese, and chopped basil.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
  4. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and then add the milk and olive oil. Mix well.
  5. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Gently fold in the zucchini, feta cheese, and basil mixture.
  7. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each liner about three-quarters full.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

2. Butternut Squash and Sage Scones

Scones are a classic baked treat, but have you ever tried them with butternut squash and sage? These scones are the perfect balance of sweet and savory, making them a great addition to your breakfast or afternoon tea.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 1 cup mashed roasted butternut squash
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh sage
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Add the cold butter to the dry ingredients and use your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in the mashed butternut squash and chopped sage until well combined.
  5. Pour in the buttermilk and mix until a soft dough forms.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together.
  7. Pat the dough into a circle about 1 inch thick and cut it into wedges.
  8. Place the scones onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-18 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Allow the scones to cool slightly before serving.

3. Spinach and Feta Bread

This savory bread is not only visually stunning but also incredibly delicious. The combination of spinach and feta cheese creates a burst of flavors that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, instant yeast, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add the warm water and olive oil to the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes or until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and knead in the chopped spinach, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped sun-dried tomatoes.
  6. Shape the dough into a loaf and place it in a greased loaf pan.
  7. Cover the pan with a kitchen towel and let the dough rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  9. Bake the bread for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown and baked through.
  10. Allow the bread to cool completely before slicing.

Conclusion

Baking with vegetables opens up a whole new world of flavors and possibilities. From zucchini muffins to butternut squash scones and spinach bread, these surprising and delicious savory bakes will impress your friends and family. So, don’t be afraid to get creative in the kitchen and explore the wonderful world of vegetable-based baking. Your taste buds will thank you!
