Flavorful Gluten-Free Chicken Pot Pie Recipe for a Comforting Meal


Are you craving a warm, comforting meal that’s both delicious and gluten-free? Look no further than this flavorful gluten-free chicken pot pie recipe! Packed with hearty vegetables, tender chicken, and a rich, creamy sauce, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re following a gluten-free diet or simply looking for a satisfying dinner option, this recipe ticks all the boxes.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon xanthan gum
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any non-dairy milk of choice)
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust:

    • In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, xanthan gum, and salt.
    • Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together.
    • Shape the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté until the vegetables are tender.
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
    • Sprinkle the gluten-free flour over the vegetables and stir to coat.
    • Gradually pour in the chicken broth and almond milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
    • Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens.
    • Stir in the shredded chicken, frozen peas, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Pot Pie:

    • Roll out the chilled pie crust on a floured surface to fit the size of your pie dish.
    • Pour the prepared chicken and vegetable filling into the pie dish.
    • Carefully place the rolled-out crust over the filling and crimp the edges to seal.
    • Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  4. Bake and Enjoy:

    • Place the pot pie in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
    • Allow the pot pie to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips and Serving Suggestions

  • For a dairy-free option, use non-dairy butter in the crust and unsweetened almond milk in the filling.
  • Feel free to customize the filling with your favorite vegetables such as mushrooms, bell peppers, or green beans.
  • Serve the gluten-free chicken pot pie with a side of fresh green salad for a well-rounded meal.

Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a cozy night in, this flavorful gluten-free chicken pot pie is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. The buttery crust, creamy filling, and tender chicken make this dish a true comfort food classic, and the fact that it’s gluten-free means everyone can enjoy it. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every bite of this homemade delight!
