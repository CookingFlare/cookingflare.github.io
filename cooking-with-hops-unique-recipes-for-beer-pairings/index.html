Cooking with Hops: Unique Recipes for Beer Pairings


Hello fellow food enthusiasts! Today, we’re delving into the world of hop-infused cooking. If you’re an adventurous home cook or simply trying to elevate your culinary skills, look no further than the hoppy treasures hidden in your beer fridge. Read on for unique recipes that will revolutionize the way you pair your favorite brews.

Hops, the cone-shaped resins produced by the Humulus lupulus plant, have been used for centuries to flavor and preserve beer. But, their potential as an ingredient doesn’t stop there. From soups to desserts, these bitter, floral, and fruity Praeteritae can enhance both savory and sweet dishes. So, grab your favorite brew and let’s get cooking!

  1. Hoppy Deviled Eggs
  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes
  • Yields: 8 servings

Ingredients:

  • 4 large hard-boiled eggs
  • 1/4 cup malt vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground hops
  • 2 tablespoons coarse-grained Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons chopped, pickled jumping jack peppers
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Cut the hard-boiled eggs in half. Remove the yolks and set them aside.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the malt vinegar, ground hops, Dijon mustard, mayonnaise, and chopped jumpin’ jack peppers.

  3. Add the egg yolks to the mixture and mash them until well-incorporated.

  4. Pipe or spoon the mixture back into the egg whites.

  5. Sprinkle with salt, ground black pepper, and a dash of hops for that extra beer flavor!

  6. Hop Shoots Salad

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 5 minutes
  • Yields: 4 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh hop shoots, washed and stems removed
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground hops
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes

Method:

  1. In a dry pan over medium heat, toast the hop shoots until they start to turn color. Set aside to cool.

  2. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, ground hops, lemon juice, and garlic.

  3. Add the hop shoots to the mixture and toss to evenly coat.

  4. Season with salt and ground black pepper, then garnish with cherry tomatoes.

  5. Serve as a side dish or light lunch.

  6. Hoppy Brown Butter Rice Pudding

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 40-45 minutes
  • Yields: 6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups brewed beer (use your favorite hoppy beer)
  • 1 cup washed and drained basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup grated hops
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Method:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the brewed beer and basmati rice. Bring to a simmer and cook until the beer is absorbed.
  2. Add the sugar, milk, grated hops, cinnamon, and salt. Stir well.
  3. Continue cooking the rice pudding over low heat for 40-45 minutes or until the rice is tender.
  4. Let the pudding cool to room temperature before serving, garnished with whipped cream if desired.

Now that you’re all set with hop-infused recipes, remember that beer isn’t just for drinking - it can be a versatile ingredient in many culinary creations. So, experiment with different brewing techniques and ingredients, and discover new culinary possibilities. Cheers to cooking with hops and raising a glass to your next gastronomic adventure!
