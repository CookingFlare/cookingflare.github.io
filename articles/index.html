Cauliflower Crust Pizza: A Low Carb Option

Are you craving pizza but trying to stick to a low carb diet? Look no further than cauliflower crust pizza! This delicious alternative to traditional pizza crust is packed with flavor and nutrients, making it the perfect guilt-free indulgence. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps of making a cauliflower crust pizza that will satisfy your cravings while keeping your carb intake in check. Ingredients 1 medium cauliflower head 2 eggs 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder 1/4 teaspoon salt Your favorite pizza toppings (e.g., tomato sauce, cheese, vegetables, and meats) Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set it aside. Wash the cauliflower and remove the leaves. Cut it into florets and pulse them in a food processor until they resemble rice-like grains. Transfer the cauliflower rice to a microwave-safe bowl and microwave it on high for 5 minutes. Let it cool for a few minutes. Place the cauliflower rice in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. This step is crucial to ensure a crisp crust. In a mixing bowl, combine the squeezed cauliflower rice, eggs, Parmesan cheese, dried oregano, garlic powder, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Transfer the cauliflower mixture onto the prepared baking sheet. Use your hands or a spatula to shape it into a thin, round crust. Aim for a thickness of about 1/4 inch. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and firm to the touch. Once the crust is ready, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, add your desired pizza toppings, such as tomato sauce, cheese, vegetables, and meats. Return the topped pizza to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Tips and Variations For a crispier crust, you can place the baked crust under the broiler for a couple of minutes after adding the toppings. Experiment with different seasonings and spices to customize the flavor of your crust. Try adding dried basil, thyme, or even a pinch of red pepper flakes for a kick. Don’t limit yourself to traditional pizza toppings. Get creative and try combinations like barbecue chicken with red onions, or pesto with cherry tomatoes and mozzarella. If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought cauliflower rice instead of making it from scratch. Just make sure to squeeze out the excess moisture as instructed.
