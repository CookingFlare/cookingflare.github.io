5 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Spinach


Spinach, a nutrient powerhouse, is not just a favorite of Popeye; it’s a versatile ingredient that can transform a mundane meal into a delicious and healthy feast. Packed with vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron and calcium, this leafy green is an excellent choice for anyone looking to boost their nutrition. In this blog post, we’ll explore five easy recipes that highlight spinach’s flavor and health benefits, making it an essential part of your culinary repertoire. Let’s dive into these delightful dishes that are simple enough for weeknight dinners yet impressive enough for entertaining guests.

1. Creamy Spinach and Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a saucepan, heat the vegetable broth over low heat. Keep it warm while you prepare the risotto.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Stir in the garlic and sliced mushrooms, cooking until the mushrooms are tender, about 5 minutes.

  3. Cook the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet, stirring to coat it with the oil and vegetables. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice is slightly toasted.

  4. Add Broth Gradually: Begin adding the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Allow the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 18-20 minutes.

  5. Finish with Spinach and Cheese: When the rice is nearly done, stir in the chopped spinach and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the spinach is wilted.

  6. Serve: Enjoy this creamy risotto warm, garnished with extra Parmesan if desired.

2. Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breast

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • ½ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Toothpicks or kitchen twine

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, combine the chopped spinach, crumbled feta cheese, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Stuff the Chicken: Using a sharp knife, create a pocket in each chicken breast. Stuff each pocket with the spinach and feta mixture, securing with toothpicks or tying with kitchen twine.

  4. Sear the Chicken: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the stuffed chicken breasts and sear for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown.

  5. Bake: Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F/75°C).

  6. Serve: Remove the toothpicks or twine, slice the chicken, and serve with a side of steamed vegetables or a fresh salad.

3. Spinach Quiche with Goat Cheese

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade)
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Pie Crust: Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish. Prick the bottom with a fork to prevent bubbling.

  3. Sauté Onion and Spinach: In a skillet over medium heat, sauté the chopped onion until soft. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

  4. Whisk Eggs and Milk: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.

  5. Combine Ingredients: Spread the sautéed onion and spinach mixture evenly over the pie crust. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese on top, then pour the egg mixture over everything.

  6. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.

  7. Serve: Enjoy warm or at room temperature, perfect for brunch or a light dinner.

4. Spinach and Chickpea Salad

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh spinach, torn
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Salad Base: In a large bowl, combine the torn spinach, chickpeas, diced bell pepper, and sliced red onion.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  3. Combine and Toss: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese.

  4. Serve: This refreshing salad can be served immediately or chilled in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

5. Spinach Pesto Pasta

Ingredients

  • 8 oz pasta of your choice (spaghetti, penne, etc.)
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • ½ cup walnuts or pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot, boil salted water and cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining.

  2. Make the Pesto: In a food processor, combine spinach, nuts, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. Pulse until finely chopped. With the processor running, gradually add olive oil until a smooth paste forms. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Combine with Pasta: In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta with the spinach pesto. If the mixture seems dry, add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach the desired consistency.

  4. Serve: This vibrant dish can be served warm, at room temperature, or even chilled. Top with extra Parmesan and toasted nuts for added crunch.

Conclusion

Spinach is more than just a side dish; it’s a superstar ingredient that can enhance various meals, from hearty risottos to light salads. Each of these five easy recipes showcases the versatility of spinach and its ability to complement a wide range of flavors. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a dish to impress your guests, these recipes are sure to please. So next time you’re at the grocery store, don’t forget to pick up some fresh spinach and give these recipes a try! Enjoy the journey of cooking, and savor the delicious benefits of this leafy green.
