Delightful Mexican Desserts Everyone Will Love


Mexican cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, rich history, and cultural significance. Among its many treasures, Mexican desserts stand out, offering an array of delightful treats that are both indulgent and comforting. From creamy flans to luscious tres leches cake, these desserts are sure to satisfy any sweet tooth. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most beloved Mexican desserts, delving into their origins, ingredients, and how to prepare them at home. Get ready to embark on a sweet journey through Mexico!

1. Flan: The Creamy Caramel Custard

Flan is perhaps the most iconic of all Mexican desserts. This silky custard is topped with a rich caramel sauce, making it a favorite at celebrations and family gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Caramel: In a saucepan, heat the sugar over medium heat until it melts and turns golden brown. Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking dish, tilting to cover the bottom evenly. Set aside to harden.

  2. Blend the Custard: In a blender, combine the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth.

  3. Bake the Flan: Pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the baking dish. Place the dish in a larger baking pan filled with water (this is called a water bath) and bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 50-60 minutes, or until the flan is set.

  4. Cool and Serve: Let the flan cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving. To serve, run a knife around the edges and invert onto a plate, allowing the caramel to flow over the custard.

2. Tres Leches Cake: A Soaked Delight

Tres leches cake, or three milks cake, is a sponge cake soaked in three types of milk: evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream. This dessert is a staple at birthdays and special occasions, celebrated for its moist texture and sweet flavor.

Ingredients:

  • For the cake:

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • 5 large eggs
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • ½ cup whole milk

  • For the milk mixture:

    • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
    • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
    • 1 cup heavy cream

  • For the topping:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • Fresh berries or fruit for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Bake the Cake: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch baking dish. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, beat eggs and sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually add the flour mixture and milk, mixing until just combined. Pour into the baking dish and bake for 25-30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Milk Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream.

  3. Soak the Cake: Once the cake is baked and slightly cooled, poke holes all over the top with a fork. Pour the milk mixture evenly over the cake, allowing it to soak in for at least an hour, or overnight in the fridge for best results.

  4. Top and Serve: Whip together heavy cream and sugar for the topping. Spread over the soaked cake and top with fresh berries or fruit. Slice and enjoy this creamy delight!

3. Churros: A Cinnamon-Sugar Treat

Churros are a beloved Mexican snack that can be enjoyed any time of day. These deep-fried dough sticks are often served with a side of chocolate sauce for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • For the churros:

    • 1 cup water
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs
    • Oil for frying

  • For the coating:

    • ½ cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and vegetable oil. Bring to a boil and then remove from heat. Stir in flour until well combined. Allow to cool slightly before mixing in eggs one at a time until smooth.

  2. Heat the Oil: In a deep pot, heat oil to 375°F (190°C).

  3. Pipe the Churros: Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip. Pipe out strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting them to your desired length. Fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  4. Coat and Serve: Remove churros from oil and drain on paper towels. In a bowl, mix sugar and cinnamon, then roll warm churros in the mixture. Serve with a rich chocolate dipping sauce for an extra treat!

4. Arroz con Leche: Creamy Rice Pudding

Arroz con leche, or rice pudding, is a comforting dessert that combines rice, milk, and sugar with a hint of cinnamon. This dish is not only delicious but also evokes a sense of nostalgia for many.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Ground cinnamon for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the rice under cold water. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the rice. Cook until the rice is tender, about 15-20 minutes, then drain.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In the same pot, combine the cooked rice, evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, whole milk, and cinnamon stick. Simmer over low heat for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Serve: Remove the cinnamon stick and let the pudding cool. Serve warm or chilled, sprinkled with ground cinnamon on top.

5. Buñuelos: Crispy Fried Pastries

Buñuelos are crisp, fried pastries often enjoyed during the holiday season. They can be served plain or with a drizzle of syrup, making them a versatile treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • Oil for frying
  • Cinnamon sugar for coating (1 cup sugar + 2 tablespoons cinnamon)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Add melted butter, egg, and milk, mixing until a soft dough forms. Knead briefly and let rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Roll and Cut: Divide the dough into small balls and roll each out into thin circles.

  3. Fry the Buñuelos: Heat oil in a deep skillet to 375°F (190°C). Fry the dough circles until golden brown on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  4. Coat and Serve: Dust the buñuelos with cinnamon sugar while still warm. Serve with a side of honey or syrup for added sweetness.

6. Cajeta: Goat’s Milk Caramel

Cajeta is a traditional Mexican caramel sauce made from goat’s milk. Its rich, complex flavor makes it a delightful addition to many desserts or enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups goat’s milk
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large pot, combine goat’s milk, sugar, baking soda, and vanilla bean (split and scraped). If using a cinnamon stick, add it now.

  2. Cook the Mixture: Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring constantly. Reduce heat and simmer for about 1-2 hours, continuing to stir, until the mixture thickens and turns a caramel color.

  3. Cool and Store: Remove from heat and let cool. Store in sterilized jars in the refrigerator. Use it as a topping for ice cream, pancakes, or even as a filling for pastries.

7. Pastel de Tres Leches: The Ultimate Celebration Cake

Though we touched on tres leches cake earlier, it’s worth noting variations, such as pastel de tres leches, which is often decorated with colorful fruits and whipped cream, making it a showstopper at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • Same ingredients as the standard tres leches cake recipe, but with additional fruits like strawberries, kiwi, and mango for decoration.

Instructions:

Follow the same preparation steps as the original tres leches cake. Once topped with whipped cream, create a beautiful fruit arrangement on the cake, making it visually stunning for parties.

8. Gelatina: A Colorful Treat

Gelatina, or gelatin dessert, is a ubiquitous treat in Mexico, often served in vibrant colors and festive shapes, especially during celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fruit juice (like orange or raspberry)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 packet (0.25 ounces) unflavored gelatin
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • Fresh fruits for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Dissolve Gelatin: In a small bowl, sprinkle gelatin over cold water and let it bloom for about 5 minutes.

  2. Heat the Juice: In a saucepan, heat the fruit juice and sugar until it’s warm, but not boiling. Stir in the bloomed gelatin until completely dissolved.

  3. Set the Gelatina: Pour the mixture into molds and refrigerate until set, about 4 hours. Unmold and garnish with fresh fruit before serving.

Conclusion: Savor the Sweetness of Mexican Desserts

Mexican desserts are a delightful blend of flavors, textures, and traditions. Each recipe tells a story, whether it’s the rich history of flan or the festive nature of buñuelos. By trying these recipes at home, you not only enjoy the deliciousness but also connect with the cultural heritage of Mexico. So gather your ingredients, invite friends and family, and indulge in the sweet, comforting world of Mexican desserts. Happy baking!
