How to Make Classic American Tortilla Soup: Recipe


Tortilla soup is a comforting and flavorful dish that embodies the essence of American-Mexican cuisine. Its roots can be traced to various traditional Mexican recipes, but the American version has transformed into a beloved staple, celebrated for its rich flavors and versatility. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making classic American tortilla soup, from choosing the right ingredients to serving it beautifully. So, grab your apron, and let’s dive into this delicious culinary adventure!

Ingredients

To create a classic American tortilla soup, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Soup Base:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, diced (adjust for spice preference)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (with juices)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup cooked shredded chicken (optional for a heartier soup)

For the Tortilla Strips:

  • 4 corn tortillas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt, to taste

Toppings (optional but recommended):

  • Avocado, diced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Sliced radishes

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Tortilla Strips

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cut the Tortillas: Stack the corn tortillas, and cut them into thin strips (about 1/2-inch wide).

  3. Coat with Oil: In a bowl, toss the tortilla strips with vegetable oil and a pinch of salt until evenly coated.

  4. Bake: Spread the strips on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake for about 10-15 minutes or until golden and crispy, flipping halfway through. Keep an eye on them to avoid burning.

Step 2: Make the Soup Base

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Next, add the minced garlic and diced jalapeño, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  2. Add Bell Pepper: Stir in the diced red bell pepper and cook for another 3-4 minutes until softened.

  3. Incorporate Tomatoes and Spices: Add the can of diced tomatoes (with juices), ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir and let it cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Add Broth and Corn: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and add the corn kernels. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.

  5. Add Beans and Chicken: If using, stir in the black beans and shredded chicken. Let the soup simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow all the flavors to develop.

Step 3: Adjust Seasoning

Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer more heat, consider adding a dash of hot sauce or more jalapeño. If the soup is too thick, you can add additional broth or water to achieve your desired consistency.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Ladle into Bowls: Once the soup is ready, ladle it into bowls.

  2. Top Your Soup: Garnish each bowl with crispy tortilla strips and your choice of toppings. A squeeze of fresh lime juice adds a bright note that enhances the flavors beautifully.

  3. Enjoy: Serve hot and enjoy this hearty, flavorful classic American tortilla soup. It’s perfect for a cozy dinner or a gathering with friends and family.

Variations

The beauty of tortilla soup lies in its versatility. Feel free to customize the recipe according to your preferences or dietary restrictions:

  • Vegetarian Option: Omit the chicken and use vegetable broth. You can also add more vegetables such as zucchini or spinach for extra nutrition.
  • Spicy Kick: If you love heat, consider adding diced chipotle peppers in adobo sauce or a sprinkle of cayenne pepper.
  • Different Proteins: Swap out the chicken for shrimp or even tofu for a different protein source.
  • Flavor Boost: For a smoky flavor, try adding a few drops of liquid smoke or smoked paprika.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers, tortilla soup stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it on the stove over medium heat until heated through. You may need to add a splash of broth or water as the soup may thicken when sitting.

For longer storage, consider freezing the soup. Let it cool completely, then transfer it to an airtight container or freezer bag. It can be frozen for up to 3 months. When you’re ready to enjoy, thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and reheat on the stove.

Conclusion

Classic American tortilla soup is a delightful dish that brings warmth and comfort to any meal. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner, a gathering with friends, or simply for yourself, this recipe is sure to impress. With its rich flavors, customizable ingredients, and beautiful toppings, tortilla soup captures the essence of home cooking.

So, gather your ingredients, follow this step-by-step guide, and enjoy the delicious journey of creating a bowl of classic American tortilla soup. Happy cooking!
