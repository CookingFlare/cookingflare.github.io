Five Easy Springtime Vegan Soups


As the chill of winter melts away and the world bursts into fresh blooms and vibrant greens, your kitchen deserves a seasonal refresh too. Spring is a celebration of renewal, growth, and lighter, fresher flavors. What better way to welcome the season than with a selection of vegan soups that highlight crisp vegetables, delicate herbs, and the wholesome essence of plant-based ingredients? Warm but not heavy, nourishing yet bright, these five easy springtime vegan soups will bring color, comfort, and excitement to your meal plan.

1. Spring Pea and Mint Soup

When you think of spring, sweet peas and fresh mint often top the list of seasonal delights. This soup captures the essence of springtime freshness with just a few simple ingredients that come together in silky harmony.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen green peas
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves, loosely packed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Coconut cream or homemade cashew cream (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, cooking until soft and translucent—about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the peas and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook gently for 10 minutes to meld the flavors.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and stir in fresh mint leaves and lemon juice.
  4. Using an immersion blender or countertop blender, puree until smooth and creamy. If desired, swirl in a bit of coconut or cashew cream to add richness and depth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Serve warm with a fresh sprig of mint.

Why this soup?
The natural sweetness of peas combined with the bright cooling effect of mint creates a soup that feels like spring in a bowl. It’s refreshing, light but satisfying, perfect as a starter or a light lunch.

2. Asparagus and Leek Soup with Thyme

Asparagus is one of those quintessential spring vegetables, tender and slightly grassy in flavor, pairing beautifully with the subtle sweetness of leeks and the earthiness of thyme.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 medium leeks, white and light green parts only, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegan butter
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Juice of half a lemon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil or vegan butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add leeks and cook gently until soft, around 8 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and thyme, stirring until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Stir in asparagus and vegetable broth, bringing to a simmer. Cook for 12-15 minutes, until asparagus is tender.
  4. Blend the soup until smooth. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and lemon juice to taste.
  5. Serve garnished with fresh thyme sprigs or a drizzle of olive oil.

Why this soup?
Leeks and asparagus provide the perfect synergy of gentle sweetness and fresh vegetal crunch, while thyme lends an aromatic depth that rounds out the dish. It’s great served hot or even chilled for a refreshing springtime appetizer.

3. Carrot and Ginger Soup

Bright orange carrots bursting with natural sweetness meet the spicy zing of fresh ginger in this comforting yet invigorating vegan soup. It’s a flavor combo that feels both grounding and uplifting—ideal for unpredictable spring weather.

Ingredients

  • 6 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Coconut milk or soy yogurt, for garnish (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot. Add onions and cook until softened.
  2. Stir in ginger and cumin; cook for another minute to release aromas.
  3. Add chopped carrots and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until carrots are tender, about 20 minutes.
  4. Blend soup until smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with a swirl of coconut milk or soy yogurt and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.

Why this soup?
Ginger and carrots are a classic pairing known for their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits. This soup offers a spicy warmth balanced by the sweetness of carrot — perfect to re-energize after a long day.

4. Spring Vegetable Minestrone

This vibrant, vegetable-packed minestrone celebrates the abundance of spring’s first harvests. Think peas, sugar snap peas, baby spinach, fresh tomatoes, and tender green beans spiraling together in a fragrant, tomato-based broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 1 large ripe tomato, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup small pasta (such as ditalini or elbow macaroni)
  • A handful fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Optional: nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor boost

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add zucchini and green beans; cook for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in diced tomato, peas, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil.
  4. Add the pasta and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes.
  5. Toss in baby spinach and fresh basil leaves in the last minute of cooking.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and sprinkle nutritional yeast if desired.

Why this soup?
A wonderful clear broth soup that’s brimming with vibrant veggies and noodles, this minestrone is a translation of spring’s freshness into a hearty bowl. It’s nourishing without being heavy, satisfying without weighing you down.

5. Chilled Cucumber and Avocado Soup

When the sun starts to feel warmer, sometimes a cold soup is just the ticket. This creamy, green-hued soup combines cucumber’s cooling effect with the buttery texture of avocado for a silky, refreshing dish that really shines in the spring.

Ingredients

  • 2 large cucumbers, peeled and chopped
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk or other plant milk
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill or basil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: a pinch of cayenne for subtle heat

Instructions

  1. Place cucumber, avocado, plant milk, garlic, herbs, and lemon juice in a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and cayenne if using.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.
  5. Serve cold with a sprig of fresh herb or a thin cucumber slice for garnish.

Why this soup?
Light and vibrant, this chilled soup is like a spa day in a bowl. Its creamy texture defies its light calorie profile, making it perfect for spring lunches or as a refreshing starter for a warm-weather meal.

Final Thoughts

Spring is a season of awakening, and each of these vegan soups captures that spirit in its own unique way. From soups to warm your hands and heart to chilled versions that cool and rejuvenate, they make excellent additions to any springtime menu. By focusing on fresh, seasonal ingredients and straightforward preparations, these recipes highlight the natural, unpretentious beauty of plant-based eating.

Experiment with herbs, spice levels, and texture to match your taste buds and celebrate the arrival of spring with a bowl of goodness!

Happy cooking and happy spring!
