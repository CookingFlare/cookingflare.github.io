How to Make Thai Panang Curry with Beef


If you’ve ever tasted Thai Panang curry, you know it’s an exquisite blend of creamy coconut milk, fragrant spices, and tender meat that leaves a lasting impression on the palate. Among the various versions of Thai curry, Panang stands out with its rich, slightly nutty flavor and its mellow heat level, making it accessible even to those who shy away from spicier dishes. This recipe focuses on Panang curry with beef, a classic combination that delivers a satisfying, hearty experience with every bite.

In this detailed guide, you’ll learn how to make authentic Thai Panang curry at home, from selecting ingredients to preparing the curry paste, cooking the beef just right, and balancing flavors perfectly. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a curious foodie looking to expand your culinary repertoire, this post will help you master this iconic dish with confidence.

Understanding Panang Curry: What Makes It Unique?

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s take a moment to understand what sets Panang curry apart from other Thai curries such as red or green curry.

Panang curry, also known as Phanaeng curry, originates from central Thailand. It is characterized by:

  • Thicker consistency: The curry sauce is thicker than typical Thai curry soups.
  • Sweet and nutty flavor: The use of ground peanuts or peanut butter adds a unique creaminess and subtle nuttiness.
  • Milder spice level: While still spicy, Panang’s heat is balanced with sweetness and richness.
  • Aromatics: Kaffir lime leaves lend a citrusy aroma and distinct flavor that is a signature element of Panang curry.

The combination of these components creates a harmonious dish that showcases the complexity of Thai cuisine.

Essential Ingredients for Beef Panang Curry

Achieving an authentic taste hinges on using the right ingredients. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

For the Curry Paste (Can be store-bought or homemade)

  • Dried red chilies (de-seeded for milder heat)
  • Galangal (or ginger as a substitute)
  • Lemongrass stalk
  • Shallots
  • Garlic cloves
  • Coriander root (or stems)
  • Cumin seeds
  • Coriander seeds
  • White peppercorns
  • Shrimp paste (for umami depth)
  • Salt

For the Curry

  • Beef (preferably tender cuts like sirloin or ribeye), thinly sliced
  • Coconut milk (full fat for creaminess)
  • Palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • Fish sauce (for salty, savory depth)
  • Kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • Roasted peanuts or smooth peanut butter
  • Cooking oil (neutral oil like vegetable or canola)
  • Fresh Thai basil (optional, as garnish)
  • Rice (steamed jasmine rice is ideal)

Preparing Your Ingredients

Beef Preparation

The key here is to select beef cuts that remain tender when simmered. Sirloin or ribeye are ideal because they have a good balance of tenderness and flavor. Cut the beef into thin strips against the grain to maximize tenderness.

Kaffir Lime Leaves

Use fresh kaffir lime leaves if possible, as their aroma is fresh and vibrant. If fresh leaves aren’t available, frozen or dried leaves work as alternatives, but add them earlier in cooking to release flavor.

Making the Curry Paste

While you can use store-bought Panang curry paste for convenience, making your own paste from scratch dramatically elevates the dish.

  1. Soak dried chilies in warm water until soft.
  2. Toast cumin and coriander seeds lightly to enhance aroma.
  3. Pound or blend (using a mortar and pestle or food processor) chilies, toasted seeds, garlic, shallots, galangal, lemongrass, coriander root, shrimp paste, and white peppercorns until you get a smooth paste.
  4. Adjust salt to taste.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Here’s how you bring all elements together to create the rich Panang curry:

1. Heat the Pan and Prepare the Base

In a wok or large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil over medium heat. Add 2-3 tablespoons of your homemade or store-bought Panang curry paste. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, ensuring the spices release their oils.

2. Add Coconut Milk Gradually

Pour in about 1 cup of coconut milk in two stages: add half and stir until it’s well incorporated with the curry paste, forming a thick oily base. Then add the remaining coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer. The slow addition helps the curry paste meld perfectly with the creamy coconut milk.

3. Incorporate Beef and Simmer

Add the thinly sliced beef to the pan. Stir well to coat each piece with the curry sauce. Lower the heat and let the beef simmer gently for 10-15 minutes or until tender. Avoid boiling vigorously to keep the coconut milk from splitting.

4. Flavor Balancing

Add 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce for umami and saltiness. Stir in 1 tablespoon palm sugar to bring a hint of sweetness. Taste and adjust: you want a balance of sweet, salty, spicy, and creamy.

5. Add Peanuts and Kaffir Lime Leaves

If you’re using smooth peanut butter, stir in 1-2 tablespoons now to thicken and deepen the sauce. Alternatively, add finely ground roasted peanuts that you can sprinkle on top before serving. Add the shredded kaffir lime leaves and cook for another minute for the aromatic lift.

Serving Your Thai Panang Beef Curry

For an authentic meal experience, serve the curry hot alongside a mound of steamed jasmine rice. The rice helps absorb the flavorful sauce perfectly.

Garnish with fresh Thai basil or additional kaffir lime leaves for brightness. For those who love a bit more heat, fresh sliced red chilies on the side will add a nice contrast.

Tips for Perfect Panang Curry Every Time

  • Use full-fat coconut milk: It gives the curry a rich texture and prevents it from becoming watery.
  • Don’t rush the curry paste frying: Letting the spices bloom in oil releases their full potential.
  • Simmer gently: Avoid high heat once the coconut milk is added to keep the sauce smooth.
  • Balance flavors gradually: Add fish sauce and sugar a little at a time, tasting frequently, as the flavors intensify as the curry cooks.
  • Rest before serving: Allow the curry to sit for 5-10 minutes off heat to meld flavors.

Variations to Explore

  • Protein swaps: Try chicken, pork, or tofu instead of beef for a different feel.
  • Vegetables: Add bell peppers, bamboo shoots, or green beans to incorporate texture and color.
  • Heat level: Adjust chili quantity in the paste to suit your spice tolerance.
  • Vegan version: Replace beef with mushrooms or seitan, and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce.

Final Thoughts

Making Thai Panang curry with beef at home is an immensely rewarding cooking experience. It allows you to dive deep into Thai flavors and tailor the dish to your taste preferences. This recipe not only offers a step-by-step pathway to an authentic meal but also encourages you to adapt and experiment with variations.

So next time you crave something creamy, spicy, and aromatic, give this Panang curry recipe a go. With rich coconut milk, tender beef, and the unmistakable perfume of kaffir lime, you’ll be transported to the bustling street food stalls of Thailand right from your kitchen.

Enjoy your culinary journey!
