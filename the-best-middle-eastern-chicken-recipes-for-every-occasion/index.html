The Best Middle Eastern Chicken Recipes for Every Occasion


Chicken is a versatile and universally beloved ingredient. When paired with the fragrant spices, fresh herbs, and vibrant flavors of the Middle East, it transforms into a true culinary masterpiece. From weeknight dinners to celebratory feasts, Middle Eastern chicken recipes have something for every occasion. Here’s a curated selection of the best dishes to add depth and excitement to your cooking repertoire.

1. Shawarma-Spiced Grilled Chicken

Shawarma is a cornerstone of Middle Eastern cuisine, with its rich marinade and perfectly charred exterior. This shawarma-inspired grilled chicken brings the essence of the traditional street food into your home with a simple yet flavor-packed marinade.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs or breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, and all the spices to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the bowl and coat them thoroughly with the mixture. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes per side or until fully cooked and slightly charred.
  4. Serve with warm pita bread, hummus, and a drizzle of tahini sauce for a complete Mediterranean-inspired meal.

2. Fesenjan-Inspired Chicken with Pomegranate and Walnuts

Hailing from Persian culinary traditions, Fesenjan is a rich and luxurious stew with the perfect balance of sweet and savory. This simplified chicken version channels the essence of this dish without hours of slow cooking.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 1/2 cup ground walnuts
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh pomegranate arils and chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper. Heat a large skillet over medium heat, searing the chicken skin-side down until golden, about 5-6 minutes. Flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the onion and sauté until softened and golden. Stir in the ground walnuts, cinnamon, pomegranate molasses, honey (if using), and chicken stock.
  3. Return the chicken to the skillet, cover, and simmer on low heat for 25-30 minutes, until the chicken is tender and the sauce is thickened.
  4. Garnish with fresh pomegranate arils and parsley before serving. This dish pairs beautifully with steamed saffron rice or naan.

3. Chicken Tagine with Preserved Lemons and Olives

Moroccan tagines are a celebration of bold flavors and fragrant spices, cooked slowly in a unique clay vessel. This version uses chicken, preserved lemons, and green olives for a bright yet earthy flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • 1 preserved lemon, quartered (or substitute with fresh lemon zest + juice)
  • 1 cup green olives
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock or water
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot or tagine, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sear the chicken pieces until lightly browned, then remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté the onions and garlic in the same pot until fragrant. Stir in ginger, turmeric, paprika, and the cinnamon stick.
  3. Return the chicken to the pot, adding the preserved lemon slices, olives, and chicken stock. Cover and let it simmer on low heat for 40-45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro and parsley before serving. Pair with buttery couscous or crusty bread to soak up the flavorful sauce.

4. Sumac-Rubbed Roast Chicken

Sumac, with its tart and citrusy undertones, adds incredible brightness to roast chicken. This simple recipe is perfect for a relaxed family dinner or a weekend gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons ground sumac
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, sumac, garlic powder, lemon zest, salt, and pepper to form a rub.
  3. Gently loosen the skin of the chicken and rub the mixture under the skin and all over the exterior.
  4. Roast the chicken in a baking dish for 1 hour, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Let it rest for 10 minutes before carving.
  5. Serve this bright roast chicken alongside roasted vegetables or a fresh fattoush salad.

5. Middle Eastern Chicken and Rice One-Pot Dish

Busy weeknights call for minimal effort with maximum flavor, and this one-pot chicken and rice dish delivers both. Fragrant spices, tender chicken, and perfectly cooked rice make it an all-around crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • 1 cup long-grain basmati rice, rinsed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley and toasted almonds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. In a large pot, heat some oil and sear the chicken on both sides until golden. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add onions and garlic to the pot, sautéing until soft and fragrant. Stir in the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cinnamon.
  3. Add the rice and toast for 1-2 minutes before pouring in the chicken stock. Nestle the chicken back into the pot.
  4. Cover and cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes until the rice is cooked and the chicken is tender.
  5. Garnish with parsley and toasted almonds before serving.

Final Thoughts

Middle Eastern chicken recipes are brimming with bold flavors, exotic spices, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re craving the smoky goodness of grilled shawarma or the comforting richness of a tagine, these dishes effortlessly bring a slice of the Middle East to your table. Perfect for family dinners, special celebrations, or even meal prepping, these recipes highlight the diversity and depth of this beloved cuisine.

There’s no limit to the creative ways you can incorporate Middle Eastern flavors into your cooking. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the spices, and let your kitchen be filled with the enticing aroma of these timeless recipes.
