Thai Iced Tea: A Cool and Creamy Beverage


Thai iced tea, also known as cha yen, is a refreshing and creamy beverage that originated in Thailand. This delightful drink is made with black tea, sweetened condensed milk, and various aromatic spices. The combination of these ingredients creates a unique flavor profile that is both sweet and slightly spicy, making it the perfect drink to cool down on a hot summer day. In this blog post, we will explore the traditional method of preparing Thai iced tea and provide some tips to enhance your experience.

Ingredients

To make Thai iced tea, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 cups of water
  • 4 bags of black tea
  • 1 cup of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon of ground star anise
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

  1. Begin by boiling 4 cups of water in a saucepan. Once the water is boiling, add the black tea bags and let them steep for about 5 minutes, or until the tea is strong and flavorful.

  2. While the tea is steeping, prepare the sweetened condensed milk mixture. In a separate bowl, combine the sweetened condensed milk, ground star anise, ground cardamom, and vanilla extract. Mix well to ensure that all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  3. After the tea has steeped, remove the tea bags and discard them. Let the tea cool for a few minutes before transferring it to a pitcher or jug.

  4. Once the tea has cooled, add the sweetened condensed milk mixture to the pitcher. Stir well to combine the tea and milk, ensuring that the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  5. Fill a glass with ice cubes and pour the Thai iced tea over the ice. The coldness of the ice will help to chill the tea and create a refreshing beverage.

  6. Give the drink a gentle stir to mix the flavors and enjoy your homemade Thai iced tea!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of flavor, you can add a splash of almond or coconut milk to your Thai iced tea. This will give it a creamy and nutty taste that complements the spices perfectly.

  • If you prefer a stronger tea flavor, you can use more tea bags or let the tea steep for a longer period. Adjust the steeping time according to your personal preference.

  • To make a vegan version of Thai iced tea, you can substitute the sweetened condensed milk with coconut milk or any other non-dairy milk of your choice. The result will be just as creamy and delicious.

  • If you want to add a twist to your Thai iced tea, you can experiment with different spices and flavors. Try adding a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for a warm and cozy taste, or a dash of lime juice for a tangy kick.

Conclusion

Thai iced tea is a delightful and refreshing beverage that combines the flavors of black tea, sweetened condensed milk, and aromatic spices. With its creamy texture and unique taste, it’s the perfect drink to enjoy on a hot summer day or whenever you’re in need of a cool and satisfying treat. By following the simple instructions and experimenting with different variations, you can create your own signature Thai iced tea that will impress your friends and family. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the flavors of Thailand in every sip!
