Spanish Cooking for Beginners: 10 Easy Recipes to Start


Spanish cuisine is a vibrant and diverse tapestry of flavors, influenced by the Mediterranean climate, regional produce, and centuries of cultural exchange. From the sun-drenched plains of Andalusia to the green hills of Galicia, Spanish food offers something for everyone, whether you’re craving hearty stews, fresh seafood, or simple yet delicious tapas. If you’re a beginner in the kitchen and want to start exploring the rich flavors of Spain, you’re in the right place.

In this post, we’ll explore 10 easy Spanish recipes that are perfect for beginners. These dishes are approachable, quick to prepare, and will introduce you to the foundational ingredients and techniques of Spanish cooking. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into the world of Spanish food!

1. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

Tortilla Española, also known as a Spanish omelette, is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Spain. Made with just a few simple ingredients — eggs, potatoes, onions, and olive oil — it’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. The key to making a great tortilla is getting the texture just right: golden on the outside and soft and creamy on the inside.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the potatoes and onions, cooking them slowly until they are soft but not crispy, about 15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a bowl, whisk the eggs and season with a pinch of salt.
  4. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, drain any excess oil and add them to the eggs. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Pour the egg mixture back into the skillet and cook on low heat, allowing the edges to set. Once the edges are firm, gently flip the tortilla using a plate and cook the other side for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Let the tortilla cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Enjoy this dish warm or at room temperature with a glass of Spanish wine!

2. Gazpacho

Gazpacho is a refreshing Spanish cold soup that is perfect for hot summer days. Originating from Andalusia, it’s made with fresh, ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, and a touch of garlic. It’s light, nutritious, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, red pepper, onion, and garlic.
  2. Add the olive oil, sherry vinegar, salt, and pepper, then blend until smooth.
  3. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  4. Chill the gazpacho in the fridge for at least 2 hours before serving.
  5. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve cold.

This is an excellent dish for making ahead, and it’s incredibly refreshing on a hot day.

3. Paella (Seafood Version)

Paella is perhaps the most famous Spanish dish internationally, and it comes in many variations. This seafood version is a beginner-friendly take on the traditional paella, combining succulent seafood with the rich flavors of saffron and tomatoes. Paella is a perfect dish to make for a group, and it’s easier than you might think!

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice (or any short-grain rice)
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound mussels, cleaned
  • 1/2 pound squid, sliced into rings
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lemon wedges and parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan (or wide skillet) over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and red bell pepper, sautéing until softened.
  3. Stir in the rice and cook for 2 minutes, allowing it to toast slightly.
  4. Add the broth, white wine, and saffron. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  5. Arrange the shrimp, mussels, and squid on top of the rice. Cover and let cook for about 15 minutes, until the seafood is cooked through and the rice is tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste and garnish with fresh lemon wedges and parsley.

Paella is best enjoyed fresh and hot from the pan, with a glass of chilled Spanish white wine.

4. Pan con Tomate (Tomato Bread)

Pan con Tomate is a simple yet delicious Spanish snack or appetizer. It’s often served as tapas in Spain, and all you need is rustic bread, ripe tomatoes, and a drizzle of olive oil.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of rustic bread (like baguette or country bread)
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toast the slices of bread until they are golden and crispy.
  2. Rub each slice with the cut side of a garlic clove.
  3. Rub the halved tomatoes over the bread, letting the pulp fall onto the bread while discarding the skin.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt.

Enjoy as a snack or serve with other tapas!

5. Patatas Bravas

Patatas Bravas are crispy fried potatoes served with a spicy tomato sauce. They are a popular tapa in Spain, often enjoyed with a cold beer or wine. This recipe is quick, simple, and sure to be a hit at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the potato cubes and fry them until golden and crispy, about 10-12 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a small saucepan, heat the tomato sauce, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, and a pinch of salt over low heat for 5 minutes.
  4. Drizzle the spicy tomato sauce over the fried potatoes and serve immediately.

These crispy, spicy potatoes make a perfect appetizer or side dish.

6. Croquetas de Jamón (Ham Croquettes)

Croquetas are one of Spain’s most beloved tapas, and they come in many varieties. These ham croquettes are a great introduction to making croquetas at home. Crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside, they are irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup serrano ham, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and sauté the onion until soft.
  2. Add the chopped ham and cook for another 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the butter and flour, cooking for 2-3 minutes to make a roux.
  4. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens, about 5 minutes.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg, then remove from heat.
  6. Let the mixture cool in the fridge for at least 1 hour.
  7. Once cooled, shape the mixture into small croquettes and dip them in beaten egg, then roll in breadcrumbs.
  8. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the croquettes until golden brown.

Serve these delicious croquettes as part of your tapas spread!

7. Churros

Churros are a Spanish treat that’s perfect for breakfast or dessert. Fried to crispy perfection and coated in sugar, they’re often served with a rich chocolate dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Oil for frying
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for coating)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water, sugar, salt, and butter to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, stir in the flour and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool for a few minutes before adding the egg and vanilla. Stir until smooth.
  4. Heat oil in a large pan for frying.
  5. Spoon the dough into a piping bag fitted with a star-shaped tip.
  6. Pipe the dough into the hot oil, frying until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Drain the churros on paper towels and coat with the sugar and cinnamon mixture.

Serve with a warm cup of chocolate sauce for dipping.

8. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-style Octopus)

Pulpo a la Gallega is a traditional dish from Galicia in northern Spain, where octopus is cooked to perfection and seasoned with paprika and olive oil. It’s a simple yet flavorful dish that lets the octopus shine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound octopus
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the octopus in a large pot of salted water for about 45 minutes or until tender.
  2. Slice the octopus into bite-sized pieces and place on a serving dish.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with smoked paprika, and season with salt.
  4. Garnish with lemon wedges and serve.

This dish is ideal for seafood lovers and is sure to impress.

9. Empanadas

Empanadas are Spanish turnovers filled with a variety of ingredients, from meats and vegetables to cheese. These savory pastries are easy to make and are perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of empanada dough (or make your own)
  • 1/2 pound ground beef or chicken
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olives, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the ground meat with the onion until browned. Season with cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir in the raisins and olives, then remove from heat.
  4. Roll out the empanada dough and cut into circles.
  5. Spoon a small amount of the meat mixture onto each circle, fold the dough over, and crimp the edges.
  6. Brush with the beaten egg and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden.

10. Pisto (Spanish Ratatouille)

Pisto is a Spanish vegetable stew that’s often compared to the French ratatouille. It’s made with tomatoes, zucchini, eggplant, and peppers, making it a perfect dish for vegetarians.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing until softened.
  3. Stir in the zucchini, eggplant, and tomatoes. Cook until the vegetables are tender and the flavors meld together, about 15 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.

This simple vegetable stew can be served as a side dish or over rice for a light, healthy meal.

Conclusion

Spanish cooking is all about fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and simple techniques. By starting with these beginner-friendly recipes, you’ll gain confidence in the kitchen while exploring the delicious diversity of Spanish cuisine. Whether you’re preparing a classic tortilla, savoring a bowl of gazpacho, or indulging in churros for dessert, these dishes are a perfect way to bring a taste of Spain into your home. Happy cooking!
