Easy Nut-Free Guacamole Recipe for Chip Dipping


Guacamole is a beloved Mexican dip that is not only delicious but also packed with healthy fats and nutrients. However, for individuals with nut allergies, finding a nut-free guacamole recipe can be challenging. That’s why I’m here to share with you an easy and nut-free guacamole recipe that will satisfy your taste buds and keep you safe from any nut-related allergies.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 medium-sized tomato, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds and membranes removed, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Slice the avocados in half and remove the pits. Scoop out the flesh into a medium-sized bowl.

  2. Using a fork, mash the avocados until you reach your desired consistency. Some people like chunky guacamole, while others prefer it smoother.

  3. Add the diced tomato, chopped red onion, finely chopped jalapeno pepper, minced garlic clove, lime juice, and chopped cilantro to the mashed avocados.

  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to start with a small amount of salt and adjust according to your preference.

  5. Mix all the ingredients together until well combined. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

  6. Transfer the guacamole to a serving bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, making sure it touches the surface of the guacamole to prevent browning. Alternatively, you can squeeze some extra lime juice on top to help preserve the vibrant green color.

  7. Allow the guacamole to sit in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  8. Once chilled, give the guacamole a final stir before serving. Serve with your favorite tortilla chips or vegetable sticks for a delicious and nut-free dipping experience.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier guacamole, you can leave some of the jalapeno pepper seeds or membranes intact. Adjust the amount of jalapeno according to your heat tolerance.

  • For added texture and flavor, you can add diced red bell pepper, corn kernels, or even a handful of pomegranate seeds to the guacamole.

  • To make your guacamole extra creamy, you can add a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt.

  • If you’re not a fan of cilantro, you can substitute it with fresh parsley or omit it altogether.

  • To store any leftover guacamole, transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate. Consume within 2-3 days for the best taste and quality.

Final Thoughts

With this easy nut-free guacamole recipe, you can enjoy a creamy and flavorful dip without worrying about any nut allergies. The combination of ripe avocados, fresh vegetables, and zesty lime juice creates a tantalizing blend of flavors that will keep you coming back for more. So, grab your favorite tortilla chips and dip away into this delicious and allergen-free guacamole. It’s the perfect crowd-pleasing appetizer or snack for any occasion.
