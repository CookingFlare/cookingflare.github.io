How to Make Brunch Cocktails Ahead of Time: Time-Saving Tips


Brunch is one of those delightful meals that brings together the best of breakfast and lunch, often accompanied by the perfect cocktail to elevate the experience. But when it comes to hosting a brunch, time can be a precious commodity. The good news is that you can prepare your brunch cocktails ahead of time, allowing you to enjoy the occasion with your guests rather than spending the entire time behind the bar. In this post, we’ll explore some creative and delicious brunch cocktail recipes, along with time-saving tips to help you plan and prepare like a pro.

The Brunch Cocktail Essentials

Before diving into the recipes, let’s discuss what makes a great brunch cocktail. Generally, these drinks are refreshing, vibrant, and often feature fruity, herbaceous, or bubbly elements. Here are some key ingredients you might consider incorporating into your cocktails:

  1. Fresh Fruits: Think berries, citrus fruits, and melons. Fresh fruits not only add flavor but also a beautiful visual element to your cocktails.

  2. Herbs: Fresh herbs like mint, basil, and rosemary can add an aromatic flair to your drinks.

  3. Sparkling Wine: Champagne, Prosecco, or other sparkling wines can lend a festive touch.

  4. Juices: Freshly squeezed juices (like orange, grapefruit, or cranberry) work wonders in cocktails.

  5. Liqueurs: From fruity liqueurs to herbal ones, these can enhance flavor complexity.

  6. Syrups: Simple syrup, flavored syrups, or even homemade fruit purées can add sweetness and depth.

  7. Bitters: A dash of bitters can elevate the flavor profile of your cocktails, bringing balance and complexity.

Now that we’ve covered the essentials, let’s jump into some time-saving tips for making brunch cocktails ahead of time.

Time-Saving Tips

1. Pre-Batch Your Cocktails

One of the best ways to save time is by pre-batching your cocktails. This means mixing all the ingredients ahead of time, minus any carbonation or delicate garnishes. Here’s how to do it:

  • Choose Your Recipes Wisely: Select cocktails that can be made in larger quantities. Classics like mimosas, bellinis, or sangrias are perfect for batching.

  • Mix Ingredients: Combine all the ingredients in a large pitcher or container, leaving out any fizzy elements until just before serving. For example, mix your fruit juices, spirits, and syrups, and store them in the fridge.

  • Use Airtight Containers: To keep your cocktails fresh, store them in airtight containers. This way, they’ll retain their flavors and aromas until you’re ready to serve.

2. Prepare Garnishes in Advance

Garnishes play a vital role in making your brunch cocktails visually appealing. Preparing them in advance can save you precious time on the day of your brunch. Here’s how:

  • Fruit Slices: Cut slices of citrus fruits (like lemons, limes, or oranges) and store them in an airtight container in the fridge.

  • Herbs: Wash and dry fresh herbs, then store them in a damp paper towel inside a sealed bag to keep them fresh.

  • Skewers: If you’re using fruit skewers as garnishes, assemble them ahead of time and store them in the refrigerator.

3. Opt for Make-Ahead Mixers

Instead of relying solely on fresh juices, consider making mixers ahead of time. Here are a few ideas:

  • Fruit Purees: Blend fresh fruits into a puree and store them in the fridge. These can be added to cocktails for a burst of flavor.

  • Herbal Syrups: Make simple syrups infused with herbs (like mint or basil) that can be used to sweeten and flavor your cocktails.

  • Citrus Juices: Squeeze your citrus juices a day in advance and store them in airtight containers in the fridge.

4. Keep It Simple with Sparkling Wine

If you’re planning to serve cocktails that require sparkling wine, consider using a “sparkling wine station.” Instead of mixing cocktails individually, allow guests to build their own drinks with pre-mixed juices and sparkling wine. Here’s how:

  • Set Up a Station: Prepare a table with your pre-batched juices, fruit purees, and bottles of sparkling wine.

  • Provide Glassware: Offer a selection of glassware for guests to choose from.

  • Include Garnishes: Set out the garnishes you prepared earlier, allowing guests to customize their drinks.

Delicious Brunch Cocktail Recipes

Now that you have a solid game plan for preparing brunch cocktails ahead of time, let’s explore some delightful recipes to consider.

1. Classic Mimosa

The mimosa is a brunch staple, known for its simplicity and refreshing taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of chilled sparkling wine
  • 2 cups of freshly squeezed orange juice
  • Orange slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pitcher, combine the orange juice with half of the sparkling wine.
  2. Stir gently to combine.
  3. Store the pitcher in the fridge until ready to serve.
  4. Pour into glasses and top with the remaining sparkling wine, garnishing with orange slices.

2. Berry Bellini

This fruity twist on the classic Bellini is perfect for berry lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh mixed berries (raspberries, strawberries, blueberries)
  • 1 cup of peach schnapps
  • 1 bottle of chilled Prosecco
  • Fresh berries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blend the mixed berries into a puree and strain through a sieve to remove seeds.
  2. In a pitcher, combine the berry puree with peach schnapps and stir well.
  3. Store in the fridge until ready to serve.
  4. When serving, fill glasses halfway with the berry mixture and top with Prosecco. Garnish with fresh berries.

3. Sparkling Grapefruit Rosemary Cocktail

This cocktail combines the tartness of grapefruit with the aromatic flavor of rosemary.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of freshly squeezed grapefruit juice
  • 1 cup of rosemary simple syrup
  • 1 bottle of chilled sparkling wine
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. To make the rosemary simple syrup, combine equal parts water and sugar in a saucepan with a handful of rosemary sprigs. Heat until the sugar dissolves, then let it cool and strain.
  2. In a pitcher, combine grapefruit juice and rosemary simple syrup.
  3. Store in the fridge until ready to serve.
  4. When serving, fill glasses halfway with the mixture and top with sparkling wine. Garnish with a rosemary sprig.

4. Tropical Sangria

Bring a taste of the tropics to your brunch with this fruity sangria.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of dry white wine
  • 1 cup of coconut rum
  • 2 cups of pineapple juice
  • 1 cup of diced fresh fruit (pineapple, mango, and strawberries)
  • Club soda for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the white wine, coconut rum, and pineapple juice.
  2. Add the diced fruit and stir to combine.
  3. Store the sangria in the fridge for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. When serving, fill glasses with the sangria and top with a splash of club soda.

5. Spicy Bloody Mary

For those who enjoy a savory option, the Bloody Mary is a classic choice.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of tomato juice
  • 1 cup of vodka
  • 1/4 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • Celery salt and black pepper to taste
  • Celery sticks, lemon wedges, and olives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine tomato juice, vodka, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, celery salt, and black pepper. Stir well.
  2. Store the mixture in the fridge until ready to serve.
  3. When serving, fill glasses with the Bloody Mary mix and garnish with celery sticks, lemon wedges, and olives.

Final Thoughts

Planning a brunch can be a delightful experience, especially when you have delicious cocktails ready to go. By pre-batching your drinks, preparing garnishes, and utilizing make-ahead mixers, you can save time and ensure a stress-free gathering. The recipes provided above are not only flavorful but also allow for customization, ensuring that your brunch cocktails cater to everyone’s taste.

So gather your friends, set the table, and enjoy the ease of entertaining while sipping on refreshing cocktails. Cheers to a fabulous brunch!
