Monster Mash Pancakes: A Playful Halloween Breakfast Recipe


Halloween is just around the corner, and what better way to start the day than with a fun and spooky breakfast? Introducing Monster Mash Pancakes, a delightful treat that will make your little ghouls and goblins jump out of bed with excitement. These pancakes are not only delicious but also perfect for getting into the Halloween spirit. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Green food coloring
  • Candy eyeballs
  • Chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, melted butter, egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  3. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

  4. Add a few drops of green food coloring to the batter and stir until the color is evenly distributed. Adjust the amount of food coloring to achieve your desired shade of monster green.

  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or butter.

  6. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Use the back of a spoon to spread the batter into a circular shape.

  7. Immediately place candy eyeballs onto the pancake to create the monster’s eyes. You can get creative here and make each pancake have a different number of eyes or even use different sizes of candy eyeballs.

  8. Sprinkle a few chocolate chips onto the pancake to resemble the monster’s mouth. You can create different mouth shapes or even add teeth using the chocolate chips.

  9. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown.

  10. Transfer the cooked pancakes to a plate and repeat the process with the remaining batter.

  11. Serve the Monster Mash Pancakes with a drizzle of maple syrup and a sprinkle of powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness.

Tips and Tricks

  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make a homemade version by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 cup of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using in the recipe.

  • To keep the pancakes warm while cooking the rest of the batter, place them on a baking sheet in a preheated oven at 200°F (93°C).

  • Get creative with your monster designs! Use different colors of food coloring, add sprinkles or edible glitter, or even shape the pancakes into different monster shapes using a cookie cutter.

  • Don’t forget to involve your little ones in the pancake decorating process. It’s a great way to get them excited about breakfast and Halloween!

So there you have it, a playful and delicious Halloween breakfast recipe that will make your family’s morning extra special. These Monster Mash Pancakes are sure to become a new Halloween tradition in your home. Enjoy the spooky fun and have a frightfully delightful Halloween!
