Simple Pasta Dishes for College Students


As a college student, time and money are often in short supply, but you still want to eat something delicious and satisfying. One of the easiest and most versatile meals you can make is pasta. Not only is it affordable, but it’s also quick to prepare, and you can experiment with different sauces, ingredients, and seasonings to create a wide range of dishes. Whether you’re new to cooking or just looking for some quick ideas, these simple pasta dishes are perfect for busy students on a budget.

1. Classic Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Aglio e Olio is a traditional Italian pasta dish that requires only a few ingredients and minimal preparation. Despite its simplicity, it’s packed with flavor and comes together in just about 20 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Be sure to reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes, until golden and fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. If you like a little heat, add the red pepper flakes to the pan and cook for another 30 seconds.
  4. Add the drained pasta to the skillet, tossing it in the garlic-infused oil. If the pasta seems dry, add a little reserved pasta water to create a silky sauce.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, garnish with chopped parsley, and sprinkle some grated Parmesan on top if desired.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy this simple yet flavorful dish.

2. Creamy Alfredo Pasta

If you’re craving something rich and creamy, Alfredo pasta is the perfect comfort food. While the traditional recipe calls for heavy cream, you can make a lighter version with ingredients you likely already have in your pantry.

Ingredients:

  • 200g fettuccine
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or milk
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fettuccine in a large pot of salted water according to the package directions. Drain, reserving a little pasta water.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream (or milk for a lighter version), and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Gradually stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy. If the sauce gets too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta water until you reach the desired consistency.
  5. Toss the cooked fettuccine into the sauce, making sure it’s well coated. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve with a sprinkle of fresh parsley and extra Parmesan if desired.

3. Pesto Pasta with Cherry Tomatoes

Pesto is another quick and flavorful sauce that can transform a simple pasta dish into something extraordinary. It’s a great way to use up any fresh basil you have on hand, and it pairs perfectly with pasta and cherry tomatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 200g pasta (penne, fusilli, or spaghetti)
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts or walnuts
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. To make the pesto, blend the basil, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, garlic, and olive oil in a food processor until smooth. If the mixture is too thick, add a bit more olive oil or a splash of water.
  3. Toss the cooked pasta with the pesto until fully coated.
  4. Gently stir in the halved cherry tomatoes for a burst of freshness.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.

4. Garlic Butter Shrimp Pasta

Shrimp pasta is a fantastic option when you want a more protein-packed dish that feels like a treat but doesn’t take long to prepare. The garlic butter sauce is simple, yet it adds a rich, savory flavor that elevates the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 200g spaghetti or linguine
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta in a large pot of salted water according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they turn pink and opaque.
  4. If using, add the red pepper flakes for a touch of heat.
  5. Toss the cooked pasta with the garlic butter shrimp, adding salt and pepper to taste. If the pasta seems dry, add a bit of pasta water to loosen the sauce.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges for an extra zing.

5. Veggie-Loaded Pasta Primavera

Pasta primavera is a vibrant and healthy option, packed with seasonal vegetables. It’s a great way to sneak in more veggies and make a light, flavorful meal that doesn’t take a lot of time to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 200g pasta (penne, rotini, or spaghetti)
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup peas (frozen or fresh)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced zucchini, bell pepper, and peas to the skillet. Sauté for 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta into the skillet, mixing everything together. Add the cherry tomatoes and cook for another 2 minutes until they soften.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and oregano. If desired, drizzle with a little more olive oil for added richness.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil and serve immediately.

6. One-Pot Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese is a classic comfort food that’s loved by everyone. The best part about this recipe is that it’s all made in one pot, making cleanup a breeze. Plus, it’s creamy and cheesy, perfect for a late-night snack or a satisfying dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 200g elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook according to the package instructions.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, melt the butter in a separate pot over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, making a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in the milk and bring it to a simmer. Once the mixture thickens, stir in the cheddar and Parmesan cheese until smooth and creamy.
  4. Drain the macaroni and add it to the cheese sauce, stirring until fully combined.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and serve hot.

7. Simple Carbonara

Carbonara is a rich and satisfying pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta (or bacon), and pasta. It’s a hearty meal that’s perfect for a filling dinner without a lot of effort.

Ingredients:

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted water according to the package directions. Drain, reserving a little pasta water.
  2. While the pasta cooks, sauté the pancetta or bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk the eggs, Parmesan, and black pepper until well combined.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the pancetta, and toss to combine.
  5. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour the egg mixture over the pasta. Stir vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to adjust the consistency.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.

Final Thoughts

Pasta is one of the most versatile foods you can make, and it’s perfect for college students who want something easy and affordable without sacrificing flavor. Whether you’re craving something light and fresh like pesto pasta or a rich and creamy dish like Alfredo, there’s a pasta recipe for every occasion. With these simple pasta dishes, you’ll be able to enjoy homemade meals without spending hours in the kitchen or breaking the bank. So gather your ingredients, get cooking, and enjoy a delicious pasta meal tonight!
