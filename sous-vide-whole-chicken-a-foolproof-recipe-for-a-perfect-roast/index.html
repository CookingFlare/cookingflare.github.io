Sous Vide Whole Chicken: A Foolproof Recipe for a Perfect Roast


When it comes to preparing a delicious roast chicken, there’s no technique quite like sous vide. This method allows you to achieve moist, tender, and flavorful results that are hard to replicate with traditional roasting methods. In this post, we’ll go through the entire process of cooking a whole chicken sous vide, from preparation to finishing touches, ensuring that you end up with a chicken that’s perfectly cooked and ready to impress.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it to a precise temperature in a water bath. This technique allows for even cooking and helps maintain the moisture and flavor of the food. With sous vide, you can cook your chicken to the exact doneness you desire without the risk of overcooking.

Benefits of Sous Vide Cooking for Chicken

  1. Precision: Sous vide allows you to control the cooking temperature down to the degree, ensuring that your chicken is perfectly cooked every time.
  2. Juiciness: Cooking chicken sous vide keeps it juicy and tender, preventing the dryness that often occurs with traditional roasting methods.
  3. Flavor Infusion: The vacuum-sealing process allows marinades and seasonings to penetrate the chicken more effectively, resulting in flavorful meat.
  4. Convenience: You can cook your chicken ahead of time, and finish it with a quick sear on the stove or in the oven just before serving.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried rosemary)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon smoked paprika for added flavor

Equipment Needed

  • Sous vide immersion circulator
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Vacuum sealer and bags (or ziplock bags)
  • Skillet or oven for searing (for finishing)
  • Meat thermometer (optional but recommended)

Step-by-Step Sous Vide Whole Chicken Recipe

Step 1: Preparation

  1. Season the Chicken: Begin by patting the chicken dry with paper towels. This step is crucial for achieving a nice sear later on. Once the chicken is dry, rub it all over with olive oil.

  2. Create the Marinade: In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika (if using). This aromatic mixture will infuse the chicken with delightful flavors.

  3. Apply the Marinade: Rub the marinade all over the chicken, making sure to get under the skin where possible. This ensures that the flavors penetrate deeply into the meat. If you have time, let the chicken marinate for at least an hour or up to overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor.

Step 2: Vacuum Sealing

  1. Bag the Chicken: Place the seasoned chicken into a vacuum-sealable bag or ziplock bag. If using a vacuum sealer, follow the manufacturer’s instructions to seal the bag. If using a ziplock bag, use the water displacement method: slowly submerge the bag in water, allowing the water pressure to push the air out before sealing the bag.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Set Up the Sous Vide Bath: Fill a large pot or container with water and attach the sous vide immersion circulator. Set the temperature to your desired doneness:

    • For tender, perfectly cooked chicken: 145°F (63°C) for 1.5 to 4 hours.
    • For a firmer texture: 150°F (65°C) for 1.5 to 4 hours.
    • For more traditional roast chicken texture: 165°F (74°C) for 1 to 2 hours.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, submerge the vacuum-sealed chicken in the water. Make sure it’s fully submerged and cook for the specified time. The beauty of sous vide is that the chicken won’t overcook, even if you leave it in the water a bit longer.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

  1. Remove the Chicken: After the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Allow the chicken to rest in the bag for about 10 minutes. This resting period helps redistribute the juices.

  2. Sear the Chicken: Preheat a skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully remove the chicken from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. This step is essential for achieving a crisp skin.

  3. Sear the Chicken: Add a little olive oil to the skillet, and once hot, place the chicken in the skillet, breast side down. Sear for about 3-5 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken and sear the other side for another 2-4 minutes.

  4. Optional Broiler Finish: For an even crisper skin, you can transfer the chicken to a preheated oven broiler for 2-3 minutes. Keep a close eye on it to avoid burning.

Step 5: Serving

  1. Carve the Chicken: Once the chicken is nicely browned, transfer it to a cutting board. Let it rest for a few minutes before carving.

  2. Serve and Enjoy: Serve your beautifully cooked sous vide chicken with your favorite sides. The tender meat can be paired with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Storage Tips

If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. You can also freeze the cooked chicken for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply sous vide the chicken again at 140°F (60°C) for about 45 minutes or until heated through.

Conclusion

Sous vide cooking may seem intimidating, but once you master the technique, you’ll find it’s a game-changer for preparing a whole chicken. It guarantees a juicy, flavorful roast that will impress your family and friends. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or a weeknight dinner, this foolproof sous vide whole chicken recipe will ensure you achieve perfect results every time. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your sous vide machine, and enjoy the incredible flavors of a perfectly cooked roast chicken!
