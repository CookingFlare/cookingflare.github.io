Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal) Recipe


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, and one dish that perfectly embodies this is Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry, also known as Samgyeopsal. This dish, filled with a perfect balance of savory, spicy, and smoky flavors, has become increasingly popular outside of Korea. If you’re looking to create an authentic Korean experience right at home, this recipe is a fantastic choice.

Samgyeopsal, typically made with pork belly, is a favorite at Korean BBQ restaurants. While the traditional BBQ version is usually grilled, today we’re going to bring that same wonderful taste to your kitchen with a stir-fry method that brings all the flavors together in one sizzling pan. This recipe is easy to follow, full of vibrant spices, and will leave you craving more.

Ingredients for Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry (Samgyeopsal)

Before diving into the cooking process, it’s important to gather all your ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to make the perfect Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry:

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) pork belly (sliced thinly)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for stir-frying)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (green or red), sliced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions (scallions), chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (for garnish)

Marinade Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce (optional, for an added umami flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Optional for Serving:

  • Lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
  • Steamed rice (for serving)
  • Kimchi (for serving)

The Secrets to a Perfect Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry

1. Choosing the Right Cut of Pork Belly

The key to an unforgettable Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry is, of course, the pork belly. Pork belly is rich, juicy, and tender, with layers of fat that melt beautifully when cooked. This gives the dish its distinct flavor and mouthfeel. While you can find pre-sliced pork belly at Asian grocery stores, it’s best to ask for thinly sliced pieces, which will allow for better caramelization during stir-frying.

2. Making the Flavorful Marinade

The marinade is what elevates this dish. Gochujang, a Korean fermented chili paste, is the star of the marinade. It provides that perfect combination of heat, depth, and umami. Mixing it with soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) gives the pork belly a nice spicy kick balanced by a subtle sweetness.

Adding minced garlic, ginger, and a touch of fish sauce brings in a layer of aromatic flavors, making the pork belly even more irresistible. Allow the pork belly to marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).

3. Stir-Frying the Pork Belly to Perfection

The stir-fry process is straightforward, but timing is key to getting the right texture. First, heat up a bit of vegetable oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. You want to get the pan hot enough to sear the pork belly and lock in the flavors. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork belly slices in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Let the pork belly sear for 2-3 minutes per side, allowing the caramelized bits to form.

Once the pork is nicely browned and crisp on the edges, add onions and bell peppers to the pan. These vegetables will add a subtle sweetness and crunch that contrasts beautifully with the rich pork. Stir everything together and let the vegetables soften for about 3-4 minutes.

Drizzle sesame oil over the stir-fry for a nutty aroma and rich flavor. Garnish the dish with freshly chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added texture and visual appeal.

4. How to Serve Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry

In Korea, Samgyeopsal is often served with a variety of side dishes, but this stir-fry version can be served on its own or with a few accompaniments to elevate the experience. You can serve this Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry with:

  • Lettuce leaves: Simply scoop some of the stir-fry into a lettuce leaf, wrap it up, and eat it like a wrap. The freshness of the lettuce balances the richness of the pork belly perfectly.
  • Steamed rice: A bowl of plain steamed rice is an excellent side to absorb all the spicy sauce and balance the flavors.
  • Kimchi: A traditional Korean side dish made from fermented vegetables, typically cabbage or radish, that adds a tangy, spicy, and slightly sour kick to complement the pork belly stir-fry.

5. How to Make the Dish Spicy

The level of spice in Samgyeopsal can be adjusted based on your preference. If you want a more intense heat, you can increase the amount of gochugaru or gochujang in the marinade. On the other hand, if you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of these ingredients and use a mild chili paste or omit the gochugaru entirely.

6. Variations You Can Try

While the classic Samgyeopsal is made with pork belly, there are several variations you can experiment with depending on your tastes:

  • Beef Version: You can use beef short ribs or bulgogi-style beef as a substitute for pork belly. The marinade and stir-frying method will work wonderfully with beef, giving it a sweet and spicy kick.
  • Vegetarian Version: If you want a vegetarian option, substitute the pork belly with tofu or tempeh. Marinate the tofu in the same way as the pork belly and stir-fry it until golden brown and crispy.
  • Add More Veggies: While this recipe calls for bell peppers and onions, you can add other vegetables like zucchini, mushrooms, or carrots for more variety and color.

7. Cooking Tips

  • Avoid overcrowding the pan: It’s crucial to sear the pork belly slices properly to get that crispy texture. If you add too much meat at once, the pork will steam instead of sear. Cook in batches if needed.
  • Control the heat: The marinade can burn quickly if the heat is too high. Make sure to adjust the heat to medium-high while stir-frying to avoid burning the spices in the marinade.
  • Marinate overnight for deeper flavor: While 30 minutes of marinating will still give you a delicious result, letting the pork belly marinate overnight will allow the flavors to penetrate the meat, making it even more flavorful.

The Experience of Eating Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry

Eating Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry is not just about the food—it’s about the experience. The savory, spicy, and smoky flavors combined with the tender pork belly and crispy vegetables create an unforgettable dining experience. The spicy heat from the gochujang builds slowly, while the sweetness from the honey or brown sugar provides a perfect balance. The final touch of sesame oil adds a fragrant, nutty finish that ties everything together.

Whether you’re enjoying this dish at a Korean BBQ restaurant or cooking it in your own kitchen, the combination of textures, flavors, and the fun of wrapping it in lettuce makes Samgyeopsal a truly unique and satisfying meal.

Korean Spicy Pork Belly Stir-Fry is perfect for family dinners, gatherings with friends, or a special weekend meal. With its rich flavors and easy preparation, this dish will quickly become a favorite in your household. So, grab your ingredients, fire up the stove, and get ready to enjoy the bold, vibrant flavors of Korea right at your dinner table!
