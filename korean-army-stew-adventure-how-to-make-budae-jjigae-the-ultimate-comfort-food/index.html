Korean Army Stew Adventure: How to Make Budae Jjigae, the Ultimate Comfort Food


Korean cuisine has gained immense popularity worldwide, and one dish that stands out for its unique flavors and history is Budae Jjigae, also known as Korean Army Stew. This hearty and comforting dish originated during the Korean War when resources were scarce, and locals had to make do with whatever ingredients they could find. Today, Budae Jjigae has become a beloved comfort food that brings people together. In this blog post, we will embark on a culinary adventure and learn how to make this delicious dish from scratch.

Introduction to Budae Jjigae

Budae Jjigae translates to “Army Stew” in English and is a fusion dish that combines Korean and American flavors. It was created by Korean locals who repurposed surplus food items from American military bases during the Korean War. The result is a spicy and savory soup filled with an array of ingredients such as spam, hot dogs, kimchi, instant noodles, and vegetables.

Ingredients

To make a delicious pot of Budae Jjigae, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of kimchi (aged kimchi works best)
  • 4 cups of chicken stock
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup of sliced carrots
  • 1/2 cup of sliced cabbage
  • 1/2 cup of sliced tofu
  • 1/2 cup of sliced spam
  • 1/2 cup of sliced hot dogs
  • 2 packs of instant ramen noodles
  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent.

  2. Add the sliced mushrooms, carrots, cabbage, tofu, spam, and hot dogs to the pot. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Add the kimchi, gochujang, and gochugaru to the pot. Stir well to combine the flavors and coat the ingredients evenly.

  4. Pour in the chicken stock and water. Stir in the soy sauce and sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. While the stew is simmering, cook the instant ramen noodles separately according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set them aside.

  6. After 15 minutes of simmering, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  7. Add the cooked ramen noodles to the pot and stir gently to incorporate them into the stew.

  8. Continue cooking the stew for another 5 minutes to ensure the noodles are heated through.

  9. Once the noodles are heated, remove the pot from the heat and serve the Budae Jjigae hot.

Serving Suggestions

Budae Jjigae is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or with a side of steamed rice. For an authentic Korean dining experience, serve the stew with an array of banchan (side dishes) such as kimchi, pickled radish, and seasoned spinach. The combination of flavors and textures will truly elevate your meal.

Conclusion

Making Budae Jjigae is not only about creating a delicious and comforting meal; it is about embracing the history and culture behind this unique dish. The fusion of Korean and American flavors in Budae Jjigae symbolizes resilience and creativity during challenging times. So, gather your loved ones, embark on a culinary adventure, and savor the ultimate comfort food that is Budae Jjigae!

Remember, the best thing about cooking is that you can always experiment and adjust the recipe according to your taste preferences. So, don’t be afraid to add your own twist to this classic dish. Enjoy your Budae Jjigae adventure and happy cooking!
