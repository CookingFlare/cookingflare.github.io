Easter Brunch Casserole Recipes for a Crowd-Pleasing Meal


Easter is a time of joy and celebration, and what better way to bring everyone together than with a delicious brunch? If you’re looking for crowd-pleasing meal ideas that are both satisfying and easy to make, then you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering Easter brunch casserole recipes that are sure to impress your guests. From savory to sweet, these recipes have something for everyone.

1. Cheesy Ham and Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups frozen shredded hash browns
  • 1 cup diced cooked ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the hash browns, ham, cheese, bell pepper, and onion. Spread this mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and black pepper. Pour this mixture over the hash brown mixture in the baking dish.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the casserole is set and the top is golden brown. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

2. Spinach and Feta Breakfast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 10 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the spinach until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, squeeze out any excess moisture from the spinach.
  3. In a bowl, combine the spinach, feta cheese, red onion, and dill. Spread this mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, garlic powder, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Pour this mixture over the spinach mixture in the baking dish.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the casserole is set and the top is lightly browned. Allow it to cool slightly before serving.

3. Blueberry French Toast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf French bread, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and arrange the bread cubes in a single layer.
  2. Sprinkle the blueberries evenly over the bread cubes.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and cinnamon. Pour this mixture over the bread and blueberries, ensuring all the bread is soaked.
  4. Cover the baking dish with foil and refrigerate overnight, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Remove the dish from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  6. Bake the casserole, covered, for 30 minutes. Then, uncover and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is set.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Dust with powdered sugar if desired.

These Easter brunch casserole recipes are not only delicious but also easy to make, allowing you to spend more time enjoying the company of your loved ones. Whether you prefer a savory ham and potato casserole, a flavorful spinach and feta option, or a sweet blueberry French toast casserole, there’s something here for every taste bud. So, gather your friends and family, and let these crowd-pleasing casseroles make your Easter brunch a memorable one. Happy cooking!
