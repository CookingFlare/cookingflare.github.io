Grilled Pizza: A Fun Twist for Your July 4th BBQ


As the summer sun shines brightly and the scent of barbecue fills the air, July 4th is the perfect occasion to gather friends and family for a festive celebration. While traditional burgers and hot dogs are always crowd-pleasers, why not elevate your BBQ game this year with a fun and delicious twist? Enter grilled pizza—a culinary adventure that combines the smoky flavors of the grill with the beloved comfort of pizza. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to create mouthwatering grilled pizzas that will impress your guests and make your July 4th gathering unforgettable.

Why Grilled Pizza?

Grilled pizza is not just a meal; it’s an experience. The high heat of the grill creates a perfectly crispy crust while allowing for a variety of toppings to meld together in a symphony of flavors. The smoky aroma adds an extra layer of deliciousness that you simply can’t achieve in a conventional oven. Plus, grilling pizza is a fun activity that everyone can participate in, making it a great way to bond with friends and family.

Essential Equipment

Before diving into the recipes, let’s ensure you have the right equipment for grilling pizza:

  1. Grill: Whether you have a gas or charcoal grill, both work well for grilling pizza. Just make sure it can reach high temperatures.
  2. Pizza Stone or Grill Pan: A pizza stone helps distribute heat evenly, while a grill pan can be used for smaller pizzas or if you prefer a more rustic look.
  3. Pizza Peel: This handy tool makes transferring your pizza to and from the grill a breeze.
  4. Tongs and Spatula: Essential for flipping and moving your pizzas safely.
  5. Baking Sheet: For preparing your pizza before it hits the grill.

Making the Perfect Pizza Dough

The foundation of any great pizza is its dough. Here’s a simple recipe to create a delicious homemade pizza dough that’s perfect for grilling.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water (about 110°F)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Mix the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Let it Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  5. Prepare for Grilling: Once risen, punch down the dough and divide it into 2-4 equal portions, depending on how large you want your pizzas. Roll each portion into a ball and let them rest for another 15 minutes.

Choosing Your Toppings

One of the best parts about grilled pizza is the endless topping possibilities. Here are some classic and creative topping ideas to inspire your July 4th menu:

Classic Margherita

  • Ingredients: Fresh mozzarella, tomatoes, fresh basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Instructions: Spread a thin layer of olive oil on the dough, add sliced tomatoes, and top with mozzarella. Season with salt and pepper, and finish with fresh basil after grilling.

BBQ Chicken

  • Ingredients: Cooked chicken, BBQ sauce, red onion, cilantro, and mozzarella.
  • Instructions: Toss shredded chicken in BBQ sauce. Spread a thin layer of BBQ sauce on the dough, add chicken, sliced red onion, and mozzarella. Top with fresh cilantro after grilling.

Veggie Delight

  • Ingredients: Bell peppers, red onion, mushrooms, spinach, feta cheese, and mozzarella.
  • Instructions: Sauté the vegetables lightly before adding them to the pizza. Spread olive oil on the dough, layer the veggies, and sprinkle with feta and mozzarella.

Pesto and Goat Cheese

  • Ingredients: Pesto, goat cheese, sun-dried tomatoes, and arugula.
  • Instructions: Spread pesto on the dough, crumble goat cheese on top, and add sun-dried tomatoes. Top with fresh arugula after grilling.

Grilling Your Pizza

Now that you have your dough and toppings ready, it’s time to grill your pizza!

Preheat the Grill

  1. Heat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 450°F to 500°F).
