Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry Recipe


When it comes to balancing bold flavors with sweet and savory elements, Thai cuisine stands unrivaled. One particular dish that encapsulates this harmony perfectly is the Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry. This recipe fuses succulent shrimp, juicy pineapple chunks, and fragrant curry spices to create a dish bursting with vibrant colors and flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or looking to spice up your meal rotation, this curry brings a delightful twist to your dining table, reminiscent of tropical beaches and warm evenings.

Why Choose Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry?

Thai cooking is celebrated for its intricate layering of flavors: sweet, sour, salty, and spicy notes all dance in perfect unison. The addition of shrimp provides a tender, slightly sweet seafood base that pairs exceptionally well with the fruitiness of pineapple. Meanwhile, the aromatic curry paste and creamy coconut milk create a luscious sauce that binds all the ingredients together.

This dish is not only delicious but also nutritious. Shrimp offers a lean source of protein packed with essential nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids and selenium. Pineapple adds a burst of vitamin C and natural sweetness, balancing the heat from the curry.

Now, let’s dive into preparing this exquisite Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry, breaking down each step to ensure a flawless and enjoyable cooking experience.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you begin, gather the following ingredients. Many of these might already be staples in your pantry or easily found at an international grocery store:

  • Shrimp: 1 pound, peeled and deveined (medium to large size works best)
  • Pineapple: 1 cup fresh, cut into bite-sized chunks (canned pineapple can be a substitute but fresh is preferable)
  • Thai Red Curry Paste: 2 to 3 tablespoons (adjust according to your spice tolerance)
  • Coconut Milk: 1 can (13.5 oz), full-fat for creaminess
  • Vegetable Oil: 1 to 2 tablespoons for sautéing
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Shallots or Small Onions: 2, finely chopped
  • Fish Sauce: 1 tablespoon for authentic umami
  • Brown Sugar: 2 teaspoons to balance flavors
  • Kaffir Lime Leaves: 3-4 (optional but adds vibrant citrus notes)
  • Fresh Thai Basil Leaves: A handful, roughly chopped
  • Red Bell Pepper: 1, sliced thinly for texture and color
  • Fresh Red Chili or Thai Birds Eye Chili: 1, sliced (optional, if you want extra heat)
  • Lime Juice: from half a lime, to brighten the dish
  • Cooked Jasmine Rice or Sticky Rice: For serving

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Start by peeling and deveining the shrimp if not already done. Keep them chilled until ready to cook. Chop your pineapple into uniform chunks to ensure even cooking and mouthfeel. Mince the garlic and shallots finely to release maximum flavor during seasoning.

If you’re using fresh kaffir lime leaves, remove the central vein and tear the leaves into smaller pieces to release their aromatic oils. Roughly chop the basil leaves but keep some whole for garnishing at the end. Thinly slice the red bell pepper and chili pepper for a contrast in texture and added spice.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics and Curry Paste

Heat the vegetable oil in a medium-sized wok or skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the minced garlic and chopped shallots. Sauté until they become translucent and fragrant—around 2 minutes.

Add the Thai red curry paste and cook for another minute, stirring constantly. Cooking the curry paste at this stage deepens its flavors and releases the essential oils from the spices, which will infuse the entire dish with richness.

Step 3: Incorporate Coconut Milk and Seasonings

Pour the full can of coconut milk into the pan while stirring to combine thoroughly with the curry paste mixture. Bring this base to a gentle simmer.

Now, add the fish sauce and brown sugar. The fish sauce delivers umami and saltiness, while the brown sugar balances the heat and tartness with subtle sweetness. Toss in the kaffir lime leaves if using—these will infuse a bright, citrus note.

Let this sauce simmer for about 5 minutes to meld the flavors. The coconut milk will thicken slightly, creating a silky, creamy curry sauce that clings beautifully to the shrimp and pineapple.

Step 4: Add Shrimp and Pineapple

Gently stir in the shrimp, ensuring each piece is coated with the curry sauce. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes or until the shrimp turn opaque pink and curl slightly.

Add the pineapple chunks along with the red bell pepper slices. Pineapple cooks quickly, so it only needs a couple of minutes to warm through and soften slightly, delivering bursts of juicy sweetness.

At this stage, taste your curry and adjust the seasoning. More fish sauce can be added for saltiness, or a squeeze of lime juice to introduce a fresh tang.

Step 5: Final Touches

Turn off the heat and stir in the chopped Thai basil leaves to enhance the herbal fragrance. The residual warmth will wilt the basil just enough without overpowering its fragrance.

If you like it hotter, toss in the sliced fresh chili or incorporate a dash of chili flakes.

Step 6: Serve

Scoop the Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry over steaming jasmine rice or sticky rice to soak up the delicious sauce. Garnish with a few fresh basil leaves and perhaps an extra wedge of lime on the side for an extra zing.

This dish pairs exceptionally well with a light cucumber salad or steamed greens, balancing the rich curry beautifully.

Tips and Variations for Customizing Your Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry

  • Protein Variations: If shrimp isn’t your favorite, substitute with chicken thighs, tofu for a vegetarian version, or even scallops.
  • Adjusting Spice Levels: The Thai red curry paste can vary in heat between brands. Start with less, taste, and add more if you desire an intense kick.
  • Adding Vegetables: For more texture and nutrition, toss in snap peas, baby corn, or zucchini slices.
  • Coconut Milk Alternatives: Lite coconut milk can reduce calories but expect a less creamy texture.
  • Using Canned Pineapple: Opt for pineapple chunks packed in juice rather than syrup to avoid added sugars that might imbalance the flavors.
  • Herbs: If Thai basil isn’t available, sweet basil or even fresh mint can serve as a substitute.

Nutritional Snapshot

A generous serving of Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry offers a balanced meal with lean protein, heart-healthy fats from coconut milk, and a variety of vitamins and minerals from fresh produce. The pineapples contribute fiber and vitamin C, and the curry spices may aid digestion and bring anti-inflammatory properties.

Wrapping Up

This Thai Shrimp and Pineapple Curry is a delightful fusion of tropical flavors wrapped in creamy, spicy goodness. Bright, refreshing, and fragrant, it makes a beautiful centerpiece for a weeknight dinner or impresses guests when entertaining.

The recipe is straightforward but yields a complex flavor profile that’s hard to resist. Once you’ve mastered this dish, you may find yourself craving it as often as your favorite comfort foods.

Give it a try today, and bring a tropical Thai feast to your kitchen with minimal fuss but maximum flavor!
