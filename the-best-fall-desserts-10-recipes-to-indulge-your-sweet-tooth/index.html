The Best Fall Desserts: 10 Recipes to Indulge Your Sweet Tooth


As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, it’s time to embrace the warmth and coziness of fall with some irresistible desserts. Whether you’re gathering around a bonfire, preparing for Thanksgiving, or simply indulging in a sweet treat on a cool autumn evening, these fall-inspired desserts will perfectly complement the season’s flavors. From rich pumpkin spice to comforting apple cinnamon, these treats bring all the flavors of fall together in delightful ways.

1. Classic Pumpkin Pie

No fall season is complete without a slice of pumpkin pie. This classic dessert embodies everything that makes fall so special. The smooth, spiced pumpkin filling sits perfectly inside a buttery, flaky crust, creating the ultimate comforting treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin puree
  • ¾ cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-made pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, eggs, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, salt, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Pour the mixture into the pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 40-45 minutes or until the center is set and a knife inserted comes out clean.
  5. Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy with a dollop of whipped cream!

2. Apple Crisp

Apple crisp is one of those timeless fall desserts that never gets old. The combination of tender baked apples, cinnamon sugar, and a crispy oat topping creates a perfect balance of flavors and textures. It’s simple to make and is ideal for enjoying on a chilly evening.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium-sized apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, granulated sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Spread the apples evenly in a greased 9x9-inch baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, and cubed butter. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix the ingredients until the butter forms small crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  5. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat!

3. Caramel Apple Cheesecake Bars

Cheesecake bars are a delicious way to enjoy the creamy richness of cheesecake in a more portable form. These caramel apple cheesecake bars combine the sweetness of caramel with the tartness of apples for a perfectly balanced fall dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 medium-sized apples, peeled and diced
  • ¼ cup caramel sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared dish to form the crust.
  3. In a medium-sized bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the cheesecake mixture over the crust. Scatter the diced apples on top and drizzle with caramel sauce.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the center is set and slightly golden. Allow the bars to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours before slicing and serving.

4. Cinnamon Roll Apple Pie

If you’re a fan of both cinnamon rolls and apple pie, this hybrid dessert is a must-try! Combining the gooey cinnamon filling of a cinnamon roll with the sweet, spiced apples of a traditional apple pie, this dessert is a showstopper.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can cinnamon roll dough (8 rolls)
  • 5 medium-sized apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • ¼ cup brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the cinnamon roll dough and set aside the icing that comes with it.
  3. In a large bowl, mix the sliced apples, lemon juice, sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Let the apples sit for 10 minutes to release some of their juices.
  4. Arrange the cinnamon roll dough at the bottom of a pie dish to form a crust, then layer the spiced apples on top. Dot the apples with butter.
  5. Cut the remaining cinnamon rolls into small pieces and scatter them over the apples, pressing them down slightly.
  6. Brush the top with an egg wash and sprinkle with brown sugar.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the cinnamon rolls are golden brown and the apples are tender.
  8. Drizzle the included icing over the top for an extra indulgent finish.

5. Butternut Squash Blondies

If you’re looking for a more unique fall dessert, these butternut squash blondies are an excellent choice. The rich, sweet flavor of roasted butternut squash adds a lovely depth to the traditional blondie recipe, making these a must-have for fall.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pureed butternut squash
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 stick unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the butternut squash, brown sugar, melted butter, vanilla extract, and eggs until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and stir until combined.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Let the blondies cool before cutting them into squares. Enjoy!

6. Sweet Potato Pie

Much like pumpkin pie, sweet potato pie is a beloved fall dessert that brings comforting flavors to the table. Its velvety texture and subtly sweet flavor make it the perfect way to celebrate the season.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pre-made pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Pierce the sweet potatoes with a fork and bake for 45-60 minutes, or until tender. Let them cool, then peel and mash them.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, heavy cream, eggs, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set.
  5. Let the pie cool before serving with whipped cream or a scoop of ice cream.

7. Pecan Pie Bars

If you love pecan pie but want a more portable version, these pecan pie bars are the answer. With a buttery shortbread crust, rich pecan filling, and a touch of caramel, these bars are the ultimate fall treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2 cups pecans, chopped
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup corn syrup
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and cubed butter. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture forms crumbs.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of the baking dish to form the crust, and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until golden.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pecans, brown sugar, corn syrup, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth.
  5. Pour the pecan mixture over the baked crust and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set.
  6. Let the bars cool completely before cutting into squares. Enjoy!

8. Maple Walnut Fudge

Maple and walnut are an iconic combination that screams fall. This rich and creamy maple walnut fudge will satisfy your sweet tooth and leave you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the cream, brown sugar, and maple syrup over medium heat. Stir frequently until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and butter.
  3. Add the chopped walnuts and mix well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x9-inch baking dish and let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, then cut into squares and serve.

9. Chai Spiced Cupcakes

Chai spiced cupcakes are a delightful way to enjoy the cozy flavors of fall in bite-sized form. These cupcakes are infused with a blend of warm spices, topped with creamy frosting, and are perfect for any autumn gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cardamom
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, cloves, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat together the sugar and butter until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk, and stir until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Let the cupcakes cool before frosting them with your favorite frosting.

10. Pear and Almond Tart

For a more elegant dessert to serve at your fall gatherings, a pear and almond tart is the perfect choice. The buttery tart crust is filled with a rich almond cream, topped with fresh pears, and baked to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made tart crust
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 pears, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Press the tart crust into a tart pan and set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, sugar, butter, egg, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth and creamy.
  4. Spread the almond mixture evenly over the tart crust.
  5. Arrange the pear slices on top in a decorative pattern.
  6. Drizzle the pears with honey and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the almond filling is set.
  7. Let the tart cool before slicing and serving.

These 10 fall desserts are sure to delight your taste buds and bring warmth to your home throughout the season. Whether you’re craving pumpkin, apple, or something a little more unique, these recipes have everything you need to celebrate fall with a sweet twist. Happy baking!
