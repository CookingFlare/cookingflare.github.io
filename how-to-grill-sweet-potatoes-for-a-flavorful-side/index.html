How to Grill Sweet Potatoes for a Flavorful Side


Grilling sweet potatoes is one of the most effective ways to enhance their natural sweetness and create a satisfying side dish that pairs perfectly with almost any main course. Whether you’re hosting a summer BBQ, making a cozy dinner at home, or planning a family gathering, grilled sweet potatoes can elevate any meal with their smoky, caramelized flavor and tender texture. In this blog post, we will take you through the step-by-step process of grilling sweet potatoes to perfection, including tips, tricks, and different variations to suit your taste.

Why Grilled Sweet Potatoes?

Sweet potatoes are a versatile root vegetable, known for their rich flavor and nutritional benefits. They are packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making them a healthy addition to any meal. When grilled, sweet potatoes undergo a magical transformation. The heat of the grill caramelizes the natural sugars in the potato, enhancing its sweetness, while the smoky flavor from the grill adds depth and complexity. Grilled sweet potatoes offer the perfect balance of crispy exterior and tender, creamy interior.

Besides being delicious, grilling sweet potatoes is incredibly simple, and the process allows for endless customization in terms of seasoning and toppings. You can easily adjust the seasoning to match the flavors of your main dish, whether you prefer a sweet, savory, or spicy profile.

Choosing the Right Sweet Potatoes

Before you begin grilling, it’s important to choose the right sweet potatoes. There are several varieties available, but the most common ones you will find are:

  • Orange-fleshed sweet potatoes: These are the most popular variety and have a vibrant orange interior with a slightly sweet flavor. They are perfect for grilling, as they hold up well to the heat and caramelize beautifully.

  • White-fleshed sweet potatoes: These have a milder, less sweet flavor compared to the orange variety. They also work well on the grill, but their texture is slightly firmer, making them a great choice if you prefer a more robust, less sugary flavor.

  • Purple-fleshed sweet potatoes: These are a more unusual variety with a striking purple hue. They have a slightly earthier flavor, which can add a unique twist to your grilled dish.

Regardless of the variety, make sure to choose sweet potatoes that are firm, with no bruises or soft spots. Larger potatoes may take longer to cook, so opt for medium-sized ones to ensure even cooking.

Preparing the Sweet Potatoes

Grilling sweet potatoes is relatively straightforward, but proper preparation is key to achieving the best results. Here are the steps to prepare sweet potatoes for grilling:

1. Wash and Peel (Optional)

Start by thoroughly washing the sweet potatoes to remove any dirt or debris from the skin. If you prefer, you can peel the sweet potatoes, though leaving the skin on adds texture and helps the potato hold together during grilling. The skin also contains additional nutrients, so skipping the peel can be a healthier option. If you prefer a crispier texture, keep the skin on!

2. Slice the Sweet Potatoes

Grilled sweet potatoes can be sliced into a variety of shapes, depending on your preferences and how you plan to serve them. Here are some common options:

  • Rounds: Slice the sweet potato into ½-inch thick rounds. These provide a nice, even surface for caramelization and can be easily flipped on the grill.

  • Wedges: Cut the sweet potato into wedges (like fries). This method is perfect if you want a more rustic look and thicker pieces with a soft interior.

  • Halves: If you prefer a more substantial bite, you can slice the sweet potato in half lengthwise. This creates large, meaty portions that are perfect for grilling directly on the rack.

Ensure all slices are of uniform thickness to promote even cooking and prevent some pieces from burning while others remain undercooked.

3. Preheat the Grill

While prepping the sweet potatoes, preheat your grill to medium heat, around 350°F to 400°F. It’s important to have the grill at the right temperature to ensure even cooking and prevent the sweet potatoes from burning on the outside while remaining raw in the center. For gas grills, simply turn on the burners and wait for the temperature to stabilize. For charcoal grills, allow the coals to burn down until they are covered with white ash.

Once the grill is heated, oil the grates with a brush or a folded paper towel soaked in vegetable oil to prevent the sweet potatoes from sticking.

Grilling Sweet Potatoes: Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that your sweet potatoes are prepared and your grill is preheated, let’s dive into the actual grilling process:

1. Season the Sweet Potatoes

Before placing the sweet potatoes on the grill, it’s essential to season them. A basic seasoning involves olive oil, salt, and pepper, but you can get creative with different flavors. For a sweet and savory profile, consider adding:

  • Brown sugar: Adds a rich, molasses-like sweetness that caramelizes beautifully on the grill.
  • Cinnamon and nutmeg: These spices create a warm, comforting flavor that complements the natural sweetness of the potatoes.
  • Chili powder or smoked paprika: For a smoky, slightly spicy kick.
  • Garlic powder or onion powder: Adds depth and savory flavor to balance the sweetness of the potatoes.

Toss your sliced sweet potatoes in a bowl with your desired seasonings and a little olive oil to coat them evenly.

2. Place the Sweet Potatoes on the Grill

Arrange the sweet potato slices or wedges on the preheated grill. It’s best to place them perpendicular to the grill grates to get those beautiful grill marks. Close the lid and cook for about 4 to 5 minutes on the first side, checking occasionally to make sure they don’t burn. The cooking time may vary depending on the size and thickness of your slices, so it’s important to keep an eye on them.

3. Flip and Continue Grilling

Once the first side is nicely charred and golden, use tongs to flip the sweet potatoes over. Grill for an additional 4 to 5 minutes on the second side. If you’re grilling larger sweet potato halves, it may take a bit longer. Be sure to check for doneness by piercing the sweet potatoes with a fork. They should be tender and soft on the inside with a crispy, caramelized exterior.

4. Optional: Add Toppings

Once your sweet potatoes are perfectly grilled, you can get creative with toppings. Here are some ideas to elevate your grilled sweet potatoes:

  • Fresh herbs: A sprinkle of chopped fresh parsley, cilantro, or thyme adds a pop of freshness.
  • Feta cheese: Crumbled feta or goat cheese adds a tangy contrast to the sweetness of the potatoes.
  • Yogurt or sour cream: A dollop of creamy yogurt or sour cream complements the smoky flavor and adds a cool touch.
  • Chopped nuts: Toasted pecans, walnuts, or almonds add a delightful crunch and nuttiness to balance the soft texture of the sweet potatoes.
  • Sriracha or hot sauce: For those who love heat, a drizzle of spicy sriracha or hot sauce adds a fiery kick.

5. Serve and Enjoy!

Once the sweet potatoes are grilled to perfection, serve them immediately. They pair wonderfully with grilled meats, seafood, or as a standalone vegetarian dish. If you’re serving a large crowd, consider arranging the grilled sweet potatoes on a platter and garnishing them with fresh herbs or a sprinkle of cheese to impress your guests.

Variations and Flavor Combos

Grilled sweet potatoes are incredibly versatile, so don’t hesitate to experiment with different flavor combinations. Here are some unique ideas to try:

  • Spicy Cajun Grilled Sweet Potatoes: Season your sweet potatoes with a Cajun spice blend and grill for a smoky, spicy side dish that will liven up any meal.
  • Maple and Cinnamon Grilled Sweet Potatoes: Drizzle the sweet potatoes with maple syrup before grilling and sprinkle with cinnamon for a fall-inspired flavor.
  • Lemon and Rosemary Grilled Sweet Potatoes: Toss the potatoes with lemon zest, fresh rosemary, and garlic for a fragrant, Mediterranean-inspired side.

The possibilities are endless when it comes to grilling sweet potatoes, so feel free to get creative and adjust the seasoning to suit your taste.

Conclusion

Grilled sweet potatoes are the perfect side dish to add flavor, texture, and nutrition to any meal. With their smoky, caramelized exterior and soft, tender interior, they are a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone coming back for more. Whether you’re using them to complement a steak, burger, or grilled chicken, or enjoying them on their own as a vegetarian dish, sweet potatoes are a versatile and delicious choice.

By following the simple steps outlined in this guide, you can easily create grilled sweet potatoes that are bursting with flavor and texture. So fire up your grill, experiment with seasonings, and enjoy this delectable side dish that’s sure to impress!
