Christmas Breakfast Casserole Recipes for a Crowd


Few moments herald the holiday season like waking up on Christmas morning to a house filled with loved ones and the comforting aroma of a home-cooked breakfast. Amid the laughter, present-opening frenzy, and the festive spirit, having a hearty breakfast ready to serve can relieve some of the holiday morning chaos. Enter the Christmas breakfast casserole—a crowd-pleasing dish that’s easy to prepare, customizable, and perfect for feeding a hungry family or a gathering of friends.

Imagine layers of golden hash browns, perfectly seasoned sausage, fluffy eggs, and gooey cheese all baked to perfection, complemented by seasonal ingredients and holiday flavors. A breakfast casserole allows you to prepare ahead and enjoy the morning, focusing on moments with loved ones while still delivering a meal everyone will rave about. Below, we’ll explore a variety of Christmas breakfast casserole recipes that are ideal for feeding a crowd, ensuring your holiday morning is as memorable as the meal itself.

Classic Sausage and Egg Breakfast Casserole

This traditional breakfast casserole has proven itself timeless because of its simplicity, flavor, and ability to adapt to any crowd. Warm, savory sausage paired with creamy eggs and cheese makes it the quintessential holiday breakfast dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground breakfast sausage
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 cups cubed French bread (stale or slightly toasted for better texture)
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: chopped green onions or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sausage: Heat a skillet over medium heat and brown the sausage, breaking it into small crumbles. Once cooked, remove and set aside.
  2. Layer the Casserole: Grease a baking dish (9×13 works best). Place the cubed French bread at the bottom of the dish, evenly distributing the cooked sausage on top. Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese over the sausage layer.
  3. Make the Egg Mixture: In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until fully combined. Pour the mixture evenly over the bread, sausage, and cheese.
  4. Chill Overnight: Cover the dish with foil or plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight, allowing the bread to soak up the egg mixture.
  5. Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove the casserole from the fridge, uncover, and bake for 40–50 minutes, or until the top is golden and the eggs have set. Garnish with green onions or parsley if desired.
  6. Serve Hot: Slice and serve this comforting dish to your holiday crowd. Pair it with fresh fruit or cranberry juice for a festive touch.

Sweet Potato and Bacon Breakfast Casserole

Rich, earthy, and slightly sweet, this casserole combines crispy bacon with velvety roasted sweet potatoes for a dish that adds a seasonal flair to your Christmas meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups peeled and diced sweet potatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 slices crispy bacon (crumbled)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Sweet Potatoes: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil, paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper. Spread them on a baking sheet and roast for 20–25 minutes until tender.
  2. Prepare the Base: In a greased casserole dish, layer the roasted sweet potatoes and top them with crumbled bacon.
  3. Prepare Egg Mixture: Whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Pour this mixture over the sweet potato and bacon layer.
  4. Add the Cheese: Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese evenly across the top.
  5. Bake: Lower the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C). Bake the casserole uncovered for 30–40 minutes, or until the eggs are set and cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Cool and Serve: Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. The sweet-savory flavors will be a hit with any holiday crowd!

Spinach and Mushroom Holiday Breakfast Casserole

For those seeking a lighter yet equally delicious option, this vegetarian breakfast casserole showcases earthy mushrooms, nutrient-packed spinach, and savory cheeses in perfect harmony.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh spinach (chopped)
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms (button or cremini)
  • 1 small onion (diced)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella or Gruyère cheese
  • 1 cup milk or half-and-half
  • 4 cups cubed sourdough bread
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: nutmeg for added holiday flavor

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: Heat a skillet with a tablespoon of olive oil and sauté mushrooms and onions until softened. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired.
  2. Layer Ingredients: In a greased baking dish, place cubed sourdough bread as the base. Spread the sautéed vegetable mixture evenly on top. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella or Gruyère cheese over the vegetables.
  3. Whisk the Eggs: Combine eggs and milk in a bowl, whisking thoroughly. Pour this mixture over the vegetables and bread.
  4. Refrigerate or Bake: If preparing ahead, cover the dish and refrigerate overnight. Otherwise, bake immediately at 350°F (175°C) for 40–50 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the edges are golden.
  5. Serve Warm: Garnish with fresh parsley or extra cheese and serve. The rich, cheesy notes and freshness of spinach make this casserole a crowd-pleasing centerpiece.

Potato, Ham, and Cheese Casserole

The combination of tender potatoes, smoky ham, and creamy cheese brings an indulgent treat to your holiday table. It’s a comforting recipe that’s easy to scale for larger crowds.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups diced russet potatoes (peeled and parboiled)
  • 2 cups diced cooked ham
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Potatoes: Peel and dice the potatoes, boiling them until just tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. Assemble Layers: Grease a large baking dish and layer the potatoes evenly. Top with diced ham and half of the cheddar cheese.
  3. Whisk the Mixture: Combine sour cream, milk, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Pour this mixture evenly over the potatoes and ham.
  4. Add the Final Cheese Layer: Sprinkle the remaining cheddar cheese over the top.
  5. Bake to Perfection: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the casserole for 35–45 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with parsley or sliced green onions and serve steaming hot to your guests.

Tips for a Stress-Free Holiday Breakfast

  • Make Ahead: Opt for recipes that can be prepped the night before. Most breakfast casseroles taste even better after sitting overnight, allowing flavors to meld.
  • Customizable Ingredients: Casseroles are versatile. Cater to dietary preferences by swapping out proteins, cheeses, or vegetables.
  • Serve Sides: Accompany your casserole with holiday-inspired sides like cranberry muffins, fresh fruit salads, or spiced hot chocolate.
  • Scale Recipes: Easily double the ingredients for larger crowds or use disposable foil pans for easy cleanup.

This Christmas, let breakfast casseroles take center stage in your holiday kitchen. With their delightful flavors, easy preparation, and ability to feed a group, you’ll find yourself free to indulge in the festive spirit while still treating your loved ones to a meal they’ll remember. Bring your favorite ingredients, a smile, and the warmth of home cooking to the table, and make this holiday breakfast a tradition worth repeating year after year!
