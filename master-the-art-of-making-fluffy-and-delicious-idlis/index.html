Master the Art of Making Fluffy and Delicious Idlis


Idlis are a quintessential breakfast dish in South India, known for their soft and fluffy texture. These round, steamed rice cakes are not only delicious but also incredibly versatile. Whether you enjoy them with sambar and chutney or as a base for other dishes like idli upma or idli fry, mastering the art of making perfect idlis is a skill every home cook should have. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of making fluffy and delicious idlis that will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of idli rice
  • 1 cup of urad dal (split black gram dal)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding

Equipment

  • Idli steamer or pressure cooker
  • Idli plates or moulds
  • Blender or wet grinder
  • Mixing bowls

Preparation

  1. Rinse the idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under cold running water.
  2. Soak the idli rice and fenugreek seeds together in a bowl filled with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
  3. Soak the urad dal in a separate bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
  4. Once soaked, drain the water from both the rice and dal.

Grinding the Batter

  1. In a blender or wet grinder, grind the soaked urad dal until it becomes a smooth and fluffy batter. Add water as needed, but be careful not to make the batter too watery.
  2. Transfer the urad dal batter to a large mixing bowl.
  3. Next, grind the soaked idli rice and fenugreek seeds until you achieve a smooth, fine consistency. Again, add water as needed.
  4. Transfer the rice batter to the same mixing bowl as the urad dal batter.
  5. Add salt to the batter and mix well using your hands. The salt will help with the fermentation process.

Fermentation

  1. Cover the mixing bowl with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours. The ideal temperature for fermentation is around 25-30 degrees Celsius (77-86 degrees Fahrenheit).
  2. After the fermentation process, the batter should have risen and become light and airy. You will also notice a slight sour smell, indicating that the batter is ready for steaming.

Steaming the Idlis

  1. Prepare your idli steamer or pressure cooker by adding water to the bottom pot and bringing it to a boil.
  2. Grease the idli plates or moulds with oil or ghee to prevent sticking.
  3. Pour the fermented batter into each mould, filling them about three-fourths full.
  4. Place the idli plates or moulds in the steamer or pressure cooker and cover with a lid.
  5. Steam the idlis for approximately 10-12 minutes on medium heat or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Once cooked, remove the idlis from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes.
  7. Use a spoon or idli knife to gently remove the idlis from the moulds.

Serving Suggestions

  1. Serve the idlis hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or tomato chutney.
  2. For a twist, you can also serve them with a side of spicy gunpowder (idli podi) or ghee for added flavor.
  3. Leftover idlis can be used to make delicious dishes like idli upma, idli fry, or even idli sandwiches.

Tips and Tricks

  • The key to fluffy idlis is the fermentation process. Make sure to allow enough time for the batter to ferment properly.
  • Use a wet grinder if available, as it helps in achieving a finer consistency for the batter.
  • If the weather is cold or the batter is not fermenting well, you can place the mixing bowl in a warm oven or near a warm place to aid the fermentation process.
  • Adding a small handful of flattened rice (poha) while soaking the rice can help make the idlis softer.

Now that you have mastered the art of making fluffy and delicious idlis, it’s time to impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. Enjoy these mouthwatering idlis for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and experiment with different accompaniments to create your own unique flavors. Happy cooking!
