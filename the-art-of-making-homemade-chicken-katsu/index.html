The Art of Making Homemade Chicken Katsu


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve likely come across Chicken Katsu on the menu of your favorite Japanese restaurant. This delectable dish features crispy, breaded chicken cutlets that are fried to perfection, offering a delightful crunch with every bite. While it may seem like a complex dish to recreate at home, making homemade Chicken Katsu is surprisingly simple and incredibly rewarding. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the art of making homemade Chicken Katsu, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving that signature crispy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups Panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Shredded cabbage, for serving
  • Tonkatsu sauce (or Bulldog sauce), for serving
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by preparing the chicken breasts. Place them on a cutting board and cover them with plastic wrap. Use a meat mallet or the bottom of a heavy pan to pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness of about half an inch. Season the chicken with salt and pepper on both sides.

Step 2: Set Up Your Dredging Station

Prepare three shallow dishes. In the first dish, place the all-purpose flour. In the second dish, crack the eggs and beat them lightly. In the third dish, pour the Panko breadcrumbs.

Step 3: Dredge the Chicken

Dredge each chicken breast in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the chicken in the Panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the chicken.

Step 4: Fry the Chicken

In a large skillet, heat about half an inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the breaded chicken breasts into the skillet. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through. Transfer the cooked chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the Chicken Katsu is ready, it’s time to plate up and enjoy. Slice the Chicken Katsu into strips and serve it with shredded cabbage, a generous drizzle of Tonkatsu sauce, and a side of steamed white rice. The combination of the crispy chicken, tangy sauce, and refreshing cabbage creates a harmonious and satisfying meal.

Tips for Success:

  • Ensure the oil is hot enough before frying the chicken to achieve a crispy exterior.
  • Use Panko breadcrumbs for the perfect crunchy texture.
  • Serve the Chicken Katsu immediately to preserve its crispiness.

Creating homemade Chicken Katsu allows you to tailor the dish to your preferences, whether it’s the level of seasoning, the thickness of the cutlets, or the accompanying sides. As you master the art of making Chicken Katsu, feel free to experiment with different variations, such as using pork instead of chicken or adding a spicy kick to the breading.

With this simple yet indulgent recipe, you can bring the flavors of Japan into your kitchen and impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on the delightful journey of making homemade Chicken Katsu. Your taste buds will thank you!
