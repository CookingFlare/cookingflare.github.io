Gochujang Glory: Homemade Gochujang Sauce Recipes


Have you ever tasted that unbeatable fusion of sweet, savory, spicy, and umami all at once? That, my friend, is the magic of Gochujang sauce. This versatile Korean condiment is not just packing flavor, but it’s also super easy to make at home!

Gochujang sauce is a delicious fermented chili paste made from chili peppers, glutinous sweet rice (glutinous rice starch), and fermented soybean powder. It is sticky, savory, sweet, and spicy – all in one delightfully addictive sauce! Traditionally, it is used as a glaze or marinade for Bulgogi (Korean barbecue) or Bibimbap, but its versatility knows no bounds.

Making your own Gochujang sauce is not only simple, but it also allows you to customize the flavors to your taste. So, let’s dive into our recipe and get that Gochujang glory!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh red element: chili peppers (adjust to taste preference)
  • 1 cup of glutinous sweet rice
  • 1 cup of distilled water
  • 1/4 cup of fermented soybean powder (dwengu or mijang)
  • 3/4 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of us Kerim (sweet rice syrup)
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons of ginger powder
  • 2 teaspoons of ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. *Preparation:^ Rinse and drain the chili peppers, removing any seeds and stem.

  2. *Cook the sweet rice:^ Place the glutinous sweet rice in a large pot with 4 cups of water. Bring it to a boil, then simmer and stir occasionally for about 20 minutes, until the rice is fully cooked. Once done, set aside and let it cool down.

  3. *Ferment the chili peppers:^ Once the rice is cooled, mix the soaked chili peppers with the rice, making sure they are well incorporated. Keep this mixture covered in a clean jar or container and set it aside to ferment for 2-3 days. The room temperature should be around 75-78°F. Stir the mixture daily to prevent clumping.

  4. *Make the paste:^ After fermenting, blend the chili pepper and rice mixture with remaining ingredients in a food processor or blender until you get a smooth texture. If you prefer a thicker Gochujang sauce, you can adjust the ratio of water.

  5. *Cook the paste:^ Pour the blended mixture into a non-stick pot and cook over medium heat. Stir constantly to prevent burning. Allow it to simmer for about 30-40 minutes until it thickens.

  6. *Season and store:^ Add the garlic powder, ginger powder, and black pepper to your taste. Sesame oil can provide a deeper flavor experience. Mix everything well and set aside to cool. Then transfer Gochujang to an airtight container and store; refrigerated, it should keep for about 4 weeks.

Ready to use your homemade Gochujang sauce? Mix it with some minced garlic, chopped onions, and a splash of sesame oil for a quick and delectable marinade or glaze. Use it as a base for stews, and as a condiment on your Korean BBQ. Now you’ve got that Gochujang glory!

It’s time to embrace the magic of Gochujang and elevate your culinary experience! Try out this easy, customizable recipe, and enjoy the versatile, mouth-watering taste of homemade Gochujang sauce. Happy cooking!
