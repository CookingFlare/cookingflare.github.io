Hearty Irish Stews: 5 Recipes for a Cozy Meal


There’s something incredibly comforting about a hearty bowl of stew. And when it comes to cozy meals, Irish stews are in a league of their own. With their rich flavors, tender chunks of meat, and hearty vegetables, they are the perfect dish to warm you up on a chilly day. In this blog post, we’ll explore five delicious Irish stew recipes that will leave you feeling satisfied and content.

1. Traditional Beef and Guinness Stew

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 2 hours
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds stewing beef, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can of Guinness stout
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onions, carrots, and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent and the vegetables have softened.
  3. Return the beef to the pot and add the potatoes, Guinness, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  4. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  5. Serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh thyme leaves and accompanied by crusty bread.

2. Lamb and Vegetable Stew

Preparation time: 30 minutes
Cooking time: 3 hours
Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs of rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the lamb chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the lamb from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent and fragrant.
  3. Return the lamb to the pot and add the carrots, parsnips, leeks, potatoes, beef broth, red wine, tomato paste, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  4. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 3 hours, or until the lamb is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Serve the stew piping hot, garnished with a sprig of fresh rosemary.

3. Vegetarian Guinness Stew

Preparation time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 1 hour
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can of Guinness stout
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup pearl barley

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions, carrots, and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent and the vegetables have softened.
  2. Add the potatoes, Guinness, vegetable broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30 minutes.
  4. Add the pearl barley to the pot and continue simmering for another 30 minutes, or until the barley is cooked and the flavors have melded together.
  5. Serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh thyme leaves.

4. Seafood Chowder

Preparation time: 30 minutes
Cooking time: 40 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups fish or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • 1 pound mixed seafood (such as shrimp, fish, and scallops)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until the vegetables have softened.
  2. Add the potatoes and broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
  3. Stir in the milk or cream and the mixed seafood. Cook for another 10 minutes, or until the seafood is cooked through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

5. Irish Lamb Stew with Dumplings

Preparation time: 30 minutes
Cooking time: 2 hours
Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For the dumplings:
    • 1 cup self-rising flour
    • 1/2 cup suet
    • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
    • Cold water, as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the lamb chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the lamb from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent and fragrant.
  3. Return the lamb to the pot and add the carrots, parsnips, leeks, potatoes, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  4. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the lamb is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, in a bowl, mix together the self-rising flour, suet, and parsley. Gradually add cold water and mix until a soft dough forms.
  6. Shape the dough into small dumplings and drop them into the simmering stew. Cover the pot and cook for another 20-30 minutes, or until the dumplings are cooked through.
  7. Serve the stew hot, with a generous helping of dumplings.

These five Irish stew recipes are guaranteed to warm your soul and satisfy your cravings for a cozy meal. From the traditional beef and Guinness stew to the vegetarian Guinness stew, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So grab a bowl, snuggle up under a blanket, and indulge in the heartwarming flavors of Ireland. Sláinte!
