Low-Carb Grilled Recipes for Summer Entertaining


As the weather warms up and the days get longer, there’s nothing quite like the smoky aroma of grilling outdoors. Summer grilling doesn’t just have to be about heavy, carb-loaded burgers and buns. If you’re following a low-carb lifestyle or just looking to add more nutritious options to your next barbecue, there are countless delicious, healthy recipes that can be grilled to perfection.

Grilling during the summer provides a great opportunity to explore vibrant flavors, unique ingredients, and refreshing meals that are both light and satisfying. With a little creativity, your grill can become a powerhouse for preparing mouthwatering low-carb dishes, from juicy meats to crisp veggies, that will impress your guests and make any gathering memorable.

In this post, we’ll explore some innovative, flavorful, and easy-to-follow low-carb grilling recipes that will elevate your summer entertaining game without compromising your health goals. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a casual cookout, or an elegant garden party, these low-carb grilled recipes are guaranteed to be crowd-pleasers.

1. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breast (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir until all ingredients are well-mixed.
  2. Prepare the Chicken: Add the chicken cubes to the marinade, ensuring each piece is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours for more flavor.
  3. Prepare the Grill: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and lightly oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  4. Skewer the Chicken: Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewers. Be sure to leave a little space between each piece for even cooking.
  5. Grill the Skewers: Place the skewers on the grill. Grill for 4-5 minutes on each side until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks.
  6. Serve: Remove from the grill and let the skewers rest for a couple of minutes before serving. Garnish with extra fresh herbs or lemon slices for a zesty touch.

These grilled chicken skewers are bursting with fresh, herbal flavors and have a light and satisfying texture. Perfect for serving as an appetizer or as a main course.

2. Grilled Veggie Platter with Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pesto (homemade or store-bought)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Slice all the vegetables into uniform sizes to ensure even cooking. If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for about 20 minutes before grilling to prevent them from burning.
  2. Season the Veggies: Drizzle the vegetables with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the Vegetables: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Arrange the veggies directly on the grill or thread them onto skewers. Grill each side for about 3-5 minutes, or until they’re tender and slightly charred.
  4. Prepare the Pesto: While the vegetables are grilling, warm your pesto in a small bowl or pan. If you prefer a thinner consistency, you can mix in a teaspoon of olive oil.
  5. Serve: Once the veggies are grilled, arrange them on a platter. Drizzle the warm pesto over the vegetables for a rich, herbaceous finish.

This veggie platter is colorful, full of antioxidants, and rich in flavors. The pesto adds a creamy, garlicky punch that perfectly complements the grilled veggies.

3. Grilled Salmon with Avocado Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Salmon: Rub each salmon fillet with olive oil and season with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Grill the Salmon: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the salmon fillets skin-side down on the grill. Grill for 4-5 minutes on each side or until the salmon is cooked to your preferred level of doneness.
  3. Make the Avocado Salsa: In a bowl, combine the diced avocado, tomato, red onion, cilantro, and lime juice. Gently toss to combine, being careful not to mash the avocado.
  4. Serve: Once the salmon is grilled, place it on a plate and top with the fresh avocado salsa. Garnish with extra cilantro and lime wedges.

Grilled salmon is a rich source of healthy fats and protein, and the fresh, zesty avocado salsa adds a cooling contrast to the smoky flavors of the fish. This dish is perfect for a light but satisfying summer meal.

4. Grilled Shrimp Skewers with Garlic Butter

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine the melted butter, garlic, parsley, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Add the shrimp and toss to coat evenly.
  2. Skewer the Shrimp: Thread the shrimp onto the soaked wooden skewers, arranging them in a single layer for even cooking.
  3. Grill the Shrimp: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn pink and are cooked through.
  4. Serve: Once grilled, serve the shrimp skewers with extra garlic butter for dipping and garnish with fresh parsley.

These grilled shrimp skewers are the perfect low-carb appetizer, packed with flavor from the garlic butter and fresh herbs. They cook quickly, making them ideal for entertaining.

5. Grilled Chicken Lettuce Wraps

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 head of butter lettuce (or iceberg lettuce), leaves separated
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/4 cup diced avocado
  • Lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Rub the chicken breasts with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Let the chicken marinate for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Grill the Chicken: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for about 6-8 minutes per side or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  3. Assemble the Wraps: Once the chicken is cooked, slice it into thin strips. Arrange the lettuce leaves on a platter and top each leaf with slices of chicken, red onion, cucumber, and avocado.
  4. Serve: Squeeze fresh lime juice over the wraps for a burst of flavor. Serve the lettuce wraps as a fun, hands-on meal that’s perfect for a low-carb gathering.

These chicken lettuce wraps are a great substitute for traditional tacos or sandwiches, offering a healthy and satisfying meal with a nice crunch from the lettuce.

6. Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Goat Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushrooms, stems removed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mushrooms: Clean the Portobello mushrooms by wiping them with a damp cloth. Brush both sides with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, then season with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the Mushrooms: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Grill the mushrooms for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and have nice grill marks.
  3. Top with Goat Cheese: Remove the mushrooms from the grill and immediately crumble goat cheese on top of each mushroom. The heat from the mushrooms will melt the cheese slightly.
  4. Serve: Garnish the mushrooms with fresh basil leaves and serve as a flavorful and satisfying low-carb side dish.

Grilled Portobello mushrooms are a great alternative to meat and have a rich, earthy flavor that pairs perfectly with the tanginess of goat cheese. They make a fantastic vegetarian option for your summer BBQs.

Conclusion

Low-carb grilling doesn’t have to be boring or bland. With these flavorful and easy-to-make recipes, your summer entertaining will be a hit. From grilled chicken skewers and shrimp to veggie platters and lettuce wraps, these dishes are full of fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and are sure to satisfy both low-carb dieters and non-dieters alike. Whether you’re hosting a casual cookout or a fancy garden party, these low-carb grilled recipes will keep your guests coming back for seconds—without the carbs. Happy grilling!
