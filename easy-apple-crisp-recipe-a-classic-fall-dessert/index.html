Easy Apple Crisp Recipe: A Classic Fall Dessert


As the leaves start to fall and the crisp autumn air sets in, there’s nothing quite like the warm, comforting aroma of a freshly baked apple crisp wafting from the oven. This classic fall dessert is a staple of the season, and for good reason: it’s easy to make, deliciously sweet, and perfect for serving warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

The Perfect Apple Crisp Recipe

While there are countless variations of apple crisp out there, this recipe is a tried-and-true classic that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. With just a few simple ingredients and a few minutes of prep time, you’ll be enjoying a warm, gooey apple crisp in no time.

Ingredients

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss until the apples are evenly coated with the dry ingredients.
  3. Transfer the apple mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish and set aside.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the rolled oats, brown sugar, and chopped walnuts (if using).
  5. Add the melted butter to the oat mixture and stir until the mixture is crumbly and well combined.
  6. Spread the oat mixture evenly over the top of the apple filling.
  7. Bake the apple crisp for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender.
  8. Remove the apple crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra crispy topping, try baking the apple crisp for an additional 5-10 minutes. Just keep an eye on it to make sure it doesn’t get too dark!
  • Want to add a little extra flavor to your apple crisp? Try substituting the granulated sugar with honey or maple syrup for a deeper flavor.
  • If you’re looking for a gluten-free option, simply substitute the all-purpose flour with gluten-free flour and proceed with the recipe as instructed.
  • Apple crisp is also a great make-ahead dessert. Simply prepare the recipe through step 6, then refrigerate or freeze until you’re ready to bake. Just add a few extra minutes to the baking time to ensure the topping is crispy and golden.

Conclusion

There you have it – a classic apple crisp recipe that’s sure to become a staple of your fall dessert repertoire. With its warm, comforting aroma and deliciously sweet flavor, this dessert is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the cozy ambiance of a warm and delicious apple crisp on a crisp autumn evening.
