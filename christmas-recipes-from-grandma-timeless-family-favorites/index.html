Christmas Recipes from Grandma: Timeless Family Favorites


The holiday season brings with it a sense of nostalgia, and what better way to celebrate than by indulging in the cherished recipes passed down through generations? Grandma’s kitchen, filled with the aroma of baking cookies, simmering stews, and roasting meats, holds a special place in our hearts. In this post, we’ll explore a collection of timeless Christmas recipes from grandma that not only tantalize the taste buds but also evoke warm memories of family gatherings and joyous celebrations.

The Heart of Christmas Cooking

As the winter chill envelops the world outside, the kitchen becomes a sanctuary of warmth and comfort. Grandma’s recipes often carry stories of traditions, love, and family. Each dish is a reflection of her personality and her desire to bring everyone together. From savory mains to sweet treats, these recipes have stood the test of time, making them the perfect centerpiece for any holiday gathering.

Classic Roast Turkey with Herb Butter

No Christmas feast is complete without the star of the table: the roast turkey. Grandma’s herb butter turkey is succulent, flavorful, and easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cups chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Remove the turkey giblets and pat the turkey dry with paper towels.

  2. Herb Butter: In a bowl, mix the softened butter with rosemary, thyme, sage, garlic, salt, and pepper until well combined.

  3. Butter the Turkey: Carefully loosen the skin of the turkey breast and thighs. Spread half of the herb butter under the skin and the remaining over the outside of the turkey.

  4. Stuffing: Place the quartered onion inside the turkey cavity for additional flavor.

  5. Roasting: Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan. Cover the turkey loosely with aluminum foil and roast for about 2.5 to 3 hours, removing the foil during the last hour to allow the skin to brown.

  6. Basting: Baste the turkey every 30 minutes with the pan juices for an extra moist result.

  7. Resting: Once the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C), remove the turkey from the oven and let it rest for at least 20 minutes before carving.

Grandma’s Cranberry Sauce

A festive table wouldn’t be complete without a vibrant cranberry sauce. Grandma’s version is both tart and sweet, making it the perfect accompaniment to turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine water and sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Add the cranberries, orange zest, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil.

  3. Reduce heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.

  4. Remove from heat and let cool. The sauce will thicken further as it cools.

Creamy Mashed Potatoes with Garlic

These creamy mashed potatoes are a staple on Grandma’s holiday table. Rich, buttery, and garlicky, they pair perfectly with turkey and gravy.

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chives for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain well.

  2. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and butter over low heat, adding the minced garlic until fragrant.

  3. Mash the potatoes using a potato masher or ricer. Gradually blend in the cream mixture until smooth and creamy.

  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped chives if desired.

Grandma’s Secret Stuffing Recipe

Stuffing is an essential part of any holiday meal. This recipe combines bread, herbs, and a secret ingredient that makes it unforgettable.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old bread, cubed
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon sage
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup cooked sausage (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add celery and onion, cooking until softened.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the cubed bread, sautéed vegetables, herbs, and sausage (if using). Pour in the chicken broth and mix until the bread is moist but not soggy.

  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown on top.

Grandma’s Famous Gingerbread Cookies

No Christmas is complete without gingerbread cookies. These spiced treats are fun to make and decorate with the family.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Set aside.

  2. In another bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Mix in molasses and egg until well combined.

  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until a dough forms.

  4. Divide the dough into two discs, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.

  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/4 inch thick. Cut into shapes and place on a lined baking sheet.

  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until firm. Let cool and decorate as desired.

Classic Eggnog

What’s Christmas without a glass of eggnog? Grandma’s eggnog recipe is rich, creamy, and perfect for sipping by the fire.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup bourbon or rum (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Whipped cream for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar together until pale and fluffy.

  2. In a saucepan, heat milk and cream over medium heat until steaming (do not boil). Gradually whisk the hot milk mixture into the egg mixture.

  3. Return everything to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly until thickened, about 5-10 minutes.

  4. Remove from heat and let cool. Stir in bourbon or rum if desired. Chill in the refrigerator before serving.

  5. Serve with a sprinkle of nutmeg and a dollop of whipped cream.

A Sweet Ending: Peppermint Bark

To end the meal on a sweet note, Grandma’s peppermint bark is a festive favorite that’s easy to make and delightful to share.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound white chocolate, chopped
  • 1 pound dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup crushed candy canes

Instructions:

  1. Melt the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl over simmering water, stirring until smooth. Spread it evenly on a parchment-lined baking sheet and let it set.

  2. Melt the white chocolate in the same manner, stirring in the peppermint extract.

  3. Pour the white chocolate over the dark chocolate layer and spread evenly. Sprinkle crushed candy canes on top before the chocolate sets.

  4. Once fully cooled, break into pieces and serve.

Conclusion: Cherishing Grandma’s Recipes

As we celebrate Christmas, let’s take a moment to honor the recipes that have been lovingly passed down through the years. Grandma’s cooking not only fills our bellies but also warms our hearts. These timeless family favorites are more than just dishes; they are a way to connect with our heritage, share stories, and create new memories with loved ones.

Gather your family in the kitchen, roll up your sleeves, and create your own holiday magic with these cherished recipes. Whether it’s the aroma of roast turkey filling the air or the sweet scent of gingerbread cookies baking in the oven, every bite will remind you of the love and warmth that comes from Grandma’s kitchen. Happy cooking, and may your Christmas be filled with joy, laughter, and delicious food!
