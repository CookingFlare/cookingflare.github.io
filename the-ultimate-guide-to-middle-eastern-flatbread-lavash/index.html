The Ultimate Guide to Middle Eastern Flatbread: Lavash


Lavash is a traditional Middle Eastern flatbread that has been enjoyed for centuries. Its thin and flexible texture, along with its delicious flavor, makes it a versatile staple in Middle Eastern cuisine. Whether you’re using it as a wrap, dipping it in hummus, or enjoying it alongside your favorite dishes, lavash is sure to elevate your culinary experience.

The History of Lavash

Lavash has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. It is believed to have originated in Armenia and quickly spread throughout the Middle East. The word “lavash” itself means “soft” in Armenian, which perfectly describes the bread’s texture.

Traditionally, lavash was baked in clay ovens called “tonirs.” These ovens, which are still used in some regions today, give the bread its distinctive charred and blistered appearance. In ancient times, lavash was often used as a form of currency and was considered a symbol of prosperity.

Making Lavash from Scratch

While you can easily find store-bought lavash in many grocery stores, there’s something special about making it from scratch. Not only does it allow you to control the ingredients, but it also gives you a sense of accomplishment and connection to the bread’s history.

Here’s a simple recipe to make your own lavash at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup warm water

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the olive oil and warm water.
  2. Mix the ingredients together until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a thin, rectangular shape. Aim for a thickness of about 1/8 inch.
  4. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). If you have a baking stone, place it in the oven to heat as well.
  5. Carefully transfer the rolled-out lavash onto a baking sheet or directly onto the hot baking stone if you have one.
  6. Bake the lavash for about 6-8 minutes, or until it puffs up and turns golden brown.
  7. Remove the lavash from the oven and let it cool completely before storing or serving.

Serving Suggestions

Lavash is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in many different ways. Here are a few suggestions to get you started:

  • Wraps: Fill lavash with your favorite ingredients such as grilled chicken, fresh vegetables, and a drizzle of tahini sauce for a delicious and healthy wrap.
  • Dips: Cut lavash into smaller pieces and serve with a variety of dips like hummus, baba ganoush, or tzatziki for a flavorful appetizer.
  • Pizza: Use lavash as a thin crust for homemade pizzas. Top with your favorite sauce, cheese, and toppings, and bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  • Chips: Cut lavash into triangles, brush with olive oil, sprinkle with salt, and bake until crispy for homemade lavash chips. Serve with your favorite salsa or dip.

Conclusion

Lavash is a versatile and delicious flatbread that adds a unique touch to any Middle Eastern-inspired meal. By making your own lavash at home, you can experience the joy of creating this traditional bread from scratch. Whether you’re using it as a wrap, a dipper, or a pizza crust, lavash is sure to impress your family and friends with its incredible flavor and texture. So why not give it a try and embark on a culinary journey through the Middle East with lavash as your guide?
