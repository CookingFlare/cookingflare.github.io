A Taste of Thailand: 15 Traditional Recipes to Try


Thailand is a country renowned for its vibrant flavors, rich culinary traditions, and diverse regional dishes. Thai cuisine is a true reflection of the nation’s history, culture, and geography, offering a perfect blend of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy. From the street food stalls of Bangkok to the elegant dishes served in royal kitchens, Thai food is as varied as it is delicious. If you’re looking to explore the authentic taste of Thailand, this list of 15 traditional Thai recipes will take you on a culinary journey you won’t forget.

1. Pad Thai – Stir-Fried Noodles

Pad Thai is one of Thailand’s most iconic dishes. It’s a simple yet flavorful stir-fried noodle dish made with rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, eggs, tofu, peanuts, bean sprouts, and lime. This dish balances the sweet, salty, sour, and spicy flavors, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike. Pad Thai is commonly served with a wedge of lime and crushed peanuts on the side, allowing you to adjust the flavor to your liking.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 150g shrimp or chicken
  • 1 egg
  • 100g tofu (optional)
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Crushed peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes until soft.
  2. In a wok, heat oil and scramble the egg. Once cooked, add the shrimp and tofu, frying until golden brown.
  3. Add the noodles, fish sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, and lime juice. Stir-fry for a few minutes until everything is combined.
  4. Toss in the bean sprouts and stir-fry for an additional minute.
  5. Garnish with crushed peanuts and lime wedges before serving.

2. Tom Yum Goong – Spicy Shrimp Soup

Tom Yum Goong is a hot and sour shrimp soup that is packed with bold flavors. The rich broth is made from lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, fish sauce, lime juice, and chili peppers, giving it an aromatic and spicy kick. This dish is often enjoyed as a starter in Thai meals but can easily be a main dish on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2-3 slices galangal
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies
  • 3 cups water or chicken stock
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the water or stock to a boil and add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal.
  2. Boil for 5-10 minutes to infuse the broth with the herbs and spices.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until pink.
  4. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and bird’s eye chilies.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot.

3. Green Curry – Gaeng Keow Wan

Green Curry, or Gaeng Keow Wan, is one of the most famous Thai curries. The creamy coconut milk combined with the heat of green chilies, the fragrance of basil, and the earthiness of kaffir lime leaves creates a perfect balance of flavors. It can be made with chicken, beef, pork, or vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 300g chicken or tofu, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut milk in a pot over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and stir-fry until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken or tofu, cooking until browned.
  3. Add the vegetables, kaffir lime leaves, and fish sauce, simmering for about 10 minutes.
  4. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot with steamed jasmine rice.

4. Som Tum – Green Papaya Salad

Som Tum is a refreshing and spicy green papaya salad that combines the sweetness of palm sugar, the saltiness of fish sauce, and the heat of bird’s eye chilies. This salad is often enjoyed as a side dish or a light lunch and pairs wonderfully with sticky rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small green papaya, shredded
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chilies
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Crushed peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies to release their flavors.
  2. Add the fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar to the mix.
  3. Toss in the green papaya, cherry tomatoes, and long beans, mixing well.
  4. Garnish with crushed peanuts before serving.

5. Massaman Curry – Rich and Mild

Massaman Curry is a milder and richer Thai curry that has influences from Indian and Muslim cuisines. It’s a deliciously fragrant curry made with potatoes, peanuts, coconut milk, and meat such as beef or chicken. The combination of spices like cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom gives the curry its unique flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
  • 300g beef or chicken, cut into cubes
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • Peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the coconut milk and Massaman curry paste, stirring until fragrant.
  2. Add the meat and brown on all sides.
  3. Add the potatoes, onion, cinnamon stick, and cloves, simmering for about 15-20 minutes until the meat is tender.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting seasoning as needed.
  5. Garnish with peanuts before serving with rice.

6. Khao Pad – Thai Fried Rice

Thai fried rice, or Khao Pad, is a staple in Thai households. This dish is quick, easy, and customizable with your choice of protein, whether it’s chicken, shrimp, or vegetables. The key to great Khao Pad is using day-old rice, as it ensures that the grains remain separate and not mushy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably cold)
  • 1 egg, scrambled
  • 100g shrimp or chicken
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet, and scramble the egg.
  2. Add the shrimp or chicken and cook until done.
  3. Toss in the onions and garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  4. Add the cold rice, fish sauce, and soy sauce, stir-frying for about 5 minutes.
  5. Serve with lime wedges and fresh cilantro.

7. Moo Pad Krapow – Thai Basil Pork

Moo Pad Krapow is a beloved Thai stir-fry dish made with ground pork, Thai basil, and a savory sauce. This dish is fast, flavorful, and perfect for a weeknight dinner. It’s often served with steamed jasmine rice and a fried egg on top.

Ingredients:

  • 300g ground pork
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chilies, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Fried eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and chilies until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground pork, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and oyster sauce, cooking for another 5 minutes.
  4. Toss in the fresh basil leaves, allowing them to wilt before serving.
  5. Serve with steamed rice and a fried egg on top.

8. Gai Yang – Thai Grilled Chicken

Gai Yang is a popular street food in Thailand, known for its smoky flavor and tender texture. The chicken is marinated in a mixture of herbs, spices, and fish sauce before being grilled to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon coriander root, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, coriander root, and lime juice.
  2. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour.
  3. Grill the chicken on medium heat until cooked through, about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

9. Thai Iced Tea – Refreshing Sweetness

Thai Iced Tea is a sweet and refreshing beverage that pairs perfectly with spicy dishes. The strong black tea is brewed with spices like star anise and cinnamon, then mixed with condensed milk and ice for a cooling treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons Thai tea leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons condensed milk
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Brew the tea leaves in boiling water for about 5 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea and add sugar while it’s still hot.
  3. Let the tea cool and then pour over ice.
  4. Add condensed milk and stir before serving.

10. Thai Fish Cakes – Tod Man Pla

Tod Man Pla are deep-fried fish cakes that are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. These delicious snacks are often served with a spicy dipping sauce and are perfect as an appetizer or party food.

Ingredients:

  • 300g white fish fillets, minced
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Mix the minced fish with the kaffir lime leaves, curry paste, egg, fish sauce, and sugar.
  2. Shape the mixture into small patties.
  3. Heat oil in a frying pan and cook the patties until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Serve with a dipping sauce and garnish with fresh cilantro.

11. Panang Curry – A Sweet and Spicy Delight

Panang curry is another popular Thai dish that is similar to red curry but thicker and sweeter. It’s made with coconut milk, red curry paste, and your choice of meat, often accompanied by vegetables like bell peppers and carrots.

Ingredients:

  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons Panang curry paste
  • 300g beef or chicken
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut milk in a pot and stir in the Panang curry paste until fragrant.
  2. Add the meat and cook until browned.
  3. Toss in the bell pepper and carrot, simmering for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in fish sauce and sugar, adjusting to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

12. Khao Soi – Northern Thai Curry Noodles

Khao Soi is a traditional Northern Thai dish that features egg noodles in a rich, coconut-based curry broth. It’s often topped with crispy fried noodles for added texture and served with pickled mustard greens.

Ingredients:

  • 200g egg noodles
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 chicken breast, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Pickled mustard greens for garnish
  • Crispy noodles for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions.
  2. In a pot, combine coconut milk and red curry paste, cooking until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken and simmer until cooked through.
  4. Add soy sauce and fish sauce, adjusting the seasoning.
  5. Serve the curry over the noodles, garnished with pickled mustard greens and crispy noodles.

13. Thai Mango Sticky Rice – A Sweet Finale

Mango sticky rice, or Khao Niew Mamuang, is a popular Thai dessert made with sweet coconut sticky rice paired with ripe mango slices. The creamy coconut milk complements the natural sweetness of the mango, creating a delightful treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sticky rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sticky rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk and sugar, heating until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Pour the coconut milk mixture over the cooked sticky rice, stirring to combine.
  4. Serve the sticky rice with mango slices on top.

14. Thai Style Omelette – Khai Jiao

Khai Jiao is a simple yet satisfying Thai-style omelette. The omelette is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, often served with rice and a side of chili sauce for added flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Chili sauce for serving

Instructions:

  1. Beat the eggs and mix with fish sauce.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the onions until fragrant.
  3. Pour the egg mixture into the pan and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  4. Serve with rice and chili sauce.

15. Thai Peanut Sauce – A Versatile Dip

Thai peanut sauce is a creamy, savory, and slightly sweet sauce that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, spring rolls, or vegetables. It’s made with peanuts, coconut milk, and a variety of spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine all the ingredients and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  2. Once the sauce is smooth and heated through, remove from heat and serve as a dip or drizzle.

Conclusion:

Thai cuisine offers an incredible array of flavors and dishes that are perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re in the mood for a light salad, a hearty curry, or a sweet dessert, there’s a traditional Thai recipe that can satisfy your cravings. These 15 recipes are just a small sample of the many dishes Thailand has to offer, so don’t hesitate to explore even more of the country’s culinary treasures. Happy cooking!
