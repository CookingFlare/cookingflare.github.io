Classic Italian Recipes for Cooking Newbies


Italian cuisine is known for its rich flavors, fresh ingredients, and heartwarming dishes. Whether you’re an experienced cook or just starting your culinary journey, these classic Italian recipes are perfect for cooking newbies. From pasta to pizza, these dishes will transport you to the charming streets of Italy. So, put on your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Spaghetti Carbonara

One of the most beloved Italian pasta dishes, Spaghetti Carbonara, is a simple yet decadent recipe that will impress your taste buds. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a frying pan, cook the pancetta or bacon until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the frying pan with the pancetta or bacon. Pour the egg mixture over the spaghetti and toss until well-coated.
  5. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. Serve immediately and garnish with extra Parmesan cheese and black pepper.

2. Margherita Pizza

Pizza is a staple in Italian cuisine, and the Margherita pizza is a classic that showcases the simplicity and deliciousness of Italian flavors. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Tomato sauce
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature setting (usually around 500°F or 260°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough into your desired shape and thickness. Place it on a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a border around the edges.
  4. Arrange the sliced mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  7. Remove from the oven and garnish with fresh basil leaves. Slice and serve hot.

3. Tiramisu

No Italian meal is complete without a sweet ending, and Tiramisu is the perfect dessert to satisfy your cravings. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 200ml strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 24 ladyfinger cookies (Savoiardi)
  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until creamy and pale yellow.
  2. Add the mascarpone cheese to the egg yolk mixture and whisk until well combined and smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  4. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the mascarpone mixture until fully incorporated.
  5. Dip each ladyfinger cookie into the cooled coffee, making sure not to soak them for too long.
  6. Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in a serving dish or individual glasses.
  7. Spread a layer of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  8. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of mascarpone mixture on top.
  9. Dust the top with cocoa powder.
  10. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld together.
  11. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Conclusion

Italian cuisine offers a delightful combination of flavors and textures that will please any palate. These classic Italian recipes are perfect for cooking newbies looking to explore the world of Italian cooking. From the creamy Spaghetti Carbonara to the mouthwatering Margherita pizza and the indulgent Tiramisu, these dishes will transport you to the heart of Italy. So, gather your ingredients, follow the simple instructions, and savor the taste of Italy in your own kitchen. Buon appetito!
