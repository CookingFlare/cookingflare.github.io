Coriander Creations: Bold Fresh Herb Recipes


Coriander, also known as cilantro, is a versatile herb that adds a fresh and vibrant flavor to any dish. Its distinctive taste and aroma make it a popular choice in various cuisines around the world. In this blog post, we’ll explore some bold and creative recipes that showcase the unique flavors of coriander. From appetizers to main courses, these recipes will take your taste buds on a culinary adventure.

Spicy Coriander Salsa

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the coriander leaves, jalapeno pepper, onion, and garlic. Pulse until finely chopped.
  2. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir in the lime juice and salt.
  3. Let the salsa sit for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Serve with tortilla chips or as a topping for tacos, grilled meats, or roasted vegetables.

Creamy Coriander Chicken

Preparation time: 30 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, coriander leaves, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken breasts to the bowl and coat them evenly with the yogurt mixture. Let marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken breasts and cook for about 6-8 minutes on each side, or until cooked through.
  4. Serve the creamy coriander chicken with a side of steamed rice or roasted vegetables.

Coriander Lime Quinoa Salad

Preparation time: 20 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness.
  2. In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the quinoa and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is tender and the water is absorbed.
  3. In a bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, coriander leaves, red onion, lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss until well combined.
  4. Let the salad sit for at least 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve the coriander lime quinoa salad as a light lunch or as a side dish to grilled fish or chicken.

Coriander Crusted Tofu Stir-Fry

Preparation time: 30 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the tofu into cubes and place them in a bowl. Drizzle with soy sauce and sprinkle with cornstarch. Toss to coat the tofu evenly.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, heat the remaining tablespoon of sesame oil. Add the garlic, red bell pepper, and snap peas. Stir-fry for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Return the tofu to the skillet and toss with the vegetables. Add the fresh coriander leaves and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve the coriander crusted tofu stir-fry over steamed rice or noodles.

Conclusion

Coriander is a herb that brings bold and fresh flavors to any dish. Whether you’re a fan of spicy salsas, creamy chicken dishes, refreshing salads, or tofu stir-fries, these recipes will help you explore the versatility of coriander in your kitchen. Get creative, experiment with different combinations, and let the vibrant flavors of coriander elevate your culinary creations to new heights. Enjoy!
