Homemade Granola: Simple Recipe for Kids


Are you looking for a fun and nutritious snack that your kids will love? Homemade granola is the perfect solution! Not only is it delicious, but it’s also packed with healthy ingredients that will keep your little ones energized throughout the day. In this blog post, we’ll share a simple and kid-friendly recipe for homemade granola that you can make together as a family.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts, or pecans)
  • 1 cup mixed seeds (pumpkin, sunflower, chia)
  • 1/2 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup dried fruit (raisins, cranberries, apricots)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the oats, nuts, and seeds. Mix well to ensure an even distribution of ingredients.

  3. In a small saucepan, warm the honey (or maple syrup), coconut oil, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt over low heat. Stir until the mixture is well combined and the coconut oil is melted.

  4. Pour the wet mixture over the dry ingredients and stir until everything is evenly coated.

  5. Spread the granola mixture onto the prepared baking sheet in an even layer.

  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the granola is golden brown and crispy.

  7. Remove from the oven and allow the granola to cool completely.

  8. Once cooled, stir in the dried fruit.

  9. Store the homemade granola in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two weeks.

Tips for Making Homemade Granola with Kids

Making homemade granola with kids can be a fun and educational experience. Here are some tips to make the process enjoyable for the whole family:

  • Get Creative: Encourage your kids to experiment with different combinations of nuts, seeds, and dried fruits to create their own unique granola blend.

  • Hands-On: Let your kids get involved in the mixing process. It’s a great way for them to develop their motor skills and learn about food preparation.

  • Math Lesson: Use the recipe as an opportunity to teach your kids about measurements and fractions. They can practice counting and measuring the ingredients.

  • Sensory Experience: Let your kids explore the different textures and aromas of the ingredients. This can help develop their sensory awareness and appreciation for food.

  • Healthy Choices: Use this activity as a chance to talk to your kids about the importance of healthy eating and the benefits of nutritious snacks like homemade granola.

Benefits of Homemade Granola for Kids

Homemade granola is a fantastic snack option for kids for several reasons:

  • Nutrient-Rich: It’s packed with essential nutrients from the oats, nuts, seeds, and dried fruit, providing a healthy energy boost for active kids.

  • Customizable: You can tailor the ingredients to suit your kids’ taste preferences and dietary needs, making it a versatile snack option.

  • No Artificial Additives: By making granola at home, you can avoid the preservatives and additives often found in store-bought snacks.

  • Family Bonding: Cooking together as a family creates opportunities for quality time and valuable life skills for kids.

  • Cost-Effective: Making granola at home can be more cost-effective than purchasing pre-packaged snacks, and you have control over the quality of the ingredients.

Conclusion

Homemade granola is a delicious and nutritious snack that kids will love. By involving your little ones in the cooking process, you can create cherished family memories while teaching them valuable skills. Whether enjoyed as a snack or added to yogurt or milk, homemade granola is a versatile and wholesome treat that will keep your kids satisfied and fueled for their adventures.

So, gather your ingredients, get the kids involved, and enjoy the process of making your very own homemade granola!
