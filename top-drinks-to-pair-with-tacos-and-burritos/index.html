Top Drinks to Pair with Tacos and Burritos


When it comes to indulging in tacos and burritos, it’s easy to focus solely on the flavors packed into those handheld delights. Yet, what many forget is that the perfect drink pairing can elevate the experience to an entirely new level. Whether you’re craving something bold and vibrant to match a spicy salsa or seeking a refreshing accompaniment to tame the heat, pairing your tacos and burritos with the right drink is key to enhancing the overall meal.

Explore this curated list of drink options designed to complement the rich diversity of taco and burrito flavors. From classic beverages to creative concoctions, you’ll find suggestions to satisfy every palate.

1. Classic Margarita: The Eternal Taco Companion

Few beverages are as synonymous with tacos as the classic margarita. Made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, this cocktail strikes the perfect balance between tartness and sweetness. Margaritas pair exceptionally well with tacos and burritos that feature bold flavors, such as carne asada or spicy al pastor. The citrusy notes cut through the richness of the meat and provide a refreshing contrast.

To elevate your margarita game, experiment with variations like jalapeño-infused margaritas for a spicy kick or a blood orange margarita for a fruity twist. Either way, this timeless cocktail enhances the zest of Mexican cuisine beautifully.

2. Mexican Beer: Crisp and Easy Pairing

Mexican beers are a go-to option for pairing with tacos and burritos. Known for their light flavor profiles, beer options like Corona, Pacifico, Modelo Especial, and Tecate effortlessly complement the robust flavors of the dish without overwhelming your taste buds. Their slight bitterness is particularly effective in balancing the greasiness of crispy fried fish tacos or carnitas burritos.

For an extra burst of flavor, consider turning your beer into a michelada—a savory Mexican drink made by combining beer, lime juice, tomato juice, Worcestershire sauce, and spices. A michelada adds depth and zing, making it a thrilling accomplice to spicy dishes.

3. Agua Fresca: For a Non-Alcoholic Delight

Not everyone enjoys alcoholic beverages, and for those seeking a lighter, non-alcoholic option, agua fresca is a phenomenal choice. These refreshing fruit-based drinks are made by blending fresh fruits, water, and a touch of sugar, offering a natural sweetness that pairs wonderfully with tacos and burritos.

Popular flavors include watermelon, mango, pineapple, and hibiscus (known as Jamaica). For dishes featuring tangy mole sauces or smoky barbacoa, try a tamarind-flavored agua fresca, as its sweet-tart profile complements earthy and savory notes beautifully.

4. Paloma: A Tequila Lover’s Dream

While margaritas often steal the spotlight, the Paloma deserves equal attention for its taco and burrito pairing prowess. This cocktail combines tequila, freshly squeezed grapefruit juice, lime juice, and soda for a refreshing burst of citrus flavor that complements seafood tacos and lighter burritos stuffed with vegetables or grilled chicken.

To add flair, rim your glass with a mixture of salt and chili powder for a subtly spicy finish that enhances the drink’s vibrant citrus profile.

5. Horchata: Creamy Goodness to Balance Spices

Horchata is one of the most beloved drinks in Mexican cuisine, and its creamy, cinnamon-spiced flavor profile makes it a perfect pairing for tacos and burritos laden with heat. Made from rice, almonds, or other grains, horchata is both soothing and indulgent. It works wonders with dishes featuring bold spice blends or smoky chipotle sauces.

For vegan diners, opt for plant-based versions of horchata made with almond or oat milk. The natural sweetness of the drink makes it a great choice for balancing spicier fillings like chorizo or chipotle shrimp.

6. Tequila Shots: Dive Straight Into Flavor

Tequila isn’t just reserved for cocktails—it shines bright on its own when paired properly with tacos and burritos. If you’re feeling adventurous, a neat margarita-style pairing can come in the form of a tequila shot accompanied by lime and salt. A high-quality blanco or reposado tequila complements the earthy tones of beef or pork while holding its own against spicy salsas.

For an added touch, select artisanal tequilas with nuanced flavors, such as hints of vanilla or citrus, to match the complexity of your dish.

7. Cerveza Negra: Ideal for Hearty Burritos

For burritos packed with rich fillings like barbacoa, mole, or birria, a dark Mexican beer—or cerveza negra—is the perfect match. These beers, such as Negra Modelo, often feature toasty and slightly sweet notes that harmonize beautifully with the earthy flavors of stewed or grilled meats.

The deeper flavor profiles of these beers hold up well against the heavier, richer elements of burritos stuffed with beans, cheese, and slow-cooked proteins.

8. Wine Pairings: Dare to Be Different

Wine isn’t the first beverage that comes to mind when dining on tacos or burritos, but with careful selection, it can be a surprising success. For example, a crisp Sauvignon Blanc complements fresh fish tacos and veggie burritos with bright, grassy flavors. Meanwhile, a fruit-forward Zinfandel pairs beautifully with smoky or spicy meats, offering enough boldness to cut through richer flavors.

For creamy fillings like queso or avocado-heavy dishes, a buttery Chardonnay or sparkling wine can provide a delightful counterpoint.

9. Mezcal Cocktails: Smoky and Sophisticated

Mezcal, tequila’s smoky cousin, is gaining popularity as a sophisticated pairing for Mexican cuisine. Its smoky depth harmonizes with grilled and charred taco fillings like carne asada or chipotle-marinated proteins. Cocktail options like the Mezcal Mule (mezcal, ginger beer, lime juice) or a Mezcal Negroni balance smokiness with sweetness for a dynamic pairing.

If sipping mezcal neat, garnish with an orange slice or lime wedge, and don’t forget to savor the complexity as its flavors unfold alongside your meal.

10. Sparkling Water Infusions: A Simple, Refreshing Option

Sometimes all you need is a light, effervescent drink to balance out the rich elements of tacos and burritos. Flavored sparkling waters infused with lime, cucumber, or berry essence act as a palate cleanser, especially if you’re sampling a variety of taco styles. They’re an excellent choice for those seeking a health-conscious or alcohol-free option to enjoy alongside their meal.

Making the Perfect Pairing

Ultimately, pairing drinks with tacos and burritos is all about harmonizing and contrasting flavors to elevate the dining experience. Consider the spices, sauces, and proteins in the dish and choose drinks that enhance those elements. Whether you’re a fan of boozy cocktails or prefer refreshing non-alcoholic options, there’s no shortage of pairing possibilities.

Next time you’re hosting taco night or grabbing burritos from your favorite taco truck, don’t underestimate the power of a perfect drink. The right beverage can tie together the flavors on your plate and create a more memorable meal filled with delicious synergy. Cheers to celebrating the magic of Mexican cuisine!
