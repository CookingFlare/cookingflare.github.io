Spanish Inspired Pimientos de Padron Recipe


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine, you’re likely familiar with the delightful tapas that are so integral to the dining experience in Spain. One such tapas dish that has gained popularity around the world is Pimientos de Padron. These green peppers, known for their mild and slightly sweet flavor, are typically fried and served as a simple yet flavorful appetizer. Today, I’m excited to share with you my take on this classic Spanish dish, with a few twists to make it even more irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound Pimientos de Padron peppers
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt, to taste
  • Optional: Flaky sea salt for finishing

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Peppers:

    • Rinse the Pimientos de Padron peppers under cold water and pat them dry with a kitchen towel. It’s important to ensure that the peppers are completely dry before cooking to achieve a nice blistered exterior.

  2. Heat the Pan:

    • Place a large skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat and add the olive oil. Allow the oil to heat up for a minute or two until it shimmers slightly.

  3. Fry the Peppers:

    • Carefully add the peppers to the hot pan in a single layer. Be cautious, as the oil may splatter a bit upon adding the peppers. Let the peppers cook undisturbed for 2-3 minutes, allowing them to blister and char on the bottom.

  4. Toss and Cook:

    • After a few minutes, use tongs to toss the peppers and redistribute them in the pan. Continue cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the peppers are blistered and softened to your liking.

  5. Season and Serve:

    • Once the peppers are cooked, transfer them to a serving plate and sprinkle with sea salt. For an extra touch of flavor and texture, you can also sprinkle some flaky sea salt over the peppers before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Pimientos de Padron are best enjoyed hot, straight from the pan. They make a wonderful appetizer or side dish, particularly when served alongside a glass of Spanish wine or a refreshing cocktail. The simplicity of this dish allows it to pair well with a wide range of flavors, making it a versatile addition to any meal.

Tips and Variations

  • Charred Lemon Aioli: For a creamy and zesty accompaniment, consider serving the Pimientos de Padron with a charred lemon aioli. Simply grill a lemon until charred, then squeeze the juice into a bowl of mayonnaise and mix well.

  • Spicy Variation: If you prefer a bit of heat, you can experiment with adding a sprinkle of smoked paprika or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the peppers during the cooking process.

  • Grilled Preparation: While the traditional method involves frying, you can also achieve fantastic results by grilling the peppers on a hot grill or barbecue. This method imparts a delightful smokiness to the peppers.

Final Thoughts

Pimientos de Padron is a prime example of the beauty of simplicity in cooking. With just a few quality ingredients and a bit of care, you can create a dish that delights the senses and transports you to the sun-drenched streets of Spain. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So, grab a pan, heat up some oil, and savor the irresistible flavor of these Spanish-inspired peppers. ¡Buen provecho!
