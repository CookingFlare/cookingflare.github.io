Classic Greek Tiropita: A Cheese and Filo Pastry Delight


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine, then you’ve probably tried or at least heard of tiropita. This classic Greek dish combines two beloved ingredients: cheese and filo pastry. With its flaky layers and rich, cheesy filling, tiropita is a true delight for the taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll take a closer look at how to make this mouthwatering treat from scratch.

Ingredients

To make tiropita, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 200g ricotta cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 12 sheets of filo pastry
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, eggs, parsley, dill, mint, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. Lay one sheet of filo pastry on a clean surface and brush it with melted butter. Repeat this process with five more sheets, layering them on top of each other and brushing each sheet with butter.

  4. Spoon the cheese mixture onto the layered filo pastry, spreading it evenly. Make sure to leave a small border around the edges.

  5. Repeat step 3 with the remaining six sheets of filo pastry, layering them on top of the cheese filling and brushing each sheet with butter.

  6. Fold the edges of the bottom layer of filo pastry over the top layer to seal the tiropita. Brush the top with more melted butter.

  7. Using a sharp knife, cut the tiropita into squares or triangles, depending on your preference.

  8. Place the tiropita in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.

  9. Remove the tiropita from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Tiropita can be enjoyed as a main dish or as part of a meze platter. It pairs well with a fresh Greek salad and some tzatziki sauce for dipping. You can also serve it as an appetizer at parties or gatherings.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese in the filling. Traditional tiropita uses feta and ricotta, but you can also add some grated Parmesan or Gruyere for extra flavor.
  • If you don’t have access to fresh herbs, you can use dried herbs instead. Just remember to adjust the quantities accordingly, as dried herbs are more potent.
  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add some sautéed spinach or caramelized onions to the cheese filling.
  • Leftover tiropita can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Simply reheat it in the oven or microwave before serving.

Enjoy the Flavors of Greece!

Now that you have the recipe for classic Greek tiropita, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get cooking. This cheese and filo pastry delight is sure to impress your family and friends with its delicious taste and beautiful presentation. So go ahead, embrace your inner Greek chef, and enjoy the flavors of Greece right in your own kitchen!
