5 Iconic Swedish Dishes to Celebrate Midsummer


Midsummer in Sweden is a festival of light, warmth, and community, celebrated each June with joyous gatherings, traditional music, and, of course, an abundance of delicious food. As the sun barely sets and flowers bloom in vibrant colors, families and friends come together to mark the longest day of the year with festivities. Central to the Midsummer celebration is a rich tapestry of iconic Swedish dishes that reflect the country’s culinary heritage. Here’s a guide to five must-try dishes that will elevate your Midsummer festivities.

1. Herring in Various Forms (Sill)

No Midsummer table is complete without herring, a quintessential Swedish dish that captures the essence of traditional cuisine. Herring can be prepared in numerous ways, but the most popular are pickled, marinated, and fried.

Pickled Herring

Pickled herring (sill) is available in a variety of flavors, such as mustard, onion, and dill. To prepare your own pickled herring at home, follow this simple recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 500g herring fillets
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 10-12 whole black peppercorns
  • 4-5 whole allspice berries
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • Fresh dill (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the herring fillets under cold water and pat them dry.
  2. In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, and sugar. Heat gently until the sugar dissolves, then remove from heat.
  3. Add the sliced onion, peppercorns, allspice, bay leaves, and dill to the mixture.
  4. Layer the herring fillets in a glass jar and pour the pickling liquid over them.
  5. Seal the jar and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before serving.

Serve your pickled herring with boiled new potatoes, sour cream, and a sprinkle of chives for an authentic Swedish experience.

2. Boiled New Potatoes (Nypotatis)

Boiled new potatoes are a staple at Midsummer celebrations, providing a perfect complement to herring and other dishes. These potatoes are typically small and have a tender, buttery flavor.

How to Prepare

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg new potatoes
  • Fresh dill
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Scrub the new potatoes to remove any dirt, as they are often sold unpeeled.
  2. Place the potatoes in a pot and cover with cold water. Add a generous amount of salt.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the potatoes are fork-tender, approximately 15-20 minutes.
  4. Drain and let the potatoes sit for a few minutes to dry.
  5. Serve warm, garnished with fresh dill.

These boiled new potatoes are best enjoyed with butter and dill, enhancing their natural flavor and making them irresistible.

3. Swedish Meatballs (Köttbullar)

Swedish meatballs are beloved around the world and a must-have during Midsummer gatherings. These savory morsels are typically served with creamy gravy and lingonberry sauce, offering a delightful balance of flavors.

Classic Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground beef
  • 250g ground pork
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Butter for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the breadcrumbs with milk and let them soak for a few minutes.
  2. In a skillet, sauté the chopped onion in a bit of butter until soft. Let cool.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, soaked breadcrumbs, sautéed onion, egg, allspice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Form small balls (about 1 inch in diameter) and set aside.
  5. In a frying pan, heat butter and fry the meatballs until browned on all sides and cooked through.
  6. Serve with creamy gravy made from pan drippings, and don’t forget the lingonberry sauce for a traditional touch.

These meatballs are comfort food at its finest and are sure to be a hit at your Midsummer feast.

4. Gravlax (Gravlax)

Gravlax is a traditional Swedish dish made from salmon cured with a mixture of salt, sugar, and dill. This dish is not only delicious but also a stunning centerpiece for any Midsummer table.

Simple Gravlax Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg of salmon fillet, skin on
  • 100g coarse sea salt
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 bunch of fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tsp white pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the salt, sugar, dill, and white pepper.
  2. Place the salmon fillet skin-side down on a large piece of plastic wrap. Rub the cure mixture all over the salmon.
  3. Wrap the salmon tightly in the plastic wrap and place it in a tray. Put a weight on top to help with the curing process.
  4. Refrigerate for 48 hours, turning the salmon every 12 hours.
  5. Once cured, slice the salmon thinly at a diagonal and serve with mustard sauce (hovmästarsås), on crispbread, or alongside new potatoes.

Gravlax is not only delicious but also visually appealing, making it a perfect addition to your Midsummer spread.

5. Strawberries with Cream (Jordgubbar med Grädde)

No Midsummer celebration can conclude without a sweet touch, and strawberries with cream is the quintessential dessert for this occasion. When strawberries are in season, they are at their sweetest and most flavorful.

Preparation

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh strawberries, hulled
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (to taste)
  • 300ml heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the hulled strawberries with sugar. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to release their juices.
  2. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. If desired, add vanilla extract for flavor.
  3. Serve the strawberries in individual bowls, topped with a generous dollop of whipped cream.

This simple yet delightful dessert perfectly captures the essence of summer and is the ideal way to end your Midsummer feast.

Conclusion

Celebrating Midsummer in Sweden is a feast for the senses, and these five iconic dishes are at the heart of the festivities. From the tangy pickled herring to the sweet strawberries with cream, each dish reflects the flavors of the season and the warmth of gathering with loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a Midsummer party or just want to bring a taste of Sweden to your table, these recipes will surely delight your guests and create lasting memories. Enjoy the sunshine, the laughter, and the delectable food that brings us all together! Happy Midsummer!
