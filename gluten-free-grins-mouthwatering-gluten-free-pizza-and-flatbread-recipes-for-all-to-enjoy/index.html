Gluten-Free Grins: Mouthwatering Gluten-Free Pizza and Flatbread Recipes for All to Enjoy


Are you tired of feeling left out when it comes to enjoying delicious pizzas and flatbreads with your friends and family? Well, fret no more! Say goodbye to those gluten-filled days and hello to a whole new world of tantalizing, mouthwatering gluten-free pizza and flatbread recipes that everyone will love. Let’s dive right into the goodness and explore some scrumptious, inventive creations that cater to those with gluten sensitivities and allergies.

Fluffy Gluten-Free Pizza Dough

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups of gluten-free all-purpose flour blend
  2. 2 tsp of baking powder
  3. 1/2 tsp of xanthan gum (ensure your flour blend already contains xanthan gum, and omit if it does)
  4. 1 tsp of salt
  5. 1/2 cup of warm water
  6. 2 tbsp of olive oil
  7. 1 egg, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit (220 degrees Celsius) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, xanthan gum (if not included in the blend), and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm water, olive oil, and beaten egg. Slowly pour in the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients while mixing continuously.
  4. Once the dough forms, place it on a well-floured surface and gently knead for 2-3 minutes. The dough should be smooth and not too sticky.
  5. Split the dough into two equal parts and shape them into tight balls. Cover them with a damp cloth and let them rest for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Roll out each dough ball on a floured surface to your desired thickness and transfer them to the prepared baking tray.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown. Let them cool for a couple of minutes before adding your toppings.

Topping Suggestions:

  1. Vegan Tomato Sauce: Sautee 2 minced garlic cloves in a little olive oil for a minute. Add in a can of tomato sauce, 1 tsp dried basil, and salt & pepper to taste. Cook until heated through.
  2. Margherita Pizza: Layer your favorite vegan cheese, fresh basil leaves, and juicy cherry tomatoes atop your pizza before baking for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Spicy Hawaiian Pizza: Top your flatbread with BBQ sauce instead of tomato sauce, vegan-friendly ham slices, pineapple pieces, and red pepper flakes to add a kick.

Quinoa & Chia Seed Flatbreads

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup of cooked quinoa
  2. 1/3 cup of chia seeds
  3. 1/2 cup of water
  4. 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  5. 2 cloves of minced garlic
  6. 1/4 cup of olive oil
  7. Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by cooking the quinoa according to package instructions and allowing it to cool. Meanwhile, mix the chia seeds and water in a bowl, let the mixture sit for 10 minutes or until it thickens (forming a “chia gel”).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chia gel, parsley, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  3. Scoop about 1/3 cup of the mixture onto a prepped baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Flatten the balls slightly to create a disk shape, and then use a fork to press down, creating small perforations.
  4. Bake at 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius) for 25-30 minutes or until the edges turn golden brown. Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before enjoying.

Topping Suggestions:

  1. Vegan White Pizza: Spread a generous amount of cashew “ricotta” (blend soaked cashews, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and salt in a food processor) on the flatbread, followed by sautéed spinach and artichoke hearts. Bake for 5-7 minutes, then drizzle with a little olive oil and sprinkle with red pepper flakes.
  2. Roasted Veggie Flatbread: Cut up your favorite veggies (zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and cherry tomatoes), toss them with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast in a preheated oven at 400 degrees Fahrenheit (200 degrees Celsius) for 20-25 minutes. Spread a thin layer of vegan pesto on your flatbread and layer your roasted veggies on top before baking for an additional 10 minutes.

Now that you have mouthwatering recipes at your disposal, include your gluten-sensitive friends in your pizza night and flatbread feasts! Share the love, and embrace the joy of sharing great food with others. Remember, a life without gluten doesn’t mean a life without delectable and scrumptious treats. After all, life is too short not to indulge in some gluten-free goodness!
