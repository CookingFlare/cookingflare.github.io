Creamy Garlic Aioli: A Homemade Condiment for Garlic Lovers


Aioli, a classic Provençal condiment, is a staple in many Mediterranean cuisines. Its creamy texture and rich flavor make it a perfect accompaniment to various dishes, from grilled meats to vegetables and bread. In this recipe, we’ll explore the world of garlic aioli, a variation that elevates the traditional recipe by incorporating the pungency of garlic.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) neutral-tasting oil, such as canola or grapeseed
  • 1/4 cup (60 ml) freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley or chives for garnish

Instructions

To begin, combine the oil, lemon juice, garlic, egg yolk, and Dijon mustard in a blender or food processor. Blend the mixture on low speed until smooth, stopping occasionally to scrape down the sides of the blender. This will help to ensure that all the ingredients are well incorporated.

Transfer the mixture to a bowl and whisk in salt and pepper to taste. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

After the mixture has chilled, give it a good stir and taste. If it’s too thick, you can thin it out with a little more lemon juice or oil. If it’s too thin, you can add a little more egg yolk. Adjust the seasoning as needed.

Tips and Variations

  • For a lighter aioli, use less garlic or omit the garlic altogether. For a stronger flavor, use more garlic or add roasted garlic puree.
  • To make a smoky aioli, add 1-2 teaspoons of smoked paprika or chipotle peppers in adobo sauce.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices, such as chopped fresh parsley, dill, or cilantro, or ground cumin, coriander, or paprika.
  • For a vegan aioli, replace the egg yolk with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water) or aquafaba (the liquid from canned chickpeas).

Storage and Shelf Life

Homemade aioli can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. It’s best to use it within 3-4 days for optimal flavor and texture. If you notice the aioli has separated or developed an off smell, it’s best to discard it and make a fresh batch.

Serving Suggestions

Creamy garlic aioli is a versatile condiment that can be used in a variety of dishes. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Use it as a dip for crudités, crackers, or bread
  • Spread it on sandwiches, wraps, or burgers
  • Top grilled meats, fish, or vegetables with a dollop of aioli
  • Mix it with mayonnaise or sour cream for a tasty dip
  • Add it to pasta sauces, soups, or stir-fries for an extra boost of flavor

In conclusion, homemade creamy garlic aioli is a simple yet impressive condiment that can elevate many dishes. With its rich, creamy texture and pungent garlic flavor, it’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead, give it a try, and discover the world of garlic aioli for yourself!
