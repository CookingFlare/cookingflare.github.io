Cooking Greek Style with Herbs and Spices


Greek cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, aromas, and textures, deeply rooted in tradition and influenced by the Mediterranean landscape. One of the most captivating aspects of Greek cooking is its emphasis on fresh herbs and spices, which elevate simple ingredients into extraordinary dishes. This blog post will guide you through the enchanting world of Greek flavors, offering tips, techniques, and a selection of delightful recipes that showcase the magic of herbs and spices in Greek cooking.

The Essence of Greek Cuisine

At the heart of Greek cuisine lies the philosophy of using fresh, seasonal ingredients. This approach not only ensures the best flavors but also aligns with healthy eating practices. Greeks have a profound appreciation for herbs and spices, using them not just for flavor but also for their nutritional and medicinal properties. Common herbs such as oregano, thyme, rosemary, and dill are staples in Greek kitchens, while spices like cinnamon, allspice, and nutmeg add warmth and depth to dishes.

The Key Herbs and Spices in Greek Cooking

Understanding the essential herbs and spices used in Greek cooking is the first step to embracing this culinary tradition. Here’s a closer look at some of the most popular ones:

  • Oregano: Often considered the king of Greek herbs, oregano is used in everything from marinades to salads. Its robust flavor pairs beautifully with meats, vegetables, and, of course, the classic Greek salad.

  • Dill: This delicate herb is a favorite in Greek cuisine, especially in seafood dishes and dips like tzatziki. Its fresh, anise-like flavor complements yogurt and cucumber perfectly.

  • Thyme: Thyme adds an earthy, aromatic quality to dishes. It’s commonly used in roasted meats, stews, and as a seasoning for vegetables.

  • Rosemary: With its strong, woody scent, rosemary is ideal for roasted meats and potatoes. Its flavor is bold, making it a favorite in marinades and dressings.

  • Mint: Often associated with desserts, mint also finds its way into savory dishes, such as stuffed grape leaves (dolmades) and salads.

  • Cinnamon: This warm spice is used in both sweet and savory dishes. In Greek cooking, it’s often found in meat stews, pastries, and desserts like baklava.

  • Paprika: Adding a hint of sweetness and color, paprika is frequently used in various Greek dishes, particularly in sauces and stews.

  • Allspice: This aromatic spice brings warmth and complexity, often found in meat dishes and some traditional desserts.

Essential Cooking Techniques

Greek cooking celebrates simplicity and the beauty of fresh ingredients. Here are some essential techniques to master:

Marinating

Marinating is a critical step in Greek cooking that infuses meat and vegetables with flavor. A classic Greek marinade typically includes olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and a generous amount of herbs. Allowing your ingredients to marinate for several hours or overnight enhances the depth of flavor.

Grilling

Grilling is a popular cooking method in Greece, especially during the summer months. Whether it’s meats, vegetables, or even seafood, grilling adds a smoky flavor that complements the freshness of the ingredients. Be sure to preheat your grill and brush the grates with olive oil to prevent sticking.

Baking

Baking is another fundamental technique, especially for dishes like moussaka and spanakopita. These meals often feature layers of flavor, with herbs and spices enhancing the overall taste. Baking allows the ingredients to meld beautifully, creating comforting, hearty dishes.

Sautéing

Sautéing is a quick and effective way to bring out the flavors of herbs and spices. Starting with olive oil and garlic, you can add your chosen ingredients, allowing them to cook until tender and fragrant. This technique is often used for side dishes and sauces.

Recipes to Try

Greek Salad with Herb Vinaigrette

Ingredients:

  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 100g olives (Kalamata preferred)
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, feta cheese, and olives.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately as a refreshing side dish or light meal.

Grilled Lemon-Oregano Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh oregano, chopped (or 2 teaspoons dried oregano)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place chicken thighs in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Remove chicken from the marinade and grill for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through and the skin is crispy.
  5. Let rest for a few minutes before serving. Pair with a Greek salad for a complete meal.

Spanakopita (Spinach Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 400g spinach, fresh or frozen, thawed and drained
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 package phyllo pastry (about 20 sheets)
  • 100g butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onion until soft. Add the spinach and cook until wilted (if using fresh). Remove from heat and allow to cool.
  3. In a bowl, combine the spinach mixture with feta cheese, dill, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Lay one sheet of phyllo pastry on a clean surface and brush with melted butter. Place another sheet on top and brush again. Repeat until you have about 5 layers.
  5. Place a generous amount of the spinach filling along one edge and roll the phyllo pastry to form a log. Seal the ends and place on a baking sheet.
  6. Brush the top with more melted butter and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Moussaka

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • 500g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon allspice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce (see below)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Béchamel Sauce:

  • 50g butter
  • 50g flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (Kefalotyri or Parmesan)
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Salt the eggplant slices and let them sit for 30 minutes to draw out moisture. Rinse and pat dry.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until soft. Add the ground meat, breaking it apart, and cook until browned. Stir in diced tomatoes, cinnamon, allspice, parsley, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 15 minutes.
  4. In a separate pan, prepare the béchamel sauce by melting butter, whisking in flour, and gradually adding milk until thickened. Stir in cheese, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  5. In a baking dish, layer half of the eggplant, followed by the meat mixture, then the remaining eggplant. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top.
  6. Bake for 45 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

Greek Yogurt with Honey and Walnuts

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Divide the Greek yogurt into serving bowls.
  2. Drizzle honey over the yogurt.
  3. Top with chopped walnuts and a sprinkle of cinnamon if desired.
  4. Serve as a delicious dessert or breakfast option.

Conclusion

Cooking Greek style with herbs and spices is not just a culinary adventure; it’s an invitation to embrace a lifestyle rich in flavor, health, and tradition. By incorporating these essential herbs and spices into your cooking, you can transform everyday meals into extraordinary experiences that celebrate the vibrant culture of Greece.

Experiment with the recipes provided, and don’t hesitate to adapt them to your taste. The beauty of Greek cuisine lies in its flexibility and the joy of sharing delicious food with loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet meal at home, the flavors of Greece will transport you to sun-drenched shores and bustling tavernas. Enjoy the journey of cooking and savor every bite!
