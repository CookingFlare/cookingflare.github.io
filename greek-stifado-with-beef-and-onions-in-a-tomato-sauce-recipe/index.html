Greek Stifado with Beef and Onions in a Tomato Sauce Recipe


If you’re looking to take your taste buds on a trip to the Mediterranean, look no further than this delicious Greek Stifado recipe. This hearty dish combines tender beef, caramelized onions, and a rich tomato sauce to create a truly satisfying meal. With its deep flavors and comforting aroma, Greek Stifado is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg beef, cut into cubes
  • 1 kg onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 400 g canned tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for cooking

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned on all sides. This will help seal in the juices and add depth to the flavor. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the sliced onions and cook over medium heat until they start to caramelize. Stir frequently to prevent them from sticking to the bottom of the pot. This step is crucial as it adds a sweet and savory flavor to the dish.

  3. Once the onions are golden brown, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter.

  4. Return the beef to the pot and add the canned tomatoes, tomato paste, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cloves, dried oregano, dried thyme, sugar, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together. The slow cooking process will ensure the meat becomes fork-tender and the sauce develops a rich taste.

  6. While the stifado is simmering, make sure to check on it occasionally, stirring gently to prevent sticking. If the liquid reduces too much, you can add a little water to maintain the desired consistency.

  7. Once the stifado is ready, remove the bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

  8. Serve the Greek Stifado hot, accompanied by your choice of side dish. It pairs well with fluffy rice, creamy mashed potatoes, or crusty bread. The choice is yours!

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve just prepared a delectable Greek Stifado with beef and onions in a flavorful tomato sauce. This traditional Greek dish is perfect for cozy family dinners or special occasions. The tender beef, caramelized onions, and aromatic spices come together to create a symphony of flavors that will transport you straight to the sunny shores of Greece. So grab a fork, dig in, and enjoy the taste of the Mediterranean in your own home.

Remember, cooking is all about experimenting and making the dish your own. Don’t be afraid to add your personal touch by adjusting the seasoning or adding extra spices. Just have fun with it! And if you have any leftovers, the stifado will taste even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. So go ahead, give this Greek Stifado recipe a try, and let your taste buds embark on a culinary adventure.
