Easter Recipes for a Southern-Style Celebration


Easter is a time for family, reflection, and, of course, indulgent meals that bring everyone together. If you’re hosting a Southern-style celebration, you know the importance of making each dish count. With bold flavors, rich traditions, and plenty of comfort food, a Southern Easter feast is a celebration of the season’s best produce, meats, and baked goods.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through some of the most popular Southern-inspired Easter recipes that will fill your table with delicious dishes, from starters to desserts. Whether you’re hosting a crowd or enjoying a quiet meal with family, these recipes are sure to make your Easter memorable.

Traditional Southern Ham

No Southern Easter celebration is complete without a beautiful, glazed ham gracing the center of the table. Ham is a quintessential dish, bringing a rich, smoky flavor that perfectly complements the lighter sides of the meal. To make a standout ham, begin with a bone-in, spiral-cut ham for a simple yet impressive presentation. The glaze should be sweet, tangy, and just a little bit spicy for that perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 spiral-cut bone-in ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar
  • ¼ cup bourbon (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F.
  2. Place the ham in a roasting pan, cut side down.
  3. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, bourbon, Worcestershire sauce, honey, and cloves. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture has thickened slightly.
  4. Brush the glaze generously over the ham, covering it completely.
  5. Roast the ham, basting with the glaze every 20 minutes, until heated through and caramelized, about 2 to 2.5 hours.
  6. Let the ham rest for 15 minutes before carving.

This ham pairs beautifully with classic Southern sides and is perfect for Easter.

Creamy Southern Grits

Grits are a staple in Southern cuisine, and they are the perfect dish to accompany ham or any other Easter entrée. Whether you serve them plain, cheesy, or with a little extra flair, grits are always a crowd-pleaser. For this recipe, we’re taking it up a notch with a creamy, cheesy version that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup stone-ground grits
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Bring the water or chicken broth to a boil in a large saucepan. Gradually add the grits, stirring constantly to prevent clumping.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Simmer the grits for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they’re tender and thickened.
  3. Stir in the butter, cheddar cheese, and heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve hot with a garnish of additional cheese or green onions if desired.

These creamy grits are a perfect side dish to complement the richness of the ham and the lightness of other Easter sides.

Southern Biscuits

No Southern meal is complete without a basket of fresh biscuits, and Easter is no exception. These biscuits are buttery, flaky, and soft, with just the right amount of rise. Serve them with honey, jam, or alongside your ham for a true Southern experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • ¾ cup buttermilk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter for brushing

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cut the cold butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add the buttermilk and stir until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold the dough in half and pat it down again. Repeat this folding and patting process two more times to create flaky layers.
  6. Use a round cutter to cut out biscuits and place them on a baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown.
  8. Brush with melted butter before serving.

These biscuits are the perfect addition to any Southern Easter spread. Serve them fresh from the oven for the best results!

Southern Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs are a Southern favorite at nearly every holiday meal, and Easter is no exception. They’re simple to make, can be prepared in advance, and are always a hit with guests. With a creamy filling and a sprinkle of paprika, these deviled eggs are a classic side dish that never disappoints.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Paprika for garnish

Directions:

  1. Place the eggs in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the eggs from the pot and cool them under cold running water.
  3. Once the eggs are cool, peel them and slice them in half lengthwise. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
  4. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white wine vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth and creamy.
  5. Spoon or pipe the filling back into the egg whites.
  6. Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika and serve.

These deviled eggs are a must-have at any Easter gathering, and their creamy, tangy filling is always a favorite.

Southern Collard Greens

Collard greens are a Southern staple and an essential dish for any Easter spread. With their rich, earthy flavor and slightly bitter taste, they balance out the sweetness of the ham and other dishes. These greens are cooked low and slow with a bit of ham hock for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds collard greens, washed and chopped
  • 1 smoked ham hock
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a little oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the ham hock, chicken broth, sugar, and collard greens. Stir to combine.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook, covered, for 1-1.5 hours, until the greens are tender and the flavors have melded together.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

Collard greens are a perfect Southern side that adds both flavor and tradition to your Easter meal.

Classic Southern Pecan Pie

For dessert, you can’t go wrong with a classic Southern pecan pie. With its gooey filling and crunchy pecans, this pie is the epitome of Southern sweetness. It’s the perfect way to end your Easter meal on a high note.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups pecan halves

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Stir in the pecans until evenly distributed.
  4. Pour the mixture into the pie crust.
  5. Bake for 50-55 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.
  6. Let the pie cool completely before slicing and serving.

This rich, indulgent pie is the perfect way to cap off a Southern Easter meal.

Conclusion

A Southern-style Easter celebration is a feast for the senses, with delicious flavors, comforting dishes, and cherished traditions. From the savory main course of glazed ham to the decadent pecan pie for dessert, every bite tells the story of Southern hospitality and home-cooked goodness. Whether you’re cooking for a large crowd or a smaller family gathering, these recipes will ensure your Easter celebration is filled with warmth, joy, and unforgettable meals.

So gather your loved ones, get the ingredients ready, and let the magic of Southern cooking fill your home this Easter!
