Instant Pot Beef and Vegetable Soup: Hearty and Nourishing


There’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of beef and vegetable soup to warm you up on a chilly day. This hearty, nourishing soup is packed with tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and rich, flavorful broth. What’s even better? You can make it effortlessly in your Instant Pot! This recipe not only saves time but also preserves the nutrients and flavors, making it a perfect addition to your weekly meal plan.

Ingredients

To create this delicious Instant Pot Beef and Vegetable Soup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Beef and Broth Base

  • 1 pound of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Vegetables

  • 3 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup of green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1 cup of corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes, undrained

Instructions

Preparing the Beef and Onions

  1. Sear the Beef: Set your Instant Pot to the ‘Sauté’ setting and allow it to heat up. Add the olive oil. Once hot, add the beef stew meat. Brown the meat on all sides, which should take about 5 minutes. This step adds depth of flavor to your soup.

  2. Cook the Onions and Garlic: Add the chopped onion to the pot and sauté for another 3-4 minutes until they become translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

Adding Broth and Seasonings

  1. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in a cup of water to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. These bits are full of flavor and will enhance your soup.

  2. Add Broth and Seasonings: Pour in the beef broth. Add the bay leaf, dried thyme, dried rosemary, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

Incorporating Vegetables

  1. Layer the Vegetables: Add the sliced carrots, chopped celery, diced potatoes, green beans, peas, corn, and diced tomatoes with their juice. Do not stir after adding the tomatoes as this can cause a burn warning in some models of Instant Pots.

Cooking the Soup

  1. Pressure Cook: Secure the lid on your Instant Pot, ensuring the valve is set to the ‘Sealing’ position. Select the ‘Pressure Cook’ or ‘Manual’ setting and set the timer for 20 minutes on high pressure.

  2. Natural Release: Once the cooking cycle is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully switch the valve to ‘Venting’ to release any remaining pressure. Remove the lid once safe to do so.

Final Touches

  1. Taste and Adjust Seasoning: Remove the bay leaf from the soup. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. If you prefer a thicker soup, you can mash some of the potatoes with a fork or use an immersion blender to blend a small portion of the soup.

  2. Serve: Ladle the hot, hearty soup into bowls and serve immediately. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

Tips for Making the Best Beef and Vegetable Soup

  • Quality Ingredients: Use fresh, high-quality beef and vegetables to ensure the best flavor and nutritional value.
  • Cut Even Pieces: Cut the vegetables and beef into uniform, bite-sized pieces for even cooking and a better eating experience.
  • Season Generously: Don’t be shy with the seasonings. The herbs and spices are crucial for developing a rich, flavorful broth.
  • Deglaze Properly: Make sure to deglaze the pot thoroughly to avoid any burnt bits that can affect the flavor and function of your Instant Pot.
  • Adjust Consistency: If you like a thicker soup, you can either blend a portion of it or add a slurry of cornstarch and water towards the end of the cooking time.

Nutritional Benefits

This Instant Pot Beef and Vegetable Soup is not just delicious; it’s also packed with nutrients. Here’s a breakdown of the health benefits:

  • Beef: Provides high-quality protein essential for muscle repair and growth. It’s also a good source of iron, zinc, and B vitamins.
  • Vegetables: Carrots, celery, potatoes, green beans, peas, and corn offer a wealth of vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They contribute to overall health by supporting the immune system, digestion, and cardiovascular health.
  • Tomatoes: Rich in antioxidants, particularly lycopene, which has been linked to reduced risk of heart disease and cancer.

Storing and Reheating

This soup stores well, making it ideal for meal prep.

Refrigeration

  • Store: Allow the soup to cool completely before transferring it to airtight containers. Store in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
  • Reheat: To reheat, simply microwave individual portions or warm on the stovetop over medium heat until heated through.

Freezing

  • Store: For longer storage, freeze the soup in freezer-safe containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 3 months.
  • Reheat: Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating. You can reheat directly from frozen in a microwave or on the stovetop, though it will take longer.

Serving Suggestions

Pair this hearty soup with some crusty bread or a side salad for a complete meal. Here are a few ideas:

  • Crusty Bread: A slice of warm, crusty bread is perfect for soaking up the flavorful broth.
  • Salad: A light, fresh salad with a tangy vinaigrette can complement the rich, hearty soup.
  • Grilled Cheese: A classic grilled cheese sandwich pairs wonderfully with this soup for a comforting meal.

Conclusion

This Instant Pot Beef and Vegetable Soup is a wholesome, satisfying dish that brings comfort and nutrition to your table. Its simplicity and rich flavors make it a go-to recipe for busy weeknights or cozy weekend dinners. With minimal effort and maximum flavor, this soup is sure to become a family favorite. So, grab your Instant Pot and get ready to enjoy a bowl of hearty, nourishing goodness!
