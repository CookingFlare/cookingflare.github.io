Delicious Indian Street Snacks: 7 Recipes for Every Occasion


Indian street food is a vibrant and diverse culinary world, offering a variety of mouth-watering snacks that are perfect for any occasion. From crispy and crunchy to spicy and tangy, these delightful treats are sure to tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more. In this post, we'll explore 7 delicious Indian street snack recipes that are perfect for satisfying your cravings and impressing your friends and family. Recipe 1: Paneer Tikka Rolls Paneer tikka rolls are a popular Indian street snack that consists of marinated paneer (Indian cheese) cooked in a rich and creamy tomato sauce, wrapped in a soft and fluffy bread roll. Ingredients:

250g paneer, cut into small cubes 1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon garam masala 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 large onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup tomato puree 2 tablespoons butter 6-8 bread rolls

Instructions:

In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, lemon juice, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and cayenne pepper. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan and saut√© the chopped onion until translucent. Add the marinated paneer and cook until lightly browned. Stir in the tomato puree and cook for 2-3 minutes. Serve the paneer mixture on top of a bread roll, drizzled with melted butter and seasoned with salt.

Recipe 2: Sev Puri Sev puri is a popular street snack originating from the state of Gujarat, consisting of crispy puris topped with chutneys, onions, and chutneys. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking powder 1/4 cup ghee or oil 1 cup warm water Vegetable oil, for frying Tamarind chutney Green chutney Onions, for garnish Teekisk chutney

Instructions:

Knead the flour, salt, and baking powder to form a smooth dough. Divide the dough into small portions and roll out into thin circles. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the puris until crispy. Drain the puris on paper towels. Serve the puris topped with tamarind chutney, green chutney, onions, and teekisk chutney.

Recipe 3: Raj Kachoris Raj kachoris are a popular snack from the state of Rajasthan, consisting of crispy puris filled with spiced potatoes and onions. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking powder 1/4 cup ghee or oil 1 cup warm water Vegetable oil, for frying 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed Onions, chopped Chopped cilantro Spices (amchur powder, cumin powder, coriander powder)

Instructions:

Knead the flour, salt, and baking powder to form a smooth dough. Divide the dough into small portions and roll out into thin circles. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the puris until crispy. Fill the puris with the mashed potatoes, onions, and spices. Serve the kachoris garnished with cilantro.

Recipe 4: Badaam Shrikhand Badaam shrikhand is a creamy and sweet dessert drink originating from the state of Maharashtra. Ingredients:

250g grated paneer 1 cup milk 1 cup heavy cream 1 tablespoon rose water 1 tablespoon saffron threads, soaked in 1 tablespoon milk Chopped almonds and pistachios, for garnish

Instructions:

In a blender, blend the paneer, milk, heavy cream, rose water, and saffron until smooth. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Serve the shrikhand garnished with almonds and pistachios.

Recipe 5: Mirchi Bada Mirchi bada is a spicy and delicious snack from the state of Rajasthan. Ingredients:

2 cups besan (gram flour) 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking soda 1/2 cup oil 4-6 chilies, chopped Chopped cilantro

Instructions:

Knead the besan, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and baking soda to form a stiff dough. Divide the dough into small portions and shape into small balls. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the balls until golden brown. Drain the fritters on paper towels and serve hot with chilies.

Recipe 6: Dabeli Dabeli is a popular street snack from the state of Gujarat. Ingredients:

1 cup dabeli seeds 1 cup chutney 2 tablespoons butter 2 tablespoons mustard oil 1 cup chopped onions 1 cup chopped coriander leaves Chopped nuts, for garnish

Instructions:

Heat the seeds in a pan and roast until fragrant. Grind the seeds into a fine powder. Mix the powder with chutney, butter, mustard oil, onions, and coriander leaves. Serve the mixture in a bread roll and garnish with nuts.

Recipe 7: Chivda Chivda is a crunchy and flavorful snack popular in the state of Gujarat. Ingredients:

2 cups rice flakes 1 cup puffed rice 1 cup wheat flakes 1 cup grated coconut 1/2 cup sugar Chopped nuts, for garnish Spices (cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder)

Instructions:

Mix the rice flakes, puffed rice, wheat flakes, and grated coconut in a bowl. Heat the mixture in a pan until fragrant. Stir in sugar and spices. Serve the chivda hot, garnished with nuts.

In conclusion, Indian street food is a world of flavors and textures, offering a variety of delightful snacks that cater to every taste bud. These 7 recipes showcase the diversity of Indian street food, from crispy puris to creamy desserts. Whether you're in the mood for something spicy or sweet, these recipes will surely satisfy your cravings and leave you wanting more.
