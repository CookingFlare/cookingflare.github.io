Vegan Churros: Crispy, Sweet, and Perfectly Fried


Churros are a beloved treat, known for their golden exterior, crunchy texture, and sweet cinnamon-sugar coating. Whether you’ve had them from a street vendor, at a fair, or in a cozy café, these fried pastries are a perfect indulgence. However, if you’re vegan or just prefer plant-based alternatives, making churros at home without eggs or dairy is absolutely possible. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through how to make the perfect vegan churros – crispy, sweet, and perfectly fried.

What Are Churros?

Originating in Spain, churros are a type of fried dough pastry that’s often served as a breakfast snack or dessert. They are typically enjoyed with hot chocolate for dipping, but they can also be paired with a variety of other sweet sauces. The dough is simple, usually made with water, flour, salt, and sugar, which is then deep-fried and coated in a mixture of sugar and cinnamon.

Traditionally, churros contain eggs and dairy, but creating a vegan version requires a few substitutions. Thankfully, you can still achieve that signature crispy texture and sweet flavor without the use of animal products.

Why Vegan Churros?

There are several reasons why you might want to try vegan churros:

  • Dietary preferences: Many people follow a vegan diet for ethical, environmental, or health reasons. By making churros at home, vegans can enjoy this treat without having to worry about hidden animal-derived ingredients.
  • Food allergies: For those with dairy or egg allergies, this recipe is a fantastic alternative.
  • Customization: Making churros at home gives you full control over the ingredients, so you can adjust them to your taste preferences or dietary needs.

Key Ingredients for Vegan Churros

To make the perfect vegan churros, you’ll need a few simple ingredients. Don’t worry, they’re easy to find, and you likely already have most of them in your pantry. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Churros:

  1. Water: The base of the dough. You’ll need around 1 cup (240 ml).
  2. Flour: All-purpose flour works perfectly here. It gives the churros their chewy, soft texture on the inside while staying crisp on the outside.
  3. Sugar: Just a little bit to sweeten the dough. You can use regular white sugar, coconut sugar, or any sweetener you prefer.
  4. Vegan Butter: This provides richness and a buttery flavor. If you prefer a lighter option, you can use coconut oil or another plant-based fat.
  5. Vanilla Extract: For that lovely aromatic touch. It enhances the flavor of the churros, making them even more irresistible.
  6. Salt: A pinch of salt helps to balance out the sweetness.
  7. Baking Powder: This helps the churros puff up a bit when fried, ensuring they are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

For the Cinnamon Sugar Coating:

  1. Sugar: Use regular granulated sugar or opt for a more natural option like cane sugar.
  2. Cinnamon: Ground cinnamon is essential for that warm, spicy-sweet flavor. You can increase or decrease the amount based on your preferences.

For Frying:

  • Vegetable Oil: You’ll need enough to submerge the churros and fry them to a perfect golden crisp. Choose a neutral oil with a high smoke point, like sunflower oil or canola oil.

How to Make Vegan Churros

Making vegan churros is straightforward and requires just a few simple steps. Below is a step-by-step guide to get you that crispy, golden perfection.

Step 1: Make the Dough

Start by heating the water, vegan butter, and sugar in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir it occasionally to make sure everything is well combined and the butter has melted completely. Once it’s bubbling slightly, remove the pan from the heat.

Next, sift the flour and baking powder into the saucepan. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture comes together and forms a smooth dough. The dough should pull away from the sides of the pan and form a soft ball. If it’s too sticky, you can add a bit more flour, but be careful not to add too much.

Once the dough is ready, stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt. Mix everything well until it’s fully incorporated.

Step 2: Heat the Oil

Now it’s time to heat the oil for frying. Fill a deep frying pan or a pot with vegetable oil (about 2-3 inches deep) and heat it over medium-high heat. The oil should reach a temperature of around 350°F (175°C). You can test if the oil is hot enough by dropping a small piece of dough into the oil; if it bubbles and rises to the surface, it’s ready.

If you don’t have a thermometer, you can also use a wooden spoon—dip it into the oil, and if small bubbles form around it, the oil is good to go.

Step 3: Prepare the Cinnamon Sugar Coating

While the oil is heating up, mix the sugar and cinnamon together in a shallow bowl. This coating will give the churros their signature sweetness and warmth. Set it aside until your churros are fried.

Step 4: Pipe and Fry the Churros

To pipe the churros, fill a pastry bag fitted with a large star tip (or a plastic bag with the tip cut off) with the churro dough. This star tip is essential for creating the ridged texture that churros are known for.

Once the oil is hot, carefully pipe the dough directly into the oil, forming long strips of churros. Be sure not to overcrowd the pan; fry in batches if necessary. Fry each batch for 2-3 minutes or until the churros are golden brown and crispy on the outside.

Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the churros from the oil, letting any excess oil drain off. Immediately transfer the churros to the cinnamon-sugar mixture and coat them evenly.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once coated, your vegan churros are ready to serve! They’re best enjoyed while they’re still warm and crispy. You can serve them as they are, or with a side of vegan chocolate sauce or caramel for dipping.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Churros

Making churros is relatively easy, but there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure they turn out just right:

  • Oil Temperature: It’s crucial to maintain the right oil temperature when frying. If the oil is too hot, the churros will burn before they cook through. If the oil is too cold, the churros will absorb too much oil and become greasy. Use a thermometer to monitor the temperature if you have one.
  • Do Not Overcrowd the Pan: Fry the churros in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. This ensures they cook evenly and become crispy all around.
  • Don’t Let the Dough Sit Too Long: Once the dough is ready, get frying right away. The dough should be piped into the oil while it’s still soft and malleable. If left to sit for too long, it can harden and become difficult to pipe.
  • Extra Crispiness: For extra crisp churros, you can let the churros cool for a minute after frying, then give them another quick dip in the cinnamon sugar mixture before serving.

Vegan Churros Variations

While the classic cinnamon-sugar churro is always a hit, there are plenty of ways to experiment and make this treat your own. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Chocolate Drizzle: Top your churros with a rich vegan chocolate sauce or drizzle some melted dark chocolate for an extra indulgent treat.
  2. Filled Churros: Pipe the churro dough into shorter lengths and fill them with vegan chocolate, caramel, or even fruit preserves before frying.
  3. Spiced Sugar: Add a dash of nutmeg, cardamom, or cayenne pepper to the cinnamon-sugar mix for a spicy twist.
  4. Glazed Churros: For a sweeter touch, make a vegan glaze using powdered sugar, almond milk, and vanilla extract to drizzle over the churros.

Storing and Reheating Churros

Churros are best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, you can store them. Place the cooled churros in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2-3 days. To reheat them, bake them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 5-7 minutes to restore some crispiness.

If you want to freeze churros for later, simply freeze them on a baking sheet until solid, then transfer them to a freezer-safe bag or container. When you’re ready to enjoy them, heat them in the oven for about 10-15 minutes at 350°F (175°C).

Why You’ll Love These Vegan Churros

Vegan churros are a delightful treat that can be made easily at home. With their perfectly crispy exterior, soft interior, and sweet cinnamon-sugar coating, they’re a crowd-pleaser for everyone – whether you’re vegan or not. Not only are these churros free from eggs and dairy, but they’re also customizable and fun to make. Once you try making them yourself, you may never go back to store-bought versions again!

Give this recipe a try and let the crispy, golden goodness of homemade vegan churros take your taste buds on a delicious journey. Happy frying!
