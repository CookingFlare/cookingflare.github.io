Quick and Easy BBQ Recipes for Last-Minute July 4th Plans


July 4th is one of the most anticipated holidays of the year, bringing together families, friends, and neighbors to celebrate with fireworks, barbecues, and plenty of good food. However, not everyone has the luxury of planning weeks in advance for the perfect cookout. If you’re in a pinch and need some quick and easy BBQ recipes to impress your guests without spending hours in the kitchen, this post is for you!

Whether you’re hosting a last-minute backyard party or need some delicious dishes to bring to a gathering, these BBQ recipes are simple, flavorful, and sure to please a crowd. So, fire up the grill, and let’s get cooking!

1. Grilled BBQ Chicken Drumsticks

Chicken drumsticks are a classic BBQ favorite, and they couldn’t be easier to prepare. With a smoky, sweet BBQ glaze, these drumsticks are crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, making them the perfect choice for a quick cookout.

Ingredients:

  • 10 chicken drumsticks
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Pat the chicken drumsticks dry with paper towels and season them with olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
  3. Place the drumsticks on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes on each side, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  4. Once the chicken is almost fully cooked, brush a generous amount of BBQ sauce on each drumstick and cook for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the sauce to caramelize.
  5. Remove from the grill and let rest for a few minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • You can marinate the chicken in BBQ sauce for an hour or overnight for extra flavor.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use pre-cooked drumsticks and just heat them up on the grill with the sauce.

2. Grilled BBQ Ribs

Ribs are the crown jewel of any BBQ cookout, but they don’t have to take hours to prepare. This quick and easy recipe ensures tender, flavorful ribs in a fraction of the time it typically takes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ rub (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Remove the silver skin from the ribs (this is the thin membrane on the back of the ribs) and season both sides with BBQ rub.
  3. Place the ribs on the grill over indirect heat (to the side of the grill, not directly over the flames) and cook for about 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally to prevent burning.
  4. Once the ribs are tender and slightly crispy, brush with BBQ sauce and cook for another 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to set.
  5. Remove from the grill, slice, and serve.

Tips:

  • If you’re in a rush, you can use a pressure cooker or slow cooker to cook the ribs beforehand and just finish them on the grill to develop that smoky flavor.
  • Feel free to use your favorite BBQ sauce or create a custom blend with ingredients like honey, mustard, and vinegar.

3. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Vegetables on the grill are not only delicious but also a great way to offer a lighter option at your BBQ. Grilled veggie skewers are colorful, flavorful, and can be customized to suit any taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz mushrooms, whole or halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme, or oregano)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between peppers, zucchini, onion, and mushrooms.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and fresh herbs.
  4. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and serve immediately.

Tips:

  • You can customize these veggie skewers with your favorite vegetables like cherry tomatoes, eggplant, or asparagus.
  • Soak the wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes before grilling to prevent them from burning.

4. BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwiches

Pulled pork is another BBQ classic that doesn’t need hours to prepare. By using pre-cooked pork and a bit of sauce, you can have a delicious sandwich in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked pulled pork (store-bought or leftovers)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Coleslaw (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Reheat the pulled pork in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring in BBQ sauce to coat the meat evenly.
  2. Once heated through, pile the pulled pork onto the hamburger buns.
  3. Top with coleslaw if desired and serve.

Tips:

  • If you have extra time, make your own pulled pork using a slow cooker, but the store-bought version is just as delicious and will save you time.
  • Add pickles or onions for an extra tangy kick.

5. Grilled Shrimp Skewers

For a lighter seafood option, grilled shrimp skewers are a perfect addition to your BBQ spread. They’re fast to cook and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  4. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, until they are pink and opaque.
  5. Remove from the grill, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve.

Tips:

  • If you’re grilling shrimp on wooden skewers, soak them in water for about 30 minutes before cooking to prevent them from burning.
  • Serve the shrimp with a side of garlic butter or a squeeze of fresh lemon for added flavor.

6. BBQ Grilled Corn on the Cob

Corn on the cob is another must-have for a 4th of July BBQ. Grilled corn is incredibly easy to prepare and packed with smoky flavor that pairs perfectly with your BBQ dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the corn with melted butter and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the corn, turning every few minutes, until the kernels are tender and lightly charred, about 12-15 minutes.
  4. Remove from the grill and serve with additional butter and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.

Tips:

  • You can also wrap the corn in foil with some butter and herbs, then grill it for a softer texture.
  • Try different seasonings like chili powder, Parmesan cheese, or smoked paprika for unique flavors.

7. Grilled BBQ Chicken Wings

Chicken wings are always a hit at BBQs, and they’re incredibly versatile. Whether you like them spicy, sweet, or tangy, these grilled BBQ wings will satisfy everyone’s cravings.

Ingredients:

  • 12 chicken wings
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp paprika

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Season the chicken wings with olive oil, salt, pepper, and paprika.
  3. Grill the wings for about 5-7 minutes on each side, until they’re crispy and fully cooked.
  4. Brush with BBQ sauce and cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the sauce to caramelize.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or green onions.

Tips:

  • Marinate the wings in BBQ sauce for a few hours or overnight for more flavor.
  • If you prefer crispy wings, cook them over direct heat for the first few minutes and then move them to indirect heat to finish.

8. BBQ Grilled Pineapple

Grilled fruit can be a surprising and delicious addition to your BBQ spread, and pineapple is especially fantastic when cooked on the grill. The natural sweetness caramelizes and adds a tropical flair to your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled, cored, and sliced into rings
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the pineapple rings with honey and sprinkle with cinnamon.
  3. Grill the pineapple for about 3-4 minutes per side, until it’s caramelized and has grill marks.
  4. Remove from the grill and serve as a side dish or dessert.

Tips:

  • You can also add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili flakes for a spicy kick.
  • Serve the grilled pineapple with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a sweet treat.

Final Thoughts

With these quick and easy BBQ recipes, you can be ready for any last-minute 4th of July plans. Whether you’re grilling up chicken, ribs, veggies, or even fruit, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and keep them coming back for more. The best part? They’re all simple to prepare, so you can spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the celebration.

Remember, BBQ is all about having fun and creating delicious food to share with others. So don’t stress—these quick recipes will make your holiday meal memorable, and you’ll have plenty of time to relax and enjoy the fireworks. Happy grilling!
