Middle Eastern Cooking for Entertaining: Recipes for a Crowd


The heart of Middle Eastern cooking lies in its embrace of community, tradition, and celebration. With its vibrant spices, layered flavors, and abundant sharing culture, Middle Eastern cuisine is perfect for hosting a crowd. The recipes not only cater to a wide variety of dietary preferences but also evoke warmth and a sense of togetherness that makes entertaining an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re planning a festive gathering or a casual dinner party, this guide will walk you through creating a memorable feast inspired by the culinary traditions of the Middle East.

Setting the Stage for Middle Eastern Hospitality

Middle Eastern hospitality is more than just a meal—it’s an immersive experience. It begins with heartfelt welcomes and extends to an intricately laid table featuring a range of small plates, mains, and indulgent desserts alongside aromatic beverages. To entertain like a Middle Eastern host, offer a variety of dishes that incorporate fresh produce, nuts, dried fruits, herbs, and spices. And don’t forget bread—flatbreads and pita serve as essential accompaniments to almost every dish.

Now let’s dive into the recipes that will make your table both delicious and visually inviting.

Appetizers and Mezze for Sharing

A traditional Middle Eastern gathering often begins with a mezze spread, a collection of small plates to whet the appetite. This approach offers a little something for everyone and gets the conversation flowing.

Classic Hummus with a Twist

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus extra for garnish
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blend chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin in a food processor.
  2. Slowly drizzle in olive oil while blending until smooth.
  3. Adjust seasoning with salt, and add a sprinkle of smoked paprika for a smoky kick.
  4. Serve in a wide bowl with olive oil drizzled on top. Pair with fresh pita or vegetable sticks.

Muhammara (Roasted Red Pepper and Walnut Dip)

Ingredients:

  • 2 large roasted red peppers (jarred works too)
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender until smooth and thick, adjusting with olive oil for desired consistency.
  2. Serve chilled or at room temperature, garnished with crushed walnuts and a drizzle of pomegranate molasses.

Pair hummus and muhammara with warm, fluffy flatbreads or crispy pita chips. Add bowls of olives, tabbouleh, or marinated labneh cheese balls for variety.

Show-Stopping Mains

The main course is where the Middle Eastern table truly shines, offering hearty, flavor-packed dishes perfect for a group.

Chicken Makloubeh (Upside-Down Rice Dish)

Makloubeh is a festive centerpiece that delivers a noble blend of spices, tender chicken, and vegetables arranged with fragrant rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked and rinsed)
  • 2 eggplants, sliced and fried
  • 2 large potatoes, sliced and fried
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cardamom pods
  • 2 teaspoons ground allspice
  • 2 teaspoons ground turmeric
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté onion until golden. Add chicken and spices, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Remove chicken and layer cooked potatoes and eggplants in the pot. Place chicken pieces on top.
  3. Cover with rice and pour in chicken stock. The liquid should just cover the rice.
  4. Cook over low heat for about 20 minutes, allowing the rice to absorb the stock.
  5. Let rest for 10 minutes, then invert the pot onto a platter for a dramatic “reveal.”

Serve makloubeh with a side of cucumber and yogurt salad for a cooling contrast.

Vegetarian Moussaka with Lentils

For your vegetarian guests, moussaka is an ideal crowd-pleaser. This version substitutes meat with protein-rich lentils.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggplants and 2 zucchinis, sliced and roasted
  • 2 cups cooked green or brown lentils
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil, for roasting and cooking
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast eggplant and zucchini until tender.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft, then add lentils, tomatoes, and spices. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. In a casserole dish, layer vegetables and lentil mixture, finishing with a top layer of roasted eggplant.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes.

Present moussaka alongside crusty bread and a simple herb salad for a substantial vegetarian option.

Sides That Complete the Feast

Middle Eastern meals are incomplete without a selection of vibrant sides. These dishes enhance the main course and provide variety.

Fattoush Salad

This refreshing, crunchy salad is a colorful addition to your table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 red onion, sliced thin
  • 1 pita, toasted and broken into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons sumac powder
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss vegetables and toasted pita in a large bowl.
  2. Whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, sumac, and salt, then drizzle over the salad before serving.

Spiced Rice Pilaf with Nuts and Raisins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup slivered almonds and pistachios
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice with turmeric and a pinch of salt.
  2. In a skillet, toast nuts and raisins in butter with cinnamon.
  3. Mix seasoned nuts and raisins into the cooked rice for flavor and texture.

Desserts to Impress

What better way to end a Middle Eastern feast than with sweet treats paired with tea or coffee?

Baklava

Although time-intensive, baklava is a crowd favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios)
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon orange blossom water

Instructions:

  1. Layer phyllo sheets in a pan, brushing each with butter. Add a layer of finely chopped nuts mixed with sugar and cinnamon.
  2. Repeat layering until all ingredients are used, finishing with phyllo dough.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 35 minutes, then pour a mix of honey, water, and orange blossom water over the hot baklava.

Beverages to Elevate the Meal

Pair your feast with classic Middle Eastern beverages like mint tea, cardamom-spiced coffee, or pomegranate juice. These drinks cleanse the palate and complement the robust flavors of the meal.

Middle Eastern cooking offers endless possibilities for creating a shared, celebratory experience. With its emphasis on fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and communal spirit, this menu ensures your guests will leave with full stomachs and unforgettable memories. Try incorporating these recipes into your next gathering, and experience the magic of Middle Eastern entertaining firsthand.
