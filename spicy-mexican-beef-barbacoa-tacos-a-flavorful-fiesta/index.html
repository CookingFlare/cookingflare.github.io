Spicy Mexican Beef Barbacoa Tacos: A Flavorful Fiesta


When it comes to Mexican cuisine, few dishes evoke the same level of excitement and anticipation as the humble taco. But what sets these delicious little bundles apart from the rest is the rich, bold flavors that come together to create a truly unforgettable eating experience. And that’s precisely what we’re going to explore in this recipe for Spicy Mexican Beef Barbacoa Tacos – a dish that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Mexico City and leave you craving for more.

The Magic of Barbacoa

Barbacoa is a traditional Mexican cooking method that involves slow-cooking meats in a rich, flavorful broth until they’re tender and falling apart. It’s a technique that requires patience, but the end result is well worth the wait. For this recipe, we’re using beef short ribs, which are perfect for slow-cooking and absorb the flavors of the broth like a sponge. The result is a tender, juicy meat that’s infused with the deep, rich flavors of chilies, spices, and herbs.

The Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 medium onions, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried guajillo chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Sliced radishes, for serving
  • Chopped cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the lard or oil over medium heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they’re softened and lightly browned, about 10 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, ancho chilies, guajillo chilies, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute.
  3. Add the beef short ribs to the pot, stirring to coat them evenly with the spice mixture. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the meat is browned on all sides.
  4. Add the beef broth, water, and tomato paste to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover the pot and transfer it to the oven. Braise the beef for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, or until it’s tender and falling apart.
  5. Remove the pot from the oven and let it cool slightly. Using two forks, shred the beef into bite-sized pieces. Strain the broth and discard the solids. Add the shredded beef back to the pot and stir to combine with the broth.
  6. Warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds.
  7. Assemble the tacos by spooning the beef mixture onto a warmed tortilla and topping with a sprinkle of cilantro, a squeeze of lime juice, and a few slices of radish. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra boost of flavor, add 1-2 tablespoons of Mexican oregano or epazote to the pot during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  • Substitute beef brisket or beef shank for the short ribs, if you prefer.
  • Add some heat to your tacos by sprinkling a pinch of diced jalapeño or serrano pepper on top.
  • Experiment with different types of chilies or spices to give the dish your own unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

And there you have it – a recipe for Spicy Mexican Beef Barbacoa Tacos that’s sure to become a staple in your household. With its rich, bold flavors and tender, juicy meat, this dish is perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in with family and friends. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and get ready to fiesta your way to a flavorful and unforgettable eating experience. ¡Buen provecho!
