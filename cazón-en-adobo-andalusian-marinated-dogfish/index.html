Cazón en Adobo: Andalusian Marinated Dogfish


If you’re looking to explore the vibrant and diverse flavors of Andalusian cuisine, then you simply cannot miss out on Cazón en Adobo. This traditional dish hails from the sunny region of Andalusia in southern Spain and features marinated dogfish that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. The combination of aromatic spices and tangy vinegar creates a flavor profile that is truly irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 500g of dogfish fillets
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of white vinegar
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • Salt to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Start by cleaning the dogfish fillets thoroughly. Remove any skin or bones, and cut them into bite-sized pieces. Rinse them under cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel.

  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, water, vinegar, minced garlic, paprika, cumin, oregano, and salt. Mix well until you have a smooth batter.

  3. Place the dogfish fillets into the batter, ensuring that each piece is well-coated. Allow them to marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for the best results, let them sit in the marinade for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.

  4. Heat olive oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the marinated dogfish pieces into the pan, making sure not to overcrowd them. Fry them in batches if necessary.

  5. Cook the dogfish for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels, which will help absorb any excess oil.

  6. Serve the Cazón en Adobo immediately while still hot. You can enjoy it as an appetizer, accompanied by a squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a dollop of tangy aioli sauce. For a complete Andalusian experience, serve it alongside a glass of chilled fino or manzanilla sherry.

Tips and Variations

  • Dogfish substitution: If you can’t find dogfish, you can use other firm white fish such as cod or haddock as a substitute. The result will still be delicious!
  • Adjusting the spices: Feel free to adjust the spices and seasonings according to your taste preferences. You can add a pinch of cayenne pepper for some extra heat or a splash of white wine vinegar for a tangier flavor.
  • Serving suggestions: Cazón en Adobo is often served as tapas in Andalusia, but you can also enjoy it as a main course alongside a fresh salad or some crispy fries.

Conclusion

Cazón en Adobo is a true gem of Andalusian cuisine, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of the region. The delicate dogfish, marinated in a flavorful blend of spices and vinegar, offers a delightful explosion of taste and texture. Whether you’re a fan of seafood or looking to explore new flavors, this traditional dish is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Andalusia with Cazón en Adobo. ¡Buen provecho!
