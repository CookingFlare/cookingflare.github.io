Chipirones en su Tinta: Spanish Squid in Ink Sauce


Chipirones en su Tinta, or Spanish Squid in Ink Sauce, is a classic Spanish dish that showcases the rich flavors of squid cooked in its own ink. This dish is a true delight for seafood lovers, and its unique presentation is sure to impress your guests. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating this traditional Spanish delicacy, step by step.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg fresh chipirones (baby squid)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup fish or vegetable stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Clean the squid: Start by cleaning the chipirones. Remove the tentacles and set them aside. Peel off the outer skin and discard. Rinse the squid under cold water to remove any remaining impurities. Pat them dry with a paper towel.

  2. Prepare the ink sauce: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they turn translucent and fragrant. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until they soften. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for a couple of minutes to reduce slightly.

  3. Cook the squid: Add the cleaned squid to the skillet, along with the tentacles. Stir well to coat them in the sauce. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the squid simmer gently for about 1 hour, until tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  4. Thicken the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the flour with a few tablespoons of fish or vegetable stock until smooth. Gradually pour this mixture into the skillet, stirring continuously. This will help thicken the sauce. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes, until the sauce reaches a desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Serve and garnish: Transfer the Chipirones en su Tinta to a serving dish. Sprinkle with freshly chopped parsley for a vibrant touch. This dish is traditionally served with white rice or crusty bread to soak up the delicious ink sauce.

Tips and Variations

  • If you can’t find fresh squid, you can use frozen squid rings instead. Just make sure to thaw them thoroughly before cooking.
  • For an extra kick of flavor, add a pinch of smoked paprika or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce.
  • You can also add a splash of brandy or sherry to the dish for a more complex flavor profile.
  • Chipirones en su Tinta can be prepared in advance and reheated before serving. In fact, the flavors often deepen and improve after a day or two.

Final Thoughts

Chipirones en su Tinta is a dish that perfectly captures the essence of Spanish cuisine. The tender squid, infused with the rich flavors of the ink sauce, creates a truly memorable dining experience. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to indulge in a delicious seafood dish, this recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and embark on a culinary journey to Spain with Chipirones en su Tinta!
