Fermented Pickled Jalapenos: Adding Heat to Your Recipes


Fermented pickled jalapenos, also known as “jalapeno peppers” for short, are a popular condiment in many cuisines, particularly in Mexican and Tex-Mex cooking. These spicy little peppers are packed with flavor, crunch, and a kick of heat that can elevate any dish. In this article, we will explore the process of making fermented pickled jalapenos at home, and provide some inspiration on how to use them in your recipes.

What are Fermented Pickled Jalapenos?

Fermented pickled jalapenos are made by fermenting jalapeno peppers in a brine solution, which allows the natural bacteria on the peppers to break down the sugars and develop a tangy, slightly sour flavor. This process also helps to preserve the peppers, making them a great addition to your pantry.

Ingredients and Equipment Needed

To make fermented pickled jalapenos, you will need the following ingredients and equipment:

  • 1 pound of jalapeno peppers
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of vinegar (such as apple cider vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of pickling spice (optional)
  • A clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid
  • A fermentation weight or stone
  • Cheesecloth or a coffee filter

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making fermented pickled jalapenos is a simple process that requires some patience. Here’s a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Wash and Prepare the Jalapenos: Wash the jalapeno peppers thoroughly with water to remove any dirt or debris. Remove the stems and any leaves that may be attached. Slice the peppers into thin rings or leave them whole, depending on your preference.
  2. Create the Brine: In a large bowl, combine the water, vinegar, salt, and sugar. Stir until the salt and sugar are dissolved.
  3. Add the Jalapenos to the Brine: Add the sliced or whole jalapenos to the brine solution. Make sure they are fully submerged in the liquid.
  4. Add Pickling Spice (Optional): If you’re using pickling spice, add it to the brine solution. This will give your pickled jalapenos an extra boost of flavor.
  5. Transfer to a Fermentation Jar: Transfer the jalapenos and brine solution to a clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid. Make sure the peppers are still submerged in the liquid.
  6. Add a Fermentation Weight or Stone: Place a fermentation weight or stone on top of the peppers to keep them submerged in the brine solution. This will help to prevent mold from forming.
  7. Cover with Cheesecloth or a Coffee Filter: Cover the jar with cheesecloth or a coffee filter to keep dust and other contaminants out.
  8. Ferment the Jalapenos: Place the jar in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard. Allow the jalapenos to ferment for 3-4 weeks. You may start to see bubbles forming on the surface of the brine solution, which is a sign that the fermentation process is underway.
  9. Check the Jalapenos: After 3-4 weeks, check the jalapenos to see if they have reached the desired level of fermentation. They should be slightly sour and tangy, with a crunchy texture. If they are not fermented to your liking, you can continue to ferment them for another week or two.

Using Fermented Pickled Jalapenos in Recipes

Fermented pickled jalapenos are a versatile condiment that can be used in a variety of dishes. Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • Add them to sandwiches and wraps: Sliced fermented pickled jalapenos make a great addition to sandwiches, wraps, and tacos.
  • Use them as a topping: Fermented pickled jalapenos are a great topping for tacos, salads, and soups.
  • Add them to soups and stews: Fermented pickled jalapenos can add a burst of flavor and heat to soups and stews.
  • Make a spicy salsa: Combine fermented pickled jalapenos with some diced onions, garlic, and cilantro for a spicy salsa.
  • Use them as a garnish: Fermented pickled jalapenos can be used as a garnish for cocktails and other drinks.

Tips and Variations

Here are some tips and variations to keep in mind when making fermented pickled jalapenos:

  • Adjust the level of heat: If you prefer a milder flavor, you can use only half of the jalapenos or substitute in some milder peppers, such as Anaheim or bell peppers.
  • Add some acidity: If you prefer a tangier flavor, you can add some lemon juice or vinegar to the brine solution.
  • Experiment with spices: You can add some additional spices, such as mustard seeds or coriander, to the brine solution for extra flavor.
  • Make them chunky: If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can leave the jalapenos in larger pieces instead of slicing them thinly.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled jalapenos are a delicious and versatile condiment that can add heat and flavor to a variety of dishes. With these simple steps and tips, you can make your own fermented pickled jalapenos at home and enjoy them for months to come. Experiment with different spices and recipes to find your favorite way to use them.
