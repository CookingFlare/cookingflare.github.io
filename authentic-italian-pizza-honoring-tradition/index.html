Authentic Italian Pizza: Honoring Tradition


Pizza, the beloved Italian dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all over the world. But what makes an authentic Italian pizza so special? It’s not just about the toppings or the dough, it’s about honoring tradition and embracing the rich history behind this iconic dish. In this blog post, we will dive deep into the secrets of authentic Italian pizza and learn how to recreate this culinary masterpiece in your very own kitchen.

The Foundation: The Dough

The foundation of any great pizza lies in its dough. Authentic Italian pizza dough is a work of art, crafted with care and precision. To make the perfect dough, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500 grams of high-quality Italian flour
  • 325 milliliters of lukewarm water
  • 10 grams of salt
  • 5 grams of active dry yeast
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and add the yeast.
  2. Slowly pour the lukewarm water into the well and gently mix the ingredients together using your hands. Continue kneading until the dough comes together and becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rest in a warm place for at least 2 hours, allowing it to rise and develop its flavor.

The Sauce: Simplicity is Key

Authentic Italian pizza sauce is all about simplicity. The flavors of the tomatoes should shine through, complementing the other ingredients without overpowering them. Here’s how to make a traditional Italian pizza sauce:

  • 1 can of San Marzano tomatoes (preferably crushed)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes to the pan and season with salt and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 20-30 minutes, allowing it to thicken and intensify in flavor.

Toppings: Fresh and Flavorful

When it comes to toppings, authentic Italian pizza celebrates the simplicity of fresh, high-quality ingredients. Here are a few classic options to consider:

  • Fresh mozzarella cheese, thinly sliced or torn into small pieces
  • Basil leaves, torn
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced black olives
  • Prosciutto or other cured meats
  • Mushrooms
  • Fresh arugula

Feel free to mix and match these toppings to create your own unique flavor combination. Remember, less is more when it comes to authentic Italian pizza.

Baking: The Perfect Crust

Now that you have your dough, sauce, and toppings ready, it’s time to bake your pizza to perfection. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible (usually around 500°F or 260°C) and follow these steps:

  1. On a lightly floured surface, divide the dough into individual portions, depending on the size of the pizzas you want to make.
  2. Roll out each portion of dough into a thin, round shape, about 10-12 inches in diameter.
  3. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a pizza stone or a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Spread a thin layer of sauce onto the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  5. Add your desired toppings, scattering them evenly over the sauce.
  6. Carefully transfer the pizza into the preheated oven and bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  7. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Honoring Tradition: The Italian Way

Authentic Italian pizza is not just about the flavors; it’s about embracing the Italian way of life. Enjoy your pizza with family and friends, savoring each bite and taking the time to appreciate the craftsmanship that goes into creating this culinary masterpiece. Pair your pizza with a glass of Italian wine or a refreshing Italian soda for the complete experience.

So, the next time you crave a slice of pizza, channel your inner Italian and honor tradition by making your very own authentic Italian pizza. With fresh ingredients, a little bit of patience, and a lot of love, you’ll be able to create a pizza that would make any Italian proud.

Buon appetito!
