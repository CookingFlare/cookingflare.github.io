Savory Short Rib Ragu with Pappardelle


Few dishes evoke the feeling of warmth and satisfaction quite like a hearty bowl of pasta tossed with a rich, deeply flavorful sauce. Today, we’re diving into the world of savory short rib ragu served alongside silky pappardelle—a dish that’s as elegant as it is comforting. Whether you’re crafting a meal for a celebratory occasion or simply seeking solace on a chilly evening, this recipe is destined to become a favorite in your culinary rotation.

Why Short Ribs?

Short ribs are a cut of beef that excels in braised dishes due to their marbling and connective tissue. When cooked low and slow, they transform into tender morsels imbued with incredible flavor, making them the ideal centerpiece for a ragu. Their richness melds beautifully with a robust tomato-based sauce flavored by aromatic vegetables, earthy herbs, and, of course, a splash of good red wine.

Using pappardelle, the wide ribbons of pasta known for their ability to hold hearty sauces, takes this recipe from special to sublime. Their tender texture is the perfect base for the bold, savory ragu.

Ready to dive into the steps? Here’s how to craft the perfect short rib ragu with pappardelle from scratch.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Creating this dish is as much about layering flavors as it is about using high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Short Rib Ragu:

  • 4–5 beef short ribs (bone-in, about 2 pounds)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1½ cups red wine (choose a dry red, like Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (28-oz)
  • 1 cup beef stock (low-sodium is best)
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Pappardelle:

  • 12 ounces fresh or dried pappardelle pasta
  • Salt (for the pasta water)

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh parmesan cheese, grated
  • Parsley, chopped (for a pop of color and freshness)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Cooking savory short rib ragu is an experience in patience and precision. The layering of flavors and slow braising yields a sauce that’s well worth the wait. Follow these steps for a dish that’s guaranteed to impress.

1. Prepare the Short Ribs

Begin by seasoning the short ribs generously with salt and black pepper. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the short ribs and sear on all sides until well-browned, about 8–10 minutes. Browning the short ribs intensifies their flavor, so don’t skip this crucial step!

Once seared, remove the short ribs from the pot and set them aside.

2. Build the Flavor Base

Reduce the heat to medium and add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Toss in the diced onion, carrots, and celery, cooking until softened and fragrant—about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and stir for another minute.

Next, stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes, allowing it to darken and caramelize slightly. This will add depth to the sauce.

3. Deglaze with Wine

Pour the red wine into the pot, stirring and scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. Bring the wine to a simmer and let it reduce by about half—this concentrates its flavors and creates a robust base for the sauce.

4. Combine the Ingredients

Return the short ribs to the pot, nestled into the vegetable mixture. Add the crushed tomatoes, beef stock, fresh thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves. Stir everything together, ensuring the short ribs are mostly submerged in the sauce.

5. Low and Slow Cooking

Cover the pot and reduce the heat to low, allowing the ragu to gently simmer for 2½ to 3 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The magic of this dish is in the slow cooking process—over time, the short ribs will become tender enough to pull apart with ease.

As the short ribs cook, taste the sauce occasionally and adjust the seasoning with additional salt or pepper if needed.

6. Shred the Meat

Once the short ribs are fall-apart tender, remove them from the pot and discard the bones. Shred the meat with two forks, then return it to the sauce. Remove the thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves (they’ve served their purpose). Let the ragu simmer uncovered while you prepare the pasta. This final step allows the flavors to marry together.

7. Cook the Pappardelle

While the ragu simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pappardelle according to package instructions until al dente, reserving about ½ cup of pasta water before draining. This reserved water can be used to loosen the sauce if needed.

Toss the drained pappardelle in the ragu, ensuring every strand is coated with the rich, savory sauce. If necessary, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to help bind the sauce to the pasta.

8. Serve and Garnish

Transfer the ragu-laden pappardelle to serving bowls and finish with garnishes of your choice. A shower of freshly grated parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped parsley elevate the dish with added flavor and visual appeal.

Tips for Success

  • Select Quality Ingredients: The better the components, the richer the final dish. Use fresh herbs and a wine you’d enjoy drinking—it makes all the difference.
  • Consistency is Key: The ragu should be thick and velvety. If your sauce is too thin, let it simmer uncovered for a bit to reduce, stirring occasionally.
  • Make Ahead Option: Short rib ragu is one of those recipes that tastes even better the next day. Prepare it ahead of time and reheat gently for busy weeknight dinners or meal prep.

Pairing Suggestions

No exquisite dinner is complete without thoughtful pairings. To enhance the dining experience, consider serving short rib ragu with pappardelle alongside a crusty baguette or focaccia for sopping up the sauce. A light salad with tangy vinaigrette serves as a refreshing counterpoint to the richness of the dish. For beverages, a full-bodied red wine—such as Barolo or Chianti—complements the earthy flavors beautifully, while sparkling water with lemon wedges cleanses the palate.

Closing Thoughts

Savory short rib ragu with pappardelle is not just a recipe—it’s an experience. It fills your home with tantalizing scents, wraps you in the joy of slow cooking, and delivers a dish that’s comforting, luxurious, and unforgettable. Perfect for intimate dinners or family gatherings, this meal is sure to earn a permanent spot in your repertoire.

Take your time, savor the process, and share it with those you love. After all, food is more than sustenance—it’s connection, tradition, and art, all on one plate. Happy cooking!
