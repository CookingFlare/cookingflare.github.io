Perfectly Soft and Buttered Dinner Rolls: A Classic Recipe


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked dinner rolls wafting through the house. These perfectly soft and buttered dinner rolls are a classic recipe that will have your family and friends coming back for seconds (and maybe even thirds!). With their fluffy texture and rich buttery flavor, these rolls are the ultimate comfort food. Whether you’re hosting a holiday feast or simply want to elevate your weeknight dinner, this recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm milk, melted butter, and eggs. Make sure the milk is warm, not hot, as hot milk can kill the yeast.

  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until a soft dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, gradually add more flour, a tablespoon at a time, until it becomes manageable.

  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic. Kneading helps develop the gluten, which gives the rolls their soft and chewy texture.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Allow the dough to rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  6. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it back to the floured surface and divide it into 16 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a smooth ball by tucking the edges underneath.

  7. Arrange the dough balls in a greased baking dish, leaving a little space between each roll to allow them to expand during baking. Cover the dish with the kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the rolls rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they have doubled in size once again.

  8. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  9. Once the rolls have risen, brush the tops generously with melted butter. This will give them a beautiful golden color and add an extra layer of flavor.

  10. Bake the rolls in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  11. Remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. These rolls are best enjoyed warm, straight from the oven, but they will stay soft and delicious for a couple of days if stored in an airtight container.

Serving Suggestions

These perfectly soft and buttered dinner rolls are incredibly versatile. Serve them as a side dish for roast turkey, beef, or chicken. They also make a fantastic addition to a soup or stew, allowing you to soak up every last drop of flavor. And let’s not forget about their potential as sandwich bread. Slice them in half, fill them with your favorite deli meats and cheeses, and you’ve got yourself a mouthwatering lunch option.

Conclusion

Perfectly soft and buttered dinner rolls are a timeless classic that will never go out of style. With their fluffy texture and rich buttery flavor, they are the perfect accompaniment to any meal. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow and guarantees delicious results. So go ahead, roll up your sleeves, and give these dinner rolls a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
