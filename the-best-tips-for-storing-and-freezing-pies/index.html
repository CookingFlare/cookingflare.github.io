The Best Tips for Storing and Freezing Pies


Pies are a beloved treat, whether served for special occasions or enjoyed as a simple dessert after dinner. Whether it’s a sweet fruit pie, a creamy custard pie, or a savory pot pie, these baked goods often take time and care to prepare. Because pies are typically made in large quantities, there might be leftovers, or you may want to prepare them in advance. Proper storage and freezing techniques can help preserve the freshness and flavor of your pies, so you can enjoy them long after baking. In this post, we’ll explore the best methods for storing and freezing pies, ensuring your delicious creations last longer and taste just as good as the day they were made.

Why Proper Pie Storage Matters

Properly storing your pie prevents spoilage and helps maintain the texture, taste, and overall quality of the pie. Since pies are typically made from perishable ingredients like fruit, dairy, and eggs, they can spoil quickly if not stored correctly. Storing pies in the right way also helps avoid soggy crusts, freezer burn, and a loss of flavor, all of which can occur if pies aren’t stored properly. Whether you’re storing a freshly baked pie or one you’ve prepared for future enjoyment, knowing the best practices will ensure your pies remain just as mouthwatering as the day you made them.

Storing Pies at Room Temperature

Some pies, particularly fruit pies, are fine to store at room temperature for a short period. However, the key to successful storage is keeping them away from heat and moisture. Here’s how you can store your pies at room temperature:

Fruit Pies

Fruit pies such as apple, cherry, peach, and blueberry are relatively durable and can be left at room temperature for up to two days. After baking, allow the pie to cool completely at room temperature to prevent condensation, which could cause the crust to become soggy. Once cool, cover the pie loosely with aluminum foil or wax paper. If you’re not planning to finish the pie within two days, refrigeration is a better option, especially if the pie contains perishable fillings.

Custard and Cream Pies

Custard pies, like pumpkin or sweet potato pies, and cream pies, such as banana cream or chocolate cream pies, should never be left out at room temperature for more than two hours due to the dairy and egg content. After cooling to room temperature, these pies should be refrigerated immediately to prevent foodborne illness.

Savory Pies

Savory pies, including chicken pot pies, meat pies, and quiches, should also be refrigerated after cooling. Like custard pies, they contain dairy and eggs, which require refrigeration to keep them safe to eat. Room temperature storage is not suitable for savory pies, as the filling will spoil quickly if left out.

Refrigerating Pies

For longer storage or when your pie is made with perishable ingredients, refrigerating the pie is the best option. Refrigeration will keep the pie fresh for several days, but it’s important to cover the pie properly to prevent it from drying out or absorbing any odors from other foods in the fridge. Here are some tips for refrigerating pies effectively:

How to Store Pies in the Refrigerator

  1. Cool Completely: Allow your pie to cool completely at room temperature before transferring it to the refrigerator. Storing a hot pie in the fridge can cause condensation, which will lead to a soggy crust.
  2. Cover with Plastic Wrap or Aluminum Foil: Wrap the pie tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil to keep it sealed. You can also use a pie container with a tight-fitting lid to avoid air exposure.
  3. Store on a Flat Surface: Place the pie on a flat surface, such as a shelf in your fridge. This will prevent the pie from tilting and causing the filling to shift.

How Long Can Pies Be Stored in the Refrigerator?

  • Fruit pies: Stored properly, fruit pies can last in the refrigerator for up to four days.
  • Custard and cream pies: These should be eaten within two to three days when stored in the refrigerator.
  • Savory pies: Meat pies and savory pies can be refrigerated for up to three to four days.

Freezing Pies for Long-Term Storage

Freezing is a fantastic way to extend the shelf life of your pie and enjoy homemade pie even months after baking. Whether you have leftover pie or you’re planning ahead, freezing pies allows you to store them without losing the texture or flavor. Freezing pies requires a few extra steps, but it’s worth it for the convenience and long-lasting freshness.

Freezing Unbaked Pies

The best way to freeze a pie is to freeze it before baking. This is especially beneficial for fruit pies and savory pies, as the filling and crust will bake together fresh when you’re ready to enjoy them. Here’s how you can freeze unbaked pies:

  1. Prepare the Pie: Make the pie as you normally would, whether it’s a fruit pie, meat pie, or savory creation. Assemble the pie with the crust and filling, but don’t bake it yet.
  2. Wrap and Freeze: After assembling the pie, cover it tightly with plastic wrap. Then, wrap the entire pie with a layer of aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Label the pie with the date and type of pie.
  3. Freeze Flat: Place the pie on a baking sheet or tray to freeze it flat. This helps maintain the pie’s shape as it freezes. Once the pie is frozen solid (usually after several hours), you can remove it from the tray and store it in a freezer-safe bag or container.
  4. Freeze for Up to 3 Months: Unbaked pies can be stored in the freezer for up to three months. After that, the pie may start to lose its quality, although it will still be safe to eat.

Freezing Baked Pies

If you already have a pie that’s been baked, you can still freeze it for later. Baked pies are ideal for freezing when you want to store a fully prepared dessert for future enjoyment. Here’s how to freeze a baked pie:

  1. Cool the Pie Completely: Let the pie cool completely before freezing. Freezing a hot pie can cause ice crystals to form and affect the texture of the filling.
  2. Wrap the Pie: Cover the cooled pie with plastic wrap, then wrap it with a layer of aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. It’s important that the pie is tightly wrapped to keep air from getting in.
  3. Freeze the Pie: Place the wrapped pie on a baking sheet and freeze it flat until solid. After that, you can transfer the pie to a freezer-safe bag or container for more convenient storage.
  4. Label and Date: Label the pie with the date it was frozen so you can track how long it’s been in the freezer.

Thawing and Reheating Frozen Pies

When you’re ready to enjoy a frozen pie, you’ll want to thaw and reheat it properly to restore its original flavor and texture. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Thaw the Pie: For best results, thaw your pie in the refrigerator overnight. This will allow the pie to thaw slowly and prevent the crust from becoming soggy. If you’re in a hurry, you can also thaw the pie at room temperature for a few hours.
  2. Reheat the Pie: To get a crispy crust, reheat the pie in the oven rather than the microwave. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie on a baking sheet and heat for 20-30 minutes, depending on the size of the pie. For cream pies, it’s best to serve them chilled without reheating.

Freezing Savory Pies

Freezing savory pies, such as pot pies or quiches, works similarly to freezing sweet pies. However, keep in mind that savory pies with creamy fillings may need a bit of extra care when reheating to prevent curdling. Follow the same procedure for freezing unbaked or baked savory pies, and ensure they are properly wrapped and stored in an airtight container to maintain flavor and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Freezing Pies

While freezing pies is relatively easy, there are some common mistakes that can affect the quality of your pies. Avoid the following errors to ensure your pies stay fresh and delicious:

  1. Not Cooling the Pie Before Freezing: It’s essential to let your pie cool completely before freezing. Freezing a hot pie will result in condensation, which can make the crust soggy.
  2. Freezing a Pie Without Wrapping It Properly: Make sure to wrap your pie tightly in plastic wrap and foil. Failing to do so can lead to freezer burn and dry, flavorless crusts.
  3. Storing Pies for Too Long: While pies can be frozen for months, keeping them frozen for too long can degrade their taste and texture. Aim to eat your frozen pies within three months for the best results.

Final Thoughts

With the right storage and freezing techniques, you can enjoy your homemade pies for longer and even have them ready to bake or serve whenever you need them. Whether you’re making pies for a special occasion or preparing meals in advance, proper storage and freezing are key to ensuring your pies stay fresh and flavorful. By following these tips, you’ll be able to savor the deliciousness of your pies long after the last slice has been cut.

Happy baking, and may your pies always be as perfect as the day they were made!
