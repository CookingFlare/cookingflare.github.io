Exploring the Flavors of Shandong Cuisine: Authentic Recipes


Shandong cuisine, also known as Lu cuisine, is one of the eight major culinary traditions in China. Originating from the eastern coastal province of Shandong, this cuisine is known for its fresh and flavorful dishes that showcase a balance of taste, texture, and aroma. In this blog post, we will take a journey through the authentic recipes of Shandong cuisine, exploring the unique flavors and techniques that make it so special.

1. Braised Prawns with Scallions and Ginger

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh prawns
  • 4 scallions, chopped
  • 2 slices of ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper powder
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Clean the prawns by removing the heads and shells, leaving the tails intact. Devein the prawns and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or frying pan over medium heat. Add the ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant.
  3. Add the prawns to the pan and stir-fry for about 2 minutes until they turn pink.
  4. Add the chopped scallions, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sugar, salt, and white pepper powder. Stir-fry for another 2 minutes until the prawns are fully cooked and coated in the sauce.
  5. Transfer the prawns to a serving plate and garnish with some more chopped scallions. Serve hot with steamed rice.

2. Sweet and Sour Pork

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork tenderloin, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons white rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper powder
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or frying pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
  2. Add the pork pieces to the pan and stir-fry until they are browned and cooked through.
  3. Add the bell peppers and onion to the pan and stir-fry for about 2 minutes until they are slightly softened.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, white rice vinegar, ketchup, sugar, salt, and white pepper powder. Pour the sauce into the pan and stir-fry for another 2 minutes until everything is well-coated.
  5. Transfer the sweet and sour pork to a serving plate and garnish with some chopped scallions. Serve hot with steamed rice.

3. Spring Onion Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped spring onions
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and boiling water. Stir with a spoon until a rough dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Divide the dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a thin circle.
  4. Brush the surface of each dough circle with vegetable oil. Sprinkle the chopped spring onions and salt evenly.
  5. Roll up each dough circle tightly and then coil it into a spiral shape. Flatten the spiral with your palm and roll it out into a pancake shape.
  6. Heat a frying pan over medium heat. Cook each pancake for about 2 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Cut the spring onion pancakes into wedges and serve hot as an appetizer or side dish.

Conclusion

Shandong cuisine is a true delight for food enthusiasts who appreciate the rich and diverse flavors of Chinese cuisine. From the succulent braised prawns to the tangy sweet and sour pork, and the crispy spring onion pancakes, these authentic recipes will transport your taste buds to the vibrant culinary landscape of Shandong. Give them a try and experience the unique flavors that have made this cuisine so renowned. Enjoy your culinary journey!
