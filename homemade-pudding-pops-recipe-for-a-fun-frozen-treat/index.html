Homemade Pudding Pops Recipe for a Fun Frozen Treat


When the summer heat is relentless, nothing beats the icy delight of a sweet frozen treat. For a dessert that brings smiles to kids and adults alike, homemade pudding pops are the perfect solution. These creamy, dreamy frozen desserts are incredibly easy to whip up, highly customizable, and guaranteed to be a hit with everyone in the household. In this recipe blog, you’ll discover all the tips, tricks, and variations to create irresistible pudding pops right in your own kitchen.

Why Make Pudding Pops at Home?

Homemade pudding pops not only taste better than store-bought alternatives, but they also give you complete control over the ingredients. Whether you’re looking to avoid artificial flavors, accommodate food allergies, or create fun flavor combinations, making pudding pops from scratch lets you tailor every aspect to your preferences. Plus, they’re surprisingly economical and come with the added bonus of being a delightful activity to share with your little ones.

Essential Ingredients for Homemade Pudding Pops

The ingredients for pudding pops are surprisingly simple, and you may already have most of them in your pantry. Here’s what you’ll need to get started:

  • Instant pudding mix or homemade pudding: Both options work wonderfully, depending on the time and effort you’d like to invest. Store-bought pudding mix is a great shortcut, while homemade pudding lets you fine-tune the flavor.
  • Whole milk: For a creamy texture, whole milk is the best option, but you can swap it for plant-based alternatives like almond milk or oat milk for a dairy-free version.
  • Sweetener: If preparing homemade pudding, you’ll need your choice of sweetener. Granulated sugar or natural sweeteners like honey or maple syrup work well.
  • Flavor additions: Get creative with extracts like vanilla, almond, or mint to add a personal touch.
  • Mix-ins and toppings: From chocolate chips to crushed cookies and fresh fruit, these bring texture and bursts of flavor to your pudding pops.

Optional ingredients, such as cocoa powder for a rich chocolate flavor or a dollop of whipped cream for added creaminess, can also elevate the experience.

Equipment You’ll Need

Aside from your ingredients, you’ll only need a few basic kitchen tools to simplify the process:

  • Blender or whisk to mix your pudding smoothly.
  • Popsicle molds for freezing your pudding into pop form. If you don’t have popsicle molds, small disposable cups and wooden popsicle sticks will work just as well.
  • Mixing bowl for blending your ingredients.

Step-by-Step Recipe for Homemade Pudding Pops

Step 1: Prepare the Pudding Base

Start by preparing your pudding. If you’re using an instant pudding mix, follow the directions on the packaging, substituting whole milk or your favorite alternative milk. Be sure the mixture is smooth and free of lumps.

For a homemade pudding, combine two cups of milk (or non-dairy milk), ¼ cup sugar, ¼ cup cornstarch, and a pinch of salt in a saucepan. Heat over medium heat while whisking constantly until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and stir in one teaspoon of vanilla extract or any other flavoring of your choice.

Let your pudding cool slightly before proceeding to the next step.

Step 2: Customize Your Flavor and Texture

This is where the fun begins! Your pudding base is essentially a blank canvas, waiting for you to add your personal style. Mix in cocoa powder for a chocolate twist, or swirl in some caramel or fruit purees for extra sweetness. For texture, stir in cookie crumbs, candied nuts, mini marshmallows, or diced fruit.

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can divide your pudding base into portions and make different flavors in a single batch. For example, chocolate and peanut butter combinations, lemon zest with fresh blueberries, or even a swirl of strawberry and cream.

Step 3: Pour Into Popsicle Molds

Carefully pour your pudding mixture into your popsicle molds. If you’re layering different flavors, pour each layer one at a time, allowing the first layer to firm up in the freezer for 5-10 minutes before adding the next. If you’re adding stick-in ingredients like cookie pieces or fruit chunks, distribute them evenly as you pour to ensure every bite gets some.

Leave a small amount of room at the top of each mold to allow for expansion as the pops freeze. Insert the popsicle sticks or lids with sticks built in.

Step 4: Freeze

Place your molds in the freezer and let the pudding pops chill for at least 4 hours, or until fully set. For the best texture and ease of removal, let them freeze overnight.

Tips for Perfect Pudding Pops

  • Achieving Creaminess: If you’re worried about ending up with an icy texture, try mixing in a tablespoon of heavy cream or yogurt to the pudding base. This addition helps maintain a smooth and creamy consistency.
  • Keeping Popsicle Sticks Centered: For makeshift molds without built-in sticks, cover your mold with aluminum foil, then insert the sticks through the foil. The foil holds the sticks upright while the pudding freezes.
  • Removing Popsicle Molds with Ease: Run the outer walls of the molds under warm water for a few seconds. The heat will loosen the sides, allowing you to easily slide out the pudding pops without damaging their shape.

Flavor Variations to Inspire Creativity

One of the joys of making pudding pops at home is experimenting with endless flavor combinations. Here are some ideas to get your creative juices flowing:

  • Cookies and Cream: Mix crushed sandwich cookies into a vanilla pudding base for a nostalgic, crowd-pleasing flavor.
  • Fruity Delight: Fold puréed strawberries, blueberries, or peaches into vanilla pudding. Combine flavors for a rainbow-inspired treat.
  • Choco-Mint Bliss: Add a few drops of peppermint extract and a handful of mini chocolate chips to a chocolate pudding base.
  • S’mores: Swirl bits of crushed graham crackers, melted chocolate, and mini marshmallows into your pudding before freezing.
  • Caramel Espresso: Stir in coffee extract and caramel sauce for a sophisticated twist.
  • Tropical Paradise: Use coconut milk as the base and mix with chunks of mango or pineapple and a hint of lime zest.

Storing and Serving Suggestions

Keep your pudding pops stored in their molds or wrapped individually in plastic wrap after unmolding. This prevents freezer burn and preserves freshness. They’re best enjoyed within two weeks, though they’re likely to disappear much sooner!

For serving, consider presenting them on a platter with bowls of toppings like sprinkles, melted chocolate, or chopped nuts for dipping. Parties and gatherings become instantly more exciting with these DIY fun extras!

Final Thoughts

Homemade pudding pops are more than just a delicious frozen dessert—they’re also a wonderful way to bring creativity and joy into your kitchen. From classic flavors to bold experiments, these treats allow you to express your personality with every bite. So, clear some freezer space, gather your ingredients, and get started on creating a batch of pudding pops that will leave everyone begging for seconds. Whether it’s a sultry summer day or just a craving for something cold and creamy, pudding pops are always a good idea.

Indulge in the fun, and let every pop you make be a little celebration of the sweet things in life!
