Mexican Empanadas Recipe with Beef and Onions


Empanadas are a popular Mexican dish that are loved for their delicious fillings and crispy, golden crusts. These tasty turnovers can be filled with a variety of ingredients, but one classic combination that never fails to impress is beef and onions. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make mouthwatering Mexican empanadas filled with savory beef and caramelized onions.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup cold water

For the filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt for the dough. Add the cold butter and use your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Slowly add the cold water to the flour mixture, mixing with a fork until the dough comes together. Knead the dough gently on a lightly floured surface until it becomes smooth and elastic. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until they become soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  4. Push the onion mixture to one side of the skillet and add the ground beef to the other side. Cook the beef until it is browned and cooked through, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Add the cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper, and stir everything together until well combined. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles from the dough. Place a spoonful of the beef and onion filling in the center of each circle.

  7. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Use your fingers or a fork to press and seal the edges of the empanadas. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  8. Bake the empanadas for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Mexican empanadas with beef and onions are delicious on their own, but you can enhance the flavors by serving them with some fresh salsa, guacamole, or sour cream. These empanadas make a fantastic appetizer for parties or a satisfying main course when served alongside a crisp green salad. They also freeze well, so you can make a large batch and enjoy them whenever you’re in the mood for a tasty treat.

Final Thoughts

These Mexican empanadas with beef and onions are a true delight for your taste buds. The combination of savory beef and caramelized onions wrapped in a crispy, golden crust is simply irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to enjoy a delicious homemade meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of Mexico with these delightful empanadas!
