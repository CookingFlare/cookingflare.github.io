Hearty Nut-Free Pumpkin Soup Recipe for Fall


Fall is the perfect time to cozy up with a warm bowl of soup, and what better way to embrace the season than with a hearty nut-free pumpkin soup? This recipe is not only delicious and comforting, but it’s also perfect for those with nut allergies. So, let’s dive into this flavorful pumpkin soup that will surely become a staple in your fall menu.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized pumpkin, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  2. Add the diced pumpkin, chopped carrots, and celery to the pot. Stir well to combine the ingredients, and let them cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Pour in the vegetable broth, coconut milk, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the pumpkin and vegetables are tender.

  5. Once the soup has cooked through, use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the mixture until smooth and creamy. Be careful not to blend hot soup in a regular blender all at once; instead, blend it in batches, allowing it to cool slightly before blending.

  6. Return the pureed soup to the pot and heat it over low heat for a few more minutes, just until it’s warmed through.

  7. Serve the nut-free pumpkin soup hot, garnished with fresh parsley for an added touch of freshness. You can also add a drizzle of coconut milk or a sprinkle of ground cinnamon on top for extra flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a chunkier soup, you can reserve some of the cooked vegetables before blending and add them back into the soup after pureeing.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can roast the diced pumpkin in the oven before adding it to the soup. Simply toss the pumpkin cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast them at 400°F (200°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are caramelized and tender.

  • To make the soup creamier, you can replace the coconut milk with heavy cream or Greek yogurt. Just keep in mind that this will no longer make it nut-free.

  • If you like a bit of heat, you can add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the soup for a spicy kick.

  • This pumpkin soup is perfect as a starter or a light meal on its own. You can also serve it with a side of crusty bread or a green salad for a more substantial meal.

Now that you have this delightful nut-free pumpkin soup recipe, you can enjoy the flavors of fall in a comforting and allergen-friendly way. Get ready to savor every spoonful of this hearty soup that will warm your soul and become a favorite during the autumn season.
