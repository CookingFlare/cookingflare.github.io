Summer Grilled Recipes That Are Perfect for a Picnic


Summer is the perfect season to fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and head out for a relaxing picnic. Whether you’re lounging by the beach, enjoying a day in the park, or basking in the sun in your own backyard, nothing beats the flavors of summer grilling. From juicy burgers and savory skewers to grilled veggies and refreshing salads, there are endless possibilities to explore when it comes to picnic-friendly grilled recipes.

In this guide, we’ll share some of the best summer grilled recipes that are not only delicious but also perfect for packing up and bringing along to your next outdoor gathering. These recipes are simple to make, full of flavor, and designed to impress your guests.

1. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Grilled vegetable skewers are an absolute crowd-pleaser and a great vegetarian option for your picnic. They are versatile, easy to prepare, and offer a medley of vibrant, smoky flavors that everyone will love. For the perfect grilled veggie skewers, you can use a combination of your favorite vegetables—like bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and mushrooms.

Ingredients:

  • 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red onion, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup button mushrooms, whole
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between different veggies for color and texture.
  3. Drizzle the vegetable skewers with olive oil, then sprinkle with garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and serve immediately or pack them up for your picnic.

Tips:

  • You can prepare the skewers the night before and store them in the fridge, making it even easier when it’s time to grill.
  • Feel free to add other veggies like eggplant, sweet potatoes, or asparagus to change things up.
  • For extra flavor, marinate the veggies in a balsamic glaze or a lemon herb mixture before grilling.

2. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Breasts

Grilled chicken breasts are a classic choice for any picnic, but with the right marinade, they can become an unforgettable dish. The bright and zesty flavor of lemon paired with fresh herbs creates a refreshing and juicy chicken that everyone will enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over the chicken. Seal the bag or cover the dish, then refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to overnight).
  3. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  4. Remove the chicken breasts from the marinade and grill for 6-7 minutes per side or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  5. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it. Serve as-is or on a sandwich bun for a delicious grilled chicken sandwich.

Tips:

  • If you want to add more depth of flavor, consider using a mix of different citrus fruits like oranges and limes in the marinade.
  • To keep the chicken moist and tender, avoid overcooking it. Using a meat thermometer ensures perfect results every time.

3. Grilled Shrimp Tacos

For seafood lovers, grilled shrimp tacos are a fantastic option for a summer picnic. Grilled shrimp is flavorful, quick to prepare, and pairs wonderfully with a variety of toppings like creamy avocado, tangy slaw, and fresh salsa. Plus, they’re perfect for assembling right at the picnic table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage or slaw mix
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh salsa (or pico de gallo)
  • Lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, paprika, cumin, garlic powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper until well-coated.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto skewers or place them directly on the grill. Grill for about 2-3 minutes per side until they turn pink and are cooked through.
  4. While the shrimp cooks, warm the tortillas on the grill for 1-2 minutes.
  5. To assemble the tacos, place a few shrimp on each tortilla, then top with shredded cabbage, avocado slices, salsa, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  6. Serve immediately or pack them up and serve at your picnic.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, add a drizzle of creamy sauce like sour cream, chipotle mayo, or a yogurt-based dressing.
  • Shrimp cooks quickly, so keep a close eye on it to avoid overcooking.
  • If you’re prepping ahead of time, you can marinate the shrimp for a few hours in the fridge before grilling.

4. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Cilantro-Lime Butter

No summer barbecue or picnic is complete without corn on the cob. Grilled corn has a smoky sweetness that pairs perfectly with a rich and zesty cilantro-lime butter. It’s easy to make and a great side dish that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the corn with a little olive oil and season with salt. Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are lightly charred.
  3. While the corn is grilling, make the cilantro-lime butter by mixing the softened butter, cilantro, lime zest, lime juice, and salt in a small bowl.
  4. Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and brush it generously with the cilantro-lime butter.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the smoky, zesty goodness.

Tips:

  • To add some heat, sprinkle the corn with chili powder or cayenne pepper before grilling.
  • For a creamy twist, mix some grated parmesan cheese into the cilantro-lime butter.

5. Grilled Watermelon Salad

If you’re looking for a refreshing and unique picnic dish, a grilled watermelon salad is the perfect choice. Grilling watermelon enhances its sweetness and brings out a smoky flavor that pairs beautifully with fresh herbs, tangy feta, and a light vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small seedless watermelon, cut into thick wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the watermelon wedges with olive oil and season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the watermelon for about 2-3 minutes per side, just until grill marks appear and the fruit is slightly caramelized.
  4. Remove the watermelon from the grill and slice it into bite-sized cubes.
  5. In a large bowl, toss the grilled watermelon with balsamic vinegar, feta cheese, and fresh mint.
  6. Serve the salad cold or at room temperature for a refreshing picnic treat.

Tips:

  • For an extra layer of flavor, drizzle the salad with honey or a balsamic glaze.
  • You can also add some arugula or mixed greens to the salad for more texture.

6. Grilled Sausages with Peppers and Onions

Grilled sausages with peppers and onions is a classic picnic dish that’s hearty, flavorful, and satisfying. The smoky sausages paired with caramelized onions and bell peppers create a mouthwatering combination that will be the star of any picnic spread.

Ingredients:

  • 6 Italian sausages (or your favorite variety)
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 hoagie rolls or baguette slices

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the sausages for about 6-7 minutes per side until fully cooked and browned.
  3. While the sausages are grilling, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the bell peppers and onions and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until they are softened and lightly caramelized.
  4. Once the sausages are done, remove them from the grill and place them in the hoagie rolls.
  5. Top the sausages with the sautéed peppers and onions, and serve hot.

Tips:

  • For a kick of flavor, add a few dashes of hot sauce or mustard to the sausages before serving.
  • If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook the sausages on a stovetop grill pan.

Conclusion

Grilling is one of the best ways to enjoy summer food, and these picnic-friendly recipes offer the perfect combination of flavors and ease. From veggie skewers and juicy grilled chicken to fresh salads and smoky grilled fruit, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and make your next outdoor gathering unforgettable.

Whether you’re packing a picnic basket for the beach or hosting a barbecue in your backyard, these summer grilled recipes are all about making the most of the season’s fresh ingredients and the smoky goodness of the grill. So gather your friends, fire up the grill, and enjoy some delicious food under the summer sun!
