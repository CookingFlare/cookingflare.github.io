How to Make a Classic Caesar Dressing from Scratch


A classic Caesar salad is a timeless dish that has stood the test of time, and one of its most iconic components is the dressing. This creamy, tangy, and savory dressing brings the entire salad together, transforming simple ingredients into something extraordinary. While you can find Caesar dressing in most supermarkets, there is nothing quite like the flavor of homemade Caesar dressing. By making it from scratch, you gain full control over the quality of ingredients and the balance of flavors.

In this post, we will walk you through the process of creating a traditional Caesar dressing at home. With a few simple ingredients and some easy steps, you’ll be able to enjoy a fresh, authentic dressing that will elevate your salads to a whole new level. Let’s dive into the recipe and explore the secrets behind this beloved dressing.

Ingredients You Will Need

Making Caesar dressing from scratch requires just a handful of ingredients. The good news is, you likely already have most of them in your kitchen. Here is what you need:

  • Egg yolks: The base of a traditional Caesar dressing. Egg yolks create a creamy texture and act as an emulsifier, helping the oil and vinegar combine smoothly.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a robust flavor that is a hallmark of Caesar dressing.
  • Anchovies: While anchovies are optional for some recipes, they are key in authentic Caesar dressing. Their salty umami flavor deepens the taste of the dressing.
  • Dijon mustard: A small amount of Dijon mustard enhances the richness of the dressing and adds a tangy bite.
  • Lemon juice: Freshly squeezed lemon juice balances the creaminess of the dressing with a zesty brightness.
  • Worcestershire sauce: This sauce contributes a unique savory depth and a touch of spice.
  • Olive oil: A high-quality olive oil will give the dressing its silky texture and a fruity undertone.
  • Parmesan cheese: Freshly grated Parmesan cheese is essential for that salty, nutty flavor that defines Caesar dressing.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper: To taste, these seasonings will round out the flavors.

Now that you know what ingredients are necessary, let’s go over how to make the dressing itself.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Classic Caesar Dressing

Step 1: Prepare the Garlic and Anchovies

Start by mincing the garlic and anchovies. The garlic should be finely chopped to release its flavor, while the anchovies need to be mashed into a paste. Anchovies are quite salty, so it’s important to use them sparingly. If you’ve never worked with anchovies before, don’t be intimidated—once blended into the dressing, their flavor becomes subtle and deeply savory.

To mash the anchovies, place them on your cutting board and use the side of your knife to crush them. Keep working at them until they form a paste.

Step 2: Combine Egg Yolks and Mustard

In a medium-sized bowl, combine the egg yolks with the Dijon mustard. Whisk them together until smooth. The egg yolks provide the creamy texture for the dressing and help bind the other ingredients together. The mustard adds a slight sharpness and acts as an emulsifier to help the oil and vinegar blend seamlessly.

Step 3: Add the Garlic and Anchovy Paste

Add the minced garlic and mashed anchovy paste to the egg yolks and mustard mixture. Whisk everything together until well combined. The garlic will give the dressing its pungent flavor, while the anchovies will infuse the mixture with a rich, salty depth.

Step 4: Mix in the Lemon Juice and Worcestershire Sauce

Next, squeeze the juice of half a lemon into the mixture. Fresh lemon juice provides the perfect balance to the richness of the egg yolks and oil. It adds a fresh, tangy zing that helps cut through the creaminess of the dressing.

Follow with a few dashes of Worcestershire sauce. This ingredient adds a savory complexity that enhances the overall flavor. Don’t overdo it, as it can be quite potent, but just enough will give your Caesar dressing its signature depth.

Step 5: Slowly Drizzle in the Olive Oil

At this point, it’s time to start adding the olive oil. Drizzle the olive oil slowly into the mixture while whisking constantly. It’s important to add the oil gradually so that it emulsifies properly with the other ingredients. Emulsification means that the oil combines smoothly with the egg yolks and other liquids, creating that rich, velvety texture. If you pour the oil too quickly, the dressing may separate, so be patient during this step.

Step 6: Add Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese

Once the oil has been fully incorporated and the dressing has reached a creamy consistency, it’s time to add the Parmesan cheese. Grate the cheese yourself for the best flavor—pre-grated cheese often lacks the same depth. Add a generous amount of cheese and whisk until the dressing is smooth and creamy. The cheese will melt into the mixture, thickening it and contributing its signature nutty flavor.

Step 7: Season to Taste

Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. You may want to add more lemon juice for extra tang or more anchovy paste for a deeper umami flavor. Adjust the seasonings until you achieve the perfect balance of creamy, tangy, salty, and savory.

Step 8: Let the Dressing Rest

While you could use the dressing immediately, it’s a good idea to let it rest for about 30 minutes in the refrigerator. This allows the flavors to meld together and intensify. The dressing will also thicken slightly as it cools, making it even creamier.

Tips for the Best Caesar Dressing

  • Use high-quality olive oil: The olive oil plays a major role in the flavor of the dressing, so use the best you can find. Extra virgin olive oil will give the dressing a smooth, slightly fruity taste that’s perfect for this recipe.
  • Grate your Parmesan: Freshly grated Parmesan cheese is essential. Pre-grated cheese often lacks flavor and texture, so take the extra time to grate it yourself.
  • Fresh garlic is key: Fresh garlic provides a punch of flavor that dried garlic simply can’t match. It’s worth it to use fresh cloves.
  • Don’t skip the anchovies: While anchovies might seem like an unusual ingredient, they are what gives Caesar dressing its signature savory taste. Don’t worry—you won’t taste individual fish, just the delicious umami flavor they provide.
  • Make extra: Caesar dressing stores well in the refrigerator for about 4-5 days. Make a double batch and use it for other recipes, like drizzling over grilled vegetables, roasted meats, or even as a dipping sauce for crispy fries.

How to Serve Caesar Dressing

While the most popular use of Caesar dressing is for Caesar salad, there are plenty of other ways to enjoy it. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Classic Caesar Salad: Toss the dressing with crisp Romaine lettuce, croutons, and more freshly grated Parmesan for a classic Caesar salad.
  2. Grilled Vegetables: Use Caesar dressing as a creamy sauce for roasted or grilled vegetables, such as asparagus, broccoli, or Brussels sprouts.
  3. Sandwiches and Wraps: Spread Caesar dressing on a sandwich or wrap for an extra burst of flavor. It pairs wonderfully with chicken or turkey.
  4. Dip for Fries or Veggies: Use your homemade Caesar dressing as a dip for crispy fries or raw vegetables like carrots, celery, or cucumber.

Conclusion

Making Caesar dressing from scratch is simple and incredibly rewarding. With just a few ingredients, you can create a rich, creamy, and flavorful dressing that will take your salads to the next level. By following the steps and tips outlined above, you’ll master this classic recipe in no time. Whether you’re serving it with a traditional Caesar salad or using it in other dishes, your homemade dressing will surely impress. Enjoy the process and, most importantly, enjoy the delicious results!
