Grilled Potatoes Three Ways: Recipes for Spud Lovers


If you’re a fan of potatoes, then you’re in for a treat! Grilling potatoes brings out their natural earthy flavor and adds a delightful smoky char that takes these humble tubers to a whole new level. In this blog post, I’ll share three fantastic grilled potato recipes that are perfect for spud lovers. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, looking for a delicious side dish, or simply craving some hearty comfort food, these recipes have got you covered.

Classic Grilled Potato Wedges

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Wash the potatoes thoroughly and cut them into wedges.
  3. In a bowl, toss the potato wedges with olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper until they are evenly coated.
  4. Place the potato wedges on the grill and cook for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and tender.
  5. Remove from the grill and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

These classic grilled potato wedges are crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside, and bursting with flavor. They make a fantastic accompaniment to grilled meats or a satisfying snack on their own.

Cheesy Grilled Potato Foil Packets

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Aluminum foil

Instructions:

  1. Slice the potatoes into thin rounds.
  2. Lay out four sheets of aluminum foil, each large enough to wrap a potato packet.
  3. Divide the potato slices among the foil sheets.
  4. Sprinkle each portion with salt, pepper, and green onions.
  5. Place a tablespoon of butter on top of each portion.
  6. Wrap the foil tightly around the potatoes to create sealed packets.
  7. Grill the foil packets over medium heat for 20-25 minutes until the potatoes are tender.
  8. Carefully open the packets, sprinkle the cheddar cheese over the potatoes, and grill for an additional 5 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

These cheesy grilled potato foil packets are a delightful combination of creamy, cheesy, and savory flavors. They make a fantastic side dish for any grilled meal and are sure to be a hit at your next cookout.

Herb-Infused Grilled Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs baby potatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the halved baby potatoes to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the herb-infused marinade.
  4. Place the potatoes on the grill and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Transfer the grilled potatoes to a serving bowl, garnish with chopped fresh parsley, and serve warm or at room temperature.

This herb-infused grilled potato salad is a refreshing twist on the classic potato salad. The combination of fresh herbs and smoky grilled potatoes creates a delightful medley of flavors that is perfect for summer gatherings and picnics.

So there you have it - three mouthwatering grilled potato recipes to satisfy your spud cravings. Whether you prefer classic wedges, cheesy foil packets, or herb-infused potato salad, these recipes are guaranteed to elevate your potato game and impress your family and friends. Get your grill ready and indulge in the irresistible allure of grilled potatoes!
