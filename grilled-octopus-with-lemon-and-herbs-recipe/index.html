Grilled Octopus with Lemon and Herbs Recipe


Grilled octopus is a delightful dish that combines the tenderness of the octopus with the freshness of lemon and herbs. The charred flavors from the grill add a smoky depth to the dish, making it a perfect choice for seafood lovers. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of grilling octopus to perfection, ensuring that it remains tender and succulent on the inside while acquiring a beautiful char on the outside.

Ingredients

  • 1 octopus (approximately 3 pounds)
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh oregano, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by cleaning the octopus. Rinse it thoroughly under cold water to remove any residual sand or debris. Remove the beak, eyes, and ink sac if present.

  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the octopus and let it cook for about 30 minutes or until tender. You can test the tenderness by inserting a fork into the thickest part of the octopus. If it easily pierces through, it’s ready.

  3. Once cooked, remove the octopus from the pot and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, using a sharp knife, cut the octopus into manageable pieces.

  4. In a bowl, combine the lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped parsley, chopped oregano, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a marinade.

  5. Place the octopus pieces in the marinade and let them sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  6. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If using a charcoal grill, make sure the coals are evenly lit and glowing red.

  7. Once the grill is hot, remove the octopus from the marinade, shaking off any excess liquid. Place the octopus pieces directly on the grill grates.

  8. Grill the octopus for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until it develops a charred appearance. Be careful not to overcook it, as it can become tough and chewy.

  9. Once grilled to perfection, remove the octopus from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.

  10. Serve the grilled octopus with a drizzle of olive oil and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Garnish with additional chopped parsley and oregano if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To enhance the flavor of the octopus, you can add additional herbs such as thyme or rosemary to the marinade.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, consider adding a pinch of red pepper flakes to the marinade.
  • Grilled octopus pairs well with a variety of side dishes, such as roasted potatoes, Greek salad, or grilled vegetables.
  • Leftover grilled octopus can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It can be enjoyed cold or reheated gently in a pan with a little olive oil.

Enjoy the unique combination of flavors and textures in this grilled octopus with lemon and herbs recipe. The tender meat, infused with the tanginess of lemon and the aromatic herbs, will surely impress your taste buds. So fire up the grill and indulge in this delightful seafood dish that is perfect for any occasion.
