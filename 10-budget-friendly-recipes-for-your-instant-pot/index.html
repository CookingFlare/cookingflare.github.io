10 Budget-Friendly Recipes for Your Instant Pot


The Instant Pot has become a kitchen staple for good reason. This versatile appliance makes cooking efficient and energizes weeknight meals with flavor-packed dishes that usually take hours to perfect. For budget-conscious households, the Instant Pot also shines as a brilliant tool for preparing affordable, hearty, and delicious meals. From soups and stews to hearty mains and creative sides, we’ve got ten budget-friendly Instant Pot recipes to help you stretch your dollar without sacrificing flavor.

1. Hearty Lentil Soup

Lentils are a top-tier ingredient for any budget-friendly dish. They’re affordable, packed with protein, and endlessly versatile. With the Instant Pot, you can have a rich and hearty lentil soup ready in under an hour.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and add a drizzle of oil. Sauté onions, carrots, celery, and garlic for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add cumin, smoked paprika, lentils, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Give it a good stir.
  3. Cancel “Sauté,” seal the lid, and pressure cook on “High” for 15 minutes.
  4. Allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then do a quick release.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then serve with a side of bread or salad for a complete meal.

2. Chicken and Rice Burrito Bowls

Skip takeout! This recipe is perfect for a crowd-pleasing dinner that doesn’t break the bank. Plus, it’s customizable for picky eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (more affordable than chicken breasts)
  • 1 cup white or brown rice
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup salsa
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken thighs in the Instant Pot. Season generously with chili powder, garlic powder, and cumin.
  2. Add rice, chicken broth, and salsa to the pot (make sure the rice is submerged).
  3. Place the frozen corn and black beans on top without stirring.
  4. Seal the lid and pressure cook on “High” for 12 minutes.
  5. Allow natural release for 10 minutes, then shred the chicken with forks and fluff the rice before serving.

3. Creamy Mac and Cheese

Ditch the boxed version—this Instant Pot mac and cheese is cost-effective and cheesy goodness at its finest!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Add macaroni, water, butter, and salt into the Instant Pot. Stir slightly to prevent sticking.
  2. Pressure cook on “High” for 4 minutes. Perform a quick release.
  3. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode, stir in milk, and gradually add the cheese until melted.
  4. Stir well and serve warm!

4. Savory Pulled Pork

Pulled pork may sound like a splurge, but buying a budget-friendly pork shoulder can make this a cost-effective household favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder (or Boston butt)
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rub the pork shoulder with spices, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add sliced onion and chicken broth to the Instant Pot, then place the pork shoulder on top.
  3. Pressure cook on “High” for 60 minutes. Once complete, let the pressure release naturally for 15 minutes.
  4. Remove the pork, shred it, then mix with barbecue sauce. Serve on buns or with coleslaw!

5. Vegetarian Chili

This vegetarian-friendly chili is rich in fiber and flavor while being light on your wallet. Use pantry staples to make this dish practical for any weeknight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onions, garlic, and bell peppers in the Instant Pot using “Sauté” mode for 4-5 minutes.
  2. Add all remaining ingredients and stir well.
  3. Pressure cook on “High” for 15 minutes. Perform a quick release and give it another stir.
  4. Top with shredded cheese, sour cream, or tortilla chips.

6. Budget-Friendly Beef Stew

Using inexpensive cuts like beef chuck roast makes this hearty stew a great way to use your Instant Pot without overspending.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into cubes
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 potatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary

Instructions:

  1. On “Sauté” mode, brown the beef cubes in the Instant Pot for 3-4 minutes. Set aside.
  2. Add onions and tomato paste, cooking for another 3 minutes. Deglaze the pan with a splash of broth.
  3. Add beef, carrots, potatoes, remaining broth, and seasonings to the pot.
  4. Pressure cook on “High” for 35 minutes. Perform a natural pressure release for 10 minutes.

7. Instant Pot Oatmeal

Breakfast on a budget is easier than ever with steel-cut oatmeal prepared overnight or in the morning using your Instant Pot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup steel-cut oats
  • 3 cups water
  • pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Place oats, water, and salt in the Instant Pot.
  2. Pressure cook on “High” for 4 minutes, then allow natural release for 10 minutes.
  3. Stir and top with fruits, nuts, or honey for added flavor.

8. Potato Leek Soup

Potato leek soup is hearty, creamy, and costs just a few dollars to make. Pair it with some crusty bread, and you’ll have a comforting meal.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 leeks, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté sliced leeks with butter in the Instant Pot for 5 minutes.
  2. Add potatoes, vegetable broth, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Pressure cook on “High” for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Blend the soup using an immersion blender until creamy. Stir in the heavy cream and serve.

9. Sweet and Savory Teriyaki Chicken

Anyone can recreate the sweet and tangy magic of teriyaki chicken with just a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ginger

Instructions:

  1. Combine soy sauce, honey, water, garlic, and ginger in the Instant Pot.
  2. Place chicken thighs in the sauce.
  3. Pressure cook on “High” for 10 minutes. Perform a quick release.
  4. Thicken sauce with cornstarch (if desired) and serve over rice.

10. Budget-Friendly Beans and Rice

Simple, nutritious, and packed with protein, beans and rice is a classic dish that’s perfect for feeding a family on a budget.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried black beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 4 cups water or broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin

Instructions:

  1. Add all ingredients to the Instant Pot.
  2. Pressure cook on “High” for 22 minutes. Perform a natural release for 10 minutes.
  3. Fluff with a fork and season before serving.

These budget-friendly Instant Pot recipes prove that you don’t need an extravagant grocery list to create meals packed with flavor. With a little creativity and basic kitchen staples, your Instant Pot can help put delicious and affordable meals on the table every day of the week.
