10 Easy Vegan and Vegetarian Lunchbox Recipes for Kids


Packing a nutritious and delicious lunch for your kids can sometimes feel like a challenge, especially if you’re aiming to keep it healthy, kid-friendly, and quick to prepare. Whether you have a picky eater or a child who loves to try new flavors, there are plenty of vegan and vegetarian lunchbox recipes that are simple to make and packed with essential nutrients. In this post, we’ll explore 10 easy vegan and vegetarian lunchbox ideas that will have your little ones excited for lunchtime.

1. Vegan Veggie Wraps

Wraps are a great way to pack a variety of veggies into your child’s lunch, and they’re easy to customize based on what your child likes. You can use a whole wheat tortilla or a gluten-free wrap if your child has dietary restrictions. For this recipe, spread a thin layer of hummus on the wrap, then layer in some shredded carrots, cucumber slices, bell pepper strips, and spinach. Roll it up tightly and slice into bite-sized pieces. You can add some avocado or a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for added flavor and nutrients.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Easy to hold and eat, even for younger children
  • Packed with fiber, vitamins, and minerals
  • Can be easily modified based on your child’s preferences

2. Mini Vegan Pizzas

Making mini pizzas is a fun way to involve your kids in the kitchen. For a vegan version, start with whole wheat pita bread or English muffins as the base. Spread a small amount of tomato sauce on top and add some vegan cheese (you can also use a dairy-free mozzarella alternative). Let your child add their favorite veggie toppings like mushrooms, olives, bell peppers, or spinach. Bake in the oven for about 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Cut into quarters and pack them in the lunchbox.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Kids love pizza, and they’ll enjoy making their own mini versions
  • Packed with fiber and healthy fats
  • A great way to introduce kids to new veggies

3. Chickpea Salad Sandwiches

Chickpeas are a fantastic source of protein, and they make an excellent base for a vegan “chicken” salad sandwich. To make the filling, mash cooked chickpeas with a fork and mix with vegan mayo, mustard, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Add chopped celery, onions, and dill for extra flavor and crunch. Spread the chickpea mixture on whole grain bread or a soft roll and pack it in your child’s lunchbox. You can also use the mixture as a dip for crackers or veggie sticks.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Packed with protein, fiber, and healthy fats
  • Can be made in advance and stored in the fridge
  • Customizable with different veggies and seasonings

4. Vegan Mac and Cheese

Who doesn’t love mac and cheese? You can make a vegan version that is just as creamy and delicious. Cook elbow macaroni according to package instructions, then make a quick cheese sauce by blending soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, and turmeric until smooth. Mix the sauce with the pasta and pack it into a thermos or container to keep it warm until lunchtime. Add steamed broccoli or peas for an extra boost of nutrition.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • A kid-friendly comfort food with a healthy twist
  • Provides protein and healthy fats from the cashews
  • Can be packed with veggies for added nutrition

5. Veggie Sushi Rolls

Sushi rolls don’t have to be complicated or require raw fish. You can make simple veggie sushi rolls that are fun to eat and packed with nutrients. Start by laying out a sheet of nori (seaweed), and spread a thin layer of cooked sushi rice on top. Add thin slices of cucumber, avocado, and carrots, then roll it up tightly. Slice into bite-sized pieces and pack them in a lunchbox with some soy sauce for dipping.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Fun to make and eat
  • Packed with healthy fats and fiber
  • Great for introducing kids to new flavors and textures

6. Lentil and Veggie Stew

A hearty stew is a great way to ensure your child is getting a balanced meal. For this recipe, cook lentils with onions, carrots, celery, and tomatoes, and season with herbs like thyme and rosemary. Once the lentils are tender, stir in some leafy greens like spinach or kale for added nutrients. This stew can be made in bulk and stored in the fridge for several days. You can pack it in a thermos to keep it warm, or serve it chilled if your child prefers.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • High in protein and fiber, helping kids stay full longer
  • Packed with vegetables for essential vitamins and minerals
  • Easy to make in large batches for meal prep

7. Homemade Veggie Nuggets

If your child loves nuggets, try making a healthier homemade version using veggies and beans. Start by blending cooked sweet potatoes or cauliflower with cooked chickpeas, breadcrumbs, and seasonings like garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika. Shape the mixture into small nugget-sized patties and bake them in the oven until golden and crispy. These nuggets are a great way to sneak in some vegetables while keeping your child’s lunch fun and familiar.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Kid-friendly and easy to eat with hands
  • Packed with fiber and protein
  • Can be made in advance and frozen for later use

8. Vegan Burrito Bowl

Burrito bowls are a great lunch option that’s easy to pack and customize based on your child’s tastes. Start with a base of brown rice or quinoa, then add black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, avocado, and a dollop of vegan sour cream. Top it with some shredded lettuce or spinach and a sprinkle of lime juice. You can even add salsa or guacamole for extra flavor. Keep the ingredients separate in different containers if you’re worried about them getting soggy before lunchtime.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Packed with plant-based protein and fiber
  • Can be easily customized to suit different tastes
  • Full of vitamins and minerals from the veggies

9. Vegan “Meatball” Sub

Making a vegan meatball sub is an easy way to enjoy a classic lunch in a healthy, plant-based version. For the “meatballs,” use lentils or chickpeas as the base, and blend them with breadcrumbs, garlic, and Italian seasoning. Roll the mixture into balls and bake them until golden. Serve the meatballs in a whole grain sub roll with marinara sauce and some vegan cheese (optional). Pack this in your child’s lunchbox with some extra sauce for dipping.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • A fun and familiar lunch option that’s vegan-friendly
  • High in plant-based protein and fiber
  • Can be made ahead and frozen for convenience

10. Fruit and Nut Butter Snack Box

If your child prefers simple, no-cook options, create a snack box filled with fresh fruit and a side of nut butter for dipping. Pack some apple slices, banana halves, or grapes alongside a small container of almond or peanut butter. You can also include some whole grain crackers or rice cakes for added crunch. This easy, nutrient-packed lunch is perfect for busy mornings when you don’t have a lot of time to prepare something elaborate.

Why It’s Great for Kids:

  • Packed with healthy fats and protein
  • Provides natural sugars from the fruit for energy
  • Simple and quick to prepare

Final Thoughts

Preparing vegan and vegetarian lunchbox recipes for kids doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. With a little planning and creativity, you can create meals that are both nutritious and exciting for your little ones. From wraps and pizzas to salads and hearty stews, the options are endless. These recipes not only provide essential vitamins, minerals, and protein but also introduce kids to a wide variety of plant-based foods that can help them develop healthy eating habits for life.

Next time you’re packing lunch for your child, try out some of these easy and fun vegan and vegetarian lunchbox ideas. Your kids will be delighted, and you’ll feel great knowing they’re getting the nutrients they need to thrive throughout the school day.
