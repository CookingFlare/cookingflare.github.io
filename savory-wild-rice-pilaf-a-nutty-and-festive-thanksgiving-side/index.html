Savory Wild Rice Pilaf: A Nutty and Festive Thanksgiving Side


Wild rice, with its nutty flavor and chewy texture, is a perfect addition to any Thanksgiving celebration. In this recipe, we’ll combine it with a medley of aromatics, herbs, and spices to create a savory pilaf that’s sure to become a new family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup wild rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth, warmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and oyster)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. Rinse the wild rice in a fine-mesh strainer and drain well. In a medium saucepan, bring the warmed chicken broth to a boil. Add the wild rice, cover, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 40-45 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  2. While the rice is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Add the mixed mushrooms to the skillet and cook, stirring occasionally, until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the dried thyme and paprika. Cook for 1 minute, until the spices are fragrant.
  6. Once the wild rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork to separate the grains. Add the cooked wild rice to the skillet with the mushroom mixture and stir to combine.
  7. Season the pilaf with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Transfer the pilaf to a serving dish and sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor to the pilaf, you can sauté some chopped celery or carrots along with the onion and mushrooms.
  • If you prefer a creamier pilaf, you can stir in some grated cheddar cheese or crumbled feta cheese.
  • To make the pilaf more substantial, you can add some cooked sausage, such as andouille or kielbasa, to the skillet with the mushroom mixture.
  • You can also customize the pilaf to your taste by adding some dried herbs, such as sage or rosemary, or some grated nutmeg or cinnamon.

Why This Recipe Works

This savory wild rice pilaf is a perfect side dish for Thanksgiving because it’s easy to make ahead of time and can be reheated in the oven or on the stovetop. The combination of wild rice, aromatics, and spices creates a complex and satisfying flavor profile that’s sure to please even the pickiest eaters. Plus, the pilaf is a great way to use up any leftover vegetables or herbs you have on hand.

Conclusion

This savory wild rice pilaf is a delicious and festive side dish that’s sure to become a new family favorite. With its nutty flavor, chewy texture, and medley of aromatics and spices, it’s the perfect addition to any Thanksgiving celebration. So why not give it a try this year and see why it’s a recipe that’s sure to be passed down for generations to come?
