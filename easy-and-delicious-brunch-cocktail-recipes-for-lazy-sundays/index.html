Easy and Delicious Brunch Cocktail Recipes for Lazy Sundays


Lazy Sundays are the best, aren’t they? No alarm clocks, no rush hour traffic, no stressful meetings. Just a day to relax, recharge, and indulge in some delicious food and drinks. And what’s better than a refreshing brunch cocktail to start your day off right?

If you’re anything like me, you’re always on the lookout for new and exciting recipes to try. And if you’re looking for something easy to make and packed with flavor, you’re in the right place. In this post, we’ll be sharing some of our favorite brunch cocktail recipes that are perfect for lazy Sundays.

Recipe 1: Bellini Mimosa

Who says you can’t mix things up a bit when it comes to your classic mimosa? Try adding some peach puree to your champagne and orange juice for a sweet and tangy twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup champagne
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons peach puree
  • Slice of peach for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a champagne flute with ice.
  2. Pour in the champagne and orange juice.
  3. Add a splash of peach puree.
  4. Stir gently.
  5. Garnish with a slice of peach.

Recipe 2: Virgin Mary

If you’re looking for a non-alcoholic option, this Virgin Mary is a game-changer. With the addition of some fresh herbs and spices, you’ll feel like you’re sipping on a spa drink.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups tomato juice
  • 1 cup sparkling water
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a glass with ice.
  2. Pour in the tomato juice.
  3. Add the sparkling water, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, oregano, and basil.
  4. Stir well.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs.

Recipe 3: Cucumber Collins

This refreshing cocktail is perfect for warm weather. With the addition of some cucumber and lime, you’ll be transported to a tropical paradise.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1/2 ounce lime juice
  • 1/2 ounce simple syrup
  • 1/2 cup cucumber slices
  • Lime wheel for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add the gin, lime juice, and simple syrup.
  3. Muddle the cucumber slices in the shaker.
  4. Shake well.
  5. Strain into a chilled glass filled with ice.
  6. Garnish with a lime wheel.

Recipe 4: Spicy Mango Margarita

If you’re looking for something a little more adventurous, this spicy mango margarita is the way to go. With the addition of some jalapeno peppers and mango puree, you’ll be in for a real treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ounces tequila
  • 1 ounce mango puree
  • 1/2 ounce lime juice
  • 1/2 ounce agave nectar
  • 1/4 ounce jalapeno simple syrup
  • Salt for rimming glass
  • Lime wheel and slice of mango for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rim a glass with salt.
  2. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  3. Add the tequila, mango puree, lime juice, agave nectar, and jalapeno simple syrup.
  4. Shake well.
  5. Strain into the prepared glass.
  6. Garnish with a lime wheel and slice of mango.

Recipe 5: French 75

This classic cocktail is a brunch staple. With the addition of some champagne and lemon, you’ll be feeling like you’re in a French café.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1/2 ounce lemon juice
  • 1/2 ounce simple syrup
  • 1/2 cup champagne
  • Lemon twist for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup.
  3. Shake well.
  4. Strain into a chilled glass filled with ice.
  5. Top with champagne.
  6. Garnish with a lemon twist.

And there you have it, folks! Five delicious brunch cocktail recipes that are sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you’re looking for something classic and elegant or something a little more adventurous, there’s something for everyone on this list. So go ahead, grab your favorite ingredients, and get mixing!

Happy lazy Sunday, everyone!
