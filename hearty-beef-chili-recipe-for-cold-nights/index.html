Hearty Beef Chili Recipe for Cold Nights


As the days get shorter and the chill in the air intensifies, there’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of hearty beef chili to warm you up from the inside out. Whether you’re looking to feed a crowd or just craving a cozy meal for yourself, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. Packed with tender beef, flavorful spices, and a variety of textures, this chili will keep you satisfied and toasty through the coldest of nights.

In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps of creating a rich, savory beef chili that is perfect for sharing with family and friends. Plus, we’ll offer plenty of tips for customization so that you can make it your own.

Ingredients for Hearty Beef Chili

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s gather the ingredients. This recipe uses simple yet flavorful components that come together to create a dish bursting with depth and warmth. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Beef Chili Base:

  • 2 pounds of ground beef – Choose a mixture of lean and fatty cuts to get the perfect balance of flavor and texture.
  • 1 large onion, diced – Onions are key for adding savory sweetness to the chili.
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced – Garlic provides an aromatic foundation that complements the beef.
  • 1 large bell pepper, diced – A pop of color and slight sweetness adds complexity.
  • 1-2 jalapeño peppers, minced (optional) – For those who like a little heat, jalapeños add a great kick.
  • 2 cans (14.5 ounces each) diced tomatoes – Tomatoes create the base of the chili, offering acidity and richness.
  • 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed – Beans add texture and protein.
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed – Black beans add extra heartiness.
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste – Tomato paste helps thicken the chili and intensifies the tomato flavor.
  • 3 cups beef broth – The broth is essential for the chili’s depth and richness.

For the Spices and Seasoning:

  • 2 tablespoons chili powder – Chili powder is the backbone of any good chili, bringing both heat and smokiness.
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin – Cumin gives the chili an earthy warmth that balances out the other spices.
  • 1 tablespoon paprika – Adds a touch of sweetness and smokiness.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano – Oregano provides a fresh, herby note that pairs well with beef.
  • 1 teaspoon salt – For seasoning.
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper – A pinch of pepper adds subtle heat and enhances the other flavors.
  • 1 teaspoon cocoa powder (optional) – A small amount of cocoa powder adds a rich depth to the chili, enhancing the savory flavors without making it taste like chocolate.
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional) – If you like your chili spicy, a dash of hot sauce can give it an extra kick.

For Garnishes (optional but recommended):

  • Sour cream – The coolness of sour cream balances the heat and adds creaminess.
  • Shredded cheddar cheese – Melted cheese adds richness and flavor.
  • Chopped green onions – A fresh, sharp contrast to the richness of the chili.
  • Chopped cilantro – For a burst of freshness and color.
  • Tortilla chips or cornbread – To scoop or serve alongside, these provide a crispy contrast to the smooth chili.

Instructions: How to Make Hearty Beef Chili

Now that you have all the ingredients ready, it’s time to start cooking. This recipe is easy to follow, with minimal steps involved, but the result is a flavorful and comforting dish that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Step 1: Brown the Ground Beef

Heat a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef to the pot, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Brown the beef until it’s fully cooked through, which should take about 6-8 minutes. Once browned, remove any excess fat by carefully draining it.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and jalapeños (if using). Sauté the vegetables for about 5 minutes, or until they’re softened and fragrant. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Step 3: Add the Tomato Base

Now, it’s time to build the base of your chili. Stir in the tomato paste, diced tomatoes (with their juices), and beef broth. The broth will help bring everything together, creating a rich and savory sauce that will simmer and develop more flavor as it cooks.

Step 4: Stir in the Spices

Next, add all the spices: chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, cocoa powder (if using), salt, black pepper, and hot sauce (if using). Stir the mixture well to ensure that all the spices are evenly distributed throughout the chili. These spices are what truly elevate the dish, creating a perfect balance of heat, smokiness, and depth.

Step 5: Add the Beans

Once the spices have been stirred in, add the kidney beans and black beans to the pot. Beans are essential for providing substance and texture to the chili, as well as adding a bit of sweetness to balance the heat. Stir everything together to combine.

Step 6: Let It Simmer

Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the chili simmer for at least 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the more the flavors will meld and intensify, so feel free to let it go for an hour or even longer. If the chili becomes too thick during cooking, add more beef broth to reach your desired consistency.

Step 7: Taste and Adjust Seasoning

Before serving, taste the chili and adjust the seasoning as necessary. You can add more salt, pepper, or chili powder to taste. If you like your chili spicier, this is the time to add extra hot sauce or some crushed red pepper flakes.

Step 8: Serve and Garnish

Once the chili has simmered to perfection, it’s ready to serve. Ladle it into bowls and top with your favorite garnishes, such as a dollop of sour cream, a handful of shredded cheese, fresh green onions, and chopped cilantro. You can also serve it with tortilla chips or cornbread on the side for an added crunch.

Tips for the Perfect Beef Chili

  • Use the right cut of beef: While ground beef is the classic choice, you can also use beef chuck that has been chopped into small pieces for an even richer texture. The fat content in the beef contributes significantly to the flavor, so don’t skimp on the fat.
  • Let it simmer: The longer you let your chili simmer, the more flavor it will develop. A slow simmer allows the spices to infuse the broth, creating a deeper, more complex flavor profile.
  • Customize the heat: Adjust the level of spiciness by increasing or decreasing the amount of jalapeños, chili powder, and hot sauce. You can also experiment with different types of chili peppers to change the flavor.
  • Make it a one-pot meal: If you want a more filling chili, you can add additional vegetables like carrots, zucchini, or corn to the pot. These vegetables will add extra texture and sweetness, balancing out the spiciness.
  • Prepare ahead: Chili is even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld together. You can make it ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to three days, or freeze it for up to three months. Just reheat on the stovetop, adding more broth if needed.

Variations of Beef Chili

While this hearty beef chili recipe is delicious on its own, it’s also very versatile. Here are a few ways you can customize it to suit your tastes:

1. Vegetarian Chili

For a vegetarian version of this chili, simply omit the beef and add more beans (such as pinto beans or chickpeas) and vegetables. You can also use plant-based ground meat alternatives to mimic the texture of beef.

2. Spicy Chili

If you’re a fan of heat, ramp up the spiciness with extra chili powder, a variety of fresh peppers, or even a couple of tablespoons of chipotle in adobo sauce. The smoky chipotle will add depth while increasing the heat.

3. Slow Cooker Chili

To make this recipe in a slow cooker, brown the beef and sauté the vegetables in a skillet first. Then, transfer everything to the slow cooker, including all the spices, beans, tomatoes, and broth. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours. The slow cooker method allows the flavors to develop even further, and you’ll have a hands-off meal ready by dinnertime.

Why This Hearty Beef Chili Is Perfect for Cold Nights

There’s something incredibly comforting about a warm bowl of chili on a cold night. This recipe combines the richness of beef with the warmth of spices and the heartiness of beans to create a meal that feels like a hug in a bowl. Whether you’re serving it for a family dinner or bringing it to a winter potluck, this chili is sure to satisfy and keep everyone cozy.

The Health Benefits of Chili

While chili is a filling and indulgent dish, it also offers several health benefits, especially when made with lean beef and plenty of vegetables. The beans provide fiber and protein, while the tomatoes offer antioxidants like lycopene. Plus, the chili peppers used in the recipe contain capsaicin, a compound known for boosting metabolism and improving circulation.

A Family-Friendly Meal

This chili recipe is not only delicious but also easy to make in large batches, making it perfect for feeding a family or a crowd. You can serve it with various toppings, so everyone can customize their bowl to their liking. Kids will love the cheesy and creamy toppings, while adults can enjoy the full depth of flavors in the chili itself.

Conclusion

This hearty beef chili recipe is the perfect comfort food for those chilly evenings when you crave something filling and flavorful. With a rich blend of spices, tender beef, and beans, this chili is sure to become a go-to recipe for cold nights. Plus, its versatility means you can adjust the heat level, add extra vegetables, or make it in a slow cooker for ultimate convenience. Give this recipe a try, and let it warm your soul while you enjoy the cozy comforts of a home-cooked meal.
