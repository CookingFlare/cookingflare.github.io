One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Potatoes


If you’re looking for a delicious and easy weeknight dinner that requires minimal cleanup, then look no further than this One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Potatoes recipe. With just a few simple ingredients and a single pot, you can create a flavorful and satisfying meal that will impress your family and friends. So let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large oven-safe skillet or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then add them to the hot skillet, skin side down. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken thighs and cook for an additional 2 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté for 1 minute, or until fragrant.

  4. Add the halved baby potatoes to the skillet and season with salt, pepper, chopped rosemary, and thyme. Stir to coat the potatoes in the aromatic mixture.

  5. Arrange the lemon slices on top of the potatoes, then place the chicken thighs back into the skillet, skin side up. Drizzle any remaining olive oil from the skillet over the chicken and potatoes.

  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the potatoes are tender.

  7. Once cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and ensure juicy chicken.

  8. Serve the One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Potatoes hot, garnished with some fresh herbs if desired. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, try using different herbs like sage, oregano, or basil. You can also add some diced onions or bell peppers for added flavor and texture.

  • If you prefer boneless, skinless chicken breasts, you can use them instead of chicken thighs. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly, as chicken breasts cook faster than thighs.

  • Feel free to customize this recipe to your taste by adding your favorite vegetables like carrots, broccoli, or green beans. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure everything is cooked through.

  • Leftovers of this One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Potatoes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Potatoes is a simple yet flavorful dish that is perfect for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. With its zesty lemon, fragrant herbs, and tender chicken, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite. Plus, with just one pot to clean, you’ll have more time to relax and enjoy your meal. So give it a try and let your taste buds be delighted!
