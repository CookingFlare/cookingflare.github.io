Winter Baking: 8 Recipes for Comforting Desserts


As the days grow shorter and the chill of winter settles in, there’s nothing quite like the warmth of a kitchen filled with the sweet aroma of freshly baked desserts. Winter baking is a delightful way to bring comfort and joy to the season. Whether you’re cozying up by the fire or gathering with loved ones for festive celebrations, these eight comforting dessert recipes will warm your heart and satisfy your sweet tooth.

1. Spiced Apple Crisp

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work best)
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 cup of rolled oats
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Vanilla ice cream (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with half of the brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Spread the mixture evenly in a greased baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine the oats, flour, remaining brown sugar, and melted butter. Mix until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples evenly.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.

2. Chocolate Peppermint Brownies

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 cup of chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon of peppermint extract
  • Crushed candy canes (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9 inch baking pan.
  2. Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over low heat. Remove from heat and stir in the sugar, eggs, and vanilla.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the cocoa, flour, salt, and baking powder. Gradually stir this into the butter mixture.
  4. Fold in the chocolate chips and peppermint extract.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 20-25 minutes. Let cool before cutting into squares.
  6. Top with crushed candy canes for a festive finish.

3. Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tablespoon of ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 3/4 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup of molasses
  • Royal icing (for decorating)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, cream the butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in the egg and molasses.
  3. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until a dough forms. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into desired shapes. Place on the prepared baking sheets.
  6. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Once cooled, decorate with royal icing.

4. Hot Chocolate Lava Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • Whipped cream or marshmallows (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and grease four ramekins.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips and butter together, stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly.
  3. Whisk in the powdered sugar until well combined. Then add the eggs, egg yolks, and vanilla, mixing until smooth.
  4. Fold in the flour and salt until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter between the ramekins and place them on a baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 12-14 minutes. Let cool for 1 minute before inverting onto plates. Serve with whipped cream or marshmallows.

5. Eggnog Cheesecake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 packages (8 oz each) of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup of eggnog
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Press into the bottom of a springform pan.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth. Add the eggnog, eggs, vanilla, and nutmeg, mixing until combined.
  4. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust.
  5. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until set. Let cool, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.

6. Pecan Pie Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of pecans, chopped
  • 3/4 cup of corn syrup
  • 3/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, butter, sugar, and salt until crumbly. Press into the bottom of the prepared dish.
  3. Bake for 15 minutes.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla. Stir in the chopped pecans.
  5. Pour the mixture over the baked crust and return to the oven for another 30 minutes.
  6. Let cool before cutting into bars.

7. Cranberry Orange Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup of orange juice
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups of fresh cranberries
  • Zest of 1 orange

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the melted butter, orange juice, and eggs. Stir in the orange zest.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry, mixing until just combined. Fold in the cranberries.
  5. Divide the batter among the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.

8. Maple Pudding Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Gradually stir in the milk and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Pour the batter into the prepared dish. Drizzle the maple syrup over the top.
  4. Bake for 40-45 minutes. The pudding will rise and form a cake-like top with a gooey maple layer below.
  5. Serve warm, drizzled with additional maple syrup if desired.

Winter baking isn’t just about the recipes; it’s about creating memories and sharing warmth with those you love. Each of these comforting desserts brings something special to the table, whether it’s the festive flavors of gingerbread, the rich decadence of chocolate lava cake, or the wholesome goodness of spiced apple crisp. So, gather your ingredients, invite friends and family into your kitchen, and embrace the joy of baking this winter season. Enjoy the laughter, the love, and, of course, the delicious treats that make winter a little sweeter!
