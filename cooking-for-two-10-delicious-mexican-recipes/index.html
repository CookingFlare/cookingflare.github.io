Cooking for Two: 10 Delicious Mexican Recipes


Mexican cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and heartwarming traditions. When you’re cooking for two, it’s an opportunity to explore these rich culinary delights in smaller, more intimate portions. Whether you’re planning a romantic evening, catching up with a friend, or simply savoring the joy of good food, these 10 Mexican recipes will transform your mealtime into a feast of authenticity and flavor.

1. Chicken Tinga Tacos

Start your Mexican-inspired journey with Chicken Tinga tacos. This traditional dish combines shredded chicken simmered in a smoky tomato and chipotle sauce, served on warm tortillas.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas

Instructions

  1. Boil the chicken breasts in salted water until fully cooked. Shred and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, chipotle peppers, and garlic until smooth.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the onion until translucent, pour in the blended sauce, and season.
  4. Add shredded chicken and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Serve on corn tortillas with your choice of toppings like cilantro, lime, or crema.

2. Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas

Crispy tostadas topped with tangy shrimp ceviche make for a light yet flavorful dish that’s perfect for sharing.

Ingredients

  • ½ pound raw shrimp, deveined and diced
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 diced tomato
  • ½ cucumber, peeled and diced
  • ¼ red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, minced
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Salt to taste
  • Corn tostadas

Instructions

  1. Marinate the diced shrimp in lime and lemon juice for 30 minutes to “cook” it.
  2. Combine the shrimp with tomatoes, cucumber, onion, jalapeño, and cilantro. Add salt to taste.
  3. Spoon the ceviche onto tostadas and serve immediately.

3. Chiles Rellenos

Chiles Rellenos are roasted poblano peppers stuffed with cheese, dipped in egg batter, and fried to perfection, making them an unbeatable comfort food for two.

Ingredients

  • 2 large poblano peppers
  • 1 cup shredded Oaxaca or Monterey Jack cheese
  • 2 eggs, separated
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Ranchero sauce (optional) for serving

Instructions

  1. Char the poblano peppers over an open flame or under a broiler, then peel and deseed them carefully.
  2. Stuff with cheese, taking care not to tear the peppers.
  3. Beat egg whites until stiff, then mix in yolks gently.
  4. Dust the peppers with flour, coat them in the egg batter, and fry in hot oil until golden.
  5. Serve with or without ranchero sauce.

4. Guacamole and Homemade Corn Chips

Nothing says “Mexican cuisine” like creamy guacamole paired with crisp homemade chips. This simple yet satisfying dish is perfect for snacking or as an appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • ½ lime, juiced
  • ¼ small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 corn tortillas
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Mash avocados and mix in lime juice, onion, tomato, and cilantro. Season with salt.
  2. Cut corn tortillas into triangles, fry in hot oil until golden, and drain on paper towels.
  3. Serve the guacamole with warm, crispy chips.

5. Pork Carnitas

Succulent and tender pork carnitas can be easily made for two, offering a flavorful main dish for tacos or rice bowls.

Ingredients

  • ½ pound pork shoulder, cut into small chunks
  • 1 orange, juiced
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ½ teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Place pork chunks in a skillet; add orange juice, lime juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and enough water to barely cover the meat.
  2. Cook uncovered on medium heat until the liquid evaporates, and then fry the pork in its own fat until crispy.
  3. Serve carnitas in tortillas with toppings like onions, cilantro, and salsa.

6. Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

Whether it’s a side dish or a snack, Mexican street corn is a delightful combination of smoky, tangy, and cheesy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 ears of corn
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons crumbled cotija cheese
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions

  1. Grill the corn until charred.
  2. Slather each cob with mayonnaise, sprinkle with cotija cheese and chili powder, and serve with lime wedges.

7. Enchiladas Verde

These green enchiladas are bursting with bright tomatillo flavor and are easy to prepare for a comforting dinner.

Ingredients

  • 4 corn tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cooked chicken
  • 2 cups tomatillo salsa (store-bought or homemade)
  • ½ cup shredded cheese (like queso fresco or Monterey Jack)
  • Sour cream (optional)

Instructions

  1. Warm the tortillas slightly, then fill each with shredded chicken and roll tightly.
  2. Place the enchiladas in a baking dish, pour tomatillo salsa on top, and sprinkle with cheese.
  3. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15 minutes or until bubbly. Top with sour cream if desired.

8. Frijoles Charros (Mexican Cowboy Beans)

These hearty beans are loaded with smoky flavors and are perfect as a standalone dish or a side.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup pinto beans, cooked
  • 2 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • ¼ onion, diced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 pickled jalapeño, chopped

Instructions

  1. Fry bacon in a pot until crispy. Remove some of the grease, leaving a little for flavor.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and tomato to the bacon and sauté.
  3. Stir in beans and pickled jalapeño, cooking everything together for 10 minutes.

9. Beef Picadillo

This flavorful ground beef dish is versatile and can be served with rice, tortillas, or as a filling for empanadas.

Ingredients

  • ½ pound ground beef
  • 1 small potato, diced
  • 1 small carrot, diced
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • ¼ onion, chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ½ cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook ground beef in a skillet until browned, then remove excess fat.
  2. Add potato, carrot, onion, garlic, and tomato. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in peas, season with salt and pepper, and cook for another 5 minutes.

10. Arroz con Leche (Mexican Rice Pudding)

End your meal on a sweet note with a comforting bowl of arroz con leche.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup rice
  • 2 cups milk
  • ½ cup condensed milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ¼ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Raisins (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice and cook it with the cinnamon stick in water until tender.
  2. Add milk, condensed milk, and vanilla extract to the cooked rice and simmer until creamy.
  3. Serve warm, topped with raisins if desired.

These 10 Mexican recipes are perfect for crafting unforgettable meals for two. Whether it’s a casual weeknight dinner or a special occasion, these dishes embrace the bold and comforting flavors of Mexican cuisine, allowing you to embark on a culinary journey that brings tradition and flavor to your table. Give them a try, and enjoy the magic of cooking together!
