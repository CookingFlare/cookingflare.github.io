Grilling Sausages: Tips for the Perfect BBQ Links


Grilling sausages is a time-honored tradition, especially during BBQ season. Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or enjoying a casual dinner, there’s something irresistible about the sizzle and smoky aroma that fills the air when sausages hit the grill. But getting that perfect grill-marked, juicy sausage with a crispy exterior and flavorful interior requires more than just throwing them on the heat. It’s an art, and with the right technique, you can master it.

In this guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to grill sausages to perfection. From choosing the right sausages to mastering the grilling technique, we’ve got you covered.

Choosing the Right Sausages for Grilling

Before you even think about starting the grill, the first step is choosing the right sausages. The type of sausage you choose will impact both the flavor and grilling process. Here’s a breakdown of some popular sausage types:

1. Fresh Sausages

Fresh sausages are made from raw, uncooked meat and require full cooking before consumption. They come in a variety of flavors, from spicy Italian to mild breakfast varieties. These sausages need a little extra attention while grilling to ensure they’re cooked through without burning the exterior.

Popular fresh sausages:

  • Italian sausage (mild or spicy)
  • Bratwurst
  • Chorizo
  • Breakfast sausage

2. Pre-cooked Sausages

Pre-cooked sausages are already cooked during the manufacturing process, so they only need to be heated through when grilling. These sausages are typically easier to grill because they don’t require as much time on the grill, but they can still benefit from a good sear to develop flavor.

Examples of pre-cooked sausages:

  • Hot dogs
  • Kielbasa
  • Andouille

3. Sausages with Natural Casings vs. Synthetic Casings

The casing of the sausage plays an important role in how it grills. Sausages with natural casings (usually made from animal intestines) tend to hold their shape better and offer a satisfying “snap” when you bite into them. On the other hand, synthetic casings (typically made from collagen or cellulose) might not hold up as well on the grill, sometimes leading to cracks or splits.

When you’re selecting your sausages, pay attention to the casing type. Natural casings generally hold up better during grilling.

Preparation Tips Before Grilling

Once you’ve selected your sausages, it’s important to prepare them correctly before grilling. Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

1. Prick the Sausages (Optional)

Some people recommend pricking sausages with a fork or skewer before grilling to allow the fat to escape and prevent the sausages from bursting. However, this is a controversial step. While it can help prevent flare-ups, pricking sausages can also lead to a drier result since you’re letting flavorful juices escape. If you’re grilling sausages that are packed with fat and have a good casing, you may want to skip this step for a juicier result.

2. Let the Sausages Rest at Room Temperature

Cold sausages straight from the fridge will have a hard time cooking evenly on the grill. Let your sausages rest at room temperature for about 15 minutes before grilling. This will ensure more even cooking and help them cook faster once they hit the heat.

3. Lightly Oil the Grill Grates

While sausages generally have enough fat in their casings to prevent sticking, it’s still a good idea to lightly oil the grill grates before cooking. Use a paper towel soaked in vegetable oil or a grilling spray to ensure a non-stick surface.

Grilling Techniques: The Key to Perfect Sausages

Now that your sausages are prepped and ready, it’s time to fire up the grill. Grilling sausages is more than just tossing them on the grate and waiting for them to cook. You need to use the right technique to achieve that crispy exterior and juicy interior.

1. Set Up Your Grill for Two-Zone Cooking

To cook sausages evenly, you’ll want to use a two-zone grilling method. This involves setting up your grill with both direct heat (hot side) and indirect heat (cool side).

  • Hot side: This is where you’ll sear the sausages for that golden-brown exterior.
  • Cool side: This is where you’ll move the sausages to finish cooking through, ensuring they’re fully cooked without burning the outside.

To set up your grill for two-zone cooking:

  • For a gas grill, turn on one half of the burners to high heat, while leaving the other half off or on low heat.
  • For a charcoal grill, push the hot coals to one side of the grill to create a hot zone and a cooler zone.

2. Start with Indirect Heat

Start your sausages on the cooler side of the grill. Cover the grill and allow the sausages to cook gently over indirect heat. This will help them cook through without burning or overcooking the outside.

  • Cooking time: Sausages will typically take about 15-20 minutes to cook through, depending on their size and type.
  • Turn the sausages: Every 3-5 minutes, turn the sausages to ensure even cooking.

3. Finish with Direct Heat

Once your sausages are nearly cooked through, it’s time to move them to the hot side of the grill for that signature sear. This step is crucial for achieving the perfect grilled sausage texture. Place them over the direct heat and cook for 3-5 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure even browning.

The high heat will create those lovely grill marks, and the exterior will become crispy and caramelized, giving your sausages a delicious crunch.

4. Check for Doneness

The key to perfectly grilled sausages is ensuring they are fully cooked without being overdone. The best way to check for doneness is by using an instant-read thermometer. For pork and beef sausages, the internal temperature should reach 160°F (71°C). For poultry sausages, aim for 165°F (74°C).

If you don’t have a thermometer, you can also check by cutting into one of the sausages. The meat should be firm, and there should be no pink inside. The juices should run clear, not red or pink.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Grilling Sausages

While grilling sausages is relatively straightforward, there are some common mistakes that can lead to disappointing results. Let’s take a look at some of these pitfalls so you can avoid them.

1. Overcrowding the Grill

It’s tempting to load up the grill with as many sausages as possible, but overcrowding can lead to uneven cooking. If the sausages are too close together, the heat won’t circulate properly, and they might not cook through evenly.

Give each sausage enough space to breathe. This will allow them to cook evenly and develop that perfect sear.

2. Using Too High Heat from the Start

Starting with high heat may seem like a good idea, but it can result in burnt sausages that are raw on the inside. Always start with indirect heat to gently cook the sausages through. Once they’re almost done, then move them to direct heat for the finishing touch.

3. Not Turning the Sausages Enough

Turning your sausages too infrequently can result in uneven browning or burnt spots. Turn your sausages every 3-5 minutes to ensure they cook evenly on all sides.

4. Cutting the Sausages Before Cooking

Some people slice their sausages before grilling to speed up cooking or to let them cook faster. While this can be tempting, cutting sausages before grilling can cause them to dry out. Keep them whole, and only slice them once they’re fully cooked and ready to serve.

Serving Your Grilled Sausages

Grilled sausages are incredibly versatile and can be served in many different ways. Here are a few serving suggestions:

1. Classic Bun and Toppings

The most traditional way to enjoy grilled sausages is in a bun with your favorite toppings. Popular choices include:

  • Mustard (yellow, Dijon, spicy brown)
  • Sauerkraut
  • Grilled onions and peppers
  • Pickles and relish

2. Sausage Platters

If you’re looking to serve sausages without buns, you can create a sausage platter with sides like:

  • Grilled vegetables (peppers, onions, zucchini)
  • Potato salad
  • Coleslaw
  • Grilled corn on the cob

3. Sausage and Cheese Pairings

For a more sophisticated take, pair your grilled sausages with artisan cheeses, crackers, and some fresh fruit for a delicious charcuterie board.

4. Sausage Pasta or Rice Dishes

Grilled sausages can be sliced up and added to pasta dishes or rice bowls for a hearty meal. Whether it’s a rich tomato-based sauce or a savory risotto, sausages can enhance the flavor profile of many dishes.

Conclusion

Grilling sausages to perfection isn’t a difficult task, but it does require a little attention to detail and technique. By selecting the right sausages, preparing them properly, and using the right grilling methods, you can achieve a perfectly cooked, juicy sausage every time. With these tips, you’ll be the BBQ hero at your next cookout, serving up sausages that are crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and bursting with flavor.

So fire up your grill, follow these steps, and enjoy your next grilling adventure with perfectly cooked sausages!
