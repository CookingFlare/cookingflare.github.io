Sweet and Savory Thanksgiving Cranberry Recipes


Cranberries are a staple on many Thanksgiving tables, their tartness providing the perfect contrast to the rich and savory flavors of roasted meats and creamy side dishes. But beyond the classic cranberry sauce, there’s so much more you can do with this vibrant, versatile fruit. Whether you’re looking for sweet treats or savory dishes to round out your Thanksgiving spread, cranberries can be the star of your meal in ways you may not have imagined.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of sweet and savory cranberry recipes that are perfect for the Thanksgiving feast. From refreshing salads to indulgent desserts, and even hearty sides, cranberries can add a burst of color, flavor, and health benefits to your menu. Let’s dive in!

1. Classic Homemade Cranberry Sauce with an Orange Twist

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without a classic cranberry sauce. But this version is elevated with the addition of orange zest and juice, which brings out the natural sweetness of the cranberries and adds a fresh, citrusy note.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Zest and juice of one large orange
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, water, and orange zest and juice.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  4. If desired, add a cinnamon stick for a warm, spicy flavor, and a teaspoon of vanilla extract for depth.
  5. Remove the cinnamon stick and allow the sauce to cool before serving. This can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to a week.

This simple yet flavorful cranberry sauce pairs perfectly with roasted turkey, stuffing, and mashed potatoes, providing that essential tangy contrast to the savory dishes on your plate.

2. Cranberry Walnut Stuffing

Cranberries and stuffing are a match made in heaven. Adding dried cranberries to your traditional stuffing not only provides a touch of sweetness but also a burst of color and texture. The crunchy walnuts complement the cranberries beautifully, making this dish a standout on your Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old bread, cubed
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and celery, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread cubes, dried cranberries, walnuts, sautéed onion and celery, sage, salt, and pepper.
  4. Slowly pour in the broth, stirring gently to moisten the bread mixture. If you prefer a wetter stuffing, add more broth.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish and bake for 30-40 minutes, until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This cranberry walnut stuffing adds an extra layer of flavor to your Thanksgiving feast, with the perfect balance of savory herbs, tangy cranberries, and crunchy nuts.

3. Cranberry and Goat Cheese Crostini

For a light, savory appetizer that is sure to impress your guests, try cranberry and goat cheese crostini. The creaminess of the goat cheese combined with the tart cranberries and a drizzle of honey creates a bite-sized flavor explosion that will start your Thanksgiving meal off right.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 4 oz goat cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup fresh cranberries, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish
  • Olive oil for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the baguette slices on a baking sheet and brush both sides with olive oil. Toast the bread in the oven for about 8-10 minutes or until golden and crisp.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the chopped cranberries, honey, and balsamic vinegar. Stir to coat the cranberries evenly and set aside.
  3. Spread a generous layer of goat cheese on each crostini.
  4. Spoon the cranberry mixture over the goat cheese, ensuring each crostini is topped with a good amount of cranberries.
  5. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves and serve immediately.

These cranberry and goat cheese crostinis offer a savory bite with a hint of sweetness, making them the perfect appetizer for your Thanksgiving gathering.

4. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Cranberry Balsamic Glaze

For a savory side dish with a bit of zing, try roasted Brussels sprouts paired with a cranberry balsamic glaze. The slight bitterness of the Brussels sprouts is beautifully balanced by the sweet-tart flavor of the cranberries and balsamic vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the sprouts are golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  3. While the Brussels sprouts are roasting, prepare the cranberry balsamic glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the cranberries, balsamic vinegar, honey, and Dijon mustard. Cook over medium heat until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens, about 10 minutes.
  4. Once the Brussels sprouts are done, drizzle the cranberry balsamic glaze over them and toss to coat. Serve warm.

This dish provides a delightful balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors, making it an ideal addition to your Thanksgiving table.

5. Cranberry Orange Glazed Turkey

A whole turkey with a cranberry orange glaze makes for an unforgettable centerpiece at your Thanksgiving feast. The cranberry glaze is both sweet and tart, with a refreshing citrusy kick that complements the rich flavor of the turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (about 12 lbs)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Rub the turkey with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Place the turkey in a roasting pan and roast, basting occasionally, for about 3-4 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  3. While the turkey is roasting, make the cranberry orange glaze. In a saucepan, combine the cranberries, orange juice, honey, Dijon mustard, and fresh thyme. Simmer over low heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the cranberries burst and the glaze thickens.
  4. During the last 30 minutes of roasting, brush the cranberry orange glaze over the turkey every 10 minutes, allowing it to caramelize and create a beautiful, flavorful crust.
  5. Once the turkey is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest before carving. Serve with additional cranberry orange glaze on the side.

This turkey recipe brings an elegant touch to your Thanksgiving spread, with the sweet and tangy cranberry orange glaze adding complexity and depth to the classic roast turkey.

6. Cranberry Chocolate Chip Cookies

For those who crave something sweet and comforting, cranberry chocolate chip cookies are a must-try. The combination of tart cranberries and rich chocolate creates a delightful contrast in every bite, making these cookies the perfect dessert or treat to enjoy after your Thanksgiving feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, beat together the softened butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until creamy and smooth. Add the egg and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in the dried cranberries and chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are set.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These cranberry chocolate chip cookies are a perfect balance of sweet and tart, making them a great addition to your dessert table or as a sweet snack during the Thanksgiving weekend.

7. Cranberry Pecan Pie

Pecan pie is a Thanksgiving classic, but why not add a twist? This cranberry pecan pie combines the traditional richness of pecans with the tartness of cranberries, creating a pie that’s both sweet and slightly tangy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 prepared pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the cranberries, pecans, brown sugar, maple syrup, melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Stir to combine.
  3. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust, spreading it evenly.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the pie filling is set and the top is golden brown. If the crust starts to brown too quickly, cover the edges with aluminum foil.
  5. Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing and serving.

This cranberry pecan pie is the perfect dessert to finish off your Thanksgiving meal, offering a unique spin on a beloved classic.

Conclusion

Cranberries are more than just a condiment for your turkey. They can be incorporated into a variety of Thanksgiving dishes, from savory sides to decadent desserts. Whether you’re making a tangy cranberry sauce, adding cranberries to your stuffing, or serving up cranberry chocolate chip cookies, these recipes will add a burst of flavor and color to your Thanksgiving table. So, embrace the versatility of cranberries and get creative with these sweet and savory dishes this holiday season!
