Mediterranean Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms: A Meaty Delight


If you’re a fan of Mediterranean flavors and hearty, meaty dishes, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Mediterranean Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms. These earthy, robust mushrooms provide the perfect canvas for a delicious mixture of Mediterranean-inspired ingredients that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you feeling satisfied. Whether you’re a vegetarian looking for a substantial meat alternative or simply a lover of good food, these stuffed portobello mushrooms are sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the Mushrooms: Gently clean the portobello mushrooms with a damp paper towel to remove any dirt. Carefully remove the stems and scrape out the gills using a spoon to create a hollow space for the stuffing.

  3. Prepare the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chopped spinach, diced tomatoes, Kalamata olives, crumbled feta cheese, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  4. Stuff the Mushrooms: Place the cleaned portobello mushrooms on the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle the mushroom caps with a bit of olive oil and season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Spoon the filling mixture into each mushroom cap, pressing it down gently to ensure it’s packed in tightly.

  5. Bake and Enjoy: Place the stuffed mushrooms in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is heated through. Once done, remove from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

  6. Serve and Garnish: Arrange the stuffed portobello mushrooms on a serving platter and garnish with a sprinkle of fresh herbs such as parsley or basil. These mushrooms can be served as a flavorful appetizer, a satisfying main dish, or even alongside a crisp salad for a complete meal.
