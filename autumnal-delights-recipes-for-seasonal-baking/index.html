Autumnal Delights: Recipes for Seasonal Baking


As the leaves turn golden and the air grows crisp, the scents of autumn waft through the kitchen, beckoning us to bake. The season’s bounty of fruits, nuts, and spices invites us to experiment with new flavors and textures, and to create dishes that capture the essence of the season. In this post, we’ll explore the warm, comforting world of autumnal baking, and share some recipes that are sure to become new favorites.

Spiced Apple Cider Cake

This moist and flavorful cake is the perfect way to celebrate the arrival of autumn. The combination of apples, cinnamon, and nutmeg is a classic, and the addition of a crumbly streusel topping adds a delightful textural element.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups chopped apples (Granny Smith or other firm variety)
  • 1/2 cup streusel topping (see below)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease two 9-inch (23cm) round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cardamom.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Beat in eggs one at a time.
  4. Add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients and beat until just combined. Stir in the chopped apples.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops.
  6. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the batter.
  7. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of each cake comes out clean.
  8. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Streusel Topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together flour, granulated sugar, and brown sugar.
  2. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Stir in the chopped walnuts, if using.

Pumpkin Spice Muffins

These moist and flavorful muffins are the perfect way to get into the autumn spirit. The combination of pumpkin puree, cinnamon, and nutmeg is a classic, and the addition of a crunchy streusel topping adds a delightful textural element.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup streusel topping (see below)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Beat in eggs one at a time.
  4. Add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients and beat until just combined. Stir in the pumpkin puree.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  6. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the batter.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of each muffin comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Streusel Topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together flour, granulated sugar, and brown sugar.
  2. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Stir in the chopped walnuts, if using.

Cranberry Orange Scones

These flaky and flavorful scones are the perfect way to celebrate the arrival of autumn. The combination of cranberries, orange zest, and orange juice is a classic, and the addition of a crunchy streusel topping adds a delightful textural element.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cranberries
  • 1 tablespoon grated orange zest
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1/2 cup streusel topping (see below)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together heavy cream, egg, and orange juice.
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until the dough comes together.
  6. Fold in the chopped cranberries and orange zest.
  7. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together.
  8. Pat the dough into a circle that is about 1 inch (2.5cm) thick.
  9. Use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out scones.
  10. Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet and sprinkle with streusel topping.
  11. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown.
  12. Let the scones cool on a wire rack for 5 minutes, then serve warm.

Streusel Topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together flour, granulated sugar, and brown sugar.
  2. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Stir in the chopped walnuts, if using.

Conclusion

As the seasons change and the weather cools, the scents of autumn waft through the kitchen, beckoning us to bake. The recipes in this post are sure to become new favorites, and will add a touch of warmth and comfort to your holiday gatherings. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these recipes are sure to inspire you to create delicious and memorable treats. So go ahead, get baking, and let the flavors of autumn guide you!
