Tarta de Limón: Spanish-Style Lemon Tart

3 min · 534 words

If you’re a fan of citrusy desserts, then you’re in for a treat with this Tarta de Limón recipe. This Spanish-style lemon tart is the perfect combination of zesty and sweet, with a buttery crust that complements the tangy lemon filling. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving something indulgent, this Tarta de Limón is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you wanting more.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a food processor, combine the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Add this mixture to the food processor and pulse until the dough comes together.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently until smooth. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and melted butter. Add the eggs, one at a time, whisking well after each addition.
  3. Stir in the lemon juice and zest. Gradually add the flour, whisking until smooth and well combined.

Assembling the Tart:

  1. Remove the chilled dough from the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the bottom and sides of the pan, trimming any excess.
  2. Pour the lemon filling into the prepared crust and spread it out evenly.
  3. Bake the tart for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  4. Allow the tart to cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  5. Before serving, dust the tart with confectioners’ sugar for a beautiful finish.

Serving Suggestions

This Tarta de Limón pairs wonderfully with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The combination of the creamy tart filling and the crisp, buttery crust is truly delightful. Serve it as the perfect ending to a Spanish-inspired meal or simply enjoy it with a cup of coffee or tea for a delightful afternoon treat.

Final Thoughts

Tarta de Limón, with its vibrant lemon flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture, is a dessert that captures the essence of Spanish cuisine. The balance of sweet and tangy flavors makes it a beloved treat for all occasions. So, whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the world of pastry, this recipe is a must-try. Elevate your dessert game with this Tarta de Limón and savor every citrusy bite!
