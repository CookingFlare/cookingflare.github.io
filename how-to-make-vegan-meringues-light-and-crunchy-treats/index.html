How to Make Vegan Meringues: Light and Crunchy Treats


Vegans often find themselves missing out on classic desserts, especially when it comes to light and airy treats like meringues. However, with a few clever substitutions, you can craft the perfect vegan meringues that are just as delightful as their traditional counterparts. In this guide, we’ll explore the step-by-step process of making vegan meringues that are not only light and crunchy but also incredibly easy to prepare. So, let’s dive into the world of plant-based baking!

Understanding the Basics of Meringues

Meringues are typically made with egg whites, whipped to a fluffy consistency and then baked until they are crisp. The secret to achieving that signature texture lies in the protein found in egg whites. Fortunately, aquafaba, the liquid from canned chickpeas or the cooking water from dried chickpeas, serves as an excellent vegan substitute. It mimics the properties of egg whites and can be whipped into stiff peaks, making it the perfect ingredient for our vegan meringues.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create these delectable vegan treats, gather the following ingredients:

  • Aquafaba: 1 cup (from canned chickpeas or homemade)
  • Cream of tartar: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Granulated sugar: 1 cup (use organic for a fully vegan option)
  • Vanilla extract: 1 teaspoon (optional, for added flavor)
  • Cornstarch: 1 teaspoon (for added stability)
  • Lemon juice: 1 teaspoon (helps to stabilize the aquafaba)

Tools You Will Need

  • Mixing bowl
  • Electric mixer (or a whisk, but it will take longer)
  • Rubber spatula
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Meringues

1. Preparing the Aquafaba

Begin by draining the chickpeas and reserving the liquid. If you’re using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight, then boil them in fresh water until tender, reserving the cooking water. Allow the aquafaba to cool down to room temperature before you start.

2. Whipping the Aquafaba

In a clean mixing bowl, pour in the aquafaba. It’s essential that the bowl is free of any grease, as this can hinder the whipping process. Add the cream of tartar and lemon juice, which will help stabilize the meringue.

Using an electric mixer, beat the aquafaba on medium speed until it becomes frothy, then increase to high speed. Continue whipping until soft peaks form. This process can take anywhere from 5 to 10 minutes.

3. Adding Sugar Gradually

Once you achieve soft peaks, begin adding the granulated sugar gradually, about a tablespoon at a time. This slow addition allows the sugar to dissolve properly, resulting in a smoother texture. Continue to whip the mixture until stiff peaks form. You’ll know it’s ready when you can turn the bowl upside down without the mixture falling out.

4. Incorporating Cornstarch and Vanilla

Once stiff peaks have formed, gently fold in the cornstarch and vanilla extract using a rubber spatula. Be careful not to deflate the meringue – fold gently until just combined. The cornstarch helps create a chewy center, while the vanilla adds a lovely flavor.

5. Prepping the Baking Sheet

Preheat your oven to 200°F (93°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. You can use a piping bag or simply spoon the meringue mixture onto the parchment paper. If you’re using a piping bag, fit it with a star tip for elegant shapes. Otherwise, dollop the meringue mixture into small rounds, about 1.5 inches in diameter.

6. Shaping the Meringues

If you want to get creative, you can swirl the meringue to create different shapes or even make nests to fill later with fruit or vegan whipped cream. The key is to ensure that all meringues are uniform in size to ensure even baking.

7. Baking the Meringues

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for about 1 to 1.5 hours. The longer they bake, the crunchier they will become. Your goal is to dry them out without browning. After the baking time is up, turn off the oven and leave the meringues inside to cool completely. This helps them firm up and develop that delightful crunch.

8. Storing Your Vegan Meringues

Once cooled, carefully remove the meringues from the parchment paper. Store them in an airtight container at room temperature. They can last for several days, but they are best enjoyed fresh!

Flavor Variations for Your Vegan Meringues

Once you master the basic recipe, you can experiment with different flavors and colors. Here are some ideas:

  • Cocoa Meringues: Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the aquafaba after achieving soft peaks for a chocolaty twist.
  • Fruit-Flavored Meringues: Use fruit extracts like almond, raspberry, or lemon to infuse your meringues with additional flavor.
  • Colored Meringues: Add a few drops of natural food coloring to create beautiful pastel meringues for special occasions.

Serving Suggestions

Vegan meringues can be served in numerous creative ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to inspire you:

  • Meringue Nests: Create small nests and fill them with fresh berries and vegan whipped cream for a stunning dessert.
  • Meringue Sandwiches: Use two meringues to sandwich a layer of vegan chocolate ganache or coconut cream.
  • Decorative Toppings: Drizzle melted dark chocolate over the meringues for an elegant touch, or sprinkle with crushed nuts for added texture.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Making vegan meringues can be a bit tricky, especially if it’s your first time. Here are some common issues and how to resolve them:

  • Meringue Not Whipping Up: Ensure that your bowl and mixer are completely clean and free of any grease. Also, make sure your aquafaba is at room temperature.
  • Meringues Are Chewy Instead of Crunchy: This can happen if they haven’t dried out enough. Make sure to bake them until they are completely dry, and consider leaving them in the oven to cool down.
  • Cracks in Meringues: Cracks can occur if the oven temperature is too high or if the meringues are disturbed while baking. Keep the oven at a low temperature and avoid opening the door frequently.

Conclusion

Vegan meringues are a delightful treat that can easily be made at home with minimal ingredients and effort. Their light, airy texture makes them perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a casual snack or a fancy dessert for a dinner party. With a little practice, you can create beautiful, crunchy meringues that are sure to impress your friends and family. So go ahead, experiment with flavors and shapes, and enjoy the sweet satisfaction of mastering this classic dessert in a plant-based style! Happy baking!
