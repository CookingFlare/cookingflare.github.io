Korean Spicy Pork Ribs: A Finger-Licking Goodness


If you’re craving a dish that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more, look no further than Korean Spicy Pork Ribs. Bursting with bold flavors and a fiery kick, this dish is a true finger-licking goodness. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating this Korean culinary delight right in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Korean Spicy Pork Ribs, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of pork ribs
  • 1/2 cup of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional, for an extra kick)
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the gochujang, soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, rice vinegar, black pepper, salt, and cayenne pepper (if desired). Stir well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  3. Place the pork ribs in a large ziplock bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the ribs, making sure they are evenly coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap and let the ribs marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight for maximum flavor.

  4. Once marinated, remove the ribs from the refrigerator and let them come to room temperature. This will help them cook more evenly.

  5. Line a baking sheet with foil for easy cleanup. Place the ribs on the prepared baking sheet, reserving the marinade for basting later. Bake the ribs in the preheated oven for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until they are tender and the meat starts to pull away from the bones.

  6. While the ribs are cooking, baste them with the reserved marinade every 30 minutes to keep them moist and flavorful.

  7. Once the ribs are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a more succulent dish.

  8. Garnish the Korean Spicy Pork Ribs with thinly sliced green onions for a pop of freshness and color.

  9. Serve the ribs hot and enjoy the explosion of flavors with every bite. The tender meat, infused with the rich and spicy marinade, will leave you craving for more.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder version of this dish, reduce the amount of gochujang or omit the cayenne pepper.
  • For an extra layer of smokiness, you can grill the ribs instead of baking them. Just make sure to baste them regularly to prevent them from drying out.
  • Serve the Korean Spicy Pork Ribs with steamed rice and a side of kimchi for a complete Korean meal experience.

Now that you have the recipe for Korean Spicy Pork Ribs, it’s time to unleash your inner chef and get cooking! The combination of flavors in this dish will take your taste buds on a journey to Korea, where spicy and savory are the kings of the culinary world. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and treat yourself to a finger-licking goodness that will have you coming back for more. Enjoy!
