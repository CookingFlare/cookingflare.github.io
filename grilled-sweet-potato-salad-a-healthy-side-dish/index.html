Grilled Sweet Potato Salad: A Healthy Side Dish


Tired of thesame old boring side dishes? Elevate your meal with this heartwarming Grilled Sweet Potato Salad. Packed with nutrients and flavor, it’s a perfect addition to any gathering or family dinner. In this post, we’ll guide you through a simple and wholesome recipe, ideal for health enthusiasts and culinary explorers alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 8 oz baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sweet potatoes: Peel and dice the sweet potatoes into 1-inch cubes. Rinse and drain them to remove excess starch and soil. Pat the sweet potatoes dry with a clean towel to ensure even grilling.

  2. Make the marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, ground cumin, salt, and black pepper.

  3. Marinate the sweet potatoes: Pour the marinade over the sweet potatoes, ensuring they are evenly coated. Let the sweet potatoes rest in the marinade for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. This will allow flavors to meld and penetrate the potatoes.

  4. Prepare the salad components: Rinse and dry the fresh herbs. Roughly chop parsley, basil, and cilantro. Wash and dry baby spinach, keeping in mind that it’ll be cooked on the grill with the sweet potatoes. Bring dried cranberries and chopped almonds to room temperature.

  5. Fuel up the grill: Clean your grill and preheat it to medium-high heat. Oil the grate to prevent the sweet potatoes from sticking.

  6. Grill the sweet potatoes: Place the well-marinated sweet potatoes on the hot grate, and grill for about 5 minutes on each side, or until they develop grill marks and become tender. Use a pair of tongs to flip them easily. While turning, incorporate the charred bits into the dish for a richer, sweeter flavor. Grill the baby spinach on the grill’s cool side, being careful not to overcook it.

  7. Assemble the salad: In a large mixing bowl, combine the grilled sweet potatoes and spinach. Add in the chopped herbs, dried cranberries, and almonds. Toss the ingredients gently, ensuring no damage to the crispy textures.

Enjoy your quench your hunger with this zesty, warm-weather Grilled Sweet Potato Salad. This healthful side dish pairs perfectly with grilled proteins like fish, poultry, or tempeh. Add some sunshine to your plate and incorporate more nutrient-dense foods into your diet today.

Conclusion

The humble sweet potato has been given a modern makeover - grilled, flavored, and featured gloriously in this fantastic salad. Upgrade your vegetable side dishes and explore the versatile sweet potato with this hearty and satisfying recipe. It’s a delicious and wholesome way to ensure your meals are as delightful as they are nutritious. Bon appétit!
