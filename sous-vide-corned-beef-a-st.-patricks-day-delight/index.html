Sous Vide Corned Beef: A St. Patrick's Day Delight


Corned beef is a classic dish that is often associated with St. Patrick’s Day. Traditionally, it is made by brining a cut of beef, usually brisket, in a mixture of salt, sugar, and spices. The beef is then simmered for hours until it becomes tender and flavorful. While this method produces delicious results, there is another way to prepare corned beef that takes it to a whole new level: sous vide.

Sous vide is a cooking technique that involves vacuum-sealing food in a bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature for an extended period of time. This method ensures that the food is cooked evenly and retains its moisture and flavor. When applied to corned beef, sous vide takes this already amazing dish to new heights.

The Benefits of Sous Vide Corned Beef

One of the main advantages of cooking corned beef sous vide is the tenderness it produces. The low and slow cooking process allows the collagen in the beef to break down gradually, resulting in incredibly tender meat. Additionally, the flavors of the brine penetrate the meat more thoroughly, creating a more flavorful end product.

Another benefit of sous vide cooking is the convenience it offers. Once you have prepared the corned beef and sealed it in a bag, all you have to do is set the temperature on your sous vide machine and let it do its magic. You can walk away and attend to other tasks while the beef cooks to perfection. This frees up your time and allows you to focus on other elements of your St. Patrick’s Day feast.

How to Sous Vide Corned Beef

To prepare sous vide corned beef, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 corned beef brisket, about 3-4 pounds
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon juniper berries
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves

  1. Start by rinsing the corned beef under cold water to remove any excess salt. Pat it dry with paper towels.

  2. In a spice grinder or mortar and pestle, grind the black peppercorns, mustard seeds, coriander seeds, and juniper berries until coarse.

  3. Rub the minced garlic onto the surface of the corned beef, followed by the spice mixture. Make sure to coat the beef evenly with the spices.

  4. Place the seasoned corned beef in a vacuum-sealable bag. Add the fresh thyme and bay leaves to the bag, distributing them evenly around the beef.

  5. Vacuum-seal the bag, ensuring that there are no air pockets. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a zip-top bag and the water displacement method to remove the air.

  6. Preheat your sous vide machine to 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare or 145°F (63°C) for medium doneness.

  7. Once the water has reached the desired temperature, carefully lower the bag into the water bath. Make sure the bag is fully submerged.

  8. Cook the corned beef for 24-48 hours, depending on the thickness of the brisket. The longer cooking time will result in a more tender and flavorful end product.

  9. After the desired cooking time has elapsed, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the corned beef to a cutting board.

  10. Allow the corned beef to rest for a few minutes before slicing it against the grain. This will ensure that the meat remains tender and easy to chew.

  11. Serve the sous vide corned beef with your favorite St. Patrick’s Day sides, such as cabbage, potatoes, and carrots. Don’t forget to drizzle some of the cooking juices over the sliced beef for added flavor.

Conclusion

Sous vide corned beef is a game-changer when it comes to preparing this classic St. Patrick’s Day dish. The low and slow cooking method ensures a tender and flavorful end result that will impress your family and friends. Give this recipe a try and elevate your St. Patrick’s Day feast to new heights. Happy cooking and happy St. Patrick’s Day!
