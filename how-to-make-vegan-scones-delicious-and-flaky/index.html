How to Make Vegan Scones: Delicious and Flaky


Scones are a quintessential treat, perfect for breakfast, afternoon tea, or a cozy snack. Whether you’re vegan or simply looking to enjoy a lighter, plant-based version of this classic pastry, vegan scones are incredibly easy to make and can be just as delicious and flaky as their traditional counterparts. In this blog post, we’ll take you step-by-step through the process of creating the most tender, melt-in-your-mouth vegan scones that will leave everyone asking for more.

What Makes a Vegan Scone?

A traditional scone typically includes dairy products like butter, milk, or cream. However, with a few simple substitutions, you can create a vegan-friendly scone without sacrificing flavor or texture. The key to making vegan scones is to use plant-based alternatives for butter and milk, while also ensuring that the scones remain light and fluffy.

In vegan scones, we substitute dairy butter with non-dairy butter or coconut oil, and instead of regular milk, we use almond milk, soy milk, oat milk, or any other plant-based milk of your choice. These swaps do not affect the final result and can actually enhance the flavors, giving the scones a rich and satisfying taste.

Let’s dive into the recipe!

Ingredients for Vegan Scones

To create the perfect vegan scones, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup vegan butter or coconut oil, cold and cut into cubes
  • ⅓ cup organic cane sugar (or coconut sugar for a healthier option)
  • ¾ cup plant-based milk (such as almond, soy, or oat milk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1 cup of add-ins such as dried fruit (raisins, cranberries), chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds), or chocolate chips (dairy-free)

These simple ingredients will give you everything you need to make a batch of light, fluffy, and flavorful vegan scones.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Scones

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Start by measuring out all the ingredients and preparing them. Place the cold vegan butter (or coconut oil) in the freezer for a few minutes before using it. This helps the fat remain solid while incorporating it into the dough, which is crucial for creating flaky layers in your scones.

If you plan to add dried fruit, nuts, or chocolate chips, measure them out and set them aside. This will make it easier to fold them into the dough later.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. These dry ingredients are the foundation of the scones, so make sure they are evenly mixed to ensure proper rising and texture.

Step 3: Cut in the Vegan Butter

Next, add the cold cubes of vegan butter (or coconut oil) to the dry ingredients. Use a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour mixture. You want to create a coarse, crumbly texture with pea-sized pieces of fat distributed throughout the flour. This step is essential for the scones to become flaky when baked.

If you’re using coconut oil, make sure it’s solid rather than melted to achieve the same flakiness as with butter.

Step 4: Add the Wet Ingredients

In a separate small bowl, combine the plant-based milk and vanilla extract. Pour this wet mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Stir gently with a wooden spoon or spatula until the dough begins to come together. You should aim for a dough that is slightly sticky but not too wet.

If you’re adding any mix-ins like dried fruit or chocolate chips, fold them into the dough at this point. Be careful not to overwork the dough, as this can lead to tough scones.

Step 5: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has come together, transfer it onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat it into a circle about 1-inch thick. If the dough is too sticky to handle, sprinkle a little flour on top to help with shaping.

You can now cut your dough into scone shapes. Traditionally, scones are cut into triangles, but you can also use a round biscuit cutter to create circle-shaped scones. Try to handle the dough as little as possible to ensure the scones remain light and tender.

Step 6: Bake the Scones

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. Arrange the scones on the baking sheet, leaving a little space between each one to allow for expansion.

Optional: Brush the tops of the scones with a bit of plant-based milk for a golden finish, or sprinkle a small amount of sugar on top for a sweet crunch.

Place the baking sheet in the oven and bake for 18-22 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Keep an eye on them toward the end of baking to ensure they don’t burn.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Once the scones are baked, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack for a few minutes. Scones are best enjoyed warm, so serve them immediately with a dollop of vegan butter, jam, or clotted cream.

These vegan scones are light, flaky, and the perfect addition to your breakfast table or afternoon tea spread. They pair wonderfully with hot beverages like tea, coffee, or plant-based milk.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Scones

While the recipe is straightforward, a few extra tips can help you make the best scones possible:

1. Keep the Ingredients Cold

One of the secrets to flaky scones is keeping the fat cold. When the fat (vegan butter or coconut oil) melts in the oven, it creates steam, which leads to the desired flakiness. Be sure to chill your butter or oil before using it and work quickly to avoid warming it up.

2. Don’t Overmix the Dough

Overmixing the dough will result in dense, tough scones. Stir the wet and dry ingredients until just combined, and don’t worry if the dough is a little lumpy.

3. Add Flavor with Zest and Spices

If you want to experiment with different flavors, consider adding citrus zest (such as lemon, orange, or lime) to the dough. Ground spices like cinnamon or nutmeg can also add warmth and depth to your scones.

4. Make Mini Scones

If you prefer bite-sized treats, divide the dough into smaller portions and bake mini scones. Just remember that the baking time may be slightly shorter for smaller scones, so keep an eye on them.

5. Use the Right Baking Sheet

A heavy-duty baking sheet will ensure that your scones bake evenly. If you’re using a thinner baking sheet, consider placing it on a lower rack in the oven to avoid over-browning the bottom.

6. Try Different Add-ins

While raisins and cranberries are classic choices for scone add-ins, you can get creative with your mix-ins. Chopped nuts, dairy-free chocolate chips, or even fresh berries like blueberries and strawberries can all work wonderfully in this recipe.

Variations of Vegan Scones

Once you master the basic vegan scone recipe, you can experiment with different flavor profiles and textures. Here are a few fun variations to try:

1. Lemon Blueberry Scones

Add fresh or frozen blueberries and the zest of one lemon to the dough for a burst of citrusy sweetness. These scones are perfect for a summer breakfast or brunch.

2. Chocolate Chip Scones

For a sweet treat, add dairy-free chocolate chips to the dough. You can even drizzle some melted dark chocolate over the top of the scones once they’re baked for an extra indulgent touch.

3. Maple Pecan Scones

Chop up some pecans and fold them into the dough, along with a tablespoon of pure maple syrup. These scones are perfect for autumn or holiday gatherings.

4. Herb and Olive Scones

For a savory twist, try adding chopped fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, or sage, along with chopped olives. These scones pair beautifully with soups or salads.

Conclusion

Vegan scones are a wonderful alternative to traditional scones, providing the same soft, flaky texture without the use of animal products. By using simple substitutions like plant-based butter and milk, you can enjoy this delightful pastry while sticking to your dietary preferences.

Whether you choose to keep your scones plain or experiment with fun flavor combinations, this recipe is a great starting point for anyone looking to bake a delicious, vegan-friendly treat. With the right ingredients, a few handy tips, and a little patience, you’ll be enjoying the best vegan scones in no time!

Happy baking!
