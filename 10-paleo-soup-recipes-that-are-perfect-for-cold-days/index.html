10 Paleo Soup Recipes That Are Perfect for Cold Days


As the temperature drops and chilly winds sweep in, there’s nothing quite as comforting as a warm bowl of soup. For those who follow the Paleo diet, finding satisfying and nourishing soup recipes that align with this lifestyle can sometimes be a challenge. Luckily, we’ve curated a list of ten delicious and hearty Paleo soup recipes that are perfect for cold days. These soups are not only rich in flavor but also packed with nutrients, making them an ideal choice for those seeking to stay healthy during the colder months.

1. Paleo Chicken Soup

When the cold weather hits, a classic chicken soup is a go-to choice. But to make it Paleo-friendly, we’ll skip the noodles and grains and focus on nourishing ingredients like chicken, vegetables, and bone broth. This soup is a comforting meal packed with protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (or 4 chicken thighs)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups of bone broth (chicken or vegetable)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté for 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.
  2. Add the chicken and bone broth to the pot. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  3. Simmer for 45 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked.
  4. Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it. Return the shredded chicken to the soup and stir.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

2. Paleo Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash is naturally sweet and creamy, making it a perfect base for a warming soup. This Paleo version uses coconut milk to add richness without any dairy. It’s a smooth, velvety soup that will keep you cozy all season long.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, ginger, and broth to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  3. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can carefully transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Heat the soup through before serving.
  5. Garnish with fresh thyme or parsley and enjoy!

3. Paleo Beef Stew

A hearty beef stew is a quintessential cold-weather meal. This Paleo-friendly version omits the flour used for thickening in traditional stews, relying on vegetables and the natural gelatin from the beef to create a rich and satisfying broth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 parsnips, sliced
  • 2 cups of beef broth (preferably homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the beef stew meat and brown on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Sauté for 3-5 minutes until the onions are soft.
  3. Add the tomato paste, rosemary, thyme, carrots, parsnips, and beef broth to the pot. Stir to combine.
  4. Return the beef to the pot, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot.

4. Paleo Zucchini Noodle Soup

For those who love noodles but want to stick to Paleo principles, zucchini noodles (zoodles) are a great substitute. This light yet satisfying soup is full of flavor and perfect for when you want something refreshing and warm.

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken broth, tomatoes, and basil to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. Add the zucchini noodles and cook for another 3-5 minutes, or until the noodles are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

5. Paleo Tomato Soup

Tomato soup is a comforting classic, and this Paleo version is both rich and creamy without the use of dairy. Coconut milk creates a velvety texture, while fresh herbs bring a burst of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of fresh tomatoes, chopped (or 2 cans of diced tomatoes)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes, oregano, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes.
  3. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer the soup in batches to a blender.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve garnished with fresh basil.

6. Paleo Cauliflower Soup

Cauliflower is a wonderful base for creamy soups. In this recipe, it’s pureed with garlic, onion, and herbs for a rich and comforting bowl of soup.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the cauliflower, broth, and thyme to the pot. Bring to a boil and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, blend in batches in a regular blender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

7. Paleo Sweet Potato Soup

Sweet potatoes add a natural sweetness and creaminess to this delicious soup. It’s a nourishing choice that provides plenty of vitamins and fiber to keep you feeling full and satisfied.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the diced sweet potatoes, cumin, paprika, and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  3. Cook for 20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, or transfer in batches to a blender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper and serve.

8. Paleo Mushroom Soup

This earthy and flavorful soup is a fantastic option for cold days. The combination of mushrooms and herbs creates a rich broth that will warm you from the inside out.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb of mushrooms (button, cremini, or a mix), sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and sauté for 3-5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the mushrooms and thyme, and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the mushrooms to release their juices.
  3. Add the vegetable broth to the pot and bring to a simmer. Cook for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

9. Paleo Spinach and Bacon Soup

Bacon adds a smoky depth of flavor to this green soup, making it a great way to sneak in some greens while still enjoying a hearty meal. The addition of coconut milk ensures it’s creamy without the need for dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 4 cups of spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the bacon in a large pot over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken broth and spinach, and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the spinach is wilted.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve the soup topped with crispy bacon.

10. Paleo Pumpkin Soup

Pumpkin is the ultimate autumn vegetable, and this creamy soup highlights its natural sweetness and depth of flavor. Perfect for a cozy meal, it’s both comforting and nourishing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of pumpkin puree (or 1 small pumpkin, roasted and pureed)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups of vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the pumpkin puree, cinnamon, and broth to the pot, and bring to a simmer.
  3. Cook for 15 minutes, then stir in the coconut milk.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

These ten Paleo soup recipes will not only keep you warm on chilly days but also provide you with wholesome, nutrient-dense ingredients that align perfectly with a Paleo lifestyle. From savory meats to creamy vegetables and everything in between, there’s a soup for every taste and preference. So, get your pot ready and dive into these nourishing meals that are bound to become favorites during the cold months. Enjoy!
