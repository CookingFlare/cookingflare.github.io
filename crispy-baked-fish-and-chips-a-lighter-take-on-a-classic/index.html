Crispy Baked Fish and Chips: A Lighter Take on a Classic


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the classic combination of fish and chips. However, the traditional deep-fried version often leaves us feeling heavy and guilty. But fear not! I have a lighter and equally delicious alternative that will satisfy your cravings without weighing you down - Crispy Baked Fish and Chips.

Ingredients

  • 4 white fish fillets (such as cod or haddock)
  • 4 large Russet potatoes, cut into thick fries
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup milk (or buttermilk for extra tang)
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. This flavorful coating will give the fish its crispy texture.

  3. Pour the milk and melted butter into another bowl. Dip each fish fillet into the milk mixture, allowing any excess to drip off, then coat it thoroughly in the seasoned flour mixture. Place the coated fillets on the prepared baking sheet.

  4. In a separate bowl, toss the cut potatoes with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of salt. Arrange them in a single layer on another baking sheet.

  5. Place both the fish and the fries in the preheated oven. Bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the fish is golden brown and cooked through, and the fries are crispy and golden.

  6. While the fish and chips are baking, prepare a simple tartar sauce or your favorite dipping sauce to accompany the dish. A mixture of mayonnaise, chopped pickles, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt and pepper works wonders.

  7. Once the fish and chips are ready, remove them from the oven and serve immediately with the tartar sauce and lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy the light and crispy goodness guilt-free!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or smoked paprika to the flour mixture.
  • Feel free to substitute the white fish with other varieties such as tilapia or sole.
  • If you prefer a gluten-free version, you can replace the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend or almond flour.
  • To make the dish even healthier, try using sweet potatoes instead of Russet potatoes for your fries. They will add a touch of sweetness and extra nutrients.

Final Thoughts

Crispy Baked Fish and Chips offer a delightful twist on a beloved classic. By baking instead of deep-frying, you can indulge in this comfort food favorite without any guilt. The crispy coating and tender fish, paired with perfectly seasoned fries, create a satisfying meal that will leave you craving more. So, go ahead and enjoy this lighter take on fish and chips with your loved ones. It’s a dish that promises to please both your taste buds and your waistline!

Remember, cooking is all about experimentation and making the recipe your own. Feel free to adapt and personalize this recipe to suit your preferences. Happy cooking!
