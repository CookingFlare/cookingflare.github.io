Korean Braised Pork Ribs: A Flavorful and Tender Delight


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of galbi jjim, or Korean braised pork ribs. This mouthwatering dish is known for its rich flavors, tender meat, and satisfyingly sticky sauce. Today, I’m going to share with you my own recipe for Korean braised pork ribs, which is guaranteed to leave you craving for more. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of pork ribs
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the pork ribs. If your ribs are too large, you can cut them into smaller pieces to make them more manageable. Rinse the ribs under cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel. This step helps to remove any excess blood and moisture, ensuring a more flavorful dish.

  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become fragrant and slightly caramelized. This will help to build a solid foundation of flavors for your braised pork ribs.

  3. Once the onions and garlic are nicely cooked, add the pork ribs to the pot. Brown the ribs on all sides, allowing them to develop a beautiful golden crust. This step not only adds flavor but also helps to seal in the meat’s natural juices, resulting in tender and succulent ribs.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, rice wine, sesame oil, gochujang, gochugaru, honey, and water. Whisk everything together until the ingredients are well incorporated. This sauce will be the star of your braised pork ribs, delivering a complex blend of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors.

  5. Pour the sauce over the browned pork ribs in the pot. Make sure the ribs are fully submerged in the sauce. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the ribs to simmer gently for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily falls off the bone.

  6. During the cooking process, remember to check on the ribs occasionally and give them a gentle stir. This will ensure that the sauce evenly coats the ribs and that they cook evenly. If you find that the sauce is reducing too quickly, you can add a little more water to prevent it from drying out.

  7. Once the pork ribs are fork-tender and the sauce has thickened to a sticky consistency, it’s time to remove them from the heat. Carefully transfer the ribs to a serving platter, and garnish with chopped green onions for a fresh and vibrant touch.

  8. Serve your Korean braised pork ribs hot, alongside steamed rice and some banchan (Korean side dishes) for a complete and satisfying meal. The tender meat, infused with the bold flavors of the sauce, will make your taste buds dance with joy.

Conclusion

Korean braised pork ribs, or galbi jjim, are truly a delightful and flavorful dish that showcases the best of Korean cuisine. With their tender meat and sticky sauce, these ribs are perfect for a cozy family dinner or a special occasion. Give this recipe a try and experience the magic of Korean flavors in the comfort of your own kitchen. Enjoy!
