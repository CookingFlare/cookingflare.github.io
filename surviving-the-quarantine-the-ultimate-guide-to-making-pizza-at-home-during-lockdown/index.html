Surviving the Quarantine: The Ultimate Guide to Making Pizza at Home During Lockdown


Stuck at home and craving some delicious pizza? Don’t despair! You can make an incredible pizza right at home with just a few ingredients and some creativity. In this guide, you’ll find the best tips and tricks for baking perfect, homemade pizzas while in quarantine. Let’s dive in!

Choosing the Best Dough

The foundation of any great pizza is the dough. You can make dough from scratch, or for a quicker and easier option, try using store-bought pre-made pizza dough. If you choose to make your dough at home, follow these steps:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tsp. of active dry yeast, 1 tsp. of salt, and 2 tbsp. of olive oil.
  2. Gradually add warm water (about 110°F) to the dry ingredients until you achieve a sticky dough consistency.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic, then set aside to rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours.
  4. Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down, and it’s ready to use.

Crafting the Perfect Pizza Sauce

Now, let’s focus on the sauce. While store-bought sauces are available, making your own will take your pizza to the next level. Here’s a simple recipe:

  1. In a medium saucepan, gently heat 1 cup of canned crushed tomatoes, 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 tbsp. of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of dried oregano, and salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Allow the sauce to simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the flavors have melded together, remove from heat and let it cool.

Adding the Finishing Touches

Now for the most fun part - adding your favorite toppings to make your pizza uniquely yours. From classic pepperoni and classic Margherita to unique combinations like barbecue chicken and pineapple, the choices are endless. Here are some topping ideas:

  • Classic: Mozzarella, tomato sauce, and basil leaves for a simple but delicious Margherita style pizza.
  • Vegetarian: Shredded carrots, red onions, bell peppers, and black olives paired with a smoky pesto sauce and a mix of cheeses.
  • Meat-lover: Pepperoni, salami, and Italian sausage with a generous amount of mozzarella cheese.
  • Barbecue Chicken: Grilled chicken tossed in barbecue sauce, red onions, and fresh cilantro.

Baking Your Pizza

Now that you’ve got your dough, sauce, and toppings ready, it’s time to bake your dream pizza. Here’s what to do:

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature setting (usually 450-500°F). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven as it preheats.
  2. If you don’t have a pizza stone, you can use a baking sheet or cast iron skillet. Simply line with parchment paper or lightly grease with olive oil.
  3. On a lightly floured surface, roll or stretch your dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer the dough onto your preheated pizza stone or baking sheet.
  4. Spoon a generous amount of sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edge. Then, add your toppings of choice.
  5. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Carefully remove the pizza from the oven, allowing it to cool for a minute or two before slicing and serving.

Tips for Homemade Pizza Success

Here are a few final tips to ensure your homemade pizza comes out perfect every time:

  • Experiment with different types of cheeses to find the perfect combination for your taste. Some options include Asiago, fontina, or Pecorino.
  • Don’t overload your pizza with toppings. Aim for a balance of flavors, textures, and proportions.
  • To avoid a soggy pizza base, try brushing the edges of the crust with olive oil, or adding a sprinkle of parmesan cheese before baking.
  • Keep a close eye on your pizza while it’s baking, as ovens can vary in temperature and cooking times.

Now that you’re equipped with the knowledge to make a fantastic homemade pizza, grab your ingredients, spread some love, and enjoy making the most of your quarantine time. After all, sometimes the best memories are made in the kitchen!
