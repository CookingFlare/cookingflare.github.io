Easy Vegan Recipes Every College Student Should Try


Being a college student often means juggling classes, assignments, social events, and perhaps even a job. Amidst this whirlwind, finding time to prepare healthy meals can be a challenge. For those who are vegan or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet, the good news is that there are plenty of easy and delicious vegan recipes that are perfect for busy students. Here are some simple yet satisfying vegan dishes that won’t break the bank and will keep you nourished throughout your college journey.

Why Choose Vegan?

Adopting a vegan lifestyle can offer numerous benefits, especially for students. Vegan diets are rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes, providing essential nutrients while being low in saturated fats. Additionally, vegan meals are often budget-friendly, making them ideal for the student lifestyle. With the right recipes, cooking can be quick, easy, and enjoyable.

1. One-Pot Vegan Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can of kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup corn (frozen or canned)
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and bell pepper, cooking until they soften, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the black beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and corn. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and let it cook for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with avocado or cilantro if desired.

2. Vegan Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, snap peas, carrots)
  • 1 cup tofu, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the cubed tofu and cook until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Remove tofu and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the mixed vegetables and sauté for 3-5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  3. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for an additional minute.
  4. Return the tofu to the pan and pour in the soy sauce, stirring to combine. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve over cooked rice or noodles.

3. Vegan Pasta Primavera

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta (spaghetti or penne)
  • 2 cups assorted vegetables (zucchini, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the assorted vegetables and cook until they soften, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta into the skillet, seasoning with salt and pepper. Mix well.
  5. Serve garnished with fresh basil.

4. Overnight Oats

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • Toppings: fruits, nuts, seeds, or nut butter

Instructions:

  1. In a jar or bowl, combine rolled oats, almond milk, chia seeds, and maple syrup. Stir well.
  2. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. In the morning, give it a good stir and add your favorite toppings.

5. Vegan Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 cup corn (frozen or canned)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Salsa and lime for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine black beans, corn, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Warm the corn tortillas in a pan or microwave.
  3. Fill each tortilla with the black bean mixture, topped with diced avocado and salsa.
  4. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the tacos before serving.

6. Creamy Vegan Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast (for a cheesy flavor)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the vegetable broth and keep it warm on low heat.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add mushrooms and cook until tender.
  4. Stir in the Arborio rice, toasting it for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Gradually add the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently until absorbed before adding more.
  6. Once the rice is creamy and cooked, stir in nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper to taste.

7. Vegan Buddha Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa (or brown rice)
  • 1 cup chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup mixed greens (spinach, kale, arugula)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • Dressing: tahini, lemon juice, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa or brown rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a bowl, layer cooked quinoa, mixed greens, chickpeas, avocado, and grated carrot.
  3. Drizzle with tahini dressing and serve.

8. Banana Oatmeal Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Maple syrup for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the banana, rolled oats, almond milk, baking powder, and vanilla. Blend until smooth.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour batter onto the skillet to form pancakes.
  3. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  4. Serve with maple syrup.

9. Vegan Smoothie Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup frozen berries
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • Toppings: granola, sliced fruits, nuts, seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine banana, frozen berries, and almond milk. Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour into a bowl and top with granola, sliced fruits, and nuts.

10. Vegan Rice and Beans

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (brown or white)
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the black beans over medium heat with cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Serve the beans over rice, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Tips for Cooking Vegan on a Budget

  1. Plan Your Meals: Take time to plan your meals for the week. This helps avoid impulse purchases and reduces food waste.
  2. Buy in Bulk: Purchasing grains, beans, and legumes in bulk can save you money.
  3. Seasonal Produce: Choose fruits and vegetables that are in season, as they tend to be cheaper and more flavorful.
  4. Simple Ingredients: Many of these recipes utilize basic ingredients that can be used in multiple dishes.
  5. Prep Ahead: Batch cooking can save time and effort. Prepare larger quantities of grains or legumes and store them for easy access.

Conclusion

With these easy vegan recipes, college students can enjoy nutritious meals without spending hours in the kitchen. Each recipe is not only simple and quick to prepare but also delicious and satisfying. Embracing a vegan lifestyle while in college can be an enriching experience, offering both health benefits and an opportunity to explore diverse flavors from around the world. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting your culinary journey, these recipes will provide a solid foundation for your plant-based cooking adventures. So gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and enjoy the joy of cooking!
