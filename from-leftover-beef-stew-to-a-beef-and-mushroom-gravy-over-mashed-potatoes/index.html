From Leftover Beef Stew to a Beef and Mushroom Gravy over Mashed Potatoes


So, you made a delicious beef stew for dinner last night, and now you find yourself with some leftovers in the fridge. Rather than simply reheating the stew and serving it the same way, let’s transform those leftovers into a mouthwatering beef and mushroom gravy served over creamy mashed potatoes. This recipe is a fantastic way to breathe new life into your leftover beef stew and create an entirely new and impressive meal.

Ingredients

  • Leftover beef stew
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Mashed Potatoes

  1. Place the peeled and cubed potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil and let the potatoes cook until they are tender.
  3. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them and return them to the pot.
  4. Add the butter and mash the potatoes until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 2: Transform the Leftover Beef Stew

  1. In a large skillet, sauté the finely chopped onion until it becomes translucent.
  2. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they are golden brown and tender.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and mushrooms, and stir to combine. Cook for a minute to get rid of the raw flour taste.
  4. Gradually add the beef broth to the skillet, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and let the mixture simmer until it thickens to a gravy-like consistency.
  6. Add the leftover beef stew to the skillet and let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 3: Serve and Enjoy

  1. To serve, spoon a generous portion of the creamy mashed potatoes onto a plate.
  2. Ladle the beef and mushroom gravy over the mashed potatoes.
  3. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have leftover beef stew, you can use cooked beef chunks or shredded beef as a substitute.
  • Feel free to add a splash of red wine to the gravy for extra depth of flavor.
  • For a lighter version, you can use low-fat milk instead of heavy cream in the mashed potatoes.

By transforming your leftover beef stew into a rich and flavorful beef and mushroom gravy served over creamy mashed potatoes, you’re creating a whole new dining experience that feels anything but leftover. This recipe is a great way to minimize waste and maximize flavor, all while impressing your family or guests with a hearty and comforting meal. Enjoy!
