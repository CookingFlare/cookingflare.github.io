How to Cut Butternut Squash: Taming the Tough Skin


Butternut squash is a versatile and delicious vegetable that can be used in a variety of dishes, from soups and stews to roasted vegetable medleys. However, its tough skin can be intimidating for many home cooks. In this guide, we’ll show you the step-by-step process of cutting butternut squash, making it a breeze to prepare this flavorful ingredient for your next meal.

Tools You’ll Need

Before we dive into the cutting process, let’s gather the necessary tools:

  • Chef’s knife: A sharp knife with a sturdy blade will make the cutting process easier and safer.
  • Cutting board: Choose a stable and spacious cutting board to provide ample space for maneuvering the squash.
  • Vegetable peeler: While not essential, a vegetable peeler can be helpful in removing the skin if you prefer not to use a knife.

Step 1: Preparing the Squash

Start by selecting a butternut squash that feels heavy for its size and has a firm, unblemished skin. This ensures that the squash is fresh and ripe, resulting in a sweeter flavor.

Step 2: Cutting off the Ends

Place the squash on a cutting board and use your chef’s knife to cut off both ends. This will create a stable base for the squash to stand on while cutting.

Step 3: Peeling the Squash (Optional)

If you prefer not to use a knife to remove the skin, you can use a vegetable peeler to peel the squash. Simply glide the peeler along the length of the squash, removing the tough outer layer until you reach the vibrant orange flesh.

Step 4: Halving the Squash

Stand the squash upright on one of its flat ends. With a firm grip on the knife, carefully slice the squash in half lengthwise. Apply gentle pressure and let the knife do the work.

Step 5: Removing the Seeds

Using a spoon or a melon baller, scoop out the seeds and any stringy bits from the center of each squash half. You can save the seeds for roasting if desired.

Step 6: Further Preparation

At this point, you can proceed with your desired recipe. If you need cubed or sliced butternut squash, follow these additional steps:

  • Cubing: Lay each squash half flat on the cutting board and slice it into even-sized cubes. Aim for cubes that are around 1 inch in size for even cooking.
  • Slicing: Place each squash half flat on the cutting board and cut it into thin, consistent slices. Adjust the thickness according to your recipe’s requirements.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips to make the process even easier:

  • Microwave hack: If you’re short on time, you can soften the squash slightly by microwaving it for 2-3 minutes before cutting. This will make it easier to slice through the tough skin.
  • Grip and stability: When cutting, make sure to have a firm grip on the knife and keep your fingers tucked away from the blade. Use the flat, stable ends of the squash to provide support while cutting.
  • Safety first: Always exercise caution when handling sharp knives. Take your time and be mindful of your fingers to avoid any accidents.

Now that you know how to tame the tough skin of butternut squash, you can confidently add this flavorful ingredient to your favorite recipes. Whether you’re making a comforting soup or a hearty roasted vegetable medley, cutting butternut squash will no longer be a daunting task. Enjoy the process and savor the delicious results!
