How to Make a Pizza with a Cauliflower Crust


If you’ve ever wanted to enjoy pizza without the carbs or gluten, a cauliflower crust is a game-changer. It’s a fantastic alternative for those following low-carb, keto, gluten-free, or even paleo diets. This cauliflower pizza crust is crispy, flavorful, and surprisingly sturdy—allowing you to enjoy all the deliciousness of traditional pizza without compromising on your health goals.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to make a cauliflower crust pizza from scratch, including how to prepare the crust, the best toppings to add, and tips to ensure your cauliflower crust turns out perfectly every time.

Why Choose Cauliflower Crust?

The rise in popularity of cauliflower as a substitute for traditional pizza crusts can be attributed to several factors:

  • Low-Carb and Gluten-Free: Cauliflower is naturally low in carbohydrates and free from gluten, making it an ideal base for those avoiding grains or on a low-carb lifestyle.
  • Rich in Nutrients: Cauliflower is packed with vitamins and minerals such as Vitamin C, Vitamin K, folate, and fiber. It’s a nutrient-dense choice compared to regular pizza dough.
  • Versatile: You can load it up with a variety of toppings, and the neutral flavor of the cauliflower crust complements almost any combination of ingredients.

Ingredients for Cauliflower Pizza Crust

Before you get started, here’s what you’ll need to prepare the crust and toppings for your cauliflower pizza:

For the Cauliflower Crust:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower (about 4 cups riced cauliflower)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, for brushing the crust)

For the Pizza Toppings:

  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (or more to taste)
  • Your favorite pizza toppings (pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, spinach, etc.)

How to Make a Cauliflower Crust Pizza

Making cauliflower crust is fairly simple, but it does require a bit of time and patience. The key to achieving a crisp and sturdy crust is removing as much moisture from the cauliflower as possible. Follow these steps for a successful cauliflower crust pizza.

Step 1: Prepare the Cauliflower

  1. Rice the Cauliflower: Start by washing and cutting the cauliflower into smaller florets. You can use a food processor to rice the cauliflower, or you can grate it manually using a box grater. Aim for a fine, rice-like texture. You’ll need about 4 cups of riced cauliflower for one pizza crust.

  2. Cook the Cauliflower: Once your cauliflower is riced, you need to cook it to soften it and reduce its moisture. You can either steam the riced cauliflower for about 5 minutes or microwave it for 4-5 minutes in a microwave-safe bowl, covered with a paper towel. The goal is to soften the cauliflower and release excess water.

Step 2: Remove Excess Moisture

This step is essential to prevent your crust from becoming soggy. After cooking, transfer the cauliflower into a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth. Squeeze out as much liquid as you can. The more moisture you remove, the crispier your crust will be.

Step 3: Prepare the Crust Mixture

Now that the cauliflower is ready, it’s time to mix it with the other ingredients to form the pizza crust.

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the riced cauliflower, egg, shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Stir everything together until you get a thick, dough-like consistency. The egg and cheese help bind the mixture and give it structure.

Step 4: Shape the Crust

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While the oven is heating, line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. You can also use a pizza stone if you have one.

  1. Shape the Dough: Transfer the cauliflower mixture onto the prepared baking sheet. Use your hands to press it out into a circular or rectangular shape, depending on your preference. Aim for about 1/4 inch thickness to ensure a crispy crust.

  2. Optional: Brush the edges of the crust with olive oil for a golden finish. This step adds extra flavor and helps achieve a crispier texture.

Step 5: Bake the Cauliflower Crust

Place the crust in the preheated oven and bake for about 20-25 minutes. After about 15 minutes, check the crust. You want it to be golden brown and firm to the touch. If the edges are starting to brown and the center feels firm, it’s time to take it out.

Step 6: Add the Toppings

Once your crust is done, it’s time to add your pizza toppings. Here’s how to build your pizza:

  1. Spread the Sauce: Start by spreading a thin layer of pizza sauce over the crust. Be careful not to add too much, as the cauliflower crust can become soggy if overloaded with sauce.

  2. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce. If you like your pizza extra cheesy, feel free to add more cheese.

  3. Add Your Toppings: Now comes the fun part—adding your favorite pizza toppings! Whether you’re a fan of classic pepperoni, fresh vegetables like mushrooms and spinach, or something a little more adventurous, the choice is yours.

Step 7: Bake the Pizza

Place the pizza back in the oven for an additional 8-10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the toppings are cooked through. You can also broil the pizza for 1-2 minutes at the end for an extra crispy finish on top.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Once your cauliflower crust pizza is out of the oven, let it cool for a couple of minutes before slicing it. This gives the crust time to firm up, making it easier to handle. Slice it into wedges and enjoy your low-carb, gluten-free pizza!

Tips for Perfect Cauliflower Crust Pizza

  1. Remove Moisture: The more moisture you remove from the cauliflower, the crispier your crust will be. Be sure to squeeze out as much water as possible.

  2. Use a Pizza Stone: If you want an even crispier crust, a pizza stone can help distribute heat more evenly. Preheat the stone in the oven before baking your crust for the best results.

  3. Flavors and Herbs: Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to flavor the crust. Adding a bit of dried basil, rosemary, or red pepper flakes can give the crust an extra kick.

  4. Don’t Overload with Toppings: Less is more when it comes to toppings. Too many toppings can make the cauliflower crust soggy. Stick to a few favorites for the best texture.

  5. Frozen Cauliflower Rice: If you’re short on time, you can use frozen riced cauliflower. Just make sure to thaw it thoroughly and squeeze out the excess water before using it.

Customizing Your Cauliflower Crust Pizza

One of the best things about cauliflower crust pizza is its versatility. Once you’ve mastered the basic recipe, feel free to get creative with your toppings and crust variations.

  • Vegetarian: Load your pizza with fresh veggies like zucchini, bell peppers, spinach, onions, and tomatoes for a light and healthy option.
  • Meat Lovers: Top your pizza with savory meats such as sausage, bacon, pepperoni, or grilled chicken.
  • Vegan: For a vegan version, skip the cheese or use plant-based cheese alternatives. Pile on the veggies and even try a drizzle of olive oil or balsamic glaze for extra flavor.
  • Spicy: Add some heat with jalapeños, spicy sausage, or chili flakes.

Health Benefits of Cauliflower Pizza

Switching from a traditional pizza crust to cauliflower crust offers several health benefits, including:

  • Lower Carbohydrates: Cauliflower crust has far fewer carbohydrates than traditional pizza dough, making it an excellent option for those on low-carb or keto diets.
  • Higher Fiber: Cauliflower is rich in fiber, which aids in digestion and helps keep you feeling full longer.
  • Packed with Nutrients: Cauliflower is a cruciferous vegetable, meaning it’s full of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that contribute to overall health.

Conclusion

Making a cauliflower crust pizza is a fun and delicious way to enjoy pizza while sticking to a healthier lifestyle. With the right technique, you can create a crispy, flavorful crust that’s just as satisfying as traditional pizza dough. Whether you’re following a low-carb, gluten-free, or just a health-conscious diet, cauliflower pizza is a great option for satisfying your pizza cravings.

Now that you know how to make cauliflower pizza crust, the only thing left to do is gather your favorite toppings and get baking. Enjoy this healthier pizza option with family and friends, and feel good about what you’re eating without compromising on taste!
