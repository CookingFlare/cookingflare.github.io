Delicious One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice


If you’re looking for a delicious and comforting meal that’s easy to make and requires minimal cleanup, then this One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice recipe is the perfect choice. Packed with vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. The best part? It can be prepared in just one pot, making it a convenient option for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. Let’s dive into the details of this delightful recipe.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 1/4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Brown the Chicken: Start by seasoning the chicken thighs with salt and black pepper. In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs, skin side down, and cook until golden brown, about 5-6 minutes per side. Once browned, remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Saute the Aromatics: In the same pot, melt the butter and add the finely chopped onions. Saute the onions until they turn translucent, then add the minced garlic. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add the Rice and Broth: Stir in the long-grain white rice and cook for a couple of minutes to toast the grains slightly. Pour in the chicken broth, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried thyme, and dried oregano. Give everything a good stir to combine.

  4. Simmer the Dish: Return the browned chicken thighs to the pot, nestling them into the rice mixture. Bring the pot to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a lid and let the chicken and rice simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the rice is tender and the chicken is fully cooked, remove the pot from the heat. Let it sit, covered, for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a handful of pitted olives or chopped sun-dried tomatoes to the pot along with the rice and broth.
  • If you prefer a lighter option, you can use boneless, skinless chicken breasts instead of chicken thighs.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of the dish. Fresh rosemary or basil can be delightful additions.

Final Thoughts

This One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice dish is a testament to the fact that simple ingredients can come together to create something truly extraordinary. The combination of zesty lemon, fragrant herbs, and tender chicken makes for a meal that is both satisfying and refreshing. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or entertaining guests, this recipe is a fantastic addition to your culinary repertoire. Give it a try and savor the delightful flavors of this comforting one-pot wonder.
