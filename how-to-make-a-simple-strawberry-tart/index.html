How to Make a Simple Strawberry Tart


Strawberry tarts are a delightful treat that combines the sweetness of fresh strawberries with the buttery richness of a crisp pastry crust and the creamy texture of a luscious filling. This simple strawberry tart recipe is not only easy to follow, but it also allows the natural flavors of the strawberries to shine through. Whether you’re preparing it for a summer gathering or just indulging in a sweet craving, this tart is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the process of creating this delectable dessert!

Ingredients

For the Tart Crust

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons ice water
  • A pinch of salt

For the Cream Filling

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened

For the Strawberry Topping

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (optional, depending on the sweetness of strawberries)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Step 1: Preparing the Tart Crust

To start off, we’ll need to prepare the tart crust, which provides the perfect base for our strawberry tart.

  1. Mixing the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, and salt. Whisk gently to ensure that the ingredients are well mixed.

  2. Incorporating the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter into the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial for achieving a flaky texture in the crust.

  3. Adding the Egg Yolk: Create a small well in the center of the mixture and add the egg yolk. Mix gently until the dough begins to come together. If it feels too dry, add ice water one tablespoon at a time until the dough holds together without being sticky.

  4. Chilling the Dough: Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough helps to prevent it from shrinking while baking.

Step 2: Baking the Tart Shell

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Rolling Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle about ¼ inch thick. Carefully transfer it to a tart pan, pressing it into the corners and removing any excess dough from the edges.

  3. Docking the Crust: Use a fork to dock the bottom of the tart crust. This will prevent it from puffing up during baking.

  4. Blind Baking: Place a piece of parchment paper over the crust and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper, and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown.

  5. Cooling the Crust: Once baked, remove the tart shell from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

Step 3: Making the Cream Filling

While the tart crust cools, we can prepare the creamy filling that will complement our strawberries beautifully.

  1. Whipping the Cream: In a medium bowl, whip the heavy cream, vanilla extract, and powdered sugar together until soft peaks form. Be careful not to over-whip, as this can lead to a grainy texture.

  2. Blending the Cream Cheese: In another bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Gradually fold in the whipped cream until the mixture is light and fluffy. This filling should be rich and creamy, providing a perfect contrast to the tartness of the strawberries.

Step 4: Preparing the Strawberry Topping

Next, we will prepare the strawberries, which are the star of this tart.

  1. Slicing the Strawberries: Hull and slice the strawberries. If they are particularly sweet, you may not need to add extra sugar. However, if they are a bit tart, sprinkle 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar over the sliced strawberries, along with the lemon juice. Gently toss to combine and allow them to sit for about 10-15 minutes. This will help to draw out the juices and create a lovely syrup.

  2. Thickening the Juices (Optional): If you prefer a thicker topping, you can add 1 tablespoon of cornstarch to the fruit mixture. Simply mix it in and let it sit for a few more minutes to thicken up.

Step 5: Assembling the Tart

Now that we have all our components ready, it’s time to assemble the strawberry tart.

  1. Filling the Tart Shell: Take your cooled tart shell and carefully spread the cream filling evenly across the bottom. Use a spatula to smooth it out.

  2. Arranging the Strawberries: Gently arrange the sliced strawberries on top of the cream filling, either in a decorative pattern or simply piled high. The vibrant red of the strawberries will create a stunning visual contrast against the creamy filling.

  3. Drizzling the Syrup: If you have a syrup from the strawberries, feel free to drizzle it over the top for added sweetness and shine.

Step 6: Chilling and Serving

  1. Chill the Tart: Cover the tart loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld and the filling to set.

  2. Serving the Tart: When you’re ready to serve, slice the tart into wedges and enjoy! Accompanying it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream can elevate the experience even further.

Tips for the Perfect Strawberry Tart

  • Use Fresh Strawberries: For the best flavor, always opt for fresh, ripe strawberries. If they are in season, their natural sweetness will shine through without needing much added sugar.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add a splash of almond extract to the cream filling or a sprinkle of lemon zest to enhance the flavor profile.

  • Storage: If you have leftovers (which is unlikely!), store the tart in the refrigerator. It’s best enjoyed within a couple of days for optimal freshness.

  • Tart Variations: While this recipe focuses on strawberries, you can easily adapt it by using other fruits such as blueberries, raspberries, or peaches. Each fruit will bring its unique flavor, allowing for endless creativity.

Conclusion

A simple strawberry tart is a classic dessert that showcases the beauty of fresh ingredients and the joy of homemade baking. With its buttery crust, creamy filling, and vibrant strawberry topping, this tart is not only a feast for the eyes but also a delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to impress your friends and family with this stunning strawberry tart that’s sure to be the highlight of any dessert table. Enjoy your baking adventure!
