Vegan Fall Recipes: 8 Delicious Dishes for the Season


As the crisp air sets in and the leaves turn to vibrant hues of red and orange, fall offers a wonderful opportunity to dive into seasonal flavors that are both hearty and comforting. For vegans, this time of year is a dream, with an abundance of seasonal vegetables, fruits, and spices to experiment with. Whether you’re preparing a cozy meal for yourself or cooking for a family gathering, these eight vegan fall recipes will bring warmth and flavor to your kitchen. Each recipe is packed with nutrients, easy to make, and perfect for the season.

1. Butternut Squash Soup

There’s nothing quite like a bowl of rich, creamy soup on a chilly fall evening. Butternut squash is a quintessential fall ingredient, bringing a touch of natural sweetness to this velvety soup. This recipe is perfect for beginners and seasoned cooks alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread it on a baking sheet. Roast for about 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a large pot, sauté the onion, garlic, and carrot in olive oil over medium heat until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the roasted squash, vegetable broth, cumin, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Use an immersion blender or transfer the soup to a blender to puree until smooth.
  6. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, then serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

This creamy butternut squash soup is a delightful start to any fall meal and can easily be doubled for larger gatherings.

2. Stuffed Acorn Squash

Acorn squash is another fall favorite that deserves a spot on your dinner table. Stuffed with a savory filling, this dish is both filling and flavorful. The nutty taste of acorn squash pairs beautifully with quinoa, cranberries, and nuts, making it a perfect vegan main course.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squashes, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans or walnuts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sage
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Drizzle the acorn squash halves with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place them on a baking sheet, cut side down, and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  3. While the squash is roasting, cook quinoa according to package instructions. Fluff with a fork and set aside.
  4. In a bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped nuts, sage, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Once the acorn squash is cooked, flip the halves over and stuff each one with the quinoa mixture.
  6. Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes to heat through.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

This stuffed acorn squash makes for a hearty meal on its own or pairs wonderfully with a light salad for a complete fall feast.

3. Vegan Pumpkin Chili

Pumpkin isn’t just for desserts—it’s also a fantastic addition to savory dishes. This hearty vegan pumpkin chili is packed with protein and fiber, making it a filling and nutritious option for the colder months. The combination of beans, spices, and pumpkin creates a rich, flavorful stew that’s perfect for sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, bell pepper, and zucchini, and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook for another 1-2 minutes to toast the spices.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin puree, tomato paste, vegetable broth, kidney beans, and black beans. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  5. Serve the chili hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

This vegan pumpkin chili is hearty enough to be a meal on its own but also pairs beautifully with cornbread or a fresh green salad.

4. Vegan Sweet Potato Casserole

Sweet potatoes are one of fall’s most beloved vegetables, and this vegan sweet potato casserole is a perfect way to showcase their natural sweetness. Topped with a crunchy pecan topping, this casserole is a comforting and satisfying side dish for any fall gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk or any plant-based milk
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in a large pot of water for 15-20 minutes, or until tender. Drain and mash the sweet potatoes.
  3. Add the coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg to the mashed sweet potatoes and stir until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mashed sweet potatoes to a greased baking dish.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the chopped pecans, rolled oats, and melted coconut oil. Sprinkle this mixture over the sweet potatoes.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown.
  7. Serve warm as a side dish to any fall meal.

This vegan sweet potato casserole is perfect for holiday meals, family dinners, or just as a comforting dish during fall.

5. Vegan Mushroom Risotto

Mushrooms are abundant in fall, and this vegan mushroom risotto is a creamy, comforting dish that highlights their rich, earthy flavor. Using vegetable broth and non-dairy butter, this risotto is just as creamy and satisfying as its traditional counterpart.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (such as cremini or button mushrooms)
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons non-dairy butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat the olive oil and non-dairy butter over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté for 3-5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook for an additional 5 minutes until they release their moisture and begin to brown.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes to toast the rice.
  4. If using, add the white wine and cook until it has mostly evaporated.
  5. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until most of the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth. Continue until the rice is tender and creamy, about 20-25 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and serve garnished with fresh herbs.

This vegan mushroom risotto is an elegant dish that works as a side or a main course for any special fall meal.

6. Vegan Apple Crisp

For a sweet finish to your fall meal, a vegan apple crisp is an easy and delightful choice. Made with fresh apples and a crumble topping, this dessert is warm, comforting, and perfect for cozy nights. The best part? It’s simple to prepare and can be made ahead of time.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup flour (all-purpose or oat flour)
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy butter, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, maple syrup, and cinnamon. Transfer the apples to a greased baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine the rolled oats, flour, coconut sugar, and melted non-dairy butter. Stir until the mixture forms a crumbly texture.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are soft and bubbly.
  6. Serve warm, topped with dairy-free ice cream or a dollop of coconut whipped cream.

This vegan apple crisp is a simple yet satisfying dessert that highlights the flavors of fall.

7. Vegan Caramelized Onion and Spinach Pizza

Fall is the perfect time for hearty, savory pizzas. This vegan caramelized onion and spinach pizza features a sweet, savory topping of caramelized onions paired with fresh spinach, garlic, and a rich tomato sauce. It’s a delicious and comforting dish for pizza lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven according to the pizza dough instructions.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are golden and caramelized.
  3. Roll out the pizza dough and spread tomato sauce evenly over the base.
  4. Top with the caramelized onions, fresh spinach, and minced garlic. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake the pizza according to the dough’s instructions, usually 10-15 minutes, until the crust is golden and crispy.
  6. Slice and serve immediately.

This vegan caramelized onion and spinach pizza is a savory and satisfying meal that’s perfect for sharing.

8. Vegan Cinnamon Rolls

End your fall meal with a warm and gooey cinnamon roll. These vegan cinnamon rolls are soft, fluffy, and packed with the sweet, aromatic flavors of cinnamon and sugar. Topped with a simple glaze, they make a perfect dessert or breakfast for any fall morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 3/4 cup non-dairy milk, warmed
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy butter, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons almond milk

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the yeast and warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the warm non-dairy milk, coconut sugar, melted coconut oil, and salt. Stir in the yeast mixture.
  3. Gradually add the flour, one cup at a time, until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth, then cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  6. Roll the dough out into a large rectangle. Spread the softened butter over the dough and sprinkle with brown sugar and cinnamon.
  7. Roll the dough tightly and slice it into 12 pieces. Place the rolls in a greased baking dish.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  9. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar and almond milk to make a glaze. Drizzle over the warm cinnamon rolls.

These vegan cinnamon rolls are perfect for a cozy fall morning or as a sweet ending to a dinner party.

Conclusion

The fall season brings an abundance of delicious, nutritious ingredients that are perfect for creating comforting, vegan-friendly dishes. Whether you’re craving a hearty soup, a savory casserole, or a sweet dessert, these eight vegan fall recipes offer something for everyone. Embrace the flavors of the season and enjoy these dishes all autumn long. Happy cooking!
