Stuffed Bell Peppers with Quinoa and Cranberries


Stuffed bell peppers are a versatile and comforting meal that can easily be customized to suit individual tastes and dietary needs. In this recipe, we incorporate nutritious quinoa and sweet, tangy cranberries to create a flavorful twist on the classic dish.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound ground turkey or beef
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • Optional topping: fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut off the tops of the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes, ensuring that you leave a hollow space inside for the filling. Rinse the bell peppers and place them in a baking dish. You can add a small amount of water to the dish to prevent the peppers from drying out during cooking.

  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa, vegetable broth, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Allow the quinoa to simmer for about 18 minutes, or until all the liquid has been absorbed. Remove the quinoa from heat and let it rest, covered, for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork.

  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced onion and cook for 5 minutes, or until the onion begins to soften. Stir in the minced garlic and cook another minute.

  4. Add the ground turkey or beef to the skillet. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spatula, until it is no longer pink. Season with cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. The mixture should be well seasoned, but not too salty. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

  5. Stir in the cooked quinoa and cranberries to the skillet with the meat mixture. Cook for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  6. Stuff the bell peppers with the quinoa and meat mixture. If you have extra filling, you can serve it alongside the stuffed peppers.

  7. Place the stuffed bell peppers in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.

  8. Remove the stuffed bell peppers from the oven and serve immediately. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, if desired. Enjoy your delightful and nutritious Stuffed Bell Peppers with Quinoa and Cranberries!

Not only is this dish packed with flavors and textures, but it is also a nutritious and well-rounded meal. Quinoa provides a source of protein and essential amino acids, while cranberries offer antioxidant properties and a touch of sweetness to balance the savory flavors of the bell peppers and ground meat. Give this unique recipe a try and elevate your stuffed bell pepper game!
