Farmers' Market Grilled Cheese Recipe with Fig Jam and Prosciutto


Grilled cheese sandwiches have long been a comfort food staple, but they can also be a canvas for culinary creativity. By incorporating fresh ingredients from your local farmers’ market, such as artisan cheeses, seasonal produce, and flavorful condiments, you can elevate this classic dish to new heights. In this post, we’ll guide you through crafting a delicious Farmers’ Market Grilled Cheese Sandwich that features the rich flavors of fig jam and savory prosciutto.

Why Farmers’ Market Ingredients?

Shopping at farmers’ markets not only supports local farmers but also allows you to discover high-quality, seasonal ingredients. The produce is often fresher than what you find in grocery stores, which can significantly enhance the flavors of your dishes. For this grilled cheese, we’ll focus on a few key components:

  • Artisan Bread: Look for a rustic sourdough or a crusty baguette.
  • Cheese: Select a combination of creamy and sharp cheeses.
  • Fig Jam: A sweet and slightly tangy addition that pairs beautifully with savory ingredients.
  • Prosciutto: Adds a delightful salty contrast.
  • Fresh Herbs: Optional, but fresh basil or arugula can elevate the flavor profile.

Ingredients

To serve two people, gather the following ingredients:

  • 4 slices of artisan bread (sourdough or baguette)
  • 1 cup of cheese (mix of sharp cheddar and creamy brie or mozzarella)
  • 2 tablespoons fig jam
  • 4 slices of prosciutto
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or arugula (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare Your Ingredients

Start by gathering all your ingredients. Slice the cheese into manageable pieces—if you’re using brie, just remove the rind and cut it into thin slices. For cheddar, shredding or slicing works well. If you’re including fresh herbs, rinse them under cold water and pat them dry.

2. Assemble the Sandwich

Lay out two slices of bread on a clean surface. Spread 1 tablespoon of fig jam on each slice. Layer the cheese on top of the fig jam—start with a slice of brie, followed by cheddar, or your preferred cheese combination. Then, place two slices of prosciutto on each sandwich. If you’re using fresh herbs, scatter them on top for an aromatic touch. Finally, top with the remaining slices of bread.

3. Butter the Bread

Spread butter generously on the outer sides of both slices of bread. This will ensure a golden-brown crust when grilled.

4. Heat the Pan

Place a non-stick skillet or a cast-iron pan over medium heat. Allow it to warm up for a couple of minutes. You can test the heat by sprinkling a few drops of water on the pan; if they sizzle and evaporate quickly, the pan is ready.

5. Grill the Sandwich

Carefully place the assembled sandwich in the hot pan. Press down gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking. Cook for about 3-4 minutes or until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Flip the sandwich carefully and cook the other side for another 3-4 minutes. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning; you want a beautifully toasted exterior and melted cheese inside.

6. Let it Rest

Once both sides are golden and the cheese is gooey, remove the sandwich from the pan. Let it rest for a minute. This allows the cheese to set slightly, making it easier to slice.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Cut the sandwich in half diagonally for a pleasing presentation. Serve warm, perhaps alongside a simple arugula salad drizzled with olive oil and lemon juice for a refreshing contrast. You could also pair it with a cup of tomato soup for that classic grilled cheese experience.

Tips for the Perfect Grilled Cheese

  1. Use Quality Ingredients: The better the ingredients, the better the sandwich. Choose high-quality cheese and fresh bread.
  2. Don’t Rush the Cooking: Cooking on medium heat allows the cheese to melt evenly without burning the bread.
  3. Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add other ingredients like caramelized onions, roasted peppers, or a sprinkle of balsamic reduction for extra flavor.
  4. Get Creative with Cheese: Try different cheese combinations like goat cheese with sharp cheddar or mozzarella with gorgonzola.

Health Benefits of Your Ingredients

While grilled cheese might not be considered a health food, there are nutritious elements in this version that can contribute to a balanced meal:

  • Cheese: A good source of calcium and protein, it can support bone health and muscle maintenance.
  • Prosciutto: Offers protein and essential nutrients; just be mindful of portion sizes due to its sodium content.
  • Fig Jam: Figs are high in fiber and antioxidants, contributing to digestive health and overall well-being.
  • Fresh Herbs: Add flavor without calories and provide various vitamins and minerals.

Variations to Try

  1. Vegetarian Delight: Replace prosciutto with roasted vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or mushrooms.
  2. Sweet and Spicy: Add a thin layer of spicy mustard along with the fig jam for a kick.
  3. Mediterranean Twist: Incorporate olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and feta cheese for a Mediterranean flair.

Conclusion

Making a Farmers’ Market Grilled Cheese Sandwich with fig jam and prosciutto is a delightful way to celebrate the freshness of local ingredients. This recipe is versatile, easy to customize, and perfect for any occasion—whether it’s a quick lunch, a cozy dinner, or a gourmet snack. So next time you visit your farmers’ market, pick up some artisan bread, cheese, and seasonal produce, and give this elevated grilled cheese a try. You won’t be disappointed!

Enjoy your culinary adventure, and don’t forget to share your creations with friends and family!
