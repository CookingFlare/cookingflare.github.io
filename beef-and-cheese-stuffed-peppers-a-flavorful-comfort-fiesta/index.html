Beef and Cheese Stuffed Peppers: A Flavorful Comfort Fiesta


If you’re looking for a hearty and flavorful dish that’s sure to please a crowd, look no further than beef and cheese stuffed peppers. This recipe combines the rich, savory taste of beef with the gooey, melted goodness of cheese, all packed into vibrant bell peppers for a dish that’s as visually stunning as it is delicious. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, these stuffed peppers are sure to become a new favorite in your recipe repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Start by preparing the bell peppers. Slice off the tops and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside. Place the peppers in a baking dish.

  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until it’s no longer pink. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and cook until the onion is soft and translucent.

  4. Stir in the cooked rice, drained diced tomatoes, half of the shredded cheddar cheese, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Mix everything well and cook for a few more minutes until all the ingredients are combined.

  5. Spoon the beef and rice mixture into the prepared bell peppers, filling them to the top. Sprinkle the remaining cheddar cheese on top of each stuffed pepper.

  6. In a separate bowl, mix together the tomato sauce, sugar, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Pour the sauce over and around the stuffed peppers in the baking dish.

  7. Cover the dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  8. Once done, remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These beef and cheese stuffed peppers are delicious on their own, but you can also enhance the meal with a few extra touches. Consider serving them with a dollop of sour cream on top, a sprinkle of fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro, or a side of warm crusty bread to soak up the flavorful tomato sauce.

Conclusion

Beef and cheese stuffed peppers are a true comfort fiesta for the taste buds. With their vibrant colors and rich flavors, they’re a feast for the eyes and the palate. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is a great way to impress your family and friends with a dish that’s both comforting and impressive. So, grab those bell peppers and get ready to enjoy a flavorful fiesta with this beef and cheese stuffed peppers recipe!
