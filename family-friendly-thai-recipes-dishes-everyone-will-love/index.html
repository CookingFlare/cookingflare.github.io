Family-Friendly Thai Recipes: Dishes Everyone Will Love


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements. However, many people shy away from cooking Thai food at home, fearing that the dishes may be too complex or spicy for their families. The good news is that there are plenty of family-friendly Thai recipes that are not only easy to prepare but also appeal to the taste buds of both adults and children. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of Thai dishes that everyone in your family will love, from comforting curries to delicious stir-fries.

1. Thai Basil Chicken (Pad Krapow Gai)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground chicken
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving
  • Fried eggs (optional), for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Sauté Garlic and Chilies: Add the minced garlic and chopped chilies to the pan, stirring for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Add the ground chicken to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink.
  4. Add Sauces: Stir in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Add Basil: Remove the skillet from heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves until wilted.
  6. Serve: Serve the Thai basil chicken over jasmine rice, topped with a fried egg if desired.

Why Kids Will Love It:

The combination of savory chicken and fragrant basil creates a dish that is both flavorful and aromatic without being overly spicy. The option to add a fried egg on top makes it even more appealing to children.

2. Coconut Curry Soup (Tom Kha Gai)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger)
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 200 grams mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Sliced red chili, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, combine the chicken broth, coconut milk, lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add Chicken and Mushrooms: Add the sliced chicken and mushrooms to the pot. Cook until the chicken is cooked through, about 10 minutes.
  3. Season the Soup: Stir in the fish sauce and lime juice. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro and sliced red chili if desired.

Why Kids Will Love It:

The creamy coconut milk base makes this soup rich and comforting, while the tender chicken and mushrooms provide a satisfying texture. The flavors are mild enough for children, and they can enjoy slurping the soup with a spoon.

3. Thai Fried Rice (Khao Pad)

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1/2 cup cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Green onions, chopped, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Scramble the Eggs: Add the beaten eggs to the pan and scramble until fully cooked. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Stir-Fry Vegetables: In the same pan, add the mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  4. Add Rice and Protein: Add the cooked rice and your choice of protein (if using) to the pan. Stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes.
  5. Season the Rice: Stir in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar.
