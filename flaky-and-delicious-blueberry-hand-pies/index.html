Flaky and Delicious Blueberry Hand Pies


There’s something magical about biting into a warm, flaky hand pie filled with sweet and juicy blueberries. These handheld treats are perfect for picnics, parties, or simply indulging in a delicious dessert. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make these delectable blueberry hand pies from scratch. Get ready to impress your friends and family with this irresistible treat!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ¼ to ½ cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 3 cups fresh blueberries
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

For the glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter to the bowl and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. If the dough feels too dry, add a little more water. Divide the dough into two equal portions, shape into disks, and wrap each in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the blueberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Gently toss until the blueberries are coated in the mixture. Set aside.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one of the dough disks to a ⅛-inch thickness. Using a round cookie cutter or a glass, cut out circles approximately 4 inches in diameter. Repeat with the remaining dough disk.

  6. Place a spoonful of the blueberry filling onto the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough in half to enclose the filling, then press the edges together to seal. Use a fork to crimp the edges for an extra decorative touch.

  7. Transfer the hand pies to the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each one. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the blueberry filling is bubbling.

  8. While the hand pies are cooling, prepare the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled hand pies.

  9. Serve the flaky and delicious blueberry hand pies warm or at room temperature. They can be enjoyed on their own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • You can substitute the blueberries with other berries like raspberries or blackberries for a different flavor.
  • Feel free to add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg to the blueberry filling for an extra hint of warmth and spice.
  • If you prefer a sweeter glaze, you can add a little more powdered sugar or a drop of flavored extract like almond or orange.
  • These hand pies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Simply warm them in the oven for a few minutes before serving to regain their flakiness.

So there you have it - a recipe for flaky and delicious blueberry hand pies that will leave your taste buds wanting more. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress. So grab your apron, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy these irresistible treats!
