Classic Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry: A Chinese Takeout Favorite


Stir fry dishes are beloved for their quick cooking time, vibrant flavors, and ability to showcase fresh ingredients. One of the most popular stir fry recipes is the classic Beef and Broccoli. This Chinese takeout favorite is a perfect balance of tender beef, crisp broccoli, and a savory sauce that coats every morsel. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the secrets of creating a delicious Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To recreate this classic dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, and beef broth. Set aside.

  2. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the vegetable oil and swirl to coat the pan.

  3. Add the sliced beef to the hot pan and season with salt and pepper. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes, or until the beef is browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.

  4. In the same pan, add the minced garlic and grated ginger. Stir fry for 30 seconds, or until fragrant.

  5. Add the broccoli florets to the pan and stir fry for 2-3 minutes, or until they are bright green and slightly tender.

  6. Return the beef to the pan and pour in the prepared sauce. Stir fry for an additional 2 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and coated the beef and broccoli.

  7. Remove from heat and transfer the Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry to a serving dish.

  8. Serve the stir fry hot over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with sliced green onions, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier version, you can substitute the beef with chicken breast or tofu. Adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as bell peppers, carrots, or mushrooms to your stir fry for added flavor and color.

  • If you prefer a spicier dish, you can add a teaspoon of chili paste or crushed red pepper flakes to the sauce.

  • To ensure tender beef, it’s important to slice it against the grain. This will result in more tender and flavorful meat.

  • You can marinate the beef for 15-30 minutes in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, and ginger before cooking. This will add an extra layer of flavor to the dish.

Final Thoughts

Making your own Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry at home is not only satisfying, but it also allows you to control the quality of ingredients and customize the flavors to your liking. With this recipe in your arsenal, you can recreate the beloved Chinese takeout classic in the comfort of your own kitchen. So put on your chef’s hat, grab your wok, and get ready to impress your family and friends with this delicious stir fry!
