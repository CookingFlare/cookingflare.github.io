Tangy Paleo Beef and Asparagus Casserole


Are you looking for a delicious, nutritious, and easy-to-prepare meal that fits perfectly into your Paleo diet? Look no further than our Tangy Paleo Beef and Asparagus Casserole. This dish is packed with flavors and nutrients, making it an ideal choice for a family dinner or meal prep. Let’s dive into the details of this mouth-watering recipe that will surely become a staple in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

To create this delectable casserole, gather the following ingredients:

Main Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (preferably grass-fed)
  • 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 large onion (diced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 bell pepper (any color, diced)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (no added salt)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (extra virgin)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos (a soy sauce alternative)
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Optional Toppings:

  • Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)
  • Sliced almonds (for added crunch)
  • Nutritional yeast (for a cheesy flavor without dairy)

Instructions

Preparation:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This will ensure that it’s hot enough to bake your casserole evenly.
  2. Prepare the asparagus by washing, trimming off the woody ends, and cutting them into 2-inch pieces. Set aside.

Cooking the Beef Mixture:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the diced onions and sauté until they become translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Introduce the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until the beef is browned and no longer pink, approximately 7-10 minutes.
  4. Season the beef with paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Stir well to incorporate the spices evenly.

Combining Ingredients:

  1. Add the diced bell pepper to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes until slightly softened.
  2. Pour in the canned diced tomatoes (with their juice) and stir to combine all the ingredients thoroughly.
  3. Mix in the coconut milk, coconut aminos, and apple cider vinegar. Let the mixture simmer for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Assembling the Casserole:

  1. Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish lightly with olive oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Spread the asparagus pieces evenly across the bottom of the baking dish.
  3. Pour the beef mixture over the asparagus, spreading it out evenly to cover all the asparagus pieces.

Baking:

  1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven.
  2. Bake for 25 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the casserole is bubbly and the asparagus is tender.

Finishing Touches:

  1. Remove the casserole from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  2. Garnish with fresh parsley, sliced almonds, and nutritional yeast if desired. These toppings add a burst of freshness, crunch, and a hint of cheesiness that complement the casserole beautifully.

Serving Suggestions

This Tangy Paleo Beef and Asparagus Casserole is a complete meal on its own, but you can enhance your dining experience with these serving ideas:

  • Serve alongside a fresh green salad with a lemon vinaigrette to add a refreshing contrast to the warm casserole.
  • Pair with a side of cauliflower rice or mashed sweet potatoes for a hearty and filling meal.
  • Enjoy leftovers for lunch by reheating a portion in the microwave or oven; the flavors often deepen and become even more delicious the next day.

Nutritional Benefits

This casserole isn’t just delicious—it’s also packed with health benefits that align with a Paleo lifestyle:

  • Ground Beef: Provides high-quality protein and essential vitamins and minerals like B12, zinc, and iron.
  • Asparagus: Rich in vitamins A, C, E, and K, and a good source of fiber and folate.
  • Coconut Milk: Adds healthy fats, particularly medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), which are easily digestible and provide a quick source of energy.
  • Garlic and Onion: Offer anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.
  • Bell Peppers: High in vitamin C and antioxidants, supporting overall health.

Tips and Variations

To keep your meals exciting, consider these tips and variations:

  • Substitute Ground Meat: Use ground turkey, chicken, or pork instead of beef for a different flavor profile.
  • Vegetable Variations: Swap asparagus with broccoli, zucchini, or green beans based on your preference or seasonal availability.
  • Spice It Up: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño to the beef mixture for a spicy kick.
  • Herb Enhancements: Experiment with fresh herbs like basil, thyme, or rosemary to add different aromatic notes.

Conclusion

The Tangy Paleo Beef and Asparagus Casserole is a flavorful, nutrient-dense dish that is sure to satisfy your taste buds while adhering to Paleo dietary principles. With its rich combination of ground beef, fresh asparagus, and a tangy tomato-coconut milk sauce, this casserole is both comforting and nourishing. Whether you’re cooking for your family or meal prepping for the week, this recipe is a winner.

Give this recipe a try, and don’t forget to share it with others who might appreciate a wholesome and delicious Paleo meal. Happy cooking!
