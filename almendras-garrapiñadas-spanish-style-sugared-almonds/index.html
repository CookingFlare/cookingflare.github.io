Almendras Garrapiñadas: Spanish-Style Sugared Almonds


If you’ve ever strolled through the streets of Spain, you might have come across the delightful aroma of almendras garrapiñadas, or Spanish-style sugared almonds. These crunchy, sweet treats are a beloved snack throughout the country, and for good reason. They are incredibly easy to make and are the perfect blend of nutty, sugary goodness. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of almendras garrapiñadas, the ingredients you’ll need, and a step-by-step guide to making this delightful Spanish delicacy in your own kitchen.

History of Almendras Garrapiñadas

Almendras garrapiñadas have a rich history in Spain, dating back to the Moorish influence on Spanish cuisine. The Moors brought the tradition of sugaring almonds to Spain, and it quickly became a popular treat, especially during festivals and celebrations. Over time, almendras garrapiñadas became a staple at street markets and fairs, where the tantalizing scent of caramelized sugar and almonds would draw in passersby. Today, they are enjoyed not only in Spain but also around the world as a delightful snack with a touch of Spanish flair.

Ingredients

To make your own almendras garrapiñadas, you’ll need just a few simple ingredients:

  • 2 cups of whole almonds
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep the Almonds

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the almonds in a single layer on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven for about 10 minutes, or until they are golden brown and fragrant. This step helps to enhance the nuttiness of the almonds and gives them a delightful crunch.

Step 2: Make the Sugar Syrup

In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, water, and a pinch of salt. Place the saucepan over medium heat and stir the mixture until the sugar dissolves. Once the sugar has dissolved, stop stirring and allow the syrup to come to a boil. Let it boil for around 8-10 minutes, or until it reaches the soft-ball stage (about 235-240°F or 113-115°C).

Step 3: Add the Almonds

Once the sugar syrup has reached the soft-ball stage, remove it from the heat and quickly stir in the toasted almonds and vanilla extract. Stir the almonds until they are evenly coated with the syrup.

Step 4: Coat and Cool

Using a slotted spoon, transfer the almonds to a parchment-lined baking sheet, arranging them in a single layer and making sure they are not touching each other. Allow the almonds to cool completely, during which time the syrup will harden, creating a delightful sugary shell around each almond.

Step 5: Break Apart and Enjoy

Once the almonds have cooled and the sugary shell has hardened, break them apart and transfer them to an airtight container for storage. These delightful almendras garrapiñadas can be enjoyed as a snack on their own, or used to add a sweet crunch to salads, desserts, or even savory dishes.

Conclusion

Almendras garrapiñadas are a delightful example of the sweet and nutty treats that are beloved in Spanish cuisine. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can recreate the magic of these sugared almonds in your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, looking for a unique snack, or simply want to add a touch of Spanish flavor to your day, almendras garrapiñadas are sure to delight your taste buds and transport you to the vibrant streets of Spain. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create these irresistible Spanish-style sugared almonds.
