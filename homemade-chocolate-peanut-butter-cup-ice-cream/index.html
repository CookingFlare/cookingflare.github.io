Homemade Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Ice Cream


Who doesn’t love the combination of chocolate and peanut butter? It’s a classic flavor duo that never fails to satisfy our taste buds. And when you combine it with the creamy goodness of homemade ice cream, you get a dessert that is truly out of this world. Today, I’m going to share with you my recipe for Homemade Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Ice Cream. Trust me, once you try this, you’ll never want to buy store-bought ice cream again.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped peanut butter cups

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, cocoa powder, and salt. Place the saucepan over medium heat and whisk until the sugar and cocoa powder have dissolved completely.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until they are smooth and pale yellow in color. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling.

  3. Pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture, whisking constantly. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, making sure that the wrap is touching the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

  5. Once the custard is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. During the last few minutes of churning, add the chopped peanut butter cups and continue churning until they are evenly distributed throughout the ice cream.

  6. Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

  7. When you’re ready to serve, scoop the Homemade Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Ice Cream into bowls or cones and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra peanut buttery flavor, swirl in some melted peanut butter during the last few minutes of churning.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve before chilling.
  • Experiment with different mix-ins like crushed cookies, chopped nuts, or even a drizzle of hot fudge sauce.
  • Store any leftover ice cream in the freezer for up to 2 weeks. Just make sure to let it soften for a few minutes at room temperature before scooping.

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple and delicious recipe for Homemade Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Ice Cream. With just a few ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a frozen treat that will impress your family and friends. So why wait? Grab your ice cream maker and get ready to indulge in a scoop (or two) of homemade goodness. Enjoy!
