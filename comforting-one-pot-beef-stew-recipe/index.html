Comforting One Pot Beef Stew Recipe


Who doesn’t love a warm and comforting bowl of beef stew on a chilly day? This classic dish is the perfect combination of tender beef, hearty vegetables, and flavorful broth. And the best part? It’s all made in one pot, making cleanup a breeze. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make a delicious and comforting one pot beef stew that will surely become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This will help seal in the juices and add flavor to the stew.

  2. Once the beef is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Add the carrots, potatoes, and celery to the pot. Stir well to combine with the onion and garlic. Cook for another 5 minutes, or until the vegetables start to soften slightly.

  4. Return the browned beef to the pot and pour in the beef broth and water. Add the bay leaf and dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir to combine.

  5. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  6. Once the stew is cooked, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Remove the bay leaf before serving.

  7. Ladle the comforting one pot beef stew into bowls and serve hot. It pairs perfectly with a crusty bread or a side of buttered noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can add a splash of Worcestershire sauce or a tablespoon of tomato paste to the stew.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with a little water to make a slurry. Stir the slurry into the stew and cook for an additional 10 minutes, or until the stew has thickened.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in this stew to your liking. You can add mushrooms, peas, or even butternut squash for added variety.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to develop, making it even more delicious the next day.

So there you have it, a comforting one pot beef stew recipe that is sure to warm your soul. It’s a simple and hearty dish that brings together the flavors of tender beef and vegetables in a savory broth. Whether enjoyed on a cold winter night or as a comforting meal any time of the year, this beef stew is a true crowd-pleaser. Give it a try and let me know what you think!
