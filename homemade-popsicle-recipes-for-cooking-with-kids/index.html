Homemade Popsicle Recipes for Cooking with Kids


Looking for a fun and delicious way to beat the heat this summer? Why not try making homemade popsicles with your kids? Not only is it a great way to spend quality time together, but it also allows your little ones to get creative in the kitchen. In this blog post, we’ll share some easy and tasty homemade popsicle recipes that are sure to be a hit with kids of all ages.

Strawberry Lemonade Popsicles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the strawberries, lemon juice, sugar, and water. Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds, leaving a little space at the top for expansion.
  3. Insert popsicle sticks into the molds.
  4. Place the molds in the freezer and let them set for at least 4 hours, or until completely frozen.
  5. Once frozen, remove the popsicles from the molds by running them under warm water for a few seconds.

Watermelon Kiwi Popsicles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cubed watermelon
  • 1 cup peeled and sliced kiwi
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the watermelon, kiwi, and honey (if using). Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds, leaving a little space at the top for expansion.
  3. Insert popsicle sticks into the molds.
  4. Place the molds in the freezer and let them set for at least 4 hours, or until completely frozen.
  5. Once frozen, remove the popsicles from the molds by running them under warm water for a few seconds.

Yogurt Berry Popsicles

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 2 tablespoons honey

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, mixed berries, and honey. Stir until well combined.
  2. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds, leaving a little space at the top for expansion.
  3. Insert popsicle sticks into the molds.
  4. Place the molds in the freezer and let them set for at least 4 hours, or until completely frozen.
  5. Once frozen, remove the popsicles from the molds by running them under warm water for a few seconds.

Chocolate Banana Popsicles

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1/2 cup melted dark chocolate
  • 1/4 cup crushed nuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Peel the bananas and cut them in half crosswise.
  2. Insert popsicle sticks into the cut end of each banana half.
  3. Dip each banana half into the melted dark chocolate, coating all sides.
  4. Sprinkle crushed nuts (if using) over the chocolate-coated bananas.
  5. Place the bananas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze for at least 2 hours, or until the chocolate is firm.

Conclusion

Making homemade popsicles with your kids is not only a fun activity but also a great way to cool down during the summer months. With these easy and delicious recipes, you can get creative in the kitchen and create refreshing treats that the whole family will love. So grab your popsicle molds, gather your little chefs, and let the popsicle-making adventure begin!

Remember to experiment with different flavors and ingredients to create your own unique homemade popsicle recipes. The possibilities are endless, and the joy of cooking with your kids will make every bite taste even sweeter. Happy popsicle making!
