Buttery Parker House Rolls: Perfect for Dinner

Parker House rolls, known for their rich buttery flavor and tender texture, have been a classic dinner favorite for generations. Over time, the rolls have remained popular in various social gatherings, from family dinners to cocktail parties. Join us as we explore the world of Parker House rolls and share the best recipes to perfect your homemade dinner staple. Recipe: Traditional Buttery Parker House Rolls Ingredients 1 cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C) 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast 1/4 cup white sugar 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 2 tablespoons room temperature butter 1 teaspoon salt Instructions In a bowl, mix together warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy. In a separate bowl, combine the flour and softened butter. Mix well then add the yeast mixture and salt. Stir until the dough comes together. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic. Cover the bowl and allow the dough to rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Grease a baking sheet. Gently punch down the dough and divide it into equal portions. Shape each portion into a ball, then flatten slightly into a disc. Place the discs on the prepared baking sheet about 2 inches apart, cover, and let rest for another 10 minutes. Bake in a preheated oven for 15-20 minutes until golden brown. Enjoy your warm and buttery Parker House rolls with your favorite dinner dishes.
