Spice Up Your Cooking with Middle Eastern Sumac-Spiced Chicken


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor to your dinner table, look no further than Middle Eastern sumac-spiced chicken. This aromatic and tangy dish is a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine and is sure to impress your family and friends. The unique blend of spices and herbs used in this recipe creates a mouthwatering flavor profile that will transport your taste buds to distant lands. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a sumac-spiced chicken dish that will tantalize your senses and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

To start your culinary journey, gather the following ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons sumac
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Begin by marinating the chicken breasts in a mixture of sumac, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, olive oil, and freshly squeezed lemon juice. Ensure that the chicken is evenly coated with the marinade, then cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 2: Preheat the Grill

While the chicken is marinating, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you prefer, you can also use a grill pan on the stovetop for indoor cooking.

Step 3: Grill the Chicken

Once the grill is hot, place the marinated chicken breasts on the grill and cook for approximately 6-7 minutes on each side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). The sumac and other spices will create a beautiful crust on the chicken, imparting a rich flavor and enticing aroma.

Step 4: Rest and Garnish

After grilling, remove the chicken from the heat and allow it to rest for a few minutes. This step ensures that the juices redistribute, resulting in tender and succulent chicken. Before serving, garnish the sumac-spiced chicken with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and a hint of freshness.

Serving Suggestions

Middle Eastern sumac-spiced chicken pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. Consider serving it with fluffy couscous, a refreshing tabbouleh salad, or a vibrant roasted vegetable medley. The tangy and zesty flavor of the chicken complements these dishes perfectly, creating a well-balanced and satisfying meal.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, Middle Eastern sumac-spiced chicken is a delightful dish that will elevate your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a flavorful weeknight meal, this recipe is a surefire way to impress your guests and tantalize your taste buds. The exotic blend of sumac and spices brings a touch of the Middle East to your kitchen, creating a culinary experience that is both comforting and adventurous. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to spice up your cooking with this delightful Middle Eastern dish. Enjoy!
