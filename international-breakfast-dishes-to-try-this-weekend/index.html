International Breakfast Dishes to Try This Weekend


Breakfast is often called the most important meal of the day, and for good reason. It sets the tone for your energy, mood, and focus. While many of us default to the classic scrambled eggs or a simple bowl of cereal, the world’s breakfast traditions offer a treasure trove of flavors, textures, and aromas waiting to be discovered. This weekend, why not shake things up with some international breakfast dishes that bring excitement and cultural richness to your morning table?

Below, you’ll find a delicious lineup of breakfast ideas from around the globe. Each recipe reflects the unique culinary customs of its country, introducing you to ingredients and preparations you might not have explored before. Whether you’re a savory enthusiast, a sweet tooth, or crave something hearty, there’s something here for you.

1. Japanese Tamago Kake Gohan (Egg Over Rice)

Simple yet satisfying, Tamago Kake Gohan is an everyday Japanese breakfast favorite that combines steaming hot rice with a raw egg and soy sauce, stirred vigorously until creamy.

What You Need

  • 1 cup of freshly cooked short-grain rice
  • 1 fresh egg (ensure it’s sushi-grade for safety)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Optional toppings: chopped scallions, toasted seaweed flakes, sesame seeds

How to Make It

  1. Place a bowl of hot cooked rice.
  2. Crack the raw egg over the rice.
  3. Drizzle with soy sauce.
  4. Stir briskly until the egg coats the rice and becomes slightly frothy.
  5. Garnish with scallions or nori strips if you like.

This quick, protein-packed dish embodies the minimalism and umami flavors that define much of Japanese cuisine, making it a comforting and energizing start to your day.

2. French Croque Madame

The French have truly mastered the art of the delectable breakfast sandwich with their towering Croque Madame—a grilled ham and cheese sandwich topped with a fried egg.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices white bread
  • 2 tablespoons béchamel sauce (butter, flour, milk, and nutmeg)
  • 2 slices of ham (preferably Paris ham)
  • 2 slices Gruyère or Emmental cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Butter for grilling

Method

  1. Preheat your grill or skillet.
  2. Spread béchamel sauce over one side of each bread slice.
  3. On one slice, layer ham and cheese, topping with the other slice (béchamel side down).
  4. Grill the sandwich in butter over medium heat until golden and cheese has melted.
  5. Fry an egg sunny-side up.
  6. Place the egg atop the sandwich and serve immediately.

The combination of creamy béchamel, melty cheese, savory ham, and runny egg yolk creates a breakfast that feels indulgent without being overly complicated.

3. Mexican Chilaquiles Verdes

If you’re craving a breakfast that’s packed with zest, texture, and tradition, Chilaquiles Verdes is a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 8 corn tortillas, cut into triangles
  • 2 cups green salsa (tomatillo-based)
  • 1/2 cup shredded chicken (optional)
  • 1/4 cup diced white onion
  • 1/4 cup crumbled queso fresco
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Fry the tortilla triangles in hot oil until crispy, then drain on paper towels.
  2. Gently heat salsa verde in a pan.
  3. Toss the tortilla chips in the salsa until they soften slightly but still retain some crunch.
  4. Add shredded chicken if desired.
  5. Plate the chilaquiles and top with diced onions, queso fresco, sour cream, avocado slices, and cilantro.

This dish is a flavor fiesta perfect for weekend brunch, combining the earthiness of corn tortillas with tangy salsa and creamy toppings.

4. Turkish Menemen

Menemen is a traditional Turkish scrambled egg dish cooked with tomatoes, peppers, and spices, often enjoyed with crusty bread.

You’ll Need

  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper or Turkish green pepper, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Preparation

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and peppers, sautéing until soft.
  3. Stir in tomatoes and cook for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  4. Crack the eggs over the vegetable mixture and scramble gently.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Cook until eggs are just set but still moist.
  7. Garnish with parsley and serve with fresh bread.

Menemen offers a hearty, colorful breakfast that’s slightly spicy and very satisfying, ideal for a lazy morning.

5. Indian Masala Dosa

For those eager to try a savory, crispy delight, Masala Dosa from South India is a fermented rice pancake filled with spiced potato filling.

Batter Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/3 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • Salt to taste

Filling Ingredients

  • 3 boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Making the Batter

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately overnight.
  2. Grind them together into a smooth batter, using water as needed.
  3. Let the batter ferment overnight until it rises and becomes lightly bubbly.

Preparing the Filling

  1. Heat oil in a pan; add mustard seeds and curry leaves until they pop.
  2. Add onions and green chili, sauté until translucent.
  3. Mix in turmeric powder and potatoes.
  4. Add salt and cook for 3–5 minutes. Garnish with coriander.

Cooking the Dosa

  1. Heat a non-stick pan or cast iron griddle.
  2. Pour a ladle of batter, spread thin in a circular motion.
  3. Drizzle a little oil around edges.
  4. When edges begin to lift and bottom is golden brown, spoon the potato filling on one side.
  5. Fold over and serve with coconut chutney and sambar.

Masala Dosa is a brilliant introduction to Indian flavors and textures: crispy exterior coupled with a soft, spicy center.

6. Lebanese Manakish with Za’atar

Manakish is a popular Levantine flatbread topped with a mixture of za’atar, olive oil, and sometimes cheese or ground meat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Za’atar spice mix
  • Olive oil for spreading

Instructions

  1. Combine flour, yeast, salt, olive oil, and warm water. Knead until smooth.
  2. Allow dough to rise for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  4. Divide dough into small balls, roll out into thin rounds.
  5. Mix za’atar with olive oil to create a paste.
  6. Spread the za’atar mixture evenly over the dough circles.
  7. Bake 8–10 minutes until crispy and golden.

Serve fresh with yogurt, olives, and fresh veggies for a breakfast that feels both nourishing and aromatic, embracing Middle Eastern culinary tradition.

7. American Buttermilk Pancakes with Maple Syrup

No international breakfast list would be complete without mentioning the classic fluffy American pancake.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • Butter and maple syrup for serving

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients without overmixing; lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a griddle or pan over medium heat and grease lightly.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake; cook until bubbles form and edges look set.
  6. Flip and cook until golden.
  7. Serve warm with butter and generous maple syrup.

The warm, fluffy texture paired with the deep sweetness of maple produces a quintessentially comforting breakfast.

Wrapping Up Your Weekend Breakfast Adventure

Exploring international breakfasts is an enriching way to enliven your weekend mornings. From the simplicity of Japan’s egg over rice to the complex flavors of Indian masala dosa, each dish tells a story about culture, climate, and history.

Try mixing and matching these options; pair French Croque Madame with a cup of strong coffee or enjoy Turkish Menemen alongside a fresh salad. Invite friends or family to join and share recipes—food has a wonderful way of bringing people closer.

No matter which breakfast you choose, the key lies in savoring each bite and appreciating the diversity and joy that breakfast traditions from around the world bring to our tables.

Bon appétit! Or as the world says it, itadakimasu, afiyet olsun, buen provecho, shalom, and tamam!
