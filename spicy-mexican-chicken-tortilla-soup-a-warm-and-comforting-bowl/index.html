Spicy Mexican Chicken Tortilla Soup: A Warm and Comforting Bowl


When it comes to classic comfort food, few dishes can match the heartwarming flavors of Spicy Mexican Chicken Tortilla Soup. This beloved recipe combines the richness of chicken, the vibrant spices of Mexican cuisine, and the satisfying crunch of crispy tortilla strips. Whether you’re looking for a cozy meal on a chilly evening or a flavorful dish to impress your guests, this soup is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

To make this Spicy Mexican Chicken Tortilla Soup, you will need:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups of cooked shredded chicken
  • 1 cup of frozen corn kernels
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Tortilla strips, for garnish
  • Sour cream, for garnish
  • Avocado slices, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, jalapeno pepper, and diced red bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.

  2. Stir in the ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to release their flavors.

  3. Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Add the shredded chicken, frozen corn kernels, and black beans to the pot. Simmer for another 10 minutes, or until the soup is heated through.

  5. Stir in the chopped cilantro and lime juice. Taste and adjust the seasonings as needed.

  6. To serve, ladle the Spicy Mexican Chicken Tortilla Soup into bowls. Garnish with tortilla strips, a dollop of sour cream, and slices of avocado. The tortilla strips add a satisfying crunch, while the sour cream and avocado provide a creamy contrast to the spicy flavors of the soup.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder version of this soup, you can reduce the amount of jalapeno pepper or omit it altogether. Alternatively, if you like things extra spicy, feel free to add more jalapeno or even a dash of hot sauce.

  • This soup is a great way to use up leftover cooked chicken. If you don’t have any on hand, you can quickly cook some chicken breasts or thighs and shred them before adding them to the soup.

  • For a vegetarian version of this dish, simply omit the chicken and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. You can also add extra vegetables like diced zucchini or bell peppers for added texture and flavor.

  • To make this soup even heartier, you can add cooked rice or quinoa to the pot during the last few minutes of simmering. This will give the soup an extra dose of filling goodness.

  • Don’t forget to adjust the seasonings to suit your taste. If you like it spicier, add more chili powder or a pinch of cayenne pepper. If you prefer a smokier flavor, try adding a touch of smoked paprika.

Wrap Up

Spicy Mexican Chicken Tortilla Soup is a versatile and satisfying dish that is perfect for any occasion. Its bold flavors and comforting warmth make it an ideal choice for cozy nights at home or gatherings with friends. With a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a bowl of deliciousness that will transport you straight to the heart of Mexico. So grab your favorite pot and get cooking – a steaming bowl of this soup awaits!
