How to Make a Delicious Falafel Wrap


Falafel wraps are a classic Middle Eastern dish, bursting with flavors, textures, and healthy ingredients. This delicious recipe brings together crispy falafel, creamy tahini, crunchy veggies, and soft pita bread, creating a satisfying meal that’s perfect for lunch, dinner, or even as a snack. Whether you’re a falafel enthusiast or a first-time taster, making a falafel wrap at home is easier than you might think.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make an authentic and mouthwatering falafel wrap, starting from scratch with homemade falafel, to the final touches that make this wrap so irresistible.

Why Make Falafel Wraps at Home?

Making falafel wraps at home allows you to control every ingredient, ensuring that each element is fresh, flavorful, and perfectly suited to your taste. While store-bought falafel might be convenient, homemade falafel is fresher, healthier, and more customizable.

With a few basic ingredients, you can create falafel that’s crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and packed with rich, aromatic spices. Combine it with a soft pita or flatbread, some crisp veggies, and a drizzle of creamy sauce, and you’ve got a dish that’s guaranteed to impress.

Ingredients for the Falafel Wrap

For the Falafel:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (garbanzo beans), soaked overnight
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1-2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 4-6 tablespoons flour (chickpea flour or all-purpose flour)
  • Sunflower oil, for frying

For the Wrap:

  • 4 pita bread or flatbreads
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • Fresh lettuce or spinach leaves
  • A handful of pickled turnips or pickled cucumbers (optional)
  • ½ cup tahini sauce (recipe below)

For the Tahini Sauce:

  • ½ cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons water (or more, depending on desired consistency)
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • A drizzle of olive oil (optional)

How to Make Falafel Wraps

Step 1: Prepare the Falafel Mixture

The first step to making the best falafel wraps is preparing the falafel mixture. Start by draining and rinsing the soaked chickpeas. Unlike canned chickpeas, dried chickpeas hold their shape better and result in a crispier falafel.

In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is finely ground, but not pureed. You want it to be coarse, with some texture. If you don’t have a food processor, a blender can work too, but make sure to pulse in short bursts to avoid over-processing.

Once the mixture is ready, add baking powder and flour. Start with 4 tablespoons of flour and add more if needed to form a dough that holds together when shaped into balls. If the mixture feels too sticky, a bit more flour can help. Place the mixture in a bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and refrigerate it for about an hour. Chilling the dough helps the falafel keep their shape while frying.

Step 2: Form the Falafel Balls

After the mixture has chilled, it’s time to shape the falafel. Using your hands or a falafel scoop, form small balls or patties, about 1.5 inches in diameter. If you prefer a flatter falafel, you can press the balls into patties instead.

Place the formed falafel on a tray or plate, making sure they don’t touch each other. This helps maintain their shape and prevents them from sticking together.

Step 3: Fry the Falafel

Heat about 3 inches of sunflower oil in a deep pan or pot over medium-high heat. To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of the falafel mixture into the oil. If it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is ready.

Carefully lower the falafel balls into the hot oil, frying in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry the falafel for about 3-4 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside. Turn them occasionally to ensure they cook evenly.

Once the falafel is done, remove them with a slotted spoon and drain them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

Step 4: Prepare the Tahini Sauce

While the falafel is frying, you can prepare the tahini sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, and salt. Slowly add water a tablespoon at a time, whisking continuously until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. For a thicker sauce, use less water; for a runnier sauce, add more. Optionally, drizzle some olive oil into the sauce for extra richness.

Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice or salt if needed. Set the sauce aside.

Step 5: Assemble the Falafel Wrap

Now it’s time to put everything together. Lay a pita bread or flatbread on a clean surface. Start by adding a generous amount of fresh lettuce or spinach leaves in the center of the wrap. Layer in the sliced cucumber, diced tomatoes, and red onions.

Next, place a few falafel balls on top of the veggies. If you like pickled vegetables, add some pickled turnips or pickled cucumbers for an extra tangy punch.

Drizzle a generous amount of tahini sauce over the falafel and veggies. If you’re a fan of spicy food, you can also add a drizzle of hot sauce or a sprinkle of chili flakes for some heat.

Step 6: Wrap it Up

Fold in the sides of the pita and roll it up tightly, securing all the delicious ingredients inside. If the wrap feels too full or difficult to handle, you can use parchment paper to help hold the wrap together while eating.

For an added touch, you can toast the wrap on a grill pan for 1-2 minutes to give it a crispy exterior.

Tips for Making the Best Falafel Wraps

  1. Soak Chickpeas, Don’t Cook Them – For the best texture, always use dried chickpeas that have been soaked overnight. Canned chickpeas are too soft and will result in mushy falafel.

  2. Don’t Overprocess the Mixture – When blending the falafel mixture, make sure to leave some texture. A smooth puree will not hold together well and will not give you that classic falafel bite.

  3. Chill the Mixture – Refrigerating the falafel mixture before frying helps the falafel hold their shape and prevents them from falling apart in the oil.

  4. Customize the Veggies – While cucumber, tomatoes, and onions are traditional, feel free to add other veggies like shredded carrots, bell peppers, or even olives for extra flavor and crunch.

  5. Serve Immediately – Falafel wraps are best served fresh and warm, so try to assemble and eat them right after frying the falafel. If you need to prepare them ahead of time, keep everything separate and assemble the wraps just before serving.

  6. Use High-Quality Oil – The oil you use for frying can make a big difference in the flavor. Choose a neutral oil, like sunflower or vegetable oil, to let the falafel’s spices shine.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

  • Falafel Salad: If you’re looking for a lighter option, skip the wrap and serve your falafel over a bed of greens with tahini dressing and your favorite salad toppings.

  • Spicy Falafel Wrap: For a spicy twist, add sliced jalapeños or a spicy sauce like harissa to the wrap for extra heat.

  • Falafel with Hummus: Spread some creamy hummus inside the pita along with the falafel for an even richer, more satisfying wrap.

  • Falafel with Tzatziki: If you prefer a yogurt-based sauce, serve your falafel wrap with a refreshing tzatziki sauce made with Greek yogurt, cucumber, garlic, lemon, and dill.

Final Thoughts

Making a delicious falafel wrap at home is not only satisfying but also allows you to enjoy a wholesome, customizable meal. From the crispy falafel to the creamy tahini sauce, every element works together to create a mouthwatering experience. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, preparing a quick weeknight meal, or simply craving something flavorful, falafel wraps are always a hit.

So, gather your ingredients, get your falafel frying, and enjoy this Middle Eastern classic in the comfort of your own kitchen!
