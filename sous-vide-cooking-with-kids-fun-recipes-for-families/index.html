Sous Vide Cooking with Kids: Fun Recipes for Families


Sous vide cooking is often misunderstood as a complex and intimidating cooking method, but it’s actually a great way to cook with kids. With sous vide, you can achieve perfect doneness and texture every time, which is perfect for kids who are picky eaters. In this post, we’ll explore some fun and easy recipes that you can make with your kids using sous vide cooking.

Why Sous Vide Cooking is Perfect for Kids

Sous vide cooking is a great way to cook with kids because it’s easy to understand and execute. The process is simple: you place the food in a bag, seal it, and then cook it in a water bath. This means that kids can help with the process from start to finish, and they’ll love the sense of accomplishment when they see the final result.

Another benefit of sous vide cooking is that it’s a great way to teach kids about measurements and fractions. When cooking sous vide, you need to specify the exact temperature and cooking time, which requires kids to understand and work with measurements. This is a great way to build their math skills and confidence in the kitchen.

Sous Vide Recipes for Kids

Here are some fun and easy sous vide recipes that you can make with your kids:

Recipe 1: Sous Vide Chicken Tenders

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Place the chicken breasts in a bag with the flour, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Seal the bag and massage it to distribute the seasonings evenly. Cook the chicken in a sous vide machine at 130°F (54°C) for 1 hour. Remove the chicken from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. Serve with olive oil and your favorite dipping sauce.

Recipe 2: Sous Vide Hard-Boiled Eggs

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon water

Place the eggs in a bag with the vinegar and water. Seal the bag and massage it to distribute the liquid evenly. Cook the eggs in a sous vide machine at 145°F (63°C) for 1 hour. Remove the eggs from the bag and rinse them with cold water. Peel the eggs and serve.

Recipe 3: Sous Vide Steak Fajitas

  • 1 pound flank steak
  • 1/2 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup sliced onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon fajita seasoning
  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, salsa, cilantro

Place the steak in a bag with the bell peppers, onions, garlic, olive oil, and fajita seasoning. Seal the bag and massage it to distribute the ingredients evenly. Cook the steak in a sous vide machine at 130°F (54°C) for 1 hour. Slice the steak against the grain and serve it with the cooked peppers and onions, along with flour tortillas and your choice of toppings.

Recipe 4: Sous Vide Mac and Cheese

  • 8 ounces macaroni
  • 2 cups grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Cook the macaroni according to the package instructions. In a bag, combine the cooked macaroni, cheddar cheese, mozzarella cheese, heavy cream, butter, salt, and pepper. Seal the bag and massage it to distribute the ingredients evenly. Cook the macaroni in a sous vide machine at 160°F (71°C) for 30 minutes. Stir the macaroni and serve hot.

Tips for Cooking Sous Vide with Kids

Here are some tips for cooking sous vide with kids:

  • Make sure kids understand the concept of temperature and cooking time. This will help them understand the process and make adjustments as needed.
  • Encourage kids to get creative with seasonings and ingredients. Sous vide cooking is a great way to experiment with different flavors and textures.
  • Teach kids how to properly handle and store food. This includes washing their hands before cooking, keeping food at a safe temperature, and storing leftovers properly.
  • Consider using a sous vide machine with a timer and temperature control. This will help kids learn about measurements and fractions, and it will also make the cooking process easier and more efficient.

Conclusion

Sous vide cooking is a fun and easy way to cook with kids. With these recipes and tips, you can help your kids develop a love for cooking and a sense of accomplishment in the kitchen. Remember to encourage creativity and experimentation, and to teach kids about safety and proper food handling. With sous vide cooking, you can create delicious and healthy meals with your kids, and have fun doing it!
