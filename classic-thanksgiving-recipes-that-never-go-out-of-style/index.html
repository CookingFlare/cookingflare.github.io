Classic Thanksgiving Recipes That Never Go Out of Style


Thanksgiving is one of the most beloved holidays, bringing family and friends together around a table filled with mouthwatering dishes that have been passed down through generations. While many things about Thanksgiving may change over time—whether it’s the decor, new family traditions, or modern twists on old recipes—there are certain classic dishes that will always have a place on the Thanksgiving table. These timeless recipes have stood the test of time for a reason: they offer comforting flavors and a sense of tradition that make them irreplaceable. If you’re looking to celebrate the holiday with classic Thanksgiving dishes that never go out of style, look no further than these delicious recipes.

1. Roasted Turkey with Herb Butter

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without a golden, perfectly roasted turkey. This classic dish is often the centerpiece of the meal, and the key to a juicy and flavorful turkey is all in the preparation.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 lbs)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 3 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, quartered
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, sage, thyme, rosemary, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pat the turkey dry with paper towels, then rub the herb butter mixture all over the turkey, making sure to get under the skin and on top of the meat.
  4. Stuff the turkey cavity with the lemon, onion, and any leftover herbs.
  5. Tie the turkey legs together with kitchen twine and tuck the wings under the bird.
  6. Place the turkey on a rack in a roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth and olive oil into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast the turkey in the preheated oven for about 3 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C) in the thickest part of the breast.
  8. Let the turkey rest for 20-30 minutes before carving to allow the juices to redistribute.

This roasted turkey will be golden brown on the outside, succulent and tender on the inside, and packed with delicious herb flavors.

2. Creamy Mashed Potatoes

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without mashed potatoes. This classic side dish is a perfect complement to the richness of the turkey and gravy. The key to making mashed potatoes that everyone will rave about is using the right potatoes and getting the texture just right.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the cubed potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold water. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  4. Add the butter and mash the potatoes until smooth and creamy. You can use a potato masher or a hand mixer for this step.
  5. Gradually add the heavy cream and continue mashing until the potatoes reach your desired consistency.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. For an extra touch, garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

These creamy mashed potatoes are the ultimate comfort food—perfectly smooth, buttery, and rich.

3. Traditional Stuffing

Stuffing is another Thanksgiving staple that has been a part of the holiday tradition for generations. The beauty of stuffing is its versatility—you can tweak the ingredients to suit your taste, but the classic version features a savory combination of bread, herbs, and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of white bread, cut into cubes (about 10 cups)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 large eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Spread the bread cubes in a single layer on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven for about 10 minutes, or until they are golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, and garlic, and sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in the sage, thyme, salt, and pepper, then remove from heat.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the toasted bread cubes, sautéed vegetables, and chicken broth. Stir in the beaten eggs to bind everything together.
  6. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish and cover with aluminum foil.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the top is crispy and golden brown.

This stuffing has the perfect balance of herbs and vegetables, creating a savory, comforting side that pairs wonderfully with turkey and gravy.

4. Green Bean Almondine

For many, green beans are a must-have vegetable on the Thanksgiving table. This simple and elegant dish features fresh green beans sautéed in butter and topped with toasted almonds, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and blanch the green beans for about 3 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. Drain the beans and set them aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced almonds and sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until they are golden brown and fragrant.
  3. Add the garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Toss the blanched green beans in the skillet with the butter and almonds, stirring to coat them evenly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and drizzle with lemon juice for a burst of freshness if desired.

Green Bean Almondine is a light, flavorful dish that brings an elegant touch to the Thanksgiving table.

5. Homemade Cranberry Sauce

Cranberry sauce is the perfect sweet-tart contrast to the richness of the turkey and stuffing. Making it from scratch is incredibly easy, and the result is a vibrant, flavorful side that beats the canned variety every time.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp orange zest (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, and water. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Stir occasionally, and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the cranberries have burst and the sauce has thickened.
  3. If using, stir in the orange zest and cinnamon for added flavor.
  4. Remove from heat and let the cranberry sauce cool to room temperature. It will continue to thicken as it cools.
  5. Transfer to a serving dish and refrigerate until ready to serve.

This homemade cranberry sauce is the perfect balance of sweetness and tartness and can be made ahead of time to save you some stress on the big day.

6. Pumpkin Pie

For many, no Thanksgiving feast is complete without a slice of pumpkin pie. This classic dessert is spiced with cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger, and its velvety custard filling is the perfect end to a hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups canned pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, sugar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg.
  3. Add the eggs and whisk until smooth. Gradually stir in the heavy cream and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly.
  5. Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 40-45 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Let the pie cool completely before slicing and serving.

This pumpkin pie is rich, spiced, and comforting—a true Thanksgiving classic that never fails to please.

Conclusion

These classic Thanksgiving recipes are not only delicious, but they also evoke a sense of tradition that brings families and friends together each year. From the perfectly roasted turkey to the creamy mashed potatoes, savory stuffing, and rich pumpkin pie, these dishes have been enjoyed by generations of families and will continue to be cherished for many more. Whether you’re hosting your first Thanksgiving or you’re a seasoned pro, these recipes are guaranteed to create lasting memories and make your holiday meal truly special. Enjoy!
