Grilled Desserts: Sweet Endings to Every BBQ


Who says grilling is just for savory dishes? When it comes to desserts, firing up the grill can take your sweet treats to a whole new level. The combination of caramelization, smoky flavors, and the slight char from the grill grates adds a delicious twist to traditional desserts. So, if you’re looking to impress your guests with a unique and mouthwatering finale to your next BBQ, look no further. Here are some delectable grilled desserts that are sure to satisfy any sweet tooth.

Grilled Fruit Skewers with Honey Yogurt Dip

One of the easiest and most versatile grilled desserts is fruit skewers. Choose your favorite fruits, such as pineapple chunks, strawberries, and peaches, and thread them onto skewers. Brush the fruits with a mixture of honey and melted butter for a touch of sweetness. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the fruits are slightly charred and caramelized. Serve them with a side of creamy honey yogurt dip for a refreshing and healthy treat.

Grilled Banana S’mores

Take the classic campfire treat to the next level by grilling your s’mores. Instead of using graham crackers, sandwich a slice of banana and a piece of chocolate between two marshmallows. Wrap the whole thing in aluminum foil and place it on the grill. Let the heat melt the chocolate and marshmallows until they are gooey and irresistible. The grilled bananas add a caramelized sweetness that perfectly complements the chocolate and marshmallow combination.

Grilled Pound Cake with Berries and Whipped Cream

Give your pound cake a smoky twist by grilling it to perfection. Slice the pound cake into thick slices and grill them over medium heat until they are lightly toasted and have beautiful grill marks. Top the grilled slices with a medley of fresh berries, such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries. Finish it off with a dollop of whipped cream for a simple yet elegant dessert that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Grilled Watermelon Salad

Watermelon is a summer staple, and grilling it takes its sweetness to a whole new level. Cut thick slices of watermelon and grill them over high heat for just a couple of minutes on each side. The heat will caramelize the natural sugars in the watermelon, intensifying its flavor. Serve the grilled watermelon slices on a bed of arugula or mixed greens, topped with crumbled feta cheese, fresh mint leaves, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze. This unique and refreshing salad is a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors.

Grilled Cinnamon Sugar Pineapple Rings

Pineapple rings are a popular choice for grilling, and when sprinkled with cinnamon sugar, they become a mouthwatering dessert. Brush pineapple rings with a mixture of melted butter and sprinkle them generously with a mixture of cinnamon and sugar. Grill the pineapple over medium-high heat until it is nicely caramelized and has grill marks. Serve the grilled pineapple rings on their own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a tropical and satisfying treat.

Grilled Apple Crisp Foil Packets

Who says you can’t have apple crisp on the grill? This clever twist on a classic dessert is not only delicious but also easy to make. Thinly slice apples and toss them with a mixture of brown sugar, cinnamon, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Divide the apple slices among individual foil packets and top them with a generous amount of crumble topping made from oats, flour, butter, and sugar. Seal the foil packets and grill them over medium heat for about 15-20 minutes until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown. Serve the warm apple crisp with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate grilled dessert experience.

Conclusion

Grilling desserts is a fantastic way to add a unique twist to your BBQ gatherings. From grilled fruit skewers to caramelized watermelon salad, these grilled desserts will elevate your sweet endings and leave your guests in awe. So, fire up the grill, experiment with flavors, and indulge in these sweet treats that are sure to impress. Happy grilling and happy eating!

Note: To ensure the best results, always preheat your grill and lightly oil the grates before grilling desserts. Monitor the cooking time closely to prevent burning, as the sugars in the desserts can caramelize quickly. Enjoy responsibly!
