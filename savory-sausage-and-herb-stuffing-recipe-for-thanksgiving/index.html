Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing Recipe for Thanksgiving


As the leaves turn golden and the air grows crisp, the anticipation of Thanksgiving begins to fill our hearts and homes. One of the cornerstones of a memorable Thanksgiving feast is undoubtedly the stuffing. While traditional bread stuffing has its charm, a Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing brings a delightful twist that elevates your holiday meal into a culinary masterpiece. This recipe combines the rich flavors of savory sausage with aromatic herbs, creating a stuffing that is both hearty and comforting. Let’s dive into this irresistible dish.

Ingredients

To get started on your Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing, gather the following ingredients:

  • For the Stuffing:
    • 1 pound Italian sausage (mild or spicy, based on your preference)
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 3 celery stalks, diced
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • 1 teaspoon dried sage
    • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
    • ½ teaspoon black pepper
    • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
    • 8 cups day-old bread, cubed (preferably a mixture of white and whole wheat)
    • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
    • ½ cup fresh parsley, chopped
    • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
    • ¼ cup unsalted butter

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Sauté the Vegetables and Sausage

  1. Heat the Butter: In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. The butter will add a rich flavor to the stuffing.

  2. Cook the Sausage: Add the Italian sausage to the skillet, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook until the sausage is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. This step is essential as it infuses the stuffing with a hearty meat flavor.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: Once the sausage is cooked, add the chopped onion and diced celery to the skillet. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are softened. Then, stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, allowing the garlic to release its fragrant aroma.

Step 2: Add Herbs and Seasoning

  1. Incorporate the Herbs: Sprinkle the dried thyme, sage, rosemary, black pepper, and salt over the sausage and vegetable mixture. Stir well, ensuring the seasonings are evenly distributed. The combination of these herbs creates a fragrant profile that is quintessentially Thanksgiving.

Step 3: Combine with Bread and Broth

  1. Mix in the Bread: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cubed bread and the sautéed sausage mixture. Gently toss to coat the bread with the flavorful sausage and vegetable blend.

  2. Add Broth and Eggs: Pour the low-sodium chicken broth over the bread mixture, ensuring that every piece of bread is moistened. Then, add the lightly beaten eggs and fresh parsley. Mix gently until everything is combined. The eggs help bind the stuffing together, ensuring it holds its shape when baked.

Step 4: Baking the Stuffing

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This step is crucial for achieving a golden-brown crust on your stuffing.

  2. Prepare the Baking Dish: Grease a large baking dish (about 9x13 inches) with butter or cooking spray. Transfer the stuffing mixture into the dish, spreading it evenly.

  3. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. This allows the stuffing to steam and absorb all the flavors. After 30 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Let it Rest: Once baked, remove the stuffing from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes. This resting period allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

  2. Serve: Serve the Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing alongside your roast turkey or as a stand-alone dish. The stuffing will be moist, flavorful, and packed with savory goodness that everyone will love.

Tips for the Perfect Stuffing

  • Bread Choice: Using day-old bread is key for a great stuffing texture. Fresh bread can become too soggy. A mix of different breads adds depth of flavor and texture.

  • Sausage Variations: Feel free to experiment with different types of sausage. A blend of sweet and spicy sausage can create a delightful flavor contrast.

  • Herb Freshness: If you have access to fresh herbs, use them! Fresh herbs can significantly enhance the flavor profile of your stuffing.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the stuffing a day ahead. Just mix all the ingredients and store it in the refrigerator. On Thanksgiving day, simply bake it before serving.

Pairing Suggestions

This Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing pairs perfectly with classic Thanksgiving dishes. The hearty flavors complement roast turkey, glazed ham, or even a vegetarian main course. You might also consider serving it alongside:

  • Cranberry Sauce: The tartness of cranberry sauce balances the savory flavors beautifully.

  • Gravy: A rich turkey or mushroom gravy drizzled over the stuffing adds an extra layer of comfort.

  • Roasted Vegetables: Seasonal roasted vegetables, such as Brussels sprouts, carrots, and butternut squash, offer a colorful and nutritious side.

Conclusion

This Savory Sausage and Herb Stuffing recipe is sure to become a cherished part of your Thanksgiving tradition. Its comforting flavors and delightful textures will impress your family and guests alike. Whether served alongside your turkey or enjoyed as a standalone dish, this stuffing is a heartwarming addition to any holiday spread.

As you prepare for the festivities, let this recipe inspire you to create delicious memories around the dining table. Happy Thanksgiving!
