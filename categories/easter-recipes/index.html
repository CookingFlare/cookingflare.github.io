Easter Baking Bonanza: Recipes for Beginners to Pros

Welcome to our Easter Baking Bonanza! Whether you’re a baking beginner looking to impress your family or a seasoned pro eager to try new recipes, this collection has something for everyone. From classic Easter treats to innovative twists, get ready to fill your kitchen with the delightful aromas of freshly baked goodies. Classic Hot Cross Buns Ingredients: 4 cups all-purpose flour 1/3 cup sugar 1 tsp salt 1 tbsp active dry yeast 1 1/4 cups warm milk 1/4 cup melted butter 1 egg 1/2 cup dried currants or raisins 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg Instructions: In a bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy. Add melted butter, egg, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and half of the flour. Mix well. Gradually add the remaining flour and knead until the dough is smooth. Mix in the currants or raisins. Cover the dough and let it rise for about an hour. Divide the dough into balls, place them on a baking sheet, cover, and let rise again. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown. Optional: Mix powdered sugar and milk to make a glaze and pipe crosses on top of the buns. Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Ingredients: 2 cups graham cracker crumbs 1/2 cup melted butter 24 oz cream cheese, softened 1 cup sugar 3 eggs 1 tsp vanilla extract Zest of 1 lemon 1/4 cup lemon juice 1 cup fresh blueberries Instructions: Mix graham cracker crumbs and melted butter, then press into the bottom of a springform pan. Beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Gently fold in blueberries. Pour the mixture into the pan and bake at 325°F (160°C) for about 55-60 minutes. Let it cool, then refrigerate for a few hours before serving. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour 2 tsp baking soda 1/2 tsp salt 2 tsp ground cinnamon 1 1/2 cups vegetable oil 1 cup granulated sugar 1 cup brown sugar 4 eggs 2 tsp vanilla extract 3 cups grated carrots 1 cup chopped nuts (optional) Instructions: Mix flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. In another bowl, whisk oil, sugars, eggs, and vanilla. Combine wet and dry ingredients, then fold in carrots and nuts. Pour into greased pans and bake at 350°F (180°C) for 25-30 minutes. Cool before frosting with a mixture of cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla.
