Thai Spicy and Sour Soup Recipe with Shrimp and Vegetables


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing like a warm and comforting bowl of Thai Spicy and Sour Soup to lift your spirits. This recipe is a staple in Thai cuisine, and for good reason – it’s a masterclass in balancing flavors, textures, and temperatures. In this recipe, we’ll be using succulent shrimp and a variety of colorful vegetables to create a dish that’s both nourishing and exciting.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, mushrooms, and Thai basil)
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons of Thai red curry paste
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground white pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by preparing the ingredients. Cut the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces and set aside. Rinse the shrimp under cold water, pat them dry with paper towels, and set aside. Mince the garlic and grate the ginger.

Step 2: Cook the Shrimp

Heat the oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook until they’re pink and just cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the shrimp from the pot and set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Vegetables

Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until they’re tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes.

Step 4: Add the Curry Paste and Broth

Add the Thai red curry paste to the pot and cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute. Add the chicken or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil.

Step 5: Add the Coconut Milk and Seasonings

Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the coconut milk. Stir to combine, then add the fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar, and white pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the soup has thickened slightly.

Step 6: Add the Shrimp and Season

Add the cooked shrimp back to the pot and season the soup with salt to taste.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of spice, add some diced Thai chilies or serrano peppers to the pot during Step 4.
  • Substitute the shrimp with chicken, pork, or tofu for a vegetarian option.
  • Add some crunchy texture to the soup by sprinkling some toasted peanuts or cashews on top.
  • Experiment with different types of vegetables, such as zucchini, eggplant, or bell peppers, to add some variety to the dish.

Conclusion

Thai Spicy and Sour Soup is a dish that’s both familiar and exotic, comforting and exciting. With its bold flavors and textures, it’s a recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the magic of Thai cuisine for yourself!
