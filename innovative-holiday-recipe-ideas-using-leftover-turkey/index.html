Innovative Holiday Recipe Ideas Using Leftover Turkey


The holidays often leave us with cherished memories, laughter, and, most importantly, delicious feasts. But what happens when the festivities conclude and you’re left with an abundance of leftover turkey? Instead of letting it languish in the fridge, why not transform those remnants into delightful new dishes? This blog post explores innovative ways to repurpose leftover turkey, ensuring your holiday spirit lasts well into the New Year.

1. Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

One of the most comforting ways to utilize leftover turkey is by making a hearty soup. Wild rice adds a nutty flavor and delightful texture, making this dish a family favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup wild rice, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 cups turkey or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, sautéing until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in the garlic and thyme, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the wild rice and broth, bringing the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for 45 minutes or until the rice is cooked.
  4. Stir in the shredded turkey and season with salt and pepper. Heat through for another 5-10 minutes.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

2. Turkey Enchiladas

Transform that turkey into a fiesta with these flavorful enchiladas. The combination of spices and cheese makes them irresistible!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 8 corn tortillas
  • 1 can (10 oz) enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, sauté the onion until translucent. Add the shredded turkey, cumin, chili powder, and half of the enchilada sauce. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of enchilada sauce. Fill each tortilla with a generous scoop of the turkey mixture, roll, and place seam-side down in the dish.
  4. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the filled tortillas and sprinkle with cheddar cheese.
  5. Bake for 20 minutes, until bubbly. Serve with sour cream and garnish with fresh cilantro.

3. Turkey Pot Pie

Nothing says comfort food quite like a pot pie. This dish is perfect for chilly evenings and is a great way to use up leftover turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, diced
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/3 cup flour
  • 2 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onion until soft. Stir in the flour and cook for another minute.
  3. Gradually add the broth and milk, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens. Add the mixed vegetables, turkey, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the turkey mixture into a pie dish and cover with the pie crust. Cut slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Let cool slightly before serving.

4. Turkey Salad Sandwiches

For a lighter option, turkey salad sandwiches provide a refreshing twist. Perfect for a quick lunch or picnic!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, diced
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole grain bread or rolls
  • Lettuce leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Greek yogurt, and Dijon mustard.
  2. Add the diced turkey, celery, cranberries, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Spread the turkey salad on whole grain bread or rolls with lettuce.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for later.

5. Turkey Fried Rice

This turkey fried rice is a quick and delicious way to enjoy your leftovers. It’s a perfect one-pan meal that’s both satisfying and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, diced
  • 4 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed frozen vegetables
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Push the vegetables to the side and add the beaten eggs, scrambling until fully cooked.
  3. Add the cooked rice, diced turkey, soy sauce, and green onions. Stir-fry for another 5-7 minutes until heated through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.

6. Turkey Curry

Give your leftover turkey an exotic twist with a rich and aromatic curry. This dish is not only flavorful but also easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Stir in the curry powder and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the coconut milk, shredded turkey, and bell pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the spinach until wilted. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve over cooked rice.

7. Turkey and Spinach Stuffed Shells

These stuffed shells are not only beautiful but also bursting with flavor. They make an excellent dish for family gatherings or potlucks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the jumbo shells according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, mix the shredded turkey, ricotta cheese, spinach, mozzarella, salt, and pepper.
  4. Fill each shell with the turkey mixture and place them in a baking dish. Pour marinara sauce over the top and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until bubbly.

8. Turkey Quesadillas

These turkey quesadillas are quick, easy, and packed with flavor. Perfect for a weeknight dinner!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salsa for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing until soft.
  2. On one half of each tortilla, layer turkey, sautéed vegetables, and cheese. Fold the other half over.
  3. Cook each quesadilla in the skillet until golden brown on both sides and the cheese is melted, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. Cut into wedges and serve with salsa.

9. Turkey Shepherd’s Pie

Shepherd’s pie is a hearty dish that brings comfort and warmth. It’s a great way to reinvent your leftover turkey into a delightful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, diced
  • 2 cups mashed potatoes (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 cup turkey or chicken broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the onion until soft. Add the peas and carrots, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  3. Stir in the diced turkey and broth, mixing well. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Pour the turkey mixture into a baking dish and top with an even layer of mashed potatoes.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the top is golden and crispy. Let cool slightly before serving.

10. Turkey Tacos

Add some zest to your leftovers with turkey tacos. They’re fun to make and even more enjoyable to eat!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 8 corn or flour tortillas
  • 1 teaspoon taco seasoning
  • 1 cup lettuce, shredded
  • 1 cup tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cup cheese, shredded
  • Sour cream and salsa for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat shredded turkey with taco seasoning until warmed through.
  2. Warm tortillas in a separate skillet or microwave.
  3. Assemble tacos by adding turkey, lettuce, tomatoes, and cheese to each tortilla.
  4. Serve with sour cream and salsa on the side.

Conclusion

Leftover turkey doesn’t have to mean boring meals. With a little creativity, you can transform it into an array of delicious dishes that will keep the festive spirit alive. From comforting soups to vibrant tacos, these innovative holiday recipes ensure that your Thanksgiving or holiday feast continues to delight your taste buds long after the last guest has left. So, the next time you find yourself with extra turkey, don’t hesitate to get inventive in the kitchen! Enjoy your culinary adventures!
