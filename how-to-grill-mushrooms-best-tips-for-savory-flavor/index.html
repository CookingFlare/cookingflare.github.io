How to Grill Mushrooms: Best Tips for Savory Flavor


Grilling mushrooms can transform them from a simple side dish into a savory, smoky delicacy that adds depth to your meals. Whether you’re looking to enhance your barbecue menu or discover a new way to enjoy your favorite fungi, grilling mushrooms is the way to go. The combination of heat, wood, and the mushrooms’ natural umami flavors creates a perfect dish for meat lovers and vegetarians alike. This guide will walk you through the best techniques to grill mushrooms and unlock their full potential, making your grilled mushrooms the star of your next meal.

Choosing the Right Mushrooms

Not all mushrooms are created equal when it comes to grilling. Some varieties are better suited to withstand the high heat of the grill while others may fall apart or lose their flavor too easily. Here are the best types of mushrooms for grilling:

Portobello Mushrooms

Portobello mushrooms are a grilling favorite. Their large, meaty caps hold up well to the heat, and their robust flavor makes them an excellent substitute for burgers. Their size allows for easy flipping on the grill, and they can be stuffed with cheese, herbs, or other ingredients for added flavor.

Cremini Mushrooms

Cremini mushrooms, also known as baby bellas, are smaller but still packed with flavor. They have a firmer texture compared to button mushrooms, making them suitable for grilling. You can grill them whole or skewer them for an even cook.

Shiitake Mushrooms

Shiitake mushrooms are often used in Asian cuisine but can bring an earthy, umami-rich flavor to grilled dishes. Their tender caps may require a bit more care, but they are worth the effort when grilled. Shiitakes pair wonderfully with soy sauce, garlic, and herbs, creating a perfect marinade.

Oyster Mushrooms

Oyster mushrooms are delicate but full of flavor. Their wide, thin caps make them perfect for grilling on a skewer or in a grill basket. When cooked properly, they develop a lovely smoky flavor that complements a variety of dishes.

Button Mushrooms

While not as sturdy as other varieties, button mushrooms can still be grilled if handled carefully. You can thread them onto skewers or use a grill basket to ensure they don’t fall through the grates. Their mild flavor can absorb marinades beautifully.

Preparation Tips for Grilling Mushrooms

Proper preparation is essential to ensure your mushrooms grill to perfection. Follow these key steps to get the best results:

1. Clean the Mushrooms

Before grilling, it’s important to clean your mushrooms properly. Use a damp cloth or paper towel to wipe away dirt and debris. Avoid rinsing mushrooms under water as they are porous and absorb moisture, which can make them soggy when cooked.

2. Remove the Stems (if Necessary)

For mushrooms like portobellos, you may want to remove the tough stems. You can save them for making stock or composting. If you’re using smaller mushrooms, like creminis or button mushrooms, leaving the stems intact can provide more structure during grilling.

3. Slice or Keep Whole?

If you’re grilling larger mushrooms like portobellos, consider slicing them into thick pieces or leaving them whole, depending on your preference. For smaller mushrooms, such as creminis or button mushrooms, grilling them whole works best. Skewering small mushrooms ensures even cooking, while allowing larger mushrooms to retain their shape and juices.

4. Marinate or Season

Marinating mushrooms before grilling adds an extra layer of flavor. Mushrooms soak up marinades quickly, so even a short time of 15-20 minutes can make a difference. A good marinade for grilled mushrooms includes olive oil, garlic, soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, lemon juice, and a touch of herbs like thyme or rosemary. For a more intense flavor, try a spicy marinade with chili flakes or smoked paprika. Alternatively, if you don’t want to marinate, simply brush the mushrooms with olive oil and season them with salt, pepper, and any herbs or spices you like.

Grilling Techniques for Mushrooms

There are several ways to grill mushrooms, each offering a different result. Here are the most effective techniques:

Direct Grilling

Direct grilling involves placing the mushrooms directly on the grill grates. This method works best for larger mushrooms, like portobellos, or for skewered smaller mushrooms. It gives the mushrooms a beautiful char and smoky flavor.

How to Grill Mushrooms Using Direct Heat:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat, around 400°F.
  2. Place the cleaned and seasoned mushrooms directly onto the grill.
  3. Grill whole portobellos for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have grill marks. For smaller mushrooms, cook them for 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. Brush the mushrooms with additional marinade or olive oil while grilling to keep them moist and flavorful.
  5. Remove from the grill once they have a nice char and tender texture.

Indirect Grilling

For mushrooms that may be more delicate, like shiitakes or oyster mushrooms, indirect grilling is a gentler option. In this method, the mushrooms are placed off to the side of the grill, allowing them to cook using indirect heat. This technique reduces the risk of burning and ensures the mushrooms cook evenly.

How to Grill Mushrooms Using Indirect Heat:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat, and then turn off one of the burners or move the coals to one side.
  2. Place the mushrooms on the cooler side of the grill, away from direct heat.
  3. Close the lid and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally. This method allows the mushrooms to cook through without burning.
  4. Once done, move the mushrooms to the hot side of the grill for 1-2 minutes to add a bit of char before serving.

Using a Grill Basket

For smaller mushrooms or delicate varieties, a grill basket can be a real lifesaver. The basket prevents the mushrooms from falling through the grates while allowing them to absorb the smoky flavor from the grill. It’s especially useful for skewered mushrooms or small varieties like button or cremini mushrooms.

How to Grill Mushrooms in a Grill Basket:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the seasoned mushrooms in the grill basket, ensuring they are not overcrowded.
  3. Place the basket on the grill and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 8-10 minutes. The mushrooms should be tender and slightly charred.
  4. If you’re using a marinade, baste the mushrooms once or twice during cooking for enhanced flavor.

Adding Flavor with Sauces and Seasonings

While grilled mushrooms are delicious on their own, you can elevate their flavor with the right sauces and seasonings. Here are a few ideas to complement your grilled mushrooms:

Garlic Butter

A classic choice that adds richness and depth to grilled mushrooms is garlic butter. Simply melt some butter and mix in minced garlic, fresh herbs like parsley or thyme, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Brush this mixture onto the mushrooms just before serving for an extra burst of flavor.

Balsamic Glaze

Balsamic vinegar can bring a tangy, sweet note to your mushrooms. Reduce the balsamic vinegar to a syrupy consistency and drizzle it over the grilled mushrooms for a burst of acidity and sweetness.

Soy Sauce and Sesame Oil

For an Asian-inspired twist, drizzle grilled mushrooms with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. This combination enhances the natural umami of the mushrooms, adding complexity and depth to the dish.

Spicy Marinades

For those who like a little heat, try a spicy marinade made from chili paste, cayenne pepper, or smoked paprika. The spicy flavors contrast beautifully with the earthy taste of the mushrooms, giving them an extra kick.

Fresh Herbs and Parmesan

After grilling, sprinkle freshly chopped herbs like parsley, rosemary, or thyme over the mushrooms. You can also shave a bit of Parmesan cheese on top for added richness. The heat from the mushrooms will melt the cheese slightly, creating a savory and aromatic finish.

Serving Ideas for Grilled Mushrooms

Grilled mushrooms can be served as a side dish or as the main event. Here are some creative ways to incorporate them into your meals:

Mushroom Burgers

Portobello mushrooms make an excellent substitute for beef patties in burgers. Grill them whole and use them as the “bun” or patty in a burger. Top with your favorite condiments, cheese, and veggies for a satisfying vegetarian meal.

Grilled Mushroom Skewers

Skewer a mix of mushrooms, vegetables like bell peppers, onions, and cherry tomatoes, and grill them together for a colorful and flavorful dish. These skewers make for a perfect appetizer or side for a summer barbecue.

Mushroom Tacos

Grilled mushrooms can add a rich, savory filling to tacos. Pair them with toppings like avocado, cilantro, lime, and salsa for a satisfying vegetarian taco option.

Salad Topping

Grilled mushrooms make an excellent topping for fresh salads. Their smoky flavor contrasts well with the crispness of greens, and they add a meaty texture without the need for meat.

Pasta or Risotto

For a heartier meal, grilled mushrooms can be tossed with pasta or mixed into a creamy risotto. Their smoky richness complements the flavors of the sauce, adding depth and complexity to the dish.

Conclusion

Grilling mushrooms is a simple yet effective way to bring out their rich flavors and create a satisfying dish. Whether you’re using them as a side, a main, or in creative applications like burgers or tacos, grilled mushrooms can be a game-changer in your cooking repertoire. With the right variety, preparation, and grilling techniques, you’ll unlock a new world of savory, smoky flavor that will have everyone at the table coming back for more.

So fire up your grill, choose your mushrooms, and start experimenting with different seasonings and marinades. You’re in for a truly delicious treat!
