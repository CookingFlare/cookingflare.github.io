Gomae: A Nutty and Tangy Japanese Spinach Salad


If you’re looking for a refreshing and nutritious dish to add to your culinary repertoire, look no further than Gomae, a delightful Japanese spinach salad that’s bursting with flavor and texture. This simple yet elegant dish combines blanched spinach with a delectable sesame dressing, creating a harmonious blend of nutty and tangy flavors. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking to explore new flavors, Gomae is a must-try recipe that’s sure to impress your taste buds and your guests.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need to create this delightful Japanese spinach salad:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Spinach

Start by thoroughly washing the fresh spinach under cold running water to remove any dirt or impurities. Once clean, bring a pot of water to a boil and blanch the spinach for just a minute or two until it’s wilted but still vibrant green. Be sure not to overcook the spinach, as it should retain a slight crunch.

Step 2: Rinse and Squeeze

After blanching, immediately transfer the spinach to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process and lock in the vibrant green color. Once cooled, gently squeeze out the excess water from the spinach using your hands. It’s important to remove as much water as possible to prevent the salad from becoming watery.

Step 3: Toast the Sesame Seeds

In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the sesame seeds until they become golden brown and aromatic. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning, and give the skillet a shake or stir the seeds frequently for even toasting. Once toasted, transfer the sesame seeds to a bowl and set them aside to cool.

Step 4: Prepare the Dressing

In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sugar, mirin, rice vinegar, and sesame oil until the sugar has dissolved. This flavorful dressing will serve as the perfect complement to the earthy spinach and nutty sesame seeds.

Step 5: Combine and Toss

Place the squeezed spinach in a large bowl and add the toasted sesame seeds. Drizzle the sesame dressing over the spinach and gently toss everything together until the spinach is evenly coated with the dressing and sesame seeds are distributed throughout.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once the Gomae is thoroughly mixed, it’s ready to be served. Transfer the salad to a serving dish and garnish with an extra sprinkle of sesame seeds for a delightful visual and textural appeal. This Japanese spinach salad can be enjoyed as a side dish or a light appetizer, and it pairs beautifully with a variety of main courses.

Final Thoughts

Gomae is a wonderful representation of the delicate balance of flavors and textures that Japanese cuisine is known for. Its simplicity allows the natural flavors of the spinach and sesame to shine, while the tangy dressing adds a delightful zing to each bite. Whether you’re preparing a special dinner for guests or simply treating yourself to a nourishing meal, Gomae is a versatile and delicious addition to any menu. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure with this delightful Japanese spinach salad.
