How to Make Delicious Greek Style Stovetop Pizza


Greek-style stovetop pizza is a fresh and exciting take on classic pizza that you can make without the need for an oven. It’s perfect for those who want a quick, flavorful dish packed with Mediterranean flair. With a crispy yet tender crust, vibrant toppings, and rich cheeses, this pizza captures the essence of Greek cuisine while being delightfully easy to prepare right on your stovetop. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or sharing with family and friends, this recipe is guaranteed to impress. Let’s dive right into the process and see how to create this culinary masterpiece.

Ingredients

To get that authentic Greek-style flavor, you’ll need a combination of pantry staples, fresh produce, and a few Mediterranean-inspired specialties.

For the Crust:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plus more for dusting)
  • ⅓ cup warm water
  • 1½ tbsp olive oil (divided)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt

For the Sauce:

  • ¾ cup canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1 small garlic clove, finely minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp dried basil
  • Pinch of salt and pepper

For the Toppings:

  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ⅓ cup shredded mozzarella cheese (optional for added creaminess)
  • ¼ cup Kalamata olives, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved or quartered
  • ¼ cup roasted red peppers, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh spinach leaves or baby arugula
  • 1 tsp dried oregano or Greek seasoning blend
  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped (for garnish)

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Homemade crust is the foundation of any great pizza, and making a simple stovetop-friendly dough is easier than you think.

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients:
    In a medium-sized mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Stir until evenly mixed.

  2. Add the Wet Ingredients:
    Pour in the warm water and 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Using a fork or your hands, mix until the dough comes together.

  3. Knead the Dough:
    Transfer the dough to a clean, lightly floured surface. Knead it gently for about 2-3 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Let the Dough Rest:
    Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and let it rest for 15-20 minutes. This gives the gluten time to relax, making it easier to roll out.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

While the dough is resting, it’s time to whip up your aromatic Greek-inspired pizza sauce.

  1. Heat a small saucepan over medium heat and add the olive oil. When warm, toss in the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant—be careful not to burn it.

  2. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Once thickened slightly, remove the sauce from the heat and set it aside. This sauce will provide the perfect tangy base for your stovetop pizza.

Step 3: Preheat the Pan

  1. Choose a large non-stick skillet or cast-iron pan with a lid. Preheat the pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes. Add the remaining ½ tablespoon of olive oil and spread it evenly across the pan with a brush or paper towel.

  2. Be sure the pan is warm, but not excessively hot, to ensure even cooking of the crust without burning.

Step 4: Shape and Cook the Dough

  1. Once your dough has rested, roll it out into a circle or oval roughly the size of your skillet. If you don’t have a rolling pin, you can stretch the dough with your hands.

  2. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough into the preheated pan. Cook the dough on one side for about 2-3 minutes until you see bubbles forming on the surface and the underside turns golden brown.

  3. Flip the dough and immediately lower the heat slightly. This ensures the pizza won’t burn as you add the toppings.

Step 5: Add the Sauce and Toppings

  1. Spread an even layer of your prepared tomato sauce over the cooked side of the dough. Use the back of a spoon to ensure the sauce covers the entire surface, leaving a small border around the edges.

  2. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese across the sauce, followed by shredded mozzarella (if using).

  3. Add your veggie toppings in an even layer, starting with the spinach (or arugula), then piling on the olives, onions, cherry tomatoes, and roasted red peppers. Sprinkle the oregano or Greek seasoning over the top for an extra dash of flavor.

Step 6: Cover and Cook

  1. Once the pizza is fully assembled, cover the pan with its lid. This step is crucial as it traps heat and creates an oven-like effect, helping the cheese melt and toppings cook through.

  2. Allow the pizza to cook for 5-7 minutes on low-to-medium heat. Check periodically to ensure the bottom of the crust doesn’t burn.

  3. The pizza is ready when the cheese is melted, and the crust is cooked through with lightly crisped edges.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Carefully slide the finished pizza onto a cutting board. Allow it to cool for a minute or two before slicing.

  2. Just before serving, sprinkle fresh parsley or basil on top for an herbaceous pop of flavor. If you like a zesty finish, drizzle a tiny bit of olive oil or add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to enhance the Mediterranean vibe.

  3. Plate and serve the Greek-style stovetop pizza warm. Pair it with a crisp green salad or a glass of white wine to complete the meal.

Tips and Tricks for Success

  • Use a Good Pan: A heavy-bottomed skillet, such as cast iron, distributes heat more evenly, which is crucial for achieving that perfect crust.
  • Customize Your Toppings: Feel free to swap in your favorite Mediterranean ingredients like artichoke hearts, sun-dried tomatoes, or grilled zucchini.
  • Gluten-Free Alternative: Use a pre-made gluten-free pizza dough or a mix if you’re catering to dietary restrictions.
  • Watch the Heat: If your stovetop runs hot, keep the flame low to avoid burning the crust while allowing the toppings to cook properly.

Final Thoughts

Greek-style stovetop pizza is a quick, versatile, and utterly delicious dish that brings the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean right into your kitchen. It’s proof that you don’t need a fancy oven or a pizza stone to create something extraordinary. With its crispy crust, tangy sauce, and medley of fresh toppings, this pizza is a celebration of bold flavors and simple ingredients.

Why wait? Set aside some time this week to give it a try—you might just discover your new favorite way to enjoy pizza. Happy cooking!
