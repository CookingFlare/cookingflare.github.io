Crispy Comfort: Homemade Chicken Tenders Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can compete with the crispy, golden allure of homemade chicken tenders. Whether you’re serving them for a weeknight dinner, a party, or simply indulging in a late-night snack, these chicken tenders are sure to satisfy. This blog post will walk you through the entire process of creating perfectly crispy chicken tenders from scratch, discussing the ingredients, techniques, and tips for achieving that ultimate crunch.

The Allure of Homemade Chicken Tenders

Homemade chicken tenders are not just a meal; they’re an experience. The joy of biting into a crunchy exterior, followed by the tender juiciness of the chicken inside, is unmatched. Unlike store-bought versions that may contain preservatives and artificial flavors, making your chicken tenders at home allows you to control every ingredient. Plus, they’re incredibly versatile—enjoy them with your favorite dipping sauce, add them to salads, or serve them alongside a hearty portion of fries.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create the crispiest chicken tenders, you’ll need a handful of simple ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to gather:

For the Chicken Tenders:

  • 1 lb chicken breast (boneless, skinless)
  • 1 cup buttermilk (or milk with a tablespoon of vinegar to make your own)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Breading:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko works best for extra crunch)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (for a subtle smoky flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs (beaten)

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Preparing the Chicken

Step 1: Slice the Chicken

Start by cutting the chicken breasts into strips. Aim for uniform thickness to ensure even cooking. Typically, each breast can yield about 4–5 tenders depending on its size.

Step 2: Marinate in Buttermilk

In a bowl, combine the chicken strips with buttermilk, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Make sure all the chicken pieces are well-coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or up to overnight. This step not only infuses flavor but also helps to tenderize the meat.

The Breading Process

Step 3: Set Up Your Breading Station

While the chicken is marinating, set up your breading station. In three shallow dishes, prepare the following:

  1. Flour Mixture: In the first dish, combine the flour, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  2. Egg Wash: In the second dish, beat the eggs until smooth.
  3. Breadcrumb Mixture: In the third dish, mix the breadcrumbs with cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.

Step 4: Bread the Chicken

Once the chicken is done marinating, remove it from the refrigerator. Take each piece of chicken and first dip it into the flour mixture, ensuring it’s well-coated. Shake off any excess flour, then dip it into the egg wash, and finally coat it with the breadcrumb mixture. Press the breadcrumbs onto the chicken to ensure a good coating. Place the breaded tenders on a plate and let them sit for about 10 minutes; this helps the breading to adhere better during frying.

Frying the Chicken Tenders

Step 5: Heat the Oil

In a large skillet or frying pan, pour enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom by about 1/2 inch. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). You can test if the oil is ready by dropping a small piece of bread in; if it sizzles and bubbles, you’re good to go.

Step 6: Fry the Tenders

Carefully add a few chicken tenders to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 3–4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (75°C). Use a slotted spoon to remove the tenders and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

Step 7: Repeat

Continue frying the remaining chicken tenders in batches, ensuring the oil temperature remains consistent. If the oil starts to smoke, lower the heat slightly.

Final Touches and Serving Suggestions

Once all the chicken tenders are fried to perfection, it’s time to serve! You can sprinkle a little more salt over the hot tenders for added flavor.

Dipping Sauces

Homemade chicken tenders are best enjoyed with a variety of dipping sauces. Here are a few ideas to elevate your meal:

  • Classic Honey Mustard: Mix equal parts honey and Dijon mustard for a sweet and tangy dip.
  • Barbecue Sauce: A smoky barbecue sauce pairs beautifully with the crispy tenders.
  • Ranch Dressing: Creamy ranch is a classic choice that complements the crunch of the chicken.
  • Spicy Sriracha Mayo: Combine mayonnaise with sriracha for a spicy kick.

Side Dish Pairings

Consider serving your chicken tenders with sides that complement their flavor and texture. Some great options include:

  • French Fries or Sweet Potato Fries: For that classic fast-food feel, serve with crispy fries.
  • Coleslaw: A refreshing coleslaw adds a nice crunch and balances the richness of the tenders.
  • Salads: A simple green salad can lighten up your meal, providing a fresh contrast.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Tenders

  • Don’t Skip the Marinade: Marinating the chicken is crucial for flavor and tenderness.
  • Use Panko Breadcrumbs: For the crispiest texture, opt for panko breadcrumbs instead of regular breadcrumbs.
  • Monitor Oil Temperature: Keep an eye on the oil temperature to avoid greasy tenders or burnt breading.
  • Experiment with Spices: Feel free to get creative! Add your favorite spices to the flour or breadcrumb mixture for a personal touch.

Storing Leftovers

If you find yourself with leftover chicken tenders, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, place them in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 10 minutes to restore their crispiness. Avoid microwaving them, as this can make them soggy.

Conclusion

Homemade chicken tenders are a delightful comfort food that everyone can enjoy. With a few simple ingredients and some easy steps, you can create a dish that’s crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Whether you’re feeding a family, hosting friends, or just treating yourself, this homemade chicken tenders recipe will become a favorite in your kitchen. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in some crispy comfort!
