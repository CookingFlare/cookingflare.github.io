The Perfect Pizza Cheese: From Mozzarella to Burrata


When it comes to making the perfect pizza, one of the most crucial elements is the cheese. The right cheese can elevate your pizza from ordinary to extraordinary, adding a creamy, gooey, and flavorful element that complements the other toppings and crust. In this blog post, we’ll explore the world of pizza cheese, from the classic mozzarella to the indulgent burrata. Get ready to embark on a cheesy journey that will leave your taste buds wanting more!

Mozzarella: The Classic Choice

Mozzarella is the go-to cheese for pizza, and for a good reason. Its mild flavor, smooth texture, and excellent melting properties make it an ideal choice for any pizza style. Made from cow’s milk, mozzarella cheese is traditionally produced using the pasta filata method, which involves stretching the curd to create its characteristic stretchy texture.

When using mozzarella on your pizza, consider using fresh mozzarella for a truly authentic experience. Fresh mozzarella has a higher moisture content and a softer texture, which results in that coveted gooeyness when melted. Tear or slice the mozzarella into small pieces and distribute it evenly on your pizza before baking. As it cooks, the mozzarella will melt, creating a luscious layer of cheese that binds the toppings together.

Provolone: The Flavorful Alternative

If you’re looking to add a bit more flavor to your pizza, provolone cheese is an excellent choice. This semi-hard Italian cheese is known for its sharp and smoky taste, which pairs well with a variety of toppings. Provolone has a higher fat content than mozzarella, which gives it a richer and creamier texture when melted.

To incorporate provolone into your pizza, consider blending it with mozzarella for a perfect balance of flavors. The provolone will add a depth of flavor that enhances the overall taste of the pizza. Grate or thinly slice the provolone and layer it on top of the mozzarella, ensuring even coverage across the entire pizza. The result will be a pizza with a more pronounced and complex cheese profile.

Burrata: The Indulgent Delight

For those looking to take their pizza to the next level of decadence, burrata cheese is an absolute must-try. Burrata is a fresh Italian cheese made from mozzarella and cream, giving it a creamy and buttery texture that is simply irresistible. The outer shell is made of mozzarella, while the inside is filled with a blend of mozzarella and cream.

To incorporate burrata into your pizza, treat it as a topping rather than a base cheese. After your pizza has finished baking, remove it from the oven and carefully place dollops of burrata on top of the cooked pizza. The residual heat will gently melt the creamy center, creating a luxurious and creamy layer that contrasts beautifully with the other ingredients.

Experiment and Enjoy!

Now that you’re armed with knowledge about different pizza cheeses, it’s time to get creative in the kitchen! Feel free to experiment with different combinations of cheeses and toppings to find your perfect pizza. Whether you stick to the classic mozzarella or venture into the realm of provolone or burrata, remember that the key to a delicious pizza lies in using high-quality ingredients and allowing the cheese to shine.

So go ahead, gather your favorite toppings, roll out that dough, and create a pizza masterpiece that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Happy pizza-making!
