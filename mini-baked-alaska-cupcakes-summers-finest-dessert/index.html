Mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes: Summer's Finest Dessert


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in a sweet and delightful treat, and what better way to celebrate the season than with Mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes? These bite-sized masterpieces are the upscale cousin of the classic ice cream sundae, fusing together moist vanilla cupcake bases, rich chocolate ganache, and perfect, frozen mounds of vanilla ice cream. In this post, I’ll walk you through every step needed to create these shimmering, delightful confections right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Before diving into the recipe, let’s first gather the ingredients you’ll need for your Mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes:

For the Vanilla Cupcakes:

  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour

For the Chocolate Ganache:

  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

For the Vanilla Ice Cream:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2/3 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 6 large egg yolks

For the Meringue:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Just like a well-written story, the adventure of making Mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes starts with the perfect beginning. Let’s start with crafting the delectable vanilla cupcakes!

Step 1: Preheat Oven and Prepare Cupcake Pan

First, preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and prepare a cupcake pan by lining it with paper liners. This will make cleaning up a breeze later on and ensure your cupcakes don’t stick.

Step 2: Prepare Vanilla Cupcake Batter

To create the perfect cupcake base, cream together softened unsalted butter and granulated sugar using an electric mixer on a medium speed until smooth and fluffy. Beat in two eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract. Don’t forget to scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed.

Gradually whisk in the dry ingredients (baking powder and salt) before alternating with the addition of the whole milk, being sure to mix until just incorporated. Finally, fold in the flour until fully combined to create a smooth and silky batter.

Step 3: Bake the Cupcakes

Divide the batter evenly between the cupcake liners (about 3/4 full) and bake for approximately 20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Meanwhile, let’s prepare other components for these delightful treats.

Step 4: Prepare Chocolate Ganache

In a small saucepan, heat up the heavy cream over medium heat until it comes to a simmer. Remove from heat and add chopped semi-sweet chocolate to the cream. Whisk together until completely smooth and let it cool to a slightly thick, pourable consistency.

Step 5: Make Vanilla Ice Cream

To prepare the ice cream base, combine the heavy cream, milk, sugar, salt, and egg yolks in a heavy-bottomed saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon. To create a smooth and creamy ice cream, strain the mixture, then chill it in the refrigerator. Once cooled, churn the ice cream according to the manufacturer’s instructions using your ice cream maker.

Step 6: Make Meringue

Whip the egg whites with a pinch of salt until frothy, then gradually add granulated sugar, one tablespoon at a time, while continuing to whip the mix until stiff, glossy peaks form. Gently fold in the vanilla extract until well mixed.

Step 7: Assemble Mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes

Once the cupcakes have cooled, carefully core a small hole in the center of each, large enough to fit a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Fill the hole with the prepared ice cream and set it in the freezer to solidify.

Smother the top of each cupcake with a generous dollop of chocolate ganache, ensuring full, even coverage. Next, generously spoon the prepared meringue over the ganache, frosting the frozen treat completely with a crispy and luscious meringue blanket.

Step 8: Browning the Meringue

To achieve the warm, toasted appearance of a classic Baked Alaska, you can use a kitchen torch to lightly brown the meringue atop each cupcake. This results in a lovely, inviting visual appeal that can make this humble summer dessert look like a finalist in a gourmet dessert competition.

Step 9: Serve and Enjoy

If you’d like a more rustic approach, you can place your Mini Baked Alaskas under the broiler for a moment or two, taking extra caution not to burn them in the process. Finally, let these delightful concoctions stand for a few minutes to ensure the ice cream is solid again, and then, serve and enjoy these perfect mini Baked Alaska Cupcakes.
