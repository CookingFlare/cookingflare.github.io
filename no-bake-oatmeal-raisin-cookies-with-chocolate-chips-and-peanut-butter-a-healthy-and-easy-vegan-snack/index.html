No-Bake Oatmeal Raisin Cookies with Chocolate Chips and Peanut Butter: A Healthy and Easy Vegan Snack

3 min · 492 words

Are you craving a delicious treat but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen? Look no further than these no-bake oatmeal raisin cookies with chocolate chips and peanut butter. Not only are they incredibly easy to make, but they are also vegan and packed with healthy ingredients. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup vegan chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, raisins, peanut butter, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vegan chocolate chips, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

  2. Once the dough is well mixed, cover the bowl and place it in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes. This will help the dough firm up, making it easier to shape the cookies later on.

  3. After the dough has chilled, take it out of the refrigerator and scoop out small portions of the mixture. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it slightly to create a cookie shape. Place the cookies on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Repeat this process until all the dough has been shaped into cookies. If you prefer larger cookies, feel free to adjust the size accordingly.

  5. Once all the cookies are shaped, place the baking sheet back in the refrigerator and let the cookies set for at least 1 hour. This will allow them to firm up and hold their shape.

  6. After the cookies have set, they are ready to be enjoyed. Serve them as a healthy snack or dessert, and watch as everyone falls in love with these delicious treats.

Tips and Variations

  • If you’re not a fan of raisins, feel free to substitute them with dried cranberries or chopped dates. This will give your cookies a slightly different flavor but will still maintain the chewy texture.

  • For an extra crunch, you can add chopped nuts such as almonds, walnuts, or pecans to the dough. Simply mix them in with the other ingredients before shaping the cookies.

  • If you prefer your cookies to have a sweeter taste, you can increase the amount of maple syrup or add a tablespoon of your favorite sweetener, such as agave nectar or honey (if not strictly vegan).

  • If you want to make these cookies gluten-free, make sure to use certified gluten-free oats.

Final Thoughts

These no-bake oatmeal raisin cookies with chocolate chips and peanut butter are a fantastic option for those wanting a healthy and easy vegan snack. They are packed with nutritious ingredients and can be whipped up in no time. Plus, they have all the flavors and textures you expect from a classic oatmeal cookie. So, give this recipe a try and indulge in a guilt-free treat that will satisfy your sweet tooth and keep you energized throughout the day!

Remember, these cookies don’t require any baking, making them perfect for those hot summer days when you want a sweet treat without heating up the kitchen. Enjoy!
