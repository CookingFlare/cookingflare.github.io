How to Make Ice Cream with Oat Milk


If you’ve been craving a luscious, creamy ice cream but want to skip the dairy, oat milk offers an incredible alternative. Its naturally sweet, mild flavor and smooth texture make it a fantastic base for homemade ice cream. In this post, we’ll explore how to make ice cream with oat milk that rivals traditional versions in taste and creaminess—without the need for heavy cream or eggs. Get ready to elevate your dessert game with this easy, satisfying recipe!

Why Choose Oat Milk for Ice Cream?

Oat milk has surged in popularity thanks to its creamy consistency and subtle oat flavor that complements desserts beautifully. Unlike some plant-based milks, oat milk provides a natural creaminess due to its higher carbohydrate content, making it a perfect candidate to create a smooth, rich ice cream that doesn’t feel icy or grainy.

Additionally, oat milk is allergy-friendly, free from nuts, and often fortified with vitamins, making it accessible to many dietary preferences. When paired with the right ingredients, it can transform simple ice cream into an indulgent treat that’s as nutritious as it is delicious.

Essential Ingredients for Oat Milk Ice Cream

Creating your own oat milk ice cream requires just a handful of ingredients. Here’s a rundown of what you’ll need:

  • Oat Milk: The base of your ice cream. Use a barista blend or creamy variety for best results.
  • Sweetener: Maple syrup, agave nectar, or organic cane sugar all work well.
  • Thickening Agent: Cornstarch or arrowroot powder helps achieve that luscious, scoopable consistency.
  • Flavorings: Vanilla extract is classic, but you can get creative with cocoa powder, fruit puree, or spices like cinnamon or cardamom.
  • Salt: Just a pinch to balance sweetness and enhance flavor.
  • Optional Fats: Coconut oil or neutral oil can add extra richness but are not mandatory.

Step-by-Step Guide to Oat Milk Ice Cream

Step 1: Prepare the Oat Milk Base

Start by pouring 2 cups of oat milk into a medium saucepan. Add your chosen sweetener—about ½ cup—to give your ice cream the right level of sweetness. Stir gently to combine.

Next, for thickness and creaminess, whisk 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2-3 tablespoons of oat milk in a small bowl to create a slurry. This prevents lumps when added to the warm milk.

Slowly heat the oat milk and sweetener mixture over medium heat, stirring frequently. Once warm (but not boiling), whisk in the cornstarch slurry. Continue stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon, approximately 5-7 minutes.

Step 2: Add Flavor and Enhance Creaminess

Remove the saucepan from heat. Stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract for that classic flavor boost. If you want to try chocolate oat milk ice cream, add ¼ cup of unsweetened cocoa powder at this stage, whisking until smooth.

For a creamier texture, you might add 2 tablespoons of melted coconut oil or a neutral oil like avocado oil. These fats mimic the mouthfeel of dairy cream and provide extra richness.

Finally, add a pinch of salt to balance the overall sweetness and deepen the flavors.

Step 3: Chill the Mixture Thoroughly

Transfer the custard-like oat milk blend to a bowl, cover it tightly with plastic wrap or a lid, and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours or overnight. The chilling increases the mixture’s density and helps it churn better.

Step 4: Churn and Freeze

Pour the chilled oat milk base into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually about 20-25 minutes. The churning incorporates air and freezes the mixture evenly, creating a smooth texture.

If you don’t have an ice cream maker, don’t worry. Pour the mixture into a shallow container, freeze for about 30 minutes, then vigorously stir or whisk to break up ice crystals. Repeat every 30 minutes for 3-4 cycles until it reaches a creamy consistency.

Step 5: Final Freeze for Perfect Scoop

Transfer the churned ice cream into a freezer-safe container. Cover and freeze for an additional 2-4 hours to firm up to scoopable perfection.

Before serving, let your oat milk ice cream sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften slightly. This makes scooping easier and brings out the flavors.

Creative Variations and Mix-Ins

The basic oat milk ice cream recipe provides a fantastic foundation for culinary creativity. Here are some ideas to customize your batch:

  • Fruit Swirls: Fold in raspberry or strawberry puree after churning for vibrant color and fresh tartness.
  • Chocolate Chips or Chunks: Add dark chocolate chips during the last 5 minutes of churning for delightful texture contrast.
  • Nut Butters: Swirl in peanut, almond, or cashew butter for a nutty richness.
  • Spiced Flavors: Infuse the warm oat milk with cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, or star anise before chilling.
  • Coffee Ice Cream: Stir in brewed espresso or instant coffee granules into the oat milk base for a creamy vegan coffee treat.

Tips for the Perfect Oat Milk Ice Cream

  • Choose creamy oat milk: Barista blends tend to have added fats and a creamier texture than regular oat milk.
  • Don’t skip the thickener: Without eggs or cream, starch like cornstarch ensures your ice cream doesn’t freeze into a hard block.
  • Sweeten thoughtfully: Oat milk has natural sweetness, so adjust the added sugar to your taste. Mapler syrup adds complexity.
  • Avoid overheating: Heat gently to prevent oat milk proteins from breaking down and changing texture.
  • Store properly: Keep your ice cream covered airtight in the freezer to avoid ice crystals and flavor loss.

Why Making Oat Milk Ice Cream at Home Beats Store-Bought

Store-bought vegan ice cream options are improving but can often be pricey, contain emulsifiers and preservatives, or fall short on creaminess and flavor. By making your own oat milk ice cream, you control every ingredient—from sweetness to flavor intensity—ensuring a fresh, wholesome, and indulgent dessert.

Plus, the creative freedom to mix in your favorite nuts, fruits, or spices means every batch can be tailor-made for your cravings or dietary needs.

Summary: A Refreshing Twist on Classic Ice Cream

Making ice cream with oat milk is a wonderful way to enjoy a dairy-free dessert that feels indulgent and satisfying. With natural creaminess, a touch of sweetness, and endless opportunities for flavor customization, oat milk ice cream can quickly become a household favorite.

All it takes is a simple custard base thickened with cornstarch, your preferred sweetener, and some patience to chill and churn. Whether you prefer classic vanilla, rich chocolate, or inventive spiced versions, this versatile recipe delivers rich, smooth ice cream that’s both vegan and utterly delicious.

Enjoy the process, experiment with flavors, and savor the creamy bliss of homemade oat milk ice cream any time the sweet tooth strikes! If you give this recipe a try, feel free to share your creative twists and how yours turned out. There’s something magical about crafting frozen desserts at home—a truly satisfying blend of science, art, and comfort all in one scoop.
