Sous Vide Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Colorful and Flavorful Meal


Are you tired of the same old stuffed bell pepper recipe? Then it’s time to try something new and exciting with sous vide cooking! Sous vide is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method ensures that your food is cooked evenly and retains its flavors and nutrients. In this blog post, we will explore how to make delicious sous vide stuffed bell peppers that are both colorful and flavorful. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 4 bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella works well)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 155°F (68°C).

  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, cooked rice, diced onion, minced garlic, shredded cheese, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  4. Stuff each bell pepper with the ground beef mixture. Make sure to pack it tightly without overstuffing.

  5. Place the stuffed bell peppers into individual vacuum-sealed bags, ensuring they are in a single layer and not overlapping.

  6. Seal the bags using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method. If using the water displacement method, slowly lower the bag into the water bath, allowing the water to push out the air. Once most of the air is removed, seal the bag.

  7. Gently place the sealed bags into the preheated sous vide water bath. Ensure that the bell peppers are fully submerged.

  8. Cook the stuffed bell peppers for 2 hours, maintaining a consistent water temperature throughout the cooking process.

  9. After 2 hours, carefully remove the bags from the water bath. Be cautious as the bags will be hot.

  10. Open the bags and transfer the stuffed bell peppers onto a serving plate. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different fillings for your stuffed bell peppers. You can try using ground turkey or chicken instead of beef for a lighter option. Additionally, you can add other vegetables like corn, black beans, or diced tomatoes to the filling for added flavor and texture.

  • If you prefer a crispy topping, you can broil the stuffed bell peppers in the oven for a few minutes after cooking them sous vide. This will give them a nice golden crust.

  • Feel free to garnish your stuffed bell peppers with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro for an extra burst of freshness.

  • Leftover stuffed bell peppers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat them in the oven or microwave before serving.

Sous vide stuffed bell peppers are a delightful and colorful meal that will impress your family and friends. With the precise cooking technique of sous vide, you can ensure that the flavors are locked in, resulting in a truly delicious dish. So, why not give this recipe a try and elevate your stuffed bell pepper game to a whole new level? Happy cooking!
