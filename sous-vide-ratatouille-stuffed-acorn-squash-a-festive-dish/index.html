Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Acorn Squash: A Festive Dish


Are you looking for a show-stopping dish that combines the flavors of the Mediterranean with the elegance of acorn squash? Look no further than this Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Acorn Squash recipe! Bursting with vibrant colors and bold flavors, this dish is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving more.

Ingredients

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. Place the diced eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, onion, and minced garlic in a large mixing bowl. Season with salt, pepper, dried oregano, and dried basil. Toss to combine.

  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the diced tomatoes and tomato paste until well combined. Pour the tomato mixture over the diced vegetables and toss until evenly coated.

  4. Divide the vegetable mixture evenly among the halved acorn squash, filling each cavity generously. Drizzle olive oil over the stuffed squash.

  5. Place each stuffed acorn squash half in a vacuum-sealable bag, ensuring they are not overlapping. Seal the bags using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  6. Gently lower the bags into the preheated water bath, making sure they are fully submerged. Cook for 2 hours, or until the squash is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  7. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the bags from the water bath and transfer the stuffed acorn squash halves to a serving platter.

  8. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for an added touch of freshness and color. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a sous vide setup, you can still enjoy this dish by roasting the stuffed acorn squash halves in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 45-50 minutes, or until the squash is tender.

  • Feel free to customize the ratatouille filling with your favorite vegetables. You can add mushrooms, artichoke hearts, or even roasted red peppers for added depth of flavor.

  • For a vegan version, simply omit the eggplant and replace it with mushrooms or your preferred vegetable substitute.

  • This dish pairs beautifully with a side of couscous or quinoa for a complete and satisfying meal.

Make your next gathering a memorable one with this Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Acorn Squash recipe. The tender and flavorful acorn squash, combined with the robust and aromatic ratatouille filling, will transport your taste buds to the Mediterranean with every bite. Enjoy the festive flavors and the colorful presentation of this delightful dish!
