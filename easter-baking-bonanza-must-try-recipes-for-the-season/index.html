Easter Baking Bonanza: Must-Try Recipes for the Season


Welcome to our Easter Baking Bonanza! Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and of course, delicious food. This year, why not add some extra sweetness to your festivities with these must-try Easter baking recipes? From traditional favorites to creative twists, we’ve got you covered with a variety of treats that are sure to impress your loved ones. So, put on your apron, preheat the oven, and let’s get baking!

Hot Cross Buns

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/4 oz. package active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup candied cherries, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine 2 cups of flour, sugar, and yeast. Mix well.
  2. Add warm milk and butter to the flour mixture. Stir until smooth.
  3. Beat in the egg, salt, and remaining flour to form a soft dough.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 6-8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Gently fold in the raisins and candied cherries.
  6. Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
  7. Punch down the dough and shape it into small buns.
  8. Place the buns on a baking sheet, cover, and let rise again.
  9. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  10. Bake the buns for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  11. Remove from the oven and let cool. Enjoy warm with butter.

Carrot Cake Cupcakes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a cupcake pan with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined.
  5. Fold in grated carrots, pineapple, and walnuts.
  6. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  8. Let the cupcakes cool completely before frosting.

Lemon Blueberry Scones

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, cream, vanilla, and lemon zest.
  5. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined.
  6. Carefully fold in the blueberries.
  7. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and shape it into a circle.
  8. Cut the circle into 8 wedges and place them on the baking sheet.
  9. Bake for 18-20 minutes until golden brown.
  10. Let the scones cool slightly before serving.

Conclusion

Easter is the perfect time to indulge in some delightful baking adventures. Whether you prefer classic treats like hot cross buns, modern delights like carrot cake cupcakes, or tangy scones like lemon blueberry, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, gather your ingredients, embrace your inner baker, and delight your taste buds with these mouthwatering Easter recipes. Happy baking, and may your Easter be filled with joy and sweetness!
