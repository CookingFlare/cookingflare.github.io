Traditional Southern Chicken and Sausage Gumbo Recipe


If you’re looking for a taste of the deep south, then look no further than this traditional Southern chicken and sausage gumbo recipe. Gumbo is a staple in Southern cuisine, known for its rich flavors and comforting warmth. This recipe brings together tender chicken, savory sausage, and a medley of spices to create a hearty, satisfying dish that will transport you to the heart of Louisiana with each spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, diced
  • 1 pound Andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Roux: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Gradually whisk in the flour to create a roux. Stir the roux constantly for 20-25 minutes or until it reaches a dark brown color. Be careful not to burn it.

  2. Add the Holy Trinity: Once the roux is ready, add the diced onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic to the pot. Cook, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Incorporate the Meats: Add the diced chicken and sliced Andouille sausage to the pot. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink and the sausage is browned.

  4. Spice it Up: Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, bay leaves, thyme, oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Remove the bay leaves from the gumbo. Serve the gumbo over a bed of cooked white rice and garnish with chopped green onions.

Tips and Tricks

  • Patience is Key: The key to a flavorful gumbo is the roux. Take your time and be patient while stirring the roux to achieve that rich, deep color and flavor.

  • Make it Your Own: Feel free to customize the gumbo by adding shrimp, crab, or okra for a unique twist on this classic dish.

  • Let it Rest: Gumbo tastes even better the next day as the flavors have time to meld together. It’s a perfect make-ahead dish for gatherings or meal prep.

Final Thoughts

This traditional Southern chicken and sausage gumbo recipe is a labor of love that rewards you with a bowl full of soul-warming goodness. The combination of tender chicken, smoky Andouille sausage, and aromatic spices creates a dish that’s rich in flavor and history. Whether you’re a seasoned gumbo enthusiast or new to Southern cooking, this recipe is sure to become a beloved addition to your culinary repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the spirit of the South, and get ready to savor every spoonful of this comforting gumbo.
