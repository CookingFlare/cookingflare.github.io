Savor the Flavor: BBQ Pork Spare Ribs


BBQ Pork Spare Ribs - it just sounds amazing, doesn’t it? That smoky, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor, the tenderness of the meat, and the overall mouthwatering appeal make this dish irresistible. So, let’s delve right into making those delectable BBQ Pork Spare Ribs at home!

What are Pork Spare Ribs?

In the world of BBQ, pork spare ribs are a popular choice. They come from the ‘ porky ’ portion of a pig, specifically the belly, which is situated above the hog’s hind legs. These fatty ribs are phenomenal, and the marbling of the meat adds a layer of juiciness to the ribs.

Preparing the Pork Spare Ribs

Part of the magic of BBQ Pork Spare Ribs is the preparation. It includes several steps that are integral to locked-in flavor, making them fall-off-the-bone tender. Here’s a simple and scrumptious recipe to get you started:

Ingredients

  • 2 slabs of pork spare ribs
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup liquid smoke
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons onion powder
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Meat tenderizer
  • Your favorite BBQ rub (optional)

Instructions

  1. Begin by purchasing a quality cut of pork spare ribs from your local butcher. The ribs should ideally weigh about 2 to 3 pounds.

  2. Rinse the ribs under cold water, pat them dry, and place them on a clean, clean work surface.

  3. Use a sharp knife to separate the ribs from the slab. One slab is usually enough for a large family meal and leaves nice, neat portions to take for leftovers.

  4. Once you’ve separated the ribs, trim the silverskin layer - a thin, tough membrane that covers the underside of the ribs - using a sharp knife or a paper knife. It’s essential to remove this layer to make the ribs more tender.

  5. Apply a generous amount of meat tenderizer over both sides of the ribs, ensuring that each surface is well-covered.

  6. Place the ribs back in the fridge for at least an hour or up to 12 hours to fully absorb the meat tenderizer. This step will contribute to the delicious tenderness of the ribs.

  7. When you’re ready to cook, preheat the oven to 325°F. You’ll finish the ribs in a barbecue or on a grill, but starting them in the oven will save you time and energy.

  8. Place the ribs on a wire rack set inside a rimmed baking sheet. Pour a mixture of 1 cup of apple cider vinegar, half a cup of liquid smoke, half a cup of soy sauce, a half cup of honey, two tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce, two tables of paprika, two tablespoons of garlic powder, two tablespoons of onion powder, two tablespoons of chili powder, one tablespoon ground cumin, and a teaspoon of black pepper over the ribs.

  9. Cover the ribs with an aluminum foil “tent” and bake for about two hours or until the ribs are tender and nearly done.

  10. Remove the ribs from the oven, unwrap, and drain off any liquid.

  11. Light your barbecue or preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  12. At the barbecue or grill, generously apply your favorite BBQ rub to both sides of the ribs. ribs can be cooked with indirect heat for about an hour or until the ribs are ready.

  13. It’s time to glaze the ribs. Brush or mop any BBQ sauce or mixture of your choice on the ribs. Repeat the process every 15-20 minutes until the ribs are done.

  14. Your BBQ Pork Spare Ribs are ready when they are tender and pull away easily from the bone.

Final Thoughts

Making BBQ Pork Spare Ribs at home is an art that, once mastered, will have you rejoicing over barbecues, especially during the summertime. By following this recipe, your taste buds are in for a treat; the mixture of flavors proves unbeatable every time! Just be ready to savour the flavor and indulge in some finger-licking good BBQ Pork Spare Ribs.
