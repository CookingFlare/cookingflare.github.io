How to Make Seafood Paella with Chicken


Paella is more than just a dish; it’s an experience that brings together the vibrant flavors of the sea and the earthy satisfaction of tender chicken. Blending succulent seafood with juicy chicken pieces creates a remarkable fusion that is both hearty and fresh, making it a perfect centerpiece for any gathering. Dive into this comprehensive guide on how to make seafood paella with chicken, and discover the techniques and tips that will elevate your Spanish cooking game to a whole new level.

Origins and Essence of Paella

Before we jump into the recipe, it’s interesting to understand that paella originates from the Valencia region in Spain. Traditionally, it was a countryside dish, cooked over an open fire and popular among farmworkers. The beauty of paella lies in its diversity; various regions have their own versions using local ingredients. Combining chicken and seafood in paella offers a delightful contrast—the chicken imparts rich depth, while the seafood provides a burst of oceanic freshness.

Essential Ingredients for Perfect Seafood Paella with Chicken

To achieve the authentic taste, selecting the right ingredients is crucial. Here’s a list of what you will need:

  • Rice: Use short-grain or Spanish bomba rice, which absorbs liquid well without becoming mushy.
  • Chicken: Thighs and drumsticks work best for their flavor and tenderness.
  • Seafood: A combination of shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid creates complexity.
  • Vegetables: Bell peppers, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and peas add sweetness and texture.
  • Stock: Homemade or quality seafood and chicken broth are fundamental.
  • Spices: Saffron is the star, providing color and subtle aroma. Smoked paprika and black pepper enhance depth.
  • Olive oil: For sautéing and building flavor.
  • Lemon wedges: To brighten the finished dish.

Step-By-Step Guide to Making Seafood Paella with Chicken

1. Prepare the Broth

Start by preparing a flavorful broth. In a large pot, simmer fish bones or shells with chicken bones, some onion, garlic, parsley, and a bay leaf for about 30 minutes. Strain the broth and keep it warm. This combination infuses your paella with layers of savory richness.

2. Marinate the Chicken

Marinate chicken pieces in a mix of olive oil, smoked paprika, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight. This process tenderizes the meat and imbues it with smoky, aromatic notes.

3. Sauté the Chicken and Vegetables

Heat olive oil in a wide, shallow paella pan or a large skillet. Brown the marinated chicken on all sides until golden. Remove and set aside. Sauté chopped onions, bell peppers, and garlic until softened. Add diced tomatoes and cook until the mixture turns into a rich sofrito that forms the flavor base.

4. Toast the Rice

Add rice to the pan, stirring constantly with the sofrito for two to three minutes. This helps the rice absorb all the vibrant flavors. The grains will start to look translucent around the edges.

5. Add Saffron and Broth

Pour in the warm broth infused with saffron threads. Stir everything just once to distribute the rice evenly. Return the chicken pieces to the pan, nestling them among the rice.

6. Simmer Without Stirring

Here’s the key: simmer the paella gently and do not stir. Paella’s magic comes from the socarrat — the crispy rice crust that develops on the bottom. Let the rice absorb all the broth, cooking for about 15 minutes on medium heat.

7. Add Seafood

Arrange shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid over the partially cooked rice. Cover the pan loosely with foil or a lid and cook for another 10 minutes until the seafood is just cooked and the mussels and clams have opened.

8. Final Touches

Turn off the heat and let the paella rest for five minutes, allowing the flavors to settle. Sprinkle fresh chopped parsley and squeeze lemon wedges generously over the dish.

Tips to Nail Your Paella Game

  • Use the right pan: A traditional paella pan is flat and wide, ensuring even cooking and proper socarrat formation.
  • Rice choice matters: Short-grain rice is necessary for absorbing liquid without losing texture.
  • Patience with broth: Homemade or carefully prepared stock intensifies the dish’s depth.
  • Avoid stirring after adding broth: Stirring disrupts the socarrat, which is a hallmark of authentic paella.
  • Saffron quality: Authentic Spanish saffron gives the unique aroma and golden hue; avoid substitutes when possible.
  • Fresh seafood: Use fresh or properly thawed seafood for the best taste and texture.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

Paella’s versatility allows for many exciting variations. You can add chorizo for a smoky kick or substitute chicken with rabbit for a traditional Valencian touch. Adjust the seafood mix depending on availability—lobster, crab, or scallops make luxurious alternatives.

Serve your seafood paella with crusty bread and a chilled white wine such as Albariño or a dry rosé. For an authentic setting, accompany the meal with a light Spanish salad topped with Manchego cheese and olives.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have leftovers, paella reheats beautifully. Store in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to two days. When reheating, sprinkle some water or broth to rehydrate the rice, cover loosely, and warm it gently in the oven or over low heat. Avoid microwave reheating unless you add moisture, as paella can dry out quickly.

Conclusion

Mastering seafood paella with chicken is a joyous culinary adventure that blends tradition, technique, and taste. Each bite offers a symphony of smoky, savory chicken paired with the briny delight of seafood, all nestled in perfectly cooked saffron-infused rice. Whether it’s a festive celebration or a relaxed weekend feast, this colorful dish invites sharing, conversation, and savoring. Gather your ingredients, embrace the process, and enjoy the rich tapestry of flavors that this Spanish masterpiece delivers.

Enjoy your cooking journey and the delicious moments that follow!
