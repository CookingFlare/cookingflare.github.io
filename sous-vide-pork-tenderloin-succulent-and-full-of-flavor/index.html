Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin: Succulent and Full of Flavor


If you’re looking for a foolproof way to achieve perfectly tender and flavorful pork tenderloin every time, then sous vide cooking is the technique for you. Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, involves cooking food in a precisely controlled water bath at a consistent low temperature. This method ensures that the pork tenderloin cooks evenly from edge to edge, resulting in an incredibly succulent and juicy texture that’s hard to achieve through traditional cooking methods. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the simple steps to create a mouthwatering sous vide pork tenderloin that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Tenderloin

  1. Start by preparing the pork tenderloin. Trim any excess fat and silver skin from the tenderloins, then pat them dry with paper towels.

Step 2: Seasoning the Pork

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Rub the pork tenderloins with the olive oil, then generously coat them with the herb and garlic mixture, ensuring that the tenderloins are evenly seasoned.

Step 3: Vacuum Sealing

  1. Place each seasoned pork tenderloin into a separate vacuum-sealable bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a zip-top bag and remove the air using the water displacement method.

Step 4: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 140°F (60°C) for a perfectly juicy and tender pork tenderloin. If you prefer your pork cooked to a different temperature, adjust accordingly.
  2. Once the water bath reaches the desired temperature, carefully submerge the sealed pork tenderloin bags into the water. Ensure that the pork is fully submerged.
  3. Cook the pork tenderloins in the sous vide water bath for 1.5 to 2 hours. This precise cooking time will allow the pork to reach the ideal level of doneness while retaining its natural juices and flavors.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

  1. After the sous vide cooking time is complete, carefully remove the pork tenderloin bags from the water bath.
  2. Gently pat the pork tenderloins dry with paper towels and remove them from the bags.
  3. Heat a skillet over high heat and add a small amount of olive oil. Sear the pork tenderloins on all sides for a minute or two until they develop a golden brown crust.

Step 6: Serving

  1. Allow the pork tenderloins to rest for a few minutes before slicing them into medallions.
  2. Serve the succulent sous vide pork tenderloin alongside your favorite sides, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a crisp salad.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork tenderloin is a culinary delight that will elevate your cooking to new heights. The precise temperature control and extended cooking time result in a pork tenderloin that’s not only succulent and full of flavor but also consistently perfect. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a gourmet meal at home, this sous vide pork tenderloin recipe is sure to impress. Experiment with different herbs and seasonings to customize the flavor to your liking, and enjoy the tender, juicy results of sous vide cooking. Cheers to creating memorable dining experiences with this delectable sous vide pork tenderloin recipe!
