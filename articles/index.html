Lobster Bisque with Cauliflower Purée: A Luxurious Start Ingredients: 1 whole lobster (1.5 lbs) 1 tbsp olive oil 1 small onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup dry white wine 1 tsp tomato paste 1/2 cup heavy cream 1/2 cup lobster or fish stock Salt and pepper, to taste 1 serving Cauliflower Purée (recipe follows) Directions: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Wrap the lobster in foil and bake for 45 minutes, then let it cool. Crack and remove the meat, reserving the shell for the bisque. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until translucent. Deglaze with the wine and simmer for 2 minutes. Add the lobster chunks, tomato paste, and reserved lobster shell. Stir and cook for 5 minutes. Cover the ingredients with water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 45 minutes. Strain the bisque, discarding the solids. Season with salt and pepper. Blend using an immersion blender until smooth. Whisk in the heavy cream and lobster stock. Reheat the bisque and serve with a side of Cauliflower Purée. Cauliflower Purée Ingredients: 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets 4 tbsp butter 1/2 cup heavy cream Salt and pepper, to taste Directions: Boil cauliflower florets in salted water for 20 minutes, or until tender. Drain the florets, reserving 1/2 cup of the cooking liquid. In a blender, combine the cauliflower, butter, heavy cream, and cooking liquid. Blend until smooth, adjusting seasoning as needed. 2. Twist on Steak au Poivre: Creamy Mushroom Delight Ingredients: 2 ribeye steaks (12 oz each) 2 tbsp fresh coarse black pepper 2 tbsp butter 8 oz sliced mushrooms (shiitake, cremini, or oyster) 2 tbsp cognac or brandy 1/2 cup heavy cream Salt, to taste Directions: Season steaks with pepper, pressing the seasoning into the meat surface. In a large skillet, melt 1 tbsp butter over high heat. Sear the steaks for 2-3 minutes per side for rare to medium-rare doneness. Remove from the pan and keep warm. Lower the heat and melt the remaining butter. Add mushrooms, sautéing until golden. Add cognac, allowing it to reduce by half. Stir in heavy cream, salt, and steaks. Cook until the sauce thickens slightly, about 3 minutes. Plate the steaks and pour the mushroom sauce over them to serve. 3. Fluffy Chocolate Mousse with Raspberry Coulis: A Decadent Dessert Ingredients: 8 oz dark chocolate (70-85% cocoa), chopped 4 egg yolks 1 cup heavy cream, divided 1/4 cup granulated sweetener or erythritol Mixed raspberries Directions for Raspberry Coulis: In a food processor, blend the raspberries and strain the resulting pulp through a sieve to remove seeds. Set aside. In a small saucepan, bring the strained raspberry puree to a simmer and add 2 tbsp of sweetener. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring constantly. Set aside to cool. Directions for Chocolate Mousse: Melt chocolate in a heatproof bowl over a simmering pot of water, stirring until smooth. Remove from heat. In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks and 1/4 cup of sweetener in a heatproof bowl. Stirring constantly, temper the yolks by adding a little hot water while whisking. Reheat the tempered yolks over the pot of water, stirring for 2 minutes, until thickened. Remove from heat and immediately add the melted chocolate, stirring slowly. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture gently. Spoon the mousse into individual serving glasses and chill for at least 2 hours or up to 2 days. Before serving, drizzle with a tablespoon of Raspberry Coulis. Spicy Keto Buffalo Chicken Cauliflower Casserole Recipe

Are you looking for a mouth-watering, spicy, and satisfying casserole recipe that’s perfect for a keto diet? Look no further! Today, we’re going to share a unique and delicious Spicy Keto Buffalo Chicken Cauliflower Casserole recipe that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Ingredients 1 head of cauliflower, broken into florets 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces 1/2 cup buffalo wing sauce 1/4 cup ranch dressing 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon onion powder 1/2 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper, to taste 2 tablespoons olive oil Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, toss the cauliflower florets with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper until they’re evenly coated. Spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re tender and lightly browned. While the cauliflower is roasting, cook the chicken in a large skillet over medium-high heat, adding the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the buffalo wing sauce and bring it to a simmer. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, buffalo wing sauce, and ranch dressing. Stir until the chicken is well coated with the sauce. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, create a layer of the roasted cauliflower. Top it with a layer of the chicken mixture, followed by a sprinkle of shredded cheddar cheese and chopped cilantro. Repeat the layering process one more time, finishing with a layer of cheese on top. Sprinkle the paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder over the top of the cheese. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the top is lightly browned. Tips and Variations To make this recipe more spicy, add more buffalo wing sauce or use hot sauce like Frank’s RedHot. If you prefer a milder flavor, use less buffalo wing sauce or substitute it with a milder sauce like teriyaki. To make this recipe more substantial, add some chopped bacon or diced onions to the chicken mixture.
