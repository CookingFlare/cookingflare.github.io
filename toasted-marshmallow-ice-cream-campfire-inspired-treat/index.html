Toasted Marshmallow Ice Cream: Campfire-Inspired Treat


There’s something magical about the taste of toasted marshmallows over a campfire. The gooey, caramelized exterior with the soft, fluffy interior creates a flavor that instantly transports you to summer nights under the stars. Now, imagine capturing that nostalgic taste in a cool, creamy treat – Toasted Marshmallow Ice Cream. This recipe combines the smoky sweetness of toasted marshmallows with the luxurious creaminess of homemade ice cream for a campfire-inspired dessert that will delight your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup marshmallows
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Toasting the Marshmallows:

    • Preheat your broiler to high. Place the marshmallows on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Place the baking sheet under the broiler for 1-2 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden brown and toasted. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning. Once toasted, remove from the oven and let them cool.

  2. Preparing the Ice Cream Base:

    • In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and half of the sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a gentle simmer. Remove from the heat.

  3. Infusing the Marshmallow Flavor:

    • Add the toasted marshmallows to the warm cream mixture, stirring gently to incorporate. Allow the mixture to sit for 15-20 minutes, allowing the marshmallows to infuse their flavor into the cream. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the marshmallow pieces, pressing down to extract as much flavor as possible.

  4. Tempering the Eggs:

    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and the remaining sugar until light and creamy. Slowly pour the warm marshmallow-infused cream into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. This process will temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling when heated.

  5. Cooking the Custard:

    • Transfer the combined mixture back to the saucepan and place it over medium heat. Stir the mixture constantly, making sure to scrape the bottom and sides of the pan, until it thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon. Do not let it boil. Remove from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.

  6. Chilling the Custard:

    • Pour the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any cooked egg bits. Cover the surface of the custard with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to develop.

  7. Churning the Ice Cream:

    • Once the custard is thoroughly chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  8. Freezing the Ice Cream:

    • Transfer the churned ice cream into a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Cover the surface with parchment paper before sealing with the lid to prevent ice crystals from forming. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

  9. Serving:

    • When ready to serve, scoop the Toasted Marshmallow Ice Cream into bowls or cones. For an extra touch of campfire nostalgia, you can sprinkle a few crushed graham crackers over the ice cream as a garnish.

The result is a velvety smooth ice cream with a subtle smokiness and a hint of sweetness that will transport you back to those cherished moments around the campfire. Whether enjoyed on a warm summer evening or as a delightful treat any time of year, this Toasted Marshmallow Ice Cream is sure to become a beloved favorite for all ages.

So, gather around the “campfire” (or stovetop) and embark on a culinary adventure that captures the essence of summer in every spoonful. Cheers to creating new memories while savoring the flavors of days gone by!
