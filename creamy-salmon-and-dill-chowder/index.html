Creamy Salmon and Dill Chowder


If you’re a fan of rich, comforting soups with layers of flavor, then this creamy salmon and dill chowder is the perfect dish to add to your recipe rotation. This chowder is an ideal blend of tender salmon, aromatic dill, and a creamy broth that brings together a variety of ingredients in a way that is both hearty and satisfying. Whether you’re preparing it for a cozy dinner on a chilly evening or serving it as a starter at your next gathering, this chowder is sure to impress. So, let’s dive into how to make this delightful dish from scratch!

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • Rich and Creamy: The base of the chowder is rich and velvety, with a smooth texture that comes from the addition of cream and butter. It’s the kind of chowder that feels indulgent yet comforting at the same time.
  • Flavorsome Salmon: The flaky salmon chunks add a satisfying protein that infuses the soup with its natural oils and flavor. When combined with the fresh dill, it creates an irresistible depth of flavor.
  • Versatile: This chowder can be customized in various ways to suit your taste preferences or dietary needs. You can make it as rich or as light as you like, and you can even swap out the salmon for another fish or protein if desired.
  • Perfect for Any Season: While chowder is typically associated with colder months, the balance of flavors in this dish makes it great year-round. It works wonderfully in the spring and summer as a light but filling dinner, and it’s also an excellent choice for a winter meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a truly great creamy salmon and dill chowder, you’ll need a combination of fresh ingredients, pantry staples, and a few seasonings. Here’s a breakdown of everything you’ll need:

For the Chowder Base:

  • Salmon – 1 lb (about 450 grams) of fresh, skinless salmon fillets, cut into bite-sized chunks. You can use fresh or frozen salmon, just be sure to defrost the frozen fish thoroughly before using it.
  • Butter – 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter. The butter provides richness and helps sauté the aromatics at the beginning of the recipe.
  • Olive Oil – 1 tablespoon of olive oil for sautéing the vegetables.
  • Yellow Onion – 1 medium onion, finely diced. Onion forms the base of most soups and adds a nice savory flavor.
  • Celery – 2 stalks of celery, chopped. Celery adds both flavor and crunch to the soup.
  • Carrots – 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced. Carrots lend a slight sweetness that balances the savory notes.
  • Potatoes – 3 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced. Potatoes are a classic chowder ingredient and help thicken the soup while also adding heartiness.
  • Garlic – 3 cloves of garlic, minced. Garlic provides an aromatic base and deepens the flavor profile.
  • Dill – 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, chopped, plus more for garnish. Dill is the star of this chowder, bringing a fresh, herby kick that pairs perfectly with the salmon.

For the Broth:

  • Chicken Broth – 4 cups of low-sodium chicken broth. This will form the foundation of the soup and is a great way to add depth without overpowering the salmon.
  • Heavy Cream – 1 cup of heavy cream. The cream transforms the broth into a silky, indulgent texture.
  • Milk – 1 cup of whole milk. For a slightly lighter consistency compared to using all cream, the milk provides balance.
  • Flour – 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour. Flour helps thicken the chowder and gives it a velvety, smooth finish.

Seasonings:

  • Salt and Pepper – To taste. These will help bring out the natural flavors of the ingredients.
  • Lemon Juice – 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice. A squeeze of lemon brightens up the chowder and complements the salmon’s richness.
  • Bay Leaves – 1 or 2 bay leaves. Bay leaves infuse the soup with a subtle, earthy aroma as it simmers.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you’ve gathered all your ingredients, it’s time to get cooking. Follow these steps to create the perfect creamy salmon and dill chowder.

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Before you begin cooking, it’s important to prep all your ingredients. Start by dicing the onion, celery, and carrots. Then peel and dice the potatoes into bite-sized pieces. Mince the garlic and chop the fresh dill. Cut the salmon into chunks, removing any skin or bones. Set everything aside and get ready to start cooking!

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large soup pot, heat the olive oil and butter over medium heat. Once the butter has melted and starts to bubble, add the diced onion, celery, and carrots. Sauté the vegetables for about 5 minutes, or until they begin to soften. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

Step 3: Add the Potatoes and Broth

Once the vegetables are softened, add the diced potatoes to the pot, followed by the chicken broth and bay leaves. Stir to combine, then increase the heat to bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

Step 4: Thicken the Soup

In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and milk until smooth. Slowly add this mixture to the soup while stirring continuously. This will help thicken the chowder and give it that creamy texture. Continue to cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the soup has thickened slightly.

Step 5: Add the Salmon and Cream

Next, gently add the salmon chunks to the soup. Stir to combine and ensure the salmon is evenly distributed. Allow the soup to simmer for another 5-7 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. Be careful not to overcook the salmon, as it can become dry.

Once the salmon is cooked, add the heavy cream and stir until fully incorporated. Let the soup simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 6: Season and Add Dill

At this point, it’s time to season the chowder. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and a splash of lemon juice. The lemon juice will brighten the flavors and enhance the freshness of the dill. Finally, stir in the fresh dill, saving a little for garnish.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Your creamy salmon and dill chowder is now ready to serve! Ladle the chowder into bowls and garnish with a sprinkle of fresh dill. You can also add a slice of crusty bread on the side for dipping. Enjoy this rich and flavorful chowder as a comforting meal that’s sure to satisfy your cravings.

Tips for the Perfect Chowder

  • Use Fresh Salmon: Fresh salmon works best for this chowder as it has a delicate flavor and flaky texture. If you use frozen salmon, be sure to thaw it properly before adding it to the soup.
  • Add Extra Veggies: If you want to bulk up the chowder with more vegetables, consider adding sweet corn, leeks, or spinach. Just be sure to adjust the cooking time to ensure everything is tender.
  • Make It Lighter: For a lighter version of this chowder, use half-and-half or even low-fat milk instead of heavy cream. You can also use olive oil instead of butter to reduce the richness.
  • Adjust the Thickness: If you prefer a thinner chowder, simply reduce the amount of flour or increase the broth. For a thicker chowder, add more potatoes or let it simmer longer.
  • Freezing Leftovers: This chowder freezes well, so feel free to make a big batch and store the leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Just be aware that the texture may change slightly upon reheating, but the flavor will still be fantastic.

Variations to Try

1. Smoked Salmon Chowder

For a smoky twist, try using smoked salmon in place of fresh salmon. Smoked salmon adds an extra layer of flavor that pairs wonderfully with the creamy broth and dill.

2. Vegetarian Dill Chowder

If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, omit the salmon and substitute with hearty vegetables like mushrooms or zucchini. You can also add tofu or tempeh for extra protein.

3. Seafood Chowder

For a seafood lover’s version, combine salmon with other seafood like shrimp, scallops, or cod. The combination of different seafood adds depth and complexity to the chowder.

4. Spicy Salmon Chowder

If you like a bit of heat, try adding a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the soup. The heat complements the richness of the cream and the freshness of the dill.

Conclusion

This creamy salmon and dill chowder is the ultimate comfort food. It’s rich, flavorful, and brimming with fresh ingredients that make each spoonful a treat. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is simple enough to whip up on a weeknight but impressive enough to serve at a dinner party. With its creamy texture, tender salmon, and refreshing dill, this chowder is sure to become a go-to dish in your culinary repertoire.

So, the next time you’re craving something comforting and delicious, give this creamy salmon and dill chowder a try—you won’t be disappointed!
