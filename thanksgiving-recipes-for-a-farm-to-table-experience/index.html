Thanksgiving Recipes for a Farm-to-Table Experience


As Thanksgiving approaches, there’s a delightful anticipation in the air for a festive gathering filled with warmth and delicious food. This year, why not elevate your Thanksgiving experience with a farm-to-table approach? By incorporating fresh, locally sourced ingredients into your recipes, you can create dishes that not only taste amazing but also support local farmers and promote sustainability. To help you craft a Thanksgiving menu that celebrates the best of the season, here are some farm-to-table recipes that will wow your guests:

Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash, onion, carrots, and garlic with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 30-35 minutes until tender.
  3. In a large pot, combine the roasted vegetables, vegetable broth, and thyme. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth.
  5. Season with additional salt and pepper if needed. Serve hot with a drizzle of olive oil.

Herb-Roasted Turkey

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 3 hours | Total Time: 3 hours 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme, sage)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 onions, quartered
  • 4 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 2 lemons, halved

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Rinse the turkey inside and out; pat dry with paper towels.
  3. Mix the softened butter with the chopped herbs, salt, and pepper. Rub the herb butter under the skin of the turkey.
  4. Stuff the cavity of the turkey with onions, garlic, and lemons.
  5. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a roasting pan. Roast for about 13 minutes per pound, basting occasionally with pan juices.
  6. The turkey is done when a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the thigh reads 165°F (75°C).
  7. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving. Serve with pan drippings.

Cranberry Pecan Brussels Sprouts

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a baking sheet, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  3. Add the dried cranberries and chopped pecans to the baking sheet. Roast for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic glaze if desired before serving.

Apple Walnut Galette

Prep Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 40 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 refrigerated pie crust
  • 3-4 apples, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons butter, cubed
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust on a baking sheet.
  3. Arrange the apple slices in the center of the crust, leaving a border around the edges.
  4. Sprinkle the chopped walnuts, sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg over the apples. Dot with butter.
  5. Fold the edges of the crust over the apples, pleating as needed.
  6. Brush the crust with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  7. Bake for 40 minutes until the crust is golden and the apples are tender.
  8. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

By infusing your Thanksgiving table with these farm-fresh recipes, you not only create a memorable dining experience but also support local agriculture and sustainable practices. Embrace the flavors of the season and the spirit of gratitude as you savor each bite surrounded by loved ones. Cheers to a bountiful and delicious farm-to-table Thanksgiving feast!
