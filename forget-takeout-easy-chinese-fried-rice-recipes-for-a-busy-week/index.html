Forget Takeout: Easy Chinese Fried Rice Recipes for a Busy Week


Do you find yourself reaching for the phone to order takeout every time you’re too tired to cook after a long day at work? You’re not alone! However, making Chinese fried rice at home doesn’t have to be a daunting task. By using simple ingredients and techniques, you can whip up this flavorful dish in no time, leaving you with more time to enjoy your meal and spend with your loved ones. In this post, we’ll share three easy Chinese fried rice recipes that are sure to satisfy your cravings without the hassle of ordering takeout.

Recipe 1: Basic Chinese Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked and cooled rice (preferably day old)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups protein of choice (shrimp, chicken, or tofu)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat, add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil.
  2. Add the beaten eggs to the pan, scramble gently and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining tbsp of oil, followed by onion, garlic and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the cooked rice, protein of choice, scrambled eggs, soy sauce, and sesame oil to the pan. Combine all ingredients gently, ensuring not to break the rice grains.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  6. Serve hot, and enjoy your homemade Chinese fried rice!

Recipe 2: Spicy Szechuan Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked and cooled rice (preferably day old)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cups protein of choice (shrimp, chicken, or tofu)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bell peppers, water chestnuts, bamboo shoots)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 serrano peppers, finely chopped
  • 3 scallions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp chili paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat, add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil.
  2. Add the protein of choice to the pan, cook until it’s no longer pink or fully cooked through, and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining tbsp of oil, followed by onion, garlic, serrano peppers, mixed vegetables, and scallions. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the cooked rice, protein, soy sauce, chili paste, and rice vinegar to the pan. Combine all ingredients gently, ensuring not to break the rice grains.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  6. Serve hot, garnish with extra scallions or a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and enjoy the heat of your homemade Szechuan fried rice!

Recipe 3: Teriyaki Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked and cooled rice (preferably day old)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cups protein of choice (shrimp, chicken, or tofu)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 scallions, sliced
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat, add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil.
  2. Add the protein of choice to the pan, cook until it’s no longer pink or fully cooked through, and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining tbsp of oil, followed by onion, garlic, and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the cooked rice, protein, teriyaki sauce, grated ginger, scallions, and sesame oil to the pan. Combine all ingredients gently, ensuring not to break the rice grains.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  6. Serve hot, garnish with extra scallions or sesame seeds, and enjoy the sweet and savory flavors of your homemade teriyaki fried rice!

With these easy Chinese fried rice recipes, you’ll not only save time and money by skipping the takeout, but also enjoy the satisfaction of creating your own delicious meal. The versatility of these recipes lets you customize each dish based on your taste preferences and dietary needs. Master these fried rice recipes and start impressing your family and friends, all without leaving the kitchen!
