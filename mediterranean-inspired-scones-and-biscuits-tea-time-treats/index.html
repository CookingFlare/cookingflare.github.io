Mediterranean-Inspired Scones and Biscuits: Tea Time Treats


When it comes to tea time, nothing quite compares to the delightful aroma of freshly baked scones and biscuits. These comforting treats have transcended borders, and today we’ll take a deliciously adventurous journey into the Mediterranean culinary world. Infused with vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and rich textures, Mediterranean-inspired scones and biscuits offer a unique twist that will elevate your tea time experience. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or enjoying a quiet afternoon, these recipes promise to impress.

The Allure of Mediterranean Flavors

The Mediterranean region is renowned for its diverse and rich culinary heritage. From the sun-kissed coasts of Greece to the spice-laden markets of Morocco, the flavors are as varied as the landscapes. The incorporation of ingredients like olives, feta cheese, sun-dried tomatoes, and herbs can transform traditional scones and biscuits into something extraordinary.

This blog post will explore various Mediterranean-inspired recipes that celebrate these flavors while maintaining the soft, flaky texture we all love in scones and biscuits. Let’s dive into these recipes that are perfect for tea time and beyond!

Classic Mediterranean Scones with Feta and Olives

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ cup pitted and chopped Kalamata olives
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 cup milk (or buttermilk)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper. These ingredients will form the base of your scones.

  3. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter into the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Add Cheese and Olives: Gently fold in the crumbled feta cheese, chopped olives, and oregano. Ensure that these ingredients are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

  5. Incorporate the Milk: Pour in the milk (or buttermilk) gradually, stirring until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix; you want to maintain those lovely flaky layers.

  6. Shape the Scones: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Pat it into a rectangle about 1 inch thick. Cut the dough into triangles or circles, depending on your preference.

  7. Bake: Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until they are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Cool and Serve: Allow the scones to cool on a wire rack before serving. These scones are perfect warm, with a drizzle of honey or a spread of cream cheese.

Sweet Mediterranean Biscuits with Honey and Almonds

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ½ cup ground almonds (or almond flour)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or milk)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Additional honey and slivered almonds for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, and ground almonds. Mix well to ensure that the dry components are evenly incorporated.

  3. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the honey, vanilla extract, and heavy cream. Gradually add this mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until the dough comes together.

  5. Shape the Biscuits: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Pat the dough into a thickness of about 1 inch. Cut out biscuits using a round cutter.

  6. Prepare for Baking: Place the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops with the beaten egg for a golden finish. Drizzle a bit of honey on top and sprinkle with slivered almonds.

  7. Bake: Bake for 15-18 minutes until golden and puffed.

  8. Serve Warm: Let the biscuits cool slightly before serving. These sweet delights pair wonderfully with a cup of herbal tea or a dollop of yogurt.

Mediterranean Herb-Infused Savory Biscuits

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (Parmesan or Gruyère)
  • 1 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, garlic powder, rosemary, and thyme.

  3. Cut in the Butter: Incorporate the cold, cubed butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Add Cheese: Stir in the shredded cheese, ensuring it’s well distributed throughout the mixture.

  5. Mix in Milk: Gradually add the milk, mixing until just combined. Avoid overworking the dough to keep the biscuits light and fluffy.

  6. Shape the Biscuits: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently pat it into a ¾-inch thick rectangle. Cut out biscuits using your preferred cutter.

  7. Bake: Place the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.

  8. Enjoy Fresh: Serve warm with butter or as a side to soups and salads. The herb-infused aroma will tantalize your senses!

Tips for Perfect Scones and Biscuits

  • Use Cold Ingredients: Cold butter and milk create a flaky texture. Make sure to chill your ingredients before starting.
  • Don’t Overmix: Mix just until combined to ensure your scones and biscuits remain tender and light.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to customize these recipes by adding your favorite Mediterranean ingredients, such as sun-dried tomatoes or different cheeses.
  • Serve with Dips: Pair your scones and biscuits with Mediterranean dips like tzatziki, hummus, or baba ghanoush for a delightful spread.

Conclusion

Mediterranean-inspired scones and biscuits bring a unique twist to traditional tea time treats. Their rich flavors and diverse ingredients make for an exciting culinary adventure that is sure to impress your guests or brighten your day. Whether you opt for savory feta and olive scones, sweet honey and almond biscuits, or herb-infused variations, these recipes are easy to follow and versatile.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and enjoy the delightful aroma of baking as you transport your taste buds to the Mediterranean. These treats are not just perfect for tea time; they can be enjoyed at breakfast, brunch, or as a delightful snack throughout the day. Happy baking!
