Vegan Miso Marvels: 15 Fermented & Vibrant Recipes for Balanced Flavors


Miso, a humble ingredient with deep flavor profiles, holds an incredibly versatile role in the world of plant-based cooking. A staple in Asian cultures, particularly for its health benefits, miso paste is rich in umami taste and brimming with essential nutrients. Perfect for enhancing a wide range of savory dishes, explore these 15 fermented and vibrant vegan recipes that showcase the Balanced Magic of miso.

  1. Miso-Carrot Soup A comforting and nourishing meal that highlights the natural sweetness of carrots, this soup is enlivened with the umami depth of white miso. Combined with coconut milk and fragrant ginger, this recipe is vegan, gluten-free, and a sure way to warm up any chilly evening.

  • Ingredients:

    • ½ cup white miso paste
    • 2 tablespoons tahini
    • 2 cups vegetable broth
    • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
    • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
    • 1 can coconut milk
    • 2 tablespoons water
    • 2 teaspoons sesame oil (optional)

  • Instructions:

    1. In a blender, combine white miso, tahini, vegetable broth, chopped carrots, ginger, and coconut milk. Blend until smooth, adding water if needed to achieve a desired consistency.
    2. Heat sesame oil in a pot over medium heat. Transfer the blended mixture into the pot, stirring occasionally until heated through.
    3. Optionally, serve with a swirl of coconut milk and a sprinkle of sesame seeds.
  1. Miso-Glazed Tofu A quick and easy vegan appetizer that will impress any guest, this marinated tofu is seared to a perfect balance of crispy and tender with a glossy miso glaze. Pair it with a colorful array of vegetables or serve it atop a rice bowl for a satisfying meal.

  • Ingredients:

    • ¼ cup white miso paste
    • ¼ cup mirin
    • 2 tablespoons sake or rice vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cut into slices
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • ¼ cup water (for mixing with cornstarch)
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
    • Sesame seeds, for garnish

  • Instructions:

    1. In a small bowl, whisk together white miso, mirin, sake or rice vinegar, and soy sauce to make the glaze.
    2. Slice pressed tofu into desired thickness, and blot dry with a clean towel.
    3. Coat tofu slices in vegetable oil and generously brush with the miso glaze.
    4. In a separate bowl, mix ¼ cup water with cornstarch until smooth, and then brush the mix onto the tofu slices to create a crust.
    5. Heat a large pan or grill over medium heat, cook tofu slices on each side until golden brown and crispy.
    6. Drizzle remaining glaze over tofu and sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.
  1. Miso-Kombu Broth This incredibly versatile broth brings a wealth of depth to any dish—perfect for drown sin fried rice, or as the base for soups, stews, and stir-fries. Its light sweetness and subtle brininess make it a staple for your plant-based pantry.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 piece of kombu (dried kelp)
    • 1 cup white miso paste
    • 6 cups water
    • ½ teaspoon onion powder
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional)

  • Instructions:

    1. Rehydrate the kombu by soaking it in room temperature water for about 15 minutes.
    2. Drain the kombu and place it in a large saucepan, adding water, white miso paste, and onion powder.
    3. Stir the mixture until the miso has dissolved, then bring the liquid to a gentle simmer over low heat.
    4. Optionally, if you find the broth too salty, you can dilute it with soy sauce.

And there you have it – just 15 magnificent ways miso could be incorporated into your plant-based kitchen. The choice is yours to explore and adapt these ideas, or unveil even more culinary marvels with an elegant ingredient that truly is a miso marvel. So, grab that tub of miso, and let your taste buds delight in the hues and flavors of each recipe, whilst balancing the many dimensions of vegan cooking.
