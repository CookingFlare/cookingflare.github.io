A Delicious Italian-Style Focaccia Bread Recipe


If you love the aroma of freshly baked bread and the savory flavors of Italian cuisine, then you’re in for a treat. Today, we’re diving into the world of Italian-style focaccia bread, a delightful and versatile bread that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • ½ cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Optional toppings: cherry tomatoes, olives, caramelized onions

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a small bowl, combine the warm water and active dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy. This process activates the yeast, which is essential for the bread to rise and develop its characteristic flavor and texture.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture and ¼ cup of olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. This step is crucial for developing the gluten in the dough, which gives the focaccia its signature chewy texture.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

Step 5: Prepare the Pan

Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil onto a baking sheet, making sure to coat the entire surface evenly. Gently transfer the risen dough to the prepared pan and stretch it out to fit. Cover the pan with the kitchen towel and let it rise for an additional 30 minutes.

Step 6: Add Toppings

Once the dough has risen for the second time, use your fingertips to create dimples all over the surface. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the dough, allowing it to pool in the dimples. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt and fresh rosemary. If desired, you can also add cherry tomatoes, olives, or caramelized onions for extra flavor.

Step 7: Bake

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the pan in the oven and bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes or until it’s golden brown and crispy on the edges.

Step 8: Enjoy!

Once the focaccia is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, slice it into squares or wedges and serve it warm. It’s perfect on its own, dipped in olive oil and balsamic vinegar, or as a side to your favorite Italian dishes.

Final Thoughts

There’s something truly special about the simplicity and rustic charm of Italian-style focaccia bread. With its golden crust, fragrant olive oil, and aromatic rosemary, this bread is a celebration of Mediterranean flavors. Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering or simply craving a taste of Italy, this recipe is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the art of bread-making transport you to the sun-drenched hills of Tuscany. Buon appetito!
