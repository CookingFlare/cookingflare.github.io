Simple and Quick Korean Pork Belly Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re in the mood for something delicious, savory, and full of bold flavors, look no further than this simple and quick Korean pork belly stir-fry recipe. Korean cuisine is known for its balance of flavors—spicy, sweet, salty, and savory—and this recipe brings all of these elements together in a dish that’s as satisfying as it is easy to prepare. With just a few ingredients and a handful of steps, you can make a Korean-inspired meal that’s perfect for any weeknight dinner.

In this recipe, we’ll be using pork belly, a popular cut of meat in Korean cooking, which is tender, juicy, and full of flavor. When stir-fried, the pork belly develops a crispy, caramelized exterior while remaining juicy inside, making every bite a mouthwatering experience. Paired with a flavorful sauce and stir-fried vegetables, this dish is guaranteed to be a hit.

Ingredients for Korean Pork Belly Stir-Fry

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather all the ingredients you’ll need to create this tasty stir-fry:

Main Ingredients:

  • Pork Belly (500g): The star of the dish. You can also use pork belly slices or cubes, depending on your preference.
  • Vegetables: A mix of bell peppers (red, yellow, green), onions, and carrots. These vegetables add color and texture to the dish.
  • Garlic (3-4 cloves): Finely chopped or minced for an aromatic base.
  • Ginger (1-inch piece): Grated or finely minced, it pairs wonderfully with the pork belly.
  • Soy Sauce (3 tablespoons): For that rich umami flavor.
  • Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste) (2 tablespoons): Adds a spicy kick and a bit of sweetness to the dish.
  • Honey (1 tablespoon): Balances the spiciness and enhances the flavor profile.
  • Sesame Oil (2 teaspoons): Provides a nutty, fragrant finish.
  • Vegetable Oil (2 tablespoons): For stir-frying the pork belly and vegetables.
  • Sesame Seeds (1 tablespoon): Toasted sesame seeds are a garnish to add flavor and texture.
  • Green Onions (2-3 stalks): Chopped for garnish and a fresh touch.
  • Rice (optional): Serve the stir-fry over steamed rice for a complete meal.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Chili flakes or fresh chili: For additional heat if you love spice.
  • Mushrooms: Enoki or shiitake mushrooms add a rich umami flavor.
  • Tofu: If you want a vegetarian version or a variation, tofu can be substituted for pork belly.

Essential Kitchen Tools:

  • Wok or large frying pan
  • Knife and chopping board
  • Stirring spoon
  • Small bowls for sauces and garnishes

How to Prepare Korean Pork Belly Stir-Fry

The preparation for this recipe is straightforward, and in less than 30 minutes, you’ll have a mouthwatering dish to serve up. Let’s go step-by-step.

1. Preparing the Pork Belly

Start by preparing your pork belly. If you’re using pork belly slices, you can leave them as is or cut them into smaller bite-sized pieces. If you have a larger chunk of pork belly, cut it into small cubes or strips to ensure even cooking.

  • Tip: If you’re using frozen pork belly, make sure it’s completely thawed before you begin cooking.

Once your pork belly is cut, set it aside while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.

2. Preparing the Vegetables

While the pork belly is set aside, it’s time to chop the vegetables. Cut the bell peppers, onions, and carrots into thin strips or bite-sized pieces. This not only ensures that the vegetables cook evenly, but it also creates a nice texture contrast to the tender pork belly.

  • Tip: You can experiment with different vegetables based on what you have on hand, such as zucchini, mushrooms, or baby corn.

Chop the garlic and ginger finely, as these will add a flavorful base to the stir-fry.

3. Preparing the Sauce

In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, gochujang, honey, and sesame oil. This mixture will form the base of the stir-fry sauce, and the combination of soy sauce and gochujang will give you that signature Korean flavor profile—salty, spicy, and just a hint of sweetness.

Stir the sauce until it’s well combined and smooth.

4. Stir-Frying the Pork Belly

Now, it’s time to cook the pork belly. Heat your wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat and add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the pork belly pieces.

  • Tip: Don’t overcrowd the pan. If you’re cooking a large batch, cook the pork belly in batches to ensure even browning.

Cook the pork belly for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until it’s nicely browned and crispy. Pork belly has a lot of fat, so it will release some of its own fat while cooking, which helps crisp up the exterior. Once it’s cooked through and crispy, remove the pork belly from the pan and set it aside.

5. Stir-Frying the Vegetables

In the same pan, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Toss in the garlic and ginger, sautéing them for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Next, add the chopped vegetables (bell peppers, onions, and carrots) and stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes. You want the vegetables to be tender but still retain some crunch for texture.

  • Tip: Don’t overcook the vegetables; they should remain vibrant and slightly crisp.

6. Combining the Pork Belly and Sauce

Once the vegetables are cooked, return the crispy pork belly to the pan. Pour the prepared sauce over the pork and vegetables, stirring well to ensure everything is evenly coated with the flavorful sauce. Allow the stir-fry to cook for another 2-3 minutes, letting the sauce thicken slightly and glaze the pork and vegetables.

Taste the dish and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you want it spicier, add more gochujang or chili flakes. If you prefer a sweeter flavor, add a little more honey.

7. Garnishing and Serving

Once the pork belly stir-fry is ready, turn off the heat. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and freshly chopped green onions for a burst of flavor and color. Serve the stir-fry over steamed rice to complete the meal.

  • Tip: If you’re serving this dish with rice, use short-grain rice, which is commonly used in Korean cuisine and complements the stir-fry perfectly.

8. Enjoy Your Korean Pork Belly Stir-Fry

Now, you’re ready to enjoy your delicious Korean pork belly stir-fry! The crispy pork belly combined with the flavorful sauce and vibrant vegetables makes for an irresistible meal that’s sure to please everyone at the table. This dish is perfect for a quick dinner, but it’s also hearty enough to serve for special occasions or a weekend gathering.

Tips and Variations

Use Different Cuts of Pork

While pork belly is ideal for this recipe because of its tenderness and flavor, you can also use other cuts of pork such as pork shoulder or pork loin. These cuts may not have the same level of fat, so you might need to adjust the cooking time and add a little extra oil to achieve a crispy texture.

Adjusting the Spice Level

If you’re a fan of spicy food, feel free to ramp up the heat. Gochujang is spicy, but you can also add fresh chili slices or chili flakes for an extra kick. On the other hand, if you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the amount of gochujang or omit the chili altogether.

Add More Vegetables

Korean stir-fries are versatile and can accommodate a variety of vegetables. If you want to make the dish even more nutritious, you can add mushrooms, zucchini, or bok choy. For a more traditional touch, consider adding kimchi as a side dish to complement the stir-fry.

Make It a Low-Carb Version

If you’re following a low-carb or keto diet, you can skip the rice and serve the stir-fry with cauliflower rice instead. The light, fluffy cauliflower rice will absorb the savory sauce and make a perfect base for the pork belly stir-fry.

Conclusion

This simple and quick Korean pork belly stir-fry recipe is a fantastic way to bring the bold and delicious flavors of Korean cuisine into your kitchen. With tender, crispy pork belly, fresh vegetables, and a savory sauce, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or friends, this stir-fry will surely impress with its depth of flavor and satisfying textures.

So next time you’re craving something savory, spicy, and full of umami, whip up this Korean pork belly stir-fry and enjoy a truly authentic meal in no time. Happy cooking!
