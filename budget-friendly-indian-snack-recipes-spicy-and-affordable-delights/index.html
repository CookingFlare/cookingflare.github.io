Budget-Friendly Indian Snack Recipes: Spicy and Affordable Delights


Who doesn’t love a good snack? Whether you’re looking for a quick bite in between meals or planning a fun get-together with friends, Indian snacks never fail to delight. They are known for their bold flavors, unique spices, and mouthwatering combinations. But what if you’re on a budget? Don’t worry, because we’ve got you covered! In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some delicious and wallet-friendly Indian snack recipes that will satisfy your cravings without breaking the bank. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Masala Toast

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bread
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter for toasting

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the mashed potatoes, chopped onion, green chili, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Take a slice of bread and spread the potato mixture evenly on one side.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and melt a small amount of butter.
  4. Place the bread slice with the potato mixture facing down onto the pan.
  5. Toast until golden brown and crispy, then flip and toast the other side.
  6. Repeat the process for the remaining slices of bread.
  7. Serve hot with ketchup or chutney of your choice.

2. Aloo Tikki

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, bread crumbs, coriander leaves, cumin powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape them into round patties.
  4. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Carefully place the patties into the pan and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Remove from the pan and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  7. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

3. Chole Bhature

Ingredients for Chole:

  • 1 cup chickpeas, soaked overnight and boiled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Ingredients for Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions for Chole:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the boiled chickpeas and cook for 10-15 minutes on low heat.
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves and keep aside.

Instructions for Bhature:

  1. In a bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, yogurt, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Knead the mixture into a soft dough. Add water if needed.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into circular shapes.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Fry the bhature until they turn golden brown and puffy.
  7. Serve hot with chole and enjoy!

4. Veg Cutlet

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1/4 cup green peas, boiled and mashed
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped beans
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped capsicum
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Bread crumbs for coating
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, grated carrot, mashed peas, chopped beans, capsicum, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape them into round patties.
  4. Dip each patty into bread crumbs, ensuring they are coated evenly.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Carefully place the patties into the pan and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Remove from the pan and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  8. Serve hot with ketchup or mint chutney.

Conclusion

These budget-friendly Indian snack recipes are not only delicious but also easy on the pocket. With simple ingredients easily available in your kitchen, you can whip up these spicy and affordable delights in no time. So, why wait? Gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and get ready to indulge in some mouthwatering snacks that won’t break the bank. Happy cooking!
