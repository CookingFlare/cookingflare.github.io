Cooking for College: 5 Easy Recipes for Beginners


As you step into the world of college life, you may find yourself facing a new challenge: cooking for yourself. With a busy schedule filled with classes, studying, and socializing, it can be tempting to rely on takeout or instant noodles. However, cooking at home can be both enjoyable and rewarding, not to mention healthier and more economical. In this blog post, we’ll explore five easy recipes that are perfect for college students just starting their culinary journey. Each recipe is designed to be simple, quick, and delicious, ensuring that you can whip up a meal without spending hours in the kitchen.

1. One-Pan Chicken and Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, and carrots)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Chicken: In a bowl, drizzle the chicken breasts with olive oil, then season with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated.
  3. Arrange on a Baking Sheet: Place the seasoned chicken breasts on one side of a baking sheet. On the other side, spread out the mixed vegetables. Drizzle a little olive oil over the vegetables and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve: Once done, let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve with the roasted vegetables and garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Why This Recipe Works:

This one-pan meal is perfect for beginners because it requires minimal prep and cleanup. Plus, it’s a balanced dish that provides protein and vitamins, making it a great option for busy college students.

2. Easy Pasta Primavera

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta (spaghetti, penne, or your choice)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (zucchini, cherry tomatoes, bell peppers)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the rest.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the mixed vegetables and Italian seasoning, cooking until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Combine: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the vegetables. Pour in a bit of the reserved pasta water to help create a sauce. Toss everything together until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve: Plate the pasta and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Why This Recipe Works:

Pasta primavera is a versatile dish that allows you to use whatever vegetables you have on hand. It’s quick to prepare and can be made in under 30 minutes, making it ideal for a busy college lifestyle.

3. Breakfast Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 small potato, diced
  • 1/2 cup black beans (canned, rinsed)
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or your choice)
  • 1 large tortilla
  • Salsa (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Potato: In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced potato and cook until golden and tender, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Scramble the Eggs: In a bowl, whisk the eggs and season with salt and pepper. Push the potatoes to one side of the skillet and pour the eggs into the other side. Scramble until fully cooked.
  3. Add Beans and Cheese: Stir in the black beans and cheese, allowing the cheese to melt.
  4. Assemble the Burrito: Place the egg mixture in the center of the tortilla. Add salsa if desired, then fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling and roll it up tightly.
  5. Serve: Enjoy your breakfast burrito warm, or wrap it in foil for a quick meal on the go.

Why This Recipe Works:

Breakfast burritos are not only filling but also customizable.
