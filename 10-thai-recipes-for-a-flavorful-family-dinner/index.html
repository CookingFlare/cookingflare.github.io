10 Thai Recipes for a Flavorful Family Dinner


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and a perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements. If you’re looking to spice up your family dinner with some authentic Thai dishes, you’re in the right place! Here are ten delicious Thai recipes that are sure to impress your family and leave everyone asking for seconds.

1. Pad Thai

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes or until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown.
  3. Push the tofu to one side of the skillet and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble until cooked, then mix with the tofu.
  4. Add the soaked noodles, fish sauce, tamarind paste, and sugar. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until everything is well combined.
  5. Toss in the bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for an additional minute.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with crushed peanuts and lime wedges.

2. Tom Yum Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger)
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the broth to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Simmer for 5-10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add the shrimp and mushrooms, cooking until the shrimp turns pink.
  3. Stir in the bird chilies, fish sauce, and lime juice. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

3. Green Curry Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup eggplant, diced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook until no longer pink.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk, stirring to combine. Add the eggplant and bell peppers.
  4. Season with fish sauce and sugar, simmering for about 15 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

4. Massaman Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef or chicken, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 potatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat a little oil and sauté the Massaman curry paste until fragrant.
  2. Add the meat and cook until browned.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Add the potatoes and onion.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, tamarind paste, and sugar. Simmer for about 30 minutes until the meat is tender.
  5. Serve hot, topped with roasted peanuts.

5. Thai Basil Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground pork or chicken
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat oil over medium heat. Add garlic and chilies, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground meat, cooking until browned.
  3. Stir in the soy sauce and oyster sauce, cooking for another minute.
  4. Remove from heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves until wilted.
