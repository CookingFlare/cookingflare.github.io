Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup: A Vibrant and Tangy Dish

3 min · 467 words

When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival a delicious bowl of soup. And if you’re looking for a soup that’s both vibrant and tangy, then look no further than Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup. This recipe combines the sweetness of roasted red peppers with the tanginess of tomatoes, resulting in a flavor explosion that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 4 red bell peppers
  • 4 ripe tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the red bell peppers and tomatoes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast them in the oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the skins are charred and blistered.

  2. Remove the baking sheet from the oven and let the roasted vegetables cool down for a few minutes. Once they are cool enough to handle, peel off the skins from the peppers and tomatoes.

  3. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  4. Dice the roasted red peppers and tomatoes, and add them to the pot. Stir in the smoked paprika and dried basil, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the soup to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.

  6. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy. If using a regular blender, be sure to blend in batches and vent the lid to prevent any accidents.

  7. Once the soup is smooth, return it to the pot and heat it over low heat until warmed through. Taste and adjust the seasonings if necessary.

  8. Serve the Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh basil or a drizzle of olive oil. Pair it with a crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for a satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the soup.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, stir in a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk before serving.
  • Customize your soup by adding your favorite herbs and spices. Thyme, oregano, or even a touch of cayenne pepper can take this dish to the next level.
  • This soup can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed year-round. Whether you serve it as a light lunch, a comforting dinner, or even as an appetizer at your next gathering, this vibrant and tangy soup is sure to impress. So grab your ingredients, fire up the oven, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of this delicious soup.
