Instant Pot Garlic Mashed Potatoes: A Perfect Side Dish


Mashed potatoes are an essential staple at dinner tables across the globe. Perfectly creamy, flavorful, and comforting, they complete a variety of meals, whether it’s a holiday feast or a casual weeknight dinner. For many, the dream of consistently achieving smooth and luscious mashed potatoes feels more challenging than it should be. But here’s the secret — making garlic mashed potatoes in your Instant Pot simplifies the process, reduces cooking time, and guarantees a perfect result every single time.

This recipe elevates mashed potatoes with the robust flavor of garlic, creating an irresistible side dish that complements a wide range of main meals. Whether you’re serving roasted chicken, grilled steak, or a plant-based option, these creamy Instant Pot garlic mashed potatoes will steal the spotlight. Let’s dive into how to bring this dish to life!

Why Make Garlic Mashed Potatoes in an Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot revolutionized cooking by combining efficiency and versatility into one appliance. Traditional mashed potatoes require boiling water, keeping an eye on the stove, and mashing with precision to avoid lumps. The Instant Pot, however, streamlines this process. You simply pressure cook the potatoes, and they’re ready to mash effortlessly.

Additionally, cooking garlic alongside the potatoes in an Instant Pot allows the flavors to infuse deeply. The sealed environment locks in steam, preserving the flavor and moisture of the ingredients. Plus, it’s a one-pot process, which means less cleanup, leaving you more time to focus on plating, garnishing, or even relaxing before serving your meal.

Ingredients

For flavor-packed Instant Pot garlic mashed potatoes, gather the following:

  • 3 pounds of Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes (peeled and cut into roughly 1-inch chunks)
  • 6 garlic cloves (peeled and whole)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter (plus extra for serving if desired)
  • ½ cup of heavy cream (room temperature — for creamy texture)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional garnish: chopped fresh parsley or chives

Ingredient Notes

  • Potato choice: Yukon Gold potatoes are ideal for this recipe due to their creamy texture and buttery flavor. Russet potatoes work well, too, if you prefer a fluffier texture.
  • Garlic: Adjust the number of garlic cloves based on your preferred strength of garlic flavor. If you want a milder hint of garlic, reduce the cloves to two or three.
  • Heavy cream: If you’re looking for a lighter option, swap heavy cream for half-and-half, whole milk, or even unsweetened plant-based milk.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep the Potatoes and Garlic

Begin by peeling the potatoes and slicing them into uniform chunks of about 1-inch each. Uniform sizes ensure even cooking. Peel your garlic cloves and set everything aside while you prepare the Instant Pot.

2. Cook in the Instant Pot

Pour 1 cup of water into your Instant Pot. Next, place a steamer basket or rack inside (optional — this helps prevent the potatoes from directly touching the water, though it’s not strictly necessary). Add the diced potatoes and garlic cloves into the pot.

Secure the lid and set the pressure valve to the sealing position. Select the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” setting on high pressure, and set the timer for 10 minutes.

3. Quick Release and Drain

Once the timer goes off, perform a quick release by carefully moving the pressure valve to venting. When the pressure has completely released, open the lid. Drain any remaining water from the pot, if necessary.

4. Mash Potatoes and Add Flavor

One of the benefits of the Instant Pot is that it keeps everything warm, so you can mash your potatoes directly in the pot. Begin mashing with a potato masher or fork. For silky smooth mashed potatoes, consider using a potato ricer or hand mixer.

Add the unsalted butter to the warm potatoes, along with the heavy cream. Stir in salt and pepper gradually, tasting as you go to reach the perfect balance.

For extra garlic flavor, mash the cooked garlic cloves thoroughly into the potatoes. The garlic should blend seamlessly into the mix, releasing its mellow, sweet aroma.

5. Adjust Consistency

If the mashed potatoes seem too thick, add a splash of warm milk or additional heavy cream until you reach your desired texture. Be careful not to over-mix, as it can lead to gummy potatoes.

6. Garnish and Serve

Once the mashed potatoes are creamy and seasoned to perfection, transfer them to a serving dish. Garnish them with fresh parsley or chives for a pop of color and added freshness. For indulgence, drizzle with melted butter right before serving.

Tips for Perfect Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Achieving flawless mashed potatoes can be a little finicky, so here are some insider tips:

  • Cut potatoes uniformly: Cutting potatoes into even chunks ensures they cook at the same rate. This prevents some pieces from being overdone or undercooked.
  • Warm ingredients: Use room-temperature butter and heavy cream to prevent cooling down the potatoes. Warm liquids also blend better and improve the overall texture.
  • Don’t skimp on seasoning: Finding the right balance of salt and pepper is crucial. Potatoes easily absorb seasoning, so adjust carefully and taste frequently.
  • Avoid over-mashing: Overworking the potatoes can release starch and make them gluey. Mash just enough to reach your desired texture.

Pairing Suggestions

Garlic mashed potatoes are incredibly versatile. Here are some great options to pair them with:

  • Proteins: Roast turkey, baked chicken thighs, grilled steak, pork chops, or pan-seared salmon.
  • Vegetarian entrées: Serve alongside stuffed bell peppers, lentil loaf, or a mushroom Wellington for a hearty vegetarian spread.
  • Sauces: Smother these potatoes with creamy gravy, herb butter, or a drizzle of olive oil for added indulgence.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have leftover garlic mashed potatoes, don’t let them go to waste! Store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to three days. To reheat, place them in a pot over low heat, stirring continuously. Add a splash of milk or butter to rehydrate the potatoes and revive their creamy texture.

For longer storage, freeze mashed potatoes for up to a month. Thaw them in the refrigerator overnight and reheat gently. Potato texture can slightly change after freezing, but adding warm cream or stirring well will help restore their smoothness.

Why Choose This Recipe?

What makes Instant Pot garlic mashed potatoes stand out is the ease and consistency they deliver. The intense garlic flavor enhances the potatoes without overwhelming them, making this side dish suitable for many occasions. Using the Instant Pot saves time and energy, letting you enjoy homemade mashed potatoes without the hassle.

Moreover, these garlic mashed potatoes offer flexibility to customize to your preferences. Whether you love a lighter, fluffier texture or something rich and creamy, you can adjust the ingredients to suit your needs.

Closing Thoughts

Instant Pot garlic mashed potatoes bring together simplicity, flavor, and versatility, making them an ideal side dish for everyday meals and special gatherings alike. With this recipe, you’ll never need to rely on store-bought options or struggle with unevenly cooked potatoes again.

So, grab your Instant Pot, load it up with fresh potatoes and garlic, and get ready to whip up a comforting, crowd-pleasing side dish that’s sure to become a family favorite!
