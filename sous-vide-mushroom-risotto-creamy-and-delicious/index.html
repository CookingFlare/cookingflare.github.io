Sous Vide Mushroom Risotto: Creamy and Delicious


Are you in the mood for a creamy and delicious risotto that will impress your family and friends? Look no further than this Sous Vide Mushroom Risotto recipe! With the help of sous vide cooking, you’ll achieve perfectly cooked, tender mushrooms and a creamy risotto that will leave everyone craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Set your sous vide machine to 180°F (82°C) and preheat the water bath.

  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shallots and garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 2 minutes.

  3. Add the mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring the vegetable broth to a simmer.

  5. Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and stir to coat it with the butter and mushroom mixture. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice becomes translucent around the edges.

  6. Pour in the white wine and stir until it evaporates.

  7. Transfer the rice mixture to a sous vide bag and seal it, making sure to remove any excess air. Place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 45-60 minutes, or until the rice is cooked to your desired tenderness.

  8. Once the rice is cooked, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and open it. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until it melts and coats the rice.

  9. Serve the mushroom risotto immediately, garnished with fresh parsley.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a splash of truffle oil or sprinkle some dried porcini mushrooms on top before serving.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as cremini, shiitake, or oyster mushrooms, to add depth to your risotto.

  • If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can still make this recipe by cooking the risotto traditionally on the stovetop. Simply follow steps 2-7 in a large pot, adding the vegetable broth gradually and stirring frequently until the rice is cooked to perfection.

  • Leftover risotto can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply add a splash of broth or water and warm it up on the stovetop.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Mushroom Risotto is a dish that combines the rich flavors of mushrooms with the creamy texture of a perfectly cooked risotto. By using the sous vide method, you can achieve consistent results every time, ensuring that the rice is cooked to perfection and the mushrooms are tender and flavorful. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply treating yourself to a gourmet meal at home, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and indulge in the creamy and delicious goodness of Sous Vide Mushroom Risotto!
