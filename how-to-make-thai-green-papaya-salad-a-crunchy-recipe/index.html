How to Make Thai Green Papaya Salad: A Crunchy Recipe


Thai Green Papaya Salad, known as Som Tum, is a vibrant and refreshing dish that perfectly balances sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors. This salad is not only a staple in Thai cuisine but also a favorite among food enthusiasts around the world. With its crunchy texture and zesty dressing, it’s a dish that can easily become a highlight of any meal. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making this delicious salad, ensuring you can recreate the authentic taste of Thailand right in your kitchen.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make a traditional Thai Green Papaya Salad, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Salad:

  • 2 cups green papaya, shredded (about 1 medium-sized green papaya)
  • 1 cup carrots, shredded
  • 1/2 cup green beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, roughly chopped
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

For the Dressing:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed (adjust to taste)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Preparing the Ingredients

Selecting the Right Papaya

When choosing a green papaya, look for one that is firm and has a green skin. The flesh should be white or pale green. Avoid any papayas that have started to ripen, as they will be too sweet for this salad.

Shredding the Papaya and Carrots

To achieve the perfect texture, you can use a julienne peeler, a box grater, or a food processor with a shredding attachment. Shred the green papaya and carrots into thin strips. The goal is to create a crunchy texture that will hold up well against the dressing.

Preparing the Green Beans

Trim the ends of the green beans and cut them into 1-inch pieces. You can blanch them in boiling water for about 2 minutes if you prefer a softer texture, but for a crunchier salad, it’s best to leave them raw.

Halving the Cherry Tomatoes

Slice the cherry tomatoes in half to release their juices, which will add a burst of flavor to the salad.

Making the Dressing

The dressing is what brings the salad to life. In a small bowl, combine the fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar, smashed chilies, and minced garlic. Whisk the ingredients together until the sugar dissolves completely. Taste the dressing and adjust the flavors according to your preference. If you like it spicier, add more chilies; for a sweeter taste, increase the palm sugar.

Assembling the Salad

  1. Combine the Vegetables: In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded green papaya, carrots, green beans, and halved cherry tomatoes. Toss them gently to mix the ingredients evenly.

  2. Add the Dressing: Pour the dressing over the salad mixture. Using two forks or salad tongs, toss the salad gently to ensure that all the vegetables are coated with the dressing. Be careful not to bruise the vegetables; you want to maintain their crunchiness.

  3. Add the Peanuts: Just before serving, sprinkle the chopped roasted peanuts over the salad. This will add an extra layer of crunch and flavor.

  4. Garnish: Finish off your salad with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro for a burst of color and freshness.

Serving Suggestions

Thai Green Papaya Salad is incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways:

  • As a Side Dish: It pairs beautifully with grilled meats, seafood, or any Thai curry. The refreshing flavors of the salad help to balance the richness of the main dishes.

  • As a Light Meal: You can enjoy it on its own as a light lunch or dinner. Add some grilled chicken or shrimp for a more filling option.

  • As an Appetizer: Serve it as a starter at your next dinner party. The vibrant colors and flavors will impress your guests and set the tone for a delightful meal.

Tips for the Perfect Thai Green Papaya Salad

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: The key to a delicious salad is using fresh, high-quality ingredients. Whenever possible, opt for organic vegetables and fresh herbs.

  • Adjust the Spice Level: Thai cuisine is known for its bold flavors, but you can adjust the spice level to suit your taste. If you’re not a fan of heat, start with one chili and gradually add more.

  • Make It Ahead of Time: While this salad is best enjoyed fresh, you can prepare the vegetables and dressing ahead of time. Just combine them right before serving to maintain the crunchiness.
