Thanksgiving Dinner Recipes for Two


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and food, but what if you’re celebrating the holiday with just one special person? You don’t have to sacrifice the joy of a traditional Thanksgiving feast just because you’re cooking for two. With a little creativity, you can enjoy a delicious, intimate dinner that brings all the festive flavors to your table without overwhelming yourself with excess food or complicated recipes.

In this blog post, we’ll share several Thanksgiving dinner recipes for two that are both easy to make and packed with the classic flavors everyone loves. Whether you’re in the mood for a small roasted turkey, perfectly mashed potatoes, or a delightful dessert, you’ll find all the inspiration you need here to create a memorable Thanksgiving meal for two.

1. Roast Turkey Breast with Herb Butter

Roasting a whole turkey for two is impractical and unnecessary. Instead, opt for a turkey breast, which offers all the traditional flavors with less prep and fewer leftovers. Here’s how to make a succulent roast turkey breast with an aromatic herb butter that will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless turkey breast (about 2 to 3 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the herb butter: In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with garlic, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Season the turkey: Pat the turkey breast dry with paper towels. Using your hands, gently lift the skin of the turkey breast and spread a generous portion of the herb butter underneath. Rub any remaining butter on top of the skin.
  4. Roast the turkey: Place the turkey breast in a roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan and cover with foil. Roast for 45 minutes to an hour, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Rest and serve: Remove the turkey from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with your favorite sides.

The herb butter adds a rich, savory flavor to the turkey while keeping it juicy. This smaller portion ensures that you don’t have any leftover turkey that will go to waste.

2. Garlic Mashed Potatoes with Cream Cheese

No Thanksgiving dinner would be complete without mashed potatoes. This creamy, garlicky mashed potato recipe is easy to make and pairs perfectly with your turkey. The addition of cream cheese gives it an extra level of smoothness and tang.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup milk (or more for desired consistency)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes: In a large pot, cover the potatoes and garlic with water and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Mash the potatoes: Drain the potatoes and garlic, then return them to the pot. Mash the potatoes with a potato masher until smooth.
  3. Add the cream cheese and butter: Stir in the cream cheese and butter until completely melted and smooth. Gradually add milk, stirring until you reach the desired creamy consistency.
  4. Season and serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately for a comforting side dish.

These mashed potatoes are smooth, rich, and perfect for pairing with turkey, gravy, or any other Thanksgiving side.

3. Crispy Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts are a classic Thanksgiving vegetable, and when roasted to perfection, they develop a crispy, caramelized exterior and tender interior. The balsamic glaze adds a sweet and tangy flavor that complements the sprouts beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Brussels sprouts: Toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the Brussels sprouts: Roast for 20 to 25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the sprouts are golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  4. Make the balsamic glaze: While the Brussels sprouts are roasting, combine the balsamic vinegar, honey, and Dijon mustard in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, until the sauce thickens and becomes syrupy.
  5. Toss and serve: Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the roasted Brussels sprouts and toss to coat. Serve immediately.

The sweetness of the glaze perfectly balances the natural bitterness of the Brussels sprouts, making them an irresistible side dish for your Thanksgiving meal.

4. Stuffing for Two

Stuffing is one of the quintessential Thanksgiving dishes, but making a whole batch can leave you with too much leftover. This recipe for stuffing for two is the perfect amount for a small gathering. It’s flavorful and aromatic, with the perfect balance of bread, herbs, and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cubed day-old bread (French bread or sourdough works best)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/2 small onion, chopped
  • 1/2 stalk celery, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toast the bread: Arrange the bread cubes on a baking sheet and toast in the oven for 10 to 12 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  3. Cook the vegetables: In a medium skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and celery and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  4. Combine and bake: In a large bowl, combine the toasted bread cubes, sautéed vegetables, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken broth and stir to combine. Transfer the mixture to a small baking dish and bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the top is golden and crispy.

This stuffing recipe for two is full of flavor, with crispy bread cubes and savory vegetables. It’s the perfect side to serve alongside your turkey.

5. Pumpkin Pie for Two

To wrap up your Thanksgiving dinner, there’s no better dessert than a slice of classic pumpkin pie. This easy recipe for pumpkin pie for two uses a small pie dish, so you can enjoy all the flavors of the holiday without any waste.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 pre-made pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Make the filling: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, brown sugar, egg, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt until smooth and combined.
  3. Prepare the pie crust: Press the pie crust into a small pie dish (about 6 inches in diameter). Trim any excess crust and crimp the edges.
  4. Fill the crust: Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  5. Bake: Bake for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool and serve: Allow the pie to cool completely before serving. You can top it with a dollop of whipped cream if desired.

This pumpkin pie is the perfect end to a cozy Thanksgiving dinner for two. Its spiced, creamy filling and flaky crust capture all the flavors of the season.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving for two can be just as festive, delicious, and memorable as a large family gathering. By scaling down traditional recipes, you can create a delightful meal that’s just right for a more intimate celebration. From the tender turkey breast to the creamy mashed potatoes, crispy Brussels sprouts, flavorful stuffing, and comforting pumpkin pie, each dish is designed to provide you with a full Thanksgiving experience without the stress of cooking for a crowd.

So, whether you’re celebrating with your partner, a close friend, or just enjoying a quiet holiday at home, these Thanksgiving dinner recipes for two will help make your day as special and delicious as possible. Enjoy your cozy holiday and the delicious food that comes with it!
