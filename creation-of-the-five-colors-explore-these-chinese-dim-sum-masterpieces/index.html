Creation of the Five Colors: Explore these Chinese Dim Sum Masterpieces


Chinese dim sum is a delightful culinary experience, characterized by its elaborate flavors and visually stunning presentations. These bite-sized delicacies are perfect for sharing, and no dim sum gathering is complete without sampling the myriad of delectable choices on offer. One of the fascinating aspects of Chinese dim sum is “Creation of the Five Colors,” a delicious celebration that honors the vibrant hues of ingredients and striking visual appeal of these masterpieces. This culinary tradition consists of five mouthwatering dishes, each representative of a unique color theme. Excited to embark on this delectable journey? Let’s dive in and explore these Chinese dim sum masterpieces.

1. Vibrant Red: Prawn Dumplings (Har Gao)

The color red is often associated with good fortune, energy, and prosperity in Chinese culture. Naturally, it’s fitting that our first dim sum masterpiece is the vibrant red, or har gao, a popular and enticing Chinese dumpling teeming with succulent prawns.

Ingredients:

  • 200 grams of prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 1/3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 8 round-shaped wrappers, store-bought or homemade

Instructions:

  1. To ensure a harmonious marriage of flavors, combine the prawns, salt, green onions, and sesame oil in a bowl. Mix well.
  2. Lay out the dumpling wrappers on a flat surface and place a small portion of the prawn filling in the center of each one.
  3. Brush the edges of the wrapper with water, fold it diagonally, press the edges together to seal, and then gently pinch the edges to create pleats, forming a nice half-moon shape.
  4. In a pot, bring water to a boil and then lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Place the har gao in the simmering water and steam for about 8-10 minutes, or until the wrappers turn perfectly al dente.
  5. Serve with your favorite sauce – soy, chili oil, or even hoisin – and savor the flavors of good fortune in every bite.

2. Bewitching Blue: Steamed Egg White Custard Buns (Lai Wong Bao)

Our second stop on the palate expedition takes us to the color blue, symbolizing serenity and tranquility. Lai Wong Bao, a delectable steamed bun filled with silky smooth egg white custard, is a heavenly mixture of flavors that feast not just the senses but also the soul.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of egg whites (approximately 30 large eggs)
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup of dark soya sauce
  • 1/4 cup of light soya sauce

Instructions:

  1. Begin by steaming the salted egg whites until they’re fully set. Once done, transfer them to a blender and blend until they’re smooth and frothy.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the sugar, salt, and water. Heat gently until the sugar dissolves, then remove from heat.
  3. Combine the milk and cornstarch in a bowl, and then slowly whisk the mixture into the sugar syrup.
  4. Stir constantly over medium heat until the custard thickens and begins to bubble. At this point, add the egg whites, mixing continuously until the custard regains its smooth consistency.
  5. Carefully transfer the custard into pre-prepared buns and let them cool slightly before serving.

3. Invigorating Yellow: Barbecued Pork Buns (Char Siu Bao)

Vibrant yellow instills warmth and happiness, making it the perfect hue to represent the third dim sum masterpiece – the char siu bao (barbecued pork bun). A classic Cantonese delight, these buns encapsulate succulent bits of barbecued pork, wrapped in a soft and alluring yellow bun.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons of instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 5 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of cooked and shredded barbecued pork
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the warm water, instant yeast, and sugar. Set aside for about 5 minutes, until foamy.
  2. In a separate large bowl, sift the flour. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture, followed by the vegetable oil. Mix until a smooth dough is formed.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes, and then place it in a greased bowl, covering it with a damp cloth. Allow the dough to rise for approximately 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  4. Punch down the dough and knead for an additional 5 minutes. Divide the dough into medium-sized portions and shape them into balls.
  5. Flatten each ball and place a generous amount of shredded barbecued pork in the center, sealing the buns and creating pleats on the edges.
  6. Steam the buns until cooked through, and then brush them lightly with sesame oil before serving.

4. Refreshing Green: Bamboo Rice Dumplings (Lo Mai Gai)

The fourth color in our gastronomical journey is green, representing nature, growth, and harmony. Lo mai gai, or bamboo rice dumplings, pay tribute to these themes through a delightful blend of fragrant steamed rice, savory char siu (barbecued pork), and fresh eggs, all wrapped in lotus leaves and steamed in bamboo containers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of lotus leaves (fresh or frozen)
  • 6 cups of glutinous rice, washed
  • 2 cups of char siu, julienned
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 sliced ginger, to taste
  • 4 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Bamboo containers with lids

Instructions:

  1. Soak the lotus leaves in warm water for about 30 minutes to soften them. Drain and pat dry before use.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the glutinous rice, char siu, ginger slices, and hard-boiled eggs. Combine the soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar in a separate bowl, then pour this mixture over the rice and meat.
  3. Allow the rice mixture to marinate for about 2 hours.
  4. Place a piece of lotus leaf in a bamboo steamer, and then spoon the marinated rice into the center. Wrap the lotus leaf tightly around the rice, ensuring that the edges are well secured.
  5. Place the lotus leaf parcel in a bamboo container with a lid and steam for about 1 hour, or until the rice is fully cooked and tender.
  6. Remove from heat, and let it cool slightly before unwrapping and serving.

5. Elegant White: Chicken Feathers (Jau Gok)

Finally, we arrive at the color white, associated with purity, elegance, and224 ;;# simple beauty. Chicken feathers, or Jau Gok, are another delightful dim sum dish that exemplifies these qualities through subtly flavored, pillowy morsels enveloping a savory and tender chicken filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped chicken (cooked and seasoned with salt, pepper, and oyster sauce)
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped mushrooms (optional)
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped bamboo shoots (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and water. Mix until smooth, then add the oil, and knead until the dough becomes soft and pliable.
  2. Roll portions of the dough into small balls and flatten them slightly with your palm.
  3. Place a generous amount of the chicken filling in the center of each flattened dough ball, being careful not to overcrowd it.
  4. Fold the dough over the filling and pinch the edges firmly to seal. Create a small hole in the top of the dough for ventilation while steaming.
  5. Place the Jau Gok in a steamer and steam them for about 15 minutes, or until fully cooked.
  6. Brush them with oil, if desired, and serve hot.

As we journey through the vibrant colors of China’s captivating dim sum masterpieces, let the Creation of the Five Colors illuminate both your taste buds and your spirit. Each bite, rich in flavor and steeped in cultural meaning, will leave you craving more of this authentic, unforgettable culinary experience.
