Mouthwatering Mixed Berry Pie: Bursting with Freshness


There’s nothing quite like a homemade pie to bring joy to your taste buds, and this Mouthwatering Mixed Berry Pie is no exception. Packed with a medley of fresh berries, this pie is bursting with flavor and will leave you craving for more. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s dive right into this delightful dessert!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, while mixing the dough with a fork. Mix just until the dough comes together.
  4. Divide the dough into two equal halves and shape each half into a disk. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed berries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss until the berries are coated in the mixture. Set aside and let it sit for about 10 minutes.

Step 3: Assembling the Pie

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Take one of the chilled dough disks out of the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface to fit your pie dish.
  3. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides.
  4. Pour the berry filling into the prepared crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Roll out the second dough disk and place it over the filling. You can leave it whole or create a lattice pattern by cutting strips and weaving them together.
  6. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges of the pie using your fingers or a fork to seal it.
  7. Optional: Brush the top crust with a beaten egg and sprinkle some granulated sugar for a golden, crispy finish.

Step 4: Baking and Serving

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours to allow the filling to set.
  3. Slice and serve this Mouthwatering Mixed Berry Pie on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a truly indulgent treat.

Conclusion

This Mouthwatering Mixed Berry Pie is a celebration of summer’s bounty and a true delight for your taste buds. With its flaky crust and bursting berry filling, it’s the perfect dessert to showcase the freshness of seasonal berries. So, next time you have an abundance of berries on hand, don’t hesitate to whip up this irresistible pie. Your friends and family will thank you!

Remember, the key to a perfect pie lies in using the freshest ingredients and giving it the love and care it deserves. So, go ahead and create your very own Mouthwatering Mixed Berry Pie. Happy baking!

Note: This recipe yields one 9-inch pie. Feel free to adjust the quantities based on the size of your pie dish and personal preference.
