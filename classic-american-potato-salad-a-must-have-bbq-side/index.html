Classic American Potato Salad: A Must-Have BBQ Side


There’s something about a classic American potato salad that just screams summer BBQs. It’s the perfect side dish to accompany your grilled burgers, hot dogs, and ribs. With its creamy texture, tangy flavor, and satisfying crunch, this potato salad is sure to be a hit at your next outdoor gathering. In this recipe, we’ll be sharing our take on this timeless classic, complete with all the tips and tricks you need to make it a success.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of red potatoes, washed and cubed
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup of chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup of diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped dill pickles
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by boiling the red potatoes in a large pot of salted water until they are fork-tender. This should take around 10-15 minutes. Be careful not to overcook them, as you want them to hold their shape in the salad.

  2. While the potatoes are boiling, prepare the dressing. In a separate bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white vinegar, salt, and pepper. Whisk everything together until well combined. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences.

  3. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them and let them cool slightly. You want them to be warm, not hot, when adding the dressing.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked potatoes, hard-boiled eggs, celery, red onion, and dill pickles. Gently fold everything together until the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  5. Pour the dressing over the potato mixture and gently toss until all the ingredients are coated. Be careful not to overmix, as you don’t want to break up the potatoes too much.

  6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight if possible. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the potato salad to chill.

  7. Before serving, give the potato salad a final taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can also garnish with some additional chopped dill pickles or fresh herbs for extra flavor and presentation.

Tips and Variations

  • For a creamier potato salad, you can add a few tablespoons of sour cream or Greek yogurt to the dressing mixture.
  • If you prefer a tangier flavor, you can add a splash of pickle juice or a squeeze of lemon juice to the dressing.
  • Feel free to customize your potato salad by adding other ingredients like bacon bits, diced bell peppers, or even some shredded cheese.
  • If you’re serving this salad at a picnic or outdoor event, be sure to keep it chilled in a cooler or with ice packs to prevent any food safety concerns.

Conclusion

Classic American potato salad is a staple dish that brings comfort and nostalgia to any BBQ gathering. With its creamy texture, tangy flavor, and satisfying crunch, it’s no wonder this side dish has stood the test of time. Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or attending a potluck, this potato salad is sure to be a crowd-pleaser. So grab your apron, fire up the grill, and get ready to enjoy the classic flavors of summer with this must-have BBQ side.
