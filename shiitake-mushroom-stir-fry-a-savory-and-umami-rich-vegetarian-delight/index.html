Shiitake Mushroom Stir Fry: A Savory and Umami-Rich Vegetarian Delight


Are you looking for a delicious and flavorful vegetarian dish that will satisfy your taste buds? Look no further than this Shiitake Mushroom Stir Fry recipe! Packed with savory and umami-rich flavors, this dish is a delightful combination of tender mushrooms, crisp vegetables, and a mouthwatering sauce. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to add more plant-based meals to your diet, this stir fry is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and caps sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, cornstarch, and vegetable broth. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook for 5 minutes, or until they start to release their juices.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Add the sliced bell peppers and onion to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  5. Pour the sauce mixture over the vegetables in the skillet. Stir well to combine and coat the vegetables evenly.
  6. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and coats the vegetables.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and serve the Shiitake Mushroom Stir Fry over cooked rice or noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a drizzle of sriracha sauce to the stir fry.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in this recipe. Broccoli florets, snap peas, or carrots would all be great additions or substitutions.
  • If you prefer a gluten-free version, use tamari or a gluten-free soy sauce in place of the regular soy sauce.
  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms for a unique twist. Portobello or oyster mushrooms would work well in this dish.
  • Garnish the stir fry with chopped green onions or sesame seeds for added flavor and presentation.

Final Thoughts

This Shiitake Mushroom Stir Fry is a versatile and flavorful dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as a side dish. The combination of tender mushrooms, crisp vegetables, and umami-rich sauce creates a satisfying and delicious meal. With its simple preparation and customizable ingredients, this stir fry is perfect for busy weeknight dinners or weekend gatherings. Give this recipe a try and experience the savory and umami-packed flavors for yourself. Enjoy!
