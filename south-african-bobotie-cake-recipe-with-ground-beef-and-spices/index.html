South African Bobotie Cake Recipe with Ground Beef and Spices


Bobotie is a traditional South African dish that has become a beloved staple in the country’s cuisine. This unique recipe combines the flavors of ground beef, spices, and a touch of sweetness to create a mouthwatering dish that will delight your taste buds. Today, I’m going to share with you my twist on this classic recipe - the South African Bobotie Cake.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 slices of white bread, soaked in water and squeezed dry
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons chutney
  • 2 tablespoons apricot jam
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1 cup almonds, chopped
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent.

  3. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the soaked bread, milk, curry powder, turmeric powder, cumin, coriander, Worcestershire sauce, chutney, apricot jam, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well to form a thick paste.

  5. Add the paste to the skillet with the cooked beef and stir until everything is well combined. Cook for an additional 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and then add them to the beef mixture. Stir until the eggs are fully incorporated.

  7. Grease a round cake pan with some vegetable oil. Transfer the beef mixture to the pan, spreading it evenly.

  8. Sprinkle the chopped dried apricots and almonds over the top of the beef mixture, pressing them lightly into the surface.

  9. Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cake is set.

  10. Remove the Bobotie Cake from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before removing it from the pan.

  11. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

Serving Suggestions

The South African Bobotie Cake can be enjoyed on its own as a main dish, or you can serve it with some fragrant yellow rice and a side of chutney for a complete meal. The combination of flavors and textures in this dish is truly something special. The sweetness from the apricots and the nuttiness from the almonds perfectly complement the savory beef and spices.

Conclusion

The South African Bobotie Cake is a delightful twist on the classic Bobotie dish. The combination of ground beef, spices, and the addition of dried apricots and almonds takes this dish to a whole new level. Whether you’re looking to try something new or want to impress your family and friends with a unique culinary creation, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So, go ahead and give it a try - you won’t be disappointed!
