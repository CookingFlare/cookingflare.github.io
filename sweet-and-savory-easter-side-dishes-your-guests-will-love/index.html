Sweet and Savory Easter Side Dishes Your Guests Will Love


When it comes to Easter feasts, the star is often the main course—perhaps a juicy glazed ham, a tender lamb roast, or a beautifully baked chicken. Yet, every memorable meal is elevated by the supporting cast: the side dishes that bring balance, contrast, and that little extra wow factor. Whether your guests are drawn to sweet, savory, or a perfectly harmonious blend of both, this guide explores creative side dishes to make your Easter table unforgettable.

Let’s dive into a collection of sweet and savory Easter side dishes that combine bright flavors with comforting textures, giving your celebration a fresh yet classic feel.

Sweet and Savory Roasted Carrots with Thyme and Honey

Carrots are a natural fit for spring menus, and roasting them brings out their inherent sweetness while allowing savory herbs to shine.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh carrots, peeled and cut into 3-inch sticks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or ½ tsp dried)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrot sticks with olive oil, honey, thyme, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the carrots in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, tossing halfway through, until the carrots are tender and caramelized at the edges.
  5. Remove from oven and sprinkle with lemon zest for an extra pop of brightness before serving.

This dish marries sweet honey notes with the herbaceous depth of thyme, making the carrots irresistibly flavorful and perfect alongside rich main dishes.

Spring Pea and Mint Risotto

Risotto is creamy, comforting, and endlessly versatile. Incorporating fresh spring peas and mint adds a refreshing twist, fitting for an Easter brunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, kept warm
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh mint leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; sauté until translucent.
  2. Add Arborio rice and stir for about 2 minutes until the grains start to look translucent around the edges.
  3. Pour in the white wine, stirring until it’s mostly absorbed.
  4. Begin adding warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently, allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding more.
  5. After about 15 minutes, stir in peas. Continue cooking and adding broth until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 18-20 minutes total.
  6. Remove from heat, stir in Parmesan and chopped mint. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper before serving.

The peas’ crisp sweetness combined with mint’s aromatic freshness creates a risotto that feels light yet indulgent—a fantastic accompaniment to ham or roasted lamb.

Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts with Pecans

Here’s a side that offers beautiful contrast in texture and taste: crispy Brussels sprouts with a sticky maple glaze and crunchy pecans.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1/3 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar or lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then spread evenly on a baking sheet.
  2. Roast for 20-25 minutes, shaking the pan or stirring halfway through to ensure even browning.
  3. In the last 5 minutes, drizzle maple syrup over the Brussels, return to the oven, and roast until caramelized and sticky.
  4. Meanwhile, toast pecans lightly in a dry skillet for 2-3 minutes, until fragrant.
  5. Remove sprouts from the oven, toss with pecans and a splash of apple cider vinegar or lemon juice to balance the sweetness. Serve warm.

The maple-pecan combo adds a delightful nutty richness, while the citrus acidity keeps this side dish lively and balanced.

Sweet Potato and Apple Gratin

Switch up traditional potato sides with a gratin that layers thinly sliced sweet potatoes and apples, baked until golden and bubbly.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 tart apples (e.g., Granny Smith), peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère or sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Butter a medium baking dish.
  2. Arrange sweet potato and apple slices alternately in overlapping layers in the dish.
  3. Season each layer lightly with salt, pepper, and sprinkle thyme leaves.
  4. Pour cream evenly over the layered slices, then top with shredded cheese.
  5. Dot with butter and cover loosely with foil.
  6. Bake for 40 minutes, then remove foil and bake another 15-20 minutes until top is bubbly and golden.
  7. Let rest 5 minutes before serving.

This gratin’s natural sweetness of apples and sweet potatoes blends beautifully with savory cheese and thyme, making it an elegant yet comforting dish on the table.

Spring Vegetable Medley with Lemon and Garlic

A medley of fresh spring vegetables brightens the table and the palate with vivid colors and invigorating flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, peeled
  • 1 cup radishes, halved
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch carrots for 2 minutes, snap peas for 1 minute, asparagus for 2 minutes, and radishes for 1 minute to keep them crisp-tender. Immediately transfer to iced water to stop cooking and preserve color.
  2. Drain vegetables well. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned.
  3. Toss in blanched vegetables, lemon zest, and juice. Season with salt and pepper and sauté for 3-4 minutes until heated through and coated with the lemon-garlic dressing.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley or chives before serving.

This bright, tangy side boasts vibrant colors and brings a light, refreshing counterpoint to heartier main dishes.

Honey-Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Walnuts and Cinnamon

Elevate your sweet potatoes with the warmth of cinnamon, crunch of walnuts, and natural honey sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil, honey, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Spread on a baking tray lined with parchment paper in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, tossing halfway through, until tender and caramelized.
  4. In the last 5 minutes, sprinkle walnuts over the potatoes to toast them lightly.
  5. Serve warm for a side that’s both sweet and satisfyingly nutty.

This dish is a crowd-pleaser, bringing together the earthy, velvety texture of sweet potatoes with just enough sweetness and crunch to delight every palate.

Creamed Spinach with Garlic and Nutmeg

For those who prefer richer, savory sides, creamed spinach is a perfect, elegant choice with just a hint of warmth from nutmeg.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh baby spinach, washed and tough stems removed
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add spinach in batches, stirring until wilted and reduced in volume.
  3. Pour heavy cream into the pan and simmer gently for 3-4 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Stir in nutmeg and Parmesan cheese, season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cook another 2 minutes until cheese is melted and sauce is creamy. Serve hot.

This creamy spinach side balances richness with subtle spice and cheese, pairing exceptionally well with savory meats at your Easter feast.

Final Thoughts

Crafting a delicious and balanced Easter table is all about variety and harmony. Incorporating both sweet and savory side dishes like those described above creates a dynamic meal that takes guests on a flavor journey. These recipes bring seasonal freshness, comforting textures, and delightful contrasts, ensuring everyone at the table finds a new favorite.

Don’t hesitate to mix and match these sweet and savory sides to suit your guests’ tastes and preferences. After all, the best Easter celebrations are those shared over memorable food and warm company.

Have a wonderful Easter filled with delicious bites and joyful moments!
