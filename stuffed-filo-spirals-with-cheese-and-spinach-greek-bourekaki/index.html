Stuffed Filo Spirals with Cheese and Spinach: Greek Bourekaki


If you’ve ever been to Greece or indulged in Greek cuisine, you’ve probably tasted the delicious, flaky, and savory pastries known as bourekaki. These delectable treats are typically made with thin filo pastry and filled with an array of tasty ingredients, but the most common and beloved filling is a mixture of cheese and spinach. Stuffed filo spirals with cheese and spinach, also known as bourekaki, offer the perfect balance of crispy texture and creamy, flavorful filling. Whether you’re looking for a snack, appetizer, or party dish, these Greek pastries are a crowd-pleaser!

In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating homemade bourekaki. You’ll learn the essential steps to prepare this delightful dish from scratch, and discover tips for making it even more delicious. Let’s dive into the world of Greek cooking and explore how to make these irresistible stuffed filo spirals.

The Origins of Bourekaki

Bourekaki is a traditional Greek pastry that belongs to a category of dishes known as bourekia—a term that generally refers to baked or fried pastries filled with a variety of ingredients. The word “bourekaki” is a diminutive form, indicating that these are small, bite-sized versions of the typical Greek savory pie.

Bourekaki is commonly served as a snack or appetizer in Greek homes, cafés, and restaurants. The combination of filo dough, which is known for its delicate and crispy texture, with the rich and creamy filling of cheese and spinach, creates a mouthwatering dish that has stood the test of time.

This recipe is a great example of how simple ingredients can be combined to create something extraordinary. The crispiness of the filo dough contrasts perfectly with the soft and flavorful filling, making each bite a truly satisfying experience.

Ingredients for Stuffed Filo Spirals with Cheese and Spinach

To make these Greek bourekaki at home, you’ll need a few simple ingredients. The core ingredients for the filling are spinach and cheese, but you can also add herbs and spices to enhance the flavor. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Filling:

  • Spinach: Fresh spinach is the traditional choice, but you can also use frozen spinach. Make sure to drain any excess water if using frozen spinach.
  • Feta Cheese: Feta is the quintessential Greek cheese. It has a tangy and crumbly texture that complements the spinach beautifully. If you prefer a milder taste, you can use a combination of feta and ricotta cheese.
  • Ricotta Cheese: Adds a creamy, smooth texture to the filling.
  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil is the best choice for sautéing the spinach and adding richness to the filling.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a punch of flavor to the filling.
  • Onion: A small onion or shallot, finely chopped, will contribute a mild sweetness to the mix.
  • Egg: Used to bind the filling together.
  • Herbs: Fresh dill or oregano will enhance the filling with a fragrant, herby taste.
  • Salt and Pepper: Season to taste.

For the Filo Pastry:

  • Filo Dough: You can find filo dough in the frozen section of most supermarkets. Make sure to thaw it according to the package instructions before using.
  • Butter: Melted butter is brushed on each sheet of filo dough to achieve the golden, crispy layers.
  • Olive Oil: Adds a bit of richness to the filo and helps in the baking process.

Optional:

  • Sesame Seeds: For a decorative touch and extra flavor, you can sprinkle sesame seeds on top before baking.
  • Lemon Zest: A bit of lemon zest in the filling adds a refreshing, zesty flavor that pairs wonderfully with the spinach.

How to Make Bourekaki: Step-by-Step Instructions

Making stuffed filo spirals with cheese and spinach is a bit of a time investment, but it’s worth every minute when you take that first bite. The key to making perfect bourekaki is to work carefully with the filo dough, ensuring that each spiral is crisp and flaky. Follow these simple steps, and you’ll have delicious Greek pastries ready in no time!

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté the Spinach: Heat a large pan over medium heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add Garlic: Add the minced garlic to the pan and cook for another minute, stirring frequently.
  3. Cook the Spinach: Add the fresh spinach to the pan and cook until wilted. If you’re using frozen spinach, make sure to squeeze out any excess water before adding it to the pan. Stir the spinach with the onion and garlic mixture for about 5 minutes until the spinach is fully cooked and any moisture has evaporated.
  4. Season: Remove the pan from the heat and season the spinach mixture with salt, pepper, and a pinch of dried oregano or fresh dill.
  5. Combine with Cheese: Let the spinach mixture cool slightly before adding the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, and an egg to the pan. Mix everything together until well combined. The egg will help bind the mixture and give it a creamy texture.

Step 2: Prepare the Filo Dough

  1. Prepare the Filo: Lay out a clean kitchen towel on your countertop and place a large sheet of parchment paper on top. Take the filo dough from the package and unroll it. Cover the dough with a damp towel to prevent it from drying out.
  2. Brush with Butter: Using a pastry brush, lightly brush each sheet of filo dough with melted butter. Stack the sheets as you go, creating a stack of about 4-5 sheets, which will form the base for your pastry spirals.

Step 3: Assemble the Bourekaki

  1. Fill the Filo: Place a generous spoonful of the spinach and cheese filling along one edge of the filo stack. Be careful not to overfill, as it will be harder to roll.
  2. Roll the Filo: Carefully fold the edges of the filo over the filling and roll the dough tightly into a log shape. Once you’ve rolled it up, twist the log into a spiral, securing the end underneath to keep it from unraveling.
  3. Brush with Butter: Once the spirals are assembled, brush the outside of each spiral with more melted butter to ensure they bake to a beautiful golden color.

Step 4: Bake the Bourekaki

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bake: Place the bourekaki spirals on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. If desired, sprinkle sesame seeds on top of the spirals for added crunch and flavor. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the filo is golden brown and crispy.
  3. Cool: Once the bourekaki are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. The filling will be hot, so allow them to rest for a few minutes to avoid burning your mouth.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

Bourekaki can be served warm or at room temperature. They make an excellent snack for parties, a light lunch, or even a delicious appetizer to start a Mediterranean-themed dinner. Pair them with a refreshing Greek salad and a glass of white wine, and you’ll have a delightful meal that captures the essence of Greece in every bite.

Tips for Perfect Bourekaki

  • Don’t Overfill: It’s important not to overstuff the filo dough. A little filling goes a long way, and too much will cause the spirals to break or be difficult to roll up.
  • Thaw the Filo Dough Properly: If you’re using frozen filo dough, make sure to thaw it thoroughly before using. Filo dough that’s not fully thawed can be hard to work with and may tear easily.
  • Use Fresh Spinach: While frozen spinach works in a pinch, fresh spinach will give the filling a more vibrant color and flavor. Be sure to remove any excess moisture after cooking the spinach.
  • Experiment with Other Fillings: While the spinach and cheese filling is classic, feel free to experiment with other ingredients. You could add sautéed mushrooms, ground meat, or herbs like mint for a different twist.
  • Make Ahead: Bourekaki can be prepared in advance and stored in the fridge for a day or two before baking. You can also freeze them before baking. Just make sure to wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. When ready to bake, simply place them directly from the freezer into a preheated oven and bake as usual.

Why You’ll Love Bourekaki

Bourekaki is a versatile and crowd-pleasing dish that brings the flavors of Greece to your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, preparing a light meal, or simply craving something savory and delicious, these stuffed filo spirals are the perfect choice. The crispy filo dough combined with the creamy spinach and cheese filling creates a mouthwatering bite every time.

The best part about making bourekaki is that it’s a relatively easy recipe to master, and the results are always impressive. So why not try making these Greek delights at home? They’re perfect for any occasion and sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection!

Enjoy your homemade bourekaki, and embrace the flavors of Greece!
