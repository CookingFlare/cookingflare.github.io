Hearty Nut-Free Beef Stew Recipe

3 min · 583 words

Welcome to another delicious recipe on our blog! Today, we’re going to share a hearty and nut-free beef stew recipe that will warm your soul and satisfy your hunger. Packed with tender beef, flavorful vegetables, and aromatic herbs, this stew is perfect for chilly evenings or as a comforting meal any time of the year. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and brown it on all sides. This will help seal in the flavors and create a rich base for the stew.

  2. Once the meat is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté them until they become fragrant and translucent.

  3. Add the sliced carrots, chopped celery, and diced tomatoes to the pot. These vegetables will add depth and texture to the stew. Stir everything together and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Return the browned beef stew meat to the pot and pour in the beef broth. The broth will provide the stew with a savory and rich base. Add the tomato paste, dried thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 1 ½ to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have developed. This slow cooking process will ensure that all the ingredients meld together beautifully.

  6. About 10 minutes before serving, add the frozen peas to the pot. The peas will add a pop of color and freshness to the stew. Stir them in and let them cook until they are heated through.

  7. Remove the bay leaf from the pot and discard it. Serve the beef stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley for a burst of freshness and added flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize this stew recipe by adding your favorite vegetables such as potatoes, mushrooms, or green beans.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can whisk together 2 tablespoons of flour with some water and add it to the pot during the last 30 minutes of cooking. This will create a luscious, thick gravy-like consistency.
  • Leftover beef stew can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for longer storage. Simply reheat it on the stovetop or in the microwave when ready to enjoy.

Conclusion

This hearty nut-free beef stew recipe is a perfect dish for any meat lover. With its tender chunks of beef, flavorful vegetables, and aromatic herbs, it’s a wholesome and satisfying meal that will warm your heart and nourish your body. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this recipe is sure to impress. So, give it a try and savor the deliciousness of homemade beef stew. Enjoy!
