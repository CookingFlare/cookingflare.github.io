Quick and Delicious Chinese Cashew Chicken


If you’re in the mood for a quick yet flavorful Chinese dish that will have you coming back for more, look no further than this mouth-watering Chinese Cashew Chicken. This dish is a beautiful balance of tender chicken, crunchy cashews, and a savory sauce that will satisfy your taste buds. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting friends for dinner, this recipe promises to be a hit!

Chinese Cashew Chicken is known for its simple ingredients, vibrant flavors, and ease of preparation. It’s the perfect option for busy weeknights when you want something homemade but don’t have hours to spend in the kitchen. Packed with protein from the chicken and healthy fats from the cashews, this dish is as nutritious as it is delicious.

In this post, we will walk you through everything you need to know about making this quick and delicious dish from scratch. We’ll cover the ingredients, step-by-step instructions, and a few helpful tips for perfecting your Cashew Chicken each time.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make this Chinese Cashew Chicken recipe, you won’t need any fancy or hard-to-find ingredients. The key to this dish is freshness and balance, so gather the following ingredients before you get started:

For the Chicken:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (for marinating)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for stir-frying)

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce (optional but adds a lovely sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (to thicken the sauce)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (or water for a lighter option)

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted cashews (lightly roasted if you prefer)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced thinly)
  • 1 medium onion (sliced thinly)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger (grated)
  • 2 green onions (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for stir-frying)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Chinese Cashew Chicken

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken. This step is essential as it infuses the chicken with flavor and helps tenderize it. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with 1 tablespoon of soy sauce and 1 tablespoon of cornstarch. Toss the chicken to coat it evenly, and let it sit for about 15 minutes.

The cornstarch will help create a light coating that gives the chicken a crisp exterior once it’s stir-fried.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

While the chicken is marinating, you can prepare the sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar, 1 tablespoon of hoisin sauce (if using), 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, and 1 teaspoon of cornstarch. Add the chicken broth and whisk everything together until smooth. Set aside for later.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Chicken

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken in a single layer. Don’t overcrowd the pan, so if necessary, work in batches. Stir-fry the chicken for about 4-5 minutes, until it’s golden brown and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

In the same pan, add a tablespoon of sesame oil. Add the sliced red bell pepper, onion, and garlic to the pan. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 2-3 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. Add the minced ginger and cook for another minute until fragrant.

At this point, you can also add the cashews to the pan. Stir everything together to ensure that the nuts get lightly toasted and mixed in with the vegetables.

Step 5: Combine Everything

Once the vegetables are cooked and aromatic, return the cooked chicken to the pan. Pour in the prepared sauce, stirring constantly to coat the chicken and vegetables evenly. Let the sauce simmer for 2-3 minutes until it thickens up and becomes glossy.

If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can add a little more cornstarch dissolved in water to achieve your desired consistency.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Before serving, sprinkle the dish with sliced green onions for a fresh burst of flavor and color. You can serve this dish over steamed white rice or brown rice for a complete meal.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Chinese Cashew Chicken!

Tips for Perfecting Your Chinese Cashew Chicken

1. Choose the Right Chicken

For the best texture, use boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs. Both cuts are tender and cook quickly, but chicken thighs offer a richer flavor. Just make sure to cut the chicken into even, bite-sized pieces so it cooks uniformly.

2. Customize the Veggies

While red bell pepper and onion are classic choices, feel free to add other vegetables like snow peas, broccoli, or baby corn. These vegetables can give the dish a nice crunch and boost the nutritional value.

3. Use Fresh Ginger and Garlic

Fresh ginger and garlic provide a vibrant, aromatic flavor that enhances the dish. If you’re short on time, you can use pre-minced ginger and garlic, but fresh is always better!

4. Control the Sauce Thickness

The cornstarch in the sauce helps thicken it, but if you prefer a thinner sauce, you can adjust the amount of cornstarch or add extra chicken broth. The key is to simmer the sauce until it’s glossy and coats the chicken perfectly.

5. Toast the Cashews for Extra Crunch

Cashews add a crunchy texture and nutty flavor to this dish, and lightly toasting them in the pan before adding them to the stir-fry helps bring out their natural oils and flavors. Just be careful not to burn them.

6. Serve with Steamed Rice

Chinese Cashew Chicken pairs beautifully with steamed white rice or brown rice. The fluffy rice soaks up the savory sauce, making every bite a satisfying experience.

Variations of Chinese Cashew Chicken

Chinese Cashew Chicken is quite versatile, and you can play around with different flavors and ingredients to make the dish your own. Here are some variations you can try:

1. Spicy Cashew Chicken

If you enjoy a bit of heat, add red pepper flakes, sriracha, or fresh chili peppers to the stir-fry. You can adjust the spice level to suit your preferences.

2. Cashew Chicken with Broccoli

For a more vegetable-heavy dish, add some steamed broccoli to the stir-fry. The broccoli will soak up the sauce, making it a delicious and nutritious addition.

3. Cashew Chicken with Tofu

For a vegetarian or plant-based version, replace the chicken with firm tofu. Press the tofu to remove excess moisture, cut it into cubes, and follow the same steps to stir-fry it until golden brown. The tofu will absorb the flavors of the sauce just like the chicken does.

4. Sweet and Sour Cashew Chicken

If you love the combination of sweet and sour flavors, add some pineapple chunks and a dash of rice vinegar to your sauce. This will give the dish a tangy, fruity twist that complements the rich, savory chicken and crunchy cashews.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Chinese Cashew Chicken is the perfect blend of sweet, savory, and crunchy, making it irresistible to most people. The flavors are bold but not overwhelming, and the dish is versatile enough to be enjoyed by those with different dietary preferences. Plus, the quick preparation time makes it ideal for busy weeknights when you want a homemade meal without the fuss.

The best part about this dish is that it’s customizable, so you can adjust it to your liking. Whether you prefer extra veggies, more spice, or a sweeter sauce, you can easily tweak the recipe to make it your own.

If you’ve never tried making Chinese food at home, this recipe is a great place to start. It’s simple, satisfying, and sure to become a family favorite!

Final Thoughts

Chinese Cashew Chicken is a crowd-pleasing dish that’s both quick and delicious. The crispy chicken, tender vegetables, and crunchy cashews create a wonderful mix of textures and flavors. Whether you’re looking for a comforting meal or a dish to impress guests, this recipe is sure to deliver.

With just a few basic ingredients and minimal prep time, you can create an authentic-tasting Chinese dish that rivals what you’d find at your favorite Chinese restaurant. So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and get ready to enjoy a delicious homemade meal that’s both easy to make and incredibly tasty!
