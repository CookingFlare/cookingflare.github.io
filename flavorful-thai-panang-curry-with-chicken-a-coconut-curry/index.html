Flavorful Thai Panang Curry with Chicken: A Coconut Curry


If you’re looking for a dish that encapsulates the vibrant flavors of Thailand, look no further than Panang Curry with Chicken. This rich and creamy coconut curry is known for its unique blend of spices and herbs, making it a favorite among curry enthusiasts. Unlike other Thai curries, Panang is thicker and has a more pronounced sweetness due to the use of coconut milk and the signature Panang curry paste. In this post, we will guide you through the process of creating this delectable dish at home, ensuring that each bite transports you to the bustling streets of Thailand.

What is Panang Curry?

Panang Curry, or “Phanaeng,” is a type of Thai curry that originates from the central region of Thailand. It is a variation of red curry, with a thicker consistency and a slightly sweeter flavor profile, thanks to the addition of coconut milk and palm sugar. The curry paste is made from a blend of fresh herbs and spices including lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and dried red chilies. Traditionally, it is made with beef or chicken, but you can easily customize it to suit your preferences.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create a delicious Thai Panang Curry with Chicken, gather the following ingredients:

For the Curry

  • 1 lb (450g) chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons Panang curry paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (Thai basil preferred)
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

For Homemade Panang Curry Paste (optional)

If you want to make your own curry paste, you will need:

  • 2-3 dried red chilies, soaked in warm water
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon galangal, minced (or ginger if not available)
  • 3-4 shallots, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp paste (optional)
  • Zest of 1 kaffir lime
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Panang Curry Paste

If you decide to make your own Panang curry paste, blend all the ingredients for the paste in a food processor until smooth. You may need to add a bit of water to achieve the desired consistency. Once blended, set the paste aside.

2. Cook the Chicken

In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

3. Create the Curry Base

In the same skillet, add the Panang curry paste and cook it for about 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously. This step is crucial as it helps to release the flavors of the spices and herbs in the paste.

4. Incorporate Coconut Milk

Once the curry paste is fragrant, pour in the coconut milk, stirring to combine. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5 minutes. This will enrich the flavors and create a creamy base for your curry.

5. Add Chicken and Vegetables

Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, followed by the fish sauce, palm sugar, and kaffir lime leaves. Stir well to ensure the chicken is coated in the curry sauce. Next, add the sliced red bell pepper and green beans, cooking for an additional 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender yet crisp.

6. Taste and Adjust

Taste your curry and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a sweeter curry, add a little more palm sugar. If you like it spicier, consider adding more curry paste or fresh chilies.

7. Serve

Once everything is well combined and heated through, remove the skillet from the heat. Serve your flavorful Thai Panang Curry with Chicken over a bed of steaming jasmine rice. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for an aromatic finish.

Tips for the Perfect Panang Curry

  • Coconut Milk: Use full-fat coconut milk for a richer and creamier texture. Light coconut milk can be used, but it may not yield the same luxurious consistency.
  • Fresh Ingredients: Whenever possible, use fresh herbs and spices. They significantly enhance the flavor of the curry.
  • Customization: Feel free to customize the dish by adding your favorite vegetables or proteins. Tofu, shrimp, or beef are excellent alternatives.
  • Heat Level: Adjust the spice level to your preference. If you enjoy a milder curry, use less curry paste or remove some seeds from the chilies before blending.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to meld and may taste even better the next day. Reheat gently on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving again.

Conclusion

Thai Panang Curry with Chicken is a delightful dish that brings the essence of Thai cuisine to your kitchen. The harmony of flavors and textures makes it a satisfying meal that can be enjoyed any day of the week. With its creamy coconut base and fragrant spices, this curry is sure to impress family and friends alike. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on this culinary adventure that will leave you craving more!

Enjoy this aromatic journey into Thai cooking, and don’t forget to share your creation with those you love. Happy cooking!
