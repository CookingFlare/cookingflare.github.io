Delicious Winter White Bean Chili


Winter is the perfect time to indulge in hearty and warming dishes, and what better way to do that than with a bowl of delicious winter white bean chili? This comforting and flavorful chili is a great alternative to the traditional red meat chili, offering a lighter and healthier option without compromising on taste. Packed with protein, fiber, and a variety of flavorful ingredients, this winter white bean chili is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic, diced carrots, celery, and red bell pepper to the pot. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Stir in the ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1-2 minutes to toast the spices and release their flavors.

  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, white beans, diced tomatoes, and frozen corn kernels. Bring the chili to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Once the chili has thickened slightly and the vegetables are tender, season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Ladle the winter white bean chili into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro.

  7. Serve the chili with your favorite toppings such as avocado slices, a dollop of sour cream, or a sprinkle of shredded cheese.

Tips and Variations

  • For added protein, you can stir in some cooked quinoa or shredded rotisserie chicken towards the end of cooking.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, blend a portion of the chili using an immersion blender before serving.
  • Experiment with different types of white beans such as cannellini beans or navy beans for a unique twist.
  • This chili can be made in a slow cooker for added convenience. Simply sauté the vegetables and spices as directed, then transfer everything to a slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours.

Winter white bean chili is a versatile dish that can easily be customized to suit your taste preferences. Whether you’re looking for a comforting weeknight dinner or a dish to impress guests at a gathering, this recipe is a must-try. The combination of wholesome ingredients and bold flavors makes this chili a standout choice for the winter season. Enjoy the warmth and satisfaction of this delightful winter white bean chili, and don’t be surprised if it becomes a regular feature on your menu rotation!
