How to Make Fresh Herb Cream Cheese Spreads


Fresh herb cream cheese spreads are a delightful way to elevate your bagels, sandwiches, or even serve as a dip for fresh vegetables and crackers. The beauty of making your own herb cream cheese is that you can customize it with your favorite herbs and flavors, ensuring each spread is uniquely yours. In this blog post, weâ€™ll explore a variety of recipes and tips for creating delicious fresh herb cream cheese spreads that will impress your family and friends.

The Basics of Cream Cheese Spreads

Cream cheese is a versatile base for many spreads and dips. Its rich, creamy texture pairs well with a variety of flavors, making it an excellent canvas for herbs, spices, and other ingredients. When making cream cheese spreads, the quality of your cream cheese matters. Opt for a full-fat variety for a creamy and smooth consistency. If you prefer a lighter alternative, low-fat cream cheese can also work, but the texture might be slightly different.

Essential Ingredients

To make a basic fresh herb cream cheese spread, you will need:

  • 8 ounces of cream cheese (softened to room temperature)
  • 1-2 tablespoons of fresh herbs (finely chopped; more on this later)
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic (minced, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice (for brightness)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

These ingredients can be easily adjusted based on your taste preferences or the herbs you have on hand.

Choosing Your Herbs

The choice of herbs can dramatically change the flavor profile of your cream cheese spread. Here are some popular herbs to consider:

  • Chives: Mild onion flavor; great for a classic spread.
  • Basil: Sweet and aromatic; pairs well with tomatoes and mozzarella.
  • Dill: Fresh and tangy; excellent with smoked salmon or in a dip.
  • Parsley: Bright and fresh; a versatile herb that works in many recipes.
  • Cilantro: Bold and citrusy; perfect for a southwest-inspired spread.
  • Thyme: Earthy and savory; great for a more complex flavor.

Feel free to mix and match these herbs or even add in some dried herbs for added flavor.

Basic Fresh Herb Cream Cheese Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Soften the Cream Cheese: Leave your cream cheese out at room temperature for about 30 minutes, or microwave it for about 10-15 seconds. You want it soft enough to blend easily but not melted.
  2. Chop the Herbs: Finely chop your chosen herbs. If using garlic, mince it as well.

Step 2: Mix the Ingredients

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the softened cream cheese, chopped herbs, minced garlic (if using), lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Using a spatula or a hand mixer, blend until smooth and well combined. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary.

Step 3: Chill and Serve

  1. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  2. Serve your fresh herb cream cheese spread with bagels, as a sandwich spread, or as a dip for fresh veggies and crackers.

Variations on Fresh Herb Cream Cheese Spreads

1. Smoked Salmon and Dill Spread

For a sophisticated twist, add:

  • 4 ounces of smoked salmon (finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill

Follow the same mixing steps. This spread is fantastic on bagels or as a dip for crackers.

2. Basil and Garlic Cream Cheese

For an Italian-inspired version, include:

  • 1/4 cup of fresh basil (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)

This creamy spread is perfect for bruschetta or as a filling for sandwiches.

3. Spicy Cilantro and Lime Spread

For a zesty kick, mix in:

  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder (adjust based on your heat preference)

This spread pairs beautifully with tortilla chips or as a topping for tacos.

4. Herb and Cheese Blend

Add an extra layer of flavor by incorporating different cheeses. Try:

  • 1/4 cup of crumbled feta or goat cheese along with your herbs for a tangy twist.

This variation works well on salads or as a topping for grilled meats.

Storage Tips

Fresh herb cream cheese spreads can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week. If you make multiple variations, be sure to label your containers to keep track of each flavor.

  3. Freeze: They can be frozen for up to three months.

Pairing Suggestions

Fresh herb cream cheese spreads are incredibly versatile. Here are some ideas on how to enjoy them:

  • Bagels and Toast: Spread on your favorite bagel or toast for a quick breakfast.
  • Sandwiches: Use as a spread in sandwiches for added flavor and creaminess.
  • Veggie Dip: Pair with fresh vegetables such as carrots, celery, and bell peppers for a healthy snack.
  • Charcuterie Boards: Include your spreads on a charcuterie board with crackers, meats, and cheeses for a delightful appetizer.
