Baking with Seasonal Produce: Fresh and Flavorful Recipes for Every Season


As the seasons change, so do the flavors and produce available to us. Baking with seasonal produce not only ensures that you’re using the freshest ingredients but also allows you to explore a variety of flavors throughout the year. In this blog post, we’ll dive into some delicious baking recipes that highlight the best of each season’s produce. So, put on your apron and let’s get started!

Spring: Bright and Refreshing Lemon Bars

Spring brings an abundance of bright and zesty lemons, perfect for adding a burst of flavor to your baked goods. One of our favorite recipes is Lemon Bars. The combination of tangy lemon filling and buttery crust is simply irresistible. To make these delightful treats, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, powdered sugar, and softened butter. Mix until the dough forms crumbles. Press the mixture into a greased 9x9-inch baking dish, creating an even layer. Bake the crust for 15 minutes, or until golden brown.

While the crust is baking, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, flour, lemon juice, and lemon zest until well combined. Pour the lemon mixture over the hot crust, then return the dish to the oven for an additional 20 minutes, or until the filling is set.

Once cooled, dust the lemon bars with powdered sugar and cut into squares. These refreshing treats are perfect for enjoying on a sunny spring day!

Summer: Juicy Berry Galette

When summer arrives, it’s time to indulge in the sweet and juicy flavors of berries. A Berry Galette is a rustic and beautiful dessert that showcases the vibrant colors and natural sweetness of summer fruits. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water
  • 2 cups mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon milk (for brushing)
  • Turbinado sugar (for sprinkling)

To start, combine the flour, sugar, and salt in a mixing bowl. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together.

Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, in a separate bowl, toss the berries with cornstarch, sugar, and lemon juice until well coated.

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface into a rough circle. Transfer the dough to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Spoon the berry mixture onto the center of the dough, leaving a border around the edges. Gently fold the edges of the dough over the filling, creating pleats as you go.

Brush the edges of the galette with milk and sprinkle with turbinado sugar for a golden crust. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the berries are bubbly.

Allow the galette to cool slightly before serving. Top it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful summer dessert!

Autumn: Spiced Apple Cider Donuts

Autumn brings cooler temperatures and the aroma of warm spices. What better way to embrace the season than with Spiced Apple Cider Donuts? These fluffy treats are packed with the flavors of apple and cinnamon, making them the perfect accompaniment to a cozy afternoon. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Cinnamon sugar for coating

In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.

In a separate bowl, combine the apple cider, melted butter, Greek yogurt, egg, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.

Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 350°F (175°C). Drop spoonfuls of batter into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the donuts from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.

While the donuts are still warm, roll them in cinnamon sugar until coated. Serve these spiced apple cider donuts with a warm cup of cider for the ultimate autumn treat!

Winter: Decadent Chocolate Peppermint Bark

Winter calls for indulgent and comforting treats. Chocolate Peppermint Bark is a classic holiday dessert that combines the richness of chocolate with the refreshing flavor of peppermint. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • 12 ounces white chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Crushed candy canes or peppermint candies

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Melt the dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water, stirring until smooth. Pour the melted dark chocolate onto the prepared baking sheet and spread it into an even layer using a spatula.

Place the baking sheet in the refrigerator for about 10 minutes, or until the dark chocolate is set.

In the meantime, melt the white chocolate in a separate heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water. Stir in the peppermint extract until well combined. Remove the baking sheet from the refrigerator and pour the melted white chocolate over the dark chocolate layer, spreading it evenly.

While the white chocolate is still melted, sprinkle crushed candy canes or peppermint candies over the top. Gently press the candies into the chocolate to ensure they adhere.

Return the baking sheet to the refrigerator for about 15 minutes, or until the chocolate is completely set. Once set, break the bark into pieces and store in an airtight container.

This decadent Chocolate Peppermint Bark is perfect for gifting or enjoying as a festive treat during the winter months!

Conclusion

Baking with seasonal produce allows us to celebrate the flavors and bounty of each season. From bright and refreshing lemon bars in the spring to indulgent chocolate peppermint bark in the winter, there’s a delicious and flavorful recipe for every season. So, embrace the changing seasons and get creative in the kitchen with these fresh and delightful baking recipes. Happy baking!
