Brown Butter Pecan Ice Cream: A Rich and Nutty Indulgence


There’s something undeniably luxurious about a scoop of smooth and creamy ice cream, especially when it’s infused with the rich and nutty flavors of brown butter and pecans. If you’re a fan of indulgent treats, then this Brown Butter Pecan Ice Cream recipe is guaranteed to satisfy your cravings. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of creating this delectable frozen dessert from scratch.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 ½ cups pecans, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Begin by browning the butter. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the butter turns a golden brown color and gives off a nutty aroma. This process usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Be careful not to burn the butter.

  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the chopped pecans to the brown butter. Stir until the pecans are well coated. Set aside to cool.

  3. In a separate saucepan, combine the milk and heavy cream. Heat over medium heat until the mixture begins to simmer. Remove from heat.

  4. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, egg yolks, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.

  5. Gradually pour the hot milk and cream mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling.

  6. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the custard thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes.

  7. Remove the custard from heat and strain it through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any lumps.

  8. Stir in the brown butter and pecan mixture, making sure it is evenly distributed throughout the custard.

  9. Cover the mixture and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the custard to chill thoroughly.

  10. Once the custard has chilled, transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.

  11. When the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a lidded container. Cover and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.

  12. Serve the Brown Butter Pecan Ice Cream in bowls or cones, and enjoy the creamy, nutty goodness!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can add a teaspoon of maple syrup or a splash of bourbon to the custard before churning.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, you can pulse the pecans in a food processor before adding them to the brown butter.
  • Experiment with different nuts, such as walnuts or almonds, to create your own unique flavor combinations.
  • Pair your Brown Butter Pecan Ice Cream with warm desserts like apple pie or chocolate brownies for a delightful contrast of temperatures and flavors.

Now that you have the recipe for this decadent Brown Butter Pecan Ice Cream, it’s time to put your culinary skills to the test. Whether you’re making it for a special occasion or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this rich and nutty ice cream is guaranteed to impress. So grab your ingredients and get ready to savor the creamy goodness of homemade ice cream!
