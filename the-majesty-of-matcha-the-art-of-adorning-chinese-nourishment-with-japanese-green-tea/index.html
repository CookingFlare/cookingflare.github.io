The Majesty of Matcha: The Art of Adorning Chinese Nourishment with Japanese Green Tea

Culinary Mastery: Preparing Chinese Dishes with Matcha A Twinkle in the Master Chef’s Eye

With a spirit of adventurous culinary exploration, we venture into the preparation of certain Chinese dishes adorned with the velvety hues of matcha. Prepare to be enchanted by the unparalleled experience of this innovative fusion, and let’s get cooking!

1. Matcha Ginger Chicken

Pairing the zesty flavors of ginger, the umami taste of chicken, and the unique character of matcha, this dish offers a whirlwind of tastes that captivates the senses.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 inch chunk of fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons of matcha
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 3 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and mix together in a bowl with ginger, matcha, half of the soy sauce and half of the sesame oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Cover and marinate for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator.

  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add the chicken pieces with the marinade. Cook for about 5-6 minutes or until done, turning occasionally to brown evenly.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining soy sauce and sesame oil. Add honey, and mix until well combined.

  4. Serve the cooked chicken over steamed rice, topped with a drizzle of the honey-soy sauce mixture for an explosion of flavors that’ll dance on your palate.

2. Matcha Green Tea Egg Noodles

Inspired by the famous Chinese dish, egg noodles don’t stand a chance without a Chinese recipe makeover featuring the undeniable presence of matcha.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz Chinese egg noodles
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of matcha
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a large pot, cook the egg noodles, according to package instructions, for about 3-4 minutes until tender. Drain water and set noodles aside. While noodles are cooking, bring another pot of water to a rolling boil.

  2. In the same pot, bring water to a boil again, add matcha powder, soy sauce, sesame oil, green onions, and garlic. Stir and reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for about 6-8 minutes until the sauce thickens.

  3. Add the noodles to the sauce mixture, tossing until thoroughly coated. Cook for another 1-2 minutes or until the noodles reach your desired tenderness.

  4. Transfer the Matcha Green Tea Egg Noodles to a serving dish and don’t forget to admire its tempting matcha hue and tantalizing aroma before digging in!
