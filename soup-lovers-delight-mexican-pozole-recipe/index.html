Soup Lover's Delight: Mexican Pozole Recipe


Pozole, a traditional Mexican soup, has a rich and hearty flavor that warms you up from the inside. Loaded with fresh components and full of nutritious ingredients, it’s essentially a one-pot meal that’s perfect for feeding a crowd. This authentic Mexican Pozole recipe is easy to follow and delivers a robust, savory taste that your loved ones will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Pork Shoulder or Trotter (cut into medium sized chunks)
  • 3 cups Pork Stock or Chicken Stock
  • 1 small Onion (diced)
  • 2 Garlic cloves (crushed)
  • 6 Hominy Corn (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 can Tomatoes (hand-crushed)
  • 1 Tbsp Lard, vegetable oil, or Coconut Oil (for cooking)
  • 1 Tbsp Dried Epazote (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp Homemade Chicken Bouillon
  • 1/2 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Oregano
  • 2 cups Radish Cubes
  • 1 cup Cilantro Leaves (chopped)
  • 2 wedges Lime
  • 2 Tbsp Chopped Onions (optional garnish)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Pepper Flakes (optional)
  • Salt and Black Pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by seasoning the pork shoulder or trotter with ground cumin, oregano, 1/4 tsp homemade chicken bouillon, and salt. Set aside for ten minutes.
  2. Heat lard or oil in a large pot over medium flame. If using the pork shoulder, sear its edges to create a crust.
  3. Once the lard or oil is heated, add the seasoned pork and stir until all sides are browned.
  4. Upon browning, add crushed garlic and onion to the pot. Stir until the onion is translucent.
  5. Pour in the pork stock or chicken stock and let the mixture simmer. Cook for about 1.5 hours or until the meat is tender.
  6. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, rinsed hominy corn, and 1/4 chicken bouillon. Continue simmering for another 15 minutes.
  7. If you wish to include dried epazote, add it now. Epazote is a traditional Mexican herb that you can find at most markets. If you can’t find it, don’t worry—the pozole will still be delicious.
  8. Halfway through the cooking process, taste the broth and adjust seasonings to your liking. You may need additional ground cumin or oregano, salt, or black pepper.
  9. Before serving, incorporate cubed radish, chopped cilantro, and lime wedges. Adjust the heat to suit your preference, adding red chili pepper flakes if you desire.
  10. Serve hot with lettuce leaves and tortilla chips on the side.

This Mexican Pozole is rich in flavor and perfect for a fun, satisfying meal! Remember, traditions bring people together, and Mexican Pozole is a prime example. Enjoy this hearty and scrumptious soup with your loved ones and make lasting memories around the dining table.

Why Is Pozole Such a Beloved Recipe?

Mexican Pozole originated from the word ‘pozzol’, which translates to ‘foam’ in Nahuatl, an Aztec language. pottery techniques in ancient Mesoamerican cultures produced a type of corn dough that would foam when mixed with water. This foamy dough was versatile, used for bread and even beer.

However, the origins of the soup itself are diverse, with different stories attributing its invention to various groups within Mexican history.

The soup has since evolved into various styles throughout the country. Recipe variations use different meat types, such as chicken or seafood, and other ingredients like tomatillos, chilies, or tobacco seeds. Additionally, every region and family in Mexico has their way of preparing this beloved soup.

However, one thing remains the same – the loyalty that people have for pozole. Whether enjoyed around the family table or at a feast with friends, Pozole soup brings people together.

Safety Tips for Cooking Pozole

While pork shoulder is traditional for making pozole, it can be a bit fatty. Here are some tips to tackle this issue:

  1. Brown the pork shoulder on all sides to reduce the fat content.
  2. Use a stovetop slow cooker or an Instant Pot for pressure cooking. This reduces cook time and makes the meat tender.
  3. Opt for leaner cuts, such as pork loin or tenderloin.

Remember that homemade chicken bouillon often has sodium content, so taste and adjust salt accordingly.

Ways to Enhance the Flavor of Your Pozole

  • Add a toasted piece of bread to the bottom of the blender when pureeing the tomato base. This captures bread’s flavor and adds richness to the soup.
  • Garnish your finished soup withthinly sliced sawmill gravy. This pantry staple adds depth of flavor.
  • For visual appeal, sprinkle chopped lettuce over your pozole.
  • Mexican oregano has a more potent flavor than Mediterranean oregano – use to enhance flavor.

That’s it! With this Mexican Pozole recipe, you too can create a flavorful and delectable meal your family and friends are sure to love. Enjoy!
