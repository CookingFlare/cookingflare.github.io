Korean Chili Paste Stir-Fried Noodles (Jajangmyeon)


If you’re looking to elevate your homemade noodle dishes, Korean Chili Paste Stir-Fried Noodles, also known as Jajangmyeon, is the perfect recipe to explore. This popular Korean dish is an explosion of flavors, combining the savory richness of black bean paste with the heat of chili and the satisfying bite of stir-fried noodles. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe will guide you step by step on how to make a delicious bowl of Jajangmyeon.

What is Jajangmyeon?

Jajangmyeon is a beloved Korean comfort food consisting of thick, chewy wheat noodles smothered in a savory, slightly spicy black bean paste sauce. While Jajangmyeon is traditionally made with a fermented black bean paste called chunjang, the addition of Korean chili paste, gochujang, gives the dish an exciting, spicy kick. This fusion of savory, spicy, and umami flavors has made Jajangmyeon a favorite in Korean households and a must-try dish for anyone interested in Korean cuisine.

This version of Jajangmyeon is made with a homemade sauce that incorporates both chunjang and gochujang, creating the perfect balance between deep, earthy flavors and a touch of heat. The dish is typically served with stir-fried vegetables and sometimes with tender pork or beef for added richness.

Key Ingredients for Korean Chili Paste Stir-Fried Noodles

To make your own Jajangmyeon at home, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Noodles:

  • 200g fresh wheat noodles (or substitute with any other noodle of your choice, such as udon or ramen)
  • Water (for boiling noodles)
  • Salt (to season the water)

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp chunjang (fermented black bean paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable stock (or water as a substitute)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp corn starch (for thickening the sauce)

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 100g pork belly or pork shoulder (or use beef, chicken, or tofu for a vegetarian option)
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced into thin half-moons
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (for stir-frying)
  • 1/4 cup water (if needed for stir-frying)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
  • 2-3 scallions, chopped (for garnish)

How to Make Korean Chili Paste Stir-Fried Noodles (Jajangmyeon)

Step 1: Prepare the Noodles

Start by cooking the noodles. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, adding a pinch of salt. Once the water is boiling, add the wheat noodles and cook according to the package instructions, usually about 5-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent the noodles from sticking.

Once cooked, drain the noodles in a colander and set aside. You can toss them with a little bit of sesame oil to prevent them from sticking together while you prepare the sauce.

Step 2: Prepare the Stir-Fry Sauce

While the noodles are cooking, prepare the sauce. In a small bowl, combine the chunjang, gochujang, soy sauce, sugar, and chicken or vegetable stock. Stir the mixture until the sauce is smooth and well combined. Set aside.

If you want a thicker sauce, mix the corn starch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry. You will add this later to thicken the sauce.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Meat and Vegetables

Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the pork belly (or your choice of meat) and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. If you’re using tofu, ensure it’s crispy on the outside before moving on to the next step.

Once the meat is cooked, add the chopped onions, carrots, zucchini, garlic, and ginger to the pan. Stir-fry the vegetables for 2-3 minutes until they soften slightly but retain some of their crunch. The garlic and ginger will release their fragrant aromas during this process.

Step 4: Add the Sauce to the Stir-Fry

Once the vegetables have softened, pour in the sauce mixture. Stir everything together, allowing the sauce to coat the meat and vegetables. Let the sauce simmer for 3-5 minutes until it thickens slightly and becomes fragrant.

If the sauce becomes too thick, add a splash of water to reach your desired consistency. For an extra touch of richness, you can add a teaspoon of sesame oil at this point for additional flavor.

Step 5: Combine the Noodles and Sauce

Now that your stir-fry and sauce are ready, itâ€™s time to combine everything. Add the cooked noodles to the pan with the sauce. Toss the noodles in the sauce until they’re evenly coated and heated through. This step ensures that every strand of noodle absorbs the savory sauce.

If needed, you can adjust the flavor at this point by adding a pinch of salt or a dash more soy sauce, depending on your preference. Stir-fry everything for 2-3 minutes to make sure the noodles are well combined with the sauce.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Once everything is ready, transfer the Jajangmyeon to serving bowls. Garnish with chopped scallions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added texture and flavor. For an extra burst of freshness, you can add a few slices of cucumber or a side of kimchi for contrast.

Tips for Perfect Jajangmyeon

  1. Choosing the Right Noodles: While wheat noodles are traditional for Jajangmyeon, you can also use other types of noodles like udon, ramen, or even gluten-free noodles if needed. Just ensure the noodles are chewy and hearty to hold up to the rich sauce.

  2. Balancing the Spice: The gochujang adds a wonderful heat to the dish, but you can adjust the spiciness to your taste. If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of gochujang or even omit it completely.

  3. Making It Vegetarian: For a vegetarian version of this dish, simply swap the pork for tofu, mushrooms, or even tempeh. The key is to have a flavorful protein that absorbs the sauce beautifully.

  4. Thickening the Sauce: The corn starch slurry is optional, but it gives the sauce a luxurious, thick texture that clings beautifully to the noodles. If you prefer a thinner sauce, feel free to skip it.

  5. Prepping Ahead: You can prepare the sauce and vegetables in advance. Store them separately in airtight containers and cook the noodles just before serving. This can save you time on busy days.
