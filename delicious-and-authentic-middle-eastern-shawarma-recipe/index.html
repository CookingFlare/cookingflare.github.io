Delicious and Authentic Middle Eastern Shawarma Recipe


Are you craving the rich and aromatic flavors of the Middle East? Look no further than this authentic and mouthwatering Middle Eastern shawarma recipe. Shawarma is a popular street food in the Middle East, known for its succulent, marinated meat and fragrant spices. In this recipe, we’ll delve into the traditional method of preparing shawarma, ensuring that every bite is a burst of authentic Middle Eastern flavors.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 2 lbs of boneless chicken thighs or beef sirloin
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Garlic Sauce:

  • 1 cup of mayonnaise
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Pita bread or flatbreads
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Sliced red onions
  • Pickles
  • Fresh parsley

Instructions

Marinating the Meat

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.

  2. Add the chicken thighs or beef sirloin to the marinade, ensuring that the meat is evenly coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.

Preparing the Garlic Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, and salt. Adjust the seasoning to your taste and refrigerate until ready to serve.

Cooking the Shawarma

  1. Preheat the grill or broiler to medium-high heat. If using a grill, thread the marinated meat onto skewers for easy grilling.

  2. Grill the marinated meat for 6-8 minutes on each side, or until it is cooked through and has a beautifully charred exterior. If using a broiler, arrange the meat on a baking sheet and broil for the same amount of time.

  3. Once cooked, transfer the meat to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes. Thinly slice the meat across the grain.

Assembling the Shawarma

  1. Warm the pita bread or flatbreads on the grill or in a skillet.

  2. Spread a generous amount of the prepared garlic sauce on the warmed bread.

  3. Arrange the sliced meat, tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, pickles, and fresh parsley on top.

  4. Roll the bread tightly to form a shawarma wrap, and secure it with parchment paper or foil.

Serving

  1. Serve the delicious and authentic Middle Eastern shawarma with extra garlic sauce on the side and enjoy the burst of flavors with each bite.

Conclusion

Indulge in the rich and aromatic flavors of the Middle East with this delicious and authentic shawarma recipe. Whether it’s a casual weeknight dinner or a gathering with friends and family, this Middle Eastern delight is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and transport yourself to the bustling streets of the Middle East with every mouthful of this homemade shawarma.
