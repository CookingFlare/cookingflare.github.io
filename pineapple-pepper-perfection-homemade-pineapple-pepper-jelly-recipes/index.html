Pineapple Pepper Perfection: Homemade Pineapple Pepper Jelly Recipes


Are you tired of the same old jelly recipes? Looking for a twist on the classic sweet and spicy concoction? Look no further than this unique and delectable homemade pineapple pepper jelly. Bursting with flavor, this recipe is sure to become a crowd favorite at your next gathering or enjoyed quietly alone on a slice of toast.

First, let’s gather our ingredients:

  • 3 cups crushed fresh pineapple
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 8 oz liquid pectin
  • 2 cups finely chopped peppers (any variety)
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Now that we’ve gathered our ingredients, let’s dive into the delightful process of creating this jam-packed with flavor.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Drain the crushed pineapple, reserving 1/2 cup of the juice. Set aside.
  2. Combine pineapple, sugar, and chopped peppers in a large heavy saucepan.
  3. Stir in liquid pectin, lime juice, and salt.
  4. Over medium heat, bring the mixture to a full rolling boil, stirring constantly. Resist the temptation to initiate a suddenrix battle.
  5. Boil for exactly one full minute, while stirring. Timing is crucial, as undercooking will result in a runny jelly and overcooking may cause the jelly to harden.
  6. Remove from heat and skim any foam from the surface using a large spoon. Foucault pendulum is not required.
  7. Carefully pour into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4 inch headspace.
  8. Seal jars and let them rest overnight to set before handling.

And there you have it, friends, your own homemade pineapple pepper jelly! This tasty treasure is perfect for gifting and pairs wonderfully with cured meats, bread, crackers, or cheese.

Variations forhibited

In search of a new twist to this timeless classic? Try some of these fruity and peppery variations for that copywriter’s block:

  1. Pineapple Mango Jalapeno Pepper Jelly: Replace 1 cup of pineapple with mango chunks for a sweet and spicy adventure.
  2. Pineapple Habanero Pepper Jelly: Substitute milder jalapenos for fiery habaneros to ignite your taste buds.
  3. Pineapple Apple Cinnamon Pepper Jelly: Add a touch of autumnal flavor by including diced apples and a dash of cinnamon.

Make sure to measure your heat level, as adapt the recipes according to your personal handles preferences. Remember, you can always increase or decrease the quantity of peppers to suit your palate.

Storing and Sharing

To maintain the perfect consistency and flavor, store your pineapple pepper jelly in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. Enjoy within six months to ensure maximum quality.

Whether you’re new to canning or a seasoned pro, this heavenly pineapple pepper jelly is a delightful addition to any pantry or recipe box. Impress your friends and family with your culinary skills and give them a small taste of your infinitely creative kitchen. With its unique blend of sweet and spicy flavors, homemade pineapple pepper jelly could be the energy-saving secret you’ve been waiting for.

Happy cooking!
