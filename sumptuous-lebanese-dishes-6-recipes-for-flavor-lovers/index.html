Sumptuous Lebanese Dishes: 6 Recipes for Flavor Lovers


Lebanon, a country known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant culinary scene, has a plethora of mouth-watering dishes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds. From hearty mezze to savory main courses, Lebanese cuisine is a perfect blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors. In this post, we’ll explore six sumptuous Lebanese dishes that are sure to delight even the most discerning palates.

Kibbeh Bil Sanieh

Kibbeh Bil Sanieh is a classic Lebanese dish that consists of ground meat and bulgur mixed with spices and herbs, shaped into small balls and then fried. This dish is often served as an appetizer or side dish, and is a staple at Lebanese gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground meat (beef, lamb, or a combination of both)
  • 1 cup bulgur
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, bulgur, onion, parsley, cilantro, olive oil, salt, black pepper, cumin, and paprika. Mix well with your hands until the ingredients are well combined.
  2. Cover the mixture and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. When ready to cook, shape the mixture into small balls, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Fry the balls in hot oil until they are golden brown and crispy.
  4. Drain the kibbeh on paper towels and serve hot.

Fattoush Salad

Fattoush Salad is a refreshing summer salad that is perfect for hot days. It is made with toasted pita bread, mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, and feta cheese, all tossed in a zesty lemon-tahini dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 2 cups toasted pita bread, cut into small pieces
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 large cucumber, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, toasted pita bread, tomatoes, cucumber, feta cheese, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, tahini, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Serve the salad immediately, garnished with additional parsley if desired.

Shish Taouk

Shish Taouk is a popular Lebanese street food that consists of marinated chicken skewers, grilled to perfection and served with a side of garlic sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 10 wooden skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes
  • Garlic sauce (recipe below)

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chicken, yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, salt, black pepper, and paprika. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly.
  2. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight to allow the chicken to marinate.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken onto the skewers, leaving a small space between each piece.
  4. Grill the skewers for 5-7 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Serve the skewers hot with garlic sauce for dipping.

Garlic Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, sour cream, garlic, and lemon juice. Mix well to combine.
  2. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Refrigerate the sauce for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Mujaddara

Mujaddara is a hearty and comforting Lebanese dish that consists of lentils and rice cooked in a flavorful broth. It is often served with a side of yogurt and garlic sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Yogurt and garlic sauce (recipes above)

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the lentils and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic, rice, salt, black pepper, cumin, and paprika to the skillet. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the cooked lentils to the skillet and stir to combine.
  5. Serve the mujaddara hot, garnished with parsley and accompanied by yogurt and garlic sauce.

Turnips with Garlic and Lemon

Turnips with Garlic and Lemon is a simple yet flavorful side dish that is perfect for accompanying many Lebanese dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large turnips, peeled and sliced into thin rounds
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the turnip slices to the skillet and cook until tender, about 5-7 minutes per side.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice and salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Pour the lemon juice mixture over the turnips and toss to combine.
  5. Serve the turnips hot, garnished with parsley.

Baklawa

Baklawa is a classic Lebanese dessert that consists of layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and honey. It is a sweet and indulgent treat that is perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (usually found in the freezer section of most supermarkets)
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 cup chopped pistachios
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. Thaw the phyllo dough according to the package instructions.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the chopped nuts, sugar, honey, melted butter, cinnamon, and cardamom.
  4. Layer the phyllo dough in a large baking dish, brushing each layer with melted butter.
  5. Sprinkle the nut mixture evenly over the phyllo dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  6. Repeat the layering process until all of the ingredients have been used, ending with a layer of phyllo dough on top.
  7. Bake the baklawa for 45-50 minutes, or until the phyllo dough is golden brown and the nuts are fragrant.
  8. Remove the baklawa from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

In conclusion, Lebanese cuisine is a rich and diverse culinary tradition that offers a wide range of flavors and textures to explore. From hearty mezze to savory main courses, and from sweet pastries to refreshing salads, there is something for every palate and occasion. Whether you are a seasoned foodie or just looking to try something new, these six sumptuous Lebanese dishes are sure to delight and inspire.
