Step-by-Step Thanksgiving Cranberry Tart Recipe


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering loved ones around the table, sharing stories, and indulging in dishes that highlight the season’s bounty. While turkey and stuffing often steal the spotlight, a dessert that’s both stunning and delicious can wrap up the meal on a perfect note. This cranberry tart recipe offers a harmonious blend of tartness and sweetness, making it the ideal conclusion to an unforgettable holiday dinner. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to creating desserts, this recipe is designed to help you create a show-stopping tart that showcases the vibrant flavors of cranberries.

Why Choose Cranberry for Thanksgiving?

Cranberries are synonymous with autumn and Thanksgiving. Their ruby-red hue is visually stunning, and their unique tart flavor complements rich, buttery desserts perfectly. Cranberries also pair beautifully with spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and clove, enhancing the festive character of your tart. As cranberries are harvested fresh during the fall, this tart makes the most of this seasonal superstar.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let’s gather the essentials. For maximum flavor and texture, opt for fresh cranberries if available, although frozen cranberries will work fine as well.

For the Tart Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, cold and diced
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the Cranberry Filling:

  • 12 ounces (340g) fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (50ml) water
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Optional Garnish:

  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
  • Whipped cream or vanilla ice cream

Step-by-Step Instructions

This Thanksgiving Cranberry Tart Recipe unfolds in two parts: crafting the tart crust and mastering the cranberry filling. Follow along for a comprehensive guide that ensures success.

Step 1: Make the Tart Crust

The crust serves as the foundation for your cranberry tart, so it’s essential to get it just right. This buttery, flaky crust elevates the tart’s texture and complements the filling’s bold flavor.

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.

  2. Incorporate Butter: Add the cold, diced butter to the bowl. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, work the butter into the dry mixture until you achieve pea-sized crumbs.

  3. Add Egg & Water: In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Gradually pour this mixture into the flour-and-butter mixture, stirring gently until the dough comes together. Avoid overmixing to maintain a tender crust.

  4. Shape the Dough: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead gently to form a disc. Wrap the disc in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  5. Roll Out the Dough: Once chilled, roll out the dough to approximately 1/8-inch thickness. Carefully transfer it to a 9-inch tart pan, pressing the dough into the edges and trimming any overhang. Prick the base of the crust with a fork to prevent bubbling.

  6. Blind Bake: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and paper. Bake for an additional 5 minutes or until golden. Set aside to cool.

Step 2: Prepare the Cranberry Filling

The filling is the soul of this tart, bursting with vibrant cranberry flavor and warm spices.

  1. Cook the Cranberries: In a medium saucepan, combine cranberries, sugar, water, orange zest, orange juice, and the cinnamon stick. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the cranberries start to burst and create a sauce-like consistency (about 8-10 minutes).

  2. Thicken the Mixture: In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water until dissolved. Add the cornstarch slurry to the cranberry mixture and stir continuously for 2 minutes, allowing it to thicken.

  3. Remove Cinnamon Stick: Once the filling is thickened, remove the cinnamon stick and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the cranberry filling to cool slightly before assembling your tart.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake the Tart

  1. Fill the Crust: Pour the cranberry filling into the prepared tart crust, using a spatula to spread it evenly.

  2. Optional Decoration: If desired, create a decorative lattice with leftover dough or shape small cutouts (like leaves or stars) to place on top.

  3. Bake the Tart: Return the tart to the oven and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is bubbly and the crust is golden brown.

Step 4: Cool and Serve

  1. Cool: Allow the tart to cool completely in the pan before removing it to prevent breaking the crust.

  2. Garnish: Dust with powdered sugar for a classic look or serve with a dollop of whipped cream or scoop of vanilla ice cream for added indulgence.

  3. Slice and Enjoy: Cut into wedges and share with family and friends – the sweet-tart flavor will have everyone asking for seconds!

Pro Tips for Making the Best Thanksgiving Cranberry Tart

  • Use Fresh Cranberries: Fresh cranberries offer unbeatable flavor. If using frozen, don’t thaw them beforehand.
  • Take Your Time: Chilling the dough and filling thoroughly before baking helps achieve the perfect texture.
  • Balance the Flavors: The orange juice and zest add a touch of sweetness and citrusy brightness to offset the tartness of the cranberries.

Make This Tart Your Own

Feel free to adapt this recipe to your preferences. Consider adding chopped nuts, such as pecans, to the filling for extra texture or drizzling a white chocolate glaze over the top for a decadent twist.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving Cranberry Tart is the perfect marriage of seasonal flavors and textures. The combination of a buttery crust and tangy cranberry filling will leave your guests raving about your culinary skills long after the last bite. Thanksgiving dinner deserves a spectacular send-off, and with this tart on the table, you’re sure to end the feast on a high note.

Prepare, bake, and enjoy this vibrant dessert that celebrates the season in every delicious bite. Will this cranberry tart become a new tradition for your Thanksgiving table? Let’s make this holiday sweeter than ever. Happy baking!
