Flavorful Slow Cooker Beef Bourguignon Recipe


Beef Bourguignon, a classic French dish, is a celebration of rich flavors, tender meat, and aromatic vegetables. Traditionally, it requires hours of slow cooking on the stovetop, but with the help of a slow cooker, you can achieve that same depth of flavor with minimal effort. This recipe will guide you through making a flavorful Slow Cooker Beef Bourguignon that will impress your family and friends alike.

Ingredients

For the Beef Bourguignon

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 cups cremini or button mushrooms, halved
  • 2 cups red wine (preferably Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Equipment Needed

  • Slow cooker
  • Large skillet
  • Wooden spoon
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Cutting board and knife

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

  1. Season the Beef: In a large bowl, combine the beef chuck, flour, salt, and black pepper. Toss until the beef is evenly coated. This step not only adds flavor but also helps thicken the sauce during cooking.

  2. Brown the Beef: In a large skillet over medium-high heat, add the chopped bacon. Cook until crispy, then remove it from the skillet and set it aside, leaving the drippings in the pan. In the same skillet, add the seasoned beef in batches, browning on all sides. This step is crucial as it develops flavor through the Maillard reaction.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Once the beef is browned, transfer it to the slow cooker. Add the sautéed onion, garlic, sliced carrots, and halved mushrooms to the slow cooker.

Step 3: Build the Sauce

  1. Create the Broth: In a bowl, whisk together the red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, bay leaves, and Worcestershire sauce. Pour this mixture over the beef and vegetables in the slow cooker, ensuring everything is well coated.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Bourguignon

  1. Set the Slow Cooker: Cover the slow cooker with its lid and set it to low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-6 hours. The low and slow method allows the flavors to meld beautifully, resulting in a tender and flavorful dish.

Step 5: Finish and Serve

  1. Final Touches: Once the cooking time is complete, remove the bay leaves and taste the stew. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

  2. Serve: Ladle the Beef Bourguignon into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. This dish pairs wonderfully with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or buttered noodles to soak up the delicious sauce.

Tips for the Perfect Beef Bourguignon

  • Wine Selection: While any good quality red wine will work, choosing a wine that you enjoy drinking will enhance the dish’s flavor.
  • Beef Quality: For the best results, opt for well-marbled beef chuck, as the fat will render during cooking, keeping the meat moist and tender.
  • Veggie Variations: Feel free to add other vegetables like potatoes, parsnips, or pearl onions to customize your Beef Bourguignon.
  • Make Ahead: This dish tastes even better the next day, making it ideal for meal prep. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days.

Storing and Reheating

Storing

  • To store your Beef Bourguignon, let it cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. It can be kept in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 3 months.

Reheating

  • To reheat, simply warm it up in a saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally until heated through. If the sauce has thickened too much, add a splash of beef broth or water to loosen it.

Nutritional Information

This Beef Bourguignon recipe serves about 6-8 people. Here’s an approximate breakdown of the nutritional information per serving:

  • Calories: 450
  • Protein: 38g
  • Carbohydrates: 20g
  • Fat: 24g

Conclusion

Making a flavorful Slow Cooker Beef Bourguignon is not only easy but also rewarding. The slow cooking process melds the flavors beautifully, resulting in a rich, hearty dish that’s perfect for cozy family dinners or special occasions. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe will guide you through the steps to create a classic French masterpiece right in your own kitchen. So gather your ingredients, set up your slow cooker, and prepare to indulge in the warm and comforting flavors of Beef Bourguignon!
