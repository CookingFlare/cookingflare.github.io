10 Must-Try Leftover Casserole Recipes


Leftovers are a blessing and a puzzle all at once. Anyone who’s faced a fridge full of yesterday’s meals knows the challenge: how to turn those extra bits and pieces into a fresh, delicious dish without the monotony of reheating. Enter casseroles—versatile, hearty, and endlessly customizable. These comforting dishes not only revive your leftovers but also elevate them into satisfying meals your whole family will appreciate.

Below, you’ll find ten inventive casserole recipes designed specifically to breathe new life into your leftover ingredients. Whether you’re working with roasted chicken, mashed potatoes, pasta, or vegetables, these casseroles will inspire you to reduce food waste while enjoying a flavorful dinner.

1. Classic Chicken and Rice Casserole with a Twist

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 2 cups cooked rice (white, brown, or wild)
  • 1 cup steamed mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 cup cream of mushroom or cream of chicken soup
  • ½ cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs or crushed crackers for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix chicken, rice, vegetables, soup, sour cream, and half the cheese.
  3. Add garlic powder, salt, and pepper, stirring to combine everything.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish.
  5. Sprinkle the remaining cheese and breadcrumbs/cookie crumbs on top.
  6. Bake for 25–30 minutes or until bubbly and golden on top.

This casserole is a perfect vehicle for leftover chicken and rice, transforming them into a creamy, comforting dish with a crunchy topping.

2. Hearty Beef and Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked ground beef or shredded roast beef
  • 2 cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (green beans, corn, carrots)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, sauté onion until translucent, then add beef and mix in Worcestershire sauce and beef broth. Heat through.
  3. Stir in frozen vegetables and cook until just warmed.
  4. Spread the beef mixture evenly in a casserole dish.
  5. Top with a layer of mashed potatoes, smoothing out with a spoon.
  6. Sprinkle shredded cheese on top.
  7. Bake uncovered for 20-25 minutes, until cheese is melted and beginning to brown.

This casserole is akin to a shepherd’s pie remix using leftovers, rich in flavor and quick to assemble.

3. Creamy Tuna and Pasta Bake

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked pasta (penne, rotini, or macaroni)
  • 1 can tuna, drained and flaked
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli or asparagus
  • 1 cup cream of celery or cream of mushroom soup
  • ½ cup mayonnaise or cream cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss or cheddar cheese
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of dried dill or parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Combine cooked pasta, tuna, vegetables, soup, mayonnaise, and half the cheese in a mixing bowl.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs.
  4. Transfer to a greased casserole dish and top with remaining cheese.
  5. Bake for about 25 minutes, until bubbly and slightly golden on top.

Pasta casseroles like this are fantastic for using leftover noodles and canned fish, turning pantry staples into a creamy, wholesome meal.

4. Vegetarian Southwest Black Bean and Corn Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice or quinoa
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • ½ cup shredded pepper jack cheese
  • ½ cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix rice or quinoa, black beans, corn, tomatoes, onion, sour cream, and spices.
  3. Pour mixture into a baking dish and sprinkle cheese over the top.
  4. Bake for 20–25 minutes, until cheese melts and edges bubble.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This colorful casserole showcases how combining leftovers can create bold, southwestern-inspired comfort food.

5. Baked Ziti with Sausage and Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked ziti or penne pasta
  • 2 cups cooked Italian sausage, crumbled
  • 2 cups cooked spinach or kale, drained
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1½ cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or oregano, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine pasta, sausage, vegetables, marinara, and ricotta cheese.
  3. Mix well and season with fresh herbs, salt, and pepper.
  4. Transfer to a baking dish, sprinkle mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes until bubbly and cheese is golden.

Baked ziti is an Italian favorite that’s perfect for using day-old pasta and leftover meats, turning them into a cheesy, hearty casserole.

6. Creamy Broccoli and Cheese Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 2 cups steamed or roasted broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • ½ cup cream cheese, softened
  • ¼ cup milk
  • ½ cup crispy fried onions or breadcrumbs for topping
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix potatoes, broccoli, cheddar, sour cream, cream cheese, and milk until creamy.
  3. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer mixture to a greased dish and top with crispy onions or breadcrumbs.
  5. Bake until bubbly and golden on top, about 30 minutes.

This casserole reinvents leftover mashed potatoes with bright broccoli and melty cheese for a dish that’s both creamy and crunchy.

7. Leftover Turkey and Stuffing Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chopped leftover turkey
  • 2 cups leftover stuffing or dressing
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce (optional)
  • 1 cup cream of chicken soup or turkey gravy
  • ½ cup shredded Swiss or mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine turkey, stuffing, gravy or soup, and cranberry sauce (if using).
  3. Pour mixture into a casserole dish, spreading evenly.
  4. Sprinkle cheese on top.
  5. Bake for 20–25 minutes until heated through and cheese melts.

This recipe takes classic leftover turkey dinner components and melds them into a comforting casserole that’s easy and delicious.

8. Vintage Tuna Noodle Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked egg noodles
  • 1 can tuna, drained
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup cream of mushroom soup
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar or Swiss
  • ½ cup crushed potato chips or breadcrumbs for topping
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix noodles, tuna, peas, soup, milk, and half the cheese.
  3. Season to taste.
  4. Place in casserole dish and top with remaining cheese and crushed chips or breadcrumbs.
  5. Bake 25-30 minutes until hot and topping is crispy.

A timeless classic that transforms pantry staples and leftover noodles into a creamy, crunchy meal crowd-pleaser.

9. Sausage and Vegetable Gratin Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked sausage, sliced or crumbled
  • 2 cups assorted leftover roasted vegetables (zucchini, peppers, onions)
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes or polenta
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère or cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix sausage, vegetables, and mashed potatoes or polenta.
  3. Pour mixture into a casserole dish.
  4. Pour cream evenly on top, sprinkle with cheese, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Bake 30 minutes until golden and bubbling.

This gratin-style casserole uses leftover roasted vegetables and sausage to create an indulgent, creamy dish that’s elegant but easy.

10. Mediterranean Eggplant and Chickpea Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups roasted or sautéed eggplant cubes
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or couscous
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine eggplant, chickpeas, quinoa, tomatoes, garlic, oregano, and half the feta.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer to a casserole dish, sprinkle remaining feta on top.
  5. Bake for 25 minutes until heated through.

Inspired by Mediterranean flavors, this casserole uses leftovers to create a light yet protein-packed vegetable and grain dish with tangy feta.

Final Thoughts on Leftover Casserole Magic

Casseroles are an amazing way to stretch your leftover meals further without sacrificing taste or texture. These recipes demonstrate how simple fridge staples—be it chicken, mashed potatoes, pasta, or vegetables—can be transformed into crowd-pleasing dishes with just a few pantry ingredients and some creativity.

Next time you peek inside your fridge and think, “What can I do with this?” consider one of these comforting casseroles. They’re not only practical but also delicious, making every meal feel fresh and satisfying.

Happy cooking!
