Savory and Sweet: 7 Recipes for Filipino Pancit


Filipino cuisine is known for its rich flavors, vibrant spices, and mouthwatering dishes that blend different cultures and traditions. One of the most beloved Filipino noodle dishes is Pancit, a staple at family gatherings and celebrations. This delightful dish is a unique combination of savory and sweet, with various flavors and textures that will leave you craving for more. In this post, we'll delve into the world of Filipino Pancit, exploring seven mouthwatering recipes that showcase the dish's versatility and charm. Recipe 1: Classic Pancit Bihon with Vegetables Pancit Bihon is a staple Filipino noodle dish made with thin rice flour noodles, vegetables, and a savory sauce. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit bihon noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, and bean sprouts). Add 1 cup of shrimp, 1 cup of pork, and 1 cup of chicken to the pan, cooking until the meat is browned. Stir in 1 tablespoon of patis (fish sauce), 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of vinegar. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a sprinkle of green onions and a squeeze of calamansi (Filipino lime). Recipe 2: Lucky Pancit Canton with Shrimp and Pork Pancit Canton is a variation of the classic Pancit Bihon dish, made with thick wheat flour noodles and often served as a luxurious treat. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit canton noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of shrimp and 1 cup of pork to the pan, cooking until the meat is browned. Stir in 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon of hoisin sauce, and 1 tablespoon of soy sauce. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a sprinkle of green onions and a squeeze of calamansi. Recipe 3: Spicy Pancit Palabok with Sizzling Sisig Pancit Palabok is a spicy variation of the classic Pancit dish, made with thin rice flour noodles and a savory sauce flavored with chili peppers. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit palabok noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of ground pork to the pan, cooking until browned. Stir in 1 tablespoon of chili garlic sauce, 1 tablespoon of patis (fish sauce), and 1 tablespoon of vinegar. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a sprinkle of green onions and a side of sizzling sisig (Filipino-style tapas). Recipe 4: Pancit Miki with Chicken and Vegetable Skewers Pancit Miki is a variation of the classic Pancit Bihon dish, made with thick wheat flour noodles and served with chicken and vegetable skewers. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit miki noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of chicken breast to the pan, cooking until browned. Stir in 1 tablespoon of patis (fish sauce), 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of vinegar. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a side of chicken and vegetable skewers (such as chicken, pork, and vegetables marinated in patis, soy sauce, and vinegar). Recipe 5: Sweet Pancit with Mango Salsa and Grilled Shrimp Pancit with mango salsa and grilled shrimp is a unique twist on the classic dish, blending sweet and savory flavors. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit bihon noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of grilled shrimp to the pan, cooking until pink and pinky. Stir in 1 tablespoon of honey, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of lime juice. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a side of mango salsa (such as diced mango, red onion, and cilantro mixed with lime juice and honey). Recipe 6: Pan-Seared Pancit with Prawns and Green Chili Sauce Pan-seared pancit with prawns and green chili sauce is a flavorful and colorful dish that showcases the versatility of Filipino Pancit. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit bihon noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of prawns to the pan, cooking until pink and pinky. Stir in 1 tablespoon of green chili sauce, 1 tablespoon of patis (fish sauce), and 1 tablespoon of vinegar. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a sprinkle of green onions and a squeeze of calamansi. Recipe 7: Filipino-Style Pancit with Menudo and Fried Egg Filipino-style pancit with menudo and fried egg is a hearty and flavorful dish that showcases the richness of Filipino cuisine. To make this recipe, start by cooking 1 cup of pancit bihon noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of mixed vegetables. Add 1 cup of menudo (Filipino-style stew made with liver, tomatoes, and garlic) to the pan, cooking until the sauce thickens. Stir in 1 tablespoon of patis (fish sauce), 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of vinegar. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss everything together. Serve hot with a fried egg on top and a sprinkle of green onions. Conclusion Filipino Pancit is a dish that embodies the warm hospitality and rich flavors of Filipino cuisine. From its classic version to creative twists, Pancit is a dish that will leave you wanting more. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner, these 7 recipes will guide you through the world of Filipino Pancit, showcasing its versatility and charm. So go ahead, grab your ingredients, and start cooking!
