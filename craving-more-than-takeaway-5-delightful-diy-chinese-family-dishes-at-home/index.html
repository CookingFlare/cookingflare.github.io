Craving More Than Takeaway? 5 Delightful DIY Chinese Family Dishes at Home


Are you craving that elusive restaurant quality Chinese cuisine at home? Look no further! This collection of five treasured family recipes is perfect for those who want to embark on a culinary adventure in the comfort of their own kitchens. So, put on your chef’s hat, grab your wooden spoons, and let’s dive right into satisfying that Chinese food craving.

1. Deliciously Delectable Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao Chicken, also known as Gong Bao Chicken, is a spicy, stir-fried dish made with diced chicken, peanuts, vegetables, and chili peppers.

Ingredients:

  • 600g boneless, skinless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili bean sauce
  • ½ cup unsalted peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • ¼ cup green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons dried chilis, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken with the cornstarch, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine. Mix until the chicken is evenly coated.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the remaining soy sauce, sugar, and chili bean sauce. Set aside.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add peanuts and fry until golden brown, then remove and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining oil, followed by the garlic. Cook until fragrant, then add the chicken pieces. Stir-fry until chicken is cooked.
  5. Add the bell pepper, green onions, and dried chilies to the wok. Stir-fry for about 3 minutes.
  6. Pour in the sauce and bring it to a simmer. Cook for one more minute, then return the peanuts to the wok.
  7. Give everything a good toss and serve immediately with steamed rice.

2. Dazzling Dumplings: Pan-Fried Pork and Chive Dumplings

Pan-fried dumplings, also known as shengjianbao, are crispy, juicy, and utterly satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chives, finely chopped
  • 300g ground pork
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 17 round dumpling wrappers
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the ground pork with chives, garlic, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, cornstarch, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  2. Place a dumpling wrapper on your palm and scoop a tablespoon of the pork mixture into the center. Dampen the edges of the wrapper with water, then fold and pleat the dumpling, sealing the edges.
  3. Repeat until all the dumplings are assembled. Refrigerate for about 20 minutes.
  4. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Add about 6-8 dumplings and cook until the bottoms are golden brown, around 3-4 minutes.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of water to the frying pan, then cover with a lid. Allow the dumplings to steam until the water evaporates and the tops are cooked through, around 5-6 minutes.
  6. Serve these steamy little packages with your favorite dipping sauce, such as soy sauce or chili oil.

3. Royal Braised Beef and Tendon

A delectable yet hearty dish that appeals to the adventurous diner.

Ingredients:

  • 300g beef tendon, sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 400g beef, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 3 cups vegetable or beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced into thick pieces
  • 1 star anise
  • 3 black cardamom pods
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the beef and tendon with the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and one tablespoon of cornstarch. Mix until well coated, and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic, ginger, and allow to sizzle.
  3. Add the whole spices (star anise, cardamom pods, and bay leaves), stirring until aromatic.
  4. Add the marinated beef and tendon, and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Pour in the broth, bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Braise for 2 hours, checking occasionally to add more broth if needed.
  6. Once the beef and tendon are tender, remove the whole spices. Add sugar and cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce, simmering until slightly thickened.
  7. Season with salt and pepper, then garnish with chopped scallions before serving.

4. Sweet & Sour Spare Ribs

A comforting, sugary-sour dish that’s perfect for sharing with family or hosting a small feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork spare ribs, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the spare ribs with the soy sauce and set aside.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the pineapple juice and sugar until the sugar is completely dissolved.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add the ribs in batches and cook until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining oil, followed by garlic and onion. Cook until fragrant and onion is translucent. Return the ribs to the wok.
  5. Pour in the pineapple juice mixture and bring to a simmer. Cover, reduce heat, and cook for around 30 minutes, or until the meat is tender.
  6. In a bowl, whisk together the pineapple juice and cornstarch. Pour the slurry into the wok and cook until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. Add the sliced bell pepper, cover, and cook until softened. Enjoy the vibrant and scrumptious dish while still hot, with steamed rice.

5. Authentic Crispy Beer Battered Chicken Wings

A mouthwatering appetizer packed with flavor, these beer-battered chicken wings promise to astound your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 900g chicken wings, separated into drumettes and flats
  • 1 cup beer, at room temperature
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable or canola oil, for frying
  • ½ cup sliced green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the chicken wings with a handful of salt and set aside.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the beer, flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper until smooth. Let the batter rest for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Pour enough oil into a large, heavy bottomed pot, and heat to 375°F (190°C) using a thermometer.
  4. Pat the wings dry with paper towels before dipping them into the batter. Fry in two batches and do not overcrowd the pot.
  5. Cook until deep golden brown and crispy, 10-12 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon, and drain on a wire rack or paper towel-lined baking sheet.
  6. Season with coarse salt, and garnish with sliced green onions before serving.

As you embark on this delightful culinary adventure, you’ll soon find that creating these beloved Chinese dishes in your home kitchen is not only possible but also rewarding. With these mouthwatering recipes now at your fingertips, you’ll be eager to impress family and friends with your newly-acquired cooking skills. Happy cooking!
