Rhubarb Rummy: 5 Rhubarb Punchy Juice & Smoothie Recipes


Spring is in full bloom, and so is Mother Nature’s bounty of rhubarb – the vibrant crimson stalks that instantly breathe life into any dish. If you’ve been wondering what to do with this scrumptious vegetable-fruit hybrid, why not indulge in some refreshingly tangy concoctions, like punchy juices and smoothies? Today, we’ve got five delightful rhubarb recipes that will leave your taste buds tingling, ready to be whipped up in the comfort of your own kitchen.

  1. Rhubarb Ginger Beet Juice

Who says a healthy juice has to be boring? Our first recipe combines the sharp flavor of rhubarb with the spicy kick of ginger and the earthy sweetness of beetroot. Packed with antioxidants, this colorful combination will truly energize you on a lazy weekday morning or a weekend brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 stalks of rhubarb
  • 1 large beetroot
  • ½ inch piece of ginger
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 cup water

Instructions: Step 1: With a sharp knife, chop up the rhubarb, beet, and ginger into smaller pieces. Step 2: Place all ingredients into a juicer, and voilà! You have a vibrant cup full of goodness.

  1. Strawberry Rhubarb Protein Smoothie

For those who crave a post-workout treat, we have a protein-packed smoothie that will help you recover without compromising on taste. Combining rhubarb, strawberries, protein powder, and yogurt, we created the perfect balance of tartness and creaminess.

Ingredients:

  • 2 stalks of rhubarb
  • 1 cup of strawberries
  • ½ cup of plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 scoop of your protein of choice
  • ½ cup of almond milk
  • A handful of ice

Instructions: Step 1: Blend the rhubarb and strawberries together until you get a puree-like consistency. Step 2: Add the Greek yogurt, protein powder, and almond milk. Blend until smooth. Step 3: Add ice cubes to the mixture, and blend again until you reach your desired thickness. Step 4: Pour into a glass, garnish with a slice of strawberry, and enjoy!

  1. Rhubarb Lemonade Mocktail

When in the mood for a refreshing, grown-up mocktail, go for this zesty rhubarb lemonade with a touch of rosemary – a light and fruity drink that’s perfect for a picnic or a summer gathering with friends.

Ingredients:

  • 2 stalks of rhubarb
  • Juice of 3 lemons
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • A pinch of salt
  • A sprig of rosemary (for garnishing)

Instructions: Step 1: Chop the rhubarb into small pieces, and place it along with honey, salt, and half a cup of water in a saucepan. Step 2: Bring the mixture to a boil, then simmer for about 15 minutes or until the rhubarb breaks down. Step 3: Let it cool, then strain the mixture into a large pitcher to remove rhubarb pieces. Step 4: Add lemon juice and the remaining half cup of water to the pitcher. Step 5: Stir well, taste, and adjust the sweetness if needed. Step 6: Garnish with a sprig of rosemary, serve in glasses filled with ice, and indulge!

  1. Rhubarb Mango Smoothie Bowl

If you’re in the mood for a visually appealing treat, a smoothie bowl will save the day. The combination of sweet, luscious mango and tart rhubarb creates a fantastic flavor contrast. The best part? You can customize your toppings to your heart’s content!

Ingredients:

  • 2 stalks of rhubarb
  • 1 ripe mango
  • 1 cup of almond milk
  • Toppings of your choice (e.g. granola, chia seeds, berries, etc.)

Instructions: Step 1: Cut rhubarb into small pieces and blend with half a cup of almond milk until smooth. Step 2: Cut the mango, removing stone and skin, and slice it into chunks. Step 3: Blend the mango with the remaining half cup of almond milk until smooth. Step 4: Combine the two purees, pour into a bowl, and add your desired toppings. Step 5: Enjoy your vibrant and scrumptious creation!

  1. Rhubarb Orange Sorbet Mocktail

Get ready to delight your taste buds with a bright, tangy sorbet mocktail that resembles summer at its finest. A soothing blend of rhubarb and orange, this sorbet left us wanting more from the very first spoonful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 stalks of rhubarb
  • Juice of 2 oranges
  • A sprinkle of sugar
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions: Step 1: Chop up rhubarb into small pieces and place them in a saucepan. Step 2: Add orange juice, sugar, and salt. Cook the mixture on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, or until the rhubarb softens. Step 3: Remove from heat and allow it to cool completely. Step 4: Once cooled, pour the mixture into an ice-cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Step 5: Transfer the churned sorbet to a container and place it in the freezer until firm. When ready to serve, scoop the sorbet into glasses and enjoy!

Conclusion

So, grab your apron, gather the ingredients, and prepare to embark on a rhubarb-filled culinary adventure. From revitalizing juices to invigorating smoothies, each of these recipes offers a contrasting range of flavors, making the most of this versatile and seasonal bounty. Whether you opt for a light, refreshing beverage or a more indulgent, protein-packed treat, you’ll undoubtedly be left feeling reenergized and inspired to explore further with this tangy kitchen staple. Happy cooking!
