Cooking for Two: 7 Tasty and Simple Casseroles


When you’re cooking for just two people, finding recipes that strike a balance between simplicity, flavor, and just the right portion size can sometimes be a challenge. That’s where casseroles shine. They’re not only easy to put together but also incredibly versatile, with endless flavor combinations to suit every palate. From cheesy favorites to hearty classics, these seven casserole recipes will bring comfort and joy to your table. Plus, they’re designed to avoid excessive leftovers, though you might be tempted to save a little for lunch the next day.

1. Creamy Chicken and Broccoli Bake

This creamy casserole is a wholesome and satisfying choice, featuring tender chicken, fresh broccoli, and a luscious sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, cubed
  • 2 cups fresh broccoli florets
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. Steam the broccoli florets until just tender and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the cubed chicken over medium heat until no longer pink. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  4. In the casserole dish, layer the cooked rice, chicken, and steamed broccoli.
  5. Pour the heavy cream evenly over the layers, then top with shredded cheddar cheese.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top. Serve warm.

2. Sweet Potato Shepherd’s Pie

A healthier spin on the classic comfort dish, this shepherd’s pie uses a creamy sweet potato topping for a twist of sweetness paired with savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/4 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes until tender, then mash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground meat until browned. Add onions and carrots, cooking until softened.
  4. Stir in tomato paste and broth, simmering until thickened. Mix in the peas.
  5. Transfer the meat mixture to a greased small baking dish. Spread the mashed sweet potatoes evenly over the top.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes, then broil for 2-3 minutes to lightly brown the top.

3. Cheesy Bacon and Potato Casserole

With crispy bacon, gooey cheese, and tender potatoes, this dish is comfort food at its best.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 4 strips of cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. Layer the potato slices in the dish, seasoning lightly with salt and pepper between layers.
  3. Sprinkle the crumbled bacon over the potatoes. Pour the heavy cream evenly over the layers.
  4. Top with shredded mozzarella cheese and a pinch of smoked paprika.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

4. Spinach and Feta Pasta Bake

This Mediterranean-inspired casserole is both light and full of flavor, making it perfect for a quick and nutritious weeknight meal.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ounces penne or rigatoni pasta
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3/4 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. Cook the pasta according to package instructions, then drain.
  3. In the prepared dish, combine pasta, spinach, feta, cherry tomatoes, marinara sauce, and oregano. Mix gently.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until heated through and slightly golden on top.

5. Beef Enchilada Casserole

This casserole captures all the flavors of traditional enchiladas without the fuss of rolling tortillas, making it perfect for two.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 4 small tortillas, cut into triangles
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce
  • 1/2 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/4 cup diced green onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. In a skillet, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain any excess grease.
  3. Layer the tortilla triangles, cooked beef, enchilada sauce, and cheese in the casserole dish, repeating until ingredients are used up.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes. Top with diced green onions before serving.

6. Tuna Noodle Casserole

A classic comfort food staple, this tuna casserole is flavorful, creamy, and satisfyingly simple for a dinner duo.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup egg noodles
  • 1 can (5 ounces) tuna, drained
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. Cook the egg noodles according to package instructions, then drain.
  3. In a bowl, mix the noodles, tuna, peas, soup, milk, and half the cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer the mixture to the prepared dish and top with the remaining cheese.
  5. Bake for 25 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top.

7. Baked Zucchini and Ricotta Casserole

This light and veggie-forward casserole is perfect for those who want something fresh yet comforting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a small casserole dish.
  2. Layer half of the zucchini slices in the dish. Spread a layer of ricotta cheese, then sprinkle with Parmesan, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add a thin layer of marinara sauce, then repeat the layers.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the zucchini is tender and the casserole is golden on top.

Conclusion

Cooking for two doesn’t have to mean taking shortcuts on flavor or variety. These tasty and simple casseroles ensure you’re enjoying homemade meals designed just for two people, without an overload of leftovers. Whether you’re craving something hearty, cheesy, or veggie-forward, these recipes will inspire you to make the most of your meals with minimal prep and maximum flavor. Ready to dig in and embrace the magic of casseroles? Let the cooking begin!
