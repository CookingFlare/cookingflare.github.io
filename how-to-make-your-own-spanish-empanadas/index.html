How to Make Your Own Spanish Empanadas


Empanadas are one of the most beloved traditional dishes in Spanish cuisine, characterized by their delicious filling wrapped in a flaky pastry crust. Originating from Galicia in Spain, these delightful pockets can be found in various forms across Latin America, each region adding its unique twist to the recipe. Making your own Spanish empanadas at home can be a rewarding experience that not only satisfies your taste buds but also allows you to impress family and friends with your culinary skills. In this guide, we will take you step-by-step through the process of crafting these savory treats, from the dough to the filling, and even some tips for perfecting your empanadas.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup cold water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar (white or apple cider)

For the Filling (Spanish Beef Empanadas):

  • 1 pound ground beef (or chicken for a lighter option)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (green or red)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup green olives, chopped (optional)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Additional flour for dusting

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt, mixing them well to ensure an even distribution.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the chilled butter pieces to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, cold water, and vinegar. Gradually add this mixture to the flour-butter combination, mixing until a dough begins to form.

  4. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. Avoid over-kneading, as this can lead to tough empanadas.

  5. Chill the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out later.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. Cook the Meat: In a large skillet over medium heat, add the ground beef. Cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spatula as it cooks. Drain excess fat.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: Add the chopped onion, garlic, and bell pepper to the skillet. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Season the Mixture: Stir in the ground cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.

  4. Add Optional Ingredients: If using, fold in the chopped olives and hard-boiled eggs. Remove the filling from heat and let it cool completely.

Step 3: Assemble the Empanadas

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to about 1/8-inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 4-6 inches in diameter) to cut out circles of dough.

  3. Fill the Empanadas: Place a tablespoon of the cooled filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the empanadas to burst during baking.

  4. Seal the Empanadas: Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together to seal, then use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch.

  5. Apply Egg Wash: In a small bowl, beat the egg. Brush the tops of the empanadas with the egg wash to achieve a golden color when baked.

Step 4: Bake the Empanadas

  1. Arrange on Baking Sheet: Place the empanadas on the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between each one.

  2. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the empanadas are golden brown and flaky.

  3. Cool and Serve: Once baked, remove the empanadas from the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes. Serve warm, garnished with fresh parsley or cilantro.

Tips for Perfect Empanadas

  • Experiment with Fillings: While beef is a classic choice, feel free to get creative with your fillings. Chicken, cheese, vegetables, or even sweet fillings like fruit can make delicious alternatives.

  • Make Ahead: Empanadas can be made ahead of time and frozen before baking. Just place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, freeze until solid, then transfer them to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you like heat, add diced jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne pepper to your filling for an extra kick.

  • Serve with Dipping Sauces: Empanadas are fantastic on their own, but serving them with dipping sauces like chimichurri, salsa, or aioli can elevate the experience.

Conclusion

Making your own Spanish empanadas at home is not only a fun culinary project but also a chance to enjoy a taste of Spain right in your kitchen. With a flaky, buttery crust and a variety of flavorful fillings to choose from, empanadas can be customized to suit any palate. Whether you serve them at a party, as a snack, or as an impressive appetizer, these delicious pastries are sure to be a hit. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on this delightful cooking adventure! Enjoy your homemade empanadas and savor the rich flavors of Spanish cuisine.
