How to Make Korean Sweet Soy Sauce Chicken (Dakjjim)


Korean cuisine is celebrated worldwide for its bold flavors, hearty dishes, and vibrant culinary traditions. One standout dish that captures the essence of Korean cooking is Dakjjim, or Korean Sweet Soy Sauce Chicken. This irresistible braised chicken recipe combines tender, marinated poultry with a symphony of sweet and savory flavors that are absolutely mouthwatering. Perfect for weeknight dinners, special occasions, or even meal prep, this dish is easy to make and guaranteed to impress.

Why Dakjjim Stands Out

Dakjjim is a popular comfort food in Korea, combining savory chicken with a complex blend of sweet soy sauce, aromatic garlic, spicy peppers, and balanced seasonings. What makes Dakjjim so special is its rich, glossy sauce that clings to tender bite-sized pieces of chicken and hearty root vegetables, creating a filling and satisfying dish. Traditionally served as part of a Korean meal alongside steamed rice and banchan (side dishes), Dakjjim holds its own as a standalone main dish that delights the tastebuds of anyone who tries it.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s what you’ll need to prepare delicious Korean Sweet Soy Sauce Chicken:

Chicken

  • 1.5 – 2 lbs of chicken (either chicken drumettes or thighs, bone-in for maximum flavor)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons of flour or cornstarch (optional; helps thicken sauce during cooking)

Seasoning Base

  • ½ cup soy sauce (use a good quality Korean soy sauce for authentic flavor)
  • 3 tablespoons sugar (white or brown sugar both work well)
  • 2 tablespoons honey (for extra sweetness and depth)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (adds a nutty aroma to the dish)
  • 4 – 6 cloves garlic, minced or finely chopped
  • 2-inch piece of fresh ginger, finely sliced or minced
  • 1 tablespoon Korean rice wine or mirin (optional; helps tenderize the chicken)

Vegetables

  • 1 medium onion, diced into large pieces
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 2 to 3 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or button mushrooms work well)
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish

Optional Add-Ins

  • 1 to 3 dried red chili peppers or fresh Korean chili peppers (for a mild kick)
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper or white pepper
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) for extra spice
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Liquid

  • 2 to 2.5 cups of water (or chicken broth for added richness)

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by thoroughly washing the chicken pieces under cold running water and patting them dry with paper towels. If you prefer, you may lightly coat the chicken with flour or cornstarch to achieve a slightly thicker sauce later on. This step is optional, but it gives the sauce a silky consistency. Set the chicken aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

Peel and chop the carrots, potatoes, and onions into bite-sized chunks. Slice the mushrooms into thick pieces. Having the vegetables prepped ahead of time ensures they cook evenly while adding depth to the dish.

Step 3: Create the Sauce

Combine soy sauce, sugar, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, ginger, and rice wine (if using) in a medium bowl. If you enjoy a bit of heat in your dish, add Korean chili flakes and chopped red chilies to the mix. Stir everything together until the sugar dissolves completely.

Step 4: Sear the Chicken

Heat a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add a drizzle of sesame oil and allow it to warm up. Place the chicken in the pan and sear for 2 to 3 minutes on each side until lightly browned. This step locks in flavor and prepares the chicken for braising.

Step 5: Add the Vegetables and Sauce

Once the chicken is seared, add all the prepared vegetables to the pan, spreading them evenly around the chicken. Pour the sweet soy sauce mixture over the top, ensuring all the ingredients are evenly coated. Add 2 to 2.5 cups of water or chicken broth to the pan, which will blend with the seasoning to form the braising liquid.

Step 6: Simmer to Perfection

Cover the pan with a lid and reduce the heat to medium. Allow the dish to simmer for 25 to 30 minutes, occasionally stirring to prevent sticking. The chicken should be tender and fully cooked, while the vegetables should soften and absorb the sweet soy sauce. If you want a thicker sauce, remove the lid during the last 5 to 10 minutes to let the liquid reduce.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning if needed, adding a touch more honey or soy sauce for balance. Once ready, sprinkle chopped green onions and sesame seeds over the top. Serve the dish hot with steamed white rice to soak up the flavorful sauce.

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Dakjjim

  • Marinate for Extra Flavor: For even more intense flavor, marinate the chicken in the soy sauce mixture for 1 to 2 hours before cooking. This lets the seasoning penetrate deep into the meat.
  • Customize the Spice Level: While Dakjjim has a mild sweetness, you can heat things up by adding extra chili flakes or gochujang (Korean fermented chili paste).
  • Use Chicken Broth Instead of Water: If you have chicken broth on hand, use it as your liquid base to enhance the richness of the dish.
  • Balance Sweetness and Savory: Adjust the amount of sugar and soy sauce to suit your taste preferences. Some prefer a sweeter version, while others lean toward a saltier profile.
  • Make It a One-Pot Meal: Feel free to add other vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or baby corn to make this dish even more hearty and colorful.

Serving Suggestions

Dakjjim pairs beautifully with steamed white or brown rice. The sticky soy sauce glaze is perfect for scooping up with grains, making each bite even more satisfying. Consider serving this dish alongside traditional Korean side dishes such as kimchi, seasoned spinach, or pickled radish to round out the meal. If you’re entertaining guests, a light cucumber salad or japchae (stir-fried glass noodles) adds a refreshing and complementary touch.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have leftovers, transfer them to an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for up to three days. When reheating, add a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce and prevent the chicken and vegetables from drying out. Dakjjim also freezes well, making it a great option for batch cooking or meal prepping.

Final Thoughts

Creating Korean Sweet Soy Sauce Chicken (Dakjjim) at home is not only rewarding but also a wonderful way to experience the rich flavors and traditional techniques of Korean cooking. With simple ingredients and easy preparation steps, this dish quickly becomes a family favorite, offering comfort and indulgence in every bite. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner exploring Korean cuisine for the first time, Dakjjim is guaranteed to become a staple in your recipe rotation. So grab your ingredients and get ready to make a flavorful masterpiece!
