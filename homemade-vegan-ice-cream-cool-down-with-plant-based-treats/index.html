Homemade Vegan Ice Cream: Cool Down with Plant-Based Treats


When the temperature rises, there’s nothing like a refreshing scoop of ice cream to quench your thirst and satisfy your sweet tooth. Traditionally, ice cream is made with dairy products, but what if you’re lactose intolerant or simply prefer a plant-based diet? Fear not, dear vegan friends! With a few simple ingredients and some creativity, you can whip up your own delicious homemade vegan ice cream.

The Magic of Plant-Based Creaminess

Vegan ice cream may seem like an oxymoron, but it’s actually a clever combination of plant-based ingredients that mimic the creamy texture of traditional ice cream. The key players in this recipe are:

  • Coconut milk: Rich in healthy fats, coconut milk adds a luxurious creaminess to the ice cream.
  • Cannellini beans: These mild, creamy beans help to thicken the mixture and provide a smooth texture.
  • Cashew cream: Soaked cashews blended with water create a creamy, dairy-free substitute for heavy cream.

Basic Recipe: Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s cover the basics. You’ll need:

  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup cashews
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla bean powder (optional)

Step 1: Soak the Cashews

Rinse the cashews and soak them in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain and rinse the cashews again.

Step 2: Blend the Cream Base

In a blender or food processor, combine the coconut milk, cannellini beans, soaked cashews, maple syrup, water, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend on high speed until the mixture is smooth and creamy.

Step 3: Chill the Mixture

Transfer the mixture to a glass bowl or airtight container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the mixture to chill.

Step 4: Churn the Ice Cream

Once the mixture is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can also pour the mixture into a 9x13 inch baking dish and freeze for about 2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes until the desired consistency is reached.

Step 5: Add the Vanilla Bean Powder (Optional)

If you’re using vanilla bean powder, stir it in just before serving to give your ice cream an extra boost of flavor and aroma.

Tips and Variations

  • For a creamier texture, add an extra 1-2 tablespoons of cashew cream or coconut cream.
  • Experiment with different flavor combinations by adding cocoa powder, nuts, or fruit purees to the mixture.
  • If you prefer a lighter consistency, add a little more water or coconut milk to the mixture.

Conclusion

Homemade vegan ice cream is a game-changer for those who thought they had to sacrifice taste and texture for a plant-based diet. With this basic recipe and a few simple ingredients, you can create a wide range of delicious and refreshing flavors to cool down with all summer long. So go ahead, get creative, and indulge in the creamy, dreamy world of vegan ice cream!
