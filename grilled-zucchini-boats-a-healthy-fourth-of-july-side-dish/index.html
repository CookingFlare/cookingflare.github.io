Grilled Zucchini Boats: A Healthy Fourth of July Side Dish


Summer is in full swing, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious outdoor cooking. If you’re looking for a healthy and tasty side dish to accompany your Fourth of July feast, look no further than Grilled Zucchini Boats. These vibrant and flavorful boats are not only visually appealing but also packed with nutrients. Plus, they’re a great way to make use of the abundance of zucchini that is readily available during this time of year.

Ingredients

To make these Grilled Zucchini Boats, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large zucchinis
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. While the grill is heating up, prepare the zucchinis by cutting them in half lengthwise. Scoop out the flesh from the center of each zucchini half, leaving a 1/4 inch thick shell. Save the scooped-out flesh for later use.

  2. Brush the zucchini shells with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place the zucchini shells on the preheated grill, cut side down. Grill for about 5 minutes, or until the zucchini shells are slightly charred and tender.

  3. In the meantime, chop the scooped-out zucchini flesh into small pieces. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped zucchini and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Once the zucchini flesh is cooked, remove it from the heat and transfer it to a mixing bowl. Add the cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, and fresh basil to the bowl. Mix everything together until well combined.

  5. Remove the grilled zucchini shells from the grill and let them cool slightly. Once cooled, fill each zucchini shell with the tomato and feta mixture, pressing it down gently.

  6. Return the filled zucchini boats to the grill and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Remove the zucchini boats from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional fresh basil leaves, if desired.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled Zucchini Boats make a fantastic side dish for your Fourth of July celebration. They pair well with grilled meats, such as chicken or steak, and can also be enjoyed on their own as a light and healthy vegetarian option.

For a complete meal, serve these zucchini boats alongside a fresh green salad or some grilled corn on the cob. The combination of flavors and textures will surely impress your guests and leave them wanting more.

Conclusion

Grilled Zucchini Boats are a delicious and nutritious addition to any summer cookout. With their vibrant colors and fresh flavors, they are sure to be a hit at your Fourth of July gathering. So fire up the grill, get creative with your fillings, and enjoy this healthy and satisfying side dish. Happy grilling!
