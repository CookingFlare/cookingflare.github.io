10 Simple Ways to Cook Chicken for Dinner


Chicken is a staple in countless kitchens around the world, celebrated for its versatility, affordability, and ability to take on almost any flavor profile you can imagine. Whether you’re cooking for your family, meal prepping for the week, or planning a casual dinner with friends, chicken is always a reliable option that can be transformed in a myriad of ways. To simplify your weeknight meals, we’ve compiled ten easy and delicious ways to cook chicken for dinner—each method bursting with flavor and requiring minimal effort.

1. Classic Pan-Seared Chicken Breast

Pan-seared chicken is one of the quickest and easiest methods to prepare chicken. It results in a golden crust on the outside with juicy, tender meat inside. To make it, season boneless chicken breasts with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Heat a splash of olive oil or butter in a skillet over medium-high heat, and sear the chicken for 4–6 minutes per side until fully cooked. You can serve it with a simple side salad, roasted potatoes, or steamed veggies for a wholesome dinner.

2. Oven-Baked Chicken Thighs

Baking chicken thighs in the oven is a foolproof way to achieve juicy, flavorful results without standing by the stove. Rub bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs with a mix of olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic, fresh thyme, and a pinch of chili powder for a subtle kick. Arrange them on a parchment-lined baking sheet and roast in a 400°F oven for 35–40 minutes. The crispy skin and rich flavors make this dish a fan favorite, especially when paired with creamy mashed potatoes or roasted vegetables.

3. Slow Cooker Chicken Stew

If you like meals that simmer and develop rich flavors throughout the day, a slow cooker chicken stew is the way to go. Start by placing boneless chicken thighs or breasts into the slow cooker along with diced onions, carrots, celery, garlic, and potatoes. Add chicken broth, bay leaves, dried herbs like parsley and thyme, and a splash of white wine or Worcestershire sauce. Cook on low for 6–8 hours or high for 3–4 hours until the chicken and vegetables are tender. Serve hot with a side of crusty bread.

4. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken

Marinated and grilled chicken never disappoints. Whisk together olive oil, fresh lemon juice, minced garlic, parsley, rosemary, and oregano to make a flavorful marinade. Place boneless chicken breasts or thighs in the marinade for at least 30 minutes, then grill over medium-high heat for 6–7 minutes per side. You’ll end up with smoky, juicy chicken that pairs perfectly with Mediterranean-style couscous or a fresh tabbouleh salad.

5. One-Pan Chicken Stir Fry

For nights when time is tight, chicken stir fry is your best friend. Slice boneless chicken breasts into thin strips and season them with soy sauce, ginger, and a touch of cornstarch. Heat a wok or skillet with sesame oil and stir-fry the chicken until golden brown. Toss in a colorful mix of veggies like bell peppers, broccoli, snap peas, and carrots, then add a sauce made of soy sauce, honey, and a splash of rice vinegar. Serve over steamed rice or noodles for a quick, balanced dinner.

6. Homemade Chicken Alfredo

Creamy, comforting chicken Alfredo is always a hit. Start by pan-searing thinly sliced chicken breasts seasoned with Italian herbs, garlic, and salt. Prepare Alfredo sauce by melting butter and whisking in heavy cream, grated Parmesan cheese, and minced garlic, until smooth. Toss cooked fettuccine pasta with the sauce and the sliced chicken, and garnish with fresh parsley for a warm, satisfying dinner.

7. Crispy Chicken Tenders

Chicken tenders are great for kids and adults alike. Begin by cutting chicken breasts into strips. Dip each strip in beaten eggs, then coat them in a mixture of breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, and your favorite seasonings like paprika and garlic powder. Bake in a 425°F oven for 20–25 minutes or fry in oil until golden. Serve with honey mustard, ketchup, or your favorite dipping sauce alongside a side of French fries or a fresh green salad.

8. Spicy Chicken Tacos

Taco night gets even better with spicy chicken filling. Dice chicken breasts or thighs into bite-sized pieces and season them with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, and a bit of smoked paprika. Cook the chicken in a skillet with a drizzle of oil until well browned. Serve in warm tortillas and top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, avocado slices, and a dollop of sour cream or salsa. Add lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

9. Easy Sheet Pan Chicken Fajitas

Sheet pan meals make cleanup a breeze, and these chicken fajitas are no exception. Slice chicken breasts and a mix of colorful bell peppers and onions into strips. Coat everything in olive oil, lime juice, cumin, chili powder, and garlic powder. Spread it evenly on a sheet pan and roast at 425°F for 20–25 minutes. Serve the fajita mixture in warm tortillas with toppings like guacamole, sour cream, and cilantro.

10. Chicken and Rice Casserole

Warm, comforting, and packed with flavor, chicken and rice casserole is ideal for family-style dinners. Combine uncooked white rice, chicken broth, cream of mushroom soup, and a mix of spices like garlic and onion powder in a casserole dish. Nestle seasoned chicken breasts on top and sprinkle with shredded cheese. Cover with foil and bake at 375°F for 45 minutes, then uncover and bake for another 10–15 minutes until the rice is tender and the cheese is melted.

Final Thoughts

Chicken is a blank canvas that can adapt to countless recipes, cuisines, and flavors, which makes it a cornerstone of quick, flavorful dinner ideas. Whether you prefer it grilled, baked, slow-cooked, or stir-fried, the instructions above offer something for everyone and every meal prep situation. Try these ten easy chicken cooking methods to keep your weeknight dinners exciting, satisfying, and absolutely delicious. No matter which recipe you choose, these foolproof ideas are sure to make your chicken dinners the highlight of your week. Happy cooking!
