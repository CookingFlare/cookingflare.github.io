Spanish Inspired Patatas Bravas with Aioli Recipe


If you’re looking to spice up your culinary repertoire with a dish that embodies the spirit of Spanish cuisine, look no further than Patatas Bravas. This beloved tapas dish, characterized by its crispy potatoes and spicy tomato sauce, is a staple in bars and restaurants across Spain. Paired with a creamy, garlicky aioli, Patatas Bravas offer a delightful combination of flavors and textures that are sure to impress your family and friends. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of Patatas Bravas, delve into the recipe, and provide tips for creating the perfect dish at home.

A Brief History of Patatas Bravas

Patatas Bravas, which translates to “fierce potatoes,” is thought to have originated in Madrid in the mid-20th century, although variations of the dish can be found throughout Spain. Traditionally served as a tapa, Patatas Bravas are made from cubed potatoes that are fried until golden and crispy, then topped with a spicy sauce. The dish has become a symbol of Spanish cuisine, celebrated for its robust flavors and comforting qualities.

The sauce used in Patatas Bravas can vary by region, with some opting for a simple tomato sauce, while others incorporate ingredients like paprika, garlic, and chili. Regardless of the sauce, the key to a great Patatas Bravas lies in the potatoes themselves—crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside.

Ingredients for Patatas Bravas

To make an authentic Spanish-inspired Patatas Bravas with aioli, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Patatas Bravas:

  • 2 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet work well)
  • 4 cups of vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder

For the Bravas Sauce:

  • 1 cup of canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

For the Aioli:

  • 1 large egg (room temperature)
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Lemon juice (to taste)

Preparing the Patatas Bravas

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

  1. Peel and Cut: Start by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into 1-inch cubes. Try to keep the pieces uniform to ensure even cooking.

  2. Soak the Potatoes: Place the cubed potatoes in a bowl of cold water for about 30 minutes. This helps to remove excess starch and will lead to a crispier texture when frying.

  3. Dry the Potatoes: After soaking, drain the potatoes and pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels. Removing moisture is key to achieving that perfect crunch.

Step 2: Fry the Potatoes

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or a deep fryer, heat the vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to ensure accuracy, as the right temperature is crucial for frying.

  2. Fry in Batches: Carefully add the potatoes to the hot oil in batches, avoiding overcrowding the pot. Fry them for about 6-8 minutes or until they are golden brown and crispy.

  3. Drain the Potatoes: Use a slotted spoon to remove the potatoes from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Season with salt immediately.

Step 3: Make the Bravas Sauce

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become soft and fragrant (about 3-4 minutes).

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Cook for about 5 minutes until the sauce starts to thicken.

  3. Season the Sauce: Add the red wine vinegar, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir well and season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to simmer for another 5-10 minutes to develop the flavors.

Step 4: Prepare the Aioli

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the egg, Dijon mustard, red wine vinegar, and minced garlic.

  2. Blend the Aioli: Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, blend the ingredients while slowly drizzling in the olive oil. Continue blending until the mixture emulsifies and thickens to a creamy consistency.

  3. Season: Taste the aioli and season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice as desired. Adjust the flavors to your liking.

Assembling the Dish

Once the potatoes are fried, the bravas sauce is ready, and the aioli is prepared, it’s time to assemble your dish.

  1. Plate the Potatoes: In a serving dish, arrange the crispy patatas bravas.

  2. Top with Sauce: Generously drizzle the bravas sauce over the potatoes, ensuring each piece is well-coated.

  3. Add Aioli: Finish off the dish by drizzling the aioli on top. For an extra touch, you can serve additional aioli on the side for dipping.

  4. Garnish: If desired, garnish with fresh parsley or a sprinkle of paprika for added color and flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Patatas Bravas

  • Use the Right Potatoes: For the best texture, choose starchy potatoes like Yukon Gold or Russet, which become fluffy on the inside while achieving a crispy exterior.

  • Control the Oil Temperature: Maintaining the correct oil temperature is crucial. If the oil is too hot, the potatoes will cook too quickly and may burn. If it’s too cool, they’ll absorb too much oil and become greasy.

  • Experiment with Spices: Feel free to adjust the spices in the bravas sauce to suit your taste. You can add more cayenne for heat or even some fresh herbs for added flavor.

  • Make It a Meal: Consider adding protein to your dish. Grilled chicken, chorizo, or shrimp make excellent additions to a tapas spread.

Serving Suggestions

Patatas Bravas with aioli can be served as a delicious appetizer, a side dish, or even as a light main course. Pair it with a refreshing Spanish wine, such as a chilled Albariño or a bold Tempranillo, to enhance the flavors of the dish. This recipe is perfect for entertaining guests or enjoying a cozy night in.

Conclusion

Creating Spanish-inspired Patatas Bravas with aioli at home is easier than you might think. With a few simple ingredients and a bit of patience, you can master this iconic dish that captures the vibrant flavors of Spain. Whether you’re hosting a tapas night or simply looking to indulge in something delicious, this recipe is sure to impress. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Spain right from your kitchen. ¡Buen provecho!
