Sous Vide Apple Pie: A Perfectly Cooked Dessert


If you’re a fan of apple pie, you’re in for a treat with this sous vide twist on a classic dessert. The sous vide method of cooking has gained popularity for its ability to perfectly cook food to the exact desired doneness, and apple pie is no exception. By cooking the apple filling and pie crust in a water bath at a precise temperature, you can achieve a tender, flavorful apple filling and a perfectly flaky crust that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

For the Pie Filling:

  • 6-8 apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, diced

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup ice water
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for dusting)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Apple Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and cornstarch. Toss to coat the apples evenly.
  2. Drizzle the lemon juice over the apple mixture and toss again to combine.
  3. Place the apple mixture in a vacuum-sealed bag or a resealable freezer bag, removing as much air as possible before sealing. Alternatively, use a vacuum sealer for a more efficient seal.

Step 2: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).
  2. Once the water bath reaches the desired temperature, carefully lower the sealed bag of apple filling into the water.
  3. Cook the apple filling in the water bath for 1.5 to 2 hours, ensuring that the bag is fully submerged.

Step 3: Prepare the Pie Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, mixing until the dough just comes together.
  3. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out one of the chilled pie crust disks on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Once the apple filling is done cooking, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and let it cool slightly.
  4. Pour the cooked apple filling into the prepared pie crust, dot with diced butter, and cover with the second rolled-out pie crust.
  5. Crimp the edges of the pie crust together and cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Sprinkle the top with granulated sugar for a golden finish.

Step 5: Bake the Pie

  1. Place the assembled pie on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. Allow the pie to cool for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the filling to set.

Serve and Enjoy

Once the pie has cooled and set, slice it up and serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a truly decadent dessert experience. The sous vide cooking method ensures that the apple filling is perfectly tender and infused with all the warm, comforting flavors of a classic apple pie, while the pie crust bakes up flaky and golden brown. This sous vide apple pie is sure to become a new favorite in your dessert repertoire, impressing friends and family with its delicious taste and perfectly cooked texture.

So, the next time you’re craving a comforting slice of apple pie, consider trying the sous vide method for a dessert that’s perfectly cooked every time. Happy cooking!
