Spring Brunch: Recipes with Seasonal Berries


As the last wisps of winter’s chill dissipate, the world awakens to the vibrant colors and sweet aromas of spring. What better way to celebrate this season of renewal than with a delightful spring brunch, featuring the freshest, most succulent berries of the season?

In this special post, we’ll explore a selection of recipes that showcase the versatility and flavor of seasonal berries, from sweet treats to savory delights. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering, a friend’s brunch, or simply treating yourself to a relaxing morning, these recipes are sure to impress and satisfy.

Berry Blissful Muffins

Begin your spring brunch with a warm, crumbly muffin, infused with the sweetness of fresh strawberries and the subtle tang of lemon zest. This recipe yields a dozen muffins, perfect for sharing or storing for later.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups hulled and sliced strawberries
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375째F (190째C). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together melted butter, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and stir until just combined. Gently fold in the sliced strawberries and lemon juice.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Dust with confectioners’ sugar before serving.

Strawberry-Rhubarb Crisp

As the weather warms up, there’s no better way to enjoy the sweet-tart flavors of strawberries and rhubarb than in a warm, crispy dessert. This recipe serves 6-8 people and can be easily doubled or tripled for larger gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups hulled and sliced strawberries
  • 1 cup sliced rhubarb
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375째F (190째C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and rhubarb. In a small bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Add the dry mixture to the fruit and toss until the fruit is evenly coated.
  3. Transfer the fruit mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish and set aside.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the rolled oats, brown sugar, and chopped pecans (if using). Stir in the melted butter until the mixture is crumbly.
  5. Spread the oat mixture evenly over the fruit filling.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the fruit is tender. Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream if desired.

Blueberry-Lemon Poppy Seed Marmalade

Brighten up your spring brunch with a tangy, sweet marmalade infused with the citrusy flavor of lemon and the subtle crunch of poppy seeds. This recipe makes approximately 2 cups of marmalade, perfect for serving alongside toast, scones, or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the blueberries, sugar, lemon juice, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the mixture has thickened and the blueberries have broken down.
  2. Stir in the poppy seeds and salt. Continue to simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the marmalade has reached your desired consistency.
  3. Remove the marmalade from heat and let it cool to room temperature. Transfer the marmalade to an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.

Spring Berry Salad with Feta and Mint

As the weather warms up, there’s no better way to celebrate the season than with a refreshing, flavorful salad featuring the freshest berries of the season. This recipe serves 4-6 people and can be easily customized with your favorite greens and toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed spring greens (arugula, spinach, lettuce)
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, sliced strawberries, and blueberries.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil and white wine vinegar. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Top the salad with crumbled feta cheese, chopped mint leaves, and chopped pecans (if using).
  5. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for up to 2 hours before serving.

As you gather around the table with loved ones this spring, remember to savor the simple joys of the season. From sweet treats to savory delights, these recipes are sure to bring a touch of warmth and wonder to your spring brunch.
