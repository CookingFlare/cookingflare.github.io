The Best BBQ Desserts to End Your Fourth of July Celebration


The Fourth of July is all about celebration—patriotic displays, fireworks, and, of course, delicious food. While the grill takes center stage for savory BBQ dishes, it’s the desserts that truly elevate the summer experience and bring people together. As the day winds down and the sun sets, there’s no better way to wrap up a festive gathering than with a sweet treat that complements the smoky flavors of the BBQ.

Whether you’re hosting a backyard bash or attending a family picnic, these BBQ-friendly desserts are sure to make your celebration memorable. From fruity pies to creamy concoctions and fun, patriotic-themed sweets, this list of the best BBQ desserts will provide a sweet ending to your Fourth of July festivities. Get ready to fire up your oven (or grill!) and indulge in these mouthwatering recipes.

1. Patriotic Fruit Tart

A fruit tart is a fresh, light, and visually stunning dessert that will steal the show at your Fourth of July BBQ. The best part about a fruit tart is its versatility—it’s easy to make and can be customized with your favorite fruits. For a patriotic twist, use strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries to create the perfect red, white, and blue color scheme.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made tart crust or homemade shortcrust pastry
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries (or other seasonal berries)
  • Optional: Mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven according to the instructions on the tart crust packaging, or if making a homemade crust, follow the recipe instructions.
  2. Bake the tart shell until golden brown, then allow it to cool completely.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth, then add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract. Slowly add in the heavy cream and continue to beat until it reaches a smooth, fluffy texture.
  4. Fill the cooled tart shell with the cream mixture and smooth it into an even layer.
  5. Arrange the strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries in a patriotic pattern on top, creating stripes or a star pattern to resemble the American flag.
  6. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a pop of green, and refrigerate until ready to serve.

This tart offers the perfect balance of richness from the cream and tartness from the fresh fruit, making it a refreshing end to your BBQ meal.

2. Grilled Peach Cobbler

Peach cobbler is a classic summer dessert, and grilling it adds a smoky, caramelized twist. The grill imparts a rich, slightly charred flavor to the peaches, while the crumbly topping remains soft and buttery.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large peaches, halved and pitted
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • Vanilla ice cream (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. While the grill is warming up, slice the peaches in half and remove the pits.
  2. Place the peach halves on the grill, cut-side down, and cook for about 3-4 minutes until grill marks form and the peaches begin to soften. Remove them from the grill.
  3. In a bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cinnamon, baking powder, and salt. Stir in the melted butter and milk to form a batter.
  4. In a cast-iron skillet or baking dish, arrange the grilled peach halves with the cut side up. Pour the batter over the peaches, making sure it covers the fruit evenly.
  5. Grill the cobbler over indirect heat for about 30-35 minutes, until the topping is golden brown and the peaches are tender.
  6. Serve the grilled peach cobbler with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate summer treat.

The smoky flavor from the grill elevates this classic cobbler, making it an unforgettable Fourth of July dessert.

3. Red, White, and Blue Cheesecake Bars

Cheesecake is a beloved dessert for any occasion, and these red, white, and blue cheesecake bars are the perfect patriotic treat for your BBQ celebration. The creamy, tangy filling contrasts beautifully with the fresh fruit topping, and the bars are easy to slice and serve to a crowd.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Fresh strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until the mixture resembles wet sand. Press this mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking dish to form the crust.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract and sour cream.
  4. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the graham cracker crust and smooth it out with a spatula.
  5. Bake the cheesecake bars for 30-35 minutes, or until the center is set and the edges are lightly golden. Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan before transferring to the fridge to chill for at least 3 hours.
  6. Once chilled, top the cheesecake bars with fresh strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries, arranging them in a red, white, and blue pattern.

These cheesecake bars are as beautiful as they are delicious—perfect for your July 4th celebration.

4. BBQ S’mores Dip

S’mores are a quintessential campfire treat, but why not bring them to your BBQ in a more decadent form? This BBQ s’mores dip takes all the flavors of a classic s’more and transforms them into a gooey, shareable dip. It’s a crowd-pleaser that will definitely be a hit with both kids and adults.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup marshmallow fluff
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Graham crackers (for dipping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. In a cast-iron skillet or oven-safe dish, combine the chocolate chips and heavy cream.
  2. Place the dish on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chocolate is melted and smooth.
  3. Once the chocolate mixture is melted, spread it out evenly in the dish. Carefully top it with the marshmallow fluff, covering the chocolate layer.
  4. Close the grill lid and cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the marshmallow fluff is golden and bubbly.
  5. Serve the dip immediately with graham crackers for dipping.

This BBQ s’mores dip brings the nostalgic flavors of summer campfires to your outdoor BBQ in an easy and delicious format.

5. Grilled Pineapple with Honey and Cinnamon

If you’re looking for a light and refreshing dessert that pairs perfectly with grilled meats, grilled pineapple is the answer. The natural sweetness of the pineapple is enhanced by the caramelization from the grill, and the honey and cinnamon add a touch of warmth and spice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Fresh mint leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the pineapple rings for 2-3 minutes on each side until grill marks appear and the fruit softens.
  3. Drizzle the grilled pineapple with honey and sprinkle with cinnamon while it’s still warm.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve immediately.

This simple yet elegant dessert is the perfect balance of sweet, savory, and smoky, making it a great option for anyone looking for a lighter post-BBQ treat.

6. Berry Trifle

A trifle is a showstopper dessert that can be made ahead of time, which is a huge bonus when you’re hosting a Fourth of July BBQ. Layers of cake, fruit, and cream create a stunning and delicious dessert that’s sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought or homemade pound cake, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup raspberries

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream with the powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
  2. In a large trifle dish or individual serving glasses, layer cubes of pound cake, followed by whipped cream, and fresh berries. Repeat the layers until the dish is full, finishing with a layer of whipped cream and a final sprinkle of berries on top.
  3. Chill the trifle for at least 2 hours before serving.

This berry trifle is not only delicious but also adds a vibrant pop of color to your BBQ spread.

7. Apple Pie on the Grill

Apple pie is an all-American dessert that’s perfect for any summer BBQ. By grilling the pie, you get that delicious, buttery crust with the added bonus of smoky flavors that make it even more special.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts
  • 5 cups apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat and set it up for indirect grilling.
  2. Roll out the pie crust and line a 9-inch pie dish. Combine the apple slices, sugar, lemon juice, and cinnamon in a bowl, then pour the mixture into the pie crust.
  3. Dot the top with butter and cover with the second pie crust. Cut slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  4. Grill the pie over indirect heat for about 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the apples are tender.
  5. Let the pie cool slightly before serving, and enjoy with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

This grilled apple pie is a comforting and familiar dessert that adds a unique smoky twist, perfect for your summer BBQ gathering.

Conclusion

When the Fourth of July rolls around, it’s all about good food, great company, and the celebration of freedom. The desserts you serve can be just as memorable as the main BBQ dishes, offering your guests something sweet to indulge in after a delicious meal. From fresh fruit tarts to grilled peach cobbler and gooey s’mores dip, these BBQ desserts are the perfect way to end your celebration on a high note.

So this year, let your BBQ be remembered not only for the savory grilled dishes but also for these delectable, easy-to-make desserts. Whether you’re hosting a casual family get-together or an elaborate party, these recipes will ensure that your Fourth of July celebration is as sweet as the fireworks that light up the sky!
