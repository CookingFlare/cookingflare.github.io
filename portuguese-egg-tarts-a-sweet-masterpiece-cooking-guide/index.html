Portuguese Egg Tarts: A Sweet Masterpiece Cooking Guide


Portuguese egg tarts, or pastéis de nata, are a delightful pastry that has won the hearts of many across the globe. These sweet, creamy tarts have a crispy, flaky exterior with a rich, velvety custard filling that tantalizes the taste buds. Whether you’re visiting the charming streets of Lisbon or enjoying them at a local bakery, Portuguese egg tarts are a treat that can’t be missed. In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to recreate this traditional Portuguese delicacy in the comfort of your own kitchen.

History of Portuguese Egg Tarts

The origins of the pastéis de nata trace back to the 18th century in Lisbon, Portugal. The story begins in the Jerónimos Monastery, where nuns used egg whites to starch their habits. Left with an abundance of egg yolks, the nuns began experimenting with recipes, and over time, they developed a unique custard tart recipe.

In the early 19th century, the monastery was closed, and the recipe was sold to a nearby sugar refinery. There, it was refined and adapted, eventually becoming the beloved pastéis de nata we know today. The pastry’s popularity spread rapidly, and it became an iconic symbol of Portuguese baking.

Over the years, the tart has evolved, and different versions have been created, but the classic pastéis de nata remains a favorite. Whether you’re enjoying one with your morning coffee or sharing them with friends, these little pastries are a true taste of Portugal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to gather all the ingredients required to make these delectable tarts. While they may seem complicated, the ingredients are simple and easy to find. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Custard Filling:

  • 1 cup (240 ml) whole milk
  • 1 cup (240 ml) heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) cornstarch
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1 strip of lemon zest (optional)

For the Tart Shell:

  • 1 package (about 250 g) puff pastry (store-bought or homemade)
  • Butter, for greasing the tart pans
  • Extra sugar for dusting the finished tarts

How to Make Portuguese Egg Tarts

Step 1: Preparing the Custard Filling

The custard filling is the heart of any great Portuguese egg tart, and getting it just right is key to achieving the perfect texture and flavor. Follow these steps to make the rich, smooth custard:

  1. Combine milk, cream, and sugar: In a saucepan, mix the whole milk, heavy cream, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to warm up but not boil. Stir occasionally to help dissolve the sugar.

  2. Mix cornstarch: In a separate small bowl, whisk the cornstarch with a few tablespoons of cold milk or water until you form a smooth slurry. This will help thicken the custard.

  3. Whisk egg yolks: In another bowl, whisk together the egg yolks until they are smooth and slightly pale. The egg yolks will add richness to the custard.

  4. Temper the eggs: Once the milk and cream mixture has warmed up, slowly pour a small amount of the warm liquid into the egg yolks while whisking continuously. This step is called “tempering” the eggs and prevents them from curdling when mixed with the hot liquid. Gradually add more of the warm liquid to the egg yolks until it’s all combined.

  5. Cook the custard: Pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining milk mixture. Add the cinnamon stick and lemon zest if you’re using them. Cook the mixture over medium-low heat, whisking constantly until it thickens. Be patient, as this can take several minutes. Once the custard is thickened to a pudding-like consistency, remove the pan from the heat.

  6. Strain the custard: For an ultra-smooth custard, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. This will remove any lumps and bits of cooked egg, ensuring the filling is perfectly silky.

  7. Cool the custard: Let the custard cool at room temperature. You can speed up this process by placing the bowl in a larger bowl of ice water, but make sure to stir occasionally to avoid a skin from forming on the surface.

Step 2: Preparing the Tart Shells

The tart shell is just as important as the custard filling. For a crispy, flaky base, you’ll need good-quality puff pastry. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can make your own puff pastry from scratch, but store-bought puff pastry works well for this recipe, too. Here’s how to prepare the tart shells:

  1. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 450°F (230°C) and let it preheat fully.

  2. Prepare tart pans: Grease the tart pans lightly with butter. If you don’t have individual tart pans, a muffin tin can also work as a substitute.

  3. Roll out the puff pastry: Unroll the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface. If the dough is too thick, roll it out to about 1/8-inch thickness. Cut out circles of dough that are slightly larger than the tart pans you’re using.

  4. Line the pans: Carefully place the puff pastry circles into the tart pans, pressing gently into the sides and bottoms to fit the pastry into each pan. Be sure not to stretch the dough, as this can affect the final texture.

  5. Chill the shells: Place the prepared tart pans in the fridge for about 10 minutes. This helps the pastry keep its shape during baking.

Step 3: Assembling and Baking the Egg Tarts

Once your custard is cool and your tart shells are prepared, it’s time to assemble and bake your egg tarts.

  1. Fill the tart shells: Spoon the cooled custard filling into each tart shell, filling them almost to the top. Be careful not to overfill, as the custard may spill over while baking.

  2. Bake the tarts: Place the tart pans in the preheated oven and bake for about 15-20 minutes, or until the tops of the tarts are golden brown and slightly caramelized. The custard should still jiggle slightly when you shake the pan.

  3. Cool before serving: Once baked, remove the tarts from the oven and let them cool slightly before removing them from the pans. Serve warm or at room temperature for the best flavor.

Tips for Perfect Portuguese Egg Tarts

  • Use cold ingredients: Make sure your puff pastry and custard ingredients are cold, as this will help create a flaky, tender crust.
  • Watch the baking time: Each oven is different, so keep an eye on your tarts during baking. The goal is a golden, crisp exterior and a soft, slightly wobbly custard interior.
  • Dust with cinnamon or powdered sugar: Once your egg tarts are baked, you can dust them with a little ground cinnamon or powdered sugar for added flavor and presentation.
  • Serve fresh: While Portuguese egg tarts can be stored in an airtight container, they are best enjoyed fresh. The pastry will retain its crispness and the custard will be at its creamiest.

Variations of Portuguese Egg Tarts

Though the traditional recipe for pastéis de nata is unbeatable, there are plenty of ways to make them your own. Here are a few variations to consider:

  • Chocolate Portuguese egg tarts: Add a small amount of melted dark chocolate to the custard filling for a delicious twist. The chocolate adds depth and richness to the already decadent tart.
  • Fruit-filled tarts: Top the custard with fresh berries or tropical fruits like mango or passion fruit for a refreshing take on the classic egg tart.
  • Almond Portuguese egg tarts: Mix finely ground almonds into the custard for a nutty, fragrant variation. You could also sprinkle sliced almonds on top before baking.

Where to Find the Best Portuguese Egg Tarts

While it’s always a great idea to make your own pastéis de nata, if you’re ever in Portugal or a city with a strong Portuguese community, make sure to try the local version. Some of the most famous bakeries for egg tarts in Lisbon include:

  • Pastéis de Belém: Arguably the most famous bakery, Pastéis de Belém has been baking pastéis de nata since 1837. The tarts here are widely regarded as the best in Portugal.
  • Manteigaria: A modern take on the classic pastry, Manteigaria offers tarts that are crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside.
  • Fábrica de Nata: This bakery offers a variety of pastéis de nata and is a popular spot for both locals and tourists.

Conclusion

Portuguese egg tarts are a true masterpiece of pastry, combining the delicate layers of buttery puff pastry with a rich, silky custard filling. Whether you’re making them at home or enjoying them at a bakery, these tarts are sure to bring a little sweetness to your day. With this step-by-step guide, you now have all the tools you need to create your own batch of pastéis de nata that will impress anyone lucky enough to try them. Happy baking!
