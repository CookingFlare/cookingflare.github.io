How to Make Korean Spicy Kimchi Soup (Kimchi Jjigae)


Kimchi Jjigae, or Korean spicy kimchi soup, is a robust and flavorful dish at the heart of Korean cuisine. It’s a warm hug in a bowl, offering complex layers of spice, tang, and umami with every spoonful. This dish is not only comforting but also incredibly easy to prepare, making it a staple for home cooks and food enthusiasts alike. Whether you’re a seasoned lover of Korean food or trying it for the first time, this recipe will guide you through the simple steps to create restaurant-worthy Kimchi Jjigae in your own kitchen.

What Makes Kimchi Jjigae So Special?

Kimchi Jjigae is often referred to as Korea’s soul food, enjoyed across seasons and family tables. At its core, this dish is built around kimchi, the famous fermented Korean cabbage, which lends deep tangy notes, spice, and incredible depth of flavor to the broth.

What sets Kimchi Jjigae apart is its versatility. It’s the best way to use up old, sour kimchi, ensuring nothing goes to waste while enhancing the soup with intensified flavor. The addition of ingredients like pork belly, tofu, and key Korean pantry staples creates a perfectly balanced bowl that’s hearty yet nutritious.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Authentic Kimchi Jjigae

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s go over the essential ingredients you’ll need. These items are readily available at Korean grocery stores or larger supermarkets with international sections.

Main Ingredients:

  • Aged Kimchi (2 cups): Older, fermented kimchi works best since it has a richer, deeper flavor.
  • Pork Belly or Pork Shoulder (150-200 grams): This adds protein and fat for richness. You can substitute with canned tuna or skip entirely for a vegetarian version.
  • Firm Tofu (200 grams): Tofu balances the heat and adds texture to the soup.
  • Yellow Onion (1 medium): Adds sweetness and depth to the soup base.
  • Scallions (2-3 stalks): For garnish and flavor infusion.

For the Broth:

  • Kimchi Juice (½ cup): Drain the juice from your kimchi jar; it’s packed with concentrated flavor.
  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Flakes, 1-2 tablespoons): Controls the spice level.
  • Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste, 1 tablespoon): Adds both heat and a slightly sweet, smoky flavor.
  • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon): Rounds out the flavors with umami.
  • Sesame Oil (1 teaspoon): Offers a nutty aroma and richness.
  • Water or Anchovy Stock (4 cups): If you want a more authentic taste, use Korean anchovy stock, but plain water works as well.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Kimchi Jjigae

Now that you’ve gathered your ingredients, it’s time to bring your soup to life. Follow the steps carefully while keeping your personal preferences in mind. You can easily adjust the level of heat or make substitutions as suggested.

1. Prep Your Ingredients

Start by preparing everything before you begin cooking:

  • Slice the pork belly thinly into bite-sized pieces.
  • Dice the older kimchi into smaller chunks if it’s not already cut.
  • Cut the tofu into medium-sized cubes.
  • Slice the onion thinly.
  • Chop scallions into 2-3 inch pieces, leaving some finely diced for garnish.

2. Sauté the Kimchi and Pork

Heat a medium-sized pot over medium heat, and add a drizzle of sesame oil. Once heated, toss in the sliced pork belly and sauté for a minute or two until slightly browned. Add the chopped kimchi and stir together, letting it sauté alongside the pork for about 3-5 minutes. This step deepens the flavor of the kimchi and allows the pork to absorb some of those tangy spices.

3. Build Your Flavorful Broth

Pour in the kimchi juice, water or anchovy stock, and soy sauce into the pot. Stir in the gochugaru and gochujang, ensuring they blend evenly. Bring the soup to a boil over medium-high heat while stirring occasionally.

During this simmering stage, the kimchi releases all its scrumptious flavors into the broth, and the chili paste thickens the soup slightly for a hearty texture.

4. Add Tofu and Onion

Once the soup has reached a boil, lower the heat to medium and carefully add the sliced tofu and onions. Let these cook for approximately 5-8 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the spiced broth while the onions soften and sweeten.

5. Taste and Adjust

At this point, taste the soup and adjust to your liking. If you prefer a saltier flavor profile, add a little more soy sauce. For added heat, sprinkle in more gochugaru.

6. Garnish and Serve

Finally, toss in the chopped scallions a minute or two before removing the soup from the heat. This ensures they stay fresh and vibrant in your dish. Ladle the hot Kimchi Jjigae into bowls and garnish with extra scallions for a burst of color and aroma.

Tips for Elevating Your Kimchi Jjigae

Here are some tips to ensure your Kimchi Jjigae is perfect every time:

  • Use Sour Kimchi: Freshly made kimchi won’t deliver the same depth of flavor as fermented kimchi that’s been sitting for a few weeks.
  • Experiment with Fillings: Beyond pork belly, you can add clams, shrimp, or even canned tuna for a seafood twist.
  • Serve with Rice: Kimchi Jjigae pairs beautifully with freshly steamed white rice. The rice soaks up the spicy broth and provides a satisfying complement.
  • Make It Ahead: This soup tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld and intensify. Don’t hesitate to make a bigger batch and refrigerate leftovers.
  • Customize Spice Levels: If you’re sensitive to spice, reduce the amount of gochugaru and gochujang slightly. Alternatively, amp it up if you crave more heat.

Why Kimchi Jjigae Deserves a Place at Your Table

Aside from its irresistible taste, Kimchi Jjigae is steeped in tradition and brings a taste of Korean culture to your kitchen. It’s a versatile recipe that adapts to what you have on hand and invites creativity. It’s perfect for chilly evenings when you want something soothing, and its vibrant flavors make it a go-to choice for anyone who enjoys bold, spicy dishes.

Making Kimchi Jjigae is not just cooking—it’s experiencing a slice of Korea’s culinary history. The dance of spicy, savory, and tangy ingredients in your pot transforms into a meal that’s worthy of sharing with family and friends or savoring solo. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or an enthusiast, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Warm up your stove, grab your aged kimchi, and dive into the delightful world of Kimchi Jjigae. With this recipe, you’ll master a dish that brings together simplicity, authenticity, and explosive flavors in every bowl.
