Citrus Sunshine: Orange Creamsicle Cupcakes to Brighten Your Day


If you’re in need of a sweet treat that will transport you to sunny days and childhood memories, look no further than these Citrus Sunshine: Orange Creamsicle Cupcakes. Bursting with the vibrant flavors of orange and cream, these cupcakes are sure to brighten your day and satisfy your sweet tooth. So grab your apron and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

For the cupcakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1/2 cup whole milk

For the orange cream frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake pan with paper liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the orange juice and milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing just until combined.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the orange cream frosting. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, alternating with the orange juice and heavy cream. Beat until smooth and fluffy. Stir in the orange zest and vanilla extract.

  8. Once the cupcakes are completely cooled, generously frost each cupcake with the orange cream frosting, using a piping bag or an offset spatula.

  9. For an extra burst of citrus flavor, garnish each cupcake with a sprinkle of orange zest.

Tips and Variations

  • For a tangier twist, substitute some of the orange juice in the cupcakes with freshly squeezed lemon juice.

  • If you prefer a lighter frosting, you can reduce the amount of powdered sugar in the recipe.

  • To make these cupcakes even more festive, top them with colorful sprinkles or edible flowers.

  • If you’re a fan of chocolate and orange, you can add a teaspoon of cocoa powder to the cupcake batter for a delicious twist.

  • These cupcakes can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to three days.

Enjoy these Citrus Sunshine: Orange Creamsicle Cupcakes with a cold glass of milk or a cup of tea. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply craving a sweet treat, these cupcakes are sure to bring a ray of sunshine to your day. Happy baking!
