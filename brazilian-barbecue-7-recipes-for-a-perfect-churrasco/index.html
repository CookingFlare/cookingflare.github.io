Brazilian Barbecue: 7 Recipes for a Perfect Churrasco


Brazilian barbecue, known as churrasco, is an iconic culinary tradition that has captivated food lovers worldwide. Rooted in centuries of gaucho culture from Southern Brazil, this style of barbecue brings together the smoky heat of natural charcoal, premium cuts of meat, and a casual, family-centric atmosphere. A churrasco isn’t just a meal—it’s an experience. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or want to elevate your grilling game, this guide presents seven irresistible recipes that will transport you to the vibrant world of authentic Brazilian barbecue.

Essential Ingredients for a Successful Churrasco

Before diving into the recipes, preparation is key. Brazilian barbecue relies on a combination of simple yet high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Coarse sea salt: The star seasoning for churrasco is coarse salt. It enhances the meat’s natural flavor without overpowering it.
  • Charcoal or wood: Traditional churrascarias use natural wood for its rich, smoky aroma, but lump charcoal also works well.
  • Skewers: Long metal skewers are essential for holding large cuts of meat steady over the flame.
  • Cuts of beef, pork, chicken, and sausage: Brazilian churrasco celebrates variety!

Now that you’re ready with the basics, let’s explore seven must-try recipes for an unforgettable churrasco.

1. Picanha – The Star of Brazilian Barbecue

Picanha, also known as top sirloin cap or rump cap, is the crown jewel of churrasco. Its rich marbling and delectable fat cap make it a flavorful crowd-pleaser.

What You’ll Need:

  • 2 pounds of picanha (with the fat cap intact)
  • Coarse sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Slice the picanha into thick steaks (about 1.5 inches thick), being careful to keep the fat cap attached.
  2. Generously coat each steak with coarse sea salt. Let it rest for 15–20 minutes.
  3. Skewer the steaks into a C-shape (fat cap on the outside) and grill over medium heat. Begin with the fat side down for a beautifully caramelized crust.
  4. Rotate the skewer frequently to cook evenly, around 15–20 minutes for medium rare. Slice off thin pieces directly from the skewer and serve hot.

2. Frango com Alho – Garlic-Crusted Chicken Thighs

For poultry lovers, frango com alho is a standout. Smoky, tender chicken thighs are seasoned with a bold garlic marinade, making them a perfect addition to your churrasco spread.

What You’ll Need:

  • 8 boneless chicken thighs
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Coarse sea salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine minced garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the chicken thighs in the mixture for at least an hour, preferably overnight.
  3. Skewer the thighs or place them directly on the grill over medium-high heat. Cook for 8–10 minutes per side or until golden brown.
  4. Brush with leftover marinade during grilling for added flavor. Serve with lime wedges.

3. Linguiça – Brazilian-Style Sausage

Linguiça, a flavorful sausage native to Brazil, is a staple at any churrasco. Unlike other sausage options, linguiça is grilled whole, allowing its hearty, spiced flavors to shine.

What You’ll Need:

  • 2 pounds of linguiça (sweet, mild, or spicy depending on your preference)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Place the sausages directly on the grill, turning every few minutes to ensure even cooking.
  3. Grill for about 15–20 minutes until they are plump and have a crispy outer layer.
  4. Slice into bite-sized pieces and serve as an appetizer or a side dish alongside other meats.

4. Costela de Boi – Brazilian Beef Ribs

Costela de boi, or beef ribs, is a slow-cooked masterpiece. The long cooking process melts the fat, yielding tender, smoky, fall-off-the-bone ribs.

What You’ll Need:

  • 4 pounds of beef ribs
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Optional: Garlic powder and smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. Season the ribs generously with sea salt. Add garlic powder and smoked paprika for extra flavor if desired.
  2. Wrap the ribs in aluminum foil and place them on the grill over indirect heat. Use low heat (250°F to 275°F) to allow the ribs to cook slowly.
  3. After 3–4 hours, unwrap the ribs and finish them directly over the hot coals for a crispy bark.
  4. Rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

5. Coração de Frango – Chicken Hearts

For the adventurous eater, chicken hearts are a classic churrasco delicacy. These bite-sized morsels are packed with rich, meaty flavor and a hint of smokiness.

What You’ll Need:

  • 1 pound of chicken hearts
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Coarse sea salt and black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Clean the chicken hearts by removing any attached arteries.
  2. In a bowl, mix garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Marinate the hearts for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Skewer the hearts and grill them over high heat for 3–4 minutes per side.
  4. Serve immediately as a bold starter or alongside your main dishes.

6. Abacaxi com Canela – Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon

No churrasco is complete without a sweet finish, and grilled pineapple is the quintessential Brazilian dessert. The natural sugars caramelize beautifully over the heat, complemented by a warm hint of cinnamon.

What You’ll Need:

  • 1 ripe pineapple (peeled and cored)
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Slice the pineapple into thick rings or long spears.
  2. Sprinkle with cinnamon and brown sugar (if using).
  3. Grill over medium heat until lightly charred, around 3–5 minutes per side.
  4. Serve warm as a refreshing dessert or a palate cleanser.

7. Farofa – Toasted Cassava Flour Side Dish

Farofa is a quintessential Brazilian barbecue side dish. Its crunchy texture and buttery flavor pair perfectly with grilled meats.

What You’ll Need:

  • 2 cups cassava flour
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat and sauté the onion and garlic until golden.
  2. Add the cassava flour and toast it, stirring frequently, for about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in parsley and season with salt and pepper. Serve warm as a flavorful accompaniment to your churrasco.

Final Tips for a Memorable Churrasco

  1. Keep it simple: Brazilian barbecue focuses on enhancing the natural flavors of ingredients. Avoid heavy marinades or sauces.
  2. Pairings: Serve meats with side dishes like rice, black beans, farofa, and chimichurri sauce.
  3. Drinks: Complement the churrasco with traditional beverages such as caipirinhas, Brazilian beer, or fresh coconut water.
  4. Pace yourself: A churrasco is meant to be a leisurely experience. Take your time, savor each dish, and enjoy the company.

With these seven recipes and tips, you’re ready to host a churrasco that harks back to Brazilian cultural traditions while celebrating global culinary creativity. Fire up the grill, gather your guests, and let the feast begin!
