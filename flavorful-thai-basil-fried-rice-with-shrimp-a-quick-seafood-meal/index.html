Flavorful Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp: A Quick Seafood Meal


If you’re looking for a delicious, quick, and satisfying meal, you’ve come to the right place! Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp is a delightful dish that packs a punch of flavor and can be whipped up in less than 30 minutes. This recipe combines fragrant jasmine rice, succulent shrimp, and the aromatic notes of Thai basil, creating a meal that’s as pleasing to the palate as it is to the eye. Let’s dive into the world of Thai cuisine and learn how to make this incredible dish!

Why Thai Basil Fried Rice?

Thai cuisine is celebrated for its vibrant flavors and fresh ingredients. One of the key elements of this dish is Thai basil, which has a distinct flavor profile—spicy, anise-like, and slightly sweet. When paired with shrimp, it elevates the dish to a whole new level. Fried rice is a versatile dish that can be customized to your taste, making it perfect for busy weeknights or casual gatherings with friends.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to create this Flavorful Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp:

For the Fried Rice:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old for best texture)
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas (or your choice of vegetables)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves, loosely packed
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges (for serving)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper (to taste)

Optional Garnishes:

  • Sliced green onions
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Extra lime wedges

Preparation Steps

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s move on to the preparation steps.

Step 1: Prepare the Rice

If you don’t have day-old rice, you can cook jasmine rice fresh. Just ensure it’s cooled down before using it in this recipe; otherwise, the rice might become too sticky. Spread the freshly cooked rice on a baking sheet to cool quickly.

Step 2: Cook the Shrimp

In a large skillet or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and sauté for about 2-3 minutes, or until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set them aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Once hot, add the minced garlic and diced onion. Sauté for about 2 minutes, or until fragrant and the onions are translucent.

Next, toss in the diced red bell pepper and sugar snap peas. Continue to stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

Step 4: Combine Everything

Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet with the sautéed vegetables. Add the cooked jasmine rice, soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir everything together, ensuring that the rice is evenly coated with the sauces.

Step 5: Add the Thai Basil

Once everything is well mixed, add the Thai basil leaves. Stir-fry for an additional minute until the basil wilts and releases its fragrant aroma. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding freshly cracked black pepper as desired.

Step 6: Serve

Remove the skillet from heat. Serve your Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp hot, garnished with sliced green onions, chopped cilantro, and lime wedges on the side. The lime juice adds a fresh tang that perfectly complements the dish.

Tips for the Best Thai Basil Fried Rice

  1. Use Day-Old Rice: Day-old rice is drier and helps in achieving the perfect fried rice texture. If you can, prepare your rice a day in advance.

  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: If you’re making a larger batch, consider cooking in batches to prevent steaming the ingredients.

  3. Customize Your Vegetables: Feel free to add or substitute your favorite vegetables, such as carrots, zucchini, or broccoli, depending on what you have on hand.

  4. Adjust Spice Level: If you enjoy a bit of heat, add some sliced Thai chili or a dash of chili sauce when cooking.

  5. Fresh Herbs Matter: The freshness of the Thai basil is crucial for the flavor profile of this dish. Always opt for fresh over dried herbs.

Nutritional Benefits

This Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp is not only delicious but also nutritious. Shrimp is an excellent source of protein and is low in calories. Thai basil provides essential vitamins and antioxidants, while the colorful vegetables contribute dietary fiber and various nutrients. The combination of flavors and textures makes this dish both satisfying and healthy.

Final Thoughts

Thai Basil Fried Rice with Shrimp is a fantastic way to enjoy a quick seafood meal that doesn’t compromise on flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding. The vibrant colors, aromatic spices, and the fresh taste of Thai basil will transport your taste buds straight to Thailand.

So, next time you’re in need of a quick and satisfying meal, remember this flavorful recipe. It’s perfect for busy weeknights, casual dinners, or impressing friends at gatherings. Enjoy your culinary adventure, and don’t forget to savor every bite of your delicious creation!

Happy cooking!
