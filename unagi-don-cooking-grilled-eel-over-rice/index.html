Unagi Don: Cooking Grilled Eel Over Rice


Unagi Don, a beloved dish in Japanese cuisine, showcases the rich, savory flavors of grilled eel (unagi) served atop a bed of fluffy rice. This dish not only tantalizes the taste buds but also offers a glimpse into Japan’s culinary traditions. In this post, we’ll explore the origins of Unagi Don, the essential ingredients needed, and a step-by-step guide to preparing this delightful meal at home.

Understanding Unagi Don

Unagi Don, or “grilled eel over rice,” is a popular variation of donburi, which refers to a rice bowl dish topped with various ingredients. The star of Unagi Don is the unagi, which is freshwater eel that has been grilled and glazed with a sweet soy-based sauce known as kabayaki. This preparation enhances the eel’s natural flavors and gives it a beautifully caramelized appearance.

A Brief History of Unagi

Eel has been consumed in Japan for centuries, with historical records dating back to the Edo period (1603-1868). During this time, unagi became a popular dish, particularly during the hot summer months when it was believed to give people strength and vitality. The tradition of grilling eel and serving it over rice emerged as a convenient way to enjoy this delicacy.

Essential Ingredients

To create a perfect Unagi Don, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Eel

  • Unagi fillets: Fresh or frozen unagi can be used. If using frozen, ensure it’s properly thawed.
  • Soy sauce: A good quality soy sauce adds depth to the flavor.
  • Mirin: This sweet rice wine enhances the glaze’s sweetness.
  • Sake: Adds a subtle complexity to the sauce.
  • Sugar: Balances the saltiness of the soy sauce.

For the Rice

  • Japanese short-grain rice: This type of rice becomes sticky when cooked, making it ideal for holding the grilled eel.
  • Water: For cooking the rice.

For Garnish

  • Shredded nori (seaweed): Adds texture and flavor.
  • Chopped green onions: Provides a fresh contrast to the richness of the eel.
  • Pickled ginger: A palate cleanser that enhances the overall dining experience.

Preparing the Perfect Unagi Don

Step 1: Cooking the Rice

  1. Rinse the Rice: Begin by measuring out 2 cups of Japanese short-grain rice. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky.

  2. Soak the Rice: After rinsing, soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. This allows the grains to absorb water and cook evenly.

  3. Cook the Rice: Drain the soaked rice and transfer it to a rice cooker. Add 2 cups of water (or follow the rice cooker’s instructions) and cook according to the device’s settings. If you don’t have a rice cooker, you can cook it on the stove in a pot with a tight-fitting lid. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 18-20 minutes. Let it rest, covered, for an additional 10 minutes off the heat.

Step 2: Preparing the Unagi

  1. Marinate the Eel: If using fresh unagi, it’s often sold pre-prepared for grilling. If using frozen, thaw it overnight in the refrigerator. Prepare the marinade by mixing equal parts soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar in a saucepan. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Grill the Eel: Preheat your grill or broiler. Brush the eel with the marinade and grill it for about 5-7 minutes on each side, basting it with more marinade as it cooks. The goal is to achieve a nice char and ensure the eel is cooked through.

  3. Glazing: In the last few minutes of grilling, continue basting with the marinade to create a glossy, sticky glaze on the unagi.

Step 3: Assembling the Dish

  1. Prepare the Rice Bowl: Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork. Divide the rice into individual bowls, filling each bowl generously.

  2. Add the Grilled Eel: Place the grilled unagi fillets on top of the rice. You can slice the eel into bite-sized pieces for easier eating.

  3. Garnish: Sprinkle shredded nori and chopped green onions over the eel for added flavor and texture. Add a small side of pickled ginger for contrast.

Serving Suggestions

Unagi Don is best enjoyed fresh, but there are many ways to elevate your dining experience:

  • Pair with Miso Soup: The umami-rich flavors of miso soup complement the eel’s richness.
  • Serve with a Fresh Salad: A simple cucumber or seaweed salad can provide a refreshing balance.
  • Enjoy with Green Tea: A cup of hot green tea pairs perfectly, enhancing the dish’s flavors.

Tips for the Best Unagi Don

  1. Quality Matters: Always opt for high-quality unagi from a reputable source. Fresh eel from a local fish market is ideal, but frozen eel can also be a great alternative if fresh is not available.

  2. Perfecting the Marinade: Adjust the sweetness and saltiness of the marinade according to your taste. Some prefer a sweeter glaze, while others enjoy a more savory profile.

  3. Experiment with Toppings: While shredded nori and green onions are traditional, feel free to experiment with other toppings like sesame seeds or even a quail egg to add richness.

Conclusion

Unagi Don is more than just a meal; it’s a celebration of flavors and a connection to Japanese culinary traditions. By following the steps outlined above, you can recreate this iconic dish at home, impressing family and friends with your culinary skills. Whether you’re enjoying it on a special occasion or as a comforting weeknight dinner, Unagi Don is sure to delight your palate and nourish your spirit. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and embark on a delicious journey into the world of Japanese cuisine!
