Potato Power: Cooking with Kids & Building a Greenhouse from Spuds


Are you tired of the usual cooking shows and activities with your children? Why not try a step further and turn your kitchen into a science lab filled with fun, learning, and delicious outcomes by making use of something as common as potatoes? Exciting, right? On top of that, let’s venture into the world of sustainability by building a mini-greenhouse for growing our very own culinary ingredients. Sound like an adventure? Let’s dive in!

Fun and Nourishing Spud Creations

Engage in a tasty, educational project with your children this weekend, building a solar cooker and baking some potato-based treats. Presto change-o, turning kitchen science into a fascinating experience.

Step 1: Creating a Solar Cooker

For your little ones to fully understand the concept, begin by explaining how sunlight can be harnessed to cook food. Ask them to imagine how a solar cooker works – trapping heat from the sun and using it to bake or boil dishes, kind of like a giant magnifying glass.

Here’s a simple guide to make a solar cooker using old jars:

  1. Materials needed:

    • Cardboard or thick paper
    • Aluminum foil
    • Black paper or fabric
    • Old jars with tight-fitting lids
    • Packing tape
    • Scissors or a box cutter
    • Help from mom or dad

  2. Construct the solar cooker:

    • Cut the box into an oval, big enough to fit the jars inside. You will need to leave one side open to position the jars.
    • Line the inside of the oven with aluminum foil to reflect and retain heat.
    • Make a hole in the center of the long side, where the largest diameter meets the open end – this is where your jars will go.
    • Cover the inside wall with black paper or fabric to absorb heat.
    • Place the jars in a line across the hole, leaving enough space in between for air circulation.
    • Tape the sides of the box together; make sure the opening remains uncovered.

  3. Positioning the solar cooker:

    • The secret to successful solar cooking is to aim for your oven’s opening directly at the sun.
    • Choose a sunny day for the baking experience!
    • Set up the solar cooker on a flat, stable surface, facing south and avoiding shadows.
    • Directly facing it, careful to protect your eyes!

Step 2: Potato Dishes to Whip Up

Having your solar cooker ready, it’s time to decide what tasty treats to bake. Potatoes are so versatile, and there’s plenty to choose from. From the simplest roasted delights to stuffed potato skins, the possibilities are endless!

Side Note: Potato Fun Facts

  • We all know potatoes are starchy and often filled with carbohydrates, but
  • Potatoes are also packed with nutrients and minerals, catering to a balanced diet.

1. Nutritious Roasted Potatoes:

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium-sized potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and any other seasonings to preference

Instructions:

  • Cut medium-sized potatoes into a uniform size – whether wedges, cubes, or thin slices.
  • In a large bowl, toss the cut potatoes with olive oil, salt, pepper, or any other preferred seasonings.
  • Transfer your seasoned potatoes into aluminum-foil-lined pans.
  • Place the pan inside the solar cooker and let it do its magic. Generally, it takes around 1-2 hours for the potatoes to roast thoroughly. Keep an eye on them, and remember that the more sunlight, the better!

2. Stuffed Potato Skins:

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked and crumbled bacon
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Salt, pepper, and other desired seasonings

Instructions:

  • Bake the potatoes in your oven or microwave until tender, then cut them in half lengthwise, and scoop out the insides, leaving a layer of potato in the skin.
  • Brush the potato skins with olive oil and place them cut-side down in a prepared solar cooker-lined pan. Bake for approximately 20-30 minutes or until crisp.
  • Fill the potato skins with your favorite filling: shredded cheese, bacon, salt, and pepper. Place them in the solar cooker until the cheese is melted.
  • Just before serving, top with a dollop of sour cream and any other desired toppings.

Building a Semi-Permanent Greenhouse

Now, how about taking a step further in your sustainable journey by planting your very own potato “farm” within a mini-greenhouse?

Materials Needed:

  • Large gardening pots or barrels
  • Greenhouse plastic or polyethylene
  • Wigwam sticks or cattle panels
  • Twine or zip ties
  • Potatoes
  • Soil
  • Compost or organic fertilizer

Instructions:

  1. Construct the Greenhouse:

    • Cut the greenhouse plastic to be slightly larger than the top of your barrel.
    • Secure the plastic tightly to the top of the barrel with a wire hoop, creating a seal.
    • Position the cattle panel around the sides of the barrel, using twine or zip ties to secure it.
    • Stretch and secure the plastic over the panels using twine or zip ties – make sure it is taught and tight.
    • Ventilation is crucial for plant health, so make sure to cut small slits in the plastic.

  2. Plant Your Potatoes:

    • Fill the barrel about halfway with potting soil or compost, then add a layer of potatoes on top.
    • Add more soil, leaving enough space to cover any growth.
    • Continue layering, but avoid burying the exposing stems completely - leave them exposed for natural growth.

  3. Maintain Your Garden:

    • Water consistently during the growth cycle.
    • Fertilize every couple of weeks if needed.
    • Keep an eye out for any pests – naturally occurring pests can be controlled with beneficial insects or homemade pesticides.

As your potato greenhouse thrives, you’ll be able to use the homegrown taters for even more exciting solar cooking adventures! Don’t be surprised if your kids have their hearts set on potatoes for dinner every night. The excitement of solar cooking, mixed with cultivating a mini-greenhouse, will help instill a love and appreciation for nature, food, and fun!

Let us know in the comments below about your solar cooker and mini-greenhouse creations. Happy cooking, and growing!
