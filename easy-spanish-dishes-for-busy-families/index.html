Easy Spanish Dishes for Busy Families


Cooking can be a daunting task for busy families, especially when juggling work, school, and extracurricular activities. The good news is that Spanish cuisine offers a variety of easy and delicious dishes that can be prepared with minimal fuss, perfect for hectic weeknights. In this post, we’ll explore several simple Spanish recipes that are not only quick to make but also packed with flavor. Let’s dive into the vibrant world of Spanish cooking!

1. Spanish Tortilla (Tortilla Española)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 medium potatoes
  • 1 small onion (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes: Peel the potatoes and slice them thinly (about a quarter-inch thick). If using onion, slice it thinly as well.

  2. Cook the Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat. Add the potatoes (and onion, if using) and cook, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are soft and lightly golden (about 15-20 minutes). Season with salt.

  3. Mix the Eggs: While the potatoes are cooking, crack the eggs into a mixing bowl and beat them lightly. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them from the oil (reserve some for later) and add them to the eggs. Mix gently to combine.

  4. Cook the Tortilla: Wipe out the skillet and add a bit of the reserved oil over medium heat. Pour in the egg and potato mixture and cook for about 5-7 minutes until the bottom is set and lightly browned. Use a plate to flip the tortilla over and cook the other side for another 5 minutes.

  5. Serve: Let the tortilla cool slightly before slicing it into wedges. It can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, making it a perfect dish for any meal of the day.

2. Quick Paella

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup mixed frozen peas and bell peppers
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until they are tender.

  2. Add the Rice and Spices: Stir in the arborio rice, smoked paprika, and saffron (if using). Cook for another minute to toast the rice slightly.

  3. Add the Broth: Pour in the broth, season with salt and pepper, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the liquid.

  4. Serve: Fluff the paella with a fork, and serve it with lemon wedges. This dish is colorful and can be customized with your favorite proteins like chicken, shrimp, or chorizo.

3. Chickpea and Spinach Stew (Espinacas con Garbanzos)

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (or frozen)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent (about 5 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cumin, cooking for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Add Chickpeas and Spinach: Stir in the chickpeas and spinach. If using frozen spinach, let it thaw before adding. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Simmer: Add a splash of water if needed and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Serve: Serve warm with lemon wedges for a zesty kick. This dish is nutritious, filling, and can be enjoyed on its own or with crusty bread.

4. Garlic Shrimp (Gambas al Ajillo)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium heat.

  2. Sauté Garlic: Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until the garlic is golden and fragrant (about 1-2 minutes).

  3. Cook the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the skillet, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.

  4. Serve: Sprinkle with fresh parsley and serve immediately with lemon wedges. This quick dish pairs beautifully with crusty bread to soak up the garlicky oil.

5. Pisto (Spanish Ratatouille)

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent (about 5 minutes).

  2. Add Remaining Vegetables: Stir in the zucchini, bell pepper, and eggplant. Cook for about 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Add the chopped tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and cook for another 5 minutes.

  3. Optional Egg Topping: If desired, create small wells in the pisto and crack an egg into each well. Cover the skillet and cook until the eggs are set to your liking.

  4. Serve: Enjoy this dish warm, either on its own or with crusty bread. It’s a great way to use up leftover vegetables and is incredibly versatile.

6. Spanish-Style Meatballs (Albondigas)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Meatball Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, breadcrumbs, milk, egg, minced garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  2. Form the Meatballs: Shape the mixture into small meatballs (about 1 inch in diameter).

  3. Cook the Meatballs: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the meatballs and cook until browned on all sides (about 6-8 minutes). Remove from the skillet and set aside.

  4. Simmer in Sauce: In the same skillet, add the marinara sauce and bring to a simmer. Return the meatballs to the skillet, cover, and cook for another 10-15 minutes.

  5. Serve: Serve the meatballs over pasta, rice, or with crusty bread for a hearty meal. This dish is a favorite among kids and adults alike!

7. Flan (Spanish Caramel Custard)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Make the Caramel: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the sugar until it becomes a golden caramel. Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking dish, swirling to coat the bottom.

  2. Mix the Custard: In a blender, combine the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth.

  3. Bake the Flan: Pour the custard mixture over the caramel in the baking dish. Place the dish in a larger baking pan filled with water (bain-marie) and bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 50-60 minutes, or until set.

  4. Cool and Serve: Let the flan cool before refrigerating for at least 4 hours. To serve, invert onto a plate so the caramel drizzles over the custard.

Conclusion

These easy Spanish dishes are perfect for busy families looking to bring a taste of Spain to their dinner table without spending all evening in the kitchen. With minimal prep time and simple ingredients, these recipes are designed to be both delicious and family-friendly. So gather your loved ones and enjoy the vibrant flavors of Spain, right from your home! Whether it’s a comforting tortilla, a quick paella, or a sweet flan to finish off the meal, you’ll find that Spanish cuisine offers something for everyone. Happy cooking!
