10 Creative Fall Recipes for Leftover Turkey


As the crisp fall air sets in, it’s the perfect time to indulge in hearty, comforting meals. After a festive holiday meal, leftover turkey can quickly pile up in the fridge. But rather than letting that turkey go to waste, why not transform it into something new and exciting? Whether you’re looking for a quick meal for your family or something a bit more creative, there are plenty of fall-inspired recipes that will make your leftover turkey shine. Here are 10 inventive and delicious ways to turn your leftover turkey into fall-inspired dishes that everyone will enjoy.

1. Turkey & Butternut Squash Chili

Chili is a staple of fall cuisine, with its warm, hearty flavors perfect for cooler weather. Add a twist to this classic by using leftover turkey instead of ground beef. Pair it with roasted butternut squash to add a touch of sweetness that complements the savory turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1 small butternut squash (peeled, cubed)
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  3. Add the cubed butternut squash and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the shredded turkey, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and chicken broth.
  5. Season with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  6. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  7. Serve with a dollop of sour cream and fresh cilantro for garnish.

2. Turkey and Apple Stuffed Acorn Squash

Fall is all about squashes, and acorn squash is a perfect vessel for a creative leftover turkey recipe. The sweetness of the apples pairs wonderfully with the savory turkey filling, making this dish both comforting and seasonal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash (halved and seeds removed)
  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1 apple (peeled and diced)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon sage
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut-side up, and roast for 30 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the apple in a little olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the shredded turkey, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, sage, cinnamon, and chicken broth. Stir to combine, seasoning with salt and pepper.
  5. Stuff the turkey mixture into the roasted acorn squash halves.
  6. Bake for another 10-15 minutes, until the stuffing is golden brown.
  7. Serve warm, drizzled with a bit of extra chicken broth if desired.

3. Turkey and Cranberry Quesadillas

After Thanksgiving, you probably have a bit of cranberry sauce left over, and what better way to use it than in a turkey quesadilla? The tartness of the cranberry sauce pairs perfectly with the savory turkey, creating a delightful contrast in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1/4 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack work well)
  • 4 flour tortillas
  • Butter for cooking
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Spread a thin layer of cranberry sauce on one side of each tortilla.
  2. Add the shredded turkey and cheese, then fold the tortillas in half.
  3. Heat butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Place the quesadillas in the skillet, cooking for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  5. Slice into wedges and garnish with fresh cilantro.
  6. Serve with a side of sour cream or more cranberry sauce for dipping.

4. Turkey and Sweet Potato Shepherd’s Pie

Shepherd’s pie is a classic comfort food, and using leftover turkey and sweet potatoes brings a wonderful fall twist to this dish. The sweet potatoes add a layer of sweetness that balances the savory turkey filling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 2 large sweet potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 1 carrot (diced)
  • 1 cup peas
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the sweet potatoes in a pot of water until tender, about 15 minutes. Drain, then mash with milk and butter. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. In a skillet, sauté the onion and carrot in butter until softened, about 7 minutes.
  3. Add the shredded turkey and peas, then sprinkle with flour. Stir to combine.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, stirring until the mixture thickens.
  5. Transfer the turkey mixture to a baking dish, spreading it evenly.
  6. Top with the mashed sweet potatoes and smooth them out.
  7. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20 minutes or until the top is golden brown.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy this fall comfort food favorite.

5. Turkey and Pumpkin Risotto

Pumpkin is one of the most beloved fall ingredients, and when combined with creamy risotto, it becomes a decadent dish. The addition of leftover turkey adds protein and heartiness, making this a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 can pure pumpkin puree
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 2 minutes, until lightly toasted.
  4. Slowly add the white wine (if using) and cook until it’s absorbed by the rice.
  5. Begin adding the chicken broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  6. After about 15 minutes, stir in the pumpkin puree and shredded turkey.
  7. Continue cooking until the rice is tender and creamy.
  8. Stir in Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve warm, garnished with extra cheese if desired.

6. Turkey and Mushroom Pot Pie

A warm, comforting pot pie is the epitome of fall cuisine, and using leftover turkey makes it both easy and flavorful. With a flaky crust and creamy filling, this dish is perfect for a cozy evening.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1 cup mushrooms (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 cup carrots (diced)
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, sauté the mushrooms, onions, and carrots in butter until softened.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Slowly add the chicken broth and milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
  5. Bring to a simmer and cook until thickened.
  6. Stir in the shredded turkey and peas, then season with salt and pepper.
  7. Transfer the turkey mixture into a baking dish and cover with the pie crust.
  8. Cut a few slits in the crust to allow steam to escape.
  9. Bake for 30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
  10. Serve hot, perfect for a chilly fall evening.

7. Turkey and Cornbread Casserole

Cornbread is another fall favorite, and when paired with leftover turkey, it becomes a delicious casserole that’s both filling and flavorful. This dish has a bit of everything, from savory turkey to the sweetness of cornbread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 2 cups cornbread (crumbled)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup cheese (cheddar works best)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the shredded turkey, crumbled cornbread, corn kernels, milk, cheese, eggs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until golden and set.
  4. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

8. Turkey and Pecan Salad

Fall salads don’t have to be boring. A turkey and pecan salad brings together savory turkey with the sweetness of apples and the crunch of roasted pecans. It’s a light yet satisfying meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 1 apple (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup pecans (toasted)
  • 1/4 cup red onion (thinly sliced)
  • Mixed greens (arugula, spinach, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinaigrette

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the turkey, apple slices, pecans, and red onion.
  2. Toss with mixed greens and drizzle with balsamic vinaigrette.
  3. Serve immediately for a light and refreshing fall meal.

9. Turkey and Spinach Lasagna

Lasagna is a fall classic, and by using leftover turkey, you can create a dish that’s just as hearty and comforting as the traditional beef version. With layers of turkey, spinach, and cheesy goodness, this turkey lasagna is sure to become a new family favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 9 lasagna noodles (cooked)
  • 2 cups spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté the spinach in olive oil until wilted, then combine it with the ricotta cheese.
  3. Spread a layer of marinara sauce at the bottom of a baking dish.
  4. Layer the lasagna noodles, followed by a layer of turkey, spinach-ricotta mixture, and mozzarella.
  5. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of mozzarella.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 10 minutes to brown the cheese.
  7. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

10. Turkey and Sage Breakfast Hash

Start your fall mornings off right with a savory turkey breakfast hash. Combining leftover turkey with sweet potatoes and fresh sage, this dish is the perfect way to fuel your day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey (shredded)
  • 2 sweet potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage (chopped)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced sweet potatoes and cook until tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing for another 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the shredded turkey and sage, cooking for an additional 5 minutes until everything is heated through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then serve with a fried egg on top for extra protein.

These 10 creative fall recipes make the most of your leftover turkey and turn it into a variety of delicious meals. From hearty stews and casseroles to fresh salads and comforting pies, there’s no shortage of ways to enjoy your leftovers in a new and exciting way. Whether you’re feeding a family or just looking for a quick and easy meal, these recipes will help you make the most of your turkey while embracing the flavors of fall.
