Tender and Flavorful: Slow-Cooked Pulled Pork Recipe


Are you craving a melt-in-your-mouth pulled pork that is tender, juicy, and bursting with flavor? Look no further! In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of slow-cooking the perfect pulled pork that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds of pork shoulder or Boston butt
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of paprika
  • 2 tablespoons of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 cup of chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the pork shoulder or Boston butt. Trim off any excess fat, leaving a thin layer to keep the meat moist during the long cooking process.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if desired). This flavorful rub will infuse the meat with a delicious blend of spices.

  3. Massage the spice rub onto the pork, making sure to coat it evenly on all sides. Allow the seasoned meat to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or refrigerate overnight for maximum flavor.

  4. Preheat your slow cooker on low heat. Place the seasoned pork shoulder or Boston butt into the slow cooker, fat side up, and pour in the chicken broth. The broth will add moisture and help to keep the meat tender throughout the cooking process.

  5. Cover the slow cooker and let the pork cook on low heat for 8 to 10 hours. The low and slow cooking method will allow the meat to become incredibly tender and flavorful.

  6. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the pork from the slow cooker and transfer it to a cutting board. Use two forks to shred the meat, pulling it apart into bite-sized pieces. The meat should be incredibly tender and easy to shred.

  7. Serve the pulled pork on a toasted bun or alongside your favorite side dishes. This versatile meat can be enjoyed in sandwiches, tacos, salads, or even on its own.

  8. For an extra touch of flavor, you can add your favorite barbecue sauce or a tangy coleslaw to complement the rich and savory taste of the pulled pork.

Tips for Success

  • For the best results, choose a pork shoulder or Boston butt with a good amount of marbling. This will ensure that the meat stays moist and tender throughout the cooking process.

  • Don’t rush the cooking time. Slow-cooking the pork for 8 to 10 hours will allow the flavors to develop and the meat to become incredibly tender. Patience is key!

  • Feel free to adjust the spice rub according to your personal taste preferences. If you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the amount of cayenne pepper or omit it entirely.

  • Leftover pulled pork can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well, so you can enjoy it at a later time.

Now that you have the recipe for the most tender and flavorful slow-cooked pulled pork, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking! Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a delicious homemade meal, this recipe is sure to impress. Enjoy the mouthwatering aroma and savor every bite of this delectable pulled pork creation. Happy cooking!
