The Classic Old Fashioned Truffles: A Whiskey-infused Delight


If you’re a fan of the classic Old Fashioned cocktail and have a sweet tooth, then you’re in for a treat with this delightful recipe for Old Fashioned Truffles. These decadent whiskey-infused truffles combine the rich, warming flavors of bourbon with the creamy richness of chocolate, creating a luxurious confection that is sure to impress. Whether you’re looking to indulge in a sophisticated dessert or create a unique gift for a whiskey-loving friend, these Old Fashioned Truffles are the perfect choice.

Ingredients

To create these delectable Old Fashioned Truffles, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of high-quality dark chocolate, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of bourbon whiskey
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • Zest of one orange
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chocolate

Begin by placing the finely chopped dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream and unsalted butter over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Pour the hot cream mixture over the chopped chocolate and let it sit for a minute to soften the chocolate. Gently stir the mixture until the chocolate is completely melted and the ganache is smooth.

Step 2: Add the Flavorings

Once the chocolate ganache is smooth, stir in the bourbon whiskey, pure vanilla extract, orange zest, and a pinch of sea salt. These flavorings will infuse the truffles with the distinct Old Fashioned cocktail taste, giving them a sophisticated and indulgent flavor profile.

Step 3: Chill the Ganache

Cover the bowl of chocolate ganache with plastic wrap, ensuring that the plastic wrap touches the surface of the ganache to prevent a skin from forming. Place the ganache in the refrigerator and let it chill for at least 4 hours, or until it is firm enough to scoop and roll into truffle balls.

Step 4: Form the Truffles

Once the ganache has chilled and firmed up, use a small spoon or a melon baller to scoop out portions of the ganache. Roll each portion between your palms to form smooth, round truffle balls. Place the formed truffles on a parchment-lined baking sheet and return them to the refrigerator to chill while you prepare the cocoa powder for dusting.

Step 5: Dust with Cocoa Powder

Before serving, remove the chilled truffles from the refrigerator and roll them in cocoa powder to coat. The cocoa powder not only adds a beautiful finishing touch to the truffles but also provides a delightful contrast to the creamy, whiskey-infused centers.

Serving and Storage

These Old Fashioned Truffles are best enjoyed at room temperature, allowing the flavors to fully bloom. Serve them as a sophisticated dessert alongside a glass of bourbon or wrap them in a decorative box to give as a thoughtful and indulgent gift.

Store any leftover truffles in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two weeks, though they are likely to disappear much sooner due to their irresistible appeal.

In Conclusion

With their rich, chocolatey exterior and luscious whiskey-infused centers, these Old Fashioned Truffles are a delightful indulgence for anyone who appreciates the classic cocktail. Whether you’re preparing them for a special occasion or as a unique gift, these truffles are sure to leave a lasting impression with their luxurious flavor and elegant presentation. So, channel your inner chocolatier and treat yourself or your loved ones to the sophisticated pleasure of Old Fashioned Truffles. Cheers to decadent delights!
