Flavorful Thanksgiving Grilling Recipes for a Unique Twist


Are you tired of the same old Thanksgiving recipes year after year? Why not shake things up and add a unique twist to your holiday feast? This year, try grilling your Thanksgiving meal for a burst of flavor that will leave your guests begging for seconds. From juicy turkey to perfectly charred vegetables, these flavorful Thanksgiving grilling recipes are sure to impress.

Grilled Turkey with Herb Butter

Who says you need an oven to cook the perfect Thanksgiving turkey? Grilling adds a smoky depth of flavor that you just can’t achieve with traditional roasting. Start by preparing a herb butter by mixing softened butter with minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, and sage, and a sprinkle of salt and pepper. Rub this flavorful mixture all over the turkey, making sure to get it under the skin for maximum flavor.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat and place the turkey on the grill, breast-side up. Close the lid and let the magic happen. Cook the turkey for about 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Don’t forget to baste the turkey with the herb butter every 30 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving to allow the juices to redistribute.

Grilled Sweet Potato Casserole

Forget the marshmallows and canned yams - this grilled sweet potato casserole takes Thanksgiving side dishes to a whole new level. Start by slicing sweet potatoes into 1/4-inch rounds and toss them in a mixture of olive oil, brown sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Grill the sweet potato rounds over medium heat until they are tender and slightly charred, about 4-5 minutes per side.

While the sweet potatoes are grilling, prepare a decadent pecan topping by combining chopped pecans, brown sugar, melted butter, and a touch of cinnamon. Once the sweet potatoes are done, transfer them to a baking dish and sprinkle the pecan topping over the top. Place the dish back on the grill and let it cook for an additional 10 minutes, or until the topping is golden and bubbly. Serve this sweet and savory side dish alongside your grilled turkey for a Thanksgiving feast like no other.

Grilled Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts often get a bad rap, but when grilled to perfection, they become a show-stopping side dish that even the pickiest eaters will love. Start by trimming the ends off the Brussels sprouts and cutting them in half. Toss them in a mixture of olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Preheat your grill to medium heat and place the Brussels sprouts cut-side down on the grates. Grill them for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are tender and charred, turning occasionally for even cooking. While the Brussels sprouts are grilling, reduce the remaining marinade in a small saucepan over low heat until it becomes thick and syrupy.

Once the Brussels sprouts are done, drizzle the balsamic glaze over the top and serve them immediately. The combination of smoky charred Brussels sprouts and tangy balsamic glaze will make this dish a Thanksgiving favorite.

Grilled Pumpkin Pie

Why limit grilling to savory dishes when you can also grill your dessert? Grilled pumpkin pie is a unique twist on a classic Thanksgiving dessert. Start by preparing your favorite pumpkin pie filling, whether it’s from scratch or a convenient mix. Pour the filling into a pre-made pie crust and place it on the grill over indirect heat. Close the lid and let the grill work its magic.

Grill the pumpkin pie for about 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. Keep an eye on it to ensure it doesn’t burn. Once the pie is done, let it cool completely before serving. The smoky flavor from the grill adds a whole new dimension to the traditional pumpkin pie, making it a memorable end to your Thanksgiving meal.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, break away from tradition and add a unique twist to your holiday feast by grilling your favorite dishes. From a perfectly grilled turkey with herb butter to charred Brussels sprouts with balsamic glaze, these flavorful recipes are sure to impress your guests and create lasting memories. Embrace the smoky flavors and enjoy a Thanksgiving feast like no other. Happy grilling!
