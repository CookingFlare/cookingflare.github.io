Homemade Paleo Granola Bars: A Simple and Delicious Recipe for Your Next Snack


As we all know, finding a healthy and convenient snack that meets our dietary needs can be a real challenge. Whether you’re following a paleo diet, gluten-free or simply looking for a tasty treat, granola bars are a popular choice. However, many store-bought options are loaded with added sugars, preservatives and artificial ingredients that can hinder our health goals. That’s why making your own homemade granola bars is a game-changer.

In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make delicious and nutritious homemade paleo granola bars using simple, wholesome ingredients. These bars are perfect for snacking on the go, as a post-workout treat or as a healthy breakfast option. Plus, you can customize the recipe to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts or pecans)
  • 1/2 cup dried fruit (cranberries, raisins or cherries)
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: chocolate chips, cinnamon or other spices to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8 inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the oats, nuts and dried fruit. Stir until well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the honey, coconut oil and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and stir until everything is well coated.
  5. Press the mixture into the prepared baking dish. If using, sprinkle with chocolate chips, cinnamon or other spices.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, use a sharp knife or cookie cutter to cut the granola mixture into bars. You can also use a spatula to break it up into chunks if you prefer.
  9. Store your homemade paleo granola bars in an airtight container for up to a week.

Tips and Variations:

  • To add some crunch, try adding some chopped seeds like sunflower or pumpkin seeds.
  • For a chocolatey twist, use cocoa powder or melted chocolate chips in place of some of the honey.
  • If you prefer a chewier texture, bake the granola for 15-20 minutes. If you prefer a crunchier texture, bake for 25-30 minutes.
  • Experiment with different spices like cinnamon, nutmeg or ginger to give your granola bars a unique flavor.

Benefits of Making Your Own Granola Bars:

  • Control over ingredients: By making your own granola bars, you can choose the ingredients that go into them, ensuring they meet your dietary needs and preferences.
  • Cost-effective: Making your own granola bars can be more cost-effective than buying them at the store.
  • Customization: With this recipe, you can customize the ingredients and flavors to suit your taste preferences.
  • No artificial preservatives: By using simple, wholesome ingredients, you can avoid the added preservatives found in many store-bought granola bars.

In conclusion, making your own homemade paleo granola bars is a simple and delicious way to enjoy a healthy snack that meets your dietary needs. With this recipe, you can customize the ingredients and flavors to suit your taste preferences and avoid the added preservatives found in many store-bought options. Give it a try and enjoy the benefits of making your own granola bars!
