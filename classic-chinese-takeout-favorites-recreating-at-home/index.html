Classic Chinese Takeout Favorites: Recreating at Home


Chinese takeout has become a staple in many households, offering a convenient and delicious option for those nights when cooking feels like too much of a chore. But have you ever wondered if you could recreate those classic Chinese takeout favorites in the comfort of your own home? Well, wonder no more! In this blog post, we will explore some of the most beloved Chinese takeout dishes and provide you with easy-to-follow recipes that will have you cooking like a pro in no time.

1. General Tso’s Chicken

General Tso’s Chicken is a crowd-pleaser, known for its crispy exterior and sweet and tangy sauce. Recreating this dish at home is easier than you might think. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 green onions, sliced

To make General Tso’s Chicken:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the chicken pieces with cornstarch until evenly coated.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes (if using).
  4. Pour the sauce into a large skillet or wok and heat over medium heat. Add the fried chicken and toss until coated in the sauce.
  5. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and the chicken is heated through.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with sliced green onions.

2. Beef with Broccoli

Beef with Broccoli is a classic Chinese dish that combines tender beef and crisp broccoli florets in a savory brown sauce. Here’s how you can recreate this takeout favorite at home:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots
  • Cooked rice, for serving

To make Beef with Broccoli:

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce and cornstarch. Add the sliced beef and toss until coated. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the marinated beef to the skillet and cook until browned, about 2-3 minutes. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add beef broth, oyster sauce, and hoisin sauce. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add broccoli florets and sliced carrots to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Return the cooked beef to the skillet and toss until everything is coated in the sauce.
  7. Serve hot over cooked rice.

3. Sweet and Sour Pork

Sweet and Sour Pork is a classic Chinese takeout dish that combines juicy pork, tangy pineapple, and a sweet and sour sauce. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • Cooked rice, for serving

To make Sweet and Sour Pork:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the pork pieces with cornstarch until evenly coated.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Fry the pork in batches until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together ketchup, rice vinegar, brown sugar, soy sauce, and cornstarch.
  4. Pour the sauce into a large skillet or wok and heat over medium heat. Add the pineapple chunks and bell peppers, and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  5. Add the fried pork to the skillet and toss until coated in the sauce.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and the pork is heated through.
  7. Serve hot over cooked rice.

Conclusion

With these easy-to-follow recipes, you can now recreate your favorite Chinese takeout dishes right in your own kitchen. From General Tso’s Chicken to Beef with Broccoli and Sweet and Sour Pork, these classics are sure to satisfy your cravings. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to impress your family and friends with your homemade Chinese takeout feast.

Happy cooking!
