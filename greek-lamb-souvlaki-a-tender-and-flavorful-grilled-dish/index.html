Greek Lamb Souvlaki: A Tender and Flavorful Grilled Dish


If you’re a fan of Mediterranean cuisine, you’re probably no stranger to the tantalizing flavors of Greek cuisine. One dish that stands out among the array of Greek delicacies is the succulent and aromatic Greek Lamb Souvlaki. This classic grilled dish is a staple of Greek street food and is loved for its tender, juicy meat and the vibrant blend of Mediterranean spices. In this post, we’ll delve into the art of preparing the perfect Greek Lamb Souvlaki, from marinating the meat to grilling it to perfection. So, let’s fire up the grill and embark on a culinary journey to the sun-kissed shores of Greece!

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 2 lbs (900g) lamb leg or shoulder, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Tzatziki Sauce:

  • 1 cup (240g) Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Pita bread
  • Sliced red onions
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

  1. Marinating the Lamb:

    • In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and black pepper. Add the lamb chunks to the marinade and ensure they are well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to infuse into the meat.

  2. Preparing the Tzatziki Sauce:

    • In another bowl, mix the Greek yogurt, grated and drained cucumber, minced garlic, extra virgin olive oil, chopped dill, and salt. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Cover the bowl and refrigerate until ready to serve.

  3. Skewering the Lamb:

    • Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated lamb chunks onto skewers, ensuring an even distribution to promote even cooking.

  4. Grilling the Souvlaki:

    • Place the lamb skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the lamb is cooked to your desired level of doneness and has a beautiful charred exterior.

  5. Assembling the Souvlaki:

    • Warm the pita bread on the grill for a minute on each side. To serve, place a couple of lamb skewers on a piece of warm pita bread, top with the sliced red onions, tomatoes, and a dollop of the prepared tzatziki sauce. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Tips for Perfect Souvlaki

  • For the most succulent and flavorful results, use lamb leg or shoulder that has been trimmed of excess fat and cut into evenly sized chunks.
  • Don’t skimp on the marinating time – allowing the lamb to marinate for at least 2 hours will ensure that it absorbs the full spectrum of flavors.

Final Thoughts

The Greek Lamb Souvlaki is a dish that encapsulates the essence of Greek cuisine – simple yet bursting with vibrant flavors. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this tender and flavorful grilled dish is sure to delight your taste buds. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and treat yourself to a culinary adventure that transports you to the sun-drenched landscapes of Greece! Opa!
