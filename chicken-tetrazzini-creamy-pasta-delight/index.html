Chicken Tetrazzini: Creamy Pasta Delight


When it comes to comforting, creamy pasta dishes, few recipes stand out like Chicken Tetrazzini. This timeless casserole combines tender chicken, perfectly cooked spaghetti, and a rich, savory sauce that’s truly irresistible. Whether served for a cozy family dinner or a festive gathering, Chicken Tetrazzini offers warmth and satisfaction in every bite.

What is Chicken Tetrazzini?

Originating in the early 20th century, Chicken Tetrazzini is named after Italian opera star Luisa Tetrazzini. While the dish has evolved over time, it traditionally features poultry (often chicken or turkey), pasta such as spaghetti or linguine, and a creamy mushroom sauce baked under a blanket of cheese. The magic lies in how these simple ingredients meld together to create a decadent and flavorful casserole.

Why Chicken Tetrazzini is a Crowd Favorite

  • Creaminess: The sauce is buttery, velvety smooth, and indulgently rich.
  • Hearty Protein: Tender chicken pieces provide satisfying protein that pairs perfectly with pasta.
  • Mushroom Flavor: Earthy mushrooms add depth and a subtle umami kick.
  • Cheesy Topping: A golden, bubbling cheese crust creates a perfect textural contrast.
  • Versatility: Substitute chicken for turkey, use different cheeses, or add vegetables like peas or bell peppers.
  • Great Make-Ahead: This dish reheats wonderfully and even tastes better the next day.

Ingredients Breakdown

Here’s a closer look at the staple ingredients that bring Chicken Tetrazzini to life:

  • Chicken: Skinless, boneless chicken breast or thighs work well. Cooked and shredded or cubed, they infuse the dish with protein richness.
  • Pasta: Spaghetti remains the classic choice, but linguine or fettuccine are lovely alternatives. Cook pasta al dente to avoid mushiness during baking.
  • Butter and Flour: These form the base of a roux, thickening the creamy sauce.
  • Chicken Broth and Milk: These liquids create a flavorful and luscious sauce when combined with the roux.
  • Mushrooms: Fresh cremini or white button mushrooms sautéed until tender add an earthy undertone.
  • Garlic and Onion: Aromatics bring warmth and depth.
  • Parmesan and Mozzarella Cheese: Parmesan adds a sharp bite, while mozzarella melts for gooey deliciousness.
  • White Wine (Optional): A splash of dry white wine elevates the sauce complexity.
  • Breadcrumbs: Sprinkled atop for a crunchy, golden crust.
  • Seasonings: Salt, pepper, and a pinch of thyme or nutmeg complement the flavors.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparing the Chicken

  1. Cook the chicken: Season the chicken breasts or thighs with salt and pepper. In a sauté pan with olive oil, cook over medium heat until no longer pink inside (about 7-8 minutes per side). Alternatively, you can roast or poach the chicken.
  2. Shred or dice: Once cool enough to handle, shred the chicken using two forks or chop into bite-sized cubes.

Cooking the Pasta

  1. Boil the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti or preferred pasta until al dente, typically 8-10 minutes. Drain and rinse briefly with cold water to stop cooking.

Making the Creamy Sauce

  1. Sauté vegetables: In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add finely chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until translucent. Add sliced mushrooms and cook until they release moisture and brown slightly.
  2. Create the roux: Sprinkle flour over the mushroom mixture and stir constantly to form a paste. Cook for 1-2 minutes to eliminate raw flour taste.
  3. Add liquids: Gradually whisk in chicken broth and milk, ensuring the mixture remains smooth. Add the optional white wine here if using.
  4. Simmer and thicken: Continue stirring until the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a touch of thyme or nutmeg.

Combining the Elements

  1. Mix chicken and pasta with sauce: In a large bowl or directly in the skillet, combine shredded chicken, cooked pasta, and creamy mushroom sauce. Fold in half of the grated Parmesan and mozzarella cheese.

Baking the Tetrazzini

  1. Preheat oven: Set oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Prepare casserole dish: Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick spray.
  3. Assemble: Pour the pasta and chicken mixture into the dish. Sprinkle remaining cheese and a generous layer of breadcrumbs evenly on top.
  4. Bake: Place in the preheated oven and bake uncovered for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Tetrazzini

  • Use freshly grated Parmesan: Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that affect melting.
  • Sauté mushrooms well: This step concentrates flavor and removes excess moisture.
  • Do not overcook pasta: Pasta will continue to cook in the oven, so undercooking slightly helps maintain the ideal texture.
  • Let the dish rest: After baking, allow it to sit for 5-10 minutes to set, making it easier to serve.
  • Make it ahead: Prepare everything up to the baking step, then refrigerate. When ready, pop it in the oven, adding 10-15 extra minutes to the bake time if cold.

Variations to Try

  • Turkey Tetrazzini: A great way to use up Thanksgiving leftovers.
  • Vegetarian Version: Swap chicken for sautéed mushrooms, zucchini, or artichoke hearts.
  • Add a Kick: A pinch of red pepper flakes or chopped jalapeños adds some heat.
  • Different Cheeses: Gruyère or Fontina deliver a nutty complexity.
  • Gluten-Free: Use gluten-free pasta and substitute flour in the sauce with cornstarch or a gluten-free flour blend.

Serving Suggestions

Chicken Tetrazzini is a meal on its own, but pairing it with complementary dishes enhances the dining experience.

  • Simple green salad: Crisp mixed greens with a light vinaigrette balance the richness of the casserole.
  • Garlic bread: Crunchy bread with a buttery garlic spread is perfect for sopping up leftover sauce.
  • Roasted vegetables: Asparagus, Brussels sprouts, or carrots add a fresh and colorful side.
  • Cranberry relish: In colder months, a sweet-tart cranberry side adds interest.

Nutrition Snapshot

While indulgent and creamy, Chicken Tetrazzini can be made healthier by choosing lean chicken cuts, reducing butter, and adding more vegetables. This dish delivers a satisfying balance of protein, carbohydrates, and fats, providing a well-rounded meal that keeps you full and energized.

Final Thoughts

Chicken Tetrazzini is an elegant yet homey dish that stands the test of time. Its creamy sauce, tender chicken, and perfectly baked cheese topping make it a comforting delight for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned cook looking to impress or a beginner craving a reliable crowd-pleaser, this recipe is straightforward and rewarding.

Next time you crave rich pasta, give Chicken Tetrazzini a try — it’s a creamy pasta delight that promises to satisfy and impress.

Happy cooking!

Ingredients Checklist

  • 2-3 cups cooked chicken (shredded or cubed)
  • 8 oz spaghetti or pasta of choice
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 1/2 cups milk (whole or 2%)
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt, pepper, thyme or nutmeg to taste

By focusing on fundamental technique and simple, fresh ingredients, Chicken Tetrazzini becomes more than just a casserole; it’s a creamy pasta masterpiece that brings familiarity and luxury to your dinner table. Give it a try and savor every creamy, cheesy bite!
