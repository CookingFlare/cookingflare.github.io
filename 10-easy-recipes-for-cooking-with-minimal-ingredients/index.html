10 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Minimal Ingredients


Cooking at home doesn’t have to be complicated or require a long list of ingredients. Sometimes, simplicity in the kitchen leads to the most satisfying meals. Whether you’re short on ingredients, pressed for time, or just prefer straightforward cooking, these 10 easy recipes are perfect for getting delicious meals on the table with minimal fuss. Each dish uses just a handful of core ingredients, yet delivers on flavor and satisfaction.

1. Classic Garlic Butter Spaghetti

A comforting and timeless pasta dish that’s ready in under 20 minutes and uses only a few pantry staples.

Ingredients:

  • Spaghetti (200g)
  • Butter (3 tablespoons)
  • Garlic (3 cloves, minced)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (freshly ground, to taste)
  • Parmesan cheese (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, melt butter in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned (about 1 minute).
  4. Drain pasta, reserving a little pasta water.
  5. Toss spaghetti with the garlic butter, adding a splash of pasta water if needed to create a silky sauce.
  6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  7. Serve topped with Parmesan if desired.

This minimalist dish highlights how garlic and butter can transform simple noodles into a luscious meal.

2. One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken

Perfect for busy weeknights when you want a hearty yet uncomplicated protein dish.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken thighs (boneless, skin-on, 4 pieces)
  • Lemon (1, sliced)
  • Olive oil (2 tablespoons)
  • Fresh or dried herbs (rosemary, thyme, or oregano – 1 teaspoon)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pat chicken dry, then rub all over with olive oil, herbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place chicken thighs on a baking sheet, scatter lemon slices on top and around.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through with a golden, crispy skin.
  5. Let rest 5 minutes before serving.

The citrus brightness paired with herbs and crispy skin makes this simple chicken anything but boring.

3. Creamy Avocado Toast with Egg

Elevate your breakfast or snack with a nutritious, decadent option.

Ingredients:

  • Bread (2 slices, preferably sourdough or whole grain)
  • Ripe avocado (1)
  • Egg (1 or 2)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast bread to your liking.
  2. Mash avocado with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Spread avocado evenly over the toast.
  4. Cook eggs sunny side up or poached, then place on top of the avocado.
  5. Sprinkle with more salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.

This recipe balances creamy avocado with a protein boost from the egg, making a satisfying meal with minimal ingredients.

4. Simple Tomato Basil Soup

A warm, nourishing soup made with pantry and fridge staples that’s ready in 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • Canned crushed tomatoes (1 can, 400g)
  • Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
  • Onion (1 small, diced)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Olive oil (2 tablespoons)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot and sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Add crushed tomatoes and bring to a simmer.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, add basil leaves, and cook for 15 minutes.
  4. Remove basil leaves, then blend the soup until smooth.
  5. Serve warm with an optional drizzle of olive oil.

Despite using few ingredients, this soup is rich with fresh flavors and comforting depth.

5. Tuna Salad Lettuce Wraps

Light, protein-packed, and perfect for a quick lunch or light dinner.

Ingredients:

  • Canned tuna (1 can, drained)
  • Mayonnaise (2 tablespoons)
  • Dijon mustard (1 teaspoon)
  • Celery (1 stalk, chopped, optional)
  • Romaine or butter lettuce leaves (4-6)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix tuna, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, celery (if using), salt and pepper.
  2. Spoon the mixture into lettuce leaves.
  3. Wrap and serve.

This no-fuss recipe is refreshing, healthy, and perfect for warm weather or low-carb diets.

6. Fried Rice with Egg and Green Onion

A fantastic way to transform leftover rice into a flavorful meal with a handful of ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • Cooked white rice (2 cups, preferably cold)
  • Eggs (2)
  • Green onions (2-3, chopped)
  • Soy sauce (2 tablespoons)
  • Oil (vegetable or sesame, 2 tablespoons)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
  2. Scramble eggs and set aside.
  3. Add rice to the pan, breaking up clumps, and stir-fry until heated through.
  4. Mix eggs back in, add green onions and soy sauce.
  5. Stir well and serve immediately.

This simple fried rice is quick, adaptable, and delivers satisfying umami with ease.

7. Baked Sweet Potatoes with Black Beans

An easy and nutritious vegetarian dish that’s hearty enough for dinner.

Ingredients:

  • Medium sweet potatoes (2)
  • Canned black beans (1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • Olive oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Cumin (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: sour cream or Greek yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Pierce sweet potatoes with a fork and bake directly on oven rack for 45-60 minutes.
  3. While potatoes bake, heat olive oil in a pan, add black beans, cumin, salt, and pepper. Warm through.
  4. Slice open baked sweet potatoes and stuff with black beans.
  5. Add a dollop of sour cream or yogurt if desired.

This recipe combines natural sweetness with savory protein, making it a balanced and delicious dinner.

8. Cheesy Baked Eggs in Tomato Cups

A delightful breakfast or brunch idea that feels fancy but is ridiculously easy.

Ingredients:

  • Large tomatoes (2)
  • Eggs (2)
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella, 1/2 cup)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil (for greasing)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the tops off tomatoes and scoop out the insides to create cups.
  3. Lightly oil a small baking dish, place tomato cups inside.
  4. Crack an egg into each tomato cup.
  5. Sprinkle cheese, salt, and pepper on top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until eggs are cooked to preference.

These baked eggs are visually appealing and pack a tasty punch with minimal ingredients.

9. Roasted Chickpeas with Paprika

A crunchy, healthy snack that can be whipped up quickly for nibbling or to top salads.

Ingredients:

  • Canned chickpeas (1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • Olive oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Smoked paprika (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Dry chickpeas as best as possible with a kitchen towel.
  3. Toss chickpeas with olive oil, paprika, and salt.
  4. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20-30 minutes, shaking halfway through, until crispy.

This recipe turns humble canned chickpeas into an addictive and nutritious snack.

10. Peanut Butter Banana Smoothie

A quick, creamy beverage that requires no cooking and is perfect on the go.

Ingredients:

  • Ripe banana (1)
  • Peanut butter (2 tablespoons)
  • Milk (dairy or plant-based, 1 cup)
  • Honey or maple syrup (optional, 1 teaspoon)
  • Ice cubes (a handful)

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth.
  3. Taste and add sweetener if desired.
  4. Serve immediately.

This smoothie is packed with energy and flavor from just a few common ingredients.

Final Thoughts

Minimal ingredient recipes are a great way to keep cooking simple without sacrificing flavor or nutrition. The key is to focus on quality ingredients and straightforward techniques that bring their natural tastes to the forefront. Whether feeding yourself or others, these dishes prove that delicious meals don’t need to be complicated. Give these recipes a try the next time you’re staring into a nearly empty fridge – you might be surprised by what a handful of ingredients can create.

Happy cooking!
