Whip Up a Batch of Homemade Tomato Chutney


There’s something incredibly satisfying about making your own condiments at home. The process of blending fresh ingredients, infusing flavors, and creating something utterly delicious from scratch is a joy to experience. If you’re a fan of spicy, tangy, and versatile condiments, then homemade tomato chutney is a must-try recipe for you. This flavorful chutney is a perfect accompaniment to a wide range of dishes, from Indian curries to cheese platters and sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chili flakes
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • Olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Start by blanching the tomatoes in boiling water for 1 minute, then transfer them to an ice bath. Once cooled, peel off the skins, chop the tomatoes, and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat a few tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until the onions turn translucent and the mixture becomes fragrant.

  3. Infuse the Spices: Add the mustard seeds, cumin seeds, red chili flakes, and cinnamon stick to the saucepan. Stir the spices into the onion mixture and let them toast for a minute to release their flavors.

  4. Add the Tomatoes: Once the spices are fragrant, add the chopped tomatoes to the saucepan. Stir well to combine the ingredients.

  5. Simmer and Sweeten: Pour in the apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Stir the mixture and bring it to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat and let the chutney simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  6. Incorporate the Raisins: After the chutney has simmered and thickened, add the raisins to the saucepan. Continue to simmer the chutney for an additional 15 minutes until it reaches a jam-like consistency.

  7. Cool and Store: Once the chutney has thickened to your desired consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool. Transfer the tomato chutney to sterilized glass jars and store them in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade tomato chutney is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in numerous ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Pair with Cheese: Serve the tomato chutney alongside a selection of cheeses for a delightful sweet and spicy contrast.

  • Accompany Indian Dishes: Add a dollop of tomato chutney to your favorite Indian curries, grilled meats, or rice dishes for an extra burst of flavor.

  • Sandwich Spread: Spread the chutney on sandwiches or wraps to elevate the flavor profile of your lunch.

  • Condiment for Snacks: Use the chutney as a condiment for samosas, pakoras, or any savory snack for an added kick.

In Conclusion

Whipping up a batch of homemade tomato chutney is a rewarding experience that yields a flavorful condiment bursting with the freshness of ripe tomatoes and the warmth of aromatic spices. This versatile chutney is sure to become a staple in your kitchen, adding a delightful touch to a wide array of dishes. Whether you’re a fan of spicy flavors, a lover of Indian cuisine, or simply enjoy experimenting with new recipes, this homemade tomato chutney is a must-try. Enjoy the process of creating this delicious condiment and savor the delightful results in your culinary creations!
