Cooking with Fresh Cilantro: The Best Recipes for Tacos


Cilantro is an incredibly versatile herb that brings a burst of freshness and flavor to a wide range of dishes. It’s particularly essential in Mexican cuisine, where its bright, citrusy notes can elevate tacos from simple to extraordinary. If you’re looking to take your taco nights to the next level, fresh cilantro is the key ingredient you need. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best taco recipes that make cilantro the star of the show.

The Magic of Cilantro in Tacos

Before diving into recipes, it’s important to understand what makes cilantro such a crucial addition to tacos. Fresh cilantro adds both fragrance and flavor. Its leaves have a slightly peppery taste with citrus undertones, making it the perfect complement to the bold flavors of tacos. Whether used as a garnish, in salsas, or in marinades, cilantro enhances the overall taste and balances out spicier elements in tacos.

In Mexican cuisine, cilantro is typically paired with onions, lime, and chili peppers to create a classic taco topping. However, cilantro’s potential goes beyond just garnishing tacos—it’s a key ingredient in marinades, sauces, and even taco fillings themselves.

Classic Street Tacos with Cilantro

One of the simplest and most popular ways to enjoy cilantro in tacos is through classic street tacos. These tacos are quick to make, and the fresh cilantro really shines through as a garnish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb of carne asada (beef or chicken)
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bunch of fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Salsa (your choice of hot or mild)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Carne Asada: Season your carne asada with salt and pepper. Grill it on medium-high heat for about 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Once cooked, let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it thinly against the grain.

  2. Warm the Tortillas: Heat your corn tortillas on a hot skillet for about 30 seconds on each side, just until they become soft and warm.

  3. Assemble the Tacos: Place the grilled carne asada slices onto each tortilla. Top with finely chopped onions and a generous amount of fresh cilantro. Squeeze a lime wedge over the taco and add salsa to taste.

  4. Serve: Serve your tacos with additional lime wedges and salsa on the side. Enjoy the vibrant flavors of the fresh cilantro combined with the savory beef or chicken.

These tacos are a street-food favorite for a reason. The combination of tender carne asada, fresh cilantro, and zesty lime creates a flavor explosion in every bite.

Grilled Fish Tacos with Cilantro Slaw

Fish tacos are a lighter alternative to meat-based tacos and make the perfect dish for a summer evening. The addition of a cilantro slaw enhances the tacos with a crisp, refreshing bite.

Ingredients for Fish Tacos:

  • 1 lb white fish fillets (such as tilapia, cod, or mahi-mahi)
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Lime wedges for serving

Ingredients for Cilantro Slaw:

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage (green or purple)
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fish: Preheat your grill or grill pan. Brush the fish fillets with olive oil and season with paprika, garlic powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Grill the fish for 3–4 minutes per side or until it’s fully cooked and flakes easily with a fork.

  2. Make the Cilantro Slaw: In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, julienned carrot, and fresh cilantro. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sour cream, lime juice, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper. Pour the dressing over the slaw and toss until everything is well coated.

  3. Assemble the Tacos: Warm your tortillas on the grill or in a skillet. Place a few pieces of grilled fish onto each tortilla, then top with a generous spoonful of cilantro slaw. Add a slice of avocado and a squeeze of lime juice for extra freshness.

  4. Serve: Serve these grilled fish tacos immediately, with extra lime wedges and a side of salsa if desired.

The cilantro slaw provides a creamy, tangy contrast to the smoky fish, making these tacos a true flavor delight.

Cilantro Lime Chicken Tacos

Chicken tacos are a crowd favorite, and when paired with a zesty cilantro-lime marinade, they become irresistibly flavorful. The marinade infuses the chicken with the fresh and vibrant flavors of cilantro, making these tacos a hit at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lime (zested and juiced)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small soft corn or flour tortillas
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, combine olive oil, chopped cilantro, garlic, lime juice and zest, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken breasts or thighs into a resealable plastic bag or bowl. Pour the marinade over the chicken and seal the bag or cover the bowl. Let it marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 hours for the best flavor.

  3. Cook the Chicken: Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the marinated chicken for about 6–7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked and internal temperature reaches 165°F. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.

  4. Assemble the Tacos: Slice the grilled chicken into thin strips. Warm the tortillas and place the chicken onto each tortilla. Top with sliced red onion, fresh cilantro, and a few slices of avocado.

  5. Serve: Serve with lime wedges and your favorite salsa for an extra burst of flavor.

These cilantro lime chicken tacos are bursting with fresh, zesty flavors that will leave everyone asking for more.

Pork Carnitas Tacos with Cilantro and Onion

Carnitas is slow-cooked, tender pork that is perfect for making tacos. The meat is cooked with spices, shredded, and then crisped up to create a delicious taco filling. When paired with cilantro and onions, these tacos are nothing short of amazing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 bunch of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Slow Cook the Pork: In a slow cooker, combine the pork shoulder, quartered onion, garlic, bay leaves, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Add enough water to cover the pork, then set the slow cooker to low and cook for 6–8 hours, or until the pork is tender and easily shreds with a fork.

  2. Shred the Pork: Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and shred the meat with two forks. Discard any fat and bones. If you like crispy carnitas, heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add the shredded pork. Cook for about 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pork becomes crispy and golden.

  3. Prepare the Tacos: Warm the corn tortillas on a skillet or grill. Fill each tortilla with the shredded carnitas and top with finely chopped cilantro and onion.

  4. Serve: Serve your carnitas tacos with lime wedges and salsa for extra flavor.

These carnitas tacos are a perfect combination of savory, tender pork, fresh cilantro, and the bite of onions, making for a truly mouthwatering experience.

Cilantro-Based Salsa Verde for Tacos

No taco is complete without a good salsa, and cilantro is a crucial ingredient in many Mexican salsas. Salsa verde, made with tomatillos and cilantro, is a tangy and vibrant sauce that pairs perfectly with any taco filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb tomatillos, husked and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1–2 jalapeños (depending on your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Tomatillos: In a saucepan, bring water to a boil and add the tomatillos. Boil for about 5–7 minutes until they soften and change color.

  2. Blend the Salsa: In a blender, combine the cooked tomatillos, onion, jalapeños, cilantro, garlic, lime juice, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  3. Serve: Pour the salsa over your tacos or serve it on the side as a dipping sauce.

This salsa verde will add a tangy, cilantro-infused kick to any taco filling, making it a great accompaniment to the recipes above.

Conclusion

Fresh cilantro is a must-have ingredient when it comes to creating flavorful and vibrant tacos. Whether you’re making classic street tacos, grilled fish tacos, or slow-cooked carnitas, cilantro brings a refreshing brightness that enhances every bite. The possibilities are endless when you cook with fresh cilantro, so feel free to experiment with different taco fillings, salsas, and toppings. With these recipes, you can elevate your taco game and create delicious, cilantro-infused tacos that will have everyone coming back for more.

So next time you’re planning a taco night, make sure to stock up on fresh cilantro—your taste buds will thank you!
