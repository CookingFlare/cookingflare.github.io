Tempting Mexican Wedding Cookies: A Sweet Tradition


If you’re a fan of sweet treats, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Mexican Wedding Cookies. These delectable little delights are not only delicious but also hold a special place in Mexican wedding traditions. Made with a few simple ingredients, these cookies are easy to make and will have your taste buds dancing with joy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup finely chopped pecans or almonds

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. This can be done using an electric mixer or by hand, whichever you prefer.

  3. Add the vanilla extract to the butter mixture and mix until well combined.

  4. Gradually add the flour to the butter mixture, mixing well after each addition. The dough should come together and be slightly crumbly.

  5. Stir in the chopped pecans or almonds until evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  6. Take small portions of the dough and roll them into 1-inch balls. Place the balls onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart.

  7. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until they are lightly golden brown around the edges.

  8. Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. While they are still warm, roll them in powdered sugar until they are completely coated.

  9. Transfer the cookies to a wire rack to cool completely. Once they are cooled, you can roll them in powdered sugar again for an extra coating if desired.

  10. Serve and enjoy these delightful Mexican Wedding Cookies! They are perfect for any occasion and make a great addition to your dessert table.

The Tradition Behind Mexican Wedding Cookies

Mexican Wedding Cookies, also known as Polvorones, are a traditional treat that is often served at weddings and other special occasions in Mexico. These cookies are loved for their delicate texture and buttery flavor, which makes them irresistible to anyone who takes a bite.

The name “Mexican Wedding Cookies” is derived from the tradition of serving these cookies at weddings. In Mexican culture, it is believed that the sweet and crumbly texture of these cookies symbolizes the sweetness and fragility of a marriage. The powdered sugar coating represents the sweetness that should always be present in a relationship.

The act of rolling the cookies in powdered sugar is also symbolic. It is said that the powdered sugar represents the blessings and good wishes bestowed upon the couple. By rolling the cookies in this sweet powder, it is believed that the couple will be showered with happiness and good fortune throughout their marriage.

Whether you’re planning a wedding or simply want to indulge in a sweet treat, Mexican Wedding Cookies are a must-try. They are not only delicious but also bring a touch of tradition and symbolism to any occasion. So, grab your apron and get ready to bake these tempting cookies that are sure to win the hearts of everyone who tries them.
