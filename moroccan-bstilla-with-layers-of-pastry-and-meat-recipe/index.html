Moroccan B'stilla with Layers of Pastry and Meat Recipe


If you’re looking to add a taste of the exotic to your cooking repertoire, Moroccan cuisine offers a treasure trove of flavorful and aromatic dishes. One such dish is the B’stilla, also known as Pastilla, which is a savory pie that combines layers of delicate pastry with a rich, spiced meat filling. This culinary masterpiece is a true testament to the art of layering flavors and textures, resulting in a dish that is as visually stunning as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 2 lbs of boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup of blanched almonds, toasted and chopped
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

For the Pastry:

  • 10 sheets of phyllo pastry
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup of powdered sugar

For Garnish:

  • Powdered sugar
  • Ground cinnamon

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken Filling

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the pot, along with the ground ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, nutmeg, cloves, cardamom, salt, and pepper. Cook until the chicken is browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in enough water to cover the chicken, reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender.
  4. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and allow it to cool. Shred the chicken into small pieces and set aside.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the beaten eggs, chopped cilantro, chopped parsley, and toasted almonds. Add this mixture to the pot with the remaining cooking liquid and cook, stirring constantly, until the eggs are set.
  6. Add the shredded chicken back into the pot and stir to combine. Remove from heat and set the filling aside to cool.

Step 2: Prepare the Pastry

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine the powdered sugar and ground cinnamon.
  3. Lay one sheet of phyllo pastry on a clean work surface and brush it lightly with melted butter. Sprinkle a small amount of the cinnamon-sugar mixture over the pastry.
  4. Place another sheet of phyllo pastry on top and repeat the process, brushing with butter and sprinkling with the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Repeat with the remaining sheets of phyllo pastry.

Step 3: Assemble the B’stilla

  1. Line a 9-inch pie dish with the layered phyllo pastry, allowing the edges to hang over the sides.
  2. Spoon the cooled chicken filling onto the pastry-lined dish, spreading it out evenly.
  3. Fold the overhanging pastry over the top of the filling to encase it completely.
  4. Brush the top of the pastry with any remaining melted butter.

Step 4: Bake the B’stilla

  1. Place the assembled B’stilla in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and crisp.
  2. Remove the B’stilla from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

  1. Once cooled, dust the top of the B’stilla with powdered sugar and ground cinnamon.
  2. To serve, cut the B’stilla into wedges and enjoy the delightful combination of savory, sweet, and aromatic flavors.

Conclusion

The Moroccan B’stilla is a dish that truly encapsulates the essence of Moroccan cuisine. Its delicate layers of pastry, fragrant spices, and succulent meat filling create a culinary experience that is both comforting and exotic. Whether you’re looking to impress your dinner guests or simply indulge in a unique and flavorful meal, the B’stilla is sure to satisfy your cravings for something extraordinary. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Morocco with this delightful B’stilla recipe!
