Cotton Candy Cake: Childhood Fair Faves


Bringing back memories of our favorite fair treats, cotton candy cakes are the perfect combination of whimsy and delight. With a light pink cake base mimicking cotton candy’s signature hue, and a dreamy whip cream frosting adorned with crushed pink wafer cookies, this dessert will have you reminiscing about carefree days spent at the carnival.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2-3 drops of pink food coloring

For the Frosting:

  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Crushed pink wafer cookies

For the Decorations:

  • Cotton candy (store-bought or homemade)
  • Additional crushed pink wafer cookies

Instructions

Preparing the Cake:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch cake pans, then line the bottom with parchment paper.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until combined. Set aside.

  3. In a separate large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the milk and eggs, constantly mixing until fully incorporated.

  4. Add the dry ingredient mixture to the wet mixture, stirring until smooth. Be sure not to overmix.

  5. To achieve the cotton candy effect, add the vanilla extract and pink food coloring. Mix well until a soft pink color is achieved. If you want a more vivid hue, add additional drops of food coloring.

  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans. Bake for approximately 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center of the cake comes out clean.

  7. Let the cakes cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Frosting:

  1. While the cake is cooling, prepare the frosting. In a large mixing bowl, begin whipping the heavy cream and sugar together on medium speed, gradually increasing the speed as you mix.

  2. Once the mixture starts to thicken, add the vanilla extract and continue mixing until stiff peaks form. Be cautious not to overmix as this may lead to a grainy texture.

Assembling the Cake:

  1. Begin by placing one layer of the cake on a flat plate or serving dish. Generously spread a layer of whipped cream frosting evenly over the top of the cake, being sure to reach the edges.

  2. Crumble some of the crushed pink wafer cookies and sprinkle them over the frosting. Add the second cake layer and repeat the frosting and wafer cookie process.

  3. Place the remaining whipped cream frosting on the sides and top of the cake, using an offset spatula to create a smooth and seamless finish. Once again, sprinkle the crushed pink wafer cookies on top and around the sides of the cake.

  4. To make the cotton candy pops, skewer small pieces of cotton candy onto a popsicle stick or skewer and insert into the cake. Alternatively, stick cotton candy to the back of the cake with a small amount of melted chocolate or whipped cream.

  5. Serve and savor this nostalgic confection that will transport your tastebuds to your favorite carnival fair.

Enjoy this Cotton Candy Cake recipe that captures the essence of childhood memories with its soft, fluffy texture and fun, playful flavors. This cheerful dessert is the perfect addition to your next backyard BBQ or festive gathering. So take a stroll down memory lane and create something special that will delight the senses of both kids and adults alike.
