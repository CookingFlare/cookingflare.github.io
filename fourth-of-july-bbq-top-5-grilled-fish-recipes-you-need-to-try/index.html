Fourth of July BBQ: Top 5 Grilled Fish Recipes You Need to Try


The Fourth of July is all about fireworks, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious BBQs. While burgers, ribs, and hot dogs often take center stage, it’s time to shake things up with some fresh and flavorful grilled fish recipes. Seafood on the grill offers a lighter, healthier alternative to traditional BBQ fare, and the smoky aroma enhances the natural flavors beautifully.

Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or joining friends and family for a potluck, these five grilled fish recipes are guaranteed to wow your guests. Grab your apron, fire up the grill, and dive into this mouthwatering guide to Fourth of July seafood grilling.

1. Cedar-Plank Grilled Salmon With Lemon-Dill Sauce

Few recipes capture the essence of summer like cedar-plank grilled salmon. The aromatic cedar adds depth to the salmon’s flavor while keeping it moist and flaky. Pair this dish with a bright lemon-dill sauce to create an unforgettable centerpiece for your holiday BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (about 6–8 oz each)
  • 1 cedar plank (soaked in water for at least 2 hours)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Rub the salmon fillets with olive oil, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the soaked cedar plank directly on the grill grates, allowing it to heat for a few minutes before adding the salmon.
  4. Lay the salmon fillets on the cedar plank skin side down, close the grill lid, and cook for 15–20 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, zest, and chopped dill to create the sauce.
  6. Drizzle the lemon-dill sauce over the salmon before serving.

Pro Tip:

Pair cedar-plank grilled salmon with grilled asparagus and roasted potatoes for a complete summer meal.

2. Zesty Grilled Mahi-Mahi Tacos

If you’re looking for a recipe that’s as fun as it is delicious, grilled mahi-mahi tacos are the perfect Fourth of July dish. The mild, firm fish handles grilling beautifully and takes on the smoky char while balancing the vibrant flavors of zesty lime and spices. Serve these tacos buffet-style and let guests customize their toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 4 mahi-mahi fillets (about 4–6 oz each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Tortillas (flour or corn)
  • Your favorite taco toppings (e.g., shredded cabbage, avocado slices, salsa, cilantro)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine lime juice, olive oil, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Brush the mixture over both sides of each mahi-mahi fillet.
  3. Place the fish directly on the grill grates and cook for 3–4 minutes per side, or until the fillets are opaque and slightly firm.
  4. Warm the tortillas on the grill for a few seconds.
  5. Break the grilled mahi-mahi into chunks and add them to the tortillas. Top with your preferred taco toppings.

Pro Tip:

Add a drizzle of chipotle crema or squeeze of lime juice over the tacos for a tangy finish.

3. Grilled Swordfish With Garlic Herb Butter

Impress your BBQ guests with grilled swordfish steaks that feel as luxurious as a seafood restaurant dish. The meaty firmness of swordfish makes it ideal for the grill, and the garlic herb butter adds a rich, irresistible finish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 swordfish steaks (about 6 oz each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the swordfish steaks with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the steaks on the grill and cook for 4–5 minutes per side, or until the fish is opaque and grill marks appear.
  4. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the minced garlic, parsley, thyme, and lemon juice. Stir the herb butter until well combined.
  5. Remove the fish from the grill and brush the garlic herb butter generously over each steak. Serve immediately.

Pro Tip:

Serve grilled swordfish with a summertime corn salad or a fresh arugula salad for a light yet satisfying pairing.

4. Grilled Whole Snapper With Citrus Marinade

If you’re ready to take your grilling skills up a notch, this recipe for grilled whole snapper is a showstopper. Cooking a whole fish locks in the moisture and produces a melt-in-your-mouth texture, while a tangy citrus marinade brightens every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole red snapper (about 2–3 lbs), cleaned
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat and oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  2. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, citrus juices, orange zest, garlic, cilantro, paprika, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
  3. Score the snapper by making shallow slits on both sides and pour the marinade over the fish, ensuring it gets into the scored cuts.
  4. Place the whole snapper directly on the grill and cook for 5–6 minutes per side, or until the flesh is opaque and the skin is crisp.
  5. Carefully remove the snapper from the grill and use a fork to flake the meat off the bones.

Pro Tip:

Add slices of grilled pineapple alongside the fish for a tropical twist.

5. Grilled Tilapia With Mango Salsa

Tilapia is an affordable, mild-tasting fish that grills up beautifully when paired with a vibrant mango salsa. This recipe is packed with fresh, summery flavors that your guests will savor until the last bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tilapia fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 additional lime

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the tilapia fillets with olive oil and lime juice, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the fillets on the grill and cook for 3–4 minutes per side, or until the fish is flaky and opaque.
  4. In a bowl, combine diced mango, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and lime juice to create the salsa.
  5. Serve the grilled tilapia topped with the mango salsa.

Pro Tip:

Pair this dish with a refreshing citrusy summer cocktail for the ultimate Fourth of July vibes.

Wrap-Up

With these five incredible grilled fish recipes, your Fourth of July BBQ is bound to be a memorable one. Whether you’re serving cedar-plank salmon with lemon-dill perfection or zesty mahi-mahi tacos, the flavors from the grill will bring a new dimension to your holiday feast. Grilling fish might seem intimidating at first, but with these easy-to-follow recipes and tips, you’ll master it in no time.

Seafood brings a unique flair to summer gatherings, giving your guests a break from traditional BBQ staples while adding variety to the menu. So, this Fourth of July, step outside the burger box, embrace the sizzling grill, and let the fresh flavors of fish shine! Happy grilling and celebrating!
