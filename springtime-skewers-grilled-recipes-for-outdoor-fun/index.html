Springtime Skewers: Grilled Recipes for Outdoor Fun


As the weather warms up and the days get longer, it’s time to embrace the joy of outdoor cooking. Spring is the perfect season for firing up the grill, and one of the best ways to make the most of it is by preparing skewers. Grilled skewers offer a fun, versatile, and delicious way to enjoy the flavors of spring while bringing a sense of community to your outdoor gatherings. Whether you’re hosting a family barbecue or just enjoying a quiet evening in the backyard, these skewers are sure to impress.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of grilled skewer recipes that feature fresh, seasonal ingredients and easy-to-follow techniques. From the classic chicken skewer to inventive veggie and seafood options, these recipes will keep your spring grilling season fresh, exciting, and flavorful.

Why Skewers Are Perfect for Spring Grilling

Spring is all about new beginnings and fresh ingredients, and skewers make it easy to highlight the best of what the season has to offer. Here’s why skewers are ideal for your springtime grilling adventures:

  • Customization: Skewers allow you to mix and match different proteins, vegetables, and seasonings, so you can cater to a variety of tastes.
  • Quick Cooking: The small pieces on a skewer cook faster than larger cuts, so you can enjoy your meal in less time.
  • Perfect for Sharing: Skewers make it easy for guests to enjoy a variety of flavors without committing to a single dish.
  • Portability: Perfect for picnics, barbecues, and outdoor parties, skewers are easy to transport and serve.

Let’s dive into some creative, delicious, and easy-to-make skewer recipes that will bring your spring grilling to the next level.

1. Herb-Infused Chicken Skewers

Chicken is a favorite protein for grilling, and when combined with fresh herbs and a zesty marinade, it becomes a crowd-pleaser. These herb-infused chicken skewers are simple yet full of flavor, making them perfect for any spring gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken breast, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, garlic powder, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a marinade.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and coat evenly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight for more flavor.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken, red onion, and bell pepper onto the skewers, alternating between the chicken and vegetables.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 6-8 minutes on each side, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are slightly charred.
  6. Serve hot with a side of rice or a fresh salad.

These herb-infused chicken skewers are perfect for a light and flavorful dish that highlights the season’s herbs. The tangy lemon and aromatic herbs give the chicken a fresh, vibrant flavor that pairs wonderfully with grilled vegetables.

2. Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Balsamic Glaze

For a vegetarian option that’s just as satisfying as the meat-filled ones, try these grilled vegetable skewers. The balsamic glaze adds a sweet and tangy finish that perfectly complements the smokiness from the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, cut into thick slices
  • 1 yellow squash, cut into thick slices
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper. This will be the marinade for the vegetables.
  2. Place the zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, red onion, and cherry tomatoes in a large bowl and pour the marinade over the vegetables. Toss to coat evenly and let it sit for 20 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  4. Thread the marinated vegetables onto skewers, alternating the different vegetables to create a colorful mix.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  6. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the skewers and garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

These vegetable skewers are perfect for a light, healthy meal or as a side dish to complement your grilled meats. The balsamic glaze ties everything together, adding a sophisticated touch to this simple recipe.

3. Shrimp and Pineapple Skewers with Chili Lime Marinade

Seafood lovers will appreciate these shrimp and pineapple skewers, which are packed with tropical flavors and a hint of heat. The chili-lime marinade gives the shrimp a zesty kick that pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the pineapple.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple chunks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime, juiced and zested
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for extra heat)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lime juice, lime zest, chili powder, cayenne pepper (if using), garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the shrimp to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Thread the marinated shrimp and pineapple chunks onto the skewers, alternating between the shrimp and pineapple.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.
  6. Serve the skewers with a squeeze of fresh lime juice on top and a sprinkle of cilantro for added flavor.

These shrimp and pineapple skewers offer the perfect combination of sweet and savory, with a touch of heat to balance out the flavors. They’re great for a light dinner or as an appetizer at your next outdoor gathering.

4. Steak and Mushroom Skewers with Garlic Butter

For those who prefer a heartier meal, these steak and mushroom skewers are sure to satisfy. The garlic butter adds a rich, savory flavor that makes this skewer recipe stand out.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb sirloin steak, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup cremini mushrooms, halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together olive oil, melted butter, garlic, fresh parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Thread the steak cubes and mushrooms onto the skewers, alternating between the two.
  3. Brush the skewers with the garlic butter mixture.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or longer if you prefer your steak cooked more well-done.
  6. Once cooked, remove from the grill and brush with extra garlic butter before serving.

These steak and mushroom skewers are packed with rich, savory flavors that will satisfy any meat lover. The combination of tender steak and juicy mushrooms, all coated in a buttery garlic sauce, makes for a truly indulgent grilling experience.

5. Lamb and Apricot Skewers with Mint Yogurt Sauce

For a unique and flavorful skewer recipe, try these lamb and apricot skewers. The sweetness of the apricots pairs wonderfully with the savory lamb, and the mint yogurt sauce adds a refreshing element to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lamb shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup dried apricots, halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix olive oil, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the lamb cubes to the marinade and toss to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight.
  3. Thread the marinated lamb cubes and apricot halves onto the skewers, alternating between the two.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 4-6 minutes on each side, or until the lamb is cooked to your desired doneness.
  6. While grilling, mix together the Greek yogurt, fresh mint, and lemon juice in a bowl to make the mint yogurt sauce.
  7. Serve the lamb and apricot skewers with a generous drizzle of mint yogurt sauce.

These lamb and apricot skewers offer a delightful combination of sweet and savory flavors, enhanced by the refreshing mint yogurt sauce. It’s a perfect choice for a spring gathering where you want to impress your guests with something a little different.

Conclusion

Grilled skewers are a fantastic way to enjoy the flavors of spring while making the most of the outdoor cooking season. From herb-infused chicken to vibrant vegetable combos and succulent seafood, there’s a skewer recipe for everyone. These easy-to-make skewers are perfect for family gatherings, barbecues, and picnics, and their versatility allows you to experiment with different ingredients and flavors.

So, this spring, fire up the grill, grab your skewers, and start creating delicious meals that are sure to become a seasonal tradition. Happy grilling!
