Middle Eastern Cooking with Nuts: 5 Delicious Recipes


The cuisines of the Middle East are renowned for their rich flavors, vibrant spices, and sophisticated use of textures that create unforgettable culinary experiences. One of the standout elements in this cuisine is the use of nuts. From almonds to pistachios, these small but mighty ingredients are essential in adding depth and structure to both savory and sweet dishes. Nuts play a crucial role in enhancing flavor, providing creaminess, and delivering delightful crunch.

Today, we’ll explore five mouthwatering Middle Eastern recipes that highlight nuts in all their glory. Whether you’re preparing a grand feast or a simple dinner, these dishes will bring both comfort and excitement to your table.

1. Muhammara: Walnut and Red Pepper Dip

Muhammara is a bold and earthy dip that hails from Syria. With its distinctive flavor profile of smoky roasted red peppers, sweet pomegranate molasses, and nutty walnuts, it’s the perfect appetizer or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 2 large roasted red bell peppers (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by toasting the walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant, about 3-4 minutes. Let them cool.
  2. In a food processor, combine the toasted walnuts, roasted bell peppers, breadcrumbs, pomegranate molasses, garlic clove, cumin, and smoked paprika.
  3. Blend the mixture until smooth while gradually adding olive oil to reach your desired consistency.
  4. Season the dip with salt and pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  5. Serve it with warm pita bread, pita chips, or fresh vegetable sticks.

Pro Tip: Muhammara can also be used as a spread for sandwiches or a topping for grilled chicken or fish.

2. Fesenjan: Persian Walnut and Pomegranate Stew

Fesenjan, a staple of Persian cuisine, is a rich, tangy, and slightly sweet stew that features ground walnuts and pomegranate molasses as its primary ingredients. This dish is typically served with chicken or duck, but it can also be made vegetarian by swapping the protein for mushrooms or tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds chicken thighs or breasts
  • 2 cups walnuts, finely ground
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 3/4 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • White rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the onions until golden and soft.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pot, seasoning them with turmeric, salt, and pepper. Brown them on both sides.
  3. Remove the chicken and set it aside. Stir the ground walnuts into the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes to toast them slightly.
  4. Slowly add the chicken stock to the pot, stirring to create a smooth sauce.
  5. Return the chicken to the pot, reduce the heat to low, and cover. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes.
  6. Stir in the pomegranate molasses and cinnamon. Continue simmering until the chicken is tender and the sauce thickens, about another 30-40 minutes.
  7. Serve Fesenjan over a bed of fluffy white rice.

Pro Tip: Garnish with fresh pomegranate seeds and a sprinkle of chopped parsley for added color and flavor.

3. Baklava: A Pistachio Lover’s Delight

Baklava is perhaps the most cherished Middle Eastern dessert. Layers of buttery phyllo dough are filled with a mixture of finely chopped nuts—often pistachios, walnuts, or a combination—and sweetened with a fragrant honey syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (thawed)
  • 2 cups pistachios, finely chopped
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon orange blossom water or rosewater (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.
  2. Combine the chopped pistachios, sugar, and cinnamon in a bowl.
  3. Layer 10 sheets of phyllo dough in the baking dish, brushing each layer generously with melted butter.
  4. Spread a layer of the nut mixture, then layer 5 more sheets of phyllo dough (buttered) on top. Alternate layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a top layer of phyllo.
  5. Use a sharp knife to cut the baklava into diamonds or squares before baking.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until golden.
  7. While the baklava bakes, prepare the syrup by combining honey, water, lemon juice, and orange blossom/rosewater in a saucepan. Simmer until thickened.
  8. Pour the warm syrup over the baklava as soon as it comes out of the oven. Let it soak for at least 4 hours or overnight before serving.

4. Kibbeh Nayeh with Pine Nuts

Kibbeh Nayeh is a traditional dish in Lebanese cuisine that combines finely minced raw lamb or beef with bulgur wheat, spices, and pine nuts. It’s a delicacy that requires the freshest ingredients to truly shine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound raw lamb or beef (very fresh, finely minced)
  • 1/2 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 small onion, finely grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Soak the bulgur wheat in cold water for 10 minutes, then drain thoroughly and squeeze out excess liquid.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the minced meat, bulgur, grated onion, and spices.
  3. Knead the mixture until it is cohesive and smooth.
  4. Form the kibbeh into flat patties or spread it evenly on a serving platter.
  5. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with toasted pine nuts.
  6. Serve immediately with pita bread and fresh vegetables.

Note: Always source meat from a trusted butcher if serving raw. Blending some of the meat in a food processor can improve the texture.

5. Stuffed Dates with Almonds

This quick and elegant snack or dessert highlights the natural sweetness of dates while adding the crunch of almonds. Traditionally served during holidays and special occasions, they’re also perfect as an energy-packed treat.

Ingredients:

  • 20 Medjool dates
  • 20 whole almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Crushed pistachios or shredded coconut for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Use a small knife to slit each date and remove the pit.
  2. Fill each date with a teaspoon of tahini and insert a toasted almond.
  3. Drizzle a small amount of honey over the stuffed dates.
  4. Sprinkle with crushed pistachios or shredded coconut for garnish, if desired.
  5. Arrange them on a platter and serve.

Pro Tip: These keep well in an airtight container, making them an excellent make-ahead option for gatherings.

By incorporating these nut-filled Middle Eastern recipes into your kitchen repertoire, you’re embracing a world of unique textures and flavors. Nuts elevate dishes, adding not only nourishment but also the richness that is so characteristic of Middle Eastern cooking. Experiment with these recipes and share them with friends and family—each dish tells a story, and every bite is bound to leave a lasting impression on your taste buds!
