Triple Chocolate Layer Cake Recipe


Few desserts can compete with the allure of chocolate, but when you layer it—triple it, in fact—magic truly happens. This Triple Chocolate Layer Cake is the ultimate indulgence for chocolate lovers. With rich chocolate layers, creamy ganache, and velvety frosting, it’s the centerpiece of every celebration. Whether you’re baking for a birthday, a holiday gathering, or treating yourself, this recipe brings decadence to your plate.

From tips for perfect layering to secrets behind achieving moist and fluffy cake, follow this step-by-step guide to mastering this show-stopping dessert. Let’s dive straight into making the perfect Triple Chocolate Layer Cake.

Triple Chocolate Layer Cake Recipe

Ingredients

What sets this cake apart is the combination of intense chocolate flavors in the cake layers, ganache, and frosting. Make sure to use high-quality ingredients for the best results.

For the Chocolate Cake Layers:

  • 2 ½ cups (310g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (200g) brown sugar (packed)
  • ¾ cup (75g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) buttermilk, room temperature
  • ½ cup (120ml) vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (240ml) boiling water
  • ½ cup (85g) high-quality dark chocolate chips (optional)

For the Chocolate Ganache:

  • 8 oz (225g) semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract

For the Chocolate Buttercream Frosting:

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 ½ cups (300g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • ¾ cup (75g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3–4 tbsp (45–60ml) heavy cream
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cake Layers

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line three 9-inch round cake pans with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk to combine evenly.
  3. In another bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, buttermilk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.
  4. Slowly incorporate the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the cake dense.
  5. Gradually add the boiling water to the batter, whisking continuously until smooth. This step ensures the batter is loose but airy, creating a moist texture.
  6. Fold in dark chocolate chips, if desired, for extra bursts of chocolate.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cake pans. Bake for 25–30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool for 10–15 minutes in the pans before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 2: Make the Ganache

  1. Place the chopped semi-sweet chocolate into a heatproof bowl.
  2. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it begins to simmer (avoid boiling).
  3. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit for 3–5 minutes. Then, whisk together until the mixture is completely smooth and glossy.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract for added depth of flavor.
  5. Allow the ganache to cool to room temperature, stirring occasionally. It should thicken slightly but remain spreadable.

Step 3: Prepare the Frosting

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with a hand mixer or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment until light and fluffy (about 2 minutes).
  2. Add the sifted powdered sugar and cocoa powder to the butter. Mix on low speed until fully combined.
  3. Gradually add heavy cream, one tablespoon at a time, until the frosting reaches your desired consistency.
  4. Add vanilla extract and salt, then mix for another minute to ensure everything is well incorporated.

Step 4: Assemble the Cake

  1. Once the cake layers are completely cooled, level the tops of each layer with a serrated knife or cake leveler for even stacking.
  2. Place the first cake layer on a cake stand or serving plate. Spread a generous amount of chocolate ganache over the layer, ensuring an even coat.
  3. Add the second layer and repeat the process with the ganache.
  4. Place the final layer on top. Spread a thin crumb coat of chocolate buttercream frosting over the entire cake to seal in the crumbs. Let it chill for 20–30 minutes.
  5. Once the crumb coat is set, evenly apply the remaining buttercream frosting. Use an offset spatula for smooth edges or pipe decorative swirls for a more elegant presentation.

Tips for Success

  • Use Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensuring your eggs and buttermilk are at room temperature helps create a smoother batter and better texture.
  • Choose High-Quality Chocolate: The richness of the cake depends on the flavor and quality of the chocolate used for the ganache and cake layers.
  • Cool Completely Before Frosting: Frosting warm cake layers can cause the ganache or frosting to melt, leading to uneven layers.
  • Proper Measuring: Use a kitchen scale for precision, especially when measuring ingredients like cocoa powder and flour, which can greatly impact the texture.
  • Chill Your Frosting: If the frosting feels too soft, refrigerate it for 10–15 minutes before using to ensure it holds its shape.

Storage and Make-Ahead Options

Triple Chocolate Layer Cake can be stored, covered, in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Allow the cake to sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving for the best flavor and texture.

To make ahead, bake the cake layers and wrap them tightly in plastic wrap, storing them at room temperature for up to 24 hours before assembling. The ganache and frosting can be prepared a day in advance and refrigerated. Let both soften slightly before using.

Craft Your Signature Touch

Although this recipe is decadent on its own, personalizing your Triple Chocolate Layer Cake is half the fun. Add garnishes like chocolate shavings, fresh berries, or a dusting of powdered sugar for visual appeal. You could even drizzle additional ganache over the frosted cake for a dramatic finish. Experiment with different chocolate combinations—mixing milk and dark chocolate—to create your own masterpiece.

Reasons You’ll Love This Cake
