Homemade Goodness: Classic Spaghetti and Meatballs Recipe


There’s something truly comforting about a hearty plate of classic spaghetti and meatballs. This timeless dish, rich in flavor and tradition, has the power to transport you to a cozy Italian kitchen with every bite. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this homemade recipe is sure to impress. So tie on your apron, roll up your sleeves, and let’s dive into the world of homemade goodness!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped parsley, and beaten egg. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Gently mix the ingredients together until well combined, being careful not to overmix. The mixture should be moist but still hold its shape.

  2. Shape the meat mixture into golf ball-sized meatballs. You should get around 12 meatballs from this recipe. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and refrigerate for about 30 minutes. Chilling the meatballs will help them hold their shape while cooking.

  3. While the meatballs are chilling, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  4. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the meatballs to the skillet, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding. Cook the meatballs for about 8-10 minutes, turning them occasionally, until browned on all sides. Transfer the cooked meatballs to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess grease.

  5. In the same skillet, add the marinara sauce and bring it to a simmer. Gently place the cooked meatballs into the sauce, making sure they are fully submerged. Cover the skillet and let the meatballs simmer in the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Serve the spaghetti and meatballs in individual bowls, spooning some sauce over the pasta. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a touch of freshness and color. Pair it with a side of garlic bread or a simple green salad, and you have a complete meal that will satisfy even the pickiest eaters.

Tips and Variations

  • For a lighter alternative, you can use ground turkey or chicken instead of beef and pork. The meatballs will still be juicy and flavorful.
  • Experiment with herbs and spices to add your own twist to the meatballs. Try adding dried oregano, basil, or even a pinch of red pepper flakes for some heat.
  • If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender to puree the marinara sauce before adding the meatballs.
  • Don’t forget to sprinkle some extra grated Parmesan cheese on top of the spaghetti and meatballs for an extra burst of flavor.

There you have it – a classic spaghetti and meatballs recipe that will transport you to Italy with every forkful. This homemade dish is perfect for any occasion, from a cozy family dinner to a special gathering with friends. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the homemade goodness unfold in your kitchen. Buon appetito!
