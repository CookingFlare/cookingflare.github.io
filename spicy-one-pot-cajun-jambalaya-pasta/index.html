Spicy One Pot Cajun Jambalaya Pasta


Are you ready to add a fiery twist to your pasta repertoire? Look no further than this Spicy One Pot Cajun Jambalaya Pasta recipe! Bursting with bold flavors and a kick of heat, this dish is a perfect fusion of Cajun and Italian cuisine. And the best part? It’s all made in just one pot, making it a breeze to prepare and clean up.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 ounces)
  • 1 can tomato sauce (8 ounces)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (adjust to your preferred spice level)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 8 ounces penne pasta
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and Cajun seasoning, and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, chicken broth, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, dried thyme, dried oregano, paprika, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine.

  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Stir in the penne pasta, making sure it is submerged in the sauce. Cover the pot again and cook for an additional 10-12 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente.

  6. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the heavy cream. The cream will help to mellow out the heat from the spices and add a creamy richness to the dish.

  7. Sprinkle the chopped fresh parsley over the top of the pasta for a pop of freshness and color.

  8. Serve the Spicy One Pot Cajun Jambalaya Pasta hot, and enjoy the explosion of flavors with every bite!

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize this dish to your taste preferences. Add more hot sauce if you like it extra spicy, or reduce the amount for a milder version.
  • You can substitute shrimp or sausage for the chicken if you prefer. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly.
  • If you’re not a fan of penne pasta, you can use any other short pasta shape like rotini or fusilli.
  • Garnish the pasta with grated Parmesan cheese or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

In Conclusion

This Spicy One Pot Cajun Jambalaya Pasta is a show-stopping dish that will wow your taste buds and impress your guests. With its vibrant flavors and easy preparation, it’s perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a gathering with friends. So go ahead, embrace the heat, and let this Cajun-inspired pasta take you on a flavor-packed journey. Enjoy!
