Tasty Nut-Free Blueberry Muffin Recipe


Are you looking for a delicious and nut-free blueberry muffin recipe that will satisfy your cravings for a sweet treat? Look no further! This easy-to-follow recipe will guide you through creating moist, flavorful blueberry muffins that are perfect for breakfast, snacks, or dessert. Whether you have a nut allergy or simply prefer to avoid nuts, this recipe is a delightful option for anyone.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups fresh blueberries

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and prepare a muffin tin by greasing or lining with paper liners.

  2. Prepare the Batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter and granulated sugar. Mix until well combined. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and mix until smooth. Gradually add the flour, baking powder, and salt. Pour in the milk and mix until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.

  3. Add the Blueberries: Gently fold the fresh blueberries into the batter, being careful not to crush them.

  4. Fill the Muffin Cups: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about two-thirds full.

  5. Bake the Muffins: Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Cool and Enjoy: Once baked, remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve and enjoy!

Tips for Success

  • Fresh Blueberries: For the best flavor and texture, use fresh blueberries in this recipe. If fresh blueberries are not in season, you can use frozen blueberries, but be sure to thaw and drain them before adding to the batter.

  • Avoid Overmixing: When incorporating the blueberries into the batter, be gentle to prevent the muffins from turning blue. A few streaks of blueberry juice in the batter are normal and add to the appeal of these muffins.

  • Storage: These muffins can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, they can be frozen for up to 2 months.

Conclusion

These nut-free blueberry muffins are a delightful treat that everyone can enjoy. With their tender crumb and bursts of juicy blueberries, they are sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re serving them for breakfast, packing them in lunchboxes, or enjoying them with a cup of tea, these muffins are a versatile and irresistible choice for any occasion. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake up a batch of these delectable blueberry muffins!
