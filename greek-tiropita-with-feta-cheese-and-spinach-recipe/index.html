Greek Tiropita with Feta Cheese and Spinach Recipe


Tiropita, a traditional Greek dish made with feta cheese and spinach, is a delightful treat that will transport your taste buds to the beautiful shores of Greece. This savory pastry is perfect for breakfast, brunch, or as a light lunch option. The combination of creamy feta cheese and earthy spinach is simply irresistible. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s dive into this delicious Greek Tiropita recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 package of phyllo dough
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 200g spinach, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish with olive oil or cooking spray.

  2. In a pan over medium heat, sauté the diced onion in olive oil until translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the chopped spinach to the pan and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.

  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, crumbled feta cheese, salt, black pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.

  5. Once the spinach and onion mixture has cooled, add it to the bowl with the feta cheese mixture. Mix everything together until evenly incorporated.

  6. Unroll the phyllo dough and cover it with a damp towel to prevent it from drying out. Take one sheet of phyllo dough and brush it lightly with olive oil. Place another sheet on top and repeat the process until you have 5 layers.

  7. Spread a thin layer of the feta cheese and spinach mixture evenly over the phyllo dough.

  8. Repeat the process of layering the phyllo dough and spreading the filling until all the ingredients are used. Make sure to reserve some mixture for the top layer.

  9. Fold the edges of the phyllo dough towards the center, creating a neat package. Brush the top layer with olive oil.

  10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing into individual pieces.

  12. Serve the Greek Tiropita warm and enjoy the delicious combination of flavors and textures.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add a handful of chopped fresh herbs like dill or parsley to the filling mixture.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can omit the onion and add more spinach instead.
  • Tiropita can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

Indulge in the rich flavors of Greece with this mouthwatering Tiropita recipe. The combination of creamy feta cheese, vibrant spinach, and crispy layers of phyllo dough will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re hosting a brunch or simply looking for a delicious snack, this Greek delight is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and transport yourself to the sunny Mediterranean with each bite of this traditional Tiropita. Opa!
