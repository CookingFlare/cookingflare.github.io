How to Make Homemade Apple Cider Donuts


If you love the warm, sweet aroma of donuts, you’ll absolutely fall in love with homemade apple cider donuts. These donuts have all the cozy flavors of autumn packed into a soft, cinnamon-spiced cake, with a distinct apple cider flavor that makes them the perfect treat for cooler weather. Whether you enjoy them with a cup of hot cider or serve them at a fall gathering, these donuts will leave everyone coming back for more.

Making your own apple cider donuts might seem like a daunting task, but with a bit of patience and the right ingredients, you’ll be able to recreate these delicious pastries right in your own kitchen. In this post, we’ll guide you through the entire process, from reducing the apple cider to the final sugar coating, ensuring every bite is full of flavor. Let’s dive in!

Why Make Homemade Apple Cider Donuts?

There’s something special about homemade donuts that store-bought ones just can’t replicate. By making your own apple cider donuts, you get to control the quality of the ingredients, ensuring they’re fresh and full of flavor. The homemade touch also allows you to customize the recipe to your personal taste, whether you prefer a spicier donut or one with a richer apple cider taste.

Moreover, homemade apple cider donuts are much fresher than anything you’ll find at a bakery. The warm, soft texture combined with the crunchy sugar coating creates a treat that is perfect for fall celebrations, family breakfasts, or a cozy afternoon snack.

Ingredients for Apple Cider Donuts

Before you start, gather all the ingredients you need. You don’t need anything too fancy, just some staple baking items and a good quality apple cider. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the donuts:

  • 2 cups apple cider (preferably fresh-pressed)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)

For the sugar coating:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon

Step 1: Reduce the Apple Cider

The first step to making the best apple cider donuts is to reduce the apple cider. This process concentrates the apple flavor and makes it more intense, which is crucial to creating a flavorful donut.

Pour your apple cider into a saucepan and bring it to a boil over medium heat. Once it starts to boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, stirring occasionally. The goal is to reduce the cider by about half, which will take approximately 20-25 minutes. Keep an eye on it, as it can boil over if you’re not careful.

Once the cider has reduced to about one cup, remove it from the heat and let it cool down completely. This reduced cider will give your donuts that deep, delicious apple flavor.

Step 2: Prepare the Dry Ingredients

While the cider is reducing and cooling, it’s a great time to prepare the dry ingredients. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. These spices are what will give your donuts that lovely autumnal flavor, so don’t skip them.

Once everything is well combined, set the bowl aside.

Step 3: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In another bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar together until they become slightly fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes. Once the egg mixture is well-beaten, add the melted butter, whole milk, vanilla extract, and the reduced apple cider that has cooled down. Mix everything together until the wet ingredients are well incorporated.

The cider will provide the donuts with that signature apple flavor, while the butter and milk will give them a soft, tender texture.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Now it’s time to combine your wet and dry ingredients. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough donuts. The batter should be thick but pourable. If it’s too thick, you can add a little more milk to reach the desired consistency.

Step 5: Heat the Oil

For frying, you’ll need a deep fryer or a heavy-bottomed pot. Add enough vegetable oil to the pot so that the donuts can float while frying—about 2-3 inches of oil should be sufficient. Heat the oil to 350°F (175°C). To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into the oil. If it bubbles and rises to the surface immediately, the oil is ready for frying.

Step 6: Fry the Donuts

Now comes the fun part: frying the donuts! To shape the donuts, you can either use a donut cutter or simply roll the dough into balls and shape them with your hands. If you use a cutter, be sure to dip it into some flour between each cut to prevent the dough from sticking.

Carefully drop the donuts into the hot oil one or two at a time. Don’t overcrowd the pot, as this can cause the temperature of the oil to drop. Fry the donuts for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the donuts from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 7: Coat the Donuts with Cinnamon Sugar

While the donuts are still warm, it’s time to coat them in cinnamon sugar. In a small bowl, combine the granulated sugar and ground cinnamon. Gently roll each donut in the cinnamon sugar mixture, making sure it’s fully coated on all sides. This adds the perfect amount of sweetness and spice, making the apple cider flavor pop even more.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Once all the donuts are coated, they’re ready to be enjoyed! Serve them warm with a cup of coffee, hot cider, or simply on their own. These homemade apple cider donuts are best enjoyed fresh, but you can store them in an airtight container for a few days if you have leftovers. Just reheat them slightly before serving for that warm, fresh-baked taste.

Tips for Perfect Apple Cider Donuts

While the recipe itself is straightforward, here are a few tips to ensure your homemade apple cider donuts turn out perfectly every time:

  • Use fresh cider: Freshly pressed apple cider will give your donuts the best flavor. If you can’t find fresh cider, store-bought apple cider works too, but the flavor may not be as intense.
  • Don’t overmix the batter: Overmixing the batter can result in tough donuts. Mix just until everything is combined for the best texture.
  • Check oil temperature: A thermometer is your best friend when frying donuts. Maintaining the oil at 350°F ensures a crispy exterior and a fluffy interior.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pot: Frying too many donuts at once can lower the oil temperature, making your donuts greasy. Fry in batches for the best results.
  • Coat while warm: For the best cinnamon-sugar coating, roll the donuts in the cinnamon sugar while they’re still warm from frying. This helps the sugar stick better and adds that perfect sweet touch.

Variations and Customizations

If you want to get creative, there are plenty of ways to customize your homemade apple cider donuts:

  1. Add nuts: Chopped apples, walnuts, or pecans can be added to the batter for extra flavor and crunch.
  2. Glaze the donuts: Instead of the cinnamon-sugar coating, you can dip the donuts in a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and apple cider.
  3. Spice it up: If you love the warmth of spices, consider adding ground cloves, allspice, or ginger to the batter.
  4. Fill with apple butter: For a fun twist, pipe some homemade apple butter into the center of the donuts before frying for a gooey, sweet filling.

Conclusion

Making homemade apple cider donuts is a rewarding experience that brings the flavors of fall right to your kitchen. The combination of sweet apple cider, warm spices, and a soft, fluffy texture makes these donuts a treat worth savoring. Whether you’re making them for a cozy breakfast, a fall gathering, or just because you crave something sweet, these donuts are sure to impress.

With the right ingredients, a bit of patience, and a love for baking, you’ll be able to recreate the comforting flavors of apple cider donuts that everyone loves. So, roll up your sleeves, heat up the oil, and start frying—you’re about to enjoy the best homemade apple cider donuts you’ve ever tasted!
