Rich and Creamy Seafood Alfredo


If you’re craving a deliciously indulgent dish that combines the richness of creamy pasta with the delicate flavors of the sea, then look no further than this rich and creamy seafood Alfredo. This recipe brings together succulent shrimp, tender scallops, and a smooth, velvety Alfredo sauce that will make your taste buds dance with joy. Whether you’re preparing a meal for a special occasion or simply treating yourself to something extraordinary, this seafood Alfredo is sure to impress.

Why Seafood Alfredo?

Seafood Alfredo is a luxurious and comforting dish that elevates a simple pasta meal into something extraordinary. The creamy sauce perfectly complements the fresh seafood, making every bite feel like a celebration. The contrast between the al dente pasta, tender seafood, and velvety Alfredo sauce creates a balance of textures and flavors that is nothing short of heavenly.

In this recipe, I’m using a combination of shrimp and scallops, two of the most popular seafood options for pasta dishes. Shrimp is known for its sweet, delicate flavor, while scallops bring a mild sweetness and slightly firm texture. Together, they create a beautiful harmony of taste that pairs perfectly with the creamy Alfredo sauce.

Ingredients You Will Need

For this rich and creamy seafood Alfredo, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz of fettuccine (or your preferred pasta)
  • Salt (for seasoning the pasta water)

For the Seafood:

  • 8 oz of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz of scallops (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder (optional)

For the Alfredo Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of heavy cream (preferably chilled)
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated Romano cheese
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of ground nutmeg (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by boiling a large pot of salted water. Once the water is at a rolling boil, add your fettuccine (or your preferred pasta). Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until it reaches al dente perfection. Once cooked, drain the pasta, but save about a cup of the pasta water. This starchy water will be useful in case you need to thin out the Alfredo sauce later.

Step 2: Prepare the Seafood

While the pasta is cooking, it’s time to get the seafood ready. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the shrimp and scallops to the skillet. Season the seafood with salt, pepper, and garlic powder if you’re using it. Cook the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. For the scallops, sear them for about 2-3 minutes per side until they develop a golden-brown crust and are just cooked through. Be careful not to overcook the seafood, as it can become tough and rubbery.

Once the seafood is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set it aside on a plate. Leave any remaining juices in the skillet, as they will add flavor to the sauce.

Step 3: Make the Alfredo Sauce

In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Next, sprinkle in the flour, stirring constantly to create a roux. Cook the roux for about 1-2 minutes to eliminate the raw flour taste, but don’t let it brown.

Slowly pour in the heavy cream and whole milk, whisking continuously to prevent any lumps from forming. As the mixture begins to thicken, add in the grated Parmesan and Romano cheeses. Continue to stir until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce becomes smooth and creamy. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and a pinch of ground nutmeg if desired.

At this point, if the sauce is too thick, you can add a little of the reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency. Stir the pasta water in a little at a time until the sauce is silky smooth and coats the back of a spoon.

Step 4: Combine the Pasta and Seafood

Once the Alfredo sauce is ready, add the cooked fettuccine to the skillet. Toss the pasta in the sauce until it is evenly coated. Then, carefully add the cooked shrimp and scallops back into the skillet. Gently toss everything together to combine, being careful not to break the seafood apart.

If the sauce seems a bit too thick, add more reserved pasta water to loosen it up. Keep stirring gently until the seafood and pasta are well coated in the rich, creamy sauce.

Step 5: Serve and Garnish

Once everything is beautifully combined, it’s time to serve your seafood Alfredo! Transfer the pasta and seafood to a large serving platter or individual bowls. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and a burst of freshness. You can also sprinkle a little extra Parmesan cheese on top for an extra touch of richness.

Tips for the Perfect Seafood Alfredo

  • Use Fresh Seafood: Fresh shrimp and scallops will provide the best flavor and texture. If you’re using frozen seafood, make sure to thaw it completely before cooking.

  • Don’t Overcook the Seafood: Overcooking the shrimp and scallops can result in a tough, rubbery texture. Keep an eye on the seafood while cooking, and remember that both cook very quickly.

  • Adjust the Sauce Consistency: If you prefer a thicker sauce, use less pasta water, or let the sauce simmer a little longer to reduce. If you like a thinner sauce, add more pasta water or milk until it reaches the perfect consistency.

  • Add a Touch of Lemon: For an added layer of flavor, squeeze a little fresh lemon juice over the seafood Alfredo just before serving. The acidity balances the richness of the sauce and adds a refreshing contrast.

  • Make It Spicy: If you love a bit of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the sauce as it cooks. This will give your dish a spicy kick without overpowering the delicate seafood flavors.

  • Pair with Wine: This rich seafood Alfredo pairs beautifully with a crisp white wine, such as Chardonnay or Pinot Grigio. The wine’s acidity will complement the creamy sauce while enhancing the flavors of the seafood.

Variations to Try

While the classic seafood Alfredo is a favorite for many, there are plenty of ways to customize the dish to suit your tastes. Here are a few variations you can experiment with:

1. Lobster Alfredo: Replace the shrimp and scallops with lobster tail for an even more luxurious seafood dish. The sweet lobster meat pairs wonderfully with the creamy Alfredo sauce.

2. Seafood Medley: If you’re a fan of variety, use a mix of different seafood such as mussels, clams, and squid. This will add depth of flavor and texture to the dish.

3. Vegetarian Alfredo: For a lighter, vegetarian version of Alfredo, omit the seafood and substitute with sautéed mushrooms, spinach, or roasted vegetables. The creamy Alfredo sauce still delivers a rich, comforting experience.

4. Garlic Butter Sauce: If you prefer a garlic butter sauce over a creamy one, you can skip the heavy cream and milk and simply toss the seafood and pasta in a mixture of butter, garlic, and fresh herbs.

This rich and creamy seafood Alfredo offers a balance of luxurious flavors and textures that will leave you wanting more. The combination of tender shrimp, sweet scallops, and a velvety, Parmesan-infused sauce is a match made in culinary heaven. Whether you’re preparing it for a special dinner or a weeknight treat, this dish will become a go-to favorite in your kitchen.

From its creamy sauce to the succulent seafood, every element of this dish is designed to delight. The best part is that it’s relatively easy to prepare, so you can enjoy a gourmet meal without spending hours in the kitchen. With just a few simple ingredients and a bit of love, you can create a restaurant-quality dish that will impress your family, friends, or yourself!
