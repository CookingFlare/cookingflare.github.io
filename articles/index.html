Ingredients: 4 large apples, peeled and sliced 1 cup rolled oats 1/2 cup almond flour 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted 1/4 cup maple syrup 1/4 cup chopped walnuts 2 teaspoons cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg Pinch of salt Instructions: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a baking dish. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt. Toss until the apples are evenly coated with the spices. Transfer the spiced apples to the greased baking dish, spreading them out evenly. In the same bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, chopped walnuts, and a pinch of salt. Mix until the ingredients are well combined and form a crumbly texture. Sprinkle the oat and almond mixture evenly over the apples in the baking dish, covering them completely. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown. Remove the apple crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve warm and enjoy on its own or with a scoop of dairy-free vanilla ice cream for an extra treat! Tips and Variations: Feel free to experiment with different types of apples for varying flavors and textures. If you prefer a crunchier topping, you can add more chopped walnuts or sprinkle some granola over the apple mixture before adding the crumb topping. For a touch of indulgence, drizzle some dairy-free caramel sauce over the warm apple crisp before serving. Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Homemade Dairy-Free Ice Cream Recipes to Beat the Heat

Summer is here, and what better way to beat the heat than with a refreshing bowl of homemade dairy-free ice cream? Whether you’re lactose intolerant, following a vegan diet, or simply looking for a healthier alternative, these recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth while keeping you cool. So grab your blender and let’s dive into the world of dairy-free frozen treats! Creamy Coconut Delight Ingredients: 2 cans of full-fat coconut milk 1/2 cup of maple syrup 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Pinch of salt Instructions: In a blender, combine the coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and well combined. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a lidded container and freeze for at least 4 hours, or until firm. Serve in bowls or cones, and enjoy the creamy and tropical flavors of this coconut delight! Tangy Lemon Sorbet Ingredients: 4 lemons, juiced 1 cup of water 1 cup of granulated sugar Zest of 1 lemon Instructions: In a saucepan, combine the water and sugar over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the syrup cool for a few minutes. Add the lemon juice and zest to the syrup and mix well. Pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, take the dish out of the freezer and scrape the mixture with a fork to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process for about 4 hours, or until the sorbet has a smooth and slushy consistency. Transfer the sorbet to a lidded container and freeze for an additional 2 hours, or until firm. Serve in chilled bowls, and savor the tangy and refreshing taste of this lemon sorbet! Decadent Chocolate Banana Nice Cream Ingredients: 4 ripe bananas, sliced and frozen 1/4 cup of cocoa powder 2 tablespoons of almond butter 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Instructions: In a food processor, blend the frozen banana slices until they resemble a creamy texture. Add the cocoa powder, almond butter, and vanilla extract to the food processor. Blend until all the ingredients are well incorporated. Transfer the mixture to a lidded container and freeze for at least 2 hours, or until firm. Scoop the nice cream into bowls and indulge in the rich and chocolatey goodness! Sweet and Fruity Raspberry Sorbet Ingredients: 3 cups of fresh or frozen raspberries 1/2 cup of water 1/2 cup of granulated sugar 1 tablespoon of lemon juice Instructions: In a saucepan, combine the raspberries, water, sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the raspberries break down and the sugar dissolves. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool for a few minutes. Pour the mixture into a blender and blend until smooth. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any seeds. Transfer the strained mixture to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Once the sorbet reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a lidded container and freeze for at least 4 hours, or until firm. Serve in chilled bowls and revel in the sweetness of this fruity raspberry sorbet! With these easy and mouthwatering recipes, you can satisfy your ice cream cravings while staying true to your dietary needs.
