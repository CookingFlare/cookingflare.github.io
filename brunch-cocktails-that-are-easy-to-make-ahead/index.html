Brunch Cocktails That Are Easy to Make Ahead


Brunch is one of the most celebrated occasions for socializing, relaxing, and indulging in delicious food and drinks. If you’re hosting a brunch or simply enjoying one at home with friends and family, nothing elevates the experience quite like the perfect cocktail. However, when you’re entertaining a crowd, mixing drinks on the spot can be a hassle. That’s why making cocktails ahead of time is such a game changer.

In this guide, we’ll explore a variety of brunch cocktails that are easy to make ahead, so you can spend more time enjoying your guests’ company and less time behind the bar. From classics with a twist to unique concoctions, these recipes will ensure your brunch is a hit. Whether you’re craving something fruity, bubbly, or rich in flavor, we’ve got the perfect cocktail ideas to suit every palate.

Why Make Cocktails Ahead of Time?

Preparing cocktails in advance not only saves you time, but it also ensures a smooth flow of the brunch party. Here are some benefits of making cocktails ahead of time:

  1. Less Stress: By preparing the cocktails in advance, you can avoid running around mixing drinks while your guests are enjoying themselves. It gives you more time to focus on other aspects of hosting, like food and ambiance.

  2. Consistency: When you make cocktails in advance, you can ensure that every drink tastes exactly the same, providing your guests with a consistent experience.

  3. Less Mess: Mixing drinks requires multiple ingredients, which can lead to spills and a cluttered counter. Making the cocktails ahead of time reduces the chances of a messy brunch.

  4. Chilled Drinks: Some cocktails need time to chill or infuse flavors, which is perfect for making them in advance. It helps the flavors meld together beautifully, resulting in a drink that’s even better than when it’s made on the spot.

Tips for Making Brunch Cocktails Ahead of Time

Before we dive into the recipes, here are a few key tips to make sure your cocktails are both delicious and easy to prepare:

  • Choose the Right Cocktails: Some cocktails, especially those with carbonated ingredients, should be mixed just before serving to retain their fizz. For others, you can make a large batch and store them in the fridge.
  • Infuse Flavors: Many cocktails improve with time. You can prepare flavored syrups, infusions, or alcohol-based drinks the night before to allow the flavors to blend.
  • Serve in a Pitcher or Large Glassware: When making cocktails for a crowd, serving them in pitchers or large jugs makes for easy pouring and reduces the amount of work during the brunch.
  • Consider Garnishes: Prepare garnishes ahead of time (such as sliced fruit, herbs, or rims with sugar or salt) and have them ready to add just before serving.

Now, let’s dive into some incredible brunch cocktails that are easy to make ahead!

1. Classic Mimosa

No brunch is complete without the classic mimosa. This sparkling drink is light, refreshing, and the perfect pairing for any breakfast or brunch spread. While traditionally made with just orange juice and Champagne, there are many ways to enhance the classic formula.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of Champagne (or sparkling wine)
  • 2 cups of fresh orange juice (or use a premium bottled variety)
  • Optional: A splash of Grand Marnier or orange liqueur for an extra citrus kick
  • Fresh orange slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pour the orange juice into a large pitcher and chill.
  2. When ready to serve, pour the chilled Champagne or sparkling wine into the pitcher with the orange juice. Add the optional splash of Grand Marnier for a slightly boozy twist.
  3. Stir gently to combine. Be careful not to stir too vigorously to retain the bubbles.
  4. Serve immediately in glasses and garnish with fresh orange slices.

You can make this cocktail ahead of time by combining the orange juice and Grand Marnier (if using) in the pitcher the night before. Just add the Champagne just before serving to preserve the bubbles.

2. Bellini

The Bellini is a delightful Italian cocktail made with sparkling wine and peach purée. It’s sweet, fruity, and oh-so-refreshing. Making the peach purée ahead of time saves you even more time on the day of your brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh peaches, peeled and pitted
  • 1/4 cup of sugar (optional, depending on the sweetness of your peaches)
  • 1 bottle of Prosecco or sparkling wine
  • Fresh mint for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, puree the peaches until smooth. You can add a little sugar if the peaches are not as sweet as you’d like.
  2. Transfer the peach purée into a jar or bottle and refrigerate for up to 24 hours.
  3. When ready to serve, pour the chilled peach purée into a glass or pitcher, filling each glass halfway.
  4. Top off each glass with chilled Prosecco or sparkling wine.
  5. Stir gently and garnish with a sprig of fresh mint if desired.

This cocktail is perfect for making in bulk and can be served in large pitchers for guests to serve themselves.

3. Bloody Mary

A Bloody Mary is a brunch classic known for its savory flavors. This cocktail can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge, allowing the flavors to develop even more. You can adjust the spice level to suit your preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of tomato juice
  • 1/4 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon of celery salt
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika (optional for a smoky twist)
  • 1/4 cup of vodka
  • Celery stalks, pickle spears, and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the tomato juice, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, celery salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika.
  2. Stir well and taste, adjusting the seasonings to your liking.
  3. Add vodka to the mixture and stir to combine.
  4. Cover the pitcher and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or up to 24 hours, to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. To serve, pour the Bloody Mary mixture into glasses filled with ice, and garnish with celery stalks, pickle spears, and lemon wedges.

4. Spiked Lemonade

A refreshing, tart, and sweet cocktail, spiked lemonade is perfect for a sunny brunch. This version can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator until you’re ready to serve.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 cup of simple syrup (made by dissolving equal parts sugar and water)
  • 2 cups of vodka
  • 2 cups of cold water or soda water for fizz
  • Fresh lemon slices and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the freshly squeezed lemon juice and simple syrup.
  2. Add vodka and cold water (or soda water if you prefer a fizzy drink) and stir to combine.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness with more syrup or water if needed.
  4. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
  5. To serve, pour over ice and garnish with fresh lemon slices and mint leaves.

5. Tropical Rum Punch

Bring the flavors of the tropics to your brunch table with this easy-to-make rum punch. The vibrant combination of fruit juices and rum is a surefire crowd-pleaser, and it’s easy to make in large quantities.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of pineapple juice
  • 1 cup of orange juice
  • 1/2 cup of lime juice
  • 1/2 cup of grenadine
  • 2 cups of white rum
  • 1/2 cup of dark rum (for depth)
  • Pineapple slices and maraschino cherries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the pineapple juice, orange juice, lime juice, and grenadine. Stir to combine.
  2. Add both the white rum and dark rum, mixing well.
  3. Refrigerate the mixture until ready to serve.
  4. To serve, pour over ice and garnish with pineapple slices and maraschino cherries.

6. Gin and Tonic Fizz

For those who enjoy a crisp and clean cocktail, a Gin and Tonic Fizz is a perfect choice. You can make the gin mixture ahead of time and add the tonic just before serving to keep the fizz fresh.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of gin
  • 1/2 cup of fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup of simple syrup
  • 3 cups of tonic water (add just before serving)
  • Lime wedges and fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pitcher, combine the gin, lime juice, and simple syrup.
  2. Stir and refrigerate until ready to serve.
  3. When serving, pour over ice and top with tonic water.
  4. Garnish with lime wedges and fresh mint leaves.

Conclusion

Brunch cocktails are a fun way to spice up your gathering and enhance the flavors of your meal. Making cocktails ahead of time reduces the stress of last-minute mixing and gives you more time to enjoy your brunch. Whether you prefer something fruity, sparkling, or savory, these easy-to-make brunch cocktails will leave a lasting impression on your guests. Cheers to stress-free brunches with delicious drinks that are sure to delight!
