Satisfy Your Cravings: 8 Fall Snack Recipes


When the weather turns crisp and the leaves start to fall, there’s something about the season that calls for cozy, flavorful snacks. Whether you’re curled up with a good book, hosting a small gathering, or just need a quick bite after a day of apple picking, fall snacks bring comfort and warmth to the table. Packed with seasonal flavors like cinnamon, pumpkin, apple, and maple, these eight fall snack recipes will satisfy your cravings and celebrate everything we love about autumn.

1. Cinnamon-Spiced Apple Chips

Apple season peaks in fall, making it the perfect time to whip up a healthy yet delicious treat. These homemade apple chips are crunchy, naturally sweet, and spiced with warm cinnamon—a snack that’s as wholesome as it is addictive.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium apples (Honeycrisp, Fuji, or Gala work well)
  • 1½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon sugar or coconut sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°F (93°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Core the apples and slice them into thin, even rounds (a mandoline slicer works best for this).
  3. Arrange the apple slices in a single layer on the baking sheets, ensuring they don’t overlap.
  4. Sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar if using.
  5. Bake for 1 hour, then flip the apple slices and bake for another hour, or until they’re crispy.
  6. Let them cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

Enjoy these apple chips as a guilt-free snack, or pair them with a caramel dip for an indulgent treat.

2. Pumpkin Spice Energy Bites

For a grab-and-go snack bursting with fall flavor, these no-bake pumpkin spice energy bites are perfect. Packed with oats, nut butter, and a touch of pumpkin puree, they provide just the right boost of energy on busy autumn days.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • ½ cup canned pumpkin puree
  • ¼ cup almond butter (or peanut butter)
  • 3 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup mini chocolate chips
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together oats, pumpkin puree, almond butter, honey, pumpkin pie spice, salt, and vanilla extract until fully combined.
  2. Fold in the mini chocolate chips.
  3. Roll the mixture into small, bite-sized balls.
  4. Place the bites on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set.

These bites are a delightful mix of sweet and spicy flavors, making them a seasonal favorite.

3. Maple-Glazed Pecan Snack Mix

Take your snack game to the next level with this crunchy, sweet, and savory maple-glazed pecan mix. It’s perfect for munching on during movie nights or taking on a scenic fall hike.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pecan halves
  • 1 cup pretzel sticks
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Stir in the maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt until fully combined.
  3. In a large bowl, mix pecans and pretzels, then drizzle the maple glaze over them. Toss to coat.
  4. Spread the mixture on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes, stirring once halfway through.
  6. Remove from the oven and let the mixture cool completely. Toss in the dried cranberries before serving.

This snack mix boasts a perfect balance of textures and flavors, making it an irresistible crowd-pleaser.

4. Butternut Squash Hummus with Rosemary Pita Chips

For a savory snack that embraces fall’s essence, this butternut squash hummus will be your new go-to. Creamy, slightly sweet, and earthy, it pairs beautifully with homemade rosemary pita chips.

Ingredients (Hummus):

  • 1½ cups cooked butternut squash (roasted or steamed)
  • 1 can chickpeas (15 oz), drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Ingredients (Pita Chips):

  • 4 pita bread rounds
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) for the pita chips.
  2. To make the hummus: Blend all the hummus ingredients in a food processor or blender until smooth. Add a splash of water if the consistency is too thick.
  3. For pita chips: Cut the pita rounds into triangles and arrange them on a baking sheet.
  4. Mix olive oil, dried rosemary, garlic powder, and salt in a small bowl. Brush this mixture over the pita triangles.
  5. Bake pita chips for 8-10 minutes or until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve the warm pita chips with the butternut squash hummus.

This pairing elevates snack time with a touch of gourmet sophistication.

5. Sweet Potato Fries with Spiced Yogurt Dip

Sweet potatoes are a fall staple, and baking them into crispy fries is an obvious (and delicious) choice. Pair them with a tangy, spiced yogurt dip for a snack that hits all the right notes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cut into thin fries
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Dip:

  • ½ cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the sweet potato fries with cornstarch, then mix in olive oil and spices until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the fries in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until fries are crisp and golden.
  5. Mix the dip ingredients in a small bowl while the fries bake.
  6. Serve the fries warm with the yogurt dip on the side.

This savory snack is sure to keep you coming back for more.

6. Cranberry-Orange Baked Brie Bites

Elevate your autumn snacking with these bite-sized brie treats. Perfect for entertaining, they combine the tangy sweetness of cranberry and orange with the rich creaminess of melted brie.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 wheel of brie cheese
  • ¼ cup cranberry sauce
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a mini muffin tin.
  2. Cut the puff pastry into small squares and press each square into the muffin cups.
  3. Cut the brie into small cubes and place one in each cup.
  4. Top with a teaspoon of cranberry sauce and a sprinkle of orange zest.
  5. Brush the edges of the puff pastry with egg wash for shine.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and puffed.
  7. Let cool slightly before serving.

These bites are guaranteed to be a hit at any autumn gathering.

7. Honey-Roasted Pumpkin Seeds

Don’t let those pumpkin seeds go to waste after carving your jack-o’-lantern. With a crispy coating and a touch of sweetness, these honey-roasted seeds make an excellent snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw pumpkin seeds (cleaned and dried)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, toss pumpkin seeds with honey, melted butter, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Spread the seeds in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden and crisp.
  5. Let cool completely before enjoying.

These sweet and crunchy seeds are a quintessential fall treat.

8. Caramelized Pear Crostini

These elegant yet simple crostini are perfect for fall afternoons. The combination of caramelized pears, creamy cheese, and crusty bread is nothing short of heavenly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced into rounds
  • 2 ripe pears, sliced thin
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 4 ounces cream cheese or goat cheese
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the baguette slices under a broiler until golden.
  2. In a skillet, melt the butter and honey over medium heat. Add the pear slices and cook until caramelized, about 5 minutes.
  3. Spread cheese on each toasted baguette slice and top with caramelized pears.
  4. Sprinkle with fresh thyme for an herby touch.
  5. Serve immediately.

These crostini are an excellent blend of sweet and savory perfect for snacking or serving as appetizers.

From sweet to savory, these fall snack recipes are versatile, easy to make, and embody the essence of the season. Grab your apron and give these snacks a try—autumn has never tasted so good!
