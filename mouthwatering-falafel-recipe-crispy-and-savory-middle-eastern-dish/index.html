Mouthwatering Falafel Recipe: Crispy and Savory Middle Eastern Dish


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, then you’ve probably encountered the delightful flavors of falafel. These crispy, savory, and herb-infused chickpea fritters are a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine and have gained popularity worldwide due to their delicious taste and versatility. Whether served in pita bread with fresh veggies and tahini sauce or enjoyed on their own as a flavorful appetizer, falafel never fails to impress. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through an authentic falafel recipe that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of the Middle East.

Ingredients

For the Falafel:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For Serving:

  • Pita bread
  • Lettuce
  • Tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Red onion
  • Tahini sauce

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas: Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover them with cold water. Allow them to soak overnight. The next day, drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, and baking soda. Pulse the mixture until it forms a coarse paste. Be careful not to over-process the mixture; it should have some texture.

  3. Form the Falafel: Using your hands, shape the falafel mixture into small balls or patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Fry the Falafel: In a deep, heavy-bottomed pot, heat vegetable oil to 350°F. Carefully drop the falafel into the hot oil, frying them in batches until they are golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the falafel to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

  5. Assemble and Serve: Warm the pita bread and fill it with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and a generous serving of falafel. Drizzle with tahini sauce and enjoy!

Tips and Tricks

  • Use Dried Chickpeas: It’s essential to use dried chickpeas for this recipe, as canned chickpeas will not yield the same texture and flavor.
  • Don’t Skip Soaking: Allowing the chickpeas to soak overnight ensures that they soften and become the perfect texture for the falafel mixture.
  • Control the Heat: Keep an eye on the oil temperature while frying the falafel to ensure they cook evenly without burning.

Final Thoughts

This mouthwatering falafel recipe captures the essence of Middle Eastern cuisine with its fragrant herbs and spices, crispy exterior, and tender, flavorful interior. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or new to preparing Middle Eastern dishes, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests. Experiment with different serving options and enjoy the delightful flavors of this beloved Middle Eastern dish. So, why not bring a taste of the Middle East to your kitchen and savor the deliciousness of homemade falafel?
