A French Twist on a Classic: Bouef Bourguignon Pizza


Pizza has always been a beloved dish worldwide, with countless variations and flavors. Today, we’re going to take this Italian classic and give it a French twist by combining the rich flavors of Bouef Bourguignon with the beloved pizza base. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey that will tantalize your taste buds and transport you to the beautiful vineyards of Burgundy.

Ingredients

For the Pizza Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

For the Bouef Bourguignon Topping:

  • 1 pound of beef stew meat, cut into small cubes
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup of red wine (preferably Burgundy)
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of flour
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Pizza Sauce:

  • 1 can (14 ounces) of crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Cheese Topping:

  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pizza Crust:

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
    • Gradually add the warm water and olive oil to the dry ingredients. Stir until a dough forms.
    • Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.

  2. Prepare the Bouef Bourguignon Topping:

    • In a large skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside.
    • In the same skillet, add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef and set it aside with the bacon.
    • In the same skillet, melt the butter and sauté the onion, garlic, carrot, and mushrooms until softened.
    • Add the tomato paste and flour to the skillet and stir well.
    • Slowly pour in the red wine and beef broth, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens.
    • Return the cooked bacon and beef to the skillet. Season with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme. Simmer for about 1 hour or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  3. Prepare the Pizza Sauce:

    • In a small saucepan, combine the crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Assemble and Bake the Pizza:

    • Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
    • Divide the pizza dough in half and roll out each portion into a round shape on a floured surface.
    • Transfer the rolled-out dough to a baking sheet or pizza stone.
    • Spread a generous amount of the pizza sauce over each dough round.
    • Top the sauce with the Bouef Bourguignon mixture, spreading it evenly.
    • Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese over the toppings.
    • Bake the pizzas in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    • Remove the pizzas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
    • Slice and serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully created a French-inspired twist on a classic pizza. The combination of the rich and savory Bouef Bourguignon with the crispy pizza crust and gooey cheese is a match made in culinary heaven. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a unique and delicious meal, this Bouef Bourguignon Pizza will surely impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. Bon appétit!
