How to Master the Old Fashioned: A Step-by-Step Recipe


Are you a fan of classic cocktails? If so, you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’ll be diving into the art of making the perfect Old Fashioned. This timeless drink has been a staple in cocktail bars for decades, and with good reason. Its simplicity and bold flavors make it a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts all over the world. So grab your favorite whiskey and let’s get started!

Ingredients

To make a delicious Old Fashioned, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ounces of your preferred whiskey
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters
  • A few dashes of water
  • Ice cubes
  • Orange peel, for garnish
  • Maraschino cherry, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Begin by placing the sugar cube in an Old Fashioned glass.

  2. Add 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters to the glass. The bitters will add depth and complexity to the drink, so don’t be afraid to be generous.

  3. Using a muddler or the back of a spoon, gently muddle the sugar cube and bitters together until they form a paste-like consistency.

  4. Add a few dashes of water to the glass. This will help dissolve the sugar and create a smooth texture.

  5. Next, add the 2 ounces of whiskey to the glass. Choose a whiskey that you enjoy and that pairs well with the other flavors in the cocktail. The choice of whiskey can greatly impact the overall taste of your Old Fashioned.

  6. Fill the glass with ice cubes. The ice will chill the drink and dilute it slightly, balancing out the flavors.

  7. Stir the cocktail gently for about 30 seconds, allowing the flavors to meld together. Stirring rather than shaking is preferred for an Old Fashioned, as it creates a more balanced and mellow drink.

  8. Once the cocktail is well mixed, take a vegetable peeler or a knife and cut a strip of orange peel. Twist the peel over the glass to release the oils, then place it in the drink as a garnish. The aroma of the orange oils will enhance the overall experience of the cocktail.

  9. If desired, you can also add a maraschino cherry as a garnish. While not traditional, it adds a touch of sweetness and color to the drink.

  10. Your Old Fashioned is now ready to be enjoyed! Sip it slowly and savor the flavors as they unfold on your palate. The combination of whiskey, bitters, and citrus notes from the orange peel create a well-balanced and sophisticated cocktail.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different types of whiskey to find your favorite variation of the Old Fashioned. Rye whiskey is a popular choice, as its spiciness adds an extra kick to the drink. Bourbon, on the other hand, brings a smoother and sweeter flavor profile.

  • If you prefer a sweeter Old Fashioned, you can use simple syrup instead of a sugar cube. Simply replace the sugar cube with ½ to 1 teaspoon of simple syrup, depending on your taste preferences.

  • For a smokier twist, try using a peated whiskey. The smoky notes will add a unique dimension to the classic cocktail.

  • Don’t be afraid to get creative with your garnishes. Experiment with different types of citrus peels or even add a few drops of aromatic bitters on top for an extra layer of complexity.

  • Remember, the key to a great Old Fashioned is balance. Adjust the amounts of sugar, bitters, and water to suit your personal taste. Feel free to experiment and make it your own!

Now that you have mastered the art of making the perfect Old Fashioned, it’s time to impress your friends and family with your cocktail skills. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply enjoying a quiet evening at home, the Old Fashioned is a classic choice that never goes out of style. So grab your shaker, gather your ingredients, and get ready to sip on a timeless masterpiece. Cheers!
