From Kimchi to Kombucha: Exploring the Wonders of Fermented Korean Foods


Your taste buds are in for a delightful adventure as we traverse the rich world of fermented Korean pantry staples. With its roots in ancient Korea, fermentation techniques have long been embraced for their ability to enhance flavors, improve digestion, and preserve foods for seasons on end. This tutorial will acquaint you with two essentials – kimchi and kombucha – guiding you into your home fermentation expedition.

Kimchi: The Cultural Pride of Korea

Kimchi, Korea’s national treasure, is more than just an appetizing side dish. The marriage of spicy, savory, and slightly acidic flavors creates a harmonious explosion on your palate. Beyond its illustrious gastronomic experience, kimchi symbolizes the unity of nature, as it encompasses the importance of napa cabbage representing Yang, and the spicy paste representing Yin. Traditionally, during harvest seasons, households would store away their kimchi underground in clay jars called ’nuk-dug’ to last the winter months. Let’s look at creating a vegetarian kimchi to warm up your winter kitchen.

Vegetarian Kimchi Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound napa cabbage, washed and cut into small squares
  • 2 tablespoons chili flakes
  • 2 teaspoons garlic granules
  • 2 teaspoons ginger powder
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable base seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons Schemezi (a combination of chili paste, seafood paste, and soybean paste)
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 scallion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons salt

Instructions:

  1. Soak the cabbage in cold water to soften it well.
  2. Mix the chili flakes, garlic granules, ginger powder, vegetable base seasoning, and salt; set aside.
  3. Roughly chop garlic and scatter over the cabbage. Pour Schemezi on top.
  4. Rub thoroughly to enter into the leaves.
  5. Place a plate and a weight over the mound to ensure compression.
  6. Leave in a kitchen area with no direct sunlight for around 3 days, until desired taste and texture are reached.

Kombucha: The Probiotic Powerhouse

Unlike kimchi, kombucha hails from a much more recent fermentation technique, but its implications for gut health make it just as essential. Kombuch-awesome as it is, not many know about this symbiotic relationship between tea and bacteria. This Chinese invention, adopted by Korean culture, combines black tea with SCOBY (Symbiotic Culture Of Bacteria and Yeast) to create a delicious, sugar-reducing, probiotic beverage that can help alleviate digestive issues. Please invite GTA into your kitchen with a simple recipe beginner can easily follow.

Basic Kombucha Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 gallon purified water
  • 10 bags of black tea (or 1 cup of loose leaf)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 starter batch of kombucha (scoby + liquid from previous batch)
  • 2 months of patience

Instructions:

  1. Boil water with tea bags and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Add sugar and stir until dissolved.
  3. Cool the tea to body temperature (95°F to 105°F).
  4. Pour it into a glass jar.
  5. Introduce the SCOBY and liquid from previous kombuch
  6. Cover the opening with a cloth and fasten it.
  7. Set in a dark corner; let it do its magic for 7-10 days.
  8. Once done, remove the SCOBY and reserve it for the next batch.
  9. Tunnel through a fine mesh sieve to strain your beverage.
  10. Store it in the fridge to stop fermentation and let the flavor meld.
