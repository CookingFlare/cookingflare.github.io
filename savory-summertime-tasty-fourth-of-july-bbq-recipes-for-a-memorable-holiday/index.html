Classic BBQ Favorites

Before we dive into the more adventurous recipes, let’s start with the tried-and-true classics that never go out of style. These crowd-pleasing BBQ staples are sure to satisfy even the most discerning palates.

Grilled Burgers with Caramelized Onions

  • 4 beef patties
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, and condiments (optional)

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Form the beef patties and season with salt and pepper. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Meanwhile, caramelize the onions by cooking them in olive oil over low heat for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Assemble the burgers with caramelized onions, lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, and condiments.

Classic BBQ Ribs

  • 2 racks pork ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat your grill to medium-low heat. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and season with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Grill for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone. Brush with BBQ sauce during the last 10 minutes of grilling.

Global Inspirations

As we venture beyond the classic BBQ fare, we’ll explore international flavors that add a twist to the traditional Fourth of July menu. These recipes will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more.

Korean-Style BBQ Tacos

  • 1 pound beef or pork, sliced into thin strips
  • 1/4 cup Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Sliced green onions, cilantro, and kimchi (optional)

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Marinate the beef or pork in a mixture of Gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and sesame oil for at least 30 minutes. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Serve in corn tortillas with sliced green onions, cilantro, and kimchi.

Grilled Halloumi with Honey and Thyme

  • 1 block halloumi cheese, sliced into 1/2-inch thick pieces
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the halloumi slices with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle with thyme.

Refreshing Summer Sides

No Fourth of July BBQ is complete without a selection of cool and refreshing sides that complement the smoky flavors of the grill. Here are a few ideas to get you started.

Cucumber and Feta Salad

  • 2 large cucumbers, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 block feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Combine the cucumber, feta, red onion, and parsley in a bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Grilled Corn with Garlic Butter

  • 4 ears corn, husked and silked
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the corn with garlic butter and season with salt and pepper. Grill for 10-12 minutes, turning every 2-3 minutes, or until lightly charred.

Sweet Treats

No Fourth of July celebration is complete without a sweet treat or two to satisfy your sweet tooth. Here are a few ideas to get you started.

Watermelon Patriotic Parfait

  • 2 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1 cup vanilla yogurt
  • 1 cup granola
  • 1/4 cup blueberries
  • 1/4 cup strawberries
  • 1/4 cup whipped cream
  • 1/4 cup red, white, and blue sprinkles (optional)

Layer the watermelon, yogurt, granola, blueberries, strawberries, and whipped cream in a glass or parfait dish. Top with red, white, and blue sprinkles for a patriotic touch.

Star-Spangled Berry Pie

  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pie crust

Preheat your oven to 375Â°F (190Â°C). In a bowl, combine the mixed berries, granulated sugar, and cornstarch. Let it sit for 15-20 minutes, until the berries start to release their juice. Roll out the pie crust and fill with the berry mixture. Dot the top with melted butter and brush with beaten egg. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
