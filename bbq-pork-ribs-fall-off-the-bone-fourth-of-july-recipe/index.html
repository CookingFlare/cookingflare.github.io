BBQ Pork Ribs: Fall-off-the-Bone Fourth of July Recipe


The Fourth of July is a time for gathering with friends and family, enjoying the summer weather, and of course, indulging in some delicious barbecue. One of the most beloved dishes for this occasion is BBQ pork ribs – tender, succulent, and full of flavor. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create the ultimate fall-off-the-bone BBQ pork ribs that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pounds of pork baby back ribs
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Aluminum foil

Instructions

  1. Prep the Ribs: Start by removing the membrane from the back of the ribs. This will allow the flavors to penetrate the meat more effectively. Simply use a butter knife to lift a corner of the membrane, then grip it with a paper towel and pull it off in one piece.

  2. Season the Ribs: In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and salt. Rub this seasoning all over the ribs, ensuring that they are evenly coated. This step is crucial for infusing the meat with a rich, smoky flavor.

  3. Wrap and Refrigerate: Once the ribs are seasoned, wrap them tightly in aluminum foil. Place the wrapped ribs on a baking sheet and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the meat to tenderize.

  4. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to 275°F (135°C) for indirect cooking. If you’re using a charcoal grill, place the coals on one side and the ribs on the other to create a gentle, indirect heat. For a gas grill, simply turn off one or more burners to achieve the same effect.

  5. Slow Cook the Ribs: Place the foil-wrapped ribs on the grill and close the lid. Let them cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, maintaining a consistent temperature. The low and slow cooking method is essential for achieving that tender, fall-off-the-bone texture.

  6. Unwrap and Glaze: Carefully unwrap the ribs and brush them generously with your favorite BBQ sauce. Increase the grill temperature to around 300°F (150°C) and place the ribs back on the grill, uncovered. Allow the sauce to caramelize and create a beautiful glaze, which will add a final layer of flavor to the ribs.

  7. Rest and Serve: Once the ribs are glazed, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring that each bite is as juicy as it is flavorful. Finally, slice the ribs between the bones and serve them with extra BBQ sauce on the side.

Conclusion

With this fall-off-the-bone BBQ pork ribs recipe, you’ll be the star of your Fourth of July celebration. The combination of a well-seasoned rub, slow cooking, and a flavorful glaze results in ribs that are sure to impress. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply enjoying a meal with your loved ones, these ribs are a must-try for any summer occasion. So fire up the grill, gather your favorite people, and get ready to savor every mouthwatering bite of these irresistible BBQ pork ribs!
