How to Make Tacos Using Hard Shells and Soft Shells


Tacos are one of the most beloved and versatile dishes in the world. Whether you prefer the crunch of a hard shell or the softness of a flour tortilla, tacos can be customized with a wide variety of fillings, toppings, and sauces. The beauty of tacos lies in their simplicity and the endless possibilities they offer for creativity in the kitchen. If you’re wondering how to make tacos using both hard shells and soft shells, this guide will walk you through the steps, from preparing the ingredients to assembling your tacos.

The Basics of Tacos: Hard Shell vs. Soft Shell

Before diving into the recipes, it’s essential to understand the difference between hard and soft shells.

  • Hard Shells: Traditionally made from corn tortillas, hard shells are fried or baked to create a crispy, crunchy texture. They provide a satisfying contrast to the fillings and toppings, making each bite a delightful crunch.

  • Soft Shells: These are typically made from flour tortillas, which are soft and pliable. Soft shell tacos are easier to fold and can hold more fillings than their hard-shell counterparts. The softness of the tortilla allows the flavors to meld together more easily.

Both options have their own merits, and the choice ultimately depends on personal preference. Some people love the crunch of hard shells, while others prefer the flexibility and softness of the flour tortillas. Fortunately, both can be easily made at home with a few simple ingredients and techniques.

Ingredients for Tacos

Whether you’re making tacos with hard or soft shells, you’ll need some basic ingredients to build your perfect taco. Here’s a list of essential ingredients for both:

Taco Shells

  • Hard Taco Shells: You can buy pre-made hard taco shells at the store, or you can make your own by frying or baking corn tortillas. For a healthier option, you can opt for baked hard shells.

  • Soft Taco Shells: Flour tortillas are the most common choice for soft shells, but you can also use corn tortillas if you prefer. For a more authentic Mexican experience, consider using homemade tortillas.

Protein Options

  • Ground Beef: The classic taco filling, seasoned with taco seasoning.
  • Chicken: Shredded chicken cooked with spices for a flavorful taco option.
  • Pork: Carnitas, or slow-cooked pulled pork, is a popular choice for tacos.
  • Vegetarian: You can fill your tacos with seasoned beans, roasted vegetables, or tofu for a meatless option.

Taco Toppings

  • Cheese: Shredded cheddar, Monterey Jack, or Mexican blend cheese are popular choices.
  • Lettuce: Shredded iceberg lettuce adds a fresh crunch to your tacos.
  • Tomatoes: Diced tomatoes provide a juicy and flavorful topping.
  • Onions: Chopped red or white onions add a savory kick.
  • Cilantro: Fresh cilantro enhances the flavor and adds a touch of brightness.
  • Avocado: Sliced or mashed avocado (or guacamole) for a creamy texture.
  • Sour Cream: A classic addition for extra creaminess and tang.
  • Salsa: Whether mild, medium, or hot, salsa is a staple topping for tacos.

Additional Toppings

  • Pickled Jalapeños: For a spicy kick.
  • Lime Wedges: Fresh lime juice adds acidity and balances the richness of the fillings.
  • Hot Sauce: A favorite for taco enthusiasts who love a bit of heat.

How to Make Hard Shell Tacos

Hard shell tacos offer a satisfying crunch that complements the flavorful fillings inside. If you’re using store-bought hard shells, the process is quick and easy. However, making your own hard shells from scratch adds a personal touch to your tacos.

Step 1: Prepare the Hard Taco Shells

If you’re using store-bought hard taco shells, simply follow the instructions on the package for warming them up. Most hard taco shells require heating in the oven for a few minutes to become crispy and hot.

If you want to make homemade hard taco shells:

  1. Fry Corn Tortillas: Heat a few tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place a corn tortilla in the oil and fry it for about 1-2 minutes on each side until crispy and golden brown.
  2. Shape the Shells: While frying, you can shape the taco shells by using tongs to fold the tortilla in half while it cooks. This will create the classic taco shell shape.
  3. Drain the Oil: Once fried, remove the taco shell from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

You can choose from a variety of fillings depending on your preference. Here’s how to make a classic ground beef filling:

  1. Cook the Ground Beef: In a skillet over medium heat, brown 1 lb of ground beef until fully cooked, breaking it apart with a spoon.
  2. Add Seasoning: Stir in taco seasoning (either store-bought or homemade) along with a bit of water and simmer for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.
  3. Optional: You can also add diced onions and garlic for extra flavor while cooking the beef.

Step 3: Assemble the Tacos

  1. Take a warm hard taco shell and fill it with the seasoned beef or other filling of choice.
  2. Add your favorite toppings like cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and avocado.
  3. Squeeze some lime juice on top and finish with a dollop of sour cream and a drizzle of salsa or hot sauce.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy!

Serve your hard shell tacos with a side of Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh salad for a complete meal. Enjoy the crunchy, flavorful taco experience!

How to Make Soft Shell Tacos

Soft shell tacos are perfect for those who prefer a more pliable tortilla that can hold a variety of fillings. The process of making soft shell tacos is very similar to hard shell tacos, but with a few key differences.

Step 1: Warm the Soft Shells

  1. Store-bought Flour Tortillas: Warm the tortillas on a dry skillet over medium heat for 30-60 seconds on each side until soft and slightly browned.
  2. Homemade Flour Tortillas: You can make your own flour tortillas from scratch by mixing flour, water, salt, and a bit of oil, then rolling them out and cooking them on a hot skillet.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

You can use any of the same fillings that work for hard shell tacos, but soft shells are especially great for holding more fillings. A popular choice is carnitas, or slow-cooked pulled pork. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Slow-Cook the Pork: Season a pork shoulder with cumin, garlic powder, oregano, and salt. Brown the pork on all sides in a hot skillet, then transfer it to a slow cooker. Add some onion, garlic, and a bit of chicken broth, and cook on low for 6-8 hours until the meat is tender and can be shredded easily.
  2. Shred the Pork: Once cooked, shred the pork with two forks and mix it with the juices from the slow cooker to keep it moist.

Step 3: Assemble the Tacos

  1. Take a warm soft tortilla and add your desired filling.
  2. Top it with shredded cheese, lettuce, diced tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and other toppings of choice.
  3. Squeeze some lime juice over the taco and finish with a drizzle of salsa or a spoonful of guacamole.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy!

Soft shell tacos are incredibly versatile, so feel free to experiment with different fillings and toppings. Pair them with chips, guacamole, or a cold beverage for a delightful meal.

Tips for Perfect Tacos

1. Use Fresh Ingredients

The fresher your ingredients, the better your tacos will taste. Freshly chopped vegetables, homemade salsa, and freshly squeezed lime juice can take your tacos to the next level.

2. Don’t Overfill the Tacos

While it’s tempting to load up your tacos with as many fillings as possible, it’s important not to overfill them. A taco that’s too stuffed may tear or spill, making it difficult to eat.

3. Add a Variety of Textures

A good taco has a variety of textures: crunchy, soft, creamy, and juicy. Make sure to include ingredients like lettuce, crispy taco shells, and creamy avocado to balance out the flavors.

4. Be Creative with Fillings

Don’t feel limited to just ground beef. Try different meats like shredded chicken, carnitas, or even fish tacos. For vegetarians, black beans, roasted veggies, and even grilled mushrooms make excellent taco fillings.

5. Make Your Own Taco Seasoning

Homemade taco seasoning can elevate your taco fillings. You can mix ingredients like cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, paprika, and a bit of salt to create a flavorful seasoning blend. Adjust the spices to suit your taste.

Conclusion

Whether you prefer hard shell tacos or soft shell tacos, both options offer delicious and customizable ways to enjoy this classic Mexican dish. The key to great tacos lies in using fresh ingredients, experimenting with different fillings, and adding your favorite toppings. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or making a quick weeknight dinner, tacos are always a crowd-pleaser. So grab your taco shells, fill them with your favorite ingredients, and enjoy a tasty meal that’s sure to satisfy!
