Finger-Licking Good Greek BBQ Ribs Recipe


If you’re in search of a mouthwatering dish that will impress your family and friends, look no further than this Finger-Licking Good Greek BBQ Ribs recipe. This delectable twist on traditional BBQ ribs infuses the rich, savory flavors of Greece, making it a standout option for your next backyard gathering or family dinner. The combination of aromatic herbs, tangy citrus, and a hint of sweetness creates a symphony of flavors that will have everyone coming back for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to dive into a culinary adventure that’s as easy to prepare as it is enjoyable to eat!

Ingredients

For the Ribs

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs (about 4-5 pounds total)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

For the Marinade

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

For the BBQ Sauce

  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Ribs

Begin by preheating your oven to 300°F (150°C). This low and slow cooking method will ensure that your ribs become tender and juicy. While the oven is heating, remove the silver skin from the back of the ribs. This membrane can prevent flavors from penetrating the meat, so it’s best to remove it for optimal taste.

Once the silver skin is off, season the ribs generously with salt and black pepper on both sides. This will enhance the natural flavors of the meat.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, minced garlic, honey, Dijon mustard, oregano, thyme, smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper (if using). Whisk these ingredients together until well blended.

Step 3: Marinate the Ribs

Place the seasoned ribs in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the ribs, making sure they are fully coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, but ideally overnight. Marinating the ribs for longer will allow the flavors to penetrate deeply, resulting in a more flavorful dish.

Step 4: Prepare the BBQ Sauce

While the ribs are marinating, you can prepare the BBQ sauce. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the tomato sauce, honey, Worcestershire sauce, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Stir the mixture until it’s well combined and bring it to a gentle simmer. Allow the sauce to cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 5: Bake the Ribs

After the ribs have marinated, remove them from the fridge and let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil and place a wire rack on top. This setup will allow the heat to circulate around the ribs, ensuring even cooking.

Place the ribs on the wire rack, bone side down. Cover them tightly with another piece of aluminum foil to retain moisture during the baking process. Bake the ribs in the preheated oven for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and pulls away from the bone easily.

Step 6: Grill and Glaze

Once the ribs are cooked, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Carefully remove the ribs from the oven and discard the top layer of foil. Brush a generous amount of the prepared BBQ sauce over the ribs, ensuring they’re well coated.

Place the ribs on the grill, meat side down. Grill for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the sauce to caramelize and develop a beautiful glaze. Flip the ribs over, brush with more BBQ sauce, and grill for an additional 5-7 minutes. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the ribs are perfectly grilled and glazed, remove them from the grill and let them rest for about 10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring a juicy bite every time.

Slice the ribs between the bones and serve them with extra BBQ sauce on the side. Pair your delicious Greek BBQ ribs with traditional sides like tzatziki sauce, Greek salad, or roasted vegetables for a complete feast.

Tips for the Perfect Greek BBQ Ribs

  • Choose Quality Ribs: For the best results, opt for fresh, high-quality ribs. Baby back ribs are tender and flavorful, but you can also use spare ribs if you prefer.
  • Adjust the Heat: Modify the amount of cayenne pepper or add red pepper flakes to the marinade if you enjoy a spicier kick.
  • Experiment with Herbs: Feel free to customize the marinade by adding fresh herbs like rosemary or parsley for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Cook on a Smoker: If you have access to a smoker, consider smoking the ribs instead of grilling them for an even deeper flavor profile. Use wood chips like hickory or applewood for a unique twist.

Conclusion

This Finger-Licking Good Greek BBQ Ribs recipe is sure to be a hit at your next gathering. The infusion of Greek flavors sets these ribs apart from traditional BBQ, making them a delightful addition to your cooking repertoire. The combination of the marinade and BBQ sauce creates a perfect balance of savory, sweet, and tangy notes that will tantalize your taste buds. So, gather your ingredients, embrace your inner chef, and prepare for a culinary experience that will leave everyone raving about your cooking skills. Enjoy the process, and happy grilling!
