Provenance and Flavors: Revive the Ancient Tradition of Roman Pizza with This Recipe


Roman pizza is a timeless culinary delight steeped in history and vibrant flavors. With its roots dating back to ancient Rome, this dish has evolved over centuries and has become an icon of Italian cuisine. This ancient tradition combines simplicity with lavish flavors, proving that true Italian pizza is best when it rises from the heart of its rich culture. Join me as we revitalize this ancient tradition right in your kitchen, using fresh ingredients and ancient techniques.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams of finely ground durum wheat flour (semolina)
  • 5 grams of fresh yeast
  • 30 grams of extra virgin olive oil
  • 10 grams of salt
  • 1 pinch sugar
  • 300 grams of warm water
  • 300 grams of tomato sauce
  • 250 grams of grated mozzarella cheese
  • Abruzzese pepper or red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the dough. Place the water, fresh yeast, sugar, and a little of the flour in a bowl. Mix the ingredients gently, ensuring that everything is well combined.

  2. Cover the mixture and let it rest for about 30 minutes, allowing the yeast to activate.

  3. After 30 minutes, add the rest of the flour, salt, and olive oil into the yeast mixture. You may want to use your hands to mix all the ingredients, making sure it creates a smooth and elastic dough.

  4. Once the dough is thoroughly mixed and fully combined, place the dough on a lightly floured surface and knead for about 15 minutes or until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough in a well-oiled bowl, cover it with a towel or cling wrap, and leave it to rise overnight in a cool, dry place. This slow fermentation is key to the authentic Roman pizza recipe. You may notice that the dough ferments slower, but this process ensures that the flavor is stronger and more aromatic.

  6. The following day, preheat your oven to around 410°F (210°C). Divide the dough into 4 equal portions, and shape each portion into a ball.

  7. Roll out each ball on a lightly floured surface into a thin circle, about 8-10 inches in diameter and 1/8-inch thick. Let the dough rest for 10 minutes before stretching it to your desired size.

  8. Once your oven is preheated, dust a pizza peel or a large flat baking sheet with cornmeal, and gently transfer your stretched pizza dough onto it.

  9. Spread a generous amount of tomato sauce evenly over the surface of the pizza, covering it moderately without overdoing it.

  10. Sprinkle grated mozzarella cheese over the sauce, making sure to distribute it evenly.

  11. At this stage, you may add a pinch of Abruzzese pepper or red pepper flakes for some extra heat, and fresh basil leaves for freshness.

  12. Slide the pizza into the oven, and bake for about 8-10 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown and the cheese becomes bubbly and slightly charred at the edges.

  13. Remove the pizza from the oven using the pizza peel or a wide spatula and let it cool for a couple of minutes. Cut the Roman pizza into slices and serve it hot.

Make a meal of it

Roman pizza stands wonderfully on its own, yet it is equally welcoming to a variety of toppings. If desired, try swapping out the traditional tomato and mozzarella for ingredients like roasted eggplants, mushrooms, or thinly sliced cooked ham. Not only is the Roman pizza easy to personalize, but it’s also a surefire way to bring your classic cuisine up to speed.

Conclusion

The ancient art of Roman pizza-making may have mastered simplicity, but that doesn’t mean it lacks a touch of finesse. With a little bit of patience and quality ingredients picked straight from the source, you can revitalize the time-honored Roman tradition right in your kitchen. Enjoy the ancient flavors of Roman cuisine and take pride in replicating an authentic, timeless dining experience. Buon appetito!
