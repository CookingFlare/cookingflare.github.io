Gluten-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins: A Zesty and Moist Treat


If you’re looking for a delightful gluten-free treat that’s bursting with flavor, these Gluten-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins are just the ticket. Packed with the tangy zest of fresh lemons and the subtle crunch of poppy seeds, these muffins are a perfect companion to your morning coffee or afternoon tea. Plus, they’re incredibly moist and tender, making them a favorite among gluten-free and non-gluten-free eaters alike.

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons poppy seeds
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Wet Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, almond flour, granulated sugar, poppy seeds, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the almond milk, vegetable oil, eggs, lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl of dry ingredients and gently fold the mixture together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough muffins.

  5. Fill the Muffin Tin: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full.

  6. Bake: Bake the muffins for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

  7. Cool and Serve: Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. These muffins are best enjoyed on the day they are made, but they can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra burst of lemon flavor, you can add a simple lemon glaze to the muffins. Combine 1 cup of powdered sugar with 2-3 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice, and drizzle it over the cooled muffins.
  • If you don’t have almond flour on hand, you can substitute it with an equal amount of gluten-free all-purpose flour.
  • To make these muffins dairy-free, you can use a dairy-free milk alternative such as coconut milk or soy milk.
  • Experiment with different citrus flavors by substituting the lemon zest and juice with orange or lime for a unique twist on this classic recipe.

These Gluten-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins are a delightful way to brighten up your day with their zesty flavor and tender texture. Whether you’re gluten-free or simply love a good muffin, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in these irresistible treats!
