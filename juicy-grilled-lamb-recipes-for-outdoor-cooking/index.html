Juicy Grilled Lamb Recipes for Outdoor Cooking


Are you looking to elevate your outdoor cooking game? Look no further than juicy grilled lamb recipes! Lamb is a versatile and flavorful meat that pairs perfectly with the smoky char of the grill. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply enjoying a sunny day, these delicious lamb recipes will impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. So grab your apron, fire up the grill, and let’s get cooking!

1. Greek-inspired Grilled Lamb Chops

Ingredients:

  • 8 lamb chops
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the lamb chops in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and massage the marinade into the meat.
  4. Let the lamb chops marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  5. Remove the lamb chops from the marinade and discard the excess liquid.
  6. Grill the lamb chops for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  7. Remove the lamb chops from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a tender and juicy bite.

2. Moroccan-spiced Grilled Lamb Kebabs

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless lamb, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika, ground cinnamon, ground turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the lamb cubes to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the spice mixture. Let the lamb marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated lamb cubes onto the soaked wooden skewers, leaving a small space between each piece.
  5. Grill the lamb kebabs for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are browned and cooked to your liking.
  6. Remove the lamb kebabs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and the meat to become tender and juicy.

3. Herb-crusted Grilled Lamb Rack

Ingredients:

  • 1 lamb rack, frenched
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Season the lamb rack generously with salt and pepper.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the Dijon mustard, fresh rosemary, fresh thyme, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the herb mixture evenly over the surface of the lamb rack, pressing it gently to adhere.
  5. Place the lamb rack on the grill, bone-side down, and cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 130°F (medium-rare).
  6. Remove the lamb rack from the grill and let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to settle and ensures a tender and flavorful result.
  7. Slice the lamb rack into individual chops and serve with your favorite side dishes or a fresh salad.

Conclusion

Grilled lamb is a delicious addition to any outdoor cooking repertoire. From Greek-inspired lamb chops to Moroccan-spiced kebabs and herb-crusted lamb racks, these recipes offer a variety of flavors and cooking techniques to suit your palate. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the juicy and succulent taste of grilled lamb. Happy cooking!
