Homemade Mango Chutney: A Sweet and Spicy Accompaniment


If there’s one condiment that can instantly elevate the flavors of your meal, it’s mango chutney. This sweet and spicy concoction is not just a delightful accompaniment but also a burst of sunshine on your plate. Whether you pair it with savory dishes, use it as a dip, or spread it on sandwiches, homemade mango chutney is a versatile addition to any kitchen. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the step-by-step process of making your own batch of this delectable mango chutney.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mangoes: Start by peeling the mangoes and dicing the flesh. Set aside in a bowl.

  2. Cook the Onions: In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, sauté the finely chopped red onion until it turns translucent.

  3. Add the Spices: Once the onions are soft, add the ground ginger, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and red pepper flakes to the pan. Stir the spices for a minute or until they become fragrant.

  4. Combine the Mangoes: Add the diced mangoes to the pan and stir well to combine with the onions and spices.

  5. Sweeten and Vinegar: Pour in the apple cider vinegar and sprinkle the brown sugar over the mango mixture. Stir until the sugar dissolves.

  6. Simmer: Drop in a cinnamon stick and let the chutney simmer over low heat for about 45 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  7. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the chutney and add salt as needed. You can also adjust the sweetness or spiciness at this stage to suit your preference.

  8. Cool and Store: Once the chutney has thickened to your liking, remove it from the heat and let it cool. Transfer the chutney to sterilized jars and store them in the refrigerator for up to a month.

Serving Suggestions:

Homemade mango chutney pairs wonderfully with a variety of dishes. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Curries: Add a dollop of mango chutney to your favorite curry for a burst of fruity sweetness.

  • Cheese Platter: Serve mango chutney alongside a selection of cheeses for a delightful flavor contrast.

  • Sandwiches and Wraps: Spread mango chutney on sandwiches or wraps for a tropical twist.

  • Grilled Meats: Use mango chutney as a glaze or marinade for grilled chicken, pork, or fish.

Final Thoughts:

Making your own homemade mango chutney is not only a rewarding culinary experience but also a fantastic way to add a touch of exotic flavor to your meals. The balance of sweet and spicy in this condiment makes it a versatile addition to your pantry. Experiment with different spice levels and enjoy the process of creating your own signature mango chutney. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a flavorful journey with this delightful homemade mango chutney recipe!
