Paleo-Friendly Asian Chicken Recipes: Quick and Flavorful


Asian cuisine is known for its bold flavors and vibrant colors, making it a favorite among food enthusiasts around the world. For those following a paleo diet, finding flavorful and healthy recipes can be a challenge. But fear not! In this blog post, we will explore some quick and flavorful Paleo-friendly Asian chicken recipes that will satisfy your cravings without compromising your dietary goals.

1. Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 tablespoon arrowroot starch (optional, for thickening)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, honey, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add the chicken strips. Cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the bell pepper, broccoli, and snap peas to the skillet. Cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring well to coat. If desired, mix the arrowroot starch with a little water to make a slurry and add it to the skillet to thicken the sauce.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot over cauliflower rice or zucchini noodles.

2. Thai Basil Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and chili pepper, and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the skillet and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the bell pepper and mushrooms to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the fish sauce, coconut aminos, and honey. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring well to coat.
  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the fresh basil leaves. Let the heat of the dish wilt the basil slightly.
  6. Serve the Thai basil chicken hot with lime wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

3. Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • Green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the coconut aminos, sesame oil, honey, garlic, ginger, and sesame seeds. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are evenly coated. Let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers.
  4. Grill the chicken skewers for 5-7 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Remove the skewers from the grill and garnish with sliced green onions.
  6. Serve the Korean BBQ chicken skewers hot as an appetizer or as part of a main course with a side of cauliflower rice.

These Paleo-friendly Asian chicken recipes are perfect for those looking to add some international flair to their paleo diet. Whether you’re a fan of the sweet and savory teriyaki flavors, the aromatic Thai basil, or the bold and smoky Korean BBQ, these recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds while keeping you on track with your dietary goals. So go ahead, unleash your inner chef, and enjoy these quick and flavorful dishes today!

Note: Remember to always check the ingredient labels to ensure that the products you use are indeed paleo-friendly.
