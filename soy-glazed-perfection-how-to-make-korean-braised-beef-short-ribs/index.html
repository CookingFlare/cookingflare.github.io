Soy-Glazed Perfection: How to Make Korean Braised Beef Short Ribs


If you’re a fan of hearty, flavorful dishes, then Korean braised beef short ribs should be at the top of your must-try list. This classic Korean dish, also known as Galbi Jjim, is a true masterpiece of flavors and textures. The tender, succulent beef ribs are braised in a rich and savory soy glaze, resulting in a dish that is both comforting and utterly delicious. In this recipe, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to create soy-glazed perfection right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Korean braised beef short ribs, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 pounds of beef short ribs
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of rice wine (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (160°C). Place the beef short ribs in a large bowl and season them generously with salt and pepper. Allow them to sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes while you prepare the glaze.

  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the soy sauce, water, brown sugar, diced onion, minced garlic, sesame oil, and grated ginger. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar. Once it reaches a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Pour the glaze over the beef short ribs, ensuring that they are evenly coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

  4. Once the ribs have finished marinating, transfer them to a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot. Pour any remaining marinade over the ribs, and add the rice wine, if using. Cover the pot with a lid and place it in the preheated oven.

  5. Allow the ribs to braise for about 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and easily falls off the bone. Every hour, check on the ribs and baste them with the glaze to ensure they stay moist and flavorful.

  6. If you prefer a thicker glaze, you can make a slurry by combining 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water. Remove the ribs from the pot and set them aside. Place the pot on the stovetop over medium heat and gradually whisk in the cornstarch slurry. Cook the glaze, stirring constantly, until it thickens to your desired consistency.

  7. Serve the Korean braised beef short ribs hot, garnished with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds. These ribs are traditionally enjoyed with steamed rice and kimchi, but feel free to pair them with your favorite side dishes.

Enjoy the Flavors of Korea

Korean braised beef short ribs are a true delight for the senses. The tender, melt-in-your-mouth meat, combined with the rich and savory soy glaze, creates a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving more. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply looking for a new and exciting dish to try, this recipe is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to experience soy-glazed perfection in every bite.
