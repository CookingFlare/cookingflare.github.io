Pan-Seared Scallops with Lemon and Herbs Recipe


Pan-seared scallops are a delicacy that can elevate any meal to a whole new level. Their tender texture, buttery flavor, and beautiful golden crust make them a favorite among seafood enthusiasts. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to prepare pan-seared scallops with a zesty twist of lemon and a hint of fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of fresh scallops
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by patting the scallops dry with a paper towel. This step is crucial to ensure a nice sear on the outside. Season the scallops with salt and pepper on both sides.

  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add the olive oil. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the scallops in the skillet, making sure not to overcrowd them. Leave enough space between each scallop to allow for even cooking.

  3. Allow the scallops to cook undisturbed for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they develop a golden-brown crust. Do not flip them too early as this may prevent the crust from forming.

  4. While the scallops are cooking, melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for about a minute until fragrant. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the lemon zest and juice. This lemon-infused butter will add a burst of freshness to the scallops.

  5. Once the scallops are done, transfer them to a plate and tent them with foil to keep them warm. Pour the lemon butter sauce over the scallops, ensuring they are coated evenly.

  6. Sprinkle the fresh parsley over the scallops for a pop of color and a touch of herbal aroma. The parsley will complement the lemony flavors and add a subtle earthiness to the dish.

  7. Serve the pan-seared scallops immediately as they are best enjoyed when they are still warm. They can be served as an appetizer, a main course, or even as part of a surf and turf dish. Pair them with a side of roasted vegetables, a light salad, or a bed of creamy risotto for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • To achieve a perfect sear, make sure the scallops are dry before cooking. Excess moisture can prevent them from browning properly.

  • Use a non-stick skillet or a well-seasoned cast-iron pan for best results. This will prevent the scallops from sticking and ensure an even cooking temperature.

  • Experiment with different herbs such as thyme, dill, or chives to add your own personal touch to the dish.

  • For a slightly spicy kick, sprinkle a pinch of red pepper flakes over the scallops before searing them.

  • If you prefer a richer flavor, substitute half of the olive oil with melted clarified butter (ghee) for cooking the scallops.

Enjoy this delightful pan-seared scallop recipe with lemon and herbs. Its simplicity allows the natural flavors of the scallops to shine, while the lemon and herbs add a refreshing and aromatic twist. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a gourmet meal, these scallops are sure to impress. Bon appétit!
