Sous Vide Beef Stir Fry: Tender and Flavorful


Are you tired of tough and chewy stir-fry beef? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll show you how to achieve tender and flavorful beef using the sous vide cooking method. Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, involves cooking food in a precisely controlled water bath for an extended period. This technique ensures that the meat is cooked evenly and retains its natural juices, resulting in melt-in-your-mouth goodness.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C).

  2. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, sugar, garlic, and ginger. Mix well to create a marinade.

  3. Place the beef slices in a ziplock bag or a vacuum-sealed bag. Pour the marinade over the beef, ensuring all slices are coated evenly. Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible.

  4. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath. Cook the beef for 2 hours, allowing the flavors to infuse and the meat to become tender.

  5. While the beef is cooking, prepare the vegetables. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the bell peppers, onion, and broccoli. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Once the beef is done cooking, remove it from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. Heat another tablespoon of vegetable oil in a separate skillet over high heat. Add the beef slices and sear them for 1-2 minutes per side, until they develop a nice crust. Be careful not to overcook the beef at this stage.

  7. Add the seared beef to the skillet with the vegetables. Toss everything together, ensuring the beef and vegetables are well combined. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to heat everything through.

  8. Serve the sous vide beef stir fry over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds or chopped green onions for an extra touch of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier kick, add a teaspoon of chili flakes or a sliced chili pepper to the stir fry.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences. Snow peas, mushrooms, or carrots would all make great additions.
  • If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can achieve similar results by cooking the beef in a low-temperature oven (around 135°F/57°C) for a longer period. Just make sure to monitor the internal temperature of the meat using a meat thermometer.

Now that you’ve mastered the art of sous vide beef stir fry, you can enjoy tender and flavorful beef every time you make this dish. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests, this recipe is sure to impress. So, grab your sous vide machine and get ready to elevate your stir fry game to a whole new level!
