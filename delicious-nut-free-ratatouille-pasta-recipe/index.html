Delicious Nut-Free Ratatouille Pasta Recipe


Are you a fan of the classic French dish, ratatouille, and also love a comforting bowl of pasta? If so, you’re in for a treat! This nut-free ratatouille pasta recipe combines the rich flavors of traditional ratatouille with the heartiness of pasta, creating a delightful fusion dish that will satisfy your cravings. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this recipe is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the details and get cooking!

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants, diced
  • 3 medium zucchinis, diced
  • 2 bell peppers (red and yellow), diced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) of crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 pound of your favorite pasta (penne or fusilli work well)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ratatouille: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the diced eggplants, zucchinis, and bell peppers on a baking sheet. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons of olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast the vegetables in the oven for 25-30 minutes until they are tender and slightly caramelized.

  2. Make the Sauce: While the vegetables are roasting, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Then, add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, and dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Combine the Ratatouille and Sauce: Once the roasted vegetables are done, add them to the skillet with the tomato sauce. Stir gently to combine, allowing the flavors to mix and mingle. Let the ratatouille simmer for an additional 10 minutes, ensuring that the vegetables are well-coated with the sauce.

  4. Cook the Pasta: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the cooking water.

  5. Bring It All Together: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the ratatouille sauce. Toss everything together, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce and help it coat the pasta evenly.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Plate the nut-free ratatouille pasta in individual bowls, garnishing with a sprinkle of fresh herbs like basil or parsley. This dish pairs wonderfully with a glass of red wine and some crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

Conclusion:

There you have it – a delightful nut-free ratatouille pasta that brings together the rustic flavors of ratatouille with the comforting appeal of pasta. This recipe is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a celebration of fresh, wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this dish is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and savor the joy of creating this delectable dish in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
