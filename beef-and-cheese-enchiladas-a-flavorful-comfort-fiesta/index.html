Beef and Cheese Enchiladas: A Flavorful Comfort Fiesta


Are you craving a delicious and satisfying meal that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Mexico? Look no further than these Beef and Cheese Enchiladas! Packed with flavor and oozing with melted cheese, these enchiladas are the perfect dish to satisfy your comfort food cravings. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or enjoying a cozy meal for two, this recipe is guaranteed to please.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sour cream (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat from the skillet.

  3. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.

  4. Stir in the ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the spices to release their flavors.

  5. Pour in the tomato sauce and chicken broth, and stir until well combined. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. While the beef mixture is simmering, warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for a few seconds on each side. This will make them easier to roll without cracking.

  7. Spoon a generous amount of the beef mixture onto each tortilla and sprinkle with shredded cheddar cheese. Roll up the tortillas and place them seam-side down in the greased baking dish.

  8. Once all the enchiladas are assembled in the baking dish, pour any remaining beef mixture over the top. Sprinkle with the remaining shredded cheddar cheese.

  9. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  10. Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with a dollop of sour cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a diced jalapeño to the beef mixture or top the enchiladas with sliced jalapeños before baking.
  • Make it vegetarian by swapping the ground beef for black beans or roasted vegetables.
  • Customize your enchiladas by adding your favorite toppings such as diced tomatoes, sliced avocado, or chopped green onions.
  • Serve with a side of Mexican rice and refried beans for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Beef and Cheese Enchiladas are a crowd-pleasing dish that brings a burst of flavors to your table. The combination of seasoned ground beef, gooey melted cheese, and tangy tomato sauce creates a fiesta of flavors in every bite. Whether you’re craving a comforting meal or hosting a Mexican-themed dinner party, these enchiladas are sure to impress. So put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to embark on a flavorful journey with Beef and Cheese Enchiladas!
