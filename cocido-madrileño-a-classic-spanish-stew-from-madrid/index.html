Cocido Madrileño: A Classic Spanish Stew from Madrid


Cocido Madrileño is a traditional Spanish stew that originates from the vibrant city of Madrid. This hearty and flavorful dish has been enjoyed by locals and visitors alike for generations. The recipe is simple yet rich in taste, making it a staple in Spanish cuisine. In this blog post, we will explore the history, ingredients, and step-by-step instructions to create your own delicious Cocido Madrileño at home.

History

Cocido Madrileño has a long history that reflects the cultural heritage of Madrid. It is believed to have originated in the 16th century during the reign of Philip II. The stew was initially enjoyed by the lower classes as a way to make the most of affordable ingredients. Over time, it gained popularity among all social classes and became a beloved dish across Madrid.

Ingredients

To make Cocido Madrileño, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500 grams of beef (preferably a mix of lean and fatty cuts)
  • 250 grams of pork (such as pork belly or bacon)
  • 250 grams of cured Spanish chorizo
  • 2 fresh Spanish morcilla sausages (blood sausages)
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped into thick slices
  • 1 small cabbage, cut into wedges
  • 1 leek, cleaned and cut into thick slices
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, peeled and chopped
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon of sweet paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add the beef, pork, chorizo, and morcilla sausages. Cover with water and bring to a boil over medium heat. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.

  2. Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the meat simmer for about 1 hour. This will help create a flavorful broth.

  3. After an hour, add the potatoes, carrots, cabbage, leek, garlic, onion, bay leaf, sweet paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.

  4. Continue simmering the stew for another 1 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through. The longer you cook the stew, the more intense the flavors will become.

  5. Remove the meats from the pot and set them aside. Strain the broth and reserve it for later.

  6. Serve the Cocido Madrileño in a large bowl, starting with the broth. Arrange the meats and vegetables on a separate plate.

  7. Traditionally, Cocido Madrileño is served in courses. Begin with the broth as a soup, followed by the meats and vegetables as the main course. You can also serve the stew with a side of crusty bread or rice.

Enjoy the flavors of Madrid with this delightful Cocido Madrileño recipe. It is a perfect dish to warm you up on a cold winter’s day or to share with friends and family. The combination of tender meats, hearty vegetables, and aromatic spices will transport you to the streets of Madrid, where this stew has been enjoyed for centuries.

Note: Remember to adjust the quantities of the ingredients based on the number of servings you desire.
