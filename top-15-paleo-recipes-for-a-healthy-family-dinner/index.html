Top 15 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy Family Dinner


If you’re looking to create delicious and nutritious meals that everyone in the family will love, while sticking to a diet that aligns with the principles of Paleo eating, you’re in the right place! The Paleo diet focuses on whole foods that our ancestors ate, eliminating processed foods, grains, and dairy. Not only is this way of eating good for your health, but it also encourages you to enjoy fresh, flavorful ingredients. In this post, we’ll explore 15 fantastic Paleo dinner recipes that will make your family look forward to meal time.

1. Paleo Chicken Alfredo

This creamy and rich Chicken Alfredo uses cauliflower as the base for a luscious sauce that mimics traditional Alfredo. Instead of pasta, spiralized zucchini or sweet potato noodles are the perfect alternative. With juicy, grilled chicken and a touch of garlic, this dish will leave your family craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, grilled
  • 2 zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, chopped
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower until tender, then blend it with almond milk, garlic, nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  2. Grill the chicken breasts and slice them thinly.
  3. Sauté the spiralized zucchini in a bit of olive oil until tender.
  4. Toss the zucchini noodles with the cauliflower Alfredo sauce and top with the grilled chicken. Serve warm.

2. Paleo Beef Stir-Fry

A quick and easy recipe that’s packed with vegetables and savory flavors. This stir-fry combines tender beef strips with a variety of colorful vegetables, all cooked in a savory sauce. It’s a one-pan wonder that is perfect for busy nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the beef and cook until browned, then remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add sesame oil and sauté the vegetables until tender-crisp.
  4. Return the beef to the pan and stir in coconut aminos, salt, and pepper. Serve immediately.

3. Paleo Baked Salmon with Asparagus

This simple yet flavorful dish features baked salmon, seasoned with herbs and lemon, paired with roasted asparagus for a perfectly balanced meal. It’s high in omega-3 fatty acids and full of vibrant, fresh flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the salmon fillets on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and top with lemon slices and fresh dill.
  3. Arrange the asparagus around the salmon, drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily.

4. Paleo Shepherd’s Pie

A hearty and comforting dish that brings together ground meat, vegetables, and a rich mashed cauliflower topping. This Paleo Shepherd’s Pie is perfect for a family dinner, providing protein, fiber, and plenty of nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 cups cauliflower florets
  • 1/4 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Cook the cauliflower in a pot of boiling water until soft, then mash it with coconut milk and ghee.
  2. In a skillet, cook the ground meat until browned, then add the onion and carrots. Sauté until tender.
  3. Stir in peas and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Spread the meat mixture in a baking dish and top with mashed cauliflower.
  5. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15 minutes, then broil for 2-3 minutes to brown the top.

5. Paleo Chicken Tacos

Skip the tortillas and create Paleo chicken tacos with lettuce wraps instead. This recipe features seasoned chicken, avocado, and a homemade salsa for a fresh and flavorful dinner that everyone will enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts, grilled and sliced
  • 8 large lettuce leaves (such as Romaine)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh salsa
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, then grill until fully cooked.
  2. Slice the chicken and set aside.
  3. Serve the chicken in lettuce leaves, topped with avocado slices and fresh salsa. Enjoy as a fun, healthy dinner option!

6. Paleo Meatballs with Zucchini Noodles

These juicy, flavorful meatballs are made with grass-fed beef or turkey and served on top of spiralized zucchini noodles for a healthy twist on a classic Italian dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 2 zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 cup homemade tomato sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the ground meat, egg, almond flour, parsley, salt, and pepper. Form into meatballs and place on a baking sheet.
  3. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until cooked through.
  4. While the meatballs bake, sauté the zucchini noodles in a pan with olive oil until tender.
  5. Serve the meatballs over the zucchini noodles with a generous spoonful of tomato sauce.

7. Paleo Stuffed Bell Peppers

These colorful stuffed bell peppers are packed with ground meat, vegetables, and spices. They’re easy to make, full of flavor, and provide a nutritious, satisfying meal for the whole family.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté the onion in olive oil until soft, then add the ground meat and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Stuff the bell peppers with the meat mixture and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.

8. Paleo Lemon Garlic Shrimp

This light and flavorful shrimp dish is bursting with lemon and garlic, making it a great option for a quick and healthy family dinner. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables or over cauliflower rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  4. Squeeze the lemon juice over the shrimp and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

9. Paleo Eggplant Parmesan

Eggplant Parmesan is a favorite Italian dish that can easily be made Paleo-friendly. This version uses almond flour to coat the eggplant slices, providing a crispy, satisfying texture without breadcrumbs.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups homemade tomato sauce
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Dip each eggplant slice into beaten egg, then coat with almond flour.
  3. Arrange the slices on a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  4. Top each eggplant slice with tomato sauce and bake for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve.

10. Paleo Cauliflower Fried Rice

A healthier alternative to traditional fried rice, this cauliflower version is full of flavor and packed with vegetables. It’s a great way to sneak in some extra veggies while still enjoying the taste of a classic dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, grated into rice-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup carrots, diced
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 2 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped

Directions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet and sauté the peas, carrots, and cauliflower rice until tender.
  2. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and scramble the eggs in the other side.
  3. Stir everything together and add coconut aminos and green onions. Serve immediately.

11. Paleo Turkey Burgers

These juicy turkey burgers are simple to prepare and perfect for a family meal. Serve them with avocado and a side of roasted sweet potato fries for a well-rounded, healthy dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Avocado slices for topping

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill or a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Mix the ground turkey, almond flour, egg, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Form into patties.
  3. Grill or cook the patties for 4-5 minutes per side, until cooked through.
  4. Serve the turkey burgers with avocado slices on top.

12. Paleo Butternut Squash Soup

A cozy and comforting soup that’s rich and creamy without any dairy. The butternut squash provides natural sweetness, while the ginger adds a touch of warmth. This soup is perfect for chilly nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a pot and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until soft.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer and cook until the squash is tender, about 20 minutes.
  3. Blend the soup with an immersion blender or in a regular blender until smooth. Season with salt and pepper and serve warm.

13. Paleo Chicken and Vegetable Soup

This nutrient-dense chicken soup is filled with colorful vegetables and tender chicken. It’s perfect for a wholesome, nourishing dinner the whole family will love.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup celery, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until soft.
  2. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the shredded chicken and spinach, then cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

14. Paleo Grilled Steak with Chimichurri

Grilled steak paired with a zesty chimichurri sauce is a simple yet flavorful dinner that will impress your family. The fresh herbs and vinegar in the sauce perfectly complement the juicy steak.

Ingredients:

  • 4 steaks of your choice
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Grill the steaks to your desired doneness.
  2. In a bowl, mix together parsley, garlic, red wine vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper to make the chimichurri sauce.
  3. Serve the steak with a generous spoonful of chimichurri on top.

15. Paleo Chicken Parmesan

Paleo Chicken Parmesan is a healthier version of the beloved classic. Using almond flour for breading and topping it with a homemade tomato sauce, this dish is just as satisfying as its traditional counterpart.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 cup homemade tomato sauce
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Coat the chicken breasts in almond flour, then bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  3. Top each chicken breast with tomato sauce and bake for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Garnish with fresh basil and serve.

These 15 Paleo dinner recipes are packed with flavor and nutrients, ensuring that you can provide your family with healthy, satisfying meals that everyone will enjoy. Whether you’re looking for a quick stir-fry, a hearty casserole, or a fresh seafood dish, there’s something on this list for every taste and preference. Happy cooking!
