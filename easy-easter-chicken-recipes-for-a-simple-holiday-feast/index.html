Easy Easter Chicken Recipes for a Simple Holiday Feast


Easter is a time for coming together, celebrating traditions, and sharing delicious food with family and friends. If you’re looking for hassle-free, delicious ideas to make your Easter meal memorable, chicken is a fantastic choice. It’s versatile, widely loved, and pairs beautifully with classic spring flavors. Whether you want to prepare a quick weeknight dinner or a show-stopping centerpiece for your holiday feast, these easy Easter chicken recipes are designed to simplify your cooking while delighting your guests.

Why Chicken for Easter?

Chicken offers a lighter alternative to heavier meats like lamb or beef, often featured during Easter celebrations. It’s also budget-friendly and cooks relatively quickly. The mild flavor of chicken acts as a perfect canvas for vibrant herbs, citrus, and fresh vegetables that symbolize spring. Plus, the recipes below include options for roasting, grilling, or braising, giving you plenty of ways to tailor your meal to your schedule and taste.

1. Herb-Roasted Lemon Chicken

This classic herb-roasted lemon chicken combines aromatic rosemary, thyme, and a zesty lemon punch for a juicy and flavorful dish that will impress at any Easter table.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 pounds)
  • 2 lemons, quartered
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Prepare the chicken: Remove giblets and pat dry. Rub the chicken inside and out with olive oil, then season all over with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff the cavity: Insert lemon quarters, garlic cloves, and herb sprigs.
  4. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine for even cooking.
  5. Place chicken breast-side up in a roasting pan. Pour chicken broth into the pan to keep the meat moist.
  6. Roast for 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  7. Rest for 10 minutes, tented with foil before slicing.

Tip: Serve with roasted spring vegetables like asparagus or baby carrots to round out the plate.

2. Honey Mustard Glazed Chicken Thighs

If you prefer a quicker recipe with big flavor, honey mustard glazed chicken thighs are a delicious option that works great for an Easter lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Mix glaze: In a bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, paprika, and garlic.
  3. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. Place thighs on a baking sheet lined with parchment.
  5. Brush half the glaze over the chicken.
  6. Bake for 30 minutes, then brush with remaining glaze.
  7. Bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until chicken is cooked through and skin is caramelized.
  8. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with a light spring salad.

3. Creamy Spinach and Artichoke Chicken Skillet

For a comforting one-pan meal, this creamy spinach and artichoke chicken skillet captures the essence of a warm, inviting holiday dinner without keeping you tied to the stove all day.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup chopped fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup marinated artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Cook chicken breasts for about 5-6 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  4. Add garlic to the skillet and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  5. Pour in heavy cream and Parmesan, stir to combine.
  6. Add spinach, artichoke hearts, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes if using. Simmer for 3-4 minutes until spinach wilts and sauce thickens.
  7. Return chicken to skillet, spoon sauce over, and heat through for another 2 minutes.
  8. Serve immediately with crusty bread or over creamy mashed potatoes.

4. Citrus and Garlic Grilled Chicken

Perfumed with fresh citrus and garlic, this grilled chicken recipe makes for a bright, healthy Easter option that’s perfect for outdoor gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Juice and zest of 2 oranges
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Whisk together orange juice and zest, lemon juice and zest, garlic, olive oil, honey, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place chicken breasts in a resealable bag or shallow dish and pour marinade over.
  3. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (up to 4 hours) in the refrigerator.
  4. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, until grill marks form and chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F.
  6. Let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.

Pro tip: Pair with a fresh spring vegetable medley or a quinoa salad to complement the citrus flavors.

5. Slow Cooker Easter Chicken with Herbs and Vegetables

For those who want a set-it-and-forget-it meal, this slow cooker herb chicken recipe is a lifesaver. It’s easy to prepare in the morning and serves a crowd with tender meat and flavorful veggies.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Layer potatoes, carrots, and onion in the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. Season chicken thighs with salt, pepper, thyme, and rosemary.
  3. Place chicken on top of vegetables.
  4. Add garlic cloves and pour chicken broth around (not over) the chicken.
  5. Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours until chicken is tender and vegetables are cooked through.
  6. Serve hot, spooning pan juices over the chicken for extra flavor.

Tips for a Successful Easter Chicken Feast

  • Plan side dishes that complement your main: Think fresh greens, spring vegetables like asparagus or peas, and elegant grains such as wild rice or herbed couscous.
  • Use fresh herbs whenever possible: They really elevate the flavors in these chicken dishes and provide that bright, vibrant spring vibe.
  • Don’t skip the resting time: Allowing roasted or grilled chicken to rest before slicing ensures juiciness and better texture.
  • Prep ahead: Many of these dishes can be prepped the day before, making your Easter day less stressful.
  • Balance your flavors: Citrus to brighten, herbs for depth, a little sweetness from honey or roasted vegetables, and a touch of garlic or spice brings the perfect harmony.

Wrapping Up Your Easter Feast with Chicken

Chicken brings versatility and ease to your Easter holiday meal planning. From the rustic herb-roasted whole chicken to the creamy one-pan skillet, these recipes cater to all levels of expertise and timing. Whether you’re cooking for a small family gathering or an extended group of friends, these options let you celebrate the season with food that’s as joyful and fresh as the springtime itself.

Enjoy experimenting with these dishes, adding your own personal twists, and creating a memorable Easter meal centered on simple, satisfying chicken dishes. Here’s to good food, great company, and a holiday filled with warmth and delight!
