Flan de Huevo: A Classic Spanish Caramel Custard


Flan de Huevo, also known as Spanish Caramel Custard, is a classic dessert that has been enjoyed for generations. This creamy and decadent dessert is loved for its smooth texture, rich flavor, and beautiful caramel topping. If you’re looking to impress your guests or simply satisfy your sweet tooth, this Flan de Huevo recipe is a must-try!

Ingredients

For the caramel:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

For the custard:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. To make the caramel, place the granulated sugar and water in a saucepan over medium-high heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely, then let it simmer without stirring for about 5-7 minutes, or until it turns a deep golden color. Remove from heat and carefully pour the caramel into a 9-inch round baking dish, swirling it around to coat the bottom evenly. Set aside to cool and harden.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until well combined. Make sure there are no lumps.

  4. Pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the baking dish. Gently tap the dish on the counter a few times to release any air bubbles.

  5. Place the baking dish in a larger roasting pan or baking dish. Fill the larger dish with enough hot water to reach halfway up the sides of the flan dish. This water bath will help the custard cook evenly and prevent cracking.

  6. Carefully transfer the roasting pan with the flan into the preheated oven. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the center of the flan is set but still slightly jiggly.

  7. Once done, remove the flan from the oven and let it cool completely in the water bath. Once cooled, remove the flan dish from the water bath and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the custard to fully set.

  8. To serve, run a thin knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it. Place a serving plate over the flan dish, then carefully flip it over to release the flan onto the plate. The caramel sauce will flow over the custard, creating a beautiful presentation.

  9. Slice and serve the Flan de Huevo chilled, garnished with a drizzle of the caramel sauce from the dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of flavor, you can infuse the custard mixture with a cinnamon stick or a strip of lemon zest while heating the milk. Just make sure to remove them before pouring the mixture into the baking dish.

  • If you prefer a firmer texture, you can increase the baking time by 10-15 minutes. Keep an eye on the flan and adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • To add a twist to the classic recipe, you can experiment with different flavors such as coffee, chocolate, or even coconut. Simply substitute a portion of the evaporated milk with your desired flavor element.

  • Leftover Flan de Huevo can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Just make sure to cover it tightly with plastic wrap or transfer it to an airtight container.

Now that you have this wonderful Flan de Huevo recipe, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in this classic Spanish dessert. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself, this creamy and caramel-infused custard is sure to impress! Enjoy the velvety texture, the luscious caramel, and the delightful flavors that make Flan de Huevo an irresistible dessert.
