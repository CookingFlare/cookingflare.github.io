Classic and Elegant: The Boulevardier Cocktail Recipe


If you’re a fan of classic cocktails, then the Boulevardier is a must-try. This elegant drink is a perfect blend of sophistication and flavor, making it a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts. Whether you’re hosting a fancy dinner party or simply want to unwind after a long day, the Boulevardier is sure to impress. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the steps of crafting this timeless cocktail. So, let’s raise a glass and dive into the world of the Boulevardier!

Ingredients

To create the perfect Boulevardier, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 ounces of bourbon
  • 1 ounce of sweet vermouth
  • 1 ounce of Campari
  • Orange twist, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Fill a mixing glass or cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add the bourbon, sweet vermouth, and Campari to the mixing glass.
  3. Stir the ingredients gently but thoroughly for about 30 seconds. This will ensure the flavors are properly combined.
  4. Strain the mixture into a chilled cocktail glass. You can also serve it over ice if you prefer.
  5. Garnish the cocktail with an orange twist. This will add a hint of citrus aroma, enhancing the overall experience.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different bourbons: The choice of bourbon can greatly influence the taste of your Boulevardier. Try different brands and find the one that suits your palate best.
  • Adjust the sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter cocktail, you can increase the amount of sweet vermouth. Conversely, if you enjoy a more bitter flavor, add a touch more Campari.
  • Serve it on the rocks: While traditionally served in a chilled cocktail glass, the Boulevardier can also be enjoyed over ice. This adds a refreshing element to the drink, perfect for hot summer days.
  • Try a smoky twist: For a unique variation, substitute the bourbon with a smoky Islay Scotch. This adds a rich and complex flavor profile to the cocktail.
  • Experiment with garnishes: While an orange twist is the classic garnish, feel free to get creative. You can try a lemon twist, a cherry, or even a sprig of fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme.

The History of the Boulevardier

The Boulevardier cocktail traces its roots back to the 1920s, during the era of Prohibition. It was first mentioned in Harry McElhone’s book “Barflies and Cocktails,” published in 1927. The drink gained popularity in Paris, where American expatriates sought refuge from the alcohol restrictions back home. The Boulevardier soon became a symbol of elegance and sophistication, and its timeless allure continues to captivate cocktail enthusiasts to this day.

Conclusion

The Boulevardier cocktail is a true classic, with its rich flavors and elegant presentation. Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or a novice bartender, this recipe is sure to impress your guests and elevate your cocktail game. So, the next time you’re in the mood for a taste of old-world charm, reach for the ingredients and let the Boulevardier whisk you away to a bygone era. Cheers!
