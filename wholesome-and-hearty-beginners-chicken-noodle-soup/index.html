Wholesome and Hearty: Beginner's Chicken Noodle Soup


As the seasons change and the weather cools, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of chicken noodle soup to soothe the soul. This classic recipe is a staple for a reason, and with a few simple ingredients and easy-to-follow steps, you can create a wholesome and hearty meal that’s perfect for a chilly evening in.

Gather Your Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s start by gathering our ingredients. For this recipe, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 8 ounces of egg noodles

Prepare Your Ingredients

Now that we have our ingredients, let’s take a few minutes to prepare them. Start by rinsing the chicken under cold water, then pat it dry with a paper towel to remove any excess moisture. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and set it aside.

Next, chop the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery into small pieces. You can use a food processor or a knife to do this, whichever you prefer. Once you’ve chopped all the vegetables, set them aside as well.

Cook the Chicken

Heat a large pot over medium-high heat, then add in a tablespoon of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add in the chicken and cook until it’s browned on all sides and cooked through. This should take about 5-7 minutes, depending on the size of your chicken pieces.

Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside on a plate. Leave the drippings in the pot, as they’ll add flavor to our soup.

Sauté the Vegetables

Reduce the heat to medium and add in the chopped onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are tender, stirring occasionally. This should take about 5-7 minutes, depending on the size of your vegetable pieces.

Add the Broth and Herbs

Once the vegetables are tender, add in the chicken broth, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine, then bring the mixture to a boil.

Add the Noodles and Chicken

Once the mixture is boiling, add in the egg noodles and cooked chicken. Stir to combine, then reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the noodles are cooked through.

Serve and Enjoy

And that’s it! Your wholesome and hearty chicken noodle soup is now ready to be served. Ladle it into bowls and enjoy with a side of crusty bread or a green salad.

This recipe makes 6-8 servings, so feel free to adjust the ingredient quantities as needed to suit your family’s size and appetite. You can also customize the recipe to your taste by adding in other ingredients, such as diced bell peppers or sliced mushrooms.

Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or a seasoned cook, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your household. With its simple ingredients and easy-to-follow steps, it’s the perfect way to warm up on a chilly evening and nourish your body and soul. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the comforting warmth of a homemade chicken noodle soup.
