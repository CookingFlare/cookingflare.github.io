Thai Street Food Recipes You Can Recreate in Your Kitchen


When it comes to street food, few cuisines can compare to the vibrant and mouthwatering offerings of Thailand. From savory satays to tantalizing noodle dishes, Thai street food is known for its balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy flavors. These dishes capture the essence of Thai culinary traditions and bring an explosion of flavors to your taste buds.

If you’re craving Thai street food but don’t have the opportunity to visit Thailand, don’t worry! With a few essential ingredients and a little creativity, you can easily recreate these iconic street food dishes right in your own kitchen. Let’s explore some of the best Thai street food recipes that you can try at home.

1. Pad Thai (Thai Stir-Fried Noodles)

Pad Thai is arguably one of the most famous Thai street foods. It’s a delicious stir-fried noodle dish that’s full of flavor, texture, and color. The combination of tamarind, fish sauce, and lime juice gives it a perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles (preferably wide)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 100g shrimp (or chicken, tofu for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 egg
  • 100g bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes (optional, adjust to taste)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles: Boil the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set them aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, and chili flakes (if using). Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Stir-fry the ingredients: Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add shrimp (or your choice of protein) and cook until browned.
  4. Add the egg: Push the shrimp to one side of the pan. Crack an egg into the pan and scramble it until cooked.
  5. Combine the noodles: Add the cooked noodles to the pan. Pour in the sauce mixture and stir-fry everything together until evenly coated.
  6. Finish the dish: Add bean sprouts, spring onions, and a handful of crushed peanuts. Stir everything together.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh lime wedges and coriander. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Pad Thai!

2. Som Tum (Green Papaya Salad)

Som Tum is a vibrant and refreshing Thai salad made with shredded green papaya, lime, chili, and fish sauce. This dish is incredibly popular in Thai street food markets, and it’s easy to recreate at home. The combination of crunchy vegetables, spicy chili, and tangy dressing makes it a perfect appetizer or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 1 carrot, peeled and shredded
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1-2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons dried shrimp (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Using a shredder or a julienne peeler, shred the green papaya and carrot into thin strips. Set them aside in a large bowl.
  2. Make the dressing: In a mortar and pestle, grind the garlic, chilies, and peanuts together until it forms a rough paste. Add fish sauce, sugar, lime juice, tamarind paste, and a little water. Stir until the sugar dissolves and everything is well combined.
  3. Mix the salad: Add the shredded papaya, carrot, tomatoes, and dried shrimp (if using) to the dressing. Toss everything together, ensuring the vegetables are well coated.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and extra crushed peanuts on top. Serve immediately and enjoy the freshness of Som Tum!

3. Moo Ping (Grilled Thai Pork Skewers)

Moo Ping is a popular Thai street food snack that consists of marinated pork skewers grilled to perfection. The marinade is a blend of savory, sweet, and aromatic ingredients, making the pork juicy and flavorful. You can serve Moo Ping as a main dish with sticky rice or as an appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork shoulder, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon coriander root, chopped (or regular coriander)
  • 1 tablespoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, brown sugar, garlic, coriander root, white pepper, and sesame oil. Stir well to dissolve the sugar.
  2. Marinate the pork: Add the sliced pork to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for a deeper flavor.
  3. Skewer the pork: Thread the marinated pork onto the skewers, making sure to pack the pieces tightly together.
  4. Grill the skewers: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium heat. Cook the skewers for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the pork is cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Serve: Serve the Moo Ping skewers with sticky rice and a side of sweet chili sauce. Enjoy the smoky, tender pork!

4. Thai Spring Rolls (Por Pia Tod)

Thai spring rolls are another popular street food item, known for their crispy texture and fresh ingredients. These fried rolls are typically stuffed with a combination of vegetables, shrimp, and glass noodles. They are served with a sweet and sour dipping sauce that adds an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 10-12 spring roll wrappers
  • 100g cooked shrimp, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cooked glass noodles
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 1/4 cup mushrooms, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Oil for frying
  • Sweet chili sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a pan, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, and cooked shrimp. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables soften.
  2. Add noodles and seasoning: Add the glass noodles, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Assemble the rolls: Place a spring roll wrapper on a clean surface. Spoon a small amount of the filling onto the wrapper and fold the sides in. Roll it tightly, sealing the edges with a little water.
  4. Fry the rolls: Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the spring rolls in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Serve: Drain the rolls on paper towels and serve with sweet chili sauce for dipping.

5. Thai Iced Tea

No Thai meal is complete without a refreshing glass of Thai iced tea. This sweet, spiced tea is typically made with black tea, condensed milk, and spices like star anise and cardamom. It’s the perfect drink to balance out the heat from spicy dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 tablespoons Thai tea leaves (or black tea leaves)
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/4 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Brew the tea: Bring 1 cup of water to a boil. Add the Thai tea leaves and steep for about 5 minutes.
  2. Sweeten the tea: Stir in the sugar while the tea is still hot, allowing it to dissolve completely.
  3. Prepare the drink: Let the tea cool down, then pour it over a glass filled with ice. Add the sweetened condensed milk and stir until the tea is creamy and sweet.
  4. Serve: Serve chilled, and enjoy the delicious Thai iced tea!

These Thai street food recipes are perfect for recreating the flavors of Thailand in your own kitchen. Whether you’re in the mood for stir-fried noodles, a refreshing salad, or some juicy grilled skewers, you can find a recipe that satisfies your cravings. By using a few key ingredients and following these easy steps, you’ll be able to enjoy the vibrant, bold, and irresistible flavors of Thai street food at home. Happy cooking and eating!
