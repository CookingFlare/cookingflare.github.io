Step-by-Step Guide to Baking Perfect Bagels at Home


Bagels are a universal breakfast and brunch favorite, known for their chewy texture and versatile toppings. While many people assume that homemade bagels are difficult to achieve, the truth is that baking bagels at home is well within your reach—and incredibly rewarding. With a few special techniques and a little patience, you can craft bakery-worthy bagels in your own kitchen. Follow this step-by-step guide to learn how to bake perfect bagels that will have your family and friends clamoring for more.

Gather Your Ingredients

Bagels require simple, staple ingredients that might already be in your kitchen. To get started, gather the following:

  • 4 cups of bread flour (high-protein flour is ideal for chewy bagels)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of granulated sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) of instant yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups of warm water (around 110°F, ensuring it’s not too hot for the yeast)
  • Water for boiling and baking soda (for the pre-bake boil)
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, poppy seeds, cinnamon sugar, everything seasoning, etc.

Step 1: Mix and Knead the Dough

In a large bowl or stand mixer, combine the bread flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. While mixing, gradually add warm water until the dough begins to come together. Once the mixture forms a cohesive dough, it’s time to knead.

Hand-kneading method: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes. The dough should become smooth, supple, and slightly elastic. You’ll know the dough is ready when it springs back gently when poked with a finger.

Using a stand mixer: Attach the dough hook, and mix on medium speed for about 7 minutes until the dough is soft and elastic.

Step 2: Allow the Dough to Rise

Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl loosely with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Allow it to rise in a warm, draft-free environment until the dough has doubled in size. This usually takes about 1 to 1.5 hours.

Pro Tip: To create a warm environment, place the covered bowl in an oven that has been preheated to 90°F and then turned off.

Step 3: Divide and Shape

After the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release the air. Transfer it to a clean surface and divide the dough into 8 equal portions. For smaller bagels, divide into 10 portions.

To shape each bagel:

  1. Roll a portion of dough into a ball using your palms.
  2. Flatten the ball slightly and use your finger or the handle of a wooden spoon to poke a hole in the center.
  3. Stretch the hole gently to ensure it is about 1-2 inches wide. The shape may look exaggerated, but the hole will shrink as the bagels rise and bake.

Place the formed bagels on a parchment-lined baking sheet, giving them enough space to expand.

Step 4: Second Rise

Let the shaped bagels rest on the baking sheet, covered with a clean kitchen towel, for about 20-30 minutes. This second rise is shorter than the first and allows the bagels to puff up slightly. While the bagels are rising, you can start preparing for the boiling and baking steps.

Step 5: Pre-Boil the Bagels

The secret to achieving the chewy texture and crispy crust of bagels lies in boiling them before baking.

Set up the boil: Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a gentle boil. Add 1-2 tablespoons of baking soda to the boiling water; this alkalizes the water slightly, giving the bagels their signature texture and slight shine.

Boil in batches: Gently place bagels into the boiling water, working in batches to avoid overcrowding. Boil each bagel for 45-60 seconds per side. Remove using a slotted spoon and place them back onto the parchment-lined baking sheet.

Pro Tip: The longer you boil the bagels, the chewier the texture will be. If you prefer lighter bagels, boil for a shorter duration.

Step 6: Add Toppings (Optional)

Once the boiled bagels are back on the baking sheet, it’s time to add your favorite toppings. Brush each bagel lightly with an egg wash (one beaten egg with a splash of water) to help the toppings adhere. Sprinkle with sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything seasoning, freshly shredded cheese, or a cinnamon-sugar blend for sweeter bagels.

Step 7: Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 425°F. Bake the bagels for about 20-25 minutes or until golden brown. If you want extra browning, rotate the baking sheet halfway through the baking time.

Once baked, let the bagels cool on a wire rack to ensure they don’t become soggy on the bottom. Resist the temptation to eat them immediately—though warm bagels are heavenly—allowing them to cool slightly improves their structure and texture.

Bagel Varieties to Try

While plain bagels are classic and allow for endless customization, don’t shy away from experimenting. Here are a few fun twists you can try:

  • Whole Wheat Bagels: Substitute half of the bread flour for whole wheat flour to add more nutritional value.
  • Cheddar Jalapeño Bagels: Sprinkle shredded cheddar and diced jalapeños on top before baking for a spicy, cheesy upgrade.
  • Asiago Cheese Bagels: Add grated Asiago cheese over the top during the final bake for a rich, nutty flavor.
  • Cinnamon Raisin Bagels: Incorporate 1 teaspoon of cinnamon and ½ cup of raisins during step one to infuse the dough with sweetness.

Storing and Serving Suggestions

Storage: Store leftover bagels in an airtight container for up to 3 days. To keep them soft, wrap each bagel individually. For longer storage, freeze bagels in a resealable freezer bag for up to 3 months.

Reheating: Toast frozen or reheated bagels directly in a toaster oven for the best texture. For softer results, microwave for 15-30 seconds.

Serving Ideas: Pair your bagels with traditional spreads like cream cheese, butter, or jam. For savory flavors, try adding avocado, smoked salmon, capers, and red onions. You can even use bagels as a base for sandwiches or mini pizzas!

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Flat Bagels: If your bagels aren’t puffing up, check the yeast’s freshness or confirm water temperatures during step one weren’t too hot to kill the yeast.
  • Too Chewy or Tough: Adjust boiling time, as longer boiling can lead to bagels that are overly chewy.
  • Burnt Tops: If the tops of your bagels are browning too quickly, consider lowering the oven temperature slightly or tenting with aluminum foil during the final minutes of baking.

Conclusion

Making perfect bagels at home might seem daunting at first, but following this step-by-step guide simplifies the process. Though there is a bit of waiting involved—from rising dough to boiling and baking—the payoff is absolutely worth it. Homemade bagels allow you to experiment with flavors, toppings, and textures to suit your preferences, and nothing beats the satisfaction of enjoying fresh bagels straight from your oven. Try out this recipe today and elevate your breakfast or brunch spread to a new level!
