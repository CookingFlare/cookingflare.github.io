Authentic Mexican Chilaquiles: A Breakfast Classic


When it comes to Mexican breakfast classics, chilaquiles holds a special place in the hearts and stomachs of many. This traditional dish is a true celebration of flavors and textures, featuring crispy tortillas, tangy salsa, creamy toppings, and a burst of fresh herbs. Whether you’re looking to start your day with a hearty and satisfying meal or impress your brunch guests with a taste of authentic Mexican cuisine, chilaquiles is the perfect choice. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create this beloved dish from scratch, allowing you to experience the magic of chilaquiles in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

For the Chilaquiles:

  • 10 corn tortillas, preferably stale
  • 2 cups of your favorite salsa (red or green)
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • Salt, to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup of crumbled queso fresco
  • 1/2 cup of Mexican crema or sour cream
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Sliced avocado, for serving
  • Optional: Cooked shredded chicken or fried eggs

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tortillas:

    • Cut the tortillas into wedges or strips. If they are not already stale, you can leave them out on a baking sheet for a few hours to dry out.

  2. Fry the Tortillas:

    • In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the tortilla pieces in batches, frying them until golden and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the fried tortillas to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  3. Assemble the Chilaquiles:

    • In the same skillet, remove any excess oil, leaving about 1 tablespoon behind. Over medium heat, add the salsa and let it simmer for a few minutes. Then, stir in the chicken broth and let the mixture come to a gentle boil. Add the fried tortillas to the skillet, tossing them gently to coat them with the salsa.

  4. Serve and Garnish:

    • Once the tortillas are coated and slightly softened, transfer the chilaquiles to serving plates. Top them with crumbled queso fresco, a drizzle of Mexican crema or sour cream, chopped red onion, and fresh cilantro leaves. Serve with sliced avocado and any additional toppings of your choice, such as cooked shredded chicken or fried eggs.

Tips and Variations:

  • Salsa Selection: The type of salsa you use will greatly influence the flavor of your chilaquiles. Whether you opt for a fiery red salsa or a tangy green salsa, choose one that you truly enjoy as it will be a central element of the dish.

  • Protein Options: While chilaquiles are traditionally vegetarian, you can certainly add protein to make them heartier. Consider topping your chilaquiles with tender shreds of cooked chicken, fried eggs, or even sautéed chorizo for a delicious twist.

  • Customize Your Toppings: Feel free to get creative with your toppings. From pickled jalapeños to sliced radishes, the beauty of chilaquiles lies in the opportunity to personalize each plate according to your taste preferences.

Final Thoughts:

Authentic Mexican chilaquiles are a breakfast classic that never fails to bring joy to the table. With a medley of textures and a symphony of flavors, this dish embodies the essence of Mexican cuisine. Whether you savor them as a comforting morning meal or as a delightful brunch centerpiece, chilaquiles are a testament to the art of transforming simple ingredients into a culinary masterpiece. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the sizzle of the skillet, and embark on a flavorful journey to experience the magic of chilaquiles firsthand. Buena provecho!
