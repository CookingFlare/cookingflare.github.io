Pumpkin Spice Treats: 10 Recipes to Celebrate Halloween


The crisp air and the turning leaves signal the arrival of fall, and with it, the irresistible allure of pumpkin spice treats. Halloween offers the perfect excuse to dive into the cozy, comforting world of pumpkin-flavored desserts and snacks that warm the soul and delight the palate. From classic desserts to inventive bites, these 10 pumpkin spice recipes offer a festive way to celebrate the season. Whether you’re hosting a spooky gathering or indulging in a seasonal treat, these recipes deliver delicious bites brimming with the comforting flavors of cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and ginger—all wrapped in velvety pumpkin goodness.

1. Classic Pumpkin Spice Muffins

Start your Halloween festivities on a sweet note with these moist and fluffy pumpkin spice muffins. The pantry staples of cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice are perfectly balanced with pumpkin puree to create a timeless fall treat that’s great for breakfast or an afternoon snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease or line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the pumpkin puree, brown sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, stirring gently until combined.
  5. Spoon the batter evenly into the muffin cups.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool before serving. These muffins stay fresh for a couple of days and even taste better warm.

2. Pumpkin Spice Latte Brownies

Bring the flavors of your favorite seasonal coffee drink to a dessert with pumpkin spice latte brownies. These fudgy brownies are infused with espresso and pumpkin spice, then topped with a cream cheese swirl that adds a luscious tang.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp instant espresso powder
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice

For the cream cheese swirl:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking pan.
  2. Combine melted butter, sugar, cocoa powder, and salt in a large bowl.
  3. Whisk in eggs and vanilla, then dissolve espresso powder in a tablespoon of hot water and stir in.
  4. Add flour and pumpkin pie spice until just combined.
  5. In a small bowl, beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, egg, and vanilla until smooth.
  6. Pour brownie batter into the pan, then dollop the cream cheese mixture on top.
  7. Swirl with a knife to create a marbled effect.
  8. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out mostly clean.
  9. Cool completely before cutting into squares.

3. Pumpkin Spice Roasted Nuts

For a simple yet addictive snack, try these pumpkin spice roasted nuts. Perfect for a party bowl or an on-the-go nibble, they blend the warmth of pumpkin spices with honey and toasted nuttiness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed nuts (pecans, walnuts, almonds)
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Mix honey, melted butter, pumpkin pie spice, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Toss the mixed nuts in the honey-spice mixture until well coated.
  4. Spread on a parchment-lined baking sheet in a single layer.
  5. Roast for 15-20 minutes, stirring halfway, until golden and fragrant.
  6. Let cool completely before serving. Store in an airtight container for up to a week.

4. Pumpkin Spice Cheesecake Bars

Imagine rich, creamy cheesecake infused with pumpkin and a swirl of autumn spices, all resting on a buttery graham cracker crust. These cheesecake bars are an elegant addition to any Halloween table.

Ingredients:

Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 5 tbsp melted butter
  • ¼ cup sugar

Filling:

  • 3 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a 9x9-inch pan with parchment paper.
  2. Combine graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Press evenly into the pan.
  3. Beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth.
  4. Mix in pumpkin puree, eggs, vanilla, and pumpkin pie spice.
  5. Pour the filling over the crust.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes until edges are set and center slightly jiggles.
  7. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate at least 4 hours before slicing.

5. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes with Maple Syrup

A weekend treat or a Halloween morning breakfast, these pumpkin spice pancakes are fluffy with a spicy pumpkin kick, perfect with a drizzle of maple syrup and a pat of butter.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tbsp melted butter
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, pumpkin spice, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, combine buttermilk, pumpkin puree, eggs, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and stir until just combined. Don’t overmix.
  4. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat and grease lightly.
  5. Pour ¼ cup batter for each pancake, cook until bubbles form, then flip and cook until golden.
  6. Serve warm with maple syrup.

6. Pumpkin Spice Bread Pudding

Warm, soft, and flavorful bread pudding gets a fall makeover with pumpkin spice and a rich custard base. This dessert is perfect served with whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups cubed day-old bread (French or brioche works well)
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together milk, pumpkin, brown sugar, eggs, pumpkin pie spice, vanilla, and salt.
  3. Add bread cubes and gently toss to coat evenly.
  4. Let sit for 10 minutes so bread absorbs custard.
  5. Pour mixture into baking dish and bake for 45-50 minutes until golden and set.
  6. Serve warm with your favorite toppings.

7. Pumpkin Spice Truffles

Indulge in bite-sized bursts of pumpkin-spiced decadence with these no-bake truffles. Coated in cocoa or shredded coconut, they’re easy to make and perfect for sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups crushed graham crackers
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • Cocoa powder or shredded coconut for rolling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, beat pumpkin puree, powdered sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla until smooth.
  2. Fold in crushed graham crackers and cream cheese.
  3. Chill the mixture for 1 hour.
  4. Using a spoon, form small balls and roll in cocoa powder or shredded coconut.
  5. Refrigerate truffles for at least 2 hours before serving.

8. Pumpkin Spice Smoothie

For a lighter treat that still celebrates pumpkin spice, try this creamy smoothie packed with flavor and the benefits of pumpkin and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1 cup almond milk (or milk of choice)
  • ½ cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or honey
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • Ice cubes as needed

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Adjust sweetness or thickness with extra syrup or ice.
  4. Pour into glasses and enjoy immediately.

9. Pumpkin Spice Sugar Cookies

Perfect for decorating and sharing, these soft sugar cookies marry classic pumpkin pie spices with buttery cookie dough.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tbsp pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 tbsp canned pumpkin puree

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, cream of tartar, pumpkin spice, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in egg, vanilla, and pumpkin puree.
  5. Gradually mix in dry ingredients.
  6. Roll dough into 1-inch balls and place on baking sheets.
  7. Flatten slightly and bake for 10-12 minutes or until edges are lightly golden.
  8. Cool on wire racks.

10. Pumpkin Spice Hot Chocolate

End your Halloween evening with a cup of rich pumpkin spice hot chocolate. Creamy and warming, it’s spiced with pumpkin and topped with whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 4 oz dark or semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • ¼ cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream and cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat milk in a saucepan over medium heat until steaming.
  2. Stir in chopped chocolate until melted and smooth.
  3. Whisk in pumpkin puree, sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla.
  4. Simmer gently for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Pour into mugs and top with whipped cream and a dusting of cinnamon.

Bringing Pumpkin Spice into Your Halloween Celebrations

These 10 recipes capture the essence of fall with the unmistakable aroma and taste of pumpkin spice. From sweet baked goods to cozy beverages, they offer a variety of ways to indulge your seasonal cravings and impress spooky guests. Whether you favor traditional classics or crave something with a twist, pumpkin’s versatility shines in every form—welcoming you into the warmth of autumn one bite at a time.

Happy Halloween and happy baking!
