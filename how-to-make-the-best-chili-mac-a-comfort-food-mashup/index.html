How to Make the Best Chili Mac: A Comfort Food Mashup


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the savory warmth and heartiness of chili mac. This classic dish combines two beloved comfort food favorites – chili and macaroni and cheese – into one dish that is sure to satisfy your cravings. Whether you’re looking to feed a crowd or simply enjoy a cozy meal on a cool evening, this easy-to-make, flavorful recipe will quickly become a household staple.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make the best chili mac that is both delicious and versatile, with plenty of tips and tricks for customizing it to your liking. From the base of the chili to the creamy, cheesy macaroni, we’ll break down each step to ensure your chili mac turns out perfect every time.

What is Chili Mac?

Chili mac is a dish that combines the bold, spicy flavors of chili with the creamy richness of mac and cheese. The idea behind chili mac is simple: combine a hearty chili (typically made with ground beef, beans, tomatoes, and spices) with macaroni pasta and cheese sauce, creating a flavorful mashup of two all-time comfort food favorites.

The beauty of chili mac lies in its flexibility. You can easily customize it based on your taste preferences. Whether you like your chili spicy or mild, whether you prefer a tomato-based chili or one with a richer, meaty flavor, chili mac can accommodate a variety of flavor profiles. Plus, the addition of macaroni and cheese takes this dish to a whole new level of comfort.

Ingredients for the Best Chili Mac

To make the best chili mac, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chili:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or turkey, if you prefer leaner meat)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes (with their juices)
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth (or chicken broth for a lighter option)
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

For the Mac and Cheese:

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni (or any pasta of your choice)
  • 3 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk is preferred for creaminess)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional, for a touch of tang)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional Toppings:

  • Sour cream
  • Chopped green onions
  • Shredded cheese
  • Jalapeño slices
  • Crumbled tortilla chips

How to Make the Best Chili Mac

Making chili mac is relatively simple, but achieving the perfect balance of flavors requires a few key steps. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Prepare the Chili

The first step in making the best chili mac is preparing the chili itself. This part of the recipe is all about developing deep, rich flavors, so take your time to cook the ingredients properly.

  1. Cook the Ground Meat: Heat a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef (or turkey) and break it up with a wooden spoon. Cook until the meat is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. If there’s excess fat, drain it off.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid burning.

  3. Add the Spices: Add the chili powder, cumin, paprika, cayenne (if using), salt, pepper, and oregano. Stir to coat the meat and vegetables evenly with the spices. Cooking the spices with the meat helps to bring out their flavors and enhance the chili’s richness.

  4. Incorporate the Tomatoes and Beans: Stir in the diced tomatoes (with their juices), tomato sauce, tomato paste, and kidney beans. Mix everything together until well combined.

  5. Simmer the Chili: Pour in the beef broth, give everything a good stir, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The chili will thicken and the flavors will meld together. Taste the chili and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can also add a pinch of sugar if the acidity from the tomatoes is too strong for your taste.

Step 2: Make the Mac and Cheese

While the chili is simmering, you can prepare the macaroni and cheese.

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta and set it aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, add the flour and whisk to combine. Continue cooking for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, to make a roux.

  3. Add the Milk: Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens, which should take about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Stir in the Cheese: Reduce the heat to low and add the shredded cheddar cheese, Dijon mustard (if using), garlic powder, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir until the cheese is melted and the sauce is smooth and creamy.

  5. Combine the Pasta and Cheese Sauce: Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir to coat the pasta evenly with the creamy cheese mixture. Set the mac and cheese aside while you finish the chili.

Step 3: Combine the Chili and Mac and Cheese

Now that both the chili and mac and cheese are ready, it’s time to combine them into one delicious, comforting dish.

  1. Combine the Chili and Mac and Cheese: Once the chili has simmered and thickened, stir in the mac and cheese, mixing everything together until the pasta is well-coated in the chili.

  2. Simmer Together: Let the chili mac simmer together for an additional 5-10 minutes on low heat to allow the flavors to blend. Stir occasionally to ensure nothing sticks to the bottom of the pot.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Once your chili mac is hot and ready, it’s time to serve. Ladle it into bowls and top with your choice of optional toppings, such as sour cream, chopped green onions, extra shredded cheese, jalapeños, or crumbled tortilla chips. The toppings add an extra layer of flavor and texture, making each bite even more satisfying.

Tips for Making the Best Chili Mac

  • Use Quality Cheese: The cheese sauce is the backbone of your mac and cheese, so use good-quality cheddar. You can also experiment with different cheeses, such as Monterey Jack, mozzarella, or a blend of cheeses, for a different flavor profile.

  • Make It Spicy: If you love spicy food, feel free to increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add diced jalapeños to the chili. You can also add hot sauce or chili flakes for an extra kick.

  • Add More Veggies: For a heartier, more nutritious version of chili mac, you can add vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, or corn to the chili. These will add color and flavor without compromising the dish’s overall taste.

  • Make It Ahead of Time: Chili mac can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. In fact, the flavors will continue to meld and develop, making it even better the next day.

  • Freeze for Later: If you’re meal prepping or want to save some for later, chili mac can be frozen for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply thaw it in the fridge overnight and reheat on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of milk if the sauce has thickened too much.

Conclusion

Chili mac is the ultimate comfort food mashup, combining the hearty flavors of chili with the creamy richness of mac and cheese. With just a few simple ingredients and steps, you can create a dish that’s sure to please everyone at the table. Whether you’re cooking for your family, hosting a gathering, or simply craving a filling, delicious meal, this recipe will become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Give it a try, and don’t forget to customize it to your preferences – after all, chili mac is all about making it your own. With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to make the best chili mac every time. Enjoy!
