Greek-style Eggplant Dip: A Creamy and Smoky Appetizer

If you’re looking for a delicious and healthy appetizer to impress your guests, Greek-style eggplant dip is the perfect choice. This creamy and smoky dip, also known as Melitzanosalata, is a staple in Greek cuisine and is loved for its rich flavor and velvety texture. It’s a wonderful addition to any meze spread or can be enjoyed as a dip with pita bread, crackers, or fresh vegetables. Ingredients 2 large eggplants 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice 1/4 cup Greek yogurt 1 tablespoon tahini Salt and pepper to taste Chopped fresh parsley for garnish Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Using a fork, pierce the eggplants in several places to prevent them from bursting during roasting. Place the whole eggplants on a baking sheet and roast them in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft. Remove the eggplants from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Once the eggplants are cool enough to handle, carefully peel off the charred skin and discard it. Place the peeled eggplants in a colander to drain any excess liquid for about 15 minutes. In a food processor, combine the roasted eggplant flesh, minced garlic, extra virgin olive oil, lemon juice, Greek yogurt, and tahini. Pulse the mixture until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Season the dip with salt and pepper to taste, and pulse again to incorporate the seasoning. Transfer the dip to a serving bowl, drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil, and garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Serving Suggestions This Greek-style eggplant dip is best served at room temperature. You can enjoy it as a spread on toasted bread or pita, as a dip for fresh vegetables, or as a flavorful accompaniment to grilled meats. It also makes a great addition to a Mediterranean-inspired charcuterie board. ...
