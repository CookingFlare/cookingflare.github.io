Vietnamese Banh Mi Sandwich Recipe with Pickled Carrots


If you’re craving a burst of flavors in a single bite, then look no further than the Vietnamese Banh Mi Sandwich. This delectable creation combines the freshness of vegetables, the tanginess of pickled carrots, and the savory goodness of various fillings, all nestled inside a crusty baguette. Today, I’ll walk you through the steps to create your very own Banh Mi Sandwich, complete with homemade pickled carrots.

Ingredients

For the Pickled Carrots:

  • 2 large carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Sandwich:

  • 1 crusty baguette
  • Mayonnaise
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 cup cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup jalapeno peppers, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1 pound protein of your choice (grilled pork, chicken, tofu, or shrimp)

Instructions

Pickled Carrots:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring until the sugar and salt dissolve completely.

  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool for a few minutes.

  3. Place the julienned carrots in a glass jar or bowl, and pour the vinegar mixture over them.

  4. Cover the jar or bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.

Sandwich:

  1. Preheat your grill or stovetop griddle to medium-high heat. Season your protein of choice with salt and pepper, and cook until fully cooked and slightly charred. Set aside to cool.

  2. Slice the baguette into individual serving sizes, about 6 inches in length.

  3. Cut each piece of bread in half lengthwise, but not all the way through, creating a pocket for the fillings.

  4. Spread a generous amount of mayonnaise on the inside of each bread half.

  5. Layer the fresh herbs, cucumber slices, and pickled carrots on one side of the bread.

  6. If desired, add thinly sliced jalapeno peppers for an extra kick of spice.

  7. Place the cooked protein on the other side of the bread.

  8. Gently press the two halves together, creating a flavorful and colorful sandwich.

  9. Serve the Banh Mi Sandwich immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different proteins such as grilled shrimp, marinated tofu, or even leftover roast beef.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, drizzle some soy sauce or hoisin sauce on the protein before assembling the sandwich.

  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, skip the protein altogether and load up on fresh vegetables for a refreshing and healthy sandwich.

  • Feel free to customize the sandwich with additional toppings such as sliced avocado, pickled daikon radishes, or a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

  • To make the Banh Mi Sandwich ahead of time, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate. This will allow the flavors to meld together, resulting in an even more delicious sandwich.

Now that you have the recipe for a mouthwatering Vietnamese Banh Mi Sandwich with pickled carrots, it’s time to unleash your culinary skills. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself to a flavorful lunch, this sandwich is guaranteed to impress. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the vibrant flavors, and get ready to experience a taste of Vietnam in every bite. Enjoy!
