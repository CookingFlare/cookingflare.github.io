10 Easy Thai Recipes You Can Make at Home


Thai cuisine is known for its bold flavors, fragrant spices, and vibrant colors, making it one of the most popular types of Asian cuisine worldwide. Whether you’re a fan of spicy curries, savory stir-fries, or refreshing salads, Thai food offers something for everyone. The best part? You don’t need to go to a Thai restaurant to enjoy these dishes; many of them are easy to prepare at home with ingredients you can find at your local grocery store or an Asian market.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through 10 simple yet flavorful Thai recipes that you can make in your own kitchen. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a beginner, these dishes are sure to impress your taste buds. So, let’s dive in!

1. Pad Thai - The Classic Thai Stir-Fry Noodles

Pad Thai is one of the most well-known Thai dishes globally, and for good reason. It’s a delightful combination of stir-fried rice noodles, crunchy peanuts, eggs, tofu or shrimp, and bean sprouts, all tossed in a tangy tamarind sauce. This dish offers the perfect balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g tofu or shrimp
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons roasted peanuts, crushed
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a large wok over medium heat. Add the eggs and scramble them until cooked.
  3. Add tofu or shrimp to the wok and stir-fry for about 3 minutes until golden.
  4. Add the garlic, tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and lime juice. Stir to combine.
  5. Toss in the cooked noodles and bean sprouts. Stir-fry everything together for another 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  6. Garnish with crushed peanuts, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy the authentic flavors of Pad Thai.

2. Tom Yum Soup - Thai Hot and Sour Soup

Tom Yum is a traditional Thai soup that’s famous for its aromatic and bold flavors. The combination of lemongrass, lime leaves, and chili creates a refreshing, tangy, and spicy broth, with a variety of vegetables and proteins like shrimp or chicken. This soup is the perfect starter for any Thai meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 4-inch pieces
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium tomato, cut into wedges
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and bird’s eye chilies. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add the shrimp, mushrooms, tomato, and onion. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the shrimp turns pink and the vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce and lime juice. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Remove the soup from heat and discard the lemongrass and lime leaves.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot. This soup is perfect for warming up on a cool evening.

3. Green Curry - A Creamy, Spicy Thai Curry

Green curry is another staple of Thai cuisine, known for its rich, creamy coconut milk base and vibrant green color. The combination of green curry paste, coconut milk, and fresh vegetables makes it a comforting and satisfying dish. You can make it with chicken, beef, pork, or tofu for a delicious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 200g chicken, beef, or tofu, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for about 2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk, chicken (or other protein), eggplant, bell pepper, and bamboo shoots. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce, sugar, and kaffir lime leaves. Adjust the seasoning to taste.
  4. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.
  5. Serve the green curry with steamed rice for a complete meal.

4. Som Tum (Papaya Salad) - A Refreshing Thai Salad

Som Tum is a traditional Thai green papaya salad that is bursting with flavor. It combines the crunchiness of raw papaya with the tanginess of lime, the heat of chilies, and the salty goodness of fish sauce. This salad is often served as a side dish but can also be enjoyed as a light main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies together to release their flavors.
  2. Add the palm sugar, fish sauce, and lime juice, then continue to pound until well combined.
  3. Add the shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, and roasted peanuts. Toss everything together gently to combine.
  4. Transfer to a serving plate and garnish with fresh cilantro.
  5. Serve immediately as a refreshing side dish.

5. Massaman Curry - A Mild, Rich Curry

Massaman curry is a flavorful Thai curry that has a more subtle, rich taste compared to the spicier green curry. With influences from Indian and Persian cuisine, this curry features potatoes, peanuts, and tender meat like beef or chicken in a sweet and aromatic coconut milk-based sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 500g beef or chicken, cut into chunks
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the Massaman curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Stir in the beef (or chicken), potatoes, and onion. Let the mixture cook for 20-30 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the potatoes are soft.
  3. Stir in the roasted peanuts, fish sauce, sugar, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Let it cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and discard the cinnamon stick and star anise.
  5. Serve the Massaman curry with steamed rice and enjoy the comforting flavors.

6. Thai Spring Rolls - Fresh and Crunchy Appetizers

Thai spring rolls are a healthy and flavorful appetizer made with fresh vegetables and sometimes shrimp, all wrapped in rice paper and served with a tangy dipping sauce. These rolls are perfect for a light meal or as an appetizer for a larger Thai feast.

Ingredients:

  • 10 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup lettuce, shredded
  • 1/2 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup cooked shrimp (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Dip the rice paper wrappers into warm water for a few seconds until soft and pliable.
  2. Place the lettuce, carrots, cucumber, mint, cilantro, and shrimp (if using) in the center of the rice paper.
  3. Carefully roll the rice paper up tightly, folding in the sides as you go.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the hoisin sauce, peanut butter, and lime juice to create the dipping sauce.
  5. Serve the fresh spring rolls with the dipping sauce on the side.

7. Thai Basil Chicken - A Stir-Fried Delight

Thai Basil Chicken (Pad Krapow Gai) is a quick and easy stir-fry that is bursting with flavors. The combination of fragrant basil, chili, and garlic makes this dish incredibly aromatic. It’s usually served with rice and topped with a fried egg, creating a deliciously satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, chopped
  • 300g ground chicken
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Cooked jasmine rice for serving
  • 2 eggs for frying (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and chilies and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground chicken and cook for about 5-7 minutes, breaking it apart with a spatula, until fully cooked.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Let the mixture cook for another 2 minutes to combine the flavors.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the fresh basil leaves.
  5. Serve the Thai Basil Chicken with jasmine rice, and top with a fried egg if desired.

8. Thai Red Curry - A Sweet and Spicy Classic

Thai red curry is another popular choice that combines tender meat or tofu with a fragrant, flavorful coconut milk-based sauce. The red curry paste gives the dish its signature color and depth of flavor, while the addition of basil and kaffir lime leaves adds complexity to the sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 300g chicken or tofu, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the red curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the chicken (or tofu), bell peppers, bamboo shoots, and kaffir lime leaves. Simmer for about 15 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce and adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve with steamed rice.

9. Thai Coconut Soup (Tom Kha Gai) - Creamy and Savory

Tom Kha Gai is a rich and aromatic soup made with chicken, coconut milk, and a variety of herbs and spices. The lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves give the soup its signature fragrance, while the coconut milk adds a creamy texture and sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 300g chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, smashed
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 3-4 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth and coconut milk to a boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and bird’s eye chilies.
  2. Let the broth simmer for about 10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  3. Add the chicken slices and cook for about 5-7 minutes until fully cooked.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce and lime juice, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve the soup hot.

10. Thai Fried Rice - A Flavorful Stir-Fry

Thai fried rice is a simple yet delicious dish that can be made with leftover rice. The key to great Thai fried rice is using day-old rice, as it’s drier and fries up better. The dish is typically made with chicken, shrimp, or pork, and flavored with fish sauce and lime juice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups day-old jasmine rice
  • 1/2 cup cooked chicken, shrimp, or pork
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken (or other protein) and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. Push the ingredients to one side and pour the beaten egg into the pan. Scramble until cooked through.
  4. Add the rice, soy sauce, fish sauce, and lime juice. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, breaking up the rice and mixing everything evenly.
  5. Stir in the green onions and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Serve the fried rice with fresh cilantro on top.

These 10 easy Thai recipes are perfect for bringing the vibrant, bold flavors of Thailand into your kitchen. Whether you’re craving a hearty curry, a refreshing salad, or a savory stir-fry, these dishes are sure to satisfy your taste buds. Try them all and discover how easy it is to create delicious Thai meals at home!
