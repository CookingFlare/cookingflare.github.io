Savory Herb-Roasted Prime Rib for a Grand Christmas Feast


There’s no better way to celebrate the holiday season than with a grand feast, and what better centerpiece for your Christmas table than a succulent, perfectly cooked prime rib? This savory herb-roasted prime rib recipe is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 (8-10 pound) prime rib roast
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).

  2. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a fragrant herb paste.

  3. Place the prime rib roast on a rack in a roasting pan, fat side up. Using your hands, rub the herb paste all over the roast, making sure to cover every inch.

  4. Roast the prime rib in the preheated oven for 15 minutes to sear the outside. This will help lock in the juices and ensure a flavorful crust.

  5. After 15 minutes, reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (165°C). Continue roasting for about 2 hours, or until a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the roast registers your desired doneness. For medium-rare, aim for an internal temperature of 135°F (57°C).

  6. Once cooked to your preferred doneness, remove the prime rib from the oven and let it rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a tender, juicy roast.

  7. Carve the prime rib into thick, succulent slices and serve alongside your favorite holiday sides. Whether it’s creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a rich gravy, this herb-roasted prime rib is the star of the show.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a more intense flavor, prepare the herb paste a day in advance and let it marinate in the refrigerator overnight. This will infuse the prime rib with even more delicious herb goodness.

  • Don’t skip the resting period! It may be tempting to dig in right away, but trust us, the wait is worth it. Your prime rib will be juicier and more tender if you allow it to rest before serving.

  • Make sure to let the prime rib come to room temperature before cooking. This will help ensure even cooking and a perfectly juicy roast.

  • If you prefer a more well-done prime rib, simply increase the cooking time. Keep an eye on the internal temperature to achieve your desired level of doneness.

Conclusion

This savory herb-roasted prime rib is the epitome of holiday indulgence. With its tender, juicy meat and flavorful herb crust, it’s the perfect centerpiece for your grand Christmas feast. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a large family celebration, this recipe is sure to impress your loved ones and create lasting memories. So, gather your ingredients, preheat that oven, and let the aromas of the festive season fill your home. Merry Christmas and happy feasting!
