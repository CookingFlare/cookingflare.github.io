Huevos Rotos: Spanish-Style Broken Eggs with Potatoes


If you’re a fan of eggs and potatoes, then you’re in for a treat with this traditional Spanish dish called Huevos Rotos, which translates to “Broken Eggs.” This simple yet delicious recipe combines crispy potatoes, runny eggs, and a medley of flavors that will leave your taste buds begging for more. So, let’s dive right into it!

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cut into thin slices
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the sliced potatoes and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy. This process will take around 10-12 minutes. Make sure to flip the potatoes occasionally to ensure even browning.

  2. While the potatoes are frying, heat another skillet with a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and bell peppers. Sauté until the vegetables become tender and slightly caramelized, which will take about 5-7 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika to enhance the flavors.

  3. Once the potatoes are crispy, transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil. Set them aside for now.

  4. In the same skillet used for frying the potatoes, crack the eggs one by one, making sure to create some distance between them. Lower the heat to medium-low and cook the eggs until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny. This should take approximately 3-4 minutes. If you prefer your eggs well-done, cook them for a bit longer.

  5. Now, it’s time to assemble the dish! Divide the crispy potatoes onto individual plates or a large serving platter. Top the potatoes with the sautéed onion and bell pepper mixture, spreading it evenly. Finally, carefully place the fried eggs on top of the potato mixture.

  6. Garnish the dish with freshly chopped parsley, if desired. The vibrant green color of the parsley will add a pop of freshness to the dish.

  7. Serve the Huevos Rotos immediately, while the eggs are still warm and the potatoes are crispy. Be sure to break the eggs and mix everything together before indulging in the explosion of flavors. The combination of the creamy yolks, the crispy potatoes, and the savory sautéed vegetables will make your taste buds dance with joy.

Final Thoughts

Huevos Rotos is a delightful dish that showcases the simplicity and elegance of Spanish cuisine. It’s a versatile recipe that can be enjoyed as a hearty breakfast, a satisfying brunch, or even a light dinner. The crunchy potatoes, the gooey eggs, and the flavorful vegetables come together harmoniously, creating a symphony of flavors that will transport you to the sunny streets of Spain.

So, the next time you’re looking to impress your family or friends with a unique and delicious meal, give Huevos Rotos a try. With its simple preparation and incredible taste, this Spanish-style broken eggs with potatoes will surely become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. Buen provecho!
