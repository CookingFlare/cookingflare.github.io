BBQ Pork Ribs: Fall-Off-The-Bone Recipes for Meat Lovers


Barbecue pork ribs are a classic favorite among meat lovers. The juicy, tender meat that falls off the bone is a true delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or simply craving some delicious ribs, these fall-off-the-bone recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings. With a few simple ingredients and some patience, you can create mouthwatering BBQ pork ribs that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Classic BBQ Pork Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper to create a dry rub.
  3. Pat the ribs dry with a paper towel and then generously sprinkle the dry rub all over the ribs, making sure to coat both sides.
  4. Place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. During the last 30 minutes of cooking, baste the ribs with your favorite BBQ sauce, flipping them occasionally to ensure even coating.
  6. Once the ribs are done, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Smoky Honey Glazed BBQ Pork Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the BBQ sauce, honey, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Heat the mixture over low heat until it comes to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pat the ribs dry with a paper towel and then place them on the grill. Cook for about 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  4. During the last 20 minutes of cooking, brush the ribs with the smoky honey glaze, flipping them occasionally to ensure even coating.
  5. Once the ribs are done, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Spicy Korean BBQ Pork Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 1 cup of Korean BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the Korean BBQ sauce, gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Mix well to create a marinade.
  3. Place the ribs in a large resealable bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and massage the marinade into the ribs, ensuring they are evenly coated. Place the bag in the refrigerator and let the ribs marinate for at least 4 hours, or overnight for better flavor.
  4. Remove the ribs from the marinade and discard the excess marinade. Place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. During the last 30 minutes of cooking, brush the ribs with some additional Korean BBQ sauce, flipping them occasionally to ensure even coating.
  6. Once the ribs are done, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

These fall-off-the-bone BBQ pork rib recipes are perfect for any meat lover. Whether you prefer a classic, smoky honey glazed, or spicy Korean twist, these recipes are sure to impress. With a little time and effort, you can create tender, juicy ribs that will have your guests begging for more. So fire up the grill, grab your favorite BBQ sauce, and get ready to indulge in some mouthwatering BBQ pork ribs. Enjoy!
