Creating Your Own Buta no Kakuni: Japanese Braised Pork Belly


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of Buta no Kakuni, a delicious and comforting dish made from braised pork belly. This melt-in-your-mouth delicacy is packed with rich flavors and a tender texture that will leave you craving for more. The great news is that you can easily recreate this mouthwatering dish in your own kitchen. In this recipe, we will guide you step by step on how to create your very own Buta no Kakuni.

Ingredients

To prepare Buta no Kakuni, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of pork belly, preferably with the skin on
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of sake
  • 1 cup of mirin
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 inches of ginger, sliced
  • 2 green onions, cut into large pieces
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions

Now that you have gathered all the ingredients, let’s start cooking!

  1. Begin by preparing the pork belly. Cut it into large chunks, approximately 2 inches in size. If the pork belly has skin, you can leave it on for added flavor and texture.

  2. Heat a large pot over medium-high heat. Place the pork belly pieces into the pot, skin side down. Allow the fat to render and the skin to crisp up slightly. This will take about 5 minutes. Flip the pork belly pieces and cook for an additional 5 minutes.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the soy sauce, sake, mirin, water, sugar, garlic, ginger, green onions, star anise pods, and cinnamon stick. Stir well to ensure all the flavors are evenly combined.

  4. Pour the sauce mixture into the pot with the pork belly. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the pork belly simmer for about 2 to 3 hours, or until it becomes tender and easily pulls apart with a fork.

  5. Once the pork belly is cooked to perfection, remove it from the pot and set it aside.

  6. Increase the heat to medium-high and let the sauce reduce until it thickens slightly, about 10 minutes. This will intensify the flavors and create a rich glaze for the pork belly.

  7. Return the pork belly to the pot and gently coat it with the thickened sauce. Allow it to simmer for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  8. Remove the pot from the heat and let the Buta no Kakuni rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to further develop.

  9. To serve, carefully transfer the braised pork belly to a serving dish. You can garnish it with some chopped green onions or sesame seeds for added visual appeal.

  10. Buta no Kakuni is traditionally enjoyed with steamed rice, as the fluffy grains perfectly complement the rich and savory flavors. Serve it alongside some pickled vegetables or a simple side salad to complete the meal.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully created your very own Buta no Kakuni. This Japanese braised pork belly dish is a true delight for the senses, with its tender meat and flavorful sauce. The slow cooking process allows the pork belly to absorb the aromas and flavors, resulting in a dish that is truly satisfying.

Feel free to experiment with different spices and seasonings to make this dish your own. Whether you’re enjoying it on a cozy night in or impressing guests with your culinary skills, Buta no Kakuni is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

So, what are you waiting for? Head to your kitchen and start cooking up this delectable Japanese dish. Your taste buds will thank you!
