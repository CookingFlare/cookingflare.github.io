The Art of Plating with Fresh Herbs: Adding Visual Appeal to Your Dishes


When it comes to creating a visually stunning dish, fresh herbs are an absolute game-changer. Not only do they add vibrant colors to your plate, but they also infuse your dishes with incredible flavors. In this blog post, we will explore the art of plating with fresh herbs and how you can use them to elevate the visual appeal of your culinary creations.

1. The Power of Presentation

They say we eat with our eyes first, and the presentation of a dish plays a crucial role in how we perceive its taste. Fresh herbs offer a simple yet effective way to enhance the visual appeal of your plates. Their vibrant green hues create a striking contrast against the backdrop of your main ingredients, instantly making your dish more visually appealing.

2. Choosing the Right Herbs

When it comes to plating with fresh herbs, it’s important to choose the right ones that complement your dish in terms of both flavor and appearance. Here are a few popular options:

  • Basil: With its bright green color and slightly sweet aroma, basil adds a touch of freshness to any dish. It pairs well with tomatoes, mozzarella, and grilled vegetables.

  • Cilantro: Known for its distinctive flavor, cilantro adds a pop of freshness to Mexican and Asian-inspired dishes. Its delicate leaves can be used as a garnish or incorporated into sauces and dressings.

  • Parsley: This versatile herb not only adds a vibrant green color to your plate, but it also offers a mild, refreshing flavor. It works well with roasted meats, seafood, and pasta dishes.

  • Mint: With its refreshing and slightly sweet taste, mint is a perfect addition to both savory and sweet dishes. It pairs well with lamb, salads, and desserts.

3. Plating Techniques

Now that you have selected your fresh herbs, let’s explore some plating techniques that will take your dishes to the next level:

  • Microgreens: These tiny, delicate greens add a burst of color and texture to your plate. Place a small handful of microgreens on top of your dish or use them to create a beautiful bed for your main ingredients.

  • Herb Sprinkles: Chop up your fresh herbs finely and sprinkle them over your dish just before serving. This technique adds a pop of color and flavor to your plate, instantly making it more visually appealing.

  • Herb Infused Oils: Create your own herb-infused oils by steeping fresh herbs in olive oil. Drizzle these flavorful oils over your dishes to add an extra layer of visual appeal.

  • Garnish with Herb Leaves: Use whole herb leaves as a garnish to add a touch of elegance to your dish. Place a sprig of basil or a few cilantro leaves strategically on top of your plate to create a visually pleasing arrangement.

4. Balancing Flavors and Colors

When plating with fresh herbs, it’s important to consider both the flavors and colors of your dish. Choose herbs that not only enhance the visual appeal of your plate but also complement the flavors of your main ingredients. For example, pairing the bright red of a tomato with the vibrant green of basil creates a visually stunning combination that also offers a delicious flavor pairing.

5. Experiment and Have Fun!

The art of plating with fresh herbs is all about creativity and experimentation. Don’t be afraid to try new combinations and techniques. Play with different colors, textures, and flavors to create visually stunning dishes that not only taste incredible but also captivate the eyes.

In conclusion, fresh herbs are a powerful tool in the art of plating. They have the ability to transform an ordinary dish into a visually stunning masterpiece. By choosing the right herbs, employing various plating techniques, and balancing flavors and colors, you can take your culinary creations to new heights. So, let your imagination run wild and start exploring the endless possibilities that fresh herbs bring to the table. Happy plating!
