Albondigas: Spanish Meatballs to Satisfy Your Cravings


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine and looking for a hearty and flavorful dish to satisfy your cravings, look no further than Albondigas, the traditional Spanish meatballs. These succulent meatballs are made with a blend of ground meat, aromatic herbs, and spices, and are simmered in a rich tomato sauce that infuses them with a burst of flavor. Albondigas are versatile and can be served as an appetizer, a main course, or even as a tapas dish for your next gathering.

Ingredients

To make these mouthwatering Albondigas, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g ground beef
  • 250g ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the tomato sauce:

  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and milk. Let it sit for a few minutes until the breadcrumbs absorb the milk.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the ground beef, ground pork, chopped onion, minced garlic, parsley, cumin, paprika, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the soaked breadcrumbs and egg to the meat mixture and mix well until all the ingredients are combined.

  3. Shape the meat mixture into small meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up.

  4. While the meatballs are chilling, prepare the tomato sauce. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, sugar, dried basil, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well to combine. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. After the meatballs have chilled, heat a separate large skillet over medium heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil and carefully place the meatballs into the skillet. Cook the meatballs in batches, turning them occasionally, until they are browned on all sides and cooked through. This should take about 10 minutes per batch.

  7. Once the meatballs are cooked, transfer them to the skillet with the tomato sauce. Gently stir the meatballs in the sauce to coat them evenly. Cover the skillet and let the meatballs simmer in the sauce for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to mingle.

  8. Serve the Albondigas hot, garnished with fresh parsley. These meatballs are delicious on their own or served with crusty bread, rice, or pasta.

Conclusion

Albondigas, the Spanish meatballs, are a true delight for your taste buds. The combination of tender meat, aromatic herbs, and spices, simmered in a flavorful tomato sauce, creates a dish that is both comforting and full of character. Whether you’re looking for a satisfying meal or a crowd-pleasing appetizer, Albondigas are sure to impress. So why not bring a taste of Spain to your table and enjoy these delicious meatballs today?
