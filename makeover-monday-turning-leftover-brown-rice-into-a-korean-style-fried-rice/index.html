Makeover Monday: Turning Leftover Brown Rice into a Korean-Style Fried Rice


Are you staring at a container of leftover brown rice in your fridge, wondering what to do with it? Well, you’re in luck because today, we’re going to give that brown rice a delicious makeover! We’re going to turn it into a mouthwatering Korean-style fried rice that will have your taste buds dancing with delight.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of leftover brown rice
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 cup of cooked protein (chicken, tofu, or shrimp)
  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: fried egg for topping

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Before you start cooking, make sure all your ingredients are prepped and ready to go. Chop the onion, mince the garlic, and gather all the other components you’ll need for this delicious dish.

  2. Heat the Oil: Place a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat and add the vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for a few minutes until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  3. Add the Vegetables: Next, add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for a few minutes until they are tender-crisp. This is a great way to use up any leftover veggies you have in your fridge!

  4. Incorporate the Protein: Now, it’s time to add the cooked protein of your choice. Whether it’s chicken, tofu, or shrimp, this is where you can get creative and use whatever you have on hand. Stir-fry the protein with the vegetables until everything is heated through.

  5. Introduce the Rice: Once the vegetables and protein are ready, it’s time to add the star of the show – the leftover brown rice. Break up any clumps of rice as you add it to the pan, and stir-fry everything together until the rice is well-coated with the flavors from the vegetables and protein.

  6. Seasoning Time: In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and gochujang. Pour this flavorful combination over the rice and stir everything together until the rice is evenly coated. The aroma at this stage will make your mouth water!

  7. Finish with Green Onions: Add the chopped green onions to the fried rice and give it a final toss. The vibrant green color and fresh flavor of the green onions will elevate the dish to a whole new level.

  8. Taste and Adjust: Before serving, taste the fried rice and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. This is your chance to make sure the flavors are perfectly balanced to your liking.

  9. Serve and Enjoy: Transfer the Korean-style fried rice to a serving dish and, if you’re feeling extra indulgent, top it with a crispy fried egg. The runny yolk adds a luxurious touch to this already satisfying dish.

Conclusion

Voila! You’ve just transformed that leftover brown rice into a delightful Korean-style fried rice that’s bursting with flavor. This makeover not only reduces food waste but also gives you a new and exciting meal to enjoy. So, the next time you have some leftover brown rice, don’t hesitate to give this recipe a try. It’s a fantastic way to breathe new life into an ingredient that might otherwise go to waste. Happy cooking!
