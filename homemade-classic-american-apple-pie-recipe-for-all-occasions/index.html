Homemade Classic American Apple Pie Recipe for All Occasions


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the kitchen. The combination of buttery, flaky crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced apple filling is a classic American dessert that never fails to delight. Whether it’s a family gathering, a holiday celebration, or just a cozy night in, this homemade apple pie recipe is perfect for all occasions. Let’s dive into the details and learn how to create this timeless treat from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 cups Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the bowl.
  3. Use a pastry cutter or two knives to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually drizzle in the ice water, mixing with a fork, until the dough begins to come together.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead it gently until it forms a cohesive ball.
  6. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and cornstarch.
  2. Toss the apple mixture until the apples are evenly coated with the sugar and spice mixture.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Roll out one disk of the chilled pie dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Place the rolled-out dough into the pie dish, and trim any excess dough from the edges.
  4. Pour the prepared apple filling into the pie crust.
  5. Roll out the second disk of pie dough and place it over the apple filling.
  6. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  7. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  8. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet and bake for 45 to 55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. If the edges of the crust begin to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil.
  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before serving.

Serving and Enjoying

This classic American apple pie is best enjoyed warm, either on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The tender, spiced apple filling nestled within a buttery, flaky crust is a true delight for the senses. Whether it’s a special occasion or just a regular day, this homemade apple pie is bound to become a family favorite.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of making a classic American apple pie from scratch is a rewarding experience that yields a delicious, comforting dessert. With its irresistible aroma and heartwarming flavors, this pie is a timeless treat that brings people together. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create a homemade apple pie that will be cherished for years to come.
