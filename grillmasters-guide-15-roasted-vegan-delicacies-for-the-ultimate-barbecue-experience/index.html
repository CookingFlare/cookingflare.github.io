Grillmaster's Guide: 15 Roasted Vegan Delicacies for the Ultimate Barbecue Experience


Summer is the season for backyard barbecues, and while many grilling recipes center around meats, don’t forget about your vegan friends and family. With a bit of creativity, you can elevate your barbecue menu with smoky and savory vegan delicacies. Below we’ve gathered 15 tantalizing vegan recipes to captivate your taste buds and make your grill sizzle.

1. Grilled Portobello Mushroom Steaks

These hefty portobello mushroom caps are seasoned with a blend of garlic, balsamic vinegar, and soy sauce. Once marinated, let them sizzle on the grill until tender and juicy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Clean the mushroom caps with a damp paper towel and remove the stems.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and oil.
  4. Place mushroom caps in a dish and pour the marinade over them, coating evenly.
  5. Cook the mushrooms on a greased grill for 5-7 minutes on each side.

2. Grilled Zucchini and Eggplant Skewers

Filled with hearty vegetables and kissed with the smoky flavors of the grill, these skewers are a delightful party favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchini, cut into chunks
  • 2 eggplants, cut into chunks
  • ½ red onion, cut into chunks
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill on medium heat and soak the skewers in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, combine zucchini, eggplant, and onion. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly.
  3. Skewer the vegetables, alternating between each type, and place them on the grill.
  4. Cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they’re tender and slightly charred.

3. Quinoa Stuffed Peppers with Avocado Crema

Loaded with protein-rich quinoa, fresh corn, and vibrant flavors, these fiery peppers are topped with a cooling avocado crema.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 avocados, pitted and scooped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the bell pepper halves on the grill, cut-side down. Grill for 5 minutes until the skins are charred.
  3. In a bowl, mix the cooked quinoa, corn, tomatoes, and cilantro. Stuff the mixture into the grilled pepper halves.
  4. For the crema, blend the avocado, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a food processor. Stir the mixture well.
  5. Top the stuffed peppers with the avocado crema before serving.

4. Summer Veggie Platter with Herb Dip

A mandatory offer to any barbecue gathering, this colorful veggie platter comes with a zesty herb dip that lingers on everyone’s favorite list.

Ingredients:

  • Assorted vegetables (carrots, zucchini, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, cherry bombs, etc.)
  • 1 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh dill
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • ½ cup non-dairy yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Grill or boil vegetables until tender.
  2. In a food processor, add parsley, dill, garlic, yogurt, tahini, lemon, salt, and pepper. Pulse until well combined.
  3. Arrange the cooked vegetables on a large platter and serve with the herb dip.

These are just a few of the enticing vegan offerings you can transform for your ultimate barbecue experience. With a creative twist, your guests will be raving about these delicious delicacies long after the flames have died down. So, stretch your grilling muscles and experiment with these flavorful vegan delights. Don’t forget, sometimes the best culinary masterpieces can be found in plant-based paradise. Happy grilling!
