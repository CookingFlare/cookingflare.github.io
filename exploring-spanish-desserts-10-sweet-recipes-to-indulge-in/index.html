Exploring Spanish Desserts: 10 Sweet Recipes to Indulge In


Spain’s culinary heritage brims with passionate flavors, and its desserts beautifully showcase centuries of rich tradition intertwined with regional influences. If you’ve ever found yourself captivated by the allure of Spain’s vibrant food culture, diving into their sweet treats offers an irresistible window into the country’s heart and soul.

From the sun-soaked coasts of Andalusia to the lush orchards of Catalonia, Spanish desserts blend simplicity and sophistication in each bite. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a curious beginner, these 10 authentic Spanish dessert recipes invite you to savor an array of textures, ingredients, and stories that have stood the test of time.

1. Churros con Chocolate

A beloved classic found at street corners and cafés across Spain, churros are crispy, fried dough sticks dusted with sugar. When paired with rich, thick hot chocolate for dipping, they become the ultimate comfort snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Oil for frying
  • Sugar and cinnamon for coating

For the Chocolate:

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 100g dark chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch dissolved in 2 tablespoons cold milk

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine water, sugar, salt, and oil. Bring to a boil.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in flour until a dough forms.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. Using a churro press or piping bag with a star tip, pipe strips of dough into hot oil.
  4. Fry until golden and crisp. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Mix sugar and cinnamon, then toss the hot churros in the mixture.
  6. For the chocolate, warm milk in a pan. Add chopped chocolate and sugar; stir until melted.
  7. Stir in the cornstarch mixture and cook until thickened.
  8. Serve warm churros alongside the thick chocolate for dipping.

2. Tarta de Santiago (Almond Cake)

Originating from Galicia, this almond cake is famously marked with the cross of Saint James dusted in powdered sugar. It’s naturally gluten-free and boasts a moist, nutty texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups ground almonds
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Powdered sugar for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a cake pan.
  2. In a bowl, whip eggs and sugar until pale and fluffy.
  3. Fold in ground almonds, lemon zest, and cinnamon carefully.
  4. Pour into prepared pan and bake for 30-35 minutes or until set.
  5. Cool before inverting onto a plate.
  6. Place a stencil of the cross on top and dust with powdered sugar to reveal the iconic design.

3. Flan de Leche (Spanish Custard Flan)

This silky, caramel-topped custard has been a symbol of Spanish sweet indulgence for generations. Its creamy texture and sweet caramel syrup make it a family favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt sugar without stirring until it turns a deep amber caramel.
  2. Pour caramel into a round baking dish, coating the bottom evenly.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Pour over the caramel in the baking dish.
  5. Place the dish in a larger pan filled halfway with hot water (bain-marie).
  6. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 50-60 minutes, or until set.
  7. Cool and refrigerate for several hours before inverting onto a plate to serve.

4. Ensaimada

This spiral-shaped pastry from the Balearic Islands is soft, airy, and often dusted with powdered sugar. Traditionally enjoyed at breakfast or teatime, the ensaimada can be simple or filled with pumpkin jam (cabello de ángel).

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Lard or butter for shaping
  • Powdered sugar for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Combine yeast with warm water. Let it activate.
  2. Mix flour, sugar, eggs, oil, and yeast mixture to form a dough. Knead until smooth and elastic.
  3. Leave the dough to rise until doubled.
  4. Divide dough into portions. Roll each into thin sheets.
  5. Brush with lard or butter, then roll the dough into a spiral.
  6. Place on baking trays; let rise again.
  7. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes until golden.
  8. Cool and dust generously with powdered sugar.

5. Crema Catalana

Often compared to crème brûlée, Crema Catalana offers a unique take with citrus and cinnamon flavors. Its burnt sugar crust crackles delightfully into velvety custard beneath.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 lemon (zest only)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Extra sugar for caramelizing

Instructions:

  1. Heat milk with lemon zest and cinnamon until just below boiling; remove and cool slightly.
  2. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch.
  3. Slowly add the warm milk into the egg mixture while whisking.
  4. Return the mixture to a pan and cook over low heat until thick, stirring constantly.
  5. Pour into shallow ramekins and chill for at least 2 hours.
  6. Before serving, sprinkle sugar on top and caramelize with a kitchen torch or under a broiler.

6. Polvorones

These crumbly, buttery shortbreads are typically enjoyed during Christmas but are delightful anytime. Polvorones melt in your mouth with a hearty hint of roasted almonds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup roasted almonds, finely chopped
  • 1 cup lard or unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Toast the flour in a dry pan over low heat until golden, then let cool.
  2. Cream lard (or butter) with powdered sugar and cinnamon.
  3. Gradually add toasted flour and almonds, mixing until crumbly dough forms.
  4. Shape into small rounds or logs; place on baking sheets.
  5. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 15-20 minutes.
  6. Cool completely and dust with more powdered sugar before serving.

7. Turrón

A staple during Spanish holidays, turrón is a dense nougat made primarily from almonds and honey. There are many varieties, but the classic hard Alicante and soft Jijona are most iconic.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups honey
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3 cups toasted almonds
  • 2 egg whites

Instructions:

  1. Gently warm honey and sugar until dissolved.
  2. Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  3. Slowly fold the hot honey sugar mixture into egg whites.
  4. Stir in toasted almonds.
  5. Pour into a lined mold and press down firmly.
  6. Let cool and harden overnight before slicing.

8. Leche Frita (Fried Milk)

This intriguing dessert features a creamy custard center that is coated in golden breadcrumbs and fried to create a delightful contrast of textures.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Peel of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 eggs
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying
  • Cinnamon sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Heat milk with cinnamon stick and lemon peel, then remove them.
  2. Mix flour, cornstarch, and half the sugar in a bowl.
  3. Gradually whisk in warm milk, then return mixture to heat, stirring until thickened.
  4. Pour into a flat dish and refrigerate until set.
  5. Cut into squares, dip in beaten eggs, then breadcrumbs.
  6. Fry in hot oil until golden.
  7. Drain and dust with cinnamon sugar before serving.

9. Bienmesabe

From the Canary Islands, Bienmesabe (“tastes good to me”) is a luscious almond and honey cream traditionally served with ice cream or fresh fruit.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups ground almonds
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 3 egg yolks
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Make a sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water.
  2. Stir in almonds, cinnamon, and lemon zest.
  3. Simmer until thickened.
  4. Temper egg yolks with some hot syrup, then return to pot and mix.
  5. Cook gently until creamy.
  6. Cool before serving, often accompanied by ice cream or fresh figs.

10. Mantecados

Another festive favorite, mantecados are crumbly, buttery cookies often flavored with lemon or anise. Their simplicity and melt-in-the-mouth quality make them beloved year-round.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup lard, softened
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon or 1 teaspoon anise seeds
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Toast flour lightly in a low oven until golden brown; cool.
  2. Cream lard with powdered sugar and flavoring.
  3. Slowly mix in flour until a dough forms.
  4. Shape into small rounds and place on baking sheets.
  5. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Dust with powdered sugar once cooled.

Final Thoughts: Dive Into Spain’s Sweet Heritage

Exploring Spanish desserts is an invitation to experience diverse regional flavors and textures, from creamy custards to crunchy nougats and buttery cookies. Each recipe tells a story of tradition, celebration, and the joy of sharing sweets with loved ones.

As you prepare these desserts, you’ll find yourself connecting with Spain’s rich culinary mosaic—one spoonful at a time. Whether you start with the warm comfort of churros con chocolate or the delicate elegance of Tarta de Santiago, these desserts promise to captivate your palate and inspire your kitchen adventures.

Ready to embark on this taste journey? Gather your ingredients, invite friends or family around, and indulge in the irresistible sweetness of Spain. ¡Buen provecho!
