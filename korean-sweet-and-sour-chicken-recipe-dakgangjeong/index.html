Korean Sweet and Sour Chicken Recipe (Dakgangjeong)


Korean Sweet and Sour Chicken, also known as Dakgangjeong, is a beloved dish known for its crispy texture, flavorful sauce, and satisfying balance of sweet, savory, and tangy notes. Perfect for gatherings, weeknight dinners, or simply indulging your cravings for something delicious, Dakgangjeong is easy to make and guaranteed to impress your friends and family. This guide will walk you through the entire process, from preparing the chicken to perfecting the glaze, so you can recreate this iconic dish in your own kitchen.

What Makes Dakgangjeong Special?

Dakgangjeong stands apart from other fried chicken recipes thanks to its light yet supremely crispy coating and its delectable sauce that’s often highlighted by soy sauce, garlic, rice syrup, and gochujang – Korea’s beloved chili paste. The sauce clings to the chicken without making it soggy, creating an addictively crunchy bite bursting with flavor. This dish reflects the harmony that Korean cuisine is celebrated for, combining a variety of contrasting tastes and textures into one fabulous creation.

Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned enthusiast, Dakgangjeong is a recipe you’ll love to master. It’s versatile, allowing for adjustments to suit your preferences, and once you try it, you’ll want to make it over and over again.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 2 lbs of boneless, skinless chicken thighs or drumsticks (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch or potato starch (for coating)
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

For the Sauce:

  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice syrup or honey (use rice syrup for an authentic taste)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Garnish:

  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Finely chopped green onions
  • Crushed peanuts (optional, for an added crunch)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preparing the Chicken

Start by prepping the chicken. If you’re using chicken thighs or drumsticks, cut them into bite-size pieces for easy frying and eating. Rinse the chicken pieces under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. To ensure the chicken is flavorful from the inside out, season it generously with salt, black pepper, and grated ginger. Set the chicken aside to marinate lightly while you prepare the other components.

2. Creating the Crispy Coating

To achieve the signature crispness of Dakgangjeong, the chicken needs a light but effective coating. Place the cornstarch or potato starch in a large bowl and toss the chicken pieces until they’re evenly coated. The starch will help create that golden, crunchy crust when fried. Make sure the chicken isn’t overly wet before coating to avoid clumping.

3. Frying the Chicken

Heat cooking oil in a deep skillet, wok, or frying pot to about 350°F (175°C). To check if the oil is ready, drop a tiny pinch of starch into the oil — if it sizzles and floats, you’re good to go.

Lower the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil carefully to avoid splatters. Fry them in small batches to maintain the oil temperature and ensure even cooking. Each batch takes about 7 to 9 minutes. Once the chicken is golden and crispy on all sides, remove it from the oil and place it on a rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess grease.

For extra crispiness, we recommend double-frying the chicken. After all the pieces have been fried once, return them to the oil for a second fry, this time for just 2 to 3 minutes. This step removes excess moisture and solidifies the crunchy exterior.

4. Making the Sweet and Sour Sauce

While the chicken cools slightly, it’s time to prepare the star of the dish: the sweet and sour sauce. In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine soy sauce, rice syrup (or honey), brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, gochujang, minced garlic, sesame oil, and ground black pepper. Stir the mixture well and let it simmer gently. As it heats up, the sauce will begin to thicken and develop a glossy texture. Keep stirring to avoid sticking or burning.

Taste the sauce and adjust it according to your preference. If you like it sweeter, add a bit more brown sugar or rice syrup. For extra tang, drizzle in more vinegar.

5. Coating the Chicken

Once the sauce is ready, gently toss the fried chicken pieces in a large mixing bowl. Pour the warm sauce over the chicken and mix until every piece is coated evenly. The key here is to work quickly while the sauce is still hot, as it clings better to the crispy chicken.

6. Adding the Final Touches

Transfer the saucy chicken to a serving plate or bowl. Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds generously over the top for added nuttiness and a visual pop. Finely chopped green onions can also provide a fresh, slightly sharp contrast to the rich sauce. If you’re a fan of textures, crushed peanuts or cashews make an excellent addition.

Tips for Success

  1. Balance the Sauce: The beauty of Dakgangjeong lies in its balance of flavors. If you’re unsure, start with the base recipe and taste-test as you go. It’s better to under-season initially and adjust gradually than to overdo it.

  2. Double-Frying Is Key: Don’t skip the double-fry method. It makes a world of difference in achieving the crunchy texture that stays crisp even after the sauce is added.

  3. Prep Ahead: To save time, you can prepare the chicken and sauce ingredients ahead of time. The sauce can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.

  4. Customize It: While gochujang lends authenticity, you can use other chili pastes or powders if you prefer a different level of heat. For a milder version, reduce the amount of gochujang.

Why Dakgangjeong Is Perfect for Any Occasion

Dakgangjeong is a crowd-pleaser thanks to its addictive combination of crunch and flavor. It’s equally perfect as a party appetizer, a main dish for dinner, or even a snack for movie nights. Its versatility and ability to pair well with steamed rice, fresh salads, or even cold beer make it a dish that suits any occasion.

Additionally, Dakgangjeong can easily be scaled up for larger gatherings or tailored to individual servings for a cozy dinner for two. Its vibrant flavors appeal to both kids and adults, making it a universal favorite.

Conclusion

Dakgangjeong, Korean Sweet and Sour Chicken, is a dish that brings joy to the table. Its harmonious blend of crispy fried chicken and glossy, flavorful sauce creates a truly unforgettable experience. Once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll find yourself returning to it time and time again.

Whether you’re introducing friends and family to Korean cuisine or simply treating yourself to something special, Dakgangjeong is a recipe that delivers pure satisfaction with every bite. Give it a try, and watch it become a staple in your kitchen!

Now that you have this recipe in hand, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get cooking. You’re just a few steps away from enjoying a plate of irresistible Korean Sweet and Sour Chicken. Happy cooking!
