Perfectly Flaky Mediterranean Salmon en Papillote


Imagine being transported to the sun-kissed Mediterranean, surrounded by crystal-clear waters, and the aromatic scent of exotic spices. It's a feeling that's simply hard to forget, and that's exactly what our Mediterranean Salmon en Papillote recipe offers – a flavor journey that'll leave you speechless. What is Salmon en Papillote? Salmon en papillote, also known as fish cooked in parchment, is a centuries-old French culinary technique that wraps food in a piece of paper, typically parchment or aluminum, to cook and preserve flavors and nutrients. Our recipe takes the traditional method a step further, incorporating the zest and warmth of the Mediterranean with a perfectly cooked salmon fillet. How to Make This Unforgettable Meal Servings: 4

1.5 lb (680 g) salmon fillet, boneless and skinless 6 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tbsp lemon juice 1 tsp dried thyme 1 tsp dried rosemary 2 tbsp olive oil 1 zucchini, diced 1 eggplant, sliced 1 can of artichoke hearts (drained, chopped) Salt, to taste

Preparation (10 minutes)

Prepare a flavorful infusion of garlic, lemon juice, thyme, and rosemary. Line a cutting board with aluminum foil and cover with parchment or foil, or any heat-able material Lightly oil one side of two pieces of the prepared foil – 2 long and 5-inch wide; for the size you want depending on your recipe Set aside an empty foil circle as a parchment bottom.

The Assembly: Creating a Golden Flaky Pan-Fried Package

Rub both sides of the salmon with a pinch of salt and layer with minced garlic on top; a small chunk will do in each layer layer with lemon mixture over the last chunk Place fish side by the oil on that layer Place that in the same size of both long pieces folded, the oiled side. This is folded around the folded edge Arrange in order, an alternate patterned pattern with folded layer and not. The reason being, after sealing, for cooking purposes not for looks a gap in place

Adding Fresh Mediterranean Charm

Scatter diced zucchini, chopped artichokes, and thin slices of eggplant on both sides of the fish, being careful not to tear the papillote when layering toppings Brush the edge of the folds with a piece of water mixed in with just enough olive to spread on hand or with that finger Seal in parchment, a few seconds heat sealed edges is just fine since salmon is hot water fish
Prepare an oven: set at low (325 – 350 depending on oven style).

Finish & Serve Time!

Set timer depending on your baking preference. Since Salmon is such a moist filet it gets to the full point faster of what is often cooked at highest point. Place in, middle of lowest shelf for one layer and you should get desired color. You may let cool on oven shelf few minites - do not slide on other metal. Move off and into another tray once on table side or your food cart

The aromatic combination of papillote fish cooked with warm herbs and earthy vegetables transcends any flavors your taste bud might be tired of. We promise this mediterranean sensation of a delicate yet savory feast won't miss any flavor buds! Some Important Notes

Prepare to serve it within 12-15 min of sealer, don't open if less than ready for eating before hand Since it sits out in short while and once removed it’s at room so always be good time When opening leave one fold aside from all parts – 5 to 8 inches should suffice for spoon. And a final step in making food appealing – Add edible herbs, flower or garnished artichokes.

In Summary

Perfect your salmon en papillote this summer with fresh, Mediterranean touches, like kalamata, or black pitted olives in a sauce over your hot plate of lemon artichokes!
