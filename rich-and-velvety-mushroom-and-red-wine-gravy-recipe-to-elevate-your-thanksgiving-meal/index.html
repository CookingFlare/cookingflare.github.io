Rich and Velvety Mushroom and Red Wine Gravy Recipe to Elevate Your Thanksgiving Meal


As the holiday season approaches, many of us start to think about the perfect dishes to serve at our gatherings. For many families, Thanksgiving is a special time to come together and enjoy a delicious meal with loved ones. While the main course is often the focus, a rich and velvety gravy can elevate the entire meal and add an extra layer of flavor and sophistication.

In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a mouthwatering mushroom and red wine gravy that’s sure to impress your guests. This gravy is perfect for serving over roasted meats, mashed potatoes, or even as a sauce for vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and button), sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine (such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Sauté the Onion and Mushrooms

In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 5 minutes. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes.

Step 2: Add the Garlic and Red Wine

Add the minced garlic to the saucepan and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant. Then, add the red wine and bring the mixture to a simmer. Scrape the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits, and cook until the liquid has reduced by half, about 5 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Beef Broth and Flour

Add the beef broth to the saucepan and bring the mixture to a simmer. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and a splash of the hot gravy to make a slurry. Add the slurry to the saucepan and cook for 2-3 minutes, until the gravy thickens.

Step 4: Season and Finish with Thyme and Cream (Optional)

Season the gravy with salt, pepper, and dried thyme to taste. If desired, stir in the heavy cream to give the gravy a rich and velvety texture.

Step 5: Serve

Serve the mushroom and red wine gravy hot over your favorite dishes, such as roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, or roasted vegetables.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe more substantial, you can add 1-2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour to the saucepan with the butter and cook for 1-2 minutes, until the mixture is lightly browned.
  • If you prefer a lighter gravy, you can reduce the amount of red wine or substitute it with an equal amount of beef broth.
  • To make this recipe ahead of time, you can prepare the mushroom and onion mixture up to a day in advance and refrigerate it until you’re ready to finish the gravy.

With this rich and velvety mushroom and red wine gravy recipe, you’ll be sure to impress your guests and elevate your Thanksgiving meal to the next level. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the oohs and aahs from your family and friends!
