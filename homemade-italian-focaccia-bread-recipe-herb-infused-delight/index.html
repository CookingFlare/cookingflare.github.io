Homemade Italian Focaccia Bread Recipe - Herb-Infused Delight


Are you craving a taste of Italy? Look no further than this homemade Italian focaccia bread recipe. With its light and fluffy texture, herb-infused flavor, and golden crust, this bread is sure to please even the most discerning palates. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is easy to follow and guarantees delicious results every time.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 375ml lukewarm water
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Sea salt flakes

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Mix well to distribute the ingredients evenly.

  2. Gradually add the lukewarm water to the dry ingredients, mixing with a wooden spoon or your hands until a sticky dough forms. If the dough feels too dry, add a little more water, one tablespoon at a time.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process will help develop the gluten and give the bread its characteristic texture.

  4. Place the dough in a clean, lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 to 2 hours, or until it doubles in size. This rising time allows the yeast to ferment and create air pockets, resulting in a light and airy bread.

  5. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). If you have a pizza stone or baking stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well. If not, you can use a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  6. Once the dough has risen, transfer it onto a lightly oiled baking sheet or the preheated pizza stone. Gently press down on the dough to flatten it and create dimples with your fingertips. These dimples will hold the olive oil and herbs, giving the bread its signature taste.

  7. Drizzle a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil over the dough, making sure it fills the dimples and covers the surface. Sprinkle fresh rosemary leaves and sea salt flakes on top, pressing them gently into the dough.

  8. Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until it turns golden brown. The aroma of the herbs and the sight of the bread rising and browning in the oven will make your mouth water with anticipation.

  9. Once baked, remove the focaccia from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing. The bread is best enjoyed warm, but it can also be stored in an airtight container and enjoyed later.

Serving Suggestions

This homemade Italian focaccia bread is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways. Serve it as an appetizer, alongside a selection of olives, cured meats, and cheeses. Use it as a base for sandwiches, bruschetta, or panini. Or simply tear off a piece and dip it in flavored olive oil or balsamic vinegar for a delicious snack. The possibilities are endless!

Final Thoughts

Making your own homemade Italian focaccia bread is a rewarding experience that allows you to savor the flavors of Italy in the comfort of your own home. The combination of the herb-infused dough, the olive oil, and the sea salt creates a taste sensation that will transport you straight to the Mediterranean. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to impress your family and friends with this delightful Italian classic. Buon appetito!
