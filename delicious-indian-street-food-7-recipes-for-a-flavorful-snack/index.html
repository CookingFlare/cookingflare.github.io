Delicious Indian Street Food: 7 Recipes for a Flavorful Snack


Indian street food is a vibrant blend of flavors, colors, and textures, making it one of the most loved cuisines across the globe. From spicy chaat to savory fried snacks, every corner of India offers something unique for the food enthusiast. Street food in India is more than just a quick bite—it’s an experience that captures the essence of Indian culture, history, and diverse culinary traditions. Whether you’re craving something crispy, tangy, or sweet, Indian street food has it all.

In this post, we bring you seven delicious and easy-to-make Indian street food recipes that will elevate your snacking game and provide a true taste of India. These recipes are packed with flavor and require simple ingredients that are easy to find in most grocery stores. So, let’s dive into the world of Indian street food with these mouth-watering recipes.

1. Pani Puri (Gol Gappa)

Pani Puri, also known as Gol Gappa in some parts of India, is one of the most popular street food items in India. It consists of small, round, crispy puris (semolina or flour balls) filled with a tangy, spicy water, mashed potatoes, and chickpeas. The burst of flavors in every bite is what makes it so irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (suji)
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 large boiled potato, mashed
  • 1/4 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind chutney
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

Pani (Spicy Water):

  • 1 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery (or sugar)
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon black salt
  • 2 cups cold water

Instructions:

  1. Making Puri:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt.
    • Slowly add water and knead into a smooth dough. Let it rest for 20 minutes.
    • Roll the dough into small balls and flatten them into thin discs.
    • Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the puris until golden and crispy. Drain excess oil and set aside.

  2. Preparing the Pani:

    • In a bowl, mix tamarind pulp, jaggery, roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, black salt, and cold water. Stir well to combine. Adjust the taste by adding more tamarind or jaggery to balance the flavor. Set aside.

  3. Assembling Pani Puri:

    • Gently make a small hole in the center of each puri.
    • Stuff each puri with mashed potato, boiled chickpeas, tamarind chutney, chaat masala, cumin powder, and fresh coriander leaves.
    • Dip each stuffed puri into the spicy water and enjoy the burst of flavors!

2. Aloo Tikki

Aloo Tikki is a popular North Indian street snack made from spiced mashed potatoes formed into patties and shallow-fried until golden and crispy. This crispy snack is often served with tangy tamarind chutney and cool yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add ginger paste and green chili, and sauté for a minute.
    • Add the boiled mashed potatoes, garam masala, red chili powder, roasted cumin powder, and salt. Mix well until everything is evenly combined. Let the mixture cool.
    • Once cooled, shape the mixture into small round patties.

  2. Coating and Frying:

    • Roll each patty in breadcrumbs, pressing gently to coat evenly.
    • Heat oil in a pan and shallow fry the tikkis until they are golden and crispy on both sides.

  3. Serving:

    • Serve the crispy tikkis with tamarind chutney, yogurt, and fresh coriander leaves. Enjoy the crispy, savory treat!

3. Vada Pav

Vada Pav is a famous Mumbai street food that consists of a spicy mashed potato fritter served in a bun. Often called the Indian burger, it’s a hearty, flavorful snack that is easy to make and perfect for a quick bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 4 pav (soft buns)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Vada:

    • Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
    • Add chopped green chili, turmeric powder, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Sauté for a minute.
    • Add the mashed potatoes and fresh coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another 2 minutes.
    • Once the filling is cool, shape the mixture into small balls.

  2. Making the Batter:

    • In a bowl, mix gram flour, red chili powder, garam masala, and water to form a smooth batter.

  3. Frying the Vada:

    • Heat oil in a pan. Dip each potato ball into the gram flour batter and deep-fry until golden brown.

  4. Assembling the Vada Pav:

    • Cut the pav into two halves and toast them lightly in a pan.
    • Place the hot vada inside the pav, add a dash of green chutney and serve with spicy garlic chutney on the side.

4. Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a flavorful, spiced vegetable mash served with buttered pav (bread rolls). It’s a popular street food in Mumbai and is loved by people across India. The rich, tangy flavor of the bhaji combined with the soft, buttery pav is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, potatoes, cauliflower, capsicum)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 pav (bread rolls)
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Making the Bhaji:

    • Boil the mixed vegetables until soft and mash them coarsely.
    • Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
    • Add chopped tomatoes, pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes soften.
    • Add the mashed vegetables and a bit of water to adjust the consistency. Cook for 5-7 minutes while mashing the vegetables further.

  2. Toasting the Pav:

    • Slice the pav rolls and toast them with butter in a separate pan until golden.

  3. Serving:

    • Serve the bhaji with buttered pav, garnished with fresh coriander leaves and lemon wedges.

5. Bhel Puri

Bhel Puri is a crunchy, tangy, and spicy snack that is typically served in a cone or paper bowl. Made from puffed rice, vegetables, tamarind chutney, and spices, it’s a delightful and refreshing street food, especially during the hot summer months.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups puffed rice (kurmura)
  • 1/2 cup sev (crispy chickpea noodles)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup boiled potatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind chutney
  • 1 tablespoon green chutney
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Bhel Puri:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine puffed rice, sev, onion, tomato, and boiled potatoes.
    • Add tamarind chutney, green chutney, chaat masala, and salt. Toss everything together.

  2. Serving:

    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve immediately for a crispy snack.

6. Samosa

Samosas are deep-fried pastry pockets filled with a spicy, savory mixture of potatoes and peas. Crispy and crunchy, samosas are a favorite snack that can be found at every street corner in India.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 1/2 cup boiled peas
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 10-12 samosa wrappers (or spring roll wrappers)
  • Oil for deep-frying

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Filling:

    • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, salt, and amchur.
    • Add boiled potatoes and peas, and cook for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.

  2. Assembling the Samosa:

    • Take a samosa wrapper and fold it into a cone shape. Stuff the cone with the potato mixture and seal the edges using water.

  3. Frying the Samosas:

    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the samosas until golden brown.

  4. Serving:

    • Serve the hot, crispy samosas with green chutney or tamarind chutney.

7. Dhokla

Dhokla is a savory steamed cake made from fermented rice and chickpea flour. It’s a light, fluffy, and slightly tangy snack that is typically served with green chutney and fried mustard seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-green chili paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon oil

Tempering:

  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 green chili, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Batter:

    • In a bowl, mix chickpea flour, yogurt, turmeric powder, baking soda, ginger-chili paste, salt, and sugar. Add enough water to form a thick batter.

  2. Steaming the Dhokla:

    • Grease a steaming dish and pour the batter into it.
    • Steam the batter for 20-25 minutes until it’s set and cooked through.

  3. Tempering the Dhokla:

    • In a pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and green chili. Fry for a few seconds and pour the tempering over the steamed dhokla.

  4. Serving:

    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with green chutney.

Final Thoughts

Indian street food is an exciting world of flavors, textures, and spices that can turn any snack into a celebration. Whether you’re craving something crunchy, spicy, tangy, or sweet, these recipes provide a glimpse into the diverse and delicious offerings of Indian street food. By preparing these recipes at home, you can enjoy the authentic flavors of India no matter where you are.

Try these recipes for your next snack time or gathering, and let the magic of Indian street food fill your kitchen with irresistible aromas and mouthwatering flavors!
