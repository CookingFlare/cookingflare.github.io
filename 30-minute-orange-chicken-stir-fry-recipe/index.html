30-Minute Orange Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re looking for a quick and delicious meal that’s bursting with flavor, this 30-Minute Orange Chicken Stir-Fry is the perfect dish for you. Packed with vibrant colors and zesty citrus notes, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household. Not only is it a breeze to make, but it’s also a healthier alternative to the classic deep-fried orange chicken. Let’s dive into the details of this mouthwatering stir-fry!

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Additional Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 orange bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots
  • Cooked white or brown rice for serving
  • Optional garnish: sesame seeds, sliced green onions

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    • In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and cornstarch. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat. Let it marinate for 10-15 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.

  2. Prepare the Stir-Fry Sauce:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the orange juice, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and cornstarch until smooth. Set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Chicken:

    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry for 5-6 minutes or until cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  4. Cook the Vegetables:

    • In the same skillet, heat the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced bell peppers, snap peas, and carrots. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. Combine and Simmer:

    • Return the cooked chicken to the skillet with the vegetables. Pour the prepared stir-fry sauce over the chicken and vegetables. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.

  6. Serve:

    • Serve the orange chicken stir-fry over cooked rice. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Prep Ahead: Cut the chicken and vegetables ahead of time to streamline the cooking process.
  • Customize: Feel free to add your favorite vegetables such as broccoli, snow peas, or water chestnuts.
  • Spice It Up: For a kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the stir-fry sauce.

Final Thoughts

This 30-Minute Orange Chicken Stir-Fry is a delightful balance of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors. It’s a versatile recipe that allows you to experiment with different vegetables and customize it to your taste. Whether you’re cooking for a busy weeknight dinner or entertaining guests, this dish is a winner. With minimal prep and cooking time, it’s a great addition to your go-to recipes. Give it a try and savor the vibrant and aromatic essence of this delightful orange chicken stir-fry!
