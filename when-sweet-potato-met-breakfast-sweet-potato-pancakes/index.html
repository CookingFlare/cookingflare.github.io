When Sweet Potato Met Breakfast: Sweet Potato Pancakes


Good morning, fellow food aficionados! Today’s breakfast is anything but ordinary, as we delve into the sweet yet hearty world of sweet potato pancakes. Embrace the unexpected smiles and delight of this unique creation.

The Story of Sweet Potato Pancakes

Sweet potato pancakes are rather a charming culinary surprise. They were first crafted to make use of the versatile sweet potato in a dish that transcends tradition, resulting in a breakfast option that warms your heart and your plate. The rich flavors marry with your favorite pancake ingredients, leading you to question, why haven’t we always had sweet potatoes for breakfast?

Ingredients

  • 1 medium cooked sweet potato, cooled
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Make Ahead: Start by cooking your sweet potato until it reaches a tender consistency. Allow it to cool, and then mash or puree it, removing it from any skin or unwanted fibers. Measure out 1 cup of cooked sweet potato puree and set aside for use in the pancake recipe.

  2. Dry Ingredients: Combine your flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large mixing bowl. Stir the ingredients gently to integrate without developing any significant clumps.

  3. Wet Ingredients: In a separate mixing bowl, whisk the beaten egg with the milk. Add your cooled melted butter and the sweet potato puree. Distinctly mix in the vanilla extract to provide a delectable aroma.

  4. Combine and Cook: Gently incorporate the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, taking care not to overmix. Let the mixture rest for a couple of minutes and then heat your skillet or griddle to medium-low heat. Apply a small amount of butter or oil to prevent sticking.

  5. Cook the Pancakes: Using a measuring cup or a ladle, pour small circles of pancake batter onto your skillet or griddle. Allow them to cook until the edges set and the surface of the pancakes appears dry. Flip the pancakes to cook evenly on both sides, noting that they may take 1-2 minutes longer due to their rich, dense nature.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Sweet potato pancakes can be eaten by themselves, but they also take kindly to toppings like maple syrup, cinnamon butter, whipped cream, or a dollop of your favorite fruit preserves. However you choose to savor these pancakes, take a moment to appreciate the delightful encounter between sweet potatoes and breakfast cuisine.

So, why not start your day on a new and unexpected note? Bring the delicious and wholesome experience of sweet potato pancakes to your morning routine. Whether you’re seeking to expand your culinary skills or introduce a hidden gem breakfast dish to your loved ones, sweet potato pancakes have got you covered.

Good cooking, and may the flavors of the sweet potato bring happiness to your morning!
