Homemade Baguettes: A French Bread Lover's Dream


There’s something undeniably magical about the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the house. And when it comes to French cuisine, few things are as iconic and beloved as the baguette. With its crisp, golden crust and soft, airy interior, the baguette is a staple of French bakeries and a favorite around the world. While picking up a baguette from the local bakery is always a treat, there’s something truly special about pulling a batch of homemade baguettes out of the oven. In this recipe, we’ll take you through the steps to create your own delicious homemade baguettes that will rival those found in a Parisian boulangerie.

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 350ml warm water

Instructions

Step 1: Mixing the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour and salt. In a separate bowl, mix the instant yeast with the warm water until it’s fully dissolved. Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the yeast and water. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a rough dough forms.

Step 2: Kneading the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process helps develop the gluten in the dough, which is essential for the baguette’s characteristic chewy texture.

Step 3: First Rise

Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 4: Shaping the Baguettes

Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Turn it out onto a floured surface and divide it into three equal portions. Shape each portion into a rough rectangle, then fold the long sides toward the center and seal the seam. Roll the dough into a log, then taper the ends to create the classic baguette shape.

Step 5: Second Rise

Place the shaped baguettes onto a lightly floured couche or kitchen towel, leaving enough space between them. Cover them loosely with plastic wrap and let them rise for another 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until they have almost doubled in size.

Step 6: Preparing the Oven

While the baguettes are rising, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Place a shallow pan on the bottom rack of the oven, which will be used to create steam during baking.

Step 7: Scoring the Baguettes

Using a sharp knife or a razor blade, make quick, decisive slashes along the tops of the risen baguettes. This not only creates an aesthetic pattern but also allows the bread to expand and rise evenly in the oven.

Step 8: Baking

Once the oven is preheated, carefully transfer the baguettes onto a baking sheet or a preheated baking stone. Quickly pour a cup of hot water into the shallow pan at the bottom of the oven to create steam, then place the baguettes in the oven.

Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the baguettes are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 9: Cooling

Allow the baguettes to cool on a wire rack for at least 15 minutes before slicing into them. The temptation to tear into them right away will be strong, but allowing them to cool will help the texture develop fully.

Enjoy Your Homemade Baguettes!

There’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of baking your own bread, and with this recipe, you can bring the spirit of a French bakery right into your own kitchen. Whether you enjoy your homemade baguettes with a smear of butter, a slice of cheese, or simply on their own, the experience of savoring a warm slice of bread that you’ve crafted with your own hands is truly unbeatable. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to embark on a delicious journey into the world of homemade baguettes!
