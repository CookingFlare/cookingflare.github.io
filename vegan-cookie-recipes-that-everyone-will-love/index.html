Vegan Cookie Recipes That Everyone Will Love


Baking cookies is a timeless activity that brings joy to kitchens around the world. If you’re looking for treats that are free from animal products yet packed with flavor, vegan cookie recipes are the perfect solution. Whether you’re new to vegan baking or a seasoned pro, these recipes are simple to follow and are guaranteed to impress. From gooey classics to creative twists on favorite flavors, there’s something here for everyone.

Why Vegan Cookies?

Vegan cookies aren’t just for those who avoid animal products—they’re for anyone who loves delicious desserts. Using plant-based ingredients opens the door to a variety of innovative recipes that are often just as indulgent, if not more so, than conventional cookies. Plus, vegan baking often uses fewer processed ingredients and can be healthier overall, making it a choice that benefits both your taste buds and your well-being.

If you’re ready to start baking cookies that everyone will rave about, check out the irresistible recipes below!

Recipe 1: Classic Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup vegan butter or coconut oil (softened)
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar or brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water (flax egg substitute)
  • 1 cup vegan chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the flax egg: In a small bowl, mix ground flaxseed with water. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until thickened.
  2. Whisk dry ingredients: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cream butter and sugars: In a large mixing bowl, beat the vegan butter, coconut sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the vanilla extract and flax egg and mix well.
  4. Combine ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until a dough forms. Fold in the vegan chocolate chips.
  5. Form cookies: Scoop the dough onto a lined baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 12–15 minutes, or until the edges turn golden brown. Let cool and serve.

Recipe 2: Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour or all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup natural peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • ½ cup maple syrup or agave nectar
  • ⅓ cup coconut sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Combine dry ingredients: In a mixing bowl, stir together oats, flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Mix wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat peanut butter, maple syrup, coconut sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Combine wet and dry: Gradually mix the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients to form a thick dough.
  4. Form cookies: Roll small portions into balls and flatten slightly. Place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10–12 minutes, or until lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Recipe 3: Double Chocolate Almond Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ⅓ cup cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup almond butter
  • ½ cup melted coconut oil
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar
  • ¼ cup maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ⅓ cup vegan chocolate chunks or chips
  • ¼ cup chopped almonds

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Prepare wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together almond butter, melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine: Slowly fold the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients. Add the chocolate chunks and chopped almonds, stirring gently to incorporate.
  4. Shape cookies: Use a spoon to scoop dough onto a lined baking sheet. Flatten slightly.
  5. Bake: Bake at 325°F (160°C) for 12–15 minutes. Let cool before serving.

Recipe 4: Coconut Macadamia Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shredded coconut (unsweetened)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup almond flour or oat flour
  • ½ cup vegan butter, softened
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • ½ cup macadamia nuts, chopped
  • 1 tsp almond extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough: In a mixing bowl, cream together the vegan butter and powdered sugar until smooth. Stir in the almond extract.
  2. Add dry ingredients: Gradually mix the all-purpose flour, almond flour, and shredded coconut. Fold in the chopped macadamia nuts.
  3. Form cookies: Roll dough into small balls and press them onto a sheet pan lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10–12 minutes, or until the edges are slightly golden. Let cool before enjoying.

Recipe 5: Chewy Pumpkin Spice Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • ½ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: ⅓ cup vegan white chocolate chips or crushed pecans

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and pumpkin pie spice.
  2. Prepare wet mixture: In another bowl, stir together pumpkin puree, melted coconut oil, brown sugar, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine: Slowly mix the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, stirring until a dough forms. Fold in optional mix-ins like white chocolate chips or pecans.
  4. Bake: Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a lined baking sheet. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 12–15 minutes. Allow cookies to cool before serving.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Cookies

  1. Use high-quality vegan butter: A good vegan butter or coconut oil provides a rich flavor and proper texture.
  2. Don’t skip the flax egg: Flaxseed is a great egg substitute that adds binding power to vegan cookies. Simply mix ground flaxseed with water and let it sit to thicken.
  3. Chill the dough (if needed): Some recipes benefit from chilling the dough for 30 minutes or more to help the flavors meld and ensure a chewier texture.
  4. Experiment with mix-ins: Vegan cookies can include a variety of mix-ins—dried fruit, nuts, seeds, coconut, or vegan candy pieces all work beautifully.
  5. Monitor baking time: Because vegan cookies don’t rely on eggs, they can overbake quickly. Keep an eye on them and remove them from the oven when the edges are just golden.

Bonus: Storing Vegan Cookies

Vegan cookies can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 5 days. If you want to keep them longer, consider freezing them; they will stay fresh for up to 3 months when placed in a sealed bag or container. Simply thaw and enjoy later!

By following these recipes and tips, you’ll create irresistible vegan cookies that will delight family, friends, and anyone who tries them. Whether you stick with classic flavors or experiment with unique combinations, the end result is always the same: delicious cookies that are cruelty-free and packed with flavor.

Happy baking!
