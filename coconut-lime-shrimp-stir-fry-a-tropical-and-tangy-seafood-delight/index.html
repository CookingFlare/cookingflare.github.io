Coconut Lime Shrimp Stir Fry: A Tropical and Tangy Seafood Delight


If you’re craving a taste of the tropics, look no further than this Coconut Lime Shrimp Stir Fry recipe. Bursting with vibrant flavors and succulent seafood, this dish is a delightful fusion of tropical and tangy notes that will transport your taste buds to a sunny paradise. The combination of creamy coconut, zesty lime, and tender shrimp creates a mouthwatering experience that’s perfect for a refreshing summer meal. Let’s dive into the tantalizing world of Coconut Lime Shrimp Stir Fry and discover how to create this culinary masterpiece in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To prepare this tantalizing Coconut Lime Shrimp Stir Fry, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of coconut oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of coconut milk
  • Zest and juice of 2 limes
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Cooked white or brown rice for serving

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

Begin by patting the shrimp dry with paper towels to remove any excess moisture. Season the shrimp with a pinch of salt and pepper, ensuring they are evenly coated with the seasoning.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large skillet or wok, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and the onions turn translucent.

Step 3: Add the Vegetables

Next, add the sliced red and yellow bell peppers, along with the snap peas, to the skillet. Sauté the vegetables for a few minutes until they begin to soften but still maintain a vibrant color and crisp texture.

Step 4: Cook the Shrimp

Push the sautéed vegetables to one side of the skillet, and add the seasoned shrimp to the empty space. Cook the shrimp for 1-2 minutes on each side, until they turn pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp to maintain their tender texture.

Step 5: Create the Sauce

Once the shrimp are cooked, pour in the coconut milk, lime zest, lime juice, soy sauce, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Stir the ingredients together, allowing the sauce to simmer and thicken slightly while infusing the dish with tropical flavors.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Once the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency, remove the skillet from the heat. Serve the Coconut Lime Shrimp Stir Fry over a bed of cooked white or brown rice, and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves for a burst of herbal fragrance.
