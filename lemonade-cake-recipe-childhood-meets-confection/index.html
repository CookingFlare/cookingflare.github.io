Lemonade Cake Recipe: Childhood Meets Confection


Everyone has fond memories of childhood. Sunsets, ice-cream cones, and that indescribable enthusiasm for discovering new flavors and experimenting in the kitchen. One such memory is of homemade lemonade, so tart and refreshing, perfect for a summer day. With that delicious childhood memory in mind, let’s transport our taste buds back to our youth with a lemonade cake that embodies the essence of childhood innocence. Prepare to indulge yourself in the blissful world of confectionery and tantalize your taste buds. Here’s our fresh take on that classic lemonade to dip into your memory box.

Ingredients

 

For the cake:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened non-alcoholic lemon liqueur
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil

For the lemonade glaze:

  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2-3 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Additional preparation:

  • Lemon slices and zest for garnishing

Instructions

Preheat your oven:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan using butter, then dust it with flour, tapping out the excess.

Whisk and combine dry ingredients:

To begin, whisk together the purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl. Using a pastry cutter or a fork, cut the cold butter into the flour mixture until the texture resembles coarse crumbs.

Mix wet ingredients:

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, lemon liqueur, milk, lemon zest (take some for garnish), lemon juice, and vegetable oil.

Combine and bake the batter:

Pour the wet ingredients into the flour mixture. Gently stir the liquid and solid ingredients together until just combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth it out.

Bake the cake for approximately 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Prepare the lemonade glaze:

While the cake is baking, prepare the lemonade glaze by whisking together the powdered sugar and freshly squeezed lemon juice until it reaches a smooth and pourable consistency. Stir in 1 tablespoon of lemon zest for added brightness.

Glaze and garnish:

As soon as the cake is out of the oven, carefully pour the lemonade glaze over the cake while it’s still hot. Garnish the top of the cake with leftover lemon slices, zest, and a dash of lemon juice as you prefer. Allow the cake to cool slightly before serving.

Wait for Luscious Rewards

For an extra indulgence, pair your lemonade cake with a refreshing scoop of lemon sorbet or a dollop of lemon curd. You can also serve it with a chilled glass of traditional lemonade on the side for a nostalgic experience. Once you experience this dreamy lemonade cake, you’ll be reminiscing about those childhood memories in no time!

There we have it - a delicious confluence of summer and sweetness that brings back a flood of memories of sunshine days spent cooling off with a glass of tangy lemonade, right inside a moist, scrumptious cake. Try this recipe and bring some sunshine to your kitchen while captivating your family and friends with the flavors of an idyllic summer day.

All that’s left for you to do now is cuddle up in memories of your childhood, brew your ingredients, step into that rejuvenating world of lemonade, and cherish the moment! Bon appétit!
