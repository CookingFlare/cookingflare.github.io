Farm-to-Table Lentil Soup Recipe with Spinach and Lemon

Are you looking for a wholesome and flavorful soup that celebrates fresh, seasonal ingredients? Look no further than this Farm-to-Table Lentil Soup with Spinach and Lemon. Packed with nutritious lentils, vibrant spinach, and zesty lemon, this soup is not only delicious but also a perfect way to incorporate more plant-based goodness into your diet. Ingredients: 1 cup dried green lentils 4 cups vegetable broth 1 onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 carrots, chopped 2 celery stalks, chopped 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon turmeric Salt and pepper to taste 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped Juice of 1 lemon Fresh parsley for garnish Instructions: Prepare the Lentils: Start by rinsing the dried green lentils under cold water. Drain and set aside. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, chopped carrots, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant. Add Spices: Stir in the cumin, paprika, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Let the spices toast for a minute to release their flavors. Cook the Lentils: Add the rinsed lentils to the pot and pour in the vegetable broth. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the lentils are tender, usually about 20-25 minutes. Add Spinach and Lemon: Once the lentils are cooked, add the chopped spinach to the pot. Let it wilt into the soup. Squeeze in the fresh lemon juice for a burst of citrusy flavor. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more salt, pepper, or lemon juice according to your preference. Serve: Ladle the warm lentil soup into bowls. Garnish with fresh parsley for a pop of color and an extra layer of freshness. Enjoy: Serve this Farm-to-Table Lentil Soup with some crusty bread or a simple green salad for a wholesome and satisfying meal. Why Farm-to-Table? Farm-to-table cooking emphasizes using locally sourced, seasonal ingredients to create dishes that are not only delicious but also support local farmers and promote sustainability. By choosing fresh produce from your local farmers’ market or community-supported agriculture (CSA) program, you not only get the best flavors but also reduce your carbon footprint. ...
