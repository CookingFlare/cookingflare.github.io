Don't Waste That Citrus: 5 Creative Ways to Use Up Leftover Orange Peels


Are you tired of throwing away orange peels after enjoying the juicy flesh inside? Don’t fret! Those leftover orange peels can still be put to good use in your kitchen. From adding flavor to your dishes to creating DIY home remedies, orange peels have a surprising range of uses that you may not have considered. In this blog post, we’ll explore five creative ways to make the most of your leftover orange peels.

1. Citrus Zest Power

One of the simplest and most versatile uses for orange peels is to create citrus zest. Zest is the colorful outer layer of the peel that contains aromatic oils and adds a burst of flavor to your recipes. To make citrus zest, use a zester or a microplane grater to scrape off the thin layer of zest from the orange peel. This zest can be added to both sweet and savory dishes, such as cakes, cookies, marinades, and salad dressings. The vibrant citrus flavor will elevate your recipes to a whole new level.

2. Infused Water Delight

If you’re looking for a refreshing twist to your daily hydration routine, why not try making orange peel-infused water? Simply place a few clean orange peels in a jug of water and let it sit in the refrigerator overnight. The natural oils from the peels will infuse the water with a subtle citrus flavor, making your hydration experience more enjoyable. You can also add other herbs or fruits, such as mint leaves or slices of cucumber, for a personalized touch. Say goodbye to boring plain water and hello to a thirst-quenching treat!

3. All-Purpose Cleaner

Did you know that orange peels can be used to create a natural all-purpose cleaner? The oils present in the peels have powerful cleaning properties that can help eliminate grease, grime, and unpleasant odors. To make your own cleaner, simply place orange peels in a jar and cover them with white vinegar. Allow the mixture to sit for a few weeks, then strain out the peels. Dilute the infused vinegar with water (1:1 ratio) and transfer it to a spray bottle. Now you have a non-toxic cleaner that will leave your surfaces sparkling and smelling fresh.

4. DIY Aromatherapy

If you love the soothing scent of citrus, why not create your own DIY aromatherapy using orange peels? You can make a simple orange peel sachet by drying the peels and placing them in a small cloth bag or a piece of porous fabric. Hang the sachet in your closet, drawer, or car to enjoy the refreshing aroma and keep unpleasant odors at bay. You can also add dried orange peels to potpourri mixtures or homemade scented candles for a delightful burst of citrus fragrance throughout your home.

5. Orange Peel Tea

Last but not least, orange peel tea is a fantastic way to utilize leftover orange peels and enjoy their health benefits. To make orange peel tea, simply place a few clean peels in a teapot or a mug, add boiling water, and let it steep for about 5-10 minutes. The resulting tea is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients like vitamin C and antioxidants. It’s a great way to boost your immune system and enjoy a warming cup of citrus goodness.

Conclusion

Next time you peel an orange, think twice before tossing those vibrant peels in the trash. With these five creative ways to use up leftover orange peels, you can add a burst of flavor to your dishes, create refreshing infused water, make natural cleaners, enjoy DIY aromatherapy, and savor a cup of orange peel tea. Don’t let those citrus peels go to waste - get creative and discover the amazing potential they hold in your kitchen and beyond!

So, what are you waiting for? Start saving those orange peels and unlock their hidden potential. Your taste buds and your home will thank you!
