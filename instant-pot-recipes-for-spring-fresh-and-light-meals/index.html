Instant Pot Recipes for Spring: Fresh and Light Meals


As the days grow longer and flowers begin to bloom, our cravings naturally shift toward lighter dishes that capture the essence of spring. Enter the Instant Pot – the kitchen hero that turns meal prep into a breeze. This versatile appliance is perfect for creating meals that highlight the season’s freshest ingredients, from tender vegetables to lean proteins, all while saving you time and effort.

If you’re looking for inspiration to spruce up your spring meals while making the most of your Instant Pot, you’re in the right place. Below, we explore several delightful recipes tailor-made for warmer days, showcasing vibrant, seasonal flavors that the whole family will love.

1. Lemon-Herb Chicken and Asparagus

Spring is synonymous with bright and zesty flavors, and this dish delivers just that. The combination of lemon juice, fresh herbs, and tender asparagus creates a light but satisfying meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, thyme, and rosemary.
  2. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Heat olive oil, then sear the chicken breasts for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  3. Add minced garlic to the pot and sauté for 1 minute. Pour in the chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits at the bottom.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot, layering the asparagus on top. Squeeze lemon juice over the dish and sprinkle with zest.
  5. Close the Instant Pot lid and set it to “Pressure Cook” on high for 6 minutes. Allow a natural release for 5 minutes, then quick release the remaining pressure.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with extra lemon zest and fresh herbs if desired.

2. Spring Vegetable Risotto

Forget standing over the stove and stirring endlessly – the Instant Pot simplifies risotto like magic. This creamy dish features seasonal vegetables like peas and asparagus, making it a celebration of spring in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh peas
  • 1 cup chopped asparagus
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Melt the butter and olive oil, then cook the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the Arborio rice and stir to coat the grains in the butter and oil. Pour in the white wine and cook for a minute until mostly absorbed.
  3. Stir in the vegetable broth and season with salt and pepper. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 6 minutes.
  4. Once cooking is complete, quick release the pressure and stir in the peas and asparagus. The residual heat will cook them while maintaining their crisp texture.
  5. Stir in the Parmesan cheese until creamy. Serve hot with a sprinkle of more Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil if desired.

3. Honey-Mustard Glazed Salmon

Fresh seafood is a wonderful choice for spring, and this honey-mustard glazed salmon takes minimal effort while offering maximum flavor. With its sweet and tangy glaze, this dish pairs beautifully with a side of roasted spring vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Brush this glaze generously over the salmon fillets.
  2. Pour water into the Instant Pot and place the trivet inside. Lay the salmon fillets on a sheet of aluminum foil and place them on the trivet.
  3. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 3 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release for 5 minutes before carefully opening the lid.
  4. Serve the salmon warm with steamed vegetables or a fresh salad for an easy springtime feast.

4. Coconut Lime Thai Soup

Light soups are a fantastic way to embrace spring flavors, and this coconut lime Thai soup is no exception. Packed with aromatic spices, silky coconut milk, and fresh lime juice, this soup is a warming yet refreshing delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Heat the coconut oil, then sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Stir in the red curry paste and cook for 1 minute. Add the broth, coconut milk, mushrooms, carrots, and fish sauce (if using).
  3. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 5 minutes. Once the cooking cycle is complete, quick release the pressure.
  4. Stir in the lime juice, adjusting the flavor with additional lime or salt as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.

5. Herb and Spring Vegetable Frittata

Brunch just got easier with this Instant Pot frittata! Bursting with fresh vegetables and fragrant herbs, this egg dish is ideal for a spring gathering or a make-ahead weekday breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup diced zucchini
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir in the spinach, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and green onions.
  3. Grease a heatproof dish or pan that fits inside your Instant Pot. Pour the egg mixture into the dish.
  4. Pour water into the Instant Pot and place the trivet inside. Set the dish on the trivet and close the lid.
  5. Set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 15 minutes. Allow a natural release for 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  6. Carefully remove the frittata, slice, and serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips for Using Your Instant Pot This Spring

  1. Opt for Fresh, Seasonal Produce: Spring is the perfect time to utilize ingredients like asparagus, peas, spinach, leeks, and radishes. Eating seasonally not only enhances the flavor of your dishes but also supports local farmers.

  2. Leverage “Sauté” Mode: Before pressure cooking, use the sauté function to bloom spices or caramelize onions and garlic. This builds depth of flavor in your meals.

  3. Experiment with Herbs: Fresh herbs like basil, cilantro, dill, and mint can elevate even the simplest spring dishes. Add them at the end of cooking for a punch of freshness.

  4. Balance Richness with Acid: Ingredients like citrus juice, vinegars, and yogurt provide brightness to your meals, ensuring they feel light and fresh for the season.

Spring is a time of renewal, and your meals should follow the same theme. The Instant Pot makes it easy to create dishes that are quick, healthy, and full of seasonal ingredients. Whether you’re craving a zesty chicken dinner, a creamy risotto, or a comforting soup, these recipes are sure to leave you inspired and your taste buds delighted. Break out the Instant Pot and let the flavors of spring shine!
