Top 10 Holiday Cooking Hacks to Save You Time


The holiday season is upon us, and with it comes the chaos of cooking for family and friends. Long hours in the kitchen, endless pots and pans to clean, and the pressure to create the perfect meal can be overwhelming. But fear not, dear cooks! We've got the inside scoop on the top 10 holiday cooking hacks to save you time and make your season merry and bright.

  1. Plan Ahead, Save Time Before you even start cooking, take some time to plan out your menu. Decide on the dishes you want to serve, and make a list of all the ingredients you'll need. This will help you avoid last-minute trips to the store, and ensure that you have everything you need on hand.
  2. Cook in Advance Cooking in advance can be a huge timesaver. Consider preparing some dishes the day before, such as soups, stews, or even entire meals like lasagna or chicken parmesan. This will give you more time to focus on other tasks, and also help to reduce the stress of cooking for a large group.
  3. Use a 'Batching' Technique Batching is a technique that involves cooking multiple ingredients at once, rather than individually preparing each item. For example, you can cook a entire tray of vegetables at once, rather than spending time creating each individual dish. This can be especially helpful when cooking for a crowd, as it will save you time and effort.
  4. Make the Most of Leftovers Leftovers are a holiday cook's best friend. Instead of throwing out last night's dinner, turn it into a new creation. Use leftover roast beef to make a hearty beef pot pie, or transform last night's vegetables into a tasty soup.
  5. Shop Smart for Ingredients Don't waste your time running to the store for a single ingredient. Plan your shopping trip in advance, and make sure you have everything you need. Consider shopping at stores that offer online ordering and in-store pickup, to save even more time.
  6. Use Cooking Shortcuts Let's face it, not every dish needs to be made from scratch. Use cooking shortcuts like pre-made pie crusts, condensed soups, and even pre-cooked meats to save time and effort.
  7. Label and Store Food Properly A well-organized kitchen is a happy kitchen. Label and store your food properly to avoid confusion and waste. Use clear labels on your containers, and store your food in a way that makes sense for the recipe you're using.
  8. Clean as You Go Don't let your kitchen become a disaster zone! Clean as you go to avoid a huge mess at the end of your cooking session. Wipe down surfaces, clean up spills, and put away utensils as you go.
  9. Multitasking is Your Friend You don't need to be a master multi-tasker to cook a great meal. Use your time wisely and cook multiple dishes at once. For example, you can bake a ham and prepare a side dish at the same time.
  10. Take a Deep Breath and Ask for Help Finally, remember that it's okay to ask for help. The holiday season can be overwhelming, but don't let cooking stress you out. Ask a friend or family member to lend a hand, or consider enlisting the help of a professional caterer. By incorporating these top 10 holiday cooking hacks into your holiday menu, you'll be able to save time, reduce stress, and enjoy more quality time with your loved ones. Remember to plan ahead, cook in advance, and make the most of leftovers. Don't be afraid to use cooking shortcuts and multitask to get the job done. And if all else fails, just take a deep breath and remember that it's okay to ask for help.

Additional Tips and Tricks

Consider investing in a slow cooker or Instant Pot to make cooking easier and more efficient. Use a timer to ensure that your dishes are cooked to perfection. Don't be afraid to try new recipes and ingredients. Have fun with it! Cooking during the holiday season is a time to be enjoyed with loved ones.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to hosting a stress-free and delicious holiday meal. Happy cooking!
