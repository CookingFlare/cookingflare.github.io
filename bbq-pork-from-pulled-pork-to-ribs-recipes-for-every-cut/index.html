BBQ Pork: From Pulled Pork to Ribs, Recipes for Every Cut


Barbecue pork is a beloved dish enjoyed by many, and for good reason. The smoky, tender meat is a crowd-pleaser at any gathering or backyard cookout. From pulled pork sandwiches to juicy ribs, there are endless ways to enjoy this delicious meat. In this blog post, we will explore different recipes for every cut of BBQ pork, showcasing the versatility and mouthwatering flavors that this meat has to offer.

Pulled Pork

Let’s start with the classic: pulled pork. This melt-in-your-mouth dish is made by slow-cooking a pork shoulder until it becomes tender and easy to shred. The key to achieving that delicious pulled pork texture is low and slow cooking. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork shoulder (4-5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or smoker to 225°F (107°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder to make a dry rub.
  3. Rub the dry rub all over the pork shoulder, making sure to coat it evenly.
  4. Place the pork shoulder on the grill or smoker and cook for about 1.5 hours per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 195-205°F (90-96°C).
  5. Remove the pork shoulder from the grill or smoker and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  6. Using two forks, shred the pork shoulder into bite-sized pieces.
  7. Toss the shredded pork with your favorite barbecue sauce.
  8. Serve the pulled pork on buns or as a filling for tacos, and enjoy!

Ribs

Next up, we have ribs. Whether you prefer baby back ribs or spare ribs, there’s no denying the incredible flavor that comes from slow-cooking these cuts of meat. Here’s a recipe for finger-licking good ribs:

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or smoker to 225°F (107°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder to make a dry rub.
  3. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs.
  4. Rub the dry rub all over the ribs, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Place the ribs on the grill or smoker and cook for about 3-4 hours, or until the meat is tender and starts to pull away from the bones.
  6. Brush the ribs with barbecue sauce and continue cooking for another 30 minutes, or until the sauce has caramelized slightly.
  7. Remove the ribs from the grill or smoker and let them rest for a few minutes before cutting into individual portions.
  8. Serve the ribs with extra barbecue sauce on the side and enjoy!

Other Cuts

While pulled pork and ribs are the stars of the BBQ pork show, there are plenty of other cuts that can be transformed into mouthwatering dishes. Here are a few ideas:

  • Pork chops: Marinate pork chops in your favorite barbecue sauce, then grill or pan-fry them until cooked through. Serve with grilled vegetables for a complete meal.
  • Pork belly: Slow-cook pork belly until tender, then finish it on the grill or under the broiler to achieve a crispy exterior. Slice and serve with a side of coleslaw.
  • Pork tenderloin: Season pork tenderloin with a dry rub, then grill or roast it until cooked to perfection. Slice and serve with a drizzle of barbecue sauce.

The possibilities are endless when it comes to BBQ pork. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a comforting meal, these recipes will surely satisfy your taste buds. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in the smoky, flavorful world of BBQ pork. Happy cooking!
