Delightful Pain aux Raisins: A French Pastry Treat


If you’re a fan of French pastries, you’re in for a treat with this delightful Pain aux Raisins recipe. This classic pastry, also known as “raisin snails” in English, is a heavenly combination of flaky, buttery pastry, sweet pastry cream, and plump, juicy raisins. It’s a popular choice for breakfast, brunch, or a delightful afternoon snack. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of Pain aux Raisins, the key ingredients, and a step-by-step guide to creating these delectable treats in your own kitchen.

History of Pain aux Raisins

Pain aux Raisins has a rich history that dates back to France, where it is a staple in many bakeries and patisseries. The name “Pain aux Raisins” translates to “bread with raisins,” reflecting its origins as a variation of traditional bread. Over time, this pastry evolved into the delicate, spiral-shaped treat that we know and love today. It has become a beloved pastry not only in France but also in many other parts of the world, thanks to its irresistible combination of flavors and textures.

Key Ingredients

To create the perfect Pain aux Raisins, you’ll need a few key ingredients:

For the Pastry Dough:

  • All-purpose flour
  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated sugar
  • Salt
  • Active dry yeast
  • Eggs
  • Milk

For the Pastry Cream:

  • Whole milk
  • Egg yolks
  • Granulated sugar
  • Cornstarch
  • Vanilla extract

For the Filling:

  • Plump raisins
  • Warm water
  • Rum (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by preparing the pastry dough. Combine the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a bowl. In a separate bowl, mix the eggs and milk. Gradually incorporate the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, then knead the dough until it’s smooth and elastic. Next, add the softened butter and continue to knead until the butter is fully incorporated. Allow the dough to rise until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Make the Pastry Cream

While the dough is rising, prepare the pastry cream. In a saucepan, heat the milk until it’s just simmering. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat until it thickens. Remove from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer the pastry cream to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap onto the surface of the cream to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the pastry cream in the refrigerator.

Step 3: Assemble the Pain aux Raisins

Once the dough has risen, roll it out into a rectangle on a lightly floured surface. Spread the chilled pastry cream evenly over the dough, then sprinkle the raisins on top. Starting from one of the longer edges, roll the dough into a tight cylinder. Using a sharp knife, slice the cylinder into individual rolls, each about 1 inch thick. Place the rolls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving space between them to allow for rising.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven and let the Pain aux Raisins rise for a short time before baking. Once they have risen, brush the tops with an egg wash for a beautiful golden-brown finish. Bake the pastries until they are puffed and golden, then allow them to cool slightly before serving.

Enjoy Your Delightful Creation

Once your Pain aux Raisins are baked to perfection, savor the aroma of warm, flaky pastry and sweet, plump raisins. These delightful pastries are best enjoyed fresh from the oven, accompanied by a cup of freshly brewed coffee or tea. Whether you’re sharing them with friends and family or indulging in a moment of self-care, Pain aux Raisins are sure to delight your taste buds and transport you to a French patisserie.

Now that you have the recipe for these delightful Pain aux Raisins, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking. With a bit of patience and a whole lot of love, you can recreate the magic of this classic French pastry in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
