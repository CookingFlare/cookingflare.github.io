Ingredients 1 cup quinoa 2 cups almond milk (or your preferred plant-based milk) 1 apple, diced 1/4 cup raisins 1/4 cup chopped walnuts 1 tablespoon maple syrup 1 teaspoon cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract Pinch of salt Instructions Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness. Drain well. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa, almond milk, diced apple, raisins, chopped walnuts, maple syrup, cinnamon, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan with a lid. Simmer the mixture for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once the quinoa is tender and the porridge has thickened, remove the saucepan from the heat. Let the porridge sit for a few minutes to cool slightly before serving. Roasted Sweet Potato and Kale Salad: A Healthy Fall Option

Fall is the perfect time to enjoy hearty and nutritious salads that are both satisfying and packed with flavor. One such salad that embodies the essence of autumn is the Roasted Sweet Potato and Kale Salad. This combination of vibrant colors, nutritious ingredients, and delicious flavors makes it a standout dish for any fall gathering or weeknight dinner. Ingredients To create this tantalizing salad, you will need: 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed 1 bunch of kale, stems removed and leaves torn into bite-sized pieces 1 red onion, thinly sliced 1/2 cup of dried cranberries 1/2 cup of crumbled goat cheese 1/4 cup of chopped pecans 2 tablespoons of olive oil 1 tablespoon of maple syrup Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a bowl, toss the cubed sweet potatoes with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper until well coated. Spread them evenly on the prepared baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and slightly caramelized. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, prepare the kale by massaging it with 1 tablespoon of olive oil for a few minutes. This will help to soften the kale and reduce any bitterness. In a large salad bowl, combine the massaged kale, sliced red onion, dried cranberries, crumbled goat cheese, and chopped pecans. Once the sweet potatoes are ready, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Add the roasted sweet potatoes to the salad bowl and gently toss all the ingredients together until well combined. Season with additional salt and pepper, if desired. Serve the Roasted Sweet Potato and Kale Salad immediately, or refrigerate it for later use. Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Mashed Potatoes: A Fragrant Fall Side

There’s something magical about the flavors of fall. As the weather gets cooler and the leaves start to change, our taste buds crave comforting and aromatic dishes. And what better way to capture the essence of this season than with a bowl of creamy, flavorful mashed potatoes? But not just any mashed potatoes – we’re talking about Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Mashed Potatoes. Ingredients 4 large russet potatoes 1 whole head of garlic 2 tablespoons olive oil 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 1/2 cup milk 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Wash and scrub the potatoes to remove any dirt. Cut the potatoes into quarters, leaving the skin on. This will give the mashed potatoes a rustic texture. Place the potato quarters in a large pot and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook until the potatoes are fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. While the potatoes are boiling, prepare the roasted garlic. Cut off the top of the head of garlic to expose the cloves. Drizzle with olive oil and wrap tightly in aluminum foil. Place in the oven and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the cloves are soft and golden. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them and return them to the pot. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their skins and add them to the pot with the potatoes. Add the softened butter, milk, and chopped rosemary to the pot. Using a potato masher or a fork, mash the potatoes until smooth and creamy. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can also use a hand mixer or a blender. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and give everything a final stir to incorporate all the flavors. Serve the Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Mashed Potatoes hot, garnished with a sprig of fresh rosemary for an extra touch of elegance.
