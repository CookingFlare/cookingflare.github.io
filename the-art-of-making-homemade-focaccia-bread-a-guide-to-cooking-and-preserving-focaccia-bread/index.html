The Art of Making Homemade Focaccia Bread: A Guide to Cooking and Preserving Focaccia Bread


Focaccia bread, a staple of Italian cuisine, has been a favorite among bread enthusiasts for centuries. Its soft, pillowy texture and savory flavor make it a perfect accompaniment to a variety of dishes, from soups to salads to sandwiches. While store-bought focaccia may be convenient, there’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of baking a fresh batch at home. In this guide, we’ll explore the art of making homemade focaccia bread, from scratch to preservation.

Ingredients and Equipment

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients and equipment needed to make homemade focaccia bread.

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or oregano) for garnish

As for equipment, you’ll need:

  • A large mixing bowl
  • A wooden spoon or stand mixer
  • A lightly floured surface for kneading
  • A baking sheet or pizza stone
  • A sharp knife or pastry brush

The Recipe

Now that we have our ingredients and equipment ready, let’s move on to the recipe.

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Stir gently to dissolve the yeast, then let it sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy and bubbly.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

Add the olive oil, salt, flour, and sugar to the bowl. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

Step 4: Let the Dough Rise

Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Dough

Punch down the dough and shape it into a rectangle or circle, depending on your preference.

Step 6: Let the Dough Rise Again

Place the shaped dough on a baking sheet or pizza stone, cover it with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until it has puffed up slightly.

Step 7: Bake the Focaccia

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Dimple the dough with your fingers, then drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and cooked through.

Step 8: Cool and Preserve

Let the focaccia cool on a wire rack for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing. You can store it at room temperature for up to 3 days, or freeze it for up to 2 months.

Tips and Variations

Tips

  • Use a high-quality olive oil for the best flavor.
  • Don’t overmix the dough, as this can lead to a dense, tough focaccia.
  • If you’re having trouble getting the dough to rise, try using a warm water bath or a proofing box.

Variations

  • Try adding different herbs, such as rosemary or thyme, to the dough for added flavor.
  • Use a mixture of all-purpose and whole wheat flour for a nuttier, more robust flavor.
  • Add some grated Parmesan cheese to the dough for an extra burst of flavor.

Conclusion

Making homemade focaccia bread is a simple yet rewarding process that requires only a few ingredients and some basic cooking skills. With these tips and variations, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious, authentic Italian-style focaccia that’s sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you’re serving it as a side dish or using it as a base for your favorite sandwiches, homemade focaccia bread is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.
