Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Peppers: A Veggie Delight


Are you looking for a delicious and healthy recipe that will impress your guests? Look no further than Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Peppers! This mouthwatering dish combines the flavors of ratatouille with the satisfying crunch of bell peppers, making it a perfect option for vegetarians and veggie lovers alike.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 small eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for sautéing

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Ratatouille Filling

  1. Set your sous vide cooker to 185°F (85°C) and preheat the water bath.
  2. In a large skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the diced eggplant, zucchini, and yellow squash to the skillet, and cook until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and set aside.

Step 2: Preparing the Bell Peppers

  1. Slice off the tops of the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside.
  2. Rinse the peppers under cold water to remove any remaining seeds.
  3. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a pinch of salt.
  4. Blanch the bell peppers in the boiling water for 3-4 minutes, or until slightly tender. Remove them from the pot and drain.

Step 3: Stuffing the Peppers

  1. Using a spoon, carefully fill each bell pepper with the ratatouille filling, packing it tightly.
  2. Place the stuffed peppers in a vacuum-sealed bag, making sure they are in a single layer and not overlapping.
  3. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

Step 4: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Gently place the sealed bag into the preheated water bath, ensuring that the peppers are fully submerged.
  2. Cook the peppers sous vide for 1 hour, allowing the flavors to intensify and the peppers to become tender.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

  1. After 1 hour, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and open it.
  2. Transfer the stuffed peppers to a serving plate, drizzle with a little olive oil, and garnish with fresh herbs, such as basil or parsley.

Serving Suggestions

Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Peppers make a delicious main course or a side dish. They pair well with a fresh green salad or a crusty baguette. For a heartier meal, serve them with quinoa or couscous.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Peppers are a delightful way to showcase the flavors of ratatouille while adding a unique twist with the bell peppers. This recipe is not only delicious but also healthy and visually appealing. With the help of sous vide cooking, the flavors are intensified and the peppers become tender, making every bite a burst of flavor. So, why not give this recipe a try and impress your guests with this veggie delight? Enjoy!
