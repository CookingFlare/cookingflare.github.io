Korean Seafood Pancakes: A Crispy and Savory Treat


Are you craving something crispy and savory? Look no further than Korean seafood pancakes! These delicious pancakes are a popular street food in Korea, known for their delightful combination of fresh seafood and crispy batter. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making these delectable treats in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, put on your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

To make Korean seafood pancakes, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 cup assorted seafood (shrimp, squid, and/or clams), chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrots, julienned
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, water, eggs, salt, and pepper until you have a smooth batter.
  2. Add the assorted seafood, green onions, and carrots to the batter. Mix well to ensure that all the ingredients are evenly coated.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet or a frying pan over medium heat and add enough vegetable oil to coat the bottom of the pan.
  4. Scoop a ladleful of the pancake batter onto the pan and spread it out evenly into a round shape, about 6 inches in diameter. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
  5. Flip the pancake over using a spatula and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the other side is also golden brown and crispy. Repeat this process for the remaining batter, adding more oil to the pan as needed.
  6. Once cooked, transfer the pancakes to a paper towel-lined plate to remove any excess oil. Cut the pancakes into smaller pieces, if desired, for easier serving.
  7. Serve the Korean seafood pancakes hot with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, and a pinch of sugar. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier kick, add some gochujang (Korean chili paste) to the pancake batter.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of seafood, such as mussels or scallops, to create your own unique flavor combinations.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, you can omit the seafood and add more vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms instead.
  • These pancakes are best enjoyed immediately after cooking, as they tend to lose their crispiness over time. However, you can reheat them in the oven or toaster oven to regain some of their crunchiness.

Conclusion

Korean seafood pancakes are a delightful treat that combines the freshness of seafood with the satisfying crunch of a well-cooked pancake. With their simple yet flavorful ingredients, they are not only a popular street food in Korea but also a beloved dish that can be easily recreated in your own kitchen. So, why not give this recipe a try and impress your family and friends with a taste of Korea? Happy cooking!
