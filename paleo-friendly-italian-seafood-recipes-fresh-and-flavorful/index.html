Paleo-Friendly Italian Seafood Recipes: Fresh and Flavorful


When it comes to combining the richness of Italian cuisine with the health-conscious principles of the Paleo diet, seafood becomes a star ingredient. The Mediterranean diet has long been praised for its emphasis on fresh, wholesome ingredients, and Italian seafood dishes perfectly align with the Paleo way of eating. In this blog post, we’ll explore some tantalizing Paleo-friendly Italian seafood recipes that are not only bursting with flavor but also easy to prepare at home.

Grilled Lemon Garlic Shrimp Skewers

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 8 minutes

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss until well coated. Let it marinate for 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  5. Grill the shrimp skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and slightly charred.
  6. Serve the grilled lemon garlic shrimp skewers hot, garnished with additional parsley and lemon wedges.

Paleo Zucchini Noodles with Clams

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 2 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 pound fresh clams, scrubbed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken or fish broth
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chopped basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the spiralized zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss for 2-3 minutes, just until they are slightly softened.
  3. Pour in the chicken or fish broth and white wine. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the clams to the skillet, cover, and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the clams open.
  5. Discard any unopened clams.
  6. Season the zucchini noodles and clams with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if desired. Toss in the fresh basil.
  7. Serve the Paleo zucchini noodles with clams immediately, drizzled with a little extra olive oil if desired.

Baked Italian Stuffed Salmon

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chopped basil
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the sun-dried tomatoes, Kalamata olives, basil, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Divide the tomato and olive mixture evenly among the salmon fillets, pressing it gently onto the tops.
  5. Bake the salmon for 15-20 minutes, or until it flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Serve the baked Italian stuffed salmon hot, with lemon wedges on the side.

These delightful Paleo-friendly Italian seafood recipes celebrate the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean while staying true to the Paleo philosophy. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Italy at home, these recipes are sure to impress your taste buds and support your commitment to a healthy lifestyle. Buon appetito!
