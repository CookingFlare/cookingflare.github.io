Cooking with Spring Onions: 5 Tasty Recipes to Enjoy


Spring onions, also known as scallions or green onions, are a versatile and flavorful ingredient that can elevate a variety of dishes. These vibrant alliums bring a mild onion flavor and a hint of sweetness, making them perfect for everything from salads to stir-fries. In this blog post, we’ll explore five delightful recipes featuring spring onions that are sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re an experienced cook or just starting out, these recipes are simple to follow and packed with flavor. Let’s dive in!

1. Spring Onion and Potato Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 1 cup chopped spring onions (white and green parts)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced potatoes and cook until they are tender and lightly browned, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped spring onions to the skillet and sauté for another 2-3 minutes, until they are soft.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Stir in the cooked potatoes and spring onions, then fold in the cheese.
  5. Pour the mixture back into the skillet and cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until the edges begin to set.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the frittata is fully set and lightly golden on top.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes, then slice and serve warm, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

This spring onion and potato frittata makes for a hearty breakfast or a light lunch. It’s perfect for meal prep and can be enjoyed warm or cold!

2. Spring Onion Stir-Fry with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz (400 g) firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup spring onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu: Wrap the cubed tofu in a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy object on top to remove excess moisture for about 15 minutes. Then, cut into smaller cubes.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides, about 8-10 minutes. Remove the tofu from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced bell pepper and chopped spring onions to the pan. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender yet crisp.
  5. Return the tofu to the pan and pour in the soy sauce. Toss everything together and cook for an additional 2 minutes to heat through.
  6. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles.

This spring onion stir-fry is a quick and easy meal that’s packed with protein and flavor. It’s a great way to enjoy a healthy weeknight dinner!

3. Creamy Spring Onion and Spinach Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup spring onions, chopped
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or coconut milk for a dairy-free option)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Croutons and fresh herbs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped spring onions and sauté for about 5 minutes until they become soft.
  2. Add the fresh spinach to the pot and cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for 10 minutes to meld the flavors.
  4. Remove the pot from heat and use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can transfer it to a blender in batches.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream (or coconut milk) and season with salt and pepper to taste. Heat the soup gently on low until warmed through.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with croutons and fresh herbs.

This creamy spring onion and spinach soup is comforting and perfect for chilly days. It’s a delightful way to incorporate spring onions into your diet while enjoying a hearty bowl of goodness.

4. Grilled Spring Onion and Vegetable Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup spring onions, trimmed
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. In a large bowl, combine spring onions, zucchini, bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the vegetables evenly.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between spring onions and other veggies.
  4. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Serve the skewers warm as a side dish or light main course.

These grilled spring onion and vegetable skewers are perfect for summer barbecues or as a colorful addition to any meal. They’re packed with flavor and make for a great vegetarian option!

5. Spring Onion Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 cup chopped spring onions
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the flour and gradually pour in the boiling water, stirring with a fork until a dough forms. Let it cool for a few minutes.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into four equal parts. Roll each piece into a thin circle.
  4. Brush a thin layer of vegetable oil on each circle, sprinkle with salt and chopped spring onions, then roll it up tightly like a jelly roll.
  5. Coil the roll into a spiral and flatten it gently with your palm. Roll it out again into a circle about 1/4 inch thick.
  6. In a skillet, heat a little vegetable oil over medium heat. Cook each pancake for about 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve the pancakes hot with soy sauce for dipping.

These spring onion pancakes are a delicious snack or appetizer that everyone will love. They’re crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them irresistible!

Conclusion

Spring onions are not only delicious but also incredibly versatile. From savory frittatas to flavorful stir-fries, these recipes showcase the delightful taste and texture that spring onions can bring to your meals. Whether you’re looking to impress guests or simply enjoy a home-cooked dinner, these five recipes will help you make the most of this vibrant ingredient. So, grab a bunch of spring onions and start cooking! Enjoy your culinary adventure with these tasty dishes.
