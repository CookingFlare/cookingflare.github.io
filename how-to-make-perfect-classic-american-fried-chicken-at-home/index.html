How to Make Perfect Classic American Fried Chicken at Home


There’s something irresistible about the crispy, juicy goodness of classic American fried chicken. The golden-brown, crunchy exterior gives way to tender, flavorful meat, making it a beloved dish for many. While it’s easy to order fried chicken at a restaurant, there’s something special about making it at home. The aroma of sizzling oil and the satisfaction of achieving the perfect crunch are part of the experience. If you’ve ever wanted to recreate this classic dish in your own kitchen, you’re in the right place. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve the perfect classic American fried chicken at home.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Dredge:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken:

    • Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl and cover them with buttermilk. Let them soak for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight. This will help tenderize the meat and infuse it with flavor.

  2. Prepare the Dredge:

    • In a shallow dish, combine the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. This is the seasoned flour that will coat the chicken pieces before frying.

  3. Coat the Chicken:

    • Remove the chicken pieces from the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off. Dredge each piece in the seasoned flour, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Place the coated pieces on a wire rack and let them rest for 10-15 minutes to allow the coating to set.

  4. Heat the Oil:

    • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a deep-fry thermometer to monitor the temperature and ensure it remains consistent throughout the frying process.

  5. Fry the Chicken:

    • Carefully place a few pieces of chicken in the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Fry the chicken in batches, if necessary, to maintain the oil temperature. Cook the chicken until it’s golden brown and crispy, and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). This should take about 15-20 minutes, depending on the size of the pieces.

  6. Drain and Serve:

    • Once the chicken is done, use tongs to transfer it to a wire rack set over a baking sheet. This will allow any excess oil to drip off and the chicken to stay crispy. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving.

  7. Enjoy!

    • Serve your perfectly fried chicken with your favorite sides and enjoy the crunchy, flavorful goodness.

Tips and Tricks

  • For extra crispy chicken, you can double-coat the pieces by dipping them back into the buttermilk and seasoned flour after the initial dredging.

  • If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the seasoned flour, or add a pinch of cayenne to the buttermilk marinade.

  • To keep the cooked chicken warm while frying the remaining batches, place it on a wire rack set in a low-temperature oven (around 200°F or 93°C).

Conclusion

Making perfect classic American fried chicken at home is a rewarding experience that yields delicious results. With the right ingredients, techniques, and a little bit of patience, you can enjoy the satisfaction of creating this beloved dish in your own kitchen. Whether it’s for a family dinner or a weekend indulgence, homemade fried chicken is sure to be a hit. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the crispy, juicy goodness of homemade classic American fried chicken.
