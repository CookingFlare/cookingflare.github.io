Deliciously Simple Meatloaf Recipe for Comfort Food Cravings


Meatloaf, the ultimate comfort food that never fails to satisfy our cravings. A classic American dish that warms our hearts and bellies, meatloaf is a staple in many households. But what makes a great meatloaf recipe? Is it the secret ingredient, the cooking technique, or the combination of flavors? In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating a deliciously simple meatloaf that’s sure to become a family favorite.

The Perfect Blend of Ingredients

Meatloaf is all about the balance of flavors and textures. You want a harmonious blend of savory, sweet, and tangy notes that complement the rich flavor of the meat. Our recipe starts with a classic combination of ground beef, pork, and veal. Yes, you read that right – veal! The addition of veal adds a subtle tenderness and depth to the meatloaf.

Here’s the magic formula:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/4 pound ground veal
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano

The Secret to a Moist and Tender Meatloaf

One of the most common complaints about meatloaf is that it’s dry and dense. But fear not, dear reader! Our secret to a moist and tender meatloaf lies in the addition of breadcrumbs and the gentle handling of the meat mixture. Make sure to handle the meat mixture gently, avoiding overmixing, and don’t overbake the meatloaf.

Here’s how to assemble the meatloaf:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground meats, onion, garlic, breadcrumbs, egg, ketchup, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, salt, black pepper, paprika, and oregano. Mix everything together with your hands or a wooden spoon until just combined. Be careful not to overmix!
  3. Transfer the meat mixture to a loaf pan or shape it into a loaf shape on a baking sheet.
  4. Place the meatloaf in the oven and bake for 45-50 minutes or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C).

The Finishing Touches

While the meatloaf is baking, prepare the glaze. Yes, you read that right – glaze! A sweet and tangy glaze adds a delightful contrast to the savory meatloaf. Mix together:

  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce

After the meatloaf has baked for 30 minutes, brush the glaze all over the top. Return the meatloaf to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the glaze is caramelized and sticky.

Serve and Enjoy!

And there you have it, folks! A deliciously simple meatloaf recipe that’s sure to become a family favorite. Serve it with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad. The possibilities are endless!

In conclusion, meatloaf is a dish that’s all about simplicity, comfort, and love. With this recipe, you’ll be able to create a mouthwatering meatloaf that’s sure to satisfy your cravings. So go ahead, get cooking, and enjoy the warmth and comfort of a delicious homemade meatloaf!
