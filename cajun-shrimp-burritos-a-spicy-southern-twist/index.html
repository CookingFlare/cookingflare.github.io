Cajun Shrimp Burritos: A Spicy Southern Twist


If you’re craving a taste of the Southern U.S., look no further than these Cajun Shrimp Burritos. This recipe brings together the bold flavors of Cajun cuisine with the convenience of a handheld meal. Bursting with zesty spices, succulent shrimp, and a medley of fresh ingredients, these burritos are sure to tantalize your taste buds and transport you to the heart of Louisiana.

Ingredients

For the Cajun Shrimp:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Burritos:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

For the Creole Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Creole mustard
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cajun Shrimp:

    • In a bowl, toss the shrimp with Cajun seasoning, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
    • In a large skillet over medium-high heat, warm the olive oil. Add the seasoned shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and opaque. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. Make the Creole Sauce:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Creole mustard, hot sauce, minced garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to taste and set the sauce aside.

  3. Assemble the Burritos:

    • Warm the flour tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave for a few seconds to make them pliable.
    • Spread a generous spoonful of cooked rice down the center of each tortilla.
    • Top the rice with black beans, corn, red bell pepper, red onion, and the Cajun shrimp.
    • Drizzle the Creole sauce over the filling, and add a few slices of avocado.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

  4. Roll the Burritos:

    • To roll the burritos, fold in the sides of the tortilla, then roll it up tightly, tucking in the filling as you go.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Place the burritos seam-side down on a plate and cut them in half for easy serving.
    • Serve with extra Creole sauce on the side and lime wedges for a burst of citrus flavor.

A Taste of the South

These Cajun Shrimp Burritos are a celebration of Southern flavors, bringing together the warmth of Cajun spices, the richness of Creole sauce, and the freshness of vibrant vegetables. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or simply treating yourself to a flavorful meal, these burritos are a delightful way to savor the essence of Southern cuisine.

So, gather your ingredients, embrace the spirit of the South, and embark on a culinary journey with these Cajun Shrimp Burritos. With each spicy bite, you’ll experience the unmistakable charm of Southern cooking and create lasting memories around the dinner table. Enjoy every mouthwatering moment!
