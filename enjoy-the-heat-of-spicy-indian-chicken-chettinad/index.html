Enjoy the Heat of Spicy Indian Chicken Chettinad


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and fiery spices, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Spicy Indian Chicken Chettinad. Originating from the Chettinad region of Tamil Nadu in South India, this dish is known for its aromatic blend of spices and its intense heat. Get ready to embark on a culinary adventure as we dive into the world of Chettinad cuisine.

Ingredients

To recreate the authentic flavors of Spicy Indian Chicken Chettinad, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • A handful of curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the fennel seeds, black peppercorns, and dry red chilies. Sauté for a minute until the spices release their aroma.

  2. Add the finely chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown. This will take around 5-6 minutes.

  3. Next, add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Now it’s time to add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala. This step will take around 8-10 minutes.

  5. In a small bowl, mix together the red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder with a little water to form a paste.

  6. Add the spice paste to the pan and cook for a few minutes until the masala is well-coated with the spices.

  7. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and mix well with the masala. Make sure each piece is coated evenly.

  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the chicken cook for about 15-20 minutes or until it is cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  9. Once the chicken is cooked, remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. This will help to dry out any excess moisture and intensify the flavors.

  10. Add the curry leaves and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  11. Finally, season with salt to taste and give it a final mix. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Spicy Indian Chicken Chettinad pairs perfectly with steamed rice, roti, or naan bread. The rich and flavorful gravy of this dish is best enjoyed when it is soaked up by the bread or mixed with the rice. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves for an extra burst of freshness.

Conclusion

Spicy Indian Chicken Chettinad is a dish that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of South India. With its complex blend of spices and fiery heat, it is a true delight for those who crave bold flavors. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your stove, and get ready to enjoy the heat of this iconic dish.
