Grilled Watermelon Caprese Skewers: A Unique Fourth of July Appetizer


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and indulge in some delicious and refreshing appetizers. This Fourth of July, why not try something new and exciting? Introducing Grilled Watermelon Caprese Skewers, a unique and mouth-watering twist on the classic Italian Caprese salad.

What is Caprese Salad?

For those who may not be familiar, Caprese salad is a simple yet flavorful Italian dish made with fresh mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil. The sweetness of the tomatoes and the creaminess of the mozzarella are perfectly balanced by the bright, herbaceous flavor of the basil. It’s a classic combination that never goes out of style.

Why Add Watermelon?

So, why add watermelon to this classic combination? Watermelon is a natural pairing with the other ingredients, and its sweetness and refreshing texture make it the perfect addition to this skewer. The grilled watermelon adds a smoky flavor that complements the other ingredients beautifully, while its juiciness helps to keep the skewer moist and flavorful.

How to Make Grilled Watermelon Caprese Skewers

To make these delicious skewers, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 large watermelon, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10-12 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread a watermelon cube, a cherry tomato half, a mozzarella round, and a few basil leaves onto each skewer, leaving a small space between each ingredient.
  3. Brush the skewers with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the watermelon is slightly charred and the mozzarella is melted.
  5. Serve the skewers immediately, garnished with additional basil leaves if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor to your skewers, try drizzling them with a balsamic glaze or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes.
  • If you prefer a more intense tomato flavor, use beefsteak tomatoes instead of cherry tomatoes.
  • To make this recipe more substantial, add some grilled chicken or prosciutto to the skewers.
  • For a vegetarian option, substitute the mozzarella with ricotta cheese or goat cheese.

Conclusion

Grilled Watermelon Caprese Skewers are the perfect appetizer for your Fourth of July celebration. They’re easy to make, delicious, and visually stunning. The combination of sweet watermelon, tangy mozzarella, and fresh basil is a match made in heaven. So why not give them a try this summer? Your guests will love them, and you’ll be the talk of the party. Happy grilling!
