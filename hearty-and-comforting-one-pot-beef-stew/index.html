Hearty and Comforting One Pot Beef Stew


There’s nothing quite like a warm and hearty beef stew to comfort you on a chilly evening. The rich flavors, tender meat, and savory vegetables all come together in one pot to create a dish that is both delicious and satisfying. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make a hearty and comforting one pot beef stew that will warm your soul and please your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This will help seal in the juices and add flavor to the stew.

  2. Remove the meat from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  3. Add the chopped carrots, diced potatoes, and chopped celery to the pot. Stir everything together and cook for a few minutes to allow the vegetables to soften slightly.

  4. Return the browned meat to the pot and pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). The liquid should just cover the ingredients in the pot. If needed, add more broth or water.

  5. Add the bay leaves and dried thyme to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir everything together to combine the flavors.

  6. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the stew to simmer for at least 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  7. About 10 minutes before serving, stir in the frozen peas. This will add a pop of color and freshness to the stew.

  8. Remove the bay leaves before serving. Ladle the stew into bowls and enjoy with a crusty bread or a side of mashed potatoes.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can brown the meat in batches instead of all at once. This will give the stew a deeper, richer taste.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of flour with some water to make a slurry. Stir this into the stew during the last 30 minutes of cooking to thicken the broth.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables to the stew, such as mushrooms or parsnips. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure that everything is cooked to perfection.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or frozen for up to 3 months. Just be sure to thaw and reheat thoroughly before enjoying.

Conclusion

This hearty and comforting one pot beef stew is the perfect dish to warm you up on a cold day. With its tender meat, flavorful broth, and assortment of vegetables, it’s a complete meal that will leave you feeling satisfied and content. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or a crowd, this stew is sure to be a hit. So grab your favorite pot, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a bowl of pure comfort.
