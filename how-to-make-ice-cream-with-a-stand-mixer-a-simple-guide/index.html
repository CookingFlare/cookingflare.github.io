How to Make Ice Cream with a Stand Mixer: A Simple Guide


Ice cream is one of the most beloved desserts around the world, bringing joy to both casual summer picnics and elegant dinner parties. While store-bought options are convenient, there’s nothing quite like the taste of homemade ice cream. Creating your own frozen treat allows you to experiment with flavors, control the ingredients, and truly savor the satisfaction of knowing you made it yourself. Surprisingly, with a stand mixer, making creamy and delicious ice cream is easier than you might think. Let’s dive into this simple guide and show you how to whip up this delightful dessert in your own kitchen.

Why Use a Stand Mixer for Ice Cream?

If the thought of needing an ice cream maker has deterred you from trying to make ice cream at home, there’s good news: a stand mixer is a versatile tool that can handle the job effectively. With the right method, a stand mixer can churn out smooth, creamy ice cream in a matter of hours. Its powerful motor and a variety of attachments make it ideal for blending ingredients and aerating your mixture to achieve that coveted creamy texture. Plus, many premium stand mixers offer optional ice cream bowl attachments, making the process even more seamless.

Even without any special attachments, you can still create a freezer-friendly version of ice cream by leveraging your stand mixer’s whisk or paddle attachment. Not only will you save space by avoiding an additional appliance, but you’ll also discover a new use for an essential piece of kitchen equipment.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of homemade ice cream is that the ingredients are simple and can easily be customized based on your taste preferences. Below is a base recipe that serves as a starting point. You can add flavors, mix-ins, or toppings to make it uniquely yours.

Base Ice Cream Recipe:

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Optional Add-Ins:

  • Chocolate chips, nuts, or fruit pieces
  • Flavorings like cocoa powder, almond extract, or espresso
  • Swirls of caramel, fudge, or fruit preserves

This basic vanilla base is incredibly flexible, giving you the freedom to explore endless flavor combinations.

Tools You’ll Need

Here’s what you’ll need to prepare your ice cream using your stand mixer:

  1. Stand Mixer: Any model will do, but having a whisk or paddle attachment is ideal.
  2. Large Mixing Bowl: Preferably a stainless-steel bowl that chills quickly.
  3. Freezer-Proof Container: To store and harden the ice cream.
  4. Plastic Wrap: Helps prevent ice crystals from forming on the surface.
  5. Spatula: For scraping down the sides of the bowl.

Optional tools include a thermometer to check the temperature of the custard base, if you’re making a cooked ice cream base.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Ice Cream with a Stand Mixer

Ready to get started? Follow these easy steps to make your first batch of stand mixer ice cream.

Step 1: Chill Your Tools and Ingredients

Start by placing your stand mixer bowl and attachments in the freezer for at least 15 to 20 minutes before preparing the recipe. This helps to keep the mixture cold as you work. Chilled tools are especially helpful when the weather is warm or if your kitchen tends to run hot. It’s also a good idea to ensure that your heavy cream and milk are cold straight from the refrigerator.

Step 2: Make the Ice Cream Base

Combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt in your chilled mixing bowl. Using the whisk attachment of your stand mixer, blend the ingredients on medium speed for about 2-3 minutes, or until the sugar dissolves completely. If you’re making a basic no-cook ice cream, this step finishes your base.

For a cooked base, which is often a custard-style ice cream, heat the milk, cream, sugar, and a pinch of salt in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Stir the mixture occasionally, taking care not to let it boil. Once the mixture is hot, temper the eggs (if you’re using them for added richness) by slowly whisking a bit of the hot liquid into beaten egg yolks, then returning the tempered yolks to the saucepan. Cook until the custard thickens and coats the back of a spoon. Allow it to cool completely in the refrigerator before moving to the next step.

Step 3: Whip the Base (Optional Technique)

For a lighter, airier texture, you can whip the ice cream base slightly in the stand mixer before freezing. Using the whisk attachment, increase the mixer speed and whisk for 2-3 minutes until the mixture takes on some volume. This step is optional but can help create a creamier end result.

Step 4: Freeze and Churn

Pour your prepared base into a freezer-proof container and place it in the freezer for 30-40 minutes, allowing it to start firming up slightly around the edges. Once the mixture begins to set, remove it from the freezer and transfer it back into the chilled mixing bowl of your stand mixer. Using the paddle attachment, mix on low to medium speed for 3-4 minutes, scraping down the sides of the bowl as necessary. This process aerates the mixture and breaks up any ice crystals.

Repeat this step every 30 minutes for about 2-3 hours. While this manual churning method requires a bit more hands-on time, the results are well worth it. The frequent mixing helps achieve an even texture and prevents large ice crystals from forming.

Step 5: Incorporate Add-Ins

During the last churn, fold in your desired mix-ins such as chocolate chips, cookie crumbles, or fruit pieces. Be careful not to overmix—using a spatula to gently incorporate the add-ins works best. This step is where you can truly get creative and customize your ice cream.

Step 6: Final Freeze

Transfer the churned mixture into a freezer-proof container, smoothing the surface with a spatula. Cover the container tightly with plastic wrap to prevent freezer burn, then place it in the freezer for 3-4 hours (or overnight) to fully harden. If you have a lot of mix-ins, you can stir the ice cream gently a few times during this phase to evenly distribute them.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once the ice cream is set, scoop it into bowls or cones, and enjoy! If the ice cream is too hard straight from the freezer, let it sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften slightly. Garnish with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or a drizzle of chocolate sauce to elevate your presentation.

Tips for Success

  • Experiment with Flavors: Try replacing vanilla extract with other flavorings like peppermint, almond, or citrus zest. The possibilities are endless!
  • Keep Things Cool: Work in a cool environment and avoid overhandling the mixture to prevent it from warming up too quickly.
  • Layer for Fun: Alternate layers of the ice cream base with swirls of fudge, caramel, or fruit puree for a striking effect when scooped.
  • Make It Ahead: Homemade ice cream can be stored in the freezer for up to 2 weeks in an airtight container.

Conclusion

Making ice cream with a stand mixer is not only simple but also incredibly rewarding. With just a handful of ingredients and some creativity, you can create a delectable dessert that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you stick to classic vanilla or branch out into adventurous flavors, the process will leave you with a new appreciation for this timeless treat. So, grab your stand mixer, and start churning out ice cream that will impress family, friends, and maybe even yourself!
