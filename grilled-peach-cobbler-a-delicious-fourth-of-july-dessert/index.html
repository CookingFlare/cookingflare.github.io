Grilled Peach Cobbler: A Delicious Fourth of July Dessert


The Fourth of July is a time for celebration, and what better way to mark the occasion than with a mouthwatering dessert that captures the essence of summer? Grilled peach cobbler is a delightful twist on the classic cobbler recipe, infusing the sweet and juicy flavors of fresh peaches with a hint of smokiness from the grill. This dessert is perfect for outdoor gatherings, barbecues, or simply indulging in a delightful treat on a warm summer evening. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a sensational grilled peach cobbler that will leave your guests craving more.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 6-8 ripe peaches, pitted and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Zest of one lemon
  • Juice of one lemon

For the Topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and diced
  • 1/4 cup boiling water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Grill:

    • Preheat your grill to medium heat, around 350°F (175°C). Ensure that the grates are clean and lightly greased to prevent sticking.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Gently toss the ingredients together until the peaches are well coated. Set the filling aside to allow the flavors to meld while you prepare the topping.

  3. Prepare the Topping:

    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the diced butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to blend the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. Pour the boiling water over the topping and stir until just combined. Set the topping aside.

  4. Assemble the Cobbler:

    • Evenly spread the peach filling in a disposable aluminum foil pan or a cast-iron skillet. Spoon the topping over the peaches, covering the surface as evenly as possible.

  5. Grill the Cobbler:

    • Carefully place the pan or skillet on the preheated grill. Close the lid and grill the cobbler for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is bubbly, and the topping is golden brown and cooked through. Rotate the pan halfway through the cooking time to ensure even grilling.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Remove the grilled peach cobbler from the grill and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, topped with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream.

Conclusion

Grilled peach cobbler is a delightful combination of summer’s finest flavors, enhanced by the subtle smokiness from the grill. Whether you’re celebrating the Fourth of July with friends and family or simply savoring the joys of the season, this dessert is sure to impress. The tender, caramelized peaches combined with the buttery, golden topping create a delectable treat that embodies the spirit of summer. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to the irresistible taste of grilled peach cobbler. Happy grilling and happy Fourth of July!
