Croquetas de Jamon: Bite-Sized Delights from Spain

3 min · 519 words

If you’ve ever had the pleasure of visiting Spain or dining at a Spanish restaurant, you’ve likely encountered the delightful and irresistible croquetas de jamon. These bite-sized, golden nuggets of goodness are a staple of Spanish cuisine, offering a crispy exterior that gives way to a creamy, flavorful filling. In this blog post, we’re going to delve into the art of making croquetas de jamon at home, so you can savor the taste of Spain right in your own kitchen.

The History of Croquetas de Jamon

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the history of croquetas de jamon. This beloved Spanish dish has its roots in the traditional croquette, which is a small, cylindrical roll of minced meat, fish, or vegetables, coated in breadcrumbs and deep-fried. The concept of croquettes is said to have originated in France, but they quickly made their way to Spain, where they were embraced and transformed into the croquetas we know and love today.

Croquetas de jamon, specifically, are believed to have gained popularity in Spain during the 19th century. The use of Jamon Serrano, a type of dry-cured Spanish ham, gives these croquetas their distinct flavor and sets them apart from other variations.

The Recipe: Making Croquetas de Jamon at Home

Now, let’s get down to the business of recreating these delectable morsels in your own kitchen. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup finely chopped Jamon Serrano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bechamel Sauce: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and cook, stirring constantly, for about 2 minutes to form a roux. Gradually whisk in the milk, ensuring that no lumps form. Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture thickens to a smooth, creamy consistency. This is your bechamel sauce.

  2. Add the Jamon Serrano: Stir in the finely chopped Jamon Serrano and season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Remove the pan from heat and transfer the mixture to a shallow dish. Let it cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until firm.

  3. Shape the Croquetas: Once the mixture has chilled and firmed up, use a spoon to scoop out small portions and roll them into bite-sized cylinders. You can also use your hands to shape the mixture into round croquettes.

  4. Coat the Croquetas: In a shallow bowl, beat the eggs. Place the breadcrumbs in another shallow bowl. Dip each croqueta into the beaten eggs, then roll it in the breadcrumbs until evenly coated.

  5. Fry the Croquetas: In a deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated croquetas in batches, being mindful not to overcrowd the skillet. Fry the croquetas until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides, then transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once all the croquetas are fried, arrange them on a serving platter and garnish with a sprinkle of sea salt. These croquetas de jamon are best enjoyed hot, so serve them immediately and watch them disappear in no time!

Conclusion

Congratulations, you’ve just mastered the art of making croquetas de jamon! These savory, creamy, and crispy delights are sure to be a hit at your next gathering or as a special treat for yourself. Whether you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary repertoire, these bite-sized delights from Spain are a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor the taste of Spain right in your own home. Buena suerte!
