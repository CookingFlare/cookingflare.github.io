10 Quick Indian Snacks to Satisfy Your Cravings


Indian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, aromas, and vibrant colors. The country's diverse regions offer a wide array of quick and delicious snacks that can be easily prepared at home. Whether you're a foodie or a busy professional, these snacks are perfect for satisfying your cravings and nourishing your body. Here are ten quick Indian snacks to try, each with its unique flavor profile and texture.

  1. Masala Fritters These spicy fritters are made with a mixture of gram flour, spices, and herbs. Sliced onions, garlic, and chilies add a punch of flavor, while a dash of lemon juice brings everything together. Serve them as a side dish or as a snack with a cup of tea. Recipe:

1 cup gram flour 1/2 cup chopped onions 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander 1-inch piece ginger, grated 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1/4 cup chopped cilantro Vegetable oil for frying

Mix all the ingredients together, except for the cilantro, to form a smooth batter. Heat oil in a frying pan and drop the batter by spoonfuls. Fry until golden brown, then garnish with cilantro. 2. Puris with Aloo Masala Puris are crispy, deep-fried bread discs that pair perfectly with a spicy potato filling. Boil diced potatoes, then sauté them with onions, garlic, and a blend of spices. Add some garam masala and cumin powder to give it an extra boost. Recipe:

2 large potatoes, peeled and diced 1 large onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder Salt to taste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 4-6 puris (store-bought or homemade)

Boil the potatoes until tender, then mash. Sauté the onions and garlic, followed by the spices and potatoes. Spread the aloo masala on the puris and enjoy. 3. Dal Pakora This crispy fritter is made from a mixture of lentils and vegetables. Dip a slice of lime in the fritter for an extra burst of citrus flavor. Serve it with a dollop of chutney or yogurt. Recipe:

1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal) 1 tablespoon gram flour 1/2 cup chopped vegetables (onions, carrots, and bell peppers) 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon salt Vegetable oil for frying Lime slices for garnish Chutney or yogurt for serving

Mix all the ingredients together to form a smooth batter. Heat oil in a frying pan and drop the batter by spoonfuls. Fry until golden brown, then garnish with cilantro and lime. 4. Methi Nashta This snack is made from the fragrant leaves of fenugreek, cooked with onions, garlic, and spices. Serve it with a cup of tea or as a side dish. Recipe:

1 cup fenugreek leaves (methi) 2 tablespoons gram flour 1/4 cup chopped onions 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder Salt to taste Vegetable oil for frying

Chop the methi leaves and mix with the gram flour, onions, and spices. Heat oil in a frying pan and cook until the leaves are crispy, then serve. 5. Let's continue with the recipes: 6. Samosa Chaat Samosas are a popular Indian snack that can be enjoyed in various ways. Here's a recipe for a samosa chaat, a flavorful and crunchy snack that combines the goodness of samosas with a variety of chutneys and toppings. Recipe:

12 samosas 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 2 tablespoons nimbu (lemon) chutney 2 tablespoons green chutney 1 tablespoon tamarind chutney 1 tablespoon mint-coriander chutney 1/4 cup sev (fried grams) 1/4 cup Phallazong (spicy chickpea snack) Salt to taste Vegetable oil for frying

Fry the samosas until crispy and golden brown. Serve with a combination of chutneys and toppings. 7. Khichdi Khichdi is a comforting Indian dish made from a mixture of rice and lentils. It's a great snack to prepare when you're short on time. Recipe:

1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal) 1 cup basmati rice 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter) 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt to taste Water as needed

Combine the lentils and rice in a pot and cook until the lentils are soft. Add the spices and ghee, and mix well. 8. Pani Puri Pani puri is a popular Indian street food that consists of crispy, hollow puris filled with a flavorful mixture of tamarind chutney, mint-coriander chutney, and spices. Recipe:

12 puris 1 cup tamarind chutney 1 cup mint-coriander chutney 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 1/4 cup pani puri masala (available at Indian grocery stores) Salt to taste

Fill the puris with a mixture of tamarind chutney, mint-coriander chutney, and pani puri masala. 9. Raj Kachori Raj kachori is a popular Indian snack that consists of a crispy kachori (deep-fried bread) filled with a spicy mixture of potatoes, onions, and chutneys. Recipe:

12 kachoris 1/2 cup boiled and mashed potatoes 1/2 cup chopped onions 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 2 tablespoons green chutney 2 tablespoons tamarind chutney 1 tablespoon mint-coriander chutney Salt to taste Vegetable oil for frying

Fry the kachoris until crispy and golden brown. Fill with a mixture of potatoes, onions, and chutneys. 10. Bhaat aur Bhajji Bhaat aur bhajji is a simple snack made from a mixture of cooked rice and deep-fried vegetables, served with a flavorful chutney. Recipe:

1 cup cooked basmati rice 1 cup mixed vegetables (onions, carrots, green beans, and bell peppers) 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 onion, chopped 1 tablespoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt to taste 1 tablespoon tamarind chutney 1 tablespoon mint-coriander chutney

Heat the oil in a pan and sauté the vegetables until tender. Add the onion, cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt, and mix well. Combine the cooked rice and mixture in a bowl. Serve with a side of tamarind and mint-coriander chutneys.
