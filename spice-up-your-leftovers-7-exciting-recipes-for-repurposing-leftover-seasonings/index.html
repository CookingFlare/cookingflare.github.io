Spice Up Your Leftovers: 7 Exciting Recipes for Repurposing Leftover Seasonings


We’ve all been there - you cook a delicious meal, only to be left with a bunch of leftover seasonings sitting in your pantry. Instead of letting them go to waste, why not get creative and spice up your leftovers? In this post, we’ll share seven exciting recipes that will help you repurpose those leftover seasonings and transform your meals into something truly special. Let’s dive in!

1. Seasoned Roasted Veggies

Leftover herb and spice blends are perfect for adding an extra kick to your roasted vegetables. Simply toss your favorite veggies - think carrots, broccoli, and bell peppers - with olive oil, salt, and the leftover seasonings. Roast them in the oven until they’re tender and golden brown. The result? A flavorful side dish that will have your taste buds dancing.

2. Savory Spice Rub

If you have a leftover spice blend that’s too good to waste, turn it into a versatile spice rub. Mix the seasoning with a bit of olive oil or melted butter to create a paste. Rub it onto your favorite meat, whether it’s chicken, pork, or beef, and let it marinate for a few hours or overnight. Then, cook the meat as you normally would. The result? A succulent and flavorful dish that will impress your family and friends.

3. Spiced Up Oatmeal

Who says oatmeal has to be boring? Take your plain bowl of oats to the next level by adding a pinch of your leftover spice blend. Whether it’s cinnamon, nutmeg, or even a savory blend, the spices will add a depth of flavor that will make your breakfast truly satisfying. Top it off with some fresh fruits or nuts for an extra crunch.

4. Flavorful Salad Dressing

Leftover dried herbs and spices can make a fantastic addition to your homemade salad dressings. Combine your favorite oil, vinegar, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a teaspoon of your chosen spices in a jar. Shake it vigorously until the ingredients are well combined. Pour the dressing over your salad, and you’ll have a unique and flavorful creation that will make your taste buds sing.

5. Spicy Popcorn

If you’re a fan of popcorn, why not give it a kick with your leftover seasonings? Melt some butter and drizzle it over a bowl of freshly popped popcorn. Sprinkle your chosen spice blend over the top and toss it well to ensure every kernel is coated. It’s a simple yet satisfying snack that will have you reaching for seconds.

6. Infused Olive Oil

Leftover dried herbs can be used to infuse olive oil, adding a burst of flavor to your dishes. Place a handful of herbs in a bottle or jar and fill it with olive oil. Let it sit for a few days to allow the flavors to meld together. Use this infused oil as a finishing touch for your salads, pasta dishes, or even drizzled over a slice of crusty bread.

7. Flavored Butter

Last but not least, use your leftover seasonings to create a delicious flavored butter. Soften a stick of unsalted butter and mix in your chosen seasonings. Whether it’s garlic, herbs, or even a spicy blend, the butter will absorb the flavors beautifully. Roll it into a log using parchment paper and refrigerate until firm. Spread it on warm bread, melt it over grilled steaks, or use it to baste roasted chicken for an extra burst of flavor.

With these seven exciting recipes, you’ll never have to let your leftover seasonings go to waste again. Get creative in the kitchen and explore new flavor combinations that will elevate your meals to new heights. So, the next time you find yourself with leftover seasonings, don’t toss them aside - repurpose them and spice up your culinary adventures!
