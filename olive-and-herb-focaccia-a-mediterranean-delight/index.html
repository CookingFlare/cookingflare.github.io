Olive and Herb Focaccia: A Mediterranean Delight


Focaccia, a delectable flatbread native to Italy, has been loved for centuries for its crispy, pillowy texture and delightful aroma. This Mediterranean delight can be easily enhanced with a medley of fragrant herbs, juicy olives and a drizzle of olive oil. In this post, we’ll dive into the history and cultural significance of focaccia, share a scrumptious olive and herb focaccia recipe, and provide tips for achieving success every time.

History and Cultural Significance of Focaccia

The first evidence of focaccia-like bread being prepared dates back to Ancient Rome. However, it wasn’t until the 16th century that recipes for focaccia started resembling the version we know and love today. Focaccia originated in Genoa, Italy, where it remains an iconic local specialty. This easy-to-share flatbread has since its inception, been enjoyed in many Mediterranean countries and has naturally adapted to reflect regional flavors and ingredients.

Focaccia has long been a common and versatile dish. In Italian culture, it is viewed as a poor man’s bread, as it could be made from leftover ingredients like stale or low-quality flour. Yet, over the years, this simple yet flavorful bread has worked its way into Italian gastronomy and travel pamphlets alike.

Olive and Herb Focaccia Recipe

Now that we’ve given you a taste of focaccia’s fascinating history, let’s dive into the scrumptious and flavorful olive and herb version! This recipe serves about 6-8 as a side dish or appetizer. You’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup of warm water
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 2 large loaf-sized eggs
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 ½ tablespoons potato starch
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh oregano
  • 1 cup pitted and halved Kalamata olives
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Stir until dissolved, and let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes.
  2. Crack in the eggs, add olive oil, and approximately 2 cups of the flour. Mix well to combine.
  3. Incorporate the potato starch into the dough mixture. Your dough should be soft and sticky.
  4. Allow the dough to rest in the bowl for approximately 30 minutes, covered with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap. This step allows the dough to relax and become easy to work with.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the rosemary, oregano, and olives.
  6. On a floured surface, gently knead the remaining 1 cup of flour into the dough. Fold the dough over itself, rotate, and continue kneading until smooth and elastic. Remember to be gentle to avoid creating air bubbles.
  7. Stretch the dough to a 12x18-inch oval shape, then spread the olive and herb mixture evenly over the surface of the dough.
  8. Drizzle with a little extra-virgin olive oil and top with a generous pinch of salt.
  9. Bake in a preheated 425°F (220°C) oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center is cooked through. You may also use a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet for a crispy, stone oven-like texture.
  10. Enjoy your homemade olive and herb focaccia warm with family and friends!

Tips for Success

Achieving perfect focaccia doesn’t have to be rocket science. With these simple tips, you’ll be on your way to focaccia heaven!

  1. Use high-quality ingredients: The quality of your ingredients will directly affect the flavor and texture of your final product. Choose fresh, flavorful ingredients such as high-quality extra-virgin olive oil and fresh herbs.
  2. Mix and mold the dough gently: Overworking the dough might lead to a tough and heavy final result. Be gentle when kneading and folding the dough.
  3. Relax your dough: Giving the dough time to rest allows the gluten to relax, resulting in a more pliable and malleable surface for shaping.
  4. Preheat the oven and the baking stone: If using a baking stone, preheat it in the oven while you’re preparing the dough. This step ensures a crispy crust.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning: Taste the dough as you’re working through the recipe and make adjustments as needed. This is especially important for the salt, as different ovens may require a little more or less.

Focaccia is a versatile bread that can be modified with endless combinations of herbs, vegetables, cheeses, and other additions. Savor the simplicity and pure flavors of a classic olive and herb version. Perfect as a snack, a side dish, or a crowd-pleasing appetizer, there’s no denying the appeal of this Mediterranean delight.
