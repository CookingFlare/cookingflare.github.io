Indulge in the Delicate Goodness of Japanese Souffle Cheesecake


If you’re a dessert lover, you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to explore the ethereal world of Japanese Souffle Cheesecake. With its delicate texture, creamy flavor, and melt-in-your-mouth goodness, this dessert is sure to captivate your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

The Art of Japanese Souffle Cheesecake

Japanese Souffle Cheesecake is a unique twist on the classic cheesecake. It originated in Japan and has gained popularity worldwide for its light and fluffy texture. Unlike traditional cheesecakes, this heavenly creation incorporates a delicate souffle-like consistency, making it a delightful experience for both the eyes and the palate.

The Ingredients

To create this delectable dessert, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 200g cream cheese
  • 50g unsalted butter
  • 100ml milk
  • 6 large eggs, separated
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 60g cake flour
  • 20g cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

The Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 320°F (160°C). Grease a round cake pan and line the bottom with parchment paper.

  2. In a heatproof bowl, melt the cream cheese, butter, and milk over a double boiler. Stir until smooth and well combined. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and 50g of sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually add the cream cheese mixture, whisking continuously. Stir in the lemon juice and vanilla extract.

  4. Sift the cake flour and cornstarch into the mixture and gently fold until well incorporated.

  5. In a clean and dry bowl, beat the egg whites with a pinch of salt until frothy. Gradually add the remaining 50g of sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.

  6. Take one-third of the beaten egg whites and fold it into the cream cheese mixture to lighten the batter. Then, carefully fold in the remaining egg whites until no streaks remain.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

  8. Place the cake pan into a larger baking dish. Fill the larger dish with hot water, creating a water bath for the cake. This will help to ensure even baking and prevent cracking.

  9. Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for approximately 50-60 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cake is set. The center may still be slightly jiggly, but it will firm up as it cools.

  10. Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, carefully transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  11. Once cooled, refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and for the texture to become even more airy and delicate.

  12. When ready to serve, dust the top of the cheesecake with powdered sugar for an elegant finishing touch.

The Finishing Touch

Now that your Japanese Souffle Cheesecake is ready, it’s time to indulge in its delicate goodness. Slice a piece and savor the light and airy texture, combined with the creamy and subtly sweet flavor. Each bite is a heavenly experience that will transport you to a world of dessert bliss.

So, whether you’re a cheesecake aficionado or simply looking to try something new, give this Japanese Souffle Cheesecake recipe a try. It’s a delightful dessert that is sure to impress your family and friends. Bon appétit!
