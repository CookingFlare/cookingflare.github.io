Instant Pot Mac and Cheese: A Creamy Comfort Meal


Mac and cheese is one of those comfort foods that’s always welcome, whether it’s a chilly evening or a busy weeknight. But let’s face it, traditional mac and cheese can be a bit time-consuming when you’re trying to get dinner on the table in a hurry. That’s where the Instant Pot comes in, turning this classic dish into a quick and creamy delight that’s perfect for any occasion. In this post, we’ll walk you through a simple yet delicious Instant Pot mac and cheese recipe that will have your family asking for seconds.

The Magic of the Instant Pot

The Instant Pot has revolutionized the way we cook, especially when it comes to dishes that typically take a long time to prepare. The pressure cooker function allows ingredients to cook quickly and evenly, infusing flavors while keeping everything tender and moist. When it comes to mac and cheese, this kitchen gadget works like a charm, delivering a rich, creamy sauce and perfectly cooked pasta without the usual hassle.

Why Instant Pot Mac and Cheese?

You might be wondering, “What makes Instant Pot mac and cheese different from stovetop versions?” The answer is simple: convenience and creaminess. The Instant Pot allows you to cook the pasta and the cheese sauce together in one pot, eliminating the need for multiple cooking steps. No need to boil the pasta separately and then prepare the cheese sauce—everything comes together in a single pot, making clean-up a breeze.

Another great benefit is the ability to customize the recipe to your liking. Whether you prefer sharp cheddar, creamy mozzarella, or a blend of both, you can tweak the cheese choices to suit your tastes. Plus, you can add in extra ingredients like bacon, garlic, or herbs to elevate the flavor even further.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

Here’s what you’ll need to make a creamy and indulgent batch of Instant Pot mac and cheese:

For the Mac and Cheese Base:

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni (or your favorite pasta shape)
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (unsalted)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder (optional, for tang)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (freshly ground is best)

For the Cheese Sauce:

  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese (or a combination of cheddar and mozzarella for a creamier texture)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup whole milk (or heavy cream for an extra indulgent dish)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream (optional, for added creaminess)

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1/2 cup cooked bacon (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup sautéed onions or garlic
  • Chopped herbs like parsley or thyme (for garnish)

For the Top (if desired):

  • Bread crumbs (for a crispy topping)
  • More shredded cheese (for melting on top)

How to Make Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

Now that we have everything prepared, let’s walk through the steps to make this mouthwatering dish in your Instant Pot.

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics (Optional)

If you’re adding bacon, garlic, or onions to your mac and cheese, start by sautéing them in the Instant Pot. Turn the Instant Pot to the Sauté function and add a little oil or butter. Once it’s heated, add your ingredients and cook them until they’re crispy or fragrant.

For example, crispy bacon adds a savory crunch, while garlic and onions bring a deeper, sweeter flavor. Once they’re ready, remove them from the pot and set them aside for later.

Step 2: Cook the Pasta

With your sautéed ingredients out of the way, it’s time to cook the pasta. Add the elbow macaroni (or your pasta choice) into the Instant Pot, then pour in the chicken broth. Make sure the pasta is submerged in the liquid. Season with garlic powder, mustard powder, salt, and pepper, and stir it all together.

Now, secure the Instant Pot lid and ensure the valve is set to Sealing. Use the Manual or Pressure Cook function on high pressure and set the timer for 4 minutes. It will take a few minutes for the Instant Pot to come up to pressure before the cooking time starts.

Step 3: Quick Release the Pressure

Once the cooking time is up, carefully perform a Quick Release by turning the valve to Venting. Be cautious of the hot steam! After the pressure has been fully released, open the lid, and you’ll find your pasta perfectly cooked and absorbed much of the liquid.

Step 4: Make the Creamy Cheese Sauce

Now for the magic—turn the Instant Pot to the Sauté function once again. Add in the butter and let it melt into the hot pasta. This will help create a luscious base for your cheese sauce.

Next, pour in the milk (or heavy cream for extra richness) and stir until combined. Add the shredded cheddar cheese, Parmesan cheese, and sour cream, stirring continuously until the cheese has melted into a smooth, velvety sauce. If the sauce seems a little too thick, add a splash more milk to reach your desired consistency.

Step 5: Stir in the Add-ins

This is the perfect time to add any optional ingredients. If you’ve got crispy bacon, garlic, or sautéed onions, stir them into the mix now. They’ll add a delicious layer of flavor that will complement the creamy cheese sauce.

If you like your mac and cheese a little extra indulgent, feel free to top it with more shredded cheese or a handful of breadcrumbs for a crispy topping.

Step 6: Final Stir and Serve

Once everything is well combined and your mac and cheese is creamy and smooth, give it one last stir. Taste for seasoning, and add more salt or pepper if necessary.

Your Instant Pot mac and cheese is now ready to serve! Ladle it into bowls and garnish with fresh herbs, extra cheese, or crispy bacon if you like. It’s the perfect comfort food, whether you’re enjoying a cozy night in or feeding a crowd.

Tips for the Best Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

  • Use good quality cheese: The cheese is the star of the dish, so don’t skimp on it. Choose a sharp cheddar or your favorite melty cheese for the best results. Freshly grated cheese will melt better than pre-shredded cheese, which often contains anti-caking agents.

  • Adjust the consistency: If you prefer your mac and cheese a bit thicker, use less liquid. If you like it saucier, don’t hesitate to add more milk or cream to achieve your preferred texture.

  • Make it your own: Customize your Instant Pot mac and cheese by adding extras like sautéed mushrooms, spinach, or cooked sausage. The Instant Pot makes it easy to incorporate ingredients that are already cooked, so feel free to experiment.

  • Don’t skip the Quick Release: For the best texture, perform a Quick Release after cooking the pasta. This prevents the pasta from overcooking or becoming mushy.

Can I Make This Ahead of Time?

Yes, you can definitely prepare Instant Pot mac and cheese ahead of time! If you want to make it in advance, cook it as directed, but don’t add the milk and cheese just yet. Store the cooked pasta in an airtight container in the fridge.

When you’re ready to serve, heat it up in the Instant Pot with the milk and cheese, and stir until it’s hot and creamy. This way, you’ll have a delicious, homemade mac and cheese ready to go in no time.

Can I Freeze Instant Pot Mac and Cheese?

Instant Pot mac and cheese freezes well, though it’s best enjoyed fresh for optimal creaminess. If you want to freeze it, follow these steps:

  1. Let the mac and cheese cool completely.
  2. Transfer it to an airtight container or freezer-safe bag.
  3. Store in the freezer for up to 2-3 months.

To reheat, thaw it in the fridge overnight, then reheat in the Instant Pot with a little extra milk to help loosen the sauce.

Final Thoughts

Instant Pot mac and cheese is the ultimate comfort food made easy. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner, a potluck, or just a quick meal to satisfy those cheesy cravings, this recipe will never disappoint. By using the Instant Pot, you’ll save time while still getting that creamy, dreamy mac and cheese everyone loves. So go ahead, grab your Instant Pot, and treat yourself to a delicious, homemade mac and cheese tonight!
