Mexican Chiles Rellenos Stuffed with Cheese


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’ve likely come across the delightful dish known as Chiles Rellenos. This traditional Mexican recipe features poblano peppers stuffed with a savory cheese filling, coated in a light, airy batter, and fried to golden perfection. The combination of the mildly spicy poblano pepper and the gooey, melted cheese creates a flavor explosion that will leave your taste buds craving more. In this post, I’ll guide you through the process of creating authentic Mexican Chiles Rellenos in your own kitchen, so you can treat yourself to a fiesta of flavors anytime you want.

Ingredients

For the Filling

  • 4 large poblano peppers
  • 2 cups of Oaxaca cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup of cotija cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup of white onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, seeded and diced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Batter

  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt

For Frying

  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Peppers

    • Place the poblano peppers on a baking sheet and broil them in the oven, turning occasionally, until the skins are charred and blistered. This process will take about 8-10 minutes. Once done, transfer the peppers to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let them steam for 15 minutes. After steaming, gently peel off the charred skins, remove the seeds, and make a lengthwise slit to create an opening for the filling.

  2. Prepare the Filling

    • In a mixing bowl, combine the Oaxaca cheese, cotija cheese, chopped onion, minced garlic, diced tomato, and chopped cilantro. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Carefully stuff each prepared poblano pepper with the cheese filling, ensuring they are evenly filled without overstuffing.

  3. Prepare the Batter

    • In a separate mixing bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. In another bowl, whisk the egg yolks until frothy. Gently fold the egg yolks into the beaten egg whites. Then, gradually fold in the flour and salt until the batter is smooth and well-combined.

  4. Fry the Chiles Rellenos

    • In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Dip each stuffed poblano pepper into the batter, ensuring it’s completely coated. Carefully place the battered peppers into the hot oil, and fry until they are golden brown on all sides. This process should take about 3-4 minutes per side. Once fried, transfer the Chiles Rellenos to a plate lined with paper towels to drain off any excess oil.

  5. Serve and Enjoy

    • Once the Chiles Rellenos are ready, serve them hot with your choice of side dishes, such as Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh salad. You can also top them with a flavorful salsa or a drizzle of crema for an extra touch of authenticity.

Conclusion

There you have it! With this recipe, you can bring the vibrant flavors of Mexico into your home kitchen. The combination of the smoky, earthy poblano peppers and the rich, creamy cheese filling makes Chiles Rellenos a truly irresistible dish. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Mexico, these Chiles Rellenos are sure to impress and satisfy. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create this iconic Mexican delight.
