Easter Recipes to Make with Leftover Ham


Easter is a time of fresh beginnings and celebration, but it often leaves many of us with a bounty of leftover ham. Whether you’ve hosted a big family gathering or enjoyed a cozy meal just for two, the challenge remains: what to do with all that perfectly cooked ham? Spoiler alert: you don’t have to eat ham sandwiches every day until it’s gone. There’s a whole world of creative and satisfying dishes waiting to transform your leftovers into culinary delights.

Let’s explore a variety of recipes that tease out the savory richness of leftover ham and turn it into quick meals, sophisticated dishes, and comforting classics. These recipes not only bring out the best in ham but also maximize your resources—no waste, just deliciousness.

1. Ham and Cheddar Breakfast Casserole

Breakfast casseroles are a fantastic option for using leftover ham, perfect for lazy weekend brunches or holiday mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cubed leftover ham
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 4 cups cubed bread (day-old work best)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional)
  • Butter for greasing the baking dish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss the cubed bread, ham, cheddar cheese, and sliced green onions together in the baking dish.
  4. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the bread and ham.
  5. Let it sit for about 10 minutes so the bread absorbs the custard.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the casserole is set and the top is golden brown.
  7. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

This casserole is a warm and hearty way to start the day, combining creamy eggs and rich ham with crisped cheddar.

2. Creamy Ham and Pea Pasta

A quick, creamy pasta that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner or a casual get-together.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta (penne, rigatoni, or your favorite)
  • 2 cups diced leftover ham
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add diced ham and cook until lightly browned, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in peas, then pour in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese.
  5. Simmer gently, stirring, until sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Add pasta to the skillet, tossing to coat evenly. Use reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  7. Season with salt and pepper, then garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This recipe is comfort food at its best—rich, creamy, and satisfying. Plus, peas add a pop of color and sweetness to complement the salty ham.

3. Ham and Potato Soup

When the weather turns cool after Easter, a steaming bowl of ham and potato soup hit the spot.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups diced leftover ham
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • Salt, pepper, and smoked paprika to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)
  • Chopped chives or green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add onion and garlic and cook until translucent.
  2. Add diced potatoes and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Add the ham and continue to cook for 5 more minutes.
  4. Using a potato masher or immersion blender, slightly mash some of the potatoes to thicken the soup while leaving some chunks.
  5. Stir in heavy cream, and season with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  6. Heat through but do not boil.
  7. Ladle into bowls, top with shredded cheese and chives if desired.

This hearty soup is a classic way to turn leftover ham and root vegetables into a soothing meal.

4. Ham and Swiss Sliders with Honey Mustard

Perfect for game day, potlucks, or casual family dinners, these sliders are an irresistible treat.

Ingredients:

  • 12 slider rolls or small hamburger buns
  • 2 cups sliced leftover ham
  • 12 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Slice slider rolls in half horizontally and place the bottom halves in a baking dish.
  3. Lay ham evenly over the bottoms, then add Swiss cheese slices.
  4. Place the top halves of the rolls on top.
  5. In a small bowl, mix melted butter, Dijon mustard, honey, Worcestershire sauce, and poppy seeds.
  6. Brush the honey mustard mixture generously over the tops of the rolls.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for about 15 minutes or until the cheese is melted.
  8. Remove foil and bake for another 5-7 minutes until tops are golden and slightly crispy.

These sliders combine smoky ham with tangy honey mustard and melty cheese—simple finger food with big flavor.

5. Ham, Spinach, and Cheese Quiche

For a more elegant dish suitable for Easter brunch or an everyday meal, quiche is a versatile canvas for leftover ham.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade if preferred)
  • 1 1/2 cups diced leftover ham
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère or Swiss cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream or whole milk
  • Salt, pepper, and nutmeg to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Press the pie crust into a 9-inch pie pan and trim edges.
  3. Layer the ham, spinach, and cheese evenly in the crust.
  4. In a medium bowl, whisk together eggs, cream, salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the fillings in the crust.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and lightly golden.
  7. Let cool for a few minutes before slicing.

Quiche offers a sophisticated way to showcase ham with fresh vegetables and creamy texture—it’s great hot or cold.

6. Ham Fried Rice

A tasty quick-fix that’s perfect for those looking for a savory, Asian-inspired dish with minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked and chilled white rice
  • 1 1/2 cups diced leftover ham
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add garlic and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add diced ham and vegetables, sauté for a few minutes.
  4. Push ham and veggies to the side of the skillet and pour beaten eggs into the empty side.
  5. Scramble the eggs until set and then mix everything together.
  6. Add rice, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine and heat through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper, garnish with green onions.

Ham fried rice is an incredibly flexible dish, allowing you to toss in any veggies or sauces you like, making it a perfect use for leftover ham.

7. Ham and Pineapple Pizza

Turn your leftover ham into a crowd-pleasing pizza that balances salty, sweet, and cheesy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza dough (or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce or marinara
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup diced leftover ham
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned, drained)
  • 1/4 cup sliced red onions (optional)
  • Crushed red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven as directed on pizza dough instructions (usually 450°F/230°C).
  2. Roll out dough and place on a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  3. Spread pizza sauce evenly over the dough.
  4. Sprinkle half the mozzarella cheese, then add ham, pineapple, and red onion slices.
  5. Top with remaining cheese.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until crust is golden and cheese is melted.
  7. Allow to cool slightly, then sprinkle with red pepper flakes if desired.

This pizza takes the classic Hawaiian route with an easy twist, turning leftover ham into a cheesy, sweet, and savory meal.

8. Ham and Cheese Stuffed Mushrooms

A perfect appetizer or snack that’s easy to prepare and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 20 large white or cremini mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1 cup finely chopped leftover ham
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar or Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine ham, cream cheese, garlic, cheddar cheese, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon mixture into mushroom caps.
  4. Place stuffed mushrooms on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden on top.

These stuffed mushrooms are an elegant bite-sized way to enjoy leftover ham at your next gathering.

9. Split Pea and Ham Soup

An old-fashioned recipe that’s hearty, nutritious, and perfect for replenishing your family after festive days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried split peas, rinsed
  • 3 cups diced leftover ham
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onions, carrots, celery, and garlic until soft.
  2. Add split peas, ham, broth, and bay leaf.
  3. Bring to a boil and then simmer for 60-90 minutes or until peas are fully softened.
  4. Remove bay leaf and season with salt and pepper.
  5. For a creamier texture, blend half of the soup and stir back in.

Split pea and ham soup is timeless comfort food, bringing smoky depth and protein to every spoonful.

Final Thoughts

Leftover ham after Easter is a treasure trove of flavor that invites creativity. From breakfast casseroles that start your day right, to creamy pasta dishes, next-level soups, and crowd-pleasing snacks, the versatility of ham ensures you never run out of ideas. By mixing in fresh vegetables, bold seasonings, and comfort-food classics, you can transform one ingredient into numerous meals that delight your palate and stretch your budget.

Try these recipes and watch your leftover ham star in dishes that might just outshine the original Easter feast. Who knew that leftovers could be this exciting? Happy cooking!
