How to Make Classic Cocktails with Herbs and Spices


Crafting cocktails is an art that balances flavors, aromas, and presentation. While classic cocktails rely on spirits, mixers, and garnishes, herbs and spices elevate them to a whole new level, offering depth and complexity. Incorporating fresh herbs and aromatic spices into cocktail recipes not only enhances their taste but also adds a touch of sophistication. Today, we’ll guide you through the process of transforming classic cocktails into sensory experiences by introducing aromatic accents directly into your drink-making repertoire.

The Role of Herbs and Spices in Cocktails

Herbs and spices give cocktails an unmistakable edge. They play with bitterness, sweetness, and aromatic nuance to create a symphony of taste. Leading cocktail bars around the world embrace ingredients like basil, mint, rosemary, and star anise for their ability to transcend traditional mixers. Herbs like thyme or cilantro add earthiness, while spices such as cardamom or cinnamon create warmth and complexity.

Additionally, the visual appeal of fresh herbs or whole spices can make a drink more inviting and memorable. These ingredients are key to producing cocktails that engage all the senses, making each sip memorable.

Now, let’s dive straight into how to use these natural elements to recreate some timeless classics.

1. The Herbaceous Mojito

The mojito is beloved for its refreshing blend of mint, lime, sugar, and rum. A simple twist like swapping standard mint for basil or adding a sprig of thyme can revolutionize this cocktail.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz white rum
  • 1 oz freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 tsp white granulated sugar
  • 8-10 fresh mint leaves (swap with basil for variation)
  • 1 sprig of thyme (optional)
  • Club soda
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Begin by gently muddling the mint leaves, lime juice, and sugar in a sturdy glass or shaker. If you’re experimenting with basil or adding thyme, muddle those as well to release their essential oils. Be gentle here to avoid bitterness.
  2. Add the rum and fill the shaker with ice.
  3. Shake gently to combine but avoid over-diluting the flavors.
  4. Pour the contents into a tall glass and top with club soda.
  5. Garnish with a sprig of mint or basil on top to perfume the drink with freshness upon every sip.

This twist makes it a more sophisticated drink with subtle bursts of herbal nuances.

2. Spiced Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned has a timeless charm, but adding warmth through spices like cinnamon or cloves can give it depth perfect for cool evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1 sugar cube or ½ tsp simple syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • A pinch of ground clove or star anise
  • Orange peel twist for garnish
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. In an Old Fashioned glass, place the sugar cube or simple syrup and two dashes of bitters.
  2. Add the cinnamon stick and a pinch of ground clove or a piece of star anise. Muddle slightly to release their spicy aroma but avoid breaking the cinnamon stick entirely.
  3. Add a large ice cube and pour the whiskey over it.
  4. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves completely, and the drink is chilled.
  5. Garnish with an orange peel twist alongside the cinnamon stick for a visual and aromatic flourish.

This spiced variation brings complexity while maintaining the soul of this classic.

3. Lavender Gin Fizz

The Gin Fizz is a versatile cocktail that shines brightly on its own. Infusing it with the soft floral notes of dried or fresh lavender pushes it into a calming, aromatic territory.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz gin
  • ¾ oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • ¾ oz lavender simple syrup (made by simmering sugar, water, and lavender flowers)
  • 1 egg white (optional for foam)
  • Club soda
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. In a shaker, mix the gin, lemon juice, lavender simple syrup, and egg white if using for added froth.
  2. Shake vigorously without ice for 10-15 seconds to create an airy froth (dry shake).
  3. Add ice to the shaker and shake again to chill.
  4. Pour the mixture into a chilled glass and top with club soda for effervescence.
  5. Garnish with a sprig of fresh lavender or a twist of lemon.

This drink feels both vibrant and soothing, perfect for spring or summer soirées.

4. Rosemary Margarita

Margaritas are already zesty and vibrant, and the addition of rosemary introduces an earthy depth that balances the bright citrus flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz tequila
  • 1 oz freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 oz rosemary-infused simple syrup (made by steeping fresh rosemary in sugar and water)
  • Coarse sea salt for rimming the glass
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Begin by rimming the edge of a rocks glass with lime juice and dipping it into salt.
  2. In a shaker, combine the tequila, lime juice, and rosemary-infused simple syrup with ice. Shake well.
  3. Strain into the prepared glass filled with fresh ice.
  4. Garnish with a small rosemary sprig that’s been lightly toasted with a torch or flame to give it a lightly smoky aroma.

This herbal twist on a traditional margarita brings out the drink’s bright and earthy sides, perfect for pairing with spicy meals.

5. Spiced Pina Colada

The Pina Colada is the ultimate tropical escape, but incorporating warming spices like nutmeg or cinnamon can give it a holiday ambiance.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz light rum
  • 1 oz coconut cream
  • 2 oz pineapple juice
  • A pinch of grated nutmeg
  • 1 stick of cinnamon
  • Crushed ice

Instructions:

  1. Combine the rum, coconut cream, pineapple juice, and grated nutmeg in a blender with crushed ice. Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour into a chilled tropical-style glass.
  3. Garnish with a cinnamon stick or a dusting of nutmeg over the top. Optionally, you can add toasted coconut flakes for additional texture.

This warming iteration showcases how spices can redefine fruity cocktails, making them versatile for any season.

Tips for Working with Herbs and Spices

  • Muddle with Care: When muddling herbs, be gentle to avoid over-releasing tannins, which can add bitterness.
  • Choose Fresh Ingredients: Fresh herbs and high-quality whole spices yield brighter, cleaner flavors.
  • Balance is Key: Start small, adding herbs and spices incrementally to avoid overpowering the drink.
  • Infusions: Create herb- or spice-infused syrups, alcohols, or bitters for consistent and even flavor distribution.
  • Layer Aromatics: Use whole herbs or spices as garnishes to complement the flavors in the drink and provide visual appeal.

By incorporating fresh herbs and aromatic spices, these classic cocktails gain an undeniable allure, turning every drink into a multisensory experience. Experimenting with these recipes will not only refine your mixology skills but also impress your friends or guests with your ability to craft creative, elevated drinks. Whether it’s the herbal brightness of a basil mojito or the warm complexity of a spiced Old Fashioned, there’s no limit to the flavor combinations you can explore.
