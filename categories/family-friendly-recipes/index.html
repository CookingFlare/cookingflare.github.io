The beloved treat was invented in the late 1930s by Ruth Wakefield, who owned the Toll House Inn in Whitman, Massachusetts. Ruth was experimenting with her butter drop do cookie recipe when she decided to add chopped chocolate bars, expecting them to melt and disperse throughout the batter. Instead, the chocolate held its shape, and thus the chocolate chip cookie was born. Sweet and Satisfying: Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal Recipe for Breakfast

There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting bowl of oatmeal to start your day off right. And when you add the sweet and tangy flavors of apples and cinnamon into the mix, you’ve got a recipe for breakfast bliss. This Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal is not only delicious, but also packed with nutrients and fiber to keep you energized throughout the morning. So, let’s jump right into it! Ingredients 1 cup rolled oats 2 cups water 1 apple, peeled, cored, and chopped 1 tablespoon honey 1 teaspoon cinnamon A pinch of salt Optional toppings: chopped nuts, raisins, or a drizzle of maple syrup Instructions In a medium-sized saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the rolled oats and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the oats have absorbed most of the water and have reached your desired consistency, add the chopped apple, honey, cinnamon, and salt. Stir well to combine. Continue cooking for another 5 minutes, or until the apple has softened and the flavors have melded together. Remove from heat and let it sit for a minute to cool slightly. Serve the oatmeal in bowls and top with your favorite toppings, such as chopped nuts, raisins, or a drizzle of maple syrup. Enjoy your sweet and satisfying Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal while it’s still warm!
