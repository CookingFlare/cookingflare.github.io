Spicy and Sweet Thai Desserts: 5 Recipes to Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth


Thai cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, and this extends to its desserts as well. While many people associate Thai food with savory dishes, the world of Thai desserts is just as vibrant, often combining sweet and spicy elements that create a delightful experience for the palate. In this blog post, we’ll explore five tantalizing Thai dessert recipes that balance sweetness with a kick, perfect for those looking to indulge their sweet tooth in a unique way.

1. Mango Sticky Rice with a Spicy Twist

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup ripe mango, sliced
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1-2 Thai bird chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to spice preference)
  • Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain and steam the rice in a bamboo or metal steamer for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.

  2. Make the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, salt, and chopped bird chilies. Heat gently until the sugar dissolves, stirring frequently. Do not let it boil.

  3. Combine Rice and Sauce: Once the rice is cooked, place it in a bowl and pour about 1 cup of the coconut sauce over it. Stir gently to combine, and let it sit for about 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.

  4. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of the sticky rice topped with sliced mango. Drizzle with additional coconut sauce and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds for an added crunch.

This dessert beautifully balances the sweetness of the mango and coconut with a subtle heat from the chilies, making it a must-try!

2. Spicy Coconut Ice Cream

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (13.5 oz each) coconut milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1-2 teaspoons red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup crushed peanuts for topping

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the coconut milk, sugar, vanilla extract, salt, and red chili flakes. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved.

  2. Chill: Cover the bowl and refrigerate the mixture for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.

  3. Churn the Ice Cream: Pour the chilled mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  4. Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.

  5. Serve: Scoop the ice cream into bowls and top with crushed peanuts for a delightful crunch. The ice cream’s creamy coconut base perfectly balances the heat from the red chili flakes, creating a refreshing yet spicy treat.

3. Thai Spiced Banana Fritters

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1-2 teaspoons curry powder
  • Oil for frying
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Make the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed bananas, flour, coconut milk, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and curry powder. Mix until well combined and let it sit for about 10 minutes.

  2. Heat the Oil: In a deep pan or skillet, heat oil over medium heat until hot.

  3. Fry the Fritters: Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes on each side. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  4. Serve: Dust with powdered sugar before serving. The combination of sweet bananas and the warm spices from the curry powder creates a delightful flavor explosion in every bite.

4. Spicy Thai Tea Pudding

Ingredients

  • 2 cups brewed Thai tea (strong)
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1-2 teaspoons cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Base: In a saucepan, combine the brewed Thai tea, sugar, coconut milk, cornstarch, salt, and cayenne pepper. Whisk until smooth.

  2. Cook the Mixture: Heat the saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens (about 5-7 minutes).

  3. Chill: Once thickened, pour the mixture into serving cups and refrigerate until set, about 2 hours.

  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving. The rich and creamy texture of the pudding, combined with the aromatic flavors of Thai tea and a hint of heat from the cayenne, makes this dessert a unique treat.

5. Chilli Chocolate Dipped Pineapple

Ingredients

  • 1 fresh pineapple, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 200g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (optional, for smoother chocolate)

Instructions

  1. Melt the Chocolate: In a heatproof bowl, melt the dark chocolate over a double boiler or microwave until smooth. If using, mix in the coconut oil for a silkier texture.

  2. Add Spice: Stir in the cayenne pepper and sea salt until well combined.

  3. Dip the Pineapple: Dip each pineapple wedge into the chocolate mixture, allowing excess chocolate to drip off. Place on a parchment-lined tray.

  4. Chill: Refrigerate the chocolate-dipped pineapple until the chocolate hardens, about 30 minutes.

  5. Serve: Enjoy as a refreshing yet spicy dessert. The sweetness of the pineapple paired with the rich dark chocolate and the heat from the cayenne creates a perfect harmony of flavors.

Conclusion

Thai desserts offer a unique blend of sweet and spicy flavors that can satisfy any sweet tooth. From the creamy mango sticky rice to the indulgent spicy coconut ice cream, these recipes invite you to explore the diverse world of Thai sweets. Each dish reflects the vibrant culinary culture of Thailand, showcasing the beautiful balance between sweetness and heat. Don’t hesitate to experiment with the spice levels to find your perfect combination. Happy cooking!
