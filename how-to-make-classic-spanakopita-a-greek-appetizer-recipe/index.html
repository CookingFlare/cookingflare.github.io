How to Make Classic Spanakopita: A Greek Appetizer Recipe


Spanakopita, a delightful Greek pastry, is known for its flaky layers of phyllo dough, savory spinach filling, and rich feta cheese. This classic appetizer is not only a staple in Greek cuisine but also a beloved dish around the world. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, preparing a family meal, or simply craving a delicious snack, learning how to make spanakopita from scratch is a rewarding culinary adventure. In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of creating this mouthwatering dish, from the ingredients you’ll need to step-by-step instructions.

Ingredients for Spanakopita

Before diving into the preparation, let’s gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach (or 10 ounces frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese (optional for creaminess)
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried dill)
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Phyllo Layers:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (16 oz), thawed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted (or olive oil for a lighter option)
  • Cooking spray (optional)

For Serving:

  • Lemon wedges (optional)
  • Greek yogurt (optional)

Preparing the Spinach Filling

  1. Prep the Spinach: If using fresh spinach, rinse it thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or grit. Remove any tough stems. In a large pot, bring a small amount of water to a boil and add the spinach. Cook for about 2-3 minutes until wilted. Drain and cool. If using frozen spinach, ensure it is completely thawed and drained of excess moisture.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a skillet over medium heat, add a tablespoon of olive oil. Sauté the chopped onion and green onions until they become translucent, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.

  3. Combine the Filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked spinach, sautéed onions, crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese (if using), beaten eggs, dill, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

Assembling the Spanakopita

  1. Prepare the Phyllo Dough: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Unroll the phyllo dough and cover it with a damp kitchen towel to prevent it from drying out. Phyllo can be delicate, so handle it gently.

  2. Layer the Phyllo: Take one sheet of phyllo dough and lay it on a clean, flat surface. Brush it lightly with melted butter (or olive oil). Place another sheet on top and repeat the process until you have 4-5 layers. This will create a sturdy base for your spanakopita.

  3. Add the Filling: Spoon a portion of the spinach filling along one edge of the layered phyllo, leaving about an inch of space from the edges. Be careful not to overfill, as this can make it difficult to roll.

  4. Roll the Dough: Starting from the edge with the filling, carefully roll the phyllo dough over the filling to create a log shape. Tuck in the sides as you roll to seal the filling inside. Place the rolled spanakopita seam-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  5. Repeat: Continue the process with the remaining phyllo sheets and filling. You can also create triangles by cutting the rolled phyllo into smaller sections before baking.

  6. Brush and Score: Once all spanakopita are assembled, brush the tops with more melted butter. If you prefer, you can score the tops with a sharp knife to create a decorative pattern and allow steam to escape while baking.

Baking the Spanakopita

  1. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes or until the phyllo is golden brown and crispy. Keep an eye on them to avoid burning, as ovens can vary.

  2. Cool and Serve: Once baked, remove the spanakopita from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature with lemon wedges and a dollop of Greek yogurt if desired.

Tips for Perfect Spanakopita

  • Phyllo Dough Handling: Always keep the phyllo dough covered with a damp towel while working to prevent it from drying out and becoming brittle.
  • Filling Variations: Feel free to experiment with the filling. You can add other greens like chard or kale, incorporate herbs like parsley or mint, or even mix in sautéed mushrooms for added flavor.
  • Make Ahead: Spanakopita can be prepared in advance. Assemble and freeze them before baking. When ready to serve, bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time.
  • Serving Suggestions: Spanakopita pairs wonderfully with a crisp Greek salad, tzatziki sauce, or a refreshing glass of ouzo or white wine.

Conclusion

Making classic spanakopita at home is a fantastic way to bring a taste of Greece into your kitchen. With its flaky layers and savory filling, this dish is sure to impress your family and friends. Whether enjoyed as an appetizer, a side dish, or a main course, spanakopita is versatile and satisfying. We hope this recipe inspires you to try your hand at this delicious Greek treat. Happy cooking!
