Easter Brunch Cocktails: Cheers to the Holiday


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and indulging in delicious food and drinks. While brunch is a popular choice for Easter meals, why not take it up a notch and add some festive cocktails to your menu? Whether you prefer something light and refreshing or a bit more spirited, these Easter brunch cocktails are sure to impress your guests and add a touch of cheer to the holiday.

1. Bunny Bellini

What better way to start your Easter brunch than with a sparkling cocktail? The Bunny Bellini is a delightful twist on the classic Bellini, perfect for celebrating the season. To make this fruity and bubbly drink, you’ll need:

  • 1 ripe peach, peeled and pitted
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Prosecco or sparkling wine

In a blender, puree the peach, lemon juice, and sugar until smooth. Pour the mixture into champagne flutes, filling them about halfway. Top each glass with chilled Prosecco or sparkling wine. Garnish with a slice of peach or a sprig of mint, and enjoy the sweet and tangy flavors of this delightful cocktail.

2. Carrot Ginger Mimosa

Carrots are a staple of Easter, so why not incorporate them into your brunch cocktails? The Carrot Ginger Mimosa adds a unique twist to the classic mimosa by infusing it with the flavors of fresh carrots and ginger. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ounces of carrot juice
  • 1 ounce of ginger liqueur
  • Champagne or sparkling wine

In a cocktail shaker, combine the carrot juice and ginger liqueur. Shake well to mix the flavors. Strain the mixture into a champagne flute, filling it about halfway. Top with chilled champagne or sparkling wine. Garnish with a small piece of carrot or a twist of ginger, and enjoy the vibrant and refreshing taste of this Easter-inspired cocktail.

3. Peep Martini

If you’re looking for a fun and whimsical cocktail to serve at your Easter brunch, look no further than the Peep Martini. This colorful and playful drink is sure to bring a smile to everyone’s face. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ounces of marshmallow vodka
  • 1 ounce of triple sec
  • 1 ounce of pineapple juice
  • 1 ounce of lemon juice
  • Sugar for rimming the glass
  • Marshmallow Peeps for garnish

Rim a martini glass with sugar for an added touch of sweetness. In a cocktail shaker, combine the marshmallow vodka, triple sec, pineapple juice, and lemon juice. Shake well to mix the flavors. Strain the mixture into the prepared martini glass. Garnish with a festive Marshmallow Peep or two, and enjoy this fun and delicious Easter cocktail.

4. Springtime Spritzer

For those who prefer a lighter and less boozy option, the Springtime Spritzer is the perfect choice. This refreshing and effervescent drink combines the flavors of fresh cucumber, mint, and lime for a truly delightful sip. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ounces of cucumber-infused vodka
  • 1 ounce of fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 ounce of simple syrup
  • Soda water
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

In a glass filled with ice, combine the cucumber-infused vodka, lime juice, and simple syrup. Stir well to mix the flavors. Top with soda water and garnish with fresh mint leaves. Sip and savor the crisp and invigorating taste of this Springtime Spritzer.

Conclusion

Easter brunch is a wonderful opportunity to gather with loved ones and enjoy a delicious meal. By adding these festive cocktails to your menu, you’ll elevate your Easter celebration to a whole new level. From the fruity and bubbly Bunny Bellini to the whimsical Peep Martini, these cocktails are sure to bring joy and cheer to your holiday. So raise a glass, toast to the season, and cheers to a memorable Easter brunch!
