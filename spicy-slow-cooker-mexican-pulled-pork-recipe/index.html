Spicy Slow Cooker Mexican Pulled Pork Recipe


If you’re looking for a dish that combines bold flavors, tender meat, and the convenience of a slow cooker, then this Spicy Slow Cooker Mexican Pulled Pork recipe is just what you need. Perfect for taco nights, burrito bowls, or simply served with rice, this recipe promises to deliver a fiesta of flavors right in your kitchen. Let’s dive into the details of how to create this mouthwatering dish with ease.

Ingredients

To get started, gather the following ingredients:

For the Pulled Pork

  • 3 to 4 pounds pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste for spice level)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes with green chilies (such as Ro-Tel)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

For Serving

  • Corn tortillas or flour tortillas
  • Sour cream
  • Avocado slices
  • Shredded cheese
  • Lime wedges
  • Additional cilantro (for garnish)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Begin by trimming any excess fat from the pork shoulder. This will help reduce greasiness in the final dish. Once trimmed, pat the meat dry with paper towels. This step is essential for achieving a good sear.

Step 2: Season the Meat

In a small bowl, combine the salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork shoulder, ensuring it is well coated. This seasoning mix is what gives the pulled pork its signature flavor profile.

Step 3: Sear the Pork (Optional)

While not necessary, searing the pork shoulder can enhance the depth of flavor. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the pork shoulder and sear on all sides until a golden-brown crust forms—about 3-4 minutes per side. This step adds a rich flavor but can be skipped if you’re short on time.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Pork

Transfer the seared (or unseared) pork shoulder to the slow cooker. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, diced tomatoes with green chilies, chicken broth, and apple cider vinegar. The combination of these ingredients will create a savory sauce that infuses the pork with flavor as it cooks.

Step 5: Cooking Time

Cover the slow cooker and set it to low for 8 hours or high for 4 hours. The longer cooking time on low will yield the most tender and flavorful pork, but if you’re short on time, the high setting works well too. There’s nothing quite like the aroma of spices wafting through your home as the pork cooks.

Step 6: Shred the Pork

Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the pork from the slow cooker and place it on a large cutting board. Using two forks, shred the meat into bite-sized pieces. The pork should fall apart easily if it’s cooked properly.

Step 7: Mix in the Sauce

After shredding, return the pork to the slow cooker and mix it with the remaining juices. This will ensure that every bite is packed with flavor. Squeeze the juice of two limes over the pulled pork, and give it a good stir. The lime juice adds a zesty brightness that balances the richness of the meat.

Serving Suggestions

Now that your Spicy Slow Cooker Mexican Pulled Pork is ready, it’s time to serve it up! Here are a few delicious ideas for how to enjoy this dish:

Tacos

Warm some corn or flour tortillas in a skillet or directly over the flame for a few seconds. Fill each tortilla with a generous portion of pulled pork. Top with fresh cilantro, diced onions, avocado slices, and a dollop of sour cream. Squeeze extra lime juice on top for that added zing.

Burrito Bowls

Layer a bowl with rice, black beans, and a healthy serving of pulled pork. Add toppings such as pico de gallo, guacamole, shredded cheese, and a sprinkle of cilantro. This makes for a hearty meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Nachos

Spread tortilla chips on a baking sheet and top with pulled pork, shredded cheese, and sliced jalapeños. Bake in the oven until the cheese is bubbly. Serve with sour cream, guacamole, and salsa for an irresistible snack or appetizer.

Quesadillas

Fill flour tortillas with pulled pork and shredded cheese, and cook on a skillet until golden brown on both sides. Cut into wedges and serve with salsa and sour cream.

Storing and Reheating

If you find yourself with leftovers (which is likely, as this recipe makes a generous amount), store the pulled pork in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, simply warm it in a skillet over medium heat or in the microwave until heated through. You can also freeze the pulled pork for up to 3 months. Thaw it in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Tips and Variations

  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the cayenne pepper or omit it entirely. You can also add more tomatoes to balance out the heat.
  • Add Vegetables: Consider adding bell peppers, corn, or even beans to the slow cooker for added texture and nutrition.
  • Extra Flavor: For an even deeper flavor, marinate the pork shoulder in the spice mix and some lime juice overnight before cooking.
  • Cooking Method: If you don’t have a slow cooker, you can make this recipe in the oven. Place the seasoned pork shoulder in a Dutch oven with the other ingredients, cover it, and cook at 300°F (150°C) for about 4-5 hours or until tender.

Conclusion

This Spicy Slow Cooker Mexican Pulled Pork recipe is a fantastic way to bring the vibrant flavors of Mexico into your home. It’s perfect for gatherings, family dinners, or meal prep, offering versatility in how you can serve it. With minimal effort and maximum flavor, this dish is bound to become a favorite in your recipe rotation. So gather your ingredients, set your slow cooker, and get ready to enjoy a deliciously spicy meal that will impress your family and friends. Happy cooking!
