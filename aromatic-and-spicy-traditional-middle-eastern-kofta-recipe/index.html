Aromatic and Spicy: Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta Recipe


If you’re looking for a dish that combines aromatic flavors and a spicy kick, then the Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta is the recipe for you. Bursting with a unique blend of spices, this dish will transport you to the bustling streets and vibrant markets of the Middle East. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your family and friends. So let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

To make the Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped onion, minced garlic, and fresh parsley. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, ground paprika, ground nutmeg, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a spice blend.

  3. Gradually add the spice blend to the meat mixture, ensuring that it is evenly incorporated. Use your hands to mix the ingredients together, making sure not to over-mix as it can result in tough kofta.

  4. Once the mixture is well combined, cover it and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make the kofta more flavorful.

  5. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. If using a grill, make sure to oil the grates to prevent the kofta from sticking.

  6. Take a handful of the meat mixture and shape it into an elongated oval shape, resembling a small sausage. Repeat this process until all the mixture is used.

  7. Place the kofta on the preheated grill and cook for approximately 4-6 minutes on each side or until they are browned and cooked through. Make sure to turn them gently to prevent them from breaking apart.

  8. Once cooked, remove the kofta from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.

  9. Serve the Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta with warm pita bread, tahini sauce, and a side of fresh salad. You can also pair it with rice or couscous for a heartier meal.

Enjoy the Flavors of the Middle East

The Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta is a dish that embodies the rich flavors and aromas of the Middle East. With its blend of spices and succulent meat, it’s a recipe that will transport you to a different culinary world. So why not give it a try and bring a taste of the Middle East into your own kitchen? Enjoy the delicious and aromatic experience of the Traditional Middle Eastern Kofta recipe!

Note: Make sure to adjust the spice levels according to your taste preference. The cayenne pepper adds a nice kick to the kofta, but feel free to reduce or omit it if you prefer a milder flavor. Also, you can experiment with different herbs and spices to create your own unique twist on this traditional recipe.
