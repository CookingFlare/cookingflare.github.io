Homemade Caramel Apple Bars: A Taste of Autumn


As the leaves start to turn vibrant shades of red and gold, and the air becomes crisp and invigorating, there’s no denying that autumn is upon us. It’s the season of cozy sweaters, pumpkin spice lattes, and, of course, delicious apple treats. One of my favorite autumn indulgences is the classic combination of sweet, tangy apples and rich, buttery caramel. That’s why I’m excited to share with you my recipe for Homemade Caramel Apple Bars – a delightful dessert that captures the essence of fall in every bite.

To me, autumn is all about savoring the natural flavors that the season has to offer. With its juicy, crisp apples and luscious caramel, these bars are the embodiment of fall in a single bite. The buttery, crumbly crust provides the perfect base for the tender, cinnamon-spiced apple slices, while the gooey caramel drizzle adds a touch of decadence that will leave you craving more.

What sets these Homemade Caramel Apple Bars apart is the delightful contrast of textures. The Recipe

Now, without further ado, let’s dive into the recipe for Homemade Caramel Apple Bars:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup caramel sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, baking soda, and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press half of the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan.
  4. In another bowl, toss the sliced apples with granulated sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, and nutmeg until evenly coated.
  5. Spread the apple mixture over the crust in the pan, then sprinkle the remaining crumb mixture over the top.
  6. Bake for 45 to 55 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown.
  7. Allow the bars to cool slightly, then drizzle the caramel sauce over the top.
  8. Let the bars cool completely before cutting into squares.
