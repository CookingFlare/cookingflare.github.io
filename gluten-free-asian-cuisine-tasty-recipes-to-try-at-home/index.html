Gluten-Free Asian Cuisine: Tasty Recipes to Try at Home


Exploring the vibrant and diverse world of Asian cuisine is an exciting culinary journey. With bold flavors, unique textures, and aromatic herbs and spices, Asian dishes are celebrated globally for their versatility and irresistible tastes. For those embracing a gluten-free diet, creating Asian-inspired recipes at home may feel like a daunting task since many traditional ingredients like soy sauce, wheat-based noodles, and dumpling wrappers contain gluten. But fear not—delicious gluten-free Asian cuisine is absolutely achievable with the right ingredients and techniques.

In this post, we’ll share some mouthwatering gluten-free Asian recipes you can recreate in your kitchen. From savory stir-fries to delicate dumplings, these recipes are as vibrant as they are accommodating to your dietary needs. Let’s dive in!

Understanding Gluten-Free Asian Cooking

Before diving into recipes, it’s important to understand a few basics about gluten-free Asian cooking. Many traditional Asian staples like rice, rice noodles, tofu, tea, and fresh produce are naturally gluten-free. However, attention is needed when selecting condiments like soy sauce, oyster sauce, and hoisin sauce, as these often contain wheat.

When cooking gluten-free Asian dishes, make sure to stock your pantry with:

  • Tamari Sauce: A fantastic gluten-free substitute for soy sauce.
  • Gluten-Free Hoisin Sauce: Available at many specialty health or Asian grocery stores.
  • Coconut Aminos: A soy-free and gluten-free alternative to both soy sauce and tamari.
  • Gluten-Free Rice Vinegar: Ensure the label specifies that it’s free of gluten-containing additives.
  • Gluten-Free Sambal Oelek or Chili Paste: Perfect for adding a kick of heat.
  • Arrowroot Starch or Tapioca Starch: Great replacements for wheat flour in thickening sauces or creating dumpling wrappers.

Armed with the right ingredients, you’ll be able to authentically recreate traditional Asian dishes without compromising flavor. Now, let’s jump to the recipes!

1. Gluten-Free Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

A stir-fry is a quintessential Asian dish that’s as quick to prepare as it is flavorful. By swapping out soy sauce for tamari and thickening the sauce with arrowroot starch, this recipe becomes completely gluten-free.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp tamari sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp arrowroot starch, mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, toss chicken slices with 1 tablespoon tamari sauce and a pinch of salt. Let it marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat the sesame oil and olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add ginger and garlic, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant. Then, add the chicken and stir-fry until cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. Add the broccoli, red bell pepper, carrot, and green onions to the same skillet. Stir-fry vegetables for 3–4 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp.
  5. Return the chicken to the skillet. Stir in the remaining tamari and arrowroot slurry. Cook everything together for another minute until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken and vegetables.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Serve hot with steamed jasmine rice or gluten-free noodles, garnished with sesame seeds.

2. Gluten-Free Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a beloved Thai noodle dish that’s sweet, tangy, and umami-packed. Using rice noodles keeps this dish inherently gluten-free, but we’ll swap traditional soy sauce for tamari to ensure the recipe is safe for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 3 tbsp tamari sauce
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 lb shrimp or chicken (optional)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/3 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice noodles according to package instructions, then rinse with cold water and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and lime juice to make the Pad Thai sauce.
  3. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the eggs and scramble until just set. Remove eggs from the skillet and set aside.
  4. To the same skillet, add garlic and your protein of choice (shrimp or chicken). Cook until protein is fully cooked, then add noodles, the prepared sauce, and scrambled eggs back to the skillet.
  5. Toss everything together, ensuring the sauce thoroughly coats the noodles. Stir in bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for an additional 1–2 minutes.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with crushed peanuts, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.

3. Gluten-Free Chinese Dumplings (Potstickers)

Everyone loves dumplings, but finding gluten-free wrappers can be a struggle. This recipe makes homemade dumpling wrappers using gluten-free flours for a delightfully chewy texture.

Ingredients for Wrappers:

  • 1 1/2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup tapioca starch
  • 3/4 cup boiling water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Ingredients for Filling:

  • 1/2 lb ground pork or chicken
  • 1 cup Napa cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, minced
  • 1 tbsp tamari sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 tsp grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. To make the wrappers, combine gluten-free flour and tapioca starch. Slowly add boiling water and mix until a dough forms. Knead until smooth, then cover and let rest for 20 minutes.
  2. While the dough rests, prepare the filling by combining ground meat, Napa cabbage, green onions, tamari, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Mix well.
  3. Roll dough into a log and cut into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a small circle, approximately 3 inches in diameter.
  4. Place 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of each wrapper. Fold the wrapper and pleat the edges to seal the dumpling.
  5. Heat a skillet with 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Place dumplings in the skillet, flat-side down, and cook for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are golden. Add 1/4 cup water, cover, and steam dumplings for another 5 minutes until cooked through.

Final Thoughts

Gluten-free Asian cuisine opens up a world of flavors that are easy to recreate at home with just a few simple substitutions. Whether it’s a quick stir-fry, satisfying noodle dish, or comforting dim sum, these recipes showcase how gluten-free meals can be just as flavorful and satisfying as their traditional counterparts.

With the right gluten-free ingredients and an adventurous spirit, you can bring the essence of Asia into your kitchen while catering to your dietary needs. Try these recipes for your next meal, and prepare to impress family and friends with vibrant, restaurant-quality dishes!
