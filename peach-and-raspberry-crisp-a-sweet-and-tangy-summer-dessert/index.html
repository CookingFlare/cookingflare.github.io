Peach and Raspberry Crisp: A Sweet and Tangy Summer Dessert


Are you looking for the perfect summer dessert that combines the sweetness of peaches with the tanginess of raspberries? Look no further! Our Peach and Raspberry Crisp is the ultimate treat to satisfy your cravings on a hot summer day. This delightful dessert is not only easy to make but also bursting with the flavors of the season. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 4 ripe peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup raspberries
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the topping:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, raspberries, granulated sugar, all-purpose flour, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss until the fruit is evenly coated. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, all-purpose flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Mix well. Add the cold butter pieces and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the fruit mixture in the baking dish, covering it completely. Press the topping down gently with your hands to ensure it sticks to the fruit.

  5. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the fruit is bubbly and the topping is golden brown.

  6. Once baked, remove the Peach and Raspberry Crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, so serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra special treat.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a crisper topping, you can add a handful of chopped nuts, such as almonds or pecans, to the oat mixture before baking.

  • Feel free to experiment with different fruit combinations. You can substitute the peaches and raspberries with other seasonal fruits like blueberries, blackberries, or even a mix of stone fruits like plums and cherries.

  • For a gluten-free version, you can use gluten-free oats and a gluten-free flour blend in the topping.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, simply pop a serving in the microwave for a few seconds or warm it in the oven at a low temperature.

So there you have it – a mouthwatering Peach and Raspberry Crisp recipe that will make your taste buds dance with joy. This sweet and tangy summer dessert is perfect for family gatherings, picnics, or just a cozy night at home. Give it a try and let us know how you like it. Enjoy!
