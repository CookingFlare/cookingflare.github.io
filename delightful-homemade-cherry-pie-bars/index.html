Delightful Homemade Cherry Pie Bars


If you’re a fan of cherry pie but don’t want to go through the hassle of making a traditional pie, then these Delightful Homemade Cherry Pie Bars are the perfect solution. With a buttery crust, a sweet and tangy cherry filling, and a crumbly streusel topping, these bars are a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

For the cherry filling:

  • 3 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract

For the streusel topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, or until lightly golden.

  4. While the crust is baking, prepare the cherry filling. In a medium saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and almond extract. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and the cherries release their juices, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.

  5. To make the streusel topping, combine the flour, sugar, and salt in a medium mixing bowl. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until crumbly.

  6. Once the crust has baked and cooled slightly, spread the cherry filling evenly over the top. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the cherry filling, covering it completely.

  7. Return the baking dish to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the streusel topping is golden brown.

  8. Remove from the oven and allow the bars to cool completely in the baking dish before cutting into squares.

Serving and Storing

These Delightful Homemade Cherry Pie Bars are best served at room temperature. They can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. They also freeze well, so you can make a batch in advance and thaw them whenever you have a craving for a sweet treat.

Conclusion

With their buttery crust, sweet and tangy cherry filling, and crumbly streusel topping, these Delightful Homemade Cherry Pie Bars are a delicious alternative to traditional cherry pie. They are easy to make and perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply want to satisfy your sweet tooth, these bars are sure to be a hit. So go ahead and give this recipe a try – your taste buds will thank you!
