Lemon Meringue Bars: Tangy and Sweet Delight


If you’re a fan of the classic lemon meringue pie but want a more portable and easy-to-make version, then you’re in for a treat! Lemon meringue bars are the perfect combination of tangy lemon curd and sweet, fluffy meringue, all nestled atop a buttery shortbread crust. This recipe is a delightful twist on the traditional pie, and it’s sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Shortbread Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • A pinch of salt

For the Lemon Curd Filling:

  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons

For the Meringue Topping:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • ½ teaspoon cream of tartar
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Shortbread Crust:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang for easy removal.
  2. In a food processor, pulse together the flour, powdered sugar, butter, and salt until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the mixture into the prepared baking pan, ensuring an even layer, and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until lightly golden. Allow the crust to cool while preparing the filling.

Preparing the Lemon Curd Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and flour. Add the eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest, and whisk until smooth.
  2. Pour the lemon mixture over the cooled crust and return the pan to the oven. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set.

Creating the Meringue Topping:

  1. In a clean, dry bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar with an electric mixer until soft peaks form.
  2. Gradually add the granulated sugar, continuing to beat until stiff, glossy peaks form. Beat in the vanilla extract.
  3. Spread the meringue over the hot lemon filling, ensuring the meringue touches the edges of the pan to create a seal and prevent shrinking.

Finishing and Serving:

  1. Use the back of a spoon to create peaks and swirls in the meringue, then return the pan to the oven. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the meringue is golden.
  2. Allow the lemon meringue bars to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours before slicing into bars.
  3. Serve the bars chilled or at room temperature, and enjoy the delightful contrast of tangy lemon and sweet meringue in every bite!

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense lemon flavor, you can add a few drops of lemon extract to the lemon curd filling.
  • If you prefer a more stable meringue, you can bake the meringue for an additional 5-10 minutes until it’s firmer.
  • Experiment with different citrus fruits, such as lime or orange, to create unique variations of these delicious bars.

Lemon meringue bars are a delightful treat that brings together the bright, zesty flavor of lemon with the airy sweetness of meringue. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a citrusy dessert, these bars are the perfect choice for a tangy and sweet delight!
