Sopa de Mariscos: Spanish-Style Seafood Soup


Sopa de Mariscos, or Spanish-Style Seafood Soup, is a delicious and comforting dish that brings together the flavors of the sea in a rich and flavorful broth. Packed with a variety of seafood, vegetables, and aromatic herbs, this soup is a true delight for seafood lovers. Whether you’re craving a warm and hearty meal or looking to impress your guests with a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups fish stock or seafood broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pound mixed seafood (such as shrimp, mussels, clams, and fish fillets)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they turn translucent and fragrant.

  2. Add the diced red and green bell peppers, carrot, and celery to the pot. Sauté for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Sprinkle the paprika and cayenne pepper over the vegetables, stirring well to coat them evenly. Cook for an additional minute to release the flavors.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes, fish stock or seafood broth, and white wine. Add the bay leaf and season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir and bring the soup to a gentle simmer.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for about 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Meanwhile, prep the seafood. Peel and devein the shrimp, scrub the mussels and clams, and cut the fish fillets into bite-sized pieces.

  7. After the soup has simmered for 20 minutes, gently add the seafood to the pot. Be careful not to overcook the seafood – it should be tender and just cooked through. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the seafood is done.

  8. Remove the bay leaf from the soup and taste for seasoning, adjusting with salt and pepper if needed.

  9. Ladle the Sopa de Mariscos into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. Serve hot with crusty bread on the side for dipping.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a thicker soup, you can add a tablespoon of flour to the sautéed vegetables before adding the tomatoes and broth. This will help thicken the soup slightly.

  • Feel free to customize the seafood selection based on your preferences and availability. You can substitute or add other types of seafood such as scallops, lobster, or even octopus.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, squeeze some fresh lemon juice over the soup just before serving.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop, adding a splash of water or broth if needed.

Conclusion

Sopa de Mariscos is a classic Spanish dish that captures the essence of the sea in every spoonful. With its vibrant colors, aromatic herbs, and a medley of tender seafood, this soup is a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re enjoying it as a comforting meal on a chilly evening or serving it at a dinner party, Sopa de Mariscos is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, get cooking, and savor the taste of the Mediterranean with this delicious Spanish-style seafood soup. Buen provecho!
