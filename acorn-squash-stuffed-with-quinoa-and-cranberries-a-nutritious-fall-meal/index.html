Acorn Squash Stuffed with Quinoa and Cranberries: A Nutritious Fall Meal


Fall is the perfect time to indulge in hearty and comforting meals that embrace the seasonal produce. One dish that captures the essence of autumn is the delightful acorn squash stuffed with quinoa and cranberries. This recipe not only celebrates the flavors of the season but also provides a nutritious and satisfying meal. Let’s dive into the details of this wholesome dish and how you can recreate it in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Acorn Squash:

  • 2 medium acorn squash
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Quinoa Stuffing:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Acorn Squash:

    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
    • Carefully cut the acorn squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds with a spoon.
    • Brush the insides of the squash halves with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
    • Place the squash halves, cut side down, on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    • Roast in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the squash is tender when pierced with a fork.

  2. Cook the Quinoa:

    • In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil.
    • Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed.

  3. Prepare the Quinoa Stuffing:

    • In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, fresh parsley, maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, and season with salt and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  4. Assemble and Bake:

    • Once the acorn squash halves are roasted and tender, flip them over so the cut side is facing up.
    • Fill the cavities of the squash halves with the prepared quinoa stuffing, mounding it generously.
    • Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the stuffing is heated through and the tops are slightly crispy.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Carefully transfer the stuffed acorn squash to a serving platter.
    • Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley and a drizzle of maple syrup, if desired.
    • Serve hot and savor the delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Nutritional Benefits

This acorn squash stuffed with quinoa and cranberries is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a nutritional powerhouse. Acorn squash is rich in vitamins A and C, as well as fiber, while quinoa provides a complete source of protein and essential amino acids. The addition of cranberries and pecans brings a delightful sweetness and crunch, along with an array of health benefits.

Serving Suggestions

This dish can be served as a wholesome main course for a fall dinner, accompanied by a simple green salad or roasted vegetables. It also makes a stunning centerpiece for a vegetarian Thanksgiving or holiday feast, impressing both herbivores and omnivores alike with its vibrant colors and robust flavors.

Final Thoughts

Acorn squash stuffed with quinoa and cranberries is a celebration of the bountiful flavors and colors of the fall season. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a nourishing meal, this recipe is sure to delight your senses and nourish your body. Embrace the autumn harvest and savor the goodness of this nutritious and comforting dish.
