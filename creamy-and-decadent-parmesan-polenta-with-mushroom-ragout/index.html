Creamy and Decadent Parmesan Polenta with Mushroom Ragout


Polenta, a traditional Italian dish, is often overlooked in favor of its more popular cousin, risotto. However, polenta is a staple in many Italian households, especially during the colder months. When paired with a rich and savory mushroom ragout, the creamy polenta becomes a true comfort food. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making a creamy and decadent Parmesan polenta with mushroom ragout, perfect for a cozy winter evening.

Ingredients

For the polenta:

  • 2 cups of polenta cornmeal
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter

For the mushroom ragout:

  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups of mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and button), sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

Polenta

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, bring the water to a boil over high heat. Gradually whisk in the polenta cornmeal and reduce the heat to medium.
  2. Cook the polenta, stirring constantly, for 5-7 minutes or until it thickens and comes away from the sides of the pot.
  3. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper until the cheese is fully incorporated and the polenta is smooth.
  4. Add the unsalted butter and stir until melted and fully incorporated.
  5. Transfer the polenta to a serving dish and keep warm.

Mushroom Ragout

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook, stirring occasionally, until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Add the dry white wine, vegetable broth, tomato paste, and dried thyme to the skillet. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and cook the ragout, stirring occasionally, until the liquid has reduced and the mushrooms are tender, about 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assembly

  1. To assemble the dish, place the warm polenta on a serving plate or in a shallow bowl. Spoon the mushroom ragout over the top of the polenta, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  2. Sprinkle additional grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the ragout, if desired.
  3. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired.

Conclusion

This creamy and decadent Parmesan polenta with mushroom ragout is a hearty and comforting dish perfect for a cold winter evening. The creamy polenta provides a rich and velvety base, while the savory mushroom ragout adds a depth of flavor and texture. With its bold flavors and satisfying texture, this dish is sure to become a new favorite in your household.
