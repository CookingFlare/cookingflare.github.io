Savory and Sweet Corn on the Cob Recipes for Your Picnic Basket


Are you excited about planning a picnic? There’s nothing quite like enjoying the great outdoors while indulging in delicious food. And when it comes to picnic staples, corn on the cob is a must-have. Whether you prefer savory or sweet flavors, we’ve got you covered with two fantastic corn on the cob recipes that are sure to impress your picnic companions. So grab your picnic basket and let’s get cooking!

Savory Grilled Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 2 tablespoons of butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Peel back the husks of the corn, but leave them attached at the base.
  3. Remove the silk from the corn and then fold the husks back over the corn.
  4. Soak the corn in cold water for about 10 minutes.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  6. Remove the corn from the water and pat dry with a paper towel.
  7. Brush the corn with the seasoned butter mixture, making sure to coat all sides.
  8. Place the corn on the grill and cover. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn is tender and lightly charred.
  9. Remove from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes.
  10. Serve the savory grilled corn on the cob with an extra sprinkle of smoked paprika for an added burst of flavor.

Sweet and Tangy Honey Lime Corn on the Cob

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 2 tablespoons of butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Peel back the husks of the corn, but leave them attached at the base.
  3. Remove the silk from the corn and then fold the husks back over the corn.
  4. Place the corn on a baking sheet and roast in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until it’s cooked through.
  5. While the corn is roasting, prepare the honey lime butter by combining the softened butter, honey, lime juice, lime zest, chili powder, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Mix well.
  6. Remove the corn from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  7. Peel back the husks and discard, or use them as a natural handle for eating.
  8. Brush the corn with the honey lime butter, making sure to coat all sides.
  9. Serve the sweet and tangy honey lime corn on the cob as is, or sprinkle some extra chili powder for a little kick.

Conclusion

Corn on the cob is a timeless picnic favorite, and these two recipes add a delightful twist to this classic dish. Whether you prefer the savory flavors of the grilled corn or the sweet and tangy notes of the honey lime corn, both options are sure to satisfy your taste buds. So, the next time you plan a picnic, make sure to include these mouthwatering corn on the cob recipes in your basket. Happy picnicking!

Note: Remember to bring napkins for those buttery fingers!
