Nut-Free Jubilee: 55 Soaring Nut-Free Recipes for Joy and Celebration


If you’re looking to throw an unforgettable celebration with outstanding dishes that delight and amaze, you’re in for a real treat! Introducing the Nut-Free Jubilee - a collection of 55 soaring nut-free recipes for joy and celebration. This cornucopia of tantalizing dishes will cater to your guests’ needs and satisfy their taste buds, all while avoiding a commonly allergenic ingredient. So, what are you waiting for? Let’s embark on this flavorful journey and uncover delights that will elevate your gatherings to new heights!

Delectable Appetizers

Golden Beet & Blue Cheese Tartlets

Start your jubilee off with a bite of these Golden Beet and Blue Cheese Tartlets. The earthiness of the beets and the tanginess of the blue cheese make a pleasant pair, and their vibrant color will add a touch of whimsy to your party table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 cups golden beets, cooked and pureed
  • 1 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 1 sheet frozen phyllo dough, thawed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Roll out the phyllo dough and cut it into 2-inch squares.
  3. Mix the pureed beets and the egg yolks in a bowl, then stir in the blue cheese, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the mixture onto the phyllo squares and fold them into triangles, pressing the edges to seal.
  5. Place the tartlets on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Heirloom Tomato Bruschetta

This classic Italian starter boasts bright flavors and makes an elegant first impression at your celebration. Impress your guests with its simplicity and mouthwatering taste!

Ingredients:

  • 2 loaves crusty bread
  • 4 large heirloom tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup basil leaves, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a grill or broiler.
  2. Slice the bread and arrange it in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Grill or broil the bread slices until lightly toasted, then rub them with a garlic clove.
  4. Dice the tomatoes and mix them with the basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Spoon the mixture onto the toasted bread and serve immediately.

Main Dishes

Turkey and Cheddar Stuffed Sweet Potatoes

This hearty and savory dish will have your guests coming back for seconds. The combination of turkey, cheddar, and sweet potatoes creates a flavorsome fusion that can cater to everyone’s cravings.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Poke holes in the sweet potatoes and bake for about 50 minutes or until tender.
  2. In a skillet, brown the turkey, then add the onion, garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to taste.
  3. Once the sweet potatoes are cooked, cut them in half and scoop out some of the flesh (leave enough to hold the structure).
  4. Mix the sweet potato flesh with the turkey mixture, then add the shredded cheddar. Stuff the sweet potatoes and bake for another 10 minutes to let the cheese melt.

Vegetable Stir-fry with Tofu

For a feel-good, plant-based entrée, try this vibrant vegetable stir-fry. Packed with colorful vegetables, tofu, and your choice of soy or teriyaki sauce - it’s a wholesome delight that’s sure to appeal to all!

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, snow peas, zucchini)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (1-inch) piece of ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce or teriyaki sauce

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess water, then cut it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wok, and cook the tofu until golden brown.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Mix the vegetable broth and the soy or teriyaki sauce in a separate bowl.
  5. Add the sauce to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes. Serve over rice or noodles.

Desserts

Almond-Free Almond Joy Bars

Devotees of dessert_pref can rejoice! These Almond-Free Almond Joy Bars are every bit as decadent and delicious as their namesake, using coconut and cocoa for an earthy twist. They are a surefire way to end your jubilee on a sweet note!

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond-free, semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 cups shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds (optional!)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the chocolate chips and coconut oil in a double boiler over medium heat.
  3. Whisk in the condensed milk, then remove from heat.
  4. Stir in the shredded coconut, almonds (if using), and vanilla extract.
  5. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish, smoothing it out with a spatula.
  6. Refrigerate until set, then cut into bars.
