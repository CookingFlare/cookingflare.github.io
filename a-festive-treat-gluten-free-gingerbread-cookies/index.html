A Festive Treat: Gluten-Free Gingerbread Cookies


‘Tis the season to indulge in the warm and comforting flavors of gingerbread, and what better way to enjoy this festive treat than with a gluten-free twist! Whether you have a gluten sensitivity or simply want to explore new flavors, these gluten-free gingerbread cookies are a delightful addition to any holiday spread. With a perfect balance of spices and a hint of sweetness, these cookies are sure to become a favorite in your household. So, let’s dive into the recipe and bring the magic of the holidays to life with these delightful treats.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup molasses
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking powder, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, and salt until well combined.

  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy.

  4. Add Molasses, Egg, and Vanilla: Add the molasses, egg, and vanilla extract to the creamed butter and sugar, and mix until well combined.

  5. Incorporate Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a smooth dough forms.

  6. Chill the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow it to firm up.

  7. Roll and Cut: Once chilled, roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters to cut out gingerbread shapes and carefully transfer them to the prepared baking sheet.

  8. Bake: Bake the cookies for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are set. Be careful not to overbake to maintain a soft texture.

  9. Cool and Decorate: Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, you can decorate the cookies with icing or enjoy them as they are.

Tips for Success

  • Chill the Dough: Chilling the dough is crucial to prevent the cookies from spreading too much in the oven and to ensure they hold their shape.

  • Gluten-Free Flour: Use a high-quality gluten-free all-purpose flour blend for the best texture and flavor in your cookies.

  • Spice Level: Feel free to adjust the spices to suit your taste preferences. If you’re a fan of a stronger ginger flavor, add a bit more ground ginger to the dough.

  • Decorating: If you choose to decorate the cookies, let them cool completely before adding icing or other decorations to avoid melting.
