Perfectly Flaky Croissants: A French Breakfast Staple


Croissants are a quintessential French pastry known for their buttery and flaky texture. They are a delightful addition to any breakfast table, and with a little practice, you can easily make them at home. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating the perfect flaky croissants that will transport you to a charming French café.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 10g salt
  • 40g sugar
  • 10g instant yeast
  • 300ml cold water
  • 250g unsalted butter, cold

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. Slowly add the cold water to the dry mixture while stirring continuously. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a clean and lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Shape the dough into a ball and place it back into the mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rest for 1 hour at room temperature.

  5. While the dough is resting, prepare the butter block. Take the cold unsalted butter and place it between two sheets of parchment paper. Using a rolling pin, gently pound the butter to flatten it into a rectangle, about 1 cm thick.

  6. Place the flattened butter block in the refrigerator for about 15 minutes to firm up.

  7. After the dough has rested, remove it from the bowl and place it on a lightly floured surface. Roll the dough into a rectangle, about 1 cm thick.

  8. Take the chilled butter block out of the refrigerator and place it in the center of the dough rectangle.

  9. Fold the dough over the butter block, sealing the butter inside. Press the edges together to ensure the butter is fully enclosed.

  10. Roll the dough again into a rectangle, about 1 cm thick. Fold the dough into thirds, like a letter.

  11. Turn the dough 90 degrees and repeat steps 9 and 10. This process creates layers in the croissant dough, resulting in its characteristic flakiness.

  12. Repeat the folding and rolling process two more times, resting the dough in the refrigerator for 15 minutes between each fold.

  13. After the final fold, wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate it overnight. This slow fermentation process enhances the flavor of the croissants.

  14. The next day, remove the dough from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes.

  15. Roll the dough into a rectangle, about 5 mm thick. Using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter, cut the dough into triangles.

  16. Starting from the base of each triangle, roll the dough towards the tip, shaping it into a crescent.

  17. Place the shaped croissants onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Leave enough space between each croissant to allow for rising.

  18. Cover the croissants with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours, or until they have doubled in size.

  19. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).

  20. Once the croissants have risen, brush them with an egg wash made by whisking together one egg and a tablespoon of water. This will give them a golden-brown color when baked.

  21. Bake the croissants in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until they are beautifully golden and flaky.

  22. Remove the croissants from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack before serving.

Enjoy Your Homemade Croissants!

Now that you have mastered the art of making perfectly flaky croissants, you can enjoy these French breakfast staples in the comfort of your own home. Serve them warm with a pat of butter and a dollop of your favorite jam. Bon appétit!
