Mexican Beef Tamales: A Labor of Love Worth Every Bite


Tamales are a beloved traditional dish in Mexican cuisine, and for good reason. These little pockets of joy are packed with flavor and are truly a labor of love. Today, I want to share with you my recipe for Mexican Beef Tamales that will transport you straight to the heart of Mexico. So grab your apron and get ready to embark on this culinary adventure!

Ingredients

For the Beef Filling:

  • 1 pound beef chuck, cut into small cubes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup beef broth

For the Tamale Dough:

  • 2 cups masa harina (corn flour)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1/2 cup lard (or vegetable shortening)

For the Assembly:

  • 20 dried corn husks, soaked in warm water until pliable

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.

  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  3. Return the beef to the skillet and add the ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir well to coat the beef with the spices. Pour in the beef broth and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it cook for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and easily shreds apart.

  4. While the beef is cooking, prepare the tamale dough. In a large mixing bowl, combine the masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the warm water while stirring with a spoon or your hands until a smooth dough forms. In a separate bowl, beat the lard (or vegetable shortening) until fluffy, then gradually add it to the dough, mixing well until fully incorporated.

  5. Once the beef is done, remove it from the skillet and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded beef to the skillet and mix it with the remaining cooking liquid to keep it moist.

  6. Drain the corn husks and pat them dry. Take one husk and spread a thin layer of the tamale dough on it, leaving a border around the edges. Spoon a generous amount of the shredded beef filling onto the center of the dough. Fold the sides of the husk over the filling, then fold the bottom and top ends to enclose it fully. Repeat this process with the remaining husks and filling.

  7. Prepare a steamer by filling the bottom with water and placing a steam rack inside. Arrange the tamales on the rack, open side up, making sure they are tightly packed to prevent unraveling.

  8. Cover the steamer with a lid and bring the water to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low and steam the tamales for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the dough is firm and easily pulls away from the husks.

  9. Once cooked, remove the tamales from the steamer and let them cool slightly before serving. To serve, unwrap the husks and enjoy the delicious aroma and flavors of your homemade Mexican Beef Tamales.

Conclusion

Making Mexican Beef Tamales from scratch requires time and effort, but the end result is absolutely worth it. The tender beef, combined with the rich flavors of the spices and the fluffy tamale dough, creates a culinary experience that will transport you straight to Mexico. So gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and indulge in this labor of love. Trust me, every bite will be worth it!

Note: Feel free to experiment with different fillings, such as chicken or cheese, to cater to your own taste preferences. Enjoy!
