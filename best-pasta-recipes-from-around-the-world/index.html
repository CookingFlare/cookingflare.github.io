Best Pasta Recipes from Around the World


Pasta is a beloved dish enjoyed across the globe, whether in its simplest form with olive oil or transformed into decadent, flavorful dishes. While Italy is undoubtedly the home of pasta, there are countless variations, sauces, and methods of preparation that have emerged from different cultures and regions. From rich, creamy sauces to spicy, tangy twists, pasta recipes are as diverse as the places they originate from. In this article, we will explore some of the best pasta recipes from around the world, showcasing how different countries have put their unique spin on this classic dish.

1. Spaghetti alla Carbonara – Italy

When we talk about pasta, it’s impossible not to mention Spaghetti alla Carbonara, one of Italy’s most iconic pasta dishes. Originating from Rome, this dish combines simple ingredients—eggs, pecorino Romano cheese, guanciale (Italian cured pork cheek), black pepper, and spaghetti—into an unforgettable, creamy sauce. What makes this recipe stand out is its beautiful balance of flavors and textures. The egg yolks create a rich, silky sauce that clings to the pasta, while the guanciale adds a smoky depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 100g pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water according to the package instructions.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat and render the guanciale until crispy.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, pecorino, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  5. Toss the cooked spaghetti with the guanciale in the skillet, then slowly pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring constantly. Add reserved pasta water a bit at a time to achieve the desired creaminess.
  6. Serve immediately with additional black pepper and grated pecorino.

2. Pad Thai – Thailand

Though not traditionally thought of as a pasta dish, Pad Thai is a flavorful stir-fried noodle dish from Thailand that has become beloved worldwide. Made with rice noodles, shrimp, or chicken, and a tangy sauce made from tamarind, fish sauce, and lime juice, Pad Thai brings a perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors. Topped with crushed peanuts, fresh herbs, and lime wedges, it’s a dish that brings an explosion of flavor in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp or chicken, thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Crushed peanuts, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, lime juice, and soy sauce to make the Pad Thai sauce.
  3. Heat a wok or large skillet over medium heat and sauté the garlic in a little oil until fragrant. Add the shrimp or chicken and cook until tender.
  4. Push the protein to the side and scramble the eggs in the wok. Once cooked, toss everything together.
  5. Add the cooked noodles and pour the sauce over the mixture. Stir well to coat the noodles evenly.
  6. Garnish with crushed peanuts, fresh cilantro, and a lime wedge. Serve hot.

3. Pasta Aglio e Olio – Italy

One of the simplest yet most flavorful pasta dishes, Pasta Aglio e Olio is a staple in Italian cuisine. With just a handful of ingredients—garlic, olive oil, chili flakes, and parsley—this dish exemplifies the beauty of Italian cooking: simple, high-quality ingredients transformed into something extraordinary. The key to a great aglio e olio is ensuring the garlic is gently cooked in olive oil to release its flavors without burning.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 50ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over low heat. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes and sauté gently, ensuring the garlic does not burn.
  3. Once the pasta is done, reserve a cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta into the garlic oil mixture, adding a little pasta water to create a silky sauce.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

4. Beef Stroganoff – Russia

Beef Stroganoff is a rich and creamy dish with origins in Russia, often served with egg noodles or other wide pasta varieties. The creamy sauce, made from sour cream, beef broth, and mustard, envelops tender strips of beef and mushrooms. This dish is hearty, comforting, and perfect for colder weather. While it’s commonly made with beef, variations with chicken or pork are also popular.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 250ml beef broth
  • 200g sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 400g egg noodles
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, sauté the beef strips until browned, then set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté onions and garlic until soft. Add the mushrooms and cook until tender.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Slowly add the beef broth while stirring to create a smooth sauce.
  5. Stir in the sour cream and Dijon mustard. Return the beef to the pan and let everything simmer for 5-10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Serve the beef stroganoff over the cooked egg noodles, garnished with fresh parsley.

5. Sichuan Dan Dan Noodles – China

Dan Dan Noodles are a popular street food from the Sichuan province in China. With a combination of spicy, savory, and numbing flavors from Sichuan peppercorns and chili oil, this dish has a unique heat that makes it truly addictive. The noodles are served with a sauce made from sesame paste, soy sauce, and vinegar, and topped with minced pork and green onions. For those who love bold, spicy food, this is a must-try.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Chinese wheat noodles
  • 150g ground pork
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame paste (or peanut butter)
  • 1 tablespoon chili oil
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • Green onions, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chinese wheat noodles according to the package instructions, then set aside.
  2. In a pan, sauté the minced pork until browned. Add the garlic and cook for another 2 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame paste, and chili oil.
  4. To assemble, place the cooked noodles in a bowl and top with the pork mixture. Pour the sauce over the noodles and stir to combine.
  5. Sprinkle with Sichuan peppercorns and garnish with chopped green onions. Serve immediately.

6. Pasta Puttanesca – Italy

Pasta Puttanesca is another classic Italian dish, famous for its bold, briny flavors. The name “puttanesca” roughly translates to “in the style of a prostitute,” and while the origin of the name is debated, the dish itself is unmistakably full of intense, vibrant flavors. Made with anchovies, olives, capers, garlic, and tomatoes, this dish is quick to prepare yet packed with umami.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 anchovy fillets
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 tablespoons capers
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté garlic and anchovies until fragrant and the anchovies break down.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, olives, capers, and red pepper flakes. Simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to reduce slightly.
  4. Toss the pasta in the sauce, ensuring it is well coated.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve immediately.

Conclusion

Pasta is a dish that knows no borders, and the recipes we’ve explored here reflect how different cultures have embraced this simple ingredient to create something truly unique. Whether you’re in the mood for the creamy, rich flavors of an Italian Carbonara, the tangy kick of Pad Thai, or the spicy heat of Sichuan Dan Dan Noodles, there’s a pasta recipe for every palate. By experimenting with different pasta shapes, sauces, and seasonings, you can create endless variations to suit your tastes. So why not try something new today and embark on a pasta journey around the world?
