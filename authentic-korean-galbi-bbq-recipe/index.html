Authentic Korean Galbi BBQ Recipe


There’s nothing quite like the experience of savoring Korean Galbi BBQ—the perfect balance of sweet, savory, smoky, and meaty goodness that tantalizes the senses and creates unforgettable memories. This recipe will guide you step by step to recreate that mouthwatering dish in the comfort of your own kitchen, making every bite a true celebration of Korean cuisine.

Galbi, also called Kalbi, is the Korean word for “ribs.” While traditionally made with beef short ribs, this recipe can also be adapted for pork ribs. Perfect for special occasions or dinner parties, this dish showcases a sophisticated marinade packed with deep flavors that will leave you and your guests craving more. Let’s get started!

The Heart of Galbi: Choosing the Right Meat

Selecting quality meat plays a critical role in the success of your Galbi BBQ. Korean short ribs are unique because they are cut “flanken-style,” meaning the ribs are sliced across the bones to create thin strips with small, round pieces of bone inside. This cut allows the marinade to penetrate deeply while ensuring the meat cooks evenly in a relatively short amount of time. If you can’t find flanken-style short ribs at your local grocery store, ask your butcher or look for Korean markets that specifically sell this cut.

The Essence of Flavor: Creating the Marinade

The marinade is what sets Galbi apart. It’s a magical blend of salty soy sauce, sweet sugar, and fruity notes that create a deeply layered flavor profile. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients for the Marinade:

  • 1 cup soy sauce (preferably a Korean brand such as Jin-Ganjang for authenticity)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar or honey (for a rich sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 pear (preferably Asian pear, grated; it tenderizes the meat while adding sweetness)
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped yellow onion (or grated onion for a smoother texture)
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (optional, but adds a subtle tang)
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon roasted sesame seeds (optional, for added nuttiness)

This marinade not only adds wonderful flavor but also tenderizes the ribs to perfection, making them juicy and succulent once grilled.

Preparing the Korean Galbi Marinade

Step 1: Combine the Ingredients

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, start by ensuring the brown sugar or honey fully dissolves into the soy sauce. Then add the remaining marinade ingredients—grated pear, onion, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, mirin, rice wine vinegar, black pepper, and sesame seeds—and mix thoroughly. The marinade should have a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and fruity notes.

Step 2: Prepare the Short Ribs

Using your flanken-style beef short ribs, rinse them under cold water to remove any bone fragments. Pat them dry with paper towels. Place the ribs in a large zip-top bag or a shallow dish.

Marinating the Meat

Step 3: Immerse the Ribs

Pour the marinade over the ribs, ensuring they are completely submerged and well-coated. Massage the marinade into the meat to help it penetrate deeply.

Step 4: Rest and Infuse

Seal the bag or cover your dish with plastic wrap. Let the ribs marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, but for optimal flavor, allow them to soak overnight. This step ensures the ribs absorb every bit of that flavorful marinade.

Preparing for the Grill

Step 5: Preheat Your Grill

Galbi BBQ tastes best when cooked over a charcoal or gas grill, which imparts the signature smoky taste. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If using charcoal, allow the flames to die down until you have hot, glowing coals.

Step 6: Oil the Grates

Brush the grill grates lightly with vegetable oil to prevent sticking while cooking.

Grilling Your Galbi

Step 7: Grill the Short Ribs

Remove the ribs from the marinade, allowing excess liquid to drip off. Place them directly on the hot grill grates. Cook the ribs for about 3–4 minutes per side, flipping only once. The high heat will create a gorgeous caramelized crust while sealing in the juices.

Step 8: Stay Vigilant

Because the marinade contains sugar, be cautious of flare-ups or burning. Adjust the heat or move the ribs to cooler parts of the grill if needed.

Step 9: Check for Doneness

The ribs are ready when they’re beautifully caramelized with a slight char around the edges. Avoid overcooking to keep the meat tender and flavorful.

Serving Korean Galbi BBQ

Garnishes and Accompaniments

Serve your Galbi BBQ fresh off the grill! For an authentic Korean experience, accompany the ribs with:

  • Steamed white rice
  • Kimchi (spicy fermented cabbage)
  • Ssamjang (spicy dipping paste made of soybean paste, gochujang, garlic, and sesame oil)
  • Fresh lettuce leaves and perilla leaves for wrapping
  • Thinly sliced green onions or sesame seeds for garnish

Tips for an Unforgettable Galbi Experience

  • Grill Alternatives: Don’t have a grill? You can cook the ribs using a stovetop grill pan or under the broiler in your oven. While you may lose some of the smoky flavors, they’ll still taste incredible.
  • Sweetness Balance: If you prefer a less sweet marinade, adjust the amount of sugar or honey to taste.
  • Pear Swap: If Asian pears are unavailable, substitute with ripe Bosc pears or Fuji apples for similar results.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have any leftover Galbi (though there’s a good chance you won’t!), store the meat in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. To reheat, warm the ribs gently on a low-heat skillet or in the oven to avoid drying out.

The Perfect Dish for Any Occasion

Authentic Korean Galbi BBQ is more than just a meal; it’s an experience that embodies the joy of gathering and sharing delicious food with loved ones. The rich, smoky essence, perfectly grilled texture, and explosion of flavors make it ideal for holidays, parties, or casual get-togethers. Whether you’re introducing friends to Korean cuisine or treating your family to an unforgettable dinner, this Galbi recipe will make you a culinary hero.

Embrace the art of Korean barbecue and bring this iconic dish to your table today. Your taste buds will thank you, and every bite will transport you straight to the vibrant streets of Seoul!
