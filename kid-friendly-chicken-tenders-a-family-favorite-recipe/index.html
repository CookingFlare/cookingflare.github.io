Kid-Friendly Chicken Tenders: A Family Favorite Recipe


When it comes to family meals, few dishes are as universally loved as chicken tenders. These golden, crispy bites are not only a hit with kids but also a convenient and versatile option for busy parents. In this post, we’ll explore a delicious and easy-to-make recipe for kid-friendly chicken tenders that will have your family asking for seconds. Whether served with a favorite dipping sauce or alongside healthy sides, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your household. Let’s dive into the world of homemade chicken tenders!

The Appeal of Chicken Tenders

Chicken tenders are a classic comfort food that appeals to children and adults alike. Their tender texture and crispy exterior make them an irresistible choice for picky eaters. Plus, they are incredibly easy to customize, allowing you to experiment with different flavors, coatings, and dipping sauces. Homemade chicken tenders are not only healthier than their fast-food counterparts, but they also offer a great opportunity for family bonding in the kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make your own kid-friendly chicken tenders, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Tenders

  • 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breasts: Use fresh or thawed chicken for the best results.
  • 1 cup of buttermilk: This will help tenderize the chicken and add flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder: For an extra kick of flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder: Adds depth to the seasoning.
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika: Gives a beautiful color and a hint of smokiness.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.

For the Breading

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour: The base for our breading.
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs: You can use panko for extra crunch or regular breadcrumbs.
  • 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning: Adds a herby flavor profile.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional): For a little heat, adjust to your taste.
  • 2 large eggs: To help the breading stick to the chicken.

For Cooking

  • Vegetable oil: For frying, or you can use an air fryer for a healthier option.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Start by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, combine the buttermilk, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until well combined. Cut the chicken breasts into strips and place them in the marinade. Ensure that each piece is fully coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, but ideally for 2-4 hours. This marinating process will ensure that the chicken remains tender and flavorful.

Step 2: Prepare the Breading Station

While the chicken is marinating, set up your breading station. In one shallow dish, place the flour and season it with a pinch of salt and pepper. In another shallow dish, whisk the eggs until fully blended. In a third dish, combine the breadcrumbs with Italian seasoning and cayenne pepper, if using. This setup will allow for an easy assembly line when breading the chicken.

Step 3: Bread the Chicken

Once the chicken has marinated, remove it from the refrigerator. One piece at a time, take each chicken strip out of the marinade, allowing any excess liquid to drip off. First, coat the chicken in flour, shaking off any excess. Next, dip it into the egg wash, ensuring it is well coated. Finally, roll it in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the chicken. Repeat this process with all chicken strips.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken Tenders

You have two options for cooking your chicken tenders: frying or baking.

Frying Method

  1. In a large skillet, heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. The oil is ready when a breadcrumb dropped into the oil sizzles.
  2. Carefully add a few chicken tenders to the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 4-5 minutes on each side or until they are golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F).
  3. Once cooked, transfer the chicken tenders to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Baking Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place a wire rack on top. This will allow air to circulate around the chicken for even cooking.
  3. Arrange the breaded chicken tenders on the wire rack and lightly spray them with cooking oil for a crispy finish.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are golden and cooked through.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once your chicken tenders are cooked to perfection, it’s time to serve! These tenders pair beautifully with a variety of dipping sauces. Consider classic options like honey mustard, barbecue sauce, ranch dressing, or even a homemade dipping sauce of your choice. For a balanced meal, serve them alongside fresh vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a simple green salad.

Tips for Success

  1. Use Different Cuts of Chicken: While chicken breasts are the most common choice, chicken thighs can offer additional flavor and moisture. Adjust cooking times accordingly.

  2. Experiment with Seasonings: Feel free to get creative with your breading. Add different spices or herbs to the breadcrumb mixture, such as cumin, dill, or even grated Parmesan cheese for extra flavor.

  3. Make it a Family Activity: Get your kids involved in the cooking process. Allow them to help with breading the chicken or mixing the marinade. This not only makes cooking more fun but also encourages them to try new foods.

  4. Storage and Reheating: Leftover chicken tenders can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) until heated through and crispy again.

  5. Freeze for Later: If you want to make a large batch, you can freeze the breaded chicken tenders before cooking. Lay them out in a single layer on a baking sheet to freeze, then transfer them to a freezer bag. When ready to cook, you can bake them directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

Conclusion

Kid-friendly chicken tenders are a delicious and versatile dish that is sure to please the whole family. This recipe provides a simple, yet flavorful way to create a homemade version that rivals any restaurant or fast-food option. With a bit of preparation and creativity, you can make meal times exciting and enjoyable for your children.

Next time you’re in need of a quick and satisfying dinner idea, remember this chicken tender recipe. Not only will you be serving up a family favorite, but you’ll also be instilling healthy eating habits by preparing a meal that’s both nutritious and delicious. Happy cooking!
