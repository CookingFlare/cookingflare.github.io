Vegetarian Christmas Recipes for a Meatless Holiday


The holiday season brings with it the warmth of family gatherings, the joy of traditions, and, of course, the anticipation of delicious festive meals. For those opting for a meatless Christmas, either by choice or simply to try something new, creating a menu that’s hearty, festive, and satisfying is entirely possible—and deliciously exciting. From starters to desserts, vegetarian recipes can shine at your holiday table, offering vibrant flavors and comforting textures that elevate the celebration.

In this guide, you’ll find a collection of inspired vegetarian Christmas recipes designed to delight everyone, whether they’re lifelong vegetarians or simply curious about plant-based holiday dining. The dishes combine seasonal ingredients, bold spices, and creative twists that make meatless meals memorable. Each recipe celebrates the festive spirit while embracing the variety and richness of vegetarian cuisine.

Starters & Appetizers

Roasted Butternut Squash Soup with Sage and Crispy Shallots

Start your feast with a velvety roasted butternut squash soup. Its naturally sweet flavor pairs beautifully with savory sage and the crunch of caramelized shallots.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Crispy fried shallots for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Toss cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in a 400°F (200°C) oven for 25-30 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. In a large pot, sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add the roasted squash and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Stir in chopped sage, then blend the soup until smooth using an immersion blender.
  5. Adjust seasoning and serve garnished with crispy shallots.

Stuffed Mushrooms with Spinach and Ricotta

These little bites pack a punch with creamy ricotta, sautéed spinach, and garlic—perfect for nibbling alongside a glass of mulled wine.

Ingredients:

  • 24 large cremini mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • Salt, pepper, and nutmeg to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly oil a baking sheet.
  2. Sauté garlic and spinach in olive oil until spinach is wilted.
  3. Mix spinach with ricotta, Parmesan, salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  4. Fill mushroom caps with the mixture and place on the baking sheet.
  5. Drizzle with olive oil and bake for 20 minutes until mushrooms are tender and topping is golden.

Main Courses

Lentil and Mushroom Wellington

Elevate your Christmas main dish with a hearty lentil and mushroom Wellington. The flaky pastry crust encases a rich filling full of umami and festive herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, cooked and drained
  • 2 cups mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 sheet puff pastry (vegetarian-friendly)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add mushrooms and cook until moisture evaporates.
  3. Stir in lentils, herbs, soy sauce, salt, and pepper and cook for another 5 minutes. Let cool.
  4. Roll out puff pastry on a floured surface. Place the filling in the center and fold pastry over to seal.
  5. Brush with olive oil or plant-based milk and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.

Herb-Crusted Cauliflower Roast with Cranberry Glaze

A show-stopping centerpiece that’s both flavorful and festive, this cauliflower roast boasts a crunchy herb crust and sweet-tart cranberry glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower head, trimmed
  • 1 1/2 cups breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter or olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cranberry Glaze:
    • 1 cup fresh cranberries
    • 1/4 cup orange juice
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Steam cauliflower until just tender.
  2. Mix breadcrumbs, parsley, thyme, garlic, salt, pepper, and melted butter to form the herb crust.
  3. Pat cauliflower dry and brush it with melted butter, then press the crust mixture over it firmly.
  4. Roast cauliflower for 25-30 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  5. For the glaze, combine cranberries, orange juice, and maple syrup in a saucepan; simmer until cranberries pop and thicken.
  6. Brush glaze over the roasted cauliflower and serve immediately.

Sweet Potato and Chickpea Tagine

Incorporate warm spices and comforting textures with a Moroccan-inspired tagine, perfect for those seeking bold flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp harissa paste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot; sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Stir in spices and harissa paste, cooking for 1 minute.
  3. Add sweet potatoes, chickpeas, and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmer covered for 25-30 minutes.
  4. Adjust seasoning and stir in chopped cilantro before serving.

Side Dishes

Maple-Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Pecans and Dried Cranberries

This vibrant side dish balances savory, sweet, and tart flavors amidst a satisfying crunch of toasted pecans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. Drizzle with maple syrup, toss in pecans and dried cranberries, and roast for an additional 5 minutes.

Garlic Mashed Cauliflower with Chives

A low-carb yet rich alternative to traditional mashed potatoes, this creamy mashed cauliflower is a perfect comforting side.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head cauliflower, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, roasted or sautéed
  • 1/4 cup plant-based milk
  • 2 tbsp vegan butter
  • Fresh chives, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Steam cauliflower until very tender.
  2. Blend with garlic, plant-based milk, and vegan butter until smooth.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and stir in fresh chives.

Desserts

Spiced Pear and Cranberry Crisp

Finish your meal with a fruit dessert that’s warmly spiced and topped with a buttery oat crumble.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe pears, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter, cold and cubed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toss pears and cranberries with cinnamon and ginger, then place in a baking dish.
  3. Mix oats, brown sugar, and flour; cut in butter until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle crumble over fruit and bake for 30-35 minutes until golden and bubbly.

Vegan Chocolate Yule Log

Celebrate with an indulgent vegan chocolate Yule log that combines rich cake, silky mousse, and festive decoration.

Ingredients:

  • For the cake:
    • 1 cup flour
    • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
    • 3/4 cup sugar
    • 1 tsp baking powder
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1 cup plant-based milk
    • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • For the mousse:
    • 1 can coconut cream, chilled
    • 1/2 cup melted dark chocolate
    • 1/4 cup powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a jelly roll pan lined with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk dry ingredients together; in a separate bowl, combine wet ingredients.
  3. Stir wet into dry until smooth. Pour into pan and bake for 15 minutes.
  4. Turn warm cake onto a towel dusted with cocoa powder, roll gently, and cool.
  5. Whip coconut cream with powdered sugar until fluffy; fold in melted chocolate.
  6. Unroll cake, spread mousse, and reroll. Chill until set.
  7. Decorate with powdered sugar, berries, or vegan holly leaves if desired.

Final Thoughts on a Meatless Christmas Feast

A vegetarian Christmas meal can be just as indulgent and heartwarming as a traditional meat-based feast, with the added bonus of showcasing the natural flavors and textures of plant-based ingredients. These recipes offer ideas for each course to create a full, festive menu that’s sure to impress guests and satisfy even the most devoted carnivores. Experiment with your personal favorite herbs and seasonal vegetables, and don’t hesitate to add your own creative touches for a truly unique holiday celebration.

Enjoy crafting your memorable, meatless holiday feast!
