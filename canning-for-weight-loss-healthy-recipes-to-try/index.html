Canning for Weight Loss: Healthy Recipes to Try


Canning is a fantastic way to preserve the goodness of fresh produce and enjoy it all year round. Not only does it allow you to savor the flavors of summer during the colder months, but it also offers a great opportunity to create healthy recipes that support your weight loss goals. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and nutritious canning recipes specifically designed to help you on your weight loss journey. So, grab your canning jars and let’s get started!

1. Pickled Green Beans

Green beans are low in calories and packed with fiber, making them an excellent choice for weight loss. By pickling them, you add a tangy twist that enhances their flavor and makes them a delightful addition to any meal. Here’s how you can make your own pickled green beans:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 cups of white vinegar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sea salt
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled and crushed
  • 2 teaspoons of dill seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to proper canning procedures.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, sea salt, garlic, dill seeds, and red pepper flakes (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Blanch the green beans in boiling water for 2 minutes, then transfer them to an ice bath to stop the cooking process.
  4. Pack the green beans tightly into the sterilized canning jars.
  5. Pour the hot pickling liquid over the green beans, making sure to leave a ½ inch headspace.
  6. Remove any air bubbles by gently tapping the jars on the countertop.
  7. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip-tight.
  8. Process the jars in a boiling water canner for 10 minutes.
  9. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

2. Spicy Tomato Salsa

Tomatoes are rich in antioxidants and vitamins, making them a fantastic addition to any weight loss diet. By canning your own spicy tomato salsa, you can control the ingredients and enjoy a guilt-free, flavorful snack. Here’s how you can make it:

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds of ripe tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • ¼ cup of fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to proper canning procedures.
  2. In a large pot, combine the tomatoes, onions, garlic, jalapeño peppers, apple cider vinegar, lime juice, honey, cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in the fresh cilantro and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Ladle the hot salsa into the sterilized canning jars, leaving a ½ inch headspace.
  6. Remove any air bubbles by gently tapping the jars on the countertop.
  7. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip-tight.
  8. Process the jars in a boiling water canner for 15 minutes.
  9. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

3. Berry Chia Jam

Jam is often associated with high sugar content, but by making your own chia seed jam, you can enjoy the sweetness of berries without the guilt. Chia seeds are rich in fiber, omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants, making them a valuable addition to your weight loss journey. Here’s how you can make your own berry chia jam:

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of fresh or frozen mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • ¼ cup of chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of honey or maple syrup (optional, adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to proper canning procedures.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the mixed berries and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat until the berries start to break down and release their juices.
  3. Mash the berries with a fork or potato masher to your desired consistency.
  4. Stir in the chia seeds and honey or maple syrup (if using).
  5. Cook the mixture for an additional 5 minutes, stirring constantly.
  6. Ladle the hot jam into the sterilized canning jars, leaving a ½ inch headspace.
  7. Remove any air bubbles by gently tapping the jars on the countertop.
  8. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and tighten the bands fingertip-tight.
  9. Process the jars in a boiling water canner for 10 minutes.
  10. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Conclusion

Canning for weight loss allows you to enjoy the benefits of fresh, nutritious ingredients all year round. These healthy recipes provide a flavorful and guilt-free alternative to store-bought options, giving you full control over the ingredients and reducing unnecessary additives. So, grab your canning supplies and start preserving the goodness of nature while supporting your weight loss journey. Happy canning!
