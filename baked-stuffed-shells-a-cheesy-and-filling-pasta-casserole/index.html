Baked Stuffed Shells: A Cheesy and Filling Pasta Casserole


If you’re looking for a comforting and satisfying meal that will leave you begging for seconds, look no further than Baked Stuffed Shells. This cheesy and filling pasta casserole is the perfect dish for a cozy family dinner or a gathering with friends. With layers of rich flavors and gooey cheese, it’s guaranteed to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.

Ingredients:

  • 24 jumbo pasta shells
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until the beef is no longer pink. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper to the skillet. Stir well to combine, then let it simmer for about 10 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, and beaten egg. Set aside.
  6. Take a baking dish and spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce mixture on the bottom.
  7. Take each cooked pasta shell and stuff it generously with the cheese mixture. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish, arranging them in a single layer.
  8. Pour the remaining tomato sauce over the stuffed shells, covering them completely.
  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  10. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbly and the cheese is golden brown.
  11. Once baked, let the stuffed shells cool for a few minutes before serving.
  12. Garnish with some fresh parsley, if desired, and serve hot.

Tips and Variations:

  • You can experiment with different fillings for the stuffed shells. Try using a combination of spinach and ricotta cheese or adding some cooked ground sausage for extra flavor.
  • Feel free to add some grated mozzarella cheese on top of the tomato sauce before baking for an extra cheesy crust.
  • This dish can be made ahead of time and refrigerated. Simply assemble the stuffed shells, cover with plastic wrap, and store in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, remove the plastic wrap, cover with foil, and bake as directed. You may need to add a few extra minutes to the baking time to ensure the shells are heated through.

Baked Stuffed Shells are a crowd-pleasing dish that brings together the best of pasta, cheese, and savory flavors. The combination of the tender pasta shells, the rich and tangy tomato sauce, and the creamy cheese filling is simply irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just craving some comfort food, this recipe is sure to satisfy. Give it a try and watch as your guests devour every last bite!
