Paleo-Friendly Asian Beef Recipes: Quick and Flavorful


If you’re a fan of Asian cuisine and follow a Paleo diet, you’ll be delighted to discover that you can enjoy the flavors of Asia while staying true to your dietary preferences. In this blog post, we’ll explore some quick and flavorful Paleo-friendly Asian beef recipes that will satisfy your cravings and keep you on track with your health goals.

Asian Beef Stir-Fry

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Serving Size: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 tablespoon arrowroot powder
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Green onions, for garnish
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the coconut aminos, fish sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Marinate the sliced flank steak in this mixture for 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve the arrowroot powder in water to create a slurry.
  3. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the marinated beef and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove the beef from the wok and set aside.
  4. In the same wok, stir-fry the bell pepper and snow peas for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Return the beef to the wok and pour in the arrowroot slurry. Cook for another minute until the sauce thickens.
  6. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds before serving.

This Asian beef stir-fry is a perfect combination of tender beef, crisp vegetables, and a savory sauce that’s free from soy and gluten, making it an ideal Paleo-friendly dish.

Korean Beef Bulgogi

Prep Time: 20 minutes Marinating Time: 1-2 hours Cook Time: 10 minutes Serving Size: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ pounds of rib-eye or sirloin steak, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons pear juice
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, pear juice, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and honey (if using).
  2. Add the thinly sliced steak to the marinade and let it sit for 1-2 hours in the refrigerator.
  3. Heat the coconut oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated beef for 2-3 minutes until browned.
  4. Sprinkle sesame seeds and green onions over the beef before serving.

This Korean beef bulgogi recipe offers a delightful blend of sweet and savory flavors, showcasing the essence of Korean cuisine while adhering to Paleo principles.

Thai Basil Beef

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Serving Size: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon coconut sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced chili, and sauté for 1 minute.
  2. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in the coconut aminos, fish sauce, and coconut sugar. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Turn off the heat and stir in the fresh basil leaves until wilted.

This Thai basil beef recipe captures the vibrant and aromatic flavors of Thai cuisine, providing a satisfying and wholesome Paleo-friendly meal option.

Conclusion

These Paleo-friendly Asian beef recipes demonstrate that you can savor the diverse and enticing flavors of Asian cuisine while embracing a Paleo lifestyle. Whether you’re in the mood for a hearty stir-fry, a succulent bulgogi, or a fragrant Thai dish, these recipes offer a delightful fusion of flavors and culinary traditions, all while aligning with your dietary preferences. Enjoy exploring the rich tapestry of Asian flavors and embark on a culinary journey that celebrates both taste and well-being.
