Apricot Galette: A Rustic and Flaky Fruit Dessert


If you’re looking for a simple yet impressive dessert to showcase the natural sweetness of apricots, then look no further than an Apricot Galette. This rustic and flaky fruit dessert is sure to delight your taste buds and impress your guests. With its buttery crust and juicy apricot filling, it’s the perfect way to celebrate the flavors of summer.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • ¼ cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 pound fresh apricots, pitted and sliced
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon

For the glaze:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon turbinado sugar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a medium bowl, combine the sliced apricots, sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, and lemon zest. Gently toss until the apricots are coated evenly. Set aside.

  5. Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter.

  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Leaving a 2-inch border, carefully arrange the apricot slices in the center of the dough. Fold the edges of the dough over the fruit, creating pleats as you go. Press gently to seal any cracks.

  7. Brush the edges of the galette with the beaten egg to give it a golden color when baked. Sprinkle the turbinado sugar over the crust for added sweetness and texture (optional).

  8. Bake the galette in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apricots are tender. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for a few minutes.

  9. Serve the Apricot Galette warm or at room temperature. It pairs perfectly with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • If fresh apricots are not in season, you can use thawed frozen apricots for the filling. Just make sure to drain any excess liquid before using.

  • Feel free to experiment with different fruits for the filling. Peach, plum, or even a combination of berries would work well in this galette.

  • Add a sprinkle of ground cinnamon or a pinch of cardamom to the filling for an extra layer of flavor.

  • If you prefer a sweeter galette, you can increase the amount of sugar in the filling to suit your taste.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Reheat in the oven for a few minutes before serving.

So there you have it, a delicious Apricot Galette recipe that combines the simplicity of a rustic dessert with the elegance of flaky pastry and juicy apricots. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet treat, this galette is sure to impress. Give it a try and let its delightful flavors transport you to a sunny summer day.
