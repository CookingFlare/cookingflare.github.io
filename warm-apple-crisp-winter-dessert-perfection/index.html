Warm Apple Crisp: Winter Dessert Perfection


Winter is the season of cozying up by the fire, sipping hot cocoa, and indulging in comforting desserts. And what better dessert to warm your heart and soul than a delicious, homemade warm apple crisp? This classic dessert is the epitome of winter perfection, with its sweet and tart apple filling, topped with a crispy and buttery oat crumble.

Ingredients

For the apple filling:

  • 6 medium-sized apples (preferably Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the oat crumble topping:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss the apples until they are well coated with the sugar and spice mixture.

  3. Transfer the apple filling into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  4. In a separate mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt for the oat crumble topping. Add the cold cubed butter to the mixture and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  5. Sprinkle the oat crumble topping evenly over the apple filling in the baking dish, covering the apples completely.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 40-45 minutes, or until the apple filling is bubbling and the oat crumble topping is golden brown.

  7. Remove the warm apple crisp from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, straight out of the oven, but it can also be served at room temperature or reheated.

  8. Serve the warm apple crisp on its own, or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra touch of indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • For added flavor, consider mixing in a handful of dried cranberries or raisins to the apple filling before baking.
  • You can customize the oat crumble topping by adding chopped nuts, such as walnuts or pecans, or even a sprinkle of shredded coconut for extra crunch and texture.
  • If you prefer a gluten-free version, simply substitute the all-purpose flour in both the apple filling and oat crumble topping with a gluten-free flour blend or almond flour.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different apple varieties to find your perfect balance of sweetness and tartness. Some other great options include Pink Lady, Fuji, or Braeburn apples.

So, the next time you’re looking for a comforting dessert to warm you up during the winter months, give this warm apple crisp recipe a try. With its fragrant apple filling and irresistible oat crumble topping, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Enjoy the cozy flavors of winter with every delicious bite!
