Taste of Greece: Traditional Greek Chicken Souvlaki Wrap


As the sun sets over the Mediterranean, the aroma of sizzling souvlaki wafts through the air, tantalizing the taste buds of passersby. The quintessential Greek street food, souvlaki, is a staple of the country’s culinary culture, and its popularity extends far beyond Greece’s borders. Today, we’ll embark on a culinary journey to recreate the authentic flavors of Greece in the comfort of your own home. Say hello to the traditional Greek Chicken Souvlaki Wrap, a mouthwatering combination of juicy chicken, crispy pita, and tangy tzatziki sauce.

The Story Behind Souvlaki

Souvlaki, which translates to “skewers” in Greek, has its roots in ancient Greece, where skewers of meat were cooked over open flames. The modern version of souvlaki, however, is believed to have originated in the 1960s in Athens, where street vendors began serving skewers of chicken, pork, and beef to hungry locals and tourists alike. The dish quickly gained popularity, and today, souvlaki is a beloved national treasure, enjoyed by Greeks and international visitors alike.

The Recipe

To recreate the authentic flavors of Greece, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 pita breads
  • 1 cup tzatziki sauce (see below for recipe)
  • Chopped tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, garlic, oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken cubes and toss to coat.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken onto metal or wooden skewers, leaving a small space between each piece.
  4. Grill the skewers for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  5. Warm the pita breads by wrapping them in foil and heating them in the oven for 5 minutes.
  6. Assemble the wraps by placing a few pieces of souvlaki onto each pita, followed by a dollop of tzatziki sauce, some chopped tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers.

Tzatziki Sauce Recipe

  • 1 large cucumber, peeled and grated
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added crunch, top your souvlaki wrap with some crispy fried onions or crumbled feta cheese.
  • Substitute pork or beef skewers for a different flavor profile.
  • Serve with a side of creamy Greek salad or roasted potatoes for a satisfying meal.
  • Experiment with different spices and herbs to create your own unique souvlaki flavor.

Conclusion

As you take your first bite of the traditional Greek Chicken Souvlaki Wrap, the explosion of flavors will transport you to the sun-kissed streets of Greece. The combination of juicy chicken, crispy pita, and tangy tzatziki sauce is a match made in heaven, and with this recipe, you’ll be able to recreate the authentic taste of Greece in the comfort of your own home. So go ahead, fire up the grill, and get ready to indulge in a culinary adventure that will leave you craving for more.
