Authentic Mexican Drinks to Pair with Your Meals


When it comes to the vibrant and diverse culinary landscape of Mexico, it’s not just the food that steals the show. The beverages crafted in this rich culture play an equally enticing role in elevating meals from ordinary to extraordinary. In this post, we explore some of the most authentic Mexican drinks that perfectly complement a variety of traditional dishes, ensuring a delightful dining experience.

1. Agua Fresca

What is Agua Fresca?

Agua fresca translates to “fresh water,” and it’s a staple in Mexican households, especially during the hot summer months. Made with fresh fruits, water, and a touch of sugar, these refreshing drinks are not only hydrating but also bursting with flavor.

Popular Flavors

  • Horchata: A creamy blend of rice, cinnamon, and vanilla, horchata offers a sweet, milky compliment to spicy dishes like tacos al pastor.
  • Jamaica: This hibiscus flower drink has a tart, cranberry-like flavor that pairs beautifully with savory foods, balancing the richness of mole or grilled meats.
  • Tamarindo: With its tamarind fruit base, this drink offers a delightful tartness that contrasts perfectly with the hearty flavors of pozole.

Recipe: Horchata

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of white rice
  • 5 cups of water (divided)
  • 1 cup of milk (or almond milk for a vegan option)
  • 1/2 cup of sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp of ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the rice in 2 cups of water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  3. Blend the soaked rice with the soaking water until smooth.
  4. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve or cheesecloth into a pitcher.
  5. Stir in milk, sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon. Serve chilled over ice.

2. Mexican Hot Chocolate (Champurrado)

What is Champurrado?

This thick, spiced hot chocolate is a traditional Mexican drink, often enjoyed during breakfast or as an evening treat. Made with masa (corn dough), chocolate, and milk, it’s a comforting beverage that pairs well with pastries or churros.

Flavor Profile

Champurrado is rich and creamy, with a hint of cinnamon and vanilla. The unique texture from the masa adds a delightful thickness that rounds out the experience, making it a perfect pairing for sweet or savory breakfast dishes.

Recipe: Champurrado

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of milk or water
  • 1/2 cup of masa harina
  • 1/2 cup of chocolate (Mexican chocolate, chopped)
  • 1/4 cup of sugar (or to taste)
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp of ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the masa harina and water or milk, whisking until smooth.
  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook, stirring constantly, until it thickens.
  3. Add the chocolate, sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon. Continue to stir until the chocolate melts completely.
  4. Serve hot, ideally with a side of churros or conchas.

3. Tequila Cocktails

The Spirit of Mexico

Tequila, made from the blue agave plant, is a quintessential Mexican spirit that deserves a central place in any discussion about drinks. While sipping it straight is a time-honored tradition, crafting cocktails is a great way to enhance flavors and pair with food.

Classic Cocktails

  • Margarita: A classic pairing with tacos or grilled fish, the margarita combines tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur for a zesty kick.
  • Paloma: This refreshing cocktail blends tequila with grapefruit soda and lime juice, making it an excellent match for spicy dishes like carne asada.

Recipe: Classic Margarita

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz of tequila
  • 1 oz of lime juice
  • 1 oz of orange liqueur (like Cointreau or Triple Sec)
  • Salt for rimming the glass
  • Lime wedge for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rub the rim of a glass with a lime wedge and dip it into salt.
  2. In a shaker, combine tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur with ice.
  3. Shake well and strain into the prepared glass filled with ice.
  4. Garnish with a lime wedge and enjoy.

4. Mexican Beer

The Golden Elixir

Mexico has a robust beer culture, with a variety of brands offering unique flavors that pair well with various dishes. Light lagers are often consumed with tacos, while darker beers can complement hearty dishes.

Notable Brands

  • Corona: Widely recognizable, this light lager is perfect with ceviche or fish tacos, especially when garnished with a lime wedge.
  • Modelo Especial: A slightly fuller-bodied lager, it pairs excellently with grilled meats and spicy salsas.

Pairing Suggestions

  • Tacos al Pastor: Pair with a cold Corona or Modelo to balance the spiciness and acidity of the dish.
  • Mole Poblano: A darker beer like Negra Modelo complements the rich, complex flavors of this traditional sauce.

5. Cerveza de Jamaica

A Unique Twist

Cerveza de Jamaica is a craft beer brewed with dried hibiscus flowers, offering a refreshing and tart flavor profile. This innovative drink can enhance the experience of a traditional Mexican meal.

Flavor Pairings

This beer’s fruity and floral notes make it an ideal match for spicy dishes like nachos or enchiladas, providing a refreshing contrast.

Recipe: Homemade Cerveza de Jamaica

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of dried hibiscus flowers
  • 5 cups of water
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 lemon (juiced)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the water in a large pot and add the dried hibiscus flowers. Simmer for about 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture into a pitcher, discarding the flowers.
  3. Add sugar and lemon juice, stirring until dissolved.
  4. Chill and serve over ice, or mix with your favorite beer for a refreshing twist.

6. Atole

A Warm Embrace

Atole is a warm, thick drink made from masa, water, and various flavorings. It’s a traditional beverage often enjoyed during breakfast or alongside tamales, making it a comforting choice for any meal.

Flavor Variations

  • Vanilla Atole: Sweet and fragrant, this version pairs well with cinnamon rolls or sweet breads.
  • Chocolate Atole: Rich and decadent, it’s the perfect companion to pastries or as a dessert drink.

Recipe: Vanilla Atole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of masa harina
  • 4 cups of milk
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp of ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, mix masa harina with water until smooth.
  2. Gradually add milk while stirring to avoid lumps. Heat over medium until it thickens.
  3. Stir in sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Serve warm, ideally with sweet pastries.

7. Café de Olla

The Soul of Mexican Coffee

Café de olla is a traditional Mexican coffee prepared with cinnamon and piloncillo (unrefined cane sugar). This aromatic brew is often served in clay pots and complements a wide range of breakfasts, especially pastries and bread.

Flavor Experience

The combination of coffee, cinnamon, and sweetness creates a comforting drink that keeps you warm and energized, making it an excellent partner for sweet or savory dishes alike.

Recipe: Café de Olla

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of coarsely ground coffee
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 tablespoons of piloncillo (or brown sugar)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring water to a boil and add the cinnamon stick.
  2. Once boiling, remove from heat and add the coffee grounds. Let it steep for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the coffee into mugs, sweetening it with piloncillo or brown sugar.
  4. Serve hot, enjoying it alongside bolillos or pan dulce.

Conclusion

Mexican beverages offer a delightful range of flavors, textures, and aromas that can beautifully complement meals. From the refreshing notes of aguas frescas to the rich warmth of café de olla, each drink enhances the dining experience, making Mexican cuisine even more enjoyable. Whether you’re enjoying a casual meal at home or hosting a festive gathering, these authentic drinks will elevate your culinary journey into the heart of Mexico. Cheers to a flavorful adventure!
