Farm to Table Cooking: 7 Recipes for a Flavorful Fall Feast


Fall is a season that beckons with its rich bounty of harvests, offering an array of ingredients bursting with flavor, color, and texture. When you choose farm-to-table cooking, you’re not only indulging in seasonal freshness but also supporting local farmers and sustainable practices. This fall, take advantage of the crisp air and the abundance of produce that flourishes at this time of year. From hearty root vegetables to succulent apples and freshly harvested greens, fall ingredients are perfect for creating a memorable feast that celebrates the best of what nature has to offer.

In this post, we’ll dive into seven farm-to-table recipes that will help you create a flavorful fall feast, using ingredients that are fresh, local, and full of flavor. These dishes will infuse your home with the warm and comforting aromas of fall, bringing your table to life with vibrant colors and rich textures. Whether you’re preparing for a family dinner or a festive gathering with friends, these recipes will showcase the very essence of fall’s delicious offerings.

1. Butternut Squash Soup with Sage and Maple

Butternut squash is a quintessential fall vegetable that combines a natural sweetness with a creamy texture, making it ideal for soups. This recipe pairs the velvety squash with the earthy flavors of sage and the rich sweetness of maple syrup for a truly comforting dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the diced butternut squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and lightly caramelized.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Once the squash is roasted, add it to the pot with the onions and garlic. Pour in the vegetable broth, bringing everything to a simmer.
  4. Stir in the chopped sage and maple syrup, allowing the flavors to meld together. Simmer for another 10 minutes.
  5. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, or carefully transfer to a blender in batches. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
  6. For a creamier texture, stir in heavy cream before serving.

This soup is the perfect way to kick off your fall feast, offering a balance of savory, sweet, and herby notes that will warm you from the inside out.

2. Roasted Root Vegetables with Garlic and Thyme

Root vegetables are at their peak in fall, and roasting them enhances their natural sweetness. This simple yet flavorful side dish brings together carrots, parsnips, sweet potatoes, and beets, all roasted to perfection with garlic and fresh thyme.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 medium parsnips, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 medium beets, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the diced root vegetables with olive oil, minced garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper until everything is evenly coated.
  3. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on the baking sheet, making sure they are not crowded.
  4. Roast for 30-35 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are golden brown and tender.
  5. Serve warm as a side dish to any of your fall main courses.

These roasted root vegetables are the perfect companion to any fall feast, bringing a hearty, earthy flavor that complements roasted meats or grains.

3. Apple and Kale Salad with Walnuts and Balsamic Vinaigrette

Apple season is in full swing during the fall, and the crispness of fresh apples pairs beautifully with the hearty greens of kale. This salad offers a balance of sweet, tart, and savory flavors, making it a perfect addition to your fall table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups kale, washed and chopped
  • 2 apples, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chopped kale, apple slices, and toasted walnuts.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to create the dressing.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine. If using, sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese on top.
  4. Serve immediately, or allow the salad to sit for 10 minutes for the flavors to meld together.

This salad is a light yet flavorful addition to your fall feast, with the sweetness of apples and the crunch of walnuts providing a delightful contrast to the bitterness of the kale.

4. Pumpkin Risotto with Parmesan and Sage

Creamy risotto is the perfect dish to showcase the rich and comforting flavors of fall. This recipe combines pumpkin puree with the creaminess of risotto and the savory notes of Parmesan and sage, creating a rich, comforting dish that’s perfect for a fall dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1 small pumpkin, peeled, seeded, and cubed (or 1 cup canned pumpkin puree)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the pumpkin cubes and cook for another 5 minutes until softened. If using canned pumpkin puree, skip this step and add the puree directly in the next step.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing it to lightly toast.
  4. Pour in the white wine, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Begin adding the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently and waiting for the liquid to absorb before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is tender and creamy, about 20 minutes.
  6. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, fresh sage, and remaining tablespoon of butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with additional Parmesan cheese if desired.

This pumpkin risotto is the ultimate comfort food for fall, combining the creaminess of risotto with the natural sweetness of pumpkin and the fragrance of fresh sage.

5. Crispy Chicken Thighs with Apple-Cider Glaze

Nothing says fall like the sweet and tangy flavor of apple cider. In this dish, chicken thighs are seared to crispy perfection and drizzled with a savory-sweet apple-cider glaze, making it an irresistible main course.

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs, skin side down, and cook for 6-8 minutes until the skin is crispy and golden brown. Flip the chicken and cook for another 6-8 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  3. While the chicken is cooking, combine the apple cider, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, and minced garlic in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes until the glaze has thickened slightly.
  4. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set aside. Pour the apple-cider glaze into the skillet, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  5. Return the chicken to the skillet, spoon the glaze over the chicken, and cook for another 2 minutes to allow the chicken to soak up the flavors of the glaze.
  6. Serve warm with mashed potatoes or roasted vegetables.

This dish brings together the best of fall flavors with the richness of the chicken and the tangy sweetness of the apple-cider glaze.

6. Stuffed Acorn Squash with Quinoa and Cranberries

Acorn squash is a versatile and flavorful vegetable that works wonderfully as a vessel for a hearty stuffing. In this recipe, it’s filled with a mixture of quinoa, cranberries, and toasted pecans, creating a perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup toasted pecans, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the squash halves cut side down on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, and toasted pecans.
  3. Once the squash is roasted, flip the halves over and season the inside with salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff each squash half with the quinoa mixture, pressing it down gently to pack it in.
  5. Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Serve warm as a main course or side dish.

This stuffed acorn squash offers a delightful blend of textures, with the nutty quinoa, tart cranberries, and crunchy pecans providing a perfect contrast to the soft, sweet squash.

7. Pear and Gorgonzola Tart

For a sweet and savory end to your fall feast, this pear and Gorgonzola tart is a showstopper. The combination of caramelized pears, tangy Gorgonzola, and a buttery pastry crust makes for an unforgettable dessert or appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 ripe pears, sliced
  • 1/2 cup Gorgonzola cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the puff pastry on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Arrange the sliced pears in a single layer on the pastry, leaving a small border around the edges.
  3. Sprinkle the crumbled Gorgonzola cheese over the pears, drizzle with honey, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and puffed.
  5. Remove from the oven and sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves before serving.

This pear and Gorgonzola tart is the perfect balance of savory and sweet, making it a standout dish for any fall meal.

These seven recipes offer a diverse and flavorful selection of dishes that showcase the best of fall’s harvest. Whether you’re enjoying a quiet dinner at home or entertaining loved ones, these farm-to-table meals will bring the warmth and comfort of the season to your table. Enjoy the season’s bounty with these delicious dishes, and let each bite remind you of the beauty and abundance of fall!
