Tasty Nut-Free Shrimp Scampi Recipe


Shrimp scampi is a classic dish that’s beloved for its rich flavors and quick preparation time. Traditionally made with garlic, butter, and white wine, this dish is a crowd-pleaser that can be served over pasta or enjoyed on its own. In this blog post, we will explore a delicious nut-free shrimp scampi recipe that’s perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy dinner at home or a festive gathering with friends and family.

What is Shrimp Scampi?

Shrimp scampi originated from Italy but has become a popular dish in American cuisine. The term “scampi” actually refers to a type of small crustacean, but in the U.S., it has come to mean shrimp prepared in a specific style. The dish typically features shrimp sautéed in a fragrant garlic butter sauce, often accompanied by white wine and a hint of lemon.

This nut-free version ensures that everyone at your table can enjoy this delectable meal without any worries about allergens.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this tasty nut-free shrimp scampi, gather the following ingredients:

For the Shrimp Scampi:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 ounces linguine or spaghetti (or your favorite pasta)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (or chicken broth for a non-alcoholic option)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Optional Garnishes:

  • Grated Parmesan cheese (omit for a dairy-free version)
  • Extra lemon wedges, for serving

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

  1. Boil the Water: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil.
  2. Cook the Pasta: Add the linguine or spaghetti to the boiling water, cooking according to package instructions until al dente.
  3. Reserve Pasta Water: Before draining, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water. This starchy water will help thicken and enrich the sauce.
  4. Drain the Pasta: Drain the pasta and set it aside, tossing it lightly with a drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking.

Step 2: Prepare the Shrimp Scampi Sauce

  1. Heat the Oil and Butter: In a large skillet over medium heat, add the olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter. Allow the butter to melt and foam up.
  2. Sauté the Garlic: Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes to the skillet. Sauté for about 1-2 minutes until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.
  3. Add the Shrimp: Increase the heat to medium-high and add the shrimp to the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes on one side, until they begin to turn pink.
  4. Flip the Shrimp: Turn the shrimp over and let them cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until they are fully cooked and opaque. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine (or chicken broth) and scrape any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes, allowing the liquid to reduce slightly.
  6. Add Lemon Juice and Zest: Stir in the lemon juice and zest, and the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Allow the butter to melt completely, creating a rich sauce.

Step 3: Combine Pasta and Sauce

  1. Add the Pasta: Add the drained pasta to the skillet with the shrimp and sauce.
  2. Mix Well: Toss everything together, adding reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach your desired consistency.
  3. Add Fresh Parsley: Sprinkle in the chopped parsley and toss again to combine.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your nut-free shrimp scampi immediately, garnished with additional parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Pair it with a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for the Best Shrimp Scampi

  • Quality Shrimp: Use fresh or high-quality frozen shrimp for the best flavor. If using frozen shrimp, make sure to thaw them completely and pat them dry before cooking.
  • Don’t Overcook the Shrimp: Shrimp cook quickly, so watch them closely. Overcooked shrimp can become rubbery and tough.
  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the amount of red pepper flakes or omit them entirely.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add other ingredients like cherry tomatoes, spinach, or capers for added flavor and nutrition.

Nutritional Benefits of Shrimp

Shrimp is not only delicious but also offers numerous health benefits. It’s low in calories and high in protein, making it a great addition to a balanced diet. Shrimp is also a good source of selenium, vitamin B12, and omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for heart health.

Conclusion

This nut-free shrimp scampi recipe is a delightful dish that’s easy to prepare and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re serving it for a special occasion or a simple weeknight dinner, it’s sure to impress your guests and family alike.

So next time you want to indulge in a seafood dish that’s both tasty and allergen-friendly, try making this shrimp scampi. With its perfect blend of garlic, butter, and lemon, it’s a meal that will leave everyone asking for seconds. Enjoy your culinary adventure, and happy cooking!
