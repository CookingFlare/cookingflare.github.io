Don't Waste That Citrus: 5 Creative Ways to Use Up Leftover Lemon Peels


We all love a refreshing glass of lemonade or a squeeze of lemon juice in our favorite recipes. But what do you do with the leftover lemon peels? It’s a shame to waste them when there are so many creative ways to put them to good use. In this blog post, we’ll explore five innovative ways to utilize those leftover lemon peels and add a burst of citrusy flavor to your dishes.

1. Citrus Zest Salt

One of the easiest ways to use up leftover lemon peels is by making citrus zest salt. This simple yet versatile ingredient can be used to enhance the flavor of a variety of dishes. Start by drying out your lemon peels until they become crispy. Once dried, finely grind the peels in a food processor or spice grinder. Mix the ground lemon peels with sea salt, creating a delicious citrus-infused salt. Use this zesty salt to season roasted vegetables, grilled meats, or even sprinkle it over a fresh salad for an extra kick of flavor.

2. Lemon Sugar Scrub

Treat yourself to a luxurious and invigorating spa-like experience with a homemade lemon sugar scrub. Lemon peels are packed with natural oils that can nourish and exfoliate your skin. To make the scrub, combine finely grated lemon zest with granulated sugar and coconut oil. Gently massage the mixture onto your skin, focusing on rough areas like elbows and heels. Rinse off and revel in the silky smoothness of your skin. The fresh citrus scent will leave you feeling refreshed and energized.

3. Lemon Infused Vinegar

Transform ordinary vinegar into a tangy and fragrant cleaning solution by infusing it with lemon peels. Fill a clean glass jar with lemon peels and cover them completely with white vinegar. Seal the jar and let it sit for a few weeks, allowing the lemon oils to infuse into the vinegar. Once ready, strain the infused vinegar and use it as a natural cleaning solution for countertops, windows, and even as a fabric softener. Say goodbye to harsh chemicals and hello to a naturally fresh and clean home.

4. Lemon Ice Cubes

Add a burst of citrus flavor to your favorite beverages by freezing lemon peels into ice cubes. Simply cut the lemon peels into small pieces and place them in an ice cube tray. Fill the tray with water and freeze until solid. These lemon-infused ice cubes are perfect for adding a refreshing twist to water, cocktails, or even homemade lemonade. They also make beautiful garnishes for summer drinks, adding a pop of color and a hint of zesty goodness.

5. Lemon Peel Potpourri

Create a delightful and aromatic atmosphere in your home by making lemon peel potpourri. Dry out your lemon peels and combine them with other fragrant ingredients like dried herbs, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. Place the mixture in a decorative bowl or sachet and let the natural citrus scent fill the air. This homemade potpourri not only adds a pleasant fragrance to your home but also serves as a natural air freshener without any harmful chemicals.

Now that you know these creative ways to use up leftover lemon peels, you can enjoy the full potential of this versatile citrus fruit. From enhancing the flavor of your dishes to creating natural cleaning solutions and invigorating skincare products, don’t let those lemon peels go to waste. Embrace their zestful essence and explore the endless possibilities they offer.

So, the next time you enjoy a glass of lemonade or squeeze a lemon into your recipe, remember to save those peels and get creative. Your taste buds, skin, and home will thank you for it!
