Best Supermarket Tortillas: A Comprehensive Review


Tortillas are a staple in many households, providing the perfect base for wraps, tacos, burritos, quesadillas, and much more. Whether you’re a fan of soft flour tortillas, hearty corn tortillas, or prefer the versatility of whole wheat or gluten-free options, there’s a tortilla for everyone. But with so many options available in the supermarket, how do you choose the best one for your next meal?

In this comprehensive review, we’ll take a deep dive into the most popular tortillas available at supermarkets, comparing their taste, texture, price, and nutritional value. By the end, you’ll have a solid understanding of which tortillas reign supreme, helping you make an informed choice for your next grocery run.

The Basics of Tortillas

Tortillas are a type of unleavened flatbread made from either corn or wheat. The texture and taste of a tortilla depend heavily on its ingredients and preparation method. While corn tortillas are made using masa harina (corn flour) and water, flour tortillas are made with all-purpose flour, water, fat, and sometimes a leavening agent. The result is a flexible, soft bread that can be used to wrap fillings for various dishes.

In recent years, supermarket shelves have seen an explosion in the variety of tortillas available, ranging from traditional options to newer, more niche varieties like low-carb, gluten-free, and organic tortillas. It’s important to recognize the different types available and how each may best suit your culinary needs.

The Types of Tortillas Available

1. Flour Tortillas

Flour tortillas are the most common variety and are known for their soft, chewy texture and versatility. They can be used for wraps, burritos, quesadillas, or even breakfast dishes. When you buy them from the supermarket, you’ll find a range of sizes, from small taco-sized tortillas to extra-large burrito wraps.

The dough for flour tortillas is typically made from wheat flour, water, fat (often shortening or lard), and a bit of salt. Some brands add preservatives to extend shelf life, while others opt for a more natural, preservative-free version. This gives consumers options depending on their preference for ingredients.

2. Corn Tortillas

Corn tortillas, made from masa harina, are the traditional choice in many Latin American countries. They’re essential for tacos and enchiladas and have a slightly firmer texture than their flour counterparts. Corn tortillas are a great option for those seeking a gluten-free alternative since they contain no wheat.

Unlike flour tortillas, corn tortillas are often thicker and more delicate, making them more prone to cracking if not properly warmed or handled. However, they have a rich, slightly sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with savory fillings like meats, cheese, and beans.

3. Whole Wheat Tortillas

Whole wheat tortillas offer a more nutritious alternative to traditional flour tortillas, as they are made from whole wheat flour. They typically have a denser texture and a slightly nuttier flavor compared to regular flour tortillas. These tortillas are a good option for those looking to add more fiber to their diet without sacrificing flavor.

4. Gluten-Free Tortillas

For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, gluten-free tortillas are a lifesaver. Many brands have started producing gluten-free options made from rice flour, corn flour, or a blend of gluten-free grains. While the texture and taste may differ slightly from regular flour tortillas, these tortillas still provide a versatile option for tacos, burritos, and wraps.

5. Low-Carb Tortillas

Low-carb tortillas are becoming more popular, especially among those following keto, paleo, or other low-carb diets. These tortillas are typically made with almond flour, coconut flour, or a blend of other low-carb flours. They are designed to mimic the texture of traditional flour tortillas while keeping the carbohydrate content low.

How We Tested the Tortillas

To determine which supermarket tortillas are the best, we conducted a comprehensive taste test and comparison. Each tortilla was judged on the following criteria:

  • Taste: How flavorful was the tortilla on its own? Did it complement the fillings it was paired with?
  • Texture: Was the tortilla soft and pliable, or did it crack when rolled? Did it hold up well with fillings?
  • Freshness: How fresh did the tortilla taste? Was it moist and soft or dry and tough?
  • Nutritional Value: How healthy was the tortilla? Did it offer any nutritional benefits, such as fiber or protein?
  • Price: How does the tortilla compare in terms of cost? Does it provide good value for money?

Let’s dive into our top picks based on these tests.

The Best Flour Tortillas

1. Mission Flour Tortillas

Mission is a household name when it comes to tortillas, and their flour tortillas consistently perform well in taste tests. Known for their soft, flexible texture and great flavor, Mission tortillas are ideal for everything from burritos to quesadillas.

  • Taste: Mildly sweet and slightly buttery, Mission tortillas have a well-balanced flavor that works well with both savory and sweet fillings.
  • Texture: Soft, pliable, and chewy, these tortillas don’t crack easily when rolled and maintain their flexibility.
  • Price: Mission tortillas are reasonably priced and offer good value for the quality.
  • Nutritional Value: A standard Mission flour tortilla contains about 140 calories and 3 grams of protein per serving.

2. La Banderita Flour Tortillas

Another strong contender in the flour tortilla category is La Banderita. These tortillas have a slightly thicker texture than Mission tortillas, making them perfect for heartier fillings.

  • Taste: La Banderita tortillas have a richer, more savory flavor compared to some other brands, making them ideal for dishes like burritos or fajitas.
  • Texture: Slightly thicker but still soft and pliable. They have a nice chewiness that adds depth to each bite.
  • Price: La Banderita tortillas are similarly priced to Mission and often come in large value packs.
  • Nutritional Value: These tortillas have about 150 calories per serving with 4 grams of protein, making them a slightly higher-calorie option compared to Mission tortillas.

The Best Corn Tortillas

1. Guerrero Corn Tortillas

Guerrero is one of the most trusted brands for corn tortillas, known for its authenticity and quality. These tortillas are made with 100% corn masa and have a slightly thicker texture, making them perfect for tacos or enchiladas.

  • Taste: Guerrero tortillas have a rich, corn-forward flavor that gives a distinct taste to tacos and other dishes. They are mildly sweet but not overpowering.
  • Texture: Soft yet sturdy, Guerrero tortillas hold up well when filled with meats and toppings, but they are still delicate enough for a true corn tortilla experience.
  • Price: These tortillas are priced slightly higher than some of the generic brands, but they offer great taste and quality.
  • Nutritional Value: Guerrero tortillas are lower in calories (around 100 per serving) and are an excellent gluten-free option.

2. TortillaLand Corn Tortillas

TortillaLand offers an alternative to the traditional pre-cooked corn tortillas, as their tortillas are uncooked and need to be warmed before eating. This gives them a fresher taste and texture.

  • Taste: Once cooked, TortillaLand tortillas have a slightly smoky and deeply corn-flavored taste that is hard to beat.
  • Texture: They are thicker and heartier than most pre-cooked tortillas, which makes them ideal for heartier fillings.
  • Price: TortillaLand tortillas are generally more expensive due to their uncooked nature, but the quality justifies the price.
  • Nutritional Value: With about 110 calories per tortilla, they’re comparable to other corn tortilla brands and are free from preservatives.

The Best Whole Wheat Tortillas

1. Mission Whole Wheat Tortillas

For those looking for a healthier option, Mission’s whole wheat tortillas provide a balance between taste and nutrition. They are made with whole wheat flour, offering more fiber and protein than regular flour tortillas.

  • Taste: The flavor is slightly nuttier than regular flour tortillas, but it doesn’t overpower the fillings.
  • Texture: Soft and pliable, although they are a bit denser than traditional flour tortillas. They’re perfect for wraps and burritos.
  • Price: Mission whole wheat tortillas are priced similarly to their regular flour tortillas, making them an affordable choice for health-conscious shoppers.
  • Nutritional Value: Each tortilla contains about 120 calories, 3 grams of protein, and 3 grams of fiber, offering a better nutritional profile than standard flour tortillas.

The Best Gluten-Free Tortillas

1. Siete Foods Almond Flour Tortillas

Siete Foods is a popular brand in the gluten-free tortilla market, offering almond flour tortillas that are not only gluten-free but also grain-free. These tortillas are perfect for those with specific dietary needs.

  • Taste: These tortillas have a nutty, slightly sweet flavor, with a subtle almond taste that pairs well with various fillings.
  • Texture: Siete tortillas are thinner than most other gluten-free options but still manage to hold together well when used in wraps or tacos.
  • Price: Siete products are on the pricier side, but they offer exceptional quality and cater to specific dietary restrictions.
  • Nutritional Value: Siete tortillas contain around 190 calories and 6 grams of protein per tortilla, which is higher than most gluten-free alternatives.

Conclusion

The supermarket tortilla aisle can be overwhelming, with so many choices available. However, whether you’re after soft flour tortillas, hearty corn tortillas, or gluten-free options, there’s a perfect tortilla for every need. The brands we’ve highlighted in this review—Mission, La Banderita, Guerrero, and Siete—represent the best of each category, delivering excellent taste, texture, and nutritional value.

Ultimately, the best tortilla for you will depend on your personal preferences, dietary restrictions, and the types of dishes you’re preparing. But with our detailed comparison, you’ll be well-equipped to pick the tortilla that best suits your tastes and culinary needs. Happy cooking!
