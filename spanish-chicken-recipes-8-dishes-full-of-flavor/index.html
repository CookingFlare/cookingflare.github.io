Spanish Chicken Recipes: 8 Dishes Full of Flavor


When it comes to Spanish cuisine, chicken takes center stage in a variety of beloved dishes that celebrate bold flavors, vibrant spices, and hearty ingredients. Whether simmered in rich sauces, roasted with smoky paprika, or paired with earthy saffron rice, Spanish chicken recipes offer a diversity that suits any palate. If you’re looking to infuse your kitchen with tantalizing aromas and delicious, home-cooked charm, this collection of eight Spanish chicken recipes delivers everything you need.

1. Classic Pollo al Ajillo (Garlic Chicken)

Pollo al Ajillo is a simple yet flavorful dish that highlights garlic’s pungent aroma paired with tender chicken pieces. This recipe is beloved throughout Spain for its rustic charm and ease of preparation.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 8 cloves of garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (pimentón)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (optional)

Preparation:

Begin by seasoning the chicken thighs generously with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the smashed garlic cloves and sauté until fragrant but not browned, about 2 minutes. Push garlic to the side and add the chicken thighs skin-side down. Cook until the skin is crispy and golden, about 8 minutes, then flip and cook through on the other side for another 10-12 minutes.

The garlic will infuse the olive oil and chicken with its signature flavor. Once cooked, sprinkle chopped parsley on top and serve with fresh lemon wedges to brighten the dish.

2. Arroz con Pollo (Spanish Chicken and Rice)

A Spanish staple and a crowd-pleaser, Arroz con Pollo combines tender chicken with saffron-infused rice cooked with vegetables, herbs, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups short-grain Spanish rice (Bomba rice preferred)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tomato, grated or finely chopped
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

Sear the chicken pieces in olive oil over medium-high heat until brown on all sides and set aside. In the same pot, sauté onion, bell pepper, and garlic until soft. Add the grated tomato, smoked paprika, and saffron, stirring to combine. Pour in rice and stir for a couple of minutes to toast it lightly and absorb flavors.

Return the chicken to the pot, add broth, and bring to a simmer. Cook uncovered on medium-low heat for about 20 minutes, adding peas halfway through. The rice should be tender and the chicken cooked through. Adjust seasoning and let stand for five minutes before serving.

3. Pollo a la Catalana (Catalan-Style Chicken)

Distinctive for its sweet and savory flavor, Pollo a la Catalana uses a blend of cured meats and fruit, reminiscent of Catalonia’s rich culinary traditions.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 100g serrano ham, chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup peeled and chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins or sultanas
  • Fresh thyme
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

In a large pan, heat olive oil and cook chicken thighs until lightly browned. Remove and set aside. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent, then add serrano ham, tomatoes, almonds, and raisins. Pour in white wine and chicken broth, scraping up any bits from the pan. Add the chicken back, sprinkle with thyme, and simmer gently for about 30 minutes until the chicken is tender and the sauce reduced.

Serve with crusty bread or roasted potatoes to soak up the rich sauce.

4. Pollo al Chilindrón (Chicken Chilindrón)

A beloved dish from Aragon, Pollo al Chilindrón is a stew featuring peppers, tomatoes, and ham, simmered to create a deeply flavored sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted, peeled, and sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 100g jamón serrano (or prosciutto), diced
  • 2 large tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Brown the chicken pieces in olive oil and set aside. In the same pan, sauté the onions and garlic until softened, add diced ham, and fry for a minute. Add peppers and tomatoes, then sprinkle smoked paprika. Return chicken to the pan, cover, and cook over low heat for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

The sweet roasted peppers and savory ham-rich sauce are a flavorful match for the juicy chicken.

5. Pollo con Pimientos (Chicken with Peppers)

This straightforward but vibrant dish uses fresh bell peppers and a touch of sherry to enhance the natural taste of the chicken.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts or thighs
  • 2 red bell peppers, sliced
  • 2 green bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry sherry (or white wine)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper, then brown in olive oil in a large skillet. Remove and set aside. Add onions, garlic, and peppers to the pan and sauté until tender. Pour in sherry and scrape any chicken bits from the pan. Return the chicken to the skillet, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes until cooked through.

This dish shines with the fresh pepper’s sweetness and the subtle complexity of sherry.

6. Pollo con Salsa Verde (Chicken with Green Sauce)

This classic green sauce, made with parsley, garlic, and vinegar, brings a fresh herbal twist to Spanish chicken dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 1 bunch parsley, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Brown the chicken thighs in olive oil and cook through in the pan. Meanwhile, mix parsley, garlic, vinegar, a pinch of salt, and olive oil to create the green sauce. When the chicken is ready, pour the sauce over the top and let it meld for a few minutes before serving.

This bright, zesty sauce is fantastic served with roasted veggies or boiled potatoes.

7. Pollo con Almendras (Chicken with Almonds)

Bringing a subtle crunch and nutty flavor, this dish often combines toasted almonds with a chicken stew, sometimes finished with a splash of sherry.

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken thighs, skin-on
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup toasted almonds
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or sherry
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Brown the chicken in olive oil and set aside. In the same pan, sauté onions and garlic, then add toasted almonds and stir. Pour in wine and chicken stock, then return chicken to the sauce. Simmer uncovered until chicken is tender and sauce thickens slightly.

This dish pairs beautifully with rice or couscous to balance the richness of almonds.

8. Pollo a la Cazadora (Hunter Style Chicken)

The “hunter” style chicken is a rustic stew featuring tomatoes, mushrooms, and herbs, perfect for cooler weather.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • Fresh thyme and bay leaf
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Brown the chicken thighs in olive oil until golden. Remove. Sauté onion and garlic, then add mushrooms and cook until soft. Add tomatoes, wine, thyme, and bay leaf. Return chicken to the pan, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is tender and the sauce is rich and flavorful.

This dish evokes outdoor hunting lodges and countryside feasts and melds beautifully with crusty bread.

Final Thoughts

Spanish chicken recipes are a gateway to the country’s rich culinary heritage, boasting varied ingredients and techniques that provide endless inspiration. From the garlic-infused warmth of Pollo al Ajillo to the sweet-savory complexity of Pollo a la Catalana, each dish offers a unique experience steeped in tradition.

These recipes are incredibly adaptable for home cooks—perfect for both busy weeknights and leisurely weekend meals. Whether you crave the smoky depth of paprika, the fragrant earthiness of saffron, or the bright herbaceous punch of salsa verde, there’s a Spanish chicken dish waiting to become your new favorite.

Give these recipes a try and let the bold flavors of Spain fill your home kitchen. ¡Buen provecho!
