Homemade Pecan Pie: Southern Comfort in Every Bite


Looking for a classic dessert that captures the essence of Southern comfort? Look no further than Homemade Pecan Pie! This delicious and indulgent treat is a staple in Southern cuisine and will leave you craving for more with every bite. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating a homemade pecan pie that will impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup of ice water
  • 1 cup of light corn syrup
  • 1 cup of packed light brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup of unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups of pecan halves

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  3. Roll out the crust: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently against the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges.

  4. Prepare the filling: In a medium mixing bowl, combine the corn syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, vanilla extract, and eggs. Whisk until well combined.

  5. Add the pecans: Arrange the pecan halves evenly over the bottom of the pie crust. Pour the filling mixture over the pecans, making sure they are well coated.

  6. Bake the pie: Place the pie dish on a baking sheet to catch any potential drips. Bake in the preheated oven for 50 to 60 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, you can cover them with aluminum foil.

  7. Cool and serve: Once the pie is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. This will allow the filling to set further. Serve the pie at room temperature or slightly warmed, and enjoy the delightful combination of sweet, gooey filling and crunchy pecans.

Tips and Variations

  • Toasting the pecans: For an extra layer of flavor, you can toast the pecans before adding them to the pie. Simply spread the pecans on a baking sheet and toast them in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 8 to 10 minutes, or until fragrant. Let them cool before incorporating them into the filling.

  • Adding a touch of bourbon: If you want to give your pecan pie a boozy twist, you can add a tablespoon of bourbon to the filling mixture. This will enhance the flavors and add a subtle warmth to the pie.

  • Serving suggestions: Pecan pie is delicious on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The creamy and cold contrast perfectly complements the rich and sweet pie.

Now that you have this Homemade Pecan Pie recipe in your arsenal, you can bring a taste of the South to your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself, this classic dessert is sure to satisfy your cravings for Southern comfort. So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in the sweet and nutty goodness of Homemade Pecan Pie!
