10 Budget-Friendly Vegan Recipes for Every Meal


Eating a plant-based diet doesn’t have to break the bank. In fact, with the right ingredients and a little creativity, you can whip up delicious and nutritious meals on a budget. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just beginning your plant-based journey, these budget-friendly vegan recipes will keep you satisfied from breakfast to dinner.

1. Chickpea Scramble for Breakfast

A hearty, protein-packed breakfast can set you up for the day, and this chickpea scramble is an excellent, affordable alternative to traditional scrambled eggs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (or 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas)
  • ½ medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas. In a bowl, mash them with a fork or potato masher until they resemble scrambled eggs.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and sauté the onion and bell pepper until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the mashed chickpeas to the skillet along with turmeric, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together.
  4. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Adjust seasonings to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with toast or in a wrap for a filling, budget-friendly breakfast.

2. Peanut Butter Banana Oatmeal

Oatmeal is a budget-friendly breakfast option that’s easy to customize. This version is made with peanut butter and banana for added protein and natural sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups water or plant-based milk
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter
  • 1 ripe banana, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional)
  • A pinch of cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring water or plant-based milk to a boil.
  2. Stir in the oats and reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the oats are tender and have absorbed most of the liquid.
  3. Stir in the peanut butter and a pinch of cinnamon.
  4. Top with sliced banana and chia seeds for added texture and nutrients.
  5. Enjoy a filling and nourishing start to your day.

3. Lentil Soup with Spinach

Lentils are a powerhouse of protein and fiber, and they’re incredibly affordable. This lentil soup is comforting, healthy, and can easily be made in large batches for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the carrots and cook for another 3 minutes.
  3. Stir in the lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the lentils are tender.
  5. Stir in the spinach just before serving, allowing it to wilt in the hot soup.
  6. Serve hot with a slice of bread for a comforting, budget-friendly meal.

4. Stuffed Sweet Potatoes

Sweet potatoes are not only affordable but also packed with nutrients like fiber, vitamin A, and potassium. Stuffing them with black beans and avocado turns them into a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh lime juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce the sweet potatoes several times with a fork and bake them on a baking sheet for 40-45 minutes until tender.
  2. While the potatoes are baking, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the black beans, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5-7 minutes until heated through.
  3. Once the sweet potatoes are done, cut a slit down the center of each one and gently fluff the inside with a fork.
  4. Stuff each potato with the black bean mixture and top with diced avocado.
  5. Finish with a squeeze of fresh lime juice if desired.
  6. Enjoy a wholesome, nutrient-packed meal that’s as delicious as it is filling.

5. Pasta with Tomato Basil Sauce

Pasta is a classic budget-friendly meal, and when paired with a simple homemade tomato basil sauce, it becomes a comforting dish that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta of choice
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, dried basil, sugar, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the sauce until well-coated.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve with a side salad for a complete meal.

6. Veggie Stir-Fry with Rice

A veggie stir-fry is an excellent way to use up any leftover vegetables you have in the fridge. This recipe uses simple ingredients like tofu, broccoli, and carrots for a budget-friendly, filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook for 5-7 minutes until golden and crispy on all sides.
  2. Remove the tofu from the pan and set aside. In the same pan, add sesame oil, broccoli, carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  3. Add the tofu back to the pan and drizzle with soy sauce. Stir everything together and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Serve the stir-fry over a bed of cooked rice and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
  5. This dish is not only delicious but also a great way to get your daily serving of vegetables on a budget.

7. Vegan Chili

Chili is a hearty, filling meal that’s perfect for meal prep. This vegan version is packed with beans and vegetables, making it a nutritious and affordable option for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in the kidney beans, black beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve the chili with a sprinkle of nutritional yeast or fresh cilantro for added flavor.
  5. This recipe makes plenty of servings, making it ideal for batch cooking or meal prepping.

8. Crispy Baked Tofu with Veggies

Baked tofu is a great way to enjoy plant-based protein in a crispy, satisfying form. Paired with roasted vegetables, this meal is both easy to prepare and filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 sweet potato, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the tofu cubes with soy sauce, olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet.
  3. On a separate baking sheet, toss the sweet potato cubes and broccoli florets with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place both sheets in the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping the tofu halfway through.
  4. Once everything is cooked and crispy, serve the baked tofu with the roasted vegetables for a well-rounded, budget-friendly meal.

9. Vegan Buddha Bowl

A Buddha bowl is a simple, customizable meal that’s full of wholesome ingredients. This version includes brown rice, roasted vegetables, and a creamy tahini dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potato cubes
  • 1 cup roasted chickpeas (canned or homemade)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 handful of mixed greens
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Water, to thin the dressing

Instructions:

  1. Cook the brown rice according to the package instructions. Meanwhile, roast the sweet potato and chickpeas in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  2. To make the dressing, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and a bit of water until smooth and creamy.
  3. Assemble the bowl by placing the rice at the bottom, followed by the roasted sweet potato, chickpeas, avocado slices, and mixed greens.
  4. Drizzle the tahini dressing over the top and enjoy a vibrant, filling meal.

10. Vegan Pancakes

A classic breakfast favorite, these vegan pancakes are light, fluffy, and budget-friendly. They’re easy to make with just a few pantry staples.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup plant-based milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the plant-based milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to over-mix.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
  5. Pour ¼ cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  6. Serve with maple syrup, fresh fruit, or any other toppings of your choice.

Final Thoughts

Eating delicious, nutritious vegan meals on a budget is easier than you think. With simple ingredients like beans, lentils, tofu, and vegetables, you can create a variety of dishes that are both satisfying and affordable. Whether you’re preparing a quick breakfast, a hearty lunch, or a filling dinner, these vegan recipes will help you stick to your budget without sacrificing flavor. Happy cooking!
