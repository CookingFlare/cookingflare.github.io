How to Make a Simple No-Knead Focaccia Bread


Focaccia bread is one of the most beloved staples in Italian cuisine. Its golden crust, fluffy interior, and satisfying chew make it a versatile option for everything from dipping in olive oil to serving as a sandwich base or even enjoying on its own. While traditional bread-making can be time-consuming, you’ll be pleased to know that there is a simple, no-knead version of focaccia that anyone can make at home. This recipe is perfect for beginners and experts alike, saving you the hassle of kneading while delivering all the flavor and texture you love.

Why Make No-Knead Focaccia?

Bread-making often conjures up images of flour-covered hands and minutes of tedious kneading. While traditional methods have their place, busy schedules or the desire for simplicity can push us toward recipes that deliver great results with less effort. No-knead focaccia is ideal for those who want all the flavor and texture without the time commitment. Plus, it’s forgiving—whether you’re new to baking or have years of experience, this recipe will turn out delicious every time.

By focusing on slow fermentation and leveraging the power of high hydration, this no-knead method develops gluten and flavor naturally, giving you a perfectly chewy, airy bread with minimal effort.

Ingredients for No-Knead Focaccia Bread

When making no-knead focaccia bread, it’s essential to begin with quality ingredients. You won’t need anything out-of-the-ordinary; just pantry staples blended with a few fresh accents.

For the Dough

  • 2 ½ cups (315g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp instant or active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups (295ml) warm water (around 110°F)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (plus extra for the bowl and pan)

For the Topping

  • 2-3 tbsp olive oil
  • Flaky sea salt to taste
  • Fresh rosemary, thyme, or your preferred herbs (optional)
  • Sliced olives, cherry tomatoes, or garlic for additional flavor (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step One: Combine Your Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, salt, and instant yeast. Slowly pour in the warm water while stirring with a wooden spoon or rubber spatula. The dough will look sticky, shaggy, and wetter than traditional bread dough—that’s perfect!

Add in a tablespoon of olive oil and mix briefly just to combine. The oil will prevent the dough from sticking excessively as it ferments.

Step Two: Allow the Dough to Rise

Once the dough is mixed, lightly oil the bowl to prevent sticking and cover it with plastic wrap or a kitchen towel. Let the dough rise at room temperature for 12-14 hours, or overnight.

During this slow rise, the yeast will perform all the heavy lifting you’d normally achieve through kneading. The dough will become bubbly and expand significantly as gluten develops naturally.

Step Three: Prepare the Pan

About 30 minutes before you plan to bake, generously oil the bottom and sides of a baking pan or cast-iron skillet. A 9x13-inch pan works well for this recipe, but any similar-sized rectangular or circular dish will do.

Step Four: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has completed its fermentation, it’s time to transfer it to your prepared pan. Gently pour or scrape the dough into the dish, being careful not to deflate it too much. Spread it out with oiled fingers until it covers the surface evenly.

If the dough resists stretching, let it rest for 5-10 minutes to relax the gluten, then continue spreading gently.

Step Five: Final Rise

Cover the pan loosely with plastic wrap or a clean towel and allow the dough to rise again for 1-2 hours. During this time, it will become puffier and closer to its final baking shape.

Step Six: Dimple the Dough

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). After the second rise, drizzle olive oil liberally over the top of the dough. Using your fingers, press gently into the dough to create the signature focaccia dimples.

This step is not just aesthetic—it helps distribute olive oil across the surface and ensures an even bake.

Step Seven: Add Toppings

Now comes the fun part! Sprinkle flaky sea salt over the dough and, if you like, add fresh herbs such as rosemary or thyme for a fragrant finish. For added flavor, scatter sliced olives, halved cherry tomatoes, or thinly shaved garlic across the surface.

Step Eight: Bake to Perfection

Transfer the pan into the preheated oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and crisp on the edges. Keep an eye on the bread in the final few minutes to prevent overbaking.

Step Nine: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the focaccia from the oven and let it cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a cooling rack. While it’s tempting to dive right in, allowing it to cool slightly helps the structure to set up properly.

Slice into squares or wedges and serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips for Perfect No-Knead Focaccia Bread

1. Use Good Olive Oil

Since olive oil plays such a big role in both the flavor and texture of focaccia, investing in a high-quality extra-virgin olive oil will elevate the results significantly.

2. Experiment with Flavors

Focaccia is incredibly versatile, allowing you to tweak the flavor profile to suit your taste. Add lemon zest for a pop of brightness, sprinkle with grated parmesan before baking for a cheesy touch, or use caramelized onions as a topping for savory depth.

3. Pay Attention to Hydration

The dough for focaccia is wetter than typical bread recipes, and it’s meant to be that way. Resist the urge to add extra flour—it’s the high hydration level that contributes to the characteristic airy structure.

4. Avoid Overproofing

While focaccia benefits from slow fermentation, overproofing can lead to a deflated final product. Be sure to bake promptly after the second rise.

5. Bake at High Heat

The key to achieving a crisp yet tender texture is a high temperature, maintaining even baking throughout the dough. Ensure your oven is preheated and stable before placing the focaccia inside.

How to Store Focaccia Bread

No-knead focaccia bread is best enjoyed fresh but can be stored for later use. Wrap leftovers tightly in plastic wrap or store them in an airtight container to prevent drying out. To refresh the texture, warm it in the oven at 350°F for 5–10 minutes.

For longer storage, the bread can be frozen. Let it cool completely, cut it into portions, and wrap each piece individually in plastic wrap before freezing. Thaw at room temperature and reheat as needed.

Serving Suggestions

Focaccia bread pairs beautifully with countless meals and occasions. Here are a few ways to enjoy it:

  • As a Side Dish: Serve alongside soups, salads, or pasta to round out a hearty meal.
  • Dip and Spread: Pair with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, and a sprinkle of cracked pepper for a simple appetizer.
  • Sandwich Base: Slice horizontally and layer with roasted vegetables, meats, or cheeses for a gourmet sandwich.
  • Snack or Breakfast: Toast lightly, spread with butter, or drizzle with honey for a unique morning treat.

Final Thoughts

Making your own no-knead focaccia bread is a rewarding experience that combines simplicity with sophistication. Even if you’re new to baking, this recipe will give you the confidence to create something truly delicious with minimal effort. The result is a perfectly airy, flavorful bread that’s ready to steal the show at your next family dinner, casual lunch, or special occasion.

Enjoy baking this culinary classic, and get ready to impress your taste buds—and those of everyone you share it with!
