Savory Bacon and Gouda Breakfast Strudel


When it comes to breakfast, finding a balance between indulgent flavors and satisfying textures can be a delightful challenge. Enter the Savory Bacon and Gouda Breakfast Strudel—a mouthwatering pastry that combines the crispy, salty goodness of bacon with the rich, creamy taste of Gouda cheese, all wrapped in a flaky, golden-brown strudel. This dish not only elevates your breakfast game but also makes for a stunning centerpiece at brunch gatherings or family breakfasts.

The Allure of Strudel

Strudel is a type of layered pastry that originated in Austria, known for its versatility and rich filling options. While it is often associated with sweet fillings like apples or cherries, savory strudels are equally inviting. The allure of strudel lies in its delicate, flaky texture, which contrasts beautifully with hearty fillings. The Savory Bacon and Gouda Breakfast Strudel is a perfect example of how this classic pastry can be reimagined for breakfast, combining traditional flavors with modern flair.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this delectable breakfast strudel, gather the following ingredients:

For the Strudel Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2-4 tablespoons cold water

For the Filling:

  • 8 slices of thick-cut bacon
  • 1 cup shredded Gouda cheese
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped bell pepper (red or green)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped (optional)

For the Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Strudel Dough

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center.

  2. Cut in Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Incorporate Wet Ingredients: Add the sour cream and egg yolk to the mixture. Mix until combined. Gradually add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together.

  4. Knead and Chill: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead gently for about 1-2 minutes until smooth. Shape into a ball, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Cook the Bacon and Vegetables

  1. Cook the Bacon: In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels. Once cooled, crumble the bacon into small pieces.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same skillet, remove excess bacon fat, leaving about a tablespoon. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Prepare the Egg Mixture

  1. Whisk the Eggs: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Add a pinch of salt and pepper, then stir in the crumbled bacon, sautéed vegetables, and shredded Gouda cheese. Mix until evenly combined.

Step 4: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Roll the Dough: On a floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a large rectangle, about 1/8 inch thick. Aim for a width of about 12 inches and a length of 16 inches.

Step 5: Assemble the Strudel

  1. Add the Filling: Spoon the bacon and Gouda filling along one long edge of the dough, leaving about 2 inches of space from the edge.

  2. Fold and Roll: Carefully fold the edges of the dough over the filling, then roll the strudel away from you, tucking in the ends to seal in the filling.

  3. Transfer to Baking Sheet: Place the strudel seam-side down on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Step 6: Brush with Egg Wash

  1. Prepare Egg Wash: In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and water. Brush the mixture over the top of the strudel to give it a beautiful golden color when baked.

Step 7: Bake the Strudel

  1. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the strudel is golden brown and flaky.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Cool and Slice: Once baked, remove the strudel from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes. Slice it into generous portions and serve warm. Garnish with fresh chives if desired.

Tips for Perfecting Your Strudel

  • Use Cold Ingredients: Keeping your butter and sour cream cold helps create a flaky texture in the dough.
  • Don’t Overfill: While it’s tempting to add extra filling, overstuffing can lead to a messy strudel that may burst during baking.
  • Experiment with Fillings: Feel free to customize the filling. Spinach, mushrooms, or different cheeses can offer exciting variations.

Pairing Suggestions

To elevate your breakfast experience, consider pairing the Savory Bacon and Gouda Breakfast Strudel with a variety of accompaniments:

  • Fresh Fruit: A side of mixed berries or sliced melons adds a refreshing contrast.
  • Coffee or Tea: A rich cup of coffee or a soothing herbal tea complements the savory flavors beautifully.
  • Yogurt: A dollop of Greek yogurt can add creaminess and a tangy flavor that balances the richness of the strudel.

Storing and Reheating

If you have any leftovers (which is unlikely!), storing the strudel is simple:

  • Refrigerate: Wrap the cooled strudel in plastic wrap or store it in an airtight container. It can be kept in the fridge for up to 3 days.
  • Reheat: To reheat, place slices in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes, or until warmed through.

Conclusion

The Savory Bacon and Gouda Breakfast Strudel is not just a meal; it’s a culinary experience that brings joy to the breakfast table. With its combination of flaky pastry and savory filling, it’s sure to impress family and friends alike. Whether you’re enjoying it on a lazy Sunday morning or serving it at a festive brunch, this strudel is bound to become a beloved staple in your breakfast repertoire. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a breakfast that’s anything but ordinary!
