How to Make Homemade Fermented Relishes and Chutneys


Fermented relishes and chutneys are a delightful way to add complex flavors, texture, and probiotics to your meals. With a wide variety of ingredients available, you can create different combinations that suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. The beauty of fermenting your own relishes and chutneys is not only the unique flavors but also the health benefits they bring. Whether you’re a seasoned fermenter or a beginner, making homemade fermented relishes and chutneys is an incredibly rewarding kitchen project.

In this post, we will guide you through the process of creating your own fermented condiments, exploring the types of ingredients you can use, the fermentation process itself, and some of the best recipes to get started.

Why Fermented Relishes and Chutneys?

Before we dive into the process, it’s worth understanding why fermented foods like chutneys and relishes are becoming increasingly popular. Fermentation is an ancient preservation technique that enhances the nutritional value of foods. The good bacteria formed during the fermentation process can help improve gut health, boost your immune system, and support digestion.

Fermented relishes and chutneys are typically made with fruits, vegetables, and spices, and the fermentation process transforms them into tangy, flavorful condiments that can add a burst of taste to any dish. These condiments not only elevate the flavors of your food but can also last for weeks, allowing you to store them for longer periods without preservatives.

What You Need to Make Fermented Relishes and Chutneys

Before beginning your fermentation journey, there are a few basic ingredients and tools that you will need:

Key Ingredients

  1. Fruits and Vegetables: Common ingredients include cucumbers, carrots, tomatoes, apples, pears, peaches, and eggplants. You can experiment with whatever fruits and vegetables are in season or those that you enjoy the most.
  2. Salt: Non-iodized sea salt or kosher salt is recommended. The salt helps to draw out moisture and encourage the growth of beneficial bacteria while preventing harmful bacteria.
  3. Sugar: A small amount of sugar can help balance the tanginess of the fermented mixture and encourage the fermentation process, especially when fruits are involved.
  4. Spices and Herbs: Add complexity to your chutneys and relishes with spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, mustard seeds, ginger, garlic, and chili. Fresh herbs like cilantro and basil can also be added.
  5. Vinegar: Some recipes call for a bit of vinegar to kickstart the fermentation or add an additional layer of acidity, but many chutneys and relishes rely on the natural acidity from the ingredients themselves.
  6. Water: Use filtered water for the fermentation process, ensuring that no chlorine or contaminants inhibit the growth of beneficial bacteria.

Tools You’ll Need

  1. Fermentation Vessel: A glass jar with an airtight lid is ideal for fermenting. You can also use ceramic crocks or even fermentation-specific vessels if you prefer.
  2. Weighing Down the Vegetables: You’ll need something to keep the ingredients submerged in the brine. A weight, such as a fermentation stone, a small clean jar, or a plastic bag filled with brine, can work well.
  3. Airlock Lid (Optional): For advanced fermentation enthusiasts, an airlock lid can help release gases without letting air in, which can promote a cleaner ferment.

The Fermentation Process

Fermentation can be as simple or as complex as you want to make it. The core idea behind fermenting relishes and chutneys is to allow natural bacteria (lactic acid bacteria) to transform the sugars and starches in your ingredients into beneficial acids and gases, giving your condiments their characteristic tang and flavor.

Here’s how the process generally works:

  1. Prepare Your Ingredients:

    • Chop, Slice, or Grate: Depending on the texture you prefer, chop your vegetables or fruits into small pieces or shred them. Chopped cucumbers, carrots, and even apples work wonderfully for making chutneys and relishes.
    • Mix the Ingredients: Add your chosen spices and herbs to your vegetables and fruits. If making a fruit-based chutney, include sugar to balance out the tartness. In the case of more savory relishes, herbs like garlic, ginger, and chili provide an aromatic kick.

  2. Add Salt:

    • Salt is an essential part of the fermentation process. It helps to extract moisture from the vegetables or fruits, which creates the brine in which the fermentation takes place. Aim for about 1-2 tablespoons of salt per quart of vegetables.

  3. Pack the Ingredients:

    • Once your vegetables and fruits are salted and spiced, pack them tightly into a clean glass jar, pressing down to ensure there are no air pockets. The more tightly packed the vegetables are, the better the fermentation.

  4. Submerge the Ingredients:

    • Once packed, ensure that the vegetables and fruits are fully submerged in the brine (the liquid that forms from the salt drawing moisture from the ingredients). If needed, use a weight to keep everything submerged.

  5. Ferment at Room Temperature:

    • Store the jar in a cool, dark place (ideally between 60-70°F or 15-21°C). Check on it daily to make sure the ingredients stay submerged. Over time, the bacteria will begin to ferment the ingredients, creating bubbles and a tangy aroma.
    • The fermentation time will vary depending on the temperature and the ingredients used, but expect the process to take anywhere from 3 to 7 days.

  6. Taste Testing:

    • After a few days, taste the chutney or relish. If it has reached the tangy flavor you desire, it’s ready. If not, let it ferment for a few more days, checking daily.

  7. Refrigerate:

    • Once the chutney or relish has reached the right flavor, transfer it to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. The cold temperature will preserve the flavors for weeks.

Basic Fermented Chutney Recipes

Here are a few simple and delicious fermented chutney recipes to get you started:

1. Spicy Carrot and Ginger Chutney

This chutney has a vibrant color and a spicy, aromatic flavor that pairs wonderfully with meats or as a topping for rice dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large carrots, grated
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1-2 small green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon raw sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Grate the carrots and ginger, and finely chop the garlic and chilies.
  2. In a bowl, mix the carrots, ginger, garlic, and spices. Sprinkle with salt and sugar, and mix well.
  3. Pack the mixture into a jar, pressing down to remove any air pockets.
  4. Ensure the vegetables are submerged, then cover the jar and set it in a warm, dark place.
  5. Allow to ferment for 4-5 days, then refrigerate.

2. Cucumber and Dill Relish

A refreshing, classic relish that is a great condiment for sandwiches or burgers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cucumbers thinly and chop the onion. Combine them in a bowl with salt, sugar, and mustard seeds.
  2. Mix the ingredients thoroughly and let them sit for 15-20 minutes to draw out moisture.
  3. Pack the mixture into a jar, pressing down to remove any air.
  4. Submerge the vegetables in their brine and let them ferment for 4-6 days.
  5. Once fermented, refrigerate and enjoy on your favorite dishes.

3. Mango and Chili Chutney

This chutney offers a sweet and spicy profile, perfect for pairing with grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 small red chili, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Peel and chop the mangoes. Combine them with the chili, ginger, mustard seeds, cinnamon, salt, and sugar in a large bowl.
  2. Mix well and pack the mixture into a clean jar, pressing it down to remove air pockets.
  3. Submerge the mango mixture in its brine and seal the jar. Let it ferment for 4-7 days in a warm, dark place.
  4. Once ready, refrigerate and use within a few weeks.

Tips for Success

  • Use Non-Iodized Salt: Iodized salt can inhibit fermentation, so always choose non-iodized sea salt or kosher salt.
  • Cleanliness is Key: Make sure all utensils, jars, and hands are clean to prevent contamination by harmful bacteria.
  • Adjust Fermentation Time: Depending on your taste preferences, you can ferment for shorter or longer periods. If you like a milder flavor, ferment for a few days. For a more robust tang, let it ferment longer.
  • Experiment with Flavors: The beauty of making fermented chutneys and relishes is that you can experiment with different combinations of fruits, vegetables, and spices to create your own signature condiments.

Conclusion

Making homemade fermented relishes and chutneys is a fun, easy way to create healthy and flavorful condiments. With the ability to adjust the ingredients, fermentation time, and flavors, the possibilities are endless. Fermenting your own relishes not only gives you control over what you’re eating but also provides you with the health benefits of probiotic-rich foods. Whether you prefer sweet, spicy, or savory condiments, you’re sure to find a recipe that suits your taste. Start experimenting today and elevate your meals with the vibrant flavors of homemade fermented chutneys and relishes!
