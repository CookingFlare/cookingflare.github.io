Luxembourgish Judd Mat Gaardebounen


There’s something truly special about traditional Luxembourgish cuisine. Bursting with flavor and steeped in history, these dishes showcase the unique culinary heritage of the small European nation. One such dish that stands out is Judd mat Gaardebounen. If you’re looking to explore new flavors and impress your guests, this recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg smoked collar of pork (Judd)
  • 500 g dried broad beans (Gaardebounen)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Begin by soaking the dried broad beans overnight in cold water. This will help soften them, making them easier to cook.

  2. Drain the soaked beans and place them in a large pot. Cover with fresh water and bring to a boil. Simmer for about 30 minutes or until the beans are tender but not mushy. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a separate large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic, sautéing until they become translucent and fragrant.

  4. Carefully place the smoked collar of pork (Judd) into the pot, ensuring it is fully submerged in the onion and garlic mixture. Add the bay leaves, thyme sprigs, and cloves for added flavor.

  5. Pour enough water into the pot to cover the pork. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for approximately 2 hours. This slow cooking process will allow the flavors to meld together and the meat to become tender.

  6. Once the pork is fully cooked and tender, remove it from the pot and set it aside to cool slightly. Reserve the cooking liquid, as it will be used later.

  7. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Take a baking dish and carefully transfer the cooked pork into it. Place the dish in the oven for about 20 minutes, allowing the meat to develop a delicious crispy exterior.

  8. While the pork is in the oven, take the reserved cooking liquid and bring it to a boil. Add the cooked broad beans (Gaardebounen) to the pot, stirring gently to combine. Let the mixture simmer for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  9. Remove the baking dish from the oven, and using a sharp knife, thinly slice the pork. Arrange the slices on a serving platter, and generously spoon the cooked broad beans and their flavorful broth over the top.

  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with fresh thyme leaves if desired. Serve hot and enjoy the rich and comforting flavors of Luxembourgish Judd mat Gaardebounen.

The Perfect Pairings

To truly savor the flavors of Judd mat Gaardebounen, it’s best to pair it with some traditional Luxembourgish side dishes. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Gromperekichelcher (Potato Pancakes): Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, these potato pancakes are the perfect accompaniment to the rich flavors of Judd mat Gaardebounen.

  • Bouneschlupp (Green Bean Soup): This hearty soup made with fresh green beans, potatoes, and bacon is a delicious way to start your meal before diving into the main course.

  • Quetschentaart (Plum Tart): End your Luxembourgish feast on a sweet note with this traditional plum tart. The juicy plums and buttery crust create a delightful combination.

In Conclusion

Luxembourgish Judd mat Gaardebounen is a dish that encapsulates the heart and soul of the country’s cuisine. Its smoky, tender pork and flavorful broad beans make for a truly satisfying meal. Whether you’re cooking for family or friends, this traditional dish is sure to impress. So, don your apron and embark on a culinary adventure to Luxembourg right from your own kitchen!
