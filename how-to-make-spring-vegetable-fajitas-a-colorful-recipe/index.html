How to Make Spring Vegetable Fajitas: A Colorful Recipe


If you’re looking for a vibrant and flavorful dish to celebrate the colors of spring, then these spring vegetable fajitas are just the thing for you. Packed with a colorful array of fresh vegetables and a zesty blend of spices, this recipe is a delightful twist on the classic fajita, perfect for a light and healthy meal. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a newbie in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and brighten up your dining table.

Ingredients

For the Fajita Filling:

  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or green), thinly sliced
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed

For the Fajita Seasoning:

  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Avocado slices
  • Lime wedges
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Fajita Filling:

    • Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil to the skillet.
    • Add the sliced bell peppers and red onion to the skillet. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until slightly softened.
    • Add the sliced zucchinis, cherry tomatoes, corn kernels, and snap peas to the skillet. Cook for an additional 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  2. Season the Vegetables:

    • In a small bowl, mix together the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper.
    • Sprinkle the fajita seasoning over the cooked vegetables in the skillet. Toss to coat the vegetables evenly with the seasoning.

  3. Warm the Tortillas:

    • In a separate dry skillet, warm the flour tortillas over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side until they are soft and pliable.

  4. Assemble the Fajitas:

    • Spoon the seasoned spring vegetable mixture onto the warm tortillas.
    • Top with chopped cilantro, avocado slices, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.
    • Serve with sour cream or Greek yogurt on the side, if desired.

Tips for Success

  • Ensure that the vegetables are thinly sliced or chopped to ensure even cooking and easy assembly of the fajitas.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences and what’s in season.
  • For a protein boost, consider adding grilled tofu, shrimp, or chicken to the fajitas.

Conclusion

These spring vegetable fajitas are a celebration of the fresh, vibrant produce that the season has to offer. The medley of colors and flavors in this dish is a true testament to the beauty of spring. Whether you’re planning a casual weeknight dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, these fajitas are a delightful addition to any table. So, grab your skillet, embrace the colors of spring, and savor every flavorful bite of these delicious vegetable fajitas.
