Arroz Negro: A Unique Spanish Rice Dish with Squid Ink


Arroz Negro, meaning “black rice” in Spanish, is a unique and flavorful dish that originates from the coastal regions of Spain. What sets this dish apart is the use of squid ink, which gives the rice a striking black color and adds a rich umami flavor. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating this delicious and visually stunning dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of short-grain rice
  • 4 cups of fish or vegetable broth
  • 1 pound of fresh squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup of white wine
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large, deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and red bell pepper, and sauté until they become soft and translucent.

  2. Add the squid rings to the skillet and cook for about 2 minutes, until they become opaque. Remove the squid from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the grated tomato and cook for about 5 minutes, until it starts to break down and release its juices.

  4. Stir in the smoked paprika, dried thyme, and dried oregano, and cook for another minute to release their flavors.

  5. Add the rice to the skillet and stir well to coat it with the tomato mixture. Cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice starts to toast slightly.

  6. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until it has evaporated, stirring occasionally.

  7. Gradually add the fish or vegetable broth to the skillet, about 1 cup at a time, stirring well after each addition. Allow the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. This process will take about 20 minutes.

  8. While the rice is cooking, prepare the squid ink. In a small bowl, dilute the squid ink with a little bit of water to make it easier to incorporate into the rice later.

  9. Once the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid, stir in the diluted squid ink. Mix well to evenly distribute the ink throughout the rice, giving it its distinctive black color.

  10. Add the cooked squid back into the skillet and gently stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  11. Remove the skillet from the heat and cover it with a lid. Let the arroz negro rest for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  12. Serve the arroz negro hot, garnished with fresh parsley. The dish can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a simple green salad or crusty bread.

Conclusion

Arroz Negro is a truly unique Spanish rice dish that will impress your guests with its striking appearance and bold flavors. The use of squid ink adds a touch of elegance and a depth of flavor that is hard to resist. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So grab your apron and get ready to create a culinary masterpiece that will transport you to the sunny shores of Spain. ¡Buen provecho!
