Keto to Go: 100 Keto Recipes for Busy Professionals and Wholesome Meal Preppers


Are you tired of constantly trying to balance a demanding work schedule with a healthy keto lifestyle? Or perhaps you struggle to find the time to meal prep and ensure that every meal is optimized for success with a low-carb diet. Fret not! We’ve assembled a collection of 100 keto recipes designed to cater to the needs of busy professionals and food enthusiasts who love to meal prep. These scrumptious dishes will not only leave you satisfied but do so without adding time to your already busy day. Let’s dive right in!

Breakfast on the Go

1. Avocado and Bacon Egg Cups

A delicious start to your day! An entire meal wrapped up in a convenient muffin tin.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup cheddar cheese, shredded
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4-medium sized silicone muffin molds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In each muffin mold, place a small amount of chopped bacon, distribute avocado, and then crack an egg in each mold.
  3. Sprinkle cheese and some extra bacon over the eggs.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, and bake for 20 minutes, or until the eggs reach your desired doneness.
  5. Allow the egg cups to cool slightly before gently removing them from the molds.

2. Chia Pudding Mix

A make-ahead breakfast that will save you time in the morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chia seeds
  • 3 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup sugar substitute of choice (like monk fruit)

Instructions:

  1. Stir together all ingredients until blended completely.
  2. Spoon the mixture into individual airtight containers.
  3. Refrigerate overnight or for up to five days, so they’re ready to grab on your way out the door.

Lunch Solutions

3. Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breast

A protein-packed lunch that can be made in bulk and enjoyed throughout the week.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Cut a pocket in the chicken breasts, making sure not to cut all the way through.
  3. Stuff each breast with a mixture of spinach, feta, and garlic, season with salt and pepper.
  4. Wrap each breast in aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Allow them to cool before refrigerating and unsticking them from their foil casings before reheating when ready to eat.

4. Cobb Salad Jar

An innovative twist on an old classic with ingredients layered in a mason jar.

Ingredients:

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 8 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup grape tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup cooked chicken breast, chopped
  • 1/2 cup blue cheese, crumbled
  • 3 cups mixed salad greens

Instructions:

  1. In a mason jar, layer the salad ingredients in the following order: eggs, bacon, tomatoes, avocado, chicken, blue cheese, and greens.
  2. Seal the jar and refrigerate until you’re ready to enjoy your cobb salad.

Dinner Ideas

5. One-Pan Salmon and Asparagus

A delightful, low-carb meal thatâ€™s both nourishing and easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 lb asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and lemon zest (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. On a parchment-lined baking sheet, season the salmon and asparagus with olive oil, salt, pepper, and lemon zest.
  3. Place the asparagus around the salmon fillets and sprinkle minced garlic over them.
  4. Roast for 20 to 25 minutes in the oven, or until the salmon is cooked through and asparagus is tender. Sprinkle with fresh dill before serving.

6. Creamy Mushroom Chicken

A restaurant-quality meal that will be ready in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Season chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then sear in olive oil until golden brown on both sides.
  2. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Deglaze the pan with chicken broth, scraping the bottom to collect bits of flavor. Allow the mixture to simmer until reduced to half.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and allow the sauce to heat through.
  5. Return the chicken breasts to the sauce, spooning creamy mushroom sauce over the chicken during serving. Sprinkle with fresh parsley.

Keto Desserts

7. Almond Flour Brownies

A guilt-free way to satisfy your sweet tooth with zero sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup sugar substitute of choice (like monk fruit)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Mix together almond flour, cocoa powder, and melted butter in a bowl until well combined.
  3. Blend in the sugar substitute, eggs, and vanilla extract until a smooth batter forms.
  4. Grease a loaf pan and pour the batter in, spreading it evenly.
  5. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted into the center.
  6. Allow the brownies to cool before slicing and enjoying.

Conclusion

Busy professionals and regular meal preppers are faced with the challenging task of ensuring that every meal aligns with their keto diet without compromising on their time. This collection provides ample guidance on making it easier to maintain a keto lifestyle with convenience and taste in mind. Whether you’re starting your day with creamy chia pudding or wrapping up the work week with delectable almond flour brownies, remember that the journey towards a healthier life is possible with these simple and scrumptious recipes to help steer you in the right direction. Happy cooking!
