How to Make the Perfect Chicken Pot Pie at Home


As the cold winter winds start to blow, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of chicken pot pie to lift your spirits and fill your belly. But why settle for a store-bought version when you can make your own at home? With a few simple ingredients and some basic cooking techniques, you can create a deliciously flaky crust and a rich, creamy filling that will leave you and your family begging for more.

Step 1: Make the Pastry Crust

To start, you’ll need to make the pastry crust that will hold your filling together. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

Next, gradually add 1/4 cup of ice-cold water, stirring the mixture with a fork until it forms a shaggy dough. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it a few times until it comes together in a cohesive ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is chilling, you can start preparing the filling. In a large skillet, sauté 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs in 2 tablespoons of butter until it’s cooked through and shredded. Add 1 medium onion, diced, and 2 cloves of garlic, minced, to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.

Next, add 2 cups of mixed vegetables, such as peas, carrots, and corn, to the skillet and cook until they’re tender. Stir in 1 cup of all-purpose flour to coat the vegetables and cook for 1-2 minutes. Gradually add 1 1/2 cups of chicken broth, whisking constantly to avoid lumps, and bring the mixture to a boil.

Reduce the heat to a simmer and let the filling cook for 5-7 minutes, or until it’s thickened to your liking. Season the filling with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

To assemble the pie, roll out the chilled pastry dough on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Place the dough into a 9x13 inch baking dish, trimming any excess dough from the edges.

Fill the dough-lined baking dish with the prepared filling, spreading it out evenly. Roll out the remaining pastry dough to the same thickness as the first piece and use it to cover the filling. Crimp the edges of the pastry to seal the pie and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

Step 4: Bake the Pie

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (180°C) and continue baking the pie for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is hot and bubbly.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh herbs if desired. You can also serve the pie with a side of steamed vegetables or a green salad for a well-rounded meal.

With these simple steps, you can create a delicious homemade chicken pot pie that’s sure to become a family favorite. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds will thank you!
