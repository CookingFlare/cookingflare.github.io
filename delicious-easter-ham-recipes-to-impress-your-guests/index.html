Delicious Easter Ham Recipes to Impress Your Guests


Easter is a beloved holiday celebrated by millions of people around the world. One of the most traditional and iconic dishes served during this festive occasion is ham. Bringing a perfectly cooked and perfectly seasoned ham to your Easter dinner can impress your guests and make the meal special, as it serves as the centerpiece of the table. Here are some mouth-watering recipes that will rev up your Easter ham game, ensuring you’ll be the talk of the town! Remember, a well-prepared ham is just the beginning; add a delightful twist to your Easter meal with these delicious recipes!

  1. Honey-Give! Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked, spiral-sliced ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apricot jam
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions: Arrange the ham in a roasting pan. In a medium bowl, whisk together the honey, mustard, jam, brown sugar, and parsley. Pour over the ham, ensuring it’s well coated. Cover the pan with aluminum foil and let it rest for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Bake the ham for about 10-12 minutes per pound or until heated through. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes to caramelise the glaze. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  1. Memorable Maple-Mustard Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked, bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt to taste

Instructions: In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Place the ham in a roasting pan and pour the glaze over it, ensuring it’s well coated. Cover the pan with aluminum foil and let it rest for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Bake the ham for about 10-12 minutes per pound or until heated through. Remove the foil and glaze the ham to desired thickness. Season with salt to taste.

  1. Flavor-Filled Cinnamon-Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked, bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup McCormick’s Cinnamon Cupcakes
  • 1/4 cup apricot preserves
  • 1/2 cup bourbon
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions: In a medium saucepan, heat the brown sugar, cinnamon cupcakes, apricot preserves, bourbon, and apple cider vinegar. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture has thickened. Place the ham in a roasting pan and glaze with the mixture to coat. Cover the pan with aluminum foil and let it rest for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Bake the ham for about 10-12 minutes per pound or until heated through. Remove the foil and glaze again to desired thickness. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

As you gather with friends and family this Easter, surprise them with a delicious and memorable ham at the center of your table. Each of these scrumptious recipes is sure to impress your guests and make your holiday meal unforgettable. So, which recipe will you choose? Don’t delay: start shopping for ham and prepping your ingredients, and get ready to dazzle your guests with excellence!
