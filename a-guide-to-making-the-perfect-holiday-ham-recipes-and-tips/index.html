A Guide to Making the Perfect Holiday Ham: Recipes and Tips


The holiday season is a time of joy, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious food. Among the many festive dishes that grace our tables, few can rival the grandeur of a perfectly cooked holiday ham. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to create a show-stopping ham that will have your guests raving. From selecting the right ham to mastering the glaze, let’s dive into the art of ham preparation.

Choosing the Right Ham

Before you can begin the cooking process, it’s crucial to select the right ham. Here are the main types you might encounter:

Types of Ham

  1. Bone-in Ham: This is the traditional choice, known for its rich flavor and juiciness. The bone helps to keep the meat moist during cooking.

  2. Boneless Ham: This option is easier to carve and serves well for large gatherings. However, it may lack some of the depth of flavor found in bone-in varieties.

  3. Spiral-Cut Ham: Pre-sliced for convenience, spiral-cut ham is popular for its ease of serving. It’s important to keep the glaze from drying out since it’s often already pre-cooked.

  4. Country Ham: This is a salt-cured, aged ham that can have a robust flavor. It requires soaking before cooking to balance the saltiness.

Sizing

For a gathering, plan on about ½ pound per person for bone-in ham and ⅓ pound for boneless. For instance, if you’re serving 10 people, a 5-7 pound bone-in ham would suffice.

Preparing Your Ham

Once you’ve selected your ham, it’s time to prepare it for cooking. Here are the steps to get you started:

Ingredients for Basic Preparation

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (5-7 pounds)
  • 2-3 cups of apple cider or orange juice (for moistening)
  • Whole cloves (optional for studding)

Step-by-Step Preparation

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Set your oven to 325°F (163°C).

  2. Score the Ham: Using a sharp knife, score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern, about ½ inch deep. This will allow the glaze to penetrate the meat.

  3. Stud with Cloves: If desired, insert whole cloves into the intersections of the scored lines. This adds both flavor and an attractive presentation.

  4. Moistening: Place the ham in a roasting pan and pour apple cider or orange juice over it to keep it moist during cooking.

  5. Cover: Tent the ham loosely with aluminum foil to prevent it from drying out.

The Perfect Glaze

A delicious glaze elevates your ham from good to unforgettable. Here are three delightful glaze recipes to consider:

1. Honey Mustard Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup honey
  • ½ cup Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine all ingredients. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Brush half of the glaze over the ham before placing it in the oven.
  3. Reserve the rest for basting during the last 30 minutes of cooking.

2. Maple-Bourbon Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup bourbon
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a small pot, whisk together all ingredients and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Brush the glaze over the ham during the last hour of cooking for maximum flavor.

3. Pineapple Brown Sugar Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup crushed pineapple (drained)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine brown sugar and cornstarch. Stir in crushed pineapple and vinegar.
  2. Cook over medium heat until thickened, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Apply glaze during the last hour of cooking.

Cooking the Ham

Now that your ham is prepped and glazed, it’s time to cook it to perfection. Here’s how:

Cooking Time

  • For a fully cooked ham, you’ll want to heat it for about 10-12 minutes per pound.
  • Always use a meat thermometer; the internal temperature should reach 140°F (60°C).

Basting

Baste your ham with the reserved glaze every 20-30 minutes during the last hour of cooking for a beautiful caramelized finish. This not only adds flavor but also helps create that irresistible shiny crust.

Serving Your Ham

Once your ham reaches the desired temperature, it’s time to let it rest. This allows the juices to redistribute, making each slice tender and juicy.

Tips for Carving

  1. Let the ham rest for at least 20-30 minutes before carving.
  2. Use a sharp carving knife to slice against the grain for the most tender pieces.
  3. Serve with sides like roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad to balance the richness of the ham.

Holiday Ham Side Dishes

To complement your holiday ham, consider these delightful side dishes:

1. Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Creamy mashed potatoes are a classic pairing with ham. Add roasted garlic for an extra layer of flavor.

2. Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Toss Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper, then roast until crispy for a delicious side that offers a nice crunch against the ham’s softness.

3. Honey-Glazed Carrots

Sauté baby carrots in butter and honey until tender for a sweet accompaniment that echoes the glaze on the ham.

4. Green Bean Almondine

Sauté fresh green beans with slivered almonds in butter for a fresh and crunchy side dish that adds color to your plate.

Leftover Ham Ideas

After the holiday feast, you might find yourself with leftover ham. Here are a few creative ways to use it up:

1. Ham and Cheese Quiche

Whip up a savory quiche with eggs, cream, cheese, and diced ham for a delicious breakfast or brunch option.

2. Ham Fried Rice

Transform leftover ham into a quick and easy fried rice dish. Sauté rice with vegetables, soy sauce, and diced ham for a satisfying meal.

3. Split Pea Soup

Simmer leftover ham bones with split peas and vegetables to create a warm and hearty soup, perfect for winter days.

Conclusion

Making the perfect holiday ham is an art that combines careful selection, thoughtful preparation, and a touch of creativity with glazes and sides. By following this guide, you’ll not only impress your guests but also create lasting memories around the dining table. Don’t forget to enjoy the process; the holiday spirit is about sharing love and joy through food. Happy cooking!
