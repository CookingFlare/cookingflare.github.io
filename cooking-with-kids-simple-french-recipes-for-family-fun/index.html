Cooking with Kids: Simple French Recipes for Family Fun


Cooking with kids is one of the best ways to spend quality time together as a family, while teaching them a valuable life skill - cooking! French cuisine is known for its simplicity and elegance, making it an excellent choice for family cooking. In this post, we'll explore some simple French recipes that are perfect for kids to help with in the kitchen. Making Everyday Meals Special Cooking with kids doesn't have to be complicated or time-consuming. In fact, some of the most memorable meals are often the simplest ones. By sharing everyday meals, like dinner, breakfast, and snacks, you can create special moments for your family. When kids are involved in cooking, they feel a sense of ownership and accomplishment that goes beyond just eating a meal. French cuisine is all about letting the quality of ingredients shine through. By using fresh, seasonal ingredients, you can create delicious and simple meals that are perfect for family cooking. Consider using ingredients like fresh vegetables, fruits, cheese, and herbs to create a variety of dishes. Simple French Recipes for Kids Here are a few simple French recipes that are perfect for kids to help with:

  1. Omelette French-Style An omelette is a classic French dish that's easy to make and requires minimal ingredients. All you need is:

2 eggs 1 tablespoon butter 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley or chives) Salt and pepper to taste Optional: 1 tablespoon grated cheese (such as cheddar or Parmesan)

To make an omelette French-style, crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them together with a fork. Add the chopped herbs, salt, and pepper. Heat a small non-stick pan over medium heat and add the butter. Once melted, pour in the egg mixture and cook until the edges start to set. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelette and tilt the pan so the uncooked egg can flow to the top. Once the eggs are almost set, add the grated cheese if using and fold the omelette in half. 2. Grilled Cheese Sandwich - The French Way A grilled cheese sandwich is a classic comfort food, but the French have their own twist on this classic recipe. All you need is:

2 slices of bread 1-2 tablespoons butter 1-2 slices of cheese (such as Comté or Gruyère) Optional: chopped fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)

To make a French-style grilled cheese, preheat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Butter one side of each slice of bread and place one slice butter-side down in the pan. Add one or two slices of cheese, depending on how cheesy you like it. If using herbs, sprinkle a pinch on top of the cheese. Add the second slice of bread, butter-side up, and cook for 2-3 minutes or until golden brown. 3. French Fruit Salad A fruit salad is a light and refreshing dessert that's perfect for a family gathering. All you need is:

1 cup mixed fresh fruit (such as strawberries, grapes, and pineapple) 2 tablespoons honey 1 tablespoon Grand Marnier (or other liqueur of your choice) Optional: 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint

To make a French-style fruit salad, combine the mixed fruit in a bowl. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and Grand Marnier until well combined. Pour the honey mixture over the fruit and toss to coat. If using mint, sprinkle a pinch on top of the fruit. 4. Chicken and Vegetable Soufflé Soufflé is a classic French dish that's often intimidating, but it's actually quite simple to make. All you need is:

2 boneless chicken breasts 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, onions, and mushrooms) 1 cup breadcrumbs 1/4 cup grated cheese (such as Parmesan or Gruyère) 1 egg 1 tablespoon butter Salt and pepper to taste

To make a simple chicken and vegetable soufflé, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the chicken breasts into small pieces and sauté in a non-stick pan until cooked through. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until tender. In a separate bowl, mix together the breadcrumbs, grated cheese, egg, and a pinch of salt and pepper. In a greased soufflé dish, arrange the cooked chicken and vegetables. Top with the breadcrumb mixture and dot with butter. Tips for Cooking with Kids Here are a few tips for cooking with kids:

Safety first: always supervise children while they're cooking, and make sure they understand basic kitchen safety rules. Choose simple recipes: start with simple recipes that require minimal ingredients and steps. Let them help: give kids tasks they can complete, such as washing herbs or sprinkling cheese. Make it fun: play music, sing songs, and have fun in the kitchen!

Conclusion Cooking with kids is a great way to spend quality time together and teach them a valuable life skill. By sharing simple French recipes, you can create special moments and memories that will last a lifetime. Don't forget to encourage your kids to get creative and experiment with new ingredients and flavors. And remember, the most important ingredient in any recipe is love!
