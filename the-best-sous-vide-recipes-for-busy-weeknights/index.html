The Best Sous Vide Recipes for Busy Weeknights


Busy weeknights often leave us scrambling for dinner ideas that are both quick and delicious. Enter sous vide cooking—a method that allows you to prepare restaurant-quality meals in the comfort of your own kitchen, even when time is tight. With sous vide, you can set your meal to cook while you tackle other tasks, and the results are consistently tender and flavorful. This post will explore the best sous vide recipes perfect for busy evenings, along with tips and tricks to maximize your efficiency.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, involves cooking food in a vacuum-sealed bag submerged in a temperature-controlled water bath. This technique allows for precise temperature control, ensuring your food is cooked evenly and retains moisture and flavor. It’s perfect for meals that benefit from slow, gentle cooking, making it ideal for proteins like steak, chicken, and fish, as well as vegetables.

Benefits of Sous Vide for Busy Weeknights

  • Time-Saving: You can set it and forget it. Prep your ingredients, set the sous vide machine, and go about your evening.
  • Consistent Results: Achieve perfectly cooked meals every time, eliminating the guesswork.
  • Meal Prep Friendly: Cook in bulk and store for the week, making it easy to grab a meal when you’re short on time.
  • Enhanced Flavors: The vacuum-sealed environment intensifies flavors and keeps food moist.

Essential Equipment

Before diving into our favorite recipes, you’ll need a few essential tools:

  1. Sous Vide Immersion Circulator: The heart of sous vide cooking. This device heats the water to a precise temperature.
  2. Vacuum Sealer: Helps seal food in bags to prevent water from entering. If you don’t have one, heavy-duty zip-top bags can work too.
  3. Large Pot or Container: Holds water for the sous vide bath.
  4. Thermometer: While not mandatory, it’s a good backup if you want to double-check temperatures.

The Best Sous Vide Recipes for Busy Weeknights

1. Sous Vide Chicken Breast with Garlic and Herbs

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 145°F (63°C).
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, minced garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Place the chicken in a vacuum-sealed bag and remove as much air as possible.
  4. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours.
  5. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the bag and sear in a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side for a golden crust.
  6. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables or a fresh salad.

2. Easy Sous Vide Pork Chops

Ingredients:

  • 2 bone-in pork chops
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat your sous vide water bath to 140°F (60°C).
  2. Season the pork chops generously with salt and pepper. If desired, add fresh herbs.
  3. Seal the chops in a vacuum bag, ensuring they’re well spaced.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 1.5 to 2 hours.
  5. Once done, heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat and sear the chops for 2-3 minutes on each side.
  6. Pair with applesauce or a side of sautéed greens for a complete meal.

3. Sous Vide Salmon with Lemon and Dill

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • Fresh dill
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Set your sous vide machine to 125°F (52°C) for perfectly tender salmon.
  2. Season the salmon with salt and pepper, and place lemon slices and dill on top.
  3. Seal in a vacuum bag with a drizzle of olive oil.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  5. Optionally, sear the salmon skin-side down in a hot pan for crispy skin before serving.
  6. Serve with quinoa or a light salad.

4. Sous Vide Vegetables Medley

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Herbs (thyme, rosemary, or Italian seasoning)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide to 183°F (84°C).
  2. Toss the vegetables in a bowl with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  3. Place in a vacuum-sealed bag and cook for 1 hour.
  4. These sous vide vegetables can be served as a side dish or tossed into a grain bowl for a hearty meal.

5. Sous Vide Beef Short Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Set your sous vide to 165°F (74°C).
  2. Season the short ribs with salt and pepper, then whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, and garlic in a bowl.
  3. Coat the ribs with the marinade and seal in a vacuum bag.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 48 hours for meltingly tender short ribs.
  5. Finish by searing in a hot pan or on a grill for a caramelized exterior before serving.

6. Sous Vide Egg Bites

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs
  • 1/4 cup cottage cheese
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional add-ins: spinach, bell peppers, cooked bacon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide to 172°F (78°C).
  2. In a blender, combine eggs, cottage cheese, salt, and pepper until smooth. Stir in shredded cheese and any additional ingredients.
  3. Pour the mixture into silicone molds and seal with foil.
  4. Cook in the water bath for 1 hour.
  5. Allow to cool slightly before removing from molds. Enjoy as a quick breakfast or snack throughout the week.

7. Sous Vide Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh herbs (rosemary or thyme)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide to 194°F (90°C).
  2. Toss the baby potatoes with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  3. Seal in a vacuum bag and cook for 1 hour.
  4. For a crispy finish, broil or sauté the potatoes after sous vide cooking.

Tips for Success with Sous Vide Cooking

  • Plan Ahead: While sous vide cooking can be set and forgotten, some recipes require longer cooking times. It’s wise to plan your meals in advance.
  • Batch Cooking: Consider preparing multiple servings of proteins or vegetables at once. They can be refrigerated or frozen for later use.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Sous vide is excellent for infusing flavors. Don’t hesitate to try different marinades, spices, or aromatics.
  • Finish with a Sear: For meats, a quick sear post-cooking can enhance texture and flavor, providing that perfect crust.

Conclusion

Sous vide cooking is a game-changer for busy weeknights, allowing you to prepare delicious and nutritious meals without spending hours in the kitchen. With these easy-to-follow recipes, you can enjoy perfectly cooked chicken, pork, fish, and vegetables—all while managing your time efficiently. Embrace the art of sous vide and discover how it can simplify your weeknight dinners while elevating your culinary creations. Happy cooking!
