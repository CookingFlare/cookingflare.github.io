How to Make a Classic Peach Pie


Creating a classic peach pie from scratch is an experience that delights all the senses—from the sweetness of ripe peaches to the comforting aroma of a buttery crust baking in the oven. A peach pie is the epitome of summer desserts, combining the juicy freshness of seasonal fruit with the warmth of homemade comfort. With this step-by-step guide, you’ll learn how to make a show-stopping peach pie that your family and friends will request every time peaches are in season.

Understanding the Ingredients: A Recipe for Perfection

Before diving into the process, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the ingredients required for this recipe, as their freshness and quality play a pivotal role in crafting the perfect peach pie.

For the Pie Crust:

  • All-purpose flour: Serves as the base and structure of the crust.
  • Unsalted butter: Provides richness and flakiness to the dough.
  • Ice water: Crucial for keeping the butter cold while ensuring the dough binds together.
  • Granulated sugar: A hint of sweetness that complements the pie filling.
  • Salt: Enhances the flavors in the crust.

For the Peach Filling:

  • Ripe peaches: The star of the dish, packed with natural sweetness and juicy goodness.
  • Granulated sugar: Balances the tartness of the peaches and creates the syrupy filling.
  • Brown sugar: Adds depth and a hint of caramelized richness.
  • Cornstarch: Thickens the filling to ensure it holds its shape.
  • Lemon juice: Brightens the flavor and prevents browning of the peaches.
  • Ground cinnamon: Adds warmth and spices up the filling.
  • Nutmeg (optional): Elevates the flavor profile with notes of spice, tying together the sweetness.
  • Vanilla extract: A subtle addition that enhances the flavor complexity.

Step-by-Step Directions: Masterclass in Peach Pie

Step 1: Prepare Your Pie Crust

The base of any pie is its crust, and mastering the art of pie dough will ensure your dessert is memorable. Start with the following steps:

  1. Measure the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix them thoroughly with a whisk or fork.

  2. Incorporate the butter: Cut 1 cup (two sticks) of unsalted butter into small cubes. Toss the butter cubes into the dry ingredients, then use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour. The mixture should resemble coarse crumbs with visible bits of butter.

  3. Add ice water slowly: Gradually drizzle in 6–8 tablespoons of ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing just until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough—this ensures your crust will be flaky and tender.

  4. Chill the dough: Divide the dough into two equal parts, shape them into disks, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the butter to firm up.

Step 2: Prep the Peaches

Fresh peaches are the key to a juicy, flavorful filling. Here’s how you prepare them:

  1. Peel and slice the peaches: Start with 6–8 medium peaches. To peel them easily, blanch them in boiling water for 30 seconds, then transfer them to an ice-water bath. The skins will slide off effortlessly. Cut the peaches into ¼-inch slices.

  2. Toss with sugar and seasonings: In a large bowl, add the peach slices and toss them with ½ cup granulated sugar, ¼ cup brown sugar, 2 tablespoons lemon juice, 2 tablespoons cornstarch, ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Stir until the peaches are evenly coated and allow the mixture to sit for at least 10 minutes to release their natural juices.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

With your crust resting in the refrigerator, it’s time to roll it out and assemble the base of your pie.

  1. Prepare your workspace: Lightly flour your countertop or pastry mat and rolling pin to prevent sticking.

  2. Roll out the bottom crust: Take one dough disk out of the fridge and roll it into a 12-inch circle, approximately ⅛-inch thick. Gently transfer it into a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it lightly into the edges and leaving an overhang. Trim any excess dough.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

Careful assembly ensures that every bite of your peach pie is visually and texturally perfect.

  1. Add the peach filling: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the peaches into the prepared pie crust, leaving behind excess liquid. Discard or save the juice for another use—it can be a fantastic drizzle for ice cream!

  2. Create the top crust: Roll out the second dough disk for a classic double crust pie, or cut it into strips for a lattice design. If opting for a lattice top, weave the strips over the peaches for a decorative finish.

  3. Seal the edges: Press and crimp the edges of the crust to seal it. If using a double crust top, don’t forget to cut a few slits in the center for venting steam.

  4. Brush and sprinkle for a golden finish: Whisk together 1 egg and 1 tablespoon of water for an egg wash. Brush this mixture over the top crust, then sprinkle with granulated sugar to add sparkle and crunch.

Step 5: Bake Your Pie

Baking is where the magic happens. Your kitchen will fill with the unforgettable aroma of peaches and buttery dough.

  1. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 425°F (220°C) and place a baking sheet on the lower rack to catch any drips.

  2. Bake in stages: Bake the pie at 425°F for 20 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 375°F (190°C). Continue baking for an additional 30–35 minutes, or until the crust turns golden brown and the filling bubbles.

  3. Cool the pie: Allow the pie to cool for at least 2 hours to set the filling. This prevents it from spilling out when sliced.

Enjoying Your Classic Peach Pie

After cooling, slice into your freshly baked peach pie and savor the contrast of crisp crust and succulent peaches enveloped in sweet syrup. Serve warm with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an irresistibly indulgent treat.

Tips for Success

  • Choose ripe peaches: The natural sweetness and juiciness of ripe peaches make all the difference in your pie’s flavor. If fresh peaches are unavailable, frozen or canned peaches can be a substitute—ensure they’re free from added syrups.
  • Keep ingredients cold: Cold butter is vital for a flaky crust. Chill your ingredients, tools, and even your hands if needed!
  • Control moisture: Excess peach juices can make the crust soggy. Strain the filling before adding it to the pie.
  • Experiment with spices: Cinnamon and nutmeg are classics, but adding ginger or cardamom can be delightful.

A Recipe Worth Sharing

There’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of preparing a homemade pie, and this classic peach pie is a timeless recipe for any occasion. Whether you’re baking as a weekend project, celebrating a summer cookout, or simply enjoying the bounty of fresh peaches, this pie is sure to become a family favorite. Don’t forget to share the recipe—and perhaps an extra slice—with loved ones!
