Succulent Roast Chicken Recipe: A Family Favorite


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a succulent roast chicken wafting through the house on a lazy Sunday afternoon. This classic dish has been a family favorite for generations, and for good reason. The crispy golden skin, tender juicy meat, and mouthwatering flavors make it a timeless crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering, a casual dinner with friends, or simply craving some comfort food, this succulent roast chicken recipe is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, about 4-5 pounds
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 sprig of fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig of fresh thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 2 stalks of celery, cut into large chunks
  • Olive oil

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Remove the chicken from its packaging and pat it dry with paper towels. This step is crucial for achieving that coveted crispy skin.

Step 2: Season the Chicken

In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with the minced garlic. Carefully loosen the skin of the chicken and rub the garlic butter underneath, ensuring an even distribution. Season the cavity of the chicken with salt and pepper. Squeeze the lemon halves over the chicken, then place them inside the cavity along with the sprigs of rosemary and thyme.

Step 3: Truss the Chicken

Tuck the wing tips under the body of the chicken and tie the legs together with kitchen twine. Trussing the chicken helps it cook evenly and ensures that the stuffing doesn’t fall out during roasting.

Step 4: Prepare the Vegetables

In a roasting pan, toss the quartered onion, carrots, and celery with a drizzle of olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, then spread the vegetables out to create a bed for the chicken.

Step 5: Roast the Chicken

Place the trussed chicken on top of the bed of vegetables in the roasting pan. Drizzle the chicken with a bit more olive oil and sprinkle with additional salt and pepper.

Step 6: Roast and Baste

Roast the chicken in the preheated oven for about 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the skin is golden brown and the juices run clear when pierced between the leg and thigh. Baste the chicken with the pan juices every 20 minutes to ensure a moist and flavorful result.

Step 7: Rest and Carve

Once the chicken is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy meat.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the succulent roast chicken with the roasted vegetables and your choice of sides. Whether it’s creamy mashed potatoes, buttery dinner rolls, or a fresh garden salad, this dish pairs well with a variety of accompaniments.

Conclusion

This succulent roast chicken recipe is a testament to the beauty of simple, wholesome cooking. With just a handful of ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create a memorable meal that brings loved ones together around the dinner table. So, the next time you’re in the mood for a comforting and delicious dish, consider preparing this family favorite roast chicken. The satisfaction of seeing your loved ones savor each bite will make it all worth it. Enjoy!
