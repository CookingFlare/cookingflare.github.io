One-Pot Cookout Recipes for Outdoor Dining


When the weather turns warm, there’s no better way to enjoy the great outdoors than with a delicious cookout. The smell of sizzling meat, the sound of laughter, and the relaxed atmosphere make outdoor dining a cherished experience. But instead of the usual grilling routine, why not mix things up with one-pot cookout recipes? These recipes are not only incredibly easy to prepare but also minimize the mess and clean-up, allowing you to focus more on enjoying your time outside.

One-pot meals are perfect for cookouts because they allow for easy cooking and serving, all while minimizing the number of dishes to wash later. Whether you’re gathering with family, friends, or having a peaceful solo cookout, these recipes will keep you satisfied and impress your guests. In this post, we will explore a range of savory one-pot dishes that are perfect for your next outdoor meal. From hearty stews to smoky pasta, there’s something for everyone.

1. Classic Chili in a Dutch Oven

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Directions:

  1. In a large Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef or turkey and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the pot. Sauté for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables soften.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook for an additional minute to bring out the flavors.
  4. Add the kidney beans, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and broth. Stir well and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Cover the Dutch oven and let it cook for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed before serving. Serve hot with toppings like shredded cheese, sour cream, or sliced jalapeños.

This classic chili is rich in flavor, and the combination of ground meat, beans, and spices makes it a crowd favorite. The one-pot method means less hassle and more time spent enjoying your outdoor gathering.

2. Grilled Mediterranean Chicken and Veggie Skillet

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Season the chicken breasts with olive oil, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked. Remove from the grill and set aside.
  3. In a large cast-iron skillet, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add the zucchini, onion, and bell pepper, sautéing for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Add the cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Slice the grilled chicken into strips and place it on top of the vegetables in the skillet. Sprinkle the feta cheese and olives over the top, and cover the skillet with a lid for about 5 minutes to allow the cheese to melt.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This Mediterranean-inspired dish brings together the flavors of grilled chicken, fresh vegetables, and tangy feta cheese. It’s a well-balanced meal that’s both satisfying and light, making it perfect for a summer cookout.

3. One-Pot BBQ Pulled Pork

Ingredients:

  • 3 lb pork shoulder
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups BBQ sauce (your favorite brand)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground mustard
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Buns for serving (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a large Dutch oven, heat a bit of oil over medium-high heat. Brown the pork shoulder on all sides, about 4-5 minutes per side.
  2. Remove the pork from the pot and set aside. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot, cooking for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the BBQ sauce, chicken broth, brown sugar, smoked paprika, mustard, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Add the pork shoulder back to the pot.
  4. Cover the Dutch oven and cook the pork over low heat for 4-5 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and easily shreds.
  5. Once the pork is ready, remove it from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded pork to the sauce, and stir in the apple cider vinegar.
  6. Serve the pulled pork on buns with additional BBQ sauce and coleslaw if desired.

BBQ pulled pork is a quintessential cookout meal, and this one-pot version makes it easy to create tender, flavorful pork without too much hassle. The low and slow cooking method allows the meat to absorb all the smoky, tangy goodness of the BBQ sauce.

4. Vegetarian Spanish Paella

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups short-grain rice (like Arborio)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 ½ teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, cooking for about 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the rice, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, peas, paprika, saffron (if using), smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid.
  5. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pan from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve the paella with lemon wedges and a sprinkle of fresh parsley for a refreshing finish.

This vegetarian Spanish paella is a vibrant, flavorful dish packed with vegetables and rich spices. It’s perfect for outdoor dining and can easily be customized with different vegetables depending on what’s in season.

5. One-Pot Shrimp and Grits

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup grits (stone-ground preferred)
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a medium pot, bring the water or chicken broth to a boil. Stir in the grits, reduce the heat to low, and cook according to package instructions (typically about 15-20 minutes), stirring occasionally to prevent lumps. Once cooked, stir in butter and season with salt and pepper.
  2. While the grits are cooking, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet, seasoning with smoked paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, until the shrimp are pink and opaque.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and remove from heat.
  5. Serve the shrimp over the creamy grits, garnished with fresh parsley.

This Southern classic is rich, comforting, and perfect for a cookout. The combination of succulent shrimp and creamy grits will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of the South.

6. One-Pot Beef and Potato Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the beef stew meat on all sides, about 5-6 minutes.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot, sautéing for about 3 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the carrots, potatoes, beef broth, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let the stew simmer for about 1 hour, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed before serving.

This hearty and comforting beef and potato stew is perfect for cooler evenings or when you want a filling meal with minimal effort. The one-pot cooking method ensures the flavors meld together beautifully.

Conclusion

One-pot cookout recipes are an excellent way to enjoy the outdoors while keeping your meal prep simple and stress-free. Whether you’re craving a savory chili, a Mediterranean-inspired chicken skillet, or a Southern-style shrimp and grits, these dishes provide a variety of flavors to suit all tastes. The best part is that they require minimal clean-up, allowing you to spend more time relaxing with friends and family. So next time you plan an outdoor gathering, consider adding a one-pot meal to the menu. It’ll be a delicious and convenient way to elevate your cookout experience!
