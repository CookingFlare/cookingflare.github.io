Simply Delicious: Beginner's Teriyaki Beef Stir-Fry


Stir-fry is a popular cooking technique that can be intimidating for beginners, but it’s actually quite simple and requires minimal ingredients. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious Teriyaki Beef Stir-Fry that’s perfect for a quick weeknight dinner. With just a few ingredients and a few simple steps, you’ll be enjoying a flavorful and nutritious meal in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef strips (sirloin or ribeye work well)
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, broccoli)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

First, prepare the ingredients. Slice the beef into thin strips and season with salt. Cut the onion and bell peppers into thin slices, and chop the broccoli into florets. Mince the garlic and grate the ginger.

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.

In the same skillet, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced onion and cook until softened, about 2-3 minutes. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Add the mixed vegetables to the skillet and cook until they’re tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes. Add the cooked beef back into the skillet and stir to combine with the vegetables.

In a small bowl, whisk together the teriyaki sauce and 1 tablespoon of water. Pour the sauce over the beef and vegetables, and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and the beef is coated.

Tips and Variations:

  • Use any combination of vegetables you like or have on hand. Other options include mushrooms, snap peas, and baby corn.
  • For a spicy kick, add a few dashes of hot sauce to the teriyaki sauce.
  • Serve with steamed rice or noodles for a filling meal.
  • Add some crunch by sprinkling chopped nuts or sesame seeds on top of the stir-fry.

Nutrition Information:

Per serving (serves 4):

  • Calories: 350
  • Protein: 35g
  • Fat: 15g
  • Saturated Fat: 3g
  • Cholesterol: 60mg
  • Sodium: 400mg
  • Carbohydrates: 20g
  • Fiber: 4g
  • Sugar: 10g

This recipe is not only delicious, but it’s also packed with nutrients. The beef provides a good source of protein, while the mixed vegetables add fiber, vitamins, and minerals. The teriyaki sauce adds a touch of sweetness without adding refined sugar.

Conclusion:

With this simple and delicious Teriyaki Beef Stir-Fry recipe, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a stir-fry master. The best part is that you can customize it to your taste by using different vegetables and adding your own seasonings. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced cook, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the delicious flavors of Asia in the comfort of your own home!
