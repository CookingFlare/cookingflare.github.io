Nut-Free Voyage: 55 Journey-Enhancing Nut-Free Recipes to Savor Globally


Embarking on a culinary adventure without compromising on taste or health? Say no more! Nut-free cooking has taken the globe by storm, and we’re here to take you on a whirlwind tour of extraordinary nut-free recipes. Here is a delightful smorgasbord bursting with exotic flavors from around the world—without nuts.

  1. Greek-style Chickpea Stew (Soupa Karnavali)

Transport yourself to the sun-drenched isles of Greece with the rich, robust flavors of this hearty vegetarian Soupa Karnavali. A happy union of canned chickpeas, potatoes, spinach, and tomatoes inspired by the classic Greek vegetable stew, this satisfying meal is sure to keep your taste buds dancing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 minced garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 400g canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 chopped potato
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh lemon juice, optional

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until onion is translucent.

  2. Stir in tomato paste and cook for another minute.

  3. Toss in the chickpeas, potato, and spinach, followed by vegetable broth, tomatoes, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until potatoes are tender.

  5. Optional: finish the stew off by adding a splash of fresh lemon juice.

  6. Latin American Corn and Veggie Sauté (Elote Salad)

This vibrant Elote Salad is a rotor-rooter Latin American classic taking inspiration from Mexican street corn. Loaded with corn, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, and a zesty chipotle-lime dressing – this dish is a killer combination of colors, textures, and flavors that beg to be shared and enjoyed.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of corn, kernels removed
  • 1 small zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped cilantro
  • 1 dried chipotle pepper, rehydrated and finely chopped (adjust quantity to taste)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional toppings: feta cheese, queso fresco, or lime crema

Directions:

  1. Blanch the corn kernels in boiling water for 2 minutes, then drain.

  2. In a large bowl, combine all ingredients and gently toss to combine, being careful not to break up the tender corn kernels.

  3. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, black pepper, and additional lime juice if needed.

  4. To crank it up a notch, feel free to sprinkle your fave toppings over this crowd-pleaser.

  5. South Indian Cabbage and Green Peas Curry (Cabbage Pachadi)

Cabbage Pachadi tempts the senses with the robust flavors of coconut, mustard seeds, and curry leaves. This refreshing yet soul-warming South Indian dish is vegan, gluten-free, and surprisingly easy to whip up at home!

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida powder
  • 5-6 curry leaves, fresh
  • 1 small cabbage, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 2 green chili peppers, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • Salt and ground turmeric, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them pop. Then, add asafoetida powder and curry leaves, sautéing until leaves darken.
  2. Toss in the cabbage, green peas, grated coconut, and green chili peppers. Mix well, then cover and cook for 5-7 minutes or until vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in water and season with salt and turmeric. Cook for another 3-4 minutes to meld flavors.
  4. Serve garnished with fresh coriander leaves, preferably with rice or roti.
