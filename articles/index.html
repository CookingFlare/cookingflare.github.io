Spanish Style Tuna Stew Recipe: A Seafood Extravaganza

If you’re a seafood lover, get ready to embark on a culinary adventure with this Spanish Style Tuna Stew recipe. Bursting with bold flavors and succulent seafood, this dish is a true extravaganza for your taste buds. Ingredients 1 kg fresh tuna steaks, cut into bite-sized pieces 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 large onion, finely chopped 4 cloves of garlic, minced 2 red bell peppers, sliced 1 green bell pepper, sliced 2 ripe tomatoes, diced 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste) 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 bay leaf 1 cup dry white wine 2 cups fish or vegetable broth Salt and black pepper to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish) Instructions Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they turn golden and fragrant. Add the sliced bell peppers and continue to cook for another 5 minutes, until they start to soften. Stir in the diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, dried oregano, and bay leaf. Cook for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Increase the heat to high and add the tuna pieces to the pot. Sear the tuna for about 2 minutes on each side until it is lightly browned. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for a minute or two, allowing the alcohol to evaporate. Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the fish or vegetable broth. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the tuna is cooked through and tender. Remove the bay leaf and discard. Serve the Spanish Style Tuna Stew hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. This dish pairs beautifully with a side of crusty bread or steamed rice. How to Make Spanish Stuffed Peppers: A Flavorful Recipe

Spanish stuffed peppers, or “pimientos rellenos,” are a delightful dish that combines vibrant flavors with a satisfying texture. This recipe will guide you step-by-step to create these colorful delights that are perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to impress guests at a dinner party or simply want a comforting meal at home, these stuffed peppers are sure to please. Ingredients For the Peppers: 4 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or green) Olive oil for drizzling Salt and pepper to taste For the Filling: 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown) 1 pound ground beef or turkey (or a vegetarian substitute) 1 medium onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1 teaspoon cumin ½ teaspoon dried oregano 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend) Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped, for garnish For the Sauce: 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon smoked paprika Salt and pepper to taste Preparation Step 1: Prepare the Peppers Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Wash the bell peppers. Cut the tops off and remove the seeds and membranes. Keep the tops for later use if you desire. Drizzle the peppers with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place them upright in a baking dish. Step 2: Make the Filling In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant. Add the ground meat to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until the meat is browned and cooked through, about 7-10 minutes. Stir in the diced tomatoes, cooked rice, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and half of the shredded cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well and let it simmer for 5 minutes until heated through. Step 3: Stuff the Peppers Carefully spoon the filling into each bell pepper, packing it tightly but leaving a little space at the top. Place the tops back on the peppers if desired, or leave them open for a cheesier finish. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top of each stuffed pepper. Step 4: Prepare the Sauce In a small saucepan, heat the tomato sauce over medium heat. Stir in olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes. Step 5: Bake the Stuffed Peppers Pour the tomato sauce evenly over the stuffed peppers in the baking dish. Cover the dish with aluminum foil to keep the moisture in during baking. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is bubbly and golden. Step 6: Serve Remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro before serving. These peppers can be served as a main dish or as a side alongside a salad or crusty bread. Tips for Perfect Stuffed Peppers Choose the Right Peppers: While bell peppers are traditional, feel free to experiment with other varieties like poblano or Anaheim for a different flavor profile. Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator. Stuff the peppers just before baking for a quick meal. Add Some Heat: If you like a little spice, add some diced jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne to the filling. Vegetarian Option: Substitute the meat with lentils, quinoa, or a mix of your favorite vegetables for a delicious vegetarian version. Nutritional Benefits Spanish stuffed peppers are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Bell peppers are high in vitamins A and C, antioxidants, and dietary fiber.
