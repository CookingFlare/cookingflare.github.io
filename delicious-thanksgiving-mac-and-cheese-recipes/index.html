Delicious Thanksgiving Mac and Cheese Recipes


Thanksgiving is the season of gratitude, flavors, and hearty gatherings. While turkey often takes center stage during this festive feast, side dishes play a crucial role in complementing the occasion. One side dish that’s guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser is mac and cheese. Creamy, cheesy, and comforting, mac and cheese can be customized in endless ways to suit the Thanksgiving spirit. Whether you’re hosting an intimate gathering or a lively family dinner, these recipes will elevate your table and create lasting memories.

1. Classic Thanksgiving Mac and Cheese

Sometimes, simple is best. A classic mac and cheese recipe reminds everyone of their childhood and delivers rich, cheesy satisfaction.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz elbow macaroni
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup gouda cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp paprika

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Melt the butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk constantly until a smooth paste is formed (this is your roux).
  4. Gradually add milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens slightly.
  5. Lower the heat and add the cheddar, gouda, and mozzarella. Stir until the cheeses melt into a smooth sauce.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika.
  7. Mix the cooked macaroni into the cheese sauce until fully coated. Transfer to a baking dish.
  8. Bake for 25 minutes until golden and bubbly.

This timeless recipe pairs perfectly with roast turkey and cranberry sauce, bringing comfort and familiarity to your Thanksgiving table.

2. Lobster Mac and Cheese

Looking to add a touch of elegance to your Thanksgiving spread? Lobster mac and cheese is indulgent, sophisticated, and sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz shell pasta
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups gruyère cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup parmesan cheese, shredded
  • 2 cups fresh lobster meat, cooked and chopped
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Directions:

  1. Cook the shell pasta according to package directions, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the butter in a saucepan over medium heat and sprinkle in the flour. Stir to create a roux.
  3. Slowly pour in heavy cream and milk while whisking continuously. Add garlic powder, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  4. Lower the heat and mix in gruyère and parmesan cheese until the sauce is smooth.
  5. Stir in lobster meat, ensuring it’s evenly distributed throughout the sauce.
  6. Combine the pasta with the lobster cheese sauce and transfer it to a baking dish.
  7. Toss breadcrumbs with olive oil, then sprinkle over the top of the dish.
  8. Broil on high for 3–5 minutes or until the top is golden and crispy.

This luxurious dish is the ultimate combination of comfort food and seafood sophistication.

3. Smoky Bacon Mac and Cheese

Infuse smoky, savory flavors into your Thanksgiving side with bacon. This recipe balances creamy cheese with the crispy, smoky goodness of bacon.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz cavatappi pasta
  • 6 slices of thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup smoked gouda cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup Monterey Jack cheese, shredded
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Cook the cavatappi pasta according to the instructions on the package. Drain and set aside.
  2. Fry the bacon in a skillet until crispy and set aside on paper towels to drain excess grease.
  3. Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat and whisk in flour.
  4. Gradually pour in the milk, stirring constantly to create a creamy base. Add smoked gouda, cheddar, and Monterey Jack to create a luscious, cheesy sauce.
  5. Mix in the smoked paprika, salt, and pepper for flavor.
  6. Incorporate the bacon bits and cooked pasta into the cheese sauce.
  7. Transfer to a baking dish and bake at 375°F for 20 minutes.
  8. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

Perfectly smoky and absolutely irresistible, this bacon mac and cheese fits right into a hearty Thanksgiving meal.

4. Pumpkin Mac and Cheese

What’s Thanksgiving without pumpkin? This creative twist incorporates the rich, earthy flavors of pumpkin puree, making it uniquely seasonal.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz penne pasta
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup aged cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup fontina cheese, shredded
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crushed pecans for topping

Directions:

  1. Cook penne pasta until al dente, then drain.
  2. Melt butter in a saucepan and whisk in flour to form a roux.
  3. Gradually add milk, continuously whisking to avoid lumps. Stir in pumpkin puree until combined.
  4. Add aged cheddar and fontina cheese, stirring until melted.
  5. Mix in maple syrup, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, and pepper for a hint of sweetness and spice.
  6. Toss the pasta into the pumpkin cheese sauce, then transfer to a casserole dish.
  7. Sprinkle crushed pecans over the top and bake at 350°F for 20 minutes.

This pumpkin mac and cheese blends fall flavors with creamy indulgence for a standout dish.

5. Vegan Thanksgiving Mac and Cheese

For your plant-based guests, this vegan mac and cheese is just as creamy and delightful as the traditional version.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz elbow macaroni
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup vegan cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs

Directions:

  1. Cook elbow macaroni according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a saucepan and stir in flour until a thick paste forms.
  3. Gradually add almond milk while whisking, then stir in nutritional yeast, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add vegan cheddar cheese and stir until melted and incorporated.
  5. Toss the macaroni into the vegan cheese sauce, ensuring it’s fully coated.
  6. Transfer to a baking dish and top with panko breadcrumbs.
  7. Broil until the breadcrumbs are crispy and golden.

This vegan alternative ensures that no one misses out on the Thanksgiving mac and cheese magic.

Final Thoughts

Mac and cheese is a versatile dish that lends itself to endless creativity, making it an ideal addition to your Thanksgiving menu. From classic comforts to innovative flavors, these recipes cater to diverse preferences and bring joy to the table. Whether you’re keeping it traditional or crafting something unique, mac and cheese is bound to be a Thanksgiving tradition your guests will adore for years to come.

Create warmth, smiles, and full stomachs—and let mac and cheese steal the spotlight this Thanksgiving. Happy cooking!
