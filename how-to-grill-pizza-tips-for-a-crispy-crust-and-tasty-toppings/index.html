How to Grill Pizza: Tips for a Crispy Crust and Tasty Toppings


Grilling pizza is an art that combines the smoky, charred flavor of the grill with the delicious, melty goodness of your favorite pizza ingredients. Whether you’re looking to elevate your backyard BBQ or experiment with a different way to make pizza, grilling is the perfect method for creating a crispy, flavorful crust and vibrant, mouth-watering toppings.

If you’ve never grilled pizza before, don’t worry! This guide will take you through all the steps to make perfect grilled pizza every time. From preparing the dough to choosing the right toppings and perfecting the grilling technique, we’ve got all the tips and tricks you need.

Why Grill Pizza?

Grilling pizza isn’t just a trendy cooking method – it’s a great way to achieve a crispy, yet tender crust that you simply can’t get with a traditional oven. The intense heat from the grill sears the bottom of the pizza, creating that signature crispy texture, while the indirect heat cooks the toppings to perfection. Grilled pizza also has a distinct smoky flavor that adds depth to every bite.

Benefits of Grilled Pizza

  • Crispy Crust: The high heat of the grill helps create a crispy bottom, with a lightly charred edge, adding an irresistible texture.
  • Smoky Flavor: The grill imparts a subtle smoky flavor to the dough and toppings that you won’t get with a standard oven.
  • Quick Cooking: Grilling pizza is faster than baking in the oven, so it’s perfect for quick meals or entertaining guests.
  • Endless Variations: You can experiment with different crusts, sauces, and toppings, giving you endless creative possibilities.

The Basics of Grilling Pizza

Before we dive into the specifics, let’s cover the basic steps for grilling pizza. Follow this easy process for a flawless pizza every time.

1. Choose the Right Pizza Dough

For the best grilled pizza, you’ll want to use a dough that can withstand the heat of the grill. There are two main options when it comes to dough: store-bought and homemade.

Store-Bought Dough: If you’re looking for convenience, you can use store-bought dough. Most pizza doughs available in supermarkets are suitable for grilling, but it’s important to let them come to room temperature before you start shaping them. Cold dough can be tough to stretch and may tear easily.

Homemade Dough: Making your own pizza dough is simple and gives you more control over the texture. A basic dough recipe includes flour, yeast, water, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. If you have time, letting your dough rise for an hour or so can improve the flavor and texture.

2. Prepare the Grill

Preheat your grill to a medium-high temperature (about 450°F to 500°F). This temperature range is ideal for achieving that crispy crust while allowing the toppings to cook evenly.

  • For Charcoal Grills: Arrange the charcoal on one side of the grill, creating two zones: a hot zone for searing and a cooler zone for indirect heat. This setup will allow you to control the cooking process and avoid burning the pizza.
  • For Gas Grills: Preheat your gas grill with all burners on high for 10 to 15 minutes. Once it’s heated, you can adjust the burners to have a direct heat zone and an indirect heat zone, much like with a charcoal grill.

3. Shape the Dough

When your grill is ready, it’s time to shape the dough. Flour your work surface and your hands to prevent the dough from sticking. Using your fingers, gently stretch the dough into a round or oval shape, depending on your preference. Aim for about 10-12 inches in diameter, but you can make it larger or smaller depending on the size of your grill.

To prevent the dough from sticking to the grill, lightly oil one side of the dough with olive oil before placing it on the grill. The oiled side will be the side that hits the grill first.

4. Grill the Dough

Place the oiled side of the dough directly on the grill grates over direct heat. Close the lid and cook for about 2-3 minutes. You should see bubbles start to form on the surface of the dough, and the bottom will begin to turn golden brown. Once the bottom is nicely charred and crispy, use tongs or a spatula to flip the dough over.

5. Add Toppings

After flipping the dough, it’s time to add your toppings. Be sure to choose toppings that cook quickly, as the pizza only spends a short amount of time on the grill after being flipped. Here’s how you can layer your toppings:

  • Sauce: Spread a thin layer of sauce (tomato, pesto, or even white sauce) over the grilled side of the dough.
  • Cheese: Add your favorite cheeses – mozzarella is the classic choice, but feel free to experiment with others like parmesan, goat cheese, or cheddar.
  • Toppings: Add your preferred toppings, such as vegetables, meats, or fresh herbs. Keep in mind that grilled pizzas should be topped with ingredients that won’t release too much moisture. Otherwise, your pizza may end up soggy.

6. Finish Grilling

Once you’ve added the toppings, close the grill lid and cook the pizza for another 2-3 minutes. The heat will melt the cheese and cook the toppings. Keep an eye on the pizza to avoid burning it, especially around the edges.

The pizza is ready when the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the crust is crisp and slightly charred. Remove it from the grill using a pizza peel or large spatula.

7. Slice and Serve

Once off the grill, let your pizza rest for a minute or two before slicing. This gives the cheese a chance to set and makes cutting easier. Slice and enjoy your perfectly grilled pizza with your favorite drink!

Essential Tips for Perfect Grilled Pizza

While grilling pizza is simple, there are a few tips that can help you master the technique and elevate your pizza-making game.

1. Don’t Overload the Pizza with Toppings

Grilled pizza cooks quickly, and the more toppings you add, the more difficult it will be to cook everything evenly. Keep your toppings light and well-balanced. Too many toppings can weigh down the crust and cause uneven cooking.

2. Use a Pizza Stone or Grill Pan

If you’re worried about the dough falling through the grill grates, a pizza stone or grill pan can help. Preheat the stone on the grill before placing the pizza on it, or use a grill pan for easy pizza cooking. A stone helps distribute heat evenly and promotes a crispier crust.

3. Control the Heat

Grilling pizza requires high heat, but you must also be able to control the temperature. Too much heat will burn the crust before the toppings have a chance to cook. If your grill has hot spots, rotate the pizza to avoid uneven cooking.

4. Pre-cook Some Toppings

Some toppings, like sausage, mushrooms, or onions, release moisture and can make the pizza soggy. To avoid this, pre-cook these ingredients before adding them to the pizza. This ensures that they cook properly and don’t affect the pizza’s texture.

5. Use Fresh, Quality Ingredients

Grilled pizza is all about enhancing simple, fresh flavors. Use high-quality cheese, ripe tomatoes, fresh herbs, and good-quality olive oil. When it comes to toppings, less is often more. A few high-quality ingredients will make the pizza taste fantastic.

Popular Grilled Pizza Recipes

1. Margherita Pizza

A simple Margherita pizza is a perfect choice for grilling. Top your dough with a thin layer of tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and basil leaves. Drizzle with olive oil and a sprinkle of sea salt before grilling.

2. BBQ Chicken Pizza

Grilled BBQ chicken pizza is always a hit. Top your dough with BBQ sauce, cooked chicken (preferably grilled), red onions, and cheddar cheese. Add a sprinkle of cilantro after grilling for an extra burst of flavor.

3. Veggie Delight Pizza

For a veggie pizza, use grilled zucchini, bell peppers, mushrooms, and onions. Add some feta cheese or goat cheese and drizzle with a balsamic glaze for a sophisticated touch.

4. Prosciutto and Arugula Pizza

Top your grilled dough with fresh mozzarella, prosciutto, and a handful of arugula. After grilling, drizzle with a little olive oil and a squeeze of lemon juice for a fresh, light pizza.

5. Four Cheese Pizza

Go all out with a four-cheese pizza. Use a blend of mozzarella, parmesan, goat cheese, and ricotta. For a touch of elegance, add some fresh thyme or rosemary after grilling.

Conclusion

Grilling pizza is a fun and delicious way to create the perfect pie. With a little practice and the right techniques, you can master the art of grilling pizza and enjoy a crispy, flavorful crust with every bite. By following these tips and experimenting with your favorite toppings, you’ll soon become a grilling pizza pro.

Remember, the key to a successful grilled pizza is not just in the technique but in the ingredients. Use high-quality dough, fresh toppings, and a grill that’s properly preheated for the best results. Whether you’re making a classic Margherita or trying something creative like BBQ chicken, grilling pizza is a great way to enjoy a homemade, restaurant-quality meal at home. Happy grilling!
