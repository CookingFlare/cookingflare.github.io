Cherry Pie Bars: All the Flavor of Pie in a Bar


If you’re a fan of cherry pie but find the process of making a traditional pie a bit daunting, then cherry pie bars might be just the thing for you. These delightful treats offer all the delicious flavor of a classic cherry pie but in a convenient, handheld bar form. Perfect for picnics, potlucks, or simply enjoying with a cup of coffee, cherry pie bars are a versatile and crowd-pleasing dessert option.

Ingredients

For the Crust and Topping:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Cherry Filling:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust and Topping

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt for the crust and topping. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or two forks until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Stir in the beaten egg and vanilla extract until the dough comes together.
  4. Set aside 1 1/2 cups of the mixture for the topping and press the remaining mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is lightly golden.

Step 2: Make the Cherry Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice for the filling. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the cherries soften, about 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the cherry filling from the heat and stir in the almond extract. Let the filling cool slightly.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Spread the cherry filling evenly over the partially baked crust.
  2. Crumble the reserved crust and topping mixture over the cherry filling.
  3. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown.
  4. Allow the cherry pie bars to cool completely in the pan before cutting into bars.

Serving and Storing

Once cooled, cut the cherry pie bars into squares and serve. These bars are best enjoyed within a few days of baking and can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature. For longer storage, they can be refrigerated or even frozen for up to a month.

Final Thoughts

Cherry pie bars are a fantastic way to enjoy the classic flavors of cherry pie without the fuss of making a traditional pie. Their portable nature makes them an excellent option for parties and gatherings, and they can be easily customized with different fruit fillings to suit your preferences. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these cherry pie bars are sure to be a hit with everyone who tries them. So, give this recipe a try and enjoy the alluring taste of cherry pie in a convenient bar form!
