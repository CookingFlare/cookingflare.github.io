Savory and Satisfying: Beef Stroganoff Recipe


Beef Stroganoff is a classic Russian dish that has become a favorite in many households around the world. This rich and hearty dish features tender strips of beef cooked in a creamy mushroom sauce, served over a bed of egg noodles or rice. It’s a perfect comfort food that is sure to satisfy even the pickiest of eaters. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious and flavorful Beef Stroganoff that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef sirloin, cut into thin strips
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Egg noodles or rice, cooked according to package instructions

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until the onions are soft and translucent.

  2. Add the mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown. Remove the mushrooms from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the beef strips and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.

  4. Reduce the heat to low and add the flour to the skillet, stirring constantly to make a roux. Cook the roux for a minute or two, until it turns golden brown.

  5. Slowly whisk in the beef broth, making sure to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens.

  6. Return the beef and mushrooms to the skillet and stir to coat them in the sauce. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the beef is cooked to your desired level of doneness.

  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the sour cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  8. Serve the Beef Stroganoff over a bed of cooked egg noodles or rice. Garnish with chopped parsley for an extra touch of freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier version, you can use lean cuts of beef such as tenderloin or top round.
  • Feel free to add some extra vegetables to the dish, such as bell peppers or peas, to make it even more nutritious and colorful.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can add a slurry of cornstarch and water to the skillet and cook until the sauce reaches your desired consistency.
  • To make this recipe vegetarian, you can substitute the beef with tofu or seitan, and use vegetable broth instead of beef broth.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a skillet or microwave before serving.

Beef Stroganoff is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for lunch or dinner. Its creamy and savory flavors make it a crowd-pleaser at any gathering. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this recipe is sure to impress. So, go ahead and give it a try – you won’t be disappointed! Enjoy!
