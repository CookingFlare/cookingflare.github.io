Delicious Homemade Baguette Recipe: Perfect Crust, Soft Inside


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the kitchen. One type of bread that never fails to delight is the classic French baguette. With its crisp, golden crust and soft, airy interior, it’s the epitome of artisanal baking. While the perfect baguette may seem like a lofty goal for a home baker, fear not! With the right recipe and technique, you can achieve bakery-quality baguettes right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 350ml water

Instructions

Step 1: Mixing the Dough

Start by mixing the bread flour and salt in a large mixing bowl. In a separate container, combine the instant yeast with water and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy. Once the yeast is activated, pour it into the flour mixture.

Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10-15 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

Step 2: First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 3: Shaping the Baguettes

Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it by pressing down on it. Turn it out onto a floured surface and divide it into three equal portions. Shape each portion into a rough rectangle, then fold the top edge of the rectangle down to the center and press it into the dough. Fold the bottom edge up to the center and press it into the dough. Finally, fold the dough in half along the center and seal the edges by pressing firmly.

Place the shaped baguettes onto a floured couche or parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving enough space between each one. Cover them loosely with a kitchen towel and let them rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.

Step 4: Preparing the Oven

While the baguettes are rising, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). If you have a baking stone, place it in the oven to heat up as well. If not, a sturdy baking sheet will do the trick.

Step 5: Scoring and Baking

Once the baguettes have finished their final rise, it’s time to prepare them for the oven. Use a sharp knife or bread lame to make 3-4 diagonal slashes along the top of each baguette. This not only adds a decorative touch but also allows the bread to expand as it bakes.

Carefully transfer the baguettes to the preheated oven, either directly onto the baking stone or onto the baking sheet. If you have a spray bottle, spritz some water into the oven to create steam, which will help develop a crisp crust. Bake the baguettes for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 6: Cooling and Enjoying

Once the baguettes are done, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. It may be tempting to tear into them right away, but allowing them to cool will help the interior develop its characteristic softness.

When the baguettes have cooled, slice into them and savor the delightful contrast of the crispy crust and pillowy interior. Enjoy them fresh with a smear of butter or use them to make the perfect French sandwich.

Conclusion

With a little time and patience, you can create your own batch of delicious, homemade baguettes that rival those from a Parisian bakery. The process of mixing, kneading, shaping, and baking may seem daunting at first, but the reward of biting into a freshly baked baguette is well worth the effort. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and embark on the satisfying journey of crafting your own perfect baguette.
