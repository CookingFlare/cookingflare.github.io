Sweet Potato Pie Recipe for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and indulgent desserts. While pumpkin pie may steal much of the spotlight, sweet potato pie is a Southern classic that deserves a special place on your Thanksgiving dessert table. Rich, creamy, and spiced with the warm flavors of the season, sweet potato pie offers a heartwarming finish to any festive meal. This recipe is easy to follow, highlights the natural sweetness of vibrant sweet potatoes, and is guaranteed to bring smiles to everyone gathered around the table.

Why Sweet Potato Pie Deserves a Spot at Thanksgiving

Sweet potato pie is more than just a dessert; it’s a nostalgic favorite often tied to traditions and comfort. Many families have cherished memories of loved ones preparing this pie, its aromatic spices filling the air while everyone gathers and waits for the celebratory meal. Unlike pumpkin pie, sweet potato pie has a denser, richer texture that balances perfectly with warm spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and clove.

Not only is it delicious, but sweet potato pie is also relatively simple to prepare. It’s made predominantly with wholesome ingredients, many of which you may already have in your pantry. Plus, sweet potatoes are packed with vitamins, antioxidants, and fiber, making this dessert a slightly healthier option compared to other sugary pies.

The Perfect Sweet Potato Pie Recipe

Ingredients

For the base:

  • 1 homemade or store-bought pie crust, prepped and ready

For the filling:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes (about 1 ½ pounds)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup whole milk (or evaporated milk for a richer filling)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • Pinch of salt

For the topping (optional):

  • Whipped cream, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Cook the Sweet Potatoes

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Wash and peel the sweet potatoes, then cut them into chunks. Place them in a pot of boiling water and cook until tender, about 20–25 minutes. Drain and allow them to cool slightly, then carefully mash until smooth using a potato masher or a fork. Alternatively, you can use a food processor for an even creamier texture.

Pro tip: For extra richness and depth of flavor, you can roast the sweet potatoes in the oven instead of boiling them. Wrap each potato in aluminum foil and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 50–60 minutes until soft.

2. Prepare the Filling

In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes and melted butter, stirring until smooth and well-incorporated. Add both sugars (granulated and brown sugar), then mix thoroughly. Gradually whisk in the milk, ensuring the mixture is creamy and lump-free. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to create a custard-like consistency.

Once mixed, stir in the vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and a pinch of salt. Taste the filling; adjust spices or sweetness as needed based on your preference.

3. Assemble the Pie

Roll out your pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish, trimming any overhanging edges and crimping the sides for a decorative finish. If using store-bought crust, follow the package instructions to prepare it for baking.

Pour the sweet potato filling into the prepared pie crust, smoothing the top with a spatula to ensure it’s evenly distributed. The pie will puff slightly while baking, so leave a little room at the top to prevent overflow.

4. Bake to Perfection

Place the pie on the middle rack of your preheated oven and bake for 50–60 minutes or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. To check doneness, insert a toothpick or knife into the center; if it comes out clean, the pie is ready. Keep an eye on the crust while baking. If it starts to brown too quickly, loosely cover the edges with aluminum foil to prevent over-baking.

Let the pie cool completely in the pie dish before slicing. Cooling allows the filling to set properly, enhancing its flavors and texture.

Serving Suggestions

Sweet potato pie is a versatile dessert that pairs beautifully with various toppings. Serve it with a dollop of freshly whipped cream for a classic touch, or drizzle a bit of caramel or maple syrup for added indulgence. You can also garnish the pie with toasted pecans or a light sprinkle of powdered sugar for a festive presentation.

For an inventive twist, consider adding a layer of streusel topping before baking. Mix brown sugar, butter, and flour with a pinch of cinnamon, crumble it on top of the pie, and bake as usual. The crunchy topping adds an irresistible texture that complements the creamy filling.

Make Ahead and Storage Tips

One of the joys of sweet potato pie is its ability to be prepared in advance, making it a stress-free addition to your Thanksgiving spread. After baking, cover the cooled pie tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and store it in the fridge for up to three days. If you’re preparing it ahead of time, consider reheating slices in the oven at 300°F (150°C) for 10 minutes before serving to restore the fresh, warm texture.

To freeze sweet potato pie, wrap it securely in plastic wrap, followed by a layer of foil. It will keep in the freezer for up to two months. When you’re ready to serve, let the pie thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat gently in the oven.

Tips for the Best Sweet Potato Pie

  • Choose the right sweet potatoes: Opt for orange-fleshed sweet potatoes, such as the Garnet or Beauregard varieties, for their natural sweetness and vibrant color.
  • Focus on the spices: Balance is key when using spices. Cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves should enhance the sweet potato flavor without overpowering it.
  • Don’t skimp on butter: Butter gives this pie its rich and indulgent decadence, so use it generously.
  • Ensure a creamy filling: Properly mash or puree the sweet potatoes for a velvety consistency. Smooth filling creates the perfect texture when baked.
  • Blind bake your crust if needed: If you want extra insurance against a soggy bottom, blind bake your crust for five minutes before filling.

A Dish to Bring Everyone Together

The beauty of sweet potato pie lies in its simplicity, timeless appeal, and the joy it brings to those who savor it. As Thanksgiving draws closer, this dessert provides a way to honor tradition while embracing the flavors of the season. Its sweetness and spice make it a crowd-pleaser, and the moments spent preparing it can become cherished memories of your own.

There’s something magical about transforming wholesome ingredients into a dessert that bridges connection and celebration. Whether you’re an experienced baker or trying your hand at this recipe for the first time, sweet potato is forgiving, nostalgic, and endlessly satisfying. By adding this pie to your Thanksgiving menu, you’re guaranteed to create a new holiday favorite for everyone to enjoy.

So, preheat your oven, dust off your rolling pin, and indulge in the wonderful tradition of baking a sweet potato pie. It might just become the highlight of your holiday feast.
