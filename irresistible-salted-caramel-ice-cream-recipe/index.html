Irresistible Salted Caramel Ice Cream Recipe


Who doesn’t love the creamy, sweet, and slightly salty indulgence of salted caramel ice cream? This delectable frozen treat is a favorite for many, and making it at home is easier than you might think. With a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create a batch of homemade salted caramel ice cream that will rival any gourmet scoop you’d find in an ice cream parlor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Salted Caramel

  1. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.

  2. Once the sugar has dissolved, stop stirring and increase the heat to medium-high. Allow the mixture to boil, swirling the pan occasionally, until it turns a deep amber color. This should take about 10-12 minutes.

  3. Once the caramel reaches the desired color, remove the pan from the heat and carefully pour in the heavy cream while stirring constantly. Be cautious as the mixture will bubble vigorously.

  4. Return the pan to low heat and stir until any hardened caramel is dissolved. Add the sea salt and stir until well combined. Set the caramel aside to cool slightly.

Step 2: Make the Ice Cream Base

  1. In a separate saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it begins to steam, but not boil.

  2. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon.

  3. Strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the custard to cool to room temperature.

Step 3: Combine and Chill

  1. Once the caramel and custard are both at room temperature, slowly pour the caramel into the custard, whisking constantly until well combined.

  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, ensuring it touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.

Step 4: Churn the Ice Cream

  1. Once the mixture is thoroughly chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually about 20-25 minutes, until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  2. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Cover the surface directly with parchment paper or wax paper to prevent ice crystals from forming, then seal the container with a lid. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. When ready to serve, allow the ice cream to sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly for easier scooping. Serve in bowls or cones, and if desired, drizzle with additional salted caramel sauce for an extra indulgent treat.

Conclusion

There’s something truly satisfying about creating your own ice cream from scratch, and this irresistible salted caramel ice cream is no exception. With its rich caramel flavor and just the right amount of saltiness, it’s a dessert that’s sure to impress. Whether enjoyed on its own or as a decadent accompaniment to a warm dessert, this homemade ice cream is a delightful indulgence that will have everyone coming back for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every spoonful of this delightful frozen treat.
