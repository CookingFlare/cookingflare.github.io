Healthy Grilling Recipes: 10 Light Options for Summer


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the great outdoors while savoring delicious meals. However, grilling doesnâ€™t have to mean heavy, calorie-laden dishes. In fact, there are countless ways to create light, healthy, and flavorful options that will satisfy your cravings while keeping your meals nutritious. Here are ten delightful recipes that are perfect for summertime grilling.

1. Grilled Lemon-Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into squares
  • 1 zucchini, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add chicken cubes to the marinade, cover, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken, bell pepper, and zucchini onto skewers.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Serve hot with a side of fresh salad.

2. Grilled Vegetable Platter with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine balsamic vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the vegetable slices with the balsamic mixture.
  4. Grill the vegetables for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until tender and slightly charred.
  5. Drizzle with additional balsamic glaze before serving.

3. Spiced Grilled Shrimp Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime wedges, and diced tomatoes

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, toss shrimp with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Skewer shrimp to make flipping easier.
  3. Grill shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.
  4. Warm corn tortillas on the grill for a minute.
  5. Assemble tacos by placing grilled shrimp on tortillas and adding desired toppings.

4. Grilled Peach and Arugula Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush peach halves with a little olive oil and place them cut-side down on the grill.
  3. Grill for about 4-5 minutes until grill marks appear and peaches soften.
  4. In a large bowl, toss arugula with olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add grilled peaches, top with feta and walnuts, and serve immediately.

5. Mediterranean Grilled Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 teaspoons dried dill
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, dill, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the mixture over the salmon fillets.
  4. Place salmon skin-side down on the grill and cook for 6-8 minutes. Flip carefully and grill for an additional 2-3 minutes until cooked through.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

6. Grilled Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup salsa

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine quinoa, black beans, corn, cumin, salt, pepper, and salsa.
  3. Stuff the halved bell peppers with the quinoa mixture.
  4. Place stuffed peppers on the grill and close the lid. Grill for about 15-20 minutes until the peppers are tender.
  5. Serve warm with a dollop of Greek yogurt if desired.

7. Grilled Shrimp and Avocado Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss shrimp with olive oil, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are pink and opaque.
  4. In a large bowl, combine grilled shrimp, avocados, tomatoes, and red onion.
  5. Garnish with cilantro and serve immediately.

8. Grilled Lemon-Garlic Cauliflower Steaks

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, sliced into 1-inch thick steaks
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush both sides of the cauliflower steaks with the mixture.
  4. Grill cauliflower for about 5-7 minutes on each side until tender and charred.
  5. Serve warm as a side dish or a main course.

9. Grilled Chicken and Pineapple Kabobs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cubed
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, cubed
  • 1 green bell pepper, cubed
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • Skewers

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine chicken, pineapple, bell peppers, and teriyaki sauce. Let marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  3. Thread chicken, pineapple, and bell peppers onto skewers.
  4. Grill kabobs for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally until chicken is cooked through.
  5. Serve with extra teriyaki sauce for dipping.

10. Grilled Watermelon with Feta and Mint

Ingredients:

  • 1 small watermelon, cut into thick wedges
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place watermelon wedges on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until grill marks appear.
  3. Remove from the grill and sprinkle with feta cheese and fresh mint leaves.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic glaze if desired and serve as a refreshing dessert or appetizer.

Conclusion

Grilling is an excellent way to prepare healthy meals during the summer months. With these ten light options, you can enjoy the flavors of summer without the extra calories. Each recipe is packed with nutrients and vibrant flavors, making them perfect for family gatherings, picnics, or casual weeknight dinners. So fire up that grill and enjoy these healthy grilling recipes today!
