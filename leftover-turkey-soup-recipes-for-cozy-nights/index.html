Leftover Turkey Soup Recipes for Cozy Nights


When the holiday season winds down, many of us find ourselves staring at a fridge full of leftover turkey, wondering how to transform those hearty slices into something new and comforting. One of the best ways to savor turkey beyond its initial feast is by turning it into warm, soothing soup perfect for chilly evenings. Rich, flavorful, and easy to customize, leftover turkey soup recipes turn scraps into supper that hugs you from the inside out.

In this post, we’ll explore multiple inventive and satisfying recipes designed not only to use up that turkey but also to deliver variety for your dinner table. Whether you prefer classic broths, creamy textures, or spice-laden bowls, there’s something here to suit every craving and keep those cozy nights flavorful.

The Basics: Preparing Leftover Turkey for Soup

Before diving into recipes, ensure your turkey is ready to shine in your soup pot. Use mostly white and dark meat scraps, shredded or chopped into bite-sized pieces. Remove any large bones, skin (unless you like extra fat and flavor), and fatty bits that can cause the broth to be greasy or heavy.

If your turkey is dry, don’t worry—cooking it in broth will restore moisture. In fact, soups are fantastic turkey rehabs, as the meat absorbs the surrounding stock’s warmth and spices. Reserve any turkey carcass if you have it, as it can create a rich homemade broth when simmered with aromatics.

Classic Turkey Noodle Soup

The hallmark of leftover turkey soup, this recipe takes inspiration from comforting chicken noodle soup but elevates it with the deep flavor of roasted turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups turkey broth (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup celery, diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1½ cups egg noodles or pasta of choice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until vegetables soften, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Pour in the turkey broth, then add thyme, sage, and the bay leaf.
  3. Bring to a gentle boil and cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing flavors to blend.
  4. Add the turkey and noodles, cooking until noodles are tender—usually 6-8 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Remove the bay leaf before serving.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve piping hot.

This soup embodies everything you’d want on a cold night: warmth, chunks of tender turkey, and a soothing broth full of herbaceous aroma.

Creamy Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

For a creamier texture and more complex grain addition, wild rice is a superb partner. It adds a nutty flavor that complements turkey beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup wild rice, rinsed
  • 6 cups turkey broth
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • ½ cup carrots, diced
  • 3 cups milk or half-and-half
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme sprigs for garnish (optional)

Method:

  1. In a medium pot, cook wild rice in turkey broth until tender, about 45 minutes.
  2. While rice cooks, melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, celery, carrots, mushrooms, and garlic. Cook until soft, about 7-10 minutes.
  3. Stir in flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to form a roux, stirring constantly.
  4. Gradually whisk in the milk or half-and-half, ensuring no lumps form.
  5. When the rice is ready, add it and remaining broth to the vegetable mixture.
  6. Stir in turkey pieces and thyme, cooking through but avoiding boiling after adding dairy.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

This hearty soup is rich in both nutrition and flavor, perfect for those who crave something creamy but not overly heavy.

Spiced Turkey and Sweet Potato Soup

A twist on tradition, this version uses the sweetness of sweet potatoes and a complex spice profile to create a soup that’s both comforting and exciting.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups turkey broth
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 2 cups sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cilantro for garnish

Method:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add spices – cumin, paprika, cayenne, coriander – and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  3. Pour in turkey broth, diced tomatoes, and sweet potatoes. Bring to a simmer and cook until potatoes soften, about 15 minutes.
  4. Add turkey meat and heat through.
  5. Stir in lime juice for brightness, then season with salt and pepper.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve warm.

This soup balances smoky warmth with a touch of sweet and tang, creating complexity in each spoonful.

Hearty Turkey and Bean Soup

Beans add protein, fiber, and a creamy texture to turkey soup, perfect for a nutritious, filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups turkey broth
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) white beans (cannellini or navy), drained and rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese for topping (optional)

Method:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion, carrots, celery, and garlic until tender.
  2. Add broth, herbs, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer 15 minutes to meld flavors.
  4. Add turkey and beans, warming everything through.
  5. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and crusty bread.

This soup is simple but satisfying, excellent on nights when you crave hearty, rustic flavors.

Tips for Making the Most of Leftover Turkey in Soups

  • Broth matters: Homemade broth from turkey bones imparts incredible depth. If you don’t have time, a good-quality store broth works just fine.
  • Balance moisture: Turkey tastes best when broth or soup base isn’t too oily or thick. Skim fat as needed.
  • Use herbs generously: Thyme, sage, rosemary, and parsley enhance the turkey flavor without overpowering.
  • Add variety: Don’t hesitate to mix in vegetables like kale, spinach, or peas for extra color and nutrients.
  • Freeze leftovers: Turkey soups freeze beautifully. Store them in airtight containers and thaw gently when ready to enjoy.

Wrapping it Up

Turning leftover turkey into soup is more than just a practical way to reduce waste—it’s an opportunity to craft delicious, soul-warming meals that transform your evenings. Whether you’re reaching for classic comfort with noodles, indulging in creamy wild rice, or experimenting with spiced sweet potato, each recipe proves that turkey can be endlessly versatile.

Next time that roasted bird leaves you with plenty of leftovers, remember these recipes to transform what’s left into culinary delight that invites comfort, warmth, and satisfaction in every bowl.

Happy cooking and cozy nights ahead!
