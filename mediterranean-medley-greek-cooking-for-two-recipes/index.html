Mediterranean Medley: Greek Cooking for Two Recipes


Are you and your partner looking to spice up your dinner routine? Look no further than the vibrant and flavorful world of Greek cuisine. With its rich history and diverse ingredients, Greek cooking offers a medley of mouthwatering dishes that are perfect for a memorable dinner for two. In this blog post, we will explore three easy and delicious Greek recipes that will transport you to the sunny shores of the Mediterranean.

1. Spanakopita (Spinach and Feta Filo Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 8 sheets filo pastry
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the spinach, feta cheese, dill, eggs, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

  2. Brush a baking dish with olive oil. Layer four sheets of filo pastry, brushing each sheet with olive oil before adding the next one. Spoon the spinach and feta mixture over the filo pastry.

  3. Layer the remaining four sheets of filo pastry on top of the spinach mixture, brushing each sheet with olive oil. Fold the edges of the pastry inward to seal the pie.

  4. Bake the spanakopita in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy. Allow it to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Enjoy the crunchy filo pastry, creamy feta cheese, and the vibrant flavors of the spinach and dill in this classic Greek dish.

2. Souvlaki (Grilled Marinated Meat Skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken or pork, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken or pork pieces to the marinade, making sure each piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, allowing the flavors to meld.

  2. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Thread the marinated meat onto the soaked wooden skewers, leaving a small gap between each piece.

  3. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and has a nice char on the outside.

Serve the souvlaki with a side of Greek salad, tzatziki sauce, and warm pita bread for a complete and satisfying meal.

3. Baklava (Sweet Nut Pastry)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1 cup almonds, chopped
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 12 sheets filo pastry
  • Syrup:
    • 1/2 cup honey
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, combine the chopped walnuts, almonds, sugar, and ground cinnamon. Mix well to evenly distribute the flavors.

  2. Brush a baking dish with melted butter. Layer six sheets of filo pastry in the dish, brushing each sheet with melted butter before adding the next one. Sprinkle a generous amount of the nut mixture over the filo pastry.

  3. Layer the remaining six sheets of filo pastry on top of the nut mixture, brushing each sheet with melted butter. Using a sharp knife, score the top layer of filo pastry into diamond or square shapes.

  4. Bake the baklava in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crisp.

  5. While the baklava is baking, prepare the syrup. In a small saucepan, combine the honey, water, sugar, lemon juice, and cinnamon stick. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes. Remove the cinnamon stick and set the syrup aside to cool slightly.

  6. Once the baklava is out of the oven, pour the warm syrup over the pastry. Allow the baklava to cool completely before serving, allowing the syrup to soak into the layers.

Indulge in the sweet, nutty goodness of this traditional Greek dessert, and savor the delicate layers of filo pastry and fragrant honey syrup.

Conclusion

Greek cuisine is a treasure trove of vibrant flavors and simple yet satisfying dishes. These three Greek recipes - spanakopita, souvlaki, and baklava - are just a taste of the culinary delights that await you. So gather your loved one, put on some Greek music, and embark on a Mediterranean culinary journey right in your own kitchen. Opa!
