The Best Greek Roasted Lamb with Garlic and Herbs


When it comes to Mediterranean cuisine, few dishes stand out as gloriously as Greek roasted lamb with garlic and herbs. This dish embodies the essence of Greek culinary tradition, showcasing vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients that have been celebrated for centuries. Whether you’re preparing for a festive gathering or a cozy family dinner, this recipe promises to elevate your dining experience to new heights.

The Allure of Greek Roasted Lamb

Lamb is a staple in Greek cooking, revered for its rich flavor and tender texture. Roasting lamb with garlic and herbs not only enhances its natural taste but also infuses it with aromatic notes that create an unforgettable culinary experience. The combination of garlic, olive oil, and fresh herbs, such as rosemary and thyme, transforms the meat into a succulent masterpiece that will leave your guests raving.

Ingredients

To prepare the best Greek roasted lamb, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Lamb:

  • 4-5 pounds of leg of lamb, bone-in
  • 6-8 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh oregano, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For the Marinade:

  • 1 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme, or parsley)
  • Lemon wedges

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Marinate the Lamb

The key to achieving a flavorful roast lies in the marinade. Begin by preparing the marinade in a large bowl. Combine the Greek yogurt, ground cumin, paprika, ground coriander, and cinnamon. Mix thoroughly until you have a smooth paste.

Next, place the leg of lamb in a large ziplock bag or a non-reactive dish. Pour the marinade over the lamb, ensuring it is well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This allows the flavors to penetrate the meat deeply.

Step 2: Prepare the Herb and Garlic Mixture

While the lamb marinates, prepare the garlic and herb mixture. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, olive oil, chopped rosemary, thyme, oregano, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well until you have a fragrant paste.

Step 3: Preheat the Oven

When you’re ready to roast the lamb, preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). This low and slow cooking method ensures the meat becomes tender and juicy.

Step 4: Combine Marinades and Prepare for Roasting

Remove the lamb from the marinade and gently scrape off any excess yogurt. Pat the meat dry with paper towels, as this will help achieve a crispy exterior. Place the lamb in a roasting pan.

Using your hands, rub the garlic and herb mixture all over the lamb, making sure to get into the crevices. This step is essential for maximizing flavor. If desired, you can also use a sharp knife to make small incisions in the meat and insert slivers of garlic for an extra punch.

Step 5: Roast the Lamb

Cover the roasting pan with aluminum foil to keep the moisture in. Roast the lamb in the preheated oven for about 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare. If you prefer it well-done, cook it to 160°F (71°C).

During the last 30 minutes of roasting, remove the foil to allow the lamb to brown beautifully. Baste the meat with the pan juices every 15 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

Step 6: Rest and Serve

Once done, remove the lamb from the oven and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring each slice is tender and juicy.

After resting, carve the lamb into thick slices and arrange them on a serving platter. Drizzle with any remaining pan juices, and garnish with fresh herbs and lemon wedges.

Side Dishes to Complement Your Lamb

A Greek feast is never complete without a selection of delightful side dishes. Here are some ideas to serve alongside your roasted lamb:

Greek Salad

A fresh Greek salad featuring ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, Kalamata olives, and feta cheese drizzled with olive oil and lemon juice is a refreshing complement to the rich flavors of the lamb.

Roasted Potatoes

Crispy roasted potatoes seasoned with garlic and herbs make the perfect side. Toss them with olive oil, salt, and oregano before roasting to enhance their flavor.

Tzatziki Sauce

This cool and creamy yogurt sauce with cucumber and garlic is a classic Greek accompaniment that pairs beautifully with the roasted lamb.

Grilled Vegetables

Seasonal vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, and eggplant drizzled with olive oil and grilled to perfection add a colorful and healthy touch to the meal.

Final Thoughts

Greek roasted lamb with garlic and herbs is not just a dish; it’s an experience that brings friends and family together around the table. The combination of tender, flavorful meat with aromatic herbs and spices creates a symphony of taste that is truly unforgettable.

As you prepare this dish, take your time to embrace the process—marinating, seasoning, and roasting. The joy of cooking is in the journey, and sharing this culinary creation with loved ones will make every effort worthwhile.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on this delicious journey into Greek cuisine. Your taste buds will thank you!
