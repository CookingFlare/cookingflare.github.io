Baking for Beginners: Simple Recipes to Get You Started

3 min · 615 words

Are you ready to embark on a delicious journey into the world of baking? Whether you’re a complete novice or have dabbled in a bit of baking before, this blog post is here to guide you with some simple and foolproof recipes that will help you get started. From fluffy cakes to golden cookies, these recipes will have you feeling like a master baker in no time!

1. Classic Vanilla Cupcakes

Let’s begin with a timeless favorite – vanilla cupcakes! These light, fluffy, and oh-so-delicious treats are perfect for any occasion. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake pan with paper liners.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter equally among the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely before frosting.

2. Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies

Next up, let’s indulge in some irresistible chewy chocolate chip cookies. These cookies are a crowd-pleaser and never fail to bring a smile to anyone’s face. Gather these ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each cookie for spreading.
  8. Bake for 9-11 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Flaky Apple Pie

No baking journey is complete without a classic apple pie. This recipe will have you savoring the sweet aroma of warm apples and cinnamon in no time. Here are the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts (or homemade if you’re feeling adventurous)
  • 6 cups peeled, cored, and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Golden Delicious work well)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, salt, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  3. Line a pie dish with one of the pie crusts.
  4. Spoon the apple mixture into the pie crust, making sure it is evenly distributed.
  5. Dot the top of the apple mixture with the small pieces of butter.
  6. Place the second pie crust over the filling and crimp the edges to seal. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

Baking can be an incredibly rewarding and satisfying hobby, but it can also feel intimidating for those just starting out. The idea of measuring precise ingredients, following intricate instructions, and waiting patiently for your creation to rise and bake to perfection can be enough to make even the most enthusiastic home cook hesitate.

However, with the right mindset and a few simple recipes under your belt, anyone can become a confident baker. In this blog post, we’ll explore some easy-to-follow recipes that will have you whipping up delectable baked goods in no time.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Let’s start with a timeless classic - the humble chocolate chip cookie. These buttery, gooey treats are the perfect gateway into the world of baking. The best part? They’re incredibly easy to make, requiring just a handful of simple ingredients.

To begin, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a medium-sized bowl, cream together 1 cup of softened unsalted butter and 3/4 cup of granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in 1 large egg and 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract.

In a separate bowl, whisk together 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Fold in 1 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips.

Scoop the dough by the tablespoonful onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are still slightly soft. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes

Breakfast is the most important meal of the day, and what better way to start your morning than with a stack of fluffy, golden-brown pancakes? This easy-to-follow recipe will have you whipping up a batch of homemade pancakes in no time.

In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.

In a separate bowl, combine 1 1/2 cups of buttermilk, 2 large eggs, and 3 tablespoons of melted unsalted butter. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, being careful not to overmix.

Heat a large non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with a small amount of butter or cooking spray. Scoop the batter by 1/4 cup portions onto the hot surface, cooking for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.

Serve the pancakes warm, with your favorite toppings such as maple syrup, fresh fruit, or a dusting of powdered sugar. These fluffy buttermilk pancakes are sure to become a weekend breakfast staple in your household.

Moist and Flavorful Banana Bread

Banana bread is the perfect way to use up those overripe bananas sitting on your kitchen counter. This moist and flavorful recipe is a great addition to your baking repertoire, and it’s surprisingly simple to make.

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan. In a large bowl, mash 3 ripe bananas until smooth. Add 1/2 cup of unsalted butter (melted and slightly cooled), 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 2 large eggs, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Whisk the ingredients together until well combined.

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 3/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, being careful not to overmix.

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 55-65 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Slice and serve the banana bread as is, or try it with a smear of creamy peanut butter or a drizzle of honey. This moist and flavorful loaf is the perfect accompaniment to your morning coffee or afternoon tea.

Buttery Homemade Biscuits

No baking repertoire is complete without a reliable biscuit recipe. These tender, flaky biscuits are the perfect side for hearty stews, sausage gravy, or simply enjoyed on their own with a pat of butter.

Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.

Cut in 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in 3/4 cup of cold buttermilk, just until the dough comes together.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Using a rolling pin, roll the dough out to a thickness of about 1/2 inch. Use a biscuit cutter or a sharp knife to cut out the biscuits, being sure to cut straight down without twisting.

Arrange the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet, making sure they are not touching. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown. Serve the biscuits warm, with your choice of toppings or accompaniments.

Soft and Chewy Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

For those who prefer a heartier, more wholesome cookie, these soft and chewy oatmeal raisin cookies are the perfect choice. The combination of rolled oats, plump raisins, and warm spices creates a delightful treat that’s sure to satisfy.

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of baking soda, 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.

In a large bowl, cream together 1/2 cup of unsalted butter (softened) and 3/4 cup of packed brown sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in 1 large egg and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Fold in 1 1/2 cups of rolled oats and 1 cup of raisins.

Scoop the dough by the tablespoonful onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are still slightly soft. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These soft and chewy oatmeal raisin cookies are the perfect snack or dessert, and they’re sure to become a new favorite in your baking repertoire.

Conclusion

Baking can be an incredibly rewarding and satisfying hobby, and with these simple recipes, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a confident baker. From classic chocolate chip cookies to fluffy buttermilk pancakes, these easy-to-follow recipes will have you whipping up delectable baked goods in no time.

Remember, the key to successful baking is to embrace the process, measure your ingredients carefully, and have fun experimenting with different flavors and techniques. So, preheat your oven, gather your ingredients, and let’s get baking!
