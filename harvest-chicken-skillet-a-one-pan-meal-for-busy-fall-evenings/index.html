Harvest Chicken Skillet: A One-Pan Meal for Busy Fall Evenings


As the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and gold, and the air becomes crisp with the arrival of fall, our cravings for warm, comforting meals grow stronger. There’s something undeniably cozy about the autumn season, and what better way to embrace it than with a hearty, one-pan meal that captures the essence of harvest flavors? This Harvest Chicken Skillet is a perfect blend of savory and sweet, bringing together the best of fall produce and tender chicken in a delightful medley of flavors. Best of all, it’s a convenient dish that requires only one pan, making it ideal for those busy fall evenings when time is of the essence.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cups butternut squash, cubed
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1 apple, cored and sliced
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by seasoning the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breasts and sear them for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Once done, transfer the chicken to a plate and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and diced onion. Sauté them for a few minutes until they turn fragrant and translucent.

  4. Add the cubed butternut squash, halved Brussels sprouts, and sliced apple to the skillet. Cook the vegetables and apple for about 5-7 minutes, or until they start to soften and caramelize slightly.

  5. Stir in the dried cranberries, chicken broth, maple syrup, and dried thyme. Allow the mixture to simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Return the seared chicken breasts to the skillet, nestling them among the vegetables and fruits. Let the dish simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the flavors have melded beautifully.

  7. Once the chicken is cooked through, and the vegetables are tender, garnish the skillet with fresh parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

  8. Serve the Harvest Chicken Skillet directly from the pan, allowing everyone to dig in and savor the delightful combination of flavors.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

The Harvest Chicken Skillet is not just a meal; it’s an experience that embodies the warmth and richness of the fall season. The tender chicken, caramelized vegetables, sweet apple slices, and burst of tangy cranberries create a symphony of flavors that is both comforting and exciting. The subtle hint of maple syrup adds a touch of sweetness, balancing out the savory elements of the dish. This one-pan wonder is not only a delight for the senses but also a practical choice for busy evenings when you want a wholesome meal without the hassle of multiple pots and pans.

Tips for Customization

Feel free to customize the Harvest Chicken Skillet to suit your taste preferences. You can substitute the butternut squash with sweet potatoes or add a handful of pecans for a delightful crunch. For a hint of warmth, sprinkle some cinnamon over the dish before serving. Additionally, if you prefer a saucier consistency, you can increase the amount of chicken broth and maple syrup to create a luscious glaze that coats the chicken and vegetables.
