Exploring the Classic Paper Plane: A Balanced Whiskey Cocktail


If you’re a fan of whiskey-based cocktails, then the Paper Plane is a must-try. This classic cocktail, created by bartender Sam Ross in 2007, has gained popularity for its balanced and refreshing taste. It’s a perfect blend of bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, and fresh lemon juice, creating a symphony of flavors that dance on the palate. In this blog post, we’ll take a deep dive into the world of the Paper Plane cocktail, exploring its history, ingredients, and the step-by-step process of crafting this delightful libation.

The History of the Paper Plane Cocktail

The Paper Plane cocktail has a relatively short but colorful history. It was invented by Sam Ross, a renowned bartender who has made significant contributions to the cocktail world. The cocktail first appeared on the menu at The Violet Hour in Chicago, and it quickly gained a cult following among cocktail enthusiasts. The inspiration for the name “Paper Plane” is said to be derived from the M.I.A. song “Paper Planes,” which was playing in the background as Sam Ross experimented with the cocktail’s ingredients.

Ingredients

To create the perfect Paper Plane cocktail, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 3/4 oz Bourbon
  • 3/4 oz Aperol
  • 3/4 oz Amaro Nonino
  • 3/4 oz Fresh Lemon Juice

Crafting the Perfect Paper Plane

Now that we’ve covered the history and ingredients, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making a Paper Plane cocktail.

  1. Gather Your Tools: Before you begin, ensure you have a cocktail shaker, a jigger, a fine strainer, and a coupe glass.

  2. Measure the Ingredients: Using a jigger, measure out 3/4 oz of bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, and fresh lemon juice.

  3. Combine the Ingredients: In a cocktail shaker filled with ice, pour the measured ingredients. Shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds to chill the mixture and properly dilute the drink.

  4. Strain and Serve: Using a fine strainer, pour the cocktail into a chilled coupe glass. The fine strainer will ensure that no ice chips or citrus pulp make their way into the final drink.

  5. Garnish and Enjoy: For a finishing touch, you can garnish the cocktail with a lemon twist. Now, savor the delightful balance of flavors in your expertly crafted Paper Plane cocktail.

The Flavor Profile

The Paper Plane cocktail offers a harmonious blend of sweet, bitter, and citrusy flavors. The bourbon provides a robust and oaky base, while the Aperol adds a subtle bitterness. The Amaro Nonino brings a touch of sweetness and complexity, and the fresh lemon juice provides a bright, citrusy zing. When combined, these ingredients create a cocktail that is both refreshing and full of character.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Paper Plane cocktail is a true gem in the world of whiskey-based cocktails. Its balanced flavor profile and intriguing history make it a favorite among cocktail aficionados. Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or a home bartender looking to expand your repertoire, the Paper Plane is a cocktail that deserves a special place in your recipe book. So, gather your ingredients, shake up this delightful libation, and raise a glass to the timeless art of cocktail crafting. Cheers!
