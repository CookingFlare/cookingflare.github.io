Indulgent Pecan Pie Bars: A Thanksgiving Dessert Staple


Thanksgiving is a time for indulgence, and what better way to satisfy your sweet tooth than with a delicious batch of pecan pie bars? These delectable treats are a twist on the classic pecan pie, offering all the rich flavors and gooey goodness in a convenient and portable form. Whether you’re hosting a Thanksgiving feast or simply looking for a delightful dessert to enjoy with your loved ones, these indulgent pecan pie bars are sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups light corn syrup
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups pecans, roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and powdered sugar. Add the cold and cubed unsalted butter, and using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Press the mixture into the prepared baking dish, creating an even layer. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, or until lightly golden.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, light corn syrup, granulated sugar, melted unsalted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  5. Gently fold in the chopped pecans, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

  6. Pour the pecan filling over the partially baked crust, spreading it out evenly.

  7. Return the baking dish to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown. The center should still have a slight jiggle when gently shaken.

  8. Remove the pecan pie bars from the oven and allow them to cool completely in the baking dish.

  9. Once cooled, carefully lift the parchment paper to remove the bars from the dish. Place them on a cutting board and slice into squares or rectangles, depending on your preference.

  10. Serve the indulgent pecan pie bars at room temperature, or for an extra special treat, warm them slightly and top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on traditional pecan pie bars, try adding a sprinkle of sea salt to the top of the bars before baking. The combination of sweet and salty flavors is simply irresistible.

  • If you prefer a more decadent dessert, drizzle the pecan pie bars with melted chocolate or caramel sauce before serving.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of nuts if you’re not a fan of pecans. Walnuts or almonds would work well in this recipe too.

  • These pecan pie bars can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They also freeze well, so you can enjoy them even after Thanksgiving is over.

  • Serve these indulgent pecan pie bars alongside a cup of hot coffee or tea for a cozy and comforting dessert experience.

Conclusion

Indulgent pecan pie bars are a Thanksgiving dessert staple that will delight your taste buds and impress your guests. With their buttery crust, gooey filling, and crunchy pecans, these bars offer all the flavors of a classic pecan pie in a convenient and bite-sized form. Whether you’re celebrating Thanksgiving or simply craving a sweet treat, give this recipe a try and savor the indulgence.
