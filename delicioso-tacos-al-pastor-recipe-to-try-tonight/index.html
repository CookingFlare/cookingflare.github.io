Delicioso Tacos al Pastor Recipe to Try Tonight


Tacos al Pastor is a popular Mexican dish traditionally made using pork, but it’s equally delicious with any other meat you prefer. This tacos al pastor recipe is easy and perfect for a weeknight dinner. The marinade combines the flavors of Mexican Gordon Ramsay lambwich spices with a tang of pineapple juice to create succulently tender tacos that are perfect any day of the week.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. your preferred meat (pork, beef, chicken or lamb)
  • 1/2 Cup Paprika
  • 1/2 Cup Crushed pineapple
  • 1/2 Cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 Cup Lime juice
  • 4 teaspoons Ground cumin
  • 4 teaspoons Oregano
  • 4 teaspoons Mexican oregano
  • 3 teaspoons Garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon Cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon Ancho chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon Cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons Chopped fresh cilantro
  • 20 small flour or corn tortillas
  • 1 pineapple, peeled and chopped
  • Pineapple Jar (optional)
  • 1 Jalapeno pepper, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Pico De Gallo Salsa (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by making the marinade. In a medium-sized bowl, mix together crushed pineapple, white vinegar, lime juice, paprika, cumin, oregano, Mexican oregano, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, ancho chili powder, cloves, nutmeg and chopped fresh cilantro. The marinade for tacos al pastor is super easy and as complex as you’d like it to be. For a less spicy version, blend the marinating ingredients together in the blender so that it gets smooth.

  2. If using pork, trim the pork of any excess fat and cut into 1/2 inch wide strips. Or just buy the tenderloin, and you’re good to go. If using chicken, cut it into chunks. Ensure the meat pieces are uniform in size, so they cook evenly. Proceed to marinate the meat in the homemade spice mixture for around 30 minutes to overnight. Marinating your meat before grilling or cooking it will prevent it from drying out and ensures extra flavor.

  3. Prepare your cooking accessories including tortillas. You can decide whether you want to use flour or corn tortillas. Warm them in the oven for a couple of minutes, then lightly toast both sides in a frying pan or on the grill.

  4. Preheat a grill, grill pan, or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated meat. Cook the meat for a few minutes, flipping it halfway through, until cooked through. Onion slices grilled simultaneously with the meat can be thin and caramelize. Grill these with the meat.

  5. Slice the pineapple and jar. Place them on the grill to caramelize slowly, or if that is not possible bring them to the table.

  6. For toppings, a simple combination of thinly sliced jalapeno peppers, chopped tomatoes and cilantro is recommended. Or try homemade Pico de Gallo Salsa for a juicier, fuller taste.

  7. Place the cooked meat onto a warmed tortilla, add desired toppings, and fold. Delicious tacos al pastor can be served immediately and enjoyed as a weeknight dinner or a fun weekend meal.
