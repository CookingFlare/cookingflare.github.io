Comforting Chicken and Mushroom Pot Pie: A Family Favorite


There’s something truly special about a warm and comforting pot pie. The flaky crust, the savory filling, and the delicious aroma that fills the kitchen as it bakes - it’s the epitome of comfort food. And when it comes to pot pies, one classic combination never fails to impress: chicken and mushroom. In this recipe, we’ll be diving into the secrets of creating a mouthwatering Chicken and Mushroom Pot Pie that will become a family favorite in no time.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or cubed
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they become tender and golden brown.

  4. Sprinkle the flour over the mixture in the skillet and stir well to combine. Cook for about a minute to cook off the raw flour taste.

  5. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly to prevent any lumps from forming. Continue to cook and stir until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle simmer.

  6. Add the shredded or cubed chicken to the skillet, along with the dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together until well combined.

  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and set it aside to cool slightly.

  8. While the filling is cooling, unroll one of the pie crusts and gently press it into the bottom of a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess crust hanging over the edges.

  9. Pour the chicken and mushroom filling into the pie dish, spreading it evenly.

  10. Unroll the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Press the edges of the crust together with the bottom crust to seal them. Use a sharp knife to cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  11. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet (to catch any potential drips) and transfer it to the preheated oven.

  12. Bake for about 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  13. Once the pot pie is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This comforting Chicken and Mushroom Pot Pie pairs perfectly with a side of buttery mashed potatoes or a fresh green salad. It’s a hearty and satisfying meal that will warm both your body and your soul.

Make It Your Own

Feel free to get creative with this recipe and make it your own. You can add additional vegetables like carrots, peas, or corn for extra color and flavor. You can also experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste preferences.

Conclusion

There’s no denying the appeal of a classic Chicken and Mushroom Pot Pie. Its rich and savory flavors, combined with the comforting texture of the flaky crust, make it a dish that will always be welcomed at the family table. So why not give this recipe a try? It’s sure to become a family favorite in no time. Enjoy!
