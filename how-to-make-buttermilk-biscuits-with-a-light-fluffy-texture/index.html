How to Make Buttermilk Biscuits with a Light Fluffy Texture


When it comes to comfort food, few things rival the appeal of a batch of warm, freshly baked buttermilk biscuits. These biscuits, with their light, flaky layers and subtly tangy flavor, can be the perfect addition to any meal—whether it’s a hearty breakfast, a cozy dinner, or just as a snack to enjoy with a cup of tea.

However, making the perfect buttermilk biscuit with that delicate, light, and fluffy texture can feel like a bit of a challenge. Don’t worry though; with a few key tips and tricks, you’ll be able to create the biscuits of your dreams right at home.

In this post, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of making buttermilk biscuits, ensuring they come out soft, fluffy, and with a golden-brown, buttery finish.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we jump into the method, let’s gather everything you’ll need to make the perfect buttermilk biscuits:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour: The flour is the base of your biscuit dough and gives it structure.
  • 2 tsp baking powder: This helps your biscuits rise and gives them that fluffy, light texture.
  • 1 tsp baking soda: A little extra leavening is key to achieving a biscuit that’s soft and airy.
  • 1/2 tsp salt: Essential to balance the flavors and enhance the richness of the butter.
  • 6 tbsp cold unsalted butter: Butter is the star of the show here. It adds flavor and flakiness to the biscuit.
  • 3/4 cup cold buttermilk: Buttermilk provides the tangy flavor and reacts with the baking soda for leavening. Make sure it’s cold to ensure the right texture.
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional): A small amount of sugar can help with browning and give a subtle sweetness to your biscuits.
  • Additional flour for dusting: You’ll need some flour to help with handling the dough.

Tools You’ll Need

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Pastry cutter or fork
  • Rolling pin (optional)
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper or silicone baking mat
  • Knife or biscuit cutter
  • Oven preheated to 425°F (220°C)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Buttermilk Biscuits

1. Prepare Your Work Surface and Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C). The high temperature ensures that your biscuits bake quickly and develop that perfectly golden exterior while remaining soft and airy on the inside.

Next, prepare your work surface. Lightly dust it with flour to prevent the dough from sticking. Also, get your baking sheet ready by lining it with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. This helps the biscuits bake evenly and ensures they won’t stick to the pan.

2. Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and sugar (if using). Whisk these dry ingredients together until they are well combined. This will ensure that your biscuits rise evenly and have a consistent texture throughout.

3. Cut in the Cold Butter

The next step is key to getting that light, flaky texture we all love in a good biscuit: cutting in the butter. For this step, your butter needs to be cold—straight from the fridge. Cut it into small cubes and add it to your flour mixture.

Use a pastry cutter, a fork, or your fingertips to work the butter into the dry ingredients. The goal is to break the butter into small pieces and distribute it throughout the flour. You should end up with a crumbly mixture with small pea-sized bits of butter. This process is what creates those flaky layers in the biscuit as the butter melts during baking.

4. Add the Buttermilk

Once the butter is properly incorporated, it’s time to add the buttermilk. Pour the cold buttermilk into the bowl with your dry ingredients and gently stir using a spoon or spatula. Be careful not to overwork the dough—stir just until the mixture begins to come together. If the dough looks a bit shaggy or crumbly, that’s okay. You don’t want a smooth dough, as overworking it can result in tough biscuits.

5. Knead the Dough Lightly

Once the dough has mostly come together, turn it out onto your lightly floured work surface. With lightly floured hands, gently knead the dough for about 5-6 turns, just until it holds together. Avoid kneading too much, as this can develop the gluten and lead to dense biscuits. The goal is to keep the dough as light and airy as possible.

6. Roll Out the Dough

Using a rolling pin, gently roll out the dough to about 1-inch thickness. If you don’t have a rolling pin, you can press the dough out with your hands. Don’t roll it out too thinly—this is key to ensuring your biscuits are thick, light, and fluffy.

7. Cut Out the Biscuits

Now it’s time to cut out your biscuits! Use a sharp biscuit cutter or a drinking glass to cut the dough into rounds. Press down firmly, but don’t twist the cutter, as twisting can cause the biscuits to rise unevenly. Gather any scraps of dough, gently press them together, and continue cutting until all the dough is used up.

8. Arrange on the Baking Sheet

Place the cut biscuits on the prepared baking sheet. For biscuits with soft, tender sides, arrange them so they are close together, touching each other. If you prefer a little more crispness around the edges, space them out slightly on the baking sheet.

9. Bake the Biscuits

Pop the biscuits into the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top. Keep a close eye on them in the final few minutes to ensure they don’t over-brown.

10. Serve and Enjoy!

Once the biscuits are golden brown, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly on a wire rack. These biscuits are best enjoyed warm, straight from the oven. You can serve them as they are, or with a pat of butter, a drizzle of honey, or a dollop of jam for added flavor.

Tips for Making the Best Buttermilk Biscuits

  • Use cold ingredients: The butter and buttermilk should be cold. Cold butter helps to create that flaky texture, while cold buttermilk contributes to the lightness of the dough.
  • Don’t overwork the dough: Overworking the dough can make the biscuits tough. Mix and knead the dough gently, and stop as soon as it comes together.
  • Use a light hand when rolling out the dough: Rolling the dough too thin can cause it to lose its rise. Aim for about 1-inch thickness.
  • Bake immediately: Once your biscuits are cut, get them into the oven as quickly as possible. The longer the dough sits, the more it can lose its rise.
  • Avoid twisting the cutter: Twisting the cutter while cutting the biscuits can prevent them from rising properly. Press straight down for the best results.

Variations You Can Try

Once you’ve mastered the classic buttermilk biscuit recipe, you can experiment with different variations:

  • Cheddar Biscuits: Add shredded cheddar cheese to the dry ingredients for a savory twist. You can even sprinkle a little extra cheese on top before baking.
  • Garlic Biscuits: Add minced garlic or garlic powder to the dough for a flavorful biscuit. A little garlic butter on top after baking makes it even better!
  • Herb Biscuits: Incorporate fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or parsley into your dough for a fragrant, herby version.

Storing and Reheating Biscuits

Buttermilk biscuits are best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, you can store them at room temperature for up to 2 days. To reheat, wrap them in foil and warm them in a 350°F oven for about 10 minutes, or until heated through.

Final Thoughts

Making perfect buttermilk biscuits at home is a rewarding experience that brings the comforting taste of homemade baking into your kitchen. By using cold butter, cold buttermilk, and handling the dough gently, you’ll be able to create biscuits with a light, fluffy texture every time. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to enjoy a batch of warm, flaky biscuits that will make everyone at the table smile.
