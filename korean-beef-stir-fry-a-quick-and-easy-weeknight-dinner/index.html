Korean Beef Stir-Fry: A Quick and Easy Weeknight Dinner


When it comes to whipping up a delicious and satisfying meal on a busy weeknight, Korean Beef Stir-Fry is a go-to dish that never disappoints. Bursting with savory flavors, tender beef, and crisp vegetables, this stir-fry is a perfect balance of sweet, salty, and umami goodness. Plus, it’s quick and easy to make, making it an ideal choice for those hectic evenings when you need a flavorful meal without the fuss.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of thinly sliced beef (such as sirloin or ribeye)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade and let it sit for at least 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Create the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to create a slurry. This will thicken the sauce later on.

  3. Cook the Beef: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the marinated beef, reserving the marinade, and cook until the beef is browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.

  4. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same skillet, heat the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced bell peppers and snap peas, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.

  5. Combine and Finish: Return the cooked beef to the skillet with the vegetables. Pour in the reserved marinade and the cornstarch slurry. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the beef and vegetables. This should take about 2 minutes.

  6. Serve: Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the sliced green onions. Serve the Korean Beef Stir-Fry over steamed white rice and garnish with toasted sesame seeds for an extra burst of flavor and texture.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra kick of spice, add a teaspoon of Korean chili flakes (gochugaru) to the marinade.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in the stir-fry based on your preferences. Broccoli, carrots, or mushrooms would all be great additions.
  • Make sure your skillet or wok is very hot before adding the beef to ensure it gets a nice sear.

The Perfect Weeknight Meal

Korean Beef Stir-Fry is a fantastic recipe to have in your arsenal for those nights when time is of the essence but flavor is still a priority. The combination of tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and a rich, savory sauce makes for a meal that’s sure to please everyone at the table. Plus, the simplicity and speed of this dish make it a winner for busy weeknights. So, the next time you’re in need of a quick and easy dinner solution, give this Korean Beef Stir-Fry recipe a try and savor every delicious bite.
