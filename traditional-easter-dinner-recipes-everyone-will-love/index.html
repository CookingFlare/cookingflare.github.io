Traditional Easter Dinner Recipes Everyone Will Love


Easter is one of those holidays that brings family and friends together around a table full of delicious food. From hearty main courses to indulgent desserts, the traditional Easter dinner is often filled with flavors that bring comfort and joy. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or keeping it intimate, having a variety of mouthwatering dishes to choose from ensures that everyone’s tastes are satisfied. In this post, we will explore traditional Easter dinner recipes that will impress your guests and make your celebration memorable.

1. Classic Roast Lamb with Garlic and Rosemary

When it comes to traditional Easter dinners, roast lamb is often the centerpiece of the meal. Known for its tender texture and rich flavor, lamb pairs beautifully with a variety of herbs and spices. The classic combination of garlic and rosemary enhances the natural flavors of the meat, making it a beloved choice for Easter dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 leg of lamb (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped rosemary, olive oil, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the garlic and rosemary mixture all over the leg of lamb, making sure to coat it evenly.
  4. Place the lamb in a roasting pan and roast for 1.5-2 hours, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness (145°F for medium-rare, 160°F for medium).
  5. Once done, remove the lamb from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender roast.

Pro Tip:

For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a few sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme to the roasting pan for added aroma while the lamb cooks.

2. Glazed Ham with Brown Sugar and Pineapple

Ham is another traditional Easter dish that often makes an appearance at the dinner table. The sweet and savory combination of brown sugar, pineapple, and ham creates a mouthwatering glaze that complements the salty richness of the meat. It’s a dish that never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 spiral-sliced ham (about 8 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • Pineapple rings (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine brown sugar, Dijon mustard, pineapple juice, apple cider vinegar, and honey. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the glaze thickens (about 10-15 minutes).
  3. Place the ham in a roasting pan, and brush with a generous amount of the glaze.
  4. Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake for about 1.5 hours, basting with more glaze every 30 minutes.
  5. For the last 20 minutes of cooking, uncover the ham and continue baking until the glaze caramelizes and the ham reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C).
  6. Garnish with pineapple rings if desired and serve.

Pro Tip:

To create a more visually appealing dish, you can stud the ham with cloves and add maraschino cherries for a festive touch.

3. Scalloped Potatoes

No Easter dinner is complete without a creamy side dish to complement the main course. Scalloped potatoes are a classic choice, with their layers of tender potatoes and rich, cheesy sauce. This dish is sure to be a crowd-pleaser at your celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to create a roux.
  3. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Continue to cook and whisk until the sauce thickens (about 5-7 minutes).
  4. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Layer the sliced potatoes in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Pour the cheese sauce over the potatoes, making sure they are evenly coated.
  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 45 minutes. After 45 minutes, remove the foil and continue baking for another 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown.
  7. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Pro Tip:

For added flavor, you can sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top before baking for a deliciously crispy crust.

4. Roasted Vegetables

A colorful assortment of roasted vegetables makes the perfect accompaniment to your Easter meal. Roasting brings out the natural sweetness of vegetables and adds a caramelized finish that complements the richness of meats like lamb and ham.

Ingredients:

  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrots, parsnips, onion, and zucchini with olive oil, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the vegetables evenly on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve hot as a colorful and healthy side dish.

Pro Tip:

For a more festive touch, you can add baby potatoes or bell peppers to the mix for extra color and flavor.

5. Hot Cross Buns

Hot cross buns are a classic Easter treat, often served on Good Friday, but they make a delicious addition to any Easter dinner. These sweet, spiced buns are topped with a simple icing cross and are perfect for breakfast, dessert, or as a side to your holiday meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup currants or raisins
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup milk (for glaze)
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar (for glaze)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, cinnamon, and currants or raisins. Add the yeast mixture, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal portions. Shape each portion into a ball and place on a greased baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. While the buns are baking, prepare the glaze by mixing 1/4 cup milk and powdered sugar together.
  9. Once the buns are out of the oven, drizzle the glaze over the top in the shape of a cross.

Pro Tip:

For a richer flavor, you can add a tablespoon of orange zest or lemon zest to the dough.

6. Classic Carrot Cake

For dessert, a classic carrot cake is always a hit during Easter celebrations. It’s moist, flavorful, and topped with a rich cream cheese frosting that’s simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the eggs, oil, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Stir in the grated carrots and chopped walnuts (if using).
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared cake pans.
  7. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cakes to cool completely before frosting.
  9. To make the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat until fluffy.
  10. Spread a layer of frosting on top of one cake, then place the second cake on top. Frost the top and sides of the cake with the remaining frosting.
  11. Garnish with additional chopped walnuts or shredded coconut, if desired.

Pro Tip:

For an extra touch of flavor, you can add pineapple or shredded coconut to the cake batter.

With these traditional Easter dinner recipes, you can create a memorable meal that will delight your guests. From savory roasts and hearty sides to sweet treats and baked goods, these dishes will bring comfort and joy to your celebration. Whether you stick with the classics or add your own unique twist, these recipes are sure to become favorites year after year. Happy cooking and enjoy your Easter feast!
