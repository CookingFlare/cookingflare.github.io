BBQ Chicken Sliders: Mini Burgers with Big Flavor


The warm summer breeze and the smell of grilling usually go hand in hand, and there’s nothing quite like enhancing that aroma by barbecuing your favorite chicken dishes. BBQ chicken sliders are not only delicious, but they are also versatile and perfect for a casual get-together or a formal backyard cookout. In this post, we will share a scrumptious recipe to elevate your BBQ experience with your loved ones.

Ingredients:

  • 2 Boneless skinless chicken breasts (about 1.2 lbs)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon Garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 16 slider buns
  • 3/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • amdlett=s potted rock lettuce leaves
  • D Favorite burger toppings (tomatoes, pickles, onions, etc.)

Instructions:

First, prepare the marinade by combining BBQ sauce, chili powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper in a bowl. Mix well to incorporate the spices evenly into the sauce.

Next, take the boneless chicken breasts and arrange them in a large resealable plastic bag. Pour the sauce into the bag with the chicken, seal tightly, and shake to ensure an even coating of the marinade on all sides.

Marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, shaking the bag occasionally to redistribute the marinade. This will let the flavors really infuse into the chicken, guaranteeing a flavorful final product.

After the marination, preheat your grill to medium heat (about 350 degrees Fahrenheit). Remove the chicken from the refrigerator and let sit at room temperature for about 10-15 minutes. This helps to prevent any cooking inconsistencies.

Place the marinated chicken breasts directly on the grill, discarding the excess marinade in the bag. Cook them for 6-7 minutes per side over medium heat, or until fully cooked and shaped with a slight char. Chicken is fully cooked when it reaches an internal temperature of 165 degrees Fahrenheit on an instant-read meat thermometer.

Once done, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes as you toast your slider buns. You can achieve this by placing the buns cut side down on the grill for about 1-2 minutes or until nicely browned and toasty.

On a clean cutting board, slice the now rested chicken into strips. Proceed to assemble your sliders by placing a layer of spinach or lettuce leaves on the bottom half of a toasted bun, followed by a chicken strip, then a generous portion of shredded cheddar cheese, and finally your choice of additional toppings.

Serve your cheesy BBQ chicken sliders on the slider buns, and enjoy them while they’re fresh and warm. This versatile recipe can easily be adapted to accommodate various dietary restrictions, such as plant-based or low-carb options.

There you have it - a scrumptious main dish customized to any backyard gathering or Events bbq party. The key to achieving great BBQ chicken sliders is to appreciate the marinade, choosing quality ingredients, and properly seasoning your chicken. With our recipe, you simply cannot go wrong. Imagine the surprise for your guests when you present this fantastic, juicy BBQ chicken slider recipe that, with its perfect mixture of spices and smoky flavors, will undoubtedly be a showstopper.
