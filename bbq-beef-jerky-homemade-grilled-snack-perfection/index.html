BBQ Beef Jerky: Homemade Grilled Snack Perfection


If you’re a fan of savory, smoky flavors and enjoy snacking on protein-packed treats, then homemade BBQ beef jerky is an absolute must-try. This delectable grilled snack is not only bursting with flavor but also offers a great way to satisfy your cravings while keeping things healthy. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating your very own BBQ beef jerky at home, using simple ingredients and a grill to achieve that perfect balance of tenderness and chewiness.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef (such as sirloin or flank steak)
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 cup of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of liquid smoke
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Cooking spray

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: Start by placing the beef in the freezer for about 1-2 hours. This will make it easier to slice thinly. Once chilled, slice the beef into 1/4 inch thick strips, cutting against the grain for a chewier texture.

  2. Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, honey, liquid smoke, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Mix well to ensure that the flavors are evenly distributed. Place the beef strips in a large, resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over the beef. Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible, and massage the beef to coat it thoroughly with the marinade. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.

  3. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, around 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease the grill grates with cooking spray to prevent the beef from sticking.

  4. Grill the Beef: Remove the beef strips from the marinade and pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess marinade. Arrange the strips on the grill grate, leaving a small gap between each piece to allow for even cooking. Grill the beef for 3-4 hours, flipping the strips every hour to ensure that they cook evenly. The jerky is ready when it’s dry to the touch and has a chewy, leathery texture.

  5. Cool and Store: Once the beef jerky is grilled to perfection, let it cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container or resealable bags. Properly stored, the beef jerky can last for up to 2 weeks, although it’s so delicious that it’s unlikely to stick around for that long!

Enjoy Your Homemade BBQ Beef Jerky!

There you have it – your very own homemade BBQ beef jerky, made with love and packed with mouthwatering flavors. Whether you’re planning a camping trip, a road trip, or just want a satisfying snack to munch on, this grilled treat is sure to hit the spot. Feel free to adjust the seasonings to suit your taste preferences, and experiment with different cuts of beef for varied textures. Once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll never go back to store-bought beef jerky again. So fire up the grill and get ready to indulge in the irresistible taste of homemade BBQ beef jerky!
