Crispy Mexican Churros: Sweet Treats You Can Make at Home


Churros, those delectable, sugary treats that have become synonymous with Mexican street food, are not only a favorite among dessert lovers but also surprisingly easy to make at home. With their crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior, churros are the perfect combination of texture and flavor. Whether you enjoy them as a sweet snack or a delightful dessert, learning how to make churros in your own kitchen is a rewarding experience that will impress family and friends alike. This guide will walk you through every step of the process, from preparing the dough to frying the churros and serving them with delicious dipping sauces.

The History of Churros

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the rich history of churros. Originating in Spain, churros were brought to Latin America by Spanish colonizers. Over the years, they have evolved into a beloved treat, especially in Mexico, where they are often enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack. Traditionally, churros are served with a cup of thick hot chocolate for dipping, making them an irresistible treat.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make crispy Mexican churros, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Churros:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs

For the Cinnamon Sugar Coating:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Chocolate Dipping Sauce (optional):

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Equipment You’ll Need

  • Heavy-bottomed saucepan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Pastry bag with a star tip
  • Deep frying pan or pot
  • Candy thermometer (optional)
  • Slotted spoon
  • Paper towels

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by making the churro dough. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.

Once the water is boiling, remove the saucepan from heat and add 1 cup of all-purpose flour all at once. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture forms a ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan. Allow the dough to cool for a few minutes.

Next, add 2 large eggs to the dough, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Continue mixing until the dough is smooth and glossy. This step is crucial for achieving the right texture in your churros.

Step 2: Prepare the Cinnamon Sugar Coating

While your dough cools, prepare the cinnamon sugar coating. In a small bowl, combine 1/2 cup of granulated sugar with 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon. Mix well and set aside. This sweet coating will give your churros that signature flavor once they are fried.

Step 3: Heat the Oil

In a deep frying pan or pot, pour enough vegetable oil to fill it about 2 to 3 inches deep. Heat the oil over medium heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). You can use a candy thermometer to check the temperature, or you can drop a small piece of dough into the oil; if it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is ready.

Step 4: Pipe the Churros

Once the oil is hot, it’s time to pipe the churros. Transfer the dough into a pastry bag fitted with a large star tip. Carefully pipe strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting them to your desired length with scissors. Fry the churros in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pan.

Cook the churros for about 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the churros from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 5: Coat the Churros

While the churros are still warm, roll them in the cinnamon sugar mixture until they are fully coated. This step adds an extra layer of sweetness and flavor to the churros that is simply irresistible.

Step 6: Prepare the Chocolate Dipping Sauce (Optional)

If you want to take your churros to the next level, consider making a chocolate dipping sauce. In a small saucepan over low heat, combine 1 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips with 1/2 cup of heavy cream. Stir continuously until the chocolate is melted and the mixture is smooth. Remove it from the heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract for added flavor. Serve the warm sauce alongside your churros for dipping.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy!

Your homemade crispy Mexican churros are now ready to be enjoyed! Serve them warm with the chocolate dipping sauce and perhaps a side of fresh fruit or whipped cream for an added treat. Churros are best enjoyed fresh, but if you have any leftovers, they can be stored in an airtight container for up to two days. Reheat them in the oven to restore their crispy texture.

Tips for Perfect Churros

  1. Don’t skip the cooling step: Allowing the dough to cool slightly before adding the eggs helps prevent the eggs from cooking too quickly, ensuring a smooth consistency.

  2. Keep an eye on the oil temperature: Maintaining the right oil temperature is crucial for achieving crispy churros. If the oil is too hot, the churros will brown too quickly and remain raw inside. If it’s too cool, they will absorb excess oil and become greasy.

  3. Experiment with flavors: Feel free to get creative with your churros! You can add a touch of vanilla extract or almond extract to the dough for extra flavor. Additionally, consider drizzling caramel sauce or adding a sprinkle of sea salt for a modern twist.

  4. Make mini churros: For a fun variation, try piping mini churros. They’re perfect for serving as bite-sized treats at parties or gatherings.

  5. Serve with a variety of dips: In addition to chocolate sauce, consider offering a variety of dips, such as dulce de leche, caramel sauce, or even fruit compote, to cater to different taste preferences.

Final Thoughts

Making crispy Mexican churros at home is not only a fun and rewarding culinary adventure but also a delicious way to treat yourself and your loved ones. Whether you enjoy them at a family gathering, a festive celebration, or as an indulgent snack, churros are sure to bring joy and sweetness to any occasion.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and dive into the delightful world of churro-making. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be serving up crispy, golden churros that rival those found at your favorite street vendor. Happy cooking!
