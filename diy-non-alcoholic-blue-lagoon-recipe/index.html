DIY Non-Alcoholic Blue Lagoon Recipe


The Blue Lagoon is a refreshing, vibrant, and visually stunning beverage that has captivated drink enthusiasts for years. Its electric blue color and tropical flavor make it an excellent choice for a fun, non-alcoholic drink for parties, gatherings, or a relaxing evening at home. Whether you’re hosting a summer party or simply want to enjoy a beautiful, thirst-quenching drink, this non-alcoholic Blue Lagoon recipe is sure to impress.

In this blog post, we’ll explore how to create a delicious, alcohol-free version of the Blue Lagoon that’s perfect for all ages. Not only is this recipe easy to make, but it also incorporates natural ingredients that ensure a fantastic flavor and a stunning appearance. So, let’s dive into this DIY recipe for a delightful Blue Lagoon drink that you can enjoy any time!

Ingredients for Non-Alcoholic Blue Lagoon

Making a non-alcoholic Blue Lagoon requires only a few simple ingredients that are easy to find at any local grocery store. You don’t need any special equipment or exotic ingredients to make this drink, just a bit of creativity and the right mix of flavors. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Blue Curacao Syrup (Non-Alcoholic) – 2 oz (60 ml)
    Blue Curacao syrup provides that signature bright blue color and citrusy flavor. Be sure to look for a non-alcoholic version of Blue Curacao syrup. This ingredient is key to creating that vibrant blue look and tangy taste.

  • Lemonade – 6 oz (180 ml)
    Lemonade adds a refreshing and slightly tart base to the drink, perfectly balancing the sweetness of the syrup and enhancing the citrus notes.

  • Pineapple Juice – 3 oz (90 ml)
    Pineapple juice introduces a tropical sweetness to the Blue Lagoon. It pairs beautifully with the tartness of the lemonade and the vibrant blue syrup.

  • Club Soda or Sparkling Water – 2 oz (60 ml)
    To add a fizzy, refreshing quality to the drink, club soda or sparkling water is a must. It lightens the sweetness of the juice combination and makes the drink feel more refreshing.

  • Lime or Lemon Wedges – For garnish
    Garnish the drink with a fresh lime or lemon wedge to bring a little extra zest and a pop of color. This also makes the drink look even more appealing and festive.

  • Ice Cubes – As needed
    Ice cubes are essential to keep the Blue Lagoon nice and chilled. You can even use crushed ice if you prefer a slushy-like texture.

Optional additions:

  • Mint Leaves – For garnish
    A sprig of fresh mint will not only add a lovely aroma but also enhance the visual appeal of your drink.

How to Make Non-Alcoholic Blue Lagoon

Making the perfect Blue Lagoon doesn’t take much time or effort. In fact, it’s incredibly simple to prepare and fun to customize. Follow these easy steps to create your own tropical, alcohol-free Blue Lagoon.

Step 1: Prepare Your Glass

Start by selecting a tall, clear glass to showcase the vibrant blue color of the drink. The transparency of the glass will allow the Blue Lagoon’s striking appearance to shine through. Fill the glass with ice cubes (or crushed ice) to chill it, giving you a refreshing base for the drink.

Step 2: Add Blue Curacao Syrup

Pour 2 oz (60 ml) of non-alcoholic Blue Curacao syrup into the glass over the ice. The syrup is thick and sweet, so it will form the drink’s signature blue base. Watch as the bright blue color swirls into the glass!

Step 3: Mix in Lemonade and Pineapple Juice

Now, add 6 oz (180 ml) of lemonade and 3 oz (90 ml) of pineapple juice to the glass. Use a spoon or a stirrer to gently mix the ingredients together. This combination of lemonade and pineapple juice will not only enhance the flavor profile but will also create a tropical, fruity base that blends perfectly with the Blue Curacao syrup.

Step 4: Add Sparkle and Fizz

To bring a touch of sparkle, pour 2 oz (60 ml) of club soda or sparkling water into the glass. The fizziness adds a fun, refreshing element to the drink while balancing out the sweetness of the syrup and juices.

Step 5: Stir and Garnish

Give everything a gentle stir to make sure the ingredients are well combined. The Blue Lagoon should be a lovely shade of blue with a light, fizzy texture. To finish, garnish the drink with a wedge of lime or lemon on the rim of the glass. You can also add a few fresh mint leaves for an added burst of color and fragrance.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Your non-alcoholic Blue Lagoon is now ready to serve! Pour it into a festive drink holder, top it with a colorful straw, and enjoy this tropical mocktail. Whether you’re sipping it by the poolside or enjoying a cozy evening at home, this drink is sure to transport you to a relaxing, tropical paradise.

Tips for the Perfect Non-Alcoholic Blue Lagoon

Use Fresh Ingredients

Fresh ingredients always make a huge difference in the flavor and overall quality of your drink. If possible, use freshly squeezed lemon juice instead of bottled lemonade for a more vibrant and natural taste. The same goes for using fresh pineapple juice instead of pre-packaged juice, which can sometimes have added sugars and preservatives.

Adjust the Sweetness

While Blue Curacao syrup already provides some sweetness, you can adjust the level of sweetness to your liking by adding a little extra sugar or honey to the mix. If you prefer a less sweet drink, consider reducing the amount of syrup and adding a bit more club soda or sparkling water.

Experiment with Garnishes

The garnish is an important part of the Blue Lagoon experience. While lime wedges and mint leaves are classic options, you can get creative and try other fruits like orange slices or berries. This not only enhances the flavor but also makes your drink look even more festive and inviting.

Chill Your Ingredients

For an extra-cold drink, you can chill your lemonade, pineapple juice, and Blue Curacao syrup before mixing. This way, you’ll spend less time waiting for your drink to cool down and can enjoy it immediately.

Make a Larger Batch for a Party

If you’re planning to serve Blue Lagoon at a party or gathering, it’s easy to scale up the recipe. Simply multiply the ingredients to make a large pitcher, and serve the drink in individual glasses with ice. Just be sure to adjust the proportions to maintain the right balance of flavors.
