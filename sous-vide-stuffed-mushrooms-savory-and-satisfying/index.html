Sous Vide Stuffed Mushrooms: Savory and Satisfying


If you’re a mushroom lover like me, then you’re in for a treat! Today, I’ll be sharing with you a delicious and easy recipe for Sous Vide Stuffed Mushrooms. These savory and satisfying bite-sized delights are perfect as an appetizer or a light meal. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of button mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your Sous Vide machine to 165°F (74°C).

  2. Clean the mushrooms by gently wiping them with a damp paper towel. Remove the stems and set them aside.

  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, and sauté until they become fragrant and translucent.

  4. Chop the mushroom stems finely and add them to the skillet. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the mushroom stems are tender.

  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and transfer the mixture to a bowl. Let it cool for a few minutes.

  6. In the same bowl, add the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix everything together until well combined.

  7. Take each mushroom cap and stuff it generously with the breadcrumb mixture. Place the stuffed mushrooms in a single layer inside a vacuum-sealed bag.

  8. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method. Make sure to remove as much air as possible from the bag.

  9. Gently place the sealed bag into the preheated water bath of your Sous Vide machine. Cook the mushrooms for 1 hour.

  10. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the stuffed mushrooms to a serving dish.

  11. Serve the Sous Vide Stuffed Mushrooms hot, garnished with some fresh parsley. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize the stuffing by adding other ingredients such as cooked bacon, sautéed spinach, or even a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for a spicy kick.

  • If you prefer a crispy topping, you can broil the stuffed mushrooms in the oven for a couple of minutes after they have been cooked sous vide.

  • These stuffed mushrooms can be prepared in advance and refrigerated until ready to cook. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly if cooking from chilled.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms like cremini or portobello for a richer flavor.

The Magic of Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide cooking is a technique that involves cooking food in a temperature-controlled water bath, resulting in perfectly cooked and evenly heated dishes. It’s a method that has gained popularity among chefs and home cooks alike for its ability to retain the natural flavors and textures of the ingredients.

When it comes to stuffed mushrooms, cooking them sous vide ensures that they are cooked to perfection, with the filling being moist and flavorful. The low and slow cooking process allows the mushrooms to absorb all the delicious flavors, creating a mouthwatering bite every time.

So, why not give these Sous Vide Stuffed Mushrooms a try? They are not only easy to make but also a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or looking for a delicious appetizer, these savory and satisfying mushrooms will surely impress. Enjoy!
