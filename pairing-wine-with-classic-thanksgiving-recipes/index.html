Pairing Wine with Classic Thanksgiving Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time of year when families gather together to celebrate and give thanks. It’s also a time when delicious food takes center stage. From the traditional roast turkey to the mouthwatering pumpkin pie, Thanksgiving meals are a feast for the senses. But have you ever wondered which wines pair best with these classic Thanksgiving recipes? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of pairing wine with your favorite Thanksgiving dishes.

Roast Turkey and Pinot Noir

The star of the Thanksgiving table, the roast turkey, deserves a wine that can complement its rich flavors. Enter Pinot Noir, a versatile and elegant red wine that pairs beautifully with turkey. The light to medium body of Pinot Noir allows it to enhance the flavors of the turkey without overpowering them. Look for a Pinot Noir with notes of red fruit, earth, and spice to complement the savory flavors of the roasted bird.

Mashed Potatoes and Chardonnay

Creamy and buttery, mashed potatoes are a Thanksgiving staple. To balance the richness of this side dish, reach for a crisp and refreshing Chardonnay. The bright acidity of the wine cuts through the richness of the potatoes, creating a harmonious pairing. Opt for a Chardonnay with flavors of apple, pear, and a hint of oak to elevate your mashed potato experience.

Cranberry Sauce and Riesling

The tangy and sweet flavors of cranberry sauce require a wine with a touch of sweetness and acidity. Riesling, with its vibrant fruit flavors and refreshing acidity, is the perfect match. Look for a slightly off-dry Riesling with notes of citrus, peach, and honey to complement the tartness of the cranberry sauce. The combination of the wine’s sweetness and acidity will create a delightful contrast with each bite.

Stuffing and Syrah/Shiraz

The aromatic and herbaceous flavors of stuffing call for a wine with bold flavors and strong tannins. Syrah/Shiraz fits the bill perfectly. This robust red wine boasts dark fruit flavors, peppery notes, and a hint of smokiness that pairs wonderfully with the savory flavors of stuffing. The wine’s tannins help cleanse the palate and prepare it for the next delicious bite.

Pumpkin Pie and Port

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without a slice of pumpkin pie. To enhance the flavors of this classic dessert, look no further than Port. This fortified wine from Portugal is rich, sweet, and full-bodied, making it the perfect complement to the creamy and spiced flavors of pumpkin pie. Opt for a Tawny Port with its nutty and caramel notes for a truly decadent pairing.

Conclusion

Pairing wine with classic Thanksgiving recipes is an art that can elevate your holiday feast to new heights. Whether you’re enjoying roast turkey, mashed potatoes, cranberry sauce, stuffing, or pumpkin pie, there’s a wine out there waiting to enhance the flavors of your favorite dishes. So, this Thanksgiving, don’t forget to raise a glass and toast to good food, good company, and the perfect wine pairing. Cheers!
