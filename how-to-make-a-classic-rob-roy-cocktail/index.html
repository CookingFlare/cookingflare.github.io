How to Make a Classic Rob Roy Cocktail


The Rob Roy cocktail is a timeless drink that carries with it a rich history and a smooth, sophisticated flavor profile. Often considered a variation of the Manhattan, the Rob Roy is typically made with Scotch whisky instead of bourbon or rye, giving it a distinct twist. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or simply looking to enjoy a well-crafted drink, the Rob Roy cocktail is an excellent choice. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about making this classic cocktail, from its history to the step-by-step preparation.

The History of the Rob Roy Cocktail

Named after the Scottish folk hero, Rob Roy MacGregor, this cocktail was first created in New York City in 1894. The drink was created by bartender Murray Stough at the Waldorf-Astoria Hotel during the premiere of an operetta titled Rob Roy by composer Reginald de Koven. The operetta was inspired by the legendary Scottish outlaw, Rob Roy MacGregor, and the drink was named in his honor.

What makes the Rob Roy stand out from other classic cocktails is the use of Scotch whisky. While the Manhattan typically calls for bourbon or rye whiskey, the Rob Roy takes the opportunity to showcase the unique characteristics of Scotch whisky—whether that be a smoky, peaty flavor or a smoother, more malt-forward profile. This makes the Rob Roy a drink that’s especially appealing to whisky enthusiasts and those who enjoy exploring different types of spirits.

Key Ingredients for a Rob Roy Cocktail

The beauty of the Rob Roy lies in its simplicity. The ingredients are straightforward and easy to source, yet they come together to create a perfectly balanced drink. To make a classic Rob Roy cocktail, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 oz (60 ml) Scotch whisky
    Choose a quality Scotch whisky for the best results. For a traditional Rob Roy, you may want to use a blended Scotch. However, if you prefer a smokier flavor, a single malt Scotch might be more to your liking.

  • 1 oz (30 ml) sweet vermouth
    Sweet vermouth adds a touch of sweetness and complexity to the cocktail. This ingredient is essential to creating the classic flavor profile of the Rob Roy.

  • 2 dashes of Angostura bitters
    A few dashes of Angostura bitters add depth and balance to the drink, cutting through the sweetness of the vermouth and bringing out the flavors of the Scotch.

  • Garnish: Maraschino cherry or a lemon twist
    A garnish is an important finishing touch for the Rob Roy. A maraschino cherry is the traditional garnish, but some variations use a twist of lemon to add a citrusy note to the drink.

Choosing the Right Scotch Whisky for Your Rob Roy

The choice of Scotch whisky can dramatically change the flavor of your Rob Roy. Since Scotch is available in a wide variety of styles, each with its own distinct characteristics, selecting the right bottle for your cocktail is crucial. Here are a few tips for choosing the best Scotch for your Rob Roy:

  • Blended Scotch whisky: Blended Scotch is the most commonly used in a traditional Rob Roy. Brands like Johnnie Walker Black Label or Chivas Regal offer smooth and approachable flavors that complement the sweet vermouth and bitters in the cocktail. Blended Scotch whiskies tend to be balanced, with some slight smokiness and malty sweetness.

  • Single malt Scotch whisky: For a more complex and robust flavor, you might prefer a single malt Scotch. Single malts are made from malted barley and distilled at a single distillery, resulting in unique flavors. If you like a peaty, smoky profile, consider using a single malt from distilleries like Laphroaig or Ardbeg.

  • Aged Scotch whisky: Older Scotch whiskies tend to be richer and more nuanced. If you’re looking to elevate your Rob Roy, consider using an aged bottle, such as a 12-year-old Glenfiddich or Glenlivet, to add depth to your drink.

Keep in mind that you don’t need to use the most expensive Scotch available for a Rob Roy. However, using a high-quality Scotch can elevate the drink and enhance your overall experience.

The Tools You’ll Need to Make a Rob Roy Cocktail

Before you get started, it’s essential to have the right tools on hand. While you don’t need anything overly fancy, having a few key bar tools will make the preparation process smoother and more efficient. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Cocktail shaker or stirring glass: A cocktail shaker is ideal for mixing the ingredients and chilling them. Alternatively, a stirring glass is an excellent choice if you prefer stirring the drink rather than shaking it.

  • Bar spoon: If you’re stirring your Rob Roy, a bar spoon will help you mix the ingredients evenly.

  • Strainer: A strainer ensures that no ice or other solids end up in your glass when you pour the finished drink.

  • Jigger: A jigger is a precise measuring tool that allows you to measure your ingredients accurately. This is important for maintaining the correct balance of flavors in your Rob Roy.

  • Glassware: The Rob Roy is typically served in a coupe glass or a classic cocktail glass. These glasses have a wide, shallow bowl that allows the drink to be sipped elegantly.

How to Make a Rob Roy Cocktail

Now that you have everything you need, it’s time to make your Rob Roy cocktail! Follow these simple steps for a perfectly balanced drink:

Step 1: Measure Your Ingredients

Start by measuring out your ingredients. You’ll need 2 oz of Scotch whisky, 1 oz of sweet vermouth, and 2 dashes of Angostura bitters. Using a jigger will help ensure that the proportions are just right.

Step 2: Add the Ingredients to a Mixing Glass

If you’re stirring your Rob Roy, add the Scotch whisky, sweet vermouth, and bitters to a mixing glass or a cocktail shaker. Stirring is the preferred method for making a Rob Roy, as it maintains the drink’s clarity and smooth texture.

Step 3: Stir or Shake

Using a bar spoon, stir the ingredients for about 30 seconds. If you prefer a slightly more diluted drink or prefer to shake, you can add ice to a cocktail shaker and shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds. However, stirring will yield a smoother, more refined texture that’s typical of this cocktail.

Step 4: Strain Into a Glass

Once your Rob Roy is well-mixed, use a strainer to pour the drink into a chilled coupe glass or cocktail glass. Be sure to strain out any ice or bits of bitters for a clean pour.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Now it’s time to garnish your Rob Roy. Traditionally, a maraschino cherry is used, adding a touch of sweetness and color to the drink. Alternatively, you can use a twist of lemon to provide a subtle citrus aroma. Place the garnish carefully on top of the drink.

Serve your Rob Roy immediately, and enjoy the smooth, sophisticated flavors of this classic cocktail.

Variations of the Rob Roy Cocktail

While the classic Rob Roy is delicious on its own, you can experiment with different variations to suit your preferences. Here are a few ways you can put your own twist on the traditional recipe:

Dry Rob Roy

If you prefer a less sweet cocktail, try a Dry Rob Roy. This variation uses dry vermouth instead of sweet vermouth, resulting in a drier, crisper drink. The Dry Rob Roy is perfect for those who enjoy a more minimalist flavor profile.

Perfect Rob Roy

The Perfect Rob Roy is a balanced alternative that uses equal parts sweet and dry vermouth. This variation is less sweet than the classic Rob Roy but still retains the complexity and smoothness that make the original so appealing.

Rob Roy with a Twist of Citrus

For an added burst of freshness, consider garnishing your Rob Roy with a twist of lemon or orange peel instead of the traditional maraschino cherry. This variation adds a light, citrusy aroma that enhances the drink’s complexity.

Smoky Rob Roy

If you love smoky flavors, try using a peated single malt Scotch whisky in your Rob Roy. This variation will add a layer of smokiness that pairs beautifully with the vermouth and bitters, giving your drink a unique character.

Pairing the Rob Roy Cocktail

The Rob Roy is a versatile cocktail that pairs well with a wide variety of foods. Its smooth, slightly sweet, and complex flavors make it an excellent choice for a variety of occasions. Here are a few food pairing ideas:

  • Cheese: A Rob Roy pairs wonderfully with a variety of cheeses, especially strong-flavored varieties like blue cheese, aged cheddar, or Gouda. The rich flavors of the cheese complement the depth of the Scotch whisky.

  • Charcuterie: The salty, savory flavors of charcuterie boards featuring meats like prosciutto, salami, and pâté are an excellent match for the Rob Roy. The combination of savory and smoky flavors enhances the drink’s profile.

  • Grilled meats: If you’re serving grilled steak, lamb, or even barbecued meats, the Rob Roy’s bold character can stand up to these rich flavors.

  • Dark chocolate: For dessert, dark chocolate pairs beautifully with a Rob Roy, particularly chocolate with a high cocoa content. The bitter notes of the chocolate balance the sweetness of the vermouth.

Final Thoughts

The Rob Roy cocktail is a true classic that has stood the test of time. Whether you’re enjoying it in its traditional form or experimenting with variations, this cocktail offers a sophisticated and enjoyable drinking experience. With its simple ingredients and easy preparation, the Rob Roy is perfect for both seasoned cocktail enthusiasts and those just starting to explore the world of mixed drinks.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a classic cocktail with a twist, grab a bottle of Scotch and give the Rob Roy a try. It’s a drink that never goes out of style!
