Mediterranean-Inspired Frittata: A Versatile Brunch Dish


When it comes to brunch, there’s nothing quite like a frittata to steal the show. This Italian egg-based dish is not only incredibly versatile but also a perfect canvas for incorporating Mediterranean flavors. Whether you’re hosting a weekend brunch or looking for a quick and satisfying meal, a Mediterranean-inspired frittata is a delightful option that’s bound to impress your taste buds.

Ingredients

Base Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk or heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Vegetables:

  • 1 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/4 cup sliced black olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions

Herbs and Spices:

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Cheese:

  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and position a rack in the upper third of the oven.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk or cream, salt, and pepper until well combined. Set aside.
  3. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the diced bell peppers and cook for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
  4. Add the red onions, garlic, and a pinch of salt, sautéing for another 2 minutes until the onions are translucent.
  5. Toss in the cherry tomatoes, spinach, black olives, and sun-dried tomatoes, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes until the spinach wilts and the tomatoes begin to soften.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet, gently stirring to distribute the ingredients evenly.
  7. Sprinkle the top with crumbled feta cheese, grated Parmesan, dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes (if using).
  8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the middle and the edges are golden brown.
  9. Once done, remove the skillet from the oven and let the frittata cool for a few minutes before sprinkling with fresh parsley.
  10. Carefully slide the frittata onto a cutting board, slice into wedges, and serve.

Serving Suggestions

This Mediterranean-inspired frittata is a delicious dish on its own, but it pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. Consider serving it with a simple mixed green salad dressed in a light vinaigrette, crusty artisan bread, or roasted potatoes for a heartier brunch spread. The frittata also makes for a delightful addition to a brunch buffet or a picnic.

Conclusion

The beauty of a frittata lies in its adaptability, making it a go-to dish for any occasion. With the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean, this frittata is as visually appealing as it is delicious. Whether you’re a fan of brunch, a busy home cook, or simply someone who appreciates a satisfying meal, this Mediterranean-inspired frittata is a must-try. Experiment with various ingredients and make it your own, embracing the versatility and charm of this timeless brunch classic.
