Classic Italian Pasta Recipes for Cooking Newbies


As a cooking newbie, it can be intimidating to venture into the world of Italian cuisine, especially when it comes to pasta dishes. With countless options and techniques to master, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. However, don’t let fear hold you back! With these classic Italian pasta recipes, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a pasta pro in no time.

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

One of the simplest yet most flavorful pasta dishes, Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a staple in Italian cuisine. This recipe requires just a few ingredients, including spaghetti, garlic, olive oil, and red pepper flakes.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 4-6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook spaghetti according to package instructions.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-low heat.
  3. Add thinly sliced garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4-5 minutes or until golden brown.
  4. Add red pepper flakes and cook for an additional minute.
  5. Drain spaghetti and add to the skillet with garlic and oil. Toss to combine, ensuring pasta is well coated.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Garnish with chopped parsley, if desired.

Fettuccine Alfredo

Rich and creamy, Fettuccine Alfredo is a classic Italian dish that’s sure to impress. This recipe requires just a few ingredients, including fettuccine, butter, cream, Parmesan cheese, and nutmeg.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fettuccine
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook fettuccine according to package instructions.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat.
  3. Add heavy cream and bring to a simmer.
  4. Reduce heat to low and let cream simmer for 2-3 minutes or until slightly thickened.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth.
  6. Add cooked fettuccine to the skillet and toss to combine, ensuring pasta is well coated.
  7. Season with salt, black pepper, and nutmeg to taste.

Penne alla Vodka

Spicy and savory, Penne alla Vodka is a popular Italian pasta dish that’s easy to make and packed with flavor. This recipe requires just a few ingredients, including penne, vodka, tomato sauce, and Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz penne
  • 1 cup vodka
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook penne according to package instructions.
  2. In a large skillet, combine vodka and tomato sauce. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let sauce simmer for 5-7 minutes or until slightly thickened.
  4. Stir in Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth.
  5. Add cooked penne to the skillet and toss to combine, ensuring pasta is well coated.
  6. Season with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes to taste.

Tips and Tricks for Cooking Newbies

  • Always cook pasta al dente, meaning it should still have a bit of bite or chew. Overcooking pasta can result in a mushy, unappetizing texture.
  • Use high-quality ingredients, such as fresh vegetables and good-quality cheese. This will make a big difference in the flavor and texture of your dish.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment and try new ingredients. Italian cuisine is all about simplicity and letting the ingredients shine, so don’t be afraid to get creative.
  • Practice makes perfect! The more you cook, the more comfortable you’ll become with different techniques and ingredients.

With these classic Italian pasta recipes, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a pasta pro. Remember to always use high-quality ingredients, cook pasta al dente, and don’t be afraid to experiment. Happy cooking!
