Spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta: A Fiery and Flavorful Seafood Pasta

If you’re a fan of bold flavors and seafood, then this Spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta recipe is a must-try. This dish is a delightful fusion of spicy Cajun seasoning, succulent shrimp, and al dente pasta, all coming together to create a fiery and flavorful experience for your taste buds. Ingredients 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined 12 ounces of fettuccine pasta 2 tablespoons of olive oil 1 tablespoon of Cajun seasoning 1 teaspoon of paprika 1 teaspoon of garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon of onion powder 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon of dried thyme 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste) Salt and black pepper to taste 3 cloves of garlic, minced 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved 1 cup of heavy cream 1/2 cup of chicken broth Fresh parsley for garnish Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp In a bowl, toss the shrimp with Cajun seasoning, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried oregano, dried thyme, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper until the shrimp are evenly coated with the seasoning. Step 2: Cook the Pasta Cook the fettuccine pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. Step 3: Sauté the Shrimp In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside. Step 4: Prepare the Sauce In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and sliced bell peppers. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the peppers begin to soften. Add the cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes. Pour in the heavy cream and chicken broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Allow it to cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce slightly thickens. Step 5: Combine the Pasta and Shrimp Add the cooked fettuccine pasta to the skillet with the sauce and toss to coat the pasta evenly. Gently fold in the sautéed shrimp, ensuring they are well incorporated into the pasta and sauce. Step 6: Serve and Garnish Once the shrimp and pasta are heated through, remove the skillet from the heat. Garnish the Spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta with fresh parsley before serving. Serving Suggestion This Spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta pairs wonderfully with a crisp white wine, such as a Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio. For a complete meal, consider serving it alongside a side salad or garlic bread. ...
