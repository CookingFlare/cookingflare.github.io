Flatbread Pizza with Prosciutto and Arugula


Flatbread pizza with prosciutto and arugula is a delightful, gourmet-inspired dish that combines the crispiness of flatbread with the rich, savory flavor of prosciutto and the refreshing bite of arugula. This easy-to-make recipe is ideal for a quick weeknight dinner or a sophisticated appetizer for your next dinner party. The combination of salty prosciutto, peppery arugula, and creamy mozzarella on a crispy flatbread base creates a satisfying meal that’s both indulgent and refreshing.

Ingredients

To make this flatbread pizza with prosciutto and arugula, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Flatbread:

  • 2 pre-made flatbreads (you can use whole wheat, white, or gluten-free if preferred)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing)
  • 1 garlic clove, minced (optional, for extra flavor)

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup fresh mozzarella cheese, shredded (or fresh mozzarella balls, sliced)
  • 6-8 slices of prosciutto (thinly sliced)
  • 2 cups fresh arugula
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic glaze or reduction
  • 1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • A pinch of sea salt (to taste)
  • Fresh lemon zest (optional, for added citrus aroma)

For the Balsamic Glaze (if making from scratch):

  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional for sweetness)

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). A hot oven is essential for achieving that crispy flatbread base that serves as the perfect foundation for this pizza.

Step 2: Prepare the Flatbread

While the oven is heating, prepare your flatbreads. If you’re using pre-made flatbreads, simply brush each one with a light coat of olive oil. If you prefer to add a hint of garlic flavor, mix the minced garlic into the olive oil and brush it over the flatbread. This step adds depth to the flavor of your crust. After brushing, place the flatbreads directly on a baking sheet or pizza stone.

Step 3: Bake the Flatbread

Place the flatbread in the preheated oven and bake for about 5-7 minutes or until it begins to turn golden brown. This step gives the crust a crisp texture before adding the toppings. Keep an eye on the flatbread to ensure it doesn’t burn.

Step 4: Add the Toppings

Once your flatbread is crispy and golden, it’s time to layer on the toppings. Start by evenly distributing the shredded mozzarella cheese across the surface of the flatbread. Be sure to leave a small border around the edges to maintain that crispy crust. If you’re using fresh mozzarella balls, slice them thinly and scatter them over the pizza.

Next, arrange the prosciutto slices on top of the cheese. Prosciutto is known for its delicate, melt-in-your-mouth texture, so there’s no need to overdo it—6-8 slices should be just right. You can slightly fold the prosciutto for added texture and visual appeal.

Step 5: Bake Again

Return the flatbread with toppings to the oven and bake for another 5-7 minutes. You want the cheese to melt fully and the prosciutto to crisp up slightly around the edges. The flatbread should remain perfectly crispy while the cheese and prosciutto become irresistibly golden.

Step 6: Add Fresh Arugula and Balsamic Glaze

Once the flatbread pizza is out of the oven, it’s time to add the final touches. Top the pizza with fresh arugula—this will give your dish a peppery, slightly bitter bite that balances out the rich and salty flavors of the prosciutto and cheese.

Drizzle a balsamic glaze or reduction over the pizza for an added layer of sweetness and acidity. The balsamic glaze complements the prosciutto beautifully, enhancing its flavors without overpowering the dish. If you’d like to make your own balsamic glaze, simply heat balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat until it reduces by half, then stir in honey or maple syrup if desired for sweetness.

Step 7: Season and Serve

Finish off your flatbread pizza with a light sprinkle of freshly cracked black pepper and a pinch of sea salt to taste. Optionally, add a little lemon zest for an extra citrusy kick that pairs wonderfully with the arugula.

Cut the pizza into slices and serve immediately. The crispy flatbread crust, creamy cheese, salty prosciutto, and fresh, peppery arugula will delight your taste buds with every bite.

Why This Flatbread Pizza Works

This flatbread pizza recipe is all about balance. The crispy flatbread provides the perfect crunch, while the mozzarella cheese adds creaminess. The prosciutto, with its salty, savory flavor, contrasts beautifully with the peppery arugula. Finally, the balsamic glaze ties everything together with its sweetness and tang, creating a harmonious combination of flavors and textures.

What makes this pizza particularly appealing is its versatility. You can easily customize it based on your preferences or what you have available. For example, try adding a few fresh figs for sweetness or some red onion for a mild bite. If you’re a fan of goat cheese, swap it out for mozzarella for a more tangy twist. The possibilities are endless!

Tips for the Perfect Flatbread Pizza

  • Use High-Quality Prosciutto: The prosciutto is one of the star ingredients of this pizza, so it’s important to use the best quality you can find. Look for thinly sliced prosciutto di Parma, which is known for its delicate, melt-in-your-mouth texture.
  • Don’t Overload the Pizza: Keep the toppings light and balanced. Flatbread pizza is all about the crispy base, so don’t load it up with too many ingredients, which could make the crust soggy.
  • Serve Immediately: Flatbread pizzas are best when served fresh out of the oven. The contrast between the crispy crust, melted cheese, and fresh arugula is key to the dish’s success, so try to avoid letting it sit for too long before serving.
  • Make It Your Own: The great thing about flatbread pizza is how customizable it is. Feel free to experiment with different cheeses, meats, and vegetables. You can even make a vegetarian version by swapping the prosciutto for mushrooms or roasted vegetables.

Pairing Wine with Flatbread Pizza

A flatbread pizza with prosciutto and arugula pairs beautifully with a variety of wines. For a red wine, go for a light, fruity wine such as Pinot Noir or a Chianti. If you prefer white wine, a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or a Chardonnay will complement the flavors of the cheese and prosciutto nicely.

For non-alcoholic options, consider pairing the pizza with a sparkling water or an iced herbal tea. A refreshing cucumber or mint tea would work well with the peppery arugula, while the bubbles in sparkling water will help cleanse your palate between bites.

Making Flatbread Pizza Your Own

One of the best aspects of flatbread pizza is how easily it adapts to different tastes and dietary needs. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, gluten-free flatbread is an excellent substitute. You can also play around with the toppings to suit various dietary preferences, such as using dairy-free cheese for those avoiding dairy, or adding extra vegetables for a heartier meal.

If you want to make the pizza a bit more filling, add a side of roasted vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or eggplant. You could also make a side salad with a tangy vinaigrette to add freshness and crunch.

Conclusion

Flatbread pizza with prosciutto and arugula is the perfect blend of flavors and textures, making it a favorite for many. Its versatility and ease of preparation make it ideal for a quick dinner or a crowd-pleasing appetizer. With the crispy flatbread, creamy mozzarella, savory prosciutto, and peppery arugula, every bite is an explosion of flavor. Add a touch of balsamic glaze, a sprinkle of pepper, and you have a pizza that’s both delicious and visually stunning. So, next time you’re craving a pizza that’s simple yet sophisticated, this flatbread pizza with prosciutto and arugula should be your go-to recipe. Enjoy!
