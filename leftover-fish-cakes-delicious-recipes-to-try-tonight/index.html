Leftover Fish Cakes: Delicious Recipes to Try Tonight


Fish cakes are one of those magical dishes that transform yesterday’s leftovers into tonight’s gourmet delight. If you have a surplus of cooked fish, whether from a simple dinner or a festive feast, fish cakes offer a versatile and satisfying way to reinvent those scraps into something fresh, flavorful, and fun to make. In this post, we’ll dive into the art of creating irresistible leftover fish cakes that will have your family and friends asking for seconds — maybe even thirds.

Why Fish Cakes Are the Ultimate Leftover Solution

Fish cakes are a classic comfort food with an exciting mix of textures: crispy on the outside, tender and flavorful on the inside. The beauty lies in how fish cakes make great use of any flaky cooked fish — including cod, haddock, salmon, or pollock — and transform it with just a few staple pantry ingredients.

Not only are they budget-friendly and easy to prepare, but they also lend themselves to endless creativity. From Mediterranean spices to Asian-inspired sauces, fish cakes adapt to many taste preferences. Plus, they’re perfect for weeknight dinners, lunchboxes, or casual gatherings.

Essential Ingredients for Perfect Fish Cakes

Before we jump into the recipes, let’s talk about the building blocks that form the foundation of delicious fish cakes:

  • Cooked fish: Properly flaked and free from bones. Leftover baked, poached, or grilled fish works great.
  • Potatoes or breadcrumbs: These provide structure and help bind the mixture. Mashed potatoes create a creamy interior, while breadcrumbs offer a firmer texture.
  • Aromatic vegetables: Onions, leeks, garlic, or scallions add flavor and moisture.
  • Herbs and spices: Parsley, dill, chives, paprika, cumin, and curry powder are popular options.
  • Egg: Acts as a binder to hold everything together.
  • Seasoning: Salt, pepper, and sometimes a splash of lemon juice for zing.

Basic Fish Cake Recipe to Get You Started

This straightforward recipe is a foundation you can build upon with additional flavors and ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked fish, flaked
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes or ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or butter for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flaked fish, mashed potatoes (or breadcrumbs), onion, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the beaten egg to the mixture and stir until well combined. The mixture should hold together when pressed. If it’s too loose, add more breadcrumbs.
  3. Shape the mixture into patties, about the size of your palm.
  4. Heat oil or butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  5. Fry the fish cakes for 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve warm with your favorite dipping sauce.

Flavor Variations to Elevate Your Fish Cakes

The basic recipe is just the beginning. Here are a few exciting flavor twists you can try to cultivate new favorites.

1. Mediterranean Fish Cakes

Add a mixture of capers, sun-dried tomatoes, and fresh oregano to your fish mixture. Serve alongside a lemon-tahini sauce or tzatziki for a fresh and tangy finish.

2. Asian-Inspired Fish Cakes

Stroke the mixture with grated ginger, finely chopped scallions, and a splash of soy sauce. Incorporate a little sesame oil for depth and a sprinkle of chili flakes for heat. Serve with a dipping sauce made of rice vinegar, soy sauce, and a touch of honey.

3. Curried Fish Cakes

Add curry powder, cumin, coriander, and turmeric to the mixture for a fragrant, warming flavor. Mix in some finely chopped cilantro and diced green chilies. Pair with a cooling yogurt or mint chutney.

4. Herb and Lemon Fish Cakes

Mix in lemon zest, chopped fresh dill, and chives. These bright, fresh flavors make the fish cakes light and aromatic. Serve with a simple aioli or lemon mayo to enhance the citrus notes.

5. Smoked Fish Cakes

If you have smoked fish leftovers, use them to take your cakes in a smokier direction. Add finely diced shallots and a bit of horseradish for a sharp bite. These pair wonderfully with a pea purée or beet relish.

Tips for Perfect Fish Cakes Every Time

  • Drain excess moisture: If your fish or potatoes seem wet, squeeze out excess liquid with a clean kitchen towel. Too much moisture will make the cakes fall apart.
  • Notice the texture: The mixture should be moist but sturdy enough to hold its shape. Adjust by adding more mashed potatoes or breadcrumbs as needed.
  • Chill before cooking: Letting fish cakes rest in the fridge for 20-30 minutes helps firm them up and makes frying easier.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan: Giving your fish cakes enough space while frying ensures they form a crisp crust rather than steaming.
  • Try baking for a lighter option: Instead of pan-frying, place the fish cakes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, brush lightly with oil, and bake at 400°F (200°C) for about 15 minutes, flipping halfway through.

Complement Your Fish Cakes with These Sides and Sauces

Fish cakes truly shine when paired with the right accompaniments.

Sides:

  • Crisp green salads dressed with citrus vinaigrette
  • Roasted or steamed seasonal vegetables
  • Creamy coleslaw for crunch and tang
  • Sweet potato fries or classic chips for traditional charm
  • Light quinoa or couscous salads for a wholesome touch

Sauces:

  • Tartare sauce, made from mayonnaise, pickles, capers, and herbs
  • Aioli with garlic and lemon for a richer, more luxurious dip
  • Spicy sriracha mayo or chili jam to add a kick
  • Simple lemon yogurt sauce for freshness

How to Store and Reheat Leftover Fish Cakes

If you make more fish cakes than can be eaten at once, don’t worry — they store well. Allow them to cool completely before wrapping in plastic wrap or placing in an airtight container. Refrigerate for up to 2 days. To reheat, bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes or pan-fry for a few minutes on each side until heated through.

Fish cakes also freeze nicely. Place them on a baking sheet in a single layer and freeze until firm. Then transfer to a freezer bag or container. They’ll keep well for up to 3 months. Reheat from frozen, either baking or pan-frying, adding a few extra minutes to cook through.

Creative Ways to Use Fish Cakes Beyond the Plate

Fish cakes are more than just a main course; with a little imagination, they become the star of various meals.

1. Fish Cake Sandwiches

Layer a golden fish cake in a soft bun with lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and a spread of aioli or tartare sauce. This makes for a satisfying lunch or dinner with minimal fuss.

2. Fish Cake Salad Bowls

Top leafy greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and avocado with crumbled fish cakes. Drizzle with a citrus dressing and scattered herbs for a light, protein-packed salad.

3. Fish Cake Tacos

Wrap fish cakes in warm tortillas with shredded cabbage, cilantro, and a drizzle of chipotle mayo. A fresh and playful way to enjoy them.

4. Breakfast Fish Cakes

Serve fish cakes alongside poached eggs and sautéed spinach for a unique and hearty breakfast or brunch.

Final Thoughts

Leftover fish cakes are a fantastic example of turning the ordinary into the extraordinary. They honor the fish you have on hand and celebrate fresh, bold flavors from around the world. Their adaptability means you can keep trying new variations until you find your own signature fish cake style.

Next time you glance at that leftover fish in your fridge, embrace the prospect of fish cakes for dinner. It’s a delightful, nutritious, and surprisingly quick solution that brings coziness to your table. Happy cooking and enjoy every bite!
