Creative Thanksgiving Leftover Pasta Recipes


Welcome to our blog post on Creative Thanksgiving Leftover Pasta Recipes! Thanksgiving is a time for indulging in delicious food and spending quality time with loved ones. But what do you do with all those leftovers once the feast is over? Well, we’ve got you covered with some unique and mouthwatering pasta recipes that will transform your Thanksgiving leftovers into culinary masterpieces. So, let’s get cooking!

Recipe 1: Turkey Tetrazzini Pasta Bake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 8 oz linguine pasta
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the linguine pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the mushrooms and garlic until mushrooms are tender.
  4. Add the shredded turkey, peas, chicken broth, and heavy cream to the skillet. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add the cooked linguine pasta to the skillet and toss everything together.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  8. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Recipe 2: Cranberry and Brussels Sprouts Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz penne pasta
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the penne pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the Brussels sprouts and sauté until slightly tender.
  3. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the cooked penne pasta, dried cranberries, and chopped walnuts to the skillet. Toss everything together until well combined.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese on top.

Recipe 3: Sweet Potato and Sage Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes, mashed
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh sage leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fettuccine pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the mashed sweet potatoes, chopped sage leaves, and chopped pecans to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  4. Add the cooked fettuccine pasta to the skillet and toss everything together until well coated.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy the unique combination of flavors!

Recipe 4: Stuffing Stuffed Shells

Ingredients:

  • 12 jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups leftover Thanksgiving stuffing
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. In a bowl, combine the leftover Thanksgiving stuffing with 1/4 cup of marinara sauce. Mix well.
  4. Stuff each cooked pasta shell with a spoonful of the stuffing mixture and place them in a greased baking dish.
  5. Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the stuffed shells and sprinkle with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

These Creative Thanksgiving Leftover Pasta Recipes are a fantastic way to make the most of your Thanksgiving leftovers. They are not only delicious but also easy to prepare. So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of turkey, cranberries, or sweet potatoes, give these recipes a try. Your taste buds will thank you!

Remember to get creative and add your own personal touch to these recipes. Feel free to experiment with different ingredients and flavors. After all, cooking is all about having fun and enjoying the process. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!

Note: These recipes are intended for inspiration. Please adjust the quantities and ingredients according to your preferences and available leftovers.
