Korean Street Food Recipes You Can Make at Home


Korean street food has become a global sensation, bringing the vibrant flavors and textures of Korea’s bustling markets straight to your plate. From savory snacks to sweet treats, these delicious street foods offer a taste of the rich culture and culinary heritage that South Korea is known for. Many of these dishes are quick to make and require only a handful of ingredients, making them perfect for anyone wanting to enjoy Korean flavors without leaving the comfort of their own home.

In this guide, we’ll explore how to recreate some of the most iconic Korean street food dishes. Whether you’re craving crispy, chewy, spicy, or sweet, these recipes will take you on a culinary journey through the streets of Seoul and beyond.

1. Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cakes)

Tteokbokki is one of the most famous and beloved Korean street foods, enjoyed by locals and visitors alike. The dish consists of chewy rice cakes, stir-fried in a sweet and spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste) sauce. It’s a warm, comforting snack that’s packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of Korean rice cakes (tteok)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cups of water or chicken stock
  • 1 boiled egg (optional)
  • Fish cakes (optional)
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice cakes: If using frozen rice cakes, soak them in warm water for 30 minutes before cooking.
  2. Cook the onions and garlic: In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onions and sauté for about 2 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  3. Make the sauce: Stir in the gochujang, sugar, soy sauce, and rice vinegar. Add the water (or chicken stock) and stir everything together to form a smooth sauce.
  4. Add the rice cakes: Add the rice cakes to the pan and stir to coat them with the sauce. Let everything simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and the rice cakes become soft and chewy.
  5. Optional additions: You can add fish cakes or a boiled egg to the dish for extra flavor.
  6. Serve: Once the rice cakes are fully cooked and the sauce has thickened, remove from heat. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving.

This dish is spicy, savory, and satisfying — the perfect snack to share with friends or enjoy as a late-night treat.

2. Hotteok (Korean Sweet Pancakes)

Hotteok is a popular Korean street food, especially in the colder months. This sweet and chewy pancake is filled with a warm brown sugar, cinnamon, and nut filling, making it a delicious treat for any time of the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Make the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Gradually add the warm water, mixing until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  2. Prepare the filling: In a small bowl, mix the brown sugar, cinnamon, and chopped walnuts (if using).
  3. Form the pancakes: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into small balls (about the size of a golf ball). Flatten each ball into a disk, place a spoonful of the filling in the center, and pinch the edges to seal.
  4. Fry the hotteok: Heat a skillet or frying pan over medium heat and add a small amount of oil. Place the filled dough balls in the pan and press them down gently with a spatula. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy. Make sure to flip the pancakes carefully, as the filling can spill out.
  5. Serve: Remove the hotteok from the pan and let them cool slightly before serving. The inside will be gooey and sweet, while the outside is crisp and golden.

These sweet pancakes are a warm, comforting treat perfect for cold weather or as a dessert after a meal.

3. Odeng (Fish Cake Skewers)

Odeng, or fish cakes on skewers, is a popular snack found at many Korean street food stalls. These skewers are served with a simple broth and are often enjoyed during the chilly winter months. They’re light, savory, and a great way to get a taste of authentic Korean street food.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pack of Korean fish cakes (odeng)
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, bring the water or chicken broth to a boil. Add soy sauce, sugar, salt, and green onions. Stir to combine.
  2. Cook the fish cakes: Cut the fish cakes into bite-sized pieces if necessary. Add them to the broth and cook for about 5-7 minutes until heated through.
  3. Skewer the fish cakes: Once the fish cakes are cooked, skewer them with wooden sticks. If you prefer, drizzle with sesame oil for extra flavor.
  4. Serve: Serve the skewers hot with the flavorful broth. The broth is typically served in small cups alongside the skewers for dipping.

Odeng is a warm, satisfying snack that’s perfect for any occasion. The fish cakes soak up the savory broth and provide a tender bite, making it a comfort food for many.

4. Bulgogi (Korean BBQ Beef)

Bulgogi, or Korean BBQ beef, is a savory dish that’s enjoyed both as a street food snack and as a main course. This marinated beef is grilled or stir-fried, and it has a rich, umami flavor that’s irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (optional, for spice)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the beef: In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, green onions, rice vinegar, and gochujang (if using). Add the thinly sliced beef and mix to ensure the beef is coated evenly. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight for maximum flavor.
  2. Cook the beef: Heat a grill or a large skillet over medium-high heat. If using a skillet, add a little sesame oil to prevent sticking. Add the marinated beef and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes until the beef is cooked through and caramelized.
  3. Serve: Garnish with sesame seeds and serve with steamed rice and vegetables, or enjoy as part of a larger meal with banchan (Korean side dishes).

Bulgogi is flavorful, juicy, and tender, and it’s a great dish to make at home when you want to experience the rich flavors of Korean BBQ without the need for a grill.

5. Bungeoppang (Fish-Shaped Pastry)

Bungeoppang is a popular Korean street food that consists of a fish-shaped pastry filled with sweet red bean paste. It’s crispy on the outside and sweet on the inside, making it the perfect snack for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sweet red bean paste (anko)

Instructions:

  1. Make the batter: In a large bowl, mix together the flour, baking powder, sugar, milk, egg, and vanilla extract to form a smooth batter.
  2. Prepare the mold: If you have a fish-shaped mold, preheat it according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If not, you can use a regular waffle iron or griddle.
  3. Cook the bungeoppang: Lightly grease the mold with butter or cooking spray. Spoon a small amount of batter into the mold, then add a dollop of red bean paste. Cover the paste with more batter, then close the mold. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.
  4. Serve: Carefully remove the bungeoppang from the mold and let it cool slightly before serving.

This sweet treat is fun to make and eat, with its crispy exterior and sweet filling. It’s perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth or for sharing with friends.

Conclusion

Korean street food is a delicious and diverse part of the country’s culinary landscape. Whether you’re craving a spicy snack, a sweet treat, or something savory, there’s a Korean street food recipe that will satisfy your taste buds. From the chewy rice cakes of tteokbokki to the sweet fish-shaped pastries of bungeoppang, you can now bring these iconic street foods into your kitchen and enjoy them at home.

The best part? These dishes are incredibly adaptable, and you can customize them to your liking with different fillings, toppings, and spice levels. So next time you’re in the mood for something flavorful and unique, why not try your hand at making these Korean street food favorites?
