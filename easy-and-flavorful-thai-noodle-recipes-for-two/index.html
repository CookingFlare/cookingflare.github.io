Easy and Flavorful Thai Noodle Recipes for Two


If you’re craving the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen, you’re in the right place! Thai noodle dishes are not only delicious, but they’re also incredibly versatile, allowing you to customize ingredients based on your preferences. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of easy and flavorful Thai noodle recipes perfect for two servings. From pad Thai to spicy basil noodles, let’s dive into these mouthwatering dishes that bring a taste of Thailand to your home.

The Essentials of Thai Noodles

Before we get into the recipes, let’s take a moment to discuss the types of noodles commonly used in Thai cooking. Understanding these will help you choose the right kind for each dish.

Types of Thai Noodles

  1. Rice Noodles: These are perhaps the most iconic Thai noodles, often used in dishes like pad Thai and drunken noodles. They come in various widths, from thin vermicelli to wide flat noodles.

  2. Egg Noodles: Commonly used in stir-fried dishes, egg noodles have a rich flavor and chewy texture. They work well in recipes like pad see ew.

  3. Soba Noodles: Made from buckwheat, soba noodles offer a nutty flavor and are great for cold salads or soups.

  4. Udon Noodles: Thick and chewy, these Japanese noodles can also be used in Thai-inspired dishes, providing a unique twist.

Key Ingredients

Thai cooking emphasizes fresh ingredients and bold flavors. Here are some staples you’ll want to have on hand:

  • Soy Sauce: A key seasoning that adds depth and umami.
  • Fish Sauce: Essential for authentic Thai flavor, it adds a salty, savory taste.
  • Oyster Sauce: Provides sweetness and richness to stir-fried dishes.
  • Chili Paste: For those who love heat, chili paste adds spice and flavor.
  • Fresh Herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, and mint are commonly used to brighten dishes.
  • Lime Juice: Fresh lime juice adds acidity and balances flavors.

Now that you have an overview of the noodles and essential ingredients, let’s jump into our delicious recipes!

Recipe 1: Classic Pad Thai for Two

Ingredients

  • 4 oz rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup peanuts, crushed
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in hot water for about 20 minutes until they are soft. Drain and set aside.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.

  3. Add the Egg: Push the garlic to one side of the pan and pour the beaten egg into the other side. Scramble the egg until fully cooked.

  4. Combine Ingredients: Add the soaked noodles to the pan, along with the bean sprouts, fish sauce, sugar, lime juice, and chili paste (if using). Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated and heated through.

  5. Serve: Divide the pad Thai into two bowls and top with crushed peanuts, chopped green onions, and fresh cilantro. Enjoy!

Recipe 2: Spicy Basil Noodles for Two

Ingredients

  • 4 oz egg noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Noodles: Boil the egg noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  2. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Then, add the sliced bell pepper, broccoli, and mushrooms. Stir-fry until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Add Noodles and Sauce: Toss in the cooked noodles, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chili paste (if using). Stir until everything is evenly mixed and heated through.

  4. Finish with Basil: Remove the pan from heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves. The residual heat will wilt the basil.

  5. Serve: Plate the spicy basil noodles and enjoy the burst of flavors!

Recipe 3: Drunken Noodles (Pad Kee Mao) for Two

Ingredients

  • 4 oz wide rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced (red and green)
  • 1 cup sliced chicken or tofu
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the wide rice noodles in hot water until softened, about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. Cook the Chicken or Tofu: In a large pan or wok, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant. Add the chicken or tofu and stir-fry until cooked through.

  3. Add Vegetables: Toss in the bell peppers and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.

  4. Mix in the Noodles and Sauce: Add the soaked noodles to the pan, followed by the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir-fry until the noodles are evenly coated and heated through.

  5. Finish with Basil: Remove from heat and stir in a generous handful of fresh Thai basil leaves.

  6. Serve: Dish out the drunken noodles and serve with lime wedges on the side for a zesty kick.

Recipe 4: Coconut Curry Noodles for Two

Ingredients

  • 4 oz rice noodles
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, snow peas)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the Noodles: Prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Set aside.

  2. Make the Curry Base: In a pan, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant. Stir in the red curry paste and cook for another minute.

  3. Add Coconut Milk: Pour in the coconut milk and stir until combined. Bring to a gentle simmer.

  4. Add Vegetables: Add the mixed vegetables and cook until they are tender-crisp.

  5. Combine with Noodles: Add the cooked noodles to the pan, along with soy sauce. Toss to combine and heat through.

  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and enjoy the creamy, fragrant coconut curry noodles.

Tips for Perfecting Thai Noodles

  • Fresh Ingredients: Use fresh vegetables and herbs for the best flavor. Thai cuisine relies heavily on the freshness of ingredients.

  • Adjust Spice Levels: Customize the spiciness of each dish according to your taste. Add more chili paste or fresh chilies for extra heat.

  • Experiment with Proteins: Feel free to substitute chicken, shrimp, or tofu in these recipes. Each protein brings its unique flavor and texture.

  • Presentation Matters: A little garnish can go a long way! Fresh herbs, lime wedges, and crushed nuts not only enhance flavor but also make your dish look appealing.

Conclusion

These easy and flavorful Thai noodle recipes are perfect for a cozy dinner for two. With a little preparation and the right ingredients, you can create authentic Thai dishes right in your own kitchen. Whether you opt for the classic pad Thai or venture into the spicy world of drunken noodles, each dish promises a delightful explosion of flavors. So grab your ingredients, fire up the stove, and enjoy a culinary journey to Thailand without leaving your home! Happy cooking!
