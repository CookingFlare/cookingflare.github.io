The Ultimate Paleo BBQ Recipes for a Summer Feast


Summer is here, and there’s no better way to celebrate the season than with a sizzling barbecue packed with flavors, textures, and ingredients your friends and family will love. For those who follow the paleo lifestyle, a barbecue feast offers the perfect opportunity to create dishes that are nutrient-dense, satisfying, and free of processed ingredients. In this post, we’ll explore a collection of paleo BBQ recipes that will transform your summer cookout into an unforgettable event. From smoky grilled meats to crisp, refreshing sides and uniquely delicious sauces, this menu has everything you need to master a paleo summer feast.

Smoked Paprika Grilled Chicken Drumsticks

Grilled chicken is a barbecue classic, and with a few paleo-friendly tweaks, you can elevate this humble dish into a showstopper. Smoked paprika adds a rich, smoky flavor, while garlic and herbs complement the savory profile perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of chicken drumsticks
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, combine smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried thyme, olive oil, salt, and black pepper. Mix until it forms a paste.
  2. Season the Drumsticks: Rub the seasoning paste generously all over the chicken drumsticks. Place them in a sealable bag or container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or up to overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high and brush the grates with oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Grill the Drumsticks: Place the drumsticks on the grill and cook for 20–25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is crispy and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Finish with Lemon: Squeeze lemon juice over the drumsticks right before serving for a burst of fresh flavor.

Honey-Lime Grilled Shrimp Skewers

These shrimp skewers are light, refreshing, and perfectly suited for the paleo lifestyle. The combination of tangy lime and sweet honey adds a tropical twist while enhancing the natural sweetness of the shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons raw honey
  • Juice and zest of 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon coconut aminos
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon avocado oil
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Directions:

  1. Mix the Marinade: In a bowl, whisk together honey, lime juice, lime zest, coconut aminos, minced garlic, and avocado oil.
  2. Marinate the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  3. Thread the Skewers: If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 20 minutes. Thread the marinated shrimp onto the skewers.
  4. Grill the Skewers: Heat the grill to medium-high. Cook the shrimp skewers for 2–3 minutes per side or until the shrimp turn pink and opaque.
  5. Garnish: Sprinkle fresh cilantro over the skewers before serving.

Sweet Potato and Bacon Salad

Potato salad is a barbecue staple, but for a paleo twist, we’re using nutrient-rich sweet potatoes instead of regular potatoes. Add crispy bacon and a creamy, dairy-free avocado dressing for a side dish that’s both hearty and refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 slices of paleo-friendly bacon
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Sweet Potatoes: Boil the diced sweet potatoes in salted water until tender but not mushy, about 10–12 minutes. Drain and let cool.
  2. Cook the Bacon: In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove and crumble into small pieces. Reserve some of the bacon fat for the dressing.
  3. Make the Dressing: Blend avocado, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and a spoonful of bacon fat until smooth. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Assemble the Salad: In a large bowl, toss the sweet potatoes with the bacon bits, sliced green onions, and creamy avocado dressing. Chill for 30 minutes before serving.

Herb-Crusted Grilled Cauliflower Steaks

Vegetarians at your barbecue won’t feel left out with these flavor-packed cauliflower steaks. Seasoned generously with fresh herbs and spices, they’re hearty enough to rival any meat dish on the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Cauliflower: Slice the cauliflower vertically into thick “steaks,” aiming for pieces about 1 inch thick.
  2. Season the Steaks: Brush both sides of each cauliflower steak with olive oil and sprinkle the spices evenly.
  3. Grill the Cauliflower: Heat the grill to medium and place the cauliflower steaks directly on the grates. Cook for 4–5 minutes per side until slightly charred and tender.
  4. Serve: Garnish with additional parsley or rosemary for an extra burst of flavor.

Classic Paleo BBQ Sauce

No barbecue is complete without a smoky, tangy sauce to complement grilled meats and veggies. This paleo BBQ sauce is free of refined sugars and artificial ingredients but doesn’t compromise on flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tomato sauce (unsweetened and paleo-friendly)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut aminos
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons raw honey
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a saucepan, whisk together all ingredients over medium heat.
  2. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Cool and Store: Allow the sauce to cool before serving. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

Grilled Pineapple with Coconut Cream

End your paleo barbecue on a sweet note with this tropical dessert. Grilled pineapple caramelizes beautifully, while whipped coconut cream adds a luscious, dairy-free topping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple, sliced into rings
  • 1 cup chilled coconut cream
  • 2 tablespoons raw honey
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Coconut Cream: Using a chilled mixing bowl, whip the coconut cream with honey, vanilla, and cinnamon until light and fluffy. Keep refrigerated until ready to serve.
  2. Grill the Pineapple: Place the pineapple rings on the grill over medium heat and cook for 2–3 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the pineapple softens slightly.
  3. Serve: Top each pineapple ring with a dollop of coconut cream and an extra sprinkle of cinnamon.

Tips for Paleo Barbecuing Success

  1. Choose Quality Ingredients: Opt for grass-fed meats, wild-caught seafood, organic vegetables, and other unprocessed foods for the best flavor and nutritional benefits.
  2. Use the Right Tools: Aluminum trays, skewers, and meat thermometers can simplify grilling and ensure your dishes cook evenly.
  3. Balance Flavors: Pair savory grilled meats with refreshing salads and tangy sauces to create a balanced menu.
  4. Keep It Simple: Let the natural flavors of your ingredients shine by avoiding overly complex recipes.
  5. Embrace Variety: Offer a mix of proteins, vegetables, and sides to cater to different tastes and dietary needs.

A paleo barbecue can be the highlight of your summer gatherings, even for those who don’t strictly follow the lifestyle. The recipes above showcase how delicious and satisfying healthy eating can be—especially when shared around the grill with loved ones. Fire up your barbecue, and let these dishes take center stage at your next summer feast!
