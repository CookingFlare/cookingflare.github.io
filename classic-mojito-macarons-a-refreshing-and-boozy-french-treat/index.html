Classic Mojito Macarons: A Refreshing and Boozy French Treat


Are you a fan of macarons? How about mojitos? Well, get ready to have your taste buds tantalized with this unique twist on a classic French treat. Introducing Classic Mojito Macarons, a refreshing and boozy dessert that combines the elegance of French baking with the zesty flavors of a classic mojito.

Ingredients

For the macaron shells:

  • 150g almond flour
  • 150g powdered sugar
  • 110g egg whites (aged for at least 24 hours)
  • 30g granulated sugar
  • Green food coloring (optional)

For the mojito buttercream filling:

  • 150g unsalted butter, softened
  • 250g powdered sugar
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons white rum
  • Fresh mint leaves, finely chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a food processor, combine the almond flour and powdered sugar. Pulse until well combined and then sift the mixture into a large bowl. Set aside.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the aged egg whites until foamy. Gradually add the granulated sugar and continue whisking until stiff peaks form. At this stage, you can add a few drops of green food coloring if desired.

  4. Gently fold the almond flour mixture into the egg whites until fully incorporated. Be careful not to overmix as this can deflate the batter.

  5. Transfer the batter into a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Pipe small circles onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each macaron shell.

  6. Tap the baking sheet firmly on the counter to remove any air bubbles. Let the macarons sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to form a skin. This will help create the characteristic smooth top.

  7. Bake the macarons for 15-18 minutes, or until they are set and easily lift off the parchment paper. Remove from the oven and let them cool completely on a wire rack.

  8. While the macarons are cooling, prepare the mojito buttercream filling. In a mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar and continue beating until light and fluffy.

  9. Add the lime zest, lime juice, and white rum to the buttercream. Mix until well combined and smooth.

  10. Once the macarons have cooled, pair them up according to size. Spoon a dollop of the mojito buttercream onto the flat side of one shell and sandwich it with another shell.

  11. Optional: Roll the edges of the macarons in finely chopped fresh mint leaves for an extra burst of minty flavor and a beautiful presentation.

  12. Place the filled macarons in an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 24 hours to allow the flavors to meld together.

  13. Serve chilled and enjoy these delightful Classic Mojito Macarons with a refreshing mojito cocktail or simply on their own.

Conclusion

Classic Mojito Macarons are a perfect blend of elegance and freshness. With their delicate almond shells and zesty mojito buttercream filling, these macarons are sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you’re hosting a summer soirée or simply craving a taste of something unique, give these boozy French treats a try. Cheers to indulgence and bon appétit!
