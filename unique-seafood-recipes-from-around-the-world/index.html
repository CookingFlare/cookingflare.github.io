Unique Seafood Recipes from Around the World


Seafood has long captivated food lovers around the globe with its diverse textures, flavors, and cooking styles. From the icy fjords of Scandinavia to the sun-soaked coasts of Southeast Asia, unique seafood dishes bring vibrant culture and history right to your plate. Today, we journey through distinctive recipes, each telling a flavorful story, and offer you the chance to recreate these globally inspired seafood delights in your own kitchen.

Nordic Delight: Gravlax with Dill Mustard Sauce

The Nordic countries, known for their pure waters and cold climates, have perfected the art of preserving seafood. Gravlax is a classic Scandinavian dish featuring cured salmon that is both simple to prepare and remarkably flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh salmon fillet, skin on
  • 1/4 cup coarse sea salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • A handful of fresh dill, chopped

For the Dill Mustard Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh dill

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cure Mix: Combine sea salt, sugar, and black pepper in a bowl.
  2. Cure the Salmon: Place half the dill on a large piece of plastic wrap. Lay the salmon on top, skin side down. Rub the cure mix thoroughly over the salmon, cover with the remaining dill, wrap tightly, and place in a dish.
  3. Refrigerate: Place a weight on top (e.g., a plate with cans) and refrigerate for 24-48 hours, turning every 12 hours.
  4. Make the Sauce: Whisk together mustard, sugar, and vinegar. Slowly drizzle in oil until emulsified. Stir in dill.
  5. Serve: Slice thinly and drizzle with dill mustard sauce or serve alongside rye bread and lemon wedges.

Why it stands out: Gravlax highlights the nuanced balance of salt and sweetness, giving the salmon a luscious texture that’s perfect as an appetizer.

West African Treasure: Nigerian Pepper Soup with Catfish

Spicy, aromatic, and warming, Nigerian pepper soup is a beloved dish across West Africa. Using fresh catfish adored for its delicate flavor, this hearty soup is both medicinal and soulful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh catfish, cleaned and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups water or fish stock
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped (adjust to heat preference)
  • 1 tbsp calabash nutmeg (or substitute with regular nutmeg)
  • 2 tbsp ground pepper soup spices (a blend of alligator pepper, cloves, and negro pepper)
  • 4-5 sprigs scent leaves or fresh basil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil Aromatics: In a large pot, combine water or fish stock, onion, scotch bonnet peppers, and pepper soup spices. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add Catfish: Add fish chunks, reduce heat, and let simmer for about 15-20 minutes until the fish is cooked through but still tender.
  3. Season and Finish: Add salt and scent leaves or basil. Simmer for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Serve Hot: Traditionally served alone or with boiled yams or plantains.

Experience: This spicy infusion delivers layers of heat and earthiness, ideal for chilly evenings or when craving a bold, comforting meal.

Mediterranean Elegance: Spanish Zarzuela de Mariscos (Seafood Stew)

Zarzuela de Mariscos is a luxurious Spanish seafood stew combining a medley of fresh seafood with tomatoes, garlic, and fragrant herbs — a colorful, hearty dish from Catalonia.

Ingredients:

  • 200g mussels, cleaned
  • 200g clams, cleaned
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g firm white fish, cut into chunks
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, chopped (or 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Base: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and red bell pepper, sauté till softened.
  2. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Stir in tomatoes and smoked paprika. Cook for 10 minutes to develop the sauce.
  3. Add Wine and Simmer: Pour in white wine and simmer for another 10 minutes, reducing slightly.
  4. Add Seafood: Add fish chunks first and cook for 5 minutes. Then add shrimp, mussels, and clams. Cover and cook until shellfish open (discard any unopened).
  5. Season and Garnish: Season with salt and pepper, sprinkle with fresh parsley.
  6. Serve: Accompany with crusty bread or saffron rice.

Why try it: Zarzuela beautifully showcases how diverse seafood, herbs, and wine create complex yet accessible Mediterranean flavors.

East Asia’s Comfort: Korean Spicy Braised Mackerel (Godeungeo Jorim)

In Korea, mackerel is a popular fish prized for its rich fatty texture. Braised in a spicy, sweet soy sauce-based sauce, Godeungeo Jorim is a comforting staple that’s perfect with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole mackerels, cleaned and cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1-inch ginger, minced
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Sauce: Mix soy sauce, gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, garlic, and ginger in a bowl.
  2. Layer Ingredients: Lay half the onions in a wide pan, place mackerel pieces on top, pour the sauce over, and add water.
  3. Simmer: Cover and simmer on medium-low for 20 minutes until mackerel is cooked and sauce thickens.
  4. Add Garnish: Drizzle sesame oil and garnish with green onions before serving.
  5. Serve: Traditionally paired with steamed rice and side dishes (banchan).

Flavor profile: This dish balances an umami kick with delicate sweetness and spice, offering a uniquely Korean comfort food experience.

Pacific Island Charm: Tahitian Poisson Cru (Raw Fish Salad)

Tahiti’s Poisson Cru is a refreshing dish based on raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk, infused with fresh vegetables — a vibrant expression of island life.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh firm white fish (like tuna or mahi-mahi), diced
  • 1 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate Fish: Place the diced fish in a bowl, cover with lime juice, and refrigerate for 10-15 minutes until fish turns opaque.
  2. Mix Vegetables: In a separate bowl, combine coconut milk, cucumber, tomatoes, carrot, and onion.
  3. Combine: Drain the fish slightly (keep some lime juice for tang), add to the coconut milk and vegetable mix, salt to taste.
  4. Chill and Serve: Let it chill for 10 minutes and serve as a light meal or appetizer.

Why it’s special: Poisson Cru celebrates fresh, natural flavors with bright acidity balanced by creamy coconut — a tropical seafood masterpiece.

South American Heat: Brazilian Moqueca (Fish Stew)

Originating from Bahia, Brazil, Moqueca is a spicy and aromatic fish stew with African and Portuguese influences, featuring coconut milk, palm oil, and fresh coriander.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb firm white fish fillets (cod or snapper), cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp dendê oil (palm oil) or substitute with olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or yellow), sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 medium ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Layer Vegetables: In a deep skillet, heat oil. Layer half the onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes.
  2. Add Fish: Season fish with salt and lime juice, place on top of vegetables, and cover with remaining vegetables.
  3. Cook: Pour coconut milk over, cover, and simmer gently for 20-25 minutes.
  4. Add Herbs: Sprinkle with cilantro before serving.
  5. Serve: Traditionally served with rice and farofa (toasted manioc flour).

What it offers: Moqueca’s richness and layered spices transport you to the vibrant Brazilian coast, capturing the essence of tropical comfort food.

Bringing Global Seas to Your Table

These unique seafood recipes open a window into distant shores through taste. Whether you’re craving the smoky cure of northern gravlax, the fiery warmth of African pepper soup, or the tropical freshness of Tahitian poisson cru, each recipe provides a gateway to cultural richness and delicious complexity. Seafood, when approached with respect for local traditions and ingredients, becomes a universal language of culinary artistry.

Embark on your seafood adventure, experiment boldly, and above all, savor the astonishing variety of the world’s ocean bounty. Every bite is a story, every dish a celebration.
