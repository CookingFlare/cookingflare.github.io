Baking with Fresh Peaches: Celebrating Summer's Juicy Fruit


Summer is the peak season for fresh peaches, and what better way to celebrate this sweet and juicy fruit than by baking with it? In this post, we’ll explore the world of peach baking, from classic peach cobblers to innovative peach upside-down cakes. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, we’ll guide you through the process of transforming fresh peaches into a delightful dessert.

Peach Basics

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s talk about the importance of choosing the right peaches. When selecting peaches, look for those that are heavy for their size, have a sweet aroma, and a slight give when pressed. Avoid peaches with soft spots, bruises, or a sour smell.

Classic Peach Cobbler

A classic peach cobbler is a quintessential summer dessert. The combination of juicy peaches, crispy biscuit topping, and sweet vanilla ice cream is a match made in heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh peaches, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup biscuit dough (homemade or store-bought)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine sliced peaches, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until the peaches are well coated.
  3. Transfer the peach mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  4. Drizzle the melted butter over the peaches.
  5. Roll out the biscuit dough to a thickness of about 1/4 inch. Place the dough on top of the peaches, pressing the edges to seal.
  6. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the biscuit is golden brown.
  7. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream.

Peach Upside-Down Cake

Peach upside-down cake is a show-stopping dessert that’s perfect for special occasions. The caramelized peaches on the bottom of the cake add a depth of flavor and texture that’s hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh peaches, sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine sliced peaches, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. Mix until the peaches are well coated.
  3. In a separate mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Pour the batter over the peach mixture in a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  7. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes.
  9. Invert the cake onto a serving plate and serve warm.

Peach Crumble Bars

Peach crumble bars are a twist on the classic peach cobbler. The addition of a crunchy oat topping takes this dessert to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh peaches, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine sliced peaches, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until the peaches are well coated.
  3. Transfer the peach mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  4. In a separate mixing bowl, combine rolled oats, brown sugar, and cold butter. Mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Spread the crumb mixture evenly over the peach mixture.
  6. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the crumb topping is golden brown.
  7. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream.

Conclusion

Baking with fresh peaches is a delightful way to celebrate summer’s sweet and juicy fruit. Whether you’re a classic cobbler fan or looking to try something new, these recipes are sure to satisfy your peach cravings. So go ahead, get baking, and enjoy the sweet taste of summer!
