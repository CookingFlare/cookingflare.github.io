How to Make Baozi (Chinese Steamed Buns) at Home


Baozi, also known as Chinese steamed buns, are a beloved staple in Chinese cuisine, enjoyed for their pillowy texture and flavorful fillings. These delicious dumplings can be filled with anything from tender meats to vibrant vegetables, making them incredibly versatile and satisfying. Whether served as breakfast, lunch, or as a snack alongside tea, their soft, fluffy exterior and savory fillings are sure to win over your taste buds.

Making baozi at home might seem daunting at first, but with a bit of practice and patience, you’ll soon be crafting these delectable buns like a seasoned pro. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to make baozi from scratch, providing tips on dough preparation, creating perfect fillings, and mastering the steaming process. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make baozi, you’ll require two main components: the dough and the filling. Here’s what you’ll need to assemble the perfect baozi.

For the Dough:

  • All-purpose flour (3 cups): Provides structure and softness to the baozi dough.
  • Instant yeast (1 packet or 2¼ tsp): Helps the dough rise and creates a fluffy texture.
  • Sugar (2 tbsp): Gives the dough a subtle sweetness that complements the savory fillings.
  • Milk (lukewarm, about 1 cup): Adds richness to the dough while keeping it soft and pliable.
  • Vegetable oil (2 tbsp): Incorporates moisture and prevents the dough from drying.
  • Salt (½ tsp): Enhances the flavor of the dough.

For a Basic Pork Filling:

  • Ground pork (300g): The base meat for classic baozi filling.
  • Green onions (2 finely chopped): Bring freshness and a hint of spice.
  • Ginger (1 tsp, finely grated): Adds warm, aromatic notes.
  • Soy sauce (2 tbsp): Provides an umami boost.
  • Oyster sauce (1 tbsp): Enhances the richness of the pork mixture.
  • Sesame oil (1 tsp): Adds nuttiness to the filling.
  • Cornstarch (1 tbsp): Helps bind the filling together.
  • Salt and pepper (to taste): Balances and heightens the overall flavor.

Feel free to experiment with vegetarian fillings—such as mushrooms, cabbage, carrots, or tofu—for a plant-based alternative!

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Baozi

1. Prepare the Dough

The dough is the foundation of baozi, so it’s critical to get it just right. Follow these steps for a smooth and airy dough:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, mix the instant yeast with a few tablespoons of warm milk and a pinch of sugar. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until bubbles form, signaling that the yeast has been activated.

  2. Mix the ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, and yeast mixture. Gradually add the lukewarm milk and vegetable oil while stirring. Mix until you form a shaggy dough.

  3. Knead the dough: Transfer the dough onto a clean, lightly floured surface. Knead for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth, elastic, and tacky but not sticky. If necessary, add a little more flour to prevent sticking.

  4. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1½ to 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

2. Make the Filling

While the dough is rising, prepare your filling. If you’re making the classic pork filling:

  1. Combine ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, add ground pork, chopped green onions, grated ginger, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly until well combined.

  2. Adjust seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. (Keep in mind that raw pork should not be eaten; frying a small test piece to check flavor is highly recommended.)

  3. Chill the filling: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to use. Chilling helps the mixture firm up, making it easier to handle when forming the baozi.

3. Assemble the Baozi

Now comes the fun part—assembling your baozi!

  1. Divide the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to your work surface and divide it into 12-16 equal portions. Roll each portion into a smooth ball.

  2. Shape the wrappers: Flatten each ball with your hands or a rolling pin into thin rounds, about 4-5 inches in diameter. Make the edges slightly thinner than the center to ensure even folding.

  3. Add the filling: Spoon 1-2 tablespoons of filling into the center of each wrapper. Be careful not to overfill, as this may cause the baozi to tear during cooking.

  4. Fold the baozi: Bring the edges of the wrapper together over the filling and pleat them, pinching gently at the top to seal. This creates the iconic shape of baozi. Don’t worry if your folds aren’t perfect—it takes practice!

4. Steam the Baozi

Steaming baozi is what ultimately gives them their fluffy texture. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Prepare your steamer: Fill a pot or wok with water and bring it to a boil. Line the steamer baskets with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking.

  2. Place the baozi: Arrange the buns in the steamer, ensuring there is space between each to allow for expansion during cooking.

  3. Steam the buns: Cover the steamer with its lid and reduce the heat to medium. Steam for 12-15 minutes, or until the buns are cooked through and puffed up.

  4. Rest before serving: Turn off the heat and let the buns sit in the steamer for 2 minutes before removing them. This prevents them from collapsing due to sudden temperature changes.

Tips for Perfect Baozi

  • Use room-temperature ingredients: This helps the dough rise properly.
  • Don’t overcrowd the steamer: Give the buns space to expand without sticking to each other.
  • Seal tightly: Ensure the top of each bun is pinched securely to avoid filling leaks.
  • Experiment with fillings: From barbecued pork (char siu) to red bean paste or even spicy chicken, the possibilities are endless.

Serving Baozi

Baozi are best enjoyed fresh off the steamer, warm and fluffy. Serve them alongside a side of vinegar dipping sauce, soy sauce, or chili oil for an added flavor kick. Pair with a bowl of soup or tea for a truly comforting meal.

Any leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, simply steam them again to restore their pillowy softness.

Final Thoughts

Making baozi at home is a rewarding culinary adventure that connects you to rich Chinese traditions. While it may take a bit of practice to perfect the dough and pleating technique, the end result is well worth the effort. The combination of fluffy, steamed buns and mouthwatering homemade fillings is truly irresistible.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to turn your kitchen into a baozi-making haven! With this detailed guide and some patience, you’ll soon be serving up steamed buns that rival those from the best dim sum restaurants. Happy cooking!
