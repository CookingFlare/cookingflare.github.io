Traditional Persian Dishes: 6 Recipes for a Feast


Persian cuisine is known for its rich flavors, aromatic spices, and vibrant colors. It’s a beautiful tapestry woven from centuries of culture, showcasing ingredients that tell a story of its heritage. In this post, we’ll explore six traditional Persian dishes that are sure to impress your family and friends, transforming any gathering into a grand feast.

1. Chelo Kebabs

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg of ground lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, grated
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon sumac
  • 1 tablespoon saffron, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Basmati rice (for serving)
  • Fresh herbs (mint, parsley, and dill) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine the ground meat, grated onion, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, turmeric, and sumac. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Shape the Kebabs: Wet your hands and take a handful of the meat mixture. Shape it onto skewers, forming elongated patties. Press them firmly to ensure they hold their shape during cooking.
  3. Marinate: Brush the kebabs with olive oil and let them marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Cook the Kebabs: Preheat a grill or BBQ to medium-high heat. Grill the kebabs for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until they are evenly cooked and have a nice char.
  5. Serve: Fluff the basmati rice with a fork and spoon onto a platter. Arrange the kebabs on top, drizzle with saffron water, and garnish with fresh herbs.

2. Fesenjan (Pomegranate Walnut Stew)

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken thighs (or duck)
  • 2 cups walnuts, finely ground
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cups pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pomegranate seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Onions: In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Brown the Chicken: Add the chicken thighs to the pot, browning them on all sides. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Add Walnuts: Stir in the ground walnuts and cook for about 5 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Pour in the chicken stock, pomegranate molasses, sugar, and cinnamon. Bring to a simmer and reduce the heat.
  5. Cook Slowly: Let it simmer for at least an hour, stirring occasionally until the chicken is tender and the sauce is thickened.
  6. Serve: Garnish with pomegranate seeds and serve with steamed rice.

3. Tahchin (Saffron Rice Cake)

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons saffron, dissolved in warm water
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or lamb, shredded (optional)
  • 1/2 cup barberries (zereshk), soaked in warm water
  • 1/2 cup slivered almonds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or butter for greasing

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak it for at least 1 hour, then boil in salted water until partially cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Mixture: In a bowl, mix yogurt, eggs, saffron water, and a pinch of salt. Stir in the partially cooked rice until well combined.
  3. Layer the Ingredients: Grease a round baking dish. Spread half of the rice mixture on the bottom and create a layer with shredded chicken or lamb if using. Top with the remaining rice mixture.
  4. Bake: Cover with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 1 hour. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until golden brown on top.
  5. Serve: Let it cool slightly before flipping onto a serving platter. Garnish with soaked barberries and slivered almonds.

4. Ghormeh Sabzi (Herb Stew)

Ingredients:

  • 500 g beef or lamb, cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cups fresh herbs (parsley, cilantro, and fenugreek)
  • 1 cup dried kidney beans, soaked
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 dried limes (limoo amani)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Water for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Meat: In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden. Add the meat and brown.
  2. Add Spices: Stir in the turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Incorporate Herbs: Chop the fresh herbs and add them to the pot. Cook for another 5-10 minutes.
  4. Combine Beans and Limes: Add the soaked kidney beans, dried limes, and enough water to cover the ingredients. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until everything is tender.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with rice, and enjoy the rich, herbaceous flavors.

5. Zereshk Polow (Barberry Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup barberries, rinsed
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron, dissolved in warm water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Slivered almonds or pistachios for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice and soak for at least 1 hour. Boil in salted water until partially cooked, then drain.
  2. Sauté Onions: In a pan, melt butter and sauté sliced onions until golden. Add the barberries, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Cook for a few minutes until the barberries soften.
  3. Combine: Layer half the rice in a pot, then spread the barberry mixture over it, followed by the remaining rice. Drizzle with saffron water.
  4. Steam the Rice: Cover tightly and steam on low heat for about 30 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.
  5. Serve: Fluff the rice gently before serving. Garnish with slivered almonds or pistachios for an added crunch.

6. Baklava (Persian Sweet Pastry)

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (about 450g)
  • 200 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 300 g mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, almonds), finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Nut Filling: In a bowl, mix the chopped nuts with ground cinnamon. Set aside.
  2. Layer the Phyllo: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Brush a baking dish with melted butter. Layer half of the phyllo sheets, brushing each sheet with butter.
  3. Add Nut Mixture: Spread the nut mixture evenly over the layered phyllo. Layer the remaining phyllo sheets on top, again brushing each one with butter.
  4. Cut the Baklava: Before baking, cut the baklava into diamond shapes.
  5. Bake: Bake for about 40-45 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Prepare the Syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, honey, and vanilla. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Pour the syrup over the hot baklava after removing it from the oven.
  7. Cool and Serve: Allow the baklava to cool and absorb the syrup. Serve in pieces and enjoy the sweet, nutty flavors.

Conclusion

Exploring traditional Persian dishes is like embarking on a culinary journey through history and culture. Each recipe carries its own story and character, from the rich, spiced kebabs to the sweet, flaky baklava. These six dishes not only represent the essence of Persian cuisine but also create an atmosphere of warmth, hospitality, and celebration. Whether you are hosting a special occasion or simply want to indulge in a delicious meal, these recipes are sure to bring your table to life. Enjoy your cooking adventure and the delightful flavors of Persia!
