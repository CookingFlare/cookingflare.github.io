Savor the Flavor: 10 Lip-Smacking Recipes for Leftover Shrimp


If you’re a seafood lover, chances are you’ve found yourself with some leftover shrimp from time to time. Rather than letting it go to waste, why not transform those succulent morsels into a mouthwatering meal? In this blog post, we’ll explore 10 delicious recipes that will make you savor every last bite of your leftover shrimp. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Shrimp Scampi Pasta

Start by sautéing some minced garlic and red pepper flakes in olive oil. Add your leftover shrimp and cook until heated through. Toss in cooked pasta, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a sprinkle of parsley. Give it a good stir and serve with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese on top. This classic dish is simple yet bursting with flavor.

2. Shrimp Tacos

In a bowl, mix together mayonnaise, lime juice, and chipotle powder to create a tangy and spicy sauce. Warm up some tortillas and fill them with your leftover shrimp, shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, and a drizzle of the chipotle mayo. These shrimp tacos are perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

3. Shrimp Fried Rice

Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok. Add chopped onions, carrots, and peas, and cook until tender. Push the veggies to one side and crack in a couple of eggs. Scramble them and then mix them with the veggies. Add your leftover shrimp and cooked rice, along with soy sauce and sesame oil. Stir-fry everything together until well combined. This flavorful shrimp fried rice is a great way to use up your leftovers.

4. Shrimp Salad

In a bowl, combine your leftover shrimp with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, sliced cucumbers, and diced avocado. Drizzle with a tangy vinaigrette made from olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and honey. Toss everything together and enjoy a refreshing and light shrimp salad.

5. Shrimp and Grits

Cook some grits according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, cook bacon until crispy, then remove and crumble it. Sauté chopped onions and garlic in the bacon fat, then add your leftover shrimp and cook until heated through. Serve the shrimp and bacon mixture over a bed of creamy grits. This Southern-inspired dish is sure to satisfy your cravings.

6. Shrimp Quesadillas

Spread a layer of shredded cheese on a tortilla and top it with your leftover shrimp, sliced bell peppers, and diced onions. Place another tortilla on top and cook in a skillet until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is crispy. Cut into wedges and serve with salsa and sour cream. These shrimp quesadillas are perfect for a quick and satisfying lunch or dinner.

7. Shrimp Stir-Fry

Heat some vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet. Add diced bell peppers, sliced onions, and minced garlic. Stir-fry until the veggies are tender-crisp. Add your leftover shrimp and a sauce made from soy sauce, honey, and ginger. Cook until the shrimp is heated through and coated in the sauce. Serve over steamed rice for a delicious and healthy meal.

8. Shrimp and Avocado Crostini

Toast some baguette slices until crispy. In a bowl, mash ripe avocados with lime juice, salt, and pepper. Spread the avocado mixture onto the toasted baguette slices. Top with your leftover shrimp and garnish with chopped cilantro or parsley. These elegant shrimp and avocado crostini make for a delightful appetizer or light lunch.

9. Shrimp and Corn Chowder

In a pot, cook diced bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside. In the same pot, sauté diced onions, celery, and carrots until softened. Add diced potatoes, corn kernels, chicken broth, and milk. Bring to a boil and then simmer until the potatoes are tender. Add your leftover shrimp and the cooked bacon. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme. Let it simmer for a few more minutes, and you’ll have a comforting and flavorful shrimp and corn chowder.

10. Shrimp and Spinach Stuffed Mushrooms

Remove the stems from large mushrooms and place them on a baking sheet. In a bowl, mix together cream cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped spinach, and your leftover shrimp. Spoon the mixture into the mushroom caps and sprinkle with breadcrumbs. Bake in the oven until the mushrooms are cooked and the filling is golden and bubbly. These stuffed mushrooms make for an impressive appetizer or a tasty side dish.

And there you have it, 10 lip-smacking recipes that will make you savor the flavor of your leftover shrimp. From pasta to tacos, salads to chowders, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So the next time you find yourself with some leftover shrimp, don’t let it go to waste. Get creative in the kitchen and indulge in these delicious dishes. Happy cooking!
