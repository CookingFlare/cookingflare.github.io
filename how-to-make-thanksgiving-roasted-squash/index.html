How to Make Thanksgiving Roasted Squash


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with family and friends, indulging in delicious food, and celebrating the season. Among the many sides that grace the holiday table, roasted squash stands out as a beautiful, flavorful dish that brings a comforting warmth to any Thanksgiving meal. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, learning how to make roasted squash for Thanksgiving is simple, and the results are truly impressive.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about making the perfect roasted squash, from selecting the right variety to seasoning it just right. By the end of this post, you’ll have a clear understanding of how to prepare and serve this delightful vegetable as part of your Thanksgiving spread.

Why Roasted Squash Makes the Perfect Thanksgiving Side Dish

Before diving into the recipe, it’s worth exploring why roasted squash is such a fantastic addition to your Thanksgiving table.

Versatility

Squash comes in a variety of types and flavors, making it an adaptable dish that can complement many different main courses, such as turkey, ham, or a vegetarian option. From sweet to savory, the natural sweetness of squash pairs beautifully with a range of seasonings, from cinnamon and nutmeg to thyme and garlic. This flexibility makes it a favorite for holiday meals where a mix of tastes is appreciated.

Health Benefits

Beyond its great taste, squash offers numerous health benefits. It is rich in fiber, vitamins A and C, potassium, and antioxidants, making it a nutritious addition to your meal. Squash is also a low-calorie food, so it’s a perfect choice for those looking to maintain a balanced diet while still enjoying the indulgence of Thanksgiving.

Easy Preparation

Roasting squash is a simple and hands-off method of cooking that doesn’t require constant attention. After prepping the squash, all you need to do is toss it in oil and seasoning, then let the oven work its magic. This gives you more time to focus on preparing other dishes or spending time with your loved ones.

Now, let’s get into the step-by-step guide to making the best Thanksgiving roasted squash.

Selecting the Right Squash for Roasting

Choosing the right type of squash is crucial to making a delicious roasted dish. Here are a few popular varieties you can choose from:

Butternut Squash

Butternut squash is one of the most popular choices for roasting. It has a sweet, nutty flavor with a smooth, creamy texture that becomes tender when roasted. The flesh is orange and slightly sweet, which makes it perfect for pairing with savory herbs or even sweet spices like cinnamon and maple syrup.

Acorn Squash

Acorn squash has a mild flavor and slightly firmer texture than butternut squash. It’s also on the sweeter side, but not as rich. The deep green skin and bright orange flesh make it a visually appealing choice for holiday meals.

Kabocha Squash

Kabocha squash, also known as Japanese pumpkin, is known for its dense texture and slightly sweet, earthy flavor. It is often considered one of the richest tasting varieties of squash, and it holds up well when roasted.

Delicata Squash

Delicata squash is a smaller variety with a creamy texture that’s perfect for roasting. It has a mild flavor, similar to that of corn, and its thin skin makes it easy to roast without peeling. This squash variety works great in salads or as a side dish.

Spaghetti Squash

Spaghetti squash is unique in that its flesh falls into spaghetti-like strands when cooked. Though it’s a bit different from other squash varieties, it can be roasted and served as a low-carb alternative to pasta. It has a subtle flavor that pairs well with tangy sauces or garlic butter.

For the purposes of this recipe, we’ll focus on butternut squash, but feel free to experiment with other varieties to suit your taste and style.

Ingredients Needed

To make roasted squash, you don’t need much – just a few simple ingredients will do. Here’s what you’ll need to gather:

  • 1 medium butternut squash (or your preferred variety)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder (optional, for a savory touch)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika (optional, for depth of flavor)
  • Fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary (optional, for added fragrance)
  • 1 tablespoon of maple syrup or honey (optional, for extra sweetness)

These ingredients can be easily found at most grocery stores, and many of them are staples in any kitchen.

Step-by-Step Guide to Roasting Squash for Thanksgiving

Now that you’ve selected your squash and gathered your ingredients, it’s time to begin the roasting process. Let’s go over the steps you’ll need to follow to ensure a perfectly roasted squash every time.

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). This temperature is perfect for roasting squash, as it ensures a crispy exterior and a tender interior.

Step 2: Prepare the Squash

To prepare your squash, start by cutting off both ends. Then, stand the squash upright and use a sharp knife to peel off the skin. Butternut squash has a tough skin, so take your time with this step to avoid any injuries. Once peeled, cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds using a spoon. You can save the seeds for roasting later, or discard them.

After removing the seeds, cut the squash into 1-inch cubes. This size allows the squash to cook evenly and ensures that every piece is perfectly tender.

Step 3: Season the Squash

Transfer the squash cubes to a large mixing bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and toss to coat evenly. Next, sprinkle the squash with salt, pepper, cinnamon, garlic powder, and smoked paprika, if using. Toss again to ensure the seasoning is evenly distributed. If you’d like a touch of sweetness, you can also drizzle the squash with maple syrup or honey at this stage.

You can also add fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme for an aromatic flavor. Gently toss the herbs with the squash, making sure they don’t get too crushed. These will impart a fragrant, earthy aroma as the squash roasts.

Step 4: Arrange on a Baking Sheet

Once your squash is seasoned, arrange the cubes in a single layer on a large baking sheet. Make sure the pieces aren’t overcrowded, as this will allow them to roast more evenly and achieve that coveted crispy texture. If necessary, use two baking sheets to spread the squash out.

Step 5: Roast the Squash

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast the squash for about 25 to 30 minutes, or until it is golden brown and tender. Halfway through the roasting process, use a spatula to flip the squash cubes to ensure even cooking.

You’ll know the squash is done when it’s easily pierced with a fork and has a nice caramelized exterior. If you like a bit more crispiness, you can leave it in for an extra 5–10 minutes.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once the squash is roasted to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Transfer the squash to a serving platter and garnish with additional fresh herbs, if desired. You can also sprinkle a bit more salt and pepper for taste.

Roasted squash is best enjoyed while it’s still warm, but it can also be served at room temperature. Pair it with your Thanksgiving main dishes, such as turkey or a hearty vegetarian main, for a truly satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations for the Best Roasted Squash

To ensure that your roasted squash turns out perfectly every time, here are a few additional tips and variations:

1. Experiment with Different Squash Varieties

As mentioned earlier, different types of squash bring unique flavors and textures to the table. Try mixing and matching varieties for a beautiful and diverse roasted vegetable medley. You could combine butternut, acorn, and delicata squash for a colorful and flavorful dish.

2. Add a Crunchy Topping

For added texture, consider topping your roasted squash with toasted nuts or seeds. Chopped pecans, walnuts, or pumpkin seeds can be sprinkled on top right before serving to add a crunchy element that contrasts nicely with the soft squash.

3. Try Different Seasonings

While cinnamon and garlic are great go-to seasonings for roasted squash, don’t be afraid to get creative. Other spices like cumin, coriander, or ginger can add an exotic flair to your dish. For a spicy kick, you can add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili flakes.

4. Make it a Sweet and Savory Dish

For a more complex flavor profile, you can balance the natural sweetness of the squash with savory ingredients like balsamic vinegar, goat cheese, or caramelized onions. This adds depth and complexity to the dish, making it a standout side.

5. Roast with Other Vegetables

Squash pairs wonderfully with other roasted vegetables, such as carrots, Brussels sprouts, or parsnips. Consider roasting them together for a beautiful and hearty vegetable platter. Toss the vegetables with similar seasonings for a cohesive flavor, or mix things up with different herbs for variety.

Conclusion

Roasted squash is a simple yet elegant dish that elevates any Thanksgiving meal. Whether you choose to make it with butternut squash or explore other varieties, this recipe is sure to become a favorite for your holiday table. The natural sweetness of the squash, combined with savory seasonings and a hint of caramelization, creates a perfect balance of flavors.

By following the steps in this guide, you can easily prepare a delicious roasted squash that will impress your guests and leave them coming back for more. With just a few ingredients and minimal effort, you can create a dish that’s both nutritious and delightful. Enjoy your Thanksgiving meal with this tasty side dish, and don’t forget to share the recipe with friends and family!
