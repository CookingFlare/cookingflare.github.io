Eggless Sweet Potato Casserole: A Perfect Side Dish for Holidays


The holiday season is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in delicious dishes that warm the heart and soul. One dish that never fails to impress is the classic sweet potato casserole. However, for those who follow a vegan or vegetarian diet, or those who have dietary restrictions, traditional sweet potato casseroles with eggs can be off-limits. But fear not, because I have the perfect solution for you - an eggless sweet potato casserole that is just as delectable and satisfying!

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, solid

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.

  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the sweet potato cubes. Cook until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain the sweet potatoes and transfer them to a mixing bowl.

  3. Mash the sweet potatoes with a fork or a potato masher until smooth and no lumps remain. Add the almond milk, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Stir well to combine.

  4. In a separate small bowl, combine the chopped pecans, brown sugar, flour, and solid coconut oil. Mix until the ingredients are well combined and crumbly.

  5. Transfer the sweet potato mixture to the greased baking dish and spread it evenly. Sprinkle the pecan topping over the sweet potato mixture, covering it completely.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is heated through.

  7. Remove the casserole from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish is best enjoyed warm, but leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a sweeter casserole, you can increase the amount of maple syrup or add a sprinkle of brown sugar on top before baking.

  • For a nut-free version, you can omit the pecans or replace them with a different type of nut or seed, such as sunflower seeds or chopped almonds.

  • To make this dish gluten-free, use a gluten-free flour blend or almond flour instead of all-purpose flour.

  • If you want to add a hint of citrus flavor, you can zest an orange and mix it into the sweet potato mixture.

  • This eggless sweet potato casserole pairs perfectly with a dollop of vegan whipped cream or a scoop of dairy-free ice cream.

Enjoy this delicious and vegan-friendly sweet potato casserole as a side dish for your holiday meals. Its creamy texture, warm spices, and crunchy pecan topping make it a crowd-pleaser that everyone will love. Remember, you don’t need eggs to create a mouthwatering dish. Happy cooking and happy holidays!
