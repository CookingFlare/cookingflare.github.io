Nut-Free Dominion: 30 Dominant Nut-Free Recipes for Culinary Supremacy


For those who suffer from nut allergies or choose to avoid nuts in their diet for various reasons, finding delicious and diverse recipes can be a daunting task. Fear not, as we present to you ‘Nut-Free Dominion,’ a collection of 30 utterly scrumptious recipes that will elevate your culinary game while ensuring you can freely indulge in a myriad of flavors without the worry of nut cross-contamination.

  1. Spinach and Artichoke Dip Ingredients:
  • 1 (14 oz) can of artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 1 (10 oz) box of frozen spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1 cup of mayo
  • 1 cup of grated parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of chopped green onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions: Combine all ingredients in a dish and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes or until the dip is hot, bubbly, and slightly golden. Serve with any assortment of fresh vegetables, chips, or crackers.

  1. Avocado and Black Bean Salad Ingredients:
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup of red onion, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions: Mix all ingredients together gently in a large bowl. Allow the flavors to meld together for at least 30 minutes before serving. This nutrient-dense salad is a hearty side dish or can serve as a main course with the addition of grilled chicken or tofu.

  1. Quinoa Veggie Stir Fry Ingredients:
  • 1 cup of cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce or tamari
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions: Heat the olive oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 3-4 minutes or until crisp-tender. Mix in cooked quinoa, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving.

  1. Lemon-Herb Hummus with Pita Chips Ingredients:
  • 1 (15 oz) can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 tsp of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 pita bread, sliced into wedges

Instructions: Pulse chickpeas, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper in a food processor until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as desired. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and arrange pita wedges on a lined baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden and crispy. Serve hummus with pita chips for a delightful appetizer.

  1. Roasted Vegetable Pasta Ingredients:
  • 1 lb of penne pasta
  • 2 zucchi
  • 2 bell peppers, any color
  • 1 red onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 4 tbsp of olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Instructions: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Cut the zucchini, bell peppers, and red onion into bite-sized pieces. In a large bowl, toss vegetables with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Spread vegetables on a lined baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes, or until tender and lightly caramelized. Toss with cooked penne pasta and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.

  1. Vegan Coconut Mango Cheesecake Ingredients:
  • 1 1/2 cups of raw cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours
  • 1 13.5 oz can of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of raw mango, cubed
  • 1/4 cup of coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp of vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp of lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup of cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp of cinnamon
  • Cocoa powder and shredded coconut for garnish

Instructions: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Blend soaked cashews, coconut milk, mango, coconut oil, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and lemon juice in a high-speed blender until smooth. In a separate bowl, whisk together cornstarch, cocoa powder, and cinnamon. Add to the blended mixture and pulse just until combined. Pour batter into a 9-inch springform pan, covering the bottom and the sides. Bake for 35 minutes or until the edges are set but the center is slightly jiggly. Allow to cool completely before chilling for at least 4 hours in the refrigerator. Garnish with cocoa powder and shredded coconut before serving.

  1. Cauliflower Mac and Cheese Ingredients:
  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 cups of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: breadcrumbs, panko, or crushed crackers for topping

Instructions: Steam cauliflower florets until tender, about 10-12 minutes. In a large saucepan, melt butter and whisk in flour. Gradually whisk in milk and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens. Add garlic and cook for another minute. Remove from heat and stir in shredded cheddar cheese until melted and creamy. Season with salt and pepper. Blitz steamed cauliflower in a food processor until it resembles the texture of macaroni. Combine cauliflower and cheese sauce in a large bowl and mix well. Pour into a greased 9x13 baking dish, and optional sprinkle with breadcrumbs, panko, or crushed crackers. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30 minutes or until bubbly and golden.
