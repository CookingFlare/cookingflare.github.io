Brews and Bites: Pairing Craft Beers with Innovative Fusion Dishes


Craft beer and culinary fusion are two of the most exciting movements in the world of food and drink today. As enthusiasts and aficionados of both, we couldn’t help but wonder: what happens when these worlds collide? The result is a vibrant, innovative, and delicious realm of gastronomic adventure. In this post, we’ll explore some enchanting pairings that bring together exquisite craft beers with daring fusion dishes. So, grab a pint, pour a glass, and let’s get started.

1. Mexican-Style Street Tacos with a Citrusy IPA

Everyone loves tacos, but why not give them a twist by pairing them with the refreshing flavors of a citrusy IPA? The Citrusy IPA boasts a delightful balance of hops and bitterness, punctuated by hints of orange, grapefruit, and other fruity notes. To create these mouth-watering Mexican-Style Street Tacos, you’ll need:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Marinated flank steak or chicken
  • Diced onions
  • Cilantro
  • Sliced radishes
  • Diced pineapple
  • Lime juice
  • Salt and pepper

Here’s how to bring them all together:

First, marinate the flank steak or chicken in a blend of lime juice, a touch of chipotle, salt, pepper, and your favorite Mexican spices. Make sure to cover all sides of the meat, and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, but preferably overnight. Cook the meat to your desired temperature, slice, and set it aside.

Now, heat your corn tortillas on a skillet or grill until they become slightly charred. Assemble your tacos by placing the protein, diced onions, radishes, cilantro, and pineapple in separate stacks on each tortilla. Squeeze a little lime juice and a dash of salt, then fold them up to enjoy. The fruity notes and citric punch of the IPA complement the spicy flavors of the tacos, creating a harmonious dance on your palate.

2. Smoked Gouda Mac and Cheese with Saison

Nothing says comfort food quite like a bowl of warm, gooey mac and cheese. But, when you smoke Gouda into the mixture, it takes this classic dish to the next level. And what better pairing than a complex Saison to go with it? The fruity and spicy notes found in Saisons cut through the richness of the mac and cheese, allowing the smoky Gouda to shine.

To prepare this tantalizing fusion, gather these ingredients:

  • Elbow macaroni
  • Smoked Gouda, grated
  • Sharp cheddar, grated
  • Cream cheese
  • Heavy cream
  • Flour
  • Salt and pepper
  • Panko breadcrumbs

Begin by cooking the macaroni according to package instructions. While it’s cooking, melt some butter in a pot and whisk in flour to create a roux. Gradually add in the heavy cream, stirring constantly to avoid any lumps. Once the sauce begins to thicken, add the cream cheese and shredded smoked Gouda. Cook until the cheese has melted, and then mix in the cooked macaroni, cheddar cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Finally, bake the mac and cheese in a greased dish, topped with panko breadcrumbs, for about 15-20 minutes, or until golden and bubbly. The Saison’s subtle citrus and fruity notes are a perfect complement to the smokiness of the cheese.

3. Matcha Green Tea-Brined Pork Belly with a Fruit-forward Blonde Ale

Brining pork belly in matcha green tea before cooking imparts an earthy sweetness to the flesh, while a fruit-forward Blonde Ale balances the rich fat with its refreshing character. This matcha green tea-brined pork belly dish is one-of-a-kind and is sure to impress.

For this recipe, you’ll need:

  • Pork belly
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Matcha green tea powder
  • Soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Citrus fruit juice (orange, lemon, or lime)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Blonde Ale

To begin, create a brine solution with salt, sugar, matcha green tea powder, and soy sauce. Bring this to a boil and let it cool down, then place your pork belly in the mixture and refrigerate for at least 8 hours. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Remove the pork belly from the brine, pat it dry, and place it on a wire rack. Brush the skin with a combination of honey, citrus fruit juice, and vegetable oil. Roast the pork belly in the oven until the skin is crispy and the fat is rendered out. After that, finish it in a hot oven for additional browning. Finally, pair this delectable main course with a fruit-forward Blonde Ale for a truly harmonious match.

4. Korean BBQ Tostada with a Blackberry-Infused Dark Lager

Rethink your tostada game by exploring the robust flavors of Korean BBQ and combine it with the snappy sweetness of a blackberry-infused dark lager for a phenomenal pairing.

Ingredients for the tostada:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Marinated ribeye or pork belly
  • Kimchi
  • Sliced radish
  • Avocado
  • Cilantro
  • Cooked black beans
  • Korean BBQ sauce
  • Sesame seeds
  • Lime wedges

For the beer infusion technique, steep blackberry tea in a dark lager for a few days, then bottle the brew to capture its unique flavor profile.

First, marinate your ribeye or pork belly in a mixture of soy sauce, honey, garlic, and ginger for at least four hours, or preferably overnight. Cook the meat to your desired temperature and slice thinly. Warm your corn tortillas in a skillet, then layer the Korean BBQ-marinated meat, kimchi, radish, avocado, cilantro, cooked black beans, and a drizzle of Korean BBQ sauce. Finish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and a lime wedge. The fruitiness of the blackberry-infused dark lager complements the tangy and sweet flavors of the Korean BBQ tostada without being overpowering.

There you have it: a menu bursting with exciting dishes and beers that are sure to tickle taste buds and impress even the most discerning palates. So, go ahead and embrace the magical world of craft beers and fusion dishes; it’s an adventure worth exploring. Cheers!
