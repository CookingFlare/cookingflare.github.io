Satisfying Hungarian Goulash: 5 Recipes for Comfort Food Lovers


If you’re a fan of hearty and comforting meals, then Hungarian Goulash is sure to be right up your alley. This traditional Hungarian dish is packed with flavors and will warm you up from the inside out. Whether it’s a chilly winter evening or you simply crave something delicious, goulash is the answer. In this blog post, we’ll explore five different recipes for Hungarian Goulash that will satisfy any comfort food lover. Let’s dive in!

Classic Beef Goulash

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons Hungarian paprika
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the beef cubes and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the Hungarian paprika and caraway seeds. Stir well to coat the onions and garlic.
  4. Return the beef to the pot and add the bell peppers, tomatoes, and beef broth. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  6. Serve the goulash hot with some crusty bread or over a bed of fluffy mashed potatoes.

Vegetarian Goulash

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons Hungarian paprika
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, diced
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  2. Stir in the Hungarian paprika and caraway seeds, coating the onions and garlic evenly.
  3. Add the carrots, potatoes, bell peppers, and tomatoes to the pot. Pour in the vegetable broth and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Adjust the seasoning if needed and serve the vegetarian goulash hot. It pairs well with crusty bread or fluffy rice.

  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Sauté until they become translucent.

  2. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic. Sauté until they become translucent.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  6. Serve the chicken goulash hot with some crusty bread or over a bed of fluffy rice.
