How to Make Classic American Ceviche: Seafood Recipe


Ceviche is a delightful and refreshing dish that has captured the hearts and palates of seafood lovers across the United States. This classic American ceviche is a bright, zesty dish that showcases the freshness of the ocean, beautifully complemented by vibrant herbs and spices. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a beach party, or simply craving a light, healthy meal, this ceviche recipe is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the world of ceviche and learn how to prepare this delicious seafood dish step-by-step.

What is Ceviche?

Ceviche is a dish that originated from Latin America, particularly popular in Peru, where it has been prepared for centuries. The traditional preparation involves marinating raw fish or seafood in citrus juices, typically lime or lemon, which “cooks” the fish through a process called denaturation. The acidity of the citrus juice alters the texture and flavor of the seafood, making it tender and infused with a tangy taste. In this American twist on ceviche, we incorporate a variety of ingredients that add complexity and vibrancy to the dish.

Ingredients

To create a classic American ceviche, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Seafood

  • 1 pound fresh white fish (such as cod, halibut, or snapper) or a mix of seafood (shrimp, scallops, and crab)
  • Juice of 4-5 limes (freshly squeezed)
  • Juice of 1 lemon (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Vegetables and Herbs

  • 1 medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, diced (remove seeds for less moisture)
  • 1-2 jalapeños, finely chopped (adjust spice level to your preference)
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 avocado, diced (optional)

Additional Flavorings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin (optional for extra depth)
  • Tortilla chips or plantain chips for serving

Preparing the Seafood

Selecting Fresh Seafood

The key to a successful ceviche is using the freshest seafood available. When choosing your fish or seafood, look for:

  • Freshness: The seafood should have a clean, ocean-like smell. Avoid anything that smells fishy or off-putting.
  • Texture: Fresh fish should feel firm to the touch and have a vibrant color. Avoid dull or discolored pieces.
  • Sustainability: Whenever possible, opt for sustainably sourced seafood to support local fisheries and protect marine ecosystems.

Cutting and Marinating the Seafood

  1. Cut the Seafood: Using a sharp knife, cut your fish or seafood into small, bite-sized cubes (about 1/2-inch pieces). If using shrimp or scallops, make sure to clean and devein them properly.

  2. Marinate: In a glass or ceramic bowl (avoid metal, which can react with the citrus), combine the seafood with lime juice, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well to ensure all pieces are evenly coated. The acidity will begin to “cook” the seafood, which typically takes about 30 minutes to 2 hours, depending on the thickness of the pieces and your desired level of doneness.

Preparing the Vegetables and Herbs

While your seafood is marinating, it’s time to prepare the vegetables and herbs that will add flavor and texture to your ceviche.

  1. Chop the Vegetables: Finely chop the red onion, jalapeños, and tomatoes. If you prefer a milder flavor, soak the chopped onion in cold water for 10-15 minutes to reduce its sharpness.

  2. Prepare the Cilantro: Rinse the cilantro thoroughly to remove any dirt or grit. Pat dry and chop it finely.

  3. Dice the Avocado: If using avocado, cut it into small cubes just before serving to prevent browning.

Combining the Ingredients

After the seafood has marinated to your liking, it’s time to combine all the ingredients into a delicious ceviche mix.

  1. Drain Excess Juice: If there’s an excess of liquid in the bowl after marination, drain some of it out, but retain enough to keep the ceviche moist.

  2. Mix in the Vegetables and Herbs: Add the chopped red onion, jalapeños, tomatoes, and cilantro to the bowl. Gently fold the ingredients together with a spatula or spoon to combine, being careful not to break up the fish pieces.

  3. Season: Add olive oil, black pepper, garlic powder, and cumin (if using). Mix gently to combine all flavors.

  4. Taste and Adjust: Before serving, taste the ceviche and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You may want to add a bit more lime juice, salt, or pepper based on your preference.

Serving Suggestions

Ceviche is best served fresh, immediately after combining all ingredients. Here are some serving suggestions to elevate your ceviche experience:

  • Chilled Bowls: Serve the ceviche in chilled bowls to enhance the refreshing taste.
  • Garnish: Top with additional cilantro or a slice of lime for a beautiful presentation.
  • Accompaniments: Offer tortilla chips or plantain chips on the side for a delightful crunch.
  • Pairing: Pair your ceviche with a light, crisp white wine or a refreshing cocktail, like a mojito or a margarita.

Tips for the Perfect Ceviche

  1. Freshness is Key: Always use the freshest seafood you can find for the best flavor and texture.
  2. Marination Time: Keep a close eye on the marination time. Over-marinating can lead to a mushy texture.
  3. Adjust for Spice: Feel free to adjust the amount of jalapeño based on your spice tolerance. For a milder ceviche, you can omit it or use a milder pepper.
  4. Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to add your twist! Incorporate diced mango or pineapple for a sweet touch or add coconut milk for a creamy variation.

Conclusion

Making classic American ceviche is a delightful culinary adventure that allows you to explore the fresh flavors of the sea. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a vibrant and refreshing dish that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re enjoying it as a light lunch or serving it at a gathering, this ceviche recipe is sure to impress your friends and family. So gather your ingredients, unleash your creativity, and savor the taste of summer with this incredible seafood recipe! Enjoy!
