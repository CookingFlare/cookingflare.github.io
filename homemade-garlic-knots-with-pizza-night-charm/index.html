Homemade Garlic Knots with Pizza Night Charm


When it comes to pizza night, one way to elevate the experience is by adding a show-stopping side that complements the main dish perfectly. Enter homemade garlic knots: soft, pillowy bites of bread coated in decadent garlic butter, adorned with fresh herbs and a sprinkle of cheese. These irresistible treats can transform a casual pizza night into an occasion to remember.

Making garlic knots at home may sound ambitious, but the process is simpler than you might think—and infinitely rewarding. With a few basic ingredients, thoughtful techniques, and a pinch of creativity, you can whip up a batch of garlic knots that delight the senses and bring people together at the dinner table.

Why Garlic Knots Are the Perfect Sidekick

Garlic knots are much more than a side dish; they’re a celebration of comforting flavors. The buttery garlic coating and herbaceous finish bring rich, savory notes that pair beautifully with tangy marinara sauce or a creamy dipping sauce. Plus, whether you’re serving classic cheese pizza, a loaded veggie version, or a meaty, hearty pie, garlic knots complement the depth of flavor without overshadowing the main star.

At the same time, garlic knots are approachable enough for everyone, from picky eaters to adventurous foodies. The sheer simplicity combined with artisan-like charm wins hearts across every table. And there’s something undeniably satisfying about pulling apart a knot and savoring its chewy, garlicky goodness.

The Foundation: Dough Done Right

Homemade dough is the key to garlic knots that boast both a perfect texture and an authentic taste. While store-bought dough is an option for quicker results, crafting your own dough brings a depth of flavor and freshness that can’t be replicated. It also lets you adjust the ingredients to your liking. Here’s our recipe for foolproof garlic knot dough:

Ingredients:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 ¼ cups warm water

Step-by-Step Garlic Knot Dough Preparation

  1. Activate the Yeast
    Begin by combining warm water with sugar and yeast in a small bowl. Let the yeast bloom for 5-10 minutes until foamy. This ensures the yeast is alive and ready to create the perfect rise.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. This step ensures even seasoning and eliminates any clumps in the flour.

  3. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients
    Add the olive oil and bloomed yeast mixture to the dry ingredients. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.

  4. Knead to Perfection
    Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Proper kneading is essential for developing the gluten, which will give the garlic knots their signature chewiness.

  5. Allow to Rise
    Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

Shaping the Knots

Once your dough has risen, it’s time for the fun part—shaping the knots. This process doesn’t require perfection; in fact, the rustic look adds character.

  1. Divide and Roll
    Punch down the dough and divide it into 12-16 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a rope about 6-8 inches long.

  2. Tie the Knot
    Take each rope and tie it into a simple knot, tucking the ends underneath. Place the shaped knots onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving space between them to allow for further rising.

  3. Second Rise
    Cover the knots with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes. This second rise will ensure they’re light and fluffy when baked.

Garlic Butter Magic

No garlic knot is complete without its magical coating of garlic butter. The key here is balance—strong garlic flavor tempered by creamy butter and accented with fragrant herbs.

Ingredients for Garlic Butter:

  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh basil, chopped (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Preparing the Garlic Butter

  1. Melt and Infuse
    In a small saucepan or skillet, melt the butter over low heat. Add olive oil, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, making sure not to burn the garlic.

  2. Remove from Heat
    Stir in the parsley and basil. The heat will gently release the herbs’ aromas without cooking them. Set aside as you bake the knots.

Baking the Knots

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the knots for 15-18 minutes, or until they’re golden brown on top and the aroma fills your kitchen. Avoid overbaking to maintain their soft interior.

The Finishing Touch

While the knots are still warm, brush them generously with the garlic butter. Ensure every nook and cranny is coated for maximum flavor. Finish with a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese for added depth. Serve on a platter alongside your pizza and a tangy marinara for dipping.

Tips for an Extra Special Pizza Night

  1. Make It Interactive
    Lay out toppings, sauces, and seasonings for a DIY pizza station. Let family and friends create their own pizza masterpieces while the garlic knots bake.

  2. Add Variety
    Shake up your garlic knots by experimenting with different herbs, adding crushed red pepper flakes for heat, or swapping out Parmesan for Pecorino Romano.

  3. Incorporate Dipping Sauces
    Serve the knots with an array of dips, such as marinara, garlic aioli, or creamy ranch. This adds a delightful layer of indulgence.

Leftovers Worth Keeping

If you happen to have leftovers (a rarity with garlic knots!), store them in an airtight container or zip-top bag. Reheat them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 5 minutes, or enjoy them cold—they’re that good. Additionally, leftover garlic knots can double as a base for sliders, a snack during movie night, or even a side for soups or pasta dishes.

Bringing It All Together

Homemade garlic knots are more than a recipe—they’re a bonding experience, a culinary adventure, and the perfect accompaniment to pizza night. The combination of golden, fluffy bread and fragrant garlic butter takes a humble side and transforms it into a centerpiece of joy. Not only are these knots delicious, but crafting them with loved ones can turn dinner prep into a memory-making moment.

So, the next time you’re planning a pizza night, don’t just settle for store-bought sides. Roll up your sleeves, whip up a batch of homemade garlic knots, and let the aroma of freshly baked bread and garlicky goodness bring smiles to everyone’s faces.
