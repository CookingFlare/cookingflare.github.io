Seasonal Seafood: Cooking with Fresh Spring Catches


Spring is a season of renewal—not just for nature but also for your palate. As the chill of winter recedes, the waters begin to teem with an array of fresh seafood that brings vibrant flavors and textures to the table. From the delicate sweetness of freshly caught shrimp to the briny richness of clams and the tender flakiness of early-season fish, spring’s bounty invites you to explore cooking with fresh, seasonal seafood.

In this blog post, we’ll dive into an assortment of spring’s most exciting seafood picks. You’ll discover how to select the freshest catches, tips for perfect preparation, and inspired recipes that celebrate the best the season has to offer.

Why Cook with Seasonal Seafood?

Selecting seafood in season not only ensures superior flavor but also promotes sustainability and supports local fisheries. Seasonal seafood is often fresher, as it hasn’t been subjected to long transport times or extensive freezing.

Spring seafood tends to be lighter and more delicate than the heartier winter varieties, perfectly matched to the freshness and brightness of spring herbs, vegetables, and ingredients that come back to markets this time of year.

Fresh Spring Seafood Highlights

1. Wild-caught Shrimp

Spring shrimp are plump, sweet, and tender, bursting with ocean umami. They’re incredibly versatile — perfect sautéed, grilled, or boiled for salads and pasta dishes. Look for shrimp that smell clean and briny, with firm, translucent flesh.

2. Clams and Mussels

May and early June mark the beginning of clam and mussel season in many coastal areas. Their natural brininess lends a perfect salty bite to soups, stews, and pasta. Be sure no shells are cracked, and discard those that remain open after a tap test.

3. Striped Bass

A prized fish of the spring months, striped bass offers a firm, white flesh that’s both moist and delicate. Excellent for baking or grilling, it pairs beautifully with fresh herbs and citrus to highlight its subtly sweet flavor.

4. Scallops

Spring scallops are tender and buttery, with a slight sweetness and a melt-in-your-mouth texture. Look for dry-packed scallops with little to no preservatives for the freshest flavor.

5. Soft-shell Crabs

Usually available in late spring, soft-shell crabs are a seasonal delicacy. Their crispy exterior and creamy interior make for an indulgent treat, often best enjoyed pan-fried with simple seasoning.

How to Choose and Store Fresh Seafood

Picking the Freshest Catch

  • Smell: Fresh seafood should evoke a clean, ocean-like scent—not fishy or ammonia-like.
  • Appearance: Shellfish should have tightly closed shells. Fish eyes should be clear and bright, not cloudy or sunken.
  • Texture: Flesh should be firm and spring back when pressed.

Storage Tips

  • Keep Cool: Store seafood on ice or in the coldest part of your refrigerator and use it within a day or two.
  • Avoid Cross-Contamination: Keep seafood separate from other foods to prevent dripping and contamination.
  • Prep Just Before Cooking: For maximum freshness and flavor, clean and prepare seafood right before cooking.

Cooking Techniques Ideal for Spring Seafood

Spring seafood often benefits from quicker, lighter cooking methods that preserve delicate texture and flavor:

  • Sautéing: Perfect for shrimp, scallops, and soft-shell crabs. Use a hot pan with a bit of oil or butter for a golden crust.
  • Steaming: Ideal for clams and mussels, steaming brings out their natural juices, perfect as a base for broths.
  • Grilling: Striped bass and shrimp shine on the grill, adding a smoky depth while keeping the flesh moist.
  • Poaching: A gentle poach in a fragrant court bouillon keeps fish tender and infuses subtle flavor.

Spring Seafood Recipes to Celebrate the Season

Lemon Herb Grilled Striped Bass with Spring Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 4 striped bass fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Assorted spring vegetables (asparagus, baby carrots, snap peas)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, garlic, lemon juice and zest, thyme, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush both sides of the bass fillets with the herb mixture.
  4. Toss spring vegetables lightly with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Grill fish for about 4 minutes per side, or until opaque and easily flaked with a fork.
  6. Grill vegetables for 5-6 minutes until tender but crisp.
  7. Serve fish atop the grilled vegetables with an extra lemon wedge.

Steamed Clams with White Wine and Garlic

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs fresh clams (littleneck or cherrystone)
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Crusty baguette (for dipping)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse clams under cold water and scrub shells to remove grit.
  2. In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat and sauté garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add white wine and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add clams, cover tightly with a lid, and steam for 5-7 minutes until all shells open.
  5. Discard any unopened clams.
  6. Stir in fresh parsley and serve immediately with crusty bread for soaking up the broth.

Pan-Seared Spring Shrimp with Asparagus and Pea Shoot Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 cups fresh pea shoots
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add shrimp, season with salt and pepper, and sauté for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  3. Remove shrimp and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add asparagus and a splash of water, cooking until tender-crisp (about 4 minutes). Season lightly.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk lemon juice, honey, garlic, and remaining olive oil.
  6. Toss asparagus and pea shoots with the lemon dressing.
  7. Serve shrimp atop the salad for a light, refreshing dish.

Crispy Soft-Shell Crab Sandwich with Spring Slaw

Ingredients:

  • 4 soft-shell crabs, cleaned
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 4 brioche buns
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrot
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour with Old Bay seasoning in a shallow dish.
  2. Dredge soft-shell crabs in the seasoned flour, shaking off excess.
  3. Heat oil in a deep skillet or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  4. Fry crabs until golden and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  5. In a bowl, whisk mayonnaise, vinegar, mustard, salt, and pepper. Toss with cabbage and carrot.
  6. Toast brioche buns lightly.
  7. Assemble sandwiches by layering soft-shell crab and slaw on the buns.
  8. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

Pairing Ideas: Elevate Your Spring Seafood Meal

  • Wines: Crisp, light whites such as Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Grigio, or un-oaked Chardonnay complement the fresh, bright flavors of spring seafood.
  • Herbs: Dill, tarragon, chervil, and mint add a refreshing nuance without overpowering delicate seafood.
  • Sides: Spring sides like pea purée, baby potatoes with fresh herbs, and simple grain salads bring seasonal freshness to the plate.

Final Thoughts

Embracing seasonal seafood during spring opens up a treasure trove of flavors and culinary possibilities. Each fresh catch carries the essence of its environment and the promise of sunny days ahead. Whether you’re grilling striped bass, steaming clams, or frying soft-shell crabs, you’re creating dishes that celebrate the essence of the season.

Cooking with fresh spring seafood is more than just a meal—it’s an experience that connects table and tide, flavor and freshness. So next time you visit the market, seek out these seasonal delights, experiment with these recipes, and let spring’s ocean bounty shine on your plate.
