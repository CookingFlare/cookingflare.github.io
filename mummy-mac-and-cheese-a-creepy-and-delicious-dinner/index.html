Mummy Mac and Cheese: A Creepy and Delicious Dinner


Are you ready to take your mac and cheese game to a whole new level? Look no further than this spooky and scrumptious Mummy Mac and Cheese recipe! It’s the perfect dish to serve at Halloween parties or any time you want to add a touch of creepy fun to your dinner table. With its gooey cheese, crispy mummy wraps, and hidden surprise in every bite, this dish is sure to delight both kids and adults alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 roll of refrigerated crescent dough
  • 2 small black olives

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.

  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour to create a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture is smooth and bubbly.

  4. Gradually whisk in the milk, making sure to eliminate any lumps. Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and well combined. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper, and stir until fully incorporated.

  6. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until every noodle is coated in the creamy goodness.

  7. Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to the greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.

  8. Unroll the crescent dough and cut it into thin strips. Lay the strips over the mac and cheese, crisscrossing them to create a mummy-like appearance. Leave a small opening for the eyes.

  9. Slice the black olives in half and place them in the opening to create the mummy’s eyes.

  10. Bake the Mummy Mac and Cheese in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and the mummy wraps are golden brown.

  11. Once cooked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. The mummy wraps will become crispier as they cool.

  12. Serve this spooktacular dish to your hungry guests and watch as they unravel the cheesy mummy goodness. Enjoy!

Spooky Tips:

  • If you want to add a little extra creepiness to your Mummy Mac and Cheese, you can mix in some diced cooked bacon or chopped spinach before baking.
  • Feel free to experiment with different cheese combinations for a unique flavor. Try adding some mozzarella or pepper jack cheese for a little extra kick.
  • Serve this dish with a side of “blood” marinara sauce for dipping. Just mix some marinara sauce with a few drops of red food coloring for a ghoulish effect.

So there you have it, a deliciously spooky twist on the classic mac and cheese recipe. Whether you’re hosting a Halloween party or just looking to add some fun to your dinner routine, this Mummy Mac and Cheese is sure to be a hit. Get creative with your mummy wrapping skills and enjoy the cheesy goodness that lies beneath. Happy cooking!
