Deliciously Easy Eggplant Parmesan Recipe


Eggplant Parmesan, a classic Italian dish, is a beloved comfort food that brings layers of flavor and delightful textures to the table. This recipe will guide you through creating a deliciously easy Eggplant Parmesan that is not only satisfying but also straightforward enough for weeknight cooking. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, you’ll have no trouble mastering this recipe.

Ingredients

To make this Eggplant Parmesan, gather the following ingredients:

For the Eggplant:

  • 2 medium-sized eggplants
  • Salt (for sweating the eggplants)
  • Olive oil (for frying)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (for dredging)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs (preferably Italian-style)

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

For Assembly:

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly chopped parsley (optional for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Eggplants

  1. Slice the Eggplants: Start by slicing the eggplants into 1/4-inch thick rounds. Aim for uniform thickness to ensure even cooking.

  2. Sweat the Eggplants: Lay the slices on a large baking sheet or cutting board and sprinkle both sides generously with salt. Let them sit for about 30 minutes. This process will draw out excess moisture and bitterness from the eggplants. After 30 minutes, rinse the slices under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.

Step 2: Bread the Eggplants

  1. Set Up a Dredging Station: In one bowl, place the flour. In a second bowl, beat the eggs. In a third bowl, pour the breadcrumbs.

  2. Dredge the Eggplant Slices: Dip each eggplant slice first in the flour, shaking off the excess, then in the beaten eggs, and finally coat them evenly with breadcrumbs. Set the breaded slices aside on a plate.

Step 3: Fry the Eggplant

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of olive oil over medium heat. Make sure the oil is hot enough to sizzle when you add a slice of eggplant.

  2. Fry the Eggplants: Working in batches, add the breaded eggplant slices to the skillet. Fry each side for about 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. Use tongs to flip them carefully. Once cooked, transfer the fried eggplant to a paper towel-lined plate to absorb excess oil.

Step 4: Make the Tomato Sauce

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a medium saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Next, add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir in the dried basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 5: Assemble the Eggplant Parmesan

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Layer the Ingredients: In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom. Place a layer of fried eggplant slices over the sauce. Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella cheese and a layer of Parmesan cheese on top.

  3. Repeat the Layers: Continue layering the sauce, eggplant, mozzarella, and Parmesan until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.

Step 6: Bake

  1. Bake the Dish: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. This will help the cheeses melt and blend the flavors.

  2. Uncover and Brown: After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  3. Cool and Serve: Once baked, let the Eggplant Parmesan sit for about 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the layers to set and makes it easier to slice.

Step 7: Garnish and Enjoy

Before serving, sprinkle freshly chopped basil and parsley on top for a burst of color and flavor. Pair your delicious Eggplant Parmesan with a simple green salad or some garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing the Right Eggplant: Look for firm, glossy eggplants without blemishes. Smaller eggplants tend to be less bitter and have fewer seeds.

  • Make-Ahead Option: You can prepare the Eggplant Parmesan in advance. Assemble it up to the baking stage, cover it tightly with foil, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to bake, simply add a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

  • Freezing: Leftovers can be frozen. Just slice portions, wrap them tightly in plastic wrap, and store them in an airtight container. When you’re ready to enjoy, thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven until warmed through.

Nutritional Information

Eggplant Parmesan is not only delicious but can also be a nutritious option. Eggplant contains antioxidants and is low in calories. The use of fresh ingredients like tomatoes and herbs adds vitamins and minerals, making this dish a healthier choice.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your Eggplant Parmesan with:

  • A side of garlic bread
  • A crisp Caesar or mixed greens salad
  • A glass of red wine to enhance the dining experience

Conclusion

This Deliciously Easy Eggplant Parmesan recipe is a fantastic way to enjoy a comforting, homemade Italian meal. With layers of fried eggplant, rich tomato sauce, and gooey cheese, it’s a dish that is sure to please everyone at your table. Follow this recipe, and you’ll have a flavorful, satisfying dish that showcases the wonderful versatility of eggplant. Enjoy your culinary creation and share it with family and friends!
