Easy Thai Grilled Chicken Skewers with Lemongrass


When it comes to bursting flavors and irresistible aromas, nothing quite beats Thai grilled chicken skewers infused with lemongrass. This dish is an absolute crowd-pleaser, renowned for its balance of smoky char, zesty citrus notes, and the subtle sweetness that lemongrass delivers. If you’ve been craving a simple yet sophisticated recipe that transports your taste buds straight to the streets of Thailand, you’re in the right place.

Why Lemongrass Is the Star Ingredient

Lemongrass is one of the treasures of Southeast Asian cuisine. It’s a stalky herb with a fresh, lemony aroma that lends a bright and slightly grassy flavor to dishes. Unlike lemon, lemongrass imparts subtle complexity, making it an ideal partner for grilled chicken. When combined with garlic, ginger, and a touch of chili, lemongrass turns ordinary chicken into a flavor-packed masterpiece.

Ingredients Overview: What You Need and Why

Here’s a quick look at the key ingredients and their flavor contributions:

  • Chicken thighs: Juicy and tender, thighs stand up well to grilling without drying out.
  • Fresh lemongrass stalks: Provides aromatic citrus notes.
  • Garlic and ginger: Classic Thai aromatics enhancing warmth and depth.
  • Fish sauce: Adds umami and authentic savory notes.
  • Brown sugar or palm sugar: Balances the saltiness with mild sweetness.
  • Lime juice: Brightens the marinade with acidity.
  • Shallots: Adds subtle sweetness and texture.
  • Fresh chili or chili flakes: Adds just the right kick of heat.
  • Cilantro and fresh mint (optional): For garnish and fresh herbal brightness.

Step-By-Step Guide to Thai Grilled Chicken Skewers with Lemongrass

Preparing the Marinade

  1. Peel and finely chop the lemongrass. Focus on the tender lower halves. This is where the most flavor resides.
  2. Crush or mince garlic and ginger. Fresh is key here to elevate the fragrance.
  3. Mix fish sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, and chopped shallots in a bowl. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Add lemongrass, garlic, ginger, and thinly sliced chili. Let all the flavors mingle for at least 10 minutes.

Marinating the Chicken

  • Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces or thin strips. This ensures quick and even grilling.
  • Toss the chicken in the marinade, making sure each piece is well-coated.
  • Cover and refrigerate for a minimum of 1 hour. For a deeper infusion, 4 hours or overnight works wonders.

Skewering and Grilling

  • Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto wooden or metal skewers, leaving a little space between each piece.
  • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grilling surface to prevent sticking.
  • Grill each skewer for 3-4 minutes per side, or until you see beautiful grill marks and the chicken is cooked through.
  • Avoid overcooking to keep the meat juicy.

Serving Suggestions

  • Arrange your skewers on a platter garnished with freshly chopped cilantro and mint leaves.
  • Serve alongside jasmine rice or sticky rice.
  • A spicy cucumber salad or a light green papaya salad complements the dish beautifully.
  • Don’t forget a drizzle of lime wedges on the side for an extra acidic punch.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing chicken thighs makes a huge difference. Breast meat tends to dry out on the grill.
  • If you don’t have fresh lemongrass, frozen or minced versions can work in a pinch, though fresh always delivers a brighter flavor.
  • Soak wooden skewers for at least 30 minutes before grilling to prevent burning.
  • For a smoky flavor boost, add a charcoal grill or wood chips to your gas grill.
  • Experiment with chili levels — Thai bird’s eye chilis bring authentic heat, but jalapeños can make the dish more approachable.

Variations to Try

  • Lemongrass Beef Skewers: Swap chicken for thin slices of beef sirloin, marinated in the same mixture. Grill to medium-rare for a rich, meaty twist.
  • Vegetarian Option: Use firm tofu or portobello mushrooms, marinated and grilled similarly, for a delicious plant-based version.
  • Peanut Dipping Sauce: Combine peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, garlic, and a dash of chili for an irresistible dip.

Nutrition Snapshot

Thai grilled chicken skewers with lemongrass are not just delicious — they’re also a balanced meal component. Chicken thighs provide protein essential for muscle repair and satiety, while lemongrass offers antioxidants and digestive benefits. The marinade’s simplicity means you avoid excessive sugar and heavy additives found in many takeout options.

This recipe epitomizes effortless elegance in home cooking. By combining a handful of accessible ingredients with straightforward steps, you can create an authentic, aromatic, and flavorful dish that feels like a mini-vacation to Thailand. Whether you’re hosting friends or enjoying a simple weeknight dinner, these Thai grilled chicken skewers with lemongrass promise to impress every palate and elevate your grilling game.

Ready to fire up the grill and bring Thailand into your backyard? Get your lemongrass ready and let the sizzle begin!
