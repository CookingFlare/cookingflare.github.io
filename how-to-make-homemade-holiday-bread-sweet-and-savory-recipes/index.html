How to Make Homemade Holiday Bread: Sweet and Savory Recipes


As the holiday season approaches, the aroma of freshly baked bread wafts through homes, filling them with warmth and inviting nostalgia. Homemade bread is an integral part of the holiday celebrations, a tradition that transcends borders and generations. In this post, we'll delve into the art of baking sweet and savory holiday bread, sharing recipes that will captivate your taste buds and leave you yearning for more. Sugar and Spice: Classic Fruitcake There's something magical about fruitcake. Its dense, rich flavor and sweet aroma evoke memories of yesteryears, transporting us to merry gatherings and holiday gatherings around the Christmas tree. Our take on this traditional dessert incorporates rum-soaked raisins, golden amber syrup, and toasted almonds for an added depth of flavor. Ingredients:

1 cup (200g) unsalted butter, softened 1 1/2 cups (300g) granulated sugar 4 large eggs 2 cups (250g) golden syrup 1 teaspoon ground ginger 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour 1 cup (120g) raisins 1/2 cup (60g) chopped almonds 1/2 cup (60g) rum

Instructions

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the golden syrup, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, raisins, and almonds. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet, folding until just combined. Transfer to a greased loaf pan and bake at 275°F (135°C) for 50 minutes. After 15 minutes, turn the cake upside down and insert a cherry. While the cake is baking, reheat the rum and brush it onto the cake after 20 and 40 minutes of baking.

Herby Flatbread for the Antipasto Buffet While traditional holiday bread trends may point to rich, hearty loaves, the elegance of Italian herbed flatbread cannot be overlooked. Blending classic Savoy-made herbs with fresh basil and bright sun-dried tomatoes will elevate your upcoming holiday gatherings. This easy flatbread recipe demands precise measurements for rich results. Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups (190g) warm water 1/4 cup (60g) olive oil 2 tablespoons dried oregano 1 tablespoon dried thyme 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons sun-dried tomatoes, finely chopped 3 cups (375g) whole wheat flour 1 teaspoon salt 1 tablespoon active dry yeast

Instructions

In a mixing bowl, combine olive oil, warm water, oregano, thyme, rosemary, and garlic. Mix well. In a separate large bowl, whisk together flour, chopped tomatoes, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, folding until a dough forms. Add yeast to the dough and knead for 10 minutes. Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces, forming each into a round, 1/4-inch-thick round. Brush with olive oil and smooth using your palms. Bake the flatbreads in the oven at 400°F for 10 minutes.
