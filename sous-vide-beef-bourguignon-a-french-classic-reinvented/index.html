Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon: A French Classic Reinvented


Beef Bourguignon, a traditional French stew, is a beloved dish known for its rich flavors and tender meat. However, with the advent of modern cooking techniques, we can now elevate this classic recipe to new heights. In this recipe, we will explore how to reinvent Beef Bourguignon using the sous vide method, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth dish that will impress even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, preferably chuck roast, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups red wine, preferably Burgundy
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C).

  2. Season the beef stew meat with salt and pepper. Place the seasoned meat in a vacuum-sealed bag, ensuring that it is in a single layer. If using a regular plastic bag, you can use the water displacement method to remove the air.

  3. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully submerge the bag of beef into the water. Make sure the bag is fully submerged and leave it to cook for 24 hours. The long cooking time will result in incredibly tender meat.

  4. While the beef is cooking, prepare the other ingredients. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sliced carrots and cook until they are softened and slightly caramelized, about 5 minutes.

  5. Add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook for another 3 minutes, until the mushrooms have released their moisture and are beginning to brown.

  6. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well to coat everything evenly. Cook for 2 minutes, allowing the flour to cook and remove any raw taste.

  7. Deglaze the skillet by adding the red wine, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaves, and dried thyme. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  8. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for about 15 minutes, until the sauce has thickened slightly.

  9. Once the beef has finished cooking in the sous vide bath, remove it from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. Reserve any juices that have accumulated in the bag.

  10. In a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the beef in batches until it is browned on all sides. This step will help develop a rich flavor and enhance the texture of the meat.

  11. Return all the seared beef to the pot and pour in the sauce, along with any reserved juices from the sous vide bag. Stir everything together, making sure the meat is well-coated in the sauce.

  12. Cover the pot and transfer it to a preheated oven at 325°F (165°C). Let the beef braise in the oven for 2 to 3 hours, until the meat is fork-tender and the flavors have melded together.

  13. Once the cooking time is up, remove the pot from the oven and skim off any excess fat from the surface. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  14. Serve the sous vide Beef Bourguignon hot, garnished with fresh parsley. This dish pairs perfectly with crusty bread or creamy mashed potatoes.

Conclusion

By using the sous vide cooking method, we have reinvented the classic Beef Bourguignon, transforming it into a dish that is even more tender and flavorful. The long, slow cooking time allows the beef to become incredibly succulent, while the traditional ingredients and flavors of red wine, mushrooms, and onions create a rich and satisfying sauce. Whether you’re looking to impress dinner guests or simply treat yourself to a gourmet meal, this Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon is sure to delight your taste buds and leave you craving for more.
