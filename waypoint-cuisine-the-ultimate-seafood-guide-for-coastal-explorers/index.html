Waypoint Cuisine: The Ultimate Seafood Guide for Coastal Explorers


Embark on a culinary adventure along the coast, where the ocean’s bounty meets the trail’s end. An explorer’s appetite isn’t simply nourished by the day’s adventures but also fueled by the local flavors and treasures found. Embrace the coast like a voyageur, and let Waypoint Cuisine be your guide to the ultimate seafood experience with our coastal-inspired recipes.

Seaside Sensations: A Coastal Pantry

Start by stocking up on a coastal pantry with essential flavors and ingredients that scream “seafood.” From cracked black pepper to fragrant herbs like dill, tarragon, and rosemary, your dishes are guaranteed to take on an oceanic allure. Balance these flavors with tart, citrusy elements like lemons, limes, and a small selection of sour pickles to convey depth and contrast.

Don’t forget to have a good selection of local spices such as sea salt, cayenne pepper, and Old Bay seasoning, which are perfect for enhancing these coastal flavors. Incorporate a variety of rich, smoky elements, like smoked paprika and liquid smoke, which will bring out the essence of grilling and slow-cooking techniques in your meals.

Grilled Coastal Seafood

Sultan of the Sea: Grilled Swordfish Steaks

Imagine waves crashing against rocky shores while savoring the succulent, smoky taste of grilled swordfish steaks. This bold and hearty fish is a perfect canvas for grilling, a technique that enhances its rich flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 swordfish steaks (about 6 oz. each)
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon
  • Sea salt
  • Freshly-cracked black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill on medium-high heat.
  2. Drizzle olive oil on swordfish steaks and rub with a halved lemon. Season with sea salt and a generous amount of black pepper.
  3. Place the swordfish steaks on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side. They are done when the flesh turns opaque and separates easily with a fork.
  4. Serve the steaks with more grilled lemon wedges and a sprinkle of kosher salt.

Beachside Catch: Grilled Lemon-Herb Shrimp Skewers

Transport yourselves to a beachside paradise with these scrumptious grilled lemon-herb shrimp skewers. They are a light and refreshing appetizer that captures the essences of coastal cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. extra-large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh dill, chopped
  • Fresh tarragon, chopped
  • Sea salt
  • Freshly-cracked black pepper
  • 4 wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill on medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, mix the shrimp with olive oil, lemon juice, dill, tarragon, salt, and pepper. Allow the shrimp to marinate for about 15 minutes.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto the skewers, with about 5 per stick. Leave a little space between each shrimp for even cooking.
  4. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 3 minutes on each side, until they turn pink and are no longer translucent.

Chilled Coastal Delicacies

Coastal Gaze: Chilled Poached Lobster

Feeling reflective and watching the horizon? Treat yourself to the sophistication of chilled poached lobster. This dish embodies a serene coastal ambiance in every luscious bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 live lobster (about 1.5 lbs)
  • Water
  • Whole peppercorns
  • Bay leaf
  • Lemon
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh tarragon, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a large pot, add peppercorns, a pinch of salt, and a bay leaf. Submerge the lobster head-first, bring it back to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Poach for 15 minutes, adjusting the heat to keep the water simmering.
  2. Let the lobster cool in the poaching liquid before placing it in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the lobster from the poaching liquid and halve it lengthwise. Remove the tender meat from the claws and tail, and chop into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Mix the lobster meat with olive oil, lemon juice, tarragon, salt, and pepper. Chill for an additional 15 minutes before serving.
