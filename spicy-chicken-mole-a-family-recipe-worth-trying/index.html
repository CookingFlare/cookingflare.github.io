Spicy Chicken Mole: A Family Recipe Worth Trying


As the aroma of chocolate, spices, and cherries wafts through the air, it's impossible not to be drawn in by the allure of a classic Mexican dish: mole poblano. But for those who dare to venture beyond the familiar flavors, I invite you to try a variation of this beloved sauce: Spicy Chicken Mole. This family recipe, passed down through generations, combines the rich flavors of mole with the heat of spicy chicken, making it a true crowning jewel of Mexican cuisine. A Rich History Mole, a quintessential Mexican sauce, has a storied past that dates back to the Aztecs. This complex blend of spices, chocolate, nuts, and seeds was originally used as a gift to Aztec rulers. Over time, mole spread throughout Mexico, with each region developing its own unique variations. Today, mole remains a staple of Mexican cooking, with its rich flavors and aromas transporting those who taste it to a bygone era. A Spicy Twist While traditional mole is a labor of love, requiring hours of preparation, our Spicy Chicken Mole recipe is a more streamlined version that still maintains the authentic flavors. The addition of spicy chicken adds a bold twist to the classic sauce, making it perfect for those who crave a little heat. To make this dish, you'll need:

1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs 2 ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded 1/4 cup raisins 1/4 cup almonds 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1 tsp ground cumin 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper 1 can (10 oz) chipotle peppers in adobo sauce 1/4 cup dark chocolate 1 tsp dried oregano Salt and pepper, to taste

Preparing the Spices and Chilies To begin, toast the almonds and ancho chilies in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant. Grind the toasted almonds in a spice grinder or food processor until finely ground. In a separate bowl, combine the ground cinnamon, cumin, and cayenne pepper. Roasting the Garlic and Spices Next, wrap the garlic cloves in foil and roast in a 400°F oven for 15-20 minutes, or until tender. Remove the garlic from the oven and let cool, then mince. In a separate pan, toast the ground spices over low heat for 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant. Blending the Sauce Now it's time to blend the sauce. In a food processor or blender, combine the roasted garlic, toasted almonds, ancho chilies, raisins, chipotle peppers, dark chocolate, and toasted spice blend. Blend until smooth, adding chicken broth as needed to achieve the desired consistency. Cooking the Chicken In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on both sides and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes per side. Transfer the chicken to a plate and set aside. Finishing the Mole With the chicken cooking, it's time to finish the mole. In the same skillet used to cook the chicken, add another tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the blended sauce and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5-7 minutes or until the sauce has thickened. Stir in the oregano, salt, and pepper to taste. Assembling the Dish To assemble the dish, place a portion of the cooked chicken on a plate or platter. Spoon the spicy chicken mole sauce over the chicken, garnishing with fresh cilantro or scallions, if desired. Tasting Notes This Spicy Chicken Mole recipe is a symphony of flavors, with the heat of the chipotle peppers balanced by the richness of the chocolate and spices. The toasted almonds add a satisfying crunch, while the raisins bring a touch of sweetness. This dish is perfect for special occasions or as a family staple, as it's sure to become a beloved favorite. Variations and Substitutions Feel free to modify this recipe to suit your tastes. For a milder sauce, omit the chipotle peppers or substitute with milder peppers, such as Anaheim. For added heat, add more chipotle peppers or increase the amount of cayenne pepper. Conclusion Spicy Chicken Mole is a true delight, perfect for adventurous eaters and those looking to broaden their culinary horizons. This family recipe has been passed down through generations, and with its rich flavors and aromas, it's sure to become a treasured staple in your home. So, the next time you're craving something new and exciting, give Spicy Chicken Mole a try. Your taste buds will thank you. Tips and Resources

For a more authentic flavor, use Mexican chocolate, such as Ibarra or Abuelita. If using chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, be sure to strain the sauce before using to avoid excess salt. For a vegetarian or vegan version, substitute chicken with portobello mushrooms or tempeh. Experiment with different spices and chilies to create your own unique variations.
