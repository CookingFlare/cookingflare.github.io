Quick Holiday Recipes for Last-Minute Entertaining


As the holiday season approaches, it often feels like there are endless gatherings to prepare for. Whether it’s a surprise visit from friends, a work party, or a family get-together, you might find yourself scrambling for quick and delicious recipes that will impress your guests without taking too much of your time. Here are some delightful last-minute holiday recipes that are simple to whip up, yet elegant enough to leave a lasting impression.

1. Festive Cranberry Brie Bites

These bite-sized appetizers are a perfect mix of sweet and savory, making them a holiday favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 wheel of brie cheese
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh rosemary for garnish
  • Egg wash (1 egg mixed with 1 tablespoon of water)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. Cut it into 2-inch squares.
  3. Place a small piece of brie cheese in the center of each square and top with a spoonful of cranberry sauce.
  4. Fold the corners of the pastry square towards the center and pinch to seal.
  5. Brush the tops with the egg wash for a golden finish.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the pastry is puffed and golden.
  7. Garnish with fresh rosemary before serving.

Tips:

  • Make these ahead of time and freeze them unbaked. Bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.
  • Pair with a crisp white wine for an elegant touch.

2. Savory Stuffed Mushrooms

These savory stuffed mushrooms are a crowd-pleaser and can be made in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 20 large button mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine cream cheese, Parmesan, breadcrumbs, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff each mushroom cap with the mixture and place them on a baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the tops are golden brown.

Tips:

  • Experiment with different cheeses, such as feta or goat cheese, for a unique twist.
  • You can also add cooked sausage or spinach to the stuffing for added flavor and texture.

3. Holiday Spinach and Artichoke Dip

A classic crowd favorite, this creamy dip can be prepared in minutes and served with crackers, bread, or veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1 cup canned artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1 cup cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine all ingredients until well blended.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish and smooth the top.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the dip is bubbly and golden on top.
  5. Serve warm with tortilla chips or fresh vegetables.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, add a dash of hot sauce or some crushed red pepper flakes.
  • You can make this dip ahead of time and store it in the fridge. Just pop it in the oven when ready to serve.

4. Spiced Holiday Nuts

These sweet and spicy nuts are perfect for snacking and make an excellent addition to any holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups mixed nuts (almonds, pecans, walnuts, cashews)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the nuts with maple syrup, olive oil, cinnamon, cayenne, and salt until well coated.
  3. Spread the mixture evenly on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast in the oven for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden brown.
  5. Allow to cool before serving.

Tips:

  • Feel free to experiment with spices; rosemary or thyme can add a delightful twist.
  • Store any leftovers in an airtight container to maintain freshness.

5. One-Pot Holiday Pasta

This one-pot pasta is a hearty and festive dish that takes less than 30 minutes to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces of pasta (penne or fusilli work well)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup spinach
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine pasta, vegetable broth, cherry tomatoes, and Italian seasoning.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes or until pasta is al dente.
  3. Stir in spinach and heavy cream, cooking for another 2 minutes until the spinach wilts.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

Tips:

  • Add cooked chicken or shrimp for additional protein.
  • This dish can be made vegetarian by using vegetable broth.

6. Gingerbread Cookie Bars

These gingerbread cookie bars are a delightful dessert that captures the flavors of the season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in molasses and eggs.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together flour, ginger, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients until well combined.
  5. Spread the dough evenly in the prepared pan.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Allow to cool before cutting into bars.

Tips:

  • Top with a simple icing made from powdered sugar and milk for an extra festive touch.
  • These bars can be stored in an airtight container for up to a week.

7. Peppermint Chocolate Bark

This no-bake treat is perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth while offering a festive touch.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • 12 ounces white chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup crushed peppermint candies or candy canes

Instructions:

  1. Melt the dark chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl or over a double boiler until smooth.
  2. Spread the melted dark chocolate evenly on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Melt the white chocolate and drizzle it over the dark chocolate. Use a toothpick to swirl them together for a marbled effect.
  4. Sprinkle crushed peppermint candies evenly on top.
  5. Refrigerate until set, then break into pieces.

Tips:

  • For a richer flavor, sprinkle a little sea salt on top before refrigerating.
  • Package the bark in decorative bags for a thoughtful homemade gift.

8. Holiday Fruit and Cheese Board

A beautifully arranged cheese board makes for an elegant and easy appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • Various cheeses (cheddar, brie, blue cheese, gouda)
  • Assorted cured meats (salami, prosciutto)
  • Fresh fruits (grapes, figs, apple slices)
  • Nuts (almonds, walnuts)
  • Crackers and breadsticks
  • Honey or fig jam for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Select a large wooden board or platter for assembling.
  2. Place the cheeses and meats in various sections of the board.
  3. Fill in gaps with fresh fruits and nuts.
  4. Arrange crackers and breadsticks artfully around the platter.
  5. Serve with small bowls of honey or jam for dipping.

Tips:

  • Choose a variety of textures and flavors to keep it interesting.
  • Fresh herbs, such as rosemary or thyme, can add a nice decorative touch.

Conclusion

With these quick holiday recipes, you can create a festive and delicious spread that will wow your guests, even on short notice. Each recipe is designed to be simple yet impressive, allowing you to enjoy the holiday spirit without the stress of elaborate preparations. So, gather your ingredients, put on some holiday music, and get ready to entertain with ease! Happy cooking!
