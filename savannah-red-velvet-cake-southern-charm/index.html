Savannah Red Velvet Cake: Southern Charm


Welcome, fellow food enthusiasts, to yet another culinary journey inspired by the Deep South! Today, we’re diving headfirst into the delectable world of the Savannah Red Velvet Cake — a classic dessert that’s as enchanting as the coastal city of Savannah itself. As you read on, let’s weave through the rich history, tradition, and flavors that make this southern staple a timeless treasure. Discover how to prepare this iconic dessert and bring a touch of Southern charm to your kitchen.

Unraveling the Red Velvet Journey

The name “Red Velvet” alone conjures up images of velvety smooth texture, rich taste, and oh-so-festive color. While some might think that this cake has roots in the Northern parts of our country, it’s actually a proud Southerner. Its recipe traces back to the early 20th century and the culinary brilliance of the South.

Tradition tells that its vibrant red hue initially came from the beet juice used instead of costly cocoa powder, giving the cake a subtle sweetness that wouldn’t overwhelm the palate. Over time, as cocoa powder prices dropped, the beet juice was replaced with cocoa for a similar flavor effect, but maintained the vivid red color beloved by southern cakes.

Unlocking the Secrets

Our Savannah Red Velvet Cake embraces this history, incorporating its simplicity and elegance into every step of preparation. Ready to dive in?

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup pure cocoa powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon red food coloring
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk (or make your own by mixing 1 tablespoon of vinegar with 1/2 cup of milk)
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar (for the buttermilk)
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar (added later)

Instructions:

Cake:

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and generously grease two 9-inch round cake pans with butter or non-stick cooking spray.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. If you’re using an electric mixer, this could take 3-5 minutes. Do this manually with a whisk if you’re going old-school — either way works!

  4. Blend Wet Ingredients: Add the cocoa powder, eggs, vanilla extract, and red food coloring to the butter mixture. Whisk until fully combined.

  5. Pour in the Acid: The secret to a perfect Red Velvet Cake lies in its acidity. Mix the white vinegar and buttermilk in a separate small cup and gradually add this buttermilk mixture to your batter, whisking continuously.

  6. Flour in Three Baths: Alternately add the dry ingredients and the buttermilk mixture to the mixing bowl, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Distribute this mixture evenly between the two cake pans.

  7. Bake to Perfection: Place the pans in the oven and bake for approximately 30 minutes. You’ll know the cakes are done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  8. Cool Down: Allow your cakes to cool in their pans for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Frosting:

  • Cream Cheese Base: Beat 8 ounces of cream cheese and 1 cup of softened unsalted butter together until light and fluffy.
  • Flavor the Frosting: Add 2 cups of powdered sugar. To enhance the Southern touch, add 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract and mix well.
  • Whip it: Whisk in 1/8th teaspoon of salt and 2 tablespoons of heavy cream (or more, as needed, to reach your desired spreading consistency).

Assemble:

Once your cakes and frosting are ready, it’s time to bring it all together. Place one cake layer on your serving plate and spread a layer of frosting over the top. Then carefully set your second cake layer on top, and finish by coating the entire cake with the remaining frosting.

A Dash of Southern Charm

Your Savannah Red Velvet Cake is now complete. Garnish it with fresh strawberries, a dollop of whipped cream, or a sprinkle of edible flowers for a charming final touch. Remember to serve it with a big smile, for a taste of Southern hospitality can make any meal feel like a party.

May your kitchen be filled with the indulgent aroma of the Savannah Red Velvet Cake, creating memories as rich and deep as the flavor itself. Offer this classic dessert to those you love, and share the charm of the South with every bite!
