Delicious Nut-Free Zucchini Bread Recipe


Zucchini bread is a classic treat that is loved by many. However, for those with nut allergies, finding a delicious nut-free version can be a challenge. But fear not, because I have the perfect recipe for you! This nut-free zucchini bread is moist, flavorful, and packed with wholesome ingredients. It’s the perfect way to use up those summer zucchinis and satisfy your sweet tooth. So let’s dive right in and get baking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated zucchini
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the grated zucchini, melted butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter, as this can result in a dense loaf.

  4. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  5. Once baked, remove the zucchini bread from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This nut-free zucchini bread is delicious on its own, but you can also get creative with your toppings and additions. Here are a few serving suggestions to enhance your zucchini bread experience:

  • Spread a generous amount of cream cheese frosting on top for a sweet and tangy twist.
  • Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful dessert.
  • Toast a slice and spread it with butter or your favorite nut-free spread for a tasty breakfast treat.
  • Add some chopped dried fruits, such as raisins or cranberries, to the batter before baking for an extra burst of flavor.

Storage

To keep your nut-free zucchini bread fresh and moist, store it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. Simply thaw it at room temperature before enjoying.

Final Thoughts

This nut-free zucchini bread recipe is a winner for both those with nut allergies and those without. It’s a versatile treat that can be enjoyed any time of the day, whether as a snack, dessert, or breakfast option. The grated zucchini adds moisture and a subtle vegetable flavor, making it a healthier alternative to traditional breads. So grab those zucchinis from your garden or local market and get baking! Your taste buds will thank you.
