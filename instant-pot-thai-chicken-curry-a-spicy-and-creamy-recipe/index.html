Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry: A Spicy and Creamy Recipe


If you’re a fan of rich, bold flavors and creamy textures, then this Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry will soon become one of your go-to dishes. The Instant Pot makes this Thai curry not only quick but also incredibly easy to prepare, allowing you to enjoy a delicious and authentic meal in under an hour. Bursting with flavors from fragrant spices, coconut milk, and tender chicken, this recipe is perfect for weeknight dinners or when you’re craving something comforting and spicy.

Why You’ll Love This Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry

This Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry is everything you could want in a dish: it’s flavorful, creamy, and has just the right amount of spice. The Instant Pot cuts down cooking time while infusing the chicken and vegetables with the rich, aromatic flavors typical of Thai curry. Plus, it’s customizable, so whether you prefer a milder or spicier curry, this recipe can easily be adjusted to suit your taste buds.

Key Ingredients

The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity and the use of authentic ingredients that come together to create a mouthwatering curry. Below are the key ingredients you’ll need:

  • Chicken: Boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts work well in this dish. Chicken thighs are particularly juicy and tender, making them the ideal choice for curry.
  • Coconut Milk: The creamy base of this curry comes from full-fat coconut milk. It adds richness and a subtle sweetness that balances the heat from the curry paste.
  • Thai Red Curry Paste: This is the heart of the dish. Red curry paste contains a mix of red chilies, garlic, lemongrass, ginger, and other spices, giving the curry its signature bold flavor. You can adjust the amount of curry paste to control the level of spiciness.
  • Vegetables: Commonly used vegetables in Thai curry include bell peppers, carrots, and onions. These add texture, color, and a slight sweetness to the dish.
  • Fish Sauce: This fermented condiment adds depth and umami flavor, a characteristic of many Thai dishes. It’s an essential ingredient, so don’t skip it!
  • Lime: Fresh lime juice brightens up the curry and adds a zesty, tangy element that cuts through the richness of the coconut milk.
  • Fresh Basil and Cilantro: These fresh herbs are added at the end for a burst of freshness and a lovely aroma.

Instructions for Making Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by preparing all your ingredients. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces (if using chicken breasts, feel free to shred it after cooking). Chop your vegetables into even-sized pieces so they cook evenly. You’ll also want to mince the garlic and ginger to release their flavors more effectively.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Once it’s hot, add a tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and chopped onions. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes, or until the onions are soft and translucent. The aroma of the garlic and ginger will fill the air, giving you a preview of the deliciousness to come.

Step 3: Add the Curry Paste

Once the aromatics have softened, add the red curry paste to the pot. Stir it into the garlic, ginger, and onions, allowing it to cook for about 1-2 minutes. This helps to bloom the curry paste, releasing all the spices and oils, which will give your curry that deep, complex flavor.

Step 4: Add the Chicken and Vegetables

Now, add the chicken pieces to the pot. Stir them around, ensuring they are coated with the curry paste. Then, add the chopped bell peppers, carrots, and any other vegetables you prefer. Stir everything together so the vegetables are evenly distributed.

Step 5: Pour in the Coconut Milk and Fish Sauce

Next, pour in the full-fat coconut milk. The creamy coconut milk will form the base of the curry, creating that luscious, velvety texture. Add the fish sauce, sugar (if desired), and a pinch of salt. Stir to combine everything, ensuring the chicken and vegetables are evenly submerged in the coconut milk.

Step 6: Pressure Cook the Curry

Secure the lid on your Instant Pot and set the valve to the “Sealing” position. Set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 8 minutes. Once the cooking cycle completes, allow the pressure to naturally release for 5 minutes, then manually release any remaining pressure.

Step 7: Finish the Curry

Once the pressure has fully released, remove the lid, and give your curry a good stir. The chicken should be tender and fully cooked, and the vegetables should still have a slight crunch. Taste the curry, and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add more fish sauce for saltiness, a squeeze of lime for tang, or more curry paste for extra heat.

Step 8: Serve and Garnish

Spoon the curry into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped cilantro and Thai basil. These herbs add a vibrant freshness that contrasts nicely with the richness of the curry. If you like a little extra heat, you can add a few slices of fresh chili on top.

Step 9: Enjoy!

Serve this Thai chicken curry with a side of jasmine rice or rice noodles. The rice helps absorb the flavorful sauce, making every bite even more satisfying. For an extra treat, you can even serve it with a side of crispy spring rolls or Thai-style papaya salad.

Tips for the Perfect Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry

While this recipe is relatively easy to follow, there are a few tips and tricks that will help you elevate your Thai curry game:

1. Use the Right Curry Paste

The quality of your curry paste will play a significant role in the flavor of your curry. Choose a good-quality Thai red curry paste for the best taste. If you can’t find red curry paste, you can also experiment with yellow or green curry paste for a different flavor profile.

2. Adjust the Spice Level

Thai red curry paste can be quite spicy, so feel free to adjust the amount you use based on your heat tolerance. If you prefer a milder curry, start with one or two tablespoons of paste and taste before adding more. On the other hand, if you love spice, don’t be afraid to go heavier on the curry paste or even add some fresh chilies.

3. Use Fresh Coconut Milk

While canned coconut milk works perfectly in this recipe, using fresh coconut milk will take your curry to the next level. Fresh coconut milk has a lighter, more natural flavor that complements the other ingredients beautifully.

4. Let the Curry Rest

After the curry is finished cooking, allow it to sit for a few minutes before serving. This gives the flavors a chance to meld together, making each bite more flavorful. Plus, it allows the sauce to thicken slightly.

5. Customize Your Veggies

While bell peppers and carrots are traditional in Thai curries, feel free to add other vegetables such as zucchini, eggplant, or bamboo shoots. The Instant Pot will cook everything evenly, so you don’t need to worry about overcooking your veggies.

Making This Recipe Ahead of Time

One of the best parts about this Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry is that it can be made ahead of time. If you’re preparing for a busy week, simply follow the recipe up to the point of pressure cooking, then store the curry in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. Reheat the curry on the stovetop or in the microwave, and serve it fresh with rice.

You can also freeze this curry for up to 3 months. To freeze, allow the curry to cool completely, then transfer it to freezer-safe containers. When you’re ready to enjoy it, defrost it overnight in the refrigerator and reheat it on the stovetop.

Nutritional Information

This Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry is a balanced and nutritious meal, especially when paired with a side of rice. Here’s a general breakdown of the nutritional content per serving (based on 4 servings):

  • Calories: 400-500 (depending on the amount of coconut milk and rice used)
  • Protein: 30g (from the chicken)
  • Fat: 25g (from the coconut milk)
  • Carbohydrates: 20g (mainly from the vegetables and rice)
  • Fiber: 4g (from the veggies)

It’s a satisfying dish that offers a good source of protein and healthy fats from the coconut milk, making it perfect for a hearty meal.

Final Thoughts

This Instant Pot Thai Chicken Curry recipe is an absolute game-changer. It’s packed with rich flavors, creamy coconut milk, and tender chicken, all balanced with the perfect amount of heat. The Instant Pot makes it so easy to prepare, cutting down the cooking time while delivering a dish that’s full of depth and authenticity. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or meal prepping for the week, this recipe is sure to impress!

Try this recipe, and you’ll soon be making it on repeat. It’s the perfect fusion of convenience and flavor, making it an essential addition to your dinner rotation!
