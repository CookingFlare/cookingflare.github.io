How to Make Middle Eastern Rice Pudding (Roz Bel Laban)


Rice pudding is a beloved dessert that spans cultures and continents. In the Middle East, one of the most iconic variations of rice pudding is Roz Bel Laban, a comforting and creamy dessert made with rice, milk, sugar, and a touch of delicate flavoring. It’s a dish that is cherished across the region, enjoyed as a treat for celebrations, family gatherings, or simply as a sweet comfort at the end of a meal. If you’ve ever had the chance to experience Middle Eastern cuisine, Roz Bel Laban is a dish that embodies the rich flavors and traditions of the area.

In this post, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of creating this delightful dessert at home, from selecting the right ingredients to serving it with traditional garnishes.

What is Roz Bel Laban?

Roz Bel Laban, often translated as “rice with milk,” is a classic Middle Eastern dessert known for its creamy texture and subtly sweet taste. The dish consists of cooked rice that is simmered in a rich mixture of milk and sugar until it thickens to a pudding-like consistency. It’s commonly flavored with ingredients such as vanilla, rose water, or orange blossom water, giving it a unique fragrance and depth of flavor. Some versions include a sprinkling of ground cinnamon or crushed pistachios on top for an extra burst of flavor and texture.

While rice pudding is enjoyed in many cultures worldwide, Roz Bel Laban has its own distinctive profile, shaped by the regional spices and ingredients that make it a special treat in the Middle East. This dessert is typically served chilled, making it perfect for warm weather or as a refreshing finale to a rich meal.

The Essential Ingredients for Roz Bel Laban

Before diving into the cooking process, it’s important to understand the key ingredients that bring this dish to life. While the recipe itself is simple, the quality of ingredients can make a huge difference in the final result.

  1. Rice: Short-grain rice is the best choice for Roz Bel Laban, as it absorbs the liquid well and releases starch to help thicken the pudding. The texture of short-grain rice is also much creamier than long-grain varieties, which makes it ideal for this dessert.

  2. Milk: Whole milk is traditionally used in Roz Bel Laban to achieve a creamy and rich texture. For a lighter version, you can substitute with 2% milk, but the dessert may not be as indulgent.

  3. Sugar: The sweetness in Roz Bel Laban comes from sugar, which balances the richness of the milk and the rice. You can adjust the amount of sugar to suit your taste, though it is typically around half a cup for a balanced flavor.

  4. Vanilla: A splash of vanilla extract adds a warm, aromatic flavor that complements the richness of the pudding. It’s a key component in rounding out the flavor profile of Roz Bel Laban.

  5. Rose Water or Orange Blossom Water: These floral waters are the signature flavorings in Middle Eastern rice puddings. Rose water provides a delicate, floral note, while orange blossom water gives a citrusy, fragrant aroma. Both are essential to creating the authentic taste of Roz Bel Laban.

  6. Cornstarch: Cornstarch is used to thicken the pudding, giving it the right consistency. It helps achieve the creamy, velvety texture that makes Roz Bel Laban so irresistible.

  7. Cinnamon and Pistachios (Optional): To garnish and add texture, cinnamon and crushed pistachios are often sprinkled on top of the pudding before serving. These additions provide a wonderful contrast to the creamy rice pudding.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Roz Bel Laban

Making Roz Bel Laban is a straightforward process, but it requires a bit of patience to ensure the rice and milk cook together perfectly. Follow these detailed steps to create a rich, creamy, and flavorful Middle Eastern rice pudding:

Step 1: Rinse the Rice

Start by rinsing your short-grain rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky. Itâ€™s important to use the right type of rice for this dessert, as it will directly affect the final texture.

Step 2: Cook the Rice

In a large saucepan, add the rinsed rice and cover it with water. Bring the water to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the rice is fully cooked. This should take about 10-15 minutes, depending on the type of rice you’re using. Once the rice is soft and tender, drain any excess water.

Step 3: Prepare the Milk Mixture

In the same saucepan, pour in the whole milk, and add the sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar. At this point, you can add the vanilla extract, rose water, or orange blossom water, depending on your preference. These flavorings give Roz Bel Laban its signature taste and fragrance.

Step 4: Thicken the Pudding

While the milk is warming up, dissolve the cornstarch in a small amount of cold water to create a smooth slurry. Once the milk mixture is hot but not boiling, add the cornstarch slurry, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue cooking the mixture for 5-10 minutes, until it begins to thicken and has a pudding-like consistency.

Step 5: Combine Rice and Milk

Once the milk mixture has thickened, add the cooked rice to the saucepan. Stir the rice into the milk mixture and let it simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes, ensuring that the rice absorbs some of the milk and becomes even creamier. Continue stirring occasionally to prevent the pudding from sticking to the bottom of the pan.

Step 6: Cool the Pudding

Once the rice pudding has reached the desired thickness, remove the pan from the heat. Let it cool to room temperature, stirring occasionally to keep the rice from clumping together. Once it has cooled slightly, transfer the pudding to individual serving bowls or a large dish.

Step 7: Chill the Pudding

After the pudding has cooled to room temperature, place it in the refrigerator to chill for at least two hours. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the texture to set. The cold pudding is what gives Roz Bel Laban its delightful, refreshing quality.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Before serving, sprinkle the rice pudding with a dusting of cinnamon or ground pistachios for an added burst of flavor and texture. You can also add a few extra drops of rose water or orange blossom water on top to enhance the aroma.

Serve the pudding cold, and enjoy the smooth, creamy goodness of Roz Bel Laban!

Tips for Perfecting Your Roz Bel Laban

  • Adjust the sweetness: The sweetness of the pudding is highly customizable. Taste the milk mixture as you go, and feel free to add more sugar if you prefer a sweeter pudding. The amount of sugar in this recipe is based on traditional Middle Eastern preferences, but it can be reduced or increased according to your taste.

  • Experiment with flavorings: While rose water and orange blossom water are traditional, you can experiment with other flavorings such as a hint of saffron for a luxurious twist, or even a little bit of ground cardamom for a spiced note.

  • Texture variation: If you prefer a smoother pudding, you can blend the pudding with an immersion blender after cooking. For a more textured version, leave the rice in its whole form.

  • Use full-fat milk: For a richer pudding, always opt for whole milk. This will give you the best creamy texture. Skim milk or plant-based alternatives may result in a thinner, less indulgent pudding.

  • Vegan adaptation: If you’re looking for a vegan version, substitute the dairy milk with coconut milk, almond milk, or another plant-based milk of your choice. You can also replace the cornstarch with arrowroot powder for thickening.

Serving Suggestions

Roz Bel Laban can be served as a standalone dessert, or it can be paired with other Middle Eastern treats such as baklava, kanafeh, or ma’amoul cookies. Its creamy texture and subtly sweet flavor complement a variety of other dishes, making it a perfect addition to a Middle Eastern feast.

This rice pudding is also great as an after-dinner dessert when you need something light yet satisfying. Its chilled, creamy consistency makes it especially refreshing during hot summer months.

Variations of Roz Bel Laban

While the basic recipe for Roz Bel Laban is quite traditional, there are numerous ways to make this dish your own. Here are a few variations to consider:

  • Pistachio Roz Bel Laban: After cooking the pudding, fold in finely chopped pistachios for added texture and flavor. Garnish with more crushed pistachios on top before serving.

  • Saffron Rice Pudding: For a luxurious touch, you can add a few strands of saffron to the milk mixture. This will infuse the pudding with a beautiful color and a subtle floral flavor.

  • Chocolate Rice Pudding: If you’re a chocolate lover, consider stirring in some cocoa powder or melted chocolate once the pudding is off the heat. This creates a rich, decadent version of Roz Bel Laban.

  • Fruit Toppings: While traditionally served plain or with cinnamon and pistachios, you can top your rice pudding with fresh berries, pomegranate seeds, or dried fruits like apricots for a burst of color and flavor.
