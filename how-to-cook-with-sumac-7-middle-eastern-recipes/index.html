How to Cook with Sumac: 7 Middle Eastern Recipes


Sumac is a transformative spice with a vivid reddish-purple hue, offering a tangy, citrusy flavor that complements a variety of dishes. Its versatility and brightness have made it a cornerstone in Middle Eastern cuisine—and it’s time for you to learn how to harness its magic in your cooking. Below are seven inspiring recipes that showcase the brilliance of sumac while giving you a chance to explore the rich, bold flavors of the Middle East.

1. Sumac Chicken with Onions and Za’atar

This classic Levantine dish centers around juicy, roast chicken infused with sumac’s citrusy notes. Serve it with warm pita or fragrant basmati rice for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large red onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons ground sumac
  • 1 tablespoon za’atar
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the olive oil, sumac, za’atar, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken pieces and sliced onions, ensuring even coating.
  3. Spread the chicken and onions on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Sprinkle any remaining marinade over the top.
  4. Roast for 40–45 minutes or until the chicken is golden brown and fully cooked.
  5. Serve hot, paired with a side of yogurt for added creaminess.

2. Sumac-Spiced Lentil Soup

Sumac’s bright tang elevates the earthy, hearty flavors in this lentil soup. It’s a simple yet satisfying dish perfect for chilly evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground sumac
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the lentils, cumin, and vegetable stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes or until the lentils are soft.
  3. Stir in the sumac, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup to your desired consistency.
  4. Garnish with additional sumac and serve with crusty bread.

3. Fattoush Salad

Fattoush is a vibrant Lebanese salad that perfectly blends fresh vegetables, crispy pita chips, and a tangy sumac dressing. It’s light, refreshing, and quick to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pita breads
  • 3 cups mixed lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sumac
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toast the pita bread until crisp. Break it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, bell pepper, and radishes.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, sumac, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to make the dressing. Pour over the salad and toss well.
  4. Sprinkle the crispy pita on top and serve immediately.

4. Sumac Roasted Vegetables

Roasting vegetables with sumac enhances their natural sweetness while infusing them with a tangy twist—perfect as a side dish or a vegetarian main.

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground sumac
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the vegetables with olive oil, sumac, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the vegetables on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 25–30 minutes, tossing halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with extra sumac for added brightness.

5. Sumac-Dusted Grilled Fish

Sumac’s tangy flavor pairs beautifully with fish, especially when cooked over a grill. This recipe works equally well with fillets like salmon, sea bass, or snapper.

Ingredients:

  • 4 fish fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground sumac
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rub the fish fillets with olive oil, sumac, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium heat.
  3. Cook the fish for 3–4 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets.
  4. Serve with lemon wedges and a simple cucumber-yogurt salad for a refreshing accompaniment.

6. Labneh with Sumac and Olive Oil

Labneh, a thick and tangy strained yogurt, transforms into an irresistible dip with the addition of sumac and olive oil. Perfect with fresh bread or raw vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups labneh (or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground sumac
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Spread the labneh onto a shallow serving dish.
  2. Drizzle olive oil over the top and sprinkle with sumac, minced garlic (if using), and salt.
  3. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve with warm pita, crudités, or crackers.

7. Sumac-Infused Rice Pilaf

This aromatic rice dish brings depth to any meal, thanks to the gentle zestiness of sumac and the warm undertones of Middle Eastern spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 3 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground sumac
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • ¼ cup slivered almonds, toasted
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Heat the butter or olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until golden.
  3. Stir in the rice, sumac, cinnamon, and salt, coating the grains in the spiced oil.
  4. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 15–20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork and stir in the toasted almonds before serving.

Sumac is more than just a seasoning; it’s a gateway to the vibrant, aromatic world of Middle Eastern cuisine. Incorporating these recipes into your repertoire will give you a deeper appreciation for this zesty spice while expanding your culinary horizons. Whether you’re crafting a comforting family dinner or impressing guests with exotic flavors, sumac has a rightful place on your spice rack. Happy cooking!
