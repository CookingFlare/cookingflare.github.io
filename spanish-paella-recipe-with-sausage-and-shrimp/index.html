Spanish Paella Recipe with Sausage and Shrimp


If you’re looking to add a touch of Spanish flavor to your culinary repertoire, then look no further than this delicious and authentic Spanish Paella recipe. Bursting with the vibrant colors and flavors of saffron-infused rice, succulent shrimp, and savory sausage, this dish is a true celebration of Spanish cuisine. So roll up your sleeves, grab your apron, and let’s dive into the world of Paella!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Arborio rice
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound of Spanish chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1 teaspoon of saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, combine the saffron threads with 2 tablespoons of warm water. Set aside and let the saffron infuse.

  2. Heat a large paella pan or a wide and shallow skillet over medium heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil and sauté the sliced chorizo until lightly browned. Remove the chorizo from the pan and set aside.

  3. In the same pan, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced bell peppers. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant.

  4. Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir to coat it with the flavors of the vegetables. Cook for a few minutes until the rice turns translucent.

  5. Pour in the chicken broth, along with the infused saffron water and smoked paprika. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir, then bring the mixture to a gentle boil.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the rice simmer, uncovered, for about 15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

  7. After 15 minutes, arrange the shrimp and cooked chorizo on top of the rice. Sprinkle the frozen peas evenly over the pan. Cover the pan with a lid or aluminum foil and continue cooking for another 10-15 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.

  8. Once the shrimp are cooked, remove the pan from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the rice to absorb any remaining liquid.

  9. Serve the Spanish Paella hot, garnished with fresh parsley and lemon wedges for squeezing over the dish. The vibrant colors and enticing aroma will surely impress your guests.

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize this recipe by adding other seafood such as mussels or clams.
  • For a vegetarian version, omit the sausage and shrimp and add more vegetables like artichokes and green beans.
  • If you don’t have saffron, you can substitute it with turmeric for a similar golden color.
  • Remember to use a wide and shallow pan to ensure the rice cooks evenly.
  • Leftover paella can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day, but it’s best when eaten fresh.

Now that you have the recipe for this classic Spanish Paella with sausage and shrimp, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking! The flavors and aromas of this dish will transport you straight to the sunny streets of Spain. So invite your friends and family, uncork a bottle of Spanish wine, and savor the taste of Spain right in your own home. ¡Buen provecho!
