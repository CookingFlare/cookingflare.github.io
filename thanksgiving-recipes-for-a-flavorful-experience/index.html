Thanksgiving Recipes for a Flavorful Experience


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, sharing beautiful memories, and indulging in delicious food. This year, elevate your feast with these unique, mouth-watering recipes that will leave your family and friends asking for seconds. From appetizers to desserts, discover a variety of dishes that will make your Thanksgiving celebration unforgettable.

Thanksgiving Appetizers

Roasted Garlic and Rosemary Pub Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. round of Brie
  • 4 oz. goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup crushed graham crackers
  • 1/2 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Cut Brie into slices and layer goat cheese on top.
  3. In a small bowl, combine graham crackers, Parmesan cheese, and half of the olive oil.
  4. Spread the crumb mixture over the cheeses.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, rosemary, remaining olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Drizzle the garlic and rosemary mixture over the crumb topping.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve warm with crackers or crusty bread.

Cranberry and Pecan Cheese Ball

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup freshly picked cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix cream cheese, chopped pecans, cranberries, and honey until well combined.
  2. Season with salt.
  3. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  4. On a serving platter, shape the mixture into a ball.
  5. Serve with crackers or sliced bread.

Main Dishes

Turkey with Scarve Gravy

Ingredients (for 10-12 people):

  • 1 12-15 pound turkey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Rub salt and pepper onto the turkey.
  3. Melt butter and let it solidify.
  4. In a large baking dish, place the turkey breast-side up and cover with the solidified butter.
  5. In a covered roasting pan, add chicken broth and water.
  6. Roast the turkey for approximately 3 hours, basting occasionally with the pan juices.
  7. To make gravy, remove the turkey from the oven and let it rest.
  8. In a medium saucepan, whisk together flour and reserved pan juices.
  9. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  10. Add water to adjust the consistency, if needed.
  11. Serve the turkey with the flavorful gravy.

Glazed Spiral Ham

Ingredients (for 8-10 people):

  • 7-8 pound fully-cooked spiral ham
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon whole-grain mustard
  • 2 tablespoons butter, at room temperature

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a saucepan, combine brown sugar, pineapple juice, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, and whole-grain mustard.
  3. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture blends smoothly.
  4. Remove all but 1 tablespoon of butter from the original wrapping.
  5. Place the spiral ham in a roasting pan and evenly brush with butter.
  6. Bake for approximately 10 minutes per pound, basting occasionally with the glaze.
  7. Serve heated.

Side Dishes

Sautéed Green Beans with Almonds

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil.
  2. Add green beans and cook for approximately 5-6 minutes, until tender.
  3. Drain the beans and pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
  4. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  5. Add sliced almonds and sauté until golden brown.
  6. Add the green beans to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, until heated through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve warm with your favorite Thanksgiving dishes.

Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallow Topping

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3/4 cup butter, at room temperature
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large bowl, combine mashed sweet potatoes, brown sugar, eggs, milk, butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Mix everything until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until heated through.
  6. Remove from the oven and evenly spread the chopped marshmallows over the top.
  7. Place the dish under a broiler and broil for 2-3 minutes, until the marshmallows are golden brown.
  8. Serve warm.

Desserts

Pumpkin Pie

Ingredients (for 8 people):

  • 1 9-inch unbaked pie crust
  • 2 cups canned pumpkin purée
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons white cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • A pinch of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large bowl, mix pumpkin purée, granulated sugar, brown sugar, white cornstarch, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and salt.
  3. Add eggs and heavy cream, and mix until everything is well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into the unbaked pie crust.
  5. Bake for 10 minutes at 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  6. Reduce oven temperature to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and bake for another 40-50 minutes.
  7. Let the pie cool and serve with a dollop of whipped cream.

Cranberry and Orange Bread Pudding

Ingredients (for 6 people):

  • 2 cups day-old bread, cubed
  • 1 cup cranberries
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup orange juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large bowl, soak bread cubes in milk for 15-20 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix eggs, granulated sugar, flour, vanilla extract, and cinnamon.
  4. Stir in cranberries and raisins.
  5. Add the bread and egg mixture together.
  6. Grease a 9x5 inch baking dish and pour the melted butter in the dish, making sure the butter covers the entire surface.
  7. Pour the bread and egg mixture into the prepared baking dish.
  8. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until golden brown.
  9. Meanwhile, in a saucepan, heat the orange juice over medium heat.
  10. Serve the bread pudding warm, drizzled with the orange juice.

Turkey enthusiasts, get ready to bring a burst of flavors to your Thanksgiving table with these delectable, distinctive recipes that are sure to impress your loved ones this holiday season. From appetizers to desserts, your feast is sure to be unforgettable as you savor every delicious bite. Happy Thanksgiving!
