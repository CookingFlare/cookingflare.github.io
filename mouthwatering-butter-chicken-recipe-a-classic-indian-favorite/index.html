Mouthwatering Butter Chicken Recipe: A Classic Indian Favorite


Butter chicken, known as “Murgh Makhani,” is a beloved dish that has captured the hearts of food lovers around the globe. This North Indian classic features tender chicken pieces marinated in a blend of spices, cooked in a rich and creamy tomato sauce, and finished with a touch of butter. The result is a dish that is not only bursting with flavor but also wonderfully comforting. In this post, we will delve into the origins of butter chicken, explore its ingredients, and guide you through the step-by-step process of creating this mouthwatering dish in your own kitchen.

The Origins of Butter Chicken

Butter chicken traces its roots back to the 1950s in Delhi, India. It is said to have been created by the chefs at the Moti Mahal restaurant, who were looking for a way to utilize leftover chicken. They simmered the chicken in a luscious tomato-based gravy made with butter, cream, and a medley of spices. This innovative dish quickly gained popularity and has since become a staple in Indian households and restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this authentic butter chicken recipe, gather the following ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • Chicken: 500 grams of boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into cubes.
  • Yogurt: 1 cup (preferably thick yogurt or Greek yogurt).
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tablespoons.
  • Lemon juice: 2 tablespoons.
  • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon.
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste).
  • Turmeric powder: ½ teaspoon.
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon.

For the Gravy:

  • Butter: 3 tablespoons (unsalted).
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (to balance the butter).
  • Onion: 1 large onion, finely chopped.
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon.
  • Tomatoes: 3 large tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped.
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste).
  • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon.
  • Kasuri methi: 1 teaspoon (dried fenugreek leaves).
  • Heavy cream: ½ cup.
  • Salt: to taste.
  • Fresh coriander: for garnish.

Preparing the Chicken Marinade

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well until smooth. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight. This step allows the flavors to penetrate the chicken, making it tender and flavorful.

Cooking the Butter Chicken

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

  1. Heat the Pan: In a large skillet or heavy-bottomed pan, heat the butter and oil over medium heat. The combination of butter and oil prevents the butter from burning and adds a rich flavor to the dish.

  2. Sauté Onions: Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. This process should take about 8–10 minutes. The caramelization of the onions will contribute to the depth of flavor in the gravy.

  3. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

Step 2: Cook the Tomatoes

  1. Incorporate Tomatoes: Add the pureed or chopped tomatoes to the pan. Cook for about 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the tomatoes are softened and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.

  2. Add Spices: Mix in the red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Allow the spices to bloom in the tomato mixture for a couple of minutes.

Step 3: Add the Marinated Chicken

  1. Cook the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan, stirring to coat them in the tomato sauce. Cook for about 10–15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. You can cover the pan to help the chicken cook evenly.

  2. Add Cream and Fenugreek: Once the chicken is cooked, reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Add the kasuri methi for an aromatic touch. Simmer the gravy for an additional 5–10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Adjust Consistency: If you prefer a thinner gravy, you can add a splash of water or more cream, depending on your taste.

Final Touches

Before serving, taste the butter chicken and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You may want to add more salt or spice according to your preference. Once satisfied, remove the pan from heat and garnish with freshly chopped coriander for a burst of freshness.

Serving Suggestions

Butter chicken is best enjoyed with:

  • Naan: Soft, pillowy naan bread is perfect for scooping up the creamy chicken.
  • Rice: Steamed basmati rice or jeera rice complements the dish beautifully, soaking up the delicious gravy.
  • Raita: A side of cucumber or mint raita can provide a cooling contrast to the spices in the dish.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers, butter chicken can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, place it in a saucepan over low heat, adding a splash of water or cream to loosen the gravy.

Tips for the Perfect Butter Chicken

  • Marination Time: The longer you marinate the chicken, the more flavorful and tender it will be. Overnight marination is ideal.
  • Use Quality Ingredients: Fresh spices and high-quality chicken will significantly enhance the dish’s flavor.
  • Adjusting Spice Levels: If you’re sensitive to heat, reduce the amount of red chili powder. You can also add a bit of sugar to balance the spice.
  • Experiment with Ingredients: Feel free to add vegetables like bell peppers or peas for added texture and nutrition.

Conclusion

Butter chicken is more than just a dish; it’s an experience that brings warmth and comfort to the dining table. With its rich, creamy sauce and tender chicken, it’s no wonder this classic Indian favorite is loved by many. Whether you’re making it for a special occasion or a weeknight dinner, this recipe is sure to impress family and friends alike. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in the deliciousness of homemade butter chicken!
