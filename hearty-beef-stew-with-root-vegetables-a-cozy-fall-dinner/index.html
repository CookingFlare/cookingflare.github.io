Hearty Beef Stew with Root Vegetables: A Cozy Fall Dinner


Fall is the season of comfort food, and what better way to warm up on a chilly evening than with a hearty bowl of beef stew? This classic dish is not only delicious but also packed with wholesome ingredients that will keep you satisfied. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a flavorful beef stew with root vegetables that will become a staple in your fall dinner rotation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 turnips, peeled and diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat in batches, ensuring that each piece is browned on all sides. This step helps to seal in the flavors and create a rich, savory base for the stew.

  2. Once all the meat is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the carrots, parsnips, turnips, and potatoes to the pot. Stir well to combine the vegetables with the onion and garlic. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Return the beef stew meat to the pot, along with any juices that may have accumulated. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). The wine adds a depth of flavor to the stew, but it can be omitted if preferred.

  5. Stir in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. These ingredients will enhance the taste and aroma of the stew, creating a robust and comforting dish.

  6. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

  7. Once the stew is cooked, remove the bay leaves and discard them. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If desired, you can thicken the stew by creating a slurry with cornstarch and water, then stirring it into the pot. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes to allow the stew to thicken.

  8. Serve the hearty beef stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. This dish pairs perfectly with crusty bread or fluffy mashed potatoes, making it a complete and satisfying fall dinner.

Conclusion:

With its tender beef, flavorful root vegetables, and rich broth, this hearty beef stew is the perfect comfort food for those cozy fall evenings. The combination of ingredients creates a dish that is both satisfying and nourishing, warming you from the inside out. So go ahead and give this recipe a try, and savor the flavors of fall with every spoonful. Happy cooking!
