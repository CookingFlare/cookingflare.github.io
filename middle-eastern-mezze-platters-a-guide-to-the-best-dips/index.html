Middle Eastern Mezze Platters: A Guide to the Best Dips


Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and vibrant dishes that celebrate shared gatherings and communal dining. Among its many treasures, the mezze platter holds a special place, offering an array of dips, spreads, and small bites that are perfect for entertaining, snacking, or simply enjoying a casual feast. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best Middle Eastern dips that make a mezze platter truly unforgettable.

The Mezze Philosophy

The concept of mezze revolves around sharing. It’s about bringing people together to savor a collection of dishes that balance salty, tangy, creamy, and savory elements. A mezze platter often features dips and spreads alongside pita bread, grilled vegetables, olives, nuts, and sometimes bite-sized portions of meat or seafood. What makes mezze so versatile is its adaptability—it can be as simple or elaborate as you like, depending on the occasion.

Dips are the cornerstone of any mezze platter. Each one adds unique flavors and textures that complement the other dishes on the table. While variations exist across the Middle East, certain dips are universally loved and iconic within the culinary world.

Must-Have Mezze Dips

Hummus

No mezze platter is complete without hummus, arguably the most well-known Middle Eastern dip. Made from pureed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, and a touch of garlic, hummus is as nutritious as it is flavorful. Its creamy texture can be enhanced with toppings like paprika, cumin, pine nuts, or drizzles of high-quality olive oil.

The beauty of hummus lies in its versatility. While classic hummus is a crowd-pleaser, regional variations abound. Roasted red pepper hummus, beet hummus, or spicy harissa-infused hummus can elevate your mezze platter with creative and colorful twists. Fresh vegetables like carrots, cucumbers, and bell peppers serve as dipping vessels, while warm pita bread makes for the perfect pairing.

Baba Ghanoush

For those who love smoky and earthy flavors, baba ghanoush is a must-have. This dip is crafted from roasted and charred eggplants that are blended with tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, and garlic. The result is a luscious, smoky spread that captures the essence of Middle Eastern cuisine.

To make a truly exceptional baba ghanoush, choosing the right eggplant is essential. Opt for medium-sized eggplants with smooth skin and fewer seeds. Charring the eggplants over an open flame or grill is key to achieving that signature smoky taste. Once blended, you can garnish baba ghanoush with pomegranate seeds, parsley, or a dash of sumac for added visual appeal and flavor.

Labneh

Labneh is the answer for those seeking a creamy, tangy dip that’s lighter than traditional cream-based spreads. This strained yogurt is thick and velvety, making it ideal for dipping, spreading, or even drizzling. Labneh is often seasoned with olive oil, za’atar, or fresh herbs to bring out its vibrant flavors.

Creating labneh at home is surprisingly straightforward. Simply strain plain yogurt through a cheesecloth to remove excess liquid, leaving behind a rich and creamy base. Serve it alongside fresh pita bread, crunchy radishes, or cucumber slices. Its refreshing taste also balances the spicier elements of a mezze platter beautifully.

Muhammara

Originating from Syria, muhammara is a flavorful dip made from roasted red peppers, walnuts, olive oil, and a touch of pomegranate molasses. The combination of sweet, smoky, and nutty flavors makes this dip a standout addition to any mezze platter.

The secret to the perfect muhammara is roasting the peppers to bring out their natural sweetness and achieving a smooth yet slightly textured consistency. While walnuts are the traditional choice, some variations may include almonds or breadcrumbs for added texture. Muhammara pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, flatbreads, or simply as a bold dip for raw vegetables.

Tzatziki

Though often associated with Greek cuisine, tzatziki has found its way into Middle Eastern mezze culture as a refreshing yogurt-based dip. Made with strained yogurt, cucumber, garlic, olive oil, and dill, tzatziki is light, cool, and herbaceous—an ideal counterpoint to spicier mezze dishes.

What sets tzatziki apart is its adaptability. You can adjust its tanginess by varying the amount of lemon juice, or boost its herbal character with fresh mint. It’s perfect for dipping or dolloping over grilled kebabs, roasted vegetables, or crispy falafel.

Toum

Garlic lovers, rejoice! Toum is a bold and creamy Lebanese garlic dip that brings an intense punch of flavor to the mezze table. Made from raw garlic emulsified with oil, lemon juice, and water, this dip is undeniably robust and pairs beautifully with grilled meats, seafood, or vegetables.

The key to making toum is patience. Emulsification requires slow and steady blending to achieve the desired consistency without curdling. What results is a smooth, fluffy dip with unmistakable garlicky notes that will leave you craving more.

Tarator

Tarator, often considered the predecessor of modern tahini-based dips, is a simple yet timeless addition to mezze platters. At its base, tarator is made with tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and water, but it’s often spruced up with parsley or toasted pine nuts for added texture and flavor.

It serves as a versatile complement, whether drizzled over falafel, used as a dressing for salads, or simply enjoyed with warm pita bread. Its nutty, creamy profile makes tarator a universally loved choice for any mezze spread.

Building the Perfect Mezze Platter

When curating a mezze platter, balance is key. Aim to include a variety of dips that cater to different flavor profiles—creamy, smoky, tangy, spicy, and nutty. Arrange the dips in small bowls with garnishes that enhance their visual allure: a sprinkle of paprika on hummus, pomegranate seeds on baba ghanoush, or a swirl of olive oil on labneh.

Pair the dips with pita bread, flatbread crackers, or lavash, as well as fresh veggie sticks such as carrots and cucumbers. Grilled eggplant slices, roasted cauliflower, and cherry tomatoes on toothpicks can also serve as delightful accompaniments. To complete the platter, add a selection of olives, pickled vegetables, and spiced nuts to create layers of flavor and texture.

Mezze Etiquette

A mezze platter isn’t just about the food—it’s about fostering connections and creating joy around the table. Traditionally served as a communal appetizer, mezze invites people to share conversation and bites in a casual, unhurried manner. Encourage your guests to sample and savor each dip, mixing and matching flavors to discover combinations that excite their taste buds.

Making Mezze at Home

Creating a mezze platter doesn’t require mastering every dish. Start simple by focusing on one or two homemade dips and supplementing those with store-bought options. Over time, you can experiment with new recipes and ingredients, tailoring the platter to your preferences and dietary needs.

Many mezze dips can be made ahead of time, allowing you to spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the feast with friends and family. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or indulging in a solo meal, mezze platters bring the rich flavors and hospitality of the Middle East straight into your home.

Middle Eastern mezze platters celebrate the art of sharing and the magic of bold flavors. With dips like hummus, baba ghanoush, labneh, muhammara, and toum, you can craft an unforgettable culinary experience that transports diners to the vibrant streets and bustling markets of the Middle East. Whether you’re exploring new recipes or revisiting beloved classics, the world of mezze is always ready to tantalize your taste buds and inspire your creativity in the kitchen.
