Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso and Whipped Cream and Chocolate Shavings: A Valentine's Day Dessert Recipe


Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso and Whipped Cream and Chocolate Shavings: A Valentine’s Day Dessert Recipe

Valentine’s Day is a time to celebrate love, and what better way to do so than with a decadent and romantic dessert? This Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake is a delightful twist on the classic Italian dessert, combining the rich flavors of espresso, creamy mascarpone, and delicate ladyfingers. Whether you’re planning a special dinner for your significant other or hosting a Galentine’s Day gathering, this indulgent treat is sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups strong brewed espresso, cooled
  • 1 tablespoon coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 16 ounces mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 24-30 ladyfinger cookies
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Dark chocolate bar for shavings

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Espresso Mixture:

    • In a shallow dish, combine the cooled espresso and coffee liqueur, if using. Set aside.

  2. Whip the Cream:

    • In a large mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, ¼ cup of sugar, and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Transfer the whipped cream to another bowl and set aside.

  3. Prepare the Mascarpone Filling:

    • In the same mixing bowl (no need to clean it), beat the mascarpone cheese and the remaining ½ cup of sugar until smooth and creamy.

  4. Assemble the Cake:

    • Dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture for about 1-2 seconds, ensuring they are soaked but not soggy.
    • Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of an 8-inch springform pan, breaking them as needed to fit.
    • Spread half of the mascarpone filling over the ladyfingers, then top with a layer of whipped cream.
    • Repeat the layers with the remaining ladyfingers, mascarpone filling, and whipped cream.

  5. Chill and Garnish:

    • Cover the cake with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.
    • Before serving, dust the top of the cake with unsweetened cocoa powder and use a vegetable peeler to create chocolate shavings from the dark chocolate bar. Sprinkle the chocolate shavings over the cake for a beautiful finish.

Serving:

This Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake is best enjoyed chilled, straight from the refrigerator. Slice it into generous portions and serve with a side of fresh berries or a drizzle of raspberry coulis for an extra touch of romance. Pair it with a glass of dessert wine or a frothy cappuccino to complete the experience.

Final Thoughts:

With its luscious layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers, velvety mascarpone filling, and dreamy whipped cream, this Tiramisu Cake is a labor of love that’s worth every moment spent in the kitchen. Whether you’re celebrating with your sweetheart, friends, or simply indulging in a solo treat, this dessert is guaranteed to make hearts flutter on Valentine’s Day.

So, go ahead and embark on this culinary journey to create a dessert that’s as timeless as love itself. It’s a sweet reminder that, indeed, till death do us part, there will always be Tiramisu to bring us together in sweetness and joy. Cheers to love and delicious desserts!
