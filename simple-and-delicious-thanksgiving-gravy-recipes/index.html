Simple and Delicious Thanksgiving Gravy Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time to come together with family and friends to share delicious food, and there’s one dish that stands out every year: the gravy. The rich, flavorful sauce made from turkey drippings, stock, and seasonings elevates your entire meal. Whether you’re serving roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, or stuffing, the right gravy can make all the difference. While it may seem like a daunting task, making homemade gravy is actually quite simple and rewarding. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of gravy recipes that are sure to impress your guests and elevate your Thanksgiving spread.

The Basics of Gravy Making

Before diving into specific recipes, it’s important to understand the basic components of making gravy. At its core, gravy is made from fat, flour, liquid, and seasonings. The key to making a great gravy lies in the balance of these elements. The fat comes from the drippings of your turkey, which give the gravy its rich flavor. The flour helps to thicken the gravy, while the liquid—usually turkey stock or broth—adds the necessary volume and flavor. The seasonings can include anything from salt and pepper to fresh herbs like rosemary and thyme.

The best part? Gravy is highly customizable to suit your taste, so feel free to get creative and experiment with different flavors and textures. Here are some fantastic Thanksgiving gravy recipes you can try.

Classic Turkey Gravy Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups turkey drippings (from your roasted turkey)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups turkey stock (or chicken broth)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as rosemary or thyme), optional
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (optional for extra richness)

Instructions:

  1. Collect the drippings: After your turkey has finished roasting, remove it from the roasting pan and set it aside to rest. Pour the drippings from the pan into a fat separator or a heatproof measuring cup, allowing the fat to rise to the top.

  2. Make the roux: In a medium saucepan, heat about 1/4 cup of the fat (discard the remaining fat or save it for another recipe). Once hot, add the flour and whisk it continuously for about 2-3 minutes, or until the mixture turns a golden brown. This creates a roux, which will help thicken the gravy.

  3. Add the stock: Slowly whisk in the turkey stock, a little at a time, to avoid any lumps. Continue to whisk until the gravy begins to thicken. If the gravy becomes too thick, you can add more stock or water to achieve your desired consistency.

  4. Season the gravy: Season the gravy with salt, pepper, and fresh herbs if desired. Simmer the gravy for another 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. For extra richness, stir in a tablespoon of butter at the end.

  5. Serve: Once the gravy has thickened to your liking, remove it from the heat and serve it hot over your turkey, mashed potatoes, and stuffing.

This classic turkey gravy is the foundation of a perfect Thanksgiving meal, offering a savory and smooth consistency that pairs perfectly with all your favorite sides.

Easy Make-Ahead Turkey Gravy

For many, Thanksgiving day can be stressful with all the cooking that needs to be done. The last thing you want to worry about is making gravy at the last minute. Fortunately, you can make a simple and delicious gravy ahead of time to save yourself some stress on the big day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups turkey stock
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional for a deeper flavor)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the aromatics: In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for about 5 minutes, or until the onion becomes soft and translucent.

  2. Add the stock: Pour in the turkey stock and bring it to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine. If you want a deeper, more umami-rich flavor, you can add a tablespoon of soy sauce at this point.

  3. Thicken the gravy: In a small bowl, whisk together the flour with a couple of tablespoons of water to make a smooth paste. Gradually whisk this mixture into the simmering stock. Continue to cook for 5-10 minutes, until the gravy thickens to your liking.

  4. Season and store: Season the gravy with salt and pepper. Allow it to cool slightly before transferring it to an airtight container. Store it in the refrigerator for up to three days.

  5. Reheat and serve: On Thanksgiving day, simply reheat the gravy over low heat. If the gravy is too thick, you can thin it with additional turkey stock or water.

This easy make-ahead gravy recipe will save you time and energy on Thanksgiving day, allowing you to focus on other important tasks.

Brown Butter Sage Gravy

For those who love rich, nutty flavors, brown butter sage gravy is a delicious twist on the traditional recipe. The brown butter adds a deep, toasty flavor that perfectly complements the earthiness of the sage.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups turkey stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream (optional for extra richness)

Instructions:

  1. Brown the butter: In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Continue cooking the butter, swirling the pan occasionally, until it turns golden brown and begins to smell nutty. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Add the sage: Once the butter is browned, add the chopped sage leaves and cook for another minute. The sage will infuse the butter with its earthy aroma.

  3. Make the gravy: Whisk in the flour and cook for about 2 minutes to form a roux. Slowly add the turkey stock while whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the gravy has thickened, about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Finish the gravy: Season the gravy with salt and pepper to taste. For a creamier texture, you can stir in a tablespoon of heavy cream at the end.

  5. Serve: Pour the brown butter sage gravy over your turkey or mashed potatoes for a rich and flavorful addition to your Thanksgiving feast.

This brown butter sage gravy is a fantastic alternative for anyone looking to add a gourmet touch to their holiday meal.

Mushroom Gravy for Vegetarians

Not everyone on your guest list may eat turkey, so it’s important to have a vegetarian-friendly gravy option that still packs plenty of flavor. This mushroom gravy uses earthy mushrooms as the base, making it a savory and satisfying alternative to traditional turkey gravy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced (cremini or button mushrooms work well)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the mushrooms and aromatics: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the mushrooms, onion, and garlic, cooking for about 5-7 minutes, until the mushrooms are browned and the onion is softened.

  2. Add the flour: Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to form a roux.

  3. Add the stock: Slowly add the vegetable stock, whisking to incorporate the flour mixture. Bring the gravy to a simmer, allowing it to thicken, about 5-10 minutes.

  4. Season and serve: Season the gravy with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme if desired. Pour the gravy over mashed potatoes or roasted vegetables for a hearty, vegetarian option.

This mushroom gravy is a wonderful addition to your Thanksgiving table, offering a rich, savory flavor that everyone will enjoy.

Tips for Perfect Gravy Every Time

While making gravy is fairly straightforward, there are a few tips that can help ensure your gravy turns out perfect every time:

  1. Use homemade stock: For the best flavor, use homemade turkey or chicken stock. If you don’t have time to make stock from scratch, store-bought stock works well, but opt for a high-quality, low-sodium version.

  2. Don’t rush the roux: The roux (the mixture of fat and flour) is a critical step in making gravy. Be sure to cook it until it turns golden brown, as this adds depth and flavor to the gravy.

  3. Whisk continuously: To avoid lumps in your gravy, always whisk continuously when adding the stock to the roux. This will help the ingredients combine smoothly.

  4. Adjust consistency: If your gravy is too thick, add more stock or water to thin it out. If it’s too thin, allow it to simmer for a bit longer to thicken.

  5. Flavor balance: Taste your gravy as you go and adjust the seasoning to your liking. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different herbs, spices, or even a splash of wine for extra complexity.

Conclusion

Gravy is the ultimate comfort food, and it’s a key component of any Thanksgiving meal. Whether you’re preparing the classic turkey gravy, a brown butter sage version, or a vegetarian mushroom gravy, the options are endless. With a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create the perfect gravy to complement your holiday spread. Remember, the secret to great gravy is all in the balance—so taste, adjust, and savor every bite. Happy Thanksgiving!
