Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes: A Tangy and Crispy Side


When it comes to classic Greek dishes, it’s hard to beat the irresistible flavors of Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes. This simple yet incredibly delicious side dish is a staple in Greek cuisine, known for its tangy zest and crispy texture. Whether you’re hosting a Greek-themed dinner or simply looking to add a burst of Mediterranean flavor to your meals, these potatoes are sure to impress.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the key ingredients you’ll need to recreate this mouthwatering dish:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 1/2 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is heating up, you can begin preparing the potatoes.

  2. Prep the Potatoes: After peeling the potatoes, cut them into wedges of uniform size. This ensures even cooking and a consistent texture throughout the dish.

  3. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until well combined.

  4. Coat the Potatoes: Place the potato wedges in a large baking dish, then pour the marinade over them, ensuring that each wedge is evenly coated. This step is crucial for infusing the potatoes with the vibrant flavors of the marinade.

  5. Add the Broth: Once the potatoes are coated with the marinade, pour the chicken or vegetable broth into the baking dish. The broth adds moisture and depth of flavor to the dish, resulting in tender and flavorful potatoes.

  6. Roast to Perfection: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and roast the potatoes for approximately 45-50 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside, yet tender on the inside. Remember to toss the potatoes halfway through the cooking process to ensure even browning.

  7. Garnish and Serve: Once the potatoes are beautifully roasted, remove them from the oven and garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness. These Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes are best served hot, allowing your guests to savor the delightful blend of tangy lemon and savory herbs.

Serving Suggestions

These tantalizing Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes are a versatile side dish that pairs exceptionally well with a variety of main courses. Whether you’re serving them alongside grilled lamb, roasted chicken, or even a hearty vegetarian stew, the zesty flavors of the potatoes complement a wide range of dishes. For a complete Greek dining experience, consider pairing them with tzatziki and a crisp Greek salad.

Final Thoughts

With their crispy exterior and vibrant citrusy flavor, Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes are a delightful addition to any meal. Whether you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or simply looking to elevate your side dish game, these potatoes are a must-try. The next time you’re in the mood for a taste of the Mediterranean, whip up a batch of these tangy and crispy delights – your taste buds will thank you!
