Mixed Berry Slab Pie: Feeding a Crowd with Ease


When it comes to feeding a large group of people, it can be overwhelming to come up with a recipe that’s both delicious and easy to scale up. That’s why I’m excited to share with you my recipe for Mixed Berry Slab Pie, a show-stopping dessert that’s perfect for gatherings of all sizes.

The Beauty of a Slab Pie

A slab pie is essentially a large, rectangular pie that’s perfect for feeding a crowd. It’s made by pouring the filling into a large baking dish, topping it with a crust, and baking until golden brown. The best part? It’s incredibly easy to make and requires minimal fuss.

The Perfect Filling

For this recipe, we’re using a mix of fresh berries - blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries - to create a sweet and tangy filling. You can use fresh or frozen berries, depending on what’s in season and what you have on hand. Simply mix the berries with a little sugar, flour, and lemon juice, and you’re ready to go.

The Crust

The crust is the backbone of any good pie, and for this recipe, we’re using a simple all-butter crust. It’s made by combining cold butter with flour, salt, and ice-cold water, and then rolling it out to fit your baking dish. Don’t be afraid to get a little rough with the crust - it’s supposed to be rustic and imperfect!

Assembly and Baking

Once you’ve rolled out the crust, place it in your baking dish and fill with the berry mixture. Top with another piece of crust, crimping the edges to seal the pie. Cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape, and bake until golden brown.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the pie even more impressive, try adding a lattice top crust or a sprinkle of sugar on top before baking.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use a store-bought pie crust or even a graham cracker crust.
  • Experiment with different types of berries or add a splash of liqueur (like Grand Marnier or Cognac) to the filling for added depth of flavor.

The Verdict

Mixed Berry Slab Pie is the perfect solution for feeding a crowd with ease. It’s easy to make, feeds a large group, and is sure to impress. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, a potluck, or just a casual gathering with friends, this pie is sure to be a hit.

Recipe

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water

For the filling:

  • 2 cups mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball.
  4. Roll out the dough to fit a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine berries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Mix until well combined.
  6. Pour the berry mixture into the baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  7. Roll out the remaining dough to fit the top of the pie. Place on top of the filling and crimp the edges to seal.
  8. Cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  9. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
  10. Let cool before serving.

I hope you enjoy this recipe for Mixed Berry Slab Pie! It’s sure to be a hit at your next gathering.
