Korean Fried Chicken: Crispy, Juicy, and Irresistible


Are you ready to take your taste buds on a journey to Korea? Look no further than Korean Fried Chicken! This delectable dish is known for its irresistible crispy exterior and juicy, flavorful meat. Whether you’re a fan of spicy or non-spicy options, Korean Fried Chicken has something to satisfy every palate. In this blog post, we will explore the secrets to achieving that perfect crispy and juicy texture that will keep you coming back for more.

Ingredients

To create the ultimate Korean Fried Chicken, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Chicken wings or boneless chicken pieces
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • Cornstarch
  • Baking powder
  • Garlic powder
  • Ginger powder
  • Soy sauce
  • Rice syrup
  • Vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepping the Chicken: Start by washing the chicken pieces thoroughly and patting them dry with paper towels. This will help to remove excess moisture and ensure a crispier coating.

  2. Marinating the Chicken: In a mixing bowl, combine the chicken pieces with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and ginger powder. Massage the spices into the chicken, ensuring that each piece is well-coated. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Coating the Chicken: In a separate bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and baking powder. This combination will give the chicken a light and crispy texture. Take each marinated chicken piece and coat it generously with the flour mixture. Shake off any excess flour, ensuring an even coating.

  4. Frying the Chicken: Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or a deep fryer to approximately 350°F (175°C). Gently place the coated chicken into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the chicken in batches, if necessary, to ensure even cooking. Fry the chicken until it turns golden brown and crispy, which should take around 10-12 minutes.

  5. Draining and Resting: Once the chicken is cooked, carefully remove it from the hot oil using a slotted spoon or tongs. Place the fried chicken on a wire rack or a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender bite.

  6. Creating the Sauce: Now, it’s time to add the finishing touch to your Korean Fried Chicken. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce and rice syrup. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until well combined and slightly thickened. If you prefer a spicy kick, you can add some gochujang (Korean chili paste) or some hot sauce to the mixture.

  7. Coating the Chicken with Sauce: Place the fried chicken in a large mixing bowl and pour the sauce over it. Gently toss the chicken in the sauce until each piece is evenly coated. The sticky, sweet, and savory sauce will take your Korean Fried Chicken to the next level.

  8. Garnishing and Serving: To add a touch of freshness and crunch, garnish your Korean Fried Chicken with sesame seeds and chopped green onions. Serve it hot with a side of pickled radish or a fresh salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve mastered the art of Korean Fried Chicken. This dish is perfect for family gatherings, parties, or simply when you’re craving something crispy, juicy, and packed with flavor. With its crunchy exterior and tender meat, Korean Fried Chicken is a crowd-pleaser that will leave everyone wanting more. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and get ready to savor the irresistible taste of Korean Fried Chicken.
