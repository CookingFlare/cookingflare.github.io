No-Nut Granola Bars: A Nut-Free Snack for On-the-Go


Granola bars are a popular snack choice for those who are constantly on the go. However, for individuals with nut allergies, finding a suitable granola bar can be a challenge. Many store-bought options contain nuts or are processed in facilities that handle nuts, making them off-limits for those with allergies. That’s where these homemade no-nut granola bars come in. Packed with wholesome ingredients and bursting with flavor, these bars are the perfect nut-free snack for anyone looking for a convenient and nutritious option.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup crispy rice cereal
  • 1/2 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup sunflower seed butter
  • 1/4 cup ground flaxseed
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the oats, crispy rice cereal, shredded coconut, dried cranberries, and raisins. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a small saucepan, heat the honey and sunflower seed butter over low heat. Stir until the mixture is smooth and well combined.

  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the ground flaxseed, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Stir until everything is well incorporated.

  4. Pour the honey and sunflower seed butter mixture over the dry ingredients. Use a spatula or your hands to mix everything together, ensuring that all the dry ingredients are coated evenly.

  5. Transfer the mixture to a lined baking dish or tray. Press down firmly to create an even layer.

  6. Place the baking dish or tray in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until the mixture is firm and set.

  7. Once the mixture has set, remove it from the refrigerator and cut it into bars of your desired size and shape.

  8. Store the no-nut granola bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, or refrigerate them for a longer shelf life.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize these granola bars by adding your favorite mix-ins, such as chocolate chips, dried apricots, or chia seeds. Just make sure to choose options that are nut-free and suit your taste preferences.

  • For an extra crunch, you can toast the oats and shredded coconut in a dry skillet over medium heat for a few minutes before mixing them with the other ingredients.

  • If you prefer a sweeter granola bar, you can increase the amount of honey or add a drizzle of melted chocolate on top before refrigerating.

  • These bars are not only nut-free but also gluten-free, making them a great option for individuals with multiple dietary restrictions.

  • Don’t limit yourself to just snacking on these granola bars. They can also be crumbled over yogurt, enjoyed with a glass of milk, or used as a topping for smoothie bowls.

Conclusion

No-nut granola bars are a fantastic alternative for individuals with nut allergies or anyone who prefers a nut-free snack option. By making your own granola bars at home, you have full control over the ingredients and can ensure that they meet your dietary needs and taste preferences. These bars are not only delicious but also packed with wholesome ingredients that will keep you energized throughout the day. So, the next time you’re in need of a quick and convenient snack, give these no-nut granola bars a try and enjoy the nut-free goodness they have to offer.
