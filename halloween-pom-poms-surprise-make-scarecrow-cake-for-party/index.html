Halloween Pom-Poms Surprise: Make Scarecrow Cake for Party


Ever wanted to serve a cake that is not only delicious but also an eye-catching conversation starter at your Halloween party? If yes, then look no further! Presenting the Scarecrow Cake, a fun and whimsical dessert that is sure to impress your guests. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of making this delightful cake that resembles a scarecrow, complete with pom-poms for “straw” and a wonderful surprise hidden inside.

Ingredients For the Cake:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup Boiling Water

For the Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Yellow Food coloring
  • Black icing or frosting for face and pupils

For the Pom-Poms:

  • Yellow fondant
  • Black edible paint

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare two 9-inch round cake pans by greasing them and lining the bottom with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Stir until well mixed.

  3. In a different bowl, whisk together the milk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Add this wet mixture to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.

  4. Add the boiling water to the batter and mix until smooth. The batter will be thin, and that’s perfectly normal.

  5. Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Let the cakes cool completely on wire racks.

  7. Once cooled, remove the cakes from the pans and level the tops to create even surfaces.

  8. For the frosting, beat the softened butter in a large bowl until creamy. Gradually add in the powdered sugar and cocoa powder, alternating with the heavy cream. Add the vanilla extract and yellow food coloring, adjusting the amounts as needed to achieve the desired consistency and color. Set aside.

  9. To create the scarecrow face, roll out the yellow fondant and cut two circles for the eyes, a smaller circle for the nose, and an oval shape for the mouth. Attach them to the cake using edible glue or water, and add pupils and details with black edible paint.

  10. Apply a layer of frosting on one of the cake layers, followed by the remaining layer. Spread a generous amount of frosting on top and around the sides of the cake. Refrigerate the cake for about an hour to allow the frosting to set.

  11. Once the frosting has set, use an offset spatula to create a swirled effect around the sides and top of the cake to resemble straw.

  12. For the hidden surprise, fill the cake with your favorite Halloween-themed candies or treats, such as gummy worms, spiders, or eyeballs. This delicious twist will add an element of surprise for your guests as they dig into the cake.

Your Scarecrow Cake is now ready to be served! This charming, creative, and delicious treat is perfect for any Halloween party. Serve it up on a platter and watch your friends and family delight in your culinary masterpiece. Happy haunting, and enjoy the frightfully fun treats!
