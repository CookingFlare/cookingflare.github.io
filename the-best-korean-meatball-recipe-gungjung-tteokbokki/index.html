The Best Korean Meatball Recipe (Gungjung Tteokbokki)


The rich culinary tradition of Korea is a harmonious blend of bold flavors, colorful ingredients, and deeply communal traditions. Among the array of dishes, Gungjung Tteokbokki holds a special place for its balance of savory, sweet, and umami-laden flavors. Gungjung Tteokbokki, often referred to as “royal court tteokbokki,” is a milder counterpart to the spicy tteokbokki that has gained worldwide fame. This dish combines chewy Korean rice cakes with tender meatballs, vibrant vegetables, and a luscious sauce, creating a truly royal experience for your taste buds.

In this blog post, you’ll learn how to make Gungjung Tteokbokki with Korean-style meatballs from scratch. This dish works wonderfully as a standalone meal or a centerpiece for your next dinner party. Get ready to bring a touch of Korean royalty to your kitchen!

Why Gungjung Tteokbokki with Korean Meatballs?

Unlike its fiery cousin flooded with red pepper paste, Gungjung Tteokbokki boasts flavors rooted in tradition and refined elegance. Originating from the Joseon dynasty, this dish was once served to Korean royalty, and its ingredients reflect the historical significance. The pairing of juicy Korean meatballs with the mellow, soy-sauce-based tteokbokki opens up a gateway to the flavors of classical Korean cuisine. Think of it as an elevated comfort dish—accessible, inviting, and utterly satisfying.

Ingredients

Here’s everything you’ll need to create the best Gungjung Tteokbokki with homemade Korean meatballs:

For the Korean Meatballs:

  • Ground beef and/or ground pork (1 lb) – A mix of beef and pork ensures juicy and flavorful meatballs.
  • Minced garlic (1 tbsp) – Essential for that unmistakable Korean aroma.
  • Minced ginger (1 tsp) – Adds depth of flavor to the meatballs.
  • Soy sauce (2 tbsp) – For seasoning and umami richness.
  • Sugar (1 tsp) – A touch of sweetness to balance the soy sauce.
  • Sesame oil (1 tbsp) – Korean cooking is incomplete without nutty sesame oil.
  • Breadcrumbs (⅓ cup) – Helps bind the meatballs.
  • Egg (1 large) – Adds structure and moisture to the meatballs.
  • Ground pepper (½ tsp) – Just a hint of spice to accentuate the flavors.

For the Gungjung Tteokbokki:

  • Korean rice cakes (tteok) (1 lb) – Look for cylindrical or sliced rice cakes from your local Asian market.
  • Soy sauce (3 tbsp) – Forms the base of the sauce.
  • Oyster sauce (1 tbsp) – Introduces depth and umami to the mix.
  • Sugar (1 tbsp) – Balances the salty flavors.
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced) – Creates that quintessential Korean flavor profile.
  • Sesame oil (1 tbsp) – Adds a rich nuttiness.
  • Shiitake mushrooms (½ cup, thinly sliced) – Adds umami and variation in texture.
  • Carrot (1 small, julienned) – Brings a pop of color and sweetness.
  • Spinach (1 cup, fresh) – For a touch of greenery and balance.
  • Green onion (2 stalks, chopped) – Garnishes the dish beautifully.

Instructions

Step One: Preparing the Korean Meatballs

  1. Mix the meatball ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef (and/or pork), minced garlic, minced ginger, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, breadcrumbs, egg, and pepper. Use your hands or a spoon to mix everything until well combined, but try not to overwork the mixture.
  2. Shape the meatballs: Roll the mixture into evenly sized balls, about the size of a golf ball. This recipe should yield 12 to 16 meatballs depending on size.
  3. Cook the meatballs: Heat a skillet over medium heat and add a drizzle of sesame or vegetable oil. Cook the meatballs in batches for about 8–10 minutes, rotating occasionally until browned on all sides and cooked through. Set the cooked meatballs aside on a plate.

Step Two: Preparing the Rice Cakes

  1. Soften the rice cakes: If the rice cakes are hard or refrigerated, soak them in warm water for about 15 minutes to soften them. Drain thoroughly before using.
  2. Mix the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, garlic, and sesame oil until smooth.

Step Three: Cooking the Gungjung Tteokbokki

  1. Sauté the veggies: In the same skillet used for meatballs, add a touch more oil if needed and sauté the shiitake mushrooms, julienned carrot, and spinach over medium heat. Cook until softened, about 4–5 minutes.
  2. Combine the rice cakes and sauce: Add the softened rice cakes to the skillet, followed by the prepared sauce. Stir gently to coat everything evenly. Let the rice cakes simmer with the sauce for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally until they become soft and chewy and the sauce thickens slightly.
  3. Add the meatballs: Return the cooked meatballs to the skillet and gently toss them with the rice cakes and vegetables. Allow everything to cook together for 2–3 minutes so the flavors can meld.

Step Four: Final Touches

  1. Sprinkle chopped green onions over the dish as a garnish for freshness and color.
  2. Optional: Add a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds for added texture and nutty flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Gungjung Tteokbokki is best served hot, straight out of the skillet for maximum flavor and texture. Pair it with a side of kimchi for an authentic Korean dining experience or serve with a bowl of steamed rice to stretch the meal further. A light cucumber salad or pickled radishes on the side add a refreshing contrast to the dish.

This Korean meatball rendition makes for a fantastic centerpiece on your table, whether you’re hosting friends or treating yourself to a special weekday dinner. The combination of savory meatballs, chewy rice cakes, and a mellow soy-based sauce is nothing short of perfection.

Tips for Perfecting Your Gungjung Tteokbokki

  • Adjust sweetness and saltiness: The sauce can be tailored to your taste palate. If you prefer a slightly sweeter dish, add a touch more sugar. For a saltier profile, increase the soy sauce, but do so in small increments.
  • Rice cake texture: The key to perfect rice cakes is ensuring they’re softened adequately before cooking. If they’re still stiff after soaking, boil them briefly for about 2–3 minutes.
  • Make-ahead meatballs: To save time, the meatballs can be prepared a day ahead and refrigerated. Reheat them in the skillet before adding them to the rice cakes.

Elevate Your Cooking with Korean Flavors

Gungjung Tteokbokki with Korean meatballs strikes a fine balance between tradition, flavor, and versatility. It’s a dish that brings Korean cultural history to life while offering a meal that anyone can enjoy. From its tender meatballs to its chewy rice cakes and harmonious sauce, every bite is a celebration of the meticulous care that defines Korean cuisine.

Ready to try your hand at this royal creation? With just a few simple steps, you’ll have a dish that surprises, delights, and transports your taste buds to the heart of Korea. Happy cooking!
