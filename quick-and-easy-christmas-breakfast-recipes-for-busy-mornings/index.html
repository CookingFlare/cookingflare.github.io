Quick and Easy Christmas Breakfast Recipes for Busy Mornings


The holiday season is filled with festive celebrations, cozy moments, and, of course, delicious food. But let’s face it—Christmas mornings can be hectic. Whether you’re wrapping up last-minute presents, getting ready for a holiday feast, or just soaking in the joy of the day, the last thing you want to worry about is spending hours in the kitchen. That’s where these quick and easy Christmas breakfast recipes come in. These dishes are perfect for busy mornings, yet they still feel festive enough to celebrate the occasion. Here’s a collection of simple, flavorful options that will satisfy your family and get you back to the festivities in no time.

1. Cinnamon Roll Casserole

Cinnamon rolls are a Christmas classic, but they can take a while to prepare. Instead of rolling, cutting, and baking each individual cinnamon roll, make a casserole that’s just as indulgent but much easier to put together. Here’s how to make this mouthwatering dish:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of refrigerated cinnamon rolls (with icing)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • A pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick spray.
  2. Open the cinnamon roll cans and cut each roll into quarters. Spread the pieces evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Pour the egg mixture over the cinnamon roll pieces, making sure everything is coated evenly.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the casserole is golden and cooked through.
  5. Drizzle the icing from the cinnamon rolls over the casserole once it’s out of the oven, and finish with a dusting of powdered sugar.

This cinnamon roll casserole is the perfect balance of convenience and indulgence. It can even be prepared the night before and popped into the oven Christmas morning for a stress-free breakfast.

2. Holiday Breakfast Quesadillas

If you’re looking for a savory option that can be prepared quickly, breakfast quesadillas are a fantastic choice. With just a few ingredients, you can create a warm and satisfying breakfast that will fuel you through the morning.

Ingredients:

  • 4 flour tortillas
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a festive cheese blend)
  • 1/4 cup cooked bacon or sausage, crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salsa or sour cream (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Scramble the eggs in a bowl and cook them in a non-stick skillet over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until they are just set. Set aside.
  2. Heat a separate skillet over medium heat and add the butter.
  3. Place one tortilla in the skillet, and sprinkle with cheese, crumbled bacon or sausage, and scrambled eggs. Top with another tortilla.
  4. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, pressing lightly with a spatula to ensure the cheese melts and the tortillas crisp up.
  5. Remove from the pan, cut into wedges, and serve with salsa or sour cream.

This breakfast quesadilla recipe is quick to make, filling, and can easily be adapted to suit various tastes—whether that means adding veggies or switching up the protein.

3. Christmas Morning Smoothie Bowls

If you prefer something light and refreshing, smoothie bowls are a perfect option. They’re quick to prepare, and you can customize them with all your favorite toppings to make them as festive as you like.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas, frozen
  • 1/2 cup of frozen mixed berries
  • 1/2 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup of almond milk (or any milk of your choice)
  • 1 tablespoon of honey (optional)
  • Toppings: granola, chia seeds, shredded coconut, pomegranate seeds, fresh berries, and a drizzle of honey

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the frozen bananas, mixed berries, Greek yogurt, almond milk, and honey. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Pour the smoothie mixture into bowls.
  3. Top with your favorite toppings—granola, fresh berries, shredded coconut, chia seeds, and pomegranate seeds will all add a festive touch.
  4. Drizzle with extra honey for added sweetness if desired.

Smoothie bowls are a nutritious and festive way to start the day, plus they require minimal effort. You can also make the smoothie base ahead of time and freeze it in individual portions for even quicker prep on Christmas morning.

4. Overnight Oats with Spiced Apples

For a cozy and comforting breakfast, try overnight oats. This no-cook option is ideal for busy mornings and can be prepped the night before so you can enjoy it as soon as you wake up.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 apple, diced
  • A handful of chopped pecans or walnuts
  • Additional maple syrup (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. In a mason jar or bowl, combine the oats, milk, yogurt, maple syrup, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Stir well to combine.
  2. Cover and refrigerate overnight, allowing the oats to absorb the liquid and soften.
  3. The next morning, warm up the oats in the microwave for 30-60 seconds or enjoy them cold.
  4. Top with diced apples, chopped nuts, and a drizzle of maple syrup.

This overnight oats recipe is not only quick but also customizable. You can swap in different fruits, such as pears or cranberries, or experiment with various nuts and seeds for added texture.

5. Baked French Toast Sticks

French toast is a holiday favorite, but it can be time-consuming to make in the traditional way. Baked French toast sticks are a quick and easy alternative that still delivers that comforting, sweet flavor everyone loves.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of thick-cut bread (such as brioche or challah)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for greasing)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking sheet with butter or non-stick spray.
  2. Cut the bread into thick strips, about 2 inches wide.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Dip each strip of bread into the egg mixture, making sure it’s fully coated, then place it on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping the French toast sticks halfway through, until golden and crispy.
  6. Dust with powdered sugar and serve with maple syrup for dipping.

These French toast sticks are a fun, easy, and delicious breakfast that kids and adults alike will love. Plus, they can be made in advance and reheated, making them ideal for a busy holiday morning.

6. Breakfast Casserole with Sausage and Veggies

If you’re craving something hearty that can feed a crowd, a breakfast casserole is the way to go. This recipe is easy to prepare and can be made the night before, making Christmas morning stress-free.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage (or turkey sausage)
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or your choice)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme or rosemary

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned, breaking it up as it cooks. Add the onion and bell pepper, cooking until softened.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  4. Combine the cooked sausage mixture with the egg mixture, then pour it into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. Sprinkle with cheese and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the casserole is golden brown on top.
  6. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

This savory casserole is the ultimate breakfast for a busy family. It’s filling, flavorful, and can be customized with different vegetables or even swapped out with bacon or ham for variety.

Conclusion

Christmas morning doesn’t have to be a stressful cooking marathon. With these quick and easy recipes, you can enjoy a delicious and festive breakfast without spending hours in the kitchen. Whether you prefer something sweet, savory, or a little bit of both, these dishes are sure to please everyone at the table. Best of all, many of these recipes can be prepared ahead of time, leaving you with more time to relax and enjoy the holiday celebrations. Merry Christmas and happy cooking!
