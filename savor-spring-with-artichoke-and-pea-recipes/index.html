Savor Spring with Artichoke and Pea Recipes


Spring is a season of renewal, bursting with fresh greens, crisp flavors, and vibrant colors. Among the standout stars of this season’s produce are artichokes and peas—two ingredients that perfectly embody the essence of spring. Their bright flavors, unique textures, and versatile nature make them ideal for creating dishes that are light yet satisfying, elegant yet approachable. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or exploring new ingredients, these artichoke and pea recipes will inspire your kitchen creativity and bring a refreshing twist to your spring menu.

Why Artichokes and Peas?

Artichokes and peas are more than just seasonal ingredients; they’re powerhouses of nutrition and culinary delight. Artichokes pack a punch of fiber, antioxidants, and minerals, while peas offer sweetness, protein, and vibrant color. Together, they create a symphony of flavors and textures—earthy and tender artichokes meet crisp and sweet peas in dishes that celebrate the season’s bounty.

Moreover, both ingredients adapt beautifully to a variety of cooking methods, from steaming and roasting to pureeing and sautéing. Their compatibility with herbs like mint, basil, and parsley enhances their freshness, while bold partners like garlic, lemon, and Parmesan add depth. Ready to savor spring like never before? Let’s dive into these delicious recipes.

1. Lemon Garlic Artichoke and Pea Risotto

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 2 large artichokes, cleaned and trimmed (or 1 can of artichoke hearts, drained)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, kept warm
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prep the artichokes: If using fresh artichokes, peel outer leaves until you reach the tender parts, trim the tips, and slice hearts thinly. If canned, simply rinse and slice.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; sauté until translucent and fragrant.
  3. Toast the rice: Stir in Arborio rice and cook for 2 minutes, allowing it to lightly toast and become glossy.
  4. Add wine: Pour in white wine, stirring constantly until absorbed.
  5. Cook risotto: Begin adding warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until liquid is almost absorbed before adding the next ladle.
  6. Add veggies: When the rice is halfway cooked (about 10 minutes in), stir in artichokes and peas.
  7. Finish with lemon and cheese: Once rice is creamy and al dente, stir in lemon juice and zest, Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  8. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley or basil. Serve warm and enjoy a bright, creamy taste of spring.

2. Spring Salad with Grilled Artichokes and Fresh Peas

Ingredients

  • 4 medium artichokes
  • 1 cup fresh peas, shelled
  • Mixed salad greens (arugula, spinach, butter lettuce)
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare artichokes: Trim tops and stems, remove tough outer leaves, and cut artichokes in half. Steam until tender (about 15-20 minutes), then pat dry.
  2. Grill artichokes: Heat grill or grill pan to medium-high. Brush artichokes with olive oil, season lightly with salt, and grill cut sides until charred and golden.
  3. Blanch peas: Quickly blanch fresh peas in boiling water for 1 minute, then cool in ice water to retain color and crunch.
  4. Mix dressing: Whisk lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to create a zesty dressing.
  5. Assemble salad: In a large bowl, combine salad greens, grilled artichokes, peas, red onion, and feta. Drizzle dressing on top and toss gently.
  6. Serve immediately to enjoy crisp, smoky, and fresh flavors.

3. Creamy Artichoke and Pea Soup with Mint

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large leek or onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups fresh or thawed peas
  • 2 cups artichoke hearts (fresh or canned)
  • ½ cup heavy cream or coconut milk for dairy-free
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint leaves
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté base: Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add leek and garlic, sauté until soft but not browned.
  2. Simmer veggies: Add broth, peas, and artichokes. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Blend soup: Using an immersion blender or regular blender (in batches), puree soup until smooth.
  4. Add cream and mint: Stir in cream or coconut milk, fresh mint, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  5. Heat gently: Warm soup through without boiling.
  6. Serve: Garnish with extra mint leaves or a drizzle of cream. A perfect starter or light lunch to brighten spring days.

4. Pasta with Artichokes, Peas, and Lemon Basil Pesto

Ingredients

  • 12 oz pasta (penne, farfalle, or linguine)
  • 1 cup fresh peas
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup pine nuts or walnuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • ½ cup olive oil, plus more if needed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta: Boil pasta in salted water until al dente; add peas in the last 2 minutes of cooking. Drain, reserving ½ cup of pasta water.
  2. Prepare pesto: In a food processor, blend basil, Parmesan, nuts, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil until smooth. Add pasta water to adjust consistency.
  3. Sauté artichokes: In a skillet, warm a tablespoon of olive oil. Toss sliced artichokes until lightly browned.
  4. Combine: Mix pasta, peas, and sautéed artichokes with pesto sauce. Toss until well coated.
  5. Serve: Garnish with extra Parmesan and lemon zest for a fresh, fragrant meal that screams springtime.

5. Artichoke and Pea Frittata

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, chopped
  • ¾ cup fresh peas
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan or feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small shallot, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (chives, parsley, or tarragon) for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook shallots and veggies: Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet; sauté shallots until soft. Add artichokes and peas, cooking for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Beat eggs and season: In a bowl, whisk eggs with salt, pepper, and cheese.
  4. Pour eggs over veggies: Stir gently in the skillet to distribute ingredients evenly.
  5. Bake: Transfer skillet to oven and bake for 12-15 minutes until eggs are set and golden.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature: Garnish with fresh herbs for a perfect brunch or light dinner.

Tips for Cooking Artichokes and Peas

  • Choosing artichokes: Select firm, heavy heads with tightly packed leaves. Avoid any with browning or dried-out tips.
  • Prep tricks: For fresh artichokes, keep a lemon water bath nearby to prevent browning while trimming.
  • Peas: Fresh peas are sweeter and more vibrant but frozen peas work well too, offering convenience and consistency.
  • Combining flavors: The natural sweetness of peas pairs beautifully with the slightly nutty, earthy artichoke; balance them with acid (lemon juice), creamy textures (cheese or cream), and fresh herbs for depth.

Spring cooking invites lightness, freshness, and bursts of color on the plate. Artichokes and peas, when used creatively, can transform everyday meals into celebrations of the season. From creamy soups to vibrant salads, from comforting risottos to elegant pasta dishes, their versatility knows no bounds. Experiment, savor, and enjoy the freshest tastes spring has to offer with these artichoke and pea recipes that are as delicious as they are inspiring.

Happy cooking!
