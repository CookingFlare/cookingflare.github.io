Irresistible Nut-Free Chocolate Peanut Butter Cups


When it comes to satisfying your sweet tooth, there’s nothing quite like the combination of chocolate and peanut butter. However, for those with nut allergies, enjoying this classic flavor pairing can be a challenge. That’s where these irresistible nut-free chocolate peanut butter cups come in. Perfect for anyone with nut allergies or those simply looking for a delicious and easy-to-make treat, these homemade chocolate peanut butter cups are sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Chocolate Shell:

  • 2 cups of high-quality chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons of coconut oil

For the Peanut Butter Filling:

  • 1 cup of creamy peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup of powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chocolate Shell

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and coconut oil.
  2. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring each time, until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth.

Step 2: Line the Muffin Tin

  1. Line a muffin tin with paper or silicone cupcake liners.

Step 3: Layer the Chocolate Shell

  1. Spoon a small amount of the melted chocolate into the bottom of each cupcake liner, using a spoon to spread the chocolate up the sides of the liner.

Step 4: Prepare the Peanut Butter Filling

  1. In a separate bowl, combine the creamy peanut butter, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, melted butter, and salt. Mix until smooth and well combined.

Step 5: Add the Peanut Butter Filling

  1. Spoon a small amount of the peanut butter filling on top of the chocolate layer in each cupcake liner, smoothing it out to create an even layer.

Step 6: Seal with Chocolate

  1. Pour the remaining melted chocolate over the peanut butter layer in each cup, using a spoon to spread the chocolate and ensure the peanut butter is fully covered.

Step 7: Chill and Serve

  1. Place the muffin tin in the refrigerator and chill for at least 1 hour, or until the chocolate is set.
  2. Once set, remove the peanut butter cups from the liners and serve.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, consider adding a sprinkle of sea salt on top of the peanut butter layer before sealing with the remaining chocolate.
  • Experiment with different types of chocolate for the shell, such as dark chocolate or white chocolate, to customize the flavor of your peanut butter cups.
  • Store the peanut butter cups in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week for a quick and indulgent treat.

These nut-free chocolate peanut butter cups are not only a delicious alternative for those with nut allergies, but they’re also a crowd-pleaser for anyone who loves the classic combination of chocolate and peanut butter. Whether you’re making them for a special occasion or simply as a delightful homemade treat, these irresistible peanut butter cups are sure to become a staple in your dessert repertoire. Enjoy the simplicity and satisfaction of creating your own nut-free chocolate peanut butter cups right in your kitchen!
