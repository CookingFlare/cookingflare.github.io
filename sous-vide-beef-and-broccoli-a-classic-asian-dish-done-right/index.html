Sous Vide Beef and Broccoli: A Classic Asian Dish Done Right


Beef and broccoli is a beloved classic Asian dish that has found its way onto countless dinner tables around the world. The combination of tender beef, crisp broccoli, and a savory sauce is simply irresistible. Today, we’re going to take this classic dish to a whole new level by introducing the sous vide cooking technique. By cooking the beef and broccoli in a water bath at a precise temperature, we can ensure that every bite is perfectly cooked and bursting with flavor.

The Sous Vide Advantage

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing the ingredients in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking them in a water bath at a controlled temperature. This technique allows for precise temperature control, ensuring that the beef is cooked to the perfect level of doneness while retaining its natural juices and tenderness. The result is a melt-in-your-mouth texture that you won’t be able to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: red pepper flakes for a spicy kick

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the water bath to 130°F (54°C).

  2. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, brown sugar, ginger, minced garlic, and optional red pepper flakes. Mix well until the cornstarch is fully dissolved.

  3. Season the thinly sliced flank steak with salt and pepper. Place the steak in a vacuum-sealed bag and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag, ensuring that all the air is removed.

  4. Submerge the sealed bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours. This slow cooking process will allow the flavors to meld together and the beef to become tender.

  5. After 2 hours, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully open it. Be cautious of the hot steam that will escape. Set the beef aside.

  6. In a large skillet or wok, heat some oil over high heat. Add the broccoli florets and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are bright green and slightly tender.

  7. Add the cooked beef to the skillet and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the beef is heated through and coated with the sauce.

  8. Serve the sous vide beef and broccoli over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds or sliced green onions for added freshness and visual appeal.

Get Creative!

While this recipe stays true to the classic beef and broccoli flavors, feel free to get creative and make it your own. You can add other vegetables like bell peppers, snow peas, or carrots to the stir-fry for added crunch and color. Experiment with different sauces or marinades to suit your taste buds. The sous vide cooking technique provides the perfect canvas for your culinary imagination.

Conclusion

Sous vide beef and broccoli is a game-changer when it comes to this classic Asian dish. The precise temperature control ensures that the beef is incredibly tender, while the vibrant flavors of the marinade and stir-fry sauce elevate the dish to new heights. So, why settle for ordinary when you can have extraordinary? Give this sous vide beef and broccoli recipe a try and prepare to be blown away by the results.
