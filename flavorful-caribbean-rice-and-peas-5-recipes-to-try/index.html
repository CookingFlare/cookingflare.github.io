Flavorful Caribbean Rice and Peas: 5 Recipes to Try


Caribbean cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromas, and spices, and rice and peas is a staple dish that embodies the essence of the region. This classic combination of rice, peas, and spices is a staple in many Caribbean households, and its versatility allows for endless variations and creative interpretations. In this post, we’ll explore five mouth-watering recipes that showcase the diversity and richness of Caribbean rice and peas.

Recipe 1: Jamaican Jerk Rice and Peas

This recipe is a classic Jamaican take on the traditional dish, infused with the bold flavors of jerk seasoning. To make it, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of peas according to package instructions. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a pan and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 Scotch bonnet pepper until the onion is translucent. Add 1 teaspoon of jerk seasoning, 1 teaspoon of thyme, and 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, and stir well. Add the cooked rice and peas to the pan, stirring until the rice is well coated with the jerk seasoning mixture. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro.

Recipe 2: Trinidadian Curry Rice and Peas

This recipe takes a cue from Trinidad and Tobago’s Indian influences, adding a rich and creamy curry flavor to the dish. To make it, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of peas according to package instructions. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 curry powder until the onion is translucent. Add 1 can of coconut milk, 1/2 cup of water, and 1 teaspoon of curry powder, and stir well. Add the cooked rice and peas to the pan, stirring until the rice is well coated with the curry sauce. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro.

Recipe 3: Haitian Spicy Rice and Peas

This recipe takes a bold and spicy turn, inspired by Haitian cuisine’s love of bold flavors. To make it, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of peas according to package instructions. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 Scotch bonnet pepper until the onion is translucent. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin, 1 teaspoon of paprika, and 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper, and stir well. Add the cooked rice and peas to the pan, stirring until the rice is well coated with the spicy mixture. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro.

Recipe 4: Barbadian Coconut Rice and Peas

This recipe takes a creamy and tropical turn, inspired by Barbados’s love of coconut. To make it, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of peas according to package instructions. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a pan and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 cup of shredded coconut until the onion is translucent. Add 1 can of coconut milk, 1/2 cup of water, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and stir well. Add the cooked rice and peas to the pan, stirring until the rice is well coated with the coconut milk mixture. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro.

Recipe 5: Guyanese Rice and Peas with Pigeon Peas

This recipe takes a unique turn, using pigeon peas instead of the traditional kidney peas. To make it, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and 1 cup of pigeon peas according to package instructions. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a pan and sauté 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 1 teaspoon of cumin until the onion is translucent. Add 1 can of coconut milk, 1/2 cup of water, and 1 teaspoon of thyme, and stir well. Add the cooked rice and peas to the pan, stirring until the rice is well coated with the coconut milk mixture. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro.

These five recipes showcase the incredible diversity of Caribbean rice and peas, each with its unique flavors and twists. Whether you’re looking for a bold and spicy dish or a creamy and tropical one, there’s something for everyone in this collection. So go ahead, experiment with these recipes, and discover the flavors of the Caribbean!
