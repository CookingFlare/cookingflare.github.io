Sous Vide Beef Short Ribs: Fall-Off-The-Bone Goodness


Who doesn’t love tender, succulent beef short ribs that practically melt in your mouth? If you’re a fan of fall-off-the-bone goodness, then you’re in for a treat with this sous vide beef short ribs recipe. Sous vide cooking is a technique that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This method ensures that the meat is cooked evenly and retains its natural flavors and juices. So, let’s dive into this mouthwatering recipe!

Ingredients

  • 4 beef short ribs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 140°F (60°C).

  2. Season the beef short ribs generously with salt and pepper on all sides.

  3. Place each short rib in a separate vacuum-sealed bag and add a sprig of thyme and a quarter of the minced garlic to each bag. Drizzle each rib with olive oil.

  4. Seal the bags using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  5. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, lower the sealed bags into the water bath, making sure they are fully submerged. Cook the short ribs for 24 hours.

  6. After 24 hours, carefully remove the bags from the water bath and pat dry the short ribs with a paper towel.

  7. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil. Sear the short ribs on all sides until they develop a beautiful brown crust. This step adds extra flavor and texture to the ribs.

  8. In a small saucepan, combine the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it reduce for about 5 minutes, until it thickens slightly.

  9. Serve the sous vide beef short ribs with the reduced sauce and your favorite sides. Mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh green salad all make great accompaniments to this dish.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a more tender texture, you can cook the short ribs for up to 48 hours. This will result in extremely tender meat that practically falls apart.

  • Feel free to experiment with different seasonings and marinades. You can add spices like paprika, cumin, or chili powder to the short ribs before sealing them in the bags. This will infuse the meat with additional flavors.

  • For a smoky twist, you can finish the short ribs on a grill or in a hot oven for a few minutes after searing them in the skillet. This will give the ribs a delicious smoky flavor.

  • Leftover sous vide beef short ribs can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Simply reheat them gently in a sous vide water bath or on a low heat stovetop.

Now that you have this fabulous sous vide beef short ribs recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to impress your family and friends with a dish that is guaranteed to please. The long cooking time might seem daunting, but trust me, the end result is well worth the wait. So, get your sous vide setup ready and prepare to indulge in the ultimate fall-off-the-bone goodness!
