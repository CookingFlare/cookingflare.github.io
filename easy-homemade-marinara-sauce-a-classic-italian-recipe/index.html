Easy Homemade Marinara Sauce: A Classic Italian Recipe


If there’s one thing that can elevate a simple pasta dish to a culinary masterpiece, it’s a delicious marinara sauce. This classic Italian recipe has been passed down through generations, and for good reason. Its rich, robust flavor and versatility make it a staple in any kitchen. The best part? It’s incredibly easy to make at home, allowing you to enjoy the authentic taste of Italy right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to whip up this mouthwatering marinara sauce:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (optional, to balance the acidity of the tomatoes)

Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, it’s time to bring this marinara sauce to life. Here’s a step-by-step guide to creating the perfect homemade marinara:

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Start by pouring the can of whole peeled tomatoes into a bowl. Using your hands, crush the tomatoes until they reach your desired consistency. Some prefer a chunkier sauce, while others prefer a smoother texture. It’s all about personal preference.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, which should take about 5 minutes. Then, add the minced garlic and continue to sauté for another minute until it becomes fragrant.

  3. Add the Tomatoes: Once the aromatics are ready, carefully add the crushed tomatoes to the saucepan. Stir well to combine the ingredients.

  4. Season and Simmer: Now it’s time to add the dried oregano, basil, crushed red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper to the sauce. If you prefer a slightly sweeter sauce, you can also add a teaspoon of sugar at this stage. Stir everything together and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer.

  5. Let it Simmer: Allow the sauce to simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken to the desired consistency.

  6. Taste and Adjust: Once the sauce has simmered, give it a taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more salt, pepper, or red pepper flakes to suit your taste preferences.

  7. Serve or Store: Your homemade marinara sauce is now ready to be used in your favorite pasta dishes, as a dipping sauce for bread, or even as a base for homemade pizza. If you have leftovers, allow the sauce to cool completely before storing it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Final Thoughts

There you have it – a simple, yet incredibly flavorful marinara sauce that will take your Italian cooking to the next level. The beauty of this recipe lies in its versatility; you can customize it to your taste preferences and use it in a variety of dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this easy homemade marinara sauce is sure to become a go-to recipe in your repertoire. Buon appetito!
