Ocean Trophy: Showstopping Seafood Recipes to Win the Cooking Competition


Welcome, esteemed culinary contestants, to the grand finale of the Ocean Trophy, the premier seafood cooking competition! It’s time to dust off your chef hats, put on your creative thinking caps, and prepare to dazzle the judges and wowing your proud audience with an array of exquisite seafood dishes. In this blog post, we’ve gathered a trio of masterfully crafted recipes guaranteed to elevate your skills from a mere contender to a bona fide champion. So, fire up those stoves, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into the enticing world of submerged and scrumptious marine fare.

1. Spectacular Seared Tuna with Avocado Coulis and Mango Salsa

The first of our winning recipes is a combination of flavors that dance delightfully on the tongue. Impress the judges and tantalize your guests with this impeccably balanced dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tuna steaks (approximately 6 ounces each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 ripe avocado, pitted and peeled
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 sweet red pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced

Instructions:

  1. Begin by seasoning the tuna steaks with salt, pepper, and sesame oil, allowing them to marinate at room temperature for at least 20 minutes to absorb the flavors.

  2. Next, prepare the avocado coulis by blending the ripe avocado, vegetable broth, and salt until smooth, adding small amounts of water if necessary to achieve desired consistency. Set aside.

  3. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the diced mango, finely chopped red onion, sweet red pepper, and freshly chopped cilantro. Drizzle the lime juice over the mixture and gently toss to combine. This vibrant and zesty mango salsa will add a luscious twist to your seared tuna.

  4. Heat your skillet over high heat and coat lightly with olive oil. Once hot, add the marinated tuna steaks and sear for approximately 1-2 minutes on each side, depending on the desired level of doneness. Be sure not to overcook for a tender and succulent result.

  5. To plate, spread a generous pool of avocado coulis on the center of your serving plate. Gently place the seared tuna atop the creamy avocado base, and artfully arrange the refreshing mango salsa around the tuna.

  6. Garnish with microgreens and welcomed applause from your impressed audience, as you gather your well-earned praises from the judges.

2. Oceanic Seafood Paella

Paella, a proud culinary tradition hailing from Spain, is a vibrant and flavorful dish, showcasing an exquisite array of delectable seafood. Prepare to transport your guests to a sun-soaked Spanish haven with this showersstopping rendition!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bomba or calasparra rice
  • 1/4 cup saffron threads
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 8 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 mussels, scrubbed and cleaned
  • 1 lobster tail, cut into medallions
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 medium-size onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Chorizo, sliced into half-moons (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by soaking the saffron threads in warm water to release their vibrant color and aroma, setting aside for later use.

  2. In a large paella pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic, saut√©ing until softened and fragrant. If desired, stir in slices of chorizo for added depth of flavor.

  3. Introducing smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the onion mixture, allowing the spices to bloom and enrich the base of your paella.

  4. Pour in the rice, stirring it gently to ensure each grain is coated with the flavorful oil mixture. Let the rice toast lightly for about 3-4 minutes before incorporating the saffron-infused warm water, reserving the soaking liquid. Make sure the liquid level is just slightly above the level of the rice.

  5. Allow the paella to simmer, uncovered, for approximately 15-20 minutes or until the rice is almost tender.

  6. Carefully arrange your assortment of seafood, including shrimp, mussels, and lobster medallions, distributing evenly across the pan. Add frozen peas for added color and texture. Drizzle in the remaining saffron liquid and the juice of a fresh lemon for a pop of tangy brightness.

  7. Return the paella to the simmer, covering it with aluminum foil and allowing the seafood to cook and mingle with the aromatic rice for about 10 minutes, or until the shrimp and mussels have opened and are cooked through. Remember to discard any unopened shells.

  8. Allow the paella to rest for a few minutes prior to serving, acquiring a delicious crispy crust known as “socarrat.” Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges and an eager crowd waiting to relish in your culinary creations.

3. Tempting Trio Tarte Tatin with Seafood Medley

Capture the hearts of your judges and guests with a spin on the French classic‚Äîbuttery, sugary, and bursting with a trio of the ocean’s most delectable jewels. This seafood Tarte Tatin will be a lasting memory of your Ocean Trophy victory.

Ingredients:

  • 2 sheets pre-made puff pastry
  • 8 small puff pastry rounds (optional)
  • Six 6-inch tart rings or a 10-inch Tarte Tatin mold
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 ripe pears, cored and halved
  • 1 cup seafood stock
  • 8 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lobster tail, cut into medallions
  • Fresh thyme and bay leaf for the seafood stock

Instructions:

  1. To begin, prepare your seafood stock by bringing water to a boil, adding a few fresh thyme sprigs and a bay leaf. Gently submerge the peeled shrimp and deveined lobster tail into the simmering liquid, allowing them to cook until just tender, approximately 2-3 minutes. Strain the cooked seafood and stock, reserving both for later use.

  2. In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat and evenly coat the bottom with granulated sugar, allowing it to caramelize to a honeyed amber tone.

  3. Nestle the pear halves, cut side down, into the warm caramel mixture. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, gently shaking the pan occasionally for even cooking. Once the pears have achieved a tender texture, use a slotted spoon to carefully transfer them to a plate, allowing excess caramel to drizzle back into the pan. Flip to cool slightly on their sides.

  4. Preheat your oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C) and arrange the reserved tart rings or Tarte Tatin mold in a baking sheet.

  5. Reheat the caramel pan over medium heat, deglazing with the reserved seafood stock to lift any flavorful bits clinging to the bottom. Add the slightly cooled pears back into the caramel, gently arranging them within the rings or Tarte Tatin mold.

  6. Cover the fruits with a single layer of puff pastry, either laying whole or carefully cutting and placing the pre-made rounds to fit snugly into each ring. Optionally, score a lattice design into the pastry to allow steam to escape and create an impressive presentation.

  7. Place the baking sheet into the preheated oven, allowing the Tarte Tatin to bake for about 25-30 minutes or until the puff pastry has risen and turned a golden brown.

  8. Allow the Tarte Tatin to cool slightly before gently running a knife around the outer rim of each ring or mold. Invert the tarts onto a serving platter, still on the tart ring or, if using a Tarte Tatin mold, a large, flat-bottomed plate. Let the warm caramel drizzle down the sides of the pears and surrounding pastry crust.

  9. Arrange the cooked shrimp and lobster medallions atop the pear halves, sitting elegantly within the caramel-infused pastry. Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or thyme, and proudly display your showstopping creation just beyond reach, teasing the judges and enticing them further into wanting to taste this decadent treasure.
