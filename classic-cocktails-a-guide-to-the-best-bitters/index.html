Classic Cocktails: A Guide to the Best Bitters


When it comes to crafting the perfect cocktail, the right ingredients can make all the difference. Among these, bitters stand out as a crucial component that can elevate a drink from ordinary to extraordinary. This guide will delve into the world of bitters, exploring their history, types, and how to use them to create classic cocktails that impress.

What Are Bitters?

Bitters are concentrated flavoring agents made from a variety of herbs, spices, roots, and fruits. They are typically infused in alcohol, resulting in a potent liquid that adds depth and complexity to cocktails. While they are often used in small quantities, their impact on the overall flavor profile of a drink is significant.

A Brief History of Bitters

The history of bitters dates back to ancient times when herbal tinctures were used for medicinal purposes. In the 19th century, bitters began to gain popularity in the cocktail scene, with brands like Angostura and Peychaud’s leading the charge. These early bitters were often marketed as health tonics, but as the cocktail culture blossomed, they became essential ingredients in many classic recipes.

Types of Bitters

Bitters come in a wide variety of flavors, each contributing its unique character to cocktails. Here are some of the most popular types of bitters you should know about:

1. Aromatic Bitters

Aromatic bitters are perhaps the most well-known type, with Angostura bitters being the most iconic. These bitters are characterized by their complex blend of spices and herbs, making them a versatile addition to many cocktails. They are commonly used in classics like the Old Fashioned and the Manhattan.

2. Orange Bitters

Orange bitters add a bright, citrusy note to cocktails. They are made from the peels of bitter oranges and often include spices like coriander and cardamom. Orange bitters are essential in drinks like the Martini and the Daiquiri, where they enhance the overall flavor without overpowering the other ingredients.

3. Peychaud’s Bitters

Originating from New Orleans, Peychaud’s bitters are known for their distinct anise flavor and vibrant red color. They are a key ingredient in the Sazerac, a classic cocktail that showcases the bitters’ unique profile. Peychaud’s bitters can also be used in other cocktails to add a touch of sweetness and complexity.

4. Celery Bitters

Celery bitters offer a savory twist to cocktails, making them an excellent choice for drinks that require a touch of umami. They are often used in Bloody Marys and other savory cocktails, providing a refreshing contrast to the usual sweet and sour flavors.

5. Chocolate Bitters

For those who enjoy a hint of sweetness, chocolate bitters can add a rich, decadent flavor to cocktails. They work well in dessert cocktails and can also enhance the flavor of whiskey-based drinks, creating a delightful balance of flavors.

How to Use Bitters in Cocktails

Using bitters in cocktails is an art that requires a delicate touch. Here are some tips on how to incorporate bitters into your mixology:

1. Start Small

Bitters are potent, so it’s essential to start with just a few dashes. You can always add more if needed, but it’s challenging to correct a drink that has been over-bittered.

2. Pairing Flavors

Consider the flavor profile of your cocktail when choosing bitters. For example, aromatic bitters work well with whiskey, while orange bitters complement gin and vodka. Experiment with different combinations to find the perfect match.

3. Layering Flavors

Bitters can be used to enhance the complexity of a cocktail. Try using multiple types of bitters in a single drink to create a more nuanced flavor profile. For instance, combining aromatic and orange bitters can add depth to a classic Martini.

4. Garnishing

Bitters can also be used in conjunction with garnishes to enhance the overall presentation and flavor of a cocktail. A few dashes of orange bitters in a gin and tonic, paired with a fresh orange slice, can elevate the drink’s aesthetic and taste.

Classic Cocktails Featuring Bitters

Now that you have a better understanding of bitters and how to use them, let’s explore some classic cocktails that showcase these essential ingredients.

Old Fashioned

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange twist (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a rocks glass, muddle the sugar cube and bitters together.
  2. Add a splash of water to dissolve the sugar.
  3. Fill the glass with ice and pour in the whiskey.
  4. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Garnish with an orange twist.

Manhattan

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz rye whiskey
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry (for garnish)
