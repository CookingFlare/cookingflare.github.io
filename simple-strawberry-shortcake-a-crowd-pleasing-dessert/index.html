Simple Strawberry Shortcake: A Crowd-Pleasing Dessert


There’s nothing like a warm summer day to remind us of the joys of sweet and refreshing desserts. Among the many delightful treats that can brighten up our lives, strawberry shortcake stands out as a classic favorite that never goes out of style. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a simple yet impressive strawberry shortcake that’s sure to please even the most discerning palates.

The Magic of Strawberry Shortcake

Strawberry shortcake is a timeless dessert that has been a staple of American cuisine for generations. The combination of sweet strawberries, fluffy shortcake, and creamy whipped cream is a match made in heaven. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a potluck dinner, or simply looking for a special treat to brighten up your day, strawberry shortcake is the perfect dessert to impress your friends and family.

The Essential Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients you’ll need to make this delicious dessert:

  • 2 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 3/4 cup of heavy cream, cold
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Vanilla extract (optional)

The Simple Recipe

Now that we have our ingredients, let’s get started with the recipe. Here’s a step-by-step guide to making the perfect strawberry shortcake:

  1. Make the Shortcake: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and beaten egg. Add this mixture to the dry ingredients and stir until the dough comes together in a shaggy mass. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it becomes smooth and pliable. Roll the dough out to a thickness of about 1 inch (2.5 cm) and use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out the shortcakes. Gather the scraps, re-roll the dough, and cut out additional shortcakes.
  2. Bake the Shortcakes: Place the shortcakes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space between each cake. Brush the tops with a little extra heavy cream and sprinkle with granulated sugar. Bake the shortcakes for 18-20 minutes, or until they’re golden brown and firm to the touch.
  3. Prepare the Strawberries: In a large bowl, toss the sliced strawberries with a tablespoon or two of granulated sugar. Let them sit for about 15-20 minutes, until they start to release their juice and become syrupy.
  4. Assemble the Shortcakes: Split the warm shortcakes in half horizontally using a serrated knife. Spoon some of the sweetened strawberries over the bottom half of each shortcake, leaving a small border around the edges. Top with the top half of the shortcake and serve immediately.

Tips and Variations

To take your strawberry shortcake to the next level, try these tips and variations:

  • Use fresh strawberries for the best flavor and texture.
  • Add a sprinkle of sugar to the shortcakes before baking for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Try using different types of milk or cream, such as almond milk or coconut cream, for a non-dairy version.
  • Add a splash of vanilla extract to the whipped cream for an extra hint of flavor.
  • Top with fresh mint leaves or chopped nuts for a pop of color and added texture.

Conclusion

And there you have it – a simple yet impressive recipe for strawberry shortcake that’s sure to delight your friends and family. With its sweet and tangy flavors, fluffy shortcake, and creamy whipped cream, this dessert is a crowd-pleaser that’s sure to become a new favorite. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the sweet taste of summer!
