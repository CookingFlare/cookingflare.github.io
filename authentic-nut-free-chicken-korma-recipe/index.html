Authentic Nut-Free Chicken Korma Recipe


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, you’ve likely heard of chicken korma. This creamy and flavorful dish is a staple in Indian cooking, known for its rich blend of spices and luxurious texture. However, for those with nut allergies, traditional korma recipes that include cashews or almonds can be off-limits. But fear not, because I have a delicious and authentic nut-free chicken korma recipe that will satisfy your cravings and accommodate your dietary needs.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt to taste

For the Korma Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, cayenne pepper, and salt. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. In a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat the ghee or vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

  3. Add the marinated chicken to the skillet, stirring occasionally, and cook until the chicken is browned on all sides.

  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, garam masala, ground turmeric, ground coriander, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the spices to bloom and the tomatoes to soften.

  5. Pour in the chicken broth, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened.

  6. Stir in the heavy cream and let the korma simmer for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken further.

  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Serve this nut-free chicken korma hot with fluffy basmati rice or warm naan bread. The creamy, aromatic sauce pairs perfectly with the tender chicken, creating a dish that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Final Thoughts

This nut-free chicken korma recipe stays true to the authentic flavors of traditional Indian cuisine while catering to those with nut allergies. The blend of aromatic spices, creamy texture, and tender chicken makes this dish a standout in any culinary repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting homemade meal, this chicken korma is a must-try recipe that will leave everyone coming back for seconds. Enjoy!
