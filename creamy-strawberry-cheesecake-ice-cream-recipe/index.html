Creamy Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream Recipe


Who doesn’t love the delightful combination of creamy ice cream and the rich flavors of cheesecake? If you’re a fan of both, then this Creamy Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream recipe is a must-try! This delightful frozen treat is perfect for a hot summer day or as a sweet ending to any meal. With fresh strawberries, cream cheese, and a touch of vanilla, this homemade ice cream is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Strawberries: In a medium bowl, toss the chopped strawberries with 1/4 cup of sugar. Let the strawberries sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes, allowing them to release their juices and create a syrupy mixture.

  2. Make the Strawberry Puree: After the strawberries have macerated, transfer them to a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Set the puree aside for later use.

  3. Prepare the Cream Cheese Mixture: In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with the remaining 3/4 cup of sugar until smooth and creamy.

  4. Add the Milk and Cream: Gradually pour in the whole milk and heavy cream into the cream cheese mixture, stirring until well combined. Add the vanilla extract and mix thoroughly.

  5. Incorporate the Strawberry Puree: Gently fold the strawberry puree into the cream cheese mixture, ensuring that it’s evenly distributed.

  6. Chill the Mixture: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This step is crucial for achieving a rich and creamy texture in the finished ice cream.

  7. Churn the Ice Cream: Once the mixture is thoroughly chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. As the ice cream begins to set, sprinkle in the graham cracker crumbs, allowing them to mix evenly throughout the ice cream.

  8. Freeze and Enjoy: Transfer the churned ice cream into a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.

  9. Serve and Indulge: When ready to serve, scoop the Creamy Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream into bowls or cones. Garnish with fresh strawberries or a sprinkle of graham cracker crumbs for an extra touch of flavor and crunch.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, try using other berries such as raspberries or blueberries to create different fruity variations of this cheesecake ice cream.
  • To add a crunchy element, consider mixing in crushed graham crackers, cookie crumbs, or even chopped nuts during the churning process.
  • If you prefer a tangier flavor, a tablespoon of lemon juice added to the strawberry puree can provide a delightful contrast to the sweetness of the ice cream.

Final Thoughts

This Creamy Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream is a luscious and indulgent dessert that captures the essence of summer in every spoonful. With the vibrant flavor of fresh strawberries and the creamy richness of cheesecake, this homemade treat is sure to impress your family and friends. So, grab your ingredients, whip up a batch, and savor the joy of making your very own creamy, dreamy ice cream at home.
