Egg-Free Sweet and Sour Meatballs: A Tangy and Savory Dish


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying dish that’s both tangy and savory, look no further than these egg-free sweet and sour meatballs. Packed with flavor and easy to make, these meatballs are the perfect addition to any meal. Whether you’re serving them as an appetizer, a main course, or even as a party snack, these meatballs are sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

For the sweet and sour sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, chopped onion, minced garlic, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter, and place them on the prepared baking sheet. You should get around 24 meatballs.

  4. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they are cooked through and browned on the outside.

  5. While the meatballs are baking, prepare the sweet and sour sauce. In a saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, pineapple juice, soy sauce, cornstarch, and red pepper flakes (if using). Whisk until smooth.

  6. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring constantly. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

  7. Once the meatballs are cooked, transfer them to a serving dish and pour the sweet and sour sauce over them. Gently toss the meatballs in the sauce until they are evenly coated.

  8. Serve the egg-free sweet and sour meatballs hot, garnished with chopped green onions or sesame seeds if desired. They can be enjoyed on their own as an appetizer or paired with rice or noodles for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the meatballs gluten-free, you can use gluten-free breadcrumbs or substitute them with crushed gluten-free crackers.

  • If you prefer a spicier sweet and sour sauce, you can increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add a dash of hot sauce.

  • For a touch of freshness and crunch, you can add diced bell peppers and pineapple chunks to the sauce.

  • Leftover meatballs can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. They can also be frozen for longer storage. Just thaw them in the refrigerator before reheating.

These egg-free sweet and sour meatballs are a crowd-pleasing dish that will satisfy your cravings for something tangy and savory. With their juicy meatballs and flavorful sauce, they’re a perfect balance of sweet and sour. Give this recipe a try and get ready to enjoy a mouthwatering dish that will leave you wanting more!
