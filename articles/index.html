Grilled Pineapple Salsa Verde: A Tangy Fourth of July Condiment

Grilling is a quintessential activity during the Fourth of July celebrations. The smoky flavors and charred marks on the food add an extra level of deliciousness. While burgers and hot dogs are the stars of the show, it’s always fun to spice things up with a unique condiment. Enter Grilled Pineapple Salsa Verde – a tangy and refreshing twist on the classic salsa verde. Ingredients 1 ripe pineapple 1 jalapeño pepper 1 small red onion 2 cloves of garlic 1 bunch of fresh cilantro Juice of 2 limes Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Start by preparing the pineapple. Remove the skin and slice it into rings, about 1/2 inch thick. Place the pineapple rings directly on the grill grates and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they have nice grill marks. While the pineapple is grilling, prepare the other ingredients. Finely chop the jalapeño pepper, red onion, garlic, and cilantro. Set aside. Once the pineapple is grilled, remove it from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, chop it into small pieces. In a bowl, combine the grilled pineapple, chopped jalapeño, red onion, garlic, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed. Taste and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. You can add more lime juice for extra tanginess or more jalapeño for a spicier kick. Let the Grilled Pineapple Salsa Verde sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve the salsa with your favorite grilled meats, tacos, or tortilla chips. It adds a delightful burst of flavor and a tropical twist to any dish. Why You’ll Love This Recipe Grilled Pineapple Salsa Verde is a crowd-pleaser for several reasons. First and foremost, it’s bursting with flavors. The smoky sweetness of the grilled pineapple pairs perfectly with the tanginess of the lime juice and the freshness of the cilantro. The jalapeño adds just the right amount of heat to balance out the sweetness. ...
