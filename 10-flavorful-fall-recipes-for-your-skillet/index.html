10 Flavorful Fall Recipes for Your Skillet


As the crisp air of fall settles in, it’s the perfect time to embrace the comforting flavors that define the season. The rich, warm aromas of roasted vegetables, savory meats, and hearty stews can fill your kitchen, making it the ultimate place to unwind. One of the best tools to create those comforting meals is a trusty skillet. Whether you’re cooking up a quick dinner for yourself or preparing a meal to share with family and friends, a skillet is versatile, easy to use, and perfect for the season. In this post, we’ll dive into 10 flavorful fall recipes that you can easily prepare in your skillet.

1. Skillet Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Risotto is a cozy fall dish that’s both filling and flavorful. Butternut squash adds a natural sweetness and vibrant color, while fresh sage leaves bring an earthy, aromatic note that elevates this dish to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced butternut squash and cook until tender, about 7-10 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice, allowing it to toast slightly for 2 minutes.
  4. Add the white wine, stirring until it’s mostly absorbed. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring frequently, and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more.
  5. Once the rice is tender and creamy, stir in the roasted squash, Parmesan cheese, and fresh sage. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve warm, topped with additional sage if desired.

2. Apple Cinnamon Skillet Oats

A warm, hearty breakfast filled with seasonal flavors can set the tone for your entire day. This apple cinnamon skillet oats recipe combines the sweetness of apples with the spice of cinnamon, creating a perfect fall breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups almond milk or milk of choice
  • 1 large apple, cored and diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Chopped nuts (walnuts or pecans) for topping (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a skillet, combine the rolled oats, almond milk, diced apple, cinnamon, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Stir occasionally and cook for 5-7 minutes, until the oats have absorbed most of the liquid and are creamy.
  3. Remove from heat and let it sit for a couple of minutes to thicken.
  4. Top with chopped nuts and extra maple syrup, if desired.

3. Skillet Pumpkin Chili

When the weather cools down, there’s nothing more satisfying than a big bowl of chili. This pumpkin chili recipe adds a seasonal twist to the classic with the creamy, rich taste of pumpkin.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet, brown the ground turkey or beef over medium heat until fully cooked. Remove excess fat if necessary.
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the skillet, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin puree, diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Let the chili simmer on low heat for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the chili becomes too thick, add a bit of water to reach your desired consistency.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with cornbread or tortilla chips.

4. Skillet Maple-Glazed Chicken Thighs with Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Maple syrup and chicken are a winning combination in this savory and sweet dish. The rich maple glaze caramelizes beautifully on the chicken thighs, while the roasted Brussels sprouts provide the perfect side to balance out the flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together maple syrup, Dijon mustard, soy sauce, and olive oil.
  3. Heat an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper. Add the chicken to the skillet and cook skin-side down for about 5 minutes, until crispy.
  4. Flip the chicken thighs over and pour the maple glaze over them.
  5. Arrange the halved Brussels sprouts around the chicken in the skillet. Drizzle the sprouts with a little olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 25-30 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and the Brussels sprouts are caramelized.
  7. Serve the chicken thighs with the roasted Brussels sprouts and enjoy.

5. Skillet Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

These vegetarian tacos are packed with flavor and nutrition. The sweetness of roasted sweet potatoes pairs perfectly with the savory black beans, making it a satisfying fall meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Fresh toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime wedges, and salsa

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the diced sweet potatoes in olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and lightly caramelized.
  3. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, heat a skillet over medium heat. Add the black beans, warming them through for about 5 minutes.
  4. Once the sweet potatoes are done, combine them with the black beans in the skillet. Toss gently to combine and heat through.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet for 1-2 minutes per side.
  6. Assemble the tacos by spooning the sweet potato and black bean mixture into each tortilla. Top with fresh avocado, cilantro, lime wedges, and salsa.

6. Skillet Apple-Pecan Pork Chops

Pork chops are a perfect main dish for fall, and the combination of apples and pecans adds both sweetness and crunch. This recipe is simple, yet it delivers a hearty and flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 apples, sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper.
  2. Brown the pork chops in the skillet for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the sliced apples, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the apples begin to soften.
  4. Stir in the chopped pecans and return the pork chops to the skillet. Cook for another 2 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Serve the pork chops with the apple-pecan mixture spooned on top.

7. Skillet Cranberry-Orange Glazed Salmon

Salmon pairs wonderfully with the tangy sweetness of cranberry and orange. This recipe is light yet flavorful, making it a great dish for fall dinner gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper.
  2. Cook the salmon fillets for 4-5 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through. Remove the salmon from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the fresh cranberries, orange juice, honey, and thyme. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the cranberries burst and the glaze thickens.
  4. Return the salmon fillets to the skillet, spooning the cranberry-orange glaze over them. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Serve the salmon with extra glaze on top and enjoy the seasonal flavors.

8. Skillet Roasted Carrots and Parsnips with Garlic Butter

These roasted root vegetables are the perfect side dish for any fall meal. The garlic butter brings out the natural sweetness of the carrots and parsnips, making them irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2 large parsnips, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and thyme, cooking for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Toss the carrot and parsnip sticks in the garlic butter, ensuring they are evenly coated. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 25-30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve warm as a side dish.

9. Skillet Pumpkin Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese gets a cozy fall twist with the addition of pumpkin. This creamy and comforting dish is perfect for a fall dinner or potluck.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Cook the elbow macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the milk and pumpkin puree, stirring until combined.
  3. Stir in the cheddar and Parmesan cheeses, allowing them to melt into a creamy sauce. Add the nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss the cooked macaroni in the sauce, ensuring it’s fully coated.
  5. Serve hot with extra cheese on top if desired.

10. Skillet Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

This fresh salad balances the sweetness of pears with the sharpness of Gorgonzola, creating a fall salad that’s perfect as a side or a light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pears, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled Gorgonzola cheese
  • 1/4 cup walnuts, chopped
  • Balsamic vinegar for drizzling

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced pears and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until slightly caramelized.
  2. Toss the mixed greens, Gorgonzola cheese, and walnuts in a large bowl.
  3. Add the caramelized pears on top and drizzle with balsamic vinegar.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the fresh, fall flavors.

These 10 skillet recipes are a wonderful way to enjoy the seasonal produce and comforting flavors of fall. Whether you’re craving a cozy breakfast, a hearty dinner, or a fresh salad, there’s something for everyone. Each dish is packed with seasonal ingredients that will make your kitchen feel like the heart of autumn. So grab your skillet and start cooking up these delicious fall-inspired meals!
