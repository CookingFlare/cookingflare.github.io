Ramen, but Make It Healthy: Lighter Chinese Noodle Recipes


Craving the flavor and warmth of noodle dishes but feeling guilty about the calories and fat? Fear not, for we’re about to introduce you to a healthier twist on the classic, delicious Ramen noodles. With these lighter Chinese noodle recipes, you can indulge without worrying about the scale! Let’s prepare to experience a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that’ll keep your taste buds dancing, and your waistline grateful. So grab those chopsticks, let’s get cooking!

1. Veggie-Packed Lo Mein

Lo Mein is a definite crowd-pleaser, and it’s so simple to make! By packing this dish with fresh vegetables, you’ll feel free to enjoy your bowl without any guilt.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. 100% whole wheat noodles
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cups assorted julienne vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, zucchini, broccoli)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bok choy
  • 2 cups cooked shrimp or tofu (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook according to package instructions. When done, drain and rinse with cold water to prevent overcooking. Set aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds or until aromatic.
  3. Add all of your veggies, starting with the harder and taking your time to cook them down, like carrots and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, and then add the more delicate veggies like zucchini, broccoli, and bok choy.
  4. If you’re adding shrimp or tofu, cook them until they’re fully heated through.
  5. Combine the noodles with the vegetable mixture and season with soy sauce. Stir everything together, ensuring the noodles and veggies are fully coated in the sauce.
  6. Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy your guilt-free, veggie-packed Lo Mein.

2. Spicy Sriracha Zoodle Stir-fry

Who said you can’t play with your food? This dish takes a classic and lightens it up using zucchini noodles, also known as “zoodles.” This fun and healthy twist on a Chinese favorite is sure to impress!

Ingredients:

  • 4 large zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon Sriracha (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup thinly sliced red bell pepper
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded (optional)
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, add zucchini noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds or until aromatic.
  3. Add sliced red bell pepper, mushrooms, and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Optionally, add cooked chicken and heat through.
  5. Combine zucchini noodles with the vegetable mixture. Add Sriracha and mix thoroughly, coating all the noodles and veggies in the tangy, spicy sauce.
  6. Garnish with sliced green onions and fresh cilantro.
  7. Toss, and your Spicy Sriracha Zoodle Stir-fry is ready to be devoured!

3. Buddha Bowl Noodle Salad

Buddha Bowl Noodle Salad…say that five times fast! This playful dish is a delightful mix of flavors and textures that bring an explosion of taste with every bite. The perfect dish to share with friends and family without any guilt.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. cooked whole wheat noodles
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 cups mixed chopped vegetables (cucumber, red bell pepper, matchstick carrots)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh herbs (cilantro, parsley, and mint)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil water and cook whole wheat noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent overcooking. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked noodles, mixed chopped vegetables, cooked quinoa, and chickpeas.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, soy sauce, salt, and pepper to create a light and tangy dressing.
  4. Pour the dressing over the noodle and vegetable mixture and toss everything together to ensure even coating.
  5. Top the Buddha Bowl Noodle Salad with fresh herbs.
  6. Serve immediately or chill in the refrigerator for an hour before serving, if desired.

Enjoy these delightful lighter Chinese noodle recipes without feeling guilty! Feel free to experiment and swap ingredients or add extras based on your dietary preferences. Happy cooking!
