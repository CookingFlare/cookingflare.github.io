How to Preserve Citrus: Canning Recipes for Oranges and Lemons


Citrus fruits like oranges and lemons are the bright jewels of winter, bursting with vibrant flavor and versatile uses. From refreshing drinks to zesty desserts, they bring sunshine into our kitchens even in the coldest months. But what if you want to savor the tantalizing tang of citrus all year long? The answer lies in preserving these golden fruits through canning. Learning how to preserve citrus not only minimizes food waste but also ensures you have a pantry stocked with vibrant flavors for every season.

In this guide, we’ll take an in-depth look at how to preserve oranges and lemons through tried-and-true canning methods. Whether it’s marmalades, whole preserved lemons, or orange segments in syrup, you’ll discover all the tools and techniques needed to keep citrus fruits at their peak for months.

Why Preserve Citrus?

Canning citrus fruits isn’t just about extending their shelf life; it’s about infusing your kitchen creations with their aromatic zest and tart sweetness. Beyond their bold taste, oranges and lemons are also packed with vitamin C, antioxidants, and essential oils that add complexity to both sweet and savory dishes. Preserving them ensures you never run out of this culinary cornerstone.

From a practical standpoint, canning citrus can also save you money. Citrus fruit reaches peak quality and affordability during its harvest season, typically through late autumn and winter. By preserving a batch during this time, you can enjoy fresh-tasting citrus at a fraction of the cost of out-of-season produce.

Essential Equipment for Canning Citrus

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to gather the right tools to ensure safe and effective canning. Here’s a checklist to have on hand:

  • Canning jars with lids and rings: Opt for sterilized mason jars to store your citrus preserves safely.
  • Water bath canner: A large pot with a rack for processing the filled jars.
  • Jar lifter: A specialized set of tongs to handle hot jars during and after processing.
  • Citrus juicer or reamer: For extracting fresh juice with ease.
  • Zester or microplane grater: Perfect for recipes that call for citrus zest.
  • Sharp knife and cutting board: Precision tools to segment and prepare citrus fruits.
  • Large saucepan: To cook syrups, marmalades, or other preserves.
  • Fine-mesh strainer: Useful for separating seeds and pulp.

Having these tools ready ensures smooth canning and reduces stress during the process.

Tips for Selecting and Preparing Citrus

The success of any canning project begins with choosing the right fruit. Follow these tips for selecting and preparing citrus:

  1. Pick Fresh and Firm Fruit: Look for brightly colored oranges and lemons without blemishes or softness. The firmer the fruit, the easier it is to segment and can.

  2. Organic Is Best: Since many canning recipes incorporate citrus rind, purchasing organic fruit helps reduce potential pesticide exposure.

  3. Wash Thoroughly: Use a brush to scrub the peel under running water to remove wax, dirt, or residue.

  4. Blanch Carefully: If you’re candying the peel or preserving whole lemons, blanching loosens the waxy outer coating and makes the fruit easier to work with.

By properly selecting and preparing your citrus, you set a firm foundation for your preserves to shine.

Canning Recipe #1: Classic Orange Marmalade

Orange marmalade is a quintessential way to enjoy preserved citrus. Its glossy, bitter-sweet spread is perfect on toast, scones, or as a glaze for roasted meats. This recipe yields approximately 4-5 half-pint jars.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large oranges
  • 1 lemon
  • 6 cups granulated sugar
  • 6-7 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Citrus: Peel the oranges and lemon, removing as much white pith as possible. Chop the peels into thin strips and set them aside. Segment the fruit by cutting between the membrane walls and remove any seeds.

  2. Cook the Mixture: In a large saucepan, combine the peel strips, citrus segments, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then simmer for about 45 minutes or until the peel has softened.

  3. Add Sugar: Stir in the sugar and return to a boil, constantly stirring until the sugar dissolves. Continue cooking until the mixture reaches a temperature of 220°F (or passes the gel test).

  4. Jar and Process: Ladle the hot marmalade into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4-inch of headspace. Wipe the rims, seal the jars with lids and rings, and process them in a water bath canner for 10 minutes.

  5. Cool and Store: Remove jars from the canner, place them on a towel-lined surface, and let them cool overnight. Check seals and store in a cool, dark place.

Canning Recipe #2: Preserved Lemons in Salt

Preserved lemons are a staple in Middle Eastern and North African cuisine, adding tangy depth to tagines, salads, and marinades. These salted lemons are incredibly easy to make and require minimal ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 small lemons
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt per lemon
  • Fresh lemon juice (as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Slice and Salt: Wash the lemons thoroughly and slice an “X” into the base of each, leaving the fruit attached at the top. Sprinkle kosher salt into the cuts and press each lemon tightly into a sterilized jar.

  2. Pack the Jar: As you add lemons to the jar, press down to squeeze out juices. Add more salt between layers to ensure each lemon is coated.

  3. Top with Juice: Once the jar is full of lemons, pour fresh lemon juice to completely submerge the fruit. Seal the jar tightly.

  4. Ferment and Store: Store the jar at room temperature for 2-3 weeks, turning it occasionally to distribute the salt. After fermentation, preserved lemons can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.

Canning Recipe #3: Orange Segments in Light Syrup

For a simple and versatile preserve, citrus segments in syrup are the way to go. These luscious slices retain their natural brightness and are great for desserts, cocktails, or snacking.

Ingredients:

  • 10 large oranges
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups sugar

Instructions:

  1. Segment the Oranges: Peel and segment the oranges, removing all traces of pith and membrane. Be meticulous to avoid bitterness.

  2. Prepare the Syrup: In a saucepan, combine water and sugar. Heat gently, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Simmer for about 5 minutes, then remove from heat.

  3. Pack and Cover: Pack the orange segments into sterilized jars. Pour the hot syrup over the fruit, leaving 1/2-inch headspace.

  4. Process the Jars: Wipe the rims, seal with lids and rings, and process the jars in a water bath canner for 10 minutes.

  5. Cool Down: Let the jars cool overnight, ensuring they’ve sealed properly. Store in a cool place for up to a year.

Using Your Preserved Citrus

Preserved oranges and lemons are fantastic pantry staples that can be used in countless ways. Here are some ideas:

  • Spread orange marmalade on warm biscuits or whisk it into salad dressings for a sweet, tangy kick.
  • Add a slice of preserved lemon to simmering stews for an explosion of citrusy flavor.
  • Serve orange segments in syrup over ice cream or yogurt for a delectable dessert.

Conclusion

Preserving citrus through canning is more than just a practical way to extend fruit freshness—it’s an artistry that transforms humble oranges and lemons into gourmet treasures. With these recipes for orange marmalade, preserved lemons, and citrus segments in syrup, you can capture the essence of the season and enjoy it all year round. Plus, by mastering these simple canning techniques, you’ll never look at citrus the same way again.

Embrace the joy of canning and bring a little bit of sunshine to your pantry shelves. Whether you’re brightening up breakfast with marmalade or infusing your favorite recipes with preserved lemons, these homemade delights are sure to become kitchen staples. Happy preserving!
