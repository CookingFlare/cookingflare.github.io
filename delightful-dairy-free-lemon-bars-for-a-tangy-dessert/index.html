Delightful Dairy-Free Lemon Bars for a Tangy Dessert


Lemon bars are a classic dessert that never fails to brighten up any occasion with their zesty, tangy flavor. Whether you’re a fan of citrusy treats or simply looking for a dairy-free dessert option, these delightful dairy-free lemon bars are sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. With a buttery almond flour crust and a silky smooth lemon filling, these bars are a perfect balance of sweet and sour, making them an ideal treat for warm weather gatherings, picnics, or simply as a refreshing after-dinner indulgence.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup arrowroot powder
  • Pinch of turmeric (for color, optional)

For Dusting (optional):

  • Powdered sugar or coconut flour

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8 inch baking dish and line it with parchment paper, leaving an overhang for easy removal.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Stir until the mixture comes together and resembles a dough.

  3. Press the dough into the bottom of the prepared baking dish, ensuring an even layer across the bottom. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is lightly golden. Remove from the oven and let it cool while preparing the lemon filling.

  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the freshly squeezed lemon juice, lemon zest, coconut cream, maple syrup, arrowroot powder, and turmeric (if using). Ensure that the mixture is well combined and smooth.

  5. Pour the lemon filling over the cooled crust, then return the baking dish to the oven. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set.

  6. Once done, remove the lemon bars from the oven and allow them to cool at room temperature. Once cooled, transfer the dish to the refrigerator and chill for at least 2 hours, or until the bars are firm.

  7. Once chilled, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the dish. Place them on a cutting board and, if desired, dust the top with powdered sugar or coconut flour for a decorative finish.

  8. With a sharp knife, carefully cut the bars into squares or rectangles. Serve and enjoy these delightful dairy-free lemon bars!

Tips and Variations

  • For a gluten-free option, ensure that all ingredients used are certified gluten-free.
  • Feel free to adjust the sweetness of the lemon filling to suit your preference by adding more or less maple syrup.
  • Add a pop of color to the lemon filling by incorporating a small amount of turmeric, which also enhances the vibrant citrus hue.

Final Thoughts

These delightful dairy-free lemon bars are a wonderful way to indulge in a tangy yet sweet dessert while accommodating dietary preferences. Their bright and refreshing flavor makes them a delightful addition to any gathering or as a treat for yourself. Whether you’re dairy-free, gluten-free, or simply love the taste of lemon, these bars are a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth. Enjoy the process of making these bars and savor the delightful flavors with every bite!
