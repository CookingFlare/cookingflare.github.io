One Pot Chicken and Rice Casserole


If you’re looking for a delicious and convenient meal that requires minimal cleanup, look no further than this One Pot Chicken and Rice Casserole recipe. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a hearty and satisfying dish that the whole family will love. Plus, the best part is that everything cooks in one pot, making it a breeze to prepare and clean up afterward.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs (680g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup (200g) long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup (150g) frozen peas
  • 2 cups (480ml) chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by seasoning the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, and paprika. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a drizzle of olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken thighs and cook until browned on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, minced garlic, and diced red bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the oil and vegetables. Allow the rice to cook for a minute or two until it starts to become slightly translucent.

  4. Pour in the chicken broth, dried thyme, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Give everything a good stir to combine.

  5. Nestle the chicken thighs back into the pot, making sure they are submerged in the liquid. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer gently for 20 minutes.

  6. After 20 minutes, remove the lid and scatter the frozen peas over the top of the casserole. Give everything a gentle stir to incorporate the peas.

  7. Cover the pot again and let the casserole simmer for an additional 5 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.

  8. Once everything is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the casserole to thicken slightly.

  9. Serve the One Pot Chicken and Rice Casserole hot, garnished with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as carrots, corn, or broccoli. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • For an extra boost of flavor, you can use chicken stock instead of chicken broth. Additionally, you can add a splash of white wine or lemon juice to brighten up the dish.

  • If you prefer a crispy top, you can transfer the casserole to a baking dish after step 6 and broil it for a few minutes until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop for a quick and easy meal.

Enjoy this One Pot Chicken and Rice Casserole as a comforting and satisfying meal that requires minimal effort and delivers maximum flavor. With its simple ingredients and easy preparation, it’s a recipe that you’ll want to keep in your regular rotation. Give it a try and taste the magic of a delicious one-pot wonder!
