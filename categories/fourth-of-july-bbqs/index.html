Grilled Vegetable Panini: A Tasty Fourth of July Lunch

Are you looking for a delicious and healthy option to celebrate Fourth of July? Look no further! This Grilled Vegetable Panini is the perfect choice for a tasty and satisfying lunch. Packed with fresh vegetables and bursting with flavor, this recipe will impress your guests and leave them wanting more. Ingredients 1 zucchini, sliced lengthwise 1 yellow squash, sliced lengthwise 1 red bell pepper, seeded and sliced 1 red onion, sliced 1 eggplant, sliced 4 slices of your favorite bread (sourdough or ciabatta work well) 4 slices of provolone cheese 1/4 cup of pesto sauce Salt and pepper to taste Olive oil for brushing Instructions Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Brush the sliced vegetables with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place the vegetables on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have grill marks. Remove the vegetables from the grill and set aside. Take the bread slices and spread a generous amount of pesto sauce on one side of each slice. Layer the grilled vegetables on top of the pesto sauce, alternating the different vegetables to create a colorful and flavorful panini. Top the vegetables with a slice of provolone cheese. Place the remaining bread slices on top to form sandwiches. Preheat a panini press or a skillet over medium heat. If using a panini press, place the sandwiches in the press and cook for 4-5 minutes, or until the bread is crispy and the cheese has melted. If using a skillet, place the sandwiches in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, using a spatula to press them down gently. Remove the panini from the heat and let them cool for a minute before slicing them in half. Serve the Grilled Vegetable Panini warm and enjoy! Tips and Variations Feel free to experiment with different vegetables based on your preference and what’s in season. Asparagus, mushrooms, or even cherry tomatoes can be great additions to the panini. If you’re not a fan of pesto sauce, you can substitute it with hummus, aioli, or your favorite sandwich spread. Add a touch of freshness to your panini by tossing some arugula or baby spinach leaves in a little olive oil and lemon juice, then layering them on top of the grilled vegetables. For some added protein, you can include grilled chicken, turkey, or even tofu slices to make it a heartier meal. Conclusion Grilled Vegetable Panini is a fantastic alternative to the traditional Fourth of July barbecue fare. It’s a lighter option that still packs a punch in terms of flavor and satisfaction. With the vibrant colors of the grilled vegetables and the gooey melted cheese, this panini is not only a treat for your taste buds but also a feast for your eyes. ...
