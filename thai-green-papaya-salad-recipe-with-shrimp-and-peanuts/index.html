Thai Green Papaya Salad Recipe with Shrimp and Peanuts


This delicious and refreshing Thai Green Papaya Salad with Shrimp and Peanuts is a perfect dish for those hot summer days. Packed with vibrant flavors and textures, this salad will transport you straight to the streets of Thailand. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or looking to try something new, this recipe is a must-try!

Ingredients

  • 1 green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 1 cup cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, roughly chopped
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded green papaya, cooked shrimp, roasted peanuts, chopped Thai bird’s eye chilies, and minced garlic.

  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar until the sugar dissolves. Taste the dressing and adjust the flavors according to your preference. If you prefer a sweeter salad, add a bit more sugar. For more tanginess, add more lime juice.

  3. Pour the dressing over the papaya mixture and toss well to ensure everything is evenly coated. Use your hands or a pair of tongs to gently massage the dressing into the papaya to help tenderize it.

  4. Add the cherry tomatoes and fresh cilantro leaves to the salad and gently toss to combine.

  5. Taste the salad and season with salt if needed. Remember that the fish sauce already adds saltiness, so adjust accordingly.

  6. Allow the salad to sit for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. This also gives the papaya a chance to absorb the dressing.

  7. Serve the Thai Green Papaya Salad with Shrimp and Peanuts as a refreshing appetizer or as a light main course. Garnish with extra chopped peanuts and cilantro leaves for added crunch and freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • If you can’t find green papaya, you can substitute it with jicama or even cucumber for a different twist.
  • For a vegetarian version, omit the shrimp and add some tofu or extra peanuts for protein.
  • If you prefer a milder spice level, remove the seeds from the Thai bird’s eye chilies or replace them with milder chili varieties.
  • Feel free to add additional vegetables such as shredded carrots, bean sprouts, or even mango for added sweetness and texture.
  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a handful of fresh mint leaves or Thai basil to the salad.

Enjoy this Thai Green Papaya Salad with Shrimp and Peanuts as a light and refreshing meal. It’s bursting with flavors and textures that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Thailand. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply looking for a quick and delicious meal, this salad is sure to impress. Try it out and let your taste buds take a trip to Southeast Asia!
