Spicy Kung Pao Chicken Stir Fry: A Chinese Takeout Classic


If you’re a fan of Chinese takeout, then you’ve likely come across the tantalizing aroma and bold flavors of Kung Pao Chicken. This spicy stir-fry dish is a staple of Sichuan cuisine and has made its way into the hearts and stomachs of food enthusiasts around the world. The combination of tender chicken, crunchy peanuts, and a fiery, savory sauce makes Kung Pao Chicken a beloved classic that never fails to impress.

The Legend of Kung Pao Chicken

Legend has it that Kung Pao Chicken is named after Ding Baozhen, a Qing Dynasty official with the title “Gongbao.” The dish was reportedly one of his favorites, and it was eventually named after him, using the alternate spelling “Kung Pao” in English. Over the years, the dish has evolved and adapted to different tastes, but the core elements of heat, savory flavors, and contrasting textures remain constant.

Ingredients for Spicy Kung Pao Chicken Stir Fry

To recreate the magic of Kung Pao Chicken at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of Chinese black vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of Sichuan peppercorns, crushed
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted peanuts
  • 3-4 dried red chili peppers
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a bowl, combine the diced chicken with the cornstarch and 1 tablespoon of soy sauce. Toss the chicken until it’s well-coated and set it aside to marinate for about 15 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

In another bowl, mix the remaining 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, Chinese black vinegar, hoisin sauce, and sesame oil. Set the sauce aside for later use.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Chicken

Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it’s cooked through and golden brown. Once the chicken is cooked, transfer it to a plate and set it aside.

Step 4: Infuse the Flavors

In the same wok or skillet, add the garlic, ginger, Sichuan peppercorns, peanuts, and dried red chili peppers. Stir-fry these aromatics until they become fragrant, which should take about a minute.

Step 5: Combine and Finish

Return the cooked chicken to the wok and pour the prepared sauce over the ingredients. Toss everything together until the chicken is well-coated with the sauce. Stir in the sliced green onions and continue to cook for another minute.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once everything is heated through and well combined, transfer the Spicy Kung Pao Chicken Stir Fry to a serving dish. Garnish with additional sliced green onions if desired, and serve it alongside a generous portion of cooked white rice.

The Final Word

In just a few simple steps, you can bring the excitement of Chinese takeout into your own kitchen with this Spicy Kung Pao Chicken Stir Fry recipe. The marriage of spicy, savory, and nutty flavors will surely make this dish a regular on your home-cooked menu. So, grab your wok, don your apron, and get ready to experience the joy of creating this Chinese takeout classic right in your own kitchen!
