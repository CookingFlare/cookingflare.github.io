How to Make Homemade Falafel with Tahini Sauce


Cooking up a storm in the kitchen can be a therapeutic experience, especially when you get to create something as flavorful and satisfying as homemade falafel with creamy tahini sauce. In this recipe, we'll take you through the process of making crispy and delicious falafel from scratch, paired with a rich and creamy tahini sauce that's sure to become a staple in your kitchen. The Magic of Falafel For many, falafel is more than just a tasty street food - it's a cultural phenomenon that brings people together. Born in the Middle East, this crispy, flavorful chickpea delight has traveled the world, adapting to local tastes and cuisines. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a curious home cook, making falafel from scratch can be a rewarding experience. Gather Your Ingredients Before we begin cooking, let's take a look at the ingredients you'll need to make this mouthwatering falafel and tahini sauce. For the Falafel:

1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight and drained 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ghee or oil 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon baking powder 2 tablespoons lemon juice Chopped parsley, for garnish

For the Tahini Sauce:

1/2 cup tahini 1/4 cup lemon juice 1/4 cup olive oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon honey Salt, to taste Water, as needed

Making the Perfect Falafel Falafel's secret to success lies in the combination of crispy exterior and fluffy interior. To achieve this delicate balance, we need to soak the chickpeas overnight and then blend them with a mix of aromatic spices and herbs.

Prepare the chickpeas: Rinse the soaked chickpeas and drain them thoroughly.

Blend the chickpeas: Add the chickpeas to a food processor with garlic, ghee or oil, cumin, coriander, salt, baking powder, and lemon juice. Process until the mixture is well combined and slightly chunky.

Shape the falafel: Using your hands, shape the falafel mixture into small patties or balls, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. You should end up with around 20-25 falafel.

Fry the falafel: Heat about 2-3 inches of oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel until they're golden brown on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain the falafel on paper towels and set them aside.

Creating the Creamy Tahini Sauce While the falafel is frying, let's move on to making the creamy tahini sauce, a perfect accompaniment to our crispy falafel.

Blend the ingredients: In a blender or food processor, combine tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, and honey. Process until smooth and creamy.

Adjust the consistency: If the sauce is too thick, add a little water. If it's too thin, add a bit more tahini. Season the sauce with salt to taste.

Assembly and Serving Now that we have both our falafel and tahini sauce ready, it's time to put them together.

Serve with falafel: Place a few falafel on each pita or flatbread. Drizzle the creamy tahini sauce over the falafel.

Add the finishing touches: Top with chopped parsley, pickled turnips or cucumbers, and a sprinkle of sumac, if desired.

With these simple steps, you too can create delicious homemade falafel paired with a rich tahini sauce. This flavorful combination is sure to become a staple in your kitchen, perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a weekend gathering with friends and family. Tips and Variations For a crisper exterior and a fluffier interior, be sure to not over-process the falafel mixture. Adding a bit of aquafaba (the liquid from canned chickpeas) can also help create a lighter falafel. Experiment with different spices and herbs to give your falafel a unique flavor profile. Some options include:

Dried oregano and thyme for a Mediterranean twist Ground cayenne pepper for a spicy kick Grated ginger for an Asian-inspired flavor

Feel free to customize the tahini sauce by adding a squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a pinch of cumin to suit your taste. Conclusion Making homemade falafel with creamy tahini sauce is a rewarding experience that brings people together. From soaking the chickpeas to assembling the final dish, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the delicious flavors of the Middle East in the comfort of your own home.
