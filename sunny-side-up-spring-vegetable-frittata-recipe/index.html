Sunny Side Up: Spring Vegetable Frittata Recipe


If you’re looking for a delightful way to welcome the vibrant flavors of spring into your kitchen, a sunny side up spring vegetable frittata is the perfect dish to try. This versatile and easy-to-make recipe allows you to showcase the best produce of the season while creating a satisfying and visually appealing meal. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, preparing a quick weeknight dinner, or simply craving a taste of spring, this frittata is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as parsley or chives) for garnish

Instructions

Preparing the Vegetables

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.

Cooking the Vegetables

  1. In a 10-inch oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced red onion and sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the asparagus to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender-crisp.
  3. Stir in the cherry tomatoes and cook for 1-2 minutes, just until they begin to soften.
  4. Add the fresh spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, about 1-2 minutes.

Assembling the Frittata

  1. Once the vegetables are cooked, pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet.
  2. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese evenly over the top of the frittata.
  3. Let the mixture cook undisturbed on the stovetop for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges begin to set.

Finishing in the Oven

  1. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the center and the edges are golden brown.
  2. Carefully remove the skillet from the oven and let the frittata cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  3. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve warm.

Serving Suggestions

This sunny side up spring vegetable frittata is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, lunch, or dinner. Serve it with a side of mixed greens dressed in a light vinaigrette for a refreshing and balanced meal. Alternatively, pair it with crusty bread, fresh fruit, or roasted potatoes for a heartier option. The frittata can also be made ahead of time and served at room temperature, making it a perfect option for picnics or packed lunches.

Conclusion

Embrace the essence of spring with this delightful sunny side up spring vegetable frittata. Its vibrant colors, fresh flavors, and ease of preparation make it a standout dish for any occasion. Experiment with your favorite seasonal vegetables, herbs, and cheeses to create a frittata that suits your taste. This recipe is not only a celebration of spring’s bounty but also a testament to the joy of simple, wholesome cooking. Enjoy the process of preparing and sharing this frittata with your loved ones, and savor the delightful flavors of the season.
