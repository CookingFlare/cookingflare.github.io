Top 5 Holiday Pie Recipes Your Guests Will Love


The holiday season is upon us, and what's a celebration without an assortment of delicious pies? From classic fruits like apple and cherry to creamy custard and nutty all-spice, there's a pie to please every palate. In this post, we'll explore the top 5 holiday pie recipes that are sure to impress your guests and become a new family favorite. Recipe 1: Classic Apple Pie with a Twist There's no denying the allure of a traditional apple pie. But, why settle for the ordinary when you can elevate it to new heights? Our version of classic apple pie incorporates a surprisingly scrumptious addition: lemon verbena leaves. These fragrant leaves add an unexpected brightness to the traditional pie filling, creating a delightful interplay of flavors. Ingredients:

2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1 tsp salt 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter 1/2 cup ice water 8-10 medium apples, peeled and sliced 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1 tsp ground nutmeg 1 tsp lemon verbena leaves, chopped 1/4 cup granulated sugar

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add butter and mix until crumbly. Gradually pours ice water, mixing until dough forms. Roll out the dough to a thickness of 1/8 inch, placing it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the dough with the sliced apples, mixing in cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon verbena leaves. Cover with a top crust or crumb topping and bake for 45-50 minutes.

Recipe 2: Cherry Pie with a Hint of Almond Cherry pie is a timeless holiday classic. Our version packs an enticing twist: a delicate almond flavor. The essence of almonds subtly complements the tart cherry filling, generating a charming combination that elevates this beloved dessert to new heights. Ingredients:

2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1 tsp salt 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter 1/2 cup ice water 2 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted 1 tsp almond extract 1 tsp grated lemon zest 1/4 cup granulated sugar

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add butter and mix until crumbly. Gradually pours ice water, mixing until dough forms. Roll out the dough to a thickness of 1/8 inch, placing it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the dough with the pitted cherries, mixing in almond extract, lemon zest, and granulated sugar. Cover with a top crust or crumb topping and bake for 40-45 minutes.

Recipe 3: Pumpkin Pie with Nutmeg Whipped Cream For a delightful departure from fruit-based pies, this creamy pumpkin pie showcases the depth of spices in a sweet and rich dessert. Paired with nutmeg-infused whipped cream, it's sure to become your guests' favorite at this year's holiday gathering. Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups cooked, mashed pumpkin 1 1/2 cups heavy cream 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg 1/4 tsp salt Whipped cream, flavored with ground nutmeg

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). In a blender or food processor, combine pumpkin, heavy cream, granulated sugar, eggs, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Blend until fully combined, then pour the mixture into a 9-inch pie dish. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce oven temperature to 350°F (180°C) and continue baking for an additional 30-40 minutes.

Recipe 4: Chocolate Pecan Pie Apollo's flagship treat is a no-brainer for a sophisticated crowd – rich, velvety chocolate, drizzled with an easy pecan filling. Completing this chocolate pecan pie experience is a simple almond extract, sending this tantalizing holiday dessert straight to stardom. Ingredients:

1 cup packed light brown sugar 1/2 cup melted unsalted butter 1/2 cup heavy cream 2 tsp pure vanilla 4 large eggs 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips 1 cup chopped pecans 1 tsp almond extract

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). In a medium bowl, combine brown sugar, melted butter, heavy cream, and vanilla extract. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until light and fluffy. Melt chocolate chips over low heat or using a double boiler, then whisk in chopped pecans and almond extract. Pour mixture into a 9-inch pie dish, baking for 35-40 minutes.

By experimenting with an injection of personal taste and creativity, our holiday pie recipes are sure to foster unbreakable bonds among the family during your next festive gathering.
