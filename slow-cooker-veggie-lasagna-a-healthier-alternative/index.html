Slow Cooker Veggie Lasagna: A Healthier Alternative


Are you tired of the same old lasagna recipe that's heavy on the meat and cheese? Look no further! This slow cooker veggie lasagna recipe is a game-changer for anyone looking for a healthier alternative to the classic Italian dish. The Benefits of a Veggie Lasagna Not only is this recipe a healthier option, but it's also a great way to get your daily dose of vegetables. By using a variety of colorful vegetables, you'll be adding a boost of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants to your meal. Plus, by using a slow cooker, you'll be cooking with minimal oil and no added salt. The Recipe Ingredients:

1 cup of lasagna noodles 2 cups of marinara sauce 1 cup of ricotta cheese 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese 1 cup of frozen spinach, thawed and drained 1 cup of sliced mushrooms 1 cup of sliced bell peppers 1 cup of sliced zucchini 1 small onion, chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 cup of chopped fresh basil Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. In a large skillet, sauté the mushrooms, bell peppers, and zucchini in a little bit of olive oil until they're tender. Add the chopped onion and cook until it's translucent. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese. Mix well to combine. In the slow cooker, create a layer of marinara sauce on the bottom. Arrange 4 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce. Spread half of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by half of the sautéed vegetables. Repeat the layers, starting with the marinara sauce, noodles, ricotta cheese mixture, and sautéed vegetables. Top the final layer of noodles with the remaining marinara sauce and sprinkle with mozzarella cheese. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 3-4 hours or high for 1-2 hours. Remove the lid and sprinkle with chopped fresh basil. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

To add some extra flavor, you can add a sprinkle of dried oregano or basil to the ricotta cheese mixture. If you prefer a creamier sauce, you can add a tablespoon or two of heavy cream or half-and-half to the marinara sauce. To make this recipe more substantial, you can add some cooked ground turkey or beef to the sautéed vegetables. If you don't have a slow cooker, you can make this recipe in the oven. Simply assemble the lasagna in a baking dish and bake at 375°F for 30-40 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Nutrition Information Per serving (serves 6-8):

Calories: 350 Fat: 15g Saturated fat: 8g Cholesterol: 30mg Sodium: 400mg Carbohydrates: 40g Fiber: 5g Sugar: 10g Protein: 20g

Conclusion This slow cooker veggie lasagna recipe is a healthier alternative to the classic Italian dish. By using a variety of colorful vegetables and a slow cooker, you'll be cooking with minimal oil and no added salt. This recipe is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the benefits of a healthier lasagna!
