Paleo Chicken Tenders Recipes: 5 Grain-Free and Delicious Options


Are you following a paleo diet and craving some crispy, flavorful chicken tenders? Look no further! In this article, we will explore five grain-free and delicious options for paleo chicken tenders. These recipes are not only healthy and nutritious but also incredibly tasty. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Almond-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix together almond flour, garlic powder, paprika, sea salt, and black pepper.
  3. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, then coat it in the almond flour mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce or alongside a salad for a complete meal.

2. Coconut-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 1 cup shredded coconut (unsweetened)
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix together shredded coconut, almond flour, garlic powder, sea salt, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, then coat it in the coconut mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Enjoy these delightful chicken tenders with a tangy pineapple salsa for a tropical twist!

3. Cassava-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 1 cup cassava flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix together cassava flour, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, sea salt, and black pepper.
  3. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, then coat it in the cassava flour mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serve these cassava-crusted chicken tenders with a creamy avocado dip for a delightful flavor combination.

4. Pecan-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 1 cup pecans, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix together chopped pecans, coconut flour, garlic powder, dried thyme, sea salt, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, then coat it in the pecan mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. These pecan-crusted chicken tenders pair perfectly with a tangy honey mustard dipping sauce.

5. Zucchini-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 2 cups shredded zucchini
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the shredded zucchini in a clean kitchen towel and squeeze out any excess moisture.
  3. In a shallow dish, mix together almond flour, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, dried oregano, sea salt, and black pepper.
  4. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, then press it into the zucchini to coat, and finally coat it in the almond flour mixture.
  5. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve these zucchini-crusted chicken tenders with a creamy ranch dipping sauce for a refreshing twist.

And there you have it – five grain-free and delicious options for paleo chicken tenders. Whether you prefer the nuttiness of almonds or the tropical sweetness of coconut, there’s a recipe here to satisfy your cravings. So, go ahead and give these recipes a try. Your taste buds will thank you! Happy cooking!
