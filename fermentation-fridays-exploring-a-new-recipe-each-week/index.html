Fermentation Fridays: Exploring a New Recipe Each Week


Hello, fellow food enthusiasts! Welcome to another exhilarating edition of Fermentation Fridays, where we explore the bustling world of fermented foods. This week, we’re diving into an extraordinary and mouth-watering recipe that is bound to bolster your culinary horizons. So, put on your aprons, and let’s embark on this scrumptious journey together.

Our featured recipe for this week is the delightful and gut-friendly:

Sourdough Baguette

Before we delve into the intriguing world of sourdough-making, let’s first grasp its essence. Sourdough fermentation is a natural process that instills a striking tang to bread, rendering it easier to digest and positively influencing your gut bacteria. Now, are you ready to discover the enchanting realm of sourdough baguette?

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 100g active sourdough starter
  • 350ml room-temperature water
  • 10g salt

Instructions:

  1. First things first: ensure your sourdough starter is active and robust. If you lack a starter, fret not! Brewers out there often create their feisty starters through a process called “sourdough emergency kit.” But we won’t delve into that now.
  2. In a sizable mixing bowl, combine the flour, water, and sourdough starter thoroughly. Remember to ensure your room-temperature water – no shortcuts here!
  3. Once the dough is well-combined, gradually add the salt, kneading well to integrate it thoroughly.
  4. Transfer the dough to a well-floured surface and knead for five minutes. You’ll notice the dough becoming more pliable and shiny. That’s the power of fermentation at play!
  5. Place the dough in a lightly floured container and cover with plastic wrap. Permit your bread to ferment and earn its admirable tang. You’ll require 1-4 hours at room temperature, depending on how sour you’d like your bread. Trust your instincts!
  6. After fermentation, carefully remove the dough from the container. Divide it into equal parts to form your baguettes. They should be as long and thin as your heart desires. Patience, friends; patience is key.
  7. Allow the shaped baguettes to rest for another 40 minutes, tucked snugly in a lightly floured space.
  8. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). While it heats, prepare your baguettes for their final journey. Make those classic, jagged slashes with a bread lame or a serrated knife. Remember to score diagonally, not horizontally.
  9. Place a baking stone or an upside-down baking sheet in the oven to serve as a “baking surface.” Give it ample time to heat up while you’re preheating.
  10. Flour your baguettes, and have them meet their destiny atop the hot baking surface. Pour yourself a glass of your favorite brew as you listen to the delicious sounds of crust formation.
  11. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until your baguette is splendidly golden brown. Feel free to slide a few herbs under that carefully crafted crust for extra flavor.
  12. Promptly remove your tempting sourdough baguette from its scorching abode. Leaden your worthy creation to the realm of cool down before slicing, so the baguette holds its structural integrity.
  13. Lastly, revel in the joy of sharing your freshly baked sourdough baguette with friends and family. Bask in their praises, and remember – it’s all thanks to Fermentation Fridays!

Time to bid farewell to this week’s edition of Fermentation Fridays. We hope you’ve enjoyed this scrumptious journey into the realm of sourdough baguette creation. Remember, every glorious loaf of bread starts with love – so, keep on fermenting, baking, and loving! Until next week, bon appétit!
