Korean Samgyeopsal (Grilled Pork Belly) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, unique textures, and mouthwatering combinations, and one of its most iconic dishes is Samgyeopsal—grilled pork belly. This dish has captured the hearts of food lovers worldwide with its simplicity, rich flavor, and interactive dining experience. Whether you’re new to Korean BBQ or looking to recreate this restaurant favorite at home, this guide will walk you through the steps to make Samgyeopsal from start to finish.

What is Samgyeopsal?

Samgyeopsal (삼겹살) is a popular Korean BBQ dish that consists of thick slices of pork belly, which are grilled at the table and typically eaten with a variety of accompaniments such as fresh vegetables, sauces, and banchan (side dishes). The name “Samgyeopsal” literally translates to “three-layered flesh,” referring to the layers of meat and fat in the pork belly that create a tender and juicy bite when grilled.

This dish is usually cooked at the table on a grill, allowing diners to enjoy the cooking process and interact with their food. It’s served with a range of dipping sauces, sides, and vegetables, making it a fun, flavorful, and customizable meal for gatherings, parties, or casual dinners.

Ingredients for Samgyeopsal

The beauty of Samgyeopsal lies in its simplicity. To make this delicious dish at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pork Belly:

  • Pork belly slices – You can usually find pork belly at most butcher shops or Asian grocery stores. Look for thick slices with visible layers of fat and meat. Aim for about 1-1.5 inches thick, as this will give you the ideal texture when grilled.
  • Sesame oil (optional) – A small amount of sesame oil can be used to brush the pork belly before grilling to enhance flavor.

For the Dipping Sauce (Samjang):

  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste) – Adds a spicy and savory kick to the sauce.
  • Doenjang (Korean soybean paste) – Provides a rich umami flavor.
  • Garlic – Minced or grated garlic adds a nice depth to the sauce.
  • Sesame oil – Adds a nutty and aromatic flavor.
  • Honey or sugar – For a touch of sweetness.
  • Rice vinegar or lemon juice – To balance the flavors and provide a bit of acidity.
  • Salt – To taste.

For the Accompaniments:

  • Lettuce leaves – Typically, sangchu (a variety of Korean lettuce) or perilla leaves are used. These will serve as wrappers for the pork belly.
  • Garlic cloves – Fresh garlic is often grilled along with the pork belly, and slices are used in wraps.
  • Green peppers – Mildly spicy Korean green peppers (or any fresh pepper) are often served for additional flavor.
  • Kimchi – A must-have side for any Korean meal. Its sour, spicy, and fermented flavor complements the richness of the pork belly.
  • Steamed rice – A simple side of plain steamed rice rounds out the meal.
  • Pickled radish – A common side dish to cut through the richness of the pork.

How to Prepare Samgyeopsal

While Samgyeopsal is incredibly simple to prepare, there are a few steps you’ll want to follow to ensure the perfect result. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how to prepare this Korean BBQ dish at home.

Step 1: Prepare the Pork Belly

Start by selecting fresh and high-quality pork belly slices. When you buy pork belly, aim for well-marbled slices, as the fat is key to getting that tender, juicy texture after grilling.

  1. Slice the Pork Belly: If your pork belly slices aren’t already cut into thick strips, ask your butcher to do it, or cut it yourself into 1-1.5 inch thick slices. The goal is to have pieces that are thick enough to maintain their texture while grilling, but thin enough to cook quickly on the grill.
  2. Optional Marinate: Although Samgyeopsal is often served without marination to showcase the pork’s natural flavors, you can lightly season the pork belly with some sesame oil, salt, and pepper if you prefer.

Step 2: Prepare the Samjang Dipping Sauce

A key component of Samgyeopsal is the accompanying Samjang sauce. This thick, spicy paste adds a rich, flavorful kick to each bite of grilled pork. Here’s how to make your own Samjang sauce:

  1. In a small bowl, combine about 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean chili paste) with 1 tablespoon of doenjang (Korean soybean paste).
  2. Add 1 minced garlic clove, 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar, and a dash of rice vinegar or lemon juice for acidity.
  3. Mix all the ingredients until smooth. Taste and adjust the seasoning according to your preferences, adding more honey for sweetness or gochujang for extra heat.

This homemade Samjang will provide the perfect balance of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors that complement the richness of the pork belly.

Step 3: Prepare the Vegetables and Accompaniments

While the pork is the star of the show, the accompaniments are what elevate this dish and make it so enjoyable. Here are the key accompaniments you’ll need to prepare:

  1. Lettuce and Perilla Leaves: Wash and dry a few leaves of lettuce and perilla (wild sesame) leaves. These leaves are essential for wrapping the grilled pork belly along with the garlic, green peppers, and dipping sauce.
  2. Garlic: Slice a few cloves of garlic thinly. Garlic adds a wonderful kick and fragrance when grilled.
  3. Green Peppers: Slice some fresh Korean green peppers or any mild chili peppers you prefer. These can be eaten as-is or grilled alongside the pork belly.
  4. Kimchi: Serve a side of kimchi for an authentic experience. You can use any type of kimchi you like, but the traditional napa cabbage kimchi works best with Samgyeopsal.
  5. Pickled Radish: Sweet and tangy pickled radish is the perfect contrast to the fatty pork belly and can be served alongside for added texture and flavor.

Step 4: Grilling the Pork Belly

The grilling process is an essential part of Samgyeopsal, and it’s what makes the dish interactive and fun. You can grill the pork belly on a table-top grill, a cast iron pan, or even an outdoor BBQ grill.

  1. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a tabletop grill, ensure it’s properly preheated so the pork belly cooks evenly.
  2. Grill the Pork Belly: Place the pork belly slices on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until the pork is golden brown and crispy on the outside. The fat should render down and become crispy while the meat remains tender and juicy.
  3. Grill Garlic and Green Peppers: While the pork belly is grilling, add a few slices of garlic and whole green peppers to the grill. The garlic will roast and become sweet, while the peppers will soften slightly and develop a smoky flavor.

Step 5: Assembling the Wraps

Once the pork belly is grilled to perfection, it’s time to assemble your Samgyeopsal wraps. This part is what makes Samgyeopsal such an enjoyable and customizable experience.

  1. Grab a Leaf: Start by taking a lettuce or perilla leaf and placing it in your hand.
  2. Add Pork Belly: Place a piece of grilled pork belly on the leaf. Add a slice of grilled garlic, a green pepper, and a generous dollop of Samjang sauce.
  3. Wrap and Eat: Wrap the leaf around the ingredients to form a little packet, and pop the whole wrap in your mouth. The combination of the fatty, juicy pork, crunchy garlic, and spicy sauce is absolutely delicious.

Step 6: Enjoy the Meal

Samgyeopsal is best enjoyed with good company and plenty of laughter. The communal nature of Korean BBQ means that everyone can grill their own meat and customize their wraps with different combinations of sides and sauces.

Serve the Samgyeopsal alongside bowls of steamed rice, kimchi, and other banchan, and enjoy a truly authentic Korean BBQ experience in the comfort of your home.

Tips for the Perfect Samgyeopsal

  1. Use Fresh Pork Belly: The quality of the pork belly is crucial for getting that tender, juicy texture. Always choose fresh pork belly with a good balance of fat and meat.
  2. Grill at the Right Temperature: Make sure your grill is preheated to medium-high heat. Cooking at the right temperature ensures the pork belly gets crispy on the outside while remaining tender and juicy on the inside.
  3. Don’t Overcrowd the Grill: If you’re grilling a lot of pork belly, cook it in batches to ensure each piece gets enough space to cook evenly. Overcrowding the grill can lead to uneven cooking.
  4. Let the Pork Rest: After grilling, let the pork belly rest for a minute or two before assembling the wraps. This helps retain the juices and enhances the flavor.
  5. Experiment with Sides: While lettuce and kimchi are traditional, feel free to get creative with the sides. Add some sliced onions, grilled mushrooms, or even a fried egg for a more filling meal.

Conclusion

Making Korean Samgyeopsal at home is a fun and delicious experience that allows you to enjoy the flavors of Korea without leaving your kitchen. By following these steps and using fresh ingredients, you can easily recreate this popular dish and enjoy a delicious Korean BBQ meal in the comfort of your home. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, family, or friends, Samgyeopsal is sure to be a crowd-pleaser every time. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and enjoy the flavors of this iconic Korean dish!
