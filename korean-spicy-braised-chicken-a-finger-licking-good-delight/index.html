Korean Spicy Braised Chicken: A Finger-Licking Good Delight


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine and crave a dish that packs a punch of flavor, then look no further than Korean Spicy Braised Chicken. This dish is a tantalizing combination of succulent chicken pieces bathed in a rich and spicy sauce that will leave you wanting more. With its perfect balance of sweetness, spiciness, and umami, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken pieces (thighs or drumsticks)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (or mirin)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Cook until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant, about 3 minutes.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, soy sauce, honey, rice wine, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce into the pot with the onions, garlic, and ginger. Stir well to combine.

  4. Return the chicken pieces to the pot, ensuring they are fully coated in the spicy sauce. Add the chicken broth and water, then bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the chicken to simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the meat is cooked through and tender.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside. Increase the heat to medium-high and cook the sauce for an additional 5 minutes, or until it thickens slightly.

  7. While the sauce is simmering, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces using two forks. Once the sauce has thickened, return the shredded chicken to the pot and stir well to coat it in the delicious sauce.

  8. Serve the Korean Spicy Braised Chicken over steamed rice, garnished with chopped green onions and sesame seeds. The combination of flavors and textures will leave you craving for more.

Tips

  • If you prefer a spicier dish, feel free to increase the amount of gochujang according to your taste.
  • For a complete meal, serve the Korean Spicy Braised Chicken with a side of steamed vegetables or pickled radishes.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Simply reheat in a microwave or on the stovetop before enjoying.

So there you have it – a recipe for Korean Spicy Braised Chicken that will surely become a staple in your home. Its bold flavors and tender meat make it a true finger-licking delight. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or simply looking to explore new flavors, this dish is sure to satisfy your taste buds. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure with this Korean classic. Enjoy!
