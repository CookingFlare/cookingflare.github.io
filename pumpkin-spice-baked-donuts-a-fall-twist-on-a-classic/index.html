Pumpkin Spice Baked Donuts: A Fall Twist on a Classic


As the leaves start to change color and the air becomes crisp, it’s time to embrace all things fall, including the delicious flavors that come with it. One of the most iconic flavors of the season is pumpkin spice, and what better way to enjoy it than with a batch of homemade pumpkin spice baked donuts? These delightful treats are a twist on the classic donut, offering a healthier alternative with all the warmth and comfort of traditional fall flavors.

Ingredients

For the donuts:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup canned pumpkin
  • 1/2 cup milk

For the glaze:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3-4 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a donut pan with non-stick cooking spray.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves.

  3. In a separate large bowl, whisk together the vegetable oil, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until well combined.

  4. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract, canned pumpkin, and milk until smooth.

  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.

  6. Spoon the batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about 2/3 full.

  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until the donuts spring back when lightly touched. Allow the donuts to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. While the donuts are cooling, prepare the glaze. In a medium bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  9. Once the donuts are completely cooled, dip the tops of each donut into the glaze, allowing any excess glaze to drip off. Place the glazed donuts back on the wire rack to set.

  10. Serve and enjoy your homemade pumpkin spice baked donuts with a hot cup of coffee or cider for the perfect fall treat!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent treat, sprinkle chopped pecans or walnuts on top of the glaze before it sets.
  • If you prefer a denser texture, substitute the milk with buttermilk for a richer flavor.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of spices to suit your taste preferences, adding more or less of each spice to customize the flavor profile of the donuts.

Final Thoughts

Pumpkin spice baked donuts are a delightful way to welcome the autumn season and enjoy the comforting flavors of fall. Whether you’re hosting a cozy brunch or simply craving a sweet treat, these donuts are sure to impress with their warm spices and tender texture. Embrace the spirit of fall and indulge in the irresistible aroma of pumpkin spice with this delightful twist on a classic favorite. Happy baking!
