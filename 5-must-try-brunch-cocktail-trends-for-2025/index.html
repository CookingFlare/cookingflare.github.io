5 Must-Try Brunch Cocktail Trends for 2025


Brunch is more than just a late breakfast or early lunch; it’s a celebration of the weekend, an opportunity to relax, unwind, and enjoy delicious food paired with delightful beverages. When it comes to brunch cocktails, the possibilities are endless. But what makes a brunch cocktail truly stand out in 2025? Whether you’re a seasoned mixologist or a brunch enthusiast looking to elevate your next gathering, these five must-try brunch cocktail trends for 2025 are sure to impress your guests and add an exciting twist to your brunch spread.

1. Low-Alcohol Cocktails with Big Flavor

As health-conscious choices continue to trend, many people are looking to indulge in cocktails without overdoing it on alcohol. Enter the world of low-alcohol cocktails—drinks that are just as flavorful but with a lighter alcoholic punch. These cocktails allow brunch-goers to sip leisurely and enjoy a more sustainable drinking experience.

The Rise of Light Spirits

Low-alcohol spirits like vermouth, Aperol, and sherry have been steadily gaining popularity. These spirits bring bold flavors to the table without the intense effects of stronger liquors. Instead of heavy, boozy concoctions, low-alcohol cocktails embrace fresh herbs, citrus, and botanicals to create complex, refreshing flavors.

For a simple yet tasty low-alcohol brunch cocktail, try the Aperol Spritz. This vibrant drink is a perfect balance of bitterness and sweetness, with Aperol mixed with prosecco and a splash of soda water. It’s light, refreshing, and ideal for daytime sipping.

What Makes Low-Alcohol Cocktails Perfect for Brunch?

The key appeal of these drinks is their balance of taste and moderation. They allow people to enjoy a drink that enhances the brunch experience without overwhelming their senses. Plus, many of these drinks are beautifully garnished with citrus wedges, fresh herbs, or edible flowers, adding visual appeal to your brunch setup.

2. Botanical Cocktails and Herbal Infusions

Herbs and botanicals have always played a role in cocktail culture, but in 2025, they’re taking center stage. From floral notes to earthy flavors, the world of botanicals is vast and versatile. Whether it’s a fresh herb like basil or a more complex infusion of lavender or rosemary, botanical cocktails add a sophisticated edge to your brunch menu.

Herbal Spirits and Homemade Infusions

In 2025, we’ll see more homemade herb-infused syrups, bitters, and garnishes making their way into the world of brunch cocktails. You can easily infuse spirits like gin, vodka, or rum with herbs like thyme, mint, or sage. The process is simple and gives your cocktails a fresh, homemade flair that guests will appreciate.

One drink that is perfect for this trend is the Lavender Collins. This cocktail features lavender syrup, gin, lemon juice, and soda water, creating a refreshing yet aromatic drink that’s ideal for any brunch table.

Why Herbal Cocktails Are on Trend

Botanical ingredients like lavender, rosemary, and thyme aren’t just for culinary purposes; they add depth and flavor to cocktails, creating a balanced experience between savory and sweet. These drinks are perfect for brunch because they pair beautifully with light dishes like scrambled eggs, avocado toast, or fresh salads. Plus, they evoke a sense of tranquility and elegance—just what you need when relaxing with friends or family.

3. Fruit-Forward Cocktails with Exotic Flavors

As exotic fruits continue to influence culinary trends, brunch cocktails are no exception. In 2025, expect to see more tropical and lesser-known fruits making an appearance in your cocktails. Think beyond the usual citrus fruits—bring in the tang of passion fruit, the sweetness of guava, or the exotic flavor of dragon fruit.

The Tropical Twist

Fruit-forward cocktails are perfect for brunch because they offer vibrant colors, refreshing flavors, and an overall uplifting experience. Instead of a traditional mimosa, why not try a Guava Fizz? This cocktail combines fresh guava juice, gin, lime, and a splash of soda water for a lively and zesty drink.

Other fruits to explore in your brunch cocktails include:

  • Mango – Add a tropical punch with fresh mango purée or juice.
  • Pineapple – For a sweet, tart twist, pineapple pairs perfectly with rum or tequila.
  • Lychee – This sweet and floral fruit adds an exotic, refreshing touch to cocktails.

Why Fruit-Forward Cocktails Will Steal the Spotlight

Exotic fruits bring both flavor and flair to brunch cocktails. Not only do they elevate the visual appeal of your drinks, but they also add a burst of flavor that pairs perfectly with classic brunch dishes like French toast, pancakes, and fresh fruit salads. The tropical sweetness balances out any sharpness from alcohol, making it a delightful drink for leisurely sipping.

4. Brunch-Inspired Classics with a Modern Twist

As much as we love experimenting with new ingredients, there’s something comforting about classic brunch cocktails. In 2025, expect a resurgence of iconic drinks like the Bloody Mary, Mimosa, and Bellini—but with innovative twists that elevate them beyond their traditional forms.

A New Take on Old Favorites

  • Smoked Bloody Mary: Smoky flavors are a big trend in cocktails, and incorporating them into a Bloody Mary adds an exciting depth. Infuse your tomato juice with smoky paprika or use smoked salt to rim your glass. You can also incorporate smoked vodka for an even richer flavor.

  • Citrus Mimosa: While the classic mimosa typically uses orange juice, try using a blend of grapefruit, blood orange, or even pomegranate juice for a more vibrant and unique take. This adds a touch of sophistication while maintaining the refreshing essence of the traditional mimosa.

  • Frozen Bellini: While the Bellini is a classic combination of prosecco and peach purée, blending these ingredients with ice creates a fun frozen variation. It’s a playful twist that’s perfect for warm-weather brunches.

Why Classic Cocktails Will Always Be in Style

While trends come and go, the timeless nature of these cocktails ensures they’ll always have a place on the brunch table. The key is to update them with unique ingredients, garnishes, or presentation styles to keep them relevant in 2025. Whether it’s an elegant Bloody Mary or a frosty Bellini, these updated classics will never go out of style.

5. Non-Alcoholic Brunch Cocktails for All

Non-alcoholic cocktails, or mocktails, have seen a sharp rise in popularity over the past few years, and 2025 is no exception. As more people seek to reduce their alcohol consumption or simply enjoy a drink without the buzz, non-alcoholic cocktails are being crafted to offer all the flavor and none of the alcohol.

The Rise of Non-Alcoholic Spirits

Non-alcoholic spirits are becoming more sophisticated, offering the complexity and depth of their alcoholic counterparts. Whether it’s non-alcoholic gin, rum, or whiskey, these spirits allow bartenders to craft drinks that are just as exciting as their boozy versions.

A great non-alcoholic cocktail to try at brunch is the Cucumber Mint Cooler. With non-alcoholic gin, cucumber, mint, and lime, this refreshing drink is both hydrating and flavorful. Another great option is a Virgin Mojito, made with fresh mint, lime, and soda water, creating a light and invigorating drink that’s perfect for daytime sipping.

Why Non-Alcoholic Cocktails Are Here to Stay

With an increasing focus on wellness and mindfulness, non-alcoholic cocktails are perfect for those who want to enjoy the social experience of brunch without consuming alcohol. These drinks can still offer bold flavors, beautiful presentation, and a sense of occasion, making them a fantastic option for guests who prefer to skip the alcohol but still want to be part of the festivities.

Conclusion

2025 promises to be an exciting year for brunch cocktails. From low-alcohol options to botanical infusions, fruit-forward concoctions, revamped classics, and non-alcoholic beverages, there’s a cocktail trend for everyone. The key to success in brunch cocktail crafting is balance—ensuring the flavors complement each other and enhance the overall brunch experience. Whether you’re hosting a Sunday brunch or simply enjoying a quiet morning with friends, these trends will ensure that your cocktails stand out and elevate your gathering to the next level. Cheers to a flavorful and refreshing 2025!
