Instant Pot Korean BBQ Beef: A Tasty, Authentic Recipe


Korean cuisine is celebrated worldwide for its bold flavors, vibrant dishes, and comforting appeal. Among its most beloved staples is Korean BBQ beef, a dish that perfectly balances sweet, savory, and smoky flavors. Traditionally prepared on an outdoor grill or stovetop, this recipe can be easily adapted for the Instant Pot, preserving all the authentic tastes while cutting down on cooking time. If you’ve been searching for a way to recreate the irresistible experience of Korean BBQ at home, this recipe will undoubtedly hit the mark.

Why Choose the Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot has revolutionized home cooking by speeding up processes that usually take hours. It’s perfect for Korean BBQ beef because the pressure-cooking function tenderizes the meat, allowing the liquid marinade to deeply infuse it with flavor. Plus, the sauté mode lets you sear the beef before pressure cooking, adding a beautiful caramelized crust that enhances its taste. With the Instant Pot, prepping homemade Korean BBQ becomes a much easier feat, perfect for anyone juggling a busy schedule but still craving delicious home-cooked meals.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Korean BBQ Beef

To create authentic Korean BBQ beef, the marinade is key. Using traditional ingredients ensures the dish reflects the true flavors of Korean cuisine. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Beef:

  • 2 pounds of beef sirloin, flank steak, or short ribs (thinly sliced or cut into bite-sized pieces)

For the Marinade:

  • ½ cup soy sauce (preferably low-sodium)
  • ⅓ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 medium-sized Asian pear, grated (or substitute with a ripe pear or apple)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste; adjust for spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes; optional for extra heat)
  • 1 teaspoon white miso paste (optional for extra depth)
  • 2 green onions, chopped

For Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds (toasted)
  • Additional green onions (thinly sliced)
  • Optional: Fresh cilantro

Optional Sides:

Korean BBQ beef is often served alongside various sides, such as steamed jasmine rice, kimchi, pickled radish, or a simple cucumber salad.

Preparation Steps

Achieving restaurant-quality Korean BBQ beef starts with properly preparing your ingredients and following each step with care. Here’s how to make this dish from start to finish:

1. Prepare the Marinade

Start by combining all the marinade ingredients in a mixing bowl. Whisk them together until the brown sugar dissolves and everything is well incorporated. The grated Asian pear adds natural sweetness and tenderizes the beef, while the ginger and garlic infuse the marinade with aromatic depth.

Taste-test your marinade and adjust any seasoning as needed. If you want a slightly sweeter profile, add more brown sugar; to increase the savory notes, add a bit more soy sauce.

2. Marinate the Beef

Place the sliced beef into a large resealable bag or container. Pour the marinade over the beef, ensuring every piece is well coated. Seal tightly and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. For best results, marinate the beef overnight; the longer it sits, the more flavorful and tender it will become.

Cooking the Beef in the Instant Pot

3. Sear the Beef (Optional but Recommended)

Before pressure cooking, take advantage of the Instant Pot’s sauté function to sear the beef. This step adds a layer of caramelization that enhances the flavor of the dish.

  • Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode.
  • Add a drizzle of sesame oil to the pot and let it heat up.
  • Remove the beef from the marinade (save the marinade for later) and place a few pieces at a time into the pot; try not to overcrowd.
  • Sear the beef for 2–3 minutes per side until lightly browned. Remove each batch and set aside.

4. Pressure Cook the Beef

Once all the beef is seared, switch off the sauté function. Place the seared beef back into the pot and pour the reserved marinade on top.

  • Add ¼ cup of water or beef broth to prevent sticking.
  • Lock the Instant Pot lid and set it to manual high pressure for 20 minutes.

After the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for about 10 minutes before performing a quick release. This ensures the juices remain locked inside the meat.

Finishing Touches

5. Reduce the Sauce

Once the beef is tender and cooked to perfection, remove it from the Instant Pot and place it on a serving platter. Turn the Instant Pot back to “Sauté” mode and simmer the remaining sauce for 5–7 minutes to reduce it into a glaze-like consistency.

Pour this luscious sauce over the beef before serving. It’s what makes this dish shine with glossy, intensified flavor.

6. Garnish

Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over the top for a nutty crunch and vibrant green onions for a fresh punch. Optionally, garnish with chopped cilantro if you love a hint of herbal brightness.

Serving Suggestions

Korean BBQ beef is incredibly versatile. This dish is traditionally served with steamed rice and an array of banchan (Korean side dishes) such as kimchi, pickled veggies, or sautéed spinach. If you prefer a lower-carb option, serve it over a bed of broccoli or cauliflower rice. You can also use the beef as a filling for lettuce wraps—simply place a spoonful of beef into a crisp leaf of romaine, butter lettuce, or perilla, and add fresh veggies for crunch.

For gatherings, consider serving this Instant Pot Korean BBQ beef family-style. Pair it with Korean-style pancakes (pajeon) or sweet potato noodles (japchae) for a complete feast!

Tips for Perfect Korean BBQ Beef

  1. Choose the Right Cut of Beef: Thinly sliced sirloin or flank steak works best for this recipe. If you prefer a richer flavor, use short ribs but ensure they’re sliced for quicker cooking.

  2. Don’t Skip the Asian Pear: While you can substitute it, an authentic Korean marinade almost always contains Asian pear. Its enzymes help break down the meat fibers while contributing to the sweet and tangy profile.

  3. Watch the Cooking Time: Pressure cooking is quick and effective, but overcooking may dry out the beef. Stick to the recommended times to retain tenderness.

  4. Adjust the Heat: If you’re sensitive to spice, reduce the gochujang and eliminate the gochugaru. On the other hand, spice lovers can add red pepper flakes or extra chili paste for an extra kick.

  5. Double the Sauce: If you love extra sauce to drizzle over rice or veggies, make a double batch of the marinade. Simmer the excess sauce into a reduction after cooking.

Why This Recipe Works

This Instant Pot Korean BBQ beef recipe simplifies preparation without compromising authenticity. By pressure cooking, the marinade embeds itself deeply into the meat, giving you a dish that’s bursting with flavor in a fraction of the time. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just treating yourself to a weeknight indulgence, this recipe delivers the perfect balance of sweetness, spice, and umami.

If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, this recipe will become a regular in your rotation. And if you’re new to the Korean food experience, it’s a delicious entry point to discover the magic of bold, vibrant flavors.

Make this Instant Pot Korean BBQ beef tonight and savor the taste of authentic Korean cuisine right in your own kitchen. Happy cooking!
