Easter Sunday Specials: Recipes for a Joyful Gathering


As the air fills with the unmistakable scent of spring, Easter Sunday brings together friends and family to celebrate a joyous occasion. This year, why not set the mood with an array of delightful recipes that are sure to impress and be remembered? From appetizers to desserts, indulge in flavors that evoke memories of gatherings past while creating new traditions.

  1. Mini Spinach and Feta Pie

This irresistible bite-sized spinach and feta pie is perfect for starting any feast off on the right foot. Made with a delectable phyllo dough and packed with fresh spinach and tangy feta cheese, your guests will surely ask for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of phyllo dough
  • 1 cup of chopped fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp. grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp. unsalted butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Grease a 9x13 baking dish.

  2. Thaw the phyllo dough per package instructions. Unroll the dough and cut it into 24 squares.

  3. In a bowl, combine spinach, feta cheese, eggs, and parmesan cheese.

  4. Brush each phyllo square with melted butter.

  5. Place the stack of squares into the baking dish and spoon spinach mixture evenly onto each square. Sprinkle lightly with additional feta cheese.

  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown. Allow to rest for 5 minutes before serving.

  7. Easter Carrot Cake

Who doesn’t love a good carrot cake? This Easter carrot cake is a scrumptious addition to any dessert spread. With a heavenly combination of moist spices, colorful carrots, and creamy frosting, it’s sure to become an Easter favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

For the frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

  3. In a separate bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in vanilla.

  4. Gradually add the flour mixture, mixing until just combined. Fold in carrots and pineapple.

  5. Stir in chopped walnuts.

  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  7. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before removing and transferring to wire racks to cool completely.

  8. To prepare frosting, cream butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar, mix in milk, and add vanilla extract.

  9. Once cakes are completely cooled, place one cake onto a plate and spread half the frosting over the top. Place the second cake on top and spread the remaining frosting over the top and sides of the cake.

  10. Easter Lamb Cookies

Gather the little ones to help make these adorable and tasty Easter lamb cookies. Using a sugar cookie base and royal icing, create a pastoral scene that’s sure to bring smiles to everyone’s faces.

Ingredients:

  • Sugar cookie mix (ingredients displayed on package)
  • Egg-white and milk mixture (ingredients displayed on package)
  • Shaped cookie cutters, such as lamb heads
  • Royal icing in green and purple

Instructions:

  1. Prepare sugar cookie dough according to package instructions.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8-inch thickness.
  3. Using a lamb-shaped cookie cutter, press out cookie shapes and place them on a lined baking sheet.
  4. Follow egg-white and milk mixture instructions for desired cookie outcome (glazing or brushing).
  5. Bake according to package instructions.
  6. Allow cookies to cool completely.
  7. With piping bags filled with royal icing, outline and fill in the lamb shapes.

As the sun sets on another Easter Sunday, gather around the table with loved ones as you savor the memories created through these enjoyable recipes. Delight in conversations and laughter as you share the joy of the season. Happy cooking, and best wishes for a memorable Easter gathering!
