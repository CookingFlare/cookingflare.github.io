Korean Spicy Braised Chicken: A Finger-Licking Good Dish


Welcome to our blog, where we explore the vibrant and delicious world of Korean cuisine. Today, we have an irresistible recipe for you: Korean Spicy Braised Chicken. This dish is packed with flavor, spice, and tender chicken that will leave you wanting more. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of chicken (preferably bone-in, skin-on thighs or drumsticks)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine or mirin
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the minced garlic, grated ginger, soy sauce, gochujang, gochugaru, honey, and rice wine. Mix well to create a thick paste.

  2. Add the chicken pieces to the bowl and coat them evenly with the spicy paste. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, deep skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  4. Carefully add the marinated chicken pieces to the skillet, skin-side down. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until the skin becomes crispy and browned.

  5. Flip the chicken pieces and add the water to the skillet. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the skillet. Allow the chicken to simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until it becomes tender and cooked through.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set it aside. Increase the heat to medium-high and let the sauce reduce for about 5 minutes, or until it thickens slightly.

  7. Return the chicken to the skillet and coat it with the thickened sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  8. Transfer the Korean Spicy Braised Chicken to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, you can add more gochugaru or gochujang to the marinade.
  • If you prefer a milder version of this dish, you can reduce the amount of chili paste and flakes.
  • Feel free to experiment with different cuts of chicken, such as wings or breast, but keep in mind that the cooking time may vary.
  • To make it a complete meal, you can add vegetables like carrots, potatoes, or bell peppers to the skillet while cooking the chicken.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Braised Chicken is a dish that exemplifies the bold and dynamic flavors of Korean cuisine. The combination of spicy gochujang, savory soy sauce, and tender chicken creates a truly finger-licking good experience. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a delicious homemade meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure with this mouthwatering Korean dish. Enjoy!
