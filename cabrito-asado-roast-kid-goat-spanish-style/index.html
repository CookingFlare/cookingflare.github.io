Cabrito Asado: Roast Kid Goat, Spanish-Style


If you’re looking to impress your guests with a unique and flavorful dish, Cabrito Asado is a must-try. This Spanish-style roast kid goat dish is a true delight for anyone who appreciates bold flavors and succulent meats. In this recipe, we’ll explore the traditional method of preparing Cabrito Asado, allowing you to bring the authentic flavors of Spain to your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To begin your culinary journey into the world of Cabrito Asado, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 whole kid goat, approximately 15-20 pounds
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup of dry white wine
  • 1 cup of chicken stock

Preparation

Step 1: Marinating the Goat

Start by creating a flavorful marinade for the kid goat. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, and oregano. Add the olive oil and lemon juice, and season the mixture with salt and black pepper. Rub the marinade all over the goat, ensuring that it is thoroughly coated. Cover the goat and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat.

Step 2: Roasting the Cabrito

After the marinating process, preheat your oven to 325°F. Place the marinated kid goat on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour the white wine and chicken stock into the bottom of the pan to create a flavorful base for the roasting process. Cover the pan with foil and roast the goat for approximately 2.5 to 3 hours, basting it occasionally with the pan juices.

Step 3: Uncovering and Browning

Once the initial roasting time has elapsed, remove the foil from the pan. Increase the oven temperature to 425°F and continue roasting the goat for an additional 30 minutes, or until the skin is golden brown and crispy. This step will ensure that the exterior of the goat develops a beautiful caramelized crust, adding texture and depth of flavor to the dish.

Step 4: Resting and Serving

After the final roasting stage, carefully remove the Cabrito Asado from the oven and let it rest for 20-30 minutes before carving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute within the meat, ensuring a moist and tender result. Once rested, carve the goat into portions and serve it with your choice of side dishes, such as roasted vegetables, potatoes, or a fresh green salad.

The Art of Cabrito Asado

Cabrito Asado is a dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of Spain. Its robust flavors and tender meat make it a centerpiece dish that is perfect for special occasions or gatherings with friends and family. The process of slow-roasting the kid goat allows the meat to become incredibly tender, while the aromatic marinade infuses it with a medley of fragrant herbs and citrus notes.

When preparing Cabrito Asado, it’s essential to embrace the traditional techniques and take your time with each step, allowing the flavors to develop and the meat to reach perfection. While this recipe requires patience and attention to detail, the end result is a masterpiece of Spanish cuisine that will leave a lasting impression on all who have the pleasure of savoring it.

So, if you’re ready to embark on a culinary adventure and delight your senses with the authentic flavors of Spain, consider preparing Cabrito Asado for your next special occasion. With its rich history and unparalleled taste, this Spanish-style roast kid goat is sure to elevate your cooking repertoire and create unforgettable dining experiences for you and your guests. Cheers to the art of Cabrito Asado!
