Hoisin Sauce Stir Fry Chicken with Veggies Recipe


Stir fry dishes are a fantastic way to combine fresh ingredients, vibrant flavors, and quick cooking all in one pan. When it comes to an irresistible stir fry, the combination of tender chicken and crisp vegetables coated in a rich, sweet, and savory hoisin sauce stands out as a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. This Hoisin Sauce Stir Fry Chicken with Veggies recipe will take your weeknight dinners to a whole new level with its simplicity and depth of flavor.

Why Hoisin Sauce?

Hoisin sauce is a thick, fragrant sauce often described as a blend of sweet, salty, and umami notes. Originating from Chinese cuisine, it’s made from fermented soybeans, garlic, vinegar, and various spices. This sauce adds a luxurious glaze to whatever it touches and pairs beautifully with stir-fried chicken and fresh veggies. Unlike soy sauce which is salty, hoisin offers a slightly sweet and tangy profile that elevates the dish without overpowering the natural flavors of the ingredients.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the cooking process, gather the following for a well-balanced dish:

For the Stir Fry:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any neutral oil suitable for high heat)
  • 1 cup red bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas or snow peas
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned or thinly sliced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated

For the Hoisin Sauce Mixture:

  • 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar (optional, for extra sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons cold water (for thickening)

Garnishes (optional but recommended):

  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley
  • Additional sliced green onions

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Prep Your Ingredients

Start by thinly slicing the chicken. The thinner the slices, the faster and more evenly they cook in the hot pan. Chop and prepare all your vegetables before heating your wok or skillet because stir frying is a quick process that requires everything ready to go.

2. Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together hoisin sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and honey. Set aside. This balance of ingredients ensures that the sauce is tangy, sweet, and savory.

3. Stir Fry the Chicken

Heat your wok or large skillet over medium-high heat and add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Once shimmering, add the sliced chicken in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding — this helps achieve a nice sear. Stir fry the chicken for about 4-5 minutes until cooked through and lightly golden. Remove the chicken and set it aside.

4. Cook the Veggies

Add the remaining oil to the pan. Toss in the garlic and ginger first, stirring constantly for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Then add the carrots and broccoli, as these take a bit longer to soften. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes before adding the bell peppers and peas. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, ensuring the veggies stay bright and crisp.

5. Combine and Finish

Return the chicken to the pan along with the sauce mixture. Stir to coat all ingredients evenly. Pour in the cornstarch slurry while stirring constantly. This will thicken the sauce, creating a glossy finish that clings to chicken and vegetables. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until everything is heated through and the sauce has thickened.

6. Serve and Garnish

Transfer the stir fry to a serving dish and garnish with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions or fresh herbs if desired. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed jasmine rice, brown rice, or noodles.

Tips for the Best Hoisin Sauce Stir Fry

  • Use High Heat: Stir fry thrives on intense heat, which cooks food quickly and locks in texture and flavor. Use a heavy-bottomed pan or wok for best results.

  • Prep Ahead: Have all ingredients prepped and near your stove—stir frying moves fast, and you won’t want to stop mid-way to chop something.

  • Chicken Alternatives: For variety, try sliced pork tenderloin or firm tofu as protein options. Adjust cooking times accordingly.

  • Vegetable Variations: Don’t hesitate to swap in or add your favorite stir fry vegetables such as zucchini, mushrooms, baby corn, or bok choy.

  • Hoisin Sauce Substitute: If you don’t have hoisin sauce, you can create a quick blend of soy sauce, peanut butter, honey, and a dash of five-spice powder, but the classic version is truly unmatched.

Nutritional Highlights

This stir fry recipe offers a balanced meal rich in protein and fiber because of the chicken and fresh vegetables. Hoisin sauce adds flavor without heavy use of oils or fats, making it a great option for those who want a tasty but nutritious dinner.

Making It Ahead and Storage

If you want to prep in advance, keep the chicken and vegetables separate until cooking. Pre-mix the sauce and store it in the refrigerator for a day or two. After cooking, this stir fry holds well in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove or in a microwave, adding a sprinkle of water if the sauce thickens too much.

The Hoisin Sauce Stir Fry Chicken with Veggies recipe offers a perfect harmony of savory, sweet, and fresh flavors in a dish that’s quick to prepare and satisfying. Whether you’re cooking for family or impressing guests, this stir fry is sure to become a favorite in your recipe rotation. The vibrant colors and diverse textures of the vegetables combined with the luscious hoisin glaze bring a delight to both the eyes and the palate.

Feel free to personalize this recipe as you like, adding or subtracting spices and ingredients to suit your own taste. Happy cooking!
