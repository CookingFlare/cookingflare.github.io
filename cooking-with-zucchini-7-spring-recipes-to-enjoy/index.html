Cooking with Zucchini: 7 Spring Recipes to Enjoy


As the weather warms up and the days grow longer, our taste buds start to crave lighter, fresher flavors. And what better way to welcome the season than with a basket full of zucchini? This versatile summer squash is not only delicious, but it’s also packed with nutrients and easy to incorporate into a variety of dishes.

In this post, we’ll explore 7 spring recipes that showcase the best of zucchini’s flavor and texture. From savory main courses to sweet treats, we’ll dive into the world of zucchini cooking and discover new ways to enjoy this seasonal superstar.

Zucchini and Feta Omelette

Start your day off right with a fluffy omelette packed with the sweetness of zucchini and the tanginess of feta. Simply chop 1/2 cup of zucchini into small pieces and sauté them in a pan with a tablespoon of olive oil until they’re tender. Beat 2 eggs with a fork, then add in the cooked zucchini, 1/4 cup of crumbled feta, and a sprinkle of salt and pepper. Cook the mixture in a non-stick pan until the eggs are set, then fold the omelette in half and serve hot.

Zucchini Noodle Stir-Fry

Zucchini noodles, or “zoodles,” are a great low-carb alternative to traditional pasta. Simply spiralize 2 medium zucchinis and set them aside. In a wok or large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add in 1/2 cup of sliced bell peppers, 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms, and 1/4 cup of chopped green onions. Cook until the vegetables are tender, then add in the zoodles and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they’re coated in the flavorful sauce. Season with soy sauce and serve hot.

Zucchini and Tomato Soup

There’s nothing like a warm, comforting bowl of soup to brighten up a spring day. This recipe combines the sweetness of zucchini with the tanginess of tomatoes for a delicious and satisfying meal. Simply sauté 1 medium onion, 2 cloves of garlic, and 2 cups of chopped zucchini in a pot with 2 tablespoons of olive oil until the vegetables are tender. Add in 2 cups of chopped tomatoes, 4 cups of chicken broth, and 1/2 cup of heavy cream. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then puree it in a blender until smooth. Serve hot, garnished with a sprinkle of basil and a dollop of sour cream.

Zucchini and Cheese Fritters

These crispy fritters are the perfect snack or side dish for a spring gathering. Simply grate 1 medium zucchini and mix it with 1/2 cup of grated cheddar, 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup of cornmeal, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 egg and 1/4 cup of buttermilk. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Drop the mixture by tablespoonfuls into a hot skillet coated with 1/4 inch of oil. Cook until the fritters are golden brown, then drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Zucchini and Hummus Wrap

This refreshing wrap is perfect for a quick lunch or snack. Simply spread 2 tablespoons of hummus on a whole wheat tortilla, then top with 1/2 cup of sliced zucchini, 1/4 cup of sliced cucumber, and 1/4 cup of crumbled feta. Add a sprinkle of salt and pepper to taste, then roll up the wrap and slice in half.

Zucchini Bread

There’s something special about the smell of freshly baked bread wafting from the oven. This moist and flavorful zucchini bread is the perfect treat for a spring morning. Simply grate 1 medium zucchini and mix it with 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted. Beat in 2 large eggs and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, then stir until just combined. Pour the batter into a greased loaf pan and bake at 350°F for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Zucchini and Carrot Muffins

These delicious muffins are the perfect way to use up any leftover zucchini or carrots. Simply grate 1 medium zucchini and 1 medium carrot, then mix them with 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted. Beat in 2 large eggs and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, then stir until just combined. Pour the batter into a greased muffin tin and bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

As you can see, zucchini is an incredibly versatile ingredient that can be used in a wide variety of dishes. Whether you’re looking for a healthy snack, a delicious main course, or a sweet treat, zucchini is the perfect addition to your spring menu. So go ahead, get creative, and start cooking with zucchini today!
