Vegan Oatmeal Cream Pies: Sweet, Soft, and Nostalgic


Nothing transports us back to childhood like the decadent delight of oatmeal cream pies—a treat that blends wholesome comfort with indulgent sweetness. These soft, chewy cookies sandwiched around creamy vanilla filling are the perfect balance of nostalgia and modern culinary ingenuity. In this recipe, we’re giving the classic dessert a twist by making it 100% vegan. Whether you’re a long-time vegan or simply looking for a mouthwatering dessert that skips animal products, these oatmeal cream pies are guaranteed to win hearts.

Let’s dive straight into creating this irresistible dessert: handmade oatmeal cookies with a smooth and luscious dairy-free cream filling.

The Magic of Vegan Baking

Turning traditional oatmeal cream pies into a vegan masterpiece is easier than you think. While conventional recipes rely on butter, eggs, and dairy, you can achieve the same level of rich texture and flavor by substituting alternative ingredients. Vegan baking gives you room to get creative while being mindful of the planet and your health.

To craft the ideal oatmeal cookie, we’ll use vegan butter for richness and flaxseed as an egg replacer to bind the dough. These swaps ensure the cookies remain soft, chewy, and delicious—exactly what you’d expect from a stellar oatmeal cream pie. Meanwhile, our dairy-free filling relies on coconut cream or vegan shortening to provide the perfect creamy consistency.

Ingredients for Vegan Oatmeal Cream Pies

Here’s everything you’ll need to create your batch of sweet nostalgia, broken down into cookie and filling components:

Oatmeal Cookies:

  • 1 cup vegan butter, softened
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp ground flaxseed (flax egg substitute)
  • 5 tbsp water
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg (optional, for extra warmth)
  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional, for texture and flavor balance)

Cream Filling:

  • 1/2 cup vegan butter or vegan shortening
  • 2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2-3 tbsp coconut cream (or almond milk for a lighter option)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep Work Makes Everything Easy

Before you start, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Now let’s get our flax egg ready by combining the ground flaxseed with water. Stir well, and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it thickens into a gelatinous consistency.

2. Bake the Oatmeal Cookies

Cream the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, beat the vegan butter with the brown sugar and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add your prepared flax egg and vanilla extract, blending them into the mixture until fully incorporated.

Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture, stirring until a dough forms. Fold in the rolled oats and raisins (if using) for texture and chewiness.

Shape and Bake

Using a tablespoon or small cookie scoop, drop dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Flatten each mound slightly to create a uniform shape. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.

Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling properly ensures they stay soft and chewy—ideal for sandwiching.

3. Whip Up the Cream Filling

In a medium mixing bowl, beat the vegan butter or shortening until smooth and creamy. Slowly add the powdered sugar, a little at a time, while mixing continuously to avoid clumps. Once the powdered sugar is fully incorporated, add the coconut cream one tablespoon at a time until the filling reaches your desired consistency. Stir in vanilla extract to enhance the flavor.

Taste-test your filling to ensure its sweetness complements the cookies. If you prefer a lighter cream, substitute coconut cream with almond milk.

4. Assemble Your Vegan Oatmeal Cream Pies

Now for the fun part—construction! Pair up the cookies based on their size for an even look. Using a piping bag or simply a spoon, add a generous dollop of cream filling to the flat side of one cookie. Place its partner on top and gently press to spread the filling evenly to the edges. Repeat with the remaining cookies and filling.

Pro Tips for Perfect Vegan Oatmeal Cream Pies

  • Store Smart: Keep these treats fresh longer by storing them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. They taste even better chilled!
  • Customize Texture: Not a fan of raisins? Swap them for vegan chocolate chips, or omit them entirely for a classic oatmeal cookie.
  • Spices Matter: Adjust the cinnamon and nutmeg quantities to suit your flavor preferences. Some people enjoy a heavier spice mix for a warm and cozy dessert.
  • Batch Size: Planning for a party or gathering? Double the recipe and create smaller cookies for perfectly portioned, bite-sized indulgence.

The Nostalgic Charm of Vegan Oatmeal Cream Pies

There’s something magical about biting into a soft oatmeal cookie paired with velvety cream filling. It’s a dessert that feels personal yet universally adored, making it an ideal treat for any occasion. These vegan oatmeal cream pies not only honor a beloved classic but elevate it with plant-based ingredients that are both ethical and delicious.

What makes these treats even better is their versatility—you can enjoy them with coffee, pack them in school lunches, or serve them at large gatherings. Every creamy bite will have your friends and family asking for the recipe—or an extra serving!

Take the time to bake a batch, share them with loved ones, or indulge solo. There’s truly no wrong way to enjoy vegan oatmeal cream pies. With their soft, chewy texture and creamy nostalgia, they’re bound to become a staple in your dessert repertoire.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your apron, preheat your oven, and channel your inner pastry chef. These plant-based oatmeal cream pies are the tender, sweet, and timeless dessert you deserve!

Enjoy baking, and dive into the delightful world of vegan desserts.
