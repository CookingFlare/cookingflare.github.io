Apple Cranberry Stuffed Pork Tenderloin: Elegant Fall Entree


As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, it’s the perfect time to embrace the flavors of fall in our cooking. One of the most delightful ways to celebrate the season is with an Apple Cranberry Stuffed Pork Tenderloin. This elegant dish not only looks impressive on the plate but also combines the savory richness of pork with the sweet-tart flavors of apples and cranberries, making it a standout centerpiece for any autumn gathering.

The Allure of Pork Tenderloin

Pork tenderloin is one of the most tender cuts of pork, known for its mild flavor and versatility. It’s a lean meat that can be prepared in various ways, making it an excellent choice for both casual and formal dining. When paired with a luscious stuffing, it transforms into a dish that delights the senses and warms the heart.

Why Stuff It?

Stuffing pork tenderloin adds dimension and complexity to the dish. The stuffing not only infuses the meat with flavor but also keeps it moist during cooking. The combination of apples and cranberries not only enhances the taste profile but also provides a beautiful color contrast that makes the dish visually appealing.

Ingredients

To create this delicious Apple Cranberry Stuffed Pork Tenderloin, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pork Tenderloin

  • 2 pork tenderloins (about 1 pound each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Stuffing

  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups fresh bread crumbs (or cubed stale bread)
  • 1 cup Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans (optional)
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Glaze

  • 1/4 cup apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Preparing the Stuffing

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a skillet over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Once hot, add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, approximately 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the sautéed onion and garlic with the bread crumbs, diced apples, dried cranberries, thyme, sage, pecans (if using), and parsley. Season with salt and pepper. Pour in the chicken broth gradually, mixing until the stuffing is moist but not soggy. Set aside.

Step 2: Preparing the Pork

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Tenderloins: Pat the pork tenderloins dry with paper towels. With a sharp knife, carefully slice each tenderloin lengthwise, making sure not to cut all the way through. Open them up like a book.

  3. Season: Season the inside and outside of the tenderloins generously with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Stuffing the Tenderloin

  1. Stuff the Pork: Take the stuffing mixture and fill each tenderloin evenly, pressing gently to pack it in.

  2. Secure the Tenderloin: Use kitchen twine to tie the tenderloins at intervals to hold the stuffing in place. Alternatively, you can use toothpicks to secure the opening.

Step 4: Searing and Roasting

  1. Sear the Tenderloin: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the remaining tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the stuffed tenderloins and sear for about 3-4 minutes on each side until browned.

  2. Prepare the Glaze: While the pork is searing, whisk together the apple cider, brown sugar, and Dijon mustard in a small bowl.

  3. Roast in the Oven: Pour the glaze over the seared tenderloins and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Roast for about 25-30 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).

Step 5: Resting and Slicing

  1. Rest the Meat: Once cooked, remove the tenderloins from the oven and let them rest for at least 10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful bite.

  2. Slice and Serve: Carefully remove the kitchen twine or toothpicks and slice the tenderloin into medallions. Serve warm with your favorite sides, and drizzle with the remaining glaze from the skillet.

Suggested Sides

To complement your Apple Cranberry Stuffed Pork Tenderloin, consider serving it alongside:

  • Roasted Brussels Sprouts: Tossed with olive oil, salt, and pepper, roasted until crispy.
  • Garlic Mashed Potatoes: Creamy potatoes blended with roasted garlic for a rich flavor.
  • Maple-Glazed Carrots: Sweet, tender carrots glazed with maple syrup and a sprinkle of thyme.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have leftovers, they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave, ensuring not to overcook the pork.

Conclusion

The Apple Cranberry Stuffed Pork Tenderloin is not just a meal; it’s an experience that brings the warmth and flavors of fall to your table. Its beautiful presentation makes it an ideal choice for holiday gatherings, family dinners, or special occasions. By following these steps, you can create a dish that is both delicious and visually stunning, sure to impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds.

Embrace the spirit of the season and let this recipe become a cherished part of your autumn culinary repertoire. Happy cooking!
