Moroccan Zaalouk Recipe with Eggplant and Tomatoes


If you’re a fan of Moroccan cuisine or looking to explore new flavors, then this Zaalouk recipe is a must-try. Zaalouk is a traditional Moroccan dish made with eggplant and tomatoes, cooked to perfection with aromatic spices. It’s a versatile dish that can be served as a side dish, dip, or even a spread for sandwiches. The rich flavors and vibrant colors of this dish will surely impress your taste buds.

Ingredients

To make this delicious Zaalouk, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 4 ripe tomatoes
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by roasting the eggplants. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce the eggplants with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Roast them in the oven for about 45 minutes or until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.

  2. While the eggplants are roasting, prepare the tomatoes. Bring a pot of water to a boil and score a small ‘X’ on the bottom of each tomato. Carefully drop the tomatoes into the boiling water and blanch them for about 1 minute. Remove them from the water and transfer them to a bowl of ice water to cool. Peel off the skin and dice the tomatoes.

  3. Once the eggplants are roasted, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly. Peel off the charred skin and discard. Mash the roasted eggplant flesh with a fork or blend it in a food processor until smooth.

  4. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.

  5. Add the diced tomatoes to the skillet and cook for about 5 minutes until they start to break down and release their juices.

  6. Stir in the mashed eggplant, cumin, paprika, coriander, and cinnamon. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all the flavors are well combined and the mixture thickens.

  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and let the Zaalouk cool to room temperature. This dish is best served at room temperature or slightly chilled.

  8. Once cooled, transfer the Zaalouk to a serving dish and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro.

Serving Suggestions

Zaalouk can be served in various ways. It can be enjoyed as a side dish alongside grilled meats or fish. It can also be served as a dip with warm pita bread, crackers, or vegetable sticks. Additionally, you can use Zaalouk as a flavorful spread for sandwiches or wraps. The possibilities are endless!

Conclusion

Moroccan Zaalouk is a delightful dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of Moroccan cuisine. The combination of roasted eggplants, juicy tomatoes, and aromatic spices creates a harmonious blend of tastes and textures. Whether you’re a fan of Moroccan food or simply looking to try something new, this Zaalouk recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure to Morocco right in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
