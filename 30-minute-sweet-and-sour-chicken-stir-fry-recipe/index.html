30-Minute Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re looking for a quick and delicious meal that will satisfy your craving for sweet and tangy flavors, this 30-Minute Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry is the perfect recipe for you. Packed with colorful vegetables, tender chicken, and a homemade sweet and sour sauce, this stir-fry is not only easy to make, but it’s also bursting with flavor. Plus, it can be on your table in just half an hour, making it a great option for busy weeknights.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need for this mouthwatering dish:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks, drained
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the ketchup, soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, cornstarch, and chicken broth. Set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and season with salt and pepper. Cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-6 minutes.

  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the sliced bell peppers and onion. Cook until the vegetables are crisp-tender, about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Return the chicken to the skillet and add the pineapple chunks. Pour the sweet and sour sauce over the chicken and vegetables. Stir well to coat everything evenly.

  5. Continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes, until the sauce thickens and everything is heated through.

  6. Serve the Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry over cooked rice and enjoy immediately!

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding or substituting your favorite vegetables. Broccoli, carrots, and snap peas would all be great additions.

  • For a spicier version, add some red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha to the sauce.

  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, you can substitute tofu for the chicken. Just make sure to press it to remove any excess moisture before cooking.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop before serving.

  • This Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry is also great for meal prep. You can make a double batch and divide it into individual containers for easy grab-and-go lunches throughout the week.

Final Thoughts

With its vibrant colors, bold flavors, and quick cooking time, this 30-Minute Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry is a winner in every way. It’s a versatile dish that can be easily customized to suit your taste preferences, and it’s a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your diet. So, the next time you’re in need of a quick and satisfying meal, give this recipe a try. You won’t be disappointed!
