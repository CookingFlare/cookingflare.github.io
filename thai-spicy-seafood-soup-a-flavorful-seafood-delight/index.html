Thai Spicy Seafood Soup: A Flavorful Seafood Delight


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine and seafood, then you’re in for a treat with this Thai Spicy Seafood Soup recipe. Bursting with the vibrant flavors of lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal, this soup is a delightful combination of spicy, sour, and savory tastes that will transport you to the bustling streets of Thailand. Not only is it a flavor explosion, but it’s also surprisingly easy to make, making it perfect for both weeknight dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients

For the Broth:

  • 4 cups of seafood stock
  • 3 stalks of lemongrass, bruised and chopped
  • 6 kaffir lime leaves
  • 3 slices of galangal
  • 4 shallots, halved
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons of palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of lime juice

For the Seafood:

  • 300g of prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 200g of squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 200g of mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 200g of firm white fish fillets, cut into chunks

For the Garnish:

  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Thinly sliced red chilies
  • Sliced shallots
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring the seafood stock to a gentle boil. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, shallots, and tomatoes. Simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse the broth.

  2. Once the broth is fragrant, add the fish sauce, tamarind paste, palm sugar, and lime juice. Adjust the seasoning to your taste by adding more fish sauce, sugar, or lime juice if necessary.

  3. Carefully add the prawns, squid, mussels, and fish into the broth. Cook for 3-5 minutes or until the seafood is cooked through and the mussels have opened. Discard any mussels that do not open.

  4. Once the seafood is cooked, ladle the soup into serving bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves, thinly sliced red chilies, shallots, and a wedge of lime.

  5. Serve the Thai Spicy Seafood Soup hot with steamed jasmine rice or enjoy it on its own as a light, flavorful meal.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra kick of heat, you can add a spoonful of Thai red curry paste to the broth.
  • If seafood stock is not available, you can use a combination of water and clam juice as a substitute.
  • Be cautious with the fish sauce as it is quite salty, so it’s best to start with a small amount and adjust as needed.

Final Thoughts

This Thai Spicy Seafood Soup is a celebration of the incredible flavors that Thai cuisine has to offer. With its bold and aromatic broth and a medley of tender seafood, it’s a dish that will undoubtedly impress your family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned Thai food enthusiast or new to the world of Thai cooking, this recipe is a must-try for anyone who appreciates the exciting flavors of Southeast Asia. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Thailand in the comfort of your own kitchen. Enjoy!
