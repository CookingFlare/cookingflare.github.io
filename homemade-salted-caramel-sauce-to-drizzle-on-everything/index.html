Homemade Salted Caramel Sauce to Drizzle on Everything


If there’s one thing that can elevate a dessert or even a simple breakfast to a whole new level of indulgence, it’s homemade salted caramel sauce. This luscious, buttery, and slightly salty sauce is incredibly versatile and can be used to drizzle over ice cream, cakes, pies, pancakes, waffles, and so much more. The best part? It’s surprisingly easy to make at home, and once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll never go back to store-bought caramel sauce again.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt (adjust to taste)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sugar: Start by heating the granulated sugar in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium heat. Use a whisk to stir the sugar as it begins to melt. The sugar will clump at first, but keep stirring, and it will eventually melt into a smooth amber-colored liquid. Be careful not to burn it.

  2. Add the Butter: Once the sugar has completely melted and turned a deep amber color, add the butter to the saucepan. Be cautious as the mixture will bubble rapidly when the butter is added. Whisk the butter into the caramel until it is completely melted.

  3. Pour in the Cream: Slowly pour in the heavy cream while stirring continuously. Again, be cautious as the mixture will bubble when the cream is added. Let the mixture boil for a minute. It will rise in the pan as it boils, so make sure you’re using a saucepan with enough room.

  4. Add Flavor: Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and sea salt. The salt adds a wonderful contrast to the sweetness of the caramel and enhances its flavor. Taste the sauce and adjust the salt to your preference.

  5. Cool and Store: Allow the caramel sauce to cool for a few minutes in the saucepan before transferring it to a heatproof jar or container. Let it cool to room temperature before placing it in the refrigerator. The sauce will thicken as it cools.

Tips and Tricks

  • Use a heavy-bottomed saucepan to prevent the sugar from burning.
  • Be cautious when adding the butter and cream as the mixture will bubble vigorously.
  • Stir the caramel sauce continuously to ensure an even consistency.
  • Store the sauce in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

Serving Suggestions

Once you’ve made a batch of this heavenly homemade salted caramel sauce, the possibilities are endless. Drizzle it over vanilla ice cream and top with chopped nuts for a simple yet luxurious dessert. Use it as a filling for layer cakes or drizzle it over a warm apple pie. For a decadent breakfast treat, pour it over fluffy pancakes or waffles. You can even mix it into your morning coffee for a sweet and indulgent pick-me-up.

The beauty of homemade salted caramel sauce lies in its ability to effortlessly elevate any dish, transforming the ordinary into the extraordinary with its rich and buttery flavor profile. There’s no denying the allure of this indulgent treat, and once you’ve experienced the magic of homemade caramel, you’ll never look back.

So, the next time you’re craving a touch of sweetness, whip up a batch of this homemade salted caramel sauce and prepare to be amazed by the delectable results. It’s a simple yet luxurious addition to your culinary repertoire that will have you reaching for it time and time again.
