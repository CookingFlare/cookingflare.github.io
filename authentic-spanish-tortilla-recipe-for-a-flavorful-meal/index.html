Authentic Spanish Tortilla Recipe for a Flavorful Meal


If you’re looking for a dish that embodies the essence of Spanish cuisine, look no further than the classic Spanish tortilla. This hearty and versatile dish, also known as tortilla de patatas, is a staple in Spanish households and is enjoyed as a tapa, a light meal, or even as a filling for a sandwich. Made with simple ingredients yet bursting with flavor, the authentic Spanish tortilla is a must-try for any food enthusiast.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes
  • 1 large onion
  • 6 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes and Onions

Peel the potatoes and slice them into thin rounds. Similarly, peel the onion and slice it into thin half-moons. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onions to the skillet, ensuring that they are fully submerged in the oil. Cook the potatoes and onions over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and the onions are translucent. This process typically takes around 15-20 minutes. Once cooked, remove the potatoes and onions from the skillet using a slotted spoon, allowing the excess oil to drain.

Step 2: Beat the Eggs

In a large bowl, crack the eggs and beat them until they are well combined. Season the beaten eggs with a generous pinch of salt, keeping in mind that the potatoes and onions will also contribute to the overall seasoning of the tortilla.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

Add the cooked potatoes and onions to the beaten eggs, gently folding them together to ensure that the potatoes and onions are evenly distributed throughout the egg mixture.

Step 4: Cook the Tortilla

In the same skillet used to cook the potatoes and onions, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, pour the egg, potato, and onion mixture into the skillet, spreading it out evenly. Cook the tortilla over medium heat, running a spatula around the edges to prevent sticking. After about 4-5 minutes, when the bottom of the tortilla is golden brown and the top is still slightly runny, it’s time to flip it.

Place a large plate over the skillet and carefully invert the skillet so that the tortilla transfers onto the plate. Once the tortilla is on the plate, slide it back into the skillet to cook the other side. Cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the tortilla is fully set and golden brown on both sides.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the tortilla is cooked to perfection, remove it from the skillet and let it cool for a few minutes. Traditionally, Spanish tortilla is served at room temperature, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully. Cut the tortilla into wedges or squares and serve it as a tapa, a light meal, or even as a filling for a sandwich. Pair it with a crisp green salad and a glass of Spanish red wine for an authentic and flavorful Spanish dining experience.

Conclusion

The authentic Spanish tortilla is a true testament to the simplicity and elegance of Spanish cuisine. With just a handful of humble ingredients and a few simple steps, you can create a dish that is rich in flavor and steeped in tradition. Whether you’re hosting a tapas night or simply craving a taste of Spain, the Spanish tortilla is guaranteed to delight your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Madrid or Barcelona. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the authentic flavors of Spain with this delightful tortilla recipe.
