How to Make Delicious and Nutritious Chinese Steamed Whole Fish


Steaming is a traditional Chinese cooking method that preserves the natural flavors of the ingredients while maintaining their nutritional value. One classic dish that highlights the beauty of steaming is Chinese Steamed Whole Fish. This recipe is not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making a mouthwatering Chinese Steamed Whole Fish that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish (such as sea bass or snapper), scaled and gutted
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine or dry sherry
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, julienned
  • 2 green onions, sliced lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Steamer: Fill a large pot with water and place a steaming rack inside. Make sure the water level is below the rack. Bring the water to a boil over high heat.

  2. Prepare the Fish: Rinse the whole fish under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Make a few diagonal cuts on both sides of the fish, about 1 inch apart. This will help the flavors penetrate the flesh during steaming.

  3. Season the Fish: Rub the fish with salt, both inside and out. Place the fish on a heatproof plate that fits inside your steamer.

  4. Add Flavors: Scatter the julienned ginger and sliced green onions on top of the fish. Drizzle the soy sauce and rice wine or dry sherry evenly over the fish. For an extra kick, you can also add a few drops of sesame oil.

  5. Steam the Fish: Carefully place the plate with the fish onto the steaming rack in the pot. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Steam the fish for about 10-15 minutes, depending on its size. The fish is cooked when the flesh is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.

  6. Prepare the Sauce: While the fish is steaming, heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Once hot, remove from heat and set aside.

  7. Serve: Carefully remove the plate with the steamed fish from the steamer. Drizzle the hot vegetable oil over the fish to release its flavors and aroma. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves for added freshness and color.

  8. Enjoy: Serve the Chinese Steamed Whole Fish immediately with steamed rice and your favorite vegetables. Don’t forget to pour some of the flavorful sauce from the plate over the fish and rice for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • Choose a fresh whole fish with clear and bright eyes. This indicates its freshness.
  • You can customize the flavors by adding minced garlic, chili peppers, or even fermented black beans to the fish before steaming.
  • Experiment with different types of fish to find your favorite. Some popular choices include sea bass, snapper, and tilapia.
  • If you don’t have a steamer, you can still make this dish by using a large skillet with a tight-fitting lid. Place a heatproof plate or a bamboo steamer inside the skillet, add water, and steam the fish as directed.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as bok choy, mushrooms, or carrots to the plate with the fish for a complete and nutritious meal.

Chinese Steamed Whole Fish is a show-stopping dish that is not only visually appealing but also bursting with flavors. The delicate and tender fish, combined with the aromatic ginger and green onions, creates a harmonious taste that is both satisfying and healthy. So, next time you want to impress your guests or simply treat yourself to something special, give this Chinese steamed delicacy a try. Enjoy the art of steaming and savor the taste of tradition!
