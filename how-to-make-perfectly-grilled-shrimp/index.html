How to Make Perfectly Grilled Shrimp


Grilled shrimp is a perfect choice for a light and delicious meal, and it’s incredibly versatile. Whether you’re preparing for a barbecue, hosting a family dinner, or just looking for a quick, healthy recipe, grilled shrimp can satisfy all your cravings. In this guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know to achieve perfectly grilled shrimp every time, from the preparation to the grilling techniques. By the end, you’ll have a foolproof recipe for shrimp that is juicy, flavorful, and charred to perfection.

Why Grilled Shrimp?

Shrimp are one of the most popular seafood choices because they cook quickly, have a delicate flavor, and offer an impressive amount of protein for their size. Grilling shrimp enhances their natural sweetness while adding a slight smoky flavor that perfectly complements their texture. When prepared correctly, grilled shrimp can be tender on the inside, slightly crispy on the outside, and bursting with flavor.

Unlike other proteins, shrimp cook in mere minutes, which makes them an ideal option for busy weeknights or spontaneous gatherings. In this post, we’ll not only teach you how to grill shrimp but also how to marinate, season, and cook them to perfection.

Choosing the Right Shrimp for Grilling

Fresh vs. Frozen Shrimp

One of the most important factors in grilling shrimp is selecting the right shrimp. You’ll find shrimp sold in both fresh and frozen forms, and while both can be used for grilling, frozen shrimp are often more readily available and typically cheaper. When choosing between fresh or frozen shrimp, here are a few tips:

  • Fresh shrimp: If you have access to fresh shrimp, this is always the best option, as they will have the most flavor and the best texture. Fresh shrimp usually have a translucent and slightly glossy appearance. Be sure to purchase shrimp that have been properly handled and kept cold.
  • Frozen shrimp: Most shrimp you find at the store will be frozen. Although they may lose some of their natural sweetness, they can still make for great grilled shrimp when properly thawed. Be sure to thaw frozen shrimp in the refrigerator overnight for the best results. Never thaw shrimp in warm water, as this can affect their texture.

Size Matters

Shrimp come in various sizes, and the size of shrimp you choose will affect how long it takes to grill them. Shrimp are typically labeled by the number of shrimp per pound. For grilling, it’s best to go with medium to large shrimp (31-40 shrimp per pound or 21-25 shrimp per pound). These sizes are large enough to develop a nice char without overcooking. Jumbo shrimp (less than 21 shrimp per pound) are also excellent for grilling but require more time on the grill.

Preparing Shrimp for Grilling

Before grilling shrimp, it’s essential to properly prepare them. Here’s a step-by-step guide to ensure they are ready for the grill.

1. Peel the Shrimp

While shrimp can be grilled with the shell on, it’s usually best to peel them for convenience and ease of eating. Peeling shrimp involves removing the shell and the vein. You can leave the tails on or remove them, depending on your preference. Some people prefer to keep the tails on for a more rustic presentation, while others prefer to remove them for a cleaner look.

To peel the shrimp:

  • Remove the shell by pinching the bottom of the shrimp and gently pulling it away from the body.
  • Use a small knife to cut along the back of the shrimp, making a shallow incision to expose the dark vein.
  • Remove the vein by pulling it out with your fingers or using the tip of the knife.

2. Devein the Shrimp

Deveining shrimp is not always necessary, but it’s often done for aesthetic reasons. The vein is actually the shrimp’s digestive tract, and although it’s safe to eat, many people find it unappealing. To devein the shrimp, simply make a shallow cut along its back and remove the vein with a toothpick or your fingers.

3. Marinate the Shrimp

Marinating shrimp before grilling helps infuse them with flavor and tenderness. Because shrimp are delicate, they don’t need to be marinated for hours, and a short marinating time is often all that’s required. You can marinate shrimp for anywhere between 15 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the flavor intensity you desire.

For a simple marinade, combine olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and any herbs or spices you enjoy (such as parsley, paprika, or chili flakes). You can also experiment with different marinades, such as a sweet and tangy BBQ sauce, a smoky chipotle marinade, or a zesty citrus dressing.

4. Skewer or Grill Directly

Skewering shrimp helps prevent them from falling through the grates, especially if you’re grilling smaller shrimp. If you prefer, you can grill shrimp directly on the grates without skewering them, but skewering can make the grilling process easier.

To skewer shrimp:

  • Use metal or wooden skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning).
  • Thread the shrimp onto the skewers, placing them so they are spaced evenly, but not tightly packed together.

Grilling the Shrimp

Now that your shrimp are prepped and ready, it’s time to get them on the grill. Grilling shrimp is a quick process, and there are a few key tips to follow to avoid overcooking them.

1. Preheat the Grill

Before placing shrimp on the grill, preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375-400°F). A hot grill helps to sear the shrimp quickly, which locks in their flavor and moisture. Whether you’re using a gas grill, charcoal, or a grill pan, it’s important that the grill is hot and ready to go.

2. Oil the Grates

To prevent the shrimp from sticking to the grill, lightly oil the grates. You can do this by using a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil or a spray oil. Be careful not to use too much oil, as it can cause flare-ups and uneven cooking.

3. Grill the Shrimp

Place the shrimp on the grill, either directly on the grates or on skewers. If grilling directly on the grates, make sure there’s enough space between each shrimp so they can cook evenly. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, turning them once they develop a nice golden-brown char. Be cautious, as shrimp cook quickly and can easily become overcooked. Overcooked shrimp are tough and rubbery, so keep a close eye on them.

As a rule of thumb, shrimp are cooked when they turn opaque and pink. They should have a nice, crispy exterior, but the inside should remain juicy and tender.

4. Avoid Overcrowding

If you’re grilling a large batch of shrimp, it’s important not to overcrowd the grill. Overcrowding will lower the temperature of the grill and prevent the shrimp from cooking evenly. If necessary, grill the shrimp in batches.

Tips for Perfectly Grilled Shrimp

  • Don’t Overcook: The key to perfectly grilled shrimp is to not overcook them. Shrimp cook in a matter of minutes, and the moment they turn opaque, they are done. If in doubt, it’s better to undercook shrimp slightly than to overcook them.
  • Rest the Shrimp: After grilling, let the shrimp rest for a couple of minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the shrimp.
  • Serve Immediately: Grilled shrimp are best served right off the grill when they are still warm. You can pair them with a variety of sides, such as grilled vegetables, salads, rice, or even on top of pasta.

Creative Ways to Serve Grilled Shrimp

Grilled shrimp can be enjoyed in many different ways. Here are a few creative serving ideas:

  • Shrimp Tacos: Serve grilled shrimp in soft corn or flour tortillas with avocado, slaw, and a drizzle of creamy sauce.
  • Shrimp Salad: Toss grilled shrimp into a fresh salad with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and a light vinaigrette.
  • Shrimp Skewers: Serve shrimp skewers as an appetizer or main dish, paired with a dipping sauce like garlic butter or spicy aioli.
  • Shrimp Pasta: Add grilled shrimp to your favorite pasta for a light, flavorful meal. Try it with a lemon garlic butter sauce or a creamy Alfredo sauce.
  • Shrimp on a Platter: For a crowd-pleasing dish, serve grilled shrimp on a large platter with lemon wedges, fresh herbs, and a variety of dipping sauces.

Conclusion

Perfectly grilled shrimp are an easy and delicious addition to any meal. With the right preparation and grilling technique, you can create a dish that’s juicy, flavorful, and full of smoky goodness. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply looking for a quick and healthy meal, this recipe will guide you to perfectly grilled shrimp every time.

With a little practice, you’ll master the art of grilling shrimp and be able to serve up a dish that will impress your family and friends. So fire up the grill and start experimenting with different marinades, spices, and accompaniments to make the perfect grilled shrimp that suits your taste!
