Christmas Recipes for Kids: Fun and Easy Dishes to Make Together


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to celebrate than by getting the kids involved in the kitchen? Cooking together not only creates cherished memories but also sparks creativity and encourages teamwork. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of fun and easy Christmas recipes that kids can help with. These delightful dishes are perfect for family gatherings, festive parties, or simply to enjoy at home. From savory snacks to sweet treats, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Let’s dive into the festive world of cooking!

1. Christmas Tree Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza dough
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Assorted toppings (pepperoni, bell peppers, olives, etc.)
  • Fresh basil leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Shape the Dough: Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface into a triangle shape, resembling a Christmas tree. Use a sharp knife to cut the dough into a tree shape, ensuring to keep the trimmings for later.

  3. Add Sauce and Cheese: Spread a generous amount of pizza sauce on the dough and sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.

  4. Decorate with Toppings: Let the kids get creative! Use the assorted toppings to decorate the tree. You can use pepperoni as ornaments, bell pepper strips as garlands, and olives as the tree trunk.

  5. Bake the Pizza: Transfer the decorated pizza onto a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

  6. Serve: Once baked, let it cool slightly. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before slicing and serving.

Why Kids Will Love It:

Kids will enjoy personalizing their pizza, and this dish provides a great opportunity to discuss healthy toppings while making it fun!

2. Reindeer Sandwiches

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of whole grain bread
  • 4 tablespoons of peanut butter (or cream cheese for nut-free)
  • 1 banana
  • Pretzel sticks
  • Raisins or chocolate chips
  • Red cherry or grape

Instructions:

  1. Spread the Filling: Spread peanut butter or cream cheese generously on each slice of bread.

  2. Assemble the Sandwiches: Place two slices on top of each other to make two sandwiches.

  3. Create Reindeer Features: Use pretzel sticks as antlers by sticking them into the top of the sandwich. Slice the banana into rounds and use one piece as the reindeer’s nose. Place it at the front of the sandwich.

  4. Add Eyes: Use raisins or chocolate chips to make the reindeer’s eyes by pressing them into the bread.

  5. Serve: Cut the sandwiches into fun shapes, or leave them as is for a playful presentation.

Why Kids Will Love It:

These sandwiches are not only delicious but also offer a fun, hands-on way to engage kids in making their own lunch. Plus, who doesn’t love a cute reindeer?

3. Festive Fruit Kabobs

Ingredients:

  • Various fruits (strawberries, grapes, pineapple, kiwi, bananas)
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Christmas-themed cookie cutters (for fun shapes)
  • Skewers or lollipop sticks

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fruit: Wash and cut the fruits into bite-sized pieces. Use the cookie cutters to create fun shapes from the pineapple and melon.

  2. Assemble the Kabobs: Let the kids thread the fruit pieces onto the skewers in any order they like, alternating with mini marshmallows for a festive touch.

  3. Serve: Arrange the kabobs on a platter and enjoy!

Why Kids Will Love It:

These colorful fruit kabobs are visually appealing and a healthy treat. Kids can experiment with flavors and textures, making it a creative outlet.

4. Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup molasses
  • Royal icing for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, whisk together the flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking soda, and salt.

  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a separate bowl, cream the softened butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg and molasses, mixing thoroughly.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture, stirring until a dough forms. Chill the dough for at least 1 hour.

  4. Roll and Cut: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface and use cookie cutters to cut out shapes.

  5. Bake the Cookies: Place the cookies on a baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes. Allow them to cool completely before decorating.

  6. Decorate: Use royal icing to decorate the cookies. Kids can let their imaginations run wild with designs!

Why Kids Will Love It:

Decorating gingerbread cookies is a classic Christmas activity that kids adore. The hands-on approach and delicious results make it a holiday favorite.

5. Snowman Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pancake mix
  • 1 cup water (or milk for richer flavor)
  • Whipped cream
  • Chocolate chips
  • Mini candy canes (for arms)
  • Sliced fruit (for accessories)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Pancake Batter: In a bowl, mix the pancake mix with water or milk until smooth.

  2. Cook the Pancakes: Pour the batter onto a hot griddle, forming three pancakes of varying sizes (large, medium, small) to stack for the snowman.

  3. Stack the Pancakes: Once cooked, stack the pancakes starting with the largest on the bottom.

  4. Decorate the Snowman: Use whipped cream to frost the pancakes and chocolate chips for the eyes and buttons. Add mini candy canes as arms, and sliced fruit for a scarf or hat.

Why Kids Will Love It:

Everyone loves pancakes! Kids will enjoy making their own snowman and decorating it with their favorite toppings.

6. Candy Cane Bark

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound white chocolate chips
  • 1 cup crushed candy canes
  • ½ teaspoon peppermint extract (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the white chocolate chips in 30-second intervals, stirring in between until smooth.

  2. Add Flavoring: If desired, mix in peppermint extract for an extra burst of flavor.

  3. Prepare the Baking Sheet: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  4. Spread the Chocolate: Pour the melted chocolate onto the parchment paper and spread it into an even layer.

  5. Sprinkle with Candy Canes: Immediately sprinkle the crushed candy canes over the melted chocolate, pressing down lightly.

  6. Cool and Break: Allow the bark to cool and harden in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes. Once set, break into pieces.

Why Kids Will Love It:

Making candy cane bark is simple and allows kids to get involved in the process of melting and decorating, plus it’s a sweet treat they can share with family and friends.

7. Holiday Popcorn Mix

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups popped popcorn
  • 1 cup mixed nuts (optional)
  • 1 cup pretzel sticks
  • 1 cup M&M’s or holiday-themed candies
  • 1 cup melted chocolate (white or dark)
  • Sprinkles for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Popcorn: In a large bowl, combine popped popcorn, nuts, pretzels, and candies.

  2. Drizzle with Chocolate: Pour melted chocolate over the popcorn mixture and gently stir to coat all the ingredients.

  3. Add Sprinkles: While the chocolate is still wet, sprinkle your festive decorations over the mix.

  4. Cool and Serve: Allow the mixture to cool so the chocolate hardens before serving in festive bags or bowls.

Why Kids Will Love It:

This popcorn mix is a fun and interactive recipe. Kids will love the combination of flavors and the chance to customize their own snack mix.

Conclusion

Cooking together during the Christmas season is a wonderful way to bond with children while teaching them valuable kitchen skills. These fun and easy Christmas recipes not only promote creativity and teamwork but also allow for delicious results that can be enjoyed by the whole family. From savory pizzas to sweet treats, each recipe offers a unique opportunity to celebrate the spirit of the holiday season.

So gather your ingredients, put on your aprons, and let the festive cooking begin! Happy holidays!
