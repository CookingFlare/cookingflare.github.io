5 Easy Middle Eastern Dishes for Beginners


Middle Eastern cuisine is a world of flavors, aromas, and textures that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more. This vibrant region, spanning across countries such as Turkey, Lebanon, Iran, and Iraq, offers a rich culinary heritage, with each country adding its unique twist to the dishes. For those new to the world of Middle Eastern cooking, it can be overwhelming to navigate the various ingredients, spices, and cooking techniques. Fear not, dear cooks! In this post, we'll explore five easy Middle Eastern dishes that are perfect for beginners, requiring minimal ingredients and straightforward cooking methods. Recipe 1: Tabbouleh (Salad of Bulger Greens) Begin your Middle Eastern cooking journey with Tabbouleh, a simple yet flavorful salad originating from Lebanon. This refreshing dish consists of bulgur, parsley, tomatoes, mint, onions, and lemon juice. Ingredients:

1 cup bulgur 1 bunch fresh parsley 2 large tomatoes, diced 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint 1/2 cup chopped red onion 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions:

Rinse the bulgur and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and squeeze out excess water. In a large bowl, combine the bulgur, parsley, tomatoes, mint, and red onion. Squeeze the lemon juice over the mixture and toss well. Serve Tabbouleh as a side dish or include it in your main course.

Recipe 2: Falafel (Crunchy Chickpea Patties) Falafel, a beloved street food in the Middle East, is a staple in Eastern cuisine. These crispy chickpea patties are a great starting point for beginners. Ingredients:

1 cup dried chickpeas 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon coriander 1 teaspoon paprika 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

Soak the chickpeas in water for 8 hours or overnight. Drain and mash using a fork or potato masher. In a bowl, mix together the mashed chickpeas, onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Shape the mixture into small patties and coat with flour. Fry the patties in hot oil until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

Recipe 3: Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma) Stuffed grape leaves, or Dolma, are a classic Middle Eastern dish, which combines flavors and textures in a delightful package. Ingredients:

12 grape leaves 1 cup rice 1 cup ground meat (beef or lamb) 1 onion, finely chopped 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon allspice 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions:

Fill a large pot with water and submerge the grape leaves. On a large plate, lay a grape leaf, with the stem end facing up. Place 1 tablespoon of rice mixture in the center of the leaf. Fold the stem end over the filling, then roll the leaf tightly into a cylindrical shape. Repeat with the remaining leaves and filling. Place the dolma in the pot and simmer for 30 minutes.

Recipe 4: Shakshuka (North African and Middle Eastern Omelette) Shakshuka, a vibrant egg dish originating from North Africa, is a staple in many Middle Eastern countries. This egg dish marries flavors of tomatoes, spices, and eggs in a single plate. Ingredients:

2 large onions, grated 2 large bell peppers, diced 2 large tomatoes, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 4 eggs Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

Heat oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and bell peppers and sauté until softened. Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Sauté for 1 minute. Add the diced tomatoes and continue cooking for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Make 4 wells in the skillet and crack an egg into each well. Cover and cook until the eggs are set. Garnish with chopped parsley.

Recipe 5: Saj Bread (Pan-Fried Flatbread) Saj bread, a Middle Eastern flatbread, is a versatile base for many meals. Flaky, soft, and fragrant, Saj bread is a perfect accompaniment to the above recipes. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon sugar 1/4 teaspoon active dry yeast 1/2 cup lukewarm water Vegetable oil for brushing

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Gradually add the lukewarm water and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 10 minutes and let it rest for 1 hour. Divide the dough into small pieces and roll out into thin circles. Brush the tops with oil and cook in a hot skillet until puffed and golden brown.

Conclusion Middle Eastern cuisine offers a world of flavors and textures waiting to be explored. Starting with these five easy recipes will lay a strong foundation for your culinary journey through the Middle East. From the crunchy Tabbouleh to the savory Falafel, and the stuffed grape leaves, every dish is a new experience that will take you on a gastronomic adventure. Begin your journey today and taste the magic of the East!
