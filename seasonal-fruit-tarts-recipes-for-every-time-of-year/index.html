Seasonal Fruit Tarts: Recipes for Every Time of Year


As the seasons change, so do the flavors and textures of the fruits that are available. But one thing remains constant: the joy of biting into a sweet and tangy fruit tart. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making seasonal fruit tarts, and provide you with recipes for every time of year.

Spring

Spring is the perfect time to showcase the sweetness of strawberries and the tartness of rhubarb. Our Strawberry Rhubarb Tart is a classic combination that’s sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 cups sliced strawberries
  • 1 cup sliced rhubarb
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and rhubarb.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and salt.
  5. Add the sugar mixture to the fruit and toss until the fruit is evenly coated.
  6. Arrange the fruit mixture on the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  7. Fold the edges of the pastry up over the fruit to form a crust.
  8. Brush the egg wash over the pastry to give it a golden brown color.
  9. Bake the tart for 40-50 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the fruit is tender.
  10. Allow the tart to cool before serving. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.
