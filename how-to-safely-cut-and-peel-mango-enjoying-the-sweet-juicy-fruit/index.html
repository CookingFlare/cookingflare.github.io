How to Safely Cut and Peel Mango: Enjoying the Sweet Juicy Fruit


Mangoes are one of nature’s most delicious and refreshing fruits. Their vibrant colors, sweet aroma, and juicy flesh make them a perfect treat on a hot summer day. However, cutting and peeling a mango can be a challenge for many people. The slippery skin and large seed can make it difficult to handle. In this guide, we will show you how to safely cut and peel a mango so you can enjoy its sweet and juicy goodness without any hassle.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the necessary tools on hand. You will need a sharp knife, a cutting board, and a spoon. The sharp knife will help you slice through the mango’s tough skin, while the spoon will be used to scoop out the flesh.

Step 2: Choose a Ripe Mango

Selecting a ripe mango is crucial for a delicious eating experience. Look for mangoes that are slightly soft to the touch and have a fruity aroma. Avoid mangoes that are overly soft or have any signs of mold or discoloration.

Step 3: Wash the Mango

Before you start cutting, wash the mango under cold running water to remove any dirt or pesticides from the skin. Pat it dry with a clean towel.

Step 4: Remove the Stem

Hold the mango upright with the stem facing upwards. Using a sharp knife, slice off the stem, just above the top of the mango.

Step 5: Slice Off the Cheeks

With the mango still upright, make two vertical cuts on either side of the pit. These cuts should be about 1/4 inch away from the center. You should end up with two large mango “cheeks.”

Step 6: Score the Flesh

Hold one mango cheek in your hand, skin side down. Using a sharp knife, make lengthwise and crosswise cuts into the flesh, being careful not to cut through the skin. This will create a checkerboard pattern.

Step 7: Scoop Out the Flesh

Hold the scored mango cheek over a bowl or plate. Using a spoon, scoop out the flesh, sliding the spoon between the skin and the flesh. Be gentle to avoid squishing the mango.

Step 8: Repeat with the Other Cheek

Repeat steps 6 and 7 with the other mango cheek. You can also repeat the process with the remaining sides of the mango if desired, but be cautious of the pit in the center.

Step 9: Cut the Remaining Flesh from the Pit

To get every last bit of mango flesh, hold the pit firmly and carefully slice off any remaining fruit. Be cautious while doing this to avoid any accidents.

Step 10: Enjoy Your Mango!

Now that you have successfully cut and peeled your mango, it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor. You can eat the mango as is, add it to smoothies, salads, or desserts, or even make mango salsa. The possibilities are endless!

Conclusion

Cutting and peeling a mango doesn’t have to be a daunting task. By following these simple steps, you can safely and easily enjoy the sweet and juicy fruit. Remember to choose a ripe mango, wash it thoroughly, and use the right tools. With a little practice, you’ll become a mango-cutting pro in no time. So go ahead, grab a mango, and indulge in its tropical goodness!
