Korean Beef Soup (Galbitang) Recipe: A Comforting Classic


If you’re looking to bring warmth and comfort to your dining table, Korean Beef Soup, or Galbitang, may be the perfect dish to try. This deeply flavorful traditional soup is prized for its rich broth and tender beef short ribs. Whether you’re coming in from a chilly day or simply craving a hearty, nourishing meal, Galbitang delivers a soul-soothing experience that has been cherished in Korean households for generations.

Its robust flavors, nourishing ingredients, and comforting aroma make this dish truly special. The best part? It’s a meal fit for any occasion—whether it’s a quiet weeknight dinner or a celebratory feast. Let’s dive into everything you need to know about this savory classic and how to make it right in your kitchen.

What Is Galbitang?

Galbitang, which directly translates to “short rib soup,” is a Korean dish traditionally made with beef short ribs simmered in water until the flavors meld together into a clear, delicate, and nourishing broth. It’s often seasoned with garlic, green onions, soy sauce, and a touch of sesame oil for an aromatic lift.

Historically, Galbitang was considered a dish for special occasions due to the prized ingredient—beef short ribs. Today, it remains a popular choice for holidays, family gatherings, or whenever people crave a warm, comforting bowl of homemade soup. Its appeal lies in the balance between simplicity and depth of flavor, making it a dish people of all cooking skill levels can enjoy preparing.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Galbitang’s beauty lies in its versatility and health benefits. Not only is it packed with protein from the beef and nutrients from fresh vegetables, but it’s also a dish that accommodates personal preferences. You can adjust the seasoning to match your desired level of saltiness, add more vegetables to make it heartier, or customize it with toppings such as shredded egg, green onions, or glass noodles.

Beyond its nutritious ingredients, Galbitang is a perfect make-ahead dish, as the flavor deepens as it sits. The cooking process itself requires some time, but the steps are straightforward, and the results are always worth it. It’s also naturally gluten-free, and with a few substitutions, it can easily be made dairy-free as well.

Ingredients for Galbitang

To prepare this Korean short rib soup, you’ll need a mix of fresh ingredients and pantry staples. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Broth:

  • Beef short ribs (2-3 pounds): The star of the dish, these ribs provide the hearty meat and marrow that make the broth rich and flavorful.
  • Water (10-12 cups): The foundation for the soup.
  • Radish (1 medium, Korean radish or daikon): Adds a natural sweetness and balances the richness of the broth.
  • Garlic (6-8 cloves, peeled): Essential for depth of flavor.
  • Onion (1 medium): Provides a subtle aromatic sweetness to the soup.

For Seasoning:

  • Soy sauce (2-3 tablespoons): For seasoning and an added layer of umami.
  • Salt (to taste): Enhances the natural flavors.
  • Ground black pepper (1 teaspoon): Adds a gentle kick.
  • Green onion (2-3 stalks, finely chopped): Offers freshness.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Glass noodles (dangmyeon): For added texture.
  • Egg (1, beaten or shredded for garnish): Optional but highly recommended.
  • Sesame oil (1 teaspoon): A drizzle for nuttiness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef Short Ribs

Start by preparing the short ribs. This step ensures both the tenderness of the meat and the clarity of the broth. Rinse the short ribs under cold running water to remove any excess blood or impurities. Then, soak them in cold water for about an hour, changing the water once or twice. This process will also help rid the meat of excess blood, ensuring a cleaner broth.

After soaking, place the rinsed ribs in a large pot, cover them with water, and bring it to a rapid boil for about 10 minutes. As it boils, you’ll likely notice foam and impurities rise to the surface. Skim this off. Once done, discard the water and rinse both the pot and the short ribs thoroughly.

Step 2: Build the Broth Base

In the clean pot, add the blanched short ribs, peeled garlic cloves, a peeled onion, and the Korean radish (sliced into large chunks). Add about 10-12 cups of water, making sure the ribs are fully submerged. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 2-3 hours.

During simmering, skim off any foam or oil that collects on the surface. This step ensures your broth stays clear. The longer the soup simmers, the more flavorful and tender the short ribs will become, so patience is key here.

Step 3: Season the Soup

Once the broth has developed its signature richness and the ribs are fork-tender, remove the onion and radish chunks. You can discard the onion, but slice the radish into smaller pieces and return them to the pot. Season the broth with soy sauce, salt, and black pepper to your taste. Season gradually to avoid overpowering the natural flavor of the beef.

If you plan to add glass noodles, soak them in cold water for 10-15 minutes, then cook them separately according to their package instructions. Once cooked, add them to the soup during the final stages of cooking.

Step 4: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the soup into bowls, making sure to include a few short ribs, radish slices, and noodles (if using) in each serving. Garnish with chopped green onions and, if desired, shredded egg strips. Finish with a drizzle of sesame oil for a nutty finish.

Serve Galbitang hot, usually with a side of steamed rice and traditional Korean banchan (side dishes) like kimchi, pickled radish, or seasoned spinach.

Tips for the Best Galbitang

  1. Blanch the Short Ribs: Don’t skip the initial step of boiling and rinsing the ribs. This removes impurities and ensures a clear, clean broth.
  2. Use Quality Ingredients: Fresh garlic, high-quality beef, and Korean radish elevate the overall flavor.
  3. Simmer Slowly: Low and slow cooking helps extract all the rich flavors from the beef and bones.
  4. Adjust Seasoning Gradually: Soy sauce and salt enhance the flavor but shouldn’t dominate it, so add them incrementally.
  5. Make It Ahead: The soup tastes even better after sitting for a day, so consider making it in advance and reheating it.

Health Benefits of Galbitang

Galbitang is not only a comforting meal but also a highly nutritious one. The beef short ribs provide high-quality protein, while the broth, rich in collagen and minerals from the bones, is excellent for joint and skin health. Garlic is well-known for its immune-boosting properties, and radish aids in digestion. The soup itself is light yet hearty, making it an excellent choice for those who need a wholesome, stomach-soothing meal.

Celebrate the Essence of Korean Comfort Food

Galbitang embodies everything a comforting meal should be: aromatic, hearty, and deeply satisfying. While it may require time to prepare, the process is simple enough for anyone to try. The result is a bowl of soup that not only nourishes the body but also warms the heart.

Whether it’s your first time exploring Korean cuisine or you’ve been a fan for years, this Korean Short Rib Soup will undoubtedly earn a spot in your recipe rotation. Try it for your next family meal or when you’re looking to impress your dinner guests—they’ll surely be coming back for seconds. Happy cooking!
