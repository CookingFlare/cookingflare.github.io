Sous Vide Chicken and Mushroom Risotto: Creamy and Delicious


Risotto, a classic Italian dish, is a staple in many cuisines around the world. Its creamy texture and rich flavor profile make it a perfect accompaniment to a variety of ingredients. In this recipe, we’ll combine the tender and juicy sous vide chicken with the earthy flavor of mushrooms and the comforting warmth of risotto. The result is a dish that is both familiar and innovative, perfect for a cozy dinner or a special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and button)
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, warmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

The key to this recipe is the sous vide chicken, which ensures a tender and juicy texture that’s hard to achieve with traditional cooking methods. Begin by seasoning the chicken with salt and pepper.

Next, heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Add the Arborio rice to the saucepan and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice is coated in oil and slightly toasted. Add the white wine (if using) and cook until the liquid is almost completely absorbed, stirring frequently.

Add 1 cup of warmed broth to the saucepan and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is mostly absorbed. Repeat this process, adding the broth in 1-cup increments and waiting for it to be absorbed before adding more. This process should take about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and creamy.

While the risotto is cooking, prepare the mushrooms. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat and add the sliced mushrooms. Cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Once the risotto is cooked, stir in the Parmesan cheese and cook until melted and creamy. Add the cooked mushrooms to the risotto and stir to combine.

To cook the sous vide chicken, preheat the sous vide water bath to 130°F (54°C). Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then place it in a sous vide bag with any desired aromatics (such as thyme or lemon slices). Seal the bag and cook for 1-2 hours, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

Remove the chicken from the sous vide bag and slice it into thin strips. Add the chicken to the risotto and stir to combine.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add some sautéed asparagus or bell peppers to the risotto.
  • If you prefer a creamier risotto, add some grated Parmesan cheese to the saucepan during the cooking process.
  • To make this dish more substantial, add some cooked and shredded chicken or pork to the risotto.
  • Experiment with different types of mushrooms, such as oyster or chanterelle, for unique flavor profiles.

Conclusion

This sous vide chicken and mushroom risotto is a dish that’s sure to impress. The tender and juicy chicken, combined with the creamy and comforting risotto, is a match made in heaven. The earthy flavor of the mushrooms adds depth and complexity to the dish, making it perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds will thank you.
