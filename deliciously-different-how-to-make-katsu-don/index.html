Deliciously Different: How to Make Katsu Don


Katsu Don is a beloved Japanese dish that merges the crispiness of katsu (breaded and fried meat cutlet) with a comforting savory rice bowl, creating a harmony of textures and flavors that is hard to beat. Perfect for any day of the week, Katsu Don is an ideal choice for both novice cooks and seasoned chefs looking for a new culinary adventure. This post will take you through the step-by-step process of creating this delicious dish, highlighting important tips, variations, and even a bit of history behind it.

Ingredients You’ll Need To prepare Katsu Don, you will need the following ingredients: For the Katsu:

2 boneless pork cutlets (or chicken breast) Salt and pepper, to taste 1 cup all-purpose flour 2 eggs, beaten 1.5 cups panko breadcrumbs Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Sauce:

1 cup dashi stock (or chicken broth) 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon mirin 1 tablespoon sugar 2 eggs 1 tablespoon chopped scallions (for garnish)

For Serving:

2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice Additional scallions or other toppings as desired

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Katsu Don Step 1: Preparing the Rice Begin by washing the short-grain rice under running cold water until the water runs clear. This process removes excess starch and ensures fluffy rice.

Rinse the rice thoroughly and let it soak in water for about 30 minutes. This helps to soften the grains and achieve the desired texture. Drain the soaked rice and transfer it to a rice cooker with the appropriate amount of water (usually a 1:1 ratio of rice to water, but depend on your rice cooker’s instructions). Cook the rice until fluffy, usually around 30 minutes. Once done, let it sit for an additional 10 minutes before fluffing it with a fork.

Step 2: Preparing the Katsu While the rice is cooking, you can prepare the katsu:

Start by tenderizing the pork cutlets. Lightly pound them to an even thickness of about 1/2 inch. This ensures even cooking and helps to make the cutlets tender. Season both sides of the cutlets with salt and pepper. Set up a breading station: Place flour in one shallow dish. Pour the beaten eggs into a second dish. Spread the panko breadcrumbs in a third dish.

Dredge each cutlet in flour, shaking off excess. Dip into the beaten eggs, ensuring they are fully coated. Finally, coat the cutlets with panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere and ensure an even crust.

Step 3: Frying the Katsu Now it’s time to cook the katsu:

In a large frying pan or deep skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat until shimmering (about 350°F or 175°C). Carefully place the breaded cutlets in the hot oil, cooking until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes on each side. Once cooked, transfer the katsu to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Allow them to rest while you prepare the sauce.

Step 4: Creating the Sauce The sauce is key to tying together the flavors in this dish.

In a separate pan, combine dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture comes to a gentle simmer. After about 2-3 minutes, carefully crack the eggs into the sauce, whisking gently to incorporate. Let the mixture simmer for an additional 2 minutes until the eggs are slightly set but still tender.

Step 5: Assembling the Katsu Don Now comes the exciting part — assembling your Katsu Don!

Take a serving bowl and spoon a generous amount of fluffy rice into the bottom. Slice the crispy katsu into strips and place them confidently atop the rice. Pour the egg-and-sauce mixture over the katsu, allowing some to trickle down to the rice for added flavor. Finish with a sprinkle of chopped scallions as a garnish.

Serving Suggestions Katsu Don is fantastic on its own, but you can enhance the experience with a few additional touches:

Pickled Vegetables: A side of pickled vegetables can provide a refreshing contrast to the savory aspects of Katsu Don. Japanese Salad: A light salad featuring sesame dressing complements the richness of the dish and adds some freshness. Miso Soup: Pairing your Katsu Don with a warm bowl of miso soup rounds out the meal, providing a comforting and traditional touch.

Variations to Explore While the classic pork or chicken katsu is unbeatable, feel free to experiment with these variations:

Vegetable Katsu Don: Swap out meat for hearty vegetables like eggplant or zucchini, breaded and fried for a delightful vegetarian version. Spicy Katsu Don: Add a touch of heat by incorporating chili oil or sriracha into the sauce for a spicy kick. Cheese Katsu Don: For cheese lovers, try adding a slice of melty cheese under the katsu for an indulgent twist. Seafood Katsu Don: Substitute the meat with shrimp, squid, or fish fillets for a delightful seafood iteration.

Tips for Perfecting Your Katsu Don

Oil Temperature: Maintaining the correct oil temperature is crucial for achieving the perfect katsu. Too hot will result in burnt breadcrumbs, while too low will make the katsu greasy. Rest Time: Allow your fried katsu to sit for a few minutes before cutting to retain moisture and enhance the texture. Fresh Ingredients: Using fresh dashi stock and high-quality ingredients will elevate the flavor of your Katsu Don significantly. Cooking Time: Don’t rush the process. Cooking the katsu until golden brown ensures a perfect crunch and enhances the overall dish.

History of Katsu Don Katsu Don originated in Japan during the Meiji period (1868-1912), a time when Western influence began to permeate Japanese cuisine. The word "katsu" itself derives from the Japanese term "katsuretsu," meaning cutlet. The dish gained popularity as a fast yet fulfilling meal, particularly among students and workers. Katsu Don embodies the essence of comfort food with its crispy texture and warming flavors, symbolizing the blending of Western and Japanese culinary traditions.

Final Thoughts Katsu Don is a dish that brings comfort and joy with every bite. The harmony of crispy katsu, savory sauce, and fluffy rice is not only delightful but also incredibly satisfying. Whether enjoyed on a casual weeknight or served as a centerpiece for a gathering, Katsu Don promises to impress. With this recipe and guidance, you have all the tools you need to craft your own deliciously different Katsu Don at home. Enjoy the cooking journey and savor the heartwarming flavors of this Japanese classic!
