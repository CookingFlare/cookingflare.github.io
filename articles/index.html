In this blog post, we will delve into the fascinating history of British curries and explore six classic recipes that showcase the diversity and depth of this beloved cuisine. A Brief History of British Curries The origins of British curry are often shrouded in mystery, but historians agree that the dish was first introduced to the UK by Indian sailors and traders who traveled to Britain during the colonial era. The first written record of curry in British cuisine dates back to 1772, when a recipe for "curry" was published in a book called "The Art of Cookery Made Plain and Easy" by Hannah Glasse. This early recipe used a mixture of spices, including cumin, coriander, and turmeric, to create a flavorful sauce for meat and vegetables. In the early 19th century, British expatriates and members of the upper class who had traveled to India developed a taste for the rich and aromatic curries they encountered during their travels. Upon their return to the UK, they introduced their own version of curry to their friends and family, using a combination of spices and cooking techniques they had learned during their time in India. This pioneering effort helped to popularize curry in Britain, and by the mid-19th century, it had become a staple of British cuisine.
