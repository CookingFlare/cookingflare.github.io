Baking with Edible Flowers: Beautiful and Tasty Baked Creations


Have you ever thought about incorporating edible flowers into your baking creations? Not only do edible flowers add a pop of color and elegance to your dishes, but they also introduce unique flavors and aromas that can take your baked goods to the next level. From delicate lavender-infused cakes to vibrant hibiscus macarons, the possibilities are endless when it comes to baking with edible flowers. In this blog post, we’ll explore the wonderful world of edible flowers and share some delightful recipes for you to try at home.

Understanding Edible Flowers

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to understand edible flowers. Not all flowers are safe to eat, so it’s essential to use only flowers that are labeled as edible. Popular edible flowers include roses, lavender, violets, hibiscus, and chamomile. These flowers not only look beautiful but also offer subtle flavors that can enhance your baked treats.

Lavender Lemon Drizzle Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon dried lavender flowers
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the lemon zest and dried lavender flowers.
  4. Gradually mix in the flour and milk until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. While the cake is baking, prepare the drizzle by mixing the lemon juice and powdered sugar.
  7. Once the cake is done, poke holes on the surface and pour the lemon drizzle over the cake.
  8. Let the cake cool before slicing and serving.

Hibiscus Rose Macarons

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 3 egg whites
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon hibiscus powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water
  • Pink food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, sift together the almond flour, powdered sugar, and hibiscus powder.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until foamy, then gradually add the granulated sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.
  4. Fold in the almond flour mixture, rose water, and pink food coloring until the batter is smooth and shiny.
  5. Transfer the batter to a piping bag and pipe small circles onto the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Let the macarons sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to form a skin.
  7. Bake the macarons for 15-18 minutes or until set.
  8. Once cooled, sandwich the macarons with your favorite filling.

In Conclusion

Baking with edible flowers is a delightful way to add a touch of whimsy and elegance to your baked creations. Whether you’re hosting a tea party or simply want to impress your loved ones, incorporating edible flowers into your desserts is sure to make a statement. So next time you’re in the kitchen, don’t be afraid to experiment with these beautiful and tasty floral delights. Happy baking!

Remember, the key to successful baking with edible flowers is using fresh, organic flowers that are free from pesticides. Be sure to wash the flowers thoroughly before use, and always start with a small amount to test the flavor intensity. With a bit of creativity and a dash of floral inspiration, you’ll be well on your way to creating stunning floral treats that are as delicious as they are beautiful. Enjoy the process and savor the sweet floral notes in every bite!

I hope you enjoyed these delightful baking recipes with edible flowers. Let me know if you have any questions or if you’d like to explore more floral-infused creations. Happy baking!
