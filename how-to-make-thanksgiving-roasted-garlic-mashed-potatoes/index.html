How to Make Thanksgiving Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and, above all else, a feast that leaves everyone feeling cozy and satisfied. One dish that’s an absolute must-have on your Thanksgiving table is mashed potatoes—a creamy, comforting side that pairs beautifully with turkey, stuffing, gravy, and all the fixings. But this year, why not elevate this traditional dish by incorporating the rich, nutty flavor of roasted garlic? Roasted garlic mashed potatoes add depth and sophistication to a classic recipe, making them an unforgettable addition to your holiday spread.

In this detailed guide, we’ll walk you through how to make Thanksgiving roasted garlic mashed potatoes that are creamy, indulgent, and bursting with flavor. Each step is carefully outlined to ensure your dish turns out perfect every time. Whether you’re hosting a feast or attending a potluck, this recipe is bound to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

Before diving into the cooking process, gather all the ingredients to ensure a smoother cooking experience. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Mashed Potatoes:

  • 3 lbs. of potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet are ideal for their texture and flavor)
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 stick of unsalted butter (cut into cubes)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Roasted Garlic:

  • 1 large head of garlic
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • Salt

Optional Ingredients (for extra flavor):

  • Fresh chives for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Sour cream for added creaminess

The Perfect Potatoes: Which Type to Choose

Choosing the right type of potato is essential for achieving a creamy and flavorful mashed potato dish. Yukon Gold potatoes are often preferred for their naturally buttery taste and silky texture. Russet potatoes, on the other hand, have a starchier consistency and fluff up beautifully when mashed. If you’re looking for ultimate creaminess, feel free to use a combination of both.

Once you’ve selected your potatoes, make sure they’re fresh and firm to the touch. Avoid using any with green spots or sprouting.

How to Roast Garlic

Roasting garlic is the secret to transforming ordinary mashed potatoes into an exceptional dish. Roasted garlic offers a mild, caramelized sweetness that adds depth and complexity without overpowering the other flavors.

Steps for Roasting Garlic:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Garlic: Remove the outer layers of the garlic head, leaving the individual cloves intact. Slice off the top of the bulb (about 1/4 inch) to expose the tops of the cloves.
  3. Season and Wrap: Place the garlic head on a sheet of aluminum foil, drizzle it with olive oil, and sprinkle a pinch of salt on top. Wrap the garlic tightly in the foil.
  4. Roast: Put the wrapped garlic directly on the oven rack or on a baking tray. Roast for 35–40 minutes until the cloves are soft, golden-brown, and fragrant.
  5. Cool and Extract: Allow the roasted garlic to cool for a few minutes before squeezing the softened cloves out of their skins. You should end up with a paste-like consistency. Set aside.

Preparing the Potatoes

Steps to Cook the Potatoes:

  1. Peel and Cut: Peel the potatoes and cut them into evenly sized chunks to ensure even cooking. If you prefer a rustic texture, you can leave the skins on, but make sure to scrub them thoroughly first.
  2. Boil: Place the potato chunks in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a generous pinch of salt to the water to season the potatoes from the inside out. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 15–20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Drain: Once cooked, drain the potatoes thoroughly and return them to the pot. Allow them to sit for a minute, uncovered, so any excess water can evaporate.

Creating the Creamiest Mashed Potatoes

Steps for Mashing:

  1. Mash by Hand or Use a Ricer: If you prefer smooth mashed potatoes, use a potato ricer for perfectly fluffy results. For a chunkier texture, stick with a traditional hand masher.
  2. Heat the Milk and Butter: In a small saucepan, heat the milk, heavy cream, and butter together over medium heat until the mixture is warm and the butter is fully melted. Heating these ingredients prevents the mashed potatoes from cooling down too quickly.
  3. Combine: Slowly pour the warm milk mixture into the potatoes while mashing, mixing thoroughly to incorporate. Add it in increments to ensure you achieve the desired consistency.
  4. Add Roasted Garlic: Fold the roasted garlic paste into the mashed potatoes, stirring until evenly distributed. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Optional Enhancements

Thanksgiving roasted garlic mashed potatoes are wonderful on their own, but adding a few extra ingredients can elevate them even further:

  • Fresh Chives: Chop fresh chives and sprinkle them over the mashed potatoes for a burst of freshness and color.
  • Grated Parmesan Cheese: Stir in some Parmesan for a nutty, umami-rich flavor.
  • Sour Cream: For an extra layer of creaminess, mix in a couple of tablespoons of sour cream.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the roasted garlic mashed potatoes in a large, warm bowl and create a small well in the center. Melt a pat of butter into the well for a simple but elegant presentation. Garnish with fresh chives or parsley for a pop of color.

These mashed potatoes pair perfectly with Thanksgiving staples like roasted turkey, cranberry sauce, green bean casserole, and gravy. You can also double the recipe if you’re serving a larger crowd—trust me, these potatoes will go fast!

Tips for Make-Ahead Success

Because Thanksgiving can feel like a whirlwind of cooking, making your roasted garlic mashed potatoes ahead of time can help streamline your process. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Prepare and Refrigerate: Once the mashed potatoes are made, transfer them to an airtight container. Keep them refrigerated for up to two days.
  2. Reheat with Care: When ready to serve, reheat the potatoes over low heat in a pot, adding a splash of milk or cream to loosen the consistency. Stir frequently to prevent sticking. You can also reheat them in the oven in a covered dish at 350°F (175°C).
  3. Refresh Flavors: Stir in a little extra butter and roasted garlic before serving to rejuvenate the flavors.

The Perfect Thanksgiving Side

Thanksgiving roasted garlic mashed potatoes are sure to be the highlight of your holiday meal. Their creamy texture, buttery richness, and roasted garlic undertone make them an irresistible side that complements the festive flavors of Thanksgiving. Not only are they easy to prepare, but they also offer a sophisticated twist on classic mashed potatoes—one that your guests will rave about long after dinner’s over.

Make this recipe part of your Thanksgiving tradition, and don’t be surprised if these mashed potatoes steal the spotlight from the turkey!
