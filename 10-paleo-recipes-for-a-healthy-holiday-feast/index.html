10 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy Holiday Feast


When the holidays roll around, the aroma of roasted meats, spiced sides, and decadent desserts fills every home. It’s a time for indulgence, love, and delicious feasts. But sticking to healthy eating habits doesn’t mean you have to miss out on enjoying those classic flavors. If you follow the Paleo diet—or simply want to serve a wholesome and satisfying menu for your holiday gathering—this collection of 10 recipes will keep things festive, flavorful, and guilt-free.

These paleo-friendly recipes focus on whole, nutrient-rich ingredients without sacrificing the classic holiday experience. From hearty mains to indulgent desserts, we’ve got you covered to throw the perfect holiday feast!

1. Herb-Crusted Prime Rib Roast

Nothing says holiday centerpiece like a perfectly cooked prime rib roast. This juicy, flavorful dish is seasoned with a simple, yet stunning, herb crust.

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs grass-fed bone-in prime rib
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tbsp sea salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/3 cup olive oil

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F.
  2. Rub the prime rib roast with olive oil. Combine minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper, and massage the mixture evenly over the roast.
  3. Place the roast in a roasting pan, bone side down.
  4. Roast at 450°F for 20 minutes to sear, then reduce the temperature to 325°F and cook for 1.5-2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (medium-rare).
  5. Let the roast rest for 15-20 minutes before carving and serving.

2. Spiced Butternut Squash Soup

Warm, velvety, and brimming with fall flavors, this butternut squash soup is the perfect starter for your holiday meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 coconut milk can
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil

Directions

  1. Heat avocado oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the butternut squash, cinnamon, and nutmeg, stirring to coat. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, until the squash is tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to purée the soup until smooth, then stir in coconut milk for creaminess.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve warm.

3. Maple-Glazed Carrots

These vibrant, tender carrots with a hint of maple sweetness make for a beautiful and nutrient-rich side.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs carrots, peeled and sliced lengthwise
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp sea salt

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
  2. Arrange the carrots on a baking sheet. Melt ghee and whisk in maple syrup, ginger, and salt before pouring over the carrots. Toss to coat.
  3. Roast for 25 minutes, tossing halfway through, until the carrots are caramelized and tender.

4. Cranberry-Orange Relish

Skip the store-bought sauce filled with sugar and additives. Make this tart, bright cranberry-orange relish to complement your main dish perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh cranberries
  • 1 medium orange, peeled and segmented
  • ¼ cup raw honey
  • ½ tsp cinnamon

Directions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a food processor.
  2. Pulse until coarsely chopped, or process longer for a smoother texture.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to let the flavors meld.

5. Cauliflower Mashed “Potatoes”

Mashed cauliflower is a creamy, low-carb alternative to traditional mashed potatoes, perfect for a Paleo feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp ghee or grass-fed butter
  • 1/3 cup coconut milk
  • Fresh chives, chopped (optional)

Directions

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, about 10 minutes.
  2. Add the steamed cauliflower, garlic, ghee, and coconut milk to a food processor. Blend until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and top with fresh chives.

6. Savory Almond Flour Stuffing

This hearty paleo stuffing is loaded with veggies, herbs, and protein for a satisfying side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, chopped
  • ½ lb ground pork or sausage (sugar-free)
  • 2 eggs, whisked
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground pork until browned. Add the onions, celery, and mushrooms, sautéing until softened.
  3. Remove from heat, mix in the almond flour, eggs, and herbs, and combine well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish and bake for 25 minutes.

7. Honey-Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

If you need to win over your family on Brussels sprouts, this recipe featuring crispy bacon and a touch of honey will do the trick.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices nitrate-free bacon, chopped
  • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil and honey. Arrange on a baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle the chopped bacon over the Brussels sprouts.
  4. Roast for 25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until crispy and caramelized.

8. Garlic and Lemon Green Beans

Simple and fresh, these green beans bring a pop of brightness to your feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb green beans, trimmed
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Directions

  1. Steam the green beans for 3-5 minutes, until tender-crisp.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  3. Toss the green beans in the garlic oil and drizzle with lemon juice before serving.

9. Paleo Pumpkin Pie

No holiday meal is complete without dessert. This paleo pumpkin pie is creamy, spiced, and entirely grain-free.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ¼ cup raw honey
  • 1 ½ cups pumpkin purée
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ginger
  • ½ tsp nutmeg

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
  2. Mix almond flour, coconut oil, and 1 tbsp honey to make the crust, then press it into a pie dish.
  3. In a bowl, mix pumpkin purée, remaining honey, eggs, coconut milk, and spices. Pour into the crust.
  4. Bake for 45 minutes, or until set and golden.

10. Dark Chocolate Coconut Bites

A slightly indulgent yet clean treat to end the evening on a sweet note.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 3 tbsp coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tbsp raw honey
  • ½ cup dark chocolate (75% or higher), melted

Directions

  1. Combine the shredded coconut, coconut oil, and honey, and form small balls. Refrigerate until firm.
  2. Dip each ball into melted chocolate to coat, then refrigerate again until set.

With these paleo-friendly recipes, you can enjoy a holiday feast that’s delicious and nourishing without compromising your health goals. Each dish combines traditional holiday flavors with clean, natural ingredients, ensuring everyone at the table will be satisfied. This holiday season, celebrate deliciously and wholesomely!
