Thanksgiving Brunch Recipes for a Cozy Morning


Thanksgiving is a time for gratitude, family gatherings, and—of course—great food. While Thanksgiving dinner often takes center stage, the morning brunch holds an undeniable charm. It’s the perfect opportunity to kick off the holiday with a delicious spread that’s warm, comforting, and just festive enough to set the tone for the day. This year, why not elevate your Thanksgiving morning with a brunch menu that reflects the cozy vibe of the season? From savory dishes to sweet treats, these recipes are designed to delight everyone gathered around the table.

1. Sweet Potato and Sage Breakfast Casserole

For those looking to incorporate classic Thanksgiving flavors into their brunch, this Sweet Potato and Sage Breakfast Casserole is an autumnal masterpiece. Creamy sweet potatoes offer a hint of natural sweetness, while earthy sage provides a depth of flavor that feels right at home during Thanksgiving celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tsp ground sage
  • ½ cup shredded Gruyère or cheddar cheese
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp pepper
  • Olive oil for roasting
  • Optional: cooked and crumbled breakfast sausage or bacon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread the sweet potatoes on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and roast until tender (about 25 minutes).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, ground sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grease a 9x13 baking dish. Layer the roasted sweet potatoes in the dish, followed by any optional cooked sausage or bacon.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the sweet potatoes and sprinkle cheese on top.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes until the casserole is set and golden brown on top. Serve hot.

2. Apple Cinnamon Pancakes with Maple Butter

Nothing says cozy like the scent of apple and cinnamon wafting through your kitchen. These pancakes are the essence of fall and pair beautifully with a dollop of maple butter.

Ingredients:

For the Pancakes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 apple, grated

For the Maple Butter:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tbsp pure maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat eggs, milk, and brown sugar. Fold in the grated apple and dry ingredients until combined.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and cook pancakes until golden on each side.
  4. For the maple butter, combine the softened butter and maple syrup in a bowl and mix until creamy. Serve the pancakes warm with the maple butter.

3. Pumpkin Spice Waffles

Classic flavors of pumpkin spice meet the crisp texture of homemade waffles in a recipe that will have everyone asking for seconds. These waffles are ideal served with whipped cream or a sprinkle of candied pecans.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp pumpkin spice
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • ¼ cup melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your waffle iron and grease lightly with oil or cooking spray.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together dry ingredients: flour, sugar, pumpkin spice, baking powder, and baking soda.
  3. In another bowl, mix wet ingredients: eggs, pumpkin puree, milk, and melted butter.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients until smooth. Pour the batter into the preheated waffle iron and cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Serve with your favorite toppings.

4. Savory Herb Quiche

This quiche blends creamy eggs, fresh herbs, and nutrient-packed greens to make a savory breakfast option that’s both elegant and comforting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cups fresh spinach or kale
  • ½ cup feta cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped chives
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out your pie crust into a nine-inch pie dish and prick the bottom with a fork.
  2. Sauté the spinach or kale until wilted, then spread it evenly over the pie crust.
  3. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, chives, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Pour the mixture into the crust and top with feta cheese.
  4. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is cooked through and lightly firm. Let it cool for 5 minutes before slicing.

5. Cranberry Orange Scones

Pair your warm cup of coffee or tea with these tender cranberry orange scones, bursting with seasonal flavors. They’re the perfect balance of tangy and sweet, with a crumbly texture that feels like a warm hug.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • ½ cup cold butter, cubed
  • ½ cup fresh cranberries
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup orange juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (204°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, sugar, and orange zest.
  3. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in cranberries.
  4. Quickly mix in heavy cream and orange juice until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface, shape into a round disc, and cut into wedges. Place the scones on the baking sheet and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until golden brown.

6. Pecan Pie Oatmeal

Oatmeal gets a holiday twist in this recipe inspired by the flavors of pecan pie. Warm, nutty, and just sweet enough, it’s an excellent way to infuse holiday spirit into a nutritious breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk or water
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional toppings: drizzle of caramel, whipped cream, or extra pecans

Instructions:

  1. Cook oats in milk or water according to the package instructions.
  2. Stir in brown sugar, cinnamon, vanilla extract, and chopped pecans.
  3. Spoon into bowls and garnish with your favorite toppings. Serve warm.

7. Spiced Chai Latte

No Thanksgiving brunch is complete without a comforting beverage. Add a festive flair to your table with homemade spiced chai lattes that are aromatic, creamy, and just the right amount of indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 black tea bags
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp cardamom
  • ¼ tsp cloves

Instructions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a saucepan and steep the tea bags for 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in milk, sugar, and spices, then reheat over low heat until warmed through.
  3. Strain into mugs and enjoy with your Thanksgiving brunch.

Final Thoughts

Thanksgiving brunch is a wonderful way to bring family and friends together and savor the warmth of the season before the hustle of dinner preparations. Whether you’re preparing savory casseroles, sweet breakfast treats, or cozy beverages, these recipes will make the morning extra special. Take your time, gather around the table, and enjoy the flavors of a holiday filled with joy and gratitude.
