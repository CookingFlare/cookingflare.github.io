Middle Eastern Street Food: 10 Recipes You Can Make at Home


Middle Eastern street food is celebrated for its bold flavors, vibrant spices, and its ability to transport you to bustling marketplaces where each corner emanates the aroma of freshly baked bread, smoky grills, and simmering sauces. If you’ve ever dreamed of recreating those authentic flavors in the comfort of your own kitchen, you’re in luck. Here are 10 remarkable Middle Eastern street food recipes you can prepare at home, giving you a taste of this alluring cuisine without booking a flight.

1. Shawarma Spiced Chicken or Beef Wraps

Shawarma is the cornerstone of Middle Eastern street food. This dish features thinly sliced marinated meat wrapped in soft pita bread with a tangy garlic sauce and fresh vegetables. To make this at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs of chicken thighs or beef strips
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced

Method:

  1. Combine all the marinade ingredients, coat the meat, and let it rest for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Grill or roast the meat until perfectly tender.
  3. Serve wrapped in pita bread with garlic sauce, thinly sliced onions, tomatoes, and pickles for the authentic shawarma experience.

2. Falafel

A vegetarian favorite, falafel is made from ground chickpeas or fava beans blended with fresh herbs and spices. Deep-fried to golden perfection, these crispy balls are typically served in pita bread with a dollop of tahini sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Method:

  1. Blend all ingredients in a food processor until coarse.
  2. Refrigerate for 1 hour to firm up the mixture.
  3. Shape into balls or patties, and deep fry until crispy.
  4. Serve with pita, lettuce, tomatoes, and tahini sauce.

3. Manakish (Middle Eastern Flatbread)

Manakish is a popular Lebanese street food that resembles a Middle Eastern pizza. It’s topped with za’atar, cheeses, or minced meat for a diverse range of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 packet instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

For the topping:

  • 3 tbsp za’atar spice
  • 3 tbsp olive oil

Method:

  1. Combine yeast, warm water, and sugar; let sit for 10 minutes.
  2. Mix with flour and olive oil to form a dough. Knead and let rise for 1 hour.
  3. Roll into discs, spread with za’atar mixed with olive oil.
  4. Bake at 450°F until golden brown.

4. Kefta Kebabs

Kefta is a juicy, grilled kebab made with spiced ground meat, often beef or lamb. These kebabs provide a smoky, juicy flavor that’s hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely grated
  • 1/2 bunch fresh parsley, minced
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Method:

  1. Mix all ingredients, and shape into long, slim sausages on skewers.
  2. Grill over medium heat until cooked through.
  3. Serve with warm flatbread, a side of hummus, or a tangy yogurt sauce.

5. Sfiha (Middle Eastern Meat Pies)

Sfiha, also known as Lebanese meat pies, are savory pastries filled with spiced ground meat. They make for a delicious snack or main course.

Ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 packet yeast

For the filling:

  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tsp seven-spice mix

Method:

  1. Make the dough and let it rise.
  2. Sauté the filling ingredients until cooked.
  3. Roll the dough into small discs, add filling, and fold edges to form a pie.
  4. Bake at 400°F until golden.

6. Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma)

Loved across the Middle East, dolmas are grape leaves stuffed with a fragrant rice mixture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves, rinsed
  • 1 cup short-grain rice
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried mint
  • Juice of 2 lemons

Method:

  1. Combine rice, tomatoes, onions, mint, and olive oil for the filling.
  2. Stuff grape leaves with the mixture and roll tightly.
  3. Arrange tightly in a pot, cover with water and simmer for 45 minutes. Serve cold or warm.

7. Hummus

Hummus needs no introduction — this velvety chickpea dip is a quintessential Middle Eastern staple.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin

Method:

  1. Blend all ingredients until creamy.
  2. Garnish with olive oil, paprika, or parsley.
  3. Serve with pita bread or fresh vegetables.

8. Tabbouleh Salad

A fresh side that accompanies any street food meal perfectly, tabbouleh is made with parsley, bulgur wheat, and fresh vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint
  • 5 tomatoes, diced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • Juice of 3 lemons
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Method:

  1. Soak bulgur in hot water for 10 minutes, then drain.
  2. Mix with parsley, mint, and vegetables.
  3. Toss with lemon juice and olive oil for a refreshing salad.

9. Kunafa (Sweet Cheese Pastry)

For dessert lovers, kunafa is a must-try. This sweet, cheesy pastry is soaked in sugar syrup and topped with finely ground pistachios.

Ingredients:

  • 500g shredded phyllo dough
  • 2 cups sugar syrup
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese or akkawi cheese
  • 1/2 cup butter

Method:

  1. Layer half the phyllo dough in a greased baking dish, drizzle with butter, and add cheese filling.
  2. Top with remaining phyllo dough, bake at 375°F until golden.
  3. Pour sugar syrup over the warm pastry and top with pistachios.

10. Arayes (Stuffed Pita with Meat)

A lesser-known delight, arayes is pita bread loaded with spiced meat and grilled to crispy perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pita breads
  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Method:

  1. Stuff pita pockets with the seasoned meat mixture.
  2. Grill or toast until crispy and meat is fully cooked.
  3. Slice and serve with a side of tahini or a fresh salad.

Bringing Middle Eastern Street Food to Your Table

Middle Eastern street food offers a sensory explosion of flavors, textures, and aromas that undeniably rank among the best comfort foods globally. With these easy-to-follow recipes, you’ll be able to replicate the vibrant tastes of shawarma stalls, falafel stands, and bustling roadside grills right in your own kitchen.

Whether for family dinners, entertaining guests, or exploring new cuisines, these dishes are bound to leave a lasting impression. From savory to sweet, each recipe highlights the diverse culinary heritage of the Middle East while reminding us that food truly connects people across cultures.
