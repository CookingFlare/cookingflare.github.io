How to Make Classic Korean Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu


Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개), a beloved Korean comfort food, is a hearty stew made with fermented kimchi, tofu, and a variety of seasonings that bring warmth and depth of flavor to every spoonful. Whether you’re familiar with Korean cuisine or just beginning to explore its rich and flavorful dishes, this dish is a must-try. Known for its savory, spicy, and slightly tangy taste, Kimchi Jjigae is perfect for any occasion—whether it’s a rainy day or a cozy dinner with family and friends. This simple yet robust stew is not only delicious but also offers numerous health benefits, thanks to the presence of kimchi, which is packed with probiotics and vitamins.

This recipe for Classic Korean Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu is easy to follow and can be prepared in under 45 minutes. The combination of the umami-rich broth, tender tofu, and spicy kimchi creates a flavor explosion in your mouth. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to indulge in a comforting bowl of this classic Korean dish.

Ingredients for Korean Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu

To prepare a delicious pot of Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Broth:

  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced thinly
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated (optional, for added warmth)
  • 1-2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) or gochujang (Korean chili paste) depending on desired spice level
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional but recommended for authentic flavor)
  • 2 cups kimchi (preferably well-fermented)
  • 1½ cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon Korean anchovy broth (optional, but gives a deep umami flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (balances the acidity of the kimchi)

For the Tofu:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, cubed (press out excess moisture if needed)

For Garnishing:

  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • A drizzle of sesame oil (optional for finishing)

Directions for Making Classic Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Before you begin cooking, make sure to gather and prepare all the ingredients. Start by slicing the onion and mincing the garlic. Grate the ginger if you’re using it. Cube the tofu into bite-sized pieces. If you’re using freshly made or store-bought kimchi, be sure it’s fermented and has a nice tang to it—older kimchi works best for this recipe.

Step 2: Start the Broth

Heat the sesame oil in a large pot over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the sliced onions and sauté them until they are soft and translucent. This should take about 4-5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until the garlic becomes fragrant.

Step 3: Add the Kimchi

Add the kimchi to the pot along with the gochugaru (or gochujang). Stir everything together, allowing the kimchi to absorb the flavors of the onions, garlic, and spices. The gochugaru will give your broth that signature Korean red hue, while also enhancing the heat level. Let the kimchi cook down for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it becomes soft and the flavors meld together.

Step 4: Pour in the Liquid

Next, pour in the water (or vegetable broth) and the Korean anchovy broth (if using). Stir to combine. At this point, you should start to see the kimchi and other ingredients forming a flavorful base for your stew. Add the soy sauce and fish sauce for a savory depth of flavor. Sprinkle in a teaspoon of sugar to balance the tanginess of the kimchi. Stir everything well, and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium-high heat.

Step 5: Simmer the Stew

Once the broth comes to a boil, lower the heat to a simmer and let the stew cook for about 20-30 minutes. During this time, the flavors will deepen, and the kimchi will become even more tender. This is also when you’ll want to taste the broth and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you want more spice, feel free to add additional gochugaru or gochujang. For extra saltiness, add a splash more soy sauce or fish sauce.

Step 6: Add the Tofu

After the stew has simmered and the flavors have developed, it’s time to add the tofu. Gently stir in the cubed tofu and allow it to heat through in the broth. Be careful not to break the tofu, as it can be delicate. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, so the tofu absorbs some of the flavorful broth.

Step 7: Final Adjustments and Garnishing

Taste the stew one last time to ensure it’s perfectly seasoned. You can adjust the saltiness or spice level to your preference. Once you’re satisfied with the taste, remove the pot from heat.

To serve, ladle the kimchi jjigae into individual bowls. Garnish with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds. For added richness, you can drizzle a little sesame oil on top for a fragrant finish.

How to Serve Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi Jjigae is best enjoyed hot and served with a bowl of steamed white rice. The heat from the stew combined with the cooling effect of the rice makes for a perfect contrast. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can even serve it with a side of kimchi or pickled vegetables to complement the dish. Korean meals are often served with a variety of banchan (side dishes), so feel free to pair your Kimchi Jjigae with small dishes of sautéed vegetables, seasoned seaweed, or even fried fish cakes for a complete meal.

Variations on Classic Kimchi Jjigae

While the traditional recipe calls for tofu, there are many ways to customize your Kimchi Jjigae. Here are some ideas for variations:

1. Add Pork or Beef

A common variation of Kimchi Jjigae includes adding pork or beef for a heartier stew. Pork belly or shoulder is commonly used, providing a rich and savory flavor. Simply add bite-sized pieces of meat along with the kimchi in step 3 and let it cook with the broth.

2. Vegetarian or Vegan Kimchi Jjigae

If you’re following a vegetarian or vegan diet, it’s important to ensure that your kimchi is free from any animal-based products (such as fish sauce). You can substitute the fish sauce and anchovy broth with a vegan-friendly option like mushroom broth or soy sauce.

3. Add Mushrooms

For an earthy twist, you can incorporate mushrooms such as shiitake, oyster, or button mushrooms into the stew. Simply add them when you add the tofu, and they will cook perfectly in the simmering broth.

4. Include Zucchini or Sweet Potatoes

Adding vegetables like zucchini or sweet potatoes can add a touch of sweetness and texture to your stew. Chop them into bite-sized pieces and include them when adding the tofu. These vegetables will absorb the flavor of the broth and complement the tanginess of the kimchi.

Health Benefits of Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi Jjigae is not only delicious but also nutritious. The fermented kimchi provides beneficial probiotics, which can support gut health and boost the immune system. Tofu is an excellent source of plant-based protein, making the dish both filling and heart-healthy. Additionally, the broth contains various spices like garlic and ginger, which have anti-inflammatory properties and may help in digestion.

Final Thoughts on Korean Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu

Kimchi Jjigae with tofu is a comforting, flavorful, and versatile dish that captures the essence of Korean home-cooked meals. Its combination of tangy, spicy, and savory flavors creates a balance that’s truly satisfying. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or sharing with loved ones, this dish is bound to impress. Easy to make, customizable to your taste, and packed with nutrients, Kimchi Jjigae should become a staple in your kitchen.

Now that you know how to make this classic Korean stew, give it a try next time you’re craving something hearty and flavorful. Enjoy the cozy warmth and deep flavors of this timeless dish—perfect for any season, but especially comforting on a cold day.
