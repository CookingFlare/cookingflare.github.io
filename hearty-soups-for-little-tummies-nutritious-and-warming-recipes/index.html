Hearty Soups for Little Tummies: Nutritious and Warming Recipes


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing quite like a hearty bowl of soup to warm you up from the inside out. And when it comes to feeding our little ones, soups are not only comforting but also a great way to pack in essential nutrients. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some delicious and nutritious soup recipes that are perfect for little tummies.

1. Creamy Carrot and Sweet Potato Soup

This creamy and vibrant soup is not only visually appealing but also packed with vitamins A and C, as well as fiber. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped carrots and sweet potato to the pot, and cook for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the soup until smooth. If using a regular blender, be sure to allow the soup to cool slightly before blending.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper to taste. Heat the soup for a few more minutes until warmed through.
  6. Serve the soup hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh herbs or a dollop of yogurt.

2. Chicken and Vegetable Noodle Soup

This classic soup is a favorite among kids and adults alike. Packed with protein from the chicken and a medley of vegetables, it’s a complete meal in a bowl. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup egg noodles
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the mixed vegetables to the pot and cook for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Add the shredded chicken and egg noodles, and simmer for about 10 minutes or until the noodles are cooked through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh parsley or dill.

3. Lentil and Spinach Soup

Lentils are an excellent source of protein and fiber, making them a fantastic addition to any soup. This lentil and spinach soup is not only nutritious but also incredibly flavorful. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, carrot, and celery, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the rinsed lentils and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the lentils are tender.
  3. Stir in the chopped spinach and cumin, and cook for an additional 5 minutes until the spinach wilts.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the soup hot, accompanied by crusty bread.

Conclusion

These hearty soups are not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients to keep little tummies happy and healthy. Whether you’re looking for a creamy and colorful option or a classic chicken noodle soup, these recipes are sure to please even the pickiest eaters. So grab a ladle, get cooking, and warm up your little ones’ tummies with these nutritious and comforting soups.
