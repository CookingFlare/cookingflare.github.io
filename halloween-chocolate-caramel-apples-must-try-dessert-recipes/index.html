Halloween Chocolate Caramel Apples: Must-try Dessert Recipes


Isn’t there something undeniably magical about Halloween? The ghoulish makeup, haunting decorations, eerie sounds, and of course, the best part—Mmm…treats! To kick off this year’s Halloween celebration, let’s spice things up with spooky fun and incorporate a delectable twist on a classic favorite: the Chocolate Caramel Apple. It’s the perfect treat to woo your trick-or-treaters or delight your dessert-loving friends during Halloween parties. Grab your apples, and let’s get icing those tasty delights!

Ingredients for Chocolate Caramel Apples:

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples (preferably firm, most likely Granny Smith or Fuji)
  • 2 cups October gold chocolate chips
  • 1 bag of Salted Caramel Turtles or homemade salted caramel
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

Equipment:

  • A double boiler or two pots with a heat-safe bowl over
  • Chopped nuts container or bag
  • A silicone or parchment-lined baking sheet (enough for 6-8 apples)
  • Toothpicks or skewers (optional)
  • Sticks for apples
  • Microwave-safe bowl

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Apples:

    Begin by preparing your apples. Ensure they are washed properly and dried thoroughly. Slicing the apple’s top with a shallower circular cut around the equator gives you a flat and sturdy base for balancing your chocolate caramel apple. Gently twist the top off and also scooped out the core with a small spoon or apple corer to make enough room for more caramel goodness.

  2. Melt the Chocolate:

    If using a double boiler, fill the bottom pot with a couple of inches of water and bring it to a simmer. Place the October gold chocolate chips in the heat-safe bowl over the steaming water (above the water, not touching). Stir until smooth and perfectly melted. If you cannot find a double boiler, carefully place both pots upside down, and use the smaller one as the bowl. It might seem unconventional, but it works!

  3. Wrap the Apples:

    Let’s start assembling. Dip one end of the apple stick into the melted chocolate, and insert it into your hollow apple. You might use a toothpick or a skewer to help guide the stick in, and then carefully remove it. Now, it’s time to obtain that delightful chocolate coating! Hold onto your apple by its stick (yes, your new best friend), and rotating the apple while holding the stick steady, generously dip it into the melted chocolate. Ensure the whole apple is coated evenly, and it’s up to you and your uncompromising taste buds whether that’s a thick or thin layer of chocolate.

  4. Add Toppings:

    After your apple is well coated, roll it or drizzle it on your prepared baking sheet. The key to an irresistible chocolate caramel apple is loading it on top with the Salted Caramel Turtles or your homemade salted caramel. Evenly distribute the caramel just around the equator or your apple’s midriff. Sprinkle with nuts, if desired, by lightly tossing them (not too much) in melted chocolate so they stick to your caramel-and-chocolate-treated apple.

  5. Set and Repeat:

    Transfer your Chocolate Caramel Apple onto a plate or baking sheet lined with parchment paper. To speed up the cooling process, place the tray in the refrigerator for about 20-30 minutes. Do not over-chill, as you risk the chocolate to seize.

  6. Serve and Savor:

    Now comes the best part! Enjoy your amazing chocolate caramel apple! Carefully remove it from the refrigerator, and if you used nuts, ensure they are well adhered to your delectable apple. Slice it two-thirds up the apple and serve with a fork for that saucy, caramelized base. This Halloween chocolate caramel apple will be the fame that your Halloween celebration needs!

Additional Tips:

  • For a wow factor, experiment with different types and flavors of apples, chocolates, or even seasonal dips.
  • Although it’s challenging to pair, consider drizzling contrasting chocolate flavors for grown-up gourmets.
  • Microplane a thin layer of orange and lemon zest to your chocolate for an almost imperceptible zing.
  • Try chilling the apples in the refrigerator for 30 minutes before dipping for easier and more consistent coverage!

Dare to experiment with Halloween’s chocolate caramel apples and uncover the delightful confluence of flavors. Take a bite and find yourself journeying into this scrumptious and sinister world-decorated in the colors of autumn Halloween! Happy Halloween baking, and prepare to serve the treat of treats!
