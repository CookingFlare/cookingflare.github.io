How to Make Thanksgiving Baked Apples with Cinnamon


Thanksgiving is a time of togetherness, gratitude, and, of course, unforgettable flavors. While pies and turkey reign supreme on the holiday table, there’s something magical about adding a dish that’s equal parts warm, comforting, and simple: baked apples. Filled with aromatic spices like cinnamon, these baked apples are a perfect Thanksgiving dessert, blending the festive essence of fall with wholesome, comforting goodness. Whether served solo or alongside a scoop of vanilla ice cream, this recipe is bound to earn a spot among your Thanksgiving favorites.

Why Baked Apples Are Perfect for Thanksgiving

Thanksgiving dinners are often dominated by rich, heavy dishes. From mashed potatoes drenched in gravy to decadent pies topped with whipped cream, it’s easy for your menu to feel indulgent. Among these hearty offerings, baked apples provide balance. They have the sweetness of dessert without being overly dense, and their fragrant spices bring out the best of what fall has to offer. Plus, they’re an excellent option for guests who prefer a lighter treat or are looking for a gluten-free alternative to traditional holiday desserts.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make these baked apples shine, you’ll need just a handful of pantry staples. Here’s what you’ll need to gather:

  • 6 large apples (firm varieties work best, such as Honeycrisp, Granny Smith, Fuji, or Pink Lady)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional, for added texture)
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries or raisins (optional, for a touch of tart sweetness)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup apple cider or water (for baking)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional garnish: whipped cream or vanilla ice cream

Choosing the Right Apples

For baked apples, it’s important to pick the right variety. Firm apples that hold their shape during baking are key, as softer varieties may collapse or turn mushy. Granny Smith apples offer a tangy flavor that pairs well with the sweetness of brown sugar and cinnamon, while Honeycrisp apples are naturally sweet and juicy. Fuji and Pink Lady apples are excellent middle-ground options, offering both a firm texture and balanced flavor.

Preparing Your Apples

To start, wash the apples thoroughly to remove any wax or residue. Slice off the tops of each apple, creating a flat “lid” surface that you’ll use later. Next, carefully core each apple, removing the seeds and creating a hollow cavity for the filling. A spoon or melon baller works perfectly for this task. Be sure not to carve all the way through the bottom of the apple—you want the filling to stay contained during baking.

Making the Filling

The filling is where all the magic happens. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, ground cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and a pinch of salt. If you’re using walnuts or pecans, toss those in as well, along with the dried cranberries or raisins. The butter will act as a binder, so add it in last, mixing until the filling forms a slightly crumbly mixture.

Stuffing Your Apples

Using a spoon, fill each hollowed apple with the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Pack the filling gently but firmly to ensure every bite of the baked apple is infused with the warm flavors of fall. Don’t worry if your filling comes slightly above the rim of the apple—it will settle as the apples bake, and a generously filled apple always looks more inviting.

Preparing for Baking

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Arrange the stuffed apples in a baking dish, making sure they’re standing upright. Pour the apple cider or water into the bottom of the dish to prevent the apples from drying out during baking and to create a flavorful sauce. Before placing the apples in the oven, cover the baking dish loosely with aluminum foil. This will help the apples cook evenly and retain their moisture.

Baking the Apples

Bake the apples covered for about 25 minutes. Afterward, remove the foil and continue baking for another 20 to 25 minutes, or until the apples are tender but not falling apart. To test for doneness, pierce one apple with a fork—it should slide in easily.

As the apples bake, your kitchen will fill with the irresistible aromas of cinnamon and nutmeg mingling with the natural sweetness of the apples. This fragrant warmth is part of what makes baked apples such an inviting Thanksgiving dessert.

Serving Suggestions

Once the apples are tender and golden, remove them from the oven and let them cool for 5–10 minutes. Transfer them to individual serving plates and drizzle the spiced apple cider sauce from the bottom of the baking dish over each apple. For an extra festive touch, add a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The creaminess contrasts beautifully with the warm, spiced apples, creating a dessert that feels both rustic and indulgent.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

These Thanksgiving baked apples are more than just a dessert—they’re a celebration of the season. The natural sweetness of the apples is enhanced by the warmth of cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger, while the brown sugar adds a caramel-like richness. The optional mix-ins, such as pecans or dried cranberries, add delightful texture and complexity, ensuring every bite is a treat. Best of all, the recipe is incredibly versatile: you can adjust the spices or toppings to suit your preferences, and it’s easy to scale up or down depending on your guest list.

Tips for Success

  1. Choose Apples Wisely: As mentioned earlier, opt for firm apples that hold their shape during baking.
  2. Don’t Skip the Sauce: The apple cider or water ensures the apples stay moist while creating a flavorful base that elevates the dish. If you prefer, you can use orange juice or a spiced wine instead.
  3. Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling and hollow out the apples in advance, storing them separately until you’re ready to bake. This saves you valuable time on Thanksgiving Day.
  4. Customize the Flavors: Not a fan of walnuts or pecans? Swap them out for slivered almonds. Want more spice? Add a pinch of cardamom or allspice to your filling.

The Festive Culinary Reward

These baked apples with cinnamon are a true testament to how simple ingredients can create something extraordinary. Perfect for Thanksgiving or any other autumn gathering, they bring together comfort, nostalgia, and celebration in a single dish. Whether served solo or paired with accompaniments like ice cream, caramel drizzle, or even a sprinkle of powdered sugar, they’re guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

So as you plan your Thanksgiving feast, give this recipe a try. It’s a dessert that combines ease, elegance, and the undeniable charm of fall into a dish your guests will remember long after the last bite. Warm, spiced, and irresistibly inviting, these baked apples are the perfect addition to your Thanksgiving tradition.
