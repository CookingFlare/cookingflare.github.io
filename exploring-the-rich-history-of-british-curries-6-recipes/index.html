Exploring the Rich History of British Curries: 6 Recipes


British curry has a rich and complex history that spans over two centuries. From its humble beginnings in the early 19th century to its current status as a beloved national dish, British curry has evolved significantly over the years, influenced by various cultures and traditions. In this blog post, we will delve into the fascinating history of British curries and explore six classic recipes that showcase the diversity and depth of this beloved cuisine. A Brief History of British Curries The origins of British curry are often shrouded in mystery, but historians agree that the dish was first introduced to the UK by Indian sailors and traders who traveled to Britain during the colonial era. The first written record of curry in British cuisine dates back to 1772, when a recipe for "curry" was published in a book called "The Art of Cookery Made Plain and Easy" by Hannah Glasse. This early recipe used a mixture of spices, including cumin, coriander, and turmeric, to create a flavorful sauce for meat and vegetables. In the early 19th century, British expatriates and members of the upper class who had traveled to India developed a taste for the rich and aromatic curries they encountered during their travels. Upon their return to the UK, they introduced their own version of curry to their friends and family, using a combination of spices and cooking techniques they had learned during their time in India. This pioneering effort helped to popularize curry in Britain, and by the mid-19th century, it had become a staple of British cuisine. Evolution of British Curries Over the years, British curries have undergone significant changes, influenced by various cultural and culinary traditions. Some of the key developments in the evolution of British curries include:

Colonial era: During the colonial era, British curries were heavily influenced by Indian cuisine, with a focus on rich and complex sauces made with a variety of spices, herbs, and chutneys. Punjabi influence: In the early 20th century, Punjabi immigrants from India introduced their own version of curry, characterized by bold and spicy flavors. British innovation: To cater to local tastes, British chefs began to experiment with new ingredients and cooking techniques, resulting in curries that were both authentic and innovative.

Six Classic British Curry Recipes Here are six classic British curry recipes that showcase the diversity and depth of this beloved cuisine:

  1. Classic Chicken Tikka Masala This iconic recipe is a staple of British curry houses and has been a favorite among locals and visitors alike. It's a rich and creamy sauce made with the following ingredients:

1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces 1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups chicken broth 1 cup heavy cream Fresh cilantro, for garnish

  1. Korma Recipe This mild and creamy curry is a popular choice among vegetarians and vegans. It's made with the following ingredients:

1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups vegetable broth 1 cup coconut cream 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, and green beans) Fresh cilantro, for garnish

  1. Madras Recipe This spicy curry is a favorite among those who enjoy bold and fiery flavors. It's made with the following ingredients:

1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups chicken broth 1 cup tomato sauce 1 cup diced chicken or beef Fresh cilantro, for garnish

  1. Vindaloo Recipe This tangy and spicy curry is a popular choice among those who enjoy bold and complex flavors. It's made with the following ingredients:

1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups chicken broth 1 cup white wine vinegar 1 cup diced chicken or beef Fresh cilantro, for garnish

  1. Tandoori Chickpea Recipe This flavorful curry is a popular choice among vegetarians and vegans. It's made with the following ingredients:

1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups chickpeas 1 cup diced onion 1 cup diced bell pepper Fresh cilantro, for garnish

  1. Balti Chicken Recipe This spicy curry is a popular choice among those who enjoy bold and fiery flavors. It's made with the following ingredients:

1 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons garam masala 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 2 cups chicken broth 1 cup tomato sauce 1 cup diced chicken or beef Fresh cilantro, for garnish

From its humble beginnings in the early 19th century to its current status as a beloved national dish, British curry has evolved significantly over the years, influenced by various cultures and traditions.
