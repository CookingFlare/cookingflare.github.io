Homemade Gnocchi Recipe - Italian Potato Pasta Perfection


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’ve probably heard of gnocchi before. These delicious little potato dumplings are a staple in Italian cooking, and once you try them, you’ll understand why. Today, I’m going to share with you my secret recipe for homemade gnocchi that will have you feeling like a true Italian chef in no time.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 large egg

Instructions

  1. Start by boiling the potatoes with their skins on. This helps to keep them from getting too waterlogged. Once they’re tender and easily pierced with a fork, drain them and let them cool slightly.

  2. Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, peel off the skins and discard them. Mash the potatoes in a large bowl until they’re nice and smooth. You can use a potato masher or a fork for this step.

  3. Add the flour, salt, and egg to the mashed potatoes. Mix everything together until a dough forms. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the gnocchi tough.

  4. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a long rope, about 1/2 inch in diameter. Cut the ropes into 1-inch pieces.

  5. To give your gnocchi that classic shape, take each piece and press it gently against the tines of a fork. This will create ridges that help the sauce cling to the gnocchi.

  6. As you shape the gnocchi, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. This will prevent them from sticking together.

  7. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the gnocchi into the boiling water and cook them for about 2-3 minutes. You’ll know they’re done when they float to the surface.

  8. Using a slotted spoon, remove the cooked gnocchi from the water and transfer them to a plate. Repeat this process until all the gnocchi are cooked.

  9. At this point, you can serve the gnocchi with your favorite sauce. Whether it’s a simple marinara, a creamy alfredo, or a rich pesto, the choice is yours. Top it off with some grated Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs, and you’re ready to indulge in your homemade gnocchi.

Tips and Tricks

  • Be sure to use russet potatoes for this recipe. They have a high starch content, which helps to give the gnocchi their light and fluffy texture.
  • Don’t be tempted to use too much flour in the dough. Adding too much can result in dense gnocchi. The dough should be slightly sticky, but still manageable.
  • If you want to get ahead, you can freeze the uncooked gnocchi on a baking sheet. Once frozen, transfer them to a freezer bag for easy storage. When you’re ready to cook them, simply boil them straight from the freezer, adding an extra minute or two to the cooking time.

Now that you have the secret to making homemade gnocchi, it’s time to get in the kitchen and start cooking. With a little practice, you’ll be serving up a plate of Italian potato pasta perfection that will impress even the most discerning foodie. Buon appetito!
