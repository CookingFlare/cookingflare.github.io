Satisfying Shrimp and Vegetable Lo Mein Stir Fry: A Chinese Classic


There’s something undeniably satisfying about a delicious plate of Shrimp and Vegetable Lo Mein Stir Fry. This Chinese classic brings together succulent shrimp, vibrant vegetables, and flavorful noodles in a harmonious dance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving more. So, let’s dive into the art of creating this delectable dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Lo Mein:

  • 8 ounces lo mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup snow peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup bell peppers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

For garnish:

  • Sesame seeds
  • Sliced green onions

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lo Mein Noodles: Cook the lo mein noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and rinse with cold water to prevent them from sticking together. Toss the noodles with a drizzle of sesame oil to add flavor and prevent clumping.

  2. Sauté the Shrimp: In a large wok or skillet, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set them aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same wok or skillet, add a bit more oil if needed. Sauté the snow peas, carrots, bell peppers, and broccoli for 3-4 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. Add the green onions, garlic, and ginger, and cook for an additional minute.

  4. Create the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes. Pour the sauce into the wok or skillet with the vegetables, and bring it to a simmer.

  5. Combine and Toss: Add the cooked shrimp and lo mein noodles to the wok or skillet. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated in the sauce and the shrimp and vegetables are evenly distributed.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle the lo mein with sesame seeds and sliced green onions for a pop of color and flavor. Serve the Shrimp and Vegetable Lo Mein Stir Fry hot and enjoy the symphony of flavors with every bite.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian twist, simply omit the shrimp and add more of your favorite vegetables or tofu for a protein boost.
  • Experiment with different vegetables such as snap peas, bean sprouts, or baby corn to customize the dish to your liking.
  • Feel free to adjust the level of spice by adding more or less red pepper flakes according to your preference.

In Conclusion

With its vibrant colors, aromatic flavors, and satisfying textures, this classic dish is a testament to the art of stir-frying.
