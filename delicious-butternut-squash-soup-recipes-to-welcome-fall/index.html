Delicious Butternut Squash Soup Recipes to Welcome Fall


As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, there’s nothing quite like the comforting warmth of a bowl of butternut squash soup to embrace the essence of fall. This vibrant orange vegetable, known for its sweet, nutty flavor, is a staple of autumn cuisine, providing a perfect canvas for a variety of spices and ingredients that make each recipe unique. Below, we will explore several delicious butternut squash soup recipes that are sure to delight your taste buds and bring a touch of seasonal warmth to your table.

The Basics of Butternut Squash Soup

Before diving into the recipes, let’s discuss the fundamentals of creating a delicious butternut squash soup. The key ingredients typically include fresh butternut squash, onion, garlic, vegetable or chicken broth, and a splash of cream or coconut milk for richness. Spices such as nutmeg, cinnamon, and ginger can elevate the flavor profile, while toppings like croutons, seeds, or herbs add texture and visual appeal.

Selecting the Perfect Butternut Squash

When choosing butternut squash, look for specimens that are heavy for their size, with a matte skin free of blemishes. A good squash should be firm and have a uniform shape, which makes for easier peeling and cutting. If you’re purchasing pre-cut squash, ensure it’s fresh and has not started to turn brown.

Preparation Tips

Preparing butternut squash can be a bit daunting due to its tough exterior. Here are a few tips to make the process smoother:

  1. Use a Sharp Knife: A sharp knife will make cutting through the squash easier.
  2. Microwave Softening: If you’re struggling to cut through, microwave the whole squash for a minute or two to soften it slightly.
  3. Peeling: Use a vegetable peeler to remove the tough skin, then cut the squash into manageable cubes.

Classic Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Cook the Squash: Add the cubed butternut squash to the pot, mixing it with the onions and garlic. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Add Broth and Spices: Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, then sprinkle in the nutmeg and cinnamon. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.

  4. Blend the Soup: Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, carefully transfer the soup to a countertop blender, blend in batches if necessary, and return it to the pot.

  5. Finish with Cream: Stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk, adjusting the consistency with more broth if necessary. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley or chives. Enjoy with crusty bread or a side salad.

Curried Butternut Squash Soup

For a more exotic twist, this curried butternut squash soup infuses traditional flavors with a hint of spice that will warm your soul.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Base: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft. Stir in garlic and ginger, cooking for an additional minute.

  2. Spice It Up: Add the curry powder and stir until the spices are fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  3. Incorporate the Squash: Add the cubed butternut squash and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook until the squash is tender, roughly 20 minutes.

  4. Blend and Add Coconut Milk: Blend the soup until smooth. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving. This soup pairs wonderfully with naan or rice.

Roasted Butternut Squash and Apple Soup

Combining the sweetness of apples with the earthiness of butternut squash creates a delightful soup that captures the flavors of fall.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pumpkin seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Roast the Squash and Apples: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the butternut squash and apples in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them on a baking sheet and roast for about 25-30 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.

  2. Sauté the Onion: In a large pot, heat a little olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the roasted squash and apples to the pot. Pour in the broth and add the cinnamon. Bring to a boil, then let it simmer for about 15 minutes.

  4. Blend: Blend the soup until smooth, adjusting the consistency with additional broth if needed.

  5. Serve: Garnish with pumpkin seeds for a crunchy texture and serve with a slice of warm bread for a satisfying meal.

Butternut Squash and Sage Soup

This recipe highlights the classic pairing of butternut squash and sage, delivering a savory and aromatic soup perfect for chilly evenings.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried sage)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and sauté until soft, then stir in garlic and sage, cooking for another minute.

  2. Add Squash and Broth: Stir in the cubed butternut squash and pour over the broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until the squash is tender.

  3. Blend and Incorporate Cream: Blend the soup until smooth, then stir in the heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Serve: Garnish with additional chopped sage if desired. This soup is excellent with a sprinkle of parmesan cheese on top.

Tips for Enjoying Butternut Squash Soup

  1. Batch Cooking: Make a large batch of soup and freeze portions for quick meals later in the season.
  2. Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to add your favorite spices or herbs. Thyme, rosemary, and chili flakes can add a new dimension to your soup.
  3. Pairing: Serve your butternut squash soup with a light salad or a sandwich to create a well-rounded meal.

Conclusion

As the days grow shorter and the weather cooler, these butternut squash soup recipes provide not just nourishment, but also a taste of the warmth and comfort of the season. Whether you prefer the classic flavors or are intrigued by spicier, more adventurous combinations, there’s a butternut squash soup recipe here for everyone to enjoy. So, gather your ingredients, get cozy in your kitchen, and let the scents of fall fill your home with these delightful soups that celebrate one of autumn’s most beloved vegetables. Happy cooking!
