A Fermented Twist on Classic Recipes: Revamping Your Culinary Repertoire


Spice up your cooking routine with a tangy twist on your favorite classic dishes! Incorporating fermented ingredients brings elevated flavors, juicy textures, and a stroke of uniqueness to every meal. Get ready to broaden your culinary expertise and impress your guests with brand-new takes on your all-time favorite recipes!

fermentation essentials

Before delving into recipe aftershocks, a brief overview of fermentation and its basics is critical for beginners to grasp.

types of fermentation

Fermentation has two main types in cooking:

  1. Lactic Acid Fermentation - involves several types of bacteria that convert sugars into lactic acid. This variety includes yogurt-making, pickling, and sauerkraut creation.
  2. Alcohol Fermentation - is where yeasts or certain molds consume sugars to produce alcohol - a primary method in bread making, brewing, and wine creation.

fermentation vessels and storage

Our fermenting vessels shape the finished product’s texture and flavor. Using glass or ceramic containers keeps the process transparent, making the fermentation process easily monitored. Earthenware and crocks are ideal for long-term fermentations, while thin glass jars offer quick access for daily monitoring. Proper storage requires a warm, stable environment away from direct sunlight.

classics with a ferment twist

Now, it’s time to explore new horizons of cuisine!

Kimchi Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked white rice
  • 1 cup of cubed kimchi
  • 1/2 cup of chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup of chopped bell pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds
  • Green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice and set it aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil on medium-high heat.
  3. Add onion and bell pepper and cook until softened.
  4. Combine kimchi and rice, then fry for 2 minutes.
  5. Create a well in the center of the skillet, and crack the eggs. Stir well once cooked.
  6. Mix soy sauce and continue to cook for another minute.
  7. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions before serving.

Fermented Garlic Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 8 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup of your fermented garlic in oil
  • 1/3 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup of sweet potato juice
  • 1 teaspoon of honey
  • 1 teaspoon of cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken thighs in fermented garlic in oil mixture overnight.
  2. In a large bowl, mix remaining components and coat chicken thighs thoroughly.
  3. Heat vegetable oil on medium-high in a large skillet for 3 minutes.
  4. Add thighs and fry for 5 minutes each side, or until cooked.
  5. Dilute sauce with 1 tablespoon of water if necessary and serve alongside chicken.

Tangy Ginger Beer

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter of water
  • 2 cups of molasses
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of lime zest
  • 1/3 cup of active fermentation brine
  • 2 tablespoons of soldomel (like molasses plus ginger) as a fermentation starting base

Instructions:

  1. Boil water and remove from heat.
  2. Add molasses, stirring the mix well.
  3. Strain ginger, lime zest, and brine, letting the solids settle in the pot.
  4. Mix well, cool to room temperature, and transfer to a sealed jar.
  5. Ferment for 3 days at room temperature before refrigerating; strain solids, bottle, and serve.

conclusion

Fermentation offers an interactive experience and depth for culinary enthusiasts to experiment with. Turn up the heat on traditional mains or snack treats with refreshing new ferments.

Develop your culinary creativity and discover how simple fermentation can impact your menu choices. Happy cooking and fermenting!
