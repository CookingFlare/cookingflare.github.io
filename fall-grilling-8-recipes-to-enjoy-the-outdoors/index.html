Fall Grilling: 8 Recipes to Enjoy the Outdoors


As the crisp air of autumn settles in, it’s the perfect time to fire up the grill and savor the flavors of the season. Fall grilling offers a unique opportunity to explore a wide range of ingredients, from hearty meats to vibrant seasonal vegetables. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering or simply enjoying a cozy evening outdoors, these eight grilling recipes are sure to elevate your autumn culinary experience.

1. Grilled Apple Cider Chicken

Fall and apples go hand in hand, so why not bring this seasonal fruit to your grill? Grilled apple cider chicken is a fantastic way to combine the smoky char of grilling with the sweet, tangy essence of apple cider. The marinade infuses the chicken with flavors that are both savory and subtly sweet, making it a perfect dish for a fall evening.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh apple slices (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together apple cider, Dijon mustard, olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a large resealable bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Seal and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for more flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade, discarding the excess.
  4. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Garnish with fresh apple slices and serve with roasted vegetables or a light salad for a complete fall meal.

2. Grilled Butternut Squash with Maple Glaze

Butternut squash is a fall favorite, and grilling it with a maple glaze takes its natural sweetness to new heights. The caramelized exterior combined with the smoky flavors from the grill makes for an irresistible side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cut into 1-inch slices
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the butternut squash slices with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Place the squash slices on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and have grill marks.
  3. While the squash is grilling, combine the maple syrup, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a small bowl.
  4. Once the squash is done, brush the maple glaze over the top of each slice and continue grilling for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the glaze to caramelize.
  5. Remove from the grill and garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

3. Grilled Steak with Fall Herb Butter

As the weather cools down, a juicy steak grilled to perfection is always a great choice. Adding a fall-inspired herb butter made with rosemary, sage, and thyme creates a rich, aromatic topping that pairs beautifully with the smoky flavors of the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (or your preferred cut)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat. Rub the steaks with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the steaks for 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, or longer if desired. Use a meat thermometer to ensure your steaks reach the desired internal temperature.
  3. While the steaks are grilling, prepare the herb butter. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter with rosemary, sage, thyme, and garlic. Mix until well combined.
  4. Once the steaks are done, remove them from the grill and let them rest for 5 minutes. Top each steak with a generous spoonful of the herb butter before serving.

4. Grilled Pumpkin and Halloumi Skewers

Pumpkin isn’t just for pies and lattes—grilling it brings out a whole new depth of flavor. Paired with halloumi cheese, which holds up beautifully on the grill, these skewers make for a delicious and unique fall dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cubed pumpkin (peeled and deseeded)
  • 8 oz halloumi cheese, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. In a bowl, toss the pumpkin cubes with olive oil, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Thread the pumpkin cubes and halloumi cheese onto skewers, alternating between the two.
  3. Grill the skewers for 4-6 minutes, turning occasionally until the pumpkin is tender and the cheese is golden and slightly crispy.
  4. Remove the skewers from the grill and garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving.

5. Grilled Sausage and Apple Skewers

The combination of sausage and apples is a classic fall pairing that’s perfect for the grill. The sweet, juicy apples complement the savory sausages, creating a dish that’s both comforting and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 4 sausages (chicken, pork, or your preferred type), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 apples, cored and cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. In a bowl, toss the sausage pieces with olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Thread the sausages and apple chunks onto skewers, alternating between the two.
  3. Grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes, turning frequently until the sausages are cooked through and the apples are slightly caramelized.
  4. Serve with a side of mashed potatoes or a fresh green salad for a hearty fall meal.

6. Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Balsamic Glaze

Portobello mushrooms are an excellent vegetarian grilling option that can easily stand in for meat. Grilled to perfection and topped with a balsamic glaze, they offer a rich, savory flavor with just the right amount of tang.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms, stems removed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Brush the mushrooms with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the mushrooms for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have grill marks.
  3. While the mushrooms are grilling, combine the balsamic vinegar, honey, and thyme in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over low heat and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the glaze has thickened.
  4. Once the mushrooms are done, drizzle the balsamic glaze over the top and serve with a side of roasted potatoes or a fresh salad.

7. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chipotle Butter

Corn on the cob is a must-have side dish for any fall grilling session. Grilling the corn adds a smoky depth to its natural sweetness, and when paired with a creamy chipotle butter, it’s a game-changer.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon chipotle chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the corn directly on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the kernels are slightly charred and tender.
  2. While the corn is grilling, mix the softened butter with chipotle chili powder, garlic powder, and salt.
  3. Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and slather it with the chipotle butter.
  4. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro and serve.

8. Grilled Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

For a light and refreshing fall salad, grilled pears and gorgonzola cheese are the perfect combination. The sweetness of the pears, coupled with the tangy, creamy gorgonzola, creates a flavor profile that’s both bold and balanced.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pears, halved and cored
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or your choice)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled gorgonzola cheese
  • 1/4 cup toasted walnuts
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Brush the pear halves with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the pears cut-side down for 4-5 minutes, until grill marks form and the pears are slightly softened.
  3. Arrange the mixed greens on a platter. Top with the grilled pears, crumbled gorgonzola, and toasted walnuts.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and serve as a starter or side dish to any fall meal.

Conclusion

Fall grilling is a wonderful way to embrace the flavors of the season while enjoying the outdoors. Whether you’re grilling hearty meats, seasonal vegetables, or sweet fruits, the options are endless. These eight recipes provide a perfect mix of savory, sweet, and smoky flavors that capture the essence of autumn. Fire up your grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the tastes of fall—there’s no better way to enjoy the season!
