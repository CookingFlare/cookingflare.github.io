Beef and Broccoli: A Classic Chinese-American Dish


Beef and Broccoli is a beloved dish that has become a staple in Chinese-American cuisine. The tender beef combined with the vibrant green broccoli, all coated in a savory, umami-packed sauce, makes this dish a favorite in many households. In this recipe, we will explore how to create this flavorful and comforting dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, combine the thinly sliced flank steak with 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, and a pinch of black pepper. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse into the beef.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In another bowl, whisk together the remaining soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, cornstarch, and beef broth until the cornstarch is fully dissolved. This will create a rich and velvety sauce that will coat the beef and broccoli beautifully.

  3. Blanch the Broccoli: Bring a pot of water to a boil and blanch the broccoli florets for 2 minutes. Remove the broccoli and immediately plunge it into an ice bath to stop the cooking process. This will help the broccoli retain its vibrant green color and crisp texture.

  4. Sear the Beef: In a large skillet or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over high heat. Add the marinated beef and sear it for 2-3 minutes until it is browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.

  5. Stir-Fry the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.

  6. Combine and Simmer: Return the seared beef to the skillet, then add the blanched broccoli. Pour the prepared sauce over the beef and broccoli, stirring to coat everything evenly. Allow the sauce to simmer and thicken for 2-3 minutes, ensuring that the beef and broccoli are well-coated in the flavorful sauce.

  7. Serve and Garnish: Transfer the beef and broccoli to a serving dish. Garnish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and sliced green onions for a pop of color and added flavor.

Tips for Perfection

  • Slice the Beef Thinly: Slicing the flank steak thinly against the grain ensures that the beef remains tender and easy to chew.

  • Prep Ahead: You can marinate the beef and prepare the sauce ahead of time, making it a great option for a quick weeknight meal.

  • Adjust the Sauce: Feel free to adjust the amount of sauce based on your preference. Some enjoy a saucier dish, while others prefer a lighter coating.

  • Serve with Rice: Beef and Broccoli pairs wonderfully with steamed white rice, allowing the sauce to soak into the rice for an extra burst of flavor.

Final Thoughts

Beef and Broccoli is a delightful combination of tender beef, crisp broccoli, and a rich, savory sauce that brings a burst of umami to every bite. Whether you’re a fan of Chinese-American cuisine or simply looking to expand your culinary repertoire, this dish is sure to become a new favorite. With its simple preparation and satisfying flavors, Beef and Broccoli is a classic that continues to stand the test of time.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of Chinese-American comfort food, consider whipping up a batch of Beef and Broccoli in your own kitchen. It’s a dish that brings warmth, flavor, and a touch of nostalgia to every meal. Enjoy!
