A Classic French Dessert: Cherry Clafoutis


Who can resist the allure of a classic French dessert? With its delicate combination of sweet cherries and a custard-like batter, Cherry Clafoutis is a delightful treat that has been enjoyed for generations. In this recipe, we’ll take you through the steps to create your very own Cherry Clafoutis, allowing you to savor the taste of France in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients

To make a scrumptious Cherry Clafoutis, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh cherries, pitted
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch pie dish or a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs lightly. Gradually add the milk and vanilla extract, whisking continuously.

  4. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, whisking constantly to form a smooth batter.

  5. Arrange the pitted cherries in a single layer at the bottom of the greased dish.

  6. Pour the batter over the cherries, ensuring they are evenly covered.

  7. Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 40-45 minutes, or until the clafoutis is set and golden brown on top. You can check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center of the clafoutis; if it comes out clean, it’s ready!

  8. Once baked, remove the clafoutis from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes.

  9. Sprinkle powdered sugar over the top of the clafoutis for a beautiful finishing touch.

  10. Serve warm or at room temperature. It pairs wonderfully with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • Traditional recipes often leave the cherry pits intact to enhance the flavor of the clafoutis. However, if you prefer a more convenient eating experience, feel free to pit the cherries before baking.

  • You can experiment with different fruits in place of cherries. Plums, peaches, and berries can all be used to create unique and delicious variations of this classic dessert.

  • For an extra touch of indulgence, add a sprinkle of ground cinnamon or a splash of almond extract to the batter.

  • Clafoutis is best enjoyed fresh, but any leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to two days. Reheat individual servings in the microwave for a quick and easy treat.

Conclusion

Cherry Clafoutis is a classic French dessert that embodies the essence of simplicity and elegance. With its tender, custard-like texture and bursts of sweet cherries, it’s no wonder this dessert has stood the test of time. Whether you choose to enjoy it as a special treat or serve it to impress your guests, this recipe is sure to delight. So go ahead, indulge in the flavors of France with a homemade Cherry Clafoutis. Bon appétit!
