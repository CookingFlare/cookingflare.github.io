Cooking for Two: 10 Quick and Easy Stir-Fry Recipes


Stir-fry dishes are the ultimate solution for those busy evenings when time is limited but your taste buds crave something flavorful and satisfying. The freshness of the ingredients, the speed of preparation, and the satisfying crunch of crisp veggies make stir-frying a go-to cooking method for many. But when you’re cooking for two, portion control and simplicity are key. This guide takes you through ten quick and easy stir-fry recipes that are perfect for two people. These meals deliver bold flavors, balanced nutrition, and a delightful variety of textures—all cooked and ready to enjoy in no time.

1. Garlic Ginger Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced thin
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add chicken slices and stir-fry until golden brown, about 4-5 minutes.
  3. Stir in garlic and ginger, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Toss in bell peppers and sugar snap peas, stirring continuously until tender-crisp, about 3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce, mix well, and serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

This dish offers a perfect balance of protein and vegetables while the garlic and ginger pack a flavorful punch.

2. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch (optional)
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Coat the beef slices lightly with cornstarch if desired—it helps create a thicker sauce.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet or wok on high heat and stir-fry the beef until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add onion and broccoli to the same pan, stirring until slightly tender.
  4. Return the beef to the pan and pour in oyster sauce. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes ensuring everything is coated evenly. Serve immediately.

This classic pairing is one of the quickest ways to get a restaurant-quality dish at home.

3. Spicy Shrimp Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup zucchini slices
  • 1 red chili, finely sliced
  • 1 tsp chili paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in your wok and toss in the chili slices.
  2. Add shrimp and cook for 2 minutes on each side until they turn pink.
  3. Mix in zucchini slices and cook for another 2-3 minutes until tender.
  4. Stir in chili paste and soy sauce, ensuring the dish is evenly coated.
  5. Serve over a bowl of whole-grain rice for a spicy, protein-packed meal.

For spice lovers, this stir-fry’s heat level can be adjusted by the amount of chili paste used.

4. Teriyaki Tofu Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup bok choy, chopped
  • 3 tbsp teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess moisture, then cube it.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in the pan and sauté the tofu until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Stir-fry mushrooms and bok choy for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Return tofu to the pan and pour in teriyaki sauce, stirring until everything is well-coated.
  5. Enjoy this plant-based delight as-is or over a bed of quinoa.

The teriyaki sauce elevates the dish with a sweet-savory flavor perfect for tofu enthusiasts.

5. Cashew Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken thighs, diced
  • ½ cup unsalted roasted cashews
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a warm wok and cook chicken until browned.
  2. Add cashews and stir-fry for an additional minute.
  3. Toss in green bell peppers and hoisin sauce, cooking until peppers are tender.
  4. Serve with jasmine rice for a nutty, flavorful experience.

The crunchiness of cashews combined with tender chicken hits all the right notes.

6. Sesame Beef Stir-Fry with Snow Peas

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in the pan and cook beef until browned.
  2. Add snow peas and stir-fry for 3 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle sesame seeds over the dish and stir in soy sauce. Cook for 2 more minutes, then serve.

This recipe marries simplicity with bold sesame flavors for a quick weeknight dinner.

7. Pineapple Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 chicken breast, diced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sweet and sour sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Stir-fry chicken pieces in olive oil until cooked through.
  2. Add pineapple and bell peppers, cooking for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Mix in sweet and sour sauce to bring all flavors together.
  4. Serve atop warm rice or rice noodles.

The natural sweetness of pineapple pairs wonderfully with the mild chicken and vibrant sauce.

8. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Peanut Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup assorted vegetables (e.g., carrots, broccoli, bell peppers)
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil and stir-fry your vegetables until tender-crisp.
  2. Meanwhile, mix peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, and water into a smooth sauce.
  3. Toss sauce into the vegetables and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Serve with brown rice or as-is for a flavorful, plant-based stir-fry.

This peanut sauce adds rich and creamy depth to your vegetable mix.

9. Sweet Chili Pork Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pork tenderloin, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 3 tbsp sweet chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil and cook pork slices until no longer pink.
  2. Add red bell pepper and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Mix in sweet chili sauce, coating everything nicely, and cook for another 1-2 minutes. Serve hot.

The combination of savory pork and sweet chili sauce makes this dish irresistibly tasty.

10. Lemon Basil Shrimp Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 10 large shrimp, peeled
  • 1 zucchini, spiralized (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet and cook shrimp until pink.
  2. Add zucchini and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until tender.
  3. Squeeze lemon juice over the dish and add fresh basil leaves. Stir well before serving.

This light and fresh stir-fry is ideal for a summery, citrusy dinner.

Stir-frying is not only quick but also incredibly versatile. You can mix and match proteins, vegetables, and sauces to create unique combinations for endless possibilities. Whether you’re cooking with chicken, beef, tofu, shrimp, or focusing solely on vegetables, these simple recipes make it easy to put together delicious meals for two. Each recipe is tailored for efficiency without sacrificing flavor, ensuring you spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your food.
