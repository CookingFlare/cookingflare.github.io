10 Must-Try BBQ Recipes for Your Fourth of July Celebration


The Fourth of July is the perfect time to gather with friends and family, celebrate the joys of summer, and enjoy delicious food together. When it comes to creating the ultimate backyard BBQ spread, nothing beats the flavors of perfectly grilled meats, savory sides, and refreshing beverages. If you’re looking to elevate your BBQ game this year, look no further! Here are ten must-try BBQ recipes that will make your Fourth of July celebration truly unforgettable.

1. Classic BBQ Ribs

No Fourth of July BBQ is complete without a rack of juicy, tender BBQ ribs. This classic recipe combines a homemade rub with a sweet and tangy BBQ sauce, resulting in melt-in-your-mouth goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 cup BBQ sauce (your choice)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat (around 300°F).
  2. Remove the silver skin from the ribs and pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together brown sugar, paprika, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, and cumin.
  4. Rub the spice mixture evenly over both sides of the ribs.
  5. Place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down. Close the lid and cook for 2-2.5 hours, turning occasionally, until the ribs are tender.
  6. Brush the ribs with BBQ sauce during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  7. Remove from the grill and let them rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

These BBQ ribs are smoky, sweet, and savory—guaranteed to be the star of your summer celebration!

2. Grilled Chicken Wings with Honey Mustard Glaze

Chicken wings are a crowd favorite at any BBQ, and these grilled wings are smothered in a delectable honey mustard glaze that balances sweetness and tang.

Ingredients:

  • 12 chicken wings
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the honey mustard glaze:

  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the chicken wings with olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the wings over medium-high heat for 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy.
  4. While the wings are cooking, whisk together the Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, and garlic powder to make the glaze.
  5. Once the wings are cooked through, remove them from the grill and toss them in the honey mustard glaze.
  6. Serve with extra glaze on the side for dipping.

These wings are a hit every time, and their sweet and tangy flavor will leave your guests craving more!

3. Grilled Veggie Skewers

For your vegetarian guests, or anyone craving something light and fresh, these grilled veggie skewers are a perfect option. With a blend of seasonal vegetables and a flavorful marinade, these skewers are both colorful and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between zucchini, squash, bell pepper, onion, and cherry tomatoes.
  4. Brush the skewers with the marinade.
  5. Grill the skewers for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

These skewers are a vibrant and flavorful addition to your BBQ spread and are sure to be enjoyed by all!

4. Grilled Shrimp Tacos with Lime-Cilantro Slaw

If you’re looking to add a coastal twist to your BBQ, these grilled shrimp tacos with lime-cilantro slaw will transport your taste buds straight to the beach. The shrimp are perfectly seasoned, and the slaw adds a fresh, zesty crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas

For the lime-cilantro slaw:

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the shrimp in olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, until they turn pink and are cooked through.
  4. While the shrimp are grilling, combine the cabbage, cilantro, lime juice, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper in a bowl to make the slaw.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas on the grill for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a few grilled shrimp on each tortilla and topping with a generous portion of lime-cilantro slaw.
  7. Serve with extra lime wedges on the side.

These shrimp tacos are light, flavorful, and perfect for a festive Fourth of July gathering.

5. BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwiches

For a truly indulgent BBQ experience, pulled pork sandwiches are a must. Slow-cooked, tender pork is smothered in smoky BBQ sauce and served on a soft bun, making it the ultimate comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 lb pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your choice)
  • 8 hamburger buns

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill or smoker to 225°F.
  2. In a bowl, combine paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper to create a dry rub.
  3. Rub the spice mixture all over the pork shoulder.
  4. Place the pork shoulder on the grill or smoker and cook for 6-8 hours, until the pork is tender and pulls apart easily with a fork.
  5. Shred the pork with two forks and toss it in BBQ sauce.
  6. Serve the pulled pork on hamburger buns with extra BBQ sauce on the side.

These pulled pork sandwiches are smoky, savory, and the perfect addition to your BBQ feast.

6. Grilled Sausages with Peppers and Onions

Sausages grilled to perfection and topped with sautéed peppers and onions make for an irresistible BBQ dish. The smoky sausages paired with the sweetness of the peppers and onions create a flavor explosion in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 6 sausages (your choice of flavor)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the sausages for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are cooked through and have nice grill marks.
  3. While the sausages are grilling, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced peppers and onions and cook for 5-7 minutes, until they are soft and caramelized.
  4. Serve the grilled sausages with the sautéed peppers and onions on the side or piled on top.

This dish is hearty, flavorful, and guaranteed to be a favorite at your Fourth of July BBQ.

7. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Grilled corn on the cob is a classic BBQ side dish, and when topped with a rich, herbed butter, it becomes a standout treat. The charred kernels and flavorful butter create a perfect balance.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are slightly charred and tender.
  3. While the corn is grilling, mix together the softened butter, parsley, cilantro, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Once the corn is done, brush each ear with the herb butter.
  5. Serve immediately for a burst of flavor in every bite.

Grilled corn on the cob is a perfect side dish to complement your main BBQ offerings.

8. Grilled Beef Burgers with All the Fixings

A BBQ isn’t complete without a juicy beef burger. These grilled burgers are packed with flavor and topped with all your favorite fixings, from lettuce and tomato to cheese and pickles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (80% lean)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 burger buns
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese (optional)
  • Lettuce, tomato, and pickles for topping

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Form the ground beef into 4 patties, and season with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Grill the burgers for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  4. If you’re using cheese, place a slice on each burger during the last minute of grilling to melt.
  5. Toast the burger buns on the grill for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Assemble the burgers by placing the patty on the bun and adding your desired toppings.

These burgers are juicy, flavorful, and perfect for any BBQ.

9. Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon Sugar

For a refreshing and slightly sweet dessert, grilled pineapple with cinnamon sugar is the perfect treat. The caramelized edges of the pineapple paired with the warm cinnamon-sugar coating create a simple yet indulgent dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Mix the sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  3. Grill the pineapple rings for 3-4 minutes per side, until they have nice grill marks and are slightly caramelized.
  4. Sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture over the grilled pineapple.
  5. Serve warm for a refreshing end to your BBQ meal.

This sweet and smoky dessert is a perfect way to round off your Fourth of July celebration.

10. S’mores Barbecue Nachos

For a fun and unique twist on traditional s’mores, these BBQ nachos are loaded with graham crackers, chocolate, marshmallows, and a drizzle of caramel sauce. They’re a sweet, gooey, and decadent treat that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1/2 cup caramel sauce
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-low heat.
  2. Layer a sheet of aluminum foil with graham cracker crumbs, chocolate chips, and marshmallows.
  3. Grill for 5-7 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden and the chocolate is melted.
  4. Drizzle with caramel sauce and sprinkle with chopped nuts if desired.
  5. Serve immediately with a spoon for an indulgent, shareable dessert.

These s’mores nachos are a sweet way to end your BBQ celebration on a high note.

Conclusion

With these 10 must-try BBQ recipes, your Fourth of July celebration is sure to be a hit! From juicy ribs to refreshing veggie skewers and indulgent desserts, these dishes offer something for everyone. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and get ready to enjoy a delicious feast filled with summer flavors. Happy grilling!
