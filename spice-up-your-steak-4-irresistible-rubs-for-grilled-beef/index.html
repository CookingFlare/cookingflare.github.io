Spice Up Your Steak: 4 Irresistible Rubs for Grilled Beef


When it comes to grilled beef, a good steak is always a crowd-pleaser. But let’s face it, a plain old grilled steak can get a bit…boring. That’s where a flavorful rub comes in. A well-crafted rub can elevate your steak game, adding depth, complexity, and a burst of flavor to every bite. In this post, we’ll explore four irresistible rubs that will spice up your grilled beef and leave your taste buds begging for more.

Rub 1: Classic Steakhouse Rub

Inspired by the classic steakhouses of the American West, this rub combines the richness of coffee and the earthiness of thyme to create a flavor profile that’s both familiar and comforting. To make it, simply mix together 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of kosher salt, 1 tablespoon of freshly ground black pepper, 1 tablespoon of instant coffee powder, and 2 sprigs of fresh thyme.

Rub 2: Spicy Chipotle Rub

For those who like a little heat in their lives, this spicy chipotle rub is a game-changer. Smoked paprika and chipotle peppers in adobo sauce add a deep, smoky heat that’s balanced by the brightness of lime juice and the earthiness of cumin. To make it, combine 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of kosher salt, 1 tablespoon of smoked paprika, 1 tablespoon of chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lime juice, and 1 teaspoon of ground cumin.

Rub 3: Mediterranean Herb Rub

Inspired by the sun-kissed hills of the Mediterranean, this herb rub combines the freshness of oregano and thyme with the warmth of garlic and lemon. It’s the perfect accompaniment to a grilled ribeye or strip steak. To make it, mix together 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of kosher salt, 1 tablespoon of freshly ground black pepper, 2 sprigs of fresh oregano, 1 sprig of fresh thyme, 1 clove of garlic, minced, and 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice.

Rub 4: Asian-Inspired Five-Spice Rub

For a flavor profile that’s both exotic and familiar, try this Asian-inspired five-spice rub. A combination of cinnamon, cloves, and star anise adds warmth and depth, while the sweetness of brown sugar and the brightness of soy sauce balance out the flavors. To make it, combine 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of kosher salt, 1 tablespoon of five-spice powder, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of freshly ground black pepper, and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger.

Tips and Tricks

  • Always pat your steak dry with paper towels before applying the rub to ensure the flavors penetrate evenly.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different types of meat – these rubs work beautifully on pork chops, chicken breasts, and even vegetables.
  • When grilling, make sure to cook your steak to your desired level of doneness. For a perfectly grilled steak, aim for an internal temperature of 130°F to 135°F for medium-rare.
  • To make the rubs ahead of time, simply store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.

With these four irresistible rubs, you’ll never have to settle for a boring grilled steak again. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a culinary newbie, these recipes are sure to impress. So go ahead, spice up your steak, and get ready to fire up the grill!
