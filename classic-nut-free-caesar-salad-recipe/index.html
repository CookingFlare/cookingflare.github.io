Classic Nut-Free Caesar Salad Recipe


Are you a salad lover looking for a classic Caesar salad recipe that is nut-free? Look no further! This recipe is a perfect blend of flavors and textures, and it’s also safe for those with nut allergies. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 head of romaine lettuce, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup croutons
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, mashed (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large salad bowl, add the chopped romaine lettuce. Make sure to wash and dry the lettuce thoroughly to remove any dirt or grit.

  2. Add the croutons to the bowl. You can use store-bought croutons or make your own by cutting bread into small cubes and toasting them in the oven until crispy.

  3. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the lettuce and croutons. The cheese adds a rich and tangy flavor to the salad.

  4. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, mashed anchovy fillets (if using), lemon juice, and olive oil. This creamy dressing is the heart of a classic Caesar salad.

  5. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss well to ensure that every leaf is coated in the delicious dressing. Make sure to use enough dressing to evenly coat the salad without making it soggy.

  6. Season the salad with salt and pepper to taste. The anchovies in the dressing already add a salty kick, so be mindful of how much extra salt you add.

  7. Serve the salad immediately after tossing to ensure the freshness of the ingredients. The crispiness of the romaine lettuce and the crunch of the croutons make this salad a delight to eat.

  8. Optionally, you can garnish the salad with some additional grated Parmesan cheese and freshly ground black pepper for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • If you’re a vegetarian or simply don’t enjoy anchovies, you can omit them from the dressing. The other ingredients will still create a delicious Caesar salad flavor.

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add grilled chicken or shrimp to make it a more substantial meal.

  • If you’re watching your calorie intake, you can use a light mayonnaise or substitute it with Greek yogurt for a healthier dressing option.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of lettuce or add other vegetables like cherry tomatoes or sliced cucumbers to customize the salad to your liking.

  • To make the croutons extra flavorful, toss them in melted butter and sprinkle them with garlic powder or Italian seasoning before toasting them in the oven.

Final Thoughts

This classic nut-free Caesar salad recipe is a timeless favorite that is sure to please everyone at your table. The creamy dressing, crunchy croutons, and tangy Parmesan cheese create a harmonious combination of flavors that will leave you craving more. Whether you’re serving it as a side dish or a main course, this salad is a delicious and nutritious addition to any meal. So why wait? Give it a try and prepare to be amazed by the simplicity and deliciousness of this classic recipe!
