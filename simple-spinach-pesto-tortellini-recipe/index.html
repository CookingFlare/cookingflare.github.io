Simple Spinach Pesto Tortellini Recipe


Fresh, light, and packed with flavor, spinach pesto tortellini is one of those recipes that brings gourmet-level taste to your table without demanding hours in the kitchen. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or an impressive dish to serve at a family gathering, this recipe ticks all the boxes. Combining the vibrant flavors of homemade spinach pesto with pillowy tortellini, you’re in for a treat that’s as satisfying as it is easy to whip up.

This recipe celebrates simplicity, showcasing a handful of fresh ingredients that come together harmoniously. It’s a fantastic way to introduce more greens into your meals while enjoying a rich and creamy sauce that feels indulgent. Plus, spinach pesto is a game-changer—it’s versatile, nutrient-packed, and provides a delightful twist to the classic basil pesto.

Let’s jump straight into crafting this delicious dish!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this spinach pesto tortellini recipe, here’s what you need for both the pesto and the tortellini:

For the Spinach Pesto:

  • 4 cups fresh spinach leaves (packed)
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves (optional, for added flavor balance)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts or walnuts
  • 1/3 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (plus more if needed for desired consistency)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Tortellini:

  • 16 ounces (1 pound) fresh or store-bought tortellini (cheese-filled works beautifully!)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (optional, for added creaminess)
  • Extra Parmesan for garnish

Instructions: Step-by-Step Guide

Making the Spinach Pesto

  1. Prepare Your Ingredients: Wash the spinach and basil leaves thoroughly and pat them dry. This helps avoid a watery pesto and ensures the vibrant green color shines through.

  2. Toast the Nuts: Lightly toast the pine nuts or walnuts in a dry pan over medium heat. Keep a close eye so they don’t burn. Toasting enhances their natural nutty flavor, adding depth to your pesto.

  3. Blend the Pesto: In a food processor, combine the spinach, basil (if using), toasted nuts, garlic, Parmesan cheese, and lemon juice. Pulse a few times to break everything down. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while processing until the mixture becomes smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more olive oil if it’s too thick.

  4. Season: Taste your spinach pesto and add salt and pepper as needed. If you want a tangier twist, a bit more lemon juice works wonders—just remember to balance the flavors.

  5. Set Aside: Once done, transfer the pesto to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap or a lid to keep the vibrant green color intact.

Preparing the Tortellini

  1. Cook the Tortellini: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the tortellini according to the package instructions. Fresh tortellini usually cooks in just 3-5 minutes, while frozen tortellini might take 8-10 minutes. The goal is to achieve al dente perfection, so keep an eye on it!

  2. Drain and Toss: Once cooked, reserve about 1/4 cup of the pasta water in case you need it later. Drain the tortellini well and set aside.

  3. Create the Sauce Base (Optional): In a large skillet, heat the olive oil and butter over medium heat until melted. If you’re using heavy cream for extra richness, pour it in now. Stir gently and allow it to simmer until well combined.

  4. Add the Pesto: Reduce the heat to low and fold in the spinach pesto. Blend the sauce thoroughly with the base until it coats the back of a spoon. If the sauce thickens too much, add a splash of the reserved pasta water for a silkier texture.

  5. Combine: Add the cooked tortellini to the skillet and gently toss to coat every piece with the luscious spinach pesto sauce. Be careful not to break the delicate tortellini.

Serving Your Spinach Pesto Tortellini

Plate the tortellini while it’s hot. Sprinkle freshly grated Parmesan cheese on top for an extra cheesy punch. You can also garnish with a handful of chopped basil or a drizzle of olive oil for that Instagram-worthy finish. This dish pairs beautifully with a crisp side salad or some warm garlic bread for a complete meal that’s sure to wow everyone at the table.

Spinach pesto tortellini is best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, they’ll keep well in the fridge for up to 3 days in an airtight container. Reheat gently on the stovetop or microwave, adding a splash of water or cream to revive the sauce’s creamy texture.

Customizing Your Spinach Pesto Tortellini

This recipe isn’t just delicious—it’s also incredibly adaptable. Here are some ideas to make it your own:

  1. Add Protein: Grilled chicken, sautéed shrimp, or crispy pancetta can be phenomenal additions if you want to pack the dish with a bit more substance.

  2. Include Veggies: Toss in some cherry tomatoes, roasted bell peppers, or steamed broccoli for added color and nutritional value.

  3. Try Different Greens: While spinach is the star here, you can experiment with other greens like arugula or kale for unique flavor profiles.

  4. Go Vegan or Dairy-Free: Swap Parmesan for a dairy-free alternative, and use vegan tortellini if available to suit dietary restrictions.

  5. Nut-Free Version: Replace the nuts in the pesto with sunflower seeds for a completely nut-free recipe.

Why Spinach Pesto Tortellini is a Must-Try

Spinach pesto tortellini isn’t just a meal—it’s an experience. The velvety tortellini with creamy filling provides the perfect base for the zesty, earthy pesto. The spinach pesto not only adds bold flavor but also brings extra vitamins and antioxidants to the table, making this dish both indulgent and nutritious.

This recipe is ideal for busy individuals who crave homemade meals but don’t have hours to spare. It’s quick to prepare, easy to customize, and doesn’t require advanced culinary skills—a win-win for novices and seasoned cooks alike.

If you’re new to making homemade pesto, this recipe is a great stepping stone to mastering it. Once you see how simple and versatile it is, you’ll find yourself experimenting with more variations, from classic basil pesto to unique blends using ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes or roasted red peppers.

Pro Tips for Success

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: The fresher your spinach, basil, and garlic, the better your pesto will taste. Aim to use them soon after buying to maximize flavor.

  2. Mind the Nuts: Toasting nuts is optional but recommended for maximum flavor. Just be cautious not to over-toast, as this can lend a bitter taste.

  3. Don’t Skip the Lemon Juice: The acidity of the lemon both brightens the pesto and helps preserve its vibrant green hue.

  4. Reserve That Pasta Water: The starchy water from cooking tortellini is a natural thickener and can help emulsify the sauce.

  5. Work Quickly With Tortellini: Dried-out tortellini isn’t pleasant, so toss it with the sauce immediately after draining to keep it moist and flavorful.

Spinach pesto tortellini is a dish you’ll find yourself returning to time and time again. It’s proof that simple ingredients can create extraordinary results. Gather your ingredients and give this recipe a try—you won’t be disappointed with the incredible flavors waiting to take center stage at your table.
