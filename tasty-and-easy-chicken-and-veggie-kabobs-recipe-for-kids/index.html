Tasty and Easy Chicken and Veggie Kabobs Recipe for Kids


Are you looking for a fun and delicious way to get your kids excited about eating their veggies? Look no further! These tasty and easy chicken and veggie kabobs are not only a breeze to make, but they are also a hit with kids of all ages. Packed with colorful veggies and flavorful chicken, these kabobs are perfect for a family barbecue or a simple weeknight dinner. Let’s dive into the recipe and make some magic happen in the kitchen!

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • Wooden or metal skewers
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preparation: If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during cooking. Meanwhile, prepare the chicken and veggies by cutting them into the specified sizes.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with a drizzle of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the chicken evenly and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.

  3. Assemble the Kabobs: Take the soaked wooden skewers or metal skewers and start threading the marinated chicken, bell peppers, red onion, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes onto the skewers, alternating the ingredients to create colorful patterns.

  4. Grill the Kabobs: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the grill grates with oil to prevent sticking. Carefully place the assembled kabobs on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the veggies are slightly charred.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the kabobs are cooked to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. Serve the kabobs with a side of rice, couscous, or a fresh salad for a complete and nutritious meal.

Tips for Success

  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought Italian dressing or teriyaki sauce to marinate the chicken for extra flavor.
  • Encourage your kids to help assemble the kabobs. It’s a great way to get them involved in the kitchen and excited about the meal.
  • Feel free to customize the veggies based on your kids’ preferences. Mushrooms, cherry tomatoes, and chunks of pineapple also work well on kabobs.

Why Kids Love This Recipe

These chicken and veggie kabobs are not only visually appealing but also offer a variety of flavors and textures that kids enjoy. The colorful veggies and tender chicken make eating healthy a fun and enjoyable experience. By involving your kids in the cooking process and allowing them to create their own kabobs, you can instill a sense of pride and excitement about the meal.

Final Thoughts

With this simple and delicious recipe, you can introduce your kids to the joys of grilling and healthy eating. These chicken and veggie kabobs are a versatile option for family gatherings, weeknight dinners, or even lunchbox surprises. So fire up the grill, gather the family, and savor the delightful flavors of these tasty kabobs. Happy cooking!
