Authentic Spanakopita Recipe: Greek Spinach Pie Made Easy


Spanakopita, the beloved Greek spinach pie, is a cherished gem of Mediterranean cuisine. Packed with nutritious spinach, creamy feta, fragrant herbs, and encased in flaky layers of golden phyllo dough, this dish is as satisfying as it is visually stunning. Whether served as an appetizer or the star of your meal, Spanakopita is a timeless treasure that brings people together around the dinner table. Today, we’re sharing a foolproof, authentic recipe that makes this Greek classic easy and accessible for home cooks.

A Brief History of Spanakopita

Spanakopita has been a staple of Greek cuisine for centuries, paying homage to Greece’s agricultural bounty and food traditions. Its roots date back to ancient Mediterranean civilizations, where phyllo dough (meaning “leaf” in Greek) was crafted to envelop flavorful fillings. Over the years, Spanakopita has become synonymous with hospitality, and it’s often served at family gatherings, celebrations, and special occasions. The perfect marriage of flaky pastry and hearty spinach makes this dish irresistible and universally loved.

Ingredients Overview

Before diving into the recipe, let’s break down the core ingredients that make Spanakopita shine. Each component plays a crucial role in creating the perfect balance of flavor and texture.

Spinach

Fresh spinach is the star of this recipe. Packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, spinach creates the hearty filling for this pie. While fresh spinach is preferred, frozen spinach can also be used if it’s thoroughly defrosted and drained.

Feta Cheese

A traditional Greek staple, feta cheese is an essential ingredient that gives Spanakopita its tangy, salty richness. Opt for authentic Greek feta to ensure the best flavor.

Aromatic Herbs

The addition of parsley, dill, and green onions brings depth and freshness to the filling, delivering that unmistakable Mediterranean essence.

Eggs

Eggs act as a binder for the filling, giving it structure and a rich layer of flavor.

Phyllo Dough

This delicate, paper-thin pastry is the hallmark of Spanakopita. Its flaky texture and buttery richness are key to the pie’s appeal. Phyllo dough can often be found in the frozen section of most supermarkets.

Olive Oil and Butter

Greek olive oil serves as the glue that holds the dough together, while melted butter adds luxurious flavor to every bite.

Tools You’ll Need

To prepare authentic Spanakopita, you’ll need the following kitchen tools:

  • A large skillet
  • Mixing bowls
  • A sharp knife and cutting board
  • A pastry brush
  • A rectangular baking dish
  • Clean kitchen towels or cheesecloth (for draining spinach)

Step-By-Step Spanakopita Recipe

Servings: 8

Prep Time: 30 Minutes

Cook Time: 45 Minutes

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 1 lb fresh spinach (or 1 lb frozen spinach, defrosted and squeezed dry)
  • 8 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

For the Phyllo Layers:

  • 1 pack phyllo dough, thawed according to package instructions
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

1. Prepare the Filling

Start by cleaning and trimming the spinach. If using fresh spinach, blanch it in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then transfer it to a bowl of ice water to cool. Drain thoroughly and squeeze out any excess moisture using a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth. Chop the spinach into small, manageable pieces.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the spinach, crumbled feta, green onions, dill, parsley, and beaten eggs. Season the mixture with salt and black pepper to taste. Be mindful when adding salt, as feta is naturally salty. Stir well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Set the filling aside.

2. Prep the Phyllo Dough

Phyllo dough can be delicate, so handle it with care. Place the dough sheets between two damp kitchen towels to prevent them from drying out. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a rectangular baking dish with olive oil.

3. Assemble the Spanakopita

Layering the phyllo dough is crucial for achieving a golden, flaky texture. Work quickly but gently.

Start by placing one sheet of phyllo dough at the bottom of the greased baking dish. Lightly brush the dough with the melted butter and olive oil mixture. Repeat this process, layering 6-8 sheets of phyllo, brushing each layer with butter and oil.

Spread the spinach filling evenly over the layered phyllo base. Then, cover the filling with another 8 layers of phyllo dough, brushing each layer with the butter-oil mixture. Tuck the edges neatly to seal the filling inside the flaky crust.

Brush the very top layer generously with butter and sprinkle a few drops of water to prevent the dough from over-crisping as it bakes.

4. Score the Pie

Using a sharp knife, lightly score the top layers of the phyllo dough to create small squares or diamond shapes. Be careful not to cut all the way through the filling; this step allows steam to escape and makes portioning easier after baking.

5. Bake to Perfection

Transfer the baking dish to the preheated oven and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crisp. Keep an eye on the pie as it bakes, and rotate the dish halfway through for even cooking.

6. Cool and Serve

Allow the Spanakopita to cool for 10-15 minutes after baking. This short resting period helps the layers set and makes it easier to slice into neat portions. Serve warm as an appetizer, side dish, or centerpiece for your meal. Pair it with tzatziki sauce, a crisp green salad, or some marinated olives for the ultimate Mediterranean spread.

Tips for Success

  • Defrost the Phyllo Dough Properly: Always thaw phyllo dough in the refrigerator overnight to prevent cracks and tearing during use.
  • Prevent Soggy Layers: Drain the spinach fully to remove any excess moisture that could compromise the pie’s texture.
  • Work Quickly: Phyllo dough dries out rapidly, so gather all your tools and ingredients before starting the layering process.
  • Customize to Taste: Feel free to add additional herbs and spices to the filling, such as nutmeg, mint, or oregano, to suit your palate.
  • Make-Ahead Option: Assemble the Spanakopita ahead of time, cover it tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate. Bake it fresh when ready to serve.

Why Spanakopita is Worth Mastering

Mastering the art of Spanakopita not only allows you to create a dish that’s brimming with flavor but also connects you to the rich culinary traditions of Greece. Its combination of simple ingredients transforms into something truly special—a flaky, buttery crust that contrasts beautifully with the savory spinach-feta filling. Every bite is a celebration of wholesome, authentic flavors.

Plus, this recipe is endlessly versatile. You can create individual spinach pies, called spanakopitakia, for portable, grab-and-go portions. Or, if you prefer, use puff pastry as a substitute for phyllo dough to save on prep time without compromising taste.

Wrap-Up

Spanakopita is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of culture, flavor, and connection. Whether you’re preparing it for family, friends, or simply yourself, this authentic Greek spinach pie is guaranteed to delight and impress. With this easy-to-follow recipe, you’ll be able to embrace the spirit of Mediterranean cooking and enjoy a dish that’s as nourishing as it is delicious. Give it a try, and you’ll soon find that Spanakopita becomes a regular feature in your cooking repertoire!
