Homemade Tart Cherry Jam Recipe: A Sweet Spread for All Occasions


Who doesn’t love a dollop of sweet, tangy jam on their morning toast or scone? If you’re a fan of homemade preserves, then you’re in for a treat with this Homemade Tart Cherry Jam recipe. Made with juicy, ripe tart cherries, this jam will elevate your breakfast and add a burst of flavor to your favorite desserts. Plus, it’s incredibly easy to make! So, let’s dive right into the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of fresh tart cherries, pitted and halved
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Mix well and let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes. This will allow the cherries to release their juices and meld with the other ingredients.

  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Stir frequently to prevent the sugar from burning. As the cherries cook, they will soften and release even more juice.

  3. Once the mixture has reached a simmer, reduce the heat to low and continue to cook for about 45 minutes to an hour. Stir occasionally to ensure the jam doesn’t stick to the bottom of the pan. The jam will gradually thicken and develop a glossy appearance.

  4. To test if the jam is ready, place a small plate in the freezer for a few minutes. Spoon a small amount of the jam onto the cold plate and run your finger through it. If the jam holds its shape and doesn’t immediately fill the gap, it’s done. If not, continue cooking for a few more minutes and test again.

  5. Once the jam has reached the desired consistency, remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. The jam will thicken further as it cools.

  6. While the jam is still warm, carefully transfer it to sterilized jars, leaving about ¼ inch of headspace at the top. Seal the jars tightly and let them cool completely before storing them in the refrigerator.

  7. Your Homemade Tart Cherry Jam is now ready to be enjoyed! Spread it on toast, scones, or use it as a filling for pastries and cakes. The possibilities are endless!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a smoother jam, you can use an immersion blender or a food processor to blend the cooked cherries before transferring them to the jars.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a pinch of cinnamon or a splash of almond extract to the jam during the cooking process.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved, depending on the quantity of cherries you have on hand.

  • If you don’t have access to fresh tart cherries, you can use frozen cherries instead. Just make sure to thaw them before using in the recipe.

  • Homemade Tart Cherry Jam makes a wonderful homemade gift. Simply package it in a decorative jar, add a pretty label, and share the love with your friends and family.

So there you have it – a simple and delicious recipe for Homemade Tart Cherry Jam. With just a few ingredients and some patience, you can create a homemade spread that will impress your taste buds and elevate your breakfast or dessert game. So go ahead, grab a spoonful, and indulge in the sweet and tangy goodness of homemade jam!
