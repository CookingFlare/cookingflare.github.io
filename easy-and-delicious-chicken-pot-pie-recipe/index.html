Easy and Delicious Chicken Pot Pie Recipe


There’s something incredibly comforting about a chicken pot pie. It’s the kind of dish that wraps you in warmth and brings a bit of nostalgia with every bite. Whether you’re preparing it for a family dinner, special occasion, or simply indulging yourself on a cozy evening, this recipe will deliver a flaky, golden crust paired with a creamy, flavor-packed filling that’s nothing short of unforgettable. The best part? It’s straightforward to make with just a handful of ingredients you’re likely to already have in your kitchen.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Chicken pot pie is a classic crowd-pleaser, and this recipe simplifies the process without sacrificing the rich and traditional flavors of this beloved dish. By opting for an easy preparation method, you can comfortably whip this up on a busy weeknight or spend less time in the kitchen when entertaining friends and family. Featuring tender chicken, wholesome vegetables, and an irresistibly creamy sauce encased in buttery, flaky crusts, this dish truly offers everything you could want in a savory homemade pie.

This recipe captures that “made-from-scratch” taste while saving you time and effort. With minimal prep and just the right touch of homecooked goodness, you’ll be adding this one to your regular meal rotation in no time.

Ingredients Needed for Chicken Pot Pie

Here’s what you’ll need to make this flavorful chicken pot pie:

For the Chicken Filling:

  • 2 cups of cooked, shredded or diced chicken (rotisserie chicken works beautifully for convenience)
  • 1 cup of frozen peas and carrots (no need to thaw)
  • 1 cup of diced celery
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 3 tablespoons of flour (for thickening)
  • 1 ¾ cup of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Pinch of garlic powder

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts (store-bought works great, or you can use homemade if you prefer)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep the Ingredients

Before diving into the cooking process, ensure all of your ingredients are prepped and ready to go. Dice and shred your chicken, chop your vegetables, and measure out your ingredients. This saves time and keeps everything organized as you cook.

If you’re using uncooked chicken, poach or bake it first, season with a little salt and pepper, then shred or dice it.

2. Make the Savory Filling

In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the diced onions and celery, stirring until they soften and start to become translucent (around 3-5 minutes). Sprinkle in the flour and stir constantly. Let it cook for about 1-2 minutes to form a roux, which will help thicken your sauce.

Next, slowly pour in the chicken broth while stirring to prevent lumps from forming. Follow this with the milk, continuing to stir until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy. This usually only takes about 4-5 minutes.

Season the creamy mixture with salt, pepper, thyme, and garlic powder to taste. Once the sauce has reached a thick, gravy-like consistency, gently fold in the cooked chicken and frozen peas and carrots. Stir until everything is well-coated and evenly distributed. Remove the skillet from the heat and allow the filling to cool slightly.

3. Prepare the Pie Crust

Roll out your pre-made or homemade pie crust and gently press it into a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges, leaving about half an inch. Set the second crust aside for later.

Transfer the slightly cooled chicken filling into the prepared pie crust. Spread it out evenly to ensure every slice will have a hearty and balanced filling.

4. Top and Seal the Pie

Carefully place the second pie crust on top of the filling. Pinch the edges of both crusts together to seal them. If desired, use a fork to create a decorative crimped edge along the perimeter.

Take a sharp knife and cut a few small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape while baking. Brush the top with the beaten egg for a beautiful golden brown finish.

5. Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 400°F (204°C). Once heated, place the assembled pie on the middle rack. Bake for about 30-35 minutes, or until the crust turns golden brown and flaky, and the filling bubbles slightly through the slits in the crust.

Keep a close eye during the last few minutes to ensure the edges don’t burn. If the edges brown too quickly, cover them gently with strips of aluminum foil.

6. Rest and Serve

Once the pie is out of the oven, resist the urge to dive in immediately. Allow the pie to rest for about 10-15 minutes before slicing. This helps the filling set and makes it easier to cut neat slices.

Serve warm, and enjoy!

Tips and Tricks for Perfect Chicken Pot Pie

  1. Make it Ahead of Time
    This recipe is perfect for meal prepping. You can assemble the pie ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to a day before baking.

  2. Customize the Vegetables
    While peas, carrots, onions, and celery are classic, feel free to swap in your favorites, like diced potatoes, corn, or green beans. Fresh vegetables work just as well as frozen ones; just make sure to cook them briefly before using.

  3. Use Store-Bought Rotisserie Chicken
    To save time, pick up a rotisserie chicken from the grocery store. Its pre-cooked, juicy meat is perfect for this recipe.

  4. Try a Puff Pastry Top
    If you’re feeling adventurous, swap out the pie crust for puff pastry. It creates an extra-crispy, flaky topping that pairs beautifully with the creamy filling.

  5. Freeze for Later
    Chicken pot pie is wonderfully freezer-friendly. Assemble the pie, wrap it tightly in foil, and freeze it for up to 3 months. When ready to eat, bake directly from frozen, adding about 20-30 minutes to the cooking time.

Pairing Suggestions

Chicken pot pie is a filling meal on its own, but you can pair it with a simple side for a complete dinner. A fresh, crisp green salad or tangy coleslaw complements the richness of the pie beautifully. You could also serve it alongside roasted vegetables or a loaf of warm, crusty bread to soak up any leftover filling.

Final Thoughts

With its buttery crust and creamy filling, it delivers all the comfort food vibes you could dream of with minimal fuss. Give it a try, and chances are high that it’ll become a cherished staple in your kitchen.
