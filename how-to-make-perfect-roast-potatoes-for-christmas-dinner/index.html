How to Make Perfect Roast Potatoes for Christmas Dinner


Christmas dinner is a time for family, friends, and feasting. Among the various dishes that grace the holiday table, roast potatoes hold a special place. Crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside, they are the perfect accompaniment to your festive meal. In this guide, we’ll explore the secrets to achieving the perfect roast potatoes that will impress your guests and elevate your Christmas dinner.

Choosing the Right Potatoes

The first step to perfect roast potatoes starts with selecting the right type of potato. For roasting, the best varieties are Maris Piper or King Edward. These potatoes have a high starch content, which contributes to their fluffy interior while achieving a crisp exterior when roasted.

Preparing the Potatoes

  1. Peeling and Chopping: Start by peeling your potatoes. Once peeled, chop them into even-sized chunks, approximately 1 to 2 inches. This ensures they cook evenly. Aim for uniformity to avoid some pieces being overcooked while others remain undercooked.

  2. Soaking: After chopping, soak the potatoes in cold water for at least 30 minutes. This step is crucial as it removes excess starch, leading to an even crispier potato. If you have the time, soaking them overnight in the refrigerator can yield even better results.

  3. Parboiling: Drain the soaked potatoes and place them in a large pot of salted water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Parboil the potatoes for about 10 minutes until they are just tender but not falling apart. This step helps to create a fluffy interior.

  4. Draining and Fluffing: Once parboiled, drain the potatoes in a colander. Allow them to steam dry for a few minutes. This is where the magic happens—use a fork to gently fluff the edges of the potatoes. This fluffing creates more surface area, which will become wonderfully crispy during roasting.

Choosing the Right Fat

The choice of fat can make or break your roast potatoes. Traditional options include goose fat or duck fat, both of which impart incredible flavor and crispness. However, for a vegetarian or lighter option, olive oil or sunflower oil works beautifully as well.

Preheating the Fat

  1. Roasting Tray: Before adding the potatoes, preheat your roasting tray with the fat of your choice. Place the tray in the oven while it preheats to 425°F (220°C). This ensures that the fat is sizzling hot when you add the potatoes, creating a crispy exterior right from the start.

  2. Adding the Potatoes: Carefully add the fluffed potatoes to the hot fat in the roasting tray. Be cautious, as the oil may splatter. Toss the potatoes gently to coat them evenly in the fat.

Seasoning for Flavor

Seasoning is an essential component of delicious roast potatoes.

  1. Salt: Coat the potatoes generously with sea salt. This enhances their flavor and aids in creating a crispy crust.

  2. Herbs and Spices: For added depth, consider incorporating fresh herbs. Rosemary and thyme are classic choices. Toss a few sprigs into the roasting tray before placing it in the oven. Garlic cloves, either whole or smashed, can also add a delightful aroma and flavor to your roast potatoes.

The Roasting Process

  1. Initial Roast: Place the roasting tray in the preheated oven and roast for 20 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door during this time, as it will cause the temperature to drop and affect the crisping process.

  2. Turning for Even Crispness: After the initial 20 minutes, use a spatula to gently turn the potatoes. This will help them brown evenly. Return the tray to the oven for another 20-30 minutes.

  3. Final Crisping: Keep a close eye on the potatoes during the last 10 minutes of roasting. You want them to develop a deep golden color. If they seem to be browning too quickly, reduce the oven temperature slightly.

Serving Suggestions

Once your perfect roast potatoes have achieved a golden, crispy exterior, it’s time to serve them. Transfer them to a serving dish and sprinkle with a bit more sea salt, along with freshly chopped herbs for garnish.

Pairing with Your Christmas Dinner

Roast potatoes are incredibly versatile, making them a great companion for a variety of Christmas dishes. Whether you’re serving them alongside a juicy roast turkey, succulent beef, or a vegetarian nut roast, their flavor will complement any main course beautifully.

Storage and Reheating Tips

If you have leftovers (though they are unlikely!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. To reheat, spread the potatoes on a baking sheet and place them in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) until heated through and crispy again. This method helps to revive their texture.

Final Thoughts

Perfect roast potatoes can elevate your Christmas dinner from ordinary to extraordinary. With the right potatoes, preparation, fat, and seasoning, you can create a dish that not only satisfies but becomes the highlight of your festive meal. Whether it’s the delightful crunch or the fluffy interior, your guests will be clamoring for seconds.

As you gather around the table this holiday season, remember the joy that comes from sharing good food with loved ones. With these tips in hand, you’re well on your way to making the perfect roast potatoes that will surely steal the show. Happy cooking, and Merry Christmas!
