Deadly Delicious Dip: Roasted Garlic & Ghost Pepper Salsa


Embark on a culinary adventure with our Roasted Garlic & Ghost Pepper Salsa, a deadly mix of rich flavors that’ll have your taste buds cheering. This dip, with the perfect balance of roasted garlic’s sweetness and ghost pepper’s scorching heat, is guaranteed to rock your world!

Ingredients:

  • 3 Large Poblano Peppers
  • 2 lbs of Choice Tomatoes (preferably Roma or San Marzano)
  • 1 Medium White Onion
  • 1 cup Fresh Cilantro
  • 2 Medium Garlic Heads
  • 1 Ghost Pepper (adjust according to your heat preference)
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Ground Cumin
  • Juice of 1 Lime
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1/2 cup Water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Your Oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Begin by throwing those two garlic heads into the oven. Sit tight; they’ll need about 45 minutes to achieve the perfect softness and aroma.

  3. Meanwhile, on the stovetop, give those roasted Poblano peppers a heads-up. Grill them over medium heat until their skin is completely blackened. Then, put them in a sealed plastic baggie to sweat out the charring for about 15 minutes. Detach the stems, peel, and discard the skin. Blend them into a smooth purée.

  4. Grab your trusty oven mitt and pull out those golden garlic, allowing them to cool. Then, once they’re cool enough to handle, squeeze out the soft, fragrant cloves. No need to peel; just mash to a paste using a fork.

  5. Hint of spice! Time to tackle that spicy ghost pepper. Wear gloves to avoid any fiery encounters; then, finely chop the hell raiser. We understand if you can’t take the heat, but feel free to adjust to your desired fiery level.

  6. It’s time to get the tomato party started! Halve the tomatoes, ensuring the seeds stay intact (they’re nature’s thickener). Place them cut-side up onto a baking sheet lined with some parchment paper, and slide it into your oven beside the remaining garlic. Bake them for about 30 minutes or until they are quite shrinked.

  7. When the verde stars align, pull out that sheet of fire-roasted tomatoes and cool them down. Once they’re cool, add them, along with their juice, to the puréed Poblano peppers. Grab that freshly squeezed lime juice and pour it in to achieve that tangy zing.

  8. Dice that onion and roughly chop the cilantro. Add your garlic paste, the chopped ghost pepper, cumin, salt, water, olive oil to that party mix of peppers and tomatoes. Then, let your culinary magic reign as you mix everything together until smooth. If you favor a texture in your salsa, a little bit of chunk remains.

  9. Test the fire! Give a taste to tweak any seasoning. If you’re happy with the deadly delicious concoction, it’s time for chilling. Stick it in the refrigerator for at least two hours but preferably overnight. That will allow the flavors to come together and make magic happen.

  10. Ta-da! Serve your Roasted Garlic & Ghost Pepper Salsa alongside tortilla chips, and be prepared for happiness-inducing snacking. This sizzling salsa goes beyond the ordinary, promising a journey through rich, fiery, and tangy flavors.

Embarking on this fiesta of flavors, each bite of your Roasted Garlic & Ghost Pepper Salsa will leave you smacking your lips, asking for more. So, dare to indulge in our Deadly Delicious Dip and relish the spice of life!
