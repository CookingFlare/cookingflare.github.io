Moist and Flavorful Apple Cinnamon Cupcakes: A Fall-Inspired Treat


As the leaves start to change color and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to indulge in the warm and comforting flavors of fall. And what better way to do that than with delicious, moist, and flavorful apple cinnamon cupcakes? These little treats are a delightful combination of the sweet, slightly tart flavor of apples and the warm, spicy notes of cinnamon, making them a perfect addition to your autumn baking repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Cupcakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cups finely chopped apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)

For the Cinnamon Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake tin with paper liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon.

  3. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the sour cream and milk. Mix until just combined. Gently fold in the chopped apples.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.

  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  7. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the cinnamon cream cheese frosting. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon, and beat until creamy and well combined.

  8. Once the cupcakes are completely cool, frost them with the cinnamon cream cheese frosting using a piping bag or offset spatula.

  9. For an extra touch of fall flavor, you can sprinkle a little extra ground cinnamon on top of the frosted cupcakes.

Serving and Storing

These moist and flavorful apple cinnamon cupcakes are best enjoyed within a few days of baking. Store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator to maintain their freshness. Serve them at your next autumn gathering, or simply savor them as a cozy treat with a cup of hot apple cider or a steaming mug of coffee.

Conclusion

With their delightful blend of seasonal flavors and moist, tender crumb, these apple cinnamon cupcakes are sure to become a fall baking favorite. Whether you’re hosting a fall-themed gathering or simply craving a taste of autumn, these cupcakes are the perfect way to celebrate the season. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to fill your home with the irresistible aroma of apple and cinnamon as you bake up a batch of these delightful fall-inspired treats.
