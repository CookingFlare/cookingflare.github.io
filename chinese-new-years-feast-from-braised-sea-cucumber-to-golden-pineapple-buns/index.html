Chinese New Year's Feast: From Braised Sea Cucumber to Golden Pineapple Buns


Chinese New Year is celebrated with great excitement throughout China and globally, marking the beginning of the Lunar Year. At the heart of this festive season is the Chinese New Year’s feast, a splendid culinary celebration bursting with rich flavors and diverse dishes. In this blog post, we’ll explore the delightful entrees of Braised Sea Cucumber and the scrumptious Golden Pineapple Buns, while maintaining the festive atmosphere.

Beginning with the Braised Sea Cucumber

The braised sea cucumber is a traditional and aptly luxurious dish that marks the celebration of the Chinese New Year. This delicacy, believed to bring longevity and prosperity, is a mandatory dish at any Chinese New Year’s feast. So, let’s begin by preparing a mouthwatering braised sea cucumber!

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh sea cucumber
  • 50g dried black mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp ginger juice
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 cup chicken stock or water
  • 2 tbsp rice wine (shaoxing wine)
  • Salt and white pepper
  • Chopped pork or chicken skin (optional)

Preparation:

1. Preparation of Sea Cucumber:

a) Rinse the sea cucumber thoroughly under running water, removing any sand or dirt. b) Prick the sea cucumber with a fork in several places to help expel air. c) Soak the sea cucumber in water with 1 teaspoon of salt for 3-4 hours, changing the water at least twice, before using. d) Rinse the sea cucumber again under running water and pat dry with paper towels. e) Slice the sea cucumber into bite-size pieces.

2. Preparation of Black Mushrooms:

a) Soak the dried black mushrooms in warm water for about 20 minutes or until soft. b) Rinse and remove the stems of the mushrooms. c) Slice the caps into bite-size pieces.

3. Cooking the Braised Sea Cucumber:

a) Heat the oil in a pot over medium heat. b) Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. c) Add ginger juice, minced garlic, and sliced mushrooms. Cook for about 2 minutes. d) Add sliced sea cucumber and cook for another minute. e) Season with salt, white pepper, fish sauce, and 1 tablespoon of rice wine; stir well. f) Pour in the chicken stock or water slowly, bringing it to a gentle simmer. g) Add the optional chopped pork or chicken skin and cook for about 2 hours. h) About 15 minutes before the dish is done, if the sauce is too watery, add 1-2 tablespoons of corn starch mixed in water to thicken. i) Turn off heat and sprinkle with some chopped green onions. Serve hot.

Next, the Golden Pineapple Buns

After satiating our appetite with the ecologically-rich Braised Sea Cucumber, our next journey leads to a golden crescendo – the delectable Golden Pineapple Buns. These delicate pastries are a beloved Hong Kong specialty that perfectly merge sweetness and flakiness.

Ingredients:

  • 500g bread flour
  • 190ml warm water
  • 45g sugar
  • 35g shortening
  • 15g dry yeast
  • 8g salt
  • 50g pineapple jam
  • 1 egg beaten (for egg wash)

Preparation:

1. Bread Dough:

Combine warm water, sugar, and yeast in a bowl. Let it sit for 10 minutes until frothy. In a large mixing bowl, add bread flour, shortening, salt, and the yeast mixture. Knead the dough for approximately 10 minutes until it forms a smooth and elastic texture. Place the dough in an oiled bowl and let it rise for about 1-1.5 hours in a warm and dark place, or until doubled in size.

2. Pineapple Filling:

In a separate bowl, mix pineapple jam, 1 tablespoon of water, and pineapple tidbits.

3. Putting It All Together:

After the dough has risen, deflate the dough and divide it into equal portions. Roll each portion into a round shape, akin to naan bread. Place a tablespoon of pineapple filling in the center of each portion. Fold the edges and pinch together to create a sealed dumpling-like bun. Ensure the filling is adequately covered by the dough to prevent spilling.

4. Shape the Golden Pineapple Buns:

To achieve the iconic golden pineapple shape, divide the edges of the dough into four equal portions. Take one portion from the bottom and pinch it upwards, creating a spiral effect. Repeat the process until the entire surface forms a golden pineapple-like rope. Optional, brush the top with egg wash before baking.

5. Baking:

Preheat your oven to 200°C. Arrange the shaped buns on a parchment-lined baking sheet, ensuring a 2-inch space between each bun. Allow them to rise for 30 minutes. Brush each bun gently with the egg wash and bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.

Conclusion

Celebrating Chinese New Year wouldn’t be complete without indulging in the sumptuousness of the Braised Sea Cucumber and savoring the sweetness of Golden Pineapple Buns. The rich history, tradition, and flavors enveloped in these dishes reignite the essence of Chinese New Year’s feast. Delight your guests and family this festive season with these cherished recipes. As the year unfurls its bright petals, conjure dishes that combine millennia-old customs with the exquisite taste of freshly baked pastries to extend joy, prosperity, and happiness during this special time of year. Happy Chinese New Year!
