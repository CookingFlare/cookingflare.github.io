How to Make Your Own Flour Tortillas


Homemade flour tortillas are a kitchen staple that bring unmatched flavor and texture to your meals. Whether you’re crafting burritos, tacos, quesadillas, or simply pairing them with a bowl of hearty soup, nothing beats the taste of a warm, freshly made tortilla. The process is surprisingly simple, and with just a few basic pantry items, you can make a batch of soft, pliable tortillas that elevate any dish. In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know for making your own flour tortillas at home—from selecting your ingredients to mastering the cooking technique.

Why Homemade Flour Tortillas Are Worth It

While store-bought tortillas are convenient, they’re often laden with preservatives and lack the flavor of homemade versions. Making flour tortillas from scratch lets you control the ingredients and texture, ensuring a fresher final product. Plus, the process can be deeply satisfying. Once you’ve tried making your own, you’ll likely never look back!

Homemade tortillas have a soft yet sturdy structure, making them perfect for rolling, folding, or simply enjoying fresh off the griddle. They’re also incredibly versatile: you can use them to make wraps, serve them alongside scrambled eggs, or slather them with butter for a simple snack.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

One of the best aspects of homemade flour tortillas is their simplicity. There’s a good chance you already have everything you need in your pantry. Here’s the list of essentials:

1. All-Purpose Flour
The foundation of any good tortilla is the flour. All-purpose flour works wonderfully, creating soft and tender tortillas that hold up well to fillings. If you’re seeking a healthier alternative, you can substitute a portion of all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour for added fiber.

2. Salt
A touch of salt enhances the flavor of the tortillas, allowing the natural richness of the flour to shine through.

3. Baking Powder (Optional)
Some recipes include baking powder to help the tortillas puff up slightly as they cook. It’s not a strict requirement, but it does add a lovely lightness.

4. Fat
Traditionally, lard is used to create tortillas with a soft, rich texture. However, you can swap this out for vegetable oil, butter, or even shortening, depending on your preference.

5. Water
Warm water binds the ingredients together and ensures the dough is pliable. It’s essential to use water that’s not too hot or cold to achieve the right consistency.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Flour Tortillas

Step 1: Mix the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon of salt. If you’re using baking powder, add ½ teaspoon at this stage. Stir with a whisk or fork to evenly distribute the dry ingredients.

Next, add 3 tablespoons of fat (lard, butter, or vegetable oil) to the bowl. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work the fat into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Gradually pour in ¾ cup of warm water, mixing as you go. You can use a spoon initially, but once the dough starts forming, switch to kneading with your hands. The dough should be soft and pliable but not sticky. If it feels too dry, add a tablespoon of water at a time until it comes together.

Step 2: Knead and Rest the Dough

Transfer the dough to a clean surface and knead it for about 5–7 minutes. This step activates the gluten in the flour, giving your tortillas structure and elasticity.

Once the dough is smooth and slightly springy to the touch, form it into a ball and cover it with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. Allowing the dough to relax makes it easier to roll out later and contributes to the finished texture of the tortillas.

Step 3: Divide and Roll

After resting, divide the dough into equal portions. For standard-sized tortillas, aim for about 8–10 balls. If you’re making larger tortillas for burritos, you can divide the dough into 6 portions.

Roll each portion into a smooth ball between your palms, then flatten it slightly. On a lightly floured surface, use a rolling pin to roll out each ball into a thin, round disc. Aim for a thickness of about ⅛-inch; you want them thin enough to cook quickly but not so thin that they tear.

Pro tip: Rotate the dough a little after each pass with the rolling pin to maintain a circular shape. It won’t be perfect—and that’s okay! The rustic edges add charm to homemade tortillas.

Step 4: Cook the Tortillas

Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Cast-iron pans work particularly well because they retain heat consistently, but any sturdy nonstick pan will do.

Once the pan is hot, place a rolled-out tortilla into the skillet. Cook for about 30–45 seconds, or until the surface starts to bubble and you see golden brown spots underneath. Flip the tortilla with a spatula and cook for another 30 seconds on the other side.

Keep cooked tortillas warm by stacking them and covering them with a clean kitchen towel as you go. This helps them retain their softness and prevents them from drying out.

Tips for Success

  • Don’t Overcook: Tortillas only need a short time on the pan. Overcooking can make them dry and brittle. Aim for golden spots without letting the dough become crisp.
  • Consistency Is Key: If the dough feels too sticky to work with, sprinkle your work surface with a little flour and knead it gently until it’s smooth.
  • Keep Them Warm: A stack of tortillas wrapped in a towel will stay warm and pliable for about an hour. For longer storage, consider reheating them in a microwave or oven.
  • Experiment with Sizes: Smaller tortillas work great for tacos, while larger ones are perfect for wraps or burritos.

Storing Your Homemade Tortillas

If you don’t finish your batch immediately (as tempting as that may be), you can store leftover tortillas for later use.

  1. Refrigeration: Store cooled tortillas in an airtight bag or container for up to 5 days.
  2. Freezing: For longer storage, layer parchment paper between each tortilla to prevent sticking. Place them in a resealable freezer bag and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before reheating.

Creative Ways to Use Flour Tortillas

Homemade tortillas are incredibly versatile. Here are just a few ideas beyond the usual taco night:

  • Breakfast Wrap: Fold scrambled eggs, bacon, and avocado into a warm tortilla for a hearty breakfast.
  • Tortilla Chips: Cut into wedges and fry or bake for crispy, homemade chips.
  • Dessert Tortillas: Spread with Nutella, sprinkle with sliced strawberries, and roll for a quick dessert.
  • Soup Companion: Tear into pieces and dip into hot soup for a comforting addition.

Final Thoughts

Making your own flour tortillas is one of those kitchen projects that rewards you with delicious, homemade flavor while leaving you with the satisfaction of mastering a classic recipe. With just a few simple ingredients and some straightforward techniques, you’ll be crafting tortillas that are leagues ahead of anything from the grocery store.

Try this recipe for your next meal and discover how easy it is to create soft, flavorful tortillas in your very own kitchen.
