Sizzling Cajun Shrimp and Sausage Skillet: A Spicy Cajun Feast


If you’re craving a hearty, flavorful dish that’s as easy to make as it is satisfying, look no further than this sizzling Cajun shrimp and sausage skillet recipe. Bursting with bold spices and a mix of tender shrimp and savory sausage, this Cajun-inspired meal is perfect for any occasion, from weeknight dinners to weekend gatherings with friends and family.

Ingredients

To create this delicious Cajun feast, you’ll need:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 oz Andouille sausage, sliced into rounds
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced (any color you prefer)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Sear the Sausage

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced Andouille sausage and cook until nicely browned, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove the sausage from the skillet and set aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

  1. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Add the sliced bell pepper and onion, season with a pinch of salt and pepper, and sauté until softened, about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, stirring constantly until fragrant.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

  1. Stir in the diced tomatoes (along with their juices), chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, paprika, and dried thyme. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  2. Reduce the heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce slightly thickens.

Step 4: Cook the Shrimp

  1. Add the seared sausage back into the skillet along with the peeled and deveined shrimp. Cook for 5-7 minutes or until the shrimp are pink and opaque, stirring occasionally.

  2. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Remove the skillet from the heat. Serve the Cajun shrimp and sausage over cooked rice, garnished with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing Ingredients: For the best flavor, opt for fresh shrimp and Andouille sausage. The Andouille sausage adds a distinct smoky flavor that complements the spices perfectly.

  • Customize the Spice Level: Adjust the amount of Cajun seasoning and paprika according to your spice preference. If you prefer it spicier, add a pinch of cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes.

  • Make it Creamier: For a richer sauce, increase the amount of heavy cream or finish the dish with a dollop of sour cream or a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

  • Side Dish Options: Besides rice, this Cajun shrimp and sausage skillet pairs well with crusty bread, pasta, or even over creamy mashed potatoes for a comforting twist.

Final Thoughts

This sizzling Cajun shrimp and sausage skillet is a one-pan wonder that delivers on both flavor and ease of preparation. With its robust Cajun spices, tender shrimp, and savory Andouille sausage, it’s sure to become a favorite in your recipe rotation. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just for yourself, this dish promises to impress with its bold flavors and comforting appeal. So, grab your skillet and get ready to savor a taste of Louisiana right in your own kitchen!
