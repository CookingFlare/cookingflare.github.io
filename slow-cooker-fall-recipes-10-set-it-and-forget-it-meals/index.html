Slow Cooker Fall Recipes: 10 Set-It-and-Forget-It Meals


When the leaves begin to change and crisp air fills the evenings, fall beckons us to swap out summer salads for hearty, comforting meals. This time of year is perfect for cozy cooking that warms the soul, and what better way to do that than with a slow cooker? Slow cookers are the ultimate kitchen hero for busy days or lazy weekends. They allow you to prepare incredible dishes packed with flavor with minimal effort. Just set it, forget it, and let the slow simmer bring out the best in your ingredients.

Below, you’ll find ten irresistible fall recipes crafted to delight your taste buds and keep you warm during cooler days. These meals are perfect for anyone—whether you’re feeding a crowd or looking to enjoy leftovers throughout the week. Grab your slow cooker and let’s dive into mouthwatering recipes!

1. Savory Beef Stew with Root Vegetables

Fall is synonymous with warm, hearty stews, and this slow cooker beef stew delivers on all fronts. Cubes of tender beef mingle with earthy carrots, parsnips, and potatoes in a rich, herb-infused broth. Start by searing the beef in a hot skillet to lock in flavor before adding it to your slow cooker. Add beef broth, tomato paste, garlic, onions, bay leaves, and your chosen vegetables. Let it cook on low for 8 hours for melt-in-your-mouth tenderness.

Tip: Pair this stew with crusty bread for dipping into the savory broth.

2. Creamy Butternut Squash Soup

Few things scream autumn like butternut squash. Transform this fall favorite into a velvety soup that’s both easy and elegant. Simply cut up butternut squash, carrots, celery, and onions, then add them to your slow cooker with vegetable broth. After 6 hours on low, blend everything together until smooth (an immersion blender works wonders). For extra creaminess, stir in a bit of coconut milk or heavy cream just before serving.

Optional Garnish: Toasted pumpkin seeds or a drizzle of balsamic glaze.

3. Apple-Cinnamon Pork Chops

Apples and cinnamon create a uniquely autumn flavor profile that pairs beautifully with tender pork chops. Layer boneless pork chops, sliced apples, and onion in your slow cooker, and sprinkle generously with cinnamon, nutmeg, and a touch of brown sugar. Pour over a mixture of apple cider and chicken broth, then cook on low for 6-7 hours. The result is a tangy-sweet main dish that’s perfect for family dinners.

4. Pumpkin Chili

Why settle for ordinary chili when you can add a touch of autumn flair? This pumpkin chili is hearty, healthy, and bursting with fall flavors. Start with ground turkey or beef, canned pumpkin puree, beans (black, kidney, or chili beans), tomatoes, garlic, and your favorite chili seasonings. It’s all about layering flavors in the slow cooker, letting them meld together over 6-8 hours. The pumpkin adds a subtle sweetness and creamy texture that sets this chili apart.

5. Slow Cooker Chicken and Wild Rice Soup

This comforting dish is packed with nourishing goodness. Perfect for chilly evenings, the classic combination of tender chicken, nutty wild rice, mushrooms, carrots, and celery comes together seamlessly in a slow cooker filled with chicken broth and a medley of herbs. Let it cook for 7-8 hours on low. Stir in heavy cream or unsweetened almond milk toward the end for a touch of decadence.

Serving Suggestion: Sprinkle with fresh parsley before serving for a pop of color.

6. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili

This vegetarian chili is hearty enough to satisfy meat lovers, thanks to its robust flavor and earthy components. Combine cubed sweet potatoes, black beans, diced tomatoes, onion, and a blend of smoky spices like paprika, chili powder, and cumin in a slow cooker. Let the ingredients cook on low for 6 hours. Finish with a squeeze of lime and serve with avocado slices or sour cream.

7. Spiced Pear and Ginger Compote

Desserts deserve the slow cooker treatment, too. This spiced pear and ginger compote is autumn in a bowl. Pears, fresh ginger, cinnamon sticks, and orange zest simmer in your slow cooker alongside a splash of maple syrup. After 4 hours on low, you’ll have fragrant, tender pears swimming in a lightly sweetened syrup. Serve the compote over yogurt, oatmeal, or vanilla ice cream.

8. Classic Pot Roast

A quintessential fall dish, pot roast becomes even more flavorful when cooked low and slow. Sear a chuck roast on all sides and place it in your slow cooker with potatoes, carrots, onions, beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and herbs like thyme and rosemary. Set it to low and cook for 8 hours. By the time dinner rolls around, your kitchen will smell as comforting as the dish itself.

Pro Tip: Save the leftovers for sandwiches, sliders, or hash.

9. Slow Cooker Stuffed Peppers

Stuffed peppers make a versatile and colorful fall meal that’s as simple as it is satisfying. Hollow out bell peppers and fill them with a mixture of cooked rice, ground beef (or plant-based crumbles), diced tomatoes, and your favorite spices. Arrange the peppers in your slow cooker, add tomato sauce, and let them cook on low for 6 hours. Finish with a sprinkling of shredded cheese for the last 10 minutes and let it melt into the filling.

10. Maple Bacon Brussels Sprouts

Love them or hate them, Brussels sprouts become downright irresistible when paired with maple syrup and bacon. Toss halved Brussels sprouts in your slow cooker along with maple syrup, chopped bacon, onion, and a little balsamic vinegar. Cook on low for 4-5 hours, stirring occasionally, for a tender but caramelized side dish. This recipe could easily steal the spotlight at your next fall dinner party.

Slow Cooker Success Tips

To achieve the most flavorful, perfectly cooked meals this fall, consider these tips:

  • Don’t Overcrowd: Give your ingredients room to cook evenly by avoiding overfilling your slow cooker.
  • Layer Wisely: Place denser foods (like root vegetables or meat) closer to the heat source at the bottom, with lighter ingredients on top.
  • Keep the Lid On: Resist the urge to peek—it disrupts the cooking process and releases valuable heat.
  • Add Dairy Last: Ingredients like milk, cream, and cheese are best added during the last hour for ideal texture.

These ten slow cooker recipes are not only easy but embody all things cozy and delicious for fall. With minimal prep and maximum flavor, they’re perfect options for busy weekdays or relaxed weekends. Slow down, savor the season, and enjoy these delightful meals with friends and family.
