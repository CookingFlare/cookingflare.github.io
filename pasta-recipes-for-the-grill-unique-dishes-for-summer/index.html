Pasta Recipes for the Grill: Unique Dishes for Summer


When summer arrives, it’s time to step away from the stove and take advantage of the outdoor grill. Grilling offers an exciting and flavorful way to cook, adding that perfect smokey char to food. While grilling is often associated with meats and vegetables, one category of food that is often overlooked for grilling is pasta. Yes, you can grill pasta! With a little creativity, you can transform your favorite pasta dishes into delicious, smoky creations. In this post, we’ll explore unique and flavorful pasta recipes that bring the best of grilling to your summer meals.

Grilled Pesto Pasta Salad

Start your grilling adventure with a refreshing and vibrant grilled pesto pasta salad. This dish is a perfect combination of smoky, charred pasta and the fresh, herbaceous flavor of pesto. To make this dish, follow these simple steps.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of your favorite pasta (penne or fusilli work well)
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh mozzarella balls (optional)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • A handful of arugula for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a bit of pasta water for later.
  2. Make the Pesto: In a food processor, blend the basil, garlic, pine nuts, and Parmesan. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture becomes a smooth paste. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Grill the Pasta: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Toss the cooked pasta with a small amount of olive oil to prevent sticking. Grill the pasta on a grill-safe pan or directly on the grill for about 5 minutes, until you get a nice char.
  4. Combine: Toss the grilled pasta with the fresh pesto, adding a little reserved pasta water if necessary to achieve the right consistency. Stir in the cherry tomatoes, mozzarella, and lemon zest.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Top with a handful of arugula for a peppery bite, and serve warm or at room temperature.

This grilled pesto pasta salad offers a perfect balance of smokiness and fresh, vibrant flavors. It works great as a side dish or a light main course on a hot summer day.

Grilled Mac and Cheese Bites

Mac and cheese is a classic comfort food, but when grilled, it turns into a whole new experience. These grilled mac and cheese bites are crispy on the outside, gooey on the inside, and full of delicious flavor. They’re perfect for summer gatherings, serving as both a fun appetizer and a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped chives
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mac and Cheese: Cook the macaroni in boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Add the cheeses and stir until fully melted and smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Combine: In a bowl, whisk the egg. Add the cooked macaroni to the bowl, pour the cheese sauce over it, and stir to combine.
  4. Form the Bites: Scoop spoonfuls of the cheesy pasta and roll them into small balls. Then, roll each ball in breadcrumbs to coat.
  5. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the mac and cheese balls on a grill-safe pan or grill basket. Grill for 8-10 minutes, turning halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  6. Serve: Sprinkle with chopped chives and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

These grilled mac and cheese bites are the ultimate comfort food with a smoky twist, ideal for your next cookout or BBQ.

Grilled Shrimp Scampi Pasta

Grilled shrimp adds a delicious depth of flavor to the classic shrimp scampi pasta. The smoky taste from the grill elevates the rich garlic butter sauce, creating a dish that’s bursting with flavor. If you love seafood, this grilled shrimp scampi pasta will be your go-to dish for summer dining.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of linguine or spaghetti
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Shrimp: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Toss the shrimp in olive oil, lemon zest, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the Shrimp: Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque.
  4. Make the Garlic Butter Sauce: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Add the white wine and simmer for a couple of minutes to reduce the liquid. Stir in the lemon juice.
  5. Combine: Toss the cooked pasta in the garlic butter sauce. Add the grilled shrimp and mix everything together. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve: Garnish with chopped parsley and additional lemon zest, and serve warm.

This grilled shrimp scampi pasta is a perfect blend of smoky grilled shrimp and tangy, buttery pasta. It’s a flavorful, satisfying dish that’s perfect for summer entertaining.

Grilled Mediterranean Pasta

Grilled Mediterranean pasta brings together the best flavors of the Mediterranean with a smoky twist. With roasted vegetables, olives, and feta cheese, this dish is a feast for the senses, making it an ideal option for a light, flavorful dinner on a summer evening.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound penne or farfalle pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1/2 red onion, sliced into rings
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Grill the Vegetables: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Toss the vegetables with olive oil, salt, pepper, and oregano. Grill the vegetables until they are tender and slightly charred, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Combine: In a large mixing bowl, combine the grilled vegetables with the pasta, adding in the olives and feta cheese. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar and additional olive oil.
  4. Serve: Toss everything together and serve warm or at room temperature.

The grilled Mediterranean pasta is a fresh, healthy dish that’s perfect for any summer gathering. The smoky grilled vegetables and creamy feta make it a delightful choice for your grilling menu.

Grilled Pasta with Sausage and Peppers

For a heartier option, try grilled pasta with sausage and peppers. This dish combines grilled Italian sausage, sweet peppers, and a rich tomato sauce to create a savory, satisfying meal perfect for the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound rigatoni or ziti pasta
  • 2 Italian sausages (sweet or spicy, depending on preference)
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Grill the Sausage and Vegetables: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the sausages until fully cooked, about 6-8 minutes per side. Grill the peppers and onions until tender and slightly charred, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Slice the Sausage: Once the sausages are cooked, slice them into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Combine: In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta with the grilled sausage, peppers, and onions. Pour over the marinara sauce and toss everything together.
  5. Serve: Garnish with Parmesan cheese and fresh basil. Serve hot.

This grilled pasta with sausage and peppers is a hearty, flavorful dish that will satisfy any crowd. The smoky sausage and peppers pair perfectly with the rich marinara sauce, making it an ideal summer dinner.

Grilled Pasta with Veggies and Feta

Grilled pasta with veggies and feta is a light, refreshing option that combines grilled vegetables with creamy feta for a satisfying yet healthy meal. This recipe is perfect for those who want to enjoy a vegetarian pasta dish with a smoky twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound farfalle pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Fresh thyme for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Grill the Vegetables: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Toss the vegetables with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Grill until charred and tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Combine: In a large bowl, toss the grilled vegetables with the cooked pasta. Add crumbled feta cheese and mix.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh thyme and serve warm or chilled.

This grilled pasta with veggies and feta is a light and healthy choice that brings the best of grilled summer flavors to your plate.

Conclusion

Grilling pasta may seem unusual, but once you’ve tried these unique pasta dishes, you’ll wonder why you didn’t do it sooner. Whether you’re serving a smoky pesto pasta salad, crispy mac and cheese bites, or a hearty sausage and peppers dish, grilling pasta offers a creative way to elevate your summer meals. The next time you fire up the grill, try one of these recipes, and enjoy a deliciously smoky twist on your favorite pasta dishes.

Happy grilling!
