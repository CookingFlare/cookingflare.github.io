Vegan Cinnamon Apple Crumble: Sweet and Comforting


What could be more comforting and satisfying than a warm apple crumble fresh out of the oven? The sweet-spiced aroma of cinnamon and baked apples fills your kitchen, reminding you of home, cozy evenings, and the joys of simple, hearty desserts. This recipe for Vegan Cinnamon Apple Crumble is not only irresistibly delicious but also incredibly easy to make. Whether you’re cooking for friends, family, or simply treating yourself, this plant-based version of the classic dessert will wow everyone’s taste buds while sticking to wholesome, cruelty-free ingredients.

Why Choose a Vegan Apple Crumble?

If you’re looking to make your desserts a little healthier or simply wish to embrace plant-based living, this vegan apple crumble is the perfect choice. Not only does using vegan substitutes keep this recipe dairy-free, but it also ensures your dessert is free from cholesterol while still being rich, indulgent, and packed with flavor. What’s more, you won’t be sacrificing texture or taste in this recipe—the buttery crumble topping and tender apples blend perfectly into every bite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity, requiring only pantry staples and seasonal ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to create this mouthwatering vegan cinnamon apple crumble:

For the Apple Filling:

  • 6 medium-sized apples (choose sweet varieties like Fuji, Gala, or Honeycrisp for natural sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice (to prevent browning and balance sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut sugar or brown sugar (adds depth and a caramel-like touch)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (the essential spice for that warm, cozy flavor)
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg (optional, but elevates the spice profile)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for an extra layer of sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or arrowroot powder (to thicken the filling)

For the Crumble Topping:

  • 1 cup rolled oats (use old-fashioned for a chunkier texture)
  • 3/4 cup almond flour (or substitute with whole wheat or all-purpose flour)
  • 1/3 cup coconut oil (solid at room temperature, or chilled vegan butter)
  • 1/3 cup coconut sugar or brown sugar (to sweeten)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (to tie the topping with the filling)
  • Pinch of sea salt (to enhance flavor)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Apples

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C), so it’s ready to bake your crumble. While the oven warms up, peel, core, and slice your apples into thin wedges. Aim for slices that are about 1/4 inch thick—they’ll cook evenly and become beautifully tender.

Place the sliced apples in a large mixing bowl. Add the lemon juice, coconut sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla extract, and cornstarch. Toss everything together until the apple slices are evenly coated. Set the filling aside while preparing the crumble.

Step 2: Create the Crumble Topping

In a separate mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Mix these dry ingredients together with a spoon or whisk. Next, add the solid coconut oil or chilled vegan butter. Using your hands or a pastry cutter, mix the fat into the dry ingredients until a crumbly texture forms. The mixture should resemble coarse sand with a few larger clumps—these buttery chunks are what give the topping its signature texture!

Step 3: Assemble the Crumble

Take out your favorite baking dish (a 9x9-inch pan works perfectly) and lightly grease it with a small amount of coconut oil. Spread the apple filling evenly across the bottom of the dish. Then, sprinkle the crumble topping over the apples, covering them completely. Don’t press the topping down; the light, airy texture will help it bake into a golden-brown crust.

Step 4: Bake Until Perfect

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 40 to 45 minutes. You’ll know it’s ready when the topping turns golden and crisp, and the apples underneath become bubbly and tender. If the crumble begins to brown too quickly, you can tent it with foil halfway through the baking time to avoid burning.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once baked, let the vegan cinnamon apple crumble cool for 5 to 10 minutes. This brief rest allows the juices to settle slightly, making it easier to scoop and serve. This dessert pairs beautifully with a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of dairy-free custard.

Tips for Making the Best Apple Crumble

1. Choose the Right Apples

Sweet, crisp apples work best for this recipe. If you prefer a tangier profile, mix in some Granny Smith apples alongside sweeter varieties for a balance of tartness and natural sugars.

2. Customize Your Topping

The crumble topping is incredibly versatile. You can add chopped nuts, such as almonds or pecans, for extra crunch or swap out some of the almond flour for desiccated coconut for a tropical twist.

3. Make It Gluten-Free

For a gluten-free version, replace almond flour with gluten-free all-purpose flour or oat flour. Ensure that your oats are certified gluten-free if necessary.

4. Add Extra Flavor

Want more spice? Feel free to sprinkle a touch of cardamom or ground ginger into both the filling and topping. For sweetness variations, use maple syrup instead of coconut sugar in the filling for a more complex flavor.

Why Vegan Apple Crumble Deserves a Place in Your Recipe Collection

This vegan cinnamon apple crumble is the ultimate plant-based dessert for any occasion. Whether it’s a potluck, a cozy after-dinner treat, or a celebration of apple season, it’s sure to impress. You’ll love how straightforward it is to prepare, and the wholesome ingredients will leave you feeling satisfied without guilt.

Plus, this recipe is easily adaptable to suit your preferences. Whether you’re switching up the spices or adding your own creative flair, each version promises the same level of indulgence and warmth. After just one bite, you’ll see why this crumble is destined to become a staple in your dessert rotation.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (it’s rare, but possible!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, place a serving in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10 minutes or microwave gently for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Closing Thoughts

This Vegan Cinnamon Apple Crumble effortlessly combines nostalgia with conscious eating, making it a dessert you can feel good about enjoying and sharing. With a rich blend of textures and flavors, it’s proof that plant-based baking can be just as decadent and delicious as traditional recipes. If you’re looking for a dish that warms the soul, this crumble has everything you’re craving. So, grab your apples, dust off your baking dish, and treat yourself to the sweet comfort of this delightful dessert—you won’t regret it!
