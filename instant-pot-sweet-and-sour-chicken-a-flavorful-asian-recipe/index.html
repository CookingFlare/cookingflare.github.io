Instant Pot Sweet and Sour Chicken: A Flavorful Asian Recipe


If you’re craving the perfect balance of tangy, sweet, and savory in a deliciously juicy chicken dish, then look no further than this Instant Pot Sweet and Sour Chicken. With just a few simple ingredients and the magic of your Instant Pot, you can whip up this classic Asian-inspired dish in a fraction of the time it would take to prepare it the traditional way. This easy-to-make recipe brings the flavors of a takeout favorite right into your kitchen, and it’s a surefire way to satisfy your taste buds with minimal effort.

Why Make Sweet and Sour Chicken in an Instant Pot?

Sweet and sour chicken is a beloved dish worldwide, known for its delicious combination of flavors that seem to dance on the taste buds. It often involves cooking battered and fried chicken pieces and then tossing them in a tangy-sweet sauce made with ingredients like vinegar, sugar, ketchup, and soy sauce. While this method is delicious, it can be a bit time-consuming and labor-intensive.

Enter the Instant Pot.

The Instant Pot allows you to create an authentic, mouthwatering sweet and sour chicken without all the frying and fuss. The beauty of using the Instant Pot is that it retains all the delicious flavors, and the pressure cooking method locks in moisture, resulting in juicy, tender chicken every time. Plus, it cuts the cooking time down considerably, making this a weeknight-friendly meal that the whole family will love.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Sweet and Sour Chicken

For the Chicken:

  • 1.5 lbs of boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil (or vegetable oil)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Sweet and Sour Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice (fresh or canned)
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or coconut aminos for a gluten-free version)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for thickening the sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)

For the Veggies (Optional, but recommended for extra flavor and texture):

  • 1 bell pepper, sliced into thin strips
  • 1/2 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup carrots, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1/4 cup green onions, sliced, for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions for Instant Pot Sweet and Sour Chicken

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by cutting your chicken into bite-sized pieces. If you’re using chicken breasts, make sure to trim off any excess fat. If you prefer dark meat, chicken thighs work beautifully in this recipe and tend to be more flavorful and juicy.

For the sauce, gather all your ingredients and mix them in a bowl. You can whisk the vinegar, pineapple juice, ketchup, brown sugar, soy sauce, garlic powder, ginger powder, and red pepper flakes together. If you want a thicker sauce, make a slurry by mixing the cornstarch with a tablespoon of cold water in a separate bowl, and then add it to the sauce mixture. This will help give the sauce the perfect consistency after it cooks.

For the vegetables, slice the bell pepper, onion, and carrots. If you’re using fresh pineapple, cut it into small chunks. Set everything aside and prepare the Instant Pot.

Step 2: Sauté the Chicken

Turn on the Instant Pot and set it to the “Sauté” mode. Once the pot is hot, add the olive oil and swirl it around to coat the bottom. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and season them with a pinch of salt and pepper. Sauté the chicken for about 3-5 minutes until it’s lightly browned on all sides.

You don’t need to cook the chicken through at this stage; the pressure cooking will finish the job. The goal is just to get a bit of color and flavor on the chicken before you pressure cook it with the sauce.

Step 3: Add the Sweet and Sour Sauce

Once the chicken is browned, pour the sweet and sour sauce you prepared earlier over the chicken. Use a spatula to scrape any browned bits off the bottom of the pot (this adds great flavor). If you’re using vegetables, like bell peppers, onions, carrots, or pineapple, now is the time to add them to the Instant Pot as well. Stir everything together so the chicken and veggies are evenly coated with the sauce.

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Chicken

Secure the Instant Pot lid and make sure the valve is set to the “Sealing” position. Set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” mode on high pressure for 10 minutes. Once the cooking time is up, allow the pot to naturally release pressure for about 5 minutes before performing a quick release to release any remaining pressure.

Be cautious when performing a quick release as hot steam can escape rapidly from the vent. Always use a towel or oven mitt to help with this step.

Step 5: Thicken the Sauce (Optional)

If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can thicken the sauce by using the sauté function once again. After the pressure has been released, set the Instant Pot back to “Sauté” mode and allow the sauce to simmer for 3-5 minutes until it reaches the desired consistency. If you’d like it even thicker, you can add a little more cornstarch mixed with water, but remember, a little goes a long way.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Once the sauce has thickened to your liking, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add a pinch more salt, a drizzle of soy sauce, or even a little more sugar if you like it sweeter.

Serve your Instant Pot sweet and sour chicken over a bed of fluffy rice. Garnish with freshly sliced green onions or even a few sesame seeds for a nice touch. You can also add extra vegetables like steamed broccoli or snap peas on the side for a well-rounded meal.

Tips and Variations for Perfect Sweet and Sour Chicken

1. Chicken Thighs vs. Chicken Breasts:

Chicken thighs are more forgiving than chicken breasts and tend to stay juicier when cooked under pressure. If you’re looking for a slightly richer flavor, go with chicken thighs. However, chicken breasts will also work well and result in a leaner dish.

2. Adjust the Sweetness:

The sweetness of the dish comes from brown sugar, but you can adjust the level of sweetness according to your taste. If you like your sweet and sour chicken on the sweeter side, increase the amount of sugar or pineapple juice. If you prefer a more savory flavor, reduce the sugar slightly.

3. Make It Spicy:

If you love a little heat, you can increase the amount of red pepper flakes or even add a splash of sriracha sauce to the sweet and sour sauce. The spiciness will balance beautifully with the sweetness of the sauce.

4. Gluten-Free Version:

To make this dish gluten-free, simply substitute soy sauce with coconut aminos, which has a similar flavor but is free from gluten. You’ll still get the savory umami flavor without the gluten!

5. Add More Veggies:

Feel free to add extra vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, or snap peas for more texture and color. The Instant Pot’s pressure cooking will help tenderize the vegetables while keeping them vibrant.

6. Serving Suggestions:

For a more filling meal, pair your sweet and sour chicken with steamed jasmine rice, quinoa, or even cauliflower rice for a low-carb option. You could also serve it with fried rice or noodles if you’re in the mood for a heartier meal.

Why This Recipe is Perfect for Busy Weeknights

One of the best things about making sweet and sour chicken in the Instant Pot is how little time it takes. Traditional recipes may involve hours of cooking, frying, and simmering, but with the Instant Pot, you get a flavorful dish ready in just under 30 minutes! It’s perfect for busy families, meal prep, or anyone looking for a delicious dinner without a lot of fuss.

In addition to the speed, the Instant Pot also locks in flavors and moisture, resulting in tender, juicy chicken every time. There’s no need to worry about dry or overcooked chicken; the Instant Pot ensures a perfect meal every time.

Conclusion

This Instant Pot Sweet and Sour Chicken recipe is a game-changer for anyone who loves Asian-inspired dishes but doesn’t have hours to spend in the kitchen. With tender chicken, a sweet and tangy sauce, and plenty of flavor, this dish will become a family favorite in no time. Whether you’re serving it over rice or alongside veggies, it’s a complete meal that’s sure to impress.

So, next time you’re craving takeout, skip the delivery and make this delicious, restaurant-quality sweet and sour chicken in your Instant Pot. You’ll be amazed at how easy it is, and how much better it tastes than anything you’d get from a local takeout joint!
