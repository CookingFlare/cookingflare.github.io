Classic Nut-Free Mac and Cheese Recipe


Are you looking for the ultimate comfort food that’s easy to make and loved by both kids and adults? Look no further than this Classic Nut-Free Mac and Cheese recipe! Creamy, cheesy, and oh-so-satisfying, this dish is a timeless favorite that never fails to impress. Plus, it’s nut-free, making it a safe and delicious option for those with nut allergies. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat the oven, and let’s dive into the savory world of homemade mac and cheese!

Ingredients

  • 16 oz (450g) elbow macaroni
  • 4 tbsp (55g) unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup (30g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups (600ml) whole milk
  • 2 cups (200g) sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup (100g) mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup (50g) Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup (45g) breadcrumbs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk constantly until the mixture turns golden and starts to foam.

  4. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Cook the mixture for a few minutes until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and add the cheddar, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese to the sauce. Stir until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth.

  6. Season the cheese sauce with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasonings according to your preference.

  7. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce, stirring until the macaroni is evenly coated.

  8. Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  9. Sprinkle the breadcrumbs over the top of the mac and cheese.

  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips for Success

  • For an extra flavor boost, consider adding a dash of paprika or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the cheese sauce.

  • Want to make it a complete meal? Add cooked and chopped bacon, diced ham, or even steamed broccoli to the mac and cheese before baking.

  • If you’re looking to make this dish ahead of time, prepare the mac and cheese as instructed but refrain from adding the breadcrumbs. Cover the dish with foil and refrigerate for up to 2 days. When ready to bake, sprinkle the breadcrumbs on top and bake as directed.

Conclusion

In conclusion, this Classic Nut-Free Mac and Cheese recipe is a surefire way to win over the hearts and taste buds of everyone at your table. Whether it’s a cozy family dinner, a potluck with friends, or just a weeknight craving, this dish is a timeless crowd-pleaser. Its creamy, cheesy goodness will have you coming back for seconds every time. So, gather your ingredients, channel your inner chef, and get ready to savor the irresistible comfort of homemade mac and cheese!
