How to Bake the Best Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread


There’s nothing quite as satisfying as the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through your home. But when you take it a step further and create a loaf that’s packed with savory garlic and herb goodness, it becomes an entirely different experience. Garlic and herb pull-apart bread is the perfect combination of flavors, and it’s an ideal choice for family dinners, holiday gatherings, or just a cozy weekend treat. In this post, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to bake the best garlic and herb pull-apart bread that’s soft, flavorful, and irresistibly easy to share.

What Makes Pull-Apart Bread So Special?

Pull-apart bread is a delightful twist on traditional loaves, designed to be torn apart by hand rather than sliced. The soft, pillowy texture and pull-apart nature make it an ideal choice for sharing, dipping, or even as a side dish. It’s often made with a buttery, flavorful filling, and garlic and herbs add a perfect punch of taste. The combination of crispy edges and tender, buttery interior makes each bite a joy. Plus, it’s the perfect recipe for beginners or seasoned bakers, as it doesn’t require any complicated shaping or techniques.

If you’ve never baked pull-apart bread before, you’re in for a treat. In this guide, we’ll cover everything from the ingredients to the baking tips, ensuring you end up with a loaf that’s as flavorful as it is visually appealing.

Ingredients for Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

To bake the best garlic and herb pull-apart bread, you’ll need a few key ingredients. These ingredients are simple yet come together in the most delightful way. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour – All-purpose flour is perfect for this bread, as it gives you the ideal texture.
  • 1 tablespoon sugar – A touch of sugar helps to activate the yeast and gives the dough a slight sweetness.
  • 1 teaspoon salt – Salt brings out the flavors and balances the richness of the garlic and butter.
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast – Instant yeast is perfect for this recipe as it rises quickly, and there’s no need for proofing.
  • 1/2 cup warm milk – Milk makes the dough softer and adds richness.
  • 1/2 cup warm water – Warm water helps activate the yeast and keeps the dough from becoming too dry.
  • 1/4 cup olive oil – Olive oil adds moisture and enhances the flavor of the bread.
  • 1 large egg – An egg helps the dough come together and adds richness to the texture.

For the Garlic and Herb Butter Filling:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened) – Butter is the base of the filling, ensuring that each piece of bread is tender and flavorful.
  • 5-6 garlic cloves (minced) – Fresh garlic is key for this recipe. It adds a bold and aromatic flavor that infuses the bread with goodness.
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley (chopped) – Fresh parsley adds a bright, herby flavor that contrasts beautifully with the rich butter.
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary (chopped) – Rosemary gives the bread an earthy depth, complementing the garlic and parsley.
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme – Dried thyme is a warm, slightly sweet herb that pairs perfectly with garlic.
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt – A little salt balances the flavors and enhances the garlic’s punch.

How to Make Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

Now that you have all your ingredients, it’s time to get baking! Here’s a simple step-by-step guide to making garlic and herb pull-apart bread from scratch.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, warm water, and sugar. Stir it together and sprinkle the instant yeast on top. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy. If it doesn’t foam, your yeast may be expired, so it’s best to use fresh yeast for the best results.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Once the yeast mixture is ready, pour it into the flour mixture, along with the olive oil and egg. Mix everything together until the dough starts to come together.

Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If you have a stand mixer, you can use the dough hook to knead the dough for about 4-5 minutes. If the dough feels sticky, you can add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much as you want the dough to remain soft.

Step 2: Let the Dough Rise

Once the dough is kneaded, place it in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. You can also use an oven with the light on to create a warm environment. This step is crucial to get the perfect texture for your bread.

Step 3: Make the Garlic and Herb Butter

While your dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the garlic and herb butter filling. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the softened butter with the minced garlic, chopped parsley, rosemary, thyme, and salt. Stir everything together until well combined. The butter should be soft and spreadable, but not too melted. Set this aside for later.

Step 4: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a floured surface and roll it out into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. You want the dough to be large enough to cut into squares for pulling apart.

Spread the garlic and herb butter evenly over the surface of the dough, making sure every corner is covered. Once the butter is spread, cut the dough into squares (about 2-3 inches per square). Stack the squares in a greased 9x9-inch baking dish or a round cake pan. The pieces should be stacked closely, almost like a tower, allowing them to rise and pull apart easily later.

Step 5: Second Rise

Cover the pan with a kitchen towel and let the dough squares rise for another 30-45 minutes. During this time, the dough will puff up, filling in the gaps between the squares and becoming soft and airy. It’s important not to rush this step as the second rise gives the bread its signature texture.

Step 6: Bake the Bread

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Once the dough has risen, bake the bread in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped. You can also check the internal temperature of the bread with a thermometer; it should reach around 190°F (88°C) when fully baked.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once your garlic and herb pull-apart bread is out of the oven, allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. The bread should be easy to pull apart, with each piece loaded with garlic butter and herbs. You can serve it as is or pair it with a marinara sauce or creamy dipping sauce for an extra layer of flavor. This bread is perfect for sharing with family and friends at any gathering.

Tips for Perfect Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

  • Use fresh garlic: While garlic powder can be a substitute, fresh garlic provides the most robust flavor. Be sure to mince it finely for an even distribution throughout the bread.
  • Let the dough rise properly: Don’t rush the rising process. Both rises are essential for achieving a soft, airy texture in the bread. If your kitchen is cold, try placing the dough in an oven with just the light on to encourage proper rising.
  • Don’t overwork the dough: Overworking the dough can lead to a denser bread. Knead just until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  • Experiment with herbs: While this recipe calls for rosemary, thyme, and parsley, feel free to swap in other herbs like basil, oregano, or chives to suit your tastes.
  • Add cheese for extra flavor: If you’re a fan of cheesy bread, consider adding shredded mozzarella or parmesan between the layers of dough for an extra cheesy pull-apart experience.

Variations of Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

While the classic garlic and herb combination is a winner, there are several ways you can customize this pull-apart bread to fit your preferences or occasion. Here are a few fun variations:

1. Cheesy Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

Add shredded mozzarella, cheddar, or a blend of your favorite cheeses to the layers of dough for a cheesy twist. The cheese will melt and add a gooey, indulgent texture to each pull-apart piece.

2. Spicy Garlic Pull-Apart Bread

For those who love a little heat, add some crushed red pepper flakes to the garlic and herb butter filling. This will give your bread a subtle kick that pairs beautifully with the savory garlic.

3. Bacon Garlic Pull-Apart Bread

If you’re a bacon lover, crumble cooked bacon into the garlic and herb butter. The crispy bacon will add both crunch and flavor, making this a perfect option for a more hearty treat.

4. Vegan Garlic and Herb Pull-Apart Bread

For a plant-based version, replace the butter with vegan butter and the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water). You can also substitute almond milk for regular milk.

Final Thoughts

Garlic and herb pull-apart bread is a comforting and crowd-pleasing dish that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re serving it as a side dish, appetizer, or just a snack, this bread’s combination of tender dough, aromatic garlic, and fresh herbs makes it truly irresistible. Follow these steps, and you’ll have a loaf that’s golden brown, flavorful, and easy to share with friends and family. Happy baking!
