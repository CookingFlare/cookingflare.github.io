Gluten-Free Chicken and Dumpling Soup


There’s something inherently comforting about a bowl of chicken and dumpling soup. It’s the perfect meal to warm you up on a cold day, soothe a sore throat, or provide comfort after a long week. But if you or someone in your family follows a gluten-free diet, you might have thought this beloved classic was off the table. Fear not! With a few simple ingredient swaps and some careful attention to detail, you can enjoy a delicious, hearty, and gluten-free version of chicken and dumpling soup that everyone will love.

Why Gluten-Free Chicken and Dumpling Soup?

The traditional chicken and dumpling soup is made with wheat-based dumplings and a rich, creamy broth. The problem with that for those avoiding gluten is obvious. But gluten-free cooking has come a long way, and it’s easier than ever to create gluten-free versions of almost any dish, including this one. By using gluten-free flour blends, you can create tender, fluffy dumplings that hold up well in the broth without sacrificing texture or taste. Plus, the soup itself can be made without any gluten ingredients, making it a meal everyone can enjoy.

This gluten-free chicken and dumpling soup is not only a great alternative for those who need to avoid gluten, but it also caters to anyone who loves comfort food that’s warm, hearty, and packed with flavor. It’s packed with tender chicken, a variety of fresh vegetables, and homemade dumplings that are as soft and fluffy as the traditional ones you’ve come to love. This soup is sure to be a hit at your dinner table, whether you’re gluten-sensitive or not!

Key Ingredients for Gluten-Free Chicken and Dumpling Soup

To make this gluten-free chicken and dumpling soup, you’ll need to gather a few key ingredients. Let’s break down the essential components, and I’ll also give you some tips on what you can swap out based on your preferences or dietary needs.

For the Soup Base:

  • Chicken: You can use boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs. I prefer thighs for their richness and tenderness, but breasts are a leaner option if that’s your preference. Rotisserie chicken is a great shortcut if you’re in a hurry.
  • Carrots: These add sweetness and color to the soup, balancing the savory flavors of the chicken and broth.
  • Celery: A classic addition to any soup, celery brings a mild, slightly peppery flavor and contributes to the texture of the soup.
  • Onion: A staple in soups, onion brings depth and richness to the flavor profile.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds that aromatic touch that elevates the flavor of the broth.
  • Chicken Broth: You’ll want a good-quality gluten-free chicken broth or stock to form the base of the soup. Be sure to check labels for any hidden gluten ingredients.
  • Thyme and Bay Leaves: These aromatic herbs bring depth and earthiness to the broth, making the soup fragrant and savory.
  • Salt and Pepper: Always essential for seasoning and balancing the flavors.

For the Dumplings:

  • Gluten-Free Flour Blend: To keep this recipe gluten-free, a gluten-free flour blend is a must. Make sure it’s a 1:1 flour blend for baking, which can mimic the consistency of traditional all-purpose flour.
  • Baking Powder: This helps the dumplings rise, giving them that light and fluffy texture we all crave.
  • Butter: Adds richness and flavor to the dumplings.
  • Eggs: Eggs provide structure and moisture to the dumplings, ensuring they hold together well.
  • Milk: A splash of milk gives the dumplings the right amount of moisture, keeping them soft and tender.
  • Fresh Parsley: Fresh herbs like parsley give the dumplings a lovely pop of color and freshness. You can also use thyme or chives for a different flavor profile.

How to Make Gluten-Free Chicken and Dumpling Soup

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by cooking the chicken. You can either cook it in the soup directly, or if you’re using rotisserie chicken, simply shred it after heating it. For the stovetop method, add the chicken to a large pot with a bit of olive oil. Sauté the chicken until it’s golden on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. If you prefer a quicker option, you can also cook the chicken separately by boiling it in water or stock, then shredding it before adding it to the soup.

Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside. When it’s cool enough to handle, shred the meat using two forks or your hands. Set the shredded chicken aside for later.

Step 2: Prepare the Soup Base

In the same pot, add a little more olive oil or butter, and sauté the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Let them cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they start to soften. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.

Next, pour in the chicken broth. Add the thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Bring the broth to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook gently for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Step 3: Make the Dumplings

While the soup is simmering, it’s time to prepare the dumplings. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Add the cold butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and milk together. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until everything comes together into a sticky dough. If the dough seems too dry, add a little more milk, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches the right consistency.

Step 4: Add the Dumplings to the Soup

Once the broth has simmered and the vegetables are tender, it’s time to add the dumplings. Carefully drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough into the simmering soup, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. You can use a tablespoon to scoop the dough, or if you want smaller dumplings, use a teaspoon. Cover the pot with a lid and let the dumplings steam for about 15-20 minutes. Don’t lift the lid during this time, as the dumplings need the steam to cook through properly.

Step 5: Add the Chicken and Final Seasoning

After the dumplings have cooked through and are fluffy and light, add the shredded chicken back into the soup. Stir gently to combine, and let everything heat through for a few minutes. Taste the soup again and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if necessary.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Serve the gluten-free chicken and dumpling soup hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh parsley if desired. It’s the perfect comfort food to enjoy on a chilly evening or anytime you need something filling and satisfying. The tender chicken, hearty vegetables, and fluffy dumplings come together in a way that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Chicken and Dumpling Soup

  • Use high-quality chicken broth: The broth is the base of this soup, so make sure you use a good one. Look for a gluten-free chicken broth or stock, and check the label for any hidden gluten sources. If you want to make your own, homemade chicken broth can add an extra depth of flavor.

  • Make the dumplings in batches: Depending on the size of your pot, it’s best to make the dumplings in small batches to avoid overcrowding. This ensures that each dumpling has enough space to cook properly and stay fluffy.

  • Don’t skip the herbs: Fresh herbs can really elevate the flavor of your soup. Don’t skip the thyme and bay leaves—they add a beautiful earthy aroma and richness to the broth. Fresh parsley sprinkled on top adds a fresh contrast to the savory flavors.

  • Experiment with different vegetables: While carrots, celery, and onions are the classic choices, feel free to add other vegetables like peas, parsnips, or potatoes. Just be sure to chop them into small, uniform pieces so they cook evenly.

  • Make-ahead option: This soup is even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld. If you plan to make it ahead of time, store the soup and dumplings separately to prevent the dumplings from becoming soggy. Reheat when ready to serve.

Conclusion

This gluten-free chicken and dumpling soup is a game-changer for anyone who loves comfort food but needs to avoid gluten. It’s easy to make, satisfying, and packed with all the rich flavors of the classic version. Whether you’re gluten-sensitive, cooking for someone with dietary restrictions, or simply looking for a cozy, delicious meal, this soup is sure to hit the spot. Make it for dinner tonight and enjoy a warm, hearty bowl of comfort that everyone will adore!
