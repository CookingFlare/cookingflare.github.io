Exploring Global Cuisine with Sous Vide Recipes


In recent years, sous vide cooking has taken the culinary world by storm, offering home cooks and professional chefs alike a unique method to create perfectly cooked dishes. This innovative technique, which involves vacuum-sealing food and cooking it in a temperature-controlled water bath, allows for an unparalleled level of precision. As we embark on a journey to explore global cuisine through the lens of sous vide, we’ll uncover recipes from various cultures that highlight the versatility and simplicity of this cooking method.

The Sous Vide Technique: A Brief Overview

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to understand the sous vide technique. The term “sous vide” translates from French to “under vacuum.” The process involves sealing food in a plastic bag or jar and immersing it in a water bath at a consistent temperature for an extended period. This method ensures that the food is cooked evenly, retaining moisture, flavor, and nutrients.

Advantages of Sous Vide Cooking:

  1. Precision: Sous vide allows for precise temperature control, ensuring that proteins are cooked to the perfect level of doneness.
  2. Flavor Retention: The vacuum-sealing process locks in flavors and prevents moisture loss, resulting in juicy and flavorful dishes.
  3. Convenience: Preparing meals sous vide can be done in advance, allowing you to focus on other tasks while the food cooks.
  4. Versatility: This method can be applied to a variety of ingredients, from meats and vegetables to desserts.

Now that we have a foundational understanding of sous vide cooking, let’s explore recipes from around the world that showcase this technique.

1. Sous Vide Thai Chicken Satay

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 3 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, mix together peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, fish sauce, brown sugar, garlic, coriander, cumin, and turmeric until well combined.
  2. Marinate the Chicken: Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and add them to the marinade. Seal in a plastic bag, removing excess air, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for best flavor.
  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C). Place the marinated chicken in the water bath and cook for 1.5 hours.
  4. Prepare Skewers: Once cooked, thread the chicken pieces onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
  5. Sear the Skewers: Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the skewers for 2-3 minutes on each side until lightly charred.
  6. Serve: Serve the chicken satay with a side of peanut sauce and fresh cucumber slices.

2. Sous Vide Italian Osso Buco

Ingredients:

  • 2 veal shanks (about 1.5 inches thick)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the Veal: Season the veal shanks with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the shanks for 3-4 minutes on each side until browned.
  2. Prepare the Sous Vide Bag: Place the seared shanks in a vacuum-seal bag with the onion, carrots, celery, garlic, white wine, chicken stock, thyme, and lemon zest. Seal the bag.
  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C) and cook the osso buco for 24 hours.
  4. Finish and Serve: After cooking, remove the shanks from the bag and strain the cooking liquid. Serve the osso buco with a drizzle of the strained sauce and garnish with chopped parsley.

3. Sous Vide Japanese Salmon Teriyaki

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Chopped green onions and sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, ginger, and sesame oil.
  2. Marinate the Salmon: Season the salmon fillets with salt and place them in a vacuum-seal bag with the marinade. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 125°F (52°C) and cook the salmon for 45 minutes.
  4. Sear the Salmon: Once cooked, remove the salmon from the bag and pat dry. Sear in a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side for a crispy exterior.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds. Serve with steamed rice and vegetables.

4. Sous Vide Mexican Mole Pork

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mole sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the Pork: Season the pork shoulder with salt and pepper. In a skillet, heat vegetable oil and sear the pork on all sides until browned.
  2. Prepare the Sous Vide Bag: Place the seared pork in a vacuum-seal bag with the onion, garlic, mole sauce, and chicken broth. Seal the bag.
  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C) and cook the pork for 24 hours.
  4. Shred and Serve: After cooking, shred the pork with two forks. Serve with warm tortillas and garnish with fresh cilantro.

5. Sous Vide French Ratatouille

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, combine eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, onion, garlic, diced tomatoes, olive oil, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Prepare the Sous Vide Bag: Transfer the mixture to a vacuum-seal bag and seal it.
  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C) and cook the ratatouille for 2 hours.
  4. Serve: Once cooked, serve the ratatouille warm, garnished with fresh herbs or crumbled feta cheese.

Conclusion

Exploring global cuisine through sous vide recipes opens up a world of culinary possibilities. From the vibrant flavors of Thai chicken satay to the rich and hearty Italian osso buco, each dish showcases the unique characteristics of its culture while benefiting from the precision of sous vide cooking. As you experiment with these recipes, don’t hesitate to make adjustments and infuse your personal touch. The versatility of sous vide allows for endless creativity in the kitchen, making it an exciting method to explore and enjoy the diverse flavors of global cuisines. Happy cooking!
