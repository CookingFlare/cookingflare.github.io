How to Prepare Arni Psito: Greek Style Roasted Lamb


Few dishes capture the heart of Greek cuisine like Arni Psito, a traditional roasted lamb dish known for its tender meat, aromatic spices, and rich flavors. This classic recipe has been a centerpiece of Greek feasting traditions for centuries, served at celebrations, Sunday family gatherings, or during special holidays like Easter. The slow-roasting technique, combined with Mediterranean herbs and fresh lemon juice, creates a succulent masterpiece worthy of every bite.

In this blog post, you’ll learn step-by-step how to prepare Arni Psito at home for an authentic Greek experience your guests won’t forget. From selecting the right cut of lamb to seasoning and roasting it to perfection, we’ll cover it all. Let’s dive straight into creating this delicious roasted lamb dish!

Ingredients for Arni Psito

To recreate this traditional Greek recipe, you’ll need fresh, high-quality ingredients that will bring out the flavors of this incredible roasted lamb dish. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Lamb:

  • 1 leg of lamb (3-4 lbs), bone-in for maximum flavor
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, extra virgin preferred
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 1 tsp dried oregano, Greek oregano for authenticity
  • 1 tsp thyme, dried or fresh
  • 1 tbsp rosemary, freshly chopped or dried
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)

For the Roasted Vegetables:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced into thick chunks
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • Extra olive oil, for drizzling over the vegetables
  • Fresh lemon juice, for added brightness
  • 1/2 tsp oregano, for seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Additional:

  • Water or white wine (about 1 cup)
  • Cooking twine, if needed for trussing the lamb

These ingredients reflect the straightforward yet bold flavors of Greek cooking, where simplicity allows the natural taste of the meat and herbs to shine.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Lamb

The key to making perfect Arni Psito begins with proper preparation. Before anything else, trim any excess fat from the lamb leg, leaving some for flavor. Fat helps keep the meat moist during roasting but don’t overdo it.

Next, marinate the lamb:

  1. In a bowl, mix the minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, zest, oregano, thyme, and rosemary. Add a generous sprinkle of salt and black pepper to the mixture.
  2. Use your hands or a brush to rub this flavorful marinade all over the lamb. Make sure every inch is well-coated, including underneath any folds in the meat.
  3. For extra flavor, make small slits in the lamb with a sharp knife and insert additional minced garlic and rosemary into the cuts. This step infuses the meat as it roasts.

Once seasoned, let the lamb marinate for at least 1 hour at room temperature or up to 12 hours in the refrigerator. The longer the marination, the deeper the flavors.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

Prepping the vegetables ensures they roast beautifully alongside the lamb. In a mixing bowl, toss the potato wedges, carrot chunks, and onion slices with olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper. The combination of citrus and herbs will complement the lamb perfectly, creating a well-rounded Mediterranean flavor profile.

Set the seasoned vegetables aside while you prepare the roasting pan.

Step 3: Assemble the Roasting Pan

Choose a large roasting pan or dish that can fit both the lamb leg and the vegetables comfortably. Lightly grease the pan with olive oil. Arrange the vegetables in a single layer at the bottom of the pan, creating a flavorful bed for the lamb to rest on.

Place the lamb leg directly on top of the vegetables or use a rack if preferred—this allows the juices to drip down and flavor the base while preventing soggy potatoes. Add about one cup of water or white wine to the pan to create steam during cooking, which prevents drying and enhances the lamb’s tenderness.

Cover the roasting pan with aluminum foil to seal the moisture, but don’t press the foil too tightly; leave some space for steam circulation.

Step 4: Roast to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C), a relatively low temperature for slow roasting. The hallmark of Arni Psito’s succulent texture comes from cooking the lamb slowly over several hours.

  1. Start by cooking the lamb, covered, for about 2.5 to 3 hours. Check the pan every hour to ensure there’s still liquid for steaming—add water or wine as necessary.
  2. After 2-3 hours, remove the foil to allow the lamb to brown and crisp up on the outside. Increase the temperature slightly to 375°F (190°C), and roast uncovered for an additional 30-45 minutes.

Use a meat thermometer to ensure the lamb is cooked to the desired doneness. For medium-rare lamb, aim for an internal temperature of 135°F (57°C). For medium, go up to 145°F (63°C). Remember that the lamb will continue cooking slightly after being removed from the oven, so don’t overdo it.

Step 5: Rest the Lamb and Serve

Once done, remove the lamb from the oven and transfer it to a cutting board. Tent it loosely with foil and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes before carving. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring every slice is flavorful and moist.

During the resting period, take a look at the vegetables. They should be perfectly roasted, infused with the lamb’s juices and seasoning. If you want extra caramelization, you can return the vegetables to the oven for another 10 minutes at high heat.

Carve the lamb into thick slices and arrange them on a serving platter. Surround the lamb with the roasted vegetables for a stunning presentation. Drizzle any pan juices over the top for an extra burst of flavor, or serve the juices as a simple sauce on the side.

Tips for the Best Arni Psito

  • Choose the right lamb: Opt for a young lamb leg for tenderness. Always select high-quality meat from a trusted butcher for the best results.
  • Marinate overnight: If you have time, marinate the lamb for 12-24 hours in the fridge. The longer marination enhances the dish’s depth and aroma.
  • Use fresh herbs: While dried herbs work well, fresh oregano and rosemary offer more robust flavors that elevate the dish to restaurant-quality.
  • Pair with traditional sides: Arni Psito pairs wonderfully with a Greek salad, tzatziki sauce, or freshly baked pita bread.
  • Experiment with seasoning: Though this recipe is classic, some variations include adding cinnamon or nutmeg for a subtle earthiness.

Closing Thoughts

Arni Psito is much more than a meal—it’s a culinary journey straight to the heart of Greece. With its irresistible aroma, tender meat, and herbaceous character, this roasted lamb dish embodies the warmth and joy of Mediterranean cooking. Whether you’re hosting a Greek-themed dinner night or celebrating a special occasion, preparing Arni Psito will leave your guests raving about your culinary skills.

Take your time with each step, savor the process, and let this Greek masterpiece become a cherished addition to your menu. Enjoy your Arni Psito with loved ones and pair it with a glass of crisp white wine or even a traditional Greek ouzo for the ultimate experience. Happy roasting!
