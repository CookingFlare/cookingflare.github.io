How to Make Spanish Empanadas: A Delicious Handheld Snack


Spanish empanadas are an irresistible, savory handheld treat that combines bold flavors, flaky pastry, and a sense of tradition. These pocket-sized delights are deeply rooted in Spanish cuisine, offering a hearty dose of comfort and versatility. Whether served as an appetizer, a snack, or even a main course with sides, Spanish empanadas can brighten any meal with their rich and satisfying filling encased in golden, tender pastry.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to make authentic Spanish empanadas from scratch. From crafting perfectly flaky pastry dough to making a traditional filling, you’ll discover that these savory snacks are both rewarding to make and loved by everyone at your table.

What Are Empanadas?

Empanadas have long been a staple of Spanish cuisine, with roots that trace back centuries. The term “empanada” comes from the Spanish word “empanar,” which means “to wrap or coat in bread.” While the concept of encasing food in bread or dough is universal across cultures, Spain holds a special place in the history of empanadas.

Spanish empanadas typically feature fillings like spiced beef, chicken, vegetables, chorizo, or seafood, reflecting the versatile nature of this dish. The pastry dough itself is rich and buttery, making it a perfect vehicle for the bold flavors inside. Empanadas are baked or fried, creating a crispy or flaky texture that complements the filling beautifully.

Ingredients for Spanish Empanadas

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s start with the ingredients you’ll need to make the pastry dough and the filling.

For the Dough

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 stick (½ cup) unsalted butter (cold and cubed)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon paprika (optional, for flavor and color)
  • ½ cup cold water

For the Filling

Spanish empanadas are endlessly versatile, but let’s go with a classic spiced beef filling featuring vibrant Mediterranean flavors.

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ pound ground beef
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon chili powder (optional, for a hint of heat)
  • ½ cup diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup chopped green olives
  • ¼ cup raisins (optional, for a sweet contrast)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 beaten egg (for sealing and brushing the pastry before baking)

How to Make Spanish Empanadas

Follow this step-by-step method to prepare your homemade Spanish empanadas, with a balance of crisp pastry and bold fillings.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by making the empanada dough. The secret to great pastry is keeping everything cold so the butter creates pockets of flakiness when baked.

  1. Mix dry ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and paprika (if using). Stir well to create a uniform mix.

  2. Cut in the butter
    Add the cubed cold butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add cold water
    Gradually pour the cold water into the dry mixture. Mix until it comes together into a cohesive dough. Be careful not to overmix; you want the dough to remain tender.

  4. Let the dough rest
    Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This helps the gluten relax and keeps the butter firm.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is chilling, turn your attention to the filling. The spiced beef filling is rich and perfectly seasoned with Spanish-inspired flavors.

  1. Cook the aromatics
    Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until softened and fragrant, about 3 minutes.

  2. Brown the beef
    Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon. Cook until browned and no longer pink.

  3. Season the filling
    Stir in smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Let the spices toast for a minute to infuse the beef with vibrant flavor.

  4. Add tomatoes and olives
    Mix in the diced tomatoes and chopped green olives. Simmer for 5–7 minutes, allowing the mixture to thicken slightly.

  5. Optional: Add raisins
    Stir in raisins if you like a touch of sweetness in your empanadas. The balance of savory and sweet is a hallmark of Spanish cuisine.

  6. Cool the filling
    Remove the skillet from heat and let the mixture cool completely. A cooled filling is much easier to work with when stuffing your empanadas.

Step 3: Assemble and Shape the Empanadas

Once your dough has rested and your filling has cooled, it’s time to put everything together.

  1. Roll out the dough
    On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to about ⅛ inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 4–5 inches in diameter) to cut out circles.

  2. Fill the empanadas
    Place a spoonful of the filling onto one side of each dough round, leaving enough space to fold and seal the edge.

  3. Seal the edges
    Brush the edges of the dough lightly with beaten egg. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly with your fingers, then crimp them with the tines of a fork for a decorative and secure seal.

  4. Repeat
    Continue this process with all the dough circles and filling. Once assembled, arrange the empanadas on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Step 4: Bake the Empanadas

Although empanadas can be fried, a baked version is easier and produces a flaky, golden finish that highlights the buttery pastry.

  1. Brush with egg wash
    Lightly brush the tops of each empanada with the remaining beaten egg. This will give them a shiny, golden appearance when baked.

  2. Bake
    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the empanadas for 20–25 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crisp.

  3. Cool slightly
    Once baked, let the empanadas cool slightly on a wire rack before serving. This will allow the filling to set and the pastry to firm up.

Serving Suggestions for Spanish Empanadas

Spanish empanadas are incredibly versatile and can be served in several ways:

  • Pair them with a refreshing salad and Spanish gazpacho for a complete meal.
  • Serve them hot or at room temperature as a party appetizer alongside a spicy dipping sauce or aioli.
  • Enjoy them with a glass of Spanish red wine, like Rioja, for a cozy snack.

Tips for Perfect Spanish Empanadas

  • Keep your ingredients cold: Cold dough leads to flakier pastry, so avoid letting the butter soften too much while working.
  • Experiment with fillings: Besides beef, try chicken, seafood, or vegetarian variations using sautéed spinach, mushrooms, or roasted peppers.
  • Crimp the edges properly: A well-sealed empanada is less likely to open during baking or frying.
  • Freeze extras: Empanadas freeze beautifully, making them a convenient snack or meal for later. Simply freeze them unbaked and bake straight from frozen (add a few minutes to the baking time).

Final Thoughts

Spanish empanadas are a culinary treasure that encapsulates the essence of Spanish cuisine—simple, bold, and utterly satisfying. Making them from scratch may require some effort, but the pride of creating these golden, flaky pastries brimming with delicious filling is well worth it. Plus, they’re a fantastic way to bring your family or friends together over great food.

Whether you choose the classic spiced beef filling or get creative with your own variation, Spanish empanadas will quickly become one of your favorite handheld snacks for any occasion. Give this recipe a go, and enjoy the rich flavors and textures of this beloved Spanish dish!
