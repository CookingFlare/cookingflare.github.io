10 Cheap and Easy Dessert Recipes for Sweet Tooths


Few things bring more joy after a meal than a sweet treat. Whether you’re hosting guests, feeding family, or indulging solo, dessert doesn’t have to cost a fortune, and it certainly shouldn’t be complicated. If you’re looking for budget-friendly and delicious dessert ideas that require minimal effort, you’re in the right place. Here are ten fantastic dessert recipes to satisfy your sweet tooth without breaking the bank.

1. One-Bowl Brownies

Brownies are a timeless classic, and this recipe keeps things simple. Who can resist gooey chocolate goodness? Plus, using just one bowl saves time on cleanup.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a square baking pan.
  2. In a bowl, mix melted butter, sugar, and cocoa powder until smooth.
  3. Stir in eggs, vanilla extract, flour, and salt until just combined.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 20-25 minutes.
  5. Let the brownies cool before slicing. Enjoy with an optional dusting of powdered sugar.

2. No-Bake Peanut Butter Bars

For peanut butter lovers, this no-bake recipe combines affordability, simplicity, and decadence. Perfect for when you’re short on time but craving indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • ½ cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Mix melted butter, graham cracker crumbs, powdered sugar, and peanut butter in a bowl until combined.
  2. Press the mixture into a greased 8x8 pan.
  3. Melt chocolate chips in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  4. Spread melted chocolate over the peanut butter layer.
  5. Chill in the fridge for 2 hours, then cut into squares.

3. Rice Krispie Treats with a Twist

Rice Krispie treats are easy, cheap, and endlessly customizable. This twist incorporates a drizzle of chocolate or some fun sprinkles for an extra wow factor.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 10 oz marshmallows
  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • Optional: melted chocolate, sprinkles, or candy pieces

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add marshmallows and stir until fully melted.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in the cereal until coated.
  3. Press the mixture evenly into a greased baking dish.
  4. Drizzle melted chocolate or top with sprinkles, then let it set before slicing into bars.

4. Banana Bread Mug Cake

If you only have a minute and a ripe banana, this mug cake is a lifesaver. It’s sweet, quick, and the perfect portion size for one.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe banana (mashed)
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp melted butter
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • ½ tsp baking powder

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe mug, combine all ingredients until smooth.
  2. Microwave on high for 1-2 minutes, or until the cake is set.
  3. Let cool slightly and enjoy straight out of the mug.

5. Three-Ingredient Peanut Butter Cookies

You likely already have these ingredients in your pantry. These cookies are chewy, simple, and naturally gluten-free.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix peanut butter, sugar, and egg in a bowl until combined.
  3. Roll the dough into small balls, place on the sheet, and flatten with a fork.
  4. Bake for 8-10 minutes. Let cool before serving.

6. Apple Cinnamon Crisps

Transform fresh apples into a sweet, crunchy dessert. This healthy option is as simple as it gets.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large apples (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°F (93°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place apple slices on the sheet and sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar.
  3. Bake for 2 hours, flipping halfway through, until crispy.
  4. Cool before enjoying. They’re great on their own or dipped in caramel sauce.

7. Chocolate-Dipped Pretzels

This quick dessert is a great combination of salty and sweet. Perfect for parties, gifting, or midweek snacking.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pretzel rods or mini pretzels
  • 1 cup melted chocolate chips
  • Optional: crushed nuts, sprinkles, or sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt chocolate in the microwave, stirring until smooth.
  2. Dip the pretzels halfway into the chocolate and let excess drip off.
  3. Place on parchment paper and immediately add your desired toppings.
  4. Let cool until the chocolate hardens.

8. Vanilla Pudding with Cookie Crumbs

Homemade vanilla pudding topped with crushed cookies is an easy, cost-effective dessert that feels luxurious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Crushed cookies for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, mix cornstarch and sugar. Gradually whisk in milk.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  3. Remove from heat, stir in vanilla extract, and pour into serving dishes.
  4. Chill for a few hours, then top with crushed cookies before serving.

9. Frozen Yogurt Bark

Healthy, delicious, and endlessly customizable, frozen yogurt bark is a simple snack that doubles as a dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain or flavored yogurt
  • ½ cup mixed fruits
  • Optional: honey or granola

Instructions:

  1. Spread yogurt evenly on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Sprinkle with mixed fruits and drizzle with honey or granola if desired.
  3. Freeze for 3 hours or until set. Break into pieces and serve.

10. Homemade Chocolate Pudding

Pudding is a comfort dessert, and making it from scratch adds a personal touch without needing much time or money.

Ingredients:

  • 2⅓ cups milk
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together sugar, cocoa powder, and cornstarch.
  2. Gradually whisk in milk and cook over medium heat, stirring often.
  3. Allow the mixture to thicken, remove from heat, and stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Serve warm or chilled. Add whipped cream if desired.

Each dessert on this list proves that delicious treats don’t have to take a toll on your wallet or your schedule. With just a few pantry staples and minimal effort, you can whip up something delightful that will satisfy your sweet tooth and impress those around you. Happy baking—or no-baking!
