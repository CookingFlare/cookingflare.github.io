Cooking for Two: Simple and Quick Mexican Recipes


Cooking for two can be both a challenge and an opportunity to experiment with flavors. When it comes to making delicious meals for two, there’s no need for elaborate dishes that take hours to prepare. Mexican cuisine offers a wide array of bold and vibrant flavors that can be put together quickly, making it the perfect choice for a cozy dinner for two.

Whether you’re craving tacos, burritos, or something new, these simple and quick Mexican recipes will satisfy your taste buds while requiring minimal effort and time. From sizzling fajitas to savory quesadillas, each recipe is designed to be easy, accessible, and full of flavor.

1. Classic Chicken Fajitas

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fajita seasoning (or homemade)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • Optional toppings: Sour cream, guacamole, salsa, shredded cheese

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the chicken: Start by cutting the chicken breasts into thin strips. Season them with salt, pepper, and fajita seasoning. Set aside to marinate for about 10 minutes.

  2. Cook the veggies: Heat one tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the bell peppers and onions to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes until they soften and begin to char. Remove them from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Cook the chicken: In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Add the chicken strips and cook for 5-6 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and slightly browned.

  4. Assemble the fajitas: Warm the tortillas in a separate skillet or microwave. Once the chicken is cooked, return the peppers and onions to the pan and toss everything together. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the mixture for added flavor.

  5. Serve: Spoon the chicken and veggie mixture onto each tortilla. Top with sour cream, salsa, shredded cheese, or guacamole, if desired. Fold the tortilla and enjoy!

Pro Tip: If you want to add a little heat, throw in some sliced jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce. This dish is both fresh and zesty, making it a fantastic choice for a quick meal.

2. Easy Beef Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 4 small taco shells
  • Lettuce, shredded
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • Sour cream
  • Salsa

Instructions:

  1. Cook the beef: Heat a skillet over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.

  2. Season the beef: Add the diced onion and cook until it softens. Stir in the taco seasoning and water. Let it simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend and thicken.

  3. Prepare the toppings: While the beef is cooking, chop the lettuce, tomatoes, and set out your other desired toppings like cheese, sour cream, and salsa.

  4. Assemble the tacos: Warm the taco shells in the oven or microwave. Once warmed, spoon the beef mixture into each shell. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheese, sour cream, and salsa.

  5. Serve: Serve your tacos immediately while they’re hot and crispy.

Pro Tip: For extra flavor, add a sprinkle of fresh cilantro or a squeeze of lime juice to your tacos.

3. Cheesy Mexican Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 2 flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a Mexican blend)
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken or beef (optional)
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Sour cream and salsa for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a small bowl, mix together the shredded cheese, diced onions, bell peppers, and any cooked chicken or beef if using.

  2. Cook the quesadilla: Heat a large skillet over medium heat and melt the butter. Place one tortilla in the pan and sprinkle the cheese mixture evenly over the top. Place the second tortilla on top.

  3. Grill the quesadilla: Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until the tortillas are golden and the cheese has melted inside. You can press it down lightly with a spatula to ensure an even cook.

  4. Serve: Remove from the pan and slice into wedges. Serve with sour cream and salsa on the side for dipping.

Pro Tip: For a different twist, try adding some fresh spinach or black beans to the filling for an extra dose of flavor.

4. Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon sour cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1/4 cup crumbled cotija cheese (or feta cheese)
  • Chopped cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Grill the corn: Preheat the grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally until charred and cooked through.

  2. Make the sauce: While the corn is grilling, mix together the mayonnaise, sour cream, chili powder, and lime juice in a small bowl.

  3. Coat the corn: Once the corn is cooked, brush the mayo-sour cream mixture onto each ear of corn. Sprinkle the corn with crumbled cotija cheese and a bit of chopped cilantro.

  4. Serve: Serve immediately as a delicious side dish or appetizer.

Pro Tip: For an extra kick, sprinkle a little more chili powder or cayenne pepper over the top before serving.

5. Simple Mexican Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup white rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 onion, finely diced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the rice and cook, stirring frequently, until it turns golden brown, about 5 minutes.

  2. Sauté the aromatics: Add the diced onion and garlic to the rice and cook for 2 minutes until softened.

  3. Simmer the rice: Add the tomato sauce, chicken broth, salt, pepper, and cumin. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then cover the pot and reduce the heat to low. Cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.

  4. Serve: Fluff the rice with a fork and serve alongside your Mexican dishes.

Pro Tip: For extra flavor, try stirring in a bit of lime zest or fresh cilantro before serving.

6. Vegetarian Mexican Tostadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 tostada shells
  • 1/2 cup refried beans (or black beans)
  • 1/4 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Salsa

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the beans: If using refried beans, heat them up in a small pot over low heat until warm. If using black beans, drain and rinse them, then heat them in a pot.

  2. Assemble the tostadas: Spread a layer of beans over each tostada shell. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheese, and a dollop of sour cream.

  3. Serve: Drizzle with salsa and serve immediately for a crunchy, satisfying vegetarian meal.

Pro Tip: Add a few slices of avocado or a sprinkle of hot sauce for an extra punch of flavor.

7. Churros for Two

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Make the dough: In a small saucepan, bring the water and butter to a boil. Add the flour and stir until the dough forms. Remove from heat and let it cool for a minute. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract.

  2. Fry the churros: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Use a pastry bag or a plastic bag with the tip cut off to pipe the dough into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  3. Coat the churros: In a small bowl, mix together the sugar and cinnamon. Once the churros are cooked, roll them in the sugar-cinnamon mixture.

  4. Serve: Serve your churros warm with a side of chocolate sauce for dipping.

Pro Tip: For a unique twist, drizzle the churros with caramel sauce or sprinkle them with a pinch of sea salt.

Final Thoughts

Cooking for two doesn’t have to be a daunting task. With these simple and quick Mexican recipes, you can create flavorful, satisfying meals in no time. From fajitas to churros, Mexican cuisine offers a variety of dishes that are perfect for sharing. Whether you’re enjoying a quiet dinner at home or hosting a small gathering, these recipes will bring the bold flavors of Mexico to your table with minimal effort. So grab your ingredients, fire up the stove, and enjoy a delicious Mexican meal for two!
