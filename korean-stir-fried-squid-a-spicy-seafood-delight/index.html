Korean Stir-Fried Squid: A Spicy Seafood Delight


If you’re a fan of seafood and bold flavors, then Korean stir-fried squid is a dish that deserves a spot in your recipe collection. This spicy and savory dish is a staple in Korean cuisine, known for its vibrant colors and mouthwatering aroma. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe will take your taste buds on an unforgettable adventure.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fresh squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 3-4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prep the Squid: Start by cleaning the squid thoroughly and slicing it into rings. If you’re using frozen squid, make sure it’s fully thawed before cooking. Pat the squid dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the Korean chili paste, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Mix well until the sugar is dissolved. This sauce will be the star of the dish, infusing the squid with its spicy and savory flavors.

  3. Stir-Fry: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Then, add the sliced onion and julienned red bell pepper. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  4. Add the Squid: Once the vegetables are slightly softened, add the prepared squid to the pan. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the squid turns opaque and curls up. Be careful not to overcook the squid, as it can become tough and rubbery.

  5. Incorporate the Sauce: Pour the prepared sauce over the stir-fried squid and vegetables. Toss everything together to ensure the squid is coated evenly with the flavorful sauce. The vibrant red color of the sauce will beautifully coat the squid, creating an enticing visual appeal.

  6. Finish with Green Onions: Add the green onion pieces to the pan and give everything a final toss. The green onions will add a fresh, aromatic note to the dish, balancing out the spiciness of the sauce.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Transfer the Korean stir-fried squid to a serving plate and sprinkle with roasted sesame seeds for a delightful nutty flavor. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed rice, allowing you to savor the rich flavors and textures of the squid and vegetables.

Tips for Success

  • Fresh Squid: Opt for fresh squid whenever possible, as it will yield a more tender and flavorful result compared to frozen squid.
  • Adjusting Spice Level: If you prefer a milder or spicier dish, feel free to adjust the amount of Korean chili paste according to your taste preferences.
  • Quick Cooking: Stir-frying squid is a quick process, so make sure all your ingredients are prepped and ready to go before you start cooking.

Korean stir-fried squid is a dish that celebrates the natural sweetness of squid, enhanced by the bold flavors of Korean seasonings. The tender texture of the squid paired with the crunch of vegetables creates a delightful contrast in every bite. This recipe is a perfect introduction to Korean cuisine, showcasing the harmonious balance of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors that make it so beloved.

So, the next time you’re craving a seafood dish with a kick, give Korean stir-fried squid a try. It’s a culinary adventure that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Korea, where bold flavors and fresh ingredients reign supreme. Happy cooking!
