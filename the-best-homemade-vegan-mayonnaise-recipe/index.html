The Best Homemade Vegan Mayonnaise Recipe


When it comes to making plant-based versions of traditional condiments, vegan mayonnaise stands out as one of the most versatile and delicious alternatives. Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to reduce your intake of animal products, this homemade vegan mayo recipe will elevate your dishes and offer a creamy, flavorful substitute to store-bought versions. The best part? It’s quick, easy, and requires just a few simple ingredients you probably already have in your pantry.

In this blog post, we will walk through the step-by-step process of making your very own vegan mayo, explore its variations, and provide tips on how to store and use it in various dishes. By the end of this guide, you’ll be ready to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills while keeping things cruelty-free!

What Is Vegan Mayonnaise?

Vegan mayonnaise is a plant-based alternative to traditional mayonnaise, which typically contains eggs, oil, vinegar, and seasonings. The core difference is that vegan mayo uses plant-based ingredients to achieve that same creamy texture and tangy flavor. The main ingredients in vegan mayonnaise are usually oil, plant-based milk, mustard, vinegar or lemon juice, and seasonings, creating a smooth and creamy texture that can be used in a variety of recipes like sandwiches, salads, and sauces.

The beauty of homemade vegan mayonnaise is that you have complete control over the ingredients, which means you can customize the taste, texture, and consistency to suit your preferences. Plus, homemade mayo is free from preservatives, additives, and artificial ingredients that often appear in store-bought versions.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this recipe, you’ll need just a few simple, easily accessible ingredients. Below is a list of everything you’ll need to make the best homemade vegan mayo:

  • 1 cup neutral-flavored oil (sunflower, canola, or avocado oil work well)
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened plant-based milk (almond, soy, oat, or cashew milk are great options)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (for that tangy kick)
  • 2 teaspoons apple cider vinegar or lemon juice (to add acidity and enhance the flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper (optional, for seasoning)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional, for added depth of flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder (optional, for extra richness)

Optional ingredients:

  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup or agave (if you prefer a slightly sweeter mayo)
  • 1 teaspoon nutritional yeast (for a cheesy flavor, optional)

Now that you have everything you need, let’s dive into the recipe!

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Vegan Mayonnaise

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you start, make sure that all your ingredients are at room temperature. This will help ensure a smoother, more stable emulsion. If you’re using oil straight from the fridge, it may cause the mayo to separate or not blend as smoothly.

Step 2: Blend the Wet Ingredients

In a tall, narrow container (a mason jar or any tall mixing jar will work), combine the plant-based milk, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar (or lemon juice), salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Using an immersion blender (also known as a stick blender), blend everything on medium speed until the ingredients are well combined and the mixture starts to thicken slightly.

If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can use a regular blender or food processor. Just make sure the container is deep enough so that all the ingredients stay submerged during blending.

Step 3: Add the Oil

While blending, very slowly start adding the oil in a thin, steady stream. It’s crucial that you add the oil gradually to help the mayo emulsify properly. Keep blending until all the oil has been incorporated and the mixture has thickened to a creamy consistency.

If you’re using a regular blender, you can start adding the oil in small amounts, and then gradually increase the speed. If using a food processor, add the oil slowly through the feeding tube while the processor is running.

Step 4: Taste and Adjust Seasonings

Once the mayo is thick and smooth, taste it! You may find that it needs a little extra salt, vinegar, or mustard to suit your taste buds. If you like a sweeter mayo, you can add a small amount of maple syrup or agave syrup. For a more pronounced flavor, you can increase the amount of garlic powder or add a pinch of smoked paprika.

Step 5: Store the Mayonnaise

Transfer the freshly made vegan mayonnaise into a clean, airtight container. Store it in the refrigerator for up to a week. Since this recipe doesn’t contain preservatives, it’s best to use it within this time frame.

Tips for Perfecting Your Homemade Vegan Mayo

  • Consistency: If you prefer a thinner mayo, you can add a bit more plant-based milk. For a thicker consistency, reduce the amount of oil or use a bit less liquid.
  • Oil Choices: While neutral oils work best for mayo, you can experiment with oils like olive oil. Just keep in mind that olive oil will add a more robust flavor, so it may not be as neutral-tasting.
  • Blending Equipment: While an immersion blender is ideal for making vegan mayo, you can use a high-speed blender or a food processor. If using a blender, it’s essential to make sure the ingredients stay submerged while blending.
  • Flavor Customization: Feel free to add herbs, spices, or seasonings to match your flavor preferences. You can experiment with smoked paprika, cumin, or chili powder for a different flavor profile.
  • Storing: Make sure the jar or container you use for storage is well-sealed to keep the mayo fresh. Refrigerate it immediately after making it to ensure it stays as fresh as possible.

How to Use Vegan Mayonnaise

Vegan mayo is incredibly versatile and can be used in various recipes. Here are some of the most popular ways to enjoy your homemade mayo:

1. Sandwiches and Wraps

The most obvious use for vegan mayo is as a spread on sandwiches, wraps, or veggie burgers. It adds a creamy texture and rich flavor that enhances the other ingredients in your meal. Pair it with fresh vegetables like tomatoes, lettuce, and cucumbers, or use it to elevate your veggie wraps.

2. Dips and Sauces

Use your homemade vegan mayo as a base for dips and sauces. Combine it with herbs, garlic, and lemon juice to make a tangy garlic aioli, or stir in some sriracha or chipotle powder for a spicy kick. It also works well as a base for ranch dressing, Caesar dressing, or tartar sauce.

3. Salads

Vegan mayo is an excellent ingredient for creamy salads. Toss it with chickpeas, potatoes, or pasta to make potato salad, coleslaw, or a pasta salad. Its smooth texture and subtle tangy flavor make it the perfect binding agent for these dishes.

4. Vegan Sushi Rolls

For those who enjoy sushi, vegan mayo can be a great accompaniment to vegan sushi rolls. You can even use it to drizzle over avocado rolls or create a spicy mayo by adding chili sauce.

5. Roasted Vegetables

Vegan mayo can also be used to coat roasted vegetables for an added layer of richness. For example, toss some cauliflower, sweet potatoes, or Brussels sprouts in a bit of mayo before roasting them for a delightful, creamy texture.

6. Vegan Deviled Eggs

Yes, you can even make a vegan version of deviled eggs using your homemade mayo. Combine mashed tofu with a bit of vegan mayo, mustard, and turmeric to create a creamy filling. Pipe this mixture into halved avocado or vegan egg substitutes for a delicious plant-based appetizer.

Common Vegan Mayonnaise Variations

1. Garlic Vegan Mayonnaise

If you love garlic, you can easily turn this recipe into garlic mayo by adding a clove or two of fresh minced garlic. Alternatively, use garlic powder for a more subtle garlic flavor. This variation is great for dipping fries or as a topping for vegan burgers.

2. Lemon and Dill Mayonnaise

For a tangy, herb-infused mayo, add fresh or dried dill and a little extra lemon juice. This combination works beautifully with potato salad, grilled vegetables, and as a spread for sandwiches or wraps.

3. Smoky Vegan Mayonnaise

To give your mayo a smoky twist, add smoked paprika or chipotle powder to taste. This variation pairs well with roasted vegetables, vegan barbecue sandwiches, and as a dip for tortilla chips.

4. Spicy Sriracha Mayonnaise

If you’re a fan of heat, adding sriracha or any hot sauce of your choice will give your vegan mayo a spicy kick. This version works great as a dipping sauce for fries, onion rings, or as a topping for tacos.

Final Thoughts

Making your own vegan mayonnaise is incredibly easy, and the taste is far superior to most store-bought options. By using simple ingredients and a quick blending process, you can create a creamy, flavorful condiment that complements a wide variety of dishes. Plus, knowing exactly what goes into your mayo makes it a healthier, preservative-free choice for your meals.

Now that you’ve learned how to make the best homemade vegan mayo, experiment with different flavors and incorporate it into your favorite recipes. Whether you use it as a sandwich spread, a dip, or a base for creamy salads, your homemade mayo will add a delicious touch to any dish!

Enjoy creating your perfect vegan mayo and share it with others—you’ll be amazed at how easy and satisfying it is to make your own delicious, plant-based condiments!
