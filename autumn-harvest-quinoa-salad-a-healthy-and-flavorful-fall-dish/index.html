Autumn Harvest Quinoa Salad: A Healthy and Flavorful Fall Dish


Fall is the perfect time to indulge in the abundance of seasonal produce. And what better way to celebrate the flavors of autumn than with a hearty and nutritious salad? Introducing the Autumn Harvest Quinoa Salad – a delicious combination of wholesome ingredients that will leave you feeling satisfied and nourished.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup butternut squash, cubed
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is fluffy and cooked through. Set aside to cool.

  2. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, spread out the butternut squash and Brussels sprouts. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted butternut squash, Brussels sprouts, pomegranate seeds, dried cranberries, pecans, red onion, and fresh parsley.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Allow the salad to sit for a few minutes to let the flavors meld together. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Why You’ll Love It

The Autumn Harvest Quinoa Salad is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Let’s take a closer look at some of the key ingredients and their benefits:

Quinoa: This ancient grain is a complete protein, containing all nine essential amino acids. It’s also rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making it an excellent choice for a healthy diet.

Butternut Squash: Bursting with vitamins A and C, as well as potassium and fiber, butternut squash adds a sweet and nutty flavor to the salad. It’s also low in calories, making it a great option for weight management.

Brussels Sprouts: These mini cabbages are loaded with nutrients, including vitamins K and C, folate, and fiber. They add a satisfying crunch and a slightly bitter note to the salad.

Pomegranate Seeds: Packed with antioxidants, pomegranate seeds not only add a burst of color but also provide a refreshing and tangy taste. They are also believed to have anti-inflammatory properties.

Dried Cranberries: Sweet and tart, dried cranberries offer a pop of flavor and a dose of antioxidants. They are also a good source of fiber and vitamin C.

Pecans: Not only do pecans provide a satisfying crunch, but they are also a rich source of healthy fats, fiber, and essential minerals like manganese and copper.

Red Onion: Adding a touch of sharpness, red onion not only enhances the flavor but also provides a dose of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Fresh Parsley: Adding a bright and herbaceous flavor, parsley is rich in vitamins K, C, and A. It also contains antioxidants and has been linked to improved digestion and reduced inflammation.

Conclusion

The Autumn Harvest Quinoa Salad is a delightful combination of autumnal flavors, textures, and nutrients. It’s a versatile dish that can be served as a main course or a side dish, perfect for a cozy fall dinner or a holiday gathering. So, embrace the season and enjoy the bountiful flavors of autumn with this healthy and flavorful salad. Bon appétit!
