Bring on the Heat: Homemade Ghost Pepper Hot Sauce


If you’re a fan of all things spicy, then you’re in for a treat with this homemade ghost pepper hot sauce recipe. Ghost peppers, also known as Bhut Jolokia, are one of the hottest peppers in the world, measuring over 1,000,000 Scoville heat units. This fiery pepper is not for the faint of heart, but if you’re ready to bring on the heat, then let’s dive into making your very own ghost pepper hot sauce.

Ingredients

  • 10-12 fresh ghost peppers
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by wearing gloves to handle the ghost peppers, as the capsaicin in these peppers can cause intense irritation. Remove the stems from the peppers and roughly chop them. Peel the garlic cloves and chop the onion and tomatoes.

  2. Sauté: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until they are soft and fragrant.

  3. Add Peppers: Next, add the chopped ghost peppers to the saucepan and continue to cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Tomatoes and Seasoning: Once the ghost peppers have softened slightly, add the chopped tomatoes, sugar, salt, and black pepper to the saucepan. Stir well and let the mixture simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the tomatoes have broken down and the flavors have melded together.

  5. Blend: Remove the saucepan from the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly. Carefully transfer the contents to a blender and blend until smooth. Be cautious of the fumes when you open the blender, as they can be quite potent.

  6. Strain and Bottle: After blending, strain the hot sauce through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any remaining solids. Then, pour the hot sauce into sterilized glass bottles or jars. Allow the hot sauce to cool completely before sealing the bottles.

  7. Storage: Store the homemade ghost pepper hot sauce in the refrigerator for up to 6 months. The flavors will continue to develop over time, so feel free to let it age for a more intense flavor.

Serving Suggestions

This homemade ghost pepper hot sauce is not for the faint-hearted, but for those who love a good kick of heat in their dishes. Use it sparingly to add a fiery punch to your favorite foods. Drizzle it over tacos, stir a few drops into soups or stews, or use it as a marinade for grilled meats. Just remember, a little goes a long way with ghost pepper hot sauce.

Safety Precautions

When working with ghost peppers, it’s essential to take safety precautions. Always wear gloves when handling the peppers, and be mindful of the fumes when cooking and blending the hot sauce. Keep the kitchen well-ventilated and avoid inhaling the potent vapors. Additionally, be sure to wash your hands thoroughly after handling the peppers to prevent accidental contact with sensitive areas.

Final Thoughts

Homemade ghost pepper hot sauce is a bold and intense addition to any spicy food lover’s pantry. With its vibrant color and explosive heat, this hot sauce is not only a culinary experience but also a testament to the power of the ghost pepper. So, if you’re ready to take your taste buds on a fiery adventure, try making your own batch of homemade ghost pepper hot sauce and savor the heat in every drop. Remember, handle with care and enjoy the spicy journey!
