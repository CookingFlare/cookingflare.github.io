Peruvian Anticuchos with Grilled Beef Heart Recipe


If you’re looking to expand your culinary horizons and delve into the world of Peruvian cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this authentic Anticuchos recipe featuring grilled beef heart. Anticuchos are a beloved street food in Peru, traditionally made with marinated skewered meats, and they offer a tantalizing blend of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Lima. In this recipe, we’ll explore the rich history of Anticuchos and guide you through the process of preparing this mouthwatering dish at home.

The History of Anticuchos

Anticuchos have a fascinating history that dates back to pre-Columbian times when the Inca civilization roamed the Andean region. The dish has evolved over centuries, influenced by Spanish, African, and Asian culinary traditions, resulting in the unique fusion of flavors that characterizes Peruvian cuisine today. Originally, Anticuchos were made with llama or alpaca meat, but over time, beef heart has become the predominant choice, celebrated for its robust flavor and tender texture.

In Peru, Anticuchos are a popular street food, often sold by vendors grilling the marinated skewers over open flames, infusing the meat with a smoky, charred essence. They are commonly served with a side of boiled potatoes or corn, adding a comforting touch to the experience. The dish holds a special place in Peruvian culture, often enjoyed during festivals, celebrations, and casual gatherings, making it a cherished symbol of communal dining and culinary heritage.

Grilled Beef Heart Anticuchos Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef heart, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup red wine vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon aji panca paste (Peruvian red pepper paste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 8-10 skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the red wine vinegar, minced garlic, aji panca paste, ground cumin, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk in the vegetable oil until the marinade is well combined.

  2. Add the beef heart cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are fully coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and permeate the meat.

  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  4. Thread the marinated beef heart cubes onto the soaked skewers, dividing them evenly.

  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the meat is charred and cooked to your desired level of doneness.

  6. Once cooked, remove the skewers from the grill and allow them to rest for a few minutes.

  7. Serve the Anticuchos hot, accompanied by boiled potatoes, corn, or a refreshing salsa criolla for an authentic Peruvian dining experience.

Final Thoughts

Preparing Peruvian Anticuchos with grilled beef heart is a culinary adventure that will introduce you to the vibrant flavors and cultural heritage of Peru. Embrace the opportunity to savor this beloved street food in the comfort of your own home, and share the experience with friends and family to transport them to the bustling streets of Lima. With its tantalizing blend of spices, tender beef heart, and smoky aroma, Anticuchos are sure to become a cherished addition to your culinary repertoire, offering a taste of Peruvian tradition with every succulent bite.

So, fire up the grill, immerse yourself in the spirit of Peruvian cuisine, and embark on a flavorful journey with this delightful Anticuchos recipe.
