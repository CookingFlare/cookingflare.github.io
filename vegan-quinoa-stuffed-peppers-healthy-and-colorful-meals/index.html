Vegan Quinoa Stuffed Peppers: Healthy and Colorful Meals


If you’re looking for a vibrant and wholesome vegan dish that’s as delicious as it is nutritious, then look no further than these Vegan Quinoa Stuffed Peppers. Packed with protein-rich quinoa, an array of colorful vegetables, and an assortment of aromatic herbs and spices, these stuffed peppers are a feast for the eyes and the taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan cook or just venturing into the world of plant-based cuisine, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your meal rotation.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels, fresh or frozen
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

For the Avocado Cream Sauce:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water, as needed

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  2. Prepare the Peppers: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the peppers in a baking dish, cut side up.

  3. Make the Filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Stir in the black beans, diced tomatoes, corn, cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika. Cook for 5 minutes, then add the cooked quinoa and mix well. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Stuff the Peppers: Spoon the quinoa and vegetable mixture into the prepared bell peppers, packing it in tightly. Place the pepper tops back on, and cover the dish with foil.

  5. Bake: Bake the stuffed peppers for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.

  6. Make the Avocado Cream Sauce: While the peppers are baking, prepare the avocado cream sauce. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocados, cilantro, garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth, adding water as needed to achieve a creamy consistency.

  7. Serve: Once the peppers are done, remove them from the oven and uncover. Drizzle with the avocado cream sauce and garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley.

Nutritional Benefits

These Vegan Quinoa Stuffed Peppers are not only a visual delight but also a powerhouse of nutrition. Quinoa is a complete protein, providing all nine essential amino acids, while bell peppers are rich in vitamins A and C. The black beans add a good dose of fiber and plant-based protein, making this dish a well-rounded and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

With their vibrant colors, robust flavors, and wholesome ingredients, these Vegan Quinoa Stuffed Peppers are a celebration of plant-based eating. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, this dish is bound to impress. The combination of protein-packed quinoa, nutrient-rich vegetables, and zesty avocado cream sauce creates a culinary experience that’s as nourishing as it is delicious. So, next time you’re craving a healthy and colorful meal, give these stuffed peppers a try and savor the goodness of plant-based eating!
