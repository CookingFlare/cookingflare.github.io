Boozy Christmas Desserts: Recipes with a Kick


The holiday season is upon us, and with it comes the opportunity to indulge in some decadent treats that not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also provide a delightful hint of cheer. This year, why not elevate your Christmas dessert table with a selection of boozy desserts that will leave your guests buzzing with joy? From rich chocolate cakes to creamy trifles, these recipes are sure to impress and spread festive cheer. Let’s dive into the world of boozy Christmas desserts, where each recipe is infused with your favorite spirits, making every bite a celebration.

1. Eggnog Cheesecake with Rum Topping

Ingredients:

For the Cheesecake:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 (8-ounce) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup dark rum

For the Rum Topping:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons dark rum
  • Ground nutmeg for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust:

    • Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
    • In a bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until well combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes and then let it cool.

  2. Make the Cheesecake Filling:

    • In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add sugar and mix until combined.
    • Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then, incorporate eggnog, vanilla extract, nutmeg, cinnamon, and dark rum. Beat until smooth.

  3. Bake the Cheesecake:

    • Pour the cheesecake filling into the cooled crust. Bake for about 1 hour, or until the center is set but still slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven with the door slightly ajar for about an hour.

  4. Prepare the Rum Topping:

    • In a separate bowl, whip the cream until soft peaks form. Gradually add powdered sugar and dark rum, and continue to whip until stiff peaks form.

  5. Serve:

    • Spread the rum topping over the cooled cheesecake and dust with ground nutmeg. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.

2. Chocolate Bourbon Pecan Pie

Ingredients:

For the Pie Crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 4 to 5 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons bourbon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups pecan halves
  • ½ cup semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Make the Pie Crust:

    • In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water until the dough comes together. Shape into a ball, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Preheat Oven:

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    • On a floured surface, roll out the dough to fit a 9-inch pie pan. Place the dough into the pan and trim the edges.

  4. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a mixing bowl, whisk together corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, melted butter, bourbon, and vanilla extract until smooth. Stir in pecans and chocolate chips.

  5. Assemble and Bake:

    • Pour the filling into the prepared pie crust. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until set. Let cool before slicing.

3. Bourbon Chocolate Mousse Cups

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons bourbon
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate:

    • In a heatproof bowl, combine chocolate chips and butter. Melt over a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly.

  2. Whip Egg Whites:

    • In a clean bowl, beat egg whites with a pinch of salt until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.

  3. Combine Mixtures:

    • Stir egg yolks and bourbon into the melted chocolate. Gently fold in the whipped egg whites until combined.

  4. Whip Cream:

    • In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. Fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture until smooth and light.

  5. Chill and Serve:

    • Spoon the mousse into individual cups and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving. Garnish with chocolate shavings or a sprinkle of cocoa powder.

4. Spiced Rum Fruitcake

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed dried fruit (raisins, cranberries, apricots)
  • ½ cup chopped nuts (walnuts, pecans)
  • 1 cup spiced rum
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Soak the Fruit:

    • In a bowl, combine dried fruit and nuts. Pour spiced rum over the mixture and let it soak overnight.

  2. Preheat Oven:

    • Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Grease and line a loaf pan with parchment paper.

  3. Mix Dry Ingredients:

    • In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.

  4. Cream Butter and Sugar:

    • In a separate bowl, cream together butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time.

  5. Combine Mixtures:

    • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the soaked fruit and rum mixture. Stir until just combined.

  6. Bake:

    • Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 1 hour or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow to cool before slicing.

5. Red Wine Poached Pears

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe pears, peeled and cored
  • 1 bottle red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anise
  • Zest of 1 orange

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Poaching Liquid:

    • In a large saucepan, combine red wine, sugar, cinnamon stick, star anise, and orange zest. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.

  2. Poach the Pears:

    • Add the pears to the saucepan, ensuring they are submerged in the liquid. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender.

  3. Cool and Serve:

    • Remove the pears from the liquid and let them cool. You can reduce the remaining poaching liquid to a syrupy consistency if desired. Serve the pears drizzled with the syrup.

Conclusion

These boozy Christmas desserts are sure to add a festive flair to your holiday gatherings. Each recipe brings a unique blend of flavors, textures, and, of course, a delightful kick that will elevate your dessert game. So, gather your loved ones, pour a glass of your favorite spirit, and enjoy creating these unforgettable sweet treats. Whether you’re hosting a holiday party or simply indulging in some festive spirit, these desserts are a perfect way to celebrate the season. Happy holidays and happy baking!
