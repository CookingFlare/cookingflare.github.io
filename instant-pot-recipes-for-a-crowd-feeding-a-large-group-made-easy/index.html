Instant Pot Recipes for a Crowd: Feeding a Large Group Made Easy


When you’re hosting a gathering, feeding a large group can feel like a monumental task. From meal prep to managing cooking times, it can quickly become overwhelming. That’s where the Instant Pot shines. This multi-functional kitchen gadget is a lifesaver for busy hosts, enabling you to whip up large quantities of food with ease, all while saving time and energy. Whether it’s a casual family get-together, a holiday feast, or a game-day party, your Instant Pot can deliver delicious, crowd-pleasing meals without the hassle.

Below, you’ll find a variety of Instant Pot recipes that work perfectly for feeding a crowd. These dishes are hearty, flavorful, and easy to scale up or down based on your group size. And the best part? With the Instant Pot, you’ll spend less time glued to the kitchen and more time enjoying the company of your guests.

1. Pulled Pork for a Party

Pulled pork is a classic dish that’s perfect for feeding a large group. It’s versatile, pairs well with a variety of sides, and can be used for sandwiches, tacos, sliders, or even served on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 lbs pork shoulder (or pork butt)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cut the pork shoulder into large chunks to ensure it fits in the Instant Pot.
  2. In a small bowl, mix brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Rub the mixture all over the pork.
  3. Add the chicken broth to the Instant Pot and place the pork pieces inside.
  4. Close the lid, set the valve to “sealing,” and pressure cook on high for 60 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for 15 minutes, then quick release the remaining pressure.
  5. Shred the pork using two forks. Mix in BBQ sauce, and toss to coat evenly.
  6. Serve with buns, coleslaw, or tortillas, and watch it disappear in minutes!

2. Vegetarian Chili

Hearty, comforting, and packed with protein, vegetarian chili is a fantastic option for feeding vegetarians and meat-eaters alike. This recipe is simple to prepare and keeps guests satisfied.

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add a splash of olive oil, then sauté onion, garlic, and bell peppers for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add diced carrots, black beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes (with their juice), vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Close the lid, set the valve to “sealing,” and pressure cook on high for 10 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before quick-releasing any remaining pressure.
  4. Stir in corn kernels and let the chili sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve with toppings such as shredded cheese, sour cream, or fresh cilantro.

3. Creamy Chicken Alfredo Pasta

For a crowd that loves comfort food, this creamy chicken Alfredo pasta is a guaranteed hit. It’s rich, decadent, and comes together in one pot—no need to cook the pasta separately.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cubed
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 lb penne pasta
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add olive oil and cook the chicken cubes, seasoning with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the chicken is lightly browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Pour in chicken broth, scraping the bottom to deglaze the pot. Add uncooked pasta, ensuring it is evenly submerged.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot. Close the lid, set the valve to “sealing,” and pressure cook on high for 6 minutes. Quick release the pressure once cooking is completed.
  5. Stir in heavy cream and Parmesan cheese, mixing until the sauce is creamy and well combined. Let the pasta sit for a few minutes to thicken before serving.

4. Beef and Vegetable Stew

This classic beef stew is hearty, satisfying, and easily scaled up to serve a larger group. It’s perfect for cold-weather gatherings or any time you want a cozy one-pot meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch mixed with water for thickening

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode. Add a splash of olive oil, then brown the beef cubes in batches. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and mushrooms to the pot, sautéing for 2-3 minutes. Stir in tomato paste and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Deglaze the pot with a splash of beef broth, scraping the bottom to loosen any browned bits. Add the beef back in, followed by carrots, potatoes, remaining broth, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Close the lid, set the valve to “sealing,” and pressure cook on high for 35 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then quick release the rest.
  5. Stir in the cornstarch slurry to thicken the stew. Let it simmer on “Sauté” mode for a few minutes until thickened to your liking. Serve with crusty bread or over rice.

5. Massive Batch of Rice and Beans

Rice and beans are a budget-friendly and versatile dish that’s perfect for feeding a large group. Serve it as a main course or as a side to complement other dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 cup dried black beans (soaked overnight)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and add a bit of olive oil. Sauté onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add soaked black beans and rice to the pot, followed by broth, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Close the lid, set the valve to “sealing,” and pressure cook on high for 22 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick release the remaining pressure.
  4. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve with lime wedges, chopped cilantro, or a drizzle of hot sauce.

With these Instant Pot recipes, feeding a large group has never been simpler. From comforting stews to versatile dishes like pulled pork or rice and beans, the Instant Pot is your secret weapon for hosting stress-free gatherings. These recipes are easy, scalable, and sure to leave your guests raving about your cooking.
