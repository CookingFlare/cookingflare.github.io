Creamy and Dairy-Free Alfredo Sauce Recipe for Pasta Lovers

If you’re a pasta lover but have dietary restrictions that prevent you from enjoying traditional alfredo sauce, fear not! This creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce recipe is here to satisfy your cravings while accommodating your dietary needs. Made with simple ingredients and bursting with flavor, this sauce will take your pasta dishes to a whole new level. Ingredients 1 cup raw cashews, soaked in water for at least 4 hours 2 tablespoons olive oil 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup unsweetened almond milk 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Drain the soaked cashews and rinse them under cold water. In a blender, combine the cashews, olive oil, minced garlic, almond milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard. Blend until smooth and creamy. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and pour in the sauce from the blender. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool for a few minutes before serving. Pour the creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce over your favorite cooked pasta and toss until well coated. Serve hot and garnish with fresh parsley or basil for an extra burst of flavor. Tips and Variations To add some extra richness to the sauce, you can stir in 1/4 cup of coconut cream or a tablespoon of vegan butter during step 3. For a cheesy twist, sprinkle some dairy-free parmesan or grated vegan cheese on top before serving. If you prefer a thinner sauce, you can adjust the consistency by adding a little more almond milk. Feel free to get creative and customize this recipe to your liking. You can add sautéed mushrooms, roasted cherry tomatoes, or steamed broccoli to the sauce for added texture and flavor. The Benefits of Dairy-Free Alfredo Sauce Not only is this creamy and dairy-free alfredo sauce delicious, but it also offers a range of health benefits. By using cashews as the base, you’re incorporating heart-healthy fats and plant-based protein into your meal. Cashews are also a great source of minerals like magnesium and zinc, which are essential for overall well-being. ...
