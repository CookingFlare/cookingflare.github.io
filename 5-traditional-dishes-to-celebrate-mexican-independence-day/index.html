5 Traditional Dishes to Celebrate Mexican Independence Day


Mexican Independence Day is a joyous occasion that commemorates the country's struggle for freedom and its rich history. Each year on September 16th, Mexicans all over the world gather to celebrate this special day with an array of delectable food, lively music, and vibrant festivities. In this post, we'll delve into five traditional Mexican dishes that are staples of the country's culinary heritage, making them perfect additions to your Independence Day celebration.

  1. Chiles Rellenos This beloved national dish dates back to the pre-Hispanic era, with its name 'chiles rellenos' literally translating to 'stuffed peppers.' The dish consists of roasted poblano peppers stuffed with cheese, battered, and fried to perfection. To make this iconic recipe, start by selecting four poblano peppers and scorching them over an open flame until their skin is blistered and charred. Once the peppers have cooled, peel them, remove the seeds, and fill each with shredded queso fresco. Cut the cheese carefully so as not to pierce the inner walls of the pepper, as the cheese serves as a crucial part of the exterior. Then: Ingredients:

4 poblano peppers 1 cup queso fresco, crumbled 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 cup milk Vegetable oil for frying

Dip each of the stuffed poblano peppers into the prepared batter, thoroughly covering both the stuffed part and the exterior walls of the peppers. Carefully place the battered peppers in deep oil and fry until the exterior becomes golden brown. Once cooked, carefully place the chiles rellenos on a plate, topping them off with a drizzle of your preferred sauce, be it enchilada sauce, salsa, or Salsa roja (Red Salsa).
