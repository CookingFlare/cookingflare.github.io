Mouthwatering Easter Ham: A Must-Try Recipe for a Memorable Holiday


Easter is a time of celebration, and what better way to gather your loved ones than with a mouthwatering Easter ham? This classic dish is a centerpiece that is sure to impress, and with our must-try recipe, you’ll be able to create a memorable holiday feast. So, let’s dive right in and discover the secrets to making the perfect Easter ham.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8-10 pound) bone-in ham
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Place the ham on a large roasting pan, fat side up.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, honey, cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  3. Using a sharp knife, score the fat on the ham in a diamond pattern. Be careful not to cut too deep, as you want to keep the ham intact.

  4. Pour half of the glaze mixture over the ham, making sure to spread it evenly. Reserve the remaining glaze for later use.

  5. Cover the roasting pan with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for approximately 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).

  6. Every 30 minutes, baste the ham with the pan drippings. This will help to keep the ham moist and flavorful.

  7. During the last 15 minutes of baking, remove the foil and brush the remaining glaze over the ham. This will give it a beautiful, caramelized finish.

  8. Once the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C), remove the ham from the oven and let it rest for 15-20 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in a tender and juicy ham.

  9. Carve the ham into thin slices and serve with your favorite Easter side dishes. Whether it’s creamy mashed potatoes, buttery green beans, or fluffy dinner rolls, this mouthwatering Easter ham will be the star of the show.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can marinate the ham overnight in the glaze mixture. This will infuse the meat with a sweet and tangy taste.

  • If you prefer a spicier ham, you can add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a few dashes of hot sauce to the glaze mixture.

  • Leftover ham can be used in a variety of dishes, such as sandwiches, omelets, or casseroles. Its versatility makes it a great ingredient to have on hand for post-Easter meals.

  • If you’re hosting a larger gathering, you can easily double the recipe and use a larger ham. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly.

Conclusion

Easter is a time to come together and celebrate with family and friends. And what better way to make the occasion memorable than with a mouthwatering Easter ham? This must-try recipe will guide you through the process of creating a succulent and flavorful ham that will impress everyone at the table. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let the cooking begin. Happy Easter and happy feasting!
