Garnishing with Fresh and Dried Kiwi


When it comes to garnishing your culinary creations, the vibrant and refreshing flavor of kiwi can be a delightful addition. Whether you’re looking to add a pop of color to a dessert or a hint of tanginess to a savory dish, fresh and dried kiwi can elevate the visual appeal and taste of your dishes. In this blog post, we’ll explore creative ways to incorporate fresh and dried kiwi into your garnishing repertoire.

Fresh Kiwi Garnishes

Kiwi Flower Slices

Fresh kiwi slices can be transformed into beautiful and intricate flower shapes, adding an elegant touch to your desserts. To create kiwi flower slices, start by peeling a ripe kiwi and slicing it into thin rounds. Then, use a small flower-shaped cookie cutter to cut out the slices. These delicate kiwi flowers can be used to adorn cakes, tarts, or fruit platters, adding a burst of color and a hint of sweetness.

Kiwi Ribbons

For a more whimsical and playful garnish, consider creating kiwi ribbons. Using a vegetable peeler, carefully peel the skin off a kiwi, creating long, thin ribbons. These kiwi ribbons can be curled and twisted to add a fun and dynamic element to your dishes. Try weaving them into a salad, draping them over a cocktail, or using them to decorate a platter of hors d’oeuvres for a visually stunning presentation.

Kiwi Spears

If you’re looking for a simple yet effective way to add a refreshing touch to your beverages, kiwi spears are an excellent choice. Cut a kiwi into long, slender spears and use them to garnish cocktails, mocktails, or fruit-infused water. The vibrant green color and juicy texture of fresh kiwi spears can instantly elevate the visual appeal of any drink, making it an eye-catching addition to your beverage service.

Dried Kiwi Garnishes

Kiwi Chips

Dried kiwi chips are a versatile and shelf-stable garnish that can be used in a variety of dishes. To make kiwi chips, slice fresh kiwi into thin rounds and lay them out on a baking sheet. Bake them at a low temperature until they are dried and slightly crispy. These kiwi chips can be used to add a sweet and tangy crunch to salads, yogurt parfaits, or even savory dishes like roasted chicken or grilled fish.

Kiwi Powder

For a unique and flavorful garnish, consider making kiwi powder. Dehydrated kiwi slices can be ground into a fine powder using a blender or food processor. This vibrant green powder can be sprinkled over desserts, smoothie bowls, or savory dishes to add a burst of kiwi flavor and a striking visual accent. Kiwi powder is a creative way to infuse dishes with the essence of kiwi while also adding a touch of whimsy to the presentation.

Kiwi Ribbons

Similar to fresh kiwi ribbons, dried kiwi ribbons can be made using a vegetable peeler to create long, thin strips of dried kiwi. These chewy and intensely flavored ribbons can be used to adorn cakes, ice cream sundaes, or cheese platters, adding a concentrated burst of kiwi flavor and a playful texture to your culinary creations.

Incorporating fresh and dried kiwi into your garnishing repertoire can open up a world of creative possibilities in the kitchen. Whether you’re aiming to add a touch of elegance to a dessert or a surprising twist to a savory dish, kiwi garnishes can bring color, flavor, and visual appeal to your culinary creations. Experiment with these ideas and let the vibrant essence of kiwi inspire your next garnishing adventure!
