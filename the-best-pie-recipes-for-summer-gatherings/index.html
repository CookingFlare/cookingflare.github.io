The Best Pie Recipes for Summer Gatherings


Summer gatherings are the perfect occasion to indulge in delicious, refreshing desserts, and there’s no better way to impress guests than with a homemade pie. Whether it’s a backyard barbecue, a family reunion, or a casual get-together, pies have a magical ability to bring people together. The buttery crusts, fresh fruit fillings, and the sweet aromas drifting through the air are the hallmarks of a successful summer event. Below, we’ll dive into some of the best pie recipes that are sure to be a hit at your next summer gathering. These recipes offer a blend of classic favorites and refreshing twists that will make your summer pies the star of the show.

1. Classic Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

Few pies are as beloved during the summer as strawberry rhubarb pie. This tart and sweet combination is a seasonal favorite that’s guaranteed to bring smiles to every face. The key to a perfect strawberry rhubarb pie lies in the balance of the sweet strawberries and the tart rhubarb, creating a harmonious flavor that is neither too sweet nor too sour.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 ¼ cups sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 batch of pie dough (for a 9-inch pie)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rhubarb, strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Stir to coat the fruit evenly.
  3. Roll out the bottom pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie pan. Pour the fruit filling into the crust.
  4. Roll out the top crust and place it over the pie, crimping the edges to seal. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  5. Brush the top crust with a beaten egg for a golden, glossy finish.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  7. Let the pie cool completely before serving. Enjoy the sweet and tart flavors of this classic summer pie!

2. Lemon Meringue Pie

Lemon meringue pie is a showstopper at any summer gathering. The tangy lemon filling paired with the sweet, fluffy meringue creates a refreshing contrast that perfectly complements a warm summer day. This pie is ideal for those who love a citrusy bite and a light, airy dessert.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, chilled
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • Pinch of salt

For the lemon filling:

  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

For the meringue:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Start by making the pie crust. In a food processor, pulse the flour, salt, and butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and bake for 10-12 minutes until golden. Remove from the oven and let it cool.
  4. For the lemon filling, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and water in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and begins to boil. In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Gradually pour a small amount of the hot mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Slowly return the egg mixture to the saucepan while continuing to whisk. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Remove the filling from heat and stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter until smooth.
  6. Pour the lemon filling into the cooled pie crust.
  7. For the meringue, beat the egg whites, vanilla extract, and cream of tartar with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. Gradually add the sugar and continue to beat until stiff peaks form.
  8. Spread the meringue over the lemon filling, making sure to seal the edges.
  9. Bake the pie for 10-15 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown.
  10. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving. The creamy lemon filling paired with the sweet meringue is sure to be a crowd-pleaser!

3. Peach Pie with a Cinnamon Streusel Topping

Peach pie is a quintessential summer dessert, especially when peaches are in season. The natural sweetness of ripe peaches, combined with a hint of cinnamon and a crumbly streusel topping, creates a dessert that’s both comforting and refreshing. This pie is perfect for those who enjoy a bit of spice with their fruity pies.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, cold
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • Pinch of salt

For the filling:

  • 5 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

For the streusel topping:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup packed brown sugar
  • ¼ cup rolled oats
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the pie crust by combining the flour, salt, and butter in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the cold water until the dough comes together. Refrigerate the dough for at least 30 minutes before rolling it out and fitting it into a 9-inch pie pan.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Stir to coat the peaches evenly with the mixture.
  4. Pour the peach filling into the prepared pie crust.
  5. To make the streusel topping, combine the flour, brown sugar, oats, cinnamon, and butter in a bowl. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to blend the ingredients until the mixture becomes crumbly.
  6. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the peach filling.
  7. Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is bubbling and the topping is golden brown.
  8. Let the pie cool before serving. This pie is perfect when served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top.

4. Key Lime Pie

For those who love a tart, citrusy dessert, key lime pie is the ultimate summer treat. The creamy, tangy filling sits on a buttery graham cracker crust, offering the perfect balance of sweet and sour. This easy-to-make pie will add a zesty twist to your summer gathering.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar

For the filling:

  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup fresh key lime juice (or regular lime juice)
  • Zest of 2 limes

For the topping:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. To make the crust, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar in a bowl. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch pie pan and bake for 8-10 minutes, until golden. Let the crust cool.
  3. For the filling, whisk the egg yolks in a bowl. Stir in the sweetened condensed milk, lime juice, and lime zest. Pour the filling into the cooled crust.
  4. Bake the pie for 15 minutes or until the filling is set. Let it cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 3 hours to chill.
  5. For the topping, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a bowl until stiff peaks form. Spread the whipped cream over the chilled pie.
  6. Slice and serve the refreshing key lime pie, garnished with additional lime zest if desired.

5. Blueberry Pie

Blueberry pie is a timeless classic that captures the essence of summer in every bite. Bursting with juicy blueberries and paired with a flaky, buttery crust, this pie is sure to please fruit lovers and dessert aficionados alike.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh blueberries
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 batch of pie dough (for a 9-inch pie)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, cinnamon, and salt. Stir gently to coat the berries with the mixture.
  3. Roll out the pie dough and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Pour the blueberry filling into the crust.
  4. Roll out the second batch of dough and place it on top of the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal, and cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Allow the pie to cool before serving. Fresh blueberries paired with a flaky crust create a perfect summer pie!

Conclusion

Summer gatherings are the perfect opportunity to showcase your baking skills with delicious, refreshing pies that everyone will enjoy. Whether you prefer the tangy kick of lemon meringue, the comforting flavors of peach pie, or the sweet and tart combination of strawberry rhubarb, these pie recipes will undoubtedly elevate your summer festivities. Don’t forget to pair them with a scoop of ice cream, fresh whipped cream, or even a dollop of yogurt to add an extra touch of indulgence. No matter which pie you choose, you’re sure to create memories that will last long after the last bite.
