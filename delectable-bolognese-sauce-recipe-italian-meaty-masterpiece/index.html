Delectable Bolognese Sauce Recipe - Italian Meaty Masterpiece


If there’s one dish that perfectly embodies the rich and comforting flavors of Italian cuisine, it’s the classic Bolognese sauce. This meaty masterpiece is a staple in many Italian households, and for good reason. The combination of tender ground meat, aromatic vegetables, and savory spices creates a symphony of flavors that will delight your taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this Delectable Bolognese Sauce Recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (28 ounces)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and pork, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, and cook until browned. Remove the meat from the skillet and set aside.

  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and the onions are translucent, about 5 minutes.

  3. Return the cooked meat to the skillet and stir to combine with the vegetables. Pour in the red wine and simmer for a few minutes, allowing the alcohol to cook off.

  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer you let it simmer, the richer the flavors will become.

  6. About 30 minutes before serving, pour in the whole milk and stir into the sauce. This will add a creamy richness and balance out the acidity of the tomatoes.

  7. Cook your favorite pasta according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta and toss it with a ladleful of the Bolognese sauce. Serve the pasta in bowls, topped with more sauce and a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese.

Tips and Variations

  • For a lighter version, you can use ground turkey or chicken instead of beef and pork.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, substitute the ground meat with finely chopped mushrooms or cooked lentils.
  • You can customize the sauce by adding a splash of Worcestershire sauce or a pinch of red pepper flakes for a kick of heat.
  • Leftover Bolognese sauce can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for future use. It tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together.

Final Thoughts

The Delectable Bolognese Sauce Recipe is a true Italian culinary gem that will transport you to the streets of Bologna with every bite. The combination of aromatic vegetables, savory meats, and fragrant spices creates a sauce that is both hearty and comforting. Whether served over pasta or used as a base for lasagna, this Bolognese sauce is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So grab your apron, fire up the stove, and get ready to indulge in this Italian meaty masterpiece. Buon appetito!
