How to Make a Fruit Galette: Simple and Delicious


There are few desserts as charmingly rustic and versatile as a fruit galette. Its freeform shape, golden crust, and luscious fruit filling make it the perfect treat for dinner parties, casual gatherings, or simply indulging at home. While a fruit galette may look impressive, it’s surprisingly straightforward to prepare. With just a handful of ingredients and a few easy steps, you can create a mouthwatering dessert that highlights the natural sweetness of fresh fruits.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through creating a delightful fruit galette from scratch. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a complete beginner, this recipe will show you that delicious results don’t require complicated techniques. Let’s dive right into it.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a perfect fruit galette, you’ll need two main components: the crust and the filling. Here’s what you’ll need for each:

For the Galette Crust

  • All-purpose flour: 2 ½ cups, plus extra for rolling
  • Granulated sugar: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: ½ teaspoon
  • Unsalted butter: 1 cup (cold, diced into small cubes)
  • Ice water: 6–8 tablespoons
  • Optional: Egg wash (made from 1 egg whisked with 1 tablespoon water) for a golden crust

For the Fruit Filling

  • Fresh fruit: 4–6 cups (berries, stone fruits, apples, pears—choose your favorite or mix them)
  • Granulated sugar: ⅓ cup (more if your fruits are tart)
  • Cornstarch: 2 tablespoons (to thicken the juices)
  • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Vanilla extract: 1 teaspoon
  • Optional: Ground cinnamon or nutmeg (for added warmth)
  • Optional: Additional granulated sugar for sprinkling on the crust before baking

Step 1: Make the Crust

The crust is the foundation of a galette, providing a flaky, buttery counterpoint to the vibrant fruit filling.

  1. Combine dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt.

  2. Add butter: Toss the cold butter cubes into the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. It’s crucial that the butter remains cold to create a flaky texture in the baked crust.

  3. Incorporate ice water: Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough starts to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough—stop mixing as soon as it holds together in a ball.

  4. Chill the dough: Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least one hour. This allows the butter to firm up and the gluten in the flour to relax, ensuring a tender crust.

Step 2: Prepare the Fruit Filling

While your dough chills, you can prepare the fruit filling.

  1. Wash and slice the fruit: Make sure your fruit is fresh and ripe. Wash thoroughly, peel if necessary, and slice or chop into evenly sized pieces. For delicate berries, simply hull them if needed.

  2. Toss with sugar and spices: In a mixing bowl, combine the cut fruit, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and any optional spices. The sugar enhances the fruit’s sweetness while the cornstarch thickens the juices during baking. Stir gently to coat the fruit evenly without breaking it apart.

Step 3: Assemble the Galette

Once your dough has chilled and filling is ready, it’s time to assemble the galette. This is the most fun and creative part of the process!

  1. Roll out the dough: Lightly flour a clean surface and roll the dough into a rough circle about 12–14 inches in diameter. Make sure the circle isn’t too thin to prevent tears. Transfer it to a parchment-lined baking sheet.

  2. Add the fruit filling: Spoon the prepared fruit mixture onto the center of the dough, leaving a 2–3 inch border around the edges.

  3. Fold the edges: Gently fold the dough edges over the fruit, pleating as you go to create a rustic crust. The folds don’t need to be perfect—the charm of a galette lies in its freeform shape.

  4. Brush with egg wash: If you’re using egg wash, brush it onto the crust edges to encourage browning. Sprinkle a little sugar over the crust for added sweetness and crunch.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

  1. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C). Allow it to fully preheat before baking.

  2. Bake the galette: Place the baking sheet with your galette into the oven and bake for 40–50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the fruit filling is bubbling.

  3. Cool before serving: Remove the galette from the oven and let it cool for about 10–15 minutes. This allows the juices to set, making it easier to slice and serve.

Serving Ideas

A fruit galette is wonderful on its own, but it can be elevated with a few additions:

  • Whipped cream: A dollop of lightly sweetened whipped cream ensures you have a creamy contrast to the flaky crust.
  • Ice cream: Serve warm slices with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an indulgent pairing.
  • Yogurt or crème fraîche: These tangy options beautifully complement the tartness of the fruit.

Tips for Success

To make your fruit galette even better, keep these expert tips in mind:

  1. Use seasonal fruit: Fruits that are in season are juicier, sweeter, and fresher. Experiment with combinations like berries in summer, apples in fall, or pears and cranberries in winter.

  2. Prevent soggy crust: Sprinkle a thin layer of breadcrumbs, crushed nuts, or even a powdering of sugar directly on the rolled-out dough before adding the fruit. This helps absorb excess juices.

  3. Don’t overfill: While tempting, resist adding too much filling. A moderate amount ensures the galette maintains its shape and doesn’t leak excessively during baking.

  4. Make ahead: The dough can be prepared a day in advance and kept in the fridge. You can also freeze the dough for up to a month.

Why Fruit Galettes Are Perfect for Any Occasion

A fruit galette strikes the perfect balance between elegance and simplicity. It showcases the natural beauty and flavor of fresh fruit rather than hiding it under heavy toppings. The buttery crust provides a rich, flaky backdrop, while the juicy filling steals the show with its vibrant, satisfying taste. It’s the kind of dessert that feels both effortless and luxurious.

Whether you’re serving it at a holiday gathering or enjoying it fresh out of the oven with a cup of coffee, this galette recipe will surely become a cherished addition to your repertoire. Its rustic charm makes it inviting, while its fresh ingredients ensure it’s as wholesome as it is delicious.

So roll up your sleeves and give this recipe a try. You’ll quickly discover that once you’ve mastered the fruit galette, the possibilities are endless. Happy baking!
