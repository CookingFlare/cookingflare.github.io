Springtime BBQ Recipes: Grilled Goodness for the Season


As the days grow longer and the air carries the sweetness of blooming flowers, spring beckons us outside. This season is more than just a transition between winter’s chill and summer’s heat—it’s an invitation to fire up the grill and celebrate the arrival of warmer weather with family and friends. Whether you’re a barbecue novice or an experienced grill master, this collection of spring-inspired BBQ recipes is sure to bring fresh flavors and vibrant colors to your outdoor table.

1. Lemon-Herb Grilled Chicken Thighs

Spring epitomizes freshness, and there’s no better way to celebrate the season than with bright, zesty flavors. Lemon-herb grilled chicken thighs are juicy, aromatic, and perfect for any BBQ gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 lemons (zested and juiced)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme (or ½ tsp dried)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the chicken thighs in a resealable plastic bag or large bowl. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is coated. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight for deeper flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium heat. Oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill for approximately 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  5. Serve hot with a spring salad or roasted seasonal vegetables.

The lemon-herb marinade not only infuses the chicken with flavor but also keeps it tender and juicy. Paired with fresh greens, this dish embodies the essence of spring.

2. Grilled Asparagus and Prosciutto Skewers

Asparagus is a quintessential spring vegetable, and grilling enhances its natural sweetness. Combine it with salty prosciutto for an elegant and savory appetizer or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh asparagus (trimmed)
  • 10 slices prosciutto (cut in half lengthwise)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes if using.
  2. Wrap each asparagus spear with a strip of prosciutto.
  3. Thread 4-5 wrapped spears onto each skewer, leaving slight space between them.
  4. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Brush the skewers with this mixture.
  5. Heat the grill to medium-high. Place skewers directly on the grill grates and cook for 4-5 minutes per side or until the asparagus is tender and slightly charred.
  6. Serve immediately.

These skewers are not only visually appealing but also burst with the rich combination of smoky, salty, and sweet flavors. They’ll disappear as fast as you can make them!

3. Honey-Mustard Grilled Salmon

Salmon is another fantastic choice for spring BBQs. Its rich, buttery texture pairs wonderfully with the tanginess of honey and mustard, creating a light and satisfying dish that’s as healthy as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (skin-on)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, lemon juice, smoked paprika, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels and brush the glaze generously over the top.
  3. Heat the grill to medium and lightly oil the grates.
  4. Place the salmon fillets skin-side down on the grill. Cook for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets, until the flesh flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve with grilled asparagus, steamed rice, or a citrusy couscous salad.

This honey-mustard marinade balances sweet and tangy flavors with a hint of smokiness, making it a crowd-pleaser for your springtime gatherings.

4. Smoky Grilled Corn with Herb Butter

No BBQ is complete without corn on the cob sizzling on the grill. Upgrade this classic side with a smoky char and herb-infused butter for a fresh, spring-ready twist.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn (husked)
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 tsp fresh chives (chopped)
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high.
  2. Mix softened butter with parsley, chives, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Set aside.
  3. Carefully brush each ear of corn with a light coating of olive oil or melted butter.
  4. Place the corn directly on the grill grates and cook, turning occasionally, until charred on all sides (about 10-12 minutes).
  5. Remove the corn from the grill and spread the herb butter generously over each cob while still warm. Serve immediately.

The smoky char enhances the natural sweetness of the corn, while the herb butter elevates it into a more sophisticated dish perfect for springtime tables.

5. Grilled Pineapple and Pound Cake Dessert Skewers

Finish off your BBQ menu with a showstopping dessert that’s simple, fresh, and incredibly satisfying. Grilled pineapple pairs beautifully with buttery pound cake for a unique sweet treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fresh pineapple (peeled, cored, and cut into chunks)
  • 1 store-bought pound cake (cut into cubes)
  • ¼ cup melted butter
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Alternate threading pineapple chunks and pound cake cubes onto the skewers.
  3. Mix melted butter with cinnamon and honey. Brush this mixture over the skewers.
  4. Heat the grill to medium. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the pineapple starts to caramelize and the pound cake is lightly toasted.
  5. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, if desired.

These dessert skewers capture the spirit of spring with their bright pineapple flavor and comforting, soft pound cake. It’s a crowd-pleasing, low-stress dessert that wraps up any BBQ perfectly.

Tips for a Successful Spring BBQ

  1. Choose Fresh, Seasonal Ingredients: Spring offers a bounty of fresh produce like asparagus, peas, radishes, strawberries, and herbs. Incorporating these seasonal gems into your dishes ensures vibrant flavors.

  2. Prep Ahead of Time: To make grilling stress-free, marinate meats, prepare skewers, and create sauces or dips in advance.

  3. Keep the Grill Clean and Maintained: A properly cleaned grill not only extends its life but also ensures your food tastes its best without unwanted smokiness from leftover char.

  4. Optimize Flavors with Fresh Herbs: Herbs like rosemary, thyme, dill, and parsley add brightness to any dish and should be used abundantly in spring BBQ recipes.

  5. Pair with Refreshing Drinks: Serve spring-inspired beverages, such as lemonade, iced tea, or cucumber-mint coolers, to complement your grilling creations.

This spring, let your BBQ become more than just a meal—make it a celebration of the season. From the savory smokiness of grilled meats to the sweet surprise of pineapple skewers, these recipes bring nature’s freshness to your table. With a little creativity and some time spent outdoors, you can create flavor-filled memories to welcome spring with open arms and hungry appetites. Happy grilling!
