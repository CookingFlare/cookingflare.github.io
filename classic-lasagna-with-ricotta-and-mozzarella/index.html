Classic Lasagna with Ricotta and Mozzarella


Few dishes evoke the warm comfort of home and tradition quite like a classic lasagna. Layers of tender pasta, rich tomato sauce, creamy ricotta, and melting mozzarella come together in a symphony of flavor that’s both hearty and inviting. Whether you’re preparing a cozy family dinner or impressing guests at a gathering, this lasagna recipe is a timeless choice that never fails to delight.

Why This Classic Lasagna Stands Out

Lasagna recipes abound, but the charm of the classic ricotta and mozzarella version lies in its simplicity and balance. Each ingredient plays a crucial role, creating layers that speak to Italian roots without overwhelming your palate. The combination of creamy ricotta with gooey mozzarella cheese provides a luscious texture contrast, while the meat-infused tomato sauce adds depth and savoriness.

This recipe is designed to be approachable yet authentic, using quality ingredients and traditional techniques. The result is a lasagna that’s both richly satisfying and elegantly familiar.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s a comprehensive list to gather before you begin:

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably 80/20 for flavor)
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage (casings removed)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 28 ounces canned crushed tomatoes
  • 6 ounces tomato paste
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped (or 2 teaspoons dried)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a gentle kick)

For the Cheese Mixture:

  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese (whole milk ricotta for creamier texture)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Other Essentials:

  • 9 to 12 no-boil lasagna noodles (or traditional noodles boiled until al dente)
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Additional grated Parmesan for topping

Step-by-Step Guide to the Perfect Classic Lasagna

1. Preparing the Robust Meat Sauce

Start by heating a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add your ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon as it browns. Once the meat is cooked through and no longer pink, drain excess fat to keep the sauce from becoming greasy. This step is crucial for a clean, rich flavor.

Add the finely chopped onion and garlic to the pan. Sauté until the onions become translucent and fragrant, about 4-5 minutes. Then, stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce, basil, oregano, sugar, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.

Reduce heat to low and let the sauce simmer gently for 30 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. This slow simmer allows the flavors to meld beautifully, concentrating the sauce without becoming overly thick.

2. Crafting the Creamy Ricotta Filling

While your sauce simmers, mix the ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, parsley, salt, and pepper in a bowl. The egg acts as a binder, helping the cheese mixture hold its shape within the lasagna layers. Fresh parsley adds a bright herbal note, elevating the richness of the cheese. Adjust seasoning if needed.

3. Preparing the Pasta Noodles

If you have chosen no-boil noodles, there’s no need for pre-cooking—these noodles soften perfectly during baking. For traditional dried pasta, boil according to the package instructions until al dente, then drain and set aside on a lightly oiled surface to prevent sticking.

4. Assembling the Layers

Now comes the satisfying part: building your lasagna. Use a 9x13-inch baking dish for ideal layer thickness. Begin by spreading a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom—this prevents sticking and adds moisture.

Next, lay down 3 to 4 noodles side by side to cover the sauce completely. Spread one-third of your ricotta cheese mixture evenly over the noodles, followed by a generous spoonful of meat sauce. Sprinkle one cup of shredded mozzarella over the sauce layer. Repeat this layering process two more times: noodles, ricotta, meat sauce, mozzarella.

Finish with a final layer of noodles and the remaining meat sauce. Top heavily with the remaining mozzarella and a light sprinkle of grated Parmesan for a golden finish.

5. Baking to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cover the lasagna tightly with aluminum foil. To prevent the cheese from sticking to the foil during baking, spray the underside of the foil lightly with cooking spray or tent it carefully above the edges of the dish.

Bake covered for 25 minutes. Then remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

For extra browning, switch to the broil setting and watch carefully for 2-3 minutes.

6. Resting Before Serving

Lasagna is tempting fresh out of the oven, but allowing it to rest for at least 15-20 minutes is vital. This resting period helps the layers set, making slicing cleaner and letting the flavors meld further. Serve warm, paired with a crisp green salad or garlic bread.

Tips to Elevate Your Classic Lasagna

  • Quality Cheese: Using whole milk ricotta creates creaminess that’s far superior to low-fat varieties. For mozzarella, opt for fresh shredded cheese rather than pre-shredded kinds with anti-caking agents.

  • Fresh Herbs: Adding fresh basil and parsley brightens the dish and contributes authentic Italian flavor.

  • Sauce Variations: Swap ground beef and sausage for ground turkey or mushrooms for a lighter or vegetarian take.

  • Make Ahead: Lasagna can be assembled a day in advance and refrigerated before baking. This allows flavors to deepen and simplifies mealtime.

  • Freezing: This dish freezes spectacularly. After baking and fully cooled, cut into portions and freeze wrapped tightly. Reheat in the oven covered until warmed through.

The Perfect Pairings

Lasagna begs for simple, complementary sides:

  • Salads: A crisp Caesar or arugula salad with lemon vinaigrette cuts through the richness.

  • Bread: Classic garlic bread or a rustic baguette helps scoop up every last bit of sauce.

  • Wine: Medium-bodied reds like Chianti or Sangiovese echo the tomato and herb notes perfectly.

  • Desserts: Finish with a light dessert such as a lemon sorbet or fresh berries to balance the hearty feast.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make lasagna vegetarian?

Absolutely! Replace meats with sautéed vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, spinach, or eggplant. You can also add lentils or plant-based meat substitutes for protein.

What if I can’t find no-boil noodles?

Regular dried lasagna noodles work perfectly fine, just boil until al dente before assembling. Be sure to drain well to prevent watery layers.

How do I prevent the lasagna from being soggy?

Draining the cooked meat well, simmering your sauce to thicken, and not over-saucing the layers all help prevent sogginess.

Can I use different cheeses?

You can experiment with provolone or fontina for the mozzarella layer, or add a layer of shredded Asiago or Pecorino Romano for an intensified flavor.

Classic lasagna with ricotta and mozzarella embodies the essence of comforting Italian cooking. It’s a dish that unites family and friends around the table, bringing joy with every savory bite. This recipe prioritizes authentic flavor, straightforward techniques, and adaptability, making it ideal for both new cooks and seasoned chefs alike. Gather your ingredients, don your apron, and prepare to savor the magic of a homemade lasagna masterpiece. Buon appetito!
