Savory Spinach and Artichoke Tart: Perfect for Entertaining


If you’re looking for a show-stopping dish that’s perfect for entertaining, look no further than this savory spinach and artichoke tart. This delightful tart is a beautiful combination of flaky pastry, creamy spinach and artichoke filling, and a hint of aromatic herbs. It’s a versatile dish that can be served as an appetizer, a main course, or even as part of a brunch spread. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, a family gathering, or simply want to impress your loved ones, this tart is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups canned artichoke hearts, chopped
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Making the Pastry:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, a few tablespoons at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). On a floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle large enough to line a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough to the tart pan, pressing it gently into the corners and trimming any excess dough.

  4. Line the pastry with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Blind bake the pastry for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Set aside to cool.

Making the Filling:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant.

  2. Add the chopped spinach and artichoke hearts to the skillet, and cook until the spinach is wilted and the artichokes are heated through. Remove from the heat and let the mixture cool slightly.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the Parmesan cheese, cream cheese, sour cream, and dried thyme. Add the cooled spinach and artichoke mixture, and stir until everything is well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assembling and Baking the Tart:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the spinach and artichoke filling evenly into the pre-baked tart shell.

  2. Place the tart in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.

  3. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.

Serving Suggestions

This savory spinach and artichoke tart is delicious both warm and at room temperature, making it a versatile dish for entertaining. You can serve it as an appetizer, alongside a fresh green salad for a light meal, or as part of a brunch spread with some fresh fruit and mimosas. It pairs beautifully with a crisp white wine or a refreshing iced tea.

Final Thoughts

This savory spinach and artichoke tart is a delightful dish that’s perfect for entertaining. With its flaky pastry, creamy filling, and flavorful combination of spinach and artichoke, it’s sure to impress your guests and leave them coming back for more. Whether you’re hosting a special occasion or simply want to elevate your everyday meals, this tart is a wonderful addition to any menu. So, roll up your sleeves, get creative in the kitchen, and treat your loved ones to this delectable savory tart!
