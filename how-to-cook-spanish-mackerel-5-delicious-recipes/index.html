How to Cook Spanish Mackerel: 5 Delicious Recipes


Spanish mackerel, with its sleek silver body and tender, flavorful flesh, is a favorite among seafood lovers. This oily fish is rich in omega-3 fatty acids and has a distinct taste that can stand up to bold spices and marinades, or be enjoyed simply grilled with a touch of lemon. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just diving into seafood recipes, learning how to properly prepare Spanish mackerel can open up a world of culinary possibilities.

In this post, we’ll explore five mouthwatering recipes that showcase the versatility of Spanish mackerel. From spicy marinades to subtle herb infusions, these recipes are crafted to bring out the best flavors in this delicious fish.

What Makes Spanish Mackerel Special?

Before diving into the recipes, it’s helpful to understand the characteristics of Spanish mackerel. This fish has firm, flaky flesh with moderate oil content, making it ideal for grilling, baking, pan-searing, and even smoking. Its texture holds up well during cooking, and it readily absorbs marinades, allowing for diverse preparations.

Purchasing and Preparing Spanish Mackerel

  • Freshness Matters: Look for fish with bright, clear eyes and firm flesh. If buying fillets, ensure they have a fresh sea aroma and a lustrous appearance.
  • Cleaning: If you buy whole fish, clean the Spanish mackerel by gutting and removing scales. For fillets, rinse under cold water and pat dry.
  • Handling: Use a sharp knife to retain the integrity of the fillet. Spanish mackerel’s skin is edible and crisps nicely when cooked, so keep it on for pan-searing or grilling.

Recipe 1: Grilled Spanish Mackerel with Citrus Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 Spanish mackerel fillets (6-8 oz each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Marinade: In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, orange zest, garlic, parsley, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the Fish: Place the mackerel fillets in a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the fish, ensuring even coating. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the Grill: Set your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Cook the Fish: Place the fillets skin-side down on the grill. Cook for about 4-5 minutes per side or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve: Garnish with additional fresh herbs and lemon wedges.

This recipe enhances the natural richness of Spanish mackerel with zesty citrus and fragrant herbs, perfect for a summer cookout.

Recipe 2: Spanish Mackerel Curry with Coconut Milk

Ingredients:

  • 4 Spanish mackerel fillets
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • Salt and chili flakes to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add Spices: Stir in curry powder and turmeric, cooking for another minute.
  3. Add Liquids: Pour in coconut milk and diced tomatoes. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Cook the Fish: Gently add the Spanish mackerel fillets into the curry sauce. Cover and let simmer for 10-12 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  5. Season: Add salt and chili flakes according to your taste.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle with fresh cilantro. Serve with steamed rice or warm naan bread.

The warmth of curry spices pairs beautifully with the oily texture of Spanish mackerel, creating a comforting, fragrant dish.

Recipe 3: Pan-Seared Spanish Mackerel with Garlic Butter Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 3 Spanish mackerel fillets
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Fresh chives, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Season the Fish: Pat dry the fillets and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Sear the Fish: Heat olive oil in a large non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add fillets skin-side down. Cook for 4 minutes without moving to get the skin crisp.
  3. Flip and Add Butter: Turn the fillets over. Add butter and garlic to the pan. Spoon the melted garlic butter over the fish as it finishes cooking, about 3 minutes more.
  4. Finish with Lemon: Remove from heat. Squeeze lemon juice over the fish and sprinkle with chives.
  5. Serve Immediately: Delicious with a side of sautéed vegetables or a fresh salad.

This simple, elegant method highlights the crispy skin and rich flavor of Spanish mackerel, with butter and garlic elevating every bite.

Recipe 4: Baked Spanish Mackerel with Mediterranean Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 4 Spanish mackerel fillets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Fresh basil for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Oven: Set oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare Vegetables: Toss bell pepper, squash, zucchini, and onion with olive oil, oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Arrange in Baking Dish: Spread the vegetables evenly on the bottom of a large baking dish. Place the Spanish mackerel fillets on top.
  4. Bake: Cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is just cooked through and vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil.

This recipe turns the oven into a one-pan wonder, combining Spanish mackerel with a colorful medley of vegetables for a nutritious and satisfying meal.

Recipe 5: Spanish Mackerel Tacos with Mango Salsa

Ingredients:

For the Fish:

  • 4 Spanish mackerel fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper

For Mango Salsa:

  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste

For Serving:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Shredded cabbage or lettuce
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Mango Salsa: In a bowl, combine mango, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, cilantro, and salt. Mix well and refrigerate until ready to serve.
  2. Season the Fish: Brush Spanish mackerel with olive oil. Sprinkle chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper evenly on both sides.
  3. Cook the Fish: Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the fillets skin-side down for 4-5 minutes, flip, and cook another 3 minutes until the fish flakes easily.
  4. Assemble Tacos: Warm tortillas. Break the fish into chunks and place on tortillas. Top with mango salsa and shredded cabbage or lettuce.
  5. Serve: Garnish with lime wedges for an added burst of freshness.

These vibrant tacos bring a tropical twist to Spanish mackerel, pairing the fish’s rich flavor with sweet and spicy mango salsa—a perfect balance of tastes and textures.

Final Tips for Cooking Spanish Mackerel

  • Don’t Overcook: Oily fish like Spanish mackerel can become dry if overdone. Aim for an internal temperature around 130°F (54°C).
  • Skin-On For Flavor: Keeping the skin on enhances flavor and texture; it crisps beautifully when cooked properly.
  • Experiment with Herbs and Spices: Spanish mackerel stands up to strong Mediterranean flavors, Asian spices, and Caribbean seasonings alike.
  • Pairing: It pairs well with fresh salads, roasted vegetables, or simple rice dishes to complement its robust flavor without overpowering it.

Cooking Spanish mackerel at home can be an exciting journey into bold flavors and simple, satisfying seafood meals. These five recipes offer different styles and techniques to suit your mood and occasion. Try them out and discover your favorite way to enjoy this remarkable fish!

Feel free to experiment and share your own Spanish mackerel creations. Cooking seafood is an art, and every cook brings their own personality to the plate. Enjoy!
