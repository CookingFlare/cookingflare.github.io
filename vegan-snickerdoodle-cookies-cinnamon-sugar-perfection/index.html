Vegan Snickerdoodle Cookies: Cinnamon-Sugar Perfection


Snickerdoodle cookies have been a beloved classic for generations, with their irresistible combination of cinnamon and sugar. These cookies have a perfect balance of chewy and slightly crisp textures, making them a go-to treat for any occasion. But what if you could enjoy them while keeping your dietary choices plant-based? Yes, you can! In this post, we’ll dive into how to make vegan snickerdoodle cookies that are just as delightful as the traditional ones, with no animal products involved. Let’s explore how to make these cinnamon-sugar masterpieces from scratch, and discover the magic that comes from swapping a few ingredients to create a fully vegan-friendly version.

Why Go Vegan with Snickerdoodle Cookies?

Before we get into the recipe, you may wonder why you would want to make vegan snickerdoodle cookies. The reasons are plenty, and they go beyond just following a plant-based diet. Here are a few benefits:

  • Ethical Eating: By choosing plant-based ingredients, you’re making a compassionate choice that minimizes harm to animals and supports sustainable farming practices.

  • Healthier Option: Vegan baked goods often contain less cholesterol and saturated fat, which can contribute to better heart health. Plus, you can avoid refined sugars and oils typically used in traditional recipes.

  • Dietary Preferences: Whether you follow a vegan lifestyle, have lactose intolerance, or want to reduce your environmental footprint, vegan snickerdoodles offer a tasty alternative that caters to various needs.

Vegan Baking Substitutions for Snickerdoodles

In order to make snickerdoodle cookies vegan-friendly, we’ll need to substitute a few key ingredients:

  • Eggs: Instead of using eggs, which traditionally bind the ingredients and help with rising, we’ll opt for a flax egg. A flax egg is made by mixing ground flaxseed with water, allowing it to thicken and become gelatinous, much like an egg.

  • Butter: Snickerdoodle recipes typically call for butter, which adds richness and a tender crumb. We can easily swap this out for vegan butter or coconut oil, both of which provide the same texture without any dairy.

  • Milk: Regular cow’s milk can be replaced with a variety of plant-based milk options, such as almond, soy, oat, or coconut milk. Each type of milk has its own subtle flavor, but any will work beautifully in snickerdoodle dough.

  • Sugar: Most traditional snickerdoodle recipes call for granulated sugar, but it’s essential to ensure that the sugar used is vegan-friendly. Some sugars are processed with bone char, so opt for organic or unrefined sugars that are certified vegan.

With these simple swaps, we can make snickerdoodle cookies that are both delicious and vegan-friendly!

The Perfect Vegan Snickerdoodle Cookie Recipe

Here’s how to create your very own batch of vegan snickerdoodle cookies, each bite bursting with cinnamon-sugar perfection.

Ingredients

For the cookie dough:

  • 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup vegan butter (or coconut oil), softened
  • 1 cup organic sugar (make sure it’s vegan-friendly)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour (use gluten-free flour if preferred)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional, for extra flavor)

For the cinnamon-sugar coating:

  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon

Instructions

1. Prepare the Flax Egg

To begin, we need to make the flax egg. In a small bowl, mix the ground flaxseed with water. Stir well and set it aside for about 5 minutes. During this time, the flax mixture will thicken and become gelatinous, which will act as a binder in place of the egg.

2. Cream Together the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the softened vegan butter, organic sugar, and brown sugar. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the mixture until it’s light and fluffy. This will take around 2-3 minutes. Once the sugars are fully creamed, add in the vanilla extract and flax egg, and mix until well combined.

3. Combine Dry Ingredients

In another bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, cream of tartar, salt, and cinnamon (if you’re adding it to the dough). The cream of tartar is an essential ingredient in snickerdoodle cookies, giving them their signature tangy flavor and chewy texture. Once mixed, gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until fully incorporated.

4. Chill the Dough

Once the dough has come together, cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp towel. Let it chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to an hour. Chilling the dough will prevent the cookies from spreading too much during baking, giving them that perfect shape and texture.

5. Preheat the Oven

While the dough chills, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper to ensure the cookies bake evenly and don’t stick to the pan.

6. Roll the Dough into Balls

After the dough has chilled, use a tablespoon or cookie scoop to form small balls of dough. Roll each ball between your hands to smooth it out. Then, roll each dough ball in the cinnamon-sugar mixture, making sure it’s fully coated. This step gives the snickerdoodles their signature golden, sweet crust.

7. Bake the Cookies

Place the dough balls onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are slightly firm to the touch. The cookies will continue to firm up as they cool, so be careful not to overbake them.

8. Let Cool

Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. This cooling period helps the cookies set properly and allows you to enjoy them at the perfect temperature.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Snickerdoodle Cookies

  • Don’t skip the chilling step: Chilling the dough is crucial to achieving the ideal texture. If you skip this step, your cookies might spread too thin and become overly crispy. A little patience here will go a long way in ensuring a soft, chewy bite.

  • Use room temperature butter: If your vegan butter is too cold, it may not cream properly with the sugar, resulting in a dense dough. Be sure to let it soften at room temperature for the best results.

  • Bake one batch at a time: If you’re baking multiple sheets of cookies, bake them one at a time for the most even baking. If you place too many trays in the oven, the cookies may not cook evenly, and they might not rise properly.

  • Adjust cinnamon-sugar ratio: Feel free to adjust the cinnamon and sugar ratio to your personal preference. If you love cinnamon, go ahead and increase the amount. Some people like a bolder cinnamon flavor, while others prefer a more balanced blend of sugar and spice.

  • Experiment with add-ins: While classic snickerdoodles are perfect as they are, you can also experiment with different add-ins. Vegan chocolate chips, chopped nuts (like walnuts or pecans), or even a dash of cardamom can give your cookies a unique twist.

Storing Vegan Snickerdoodle Cookies

These vegan snickerdoodle cookies will stay fresh for up to a week when stored in an airtight container at room temperature. To extend their freshness, you can also freeze the dough for future use. Simply scoop the dough into balls and freeze them on a baking sheet. Once frozen, transfer the dough balls to a freezer-safe container or bag. When you’re ready to bake, just roll them in cinnamon-sugar and bake straight from the freezer—no need to thaw!

Why You’ll Love Vegan Snickerdoodle Cookies

The best part about these vegan snickerdoodle cookies is how close they come to the traditional version, with all the flavors you love and none of the animal products. They’re soft, chewy, and perfectly spiced with cinnamon, providing that nostalgic taste you crave. Plus, the cinnamon-sugar coating is simply irresistible, giving each bite that crunchy, sweet exterior while keeping the inside delightfully soft.

Whether you’re a vegan, someone with dietary restrictions, or just a fan of delicious cookies, these vegan snickerdoodle cookies are sure to become a household favorite. They’re perfect for sharing with friends, serving at family gatherings, or simply enjoying on your own with a cup of tea or coffee. Make a batch today, and savor the warm, comforting taste of cinnamon and sugar—completely plant-based!
