Classic Deviled Eggs: The Ultimate Party Snack


Deviled eggs are a classic party snack that never goes out of style. With their creamy filling and delightful presentation, they are sure to be a hit at any gathering. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or a fancy dinner party, deviled eggs are a versatile and crowd-pleasing appetizer that will leave your guests craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Paprika, for garnish
  • Fresh chives, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by boiling the eggs. Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover them with water. Bring the water to a boil over medium heat. Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the eggs simmer for 10 minutes.

  2. While the eggs are boiling, prepare an ice bath. Fill a large bowl with ice and water. Once the eggs are done cooking, use a slotted spoon to transfer them to the ice bath. Let them sit in the ice bath for at least 5 minutes to cool and stop the cooking process.

  3. Once the eggs are cool, gently tap each egg on a hard surface to crack the shell. Roll the egg between your hands to loosen the shell, then peel it off. Rinse the peeled eggs under cold water to remove any remaining shell fragments.

  4. Slice each egg in half lengthwise. Carefully remove the yolks and place them in a separate bowl. Set the egg whites aside.

  5. Mash the egg yolks with a fork until they are crumbly. Add the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white vinegar, salt, and black pepper to the bowl with the yolks. Mix well until the ingredients are fully combined and the filling is smooth and creamy.

  6. Spoon the yolk mixture into the hollowed-out egg whites. You can use a piping bag or simply use a spoon to fill the eggs. Be generous with the filling, but make sure not to overfill them.

  7. Sprinkle the deviled eggs with paprika for a touch of color and flavor. You can also garnish them with fresh chives for an extra pop of freshness.

  8. Once the eggs are filled and garnished, they are ready to serve. Arrange them on a platter and watch them disappear within minutes. Deviled eggs are best enjoyed fresh, so make sure to serve them right away.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with the filling by adding different herbs or spices. You can try adding chopped dill, minced garlic, or even a dash of hot sauce for a kick of heat.
  • For a fancy twist, garnish the deviled eggs with smoked salmon, caviar, or crispy bacon bits. These toppings will take your deviled eggs to a whole new level of indulgence.
  • If you’re looking for a healthier option, you can substitute Greek yogurt for some or all of the mayonnaise in the filling. This will reduce the fat content without compromising on taste.
  • Deviled eggs can be prepared in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Just make sure to cover them tightly with plastic wrap to prevent them from drying out.

So, whether you’re hosting a casual get-together or a formal dinner party, classic deviled eggs are the perfect party snack. With their creamy filling and endless variations, they are sure to be a crowd favorite. So go ahead and give this recipe a try. Your guests will thank you!
