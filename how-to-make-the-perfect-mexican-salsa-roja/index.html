How to Make the Perfect Mexican Salsa Roja


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you know that a good salsa can make all the difference in a dish. Salsa roja, or red salsa, is a classic and versatile condiment that adds a burst of flavor to tacos, burritos, enchiladas, and more. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make the perfect Mexican salsa roja from scratch. Get ready to elevate your dishes with this delicious and authentic salsa!

Ingredients

  • 6 ripe tomatoes
  • 2-3 serrano chilies
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons of water (if needed)

Instructions

  1. Prep the Ingredients: Start by prepping the ingredients. Wash the tomatoes and serrano chilies thoroughly. If you prefer a milder salsa, you can remove the seeds and membranes from the chilies. Peel the garlic cloves and roughly chop the onion.

  2. Roast the Tomatoes and Chilies: In a dry skillet over medium-high heat, place the tomatoes and serrano chilies. Roast them, turning occasionally, until they are charred in spots and softened. This process will take about 10-15 minutes. Remove from the skillet and let them cool.

  3. Blend the Salsa: Once the tomatoes and chilies have cooled, place them in a blender along with the chopped onion, garlic cloves, salt, and black pepper. Blend until you achieve a smooth consistency.

  4. Cook the Salsa: In a saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Pour the blended salsa into the saucepan and let it cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will help the flavors meld together and the salsa to thicken slightly.

  5. Adjust the Seasoning: Taste the salsa and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the salsa is too thick, you can add a tablespoon or two of water to reach your desired consistency.

  6. Add Fresh Cilantro: Once the salsa is cooked to your liking, stir in the chopped fresh cilantro. This will add a bright and fresh flavor to the salsa.

  7. Cool and Serve: Remove the salsa from the heat and let it cool to room temperature. Once cooled, it’s ready to be served with your favorite Mexican dishes.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can char the tomatoes and chilies on a grill instead of a skillet.
  • For a tangy twist, add a squeeze of fresh lime juice to the salsa.
  • If you like your salsa extra spicy, you can add an extra serrano chili or even a jalapeño for an extra kick.

Final Thoughts

Making your own Mexican salsa roja at home is not only easy but also incredibly rewarding. The vibrant flavors of the roasted tomatoes and chilies, combined with the freshness of cilantro, create a salsa that’s hard to resist. Whether you’re hosting a Mexican-themed dinner party or simply craving some authentic flavors, this salsa roja recipe is sure to impress. So, next time you’re in the mood for some homemade Mexican fare, don’t forget to whip up a batch of this perfect salsa roja!
