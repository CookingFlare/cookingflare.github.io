How to Make Grilled Cheese Sandwiches on the BBQ


Grilled cheese sandwiches evoke a sense of comfort like few other dishes. When made on a barbecue grill, they transform into smoky, crispy, melty masterpieces that elevate a simple sandwich to new heights. If you’ve been sticking to pan-frying your grilled cheese or toasting it in an oven, it’s time to bring your sandwich game outdoors and onto the BBQ.

In this guide, you’ll learn everything you need to craft perfect grilled cheese sandwiches on your barbecue grill — from prepping the bread and choosing the right cheese to techniques that give you the perfect crust and melted interior. Bonus: there are some delicious recipe ideas and tips for experimenting with this BBQ favorite.

Why Make Grilled Cheese on the BBQ?

Grilling a sandwich over an open flame or charcoal introduces a subtle smoky flavor that can’t be replicated on stovetops or indoor grills. Plus, the grill’s high heat allows the bread to crisp up beautifully, producing that satisfying crunch that many grilled cheese lovers crave. The outdoor cooking experience also adds to the fun — perfect for summer cookouts or casual weeknight dinners.

Essential Ingredients for BBQ Grilled Cheese Sandwiches

Before firing up the grill, gather these must-haves:

  • Bread: Thick-sliced artisan bread, sourdough, or white sandwich bread works well. Opt for something sturdy that won’t easily fall apart but still crisps to golden perfection.
  • Cheese: Choose cheeses that melt well, such as cheddar, American, provolone, gouda, or mozzarella. Combining cheeses can add complexity to flavor and texture.
  • Butter or Oil: Softened butter or olive oil to coat the bread, helping achieve a golden-brown crust.
  • Optional Add-ins: Tomatoes, bacon, avocado slices, caramelized onions, pickles, or fresh herbs can transform the sandwich from simple to gourmet.

Step-By-Step: How to Grill the Perfect BBQ Grilled Cheese Sandwich

1. Preheat and Prepare the Grill

Make sure your grill is clean and preheated to medium heat, roughly 350°F to 375°F. For charcoal grills, set up a two-zone fire with coals concentrated to one side for direct heat grilling and cooler indirect heat on the other side.

Using medium heat prevents the bread from burning before the cheese melts.

2. Butter Your Bread (Or Brush With Oil)

Evenly spread softened butter on one side of each slice of bread. This will be the exterior that hits the grill surface. If you prefer, brush olive oil for a different flavor note and crispiness.

3. Assemble the Sandwich

  • Place cheese slices generously on the unbuttered side of one bread slice.
  • Add any extras like tomato or bacon here if desired.
  • Top with the other slice, buttered side facing out.

Make sure your ingredients are evenly distributed for even melting.

4. Grill the Sandwich

  • Place the sandwich on the grill, buttered side down.
  • Close the lid to trap heat and promote melting.
  • Grill for 3-5 minutes until you see beautiful grill marks and the bread turns golden brown.
  • Flip carefully using a spatula. Press lightly to compact the sandwich for better grilling.
  • Grill the other side another 3-5 minutes until crisp and cheese is gooey.

5. Check for Doneness and Melted Cheese

Peek inside to ensure the cheese is thoroughly melted. If the bread crisps up too fast but cheese isn’t melting, move the sandwich to the cooler indirect heat area, cover, and let it finish cooking.

6. Rest, Slice, and Serve

Let the sandwich rest for a minute or two after grilling so the cheese sets slightly. Then slice it diagonally for classic presentation.

Tips for Elevating Your BBQ Grilled Cheese

  • Use a Cast Iron Skillet or Grill Pan: This can prevent cheese from melting through grill grates and offers more control over heat.
  • Experiment with Cheese Blends: Combine a sharp cheddar with creamy mozzarella or smoked gouda for flavor complexity.
  • Add Crunchy Elements Inside: Crispy bacon, toasted nuts, or even pickles bring texture contrast.
  • Incorporate Fresh Herbs: Basil, thyme, or rosemary layered inside add an aromatic touch.
  • Avoid Over-stuffing: Too many fillings can cause slipping and uneven grilling.

Flavor-Boosting Variations of BBQ Grilled Cheese

Classic BBQ Cheddar with Tomato

  • Bread: Thick sourdough
  • Cheese: Sharp cheddar and mozzarella blend
  • Add: Fresh tomato slices and cracked black pepper

Smoky Gouda and Caramelized Onion

  • Bread: Rustic country loaf
  • Cheese: Smoked gouda
  • Add: Thin caramelized onions and a smear of grainy mustard

Spicy Jalapeño and Pepper Jack

  • Bread: White sandwich bread
  • Cheese: Pepper jack
  • Add: Sliced fresh jalapeños and a drizzle of chipotle mayo after grilling

Mediterranean Caprese Grilled Cheese

  • Bread: Ciabatta or rustic Italian bread
  • Cheese: Fresh mozzarella
  • Add: Tomato slices, fresh basil leaves, and a splash of balsamic glaze post-grilling

Tools That Make BBQ Grilled Cheese Easier

  • Non-stick grilling spatula: For easy flipping and retrieval without sandwich mishaps.
  • Cast iron skillet: Helps manage heat and keeps cheese contained.
  • Grill baskets: Good if you want to make multiple sandwiches and flip simultaneously.
  • Thermometer: Useful to monitor exact grill temperatures for cooking control.

Safety and Cleanup Tips for Outdoor Grilling

  • Keep the grill lid closed when possible to maintain heat.
  • Watch for flare-ups from dripping butter; reduce heat or move sandwich to indirect area if necessary.
  • Clean grill grates before cooking to avoid sticking.
  • After grilling, let the grill cool before scraping down and wiping any residue.

Making It a BBQ Event: Pairings and Presentation

Grilled cheese sandwiches made on the BBQ pair wonderfully with hearty sides and drinks such as:

  • Creamy tomato soup, chilled cucumber salad, or classic coleslaw
  • Crispy sweet potato fries or grilled corn on the cob
  • Cold drinks like craft beer, freshly brewed iced tea, or lemonade

Serve the sandwiches hot off the grill while crusty and cheesy — the perfect centerpiece for an outdoor meal.

Conclusion

Grilled cheese sandwiches on the barbecue combine the comforts of melty cheese and toasted bread with the unique smoky charm of outdoor grilling. With practice, simple tips, and endless variations, you can master crafting BBQ grilled cheese sandwiches that impress friends and family alike.

Fire up your grill, dust off your favorite cheeses, and make your next grilled cheese an unforgettable outdoor treat. Happy grilling!
