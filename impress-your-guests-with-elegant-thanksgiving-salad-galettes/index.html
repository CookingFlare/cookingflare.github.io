Impress Your Guests with Elegant Thanksgiving Salad Galettes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in a feast that celebrates gratitude and togetherness. While the main course often takes center stage, it’s important not to overlook the importance of a refreshing and elegant salad to complement your Thanksgiving spread. This year, impress your guests with these delightful Thanksgiving salad galettes that are as beautiful as they are delicious.

Ingredients

For the Galette Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Salad:

  • Mixed salad greens
  • Pomegranate seeds
  • Candied pecans
  • Goat cheese crumbles

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt for the galette dough. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough chills, prepare the salad ingredients. Wash and dry the mixed salad greens, and set them aside. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to create the dressing. Set aside.

  5. Remove the dough from the refrigerator and place it on a lightly floured surface. Roll out the dough into a large circle, about 1/4 inch thick. Carefully transfer the dough circle onto the prepared baking sheet.

  6. Arrange the mixed salad greens on top of the dough, leaving a border of about 2 inches. Sprinkle the pomegranate seeds, candied pecans, and goat cheese crumbles evenly over the greens.

  7. Gently fold the edges of the dough over the filling, creating pleats as you go. This will give your galette a rustic, homemade look.

  8. Brush the edges of the galette with beaten egg for a golden finish, if desired.

  9. Bake the galette in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly.

  10. Drizzle the dressing over the galette before serving, or serve it on the side for guests to dress their own portions.

  11. Slice the galette into wedges and serve as a stunning centerpiece to your Thanksgiving table.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize your galettes by adding other seasonal ingredients such as roasted butternut squash, sliced apples, or dried cranberries.

  • For a vegetarian option, omit the goat cheese or replace it with a vegan cheese alternative.

  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making your own galette dough.

  • Experiment with different dressings to find your favorite flavor combination. A honey mustard vinaigrette or a creamy herb dressing would both work beautifully with these galettes.

  • These galettes can also be made ahead of time and served at room temperature. Just be sure to dress the salad right before serving to keep the greens fresh.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, elevate your salad game with these elegant Thanksgiving salad galettes. Not only are they visually stunning, but they also offer a delightful combination of flavors and textures that will impress your guests. Whether you’re serving a vegetarian crowd or looking to add a refreshing touch to your traditional Thanksgiving feast, these galettes are sure to be a hit. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create a Thanksgiving salad masterpiece that will leave your guests in awe.
