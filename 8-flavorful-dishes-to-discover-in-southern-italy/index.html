8 Flavorful Dishes to Discover in Southern Italy


Southern Italy, a region renowned for its sun-kissed landscapes, ancient ruins, and delectable cuisine, is a haven for food enthusiasts. From the rugged coastline of Calabria to the picturesque hills of Puglia, this southern region boasts an incredible diversity of flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques. In this culinary journey, we will delve into eight mouth-watering dishes that will transport you to the heart of Southern Italy.

  1. Traditional Calabrian 'Nduja with Bread and Olives Located in the toe of Italy, Calabria is famous for its spicy, flavorful 'nduja, a type of pork spread made from pig fat, meat, and red chili peppers. This rustic dish pairs 'nduja with crusty bread and a side of ripe olives, showcasing the bold flavors of this southern region. Pair it with a glass of Primitivo wine for an authentic experience.
  2. Pugliese Fava Bean Purée with Cherry Tomatoes In Puglia, fava beans are a staple ingredient during the spring season. A local specialty is a creamy fava bean purée blended with cherry tomatoes and herbs, often served as a side dish. This hearty and comforting recipe highlights the region's focus on using fresh, seasonal ingredients.
  3. Apulian Grilled Eel with Garlic and Lemon Apulia's coastal waters abound with fresh seafood, and grilling eel is a cherished local tradition. This dish showcases the beauty of simplicity by pairing eel with a classic duo of garlic and lemon, allowing the delicate flavor of the fish to shine.
  4. Lucanian Fritto Misto with Artichokes and Zucchini Lucania's love affair with fritto misto, a mixed fry of vegetables and seafood, is a true reflection of this region's zest for life. A typical Lucanian take on this beloved dish includes artichokes and zucchini, intertwined with a delicate crunch of fried Calabrian peppers.
  5. Campanian Eggplant Caponata with Sardines and Capers Campania, particularly the island of Capri, is known for its tantalizing eggplant caponata, a Sicilian-Mediterranean inspired chutney mixed with sardines and capers. The rich, velvety texture of this sumptuous dish cannot be missed.
