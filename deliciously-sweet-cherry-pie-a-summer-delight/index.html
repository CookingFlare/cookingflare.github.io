Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie: A Summer Delight


Nothing says summer quite like a perfectly baked cherry pie. With its vibrant red filling and flaky golden crust, this classic dessert is a true delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply want to indulge in a sweet treat, this recipe is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 cups fresh cherries, pitted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the crust. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape them into disks. Wrap each disk in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In the meantime, prepare the filling by combining the pitted cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract in a mixing bowl. Gently toss the ingredients together until the cherries are evenly coated.

  5. Roll out one of the chilled dough disks on a lightly floured surface to fit your pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled dough to the dish and press it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges.

  6. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared crust, spreading it out evenly.

  7. Roll out the second dough disk to create a lattice pattern or a solid crust for your pie. If making a lattice, cut the rolled dough into thin strips and weave them over the filling. If making a solid crust, simply place the rolled dough on top of the filling and cut a few slits to allow steam to escape.

  8. Brush the top crust with a beaten egg for a glossy finish, if desired.

  9. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  10. Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 2 hours before serving. This will give the filling time to set.

Serving Suggestions

Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie can be enjoyed on its own or served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. The combination of the warm, gooey filling and the cool, creamy ice cream is simply heavenly.

Tips and Tricks

  • To save time, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making it from scratch. Just make sure to follow the package instructions for baking.

  • If fresh cherries are not in season, you can also use frozen cherries. Thaw them before using and adjust the sugar accordingly.

  • For a decorative touch, sprinkle some coarse sugar on top of the pie crust before baking. This will give it a beautiful sparkle and added sweetness.

  • Leftover pie can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply place a slice in the microwave for a few seconds or warm it in a preheated oven for a few minutes.

Conclusion

Deliciously Sweet Cherry Pie is the perfect dessert to celebrate the flavors of summer. With its buttery crust and juicy cherry filling, it’s a true crowd-pleaser. So, gather your loved ones, slice into this delectable treat, and savor the taste of summer in every bite. Happy baking!
