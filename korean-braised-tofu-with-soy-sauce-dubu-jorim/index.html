Korean Braised Tofu with Soy Sauce (Dubu Jorim)


If you’re looking to add a comforting and flavorful dish to your cooking repertoire, Korean braised tofu with soy sauce, also known as Dubu Jorim, is a fantastic option. This dish brings together the delicate texture of tofu and the savory depth of soy sauce, creating a mouthwatering combination that is both simple and satisfying. Whether you’re new to Korean cuisine or a seasoned cook, Dubu Jorim is an easy yet impressive recipe that’s perfect for any meal.

In this blog post, we will walk you through everything you need to know to create this delicious dish from scratch. We’ll cover the ingredients, step-by-step instructions, tips, and some variations to suit your taste. Let’s dive into the art of making Dubu Jorim!

What Is Dubu Jorim?

Dubu Jorim is a popular Korean side dish made by braising tofu in a savory soy-based sauce. The tofu soaks up the sauce’s flavors while being simmered until golden brown and slightly crispy on the outside. This dish is typically served as part of a Korean banchan (side dish) spread and is loved for its rich umami taste, balanced by a touch of sweetness and heat. It pairs wonderfully with steamed rice, kimchi, or other Korean comfort foods.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • Simple Ingredients: Dubu Jorim requires only a few basic ingredients that are easy to find at your local grocery store or Asian market.
  • Vegetarian-Friendly: Tofu, the main ingredient, is an excellent source of plant-based protein, making this dish suitable for vegetarians and vegans alike.
  • Quick and Easy: This dish comes together quickly, making it a great weeknight meal or a side dish for a larger Korean feast.
  • Full of Flavor: The combination of soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, and a hint of sweetness brings a complex yet balanced flavor profile to the dish.

Ingredients for Korean Braised Tofu with Soy Sauce (Dubu Jorim)

Before you get started, gather these simple ingredients:

  • Firm tofu (14 oz / 400g): Firm tofu works best for this dish because it holds its shape during braising. You can also use extra-firm tofu for a firmer texture, but regular firm tofu will provide the right balance.
  • Soy sauce (3 tablespoons): A good quality soy sauce is key to giving the dish a rich, savory flavor. You can use regular or low-sodium soy sauce, depending on your preference.
  • Sesame oil (1 tablespoon): This adds a fragrant nutty flavor to the dish, making it quintessential in Korean cooking.
  • Garlic (3-4 cloves, minced): Garlic enhances the savory depth of the dish, infusing it with aromatic goodness.
  • Sugar (1 teaspoon): A small amount of sugar balances the salty soy sauce and creates a subtle sweetness in the dish.
  • Water (1/2 cup): To help braise the tofu and create a flavorful sauce.
  • Gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes, optional, 1 teaspoon): For a bit of heat and color. You can adjust this depending on your spice tolerance.
  • Green onions (1-2, chopped): For garnish, green onions add a fresh, slightly pungent touch that complements the braised tofu.
  • Sesame seeds (1 teaspoon, optional): Toasted sesame seeds add a lovely crunch and additional nutty flavor, making your dish visually appealing as well.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Dubu Jorim

Step 1: Prepare the Tofu

Start by draining the tofu to remove excess moisture. Place the tofu block on a cutting board and use a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to press it gently. You want to press out as much liquid as possible to ensure the tofu absorbs the braising sauce properly. After pressing, cut the tofu into thick slices—about 1/2 inch thick. You should end up with around 8-10 pieces.

Step 2: Sear the Tofu

In a non-stick skillet or frying pan, heat a little bit of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, gently add the tofu slices to the pan. Sear the tofu pieces until they are golden brown on both sides, which should take about 3-4 minutes per side. You want a nice crispy outer layer while keeping the inside tender and soft. Once done, remove the tofu from the pan and set it aside.

Step 3: Make the Braising Sauce

In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium-low. Add the minced garlic to the pan and sauté for about 30 seconds, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.

Now, add the soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, water, and gochugaru (if using) to the pan. Stir the mixture to combine, and let it simmer for 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 4: Braise the Tofu

Return the seared tofu slices to the skillet, carefully arranging them in the sauce. Spoon some of the sauce over the tofu slices, ensuring they are well-coated. Let the tofu braise in the sauce for about 5-7 minutes, occasionally spooning the sauce over the tofu to allow it to soak in the flavors. You’ll notice that the sauce thickens slightly as it reduces.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the tofu has absorbed the flavors and the sauce has thickened, it’s time to serve! Garnish the braised tofu with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added crunch and color. Serve the Dubu Jorim hot with steamed rice, kimchi, or other Korean side dishes.

Tips for the Perfect Dubu Jorim

  • Use firm tofu: Tofu with a firm texture will hold up better during the braising process and absorb the sauce without falling apart. Pressing out the water from the tofu is a crucial step to ensure the tofu doesn’t turn soggy.
  • Adjust the sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter braising sauce, feel free to add more sugar. On the other hand, if you want it saltier, you can increase the amount of soy sauce.
  • Experiment with spice levels: You can adjust the level of heat by increasing or decreasing the amount of gochugaru. If you prefer a milder dish, you can omit it entirely, or substitute it with a small amount of chili flakes.
  • Add vegetables: Feel free to add vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, or zucchini to the sauce for extra texture and flavor. Simply sauté them along with the garlic before adding the soy sauce mixture.
  • Reheat for leftovers: Dubu Jorim tastes even better the next day, as the tofu continues to absorb the sauce. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge, and reheat them on the stovetop or in the microwave.

Variations of Dubu Jorim

While the traditional Dubu Jorim recipe is delightful on its own, there are many ways to customize the dish to suit your tastes or dietary needs.

1. Vegan Version with Mushroom

For a heartier, vegan version of Dubu Jorim, try adding mushrooms. The earthy umami of mushrooms complements the tofu and soy sauce beautifully. You can sauté mushrooms like shiitake, button, or enoki mushrooms with the garlic before braising them with the tofu in the sauce.

2. Spicy Dubu Jorim

If you enjoy spicier dishes, you can increase the amount of gochugaru or add fresh sliced chili peppers into the braising sauce. This will kick up the heat and provide an extra layer of flavor to the dish.

3. With Kimchi

For a bolder twist, consider adding some chopped kimchi to the sauce. The tangy, fermented flavors of kimchi enhance the overall flavor profile and bring a nice contrast to the sweetness of the soy sauce.

4. Dubu Jorim with Beef

Although traditionally made with tofu, you can also add thinly sliced beef to this dish for a non-vegetarian version. The beef will absorb the soy-based sauce and create a savory, meaty addition to the dish.

Serving Suggestions for Dubu Jorim

Dubu Jorim is typically served as a side dish in a Korean meal, but it also makes a great main dish when paired with steamed rice and other banchan (side dishes). Here are a few ideas on how to serve it:

  • With steamed white rice: The tofu’s savory flavors pair perfectly with plain steamed rice, making for a comforting and balanced meal.
  • As a side dish: Dubu Jorim can be served alongside other traditional Korean banchan like kimchi, pickled vegetables, or sautéed spinach.
  • With a bowl of soup: Serve Dubu Jorim with a bowl of Korean soup, such as a miso soup or a spicy kimchi jjigae, for a more complete meal.

Final Thoughts

Korean braised tofu with soy sauce (Dubu Jorim) is a delicious, simple, and versatile dish that can be easily adapted to suit your personal tastes. With its rich umami flavor, tender tofu, and aromatic soy sauce braising liquid, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re a fan of traditional Korean cuisine or just looking to try something new, Dubu Jorim is an excellent choice for a satisfying meal.

So next time you’re in the mood for a flavorful side dish or a vegetarian-friendly main, give this recipe a try and enjoy the savory goodness of Korean braised tofu!
