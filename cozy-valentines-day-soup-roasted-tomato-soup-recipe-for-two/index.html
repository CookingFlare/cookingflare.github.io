Cozy Valentine's Day Soup: Roasted Tomato Soup Recipe for Two


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a cozy, heartwarming meal? This Roasted Tomato Soup recipe is the perfect dish to share with your loved one on this special day. With its rich flavors and comforting aroma, it’s sure to set the mood for a romantic evening. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Slice the tomatoes in half and place them on a baking sheet, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt, pepper, dried basil, and dried oregano.

  2. Roast the tomatoes in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes, or until they are soft and slightly caramelized. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

  3. In a large pot, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become fragrant and translucent.

  4. Once the roasted tomatoes have cooled, remove the skins and transfer them to the pot with the onion and garlic. Stir well to combine all the flavors.

  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, carefully puree the soup until smooth and creamy. If using a regular blender, be sure to blend in small batches and vent the lid to prevent any accidents.

  7. Return the soup to the pot and stir in the heavy cream. Heat the soup over low heat until it is warmed through, but do not let it boil.

  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the soup is too thick, you can add a little more vegetable broth to achieve your desired consistency.

  9. Serve the Roasted Tomato Soup in warm bowls, garnished with fresh basil leaves. Pair it with some crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a smoother soup, you can strain it through a fine-mesh sieve after blending to remove any remaining tomato seeds or skin.

  • For a touch of indulgence, you can sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top of the soup before serving.

  • If you like a bit of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to give the soup a spicy kick.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved to suit your needs. It’s a versatile dish that can be adjusted to feed a larger crowd or to enjoy as a solo meal.

  • Leftover soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Cozy up with your loved one this Valentine’s Day and savor the delicious flavors of this Roasted Tomato Soup. Its simplicity and warmth make it the perfect dish to celebrate love and togetherness. So, light some candles, set the table, and enjoy a romantic evening filled with love and good food. Happy Valentine’s Day!
