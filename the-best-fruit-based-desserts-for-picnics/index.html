The Best Fruit-Based Desserts for Picnics


When the sun is shining and the sky is blue, there’s no better way to celebrate the beauty of nature than with a picnic. What better way to enhance your outdoor feast than with delightful, fruit-based desserts that not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also bring a refreshing touch to your meal? From fruity tarts to luscious parfaits, let's explore some of the best fruit-based desserts that are perfect for picnics.

  1. Classic Strawberry Shortcake Ingredients:

1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced 1/4 cup granulated sugar 2 cups all-purpose flour 1 tablespoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, cold and cubed 1 cup heavy cream 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

Prepare the Strawberries: In a bowl, combine sliced strawberries and sugar. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to create a syrupy mixture, stirring occasionally.

Make the Shortcakes: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

Add Cream: Stir in the heavy cream and vanilla until just combined. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Pat into a 1-inch thick circle and cut into rounds.

Bake: Bake for 12-15 minutes until golden. Let them cool slightly.

Assemble: Split the shortcakes in half, spoon strawberries along with their juices on the bottom half, and top with whipped cream before placing the other half on top.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Strawberry shortcake captures the essence of summer and is easy to transport. The individual components can be packed separately and assembled fresh at your picnic spot. 2. Peach Cobbler Bars Ingredients:

4 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 cup all-purpose flour 3/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted 1 egg

Instructions:

Prepare Peaches: Toss sliced peaches with lemon juice and set aside.

Make the Batter: In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Stir in melted butter and egg until combined.

Create Layers: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread half of the batter into a greased 9x13 inch pan. Layer the peaches on top and then spread the remaining batter over the peaches.

Bake: Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown. Allow to cool completely before slicing into bars.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Peach cobbler bars are easily portable and make for a wonderful dessert that can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature. Plus, they bring a rustic charm to your picnic spread. 3. Mango Coconut Chia Pudding Ingredients:

1 cup coconut milk 1/4 cup chia seeds 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup 1 ripe mango, cubed Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, whisk together coconut milk, chia seeds, and sweetener. Allow it to sit for 10 minutes, then whisk again to prevent clumping.

Refrigerate: Cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight until it thickens.

Serve: When ready to serve, stir well, top with fresh mango cubes, and garnish with mint leaves.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Chia pudding is a nutritious and filling dessert that can be prepared ahead of time. Its refreshing coconut and mango combination is magical, perfect for beating the summer heat. 4. Berry Tart with Almond Crust Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups almond flour 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted 2 tablespoons honey 1/4 teaspoon salt 2 cups mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries) 1 cup Greek yogurt 2 tablespoons honey (for yogurt)

Instructions:

Make the Crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, combine almond flour, melted coconut oil, honey, and salt. Press the mixture into the bottom of a tart pan evenly.

Bake the Crust: Bake for 10-12 minutes until lightly golden. Allow it to cool completely.

Layer Yogurt and Berries: In a separate bowl, mix Greek yogurt with honey until well combined. Spread the yogurt mixture over the cooled crust and top with mixed berries.

Chill and Serve: Let the tart chill in the fridge for about 30 minutes before slicing.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: This berry tart is eye-catching and utterly delicious. It is easy to slice and serve, offering a blend of textures and flavors that guests will love. 5. Watermelon Fruit Pizza Ingredients:

1 large watermelon, sliced into 1-inch thick rounds 1 cup Greek yogurt 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 2 tablespoons honey 2 cups assorted fresh fruit (kiwi, berries, bananas, etc.) Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Prepare the Watermelon Base: Cut the watermelon into thick rounds and place them on a large platter.

Make the Yogurt Spread: In a bowl, combine Greek yogurt, vanilla extract, and honey. Mix well.

Assemble the Pizza: Spread a generous layer of the yogurt mixture on each watermelon round. Top with assorted fruits and garnish with mint leaves.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Watermelon fruit pizza is not only visually appealing but also refreshingly hydrating. It’s easy to grab and eat, making it ideal for a picnic setting. 6. Lemon Blueberry Muffins Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup sugar 2 teaspoons baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/3 cup vegetable oil 1 large egg 1/2 cup milk Zest of 1 lemon 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.

Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together vegetable oil, egg, milk, and lemon zest.

Combine and Add Blueberries: Add the wet ingredients to the dry, stirring just until combined. Gently fold in the blueberries.

Bake: Fill muffin cups about 2/3 full and bake for 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Lemon blueberry muffins are wonderful for breakfast picnics or as a light dessert. They are easy to pack and provide a delightful burst of flavor with every bite. 7. Chocolate Chip Banana Bread Ingredients:

3 ripe bananas, mashed 1/3 cup melted butter 1 teaspoon baking soda Pinch of salt 3/4 cup sugar 1 large egg, beaten 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.

Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, mix mashed bananas with melted butter. Add baking soda and salt. Stir in sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla, then mix in flour and chocolate chips.

Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 60-65 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Banana bread is moist, flavorful, and easy to slice for sharing, making it a practical choice for any picnic basket. Plus, the combination of chocolate and banana is simply irresistible. 8. Frozen Fruit Pops Ingredients:

2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries) 1 cup coconut water or fruit juice 2 tablespoons honey (optional)

Instructions:

Blend Fruit: In a blender, combine mixed berries, coconut water or juice, and honey. Blend until smooth.

Pour into Molds: Pour the mixture into popsicle molds, leaving a small space at the top.

Freeze: Insert sticks and freeze for at least 4 hours or until solid.

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics: Frozen fruit pops provide a refreshing treat on a hot day. They are naturally sweet, wholesome, and easy to transport, making them a delightful addition to any picnic. Conclusion The beauty of picnic desserts lies in their simplicity and freshness. Fruit-based desserts not only bring exquisite flavor and color to your picnic spread but also cater to a variety of palates and preferences. Whether it’s a classic strawberry shortcake or refreshing watermelon fruit pizza, these desserts are sure to enhance your outdoor experience and leave everyone with sweet memories. So next time you plan a picnic, consider packing one or more of these delightful fruit-based desserts. They are not just treats, but delicious ways to enjoy the wonderful bounty of nature, ensuring that your picnic is a delightful experience for everyone involved. Enjoy the flavors, the company, and the great outdoors!
