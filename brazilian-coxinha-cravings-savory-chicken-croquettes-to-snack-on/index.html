Brazilian Coxinha Cravings: Savory Chicken Croquettes to Snack On


If you’re a fan of Brazilian cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of coxinha. These delightful little snacks are a popular street food in Brazil, and for good reason. Coxinha is a savory chicken croquette that is crispy on the outside and filled with a flavorful chicken filling on the inside. It’s no wonder that coxinha has become a favorite snack for many people around the world.

The History of Coxinha

Coxinha has a fascinating history that dates back to the 19th century in Brazil. Legend has it that the name “coxinha” comes from the word “coxa,” which means “thigh” in Portuguese. The shape of the coxinha resembles a chicken thigh, hence the name.

Originally, coxinha was made with leftover chicken meat, which was shredded and mixed with spices and seasonings. The mixture was then shaped into a teardrop or drumstick shape, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried until golden brown. Over time, coxinha became a beloved snack that was enjoyed by people from all walks of life.

The Perfect Coxinha Recipe

Now that you know a bit about the history of coxinha, it’s time to dive into the recipe. Making coxinha from scratch may seem intimidating, but with a little practice, you’ll be able to recreate these delicious snacks in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Breadcrumbs for coating
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  2. Add the shredded chicken to the skillet and stir well to combine with the onion and garlic mixture. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Pour in the chicken broth and simmer the mixture for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. In a separate saucepan, bring the water and butter to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and gradually add the flour, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Keep stirring until the mixture forms a smooth, thick dough.

  5. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and let it cool for a few minutes until it’s comfortable to touch. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into small balls.

  6. Flatten each ball into a disk shape and place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center. Fold the dough over the filling, shaping it into a teardrop or drumstick shape.

  7. Roll the coxinha gently in breadcrumbs, ensuring that they are coated evenly. Repeat this process until all the dough and filling have been used.

  8. Heat oil in a deep fryer or a large pot until it reaches about 350°F (175°C). Carefully drop a few coxinhas into the hot oil and fry them until they turn golden brown, usually around 5-6 minutes. Remove them from the oil and let them drain on a paper towel.

  9. Serve the coxinhas hot and enjoy them as a savory snack or appetizer. They pair perfectly with a spicy dipping sauce or a tangy salsa.

Final Thoughts

Coxinha is a delightful snack that is sure to satisfy your cravings for something savory and delicious. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply looking for a tasty snack for yourself, coxinha is a fantastic choice. With its crispy exterior and flavorful chicken filling, it’s no wonder that coxinha has become a beloved Brazilian treat.

So, why not give this recipe a try? Make a batch of coxinhas and experience the joy of Brazilian street food right in your own home. Your taste buds will thank you!
