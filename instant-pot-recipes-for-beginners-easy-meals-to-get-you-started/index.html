Instant Pot Recipes for Beginners: Easy Meals to Get You Started


For anyone looking to simplify their time in the kitchen without sacrificing delicious home-cooked meals, the Instant Pot is a game-changer. This versatile appliance combines several cooking methods—pressure cooking, slow cooking, sautéing, steaming, and more—into one device. It’s perfect for those just starting their journey with cooking or for experienced cooks seeking time-saving options. If you’re a beginner, diving into Instant Pot recipes may seem daunting at first, but this guide will arm you with the confidence and easy recipes to get started.

Why the Instant Pot is Perfect for Beginners

One of the primary reasons beginners love the Instant Pot is its convenience. It significantly reduces cooking time when compared to traditional methods, making even complex recipes attainable for novice cooks. Another major advantage is the “set it and forget it” functionality. With the Instant Pot, there’s no need to babysit your stove or constantly check your food. Simply follow the recipe instructions, adjust the time, and let the pot work its magic.

Additionally, the Instant Pot retains more moisture and nutrients due to the tightly sealed cooking environment. This means dishes, particularly soups, stews, and vegetables, come out tasting richer and healthier. Now, let’s dive into some easy and delicious recipes that will help you maximize your Instant Pot from day one!

1. Classic Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

There’s no better comfort food for beginners to try than mac and cheese. This recipe is creamy, cheesy, and comes together in less than half an hour.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni (or any pasta shape of your choice)
  • 4 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk (or heavy cream for extra richness)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Optional: Crushed black pepper or paprika for garnish

Directions:

  1. Add macaroni, water (or broth), butter, and salt to the Instant Pot. Stir to mix.
  2. Close the lid and set the pressure valve to “Sealing.” Cook on Manual/High Pressure for 4 minutes.
  3. When the timer is done, perform a quick pressure release by carefully turning the valve to “Venting.”
  4. Open the lid and stir the pasta, making sure no water is left at the bottom. If there’s residual liquid, drain it.
  5. Turn the Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting. Stir in milk and cheese gradually, allowing the cheese to melt into a creamy sauce.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot and garnish with black pepper or paprika.

2. Hearty Instant Pot Beef Stew

This beef stew is a foolproof one-pot meal that feels impressively gourmet while being beginner-friendly. Juicy chunks of beef fall apart in a rich and savory broth, complemented by tender vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat (cut into bite-sized chunks)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, sliced into rounds
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme or Italian seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 tablespoons water (for slurry)

Directions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” and wait until it’s hot. Add olive oil and brown the beef in batches until all sides are seared. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add carrots, potatoes, broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir everything well.
  4. Return the beef to the pot. Close the lid and set the pressure valve to “Sealing.” Cook on Manual/High Pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. Once cooking is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  6. For a thicker stew, mix cornstarch with water to create a slurry. Turn on the “Sauté” function and stir the slurry into the stew until it reaches your desired consistency. Serve warm with crusty bread.

3. Instant Pot Lemon Garlic Chicken

This light and flavorful chicken dish is perfect for quick weeknight meals. It pairs beautifully with rice, pasta, or steamed vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: Chopped parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” and add olive oil. Once hot, sear the chicken breasts for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add minced garlic to the pot and sauté until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Pour in the chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits.
  3. Stir in lemon juice, lemon zest, and Italian seasoning.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot. Close the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Cook on Manual/High Pressure for 8 minutes.
  5. Perform a quick pressure release. Open the lid and check the chicken for doneness (internal temperature should reach 165°F). If needed, cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  6. Garnish with parsley and serve with your favorite sides.

4. Simple Instant Pot Oatmeal

Start your mornings right with this creamy and hearty oatmeal that requires almost no effort.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats (or steel-cut oats)
  • 2 cups water or milk
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: Fresh fruit, honey, nuts, cinnamon, or maple syrup

Directions:

  1. Combine oats, water (or milk), and salt in the Instant Pot. Stir gently to prevent sticking.
  2. Close the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Cook on Manual/High Pressure for 5 minutes for rolled oats or 10 minutes for steel-cut oats.
  3. Perform a natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then do a quick release to release any remaining pressure.
  4. Stir the oatmeal and serve hot with toppings of your choice.

5. Versatile Instant Pot Hard-Boiled Eggs

Hard-boiled eggs are a kitchen staple, and the Instant Pot delivers perfectly cooked eggs every time with zero guesswork.

Ingredients:

  • Eggs (as many as you like, up to a dozen)
  • 1 cup water

Directions:

  1. Pour water into the Instant Pot and place the metal trivet inside. Carefully arrange the eggs on the trivet.
  2. Close the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Cook on Manual/High Pressure for 5 minutes.
  3. Allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Immediately transfer the eggs to an ice water bath to stop the cooking process. Peel and enjoy!

Tips for Success with Your Instant Pot

  1. Read the Manual: Familiarize yourself with the buttons and functions of your Instant Pot before cooking. Understanding the basics will make using it much simpler.
  2. Liquid is Essential: The Instant Pot needs liquid to create pressure, so always make sure there’s at least 1 cup of liquid in your recipes.
  3. Don’t Overfill: Never fill your Instant Pot beyond the maximum fill line, especially when cooking foods like rice or beans that expand.
  4. Double Check the Vent: Always ensure the valve is set to “Sealing” when pressure cooking, or the pot won’t build pressure properly.
  5. Natural vs. Quick Release: Some recipes require a natural release to retain texture and flavor, while others benefit from a quick release to stop cooking immediately. Be cautious when releasing steam to avoid burns.

The Instant Pot is a powerful tool that makes cooking accessible, especially for beginners. With minimal effort, you can create satisfying meals that taste like they took hours to prepare. Start with these simple recipes to develop confidence and experiment with new flavors along the way. Before you know it, your Instant Pot will become your ultimate kitchen companion!
