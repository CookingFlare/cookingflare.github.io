Simple Grilled Appetizers to Kick Off Your Fourth of July BBQ


Fourth of July is all about celebrating freedom, community, and the joy of good food. It’s a time when families and friends gather together for a day filled with fun, fireworks, and of course, an incredible feast. If you’re hosting or attending a BBQ this year, you may want to consider starting the celebration with some easy-to-make, crowd-pleasing grilled appetizers. Grilled appetizers are a perfect way to kick off the event and get everyone excited for the main course. Plus, they’re simple to prepare, delicious, and can be tailored to suit various tastes.

Here, we’ll walk you through some mouth-watering ideas for simple grilled appetizers that will have your guests raving and are sure to be a hit at any Fourth of July BBQ. Whether you’re a grilling novice or a seasoned pro, these recipes are easy to execute and require minimal prep.

1. Grilled Caprese Skewers

A classic summer combination, Caprese skewers are simple, colorful, and packed with fresh flavors. This appetizer is a great way to incorporate the vibrant colors of the season and a little bit of Italy into your BBQ. The combination of juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fresh basil drizzled with balsamic glaze offers a bite-sized taste of summer that your guests will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 pack fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Wooden skewers

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves onto the skewers. You can alternate the ingredients to create a beautiful pattern.
  3. Brush each skewer lightly with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until the cheese is slightly melted and the tomatoes are charred but still firm.
  5. Once off the grill, drizzle with balsamic vinegar and serve immediately.

These grilled Caprese skewers are an excellent choice because they are light, refreshing, and perfect for a summer BBQ. They are also vegetarian-friendly and can be made ahead of time for convenience.

2. Grilled Shrimp Cocktail Skewers

For seafood lovers, grilled shrimp cocktail skewers are a winning appetizer. The smoky flavor from the grill enhances the natural sweetness of the shrimp, making this dish irresistible. Paired with a tangy homemade cocktail sauce, these shrimp skewers are sure to make a statement at your Fourth of July BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

For Cocktail Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 2 tbsp horseradish
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp hot sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until well-coated.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto skewers (3-4 shrimp per skewer, depending on size).
  4. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side or until they are pink and opaque.
  5. While the shrimp are grilling, combine all cocktail sauce ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
  6. Once the shrimp are done, remove them from the skewers and serve with a side of cocktail sauce and lemon wedges.

Grilled shrimp cocktail skewers are a great way to elevate your appetizer game without much effort. They are a sophisticated and flavorful option that can be enjoyed by both seafood enthusiasts and casual eaters alike.

3. Grilled Bacon-Wrapped Jalapeño Poppers

Bacon-wrapped jalapeño poppers are the perfect combination of smoky, spicy, and creamy. The heat from the jalapeños is balanced by the cream cheese filling, while the bacon adds a crispy, savory crunch. These poppers are an absolute crowd favorite and one of the most popular grilled appetizers to serve at a BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 12 fresh jalapeños
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 12 strips bacon
  • Toothpicks (for securing the bacon)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice the jalapeños in half lengthwise and remove the seeds and membranes (be careful not to touch your face after handling the peppers).
  3. In a bowl, mix the cream cheese, shredded cheddar, garlic powder, and onion powder together until smooth.
  4. Stuff each jalapeño half with the cream cheese mixture.
  5. Wrap each stuffed jalapeño with a slice of bacon and secure with a toothpick.
  6. Grill the bacon-wrapped jalapeños for about 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally until the bacon is crispy and the cheese is melted.
  7. Serve hot, and watch them disappear!

These poppers are a great way to add a spicy kick to your appetizer spread, and the bacon makes them irresistible. If you want to make them even spicier, feel free to leave some of the seeds in the jalapeños for extra heat.

4. Grilled Vegetable Skewers

For those who prefer vegetarian options, grilled vegetable skewers are a fantastic choice. They are colorful, healthy, and full of flavor. You can choose a variety of veggies, such as bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, mushrooms, and red onions, and grill them to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8 oz mushrooms
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between the different types to create a colorful and balanced skewer.
  3. Brush the veggies with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and fresh herbs.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 3-5 minutes on each side, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy the fresh, smoky flavor.

Grilled vegetable skewers are versatile and easy to prepare. They pair well with just about any main course and are a great way to showcase seasonal produce. You can even customize the vegetable combinations to suit your guests’ preferences.

5. Grilled Mini Quesadillas

Mini quesadillas are a fun and flavorful appetizer that adds a Mexican twist to your BBQ spread. These bite-sized quesadillas are filled with cheese, grilled chicken, or veggies, and cooked to golden perfection on the grill. They are easy to eat, customizable, and perfect for dipping into your favorite salsa or guacamole.

Ingredients:

  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • 1/2 cup grilled chicken, diced (optional)
  • 1/4 cup black beans, drained and rinsed (optional)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salsa, sour cream, or guacamole for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. On half of each tortilla, sprinkle a generous amount of shredded cheese. Add a few pieces of grilled chicken or black beans if desired.
  3. Fold the tortillas in half to create a half-moon shape.
  4. Brush each side of the tortilla with olive oil.
  5. Grill the quesadillas for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and the cheese has melted inside.
  6. Cut the quesadillas into wedges and serve with salsa, sour cream, or guacamole.

These mini quesadillas are perfect for a laid-back, festive BBQ. They’re quick to make, satisfying, and a great way to use up any leftover grilled chicken.

6. Grilled Hummus and Pita Chips

If you’re looking for a lighter appetizer to kick off your Fourth of July celebration, grilled pita chips served with creamy hummus are a fantastic option. The warm, crispy pita chips are perfect for dipping into the smooth, garlicky hummus, and it’s a great way to start the meal with a healthy yet delicious bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pita bread rounds
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice the pita rounds into wedges and brush each wedge with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the pita chips for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are crisp and golden.
  4. Serve the chips with a bowl of hummus for dipping.

This appetizer is incredibly simple but packs a lot of flavor. It’s light, healthy, and perfect for balancing out the richness of other grilled dishes. Plus, it’s a great option for vegan and vegetarian guests.

Conclusion

When planning a Fourth of July BBQ, grilling appetizers is a great way to kick off the festivities. From savory grilled shrimp skewers to spicy bacon-wrapped jalapeño poppers, these appetizers are sure to please your guests and set the tone for a fun-filled day. Plus, many of these recipes can be easily customized to suit different dietary preferences, making them a perfect choice for diverse gatherings.

Grilled appetizers are not only easy to prepare, but they also add an extra layer of flavor and excitement to your BBQ spread. So fire up the grill, try out these simple recipes, and enjoy a memorable Fourth of July celebration with friends and family!
