Simple and Tasty Pancake Recipes for Kids


Are you looking for a fun and delicious breakfast idea for your little ones? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some simple and tasty pancake recipes that are sure to be a hit with kids of all ages. These recipes are easy to make, require minimal ingredients, and can be customized to suit your child’s taste preferences. So let’s get started!

1. Classic Fluffy Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Cooking spray or additional butter for greasing the pan

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or butter.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  7. Serve with your child’s favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, fresh fruits, or whipped cream.

2. Chocolate Chip Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • Cooking spray or additional butter for greasing the pan

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Gently fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or butter.
  6. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve with a sprinkle of additional chocolate chips and a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

3. Banana Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • Cooking spray or additional butter for greasing the pan

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Gently fold in the mashed banana.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or butter.
  6. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve with sliced bananas, a drizzle of honey, or a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Conclusion

Pancakes are a versatile and delicious breakfast option that kids love. With these simple and tasty pancake recipes, you can easily whip up a batch of fluffy, flavorful pancakes for your little ones to enjoy. Whether you prefer classic pancakes, chocolate chip pancakes, or banana pancakes, these recipes are sure to bring a smile to your child’s face. So why not surprise them with a special pancake breakfast this weekend? Happy cooking!

Note: Remember to always supervise young children in the kitchen and ensure they are safe while cooking.
