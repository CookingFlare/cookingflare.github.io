Easy Cookie Recipes for the Holiday Season


The holiday season is a magical time filled with warmth, joy, and, of course, delicious treats! Among the array of festive goodies, cookies hold a special place in our hearts. Whether you’re baking for a family gathering, a cookie swap, or just to enjoy with a cup of hot cocoa, easy cookie recipes are a must-have in every holiday baker’s repertoire. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of simple cookie recipes that are perfect for the holiday season, ensuring that your cookie jar is brimming with delightful flavors.

Classic Sugar Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • Colored sugar or sprinkles for decoration

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Roll the dough into balls and place them on ungreased baking sheets. Flatten slightly and sprinkle with colored sugar or sprinkles.
  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

These classic sugar cookies are versatile and can be decorated in countless ways, making them a favorite for cookie decorating parties.

Chewy Ginger Molasses Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup molasses
  • Additional sugar for rolling

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the egg and molasses until well incorporated.
  5. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until just combined.
  6. Roll the dough into balls and then roll each ball in sugar.
  7. Place on baking sheets, leaving space between each cookie.
  8. Bake for 8-10 minutes, until the edges are set but the centers are still soft.
  9. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

These chewy ginger molasses cookies are packed with warm spices, making them perfect for holiday gatherings.

Peppermint Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup crushed peppermint candies

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth.
  4. Beat in the vanilla extract and eggs until well blended.
  5. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients, then fold in the chocolate chips and crushed peppermint.
  6. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto ungreased baking sheets, spacing them apart.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly browned.
  8. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

These peppermint chocolate chip cookies are a delightful twist on the classic, bringing festive cheer with every bite.

Snickerdoodles

Ingredients

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar (for rolling)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon (for rolling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. In a small bowl, combine the granulated sugar and cinnamon.
  7. Roll the dough into balls, then roll each ball in the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  8. Place on baking sheets and bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are set.
  9. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Snickerdoodles are soft, cinnamon-sugar coated cookies that are a holiday classic, perfect for sharing with loved ones.

Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips and cocoa powder together until smooth. Let cool slightly.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar and oil until well combined.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, stirring after each addition, then stir in the vanilla.
  5. Gradually mix in the melted chocolate mixture.
  6. In another bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  7. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture until fully combined.
  8. Refrigerate the dough for at least 30 minutes.
  9. Roll the chilled dough into balls, then roll each ball in powdered sugar.
  10. Place on baking sheets and bake for 10-12 minutes, until the edges are set but the centers are soft.
  11. Let cool before enjoying.

Chocolate crinkle cookies are visually stunning and offer a rich, chocolatey flavor that is sure to impress.

Holiday Spritz Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 cups unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Colored sugar or sprinkles for decoration

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. In another bowl, mix together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Fill a cookie press with the dough and press onto ungreased baking sheets.
  7. Sprinkle with colored sugar or sprinkles before baking.
  8. Bake for 8-10 minutes, until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

These festive spritz cookies are a delightful addition to any holiday cookie platter, bringing both color and flavor.

Conclusion

The holiday season is the perfect time to indulge in baking and share delicious cookies with family and friends. Whether you prefer classic sugar cookies, spiced ginger molasses, or decadent chocolate variations, these easy cookie recipes will surely bring joy to your gatherings. Not only are these recipes simple to follow, but they also offer a chance to get creative with decorations and flavors.

Baking cookies during the holidays is more than just a culinary activity; it’s a way to create memories, share love, and enjoy the festive spirit. So gather your ingredients, put on your favorite holiday music, and start baking these delightful treats that will make your holiday season even sweeter!

Happy baking!
