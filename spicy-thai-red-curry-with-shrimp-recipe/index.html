Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp Recipe


If you’re a fan of bold flavors, vibrant colors, and a perfect blend of sweet, salty, and spicy, then this Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp recipe is a must-try. This dish is an absolute feast for the senses and will quickly become a favorite in your weekly meal rotation. With succulent shrimp, a rich coconut milk-based curry, and a hint of spiciness from the red curry paste, every bite of this dish is bursting with delicious flavors. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply want a comforting yet exotic meal, this recipe has got you covered.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Shrimp: 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined.
  • Coconut milk: 1 can (13.5 oz) of full-fat coconut milk for richness.
  • Red curry paste: 2-3 tablespoons, depending on your spice preference. Thai red curry paste is essential for this dish.
  • Fish sauce: 2 tablespoons, for that salty, umami kick that enhances the flavor.
  • Brown sugar: 1 tablespoon to balance out the spiciness.
  • Lime juice: 1 tablespoon, freshly squeezed for a zesty and tangy flavor.
  • Kaffir lime leaves: 3-4 leaves, torn into pieces. These add a citrusy aroma and fresh flavor.
  • Bamboo shoots: ½ cup, sliced thin. They add a lovely crunch and texture.
  • Red bell pepper: 1 large, sliced into thin strips.
  • Carrots: 1 large, sliced into thin strips or julienned for color and sweetness.
  • Thai basil leaves: ¼ cup for garnish, offering a fresh and aromatic finish.
  • Vegetable oil: 1-2 tablespoons, for sautéing.
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced.
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon, freshly grated.
  • Chili peppers: 1-2, finely sliced (optional, for extra heat).
  • Jasmine rice: Cooked, to serve the curry over.

Optional Ingredients

  • Baby corn: Adds a delightful crunch and sweetness, sliced thinly.
  • Mushrooms: ½ cup of thinly sliced mushrooms for an earthy taste.
  • Coriander: Fresh leaves for garnish.

Cooking Instructions

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this flavorful Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp.

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

The key to a successful curry is preparation. Before you begin cooking, make sure all your ingredients are ready. Peel and devein the shrimp, slice your vegetables, and have your coconut milk, fish sauce, and curry paste within easy reach. This will allow you to move quickly and efficiently through the cooking process.

Step 2: Heat the Oil and Sauté Aromatics

In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chili peppers (if using). Sauté the aromatics for about 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter. The fragrance of garlic and ginger will immediately fill your kitchen, setting the stage for the delicious curry that’s about to unfold.

Step 3: Add the Red Curry Paste

Once the garlic and ginger are nicely sautéed, add the red curry paste. Stir it into the oil and aromatics, letting it cook for 1-2 minutes. This step is essential because it brings out the depth and complexity of the red curry paste, allowing the spices to bloom. You’ll notice the curry paste darken and become more fragrant as it cooks.

Step 4: Pour in the Coconut Milk

Next, slowly pour in the can of coconut milk, stirring as you go to ensure the curry paste integrates smoothly. The coconut milk will immediately transform the curry base into a creamy, velvety sauce. Stir in the fish sauce, brown sugar, and lime juice, adjusting the sweetness and saltiness to taste. The coconut milk, fish sauce, and sugar create a well-rounded base with the perfect balance of flavors.

Step 5: Add Vegetables and Simmer

Once the coconut milk has been incorporated into the curry, add the sliced red bell pepper, carrots, bamboo shoots, and kaffir lime leaves. Give the curry a good stir, and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still retain some crunch. The kaffir lime leaves will infuse the curry with a subtle citrus aroma that will enhance the overall flavor profile.

Step 6: Add the Shrimp

Now, it’s time to add the star of the dish – the shrimp! Gently slide the shrimp into the curry and stir them in. Let the shrimp cook for 3-4 minutes, or until they turn pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook them, as shrimp can become tough and rubbery if left in the heat for too long.

Step 7: Final Adjustments

Once the shrimp are cooked through, give the curry a final taste and adjust the seasoning. You may want to add a little more fish sauce for saltiness, brown sugar for sweetness, or lime juice for acidity, depending on your preference. You can also add a pinch of salt or more chili peppers for heat, depending on how spicy you like your curry.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Turn off the heat, remove the curry from the stovetop, and discard the kaffir lime leaves. Serve the Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp over a bed of warm, fluffy jasmine rice. Garnish with freshly torn Thai basil leaves and coriander, and serve immediately.

Tips for the Perfect Thai Red Curry

1. Adjust the Spice Level

The beauty of Thai curry is that you can adjust the spice level to suit your taste. If you like it mild, start with 2 tablespoons of red curry paste and reduce the amount of chili peppers. If you love a bit of heat, feel free to add more curry paste or extra chili peppers.

2. Use Fresh Ingredients

While some ingredients like curry paste and coconut milk may come from cans or jars, try to use fresh vegetables and herbs whenever possible. Fresh herbs, like Thai basil, make a world of difference in the final taste.

3. Customize Your Vegetables

This curry is versatile, and you can add whatever vegetables you like. Mushrooms, baby corn, or even zucchini can be great additions. The more colorful your vegetables, the more vibrant the dish will be!

4. Make it Ahead of Time

Curries often taste even better the next day as the flavors have more time to meld together. You can make this dish ahead of time, store it in the refrigerator, and reheat it when you’re ready to serve. Just be sure to add the shrimp in the final cooking stages, as shrimp can become tough if reheated too long.

5. Use High-Quality Shrimp

When choosing shrimp for this dish, opt for fresh, high-quality shrimp. If using frozen shrimp, make sure they’re fully thawed before cooking to avoid watery curry.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • Balanced Flavors: The combination of creamy coconut milk, savory fish sauce, and spicy red curry paste creates a perfect balance of flavors. The sweet heat from the curry paste, the salty umami from the fish sauce, and the richness of coconut milk come together in harmony.

  • Quick and Easy: This dish is quick to prepare, making it perfect for a weeknight dinner. Most of the ingredients are pantry staples, and the cooking process is simple and straightforward.

  • Customizable: Feel free to adjust the heat, swap vegetables, or even use chicken or tofu instead of shrimp. This recipe is highly adaptable to your personal preferences.

  • Comforting and Satisfying: The creamy sauce, paired with the tender shrimp and vegetables, is incredibly comforting and satisfying. It’s like a warm hug in a bowl, perfect for any day.

Pairing Suggestions

This dish pairs beautifully with a variety of sides, but the classic choice is steamed jasmine rice, which soaks up the delicious sauce. You can also serve it with sticky rice, quinoa, or even a simple cucumber salad to balance out the spiciness. A refreshing Thai iced tea or coconut water makes for a perfect beverage to complement the rich, bold flavors of this curry.

Conclusion

Making Spicy Thai Red Curry with Shrimp at home is a wonderful way to enjoy the rich and bold flavors of Thai cuisine without having to visit a restaurant. With a few key ingredients and a bit of time, you can create a restaurant-quality dish in your own kitchen. Whether you’re new to Thai cooking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is a crowd-pleaser that’s sure to impress.

Give this recipe a try, and enjoy the explosion of flavors that come with every bite. Once you taste the creamy, spicy, and aromatic combination of shrimp and Thai red curry, you’ll be hooked. Happy cooking!
