Easy Recipe for Chinese Pork and Cabbage Stir-Fry


Chinese cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, thoughtful preparation, and well-balanced textures. Among the many traditional dishes, pork and cabbage stir-fry stands out as a classic that combines simplicity with taste. Whether you’re an experienced home cook or someone just looking to try something new, this dish is perfect for a quick and satisfying meal. With tender pork, crunchy cabbage, and aromatic seasonings, you can bring a taste of China to your kitchen without a complicated process.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Pork and cabbage stir-fry is a fantastic one-pan dish that’s easy to prepare and packed with flavor. It’s also very versatile. You can tweak it according to your preferences or even substitute ingredients based on what you have in your pantry. Because this recipe doesn’t require any exotic tools, it’s perfect for home cooks who want to prepare authentic Chinese food using just a wok or a large skillet.

This stir-fry works beautifully as a standalone meal or paired with steamed rice or noodles, making it a go-to option for when you need a quick yet nourishing dinner.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this recipe, simplicity is key. These ingredients are staples in Chinese cooking and are easy to find at any grocery store.

Main Ingredients:

  • Pork: 1 pound (450g) of thinly sliced pork. Pork shoulder or tenderloin works best for its tenderness and flavor, but you can use whatever cut you prefer.
  • Cabbage: About 6 cups of shredded green cabbage. Napa cabbage is a great alternative, offering slightly sweeter tones.
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced. Garlic enhances the aroma and depth of the dish.
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon of fresh, finely minced ginger. Adds warmth and a distinct flavor.
  • Carrots (optional): 1 medium carrot, julienned or thinly sliced, for added crunch and color.

For the Marinade:

  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons for a rich umami base.
  • Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry): 1 tablespoon for added flavor depth.
  • Cornstarch: 1 teaspoon to coat the pork for improved texture.
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon for a nutty aroma.

For the Sauce:

  • Oyster sauce: 2 tablespoons to provide a savory touch.
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons for saltiness and depth.
  • Chicken broth (or water): 2 tablespoons to loosen the sauce.
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon for balance.

Optional Garnishes:

  • Green onions: Thinly sliced for added freshness.
  • Sesame seeds: Toasted for a nutty crunch.

Preparation

Step 1: Prep the Pork

Start by slicing the pork thinly against the grain to ensure tender bites. In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, and cornstarch to create your marinade. Toss the pork slices in the mixture and let it marinate for at least 15–20 minutes. This step helps tenderize the pork and infuse it with flavor.

Step 2: Prep the Vegetables

While the pork marinates, shred the cabbage into thin slices. You can peel away individual leaves, stack them, and slice them into strips for even cuts. If you’re using carrots, julienne them into matchstick shapes or thin ribbons. Mince the garlic and ginger so they’re ready for cooking.

Cooking Instructions

Step 3: Heat the Pan

Place a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of oil—vegetable or peanut oil works best as they have a high smoke point. Once the oil shimmers, you’re ready to start cooking.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Pork

Add the marinated pork into the pan in a single layer. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until the pork starts to brown and is almost cooked through. Remove the pork from the pan and set it aside.

Step 5: Sauté Garlic and Ginger

Without cleaning the pan, add another tablespoon of oil if necessary. Toss in the minced garlic and ginger, stirring for about 30 seconds or until aromatic. Be careful not to burn them since they cook quickly.

Step 6: Cook the Vegetables

Add the shredded cabbage and optional carrot slices to the pan. Stir-fry everything for 3–5 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid overcooking. The cabbage will begin to soften, but it should still retain some crunch for texture.

Step 7: Combine Ingredients

Return the cooked pork to the pan. Pour the sauce mixture (oyster sauce, soy sauce, chicken broth, and sugar) over the pork and vegetables. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes to blend flavors and coat everything evenly. The sauce should slightly thicken as it cooks.

Step 8: Finish and Serve

Taste the stir-fry and adjust seasoning if necessary. You can add more soy sauce for saltiness or a touch of sugar if you enjoy sweetness. Transfer the stir-fry to a serving dish and garnish with green onions and toasted sesame seeds for a polished presentation. Serve hot with steamed rice, fried rice, or noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • Protein Alternatives: If you don’t eat pork, substitute chicken, shrimp, or tofu. The marinade works well with all of them.
  • Veggie Swap: Beyond cabbage, you can add bell peppers, bok choy, or mushrooms for a different flavor profile.
  • Spicy Twist: Toss in a few dried chili peppers or a teaspoon of chili paste for heat.
  • Batch Cooking: Double the recipe and enjoy leftovers the next day. Reheat in a pan with a splash of water to revive the flavors.

Why Choose Homemade Over Takeout?

Making pork and cabbage stir-fry at home allows you to control the ingredients, especially salt and oil levels, making it healthier and more personalized than restaurant versions. Homemade stir-fry also lets you experiment with flavors and discover what works best for your palate.

Plus, there’s the added joy of creating a meal entirely from scratch—turning simple ingredients into something that feels elevated. If you’re hosting guests or family meals, this dish offers a perfect balance of flavor, ease of preparation, and cultural flair.

Pairing Ideas

For a complete meal, consider pairing pork and cabbage stir-fry with:

  • Classic Steamed Rice: The neutral flavor complements the savory stir-fry perfectly.
  • Egg Fried Rice: A slightly richer pairing that works well with dishes like this one.
  • Hot and Sour Soup: Another favorite in Chinese cuisine, offering a warming side to your meal.

Conclusion

Chinese pork and cabbage stir-fry is a simple yet satisfying dish that showcases the essence of Chinese cooking: bold flavors, fresh ingredients, and easy preparation. Whether you’re making it for a cozy dinner or doubling up for a meal prep session, this recipe is bound to become a staple in your home.

Give it a try tonight and savor the balance of tender pork, crispy vegetables, and fragrant sauces. Your kitchen will smell amazing, and your taste buds will thank you!
