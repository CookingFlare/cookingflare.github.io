Savor the Flavors of Authentic Cantonese Sweet and Sour Fish


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to explore the delightful flavors of authentic Cantonese Sweet and Sour Fish. This dish is a perfect blend of tangy and sweet, with a hint of spice that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

To create this mouthwatering dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g white fish fillets (such as tilapia or cod)
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks, drained

Instructions

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, let’s dive into the cooking process:

  1. In a bowl, combine the cornstarch, flour, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  2. Coat the fish fillets in the cornstarch mixture, ensuring they are evenly coated on all sides.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully add the coated fish fillets and cook until golden brown and crispy. This should take about 3-4 minutes per side. Once cooked, remove the fish from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the bell peppers, onion, and minced garlic. Stir-fry for about 2 minutes, or until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the rice vinegar, ketchup, soy sauce, and sugar until well combined. Pour the sauce mixture into the skillet with the vegetables. Stir well to coat the vegetables evenly.
  6. Add the pineapple chunks to the skillet and continue to cook for another 2 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  7. Finally, return the cooked fish fillets to the skillet and gently toss them with the sauce and vegetables. Make sure each piece is well-coated.
  8. Remove the skillet from heat and transfer the Sweet and Sour Fish to a serving dish.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier version, you can bake the fish fillets instead of frying them. Simply place the coated fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake at 400°F (200°C) for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through and crispy.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables in this dish based on your preference. You can add carrots, snow peas, or even baby corn to enhance the flavors and textures.
  • For an extra kick of spice, add a teaspoon of chili sauce or red pepper flakes to the sauce mixture.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water and add it to the skillet during the last minute of cooking. Stir well to thicken the sauce.

Final Thoughts

Cantonese Sweet and Sour Fish is a true delight for your taste buds. The combination of crispy fish, tangy sauce, and colorful vegetables creates a harmonious balance of flavors. This authentic Chinese dish is perfect for a family dinner or a special gathering with friends. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and savor the flavors of this Cantonese classic. Enjoy!
