Easy Holiday Hacks: Time-Saving Recipes for Busy Celebrations


The holiday season is a time for joy, laughter, and creating lasting memories with loved ones. However, it can also be a hectic and stressful period, especially for those responsible for preparing meals and hosting gatherings. If you find yourself juggling a busy schedule but still want to serve delicious and impressive dishes, these time-saving recipes will be your holiday lifesavers.

1. Instant Pot Cranberry Orange Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in ham
  • 1 cup cranberry juice
  • 1/2 cup orange marmalade
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. Place the ham in the Instant Pot.
  2. In a bowl, mix the cranberry juice, orange marmalade, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and ground cloves.
  3. Pour the mixture over the ham.
  4. Close the Instant Pot and set it to high pressure for 15 minutes.
  5. Once done, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes before quick-releasing the remaining pressure.
  6. Transfer the ham to a serving platter, then pour the glaze from the Instant Pot over the ham before serving.

This succulent and flavorful ham will be a showstopper at your holiday table, and the best part is that the Instant Pot does all the work, leaving you with more time to spend with your guests.

2. One-Pan Garlic Herb Roasted Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • Assorted vegetables (such as baby potatoes, carrots, Brussels sprouts, and bell peppers)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Wash and chop the vegetables into bite-sized pieces and place them on a baking sheet.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, minced garlic, dried thyme, and dried rosemary.
  4. Drizzle the herb mixture over the vegetables and toss to coat evenly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and golden brown.

This simple yet elegant side dish not only adds a burst of color to your holiday spread but also requires minimal effort, giving you more time to savor the festive atmosphere.

3. Quick and Easy Tiramisu Trifle

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/3 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 package ladyfinger cookies
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form.
  2. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to whip until stiff peaks form.
  3. Gently fold in the mascarpone cheese until smooth and creamy.
  4. In a shallow dish, combine the cooled coffee and coffee liqueur.
  5. Dip the ladyfinger cookies into the coffee mixture and arrange a layer of dipped cookies in the bottom of a trifle dish.
  6. Spread a layer of the whipped cream mixture over the cookies.
  7. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of the whipped cream mixture on top.
  8. Dust the top with cocoa powder.
  9. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

This delectable tiramisu trifle offers a delightful twist on the classic Italian dessert and can be assembled in no time, allowing you to indulge in a sweet treat without spending hours in the kitchen.

Conclusion

With these easy holiday hacks, you can spend less time stressing in the kitchen and more time enjoying the festive season with your loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or an intimate dinner, these time-saving recipes are sure to impress without keeping you tied to the stove. Embrace the joy of the holidays and savor every moment, knowing that delicious meals can be effortlessly prepared, leaving you with more opportunities to create cherished memories with family and friends. Happy holidays and happy cooking!
