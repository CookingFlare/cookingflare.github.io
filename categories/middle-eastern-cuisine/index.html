Tantalize Your Tastebuds with Middle Eastern Muhammara Crostini

If you’re looking to add a touch of Middle Eastern flair to your appetizer game, then look no further than Muhammara Crostini. This delightful dish is a perfect blend of smoky, spicy, and savory flavors that will leave your guests craving for more. Muhammara, a traditional Syrian red pepper and walnut dip, takes center stage in this recipe, bringing a burst of exotic flavors to a classic Italian appetizer. Ingredients 1 loaf of French baguette, sliced 1 cup walnuts, toasted 2/3 cup breadcrumbs 3-4 roasted red bell peppers 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses 3 tablespoons olive oil Salt to taste Fresh parsley, for garnish Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Muhammara In a food processor, combine the toasted walnuts, breadcrumbs, roasted red bell peppers, minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper, and pomegranate molasses. Pulse the mixture until it forms a coarse paste. While the food processor is running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the mixture becomes smooth and well combined. Season with salt to taste and give it a final pulse to incorporate the seasoning. Step 2: Prepare the Crostini Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet and brush them lightly with olive oil. Toast the baguette slices in the preheated oven for 8-10 minutes, or until they are golden and crisp. Step 3: Assemble the Muhammara Crostini Once the crostini are toasted, spread a generous amount of the prepared Muhammara on each slice. Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness. Serving Suggestions These Muhammara Crostini are best served fresh and can be the perfect addition to any gathering or as a delightful snack for a cozy night in.
