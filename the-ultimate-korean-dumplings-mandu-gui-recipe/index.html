The Ultimate Korean Dumplings: Mandu Gui Recipe


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’ve probably come across mandu at some point. These delectable Korean dumplings are a staple in Korean households and are enjoyed by people all over the world. While mandu can be steamed, boiled, or pan-fried, one of the most popular ways to prepare them is by making Mandu Gui, a delicious grilled version that’s sure to tantalize your taste buds. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make the ultimate Korean dumplings, Mandu Gui, from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup finely chopped kimchi
  • 1 cup finely chopped firm tofu
  • 1 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Dumpling Wrappers:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped green onion

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, kimchi, tofu, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, black pepper, and salt. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the filling while you prepare the dumpling wrappers.

  2. Make the Dumpling Wrappers:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, water, and salt. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  3. Form the Dumplings:

    • After the dough has rested, roll it out on a floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a round cutter or a glass to cut out circles of dough. Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling and pinch the edges to seal, creating a half-moon shape.

  4. Grill the Dumplings:

    • Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grill grates or pan. Place the dumplings on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy.

  5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sesame seeds, sugar, and green onion. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Transfer the grilled mandu to a serving platter and serve them hot with the dipping sauce on the side.

Tips and Tricks

  • To make the dumpling wrappers ahead of time, you can store them in the refrigerator for up to 2 days or freeze them for later use.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can replace the ground pork with finely chopped mushrooms or tofu.
  • Experiment with different fillings such as shrimp, cabbage, or glass noodles to create your own unique mandu recipe.

Final Thoughts

Mandu Gui is a delightful dish that brings together the wonderful flavors of Korean cuisine in a simple and satisfying way. Whether you’re serving them as an appetizer, a snack, or a main course, these grilled Korean dumplings are sure to impress your family and friends. So, roll up your sleeves, get creative with the fillings, and enjoy the process of making and savoring these delicious mandu. Happy cooking!
