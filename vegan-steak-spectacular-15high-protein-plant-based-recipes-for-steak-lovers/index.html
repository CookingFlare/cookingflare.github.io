Vegan 'Steak' Spectacular: 15High-Protein Plant-Based Recipes for Steak Lovers


For those who love the hearty satisfaction of a steak but prefer a plant-based diet, the idea of indulging in a rich, flavorful, and filling “steak” without animal products may seem daunting. However, with the right ingredients and cooking techniques, creating a vegan steak experience that rivals the real thing is entirely possible. Whether you’re transitioning to a plant-based lifestyle, or you’re simply looking to reduce your meat consumption, these 15 high-protein vegan “steak” recipes will prove that the magic of a great steak doesn’t have to come from a cow.

In this article, we will explore a range of recipes that use whole food plant-based ingredients such as seitan, tempeh, mushrooms, and legumes to create delicious, satisfying, and protein-packed vegan steaks. From smoky grilled steaks to tender “filets” and sizzling “rib-eyes,” each recipe is designed to give you the flavor and texture of steak without compromising on taste or nutrition.

1. Seitan Ribeye Steak

Seitan, also known as wheat meat, is a fantastic plant-based protein source with a chewy, meaty texture that works wonderfully in steak dishes. This recipe mimics the hearty bite of a traditional rib-eye steak while being 100% vegan. Seitan is made from gluten, which is the protein in wheat, and it’s packed with protein.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups vital wheat gluten
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • ¼ cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp liquid smoke (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the vital wheat gluten, nutritional yeast, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together vegetable broth, soy sauce, olive oil, tahini, and liquid smoke.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5 minutes to develop the gluten.
  4. Shape the dough into a steak shape, about 1 inch thick.
  5. Steam the seitan steaks for 30-40 minutes until firm and cooked through.
  6. Grill or pan-fry the seitan steaks for 3-5 minutes on each side, until crispy and golden brown.
  7. Serve with your favorite vegan sides and enjoy!

2. Tempeh Filet Mignon

Tempeh, made from fermented soybeans, offers a dense and hearty texture that works beautifully as a high-protein, plant-based alternative to filet mignon. This recipe brings together the umami-rich flavors of balsamic vinegar and garlic to create a sophisticated, indulgent vegan steak.

Ingredients:

  • 2 blocks of tempeh
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh thyme (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Slice the tempeh blocks into 1-inch thick slices.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, olive oil, maple syrup, garlic, and black pepper.
  3. Place the tempeh slices in the marinade, and let them sit for at least 30 minutes (preferably overnight for more flavor).
  4. Heat a pan over medium-high heat and cook the tempeh slices for 4-5 minutes on each side, until crispy and caramelized.
  5. Garnish with fresh thyme and serve with roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes for a truly luxurious meal.

3. Portobello Mushroom Steaks

Portobello mushrooms are naturally thick, meaty, and packed with umami flavors. When grilled, they become tender, juicy, and perfect for creating vegan “steaks.” This simple recipe lets the mushrooms shine with minimal ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Remove the stems from the portobello mushrooms and gently clean the caps with a damp cloth.
  2. In a bowl, mix together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.
  3. Brush the mushrooms with the marinade and let them sit for 10 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  4. Preheat the grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the mushrooms for about 5-7 minutes per side, until they are tender and have a nice char.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with quinoa or a simple salad.

4. Chickpea Steak with Lemon Herb Sauce

Chickpeas are not just for salads or hummus—they can also be transformed into a protein-rich, flavorful steak alternative. This chickpea steak is paired with a zesty lemon herb sauce that brings brightness to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • ½ cup oat flour (or chickpea flour)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Lemon Herb Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the cooked chickpeas, oat flour, olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  2. Shape the mixture into two “steak” patties.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium heat and cook the chickpea steaks for 4-5 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.
  4. For the lemon herb sauce, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, and parsley until smooth.
  5. Drizzle the sauce over the chickpea steaks and serve with steamed broccoli or roasted potatoes.

5. Lentil and Mushroom “Steak”

Lentils, combined with mushrooms, create a flavorful and hearty vegan steak that’s packed with protein and fiber. This recipe is not only delicious but also budget-friendly and easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked green or brown lentils
  • 1 cup finely chopped mushrooms (button or cremini)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, sauté the mushrooms in olive oil until soft and browned, about 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils, sautéed mushrooms, soy sauce, nutritional yeast, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Use your hands to shape the mixture into thick, steak-like patties.
  4. Pan-fry the lentil steaks in a skillet over medium heat for 5 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.
  5. Serve with mashed sweet potatoes or a simple side salad.

6. Jackfruit Steak with Spicy BBQ Sauce

Jackfruit, a tropical fruit often used in vegan pulled pork recipes, can also be transformed into a tender, shreddable steak. When paired with a smoky, spicy BBQ sauce, it makes for a delectable vegan steak alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans young green jackfruit in brine, drained and shredded
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the shredded jackfruit and sauté for 5-7 minutes until it begins to crisp up.
  2. Add the barbecue sauce, smoked paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to coat the jackfruit evenly in the sauce.
  3. Continue to cook for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the jackfruit to become tender.
  4. Serve the jackfruit steak on a bun with vegan coleslaw or as a main dish with a side of grilled vegetables.

7. Tempeh & Tofu Steak Fajitas

For a delicious twist on classic fajitas, combine tempeh and tofu for a double-protein, plant-based steak filling. The smoky fajita seasoning, combined with crispy tempeh and creamy tofu, creates a flavorful meal that’s sure to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and sliced into thin strips
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Tortillas and toppings (avocado, salsa, cilantro, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, toss the tempeh and tofu strips with olive oil, soy sauce, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat a pan over medium heat and cook the tempeh and tofu for 4-5 minutes per side, until golden and crispy.
  3. Serve the tempeh and tofu steak in tortillas with your favorite fajita toppings like avocado, salsa, and fresh cilantro.

8. Grilled Cauliflower “Steaks”

Cauliflower can be transformed into thick, hearty “steaks” that hold up beautifully on the grill. This simple yet flavorful recipe highlights the natural sweetness of cauliflower, while smoky spices make it extra delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cauliflower head into 1-inch thick “steaks.”
  2. Brush both sides of the cauliflower steaks with olive oil and sprinkle with smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the cauliflower steaks for 4-5 minutes on each side, until tender and slightly charred.
  4. Serve with a drizzle of tahini sauce or your favorite vegan dressing.

9. Chickpea & Quinoa Vegan “Steak” Salad

This protein-packed vegan steak salad is loaded with flavor and nutrients. The combination of chickpeas, quinoa, and roasted vegetables creates a hearty and filling meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1 cup mixed greens (spinach, arugula, etc.)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • Lemon-tahini dressing (for drizzling)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, mixed greens, cucumber, tomato, and bell pepper.
  2. Drizzle with lemon-tahini dressing and toss to combine.
  3. Serve chilled or at room temperature for a refreshing and filling steak salad.

10. Seitan and Mushroom Steaks with Red Wine Sauce

Seitan’s firm texture makes it perfect for pairing with rich, savory sauces. In this recipe, seitan steaks are smothered in a luxurious red wine sauce, combined with mushrooms for an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 seitan steaks (from the earlier recipe)
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup red wine (or vegetable broth)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tbsp vegan butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Cook the seitan steaks for 3-4 minutes on each side until crispy and golden.
  2. Remove the steaks from the pan and set aside. In the same pan, add the mushrooms and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the red wine, thyme, salt, and pepper, and simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce reduces by half.
  4. Stir in the vegan butter to create a rich, velvety sauce.
  5. Serve the seitan steaks with the red wine sauce and your favorite vegetables.

11. Smoky Tempeh “Steak” Tacos

These smoky tempeh tacos are perfect for taco night, offering a satisfying, protein-rich vegan steak that’s packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of tempeh, sliced into strips
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Corn tortillas
  • Toppings (salsa, avocado, cilantro)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the olive oil, soy sauce, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and chili powder.
  2. Toss the tempeh strips in the marinade and let them sit for 15 minutes.
  3. Heat a pan over medium heat and cook the tempeh strips for 5-7 minutes, until crispy.
  4. Serve the tempeh “steak” in tortillas with salsa, avocado, and cilantro.

12. Vegan Steak and Potatoes

A classic combination, vegan steak and potatoes is a comforting, protein-packed meal that’s sure to please any steak lover. The rich flavors of seitan or tempeh pair beautifully with crispy roasted potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 seitan steaks or tempeh steaks (your choice)
  • 4 large potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the potato wedges in olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper, and roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  3. While the potatoes roast, cook your seitan or tempeh steaks in a skillet until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve the steaks alongside the roasted potatoes for a satisfying and hearty meal.

13. Mushroom Wellington with Seitan

For a special occasion or just a delicious weekend meal, mushroom Wellington made with seitan offers a luxurious and impressive alternative to the traditional beef Wellington.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of seitan
  • 1 cup mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 sheets puff pastry (vegan)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté the mushrooms in olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the seitan and soy sauce, and cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Roll out the puff pastry and place the mushroom-seitan mixture in the center.
  4. Fold the pastry around the filling and bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.
  5. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables.

14. Grilled Tofu Steaks with Chimichurri Sauce

For a tangy, zesty twist, these tofu steaks are grilled to perfection and served with a bright chimichurri sauce made from fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, sliced into 1-inch steaks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Chimichurri Sauce:

  • ½ cup parsley
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess moisture, then slice it into 1-inch steaks.
  2. Marinate the tofu steaks in olive oil, soy sauce, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper for 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan and cook the tofu steaks for 3-5 minutes on each side until charred and crispy.
  4. To make the chimichurri sauce, combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth.
  5. Serve the tofu steaks with the chimichurri sauce and enjoy!

15. BBQ Seitan “Steak” Sandwiches

For a vegan take on a classic BBQ steak sandwich, this recipe uses seitan for its meat-like texture and is paired with tangy barbecue sauce and fresh veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 2 seitan steaks
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 whole-grain sandwich buns
  • Sliced onions
  • Lettuce, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Grill or pan-fry the seitan steaks for 3-4 minutes on each side.
  2. Brush the seitan steaks with BBQ sauce and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until caramelized.
  3. Serve the BBQ seitan in sandwich buns with onions and lettuce.

With these 15 high-protein vegan steak recipes, you can enjoy the savory flavors and hearty textures of a classic steak meal—without any of the animal products. Whether you’re craving a grilled portobello, a seitan rib-eye, or a tempeh fajita, these recipes offer endless possibilities for delicious, plant-based steak alternatives. Try them all and discover the bold, meaty flavors that plant-based cooking has to offer!
