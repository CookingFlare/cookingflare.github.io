Classic Italian Pasta Recipes for Novice Cooks


If you’re a novice cook looking to impress your friends and family with delicious Italian cuisine, mastering some classic pasta recipes is a great place to start. Italian cuisine is known for its simplicity and bold flavors, making it an ideal choice for beginner cooks. In this blog post, we’ll explore three classic Italian pasta recipes that are easy to prepare and guaranteed to delight your taste buds.

Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 4 ounces pancetta, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the eggs, grated cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper in a bowl. Set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta over medium heat until it becomes crispy and golden. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve a cup of the pasta water and then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the pancetta and garlic, tossing to combine.
  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, stirring vigorously to coat the pasta. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.
  7. Season with salt and extra black pepper if needed. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parsley.

Fettuccine Alfredo

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces fettuccine
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Nutmeg (optional)
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fettuccine in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and butter over medium heat until the butter has melted.
  3. Add the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese to the saucepan, stirring constantly until the cheese has melted and the sauce has thickened.
  4. Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired.
  5. Add the cooked fettuccine to the sauce, tossing to coat the pasta evenly.
  6. Transfer the fettuccine Alfredo to a serving dish and garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Pasta Puttanesca

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 anchovy fillets, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup pitted Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1/4 cup capers, drained
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, chopped anchovies, and red pepper flakes, cooking until the garlic becomes fragrant and the anchovies begin to dissolve.
  3. Stir in the chopped olives and capers, cooking for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, bringing the sauce to a simmer. Cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Season the sauce with salt and black pepper to taste.
  6. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet, tossing to coat the pasta with the flavorful sauce.
  7. Transfer the pasta puttanesca to a serving platter and garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

These classic Italian pasta recipes are perfect for novice cooks who want to explore the rich flavors of Italian cuisine. Whether you choose to create the creamy indulgence of Fettuccine Alfredo, the bold and spicy Pasta Puttanesca, or the comforting simplicity of Spaghetti Carbonara, you’re sure to delight your taste buds and impress your dinner guests. Buon appetito!
