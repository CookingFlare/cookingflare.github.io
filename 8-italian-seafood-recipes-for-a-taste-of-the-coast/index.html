8 Italian Seafood Recipes for a Taste of the Coast


Italian coastal cuisine boasts an array of flavors and ingredients inspired by the Mediterranean Sea. When it comes to seafood, Italy offers a wide range of options, from simple to elaborate dishes that showcase the richness of its marine bounty. Here are 8 Italian seafood recipes to tempt your taste buds, transporting you to the coast without leaving your home. Seafood Risotto alla Pescatora Risotto alla Pescatora is a classic Italian seafood dish that gathers various types of fish and shellfish. The rich flavors and creamy texture of the risotto perfectly partner with the variety of seafood to create a memorable dining experience. Prepare this hearty dish by combining Arborio rice with sautéed onions, garlic, and white wine. Add fish stock, gradually stirring in the liquid, creating a smooth, sticky consistency. Add diced clams, mussels, shrimp, and scallops. Finish with grated Parmesan and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Branzino al Forno con Fagioli This baked sea bass with fava beans and cherry tomatoes is a traditional Italian recipe from the coastal regions of Puglia. The mild flavor of the branzino pairs elegantly with the sweetness of the fava beans and the acidity of the cherry tomatoes, creating a delightful balance of flavors. Season the branzino with salt and pepper, then bake it with sliced fava beans and cherry tomatoes in the oven. Serve with a side of crusty bread to mop up the juices. Fritto Misto di Mare Fritto Misto di Mare is a tossed mix of fried seafood and vegetables. This Italian classic can be found in many seafood restaurants along the coast. The dish combines various types of seafood, such as shrimp, scallops, and squid, with vegetables like zucchini and bell peppers. Simply marinate the seafood and vegetables in a mixture of olive oil, garlic, and herbs, then coat with a light batter. Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy, and serve immediately, garnished with a squeeze of lemon. Cioppino alla Romana This Italian-American fish stew gained popularity in the coastal towns of California, but its roots remain firmly planted in Italy. Cioppino alla Romana is a hearty, flavorful fish stew originating from the Tuscan coast. Combine tender chunks of fish (such as halibut, hake, or cod), shellfish (like mussels and clams), onions, garlic, celery, and tomatoes in a flavorful broth. Season with fennel seeds and a pinch of saffron for added depth of flavor. Serve with a sprinkle of parsley and a side of crusty bread to mop up the juices. Calamari Ripieni con Spinaci These stuffed squid rings are a staple of Italian coastal cuisine. A delicate filling of spinach, garlic, and feta cheese, wrapped around succulent squid rings, provide a mouthwatering combination of flavors and textures. To prepare, pair squid rings with a rump of spinach and creamy feta cheese, then tumble the mixture in a skillet with garlic and olive oil until the spinach is wilted. Fill the squid rings with the spinach mixture, then seal with a toothpick and bake in the oven until golden brown. Zuppa di Pesce alla Napoletana This Neapolitan fish stew gained fame for its simplicity and bold flavors. The broth, flavored with tomatoes, garlic, and herbs, serves as the perfect base for tender chunks of fish and shellfish. Add the seafood to the broth during the last minute of cooking to preserve its texture and color. Season with grated lemon zest and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil to complete this classic Italian dish. Fritto di Ricci Fritto di Ricci, a popular Italian appetizer from the coastal regions, consists of fried sea urchin and vegetables. The delicate texture of the sea urchin pairs surprisingly well with crispy fried vegetables like zucchini and bell peppers. Prepare the sea urchin and vegetables by coating them with a light batter, then fry in hot oil until crispy. Serve immediately with a squeeze of lemon. Polpo alla Mediterranea This spicy scorpionfish stew is an authentic Italian recipe inspired by the rich Mediterranean diet. A mix of vegetables and herbs creates a profound depth of flavor, while the spiciness of the sauce adds a lively kick. Combine fish pieces (such as scorpionfish, swordfish, or tuna), onions, garlic, and bell peppers in a flavorful sauce, seasoned with a pinch of saffron and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes. Serve with a side of crusty bread to mop up the juices. These Italian seafood recipes form a palette of flavors inspired by the Mediterranean coast. By cooking and experimenting with these recipes, you can taste the hearty flavors of Italy's floating coastline.
