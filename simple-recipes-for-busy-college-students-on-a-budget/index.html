Simple Recipes for Busy College Students on a Budget


Being a college student means balancing studies, extracurricular activities, social life, and sometimes a job, all while trying to eat healthy and delicious food. But the biggest challenge for many students is sticking to a budget while preparing meals. Many students find themselves reaching for instant noodles or takeout because they seem like the quickest and cheapest options. However, with a bit of creativity and some basic cooking skills, you can prepare simple, nutritious, and affordable meals that will keep you satisfied and energized throughout the day.

Here’s a collection of budget-friendly, easy-to-make recipes that are perfect for busy college students. These dishes are quick, nutritious, and won’t break the bank.

1. One-Pot Pasta with Tomato Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of pasta (spaghetti, penne, or any type you prefer)
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onions and garlic: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook for 2-3 minutes until they become soft. Add minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute.
  2. Add tomatoes and seasoning: Pour in the canned diced tomatoes (with their juice), dried basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir well and let it simmer for 5 minutes to combine the flavors.
  3. Cook the pasta: Add the pasta to the pot along with 4 cups of water. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for about 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally until the pasta is cooked and the sauce has thickened.
  4. Serve and garnish: Serve the pasta hot, topped with freshly grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

This one-pot pasta recipe is not only super easy but also a great way to have a comforting meal in under 30 minutes. The ingredients are simple and inexpensive, making it a perfect go-to dish for busy students.

2. Veggie Stir-Fry with Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (white, brown, or jasmine)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or sesame oil
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, onions, broccoli, etc.)
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (fresh or ground)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Begin by cooking the rice according to package instructions. While it cooks, prepare the stir-fry ingredients.
  2. Prepare the vegetables: Chop your mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. If you’re using frozen veggies, make sure they’re defrosted before cooking.
  3. Cook the stir-fry: In a large skillet or wok, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger, and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the vegetables and cook for 5-7 minutes until they start to soften but still have some crunch.
  4. Scramble the egg (optional): Push the veggies to the side of the skillet and crack an egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg until fully cooked, then mix it in with the vegetables.
  5. Add the rice and soy sauce: Once the rice is done, add it to the skillet with the vegetables and egg. Pour in the soy sauce, and stir everything together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve: Serve the veggie stir-fry hot, and enjoy a flavorful, budget-friendly meal.

This veggie stir-fry is a great way to use up leftover vegetables and rice. You can customize the ingredients based on what’s available in your pantry, and it’s a filling, balanced meal that’s ready in less than 30 minutes.

3. Breakfast Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tortilla (flour or corn)
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or your favorite type)
  • 1/4 cup black beans (canned or cooked)
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • 1 tablespoon sour cream (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil or butter for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Scramble the eggs: In a small bowl, crack the eggs and beat them with a pinch of salt and pepper. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat, and add a small amount of oil or butter. Pour in the eggs and scramble until fully cooked, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Prepare the tortilla: While the eggs cook, warm the tortilla on a separate skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, just until it becomes soft and pliable.
  3. Assemble the burrito: Once the eggs are cooked, place them in the center of the tortilla. Add the shredded cheese, black beans, salsa, and sour cream if using.
  4. Wrap the burrito: Fold in the sides of the tortilla, then roll it up from the bottom to form a tight burrito.
  5. Serve: Cut the burrito in half, and serve with extra salsa or hot sauce on the side if desired.

This breakfast burrito is a great way to start the day or enjoy for lunch. It’s packed with protein from the eggs and beans and can be made in just a few minutes. You can also customize it by adding avocado, spinach, or leftover grilled chicken.

4. Spicy Peanut Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. noodles (any kind of noodles or pasta will work)
  • 3 tablespoons peanut butter (smooth or crunchy)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha or chili paste (optional for heat)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup water (or more for desired consistency)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Chopped peanuts and cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil a pot of water and cook the noodles according to package instructions. Once done, drain and set them aside.
  2. Make the sauce: In a separate bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, sriracha, garlic, and water. Adjust the water based on your preferred sauce consistency (more water for a thinner sauce).
  3. Toss the noodles: In a large bowl, combine the cooked noodles and peanut sauce. Toss well to coat the noodles evenly.
  4. Garnish and serve: Drizzle the sesame oil over the noodles and top with chopped peanuts and fresh cilantro for added flavor and texture.

This dish is rich, savory, and slightly spicy. The peanut butter sauce gives it a creamy texture while the sriracha adds a nice kick. It’s quick to make and can be served cold or warm, making it a versatile option for a busy student.

5. Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or cilantro), chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Chop the cucumber, tomato, and red onion into small pieces.
  2. Mix the ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, tomato, and onion.
  3. Dress the salad: Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Add fresh herbs if desired for extra flavor.
  4. Serve: This salad can be served immediately or stored in the fridge for later.

This chickpea salad is a light, refreshing, and easy-to-make dish that is high in protein and fiber. It’s perfect for a quick lunch or as a side dish to complement other meals.

6. Instant Ramen Hack

Ingredients:

  • 1 pack of instant ramen
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup frozen vegetables (like peas, carrots, or corn)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • 1 green onion, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the ramen: Boil water and cook the ramen noodles according to the instructions on the package. Once done, set aside the seasoning packet.
  2. Add vegetables: When the noodles are about halfway cooked, add the frozen vegetables to the pot. Let them cook until heated through.
  3. Add the egg: Once the noodles and vegetables are ready, crack an egg into the soup and stir gently to break it up. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes until the egg is fully cooked.
  4. Season and serve: Stir in the seasoning packet, soy sauce, and sesame oil for added flavor. Top with green onions if using, and serve hot.

Instant ramen doesn’t have to be bland or unhealthy. By adding an egg and vegetables, you can transform it into a more balanced and delicious meal in just a few minutes.

Conclusion

Being a college student doesn’t mean you have to resort to expensive takeout or unhealthy junk food. With just a few simple ingredients and a little creativity, you can make delicious, healthy meals without breaking the bank. Whether you’re looking for a quick breakfast, a filling lunch, or a comforting dinner, these budget-friendly recipes are perfect for students on the go. They’re not only easy to make, but they’re also customizable, so you can adjust them based on your personal taste and what’s available in your pantry. So next time you’re hungry and short on time, give one of these recipes a try. Your taste buds (and your wallet) will thank you!
