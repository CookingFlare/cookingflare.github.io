Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes: A Classic Side Dish


Greek cuisine is renowned for its flavorful and vibrant dishes, and one classic side dish that perfectly embodies these qualities is Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes. These potatoes are a staple in Greek households and are loved for their crispy exterior, tender interior, and zesty lemony flavor. In this recipe, I will share with you the secrets to achieving the perfect Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until well combined.

  3. Add the potato wedges to the bowl and toss them in the marinade, ensuring that each wedge is coated evenly.

  4. Transfer the potatoes to a baking dish, arranging them in a single layer. Pour the vegetable broth over the potatoes, which will help to keep them moist during the roasting process.

  5. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and roast the potatoes for about 40-45 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside, and fork-tender on the inside. Be sure to flip the potatoes halfway through the cooking time to ensure even browning.

  6. Once the potatoes are cooked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. The combination of the lemon, garlic, and herbs will infuse the potatoes with a delightful Mediterranean aroma.

  7. Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes are a versatile side dish that pairs well with a variety of main courses. Serve them alongside grilled chicken, lamb chops, or fish for a complete Greek-inspired meal. You can also enjoy them on their own as a tasty snack or appetizer.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of flavor, sprinkle some crumbled feta cheese over the roasted potatoes before serving. The creamy and tangy feta complements the citrusy flavors beautifully.
  • If you prefer a crispier texture, you can broil the potatoes for a few minutes after they are done roasting. This will give them a delicious golden crust.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of the dish. Fresh rosemary, dill, or parsley can add a refreshing twist to the potatoes.
  • If you’re looking to make this dish a bit healthier, you can substitute the russet potatoes with sweet potatoes or even cauliflower florets. The lemony marinade works well with a variety of vegetables.

Greek Lemon Roasted Potatoes are a classic side dish that brings the flavors of Greece into your home. With their crispy exterior, tender interior, and zesty lemony taste, these potatoes are sure to impress your family and guests. Enjoy them as a versatile accompaniment to your favorite main courses or savor them as a delicious snack. Give this recipe a try and experience the vibrant and delightful flavors of Greek cuisine.
