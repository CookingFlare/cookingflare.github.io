Smoked Corn and Sausage Chowder


When the weather turns chilly or you simply crave a hearty bowl of comfort, smoked corn and sausage chowder is the perfect dish to warm your soul. Packed with depth of flavor thanks to smoked ingredients and seasoned sausage, this chowder is a true showstopper for family dinners, gatherings, or even an indulgent weeknight treat. Whether served with crusty bread or on its own, this creamy dish is bound to be a hit.

Let’s dive into how you can bring this lightly smoky, savory masterpiece to your table.

Why Smoked Corn and Sausage Chowder Is Irresistible

The magic of smoked corn and sausage chowder lies in the balance of flavors and textures. The natural sweetness and slight smokiness of the corn play beautifully against the robust, seasoned sausage. Velvety potatoes add substance, while celery and onion lend a subtle vegetal undertone that ties the dish together.

Adding smoked corn elevates this chowder from standard fare to a bowl that delivers a unique flavor profile. The smokiness adds layers of complexity without overpowering the other elements. The sausage brings spiced savoriness, rounding out each spoonful. And when cooked to perfection, the creamy base envelopes everything into a complete, harmonious dish.

Ingredients for Smoked Corn and Sausage Chowder

Here’s the list of ingredients you’ll need to create a pot of smoked corn and sausage chowder that will leave everyone asking for seconds:

For the main chowder:

  • 4 ears of fresh corn (husks removed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter (for grilling/smoking corn)
  • 12 oz smoked sausage or kielbasa (sliced into bite-sized rounds)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for cooking sausage)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or half-and-half)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for extra smoky flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)

For optional garnishes:

  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Crumbled bacon
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar works wonderfully)

Preparing the Ingredients

Smoking the Corn

Fresh corn takes on an incredible transformation when smoked. Preheat your grill or smoker to 225°F. Brush the corn with olive oil or melted butter, which prevents it from drying out and assists in even cooking. Place the ears directly on the grates. Close the lid and smoke for about 40 minutes, rotating occasionally, until the kernels turn golden and develop a rich aroma. Once smoked, use a knife to cut the kernels off the cob.

If you don’t have a smoker, grill the corn over medium heat until slightly charred and smoky in flavor. While grilling doesn’t yield the same depth as smoking, it’s a solid alternative.

Cooking the Sausage

Smoked sausage is the heart of this chowder’s richness, but cooking the sausage adds more flavor. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the sliced sausage and cook until lightly browned on both sides. Remove the cooked sausage and set aside, leaving any rendered fat in the pot for sautéing vegetables.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Smoked Corn and Sausage Chowder

1. Sauté the Aromatics

In the same pot where you browned the sausage, add diced onion, celery, and garlic. Stir frequently, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes. This step releases the natural sweetness of the vegetables and enhances the base of the chowder.

2. Build the Base

Add the cubed potatoes, bay leaf, and chicken or vegetable broth to the pot. Bring everything to a gentle boil. Lower the heat to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are fork-tender, typically 10–15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

3. Blend for Creaminess

If you enjoy a thicker chowder, scoop out half of the soup and blend it using an immersion blender or stand blender. Return the blended portion to the pot and stir. This gives the chowder a velvety texture without adding any extra starch or flour.

4. Add Smoked Corn and Sausage

With the creamy consistency achieved, stir in smoked corn kernels and the browned sausage. Let the chowder simmer for another 5 minutes, so the flavors meld beautifully.

5. Incorporate Heavy Cream

Reduce the heat to low and pour in the heavy cream. Stir gently to combine, ensuring the cream doesn’t boil (to prevent curdling). Taste the chowder and adjust the seasoning by adding salt, pepper, and smoked paprika if desired. The paprika amplifies the smoky essence for those who love bold flavors.

Serving Tips and Pairings

Once the chowder reaches its creamy perfection, ladle it into bowls and garnish to your liking. Fresh parsley adds brightness, crumbled bacon amps up the smokiness, and shredded cheese offers an indulgent finish.

Pair your smoked corn and sausage chowder with crusty bread, cornbread, or even oyster crackers for dipping. This hearty dish also works beautifully with a crisp green salad on the side, adding a refreshing contrast to the thick, creamy chowder.

How to Store and Reheat Leftovers

Smoked corn and sausage chowder stores wonderfully, making it a great meal prep option. Allow the chowder to cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. It will keep in the refrigerator for up to four days or the freezer for three months.

To reheat, warm in a pot over low heat until heated through. If the chowder thickens as it cools, add a splash of broth or heavy cream during reheating to loosen it without compromising the flavor.

Final Thoughts

Smoked corn and sausage chowder is a bowl of pure comfort that combines smoky, savory, and creamy elements into a cohesive masterpiece. Whether you’re feeding a crowd or treating yourself to homemade goodness, this chowder is sure to impress. With every spoonful, you’ll savor the smoky sweetness of corn balanced by the spiced richness of sausage.

Make it tonight and enjoy a dish that’s as satisfying to prepare as it is to devour.
