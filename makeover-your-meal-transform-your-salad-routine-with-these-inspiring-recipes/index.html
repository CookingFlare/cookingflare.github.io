Makeover your Meal: Transform Your Salad Routine with these Inspiring Recipes


Salads are often viewed as the ‘boring’ meal option, primarily because of a lack of creativity in their composition. People are so familiar with the typical salad, built around leaf greens and dry toppings, they forget how much flavor and variety they can accommodate! Dive into this vibrant world of salad-making as we reveal five mouthwatering recipes that will have you ditching your traditional salad for life.

Recipe #1: Smoked Salmon & Avocado Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups Baby romaine lettuce
  • 2 Avocados, sliced
  • 8 oz Smoked salmon
  • ½ cup Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup Walnuts, toasted and chopped
  • 2 tbsp Red onion, finely chopped

For the dressing:

  • 2 tbsp Red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • 4 tbsp Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the dressing ingredients and set aside to let the flavors meld.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the romaine, tomatoes, avocado, onions, and walnuts.
  3. Cut the smoked salmon slices into bite-sized pieces and gently mix them into the salad.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss well to coat.

Recipe #2: Quinoa & Roasted Beet Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Cooked quinoa
  • 2 Medium beets, roasted and peeled
  • 2 cups Baby spinach
  • ½ cup Goat cheese, crumbled

For the honey mustard dressing:

  • 2 tbsp Honey
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 4 tbsp Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the honey mustard dressing by combining the honey, mustard, and olive oil in a small bowl. Whisk well and season with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted beets, and baby spinach.
  3. Crumble the goat cheese over the salad and then pour the dressing on top.
  4. Toss the salad until everything is evenly coated.

Recipe #3: Greek-inspired Chickpea & Feta Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans Chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 Red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 Bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • ¼ cup Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 6 oz Feta cheese, crumbled

For the Greek dressing:

  • 3 tbsp Red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 tsp Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine all the salad ingredients in a large bowl.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the dressing ingredients. Pour it over the salad and toss well.
  3. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Recipe #4: Thai-Inspired Mango & Cherry Tomato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 Mango, peeled and diced
  • ¼ cup Fresh mint, chopped
  • ¼ cup Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tbsp Red onion, thinly sliced

For the peanut dressing:

  • 3 tbsp Peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp Lime juice
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • 2 tbsp Water
  • ½ tbsp Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix all the dressing ingredients in a small bowl and whisk until smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if necessary.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, mango, mint, cilantro, and red onion.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss well until everything is evenly coated.

Recipe #5: Sweet & Savory Asian Salad with Sesame Ginger Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups Mixed greens
  • 1 can Canned beets, drained and sliced
  • 1 cup Cooked soba noodles
  • 1/2 cup Sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup Scallions, thinly sliced

For the sesame ginger dressing:

  • 2 tbsp Sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp Ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Honey
  • 2 tbsp Water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blend the dressing ingredients together in a blender or whisk thoroughly in a bowl.
  2. In your largest mixing bowl, combine the mixed greens, sliced beets, cooked soba noodles, sesame seeds, and scallions.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.

Start experimenting with these salad recipes, and before you know it, you’ll be creating your own masterpieces. Embrace the five ingredients or fewer philosophy when preparing your salads, and you’ll never feel limited again. As salads are incredibly versatile, modify these recipes to suit your taste buds or incorporate any leftovers lying around in your fridge. Happy salad-making!
