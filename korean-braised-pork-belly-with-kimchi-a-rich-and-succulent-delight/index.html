Korean Braised Pork Belly with Kimchi: A Rich and Succulent Delight


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and intricate dishes that tantalize the taste buds. Among the many culinary treasures from Korea, Braised Pork Belly with Kimchi stands out as a rich and succulent delight that is as comforting as it is delicious. This dish, known as “Bossam”, is not just a meal; it is an experience. The combination of tender, flavorful pork with spicy, fermented kimchi creates a perfect harmony that showcases the heart of Korean cooking. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the steps to create this mouthwatering dish.

The Essence of Korean Braised Pork Belly

What is Pork Belly?

Pork belly is a cut of meat from the belly of a pig, and it’s known for its rich flavor and tender texture. This cut has a high fat content, which makes it ideal for braising as it becomes incredibly tender during the cooking process. In Korean cuisine, pork belly is cherished for its ability to absorb flavors, making it a versatile ingredient in numerous dishes.

The Role of Kimchi

Kimchi is a staple in Korean households, made from fermented vegetables, primarily napa cabbage and radishes, seasoned with garlic, ginger, chili pepper, and fish sauce (or shrimp paste). It is not only a side dish but also serves as an essential component in various recipes, adding depth, heat, and a tangy flavor that complements the richness of pork belly perfectly.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare this sumptuous dish, gather the following ingredients:

For the Braised Pork Belly:

  • 2 to 3 pounds of pork belly, skin-on, cut into large chunks
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (brown sugar preferred)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (optional)

For the Kimchi:

  • 1 small napa cabbage, quartered
  • 1/4 cup of sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 green onion, chopped

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Kimchi

  1. Salting the Cabbage: Begin by preparing the napa cabbage for kimchi. In a large bowl, sprinkle the sea salt over the quartered cabbage, ensuring all leaves are coated. Let it sit for about 1 to 2 hours, allowing the salt to draw out moisture and soften the cabbage.

  2. Rinse and Drain: After the salting period, rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water to remove excess salt. Drain well.

  3. Make the Kimchi Paste: In a separate bowl, mix the fish sauce, gochugaru, minced garlic, grated ginger, and sugar. Add a little water if necessary to form a paste.

  4. Combine: Toss the rinsed cabbage with the kimchi paste, making sure to coat all the leaves. Add chopped green onions for an extra layer of flavor.

  5. Ferment: Transfer the kimchi into a clean jar or container, pressing it down to eliminate air pockets. Leave it at room temperature for 1 to 2 days to ferment, then refrigerate.

Step 2: Braise the Pork Belly

  1. Sear the Pork Belly: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat. Season the pork belly chunks with salt and pepper, then add them to the pot. Sear the meat on all sides until golden brown, about 5 to 7 minutes.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: Once the pork belly is seared, remove it from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced ginger. Sauté for about 3 minutes until the onion becomes translucent.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Return the pork belly to the pot. Add soy sauce, gochugaru, sugar, rice wine (if using), and water. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Braise: Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it braise for about 1.5 to 2 hours, turning the pork belly occasionally. The meat should be tender and easily shredded with a fork.

Step 3: Final Assembly

  1. Taste and Adjust: After braising, taste the sauce and adjust seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a thicker sauce, remove the lid and let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes to reduce.

  2. Serve: Slice the braised pork belly into bite-sized pieces and serve hot with a generous side of kimchi. This dish is often accompanied by steamed rice and additional banchan (side dishes), making it a complete meal.

Tips for the Perfect Braised Pork Belly

  • Quality of Ingredients: Use high-quality pork belly for the best texture and flavor. Look for meat with a good balance of fat and meat.
  • Fermenting Kimchi: Allow your kimchi to ferment to your desired level of tanginess. The longer it sits, the stronger the flavor will become.
  • Leftovers: This dish tastes even better the next day, as the flavors continue to meld. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Pairing Suggestions

Korean Braised Pork Belly with Kimchi is incredibly versatile and can be paired with various sides. Here are some suggestions to elevate your meal:

  • Steamed Rice: A bowl of fluffy white rice is essential to balance the rich flavors of the pork and the spiciness of the kimchi.
  • Korean Pancakes: Serve alongside savory Korean pancakes (Pajeon) for a delightful contrast in texture.
  • Pickled Vegetables: A side of pickled radishes or cucumbers can add a refreshing crunch to the meal.

Conclusion

Korean Braised Pork Belly with Kimchi is a dish that embodies the spirit of Korean cuisine. It’s rich, flavorful, and deeply satisfying—perfect for family gatherings, special occasions, or simply indulging in a comforting meal. The combination of tender pork belly and spicy kimchi is not just a feast for the palate but also a celebration of culture and tradition.

Whether you decide to make this for a cozy dinner or to impress guests at a gathering, this recipe will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Korea right from your kitchen!
