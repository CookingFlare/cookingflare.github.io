Baking Made Easy: Beginner's Lemon Drizzle Cake Recipe


Are you a beginner baker looking to impress your friends and family with a delicious treat? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making a mouthwatering Lemon Drizzle Cake that is sure to be a hit at any gathering. With its tangy lemon flavor and moist texture, this cake is a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

For the cake:

  • 200g unsalted butter, softened
  • 200g caster sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

For the drizzle:

  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 100g granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and grease a 9-inch round cake tin.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and caster sugar until light and fluffy. This can be done using a hand mixer or a wooden spoon.

  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. This will help to prevent curdling.

  4. Sift the self-raising flour and baking powder into the bowl and gently fold it in with a spatula until the batter is smooth and well combined.

  5. Add the lemon zest and lemon juice to the batter and mix until evenly distributed. The lemon zest will infuse the cake with a vibrant citrus flavor.

  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake tin and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for approximately 35-40 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

  7. While the cake is baking, prepare the lemon drizzle by combining the lemon juice and granulated sugar in a small saucepan. Heat gently until the sugar has dissolved, then set aside.

  8. Once the cake is baked, remove it from the oven and leave it to cool in the tin for 5 minutes. Then, using a skewer or toothpick, poke holes all over the surface of the cake.

  9. Slowly pour the lemon drizzle over the warm cake, allowing it to seep into the holes. This will add extra moisture and tangy flavor to the cake.

  10. Leave the cake to cool completely in the tin before removing and transferring it to a serving plate.

  11. Serve the Lemon Drizzle Cake as is, or if you want to get fancy, you can dust it with powdered sugar or top it with a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of lemon flavor, you can add a teaspoon of lemon extract to the cake batter.
  • If you prefer a less tangy drizzle, you can reduce the amount of lemon juice and sugar used.
  • This cake can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. The flavors actually intensify over time, making it even more delicious.
  • If you’re feeling adventurous, you can experiment with different citrus fruits such as oranges or limes to create your own unique twist on this classic recipe.

So there you have it, a simple and foolproof Lemon Drizzle Cake recipe that is perfect for beginners. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or just craving a sweet treat, this cake is sure to satisfy. So get your apron on and give it a try – happy baking!
