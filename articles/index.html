Homemade Strawberry Swirl Ice Cream: Sweet and Creamy

Welcome to our delightful journey into the world of homemade ice cream! Today, we’re going to explore the luscious and irresistible Homemade Strawberry Swirl Ice Cream. This sweet and creamy treat is perfect for those warm summer days when you’re craving something refreshing and indulgent. Ingredients: 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 cups heavy cream 1 cup whole milk 3/4 cup granulated sugar 4 large egg yolks 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Instructions: Step 1: Prepare the Strawberry Swirl In a saucepan, combine the sliced strawberries and 1/2 cup of sugar. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries are soft and the sugar has dissolved. This should take about 10 minutes. Once the strawberries are soft, remove the saucepan from the heat and allow the mixture to cool. Once cooled, transfer the strawberries to a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Set the strawberry puree aside. Step 2: Prepare the Ice Cream Base In a separate saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and 3/4 cup of sugar. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is hot but not boiling. Remove the saucepan from the heat. Step 3: Temper the Egg Yolks In a heatproof bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth. Gradually pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. This process, known as tempering, ensures that the eggs are gently heated and prevents them from scrambling when added to the hot mixture. Step 4: Cook the Custard Pour the egg and cream mixture back into the saucepan. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Step 5: Chill the Custard Once the custard has thickened, remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Pour the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any lumps. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, ensuring that the wrap touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. Step 6: Churn the Ice Cream After the custard has chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. As the ice cream begins to thicken, add dollops of the strawberry puree, swirling gently with a knife or spatula to create a marbled effect. Step 7: Freeze the Ice Cream Once the ice cream has reached a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a freezer-safe container. Drizzle additional strawberry puree on top and use a knife to create more swirls. Cover the container and freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm. Serving Suggestions: This Homemade Strawberry Swirl Ice Cream is best enjoyed on its own in a cone or a bowl. For an extra indulgent treat, top it with whipped cream, fresh strawberries, or a drizzle of chocolate sauce. It’s also a perfect companion to warm desserts like brownies or fruit crisps. ...

Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream: Fresh and Creamy

Are you ready to indulge in the delightful flavors of summer with a homemade treat that will leave your taste buds craving for more? Look no further than this luscious and creamy Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream recipe. With the perfect balance of fresh strawberries, crumbly shortcake, and velvety ice cream, this dessert is a celebration of everything sweet and summery. Ingredients For the Strawberry Swirl: 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 tablespoon lemon juice For the Shortcake Crumble: 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed 1/4 teaspoon baking powder Pinch of salt For the Ice Cream Base: 2 cups heavy cream 1 cup whole milk 3/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 4 large egg yolks Instructions Strawberry Swirl: In a saucepan, combine the sliced strawberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 10-12 minutes. Remove from heat and allow it to cool. Once cooled, puree the mixture until smooth and set it aside. Shortcake Crumble: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a bowl, mix the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use your fingers to rub the butter into the dry ingredients until it resembles coarse crumbs. Spread the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown. Let the crumble cool completely before breaking it into small pieces. Ice Cream Base: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and half of the granulated sugar. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar until pale and slightly thickened. Gradually pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl and let it cool completely. Assembly: Once the ice cream base has cooled, churn it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions. During the last minute of churning, gently fold in the strawberry swirl and shortcake crumble, reserving some for topping. Transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container, layering with the remaining strawberry swirl and shortcake crumble. Freeze for at least 4 hours, or until the ice cream is firm. Serving Suggestions Scoop the Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream into bowls and garnish with fresh strawberries, a dollop of whipped cream, and a sprinkle of shortcake crumble for an extra crunch. This delightful dessert is perfect for hot summer days, backyard barbecues, or any occasion that calls for a refreshing and indulgent treat. ...
