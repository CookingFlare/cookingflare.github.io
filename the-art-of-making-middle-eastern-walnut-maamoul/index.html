The Art of Making Middle Eastern Walnut Maamoul


Middle Eastern cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors and textures, and one beloved delicacy that perfectly embodies this rich culinary tradition is the Walnut Maamoul. These delicate, buttery pastries filled with a luscious walnut filling are a true delight for the senses. In this article, we will explore the art of making Middle Eastern Walnut Maamoul from scratch, guiding you through each step to create a batch of these irresistible treats.

Ingredients

  • For the dough:

    • 2 cups fine semolina
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 1/2 cup rose water
    • 1/4 cup orange blossom water

  • For the walnut filling:

    • 2 cups walnuts, finely chopped
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
    • 1 tablespoon rose water

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the walnut filling. In a mixing bowl, combine the finely chopped walnuts, granulated sugar, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, ground cloves, and rose water. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Set aside to let the flavors meld.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the fine semolina and all-purpose flour for the dough. Mix well to ensure they are thoroughly combined.

  3. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the melted butter, rose water, and orange blossom water. Use your hands to mix and knead the dough until it comes together and becomes smooth and pliable. The dough should not stick to your hands but should hold together nicely.

  4. Divide the dough into small portions, roughly the size of a golf ball. Take one portion and flatten it slightly with your palm. Place a teaspoonful of the walnut filling in the center of the dough.

  5. Gather the edges of the dough and seal it tightly, shaping it into a ball. Ensure that the filling is completely enclosed within the dough. Repeat this process with the remaining dough portions and walnut filling.

  6. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  7. Arrange the filled dough balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving a small gap between each one to allow for expansion during baking.

  8. Use the back of a fork to gently press down on the top of each dough ball, creating a decorative pattern. This will also help flatten the maamoul slightly.

  9. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 15-20 minutes, or until the maamoul turn golden brown.

  10. Once baked, remove the maamoul from the oven and let them cool completely on a wire rack before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Walnut Maamoul is best enjoyed with a cup of hot tea or Arabic coffee. Serve these delicate pastries on a platter, dusted with powdered sugar for an elegant touch. You can also store them in an airtight container for up to two weeks, ensuring their freshness and preserving their delightful flavors.

Conclusion

The art of making Middle Eastern Walnut Maamoul is a labor of love that results in a truly delectable treat. The combination of the buttery dough and the aromatic walnut filling creates a symphony of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the heart of the Middle East. So why not embark on this culinary adventure and experience the joy of creating this traditional delicacy in your own kitchen? Indulge in the art of making Walnut Maamoul and savor every bite of these exquisite pastries.
