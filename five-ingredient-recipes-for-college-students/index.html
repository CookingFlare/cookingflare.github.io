Five-Ingredient Recipes for College Students


Being a college student can be challenging, especially when it comes to cooking. With limited time, a tight budget, and minimal kitchen equipment, preparing meals can seem like a daunting task. But fear not! In this blog post, we will be sharing five delicious and easy-to-make recipes that require only five ingredients. These recipes are perfect for college students who want to enjoy tasty meals without spending hours in the kitchen or breaking the bank. Let’s dive in!

Recipe 1: Cheesy Baked Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pasta
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked pasta, marinara sauce, and dried oregano. Mix well.
  4. Transfer the pasta mixture to the greased baking dish and top it with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired, and serve hot.

Recipe 2: One-Pan Chicken and Veggies

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup baby carrots
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Place the chicken breasts on the prepared baking sheet and surround them with baby carrots and broccoli florets.
  4. Drizzle olive oil over the chicken and vegetables, and season with additional salt and pepper if desired.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Recipe 3: Avocado Toast with Fried Egg

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread slices until golden brown.
  2. Meanwhile, peel and pit the avocado, then mash it in a bowl using a fork.
  3. Spread the mashed avocado evenly onto the toasted bread slices.
  4. In a non-stick skillet, fry the eggs to your desired doneness.
  5. Place one fried egg on top of each avocado-toast slice.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes, if desired.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Recipe 4: Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breast

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. Butterfly the chicken breasts by slicing them horizontally, but not all the way through.
  3. Season the inside of the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff each chicken breast with fresh spinach leaves and crumbled feta cheese.
  5. Close the chicken breasts and secure with toothpicks if necessary.
  6. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  7. Sear the stuffed chicken breasts for about 2-3 minutes on each side.
  8. Transfer the chicken breasts to the greased baking dish and bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  9. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Recipe 5: Peanut Butter Banana Smoothie

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • Ice cubes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice the bananas.
  2. In a blender, combine the sliced bananas, peanut butter, milk, and honey (if using).
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Add ice cubes if you prefer a colder and thicker consistency.
  5. Pour the smoothie into a glass and enjoy it as a quick and nutritious breakfast or snack.

These five-ingredient recipes are perfect for college students who want to whip up delicious meals without spending a fortune or investing too much time. Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or simply looking for quick and easy recipes, these meals will satisfy your hunger and keep you fueled throughout your college journey. Give them a try and let us know which one becomes your favorite! Happy cooking!
