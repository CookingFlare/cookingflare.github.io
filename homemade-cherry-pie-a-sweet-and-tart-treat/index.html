Homemade Cherry Pie: A Sweet and Tart Treat


Indulge your taste buds with a classic American dessert that combines the sweetness of ripe cherries with the flaky perfection of a homemade pie crust. This recipe for Homemade Cherry Pie will take you on a journey of delicious flavors that will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this dessert is a delightful treat that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh cherries, pitted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 pie crusts, homemade or store-bought
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sugar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large bowl, combine the pitted cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, almond extract, and lemon juice. Mix well until the cherries are evenly coated. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Roll out one pie crust and place it into a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently against the sides and bottom. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges.

  4. Pour the cherry mixture into the pie crust, spreading it evenly. Dot the top of the cherries with small pieces of unsalted butter.

  5. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the cherry filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  6. Brush the top crust with beaten egg wash for a golden finish. Sprinkle coarse sugar on top, if desired, to add a touch of sweetness and a beautiful sparkle.

  7. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil.

  8. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set and the flavors to intensify.

  9. Serve the Homemade Cherry Pie warm or at room temperature, alone or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Each bite will bring you the perfect balance of sweetness and tartness, with the juicy cherries bursting with flavor against the buttery, flaky crust.

Tips and Variations

  • If fresh cherries are not available, you can use frozen cherries instead. Just make sure to thaw them and drain any excess liquid before using.

  • Feel free to experiment with different flavors by adding a pinch of cinnamon, nutmeg, or cardamom to the cherry filling for a hint of warmth and spice.

  • For a decorative touch, use cookie cutters to create shapes with the extra pie crust. Arrange them on top of the pie before baking for an eye-catching design.

  • If you prefer a lattice crust, simply cut the top pie crust into strips and weave them over the cherry filling.

  • Leftover pie can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Just be sure to cover it tightly with plastic wrap or place it in an airtight container to keep it fresh.

Conclusion

Homemade Cherry Pie is a timeless dessert that captures the essence of summer in every bite. The combination of sweet cherries and a buttery crust is a match made in heaven, and this recipe is a foolproof way to achieve pie perfection. So, whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply craving a comforting dessert, give this Homemade Cherry Pie a try. Your taste buds will thank you, and your family and friends will be begging for seconds. Enjoy!
