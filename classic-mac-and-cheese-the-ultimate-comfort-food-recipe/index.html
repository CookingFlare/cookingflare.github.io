Classic Mac and Cheese: The Ultimate Comfort Food Recipe


There’s something undeniably comforting about a steaming bowl of classic mac and cheese. The creamy, cheesy goodness combined with tender pasta is the epitome of comfort food. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or a dish to impress at a potluck, mac and cheese is a timeless crowd-pleaser. In this recipe, we’ll delve into the art of creating the ultimate classic mac and cheese from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Cheese Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Gruyere cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Pasta:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • Salt for boiling water

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup Panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk constantly for about 2 minutes to create a roux. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Allow the mixture to simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.

  3. Add the Cheeses: Reduce the heat to low and add the shredded cheddar and Gruyere cheese to the sauce, stirring until melted and smooth. Stir in the Dijon mustard, paprika, salt, and pepper. Continue to simmer the sauce for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Combine the Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked elbow macaroni to the cheese sauce, stirring until every piece of pasta is coated in the creamy goodness.

  5. Prepare the Topping: In a small bowl, combine the Panko breadcrumbs, melted butter, and grated Parmesan cheese.

  6. Bake the Mac and Cheese: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the prepared breadcrumb topping evenly over the mac and cheese.

  7. Bake for Perfection: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly around the edges.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Remove the mac and cheese from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. Scoop out generous helpings of this creamy delight and savor the ultimate comfort food experience.

Tips for Perfection

  • For an extra flavor kick, consider adding crispy bacon bits or diced jalapeños to the mac and cheese before baking.
  • Experiment with different cheese combinations such as fontina, mozzarella, or Monterey Jack to customize the flavor profile to your liking.
  • To make this dish ahead of time, prepare the mac and cheese up to the point of baking, cover tightly, and refrigerate. When ready to serve, simply bake as directed, allowing a few extra minutes for the chilled dish.

There you have it – the ultimate comfort food recipe for classic mac and cheese that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Whether it’s a cozy night in or a special occasion, this homemade mac and cheese is sure to warm hearts and bellies alike. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in a timeless classic that never fails to delight.

Enjoy your culinary adventure, and may your mac and cheese be forever creamy and irresistible!
