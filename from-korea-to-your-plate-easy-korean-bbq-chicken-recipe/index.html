From Korea to Your Plate: Easy Korean BBQ Chicken Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors and vibrant dishes, and Korean BBQ chicken is no exception. The combination of tender chicken marinated in a savory sauce and grilled to perfection creates a mouthwatering dish that will transport your taste buds straight to Korea. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make an easy and delicious Korean BBQ chicken right in your own kitchen. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, gochujang, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, rice vinegar, and sesame seeds. This will be the marinade for the chicken.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the chicken is well-coated. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you don’t have a grill, you can also use a grill pan or even bake the chicken in the oven.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off. Discard the remaining marinade.
  5. Grill the chicken for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C). If you’re using a grill pan or baking the chicken, adjust the cooking time accordingly.
  6. Once cooked, transfer the chicken to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes. This will help the juices redistribute and keep the chicken moist.
  7. Slice the chicken into thin strips and garnish with sliced green onions. Serve hot with steamed rice and your favorite Korean side dishes.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier kick, you can add more gochujang to the marinade.
  • For a touch of sweetness, you can substitute the honey with brown sugar or maple syrup.
  • If you don’t have gochujang, you can use sriracha or another spicy chili paste as a substitute.
  • To make it a complete Korean BBQ experience, serve the chicken with lettuce leaves and ssamjang (Korean dipping sauce) for wrapping.
  • This recipe also works well with chicken thighs or drumsticks if you prefer dark meat.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve just mastered an easy and delicious Korean BBQ chicken recipe that will impress your family and friends. The combination of flavors from the marinade creates a mouthwatering dish that captures the essence of Korean cuisine. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or prefer milder flavors, this recipe can easily be customized to suit your taste preferences. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and enjoy a taste of Korea right in your own home.
