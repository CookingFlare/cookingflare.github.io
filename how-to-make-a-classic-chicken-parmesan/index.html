How to Make a Classic Chicken Parmesan


Chicken Parmesan, also known as Chicken Parmigiana, is one of the most beloved dishes in Italian-American cuisine. With its crispy golden crust, tender chicken, gooey melted cheese, and rich marinara sauce, it’s no surprise that this dish has become a household favorite. Whether you’re preparing a meal for your family, hosting a dinner party, or simply indulging in a delicious homemade dish, this classic chicken Parmesan recipe will never disappoint.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of making the perfect Chicken Parmesan, from preparing the chicken to baking it to perfection. You’ll also find helpful tips, tricks, and variations to elevate your dish to new heights. So, let’s get started!

What is Chicken Parmesan?

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a moment to understand what makes Chicken Parmesan so special. This dish typically consists of breaded and fried chicken cutlets that are smothered in marinara sauce, topped with mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses, and then baked until bubbly and golden. The combination of crispy chicken, savory sauce, and melted cheese makes for a comforting, satisfying meal that’s sure to please everyone at the table.

Chicken Parmesan is often served over spaghetti or other pasta, but it can also be enjoyed with a side of vegetables or garlic bread. Regardless of how you serve it, this classic recipe is sure to become a staple in your home.

Ingredients for Classic Chicken Parmesan

To make the best Chicken Parmesan, you’ll need a few simple ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs for a juicier option)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup Italian-style breadcrumbs (or plain breadcrumbs seasoned with Italian herbs)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 ½ cups mozzarella cheese (preferably whole milk mozzarella, shredded)

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 1 jar (about 24 ounces) of high-quality marinara sauce (or homemade sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)

For Cooking:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by preparing your chicken breasts. If they’re too thick, you can butterfly them (slice them horizontally) to create thinner cutlets. This will ensure they cook evenly and become nice and crispy. If you prefer, you can also pound them with a meat mallet to an even thickness.

Once your chicken is the right thickness, season both sides generously with salt and pepper. This will enhance the flavor of the chicken.

Step 2: Bread the Chicken

To create that signature crispy crust, you’ll need to bread the chicken. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes:

  • In the first dish, add the flour.
  • In the second dish, whisk together the eggs and water.
  • In the third dish, combine the breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese.

First, dredge each chicken breast in the flour, making sure to coat it evenly. Next, dip the floured chicken into the egg mixture, letting any excess egg drip off. Finally, press the chicken into the breadcrumb and Parmesan mixture, ensuring an even coating on all sides. This breading will help create a deliciously crispy crust when fried.

Step 3: Fry the Chicken

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil and butter over medium-high heat. The butter will add richness, while the olive oil will prevent the butter from burning. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the breaded chicken breasts into the skillet. Fry them for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. Be sure not to overcrowd the skillet – fry the chicken in batches if necessary.

Once the chicken is cooked through and crispy on both sides, transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Step 4: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

While the chicken is frying, you can prepare the marinara sauce. If you’re using store-bought sauce, simply heat it in a small saucepan over medium heat. If you prefer homemade marinara sauce, combine crushed tomatoes, garlic, dried basil, dried oregano, and a pinch of salt in a pot. Simmer for 20-30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Once the sauce is heated through or simmered to your liking, set it aside.

Step 5: Assemble the Chicken Parmesan

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and prepare a baking dish large enough to hold all four chicken breasts.

Place the fried chicken breasts into the baking dish. Spoon a generous amount of marinara sauce over each chicken cutlet, covering it evenly. Be sure to leave a little sauce around the edges to avoid the chicken becoming soggy.

Next, top each chicken breast with a generous handful of shredded mozzarella cheese, followed by a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese. The mozzarella will melt into a delicious, gooey layer, while the Parmesan adds a bit of tang and richness.

Step 6: Bake the Chicken Parmesan

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and golden brown. You can even switch your oven to broil for the last 2-3 minutes to give the cheese a nice, crispy finish.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Once your Chicken Parmesan is out of the oven, let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the cheese to set and make it easier to cut and serve.

Serve your Chicken Parmesan with your favorite pasta, such as spaghetti, penne, or fettuccine. You can also pair it with a simple side salad and garlic bread for a well-rounded meal.

Tips for Making the Perfect Chicken Parmesan

While Chicken Parmesan is a simple and straightforward dish, there are a few tips that can help you take it to the next level:

  • Use fresh mozzarella: While shredded mozzarella works, fresh mozzarella can make a big difference in flavor and texture. Look for whole milk mozzarella for the creamiest results.
  • Make your own marinara sauce: Homemade marinara sauce can add extra depth of flavor. Use ripe tomatoes and fresh herbs for the best results.
  • Use panko breadcrumbs: If you prefer an even crunchier coating, try using panko breadcrumbs instead of traditional Italian breadcrumbs. They create an extra-crisp crust when fried.
  • Don’t skip the resting time: After frying the chicken, let it rest for a minute or two before assembling it with the sauce and cheese. This will ensure that it stays crispy and doesn’t become soggy.
  • Bake, don’t fry: If you want to cut down on calories or avoid frying, you can bake the breaded chicken breasts instead of frying them. Simply coat the chicken in breadcrumbs, place it on a baking sheet, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.

Variations on Chicken Parmesan

While the classic Chicken Parmesan is delicious on its own, you can easily customize it to suit your tastes. Here are a few variations you might enjoy:

  • Eggplant Parmesan: If you’re looking for a vegetarian version of Chicken Parmesan, try using breaded and fried eggplant slices in place of the chicken.
  • Chicken Parmesan Sandwich: Instead of serving the chicken over pasta, pile it onto a toasted bun with marinara sauce and melted cheese for a delicious chicken Parmesan sandwich.
  • Spicy Chicken Parmesan: Add some heat to your Chicken Parmesan by incorporating red pepper flakes into the marinara sauce or sprinkling them on top before baking.

Conclusion

Chicken Parmesan is a classic comfort food that combines the perfect balance of crispy, cheesy, and savory flavors. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or just a weeknight dinner, this dish is sure to please everyone at the table. By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to make a delicious, restaurant-quality Chicken Parmesan right in your own kitchen.

With a few simple ingredients and some time in the kitchen, you can create a dish that will impress even the pickiest eaters. Enjoy!
