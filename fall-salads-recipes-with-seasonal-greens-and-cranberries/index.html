Fall Salads: Recipes with Seasonal Greens and Cranberries


As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, it’s the perfect time to embrace the flavors of fall with delicious and nutritious salads. Fall salads are a great way to incorporate seasonal produce and create colorful, flavorful dishes that are both satisfying and healthy. In this post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering fall salad recipes featuring seasonal greens and cranberries that are sure to impress your taste buds and elevate your autumn dining experience.

Harvest Kale Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh kale
  • 1 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup of toasted pecans
  • 1/4 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by washing and drying the kale, then remove the tough stems and chop the leaves into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper to create the dressing.
  3. In a large salad bowl, combine the kale, dried cranberries, toasted pecans, and crumbled feta cheese.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and gently toss to coat the ingredients evenly.
  5. Serve the salad immediately and enjoy the delightful combination of earthy kale, sweet cranberries, and crunchy pecans.

Autumn Spinach Salad with Roasted Butternut Squash

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 6 cups of fresh baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup of pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup of crumbled goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper, then spread it out on the prepared baking sheet.
  3. Roast the squash for 25-30 minutes, or until it is tender and lightly caramelized.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining olive oil, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  5. In a large salad bowl, combine the baby spinach, dried cranberries, pumpkin seeds, and crumbled goat cheese.
  6. Add the roasted butternut squash to the salad, drizzle the dressing over it, and toss gently to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Serve this vibrant salad as a refreshing side dish or a satisfying main course.

Cranberry Pecan Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups of water or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup of toasted pecans
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the water or vegetable broth to a boil, then add the quinoa and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  2. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and let it cool to room temperature.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper to create the dressing.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, toasted pecans, chopped parsley, and red onion.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat all the ingredients evenly.
  6. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

Conclusion

These vibrant fall salad recipes are a celebration of seasonal flavors and textures, combining the freshness of greens with the sweetness of cranberries and the richness of nuts and cheese. Whether you’re looking for a light lunch, a colorful side dish, or a wholesome dinner, these recipes offer a delightful way to savor the essence of autumn on your plate. So, embrace the bounty of the season, and let these fall salads bring warmth and vibrancy to your table.
