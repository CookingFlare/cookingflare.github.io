Spicy Mexican Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Colorful and Flavorful Dish


If you’re looking for a vibrant and flavorful dish to spice up your dinner routine, look no further than Spicy Mexican Stuffed Bell Peppers. This dish is a celebration of colors and flavors, combining the freshness of bell peppers with the boldness of traditional Mexican spices. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and your dinner guests.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the bell peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the peppers in the prepared baking dish, standing upright.

  3. Cook the filling: In a large skillet, cook the ground beef or turkey over medium heat until browned. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent. Stir in the cooked rice, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Allow the mixture to simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Stuff the peppers: Spoon the filling mixture into the prepared bell peppers, pressing down gently to fill any gaps. Top each pepper with a generous amount of shredded cheese.

  5. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake the stuffed peppers in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  6. Garnish and serve: Remove the foil from the baking dish and bake for an additional 5 minutes to lightly brown the cheese. Once done, remove the peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Tips and Variations:

  • Vegetarian Option: For a vegetarian version, substitute the ground meat with cooked quinoa or additional black beans.
  • Spice Level: Adjust the spiciness to your preference by adding more or less chili powder.
  • Toppings: Serve the stuffed peppers with a dollop of sour cream or a drizzle of hot sauce for an extra kick.
  • Storage: These stuffed peppers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days and make excellent leftovers for lunch.

Conclusion:

Spicy Mexican Stuffed Bell Peppers are not only a feast for the eyes but also a delightful combination of textures and flavors. From the vibrant colors of the peppers to the zesty kick of the Mexican spices, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re preparing a family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, these stuffed peppers are sure to leave everyone asking for seconds. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the vibrant and bold flavors of this delightful Mexican-inspired dish.
