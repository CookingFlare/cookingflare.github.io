Savory and Spicy Jalapeno Cornbread: A Southern Delight


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked cornbread wafting through the kitchen. It’s a comforting scent that evokes memories of family gatherings and cozy evenings by the fireplace. If you’re looking to add a little kick to your cornbread, then this Savory and Spicy Jalapeno Cornbread recipe is just what you need.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced jalapenos (seeds removed for a milder flavor)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a 9-inch square baking dish.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, vegetable oil, and eggs until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cornbread.
  5. Gently fold in the shredded cheddar cheese and diced jalapenos. The cheese will melt beautifully as the cornbread bakes, adding a creamy texture and extra flavor.
  6. Pour the batter into the greased baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Once baked, remove the cornbread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. Once cooled, slice the cornbread into squares and serve. Optionally, garnish with chopped fresh cilantro for an added burst of freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a milder version of this cornbread, reduce the amount of diced jalapenos or remove the seeds before adding them to the batter.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, you can leave the seeds in the jalapenos or even add additional sliced jalapenos to the batter.
  • This cornbread pairs perfectly with chili, barbecue, or a simple bowl of soup. It’s also delicious on its own as a snack or breakfast treat.
  • If you don’t have buttermilk, you can make your own by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to 1 cup of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

In Conclusion

Savory and Spicy Jalapeno Cornbread is a delightful twist on a classic Southern recipe. The combination of cornmeal, cheddar cheese, and jalapenos creates a flavorful and slightly fiery bread that will have your taste buds dancing. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply want to elevate your next meal, this cornbread is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the taste of the South!
