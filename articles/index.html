Cut 1/2 cup of butter into the mixture until crumbly. Dissolve the yeast in warm water and add it to the flour mixture. Stir in the milk until the dough comes together. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth. Shape it into a square, wrap it in plastic, and refrigerate for 30 minutes. Roll the dough into a 12-inch square. Place the remaining butter in the center and fold the dough over it, sealing the edges. Roll the dough into a 20x10-inch rectangle, then fold it into thirds. Chill for 30 minutes. Repeat this process twice. Roll the dough into a 20x10-inch rectangle one last time. Cut into triangles, then roll each triangle into a croissant shape. Place the croissants on a baking sheet, cover, and let rise for 1-2 hours. Preheat the oven to 425°F. Brush the croissants with egg wash and bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown. Creamy Crème Brûlée: A French Custard Classic

Crème Brûlée is a classic French dessert that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all over the world. With its creamy texture, delicate flavor, and crackling caramelized sugar topping, it’s no wonder why this dessert has become a favorite among dessert lovers. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to create the perfect Crème Brûlée right in your own kitchen. Ingredients 4 egg yolks 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 cups heavy cream 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 tablespoon brown sugar Instructions Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and granulated sugar until well combined and slightly thickened. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to bubble around the edges. Remove from heat. Slowly pour the hot cream into the egg mixture, whisking continuously to prevent curdling. Stir in the vanilla extract, ensuring it is evenly distributed throughout the mixture. Place six ramekins or custard cups in a baking dish and divide the custard mixture evenly among them. Carefully pour hot water into the baking dish, around the ramekins, until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the ramekins. This water bath will help ensure even baking. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 40-45 minutes, or until the custard is set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center. Remove the ramekins from the water bath and allow them to cool to room temperature. Then, cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight, to allow the custard to fully set. Just before serving, sprinkle each custard with a thin layer of brown sugar. Use a kitchen torch to carefully caramelize the sugar until it forms a golden brown crust. If you don’t have a kitchen torch, you can place the custards under a broiler for a few minutes until the sugar caramelizes. Serve the Crème Brûlée immediately and enjoy the creamy custard with its irresistible caramelized sugar topping. Tips and Variations For a twist on the classic flavor, you can infuse the cream with other ingredients such as citrus zest, cinnamon, or even lavender. Simply add the desired ingredient to the cream while heating it, and strain it out before adding it to the egg mixture. To achieve the perfect caramelized sugar topping, make sure to evenly sprinkle the brown sugar over the custard. This will ensure an even caramelization. Additionally, using a kitchen torch will give you more control over the process and help prevent overcooking the custard. If you prefer a lighter version of Crème Brûlée, you can substitute half of the heavy cream with whole milk. The custard will still be creamy and delicious, but with a slightly lighter texture. Crème Brûlée is best enjoyed fresh, but you can refrigerate any leftovers for up to 2 days. Keep in mind that the caramelized sugar topping may soften slightly in the fridge, but the custard will still taste amazing. Now that you have the recipe for the perfect Crème Brûlée, it’s time to impress your friends and family with this classic French dessert.
