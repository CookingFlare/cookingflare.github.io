How to Make Ice Cream with Nut Butters: 4 Tasty Recipes


Ice cream is a beloved treat, and while traditional flavors like vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry have their charm, there’s something incredibly special about experimenting with nut butters. Nut butter-infused ice cream offers a rich, creamy texture and a deliciously unique flavor profile that’s both comforting and indulgent. Whether you’re looking for a healthier alternative or just want to try something new, ice cream with nut butters is a perfect way to take your frozen dessert game to the next level.

In this blog post, we’ll explore four mouthwatering nut butter ice cream recipes that will have your taste buds dancing with joy. From the creamy richness of peanut butter to the subtle sweetness of almond butter, these recipes are a delightful twist on traditional ice cream that’s sure to impress. Let’s dive in!

1. Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

Peanut butter and chocolate are a classic pairing, and they come together perfectly in this creamy, irresistible ice cream. This recipe is perfect for peanut butter lovers and those with a sweet tooth, as it combines the salty richness of peanut butter with the sweetness of chocolate chips.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup peanut butter (smooth or crunchy)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Peanut Butter Base: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar. Warm over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture begins to steam (but not boil). Once the sugar is dissolved, remove from heat.

  2. Add Peanut Butter: Stir in the peanut butter and vanilla extract until completely combined. Add a pinch of salt to balance the flavors. Make sure the peanut butter is fully dissolved into the mixture, creating a smooth, creamy base.

  3. Chill the Mixture: Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then transfer it to the refrigerator to chill for at least 3-4 hours, or overnight for best results.

  4. Churn the Ice Cream: Once the mixture is well-chilled, pour it into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This typically takes about 20-30 minutes, depending on your machine.

  5. Fold in the Chocolate Chips: When the ice cream is almost finished churning, add the chocolate chips and let the machine mix them in for the final few minutes.

  6. Freeze and Serve: Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for an additional 4-6 hours to firm up. Once frozen, scoop and serve!

Why You’ll Love It:

This peanut butter chocolate chip ice cream strikes the perfect balance between creamy, nutty, and sweet. The chocolate chips provide a delightful crunch, making each bite a satisfying experience. The peanut butter flavor is rich without being overwhelming, and the whole recipe comes together in a simple yet delicious way.

2. Almond Butter Swirl Ice Cream

For those who prefer a more subtle, nutty flavor, almond butter is a fantastic alternative to peanut butter. This almond butter swirl ice cream offers a slightly sweeter and less intense nutty taste, which pairs beautifully with a creamy vanilla base.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup almond butter (smooth)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup honey (optional, for extra sweetness)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Cream and Milk: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar. Warm over medium heat, stirring frequently until the sugar is completely dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.

  2. Incorporate the Almond Butter: Add the almond butter to the saucepan and whisk until fully blended into the cream and milk mixture. The almond butter will give the ice cream a smooth texture and a deliciously nutty flavor. Add vanilla extract and a pinch of salt to enhance the overall taste.

  3. Sweeten with Honey: If you prefer a slightly sweeter flavor, stir in the honey at this point. It will add an extra layer of richness and a mild floral sweetness that complements the almond butter perfectly.

  4. Cool the Mixture: Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then place it in the refrigerator to chill for 4-6 hours, or overnight.

  5. Churn in the Ice Cream Maker: Once chilled, pour the almond butter mixture into your ice cream maker and churn until it reaches a soft-serve consistency. This may take around 20-25 minutes.

  6. Swirl and Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container. For added almond flavor, swirl in an additional tablespoon of almond butter before freezing. Freeze for at least 4-6 hours to firm up.

Why You’ll Love It:

The almond butter provides a smooth, creamy texture with a delicate flavor that’s less overpowering than peanut butter. This recipe is perfect for anyone who loves the richness of nut butters but wants a lighter, more nuanced taste. The optional honey adds a touch of natural sweetness that enhances the overall experience.

3. Cashew Butter Caramel Ice Cream

Cashew butter has a rich, buttery flavor that works wonderfully in ice cream. Combined with a homemade caramel swirl, this cashew butter ice cream is a luxurious treat that’s both indulgent and satisfying. The sweetness of the caramel complements the cashew butter’s natural creaminess, creating a perfect harmony of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup cashew butter (smooth)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup homemade or store-bought caramel sauce
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cream Base: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and brown sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is warm.

  2. Add the Cashew Butter: Stir in the cashew butter and continue to heat until the mixture is smooth and well combined. Add vanilla extract and a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor.

  3. Chill the Mixture: Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature. Once cooled, transfer it to the refrigerator to chill for several hours or overnight.

  4. Churn in the Ice Cream Maker: After chilling, pour the mixture into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  5. Add the Caramel Swirl: Once the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a storage container. Drizzle in the caramel sauce and swirl it into the ice cream using a spoon or spatula. Be sure to leave some visible caramel ribbons for that gorgeous swirl effect.

  6. Freeze and Enjoy: Freeze the ice cream for 4-6 hours until it’s firm. Serve and enjoy the delicious combination of cashew butter and caramel.

Why You’ll Love It:

Cashew butter’s rich and smooth texture is the star of this ice cream, and the caramel adds a layer of decadence that elevates it even further. The balance of sweet and salty makes every bite heavenly. This recipe is perfect for anyone who enjoys both nut butters and caramel in their desserts.

4. Hazelnut Butter Espresso Ice Cream

For a sophisticated twist, this hazelnut butter espresso ice cream combines the nutty richness of hazelnut butter with the bold flavor of espresso. It’s a perfect dessert for coffee lovers or anyone looking for a more refined ice cream flavor that’s still indulgent and creamy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup hazelnut butter (smooth)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons espresso powder or strong brewed espresso (cooled)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine the Dairy Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is steaming.

  2. Whisk in the Hazelnut Butter: Add the hazelnut butter to the mixture, stirring until smooth. Then, add the espresso powder or cooled brewed espresso and vanilla extract. A pinch of salt will balance the sweetness and enhance the nutty flavor.

  3. Cool the Mixture: Remove from heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to ensure it’s fully chilled before churning.

  4. Churn the Ice Cream: After chilling, pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn until the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  5. Freeze and Serve: Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for 4-6 hours until firm. When ready to serve, scoop and enjoy the smooth, nutty, coffee-flavored treat!

Why You’ll Love It:

Hazelnut butter and espresso make a winning combination in this ice cream. The richness of the hazelnut pairs perfectly with the bold coffee flavor, while the creamy texture ensures every bite is pure indulgence. This recipe is a perfect dessert for coffee enthusiasts who enjoy a nutty twist in their ice cream.

Conclusion

Nut butter ice creams are a fun and delicious way to elevate your homemade frozen treats. Each recipe offers a unique flavor profile, whether you prefer the classic peanut butter and chocolate combo, the subtle sweetness of almond butter, the decadent caramel swirl with cashew butter, or the sophisticated hazelnut and espresso blend. These ice creams are not only incredibly creamy and rich, but they also provide a great way to incorporate nut butters into your diet in a fun and tasty way.

With just a few ingredients and a bit of patience, you can enjoy homemade ice cream that’s far superior to store-bought varieties. So go ahead, indulge in these nut butter ice creams and treat yourself to a delightful dessert experience that’s truly one-of-a-kind.
