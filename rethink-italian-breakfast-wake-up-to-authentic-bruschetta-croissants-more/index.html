Rethink Italian Breakfast: Wake Up to Authentic Bruschetta, Croissants & More


Breakfast is an important meal of the day, not only for nourishing and energizing ourselves but also for gently awakening our senses to the day that lies ahead. Expanding our culinary horizons and trying out different cuisines can broaden our perspectives and ensure that we never run out of ideas to delight our taste buds. Let’s take a journey to the heart of Italy and rediscover the democratic charm of their breakfast with recipes that mix the traditional with the contemporary.

Authentic Bruschetta

Bruschetta is more than just an appetizer; it’s an Italian institution. This simple yet exquisite starter can make for a delightful start to the day, marrying the rustic charm of toasted bread with the vibrant flavors of fresh tomatoes. So, let’s learn how to turn this classic bite-sized dish into an Italian breakfast staple.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 slices of rustic bread, cut half an inch thick
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven or toaster oven to 400째F (200째C).
  2. Slice the bread diagonally about a half-inch thick, brush with olive oil, and toast until golden on both sides. Set aside.
  3. Quarter the tomatoes and season them lightly with salt and pepper.
  4. To cook the garlic, heat the olive oil in a small pan over medium heat, add the minced garlic, and cook just until it becomes fragrant but not browned.
  5. Add the tomatoes to the pan, stir them around in the oil for a minute, and let them cook until the moisture evaporates and they start to brown around the edges.
  6. Once your bruschetta topping is ready, pile it onto each piece of toasted bread, and garnish with a few fresh basil leaves. Serve immediately.

Croissants: A Twist on a Classic

Croissants may hail from Austria, but these flaky, buttery pastries have captured the heart of Italy as well. They’re delightful any time of day, but elevating them to a star breakfast item couldn’t be easier.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk and active dry yeast until the yeast is dissolved.
  3. Create a hole in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the milk and yeast mixture. Knead the dough and incorporate all the ingredients, ensuring it is smooth and elastic.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a rectangle about 1/2 inch thick. Take the butter, which should be soft but not melted, and place it in the center of the rectangle. Fold the dough over the butter, ensuring it envelops it completely.
  5. Wrap the dough in plastic and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Roll out the dough again into a rectangle, fold it in thirds like a letter, and wrap it back up. Let it chill again for another 30 minutes.
  7. Repeat this rolling and folding process one more time, then refrigerate the dough for at least two hours or overnight.
  8. When you’re ready to bake, roll out the dough into a rectangle and cut it into triangles. Roll these triangles up tightly from the base to the tip, starting from the wide edge, making sure the points are tucked underneath.
  9. Place the finished croissants on a lined baking sheet, cover them with a clean cloth, and let them rise in a warm place for 30 minutes.
  10. Preheat your oven to 425째F (220째C) during the final rising period.
  11. Brush the risen croissants with the beaten egg and bake for 20-25 minutes or until they’re deep golden in color. Serve warm with jam, Nutella, or whatever your heart desires!

Renowned Ricotta Crostini

Crostini are elegant bite-sized appetizers that add a touch of refined decadence to any gathering. While traditionally paired with savory ingredients, let’s blend tradition and creativity by serving these fancy little sticks with luscious ricotta and a dash of sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. fresh ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries or cherries, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 16 slices of small, crusty bread, toasted
  • Chopped toasted walnuts, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the ricotta, chopped dried fruit, and honey.
  2. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture onto each toast, sprinkle the walnuts on top, and serve.
