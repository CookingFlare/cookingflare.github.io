A Taste of Provence: Lavender Crème Brûlée


Indulge in the flavors of Provence with this exquisite Lavender Crème Brûlée. Delicate and aromatic, this classic French dessert will transport you to the lavender fields of the South of France. With its creamy custard and caramelized sugar topping, it’s a dessert that will impress your guests and leave them wanting more.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons dried lavender buds
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for caramelizing)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).

  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, vanilla bean, and dried lavender buds. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it reaches a gentle simmer. Remove from heat and let it steep for 10 minutes to infuse the flavors of the lavender.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks until they are well combined.

  4. Slowly pour the lavender-infused cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the lavender buds and vanilla bean.

  5. Divide the custard mixture evenly among six ramekins or oven-safe dishes. Place the ramekins in a large baking dish and carefully pour hot water into the baking dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins. This water bath will help ensure even cooking and a creamy texture.

  6. Carefully transfer the baking dish to the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the custard is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.

  7. Remove the ramekins from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Once cooled, cover each ramekin with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to develop and the custard to fully set.

  8. Just before serving, sprinkle each custard with a thin, even layer of granulated sugar. Use a kitchen torch to carefully caramelize the sugar until it forms a golden-brown crust. If you don’t have a kitchen torch, you can also place the ramekins under the broiler for a few minutes, keeping a close eye to prevent burning.

  9. Allow the crème brûlée to cool for a minute or two to allow the caramelized sugar to harden. Serve immediately and enjoy the creamy, floral goodness of this Lavender Crème Brûlée.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of Provence, serve the Lavender Crème Brûlée with a sprinkle of fresh lavender buds or a dollop of lavender-infused whipped cream.
  • If you prefer a stronger lavender flavor, you can increase the amount of dried lavender buds by an additional tablespoon or two.
  • To make this recipe dairy-free, substitute the heavy cream and whole milk with a dairy-free alternative like coconut milk or almond milk.
  • Experiment with different flavor combinations by infusing the cream mixture with other herbs or spices, such as rosemary, thyme, or cardamom.

Enjoy the delicate flavors of Provence with this Lavender Crème Brûlée recipe. It’s a dessert that combines the elegance of French cuisine with the soothing aroma of lavender. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this recipe is sure to impress and transport you to the picturesque fields of Provence.
