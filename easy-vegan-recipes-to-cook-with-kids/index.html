Easy Vegan Recipes to Cook with Kids


Cooking with kids is an incredible opportunity to bond, teach life skills, and foster healthy eating habits from an early age. Introducing vegan recipes in this adventure not only diversifies their palate but also promotes compassion, sustainability, and creativity. Whether you’re a seasoned plant-based cook or just experimenting, these easy, family-friendly vegan recipes are sure to spark joy and confidence in the kitchen.

Why Cooking with Kids Matters

Engaging children in meal preparation goes beyond just making dinner. It encourages:

  • Hands-on learning: Measuring, mixing, and chopping develop motor skills and math concepts.
  • Healthy eating habits: Kids are more likely to try and enjoy meals they helped create.
  • Confidence-building: Completing a recipe boosts self-esteem.
  • Creativity: Recipes can be customized and personalized, helping kids make food their own.
  • Quality bonding time: Sharing tasks and stories brings families closer.

Now, let’s dive into some of the easiest and most enjoyable vegan recipes to cook with your little ones!

1. Rainbow Veggie Wraps with Creamy Avocado Spread

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Serves: 4

Colorful, fun to assemble, and packed with nutrients, these rainbow wraps are perfect for introducing kids to a variety of veggies. The creamy avocado spread adds richness and makes it more kid-friendly.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large whole wheat or spinach tortillas
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegan mayonnaise or plant-based yogurt
  • 1 cup shredded carrot
  • 1 cup red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cucumber strips
  • 1 cup purple cabbage, shredded
  • Handful of fresh spinach leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the spread: In a bowl, mash the avocado with lemon juice and vegan mayonnaise or yogurt. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  2. Lay out the tortillas: Place them on a clean surface or cutting board.
  3. Spread it out: Help kids spread a generous layer of the avocado mixture onto each tortilla.
  4. Add the rainbow veggies: Set up bowls of shredded carrot, sliced bell pepper, cucumber strips, purple cabbage, and spinach, and let the kids add their favorite combination onto the tortillas.
  5. Roll and slice: Show kids how to tightly roll the wraps and cut them in half at an angle. Use kid-safe knives and supervise closely.
  6. Serve: These wraps are great for lunchboxes, picnics, or a quick dinner.

Tips:

  • Customize veggies based on what your kids like or what’s in season.
  • For added protein, add a spoonful of cooked quinoa or chickpeas.

2. Mini Vegan Pizzas: Hands-On and Wholesome

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Serves: 4

Few kids resist the charm of pizza. Making mini vegan pizzas allows them to express creativity while touching on foundational cooking skills like dough preparation and ingredient selection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made vegan pizza dough (check labels to avoid dairy)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (marinara or your favorite pizza sauce)
  • 1 cup shredded vegan cheese or homemade cashew cheese
  • Assorted toppings: sliced mushrooms, olives, tomatoes, bell peppers, pineapple chunks, spinach, artichokes
  • Olive oil for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: 425°F (220°C).
  2. Divide the dough: Roll out and cut the dough into small circles using a cookie cutter or glass. Each child can have their own mini pizza base.
  3. Assemble: Brush the bases lightly with olive oil. Let kids spread tomato sauce on their base.
  4. Top with cheese: Sprinkle vegan cheese evenly.
  5. Add toppings: Encourage kids to decorate their pizzas with their favorite vegetables and fruits from the topping selection.
  6. Bake: Place pizzas on a baking sheet and bake for 12–15 minutes until crispy and golden.
  7. Cool and eat: Allow to cool slightly before enjoying.

Tips:

  • Homemade vegan cheese made from cashews, nutritional yeast, and lemon juice provides a nourishing option.
  • Let kids experiment with unusual toppings like roasted chickpeas or fresh herbs.

3. Smoothie Bowls: A Colorful and Nutrient-Packed Breakfast

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Serves: 2

Smoothie bowls are a fantastic way to sneak fruits, vegetables, and superfoods into kids’ diets. Making these together is fun, especially when it comes to decorating the top.

Ingredients:

  • 2 frozen bananas
  • 1 cup frozen mixed berries
  • 1 cup spinach or kale leaves (optional)
  • 1 cup plant-based milk (almond, oat, soy)
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter or almond butter
  • Toppings: sliced fruit, granola, chia seeds, shredded coconut, pumpkin seeds

Instructions:

  1. Blend the base: Combine frozen bananas, berries, greens (if using), plant-based milk, and nut butter in a blender. Blend until smooth and thick.
  2. Pour: Scoop the smoothie mixture into bowls.
  3. Top: Let kids decorate with their choice of toppings. They can create smiley faces, patterns, or just sprinkle freely.
  4. Serve immediately with spoons and enjoy spooning out every last drop.

Tips:

  • Use frozen fruit to give your smoothie bowl a creamy, ice-cream-like texture.
  • Swap peanut butter for seed butter if allergies are a concern.

4. Chickpea and Veggie Patties: Protein-Packed and Kid-Friendly

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes
Makes: 8 small patties

Perfect for little hands to shape and press, chickpea patties combine protein and veggies in a delicious, crispy bite. Serve with ketchup or your child’s favorite sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small carrot, grated
  • 1/2 cup zucchini, grated
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (use gluten-free if needed)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ground flaxseed + 6 tablespoons water (flax egg)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Make the flax egg: Combine ground flaxseed and water, then let it sit for 5 minutes until gel-like.
  2. Mash chickpeas: In a large bowl, mash chickpeas with a fork or potato masher.
  3. Add veggies and herbs: Stir in grated carrot, zucchini, onion, garlic, parsley, and the flax egg.
  4. Mix breadcrumbs and seasoning: Add breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper. Mix until the mixture holds together.
  5. Shape patties: Let kids form small, even-sized patties with their hands.
  6. Cook: Heat a skillet with olive oil over medium heat. Fry patties for 3–4 minutes on each side until golden.
  7. Serve warm: Pair with a side salad, in a sandwich bun, or as a snack.

Tips:

  • Baking patties on a lined sheet at 375°F (190°C) for 15–20 minutes is a less oily alternative.
  • Add spices like cumin or paprika for a flavor twist.

5. No-Bake Energy Bites: Sweet, Nutty, and Ready in Minutes

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Makes: 12 bites

A great treat to make ahead and keep in the fridge, energy bites are customizable and nutritious. Kids love rolling balls and getting hands-on with ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter or sunflower seed butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1/4 cup vegan chocolate chips or dried fruit (cranberries, raisins)
  • 2 tablespoons chia seeds or flaxseeds
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix all ingredients: Combine oats, nut butter, sweetener, seeds, chocolate chips or fruit, vanilla, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Chill slightly: Refrigerate the mixture for 15 minutes to firm up.
  3. Roll into balls: Let kids scoop spoonfuls and roll into bite-sized balls.
  4. Store: Keep energy bites in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.
  5. Enjoy a healthy snack anytime!

Tips:

  • Swap toppings for shredded coconut, chopped nuts, or crushed pretzels.
  • Use sunflower or pumpkin seed butter for nut-free versions.

Final Thoughts: Making Plant-Based Cooking a Joyful Adventure

Vegan cooking with kids doesn’t have to be complicated or restrictive. It’s about exploring flavors, textures, and colors in a playful, inclusive way. These simple recipes invite children to take part in every stage of cooking—from assembling ingredients to tasting and sharing.

Keep your kitchen stocked with versatile plant-based basics like beans, grains, nuts, and fresh produce, and your kids will be ready to cook up a storm whenever creativity strikes. And perhaps most importantly, celebrate all successes and delicious outcomes, no matter how imperfect—because every meal shared together is a win.

Happy cooking and happy eating!
