Instant Pot Chicken Curry: A Spicy and Savory Recipe


Chicken curry is a global favorite steeped in rich flavors and vibrant spices. Whether you’re accustomed to making it at home or simply love indulging at your favorite restaurant, there’s something special about the warm, comforting aroma of curry simmering in the pot. Today, we’re elevating this classic dish using the Instant Pot—a modern-day kitchen hero. This streamlined cooking method not only preserves the authentic flavor of traditional chicken curry but also allows you to save precious time without sacrificing quality. Packed with spices, tender chicken, and velvety curry sauce, this Instant Pot chicken curry is perfect for both weeknight dinners and hearty weekend meals.

Why Make Chicken Curry in the Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot has revolutionized home cooking, particularly for dishes that typically require hours of simmering to coax out deep flavors. With its pressure-cooking function, you can achieve the same rich complexity in a fraction of the time. Making chicken curry in the Instant Pot combines efficiency with authenticity, ensuring that the chicken remains tender, the sauce turns out perfectly creamy, and the spices meld together wonderfully.

Additionally, the Instant Pot minimizes the mess by allowing you to cook everything in one pot—a lifesaver when it’s time to clean up. For novice cooks and experienced chefs alike, this recipe is a game changer.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The key to a delectable chicken curry lies in its seasoning and balance of flavors. Below are the ingredients you’ll need to make this savory recipe:

Main Ingredients:

  • Chicken: 2 pounds of bone-in or boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • Onions: 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped. Onions form the base of the gravy and add sweetness.
  • Tomatoes: 2 large tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped. They provide a tangy depth to the curry.
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced.
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, grated.
  • Coconut milk: 1 cup (optional for creamy texture).

Spices:

  • Ground turmeric: 1 teaspoon for a golden hue and earthy flavor.
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon for heat (adjust according to taste).
  • Ground cumin: 1 teaspoon for a warm, nutty note.
  • Ground coriander: 1 teaspoon to add aromatic complexity.
  • Garam masala: 2 teaspoons, a quintessential blend for Indian curries.
  • Bay leaf: 1, for subtle fragrance.
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small stick, optional for an additional layer of spiced richness.

Liquids and Oils:

  • Vegetable oil or ghee: 2 tablespoons for sautéing.
  • Water: 1 cup, to adjust the consistency of the curry.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Fresh cilantro: Chopped, for garnish.
  • Green chilies: 1-2, slit lengthwise.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Ingredients

Begin by preparing all your ingredients. Chop the onions and tomatoes, mince the garlic, grate the ginger, and cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Having everything ready helps streamline the cooking process.

Step 2: Sauté Aromatics in the Instant Pot

Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Add 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee and allow it to heat. Once hot, toss in the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown—this may take about 5 minutes. Stir frequently to enhance the caramelization process.

Next, add garlic and ginger to the pot. Sauté for another 1-2 minutes, and allow the aromatics to release their fragrance. Now, add your spices: turmeric, ground cumin, coriander, and garam masala. Stir the mixture so the spices gently toast in the oil, enhancing their flavors.

Step 3: Add Tomatoes

Pour the prepared tomato puree into the Instant Pot, mixing well with the sautéed aromatics and spices. Let the mixture cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring frequently. This helps create a delicious, thick curry base and ensures the raw flavor of the tomatoes is cooked out.

Step 4: Add Chicken

Place the chicken pieces into the pot, coating them evenly with the sauce. Sauté the chicken for 2-3 minutes with the spice mixture, sealing in the flavors before pressure cooking.

Step 5: Liquid Additions

Add one cup of water to the pot, stirring well. If using coconut milk for creaminess, this is the time to pour it in. The coconut milk adds richness to the curry and balances the heat from the chili powder. Ensure everything is well-mixed.

Step 6: Pressure Cook

Cancel the “Sauté” function on the Instant Pot and seal the lid. Set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” mode on high pressure for 10 minutes. Releasing pressure can be done manually or naturally, depending on your preference and time constraints. Manual release is faster but natural release allows the flavors to deepen further.

Step 7: Final Touches

Once the pressure is released, remove the lid carefully. Stir the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the curry is too thin, switch back to the “Sauté” function to simmer it and thicken the sauce to your desired consistency.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the chicken curry to a serving dish and garnish with chopped cilantro for a fresh burst of flavor. If desired, add slit green chilies for extra heat.

Serving Suggestions

Instant Pot chicken curry pairs beautifully with steamed rice, naan, or paratha. The velvety sauce is perfect for soaking up the bread or drizzled generously over rice. For a keto-friendly option, serve it with cauliflower rice or a side of sautéed vegetables.

This dish also sits well with refreshing accompaniments like cucumber raita or a simple salad dressed with lime. The tangy and cooling flavors balance the spiciness of the curry perfectly.

Storage Tips

Any leftovers can be stored for up to 3 days in an airtight container in the refrigerator. To reheat, simply simmer the curry on the stovetop or use the “Sauté” function on the Instant Pot. You may need to add a splash of water or coconut milk to restore its creamy texture.

Why This Recipe Stands Out

The Instant Pot chicken curry recipe is a harmonious blend of ease, flavor, and nourishment. By embracing the Instant Pot, you can recreate the depth of a slow-cooked curry in significantly less time. Furthermore, this dish is versatile—customizable to suit your spice tolerance or dietary preferences, whether that means swapping regular coconut milk for a lighter version or using boneless chicken instead.

This recipe celebrates every savory, spicy note that makes chicken curry such an iconic dish while demonstrating how modern cooking techniques can amplify tradition. It’s perfect for cozy family dinners or impressing your guests with bold, vibrant flavors.

Welcome this Instant Pot chicken curry into your rotation, and you’ll quickly discover how it earns its place among your go-to weeknight meals.
