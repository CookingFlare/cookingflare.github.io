Wholesome Mediterranean Vegetarian Recipes


The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its health benefits, and it’s no wonder why. Bursting with fresh vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and healthy fats, this diet is not only nutritious but also delicious. If you’re looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these wholesome Mediterranean vegetarian recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and nourish your body.

1. Greek Spinach and Feta Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough
  • 2 lbs of fresh spinach
  • 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the fresh spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  4. Once cooled, squeeze out any excess liquid from the spinach and transfer it to a large bowl.
  5. Add the crumbled feta cheese to the spinach mixture and season with salt and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  6. Lay out a sheet of phyllo dough and brush it lightly with olive oil. Repeat with 5 more sheets, layering them on top of each other.
  7. Place the spinach and feta mixture on top of the phyllo dough and spread it out evenly.
  8. Layer 6 more sheets of phyllo dough on top of the spinach mixture, brushing each sheet with olive oil.
  9. Tuck in the edges of the phyllo dough to form a neat pie.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the pie is golden brown.
  11. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Mediterranean Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of quinoa
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup of Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup of red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water and cook according to package instructions. Once cooked, fluff it with a fork and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, diced cucumber, cherry tomatoes, Kalamata olives, red onion, parsley, and mint.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the dressing over the quinoa salad and toss until everything is well coated.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

3. Stuffed Bell Peppers with Mediterranean Couscous

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers
  • 1 cup of couscous
  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup of sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of pine nuts
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the couscous, chickpeas, sun-dried tomatoes, pine nuts, garlic, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff the mixture into the bell peppers and place them in a baking dish.
  5. Drizzle the stuffed bell peppers with olive oil and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy the flavorful Mediterranean goodness.

These recipes are not only satisfying and flavorful but also packed with nutrients. Whether you’re a committed vegetarian or simply looking to enjoy more meatless meals, these Mediterranean-inspired dishes are a perfect addition to your recipe repertoire. Embrace the vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients of the Mediterranean diet with these delightful vegetarian recipes. Happy cooking and bon appétit!
