Sous Vide Carrots with Maple Glaze


If you’ve ever wondered how to achieve perfectly tender, flavorful carrots without overcooking or losing nutrients, sous vide is the answer. This method allows precise control over cooking temperatures, ensuring consistently excellent results every time. Pairing sous vide carrots with a rich maple glaze brings out their natural sweetness while adding a delightful depth of flavor. This recipe is sure to become a favorite for family dinners or special occasions.

Why Sous Vide Works Wonders for Carrots

Unlike traditional boiling or steaming methods, sous vide cooking locks in flavor and preserves the vibrant color and texture of carrots. When cooked in a sealed bag submerged in water at a controlled temperature, vegetables retain their nutrients and moisture without becoming mushy. The sous vide technique is an excellent choice for carrots because it highlights their natural sweetness while creating an irresistible texture—tender yet slightly crisp.

Maple Glaze: A Sweet and Savory Finish

The maple glaze in this recipe is a harmonious balance of sweetness and umami. Maple syrup provides natural sweetness without overwhelming the carrots, while butter adds richness and savory notes. A touch of fresh thyme or rosemary elevates the glaze with herbaceous flavor, making it the perfect accompaniment for a side dish that feels elevated yet approachable.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Carrots with Maple Glaze

Let’s jump straight into the ingredients you’ll need for this delightful recipe:

For Sous Vide Carrots:

  • 1 lb (about 450g) fresh carrots, peeled and trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: a pinch of ground cumin or smoked paprika for added depth

For the Maple Glaze:

  • 3 tbsp maple syrup (preferably real, not artificial)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme or rosemary leaves (optional)
  • Zest of 1 orange (optional) for a refreshing citrus twist

Equipment Needed

To successfully create this recipe, you’ll need the following tools:

  • Sous vide immersion circulator
  • Large pot or sous vide water bath container
  • Vacuum-sealed bags or high-quality resealable freezer bags
  • A large skillet for glazing

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Water Bath

Fill your pot or container with water, and set the sous vide immersion circulator to 183°F (84°C). This temperature is ideal for carrots, ensuring they come out perfectly tender without losing their natural snap.

Step 2: Season the Carrots

Start by peeling and trimming the carrots. If they’re particularly thick, you can slice them lengthwise into even halves to ensure uniform cooking. Toss the carrots with olive oil, kosher salt, and black pepper. For a hint of complexity, add ground cumin or smoked paprika.

Place the seasoned carrots into vacuum-sealed bags or high-quality freezer bags suitable for sous vide cooking. Ensure the carrots are spread out in a single layer inside the bag to promote even heat distribution.

Step 3: Seal the Bags

Vacuum-seal the bags using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method. If you’re using resealable bags, try the water displacement method by carefully lowering the bag into the water bath while sealing the top to push out excess air.

Step 4: Cook the Carrots

Submerge the sealed bags in the preheated sous vide water bath, and cook for 90 minutes. The longer cooking time ensures your carrots become tender enough while retaining their firmness.

Step 5: Make the Maple Glaze

While the carrots are cooking, prepare the glaze. Combine the maple syrup, melted butter, and fresh thyme or rosemary in a small bowl. Whisk together until smooth, and set aside. If you’re feeling adventurous, a pinch of orange zest adds a refreshing touch.

Step 6: Finish with the Glaze

Once the sous vide cooking is complete, remove the carrots from their bags. Heat a large skillet over medium heat, and pour in the maple syrup mixture. Allow the glaze to gently bubble and reduce slightly. Add the carrots to the skillet, tossing them in the glaze until they are evenly coated and the syrup starts to caramelize lightly.

Step 7: Serve Warm

Transfer the glazed carrots to a serving dish, and garnish with fresh herbs if desired. The glossy, sweet maple glaze pairs beautifully with their naturally earthy flavor and vibrant color.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Carrots with Maple Glaze

  • Choose Fresh Carrots: Opt for fresh, firm carrots with a bright orange color. If using rainbow carrots, you’ll add an extra burst of visual appeal to your dish.
  • Balance the Flavors: Taste the glaze before adding it to the carrots, and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add a pinch of salt or an extra dash of orange zest for more complexity.
  • Use High-Quality Maple Syrup: Real, pure maple syrup is essential for achieving the best flavor in the glaze. Avoid imitation syrups, as they contain added sugars and artificial flavors.
  • Customize Your Spices: If you’re serving these carrots alongside an earthy main dish, experiment with spices like cinnamon or nutmeg in the glaze for a cozy, seasonal twist.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide carrots with maple glaze are an excellent side dish for a variety of meals. Pair them with roasted poultry, glazed ham, or a hearty plant-based roast. For a vegetarian feast, serve these alongside wild rice pilaf or a crisp arugula salad. Their versatility ensures they’ll shine in practically any culinary setting.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (though they might not last long!), store the carrots in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. When reheating, gently warm them in a skillet over low heat to preserve their texture and glossy glaze.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide carrots with maple glaze are a testament to how simple ingredients, combined with innovative cooking techniques, can create extraordinary results. This dish captures the essence of farm-fresh carrots, transforming them into a side dish that’s elegant yet easy to execute. Whether you’re new to sous vide cooking or a seasoned expert, this recipe is sure to bring joy to your table with its balanced flavors and impeccable texture. Give it a try, and let the magic of sous vide elevate your culinary repertoire!
