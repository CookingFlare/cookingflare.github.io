Sous Vide Pulled Pork for Your Next BBQ


Are you looking to elevate your BBQ game to a new level? Whether you’re an experienced pitmaster or a beginner in the world of grilling, sous vide pulled pork can transform your backyard BBQ into a memorable feast. This cooking method offers unmatched tenderness, juiciness, and flavor, giving you the perfect pulled pork every time. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to make sous vide pulled pork for your next BBQ.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise, controlled temperature. This slow cooking process allows the meat to cook evenly, breaking down collagen and fat, resulting in incredibly tender, juicy cuts. Unlike traditional BBQ methods that rely on direct heat and long smoking times, sous vide takes the guesswork out of cooking. You don’t have to worry about overcooking or undercooking the meat – it’s all about the precise temperature control.

Why Choose Sous Vide for Pulled Pork?

Pulled pork is traditionally made by slow-cooking a pork shoulder or butt on a smoker or in a slow cooker. While these methods work, they require constant attention and patience. With sous vide, you can achieve the same (if not better) results with less hassle. Here are some reasons why sous vide is the ultimate method for making pulled pork:

  • Precision Cooking: You control the exact temperature, ensuring the meat cooks perfectly every time. No more worrying about hot spots or cooking too fast.
  • Consistency: The sous vide method guarantees a consistent result, whether you’re cooking a small pork butt or a large one.
  • Tender, Juicy Meat: The slow cooking process at a low temperature breaks down the collagen in the meat, resulting in ultra-tender and juicy pulled pork.
  • No Need for Constant Monitoring: Once the pork is in the water bath, you don’t have to keep an eye on it, unlike grilling or smoking where you need to constantly tend to the fire.

The Key to Perfect Sous Vide Pulled Pork: Choosing the Right Cut

When it comes to making pulled pork, the best cut of meat is the pork shoulder, also known as pork butt or Boston butt. This cut is marbled with fat and connective tissue, which makes it perfect for low and slow cooking. The fat renders beautifully during the sous vide process, resulting in juicy, tender meat that’s easy to shred.

Pork Shoulder vs. Pork Loin

It’s important to distinguish between pork shoulder and pork loin when making pulled pork. Pork shoulder is the preferred cut for pulled pork because of its fat content and connective tissue, which break down during long cooking times, resulting in tender meat. Pork loin, on the other hand, is leaner and less suitable for pulled pork, as it can dry out during the cooking process.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Pulled Pork

For this sous vide pulled pork recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pork:

  • 4-5 pounds pork shoulder (also called pork butt)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp mustard powder
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar

For the Sauce (Optional, but highly recommended):

  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar (for sweetness)

Step-by-Step Guide to Sous Vide Pulled Pork

Step 1: Prepare the Pork Shoulder

First, take the pork shoulder and remove any excess skin or large chunks of fat. It’s up to you whether you want to trim it entirely or leave some of the fat intact for extra flavor. Next, generously season the pork with salt and pepper.

Now, create a spice rub by mixing the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, mustard powder, cumin, coriander, and dried thyme. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork, making sure to coat it evenly. The spices will form a flavorful crust during the sous vide cooking process.

Once the pork is fully seasoned, drizzle it with apple cider vinegar. The vinegar adds a tangy kick and helps tenderize the meat during cooking. After this, it’s time to vacuum-seal the pork.

Step 2: Vacuum Seal the Pork

Place the seasoned pork into a vacuum-seal bag. Use a vacuum sealer to remove all the air and seal the bag tightly. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a resealable ziplock bag and the water displacement method: place the pork in the bag, slowly lower it into a pot of water, and seal the bag once most of the air has been displaced.

Step 3: Set Up the Sous Vide Water Bath

Fill a large pot or sous vide container with water and set your immersion circulator to 165°F (74°C). This is the ideal temperature for tender, juicy pulled pork. If you prefer your pork to be even more tender, you can set the temperature slightly lower at 145°F (63°C), but it will require a longer cooking time.

Step 4: Sous Vide Cook the Pork

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, carefully submerge the vacuum-sealed pork shoulder into the water bath. Make sure the bag is fully submerged and not floating, as this could affect the cooking process.

Let the pork cook for 18-24 hours. While this may seem like a long time, the beauty of sous vide cooking is that the low, slow process allows the meat to become incredibly tender without the risk of overcooking. The result is perfectly cooked, fall-apart pork that’s ideal for shredding.

Step 5: Finish the Pork

After the pork has finished cooking, remove the bag from the water bath. Be careful, as the pork will be extremely hot. At this point, you can either finish the pork in a couple of different ways:

  • Crisp the Exterior: If you want a bit of crispy, caramelized crust on the pork, heat a skillet over high heat and sear the pork on all sides. This will add a nice texture and flavor contrast to the tender meat.
  • Shred the Pork: If you’re ready to dive in, simply remove the pork from the bag, discard any excess fat, and use two forks to shred the meat. It should pull apart effortlessly.

Step 6: Make the Sauce (Optional)

If you want to serve the pulled pork with a tangy sauce, combine BBQ sauce with apple cider vinegar and a touch of honey or brown sugar in a saucepan. Heat over medium-low heat until the sauce is warm and slightly thickened. Drizzle this sauce over the shredded pork before serving, or toss the pork in the sauce to coat it.

Tips for the Best Sous Vide Pulled Pork

  • Plan Ahead: Because sous vide cooking takes so long, it’s best to plan ahead. You can easily cook the pork a day or two in advance and store it in the refrigerator. When you’re ready to serve, simply reheat it in the bag or in a skillet.
  • Season Generously: The key to flavorful pulled pork is a well-seasoned spice rub. Don’t be afraid to get bold with your spices. Experiment with adding ingredients like chili powder, cumin, or smoked paprika to create a unique flavor profile.
  • Let the Pork Rest: After cooking, let the pork rest for a few minutes before shredding. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it even more juicy and flavorful.
  • Cook for Longer for Extra Tenderness: If you have the time, cooking the pork shoulder for 24 hours will yield an incredibly tender result. For less time, around 18 hours, will still give you delicious pulled pork, but it may not be as fall-apart tender.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide pulled pork is incredibly versatile and can be served in many different ways. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Classic Pulled Pork Sandwiches: Pile the shredded pulled pork onto soft hamburger buns, and top with coleslaw and your favorite BBQ sauce for a classic sandwich.
  • Pulled Pork Tacos: Use tortillas to make delicious pulled pork tacos. Add fresh toppings like salsa, cilantro, and avocado for a flavorful bite.
  • Pulled Pork Nachos: Layer tortilla chips with pulled pork, cheese, jalapeños, and other nacho toppings, then bake until the cheese is melted for a crowd-pleasing snack.
  • Pulled Pork Bowls: Serve the pulled pork over rice or in a salad bowl, with your favorite vegetables and sauce.

Conclusion

Sous vide pulled pork is the ultimate way to achieve tender, juicy, and flavorful pulled pork for your next BBQ. By following these simple steps, you can ensure that your guests will be wowed by the delicious, melt-in-your-mouth texture of your pulled pork. Whether you’re serving it in sandwiches, tacos, or bowls, sous vide pulled pork is guaranteed to become a staple in your BBQ repertoire.

So, fire up your sous vide machine, grab a pork shoulder, and let the magic of slow cooking transform your next BBQ into something truly special!
