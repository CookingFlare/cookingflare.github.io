DIY Fruit Salsa with Cinnamon Chips for Kids


Are you looking for a fun and healthy snack that your kids will love? Look no further! This DIY fruit salsa with cinnamon chips is the perfect recipe to satisfy their taste buds while also providing them with essential nutrients. It’s a great way to introduce your little ones to a variety of fruits and make snack time more exciting. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the Fruit Salsa:

  • 1 apple, diced
  • 1 cup strawberries, diced
  • 1 cup pineapple, diced
  • 1 cup kiwi, diced
  • 1 cup mango, diced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice

For the Cinnamon Chips:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine all the diced fruits - apple, strawberries, pineapple, kiwi, and mango. Mix them gently to ensure that the fruits retain their shape.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and fresh lime juice. Pour this mixture over the diced fruits and gently toss until all the fruits are coated. Set the fruit salsa aside to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Now, let’s move on to the cinnamon chips. Take the flour tortillas and brush them with melted butter on both sides. Sprinkle a mixture of sugar and cinnamon evenly over the tortillas.

  5. Stack the tortillas on top of each other and cut them into wedges using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter. Place the cinnamon chips on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer.

  6. Bake the cinnamon chips for about 10-12 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Keep an eye on them as they can burn quickly.

  7. Once the cinnamon chips are ready, remove them from the oven and let them cool completely. They will continue to crisp up as they cool down.

  8. Serve the delicious fruit salsa in a bowl and arrange the cinnamon chips around it. You can also serve them separately in a plate. Encourage your kids to dip the cinnamon chips into the fruity salsa for a burst of flavors!

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize the fruit salsa according to your child’s preferences. You can add or substitute fruits like blueberries, raspberries, or even diced watermelon.
  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can sprinkle a little bit of chili powder or a squeeze of lemon juice over the fruit salsa.
  • If your child has a nut allergy, you can omit the honey and replace it with maple syrup or agave nectar.
  • Get your kids involved in the preparation process. Let them help with washing and cutting the fruits, as well as brushing the melted butter on the tortillas. It’s a great opportunity for them to learn about different fruits and develop their culinary skills.

This DIY fruit salsa with cinnamon chips is a fantastic snack idea that combines the sweetness of fresh fruits with the delightful crunch of cinnamon chips. It’s a treat that your kids will enjoy and a wholesome way to introduce them to a variety of fruits. So why not give it a try and watch your little ones devour this colorful and delicious snack? Happy snacking!
