Vegan Molasses Cookies: Soft, Chewy, and Spiced


The comforting aroma of cookies baking in the oven is enough to draw anyone into the kitchen, especially when it comes to molasses cookies. These vegan molasses cookies bring together the warmth of spices, the rich depth of molasses, and the irresistible chewiness that cookie lovers crave. Perfect for holiday gatherings, quick desserts, or simply as an afternoon treat with a cup of tea, they’re a true testament to how plant-based baking can be just as indulgent and satisfying as traditional recipes.

This recipe maintains all the key traits you’d expect in a classic molasses cookie—soft on the inside, slightly crisp on the edges, and perfectly spiced with cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and cloves. Whether you’re vegan or just looking to enjoy a flavorful cookie without dairy or eggs, these molasses cookies are bound to become a favorite in your home. Let’s dive into what makes them so special and how you can whip up a batch today.

The Secret Behind the Perfect Vegan Molasses Cookie

Creating the perfect vegan molasses cookie hinges on achieving just the right texture and flavor. Without traditional staples like butter or eggs, this might seem daunting, but it’s easier than you think with the right plant-based substitutions.

Texture: Chewy and Soft

The hallmark of a great molasses cookie is its texture. To achieve that classic chewiness, we use plant-based fat, such as vegan butter or coconut oil, combined with the moisture provided by molasses. Both ingredients serve to keep the dough from drying out while baking, delivering that tender bite you’re aiming for. In this recipe, unsweetened applesauce or flaxseed meal acts as your egg replacement, contributing to a softer crumb without compromising on structure.

Flavor: Deep and Spiced

Molasses is, of course, the star ingredient here. It lends an earthy sweetness with caramel-like undertones while pairing harmoniously with fragrant spices like cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg. These spices not only give the cookies their signature warmth but also add complexity and balance to every bite.

Key Ingredients and Their Roles

  1. Molasses
    This dark, viscous syrup provides a rich flavor and enhances chewiness. Unsulphured molasses is recommended for baking, as it’s milder and slightly sweeter than blackstrap molasses.

  2. Vegan Butter or Coconut Oil
    These provide the fat needed for moisture and richness. Vegan butter works particularly well for a neutral flavor, while coconut oil can add a subtle hint of coconut.

  3. Brown Sugar
    Brown sugar contains natural molasses, which enhances the flavor and keeps the cookies soft. Its slightly moist nature is key for a chewy texture.

  4. Flour
    All-purpose flour gives the cookies structure, while a small amount of cornstarch in the mix can make the texture even more tender.

  5. Unsweetened Applesauce or Flax Egg
    Applesauce adds moisture and acts as the binder, while flaxseed meal and water (a flax egg) provide a similar effect with extra nuttiness.

  6. Baking Soda
    This leavening agent helps the cookies rise slightly and spread to achieve the desired shape.

  7. Warm Spices
    Cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and cloves are the traditional spices in molasses cookies. Adjust the quantity to fit your preference, but don’t skip them—they’re essential for the flavor.

Vegan Molasses Cookies Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter or coconut oil, softened
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (plus extra for rolling)
  • 1/4 cup unsulphured molasses
  • 3 tbsp unsweetened applesauce (or 1 flax egg: 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water, mixed and rested)
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, cream the vegan butter (or coconut oil) with the brown sugar and granulated sugar using a hand mixer or whisk until the mixture is smooth and fluffy.
  3. Pour in the molasses, applesauce (or prepared flax egg), and vanilla extract. Mix well to combine.

Step 2: Combine Ingredients

  1. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a soft dough forms. Avoid overmixing, as this can toughen the cookies.
  2. Cover the dough with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and chill it in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. Chilling helps the dough firm up, making it easier to handle and allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 3: Shape and Roll

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Scoop out tablespoons of dough and roll them into uniform balls. Roll each dough ball in granulated sugar for a sparkly, crisp exterior. Arrange the balls on the prepared baking sheets, leaving about 2 inches of space between each to allow for spreading.

Step 4: Bake to Perfection

  1. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10–12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers appear slightly underbaked.
  2. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Pro Tips for Perfect Vegan Molasses Cookies

  • Chill the Dough: Don’t skip the chilling step! It’s essential for preventing excessive spreading while baking and helps develop the flavors.
  • Adjust the Spices: If you prefer a spicier cookie, increase the ginger or cinnamon slightly. For a milder flavor, reduce the cloves and nutmeg.
  • Store Properly: Store cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. They’ll stay soft and flavorful as long as they’re sealed.

Why You’ll Love These Cookies

These vegan molasses cookies are a celebration of everything cozy and delicious. With their rich molasses flavor, warm spices, and irresistible texture, they’re perfect for any occasion. Plus, this plant-based recipe is simple to prepare and made with pantry staples, making it an excellent go-to treat whenever the craving strikes. Serve them with a cup of hot cocoa, herbal tea, or your favorite coffee for an experience that’ll warm your heart as much as your taste buds.

So the next time you’re in the mood for something sweet yet wholesome, try these vegan molasses cookies. Whether you’re indulging solo or sharing with friends and family, this recipe is sure to leave everyone reaching for seconds!
