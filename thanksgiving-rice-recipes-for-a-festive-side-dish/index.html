Thanksgiving Rice Recipes for a Festive Side Dish


Thanksgiving is all about gathering with loved ones, sharing delicious food, and creating unforgettable memories. While turkey often takes center stage at the Thanksgiving table, the side dishes play a crucial role in making the meal complete. One versatile and delicious side dish that can easily elevate your holiday feast is rice. Whether you prefer a savory, nutty, or sweet option, rice can be the perfect complement to the rich flavors of Thanksgiving dinner.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of Thanksgiving rice recipes that bring festive flair to your table. These rice dishes are simple to make, flavorful, and designed to impress your guests, making them an ideal choice for this special occasion.

1. Herb-Infused Rice with Roasted Vegetables

The aromatic herbs combined with the natural sweetness of roasted vegetables make this rice dish an incredible addition to your Thanksgiving spread. The earthy flavors of rosemary, thyme, and sage will remind your guests of the season, and the roasted vegetables bring a sweet and savory balance to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of long-grain white rice
  • 3 cups of vegetable broth or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup of carrots, sliced thinly
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: In a medium-sized pot, bring the vegetable broth or chicken broth to a boil. Add the rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 18 minutes or until the rice is tender and all the liquid is absorbed. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes.

  2. Roast the Vegetables: While the rice is cooking, preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the zucchini, red bell pepper, and carrots with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast the vegetables for 20–25 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly caramelized. Remove from the oven.

  3. Combine Everything: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing for 2–3 minutes until they are soft and fragrant. Stir in the chopped rosemary, thyme, and sage, cooking for another minute.

  4. Assemble the Dish: Add the cooked rice to the skillet with the sautéed onions and garlic, stirring gently to combine. Fold in the roasted vegetables, making sure the rice is evenly mixed with the vegetables and herbs. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley before serving for a pop of color. This herb-infused rice pairs beautifully with turkey or other roasted meats.

2. Cranberry Almond Rice Pilaf

Sweet, tart cranberries combined with crunchy almonds create a festive and flavorful rice pilaf that will brighten up any Thanksgiving spread. The slight sweetness of cranberries balances out the richness of the turkey and gravy, making it an excellent choice for a side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup fresh or dried cranberries
  • ½ cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh orange zest (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: In a medium saucepan, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Let it cook for about 18 minutes until the rice is tender and all the liquid is absorbed. Remove from the heat and let it sit for an additional 5 minutes.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: While the rice is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing for 4–5 minutes until soft and golden brown. Stir in the cinnamon and ground ginger, allowing the spices to bloom for about 1 minute.

  3. Combine the Ingredients: Stir in the cooked rice, fresh or dried cranberries, and the toasted almonds. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If using fresh cranberries, you may want to gently stir them in, allowing them to pop and release their juices into the rice.

  4. Finish with Orange Zest: For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle some fresh orange zest over the pilaf just before serving. This rice pilaf is both festive and flavorful, and it pairs perfectly with your Thanksgiving turkey or ham.

3. Pumpkin Spice Rice with Pecans

For those looking for a unique and slightly sweet side dish to complement the Thanksgiving meal, this pumpkin spice rice with crunchy pecans is a showstopper. The earthy flavor of pumpkin combines beautifully with the warm spices of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups brown rice
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 cup toasted pecans, roughly chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: In a large pot, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the brown rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Cook the rice for about 40–45 minutes until it’s tender and all the liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 10 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Pumpkin Mixture: In a separate saucepan, combine the pumpkin puree, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and maple syrup over medium heat. Stir occasionally and cook for 5–7 minutes until the mixture is heated through and fragrant.

  3. Combine Rice and Pumpkin: Fluff the cooked rice with a fork, then fold in the pumpkin mixture. Stir until the rice is evenly coated with the spices and pumpkin. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Finish with Pecans: Fold in the toasted pecans, adding a nice crunch to the dish. Garnish with fresh thyme for a touch of color and earthiness.

This pumpkin spice rice is a perfect side dish to serve alongside roasted meats, particularly turkey or even roasted vegetables for a vegetarian option.

4. Garlic Parmesan Rice with Spinach

For a more savory and comforting rice dish, try this garlic parmesan rice with spinach. The richness of Parmesan cheese, combined with the depth of garlic and the fresh bite of spinach, makes this dish a perfect accompaniment to your Thanksgiving feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups jasmine rice
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the jasmine rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Cook for about 18 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Remove from the heat and let it sit for 5 minutes.

  2. Sauté Garlic: While the rice is cooking, melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 2–3 minutes until fragrant but not browned.

  3. Add Spinach: Add the chopped spinach to the skillet, cooking for 1–2 minutes until wilted. Remove the skillet from the heat.

  4. Combine the Ingredients: Fluff the cooked rice with a fork, then add the sautéed garlic and spinach to the rice. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until everything is well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh basil before serving. This garlic Parmesan rice is a savory option that works wonderfully alongside turkey, mashed potatoes, and other traditional Thanksgiving dishes.

5. Bacon and Leek Rice

If you love the combination of bacon and savory flavors, this bacon and leek rice will make your Thanksgiving feast even more special. The crispy bacon adds depth and richness, while the leeks provide a mild, onion-like flavor that complements the rice perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 leek, thinly sliced (white and light green parts only)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: In a medium pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the basmati rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the rice is tender and all the liquid has been absorbed. Remove from the heat and set aside.

  2. Cook the Bacon: In a large skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy, about 5–7 minutes. Remove the bacon from the skillet and drain on paper towels.

  3. Sauté the Leeks: In the same skillet, add the olive oil and sliced leeks, cooking over medium heat until they are soft and slightly caramelized, about 5 minutes. Add the thyme and cook for an additional minute.

  4. Combine and Finish: Stir the cooked rice into the skillet with the leeks and thyme, ensuring everything is well mixed. Add the crispy bacon, and season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh chives before serving.

These Thanksgiving rice recipes offer a wonderful variety of flavors and textures to complement your holiday meal. Whether you prefer something savory, sweet, or a bit of both, rice is a perfect side dish that can be easily adapted to suit your preferences. Try one (or more) of these recipes at your Thanksgiving table, and enjoy a festive and delicious side dish that your guests will rave about.
