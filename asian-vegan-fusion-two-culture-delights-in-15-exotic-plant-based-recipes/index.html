1. Spaghettitisai with Warm Thai Peanut Sauce

Savor the fusion of classic Thai flavors with this delightful dish, featuring spaghettit-sai noodles tossed in a rich, creamy peanut sauce and garnished with chopped cilantro and roasted peanuts.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghettit-sai noodles
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup water, plus more if needed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 red chili pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghettit-sai noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine peanut butter, water, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, maple syrup, and red chili pepper. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture is well combined and slightly thickened.
  3. Gradually whisk in the almond milk, and continue heating until fully incorporated and the desired consistency is reached. If the sauce is too thick, add more water as needed.
  4. Toss the cooked noodles in the warm peanut sauce, then garnish with green onions, cilantro, and roasted peanuts. Serve immediately.

2. Teriyaki Tempeh Lettuce Wraps

A delicious Thai-inspired lettuce wrap, filled with tender teriyaki-glazed tempeh, cucumbers, red onions, and scallions for a spicy bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block tempeh, sliced into 1/4-inch strips
  • 1/4 cup tamari sauce
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp sriracha sauce
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 4 lettuce leaves (Boston or large romaine)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup scallions, thinly sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together tamari sauce, maple syrup, sriracha sauce, and grated ginger.
  2. In a frying pan, heat the tempeh strips over medium heat, then pour the teriyaki sauce mixture over the top. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until the tempeh is tender and caramelized.
  3. To assemble the lettuce wraps, place one or two tempeh strips in the center of each lettuce leaf, then top with sliced cucumber, red onion, and scallions. Fold the lettuce over to enclose the fillings, and enjoy.

3. Thai Jackfruit Curry Buns

Make your own bao buns to hold a delightful fusion of Thai red curry flavors infused with tender shredded young jackfruitâ€”a meaty-textured plant-based alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 10 ao tai (Vietnamese rice-paper thins)
  • 2 cups young jackfruit, shredded
  • 1 can (14 oz) Thai red curry paste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp garlic chili paste
  • 1 cup mung bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the Thai red curry paste, coconut milk, water, soy sauce, maple syrup, and garlic chili paste. Whisk until well combined, then simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes.
  2. Add the shredded jackfruit to the saucepan, stirring to coat, then let simmer for another 5-7 minutes, until the jackfruit has absorbed the flavors of the curry sauce.
  3. Steam the ao tai wrappers according to package instructions, then fill each with a portion of the Thai red curry jackfruit mixture. Top with mung bean sprouts, green onions, and a sprinkle of cilantro. Serve warm.

4. Udon Noodles with Tofu and Miso Broth

Embrace the umami of Japanese miso broth to enhance this flavorful dish, featuring tender udon noodles and pan-fried tofu cubes.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz udon noodles
  • 1 block firm tofu, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 cup baby spinach

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the dashi stock and water to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to low, then whisk in the miso paste and soy sauce, stirring until the miso is fully dissolved.
  2. In a frying pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Pan-fry the tofu cubes, turning occasionally, until crisp and golden brown on all sides.
  3. Cook the udon noodles according to package instructions in the miso broth, then drain. Divide the noodles into bowls, then top with the pan-fried tofu cubes and a handful of baby spinach. Drizzle each bowl with sesame oil to serve.
