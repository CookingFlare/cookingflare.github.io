Delectable Dak Galbi: A Spicy Korean Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe to Remember


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and spicy dishes, then you’re in for a treat with this delectable Dak Galbi recipe. Hailing from Korea, Dak Galbi is a tantalizing chicken stir-fry dish that will leave your taste buds begging for more. Packed with a fiery kick and a medley of vibrant vegetables, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup sweet potato, thinly sliced
  • 4-5 perilla leaves, torn into pieces (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, mirin, and sesame oil. Mix well to create a thick, spicy marinade.

  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and toss until they are well coated. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant and slightly translucent.

  4. Add the marinated chicken to the skillet and cook until it is browned and cooked through, approximately 5-7 minutes.

  5. Add the carrot, zucchini, cabbage, sweet potato, and perilla leaves (if using) to the skillet. Stir-fry for another 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the amount according to your preference for spiciness.

  7. Serve the Dak Galbi hot, garnished with additional perilla leaves if desired. It pairs perfectly with steamed rice or lettuce wraps for a refreshing touch.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add sliced green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds before serving.
  • If you prefer a milder version of Dak Galbi, reduce the amount of gochujang and gochugaru.
  • Feel free to experiment with additional vegetables such as mushrooms, bell peppers, or even broccoli to add more color and texture to the dish.
  • If you’re vegetarian, you can substitute the chicken with tofu or seitan for a scrumptious meatless alternative.

Dak Galbi is a versatile dish that allows you to get creative with your ingredients and spice levels. With its fiery flavors and vibrant colors, it’s no wonder why this dish has become a beloved staple in Korean cuisine. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the heat, and indulge in the unforgettable taste of Dak Galbi!
