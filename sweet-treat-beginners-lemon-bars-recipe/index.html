Sweet Treat: Beginner's Lemon Bars Recipe


If you’re a fan of citrusy desserts, then you’re in for a treat with this beginner-friendly lemon bars recipe. These delightful treats strike the perfect balance between tangy and sweet, making them an ideal choice for any occasion, from casual gatherings to elegant soirées. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just dipping your toes into the world of baking, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s dive into the delightful world of lemon bars!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • A pinch of salt

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2/3 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the 1 cup of flour, softened butter, 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar, and a pinch of salt. Mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Press the dough into an 8x8 inch baking pan, ensuring an even layer across the bottom.
  4. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until lightly golden.

Step 2: Making the Lemon Filling

  1. While the crust is baking, prepare the lemon filling. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and 1/4 cup of flour.
  2. Add the eggs, one at a time, and whisk until well combined.
  3. Stir in the fresh lemon juice and lemon zest until the mixture is smooth and well incorporated.

Step 3: Baking the Lemon Bars

  1. Once the crust is lightly golden, remove it from the oven and pour the lemon filling over the hot crust.
  2. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.
  3. Allow the lemon bars to cool completely in the pan.

Step 4: Serving

  1. Once cooled, dust the top of the lemon bars with confectioners’ sugar.
  2. Carefully lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper, then cut them into squares.
  3. Serve and enjoy these zesty, sweet lemon bars with a hot cup of tea or coffee.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra burst of citrus flavor, consider adding a few drops of lemon extract to the filling.
  • To achieve a perfectly even crust, use the bottom of a flat measuring cup to press the dough into the pan.
  • Store the lemon bars in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days for lasting freshness.

Conclusion

With their irresistible combination of buttery crust and luscious lemon filling, these lemon bars are a true delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re a novice baker or a seasoned pro, this recipe is a must-try for anyone with a penchant for bright, tangy flavors. So, gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and indulge in the sheer joy of homemade lemon bars – a sweet treat that’s bound to become a staple in your dessert repertoire. Happy baking!
