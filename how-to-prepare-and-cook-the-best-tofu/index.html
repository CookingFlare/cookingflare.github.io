How to Prepare and Cook the Best Tofu


Tofu, a beloved protein source in many cuisines, especially in plant-based and vegetarian dishes, is versatile, nutritious, and surprisingly delicious when prepared correctly. Whether you’re a seasoned tofu lover or a newcomer to this iconic ingredient, learning the best methods to prepare and cook tofu can elevate your meals to new heights. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the process of preparing and cooking tofu to perfection, offering helpful tips, techniques, and creative ideas to incorporate this protein-packed wonder into your everyday meals.

1. Choosing the Right Tofu for Your Dish

Before diving into cooking techniques, it’s essential to choose the right type of tofu for your dish. Tofu comes in various textures, each suitable for different types of recipes.

Silken Tofu

Silken tofu has a smooth, creamy texture and is ideal for dishes where you want a soft, custard-like consistency. It works great in smoothies, soups, sauces, desserts, and dressings. If you’re making a tofu-based dessert like tofu cheesecake or chocolate mousse, silken tofu is the way to go.

Firm and Extra-Firm Tofu

Firm and extra-firm tofu have a denser, more compact texture, making them perfect for stir-fries, grilling, baking, and even deep-frying. They can hold up to more intense cooking methods, and they’re easier to slice, cube, or crumble. When looking for tofu to sauté or grill, choose firm or extra-firm tofu for the best results.

Soft Tofu

Soft tofu is a bit more delicate than silken tofu but still has a softer texture. It’s great for soups and stews but can also be used in stir-fries if handled gently. Soft tofu is often used in savory and slightly watery dishes, and it has a more subtle flavor compared to its firmer counterparts.

For most home cooks, firm or extra-firm tofu is a popular choice due to its versatility and ability to hold its shape during cooking. You can find these varieties in most supermarkets, either in the refrigerated section or shelf-stable versions, which don’t need refrigeration until opened.

2. Pressing Tofu: The Key to Perfect Texture

Tofu is packed in water to maintain its moisture, which can affect its texture when cooking. Pressing tofu before cooking is an essential step, particularly for firm or extra-firm tofu, as it helps remove excess water, allowing the tofu to absorb marinades and seasonings more effectively and achieve a crispier texture when cooked.

How to Press Tofu:

  1. Remove the tofu from its package and drain any excess water.
  2. Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.
  3. Place the wrapped tofu on a flat surface (a cutting board works well) and add a heavy object on top, such as a cast-iron skillet, a few cans, or a tofu press if you have one.
  4. Let it press for at least 15-30 minutes. The longer you press it, the firmer and more compact the tofu will become.
  5. Unwrap the tofu and gently pat it dry with a paper towel to remove any remaining moisture.

Once pressed, the tofu will be much firmer and more suitable for stir-frying, grilling, and baking.

3. Marinating Tofu for Extra Flavor

One of the best ways to infuse tofu with flavor is by marinating it. Tofu has a neutral flavor on its own, which makes it the perfect canvas to absorb the flavors of various seasonings, spices, and herbs. You can marinate tofu for anywhere from 15 minutes to overnight, depending on how intense you want the flavor to be.

Simple Tofu Marinade Recipe:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup or agave nectar (for sweetness)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for crispiness)

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together all marinade ingredients in a bowl.
  2. Cut the pressed tofu into cubes or slices, depending on your recipe.
  3. Place the tofu pieces in a shallow dish or resealable plastic bag.
  4. Pour the marinade over the tofu, making sure each piece is well-coated.
  5. Let the tofu marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes, but for best results, let it marinate for 2-4 hours or overnight.

The longer the tofu marinates, the more flavorful it becomes. If you’re in a rush, even a quick 30-minute marinade can add a decent amount of flavor.

4. Cooking Methods: How to Achieve Perfectly Cooked Tofu

Once your tofu is pressed and marinated (or if you prefer it plain), it’s time to cook it! Here are some of the most popular and effective cooking methods to prepare tofu to perfection.

1. Sautéing

Sautéing tofu is one of the quickest and easiest ways to cook it, resulting in a golden, crispy exterior and a soft interior. This method works best with firm or extra-firm tofu.

How to Sauté Tofu:

  1. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add a small amount of oil, such as vegetable or sesame oil.
  3. Once the oil is hot, add the tofu cubes or slices in a single layer, ensuring there’s space between each piece.
  4. Let the tofu cook for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden and crispy. Avoid stirring it too often to allow it to form a crispy crust.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, or your favorite spices once the tofu is cooked.

Sautéed tofu can be added to stir-fries, salads, bowls, or enjoyed on its own with a dipping sauce.

2. Baking

Baking tofu is a hands-off cooking method that results in crispy and chewy tofu without the need for constant flipping or monitoring. It’s ideal when you want a large batch of tofu or want to prep it ahead of time for meal prep.

How to Bake Tofu:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange the marinated tofu pieces on a lined baking sheet, making sure the pieces aren’t crowded.
  3. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping the tofu halfway through.
  4. Once the tofu is golden brown and crispy on the outside, remove it from the oven and let it cool slightly.

Baked tofu is fantastic for meal prep, adding to salads, bowls, or even sandwiches.

3. Grilling

Grilling tofu is another fantastic way to infuse flavor and add a smoky char to the tofu’s exterior. Grilled tofu works especially well with thicker pieces or slices.

How to Grill Tofu:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the grill grates with oil or spray them with cooking spray to prevent sticking.
  3. Place the marinated tofu slices or cubes directly on the grill.
  4. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side until grill marks form, and the tofu has a slightly crispy outer layer.

Grilled tofu is a great addition to skewers, wraps, or served with a side of grilled vegetables.

4. Stir-Frying

Stir-frying is a fast, high-heat cooking method that creates crispy tofu pieces with a tender interior. This method works best with tofu that’s been pressed and marinated.

How to Stir-Fry Tofu:

  1. Heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add a bit of oil to the pan and wait for it to heat up.
  3. Add the tofu in a single layer and let it cook for a couple of minutes before stirring to prevent it from sticking.
  4. Stir-fry the tofu for 8-10 minutes until golden and crispy, then add vegetables and sauces to complete your stir-fry.

Stir-fried tofu pairs well with a variety of vegetables, such as bell peppers, broccoli, and carrots, making for a balanced, flavorful dish.

5. Deep-Frying

Deep-fried tofu is crispy and crunchy on the outside, with a tender, smooth interior. While this method requires more oil, it’s a treat for special occasions or when you want to indulge in crispy tofu bites.

How to Deep-Fry Tofu:

  1. Heat a deep pot or wok of oil to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and coat them lightly in cornstarch.
  3. Fry the tofu in batches, ensuring it’s not overcrowded in the oil.
  4. Fry the tofu for 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove the tofu from the oil and drain it on paper towels.

Deep-fried tofu is perfect for dipping in sauces or adding to noodle dishes.

5. Tofu Recipes to Try

Now that you know how to prepare and cook tofu, here are a few recipe ideas to incorporate this delicious ingredient into your meals:

Tofu Stir-Fry with Vegetables

A quick and healthy stir-fry with tofu and vegetables is the perfect weeknight meal. Combine sautéed tofu with stir-fried veggies like bell peppers, onions, broccoli, and snap peas. Toss it in a flavorful sauce made from soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a touch of sesame oil.

Tofu Scramble

A great breakfast or brunch option, tofu scramble is a plant-based alternative to scrambled eggs. Crumble firm tofu in a skillet and cook it with turmeric, garlic, onions, and your favorite veggies like spinach, mushrooms, and tomatoes for a flavorful and satisfying dish.

Crispy Tofu Bites with Peanut Sauce

For a quick appetizer or snack, make crispy tofu bites by pressing and baking tofu cubes until golden and crispy. Serve them with a creamy peanut dipping sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and a dash of chili flakes.

Vegan Tofu Tacos

Swap traditional meat for crispy tofu in your taco recipes. Fill soft corn tortillas with sautéed or baked tofu, fresh veggies, guacamole, and a drizzle of vegan sour cream for a delicious plant-based taco experience.

6. Storing Leftover Tofu

If you have leftover tofu, you can store it in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. To keep it fresh, place the tofu in an airtight container with some of the leftover marinade (if any), or simply add fresh water to cover the tofu. Be sure to change the water every day to keep it fresh.

For longer storage, you can freeze tofu. Freezing tofu changes its texture, making it more porous and spongy, which allows it to absorb marinades even more. Simply drain the tofu, wrap it in plastic wrap, and freeze it for up to 3 months. When ready to use, thaw it, press it, and cook as usual.

Tofu is an incredibly versatile ingredient that can be prepared and cooked in various ways to suit any dish or flavor profile. Whether you’re looking for a simple stir-fry, a savory baked tofu dish, or a crispy snack, the possibilities are endless. By following the right techniques and experimenting with different flavors, you can create the best tofu every time. So, next time you find yourself in the kitchen, grab a block of tofu and get creative with your cooking!
