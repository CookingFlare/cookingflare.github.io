How to Cook Korean Braised Short Ribs (Galbi Jjim)


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and rich history. Among the many delightful dishes, Galbi Jjim, or Korean braised short ribs, stands out as a comforting and hearty meal that warms the soul. This dish is a staple in Korean households, often served during special occasions and family gatherings. In this post, we will explore the origins of Galbi Jjim, the ingredients needed, and a step-by-step guide to creating this delicious dish at home.

The Origins of Galbi Jjim

Galbi Jjim has its roots in traditional Korean cooking, where the use of beef short ribs was a way to celebrate special occasions. The term “galbi” refers to ribs, while “jjim” means braised or steamed. This dish is characterized by its tender meat, which is slow-cooked to perfection, allowing the flavors to meld beautifully. Over the years, Galbi Jjim has evolved, with various regional variations and modern twists, but the essence of the dish remains the same.

Ingredients for Galbi Jjim

To create a mouthwatering Galbi Jjim, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Short Ribs:

  • 2.5 pounds of beef short ribs (preferably flanken-style)
  • Water (for soaking and boiling)

For the Braising Sauce:

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup rice wine (or mirin)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (about 5-6 cloves)
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes, optional for heat)

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, cut into chunks
  • 1 medium daikon radish, cut into chunks
  • 3-4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms (or any mushrooms of your choice)

For Garnish:

  • Sesame seeds
  • Chopped green onions

Preparing the Short Ribs

Step 1: Soak the Ribs

To remove excess blood and impurities, soak the short ribs in cold water for about 1-2 hours. Change the water every 30 minutes to ensure the ribs are thoroughly cleaned. This step is crucial for achieving a clean and flavorful broth.

Step 2: Blanch the Ribs

After soaking, drain the ribs and place them in a large pot. Cover the ribs with water and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. This process helps to further remove impurities and excess fat. After 10 minutes, drain the ribs and rinse them under cold water.

Making the Braising Sauce

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, rice wine, sesame oil, minced garlic, minced ginger, black pepper, and gochugaru (if using). Whisk the ingredients together until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is well combined. This sauce will infuse the short ribs with a rich, savory flavor.

Braising the Short Ribs

Step 4: Assemble the Ingredients

In a large pot or Dutch oven, arrange the blanched short ribs in a single layer. Pour the braising sauce over the ribs, ensuring they are well coated. Add the sliced onion, carrots, daikon radish, green onions, and shiitake mushrooms on top of the ribs. The vegetables not only add flavor but also contribute to the overall texture of the dish.

Step 5: Add Water

Pour enough water into the pot to cover the ribs and vegetables. This will create a flavorful broth as the dish cooks. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.

Step 6: Braise the Ribs

Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours. The longer you braise the ribs, the more tender they will become. Check occasionally to ensure there is enough liquid in the pot; add more water if necessary.

Final Touches

Step 7: Adjust the Seasoning

After 2-3 hours, taste the broth and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more soy sauce for saltiness or more sugar for sweetness, depending on your preference. If you like a bit of heat, feel free to add more gochugaru.

Step 8: Serve

Once the short ribs are tender and the flavors have melded beautifully, it’s time to serve. Carefully transfer the ribs and vegetables to a serving platter.
