How to Bake Greek Cheese Pie (Tiropita) at Home


Tiropita, a delicious Greek cheese pie, is a beloved dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of Greece. With layers of flaky phyllo pastry and a savory filling made primarily of cheese, eggs, and herbs, this dish is perfect for any occasion, from family gatherings to elegant dinners. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making this delectable pie at home, ensuring that you can impress your friends and family with your newfound culinary skills.

Ingredients

To prepare a classic Tiropita, gather the following ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 400 grams of feta cheese (crumbled)
  • 200 grams of ricotta cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup of fresh dill (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Phyllo Layers:

  • 500 grams of phyllo pastry (thawed if frozen)
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Additional butter or oil for brushing

Optional:

  • Sesame seeds for topping
  • A pinch of nutmeg for added flavor

Tools You Will Need:

  • A large mixing bowl
  • A whisk
  • A pastry brush
  • A 9x13 inch baking dish or a round pie dish
  • Parchment paper (optional)

Preparation Steps

1. Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that the Tiropita bakes evenly and achieves that perfect golden brown crust.

2. Prepare the Filling

In a large mixing bowl, combine the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, and eggs. Use a whisk to blend the ingredients until smooth. Next, add the chopped parsley and dill to the mixture. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If you’re feeling adventurous, a pinch of nutmeg can add an interesting depth of flavor. Mix everything thoroughly and set aside.

3. Assemble the Phyllo Layers

Working with phyllo pastry can be a bit tricky, but with patience and practice, you’ll master it. First, lay out your phyllo sheets on a clean surface. Keep the unused sheets covered with a damp cloth to prevent them from drying out.

  1. First Layer: Take one sheet of phyllo and brush it lightly with melted butter. Place it in the baking dish, allowing the edges to hang over the sides.

  2. Repeat: Add a second layer of phyllo, brushing it with butter again. Repeat this process until you have about 6-8 layers of phyllo, ensuring each layer is well-buttered for a flaky texture.

4. Add the Filling

Once the layers are complete, spread the cheese filling evenly over the phyllo base. Use a spatula to ensure the filling reaches the edges and is evenly distributed.

5. Top with Phyllo

Now, it’s time to cover the filling with more phyllo. Just like before, take another sheet of phyllo, brush it with butter, and place it over the filling. Repeat this process, layering 6-8 sheets on top.

6. Secure the Edges

Fold the overhanging edges of the bottom phyllo layers over the top layers to seal the filling inside. Brush the top layer with more melted butter for a beautiful golden finish.

7. Score the Surface

Using a sharp knife, score the surface of the Tiropita into squares or diamond shapes. This will make it easier to cut after baking and allows steam to escape.

8. Optional Toppings

If desired, sprinkle sesame seeds on top for added flavor and crunch.

9. Bake

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 30–40 minutes or until the top is golden brown and crispy. Keep an eye on it towards the end to prevent over-browning.

10. Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the Tiropita from the oven and allow it to cool for about 10 minutes. This resting period helps the filling set, making it easier to cut and serve. You can enjoy Tiropita warm or at room temperature.

Tips for Perfect Tiropita

  • Phyllo Handling: If you’re new to working with phyllo pastry, take your time. It’s normal for it to tear, and you can easily patch it with another piece.

  • Cheese Selection: While feta is a staple in Tiropita, you can experiment with other cheeses like goat cheese or a blend of cheeses for a unique flavor profile.

  • Herb Variations: Feel free to add other herbs like oregano or mint to the filling for an extra kick of flavor.

  • Serving Suggestions: Tiropita pairs wonderfully with a fresh Greek salad, tzatziki, or a side of olives. It also makes a great appetizer or snack.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have any leftovers (which is rare with such a delicious dish), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven to restore the crispiness of the phyllo.

Conclusion

Baking Greek Cheese Pie (Tiropita) at home is a rewarding experience that allows you to enjoy the authentic flavors of Greece. With its flaky layers and savory filling, Tiropita is sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself to something special, this dish is a perfect choice. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in this delightful Greek treat!

Happy baking!
