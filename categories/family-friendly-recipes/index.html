Quick and Easy Veggie Stir-Fry: Healthy Dinner Recipe

Are you looking for a quick and easy dinner recipe that is not only delicious but also healthy? Look no further! This veggie stir-fry recipe is packed with nutritious vegetables and bursting with flavor. It’s the perfect dish to whip up on those busy weeknights when you want something tasty and satisfying without spending hours in the kitchen. Ingredients 1 tablespoon olive oil 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 onion, sliced 1 red bell pepper, sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced 1 zucchini, sliced 1 cup broccoli florets 1 cup snap peas 1 cup mushrooms, sliced 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion to the skillet and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant. Add the sliced bell peppers, zucchini, broccoli florets, snap peas, and mushrooms to the skillet. Stir-fry the vegetables for 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and sesame oil. Pour the mixture over the vegetables in the skillet and toss to coat evenly. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Continue to stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove the skillet from the heat and serve the veggie stir-fry hot. Tips and Variations Feel free to add your favorite vegetables to this stir-fry. You can experiment with different combinations such as carrots, snow peas, or baby corn. For added protein, you can toss in some tofu, tempeh, or cooked chicken breast. To make this dish gluten-free, use tamari instead of soy sauce. Serve the stir-fry over brown rice or quinoa for a complete and filling meal. Garnish with fresh cilantro or sesame seeds for an extra burst of flavor. Why Choose Veggie Stir-Fry? Veggie stir-fry is not only a quick and easy dinner option, but it is also incredibly nutritious. Packed with a variety of colorful vegetables, this dish is rich in vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber. It’s a great way to incorporate more plant-based foods into your diet and reap the health benefits they offer. ...
