How to Grill Cabbage: Tips for a Smoky and Delicious Side


Grilling isn’t just for meat and traditional vegetables like peppers and onions; it’s a perfect cooking method for cabbage. Grilled cabbage transforms into a smoky, flavorful dish that can be enjoyed as a side or even become the centerpiece of your meal. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, looking for a new way to enjoy vegetables, or just experimenting with smoky flavors, grilled cabbage is a must-try addition to your repertoire.

In this blog post, we’ll explore everything you need to know about grilling cabbage: from choosing the right cabbage, prepping and seasoning, to expert grilling techniques that bring out its uniquely tender texture and robust flavor.

Choosing the Perfect Cabbage

The first step to grilling cabbage is picking the right variety. While any type of cabbage can be grilled, some work better than others depending on your flavor preferences and dish goals. The versatility of the vegetable allows you to experiment with a few different types:

  • Green Cabbage: The most common type, green cabbage holds its structure well on the grill. It has a mildly sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with smoky char, making it ideal for grilling.
  • Red Cabbage: Red cabbage is denser and slightly more peppery. Its vibrant color becomes even more striking after grilling, and it adds a bold, earthy element to your dish.
  • Savoy Cabbage: This variety has tender, wrinkled leaves that grill quickly and absorb seasonings well. Its buttery texture and mild flavor make it a top choice for flavorful marinades.
  • Napa Cabbage: While not as common for grilling, Napa cabbage’s elongated leaves and delicate sweetness can add variety to your grilled dishes.

When choosing cabbage, look for heads that feel heavy for their size and have tight, crisp leaves. Avoid any that have signs of wilting or blemishes, as these could affect the final taste and texture.

Prepping the Cabbage for Grilling

Preparation is key to ensuring your grilled cabbage turns out flavorful and well-cooked. Follow these simple steps to get your cabbage grill-ready:

  1. Wash and Trim: Begin by rinsing your cabbage thoroughly under cold water. Remove any damaged outer leaves.
  2. Slice into Wedges or Steaks: Cabbage can be grilled in various forms. For hearty, robust pieces, slice the cabbage into thick wedges. For a flat surface that gets more char, go for half-inch thick “steaks.”
  3. Leave the Core Intact: When slicing, make sure to leave enough of the core intact, especially for wedges. The core holds the leaves together and prevents them from falling apart on the grill.
  4. Coat Generously with Oil: To prevent the cabbage from sticking to the grill and to help it cook evenly, coat each wedge or slice with oil. Olive oil or avocado oil works beautifully due to their high smoke points and rich flavors.

Mastering Seasonings and Marinades

Cabbage is a blank canvas that absorbs seasonings well. You can keep it simple with salt, pepper, and garlic powder or elevate it with bold marinades and spice blends. Here are some flavorful ideas:

  • Classic Salt and Pepper: For a straightforward smoky flavor, brush the cabbage with olive oil and sprinkle with coarse salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
  • Garlic and Herb Blend: Combine garlic powder, smoked paprika, thyme, and oregano for a savory, herbaceous kick.
  • Asian-Inspired Marinade: Mix soy sauce, sesame oil, fresh ginger, and a little honey for a sweet-savory glaze perfect for grilled Napa or green cabbage.
  • Barbecue Twist: Use a drizzle of your favorite barbecue sauce or a dry rub of chili powder, mustard powder, and brown sugar for a sweet and smoky finish.
  • Citrus Vinaigrette: After grilling, finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon or lime juice to cut through the smoky richness.

Allow the cabbage to marinate (if using a sauce or glaze) for at least 15–20 minutes before grilling for maximum flavor infusion.

Grilling Techniques for Perfectly Smoky Cabbage

Grilling cabbage is simple, but proper technique is crucial to achieve that ideal smoky flavor and tender texture. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

1. Preheat the Grill

Start by preheating your grill to medium heat, which typically falls between 350°F and 400°F. This ensures even cooking without burning the edges. If you’re using a charcoal grill, arrange the coals for indirect heat, as cabbage cooks best without direct blasts of flame. For a gas grill, light only half the burners.

2. Oil the Grill Grates

To prevent sticking, lightly oil your grill grates before adding the cabbage. A high-heat cooking oil or a quick wipe-down with an oil-soaked paper towel works well.

3. Start with Direct Heat

Place the cabbage directly over the heat to achieve a nice sear and those desired char marks. This caramelizes the natural sugars, enhancing the sweetness. Sear each side for about 3–4 minutes.

4. Move to Indirect Heat

After searing, move the cabbage to indirect heat to allow it to cook through without burning. Close the grill lid and allow the cabbage to cook for another 10–15 minutes (depending on thickness), flipping occasionally.

5. Check for Doneness

Grilled cabbage is ready when the outer edges are evenly charred, and the inner layers are tender but still have a slight bite. Use a fork or knife to test—the center should cut easily without being mushy.

Creative Ways to Serve Grilled Cabbage

Grilled cabbage is incredibly versatile and works well as a side dish, salad base, or even incorporated into mains. Here’s how you can serve it:

  • Simple Side Dish: Drizzle wedges with balsamic reduction or finish with grated Parmesan for an easy crowd-pleaser.
  • Taco Filling: Shred grilled cabbage and toss with lime juice and cilantro to create a smoky, tangy taco topping.
  • Grain Bowls: Add slices of grilled cabbage to rice or quinoa bowls, complemented by roasted vegetables and a tahini dressing.
  • Coleslaw Remix: Chop the grilled cabbage into bite-sized pieces and toss with mayo, vinegar, and a pinch of sugar for a smoky take on coleslaw.
  • Main Vegan Dish: Serve grilled cabbage steaks as the star of your meal. Layer them with chimichurri sauce, a sprinkle of toasted nuts, or a dollop of creamy yogurt sauce.

Troubleshooting Tips and Tricks

Grilling cabbage can be straightforward, but like any cooking technique, it comes with its challenges. Here are some tips to ensure success:

  • Prevent Burning: If the outer leaves char too quickly, wrap the cabbage tightly in aluminum foil and move it to the cooler side of the grill.
  • Even Cooking: Rotate the cabbage pieces every few minutes to ensure they cook evenly. For cabbage steaks, flip them gently with a large spatula to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid Overcrowding: Give each piece of cabbage plenty of space on the grill to cook properly and develop a good sear.

Why You Should Grill Cabbage

Grilled cabbage isn’t just delicious—it’s also a nutrient-packed vegetable loaded with antioxidants, vitamins C and K, and fiber. The smoky char from grilling enhances its natural sweetness, turning this humble vegetable into something truly extraordinary. Additionally, grilling cabbage is a great way to show off your culinary creativity during the grilling season.

By following the proper preparation, seasoning, and grilling techniques, you can turn this ordinary vegetable into a smoky, caramelized masterpiece that’s bound to impress everyone at the table.

Final Thoughts

Grilling cabbage is an easy yet elevated way to add a healthy and flavorful twist to your meals. Whether you’re craving a simple smoky side or looking for ways to incorporate bold flavors into your dishes, grilled cabbage is a reliable, versatile choice. By experimenting with different spices, marinades, and serving styles, you’ll discover just how exciting this unassuming vegetable can be.

So grab a head of cabbage, fire up the grill, and get ready to enjoy a side dish that’s as flavorful as it is impressive! Grilled cabbage just might become your new barbecue favorite.
