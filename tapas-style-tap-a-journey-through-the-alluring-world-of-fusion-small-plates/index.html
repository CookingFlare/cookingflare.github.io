Tapas-style Tap: A Journey through the Alluring World of Fusion Small Plates


Tapas-style cuisine is a delightful way to explore the world of fusion food. These small plates showcase the best of international cooking techniques and ingredients, offering diners a unique gastronomic adventure with every bite. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a tantalizing tour through some of our favorite tapas recipes that seamlessly blend flavors and cultures.

Our First Stop: Smoked Salmon & Avocado Tartare Crostini

Start your culinary journey with a bang by creating these Smoked Salmon & Avocado Tartare Crostini. This dish’s rich and velvety salmon tartare is paired with smooth avocado, and it’s all topped off with a zesty lemon-zest crème fraîche. It’s sure to awaken your taste buds and whet your appetite for more global fusions.

Ingredients:

  • Crostini: bread, olive oil, salt
  • Salmon and avocado tartare: smoked salmon, avocado, red onion, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper
  • Lemon-zest crème fraîche: crème fraîche, lemon zest, salt

Instructions:

  1. Begin by making the crostini: Slice the bread into thin rounds and brush with olive oil. Sprinkle with salt and toast in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) until crispy.
  2. For the salmon and avocado tartare, finely chop the smoked salmon, avocado, red onion, and dill. Mix together in a bowl and season with lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the crème fraîche and lemon zest, and season with salt.
  4. To assemble, spread a thin layer of lemon-zest crème fraîche onto each crostini, then top with the salmon and avocado tartare.

Delightful Indian-South American Fusion: Chicken Tikka Kebab Tacos

Head down to South America but instead of their well-known ceviche, switch it up with Chicken Tikka Kebab Tacos. The spicy and smoky blend of Indian flavors with the casual and relaxed vibe of South American street food results in this bite-sized fusion delight.

Ingredients:

  • Kebab: chicken thighs, yogurt, ginger, garlic, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper
  • Taco toppings: red onion, cilantro, lime
  • Tortillas, if desired

Instructions:

  1. Begin by prepping the chicken: Chop the chicken into bite-sized pieces and mix with yogurt, grated ginger, minced garlic, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least an hour or overnight.
  2. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers and grill until cooked through.
  3. Serve in warm corn tortillas with thinly sliced red onion, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime for a simple yet delicious fusion experience.

Savory and Sweet Rumble: Mini Bacon-Wrapped Dates Stuffed with Blue Cheese

Indulge in two food worlds – sweet and savory – with this infamous Mini Bacon-Wrapped Dates Stuffed with Blue Cheese. The sticky-sweet dates paired with robust blue cheese and mouthwatering bacon will leave your guests craving more.

Ingredients:

  • Dates: pitted medjool dates
  • Blue cheese: crumbled
  • Bacon: uncooked slices

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the bacon slices in half lengthwise and then crosswise into smaller lengths.
  3. Stuff each date with a small amount of blue cheese and wrap with the bacon pieces.
  4. Place the wrapped dates on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until the bacon is crispy.
