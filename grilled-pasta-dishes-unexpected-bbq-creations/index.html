Grilled Pasta Dishes: Unexpected BBQ Creations


When we think of a summer barbecue, our minds often drift to sizzling meats, vibrant vegetables, and smoky flavors. However, one ingredient that deserves a spot on the grill is pasta. Yes, grilled pasta dishes are not only unexpected but also incredibly delightful and versatile. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of grilled pasta recipes that will elevate your next BBQ gathering to new culinary heights. Prepare to impress your friends and family with these unexpected creations that combine the char and depth of grilling with the comforting essence of pasta.

Why Grill Pasta?

Grilling pasta may sound unconventional, but it introduces a whole new level of flavor and texture to this beloved staple. When you grill pasta, you create a unique smoky flavor that enhances the dish and adds a delightful char. Additionally, grilling can help to firm up the pasta, providing a satisfying chew that contrasts beautifully with creamy sauces and fresh vegetables.

Choosing the Right Pasta

Before diving into our grilled pasta recipes, it’s essential to select the right type of pasta. Generally, short pasta shapes work best for grilling, as they hold up better on the grill and are easier to manage. Here are some excellent options:

  • Penne: Its tubular shape allows for a great sauce hold.
  • Fusilli: The spirals add texture and catch bits of grilled ingredients beautifully.
  • Orzo: This rice-shaped pasta is perfect for salads and grilling.
  • Farfalli (Bowtie): Adds an appealing visual element and holds sauces well.

Now that we’ve covered the basics let’s dive into some mouthwatering grilled pasta dishes that will have your guests raving.

Grilled Pesto Pasta Salad

Ingredients

  • 2 cups penne pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup bell peppers, diced
  • 1 cup zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup homemade or store-bought pesto
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Begin by boiling the penne pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Drizzle with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Toss the cherry tomatoes, bell peppers, and zucchini in a bowl with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  3. Grill the Vegetables: Place the seasoned vegetables on the grill. Grill for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until they are tender and have grill marks.

  4. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the grilled vegetables, pasta, and pesto. Mix until everything is well coated.

  5. Serve: Divide into serving bowls and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving. This dish is delicious warm or chilled, making it perfect for summer gatherings.

Grilled Mac and Cheese Skewers

Ingredients

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers

Instructions

  1. Cook the Macaroni: Boil the elbow macaroni according to package directions. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Mixture: In a large bowl, combine the cheddar cheese, mozzarella cheese, cream cheese, cooked macaroni, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  3. Assemble Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Using skewers, thread the mac and cheese mixture onto the skewers for easy grilling. You can also form the mixture into small balls and then skewer them.

  4. Coat with Breadcrumbs: Roll the skewered mac and cheese in breadcrumbs for a crispy texture.

  5. Grill: Brush the skewers with olive oil and grill for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until golden brown and crispy.

  6. Serve: Garnish with chopped parsley and serve warm. These skewers are a fun and interactive way to enjoy mac and cheese at your BBQ.

Grilled Pasta Primavera

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fusilli pasta
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the fusilli pasta in salted water until al dente. Drain and toss with a bit of olive oil to keep it from sticking.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In a bowl, combine asparagus, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, and carrots. Toss with olive oil, garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.

  3. Grill the Vegetables: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Grill the vegetables for about 5-8 minutes or until they are tender and slightly charred.

  4. Combine: In a large bowl, mix the grilled vegetables and cooked pasta. Toss to combine.

  5. Serve: Serve warm, topped with grated Parmesan cheese. This vibrant dish is not only beautiful but also packed with flavor, making it a perfect addition to your BBQ spread.

Grilled Pasta with Lemon and Shrimp

Ingredients

  • 2 cups linguine or spaghetti
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the linguine or spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Marinate the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine shrimp, olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, lemon zest, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate for about 15 minutes.

  3. Grill the Shrimp: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the shrimp onto skewers and grill for about 2-3 minutes per side or until they are pink and cooked through.

  4. Combine: In a large bowl, combine the grilled shrimp with the cooked pasta. Toss to mix well.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with additional lemon wedges. This dish is light, refreshing, and embodies the essence of summer.

Grilled Pasta Baked with Cheese

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rigatoni or ziti pasta
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil the rigatoni or ziti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Mixture: In a bowl, mix the mozzarella, ricotta cheese, salt, and pepper.

  3. Assemble: Preheat the grill to medium heat. In a large oven-safe pan or grill-safe dish, layer half of the marinara sauce, followed by half of the cooked pasta, and half of the cheese mixture. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of marinara and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top.

  4. Grill: Cover the dish with foil and place it on the grill. Cook for about 20-30 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil and serve hot. This grilled pasta bake is a cheesy, comforting dish that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Grilling Tips for Pasta Dishes

  • Pre-cook Your Pasta: Always cook pasta until it is al dente before grilling. This ensures it holds its shape and texture while on the grill.
  • Oil is Essential: Toss your pasta and vegetables in olive oil before grilling to prevent sticking and to enhance flavor.
  • Monitor the Grill Temperature: Keeping the grill at medium heat is key to avoiding burnt pasta or vegetables. Adjust as necessary.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to switch up ingredients based on your preferences or seasonal availability. Grilled pasta dishes can be customized endlessly!

Conclusion

Grilled pasta dishes bring an exciting twist to traditional BBQ fare. With the smoky flavors and delicious combinations of ingredients, these unexpected creations will surely impress your guests and make your summer gatherings memorable. Whether you opt for a refreshing pasta salad, indulgent mac and cheese skewers, or a light shrimp dish, grilling pasta opens up a world of culinary possibilities. So fire up that grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to delight your taste buds with these unique grilled pasta recipes! Happy grilling!
