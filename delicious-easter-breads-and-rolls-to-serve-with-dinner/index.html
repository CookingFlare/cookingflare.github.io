Delicious Easter Breads and Rolls to Serve with Dinner


Easter is a time for celebration, reflection, and of course, gathering around the table with loved ones. One of the most cherished parts of any Easter dinner is the bread served alongside the main dishes. From sweet, aromatic loaves to fluffy, savory rolls, Easter breads add warmth and tradition to the meal. If you’re looking to impress your guests this year, here’s an in-depth guide filled with delicious Easter bread and roll recipes that will complement your holiday feast beautifully.

The Tradition and Charm of Easter Breads

Bread has always played a significant role in Easter celebrations worldwide, often symbolizing renewal, abundance, and sharing. Many cultures have their own unique takes on Easter bread, whether it’s a braided loaf dotted with raisins, a lightly sweetened roll infused with citrus zest, or a rich bread filled with nuts and dried fruits.

Recreating these breads at home not only honors tradition but fills your home with irresistible aromas. These breads can be enjoyed by themselves, dipped in butter, or paired with your favorite dinner dishes, making them an indispensable part of the Easter table.

1. Classic Italian Easter Bread

This traditional Italian Easter bread, known as Pane di Pasqua, involves a sweet dough mixed with citrus zest and decorated with colored eggs, symbolizing rebirth and new life.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 cup warm milk (about 110°F)
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • Zest of 1 orange and 1 lemon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Food coloring (optional, for dyed eggs)
  • Whole eggs (for decorating, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and a pinch of sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Mix the dough: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, salt, and citrus zest. Add eggs, softened butter, and yeast mixture. Knead until you form a smooth, elastic dough (about 10 minutes by hand or 6 minutes in a mixer).
  3. First rise: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place until doubled in size (about 1 hour).
  4. Shape the bread: Divide the dough into three equal pieces and roll them into long ropes. Braid the ropes together and form a ring on a baking sheet lined with parchment.
  5. Decorate: If using whole eggs for decoration, gently press them nestled within the braid, securing them with strips of dough.
  6. Second rise: Let the braided ring rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the bread with a beaten egg wash for a shiny finish. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  8. Cool and serve.

This sweet, fragrant bread pairs wonderfully with savory Easter dishes like ham, lamb, or roast vegetables.

2. Soft Buttery Parker House Rolls

For a simple yet decadent bread option, Parker House rolls offer soft, tender crumb and a slightly buttery, flaky crust. Their pull-apart nature makes them perfect for communal sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 1/4 tsp)
  • 1 cup warm milk (110°F)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • Extra melted butter, for brushing

Instructions:

  1. Activate yeast: In a small bowl, stir warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Allow to proof for about 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Prepare dough: In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Add egg, melted butter, and yeast mixture. Stir to combine.
  3. Knead: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic for about 8 minutes.
  4. First rise: Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with a kitchen towel, and let rise until doubled in size (about 1 hour).
  5. Shape rolls: Punch down dough and divide into small balls (about golf-ball size). Roll out each ball into an oval, fold it in half, and place folded side down on a parchment-lined baking pan.
  6. Second rise: Cover and let rolls rise again for 30 minutes.
  7. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush rolls generously with melted butter. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until golden.
  8. Serve warm with more butter.

These rolls are a universal crowd-pleaser, working well alongside ham glazed with pineapple, scalloped potatoes, or a rich green bean casserole.

3. Greek Tsoureki Bread – A Spiced, Braided Delight

Tsoureki is a traditional Greek sweet bread flavored with aromatic mahleb (a spice derived from cherry seeds) and sometimes mastic resin. Its slightly sweet flavor and tender crumb make it perfect for Easter.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk (110°F)
  • 3 large eggs (2 for dough + 1 for egg wash)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp mahleb (or substitute with a mix of almond extract and cinnamon)
  • 1/2 tsp ground mastic (optional)
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Red-dyed hard-boiled eggs (optional for decoration)

Instructions:

  1. Activate yeast: In a small bowl, mix warm milk, yeast, and a teaspoon of sugar. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until bubbly.
  2. Combine dry ingredients: In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt, mahleb, mastic, and orange zest.
  3. Add wet ingredients: Beat 2 eggs and mix with butter and the yeast mixture. Add to the flour mixture and knead until smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes.
  4. First rise: Cover the dough with a damp towel, place in a warm spot, and let rise until doubled (approximately 1.5 hours).
  5. Form the bread: Divide dough into 3 or 4 equal ropes. Braid them and fold into a circle on a baking sheet.
  6. Add eggs: If using red-dyed eggs, tuck them into the braid securely.
  7. Second rise: Cover and let rest for 30-40 minutes.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush loaf with beaten egg for a glossy finish. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden.
  9. Cool: Let cool completely before slicing.

This bread’s fragrant spices pair well with moussaka, Greek-style roasted lamb, or simply enjoyed as a sweet snack with a cup of tea.

4. Honey-Herb Dinner Rolls

For those who want a savory option infused with fresh herbs and a hint of honey sweetness, these soft, slightly sweet rolls are a perfect addition.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water (110°F)
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or parsley), finely chopped
  • 1 egg (optional, for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Proof yeast: In a bowl, combine warm water, yeast, and a teaspoon of honey. Let sit 5-10 minutes until bubbling.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and chopped herbs.
  3. Make dough: Add yeast mixture, honey, and olive oil to dry ingredients. Stir and knead until smooth (about 8-10 minutes).
  4. Rise: Cover and let dough double in size for about 1 hour.
  5. Shape rolls: Punch down dough and divide into 12 equal pieces. Shape each into a ball and place on baking tray.
  6. Second rise: Cover rolls and let rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush rolls with beaten egg for shine or olive oil for softness. Bake 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  8. Cool slightly: Brush with extra olive oil and sprinkle fresh herbs before serving.

These rolls are an excellent match for roasted chicken, grilled vegetables, or spring salads.

5. Russian Kulich – Tall Easter Sweet Bread

Kulich is a tall, cylindrical bread traditionally enjoyed in Russia during Easter. It’s rich, slightly sweet, often studded with dried fruits and nuts, and topped with a glossy sugar icing.

Ingredients:

For the Kulich:

  • 4 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk (about 110°F)
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mixed dried fruits (raisins, candied orange peel, dried cranberries)
  • 1/2 cup chopped toasted almonds or walnuts
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom (optional)

For the icing:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons lemon juice or milk
  • Colored sprinkles (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Activate yeast: In a bowl, stir warm milk, yeast, and a teaspoon of sugar. Allow to foam for 10 minutes.
  2. Prepare a sponge: Mix 1 cup flour, eggs, and yeast mixture until smooth. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Add ingredients: Stir in melted butter, remaining flour, sugar, salt, vanilla, cardamom, dried fruits, and nuts to the sponge dough. Knead thoroughly until elastic (approx 8-10 minutes).
  4. First rise: Cover dough in a warm place and let rise for about 2 hours or until doubled.
  5. Prepare pans: Grease cylindrical tins or tall bread molds.
  6. Fill molds: Spoon dough into molds halfway (kulich expands considerably).
  7. Second rise: Let rise for another 30-45 minutes.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake for 40-50 minutes until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean. Cover top loosely with foil if browning too quickly.
  9. Cool: Remove from pans and cool on racks.
  10. Glaze: Mix powdered sugar with lemon juice or milk to make thick icing. Pour over cooled kulich and sprinkle with decorations.

Kulich pairs wonderfully with traditional Russian cheese spread or a dollop of fresh cream.

Tips for Baking Perfect Easter Breads & Rolls

  • Yeast Activation: Always use warm (not hot) liquids to activate yeast. Too hot, and the yeast dies; too cold, and it won’t activate properly.
  • Rising Time: Give your dough enough time to rise. The flavor and texture depend heavily on proper fermentation.
  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh yeast and good quality flour make a difference in final bread flavor.
  • Egg wash: Brushing breads and rolls with egg wash before baking gives a beautiful shiny crust.
  • Add aromatic zest: Citrus zests can add bright flavor, enhancing the festive aspect.
  • Decorate with intention: Using dyed eggs or nuts as decoration adds traditional visual appeal and invites curiosity.

Serving Suggestions

These breads and rolls are extremely versatile. Here’s how you can elevate your Easter dinner by pairing them appropriately:

  • Sweet breads like Italian Easter bread or Russian Kulich are excellent alongside rich meats, cheeses, or enjoyed for breakfast with butter and jam.
  • Savory rolls like Parker House and herb honey rolls make perfect accompaniments to roasted lamb, ham, or spring vegetable dishes.
  • Bread can also be transformed into croutons or stuffing for Easter leftovers, prolonging the festive flavors.

Conclusion

The centerpiece of an Easter dinner table is not just the main course but also the breads and rolls that bring the meal together. Whether you prefer sweet or savory, these Easter bread recipes offer something special for every palate. They connect us to centuries of tradition, fill the kitchen with tantalizing aromas, and invite everyone to gather and share in the spirit of renewal and joy.

This season, try baking one (or more!) of these delightful Easter breads and rolls. The time, aroma, and love you pour into kneading and decorating will be rewarded by full hearts and satisfied appetites.

Happy baking and Happy Easter!
