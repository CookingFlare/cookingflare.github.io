Vegan Falafel Festival: 15 Levantine Dishes to Bring the Flavors of the Middle East Home


Welcome to our Vegan Falafel Festival, where we explore the exquisite flavors and richness of Levantine cuisine through 15 delicious, plant-based recipes. From Falafel Wraps to Hummus & Pita Trio, Tabbouleh Salad, and Warm Stuffed Vine Leaves, these dishes represent the diverse nuances present in Middle Eastern cuisine. So, let’s embark on a flavorful culinary journey with a taste of fall resurgence and welcome our friends and family around a hearty plant-based feast.

1. Classic Falafel Wraps

Indulge in the iconic flavors of the Levant with Classic Falafel Wraps. Crispy falafel balls are topped with fresh parsley, pickled turnips, hummus, and tahini sauce, wrapped in a warm pita bread or lettuce leaf.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 cup flour (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pita bread or large lettuce leaves

Instructions:

  1. Blend the soaked chickpeas with the garlic, onion, and parsley in a food processor. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. If the mixture seems too loose, add the flour to help bind the falafel patties.
  3. Form patties and fry until golden brown in oil.
  4. Serve with hummus, tahini sauce, and pickled turnips, wrapped in warm pita or lettuce leaves.

2. Hummus & Pita Trio

A Levantine feast wouldn’t be complete without traditional dips. Sink your spoon into our Hummus & Pita Trio, which includes classic hummus, baba ganoush, and muhammara, served with toasted pita bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tahini jars
  • 2 lemons
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 red peppers
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pita bread

Instructions:

  1. For the hummus, blend the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic until smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast the bell pepper and onion until charred. Puree in the food processor with tahini, lemon juice, and garlic to make baba ganoush.
  3. Mix roasted red peppers, red pepper paste, breadcrumbs, walnuts, pomegranate molasses, and fresh parsley to make muhammara.
  4. Serve the dips with warm pita bread and assorted fresh vegetables.

3. Tabbouleh Salad

Fresh, colorful, and loaded with flavor, Tabbouleh Salad bridges the connection between our taste buds and the earth’s luscious bounty. bursting with parsley, mint, tomatoes, onions, and bulgur wheat, this dish embodies the essence of Levantine produce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 cups parsley
  • 1 cup mint
  • 2 lemons
  • 2 tomatoes
  • Olive oil, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the bulgur wheat according to package instructions.
  2. Finely chop the parsley, mint, onion, and tomato.
  3. Mix cooked bulgur with the vegetables and season with olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

4. Warmed Stuffed Vine Leaves

Transport your taste buds to Istanbul with Warm Stuffed Vine Leaves, tender vine leaves stuffed with fragrant rice, currants, pine nuts, and herbs. A burst of sweet and savory flavors make these stuffed leaves an unforgettable palate-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar vine leaves
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 bunch mint
  • 1/2 cup currants
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • Olive oil, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak vine leaves in warm water to soften.
  2. Sauté the onion, garlic, parsley, and mint.
  3. Mix rice, sautéed onion and herb mixture, currants, and pine nuts.
  4. Place a vine leaf at the bottom of a baking dish, with the glossy side up. Spread a small amount of rice mixture on the leaf, roll tightly, and place seam side down in the dish. Repeat until all leaves are used.
  5. Drizzle olive oil over the stuffed leaves and season with salt and pepper. Bake at 350 degrees for 45 minutes.

There’s no doubt that Levantine cuisine is a treasure trove of gastronomic delights. From the vibrant flavors of Tabbouleh Salad to the rich and diverse textures of Warm Stuffed Vine Leaves, these dishes pay homage to the culture and traditions of Middle Eastern cuisine. So let’s bring together family and friends and share these delectable plant-based dishes during a Vegan Falafel Festival. Enjoy!
