Cooking with Kids: 5 Healthy Desserts That Are Fun to Make


Cooking with kids is one of the most rewarding activities that parents and caregivers can enjoy together. Not only does it provide an opportunity for bonding, but it also helps children develop essential life skills, such as following instructions, measuring ingredients, and working together as a team. When it comes to desserts, it’s even more fun to experiment with creative and healthy options that the whole family can enjoy.

If you’re looking for ideas to make delicious and nutritious desserts with your little ones, this guide will provide you with five exciting recipes that are perfect for involving kids in the kitchen. Each recipe is packed with wholesome ingredients, easy to make, and most importantly, full of flavor. Get ready to indulge in these healthy treats that are sure to be a hit with both kids and adults!

1. Banana Ice Cream Pops

Banana ice cream pops are a fun and healthy dessert that’s as easy to make as it is delicious. Kids will love the hands-on experience of dipping frozen banana slices into yogurt and rolling them in their favorite toppings. This simple yet satisfying treat is packed with the goodness of fruit and protein-rich yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup of Greek yogurt (plain or vanilla)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate chips (optional)
  • 1/4 cup crushed nuts (almonds, walnuts, or peanuts)
  • 1/4 cup shredded coconut (optional)
  • 1/4 cup granola (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Peel the bananas and cut them into 1-inch thick slices. Place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Freeze the banana slices for at least 2 hours or until they are firm.
  3. Once the bananas are frozen, place the yogurt in a shallow bowl. If you’d like to sweeten the yogurt, stir in the honey.
  4. Carefully dip each banana slice into the yogurt, coating it completely. You can also use a spoon to help cover the bananas evenly.
  5. After dipping the banana slice in yogurt, roll it in your choice of toppings. Options include crushed nuts, chocolate chips, shredded coconut, or granola.
  6. Return the coated banana slices to the baking sheet and freeze for another 30 minutes or until the pops are fully set.
  7. Enjoy these frozen banana pops as a refreshing, guilt-free dessert!

2. Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Energy Balls

These oatmeal chocolate chip energy balls are the perfect dessert for busy afternoons. They are no-bake, easy to make, and packed with fiber, protein, and healthy fats. The kids can get involved by helping to measure, mix, and roll the dough into bite-sized balls. Plus, they can customize the flavor by adding their favorite mix-ins, like dried fruit or seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup almond butter (or peanut butter)
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup mini dark chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond butter, honey, mini chocolate chips, chia seeds, vanilla extract, and sea salt.
  2. Stir everything together until well combined. The mixture should be thick and sticky. If it’s too dry, add a tablespoon of water to help it come together.
  3. Use your hands or a spoon to roll the mixture into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter.
  4. Place the energy balls on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to firm up.
  5. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. These no-bake energy balls are great for a quick snack or dessert.

3. Fruit and Yogurt Parfaits

Fruit and yogurt parfaits are an easy way to get kids involved in creating a delicious dessert that’s packed with nutrients. With endless variations, you can use your favorite seasonal fruits and add granola or nuts for crunch. This colorful dessert is visually appealing and fun for kids to layer their own creations.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain or vanilla Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1 cup mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 banana, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granola
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pecans, or walnuts)
  • Fresh mint for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the Greek yogurt with honey or maple syrup to add sweetness (if desired).
  2. In glasses or small bowls, start by layering the yogurt at the bottom.
  3. Add a layer of mixed berries and sliced banana on top of the yogurt.
  4. Repeat the layering with more yogurt, fruits, and top with granola and chopped nuts.
  5. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a pop of color.
  6. Serve immediately or chill in the fridge until ready to enjoy. This dessert is light, creamy, and packed with antioxidants and probiotics.

4. Chia Pudding with Fruit Toppings

Chia pudding is an excellent dessert option because it’s packed with fiber, omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants. It’s a no-cook recipe that kids can help prepare the night before, allowing the pudding to set in the fridge overnight. The fun part comes when it’s time to top it off with fresh fruits and other delicious toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 2 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit (strawberries, blueberries, kiwi, mango, or peaches)
  • A handful of nuts or seeds (optional)
  • A sprinkle of cinnamon or cocoa powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Stir well and let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes, then stir again to ensure the chia seeds don’t clump together.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate overnight to allow the chia seeds to absorb the liquid and thicken.
  4. The next day, stir the chia pudding again to break up any clumps.
  5. Spoon the pudding into individual bowls or jars and top with fresh fruit, nuts, seeds, and a sprinkle of cinnamon or cocoa powder.
  6. Serve immediately or store in the fridge for up to 3 days. This healthy dessert is full of texture and flavor, making it a perfect option for a nutritious treat.

5. Baked Apple Nachos

Apple nachos are a creative twist on the classic dessert nachos, making them a healthier option with all the fun. By layering thinly sliced apples with nut butter, dark chocolate chips, and crunchy toppings, you can create a dessert that’s both sweet and satisfying. Kids can help with assembling the nachos, and they’ll love choosing their favorite toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large apples (Fuji or Gala work best)
  • 1/4 cup almond butter (or peanut butter)
  • 2 tablespoons dark chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (walnuts, pecans, or almonds)
  • 1 tablespoon shredded coconut (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Core the apples and slice them into thin rounds or wedges.
  3. Arrange the apple slices in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake the apples for about 10-12 minutes, or until they begin to soften slightly.
  5. While the apples are baking, melt the almond butter in the microwave for about 20-30 seconds, or until it becomes smooth and pourable.
  6. Once the apples are done, drizzle the melted almond butter over the apple slices.
  7. Sprinkle the dark chocolate chips, chopped nuts, shredded coconut, and cinnamon over the apples.
  8. Serve warm, and enjoy a healthier version of nachos that the whole family will love!

Final Thoughts

Cooking with kids doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. These five healthy dessert recipes are designed to be simple, fun, and engaging for kids of all ages. From frozen banana pops to fruit-filled parfaits, these desserts are not only nutritious but also a great way to spend quality time together in the kitchen.

When you cook with your children, you help foster a love of healthy eating and teach them the importance of making wholesome food choices. Plus, cooking together can be a fun and educational experience that strengthens your bond. So, the next time you’re looking for a sweet treat, try one of these healthy desserts. You’ll not only be treating your family to something delicious but also encouraging them to get creative in the kitchen!

Enjoy making these delightful desserts with your little ones, and savor every bite!
