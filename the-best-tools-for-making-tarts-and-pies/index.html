The Best Tools for Making Tarts and Pies


When it comes to baking, few things are as satisfying as creating a perfectly flaky tart or pie. The aroma of a freshly baked crust, the rich filling bubbling away, and the anticipation of that first slice are all part of the joy of baking. However, to achieve that perfect tart or pie, having the right tools is essential. In this guide, we will explore the best tools for making tarts and pies, ensuring that your baking experience is not only enjoyable but also successful.

Essential Baking Tools

1. Mixing Bowls

A good set of mixing bowls is fundamental for any baking endeavor. Look for bowls that are sturdy and come in various sizes. Glass or stainless steel bowls are excellent choices as they are durable and easy to clean. A large bowl is perfect for mixing dough, while smaller bowls can be used for preparing fillings or whisking eggs.

2. Measuring Cups and Spoons

Precision is key in baking, and having a reliable set of measuring cups and spoons is crucial. Invest in both dry and liquid measuring cups, as well as a set of measuring spoons. Stainless steel or glass options are preferred for their durability and ease of cleaning. Accurate measurements will ensure that your crusts and fillings turn out just right.

3. Pastry Blender

A pastry blender is an indispensable tool for making pie crusts and tart dough. This tool allows you to cut cold butter or shortening into flour, creating a crumbly texture that is essential for a flaky crust. Look for a sturdy pastry blender with comfortable handles to make the process easier.

4. Rolling Pin

A good rolling pin is essential for rolling out dough to the perfect thickness. There are various types of rolling pins, including traditional wooden ones and silicone options. A French rolling pin, which tapers at the ends, allows for better control and maneuverability. Choose a rolling pin that feels comfortable in your hands and suits your baking style.

5. Pastry Mat

A pastry mat provides a non-stick surface for rolling out dough and helps to keep your workspace clean. Many pastry mats come with measurements printed on them, making it easy to roll your dough to the desired size. Look for a mat that is easy to clean and can withstand high temperatures.

6. Tart and Pie Pans

The type of pan you use can significantly affect the outcome of your tart or pie. For tarts, a fluted tart pan with a removable bottom is ideal, as it allows for easy removal and presentation. For pies, a standard pie dish or a deep-dish pie pan works well. Consider using glass or ceramic pans, as they distribute heat evenly and help achieve a golden crust.

7. Pie Weights

When making a blind-baked crust, pie weights are essential to prevent the dough from puffing up. You can purchase ceramic pie weights, or you can use dried beans or rice as a substitute. Weighing down the crust ensures that it bakes evenly and maintains its shape.

8. Pastry Brush

A pastry brush is a handy tool for applying egg washes or glazes to your crusts. This step adds a beautiful shine and color to your baked goods. Look for a brush with soft bristles that can hold liquid well, making it easy to apply an even coat.

9. Food Processor

A food processor can be a game-changer when it comes to making pie and tart dough. It allows you to quickly combine ingredients and achieve the desired texture without overworking the dough. If you don’t have a food processor, a stand mixer with a paddle attachment can also work well.

10. Cooling Rack

Once your tarts and pies are baked, a cooling rack is essential for allowing them to cool evenly. This prevents the bottom from becoming soggy and helps maintain the crust’s texture. Look for a sturdy cooling rack that can hold multiple baked goods at once.

Specialty Tools for Advanced Baking

11. Lattice Cutter

If you want to create beautiful lattice tops for your pies, a lattice cutter can make the process much easier. This tool allows you to cut even strips of dough, ensuring a professional-looking finish. It’s a great addition for those who enjoy decorative pie crusts.

12. Dough Scraper

A dough scraper is a versatile tool that can help you handle dough with ease. It’s perfect for cutting, portioning, and transferring dough from your work surface to your baking pan. A bench scraper with a straight edge is particularly useful for achieving clean cuts.

13. Silicone Baking Mats

Silicone baking mats are a fantastic alternative to parchment paper. They provide a non-stick surface for baking and can be reused multiple times, making them an eco-friendly option. They are especially useful for baking tarts and pies, as they help prevent sticking and ensure even baking.
