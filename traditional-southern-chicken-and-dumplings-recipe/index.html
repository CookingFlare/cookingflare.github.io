Traditional Southern Chicken and Dumplings Recipe


If there’s one dish that embodies the heart and soul of Southern comfort food, it’s traditional chicken and dumplings. This classic recipe has been passed down through generations, bringing warmth and satisfaction to countless family gatherings and cozy Sunday dinners. The combination of tender chicken, savory broth, and fluffy dumplings creates a symphony of flavors that is both comforting and indulgent. In this post, we’ll explore the time-honored process of creating this beloved Southern dish, and you’ll soon discover why it’s a staple in so many homes.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 cups of chicken broth

For the Dumplings:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken pieces, onion, celery, carrots, garlic, salt, pepper, and chicken broth. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender.

  2. While the chicken is simmering, prepare the dumplings. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use your fingers to incorporate it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Pour in the milk and stir until just combined.

  3. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside to cool. Strain the broth to remove the vegetables and any impurities, then return it to the pot and bring it back to a simmer.

  4. While the broth is simmering, shred the cooled chicken into bite-sized pieces and add it back to the pot.

  5. Drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough into the simmering broth and chicken. Cover the pot with a lid and let the dumplings cook for about 15 minutes, or until they are cooked through and fluffy.

  6. Serve the chicken and dumplings hot, and enjoy the cozy, comforting flavors of this Southern classic.

Tips and Tricks

  • For even more flavor, you can add a sprig of fresh thyme or a bay leaf to the broth while it simmers.
  • If you prefer a thicker broth, you can mix 1/4 cup of flour with 1/2 cup of cold water and stir it into the simmering broth until it thickens.

Conclusion

Traditional Southern chicken and dumplings is more than just a dish; it’s a nostalgic journey back to the warmth and love of home-cooked meals. The simplicity of the ingredients and the heartiness of the flavors make it a timeless favorite, perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re looking for a comforting meal to soothe the soul or a dish to impress your loved ones, this recipe is sure to bring joy and satisfaction to all who gather around your table. So, embrace the tradition, savor the flavors, and create your own cherished memories with this delightful Southern classic.
