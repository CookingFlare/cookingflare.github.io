Best Thai Mango Sticky Rice Recipe


If you’ve ever had the pleasure of visiting Thailand or dining at an authentic Thai restaurant, chances are you’ve encountered the famous Mango Sticky Rice. This simple yet incredibly satisfying dessert has gained worldwide recognition for its sweet, creamy, and refreshing combination of flavors and textures. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or just looking for a new recipe to try, Mango Sticky Rice is a must-try.

This dish typically combines three essential components: perfectly cooked sticky rice, a rich coconut sauce, and ripe mangoes. While it may seem like a complicated dessert to make, it’s surprisingly easy to prepare at home with just a few ingredients. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process step-by-step to ensure you achieve a beautifully balanced and authentic Mango Sticky Rice every time.

What Makes Mango Sticky Rice So Special?

Mango Sticky Rice, or Khao Niew Mamuang, is not just a treat; it’s an experience. The combination of sweet coconut milk with the chewiness of the sticky rice and the smooth, juicy mango creates an unbeatable dessert that offers layers of flavor in every bite. This dessert is traditionally served during the summer months in Thailand, especially during the mango season, but it’s so delicious that people enjoy it year-round.

The balance of flavors is what makes Mango Sticky Rice so unique. The rich coconut sauce provides a creamy, slightly salty contrast to the sweet mango, while the sticky rice gives the dish a chewy texture that’s not only satisfying but also perfectly complements the rest of the ingredients. The best part is that Mango Sticky Rice is gluten-free, so it’s a perfect option for those with dietary restrictions.

Essential Ingredients for Mango Sticky Rice

Before diving into the recipe, let’s go over the ingredients you’ll need to make this iconic Thai dessert:

  • Sticky Rice (also known as glutinous rice or sweet rice): This rice is the foundation of the dish. It’s essential that you use sticky rice rather than regular long-grain rice. Sticky rice has a high starch content that makes it tender and sticky when cooked, creating the perfect texture for this dessert.
  • Coconut Milk: The coconut milk adds the creamy, slightly sweet flavor that elevates the sticky rice. Full-fat coconut milk is best for a rich, indulgent result.
  • Sugar: Typically, granulated sugar or palm sugar is used to sweeten the coconut milk. Palm sugar provides a richer, deeper flavor, but granulated sugar works just fine if it’s what you have on hand.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt balances the sweetness and enhances the flavors.
  • Ripe Mangoes: You’ll want to use the juiciest and sweetest mangoes available. Ataulfo (also known as honey or champagne mangoes) are a popular choice, but any ripe mango variety will work.

Now that you’re familiar with the ingredients, let’s dive into the step-by-step process for making the best Mango Sticky Rice.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Thai Mango Sticky Rice

1. Preparing the Sticky Rice

The first step in making Mango Sticky Rice is to cook the sticky rice properly. Here’s how:

Soak the Rice

Sticky rice needs to be soaked before cooking to achieve the right texture. Place the sticky rice in a large bowl and cover it with cold water. Let it soak for at least 1-2 hours, or even overnight if you have the time. Soaking the rice ensures that it becomes soft and sticky once cooked, which is essential for this dish.

Steam the Rice

Once the rice has soaked, drain it well and transfer it to a steamer basket lined with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel. The key here is to steam the rice, not boil it. Steaming helps the rice retain its stickiness and creates the ideal texture.

Steam the rice for about 20-30 minutes. You’ll want to check the rice halfway through and fluff it gently with a fork to ensure even cooking. The rice should become tender, translucent, and sticky once it’s fully cooked.

2. Making the Coconut Sauce

While the sticky rice is steaming, you can prepare the coconut sauce, which will be drizzled over the rice later. Here’s how to make it:

Heat the Coconut Milk

In a saucepan over medium heat, pour the coconut milk and heat it gently. Stir in sugar and salt, and continue to heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar has completely dissolved. Be careful not to let the mixture come to a boil, as this can cause the coconut milk to separate. You want the sauce to be smooth and well-blended.

Once the sugar has dissolved, remove the coconut milk mixture from the heat. It’s now ready to be added to the cooked sticky rice.

3. Mixing the Sticky Rice with Coconut Milk

Once the sticky rice is done steaming, transfer it to a large bowl. While the rice is still hot, pour the coconut sauce over it, saving a small portion to drizzle on top later when serving. Gently stir the rice with a wooden spoon or spatula to ensure the rice absorbs the coconut milk evenly. Let it sit for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to fully meld.

The rice should now be creamy and slightly sticky, with a rich coconut flavor that enhances its natural sweetness.

4. Preparing the Mangoes

While the rice is resting, it’s time to prepare the mangoes. Start by peeling the mangoes and cutting them into thin slices. To do this, first cut off both ends of the mango. Stand the mango upright on one of the flat ends, then slice down along the sides of the pit, creating two large halves. Cut the flesh of each mango into thin slices or cubes, depending on your preference.

The mangoes should be ripe and sweet, with a smooth texture that pairs perfectly with the sticky rice.

5. Assembling the Mango Sticky Rice

Now that all the components are ready, it’s time to assemble the dish. Spoon the coconut-infused sticky rice onto serving plates, forming a neat mound or pile. Arrange the mango slices elegantly beside the rice. Drizzle the reserved coconut sauce over the top of the sticky rice and mangoes, and garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans if desired for a touch of crunch.

Serve immediately while the dish is fresh and warm, or refrigerate and serve chilled for a refreshing treat.

Tips for the Perfect Mango Sticky Rice

  • Use quality coconut milk: The flavor of the coconut milk plays a huge role in the final taste of your Mango Sticky Rice. Make sure to use full-fat coconut milk for the best results.
  • Soak the rice properly: Soaking the sticky rice ensures that it cooks evenly and becomes soft and sticky. Don’t skip this step!
  • Choose ripe mangoes: The sweetness of the mangoes is crucial to the overall flavor of the dish. Opt for the ripest mangoes available for the best taste.
  • Serve chilled or warm: Mango Sticky Rice is delicious whether served at room temperature, chilled, or warm. If you have leftovers, refrigerate them, but be sure to serve them at your preferred temperature.

Variations and Customizations

While the traditional Mango Sticky Rice recipe is always a hit, there are plenty of ways to customize the dish based on your preferences or what you have available. Here are a few variations you can try:

1. Add a Crunchy Topping:

Some versions of Mango Sticky Rice feature a crunchy topping like toasted sesame seeds, mung beans, or crushed peanuts. These toppings add texture and a slightly nutty flavor that contrasts nicely with the creamy rice and sweet mango.

2. Coconut Flavored Rice:

For an extra coconut kick, try adding some shredded coconut to the rice before steaming it. This gives the rice an added depth of coconut flavor and a slightly chewy texture.

3. Honey Drizzle:

If you want to change up the sweetness a bit, drizzle a bit of honey over the top of the mangoes or rice. It adds a lovely floral note that complements the coconut and mango perfectly.

4. Basil or Mint Garnish:

To add a refreshing herbal element, garnish your Mango Sticky Rice with fresh basil or mint leaves. The bright, fragrant notes from the herbs work wonderfully with the richness of the coconut milk.

Conclusion

Mango Sticky Rice is a delightful Thai dessert that combines simple ingredients into something extraordinary. With its creamy coconut sauce, sweet sticky rice, and ripe, juicy mangoes, it’s a perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you’re making it for a summer gathering or simply craving something indulgent, this recipe is sure to impress. By following the steps above and incorporating some of the variations, you’ll have a dish that’s as close to the authentic street food experience as possible. Enjoy!
