Easy-to-Make Brunch Cocktails with 3 Ingredients


Brunch has become more than just a mid-morning meal—it’s a full-blown experience. Whether you’re hosting friends or preparing for a solo indulgence, no brunch is complete without refreshing cocktails to elevate the spread. But while many cocktails call for a laundry list of ingredients, we’re keeping it simple with three-ingredient options that pack flavor and elegance without the fuss. Dive into this list of effortless drinks that turn your brunch into a memorable event, leaving plenty of time to enjoy the food and company.

Classic Mimosa

Nothing says brunch quite like the iconic mimosa. Balancing tangy citrus and bubbles, this timeless cocktail is as easy as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • Orange juice (Freshly squeezed for best results)
  • Sparkling wine or champagne
  • Orange slices or zest for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a champagne flute, pour orange juice until the glass is about halfway full.
  2. Top off the remainder of the glass with sparkling wine or champagne for a lively fizz.
  3. Garnish with an orange slice or a twist of fresh zest for added aroma and a touch of visual flair.

Pro tip: For a twist, substitute the orange juice with blood orange juice or a mix of pineapple juice to shake things up.

Sweet Bellini

A brunch staple in its own right, the Bellini brings together the sweetness of peaches with the effervescence of sparkling wine for a light and fruity concoction.

Ingredients:

  • Peach purée or fresh peach slices blended
  • Prosecco or sparkling wine
  • Lemon zest or mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add peach purée to the bottom of a champagne flute, filling no more than one-third of the glass.
  2. Slowly pour in Prosecco until the glass is full, ensuring the flavors mingle naturally.
  3. Garnish with a pinch of lemon zest or a sprig of fresh mint for that finishing touch.

For a smoother texture, strain your peach purée before adding it to your drink.

Refreshing Aperol Spritz

Light, crisp, and visually stunning, Aperol Spritz is the ideal choice for a sunny brunch. With just three core ingredients, you’ll have a cocktail that feels sophisticated and effortless.

Ingredients:

  • Aperol
  • Prosecco
  • Club soda

Instructions:

  1. Fill a wine glass halfway with ice.
  2. Pour in equal parts Aperol and Prosecco, then finish with a small splash of club soda for a refreshing bite.
  3. Give your drink a gentle stir and garnish with a slice of orange for added vibrancy.

Want to create a low-alcohol version? Simply increase the ratio of club soda to Aperol.

Zesty Paloma

This Mexican-inspired favorite is zesty, invigorating, and delightfully easy to prepare. It’s perfect for brunches that include heavier fare, as its citrusy brightness helps balance the richness of the meal.

Ingredients:

  • Tequila
  • Grapefruit soda
  • Lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add ice to your glass, preferably one with a salted rim for that authentic touch.
  2. Pour tequila over the ice, topping it with grapefruit soda.
  3. Stir gently and finish the cocktail with a lime wedge squeezed into the mixture.

For a splash of sophistication, consider garnishing with a thin wheel of grapefruit alongside the lime wedge.

Crisp Vodka Soda with Cucumber

When simplicity is key, a vodka soda with cucumber makes for the perfect clean drink to enjoy mid-morning. Its light profile complements any brunch spread and leaves you refreshed without overwhelming your palate.

Ingredients:

  • Vodka
  • Club soda
  • Cucumber slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a tall glass filled with ice, pour vodka and top with club soda.
  2. Use a couple of fresh cucumber slices for garnish, or muddle them lightly in your glass to infuse the cocktail with an earthy undertone.
  3. Stir gently and enjoy.

Add a sprig of mint or a grind of black pepper for an unexpected twist.

Tangy Bloody Mary

Craving a savory option? The classic Bloody Mary delivers a bold punch of flavor and pairs perfectly with hearty brunch dishes.

Ingredients:

  • Tomato juice
  • Vodka
  • Hot sauce or Tabasco

Instructions:

  1. Pour tomato juice into a shaker filled with ice. Add vodka and a few dashes of hot sauce.
  2. Shake gently and strain into a tall glass.
  3. Garnish with celery, olives, or even pickled vegetables to make this cocktail your own.

Customize the spice level to match your brunch mood, whether mild or fiery.

Effortless Kir Royale

This French-inspired beauty is ideal for celebrations and lends a touch of class to any brunch setting.

Ingredients:

  • Crème de cassis (blackcurrant liqueur)
  • Champagne or sparkling wine
  • Fresh berries for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Begin by pouring crème de cassis into a champagne flute. A tablespoon is usually sufficient, but feel free to adjust to taste.
  2. Slowly fill the glass with champagne to avoid disrupting the layered effect.
  3. Garnish with a couple of fresh berries like raspberries or blackberries.

The Kir Royale’s fruity notes pair perfectly with lighter brunch options like fresh salads and pastries.

Bright Screwdriver

Few classic cocktails are easier than a screwdriver, which combines the hearty zest of orange juice with the kick of vodka.

Ingredients:

  • Vodka
  • Orange juice
  • Orange slice for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pour vodka into a glass filled with ice cubes.
  2. Top with orange juice, stirring briefly to combine.
  3. Garnish with a freshly sliced orange and serve immediately.

For an added dimension of flavor, swap regular orange juice for tangerine or mandarin juice.

Each of these cocktails brings a unique flavor profile to your table while remaining simple, quick, and approachable. With only three ingredients per drink, you won’t need to make multiple trips to the store or spend hours prepping in the kitchen. Whether you’re enjoying a laid-back brunch at home or hosting guests for a celebratory spread, these cocktails will effortlessly enhance your day.

Taking the time to pair your brunch cocktails with the right dishes adds an extra layer to the experience. Mimosas are perfect with fruit salads and croissants, while a Bloody Mary is practically begging to be served with eggs Benedict or avocado toast. You’ll find that these easy-to-make cocktails adapt beautifully to every brunch vibe, from casual to elegant.

So stock your fridge, grab your glasses, and toast to good food, great company, and fuss-free cocktails that make brunch the highlight of your week! Cheers!
