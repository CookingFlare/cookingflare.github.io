Chinese Xiaolongbao Recipe with Pork and Soup Dumplings


If you’re a fan of dim sum, then you’ve probably encountered the delightful little parcels of goodness known as Xiaolongbao. Originating from the Jiangnan region of China, Xiaolongbao is a type of steamed bun filled with savory pork filling and a delicious, hot broth. The magic of Xiaolongbao lies in the delicate balance of flavors and textures, making every bite a delightful experience. In this blog post, we’ll take a deep dive into the art of making authentic Chinese Xiaolongbao with pork and soup dumplings right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

For the Filling:

  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1/4 cup chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp grated ginger
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic
  • 1/4 tsp ground white pepper
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions

For the Soup:

  • 1 1/2 cups chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup gelatin

For Serving:

  • Chinese black vinegar
  • Finely shredded ginger

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add the water and knead the mixture until a smooth dough forms.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chicken stock, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, grated ginger, grated garlic, ground white pepper, and chopped green onions.
  2. Mix the filling until well combined, cover, and refrigerate while you prepare the soup.

Step 3: Prepare the Soup

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the chicken stock until hot but not boiling.
  2. Add the gelatin and stir until completely dissolved.
  3. Pour the soup into a shallow container and refrigerate until set.

Step 4: Assemble the Xiaolongbao

  1. Roll out the dough into a long log and cut it into small sections.
  2. Flatten each section into a round wrapper, ensuring the edges are thinner than the center.
  3. Place a small spoonful of the pork filling in the center of each wrapper.
  4. Add a small piece of the set soup on top of the filling.
  5. Pleat and seal the edges of the wrapper to encase the filling, ensuring there are no gaps for the soup to escape during steaming.

Step 5: Steam the Xiaolongbao

  1. Place the assembled Xiaolongbao on parchment paper in a steamer basket, leaving some space between each dumpling.
  2. Steam the Xiaolongbao over boiling water for 8-10 minutes, or until the wrappers are translucent and the filling is cooked through.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

  1. Carefully transfer the Xiaolongbao to a serving plate.
  2. Serve hot, accompanied by Chinese black vinegar and shredded ginger for dipping.

Tips for Success

  • Ensuring the dough is smooth and elastic is essential for achieving the perfect texture of Xiaolongbao wrappers.
  • When assembling the Xiaolongbao, it’s crucial to seal the dumplings tightly to prevent the soup from leaking out during steaming.
  • Steaming the Xiaolongbao over high heat will create the necessary steam pressure to cook the dumplings while keeping the soup inside.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of making Xiaolongbao with pork and soup dumplings may take some practice, but the rewards are undoubtedly worth it. The combination of tender pork filling, savory soup, and delicate wrappers creates a culinary experience that is both comforting and exciting. So, gather the ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a flavorful journey to create authentic Chinese Xiaolongbao that will surely impress your family and friends. Happy cooking!
