10 Mouthwatering Vegan Desserts for Your Sweet Tooth


Are you craving something sweet but want to stick to a plant-based diet? Don’t worry, there are plenty of delicious vegan desserts that will satisfy your sweet tooth. Here are 10 recipes to try, with ingredients and step-by-step instructions. Let’s get started!

  1. Vegan Chocolate Avocado Mousse Who says avocados are only good for savory dishes? This creamy and smooth chocolate mousse will make you forget it’s made with avocado.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup vegan chocolate chips, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Scoop out the flesh of the avocados and place it in a blender.

  2. Add almond milk, cocoa powder, maple syrup, melted chocolate chips, and vanilla extract.

  3. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  4. Pour into a serving dish, chill for at least an hour, and enjoy!

  5. Fluffy Coconut Whipped Cream This is the perfect vegan dessert to serve with fruits, pies, or any other dessert. It’s also very easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) of full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Open the can of coconut milk and scoop out the solid part into a mixing bowl.

  2. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract.

  3. Beat with an electric mixer until the mixture is fluffy and has doubled in volume.

  4. Serve immediately or store in the fridge for up to a week.

  5. Chocolate Dipped Strawberries These chocolate-covered strawberries taste like a gourmet dessert but are incredibly simple to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup vegan chocolate chips
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler, stirring constantly.

  2. Once melted, remove from heat, and let it cool slightly.

  3. Dip strawberries into the melted chocolate, making sure they’re fully coated.

  4. Place dipped strawberries on a parchment-lined tray, and let them cool at room temperature before serving.

  5. Healthy Vegan Cupcakes These cupcakes are moist and delicious, and you wouldn’t believe they’re made without any butter, eggs, or refined sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: vegan frosting of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a muffin pan with cupcake liners.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together all-purpose flour, oat flour, baking powder, and baking soda.

  3. In another bowl, whisk together applesauce, almond milk, maple syrup, coconut oil, and vanilla.

  4. Pour wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until well combined.

  5. Divide batter evenly among cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

  6. Let cool completely before frosting.

  7. Vegan Matcha Ice Cream Matcha’s unique rich flavor combines beautifully with the creamy texture of homemade ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) of full-fat coconut milk
  • 4 tablespoons matcha green tea powder
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Open the can of coconut milk and scoop out the solid part into a blender.

  2. Add matcha green tea powder, maple syrup, and salt.

  3. Blend until smooth and creamy. Taste the mixture and adjust sweetness if needed.

  4. Avocado Lime Popsicles These refreshing popsicles are perfect for hot summer days. The creamy texture of avocado combined with zesty lime is an unexpected but delightful combination.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons lime zest
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Scoop out the flesh of the avocados and place it in a blender.

  2. Add almond milk, lime juice, lime zest, and maple syrup.

  3. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  4. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds and freeze for at least 4 hours.

  5. Vegan Carrot Cake If you’re a carrot cake lover, this vegan version is a must-try. It’s moist, packed with spices, and topped with a rich and creamy cream cheese frosting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 cup vegan cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter
  • 2 cups powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and grease a 9-inch round cake pan.

  2. In a medium bowl, mix together all-purpose flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.

  3. In another bowl whisk together grated carrots and chopped walnuts.

  4. In a separate bowl, beat vegan cream cheese and vegan butter until smooth.

  5. Gradually add powdered sugar and beat until fluffy.

  6. Add wet ingredients to dry ingredients, and mix until well combined.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, smooth the top and bake for about 25-30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.

  8. Let cool completely before frosting.

  9. No-Bake Vegan Cheesecake This no-bake cheesecake is the perfect dessert for those lazy summer days.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups raw almonds
  • 1 cup dates, pitted
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups vegan cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup fresh berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. For the crust, process almonds, dates, and shredded coconut in a food processor until a dough-like consistency is formed. Press into a greased 9-inch springform pan.

  2. For the filling, beat vegan cream cheese and powdered sugar until fluffy.

  3. Add melted vegan butter and vanilla extract, blend until combined.

  4. Pour the filling onto the crust, smooth the top, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours before slicing.

  5. Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies These cookies are soft, chewy, and full of peanut butter flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup vegan peanut butter
  • 1 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together peanut butter and maple syrup.

  3. Add baking soda and salt and mix until combined.

  4. Stir in rolled oats and chocolate chips (if using).

  5. Drop spoonfuls of the dough onto the prepared baking sheet.

  6. Bake for 12-14 minutes, or until cookies are lightly golden around the edges.

  7. Blueberry Lemon Bars These vegan blueberry lemon bars combine the tanginess of lemon with the sweetness of blueberries- a perfect union of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. For the crust, process flour, rolled oats, and vegan butter in a food processor until a crumbly texture is formed. Press into the bottom of a greased 8x8-inch baking dish.
  2. For the filling, gently mix blueberries, lemon juice, and cornstarch in a saucepan.
  3. Cook over medium heat until the mixture starts to thicken, stirring occasionally.
  4. Pour the filling over the crust, and let it cool before slicing.

Now that you have 10 delicious and mouthwatering vegan desserts, satisfy your sweet tooth without compromising your dietary preference. Enjoy!
