The Hanky Panky 2.0: A Modern Twist on a Classic Gin Cocktail


The Hanky Panky, a classic gin cocktail that has been delighting palates for over a century. Created by Ada Coleman, a renowned bartender at the American Bar in London’s Savoy Hotel, in the early 1900s, this cocktail has stood the test of time. However, as with all classics, it’s essential to evolve and modernize to keep the spirit of innovation alive. In this post, we’ll explore the original recipe and then introduce a modern twist, perfectly capturing the essence of this timeless cocktail.

The Original Recipe

The classic Hanky Panky recipe is a simple yet elegant combination of gin, sweet vermouth, and Fernet-Branca, an Italian bitter liqueur. The original recipe calls for:

  • 1 1/2 oz gin
  • 1/2 oz sweet vermouth
  • 2 dashes Fernet-Branca
  • Orange peel (optional, for garnish)

Combine the gin and sweet vermouth in a mixing glass filled with ice. Add the Fernet-Branca and stir for approximately 30 seconds to chill and dilute the ingredients. Strain the mixture into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass. Express a strip of orange peel over the drink, if desired, and serve.

The Evolution: Hanky Panky 2.0

While the original recipe is a masterpiece, we can’t help but experiment and push the boundaries of this classic cocktail. For Hanky Panky 2.0, we’ll introduce a few innovative elements to elevate the flavor profile and texture.

  • Replace the sweet vermouth with a smaller amount of a more complex vermouth, such as Carpano Antica or Dolin. This will add depth and subtle fruit notes to the cocktail.
  • Add a splash of citrus, specifically grapefruit juice, to balance the bitterness of the Fernet-Branca and introduce a tangy, refreshing quality.
  • Incorporate a dash of absinthe, a herbal liqueur, to enhance the botanicals in the gin and create a more complex, mysterious flavor profile.
  • Garnish with a slice of grapefruit, rather than orange, to visually tie in with the citrus element and add a pop of color to the glass.

Here’s the updated recipe:

  • 1 1/2 oz gin
  • 1/4 oz Carpano Antica vermouth
  • 1/2 oz grapefruit juice
  • 2 dashes Fernet-Branca
  • 1 dash absinthe
  • Grapefruit slice (for garnish)

Combine the gin, Carpano Antica vermouth, grapefruit juice, and Fernet-Branca in a mixing glass filled with ice. Add the absinthe and stir for approximately 30 seconds to chill and dilute the ingredients. Strain the mixture into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass. Express a slice of grapefruit over the drink and serve.

Tasting Notes

The Hanky Panky 2.0 offers a harmonious balance of flavors, with the gin and Fernet-Branca providing a solid foundation. The Carpano Antica vermouth adds a subtle fruitiness, while the grapefruit juice injects a tangy, citrusy element. The absinthe, with its herbal notes, ties everything together, creating a complex and intriguing flavor profile.

This modern twist on the classic Hanky Panky is a testament to the versatility of gin cocktails and the importance of evolution in the world of mixology. Whether you’re a seasoned bartender or a newcomer to the world of cocktails, the Hanky Panky 2.0 is sure to delight and inspire.

Conclusion

The Hanky Panky, a timeless classic that has captured the hearts of cocktail enthusiasts for generations. By experimenting with new ingredients and techniques, we can create innovative variations that honor the original while pushing the boundaries of flavor and creativity. The Hanky Panky 2.0 is a shining example of this approach, offering a unique and captivating drinking experience that is sure to become a new favorite.
