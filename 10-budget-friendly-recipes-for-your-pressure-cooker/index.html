10 Budget-Friendly Recipes for Your Pressure Cooker


A pressure cooker can be one of the most versatile and cost-effective tools in your kitchen. It saves time and energy while transforming simple, inexpensive ingredients into hearty, flavorful dishes. Here are ten budget-friendly recipes that showcase just how much you can achieve with this powerful kitchen appliance.

1. Classic Lentil Soup

Lentils are known for their affordability and versatility. This hearty meal is packed with plant-based protein and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the pressure cooker to sauté mode. Add a splash of olive oil and sauté the onions, carrots, and garlic until softened.
  2. Stir in the cumin and cook for another minute.
  3. Add lentils, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook on high pressure for 15 minutes. Allow a natural release for 10 minutes before serving.

This dish is perfect for meal prepping and pairs wonderfully with a slice of crusty bread.

2. Chicken Drumstick Stew

Chicken drumsticks are an affordable source of protein that are superbly tender when cooked in the pressure cooker.

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken drumsticks
  • 4 medium potatoes, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brown the chicken drumsticks in the pressure cooker using the sauté function.
  2. Remove the drumsticks and sauté the onions, carrots, and potatoes until lightly golden.
  3. Return the drumsticks to the pot. Add the tomato sauce, chicken broth, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Seal and cook on high pressure for 12 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally.
  5. Stir and garnish with fresh parsley for added flavor.

This easy one-pot dish is comforting and filling, perfect for a weeknight meal.

3. Black Bean Chili

Canned beans may be cheap, but cooking dried beans in your pressure cooker is even more economical—and they taste better, too.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried black beans (soaked overnight)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onions, bell pepper, and garlic in the pressure cooker until fragrant.
  2. Stir in the chili powder and cumin, cooking for 1 minute.
  3. Add the soaked black beans, diced tomatoes, and stock.
  4. Cook on high pressure for 25 minutes. Allow natural pressure release.
  5. Serve with a dollop of yogurt, shredded cheese, or fresh cilantro.

This dish freezes well, making it a convenient, budget-friendly meal option.

4. Creamy Potato Soup

A few simple ingredients come together to create a velvety, satisfying soup that packs a ton of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • Salt, pepper, and herbs of choice

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onions and garlic in the pressure cooker.
  2. Add the potatoes, broth, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook on high pressure for 8 minutes. Do a quick release.
  4. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until creamy—or leave it chunky for texture.
  5. Stir in the milk or cream and adjust seasoning.

This comforting soup makes a wonderful main course or starter.

5. Shredded BBQ Chicken

This flavorful shredded chicken is perfect for sandwiches, tacos, or over a bowl of rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Add chicken thighs to the pressure cooker.
  2. Pour in barbecue sauce, water, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Seal the lid and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. Allow for natural release.
  4. Shred the chicken and stir it back into the sauce.

You’ll love how versatile and affordable this dish is for weeknight dinners.

6. Vegetable Biryani

Transform pantry staples into this aromatic Indian-inspired dish that’s loaded with vegetables and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 3 cups water or vegetable stock
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onions and garlic in the pressure cooker until softened.
  2. Add the garam masala and turmeric, stirring to release the spices’ aroma.
  3. Stir in the rice, vegetables, salt, and water or stock.
  4. Cook on high pressure for 6 minutes. Do a quick release.
  5. Fluff gently and serve with yogurt or chutney.

This recipe makes enough to serve a crowd or provide leftovers for lunches.

7. Beef Stew

Beef stew is a classic one-pot meal that’s easy to prepare with affordable cuts of beef.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb stew beef
  • 4 medium potatoes, quartered
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brown the stew beef in the pressure cooker using the sauté function.
  2. Add the onion and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in the potatoes, carrots, tomato paste, thyme, beef broth, salt, and pepper.
  4. Cook on high pressure for 20 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release.

This dish is rich, filling, and tastes even better the next day.

8. Rice and Beans

A classic, budget-friendly combination of rice and beans is elevated in a pressure cooker.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried beans (your choice, soaked overnight)
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onions and garlic in the pressure cooker.
  2. Add the beans, rice, broth, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Seal and cook on high pressure for 15 minutes. Allow natural release.
  4. Fluff the rice and serve with hot sauce or chopped fresh herbs.

This is a highly customizable dish that you can adapt based on what you have on hand.

9. Cabbage and Sausage Stir

Cabbage is one of the cheapest vegetables you can buy, and it pairs deliciously with sausage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small cabbage, shredded
  • 1 lb sausage (smoked or fresh)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brown the sausage in the pressure cooker and set aside.
  2. Sauté onions until soft.
  3. Add cabbage, paprika, chicken broth, salt, and pepper. Top with sausage.
  4. Cook on high pressure for 5 minutes. Do a quick release before serving.

This tasty dish is as fast as it is affordable.

10. Steel-Cut Oats with Apples and Cinnamon

Breakfast doesn’t get any easier—or cheaper—than this healthy, hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup steel-cut oats
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 apple, diced
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add oats, water, apple, cinnamon, and salt to the pressure cooker.
  2. Cook on high pressure for 4 minutes. Let pressure release naturally for another 10 minutes.
  3. Stir and top with your favorite nuts or sweetener before serving.

This dish is perfect for starting your day on the right foot.

These recipes prove that cooking on a budget doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or creativity. Best of all, your pressure cooker does most of the work for you, leaving you with plenty of extra time and energy. Start incorporating these affordable meals into your weekly routine, and you’ll see just how enjoyable and easy cooking can be!
