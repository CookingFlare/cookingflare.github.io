Chicken Teriyaki Skewers: Sweet and Savory Delight


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in the delightful flavors of teriyaki. The perfect combination of sweet and savory, teriyaki sauce has the power to elevate simple ingredients into a culinary masterpiece. One dish that truly showcases the magic of teriyaki is Chicken Teriyaki Skewers. These skewers are not only a feast for the taste buds but also a visual delight, making them a perfect addition to any gathering or a cozy family dinner. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create these succulent skewers and unlock the secrets to a truly irresistible teriyaki glaze.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Teriyaki Skewers:

  • 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

For the Teriyaki Sauce:

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Teriyaki Sauce: In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, water, brown sugar, honey, garlic, ginger, and mirin. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. In a separate bowl, mix the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to create a slurry. Slowly pour the slurry into the saucepan while whisking continuously. Reduce the heat and simmer the sauce for 2-3 minutes, or until it thickens. Set aside to cool.

  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken pieces in a bowl and pour half of the teriyaki sauce over them, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Assemble the Skewers: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken, bell peppers, and red onions onto the soaked wooden skewers, alternating the ingredients for a vibrant presentation.

  4. Grill the Skewers: Lightly oil the grill grates to prevent sticking. Place the assembled skewers on the grill and cook for 6-8 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a beautiful caramelized exterior. While grilling, baste the skewers with the reserved teriyaki sauce to enhance the flavor and glaze the skewers with a glossy finish.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the skewers are cooked to perfection, carefully remove them from the grill and place them on a serving platter. Drizzle any remaining teriyaki sauce over the skewers as a final touch. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an extra pop of flavor and visual appeal.

Tips for Success

  • To ensure tender and juicy chicken, avoid overcooking the skewers on the grill. The high heat can quickly dry out the meat, so keep a close eye on them.
  • Experiment with different vegetables like zucchini, cherry tomatoes, or mushrooms to customize the skewers to your liking.
  • If you prefer a thicker glaze, you can simmer the teriyaki sauce for a few additional minutes until it reaches your desired consistency.

In Conclusion

Chicken Teriyaki Skewers are a delightful marriage of flavors and textures, making them a crowd-pleaser at any gathering. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of Japan at home, these skewers are sure to transport your taste buds to a realm of sweet and savory perfection. Embrace the art of teriyaki and savor each succulent bite of these irresistible skewers.
