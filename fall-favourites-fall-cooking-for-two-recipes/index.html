Fall Favourites: Fall Cooking for Two Recipes


As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, it’s time to embrace the flavors of fall. Fall is the perfect season to cozy up with your loved one and enjoy delicious meals together. Whether you’re looking for a comforting dinner or a sweet treat, we’ve got you covered with these fall cooking recipes designed for two. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Butternut Squash Risotto

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash and cook until it starts to soften.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir well to coat the grains with oil.
  4. Gradually add the vegetable broth, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Continue adding the broth, a ladleful at a time, until the rice is cooked al dente.
  6. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy this creamy and comforting fall dish.

2. Apple Cider Glazed Pork Chops

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 bone-in pork chops
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper.
  3. In a skillet, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the pork chops on both sides until browned.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the apple cider, maple syrup, and Dijon mustard.
  5. Pour the glaze over the pork chops and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  7. Remove from the oven and let the pork chops rest for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Drizzle any remaining glaze over the chops and enjoy the sweet and savory flavors of fall.

3. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  6. Serve the pancakes warm with maple syrup or your favorite fall-inspired toppings.

Conclusion

Fall is a beautiful season filled with vibrant colors and delicious flavors. These fall cooking recipes for two are perfect for cozy evenings spent with your loved one. From the creamy and comforting butternut squash risotto to the sweet and savory apple cider glazed pork chops, and the indulgent pumpkin spice pancakes, these recipes will surely satisfy your fall cravings. So embrace the season and enjoy the delicious tastes of fall together!
