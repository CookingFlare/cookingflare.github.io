5 Easy Rice Dishes for Meal Prep Success


Rice is an incredibly versatile and affordable ingredient that can serve as the base for countless delicious and nutritious meals. Whether you’re meal prepping for the week or simply looking for quick and easy dinner ideas, rice dishes offer a wonderful way to save time, reduce stress, and fuel your body with balanced meals.

In this blog post, we’ll explore five easy rice dishes perfect for meal prep. These dishes are not only packed with flavor but are also customizable to suit your dietary preferences and needs. From hearty bowls to fresh salads, these rice dishes will add variety to your weekly meal prep routine while ensuring you stay full and satisfied.

1. Chicken and Veggie Stir-Fry Rice

Stir-fries are a fantastic way to incorporate a variety of vegetables and protein into a single meal, and when paired with rice, they become a filling, wholesome dish that can be enjoyed throughout the week.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 yellow bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 small onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • Optional: sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Begin by cooking your rice, whether you’re using white, brown, or jasmine rice. Make sure to follow the package instructions for the perfect texture.

  2. Prepare the stir-fry: Heat olive oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Add the veggies: To the same pan, add the garlic, onion, bell peppers, and broccoli. Stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender yet still crisp.

  4. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, honey (or maple syrup), and grated ginger. Pour this sauce over the chicken and vegetables in the pan. Stir everything together and cook for another 2-3 minutes to combine the flavors.

  5. Serve: Layer the cooked rice into meal prep containers, and top with the stir-fry mixture. Optionally, garnish with sesame seeds and green onions.

Tips for Meal Prep:

  • You can prep this dish for several days by storing the rice and stir-fry separately. This will help keep the rice fresh and prevent it from getting soggy.
  • For added variety, switch up the protein by using tofu or shrimp.

2. Vegetarian Mexican Rice Bowls

These vegetarian Mexican rice bowls are perfect for meal prep because they come together quickly, and the flavors only improve as they sit in the fridge. The combination of seasoned rice, fresh veggies, and hearty beans creates a satisfying and balanced meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 can black beans (rinsed and drained)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Optional toppings: avocado, sour cream, shredded cheese, salsa

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Start by cooking your rice. White rice is typically preferred for this dish because of its fluffy texture, but brown rice can be used for added fiber and nutrients.

  2. Season the rice: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until they become soft and fragrant, about 3 minutes. Add the cumin, paprika, chili powder, and oregano, stirring to coat the onions and garlic with the spices.

  3. Combine the ingredients: Stir in the cooked rice, black beans, and corn. Heat through, stirring occasionally, for about 5 minutes. Add the fresh cherry tomatoes and lime juice, and mix well.

  4. Assemble the bowls: Divide the rice mixture into individual meal prep containers. Top each bowl with chopped cilantro and any optional toppings like avocado or sour cream.

Tips for Meal Prep:

  • These rice bowls are perfect for a plant-based meal prep. You can add other vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or sautéed spinach to increase the nutrient density.
  • If you’re looking to increase protein content, add some grilled chicken or a dollop of Greek yogurt.

3. Curry Chicken and Rice

This dish brings rich, comforting flavors to your meal prep routine. The combination of tender chicken with aromatic spices and creamy coconut milk creates a flavorful curry that pairs perfectly with fluffy rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 can coconut milk (full-fat)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Begin by cooking the jasmine rice, following the package instructions for a fluffy texture.

  2. Cook the chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 5-7 minutes, until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Prepare the curry sauce: In the same skillet, add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and cinnamon. Cook for another 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.

  4. Make the curry: Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, stirring to combine. Bring to a simmer, then add the chicken back into the skillet. Let the curry simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Serve: Divide the cooked rice into meal prep containers, then top with the curry chicken. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Tips for Meal Prep:

  • This curry dish can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge for up to 4 days. The flavors continue to develop as it sits.
  • For a lighter version, you can substitute the coconut milk with light coconut milk or even use Greek yogurt for a creamy, tangy flavor.

4. Lemon Garlic Shrimp and Rice

For those who love seafood, this lemon garlic shrimp and rice dish offers a quick and flavorful meal that can easily be prepped in advance. The combination of shrimp, garlic, and fresh lemon adds brightness to the rich base of rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 1 lb large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup parsley (chopped)
  • Optional: red pepper flakes for heat

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Prepare your rice according to the package instructions. Jasmine rice works particularly well for this dish because of its fragrant aroma and slightly sticky texture.

  2. Cook the shrimp: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set aside.

  3. Prepare the garlic sauce: In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the lemon zest and juice, stirring to combine.

  4. Combine and serve: Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss to coat in the garlic-lemon sauce. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Serve the shrimp over the rice and garnish with fresh parsley.

Tips for Meal Prep:

  • Shrimp cooks quickly, making this dish a great option for busy days. Store the shrimp and rice separately to maintain their texture.
  • If you prefer a different protein, grilled chicken or tofu can be swapped in place of the shrimp.

5. One-Pot Spanish Rice with Chorizo

This one-pot Spanish rice with chorizo is a flavorful and hearty dish that requires minimal cleanup. The chorizo adds a smoky richness, while the rice absorbs all the bold flavors of the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 1 lb chorizo (removed from casing)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (diced)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Prepare your rice in advance, following package instructions.

  2. Cook the chorizo: In a large pot or skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chorizo and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Remove the chorizo and set aside.

  3. Sauté the veggies: In the same pot, add the onion and red bell pepper. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.

  4. Combine and simmer: Stir in the diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.

  5. Finish the dish: Stir in the cooked rice and chorizo. Mix everything together and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips for Meal Prep:

  • This dish is ideal for reheating throughout the week. The flavors deepen over time, making it an excellent choice for meal prep.
  • You can substitute the chorizo with turkey sausage for a lighter version.

These five easy rice dishes are perfect for meal prep success. They offer variety, balance, and flavor, all while making your week more organized and efficient. With simple ingredients and minimal effort, these rice-based meals can fuel your busy days and help you stay on track with your nutritional goals. So, next time you’re planning your weekly meals, don’t forget to include these easy rice dishes for a satisfying, stress-free week ahead!
