Grilled Fourth of July Vegetable Recipes for BBQ


Welcome to our blog post dedicated to delicious grilled vegetable recipes perfect for your Fourth of July barbecue! We’re excited to share with you some mouthwatering dishes that will wow your guests and add a healthy and flavorful twist to your celebrations. Fire up the grill and get ready to impress with these delectable creations!

Grilled Corn with Herb Butter

First up, we have a classic Fourth of July favorite: grilled corn on the cob. This simple yet flavorful dish is a crowd-pleaser and a staple at any BBQ. Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the husks from the corn and brush each cob with melted butter. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and pepper.

Place the corn on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred. While the corn is grilling, prepare the herb butter by mixing softened butter with finely chopped fresh herbs like parsley, cilantro, and chives. Once the corn is ready, generously spread the herb butter over each cob and serve immediately. It’s a simple, yet irresistible side dish that will have everyone coming back for seconds!

Grilled Zucchini and Eggplant Skewers

Next on the menu, we have grilled zucchini and eggplant skewers, a colorful and healthy addition to your BBQ spread. Start by cutting the zucchini and eggplant into thick slices. Thread the slices onto skewers, alternating between zucchini and eggplant. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with a mixture of dried herbs like oregano, basil, and thyme.

Grill the skewers over medium heat for about 6-8 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized. The smoky flavors from the grill will enhance the natural sweetness of the vegetables, creating a delightful medley of flavors. Serve the skewers as a side dish or even as a vegetarian main course option. Your guests will love the vibrant colors and delicious taste!

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers

For all the burger lovers out there, we have a scrumptious vegetarian option that will satisfy even the most devoted meat eaters. Grilled Portobello mushroom burgers are a hearty and flavorful alternative that will leave everyone asking for the recipe. Start by removing the stems from the Portobello mushrooms and gently brushing them with olive oil.

Grill the mushrooms over medium-high heat for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and juicy. While the mushrooms are grilling, prepare your burger buns and toppings. Assemble your burger with your favorite condiments, such as lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and cheese. The savory umami flavor of the grilled mushrooms combined with the fresh toppings creates a burger experience that is both satisfying and full of flavor.

Grilled Watermelon Salad

To finish off our Fourth of July vegetable extravaganza, we have a unique and refreshing grilled watermelon salad that will surprise and delight your taste buds. Slice a watermelon into thick wedges and brush each slice with a little olive oil. Grill the watermelon slices over high heat for about 2-3 minutes per side, until they have beautiful grill marks.

Once the watermelon slices are grilled, cut them into bite-sized pieces and place them in a bowl. Add crumbled feta cheese, fresh mint leaves, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze. Toss gently to combine, and your grilled watermelon salad is ready to serve. The smoky sweetness of the grilled watermelon paired with the tanginess of the feta cheese and the freshness of the mint creates a unique and delightful salad that will be the talk of the party.

Conclusion

There you have it – a selection of grilled Fourth of July vegetable recipes that will elevate your BBQ to a whole new level. From the classic grilled corn with herb butter to the creative grilled watermelon salad, these dishes showcase the versatility and deliciousness of grilled vegetables. So fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and enjoy a memorable Fourth of July celebration filled with flavor, fun, and fantastic food!

Happy grilling and happy Fourth of July!
