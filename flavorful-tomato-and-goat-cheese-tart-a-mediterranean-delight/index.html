Flavorful Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart: A Mediterranean Delight


If you’re looking for a dish that embodies the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean, then you’re in for a treat with this exquisite Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart recipe. Bursting with the fresh, tangy taste of tomatoes and the creamy richness of goat cheese, this tart is an absolute showstopper that will surely impress your family and friends. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

For the Tart Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4-5 ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Glaze:

  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions

Making the Tart Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Carefully transfer the dough to the pan, pressing it into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough and prick the bottom with a fork. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Blind bake the crust for 10-12 minutes, then remove the parchment paper and weights and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until lightly golden. Set aside to cool.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the balsamic vinegar and honey to make the glaze. Set aside.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix the crumbled goat cheese with the minced garlic and chopped basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assembling the Tart:

  1. Spread the goat cheese mixture evenly over the cooled tart crust.

  2. Arrange the sliced tomatoes on top of the goat cheese in a single layer, slightly overlapping.

  3. Drizzle the tomato slices with the balsamic glaze.

  4. Drizzle the extra virgin olive oil over the tomatoes and season with a pinch of salt and pepper.

Baking the Tart:

  1. Bake the tart in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the tomatoes are tender and the crust is golden brown.

  2. Once baked, remove the tart from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.

  3. Serve the Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart warm or at room temperature, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

Enjoy the Mediterranean Flavors

This Flavorful Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart is a celebration of the vibrant, sun-kissed flavors of the Mediterranean. With its flaky, buttery crust, luscious goat cheese filling, and juicy, ripe tomatoes, every bite is a symphony of taste and texture. Whether you savor it as an appetizer, a light lunch, or a delightful side dish, this tart is sure to transport your taste buds to the shores of the Mediterranean.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of the Mediterranean, whip up this tantalizing Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart and savor a slice of culinary paradise. Buon appetito!
