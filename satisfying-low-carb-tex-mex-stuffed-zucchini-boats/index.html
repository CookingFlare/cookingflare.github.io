Satisfying Low Carb Tex-Mex Stuffed Zucchini Boats


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying low-carb meal that’s bursting with flavor, then look no further than these Tex-Mex stuffed zucchini boats. This recipe is a fantastic way to enjoy the vibrant and bold flavors of Tex-Mex cuisine while keeping your carb intake in check. Not only is it a healthier alternative to traditional Tex-Mex dishes, but it’s also incredibly easy to make and customizable to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients

To make these mouthwatering Tex-Mex stuffed zucchini boats, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound ground turkey or lean ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Zucchinis

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is preheating, prepare the zucchinis by slicing them in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scoop out the seeds and create a hollow “boat” out of each zucchini half. Place the hollowed-out zucchinis on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Step 2: Cook the Filling

In a large skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant. Add the ground turkey or beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Once the meat is browned, add the diced bell pepper, corn kernels, black beans, and cherry tomatoes to the skillet. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook the mixture for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

Step 3: Stuff the Zucchinis

Divide the filling evenly among the hollowed-out zucchini boats, pressing the mixture down gently. Once all the zucchinis are filled, sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese over the top of each boat.

Step 4: Bake and Serve

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the stuffed zucchini boats for 20-25 minutes, or until the zucchinis are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Once they’re done, remove the zucchinis from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Sprinkle the chopped cilantro over the top of the boats for a burst of freshness. Serve the Tex-Mex stuffed zucchini boats with lime wedges on the side for an extra zing of flavor.

Customization Options

Feel free to customize this recipe to suit your preferences. You can add a dollop of sour cream or a drizzle of hot sauce on top of the stuffed zucchini boats for added creaminess or heat. If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply omit the ground meat and double up on the beans for a protein-packed alternative. You can also experiment with different cheese varieties or additional toppings such as diced avocado or jalapeños.

These Tex-Mex stuffed zucchini boats are not only a feast for the taste buds but also a nutritious and wholesome meal that’s perfect for anyone following a low-carb lifestyle. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or for a crowd, this recipe is sure to be a hit. Enjoy the bold flavors and satisfying textures of Tex-Mex cuisine in a healthier and guilt-free way with these delightful stuffed zucchini boats!
