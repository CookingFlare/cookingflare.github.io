Dumplings: The Savory Side of Sweet

While we often associate dumplings with savory fillings like pork or chicken, it’s time to explore their sweet side! Here are two scrumptious recipes that’ll revolutionize your understanding of dumplings.

1. Sweet Potato and Purple Yam Dumplings

Let’s start by creating a tantalizing fusion of flavors using sweet potatoes and purple yams. These exotic ingredients not only give these dumplings a unique taste but also add an irresistible visual appeal to your table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 1 large sweet potato
  • 1 large purple yam
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Begin by peeling and washing the sweet potatoes and purple yam, then cut them into chunks.
  2. Steam or boil the sweet potatoes and purple yam until tender, and mash them using a ricer or a fork.
  3. Add sugar, salt, and vanilla extract, mixing until well combined. Set aside to cool, then add the vegetable oil.
  4. Once mixtures have cooled, gradually incorporate the mashed vegetables into the flour and water mixture, kneading until you have a smooth dough.
  5. Cut the dough into small pieces, and roll each one out into thin circles. Prepare your desired dumpling shape and fill each wrapper with a spoonful of the sweet potato and purple yam mixture.
  6. Steam or pan-fry the dumplings until golden, and serve it hot with a sprinkle of powdered sugar for added sweetness.

2. Mango and Coconut Dumplings

Bring South-East Asian flavors into your kitchen with this delectable recipe featuring succulent mango and tropical coconut, wrapped up in a dumpling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 ripe mango
  • 3/4 cup desiccated coconut
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. First, prepare the mango by peeling and dicing it into small pieces. Set aside.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour and water, stirring until you achieve a smooth consistency. Knead the dough into a soft ball, cover, and let rest for 15 minutes.
  3. In another bowl, mix together desiccated coconut, coconut milk, and salt. Stir in the mango pieces, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  4. Roll out the dough into thin circles, and assemble your dumplings using your desired shape. Fill the center with the mango and coconut mixture.
  5. Steam or pan-fry the coconut and mango dumplings until golden. Drizzle a sweet and spicy dipping sauce (created using chili sauce, honey, and lime juice) over top for an extra burst of flavor.

Desserts: A Symphony of Sweet Delights

Now that we have explored the sweet side of dumplings, it’s time to dive into some irresistible Chinese desserts. Check out these delightful treats that’ll leave you craving more.

1. Red Bean Mooncakes

Famously associated with the Mid-Autumn Festival, red bean mooncakes carry a rich tea flavor that makes them a perfect after-meal treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 cups red bean paste
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, oil, and water, stirring until a dough forms. Knead the dough until smooth, then cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Take a piece of the dough and roll it out into a thin circle. Place a spoonful of red bean paste in the center, add a sprinkle of sesame seeds, then fold and pleat the dough around the filling.
  3. Steam or bake the mooncakes until golden, then serve them warm with hot Chinese tea.

2. Lotus Seed Pudding

A classic Chinese dessert with a delicate flavor and firm, yet silky texture, this lotus seed pudding is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser in any household.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lotus seeds
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup tapioca pearls
  • 1/2 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Soak the lotus seeds in water overnight, or for at least 4 hours. Drain, rinse, and then boil them in fresh water for 30 minutes, or until tender.
  2. In a larger pot, bring the soaked and boiled lotus seeds to a simmer, and add sugar, stirring until dissolved. Allow the mixture to simmer until the liquid thickens, about 10 to 15 minutes. Set aside.
  3. In a separate saucepan, boil the tapioca pearls for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Drain and rinse them under cold water.
  4. In a bowl, combine tapioca pearls and milk, mixing until well incorporated.
  5. Pour the lotus seed mixture into a serving dish, top with the tapioca and milk mixture, garnish with a hint of cinnamon, and serve chilled.
