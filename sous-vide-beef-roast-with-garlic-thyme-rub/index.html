Sous Vide Beef Roast with Garlic Thyme Rub


If you’re looking to elevate your cooking game and impress your friends and family with a tender, flavorful beef roast, then sous vide cooking is the way to go. Sous vide is a method that involves cooking food in a vacuum-sealed bag in a water bath at a precise, controlled temperature. This technique ensures that your beef roast turns out perfectly tender, juicy, and packed with flavor every single time. For this recipe, we’ll be preparing a beef roast with a garlic thyme rub, which infuses the meat with aromatic flavors, creating a mouthwatering dish that will leave everyone asking for more.

The Magic of Sous Vide Cooking

Sous vide translates to “under vacuum” in French, and it refers to cooking food in a sealed plastic bag, submerged in a water bath at a consistent temperature. The key to sous vide cooking lies in the precision of the temperature. Unlike traditional roasting or grilling, sous vide allows the meat to cook evenly, ensuring that the roast maintains its juiciness and tenderness without overcooking or drying out.

What makes sous vide especially great for beef is that it ensures your roast will be cooked to your preferred level of doneness—whether you like it medium-rare, medium, or well-done. Because of the low cooking temperatures (typically between 130°F to 160°F), sous vide helps break down the connective tissue in the meat, resulting in an incredibly tender roast.

By using sous vide, you eliminate the guesswork in cooking, ensuring that your roast will be perfect every time. Once the roast is done cooking sous vide, all that’s left is to quickly sear it to develop a crispy, golden-brown crust that locks in all the flavors.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Beef Roast with Garlic Thyme Rub

To make this recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Beef Roast:

  • 1 (3-4 lb) beef roast, such as ribeye or sirloin
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For the Garlic Thyme Rub:

  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt (or more to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons butter, for searing (optional)

Equipment Needed

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Vacuum sealer and vacuum-seal bags or resealable zipper bags
  • Cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan for searing
  • Tongs (for handling the roast during searing)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Sous Vide Beef Roast with Garlic Thyme Rub

1. Prepare the Garlic Thyme Rub

Start by preparing the garlic thyme rub, which will coat your beef roast and infuse it with wonderful aromatic flavors. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, fresh thyme, rosemary (if using), olive oil, Dijon mustard, lemon zest, black pepper, and kosher salt. Stir everything together to form a thick paste.

This rub will act as a marinade, adding flavor to the meat while it cooks in the sous vide bath. The olive oil helps to distribute the flavors evenly, while the herbs and garlic create a fragrant and savory crust when the roast is seared after sous vide cooking.

2. Season the Beef Roast

Next, take your beef roast and season it generously with salt and pepper on all sides. The seasoning helps to enhance the natural flavors of the beef and creates a flavorful exterior once seared. After seasoning, coat the roast evenly with the garlic thyme rub. Use your hands to massage the rub into the meat, making sure that every inch is covered.

Once the roast is coated, place it in a vacuum-seal bag or a resealable zipper bag, ensuring that the bag is tightly sealed around the meat. If using a vacuum sealer, make sure to seal the bag according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If using a resealable bag, the best method is the water displacement technique. Simply lower the bag into a container of water, allowing the water pressure to push the air out, and then seal the bag tightly.

3. Sous Vide Cooking

Set up your sous vide precision cooker and fill a large pot or container with water. Preheat the water bath to the desired cooking temperature. For a medium-rare roast, aim for a temperature of 135°F (57°C). For medium, set the temperature to 145°F (63°C), and for well-done, set it to 160°F (71°C). The beauty of sous vide cooking is that the meat will never overcook once it’s sealed in the bag and submerged in the water bath.

Submerge the bagged beef roast into the water bath, making sure it is completely underwater. Clip the bag to the side of the pot or container to ensure it stays submerged. Let the roast cook for 3-4 hours. The great thing about sous vide cooking is that it’s forgiving—if you leave it in the water bath for a little longer, it won’t dry out. The meat will cook evenly and remain tender, no matter the length of time within that window.

4. Searing the Beef Roast

Once your beef roast is finished cooking sous vide, carefully remove it from the bag. At this point, the roast will be tender and juicy, but it will lack a crispy, caramelized crust. To achieve that, you’ll need to sear it in a hot pan.

Heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add the butter and let it melt until it’s sizzling. Using tongs, place the beef roast into the pan and sear it for about 1-2 minutes on each side. Be sure to sear the top, bottom, and sides to create a nice, golden-brown crust that will lock in the juices and enhance the flavor.

Once the roast is perfectly seared, remove it from the pan and let it rest for about 10 minutes before slicing. Resting the meat allows the juices to redistribute throughout the roast, ensuring a juicy and tender bite every time.

5. Carving and Serving

After the roast has rested, slice it into thick, juicy slices. The meat will be evenly cooked throughout, with a tender and juicy texture that’s a result of the sous vide method. Serve your beef roast with your favorite sides—roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad would pair beautifully with this dish.

Tips for the Perfect Sous Vide Beef Roast

  • Choose the Right Cut of Beef: While you can use any cut of beef for sous vide cooking, some cuts like ribeye, sirloin, or tenderloin yield the best results. These cuts have the right amount of marbling, which helps keep the meat juicy and tender.
  • Sear Quickly: Once the roast is done cooking sous vide, make sure to sear it quickly at high heat. This will prevent the meat from losing its juiciness while developing a beautiful crust.
  • Customize the Rub: Feel free to adjust the ingredients in the garlic thyme rub to suit your personal taste. Add a pinch of chili flakes for some heat, or swap the thyme for herbs like oregano or basil for a different flavor profile.
  • Rest the Meat: Always let the roast rest for 10 minutes after searing. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring every bite is flavorful and moist.
  • Sous Vide Time Window: Don’t stress about the exact cooking time. Beef roasts can be left in the water bath for a longer period without losing their quality. This gives you the flexibility to cook the roast to perfection, even if your schedule changes.

Why Sous Vide is a Game-Changer for Beef Roasts

Sous vide cooking is quickly becoming the preferred method for preparing beef roasts for many home cooks and professional chefs alike. The beauty of this technique lies in the precision it offers, ensuring that your beef roast is cooked exactly how you like it, every single time. The controlled temperature means you won’t risk overcooking or drying out your roast. Instead, it will come out of the water bath perfectly cooked, tender, and juicy.

In addition to the flawless texture, the garlic thyme rub adds a depth of flavor that complements the natural taste of the beef. This method allows you to prepare an elevated meal with minimal effort, freeing you up to focus on preparing side dishes or spending time with your guests.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide beef roast with garlic thyme rub is an incredibly simple yet sophisticated dish that will elevate your cooking skills and impress your dinner guests. The combination of sous vide’s precision cooking and the flavorful rub ensures a beef roast that is succulent, tender, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just looking to enjoy a delicious meal with your loved ones, this recipe is guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

The sous vide method makes it easy to achieve a roast that’s cooked to perfection, while the garlic thyme rub infuses the beef with rich, aromatic flavors. So, next time you’re in the mood for a hearty, flavorful beef roast, break out your sous vide precision cooker and give this recipe a try—you won’t be disappointed!
