Greek-style Lemon Potatoes and Green Beans: A Vibrant Side Dish


If you’re looking for a vibrant and flavorful side dish to accompany your main course, look no further than Greek-style Lemon Potatoes and Green Beans. This delightful combination of tender potatoes, crisp green beans, and tangy lemon will surely elevate your meal to a whole new level. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to add some excitement to your weekday dinners, this recipe is a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 4 large Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, lemon zest, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful marinade.

  3. Add the potato wedges to the bowl and toss them gently to coat them evenly with the marinade. Allow the potatoes to marinate for about 15 minutes, ensuring they absorb all the delicious flavors.

  4. While the potatoes are marinating, bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the green beans and blanch them for about 2 minutes until they turn bright green. Drain the green beans and immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. This will help them retain their vibrant color and crisp texture.

  5. Once the potatoes have marinated and the green beans have been blanched, it’s time to assemble the dish. In a large baking dish, arrange the marinated potato wedges in an even layer. Place the blanched green beans on top of the potatoes, spreading them out evenly.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and give the potatoes and green beans a gentle toss to ensure even cooking.

  7. Return the baking dish to the oven and continue baking uncovered for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are golden brown and tender, and the green beans are crisp-tender.

  8. Once cooked, remove the baking dish from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. The Greek-style Lemon Potatoes and Green Beans can be served warm or at room temperature, making them a versatile side dish for any occasion.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the Greek-style Lemon Potatoes and Green Beans alongside grilled chicken or fish for a complete and satisfying meal.
  • Sprinkle some crumbled feta cheese over the dish before serving for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Garnish with fresh chopped parsley for a vibrant touch.

Conclusion

Greek-style Lemon Potatoes and Green Beans are a delightful addition to any meal. The tangy lemon, fragrant garlic, and earthy oregano perfectly complement the tender potatoes and crisp green beans. This vibrant side dish will not only add a pop of color to your plate but also a burst of flavor that will leave your taste buds wanting more. So, next time you’re in need of a show-stopping side dish, give this recipe a try and watch it become a household favorite. Enjoy!
