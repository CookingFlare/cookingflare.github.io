Instant Pot Barbecue Pulled Pork: A Crowd-Pleasing Recipe


Barbecue pulled pork is a quintessential American dish that never fails to please a crowd. Whether you’re hosting a casual backyard BBQ, preparing a meal for a family dinner, or looking to impress your guests at a game day party, this Instant Pot barbecue pulled pork recipe will become your go-to dish. With tender, juicy meat that falls apart effortlessly, this recipe brings the rich flavors of slow-cooked barbecue in a fraction of the time.

The Instant Pot makes cooking pulled pork easier and faster without sacrificing any of the delicious flavors that are characteristic of slow-smoked pork. In this post, we’ll walk you through each step of preparing this delicious dish, offering tips and tricks along the way to ensure your pulled pork turns out perfectly every time.

Ingredients

For the Pork

  • 4-5 pounds of bone-in pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth (or water if you prefer)

For the Dry Rub

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)

For the Barbecue Sauce

  • 1 cup of your favorite barbecue sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional for added sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce

Why Use an Instant Pot for Pulled Pork?

The Instant Pot is an amazing kitchen tool that combines multiple functions, like a pressure cooker, slow cooker, and sauté pan, all in one appliance. Using the Instant Pot for barbecue pulled pork allows you to significantly cut down on cooking time compared to traditional slow-cooking methods. The pressure cooker function infuses the pork with incredible flavors in just about an hour, making it an ideal option for busy weeknights or impromptu gatherings.

Another benefit of using the Instant Pot is that it locks in moisture, which results in incredibly juicy pulled pork. You won’t need to worry about the meat drying out or taking hours to cook. The Instant Pot is a real time-saver while still giving you a delicious, tender result.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Instant Pot Barbecue Pulled Pork

1. Preparing the Pork Shoulder

Begin by preparing the pork shoulder. You can leave the bone in, which adds extra flavor to the dish, or opt for a boneless cut if you prefer. Either way, pork shoulder is an ideal cut of meat for pulled pork because it has enough fat and connective tissue to become melt-in-your-mouth tender after cooking.

Trim any excess fat from the pork, leaving a bit of the fat cap intact to keep the meat juicy during the cooking process. Once trimmed, cut the pork shoulder into 3-4 large chunks. This will help it cook more evenly and allow the seasoning to penetrate the meat.

2. Make the Dry Rub

In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cumin, mustard powder, and cayenne pepper (if using). Mix everything well until the spices are evenly distributed.

Generously rub this spice mixture all over the pork shoulder chunks. Don’t be afraid to press the rub into the meat to ensure that the flavors really stick. Let the pork sit for about 15-30 minutes to allow the rub to absorb into the meat. If you have more time, you can even refrigerate it overnight for an even deeper flavor.

3. Searing the Pork in the Instant Pot

Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function and let it heat up. Once hot, add the olive oil to the pot. When the oil is shimmering, carefully add the seasoned pork chunks. Sear the pork on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per side, until it’s beautifully browned. This step helps build depth of flavor and creates a nice caramelized crust on the pork.

Once the pork is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside.

4. Sauté the Aromatics

Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the Instant Pot. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until softened and fragrant, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon. These bits, known as fond, are packed with flavor and will help infuse the dish with a rich base.

5. Pressure Cooking the Pork

Return the browned pork to the Instant Pot, nestling it into the onions and garlic. Pour in the chicken broth (or water) to help create the steam needed for pressure cooking.

Lock the lid in place and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 60 minutes. After the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully switch the valve to “venting” to release any remaining pressure.

6. Shred the Pork

Once all the pressure has been released, remove the pork from the Instant Pot and place it on a large cutting board or platter. Use two forks to shred the meat into bite-sized pieces. It should fall apart effortlessly at this point, as the Instant Pot’s pressure cooking has broken down the meat’s collagen, making it tender and juicy.

If you prefer a finer shred, you can use a hand mixer to shred the pork more quickly.

7. Make the Barbecue Sauce

While the pork is resting, turn the Instant Pot back to “Sauté” mode. Let the remaining liquid in the pot reduce for a few minutes until it thickens slightly. Then, add your barbecue sauce, apple cider vinegar, honey (if using), and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to combine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.

Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you like your pulled pork on the tangier side, add a bit more vinegar. If you prefer it sweeter, a little more honey will do the trick.

8. Mix the Pork with the Barbecue Sauce

Return the shredded pork to the Instant Pot and stir it into the barbecue sauce, making sure every bite is coated with the flavorful sauce. Let the mixture simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

9. Serve and Enjoy!

Your Instant Pot barbecue pulled pork is now ready to serve! This dish is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in many ways. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Classic Sandwiches: Pile the pulled pork onto a soft hamburger bun and top with coleslaw for a perfect BBQ sandwich.
  • Tacos: Serve the pulled pork in soft corn or flour tortillas, and add toppings like pickled onions, cilantro, and lime.
  • Salads: Add the pulled pork to a bed of greens with a tangy vinaigrette for a lighter, yet still flavorful, option.
  • Over Rice or Mashed Potatoes: For a hearty meal, serve the pulled pork over rice or mashed potatoes, drizzling the barbecue sauce over the top.

Tips for the Perfect Instant Pot Barbecue Pulled Pork

  • Choosing the Right Cut of Pork: Pork shoulder (or pork butt) is the best cut for pulled pork, as it has the right balance of fat and meat. If you use a leaner cut, such as pork loin, the meat may turn out dry.

  • Don’t Skip the Searing Step: While it might seem tempting to skip searing the pork, this step adds a lot of flavor to the final dish. The caramelization from browning the pork gives the pulled pork a deeper, more savory taste.

  • Adjusting the Spice Level: If you prefer your pulled pork to have a little heat, feel free to increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add some hot sauce to the barbecue sauce. Conversely, if you want a milder version, reduce the amount of chili powder or cayenne pepper.

  • Making it Ahead: You can make pulled pork in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to meld, making it even tastier the next day. You can also freeze leftovers for up to 3 months. Just make sure to store it in an airtight container or freezer bag.

Conclusion

Instant Pot barbecue pulled pork is a game-changer when it comes to serving up a flavorful, tender dish in no time. With its perfect balance of smoky, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors, this pulled pork recipe is sure to be a crowd-pleaser at your next gathering. The versatility of this dish means you can serve it in many different ways, making it a great option for any occasion. So, fire up your Instant Pot and get ready to enjoy this easy and delicious barbecue pulled pork recipe today!
