Budget-Friendly Meals for College Students: 5 Delicious Recipes Under $5


Being a college student often means living on a tight budget. But that doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice flavor and nutrition. In fact, with a little creativity and planning, you can whip up delicious meals that won’t break the bank. Here are five budget-friendly recipes that are not only easy to make but also cost under $5 each!

1. Creamy Tomato Pasta

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 200g pasta
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of cream
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions.
  2. In a separate pan, sauté the onion and garlic until golden brown.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the cream, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Drain the pasta and add it to the tomato sauce. Mix well.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

2. Veggie Stir-Fry

Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, broccoli, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of minced ginger
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a pan or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add the ginger and garlic, and sauté for 1 minute.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with rice or noodles.

3. Black Bean Quesadillas

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup of shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix)
  • 1/4 cup of salsa
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place one tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle half of the cheese evenly over it.
  3. Spread half of the black beans and salsa on top of the cheese.
  4. Top with another tortilla and press lightly.
  5. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese starts to melt.
  6. Flip the quesadilla and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until both sides are golden brown and the cheese is fully melted.
  7. Repeat the process with the remaining tortillas and ingredients.
  8. Cut each quesadilla into wedges and serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired.

4. Sweet Potato Hash

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 15 minutes Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the sweet potatoes, onion, and bell pepper. Sauté for 10-12 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  3. Sprinkle with paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. If desired, create two wells in the hash and crack an egg into each well. Cover and cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your liking.
  5. Serve hot as a standalone dish or with a side of toast.

5. Peanut Butter Banana Toast

Prep Time: 5 minutes Cook Time: 5 minutes Serves: 1

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 tablespoons of peanut butter
  • 1 ripe banana, sliced
  • Honey or maple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread to your desired level of crispiness.
  2. Spread peanut butter evenly on each slice.
  3. Arrange the banana slices on top of one slice of bread.
  4. Drizzle with honey or maple syrup if desired.
  5. Top with the other slice of bread to create a sandwich.
  6. Cut the sandwich in half and enjoy!

These budget-friendly recipes prove that cooking delicious meals as a college student doesn’t have to cost a fortune. With a little planning and creativity, you can enjoy tasty and nutritious dishes without breaking the bank. So, put on your chef’s hat, head to the kitchen, and start experimenting with these wallet-friendly recipes. Happy cooking!
