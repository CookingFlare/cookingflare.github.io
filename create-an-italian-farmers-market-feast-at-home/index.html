Create an Italian Farmers' Market Feast at Home


Are you dreaming of a warm, sun-soaked Italian farmers’ market? Bring that experience home by creating your own Italian feast using fresh, seasonal ingredients. From antipasti to desserts, dive into a mouthwatering culinary journey that will leave your taste buds dancing to an Italian tune.

Start with a Classic Italian Bread

Set the tone for your feast by creating a simple, crusty bread that truly makes any meal feel like a celebration.

classic italian bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of salt

Directions:

  1. Combine 3 cups of flour, 2 teaspoons of yeast, and 2 teaspoons of sugar in a large mixing bowl. Mix well to incorporate.
  2. Pour in 1 ½ cups of warm water, followed by 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Stir until the mixture forms a smooth and elastic dough.
  3. Let the dough rest for 5 minutes to activate the yeast.
  4. Add 1 ½ teaspoons of salt, mix, and knead for 5-7 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about an hour or until it doubles in size.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  7. Divide the dough into manageable pieces and roll out into your desired shape.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Loving L.S.D (Lingua Sans Denti)

Having a meal without enjoying a great glass of wine is simply not quintessential Italian. The wine not only complements your meal but also creates an ambiance that borders on the sublime. To make your dinner extra special, choose a rich, velvety red wine like Chianti or Brunello di Montalcino.

Craving Friuli-style Antipasto

Besides bread, start your feast with a delightful array of Friuli-style antipasto. This multi-faceted series of mouthwatering appetizers from the northeastern Italian region of Friuli will get your taste buds ready for the feast to come.

truffled egg salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  • 1 tablespoon of truffle oil
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh chives
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions:

  1. Chop the hard-boiled eggs into quarters and place them into a bowl.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of truffle oil, 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh chives, and 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley. Mix gently to combine.
  3. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  5. Bring to room temperature before enjoying.

fresh cheese with truffles

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh goat cheese
  • 1 cup of fresh Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of truffle oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Directions:

  1. Combine 2 cups of fresh goat cheese and 1 cup of fresh Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese in a bowl.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of truffle oil and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  3. Mix well and serve with crispy bread.

Follow Through with a hearty pasta dish

No Italian feast would be complete without a satisfying pasta dish. Embrace the bounty of the farmers’ market and create a hearty dish to satisfy your appetite.

mushroom and potato pasta

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh fettuccine pasta
  • 1 ½ cups of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 ½ cups of cubed potatoes
  • 4 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Directions:

  1. Cook 1 pound of fresh fettuccine pasta according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, sautée 1 ½ cups of sliced mushrooms and 1 ½ cups of cubed potatoes in 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat until tender.
  3. Add 4 finely chopped cloves of garlic and continue to sautée until fragrant.
  4. Pour in 2 cups of vegetable broth and bring to a simmer.
  5. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  6. Add the cooked pasta and toss to combine.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with freshly chopped parsley.

Indulge in a Refreshing Summer Dolce

A dolce vita-worthy dessert is the perfect way to end your Italian feast. This refreshing summer dessert will bring a smile to your face and a sweet finish to your meal.

limoncello gelato

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • ¾ cup of sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • 1 cup of limoncello
  • Fresh berries for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a medium-sized saucepan, combine 2 ½ cups of heavy cream, 1 cup of whole milk, and ¾ cup of sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it starts to steam but not boil.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together 3 large egg yolks and 1 teaspoon of lemon zest until well combined.
  3. Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly, to temper the eggs.
  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
  6. Stir in 1 cup of limoncello.
  7. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until it is completely chilled.
  8. Once chilled, churn the mixture in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  9. Transfer the gelato to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  10. Serve in small scoops, garnished with fresh berries.

Close your eyes, and you’ll feel like you’re in the heart of an Italian farmers’ market as you enjoy this authentic Italian feast created in your very own home. With every savory bite, savor the memories and flavors of a warm, sun-soaked evening spent in the company of friends and family.
