Spooky Suppers: Deliciously Gruesome Pizza Recipes for Spooky Evening Celebrations


Nothing quite sets the mood for a spooky evening celebration like a ghoulish pizza straight out of a haunting storybook. Leave the ordinary behind and embrace the macabre this Halloween with these four delightfully disturbing pizza recipes that will take your spooky suppers to the next level. From creepy crawly creatures to flesh and blood concoctions, these sinister pizzas are sure to entertain even the bravest of guests. Let’s dig into the layers and create the perfect fiendish feast!

1. Spider Bite Pizza

Ah, the arachnid! A creepy crawler that leaves many trembling in fear. Why not take that fear to the next level by incorporating these 8-legged wonders into your pizza? With ghost pepper infused sauce and a slimy green cheese spread, this pizza is sure to raise some hairs on your guests’ necks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza crust
  • 1/3 cup ghost pepper hot sauce
  • 1 cup creamy cheese spread (such as mylk or cream cheese)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup cooked and peeled medium-sized shrimp, sliced into small pieces
  • 8 black olives, sliced into pieces
  • 8 small cooked sliders (hot dog-sized)
  • 1 large garlic clove, minced

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to 425°F (218°C) and lightly greasing a baking sheet with olive oil.

  2. Spread the ghost pepper hot sauce evenly over the pre-made pizza crust, making sure to cover the entire surface.

  3. In a bowl, combine the creamy cheese spread and minced garlic, then spread this mixture over the top of the hot sauce.

  4. Layer on enough shredded mozzarella to cover the cheese mixture generously, and then place the shrimp, black olive pieces, and slider pieces strategically across the pizza to create a fearsome spider web structure.

  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred around the edges.

  6. Remove from the oven, let it cool slightly, and slice into spider web-shaped wedges. Serve this spooky fright with a side of nerve-wracking ranch dressing that adds an additional element of horror to each bite.

2. Graveyard Pizza

What better way to celebrate the spooky season than with an eerie graveyard scene on a pizza that is destined to be their final resting place? This pizza combines the classic margherita recipe with the spooky cemetery theme that will surely creep visitors out in the best of ways.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza crust
  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • 8 fresh basil leaves
  • Cherry tomatoes, chopped
  • Olive oil, for drizzling
  • Black sesame seeds, for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and prepare a lightly greased baking sheet.

  2. Spread the marinara sauce evenly over the pre-made pizza crust, leaving a small border around the edge for the crust.

  3. Sprinkle a layer of shredded mozzarella followed by shredded parmesan cheese over the sauce.

  4. Arrange the chopped cherry tomatoes over the cheese to form tombstones and the basil leaves, and then drizzle a little olive oil over the entire pizza for added moisture.

  5. Sprinkle black sesame seeds around the tomato pieces, further enhancing the eerie graveyard visuals. Don’t forget to leave some space open for the spider legs running into the graveyard.

  6. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese has browned and the crust turns golden.

  7. Serve and let your guests feast on this “tombstone” of a pizza, eliciting unexpected gasps and glares of discomfort.

3. Bloody Biter Bloody Mary Pizza

One classic Halloween cocktail with a gory twist is the Bloody Mary. With vodka-infused marinara sauce, sliced cherry tomatoes “bled” onto it, and crispy bacon strips swaying to and fro like seaweed, this pizza dish is sure to pack a punch, both in flavor and horror, making this Bloody Biter a true fright night treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza crust
  • 1/2 cup vodka-infused marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup prepared horseradish
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely diced red onion
  • 8-10 strips of crispy bacon, chopped
  • 10 cherry tomatoes, sliced in half

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and prepare a lightly greased baking sheet.

  2. Mix the vodka-infused marinara sauce and prepared horseradish thoroughly in a bowl and then spread the mixture evenly over the pre-made pizza crust.

  3. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the vodka sauce layer, followed by a generous layer of shredded parmesan.

  4. Scatter the finely diced red onion over the top, along with the chopped crispy bacon, creating a sea-like appearance.

  5. Arrange the halved cherry tomatoes strategically over the pizza, making sure to leave some empty spaces for drips as if “bleeding.”

  6. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred.

  7. Serve with a frosty mug and garnish each slice with a celery stick and pickle spear to complete this horrifying—or rather, enthralling—experience.

4. Mummy’s Munchies Meatball Pizza

Mummies have stolen many a heart with their gentle demeanor, and why not treat them to a slice of love on your spooky suppers menu? Wrapped in an enigmatic veil of mozzarella and adorned with a shocking number of meatballs and marinara sauce, this pizza serves as both a feast and a fitting tribute to the forever-somber specters from ancient Egypt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza crust
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • 1 dozen meatballs, cooked and sliced in half
  • Olive oil, for brushing crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C) and prepare a lightly greased baking sheet.

  2. Brush the pre-made pizza crust lightly with olive oil, then spread a layer of marinara sauce over the crust, leaving a little border around the edges for the crust.

  3. Sprinkle a nearly even layer of shredded mozzarella and parmesan cheeses over the sauce.

  4. Arrange the sliced meatballs all over the pizza to resemble a mummy’s wrap, strategically draping each piece to form an intricate pattern.

  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese bubbles and browns beautifully.

  6. Serve this creepy yet comforting pizza with a side of juicy surprise—marinated pickled peppers that usually guests haven’t tried before and are sure to add a ghastly twist to their meal.

Admit it – the eerie aura of these horrifyingly delicious pizzas is too tempting to resist. With these four macabre pizza recipes, you’ll be able to create a spooky feast that’ll leave your Halloween guests riddled with both fear and delight. Now go ahead, gather your ingredients, and start creating a frightfully scrumptious experience for all!
