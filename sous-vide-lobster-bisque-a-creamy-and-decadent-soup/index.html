Sous Vide Lobster Bisque: A Creamy and Decadent Soup


Are you ready to elevate your soup game to a whole new level? Look no further than this luxurious Sous Vide Lobster Bisque. This creamy and decadent soup is a true showstopper, perfect for special occasions or when you simply want to treat yourself to something extraordinary. The sous vide cooking method ensures that the flavors are locked in, resulting in a velvety smooth bisque that will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole lobsters
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Lobsters

Begin by preparing the lobsters for sous vide cooking. Using a sharp knife, carefully split the lobsters in half lengthwise. Remove the tomalley (green substance) and roe (if any) from the lobsters and set aside.

Step 2: Sous Vide Cooking

Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C). Place the lobster halves, tomalley, and roe into a sous vide bag. Add a tablespoon of butter, seal the bag, and place it in the water bath. Cook for 1 hour to ensure the lobster meat is perfectly tender.

Step 3: Prepare the Base

While the lobsters are sous viding, it’s time to prepare the base for the bisque. In a large pot, melt the remaining butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant.

Step 4: Deglaze and Simmer

Stir in the tomato paste and cook for a minute to caramelize. Deglaze the pot with white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Add the seafood stock, thyme, and bay leaves. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Blend and Strain

Once the lobster is done sous viding and the base is ready, carefully remove the lobster halves from the sous vide bags. Strain the base mixture, discarding the solids, and transfer the liquid to a blender. Add the cooked lobster tomalley and roe to the blender and blend until smooth.

Step 6: Finish the Bisque

Return the blended mixture to the pot, add the heavy cream, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer the bisque over low heat for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to come together.

Step 7: Serve

Ladle the luxurious Sous Vide Lobster Bisque into bowls, garnish with fresh chives, and serve with crusty bread or oyster crackers. Prepare to be amazed by the richness and depth of flavor in every spoonful.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Lobster Bisque is the epitome of indulgence. The sous vide cooking method ensures that the lobster is perfectly cooked, while the creamy base and aromatic flavors create a symphony for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to savor something extraordinary, this recipe is sure to impress even the most discerning palates. Treat yourself and your loved ones to this exquisite culinary delight and savor the luxurious flavors of Sous Vide Lobster Bisque.
