How to Make Spanish Rice Pudding: A Creamy Dessert Recipe


Rice pudding is a classic dessert cherished in many cultures, but the Spanish version brings a unique twist that’s both comforting and indulgently creamy. Known for its smooth texture and fragrant cinnamon-infused aroma, Spanish rice pudding, or Arroz con Leche, is a dessert you’ll want to make again and again.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a kitchen newbie, this step-by-step guide will walk you through making authentic Spanish rice pudding that’s rich in flavor yet simple enough for a weeknight treat. Let’s dive into this creamy delight!

What Makes Spanish Rice Pudding Special?

Unlike some versions of rice pudding that are thick and heavy, Spanish rice pudding maintains a perfect balance of creaminess and gentle sweetness. The key ingredients include short-grain rice, whole milk, sugar, and cinnamon. Lemon zest often adds a subtle zing, while condensed milk or cream enhances the texture without overwhelming the dish.

The result is a luscious dessert that is both soothing and satisfying—a perfect comfort food that pairs well with a hot cup of coffee or tea.

Ingredients for Creamy Spanish Rice Pudding

To make traditional Spanish rice pudding, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup short-grain rice (such as Arborio or bomba) – short-grain rice has a starchy quality that creates the pudding’s creamy consistency.
  • 4 cups whole milk – for richness. You can partially substitute with cream for extra indulgence.
  • 1 cup granulated sugar – adjust sweetness to taste.
  • 2 cinnamon sticks – these infuse the pudding with warm, aromatic spice.
  • 1 strip of lemon zest – just the yellow part, to avoid bitterness.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract – adds depth and warmth.
  • Pinch of salt – balances flavors perfectly.
  • Ground cinnamon (for garnish)

Optional ingredients:

  • Sweetened condensed milk – to enrich the pudding if you prefer a creamier, sweeter version.
  • Butter – a small knob can make the pudding exceptionally silky.
  • Raisins or chopped nuts – for added texture and sweetness, common in some variations.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Authentic Spanish Rice Pudding

Step 1: Rinse the Rice

Start by rinsing the rice under cold water. This step removes excess surface starch without washing away all of it, which helps later in achieving a silky pudding without it getting soggy.

Step 2: Warm the Milk and Infuse Flavors

In a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan, pour in the milk and add the cinnamon sticks and lemon zest. Slowly bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. Be careful not to boil the milk—just heat until you start to see steam rising and small bubbles form around the edges.

Allow the milk to infuse with the cinnamon and lemon zest for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent scorching.

Step 3: Cook the Rice

Add the rinsed rice to the infused milk. Stir well to evenly distribute the grains.

Reduce the heat to low and let the rice simmer gently. Stir occasionally—this prevents sticking and helps release the starch for a creamy texture.

Simmer uncovered for about 30 to 40 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the mixture thickens to a creamy consistency. Keep an eye on the liquid level; if it gets too low, add a splash more milk to keep the rice cooking evenly.

Step 4: Sweeten the Pudding

Once the rice is cooked to tenderness, stir in the sugar gradually. You’ll notice the pudding becomes creamier and slightly glossy.

Add the vanilla extract at this stage and a pinch of salt to enhance all the flavors.

If you want an even creamier or sweeter pudding, this is the perfect moment to stir in about ½ cup of sweetened condensed milk or a small knob of butter. Both additions enrich the texture and create a melt-in-your-mouth experience.

Step 5: Final Touches and Cooling

Remove the cinnamon sticks and lemon zest strip.

Let the pudding simmer for another 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until it reaches your desired creaminess. If it’s too thick, add a splash of milk and mix well.

Turn off the heat and let it cool slightly. Spanish rice pudding is delicious enjoyed warm or chilled.

Once cooled, cover the pudding and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving, especially if you prefer it cold.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Serve the pudding in small bowls or cups. Sprinkle a pinch of ground cinnamon on top for that classic Spanish finish.

Optionally, garnish with raisins, chopped toasted almonds, or a drizzle of honey for extra flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Spanish Rice Pudding

  • Use the right rice: Short-grain rice is best because it breaks down just enough to thicken the milk without becoming mushy.
  • Low and slow: The secret to creaminess is cooking the pudding slowly over low heat and stirring often.
  • Don’t rush cooling: Refrigerating the pudding lets the flavors meld beautifully and the texture firm up just right.
  • Experiment with milk: Try part whole milk, part cream or coconut milk for different layers of flavor.
  • Flavor it up: Adding a splash of orange flower water or almond extract can give your pudding an exciting twist.

Variations on the Classic Recipe

Spanish rice pudding adapts well to variations, allowing you to tailor it to your palate or dietary preferences.

  • Vegan version: Substitute almond, coconut, or oat milk for dairy. Use maple syrup or agave instead of sugar.
  • Spiced up: Add cardamom pods or star anise with the cinnamon for a more complex spice blend.
  • Chocolate rice pudding: Stir in a few tablespoons of cocoa powder or a handful of chocolate chips toward the end of cooking.
  • Fruit-studded: Mix in soaked raisins, fresh berries, or diced apples for texture contrast.

Pairing Suggestions

Spanish rice pudding shines on its own but pairs beautifully with light accompaniments:

  • A hot cup of espresso or spiced chai for contrast
  • A dollop of whipped cream or crème fraîche for extra decadence
  • Fresh fruit slices like orange or pear to cut through the richness

Storing Leftovers

Store any leftover Spanish rice pudding in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will keep well for 3-4 days. Reheat gently over low heat with a splash of milk if you prefer it warm.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This Spanish rice pudding recipe is more than just a dessert—it’s a cozy ritual. The aroma of cinnamon and lemon zest simmering in warm milk fills your kitchen with a sense of calm. Its creamy texture provides comfort, and the slight sweetness is perfect for satisfying any sweet tooth without feeling heavy.

With simple ingredients and straightforward steps, making Spanish rice pudding is accessible for all skill levels and adaptable to fit your flavor mood. Once mastered, it’s a dessert that feels like a warm hug on any occasion.

If you’ve never tried Spanish rice pudding before, or if you’re looking for a reliable recipe that delivers authentic flavor and creamy perfection, this guide has got you covered. Gather your ingredients and start cooking—you’ll have a luscious, traditional dessert ready to enjoy in less than an hour.
