Summer Picnic Recipes That Are Both Quick and Delicious


Summer picnics are a great way to enjoy the outdoors, spend quality time with friends and family, and indulge in some delicious food. However, the thought of packing, preparing, and transporting food can sometimes feel like a hassle. The good news is that you don’t have to spend hours in the kitchen to create the perfect picnic spread. With the right recipes, you can prepare a variety of tasty and satisfying dishes that are easy to make, transport, and enjoy in the sunshine.

In this post, we’ll share some quick and delicious summer picnic recipes that are perfect for any outdoor gathering. From refreshing salads and savory sandwiches to sweet desserts and refreshing drinks, there’s something here for everyone. So, grab your picnic basket, pack your favorite blanket, and let’s dive into these flavorful recipes.

1. Crispy Chickpea and Avocado Salad

A refreshing salad is a must-have for any summer picnic. This crispy chickpea and avocado salad is light, healthy, and packed with flavor. It’s perfect for a warm day and can be made in under 20 minutes. The crispy chickpeas add a satisfying crunch, while the creamy avocado brings richness to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chickpeas with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the chickpeas on a baking sheet in a single layer and bake for 25-30 minutes until crispy and golden.
  4. While the chickpeas bake, prepare the vegetables. In a large bowl, combine the avocado, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion.
  5. Once the chickpeas are done, let them cool slightly before adding them to the salad.
  6. Toss everything together and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This salad is not only delicious but also nutritious and will keep you energized throughout your picnic.

2. Grilled Chicken and Pesto Sandwiches

When it comes to sandwiches, simplicity is key. These grilled chicken and pesto sandwiches are a great combination of savory flavors with a fresh twist. The pesto adds a burst of basil goodness, while the grilled chicken provides the perfect amount of protein.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup pesto sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 4 sandwich rolls or baguettes
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Arugula or spinach leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  3. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked and juices run clear.
  4. While the chicken cooks, slice the sandwich rolls and toast them lightly if desired.
  5. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. Then slice it thinly.
  6. Spread a generous amount of pesto sauce on the bottom half of each sandwich roll.
  7. Layer the grilled chicken slices, mozzarella cheese, and a handful of arugula or spinach.
  8. Top with the other half of the sandwich roll and serve immediately.

These sandwiches are quick, easy to assemble, and perfect for picnics because they can be made ahead of time and wrapped for easy transport.

3. Watermelon Feta Salad

A summer picnic isn’t complete without a refreshing fruit salad. This watermelon feta salad combines the sweet juiciness of watermelon with the salty tang of feta cheese, making it a delightful combination of flavors. It’s simple to make and can be prepared in just 15 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cubed watermelon, crumbled feta, and chopped mint leaves.
  2. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and toss gently to combine.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve immediately or chill in the fridge for a few hours before serving.

This salad is light, refreshing, and the perfect way to stay cool on a hot summer day.

4. Caprese Skewers

Caprese skewers are an easy-to-eat, handheld version of the classic Italian Caprese salad. They’re perfect for picnics because they’re bite-sized, portable, and can be made in just minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 package fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic vinegar for drizzling
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Thread one cherry tomato, one fresh mozzarella ball, and one basil leaf onto each skewer or toothpick.
  2. Arrange the skewers on a serving platter and drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  3. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

These skewers are visually appealing and incredibly tasty, making them a great option for both adults and kids at your picnic.

5. Easy Homemade Lemonade

No summer picnic is complete without a refreshing drink. Homemade lemonade is a crowd-pleaser and incredibly easy to make. With just a few ingredients, you can enjoy a tangy, sweet beverage that will keep everyone cool and hydrated.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 4-6 lemons)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Ice
  • Lemon slices and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat 1 cup of water and sugar over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved to make a simple syrup.
  2. In a large pitcher, combine the remaining 3 cups of water, freshly squeezed lemon juice, and the simple syrup.
  3. Stir well and adjust sweetness by adding more sugar if needed.
  4. Fill glasses with ice and pour the lemonade over. Garnish with lemon slices and mint leaves.

This refreshing lemonade is the perfect thirst-quencher for a warm summer picnic.

6. Spinach and Feta Stuffed Pita Pockets

Pita pockets are an excellent choice for picnics since they’re portable, easy to eat, and incredibly versatile. These spinach and feta stuffed pita pockets are light yet satisfying, with the combination of creamy feta and fresh spinach creating a flavorful filling.

Ingredients:

  • 4 whole wheat pita pockets
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, wilted and chopped
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add the spinach to the pan and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the crumbled feta cheese and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cut the pitas in half and stuff each pocket with the spinach and feta mixture.
  5. Serve immediately or wrap them up for easy transport to your picnic.

These stuffed pita pockets are a great vegetarian option that’s packed with nutrients and full of flavor.

7. No-Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Bars

For those with a sweet tooth, these no-bake chocolate peanut butter bars are a must-try. They’re quick to prepare, require no baking, and deliver the perfect combination of sweet and salty. They’re the ideal treat to bring along for your picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, powdered sugar, peanut butter, and melted butter. Stir until well combined.
  2. Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch baking dish, forming an even layer.
  3. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until smooth.
  4. Pour the melted chocolate over the peanut butter mixture and spread it evenly with a spatula.
  5. Refrigerate for 2-3 hours or until the bars are set.
  6. Cut into squares and serve.

These bars are rich, decadent, and the perfect ending to your picnic feast.

Conclusion

Summer picnics don’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. With the right recipes, you can create a variety of delicious dishes that are easy to prepare, transport, and enjoy in the great outdoors. Whether you’re craving fresh salads, savory sandwiches, or sweet treats, the recipes above will ensure that your picnic is a hit. So, the next time you’re planning an outdoor gathering, try these quick and delicious picnic recipes to make the most of your time in the sun.
