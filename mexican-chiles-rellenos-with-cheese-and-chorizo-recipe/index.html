Mexican Chiles Rellenos with Cheese and Chorizo Recipe


Welcome to another exciting recipe on our blog! Today, we’re going to take you on a culinary journey to Mexico with a delicious and flavorful dish called Chiles Rellenos. This classic Mexican recipe is sure to impress your taste buds with its combination of cheese and chorizo, creating a perfect balance of spiciness and creaminess. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 1 cup Mexican chorizo, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup Monterey Jack cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Tomato sauce, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the poblano peppers on a baking sheet and roast them for about 15 minutes or until the skin is blistered and charred. Remove them from the oven and let them cool.

  2. Once the peppers have cooled, carefully remove the skin by gently peeling it off. Make a small vertical slit on the side of each pepper and remove the seeds and membranes, creating a pocket for the filling.

  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked chorizo and shredded Monterey Jack cheese. Mix well to ensure the flavors are evenly distributed.

  4. Stuff each pepper with the chorizo and cheese mixture, ensuring that they are evenly filled. Be careful not to overstuff them, as it may cause the peppers to burst during cooking.

  5. In a separate mixing bowl, whisk the egg whites until they form stiff peaks. In another bowl, whisk the egg yolks until they are well combined. Gently fold the egg yolks into the egg whites, being careful not to deflate the mixture. This will create a light and fluffy batter.

  6. In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat.

  7. While the oil is heating, dredge each stuffed pepper in flour, ensuring they are coated on all sides. Dip the floured peppers into the egg batter, making sure they are fully coated.

  8. Carefully place the battered peppers into the hot oil, one at a time, using a slotted spoon or tongs. Fry them until they are golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  9. Once the peppers are cooked, transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  10. Serve the Chiles Rellenos with a side of tomato sauce and garnish with fresh cilantro. The combination of flavors and textures will delight your taste buds.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder version, you can substitute poblano peppers with bell peppers.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese, such as cheddar or queso fresco, to add your own twist to the recipe.
  • For a vegetarian version, omit the chorizo and replace it with sautéed vegetables like mushrooms, onions, and corn.

So there you have it, a delicious and authentic Mexican Chiles Rellenos with Cheese and Chorizo recipe that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico. This dish is perfect for gatherings, special occasions, or simply when you’re craving a flavorful and satisfying meal. Enjoy the fusion of spices, cheese, and chorizo in every bite!
