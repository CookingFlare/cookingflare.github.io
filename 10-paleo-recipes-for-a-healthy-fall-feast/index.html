10 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy Fall Feast


As the leaves transform into vibrant hues of orange, red, and yellow and the crisp autumn air fills our lungs, there’s no better time to dive into hearty, nourishing meals that remain true to the Paleo lifestyle. Fall is the season of abundant harvest, warm spices, and cozy flavors that bring people together. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or enjoying a quiet evening by the fire, these 10 delicious Paleo recipes will help you create a healthy and satisfying fall feast.

1. Spiced Pumpkin Soup

Nothing says fall quite like pumpkin, and this creamy, dairy-free spiced pumpkin soup is the ultimate comfort dish for chilly evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups pumpkin puree (fresh or organic canned)
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1½ cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste
  • Coconut oil for sautéing

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat coconut oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add pumpkin puree, broth, and coconut milk. Stir well.
  3. Mix in ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 15–20 minutes. Blend with an immersion blender for a smooth texture.
  5. Serve warm, garnished with fresh parsley or a swirl of coconut cream.

2. Maple-Glazed Roasted Brussels Sprouts and Bacon

This sweet and savory side dish will make even the pickiest eaters fall in love with Brussels sprouts.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ lbs Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices sugar-free bacon, chopped
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread evenly on a baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle chopped bacon over the top.
  4. Roast for 20 minutes, then drizzle with maple syrup and toss to coat.
  5. Return to the oven and roast for another 10–15 minutes until golden brown.

3. Herb-Crusted Roast Turkey Breast

No fall feast is complete without a tender and juicy main course, and this herb-crusted roast turkey breast is sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in turkey breast (about 5–6 lbs)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • Sea salt and black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Mix olive oil, rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper into a paste.
  3. Rub the herb mixture generously over the turkey breast.
  4. Place turkey on a roasting rack in a baking dish and roast for 1½ to 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

4. Butternut Squash and Sage Mash

This creamy butternut squash mash infused with sage is a delicious, Paleo-friendly alternative to mashed potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • Sea salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam or boil butternut squash until tender.
  2. Mash the squash with ghee and sage while still hot.
  3. Add salt to taste and blend to your desired consistency. Serve warm.

5. Cranberry-Orange Sauce

Boost your feast with bright, tangy flavors by making this naturally sweetened cranberry-orange sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh cranberries
  • ½ cup orange juice
  • 2 tbsp raw honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp orange zest
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine cranberries, orange juice, honey, orange zest, and cinnamon.
  2. Cook over medium heat until cranberries burst and the mixture thickens, about 10 minutes.
  3. Cool before serving.

6. Rosemary Garlic Sweet Potato Fries

These crispy and savory sweet potato fries make for the perfect fall-inspired snack or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cut into thin wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  2. Toss sweet potato wedges with olive oil, rosemary, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a lined baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Bake for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through for even browning.

7. Apple-Cinnamon Pork Chops

Pork chops paired with apples and cinnamon create the perfect harmony of sweet and savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 2 large apples, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ cup chicken broth
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season pork chops with salt and pepper. Sear in a skillet with ghee for 3–4 minutes per side.
  2. Remove chops and set aside. Add apples, onion, cinnamon, and broth to the skillet.
  3. Cook until apples are tender, about 5 minutes.
  4. Return pork chops to the skillet and simmer for 10 minutes until fully cooked.

8. Carrot and Parsnip Slaw

This vibrant slaw is fresh, crunchy, and packed with fall flavors that complement any main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, shredded
  • 2 medium parsnips, shredded
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together apple cider vinegar, olive oil, honey, mustard, salt, and pepper.
  2. Toss dressing with shredded carrots and parsnips. Chill for 30 minutes before serving.

9. Autumn Harvest Salad with Pomegranate Seeds

This colorful salad features seasonal fruits and vegetables for a refreshing addition to your feast.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds
  • ½ cup roasted pumpkin seeds
  • 1 small apple, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup pecans
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss greens, pomegranate seeds, pumpkin seeds, apple, and pecans in a large bowl.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

10. Spiced Apple Cider

End your fall feast on a warm and festive note with this naturally sweetened spiced apple cider.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups unsweetened apple cider
  • 1 orange, thinly sliced
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1-star anise
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a large pot and bring to a gentle boil.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes. Strain before serving warm.

Your Paleo fall feast doesn’t have to compromise on flavor or variety. These 10 recipes offer a full spectrum of hearty and refreshing dishes to celebrate the season in style. The combination of wholesome, nutrient-dense ingredients with bold autumn flavors ensures that everyone at the table will leave satisfied, inspired, and ready for the coziest time of the year. Enjoy the fruits of the harvest and make this fall one of your healthiest yet!
