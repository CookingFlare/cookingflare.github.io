How to Make Cookie Dough Ice Cream: A Treat for All Ages


Cookie dough ice cream is a delightful combination of creamy vanilla ice cream and delicious morsels of cookie dough. This treat is loved by both children and adults, making it a perfect dessert for any occasion. In this blog post, we will explore how to make cookie dough ice cream at home, covering everything from the ingredients needed to the step-by-step process. Whether you’re planning a summer party, a cozy movie night, or just want to satisfy a sweet tooth, this recipe is sure to impress!

The Magic of Cookie Dough Ice Cream

Nothing beats the indulgent experience of enjoying rich, creamy ice cream combined with the soft, chewy texture of cookie dough. This recipe not only satisfies your ice cream cravings but also brings back fond memories of childhood treats. The best part? You can customize the cookie dough and ice cream flavors to create a unique dessert that reflects your tastes.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make cookie dough ice cream, gather the following ingredients:

For the Cookie Dough:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips (or your favorite mix-ins)

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Equipment Needed

Before you start, make sure you have the following equipment on hand:

  • Mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer or whisk
  • Rubber spatula
  • Ice cream maker (optional but recommended)
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Cookie Dough Ice Cream

Step 1: Prepare the Cookie Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt. This will ensure that the flour is evenly distributed and doesn’t clump together.

  2. Cream the Butter and Sugars: In a larger mixing bowl, beat the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together using an electric mixer or a whisk until the mixture is light and fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Add Vanilla: Mix in the vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter and sugar mixture. Mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to a tougher cookie dough.

  5. Fold in the Chocolate Chips: Gently fold in the mini chocolate chips (or your chosen mix-ins) until evenly distributed.

  6. Chill the Dough: Place the cookie dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Use a spoon to scoop out small portions of the dough, approximately 1 inch in size. Freeze the cookie dough balls for at least 30 minutes. This will help them hold their shape when mixed into the ice cream.

Step 2: Make the Ice Cream Base

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved.

  2. Chill the Mixture: For the best results, chill the ice cream mixture in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until it is thoroughly cold. This step is crucial for creating a smooth and creamy texture.

Step 3: Churn the Ice Cream

  1. Prepare the Ice Cream Maker: If you’re using an ice cream maker, ensure that it is properly assembled and prepped according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  2. Churn the Mixture: Pour the chilled ice cream base into the ice cream maker. Churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually for about 20-25 minutes, until the mixture reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  3. Add Cookie Dough: Once the ice cream is churned, gently fold in the chilled cookie dough balls. Be careful not to break them apart too much.

Step 4: Freeze the Ice Cream

  1. Transfer to a Container: Using a rubber spatula, transfer the cookie dough ice cream into an airtight container.

  2. Freeze: Smooth the top with the spatula and cover the container with a lid. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours or until it is firm enough to scoop.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

Once the ice cream has hardened, it’s time to indulge! Here’s how to serve your delicious homemade cookie dough ice cream:

  1. Scoop and Serve: Use an ice cream scoop to serve the cookie dough ice cream into bowls or cones.

  2. Add Toppings: Get creative with toppings! Consider adding whipped cream, extra chocolate chips, or even a drizzle of chocolate or caramel sauce for an extra indulgent treat.

  3. Enjoy: Sit back, relax, and enjoy this delightful dessert with family and friends!

Tips for the Perfect Cookie Dough Ice Cream

  • Use Heat-Treated Flour: To ensure that the cookie dough is safe to eat raw, consider heat-treating the flour. Simply spread the flour on a baking sheet and bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 5-10 minutes. Allow it to cool before using it in the cookie dough.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to get creative! You can add different flavorings to the ice cream base, such as mint extract or coffee. You can also customize the cookie dough by incorporating nuts, dried fruits, or different types of chocolate.

  • Storage Tips: To keep your ice cream fresh, store it in an airtight container in the freezer. It’s best enjoyed within a few weeks for optimal flavor and texture.

Conclusion

Making cookie dough ice cream at home is not only a fun and rewarding experience, but it also allows you to enjoy a classic treat in the comfort of your own kitchen. With just a few simple ingredients and steps, you can create a creamy, delicious ice cream that everyone will love. So gather your friends and family, whip up a batch, and savor the sweet joy of homemade cookie dough ice cream. Enjoy this treat all year round, and don’t forget to share your creations with those you love!
