Instant Pot Recipes for College Students: Budget-Friendly and Simple


For many college students, managing a balanced diet while sticking to a tight budget can be a challenge. Between classes, late-night study sessions, and social activities, cooking can often feel like a hassle. But with the help of an Instant Pot, preparing delicious, nutritious meals becomes quick, easy, and affordable.

In this blog post, we’ll dive into some of the best Instant Pot recipes tailored for college students. These recipes are budget-friendly, simple to prepare, and designed to fit into the busy lifestyle of a college student. Whether you’re new to cooking or just looking for some fresh meal ideas, you’ll find a range of options that will save you time and money while satisfying your taste buds.

Why the Instant Pot is a Game-Changer for College Students

If you’re new to the Instant Pot, it’s a versatile kitchen appliance that can do the work of a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, steamer, sauté pan, and even a yogurt maker—all in one. It can help you cook a variety of dishes in a fraction of the time compared to traditional methods. Not only does this save you valuable time, but it also means you can cook meals in bulk, perfect for prepping ahead and storing leftovers for later.

What makes the Instant Pot especially attractive to college students is its efficiency. College life is demanding, and many students often feel they don’t have time to cook. But with an Instant Pot, you can prepare meals in a fraction of the time it would take using conventional cooking methods. It’s also an energy-efficient appliance, which helps you save on utilities, making it perfect for students on a tight budget.

1. Classic Instant Pot Chili

Chili is a go-to comfort food that’s easy to make, full of flavor, and packed with protein and fiber. This Instant Pot chili recipe is perfect for students because it’s budget-friendly and can feed you for several meals. The beauty of chili is that you can customize it based on what you have in your pantry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or ground turkey for a leaner option)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 can kidney beans (15 oz), drained and rinsed
  • 1 can black beans (15 oz), drained and rinsed
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, chopped green onions

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté setting and cook the ground beef until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, beans, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  4. Pour in the broth, making sure to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  5. Seal the lid, set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 10 minutes.
  6. Once the cooking time is up, carefully release the pressure using the quick-release valve.
  7. Stir the chili and adjust seasoning to taste. Serve with your favorite toppings.

This hearty meal can be enjoyed as a standalone dish or served over rice for an added layer of comfort. Plus, it’s even better the next day, making it perfect for leftovers.

2. Instant Pot Pasta with Marinara Sauce

Pasta is a college student staple for good reason: it’s inexpensive, filling, and easy to prepare. This Instant Pot recipe cuts down the cooking time, and you don’t even need to boil the pasta separately.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta (penne or rotini work well)
  • 1 jar marinara sauce (24 oz)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté setting and add olive oil. Once hot, add the garlic powder and oregano. Sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the marinara sauce, diced tomatoes, and water (or broth). Stir well.
  3. Add the uncooked pasta and stir to ensure the pasta is submerged in the sauce.
  4. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 4 minutes.
  5. When the cooking time is up, release the pressure quickly using the quick-release valve.
  6. Stir the pasta, adding salt and pepper to taste. Serve with grated Parmesan cheese on top.

This simple pasta recipe is an excellent option for busy weeknights and can be made in bulk, so you’ll have plenty for leftovers. You can also customize it by adding vegetables, ground meat, or cheese to suit your tastes.

3. Instant Pot Rice and Beans

Rice and beans are a nutritious and inexpensive combination that provides a good amount of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients. This Instant Pot recipe is both satisfying and super easy to make, making it ideal for college students.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 can black beans (15 oz), drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 ½ cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Add the rice, black beans, diced tomatoes, onion, cumin, chili powder, and water (or broth) to the Instant Pot. Stir to combine.
  2. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 10 minutes.
  3. Once the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to naturally release for 5 minutes, then use the quick-release valve to release the remaining pressure.
  4. Stir the rice and beans, then season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Rice and beans make an excellent side dish or a simple, hearty main course. You can also add extra toppings such as avocado, sour cream, or shredded cheese for an elevated meal.

4. Instant Pot Sweet Potato and Black Bean Stew

This Instant Pot stew is not only packed with flavor but also incredibly nutritious. It’s a great vegetarian meal that’s both filling and budget-friendly. The sweet potatoes add a natural sweetness, and the black beans provide a hearty protein punch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans (15 oz), drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté setting and add a little oil. Once heated, sauté the onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the sweet potatoes, black beans, diced tomatoes, cumin, paprika, chili powder, and vegetable broth. Stir well.
  3. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 8 minutes.
  4. After the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes, then release the remaining pressure using the quick-release valve.
  5. Stir the stew, and add salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.

This stew is perfect for meal prep and can be enjoyed as a comforting meal throughout the week.

5. Instant Pot Chicken and Rice

This simple one-pot meal is full of flavor and easy to make, even for beginners. Chicken and rice is a comforting classic, and the Instant Pot makes it quick and effortless.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts (or thighs for more flavor)
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to the sauté setting and heat the olive oil. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, thyme, and paprika.
  2. Sear the chicken on both sides until browned, about 3 minutes per side. Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  3. Add the onion and garlic to the Instant Pot, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the rice, chicken broth, and place the chicken breasts back on top of the rice.
  5. Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 8 minutes.
  6. After the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to naturally release for 5 minutes, then release the remaining pressure using the quick-release valve.
  7. Shred the chicken and stir it back into the rice. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

This dish is a staple in many homes, and with the Instant Pot, it’s easy enough to whip up after a long day of classes.

Conclusion

As a college student, managing your time and budget can be a difficult balancing act. Fortunately, the Instant Pot is here to help. Whether you’re cooking a comforting bowl of chili, a flavorful stew, or a hearty chicken and rice dish, the Instant Pot takes the stress out of cooking and lets you focus on your studies.

These budget-friendly and simple recipes not only help you save time but also ensure that you’re eating nutritious meals throughout the week. With just a few ingredients, you can prepare wholesome meals that’ll last you for several days—perfect for those busy college schedules. Give these recipes a try, and you’ll quickly see how easy and enjoyable cooking can be!
