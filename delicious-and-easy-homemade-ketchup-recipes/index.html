Delicious and Easy Homemade Ketchup Recipes


Who doesn’t love a dollop of ketchup on their favorite dishes? This versatile condiment adds a burst of flavor to everything from fries to burgers to meatloaf. While store-bought ketchup is convenient, making your own homemade ketchup can take your culinary experience to a whole new level. Not to mention, you can customize the flavors to suit your taste preferences. In this blog post, we will explore two delicious and easy homemade ketchup recipes that will elevate your meals.

Classic Tomato Ketchup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (28 oz each) of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon celery salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the tomatoes (with their juices) and chopped onion. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 20 minutes until the onions are soft.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the mixture cool slightly. Use an immersion blender to puree the mixture until smooth.
  3. Return the saucepan to low heat and add the apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, salt, black pepper, celery salt, mustard powder, and ground cloves. Stir well to combine.
  4. Simmer the mixture for about 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a thick consistency.
  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the ketchup cool completely. Transfer to a jar or airtight container and refrigerate. Your classic tomato ketchup is ready to enjoy!

Spicy Sriracha Ketchup

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Sriracha sauce
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the diced tomatoes, minced garlic, white vinegar, honey, Sriracha sauce, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into a saucepan and bring it to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens.
  4. Taste the ketchup and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the ketchup cool before transferring it to a container. Refrigerate the spicy Sriracha ketchup until ready to use.

Homemade ketchup is not only delicious but also free from artificial preservatives and additives. These recipes allow you to control the ingredients and create a condiment that suits your taste buds. Experiment with different spices and flavors to make your signature ketchup that will impress your family and friends. So, the next time you reach for that store-bought bottle, consider whipping up a batch of homemade ketchup for a fresh and flavorful twist to your meals.

Enjoy the process of creating these ketchup recipes and savor the satisfaction of knowing exactly what goes into your favorite condiment!
