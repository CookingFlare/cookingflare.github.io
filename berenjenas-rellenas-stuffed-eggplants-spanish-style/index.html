Berenjenas Rellenas: Stuffed Eggplants, Spanish-Style


If you’re looking for a delicious and hearty dish that captures the essence of Spanish cuisine, look no further than Berenjenas Rellenas, or Stuffed Eggplants. This traditional Spanish dish is a savory and satisfying meal that combines the rich flavors of eggplant, ground meat, and aromatic spices. Whether you’re planning a special dinner or simply want to expand your culinary repertoire, Berenjenas Rellenas is a must-try recipe that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To prepare Berenjenas Rellenas, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized eggplants
  • 1 pound ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup grated Manchego cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking
  • 2 cups tomato sauce

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Eggplants

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the eggplants in half lengthwise and carefully scoop out the flesh, leaving about a 1/4-inch border. Reserve the scooped-out flesh and set the eggplant shells aside.

Step 2: Cook the Filling

In a large skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant. Add the ground meat and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Stir in the diced red bell pepper, tomato, and the reserved eggplant flesh. Season the mixture with paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Allow the filling to simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: Assemble the Berenjenas Rellenas

Fill each hollowed-out eggplant shell with the meat and vegetable filling, mounding it generously. Place the filled eggplants in a baking dish and spoon the tomato sauce over the top. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the eggplants are tender.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

Once the Berenjenas Rellenas are cooked, remove them from the oven and sprinkle the grated Manchego cheese over the top. Return the dish to the oven, uncovered, and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Garnish the stuffed eggplants with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Berenjenas Rellenas can be served as a main course with a side of crusty bread or a simple green salad. For a complete Spanish dining experience, pair this dish with a glass of Rioja or Tempranillo wine. The rich flavors of the stuffed eggplants complement the robust and earthy notes of these Spanish red wines.

Final Thoughts

Berenjenas Rellenas, or Stuffed Eggplants, Spanish-Style, is a timeless dish that embodies the warmth and hospitality of Spanish cooking. With its flavorful filling and tender eggplant shells, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a special meal, Berenjenas Rellenas is a delightful and satisfying dish that celebrates the art of Spanish cuisine. Try this recipe and transport your taste buds to the sun-drenched shores of Spain.
