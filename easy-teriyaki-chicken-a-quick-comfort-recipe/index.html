Easy Teriyaki Chicken: A Quick Comfort Recipe


If you’re on the hunt for a quick and delicious recipe that brings the comforting flavors of Asian cuisine to your dinner table, look no further than Easy Teriyaki Chicken. This dish is not only a crowd-pleaser but also a breeze to prepare, making it the perfect solution for busy weeknights or when you simply crave something flavorful yet uncomplicated.

What is Teriyaki Chicken?

Teriyaki chicken is a popular Japanese dish that consists of grilled or pan-fried chicken glazed in a sweet and savory sauce made from soy sauce, sake, mirin, and sugar. The term “teriyaki” refers to the cooking method, which involves grilling or broiling while basting the meat with the sauce. The result is a glossy, caramelized exterior with juicy, tender chicken inside. This dish is often served with steamed rice and vegetables, making it a well-rounded meal that’s sure to satisfy.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  1. Quick and Easy: With just a handful of ingredients and minimal prep time, this recipe can be on your table in under 30 minutes.

  2. Versatile: You can easily customize this dish by adding your favorite vegetables or adjusting the sauce’s sweetness to suit your palate.

  3. Healthy Option: Chicken is a great source of lean protein, and when paired with veggies, it creates a nutritious meal that doesn’t skimp on flavor.

  4. Family-Friendly: The sweet and savory flavor profile of teriyaki sauce appeals to both kids and adults, making it an excellent choice for family dinners.

  5. Perfect for Meal Prep: This dish can be made in bulk, stored in the fridge, and reheated for quick lunches or dinners throughout the week.

Ingredients Needed

To whip up this delicious Easy Teriyaki Chicken, gather the following ingredients:

  • Chicken: 1 pound of boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs.
  • Soy Sauce: 1/4 cup of low-sodium soy sauce for a balanced flavor without too much salt.
  • Brown Sugar: 2 tablespoons of packed brown sugar to add sweetness.
  • Mirin: 2 tablespoons of mirin (Japanese rice wine) for depth of flavor.
  • Garlic: 2 cloves of garlic, minced for that aromatic kick.
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger, grated for a hint of warmth.
  • Cornstarch: 1 tablespoon for thickening the sauce.
  • Vegetable Oil: 1 tablespoon for cooking.
  • Sesame Seeds: For garnish (optional).
  • Green Onions: Thinly sliced, for garnish (optional).
  • Steamed Rice: To serve alongside the chicken.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Begin by cutting the chicken into bite-sized pieces. This will not only help the chicken cook faster but will also allow for better absorption of the teriyaki sauce. Season the chicken lightly with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Make the Teriyaki Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, mirin, minced garlic, and grated ginger. This mixture will create the flavorful teriyaki sauce that coats the chicken. For a thicker sauce, mix the cornstarch with a few tablespoons of cold water in a separate bowl until smooth, then add it to the soy sauce mixture.

Step 3: Cook the Chicken

Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken pieces in a single layer. Cook the chicken for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it is golden brown and cooked through.

Step 4: Add the Sauce

Pour the teriyaki sauce over the cooked chicken in the skillet. Stir well to coat all the chicken pieces evenly. Allow the sauce to simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly and clings to the chicken.

Step 5: Serve

Serve the teriyaki chicken over a bed of steamed rice. Drizzle with extra sauce if desired and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions for added flavor and presentation.

Tips for the Best Teriyaki Chicken

  • Marinate the Chicken: For an even deeper flavor, consider marinating the chicken in the teriyaki sauce for 30 minutes to a few hours before cooking. This allows the flavors to penetrate the meat for a more intense taste.

  • Add Vegetables: To make the dish more wholesome, add vegetables such as bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas. Simply sauté them in the skillet before adding the chicken, or stir them in with the sauce.

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic and ginger make a significant difference in flavor compared to dried versions. Always opt for fresh when possible.

  • Adjust the Sweetness: If you prefer a less sweet teriyaki sauce, reduce the amount of brown sugar or add more soy sauce to balance the flavors.

  • Leftovers: Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stove with a splash of water to keep the chicken moist.

Pairing Suggestions

To complement your Easy Teriyaki Chicken, consider pairing it with:

  • Steamed Vegetables: Broccoli, bok choy, or green beans are excellent options that add color and nutrition to your meal.

  • Fried Rice: For a heartier meal, serve your teriyaki chicken alongside some fried rice. This dish can be made with leftover rice, mixed vegetables, and scrambled eggs for an extra boost.

  • Miso Soup: A warm bowl of miso soup can be a delightful starter that pairs wonderfully with the teriyaki flavors.

  • Salad: A light cucumber or seaweed salad can provide a refreshing contrast to the rich flavors of the teriyaki chicken.

Nutritional Information

While enjoying this Easy Teriyaki Chicken, it’s also important to be mindful of its nutritional value. Here’s a rough breakdown of the nutritional information per serving (based on 4 servings):

  • Calories: 300
  • Protein: 30g
  • Carbohydrates: 20g
  • Fat: 10g
  • Fiber: 1g
  • Sugar: 8g

These numbers can vary based on the specific ingredients used and portion sizes, but the dish remains a healthy, balanced meal option.

Conclusion

Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or a gathering of friends, this dish is sure to impress.
