Dim Sum Delights: Must-Try Recipes for Brunch


Dim sum, a Cantonese culinary tradition, is a staple at many Chinese restaurants around the world. These bite-sized dishes are perfect for brunch, offering a variety of flavors and textures that are sure to satisfy any appetite. In this post, we’ll explore some must-try dim sum recipes that are easy to make and delicious to eat.

Har Gow: Steamed Shrimp Dumplings

Har Gow, also known as Steamed Shrimp Dumplings, is a classic dim sum dish that’s both elegant and flavorful. To make Har Gow, you’ll need:

  • 1 package of round wonton wrappers
  • 1/2 cup of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup of chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Combine the shrimp, scallions, garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil in a bowl. Lay a wonton wrapper on a flat surface and place a tablespoon of the shrimp mixture in the center of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges together to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.

Steam the dumplings over boiling water for 8-10 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and tender. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Siu Mai: Steamed Pork and Shrimp Dumplings

Siu Mai, or Steamed Pork and Shrimp Dumplings, is another popular dim sum dish that’s easy to make at home. To make Siu Mai, you’ll need:

  • 1 package of round wonton wrappers
  • 1/2 cup of ground pork
  • 1/4 cup of chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Combine the pork, scallions, garlic, shrimp, soy sauce, and sesame oil in a bowl. Lay a wonton wrapper on a flat surface and place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges together to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.

Steam the dumplings over boiling water for 8-10 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and tender. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Bao: Steamed Buns

Bao, or Steamed Buns, is a staple dim sum dish that’s perfect for sopping up sauces and flavors. To make Bao, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup of warm water
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Combine the flour, yeast, warm water, sugar, and milk in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms, then knead the dough for 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it’s doubled in size.

Punch down the dough and divide it into 6-8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and flatten it slightly into a disk shape. Place a tablespoon of your favorite filling in the center of each disk, then fold the dough over the filling and press the edges together to seal the bun.

Steam the buns over boiling water for 10-12 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and tender. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Cha Siu Bao: Barbecue Pork Buns

Cha Siu Bao, or Barbecue Pork Buns, is a popular dim sum dish that’s both flavorful and aromatic. To make Cha Siu Bao, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup of warm water
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup of barbecue sauce
  • 1/4 cup of chopped scallions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Combine the flour, yeast, warm water, sugar, and milk in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms, then knead the dough for 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it’s doubled in size.

Punch down the dough and divide it into 6-8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and flatten it slightly into a disk shape. Place a tablespoon of barbecue sauce in the center of each disk, then top with a piece of cooked pork and a sprinkle of scallions. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges together to seal the bun.

Steam the buns over boiling water for 10-12 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and tender. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Cong You Bing: Scallion Pancakes

Cong You Bing, or Scallion Pancakes, is a popular dim sum dish that’s both crispy and savory. To make Cong You Bing, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of warm water
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup of chopped scallions
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Sesame oil and soy sauce for serving

Combine the flour, warm water, salt, and sugar in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms, then knead the dough for 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Divide the dough into 6-8 equal pieces and roll each piece into a ball.

Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Flatten each ball of dough into a disk shape and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the pancake is crispy and golden brown. Brush the pancake with sesame oil and sprinkle with chopped scallions. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Turnip Cake: Fried Turnip and Rice Flour Cake

Turnip Cake, or Fried Turnip and Rice Flour Cake, is a popular dim sum dish that’s both savory and sweet. To make Turnip Cake, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of cooked turnip, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of rice flour
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Scallions and soy sauce for serving

Combine the chopped turnip, rice flour, all-purpose flour, salt, and sugar in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms, then knead the dough for 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Divide the dough into 6-8 equal pieces and shape each piece into a ball.

Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Flatten each ball of dough into a disk shape and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the cake is crispy and golden brown. Brush the cake with soy sauce and sesame oil, then sprinkle with chopped scallions. Serve with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and vinegar.

Egg Tarts: Flaky Pastry and Custard Filling

Egg Tarts, or Flaky Pastry and Custard Filling, is a popular dim sum dessert that’s both flaky and creamy. To make Egg Tarts, you’ll need:

  • 1 package of frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter, melted

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Cut out circles of pastry using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass.

In a bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth. Pour the custard mixture into the pastry circles, leaving a 1/2 inch border around the edges.

Fold the edges of the pastry up over the filling, pressing the edges together to seal the tart. Brush the tops of the tarts with the melted butter and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the custard is set. Serve warm with a dusting of powdered sugar.

These dim sum recipes are sure to impress your friends and family with their flavors and textures. Whether you’re looking for a savory or sweet treat, there’s something for everyone in this collection of must-try dim sum recipes.
