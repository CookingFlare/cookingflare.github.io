10 Salad Recipes Inspired by the Flavors of the Middle East


Salads are a delightful embodiment of freshness, flavor, and vibrant colors, making them a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine. Drawing on a rich tapestry of herbs, spices, grains, and vegetables, Middle Eastern salads offer a sensory journey that delights the palate and nourishes the body. Here, we explore ten tantalizing salad recipes that celebrate the essence of Middle Eastern culinary traditions.

1. Tabbouleh Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse bulgur wheat and soak in water for 30 minutes. Drain well.
  2. In a bowl, combine bulgur wheat, parsley, mint, tomatoes, and red onion.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Serve chilled.

2. Fattoush Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chopped romaine lettuce
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup pita bread, toasted and broken into pieces
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp sumac
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine romaine lettuce, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red bell pepper, parsley, mint, green onions, and toasted pita bread.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, sumac, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Let sit for 10 minutes before serving.

3. Mediterranean Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 1/2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/2 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa thoroughly under cold water. In a saucepan, bring water or vegetable broth to a boil.
  2. Add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, Kalamata olives, feta cheese, and parsley.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

4. Balela Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/2 cup chopped cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, black beans, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Let sit for 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld. Serve chilled.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Serve immediately.

10. Moroccan Carrot Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine grated carrots, raisins, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, honey, olive oil, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Chill before serving for best flavor.

These Middle Eastern-inspired salads offer a symphony of flavors that celebrate the region’s culinary heritage. Whether you’re craving the tangy brightness of a Fattoush or the earthy richness of a Moroccan Carrot Salad, these recipes bring a taste of the Middle East to your table. Enjoy them as vibrant sides or satisfying main dishes, each salad showcasing the bounty of fresh ingredients and time-honored techniques that define Middle Eastern cuisine. Embrace the diversity of flavors and textures, and let these salads transport you to the heart of a bustling Middle Eastern market, where every bite tells a story of tradition, culture, and culinary craftsmanship.
