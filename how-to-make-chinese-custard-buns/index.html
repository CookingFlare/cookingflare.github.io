How to Make Chinese Custard Buns


Chinese custard buns, often referred to as “Nai Huang Bao” in Mandarin, are a beloved treat that hails from the rich tradition of Chinese dim sum cuisine. These fluffy, steamed buns filled with creamy, sweet custard offer a delightful combination of textures and flavors that can brighten up any meal table or tea session. Whether you’ve savored them at your favorite dim sum restaurant or are discovering them for the first time, learning how to make these custard buns at home can be a rewarding experience. With this recipe, you’ll master the art of crafting soft, pillowy buns and luscious custard filling from scratch.

Ingredients

Before diving into the recipe, gather all the necessary ingredients to ensure a smooth cooking process. This recipe is divided into two parts: the dough and the custard filling.

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the Custard Filling:

  • 2 large eggs
  • ⅓ cup (70g) granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup (40g) custard powder (preferably Chinese custard powder if available)
  • 1 cup (240ml) full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step 1: Preparing the Custard Filling

The custard filling is at the heart of these delightful buns, lending them their signature creaminess and sweetness.

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, sugar, and custard powder until fully combined and smooth. Avoid any lumps in the mixture.

  2. Cook the Filling: In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the milk, butter, and vanilla extract. Slowly whisk in the egg mixture, stirring continuously to prevent the eggs from curdling. Cook until the mixture thickens, forming a smooth custard.

  3. Cool the Custard: Once the custard has thickened, transfer it to a bowl, cover with cling film (ensuring that the film touches the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming), and let it cool completely.

  4. Chill the Filling: Refrigerating the custard for at least 2 hours will make it easier to work with when forming the buns later. You can prepare the custard filling the night before for added convenience.

Step 2: Making the Dough

The dough for Chinese custard buns is soft, slightly sweet, and perfect for steaming.

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, mix the warm milk with the instant yeast and sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until bubbles form, indicating that the yeast is active.

  2. Combine the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Create a well in the center.

  3. Form the Dough: Gradually pour the milk mixture into the well, followed by the vegetable oil. Stir with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula until the mixture comes together into a shaggy dough.

  4. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour, but avoid overloading, as this can affect the texture of the buns.

  5. Let the Dough Rise: Place the kneaded dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size. The rising time may vary depending on the room temperature.

Step 3: Assembling the Custard Buns

Now comes the most exciting part: assembling the buns!

  1. Divide the Dough: Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Divide the dough into 12 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.

  2. Flatten the Dough Balls: Using a rolling pin or your fingers, flatten each dough ball into a circular disc, about 4 inches in diameter.

  3. Add the Filling: Spoon about 1 tablespoon of chilled custard filling onto the center of each dough disc. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the buns to break during cooking.

  4. Seal the Buns: Carefully wrap the edges of the dough around the filling, pinching them tightly at the top to seal the bun. Seam-side down, place the buns on parchment paper squares to prevent sticking.

Step 4: Steaming the Buns

To achieve that signature light and fluffy texture, steaming is the key.

  1. Prepare the Steamer: Arrange the buns on a steaming tray, leaving enough space between them to expand. If you don’t have a dedicated steamer, you can use a wok or large pot with a steaming rack.

  2. Steam the Buns: Bring the water in the steamer to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and steam the buns for 12-15 minutes. Avoid opening the lid during steaming, as this can impact the buns’ rise.

  3. Cool Slightly: Remove the steamed buns from the steamer and let them cool for a couple of minutes before serving. The custard filling will be piping hot, so this step is essential to prevent burns.

Step 5: Serving Your Chinese Custard Buns

Chinese custard buns are best enjoyed fresh and warm. Their soft exterior contrasts beautifully with the creamy, sweet custard filling, making them an irresistible treat for breakfast, dessert, or a midday snack. Pair them with jasmine tea or oolong tea for an authentic dim sum experience.

For storage, you can keep leftover buns in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply steam them for 3-5 minutes, ensuring they retain their softness and warm custard center.

Tips for Success

Making Chinese custard buns is an art, but with practice, you’ll perfect the method. Here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Flour Matters: Use high-quality all-purpose flour for tender buns. For an even lighter texture, you can try substituting half of the flour with cake flour.
  • Custard Consistency: If your custard feels too thick or thin, adjust by adding a little more milk or custard powder during the cooking process. The filling should be scoopable but firm enough to hold its shape.
  • Steaming Essentials: Make sure your steamer lid is tightly secured during steaming to retain heat and moisture. A damp cloth placed inside the lid can prevent water droplets from dripping onto the buns.

Final Thoughts

Creating Chinese custard buns at home offers a fantastic mix of challenge and reward. Their fluffy texture and indulgent filling will transport you to the bustling dim sum tables of a Cantonese teahouse. Perfect for parties, family gatherings, or simply as a treat-yourself moment, these buns will quickly become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, prep your ingredients, and delight in the magic of handmade goodness. Once you taste the fruits of your labor, there’s no turning back! Happy steaming!
