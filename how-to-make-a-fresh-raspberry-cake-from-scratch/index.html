How to Make a Fresh Raspberry Cake from Scratch


Raspberry cakes are the perfect treat for any occasion. The combination of the tart and sweet raspberry flavor with the soft, fluffy cake creates a delightful balance that will please any dessert lover. If you’re looking to make a cake that’s as fresh as it is flavorful, a homemade raspberry cake from scratch is the way to go. In this guide, we’ll take you through each step of making a delicious raspberry cake that will impress your guests or serve as a special treat for yourself and your family.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To start, gather the following ingredients. Most of these are common baking staples, but the star of the show is, of course, the fresh raspberries.

For the Raspberry Cake:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh raspberries, washed and patted dry
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (for coating the raspberries)

For the Raspberry Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh raspberry puree
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1-2 tbsp milk (optional, for consistency)

For the Raspberry Puree:

  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp sugar (optional, depending on sweetness preference)

With all these ingredients ready, you’re set to make a delightful fresh raspberry cake.

Step 1: Preparing the Raspberry Puree

The first thing you’ll need to do is make the raspberry puree, which will be used in both the cake and the frosting.

  1. Cook the Raspberries: Place the fresh raspberries in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir occasionally to avoid burning. As the raspberries cook, they will break down into a chunky sauce.
  2. Mash and Strain: Use a fork or potato masher to help break down the berries. Once they’ve softened, pass the mixture through a fine sieve to remove the seeds, leaving you with a smooth raspberry puree.
  3. Add Lemon Juice and Sugar: Stir in a tablespoon of lemon juice for a tangy twist, and if you prefer a sweeter puree, add the sugar. Continue to cook for a couple of minutes until the sugar dissolves completely. Let the puree cool before using.

This raspberry puree is not only the base for the frosting but also adds moisture and flavor to the cake itself.

Step 2: Making the Cake Batter

Now, let’s move on to making the cake batter. Follow these steps to create a light and fluffy cake.

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare two 8-inch round cake pans by greasing them with butter and dusting with flour. Alternatively, you can line the pans with parchment paper to ensure the cakes come out easily.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. This ensures that the dry ingredients are well-distributed and free of lumps.

  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a large mixing bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together until the mixture becomes light and fluffy. This step typically takes about 4-5 minutes with an electric mixer. The fluffier your butter and sugar mixture, the lighter your cake will be.

  4. Add the Eggs and Vanilla: Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. Then, stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. Alternate Dry Ingredients and Milk: With the mixer on low, add a third of the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, followed by half of the milk. Continue alternating between the dry ingredients and milk, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix, which can result in a dense cake.

  6. Coat the Raspberries: Toss the fresh raspberries in a tablespoon of flour. This helps keep them suspended in the batter, preventing them from sinking to the bottom of the cake during baking. Gently fold the floured raspberries into the batter with a spatula.

  7. Bake the Cake: Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared cake pans and smooth the tops. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 3: Preparing the Raspberry Frosting

While the cakes are cooling, you can prepare the raspberry frosting. This frosting is light, creamy, and packed with fresh raspberry flavor.

  1. Cream the Butter: Start by beating the softened butter in a mixing bowl until it’s light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes.

  2. Add the Raspberry Puree: Slowly add the cooled raspberry puree to the butter, continuing to beat until the mixture is smooth.

  3. Incorporate the Powdered Sugar: Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time. Beat the frosting on low until the sugar is fully incorporated, then increase the speed to high and beat until light and fluffy.

  4. Adjust Consistency: If the frosting is too thick, add 1-2 tablespoons of milk, a little at a time, until you reach your desired consistency. If it’s too thin, add more powdered sugar to thicken it.

  5. Add Vanilla and Salt: Stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt. The salt helps to balance the sweetness of the frosting.

Step 4: Assembling the Raspberry Cake

Once the cakes are completely cool and the frosting is ready, it’s time to assemble your fresh raspberry cake. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Level the Cake Layers: If the cakes have domed on top, use a serrated knife to level the surfaces. This ensures that your cake layers sit evenly on top of each other and gives it a more professional appearance.

  2. Frost the Cake: Place the first layer of cake onto a serving plate. Spread a generous amount of raspberry frosting over the top, using a spatula to smooth it out evenly. Place the second layer of cake on top, pressing down gently to ensure it sticks.

  3. Frost the Entire Cake: Apply a thin layer of frosting to the sides and top of the cake, creating a crumb coat. Refrigerate the cake for about 15 minutes to allow the crumb coat to set. Afterward, apply a final, thicker layer of frosting, smoothing it out with a spatula or butter knife.

  4. Garnish with Fresh Raspberries: For a beautiful finish, garnish the top of the cake with additional fresh raspberries. You can also add a few mint leaves for a pop of color.

Step 5: Enjoying Your Fresh Raspberry Cake

Now that your fresh raspberry cake is assembled, it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor! This cake is perfect for birthdays, special occasions, or even as a weekend treat. The moist cake, paired with the tart-sweet raspberry frosting, creates a truly irresistible dessert.

Serve your cake with a cup of coffee or tea for an extra indulgence, or pair it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream to make it even more decadent. Your friends and family will be raving about this fresh raspberry cake for days to come!

Tips for Success

  • Fresh vs. Frozen Raspberries: Fresh raspberries are ideal for this recipe, as they provide the best flavor and texture. However, if fresh raspberries are not available, you can use frozen raspberries. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before adding them to the cake batter.

  • Cake Storage: To store your cake, keep it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you want to keep it longer, you can refrigerate it for up to a week.

  • Flavor Variations: While raspberries are the star of this cake, you can also experiment with other berries like blackberries, strawberries, or blueberries. The process for making the cake and frosting remains the same—just swap out the raspberries for your fruit of choice.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the cake layers in advance and freeze them for up to 3 months. Simply wrap the layers tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing. Thaw them at room temperature before frosting.

By following these simple steps, you’ll have a fresh raspberry cake that’s bursting with flavor and perfect for any occasion. From the light, fluffy cake to the creamy raspberry frosting, this dessert is sure to become a family favorite!
