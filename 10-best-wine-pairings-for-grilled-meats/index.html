10 Best Wine Pairings for Grilled Meats


Grilling meats is one of the most cherished culinary experiences, especially during summer months or at any outdoor gathering. There’s something magical about the smoky flavors, charred textures, and sizzling sounds of meat cooking on the grill. But what truly takes a grilled meal to the next level? The right wine pairing.

Wine and grilled meats are a classic combination, and the right wine can enhance the flavors of the meat while complementing the smoky, charred nuances that grilling imparts. In this post, we’ll explore 10 of the best wines to pair with various types of grilled meats, from tender steaks to juicy chicken and even flavorful lamb. Whether you’re hosting a BBQ party, grilling dinner for your family, or just relaxing with a glass of wine, these pairings are sure to impress.

1. Cabernet Sauvignon with Ribeye Steak

Ribeye steak is known for its rich marbling, which gives it a juicy, buttery texture and bold flavors. To complement this hearty cut, a full-bodied red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon is the perfect choice. Cabernet Sauvignon has deep, tannic qualities that pair wonderfully with the fat in the ribeye, cutting through the richness of the meat while bringing out the smoky flavors from the grill.

When selecting a Cabernet Sauvignon, look for one with dark fruit flavors, such as black currant and blackberry, along with a touch of oak, which complements the grill’s smoky notes. A well-aged bottle with soft tannins will enhance the overall experience, making each bite of ribeye steak even more satisfying.

2. Syrah/Shiraz with Grilled Lamb Chops

Grilled lamb chops, with their gamey flavor and rich seasoning, demand a wine with equal intensity. Syrah (known as Shiraz in Australia) is a fantastic pairing for lamb due to its bold flavors and smoky, peppery notes. The tannins in Syrah help balance the richness of lamb, while its dark fruit flavors, such as plum and blackberry, create a harmonious pairing.

Opt for a Syrah from a region with a warm climate, such as the Northern Rhône or Barossa Valley. The warmer temperatures allow the grapes to develop deep, complex flavors that perfectly match the intensity of grilled lamb.

3. Zinfandel with Grilled Pork Ribs

Pork ribs are a staple of BBQ culture, often glazed with a tangy sauce and cooked to perfection over the grill. A fruit-forward wine like Zinfandel complements the sweet and spicy flavors of BBQ sauce while cutting through the richness of the meat. The bold, jammy flavors of Zinfandel, such as raspberry and blackberry, make it a great match for ribs coated in a sugary or smoky glaze.

Zinfandel’s peppery finish also enhances the charred flavors of the grill, while its moderate tannins won’t overwhelm the pork. A bottle from California, particularly from Napa Valley or Sonoma County, will offer the ideal balance of ripe fruit and spice.

4. Pinot Noir with Grilled Salmon

Grilled salmon is a lighter protein compared to red meats, and a wine that won’t overpower its delicate flavors is essential. Pinot Noir, with its lighter body and subtle fruitiness, is an excellent choice for pairing with salmon. The wine’s notes of cherry, strawberry, and earthy undertones complement the rich, slightly smoky flavors of grilled salmon.

The low tannin content in Pinot Noir ensures that it doesn’t compete with the texture of the fish, and its crisp acidity helps cut through the natural oils in salmon, creating a well-rounded pairing. For the best experience, opt for a Pinot Noir from Oregon or Burgundy, where the cooler climate allows the grapes to retain their bright, fresh character.

5. Malbec with Grilled Steak Burgers

When grilling a juicy steak burger, you want a wine that can match the savory, beefy flavor of the patty without overwhelming it. Malbec, known for its deep color and bold flavors, is a fantastic option for steak burgers. With its rich notes of dark fruit, such as blackberries and plums, and its smoky undertones, Malbec is the perfect companion for a grilled burger.

The wine’s moderate tannins and high acidity balance out the fattiness of the meat while enhancing the smoky charred flavors from the grill. Look for a Malbec from Argentina, particularly from Mendoza, where the wine has a wonderful depth and structure that complements grilled meats.

6. Chardonnay with Grilled Chicken

Grilled chicken, whether seasoned simply with salt and pepper or marinated with bold herbs and spices, pairs beautifully with a full-bodied white wine like Chardonnay. The buttery, rich texture of Chardonnay complements the mild flavor of the chicken, especially when the bird is grilled to perfection with a slightly charred exterior.

A Chardonnay with a bit of oak aging adds depth and complexity, creating a harmonious balance with the smoky, caramelized notes from the grill. Look for a California Chardonnay, which often has a fuller body and rich fruit flavors, or a Burgundy Chardonnay for a more subtle, mineral-driven profile.

7. Tempranillo with Grilled Chorizo

Chorizo, with its spicy, flavorful kick, requires a wine that can handle its bold spices without getting lost in the heat. Tempranillo, a Spanish red wine, is a fantastic pairing for grilled chorizo due to its savory, earthy profile and its ability to complement the spices in the sausage. The wine’s notes of dark cherry, plum, and leather add depth to the grilled chorizo, while its moderate tannins help balance the fat and spice.

Tempranillo from Rioja or Ribera del Duero is ideal for grilled chorizo, as these regions produce wines with a balance of fruit and spice that pairs well with smoky, spicy meats.

8. Sangiovese with Grilled Veal Chops

Veal chops, tender and subtly sweet, require a wine that is equally elegant and bright. Sangiovese, the primary grape of Tuscany, is a fantastic choice for veal due to its bright acidity and medium body. The wine’s vibrant notes of red cherry and tomato-based flavors complement the delicate sweetness of veal, while its acidity helps cut through the richness of the meat.

A Chianti Classico or Brunello di Montalcino from Italy’s Tuscany region offers a perfect balance of tannins and acidity, making it the perfect match for grilled veal chops.

9. Grenache with Grilled Vegetables and Sausages

While the focus may often be on pairing wine with meats, grilled vegetables and sausages are a great addition to any BBQ spread. A versatile wine like Grenache (or Garnacha, as it is known in Spain) pairs exceptionally well with grilled vegetables and sausages. Grenache offers bright fruit flavors such as strawberry and raspberry, which complement the sweet and smoky notes from grilled vegetables, while its peppery finish enhances the flavors of sausage.

Grenache from regions like the Southern Rhône (Châteauneuf-du-Pape) or Spain’s Priorat brings the right amount of warmth and structure, making it a great all-around wine for BBQ spreads.

10. Côtes du Rhône with Grilled Beef Kebabs

Grilled beef kebabs, often seasoned with a variety of spices and vegetables, require a wine with both fruit and earthy characteristics. Côtes du Rhône, a blend of Grenache, Syrah, and Mourvèdre, offers the perfect combination of bright fruit, peppery spice, and earthy undertones. This balanced red wine enhances the smoky flavors from the grill while complementing the charred, savory beef.

The complexity of a Côtes du Rhône, particularly one from the Southern Rhône Valley, elevates the overall flavor of the kebabs, making it a standout pairing for your grilled feast.

Conclusion

Wine pairing with grilled meats is an art that combines the flavors of the food with the complexities of wine, resulting in an experience that excites the palate. Whether you’re enjoying a juicy ribeye steak, tender lamb chops, or flavorful grilled chicken, the right wine can elevate your meal and turn an ordinary BBQ into an unforgettable occasion.

From bold reds like Cabernet Sauvignon and Syrah to the elegant whites like Chardonnay and Pinot Noir, each wine offers a unique way to enhance the flavors of grilled meats. The key is to consider the intensity, flavors, and textures of both the meat and the wine to create a harmonious pairing that excites your taste buds.

Next time you fire up the grill, consider these wine pairings to take your BBQ to new heights. Your guests will be impressed, and you’ll be enjoying a perfectly balanced meal that’s truly unforgettable. Cheers!
