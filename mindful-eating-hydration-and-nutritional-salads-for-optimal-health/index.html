Mindful Eating: Hydration and Nutritional Salads for Optimal Health


In today’s fast-paced world, it’s easy to neglect the importance of a balanced diet and mindful eating. In fall 2011 I turned vegan overnight. Before this time, I suffered from no shortage of health issues, from bouts of depression to unexplained nosebleeds to skin conditions. Every time I went to the doctor, my body would express something wasn’t right but I wasn’t really listening.

I want to focus on two key aspects of meal preparation that can help us live better, healthier lives: hydration and nutrition. By infusing our diet with the right balance of hydrating foods and nutrient-rich salad combinations, we can enhance our wellness and overall vitality. Let’s explore some fantastic salad recipes to nourish our bodies and fuel our minds.

Dehydration: A Major Health Concern

Before we dive into the wonderful world of salads, let’s recognize the importance of staying hydrated. We all know water is essential for life, but its value in our day-to-day functioning cannot be overstated. Inadequate hydration can lead to tiredness, blurry vision, and even dehydration. The recommended daily water intake is roughly 2.7 liters (91 ounces) for women and 3.7 liters (125 ounces) for men, according to the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA).

To aid in hydration, we must incorporate foods into our diets that have high water content. These include vegetables such as cucumbers, lettuce, spinach, tomatoes, radishes, and certain fruits like watermelon, oranges, and strawberries.

Superfood Salads for Optimal Health

Nourishing our bodies with nutrient-rich foods not only satisfies our hunger but also nourishes our minds and spirits. Salads are an excellent, versatile dish for everyday consumption. By blending various greens, vegetables, fruits, and protein sources, we create a powerhouse of nutrients made just for you.

Let’s explore some fantastic salad recipes:

1. Summer Sunrise Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cup mixed greens
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 orange, peeled and diced
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds, toasted
  • Poppyseed dressing (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine chopped romaine and mixed greens.
  2. Top the greens with the remaining ingredients, starting with strawberries, blueberries, orange, and avocado. Finish with a sprinkle of toasted slivered almonds.
  3. Toss the salad gently with a poppyseed dressing until well combined.

2. Powerhouse Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, prepared
  • 1 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup avocado, chopped
  • 1/2 cup red kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Olive oil, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked quinoa and remaining ingredients.
  2. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil, then sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Stir gently until well mixed.

3. Teriyaki Tempeh Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed greens
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup roasted red pepper, sliced
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • Teriyaki-glazed tempeh, cubed (see recipe below)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine mixed greens, bean sprouts, shredded carrots, roasted red pepper, and sliced almonds.
  2. Toss the salad gently until well combined.
  3. Top the salad with teriyaki-glazed tempeh cubes before serving.

Teriyaki Tempeh Recipe:

  • 1 block tempeh, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (or tamari for a gluten-free option)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thicker sauce)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine tempeh cubes with olive oil, soy sauce, honey, and minced garlic. Let marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. In a small bowl, mix cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to create a slurry. Add the slurry to the tempeh marinade.
  3. Heat a small pan over medium heat. Place the marinated tempeh cubes in the pan, reserving the marinade. Cook the tempeh for 3-5 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  4. In the same marinade, whisk in the grated ginger and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer for 3-5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  5. Coat the cooked tempeh cubes in the thickened teriyaki sauce and serve.

Enjoy the Benefits of Mindful Eating

By focusing on the importance of hydration and incorporating nutrient-rich salads, we’re setting ourselves up for mindful eating and overall optimal health. Implementing these habits can help us achieve improved digestion, sustained energy, and natural weight management. Remember, small changes in our diet can have a significant impact on our well-being.

So go ahead, mix up your greens, get creative with your ingredients, and salute a healthier, happier you with every delicious bite! Cheers to nourishing and enjoyable meals!
