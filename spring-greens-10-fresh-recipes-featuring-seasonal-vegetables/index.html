Spring Greens: 10 Fresh Recipes Featuring Seasonal Vegetables


With the arrival of spring comes a bounty of vibrant, fresh greens that burst with flavor and nutrition. From tender spinach and peppery arugula to crisp snap peas and sweet ramps, the season’s vegetables invite creativity in the kitchen. These spring greens not only brighten plates but also bring refreshing lightness after the heavier fare of winter.

If you’re eager to celebrate the garden’s first offerings, you’re in luck. Here are ten inventive and irresistibly fresh recipes showcasing spring’s most beloved vegetables. Each dish is crafted to highlight the unique textures and flavors of these seasonal stars, making your meals as nourishing as they are delectable.

1. Spring Pea and Mint Soup

Start your spring greens journey with a velvety pea soup that captures the sweetness of fresh peas and the cooling zing of mint.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh or frozen shelled peas
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat and sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add peas and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in fresh mint.
  4. Blend the soup until smooth using an immersion blender or in batches.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve warm or chilled with a squeeze of lemon.

This soup is a perfect balance of sweet and herbal, ideal for a light lunch or as a starter to a springtime feast.

2. Wild Ramp and Ricotta Flatbread

Few things herald spring like wild ramps, with their garlicky onion flavor. Combine with creamy ricotta and a crispy flatbread base for an elegant yet easy dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ready-made pizza dough or flatbread base
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 bunch ramps, cleaned and roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Roll out pizza dough on a baking sheet or prepare flatbread.
  3. Spread ricotta cheese evenly over the base.
  4. Toss ramps with olive oil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.
  5. Scatter ramps over the ricotta.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes until crust is golden and ramps are tender.
  7. Slice and serve warm.

This flatbread pairs wonderfully with a fresh green salad for a quick spring supper.

3. Roasted Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan

Asparagus spears roasting in the oven transform into a tender, caramelized side that sings with bright lemon and nutty Parmesan.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange asparagus on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and lemon zest.
  4. Roast for 10-12 minutes until tender-crisp.
  5. Remove from oven, squeeze lemon juice over asparagus, and sprinkle Parmesan.
  6. Toss gently and serve immediately.

This dish is an effortless way to bring a burst of spring flavors to your table.

4. Arugula and Strawberry Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Peppery arugula meets juicy, sweet strawberries in this fresh and vibrant salad. The balsamic glaze adds a tangy-sweet finish that elevates every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup toasted pecans or walnuts
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese or feta
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze (store-bought or homemade)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine arugula, strawberries, nuts, and cheese.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and gently toss.
  3. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  4. Drizzle balsamic glaze over the salad just before serving.

This salad highlights the harmony of freshness and texture, making it a versatile starter or side.

5. Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breasts

Elevate your dinner with juicy chicken breasts filled with fresh spinach and tangy feta, a delightful spring take on a classic protein.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix chopped spinach, feta, and garlic.
  3. Cut a pocket in each chicken breast and stuff with the spinach mixture.
  4. Secure with toothpicks and season the outside of the chicken with salt and pepper.
  5. Heat olive oil in an ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Sear chicken breasts for 2-3 minutes per side until golden.
  7. Transfer skillet to oven and bake for 20 minutes or until cooked through.

Serve with a side of roasted spring vegetables for a wholesome meal.

6. Kale and Quinoa Power Bowl with Lemon Tahini Dressing

Kale makes a wonderful hearty base for salads, especially when complemented by protein-rich quinoa and a zesty tahini dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 4 cups kale, deveined and chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa according to package instructions using water or broth.
  2. Massage kale with a pinch of salt until softened.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, and water (to thin as needed).
  4. Assemble bowls with quinoa, kale, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and seeds.
  5. Drizzle dressing over the bowls and toss lightly.

This bowl is a nutrient-packed celebration of spring’s fresh greens and simple flavors.

7. Braised Swiss Chard with Garlic and Shallots

Swiss chard’s tender leaves and crunchy stems make a perfect base for this flavorful braised side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch Swiss chard, stems and leaves separated and chopped
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth or water
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil or butter in a large skillet.
  2. Add shallots and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add chard stems first and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Toss in chard leaves and pour broth or water.
  5. Cover and cook on low heat until leaves are wilted, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Stir in vinegar, season with salt and pepper.

This dish pairs wonderfully with grilled fish or as part of a vegetarian plate.

8. Fiddlehead Fern and Mushroom Stir-Fry

Fiddlehead ferns, a unique spring delicacy, are delightful when paired with savory mushrooms in a quick stir-fry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fiddlehead ferns, cleaned and blanched
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a wok or skillet.
  2. Sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add mushrooms and stir-fry until tender.
  4. Toss in fiddleheads and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Drizzle soy sauce and sesame oil, stirring to coat.
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds.

A perfect springtime side or light entrée with steamed rice.

9. Butter Lettuce Wraps with Spring Vegetable Slaw

Brighten lunch with crunchy slaw made of radishes, snap peas, and carrots wrapped in tender butter lettuce leaves.

Ingredients:

  • 8 butter lettuce leaves, washed and dried
  • 1 cup snap peas, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, shredded
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro or parsley
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine snap peas, radishes, carrot, and herbs.
  2. Toss with lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon slaw into lettuce leaves.
  4. Serve as wraps, optionally with grilled chicken or tofu.

These wraps are crisp, refreshing, and easy to customize based on your favorite spring veggies.

10. Morel Mushroom and Green Garlic Risotto

Celebrate the fleeting season of morel mushrooms with a creamy risotto flavored by fragrant green garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup diced green garlic (or substitute with mild young garlic)
  • 1 cup morel mushrooms, cleaned and sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, warmed
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan, sauté onion until translucent.
  2. Add green garlic and morels, cook until fragrant.
  3. Stir in Arborio rice to coat.
  4. Add white wine and cook until absorbed.
  5. Add broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly, until rice is creamy and tender (about 18 minutes).
  6. Stir in butter and Parmesan.
  7. Season to taste.

This risotto is a rich tribute to spring’s forest and garden bounty.

Wrapping Up Spring’s Freshness

These recipes capture the essence of spring through the season’s freshest greens and vegetables. Whether you’re preparing a light salad, a hearty main, or an elegant side, the vibrant flavors of spring greens add brightness, nutrition, and a sense of renewal to every meal.

Don’t hesitate to experiment by combining these vegetables in ways that excite your palate. As you celebrate the crisp, tender harvest that only spring can offer, you’ll find that cooking with fresh greens is not just about nutrition — it’s a joyful embrace of the season itself. Here’s to savoring the greens of spring in all their fresh glory!
