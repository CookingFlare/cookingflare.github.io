Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork Recipe


Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork (回锅肉) is one of the most beloved dishes in Chinese cuisine, known for its savory, spicy, and aromatic flavors. This dish offers a delightful balance between tender pork belly and a rich, spicy sauce, creating a flavor-packed meal that captures the essence of Sichuan cooking. The name “twice-cooked” refers to the method of cooking the pork in two stages—first by boiling, then by stir-frying with a flavorful sauce.

In this post, we’ll walk you through the steps of creating this iconic dish, from selecting the right cut of pork to the final stir-fry process. Let’s dive into this spicy, savory, and addictive dish.

Ingredients for Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork

The key to a perfect Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork lies in both the ingredients and the technique. Here’s what you’ll need for this recipe:

For the Pork:

  • 500g (1 lb) pork belly (skin-on for added texture)
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese rice wine (Shaoxing wine or dry sherry)
  • 1-2 slices of ginger (optional for added fragrance)
  • 1 spring onion, cut into large pieces (optional)

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste with chili)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (preferably dark soy sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (or a bit more for sweetness)
  • 2 teaspoons of Sichuan peppercorns (optional, for authentic numbing sensation)
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of chili paste or chili flakes (adjust based on your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening the sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil for stir-frying

For Garnish:

  • A handful of fresh cilantro (optional)
  • 1 spring onion, chopped (for garnish)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Boiling the Pork Belly

The first stage of this recipe involves boiling the pork belly to tenderize the meat and infuse it with subtle flavors. Here’s how to prepare the pork:

  1. Prepare the Pork: Cut the pork belly into thick slices of about 2 inches wide. This helps the pork hold its shape during the cooking process, and the fat will render out, giving the dish a juicy and tender texture.

  2. Blanch the Pork: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the pork belly slices, ginger slices, and a spring onion to the water. Let the water come back to a boil, and then simmer for about 30 minutes. This ensures that the pork belly becomes tender and develops a subtle, savory flavor. If you prefer, you can also add a splash of Chinese rice wine to enhance the aroma.

  3. Cool the Pork: After the pork belly has boiled, remove it from the pot and let it cool. Once cooled, slice the pork belly thinly across the grain into bite-sized pieces. It’s important to slice against the grain for a tender bite.

Step 2: Stir-Frying the Pork

Once the pork belly has been boiled and sliced, the next step is to stir-fry the meat with a flavorful combination of sauces, spices, and aromatics. This is where the “twice-cooked” part of the recipe comes into play. Here’s how to achieve the perfect stir-fried pork:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a wok or large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the Sichuan peppercorns. They’ll release their numbing fragrance and flavor, creating the characteristic tingly sensation that Sichuan cuisine is famous for.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: Add the minced garlic to the wok and stir-fry for about 20 seconds, or until fragrant. You want to release the savory aroma of the garlic without burning it.

  3. Add the Doubanjiang: Add the Sichuan Doubanjiang (fermented bean paste with chili) to the wok. This is the base of the sauce and contributes a rich, umami-packed flavor along with the perfect amount of heat. Stir-fry the paste for another 30 seconds, allowing the oils and flavors to blend together.

  4. Add the Pork Belly: Next, add the sliced pork belly to the wok. Stir-fry the pork for 3-5 minutes until the pork begins to caramelize and crisp up slightly, with the fat rendering out. The pork will absorb the spicy and savory flavors from the sauce, making it even more delicious.

  5. Incorporate the Sauce Ingredients: Add the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, chili paste (or chili flakes), and oyster sauce. Stir to coat the pork in this rich sauce mixture. Allow the flavors to meld together as the sauce thickens and clings to the pork slices.

  6. Thicken the Sauce: Mix the cornstarch with water in a small bowl to form a slurry. Add this mixture to the wok and stir well. The sauce will begin to thicken, creating a glossy, rich coating on the pork. Stir for an additional 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined and the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency.

Step 3: Final Touches

Once the pork is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened, it’s time to finish the dish and prepare it for serving.

  1. Garnish the Dish: Transfer the Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork to a serving platter. Garnish with chopped spring onions and fresh cilantro, if desired, for a burst of freshness and color.

  2. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the dish immediately with steamed rice or stir-fried vegetables. The rich, savory, and spicy flavors of the Twice-Cooked Pork pair wonderfully with a simple bowl of rice to balance out the heat.

Tips for the Perfect Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork

  • Pork Belly Selection: Choose a good-quality pork belly with a good balance of fat and meat. The fat helps to keep the pork moist and tender during the stir-frying process. If you prefer a leaner version, you can substitute the pork belly with pork shoulder, but the texture won’t be quite the same.

  • Adjusting Spice Levels: Sichuan cuisine is known for its bold and spicy flavors, but you can adjust the level of heat according to your preference. If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of chili paste or Sichuan peppercorns. For a spicier kick, add more chili paste and increase the peppercorns.

  • Doubanjiang: The quality of the Doubanjiang is crucial to the flavor of this dish. Opt for a high-quality brand from a reputable store, preferably one with a good balance of spice and umami.

  • Control the Sauce Consistency: The cornstarch slurry is key to achieving the perfect glossy sauce. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add a small amount of water or chicken broth to thin it out. Conversely, if the sauce is too thin, let it simmer for a few more minutes to reduce.

Pairing Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork

Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork is a flavorful, bold dish that pairs best with simple sides to balance out the intensity of the flavors. Here are some great options:

  • Steamed Rice: A bowl of steamed jasmine rice is the perfect companion to this spicy dish. The rice will help absorb the rich sauce and balance the heat.

  • Stir-Fried Vegetables: Pairing this pork dish with stir-fried vegetables such as bok choy, spinach, or green beans helps to add freshness and texture to the meal.

  • Chinese Soup: A light Chinese soup, like hot and sour soup or egg drop soup, can be a great starter to complement the richness of the Twice-Cooked Pork.

Conclusion

Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork is a wonderfully flavorful dish that’s perfect for anyone who enjoys bold, spicy food with a balance of savory, sweet, and umami flavors. It’s a great introduction to Sichuan cuisine and can be made at home with a few simple ingredients. Whether you’re cooking for family or hosting friends, this dish is sure to impress.

By following the steps outlined in this post, you can recreate the authentic flavors of Sichuan in your own kitchen. The twice-cooking method ensures that the pork is tender and flavorful, while the stir-fry technique adds a wonderful depth of flavor with each bite. Enjoy this classic Sichuan recipe with a bowl of rice, and savor the combination of spicy, savory, and fragrant flavors that make this dish a true standout in Chinese cuisine.
