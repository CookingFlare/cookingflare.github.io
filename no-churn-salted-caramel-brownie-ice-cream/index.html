No-Churn Salted Caramel Brownie Ice Cream


If you’re craving a dessert that perfectly blends rich chocolate, gooey caramel, and creamy ice cream without the hassle of an ice cream maker, this no-churn salted caramel brownie ice cream recipe is your new best friend. This indulgent delight combines fudgy brownies, salty-sweet caramel, and smooth vanilla ice cream in a fun, easy-to-make treat that’s perfect for any occasion.

Why No-Churn Ice Cream?

Traditional ice cream recipes often require an ice cream maker, which can be intimidating or inaccessible for many home cooks. No-churn ice cream recipes eliminate that barrier, allowing you to create luscious, homemade ice cream using just a few ingredients and your freezer. The result is just as creamy and delicious as churned ice cream but with much less effort.

This salted caramel brownie ice cream is especially rewarding because it layers textures and flavors: chewy brownie bites, luscious salty caramel swirls, and velvety ice cream. Let’s dive into the recipe, tips, and creative twists that’ll help you master this showstopper dessert.

Ingredients Breakdown

Here is what you’ll need to make the perfect no-churn salted caramel brownie ice cream:

For the Brownies:

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup (35g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups (480ml) heavy cream, chilled
  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt (adjust to taste)

For the Salted Caramel Sauce:

  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons (85g) unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) heavy cream, warmed
  • 1 teaspoon flaky sea salt

Step-by-Step Guide to No-Churn Salted Caramel Brownie Ice Cream

1. Make the Brownies

The brownies are the backbone of this ice cream, providing chewy chunks to contrast the smooth ice cream. Here’s how to get the perfect brownie:

  • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8x8 inch baking pan.
  • Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over low heat. Remove from heat and stir in sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  • Beat in cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder until well combined.
  • Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 20-25 minutes.
  • Cool completely on a wire rack, then cut into small bite-sized pieces. Set aside.

2. Prepare the Salted Caramel Sauce

A good salted caramel sauce takes the ice cream to the next level by cutting through the richness with a complex, buttery flavor:

  • In a medium heavy-bottom saucepan, heat sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly with a heat-resistant spatula or wooden spoon as the sugar melts into a thick, amber-colored liquid. Be careful not to burn it.
  • Once melted, add the butter carefully (it will bubble aggressively). Stir until melted and combined.
  • Slowly pour in the warm heavy cream while stirring continuously. Let the mixture boil for 1 minute.
  • Remove from heat and stir in flaky sea salt.
  • Allow the caramel to cool to room temperature but still be pourable.

3. Whip the Cream

Whipping the cream to the right consistency is vital for creamy, scoopable ice cream:

  • Using an electric mixer, whip the chilled heavy cream on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form.
  • Be careful not to overwhip; you want the cream light and airy, but not grainy.

4. Combine Ingredients

  • In a large mixing bowl, gently fold the sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, and sea salt into the whipped cream. Mix until fully combined but still fluffy.
  • Fold in the brownie chunks, distributing them evenly.
  • Swirl about half of the caramel sauce through the mixture, saving the other half for topping or serving.

5. Freeze

  • Transfer the mixture into a loaf pan or airtight container.
  • Drizzle the remaining salted caramel sauce over the surface, then use a knife or skewer to create a marbled effect.
  • Cover and freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight.

6. Serve and Enjoy

After freezing, the ice cream needs to soften slightly (about 5 minutes at room temperature) before scooping. Scoop generous portions into bowls or cones and, if you want, drizzle extra salted caramel sauce on top for that added wow factor.

Pro Tips for the Best Results

  • Use high-quality cocoa powder for brownies; it makes a noticeable difference in flavor.
  • Be patient melting the sugar for caramel — rushing it may cause burning or crystallization.
  • For extra crunch, consider folding in chopped pecans or walnuts along with the brownies.
  • If you want a more intense chocolate flavor, fold in chocolate chips or chunks.
  • The sea salt used in the caramel should be flaky to provide that delightful texture burst.
  • Store leftover ice cream in an airtight container to prevent freezer burn and maintain creaminess.

Variations and Add-Ons to Customize Your Ice Cream

This recipe is a fantastic base to let your creative dessert instincts run wild. Here are some ideas:

  • Espresso Swirl: Add a tablespoon of espresso powder dissolved in a little hot water to the ice cream base for a mocha twist.
  • Peanut Butter Ribbon: Swirl in peanut butter for a nutty complement to the chocolate and caramel.
  • Salted Caramel Pretzel: Fold in crushed pretzels for a satisfying salty crunch.
  • Boozy Boost: Add a few tablespoons of bourbon or rum to both the caramel sauce and ice cream base for adult-only indulgence.
  • Vegan Version: Substitute heavy cream with chilled coconut cream, and use vegan brownie recipes and vegan caramel alternatives.

Storing and Serving Suggestions

This ice cream freezes exceptionally well for up to two weeks but is best enjoyed within a few days for optimal texture and flavor. Serve in bowls, cones, or as part of a decadent dessert parfait with whipped cream and nuts on top. It also pairs wonderfully with warm fruit pies or molten chocolate cakes.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • Super simple, no fancy equipment needed
  • Combines multiple indulgent flavors in one dessert
  • Easy to customize with add-ins and swirls
  • Perfect to impress guests or satisfy family sweet tooth cravings
  • Make-ahead friendly — ideal for summer gatherings or holiday celebrations

Final Thoughts

Creating a homemade no-churn salted caramel brownie ice cream is surprisingly accessible and rewarding. It lets you enjoy artisanal flavors from your own kitchen while skipping the complexity of churning machines. The result? An indulgent mix of fudgy brownies, luscious salted caramel, and creamy ice cream that’s both nostalgic and sophisticated.

Next time you want a spectacular dessert that will wow everyone around the table, give this recipe a try. You’ll see that the best ice cream is the one you craft yourself — with love, patience, and a bit of caramel magic.
