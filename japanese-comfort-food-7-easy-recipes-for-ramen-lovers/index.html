Japanese Comfort Food: 7 Easy Recipes for Ramen Lovers


Japanese comfort food has a way of warming you from the inside out. The rich flavors, the satisfying textures, and the sense of nostalgia that come with each bite make it irresistible. If you’re a ramen enthusiast or someone looking to explore more of Japan’s homey cuisine, you’re in for a treat. This blog post will guide you through seven easy recipes that will bring the comfort and authenticity of Japanese cooking straight to your kitchen.

Whether you’re craving the umami-packed broth of a steaming bowl of ramen, the comforting embrace of a rice dish, or the crispy bite of savory gyoza, these recipes will become your go-to options when you need something filling and heartwarming. Let’s dive right in.

1. Classic Shoyu Ramen

Shoyu ramen is one of the most popular types of ramen in Japan, known for its soy sauce-based broth that strikes the perfect balance between savory, salty, and slightly sweet. It’s the type of dish that feels like a hug in a bowl, offering layers of deep flavor that get better with every spoonful. Here’s how you can recreate this comforting classic in your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced thinly
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 packs of ramen noodles
  • 2 boiled eggs (soft-boiled)
  • 1 cup sliced green onions
  • 1/2 cup nori (seaweed), shredded
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 1/2 cup cooked chicken or pork slices (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking them until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes. Add the chicken broth, water, soy sauce, and mirin. Stir and bring to a simmer. Let the broth cook for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Cook the Noodles: In a separate pot, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Ramen: Divide the cooked noodles between two bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles. Top each bowl with boiled eggs, sliced green onions, nori, bamboo shoots, and your choice of protein (chicken or pork).

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Add a drizzle of soy sauce if desired and serve your ramen hot.

2. Miso Ramen

For those who love a rich, creamy broth with a deep umami flavor, miso ramen is the perfect choice. The combination of miso paste and chicken broth creates a comforting base that’s flavorful and satisfying. Here’s how to make this popular variation.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced thinly
  • 2 tbsp miso paste
  • 2 packs ramen noodles
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions

Instructions:

  1. Make the Broth: Heat sesame oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger, sautéing for about 1 minute. Stir in the white miso paste and soy sauce, then pour in the chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Noodles: Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions, drain, and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Dish: Divide the noodles into bowls. Pour the miso broth over the noodles and add toppings like corn, bamboo shoots, boiled eggs, and chopped green onions.

  4. Serve: Top with chili oil or sesame seeds for extra flavor. Enjoy the hearty richness of this comforting bowl of miso ramen.

3. Tonkotsu Ramen

Tonkotsu ramen is a beloved dish known for its rich, creamy pork bone broth that takes hours to develop its full flavor. While it may take some time, the results are undeniably worth the effort. With this recipe, you can enjoy a bowl of authentic tonkotsu ramen from the comfort of your home.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups pork bone broth (or substitute with chicken broth)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced thinly
  • 2 packs ramen noodles
  • 1/2 cup sliced pork belly (or pork shoulder)
  • 2 boiled eggs (soft-boiled)
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, heat sesame oil and sauté garlic and ginger for about 1 minute. Add the pork bone broth, soy sauce, and miso paste, stirring to combine. Bring the broth to a simmer, cooking for 10-15 minutes to develop the flavors.

  2. Cook the Noodles: While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain the noodles and set them aside.

  3. Assemble the Ramen: Place the cooked noodles in a bowl. Pour the tonkotsu broth over the noodles. Add slices of pork, a soft-boiled egg, and chopped green onions.

  4. Serve: Optionally, top with additional toppings such as nori, bamboo shoots, or chili oil for an extra kick.

4. Chashu Pork Belly

Chashu is a flavorful, melt-in-your-mouth braised pork belly that’s commonly used as a topping for ramen. It’s slow-cooked until tender and pairs perfectly with ramen noodles. Making it at home adds a rich, savory element to your ramen experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly (boneless)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced thinly
  • 1 green onion stalk

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pork Belly: In a large pan, combine soy sauce, sake, mirin, sugar, garlic, ginger, and green onion. Place the pork belly in the pan and cover it with the liquid mixture. Bring it to a simmer and cook on low for about 2-3 hours until the pork is tender and the flavors are infused.

  2. Slice and Serve: Remove the pork belly from the pan, slice it into thin pieces, and serve it as a topping for ramen.

5. Gyoza (Japanese Dumplings)

Gyoza are a beloved snack or side dish in Japan, often enjoyed with ramen. These dumplings are stuffed with a savory filling and can be pan-fried to a crispy golden-brown perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1/4 cup chopped cabbage
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 20 gyoza wrappers
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Filling: In a bowl, combine ground pork, chopped cabbage, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Mix until all ingredients are well combined.

  2. Assemble the Gyoza: Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each gyoza wrapper. Moisten the edges of the wrapper with water, fold it in half, and pinch the edges together to form a pleated shape.

  3. Cook the Gyoza: Heat vegetable oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the gyoza in the pan and cook until the bottoms are golden brown. Add a small amount of water to the pan, cover it, and steam the gyoza for 5 minutes until cooked through.

  4. Serve: Serve the gyoza with soy sauce and a little vinegar for dipping.

6. Katsudon (Pork Cutlet Rice Bowl)

Katsudon is a hearty rice bowl topped with a crispy pork cutlet, an egg, and a flavorful sauce. It’s a comforting meal that’s perfect for a filling lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless pork chops
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg (for breading)
  • 1/2 cup flour (for breading)
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 eggs (for sauce)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup dashi (fish stock)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pork Cutlets: Coat the pork chops with flour, then dip them into beaten egg, and finally coat them with panko breadcrumbs. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat and cook the pork until golden and crispy, about 4-5 minutes on each side. Remove and set aside.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a separate pan, combine soy sauce, mirin, dashi, and sugar. Bring to a simmer and cook for 3-5 minutes.

  3. Assemble the Katsudon: Place the cooked rice in a bowl. Slice the fried pork cutlet and place it on top of the rice. Pour the simmering sauce over the pork. Crack the eggs directly into the sauce and let them cook until the whites are set but the yolks remain soft.

  4. Serve: Serve the katsudon hot, garnished with chopped green onions.

7. Takoyaki (Octopus Balls)

Takoyaki is a popular street food in Japan, made from batter filled with diced octopus, tempura bits, and pickled ginger. It’s often served with a savory sauce and bonito flakes. While it’s usually made on a special takoyaki pan, you can easily adapt the recipe to make it at home.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup takoyaki flour (or all-purpose flour)
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup diced octopus
  • 1/4 cup tempura bits
  • 2 tbsp pickled ginger, chopped
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • Takoyaki sauce (or Worcestershire sauce)
  • Bonito flakes

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a bowl, whisk together takoyaki flour, water, and egg until smooth.

  2. Make the Takoyaki: Heat a takoyaki pan (or a regular frying pan) and lightly oil it. Pour the batter into the holes, then add octopus, tempura bits, pickled ginger, and green onions into each section. Let them cook for a few minutes until golden and crispy on the outside.

  3. Serve: Drizzle takoyaki sauce over the cooked balls and sprinkle with bonito flakes for extra flavor.

Final Thoughts

Japanese comfort food offers a wide variety of flavors and textures that will satisfy any craving. Whether you prefer the rich, savory broth of ramen, the crispy satisfaction of gyoza, or the heartiness of katsudon, these easy recipes will bring a piece of Japan to your table. The beauty of Japanese comfort food lies in its versatility and the sense of warmth it brings to any meal. Enjoy these dishes with loved ones, and soon they’ll become your own favorites too!
