Family-Favorite Holiday Breakfast Recipes to Start the Day Right


The holiday season is a time for togetherness, joy, and of course, delicious food. While the main meals often steal the spotlight, breakfast is an equally important meal that sets the tone for the day. This year, gather your family around the table for some scrumptious holiday breakfast recipes that will warm hearts and fill bellies. From sweet treats to savory delights, these recipes are guaranteed to become family favorites.

1. Cinnamon Roll Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of refrigerated cinnamon rolls
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • Icing from the cinnamon roll cans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Cinnamon Rolls: Open the cinnamon roll cans and cut each roll into quarters. Place the pieces in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.

  3. Mix the Custard: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla, cinnamon, and maple syrup until well combined.

  4. Combine: Pour the egg mixture evenly over the cinnamon roll pieces, ensuring all are coated.

  5. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the casserole is puffed and golden brown.

  6. Drizzle Icing: Once cooled slightly, drizzle the icing over the top. Serve warm and watch everyone come back for seconds!

Why It’s a Favorite:

This cinnamon roll casserole combines the beloved flavors of cinnamon rolls with the ease of a casserole. It’s perfect for feeding a crowd and makes the kitchen smell amazing!

2. Savory Breakfast Quiche

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Swiss)
  • 1 cup diced vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, onions)
  • 1/2 cup cooked and crumbled bacon or sausage (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Crust: Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and pre-bake for 10 minutes to set.

  3. Mix Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and cream. Stir in the cheese, vegetables, meat (if using), salt, and pepper.

  4. Fill and Bake: Pour the mixture into the pre-baked crust. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top.

  5. Serve: Allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve with a light salad for a balanced holiday breakfast.

Why It’s a Favorite:

A quiche is versatile and can be tailored to suit everyone’s tastes. Plus, it can be made in advance and served warm or at room temperature, making it a great choice for busy holiday mornings.

3. Holiday Pancake Stack

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional toppings: whipped cream, fresh berries, maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla.

  3. Combine: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined (lumps are okay).

  4. Cook Pancakes: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet and cook until bubbles form on the surface. Flip and cook until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  5. Stack and Serve: Stack pancakes on a plate, top with whipped cream, fresh berries, and syrup. Enjoy the delightful combination of flavors!

Why It’s a Favorite:

Pancakes are a timeless breakfast option that brings comfort and smiles. The joy of stacking them high and customizing with favorite toppings makes this a highlight of any holiday breakfast.

4. Sweet Potato Hash

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (parsley or chives)

Instructions:

  1. Cook Sweet Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced sweet potatoes and cook for about 10 minutes or until they begin to soften.

  2. Add Vegetables: Stir in the bell pepper and onion. Season with salt and pepper, and cook until everything is tender, about 5 more minutes.

  3. Fry Eggs: Make four wells in the hash and crack an egg into each well. Cover the skillet and cook until the eggs are set to your liking.

  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh herbs and serve hot. This dish pairs wonderfully with toast or avocado on the side.

Why It’s a Favorite:

This vibrant and nutritious hash is a fantastic way to start the day. The sweetness of the potatoes combines beautifully with the savory eggs, creating a satisfying meal that feels wholesome.

5. Overnight Oats with Holiday Spices

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk (or almond milk)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup diced apples or pears
  • Chopped nuts for topping

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl or jar, mix rolled oats, milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and maple syrup. Stir in the diced fruit.

  2. Refrigerate: Cover and refrigerate overnight.

  3. Serve: In the morning, stir the mixture and top with nuts for added crunch. Enjoy your healthy and delightful breakfast!

Why It’s a Favorite:

Overnight oats are incredibly versatile and can be prepared the night before, saving precious time on busy mornings. The holiday spices add a festive touch that everyone will love.

6. Fruit and Yogurt Parfaits

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups Greek yogurt
  • 2 cups granola
  • 4 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Honey for drizzling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Layer Ingredients: In clear glasses or bowls, layer yogurt, granola, and berries. Repeat layers until the glasses are filled.

  2. Drizzle Honey: Finish with a drizzle of honey on top if desired.

  3. Serve: Serve immediately for a refreshing and nutritious breakfast.

Why It’s a Favorite:

These parfaits are not only visually appealing but also provide a nutritious start to the day. The combination of creamy yogurt, crunchy granola, and fresh fruit makes for a delightful breakfast that everyone can enjoy.

Conclusion

This holiday season, elevate your breakfast game with these family-favorite recipes that are sure to impress. Each dish brings its unique flavors and traditions to the table, creating cherished memories around shared meals. Whether you opt for the sweetness of a cinnamon roll casserole or the heartiness of a veggie-packed quiche, your family is bound to feel the warmth of the holidays in every bite.

So gather your loved ones, whip up a delicious holiday breakfast, and start each day with joy and laughter. Happy cooking!
