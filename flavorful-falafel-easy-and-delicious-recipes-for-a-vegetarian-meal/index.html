Flavorful Falafel: Easy and Delicious Recipes for a Vegetarian Meal


Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish that has gained international acclaim for its delicious flavor and versatility. Made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, falafel is a fantastic option for vegetarians looking to add more protein to their diet. In this blog post, we will explore some easy and mouthwatering falafel recipes that will surely tantalize your taste buds.

Classic Chickpea Falafel

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Cooking time: 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in water overnight. This will soften them and make them easier to blend.

  2. Drain the chickpeas and pat them dry using a clean towel. Transfer them to a food processor along with the onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, baking soda, and salt.

  3. Pulse the mixture until it becomes a coarse paste. You don’t want it to be too smooth; some texture is desirable.

  4. Transfer the falafel mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for 30 minutes. This will help the mixture firm up, making it easier to shape into patties.

  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot. You want enough oil to fully submerge the falafel.

  6. Shape the falafel mixture into small patties, about the size of a golf ball. Gently drop them into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per side.

  7. Remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  8. Serve the classic chickpea falafel hot with pita bread, tahini sauce, and a fresh salad. Enjoy!

Spicy Fava Bean Falafel

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 15 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried fava beans
  • 1 small red onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Start by soaking the dried fava beans in water overnight. This will soften them and make them easier to blend.

  2. Drain the fava beans and pat them dry using a clean towel. Transfer them to a food processor along with the red onion, garlic, mint leaves, parsley, cumin, coriander, chili flakes, and salt.

  3. Pulse the mixture until it becomes a coarse paste. Similar to the chickpea falafel, you want some texture in the mixture.

  4. Transfer the falafel mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up.

  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot. Ensure there is enough oil to fully submerge the falafel.

  6. Shape the falafel mixture into small patties and carefully drop them into the hot oil. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, approximately 3-4 minutes per side.

  7. Once cooked, remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to remove excess oil.

  8. Serve the spicy fava bean falafel with warm pita bread, a tangy yogurt sauce, and a side of pickled vegetables for an extra kick. Enjoy the explosion of flavors!

Conclusion

Falafel is a fantastic option for vegetarians and anyone looking to incorporate more plant-based protein into their diet. Whether you prefer the classic chickpea falafel or the spicy fava bean variation, these recipes are sure to please your taste buds. Serve them with pita bread, fresh salad, and a variety of sauces and condiments to create a satisfying and flavorful vegetarian meal.
