Traditional Middle Eastern Falafel: Gluten-Free and Flavorful


Falafel is a beloved Middle Eastern dish that has gained international popularity for its delicious flavors and vegetarian-friendly ingredients. Made from a combination of chickpeas, herbs, and spices, falafel is not only a gluten-free option but also a great source of plant-based protein. In this recipe, we will explore how to make traditional Middle Eastern falafel that is both gluten-free and bursting with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Oil for frying (such as vegetable or canola oil)

Instructions

  1. Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in cold water overnight. This will soften them and make them easier to blend.

  2. Once the chickpeas have soaked, drain and rinse them thoroughly. Place them in a large bowl.

  3. In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, salt, pepper, and baking soda. Pulse until the mixture is well combined and has a coarse texture. Be careful not to over-process, as the falafel should have some texture.

  4. Transfer the falafel mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the mixture will make it easier to shape into balls or patties.

  5. While the falafel mixture is chilling, heat oil in a deep pan or pot for frying. The oil should be hot but not smoking.

  6. Take out the chilled falafel mixture and shape it into small balls or patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. You can use your hands or a falafel scoop to achieve uniform shapes.

  7. Carefully drop the falafel balls or patties into the hot oil, frying them in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Cook for about 4-5 minutes, or until the falafel is golden brown and crispy on the outside. Flip them once during cooking to ensure even browning.

  8. Once cooked, remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  9. Serve the falafel hot with your choice of accompaniments, such as pita bread, tahini sauce, hummus, or a fresh salad. Traditional Middle Eastern condiments like pickles, tomatoes, and cucumbers also pair well with falafel.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the falafel gluten-free, ensure that the baking soda you use is labeled gluten-free.
  • For added flavor, you can include a pinch of ground cardamom or cinnamon to the falafel mixture.
  • If you prefer a healthier alternative to frying, you can bake the falafel in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 20-25 minutes, flipping them halfway through.

Falafel is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various ways. Whether you stuff it into a pita pocket, serve it over a bed of salad, or enjoy it as a snack, traditional Middle Eastern falafel is sure to delight your taste buds. So why not give this gluten-free and flavorful recipe a try and experience the true essence of Middle Eastern cuisine?
