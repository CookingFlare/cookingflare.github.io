Healthy and Hearty: Slow Cooker Beef Stew Recipe


There’s something comforting about a warm bowl of beef stew on a chilly day. The tender chunks of beef, the rich broth, and the medley of vegetables all come together to create a truly satisfying meal. And when it comes to convenience, nothing beats a slow cooker. So why not combine the two and make a healthy and hearty slow cooker beef stew?

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 stalks of celery, sliced
  • 1 cup of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This step helps to develop the flavor of the meat and gives it a nice sear. Transfer the meat to the slow cooker.

  2. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic becomes fragrant. Transfer the onion and garlic mixture to the slow cooker.

  3. Add the sliced carrots, celery, mushrooms, and frozen peas to the slow cooker.

  4. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the beef broth, red wine (if using), tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Pour this mixture over the ingredients in the slow cooker.

  5. Give everything a good stir to ensure that all the ingredients are well combined.

  6. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low heat for 8-10 hours or on high heat for 4-6 hours. The long, slow cooking time allows the flavors to meld together and the meat to become tender.

  7. Once the cooking time is up, remove the bay leaves and give the stew a taste. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  8. Serve the slow cooker beef stew hot with crusty bread or over a bed of fluffy mashed potatoes. The choice is yours!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can add a splash of balsamic vinegar or a tablespoon of soy sauce to the stew.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables based on your preference or what you have on hand. You can add potatoes, parsnips, or even green beans.
  • If you want to thicken the broth, you can mix a tablespoon of cornstarch with a little water and stir it into the stew during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for future meals.

Conclusion

There’s no denying the appeal of a slow cooker beef stew. It’s a meal that warms both the body and the soul, and with this healthy and hearty recipe, you can enjoy it without any guilt. The slow cooker does all the work for you, allowing you to come home to a delicious and satisfying meal. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and let your slow cooker work its magic. Your taste buds will thank you!
