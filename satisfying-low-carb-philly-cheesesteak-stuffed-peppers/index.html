Satisfying Low Carb Philly Cheesesteak Stuffed Peppers


If you’re a fan of the classic Philly cheesesteak but want to cut down on carbs, then this recipe is for you. These low-carb Philly cheesesteak stuffed peppers are a delicious and satisfying alternative to the traditional sandwich. Packed with flavor and easy to make, this dish is perfect for anyone looking to enjoy a low-carb, high-protein meal. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to make these mouthwatering stuffed peppers.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound of thinly sliced roast beef
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 8 slices of provolone cheese
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prep the Peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Rinse the peppers and pat them dry. Place the peppers in a baking dish, cut side up.

  3. Sauté the Onions: In a skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and sauté until they are soft and caramelized, about 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Prepare the Beef: In the same skillet, add the remaining olive oil and heat over medium-high heat. Add the thinly sliced roast beef and season with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the beef is heated through.

  5. Assemble the Peppers: Line the inside of each bell pepper with a slice of provolone cheese. Divide the sautéed onions among the peppers, layering them on top of the cheese. Then, evenly distribute the roast beef among the peppers, stacking it on top of the onions. Finally, place another slice of provolone cheese on top of the beef.

  6. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake the stuffed peppers in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.

  7. Garnish and Serve: Once the peppers are cooked, remove them from the oven and garnish with fresh parsley. Let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These low carb Philly cheesesteak stuffed peppers can be enjoyed on their own as a satisfying meal. You can also pair them with a side salad or roasted vegetables for a complete and balanced dinner. They make a great addition to any low-carb meal plan and are perfect for meal prep as well.

Conclusion

These low carb Philly cheesesteak stuffed peppers are a flavorful and satisfying twist on the classic sandwich. Whether you’re following a low-carb lifestyle or just looking for a delicious new recipe to try, these stuffed peppers are sure to become a favorite. They’re easy to make, packed with protein, and full of the flavors you love from a traditional Philly cheesesteak. Give this recipe a try and enjoy a hearty, low-carb meal that’s perfect for any occasion.
