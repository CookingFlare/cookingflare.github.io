Buttery Duchess Potatoes Recipe for a Fancy Side Dish


If you’re looking to impress your guests with a fancy side dish that is both delicious and visually stunning, then look no further than this Buttery Duchess Potatoes recipe. These elegant and creamy potato puffs are the perfect accompaniment to any special meal, whether it’s a holiday feast or an intimate dinner party. With their golden, crispy exterior and soft, buttery interior, Duchess Potatoes are sure to elevate any dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of potatoes (preferably Russet or Yukon Gold)
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives, finely chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into evenly sized chunks. Place the potatoes in a large pot of salted water and bring to a boil. Cook until the potatoes are fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes.

  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Using a potato masher or a ricer, mash the potatoes until smooth and free of lumps.

  3. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  4. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Once melted, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and heavy cream until well combined. Slowly pour the egg mixture into the mashed potatoes, stirring continuously to incorporate.

  6. Gradually add the melted butter to the potato mixture, stirring until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Transfer the potato mixture into a piping bag fitted with a large star tip. Pipe the potatoes onto the prepared baking sheet in small mounds, about 2 inches in diameter.

  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the Duchess Potatoes are golden brown and crispy on the outside.

  9. Once baked, remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before transferring to a serving platter.

  10. Garnish with freshly chopped chives for a pop of color and added flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra decadent twist, you can sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the piped potatoes before baking.
  • If you want to make the Duchess Potatoes ahead of time, you can pipe them onto the baking sheet and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, simply pop them in the oven and bake as directed.
  • To add a hint of garlic flavor, you can mix in 1-2 cloves of minced garlic into the potato mixture before piping.
  • Duchess Potatoes are a versatile side dish that pairs well with a variety of main courses. Serve them alongside roast beef, grilled chicken, or seared salmon for a complete and elegant meal.

Now that you have this impressive Buttery Duchess Potatoes recipe in your culinary repertoire, you’ll be able to effortlessly elevate any meal to new heights. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to treat yourself to a fancy side dish, these golden puffs of deliciousness are sure to impress. So go ahead, get creative, and enjoy the culinary journey of mastering the art of Duchess Potatoes!
