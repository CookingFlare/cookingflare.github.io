Berry Burst: Mixed Berry Turnovers for a Quick and Delicious Dessert


If you’re in the mood for a delightful dessert that combines the sweetness of mixed berries with the flakiness of a turnover, then you’re in for a treat! These Berry Burst Mixed Berry Turnovers are just what you need to satisfy your sweet tooth. With a buttery and crispy crust, and a burst of juicy berries, these turnovers are sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 3 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Slowly drizzle in the ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a medium bowl, combine the mixed berries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Gently toss until the berries are coated evenly. Set aside.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a 1/4-inch thick rectangle. Cut the dough into 6-inch squares.

  6. Spoon a generous amount of the berry filling onto one half of each square, leaving a small border around the edges. Fold the other half of the square over the filling, creating a triangle. Press the edges together firmly to seal the turnovers.

  7. Transfer the turnovers to the prepared baking sheet and brush the tops with a beaten egg for a golden finish (optional).

  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the turnovers are golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  9. While the turnovers are cooling, prepare the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  10. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled turnovers, and allow it to set for a few minutes before serving.

These Berry Burst Mixed Berry Turnovers are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, when the crust is still warm and flaky, and the filling is gooey and bursting with flavor. They make a perfect dessert for any occasion, whether it’s a family gathering or a cozy night in. Serve them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgence.

So why wait? Give this recipe a try and let the berry burst bring a burst of sweetness to your dessert table. Enjoy!

Note: This recipe makes approximately 6 turnovers. Feel free to double or triple the ingredients to make more, depending on your needs.
